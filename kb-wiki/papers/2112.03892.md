# Event topology and global observables in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Particle production and event topology are very strongly correlated in high-energy hadronic and nuclear collisions. Event topology is decided by the underlying particle production dynamics and medium effects. Transverse spherocity is an event shape observable, which has been used in pp and heavy-ion collisions to separate the events based on their geometrical shapes. It has the unique capability to distinguish between jetty and isotropic events. In this work, we have implemented transverse spherocity in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV using A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT). While awaiting for experimental explorations, we perform a feasibility study of dependence of transverse spherocity on some of the global observables in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies. These global observables include the Bjorken energy density ($ε_{\rm B_j}$), speed of sound ($c_{\rm s}^2$) in the medium and the kinetic freeze-out properties for different collision centralities. The present study reveals about the usefulness of event topology dependent measurements in heavy-ion collisions in contrast to proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

Transverse spherocity can separate events by geometrical shape in Pb-Pb collisions, but its sensitivity to global observables depends on the observable: Bjorken energy density and squared speed of sound are independent of spherocity selection, while kinetic freeze-out parameters show a clear dependence, especially in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (version 2.26t7, string melting mode) to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. They classified events into jetty (lowest 20% of transverse spherocity) and isotropic (highest 20%) classes for eight centrality bins, then computed the Bjorken energy density, the squared speed of sound from double-Gaussian fits to pseudorapidity distributions, and kinetic freeze-out temperature and radial flow velocity from simultaneous Boltzmann-Gibbs blastwave fits to pion, kaon, and proton pT spectra.

## Key numbers

- Bjorken energy density in all centrality classes exceeds the lattice QCD critical energy density of 1 GeV/fm^3 for deconfinement, but shows no dependence on spherocity selection.
- Squared speed of sound c_s^2 decreases from central to peripheral collisions, but is similar for all spherocity classes within uncertainty.
- For the 0-10% centrality class: mean transverse radial flow velocity <beta_T> = 0.539 +/- 0.007 (high-S0), 0.543 +/- 0.006 (S0-integrated), 0.545 +/- 0.007 (low-S0); kinetic freeze-out temperature T_kin = 0.124 +/- 0.002 GeV (high-S0), 0.123 +/- 0.002 GeV (S0-integrated), 0.124 +/- 0.002 GeV (low-S0).
- For the 70-100% centrality class: <beta_T> = 0.437 +/- 0.010 (high-S0), 0.407 +/- 0.015 (S0-integrated), 0.307 +/- 0.021 (low-S0); T_kin = 0.136 +/- 0.003 GeV (high-S0), 0.145 +/- 0.007 GeV (S0-integrated), 0.163 +/- 0.008 GeV (low-S0).
- Spherocity cuts for 0-10% centrality: low-S0 range 0 to 0.880, high-S0 range 0.953 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the use of transverse spherocity from pp collisions (where it was applied by Cuautle et al. (2014, 2015)) into heavy-ion collisions, following the authors' prior study (Mallick et al., 2020) that found anisotropic flow depends on spherocity in heavy-ion systems. It is a feasibility study using the AMPT model, awaiting experimental verification at the LHC. The results show that event-topology-dependent measurements can reveal new information about kinetic freeze-out properties, but that initial-state observables like Bjorken energy density and speed of sound are insensitive to event shape in heavy-ion collisions due to counter-balancing effects from multiplicity and mean transverse mass.
