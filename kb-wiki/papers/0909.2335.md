# Baryon Resonance Analysis from MAID

> The unitary isobar model MAID2007 has been used to analyze the recent data of pion electroproduction. The model contains all four-star resonances in the region below W=2 GeV and both single-Q^2 and Q^2 dependent transition form factors could be obtained for the Delta, Roper, D13(1520), S11(1535), S31(1620), S11(1650), D15(1675), F15(1680) and P13(1720). From the complete world data base, including also pi- data on the neutron, also Q^2 dependent neutron form factors are obtained. For all transition form factors we also give convenient numerical parameterizations that can be used in other reactions. Furthermore, we show how the transition form factors can be used to obtain empirical transverse charge densities and our first results are given for the Roper, the S11 and D13 resonances.

## Key claim
The unitary isobar model MAID2007 provides transition form factors for all 13 four-star resonances below W=2 GeV from pion electroproduction data, but the resonance-background separation becomes model-dependent above the two-pion threshold, and model uncertainties can be as large as 50% for the longitudinal amplitudes of the D13 and F15 resonances.

## Method
The unitary isobar model MAID2007 separates the T-matrix into background and resonance contributions using a K-matrix approximation, where background terms include Born terms with energy-dependent mixing of pseudovector-pseudoscalar pi-NN coupling and t-channel vector meson exchanges, while resonance contributions use Breit-Wigner forms with phases adjusted to satisfy the Fermi-Watson theorem below two-pion threshold. The model performed single-Q^2 and Q^2-dependent (superglobal) fits to the world database of pion electroproduction, including data from JLab, Mainz, Bonn, and Bates, to extract helicity amplitudes A_{1/2}, A_{3/2}, and S_{1/2} for each resonance, with the Q^2 dependence parameterized as A(Q^2) = A(0)(1 + a1 Q^2 + a2 Q^4 + a3 Q^8) exp(-b1 Q^2).

## Key numbers
- Delta(1232) magnetic form factor: G_M^*(Q^2) = 3 G_D(Q^2) exp(-0.21 Q^2/GeV^2), known up to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- Roper P11(1440) transverse helicity amplitude zero crossing at Q^2 ≈ 0.7 GeV^2, with maximum at Q^2 ≈ 2.0 GeV^2
- S11(1535) proton transverse amplitude at photon point: A_{1/2}(0) = 66.4 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- D13(1520) proton A_{3/2}(0) = 160.6 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, A_{1/2}(0) = -27.4 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- F15(1680) proton A_{3/2}(0) = 134.3 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, A_{1/2}(0) = -25.1 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- S31(1620) PDG A_{1/2} at photon point: (27 ± 11) × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Electroproduction data available in kinematic region 1100 MeV < W < 1680 MeV and 0.4 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 1.8 GeV^2
- S/M ratio for Delta(1232) reaches magnitude around 25% at Q^2 ≈ 6 GeV^2 in JLab analysis, but only around 10% in MAID analysis

## Context
This work extends the MAID unitary isobar model analysis (MAID2007) to include all 13 four-star resonances below W=2 GeV, building on previous analyses that included only 7 resonances with mostly transverse couplings. The extracted transition form factors enable empirical mapping of quark transverse charge densities for nucleon resonances, following the approach applied to the nucleon by Miller (2007) and Carlson (2007), and to the Delta(1232) by Carlson (2007). The results address the question raised by Li and Burkert (1992) about whether the Roper resonance is a radially excited 3-quark state or a quark-gluon hybrid, with the strong longitudinal coupling found here supporting the 3-quark interpretation. Future experiments in the 1800-1900 MeV region will be needed to map out form factors for the F35(1905), P31(1910), and F37(1950) resonances.
