# Collective Properties of Low-lying Octupole Excitations in $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$

> The octupole strengths of $β$-stable nucleus $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, a neutron skin nucleus $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and a neutron drip line nucleus $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$ are studied by using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculation plus the random phase approximation (RPA) with Skyrme interaction. The collective properties of low-lying excitations are analyzed by using particle-vibration coupling. The results show that the lowest isoscalar states above threshold in $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$ are the superpositions of collective excitations and unperturbed transitions from bound state to nonresonance states. For these three nuclei, both the low-lying isoscalar states and giant isoscalar resonance carry isovector strength. The ratio B(IV)/B(IS) is checked. It is found that, for $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, the ratios are equal to $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$ in good accuracy, while for $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$, the ratios are much larger than $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$. This results from the excess neutrons with small binding energies in $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$.

## Key claim

For the neutron skin nucleus $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ and the neutron drip line nucleus $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$, the lowest isoscalar octupole states near threshold are superpositions of collective excitations and decoupled strong continuum strength from transitions to nonresonance states, not purely collective modes; the ratio of isovector to isoscalar strength for these states is much larger than $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$, unlike the beta-stable nucleus $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$ where the ratio matches this formula.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the SkM* Skyrme interaction, followed by random phase approximation (RPA) solved in coordinate space with the Green's function method to include the continuum exactly. They analyzed the collective properties of low-lying octupole excitations using particle-vibration coupling, examining transition densities, radial current components, and the ratio of isovector to isoscalar strength for the three nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, the lowest isoscalar octupole state is at Ex=3.48 MeV with strength $B(\lambda=3,IS:3^{-}\rightarrow 0^{+})=5.68\times 10^5 fm^6$, corresponding to $B(E3)=0.89\times 10^5 e^2 fm^6$ (experimental value is $1.0\times 10^5 e^2 fm^6$ at 2.61 MeV).
- For $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, the isoscalar giant resonance is at about 20.5 MeV and isovector giant resonances are mainly from 25 MeV to 35 MeV.
- For $^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}$, the ratio B(IV)/B(IS) for both the low-lying state at 3.48 MeV and the giant resonance at 20.5 MeV equals $(\frac{126-82}{208})^2$ to good accuracy.
- For $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$, the lowest isoscalar state below threshold is at Ex=1.91 MeV; the ratios B(IV)/B(IS) for the four strong isoscalar peaks below 5 MeV are larger than $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$ by factor 2 to 4.
- For $^{28}_{8}O_{20}$, the ratios B(IV)/B(IS) for the three lowest isoscalar peaks are factor 3 to 5 larger than $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$.

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent HF+RPA studies of monopole, dipole, and quadrupole giant resonances in stable and drip line nuclei (referenced as work1, work2, Ham96-2, work3, Ham97-2, work4, work5) to the octupole channel, and builds on a prior study of low-lying octupole excitation in $^{60}_{20}Ca_{40}$ (Ca60). It also tests the collective model prediction (Bohr-Tassie model) and the expected ratio B(IV)/B(IS) = $(\frac{N-Z}{A})^2$ from earlier work (work3, Cat97, Sagawa). The findings enable further study of how weakly bound neutrons modify collective modes in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the coexistence of collective and non-collective strength near threshold.
