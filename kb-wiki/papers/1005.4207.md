# Quantum response of weakly chaotic systems

> Chaotic systems, that have a small Lyapunov exponent, do not obey the common random matrix theory predictions within a wide "weak quantum chaos" regime. This leads to a novel prediction for the rate of heating for cold atoms in optical billiards with vibrating walls. The Hamiltonian matrix of the driven system does not look like one from a Gaussian ensemble, but rather it is very sparse. This sparsity can be characterized by parameters $s$ and $g$ that reflect the percentage of large elements, and their connectivity respectively. For $g$ we use a resistor network calculation that has direct relation to the semi-linear response characteristics of the system.

## Key claim

For weakly chaotic systems with a small Lyapunov exponent, the Hamiltonian matrix of the driven system is very sparse rather than resembling a Gaussian ensemble, leading to a novel prediction for the heating rate of cold atoms in optical billiards with vibrating walls that deviates from common random matrix theory predictions within a wide "weak quantum chaos" regime.

## Method

The authors model a weakly chaotic billiard with a convex wall of radius R and linear size L, using a rectangular box with a curved left wall and a moving right wall (piston). They calculate eigenvalues and eigenfunctions using the boundary element method, compute the perturbation matrix elements F_nm from the normal derivatives of eigenfunctions along the piston boundary, and characterize the matrix sparsity by parameters s and g_s, where g_s is obtained from a resistor network calculation that relates to the semi-linear response characteristics of the system.

## Key numbers

- The classical hard chaos result for the absorption coefficient is G_0 = (1/2T) * C_infinity = (1/2T) * [8/(3 pi) * (mass^2 * v_E^3)/L_x], known as the "Wall formula"
- For small frequencies, the power spectrum diverges logarithmically: tilde_C(omega << 1/t_L) approximately equals mass^2 * v_E^3 * R/(2 L_x^2) * ln(2/(omega * t_R))
- The mean level spacing is Delta_0 = 2 pi/(mass * L_x * L_y)
- For the proposed experiment with Rb-85 atoms at T approximately 0.1 microK, lambda_E = 1 micron, L = 10 microns, the dimensionless Planck constant is hbar = 0.1, Delta_L = 220 Hz, and Delta_0 = 7.5 Hz
- With 10% deformation, the dimensionless bandwidth b = (Delta_R/Delta_0) is approximately 10, and the SLRT suppression factor prediction is g_s approximately 0.1

## Context

This work extends the analysis of driven chaotic systems beyond standard random matrix theory predictions, building on prior work on nuclear friction (Wall formula), cold atoms in optical billiards, and mesoscopic conductance of ballistic rings. The paper introduces the concept of "weak quantum chaos" (WQC) as distinct from "hard quantum chaos" (HQC), where the traditional condition for quantum chaos (t_R << t_H or Delta_R >> Delta_0) is satisfied but the matrix remains sparse. This enables experimental tests with cold atoms in optical billiards where the SLRT anomaly should be observable, and connects to broader studies of spectral statistics in nearly circular stadium billiards and circular billiards with rough boundaries.
