# New mechanisms of charm production

> We discuss production of charm quarks, mesons as well as nonphotonic electrons in $pp$ scattering at RHIC. The distributions in rapidity and transverse momentum of charm and bottom quarks/antiquarks are calculated in the $k_t$-factorization approach. The hadronization of heavy quarks is done by means of fenomenological fragmentation functions and semileptonic decay functions are found by fitting semileptonic decay data. Good description of the inclusive data at large transverse momenta of electrons is obtained and a missing strength at small transverse momenta of electrons is found. In addition we discuss kinematical correlations between charged leptons from different mechanisms. Reactions initiated by purely QED $γ^*γ^*$-fusion in elastic and inelastic $pp$ collisions as well as diffractive mechanism of exclusive $c \bar c$ production are included. A good description of the dilepton invariant mass spectrum of the PHENIX collaboration is achieved. Distributions in the dilepton pair transverse momentum and in azimuthal angle between electron and positron are presented. A new mechanism of exclusive production of $c \bar c$ is discussed. Corresponding results are shown and the possibility of its identification is discussed. We discuss also production of two pairs of $c \bar c$ within a simple formalism of double-parton scattering (DPS). Very large cross sections, comparable to single-$c \bar c$ production, are predicted for LHC energies. Both total inclusive cross section as a function of energy and differential distributions are shown. We discuss a perspective how to identify the double scattering contribution.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the $k_t$-factorization approach with Kwiecinski unintegrated parton distributions gives a good description of inclusive nonphotonic electron data at large transverse momenta in $pp$ scattering at RHIC, there is a missing strength at small transverse momenta of electrons, suggesting that additional production mechanisms beyond the standard gluon-gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation are needed.

## Method

The authors calculate charm and bottom quark/antiquark production in $pp$ collisions using the $k_t$-factorization approach with unintegrated gluon (parton) distributions, including both gluon-gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation subprocesses with leading-order matrix elements for off-shell partons. Heavy quark hadronization is performed using phenomenological fragmentation functions (Peterson and Braaten-Chen-Fleming-Yuan), and semileptonic decay functions for $D$ and $B$ mesons are obtained by fitting to recent CLEO and BABAR data. Additional mechanisms are included: photon-photon fusion in elastic and inelastic $pp$ collisions, exclusive diffractive $c\bar{c}$ production via the Khoze-Martin-Ryskin approach, and double-parton scattering for two $c\bar{c}$ pair production.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the Kwiecinski unintegrated parton distributions give a good description of PHENIX and STAR nonphotonic electron data for transverse momenta above approximately 2-3 GeV, with missing strength at lower transverse momenta.
- The dielectron invariant mass distribution from PHENIX is well described for $M_{e^{+}e^{-}} > 3$ GeV; the Kwiecinski UGDF underestimates data below this value while the KMR UGDF overestimates data below $M_{e^{+}e^{-}} = 2$ GeV.
- Very large cross sections for double $c\bar{c}$ production via double-parton scattering, comparable to single $c\bar{c}$ production, are predicted for LHC energies.
- The fraction of $B$ decays relative to $D$ decays in the electron/positron spectrum shows strong dependence on the choice of factorization and renormalization scales, with better agreement to STAR data obtained using Peterson fragmentation functions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark correlations (Szczurek and Łuszczak, 2006) and inclusive charm production calculations (Cacciari, Nason, and Vogt, 2005; Łuszczak, Maciuła, and Szczurek, 2009) by incorporating new mechanisms for charm production. The paper enables future experimental identification of double-parton scattering contributions at LHC energies and provides predictions for correlation observables (dilepton pair transverse momentum and azimuthal angle between electron and positron) that have not been measured experimentally, offering new ways to test the full chain of heavy quark production, fragmentation, and decay.
