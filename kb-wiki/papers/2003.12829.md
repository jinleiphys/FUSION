# Functional renormalization group study of the critical region of the quark-meson model with vector interactions

> The critical region of the two flavour quark-meson model with vector interactions is explored using the Functional Renormalization Group technique, a non-perturbative method that takes into account quantum and thermal fluctuations. Special attention is given to the low temperature and high density region of the phase diagram, which is very important for the evolution of proto-neutron stars and to construct the equation of state of compact stars. As in previous studies, without repulsive vector interaction, an unphysical region of negative entropy density is found near the first order chiral phase transition. We explore the connection between this unphysical region and the chiral critical region, especially the first order line and spinodal lines, using also different values for vector interactions. The effect of finite vector interactions in the critical region is explored. We find that the unphysical negative entropy density region appears because the $s=0$ isentropic line, near the critical region, is displaced from its $T=0$ location. For certain values of vector interactions this region is pushed to lower temperatures and high chemical potentials in such way that the negative entropy density region on the phase diagram of the model can even disapear. In the case of finite vector interactions, the location of the critical end point has a non-trivial behaviour in the $T-μ_B$ plane, which differs from that in mean-field calculations.

## Key claim

The unphysical region of negative entropy density near the first-order chiral phase transition in the quark-meson model arises because the s=0 isentropic line is displaced from its T=0 location; for certain values of vector interactions this region can be pushed to lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials such that the negative entropy density region can even disappear, with the critical end point location showing non-trivial behavior in the T-μB plane that differs from mean-field calculations.

## Method

The authors applied the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) technique within the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) to the two-flavor quark-meson model with vector interactions, using the optimized Litim regulator. The vector fields (ω0 and ρ0^3) were treated in a mean-field approximation by freezing them via saddle-point integration, while quantum and thermal fluctuations of the scalar σ and pseudoscalar π fields were included through the Wetterich flow equation. The system of coupled partial differential equations for the effective potential and entropy density was solved numerically using a grid method on a one-dimensional discretization of the σ field, with the vector fields determined self-consistently at each momentum scale k.

## Key numbers

- The isospin asymmetry parameter used was δμ = -30 MeV to describe matter with more down quarks than up quarks
- The vector coupling parameters Gω = gω/mω and Gρ = gρ/mρ were varied to study their effect on the phase diagram
- The flow equations were integrated from an ultraviolet scale Λ down to an infrared scale k=IR, with the effective potential and entropy density computed at each momentum shell

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the quark-meson model that found an unphysical negative entropy density region near the first-order chiral phase transition, first discussed in detail by Tripolt et al. (2017). The authors build on mean-field calculations by Bratovic et al. (2012) that showed vector interactions drive the first-order transition and critical end point toward lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials. The inclusion of vector interactions is motivated by applications to neutron star physics, following approaches by Drews et al. (2014) and Zhang et al. (2017), and enables future studies of proto-neutron star evolution and compact star equations of state.
