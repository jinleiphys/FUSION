# K$^-$-proton scattering and the $Λ(1405)$ in dense matter

> The scattering of antikaons with nucleons is studied in the nuclear environment. Describing the $\lam$ as a $K^-$ proton bound state, we find, that due to the Pauli blocking of intermediate states, the mass of the $Λ(1405)$ is shifted upwards in energy, above the $K^-$ proton threshold and and its width is somewhat broadened. As a consequence the s-wave $K^-$--nucleon scattering length turns attractive at finite nucleon density ($ρ\geq 0.25 ρ_0$) leading to a mean field potential for the $K^-$ of about $\sim -100 \, \rm MeV$ in nuclear matter at ground state density. Consequences for Heavy Ion collisions and possible experimental checks for the structure of the $\lam$ are discussed. (Figures will be sent on request)

# K$^-$-proton scattering and the $\Lambda(1405)$ in dense matter

## Key claim
Assuming the $\Lambda(1405)$ is a $K^-p$ bound state rather than a genuine three-quark state, Pauli blocking of intermediate proton states in nuclear matter shifts the $\Lambda(1405)$ mass upward above the $K^-p$ threshold at densities $\rho \geq 0.5 \rho_0$, causing the s-wave $K^-$-nucleon scattering length to become attractive at finite nucleon density. This prediction depends entirely on the bound-state interpretation of the $\Lambda(1405)$; if it is a genuine three-quark state, the predicted medium effects would be absent.

## Method
A separable potential model for the $K^-p$ interaction in the isospin $I=0$ channel was solved in momentum space, including coupling to the $\pi\Sigma$ channel. The propagator for the $K^-p$ intermediate state was modified by introducing a lower momentum cutoff at the Fermi momentum $k_f$ to implement Pauli blocking, while the $\pi\Sigma$ propagator remained unaffected. The T-matrix was then evaluated to obtain the in-medium scattering amplitude and the $K^-$ optical potential, with the $I=1$ contribution treated via the impulse approximation using the empirical scattering length.

## Key numbers
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ mass shifts upward with density, reaching the $K^-p$ threshold at $\rho \simeq 0.5 \rho_0$ (nuclear matter ground state density $\rho_0$).
- The s-wave $K^-$-nucleon scattering length turns attractive at densities $\rho \geq 0.25 \rho_0$.
- The resulting $K^-$ mean field potential at nuclear matter ground state density is approximately $-100 \, \text{MeV}$ (real part).
- The free-space $K^-N$ scattering length at threshold is $a_{K^-N} = -0.15 \, \text{fm}$ (repulsive).
- The empirical $I=1$ scattering length used is $a_{I=1} = 0.37 + i\,0.6 \, \text{fm}$.
- The predicted upward shift of the $\Lambda(1405)$ relative to the in-medium $K^-N$ threshold is approximately $50 \, \text{MeV}$, compared to an estimated shift of $20-30 \, \text{MeV}$ for a genuine three-quark $\Lambda$ state based on hypernuclear data ($V_\Lambda = -28 \, \text{MeV}$).
- At finite temperature $T = 150 \, \text{MeV}$, the mass shift is smaller than at zero temperature, and the eigenvalue becomes complex for densities above $\rho \simeq 1.3 \rho_0$.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological analysis of kaonic atoms by Friedman et al. (1993), who extracted $K^-$ optical potentials of $-73 - i\,109 \, \text{MeV}$ (effective $t\rho$ fit) and $-188 - i\,73 \, \text{MeV}$ (density-dependent fit). It also addresses the discrepancy between the repulsive low-density $K^-N$ scattering length and the attractive mean field predicted by Kaplan and Nelson (1987) from chiral perturbation theory, which ignored the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. The calculation supports the bound-state interpretation of the $\Lambda(1405)$ previously studied by Dalitz et al. (1967) and Siegel and Weise (1988), and provides a dynamical explanation for the shift needed in microscopic models of kaonic atoms by Brown et al. (1978) and Mares et al. (1994). The proposed experimental test via $\gamma + p \rightarrow K^+ + \Lambda(1405)$ on nuclear targets at CEBAF could distinguish between the $K^-p$ bound-state and three-quark pictures of the $\Lambda(1405)$.
