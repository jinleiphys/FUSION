# Solution to Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis in Hybrid Axion Dark Matter Model

> Following a recent suggestion of axion cooling of photons between the nucleosynthesis and recombination epochs in the Early Universe, we investigate a hybrid model with both axions and relic supersymmetric particles. In this model we demonstrate that the 7Li abundance can be consistent with observations without destroying the important concordance of deuterium abundance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid model combining axion Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) photon cooling with long-lived decaying supersymmetric particles can bring the predicted primordial 7Li abundance into agreement with observations, but only if the decaying particles also reduce the overproduced deuterium abundance that arises in the pure axion BEC model.

## Method

The authors performed Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) network calculations using Kawano's code with Sarkar's correction for 4He abundances, incorporating JINA REACLIB Database V1.0 reaction rates for light nuclei (A ≤ 10) with uncertainties. They modeled nonthermal nucleosynthesis from radiative decay of long-lived particles using the method of Kusakabe (2006), with updated 4He photodisintegration reaction rates from laser-Compton photon measurements, scanning over the particle lifetime τX and energy injection parameter ζX while adopting the reduced baryon-to-photon ratio η = 4.593 × 10⁻¹⁰ predicted by the axion BEC model.

- Baryon-to-photon ratio from axion BEC model: η = (4.593⁺⁰·²³²₋₀·²²⁸) × 10⁻¹⁰, a factor of (2/3)³/⁴ smaller than the WMAP7 value of η = (6.225⁺⁰·³¹⁴₋₀·³⁰⁹) × 10⁻¹⁰
- Adopted observational 7Li abundance range: 1.06 × 10⁻¹⁰ < ⁷Li/H < 2.35 × 10⁻¹⁰ (95% C.L. from 3D nonlocal thermal equilibrium model of metal-poor halo stars)
- Adopted observational D abundance range: 2.45 × 10⁻⁵ < D/H < 3.24 × 10⁻⁵ (2σ from Lyman-α absorption systems)
- Successful hybrid model parameters: τX = 10⁶ s, ζX = 2 × 10⁻¹⁰ GeV, with η = 4.6 × 10⁻¹⁰
- ⁶Li production contour at observed level: ⁶Li/H = 7.1 × 10⁻¹²
- Photodisintegration threshold energies: ⁷Be+γ→³He+⁴He at 1.5866 MeV, D+γ→n+p at 2.2246 MeV

## Context

This work extends the axion BEC photon cooling model proposed by Sikivie (2009) and Erken (2011), which reduced the baryon-to-photon ratio between BBN and recombination but overproduced deuterium. The authors combine this with the long-lived decaying particle approach previously studied by Ellis (1984), Kawasaki (1994), and Kusakabe (2006), which used nonthermal photons from particle decay to photodisintegrate light elements. The hybrid model enables simultaneous consistency of 7Li and D abundances, resolving a key tension in the original axion BEC model, while also producing ⁶Li at levels matching observations of metal-poor halo stars.
