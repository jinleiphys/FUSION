# Delta baryon photoproduction with twisted photons

> A future gamma factory at CERN or accelerator-based gamma sources elsewhere can include the possibility of energetic twisted photons, which are photons with a structured wave front that can allow a pre-defined large angular momentum along the beam direction. Twisted photons are potentially a new tool in hadronic physics, and we consider here one possibility, namely the photoproduction of $Δ$(1232) baryons using twisted photons. We show that particular polarization amplitudes isolate the smaller partial wave amplitudes and they are measurable without interference from the terms that are otherwise dominant.

# Delta baryon photoproduction with twisted photons

## Key claim

Twisted photons can isolate the small electric quadrupole (E2) amplitude in Δ(1232) photoproduction by enabling transitions where the dominant magnetic dipole (M1) amplitude cancels exactly in the no-recoil limit, and contributes only very slightly (at the ~1.9% level) when recoil corrections are included. This allows direct measurement of the E2 amplitude without interference from the otherwise dominant M1 term.

## Method

The authors calculated the amplitude for γ + p → Δ(1232) using Bessel-mode twisted photons, expanding the twisted photon state as a coherent superposition of plane waves with fixed longitudinal momentum k_z, fixed transverse momentum magnitude κ, and varying azimuthal angle φ_k. They derived the transition amplitude in both the no-recoil limit (treating the Δ as infinitely heavy) and including relativistic recoil corrections using Rarita-Schwinger spinors for the spin-3/2 Δ baryon, with the nucleon target described by a wave packet localized near the photon vortex line.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) recoil speed is approximately 0.27 c when produced from a stationary proton
- The nominal size of the recoil correction term is (E_Δ - M_Δ)/(2M_Δ) ≈ 1.9%
- The photon energy to excite a Δ from a nucleon at rest is E_γ = (m_Δ² - m_N²)/(2m_N)
- The Particle Data Group quotes G_E*/G_M* ratio in the 2-3% range based on plane wave measurements
- The twisted photon cross section near the vortex center has a 1/b² singularity exclusively due to the E2 amplitude, with the expansion σ_(m_γ=2,Λ=1) ∝ 3G_E*² + G_M*² + (3G_E*²)/(bπ/λ)²
- The plane wave cross section is σ^(pw) ∝ 3G_E*² + G_M*²

## Context

This work extends the application of twisted photons from atomic physics (where optical orbital angular momentum was transferred to bound electrons in ⁴⁰Ca ions, exciting S₁/₂ to D₅/₂ transitions as reported by Schmiegelow et al. (2016)) to hadronic physics at MeV-GeV energy scales. The authors build on prior theoretical work by Jentschura and Serbo (2011) and Afanasev et al. (2013) on twisted photon states, and on the comprehensive review of Δ(1232) electromagnetic excitation by Pascalutsa, Vanderhaeghen, and Yang (2006). The results enable future experiments at proposed gamma factories (such as the CERN Gamma Factory suggested by Budker et al. (2020)) or at facilities like HIGS that are developing twisted photon capabilities, to directly measure the E2 transition form factor that probes the D-wave component of the Δ baryon and nucleon wavefunctions.
