# Rapid cooling of the compact star in Cassiopea A as a phase transition in dense QCD

> We present a model of the compact star in Cassiopea A that accounts for its unusually fast cooling behavior. This feature is interpreted as an enhancement in the neutrino emission triggered by a transition from a fully gapped, two-flavor, color-superconducting phase to a crystalline phase or an alternative gapless, color-superconducting phase. By fine-tuning a single parameter -- the temperature of this transition -- a specific cooling scenario can be selected that fits the Cas A data. Such a scenario requires a massive $M\sim 2M_{\odot}$ star and is, therefore, distinctive from models invoking canonical 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ mass star with nucleonic pairing alone.

# Rapid cooling of the compact star in Cassiopea A as a phase transition in dense QCD

## Key claim
The unusually fast cooling of the compact star in Cassiopeia A is interpreted as an enhancement in neutrino emission triggered by a transition from a fully gapped, two-flavor, color-superconducting phase to a crystalline or gapless color-superconducting phase, but only if the star is massive (∼2 M⊙) and the single free parameter, the transition temperature, is fine-tuned to fit the data.

## Method
The author models the cooling evolution of a 1.93 M⊙ compact star by solving the general relativistic equations of cooling in spherical symmetry, using a phenomenological temperature-dependent parameter ζ(T) that controls the transition from the fully gapped 2SC phase (ζ > 1) to a gapless/crystalline phase (ζ < 1) at a critical temperature T*. The neutrino emissivity and specific heat of the red-green paired quarks are interpolated between the gapped and gapless regimes using a Fermi-function form, while blue quarks are treated as passively paired with a fixed gap of 100 keV. The surface temperature is related to the isothermal core temperature via an envelope model appropriate for an accreted carbon atmosphere.

## Key numbers
- The compact star model has mass M = 1.93 M⊙ and radius R = 13.32 km, giving surface gravity g_s = 1.91 in units of 10^14 cm s^−2.
- The surface temperature of Cas A is approximately 2 × 10^6 K, inferred from thermal soft X-ray spectrum fits to a nonmagnetized carbon atmosphere.
- The gap in the red-green channel is Δ₀ = 60 MeV; the blue quark pairing gap is Δ_b = 0.15 MeV (150 keV), with critical temperature T_cb = Δ_b/1.76.
- The transition temperature T* is treated as a free parameter; fits to Cas A data are shown for T* values around 0.093 MeV with transient width w = 0.2 MeV.
- The envelope model uses parameters α = 18.1 and β = 0.55 for the surface temperature relation T_s6 = (α T_9)^β g_s^{1/4}.

## Context
This work extends models of compact star cooling that invoke nucleonic pairing and Cooper pair-breaking processes (Page 2011, Shternin 2011) or slow thermal relaxation (Blaschke 2012), but differs by requiring a massive ∼2 M⊙ star containing color-superconducting quark matter, rather than a canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star. It also contrasts with a rotationally induced compression model (Negreiros 2013) that requires millisecond spin periods. The model enables future observational discrimination between competing cooling scenarios and suggests that massive compact stars older than Cas A should be X-ray invisible with current instruments.
