# Some Aspects of Three-Quark Potentials (Part II)

> We continue our investigation of the effective string model for the triply heavy quark system, mimicking that in pure $SU(3)$ gauge theory. We present analytical and numerical studies of the three-quark potential for isosceles and collinear geometries. In the general case, we derive the asymptotic expression of the potential in the infrared limit. Here we also demonstrate the universality of the string tension and interpret the transition between two distinct regimes, occurring when one of the triangle's angles formed by the quarks is equal to $\frac{2}{3}π$, as a breaking of permutational symmetry. This symmetry breaking implies the emergence of a heavy quark dressed by gluons, transforming in the two-index antisymmetric representation. Additionally, we discuss various aspects of the $Y$- and $Δ$-laws, diquarks, and connections to lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The effective string model predicts that the three-quark potential in pure SU(3) gauge theory exhibits a transition between two distinct regimes when one angle of the triangle formed by the quarks equals 2π/3, which the author interprets as a breaking of permutational symmetry rather than a simple change in the string configuration.

## Method

The author uses the gauge/string duality with a soft wall metric model (a one-parameter deformation of Euclidean AdS5 space) to describe the triply heavy quark system. A baryonic configuration is constructed from three fundamental strings governed by the Nambu-Goto action meeting at a baryon vertex (a five-brane wrapped on an internal space) in the five-dimensional bulk, with the total action being the sum of the Nambu-Goto actions plus the vertex action. The force balance equation at the vertex is solved numerically for isosceles and collinear geometries, with the model parameters fixed by fitting lattice QCD data from prior work (Andreev 2016).

## Key numbers

* String tension σ = e γ s = 0.44 GeV² (with γ = 0.176, s = 0.44 GeV², and e the base of natural logarithms)
* Model parameter κ = -0.083 (dimensionless, from fitting lattice QCD data)
* For θ = π/6 (acute apex angle), the transition between configurations occurs at v₀ = 0.929 (dimensionless parameter v = s r_v²)
* For θ = π/2 (right triangle), the transition occurs at v₀ = 0.967
* For the equilateral case (θ = π/3), the upper bound on v is v₀ = 0.978
* The difference between the constant terms in the small-ℓ and large-ℓ expansions is 267 MeV
* The difference (3/2)C_QQb - C_3Q = 4 MeV, indicating minimal impact of the baryon vertex on three infinitely long strings

## Context

This work extends the author's previous effective string model for three-quark potentials (Andreev 2016) from equilateral triangle geometry to isosceles and collinear geometries, and derives the asymptotic expression for the potential in the infrared limit for general quark configurations. The results contradict the simple Y-law and Δ-law ansätze (Cornwall 1977; Cornell potential) which lattice QCD studies (Bali; Suganuma; Koma) have shown to be oversimplified, particularly because the subtracted potential does not vanish in the infrared limit as the Y-law predicts. The model enables refined predictions for triply heavy baryon properties that remain experimentally unobserved, and the universality of the string tension and the interpretation of the 2π/3 angle as a symmetry breaking point provide new insights for lattice QCD comparisons.
