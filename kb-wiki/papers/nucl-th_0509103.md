# Helicity asymmetries in neutrino-nucleus interactions

> We investigate the helicity properties of the ejectile in quasi-elastic neutrino-induced nucleon-knockout reactions and consider the 12C target as a test case. A formalism based on a relativistic mean-field model is adopted. The influence of final-state interactions is evaluated within a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) model. Our calculations reveal that the helicity asymmetries A_l in A(\overlineν,\overlineν'N) processes are extremely sensitive to strange-quark contributions to the weak vector form-factors. Thereby, nuclear corrections, such as final-state interactions and off-shell ambiguities in the electroweak current operators, are observed to be of marginal importance. This facilitates extracting strange-quark information from the helicity asymmetry A_l.

## Key claim

The helicity asymmetry \(A_l\) in antineutrino-induced nucleon-knockout reactions on nuclei is extremely sensitive to strange-quark contributions to the weak vector form-factors, while nuclear corrections such as final-state interactions and off-shell ambiguities in the electroweak current operators are of marginal importance, facilitating the extraction of strange-quark information.

## Method

The authors adopt a relativistic mean-field model for bound nucleon wave functions and a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) for final-state interactions. They compute the helicity asymmetry \(A_l\) for quasi-elastic neutral-current \(\nu(\overline{\nu})+^{12}\mathrm{C}\rightarrow\nu(\overline{\nu})+N+(A-1)\) reactions, using the one-boson exchange approximation and evaluating the sensitivity to strange-quark contributions by varying the strangeness radius \(r_s^2\) and magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) in the weak vector form-factors, as well as the axial strange-quark contribution \(g_A^s\).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies from 200 to 5000 MeV are considered for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) target; up to 1 GeV, \(A_l\) has opposite sign for neutrinos and antineutrinos.
- At \(\varepsilon = 1000\) MeV, final-state interactions have an almost negligible effect on \(A_l\).
- The axial strange-quark contribution is taken as \(g_A^s = -0.19\), extracted from deep-inelastic double-polarized scattering experiments.
- The strangeness radius \(r_s^2\) and magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) are taken from four models: VMD, K\(\Lambda\), NJL, and CQS(K); typical values are \(\mu_s \approx -0.3\ \mu_N\) and \(r_s^2 \approx -0.01\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The VMD model predicts a Dirac form-factor \(F_1^Z\) that overshoots the non-strangeness prediction by a factor of about three.
- The largest changes in \(A_l\) are induced by variations in the strangeness radius \(r_s^2\), while varying \(\mu_s\) has a relatively modest effect.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of helicity asymmetries in neutrino-nucleus scattering by Jachowicz et al. (2004, 2005), which used a non-relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation and focused on beam energies up to 500 MeV. It also complements prior work on cross-section ratios for extracting strange-quark information, such as the ratio of proton-to-neutron knockout in neutral-current reactions (Alberico et al., 2002; Meucci et al., 2004) and the ratio of neutral-current to charged-current cross sections (Pate et al., 2005; Van der Ventel et al., 2005). The finding that \(A_l\) is robust against nuclear corrections enables a cleaner extraction of strange-quark vector form-factor contributions from future antineutrino scattering data, in contrast to parity-violating electron scattering experiments (SAMPLE, HAPPEX, G0, A4, E158) where radiative corrections complicate the extraction of the strange axial form-factor.
