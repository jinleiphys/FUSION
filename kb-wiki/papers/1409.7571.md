# AdS/CFT for the early stages of heavy ion collisions

> I give a brief introduction to the AdS/CFT correspondence targeted at heavy-ion physicists. I also review some insights to our understanding of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions coming from selected studies made using methods of the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

The AdS/CFT correspondence provides a framework for studying the evolution of a strongly coupled plasma from a nonequilibrium state to a state described by hydrodynamics, but the results apply directly only to N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, which differs from QCD in having no running coupling, an exactly conformal equation of state, and no confinement/deconfinement transition.

## Method

The author reviews selected studies using the AdS/CFT correspondence to model the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, focusing on boost-invariant equilibration, shock wave collisions, and a hybrid model. In the boost-invariant case, Einstein's equations are solved numerically in the dual gravitational description for diverse initial conditions, with the energy density expressed via an effective temperature. For shock wave collisions, numerical relativity simulations are performed for colliding shock waves of varying thickness, and the resulting energy-momentum tensor is extracted when it becomes well described by hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- For boost-invariant plasma, the transition to viscous hydrodynamics occurs when the dimensionless product w = Teff * tau > 0.6-0.7, where Teff is the effective temperature and tau is proper time.
- At the transition to hydrodynamics, the anisotropy of the energy-momentum tensor is Delta p_L = 1 - p_L/(epsilon/3) ~ 0.7, indicating that hydrodynamization is distinct from thermalization.
- For shock wave collisions, the main features of the plasma at midrapidity are insensitive to the longitudinal structure of the initial shock waves if the longitudinal separation L satisfies L < 0.26 / T_hydr, where T_hydr is the temperature at hydrodynamization.
- In the boost-invariant context, about 240 terms in the late time expansion of the effective temperature are known numerically to high accuracy.

## Context

This review extends the line of work applying the AdS/CFT correspondence to heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier studies of boost-invariant equilibration by the author and collaborators (ref5, ref6) and pioneering numerical studies of shock wave collisions (ref16). The hybrid model described (ref20) uses AdS/CFT simulations to provide initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution, achieving good agreement with ALICE data for light particle spectra. The paper enables further investigation of nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom and their incorporation into extensions of hydrodynamics, as well as continued exploration of universal features in shock wave collisions.
