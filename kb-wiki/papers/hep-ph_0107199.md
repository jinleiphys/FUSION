# Isospin violation in pion-kaon scattering

> We consider strong and electromagnetic isospin violation in near-threshold pion-kaon scattering. At tree level, such effects are small for all physical channels. We work out the complete one-loop corrections to the process pi^- K^+ -> pi^0 K^0. They come out rather small. We also show that the corresponding radiative cross section is highly suppressed at threshold.

## Key claim

Strong and electromagnetic isospin violation in near-threshold pion-kaon scattering is small at tree level for all physical channels, and the complete one-loop corrections to the process π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰ also come out rather small, with the corresponding radiative cross section highly suppressed at threshold.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to compute strong and electromagnetic isospin violation in πK scattering, working at tree level for all physical channels and at complete one-loop order for the specific channel π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰. They included virtual photon loops, π⁰η mixing, and soft photon radiation (π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰γ), using the charged pion decay constant Fπ = 92.4 MeV and expressing quark mass insertions in terms of physical meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level isospin-symmetric S-wave scattering length: a₀⁻ = 70.8 × 10⁻³/Mπ
- π⁰η mixing angle: ε = 1.00 × 10⁻²
- Tree-level scattering length for π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰: a₀⁽²⁾ = −100.1 × 10⁻³/Mπ
- One-loop isospin-symmetric correction to a₀ for π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰: (0.121 ± 0.009) relative to tree level
- One-loop strong isospin violation correction: (0.008 ± 0.002) relative to tree level
- One-loop electromagnetic isospin violation correction: −(0.009 ± 0.008) relative to tree level
- Tree-level electromagnetic correction for π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰: +0.009 relative to tree level
- Tree-level strong isospin violation correction for π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰: +0.006 relative to tree level
- Tree-level correction for π⁺K⁺ → π⁺K⁺: −0.018 relative to isospin-symmetric value
- Tree-level correction for π⁻K⁺ → π⁻K⁺: +0.018 relative to isospin-symmetric value
- Tree-level correction for π⁰K⁺ → π⁰K⁺: −2.9 × 10⁻³/Mπ (absolute shift from a₀⁺)
- Tree-level correction for π⁰K⁰ → π⁰K⁰: +2.9 × 10⁻³/Mπ (absolute shift from a₀⁺)
- Maximal photon energy at threshold for π⁻K⁺ → π⁰K⁰γ: 0.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the one-loop ChPT predictions for πK scattering lengths and range parameters from BKM (1999) by including isospin violation due to strong and electromagnetic effects, motivated by the DIRAC collaboration's planned measurement of πK atom lifetimes at CERN. The results enable precise determination of the isovector S-wave scattering length via the modified Deser formula for πK atoms, and complement similar isospin violation studies in ππ scattering by Knecht and Urech (1998) and in πN scattering by Weinberg (1977).
