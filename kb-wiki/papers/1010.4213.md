# Polarization observables in $π^0η$-photoproduction on the proton

> For $π^0η$-photoproduction on the nucleon formal expressions are developed for the five-fold differential cross section and the recoil polarization including beam and target polarizations. The polarization observables are described by various beam, target and beam-target asymmetries for polarized photons and/or polarized nucleons. They are given as bilinear hermitean forms in the reaction matrix elements divided by the unpolarized cross section. Numerical results for the linear and circular beam asymmetries for $γp\toπ^0ηp$ are obtained within an isobar model and are compared with existing data. Predictions are also given for the target asymmetry $T_{11}$, and the beam-target asymmetries $T^c_{10}$ and $T^c_{11}$ for circularly polarized photons.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete formal framework for all polarization observables in π⁰η-photoproduction on the proton, but the numerical results are obtained within a specific isobar model whose parameters were fitted to data up to a photon lab energy of 1.4 GeV, and the model assumes the reaction is dominated by D₃₃ waves with no πη resonance in this energy region.

## Method

The authors develop formal expressions for the five-fold differential cross section and recoil polarization using a multipole expansion of the T-matrix in terms of transverse electric and magnetic multipoles, with the final state described by partial wave decompositions of the πp relative motion and the η momentum. The polarization observables are expressed as bilinear Hermitian forms in the reaction matrix elements divided by the unpolarized cross section, and numerical results are computed within an isobar model that includes background terms and resonance contributions from T=3/2 states (D₃₃(1700), D₃₃(1940), P₃₃(1600), P₃₁(1750), F₃₅(1905), and P₃₃(1920)) with intermediate ηΔ(1232) and πS₁₁(1535) configurations.

## Key numbers

- Photon lab energy range: up to ω_γ = 1.4 GeV, the region where data from Kashev and FKLO were available for fitting model parameters
- The total cross section depends only on the beam-target asymmetry T̅₁₀ᶜ for circularly polarized photons: σ = σ₀ [1 + Pᶜ_γ Pᵖ₁ T̅₁₀ᶜ cos θₛ]
- At forward and backward η emission angles (θ_η = 0 and π), the asymmetries Σ̃ˡ, T̃₁₁⁰ᶜ, and T₁ₘˡ must vanish identically
- The resonance sector includes only states with isospin T = 3/2, with the D₃₃ wave dominating the reaction in the studied energy region

## Context

This work extends the formal treatment of polarization observables from single-meson photoproduction to double-meson photoproduction, specifically π⁰η production, building on the formalism developed for pion photoproduction on the deuteron by Arenhövel (2005). The numerical results are compared with existing beam asymmetry data from ELSA (Gutz, 2008) and provide predictions for target asymmetry T₁₁ and beam-target asymmetries Tᶜ₁₀ and Tᶜ₁₁ expected from upcoming MAMI measurements, similar to the analysis of circular beam asymmetry in ππ photoproduction by Strauch (2005) and Kram (2005).
