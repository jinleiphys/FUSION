# Density-Induced Hadron-Quark Crossover via the Formation of Cooper Triples

> We discuss the hadron--quark crossover accompanied by the formation of Cooper triples (three-body counterpart of Cooper pairs) by analogy with the Bose--Einstein condensate to Bardeen--Cooper--Schrieffer crossover in two-component fermionic systems. Such a crossover is different from a phase transition, which often involves symmetry breaking. We calculate the in-medium three-body energy from the three-body $T$-matrix with a phenomenological three-body force characterizing a bound hadronic state in vacuum. With increasing density, the hadronic bound-state pole smoothly undergoes a crossover toward the Cooper triple phase where the in-medium three-body clusters coexist with the quark Fermi sea. The relation to the quarkyonic matter model can also be found in a natural manner.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a smooth hadron–quark crossover can occur via the formation of Cooper triples (three-body bound states analogous to Cooper pairs) rather than a first-order phase transition, but this conclusion is based on a simplified toy model with a constant three-body force and neglects spin, flavor, gluon, and diquark degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors employ an in-medium three-body T-matrix approach for a relativistic three-color fermionic system with a phenomenological constant three-body attractive interaction. They solve for the pole of the three-body T-matrix at zero center-of-mass momentum, incorporating Fermi distribution functions to account for Pauli blocking effects, and track how the in-medium three-body binding energy evolves as a function of quark chemical potential at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass set to \(m_q \simeq 0.34\) GeV
- Three-body coupling constant \(V_3 = -2.16 \times 10^2\) (GeV)\(^{-5}\) chosen to reproduce a vacuum baryon mass \(M_{\rm B} \simeq 0.91\) GeV
- Vacuum three-body binding energy \(E_{\rm B} = 3m_q - M_{\rm B} \simeq 0.11\) GeV
- Momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV and small imaginary energy \(\delta = 2 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV used for analytic continuation
- Continuum threshold for three quarks given by \(E_{\rm th} = 3m_q - 3\mu\)

## Context

This work extends the BEC–BCS crossover concept from two-component fermionic systems (well studied in cold atomic physics) to three-component systems relevant to QCD, building on prior theoretical proposals for Cooper triples in cold atoms (PhysRevA.86.013628, PhysRevA.96.053614) and the hadron–quark crossover scenario (Masuda 2013, 10.1093/ptep/ptt045). The results connect naturally to the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2019), which predicts a peak in the speed of sound in dense matter; the present work suggests that Cooper triple formation could provide a microscopic mechanism for that peak. Future work requires more realistic models including density-dependent quark masses, linear confining potentials, diquark correlations, and color superconductivity.
