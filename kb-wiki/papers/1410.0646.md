# Uncertainty Quantification of the Pion-Nucleon Low-Energy Coupling Constants up to Fourth Order in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We extract the statistical uncertainties for the pion-nucleon ($πN$) low energy constants (LECs) up to fourth order $\mathcal{O}(Q^4)$ in the chiral expansion of the nuclear effective Lagrangian. The LECs are optimized with respect to experimental scattering data. For comparison, we also present an uncertainty quantification that is based solely on \pin{} scattering phase shifts. Statistical errors on the LECs are critical in order to estimate the subsequent uncertainties in \textit{ab initio} modeling of light and medium mass nuclei which exploit chiral effective field theory. As an example of the this, we present the first complete predictions with uncertainty quantification of peripheral phase shifts of elastic proton-neutron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper extracts statistical uncertainties for the pion-nucleon low-energy constants up to fourth order in chiral perturbation theory by fitting directly to experimental scattering data, rather than to phase-shift analyses, and finds that the resulting LECs reproduce experimental data better than phase-shift-based fits (reduced chi-squared of 2.29 versus 3.63), though no single previous analysis is entirely consistent with the new fit at the 95% confidence level.

## Method

The authors minimized a least-squares objective function comparing chiral perturbation theory predictions to experimental differential cross-sections and polarization data from pion-proton scattering processes (pi+ p -> pi+ p, pi- p -> pi- p, and pi- p -> pi0 n), using the WI08 partial-wave analysis database with a lab-frame momentum cutoff of 160 MeV. They adopted the strong amplitudes from Krebs (2012) and the electromagnetic treatment from the WI08 analysis, then performed the optimization using the TAO package, extracting covariance and correlation matrices via a standard gradient expansion of the chi-squared function at the optimum.

## Key numbers

- Reduced chi-squared of the fit: 2.29 (compared to 3.63 for the phase-shift-based fit from Krebs 2012)
- Number of data points used: 1246 (1194 differential unpolarized cross-sections and 52 differential singly-polarized cross-sections)
- Lab-frame momentum cutoff: 160 MeV
- Number of normalization coefficients: 110, with 9 floated coefficients
- Leading-order LEC c1: -1.40 +/- 0.12 GeV^-1
- Leading-order LEC c2: +1.71 +/- 0.33 GeV^-1
- Leading-order LEC c3: -4.56 +/- 0.11 GeV^-1
- Leading-order LEC c4: +3.72 +/- 0.27 GeV^-1
- Fourth-order LEC e15: -11.91 +/- 0.87 GeV^-3
- Fourth-order LEC e16: +11.43 +/- 1.23 GeV^-3
- Fourth-order LEC e18: +0.57 +/- 1.36 GeV^-3

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of pion-nucleon LECs from peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts (Entem 2002, Ekstrom 2013) and from pion-nucleon elastic scattering phase shifts (Buttiker 2000, Fettes 2000, Krebs 2012) by grounding the analysis in experimental scattering data rather than phase-shift analyses, enabling meaningful statistical uncertainty quantification for the first time. The results constrain the sub-leading long-range three-nucleon interaction that enters at fourth order and provide uncertainty estimates critical for ab initio calculations of light and medium-mass nuclei, as demonstrated by the first complete predictions with uncertainty quantification of peripheral proton-neutron scattering phase shifts at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order.
