# Electric Polarizabilities from Lattice QCD

> The response of hadrons to electromagnetic probes is highly constrained by chiral dynamics; but, in some cases, predictions have not compared well with experimental data. The lattice can be used to test the chiral electromagnetism of hadrons and ultimately confront experiment. We use background field techniques to study the electromagnetic polarizabilities of hadrons. Focusing on simulations in background electric fields, we present preliminary results for both charged and neutral particle polarizabilities. The former are extracted using a novel method.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD with background electric fields can extract electric polarizabilities of both neutral and charged hadrons, but the results are preliminary and require refinements such as handling exceptional gauge configurations and addressing infinite volume and continuum limits.

## Method

The authors used RBC/UKQCD domain-wall fermion gauge configurations with tadpole-improved clover valence quarks, coupling a classical electric field to valence quarks by multiplying the existing color links by Abelian links. For neutral particles, they measured the single-particle energy in the background field from two-point correlation functions and fit the energy expansion in powers of the electric field. For charged particles, they used a novel method involving parabolic cylinder functions to extract the polarizability from the modified two-point correlation function behavior.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion electric polarizability: α_E^{π^0} = 0.12 ± 0.78 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Neutral kaon electric polarizability: α_E^{K^0} = 0.27 ± 0.05 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Neutral K* electric polarizability: α_E^{K^{*0}} = -1.2 ± 0.4 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Neutron electric polarizability: α_E^n = 3.6 ± 1.3 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Lambda electric polarizability: α_E^Λ = 3.1 ± 0.8 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Sigma0 electric polarizability: α_E^{Σ^0} = 3.8 ± 0.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Xi0 electric polarizability: α_E^{Ξ^0} = 2.8 ± 0.3 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Charged pion electric polarizability: α_E^{π^+} = 3.4 ± 0.4 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Charged kaon electric polarizability: α_E^{K^+} = 2.8 ± 2.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Charged K* electric polarizability: α_E^{K^{*+}} = -5.9 ± 2.6 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Proton electric polarizability: α_E^{p} = 8.8 ± 5.9 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Sigma+ electric polarizability: α_E^{Σ^+} = 5.3 ± 2.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Xi- electric polarizability: α_E^{Ξ^-} = 2.5 ± 0.1 × 10^{-4} fm^3

## Context

This work extends background field techniques from earlier lattice QCD studies (Detmold 2006) to compute electric polarizabilities for a wide range of hadrons, addressing discrepancies between chiral perturbation theory predictions and experimental data for pion polarizabilities. The results enable future lattice calculations with sea quark electric charges turned on, infinite volume and continuum limits, and comparisons with upcoming experimental results from COMPASS at CERN, MAX-lab in Lund, and the high intensity gamma source at TUNL.
