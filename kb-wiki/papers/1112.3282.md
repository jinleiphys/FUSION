# Exploring the anomaly in the interaction cross section and matter radius of 23O

> New measurements of the interaction cross sections of 22,23O at 900A MeV performed at the GSI, Darmstadt are reported that address the unsolved puzzle of the large cross section previously observed for 23O. The matter radii for these oxygen isotopes extracted through a Glauber model analysis are in good agreement with the new predictions of the ab initio coupled-cluster theory reported here. They are consistent with a 22O+neutron description of 23O as well.

## Key claim

New measurements of the interaction cross section of 23O at 900A MeV resolve the previously reported anomaly by showing a smaller cross section than earlier data, consistent with both a 22O core plus valence neutron description and new ab initio coupled-cluster calculations.

## Method

The interaction cross sections of 22,23O were measured at the GSI fragment separator FRS using the transmission method with a 4.046 g/cm2 thick carbon target at approximately 900A MeV. The oxygen isotopes were produced from fragmentation of a 1A GeV 48Ca beam on a beryllium target, and the matter radii were extracted through Glauber model analysis using both Fermi and harmonic oscillator density forms. Ab initio coupled-cluster calculations were performed using a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory with three different momentum cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Interaction cross section of 22O: 1123 ± 24 mb (statistical 18.5 mb, systematic 15.3 mb)
- Interaction cross section of 23O: 1216 ± 41 mb (statistical 33.1 mb, systematic 24.7 mb)
- Matter radius of 22O: 2.75 ± 0.15 fm (Fermi density) and 2.75 ± 0.07 fm (harmonic oscillator density)
- Matter radius of 23O: 2.95 ± 0.23 fm (Fermi density) and 2.97 ± 0.11 fm (harmonic oscillator density)
- The interaction cross section of 23O is approximately 8-9% larger than that of 22O, compared to about 16% for the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be relative to 10Be
- One-neutron separation energy of 23O: 2.7 MeV

## Context

This work resolves the long-standing puzzle from Ozawa et al. (2001) who reported an anomalously large interaction cross section for 23O that could not be explained by a 22O core plus valence neutron halo model. The new measurements show consistency with both a simple 22O+neutron description and with ab initio coupled-cluster calculations, demonstrating that 23O does not exhibit a halo structure despite earlier suggestions. The results provide a benchmark for understanding shell evolution in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes and support the role of three-body forces in defining the oxygen drip-line as discussed by Otsuka et al. (2010).
