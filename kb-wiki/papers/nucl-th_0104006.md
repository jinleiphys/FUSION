# Relativistic Calculations for Incoherent Photoproduction of Eta Mesons

> We develop a relativistic model for incoherent eta-photoproduction on nuclei. The elementary process is described using an effective Lagrangian containing photons, nucleons, the S11(1535) and D13(1520) nucleon resonances, and rho, omega, and eta mesons. The nucleon and eta wavefunctions are obtained from relativistic wave equations. Final-state interactions of the outgoing particles are included via optical potentials. The effects of these interactions are found to be large and lead to reduced cross sections.The incoherent cross sections for isovector transitions are much larger than those for isoscalar ones. The dominant contributions are those from the S11 and D13 resonances. We find important interference effects between the contributions of these two resonances. We give some detailed calculations for the cross sections for incoherent eta-photoproduction on C-12. We find that the incoherent cross section for a subset of states in the excitation energy region below 17 MeV are significantly larger than those of the coherent process. These cross sections may thus be accessible experimentally.

## Key claim

The incoherent eta-photoproduction cross section on C-12 for a subset of excited states with excitation energies below 17 MeV is significantly larger than the coherent cross section, potentially making these processes accessible experimentally, but only for isovector transitions (T=1 states), not isoscalar ones.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic model for incoherent eta-photoproduction on nuclei using an effective Lagrangian with photons, nucleons, the S11(1535) and D13(1520) resonances, and rho, omega, and eta mesons. Nucleon wavefunctions were obtained from the Dirac equation with scalar and vector potentials in the relativistic mean field approach, and eta meson wavefunctions from the Klein-Gordon equation. Final-state interactions of the outgoing eta meson were included via two different optical potentials (DW1 and DW3), and calculations were performed in both the plane wave approximation (PWA) and distorted wave approximation (DWA) for C-12, O-16, and Ca-40 targets.

## Key numbers

* The summed total incoherent cross section on C-12 for excitation energies in the range 4-17 MeV is on the order of 100 nb or more for 750 MeV photons, about an order of magnitude larger than several theoretical estimates of the coherent cross section.
* The incoherent cross section for isovector transitions (T=1) is much larger than for isoscalar transitions (T=0); for example, the (2+1;16.11) state in C-12 shows strong constructive interference between S11 and D13 contributions above about 675 MeV photon energy.
* The total cross sections for the (2+1;16.11) state peak near E_gamma = 750 MeV for all parameter sets.
* The ratio of the neutron to proton amplitudes was taken as -0.80 based on the analysis by Krusche et al. (1995) for the S11-dominated process.
* The form factor at the VNN vertex uses Lambda^2 = 1.2 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends a previous relativistic model for quasifree eta-photoproduction on complex nuclei by the same authors (Blokland and Sherif, 2000) to the incoherent case, and is the second theoretical treatment of incoherent eta-photoproduction after Bennhold and Tanabe (1992), who used a nonrelativistic coupled channel isobar model. The present relativistic calculations find larger cross sections for T=1 states and stronger suppression from final-state interactions compared to Bennhold and Tanabe's nonrelativistic results, and the summed incoherent cross sections are only about two orders of magnitude below the measured inclusive cross section for C-12 (MAMI experiment, 1999), suggesting these processes may be experimentally accessible with improved resolution.
