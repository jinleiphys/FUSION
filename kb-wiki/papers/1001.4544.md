# A Skyrme-type proposal for baryonic matter

> The Skyrme model is a low-energy effective field theory for QCD, where the baryons emerge as soliton solutions. It is, however, not so easy within the standard Skyrme model to reproduce the almost exact linear growth of the nuclear masses with the baryon number (topological charge), due to the lack of Bogomolny solutions in this model, which has also hindered analytical progress. Here we identify a submodel within the Skyrme-type low energy effective action which does have a Bogomolny bound and exact Bogomolny solutions, and therefore, at least at the classical level, reproduces the nuclear masses by construction. Due to its high symmetry, this model qualitatively reproduces the main features of the liquid droplet model of nuclei. Finally, we discuss under which circumstances the proposed sextic term, which is of an essentially geometric and topological nature, can be expected to give a reasonable description of properties of nuclei.

# A Skyrme-type proposal for baryonic matter

## Key claim

The authors identify a submodel of the Skyrme effective field theory, consisting only of a sextic term (the square of the topological baryon current) and a potential term, which possesses exact Bogomolny solutions that reproduce the linear growth of nuclear masses with baryon number at the classical level. The claim is hedged by the explicit statement that this works "at least at the classical level" and that the model "qualitatively reproduces the main features of the liquid droplet model of nuclei" rather than providing a complete quantitative description.

## Method

The authors restrict the full Skyrme Lagrangian to the sextic term L_6 (the square of the topological current, representing omega meson interactions) and a potential term L_0, omitting the quadratic sigma-model term L_2 and the quartic Skyrme term L_4. They solve the static field equations analytically using a hedgehog ansatz with the standard Skyrme potential V = 1 - cos xi, obtaining compacton-type soliton solutions that saturate a Bogomolny bound. The energy and baryon number densities are computed exactly, and the resulting soliton masses and radii are compared to experimental nuclear data.

## Key numbers

- Soliton energy is exactly linear in baryon number: E = E_0 |B|, with E_0 = 64 sqrt(2) pi mu lambda / 15, set to 931.75 MeV by fixing the B=4 solution to the mass of He-4 (3727 MeV).
- For B=1, the model gives 931.75 MeV versus the experimental 939 MeV; for B=2, 1863.5 MeV versus 1876 MeV; for B=3, 2795.25 MeV versus 2809 MeV; for B=6, 5590.5 MeV versus 5601 MeV; for B=8, 7454 MeV versus 7455 MeV; for B=10, 9317.5 MeV versus 9327 MeV. The maximal deviation is about 0.7%.
- Soliton radius follows R_B = R_0 |B|^(1/3), with R_0 = (2 sqrt(2) lambda / mu)^(1/3) fixed to 1.25 fm.
- The Bogomolny constant C_1 for the standard Skyrme potential is 32 sqrt(2) / (15 pi).
- The compacton boundary occurs at z = 4/3 in the rescaled radial coordinate, corresponding to a finite support radius tilde r = |n|^(1/3).

## Context

This work extends the study of generalized Skyrme models that include higher-derivative terms, specifically the sextic term representing omega meson interactions as introduced in earlier work (Modify Sk 2). The model is closely related to the liquid droplet model of nuclei, reproducing the bulk (volume) contribution to nuclear masses with a topological origin. The authors note that similar compact soliton solutions and Bogomolny bounds have been found in the 2+1 dimensional baby Skyrme version of the model (GP, 2008; Restricted-bS, 2009). The paper enables a new "zeroth order" approximation for nuclear structure calculations, where the exact solutions of the restricted model provide a starting point, and additional terms (L_2 and L_4) can be treated as small corrections. The authors also relate their work to a recent paper (Inf-Vec, 2009) where a Bogomolny-saturating Skyrme model was derived from coupling to an infinite tower of vector mesons.
