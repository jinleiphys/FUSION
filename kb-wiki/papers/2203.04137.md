# Linearisability of divergence-free fields along invariant 2-tori

> We find conditions under which the restriction of a divergence-free vector field $B$ to an invariant toroidal surface $S$ is linearisable. The main results are similar in conclusion to Arnold's Structure Theorems but require weaker assumptions than the commutation $[B,\nabla\times B] = 0$. Relaxing the need for a first integral of $B$ (also known as a flux function), we assume the existence of a solution $u : S \to \mathbb{R}$ to the cohomological equation $B|_S(u) = \partial_n B$ on a toroidal surface $S$ mutually invariant to $B$ and $\nabla \times B$. The right hand side $\partial_n B$ is a normal surface derivative available to vector fields tangent to $S$. In this situation, we show that the field $B$ on $S$ is either identically zero or nowhere vanishing with $B|_S/\|B\|^2 |_S$ being linearisable. We are calling the latter the semi-linearisability of $B$ (with proportionality $\|B\|^2 |_S$). The non-vanishing property relies on Bers' results in pseudo-analytic function theory about a generalised Laplace-Beltrami equation arising from Witten cohomology deformation. With the use of de Rham cohomology, we also point out a Diophantine integral condition where one can conclude that $B|_S$ itself is linearisable. The linearisability of $B|_S$ is fundamental to the so-called magnetic coordinates, which are central to the theory of magnetically confined plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the restriction of a divergence-free vector field \(B\) to an invariant toroidal surface \(S\) is either identically zero or nowhere vanishing and semi-linearisable (with proportionality \(\|B\|^2|_S\)), under the weaker assumption that a solution exists to the cohomological equation \(B|_S(u) = \partial_n B\), rather than requiring the commutation \([B,\nabla\times B] = 0\) or the existence of a first integral.

## Method

The authors use Witten-deformed cohomology to relate the existence of a solution \(u\) to the cohomological equation to the condition that the pulled-back vector field \(\imath^*B\) on \(S\) is a \(P\)-harmonic vector field with \(P = e^{-u}\). They then apply Bers' pseudo-analytic function theory and the generalised Riemann-Roch theorem for genus-1 Riemann surfaces to prove that non-zero \(P\)-harmonic 1-forms on a 2-torus are non-vanishing. Finally, they use de Rham cohomology and a Diophantine integral condition to establish full linearisability when the winding number is Diophantine.

## Key numbers

* The dimension of the real vector space of \(P\)-harmonic 1-forms on an oriented Riemannian 2-torus is \(\dim \mathcal{H}_P^1(S) = 2\).
* The dimension of the real vector space of \(P\)-harmonic vector fields on an oriented Riemannian 2-torus is \(\dim \mathcal{H}_P V(S) = 2\).
* For a strong Beltrami field with \(\nabla \times B = \lambda B\) (constant \(\lambda\)) on a toroidal boundary component \(S\), if the vector \((I(C_1,u), I(C_2,u))\) formed from integrals over generating homology cycles is Diophantine (i.e., there exists \(\gamma > 0\) and \(\tau > 1\) such that \(|\langle (I_1,I_2), k \rangle| \geq \gamma \|k\|^{-\tau}\) for all \(k \in \mathbb{Z}^2\backslash\{0\}\)), then \(S\) is a Diophantine invariant 2-torus for \(B|_S\).

## Context

This work extends Arnold's Structure Theorems (Arnold, 1974) by relaxing the requirement that \(B\) and \(\nabla\times B\) commute or that a first integral (flux function) exists. It provides necessary structural features for MHD equilibria relevant to the Grad conjecture (Grad, 1967) and to numerical codes such as the Stepped Pressure Equilibrium Code (SPEC). The results enable the construction of straight field-line coordinates (Hamada coordinates) under weaker conditions, and connect to KAM-type Diophantine conditions used by Enciso, Peralta-Salas, and collaborators (Enciso, 2021) for toroidal domains.
