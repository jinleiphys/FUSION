# Proton-neutron symmetry in 92Zr, 94Mo with Skyrme interactions in a separable approximation

> Starting from a Skyrme interaction we study the properties of the low-energy spectrum of quadrupole excitations in 90,92Zr, 92,94Mo. The coupling between one- and two-phonon terms in the wave functions of excited states are taken into account. We use the finite-rank separable approximation which enables one to perform the QRPA calculations in very large two-quasiparticle spaces. Our results from the SGII Skyrme interaction in connection with the volume pairing interaction are in reasonable agreement with experimental data. In particular, we present the successful description of the M1 transition between low-energy quadrupole excitations.

# Proton-neutron symmetry in 92Zr, 94Mo with Skyrme interactions in a separable approximation

## Key claim

Using the SGII Skyrme interaction with volume pairing, the finite-rank separable approximation reproduces the mixed-symmetry (MS) quadrupole states in 92Zr and 94Mo and yields the first successful comparison between experimental M1 transition values and those calculated with a Skyrme interaction, though the results are less accurate than more phenomenological approaches like the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) or shell model.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations with spherical symmetry, then applied the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) using the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) for the residual Skyrme interaction, keeping only the l=0 Landau-Migdal terms. They extended the space to include coupling between one-phonon and two-phonon configurations in the wave functions of excited states, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis of one- and two-phonon states with energies up to 8 MeV. The SGII Skyrme force was used in the particle-hole channel with a volume zero-range pairing force, and the single-particle continuum was discretized on a basis of 12 harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

- 92Zr: QRPA 2+1 energy = 1.7 MeV, B(E2;0+gs → 2+1) = 410 e2fm4; with phonon-phonon coupling: 2+1 energy = 1.6 MeV, B(E2) = 420 e2fm4; 2+2 energy = 2.7 MeV, B(M1;2+2 → 2+1) = 0.41 μN2 (experiment: 0.37 ± 0.04 μN2)
- 94Mo: QRPA 2+1 energy = 1.2 MeV, B(E2;0+gs → 2+1) = 1730 e2fm4; with phonon-phonon coupling: 2+1 energy = 0.5 MeV, B(E2) = 1280 e2fm4; 2+3 energy = 2.3 MeV, B(M1;2+3 → 2+1) = 0.68 μN2 (experiment: 0.56 ± 0.05 μN2)
- 90Zr: QRPA 2+1 energy = 2.8 MeV, B(E2;0+gs → 2+1) = 630 e2fm4; with phonon-phonon coupling: 2+1 energy = 2.6 MeV, B(E2) = 600 e2fm4 (experiment: 643 ± 22 e2fm4)
- 92Mo: QRPA 2+1 energy = 1.9 MeV, B(E2;0+gs → 2+1) = 1170 e2fm4; with phonon-phonon coupling: 2+1 energy = 1.9 MeV, B(E2) = 1160 e2fm4 (experiment: 1036 ± 62 e2fm4)
- Pairing strength V0 = -270 MeV fm3 for volume pairing, -420 MeV fm3 for mixed, -870 MeV fm3 for surface-peaked interaction
- Rank of separable approximation: N = 45 points for the Gauss integration formula

## Context

This work extends the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) developed by Giai, Severyukhin, and collaborators (1998, 2008) to study quadrupole excitations in the A ≈ 90 mass region, specifically the N=52 isotones 92Zr and 94Mo and the N=50 isotones 90Zr and 92Mo. It builds on earlier QPM studies of mixed-symmetry states in these nuclei by Lo Iudice and Stoyanov (2000, 2004, 2006) and shell-model calculations by Werner and collaborators (2002, 2005) and Holt and collaborators (2007). The results confirm the IBM-2 level scheme for 94Mo where the 2+3 state is the mixed-symmetry state, as identified experimentally by Pietralla and collaborators (1999). The approach enables future globally applicable microscopic analysis of quadrupole excitations in neutron-rich nuclei, though the authors note that enlarging the variational space with hexadecapole phonons and three-phonon configurations would likely improve the description.
