# Survival probabilities of charmonia as a clue to measure transient magnetic fields

> We investigate time evolution of $S$-wave charmonium populations under a time-dependent homogeneous magnetic field and evaluate survival probabilities of the low-lying charmonia to the goal of estimating the magnetic field strength at heavy-ion collisions. Our approach implements mixing between different spin eigenstates and transitions to radially excited states. We show that the survival probabilities can change even by an extremely short magnetic field. Furthermore, we find that the survival probabilities depend on the initial spin states. We propose the sum of the survival probabilities over spin partners as an observable insensitive to the initial states. We also find that the sum can be approximately given as a function of $σB_0^2$ with a duration time $σ$ and the maximum strength of the magnetic field $B_0$.

## Key claim

The survival probabilities of low-lying charmonia under a time-dependent magnetic field can change significantly even for extremely short field durations, and the sum of survival probabilities over spin partners (e.g., ηc and J/ψ) is approximately independent of the initial spin configuration, scaling with the product σB₀² of the duration σ and the squared maximum field strength B₀.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for S-wave charmonium states in a homogeneous, time-dependent magnetic field using a constituent quark model with a Gaussian-type time profile B(t) = B₀ exp[-(t-t_peak)²/σ²]. They expanded the time-dependent wave function in a basis of eigenfunctions of the in-vacuum Hamiltonian (up to N=20 states) and included both the Landau level term (∝ B²ρ²) and the Zeeman term (∝ B·S) in the interaction Hamiltonian, which induces mixing between spin-singlet and spin-triplet states as well as radial excitations.

## Key numbers

- Energy difference between ηc(1S) and J/ψ(Sz=0) is ΔE = 113.3 MeV (from constituent quark model).
- Precession period for the two-level ηc(1S)-J/ψ system under constant |eB| = 1 GeV² is T ≈ 1.64 fm/c.
- Constituent quark mass m_c = 1.784 GeV; string tension √b = 0.402 GeV; α_s = 0.713; Λ = 1.020 GeV² (Gaussian smearing parameter for spin-spin potential).
- Magnetic field strengths considered cover the region up to |eB₀| = 10 GeV²; typical duration σ = 0.05 fm/c; t_peak = 0.05 fm/c.
- For the spin sum of survival probabilities of 1S charmonia, the maximum relative error between different initial phases θ is around 2%.
- Typical σ expected in experiments is approximately 0.01 fm/c; ΔE for radial excitation is approximately 500 MeV, giving σ²(ΔE)² ~ 1/1600 and exp[-σ²(ΔE)²/8] ~ 0.99992.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonia in constant magnetic fields (Alford 2013, Bonati 2015, Suzuki 2016, Yoshida 2016, Iwasaki 2018) and time-evolution studies (Guo 2015, Suzuki 2016, Dutta 2017, Hoelck 2017, Bagchi 2018) by systematically including both spin mixing and radial excitations under time-dependent fields. The proposed σB₀² scaling and the spin-sum observable provide a practical method to infer magnetic field parameters in heavy-ion collisions, where initial spin configurations are difficult to determine experimentally. The approach is motivated by estimates from Deng and Huang (2012) using the HIJING model, which found magnetic field strengths up to ~70m_π² (~1.3 GeV²) in peripheral LHC collisions.
