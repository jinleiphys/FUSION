# Angular momentum conservation and pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We have studied the effect of rigorous angular momentum conservation (AMC) in elastic, inelastic, and decay channels on pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions based on the framework of an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. We found that the constraint of AMC suppresses the absorption of both $Δ$ resonances and pions, thus considerably enhances pion production and meanwhile reduces the charged pion yield ratio. The AMC effect on the charged pion yield ratio can not be simply compensated by a density-dependent in-medium $Δ$ production cross section. Therefore, incorporating the constraint of AMC is important in obtaining the correct pion multiplicity and charged pion yield ratio by transport simulations, relevant for the extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities.

## Key claim

Incorporating rigorous angular momentum conservation (AMC) in the IBUU transport model suppresses the absorption of both Δ resonances and pions, thereby enhancing pion production and reducing the charged pion yield ratio; this effect cannot be simply compensated by a density-dependent in-medium Δ production cross section.

## Method

The authors extended the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to include spin degrees of freedom for nucleons and Δ resonances, and enforced AMC in the elastic N+N ↔ N+N, inelastic N+N ↔ N+Δ, and decay/absorption Δ ↔ N+π channels. For 2↔2 processes, the final-state relative coordinates and momenta were adjusted to conserve total angular momentum, while for Δ decay the angular distribution was derived from Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and spherical harmonics, and for N+π→Δ the Δ spin and coordinate were determined to satisfy AMC. Simulations were performed for 132Sn+124Sn collisions at 270 AMeV and Au+Au collisions at 400 AMeV and 1 AGeV, with an impact parameter b=4 fm.

## Key numbers

- Pion multiplicity increased by about 75% in Sn+Sn collisions at 270 AMeV with AMC compared to without AMC.
- Pion multiplicity increased by about 65% in Au+Au collisions at 400 AMeV with AMC.
- Pion multiplicity increased by about 38% in Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV with AMC.
- The n+n ↔ p+Δ⁻ rate was about 2 to 3 times the p+p ↔ n+Δ⁺⁺ rate in 132Sn+124Sn collisions at 270 AMeV.
- The saturation density used in the mean-field potential was ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³.
- The constant elastic baryon-baryon cross section was set to 40 mb.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of conservation-law effects in heavy-ion collisions, such as Gale (1990) on energy and angular momentum conservation, Cozma (2014) on energy conservation effects on pion production, and Liu (2023) on AMC in elastic nucleon-nucleon collisions. It also builds on the transport model evaluation project (TMEP, 2023) which reduced the π⁻/π⁺ yield ratio uncertainty to 1.6%. The results indicate that neglecting AMC may bias the extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities from experimental pion data, and that future transport simulations should incorporate AMC for accurate pion multiplicity and yield ratio predictions.
