# Hybrid Baryons, a brief review

> This contribution is a brief review of the status of hybrid baryons, which are hypothetical baryons that incorporate a gluonic excitation. We first summarize the status of hybrid mesons, since this closely related topic has seen considerable recent activity with the identification of two exotic candidates. Next we review theoretical expectations for the masses and quantum numbers of hybrid baryons, which have come from studies of the bag model, QCD sum rules and the flux tube model. Finally hybrid baryon experiment is discussed, including suggestions for experimenters at COSY.

# Hybrid Baryons, a brief review

## Key claim
Hybrid baryons (hypothetical baryons containing a gluonic excitation) are expected to exist and produce an overpopulation of baryon resonances relative to the predictions of the three-quark (qqq) quark model, but the mass of the lightest hybrid is highly model-dependent, ranging from about 1.5 GeV to 1.9 GeV, and no unambiguous experimental identification has been made.

## Method
This is a review paper that summarizes theoretical predictions for hybrid baryon masses and quantum numbers from three approaches: the bag model, QCD sum rules, and the flux tube model. The author also reviews experimental search strategies including photocouplings, electroproduction, J/Psi hadronic decays, and overpopulation of the baryon spectrum, with suggestions for experiments at COSY.

## Key numbers
- Lightest bag model hybrid baryon (1/2+ N* P11 state) predicted at about 1.6 GeV (Barnes and Close 1983); a subsequent calculation gave about 1.5 GeV (Golowich, Haqq and Karl 1983)
- Bag model prediction for lightest strange hybrid baryon (uds flavor): M(1/2+) = 1.63(4) GeV (Carlson and Hansson 1983)
- QCD sum rule estimate for lightest 1/2+ N hybrid baryon: about 2.1 GeV (Martynenko 1991); revised to about 1.5 GeV (Kisslinger and Li 1995)
- Flux tube model prediction for lightest nnn hybrid baryons: two 1/2+ N and two 3/2+ N states, all at 1.87(10) GeV (Capstick and Page 1999)
- Flux tube model prediction for Delta hybrids: degenerate at 2.09(10) GeV (Capstick and Page 1999)
- Hybrid meson exotic 1-+ candidate masses: pi1(1400) seen in eta-pi, and pi1(1600) seen in rho-pi, eta-pi and b1-pi
- Bag model prediction for lightest exotic 1-+ hybrid meson: about 1.5 GeV; flux tube model: about 1.9 GeV; lattice gauge theory: about 2.0 GeV

## Context
This review extends the discussion of hybrid mesons (where two exotic JPC candidates, pi1(1400) and pi1(1600), have been identified) to the baryon sector, where no J^P exotics exist and identification must rely on overpopulation and anomalous couplings. The paper summarizes work by Barnes and Close (1983), Golowich, Haqq and Karl (1983), Carlson and Hansson (1983), Duck and Umland (1983), Martynenko (1991), Kisslinger and Li (1995), Capstick and Page (1999), and others. It enables future experimental searches at COSY, ELSA (with the Crystal Barrel detector), and BES (with J/Psi decays) to identify hybrid baryons up to about 2.2 GeV, and highlights the need for accurate determination of conventional qqq baryon properties as a prerequisite for identifying exotics.
