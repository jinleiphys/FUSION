# Structure of A = 7 - 8 nuclei with two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory

> We solve the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) in the complete Nmax = 8 basis for A = 7 and A = 8 nuclei with two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT). We find that including the chiral EFT three-nucleon interaction in the Hamiltonian improves overall good agreement with experimental binding energies, excitation spectra, transitions and electromagnetic moments. We predict states that exhibit sensitivity to including the chiral EFT three-nucleon interaction but are not yet known experimentally.

## Key claim

Including the chiral effective field theory three-nucleon interaction in the Hamiltonian improves overall agreement with experimental binding energies, excitation spectra, transitions, and electromagnetic moments for A = 7 and A = 8 nuclei, though residual differences between theoretical and experimental energies remain that are significantly larger than the estimated basis-space dependence of the calculated results.

## Method

The authors solved the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) in the complete Nmax = 8 basis for A = 7 and A = 8 nuclei using chiral N3LO two-nucleon interactions and chiral N2LO three-nucleon interactions. They employed the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) effective interaction approach to construct effective interactions from the realistic potentials, then performed large-scale diagonalizations using the MFDn code on up to six thousand cores at NERSC.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 7Li: 38.60(44) MeV with NN+NNN interaction (experiment: 39.245 MeV)
- Ground state energy of 7Be: 36.98(43) MeV with NN+NNN interaction (experiment: 37.6004(5) MeV)
- Ground state energy of 8B: 36.35(67) MeV with NN+NNN interaction (experiment: 37.7378(11) MeV)
- 7Li ground state magnetic moment: 2.993 μN with NN+NNN at ħΩ=13 MeV and Nmax=8 (experiment: +3.256 μN)
- 7Be ground state magnetic moment: -1.127 μN with NN+NNN at ħΩ=13 MeV and Nmax=8 (experiment: -1.398(15) μN)
- 7Li point-proton rms radius: 2.114 fm with NN+NNN at ħΩ=13 MeV and Nmax=8 (experiment: 2.32(5) fm)
- 7Be point-proton rms radius: 2.276 fm with NN+NNN at ħΩ=13 MeV and Nmax=8 (experiment: 2.52(3) fm)
- 7Li B(M1; 1/2- → 3/2-): 4.068 μN2 with NN+NNN at ħΩ=13 MeV and Nmax=8 (experiment: 4.92(25) μN2)
- 7Li ground state energy covers a range of 1.33 MeV for Nmax=8 over the range of ħΩ depicted

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM calculations for A=6, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13, and 14 nuclei (Nogga 2006, Navratil 2007, Maris 2009) by pushing the full-space basis to Nmax=8 for A=7 and A=8 nuclei, where earlier calculations were primarily performed in the Nmax=6 basis. The results confirm the significance of three-nucleon interactions for binding energies and excitation spectra, and provide benchmarks for the importance-truncation NCSM approach (Roth 2009) and for NCSM/RGM reaction calculations that require larger basis expansions. The calculations also enable comparison with SRG-evolved interactions that include three-nucleon terms.
