# Analysis of the $^6$He $β$ decay into the $α+d$ continuum within a three-body model

> The beta-decay process of the $^6$He halo nucleus into the alpha+d continuum is studied in a three-body model. The $^6$He nucleus is described as an alpha+n+n system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh. The convergence of the Gamow-Teller matrix element requires the knowledge of wave functions up to about 30 fm and of hypermomentum components up to K=24. The shape and absolute values of the transition probability per time and energy units of a recent experiment can be reproduced very well with an appropriate alpha+d potential. A total transition probability of 1.6E-6 s$^{-1}$ is obtained in agreement with that experiment. Halo effects are shown to be very important because of a strong cancellation between the internal and halo components of the matrix element, as observed in previous studies. The forbidden bound state in the alpha+d potential is found essential to reproduce the order of magnitude of the data. Comments are made on R-matrix fits.

## Key claim

The beta-decay transition probability of the halo nucleus $^6$He into the $\alpha+d$ continuum can be reproduced very well by a three-body $\alpha+n+n$ model, but only when the $\alpha+d$ potential includes a forbidden bound state (the Pauli-forbidden $0s$ state of $^6$Li) and when the three-body wave function includes hypermomentum components up to at least $K=24$ and extends to relative distances of about 30 fm; the abstract's claim of "very well" is conditioned on these specific choices.

## Method

The $^6$He ground state was described as an $\alpha+n+n$ system using hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh, with the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction and a renormalized $\alpha+N$ potential from Voronchev et al. (1995). The final $\alpha+d$ scattering state was factorized into a deuteron wave function (calculated on a Lagrange-Laguerre mesh) and an $s$-wave $\alpha+d$ relative wave function obtained from several central potentials: a deep Gaussian potential (Dubovichenko, 1994), folding potentials derived from the $\alpha+N$ interaction, and a repulsive Woods-Saxon potential. The Gamow-Teller matrix element was evaluated by integrating the product of the $\alpha+d$ scattering wave function and an effective radial wave function derived from the $S=0$ component of the $^6$He wave function, with convergence checked for hypermomentum up to $K=24$ and radial integration up to $R_{\rm max}=30$ fm.

## Key numbers

- Total transition probability $W = 1.6 \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ (from abstract) and $W = 1.95 \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ for $E>0$ MeV with the Gaussian potential $V_a$ (from Table 1).
- For $E>0.37$ MeV (experimental cutoff), $W = 1.54 \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ with $V_a$, compared to the experimental value $(1.5 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ from Aabye et al. (2002) and $(7.6 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ from Borge et al. (1993).
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element for the $^6$He $\beta$ decay to the $^6$Li ground state gave $B_{\rm GT}=4.489 \lambda^2$, about 5% below the experimental value $B_{\rm GT}^{(\rm exp)}=4.745 \lambda^2$.
- The $K=2$ and $K=8$ hypermomentum components dominate the Gamow-Teller integral at $E=1$ MeV, with $I_E^{(2)}(\infty) = -9.45 \times 10^{-3}$ and $I_E^{(8)}(\infty) = 2.90 \times 10^{-2}$ for $K_{\rm max}=24$ (Table 1).
- The $^6$He binding energy was reproduced by renormalizing the $\alpha+N$ potential of Voronchev et al. (1995) by a factor 1.035.

## Context

This work extends the three-body $\alpha+n+n$ model of Descouvemont et al. (2003) to the beta-delayed deuteron decay of $^6$He, addressing the limitations of an earlier three-body calculation by Zhukov et al. (1993) that was restricted to $K=0$ and $K=2$ and overestimated the data of Borge et al. (1993). The results confirm the cancellation mechanism between internal and halo components of the Gamow-Teller matrix element found in the semi-microscopic study of Baye et al. (1994) and the microscopic cluster model of Csoto (1994), but now with a simpler three-body framework that reproduces the newer, more precise data of Aabye et al. (2002). The work enables improved $R$-matrix fits by suggesting a corrected asymptotic form for the initial-state wave function that accounts for three-body effects, and it clarifies that the forbidden bound state in the $\alpha+d$ potential is essential for obtaining the correct order of magnitude of the transition probability.
