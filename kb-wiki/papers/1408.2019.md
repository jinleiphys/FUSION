# A Review of Wave Packet Molecular Dynamics

> Warm dense matter systems created in the laboratory are highly dynamical. In such cases electron dynamics is often needed to accurately simulate the evolution and properties of the system. Large systems force one to make simple approximations enabling computationally feasibility. Wave packet molecular dynamics (WPMD) provides a simple framework for simulating time-dependent quantum plasmas. Here, this method is reviewed. The different variants of WPMD are shown and compared and their validity is discussed.

## Key claim

Wave packet molecular dynamics (WPMD) provides a computationally feasible framework for simulating time-dependent quantum plasmas, but the method requires ad hoc width constraints to prevent unphysical divergence of wave packet widths at high temperatures, and the accuracy of different variants depends strongly on the choice of wave packet form, antisymmetrization approximation, and width control scheme.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical foundations of WPMD, including Ehrenfest's theorem, the local harmonic approximation, and the Dirac-Frenkel time-dependent variational principle (TDVP), which yields equations of motion for variational parameters that take a Hamiltonian form. The review then catalogs the many variants of WPMD used in the literature, including different wave packet forms (isotropic Gaussian, anisotropic Gaussian, Hermite-Gaussian, split wave packet, periodic wave packet, periodic-Bloch wave packet, bound-free wave packets, self-similar wave packet, and harmonically constrained wave packet), various antisymmetrization approximations (Hartree, pairwise antisymmetrization, full antisymmetrization, and the empirical electron Force Field model), and different width constraint methods (harmonic constraint, periodic boundary conditions on width, and energy-based constraint).

## Key numbers

- The standard isotropic Gaussian wave packet ansatz depends on 8 real time-dependent variational parameters: position vector, momentum vector, scalar width, and conjugate width momentum.
- Full antisymmetrization requires an order N^4 operation per time step for the electron-electron potential energy expectation value, where N is the number of electrons.
- The pairwise antisymmetrization approximation (including only two-body exchange) holds when wave packets are well separated in phase space, i.e., the distance between their expectation values of position and momentum is big compared to their uncertainties.
- The scheme using Hartree form for electron-electron potential energy but full antisymmetrization for kinetic energy allows simulations at temperatures up to 30,000 K before wave packet divergence becomes an issue (Morozov and Valuev, 2009).
- The harmonically constrained wave packet introduces an adjustable parameter d that controls how large the width sigma can get, with the harmonic constraint taking the form H_Harm' = (20d/3m)(p_sigma * sigma + (14/3)d * sigma^2).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on WPMD that began with Heller (1975) and was applied to plasmas by Klakow et al. (1994a, 1994b), and it compares the many variants developed by groups including Knaup et al. (1999, 2000, 2001a, 2001b, 2002, 2003), Su and Goddard (2007, 2009), Morozov and Valuev (2009, 2012), Ebeling and Militzer (1997), Ebeling et al. (2006), and Grabowski et al. (2013). The review enables practitioners to select appropriate WPMD variants for specific warm dense matter problems, but it also highlights open questions about how to systematically control wave packet spreading without ad hoc constraints and how to treat antisymmetrization efficiently for large systems.
