# Sensitivity of the deuteron form factor to nucleon resonances

> The sensitivity of the deuteron form factor to contributions from the excited states of the nucleon is explored using a simple model of the nucleon-nucleon interaction which employs a tower of charged nucleon resonances. The model is manifestly covariant, analytically solvable, and gauge invariant. The consequences of this model are studied in the simplest possible framework. We assume that all particles have spin zero and that the tower has only three charged members, which consist of the proton, the Roper, and a higher state in the vicinity of the $D_{13}$. Nucleon-nucleon S-wave phase shifts and the deuteron form factor are calculated using this three member tower, and the results are compared to similar calculations using the proton ground state only. We conclude that the deuteron form factor is insensitive to the presence of excited states of the proton unless those states are of sufficiently low mass to produce strong inelasticities in $NN$ scattering channels.

## Key claim

The deuteron form factor is insensitive to the presence of excited states of the proton unless those states are of sufficiently low mass to produce strong inelasticities in NN scattering channels; the effect of resonances on the deuteron form factor is small when inelasticities are small, but becomes pronounced when inelasticities are large.

## Method

The authors constructed a manifestly covariant, analytically solvable, gauge-invariant model using a separable kernel with a Tabakin-style form factor and a "tower of states" containing the proton, the Roper resonance, and a higher state near the D13. They worked in the spectator formalism, neglected spin (all particles treated as scalars), and treated the neutron as always on-shell. The free parameters were fixed by fitting NN S-wave phase shifts below 350 MeV lab energy to the Nijmegen 1993 phase shifts, and the deuteron form factor was then calculated including an interaction current derived from the kernel to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the deuteron: 2.15 MeV for Model 1 (proton only), 2.02 MeV for Model 3 (three-member tower), and 2.47 MeV for Model 3I (three-member tower with lowered Roper mass); experimental value is 2.22 MeV
- Percent error in deuteron binding energy: 3.2% for Model 1, 9.0% for Model 3, 11.3% for Model 3I
- Chi-squared values for singlet phase shift fits: 6.97 (Model 1), 5.06 (Model 3), 3.66 (Model 3I)
- Chi-squared values for triplet phase shift fits: 7.60 (Model 1), 6.83 (Model 3), 6.04 (Model 3I)
- Average percent difference between Model 1 and Model 3I deuteron form factors at high Q²: 12%
- Onset of inelasticity in Model 3I: 492 MeV lab energy
- Roper mass in Model 3: 1.44 GeV; in Model 3I: 1.17 GeV
- D13 mass in both Models 3 and 3I: 1.52 GeV
- For the large inelasticity (LI) data set, inelasticity rho increased to 40-50 degrees
- Binding energy for LI data set: 2.47 MeV; for Model 1LI: 2.33 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic treatments of the NN system by Tjon and collaborators (van Faassen and Tjon, 1986) and the separable model approach of Rupp and Tjon (1988), but is the first to systematically include contributions from nucleon resonances and their corresponding interaction currents in a simple, analytically solvable, covariant, and gauge-invariant model of the deuteron form factor. The results show that virtual contributions from nucleon resonances produce only small effects on the deuteron form factor unless the resonances have sufficiently low mass to generate strong inelasticities, suggesting that future experiments at Jefferson Lab probing short-range structure may need to account for such effects only in channels with large inelasticities.
