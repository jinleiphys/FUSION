# Large-cutoff behavior of local chiral effective field theory interactions

> Interactions from chiral effective field theory have been successfully employed in a broad range of \textit{ab initio} calculations of nuclei and nuclear matter, but it has been observed that most results of few- and many-body calculations experience a substantial residual regulator and cutoff dependence. In this work, we investigate the behavior of local chiral potentials at leading order under variation of the cutoff scale for different local regulators. When varying the cutoff, we require that the resulting interaction produces no spurious bound states in the deuteron channel. We find that, for a particular choice of leading-order operators, nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and the deuteron ground-state energy converge to cutoff-independent plateaus, for all regulator functions we investigate. This observation may enable improved calculations with chiral Hamiltonians that also include three-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

For a specific choice of leading-order operator structure (the operator pair $\mathds{1},\sigma_{12}$), local chiral potentials at leading order produce cutoff-independent plateaus in nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and the deuteron ground-state energy for all investigated regulator functions, provided the interaction is constrained to produce exactly one bound state in the deuteron channel. This behavior arises because local regulators mix the leading-order contact interactions into higher partial waves, where they can compensate the attractive tensor force from one-pion exchange.

## Method

The authors constructed local chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials at leading order in coordinate space, using one-pion-exchange and four contact operator structures with local regulator functions characterized by exponents $n_1$, $n_2$, and $n$. For each cutoff value $R_0^{-1}$ in the range 0.8 to 10.0 fm$^{-1}$, they fit the two spin-isospin low-energy constants $C_{01}$ and $C_{10}$ to the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis up to laboratory energies of 50 MeV, while enforcing exactly one bound state in the deuteron channel. They then computed phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy for five different operator pairs and the LO$_{\text{n}P}$ interaction (where $C_{00}=C_{11}=0$) as functions of the inverse cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground-state energy converges to $E_d \to -1.99$ MeV for $R_0^{-1} \to \infty$ (experimental value is approximately -2.22 MeV)
- Phase shift plateaus are reached for $R_0^{-1} \gtrsim 4$ fm$^{-1}$ for the operator pair ($\mathds{1},\sigma_{12}$) with $n_1=n_2=n=2$
- For the $^1S_0$ channel, the low-energy constant $C_{01}$ scales as $C_{01}(R_0) = a R_0^b$ with $b \approx -0.98$ for $n=2$ regulators and $b \approx -0.96$ for $n=4$ regulators
- For the $^3S_1$ channel with one bound state, $C_{10} \sim \exp((r^{*,1} R_0^{-1})^b)$ with $b \approx 1.97$ and $r^{*,1} \approx 0.48$ fm
- For the $^3S_1$ channel with two bound states, $r^{*,2} \approx 0.20$ fm
- For regulators with $n=4$, fits could not be achieved beyond $R_0^{-1} = 4.4$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the study of cutoff dependence in chiral effective field theory interactions, following the analysis of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) who found strong cutoff dependences and spurious bound states in attractive tensor channels for nonlocal regulators. While Nogga et al. proposed adding explicit counterterms in those channels to restore renormalizability, the present work shows that local regulators can achieve similar cutoff-independent plateaus through regulator artifacts that mix $S$-wave contact interactions into higher partial waves. The authors note that their results differ conceptually from those of Nogga et al. because the plateaus arise from the specific operator choice and the one-bound-state constraint rather than from renormalization, and they suggest this approach may enable improved many-body calculations with reduced three-nucleon regulator artifacts, particularly for quantum Monte Carlo methods.
