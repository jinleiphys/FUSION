# Equilibration of the Gluon-Minijet Plasma at RHIC and LHC

> We study the production and equilibration of the gluon-minijet plasma expected to be formed in the central region of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at the BNL-RHIC and the CERN-LHC by solving a self-consistent relativistic transport equation. We compute the minijet production within perturbative QCD. Subsequent collisions among the semi-hard partons are treated by considering the elastic gg -> gg processes with screening of the long wavelength modes taken into account. We determine the time $τ_{eq}$ where close to ideal hydrodynamic flow sets in, and find rather similar numbers for central heavy-ion collisions at BNL-RHIC and CERN-LHC energies. The number densities, energy densities and temperatures of the minijet plasma are found to be different at RHIC and LHC, e.g. $T(τ_{eq})\sim220$ MeV and 380 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim
The gluon-minijet plasma produced in central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC reaches close-to-ideal hydrodynamic flow at a similar equilibration time of about 4-5 fm, despite significantly different initial energy densities and temperatures; the authors qualify that this result depends on self-consistent treatment of the non-equilibrium distribution function and the medium-dependent screening mass.

## Method
The authors solved a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation with a relaxation-time collision term for gluons, using initial conditions from perturbative QCD minijet production with a transverse-momentum cutoff (1.13 GeV at RHIC, 2.13 GeV at LHC). Elastic gg → gg scattering was treated with a medium-modified cross section that incorporates Debye screening of long-wavelength gluons, where the screening mass, running coupling, and all bulk quantities were computed self-consistently from the non-equilibrium distribution function at each time step. The evolution was followed from an initial proper time τ₀ = 1/p₀ until equilibration, defined as the time when the scaling exponents of energy density, temperature, and number density approach the hydrodynamic values of 4/3, 1/3, and 1 respectively.

## Key numbers
- Equilibration time τ_eq ≈ 4-5 fm for both RHIC and LHC central heavy-ion collisions.
- Temperature at equilibration: T(τ_eq) ≈ 220 MeV at RHIC and 380 MeV at LHC.
- Initial minijet transverse-momentum cutoff: p₀ = 1.13 GeV at RHIC (Au-Au at √s = 200 GeV per nucleon pair) and p₀ = 2.13 GeV at LHC (Pb-Pb at √s = 5.5A TeV).
- Energy densities at τ'_0 = 0.7 fm (RHIC) and 0.5 fm (LHC) from HIJING model: 3.2 GeV/fm³ at RHIC and 40 GeV/fm³ at LHC, which the authors note differ from their own initial conditions.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of parton equilibration using the Boltzmann equation by Heiselberg and Wang (1996) and Wong (1996), which assumed an ansatz for the time-dependence of the relaxation rate or used equilibrium distribution functions to derive the relaxation time. The present paper improves upon those by computing the relaxation time self-consistently from the non-equilibrium distribution function and by using energy-dependent minijet scales from parton saturation arguments, rather than the fixed scale used in HIJING. The results enable more quantitative predictions for signatures such as J/ψ suppression, dilepton emission, and strangeness production, and the authors note that future work should include coherent color fields and inelastic gg → ggg processes to address chemical equilibration.
