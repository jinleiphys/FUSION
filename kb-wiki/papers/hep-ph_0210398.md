# Introduction to Chiral Perturbation Theory

> This article provides a pedagogical introduction to the basic concepts of chiral perturbation theory and is designed as a text for a two-semester course on that topic. Chapter 1 serves as a general introduction to the empirical and theoretical foundations which led to the development of chiral perturbation theory. Chapter 2 deals with QCD and its global symmetries in the chiral limit; the concept of Green functions and Ward identities reflecting the underlying chiral symmetry is elaborated. In Chap. 3 the idea of a spontaneous breakdown of a global symmetry is discussed and its consequences in terms of the Goldstone theorem are demonstrated. Chapter 4 deals with mesonic chiral perturbation theory and the principles entering the construction of the chiral Lagrangian are outlined. Various examples with increasing chiral orders and complexity are given. Finally, in Chap. 5 the methods are extended to include the interaction between Goldstone bosons and baryons in the single-baryon sector, with the main emphasis put on the heavy-baryon formulation. At the end, the method of infrared regularization in the relativistic framework is discussed.

## Key claim

This paper provides a pedagogical introduction to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as an effective field theory of QCD at low energies, but explicitly states it is not intended as a comprehensive overview of results obtained over the past two decades, and the author apologizes in advance for important contributions not mentioned.

## Method

The paper develops the theoretical foundations of ChPT by first reviewing QCD's global symmetries in the chiral limit, then explaining spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the Goldstone theorem. It constructs the chiral Lagrangian for mesons order by order in a momentum and quark-mass expansion using Weinberg's power counting scheme, and extends the formalism to include baryons via the heavy-baryon formulation and infrared regularization.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is estimated as Lambda_CSB approximately equal to 4 pi F_0, where F_0 is approximately 93 MeV (the pion-decay constant in the chiral limit)
- The most general effective Lagrangian at O(p^4) in the mesonic sector contains 10 physical low-energy constants
- The most general even-intrinsic parity Lagrangian at O(p^6) in the SU(3) sector contains 90 + 4 free parameters
- The odd-intrinsic-parity Lagrangian at O(p^6) in the SU(3) sector contains 23 independent structures
- For charged pion polarizabilities, O(p^6) corrections were shown to be 12% and 24% of the O(p^4) values for alpha-bar and beta-bar, respectively
- The ratio mu_p/mu_n predicted by a simple quark model analysis is -3/2, close to the empirical value of -1.46

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the current-algebra and PCAC approaches of the 1960s, particularly the work of Gell-Mann, Adler, and others, by providing a systematic framework for calculating corrections to soft-pion predictions. It builds on the foundational work of Weinberg (1978) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) who established ChPT as a rigorous effective field theory, and follows the precedent of the review by Thomas on the cloudy bag model. The paper enables graduate students and researchers to understand both mesonic and baryonic ChPT, including modern developments such as the heavy-baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1990) and Bernard et al. (1992), as well as the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999).
