# On the Quantized Relativistic Mean Field Theory for Nuclear Matter

> We propose a quantization procedure for the nucleon-scalar meson system, in which an arbitrary mean scalar meson field $ϕ$ is introduced. The equivalence of this procedure with the usual ones is proven for any given value of $ϕ$. By use of this procedure, the scalar meson field in the Walecka's RMFT and in Chin's RHA are quantized around the mean field. Its corrections on these theories are considered by perturbation up to the second order. The arbitrariness of $ϕ$ makes us free to fix it at any stage in the calculation. When we fix it in the way of Walecka's RMFT, the quantum corrections are big, and the result does not converge. When we fix it in the way of Chin's RHA, the quantum correction is negligibly small, and the convergence is excellent. It shows that RHA covers the leading part of quantum field theory for nuclear systems and is an excellent zeroth order approximation for further quantum corrections, while the Walecka's RMFT does not. We suggest to fix the parameter $ϕ$ at the end of the whole calculation by minimizing the total energy per-nucleon for the nuclear matter or the total energy for the finite nucleus, to make the quantized relativistic mean field theory (QRMFT) a variational method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a quantized relativistic mean field theory (QRMFT) in which an arbitrary mean scalar meson field \(\phi\) is introduced as a free parameter; the key result is that when \(\phi\) is fixed in the manner of Chin's relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) the quantum correction is negligibly small and convergence is excellent, whereas when fixed in the manner of Walecka's RMFT the quantum corrections are large and the result does not converge.

## Method

The authors quantize the nucleon-scalar meson system around an arbitrary classical mean scalar meson field \(\phi\), expanding the nucleon field on eigenfunctions of the Dirac operator with effective mass \(m' = m - g_\sigma \phi\) and the scalar meson field as \(\Phi' = \Phi - \phi\). They prove the equivalence of this procedure to the usual vacuum quantization for any \(\phi\), then apply it to the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model for infinite nuclear matter, treating the fluctuation part \(H'\) as a perturbation up to second order. The energy per nucleon is minimized with respect to \(\chi = m'/m\) to obtain the equation of state, with parameters \(\alpha_\sigma\) and \(\alpha_\omega\) fitted to reproduce the binding energy and equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at equilibrium: \(b = 15.986\) MeV.
- Equilibrium density parameter: \(r_0 = 1.175\) fm.
- RMFT parameters: \(\alpha_\sigma = 362.6\), \(\alpha_\omega = 278.0\); resulting effective mass \(\chi = 0.538\); compression modulus \(K = 554\) MeV.
- RHA parameters: \(\alpha_\sigma = 230.3\), \(\alpha_\omega = 149.0\); resulting effective mass \(\chi = 0.730\); compression modulus \(K = 456\) MeV.
- QRMFT parameters: \(\alpha_\sigma = 230.3\), \(\alpha_\omega = 149.0\); resulting effective mass \(\chi = 0.730\); compression modulus \(K = 456\) MeV.
- The RHA and QRMFT give identical equations of state and the same numerical results for the parameters and calculated properties at equilibrium density.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field theory of Walecka (1974) and the relativistic Hartree approximation of Chin (1977) by introducing a quantization procedure around an arbitrary mean field, making the theory variational. It shows that RHA already captures the dominant quantum effects, while the simple RMFT does not provide a good starting point for perturbative quantum corrections. The method enables systematic inclusion of quantum fluctuations beyond RHA and is suggested for application to finite nuclei and to systems with meson condensations, such as pion condensation previously studied by the authors.
