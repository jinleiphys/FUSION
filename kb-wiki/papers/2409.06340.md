# Quantum computing for extracting nuclear resonances

> Quantum computing has been increasingly applied in nuclear physics. In this work, we combine quantum computing with the complex scaling method to address the resonance problem. Due to the non-Hermiticity introduced by complex scaling, standard quantum computing cannot solve for complex eigenvalues directly. Therefore, it is necessary to embed the non-Hermitian operator into a larger dimensional unitary operator. Additionally, for the case of two basis vectors, we improve the traditional direct measurement method and optimize the quantum circuit. Ultimately, using the $α+α$ system as an example, we obtain the complex eigenenergies from the quantum computer that are consistent with those obtained from direct Hamiltonian diagonalization.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that complex eigenenergies of nuclear resonances can be extracted from a quantum computer by embedding the non-Hermitian complex-scaled Hamiltonian into a larger unitary operator, but only after applying a global scaling and translation transformation to reduce the extreme sensitivity of the extracted eigenvalues to small measurement errors in the quantum circuit.

## Method

The complex scaling method (CSM) is applied to the nuclear Hamiltonian, transforming the resonance problem into a non-Hermitian eigenvalue problem with square-integrable wave functions. The non-Hermitian operator is embedded into a larger unitary operator to enable quantum computation, and the Hamiltonian is expressed in Pauli operators via the Jordan-Wigner transformation. For the specific case of two basis vectors, the quantum circuit is optimized to contain one ancilla qubit and two system qubits, and the measurement probabilities from two circuits with different gate parameters are used to determine both the real and imaginary parts of the resonant energy.

## Key numbers

- For the α+α system with a Gaussian interaction (two-body toy model), the G-wave resonance energy from direct diagonalization is 11.7823 − 1.7867i MeV.
- Using the two-body toy model with two harmonic oscillator basis functions at complex scaling angle θ = 25.4343°, the eigenvalue closest to the true eigenenergy is 10.79 − 2.022i MeV.
- On the Quafu-Dongling quantum computer with 8000 measurements, after readout error mitigation and using translation plus global scaling (TGS), the extracted resonance energy is 11.24 − 2.127i MeV (by averaging all eigenvalues), compared to the exact value 10.79 − 2.022i MeV.
- For the microscopic THSR framework model of α+α, the direct diagonalization result is 11.10 − 2.487i MeV; on Quafu-Dongling with TGS and readout error mitigation, the averaged eigenvalue is 11.11 − 2.472i MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of quantum computing to nuclear resonance problems by combining it with the complex scaling method, which was previously used only for bound-state calculations in quantum computing contexts such as the variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) and quantum phase estimation (QPE). The embedding technique for non-Hermitian operators follows the approach of SK (2013) and subsequent work. The method enables future studies of nuclear resonances on near-term quantum devices, particularly for systems where the number of basis functions can be kept small, as demonstrated with the THSR wave function method of Zhou (2013) and Zhang (2022, 2023).
