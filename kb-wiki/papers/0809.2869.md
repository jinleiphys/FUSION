# Stirring Strongly Coupled Plasma

> We determine the energy it takes to move a test quark along a circle of radius L with angular frequency w through the strongly coupled plasma of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory. We find that for most values of L and w the energy deposited by stirring the plasma in this way is governed either by the drag force acting on a test quark moving through the plasma in a straight line with speed v=Lw or by the energy radiated by a quark in circular motion in the absence of any plasma, whichever is larger. There is a continuous crossover from the drag-dominated regime to the radiation-dominated regime. In the crossover regime we find evidence for significant destructive interference between energy loss due to drag and that due to radiation as if in vacuum. The rotating quark thus serves as a model system in which the relative strength of, and interplay between, two different mechanisms of parton energy loss is accessible via a controlled classical gravity calculation. We close by speculating on the implications of our results for a quark that is moving through the plasma in a straight line while decelerating, although in this case the classical calculation breaks down at the same value of the deceleration at which the radiation-dominated regime sets in.

## Key claim

For a heavy test quark moving in a circle through strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma, the energy deposited into the plasma is governed either by the drag force from straight-line motion or by vacuum synchrotron radiation, whichever is larger, with a continuous crossover between regimes and evidence for significant destructive interference between the two mechanisms in the crossover region.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to map a rotating heavy quark in strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory at temperature T to a classical string spiralling downward into an AdS black hole geometry. They solve the Nambu-Goto equations of motion for the string worldsheet parameterized by radial and angular profiles ρ(u) and θ(u), where u is the bulk coordinate, and compute the energy flow down the string to determine dE/dt.

## Key numbers

- The crossover between drag-dominated and radiation-dominated regimes occurs at ω ≈ πT (1 − v²)^{3/4}, where v = Lω is the quark speed.
- The dimensionless angular frequency is defined as w = ω/(πT), and the dimensionless radius as ℓ = LπT, with v = ℓ w.
- For w = 0.05, 0.5, and 5.0, the authors present numerical solutions for the string shape ρ(u) for various values of the conserved quantity Π.
- For w = 5.0 and Π = 100, the quark moves at speed v = 0.895 with radius ℓ = 0.1789, and the string reaches the horizon at ρ(1) = 1.65004.
- Near the horizon, the angular winding diverges logarithmically as Δθ(u) ≈ (w/4) ln[(u₀ − 1)/(u − 1)].

## Context

This work extends the trailing string calculations for straight-line motion developed by Herzog et al. (2006) and Gubser et al. (2006) to the case of circular motion, which naturally includes acceleration. It enables future study of how a localized rotating perturbation deposits energy into the strongly coupled plasma, and provides a controlled setting to investigate interference between drag and radiation mechanisms that are relevant for understanding parton energy loss in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
