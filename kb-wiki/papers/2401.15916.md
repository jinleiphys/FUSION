# New Skyrme parametrizations to describe finite nuclei and neutron star matter with realistic effective masses

> The phenomenological Skyrme energy density functional theory is one of the most popular theories for dealing with finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, including neutron star matter. However, the density dependence of the effective masses of common Skyrme parametrizations is completely different from the one found in microscopic calculations. This can have drastic consequences. For instance, in a recent study we reported that in many Skyrme functionals, the neutron Fermi velocity exceeds the speed of light at densities that exist in neutron-star cores. To solve this problem, we try to construct new Skyrme parametrizations by including constraints from microscopic calculations of the effective mass in addition to binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei and different microscopic equations of state of pure neutron matter. We give the parameters of the new Skyrme forces and show that our new effective interactions can successfully describe properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter (including pure neutron matter, symmetric nuclear matter, and neutron star matter).

## Key claim

The paper constructs new Skyrme parametrizations (Sky3 and Sky4) that reproduce the density dependence of nucleon effective masses from microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, thereby avoiding the unphysical feature where neutron Fermi velocity exceeds the speed of light at neutron-star core densities, a problem present in many standard Skyrme functionals like SLy4 and BSk20.

## Method

The authors extended the standard Skyrme energy density functional by adding density-dependent terms (t4, t5) and three density-dependent contact terms with exponents αi = i/3, then fixed combinations of parameters (A1, B1, A2, B2, C1–C4, Ehigh) by fitting to microscopic BHF effective masses from Baldo (2014) and two different microscopic pure neutron matter equations of state (LZLWBS for Sky3, APR for Sky4). The remaining six parameters (ρ0, E0, K0, gradient coefficients, and spin-orbit strength W0) were determined by minimizing χ² against experimental binding energies and charge radii of eight doubly magic nuclei using the Hartree-Fock code SKHAFO, with additional constraints from finite-size instability analysis in RPA.

## Key numbers

* Sky3: χ²_Eb ≈ 1.32, χ²_Rch ≈ 2.76, χ²/dof ≈ 0.51 (dof = 8)
* Sky4: χ²_Eb ≈ 2.32, χ²_Rch ≈ 2.75, χ²/dof ≈ 0.63 (dof = 8)
* For Sky3: high-density symmetric matter constraint at ρ_high = 1.0 fm⁻³ gives E_high = 291 MeV (from LZLWBS EoS)
* For Sky4: high-density symmetric matter constraint at ρ_high = 0.96 fm⁻³ gives E_high = 204.02 MeV (from APR EoS)
* Gradient instability constraints: C^{∇ρ}_01 = -200 MeV fm⁶, C^{∇ρ}_11 = 0 MeV fm⁶ for Sky3; C^{∇ρ}_01 = -300 MeV fm⁶, C^{∇ρ}_11 = 100 MeV fm⁶ for Sky4
* Adopted errors in fit: ΔE = 2 MeV, ΔR = 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme functional approach by addressing the effective-mass problem identified in Duan (2023), where many Skyrme parametrizations (SLy4, BSk20) produce neutron Fermi velocities exceeding the speed of light at neutron-star densities. The new parametrizations follow the spirit of the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) functional (Baldo 2017) by fitting effective masses to microscopic BHF results, while also incorporating the multi-exponent density-dependent terms from the KIDS functional (Gil 2019) and the t4/t5 terms from the BSk family (Chamel 2009). The resulting forces enable consistent studies of both finite nuclei and neutron star matter without unphysical superluminal Fermi velocities.
