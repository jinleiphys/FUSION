# Coulomb Effects in Femtoscopy

> The correlation function of two identical particles - pions or kaons - interacting via Coulomb potential is computed. The particles are emitted from an anisotropic particle's source of finite lifetime. In the case of pions, the effect of halo is taken into account as an additional particle's source of large spatial extension. The relativistic effects are discussed in detail. The Bowler-Sinyukov procedure to remove the Coulomb interaction is carefully tested. In the absence of halo the procedure is shown to work very well even for an extremely anisotropic source. When the halo is taken into account the free correlation function, which is extracted by means of the Bowler-Sinyukov procedure, is distorted at small relative momenta but the source parameters are still correctly reproduced.

## Key claim

The Bowler-Sinyukov procedure for removing Coulomb effects from femtoscopic correlation functions works very well for anisotropic sources of finite lifetime when no halo is present, but when a halo (a second, spatially extended source from long-lived resonances) is included, the extracted "free" correlation function is distorted at small relative momenta, although the source parameters are still correctly reproduced.

## Method

The authors computed the two-particle correlation function for identical pions and kaons interacting via the Coulomb potential using the nonrelativistic Koonin formula, with the relative motion treated nonrelativistically in the center-of-mass frame of the pair. The source was modeled as a Gaussian in space and time with anisotropic spatial dimensions and finite lifetime, and for pions a halo component was added as an additional Gaussian source of large spatial extension. They then applied the Bowler-Sinyukov procedure to the computed Coulomb correlation functions (treating them as "experimental" data) and compared the extracted "free" correlation functions and source parameters to the known input values.

## Key numbers

- The Bohr radius for pion pairs is 388 fm; for kaon pairs it is 110 fm.
- For a source with fireball parameters R_x = 4 fm, R_y = 4 fm, R_z = 6 fm, tau = 3 fm, and pair velocity v = 0.8, the Bowler-Sinyukov procedure correctly reproduces the free correlation function in the absence of halo.
- The halo was modeled as spherical with radius R_h = 40 fm and zero lifetime (tau_h = 0).
- The emission time of particles at RHIC was observed to be as short as 1 fm/c, which is noted as part of the "HBT Puzzle" context.

## Context

This work extends earlier tests of the Bowler-Sinyukov procedure, such as those by Kisiel (2006) using Monte Carlo event generators, by providing a more detailed, mostly analytical calculation. It addresses concerns about the reliability of femtoscopy raised by the "HBT Puzzle" (Gyulassy 2001, Pratt 2003), where the unexpectedly short emission times at RHIC questioned the standard method. The study enables a quantitative check of whether experimentally obtained source parameters from Coulomb-corrected correlation functions are reliable, particularly for anisotropic sources and in the presence of a halo from long-lived resonances.
