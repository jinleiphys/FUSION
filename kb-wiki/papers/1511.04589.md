# Nucleon axial and tensor charges with dynamical overlap quarks

> We report on our calculation of the nucleon axial and tensor charges in 2+1-flavor QCD with dynamical overlap quarks. Gauge ensembles are generated at a single lattice spacing 0.12 fm and at a strange quark mass close to its physical value. We employ the all-mode-averaging technique to calculate the relevant nucleon correlation functions, and the disconnected quark loop is efficiently calculated by using the all-to-all quark propagator. We present our preliminary results for the isoscalar and isovector charges obtained at pion masses $m_π$ = 450 and 540 MeV.

# Nucleon axial and tensor charges with dynamical overlap quarks

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary lattice QCD results for nucleon axial and tensor charges at unphysical pion masses (450 and 540 MeV) using dynamical overlap quarks, with chiral extrapolation to the physical point yielding values consistent with previous lattice studies and experiment, but with statistical and discretization uncertainties that preclude definitive comparison.

## Method

The calculation uses 2+1-flavor QCD with dynamical overlap quarks on a 16³×48 lattice at a single lattice spacing of 0.12 fm, with strange quark mass near its physical value. Nucleon three-point functions are computed using the all-to-all quark propagator decomposed into low-mode (160 eigenmodes) and high-mode contributions, with the all-mode-averaging technique combining low-mode averaging (LMA) over 48 source points and truncated solver method (TSM) over 24 source points to reduce statistical noise. Charges are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point functions and renormalized in the MS-bar scheme at μ=2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.12 fm on a 16³×48 lattice
- Simulated pion masses: mπ ≈ 450 MeV (mud = 0.035) and 540 MeV (mud = 0.050)
- Strange quark mass: ms = 0.080 (physical value ms,phys = 0.081)
- Isovector axial charge at physical point: gA = 1.16(12)(9) (first error statistical, second discretization)
- Isovector tensor charge at physical point: gT = 1.31(12)(11)
- Isoscalar axial charge at physical point: gA^s = 0.63(18)(5)
- Isoscalar tensor charge at physical point: gT^s = 0.81(20)(7)
- Strange-quark axial charge: Δs = -0.11(18)(1)
- Strange-quark tensor charge: δs = -0.09(20)(1)
- Statistical error on isovector charges: typically 3% at simulated pion masses
- Statistical error on isoscalar charges: typically 10%
- Discretization error estimated at 8% from power counting (aΛQCD)² with ΛQCD = 500 MeV
- LMA uses Nsrc,low = 48 source points; TSM uses Nsrc,high = 24 source points
- Low-mode truncation: Ne = 160 eigenmodes
- Stopping condition for high-mode: |Dψpt - b| ≤ 10⁻⁷; relaxed condition for TSM estimator: 10⁻²

## Context

This work extends the JLQCD collaboration's previous calculation of the strange-quark scalar charge using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem (Ohki et al.) and direct calculation of disconnected three-point functions (Takeda et al., Ohki et al.) to the axial and tensor channels. The results are consistent with previous lattice studies of gA (reviewed in Lattice 2015) giving gA ≈ 1.10–1.25 and gT ≈ 0.95–1.15 (reviewed in CD 2015), and with experimental values from UCNA and COMPASS. The suppression of axial charges relative to the simple quark model (gA^s = 1, gA = 5/3) was previously argued in Schwinger-Dyson analyses by Yamanaka. Ongoing calculations at lighter pion masses (290–380 MeV) and on finer lattices with different chiral fermion formulations aim to improve the chiral extrapolation and reduce discretization errors.
