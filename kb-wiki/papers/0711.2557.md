# Global NLO Analysis of Nuclear Parton Distribution Functions

> Nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) are determined by a global analysis of experimental measurements on structure-function ratios F_2^A/F_2^{A'} and Drell-Yan cross section ratios σ_{DY}^A/σ_{DY}^{A'}, and their uncertainties are estimated by the Hessian method. The NPDFs are obtained in both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) of α_s. As a result, valence-quark distributions are relatively well determined, whereas antiquark distributions at x>0.2 and gluon distributions in the whole x region have large uncertainties. The NLO uncertainties are slightly smaller than the LO ones; however, such a NLO improvement is not as significant as the nucleonic case.

## Key claim

Nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) are determined at both LO and NLO with uncertainty estimates via the Hessian method, but the NLO improvement in uncertainty reduction is not as significant as in the nucleonic case, and antiquark distributions at x>0.2 and gluon distributions across all x remain poorly constrained even at NLO.

## Method

The authors performed a global chi-squared analysis of 1241 data points consisting of structure-function ratios F2^A/F2^A' (including F2^D/F2^p, F2^A/F2^D, and F2^A/F2^A' with A' not equal to D) and Drell-Yan cross section ratios sigma_DY^A/sigma_DY^A'. Nuclear modifications were parametrized by weight functions w_i(x,A,Z) multiplying nucleonic PDFs at an initial scale Q0^2=1 GeV^2, with DGLAP evolution applied to experimental Q^2 values; uncertainties were estimated using the Hessian method with Delta chi^2=13.7 for one-sigma confidence.

## Key numbers

- Total data set: 1241 points (290 for F2^D/F2^p, 606 for F2^A/F2^D, 293 for F2^A/F2^A' with A' not equal to D, and 52 for sigma_DY^A/sigma_DY^A')
- Minimum chi^2 per degree of freedom: 1.35 for LO, 1.21 for NLO
- Initial Q^2 scale: Q0^2=1 GeV^2
- Delta chi^2 value for uncertainty estimation: 13.7 (corresponding to one-sigma confidence level P=0.6826 for thirteen parameters)
- Data selection threshold: Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LO analysis (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai 2004) by providing NLO results and uncertainty estimates, following similar methodology used for polarized PDFs (AAC 2003) and fragmentation functions (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai 2007). The NPDFs are intended for interpreting heavy-ion reactions at RHIC and LHC, and for neutrino oscillation studies requiring nuclear corrections in oxygen; the authors note that future Drell-Yan measurements at J-PARC, Fermilab-E906, and GSI-FAIR, as well as electron facilities like eRHIC and eLIC, are needed to constrain the poorly-determined antiquark and gluon modifications.
