# Charm production in interactions of antiproton with proton and nuclei at ${\bar P}ANDA$ energies

> We study the production of charmed baryons in the antiproton-proton and antiproton-nucleus interactions within a fully covariant model that is based on an effective Lagrangian approach. The baryon production proceeds via the $t$-channel $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ meson-exchange diagrams. We have also explored the production of the charm-baryon hypernucleus $^{16}_{Λ_c^+}$O in the antiproton - $^{16}$O collisions. For antiproton beam momenta of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment, the 0$^\circ$ differential cross sections for the formation of $^{16}_{Λ_c^+}$O hypernuclear states with simple particle-hole configurations, have magnitudes in the range of a few $μ$b/sr.

## Key claim

The production of charmed baryons in antiproton-proton collisions and the charm-baryon hypernucleus ¹⁶_Λc⁺O in antiproton-¹⁶O collisions can be described within a fully covariant effective Lagrangian model using t-channel D⁰ and D*⁰ meson-exchange diagrams, with the caveat that the model uses largely phenomenological initial- and final-state interactions to account for distortion effects.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for charmed baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions (channels: Λ̄c⁻Λc⁺, Λ̄c⁻Σc⁺, Σ̄c⁻Σc⁺) using a single-channel effective Lagrangian model with t-channel D⁰ and D*⁰ meson-exchange diagrams. For charm-baryon hypernuclear production in antiproton-¹⁶O collisions, the bound proton and bound Λc⁺ spinors were obtained from solutions of the Dirac equation within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which treats quarks in non-overlapping nucleon bags interacting with σ and ω mesons in mean field approximation.

## Key numbers

- Threshold beam momenta: 10.162 GeV/c for Λ̄c⁻Λc⁺ production, 10.99 GeV/c for Λ̄c⁻Σc⁺ production
- At beam momentum around 15 GeV/c, total cross section for Λ̄c⁻Λc⁺ is about one order of magnitude larger than for Λ̄c⁻Σc⁺
- Coupling constants for vertices involving Λc⁺: g_ND⁰B = 13.98, g_ND*⁰B = 5.64, f_ND*⁰B = 18.37
- Coupling constants for vertices involving Σc⁺: g_ND⁰B = 2.69, g_ND*⁰B = 3.25, f_ND*⁰B = -7.88
- QMC model predicts three bound states for ¹⁶_Λc⁺O: Λc⁺ 0s₁/₂ (binding energy 12.78 MeV), Λc⁺ 0p₁/₂ (7.17 MeV), Λc⁺ 0p₃/₂ (7.20 MeV)
- Binding energy of outermost 0p₁/₂ proton in ¹⁶O: 11.87 MeV
- For beam momenta 8-15 GeV/c, 0° differential cross sections for ¹⁶_Λc⁺O states: 1.5-3.8 μb/sr (0⁻ state), 5.0-11.0 μb/sr (1⁻ state) for configuration (0p₁/₂⁻ᵖ, 0s₁/₂^Λc⁺); 0.9-2.8 μb/sr (1⁺ state), 1.6-6.0 μb/sr (2⁺ state) for configuration (0p₁/₂⁻ᵖ, 0p₃/₂^Λc⁺)
- Threshold beam momentum for ¹⁶_Λc⁺O production: 3.953 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Shyam (2014, 2016) and Haidenbauer (2010, 2014) on charmed baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions, and builds on predictions of Λc⁺ hypernuclei existence by Tyapkin (1975) and systematic studies by Tsushima (2004) using the QMC model. The results are relevant for the future PANDA experiment at FAIR, where beam momenta up to 15 GeV/c will be available, and the relatively substantial cross sections (0.9-11 μb/sr) together with the low threshold momentum (3.953 GeV/c) suggest that charm-baryon hypernuclear experiments could be performed even in the early stages of FAIR operations.
