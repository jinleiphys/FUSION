# Charm production in interactions of antiproton with proton and nuclei at ${\bar P}ANDA$ energies

> We study the production of charmed baryons in the antiproton-proton and antiproton-nucleus interactions within a fully covariant model that is based on an effective Lagrangian approach. The baryon production proceeds via the $t$-channel $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ meson-exchange diagrams. We have also explored the production of the charm-baryon hypernucleus $^{16}_{őõ_c^+}$O in the antiproton - $^{16}$O collisions. For antiproton beam momenta of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment, the 0$^\circ$ differential cross sections for the formation of $^{16}_{őõ_c^+}$O hypernuclear states with simple particle-hole configurations, have magnitudes in the range of a few $őľ$b/sr.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Shyam (2014, 2016) and Haidenbauer (2010, 2014) on charmed baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions, and builds on predictions of őõc‚Āļ hypernuclei existence by Tyapkin (1975) and systematic studies by Tsushima (2004) using the QMC model. The results are relevant for the future PANDA experiment at FAIR, where beam momenta up to 15 GeV/c will be available, and the relatively substantial cross sections (0.9-11 őľb/sr) together with the low threshold momentum (3.953 GeV/c) suggest that charm-baryon hypernuclear experiments could be performed even in the early stages of FAIR operations.
