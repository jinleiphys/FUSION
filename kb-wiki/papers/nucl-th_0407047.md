# Comment on "Partial energies fluctuations and negative heat capacities" by X. Campi et al

> Studying the energy partioning published in nucl-th/0406056v2 we show that the presented results do not fulfill the sum rule due to energy conservation. The observed fluctuations of the energy conservation test point to a numerical problem. Moreover, analysis of the binding energies show that the fragment recognition algorithm adopted by Campi et al. leads with a sizeable probability to fragments containing up to the total mass even for excitation energies as large as 3/4 of the total binding. This surprising result points to another problem since the published inter-fragment energy is not zero while a unique fragment is present. This problem may be due to either the fragment recognition algorithm or to the definition of the inter and intra-fragment energy. These numerical inconsistencies should be settled before any conclusion on the physics can be drawn.

## Key claim

The numerical results presented by Campi et al. (2004) are inconsistent because they violate the exact sum rule due to energy conservation, and the fragment recognition algorithm produces unphysical configurations such as a single fragment containing all 64 particles at excitation energies as large as 3/4 of the total binding energy, with a non-zero inter-fragment energy for that single fragment.

## Method

The authors checked the energy conservation sum rule S = K - K' + V_inter + ΔV = 0 using the published data from Campi et al. (2004), where K is the exact kinetic energy, K' is the approximate kinetic energy from fragment ground state energies, V_inter is the inter-fragment interaction, and ΔV is the configurational excitation energy. They also reconstructed the total binding energy B of the partitioned system from the published K, V_inter, ΔV, the Lennard-Jones mass table from Hill, and the reported excitation energy E*/N = 4, to examine the fragment recognition algorithm's behavior.

## Key numbers

- The average sum rule violation is ⟨S⟩ = -8.871 (in Lennard-Jones units), indicating a systematic error of a few percent.
- The variance of S is 3819, while the variance of K' (criticized by Campi et al.) is only 784, showing the sum rule fluctuations are much larger than the quantity under criticism.
- At time step 711, the total binding energy B reaches exactly B_64 = -329.62 (Lennard-Jones units), meaning the system is composed of a single fragment containing all 64 particles.
- For this single-fragment event, the reported inter-fragment energy V_inter = -29 (Lennard-Jones units), which should be zero.
- The total binding energy of the N=64 system is B_64 = -329.62, and for N=63 it is B_63 = -323.49, from the Hill mass table.
- The total energy E = E* + B_64 = -73.62, with E*/N = 4.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the numerical validity of the results in Campi et al. (2004), which criticized the experimental evaluation of kinetic energy fluctuations used to reconstruct heat capacities in phase transitions, as proposed in NegC-theo and applied in NegC-exp. The authors state that the numerical inconsistencies must be resolved before any physical conclusions can be drawn, and they mention a forthcoming paper showing that the independent fragment approximation is accurate when proper consistency tests are applied, referencing Palluto.
