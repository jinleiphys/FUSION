# Fluctuations of anisotropic flow from the finite number of rescatterings in a two-dimensional massless transport model

> We investigate the fluctuations of anisotropic transverse flow due to the finite number of scatterings in a two-dimensional system of massless particles. Using a set of initial geometries from a Monte Carlo Glauber model, we study how flow coefficients fluctuate about their mean value at the corresponding eccentricity, for several values of the scattering cross section. We also show how the distributions of the second and third event planes of anisotropic flow about the corresponding participant plane in the initial geometry evolve as a function of the mean number of scatterings in the system.

# Fluctuations of anisotropic flow from the finite number of rescatterings in a two-dimensional massless transport model

## Key claim
The fluctuations of anisotropic flow coefficients about their mean value at a given eccentricity arise not only from initial-state geometry fluctuations but also from the finite number of rescatterings per particle in the kinetic transport description, and these fluctuations increase with the average number of rescatterings per particle. The study is limited to a two-dimensional system, meaning the absolute flow values are not quantitatively meaningful for phenomenological applications, though the qualitative trends with the number of rescatterings are expected to persist in three-dimensional studies.

## Method
The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model (TGlauberMC code) to generate initial energy density profiles for lead-lead collisions at fixed impact parameters of 0, 6, and 9 fm. These profiles were converted into systems of 5 × 10^5 massless test particles per event, which were then evolved using a two-dimensional covariant transport algorithm with elastic two-to-two collisions. For each impact parameter, 10^4 initial energy distributions were generated, each converted into 10 independent particle distributions, and each evolved with three different scattering cross sections corresponding to different Knudsen numbers (mean free path over system size). The conditional probability distributions of flow coefficients at fixed eccentricity were characterized by their variance, skewness, and kurtosis as functions of the inverse mean Knudsen number (proportional to the average number of rescatterings per particle).

## Key numbers
- Impact parameters used: b = 0, 6, and 9 fm, with the impact parameter direction fixed along the x-axis
- Number of events per impact parameter: 10^4 Monte Carlo Glauber events
- Number of test particles per event: N_p = 5 × 10^5
- Number of iterations per initial energy profile: N_iter = 10, yielding a numerical uncertainty on v_n of δv_n = 1/√(N_iter · N_p) ≈ 4.5 × 10^-4
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at √s = 5.02 TeV: σ^inel_NN = (67.6 ± 0.6) mb
- Gaussian smearing width for energy density: R_N = 0.5 × √(σ^inel_NN/π) ≈ 0.7 fm
- Linear relation between average number of rescatterings and inverse Knudsen number: ⟨N_resc.⟩ ≈ 0.866 ⟨Kn⟩^-1
- Hydrodynamic limit values from fits: v_2^hydro/ε_2 = 0.2621, v_3^hydro/ε_3 = 0.2096
- Fit parameters for v_2 vs Kn: A_2 = 1.1967
- Fit parameters for v_3 vs Kn: A_3 = 0.6973, B_3 = 1.7922, C_3 = 13.945
- Dilution parameter D ≤ 0.1 in all simulations, ensuring dilute regime
- Simulation runtime: 15 fm/c for all events
- Initial-state flow fluctuations: variance σ_v^2 ≈ 1/(2N_p) = 10^-6, with skewness and excess kurtosis compatible with zero (Gaussian distribution)

## Context
This work extends the exploratory study of Vogel et al. (2007) on the conditional probability distribution of elliptic flow at fixed eccentricity, going beyond the Gaussian approximation to study higher moments (skewness and kurtosis). It contrasts with hydrodynamic approaches (Heinz 2013, Luzum 2013, Bhalerao 2020) which are too deterministic for studying fluctuations arising from finite numbers of scatterings, and with fluctuating hydrodynamics (Kapusta 2011) whose connection to microscopic fluctuations is unclear. The study uses the transport algorithm of Gombeaud and Ollitrault (2007) and the TGlauberMC code of Loizides (2014). The results enable future investigations of how finite-number-of-scatterings fluctuations affect flow observables in smaller collision systems where the number of rescatterings per particle is small (Nagle 2018).
