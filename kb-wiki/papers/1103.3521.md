# Viscous damping of r-modes: Large amplitude saturation

> We analyze the viscous damping of r-mode oscillations of compact stars, taking into account non-linear viscous effects in the large-amplitude regime. The qualitatively different cases of hadronic stars, strange quark stars, and hybrid stars are studied. We calculate the viscous damping times of r-modes, obtaining numerical results and also general approximate analytic expressions that explicitly exhibit the dependence on the parameters that are relevant for a future spindown evolution calculation. The strongly enhanced damping of large amplitude oscillations leads to damping times that are considerably lower than those obtained when the amplitude dependence of the viscosity is neglected. Consequently, large-amplitude viscous damping competes with the gravitational instability at all physical frequencies and could stop the r-mode growth in case this is not done before by non-linear hydrodynamic mechanisms.

## Key claim

The suprathermal enhancement of bulk viscosity at large r-mode amplitudes can provide a saturation mechanism that stops the gravitational instability, but only over a significant region of parameter space; if the amplitude exceeds a critical maximum value, viscous damping cannot stop r-mode growth and other non-linear hydrodynamic mechanisms are required.

## Method

The authors compute the viscous damping times of r-modes for hadronic stars, strange quark stars, and hybrid stars, incorporating the full amplitude-dependent bulk viscosity derived from weak interaction rates. They use the general-relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain equilibrium star configurations, a Newtonian slow-rotation expansion for the r-mode profile, and parameterized forms of the weak rates and shear viscosity for each matter type. The damping time integrals over the star are expressed in terms of two-parameter functions that must be tabulated numerically, with approximate analytic expressions given for the intermediate suprathermal regime where the viscosity rises linearly with the squared amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For neutron stars with modified Urca processes, the weak rate parameter is $\tilde{\Gamma} = 4.68 \times 10^{-19} \, \mathrm{MeV}^{(3-\delta)}$ with $\delta = 6$ and three non-linear coefficients $\chi_1 = 189/(367\pi^2)$, $\chi_2 = 21/(367\pi^4)$, $\chi_3 = 3/(1835\pi^6)$
- For quark matter non-leptonic processes, the weak rate parameter is $\tilde{\Gamma} = 6.59 \times 10^{-12} (\mu_q/300\,\mathrm{MeV})^5 \, \mathrm{MeV}^{(3-\delta)}$ with $\delta = 2$ and $\chi_1 = 1/(4\pi^2)$
- The 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron star model has radius 11.5 km, central density 3.43 $n_0$, and Kepler frequency 6.02 kHz
- The 2.0 $M_\odot$ strange star model has radius 11.6 km, central density 4.95 $n_0$, and Kepler frequency 7.09 kHz
- The 2.0 $M_\odot$ hybrid star model has radius 12.2 km, central density 4.89 $n_0$, and Kepler frequency 6.62 kHz

## Context

This work extends the authors' companion paper (Alford, Mahmoodifar, Schwenzer 2010) on small-amplitude r-mode instability regions to the large-amplitude suprathermal regime, building on the general bulk viscosity formalism developed in Alford et al. (2010). It provides an explicit framework for including r-mode saturation in stellar evolution analyses, superseding previous model assumptions used in Owen et al. (1998). The results enable future spindown evolution calculations that can determine whether r-mode saturation via viscous damping competes with other proposed mechanisms such as mode coupling (Bondarescu 2007, Arras 2002), decay into differential rotation (Gressman 2002, Lin 2004), or crustal friction (Bildsten 2000).
