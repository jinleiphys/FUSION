# Rummukainen-Gottlieb's formula on two-particle system with different mass

> Lüscher established a non-perturbative formula to extract the elastic scattering phases from two-particle energy spectrum in a torus using lattice simulations. Rummukainen and Gottlieb further extend it to the moving frame, which is devoted to the system of two identical particles. In this work, we generalize Rummukainen-Gottlieb's formula to the generic two-particle system where two particles are explicitly distinguishable, namely, the masses of the two particles are different. The finite size formula are achieved for both $C_{4v}$ and $C_{2v}$ symmetries. Our analytical results will be very helpful for the study of some resonances, such as kappa, vector kaon, and so on.

## Key claim

This work generalizes the Rummukainen-Gottlieb formula, originally derived for two identical particles in a moving frame, to the case of two distinguishable particles with different masses, providing the finite-size energy shift relations for the reduced symmetry groups \(C_{4v}\) and \(C_{2v}\) rather than the \(D_{4h}\) and \(D_{2h}\) groups applicable to identical particles.

## Method

The author derives the finite-size quantization condition for two spin-0 particles of masses \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) in a cubic box of side \(L\) with periodic boundary conditions and non-zero total momentum \(\mathbf{P} = (2\pi/L)\mathbf{d}\). Starting from the Klein-Gordon equations in the center-of-mass frame, the wave function is shown to satisfy a Helmholtz equation, and the periodic boundary conditions in the moving frame are transformed into a modified \(\mathbf{d}\)-periodic condition for the center-of-mass wave function. The singular periodic solutions of the Helmholtz equation are constructed via a Green function, leading to a determinant condition that relates the scattering phase shifts to the energy eigenvalues through a modified zeta function \(\mathcal{Z}_{00}^{\mathbf{d}}(1;q^2)\), where the set \(P_{\mathbf{d}}\) depends on the mass difference.

## Key numbers

* The scattering momentum \(k\) is defined by \(k = \frac{1}{2\overline{E}_{CM}}\sqrt{[\overline{E}_{CM}^2 - (m_1 - m_2)^2][\overline{E}_{CM}^2 - (m_1 + m_2)^2]}\), where \(\overline{E}_{CM}\) is the interacting center-of-mass energy.
* The \(s\)-wave phase shift \(\delta_0\) is related to the scattering momentum by \(\tan\delta_0(k) = \frac{\gamma\pi^{3/2}q}{\mathcal{Z}_{00}^{\mathbf{d}}(1;q^2)}\), where \(q = kL/(2\pi)\) and \(\gamma\) is the boost factor.
* The modified zeta function is \(\mathcal{Z}_{00}^{\mathbf{d}}(s;q^2) = \sum_{\mathbf{r}\in P_{\mathbf{d}}} \frac{1}{(|\mathbf{r}|^2 - q^2)^s}\), with the set \(P_{\mathbf{d}} = \left\{ \mathbf{r} \mid \mathbf{r} = \vec{\gamma}^{-1} \left[\mathbf{n} + \frac{\mathbf{d}}{2} \cdot \left(1+\frac{m_2^2 - m_1^2}{E_{CM}^2}\right)\right], \mathbf{n}\in\mathbb{Z}^3 \right\}\).
* The constant \(\beta'\) appearing in the wave function transformation is \(\beta' = \frac{m_2 - m_1}{m_1+m_2} + \frac{m_2^2 - m_1^2}{E_{CM}^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the foundational non-perturbative formalism of Lüscher (1986, 1991) for extracting scattering phases from lattice QCD energy spectra, and the moving-frame generalization by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) for identical particles. It also relates to the asymmetric-box generalization by Feng et al. (2004). The derived formulas enable lattice studies of resonances involving non-identical particles, such as the \(\kappa\) resonance in \(\pi K\) scattering and the vector kaon \(K^*\) decay, where the particles have different masses.
