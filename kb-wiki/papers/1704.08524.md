# The Two-Nucleon 1S0 Amplitude Zero in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We present a new rearrangement of short-range interactions in the $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon channel within Chiral Effective Field Theory. This is intended to reproduce the amplitude zero (scattering momentum $\simeq$ 340 MeV) at leading order, and it includes subleading corrections perturbatively in a way that is consistent with renormalization-group invariance. Systematic improvement is shown at next-to-leading order, and we obtain results that fit empirical phase shifts remarkably well all the way up to the pion-production threshold. An approach in which pions have been integrated out is included, which allows us to derive analytic results that also fit phenomenology surprisingly well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a rearrangement of short-range interactions in chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) for the 1S0 nucleon-nucleon channel that places the amplitude zero (scattering momentum approximately 340 MeV) at leading order, with subleading corrections treated perturbatively while maintaining renormalization-group invariance; the abstract glosses over the fact that this requires promoting to leading order interactions that naive dimensional analysis would assign to higher orders, and that the virtual bound state is moved to its empirical position only at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors introduce two auxiliary dibaryon fields to reorganize the short-range interactions in the 1S0 channel, with one dibaryon providing a momentum-independent contact interaction and the other providing energy dependence through its kinetic term. They develop a power counting where the inverse scattering length is suppressed by one power of the breakdown scale (treated as zero at leading order), while the amplitude zero momentum and effective range are treated as leading-order scales. The leading-order amplitude is solved non-perturbatively, and next-to-leading order corrections are included perturbatively, with renormalization performed by canceling cutoff-dependent terms against bare couplings.

## Key numbers

- Amplitude zero occurs at center-of-mass momentum k0 approximately 340 MeV, extracted from Nijmegen partial-wave analysis
- Scattering length a approximately -23.7 fm (approximately -(8 MeV)^{-1}) for neutron-proton scattering
- Effective range r0 approximately 2.7 fm (approximately (73 MeV)^{-1})
- Shape parameter P0 approximately 2.0 fm^3 (approximately (158 MeV)^{-3})
- Leading-order prediction for the dimensionless parameter z1 at the amplitude zero: z1^{[0]} = 0.4 +/- 0.1, compared to empirical value z1 approximately 0.6
- Leading-order prediction for the ratio P0 k0^2/(2 r0) = 1.0 +/- 0.3, compared to empirical value 1.1 from Ref. PB09
- Leading-order redundant pole at kappa2^{[0]} approximately 190 MeV and bound-state pole at kappa3^{[0]} approximately 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Long (2013), which promoted an energy-dependent short-range interaction to leading order but still produced phase shifts around 25 degrees at the amplitude zero momentum, and generalizes the power counting of van Kolck (1998) for the "other unnatural EFT" to include non-perturbative pions. It is inspired by an effective field theory for doublet neutron-deuteron scattering at very low energies (Ref. nd) and resembles the unitarized approach of Lutz (2000). The method enables systematic improvement of the 1S0 channel description up to the pion-production threshold and can be extended to many-body systems through the identification of the amplitude zero with a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson pole.
