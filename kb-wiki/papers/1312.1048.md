# Pairing effects on spinodal decomposition of asymmetric nuclear matter

> We investigate the impact of pairing correlations on the behavior of unstable asymmetric nuclear matter at low temperature. We focus on the relative role of the pairing interaction, coupling nucleons of the same type (neutrons or protons), with respect to the symmetry potential, which enhances the neutron-proton attraction, along the clusterization process driven by spinodal instabilities. It is found that, especially at the transition temperature from the normal to the superfluid phase, pairing effects may induce significant variations in the isotopic content of the clusterized matter. This analysis is potentially useful to gather information on the temperature dependence of nuclear pairing and, in general, on the properties of clusterized low-density matter, of interest also in the astrophysical context.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations between identical nucleons (neutron-neutron or proton-proton) can induce significant variations in the isotopic content of clusterized matter during spinodal decomposition, but only near the critical temperature of the normal-to-superfluid phase transition; the effect is small compared to the influence of the symmetry energy parametrization.

## Method

The authors employ a Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov framework that unifies pairing and mean-field effective interactions for asymmetric nuclear matter. They adopt a local pairing interaction acting only between identical nucleons in a spin-singlet state, with the pairing strength fitted to reproduce the zero-temperature pairing gap in pure neutron matter from Brueckner calculations using the Argonne v14 potential. The curvature matrix of the free-energy density is diagonalized to determine the spinodal instability direction and the associated isospin distillation effect, comparing results with and without pairing correlations for two different symmetry energy parametrizations (asy-stiff and asy-soft).

## Key numbers

- The maximum pairing gap in pure neutron matter at zero temperature is approximately 3 MeV, reached at a neutron density of 0.02 fm^-3.
- The critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal transition at the density where the gap is maximum is T_c = 1.8 MeV.
- The pairing interaction strength parameters are V_pi = -1157.51 MeV, eta = 0.884, and alpha = 0.256.
- At zero temperature, pairing correlations induce up to a 20% variation in the quantity gamma = (a - b) (the difference between density derivatives of chemical potentials) at total densities around 0.08 fm^-3 and asymmetry I between 0.1 and 0.2.
- The energy cutoff for the gap equations is epsilon_Lambda = 16 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spinodal instabilities and isospin distillation in asymmetric nuclear matter (Colonna et al., rep1; Baran et al., BaranNPA703) by incorporating pairing correlations that were previously neglected. The pairing interaction is treated following the approach of Chamel (2008) and uses the zero-temperature pairing gap from Brueckner calculations by Lombardo et al. The results show that while pairing effects on the instability strength are negligible, they can modify the distillation mechanism near the critical temperature, which is relevant for understanding multifragmentation in nuclear reactions (Borderie) and clustering in neutron star crusts (Lattimer, Camille). The analysis enables future extraction of information on the temperature dependence of nuclear pairing from experimental data on clusterized low-density matter.
