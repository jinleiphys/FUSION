# Reduced $E$1 $S$-factor of $^{12}$C($őĪ$,$ő≥_0$)$^{16}$O

> The astrophysical $S$-factor of $E$1 transition for $^{12}$C($őĪ$,$ő≥_0$)$^{16}$O is discussed in the $R$-matrix theory. The reduced $őĪ$-particle widths of the 1$^-_1$ ($E_x= 7.12$ MeV) and 1$^-_2$ ($E_x= 9.59$ MeV) states are extracted from the result of the potential model. The formal parameters are obtained without the linear approximation to the shift function. The resultant $E$1 $S$-factor is not strongly enhanced by the subthreshold 1$^-_1$ state if the channel radius is 4.75 fm. The calculated $ő≤$-delayed $őĪ$-particle spectrum of $^{16}$N and the $p$-wave phase shift of $őĪ$+$^{12}$C elastic scattering are also found to be consistent with the previous studies. The small channel radius leads to the low penetrability to the Coulomb barrier, and it makes the reduced $E$1 $S$-factor below the barrier. Owing to the large reduced width from the molecular structure, the $R$-matrix pole of the 1$^-_2$ state is shifted in the vicinity of 1$^-_1$. The proximity of the two poles suppresses the interference between the states. The transparency of the $őĪ$+$^{12}$C system appears to be expressed as the shrinking strong interaction region.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous potential model calculations (Katsuma 2008, 2010) and addresses the long-standing problem of determining the 12C(őĪ,ő≥)16O reaction rate, which controls the C/O ratio at the end of helium burning. The result contradicts the widely used R-matrix analyses that adopt larger channel radii (e.g., ac = 6.5 fm in Azuma 1994) and find larger E1 S-factors. The paper shows that the small channel radius leads to low Coulomb barrier penetrability and weak interference between the 1‚ąí1 and 1‚ąí2 states, enabling a consistent description of the ő≤-delayed őĪ spectrum of 16N and p-wave phase shifts of őĪ+12C elastic scattering without requiring strong enhancement from the subthreshold state.
