# Reduced $E$1 $S$-factor of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ_0$)$^{16}$O

> The astrophysical $S$-factor of $E$1 transition for $^{12}$C($α$,$γ_0$)$^{16}$O is discussed in the $R$-matrix theory. The reduced $α$-particle widths of the 1$^-_1$ ($E_x= 7.12$ MeV) and 1$^-_2$ ($E_x= 9.59$ MeV) states are extracted from the result of the potential model. The formal parameters are obtained without the linear approximation to the shift function. The resultant $E$1 $S$-factor is not strongly enhanced by the subthreshold 1$^-_1$ state if the channel radius is 4.75 fm. The calculated $β$-delayed $α$-particle spectrum of $^{16}$N and the $p$-wave phase shift of $α$+$^{12}$C elastic scattering are also found to be consistent with the previous studies. The small channel radius leads to the low penetrability to the Coulomb barrier, and it makes the reduced $E$1 $S$-factor below the barrier. Owing to the large reduced width from the molecular structure, the $R$-matrix pole of the 1$^-_2$ state is shifted in the vicinity of 1$^-_1$. The proximity of the two poles suppresses the interference between the states. The transparency of the $α$+$^{12}$C system appears to be expressed as the shrinking strong interaction region.

## Key claim

The astrophysical E1 S-factor for 12C(α,γ0)16O is not strongly enhanced by the subthreshold 1−1 state when a channel radius of 4.75 fm is used, contrary to many previous analyses that used larger channel radii and found larger S-factor values.

## Method

The author uses R-matrix theory with four 1− states (1−1, 1−2, 1−3, 1−4) and extracts reduced α-particle widths from potential model wavefunctions rather than from the conventional ANC-based formula. A higher-order correction to the linear approximation of the shift function is introduced to properly treat the large reduced width of the 1−2 molecular state, and the boundary condition is set so that the logarithmic derivative at the channel radius is approximately zero.

## Key numbers

- The E1 S-factor at Ec.m. = 300 keV is approximately 3.6 keV·b for ac = 4.75 fm, which is much smaller than recent estimates such as (100 ± 28) keV·b (Oulebsir 2012), (84 ± 21) keV·b (Tang 2010), (80 ± 18) keV·b (NACRE2), and (98 ± 7) keV·b (Zheng 2015)
- The 1−1 state has observed energy E11 = −0.0451 MeV (bound, below the α+12C threshold at Ex = 7.162 MeV) with observed reduced width γ11 = 0.345 MeV1/2 and dimensionless width θ2 = 0.128 at ac = 4.75 fm
- The 1−2 state has observed energy E21 = 2.434 MeV with observed reduced width γ21 = 0.850 MeV1/2, α-particle width Γα = 432 keV, and dimensionless width θ2 = 0.780 at ac = 4.75 fm
- The 1−3 state (Ex = 12.44 MeV) has Γ31 = (92 ± 8) keV and the 1−4 state (Ex = 13.09 MeV) has Γ41 = (45 ± 18) keV
- The ANC value used is C2 = 5.0 × 1028 fm−1
- The acceptable channel radius range is ac ≈ 4.2–5.2 fm, with the contact distance estimated as ⟨r2⟩1/2_12C + ⟨r2⟩1/2_α ≈ 4.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous potential model calculations (Katsuma 2008, 2010) and addresses the long-standing problem of determining the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction rate, which controls the C/O ratio at the end of helium burning. The result contradicts the widely used R-matrix analyses that adopt larger channel radii (e.g., ac = 6.5 fm in Azuma 1994) and find larger E1 S-factors. The paper shows that the small channel radius leads to low Coulomb barrier penetrability and weak interference between the 1−1 and 1−2 states, enabling a consistent description of the β-delayed α spectrum of 16N and p-wave phase shifts of α+12C elastic scattering without requiring strong enhancement from the subthreshold state.
