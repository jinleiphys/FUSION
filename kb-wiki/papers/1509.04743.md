# The current evaluation of Vud

> The Vud element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix can be determined from several different experimental approaches: either 0+-to-0+ superallowed nuclear beta decays, neutron decay, nuclear mirror decays, or pion beta decay. Currently all give consistent results but, because the nuclear superallowed value has an uncertainty at least a factor of seven less than all other results, it dominates the result. A new survey of world superallowed-decay data establishes the Ft values of 14 separate superallowed transitions to a precision of order 0.1% or better; and all 14 are statistically consistent with one another. This very robust data set yields the result Vud = 0.97417(21), the value we recommend.

## Key claim

The paper recommends a value of Vud = 0.97417(21) based on superallowed nuclear beta decays, but this recommendation depends on the assumption that the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis is valid and that the specific set of nuclear-structure-dependent corrections used in the analysis is correct, as demonstrated by the statistical consistency of the 14 corrected Ft values.

## Method

The authors conducted a critical survey of world data for 20 superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decays, compiling 222 individual measurements from 177 published references. They obtained world-average ft values for 18 transitions with complete data, applied radiative corrections (transition-dependent and transition-independent parts) and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to extract corrected Ft values, then tested the 14 best-determined Ft values for statistical consistency to validate the CVC hypothesis and the correction calculations before extracting Vud.

## Key numbers

- Vud = 0.97417(21) from nuclear superallowed decays (the recommended value)
- Vud = 0.9754(14) from neutron decay using the averaging method
- Vud range from neutron decay: 0.9707 to 0.9761 using the range method
- Vud = 0.9718(17) from nuclear mirror decays (five nuclei: 19Ne, 21Na, 29P, 35Ar, 37K)
- Vud = 0.9749(26) from pion beta decay
- The nuclear superallowed value has an uncertainty at least a factor of seven smaller than all other results
- The top-row CKM unitarity sum yields 0.99978(55) when combining the recommended Vud with PDG values for Vus and Vub
- Neutron mean-life from beam measurements: 888.1(20) s; from bottle measurements: 879.5(7) s

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous surveys of superallowed decay data (Hardy and Towner, 2015) and updates the correction calculations from their earlier work (Towner and Hardy, 2008). The results confirm the CVC hypothesis and the absence of a significant scalar current, and enable a precision test of CKM unitarity. The paper notes emerging tension between Vud combined with Vus from lattice QCD versus Vus/Vud from kaon-to-pion decay ratios, which will need resolution in future work.
