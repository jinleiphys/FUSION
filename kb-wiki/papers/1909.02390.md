# Initial and final state temperatures of antiproton emission sources in high energy collisions

> The momentum or transverse momentum spectra of antiprotons produced at mid-rapidity in proton-helium ($p$+He), gold-gold (Au+Au), deuton-gold ($d$+Au), and lead-lead (Pb+Pb) collisions over an energy range from a few GeV to a few TeV are analyzed by the Erlang distribution, the inverse power-law (the Hagedorn function), and the blast-wave fit, or the superposition of two-component step function. The excitation functions of parameters such as the mean transverse momentum, initial state temperature, kinetic freeze-out temperature, and transverse flow velocity increase (slightly) from a few GeV to a few TeV and from peripheral to central collisions. At high energy and in central collisions, large collision energy is deposited in the system, which results in high degrees of excitation and expansion.

## Key claim

The initial state temperature ($T_i$) and final state kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_0$) of antiproton emission sources can be extracted from transverse momentum spectra using multiple fitting functions (Erlang distribution, inverse power-law/Hagedorn function, and blast-wave fit), with both temperatures increasing slightly from a few GeV to a few TeV collision energy and from peripheral to central collisions, though the extracted values depend on the specific model or function used in the fit.

## Method

The authors analyzed antiproton transverse momentum spectra at mid-rapidity from p+He, Au+Au, d+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions across an energy range from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV using three different fitting approaches: the Erlang distribution from the multisource thermal model, the inverse power-law (Hagedorn function), and the blast-wave fit with Tsallis statistics (with entropy index q=1.01). The initial state temperature was determined from the root-mean-square transverse momentum via $T_i = \sqrt{\langle p_T^2 \rangle / 2}$, while the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_0$ and transverse flow velocity $\beta_T$ were extracted from the blast-wave fit. Monte Carlo calculations were used to transform between transverse momentum and momentum distributions where needed.

## Key numbers

- For 7.7 GeV Au+Au collisions (0-5% centrality): $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.785 \pm 0.023$ GeV/$c$, $T_i = 0.579 \pm 0.018$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.135 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $\beta_T = 0.340 \pm 0.020$ $c$
- For 200 GeV Au+Au collisions (0-5% centrality): $\langle p_T \rangle = 1.141 \pm 0.010$ GeV/$c$, $T_i = 0.864 \pm 0.008$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.220 \pm 0.020$ GeV, $\beta_T = 0.435 \pm 0.010$ $c$
- For 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions (0-5% centrality): $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.889 \pm 0.023 / 1.957 \pm 0.142$ GeV/$c$ (two-component), $T_i = 0.659 \pm 0.031 / 1.439 \pm 0.065$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.310 \pm 0.060 / 0.170 \pm 0.010$ GeV, $\beta_T = 0.500 \pm 0.010 / 0.371 \pm 0.005$ $c$
- For 200 GeV d+Au collisions (0-20% centrality): $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.972 \pm 0.015$ GeV/$c$, $T_i = 0.741 \pm 0.013$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.163 \pm 0.006$ GeV, $\beta_T = 0.287 \pm 0.006$ $c$
- For p+He collisions at 110 GeV, the Erlang distribution gave $\langle p_T \rangle$ values ranging from $0.943 \pm 0.005$ to $1.739 \pm 0.015$ GeV/$c$ depending on the momentum range

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of freeze-out parameters in heavy-ion collisions by applying multiple fitting methods (Erlang distribution, Hagedorn function, blast-wave fit) to antiproton spectra across a wide energy range from a few GeV to TeV scales, complementing earlier analyses by the STAR Collaboration (2010) and LHCb Collaboration (2013, 2014). The results enable comparison of initial and final state temperatures extracted from the same dataset using different theoretical frameworks, and provide excitation functions of $\langle p_T \rangle$, $T_i$, $T_0$, and $\beta_T$ as functions of collision energy and centrality that can be used to test models of the collision evolution from initial partonic interactions to kinetic freeze-out.
