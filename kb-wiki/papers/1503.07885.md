# Bethe states for the two-site Bose-Hubbard model: a binomial approach

> We calculate explicitly the Bethe vectors states by the algebraic Bethe ansatz method with the $gl(2)$-invariant $R$-matrix for the two-site Bose-Hubbard model. Using a binomial expansion of the n-th power of a sum of two operators we get and solve a recursion equation. We calculate the scalar product and the norm of the Bethe vectors states. The form factors of the imbalance current operator are also computed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an explicit construction of Bethe vector states for the two-site Bose-Hubbard model using a binomial expansion method, but the construction is limited to the two-site (two-well) specialization and the results are expressed in terms of a recursion equation whose solution is given in closed form only for the coefficients of the binomial expansion.

## Method

The authors apply the algebraic Bethe ansatz method with a \(gl(2)\)-invariant \(R\)-matrix to the two-site Bose-Hubbard model. They construct the monodromy matrix from Lax operators for each site, derive an algebraic relation between the \(\hat{C}\) and \(\hat{D}\) operators, and then expand the \(n\)-th power of the sum of two operators (the \(\mathfrak{\hat{\mathcal{D}}}\) operator) using a binomial expansion that accounts for non-commutativity. This expansion yields a recursion equation for the coefficients, which they solve, and they then compute the scalar product, norm, and form factors of the imbalance current operator.

## Key numbers

* The Bethe ansatz equations (BAE) are given as \(\eta^2 (v^2_i -\omega^2) = \prod ^N_{j \neq i}\frac {v_i -v_j - \eta}{v_i - v_j + \eta}\) for \(i,j = 1,\ldots,N\), where \(\eta\) and \(\omega\) are parameters and \(v_i\) are the Bethe roots.
* The recursion equation for the binomial expansion coefficients is \(C_{n+1,j} = \eta \;\sqrt{j} \; (n + 1 - j)\;C_{n,j-1} + \eta^{-1}\sqrt{n + 1 - j}\; C_{n,j}\) with condition \(C_{j,j} = 0\).
* The solution for the coefficients is \(C_{n,j} = \eta^{2j - n} \sqrt{\frac{j!}{(n-j)!}}\sum_{l=0}^{n-j} (-1)^{n+l-j}\; l^n \left(\begin{array}{c} n - j \\ l \end{array}\right)\).
* The form factor for the imbalance current operator \(\frac{\hat{N}_1 - \hat{N}_2}{N}\) is expressed as a sum involving the coefficients \(C_{n,j}\) and the factor \(\left[1-\frac{2(n-j)}{N}\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic Bethe ansatz treatment of the two-site Bose-Hubbard model (also known as the canonical Josephson Hamiltonian) previously studied by Santos et al. (2006, 2013) and others. The model is a special case of the bosonic multi-state two-well model and is related to the Schwinger bosonic realization of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, 1965; Dukelsky, 2004). The explicit construction of Bethe vectors enables calculation of scalar products and form factors, which are useful for computing correlation functions and average values of operators in integrable models, and has potential applications in planar \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory in the context of AdS/CFT integrability (Gromov et al., 2014).
