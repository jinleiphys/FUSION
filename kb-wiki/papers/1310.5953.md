# Role reversal in first and second sound in a relativistic superfluid

> Relativistic superfluidity at arbitrary temperature, chemical potential and (uniform) superflow is discussed within a self-consistent field-theoretical approach. Our starting point is a complex scalar field with a $φ^4$ interaction, for which we calculate the 2-particle-irreducible effective action in the Hartree approximation. With this underlying microscopic theory, we can obtain the two-fluid picture of a superfluid, and compute properties such as the superfluid density and the entrainment coefficient for all temperatures below the critical temperature for superfluidity. We compute the critical velocity, taking into account the full self-consistent effect of the temperature and superflow on the quasiparticle dispersion. We also discuss first and second sound modes and how first (second) sound evolves from a density (temperature) wave at low temperatures to a temperature (density) wave at high temperatures. This role reversal is investigated for ultra-relativistic and near-non-relativistic systems for zero and nonzero superflow. For nonzero superflow, we also observe a role reversal as a function of the direction of the sound wave.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a relativistic superfluid described by a complex scalar field with quartic interactions, the roles of first and second sound can reverse as temperature increases: first sound evolves from a density wave at low temperatures to a temperature wave at high temperatures, while second sound undergoes the opposite transition. This role reversal also occurs as a function of the direction of the sound wave when a nonzero superflow is present.

## Method

The authors use a complex scalar field with a φ⁴ interaction and compute the 2-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action in the Hartree approximation at two-loop level, which allows self-consistent determination of the condensate and quasiparticle masses for all temperatures below the critical temperature. They enforce the Goldstone theorem by modifying the stationarity equations, then compute the superfluid density, entrainment coefficient, and sound velocities from the resulting self-consistent mass and pressure. The model is studied for both ultra-relativistic and near-non-relativistic regimes, with zero and nonzero superflow.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for superfluidity in color-flavor locked quark matter is expected to be of order T_c ~ 10 MeV.
- For neutron matter, the critical temperature for superfluidity is about 10 keV at most, based on recent observations.
- The Euler-Mascheroni constant γ ≈ 0.5772 appears in the finite parts of the renormalized momentum sums.
- The Goldstone mode slope at zero superflow is given by ε_k⁺ = √[(M² − μ²)/(M² + μ²)] k, where M is the self-consistent mass and μ the chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous low-temperature, weak-coupling analysis (Alford et al., 2013) to arbitrary temperatures up to the critical temperature, using a self-consistent 2PI formalism that goes beyond the one-loop effective action. The results are relevant for superfluid neutron matter in compact stars, where temperatures may be comparable to the critical temperature, and for color-superconducting quark matter phases such as color-flavor locked and color-spin locked phases. The study also connects to holographic models of superfluidity (Herzog, 2008; Herzog, 2009; Amado, 2013) and to non-relativistic systems like liquid helium and ultra-cold atoms, where similar role reversal of sound modes has been observed.
