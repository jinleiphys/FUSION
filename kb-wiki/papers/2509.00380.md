# Hydrodynamic effects on spin polarization along the beam direction in Au+Au and p+Pb collisions

> We investigate hydrodynamic effects on the spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16$ TeV using the CLVisc hydrodynamic framework. We present numerical results for the second Fourier sine coefficient of the longitudinal spin polarization, $\langle P_{z} \sin 2(ϕ_{p} - Ψ_{2}) \rangle$, as a function of multiplicity (centrality) under three equilibrium scenarios: $Λ$ equilibrium, $s$-quark equilibrium, and isothermal equilibrium. We highlight the respective roles of thermal vorticity and the thermal-shear tensor in generating $\langle P_{z} \sin 2(ϕ_{p} - Ψ_{2}) \rangle$ across collision systems and scenarios.

# Hydrodynamic effects on spin polarization along the beam direction in Au+Au and p+Pb collisions

## Key claim
Hydrodynamic effects alone (thermal vorticity and thermal-shear tensor) can successfully describe the centrality dependence of longitudinal spin polarization in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, but they fail to reproduce the CMS data for p+Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV, producing a net negative signal that contradicts the measured positive values across all three equilibrium scenarios considered.

## Method
The authors used the (3+1)-D relativistic hydrodynamic model CLVisc with AMPT initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Trento-3D initial conditions for p+Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV, followed by hadronic afterburner evolution with SMASH. They computed the second Fourier sine coefficient of longitudinal spin polarization, ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p − Ψ_2)⟩, via a modified Cooper-Frye formula under three equilibrium scenarios: Λ equilibrium (using m_Λ = 1.116 GeV), s-quark equilibrium (using m_s = 0.3 GeV), and isothermal equilibrium (neglecting all temperature gradients ∂_μ T).

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: s-quark equilibrium and isothermal equilibrium results agree with experimental data, while Λ equilibrium results are inconsistent with measurements.
- p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV: thermal vorticity gives a negative contribution and thermal-shear tensor gives a positive contribution to ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p − Ψ_2)⟩ across all multiplicities, but their combined effect yields an overall negative signal that contradicts CMS experimental data.
- Particle masses used in the modified Cooper-Frye formula: m_Λ = 1.116 GeV for Λ equilibrium, m_s = 0.3 GeV for s-quark equilibrium.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hydrodynamic contributions to spin polarization in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Fu 2021, Becattini 2021, Yi 2021, Alzhrani 2022, Wu 2022) to small collision systems, motivated by recent CMS measurements of beam-direction spin polarization in p+Pb collisions (CMS 2025). The findings confirm that for Au+Au collisions, the results align with earlier calculations by Fu (2021) and Becattini (2021), but reveal a significant discrepancy for p+Pb systems that cannot be resolved by current hydrodynamic mechanisms alone. This opens the need for new polarization mechanisms specific to small collision systems in future theoretical work.
