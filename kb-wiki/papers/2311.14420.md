# An NJL model analysis of a magnetised nonextensive QCD medium

> We investigate the effect of a background magnetic field when applied to a nonextensive QCD medium in a $2+1$ flavour Nambu\textemdash Jona-Lasinio model. The effect of a constant as well as an $eB$-dependent coupling is considered. For the constant coupling, the well-known magnetic catalysis effect is observed with reduced strength in both the condensates and the transition temperatures compared to the standard extensive medium. In the case of a field-dependent coupling, we observe a competition between the nonextensive parameter, $q$ and $eB$. With sufficiently high $q$-values, the phenomenon of inverse magnetic catalysis \textemdash\, a well-known trait in effective models featuring $eB$-dependent coupling, appears to be eliminated within the range of magnetic fields examined in our present study.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a nonextensive QCD medium described by the Tsallis statistics, the inverse magnetic catalysis effect, a well-known feature of effective models with magnetic-field-dependent coupling, can be eliminated within the examined magnetic field range when the nonextensive parameter \(q\) is sufficiently high, due to a competition between \(q\) and \(eB\).

## Method

The authors use a \(2+1\) flavour Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a background magnetic field, incorporating both a constant scalar coupling \(G_S\) and an \(eB\)-dependent coupling \(G_S(\xi)\) fitted to lattice QCD data from Ferreira (2014). The Tsallis nonextensive statistics is introduced by replacing the standard exponential and logarithmic functions in the medium term of the thermodynamic potential with their \(q\)-generalised forms, while keeping the model parameters unchanged. The chiral condensates and transition temperatures are computed as functions of temperature for various values of \(q\) (1.05, 1.10, 1.15) and magnetic field strengths up to \(40\,m_\pi^2\), with the transition temperature determined from the inflection point of the average light-quark condensate.

## Key numbers

* At zero magnetic field, the chiral transition temperature \(T_{\rm CO}\) decreases from 173 MeV for \(q=1\) to 156 MeV for \(q=1.15\), a reduction of roughly 10%.
* For constant coupling at \(eB=10\,m_\pi^2\), \(T_{\rm CO}\) decreases from 182 MeV for \(q=1\) to 167 MeV for \(q=1.15\).
* For constant coupling with \(q=1.10\), \(T_{\rm CO}\) increases with magnetic field from 162 MeV at \(eB=0\) to 231 MeV at \(eB=40\,m_\pi^2\), showing magnetic catalysis with reduced strength compared to the extensive case.
* For the \(eB\)-dependent coupling in the extensive case (\(q\rightarrow1\)), the condensate average exhibits inverse magnetic catalysis; for \(q=1.10\) the IMC effect is eliminated except at the highest fields, while for \(q=1.05\) IMC reappears starting at \(eB=30\,m_\pi^2\), and for \(q=1.15\) IMC completely disappears.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the NJL model in a magnetic field, which established magnetic catalysis for constant coupling (Gusynin 1995, 1997) and inverse magnetic catalysis for \(eB\)-dependent coupling (Ferreira 2014, Farias 2014, 2016), by introducing Tsallis nonextensive statistics. It fills a gap identified by the author: existing effective-model studies of magnetised QCD media (e.g., Farias 2014, Tawfik 2016, 2017, 2021) used only extensive Boltzmann–Gibbs statistics, while nonextensive studies (Rozynek 2009, 2015; Zhao 2020, 2023) did not include a magnetic field. The results enable future investigations of magnetised QCD media with strong intrinsic fluctuations and long-range correlations, potentially relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments.
