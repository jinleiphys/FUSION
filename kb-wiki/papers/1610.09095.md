# Universal dimer-dimer scattering in lattice effective field theory

> We consider two-component fermions with short-range interactions and large scattering length. This system has universal properties that are realized in several different fields of physics. In the limit of large fermion-fermion scattering length $a_\mathrm{ff}$ and zero-range interaction, all properties of the system scale proportionally with $a_\mathrm{ff}$. For the case with shallow bound dimers, we calculate the dimer-dimer scattering phase shifts using lattice effective field theory. We extract the universal dimer-dimer scattering length $a_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff}=0.618(30)$ and effective range $r_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff}=-0.431(48)$. This result for the effective range is the first calculation with quantified and controlled systematic errors. We also benchmark our methods by computing the fermion-dimer scattering parameters and testing some predictions of conformal scaling of irrelevant operators near the unitarity limit.

## Key claim

The authors compute the universal dimer-dimer scattering length and effective range using lattice effective field theory, claiming the effective range result is the first with quantified and controlled systematic errors. The abstract's claim of "universal" properties is conditioned on the zero-range limit and large fermion-fermion scattering length, where all properties scale proportionally with \(a_\mathrm{ff}\).

## Method

The authors used lattice effective field theory with an \(\mathcal{O}(a^{4})\)-improved lattice action for two-component fermions, tuning the two-particle interaction to produce desired dimer binding energies. They computed finite-volume energies for three-particle (fermion-dimer) and four-particle (dimer-dimer) systems using the Lanczos eigenvector method, then applied Luscher's finite-volume method to extract scattering phase shifts. Continuum-limit extrapolations were performed using polynomial fits in \(a/a_\mathrm{ff}\), including conformal scaling predictions for irrelevant three- and four-particle operators.

## Key numbers

* Fermion-dimer scattering length: \(a_\mathrm{fd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = 1.176(6)\), in agreement with semi-analytic continuum calculations \(a^{cont.}_\mathrm{fd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = 1.1791(2)\) from Bedaque et al. (1997, 1998, 1999, 2002)
* Fermion-dimer effective range: \(r_\mathrm{fd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = -0.029(16)\), compared to semi-analytic value \(r^{cont.}_\mathrm{fd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = -0.0383(3)\)
* Dimer-dimer scattering length: \(a_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = 0.618(30)\), consistent with previous results \(0.60\pm0.01\) from Petrov et al. (2004, 2005) and \(0.605\pm0.005\) from Greene et al. (2009)
* Dimer-dimer effective range: \(r_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff} = -0.431(48)\), differing from earlier estimates \(r_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff} \approx 0.12\) from von Stecher et al. (2007) and \(r_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff} \sim 2.6\) from Lee et al. (2007)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of universal dimer-dimer scattering, which had established the scattering length \(a_\mathrm{dd}/a_\mathrm{ff}\) with high precision (Petrov 2004, 2005; Greene 2009; Aurel 2004) but left the effective range poorly constrained with unquantified errors. The results enable computation of the energy density of a dilute dimer gas, as described by Zwerger (2011) and Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957), and provide a benchmark for lattice methods that can be applied to nuclear systems where neutron-neutron scattering length \(|a_{nn}|\sim 19\) fm is much larger than the inverse pion mass.
