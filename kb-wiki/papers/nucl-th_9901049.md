# The O(N) Model at Finite Temperature: Renormalization of the Gap Equations in Hartree and Large-N Approximation

> The temperature dependence of the sigma meson and pion masses is studied in the framework of the O(N) model. The Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism is applied to derive gap equations for the masses in the Hartree and large-N approximations. Renormalization of the gap equations is carried out within the cut-off and counter-term renormalization schemes. A consistent renormalization of the gap equations within the cut-off scheme is found to be possible only in the large-N approximation and for a finite value of the cut-off. On the other hand, the counter-term scheme allows for a consistent renormalization of both the large-N and Hartree approximations. In these approximations, the meson masses at a given nonzero temperature depend in general on the choice of the cut-off or renormalization scale. As an application, we also discuss the in-medium on-shell decay widths for sigma mesons and pions at rest.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that consistent renormalization of the gap equations in the O(N) model at finite temperature is possible in the cut-off scheme only for the large-N approximation and with a finite cut-off, while the counter-term scheme works for both the Hartree and large-N approximations; however, in all cases the meson masses at nonzero temperature depend on the choice of cut-off or renormalization scale, a consequence of the fact that self-consistent resummation schemes can require temperature-dependent renormalization constants.

## Method

The authors apply the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to derive the effective potential for the O(N) model in the Hartree approximation, which resums only two-loop tadpole diagrams with dressed propagators. The stationarity conditions yield gap equations for the sigma meson and pion masses, which are then renormalized using both a three-dimensional momentum cut-off scheme and a counter-term renormalization scheme. The large-N limit is obtained by neglecting all contributions of order 1/N from the Hartree results.

## Key numbers

- For numerical calculations, N=4 is used to make contact with QCD, with tree-level vacuum masses set to m_sigma = 600 MeV and m_pi = 139 MeV, and pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV
- The coupling constant is given by lambda = N*(m_sigma^2 - m_pi^2)/(8*f_pi^2) at tree level
- In the chiral limit (H=0, m_pi=0) within the Hartree approximation, the cut-off scheme requires Lambda = 0 to satisfy the vacuum gap equations, making consistent renormalization impossible
- In the large-N approximation with the cut-off scheme, the mass parameter is determined by m^2 = -(m_sigma^2 - 3*m_pi^2)/2 - 4*lambda*Q_Lambda(m_pi), where Q_Lambda is the cut-off regularized integral

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the O(N) model using the CJT formalism by Amelino-Camelia (1997), who addressed renormalization in the cut-off scheme but did not present numerical solutions, and Petropoulos (1998), who solved the gap equations numerically without treating renormalization. The paper completes these investigations by discussing both renormalization schemes and presenting numerical solutions, while also addressing the well-known fact from Stevenson (1985) that the O(N) model becomes trivial in the limit Lambda -> infinity. The results enable further study of in-medium decay widths for sigma mesons and pions at finite temperature, which the authors compute to one-loop order for on-shell mesons at rest.
