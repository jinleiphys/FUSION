# Nonequilibrium evolution of quarkonium in medium

> We review recent progress in open quantum system approach to the description of quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma. A particular emphasis is put on the Lindblad equations for quarkonium and its numerical simulations.

# Nonequilibrium evolution of quarkonium in medium

## Key claim

The open quantum system approach, specifically Lindblad equations derived from NRQCD or pNRQCD, can describe the nonequilibrium evolution of quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma, but the applicability of the quantum Brownian motion regime requires the hierarchy $\tau_S \gg \tau_E$, which for bottomonium is marginal without the factor $2\pi$ in the environmental correlation time estimate. The paper emphasizes that the dissipative term $i\dot V_S/4T$ in the Lindblad operator is essential for equilibration, and without it the system never reaches proper equilibrium.

## Method

The authors derive Lindblad master equations for quarkonium in a static, homogeneous QGP using two effective field theory frameworks: NRQCD (Non-Relativistic QCD) and pNRQCD (potential Non-Relativistic QCD). They then perform numerical simulations of the NRQCD Lindblad equation in one spatial dimension with SU(3) color using the Quantum State Diffusion (QSD) stochastic unravelling method, modeling the potentials $V(r)$ and $D(r)$ with phenomenological functions. The simulation uses a lattice with $\Delta x = 1/M$, $\Delta t = 0.1M(\Delta x)^2$, $N_x = 254$, and QGP temperature $T = 0.1M$, evolving the wave function stochastically to reconstruct the reduced density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Bottomonium ground state formation time $\tau_{\rm form.} \sim (1\text{-}2) \ {\rm fm}$ for $C_F\alpha_s \sim 0.3\text{-}0.4$ and $M \sim 4.8 \ {\rm GeV}$
- Environmental correlation time $\tau_E \sim 1/(2\pi T) \sim 0.16 \ {\rm fm} / [T/(0.2 \ {\rm GeV})]$ for strongly coupled QGP with $T/T_c \sim \mathcal O(1)$
- Medium thermalization time $\tau_{\rm therm.} \sim \mathcal O(0.4\text{-}1) \ {\rm fm}$ from hydrodynamic simulations at RHIC and LHC
- Lattice simulations give $\gamma \sim -(0.7\text{-}3.8)T^3$ and $\kappa \sim (0.24\text{-}4.2)T^3$ for the pNRQCD coefficients
- Simulation parameters: $T = 0.1M$, $\Delta x = 1/M$, $\Delta t = 0.1M(\Delta x)^2$, $N_x = 254$
- Single heavy quark relaxation time $\tau_{\rm relax} = 100\pi/M$ from the model with $\eta = T^2/(\pi M)$
- Fitted equilibrium temperature from simulation: $T_{\rm fit} = 0.101M \simeq T$

## Context

This work reviews and extends the open quantum system approach to quarkonium in QGP, building on the Lindblad equation derivations by Akamatsu (2014) and Brambilla (2016, 2017). It contrasts with the classical kinetic theory approach by Yao (2017-2020) which applies when $\tau_S \lesssim \tau_E$. The numerical simulations demonstrate equilibration to a Boltzmann distribution at the medium temperature, confirming the fluctuation-dissipation relation requirement. The results enable future work on checking the dipole approximation applicability, matching to classical kinetic theory at lower temperatures near $T_c$, and elaborating initial conditions for quarkonium under thermalizing fields.
