# Dynamical coupled-channels model study of pion photoproduction

> The photoproduction of pion off nucleon is investigated within a dynamical coupled-channels approach based on the Juelich pi-N model, which has been quite successful in the description of pi-N to pi-N scattering for center-of-mass energies up to 1.9 GeV. The full pion photoproduction amplitude is constructed to satisfy the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity and hence, it is fully gauge invariant. The calculated differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy for the reactions gamma p to pi^+ n, gamma p to pi^0 p and gamma n to pi^- p are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The dynamical coupled-channels model for pion photoproduction, built on the Juelich pi-N model, achieves good agreement with experimental differential cross sections and photon spin asymmetries up to 1.65 GeV center-of-mass energy, but only for the three reactions gamma p to pi+ n, gamma p to pi0 p, and gamma n to pi- p; discrepancies at higher energies may arise from missing high-spin resonances or Lambda K and Sigma K channels not included in the present work.

## Method

The authors constructed a full pion photoproduction amplitude that satisfies the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity, ensuring gauge invariance, within a dynamical coupled-channels framework. The hadronic part is based on the Juelich pi-N model, which includes pi N and eta N stable channels plus pi Delta, sigma N, and rho N effective channels representing the resonant part of the pi pi N channel, and has been previously successful for pi-N to pi-N scattering up to 1.9 GeV. The amplitude includes s-channel diagrams with eight genuine resonances (S11(1535), S11(1650), S31(1620), P31(1910), P13(1720), D13(1520), P33(1232), D33(1700)), u-channel N and Delta exchanges, t-channel pi, rho, omega, and a1 exchanges, and an interaction current with a generalized contact term chosen to enforce gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The model is applied for center-of-mass energies up to 1.65 GeV for pion photoproduction reactions.
- The underlying Juelich pi-N model describes pi-N to pi-N scattering for center-of-mass energies up to 1.9 GeV.
- Eight genuine resonances are included in the s-channel: S11(1535), S11(1650), S31(1620), P31(1910), P13(1720), D13(1520), P33(1232), and D33(1700).
- The multipole amplitudes M1+ and E2- for isospin I=3/2 are extracted and compared to SAID analysis; agreement is good for the dominant M1+ amplitude but shows considerable disagreement for the smaller E2- amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the Juelich pi-N model, previously successful for pi-N scattering (Gasparyan 2003), to pion photoproduction by enforcing full gauge invariance via the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity (Haberzettl 1997, 2006, 2011), unlike most existing models that satisfy only current conservation. The results enable extraction of multipole amplitudes for pion photoproduction, though the authors note that more independent observables are needed to constrain the model further, as the extracted E2- amplitude shows considerable disagreement with the SAID analysis (Arndt 2002) despite good reproduction of cross sections and beam asymmetries.
