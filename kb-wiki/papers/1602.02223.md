# Electromagnetic fields with electric and chiral magnetic conductivities in heavy ion collisions

> We derive analytic formula for electric and magnetic fields produced by a moving charged particle in a conducting medium with the electric conductivity $σ$ and the chiral magnetic conductivity $σ_χ$. We use the Green function method and assume that $σ_χ$ is much smaller than $σ$. The compact algebraic expressions for electric and magnetic fields without any integrals are obtained. They recover the Lienard-Wiechert formula at vanishing conductivities. Exact numerical solutions are also found for any values of $σ$ and $σ_χ$ and are compared to analytic results. Both numerical and analytic results agree very well for the scale of high energy heavy ion collisions. The space-time profiles of electromagnetic fields in non-central Au+Au collisions have been calculated based on these analytic formula as well as exact numerical solutions.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic formulas for electric and magnetic fields produced by a moving charged particle in a conducting medium with both electric conductivity σ and chiral magnetic conductivity σχ, under the condition that σχ is much smaller than σ, without taking the relativistic limit v=1.

## Method

The authors solve Maxwell equations with Ohm currents (σE) and chiral magnetic currents (σχB) using the Green function method in momentum space. They treat σχ as a perturbation to obtain analytic expressions for the fields, and also compute exact numerical solutions for any values of σ and σχ. The analytic results are then applied to calculate space-time profiles of electromagnetic fields in non-central Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV using the UrQMD model for the particle configuration.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at RHIC at √s=200 GeV, the peak magnetic field at collision time is about 5mπ² or 1.4×10¹⁸ Gauss.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at √s=2.76 TeV, the peak magnetic field can be 10 times as large as at RHIC.
- At √s=200 GeV, the magnetic field drops by two to three orders of magnitude in about 0.5 fm/c from collision time when medium effects are not considered.
- The analytic calculations use σ=5.8 MeV and σχ=1.5 MeV for the point charge example.
- At very late time, the fields decay as Bφ,Br ∼ 1/t² and Bz ∼ 1/t.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007), Skokov (2009), Voronyuk (2011), and Deng (2012) that did not include medium effects, and by Tuchin (2013) and McLerran (2013) that included σ but not σχ. It also generalizes the analytic work of Tuchin (2014) which considered only the relativistic limit v=1. The results enable more realistic calculations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect and related phenomena in heavy ion collisions by providing analytic field expressions that include both electric and chiral magnetic conductivities.
