# Nuclear Effects in the Deuteron and Global PDF Fits

> We present a detailed study of nuclear corrections in the deuteron (D) from an analysis of data from charged-lepton deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off proton and D, as well as from dimuon pair production in pp and pD collisions and $W^\pm$ and the $Z$ boson production at pp (p$\rm \bar p$) colliders. In particular, we discuss the determination of the off-shell function describing the modification of parton distributions (PDF) in bound nucleons in the context of global PDF fits. Our results are consistent with the ones obtained earlier from the study of the ratios of DIS structure functions $F_2^A/F_2^D$ in nuclei with $A\geq4$, confirming the universality of the off-shell function. We also discuss the sensitivity to various models of the deuteron wave function and the impact of nuclear corrections on the determination of the $d$ quark distribution.

# Nuclear Effects in the Deuteron and Global PDF Fits

## Key claim
Nuclear corrections in the deuteron are non-negligible and rise rapidly at large Bjorken x, but the off-shell function describing the modification of parton distributions in bound nucleons can be extracted simultaneously with PDFs and higher-twist terms from global fits, and is consistent with the universal off-shell function obtained from heavier nuclei (A ≥ 4), supporting a unified treatment of the deuteron and heavier nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed a global NNLO QCD analysis of charged-lepton deep-inelastic scattering data off proton and deuterium targets, combined with dimuon pair production in pp and pD collisions and W± and Z boson production at pp and p-pbar colliders. The off-shell function δf(x) describing the modification of bound nucleon structure functions was parameterized with generic second and third order polynomials and extracted simultaneously with proton PDFs and higher-twist terms, using several different deuteron wave function models (Paris, Bonn, AV18, WJC-1, WJC-2) as input.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear corrections in the deuteron are a few percent negative at x ~ 0.5-0.6 with a steep rise at large x, as confirmed by a direct measurement (Griffioen 2015).
- The starting scale for PDF parametrization is μ² = Q²₀ = 9 GeV².
- The average virtuality in the deuteron is v-bar = -0.045, corresponding to an increase of the nucleon core radius of about 2% within a simple single-scale model.
- LHC data from CMS and LHCb reach values of x ~ 0.8 due to wide rapidity coverage.

## Context
This work extends the model of Kulagin and Petti (2004, 2007, 2010, 2014) which provided a unified treatment of nuclear corrections for nuclei from ³He to ²⁰⁸Pb, describing DIS structure function ratios F₂ᴬ/F₂ᴰ, Drell-Yan data, and W/Z boson production in pPb collisions at the LHC. The results differ from those reported by Accardi (2011, 2016) using a similar formalism, with differences attributed to the treatment of off-shell effects on target-mass corrections and higher-twist terms. The validated universality of the off-shell function enables more precise determination of the d quark distribution at large x from deuteron DIS data, using the higher precision available from heavier nuclear targets (A ≥ 4) to fix the off-shell function in global PDF fits.
