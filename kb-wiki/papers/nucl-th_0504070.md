# The Shell Model, the Renormalization Group and the Two-Body Interaction

> The no-core shell model and the effective interaction $V_{{\rm low} k}$ can both be derived using the Lee-Suzuki projection operator formalism. The main difference between the two is the choice of basis states that define the model space. The effective interaction $V_{{\rm low} k}$ can also be derived using the renormalization group. That renormalization group derivation can be extended in a straight forward manner to also include the no-core shell model. In the nuclear matter limit the no-core shell model effective interaction in the two-body approximation reduces identically to $V_{{\rm low} k}$. The same considerations apply to the Bloch-Horowitz version of the shell model and the renormalization group treatment of two-body scattering by Birse, McGovern and Richardson.

## Key claim
The no-core shell model and the effective interaction \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\) are formally equivalent when both are derived via the Lee-Suzuki projection operator formalism; the only significant difference is the choice of basis (harmonic oscillator versus plane wave) defining the model space, and in the nuclear matter limit the two-body approximation of the no-core shell model reduces identically to \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\).

## Method
The paper shows that both the no-core shell model and \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\) can be derived from the Lee-Suzuki projection operator formalism, with the projection operator defined by harmonic oscillator states for the shell model and by plane wave states for \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\). It recasts the discrete-state shell-model problem into a form resembling the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, then applies the renormalization group flow equations (following the approach of Schwenk for \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\) and Birse et al. for the Bloch-Horowitz version) with sums replacing integrals. The nuclear matter limit is taken by letting \(\hbar\Omega \to 0\) and \(N \to \infty\) while keeping the cutoff energy fixed, showing that harmonic oscillator wave functions asymptotically become plane waves.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff energy in one dimension is given by \((N+1/2) \hbar\Omega = E_{\rm cutoff}\), where \(N\) is the highest energy state in the model space and \(\hbar\Omega\) is the oscillator parameter.
- The asymptotic form of the Hermite polynomial for large \(n\) is \(H_{2n}(x) = \frac{(-1)^n 2^{2n}}{\sqrt{\pi}} e^{x^2/2} \Gamma(n+1/2) \cos(\sqrt{4n+1}x)\), with the argument reducing to \(r p / \hbar\) after inserting the oscillator length parameter and expressing \(n\hbar\Omega\) in terms of energy.
- The coefficient of the cosine cancels the oscillator normalization to within a factor of \(\sqrt{\pi}/2\) when Stirling's formula is used for the gamma functions.

## Context
This work unifies two previously parallel developments: the no-core shell model (as used by Haxton, Navratil, Barrett, and Zhan) and the renormalization-group-derived \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\) interaction (developed by Schwenk and others). It extends the renormalization group derivation of \(V_{{\rm low}\ k}\) to the no-core shell model and shows that the Bloch-Horowitz version of the shell model is similarly related to the two-body scattering treatment of Birse, McGovern, and Richardson. The paper enables a unified perspective where nuclear matter studies (e.g., Furnstahl) and finite-nucleus shell-model calculations (e.g., Haxton) are seen as addressing the same physics, and it suggests that model spaces should be matched to the energy and momentum scales of the problem rather than arbitrarily enlarged.
