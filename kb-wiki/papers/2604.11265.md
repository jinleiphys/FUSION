# $Δl =1$ coupling of single-particle orbitals in octupole deformed nuclei

> Conventionally, octupole deformation in nuclei has been attributed to strong $Δl=3$ couplings between opposite-parity single-particle orbitals. In this work, we demonstrate that the often-overlooked $Δl=1$ mode also plays an important role. Taking orbitals near the octupole magic number $N = 134$ as a benchmark, we systematically evaluate the $Δl = 1$ and $Δl = 3$ mixing ratios of the wave functions within the Nilsson model, interpreting the trends through matrix elements of the deformed potential. We introduce component-resolved single-particle octupole energy contributions, based on the Hellmann--Feynman relation, to quantify the contributions of each $(Δl,Δj)$ coupling. Furthermore, the impact of $Δl = 1$ coupling on the rotational structure is demonstrated via particle-rotor model calculations for $^{221}$Ra and $^{223}$Th. Our work suggests that $Δl=1$ and $Δl=3$ octupole couplings act synergistically in driving reflection asymmetry, necessitating a revised paradigm for understanding octupole correlation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the often-overlooked $\Delta l=1$ coupling mode contributes comparably to, and in some cases exceeds, the traditionally emphasized $\Delta l=3$ mode in driving octupole deformation in nuclei, specifically for orbitals near the octupole magic number $N=134$; the authors state that the two modes "act synergistically" and that this necessitates a "revised paradigm," but the claim is hedged to the specific benchmark region and deformation ranges studied ($\beta_2=0$ and $0.15$, $\beta_3=0$ to $0.15$).

## Method

The authors employed a reflection-asymmetric Nilsson model to diagonalize the single-particle Hamiltonian and compute wave function components for neutron orbitals near $N=134$, defining a channel-resolved mixing ratio $M_{\Delta l,\Delta j}^k$ to quantify the relative contribution of each $(\Delta l,\Delta j)$ coupling channel. They introduced a component-resolved single-particle octupole energy contribution $G_{\Delta l,\Delta j}^k$ based on the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to measure energy lowering from each coupling mode. Particle-rotor model calculations were performed for $^{221}$Ra and $^{223}$Th to demonstrate the impact of $\Delta l=1$ coupling on rotational structure, using deformation parameters $\beta_2=0.15$ and $\beta_3=0.10$.

## Key numbers

- Level-averaged total octupole-induced mixing reaches about 30% for both $\beta_2=0$ and $\beta_2=0.15$ at $\beta_3=0.1$, indicating substantial parity-mixing strength relatively insensitive to quadrupole shape.
- For the $\Omega=1/2$ subspace near $N=134$, the traditional $\Delta l=3$ pair $6g_{9/2}\leftrightarrow 7j_{15/2}$ has an energy difference of $0.33\,\hbar\omega_0$, while the $\Delta l=1$ pair $6i_{11/2}\leftrightarrow 7j_{15/2}$ has $0.11\,\hbar\omega_0$.
- The octupole matrix element $\bra{6i_{13/2}} H_{\text{oct}} \ket{7j_{15/2}} = -0.11$ (in units of $\hbar\omega_0$) is comparable to $\bra{6g_{9/2}} H_{\text{oct}} \ket{7j_{15/2}} = -0.15$.
- For $^{221}$Ra, the core parity splitting parameter $E(0^{-})=0.15$ MeV and spin-dependent moment of inertia parameters $(a,b)=(30,2.8)$; for $^{223}$Th, $E(0^{-})=0.207$ MeV and $(a,b)=(35,2.35)$.
- The intrinsic dipole moment was taken as $Q_{10}=0.2\,e\mathrm{fm}$ and the intrinsic quadrupole moment as $Q_{20}=170\,e\mathrm{fm}^2$ for both $^{221}$Ra and $^{223}$Th.

## Context

This work extends the conventional understanding of octupole deformation, which has been attributed primarily to $\Delta l=3$ couplings between opposite-parity orbitals such as $j_{15/2}\leftrightarrow g_{9/2}$ near $N=134$, as established in studies by Butler (1996, 2016, 2020) and Nazarewicz (1994). The paper directly challenges the traditional view by showing that $\Delta l=1$ couplings, particularly the $(\Delta l,\Delta j)=(1,1)$ channel, provide comparable or dominant contributions to parity mixing and energy lowering. This finding enables future theoretical work to incorporate both $\Delta l=1$ and $\Delta l=3$ modes in microscopic descriptions of octupole collectivity, and suggests that experimental studies of octupole-deformed nuclei in the actinide region should reconsider the interpretation of parity-doublet structures and electromagnetic transitions.
