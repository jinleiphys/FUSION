# One pion exchange and the quantum numbers of the Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) pentaquarks

> The LHCb collaboration has recently discovered three pentaquark-like states --- the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ --- close to the $\bar{D} Σ_c$ and the $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ meson-baryon thresholds. The standard interpretation is that they are heavy antimeson-baryon molecules. Their quantum numbers have not been determined yet, which implies two possibilities for the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$: $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$ and $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^{-}$. The preferred interpretation within a contact-range effective field theory is that the $P_c(4440)$ is the $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^-$ molecule, while the $P_c(4457)$ is the $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$ one. Here we show that when the one pion exchange potential between the heavy-antimeson and heavy-baryon is taken into account, this conclusion changes, with the contrary identification being as likely as the original one. The identification is however cutoff dependent, which suggests that improvements of the present description (e.g. the inclusion of subleading order corrections, like two-pion exchanges) are necessary in order to disambiguate the spectroscopy of the molecular pentaquarks.

## Key claim

When one pion exchange (OPE) is included in the effective field theory description of the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ molecular pentaquarks, the preferred quantum number identification for the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ becomes cutoff-dependent and ambiguous, with the opposite assignment (the $P_c(4440)$ as $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$ and the $P_c(4457)$ as $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^-$) being as likely as the original one from contact-range EFT.

## Method

The author constructs a pionful effective field theory (EFT) for the heavy antimeson-baryon system, incorporating both contact-range interactions and one pion exchange (OPE) potentials derived from heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) using light-quark notation. The OPE potential is projected onto partial waves for the $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ and $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ systems, and bound state equations are solved in both momentum space (with a Gaussian regulator and cutoff $\Lambda = 0.75-1.5$ GeV) and coordinate space (with a local regulator and cutoff $R_c = 0.5-1.0$ fm) to determine the binding energies and masses of the molecular pentaquark candidates.

## Key numbers

- The $P_c(4312)$ mass predicted in scenario A (where $P_c(4440)$ is $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^-$ and $P_c(4457)$ is $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$) in momentum space: $M_1^A = 4314-(4319)^V$ MeV (the superscript V indicates a virtual state at $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV)
- The $P_c(4312)$ mass predicted in scenario B (where $P_c(4440)$ is $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$ and $P_c(4457)$ is $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^-$) in momentum space: $M_1^B = 4308-4321$ MeV
- The axial couplings used: $g_1 = 0.60$ (from $D^* \to D \pi$ decays) and $g_2 = 0.84$ (from lattice QCD, with uncertainty $\pm 0.20$)
- The pion decay constant: $f_{\pi} = 132$ MeV
- The cutoff window in momentum space: $\Lambda = 0.75-1.5$ GeV
- The cutoff window in coordinate space: $R_c = 0.5-1.0$ fm
- In scenario B (r-space), the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ molecule with $J^P = \tfrac{5}{2}^-$ has binding energy $28-33$ MeV and mass $4494-4499$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the contact-range EFT analysis of the LHCb pentaquark trio by Liu et al. (2019), which preferred the identification of $P_c(4440)$ as $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^-$ and $P_c(4457)$ as $J^P = \tfrac{3}{2}^-$, by including one pion exchange contributions. The results are consistent with the recent work of Yamaguchi et al. (2019), which also considered pion exchange effects. The paper shows that leading-order pionful EFT cannot unambiguously determine the quantum numbers of these pentaquarks due to cutoff dependence and uncertainties in the axial coupling $g_2$, and that subleading order corrections (such as two-pion exchanges) are necessary to resolve the ambiguity. The analysis also predicts the existence of four additional molecular pentaquarks in the $\bar{D} \Sigma_c^*$ and $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ systems.
