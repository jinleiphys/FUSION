# Exact Multi-Instanton Solutions to Selfdual Yang-Mills Equation on Curved Spaces

> We find exact multi-instanton solutions to the selfdual Yang-Mills equation on a large class of curved spaces with $SO(3)$ isometry, generalizing the results previously found on $\mathbb{R}^4$. The solutions are featured with explicit multi-centered expressions and topological properties. As examples, we demonstrate the approach on several different curved spaces, including the Einstein static universe and $\mathbb{R} \times$ dS$_3^E$, and show that the exact multi-instanton solutions exist on these curved backgrounds.

## Key claim

The paper constructs exact multi-instanton solutions to the selfdual Yang-Mills equation on any curved space with metric of the form \(ds^2 = dt^2 + h(r) dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega_{S^2}^2\) (i.e., with \(SO(3)\) isometry), by showing that after a coordinate redefinition and field redefinition the equations become formally identical to those on flat \(\mathbb{R}^4\) studied by Witten (1977). The solutions exist provided the action remains finite, which imposes a condition on the metric factor \(h(r)\).

## Method

The authors start from a general metric with \(SO(3)\) isometry and adopt an Ansatz for the SU(2) gauge field in terms of scalar functions \(\varphi_1, \varphi_2, A_0, A_1\). They rewrite the selfdual Yang-Mills equation into a system of partial differential equations, then define a new radial coordinate \(\widetilde{r}\) and rescaled fields \((\widetilde{\varphi}_i, \widetilde{A}_i)\) so that the equations take the same form as on \(\mathbb{R}^4\). Using a gauge-fixing condition and introducing a holomorphic function \(g(z)\) (where \(z = \widetilde{r} + i t\)), they reduce the problem to solving the Liouville equation \(\nabla^2 \rho = e^{2\rho}\). The general multi-instanton solution is then given by \(g = \prod_{i=1}^k (a_i - z)/(a_i^* + z)\) for complex constants \(a_i\), with the instanton number \(n = k-1\).

## Key numbers

* The topological charge (instanton number) is \(n = k - 1\), where \(k\) is the number of factors in the product defining the function \(g\).
* For the Einstein static universe, the metric factor is \(h(r) = 1/(1 - r^2)\) with \(r \in [0, 1]\), and the new radial coordinate is \(\widetilde{r} = r / \sqrt{1 - r^2}\).
* For \(\mathbb{R} \times \text{dS}_3^E\) with parameter \(b\), the metric factor is \(h(r) = (1 - r^2/b^2)^{-1}\) with \(r \in [0, b]\), and the new radial coordinate is \(\widetilde{r} = b r / \sqrt{b^2 - r^2}\).
* A numerical example of a 4-instanton solution is shown for both curved backgrounds, with \(F^2\) remaining finite at the instanton locations.

## Context

This work generalizes the exact multi-instanton solutions on \(\mathbb{R}^4\) found by Witten (1977) to a large class of curved spaces with \(SO(3)\) isometry. It extends previous systematic analyses of spherically symmetric instanton solutions on curved spaces by Nian (2019) and earlier case-by-case constructions such as \(\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{H}^3\) by Harland (2007) and Euclidean AdS\(_4\) black hole by Sarioglu (2009). The results provide the saddle-point configurations needed for supersymmetric localization on curved backgrounds like the Einstein static universe and \(\mathbb{R} \times \text{dS}_3^E\), which are important for instanton counting and vortex counting in supersymmetric gauge theories following Pestun (2012) and related work.
