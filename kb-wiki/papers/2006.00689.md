# Muon capture rates: Evaluation within the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation

> The Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) is used in evaluation of the total muon capture ratesfor the final nuclei participating in double-beta decay. Several variants of the method are used, depending on the size of the single particle model space used, or treatment of the initial bound muon wave function. The resulting capture rates are all reasonably close to each other. In particular, the variant that appears to be most realistic, results in rates in good agreement with the experimental values. There is no necessity for an empirical quenching of the axial current coupling constant $g_A$. Its standard value $g_A$ = 1.27 seems to be adequate.

## Key claim
The Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) yields total muon capture rates for double-beta decay candidate nuclei that agree with experimental values without requiring an empirical quenching of the axial current coupling constant \(g_A\); the standard value \(g_A = 1.27\) appears adequate, but this conclusion depends on the specific variant of QRPA used, with the most realistic variant showing good agreement.

## Method
The authors evaluated total muon capture rates for nuclei participating in double-beta decay using several variants of the QRPA, differing in the size of the single-particle model space (small vs. large) and in the treatment of the bound muon wave function (with or without factorization from nuclear matrix elements). The nuclear current was reduced to nonrelativistic form including terms up to \(1/m_p\), and the bound muon wave function was obtained by solving the Dirac equation with a Fermi-type nuclear charge distribution. The residual nuclear interaction was based on Brueckner G-matrix elements from the Argonne V18 potential, with coupling constants adjusted to restore SU(4) symmetry for the corresponding double-beta decay transitions.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{76}\)Se, the muon capture rate depends on the particle-hole coupling \(g_{ph}\) and particle-particle couplings \(g_{pp}^{T=1}\) and \(g_{pp}^{T=0}\); over the realistic range of these parameters the capture rate changes by less than 10%, except for a singularity when \(g_{pp}^{T=0} > 1\).
- The effective charge \(Z_{\rm eff}\) for \(^{48}\)Ti is 17.654 (present work) compared to 17.38 from Suzuki (2018); for \(^{76}\)Se it is 23.405 vs. 23.24; for \(^{96}\)Mo it is 26.328 vs. 26.37; for \(^{110}\)Cd it is 28.069 vs. 28.20; for \(^{116}\)Sn it is 28.621 vs. 28.64; for \(^{124}\)Te it is 29.017 vs. 29.03; for \(^{134}\)Ba it is 29.922 vs. 29.99; for \(^{150}\)Sm it is 30.978 vs. 31.01.
- The muon binding energy \(\varepsilon_b\) for \(^{48}\)Ti is 1.268 MeV; for \(^{76}\)Se it is 2.760 MeV; for \(^{96}\)Mo it is 3.939 MeV; for \(^{116}\)Sn it is 5.203 MeV; for \(^{136}\)Ba it is 6.167 MeV; for \(^{150}\)Sm it is 7.140 MeV.
- The coefficients \(C_F\), \(C_{GT}\), and \(C_T\) for the present approach at neutrino energy \(E_\nu = 85\) MeV and \(g_A^{\rm eff} = 1.27\) are 0.965, 0.844, and -0.159, respectively; for the Fujii-Primakoff approach they are 1.052, 1.048, and -0.282.

## Context
This work extends previous QRPA calculations of muon capture rates for double-beta decay candidate nuclei, which had reached conflicting conclusions: some studies (Zinoviev 2006, Kolbe 2000, Marketin 2009) found none or only mild quenching of \(g_A\), while Jokiniemi (2019) required strong quenching (\(g_A^{\rm eff}/g_A \sim 0.5\)). The present results support the former conclusion and are closely related to the formalism used for evaluating \(0\nu\beta\beta\) nuclear matrix elements by Simkovic (2008). The findings enable further testing of nuclear models for neutrinoless double-beta decay, where muon capture serves as an analogous probe with similar momentum transfer and unrestricted selection rules.
