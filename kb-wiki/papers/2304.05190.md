# Thermodynamics of a rotating hadron resonance gas with van der Waals interaction

> Studying the thermodynamics of the systems produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is crucial in understanding the QCD phase diagram. Recently, a new avenue has opened regarding the implications of large initial angular momentum and subsequent vorticity in the medium evolution in high-energy collisions. This adds a new type of chemical potential into the partonic and hadronic systems, called the rotational chemical potential. We study the thermodynamics of an interacting hadronic matter under rotation, formed in an ultra-relativistic collision. We introduce attractive and repulsive interactions through the van der Waals equation of state. Thermodynamic properties like the pressure ($P$), energy density ($\varepsilon$), entropy density ($s$), trace anomaly ($(\varepsilon - 3P)/T^{4}$), specific heat ($c_{\rm v}$) and squared speed of sound ($c_{\rm s}^{2}$) are studied as functions of temperature ($T$) for zero and finite rotation chemical potential. The conserved charge fluctuations, which can be quantified by their respective susceptibilities, are also studied. The rotational (spin) density corresponding to the rotational chemical potential is explored. In addition, we explore the possible liquid-gas phase transition in the hadron gas with van der Waals interaction in the $T$ -- $ω$ phase space.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing a finite rotational chemical potential (angular velocity ω) into a van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model enhances thermodynamic quantities such as pressure, energy density, and entropy density, and can induce a liquid-gas phase transition in the T–ω phase space, but only under the condition that the ratio ω/T remains very small (ℏω/k_BT ≪ 1) to satisfy the rigid rotation and causality constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a rotating hadron resonance gas by incorporating a rotational chemical potential ω into the grand canonical ensemble, using a density operator that includes angular momentum. They introduce attractive and repulsive interactions among hadrons via the van der Waals equation of state, with separate treatments for mesons (excluded volume only) and baryons/antibaryons (both attractive and repulsive interactions). Thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, entropy density, trace anomaly, specific heat, squared speed of sound) and conserved charge susceptibilities are computed as functions of temperature for zero and finite ω, using a fixed system size of R = 5 fm to respect the causality condition.

## Key numbers

- The system size is fixed at R = 5 fm throughout the work to respect the causality condition |ω × x| ≪ 1.
- The van der Waals repulsive parameter b is derived from the hardcore radius r via b = 16πr³/3.
- The ideal HRG model matches lattice QCD data up to T ≃ 150 MeV, while the VDWHRG model can explain lattice QCD data up to T ∼ 180 MeV.
- A massless ideal gas gives c_s² = 1/3, whereas the value for a hadron gas is 1/5.
- The threshold for small systems (pp, p-Pb) to mimic heavy-ion collision behavior is ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ ≃ 10–20.

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model (Vovchenko 2016) by adding rotation, motivated by the discovery of finite hyperon polarization at STAR (2017) indicating vorticity in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior studies of rotating systems using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Jiang 2016, Wang 2018) and the exploration of deconfinement in rotating hadronic matter (Fujimoto 2021). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD at finite rotation and provide a baseline for studying the QCD phase diagram under the influence of vorticity, including the search for a critical endpoint in the T–ω plane.
