# Is the existence of a softest point in the directed flow excitation function an unambiguous signal for the creation of a quark-gluon plasma?

> The excitation function of the in-plane directed flow of nucleons is studied within a non-equilibrium transport approach. It is demonstrated that a local minimum in the excitation function of the directed flow develops, which is not related to a transition into a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase. It is a consequence of the dynamical softening of the underlying equation of state, due to the onset of resonance matter and particle production. Thus, the interpretation of this minimum as a 'smoking gun' signature for the creation of a QGP is premature.

# Is the existence of a softest point in the directed flow excitation function an unambiguous signal for the creation of a quark-gluon plasma?

## Key claim
A local minimum in the excitation function of directed flow can develop from a dynamical softening of the equation of state due to the onset of resonance matter and particle production, without any transition to a quark-gluon plasma phase. Therefore, interpreting such a minimum as a "smoking gun" signature for QGP creation is premature.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD 1.2), a non-equilibrium microscopic transport approach based on covariant propagation of constituent quarks and diquarks with mesonic and baryonic degrees of freedom, to simulate central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) reactions from 100 AMeV to 500 AGeV beam energy. The model includes multiple interactions, string excitation and fragmentation, and hadronic resonance formation and decay, but does not incorporate an explicit phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma. The directed flow of nucleons was computed as the average transverse momentum in the reaction plane, and the inelasticity was defined as the fraction of total energy carried by newly produced particles.

## Key numbers
- Directed flow of nucleons shows a prominent dip at 8 AGeV beam energy for central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) reactions with impact parameter b ≤ 3.4 fm.
- Up to 1 AGeV beam energy, the directed flow of nucleons increases; after particle production sets in, it decreases and reaches a minimum at 8 AGeV.
- At 15 AGeV beam energy, the inelasticity decreases again and the directed flow increases until 40 AGeV, after which it stays constant towards the top SPS energy.
- Simulations with an energy-independent elastic and isotropic nucleon-nucleon cross section of 40 mb show a strong and monotonous increase of directed flow with energy, in contrast to the non-monotonic behavior seen with the full collision term.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing suggestion that the excitation function of in-plane collective flow could serve as a probe for phase transitions in nuclear collisions, as proposed by Hofmann (1976), Stocker (1979), and Rischke (1995). It directly contradicts the interpretation that a minimum in directed flow excitation function uniquely signals a quark-gluon plasma transition, as argued in hydrodynamic studies by Hung and others. The authors show that a string-hadronic model without QGP can reproduce the minimum, and they identify the time evolution of directed flow as a key discriminant: in their model, directed flow increases monotonically with time without the anti-flow of nucleons at central rapidities predicted for QGP scenarios by Brachmann and Csernai (1999). This enables future experimental tests at the NA49 experiment (20, 30, and 40 AGeV runs) and the future GSI accelerator to distinguish hadronic dynamics from QGP formation by measuring flow near midrapidity.
