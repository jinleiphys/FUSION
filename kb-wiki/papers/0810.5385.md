# Thermodynamical properties of QED in 1+1 dimensions within light front dynamics

> We investigate thermodynamical properties of quantum electrodynamics in 1+1 dimensions. Discrete light cone quantization is used to compute the partition function of the canonical ensemble and the thermodynamical potential. The potential is evaluated for different system sizes and coupling strengths. We perform the continuum limit and the thermodynamical limit and present basic thermodynamical quantities as a function of temperature for the interacting system. A more accurate estimation of low lying bound state masses at non-perturbative coupling strength are determined due to the higher harmonic resolution.

# Thermodynamical properties of QED in 1+1 dimensions within light front dynamics

## Key claim
The authors compute thermodynamic quantities for QED in 1+1 dimensions using discrete light cone quantization (DLCQ) and find no evidence for the second-order phase transition previously conjectured by Elser (1996). The pressure and internal energy change smoothly with coupling strength, contradicting the earlier claim of a phase transition in this system.

## Method
The authors use discrete light cone quantization (DLCQ) to compute the partition function of the canonical ensemble for QED in 1+1 dimensions. They evaluate the thermodynamical potential for different system sizes and coupling strengths, then perform the continuum limit (K → ∞) followed by the thermodynamical limit (L → ∞). The trace of the matrix exponential in the partition function is computed via eigenvalue decomposition for small harmonic resolutions K and via a random vector routine with Trotter decomposition for larger resolutions.

## Key numbers
- Free Fermi gas test case: at resolution K = 110 and largest temperature shown, the relative error is below 1.5%
- Mass spectrum computed for m/g = 1 (non-perturbative coupling regime) up to K = 35, with six lowest mass states singled out for continuum extrapolation
- For m/g ≤ 2^(-3), slight differences appear compared to other methods (Sriganesh 1999, Mo 1992, Kroger 1998) due to suboptimal fermionic Fock representation
- At high temperatures T ≥ M₀, the internal energy density reaches u/T² ≈ π/6, while the pressure has not yet reached the high-temperature limit p/T² ≈ π/6

## Context
This work extends the non-perturbative light front thermodynamic approach first attempted by Elser (1996) for QED in 1+1 dimensions, but arrives at different conclusions regarding the conjectured phase transition. The authors apply the consistent statistical operator formulation from Raufeisen (2004) and compare their mass spectrum results with finite lattice calculations by Sriganesh (1999), variational DLCQ by Mo (1992), and fast moving frame approach by Kroger (1998). The method enables future extension to four-dimensional finite density QCD as an alternative to lattice Monte Carlo approaches that suffer from the sign problem at large chemical potential.
