# Isospin-density dependent pairing from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei

> The effective isospin-density dependent pairing interaction (P1) [S. S. Zhang, U. Lombardo and E. G. Zhao, Sci. Chin. Phys. Mech. Astro. {\bf 54}, 236 (2011)] extracted from neutron pairing gaps for $^1$S$_0$ in asymmetric nuclear matter calculations [S. S. Zhang, L. G. Cao, U. Lombardo, et al. Phys. Rev. C {\bf 81}, 044313 (2010)] is employed to study the bulk properties of Ca, Ni, Zr and Sn isotopes. The odd-even mass (OEM) staggering is calculated by the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS method (SHF + BCS) with the SkP interaction. For comparison, we study two other types of isovector effective pairing interactions. One is also extracted from pairing gaps of infinite nuclear matter by the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method but for free spectrum (P2). The other is obtained by fitting the empirical OEM (P3). An isoscalar effective pairing interaction (P4) is also adopted which is determined by fitting the empirical OEM. We find that interaction P1 can better describe the OEM staggering of Ni, Zr and Sn isotopes by $14.3 \%, 41 \%, 30.4 \%$ compared with interaction P2, in terms of root mean square deviations to the empirical OEM, respectively. On the other hand, the performance of P1 and P2 is comparable for Ca isotopes. For Ca and Ni isotopes, P1 behaves similarly as P3, but for Zr isotopes P1 is better than P3 by $\sim 34 \%$. Among the four pairing interactions studied, P4 performs the worst. Therefore, one may conclude that for neutron pairings in finite nuclei, the isovector pairings are preferred than the isoscalar one. It is quite interesting to note that the pairing interaction P1 extracted from nuclear matter calculations can describe pairing gaps of finite nuclei as well as or even better than the interaction P3 directly fitted to finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The isospin-density dependent pairing interaction P1, extracted from neutron pairing gaps in asymmetric nuclear matter using the mean-field spectrum, can describe the odd-even mass staggering of finite nuclei as well as or even better than the interaction P3 that was directly fitted to experimental finite-nuclei data, but only for certain isotopic chains (Ni, Zr, and Sn) and not universally for all chains studied.

## Method

The authors employed the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS (SHF+BCS) method with the SkP interaction to calculate binding energies and odd-even mass staggering for Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes. They compared four effective pairing interactions: P1 (isospin-density dependent, extracted from asymmetric nuclear matter with mean-field spectrum), P2 (same method but with free spectrum), P3 (isovector, fitted to empirical odd-even mass staggering), and P4 (isoscalar, fitted to empirical odd-even mass staggering). The odd-even mass staggering was extracted using the empirical three-point formula, and root mean square errors relative to experimental data from AME2016 were computed.

## Key numbers

- For Ni isotopes, P1 reduces the RMSE of OEM staggering by 14.3% compared with P2 (RMSE 0.30 MeV for P1 vs 0.35 MeV for P2).
- For Zr isotopes, P1 reduces the RMSE by 41.0% compared with P2 (RMSE 0.23 MeV for P1 vs 0.39 MeV for P2) and by approximately 34.3% compared with P3 (RMSE 0.23 MeV for P1 vs 0.35 MeV for P3).
- For Sn isotopes, P1 reduces the RMSE by 30.4% compared with P2 (RMSE 0.16 MeV for P1 vs 0.23 MeV for P2), but P3 performs better than P1 by 33.3% (RMSE 0.12 MeV for P3).
- For Ca isotopes, P1 and P2 have comparable RMSE (0.35 MeV for P1, 0.33 MeV for P2), and P1 is better than P3 by approximately 5.4%.
- The isoscalar interaction P4 performs worst, with RMSE values of 1.17 MeV (Ca), 0.94 MeV (Ni), 1.00 MeV (Zr), and 0.89 MeV (Sn), corresponding to 70-82% worse than P1.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zhang, Lombardo, and Zhao (2011) and Zhang, Cao, Lombardo, et al. (2010), who extracted the P1 interaction from asymmetric nuclear matter calculations, by testing it on finite nuclei and comparing with the P2 interaction of Margueron, Sagawa, and Hagino (2008) and the P3 interaction of Bertulani et al. (2009). The results show that pairing gaps from infinite nuclear matter can provide a good constraint for constructing neutron pairing interactions in finite nuclei, and that isovector pairing interactions are strongly preferred over isoscalar ones. This enables future tests of the same ansatz for proton pairing gaps and further studies linking nuclear matter and finite-nuclei pairing.
