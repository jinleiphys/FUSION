# Influence of initial fluctuations on geometry measures in relativistic U-U and Cu-Au collisions

> In the framework of the Glauber approach we investigate the influence of the initial fluctuations on various measures of the initial-state geometry in 63Cu+197Au and 238U+238U relativistic ion collisions. Comparing variants of Glauber model (the wounded-nucleon model, the mixed model, and the hot-spot model) we indicate sensitivity of certain observables, in particular for the azimuthal eccentricity parameters as well as for the correlation of directions of the principal axes associated with the Fourier components. We apply GLISSANDO in our analysis.

## Key claim

Initial fluctuations from hot-spot and overlaid Gamma distributions can qualitatively change the behavior of geometry measures (eccentricity parameters and event-plane correlations) in Cu+Au and U+U collisions, potentially hiding subtle features such as the "knee" structure in the eccentricity of ultra-central U+U collisions that was predicted by Voloshin (2010) based on nuclear deformation alone.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo Glauber model with deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions (including quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation parameters) to simulate 10^7 minimum-bias events each for 63Cu+197Au and 238U+238U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. They compared three variants of the Glauber model: the wounded-nucleon model (alpha=1, delta-function source strengths), the mixed model (alpha=0.145, delta-function source strengths), and the hot-spot model (sigma_bin=2 mb, overlaid Gamma distribution with kappa=2), all using a realistic Gaussian wounding profile with sigma_w=42 mb and A=0.92.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear deformation parameters used: beta_2=0.162 for Cu, -0.13 for Au, 0.28 for U; beta_4=-0.006 for Cu, -0.03 for Au, 0.093 for U (from Moller 1993)
- In the hot-spot model, sigma_bin=2 mb, meaning hot-spots occur in about 5% of binary collisions but with a weight about 20 times larger than wounded sources
- The Gamma distribution overlay used kappa=2, giving var(u)=1/kappa=0.5
- The mixed model used alpha=0.145, the value fitting multiplicity distributions at the highest RHIC collision energy
- The Gaussian wounding profile parameter A=0.92, with sigma_w=42 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The nucleon-nucleon expulsion distance d=0.9 fm was used to simulate hard-core repulsion
- The correlation coefficient between epsilon_2 and epsilon_3 in central (0-20%) Cu+Au collisions was about 8% in the hot-spot model
- The increase in all epsilon_n (n=2,3,4) from the hot-spot model compared to the mixed model was around 30-50% or higher for very central collisions

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model studies of initial geometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the wounded-nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and the mixed model of Kharzeev (2000). It directly addresses the prediction by Voloshin (2010) that the prolate deformation of uranium nuclei would produce a "knee" structure in the eccentricity of ultra-central U+U collisions, showing that initial fluctuations from hot-spots can wash out this effect. The study also extends the analysis of event-plane correlations previously investigated by Bhalerao (2011) and Jia (2012) to asymmetric Cu+Au collisions and deformed U+U collisions, demonstrating that measurements of these correlations could discriminate between different models of the initial phase. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC to determine which Glauber variant best describes the initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution.
