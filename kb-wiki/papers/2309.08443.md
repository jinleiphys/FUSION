# Evolution of $N=20,28,50$ shell closures in the $ 20 \leqslant Z \leqslant 30$ region in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> Magicity, or shell closure, plays an important role in our understanding of complex nuclear phenomena. In this work, we employ one of the state-of-the-art density functional theories, the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the density functional PC-PK1, to investigate the evolution of the $N=20,28,50$ shell closures in the $ 20 \leqslant Z \leqslant 30$ region. We show how these three conventional shell closures evolve from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line by studying the charge radii, two-neutron separation energies, two-neutron gaps, quadrupole deformations, and single-particle levels. In particular, we find that in the $ 21 \leqslant Z \leqslant 27$ region, the $N=50$ shell closure disappears or becomes quenched, mainly due to the deformation effects. Similarly, both experimental data and theoretical predictions indicate that the $N=28$ shell closure disappears in the Mn isotopic chain, also predominantly due to the deformation effects. The DRHBc theory predicts the existence of the $N=20$ shell closure in the Ca, Sc, and Ti isotopic chains, but the existing data for the Ti isotopes suggests the contrary, and therefore more investigations are needed.

## Key claim
The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 functional predicts that the N=50 shell closure disappears or becomes quenched in the Z=21–27 region primarily due to deformation effects, and that the N=28 shell closure disappears in the Mn isotopic chain also due to deformation; however, the theory predicts a persistent N=20 shell closure in Ca, Sc, and Ti chains, while existing experimental data for Ti isotopes suggest the contrary, requiring further investigation.

## Method
The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional to systematically calculate ground-state properties of all isotopes with Z=20–30 from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. They solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations using a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis expansion with angular momentum cutoff J_max = 23/2 ħ, a box size of 20 fm, and an energy cutoff of 300 MeV, using a zero-range pairing force with saturation density 0.152 fm⁻³ and pairing strength V₀ = -325 MeV·fm³. For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, the blocking effect of unpaired nucleons was included.

## Context
This work extends the DRHBc mass table calculations (DRHBc Mass Table Collaboration, 2020, 2022) to systematically study shell evolution in the Z=20–30 region, complementing prior experimental studies of N=32 and N=34 shell closures in Ca isotopes (Steppenbeck, 2013) and the doubly-magic nature of ⁷⁸Ni (Taniuchi, 2019). The results enable future experimental verification of predicted shell quenching at N=28 and N=50 in neutron-rich isotopes, and motivate beyond-mean-field studies of shape coexistence in nuclei such as ⁵³Mn, where the potential energy curve is flat with two minima separated by less than 0.5 MeV.
