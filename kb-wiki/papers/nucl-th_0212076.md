# QCD and Nuclei

> I discuss how the fundamental theory of strong interactions given by QCD can be exploited to make accurate predictions for the solar fusion processes involving few-nucleon systems and to provide a novel description of nuclear matter as well as excitations near the chiral phase transition. The tool used is effective field theory combined with the modern technique of renormalization group flow. The notion of "BR scaling" is reinterpreted in terms of the vector manifestation à la Harada and Yamawaki. It is argued that the vector manifestation scenario predicts a QCD phase structure much richer than the standard scenario discussed up to date in the literature.

# QCD and Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) combined with the vector manifestation (VM) scenario of Harada and Yamawaki predicts a richer QCD phase structure than the standard scenario, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the VM fixed point is a viable mechanism for chiral restoration, which has not yet been confirmed by lattice QCD.

## Method

The author develops a "more effective effective field theory" (MEEFT) that combines standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) realistic potentials with chiral EFT power counting, using wave functions from potentials fitted to scattering data and then fixing unknown contact terms in the Gamow-Teller operator by fitting to the accurately measured triton beta decay. For dense matter, the method uses a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian matched to QCD at a scale near the chiral scale, with a "double-decimation" strategy that interpolates between the Fermi-liquid fixed point at nuclear matter density and the vector manifestation fixed point at the chiral restoration density.

## Key numbers

- $S_{pp}(0) = 3.94 \times (1 \pm 0.004) \times 10^{-25}$ MeV-b for the solar pp fusion process
- $S_{hep}(0) = (8.6 \pm 1.3) \times 10^{-20}$ keV-b for the hep process, representing approximately 15% accuracy
- $(f_\pi^*/f_\pi)^2 (n_0) \approx 0.61$ predicted at nuclear matter density, compared with $(f_\pi^*/f_\pi)^2 (n_0) = 0.65 \pm 0.05$ extracted from pionic atom data of Yamazaki (2002)
- $\Phi (n_0) = 0.78$ for the scaling quantity $f_\pi^*/f_\pi$ at nuclear matter density, obtained from nuclear gyromagnetic ratio

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics by incorporating the vector manifestation scenario of Harada and Yamawaki (1996, 1997) into the description of both few-nucleon systems and dense matter. The MEEFT approach for light nuclei builds on the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) of realistic potentials and the chiral filter mechanism of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978), while the dense matter analysis reinterprets Brown-Rho scaling (1991) in terms of the vector manifestation fixed point. The results enable more reliable predictions for solar neutrino fluxes and provide a framework for understanding chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter that differs from the standard pion-only scenario of Son and Stephanov (2002).
