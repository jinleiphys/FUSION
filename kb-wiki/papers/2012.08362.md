# All order effective action for charge diffusion from Schwinger-Keldysh holography

> An effective action for diffusion of a conserved $U(1)$ charge is derived to all orders in the derivative expansion within a holographic model dual to the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path. A systematic approach to solution of the 5D Maxwell equations in a doubled Schwarzschild-AdS$_5$ black brane geometry is developed. Constitutive relation for the stochastic charge current is shown to have a term induced by thermal fluctuations (coloured noise). All transport coefficient functions parameterising the effective action and constitutive relations are computed analytically in the hydrodynamic expansion, and then numerically for finite momenta.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective action for U(1) charge diffusion to all orders in the derivative expansion from a holographic Schwinger-Keldysh model, showing that the constitutive relation for the stochastic charge current includes a term induced by thermal fluctuations (coloured noise), but this result is limited to the quadratic level in the dynamical fields due to the linearity of the Maxwell equations in the bulk.

## Method

The authors solve the 5D Maxwell equations in a doubled Schwarzschild-AdS₅ black brane geometry, using the Schwinger-Keldysh holographic prescription of Glorioso (2018) that complexifies the radial coordinate around the event horizon. They employ an "off-shell" holography approach, solving only the dynamical equations while leaving the constraint aside, and compute transport coefficient functions analytically in the hydrodynamic expansion and numerically for finite momenta.

## Key numbers

- The inverse temperature β = 1/T is related to the black brane horizon radius by β = π/(2r_h), where r_h is the horizon radius
- The metric function f(r) = r² - r_h⁴/r² for the Schwarzschild-AdS₅ geometry
- The Schwarzschild time discontinuity at r = r_h - ε is t_up - t_dw = -iπ/(2r_h) = -iβ/2
- The effective action contains nine transport coefficient functions w₁ through w₉, with four independent parameters: w₅, w₇ (or w₆), w₈, and w₉

## Context

This work extends the all-order resummation technique developed by Bu, Lublinsky, and collaborators (2014-2015) for computing transport coefficient functions from holography, and generalizes the second-order derivative expansion results of Glorioso (2018) for the Schwinger-Keldysh effective action. The paper also relates to the stochastic hydrodynamic framework of Crossley (2015) and the fluctuation-dissipation relations discussed by Kovtun (2003, 2011). The results enable computation of coloured noise correlators and provide a first-principles derivation of the Son-Starinets prescription for retarded correlators from the Schwinger-Keldysh holography.
