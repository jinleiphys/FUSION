# Truncated Partial-Wave Analysis for $η$-photoproduction observables via Bayesian Statistics

> A truncated partial-wave analysis is performed for $η$-photoproduction using the polarization observables $σ_0, Σ, T, E, F$ and $G$. Different truncation orders are analyzed for six energy bins within the range of $E^{lab}_γ \in [750, 1250]$ MeV. Bayesian statistics is combined with truncated partial-wave analysis for the first time to investigate the structure of emerging ambiguities and their relevance in comparison to each other. Marginal distributions for the electromagnetic multipole parameters are presented together with predictions for polarization observables which have not yet been measured, in order to determine promising future measurements able to remove remaining mathematical ambiguities.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA) of η-photoproduction using Bayesian inference rather than point estimation, which allows the authors to quantify parameter uncertainties in unprecedented detail and to study the structure of mathematical ambiguities, specifically accidental discrete ambiguities, that arise from the bilinear form of the observables and cannot be avoided in analyses of real data.

## Method

A single-energy TPWA was performed for the reaction γp → ηp using six polarization observables (σ₀, Σ, T, E, F, G) across six energy bins from E_γ^lab = 750 to 1250 MeV in 100 MeV steps. The complex electromagnetic multipole parameters (up to truncation orders ℓ_max = 1 and ℓ_max = 2) were estimated via Bayesian inference using a multivariate Gaussian likelihood with a covariance matrix accounting for correlations between profile functions, and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo was used to sample the posterior distribution. Systematic uncertainties were incorporated via multiplicative scale parameters for each data set, combined using a worst-case scenario approach.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: E_γ^lab ∈ [750, 1250] MeV, divided into six bins of 100 MeV width
- Six polarization observables used: σ₀, Σ, T, E, F, G
- σ₀ data: 5736 data points from MAMI (Kashevarov et al.), covering cos(θ) ∈ [-0.958, 0.958] and E_γ^lab ∈ [723, 1571] MeV
- T and F data: 144 data points from MAMI (Akondi et al.), covering cos(θ) ∈ [-0.917, 0.917] and E_γ^lab ∈ [725, 1350] MeV
- Σ data: 140 data points from GRAAL (Bartalini et al.), covering cos(θ) ∈ [-0.946, 0.815] and E_γ^lab ∈ [761, 1472] MeV
- E data: 84 data points from MAMI (Afzal et al.), covering cos(θ) ∈ [-0.917, 0.917] and E_γ^lab ∈ [750, 1350] MeV
- G data: 47 data points from CBELSA/TAPS (Müller et al.), covering cos(θ) ∈ [-0.889, 0.667] and E_γ^lab ∈ [750, 1250] MeV
- Combined relative systematic uncertainties for profile functions: Δ^σ₀_sys = 4.0%, Δ^G_sys = 11.7%, Δ^E_sys = 10.0%, Δ^T_sys = 8.3%, Δ^F_sys = 10.5%
- Pearson correlation coefficients between data points: absolute values below ~0.17 typically, except between σ₀ and σ₀·E where correlations range from ~0.29 to ~0.67

## Context

This work extends the model-independent TPWA approach previously applied to π⁰-photoproduction by Wunderlich (PhD thesis) using maximum likelihood estimation, now incorporating Bayesian inference for the first time in this context. The results are compared to three model-dependent approaches: the K-Matrix model of Bonn-Gatchina (Anisovich et al., 2012), the dynamical coupled-channel approach of Jülich-Bonn (Rönchen et al., 2018), and the unitarized isobar-model Eta-MAID (Drechsel et al., 1999). The Bayesian framework enables the study of accidental discrete ambiguities (first investigated by Omelaenko, 1981) and provides model-independent predictions for unmeasured polarization observables, identifying which future measurements could resolve remaining mathematical ambiguities.
