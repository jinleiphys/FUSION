# Wave Chaos in Quantum Pseudointegrable Billiards

> We clarify from a general perspective, the condition for the appearance of chaotic energy spectrum in quantum pseudointegrable billiards with a point scatterer inside.

## Key claim

Chaotic (GOE-like) energy spectra appear in quantum pseudointegrable billiards with a single point scatterer only when the renormalized coupling strength satisfies dimension-dependent inequalities; in two dimensions the system recovers integrability at high energy for any coupling, while in three dimensions the spectrum becomes chaotic at high energy for any coupling.

## Method

The authors formulate the quantum billiard with a point scatterer using self-adjoint extension theory to handle the divergence of the delta potential in dimensions d=2 and 3. They derive the eigenvalue equation via Krein's formula, approximate the spectral function by replacing the squared wavefunction at the scatterer position with its average value, and compute the width of the strong-coupling region by linearizing the eigenvalue equation around the midpoint of adjacent unperturbed levels. Numerical nearest-neighbor level spacing distributions are computed for a three-dimensional rectangular box with side lengths (1.047, 1.186, 0.8049) and unit volume.

## Key numbers

- In one dimension, the strong-coupling condition is |v^{-1}| < (pi M^{1/2})/(2^{3/2} omega^{-1/2}), where the width Delta^{(1)}(omega_m) is approximately (pi M^{1/2})/(2^{1/2} omega_m^{-1/2}).
- In two dimensions, the strong-coupling condition is |v_theta^{-1} - (M/(2pi)) ln(omega/Lambda)| < pi M/4, with a constant width Delta^{(2)} = pi M/2.
- In three dimensions, the strong-coupling condition is |v_theta^{-1} + (M^{3/2} Lambda^{1/2})/(2pi)| < (M^{3/2})/(2^{3/2}) omega^{1/2}, with width Delta^{(3)}(omega_m) = (M^{3/2})/(2^{1/2}) omega_m^{1/2}.
- For the numerical example (M=1/2, Lambda=1, Omega^{(3)}=1), the most strong coupling occurs at v_theta^{-1} = -0.05626, and the upper bound for GOE-like behavior near the 1600th eigenvalue is v_theta^{-1} = 11.3.
- The statistics in the numerical example are taken over eigenvalues omega_{100} through omega_{3100} (z_{1600}=8304).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum billiards with point scatterers, which are quasi-exactly solvable models related to mesoscopic structures with impurities. The classical motion in such systems is pseudointegrable as defined by Richens and Berry (1981), with unstable trajectories of measure zero. The paper derives explicit conditions for "wave chaos" (Albeverio and Seba, 1991) to appear, showing that quantization can induce chaotic spectra even when classical dynamics would suggest integrability. The results enable predictions of spectral statistics in mesoscopic systems with dilute impurities and clarify the dimensional dependence of the transition to chaos.
