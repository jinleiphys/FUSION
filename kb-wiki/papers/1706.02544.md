# Shell model based deformation analysis of light Cadmium isotopes

> Large-scale shell-model calculations for the even-even Cadmium isotopes 98 Cd - 108 Cd have been performed with the ANTOINE code in the π(2p 1/2 ; 1g 9/2 ) ν(2d 5/2 ; 3s 1/2 ; 2d 3/2 ; 1g 7/2 ; 1h 11/2 ) model space without further truncation. Known experimental energy levels and B(E2) values could be well reproduced. Taking these calculations as a starting ground we analyze the deformation parameters predicted for the Cd isotopes as a function of neutron number N and spin J using the methods of model independent invariants introduced by K. Kumar and D. Cline.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations in a full model space (without truncation) can reproduce known experimental energy levels and B(E2) values for even-even Cadmium isotopes from 98Cd to 108Cd, and the extracted deformation parameters from these calculations show that the ground-state quadrupole deformation increases steadily with neutron number up to 104Cd, though experimental data suggest a leveling off that the calculations do not fully capture.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code in the π(2p1/2; 1g9/2) ν(2d5/2; 3s1/2; 2d3/2; 1g7/2; 1h11/2) model space with 88Sr as an inert core, using the v3sbm effective interaction derived from the Bonn-CD potential. They then applied the method of model-independent invariants introduced by Kumar and by Cline to extract deformation parameters β and γ from the calculated E2 matrix elements for the 0+ and 2+ states.

## Key numbers

- Effective charges used: eπ = 1.7e and eν = 1.1e, fixed by fitting the theoretical B(E2) for the 8+ → 6+ transition in 98Cd and the 2+ → 0+ transitions in 102,104Cd
- The m-scheme matrix dimension for m = 0 in 108Cd was about 10^8
- The 0+ → 2+ transition covers approximately 97% of the summed E2 transition strength for medium and heavy mass nuclei
- Non-yrast transitions (sum over f=2 to M of B(E2; 0+ → 2f+)) cover only about 5-6% of the total sum
- Experimental B(E2) for heavier Cd nuclei reaches a maximal value of 1140 e²fm⁴ at neutron number 70 (A=118)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of light Cd isotopes by Ekström et al. (2015) and Boelaert et al. (2010) using the same v3sb interaction, and applies the deformation analysis methods of Kumar (1972) and Cline (1986) that have been used in studies of Ge, Kr, Mo, Ru, and Pd nuclei as well as in the Pb mass region. The results enable future comparisons with Coulomb excitation experiments planned at the HIE-ISOLDE facility, and provide a foundation for a forthcoming paper on detailed spectroscopic results including energy spectra, collective bands, and electromagnetic properties.
