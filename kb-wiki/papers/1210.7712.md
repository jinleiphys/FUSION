# An effective QCD Lagrangian in the presence of an axial chemical potential

> We consider the low energy realization of QCD in terms of mesons when an axial chemical potential is present; a situation that may be relevant in heavy ion collisions. We shall demonstrate that the presence of an axial charge has profound consequences on meson physics. The most notorious effect is the appearance of an explicit source of parity breaking. The eigenstates of strong interactions do not have a definite parity and interactions that would otherwise be forbidden compete with the familiar ones. In this work we focus on scalars and pseudoscalars that are described by a generalized linear sigma model. We comment briefly on the screening role of axial vectors in formation of effective axial charge and on the possible experimental relevance of our results, whose consequences may have been already seen at RHIC.

# An effective QCD Lagrangian in the presence of an axial chemical potential

## Key claim
The presence of an axial chemical potential μ₅ in low-energy QCD, as may arise from topological charge fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, introduces an explicit source of parity violation that causes mixing between scalar and pseudoscalar meson states. The authors demonstrate that this mixing leads to energy-dependent effective masses and the possibility of tachyonic states at high momenta, but caution that μ₅ must be treated as a perturbatively small parameter and that very high values lie beyond the domain of applicability of the effective Lagrangian.

## Method
The authors construct a generalized linear sigma model with SU(3)ₐ flavor symmetry, including scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields, and fit its parameters to experimental meson masses and widths using MINUIT. They introduce the axial chemical potential as the time component of an isosinglet axial-vector field in the non-strange sector only, modify the covariant derivatives accordingly, and solve the resulting mass-gap equations. The mixing between opposite-parity states (π-a₀ and η-σ-η') is diagonalized in momentum space, and decay widths for the resulting mixed states are computed both at rest and for moving particles.

## Key numbers
- The pion decay constant v₀ is fitted to 92.00 MeV (experimental: 92 ± 5 MeV).
- The fitted π mass is 137.84 MeV (experimental: 137 ± 5 MeV).
- The fitted a₀ mass is 980.00 MeV (experimental: 980 ± 50 MeV).
- The fitted σ mass is 599.99 MeV (experimental: 600 ± 120 MeV).
- The fitted η mass is 497.78 MeV (experimental: 548 ± 50 MeV).
- The fitted η' mass is 968.20 MeV (experimental: 958 ± 100 MeV).
- The fitted a₀ width is 60.00 MeV (experimental: 60 ± 30 MeV).
- The fitted σ width is 600.00 MeV (experimental: 600 ± 120 MeV).
- The η-η' mixing angle ψ is predicted to be 35.46°, compared to the experimental value of approximately 36.7°.
- For μ₅ = 50 MeV, the new state η̃ acquires a width of order 60 MeV at rest, corresponding to a mean free path of about 3 fm, smaller than the typical fireball size of 5 to 10 fm.
- The critical energy for tachyonic behavior in the isotriplet channel is kᶜ_π̃ = m₁m₂/(4μ₅), and in the isosinglet channel kᶜ_η̃ = m₃/(4μ₅m₅) √(m₄²m₅² - 8c²v_q²).

## Context
This work extends the study of parity breaking in QCD under extreme conditions, building on earlier analytical studies by Andrianov and Espriu (prior work) that suggested a window of baryonic chemical potentials leading to spontaneous parity violation. It is connected to the Chiral Magnetic Effect proposed by Kharzeev (2008) and related topological charge fluctuations discussed by Polyakov (1977) and Zhitnitsky (2008). The paper enables future investigation of how parity-breaking effects from an axial chemical potential might explain the anomalous dilepton yield enhancement observed in heavy ion collisions, and suggests that the pion gas in a fireball after a central heavy ion collision may consist of states with non-defined parity and energy-dependent effective masses.
