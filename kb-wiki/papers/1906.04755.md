# New Physics Probes: Atomic Parity Violation, Polarized Electron Scattering and Neutrino-Nucleus Coherent Scattering

> Atomic Parity Violation (APV) is usually quantified in terms of the weak nuclear charge $Q_W$ of a nucleus, which depends on the coupling strength between the atomic electrons and quarks. In this work, we review the importance of APV to probing new physics using effective field theory. Furthermore, using $SU(2)$ invariance, we correlate our findings with those from neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering. Moreover, we investigate signs of parity violation in polarized electron scattering and show how precise measurements on the Weinberg angle, $\sin θ_W$, will give rise to competitive bounds on light mediators over a wide range of masses and interactions strength. Lastly, apply our bounds to several models namely, Dark Z, Two Higgs Doublet Model-$U(1)_X$ and 3-3-1, considering both light and heavy mediator regimes.

## Key claim

Atomic Parity Violation (APV) provides more restrictive bounds on the new physics scale than neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering, but this conclusion relies on the assumption of SU(2) invariance to correlate the effective couplings between electrons and neutrinos; the two observables strictly probe different couplings (electron-quark versus neutrino-quark).

## Method

The authors use effective field theory to parametrize new physics contributions to parity violation in terms of an energy scale Λ and effective couplings fVu and fVd. They derive constraints on these parameters from the measured weak charge of Cesium-133 (APV) and from COHERENT neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering data, correlating the two via SU(2) invariance. For light mediators, they parametrize the effect of Z-Z' mixing on the weak mixing angle sin²θW and derive bounds on the kinetic mixing parameter ε from polarized electron scattering experiments (E158, Qweak, Moller, P2, SoLID). They apply these bounds to the Dark Z model, Two Higgs Doublet Model-U(1)X, and 3-3-1 models.

## Key numbers

* Standard Model prediction for the weak charge of Cesium: QWth = -73.16(5); experimental value: QWex = -73.16(35); 90% CL difference: |ΔQ(Cs)| < 0.6
* Bound on effective coupling from APV: fVqeff/Λ² < 4.38699 × 10⁻⁹ GeV⁻² (for Cs, Z=55, N=78)
* Bound on effective coupling from COHERENT (most restrictive): -7.7 × 10⁻⁷ < fVqeff/Λ² < 4 × 10⁻⁶ GeV⁻²
* Moller experiment projected precision: Δsin²θW = ±0.00029 at ⟨Q⟩ = 75 MeV; P2 experiment: ±0.00033 at ⟨Q⟩ = 67 MeV
* For the 3-3-1 model (model A, β=√3): lower mass bound mZ' > 1.7 TeV from APV
* For the Two Higgs Doublet Model-U(1)X in heavy mediator regime: mZ' ≥ 1.5 gX × 10⁴ GeV from APV

## Context

This work extends the literature by exploring complementarity between APV, polarized electron scattering, and neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering, rather than focusing on a single observable. It builds on prior measurements of the Cesium weak charge (Porsev 2010, Dzuba 2012) and COHERENT data (Liao 2017). The bounds on light mediators from sin²θW measurements are compared with constraints from BaBar (McKenna 2019) and LEP (Heeck 2014), and the authors find that APV provides stronger limits on Z' masses in several models than existing collider searches. The work enables future experiments like Moller, P2, and SoLID to set competitive constraints on light mediators over a wide mass range.
