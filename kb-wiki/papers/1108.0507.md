# Weak-interaction mediated rates on iron isotopes for presupernova evolution of massive stars

> During the presupernova evolution of massive stars, the isotopes of iron, $^{54,55,56}$Fe, are advocated to play a key role inside the cores primarily decreasing the electron-to-baryon ratio ($Y_{e}$) mainly via electron capture processes thereby reducing the pressure support. Electron decay and positron capture on $^{55}$Fe, on the other hand, also has a consequential role in increasing the lepton ratio during the silicon burning phases of massive stars. The neutrinos and antineutrinos produced, as a result of these weak-interaction reactions, are transparent to the stellar matter and assist in cooling the core thereby reducing the entropy. The structure of the presupernova star is altered both by the changes in $Y_{e}$ and the entropy of the core material. The aim of this paper is to report the improved microscopic calculation of Gamow-Teller (GT$_{\pm}$) strength distributions of these key isotopes of iron using the pn-QRPA theory. The main improvement comes from the incorporation of experimental deformation values for these nuclei. Additionally six different weak-interaction rates, namely electron & positron capture, electron & positron decay, and, neutrino & antineutrino cooling rates, were also calculated in presupernova matter. The calculated electron capture and neutrino cooling rates due to isotopes of iron are in good agreement with the large-scale shell model (LSSM) results. The calculated beta decay rates, however, are suppressed by three to five orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper reports improved microscopic calculations of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and six weak-interaction rates for ⁵⁴,⁵⁵,⁵⁶Fe using the pn-QRPA theory, with the main improvement being the incorporation of experimental deformation values for ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe. The calculated electron capture and neutrino cooling rates agree well with large-scale shell model (LSSM) results, but the calculated beta decay rates are suppressed by three to five orders of magnitude relative to LSSM.

## Method

The author used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions (GT₊ and GT₋) for ⁵⁴,⁵⁵,⁵⁶Fe, incorporating experimentally adopted deformation parameters from Raman et al. (1987) for ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe and the Möller-Nix mass compilation for ⁵⁵Fe. A state-by-state calculation was performed for 246 parent excited states in ⁵⁴Fe, 297 states in ⁵⁵Fe, and 266 states in ⁵⁶Fe, with transitions to 150 daughter excited states each, covering excitation energy ranges of 15-20 MeV. Six weak-interaction rates (electron capture, positron capture, electron decay, positron decay, neutrino cooling, and antineutrino cooling) were then computed on a temperature-density grid suitable for presupernova conditions.

## Key numbers

- GT₊ centroid for ⁵⁴Fe improved from 6.29 MeV to 4.06 MeV (measured centroid: 3.7 ± 0.2 MeV)
- GT₊ centroid for ⁵⁶Fe improved from 4.68 MeV to 3.13 MeV (measured centroid: 2.9 ± 0.2 MeV)
- pn-QRPA calculated GT₋ centroid for ⁵⁴Fe: 5.08 MeV (compared to 7.63 MeV from (p,n) data of Anderson et al. 1990)
- pn-QRPA calculated GT₋ centroid for ⁵⁶Fe: 5.61 MeV (compared to 8.27 MeV from (p,n) data of Rapaport et al. 1983)
- Calculated beta decay rates for ⁵⁵Fe are suppressed by three to five orders of magnitude compared to LSSM results
- Positron decay rates for ⁵⁴,⁵⁵Fe show good agreement with LSSM at T₉ = 2-3 (temperature in units of 10⁹ K); otherwise pn-QRPA rates are smaller by one to two orders of magnitude
- Antineutrino energy loss rates for ⁵⁵Fe are smaller than LSSM by up to five orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999, 2004) for sd-shell and fp/fpg-shell nuclei by incorporating experimental deformation parameters, addressing the importance of deformation emphasized by Stetcu and Johnson (2004). The results are compared with the large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations of Langanke et al. (2000) and with earlier parameterized rates by Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980) and Aufderheide et al. (1994). The calculated rates are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulators, particularly for the presupernova evolution of massive stars where ⁵⁴,⁵⁵,⁵⁶Fe were ranked by Heger et al. (2001) as among the most important nuclei for decreasing the electron-to-baryon ratio during oxygen and silicon burning phases.
