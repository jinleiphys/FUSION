# A meson-exchange piN model up to energies sqrt(s) < 2.0 GeV

> A meson-exchange piN model, previously constructed using three-dimensional reduction scheme of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a model Lagrangian involving π, η, N, Δ, rho, and σfields, is extended to energies up to 2 GeV by including the ηN channel and all the four stars πN resonances up to the F-waves. The effects of other 2 pion channels are taken into account phenomenologically. The extended model gives an excellent fit to both piN phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in all channels up to the F-waves. However, a few of the extracted resonance parameters differ considerably from the PDG values.

# Meson-Exchange πN Model up to √s < 2.0 GeV

## Key claim
The extended meson-exchange πN model gives an excellent fit to both πN phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in all channels up to the F-waves, but a few of the extracted resonance parameters differ considerably from the PDG values, including two resonances not listed in PDG and substantial deviations in masses and widths for several higher resonances.

## Method
The authors extended a previously constructed meson-exchange πN model, based on a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a Lagrangian involving π, η, N, Δ, ρ, and σ fields, to energies up to 2 GeV by coupling the ηN channel and including all four-star πN resonances up to F-waves. The effects of other 2π channels (σN, ρN, πΔ) were treated phenomenologically via a term Γ^R_{2π}(E) in the resonance propagator, characterized by a cut-off Λ_R and a 2π decay width at resonance. In each partial wave, all contributing resonances were treated symmetrically, with each resonance dressed by the presence of all others, and physical masses and widths were extracted by solving for the energy where the dressed propagator becomes purely imaginary.

## Key numbers
- The model describes πN scattering data up to a center-of-mass energy of 2.0 GeV.
- For the S₁₁ channel, the extracted physical masses and widths are: 1st resonance (1520 MeV, 130 MeV), 2nd resonance (1678 MeV, 200 MeV), 3rd resonance (1878 MeV, 508 MeV).
- For the P₃₃ channel, the extracted physical masses and widths are: 1st resonance (1233 MeV, 132 MeV), 2nd resonance (1562 MeV, 216 MeV), 3rd resonance (1827 MeV, 834 MeV).
- For the F₁₅ channel, the 2nd resonance has a physical mass of 1926 MeV and a width of only 58 MeV, much smaller than the PDG value of 490 ± 310 MeV.
- Two resonances not listed in PDG were required: S₁₁(1878) and D₁₃(1946).
- For S₃₁, the PDG values for the 2nd and 3rd resonances are (1900 ± 50 MeV, 190 ± 50 MeV) and (2150 ± 100 MeV, 200 ± 100 MeV); the model gives (1770 MeV, 430 MeV) and (1942 MeV, 416 MeV).
- For P₁₁, the PDG values for the 2nd and 3rd resonances are (1710 ± 30 MeV, 180 ± 100 MeV) and (2125 ± 75 MeV, 260 ± 100 MeV); the model gives (1803 MeV, 508 MeV) and (2247 MeV, 1020 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous meson-exchange πN models (Lee 1991, Hung 2001) from energies up to 400 MeV to 2 GeV, following an earlier extension for the S₁₁ channel only (Chen 2003). It builds on the meson-exchange approach applied by several groups (Pearce and Jennings, Goudsmit and Schulze, Jülich group, Lahiff 1999, Pena and Toki 2000, Hung 2001) to construct πN scattering models. The model enables reliable extraction of photoexcitation strengths for all resonances below 2 GeV by evaluating the contribution of the pion cloud to photopion reactions, as was done in Kamalov and Yang (1999).
