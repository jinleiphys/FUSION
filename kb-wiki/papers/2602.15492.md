# Description of nucleon elastic scattering off $^6$Li with the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method

> Background: Neutron reactions off lithium isotopes up to 50 MeV are important for nuclear data science, around the International Fusion Material Irradiation Facility (IFMIF) facility in particular. Purpose: We aim at constructing a semi-microscopic reaction model that describes neutron elastic scattering off $^6$Li up to 50 MeV taking the breakup channels of $^6$Li into account. Methods: We adopt the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with an $α+p+n$ three-body model of $^6$Li. We employ the $g$-matrix effective interaction by Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (JLM). The renormalization factors of the real and imaginary parts of the JLM interaction are treated as free parameters. Results: The renormalization parameter of the real part of the JLM interaction is found to be constant ($=1.1$), whereas that for the imaginary part has a smooth energy dependence. The four-body CDCC calculation with these parameters well describes the angular distributions of both proton and neutron elastic scatterings as well as the neutron total cross section and proton total reaction cross section. The applicable energy range is found to be from 7 MeV to 50 MeV. Conclusions: We have constructed a reliable reaction model for describing nucleon-$^6$Li scattering between 7 MeV and 50 MeV. This model can directly be applied to inelastic scattering and break reactions for $^6$Li with the help of the complex scaling method.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with an α+p+n three-body model of ⁶Li and the JLM g-matrix effective interaction, using a constant real renormalization factor (NV = 1.1) and an energy-dependent imaginary renormalization factor (NW = 0.213 ln E − 0.247), describes nucleon elastic scattering off ⁶Li between 7 MeV and 50 MeV, but the model undershoots the neutron total cross section outside this energy range.

## Method

The authors employed the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with an α+p+n three-body model of ⁶Li, using Gaussian expansion method (GEM) eigenstates to describe both bound and discretized continuum states. The nucleon-nucleon effective interaction was the g-matrix interaction of Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (JLM), with the real and imaginary renormalization factors (NV and NW) treated as free parameters determined by fitting to neutron elastic scattering cross sections at 14 energies between 5 MeV and 24 MeV and to proton elastic scattering at 50 MeV. The coupled-channel equations included both open channels (E_nI > 0) and closed channels (E_nI ≤ 0), with boundary conditions imposed at R = 20 fm.

## Key numbers

- Real renormalization factor NV = 1.1 (constant) across all energies from 5 MeV to 50 MeV
- Imaginary renormalization factor NW = 0.213 ln E − 0.247, where E is incident energy in MeV
- NW = 0.84 at 160 MeV (upper limit of JLM g-matrix interaction)
- Applicable energy range: 7 MeV to 50 MeV for neutron scattering
- Maximum orbital angular momentum L_max = 5 at 4 MeV and L_max = 23 at 72 MeV
- Gaussian range for JLM interaction: 1.2 fm for both real and imaginary parts
- Cutoff energy for Φ_nIm_I eigenstates: ε_nI ≤ 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic CDCC approach of Matsumoto et al. (2011), who used an α+d two-body model for ⁶Li and found that their model severely undershot the n-⁶Li total cross section above 25 MeV. The present study improves upon that work by using a more realistic α+p+n three-body model, including closed channels that were disregarded previously, and carefully determining the energy dependence of the JLM renormalization parameters. The model enables future application to inelastic scattering and breakup reactions of ⁶Li using complex scaling methods, and provides a foundation for extending the approach to ⁷Li reactions important for nuclear data science at facilities like IFMIF.
