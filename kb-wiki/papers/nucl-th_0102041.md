# The stability of the scalar $χ^2ϕ$ interaction

> A scalar field theory with a $χ^\dagχϕ$ interaction is known to be unstable. Yet it has been used frequently without any sign of instability in standard text book examples and research articles. In order to reconcile these seemingly conflicting results, we show that the theory is stable if the Fock space of all intermediate states is limited to a {\em finite} number of $χ{\barχ}$ loops associated with field $χ$ that appears quadradically in the interaction, and that instability arises only when intermediate states include these loops to all orders.

## Key claim

The scalar χ²φ interaction is stable if the Fock space of intermediate states is limited to a finite number of χχ̅ loops, and instability arises only when intermediate states include these loops to all orders; the theory can therefore be stable for a limited range of coupling values despite a 1959 proof by Baym showing the theory is unstable.

## Method

The authors derive a variational lower bound on the ground state energy of the χ²φ theory by expanding eigenstates as sums of free particle states with n₀ matter particles, n_pair χχ̅ pairs, and ℓ mesons, then applying Schwarz inequalities to bound the matrix elements. They also employ the Feynman-Schwinger representation (FSR), a path integral approach that replaces field integrals with integrals over covariant particle trajectories, to analyze the effects of Z-diagrams and closed χχ̅ loops separately.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for stability is given by g²_crit ≡ 4 μ̃ m̃ (n₀ + 2L) / { V² [ (√(n₀+1+L) + √L)² - 1 ]² }, where m̃ and μ̃ are effective masses, V is a coupling volume factor, n₀ is the number of matter particles, and L is the average number of χχ̅ pairs
- In the semiclassical estimate, the ground state is stable when g² < g²_crit = 2m²μ²/χ²
- For large L (number of closed loops), the critical coupling g²_crit decreases as 1/L, meaning the interaction energy of L loops grows roughly as L² while kinetic energy grows only as L

## Context

This work reconciles the 1959 proof of instability by Baym (1959) with the widespread use of the χ²φ interaction in textbooks and research articles that show no signs of instability. The authors show that quenched calculations (which exclude closed χχ̅ loops) produce stable results for a finite range of couplings, as previously found by Savkli, Tjon, and Gross (1999). The Feynman-Schwinger representation analysis demonstrates that Z-diagrams (folded trajectories) are always kinematically suppressed, while closed loops introduce negative mutual interactions that grow faster than the stabilizing kinetic energy, causing instability when loops are included to all orders.
