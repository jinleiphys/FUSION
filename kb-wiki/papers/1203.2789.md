# Magnetic excitations in nuclei with neutron excess

> The excitation of the $1^+$, $2^-$ and $3^+$ modes in $^{16}$O, $^{22}$O, $^{24}$O, $^{28}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{52}$Ca and $^{60}$Ca nuclei is studied with self-consistent random phase approximation calculations. Finite-range interactions of Gogny type, containing also tensor-isospin terms, are used. We analyze the evolution of the magnetic resonances with the increasing number of neutrons, the relevance of collective effects, the need of a correct treatment of the continuum and the role of the tensor force.

## Key claim

The paper claims that self-consistent continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations with finite-range Gogny interactions, including tensor-isospin terms, can describe magnetic excitations in neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes, but the results depend critically on the treatment of the continuum and the inclusion of tensor forces, with the tensor force lowering the energy of the main $1^+$ peaks in $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations followed by continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations for the $1^+$, $2^-$, and $3^+$ magnetic excitations in $^{16}$O, $^{22}$O, $^{24}$O, $^{28}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{52}$Ca, and $^{60}$Ca. They used four Gogny-type finite-range interactions: D1S, D1M, and two new parametrizations (D1ST and D1MT) that include a finite-range tensor-isospin term added to D1S and D1M respectively. The CRPA equations were solved by expanding channel functions on a Sturmian basis, and results were compared with discrete RPA (DRPA) calculations using a harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The least bound single-particle energy for $^{28}$O neutron $(1d_{3/2})^{-1}$ is -0.96 MeV (D1S), -0.94 MeV (D1ST), -0.87 MeV (D1M), -0.72 MeV (D1MT)
- The least bound single-particle energy for $^{60}$Ca neutron $(1f_{5/2})^{-1}$ is -3.05 MeV (D1S), -2.96 MeV (D1ST), -3.29 MeV (D1M), -3.09 MeV (D1MT)
- Total $B(M1)\uparrow$ strength for $^{22}$O: 8.796 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (DIPM with D1S), 6.225 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (CRPA with D1S), 5.599 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (CRPA with D1ST)
- Total $B(M1)\uparrow$ strength for $^{24}$O: 8.438 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (DIPM with D1S), 6.285 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (CRPA with D1S), 6.643 $\mu_N^2$ MeV (CRPA with D1ST)
- The $1^+$ strengths in $^{16}$O and $^{28}$O are orders of magnitude smaller than those in $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O
- DRPA calculations used 50 harmonic oscillator expansion coefficients and single-particle energies up to 150 MeV
- CRPA calculations used at least 10 expansion coefficients of the Sturm-Bessel basis for convergence

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent CRPA studies of electric natural-parity excitations in neutron-rich nuclei (De Donno et al., 2011) to magnetic unnatural-parity excitations. It contrasts with phenomenological RPA approaches that can adjust interaction parameters to compensate for configuration space truncation, whereas self-consistent approaches require full treatment of the continuum. The tensor force effects build on studies using Skyrme interactions (Bai et al., 2009, 2010; Cao et al., 2009) but use finite-range tensor terms rather than zero-range ones. The work enables future experimental comparisons with radioactive ion beam facilities and provides predictions for electron scattering responses in neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes.
