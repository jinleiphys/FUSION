# Structure and direct decay of Giant Monopole Resonances

> We study structure and direct decay of the Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR) at the RPA level using the Time-Dependent Energy Density Functional method in the linear response regime in a few doubly-magic nuclei. A proper treatment of the continuum, through the use of large coordinate space, allows for a separation between the nucleus and its emitted nucleons. The microscopic structure of the GMR is investigated with the decomposition of the strength function into individual single-particles quantum numbers. A similar microscopic decomposition of the spectra of emitted nucleons by direct decay of the GMR is performed. In this harmonic picture of giant resonance, shifting every contribution by the initial single-particle energy allows to reconstruct the GMR strength function. The RPA residual interaction couples bound 1-particle 1-hole states to unbound ones, allowing for the total decay of the GMR. In this article, we then intend to get an understanding of the direct decay mechanism from coherent one-particle-one-hole superpositions, while neglecting more complex configurations. Time-dependent beyond mean-field approaches should be use, in the future, to extend this method.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, within the independent-particle (RPA) picture, the energy spectra of nucleons emitted by direct decay of the Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR) can be shifted by the initial single-particle energy to reconstruct the GMR strength function, but this reconstruction holds only for the direct decay component and neglects the spreading width from 2p2h configurations.

## Method

The authors used the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method in the linear response regime with the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional to study the GMR in doubly-magic nuclei. They performed calculations on a large spherical grid (radius 600 fm, spacing 0.3 fm) to allow emitted nucleons to separate from the nucleus, then decomposed the strength function and emitted-nucleon spectra into individual single-particle quantum numbers (l, j). The energy spectra of emitted nucleons were obtained by projecting outgoing wave packets onto free-wave (neutrons) or Coulomb-wave (protons) basis states in the outer region beyond 30 fm.

## Key numbers

* GMR energy in 16O: approximately 21.4 MeV, extracted from the average oscillation period of 57.9 fm/c in the monopole moment.
* Maximum evolution time: T = 2250 fm/c, with a box radius of 600 fm ensuring that particles with energy below approximately 33 MeV do not reach the box boundary.
* No emitted particles with energy above 25 MeV were found in the calculations.
* The radial spacing used was 0.3 fm, with an inner box radius of 30 fm for the HF initial condition.
* The strength function decomposition in 16O shows the main peak at approximately 21.4 MeV with shoulders at approximately 17 MeV and approximately 31 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of GMR direct decay by Chomaz et al. (1987) and Pacheco et al. (1988), which used schematic energy density functionals to extract widths and lifetimes, by providing new information on the energy spectra of emitted nucleons and their microscopic decomposition. The approach is limited to the independent-particle picture (RPA level) and neglects the spreading width from 2p2h configurations, which is known to be important in heavier nuclei such as tin isotopes. The method enables future extension to time-dependent beyond-mean-field approaches to include more complex configurations and study the competition between direct and sequential decay.
