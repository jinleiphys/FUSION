# Neutron-proton pairs in nuclei

> A review is given of attempts to describe nuclear properties in terms of neutron--proton pairs that are subsequently replaced by bosons. Some of the standard approaches with low-spin pairs are recalled but the emphasis is on a recently proposed framework with pairs of neutrons and protons with aligned angular momentum. The analysis is carried out for general $j$ and applied to $N=Z$ nuclei in the $1f_{7/2}$ and $1g_{9/2}$ shells.

## Key claim

This review argues that the description of neutron-proton pairs in nuclei can be extended beyond standard low-spin boson models (IBM-3 and IBM-4) by introducing aligned, high-spin neutron-proton pairs (called "B pairs") with angular momentum J=2j and isospin T=0, but the validity of this truncation depends on the specific shell and interaction, and the mapping to bosons requires careful treatment of Pauli exchange effects through multi-body interactions.

## Method

The author reviews the nucleon-pair shell model (NPSM) formalism, where a basis is constructed from nucleon pairs with defined angular momentum and isospin, and applies it to aligned neutron-proton pairs (B pairs) with J=2j and T=0. The analysis uses Chen's recurrence relation to compute overlap matrix elements and construct orthonormal bases, then performs boson mapping by equating matrix elements of the fermion hamiltonian in the B-pair basis to those of a boson hamiltonian in the corresponding b-boson basis, with Pauli-forbidden states eliminated by assigning infinite energy. The method is applied to single-j shells (1f7/2 and 1g9/2) with schematic delta interactions and realistic two-body interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the 1f7/2 shell (j=7/2), aligned B pairs have angular momentum J=9 and isospin T=0.
- For the 1g9/2 shell (j=9/2), aligned B pairs have angular momentum J=9 and isospin T=0.
- In the example of 62Ga, the IBM-4 calculation reproduces low-spin T=0 levels but finds that T=0 levels with higher spin (5+, 6+, and 7+) are at significantly higher energies or absent, motivating the need for aligned-pair approaches.
- The two-body matrix elements of the shell-model hamiltonian are denoted as υ2fΓ = υ2fJT = ⟨j2 JT|Ĥ2f|j2 JT⟩, which enter the pair matrix element calculations.

## Context

This review extends the standard interacting boson model approaches (IBM-3 by Elliott and White (1980) and IBM-4 by Elliott and Evans (1981)) which use low-spin bosons (up to Jπ=3+) derived from neutron-proton pairs with isospin T=1 and T=0. The work is motivated by the proposal of Cederwall et al. (2011) that yrast states in 92Pd and neighboring nuclei are built from aligned neutron-proton isoscalar pairs with J=9, and relates to the stretch scheme of Danos and Gillet (1966, 1967) and the q-pair analysis of Daley (1987, unpublished). The nucleon-pair shell model framework (Chen 1993, 1997) provides the technical basis for testing the aligned-pair truncation and mapping to bosons, enabling future systematic studies of whether such truncations are valid in different mass regions.
