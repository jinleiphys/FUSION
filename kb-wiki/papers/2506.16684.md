# Chiral Invariant Mass Constraints from HESS J1731 347 in an Extended Parity Doublet Model with Isovector Scalar Meson

> The recent discovery of a central compact object (CCO) within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, with mass $0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} \ M_\odot $ and radius $10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km is the lightest and smallest compact object ever observed. We identify it as an ultra-light Neutron star (NS) and constrain the chiral invariant mass of nucleon $m_0$ from the observational data of NS using an extended parity doublet model with including the isovector scalar meson $a_0(980)$. We study the higher order asymmertic matter properties such as the symmetry incompressibility $K_{sym}$ and the symmetry skewness $Q_{sym}$ in the presence of $a_0$ meson. We find that $K_{sym}$ and $Q_{sym}$ is sensitive to the chiral invariant mass of nucleon $m_0$ in the presence of $a_0$ meson. We show that the equation of state in the present model satisfies all observational constraints within $2σ$ credible region including the HESS J1731-347 observation, as well as the constraint from $K_{sym}$ when $740 \,\text{ MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860 \,\text{ MeV}$ for $L_0 = $ 57.7 MeV. Yet, the $1σ$ constraint from neutron stars appears to be not fully compatible with the constraint from $K_{sym}$ from the present model.

## Key claim
The paper identifies the central compact object in HESS J1731-347 as an ultra-light neutron star and uses its observed mass and radius to constrain the chiral invariant mass of the nucleon \(m_0\) to \(740 \,\text{ MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860 \,\text{ MeV}\) for a fixed symmetry energy slope \(L_0 = 57.7\) MeV, but notes that the \(1\sigma\) constraint from neutron stars is not fully compatible with the constraint from the symmetry incompressibility \(K_{sym}\) within the model.

## Method
The authors employ an extended parity doublet model that includes the isovector scalar meson \(a_0(980)\) to describe dense nuclear matter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to generate neutron star mass-radius relations and compare them against the observational data of HESS J1731-347 (mass \(0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_\odot\), radius \(10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}\) km). They also compute higher-order asymmetric matter properties, specifically the symmetry incompressibility \(K_{sym}\) and symmetry skewness \(Q_{sym}\), and study their sensitivity to the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\).

## Key numbers
- Central compact object HESS J1731-347: mass \(0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_\odot\) and radius \(10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}\) km, the lightest and smallest compact object ever observed.
- Chiral invariant mass of nucleon constrained to \(740 \,\text{ MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860 \,\text{ MeV}\) for symmetry energy slope \(L_0 = 57.7\) MeV, satisfying all observational constraints within \(2\sigma\) credible region.
- The symmetry incompressibility \(K_{sym}\) and symmetry skewness \(Q_{sym}\) are found to be sensitive to \(m_0\) in the presence of the \(a_0\) meson.

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet model approach previously applied to neutron star phenomenology by including the isovector scalar meson \(a_0(980)\), which was not considered in earlier studies. The identification of HESS J1731-347 as an ultra-light neutron star provides a new observational anchor for constraining the chiral invariant mass, a fundamental parameter in chiral effective theories. The results enable future studies to reconcile neutron star observations with nuclear matter properties like symmetry incompressibility, though the \(1\sigma\) tension between these constraints remains an open issue.
