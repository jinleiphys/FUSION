# Liberating Efimov physics from three dimensions

> When two particles attract via a resonant short-range interaction, three particles always form an infinite tower of bound states characterized by a discrete scaling symmetry. It has been considered that this Efimov effect exists only in three dimensions. Here we review how the Efimov physics can be liberated from three dimensions by considering two-body and three-body interactions in mixed dimensions and four-body interaction in one dimension. In such new systems, intriguing phenomena appear, such as confinement-induced Efimov effect, Bose-Fermi crossover in Efimov spectrum, and formation of interlayer Efimov trimers. Some of them are observable in ultracold atom experiments and we believe that this study significantly broadens our horizons of universal Efimov physics.

## Key claim
The Efimov effect, an infinite tower of bound states with discrete scaling symmetry, can appear in systems other than three dimensions by using scale-invariant short-range interactions beyond ordinary two-body interactions, specifically two-body and three-body interactions in mixed dimensions and four-body interactions in one dimension. The authors argue that the necessary condition for the Efimov effect is the existence of a nontrivial scale-invariant interaction, which can be realized in these new settings even though the effect was previously thought to exist only in three dimensions.

## Method
The authors analyze the Schrödinger equation for N particles in various spatial dimensions, showing that scale-invariant short-range interactions require the number of relative coordinates D to equal 3. They enumerate all combinations of particle numbers and spatial dimensions satisfying D=3, including mixed-dimensional systems (e.g., 2D-3D, 1D-3D, 1D-2D mixtures) and four-body interactions in one dimension. For each system, they solve the three-body problem to compute scaling exponents and critical mass ratios, and they derive binding energies and scaling factors for Efimov trimers in these geometries.

## Key numbers
- Scaling factor for AAB Efimov trimers with bosonic A particles at mass ratio m_A/m_B = 87/41 (Rb/K): λ = 131 in pure 3D, λ = 27.7 in 2D-3D mixture, λ = 34.6 in 1D-3D mixture.
- Scaling factor for AAB Efimov trimers with bosonic A particles at mass ratio m_A/m_B = 41/87 (K/Rb): λ = 3.48×10^5 in pure 3D, λ = 59.3 in 2D-3D mixture, λ = 67.6 in 1D-3D mixture.
- Critical mass ratios for fermionic A particles to exhibit Efimov effect in p-wave channel: m_A/m_B = 13.6 in pure 3D, m_A/m_B = 6.35 in 2D-3D mixture, m_A/m_B = 2.06 in 1D-3D mixture.
- For Fermi-Fermi mixture A=40K, B=6Li (m_A/m_B = 40/6): no Efimov effect in pure 3D; scaling factor λ = 1.78×10^5 in 2D-3D mixture; λ = 22.0 in 1D-3D mixture.
- Ground state binding energy in bilayer 2D-3D mixture at unitarity (a_eff → ∞): E_0 = −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.222/d)² for m_A/m_B = 40/6; E_0 = −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.0310/d)² for m_A/m_B = 6/40.
- Ground state binding energy in biwire 1D-3D mixture at unitarity: E_0 = −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.370/d)² for m_A/m_B = 40/6; E_0 = −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.109/d)² for m_A/m_B = 6/40.
- Excited trimer scaling in bilayer 2D-3D mixture: E_{n≫1} ≈ −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.231/d)² × (11.3)^{−2n} for m_A/m_B = 40/6; E_{n≫1} ≈ −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.0311/d)² × (69.3)^{−2n} for m_A/m_B = 6/40.
- Excited trimer scaling in biwire 1D-3D mixture: E_{n≫1} ≈ −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.399/d)² × (13.6)^{−2n} for m_A/m_B = 40/6; E_{n≫1} ≈ −(ℏ²/2μ)(0.109/d)² × (76.6)^{−2n} for m_A/m_B = 6/40.

## Context
This work extends the classic Efimov effect discovered by Efimov (1970) for three identical bosons in three dimensions, which was previously thought to exist only in an interval of spatial dimensions 2.30 < d < 3.76 as shown by Nielsen (2001). The authors build on their own earlier work (Nishida 2008) identifying scale-invariant interactions in mixed dimensions, and they provide a unified framework showing that Efimov physics can appear in eight distinct systems including 2D-3D, 1D-3D, and 1D-2D mixtures as well as four-body interactions in one dimension. The results enable experimental observation of confinement-induced Efimov effects in ultracold atom mixtures such as 40K-6Li, and the interlayer/interwire Efimov trimers described here are predicted to be stable against three-body recombination, potentially allowing spectroscopy of the Efimov spectrum for the first time.
