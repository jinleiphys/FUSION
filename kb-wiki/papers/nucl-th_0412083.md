# Energy Centroids of Spin $I$ States by Random Two-body Interactions

> In this paper we study the behavior of energy centroids (denoted as $\bar{E_I}$) of spin $I$ states in the presence of random two-body interactions, for systems ranging from very simple systems (e.g. single-$j$ shell for very small $j$) to very complicated systems (e.g., many-$j$ shells with different parities and with isospin degree of freedom). Regularities of $\bar{E_I}$'s discussed in terms of the so-called geometric chaoticity (or quasi-randomness of two-body coefficients of fractional parentage) in earlier works are found to hold even for very simple systems in which one cannot assume the geometric chaoticity. It is shown that the inclusion of isospin and parity does not "break" the regularities of $\bar{E_I}$'s.

# Energy Centroids of Spin I States by Random Two-body Interactions

## Key claim

The regularities of energy centroids of spin I states under random two-body interactions, that the probability of a given spin centroid being lowest is large only for extreme spins, and that the averaged centroid follows an I(I+1) dependence, hold even for very simple systems where the two-body coefficients of fractional parentage cannot be assumed random, and remain robust when parity and isospin degrees of freedom are included.

## Method

The authors used the general shell model Hamiltonian with random two-body matrix elements (TBRE) following the ensemble of Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1999). They computed energy centroids \(\overline{E_I}\) for each spin I and the probability \(\mathcal{P}(I)\) that a given centroid is the lowest among all spins, averaging over 1000 sets of TBRE Hamiltonians. Systems studied ranged from single-j shells (j=5/2, 7/2) and bosons (l=1, 2) to many-j shells with parity and isospin, including mixed proton-neutron systems.

## Key numbers

- For four identical nucleons in two-j shells \(j_1^\pi=5/2^+\) and \(j_2^\pi=3/2^-\): \(\sum_{I^+} \mathcal{P}(I^+) = 41.3\%\) and \(\sum_{I^-} \mathcal{P}(I^-) = 58.7\%\); \(C^+ = 0.0372 \pm 0.0017\) and \(C^- = 0.0359 \pm 0.0029\) for the \(\langle \overline{E}_{I^\pm} \rangle_{\text{min}} \simeq C^\pm I^\pm(I^\pm+1)\) relation.
- For four identical nucleons in \(d_{3/2}d_{5/2}\) shells: \(C = 0.0401 \pm 0.0017\).
- For systems with isospin in \(s_{1/2}d_{3/2}d_{5/2}\) shells: \(C = 0.0354 \pm 0.0003\) for \((n_p,n_n)=(4,4)\); \(C = 0.0341 \pm 0.0001\) for (4,5); \(C = 0.0350 \pm 0.0001\) for (4,6); \(C = 0.0341 \pm 0.0002\) for (6,6).
- For systems with isospin in fictitious two-j shells \(d_{3/2}d_{5/2}\): \(C = 0.0331 \pm 0.0002\) for \((n_p,n_n)=(2,4)\); \(C = 0.0331 \pm 0.0001\) for (4,3); \(C = 0.0338 \pm 0.0001\) for (4,4).
- For d-boson systems, the predicted C from a simplified analytic model was \(-0.0806\), while TBRE gave C \(\sim -0.73\) when \(\overline{E}_{I\simeq I_{\text{max}}}\) is lowest and \(\sim 0.070\) when \(\overline{E}_{I\simeq I_{\text{min}}}\) is lowest.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Arima and Zhao (2000) and Zhao et al. (2001) that established regularities of energy centroids under random interactions, which had previously been explained by the quasi-randomness of two-body coefficients of fractional parentage. The authors show these regularities persist in simple systems where such randomness cannot be assumed, and they generalize the results to include parity and isospin degrees of freedom for the first time. The paper also identifies a systematic discrepancy between the C values predicted by the statistical approach of Mulhall and Kota (2003) and those obtained numerically, leaving the origin of the regularities as an open question for future work.
