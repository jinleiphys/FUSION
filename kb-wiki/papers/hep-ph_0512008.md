# Strong one-pion decay of ground state charmed baryons

> We evaluate the widths for the strong decays $Σ_c\toΛ_c π$, $Σ_c^*\toΛ_c π$ and $Ξ_c^{*}\toΞ_c π$. The calculations have been done within a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with wave functions that take advantage of the constraints imposed by heavy quark symmetry. Partial conservation of axial current hypothesis allows us to determine the strong vertices from an analysis of the weak axial current matrix elements. Our results are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate strong decay widths for charmed baryons using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with wave functions constrained by heavy quark symmetry, finding good overall agreement with experimental data; however, the central values for some decays (e.g., \(\Sigma_c^{*\,++}\to\Lambda_c^+\pi^+\)) lie above the experimental central values, and agreement depends on the specific quark-quark potential used.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body problem with a variational ansatz constrained by heavy quark symmetry to obtain baryon wave functions, using five different quark-quark potentials (including confining, Coulomb, and hyperfine terms from one-gluon exchange). They evaluate the pion emission amplitude via a one-quark emission model, relating it through partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) to weak axial current matrix elements computed from the wave functions. The resulting widths are averaged over the five potentials, with the spread reported as the first error and a numerical error as the second.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma(\Sigma_c^{++}\to\Lambda_c^+\pi^+) = 2.41 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.02\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental values \(2.3 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.3\) MeV (CLEO 2002) and \(2.05^{+0.41}_{-0.38} \pm 0.38\) MeV (FOCUS 2002).
- \(\Gamma(\Sigma_c^{0}\to\Lambda_c^+\pi^-) = 2.37 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.02\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental values \(2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.3\) MeV (CLEO 2002) and \(1.55^{+0.41}_{-0.37} \pm 0.38\) MeV (FOCUS 2002).
- \(\Gamma(\Sigma_c^{*\,++}\to\Lambda_c^+\pi^+) = 17.52 \pm 0.74 \pm 0.12\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental value \(14.1^{+1.6}_{-1.5} \pm 1.4\) MeV (CLEO 2005).
- \(\Gamma(\Sigma_c^{*\,0}\to\Lambda_c^+\pi^-) = 16.90 \pm 0.71 \pm 0.12\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental value \(16.6^{+1.9}_{-1.7} \pm 1.4\) MeV (CLEO 2005).
- \(\Gamma(\Xi_c^{*\,+}\to\Xi_c^0\pi^++\Xi_c^+\pi^0) = 3.18 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.01\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental upper bound \(< 3.1\) MeV (CLEO 1996, 90% C.L.).
- \(\Gamma(\Xi_c^{*\,0}\to\Xi_c^+\pi^-+\Xi_c^0\pi^0) = 3.03 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.01\) MeV (this work), compared to experimental upper bound \(< 5.5\) MeV (CLEO 1995, 90% C.L.).
- Variations in decay widths across the five quark-quark potentials are at the level of \(6-8\%\).

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic constituent quark model calculations (Rosner 1995; Pirjol and Yan 1997) by providing a fully dynamical calculation of these strong decay widths for the first time within a nonrelativistic approach, using wave functions constrained by heavy quark symmetry. The results are compared to experimental data from the CLEO Collaboration (2001, 2002, 2005) and the FOCUS Collaboration (2002), as well as to other theoretical approaches including heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (Yan 1992; Huang 1995; Cheng 1997), light-front quark models (Tawfiq 1998), and relativistic three-quark models (Ivanov 1998-1999). The agreement is generally better than most other models, though some central values exceed experimental bounds for certain potentials.
