# Pygmies, giants, and skins as laboratory constraints on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter

> Laboratory experiments sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy may place stringent constraints on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter and, thus, on the structure, dynamics, and composition of neutron stars. Understanding the equation of state of neutron-rich matter is a central goal of nuclear physics that cuts across a variety of disciplines. In this contribution I focus on how laboratory experiments on neutron skins and on both Pygmy and Giant resonances can help us elucidate the structure of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Laboratory measurements of neutron skins and electric dipole polarizability in neutron-rich nuclei can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which in turn determines neutron-star radii; however, the correlation between these observables is model-dependent, with a correlation coefficient of only 0.77 when 48 different nuclear-structure models are considered, meaning that a multi-prong approach combining several measurements is needed to reliably constrain the equation of state.

## Method

The author uses relativistic mean-field models (NL3, FSU, IU-FSU) to compute the equation of state of neutron-rich matter and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron-star structure. The electric dipole polarizability is extracted from the inverse energy-weighted sum of the dipole strength distribution, and correlations are computed between the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and various observables using covariance analysis based on the FSU interaction. The pygmy dipole resonance contribution is isolated by separating low-energy from high-energy dipole response at an ad-hoc energy threshold.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb measured by PREX: r_skin = 0.33 +0.16 -0.18 fm (with a goal of ±0.05 fm for the next run)
- Correlation coefficient between neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and electric dipole polarizability across 48 models: 0.77
- Correlation coefficient between neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and electric dipole polarizability within the FSU model: approximately 0.9
- Pygmy dipole resonance in 68Ni accounts for about 20-25% of the inverse energy-weighted sum (m_{-1}) which is proportional to the dipole polarizability

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the neutron-star equation of state through laboratory experiments, building on the PREX measurement of the neutron skin in 208Pb (Abrahamyan 2012, Horowitz 2012) and the high-resolution measurement of electric dipole polarizability in 208Pb at RCNP (Tamii 2011, Poltoratska 2012). The paper shows that combining neutron-skin measurements with electric dipole polarizability measurements on multiple nuclei can rule out models with either very soft or very stiff symmetry energy, and enables future work at FRIB to study the transition from the uniform liquid core to the non-uniform stellar crust, including exotic phases like nuclear pasta.
