# Deuteron: properties and analytical forms of wave function in coordinate space

> Static parameters of the deuteron, obtained by the wave functions for various potential models, have been chronologically systematized. The presence or absence of knots near the origin of coordinates for the radial wave function of the deuteron have been shown. Analytical forms for the deuteron wave function in coordinate space have been reviewed. Both analytical forms and parameterizations of the deuteron wave function, which are necessary for further calculations of the characteristics of the processes involving the deuteron, have been provided. In addition, the asymptotic behaviors of deuteron wave function near the origin of coordinates and for large values of distance have been analyzed in the paper. Minimization of the number of numerically calculated coefficients for new analytical forms as a product of exponential function r^n by the sum of the exponential terms Ai*exp(-ai*r^3) have been done. The optimum is N=7-10.

# Deuteron: properties and analytical forms of wave function in coordinate space

## Key claim
The paper systematically catalogs deuteron static properties and wave function parameterizations from many potential models, but notes that the presence of knots (nodes) in the radial wave functions near the origin for some potentials (e.g., OBE, Bonn, Soft core Reid68, Moscow, renormalized chiral potentials) may indicate either numerical algorithm inaccuracies or features of the potential models themselves, rather than physical predictions for the ground state.

## Method
The author compiled a chronological table of deuteron static properties (binding energy, radius, quadrupole moment, D-state probability, D/S ratio, asymptotic normalization constant) calculated from dozens of potential models and wave function parameterizations from 1940 to 2015. Analytical forms of the deuteron wave function in coordinate space were reviewed, including new parameterizations of the form r^n multiplied by a sum of exponential terms A_i * exp(-a_i * r^3), with minimization of the number of numerically calculated coefficients.

## Key numbers
- Experimental deuteron binding energy: 2.22456612(48) MeV (Garcon 2001)
- Experimental deuteron matter radius: 1.975(3) fm (Garcon 2001)
- Experimental quadrupole moment: 0.2859(3) fm^2 (Garcon 2001)
- Experimental D/S ratio eta: 0.0256(4) (Garcon 2001)
- Experimental magnetic moment: 0.8574382308(72) nuclear magnetons (Takigawa 2017)
- Optimum number of terms N for new analytical forms (r^n * sum A_i * exp(-a_i * r^3)): N = 7-10
- Soft core Reid68 (1968): binding energy 2.2246 MeV, quadrupole moment 0.27964 fm^2, D-state probability 6.4696%, D/S ratio 0.02622, asymptotic normalization constant 0.87758 fm^{-1/2}
- Argonne v18 (1995): binding energy 2.22457 MeV, matter radius 1.967 fm, quadrupole moment 0.270 fm^2, D-state probability 5.76%, D/S ratio 0.0250, asymptotic normalization constant 0.8850 fm^{-1/2}
- CD-Bonn (2001): binding energy 2.224575 MeV, matter radius 1.966 fm, quadrupole moment 0.270 fm^2, D-state probability 4.85%, D/S ratio 0.0256, asymptotic normalization constant 0.8846 fm^{-1/2}
- Knot positions for radial wave functions vary: e.g., Soft core Reid68 has knots at 0.01 fm for both u(r) and w(r); Moscow potentials have knots at 0.5 fm for u(r); some potentials (e.g., Paris, Argonne v18, Nijmegen group) show no knots

## Context
This work extends the systematic compilation of deuteron properties from earlier reviews and provides a unified reference for analytical wave function forms needed in calculations of polarization observables and form factors. It builds on decades of potential model development including the Reid (1968), Hamada-Johnston (1962), Paris (1980), Argonne v18 (Wiringa 1995), CD-Bonn (Machleidt 2001), Nijmegen group potentials (Stoks 1994), and chiral effective field theory potentials (Epelbaum 2005, 2015). The paper enables future calculations of deuteron form factors, tensor polarization, and spin observables in dp scattering by providing parameterized wave functions that avoid the inconvenience of tabulated numerical arrays.
