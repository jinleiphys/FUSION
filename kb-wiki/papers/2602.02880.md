# Contact interaction treatment of the nucleon Faddeev equation

> Working with a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector $\otimes$ vector contact interaction (SCI), a largely algebraic three-body Faddeev equation treatment of the nucleon bound state problem is introduced and used to deliver results for all nucleon charge and magnetisation distributions and their flavour separation. A strength of the SCI treatment is that it provides for a transparent understanding of this three-body approach to developing predictions for baryon observables. Comparisons of SCI results with predictions obtained in realistic-interaction Faddeev equation studies reveal the sensitivities of given observable to phenomena associated with the emergence of hadron mass.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector⊗vector contact interaction (SCI) to solve the three-body Faddeev equation for the nucleon, delivering results for all nucleon charge and magnetisation distributions and their flavour separation; however, the SCI is not a precision tool and its predictions serve primarily to reveal sensitivities of observables to the pointwise behaviour of the quark-quark interaction and phenomena associated with the emergence of hadron mass, with the caveat that SCI results must differ from those produced by QCD-connected interactions for observables sensitive to momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors work at leading-order in rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, using a contact interaction defined by a momentum-independent gluon propagator with mass scale m_G = 0.5 GeV and coupling α_IR = 0.36π. The three-body Faddeev equation is solved algebraically by exploiting the fact that the SCI makes the solution independent of quark+quark relative momenta, reducing the amplitude to 8 basis vectors (4 Dirac structures × 2 energy projections) with expansion coefficients determined by solving 16 coupled equations that reduce to 3 independent coefficients under S₃ permutation symmetry. The regularisation scheme uses proper-time regularisation with an infrared cutoff Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV and an ultraviolet cutoff Λ_uv = 0.91 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Dressed quark mass M = 0.36 GeV, obtained from the gap equation with current quark mass m = 0.007 GeV
- Pion mass m_π = 0.14 GeV and pion decay constant f_π = 0.10 GeV, used to fix the SCI parameters
- Nucleon mass m_N = 0.94 GeV, achieved by reducing the quark+quark coupling in the Faddeev equation to α_IR^N = 0.75 × α_IR (i.e., α_IR^N = 0.75 × 0.36π)
- With the full coupling α_IR (no reduction), the SCI Faddeev equation yields m_N = 0.67 GeV
- Infrared cutoff Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV, corresponding to 1/Λ_ir = 0.82 fm, stated to be in fair agreement with proton electromagnetic radii
- Ultraviolet cutoff Λ_uv = 0.91 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SCI approach, previously applied to mesons and the q(qq) picture of baryons (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2011, Wilson 2011, Segovia 2013), to the direct three-body Faddeev equation treatment of the nucleon. It follows earlier three-body Faddeev calculations using realistic interactions (Eichmann 2009, Sanchis-Alepuz 2011, Eichmann 2011, Yao 2024) and enables systematic comparisons between SCI and QCD-connected interaction results to map the sensitivity of nucleon observables to the momentum dependence of the quark-quark interaction. The paper provides a baseline for future studies of more complex baryon systems where the algebraic simplicity of the SCI can aid in identifying the most worthwhile problems for realistic three-body calculations.
