# Short-range correlations in low-lying nuclear excited states

> The electromagnetic transitions to various low-lying excited states of 16O, 48Ca and 208Pb are calculated within a model which considers the short-range correlations. In general the effects of the correlations are small and do not explain the required quenching to describe the data.

# Short-range correlations in low-lying nuclear excited states

## Key claim
Short-range correlations, modeled via a scalar Jastrow correlation function within a Correlated Basis Function approach, produce only small effects on the calculated electromagnetic transition form factors for low-lying excited states of ¹⁶O, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb, and therefore cannot explain the quenching needed to bring theoretical results into agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The authors used a Correlated Basis Function (CBF) ansatz where both ground and excited states are obtained by applying a scalar Jastrow correlation function to Slater determinants of single-particle wave functions. A cluster expansion retaining only terms linear in the correlation function was employed, and the single-particle wave functions and correlations were taken from variational energy-minimization calculations using the semi-realistic S3 interaction of Afnan and Tang. The model was applied to compute electron scattering form factors and cross sections for non-collective states dominated by one or at most two particle-hole excitations, selected via discrete RPA calculations using two different interactions.

## Key numbers
- For the 4⁻ state in ¹⁶O, the quenching factor Q for the correlated calculation is smaller than for the independent particle model (IPM), indicating that correlations enhance the cross section maximum in this case.
- For the 12⁺ state in ²⁰⁸Pb, the difference in the lowering produced by correlations between the two scattering angles shown in panels (a) and (b) is within 1%.
- The quenching factors required to reproduce the data for the 12⁺ state at two different scattering angles are statistically compatible once experimental uncertainties are considered.
- The χ² per datum values after applying quenching factors are reported in Table I (not shown in full text) for both IPM and correlated calculations across all states studied.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlation effects on ground-state charge densities (Co' 1995, Arias de Saavedra 1997) and nuclear matter quasi-elastic responses (Amaro 1998) to the case of low-lying excited states. The results contradict the proposal by Pandharipande (1984) that short-range correlations could explain the quenching of form factors, and instead support the findings of Benhar (1990) that occupation numbers from microscopic nuclear matter calculations remain close to IPM values. The authors suggest that the origin of the quenching should be sought in the coupling of single-particle excitations with collective nuclear modes, as their previous RPA work (Lallena 1988) showed that small-amplitude particle-hole pairs can heavily modify form factors.
