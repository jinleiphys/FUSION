# Effects of retarded electrical fields on observables sensitive to the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Within the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU11, we examine the relativistic retardation effects of electrical fields on the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio and neutron-proton differential transverse flow in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. Compared to the static Coulomb fields, the retarded electric fields of fast-moving charges are known to be anisotropic and the associated relativistic corrections can be significant. They are found to increase the number of energetic protons in the participant region at the maximum compression by as much as 25\% but that of energetic neutrons by less than 10\% in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. Consequently, more $π^{+}$ and relatively less $π^{-}$ mesons are produced, leading to an appreciable reduction of the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio compared to calculations with the static Coulomb fields. Also, the neutron-proton differential transverse flow, as another sensitive probe of high-density symmetry energy, is also decreased appreciably due to the stronger retarded electrical fields in directions perpendicular to the velocities of fast-moving charges compared to calculations using the isotropic static electrical fields. Moreover, the retardation effects on these observables are found to be approximately independent of the reaction impact parameter.

## Key claim

The relativistic retardation of electric fields (using the Lienard-Wiechert formalism instead of static Coulomb fields) reduces the predicted pion ratio and neutron-proton differential transverse flow by appreciable amounts (approximately 8% for the pion ratio), but these effects are approximately independent of the reaction impact parameter and the symmetry energy parameter x, meaning they constitute a systematic correction that must be applied alongside other known effects before drawing conclusions about the high-density symmetry energy from comparisons with experimental data.

## Method

The authors incorporated the full Lienard-Wiechert retarded electric fields into the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model IBUU11, saving the phase-space histories of all charged particles to compute the retarded fields at each time step. They simulated central and peripheral collisions of Au-197 on Au-197 at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon, using a range of symmetry energy parameters (x from -2 to 2) that produce different density dependences of the symmetry energy while keeping symmetric nuclear matter properties fixed. They compared the time evolution of the dynamic pion ratio, final pion multiplicities, and neutron-proton differential transverse flow between calculations using the retarded fields and those using the standard static Coulomb fields.

## Key numbers

- The number of energetic protons in the participant region at maximum compression is increased by as much as 25% when using retarded fields compared to static fields, while the number of energetic neutrons is increased by less than 10%.
- The final pion ratio is reduced by about 8% in calculations with retarded electric fields compared to those with static fields, approximately independent of the symmetry energy parameter x.
- The multiplicity of pi+ is increased by about 14% with retarded fields, while the multiplicity of pi- is increased by less than 5%.
- The beam energy used is 400 MeV/nucleon for Au-197+Au-197 reactions, where proton velocities reach about 0.7c.
- The saturation density used is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with a symmetry energy of 30.0 MeV at that density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the IBUU11 transport model that examined various uncertain aspects of pion production, including in-medium pion potential, the isovector potential of Delta(1232) resonances, neutron-skin effects (Wei 2014), and tensor-force-induced short-range correlations (Bao 2015, Yong 2016). Prior transport model studies (Li 2002, DiToro 2005, Xiao 2009, Feng 2010, Xu and Ko 2010, Guo 2013, Hong 2014, Song 2015, Wei 2016) had shown that predictions for the pion ratio remain too model-dependent to draw strong conclusions about the high-density symmetry energy. The present work shows that the relativistic retardation of electric fields is an additional systematic effect that must be included, together with the other effects mentioned, before a final conclusion regarding the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy can be reached from comparisons with FOPI collaboration data.
