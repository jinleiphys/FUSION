# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Pion Scattering from Li

> We show that the neutron and proton transition densities predicted by recent quantum Monte Carlo calculations for A=6,7 nuclei are consistent with pion scattering from 6Li and 7Li at energies near the Delta resonance. This has provided a microscopic understanding of the enhancement factors for quadrople excitations, which were needed to describe pion inelastic scattering within the nuclear shell model of Cohen and Kurath.

# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Pion Scattering from Li

## Key claim

The neutron and proton transition densities from Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations for A=6,7 nuclei are consistent with pion scattering data from 6Li and 7Li near the Delta resonance, without requiring the empirical enhancement factors for quadrupole excitations that were necessary in the shell-model approach of Cohen and Kurath. The agreement holds specifically for both pi+ and pi- scattering on 7Li, which tests the predicted neutron and proton transition densities separately.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions from the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian (Argonne v18 two-nucleon plus Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials) to compute neutron and proton transition densities for 6Li and 7Li. These densities were then used as input to Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) calculations of pion elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections at energies near the Delta resonance (100-240 MeV). The calculations contain no adjustable parameters, unlike previous shell-model work which required enhancement factors of 1.75-2.5 for quadrupole transitions.

## Key numbers

- For 6Li(0+ to 3+): QMC B(E2) = 21.1 ± 0.4 e^2·fm^4 (proton and neutron), compared to experimental 21.8 ± 4.8 e^2·fm^4
- For 7Li(3/2- to 1/2-): QMC proton B(E2) = 5.7 ± 0.1 e^2·fm^4, neutron B(E2) = 16.5 ± 0.3 e^2·fm^4, experimental proton = 7.59 ± 0.10 e^2·fm^4
- For 7Li(3/2- to 7/2-): QMC proton B(E2) = 13.2 ± 0.2 e^2·fm^4, neutron B(E2) = 34.6 ± 0.5 e^2·fm^4, experimental proton = 15.5 ± 0.8 e^2·fm^4
- For 7Li(3/2- to 5/2-): QMC proton B(E2) = 2.3 ± 0.1 e^2·fm^4, neutron B(E2) = 5.4 ± 0.2 e^2·fm^4, experimental proton = 4.1 ± 2.0 e^2·fm^4
- At E_pi = 164 MeV, the ratio |t_pi+p / t_pi+n| = |t_pi-n / t_pi-p| is approximately 3
- The AV18/UIX model underbinds 6Li and 7Li by 2-5% in GFMC calculations; VMC energies are 10% above GFMC for A=6,7
- Without enhancement factors, shell-model predictions would be a factor of about 5 lower than data for all 7Li transitions considered

## Context

This work extends the QMC approach previously applied to electroweak processes (Wiringa and Schiavilla 1998; Lapikas, Wesseling, and Wiringa 1999) to test nuclear wave functions against pion scattering, which probes both neutron and proton transition densities separately due to the strong isospin dependence of the pion-nucleon interaction. It directly addresses the long-standing problem in the shell-model approach of Lee and Kurath (1980), where empirical enhancement factors (E_N ~ 2 for quadrupole excitations) were needed to describe pion inelastic scattering from 1p-shell nuclei. The results enable future studies of pion-nucleus reactions on larger 1p-shell targets (A=9,10) and more complex processes such as pion absorption and double-charge-exchange reactions, using QMC wave functions that correctly account for nuclear correlations.
