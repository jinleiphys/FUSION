# Multi-particle correlations in qp-Bose gas model

> The approach based on multimode system of q-deformed oscillators and the related picture of ideal gas of q-bosons enables to effectively describe the observed non-Bose type behaviour, in experiments on heavy-ion collisions, of the intercept (or the "strength") $λ$ of the two-particle correlation function of identical pions or kaons. In this paper we extend main results of that approach in the two aspects: first, we derive in explicit form the intercepts of n-particle correlation functions in the case of q-Bose gas model and, second, provide their explicit two-parameter (or qp-) generalization.

## Key claim

The paper extends the q-Bose gas model to derive explicit formulas for the intercepts of n-particle correlation functions (for n >= 3) and provides their two-parameter (qp-) generalization, with the caveat that the asymptotic values of these intercepts depend only on the deformation parameters and not on momentum or temperature.

## Method

The authors use multimode systems of q-deformed oscillators (Arik-Coon and Biedenharn-Macfarlane types) and their qp-generalization to model an ideal gas of q-bosons. They derive n-particle monomode distribution functions using thermal averages with the Hamiltonian H = sum_i omega_i N_i, then compute the intercept lambda^(n) as the ratio of the n-particle distribution to the n-th power of the single-particle distribution, minus one. The derivation proceeds via induction and recursion relations for the thermal averages of deformed oscillator products.

## Key numbers

* For AC-type q-bosons, the two-particle intercept is lambda_i = q (e^(beta omega_i) - 1) / (e^(beta omega_i) - q^2), with the asymptotic limit lambda = q for large momentum or low temperature.
* For AC-type q-bosons, the three-particle intercept is lambda^(3)_AC = (1+q)(1+q+q^2)(e^(beta omega)-q)^2 / ((e^(beta omega)-q^2)(e^(beta omega)-q^3)) - 1.
* For AC-type q-bosons, the n-particle distribution function is <(a_i^dag)^n (a_i)^n> = floor(n)! / product_{r=1}^n (e^(beta omega_i) - q^r), where floor(m) = (1-q^m)/(1-q).
* For qp-bosons, the n-particle distribution function is <(A_i^dag)^n (A_i)^n> = [[n]]_{qp}! (e^(beta omega_i)-1) / product_{r=0}^n (e^(beta omega_i) - q^r p^{n-r}), where [[m]]_{qp} = (q^m - p^m)/(q-p).
* The asymptotic n-particle intercept for qp-bosons is lambda^(n,asympt)_{q,p} = -1 + [[n]]_{qp}! = -1 + product_{k=1}^n (sum_{r=0}^k q^r p^{k-r}).

## Context

This work extends the q-Bose gas model previously developed by Anchishkin, Gavrilik, and Iorgov (2000) for two-particle correlations, which successfully described the intercept parameter of two-pion correlations from STAR/RHIC data. The paper generalizes these results to n-particle correlations, motivated by experiments such as those by Adams et al. (2003) on three-pion HBT correlations from STAR, and by theoretical work by Heinz and Zhang (1997) and Csorgo (2002) on higher-order correlations. The derived formulas enable direct comparison with experimental data for 3-particle and higher-order correlations of pions and kaons from heavy-ion collisions, potentially revealing the physical meaning and admissible values of the deformation parameters p and q.
