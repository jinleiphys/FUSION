# pi-pi Scattering in Twisted Mass Chiral Perturbation Theory

> In this report we describe both I=2 and I=0 pi-pi scattering for twisted mass lattice QCD utilizing twisted mass chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. Focusing on the lattice spacing (b) corrections, we demonstrate that in the exotic I=2, I_3=+-2 channels, the leading scaling violations of pi-pi scattering at maximal twist begin at O(m_pi^2 b^2). This is not the case in any other isospin channel, for which the scaling violations at maximal twist begin at O(b^2). Furthermore, we demonstrate the existence of a mixing between the I=2, I_3 = 0 and I=0 scattering channels due to the breaking of isospin symmetry by the twisted mass term. The mixing term, although formally next-to-leading order, is relatively large, thus necessitating the use of a coupled channel analysis. We argue that this mixing likely renders the computation of the I=0 channel impractical with twisted mass lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for twisted mass lattice QCD at maximal twist, the leading discretization errors for the exotic I=2, I_3=±2 ππ scattering channels begin at O(m_π² b²) rather than O(b²), but this cancellation does not hold for any other isospin channel; furthermore, a relatively large mixing between the I=2, I_3=0 and I=0 scattering channels arises from isospin symmetry breaking by the twisted mass term, which the authors argue likely renders the computation of the I=0 channel impractical.

## Method

The authors construct the twisted mass chiral Lagrangian at next-to-leading order (NLO) using the two-step Symanzik procedure of Sharpe (1998), incorporating discretization effects proportional to the lattice spacing b. They expand the Lagrangian around the physical vacuum, derive the Feynman rules for two-, three-, and four-pion interactions arising from lattice spacing corrections, and compute the ππ scattering amplitudes for the I=2, I_3=±2 channels and the I_3=0 channels (both I=2 and I=0). The scattering parameters are expressed in terms of lattice-physical pion mass and decay constant to remove unphysical counterterms through NLO.

## Key numbers

- At maximal twist (ω=π/2), the leading discretization corrections to the I=2, I_3=±2 scattering length vanish identically: Δ m_π a_{ππ}^{I=2,±2}(π/2) = 0.
- At the lightest mass point from Boucaud (2007, 2008) with m_π ≈ 300 MeV, the pion mass splitting is Δ M'_0(ω∼π/2) / m_π² ≈ -0.33.
- For current twisted mass lattice calculations, the correction to the I=2, I_3=±2 scattering length near maximal twist is negligible provided cot ω ≥ 0.3 (making less than a 1% correction).
- The mixing term between I=2, I_3=0 and I=0 states at maximal twist is estimated to be approximately 1/3 the size of the leading-order amplitude when m_π / f_π = 2.

## Context

This work extends the twisted mass chiral perturbation theory framework of Sharpe (2004, 2005) and Aoki (2004) to ππ scattering, building on the continuum NLO calculations of Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) and the NNLO work of Knecht (1995) and Bijnens (1995, 1997). The results for the I=2, I_3=±2 channel at zero twist reduce to those of Buchoff (2008) for Wilson fermions and agree with Aoki (2008). The paper identifies that the I=0 channel, previously computed with other lattice actions by Beane (2005, 2007) and Yamazaki (2004), faces severe complications from isospin mixing in twisted mass QCD, necessitating a coupled-channel Lüscher analysis that the authors argue may be prohibitively difficult.
