# Nuclear matrix elements from lattice QCD for electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes

> Over the last decade, numerical solutions of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) using the technique of lattice QCD have developed to a point where they are beginning to connect fundamental aspects of nuclear physics to the underlying degrees of freedom of the Standard Model. In this review, the progress of lattice QCD studies of nuclear matrix elements of electroweak currents and beyond-Standard-Model operators is summarized, and connections with effective field theories and nuclear models are outlined. Lattice QCD calculations of nuclear matrix elements can provide guidance for low-energy nuclear reactions in astrophysics, dark matter direct detection experiments, and experimental searches for violations of the symmetries of the Standard Model, including searches for additional CP violation in the hadronic and leptonic sectors, baryon-number violation, and lepton-number or flavor violation. Similarly, important inputs to neutrino experiments seeking to determine the neutrino-mass hierarchy and oscillation parameters, as well as other electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes can be determined. The phenomenological implications of existing studies of electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model matrix elements in light nuclear systems are discussed, and future prospects for the field toward precision studies of these matrix elements are outlined.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nuclear matrix elements for electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes are only just beginning to achieve controlled results, with the first studies performed at unphysical quark masses and with significant uncertainties; the field requires sustained exascale computing resources to reach the precision needed to impact nuclear and high-energy physics.

## Method

The authors review the lattice QCD framework for computing nuclear matrix elements, which involves discretizing the QCD action on a four-dimensional spacetime lattice, performing Monte Carlo integration over gauge-field configurations using importance sampling, and evaluating correlation functions of quark and gluon operators. The review covers multiple fermion discretizations (Wilson, twisted-mass, staggered, domain-wall, and overlap fermions) and describes how nuclear observables are extracted from Euclidean correlation functions computed on ensembles of gauge configurations generated with hybrid Monte Carlo algorithms.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass recovered with better than percent-level precision including both QCD and QED interactions (Borsanyi 2014)
- State-of-the-art calculations target lattice volumes as large as (L/a)^3 x (T/a) >= 128^3 x 256 with lattice spacing a ~ 0.05 fm
- Calculations involve O(10^10) degrees of freedom
- Statistical uncertainty decreases as O(1/sqrt(N_cfg)) as the number of configurations N_cfg becomes large

## Context

This review extends the program of lattice QCD studies of single-hadron properties (summarized in the FLAG review, Aoki 2019) to nuclear systems with baryon number greater than one, building on earlier work by Beane (2008, 2010, 2014) and others who established the framework for two-baryon and light-nuclei calculations. The review connects to effective field theory approaches such as chiral perturbation theory and nuclear many-body techniques (Carlson 2014, Meissner 2015, Hammer 2019, Epelbaum 2019, Tews 2020), and enables future precision determinations of nuclear matrix elements needed for dark matter direct detection experiments, neutrino experiments, and searches for beyond-Standard-Model physics.
