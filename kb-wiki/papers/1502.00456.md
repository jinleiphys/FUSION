# Top-down Holographic Glueball Decay Rates

> We present new results on the decay patterns of scalar and tensor glueballs in the top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. This model, which has only one free dimensionless parameter, gives semi-quantitative predictions for the vector meson spectrum, their decay widths, and also a gluon condensate in agreement with SVZ sum rules. The holographic predictions for scalar glueball decay rates are compared with experimental data for the widely discussed gluon candidates f0(1500) and f0(1710).

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model predicts that the predominantly dilatonic scalar glueball mode (mass 1487 MeV) has a decay pattern that matches the glueball candidate f0(1710) better than f0(1500), but only if one discards the model's lowest scalar mode (855 MeV) as an artifact of the supergravity approximation; the match to f0(1710) requires assuming nearly unmixed glueball states and leaves the four-pion decay channel uncomfortably large.

## Method

The authors computed glueball decay rates by including the scalar and tensor glueball fluctuation modes of the 11-dimensional supergravity background into the action of the flavor D8 and anti-D8 probe branes in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. They expanded the Dirac-Born-Infeld action to quadratic order in the flavor gauge fields and extracted the interaction Lagrangians for glueball couplings to pions, kaons, eta mesons, and vector mesons. The mass scale MKK was fixed to 949 MeV using the experimental rho meson mass, and the 't Hooft coupling lambda was varied between 16.63 and 12.55 to produce a range of predictions.

## Key numbers

- Lowest scalar (exotic) glueball mass: 855 MeV
- Predominantly dilatonic scalar glueball mass: 1487 MeV
- Tensor glueball mass: 1487 MeV
- Relative decay width Gamma/M for dilatonic scalar to two pions: 0.009 to 0.012 (for lambda = 16.63 to 12.55)
- Relative decay width Gamma/M for tensor glueball to two pions: 0.015 to 0.019
- Relative decay width Gamma/M for exotic scalar to two pions: 0.092 to 0.122
- Experimental total relative width of f0(1500): 0.072(5)
- Experimental total relative width of f0(1710): 0.078(4)
- Holographic prediction for f0(1710) total relative width (dilatonic mode at 1722 MeV): 0.059 to 0.076
- Holographic prediction for f0(1500) decay to four pions: 0.003 to 0.005 (experimental: 0.036(3))
- Holographic prediction for f0(1710) decay to four pions (at 1722 MeV): 0.024 to 0.030

## Context

This work extends the earlier holographic glueball decay calculation of Hashimoto (2007) by including the predominantly dilatonic scalar mode and the tensor mode, correcting coefficients, and adding decays into four pions, two kaons, and two eta mesons. The results contradict the pure-glueball interpretation of f0(1500) favored by some phenomenological analyses (e.g., Close 2001, Giacosa 2005) but are more consistent with f0(1710) as a nearly unmixed glueball, as suggested by lattice studies (Gregory 2012) and linear sigma model analyses (Janowski 2014). The work enables future studies of glueball-meson mixing in holographic QCD and motivates deformations of the model away from the chiral limit to incorporate finite quark masses.
