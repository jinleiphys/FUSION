# $S$-wave $KK^*$ interaction in a finite volume and the $f_1(1285)$

> Lattice QCD simulations provide a promising way to disentangle different interpretations of hadronic resonances, which might be of particular relevance to understand the nature of the so-called $XYZ$ particles. Recent studies have shown that in addition to the well-established naive quark model picture, the axial-vector meson $f_1(1285)$ can also be understood as a dynamically generated state built upon the $KK^*$ interaction. In this work, we calculate the energy levels of the $KK^*$ system in the $f_1(1285)$ channel in finite volume using the chiral unitary approach. We propose to calculate the loop function in the dimensional regularization scheme, which is equivalent to the hybrid approach adopted in previous studies. We also study the inverse problem of extracting the bound state information from synthetic lattice QCD data and comment on the difference between our approach and the L{\" u}scher method.

# S-wave KK* interaction in a finite volume and the f1(1285)

## Key claim
The f1(1285) meson can be understood as a dynamically generated bound state from the single-channel KK* interaction, with the meson-meson component accounting for about half of its wave function. The authors propose that the dimensional regularization scheme for the finite-volume loop function is numerically more stable than the hybrid approach used in previous studies, though both yield equivalent results when treated properly.

## Method
The authors calculate energy levels of the KK* system in the f1(1285) channel in a finite cubic box using the chiral unitary approach, where the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved with the loop function G replaced by its finite-volume counterpart. The finite-volume loop function is computed in the dimensional regularization scheme by adding a finite-volume correction δG to the infinite-volume loop function, and the results are compared with those from the hybrid approach (cutoff scheme) and the Lüscher method. An inverse problem is also studied: synthetic lattice QCD energy levels from the first two levels are fitted to extract an effective potential of the form V = a + b[s - (M_K* + m_K)^2], from which the bound state mass is recovered.

## Key numbers
- f1(1285) mass at physical point: 1286 MeV (from the inverse analysis, central value)
- f1(1285) binding energy at physical point: 102.1 MeV
- Extracted potential parameters from inverse analysis: a = -157 ± 29, b = (-1.4 ± 1.1) × 10^(-4) MeV^(-2)
- Recovered bound state mass from inverse analysis: 1286 ± 37 MeV
- Composite component fraction: -g^2 ∂G/∂s = 0.50 (meson-meson component accounts for half of the f1(1285) wave function)
- D-wave to S-wave ratio for level 2 at L m_π = 2: 0.002 (negligible mixing)
- D-wave to S-wave ratio for level 4 at L m_π = 2: 0.208 (significant mixing requiring explicit treatment)
- f1(1285) masses at six PACS-CS configurations: 1367, 1442, 1412, 1506, 1635, 1785 MeV (with binding energies 126, 136, 133, 144, 156, 160 MeV respectively)
- Regularization parameters: a(μ) = -1.85, μ = 900 MeV

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume approach of Doring (2011) to the KK* system, which was previously studied in infinite volume by Roca (2005) and Geng (2006) using chiral unitary approaches. The f1(1285) is particularly suited for lattice tests because it is a single-channel bound state of two strange mesons, unlike the a1(1260) and b1(1235) studied in N_f=2 lattice QCD by Lang (2014). The results enable future lattice QCD simulations using PACS-CS configurations (Aoki 2008) to test the dynamically generated nature of the f1(1285), and the inverse analysis method provides a model-independent way to extract binding energies from lattice data at moderate box sizes.
