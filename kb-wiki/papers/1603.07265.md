# Correlations of $\boldsymbol{πN}$ Partial Waves for Multi-Reaction Analyses

> In the search for missing baryonic resonances, many analyses include data from a variety of pion- and photon-induced reactions. For elastic $πN$ scattering, however, usually the partial waves of the SAID or other groups are fitted, instead of data. We provide the partial-wave covariance matrices needed to perform correlated $χ^2$ fits, in which the obtained $χ^2$ equals the actual $χ^2$ up non-linear and normalization corrections. For any analysis relying on partial waves extracted from elastic pion scattering, this is a prerequisite to assess the significance of resonance signals and to assign any uncertainty on results. The influence of systematic errors is also considered.

## Key claim

The paper provides partial-wave covariance matrices for elastic πN scattering single-energy (SE) amplitudes, enabling correlated χ² fits that approximate the χ² of a direct fit to experimental data up to non-linear and normalization corrections, which are shown to be negligible near the minimum.

## Method

The authors generated single-energy (SE) πN partial-wave amplitudes from the WI08 energy-dependent fit by fitting data in energy bins (2–50 MeV width) using a χ² form that includes a penalty term for overall normalization. They then computed the covariance matrix from the Hessian at the χ² minimum and provided the matrices, best-fit parameters, and χ² values for each SE solution, along with transformation formulas for converting between (δ, ρ) and (Re f, Im f) representations.

## Key numbers

- Energy bin widths vary from 2 MeV (low-energy region) to 50 MeV (highest energies where data are sparse)
- At Tπ = 90 MeV (87–92 MeV bin), the SE fit with renormalization gives χ²/data = 124/121, while the SE fit without renormalization gives χ²/data = 301/121
- Phase shifts at 90 MeV SE fit: S11 = 8.11(0.11) deg, S31 = −8.11(0.10) deg, P11 = −0.71(0.09) deg, P33 = 17.16(0.05) deg
- At (δS11, δS31) = (8.42 deg, −7.28 deg), the correlated Δχ² = 56 while the uncorrelated Δχ² = 31 (only 55% of the correlated value)
- The difference between predicted and actual Δχ² at this point is only 2 on an absolute scale of χ² = 359 (0.5% difference)
- For the normalizable SE fit, maximal deviation of actual and predicted χ² is 5% at the Δχ² = 8 contour
- The normalization factor for the differential cross section at 90 MeV is N = 0.98

## Context

This work extends the SAID single-energy analyses (WI08) by providing the covariance matrices needed for statistically meaningful fits, addressing a problem where previous multi-channel analyses (e.g., BnGa, EBAC, Giessen groups) fitted πN amplitudes with subjective or uncorrelated errors. The matrices enable baryon spectroscopy fits to quantify resonance significance, and benefit chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) and unitary CHPT studies (e.g., work by Bernard, Fettes, Meissner, Hoferichter) by allowing improved determination of low-energy constants.
