# First results on double beta decay modes of Cd, Te and Zn isotopes with the COBRA experiment

> Four 1cm^3 CdZnTe semiconductor detectors were operated in the Gran Sasso National Laboratory to explore the feasibility of such devices for double beta decay searches as proposed for the COBRA experiment. The research involved background studies accompanied by measurements of energy resolution performed at the surface. Energy resolutions sufficient to reduce the contribution of two-neutrino double beta decay events to a negligible level for a large scale experiment have already been achieved and further improvements are expected. Using activity measurements of contaminants in all construction materials a background model was developed with the help of Monte Carlo simulations and major background sources were identified. A total exposure of 4.34 kg.days of underground data has been accumulated allowing a search for neutrinoless double beta decay modes of seven isotopes found in CdZnTe. Half-life limits (90% C.L.) are presented for decays to ground and excited states. Four improved lower limits have been obtained, including zero neutrino double electron capture transitions of Zn64 and Te120 to the ground state, which are 1.19*10^{17} years and 2.68*10^{15} years respectively.

## Key claim

The COBRA collaboration demonstrated that CdZnTe semiconductor detectors can achieve energy resolutions sufficient to make the contribution of two-neutrino double beta decay events negligible for a large-scale experiment, but the current prototype results are based on only 4.34 kg·days of exposure with four small detectors, and the background model identified passivation paint on detector surfaces as the dominant background source in the 2–3 MeV region.

## Method

Four 1 cm³ CdZnTe coplanar-grid semiconductor detectors (each ~6.5 g) were operated in the Gran Sasso National Laboratory (LNGS) with ~3500 mwe overburden, shielded by copper and lead with boron-loaded polyethylene and paraffin neutron shielding. The collaboration measured radioactive contaminants in all construction materials using the LNGS Ge-detector facility, developed a background model via GEANT4 Monte Carlo simulations, and performed maximum likelihood fits to search for neutrinoless double beta decay peaks in the accumulated spectrum across three data-taking periods with different hardware configurations.

## Key numbers

- Total exposure: 4.34 kg·days for the high-threshold (>600 keV) data set and 2.36 kg·days for the low-threshold (>500 keV) data set
- Energy resolution (FWHM) at 2809 keV: 5–8% for the prototype detectors; best measured resolution 2.1% at 2614 keV with a medium-quality CZT detector
- Background rate in the 2–3 MeV range: 0.34–0.59 events/(keV kg day) depending on crystal and data period
- New lower half-life limit for ⁶⁴Zn 0ν double electron capture to ground state: 1.19×10¹⁷ years (90% C.L.), improving on the previous world best of 7.0×10¹⁶ years from ZnWO4
- New lower half-life limit for ¹²⁰Te 0ν double electron capture to ground state: 2.68×10¹⁵ years (90% C.L.), with no previous limit existing
- New lower half-life limit for ¹²⁰Te 0ν β⁺EC to ground state: 1.21×10¹⁷ years (90% C.L.), improving on the previous limit of 2.2×10¹⁶ years from Kiel (2003)
- New lower half-life limit for ¹²⁰Te 0ν double electron capture to 1171 keV excited state: 9.72×10¹⁵ years (90% C.L.), with no previous limit existing
- Half-life limit for ¹¹⁶Cd 0νββ to ground state: 3.14×10¹⁹ years (90% C.L.), compared to world best 1.7×10²³ years from Danevich (2003)
- Half-life limit for ¹³⁰Te 0νββ to ground state: 9.92×10¹⁹ years (90% C.L.), compared to world best 1.8×10²⁴ years from Cuoricino
- Half-life limit for ⁷⁰Zn 0νββ to ground state: 2.24×10¹⁷ years (90% C.L.), compared to world best 9.0×10¹⁷ years from ZnWO4
- Cooling from 24°C to 10°C improved energy resolution by a factor of two below 100 keV and by 5% at 2809 keV

## Context

This work presents the first underground operation of CdZnTe semiconductor detectors for double beta decay searches, extending the concept proposed by Zuber (2001) for the COBRA experiment. The results improve on previous half-life limits for ⁶⁴Zn and ¹²⁰Te decays measured by ZnWO4 (2005) and Kiel (2003) respectively, while the limits for ¹¹⁶Cd, ¹³⁰Te, and ¹⁰⁶Cd remain far below the world best values from larger experiments such as NEMO-3, Cuoricino, and the work of Belli (1999). The identification of passivation paint as the dominant background source enables future improvements, and the collaboration plans to deploy 64 detectors to improve all limits presented.
