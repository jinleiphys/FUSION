# Systematic study of the Chiral Magnetic Effect with the AVFD model at LHC energies

> We present a systematic study of the correlators used experimentally to probe the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) using the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model in Pb--Pb and Xe--Xe collisions at LHC energies. We find a parametrization that describes the dependence of these correlators on the value of the axial current density ($n_5/\mathrm{s}$), which dictates the CME signal, and on the parameter that governs the background in these measurements i.e., the percentage of local charge conservation (LCC) within an event. This allows to deduce the values of $n_5/\mathrm{s}$ and the LCC percentage that provide a quantitative description of the centrality dependence of the experimental measurements. We find that the results in Xe--Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.44$~TeV are consistent with a background only scenario. On the other hand, the model needs a significant non-zero value of $n_5/\mathrm{s}$ to match the measurements in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$~TeV.

## Key claim

Using the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model, the authors find that the centrality dependence of charge-dependent correlators measured in Xe–Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV is consistent with a background-only scenario, while the same data in Pb–Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV require a significantly non-zero axial current density (n5/s) to be described, implying a possible CME signal in the larger system.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study using the AVFD framework, which couples 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with a hadron cascade (UrQMD) and includes event-by-event electromagnetic fields. They calibrated the model to reproduce bulk observables (charged particle multiplicity density and elliptic flow) within 15% for both collision systems, then produced samples with varying axial current density (n5/s = 0.05, 0.07, 0.1) and varying local charge conservation (LCC) percentages (33% and 50% for Pb–Pb; 15% and 30% for Xe–Xe). The dependence of the correlators Δδ₁ and Δγ₁,₁ on n5/s and LCC was parametrized via linear and quadratic fits, allowing extraction of the parameter values that simultaneously describe the experimental centrality dependence of both correlators.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field at initial time (t₀ = 0) reaches and exceeds 10¹⁶ T for both Pb–Pb and Xe–Xe collisions at LHC energies.
- The magnetic field lifetime τ_B was set to 0.2 fm/c for both collision systems.
- For Xe–Xe collisions at √s_NN = 5.44 TeV, a constant fit to the extracted n5/s values yields 0.011 ± 0.005, consistent with zero within uncertainties.
- For Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, a constant fit to the extracted n5/s values yields 0.034 ± 0.003, significantly above zero.
- The LCC percentage needed to describe the data ranges from 40% for peripheral up to around 60% for more central Pb–Pb collisions.
- The charged particle multiplicity in Pb–Pb is 60–70% higher than the corresponding value in Xe–Xe collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous CME searches by the ALICE Collaboration (Acharya 2017, Acharya 2020) that placed upper limits on the CME contribution using Event Shape Engineering, and by the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk 2013, 2014) in Au–Au collisions at lower energies. The AVFD model approach provides a quantitative framework to simultaneously extract the CME signal strength (n5/s) and the background from local charge conservation, enabling future comparisons with data from other collision systems and energies at the LHC.
