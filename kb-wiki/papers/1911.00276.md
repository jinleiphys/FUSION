# Relativistic hybrid stars with sequential first-order phase transitions and heavy-baryon envelopes

> We compute the mass, radius and tidal deformability of stars containing phase transitions from hadronic to quark phase(s). These quantities are computed for three types of hadronic envelopes: purely nuclear, hyperonic, and $Δ$-resonance--hyperon admixed matter. We consider either a single first-order phase transition to a quark phase with a maximally stiff equation of state (EOS) or two sequential first-order phase transitions mimicking a transition from hadronic to a quark matter phase followed by a second phase transition to another quark phase. We explore the parameter space which produces low-mass twin and triplet configurations where equal-mass stars have substantially different radii and tidal deformabilities. We demonstrate that while for purely hadronic stiff EOS the obtained maximum mass is inconsistent with the upper limit on this quantity placed by GW170817, the inclusion of the hyperonic and $Δ$-resonance degrees of freedom, as well as the deconfinement phase transition at sufficiently low density, produces a configuration of stars consistent with this limit. The obtained hybrid star configurations are in the mass range relevant for the interpretation of the GW170817 event. We compare our results for the tidal deformability with the limits inferred from GW170817 showing that the onset of non-nucleonic phases, such as $Δ$-resonance--hyperon admixed phase and/or the quark phase(s), are favored by this data if the nuclear EOS is stiff. Also, we show that low-mass twins and especially triplets proliferate the number of combinations of possible types of stars that can undergo a merger event, the maximal number being six in the case of triplets. The prospects for uncovering the first-order phase transition(s) to and in quark matter via measurements of tidal deformabilities in merger events are discussed.

# Relativistic hybrid stars with sequential first-order phase transitions and heavy-baryon envelopes

## Key claim

Hybrid stars containing a first-order phase transition from hadronic to quark matter, especially when combined with hyperonic and Δ-resonance degrees of freedom in the hadronic envelope, can produce low-mass twin and triplet configurations whose maximum masses and tidal deformabilities are consistent with the constraints from GW170817, provided the phase transition occurs at sufficiently low density (around 2-3 times nuclear saturation density). The paper demonstrates that purely nucleonic stiff equations of state produce maximum masses inconsistent with the upper limit from GW170817, but the inclusion of non-nucleonic phases resolves this tension.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining three types of hadronic envelopes (purely nucleonic from the DD-ME2 functional, hyperonic, and Δ-resonance-hyperon admixed matter) with a constant-sound-speed (CSS) parametrization for one or two sequential first-order phase transitions to quark matter phases (emulating 2SC and CFL color superconducting phases). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass, radius, and tidal deformability for each configuration, imposing the condition that the maximum mass of the densest quark branch reaches 2.0 solar masses, and explore the six-dimensional parameter space to identify twin and triplet configurations where equal-mass stars have different radii and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the purely nucleonic hadronic branch: 2.48 solar masses, which exceeds the upper limit from GW170817 analysis
- Maximum mass of the hybrid star branch fixed at 2.00 solar masses for the primary analysis, with exploration up to 2.30 solar masses
- Transition densities for the phase transition range from 1.555 to 3.226 times nuclear saturation density (ρ₀) depending on hadronic envelope composition
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass star (Λ₁.₄) can be as low as approximately 50-100, corresponding to a radius of about 9.5-10.5 km in the extreme case where phase transitions occur below 2ρ₀
- Mass range for twin configurations with nucleonic envelope: 0.065 to 0.095 solar masses for M^H_max from 1.800 to 0.600 solar masses
- Mass range for triplet configurations with nucleonic envelope: 0.005 to 0.011 solar masses for M^H_max from 1.800 to 0.600 solar masses
- For the case with M^H_max = 1.40 solar masses and nucleonic envelope, varying M^Q2_max from 2.00 to 2.30 solar masses reduces the twin mass range from 0.074 to 0.000 solar masses
- For the case with M^H_max = 1.40 solar masses and nucleonic envelope, varying M^Q2_max from 2.00 to 2.20 solar masses reduces the triplet mass range from 0.006 to 0.000 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the study of twin star configurations by Paschalidis (2018) and the analysis of sequential phase transitions by Alford (2017) to include multiple hadronic envelope compositions (nucleonic, hyperonic, and Δ-resonance-hyperon admixed matter). It builds on prior work by Li (2019a, 2019b) on heavy baryonic states in dense matter and their compatibility with GW170817 constraints. The results enable future discrimination between hadronic and hybrid stars in binary merger events through tidal deformability measurements, and show that low-mass triplets proliferate the number of possible star type combinations in mergers up to a maximum of six.
