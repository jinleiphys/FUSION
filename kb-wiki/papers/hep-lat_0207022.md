# The Chiral Extrapolation of Strange Matrix Elements in the Nucleon

> In current lattice simulations of nucleon properties, the up and down quark masses are significantly larger than their physical values, while the strange quark can be included in simulations with its physical mass. When the up and down quark masses are much smaller than the strange-quark mass the chiral extrapolation of strange-quark matrix elements in the nucleon from the lattice up and down quark masses to their physical values can be performed with two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, thereby avoiding the slow convergence problem of the three-flavor chiral expansion. We explore the chiral expansion of several matrix elements of strange operators in the nucleon in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory and two-flavor partial-quenched chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

When lattice simulations use up and down quark masses much smaller than the strange quark mass, the chiral extrapolation of strange-quark matrix elements in the nucleon can be performed with two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, avoiding the slow convergence problem of the three-flavor chiral expansion. This approach works because the strange quark can be simulated at its physical mass, requiring only interpolation rather than extrapolation in the strange sector.

## Method

The authors computed the chiral expansion of several strange-quark matrix elements in the nucleon up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, O(epsilon^2)) in both two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) and two-flavor partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQchiPT). They considered spin-singlet operators (scalar current and twist-2 operators), spin non-singlet operators (strange magnetic moment, helicity and transversity distributions), and the strange quark contribution to the proton spin. The calculations included one-loop diagrams with nucleon and Delta-resonance intermediate states and tree-level counterterms, with the expansion parameter epsilon ~ m_pi/Lambda where Lambda ~ 760 MeV is the energy gap to strange hadronic excitations.

## Key numbers

- The high-energy scale Lambda is estimated as m_K + m_Sigma - m_N ~ 760 MeV, which sets the radius of convergence for two-flavor PQchiPT
- The expansion parameter epsilon ~ 0.4 in the real world SU(2) expansion for low energy processes
- The two-flavor theory is expected to converge rapidly for pion masses less than approximately 300 MeV
- The Delta-nucleon mass difference is Delta ~ 300 MeV
- The physical pion mass is m_pi ~ 140 MeV
- The pion decay constant f ~ 132 MeV
- The pi N coupling constant g_A ~ 1.26
- The Delta N pi coupling g_Delta N = 1.8
- Quenched QCD calculations are expected to have O(1/N_c) ~ 30% error from large-N_c considerations

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory framework to strange-quark matrix elements in the nucleon, building on prior developments in chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1967, Gasser and Leutwyler 1984, Jenkins and Manohar 1991) and its partially-quenched extensions (Bernard and Golterman 1994, Sharpe 1997, Beane and Savage 2002). The authors note that previous determinations of strange matrix elements like m_s <N|s-bar s|N> and Delta s relied on SU(3) flavor symmetry expansions with large uncertainties from higher-order symmetry breaking effects (Savage and Walden 1997). The results enable lattice QCD simulations to perform chiral extrapolations of strange matrix elements using two-flavor chiPT when pions are lighter than about 300 MeV, avoiding the problematic convergence of three-flavor expansions. The PQchiPT results provide a tool to remove partial quenching errors from simulations with two non-degenerate light quarks and an unquenched strange quark.
