# Collective flow in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions

> We present an evidence of strong radial flow in high-multiplicity pp collisions. We analyze the CMS data on the inclusive spectra of the charged pions, kaons and protons in the LHC $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV collisions. For $<N_{\mathrm{tracks}} >\gtrsim 75$ we demonstrate the consistency of the hydrodynamic description with the (idealized) Gubser's flow. Using a one parameter fit of the model to experimental data, we obtain the initial fireball size to be of the order of 1 fm. At smaller multiplicities, the fit cannot be performed which shows a limitation of the hydrodynamic approach and provides us with falsifiability of our theory.

## Key claim
The paper claims evidence of strong radial flow in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies, but only for events with average track multiplicity above 75; for lower multiplicities the hydrodynamic fit fails, establishing a falsifiability condition for the collective flow interpretation.

## Method
The authors analyzed CMS data on charged pion, kaon, and proton inclusive spectra in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV. They fitted the data using the analytic Gubser's flow solution (a conformal, axially symmetric hydrodynamic model with equation of state epsilon = p/3) via a one-parameter fit to extract the initial fireball size parameter 1/q. The Cooper-Frye formula was used to compute particle spectra from a freezeout surface at T_f = 170 MeV, with a cutoff applied to exclude the unphysical outer tail of the Gubser solution.

## Key numbers
- For <N_tracks> = 131: 1/q = 1.05 ± 0.05 fm, epsilon_0 = 12.7, dS/deta = 204.7, v_perp^max = 0.71, T'(proton) = 574 MeV, T'(kaon) = 397 MeV
- For <N_tracks> = 98: 1/q = 1.00 ± 0.05 fm, epsilon_0 = 8.6, dS/deta = 153.1, v_perp^max = 0.68, T'(proton) = 458 MeV, T'(kaon) = 338 MeV
- For <N_tracks> = 75: 1/q = 1.00 ± 0.05 fm, epsilon_0 = 6.0, dS/deta = 117.2, v_perp^max = 0.63, T'(proton) = 394 MeV, T'(kaon) = 301 MeV
- The freezeout temperature used was T_f = 170 MeV
- The number of effective degrees of freedom in QGP was taken as f_* = 11
- The entropy per unit rapidity was related to charged particle multiplicity as dS/deta ≈ 7.5 dN_ch/deta

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic description of collective flow from heavy-ion AA collisions and pA collisions to the smaller pp system, following the discovery of the "ridge" correlation by CMS. It builds on prior analysis of pA data by Shuryak (2013) and the authors' own work on string-string interactions in multi-string systems (Kalaydzhyan 2014). The results are consistent with independent femtoscopy analysis by Hirono and Shuryak (2014) which found similar flow velocities and a slightly smaller size of about 2/3 fm. The paper establishes that hydrodynamic behavior in pp collisions appears at multiplicities similar to those in pPb collisions, and enables future studies of the initial state formation mechanism in small systems.
