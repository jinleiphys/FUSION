# Particle Ratios within EPOS, UrQMD and Thermal Models at AGS, SPS and RHIC Energies

> The particle ratios $k^+/π^+$, $π^-/K^-$, $\bar{p}/π^-$, $Λ/π^-$, $Ω/π^-$, $p/π^+$, $π^-/π^+$, $K^-/K^+$, $\bar{p}/p$, $\barΛ/Λ$, $\barΣ/Σ$, $ \barΩ/Ω$ measured at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies are compared with large statistical ensembles of $100,000$ events deduced from the CRMC EPOS $1.99$ and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) hybrid model. In the UrQMD hybrid model two types of phase transitions are taken into account. All these are then confronted to the Hadron Resonance Gas Model. The two types of phase transitions are apparently indistinguishable. Apart from $k^+/π^+$, $k^-/π^-$, $Ω/π^-$, $\bar{p}/π^+$, and $\barΩ/Ω$, the UrQMD hybrid model agrees well with the CRMC EPOS $1.99$. Also, we conclude that the CRMC EPOS $1.99$ seems to largely underestimate $k^+/π^+$, $k^-/π^-$, $Ω/π^-$, and $\bar{p}/π^+$.

## Key claim

The paper compares particle ratios from CRMC EPOS 1.99 and UrQMD hybrid model event generators with Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations and experimental data at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, finding that while HRG reproduces experimental results excellently for most ratios, the two types of phase transitions implemented in UrQMD (first-order and crossover) are apparently indistinguishable in all particle ratios studied.

## Method

The authors generated statistical ensembles of 100,000 events using CRMC EPOS 1.99 and UrQMD hybrid model version 3.4 at beam energies from 7 to 200 GeV. For the UrQMD hybrid model, two separate simulations were run implementing first-order and crossover phase transitions. The resulting particle ratios were compared with HRG model calculations using freezeout conditions based on constant entropy density normalized to T^3, and with experimental data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments including STAR, NA49, NA44, and NA57.

## Key numbers

- 100,000 events generated per energy point for both CRMC EPOS 1.99 and UrQMD hybrid model
- Beam energies studied: 7, 7.7, 9, 11, 11.5, 13, 19, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV
- HRG model overestimates k+/pi+ at sqrt(s_NN) greater than approximately 10 GeV
- HRG model overestimates Lambda/pi- at sqrt(s_NN) less than approximately 7 GeV
- The peak in Lambda/pi- occurs at energies around 10 GeV across all data sets
- CRMC EPOS 1.99 largely underestimates k+/pi+, k-/pi-, Omega/pi-, and anti-p/pi+ at all energies studied

## Context

This work extends comparisons between thermal models and event generators previously studied by Tawfik (2016) and others, specifically testing whether CRMC EPOS 1.99 and UrQMD hybrid model can reproduce experimental particle ratios across a wide energy range relevant to future facilities like NICA at JINR and FAIR at GSI. The results enable validation of these event generators for predicting particle ratios at energies where experimental data are not yet available, particularly for the intermediate temperature region of the QCD phase diagram that NICA and FAIR will explore, complementing the low baryon chemical potential region studied at LHC and top RHIC energies.
