# Perturbative probes of QCD matter at the Large Hadron Collider

> The main results on electroweak probes, jets, high-pT hadrons, heavy-flavour and quarkonia production from the first two years of heavy-ion operation at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are briefly reviewed. Data measured at center-of-mass energies sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions are compared to proton-proton (p-p) measurements in order to extract information on the properties of hot and dense strongly-interacting matter.

# Perturbative probes of QCD matter at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

Electroweak probes (photons, W, Z) in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are consistent with perturbative scaling expectations (R_AA ≈ 1), while all strongly-interacting probes (hadrons, jets, heavy-flavour mesons, quarkonia) show significant suppression due to final-state interactions in the quark-gluon plasma, but the detailed pattern of suppression depends on particle species, transverse momentum, and centrality, and the quantitative extraction of medium properties requires coupling energy-loss models to 3D viscous hydrodynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the first two years of LHC heavy-ion operation, comparing Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV to proton-proton measurements. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed as the ratio of Pb-Pb yields to p-p cross sections scaled by the nuclear overlap function T_AA(b), quantifying deviations from an incoherent superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions. Measurements cover electroweak bosons (isolated photons, W, Z), inclusive charged hadrons and pions, fully reconstructed jets (anti-k_T algorithm), photon-jet events, D and B mesons via displaced vertices, and J/psi and Upsilon states.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron suppression: R_AA ≈ 0.17 (factor of 6) at p_T = 7 GeV/c, rising to R_AA ≈ 0.5 at p_T ≈ 40–100 GeV/c in central Pb-Pb collisions
- Inclusive W production: R_AA(W) = 1.04 ± 0.07 ± 0.12; individual W^- R_AA = 1.46 and W^+ R_AA = 0.8 due to isospin effects
- D meson suppression: R_AA(D) ≈ 0.3 for p_T > 2–6 GeV/c (depending on decay channel)
- B meson suppression: R_AA(B) ≈ 0.4 for p_T > 6.5 GeV/c via secondary J/psi
- J/psi suppression: R_AA ≈ 0.5 at low p_T and R_AA ≈ 0.2 at high p_T in central Pb-Pb
- Upsilon states: R_AA(Upsilon(1S)) ≈ 0.56, R_AA(Upsilon(2S)) ≈ 0.12, R_AA(Upsilon(3S)) < 0.1
- Jet spectra in central Pb-Pb suppressed by factor of two compared to peripheral collisions
- Photon-jet balance ratio x_Jγ = p_T^jet/p_T^gamma decreases significantly with centrality for p_T^gamma > 60 GeV/c and p_T^jet > 30 GeV/c
- Energy density in Pb-Pb collisions: O(2 TeV) deposited in O(150 fm^3) at thermalisation time τ_0 = 1 fm/c, more than one order of magnitude above critical energy density ε_crit ≈ 1 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This review summarises the first LHC heavy-ion results (2010–2012) that extend and challenge findings from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. At RHIC, high-p_T hadron suppression (R_AA ≈ 0.2) and similar charm+bottom suppression were observed (Adare 2010, Abelev 2006), while at LHC energies the suppression pattern for J/psi differs markedly, less suppressed at low p_T (R_AA ≈ 0.5 vs 0.2–0.3 at RHIC) but more suppressed at high p_T (R_AA ≈ 0.2 vs 0.6 at RHIC), consistent with models including charm recombination (Braun-Munzinger 2000, Thews 2000, Andronic 2007). The Upsilon sequential suppression measurements provide new constraints on lattice QCD predictions for bottomonium dissociation temperatures (Datta 2003). Future larger datasets will enable extraction of medium transport coefficients (q_hat) and heavy-quark drag coefficients (η_D) through comparisons with 3D viscous hydrodynamics calculations (Armesto 2009, Gossiaux 2012).
