# Shear Viscosity of the outer crust of Neutron stars: Ion Contribution

> The shear viscosity of the crust might have a damping effect on the amplitude of r-modes of rotating neutron stars. This damping has implications for the emission of gravitational waves. We calculate the contribution to the shear viscosity coming from the ions using both semi-analytical methods, that consider binary collisions, and Molecular Dynamics simulations. We compare these results with the contribution coming from electrons. We study how the shear viscosity depends on density for conditions of interest in neutron star envelopes and outer crusts. In the low density limit, we find good agreement between results of our molecular dynamics simulations and classical semi-analytic calculations.

# Shear Viscosity of the Outer Crust of Neutron Stars: Ion Contribution

## Key claim

The ion contribution to shear viscosity in the outer crust of neutron stars, calculated via Molecular Dynamics simulations and semi-analytical methods, shows good agreement between the two approaches only in the low-density classical limit; at higher densities, many-body correlations captured by MD simulations cause significant deviations from the binary-collision semi-analytical results, and quantum effects become important for light ions at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors calculated the ion contribution to shear viscosity using two complementary approaches: (1) Molecular Dynamics simulations following the Kubo formalism, computing the autocorrelation function of the off-diagonal components of the pressure tensor for a Yukawa-interacting one-component plasma; and (2) semi-analytical Chapman-Enskog calculations using transport cross sections derived from phase shifts (quantum) or deflection functions (classical), with density-dependent cut-offs on angular momentum or impact parameter to account for many-body effects. The system studied was a dilute Hydrogen plasma (Z=1, A=1) and a heavy-ion plasma (Z=29.4, A=88) under conditions relevant to neutron star envelopes and outer crusts.

- For Hydrogen plasma at T=0.01 MeV and n=10^-5 fm^-3: MD result eta = 7.0(2) x 10^-6 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of shear viscosity in neutron star crusts, which previously focused on the electron contribution using the Born approximation (Flowers 1979) and including non-Born corrections (Chugunov 2007), as well as electron viscosity in nuclear pasta phases (Horowitz and Berry 2008). The ion contribution calculated here is relevant for damping r-mode oscillations in rotating neutron stars, which has implications for gravitational wave emission detectable by LIGO (Owen 2005). The results enable more complete modeling of viscous damping mechanisms in neutron star crusts, particularly for understanding the observed spin-down of newly born hot neutron stars (Lindblom 1998) and the damping at the crust-core interface (Bildsten 2000). The formalism can be extended to multi-component plasmas and to calculate other transport properties such as diffusion coefficients.
