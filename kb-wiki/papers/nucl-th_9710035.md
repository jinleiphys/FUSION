# Explicit pionic degrees of freedom in deuteron photodisintegration in the Delta-resonance region

> Photodisintegration of the deuteron above pi-threshold is studied in a coupled channel approach including N-Delta- and pi-d-channels with pion retardation in potentials and exchange currents.

# Explicit pionic degrees of freedom in deuteron photodisintegration in the Delta-resonance region

## Key claim

By including for the first time complete retardation in the pion-exchange contributions to both potentials and meson-exchange currents (MECs) within a coupled channel approach, the authors achieve good agreement with experimental total cross section data over the entire Delta-resonance energy range, whereas previous static calculations underestimated the cross section by 20-30%. The improvement is largely due to the inclusion of recoil current contributions that are absent in static approaches because of cancellation against wave function renormalization.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled channel approach that includes NN, N-Delta, and pi-d channels, using retarded pi- and rho-exchange for hadronic interactions and retarded pi-MECs for electromagnetic currents. The gamma-N-Delta coupling is taken from the model of Tanabe and Ohta (1985), which incorporates nonresonant pion rescattering contributions, making the coupling complex and energy dependent. Gauge invariance is maintained by including recoil currents and additional two-body charge densities alongside the usual vertex, meson, and contact MECs.

## Key numbers

- Previous static coupled channel calculations underestimated the total cross section in the Delta-region by about 20-30%, as reported for Wilhelm and Arenhoevel (1993) and Tanabe and Ohta (1989).
- The full calculation with complete retardation, pi-d channel, and rho-pi-gamma/omega-pi-gamma MECs gives "quite a good agreement" with experimental data over the whole energy range (no single numerical discrepancy percentage provided for the final result).
- The photon asymmetry is slightly underestimated in absolute size, but the shape is reproduced "quite well" at higher energies above 300 MeV, in contrast to prior approaches.

## Context

This work extends the coupled channel approach of Wilhelm and Arenhoevel (1993) and the unitary three-body model of Tanabe and Ohta (1989), both of which underestimated the total cross section and failed to reproduce the shape of differential cross sections and photon asymmetries above 300 MeV. The authors resolve these discrepancies by incorporating full pion retardation, which had been conjectured in Wilhelm and Arenhoevel (1993) as the likely cause of the underestimation. This enables a consistent treatment of photodisintegration up to the 2-pi threshold and provides a framework for testing the N-Delta interaction without adjustable parameters for the deuteron photodisintegration process itself.
