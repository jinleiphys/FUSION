# Searching for the Odderon in the diffractive $f_2(1270)$ photoproduction at $pA$ collisions

> Although the Odderon is an unambiguous prediction of Quantum Chromodynamics, its existence still was not confirmed experimentally. One of the processes where the Odderon contribution is expected to be dominant is the diffractive photoproduction of tensor mesons. In this paper we study the diffractive $f_2(1270)$ photoproduction in $pA$ collisions at LHC and RHIC considering the model of the stochastic vacuum to treat the non - perturbative process. We demonstrate that this process dominates the $f_2$ production at mid - rapidities, and large values for the cross section are predicted. Our results indicate that the experimental analysis of this final state is, in principle, feasible and can be used to probe the Odderon.

## Key claim

The diffractive photoproduction of the $f_2(1270)$ tensor meson in proton-nucleus collisions at LHC and RHIC is predicted to be dominated by Odderon exchange at mid-rapidities, with cross sections large enough to make experimental detection feasible; however, the predictions are model-dependent with an estimated uncertainty factor of about 2, and the photon-Odderon and photon-photon contributions become similar within that uncertainty.

## Method

The cross section for diffractive $f_2(1270)$ photoproduction via Odderon exchange in $pA$ collisions was calculated using the model of the stochastic vacuum (MSV) to treat the non-perturbative Odderon exchange, with the proton described as having a quark-diquark structure and the final proton state taken as an excited $N^*$ state (sum of $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ contributions). The photon flux from the nucleus was computed using a monopole form factor with $\Lambda = 0.088$ GeV and a minimum impact parameter $b_{min} = R_A + R_p$. The background from photon-photon fusion was estimated using the two-photon decay width of the $f_2(1270)$ from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 8.1$ TeV at mid-rapidities ($-2.0 \le Y \le +2.0$, ALICE/CMS range): photon-Odderon cross section = 8.37 $\mu b$; photon-photon cross section = 3.34 $\mu b$.
- For $pPb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 8.1$ TeV at forward rapidities ($2.0 \le Y \le 4.6$, LHCb range): photon-Odderon cross section = 0.85 $\mu b$; photon-photon cross section = 5.32 $\mu b$.
- For $pAu$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 0.2$ TeV at mid-rapidities ($-1.0 \le Y \le +1.0$, RHIC range): photon-Odderon cross section = 0.34 $\mu b$; photon-photon cross section = 0.21 $\mu b$.
- The predicted $f_2$ cross sections are larger than those for exclusive $\eta_c$ photoproduction in $pPb$ collisions by a factor of approximately 300.
- The $f_2(1270)$ decays dominantly into a pair of pions with a branching fraction of 84.2%.
- With the CMS integrated luminosity for $pPb$ collisions at 8.1 TeV in 2016 of approximately 180/nb, the predicted number of diffractive events is larger than $1.5 \times 10^6$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Odderon searches via diffractive photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons like $\eta_c$ (Kilian 1997, Cudell 1999, Berger 2000, Goncalves 2014) to the tensor meson $f_2(1270)$, motivated by its smaller mass leading to larger cross sections. The approach uses the model of the stochastic vacuum (MSV) developed by Nachtmann (1991) and applied to Odderon exchange by Cudell (1999), which successfully describes soft Pomeron-exchange processes (Shoshi 2002). The results enable experimental searches at LHC and RHIC, with the possibility of separating Odderon and photon-photon contributions using a lower cutoff on the $f_2$ transverse momentum, as the Odderon-mediated process is expected to produce $f_2$ states with larger $p_T$ than those from photon-photon fusion.
