# Standardized Cumulants of Flow Harmonic Fluctuations

> The distribution of flow harmonics in heavy ion experiment can be characterized by standardized cumulants. We first model the ellipticity and power parameters of the elliptic-power distribution by employing MC-Glauber model. Then we use the elliptic-power distribution together with the hydrodynamic linear response approximation to study the two dimensional standardized cumulants of elliptic and triangular flow ($v_2$ and $v_3$) distribution. For the second harmonic, it turns out that finding two dimensional cumulants in terms of $2q$-particle correlation functions $c_2\{2q\}$ is limited to the skewness. We also show that $c_3\{2\}$, $c_3\{4\}$, and $c_3\{6\}$, are related to the second, fourth, and sixth standardized cumulants of the $v_3$ distribution, respectively. The cumulant $c_{n}\{2q\}$ can be also written in terms of $v_n\{2q\}$. Specifically, $-(v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\})^4$ turns out to be the kurtosis of the $v_3$ event-by-event fluctuation distribution. We introduce a new parametrization for the distribution $p(v_3)$ with $v_3\{2\}$, kurtosis and sixth-order standardized cumulant being its free parameters. Compared to the Gaussian distribution, it indicates a more accurate fit with experimental results. Finally, we compare the kurtosis obtained from simulation with that of extracted from experimental data for the $v_3$ distribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the second harmonic (elliptic flow $v_2$), the experimentally accessible multiparticle correlation functions $c_2\{2q\}$ can only extract the skewness of the underlying two-dimensional flow distribution, not higher-order standardized cumulants; for the third harmonic (triangular flow $v_3$), the ratios $-(v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\})^4$ and $4(v_3\{6\}/v_3\{2\})^6$ directly correspond to the kurtosis and sixth-order standardized cumulant of the $v_3$ distribution, respectively, under the assumption of linear hydrodynamic response and the elliptic-power model for initial-state fluctuations.

## Method

The authors first fit the ellipticity $\varepsilon_0$ and power $\alpha$ parameters of the elliptic-power distribution to two-dimensional histograms of initial eccentricity components $\varepsilon_{n,x}$ and $\varepsilon_{n,y}$ generated by the MC-Glauber model (within the iEBE-VISHNU event generator) for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV across centrality bins. They then use the elliptic-power distribution together with the hydrodynamic linear response approximation $\boldsymbol{v}_n \simeq \chi_n \boldsymbol{\varepsilon}_n$ to relate the standardized cumulants of the initial anisotropy distribution $\hat{\mathcal{E}}_{pq}^{(n)}$ to those of the final flow distribution $\hat{\mathcal{V}}_{pq}^{(n)}$, and expand the multiparticle correlation functions $c_n\{2q\}$ in terms of these standardized cumulants to determine which cumulants are experimentally accessible.

## Key numbers

* For the $50-55\%$ centrality class of Pb-Pb collisions, the fitted elliptic-power parameters for $n=2$ are $\alpha = 8.699 \pm 0.076$ and $\varepsilon_0 = 0.400 \pm 0.002$; for $n=3$, $\alpha = 9.543 \pm 0.083$ and $\varepsilon_0 = 0.004 \pm 0.002$.
* The quantity $-(v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\})^4$ equals the kurtosis (excess kurtosis $\gamma_2$) of the $v_3$ event-by-event fluctuation distribution.
* The quantity $4(v_3\{6\}/v_3\{2\})^6$ equals the sixth-order standardized cumulant of the $v_3$ distribution.
* For $n=2$, the only higher-order standardized cumulant accessible from $c_2\{2\}$ through $c_2\{8\}$ is the skewness; all other cumulants of order three and above are suppressed by powers of $\hat{\mathcal{E}}_{10}^{(2)}$ or vanish by symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of flow harmonic fluctuations beyond the Gaussian approximation, building on the elliptic-power distribution introduced by Yan (2014) for initial-state eccentricities and on the cumulant framework of Borghini, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000, 2001). It generalizes the finding of Giacalone (2016) that the splitting between $v_2\{4\}$ and $v_2\{6\}$ arises from skewness, and provides a new parametrization for $p(v_3)$ with $v_3\{2\}$, kurtosis, and sixth-order standardized cumulant as free parameters, enabling more accurate fits to experimental data than a Gaussian distribution. The results enable experimental extraction of the kurtosis of the $v_3$ distribution from the ratio $-(v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\})^4$ and comparison with hydrodynamic simulations such as iEBE-VISHNU.
