# Divergence of the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the nuclear equation of state in many-body perturbation theory

> The isospin-asymmetry dependence of the nuclear matter equation of state obtained from microscopic chiral two- and three-body interactions in second-order many-body perturbation theory is examined in detail. The quadratic, quartic and sextic coefficients in the Maclaurin expansion of the free energy per particle of infinite homogeneous nuclear matter with respect to the isospin asymmetry are extracted numerically using finite differences, and the resulting polynomial isospin-asymmetry parametrizations are compared to the full isospin-asymmetry dependence of the free energy. It is found that in the low-temperature and high-density regime where the radius of convergence of the expansion is generically zero, the inclusion of higher-order terms beyond the leading quadratic approximation leads overall to a significantly poorer description of the isospin-asymmetry dependence. In contrast, at high temperatures and densities well below nuclear saturation density, the interaction contributions to the higher-order coefficients are negligible and the deviations from the quadratic approximation are predominantly from the noninteracting term in the many-body perturbation series. Furthermore, we extract the leading logarithmic term in the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the equation of state at zero temperature from the analysis of linear combinations of finite differences. It is shown that the logarithmic term leads to a considerably improved description of the isospin-asymmetry dependence at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The Maclaurin expansion of the nuclear matter free energy per particle with respect to isospin asymmetry has generically zero radius of convergence at low temperatures and high densities, meaning that including higher-order terms beyond the quadratic approximation actually worsens the description; however, at high temperatures and densities well below saturation, the higher-order interaction contributions are negligible and deviations from the quadratic approximation come mainly from the noninteracting term.

## Method

The authors computed the free energy per particle of infinite homogeneous nuclear matter using second-order many-body perturbation theory with chiral low-momentum N3LO two-body and N2LO three-body interactions (two interaction sets: n3lo414 with cutoff 414 MeV and n3lo450 with cutoff 450 MeV). They extracted the quadratic, quartic, and sextic Maclaurin coefficients A₂, A₄, A₆ numerically using finite-difference approximations, and compared the resulting polynomial parametrizations to the full isospin-asymmetry dependence of the free energy. At zero temperature, they extracted the leading logarithmic term in the isospin-asymmetry expansion from linear combinations of finite differences.

## Key numbers

- For the noninteracting Fermi gas at zero temperature, the ratio ξ = 1 − A₂/F_sym equals approximately 0.054; at infinite temperature or zero density, ξ ≈ 0.279.
- For the noninteracting Fermi gas at zero temperature, the ratio ζ₂N/ξ equals 0.646 for N=2, 0.814 for N=3, 0.883 for N=4, 0.918 for N=5, and 0.999 for N=100.
- For the noninteracting Fermi gas at infinite temperature or zero density, ζ₂N/ξ equals 0.431 for N=2, 0.604 for N=3, 0.696 for N=4, 0.754 for N=5, and 0.987 for N=100.
- At T=5 MeV and ρ=0.15 fm⁻³ for the n3lo414 interaction, the second-order np-channel NN contribution gives A₀ = −8.13 MeV, A₂ = 8.32 MeV, A₄ = −0.78 MeV, A₆ = 1.01 MeV, A₈ = −0.9 MeV, and F_sym = 8.13 MeV.
- At the same T and ρ, the total second-order NN contribution gives A₀ = −9.60 MeV, A₂ = 8.64 MeV, A₄ = −0.80 MeV, A₆ = 1.02 MeV, A₈ = −0.9 MeV, and F_sym = 8.36 MeV.
- At the same T and ρ, the total second-order NN+DDNN contribution gives A₀ = −10.80 MeV, A₂ = 10.48 MeV, A₄ = −1.24 MeV, A₆ = 0.82 MeV, A₈ = −0.8 MeV, and F_sym = 9.63 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the nuclear equation of state beyond the quadratic approximation, which had been assumed in many previous studies including the authors' own earlier work (paper2) on the liquid-gas phase transition. It builds on the finding by PhysRevC.91.065201 that second-order perturbative contributions give a singular quartic coefficient at zero temperature for a contact interaction, and generalizes this to realistic chiral interactions. The results enable more accurate modeling of the equation of state for astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron-star mergers, particularly by identifying the regimes where the quadratic approximation is reliable and where it fails.
