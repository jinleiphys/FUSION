# Fluctuations of conserved charges in strong magnetic fields from lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice QCD results of the second order fluctuations of and correlations among net baryon number, electric charge and strangeness in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD in the presence of a background magnetic field with physical pion mass $m_π=135$ MeV. To mimic the magnetic field strength produced in the early stage of heavy-ion collision experiments we use 6 different values of the magnetic field strength up to $ \sim $10$m_π^2$. We find that the correlations between baryon number and electric charge along the transition line are substantially affected by magnetic fields in the current $eB$ window, which could be useful for probing the existence of a magnetic field in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD results for second-order fluctuations and correlations of net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness at physical pion mass (135 MeV) in a background magnetic field, finding that the baryon-electric charge correlation along the transition line is substantially enhanced, up to a factor of about 2 at the highest field, which the authors propose as a possible probe for magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, but they caution that further studies are needed to connect to experimental observations.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using highly improved staggered quarks with physical light and strange quark masses, introducing a background magnetic field along the z-direction via a U(1) field in Landau gauge. They used 6 values of magnetic flux (N_b = 1,2,3,4,6) corresponding to magnetic field strengths up to approximately 10 m_π^2, on lattices with N_τ = 8 and 12, covering a temperature range from about 0.9 T_pc to 1.1 T_pc. The second-order fluctuations and correlations were computed from derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potentials at zero chemical potential, and the results were interpolated to constant magnetic field strength in physical units as the lattice spacing varied with temperature.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 135 MeV (physical value)
- Magnetic field strengths: 6 values up to approximately 10 m_π^2
- Temperature range: approximately 0.9 T_pc to 1.1 T_pc
- The ratio χ^{BQ}_{11}(eB, T_pc(eB)) / χ^{BQ}_{11}(0, T_pc(0)) reaches approximately 2 at eB ≈ 9 m_π^2
- Lattice spacings: N_τ = 8 and 12

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD study (Ding et al., 2021) which used a heavier pion mass of about 220 MeV, now reaching the physical pion mass of 135 MeV. Prior studies of conserved charge fluctuations in magnetic fields were limited to hadron resonance gas models (Fukushima, 2016; Ferreira, 2018; Bhattacharyya, 2015; Kadam, 2019) and the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Fu, 2013). The results provide a QCD baseline for quadratic fluctuations and correlations in external magnetic fields, and the proposed R_cp-like ratio of central-to-peripheral collision observables could enable experimental searches for magnetic field signals in heavy-ion collisions, though connecting to experimental observations requires further work.
