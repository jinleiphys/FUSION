# Radial Oscillations of Viscous Neutron Stars: Zero Diffusion Case

> The spectrum of radial oscillations of neutron stars is systematically studied within two frameworks of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics: the relativistic Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart theories. A correspondence is established between the discrete stellar eigenmodes and the continuous dispersion relation of perturbations around a homogeneous fluid, providing a basis for interpreting our numerical results. We analyze the Newtonian limit and assess the impact of relativistic corrections, such as the gravitational redshifting of microscopic relaxation timescales. We show that bulk viscosity can significantly affect the behavior of both hydrodynamic and nonhydrodynamic fundamental modes, and that, depending on the magnitude of the viscous effects, it is the nonhydrodynamic mode that becomes unstable beyond the turning point in a sequence of equilibrium configurations. These results provide a useful step toward systematic studies of neutron star quasinormal modes in the presence of viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper systematically studies radial oscillations of neutron stars with viscosity, showing that bulk viscosity can significantly affect both hydrodynamic and nonhydrodynamic fundamental modes, and that depending on the magnitude of viscous effects, it is the nonhydrodynamic mode that becomes unstable beyond the turning point in a sequence of equilibrium configurations, a result that depends on the specific viscous framework (relativistic Navier-Stokes or Israel-Stewart) and the choice of transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized perturbation equations for radial oscillations around a static, spherically symmetric neutron star background, using both relativistic Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart theories of viscous hydrodynamics in the zero-diffusion limit. They numerically integrate the resulting system in both frequency and time domains to compute the discrete set of mode frequencies, adopting simple prescriptions where bulk and shear viscosity coefficients are proportional to thermodynamic pressure, with either constant microscopic timescales or constant redshifted timescales. To interpret the numerical results, they establish a correspondence between the discrete stellar eigenmodes and the continuous dispersion relation of perturbations around a homogeneous fluid.

## Key numbers

- The radial mode spectrum in Navier-Stokes theory is approximated by ω_(n) = ω^PF_(n) [ -i (n/n_visc) ± sqrt(1 - (n/n_visc)^2) ], where ω^PF_(n) are perfect-fluid eigenfrequencies and n_visc ∝ τ_(η,ζ)^(-1); for n < n_visc modes are underdamped and oscillatory, while for n > n_visc they are overdamped and purely decaying.
- In Israel-Stewart theory with shear viscosity, the radial modes satisfy [ω_(n)^2 - ω^2_PF(n)] (1 - i ω_(n) τ_π) + 2 i ω_(n) ω^PF_(n) (n/n_visc) = 0, which is the quantized analogue of the Israel-Stewart dispersion relation for longitudinal perturbations around a homogeneous state.
- The causality constraint for Israel-Stewart theory requires c_s^2 + (1/(e+p)) ( (4/3)(η/τ_π) + (ζ/τ_Π) ) ≤ 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron star radial oscillations from perfect-fluid models (Chandrasekhar 1964) to include viscous effects within two competing relativistic hydrodynamic frameworks, building on prior work by Barta (2019) on radial perturbations in relativistic Navier-Stokes theory and by Redondo-Yuste (2024) on non-radial oscillations in BDNK theory. The results provide a foundation for systematic studies of neutron star quasinormal modes with viscosity, which is relevant for interpreting post-merger gravitational-wave signals from binary neutron star mergers expected to be observed by next-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope.
