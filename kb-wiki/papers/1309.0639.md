# The half-skyrmion phase in a chiral-quark model

> The Chiral Dilaton Model, where baryons arise as non-topological solitons built from the interaction of quarks and chiral mesons, shows in the high density low temperature regime a two phase scenario in the nuclear matter phase diagram. Dense soliton matter described by the Wigner-Seitz approximation generates a periodic potential in terms of the sigma and pion fields that leads to the formation of a band structure. The analysis up to three times nuclear matter density shows that soliton matter undergoes two separate phase transitions: a delocalization of the baryon number density leading to $B=1/2$ structures, as in skyrmion matter, at moderate densities, and quark deconfinement at larger densities. This description fits well into the so-called quarkyonic phase where, before deconfinement, nuclear matter should undergo structural changes involving the restoration of fundamental symmetries of QCD.

## Key claim

The Chiral Dilaton Model (CDM), which includes explicit quark degrees of freedom, exhibits a delocalization of baryon number leading to a B=1/2 phase at intermediate densities, similar to the half-skyrmion phase in skyrmion matter, but the authors caution that this phase should not be confused with the rigid crystal structure of the half-skyrmion phase and that chiral symmetry restoration cannot take place in their calculation due to the frozen dilaton field.

## Method

The authors used the Chiral Dilaton Model with quarks interacting with sigma and pion fields, applying the Wigner-Seitz approximation to replace a cubic lattice with spherical cells of radius R. They imposed boundary conditions on the fields at the cell surface related to parity operations, then solved for the quark eigenvalues and baryon density profiles as a function of cell radius to study the band structure and phase transitions at densities up to three times nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- The delocalization and band widening occur in a range of cell radii from approximately 0.8 to 1.2 fm, corresponding to a baryon density of roughly ρ_B/ρ_0 ≈ 0.85–3, where ρ_0 is nuclear saturation density.
- At very low densities (R = 2 fm), the baryon density is approximately ρ_B ≈ 0.2 ρ_0.
- Exchange of quarks between the lower band G=0⁺ and the first excited band G=1⁺ occurs at densities larger than 3 ρ_0, interpreted as a signal of deconfinement.

## Context

This work extends the study of dense matter from the Skyrme model, where a half-skyrmion phase was found by Castillejo (1989) and Kugler (1989), to a model with explicit quark degrees of freedom. The authors build on prior CDM studies by Drago (2011) and Heide (1994, 1996, 1997, 1998) and connect their results to the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran (2007). The analysis is limited by the Wigner-Seitz approximation, which does not capture long-range correlations between neighboring baryons from pion fields, and by the frozen dilaton approximation which prevents chiral symmetry restoration.
