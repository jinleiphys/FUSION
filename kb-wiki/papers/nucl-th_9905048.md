# Structure of Excited States of 10Be studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> We study structure of excited states of 10Be with the method of variation after spin parity projection in the framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. Present calculations describe many excited states and reproduce the experimental data of E2 and E1 transitions and the new data of the $β$ transition strength successfully. We make systematic discussions on the molecule-like structures of light unstable nuclei and the important role of the valence neutrons based on the results obtained with the framework which is free from such model assumptions as the existence of inert cores and clusters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) can successfully describe many excited states of ¹⁰Be and reproduce experimental E2, E1, and β transition data, but this success is conditional on the specific choice of effective interactions and the treatment of resonance states within a bound-state approximation using an artificial barrier.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations after spin-parity projection (VAP) in the AMD framework, where the nuclear wave function is a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets parameterized by complex spatial centers and spin parameters. For higher excited states, AMD wave functions were superposed and orthogonalized to lower states by varying the energy of the orthogonal component. The final wave functions for each Jπ state were determined by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and norm matrices simultaneously over all intrinsic states and K quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ¹⁰Be: 61.1 MeV with case(1) interactions and 61.3 MeV with case(2) interactions
- E2 transition strength for 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁: experimental 10.5 ± 1.1 e fm², theoretical 11 e fm²
- E2 transition strength for 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁: experimental 3.3 ± 2.0 e fm², theoretical 0.6 e fm²
- E1 transition strength for 0⁺₂ → 1⁻₁: experimental (1.3 ± 0.6) × 10⁻² e fm, theoretical 0.6 × 10⁻² e fm
- B(GT) for ¹⁰B(3⁺) → ¹⁰Be(2⁺₂): experimental 1.51 ± 0.21 (g_V²/4π), theoretical 1.7 (case 1) and 1.4 (case 2)
- B(GT) for ¹⁰B(3⁺) → ¹⁰Be(3⁺₁): experimental 0.49 ± 0.13 (g_V²/4π), theoretical 0.61 (case 1) and 0.58 (case 2)
- B(GT) for ¹⁰C(0⁺) → ¹⁰B(1⁺): experimental 6.25 (g_V²/4π), theoretical 4.4 (case 1) and 3.7 (case 2)
- Width parameter ν: 0.17 fm⁻² for case(1) and 0.19 fm⁻² for case(2)

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo et al. (1995) that used variation after parity projection only, and by Dote et al. who studied Be isotopes with constrained AMD but without full angular momentum projection. The results support the dimer model proposed by Von Oertzen (1996) describing 2α clusters with valence neutrons in molecular σ and π orbits. The calculations enable systematic comparison with new experimental β-decay data from charge-exchange reactions measured by Fujiwara et al., and provide predictions for unobserved states such as 3⁺, 4⁺, 6⁺, and 5⁻ in ¹⁰Be.
