# Non-local orbital-free density functional theory incorporating nuclear shell effects

> Incorporating nuclear shell effects within the framework of orbital-free density functional theory (DFT) has remained a longstanding challenge in nuclear physics. While the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem formally guarantees the existence of an orbital-free density functional that is capable of describing all many-body effects, including shell effects, practical attempts since the 1970s have consistently failed to capture such effects. This persistent difficulty has even led to the misconception that the orbital-free DFT is inherently unable to describe nuclear shell effects. Here we develop a {\it non-local} orbital-free DFT approach for atomic nuclei and demonstrate that nuclear shell effects can be successfully incorporated into the orbital-free DFT through the construction of a non-local kinetic energy density functional. In particular, we show that the non-local orbital-free functional yields a nucleon localization function that, as an established indicator of shell effects, exhibits consistent behavior with the exact Kohn-Sham solution.

## Key claim

The authors develop a non-local orbital-free density functional theory (OF-DFT) approach for atomic nuclei and demonstrate that nuclear shell effects can be successfully incorporated through a non-local kinetic energy density functional, disproving the misconception that OF-DFT is inherently unable to describe such effects. The claim is conditional on the specific construction of the non-local kernel via linear-response theory and the use of a density-dependent Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors construct a two-point non-local kinetic energy density functional (KEDF) by determining its kernel through the Thomas-Fermi limit and the linear-response theory of a uniform non-interacting Fermi gas, following the approach of Wang (1992). They implement this non-local KEDF with a Skyrme-type interaction (SkP) for the double magic nuclei 16O, 40Ca, 80Zr, and 140Yb, ignoring spin-orbit and Coulomb interactions. To identify shell effects without explicit orbitals, they evaluate the nucleon localization function (NLF) using the Kohn-Sham densities and compare the NLF from the non-local functional against results from semi-local functionals (Thomas-Fermi, von Weizsacker, and their combinations) and the exact Kohn-Sham solution.

## Key numbers

* The non-local functional with a constant Fermi momentum k_F = 0.80 fm^{-1} reproduces the trends of the Kohn-Sham NLFs for all four nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 80Zr, 140Yb).
* The non-local functional with a density-dependent k_F accurately replicates both the overall trends and the NLF fluctuations associated with shell effects for all four nuclei.
* The Thomas-Fermi functional (alpha=1, beta=0 in Eq. 2) yields a constant NLF of 0.5, failing to capture shell structure.
* The von Weizsacker functional (alpha=0, beta=1) yields a constant NLF of 1, failing to capture shell structure.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort since the 1970s to incorporate nuclear shell effects into orbital-free DFT, which had previously required additional shell corrections as in the Strutinsky method (Strutinsky, 1967, 1968). It contrasts with semi-local functionals (Thomas-Fermi, von Weizsacker, and their combinations) that have consistently failed to capture shell effects, as shown by Brack (1985) and Centelles (1990, 2007). The non-local approach, inspired by the kernel-based formulation of Wang (1992) for electronic systems, enables future applications to neutron star inner crusts, superheavy elements, and fission dynamics, while also providing a physically interpretable alternative to recent machine-learning-based OF-DFT approaches (Wu, 2022; Hizawa, 2023).
