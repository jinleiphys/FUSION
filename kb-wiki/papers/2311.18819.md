# Structure in the speed of sound: from neutron stars to heavy-ion collisions

> From the observation of both heavy neutron stars and light ones with small radii, one anticipates a steep rise in the speed of sound of nuclear matter as a function of baryon density up to values close to the causal limit. A question follows whether such behavior of the speed of sound in neutron-rich matter is compatible with the equation of state extracted from low-energy heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we consider a family of neutron star equations of state characterized by a steep rise in the speed of sound, and use the symmetry energy expansion to obtain equations of state applicable to the almost-symmetric nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions. We then compare collective flow data from low-energy heavy-ion experiments with results of simulations obtained using the hadronic transport code SMASH with the mean-field potential reproducing the density-dependence of the speed of sound. We show that equations of state featuring a peak in the speed of sound squared occurring at densities between 2-3 times the saturation density of normal nuclear matter, producing neutron stars of nearly M_max~2.5 M_Sun, are consistent with heavy-ion collision data.

## Key claim

Equations of state for neutron stars that feature a steep rise in the speed of sound squared at densities between 2 and 3 times nuclear saturation density, producing neutron stars with maximum masses near 2.5 solar masses, are consistent with collective flow data from low-energy heavy-ion collisions, contrary to earlier suggestions that such stiff equations of state are excluded.

## Method

The authors started with three neutron star equations of state from Tan et al. (2020, 2021) that exhibit a large peak in the speed of sound at different densities, then subtracted lepton contributions and applied the symmetry energy expansion to convert these neutron-rich equations of state into ones applicable to nearly symmetric nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions. They used the hadronic transport code SMASH with relativistic vector density functional mean-field potentials parametrized to reproduce the density dependence of the speed of sound, and compared simulation results for collective flow observables against experimental data from the HADES and STAR collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared peak occurs at densities between 2 and 3 times nuclear saturation density (n_sat) for the equations of state that are consistent with heavy-ion data
- Maximum neutron star masses reach nearly M_max approximately 2.5 solar masses for the consistent equations of state
- The three equations of state considered have peaks starting at n_B = 1.5, 2.0, and 3.0 times n_sat, respectively
- Maximum central densities for the three equations of state are approximately 3.5 n_sat, 4.5 n_sat, and 6 n_sat
- The low-energy heavy-ion collision energy range considered is 0.15 GeV to 10 GeV per nucleon incident kinetic energy, corresponding to center-of-mass energies from 1.95 GeV to 4.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oliinychenko et al. (2022), who first considered a large peak in the speed of sound within SMASH simulations and found a preference for the peak location around 2-3 n_sat, but did not thoroughly connect to neutron star equations of state. The paper directly addresses the tension raised by Fattoyev et al. (2020), who argued that massive neutron stars are excluded because their equations of state are inconsistent with heavy-ion data from Danielewicz et al. (2002). The methodology enables future combined constraints on dense nuclear matter from both neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision measurements.
