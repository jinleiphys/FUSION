# Global parameterization of $ππ$ scattering up to 2 GeV

> We provide global parameterizations of $ππ$ scattering $S0$ and $P$ partial waves up to roughly 2 GeV for phenomenological use. These parameterizations describe the output and uncertainties of previous partial-wave dispersive analyses of $ππ$, both in the real axis up to 1.12 GeV and in the complex plane within their applicability region, while also fulfilling forward dispersion relations up to 1.43 GeV. Above that energy we just describe the available experimental data. Moreover, the analytic continuations of these global parameterizations also describe accurately the dispersive determinations of the $σ/f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$ and $ρ(770)$ pole parameters.

## Key claim

The paper provides global parameterizations of ππ scattering S0 and P partial waves up to roughly 2 GeV that simultaneously describe dispersive results in the real axis up to 1.12 GeV and in the complex plane, while also fulfilling forward dispersion relations up to 1.43 GeV; above that energy the parameterizations only describe experimental data without dispersive constraints, and the authors note that none of the high-energy solutions have been checked against dispersion relations.

## Method

The authors construct analytic parameterizations for the scalar-isoscalar (S0) and vector (P) partial waves using conformal expansions below 1.4 GeV that incorporate the σ/f₀(500) and f₀(980) poles via a factorized S-matrix approach, with the f₀(980) described by a Chew-Mandelstam formalism and its pole position fixed to dispersive values. Above 1.4 GeV, they provide three separate phenomenological fits (solutions I, II, and III) matched continuously in value and derivative to the low-energy parameterization, corresponding to three incompatible experimental data sets from the CERN-Munich collaboration.

## Key numbers

- σ/f₀(500) pole position from dispersive analysis: √s_σ = (457⁺¹⁴₋₁₃) − i(279⁺¹¹₋₇) MeV with residue |g| = 3.57⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·¹³
- Conformal expansion continuation gives σ/f₀(500) pole at √s_σ = (474 ± 6) − i(254 ± 4) MeV
- Forward dispersion relations applied up to 1.43 GeV
- Partial-wave Roy and GKPY equations limited to 1.12 GeV
- Matching point between low-energy and high-energy parameterizations chosen at 1.4 GeV
- S0-wave conformal expansion uses N=5 terms for good χ²/d.o.f. in elastic region
- f₀(980) parameterization uses N=3 Chebyshev polynomials
- High-energy S0 phase requires 1 free parameter for solutions I and II, 3 for solution III
- High-energy S0 elasticity requires 1 free parameter for solution I
- Data sets extend up to 1.9 GeV (solution I), 1.8 GeV (solution II), and updated solution (- + -) from Ochs (2013)

## Context

This work extends the constrained fit to data (CFD) dispersive analysis of Garcia-Martin et al. (2011), which provided piecewise parameterizations limited to 1.43 GeV in the real axis and 1.12 GeV in partial-wave dispersion relations, by providing a single global analytic parameterization up to 2 GeV. The parameterizations enable straightforward implementation in phenomenological applications such as LHCb, Belle, and BaBar analyses, and provide analytic continuations consistent with dispersive pole determinations of the σ/f₀(500), f₀(980), and ρ(770) resonances from Garcia-Martin et al. (2011). The work also addresses the long-standing problem that simple Breit-Wigner or isobar models fail to describe the scalar-isoscalar channel correctly, particularly the broad σ/f₀(500) and its interplay with the narrow f₀(980).
