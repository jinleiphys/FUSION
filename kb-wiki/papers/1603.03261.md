# $^9$Be elastic scattering on $^{208}$Pb and $^{27}$Al within a four-body reaction framework

> We investigate the low-energy $^9$Be elastic scattering on two different targets (heavy, light) within a four-body framework using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method. The $^9$Be projectile is described in a $α+ α+ n$ three-body model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. We show that continuum couplings are important to describe the elastic cross section, especially at low energies and on heavy targets. The dipolar contribution to the elastic cross section at energies around the Coulomb barrier is important but small compared to the case of halo nuclei. The effect of the projectile low-energy resonances is also relevant. The agreement with the available experimental data supports the reliability of the method to describe reactions induced by three-body projectiles including more than one charged particle.

# $^9$Be elastic scattering on $^{208}$Pb and $^{27}$Al within a four-body reaction framework

## Key claim

Continuum couplings are important for describing $^9$Be elastic scattering, especially at low energies and on heavy targets, but the dipolar contribution to the elastic cross section at energies around the Coulomb barrier, while significant, is smaller than in the case of halo nuclei such as $^6$He or $^{11}$Li. The effect of the projectile low-energy resonances is also relevant, and the agreement with available experimental data supports the reliability of the four-body CDCC method for three-body projectiles containing more than one charged particle.

## Method

The authors describe $^9$Be as an $\alpha+\alpha+n$ three-body system using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates, diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian to obtain pseudo-states that represent both bound and continuum states. They then apply the four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method to compute elastic scattering cross sections on $^{208}$Pb and $^{27}$Al targets, including projectile-target interaction multipole couplings up to order $Q=5$ and solving coupled equations up to total angular momentum $J=301/2$. The calculations include $j^\pi=3/2^{\pm},1/2^{\pm},5/2^{\pm}$ states up to a maximum excitation energy cutoff, with the three-body force parameters adjusted to reproduce the experimental ground state energy and radius of $^9$Be.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of $^9$Be: 1.5736 MeV below the $\alpha+\alpha+n$ threshold.
- Ground state charge radius: $r_{\rm ch} = 2.50$ fm (experimental value: $2.519 \pm 0.012$ fm).
- Ground state quadrupole moment: $Q_2 = 4.82$ e fm$^2$ (experimental value: $5.29 \pm 0.04$ e fm$^2$).
- Coulomb barrier for $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb: approximately 47 MeV.
- Calculations performed at laboratory energies $E_{lab} = 60$, 44, and 38 MeV for $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb.
- THO basis parameters: $b = 0.7$ fm, $\gamma = 1.2$ fm$^{1/2}$, $\xi = 4$.
- Maximum hypermomentum $K_{max} = 10$ used for converged reaction calculations.
- Differences between four-body CDCC calculations and experimental data for $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region are below 6%.

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC calculations for $^9$Be that used two-body approximations (Pandit 2011) and three-body models with different pseudo-state methods (Descouvemont 2015, Descouvemont 2015b) to a four-body framework using the analytical THO basis. The study builds on earlier applications of the THO method to $^6$He (Casal 2013) and to $^9$Be structure (Casal 2014). The results enable systematic studies of reactions induced by three-body projectiles with more than one charged particle, and the comparison with data on both heavy ($^{208}$Pb) and light ($^{27}$Al) targets tests the reliability of the three-body description of $^9$Be. The work also highlights open questions regarding discrepancies between converged CDCC calculations and experimental data in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region for $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb at energies around and below the Coulomb barrier.
