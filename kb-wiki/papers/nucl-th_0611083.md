# Axial, induced pseudoscalar, and pion-nucleon form factors in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the nucleon form factors G_A and G_P of the isovector axial-vector current and the pion-nucleon form factor G_piN in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to and including order O(p^4). In addition to the standard treatment including the nucleon and pions, we also consider the axial-vector meson a_1 as an explicit degree of freedom. This is achieved by using the reformulated infrared renormalization scheme. We find that the inclusion of the axial-vector meson effectively results in one additional low-energy coupling constant that we determine by a fit to the data for G_A. The inclusion of the axial-vector meson results in an improved description of the experimental data for G_A, while the contribution to G_P is small.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the nucleon axial, induced pseudoscalar, and pion-nucleon form factors in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to order O(p^4), and find that including the axial-vector meson a1 as an explicit degree of freedom improves the description of experimental data for the axial form factor GA, while the contribution to the induced pseudoscalar form factor GP remains small. The improvement is not a strict chiral expansion effect but rather a resummation of higher-order contributions, as the results with and without the a1 meson do not differ at strict O(p^4).

## Method

The authors performed a manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the isovector axial-vector current form factors GA and GP and the pion-nucleon form factor GπN up to and including order O(p^4), using the reformulated infrared renormalization scheme. They included the standard pion and nucleon degrees of freedom, and additionally incorporated the axial-vector meson a1(1260) as an explicit degree of freedom using the vector-field formulation, with the a1 coupling to pions at order O(p^3) and to nucleons at order O(p^0). The single unknown low-energy coupling constant combination fA ga1 was determined by a fit to experimental data for GA in the momentum region 0 GeV^2 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 0.4 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Axial-vector coupling constant: gA = 1.2695(29) (from neutron beta decay, used as input)
- Global average axial mass from neutrino scattering: MA = (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV
- Updated neutrino scattering axial mass: MA = (1.001 ± 0.020) GeV
- Global average axial mass from pion electroproduction: M̄A = (1.068 ± 0.017) GeV
- Heavy baryon ChPT correction bringing neutrino and electroproduction axial masses into agreement: ΔMA = 0.056 GeV
- Fitted product of a1 coupling constants: fA ga1 ≈ 8.70 (dimensionless, from fit to GA data)
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling constant: gP = 8.29^{+0.24}_{-0.13} ± 0.52 (at muon capture kinematics q^2 = -0.88 mμ^2)
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy: Δ = (2.44^{+0.89}_{-0.51})% (using gπN = 13.21^{+0.11}_{-0.05})
- Monopole mass parameter for GπN: Λ = 894 MeV (for Δ = 2.44%)

## Context

This work extends previous heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of the axial form factor by Bernard et al. (1992, 1993, 1998) and Fearing et al. (1997) to a manifestly Lorentz-invariant framework, and parallels earlier work by Kubis et al. (2000) and Schindler et al. (2005) on electromagnetic form factors where vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) were included. The inclusion of the a1 meson as an explicit degree of freedom enables an improved description of GA at momentum transfers Q^2 ≥ 0.1 GeV^2 where strict O(p^4) ChPT fails, and provides a systematic framework for calculating higher-order corrections. The results for GP and GπN remain dominated by the pion pole and the Goldberger-Treiman relation, respectively.
