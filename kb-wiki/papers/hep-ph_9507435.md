# Photo- and electroproduction of the eta and eta-prime mesons

> We discuss the eta and eta prime photo- and electroproduction as a way to probe the nucleon resonance structure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that photo- and electroproduction of eta and eta-prime mesons provide a clean probe of nucleon resonance structure, but the authors hedge that their effective Lagrangian approach (ELA) is tree-level only and that firm conclusions about unitarity and coupled-channel effects cannot yet be drawn due to disputed data in the strong sector.

## Method

The authors apply an effective Lagrangian approach (ELA) at tree level to describe the photoproduction reaction γ + p → p + η, dominated by the N*(1535) resonance, and fit the model to precise Mainz photoproduction data. For eta-prime photoproduction, they extend the same ELA framework to the older ABBHHM collaboration data from 1968, incorporating s- and u-channel form factors to maintain unitarity. The electroproduction analysis uses older data from Brasse et al. and the thesis of Benmerrouche.

## Key numbers

- The electrostrong parameter ξ for N*(1535) excitation and decay extracted from Mainz data is ξ = (2.20 ± 0.15) × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹.
- The Mainz photoproduction data cover photon energies from 716 MeV to 783 MeV, with the fit shown at Eγ = 716 MeV and Eγ = 783 MeV.
- The Bates data point at Eγ = 753 MeV agrees with the ELA fit, while the point at Eγ = 729 MeV disagrees.
- For eta-prime photoproduction, the form factor cutoff parameter Λ² is of order 1 GeV².
- The electroproduction data from Brasse et al. cover Q² ≤ 3 GeV² and show flat angular distributions.

## Context

This work extends the earlier effective Lagrangian approach of Benmerrouche and Mukhopadhyay (1992) by incorporating the precise new Mainz photoproduction data from Krusche et al. (1995) and the Bates data from Dytman et al. (1995). The eta-prime photoproduction analysis is the first theoretical treatment of that reaction, motivated by quark model predictions from Capstick and Roberts (1994). The paper enables future comparisons with coupled-channel analyses by Tanabe and Bennhold (1995) and with upcoming CEBAF experiments on eta-prime photoproduction, while noting that unresolved debates between Bennhold and Svarc (1995) prevent firm conclusions about unitarity effects.
