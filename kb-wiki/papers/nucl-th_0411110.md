# A Multi-Phase Transport Model for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We describe in detail how the different components of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, that uses the Heavy Ion Jet Interaction Generator (HIJING) for generating the initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for modeling partonic scatterings, the Lund string fragmentation model or a quark coalescence model for hadronization, and A Relativistic Transport (ART) model for treating hadronic scatterings, are improved and combined to give a coherent description of the dynamics of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We also explain the way parameters in the model are determined, and discuss the sensitivity of predicted results to physical input in the model. Comparisons of these results to experimental data, mainly from heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), are then made in order to extract information on the properties of the hot dense matter formed in these collisions.

## Key claim

The AMPT model provides a coherent description of relativistic heavy ion collisions by combining multiple transport components, but its predictions depend sensitively on input parameters such as the parton screening mass and string fragmentation parameters, and the model does not assume full thermal or chemical equilibrium of the created matter.

## Method

The AMPT model uses HIJING to generate initial conditions including minijet partons and soft strings, then evolves partonic scatterings via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) with only two-body gluon elastic scatterings regulated by a Debye screening mass. Hadronization proceeds either through Lund string fragmentation (default model) or a quark coalescence model (string melting version), followed by hadronic scatterings treated with an extended version of the ART relativistic transport model that includes baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson interactions as well as baryon-antibaryon annihilation and production channels.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at RHIC at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are the primary system studied
- Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.5$ TeV are predicted for future study
- The parton screening mass $\mu = 3$ fm$^{-1}$ gives a gluon-gluon elastic cross section of about 3 mb
- Strange quark suppression factor of 0.30 relative to light quark-antiquark pairs in Lund string fragmentation
- Hadrons from string fragmentation receive an additional proper formation time of 0.7 fm/$c$
- The nucleon-nucleon soft cross section is $\sigma_s(s)=57$ mb at high energies with $p_0=2$ GeV/$c$
- Quark masses used in string melting: $m_u=5.6$ MeV/$c^2$, $m_d=9.9$ MeV/$c^2$, $m_s=199$ MeV/$c^2$
- The critical energy density for QCD phase transition is approximately 1 GeV/fm$^3$
- Kaon elastic scatterings with nucleons and baryon resonances use a constant cross section of 10 mb
- The $p\bar p$ annihilation cross section is parameterized as $\sigma_{p\bar p}=67$ mb $/p_{\rm lab}^{0.7}$ with a maximum of 400 mb imposed at low $p_{\rm lab}$

## Context

This work extends earlier transport approaches such as HIJING (Wang, 1991), ZPC (Zhang, 1997), and ART (Li, 1995) by combining them into a unified framework that includes both partonic and hadronic phases with explicit string melting for high energy density regions. The model enables systematic studies of non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy ion collisions from SPS through RHIC to LHC energies, and provides a tool to extract properties of the hot dense matter by comparing predictions to experimental data on particle yields, transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and two-particle correlations.
