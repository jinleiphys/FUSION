# Exploring the light-quark interaction

> Two basic motivations for an upgraded JLab facility are the needs: to determine the essential nature of light-quark confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB); and to understand nucleon structure and spectroscopy in terms of QCD's elementary degrees of freedom. During the next ten years a programme of experiment and theory will be conducted that can address these questions. We present a Dyson-Schwinger equation perspective on this effort with numerous illustrations, amongst them: an interpretation of string-breaking; a symmetry-preserving truncation for mesons; the nucleon's strangeness sigma-term; and the neutron's charge distribution.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) methods can provide a unified, symmetry-preserving framework to simultaneously describe meson and baryon properties from QCD's elementary degrees of freedom, with the specific claim that the infrared behavior of QCD's β-function can be charted by comparing DSE predictions with experimental data on nucleon form factors, particularly after the JLab upgrade.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator (gap equation) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) for meson bound states, using a systematically improvable truncation scheme (rainbow-ladder and beyond). For baryons, they use a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation that incorporates nonpointlike diquark correlations in the color-antitriplet channel, with the quark-quark scattering matrix approximated by retaining only the lightest J^P = 0^+ and 1^+ diquark channels. They construct a symmetry-preserving kernel for the BSE that is matched to any reasonable Ansatz for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex, using the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity as a constraint.

## Key numbers

- String breaking occurs when static quark and antiquark are separated by ≈ 1.25 fm, with critical energy E_c ≈ 1.25 GeV
- Constituent s-quark mass M_S ≈ 0.55 GeV, with m_s ≈ 25 m_u
- Dressed-quark mass at infrared momenta is roughly 100-times larger than the light-quark current-mass
- DCSB is responsible for 98% of a proton's mass
- Pseudoscalar meson mixing angles: θ_{ηη'} = -15°, θ_{π^0 η} = 1°
- Rainbow-ladder scalar meson binding measure: ε_σ^{RL} = (0.3 ± 0.1)
- BC-consistent scalar meson binding measure: ε_σ^{BC} ≲ 0.1
- Nucleon σ-term fraction: f_N^u = σ_N/M_N ≈ 6%
- Dressed-quark σ-term fractions: f_U^u = 6.5%, f_U^s = 2.4%
- Predicted nucleon strangeness σ-term: f_N^s ≈ 2.4%
- Neutron electric form factor second zero at Q^2 ≈ 11 GeV^2
- F_1^{nu}(q^2) becomes negative at q^2 ≈ 7 GeV^2; G_E^{nu}(q^2) becomes negative at q^2 ≈ 10 GeV^2; G_E^{nd}(q^2) becomes positive at q^2 ≈ 9 GeV^2
- DSE dipole mass fit: m_D = 1.06 M_N, 18% larger than empirical dipole mass m_D^{emp} = 0.89 M_N
- Transition boundary between constituent- and current-quark domains lies at p^2 ∼ 0.6 GeV^2
- To scan mass function on p^2 ∈ [0.5, 1.0] GeV^2 requires q^2 ∈ [5, 10] GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to hadron physics developed by Roberts and collaborators (1994, 2007) and Maris and collaborators (2003), providing a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme that goes beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation. The paper directly addresses the longstanding shortcoming of rainbow-ladder truncation that the vector-axial-vector meson splitting is too small (Maris, 2006), and the "missing resonance" problem in baryon spectroscopy. The results enable future comparison with JLab upgrade data on nucleon form factors at Q^2 up to 10 GeV^2, which will allow charting the infrared behavior of QCD's β-function and the momentum evolution of the dressed-quark mass function.
