# Kubo Formulae for Second-Order Hydrodynamic Coefficients

> At second order in gradients, conformal relativistic hydrodynamics depends on the viscosity eta and on five additional "second-order" hydrodynamical coefficients tauPi, kappa, lambda1, lambda2, and lambda3. We derive Kubo relations for these coefficients, relating them to equilibrium, fully retarded 3-point correlation functions of the stress tensor. We show that the coefficient lambda3 can be evaluated directly by Euclidean means and does not in general vanish.

# Kubo Formulae for Second-Order Hydrodynamic Coefficients

## Key claim

The five second-order hydrodynamic coefficients in conformal relativistic hydrodynamics can be expressed as Kubo relations involving equilibrium, fully retarded three-point correlation functions of the stress tensor; the coefficient λ₃ does not in general vanish and can be evaluated directly by Euclidean means, contrary to earlier findings where it appeared zero at lowest order in both strong and weak coupling expansions.

## Method

The authors consider a conformal fluid in flat spacetime and introduce perturbatively weak, slowly varying metric perturbations h_μν(x) that generate shear and vorticity. They expand the expectation value of the stress tensor to second order in the metric perturbation using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path formalism, relating the response to fully retarded three-point correlation functions G_{raa} of the stress tensor. By choosing specific background metric perturbations (h_xz, h_yz, h_x0, h_y0) that produce nonzero shear and vorticity at first order but generate Π^{xy} only at second order, they extract Kubo formulae for λ₁, λ₂, and λ₃ by matching the hydrodynamic constitutive relation to the correlation function expansion.

## Key numbers

- λ₃ = T²/12 for one weakly coupled real scalar field at leading order in weak coupling, evaluated from a triangle diagram contribution of T²/144 and a contact term contribution of T²/72
- η = i ∂_ω G^{xy,xy}_{ra}(ω,k)|_{ω=0=k} (Kubo relation for shear viscosity)
- κ = -∂²_{k_z} G^{xy,xy}_{ra}(ω,k)|_{ω=0=k} (Kubo relation for κ)
- η τ_π = ½ (∂²_ω G^{xy,xy}_{ra}(ω,k) - ∂²_{k_z} G^{xy,xy}_{ra}(ω,k))|_{ω=0=k} (Kubo relation for η τ_π)

## Context

This work extends the Kubo formalism for first-order transport coefficients and for the second-order coefficients τ_π and κ previously derived by Baier et al. (2008) (referred to as BRSSS) to the remaining three second-order coefficients λ₁, λ₂, and λ₃. The results put the definition of these coefficients on a solid field-theoretic footing and enable their direct calculation in both weakly coupled QCD (extending the work of Moore and York) and strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory (extending the work of Baier et al. and collaborators). The finding that λ₃ is a thermodynamic response to vorticity rather than a purely dynamical coefficient, evaluable via Euclidean correlation functions, opens the possibility of calculating it at all orders in coupling, unlike λ₁ and λ₂ which require real-time analytic continuation.
