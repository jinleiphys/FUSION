# Hadrons in dense and hot matter: implications of chiral symmetry restoration

> Recent developments on medium modifications of hadron properties in dense and hot hadronic matter are discussed. I will focus in particular on the behavior of spectral functions associated to collective scalar-isoscalar modes, kaons and vector mesons from ordinary nuclear matter to highly excited matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from SIS to SPS energies. Various theoretical approaches are presented in connection with the interpretation of experimental data. Special emphasis will be put on the role of chiral dynamics and chiral symmetry restoration. I will discuss in particular to which extent the broadening of the rho meson peak signals the onset of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed broadening and reshaping of hadronic spectral functions (particularly the rho meson) in dense and hot matter may signal the onset of chiral symmetry restoration, but this link is not absolute and the modification of spectral functions is not restricted to simple mass shifts.

## Method

The author reviews multiple theoretical approaches to in-medium hadron modifications, including the chiral reduction formalism for axial-vector mixing, QCD sum rules relating hadron spectra to fundamental condensates, many-body techniques using effective hadronic Lagrangians with chiral symmetry, and transport code calculations for heavy-ion collisions. The analysis covers scalar-isoscalar modes (sigma meson), kaons, and vector mesons (rho meson) across densities from ordinary nuclear matter to those achieved in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SIS to SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate at vacuum: approximately -(240 MeV)^3, from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation using f_pi = 94 MeV
- Nucleon sigma commutator Sigma_N approximately 45 MeV, with the pion cloud contribution Sigma_N^(pi) approximately 30 MeV
- 30% chiral symmetry restoration at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0)
- Lattice critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV for the transition to deconfined matter
- For the sigma meson spectral function at saturation density: peak height increased by a factor of 2 for alpha=0.2 and a factor of 4 for alpha=0.3 when including dropping sigma mass
- For the rho meson: QCD sum rule analysis gave a decrease of vector meson masses of about 20% at normal nuclear matter density
- For the thermal fireball model: trajectory starts at (T,rho_B)_in = (190 MeV, 2.55 rho_0) and goes to thermal freeze-out at (T,rho_B)_fo = (115 MeV, 0.33 rho_0)
- Full axial-vector mixing (epsilon=1/2) corresponding to full chiral symmetry restoration realized at T approximately 160 MeV

## Context

This review extends work on medium modifications of hadron properties, addressing the scaling laws proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) where hadron masses drop together with quark and gluon condensates, while also discussing alternative approaches that do not require dropping masses. The paper connects experimental results from the CHAOS collaboration at TRIUMF on pion-induced reactions, the CERES collaboration at CERN/SPS on dilepton production, and the KaoS and FOPI collaborations at SIS on kaon production. It enables future work on establishing the relationship between spectral function reshaping and chiral symmetry restoration, particularly through simultaneous study of chiral partner spectral functions such as the rho and a_1 mesons.
