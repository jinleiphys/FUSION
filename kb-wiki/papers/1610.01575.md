# Unexpected distribution of $\nu1f_{7/2}$ strength in the calcium isotopes at $N$=30

> The calcium isotopes have emerged as an important testing ground for new microscopically derived shell-model interactions, and a great deal of focus has been directed toward this region. We investigate the relative spectroscopic strengths associated with $1f_{7/2}$ neutron hole states in $^{47, 49}$Ca following one-neutron knockout reactions from $^{48,50}$Ca. The observed reduction of strength populating the lowest 7/2$^{-}_{1}$ state in $^{49}$Ca, as compared to $^{47}$Ca, is consistent with the description given by shell-model calculations based on two- and three-nucleon forces in the neutron $pf$ model space, implying a fragmentation of the $l$=3 strength to higher-lying states. The experimental result is inconsistent with both the GXPF1 interaction routinely used in this region of the nuclear chart and with microscopic calculations in an extended model space including the $\nu1g_{9/2}$ orbital.

## Key claim

The observed reduction in spectroscopic strength populating the lowest 7/2⁻₁ state in ⁴⁹Ca relative to ⁴⁷Ca is consistent with shell-model calculations based on two- and three-nucleon (NN+3N) forces in the neutron *pf* model space, but the extent of the reduction is larger in experiment than the *pf*-shell NN+3N calculation predicts. The experimental result is inconsistent with both the phenomenological GXPF1 interaction and with microscopic calculations in an extended model space including the ν1g₉/₂ orbital.

## Method

One-neutron knockout reactions were performed from ⁴⁸Ca and ⁵⁰Ca secondary beams produced by fragmentation of a 140-MeV/u ⁸²Se primary beam on a ⁹Be target at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The knockout residues (⁴⁷Ca and ⁴⁹Ca) were identified using the S800 spectrograph, and γ rays from excited states were detected with the GRETINA high-resolution γ-ray tracking array. Exclusive cross sections were extracted and compared to theoretical single-particle knockout cross sections to deduce experimental spectroscopic factors C²S, which were then normalized using quenching factors Rₛ from systematics.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the lowest 7/2⁻ state in ⁴⁷Ca (from ⁴⁸Ca knockout): C²S_exp^norm = 9.3(^{+1.2}_{-1.3})_{stat}(±1.9)_{sys}, consistent with the full 1f₇/₂ occupancy of 8
- Spectroscopic factor for the lowest 7/2⁻ state in ⁴⁹Ca (from ⁵⁰Ca knockout): C²S_exp^norm = 4.7(^{+0.6}_{-0.4})_{stat}(±0.9)_{sys}, significantly lower than in ⁴⁷Ca
- Ratio of spectroscopic factors for the 7/2⁻₁ state (⁵⁰Ca knockout / ⁴⁸Ca knockout): 0.51(±0.09)_{stat}(±0.15)_{sys}
- For the *pf* NN+3N calculation, the predicted ratio is 0.78; for GXPF1 and *pfg₉/₂* NN+3N, the ratio is approximately 1
- Spectroscopic factor for the ⁴⁹Ca ground state (ν2p₃/₂): C²S_exp^norm = 2.7(±0.4)_{stat}(±0.5)_{sys}
- Inclusive cross section for ⁴⁸Ca→⁴⁷Ca: 123(10) mb; for ⁵⁰Ca→⁴⁹Ca: 116(8) mb

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron single-particle occupancies in calcium isotopes, which have become a testing ground for microscopically derived shell-model interactions based on chiral effective field theory (Holt 2012, 2014; Hagen 2012). The results contradict the phenomenological GXPF1 interaction (Honma 2002, 2005) and NN+3N calculations including the ν1g₉/₂ orbital, while showing best agreement with NN+3N calculations restricted to the *pf* model space. The findings enable further refinement of shell-model interactions and raise questions about the role of the ν1g₉/₂ orbital, which is important for predicting the neutron dripline in calcium isotopes.
