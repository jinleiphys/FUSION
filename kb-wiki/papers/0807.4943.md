# Enhanced effect of quark mass variation in 229Th and limits from Oklo data

> The effects of the variation of the dimensionless strong interaction parameter Xq=mq/Lambda{QCD} (mq is the quark mass, Lambda{QCD} is the QCD scale) are enhanced about 1.5 x 10**5 times in the 7.6 eV "nuclear clock" transition between the ground and first excited states in the 229Th nucleus and about 1 x 10**8 times in the relative shift of the 0.1 eV compound resonance in 150Sm.The best terrestrial limit on the temporal variation of the fundamental constants, |delta(Xq)/Xq| < 4 x 10**-9 at 1.8 billion years ago (|d(Xq/Xq)/dt| < 2.2 x 10**-18 y**-1), is obtained from the shift of this Sm resonance derived from the Oklo natural nuclear reactor data. The results for 229Th and 150Sm are obtained by extrapolation from light nuclei where the many-body calculations can be performed more accurately. The errors produced by such extrapolation may be smaller than the errors of direct calculations in heavy nuclei. The extrapolation results are compared with the "direct" estimates obtained using the Walecka model. A number of numerical relations needed for the calculations of the variation effects in nuclear physics and atomic spectroscopy have been obtained: for the nuclear binding energy delta(E)/E ~ -1.45 delta(mq)/mq, for the spin-orbit intervals delta(Eso)/Eso ~ -0.22 delta(mq)/mq, for the nuclear radius delta(r)/r ~ 0.3 delta(mq)/mq (in units of Lambda{QCD}); for the shifts of nuclear resonances and weakly bound energy levels delta(Er) ~ 10 delta(Xq)/Xq MeV.

## Key claim

The enhancement of quark mass variation effects in the 7.6 eV nuclear clock transition in 229Th and the 0.1 eV compound resonance in 150Sm is real, contrary to a claim by Hayes (2007) that no enhancement exists; the enhancement coefficients are obtained by extrapolation from accurate many-body calculations in light nuclei, and the errors from this extrapolation may be smaller than those of direct calculations in heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon (AV18+UIX) interactions to compute binding energies and spin-orbit splittings in light nuclei (A=2-9). They then folded in Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) results for the dependence of hadron masses on the average light quark mass mq to obtain sensitivity coefficients K = (δE/E)/(δXq/Xq) and Kso = (δEso/Eso)/(δmq/mq). These coefficients were extrapolated to 229Th and 150Sm using the assumption that the relative variations of bulk binding energy and spin-orbit interaction are the same across nuclei, with common factors like A-1/3 canceling out. For the nuclear radius variation, the VMC trial wave functions were rescaled and reminimized for each hadron mass shift.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor for the 7.6 eV transition in 229Th: δω/ω = 1.5 × 10^5 × δXq/Xq, corresponding to δω = 1.2 × (δXq/Xq) MeV
- Enhancement factor for the 0.1 eV compound resonance in 150Sm: approximately 1 × 10^8 times in the relative shift
- Best terrestrial limit on temporal variation of Xq: |δXq/Xq| < 4 × 10^-9 at 1.8 billion years ago, giving |dXq/Xq/dt| < 2.2 × 10^-18 y^-1
- Average sensitivity of nuclear binding energy to quark mass: δE/E ≈ -1.45 × δmq/mq (from Table of K values for 12 light nuclei)
- Average sensitivity of spin-orbit intervals to quark mass: δEso/Eso ≈ -0.22 × δmq/mq (from 5He, 7He, and 9Be, excluding 7Li)
- Average shift of nuclear resonances and weakly bound levels: δEr ≈ 10 × δXq/Xq MeV (from light nuclei with A=6-9)
- Average sensitivity of nuclear radius to quark mass: δr/r ≈ 0.3 × δmq/mq (in units of ΛQCD)
- Spin-orbit constant in 5He: Vls = -0.83 MeV, matching the value in 229Th
- Width of the 229Th excited state: estimated at 10^-4 Hz

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Flambaum (2006) of a five-orders-of-magnitude enhancement in 229Th and contradicts the claim by Hayes (2007) that no enhancement exists. The authors provide improved enhancement coefficients compared to earlier estimates from Flambaum (2006) (0.4 × 10^5) and He (2007) (0.7 × 10^5), obtaining 1.5 × 10^5 for the new measured transition energy of 7.6 eV. The Oklo reactor limits on Xq variation improve upon earlier analyses by Dyson (1976), Gould (2006), Petrov (2006), and Fujii (2007), and the nuclear radius sensitivity results enable searches for fundamental constant variation in microwave atomic clocks such as Cs and Hg+ standards.
