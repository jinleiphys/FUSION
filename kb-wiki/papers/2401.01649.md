# Shell-model representations of the microscopic version of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model

> The structure of the irreducible collective spaces of the group $Sp(12,R)$, which many-particle nuclear states are classified according to the chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(6) \supset SO(6) \supset SU_{pn}(3) \otimes SO(2) \supset SO(3)$ of the proton-neutron symplectic model (PNSM), is considered in detail. This chain of the PNSM was shown to correspond to a microscopic shell-model version of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model. The construction of the relevant shell-model representations of the $Sp(12,R)$ group along this chain is considered for three nuclei with varying collective properties and from different mass regions. It is shown that the $SU_{pn}(3)$ basis states of the $Sp(12,R)$ representations are always Pauli allowed for $\upsilon \geq \upsilon_{0}$, but organized in a different way into different $SO(6)$ shells. This is in contrast to the case of filling the levels of the standard three-dimensional harmonic oscillator and using the plethysm operation. Although the $SU_{pn}(3)$ multiplets with $\upsilon < \upsilon_{0}$ are not all Pauli forbidden, it is safe to discard them, as it was actually done in the practical applications.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $SU_{pn}(3)$ basis states of $Sp(12,R)$ representations along the chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(6) \supset SO(6) \supset SU_{pn}(3) \otimes SO(2) \supset SO(3)$ are always Pauli allowed for seniority $\upsilon \geq \upsilon_{0}$, but are organized differently into $SO(6)$ shells compared to the standard three-dimensional harmonic oscillator filling with plethysm; the $SU_{pn}(3)$ multiplets with $\upsilon < \upsilon_{0}$ are not all Pauli forbidden but can be safely discarded in practical applications.

## Method

The author constructs shell-model irreducible representations of the $Sp(12,R)$ group along the dynamical symmetry chain that defines the microscopic version of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model within the proton-neutron symplectic model (PNSM). The symplectic basis states are classified by reducing $Sp(12,R)$ through $U(6)$ and $SO(6)$ to $SU_{pn}(3) \otimes SO(2)$ and finally $SO(3)$, and the Pauli-allowed $SU_{pn}(3)$ content is analyzed for three nuclei with varying collective properties. The construction uses the symplectic raising and lowering operators expressed as bilinear combinations of harmonic oscillator creation and annihilation operators for the two-component proton-neutron system.

## Key numbers

- For $^{20}$Ne, the leading proton-neutron $SU(3)$ multiplet is $(8,0)$, obtained from the configuration $(0)^{4}(1)^{12}(2)^{4}$ with 4 nucleons in the $\mathcal{N}=2$ $sd$ shell.
- The $Sp(12,R)$ irreducible representation for $^{20}$Ne is defined by the intrinsic $U(6)$ structure $[10,2,2,2,2,2]_{6} \equiv [8]_{6}$ with maximal seniority $SO(6)$ irrep $\upsilon_{0} = 8$.
- For $^{106}$Cd, the relevant $Sp(12,R)$ irreducible representation is 0p-0h $[12]_{6}$ with maximal seniority $SO(6)$ irrep $\upsilon_{0} = 12$.
- The harmonic oscillator energy separation between major shells is given by $\hbar\omega = 41A^{-1/3}$.

## Context

This work extends the embedding of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model into the two-component shell-model theory within the PNSM framework, previously established by Ganev (2023) and Ganev (2024). It provides the detailed construction of Pauli-allowed shell-model representations for the microscopic version of the Bohr-Mottelson model, which was defined by one of the dynamical symmetry chains of the PNSM. The results enable practical applications of this microscopic collective model to nuclei across different mass regions, showing how the $SU_{pn}(3)$ basis states are organized by $SO(6)$ seniority shells rather than by the standard plethysm reduction $U(d) \supset SU(3)$ used in earlier shell-model approaches.
