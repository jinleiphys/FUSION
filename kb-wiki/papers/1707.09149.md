# Maris polarization in neutron-rich nuclei

> We present a theoretical study of the Maris polarization effect and its application in quasi-free reactions to assess information on the structure of exotic nuclei. We discuss the uncertainties in the calculations of triple differential cross sections and of analyzing powers due the choices of various nucleon-nucleon interactions the optical potentials and limitations of the method. Our calculations explore a large number of choices for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering. Our study implies that polarization variables in (p,2p) reactions in inverse kinematics can be an effective probe of single-particle structure of nuclei in radioactive-beam facilities.

## Key claim

The Maris polarization effect in (p,2p) reactions shows a steady increase in effective polarization of knocked-out protons with neutron number, but this increase cannot be straightforwardly related to neutron-skin thickness because it also depends on the overall nuclear size and surface diffuseness, and the absolute magnitude of the analyzing power is strongly dependent on the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction and optical potential.

## Method

The authors performed Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) calculations using a partial wave expansion method for (p,2p) reactions in inverse kinematics, exploring a large number of choices for nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering. They constructed optical potentials from a folding model using Franey-Love interaction with nuclear densities from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations and sharp-cutoff models, and calculated triple differential cross sections and analyzing powers for various nuclei including tin isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca(p,2p)$^{39}$K at incident proton energy $E_p = 148$ MeV, cross sections are given in units of $\mu$b sr$^{-2}$ MeV$^{-1}$ as a function of recoil momentum $p_{A-1}$
- For (p,2p) reactions with $^6$Li, $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ca at incident proton energy of 392 MeV, analyzing powers and triple-differential cross sections were calculated
- For $^{16}$O(p,2p) reaction at 200 MeV, analyzing powers for proton knockout from $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ states were calculated with one proton detected at $30^\circ$ and the other at $-30^\circ$
- For tin isotopes at $E_p = 200$ MeV with protons detected at $\theta = 35^\circ$ and $\theta = -35^\circ$, the effective polarization increases by more than 30% along the tin isotopic chain
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: $V_0 = [-57.8 \pm 33(N-Z)/A]$ MeV, $V_{SO} = [-22 \pm 14(N-Z)/A]$ MeV, $a = 0.65$ fm, $R = 1.2A^{1/3}$ fm

## Context

This work extends the Maris polarization effect, originally proposed by Maris (1959) and developed by Jacob and Maris (1976) and others, to neutron-rich nuclei studied with radioactive beams at facilities like GSI and RIKEN. It builds on prior (p,2p) reaction studies by Roos (1978), Chant and Roos (1977, 1983), and more recent work by Aumann, Bertulani, and collaborators (2013) on quasi-free scattering in inverse kinematics. The results enable future experiments using polarization variables in (p,2p) reactions as probes of single-particle structure in exotic nuclei, though the authors caution that complementary measurements are needed to constrain optical potential and NN-interaction choices.
