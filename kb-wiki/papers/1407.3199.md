# J=0, J=J_{max} and quadrupole pairing

> We consider 2 neutrons and 2 protons in the g_{9/2} shell.Wave functions and energy levels are obtained for various interactions. The wave functions for states with total angular momentum I greater or equal to 10 are not affected by what the pairing interacton (J=0 T=1) is.Other parts of the interaction are therefore of increased importance.

## Key claim
For two neutrons and two protons in the \(g_{9/2}\) shell, wave functions of states with total angular momentum \(I \ge 10\) are completely unaffected by the choice of \(J=0\) (isospin \(T=1\)) pairing interaction, making other parts of the interaction, especially quadrupole pairing, critically important for those high-spin states.

## Method
The authors performed single-\(j\) shell model calculations for two protons and two neutrons in the \(g_{9/2}\) orbital, diagonalizing schematic interactions where only selected two-body matrix elements \(E(J)\) are nonzero (e.g., \(E(9)\), \(E(0,9)\), \(E(1,9)\), \(E(2,9)\), \(E(1,2,9)\)). They computed overlaps between the resulting wave functions and those from the realistic CCGI interaction, as well as with unitary \(9j\) coefficient vectors, and compared energy splittings between the \(I=16^+\) and \(I=0^+\) states.

## Key numbers
- Overlap of \(E(9)\) wave function with CCGI for \(I=6\): 0.6795; for \(I=8\): 0.2375; for \(I=10\): 0.6860.
- Overlap of \(E(0,9)\) wave function with CCGI for \(I=6\): 0.9361; for \(I=8\): 0.9858; for \(I=10\): unchanged at 0.6860.
- Overlap of \(E(1,2,9)\) wave function with CCGI for \(I=10\): 0.9659.
- Energy splitting \(E(16^+) - E(0^+)\) for CCGI: 5.244 MeV; for \(E(9)\): -1.0589 MeV (i.e., \(16^+\) is ground state); for \(E(1,2,9)\): 4.9467 MeV.
- Wave function coefficients \(C(J,J)\) for CCGI: \(C(0,0)=0.7725\), \(C(2,2)=0.5289\), \(C(4,4)=0.2915\), \(C(6,6)=0.1704\), \(C(8,8)=0.10210\).

## Context
This work extends the classic study of McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964) on \(f_{7/2}\) shell nuclei and the more recent calculations by the Swedish group (Qi and collaborators) on \(g_{9/2}\) shell systems like \(^{96}\)Cd. It sharpens the understanding of \(J=J_{\text{max}}\) pairing (here \(J=9\)) and shows that adding \(J=0\) pairing cures wave function problems for intermediate spins \(I=6,8\) but not for \(I=10\), where quadrupole pairing (\(J=2\)) becomes essential. The results enable future work on how proton-neutron interactions in high-spin states affect the ordering of yrast bands in \(N=Z\) nuclei near \(^{100}\)Sn.
