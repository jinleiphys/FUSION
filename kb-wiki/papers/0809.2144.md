# Shell-model calculations and realistic effective interactions

> A review is presented of the development and current status of nuclear shell-model calculations in which the two-body effective interaction is derived from the free nucleon-nucleon potential. The significant progress made in this field within the last decade is emphasized, in particular as regards the so-called V-low-k approach to the renormalization of the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. In the last part of the review we first give a survey of realistic shell-model calculations from early to present days. Then, we report recent results for neutron-rich nuclei near doubly magic 132Sn and for the whole even-mass N=82 isotonic chain. These illustrate how shell-model effective interactions derived from modern nucleon-nucleon potentials are able to provide an accurate description of nuclear structure properties.

## Key claim

This review argues that shell-model calculations using two-body effective interactions derived from modern free nucleon-nucleon potentials can describe nuclear structure properties as accurately as traditional empirical interactions, with the caveat that single-nucleon energies are generally taken from experiment, so the calculation contains essentially no free parameters.

## Method

The authors review the historical development and current status of deriving shell-model effective interactions from free nucleon-nucleon potentials, focusing on two renormalization approaches: the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method and the more recent V-low-k approach that integrates out high-momentum components via renormalization group techniques. They survey calculations performed over four decades and present selected recent results for neutron-rich nuclei near doubly magic 132Sn and for the even-mass N=82 isotonic chain, comparing predictions from different modern potentials against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Cohen and Kurath p-shell calculation determined 15 two-body matrix elements and 2 single-particle energies from 35 experimental energies of nuclei from A=8 to A=16.
- The Brown and Wildenthal sd-shell study determined 66 parameters by least-squares fit to 447 binding and excitation energies, achieving an rms deviation of 185 keV.
- The Nijmegen group final database consisted of 1787 pp and 2514 np data, with combined pp+np analysis yielding chi-squared per datum approximately 1.
- The CD-Bonn potential predicts a deuteron D-state probability of 4.85 percent, while NijmI, NijmII, and Reid93 predict 5.66, 5.64, and 5.70 percent respectively.
- The CD-Bonn potential achieves chi-squared per datum of 1.02 for 4301 data in the Nijmegen database and 1.02 for the updated 1999 database containing 5990 data total.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on realistic shell-model calculations that began with Kuo and Brown (1966) using the Hamada-Johnston potential, and is similar in spirit to the review by Hjorth-Jensen et al. (1995). It covers developments over the last decade that brought these calculations into the mainstream of nuclear structure, particularly the V-low-k approach introduced by Bogner et al. (2001-2003), and is complementary to reviews by Brown (2001), Dean (2004), Caurier et al. (2005), and Otsuka (2001) which focus on large-scale calculations and phenomenological applications.
