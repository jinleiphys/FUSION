# Novel Bayesian neural network based approach for nuclear charge radii

> Charge radius is one of the most fundamental properties of a nucleus. However, a precise description of the evolution of charge radii along an isotopic chain is highly nontrivial, as reinforced by recent experimental measurements. In this paper, we propose a novel approach which combines a three-parameter formula and a Bayesian neural network. We find that the novel approach can describe the charge radii of all $A\ge40$ and $Z\ge20$ nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation about 0.015 fm. In particular, the charge radii of the calcium isotopic chain are reproduced very well, including the parabolic behavior and strong odd-even staggerings. We further test the approach for the potassium isotopes and show that it can describe well the experimental data within uncertainties.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a hybrid model combining a simple three-parameter formula (the NP formula) with a Bayesian neural network (BNN) can describe nuclear charge radii for all nuclei with A >= 40 and Z >= 20 with a root-mean-square deviation of about 0.015 fm, but this performance depends critically on using four physically motivated input features (Z, A, delta, P) rather than just Z and A, and the validation set RMS deviation is larger (0.0187 fm) than the training set value.

## Method

The authors combine the isospin-dependent NP formula of Nerlo-Pomorska and Pomorski (1994) with a Bayesian neural network that has one hidden layer of 40 neurons. The BNN is trained to predict the residuals between experimental charge radii and the NP formula predictions, using as inputs the proton number Z, mass number A, a pairing term delta = [(-1)^Z + (-1)^N]/2, and a promiscuity factor P = (nu_p * nu_n)/(nu_p + nu_n) that encodes shell closure effects relative to magic numbers. The training set consists of 820 experimental data points from Angeli (2013), and the validation set contains 113 more recent data points from Goodacre (2020) and Li (2021), all for nuclei with A >= 40 and Z >= 20.

## Key numbers

- The D4 model (NP formula plus four-input BNN) achieves an RMS deviation of 0.0143 fm on the training set, 0.0187 fm on the validation set, and 0.0149 fm on the entire set of 933 nuclei.
- The NP formula alone yields RMS deviations of 0.0394 fm (training), 0.0300 fm (validation), and 0.0384 fm (entire set).
- The D4 model reduces the RMS deviation relative to the NP formula by 64% on the training set, 38% on the validation set, and 61% on the entire set.
- The D2 model (NP formula plus two-input BNN using only Z and A) achieves RMS deviations of 0.0161 fm (training), 0.0250 fm (validation), and 0.0174 fm (entire set), with reductions relative to NP of 59%, 16%, and 55% respectively.
- A modified NP formula using delta and P as additional degrees of freedom (Sheng, 2015) achieves an RMS deviation of about 0.0223 fm, compared to 0.0143 fm for the D4 model.

## Context

This work extends the BNN approach of Utama (2016), who used only Z and A as inputs and achieved a 50% RMS reduction relative to a relativistic mean field model but failed to reproduce odd-even staggerings in calcium isotopes. The authors are motivated by the success of An (2020, 2021) in describing calcium and potassium charge radii using a modified relativistic mean field plus BCS ansatz with Cooper pair condensation corrections. The hybrid approach enables reliable predictions with controlled uncertainties for isotopic chains where data are limited, as demonstrated for potassium isotopes, and suggests that providing physically motivated features to BNNs is crucial when data are scarce.
