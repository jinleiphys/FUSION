# Nuclear ground-state properties probed by the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach

> Using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework with separable pairing force coupled with the latest covariant density functionals, i.e., PC-L3R, PC-X, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX, we systematically explore the ground-state properties of all isotopes of Z=8-110. These properties consist of the binding energies, one- and two-neutron separation energies ($S_\mathrm{n}$ and $S_\mathrm{2n}$), root-mean-square radius of matter, of neutron, of proton, and of charge distributions, Fermi surfaces, ground-state spins and parities. We then predict the edges of nuclear landscape and bound nuclei for the isotopic chains from oxygen (Z=8) to darmstadtium (Z=110) based on these latest covariant density functionals. The number of bound nuclei predicted by PC-L3R, PC-X, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX, are 9004, 9162, 6799, and 7112, respectively. The root-mean-square deviations of $S_\mathrm{n}$ ($S_\mathrm{2n}$) yielded from PC-L3R, PCX, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX are 0.962 (1.300) MeV, 0.920 (1.483) MeV, 0.993 (1.753) MeV, and 1.010 (1.544) MeV, respectively. The root-mean-square deviations of charge radius distributions of comparing the available experimental values with the theoretical counterparts resulted from PC-L3R, PC-X, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX are 0.035 fm, 0.037 fm, 0.035 fm, and 0.034 fm, respectively. We notice pronounced differences between the empirical and theoretical root-mean-square radii of neutron at nuclei near the neutron drip line of the Mg, Ca, and Kr isotopic chains, suggesting the possible existence of the halo or giant halo phenomena.

## Key claim

Using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework with four latest covariant density functionals (PC-L3R, PC-X, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX), the authors systematically predict ground-state properties for all isotopes from oxygen (Z=8) to darmstadtium (Z=110), but the predictions are limited by the spherical harmonic oscillator basis used and the absence of deformation effects, which the authors note cause increased deviations in separation energies and charge radii away from proton magic numbers.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations using a separable pairing force combined with four covariant density functionals: two point-coupling interactions (PC-L3R, PC-X) and two meson-exchange interactions (DD-PCX, DD-MEX). The configuration space was constructed from spherical harmonic oscillator wave functions, and the calculations were carried out for all isotopic chains from Z=8 to Z=110 to obtain binding energies, one- and two-neutron separation energies, root-mean-square radii of matter, neutron, proton, and charge distributions, Fermi surfaces, and ground-state spins and parities.

## Key numbers

* Number of bound nuclei predicted: 9004 (PC-L3R), 9162 (PC-X), 6799 (DD-PCX), and 7112 (DD-MEX)
* Root-mean-square deviations of one-neutron separation energies (Sn): 0.962 MeV (PC-L3R), 0.920 MeV (PC-X), 0.993 MeV (DD-PCX), and 1.010 MeV (DD-MEX)
* Root-mean-square deviations of two-neutron separation energies (S2n): 1.300 MeV (PC-L3R), 1.483 MeV (PC-X), 1.753 MeV (DD-PCX), and 1.544 MeV (DD-MEX)
* Root-mean-square deviations of charge radius distributions compared to experimental values: 0.035 fm (PC-L3R), 0.037 fm (PC-X), 0.035 fm (DD-PCX), and 0.034 fm (DD-MEX)

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of covariant density functional theory to predict nuclear ground-state properties across a wide range of the nuclear chart, building on earlier RHB studies such as Xia (2018) and the development of the PC-L3R functional by Liu (2023). The predictions of neutron drip lines and possible halo phenomena in Mg, Ca, and Kr isotopic chains provide guidance for future experiments at radioactive-ion beam facilities including FRIB, RIKEN RIBF, and HIAF, and the calculated mass tables can serve as nuclear physics inputs for astrophysical r-process modeling.
