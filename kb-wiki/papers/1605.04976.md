# Natural orbital description of the halo nucleus 6He

> Ab initio calculations of nuclei face the challenge of simultaneously describing strong short-range internucleon correlations and the long-range properties of weakly-bound halo nucleons. Natural orbitals, which diagonalize the one-body density matrix, provide a basis which is better matched to the physical structure of the many-body wave function. We demonstrate that the use of natural orbitals significantly improves convergence for ab initio no-core configuration interaction calculations of the neutron halo nucleus 6He, relative to the traditional oscillator basis.

## Key claim

Natural orbitals, obtained by diagonalizing the one-body density matrix from an initial no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculation in the harmonic oscillator basis, significantly improve convergence for *ab initio* calculations of the neutron halo nucleus 6He relative to the traditional oscillator basis, though full convergence is still not achieved.

## Method

The authors performed NCCI calculations for 6He using the JISP16 two-body internucleon interaction plus Coulomb interaction, first in a harmonic oscillator basis with truncations Nmax up to 16 and oscillator energies from 10 to 40 MeV. They then diagonalized the scalar one-body density matrix from the ground-state wave function to obtain natural orbitals, transformed the Hamiltonian into this new basis, and repeated the NCCI calculations. Infrared basis extrapolation schemes were applied to estimate converged values for energy and proton radius.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy for 6He is 0.97 MeV, out of a total binding energy of 29.27 MeV
- The experimental RMS point-proton radius increases from 1.462(6) fm for 4He to 1.934(9) fm for 6He
- Extrapolated ground state energy at hw=20 MeV and Nmax=16 gives approximately -28.80 MeV with natural orbitals, compared to the experimental binding energy of 29.27 MeV (JISP16 underbinds by about 0.5 MeV)
- Extrapolated proton radius at hw=20 MeV and Nmax=16 gives approximately 1.82 fm, a shortfall of about 0.12 fm (6%) compared to the experimental value of 1.934(9) fm
- The improvement in convergence from natural orbitals ranges from approximately one step in Nmax near the variational minimum (hw approximately 15-20 MeV) to several steps in Nmax at the ends of the hw range

## Context

This work extends previous NCCI calculations for 6He (Caprio 2014) and applies natural orbital techniques, long used in atomic electron-structure calculations (Shull 1955, Davidson 1972), to nuclear *ab initio* calculations. The approach addresses the challenge of simultaneously describing short-range internucleon correlations and long-range halo properties. The improved basis parameter independence enables more reliable infrared extrapolation (Furnstahl 2012, More 2013) and suggests future directions including iterative natural orbital construction, use of Laguerre function starting bases, and application to excited states.
