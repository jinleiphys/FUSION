# EOSDB: The Database for Nuclear EoS

> Nuclear equation of state (EoS) plays an important role in understanding the formation of compact objects such as neutron stars and black holes. The true nature of the EoS has been a matter of debate at any density range not only in the nuclear physics but also in the astronomy and astrophysics. We have constructed a database of EoSs by compiling data from the literature. Our database contains the basic properties of the nuclear EoS of symmetric nuclear matter and of pure neutron matter. It also includes the detailed information about the theoretical models, for example the adopted methods and assumptions in individual models. The novelty of the database is to consider new experimental probes such as the symmetry energy, its slope relative to the baryon density, and the incompressibility, which enables the users to check their model dependences. We demonstrate the performance of the EOSDB through the examinations of the model dependence among different nuclear EoSs. It is reveled that some theoretical EoSs, which is commonly used in astrophysics, do not satisfactorily agree with the experimental constraints.

# EOSDB: The Database for Nuclear EoS

## Key claim
The EOSDB compiles nuclear equation of state data from the literature to enable systematic comparison of theoretical models against experimental constraints, revealing that some commonly used astrophysical EoSs do not satisfactorily agree with experimental constraints on saturation properties, symmetry energy, and incompressibility. The database is limited to zero-temperature models for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, with finite-temperature data used only when zero-temperature values cannot be derived.

## Method
The authors constructed a MySQL-based database (operated with CGI, Perl, and JavaScript) that stores bibliographic information, theoretical frameworks, constituents, and thermodynamic quantities for each EoS record. Data were extracted from open EoS tables and from published figures using the GSYS digitization software, with selection priority given to publicly available data, standard EoSs (e.g., Lattimer-Swesty, Shen, Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall), and papers containing experimental or observational constraints. Each record includes saturation density, binding energy, symmetry energy, symmetry energy slope, and incompressibility for symmetric nuclear matter (charge ratio Y_C = 0.5) and pure neutron matter (Y_C = 0.0).

## Key numbers
- 36 data sets compiled as of August 2014, including both theoretical models and experimental/observational constraints
- Symmetry energy E_sym = 31 ± 3 MeV and slope L = 54 ± 13 MeV from Chen (2010), used as reference constraints
- Incompressibility K recommended range 230–270 MeV from 208Pb and 144Sm data (Colo and Giai)
- Saturation density ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ with binding energy E_0 ≈ -16 MeV per nucleon
- Example compiled values: Shen (1998) gives ρ_0 = 0.145 fm⁻³, E_0 = -16.3 MeV, E_sym = 36.9 MeV, L = 110.8 MeV, K = 281 MeV
- Example compiled values: Lattimer-Swesty LS220 gives ρ_0 = 0.155 fm⁻³, E_0 = -16 MeV, E_sym = 29.3 MeV, L = 74 MeV, K = 220 MeV
- Massive neutron star discoveries: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun (Demorest 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun (Antoniadis 2013)

## Context
This work extends the CompOSE database (Typel 2013) for supernova EoSs by focusing on zero-temperature properties and adding symmetry energy, slope, and incompressibility as explicit searchable quantities. The EOSDB is built on the same infrastructure as the SAGA database for metal-poor stars (Suda 2008, 2011). It enables systematic comparison of theoretical EoSs against experimental constraints from nuclear compression modes (ISGMR, ISGDR), heavy-ion collisions, and neutron star observations, addressing the need identified after massive neutron star discoveries that many EoSs with exotic constituents (hyperons, quarks) cannot support 2 M_sun neutron stars (Lattimer and Prakash 2010).
