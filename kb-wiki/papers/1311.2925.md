# Coupled-cluster calculations of nucleonic matter

> Background: The equation of state (EoS) of nucleonic matter is central for the understanding of bulk nuclear properties, the physics of neutron star crusts, and the energy release in supernova explosions. Purpose: This work presents coupled-cluster calculations of infinite nucleonic matter using modern interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT). It assesses the role of correlations beyond particle-particle and hole-hole ladders, and the role of three-nucleon-forces (3NFs) in nuclear matter calculations with chiral interactions. Methods: This work employs the optimized nucleon-nucleon NN potential NNLOopt at next-to-next-to leading-order, and presents coupled-cluster computations of the EoS for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter. The coupled-cluster method employs up to selected triples clusters and the single-particle space consists of a momentum-space lattice. We compare our results with benchmark calculations and control finite-size effects and shell oscillations via twist-averaged boundary conditions. Results: We provide several benchmarks to validate the formalism and show that our results exhibit a good convergence toward the thermodynamic limit. While neutron matter with interactions from chiral EFT is perturbative, symmetric nuclear matter requires nonperturbative approaches. Correlations beyond the standard particle-particle ladder approximation yield non-negligible contributions. The saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is sensitive to the employed 3NFs and the employed regularization scheme. 3NFs with nonlocal cutoffs exhibit a considerably improved convergence than their local cousins. We are unable to find values for the parameters of the short-range part of the local 3NF that simultaneously yield acceptable values for the saturation point in symmetric nuclear matter and the binding energies of light nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that coupled-cluster calculations of nucleonic matter using chiral EFT interactions can achieve good convergence toward the thermodynamic limit, but the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter is sensitive to the employed three-nucleon forces (3NFs) and regularization scheme; the authors were unable to find parameters for the local 3NF that simultaneously yield acceptable saturation properties and binding energies of light nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations with doubles (CCD) and selected triples clusters for infinite symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, using the optimized nucleon-nucleon potential NNLOopt at next-to-next-to-leading order from chiral EFT. They employed a discrete momentum-space lattice with twist-averaged boundary conditions to control finite-size effects and shell oscillations, and compared results with the CCD ladder approximation (CCDladd) that includes only particle-particle and hole-hole ladders. Three-nucleon forces were included in the normal-ordered two-body approximation, and the authors benchmarked against auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) results using the Minnesota potential.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric nuclear matter at Fermi momenta above 1.6 fm^-1, the energy per nucleon with periodic boundary conditions differs by approximately 0.5 MeV from twist-averaged boundary conditions; below 1.6 fm^-1 the difference is less than 200 keV per nucleon.
- For neutron matter with NNLOopt, second-order many-body perturbation theory, CCD, and CCDladd give results differing by less than 1 MeV per neutron at nuclear saturation density.
- Finite-size effects in the Hartree-Fock energy for neutron matter vanish as a power law A^-1.56 in neutron number A; for symmetric nuclear matter with twist-averaged boundary conditions the exponent is A^-1.59.
- The optimized 3NF low-energy constants are c_E = -0.389 and c_D = -0.39, yielding a ^4He binding energy of -28.47 MeV.
- For symmetric nuclear matter at k_F = 1.6 fm^-1, the relative error in the Hartree-Fock potential energy with periodic boundary conditions is about 4% for 132 nucleons, reduced to about 1% with twist-averaged boundary conditions.

## Context
This work extends coupled-cluster calculations of nuclear matter from the late 1970s and early 1980s (Kummel 1978, Day 1981) and the ladder approximation formulation by Baardsen (2013), using modern chiral EFT interactions. It enables systematic inclusion of correlations beyond the particle-particle ladder approximation in a nonperturbative framework, and provides benchmarks for comparison with Monte Carlo methods (Carlson 2003, Gandolfi 2009, Gezerlis 2010), self-consistent Green's function approaches (Dickhoff 2004, Soma 2008), and nuclear density functional theory (Lunney 2003, Erler 2013). The results highlight the need for improved treatment of three-nucleon forces to simultaneously describe nuclear matter saturation and light nuclei.
