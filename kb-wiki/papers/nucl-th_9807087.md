# Elastic scattering analyses and dispersion relation constraints

> We present the results of energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses of pi-N elastic scattering data with laboratory kinetic energies below 2.1 GeV. We have considered the effect of adding dispersion-relation constraints required for a more reliable extraction of the pi-N sigma term. The results of these new fits are compared with those generated previously, using a reduced set of constraints, and with a fit which has only utilized forward constraints associated with the S-wave scattering lengths. We compare the results in terms of their resonance spectra and preferred values for the pi-N-N coupling and pi-N sigma term.

## Key claim

The authors present a new partial-wave analysis (SP98) of pion-nucleon elastic scattering data up to 2.1 GeV that incorporates additional dispersion-relation constraints on the $A^+$ and $E^{\pm}$ amplitudes beyond those used in their previous SM95 analysis, enabling a more rigorous extraction of the pion-nucleon sigma term, though the resulting sigma-term value depends on the extrapolation method and the assumed curvature correction.

## Method

Energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses were performed on a database of pion-proton elastic scattering and charge-exchange data with laboratory kinetic energies below 2.1 GeV, using a coupled-channel Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix parameterization. The fits were iteratively constrained by fixed-t dispersion relations for the $B_{\pm}$, $C^{\pm}$, $E^{\pm}$, and $A^{+}$ invariant amplitudes, with a $\chi^2$ penalty added to enforce compatibility. The sigma term was extracted by extrapolating the $A^+$ dispersion relation subtraction constants to the Cheng-Dashen point and by determining subthreshold coefficients $d^+_{00}$ and $d^+_{01}$ from forward dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

*   The SP98 solution used 96 parameters to fit 10475 $\pi^+p$ data points ($\chi^2$ = 22700), 9531 $\pi^-p$ data points ($\chi^2$ = 19515), and 1661 charge-exchange data points ($\chi^2$ = 4313).
*   The pion-nucleon coupling constant from SP98 was $g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 13.72$, consistent with the SM95 value of $13.75 \pm 0.15$.
*   The subthreshold coefficients were $d^+_{00} = -1.30 m_{\pi}^{-1}$ and $d^+_{01} = 1.27 m_{\pi}^{-1}$.
*   The sigma-term value from the subthreshold expansion was $\Sigma_d = 77$ MeV; including the curvature correction $\Delta_D = 11.9 \pm 0.6$ MeV gave $\Sigma = 92 \pm 3$ MeV from the $A^+$ extrapolation method.
*   The Delta resonance P$_{33}$(1232) pole position was found at $1211 - i50$ MeV with a residue modulus of 38 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the group's previous SM95 analysis (Arndt et al., 1995) by adding dispersion-relation constraints on the $A^+$ and $E^{\pm}$ amplitudes, which are directly connected to the pion-nucleon sigma term. The results enable a more reliable extraction of the sigma term, which parameterizes explicit chiral symmetry breaking due to light quark masses, and provide updated resonance pole positions for comparison with the Particle Data Group (1998) averages. The analysis also identifies a weak P$_{13}$ resonance pole that shifted significantly from SM95, and incorporates eta-production data for the S$_{11}$ N(1535) resonance via a separate fit (Arndt et al., 1998).
