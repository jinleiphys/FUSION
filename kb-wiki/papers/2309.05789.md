# Entropy current and entropy production in relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> We use a first-principle quantum-statistical method to derive the expression of the entropy production rate in relativistic spin hydrodynamics. We show that the entropy current is not uniquely defined and can be changed by means of entropy-gauge transformations, much the same way as the stress-energy tensor and the spin tensor can be changed with pseudo-gauge transformations. We show that the local thermodynamic relations, which are admittedly educated guesses in relativistic spin hydrodynamics inspired by those at global thermodynamic equilibrium, do not hold in general and they are also non-invariant under entropy-gauge transformations. Notwithstanding, we show that the entropy production rate is independent of those transformations and we provide a universally applicable expression, extending that known in literature, from which one can infer the dissipative parts of the energy momentum and spin tensors.

## Key claim

The entropy current in relativistic spin hydrodynamics is not uniquely defined and can be changed by entropy-gauge transformations, but the entropy production rate is invariant under those transformations and provides a universally applicable expression from which dissipative parts of the energy-momentum and spin tensors can be inferred.

## Method

The authors used a first-principle quantum-statistical method based on the local equilibrium density operator, maximizing entropy over a space-like hypersurface by constraining the mean values of energy, momentum, charge, and spin densities. They derived the thermodynamic potential current from the extensivity of the logarithm of the partition function, then obtained the entropy current and its divergence by applying continuity equations for the stress-energy tensor, number current, and spin tensor. The entropy production rate was derived by studying the change of the partition function under infinitesimal changes of the integration hypersurface using Lie derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The entropy production rate is given by the exact formula: ∇_μ s^μ = (T_S^{μν} - T_{S(LE)}^{μν}) ξ_{μν} - (j^μ - j^μ_{LE}) ∇_μ ζ + (T_A^{μν} - T_{A(LE)}^{μν}) (Ω_{μν} - ϖ_{μν}) - (1/2)(s^{μλν} - s^{μλν}_{LE}) ∇_μ Ω_{λν}, where ξ_{μν} is the thermal shear tensor and ϖ_{μν} is the thermal vorticity tensor
- The thermodynamic potential current is defined as φ^μ = ∫_0^T (dT'/T'^2) (T^{μν}[T'] u_ν - μ j^μ[T'] - (1/2) ω_{λν} s^{μλν}[T'])
- The pressure defined as p ≡ T φ·u satisfies ∂p/∂T|_{μ,ω} = s, but ∂p/∂μ|_{T,ω} ≠ n and ∂p/∂ω_{λν}|_{T,μ} ≠ S^{λν} in general

## Context

This work extends the quantum-statistical approach to relativistic hydrodynamics developed by ChGvanWeert and Zubarev by including the spin tensor, and builds on the extensivity result of Becattini (2019). It shows that the local thermodynamic relations previously assumed in spin hydrodynamics by Hattori (2019), Fukushima (2020), Daher (2022), She (2021), Hongo (2021), and Gallegos (2021, 2022) are not generally valid and are non-invariant under entropy-gauge transformations. The derived entropy production rate enables the systematic derivation of constitutive equations for the anti-symmetric part of the stress-energy tensor and the spin tensor in dissipative spin hydrodynamics.
