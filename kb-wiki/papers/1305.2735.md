# On the Possibility of Event Shape Selection in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the possibility of selecting heavy ion collision events with certain features in the initial state ("event engineering"). Anisotropic flow measurements in heavy ion reactions have confirmed the almost ideal fluid dynamical behaviour of the hot and dense quark gluon plasma state. As a consequence, it is intriguing to pursue the idea of selecting collisions with a certain special initial geometry, e.g., a large ellipsoidal or triangular deformation, by classifying events by the value of their final observed flow coefficients. This procedure could be especially interesting for azimuthally dependent jet energy loss studies. We investigate the correlation between initial state features and final state momentum space anisotropies within an event-by-event hybrid approach. We find that the finite particle number and hadronic rescattering of the final state leads to large event-by-event fluctuations in the observables that could be used to characterize the features of the initial state. This makes event engineering by final state selection difficult.

# On the Possibility of Event Shape Selection in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
While the abstract suggests that event engineering by final state selection is difficult, the paper demonstrates that finite particle number and hadronic rescattering cause such large event-by-event fluctuations in flow observables that selecting events by final state $v_2$ or $v_3$ values cannot constrain the initial state geometry to a narrow enough range for precision studies. The correlation between initial eccentricity and final flow is approximately linear on average, but the scatter is too broad for single-event selection.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD-3.1p1 hybrid approach to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV for impact parameters $b=0-12$ fm. The initial state was generated by UrQMD transport with PYTHIA for hard interactions, converted to densities at $t_{\rm start}=0.5$ fm, evolved with ideal hydrodynamics using the SHASTA algorithm with a chiral Polyakov-loop equation of state, and switched to hadronic transport via an iso-$\tau$ hypersurface at energy density $7\epsilon_0$. Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ were calculated using the event plane method with transverse momentum weighting, and initial eccentricities $\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$ were computed from the initial energy density distributions.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV simulated for impact parameters $b=0-12$ fm
- Initial start time for hydrodynamics: $t_{\rm start}=0.5$ fm
- Gaussian smearing width for initial densities: $\sigma=1$ fm
- Conversion to hadrons at energy density $7\epsilon_0$ where $\epsilon_0$ is nuclear ground state energy density
- Rapidity cut for initial state: $|y|<2$
- Transverse momentum cuts tested for high-$p_t$ particles: $p_t > 5, 10, 20$ GeV/c
- Smooth initial conditions constructed by averaging over 100 initial states for central ($b<3.3$ fm) and mid-central ($b=6-8$ fm) collisions

## Context
This work extends the study of initial state fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, following the demonstration of linear correlations between $v_2$ and $\epsilon_2$ and between $v_3$ and $\epsilon_3$ by Teaney (2010) and Gardim (2011). It directly tests the "event shape engineering" concept proposed by Schukraft (2012) for selecting events by final flow coefficients. The paper shows that the hybrid approach's finite particle sampling and hadronic rescattering produce broader $v_n$ distributions than pure hydrodynamics would suggest, limiting the applicability of event engineering for precision studies of geometric effects on jets and HBT radii. The authors call for improved theoretical approaches that can describe finite size effects on momentum correlations throughout the fireball evolution.
