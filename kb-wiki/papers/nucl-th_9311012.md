# Constraints on the $πNN$ Coupling Constant from the $NN$ System

> The sensitivity of the deuteron and of $pp$ scattering to the $πNN$ and $ρNN$ coupling constants is investigated systematically. We find that the deuteron can be described about equally well with either {\it large $π$ and $ρ$} or {\it small $π$ and $ρ$} coupling constants. However, $pp$ scattering clearly {\it requires} the strong $ρ$, but favors the weak $π$ (particularly, in $^3P_0$ at low energies). This apparent contradiction between bound-state and scattering can be resolved by either assuming charge-dependent $πNN$ coupling constants or by adding a heavy pion to the NN model. In both cases, the neutral-pion coupling constant is small ($g^2_{π^0}/4π= 13.5$).

# Constraints on the πNN Coupling Constant from the NN System

## Key claim
The deuteron can be described equally well with either large or small π and ρ coupling constants, but pp scattering requires a strong ρ while favoring a weak π coupling constant. This contradiction can be resolved by assuming charge-dependent πNN coupling constants or by adding a heavy pion, yielding a neutral-pion coupling constant of g²_π⁰/4π = 13.5.

## Method
The authors constructed six meson-exchange models (Models I through VI) for the NN interaction based on the Bonn potential, systematically varying the πNN and ρNN coupling constants. They computed deuteron properties (quadrupole moment Q_d, asymptotic D/S ratio A_D/A_S, D-state probability P_D) and pp scattering phase shifts (particularly the ³P_0 partial wave at low energies) for each model, comparing predictions against experimental data and the Nijmegen phase shift analysis.

## Key numbers
- Model I (large π and ρ: g²_π/4π = 14.4, κ_ρ = 6.1) predicts deuteron Q_d = 0.278 fm² and A_D/A_S = 0.0264; Model II (small π: g²_π/4π = 13.5, κ_ρ = 6.1) gives Q_d = 0.266 fm², substantially below the empirical value of 0.276(3) fm²
- Model III (small π and small ρ: g²_π/4π = 13.5, κ_ρ = 3.7) recovers the deuteron with Q_d = 0.274 fm² and A_D/A_S = 0.0257, but fails for pp scattering: ³P_0 phase shift at 50 MeV is 14.40 degrees versus the experimental 11.47(9) degrees
- Model II (small π, strong ρ) gives excellent ³P_0 pp phase shifts: 3.731 degrees at 10 MeV (experiment 3.729(17)), 8.612 degrees at 25 MeV (experiment 8.575(53)), and 11.57 degrees at 50 MeV (experiment 11.47(9))
- Model V (charge-dependent coupling: g²_π⁰/4π = 13.5, g²_π±/4π = 14.4) yields deuteron Q_d = 0.275 fm² and A_D/A_S = 0.0261, with ³P_0 phase shifts identical to Model II
- Model VI (heavy pion π'(1200) with g²_π'/4π = 35.0) gives deuteron Q_d = 0.273 fm² and ³P_0 phase shifts of 3.727 degrees at 10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the debate on the πNN coupling constant following the Nijmegen group's partial-wave analyses (Bergervoet 1987, Stoks 1993) which found a small neutral-pion coupling constant (g²_π⁰/4π = 13.47 ± 0.11), contradicting earlier determinations by Koch and Pietarinen (1980) and Kroll (1981) who obtained values around 14.3-14.5. The paper resolves the apparent conflict between deuteron properties and pp scattering by proposing either charge-dependent coupling constants or the inclusion of a heavy pion exchange, enabling future NN models to simultaneously describe bound-state and scattering data with a small neutral-pion coupling constant.
