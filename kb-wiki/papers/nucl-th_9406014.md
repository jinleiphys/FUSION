# Chiral and Gluon Condensates at Finite Temperature

> We investigate the thermal behaviour of gluon and chiral condensates within an effective Lagrangian of pseudoscalar mesons coupled to a scalar glueball. This Lagrangian mimics the scale and chiral symmetries of QCD. (Submitted to Z. Phys. C)

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the vanishing of the gluon condensate at high temperature drives the chiral condensate to zero, or whether the chiral condensate's disappearance is dominated by intrinsic chiral dynamics. The authors find that the conjectured strong correlation between the two condensates proposed by Campbell et al. (1990) is only partially supported by their detailed calculation, and that the glueball dynamics itself dominates the position of the minimum of the effective potential, with Goldstone bosons playing only a minor role.

## Method

The authors start from a nonlinear sigma model extended by a scalar glueball field to mimic the scale and chiral symmetries of QCD. They calculate the Gaussian effective potential (GEP) for the dilaton field at finite temperature using a variational ansatz with an arbitrary mass parameter, expanding the interaction Lagrangian to first order and including only vacuum bubbles containing at most one vertex. The minimum of the GEP determines the temperature-dependent expectation value of the dilaton field, which is then related to the gluon condensate via the trace anomaly identification.

## Key numbers

- For bag constant B^(1/4) = 140 MeV, a strong shift of the minimum of the effective potential begins at temperatures around T = 250 MeV, and above T = 300 MeV the minimum at nonzero chi is lost
- For bag constant B^(1/4) = 240 MeV, no visible shift occurs up to T = 350 MeV, and the minimum disappears only above T = 450 MeV
- The glueball mass is chosen as m_chi = 1.6 GeV, motivated by lattice calculations and experimental candidates in the 1.5-1.8 GeV region
- For broken SU(3) flavor, the bag constant cannot be reduced below B^(1/4) = 220 MeV due to the negative curvature from the g_chi*chi^3 term
- The pion decay constant is set to f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Campbell et al. (1990), who suggested regarding the dilaton expectation value as an order parameter for deconfinement and proposed a strong correlation between gluon and chiral condensates. The authors note that Campbell et al. missed the impossibility of perturbatively comparing the effective potential at the origin and at the local minimum, making it impossible to determine the order of the phase transition. The paper enables future work on the thermal behavior of condensates in effective QCD models, though the authors note that a direct comparison of the effective potential at chi=0 and at the local minimum is not possible in any perturbative approach.
