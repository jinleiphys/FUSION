# Variational multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing method applied to pairing correlations in nuclei

> Applying a variational multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing method whose purpose is to include correlations beyond the mean field in a unified way without particle number and Pauli principle violations, we investigate pairing-like correlations in the ground states of $ ^{116}$Sn,$ ^{106}$Sn and $ ^{100}$Sn. The same effective nucleon-nucleon interaction namely, the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force is used to derive both the mean field and correlation components of nuclear wave functions. Calculations are performed using an axially symetric representation. The structure of correlated wave functions, their convergence with respect to the number of particle-hole excitations and the influence of correlations on single-particle level spectra and occupation probabilities are analyzed and compared with results obtained with the same two-body effective interaction from BCS, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and particle number projected after variation BCS approaches. Calculations of nuclear radii and the first theoretical excited $0^+$ states are compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The variational multiparticle-multihole (mp-mh) configuration mixing method can describe pairing correlations in nuclei without violating particle number conservation or the Pauli principle, but the present implementation neglects the dependence of the mean-field Hamiltonian on the two-body correlation matrix \(\sigma\), meaning correlations influence single-particle states only through the one-body density matrix \(\rho\).

## Method

The authors construct a trial wave function as a superposition of Slater determinants, each being a multiple particle-hole (p-h) excitation of a Hartree-Fock-type reference state, restricted to BCS-type pair excitations (two protons or two neutrons in time-reversed states). Both the configuration mixing coefficients and the single-particle states are determined variationally using the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force, with the density-dependent term built from the correlated wave function. Calculations are performed in an axially symmetric representation, and the secular equation is solved iteratively until the one-body density matrix converges.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energies are computed for \(^{116}\)Sn, \(^{106}\)Sn, and \(^{100}\)Sn using the Gogny D1S force, with the correlation energy defined as the difference between the correlated total energy and the HF energy.
- The method uses p-h excitations involving no more than three conjugate pairs of like nucleons to accurately describe pairing correlations in superfluid nuclei, as shown in prior work (Richard 2001).
- The Fourier transform of the central part of the Gogny force shows that occupied single-particle states can couple to unoccupied states up to 80 MeV excitation energy.
- Nuclear radii and the first theoretical excited \(0^+\) states are compared with experimental data for the three tin isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the Higher Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (HTDA) approach of Ref. [ref4] by using the same Gogny effective interaction for both the mean field and the residual correlations, rather than a simplified contact force. It provides an alternative to particle-number projection methods such as variation after projection (VAP) and the Lipkin-Nogami technique, and it enables the description of both strong and weak pairing regimes without the particle-number violation inherent in BCS or HFB approaches. The method is similar to the MultiConfiguration Hartree-Fock (MCHF) method in atomic physics and the MultiConfiguration Self-Consistent Field (MCSCF) method in molecular physics.
