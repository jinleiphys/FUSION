# Relativistic bulk viscous fluids of Burgers type and their presence in neutron stars

> It is well known that a mixture of two chemical components undergoing one chemical reaction is a bulk viscous fluid, where the bulk stress evolves according to the Israel-Stewart theory. Here, we show that a mixture of three independent chemical components undergoing two distinct chemical reactions can also be viewed as a bulk viscous fluid, whose bulk stress now is governed by a second-order differential equation which reproduces the Burgers model for viscoelasticity. This is a rigorous and physically motivated example of a fluid model where the viscous stress does not undergo simple Maxwell-Cattaneo relaxation, and can actually overshoot the Navier-Stokes stress. We show that, if one accounts for muons, neutron star matter is indeed a bulk viscous fluid of Burgers type.

## Key claim

A mixture of three independent chemical components undergoing two distinct chemical reactions behaves as a bulk viscous fluid whose bulk stress follows a second-order differential equation reproducing the Burgers model for viscoelasticity, rather than the simpler Israel-Stewart (Maxwell) relaxation; neutron star matter that includes muons is shown to be a concrete physical example of such a Burgers-type bulk viscous fluid.

## Method

The author establishes a mathematical correspondence between chemistry and bulk viscosity by treating a relativistic fluid mixture with a single conserved baryon current and multiple non-conserved chemical fractions, expanding the equations of motion to first order in the reaction affinities near local equilibrium. For the case of two independent non-equilibrium fractions, the linearized dynamics of the bulk viscous stress is derived and shown to satisfy a second-order differential equation identical to the Burgers model from non-relativistic rheology. The formalism is then applied to neutron-proton-electron-muon (npeμ) matter by mapping the Burgers transport coefficients to the nuclear-physics quantities defined in prior work by Alford, Harutyunyan, and Sedrakian (2021, 2022).

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscosity coefficient ζ for the two-fraction mixture is given by ζ = Ξ^{ab} (∂Y_a/∂v)(∂Y_b/∂v), where Ξ^{ab} is the inverse of the reaction rate matrix and Y_a are the non-conserved fractions.
- The Burgers equation parameters are expressed in terms of relaxation times as λ₂ = τ₁τ₂, λ₁ = τ₁+τ₂, ζ = ζ₁+ζ₂, and χ = ζ₁τ₂+ζ₂τ₁, where τ₁ and τ₂ are the two relaxation times.
- For npeμ matter, the effective viscosity coefficient ζ_eff(ω) is given by equation (40) of Alford, Harutyunyan, and Sedrakian (2022), which the author reproduces as ζ_eff(ω) = {λ_e λ_μ [λ_e((A_n+A_p)C₁-A₁C₂)²+λ_μ((A_n+A_p)C₂-A₂C₁)²] + ω²(λ_e C₁²+λ_μ C₂²)} / {[λ_e λ_μ(A₁A₂-(A_n+A_p)²)-ω²]² + ω²(λ_e A₁+λ_μ A₂)²}.
- The bulk viscosity coefficient ζ for npeμ matter is confirmed to match equation (43) of Alford, Harutyunyan, and Sedrakian (2022).

## Context

This work extends the known result that a two-component mixture with one chemical reaction behaves as an Israel-Stewart (Maxwell) bulk viscous fluid, as established by Gavassino (2022) and others, to the case of three components with two reactions, which yields Burgers-type viscoelastic dynamics. The author shows that the Israel-Stewart theory cannot describe npeμ matter outside the Navier-Stokes regime, contradicting the assumption that Israel-Stewart theory has universal applicability for bulk viscosity. This enables more accurate modeling of bulk viscosity in proto-neutron stars and neutron-star mergers, where muons are present and the simple Maxwell-Cattaneo relaxation fails to capture the correct frequency-dependent effective viscosity.
