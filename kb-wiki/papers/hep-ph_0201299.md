# Radiative decays of ϕ-meson and nature of light scalar resonances

> Based on gauge invariance, we show that the new threshold phenomenon is discovered in the ϕradiative decays ϕ\toγa0\toγ\pi0ηand ϕ\toγf0\toγ\pi0\pi0. This enables to conclude that production of the lightest scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) in these decays is caused by the four-quark transitions, resulting in strong restrictions on the large Nc expansions of the decay amplitudes. The analysis shows that these constraints give new evidences in favor of the four-quark nature of a0(980) and f0(980) mesons.

## Key claim

Based on gauge invariance and experimental data from Novosibirsk and DAΦNE, the production of the scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) in φ radiative decays is caused by four-quark transitions via a K+K- loop mechanism, which constrains the large Nc expansions of the decay amplitudes and provides new evidence for the four-quark nature of these resonances. The author argues that the threshold phenomenon in the φ→γa0→γπ0η and φ→γf0→γπ0π0 decays establishes this mechanism at a physical level of proof.

## Method

The author analyzes the mass spectra of the φ→γπ0η and φ→γπ0π0 decays using a model that includes both the φ→γR (R = a0 or f0) signal and a π0ρ background contribution. The invariant amplitude gR(m) for the φ→γR transition is constrained by gauge invariance to be proportional to the photon energy ω(m), and the experimental spectra require this function to be smooth (nearly constant) up to m ≈ 0.99 GeV. The K+K- loop model is shown to naturally produce this behavior through a threshold phenomenon, where the real part of the loop amplitude (from virtual compact K+K- states) and the imaginary part (from real K+K- intermediate states) combine to suppress the ω(m)2 dependence.

## Key numbers

- BR(φ→γπ0η) = (0.88 ± 0.14 ± 0.09)×10-4 from SND (2000)
- BR(φ→γπ0π0) = (1.221 ± 0.098 ± 0.061)×10-4 from SND (2000)
- BR(φ→γπ0η) = (0.9 ± 0.24 ± 0.1)×10-4 from CMD-2
- BR(φ→γπ0π0) = (0.92 ± 0.08 ± 0.06)×10-4 from CMD-2
- The photon energy at m = 990 MeV is ω(990 MeV) = 29 MeV
- In the K+K- loop model, if the kaon mass were reduced by 25 MeV (to 469 MeV), the function |g(m)|2 is suppressed by the ω(m)2 law in the region 950-1020 MeV; if reduced by 50 MeV (to 444 MeV), suppression occurs in the region 900-1020 MeV
- For an isolated R resonance with mass 980 MeV and width 60 MeV, the ratio SR(920 MeV):SR(950 MeV):SR(970 MeV):SR(980 MeV) = 3:2.7:1.8:1

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Achasov and Ivanchenko, 1989) that φ radiative decays could reveal the nature of a0(980) and f0(980) mesons, and builds on the four-quark bag model states suggested by Jaffe (1977). The analysis uses experimental data from the SND and CMD-2 detectors at Novosibirsk, confirmed by DAΦNE, to argue against the conventional quark-antiquark interpretation of these scalar mesons. The results enable future studies of the OZI rule and large Nc expansions in hadron spectroscopy, and provide constraints on models of light scalar mesons that must reproduce the K+K- loop mechanism.
