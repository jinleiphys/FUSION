# T-odd gluon distribution functions in a spectator model

> We present a model calculation of T-odd transverse-momentum-dependent distributions of gluons in the nucleon. The model is based on the assumption that a nucleon can emit a gluon, and what remains after the emission is treated as a single spectator particle. This spectator particle is considered to be on-shell, but its mass is allowed to take a continuous range of values, described by a spectral function. The final-state interaction that is necessary to generate T-odd functions is modeled as the exchange of a single gluon between the spectator and the outgoing parton.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model calculation of all four T-odd gluon transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) at leading twist, using a spectator-model approach where the spectator mass is allowed a continuous range of values via a spectral function, and the final-state interaction is approximated by a single-gluon exchange; the authors note that the T-odd functions vanish at tree level and only arise from the imaginary part of the interference between the tree-level channel and the rescattering channel.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon as emitting a gluon, with the remainder treated as a single on-shell spin-1/2 spectator particle whose mass is described by a spectral function, using an effective nucleon-gluon-spectator vertex with dipolar form factors. T-odd gluon TMDs are generated by including a single-gluon exchange between the spectator and the outgoing parton, corresponding to the first-order expansion of the gauge link, and the correlators are computed separately for future-pointing ([+,+]) and mixed ([+,-]) Wilson line paths to obtain both Weizsäcker-Williams (f-type) and dipole (d-type) gluon TMDs. The four T-odd gluon TMDs (f1T^⟂g, h1T^⟂g, h1^g, h1L^⟂g) are extracted from the gluon-gluon correlator using specific projectors for different nucleon polarizations.

## Key numbers

- Color factor for f-type (Weizsäcker-Williams) T-odd gluon TMDs: C^[+,+] = -24
- Color factor for d-type (dipole) T-odd gluon TMDs: C^[+,-] = -40/3
- Ratio of d-type to f-type T-odd functions: 5/9
- Quark-target model color factor for f-type: C_q^[+,+] = -2
- Quark-target model color factor for d-type: C_q^[+,-] = -10/9
- Tree-level color factor: C^(0) = 8
- Quark-target tree-level color factor: C_q^(0) = 4/3

## Context

This work extends the spectator-model calculation of T-even gluon TMDs presented by Bacchetta, Celiberto, and Radici (2020) to the T-odd sector, building on the same framework where the spectator mass takes a continuous range of values. The calculation addresses the process dependence of gluon TMDs, which is more complex than for quarks due to the gluon's color-octet structure, and provides model predictions for the gluon Sivers function (f1T^⟂g) and other T-odd distributions that are needed for interpreting transverse single-spin asymmetries at facilities such as the EIC and COMPASS.
