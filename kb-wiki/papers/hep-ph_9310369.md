# Baryons from Quarks in the $1/N$ Expansion

> We present a diagrammatic analysis of baryons in the $1/N$ expansion, where $N$ is the number of QCD colors. We use this method to show that there are an infinite number of degenerate baryon states in the large-$N$ limit. We also show that forward matrix elements of quark bilinear operators satisfy the static quark-model relations in this limit, and enumerate the corrections to these relations to all orders in $1/N$. These results hold for any number of light quark flavors, and the methods used can be extended to arbitrary operators. Our results imply that for two flavors, the quark-model relations for the axial currents and magnetic moments get corrections of order $1/N^2$. For three or more flavors, the results are more complicated, and corrections are generically of order $1/N$. We write an explicit effective lagrangian which can be used to carry out chiral perturbation theory calculations in the $1/N$ expansion. Finally, we compare our results to what is expected from a chiral constituent quark model.

## Key claim

The paper uses a diagrammatic analysis of baryons in the 1/N expansion to show that the static quark-model relations for forward matrix elements of quark bilinear operators hold in the large-N limit, with corrections that can be enumerated to all orders in 1/N. For two light quark flavors, these corrections are of order 1/N^2, while for three or more flavors they are generically of order 1/N.

## Method

The authors construct baryon states as N-quark color-singlet states with definite spin and flavor quantum numbers, then develop a diagrammatic expansion for baryon masses and matrix elements using a time-evolution operator and Wick's theorem. They classify the allowed r-body operators in the spin-flavor Fock space that appear in this expansion, and determine their N-scaling by counting powers of 1/sqrt(N) from gluon vertices and powers of N from internal color loops. The analysis is performed for any number of light quark flavors, and an effective lagrangian is written to combine chiral perturbation theory with the 1/N expansion.

## Key numbers

- Baryon masses scale as M_B ~ N (order N) for all states with spin J ~ 1, with mass differences between states given by M_J - M_J' = (mu/N)[J(J+1) - J'(J'+1)].
- The coefficients c_s in the mass expansion M_B = M_0 + sum_s c_s <J^2>^s satisfy c_s lesssim 1/N^(2s-1).
- The coefficients c_r for r-body operators in the mass expansion satisfy c_r lesssim 1/N^(r-1).
- For two flavors, corrections to quark-model relations for axial currents and magnetic moments are of order 1/N^2.
- For three or more flavors, corrections to quark-model relations are generically of order 1/N.

## Context

This work extends the large-N analysis of baryons initiated by Witten (1979) and the Skyrme model approach of Bardakci (1984) and Manohar (1984), as well as the consistency arguments of Gervais and Sakita (1984) and the recent work of Dashen and Manohar (1993) and Jenkins (1993). The diagrammatic method provides a direct QCD derivation of the quark-model relations and their 1/N corrections, and the effective lagrangian enables simultaneous chiral and 1/N expansion calculations for baryons interacting with soft pseudo Nambu-Goldstone bosons.
