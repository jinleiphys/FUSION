# Electric Dipole Moments of Light Nuclei from Chiral EFT

> Recent calculations of EDMs of light nuclei in the framework of chiral effective field theory are presented. We argue that they can be written in terms of the leading six low-energy constants encoding CP-violating physics. EDMs of the deuteron, triton, and helion are explicitly given in order to corroborate our claim. An eventual non-zero measurement of these EDMs can be used to disentangle the different sources and strengths of CP-violation.

## Key claim

The EDMs of light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helion) can be expressed in terms of the leading six low-energy constants encoding CP-violating physics, and for N=Z nuclei isospin selection rules reduce this dependence to three constants, as seen in the deuteron case.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory (χEFT) to construct the leading CP-violating (T̸) operators at the hadronic scale in terms of six low-energy constants (LECs): d̄₀, d̄₁, ḡ₀, ḡ₁, C̄₁, and C̄₂. They calculated the EDM of each nucleus by evaluating both the T̸ dipole operator between the unperturbed nuclear wave functions and the T-conserving dipole operator between the unperturbed and T̸-admixed wave functions, using realistic phenomenological potentials for the T-conserving wave functions of ²H, ³H, and ³He.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM: dₙ = d̄₀ − d̄₁ (expressed in terms of LECs)
- Proton EDM: dₚ = d̄₀ + d̄₁ (expressed in terms of LECs)
- Deuteron EDM: d_{²H} = 2 d̄₀ + 0.0002(ḡ₀/F_π) e·fm − 0.07β₁(ḡ₀/F_π) e·fm − 0.19(ḡ₁/F_π) e·fm
- Helion EDM: d_{³He} = 0.83 d̄₀ − 0.93 d̄₁ − 0.15(ḡ₀/F_π) e·fm − 0.28(ḡ₁/F_π) e·fm − 0.01(F_π³C̄₁) e·fm + 0.02(F_π³C̄₂) e·fm
- Triton EDM: d_{³H} = 0.85 d̄₀ + 0.95 d̄₁ + 0.15(ḡ₀/F_π) e·fm − 0.28(ḡ₁/F_π) e·fm + 0.01(F_π³C̄₁) e·fm − 0.02(F_π³C̄₂) e·fm
- Existing upper limit on neutron EDM: |dₙ| < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm (Baker, 2006)
- Existing upper limit on proton EDM: |dₚ| < 7.9 × 10⁻²⁵ e·cm (from ¹⁹⁹Hg EDM, Griffith, 2009)
- QCD vacuum angle constrained to θ < 10⁻⁹

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to CP-violating interactions previously developed for the θ-term (de Vries et al., 2010) and other T̸ operators (Mereghetti et al., 2010) by providing explicit EDM expressions for light nuclei. The results enable experimental programs such as storage-ring measurements of charged light nuclei EDMs to disentangle different sources of CP-violation (θ-term, quark EDM, quark chromo-EDM, and chiral-invariant sources) through the distinct relations among the nuclear EDMs predicted from naive dimensional analysis.
