# Superfluid effects in collision between systems with small particle number

> The interpretation of the new effect of the superfluidity in reactions with small number of particles is discussed in a simple model where the exact solution is accessible. It is find that the fluctuations of observable with the gauge angle reproduce well the exact fluctuations. Then a method of projection is proposed and tested to determine the transfer probabilities between two superfluid systems.

## Key claim

The fluctuations of TDHFB observables with respect to the initial relative gauge angle can be interpreted as statistical fluctuations that reproduce exact quantum fluctuations, but this interpretation must be used with caution in realistic cases and a more rigorous method of gauge angle symmetry restoration should be considered.

## Method

A simple model inspired by the Dietrich model of nuclear Josephson effect was constructed, consisting of two systems each with Omega=4 doubly degenerate levels and N=4 particles, with a pairing interaction G=-0.2 MeV. The exact solution was obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the space of all configurations with N particles on each side, while TDHFB calculations were performed for 24 initial values of the relative gauge angle phi in the interval [0,pi]. The systems were evolved in time with a Gaussian time-dependent coupling V(t) for both weak (V0=-0.003 MeV) and strong (V0=-0.4 MeV) interaction cases.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of the exact solution: 1.6 MeV for Omega=4, N=4, G=-0.2 MeV
- Effective interaction used in HFB to match initial energy: G^eff = (4/3)G
- Time evolution parameters: a=0.3x10^44 s^-2, evolution from t=-15x10^-22 s to t=15x10^-22 s
- Weak interaction strength: V0=-0.003 MeV; strong interaction strength: V0=-0.4 MeV
- For the projection method test: G=-1 MeV, V0=-0.03 MeV, a=0.3x10^44 s^-2, single-particle energies e_i=i MeV for i=1,4

## Context

This work extends the study of Josephson effects in superfluid nuclear reactions previously investigated by Hashimoto (2016) for 20O+20O collisions and by Magierski (2016), and addresses the ambiguity raised by Bulgac (2017) regarding gauge angle symmetry in systems with fixed particle number. The projection method builds on the work of Simenel (2010) and Scamps (2013), and the overlap calculations use the Pfaffian method of Bertsch (2012). The proposed prescription for modifying the TDHFB equation of motion to remove spurious behavior after system separation will be applied to realistic calculations in future work.
