# Energy-density-functional calculations including the proton-neutron mixing

> We present results of calculations based on the Skyrme energy density functional including the arbitrary mixing between protons and neutrons. In this framework, single-particle states are superpositions of proton and neutron components and the energy density functional is fully invariant with respect to three-dimensional rotations in the isospin space. The isospin of the system is controlled by means of the isocranking method, which carries over the standard cranking approach to the isospin space. We show numerical results of the isocranking calculations performed for isobaric analogue states in the A=14 and $A=40-56$ nuclei. We also present such results obtained for high-isospin states in $^{48}$Cr, with constraints on the isospin implemented by using the augmented Lagrange method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Skyrme energy-density-functional (EDF) approach including arbitrary proton-neutron mixing at the mean-field level, controlled by the isocranking method, can quantitatively describe isobaric analogue states (IASs) across multiple mass regions, but only when the Coulomb interaction is treated and the isocranking trajectory is shifted to compensate for its isospin-symmetry-breaking effects.

## Method

The authors generalized the Skyrme EDF to include proton-neutron mixing in the particle-hole channel, implementing it within the HFODD code (v2.56e) using a Cartesian deformed harmonic-oscillator basis. They controlled the isospin of the system via the isocranking method, adding a term \(-\vec{\lambda} \cdot \hat{\vec{t}}\) to the mean-field Hamiltonian, and adjusted the isocranking frequency vector \(\vec{\lambda}\) to scan across isobaric multiplets. For calculations including the Coulomb interaction, they used a shifted semicircle parametrization of \(\vec{\lambda}\) to avoid level crossings, and for high-isospin states in \(^{48}\)Cr they employed the augmented Lagrange method to enforce integer values of \(\langle \hat{T}_x \rangle\).

## Key numbers

- For \(A=48\) isobars without Coulomb interaction, the isocranking frequency magnitude was set to \(|\vec{\lambda}| = 11\) MeV for \(T=4\) states and 6 MeV for \(T=2\) states, inferred from the difference between proton and neutron Fermi energies in the unmixed calculations for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ti, respectively.
- For \(A=48\) isobars with Coulomb interaction, the shifted semicircle parameters for the \(T \simeq 4\) multiplet were \(\lambda_{\text{off}} = -8\) MeV and \(\lambda' = 12\) MeV; for the \(T \simeq 2\) multiplet, \((\lambda_{\text{off}}, \lambda') = (-8.0, 6.4)\) MeV.
- For \(A=40-56\) isobars with \(T \simeq 4\), the shifted semicircle parameters ranged from \((\lambda_{\text{off}}, \lambda') = (-6.8, 13.6)\) MeV for \(A=40\) to \((-8.8, 12.4)\) MeV for \(A=56\).
- For \(^{48}\)Cr, the calculated quadrupole deformations for states with \(\langle \hat{T}_x \rangle = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8\) were \(\beta_2 = 0.27, 0.19, 0, 0.07, 0\), respectively.
- For the \(A=14\) isobars, the calculated energy of the \(T=1, T_z=0\) IAS in \(^{14}\)N was reproduced after shifting the entire theoretical curve by 3.2 MeV to correct for the SkM* EDF overbinding of \(^{14}\)C.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Perlińska et al. (2004) by implementing the generalized Skyrme EDF with proton-neutron mixing in the particle-hole channel, and applies the isocranking method of Satula and Wyss (2001) to control isospin. It enables future calculations including proton-neutron mixing in both the particle-hole and pairing channels, which is required by self-consistency arguments, and opens the door to isospin projection of the pnEDF states and quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations for charge-exchange reactions. The results are particularly relevant for understanding superallowed Fermi beta decay, which tests the Standard Model of particle physics.
