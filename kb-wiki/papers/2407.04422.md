# Global analysis of fragmentation functions to charged hadrons with high-precision data from the LHC

> Fragmentation functions (FFs) are essential non-perturbative QCD inputs for predicting hadron production cross sections in high energy scatterings. In this study, we present a joint determination of FFs for light charged hadrons through a global analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD. Our analysis incorporates a wide range of precision measurements from the LHC, as well as data from electron-positron collisions and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scatterings. By including measurements of jet fragmentation at the LHC in our global analysis, we are able to impose strong constraints on the gluon FFs. A careful selection of hadron kinematics is applied to ensure the validity of factorization and perturbative calculations of QCD. In addition, we introduce several methodological advances in fitting, resulting in a flexible parametrization form and the inclusion of theoretical uncertainties from perturbative calculations. Our best-fit predictions show very good agreement with the global data, with $χ^2/N_{pt}\sim 0.90$. We also generate a large number of Hessian error sets to estimate uncertainties and correlations of the extracted FFs. FFs to charged pions (kaons and protons) are well constrained for momentum fractions down to 0.01 (0.1). Total momentum of partons carried by light charged hadrons are determined precisely. Their values for $u$, $d$ quarks and gluon saturate at about 50\% for a lower cut of the momentum fraction of 0.01. Pulls from individual datasets and impact of various choices of the analysis are also studied in details. Additionally, we present an update of the FMNLO program used for calculating hadron production cross sections. Our FFs, including the error sets (denoted as NPC23), are publicly available in the form of LHAPDF6 grids.

## Key claim

The authors present the first joint determination of fragmentation functions (FFs) for charged pions, kaons, and protons at next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD that simultaneously includes jet fragmentation measurements from the LHC, but the analysis applies stringent kinematic cuts (hadron momentum fraction z > 0.01 and hadron energy or transverse momentum > 4 GeV) to ensure validity of leading-twist factorization, and the FFs for kaons and protons are only well constrained for momentum fractions down to 0.1 rather than 0.01.

## Method

The authors performed a global NLO QCD analysis of fragmentation functions for light charged hadrons (π±, K±, p/p̄) by fitting to experimental data from electron-positron annihilation (SIA), semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and proton-proton collisions including jet fragmentation measurements. The analysis used the FMNLO program for fast convolution of hard coefficient functions with FFs in z-space, employed a flexible parametrization form, included theoretical uncertainties from QCD scale variations in the covariance matrix, and generated Hessian error sets for uncertainty estimation.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit χ²/Npt ≈ 0.90 for the global dataset
- FFs to charged pions constrained for momentum fractions down to z = 0.01; FFs to kaons and protons constrained for z down to 0.1
- Total momentum of partons carried by light charged hadrons for u, d quarks and gluon saturates at about 50% for a lower cut of momentum fraction of 0.01
- Uncertainty of 3%, 4%, and 8% for FFs from gluon to pion at z = 0.05, 0.1, and 0.3 respectively
- Uncertainty of 4%, 4%, and 7% for FFs from u quark to pion, kaon, and proton at z = 0.3 respectively
- FFs from s quark to kaons are larger than from u quark by a factor of 2 to 4

## Context

This work extends previous global FF determinations by groups such as DSS (deFlorian et al., 2007-2021), HKNS (Hirai et al., 2007), AKK (Albino et al., 2008), NNFF (Bertone et al., 2018), MAPFF (Khalek et al., 2021), and JAM (Moffat et al., 2021) by including, for the first time, jet fragmentation measurements from the LHC to constrain gluon FFs. The analysis enables improved predictions for hadron production in high-energy scattering processes, including reference cross sections for heavy-ion collisions and jet charge measurements, and the resulting NPC23 FF sets are publicly available as LHAPDF6 grids.
