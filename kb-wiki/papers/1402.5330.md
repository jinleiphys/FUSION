# Fusion cross sections for reactions involving medium & heavy nucleus-nucleus systems

> Existing data on near-barrier fusion excitation functions of medium and heavy nucleus-nucleus systems have been analyzed using a simple diffused barrier formula derived assuming the Gaussian shape of the barrier height distributions. Fusion cross section is obtained by folding the Gaussian barrier distribution with the classical expression for the fusion cross section for a fixed barrier. The energy dependence of the fusion cross section, thus obtained, provides good description to the existing data on near-barrier fusion and capture excitation functions for medium and heavy nucleus-nucleus systems. The fusion or capture cross section predictions are especially important for planning experiments for synthesizing new super-heavy elements.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a simple diffused-barrier formula, derived by folding a Gaussian barrier distribution with the classical fusion cross section expression, provides a good description of near-barrier fusion excitation functions for medium and heavy nucleus-nucleus systems, but only for above-barrier energies and with the caveat that for very heavy systems the formula predicts capture cross sections rather than fusion cross sections.

## Method
The authors assumed a Gaussian shape for the distribution of fusion barrier heights, characterized by a mean barrier height \(B_0\) and a width \(\sigma_B\). They folded this distribution with the classical expression for fusion cross section for a fixed barrier, which depends on an effective radius \(R_B\). The three free parameters (\(B_0\), \(\sigma_B\), \(R_B\)) were determined individually for each reaction by fitting the resulting formula to experimental fusion excitation functions using a least-square method.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{16}\)O+\(^{144}\)Sm (Coulomb parameter \(z \approx 63.91\)): mean barrier height \(B_0 = 60.65\) MeV, width \(\sigma_B = 1.76\) MeV, effective radius \(R_B = 10.46\) fm.
- For \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{124}\)Sn (\(z \approx 118.95\)): \(B_0 = 113.36\) MeV, \(\sigma_B = 2.70\) MeV, \(R_B = 9.57\) fm.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{124}\)Sn (\(z = 116.00\)): \(B_0 = 111.93 \pm 0.44\) MeV, \(\sigma_B = 1.28 \pm 0.83\) MeV, \(R_B = 8.24 \pm 0.09\) fm (with large error bars due to few data points).
- For \(^{36}\)S+\(^{110}\)Pd (\(z = 90.94\)): \(B_0 = 85.51\) MeV, \(\sigma_B = 1.92\) MeV, \(R_B = 8.20\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of heavy-ion sub-barrier fusion recently studied by R. (2013) to a simple phenomenological model that neglects structure effects in barrier distributions. The diffused-barrier formula, previously used by W. (2004) and C. (2011), is applied to 12 medium and heavy systems. The results enable predictions of capture cross sections for very heavy systems, which can be used as input to the sticking-diffusion-survival model of S. (2003) for calculating superheavy element production cross sections.
