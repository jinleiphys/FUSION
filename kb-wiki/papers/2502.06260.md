# Axialvector diqaurk Mass and quark-diquark potential in Sigma_c

> The axialvector diquark is studied by using 2+1 flavor Lattice QCD. Being a two-quark object, diquark has a non-neutral color charge. Hence the two-point correlators of diquark fields do not have a particle pole due to the color confinement of QCD, and it is not straightforward to study the diquark mass by lattice QCD by using an exponential fit of a temporal two-point correlator. In order to avoid this difficulty, our strategy is to regard the diquark mass as a mass parameter of an effective quark-diquark model which is constructed by using an extended HAL QCD method based on equal-time quark-diquark Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. We attempt to calculate the axial-vector diquark mass and the quark-diquark potentials between a charm quark and an axial-vector diquark in the $Σ_c$ baryon. Lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculation is performed by using the 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations generated on $32^3\times 64$ lattice by PACS-CS Collaboration which corresponds to the pion mass of about 700 MeV. As a result, a quark-diquark central potential of Cornell-type and a short-ranged spin-dependent potential are obtained. However, from a quantitative point of view, the ground state convergence of the NBS wave functions are not sufficient so that we obtain a larger string tension and a smaller axial-vector diquark mass than we have expected phenomenologically.

## Key claim

The axial-vector diquark mass and quark-diquark potentials in the Σc baryon are extracted by treating the diquark mass as a parameter in an effective quark-diquark model constructed via the extended HAL QCD method, but the ground-state convergence of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions is insufficient, leading to a larger string tension and a smaller axial-vector diquark mass than phenomenologically expected.

## Method

Equal-time quark-diquark Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for the Σc baryon were computed from four-point correlators on 2+1 flavor lattice QCD gauge configurations. The Kawanai-Sasaki extension of the HAL QCD potential method was applied, requiring that the spin-dependent quark-diquark potential vanish at long distance to determine the reduced mass, from which the charm quark mass and axial-vector diquark mass were derived. The central and spin-dependent quark-diquark potentials were then obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation for the J=1/2 and J=3/2 channels.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm, a^{-1} = 2176(31) MeV, on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with pion mass m_pi ~ 702 MeV.
- Reference hadron masses: m_N ~ 1583 MeV, m_pi ~ 702 MeV, m_eta_c ~ 3025 MeV, m_J/psi ~ 3144 MeV, m_Lambda_c(1/2+) ~ 2690 MeV, m_Sigma_c(1/2+) ~ 2777 MeV, m_Sigma_c(3/2+) ~ 2859 MeV.
- Reduced mass mu ~ 600 MeV obtained from the long-distance limit of the Kawanai-Sasaki function.
- Charm quark mass m_q ~ 1950 MeV obtained by applying the Kawanai-Sasaki prescription to the c-cbar sector.
- Axial-vector diquark mass m_D ~ 867 MeV, compared to the scalar diquark mass m_SD ~ 1273 MeV from Watanabe (2021) using the same gauge configurations.
- Cornell-type fit parameters for the quark-diquark central potential: A ~ 86 MeV fm, sqrt(sigma) ~ 565 MeV; for the c-cbar central potential: A ~ 103 MeV fm, sqrt(sigma) ~ 459 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of Watanabe (2021), which studied the scalar diquark mass in the Lambda_c baryon using the same extended HAL QCD approach, to the axial-vector (bad) diquark in the Sigma_c baryon. The results contradict the phenomenological expectation that the axial-vector diquark should be heavier than the scalar diquark, likely due to insufficient ground-state convergence of the four-point correlators at long distances. The authors plan to improve results using the time-dependent HAL QCD method and variational source operators, and to study the quark mass dependence toward the physical point.
