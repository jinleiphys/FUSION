# Novel phase transition at the Unruh temperature

> We consider gas of massless fermions at certain temperature T and acceleration a. We find a second order phase transition at temperature T approaching the Unruh temperature TU. The implications for hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions (HIC) and for black-hole physics are discussed. In particular, this novel phase transition may be associated with thermalization in HIC, indicating its analogy with falling into a black hole.

## Key claim

The paper claims a second-order phase transition occurs in a gas of massless fermions when the temperature approaches the Unruh temperature from below, but this transition is only realized through an analytical continuation of Euclidean Rindler space to negative conical angle deficits, and the resulting negative energy states are argued to correspond to the interior of black holes or the initial state of heavy-ion collisions rather than being physically realizable in ordinary Minkowski vacuum.

## Method

The authors study massless Dirac fermions on Euclidean Rindler space with a conical singularity, where the angular deficit is determined by the ratio of acceleration to temperature. They construct the Green's functions from Matsubara modes, imposing finiteness on the horizon, and find that the choice of which Bessel function solution is regular changes at critical temperatures \(T_n = T_U/(2n+1)\). The renormalized stress-energy tensor is then computed from the Green's function for the region \(T_U/3 < T < T_U\) where the first mode change occurs.

## Key numbers

* The Unruh temperature is defined as \(T_U = \hbar |a| / (2\pi k_B c)\).
* For \(T \ge T_U\), the energy density of massless fermions is \(\epsilon_{s=1/2} = \frac{7\pi^2 T^4}{60} + \frac{|a|^2 T^2}{24} - \frac{17|a|^4}{960\pi^2}\).
* For \(T_U/3 < T < T_U\), the energy density becomes \(\epsilon = \frac{127\pi^2 T^4}{60} - \frac{11|a|^2 T^2}{24} - \frac{17|a|^4}{960\pi^2}\).
* The trace of the stress-energy tensor below the Unruh temperature is \(\langle \hat{T}^\beta_\beta \rangle = 2\pi T |a| \left(T^2 - \frac{|a|^2}{4\pi^2}\right)\).
* Further critical points occur at temperatures \(T_n = T_U/(2n+1)\) for \(n = 0, 1, 2, \dots\).

## Context

This work extends the geometric approach to the Unruh effect developed by Dowker (1987) and the thermodynamic formulation by Becattini (2017), which previously only considered the region \(T \ge T_U\). The paper builds on the suggestion by Kharzeev (2005) that thermalization in heavy-ion collisions could be understood as a phase transition analogous to Hawking radiation. The results enable a unified description of black hole interiors and the initial state of quark-gluon plasma, with the phase transition at the Unruh temperature providing a mechanism for hadronization and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, as well as a possible resolution to the problem of singularities inside black holes.
