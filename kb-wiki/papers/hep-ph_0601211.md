# P-Wave Charmed-Strange Mesons

> We examine charmed-strange mesons within the framework of the constituent quark model, focusing on the states with L=1. We are particularly interested in the mixing of two spin-states that are involved in $D_{s1}(2536)$ and the recently discovered $D_{sJ}(2460)$. We assume that these two mesons form a pair of states with J=1. These spin-states are mixed by a type of the spin-orbit interaction that violates the total-spin conservation. Without assuming explicit forms for the interactions as functions of the interquark distance, we relate the matrix elements of all relevant spin-dependent interactions to the mixing angle and the observed masses of the L=1 quartet. We find that the spin-spin interaction, among various types of the spin-dependent interactions, plays a particularly interesting role in determining the spin structure of $D_{s1}(2536)$ and $D_{sJ}(2460)$.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the mixing of the $J=1$ states $D_{s1}(2536)$ and $D_{sJ}(2460)$ is driven by the antisymmetric spin-orbit (ASLS) interaction, which violates total-spin conservation, and that the spin-spin interaction plays a particularly interesting role in determining the spin structure of these two mesons, but the actual mixing angle and the dominant spin-component assignment depend sensitively on the experimentally measured ratio of electromagnetic decay widths, which has large statistical errors.

## Method

The authors used a constituent quark model with a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian that includes spin-independent confinement and color Coulomb interactions plus four spin-dependent interactions (spin-spin, tensor, symmetric spin-orbit SLS, and antisymmetric spin-orbit ASLS). Without assuming explicit radial forms for the interactions, they treated all spin-dependent interactions perturbatively and expressed the five unknown matrix elements ($M_0$, $v_S$, $v_{LS}$, $v_T$, $\Delta$) in terms of the four observed masses of the $L=1$ charmed-strange meson quartet and the mixing angle $\theta$, which was extracted from the experimental ratio of electromagnetic decay widths of $D_{sJ}(2460)$ to $D_s^*\gamma$ and $D_s\gamma$.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle $\theta = -45.4^\circ$ with upper increment $+16.4^\circ$ and lower increment $-7.5^\circ$, determined from the experimental ratio $R_{\rm exp} = 0.40 \pm 0.28$ for $\Gamma(D_{sJ}\rightarrow D_s^*\gamma) / \Gamma(D_{sJ}\rightarrow D_s\gamma)$.
- Matrix element of the spin-spin interaction: $v_S = 21.0$ MeV with range $-13.1$ to $+27.5$ MeV.
- Matrix element of the symmetric spin-orbit interaction: $v_{LS} = 61.4$ MeV with range $+2.5$ to $-5.2$ MeV.
- Matrix element of the tensor interaction: $v_T = 19.7$ MeV with range $-2.1$ to $+4.3$ MeV.
- Matrix element of the antisymmetric spin-orbit interaction: $\Delta = 26.9$ MeV with range $-1.0$ to $-4.1$ MeV.
- Center-of-gravity mass $M_0 = 2513.6$ MeV.
- The quantity $(v_S - v_{LS} + 2v_T)$ changes sign at $R_{\rm exp} = 0.39$, determining which spin-state is dominant in $D_{s1}(2536)$ and $D_{sJ}(2460)$.
- Photon momenta for $D_{sJ}$ decays: $k = 322.7$ MeV for decay to $D_s^*$ and $k = 442.0$ MeV for decay to $D_s$.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model studies of charmed-strange mesons by Godfrey and Kokoski (1991) and Lucha and Schoberl (2003), who predicted masses for the $L=1$ quartet that were systematically larger than the experimental values. The authors show that earlier models underestimated the matrix elements of the SLS and tensor interactions (giving $v_{LS} \approx 27$ MeV and $v_T \approx 8$ MeV compared to their extracted $61.4$ MeV and $19.7$ MeV) and particularly underestimated the ASLS matrix element $\Delta$ (about $3$-$4$ MeV versus $26.9$ MeV), which explains why those models predicted a much smaller mass difference between $D_{s1}$ and $D_{sJ}$ ($76$ MeV experimentally) and a much higher mass for $D_{sJ}^*$. The model-independent approach enables direct extraction of spin-dependent interaction matrix elements from data, providing constraints for future potential models and clarifying the role of the spin-spin interaction in $P$-states of heavy-light systems.
