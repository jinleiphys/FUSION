# Bottomonium suppression: A probe to the pre-equilibrium era of quark matter

> We have studied the thermal suppression of the bottomonium states in relativistic heavy-ion collision at LHC energies as function of centrality, rapidity, transverse momentum etc. to explain the CMS data. Our investigation mainly spans over three problems: a) how the theoretical predictions might still be modified by the remnants of the non-perturbative confining force, b) how does the presence of a not necessarily isotropic QCD medium modify the potential (both the real and imaginary part) acting between a static quark and antiquark pair, and c) finally how the additional time-zone of pre-equilibrium partonic evolution, in addition to the above modifications, affects the bottomonium production at the LHC energies. We resolve them by correcting both the perturbative and nonperturbative terms of the $Q\bar Q$ potential in (an)isotropic QCD medium and then couple to the dynamics of the system undergoing successive pre-equilibrium and equilibrium era. Due to the tiny formation time and the large binding energy of bottomonium (1S) state, we succeed in constraining the isotropization time and the shear viscosity.

# Bottomonium suppression: A probe to the pre-equilibrium era of quark matter

## Key claim
The authors show that including both the non-perturbative confining (string) term and the effects of momentum-space anisotropy in the heavy-quark potential raises the dissociation temperatures of bottomonium states compared to calculations using only the perturbative Coulomb term in an isotropic medium. They further claim that the local equilibrium hydrodynamic regime alone cannot adequately suppress bottomonium states to match CMS data, and that an additional pre-equilibrium phase of plasma evolution must be included, which also allows constraining the isotropization time and shear viscosity.

## Method
The authors derive a complex heavy-quark potential by correcting both the Coulomb and linear (string) terms of the Cornell potential using a dielectric function obtained from the static limit of the gluon propagator in hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory, including leading-order anisotropic corrections for small anisotropy parameter ξ. They solve the Schrödinger equation numerically using a matrix method to obtain the real and imaginary parts of the binding energies for bottomonium states, then couple these in-medium properties to a dynamical model of the expanding medium that includes successive pre-equilibrium (anisotropic) and equilibrium (isotropic) phases in a Bjorken boost-invariant framework.

- Dissociation temperature for Υ(3S) in isotropic medium: 1.12 T_c; increases to 1.21 T_c for ξ = 0.6

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quarkonium suppression in anisotropic media by Dumitru et al. (2007-2009) and Burnier et al. (2009), which considered only leading anisotropic corrections to the perturbative term of the potential. The authors had previously investigated the medium modifications to both perturbative and nonperturbative terms of the Q\bar Q potential in anisotropic media (Thakur et al., 2013) and now apply that framework to compute dissociation temperatures for the full Υ family and to model dynamical suppression at LHC energies. The results enable using bottomonium (1S) states, due to their small formation time and large binding energy, as probes of the pre-equilibrium phase of quark-gluon plasma evolution and as constraints on the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio.
