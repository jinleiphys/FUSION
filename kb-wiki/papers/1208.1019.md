# The resonance absorption probability function for neutron and multiplicative integral

> The analytical approximations for the moderating neutrons flux density like Fermi spectra, widely used in reactor physics, involve the probability function for moderating neutron to avoid the resonant absorption obtained using some restrictive assumptions regarding the acceptable resonances width. By means of multiplicative integral (Volterra integral) theory for a commutative algebra an analytical expression for the probability function is obtained rigorously without any restrictive assumptions.

# The resonance absorption probability function for neutron and multiplicative integral

## Key claim
The paper shows that the probability function for a moderating neutron to avoid resonant absorption can be expressed exactly as a multiplicative (Volterra) integral, yielding an analytical expression that coincides with the narrow resonance approximation but without the restrictive assumption that the resonance width-to-energy ratio is small (ΔEᵢ/Eᵢ ≪ 1).

## Method
The authors first review the standard narrow resonance approximation, where the probability function φ(E) is derived by taking a product over discrete resonances, expanding the logarithm in a series assuming ΔEᵢ/Eᵢ ≪ 1, and converting the sum to an exponential integral. They then apply multiplicative integral theory for a commutative algebra, defining the function f(E) = −Σₐ / [ξ̄ (Σ_S + Σₐ) E] and showing that the infinite product over energy intervals converges to a multiplicative integral, which for a commutative algebra equals the exponential of a Riemann integral.

## Key numbers
- The resonance region for reactor neutron nuclear reactions of fissile nuclides is located in the 0.5–1000 eV range.
- The final expression for the probability function is φ(E) = exp( −∫_E^{E_f} [Σₐ / (ξ̄ (Σ_S + Σₐ) E)] dE ), which is identical in form to the narrow resonance approximation but derived without the assumption ΔEᵢ/Eᵢ ≪ 1.

## Context
This work extends the standard treatment of neutron slowing-down theory found in reactor physics textbooks (e.g., references cited as ref3 and ref4 in the paper), which rely on narrow, broad (infinite mass), or intermediate resonance approximations that impose restrictive assumptions on resonance widths. By applying multiplicative integral theory from Volterra (ref1) and its commutative algebra formulation (ref2), the authors provide a rigorous derivation that removes these restrictions. The result enables exact treatment of resonant absorption in Fermi-type neutron spectra and may be generalized to other types of nuclear reactions where narrow resonance approximations are currently used.
