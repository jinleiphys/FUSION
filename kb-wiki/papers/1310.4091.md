# Bethe-Salpeter scattering amplitude in Minkowski space

> The off-mass shell scattering amplitude, satisfying the Bethe-Salpeter equation for spinless particles in Minkowski space with the ladder kernel, is computed for the first time.

## Key claim
The off-mass shell Bethe-Salpeter scattering amplitude for spinless particles in Minkowski space with a ladder kernel is computed for the first time, but only for the half-off-shell case (where the incident energy is on-shell) and restricted to S-wave; the full off-shell amplitude depending on two extra variables is not yet computed.

## Method
The inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space with one-boson exchange kernel is solved directly by analytically integrating over the time component of the loop momentum, handling the four sources of singularities (propagator poles, kernel logarithmic singularities, Born term singularities, and amplitude singularities) through subtraction techniques and variable transformations. The amplitude is factorized as \(F_0 = F_0^B f_0\) to remove the strongest singularities, yielding a regular equation solved by standard numerical methods. The S-wave projection is obtained via partial wave decomposition, and phase shifts are extracted from the on-shell amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length \(a_0\) for \(\mu=0.50\) and \(\alpha=0.01\): \(-0.403 \times 10^{-1}\) (in units where \(m=1\))
- Scattering length \(a_0\) for \(\mu=0.50\) and \(\alpha=1.00\): \(-0.103 \times 10^{3}\) (in units where \(m=1\))
- First inelastic threshold for \(\mu=0.5\): \(p_s^{(1)} = 0.75\) (in units where \(m=1\))
- On-shell amplitude for \(\alpha=0.5\), \(p_s=\mu=0.5\): \(F_0^{on} = 0.753 + i0.292\), giving phase shift \(\delta = 21.2^\circ\)
- Real phase shift for \(\alpha=1.2\), \(\mu=0.5\), \(p_s=0.05\): \(124^\circ\); for \(p_s=1.5\): \(19.3^\circ\)
- Imaginary phase shift for \(\alpha=1.2\), \(\mu=0.5\), \(p_s=0.8\): \(0.033^\circ\); for \(p_s=1.5\): \(0.852^\circ\)
- Bound state binding energy from the homogeneous equation coincides within four-digit accuracy with previous work (bs1)

## Context
This work extends the Nakanishi representation method developed by the same authors (bs1, bs2) for bound state Minkowski amplitudes to scattering states, providing the first direct solution of the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space. Prior approaches either relied on Wick rotation (Tjon, 1974) or were limited to separable kernels (Burov). The computed off-shell amplitude enables calculation of transition form factors (e.g., \(\gamma^* d \to np\)) and serves as input for three-body Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations, which previously required full Minkowski solutions not yet available.
