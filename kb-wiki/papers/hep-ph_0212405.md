# Vector Manifestation in Hot and/or Dense Matter

> In this talk I summarize main features of the vector manifestation (VM) which was recently proposed as a novel manifestation of the Wigner realization of chiral symmetry in which the symmetry is restored at the critical point by the massless degenerate pion (and its flavor partners) and the rho meson (and its flavor partners) as the chiral partner. I show how the VM is realized in hot and dense QCD using the effective field theory of QCD based on the hidden local symmetry.

## Key claim

The vector manifestation (VM) proposes that at the chiral phase transition point in hot and/or dense QCD, chiral symmetry is restored by the massless degenerate pion and rho meson as chiral partners, in contrast to the conventional linear sigma model where the scalar meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion. This realization depends on the intrinsic temperature and density dependences of the hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian parameters determined through Wilsonian matching with QCD, combined with the condition that the fixed point (g, a, m_q) = (0, 1, 0) is approached.

## Method

The author uses an effective field theory based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) that includes pions, rho mesons, and quasiquark fields, with the Lagrangian parameters having intrinsic temperature and/or density dependences determined by Wilsonian matching between the HLS current correlators and those from the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD at a matching scale Λ. The renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the parameters F_π, a, g, and m_q are derived at one-loop level, and the pole mass of the rho meson is computed by including hadronic thermal and dense loop corrections from pions, rho mesons, and quasiquarks at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The VM is characterized by F_π² → 0, m_ρ² → m_π² = 0, and F_ρ²/F_π² → 1 at the critical point.
- The mixing angle ψ between π and A_1 in the broken phase is experimentally approximately ψ = π/4.
- The RGEs include the constant C = N_f/[2(4π)²] and the quasiquark contribution proportional to N_c/(6π²)g⁴(1-κ)².
- Near the critical temperature, the rho pole mass becomes m_ρ²(T) = M_ρ² + N_f g² (15 - a²)/144 T² + N_c g² (1-κ)² (1/18) T².
- Near the critical chemical potential (with T=0), the rho pole mass becomes m_ρ²(μ) = M_ρ² + μ²/(6π²)(1-κ)².

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling proposal (Brown and Rho, 1991, 1996) that the rho-meson mass scales like the pion decay constant and vanishes at the chiral phase transition, by providing a specific mechanism (the vector manifestation) within the hidden local symmetry framework. The VM was first proposed in Harada and Yamawaki (HY:VM) for large flavor QCD, and was further developed for hot QCD in Harada and Sasaki (HS:VMT) and for dense QCD in Harada, Kim, and Rho (HKR). This enables predictions for the critical temperature expressed in terms of OPE parameters, the equality of vector and axialvector susceptibilities at the critical temperature, and the violation of vector dominance for the pion electromagnetic form factor near the critical temperature.
