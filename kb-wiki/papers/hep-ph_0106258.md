# Global Analysis of Solar Neutrino Oscillations Including SNO CC Measurement

> For active and sterile neutrinos, we present the globally allowed solutions for two neutrino oscillations. We include the SNO CC measurement and all other relevant solar neutrino and reactor data. Five active neutrino oscillation solutions (LMA, LOW, SMA, VAC, and Just So2) are currently allowed at 3 sigma; three sterile neutrino solutions (Just So2, SMA, and VAC) are allowed at 3 sigma. The goodness of fit is satisfactory for all eight solutions. We also investigate the robustness of the allowed solutions by carrying out global analyses with and without: 1) imposing solar model constraints on the 8B neutrino flux, 2) including the Super-Kamiokande spectral energy distribution and day-night data, 3) including a continuous mixture of active and sterile neutrinos, 4) using an enhanced CC cross section for deuterium (due to radiative corrections), and 5) a optimistic, hypothetical reduction by a factor of three of the error of the SNO CC rate. For every analysis strategy used in this paper, the most favored solutions all involve large mixing angles: LMA, LOW, or VAC. The favored solutions are robust, but the presence at 3 sigma of individual sterile solutions and the active Just So2 solution is sensitive to the analysis assumptions.

## Key claim

After including the SNO CC measurement, five active neutrino oscillation solutions (LMA, LOW, SMA, VAC, and Just So²) and three sterile neutrino solutions (Just So², SMA, and VAC) remain allowed at 3σ, but the existence of the sterile solutions and the active Just So² solution is sensitive to analysis assumptions such as whether the ⁸B neutrino flux is constrained by solar model predictions.

## Method

The authors performed a global χ² analysis of two-neutrino oscillation solutions using the SNO CC rate, Chlorine and Gallium rates, and the Super-Kamiokande day-night recoil energy spectra (38 spectral bins plus 3 rate data points). They treated active and sterile neutrinos on equal footing by including a third parameter cos²η for the active-sterile admixture, and tested robustness by varying whether the ⁸B flux was constrained by the BP00 standard solar model, whether an enhanced CC cross section for deuterium was used, and whether only total rates were considered.

## Key numbers

- LMA active solution best-fit: Δm² = 4.5 × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan²θ = 4.1 × 10⁻¹, χ²_min = 35.3, goodness-of-fit = 59%
- LOW active solution best-fit: Δm² = 1.0 × 10⁻⁷ eV², tan²θ = 7.1 × 10⁻¹, χ²_min = 38.4, goodness-of-fit = 45%
- SMA active solution best-fit: Δm² = 4.7 × 10⁻⁶ eV², tan²θ = 3.9 × 10⁻⁴, χ²_min = 45.4, goodness-of-fit = 19%
- VAC active solution best-fit: Δm² = 4.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV², tan²θ = 2.4 × 10⁰, χ²_min = 39.0, goodness-of-fit = 42%
- Just So² active solution best-fit: Δm² = 5.5 × 10⁻¹² eV², tan²θ = 0.67 (1.5) × 10⁰, χ²_min = 45.7, goodness-of-fit = 18%
- Sterile Just So² best-fit: Δm² = 5.5 × 10⁻¹² eV², tan²θ = 0.67 (1.5) × 10⁰, χ²_min = 45.8, goodness-of-fit = 18%
- Sterile SMA best-fit: Δm² = 4.5 × 10⁻⁶ eV², tan²θ = 3.1 × 10⁻⁴, χ²_min = 46.6, goodness-of-fit = 16%
- Sterile VAC best-fit: Δm² = 4.7 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV², tan²θ = 2.7 × 10⁻¹, χ²_min = 47.2, goodness-of-fit = 15%
- Chooz reactor bound limits allowed solutions to Δm² below approximately (7-8) × 10⁻⁴ eV²
- With a hypothetical factor-of-three reduction in SNO CC error, both active and sterile SMA solutions could be eliminated at 3σ

## Context

This paper extends the pre-SNO global analyses of Bahcall, Krastev, and Smirnov (2001) and Gonzalez-Garcia and Pena-Garay (2001) by incorporating the first SNO charged-current measurement of the ⁸B ν_e flux. The results show that the LMA, LOW, and VAC large-mixing-angle solutions are robust across analysis strategies, while the SMA and Just So² solutions are more fragile. The work enables future comparisons with the SNO neutral-current measurement and motivates improved precision on the SNO CC rate to discriminate among the remaining allowed solutions.
