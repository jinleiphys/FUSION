# Vector coherent state representations and their inner products

> Several advances have extended the power and versatility of coherent state theory to the extent that it has become a vital tool in the representation theory of Lie groups and their Lie algebras. Representative applications are reviewed and some new developments are introduced. The examples given are chosen to illustrate special features of the scalar and vector coherent state constructions and how they work in practical situations. Comparisons are made with Mackey's theory of induced representations. For simplicity, we focus on square integrable (discrete series) unitary representations although many of the techniques apply more generally, with minor adjustment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that vector coherent state (VCS) theory provides a practical and physically intuitive framework for constructing unitary irreducible representations of Lie algebras, particularly for non-compact groups, by inducing representations from multi-dimensional subgroup representations and using algebraic K-matrix methods to bypass computationally intensive integration of inner products.

## Method

The paper reviews scalar and vector coherent state constructions for Lie group representations, focusing on square-integrable (discrete series) unitary representations. It develops the VCS representation by inducing from a multi-dimensional irreducible representation of a subgroup H of G, using vector-valued wave functions defined over the group or its complex extension. The inner products and orthonormal bases are determined algebraically via K-matrix theory, which enforces Hermiticity conditions on the representation operators rather than evaluating explicit integral inner products.

## Key numbers

- For the su(1,1) scalar coherent state representation, the orthonormal basis wave functions are given by \(\Psi_{\lambda n}(z) = \sqrt{\frac{(\lambda + n -1)!}{(\lambda-1)!\, n!}}\, z^n\) for \(n=0,1,2,\dots\), with lowest weight parameter \(\lambda\).
- The su(1,1) raising and lowering operator matrix elements are \(\hat{\mathcal{S}}_+ \Psi_{\lambda n} = \sqrt{(\lambda+n)(n+1)} \, \Psi_{\lambda,n+1}\) and \(\hat{\mathcal{S}}_- \Psi_{\lambda,n+1} = \sqrt{(\lambda+n)(n+1)} \, \Psi_{\lambda n}\).
- For the u(3) VCS representation, the eigenvalue of the scalar operator \(\hat\Lambda\) on basis states \(\Psi^{(\lambda)}_{jSM}\) is \(\Omega^{(\lambda)}_{jS} = (2\lambda_1-\lambda_2-\lambda_3)j - S(S+1) + s(s+1) - j(j-2)\), where \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3\) are the highest-weight labels and \(s = \frac12(\lambda_2-\lambda_3)\).

## Context

This work extends the coherent state methods of Bargmann (1961), Segal (1963), Perelomov (1972), and Onofri (1975) to vector-valued wave functions, building on earlier VCS developments by Rowe (1984) and Hecht (1987). It provides a practical alternative to the Harish-Chandra (1955) holomorphic representations for Sp(n,R) groups, which had intractable inner products, and relates to Mackey's (1968) theory of induced representations. The methods enable construction of explicit representations for Lie algebras needed in nuclear physics applications, particularly the microscopic nuclear collective model using sp(3,R) irreps.
