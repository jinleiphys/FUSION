# Phase Transition in Evolutionary Games

> The evolution of cooperative behaviour is studied in the deterministic version of the Prisoners' Dilemma on a two-dimensional lattice. The payoff parameter is set at the critical region $1.8 < b < 2.0$ , where clusters of cooperators are formed in all spatial sizes. Using the factorial moments developed in particle and nuclear physics for the study of phase transition, the distribution of cooperators is studied as a function of the bin size covering varying numbers of lattice cells. From the scaling behaviour of the moments a scaling exponent is determined and is found to lie in the range where phase transitions are known to take place in physical systems. It is therefore inferred that when the payoff parameter is increased through the critical region the biological system of cooperators undergoes a phase transition to defectors. The universality of the critical behaviour is thus extended to include also this particular model of evolution dynamics.

## Key claim
The paper infers that when the payoff parameter \(b\) in a deterministic Prisoners' Dilemma on a 2D lattice is increased through the critical range \(1.8 < b < 2.0\), the system of cooperators undergoes a phase transition to defectors, based on a scaling exponent \(\nu = 1.23 \pm 0.15\) that lies within the range known for phase transitions in physical systems. The claim is hedged by noting that the exponent agrees with the Ginzburg-Landau value of 1.304 only within its errors, and that the Ising model in two dimensions yields a range of \(\nu\) between 1.0 and 1.5.

## Method
The authors simulated a deterministic Prisoners' Dilemma on a \(120 \times 120\) lattice with periodic boundary conditions, starting with 50% cooperators (C) and 50% defectors (D) distributed randomly. After 50 time steps, they counted the number of C sites in bins of size \(\delta^2\) (with \(\delta\) from 5 to 20) over \(10^3\) runs, and computed the normalized factorial moments \(F_q\) as defined in particle and nuclear physics. They then examined the scaling of \(F_q\) with bin size and the relation \(F_q \propto F_2^{\beta_q}\), fitting the exponent \(\nu\) from \(\beta_q = (q-1)^\nu\).

## Key numbers
- The payoff parameter critical range is \(1.8 < b < 2.0\), called \(B_c\).
- The scaling exponent \(\nu\) is determined to be \(1.23 \pm 0.15\).
- This value is compared to 1.304 from Ginzburg-Landau theory and to a range of 1.0 to 1.5 for the 2D Ising model.
- Simulations used a \(120 \times 120\) lattice, with bin sizes \(\delta\) from 5 to 20, and \(10^3\) runs.

## Context
This work extends the concept of phase transition to evolutionary biology, specifically the deterministic Prisoners' Dilemma on a lattice as studied by Nowak and May (1992, 1993). It applies the factorial-moment scaling method developed for quark-hadron phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions (Hwa and collaborators, 1990s) and previously verified in quantum optics experiments on lasing thresholds (Cao and Hwa, 1995). The result suggests that the scaling exponent \(\nu\) near 1.3 may serve as a universal signature of phase transition across subnuclear, atomic, condensed-matter, and biological systems, and it enables future study of stochastic evolutionary games and multistate cooperation models.
