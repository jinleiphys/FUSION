# Correlation between the quenching of total GT+ strength and the increase of E2 strength

> Relations between the total beta+ Gamow-Teller (GT+) strength and the E2 strength are further examined. It is found that in shell-model calculations for N=Z nuclei, in which changes in deformation are induced by varying the single-particle energies, the total GT+ or GT- strength decreases monotonically with increasing values of the B(E2) from the ground state to the first excited J=2+ state. Similar trends are also seen for the double GT transition amplitude (with some exceptions) and for the spin part of the total M1 strength as a function of B(E2).

## Key claim

The paper finds that in shell-model calculations for N=Z nuclei, the total GT+ (or GT-) strength decreases monotonically as the B(E2) value from the ground state to the first excited J=2+ state increases, but this relationship is not single-valued for very small GT+ strengths where many models give zero or nearly zero GT+ values.

## Method

The authors performed complete-space shell-model calculations for the N=Z nuclei 20Ne (using the Wildenthal interaction in the sd shell) and 44Ti (using the MKB and FPD6 interactions in the fp shell). They varied the single-particle energies by a parameter x to change the spin-orbit splitting and thereby the nuclear deformation, then computed the total GT+ strength, B(E2) values, double Gamow-Teller amplitudes, and M1 strengths as functions of x.

## Key numbers

- In the single j-shell model, the total GT+ strength B_t(GT+) equals 6.0 for both 20Ne and 44Ti.
- For the realistic case (x=0), B_t(GT+) is reduced to about 0.55 in 20Ne and to 1.88 with the MKB interaction (1.27 with the FPD6 interaction) in 44Ti.
- For 54Fe, the total GT+ strength measured in (n,p) reaction is B_t(GT+)=3.5, with a B(E2) value of 980 e^2 fm^4.
- For 56Fe, the total GT+ strength measured in (n,p) reaction is B_t(GT+)=2.3, with a B(E2) value of 620 e^2 fm^4.
- The product of B(E2) and total GT+ strength from experiments is about 2170 e^2 fm^4 for 54Fe and 2254 e^2 fm^4 for 56Fe.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of Auerbach (1993) that the total GT+ strength is a monotonically descending function of B(E2) in 26Mg, now confirming the same trend in 20Ne and 44Ti. The relationship provides a practical way to estimate the quenching of GT+ strength from measured B(E2) values, as demonstrated by recent (n,p) reaction measurements on 54Fe and 56Fe by Ronn and collaborators. The results also connect to the well-established correlation between total orbital M1 strength and B(E2) values found experimentally by Bohle and Richter and theoretically by Lo Iudice, Iachello, Heyde, and Zamick.
