# High order flow harmonics of identified hadrons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with the AMPT initial conditions, we study the higher order flow harmonics of identified hadrons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Comparison with the recent ALICE measurements at 20-30\% centrality shows that our calculations nicely describe the data below 2 GeV, especially for the $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$ mass-orderings among pions, kaons and protons. We also extended the calculations to other centrality bins, which presents similar mass-ordering patterns for these flow harmonics as the ones observed at 20-30\% centrality. In the later part of this article, we explore the development of $v_n$ mass ordering/splitting during the hadronic evolution through the comparison runs from iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model and pure hydrodynamics with different decoupling temperatures.

## Key claim

Using the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with AMPT initial conditions, the authors show that their calculations "nicely describe" the ALICE data for v2, v3, and v4 mass-orderings among pions, kaons, and protons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at 20-30% centrality, but only below 2 GeV; the description is qualified by the need for fine-tuned parameters (shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08, Gaussian smearing sigma = 0.6 fm) to simultaneously fit all three harmonics.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event hybrid model iEBE-VISHNU, which couples (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with the hadron cascade model UrQMD, with a switching temperature of 165 MeV between the macroscopic and microscopic approaches. Initial energy density profiles were constructed from the AMPT string melting model (version 2.21) with a Gaussian smearing factor sigma = 0.6 fm, assuming thermalization at tau0 = 0.4 fm/c and using a specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08. Differential flow harmonics v2(pT), v3(pT), and v4(pT) of identified hadrons were calculated using the Q-cumulant method with zero pseudorapidity gap, and compared with ALICE data at 20-30% centrality, with predictions extended to 10-20%, 30-40%, and 40-50% centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio set to eta/s = 0.08, with Gaussian smearing factor sigma = 0.6 fm, to simultaneously fit v2(pT), v3(pT), and v4(pT) of all charged hadrons.
- Hydrodynamic starting time tau0 = 0.4 fm/c; switching/decoupling temperature Tsw = 165 MeV for the hybrid model.
- Comparison runs used pure hydrodynamics with decoupling temperatures Tdec = 165 MeV and Tdec = 120 MeV.
- Calculations describe ALICE data for v2, v3, and v4 of pions, kaons, and protons at 20-30% centrality below 2 GeV.
- Predictions show similar mass-ordering patterns for v3(pT) and v4(pT) at 10-20%, 30-40%, and 40-50% centralities as observed at 20-30% centrality.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow of identified hadrons at RHIC and the LHC (Adams 2003, Abelev 2014) to higher-order flow harmonics v3 and v4, following recent ALICE measurements at 2.76 TeV (ALICE 2015). It builds on the finding by Bhalerao (2015) that AMPT initial conditions with event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics describe integrated and differential flow harmonics of all charged hadrons, and extends that approach to identified hadrons using the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model. The comparison runs between pure hydrodynamics and the hybrid model demonstrate that microscopic hadronic scatterings in UrQMD are necessary to correctly describe the mass-splitting between pions and protons for v3 and v4, similar to earlier findings for elliptic flow by Song (2013).
