# Precision Global Determination of the $B\to X_sγ$ Decay Rate

> We perform the first global fit to inclusive $B\to X_sγ$ measurements using a model-independent treatment of the nonperturbative $b$-quark distribution function, with next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic resummation and $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$ fixed-order contributions. The normalization of the $B\to X_sγ$ decay rate, given by $\lvert C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^*\rvert^2$, is sensitive to physics beyond the Standard Model (SM). We determine $\lvert C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^* \rvert = (14.77 \pm 0.51_{\rm fit} \pm 0.59_{\rm theory} \pm 0.08_{\rm param})\times 10^{-3}$, in good agreement with the SM prediction, and the $b$-quark mass $m_b^{1S} = (4.750 \pm 0.027_{\rm fit} \pm 0.033_{\rm theory} \pm 0.003_{\rm param})\,\mathrm{GeV}$. Our results suggest that the uncertainties in the extracted $B\to X_sγ$ rate have been underestimated by up to a factor of two, leaving more room for beyond-SM contributions.

## Key claim

The first global fit to inclusive $B\to X_s\gamma$ measurements using a model-independent treatment of the nonperturbative $b$-quark distribution function finds that the uncertainties in the extracted decay rate have been underestimated by up to a factor of two compared to previous analyses, leaving more room for beyond-Standard-Model contributions, while the central value remains in good agreement with the Standard Model prediction.

## Method

The analysis performs a binned $\chi^2$ fit to four differential $B\to X_s\gamma$ measurements, using a factorized expression for the photon energy spectrum based on soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) with next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) resummation and $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$ fixed-order contributions. The nonperturbative $b$-quark shape function $\mathcal{F}(k)$ is expanded in a complete orthonormal basis, with the expansion truncated at order $N=3$ determined by nested hypothesis tests, and the normalization parameter $N_s = |C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^*|^2 \widehat m_b^2$ and shape-function coefficients $c_n$ are fitted simultaneously from the data.

## Key numbers

* $|C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^*| = (14.77 \pm 0.51_{\rm fit} \pm 0.59_{\rm theory} \pm 0.08_{\rm param}) \times 10^{-3}$, extracted from the global fit to inclusive $B\to X_s\gamma$ spectra
* $m_b^{1S} = (4.750 \pm 0.027_{\rm fit} \pm 0.033_{\rm theory} \pm 0.003_{\rm param})\,\mathrm{GeV}$, the $b$-quark mass in the 1S scheme determined simultaneously from the fit
* $\widehat\lambda_1 = (-0.210 \pm 0.046_{\rm fit} \pm 0.040_{\rm theory} \pm 0.056_{\rm param})\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$, the kinetic energy parameter in the invisible scheme
* Standard Model prediction: $|C_7^{\rm incl}|_{\rm SM} = 0.3624 \pm 0.0128_{c\bar c} \pm 0.0080_{\rm scale}$, yielding $|C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^*|_{\rm SM} = (14.96 \pm 0.68) \times 10^{-3}$
* The extracted $|C_7^{\rm incl} V_{tb} V_{ts}^*|^2$ uncertainty is 10.6%, about twice the 5.5% uncertainty obtained when neglecting theory and truncation uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ligeti (2008) for model-independent shape-function expansions and applies it for the first time to a global fit of inclusive $B\to X_s\gamma$ data, going beyond earlier analyses that used specific shape-function models (Benson 2004, Lange 2005, Gambino 2007). The result enables a more reliable comparison between the extracted Wilson coefficient $C_7^{\rm incl}$ and Standard Model predictions (Misiak 2015), and provides a framework that can be improved with future high-precision Belle II measurements.
