# Systematically Improvable Tensor Hypercontraction: Interpolative Separable Density-Fitting for Molecules Applied to Exact Exchange, Second- and Third-Order Møller-Plesset Perturbation Theory

> We present a systematically improvable tensor hypercontraction (THC) factorization based on interpolative separable density fitting (ISDF). We illustrate algorithmic details to achieve this within the framework of Becke's atom-centered quadrature grid. A single ISDF parameter $c_\text{ISDF}$ controls the tradeoff between accuracy and cost. In particular, $c_\text{ISDF}$ sets the number of interpolation points used in THC, $N_\text{IP} = c_\text{ISDF}\times N_\text{X}$ with $N_\text{X}$ being the number of auxiliary basis functions. In conjunction with the resolution-of-the-identity (RI) technique, we develop and investigate the THC-RI algorithms for cubic-scaling exact exchange for Hartree-Fock and range-separated hybrids (e.g., $ω$B97X-V) and quartic-scaling second- and third-order Møller-Plesset theory (MP2 and MP3). These algorithms were evaluated over the W4-11 thermochemistry (atomization energy) set and A24 non-covalent interaction benchmark set with standard Dunning basis sets (cc-pVDZ, cc-pVTZ, aug-cc-pVDZ, and aug-cc-pVTZ). We demonstrate the convergence of THC-RI algorithms to numerically exact RI results using ISDF points. Based on these, we make recommendations on $c_\text{ISDF}$ for each basis set and method. We also demonstrate the utility of THC-RI exact exchange and MP2 for larger systems such as water clusters and $\text{C}_{20}$. We stress that more challenges await in obtaining accurate and numerically stable THC factorization for wavefunction amplitudes as well as the space spanned by virtual orbitals in large basis sets and implementing sparsity-aware THC-RI algorithms.

## Key claim

The paper presents a tensor hypercontraction (THC) factorization based on interpolative separable density fitting (ISDF) that is systematically improvable via a single parameter \(c_\text{ISDF}\), which controls the tradeoff between accuracy and cost by setting the number of interpolation points as \(N_\text{IP} = c_\text{ISDF} \times N_\text{X}\). The authors demonstrate convergence to numerically exact resolution-of-the-identity (RI) results, but stress that challenges remain in obtaining accurate and numerically stable THC factorization for wavefunction amplitudes and virtual orbital spaces in large basis sets.

## Method

The authors adapt the ISDF point selection using a modified centroidal Voronoi tessellation (CVT) algorithm on Becke's atom-centered quadrature grid, looping over individual atoms to achieve linear scaling. They combine this with the least-squares THC (LS-THC) approach to determine interpolation vectors, and then develop THC-RI algorithms that use an RI auxiliary basis to evaluate the Coulomb integrals between interpolation vectors. These algorithms are applied to cubic-scaling exact exchange for Hartree-Fock and range-separated hybrids, and quartic-scaling second- and third-order Møller-Plesset perturbation theory (MP2 and MP3).

## Key numbers

- The number of interpolation points is set by \(N_\text{IP} = c_\text{ISDF} \times N_\text{X}\), where \(N_\text{X}\) is the number of auxiliary basis functions and \(c_\text{ISDF}\) is a single tunable parameter.
- The RI auxiliary basis set size \(N_\text{X}\) is roughly 3–4 times larger than the original basis set to achieve accuracy of 50–60 \(\mu E_h\) per atom (citing Eichkorn 1995, Eichkorn 1997).
- The threshold of \(10^{-12}\) in the drop tolerance for evaluating the pseudoinverse \(\tilde{\mathbf S}^{+}\) was found sufficient for the paper's purposes.
- The formation of the intermediate \(\sum_{\mu\nu}(P|\mu\nu)C_{\mu\nu}^{L}\) scales as \(\mathcal O(N_\text{AO}^2 N_\text{X} N_\text{IP})\) without sparsity, but can be reduced to \(\mathcal O(N_\text{SP} N_\text{X} N_\text{IP})\) with shell-pair screening where \(N_\text{SP}\) is the number of significant shell-pairs.

## Context

This work extends the ISDF approach previously applied to periodic boundary condition systems (Hu 2017, Lu 2017, Dong 2018) to molecular simulations using atom-centered Gaussian basis sets, addressing the lack of generality in prior LS-THC techniques (Martinez and co-workers, Parrish 2012, Kokkila-Schumacher 2015). The CVT procedure achieves both compactness and generality comparable to hand-optimized grids, unlike the radial discrete value representation (R-DVR) of Martinez and co-workers (Parrish 2013a) which was less compact for water clusters. The paper enables systematic application of THC factorization to exact exchange, MP2, and MP3 for molecules with standard Dunning basis sets, with recommendations for the \(c_\text{ISDF}\) parameter per basis set and method.
