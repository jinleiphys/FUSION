# $D$ and $D^*$ mesons in isospin asymmetric nuclear medium

> We investigate the properties of pseudoscalar $D$ and vector $D^*$ mesons in an isospin asymmetric nuclear medium using a hybrid approach that integrates the light-front quark model with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. The influence of isospin asymmetric nuclear medium is examined by utilizing the in-medium quark masses derived from the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model as an input in the light-front quark model to study the medium modification of $D$ mesons. We examine the impact of isospin asymmetry and baryon density at zero and finite temperature on the effective masses, weak decay constants, and distribution amplitudes of the pseudoscalar mesons $D^0$, $D^+$, $D_s$, and the vector mesons $D^{0*}$, $D^{+*}$, and $D_s^*$. Our results indicate significant medium-induced changes for pseudoscalar $D$ and vector $D^*$ mesons having $u/d$ as one of their constituent quarks, while a comparatively reduced effect is observed for mesons containing a strange quark. In contrast to temperature and isospin asymmetry, changes in the baryon density of the nuclear medium have a larger effect on different properties of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the properties of pseudoscalar \(D\) and vector \(D^*\) mesons are significantly modified in an isospin asymmetric nuclear medium, with baryon density having a larger effect than temperature or isospin asymmetry, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific hybrid framework combining the light-front quark model with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, and the effect is notably reduced for mesons containing a strange quark.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid approach that integrates the light-front quark model (LFQM) with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model. In-medium quark masses were first computed from the CQMF model by solving the thermodynamic potential minimization equations for different baryon densities, temperatures, and isospin asymmetries; these masses were then used as input in the LFQM, where a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian with linear confinement, Coulomb, and hyperfine potentials was solved variationally using a Gaussian trial wave function to obtain the meson masses, weak decay constants, and distribution amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (\(\rho = \rho_0\)), the effective mass of the \(D\) meson was found to decrease by approximately 50–60 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter, consistent with prior QCD sum rule and quark meson coupling model results.
- The weak decay constant of the \(D^+\) meson showed a reduction of about 15–20% at \(\rho = \rho_0\) compared to its vacuum value, with the exact percentage depending on the isospin asymmetry parameter \(\eta\).
- For the \(D_s\) meson (containing a strange quark), the medium-induced changes in mass and decay constant were less than 5% at \(\rho = \rho_0\), indicating a reduced sensitivity to the nuclear medium.
- The vector \(D^{*0}\) meson exhibited a mass shift of approximately 40–50 MeV at \(\rho = \rho_0\) in symmetric nuclear matter, with the shift decreasing by about 10–15% when the isospin asymmetry parameter was set to \(\eta = 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium meson properties, such as those on pions and kaons using the light-cone quark model and chiral SU(3) quark mean field model by Kaur (2024) and Puhan (2025), and on \(B\) mesons by Tanisha (2025). It also builds on earlier calculations of \(D\) meson mass shifts in symmetric nuclear matter using the QCD sum rule by Hayashigaki (2000) and the quark meson coupling model by Tsushima (1998). The results enable future investigations into the behavior of charmed mesons in dense astrophysical environments like neutron stars and in heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as PANDA at GSI and CBM at FAIR.
