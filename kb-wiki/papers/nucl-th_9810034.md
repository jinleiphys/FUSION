# Squeezed Correlations and Spectra for Mass-Shifted Bosons

> Huge back-to-back correlations are shown to arise for thermal ensembles of bosonic states with medium-modified masses. The effect is experimentally observable in high energy heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Huge back-to-back correlations (BBC) arise for thermal ensembles of bosonic states with medium-modified masses, and these correlations are unbounded from above, in contrast to the limited correlations from classical current formalisms. The effect is experimentally observable in high energy heavy ion collisions, surviving orders of magnitude suppression from finite emission times.

## Method

The authors model a scalar field with a medium-modified mass via a Bogoliubov transformation that relates in-medium quasi-particles (b-quanta, which are thermalized) to asymptotic observable bosons (a-quanta). They compute the single-particle and two-particle momentum distributions for a homogeneous thermal gas and then generalize to inhomogeneous, expanding systems by dividing the fluid into independent cells, each with local temperature, chemical potential, and flow fields, and introducing a probability distribution for freeze-out times to account for finite emission duration.

## Key numbers

- For phi mesons at T = 140 MeV and |k| = 0, 300, or 500 MeV, back-to-back correlations as big as 100–1000 are found for reasonable values of the medium-modified mass m*_phi.
- For a typical exponential decay with delta t = 2 fm/c and phi mesons with omega ~ 1–2 GeV, the BBC are suppressed by a factor ~10^-3 but survive with measurable strength, as large as 2–3 (the scale of HBT correlations).
- The back-to-back correlation C_2(k, -k) is unbounded: 1 ≤ C_2(k, -k) < infinity, and as |k| → infinity, C_2(k, -k) → infinity.

## Context

This work extends the Hanbury Brown–Twiss (HBT) / Goldhaber–Goldhaber–Lee–Pais (GGLP) effect to include medium-modified masses, showing that mass shifts induce two-mode squeezed states and back-to-back correlations not captured by the classical current formalism of Surdutovich et al. (1998), which predicted C_2 ≤ 3. It corrects an earlier incorrect Bogoliubov transformation by Asakawa and Csorgo (1998) that predicted large HBT correlations at identical momenta. The results enable experimental searches for in-medium hadronic mass modifications at CERN SPS and RHIC BNL heavy ion experiments, and may allow determination of the freeze-out time distribution.
