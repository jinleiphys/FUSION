# Elliptic flow in transport theory and hydrodynamics

> We present a new direct simulation Monte-Carlo method for solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation. We solve numerically the 2-dimensional Boltzmann equation using this new algorithm. We find that elliptic flow from this transport calculation smoothly converges towards the value from ideal hydrodynamics as the number of collisions per particle increases, as expected on general theoretical grounds, but in contrast with previous transport calculations.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow from the Boltzmann transport calculation smoothly converges towards the ideal hydrodynamics value as the number of collisions per particle increases, in contrast with previous transport calculations which found hydrodynamics produced 30-40% larger elliptic flow than the Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors solve the 2-dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation numerically using a new direct simulation Monte-Carlo method where particles are treated as Lorentz-contracted rods (oblate spheroids) with deterministic collisions based on impact parameter. They simulate a gas of massless particles with isotropic 2-to-2 elastic collisions and a cross section independent of center-of-mass energy, initializing particles with a Gaussian spatial distribution and thermal momentum distribution. The elliptic flow is computed as a function of the Knudsen number (Kn) and dilution parameter (D), and results are extrapolated to the hydrodynamic limit Kn=0, D=0.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic limit value of elliptic flow from the Boltzmann calculation is v2^h = 0.102 ± 0.003, compatible with the independent hydro calculation value v2^hydro = 0.101 ± 0.003
- For D << 1, the fit parameter Kn0 = 0.70 ± 0.03 in the formula v2 = v2^h / (1 + Kn/Kn0)
- An average of 2.3 collisions per particle is required to achieve 50% of the hydrodynamic limit on v2; 9.1 collisions per particle are required for 80%
- For semi-central Au-Au collisions at RHIC, an isotropic cross section of 3 mb produces ~49% of the hydro v2 value, 8 mb produces ~72%, and 20 mb produces ~87%

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of how transport theory converges to hydrodynamics, directly addressing the discrepancy found by Molnar and Huovinen (2004) where hydrodynamics produced 30-40% larger elliptic flow than Boltzmann transport. The authors argue that previous transport calculations violated the dilution condition (D << 1) required for Boltzmann equation validity, with D ≈ 0.65 in those calculations even with parton subdivision. This work enables quantitative estimates of how many partonic collisions are needed to approach ideal fluid behavior, and provides a framework for interpreting elliptic flow measurements at RHIC in terms of the Knudsen number and effective cross sections.
