# The effect of recoils on soft-drop-groomed observables in $γ$-tagged jets in a multistage approach

> We investigate medium-induced modifications to jet substructure observables that characterize hard components in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$~TeV. Using a multistage Monte Carlo simulation of in-medium jet shower evolution, we explore flavor-dependent medium effects through simulations of inclusive and $γ$-tagged jets. The results show that quark jets undergo a nonmonotonic modification compared with gluon jets in observables such as the Pb-Pb to $p$-$p$ ratio of the soft drop prong angle $r_g$, the relative prong transverse momentum $k_{T,g}$, and the groomed mass $m_g$ distributions. Due to this nonmonotonic modification, $γ$-tagged jets, enriched in quark jets, provide surprisingly clear signals of medium-induced structural modifications, distinct from effects dominated by selection bias. Further systematic studies demonstrate that these effects are dominated by recoil medium response. This work highlights the potential of hard substructures in $γ$-tagged jets as powerful tools for probing the jet-medium interactions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, in particular by enabling detailed investigations of jet-medium parton scatterings via their associated medium response. All simulations for $γ$-tagged jet analyses carried out in this paper used triggered events containing at least one hard photon, which highlights the utility of these observables for future Bayesian analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the suppression of soft-drop-groomed jet substructure observables in inclusive jet measurements arises primarily from selection bias rather than from intrinsic structural modifications of the hard component, and that gamma-tagged jets reveal genuine medium-induced modifications dominated by recoil medium response, particularly for quark jets which undergo nonmonotonic modifications compared with gluon jets.

## Method

The authors employed the JETSCAPEv3.5 AA22 tune within the JETSCAPE framework, using a multistage MATTER+LBT Monte Carlo simulation of in-medium jet shower evolution for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. They generated gamma-jet events using PYTHIA 8 with prompt photon production enabled, applied soft-drop grooming with the Cambridge-Aachen algorithm to identify hard branchings, and compared results with and without recoil and hole partons to isolate medium response effects.

## Key numbers

* Central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV were simulated using the JETSCAPEv3.5 AA22 tune
* The switching parameter between MATTER and LBT modules was set to Q^2_sw=4 GeV^2 (Q_sw=2 GeV)
* The virtuality cutoff scale for hadronization was Q_min^2=1 GeV^2
* The critical temperature for the QGP medium boundary was T_c=0.16 GeV
* Soft-drop parameters z_cut and beta control the grooming procedure, with the condition p_T2/(p_T1+p_T2) > z_cut*(DeltaR_12/R)^beta

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the JETSCAPE framework (JETSCAPE 2022, 2023) which established benchmark results for inclusive jet observables and showed monotonic suppression of the r_g distribution. The paper builds on the finding that inclusive jet measurements are dominated by selection bias, as suggested by Milhano (2017), and demonstrates that gamma-tagged jets provide cleaner access to medium-induced structural modifications. This enables future Bayesian analyses of jet-medium parton scatterings and their associated medium response, with implications for understanding the quark-gluon plasma at both RHIC and LHC energies.
