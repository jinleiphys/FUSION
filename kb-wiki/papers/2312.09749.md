# Study of a longitudinally expanding plasma with the 2PI effective action

> A central question in heavy-ion collisions is how the initial far-from-equilibrium medium evolves and thermalizes while it undergoes a rapid longitudinal expansion. In this work we use the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action for the first time to consider this question, focusing on $ϕ^4$ scalar theory truncated at three loops. We calculate the momentum distribution of quasiparticles in the medium and show that isotropization takes place. We furthermore consider the thermal mass of quasiparticles and the importance of number-changing processes.

## Key claim

Using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action truncated at three loops in \(\phi^4\) scalar theory, the authors show that a longitudinally expanding medium undergoes isotropization, but only under the condition that number-changing processes (which are absent in kinetic theory) are included, producing a 20% reduction in particle number density.

## Method

The authors solve the equations of motion for the statistical function \(F(\tau, \tau'; p_\perp, \nu)\) and spectral function \(\rho(\tau, \tau'; p_\perp, \nu)\) in Milne coordinates, assuming boost invariance and transverse homogeneity. The 2PI effective action is truncated at three loops, including tadpole and bubble diagrams, and the system is initialized at \(\tau = 0.004\) with a free spectral function and an initial occupation density \(f_0(p_\perp, \nu) = e^{-p_\perp^2/\alpha^2} e^{-\nu^2/\beta^2}\) where \(\alpha = \beta = 4.0\). An occupation density is extracted by fitting the full statistical function to a quasiparticle ansatz involving Hankel functions.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant used in the full calculation is \(g^4 = 500\).
- The vacuum mass is \(m = 2.5\) (in the same units as the typical momentum scale of 4.0).
- The initial occupation density parameters are \(\alpha = \beta = 4.0\), setting the typical momentum scale.
- The full calculation shows a \(20\,\%\) overall reduction in number density due to number-changing processes.
- The system is initialized at proper time \(\tau = 0.004\) and the effective mass is shown at \(\tau = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the 2PI effective action framework, previously applied to isotropic expansion in cosmology by Tranberg (year not given in text) and in a proof-of-concept calculation by Hatta (year not given in text), to the longitudinally expanding geometry relevant to heavy-ion collisions. It provides a unified framework that bridges classical field theory and kinetic theory, enabling future studies of isotropization in the stress-energy tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) and the decay of a background field \(\phi\) into quasiparticles, though full renormalization for UV-sensitive observables remains to be addressed.
