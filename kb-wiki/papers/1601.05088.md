# Factorization and Resummation for Generic Hierarchies between Jets

> Jets are an important probe to identify the hard interaction of interest at the LHC. They are routinely used in Standard Model precision measurements as well as in searches for new heavy particles, including jet substructure methods. In processes with several jets, one typically encounters hierarchies in the jet transverse momenta and/or dijet invariant masses. Large logarithms of the ratios of these kinematic jet scales in the cross section are at present primarily described by parton showers. We present a general factorization framework called SCET$_+$, which is an extension of Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) and allows for a systematic higher-order resummation of such kinematic logarithms for generic jet hierarchies. In SCET$_+$ additional intermediate soft/collinear modes are used to resolve jets arising from additional soft and/or collinear QCD emissions. The resulting factorized cross sections utilize collinear splitting amplitudes and soft gluon currents and fully capture spin and color correlations. We discuss how to systematically combine the different kinematic regimes to obtain a complete description of the jet phase space. To present its application in a simple context, we use the case of $e^+e^- \to $3 jets. We then discuss in detail the application to N-jet processes at hadron colliders, considering representative classes of hierarchies from which the general case can be built. This includes in particular multiple hierarchies that are either strongly ordered in angle or energy or not.

## Key claim

The paper presents SCET$_+$, a general factorization framework extending Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that enables systematic higher-order resummation of kinematic logarithms arising from hierarchies in jet transverse momenta and/or dijet invariant masses in multijet processes, which were previously described primarily by parton showers at leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Method

The authors construct SCET$_+$ by introducing additional intermediate soft/collinear modes (collinear-soft and soft-collinear modes) that resolve jets arising from additional soft and/or collinear QCD emissions, with their precise scaling determined by the considered measurement. They perform a multistage matching procedure: first matching QCD onto standard SCET, then matching onto SCET$_+$ with the appropriate intermediate modes, using collinear splitting amplitudes and soft gluon currents to capture spin and color correlations. The different kinematic regimes (purely collinear, purely soft, and soft/collinear overlap) are systematically combined and matched to the corresponding SCET in the nonhierarchical limit to obtain a complete description of the jet phase space.

## Key numbers

* For the $c+$ regime (two collinear jets): the hierarchy is characterized by $s_{12} \ll s_{13} \sim s_{23} \sim Q^2$, where $s_{ij}$ are dijet invariant masses and $Q$ is the total center-of-mass energy.
* For the $s+$ regime (one soft jet): the hierarchy is characterized by $s_{12} \sim s_{13} \ll s_{23} \sim Q^2$.
* For the $cs+$ regime (soft/collinear overlap): the hierarchy is characterized by $s_{12} \ll s_{13} \ll s_{23} \sim Q^2$.
* The standard SCET case (no hierarchies) has $m_J^2 \ll s_{12} \sim s_{13} \sim s_{23} \sim Q^2$, where $m_J$ is the typical jet mass.
* The power expansion parameter is $\lambda^2 \equiv \Tau_N/Q \sim m_J^2/Q^2$, where $\Tau_N$ is the $N$-jettiness resolution variable.

## Context

This work extends the SCET framework of Bauer et al. (2000-2002) and Beneke (2002) to handle generic jet hierarchies, building on earlier SCET$_+$ applications by Bauer (2011) for collinear regimes, Procura (2014) for simultaneous resolution variables, and Larkoski (2015) for soft regimes and nonglobal logarithms. The framework enables systematic higher-order resummation for LHC processes with jet hierarchies, including boosted object reconstruction via jet substructure, cascade decays of heavy particles, and Higgs measurements with QCD-induced jets, going beyond the leading-logarithmic accuracy of Monte Carlo parton showers.
