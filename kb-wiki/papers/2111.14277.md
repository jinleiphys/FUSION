# Precise measurement of differential cross sections of the Σ-p --> Λ n reaction in momentum range 470-650 MeV/c

> The differential cross sections of the Σ-p --> Λ n reaction were measured accurately for the Σ- momentum (p_{Σ}) ranging from 470 to 650 MeV/c at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility. Precise angular information about the Σ-p --> Λ n reaction was obtained for the first time by detecting approximately 100 reaction events at each angular step of Δcosθ = 0.1. The obtained differential cross sections show slightly forward-peaking structure in the measured momentum regions.The cross sections integrated for -0.7 < cosθ < 1.0 were obtained as 22.5 +- 0.68 (stat.) +- 0.65 (syst.) mb and 15.8 +-0.83(stat.) +- 0.52 (syst.) mb for 470<p_{Σ}(MeV/c)<550 and 550<p_{Σ}(MeV/c)<650, respectively. These results show a drastic improvement compared to past measurements of the hyperon-proton scattering experiments. They will play essential roles in updating the theoretical models of the baryon-baryon interactions.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections of the Σ⁻p → Λn reaction show a slightly forward-peaking structure in the measured momentum range 470–650 MeV/c, but the theoretical predictions from the χEFT NLO13 and NLO19 models are similar despite having quite different ΛN-ΣN coupling strengths, meaning the present data alone cannot distinguish between these coupling scenarios.

## Method

The J-PARC E40 collaboration produced Σ⁻ particles via the π⁻p → K⁺Σ⁻ reaction in a liquid hydrogen target and identified secondary Σ⁻p → Λn events by detecting the Λ → pπ⁻ decay products with the CATCH system (cylindrical scintillation fiber tracker, BGO calorimeter, and scintillator hodoscope). Background contributions from np, π⁻p, and Σ⁻p elastic scatterings were separated by simultaneously fitting four ΔE and Δp spectra with simulated templates for each possible reaction. The differential cross sections were derived from efficiency-corrected event counts binned in cosθ intervals of 0.1, using the total Σ⁻ flight length and target density.

## Key numbers

- Integrated cross section for 470 < pΣ(MeV/c) < 550 and −0.7 ≤ cosθ ≤ 1.0: 22.5 ± 0.68 (stat.) ± 0.65 (syst.) mb
- Integrated cross section for 550 < pΣ(MeV/c) < 650 and −0.7 ≤ cosθ ≤ 1.0: 15.8 ± 0.83 (stat.) ± 0.52 (syst.) mb
- Approximately 1700 Σ⁻p → Λn events detected in the 470–550 MeV/c momentum region and approximately 630 events in the 550–650 MeV/c region
- The Σ⁻ momentum was measured with an accuracy of approximately 5 MeV/c
- The π⁻ beam intensity was 2.0 × 10⁷ per spill, with a total of 1.62 × 10⁷ Σ⁻ particles used for the analysis

## Context

This work extends the systematic Σp scattering measurements of the J-PARC E40 collaboration, which previously reported accurate differential cross sections for Σ⁻p elastic scattering (Miwa, 2021). The results represent a drastic improvement over past bubble chamber measurements (Stephen, 1970), with approximately 50 times more scattering events. The data provide essential constraints for baryon-baryon interaction models including the Nijmegen ESC08c (Rijken, 2010) and ESC16 (Nagels, 2019) boson-exchange models, the fss2 quark cluster model (Fujiwara, 2007), and χEFT models NLO13 (Haidenbauer, 2013) and NLO19 (Haidenbauer, 2020). The measurements will help constrain the ΛN-ΣN coupling strength, which is critical for understanding hypernuclear structure and the equation of state of neutron stars.
