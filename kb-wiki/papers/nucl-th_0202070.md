# The $ρNN$ coupling with direct coupling and loops

> Starting from a gauge formalism of $ρ$ mesons, pions and baryons we evaluate the $ρ$ coupling to the nucleon, including the direct coupling provided by the Lagrangians, plus contributions from loops with the virtual pion cloud. We find a contribution to the magnetic $ρ$ coupling to the nucleon from pionic loops of the same size as the direct coupling, which is, however, still small compared to the empirical values. This finding goes in line with chiral formulations of the strong interaction of mesons at low energies where, unlike the scalar mesons which are mostly built of a pion (kaon) cloud, the $ρ$ meson stands as a genuine QCD state with intrinsic properties not tied to those of the pion cloud.

## Key claim

Starting from a gauge formalism of ρ mesons, pions and baryons, the authors evaluate the ρ coupling to the nucleon including both the direct coupling from the Lagrangians and contributions from loops with the virtual pion cloud; they find that the pionic loop contribution to the magnetic ρ coupling is of the same size as the direct coupling, but both together remain small compared to empirical values.

## Method

The authors use a gauge formalism for ρ mesons, pions and baryons to compute the ρNN coupling. They evaluate the direct coupling provided by the Lagrangians and add contributions from one-loop diagrams involving the virtual pion cloud. The calculation is performed within a chiral framework for meson-baryon interactions at low energies.

## Key numbers

- The pionic loop contribution to the magnetic ρ coupling to the nucleon is of the same size as the direct coupling.
- The combined direct-plus-loop contribution is still small compared to the empirical values (no explicit numerical values for the empirical comparison are given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends chiral formulations of the strong interaction of mesons at low energies, where scalar mesons are mostly built from a pion (kaon) cloud. The finding that the ρ meson stands as a genuine QCD state with intrinsic properties not tied to those of the pion cloud contrasts with the behavior of scalar mesons. The result enables further study of the nature of vector mesons in low-energy QCD and their coupling to nucleons.
