# Deuteron-like states composed of two doubly charmed baryons

> We present a systematic investigation of the possible molecular states composed of a pair of doubly charmed baryons ($Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}$) or one doubly charmed baryon and one doubly charmed antibaryon $(Ξ_{cc}\barΞ_{cc})$ within the framework of the one-boson-exchange-potential model. For the spin-triplet systems, we take into account the mixing between the ${}^3S_1$ and ${}^3D_1$ channels. For the baryon-baryon system $Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}$ with $(R,I) = (\bar{3}, 1/2)$ and $(\bar{3}, 0)$, where $R$ and $I$ represent the group representation and the isospin of the system, respectively, there exist loosely bound molecular states. For the baryon-antibaryon system $Ξ_{cc}\barΞ_{cc}$ with $(R,I) = (8, 1)$, $(8, 1/2)$ and $(8,0)$, there also exist deuteron-like molecules. The $B_{cc}\bar{B}_{cc}$ molecular states may be produced at LHC. The proximity of their masses to the threshold of two doubly charmed baryons provides a clean clue to identify them.

## Key claim

The paper claims that loosely bound, deuteron-like molecular states can exist for both the baryon-baryon system $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}$ with $(R,I) = (\bar{3}, 1/2)$ and $(\bar{3}, 0)$ and the baryon-antibaryon system $\Xi_{cc}\bar{\Xi}_{cc}$ with $(R,I) = (8, 1)$, $(8, 1/2)$ and $(8,0)$, but only for a reasonable cutoff parameter around 1.2 GeV for the baryon-baryon case and 1.5-2.0 GeV for the baryon-antibaryon case; the existence of these states depends on the chosen cutoff and the inclusion or exclusion of the contact delta interaction.

## Method

The authors employed the one-boson-exchange-potential (OBEP) model, including pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $\eta$, $K$), scalar ($\sigma$), and vector ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$, $K^*$) meson exchanges, with coupling constants derived from the quark model using known nucleon-meson couplings. They solved the Schrodinger equation for the $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}$ and $\Xi_{cc}\bar{\Xi}_{cc}$ systems, considering both spin-singlet (${}^1S_0$) and spin-triplet (${}^3S_1$ - ${}^3D_1$ mixing) channels, and introduced a monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ varied between 0.8 and 2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the $B_{cc}B_{cc}$ system with $(R,I) = (\bar{3}, 1/2)$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV (with contact term): binding energy = 0.56 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 3.45 fm, $S$-wave probability = 99.98%.
- For the $B_{cc}B_{cc}$ system with $(R,I) = (\bar{3}, 0)$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.1$ GeV (with contact term): binding energy = 0.68 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 3.23 fm, $S$-wave probability = 99.74%.
- For the $B_{cc}\bar{B}_{cc}$ system with $(R,I) = (8, 1/2)$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV (with contact term): binding energy = 0.26 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 4.76 fm.
- For the $B_{cc}\bar{B}_{cc}$ system with $(R,I) = (8, 1)$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV (with contact term): binding energy = 0.13 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 6.44 fm.
- For the $B_{cc}\bar{B}_{cc}$ system with $(R,I) = (8, 0)$ and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV (with contact term): binding energy = 0.18 MeV, root-mean-square radius = 5.66 fm.
- The mass of the doubly charmed baryon $\Xi_{cc}$ used in the calculation is 3520 MeV, taken from the SELEX Collaboration (Mattson 2002).

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic molecular states from systems of heavy mesons (e.g., Tornqvist 1993, Liu 2007-2009) and heavy baryons (e.g., Lee 2011, Li 2012) to the specific case of two doubly charmed baryons, motivated by recent observations of four-heavy-quark events at LHCb and CMS. The results suggest that $\Xi_{cc}\bar{\Xi}_{cc}$ molecular states could be searched for at the LHC, where their masses lying close to the threshold of two doubly charmed baryons would provide a clean experimental signature.
