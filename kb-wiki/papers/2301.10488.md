# Emergence of a Bose polaron in a small ring threaded by the Aharonov-Bohm flux

> The model of a ring threaded by the Aharonov-Bohm flux underlies our understanding of a coupling between gauge potentials and matter. The typical formulation of the model is based upon a single particle picture, and should be extended when interactions with other particles become relevant. Here, we illustrate such an extension for a particle in an Aharonov-Bohm ring subject to interactions with a weakly interacting Bose gas. We show that the ground state of the system can be described using the Bose polaron concept -- a particle dressed by interactions with a bosonic environment. We connect the energy spectrum to the effective mass of the polaron, and demonstrate how to change currents in the system by tuning boson-particle interactions. Our results suggest the Aharonov-Bohm ring as a platform for studying coherence and few- to many-body crossover of quasi-particles that arise from an impurity immersed in a medium.

## Key claim

The ground state of an impurity in an Aharonov-Bohm ring interacting with a weakly interacting Bose gas can be described using the Bose polaron concept, but this description is accurate only for small effective total momenta (|𝒫| ≲ 2π for weak interactions and |𝒫| ≲ π for strong interactions), beyond which the coherent propagation of the impurity breaks down.

## Method

The authors study a one-dimensional system of N bosons and a single impurity atom on a ring of length L, where only the impurity is coupled to the Aharonov-Bohm flux Φ. They transform to the co-moving frame using a coordinate-space analogue of the Lee-Low-Pines transformation, which eliminates the impurity coordinate and makes the total momentum P a good quantum number. The system is analyzed using two complementary methods: a mean-field approach (MFA) assuming all bosons occupy the same single-particle state in the co-moving frame, and the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) as an ab initio benchmark.

## Key numbers

- Boson-boson interaction strength: γ = gL/N = 0.2 (weakly interacting Bose gas)
- Number of bosons: N = 19
- Impurity-boson interaction ratios studied: c/g = 1 and c/g = 5
- The total momentum P is quantized as P = 2πn, where n is an integer
- The energy spectrum is periodic with period Φ/(2π)

## Context

This work extends the single-particle Aharonov-Bohm model (Aharonov 1959) to include interactions with a bosonic environment, connecting to the extensive literature on one-dimensional Bose polarons (Casteels 2012, Petkovic 2016, Grusdt 2017, Volosniev 2017). The results complement previous few-body studies of AB rings with non-identical particles (Romer 2000, Kyriakou 2010) and provide a platform for studying the few- to many-body crossover in polaron problems beyond ground-state energy measurements (wenz 2013). The work also suggests the AB ring as a testbed for investigating coherence properties of Bose polarons and for studying few-body analogues of the Landau critical velocity.
