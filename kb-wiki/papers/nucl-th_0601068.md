# Neutrino-nucleus reactions in the energy range 1-100 MeV

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach (SNPA) can provide reliable neutrino-nucleus cross sections at the 1% level for solar neutrino energies (Eν ≤ 20 MeV) and at the 5% level up to the pion-production threshold, but only for the deuteron (A=2) system; for heavier nuclei, approximations such as the shell model, RPA, or empirical effective operator methods are necessary, and their reliability must be gauged against experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews two theoretical frameworks for calculating low-energy neutrino-nucleus cross sections: the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach (SNPA), which uses phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials and exchange currents, and Effective Field Theory (EFT) based on chiral perturbation theory, including a hybrid EFT approach that uses SNPA wave functions with EFT-derived transition operators. For light nuclei, the authors examine ν-d reactions as the most tractable case, then discuss ν-¹²C and ν-¹⁶O reactions using shell-model and RPA treatments, and finally comment on medium-heavy and heavy nuclei where RPA or empirical effective operator methods are employed.

## Key numbers

- SNPA calculations for ν-d reactions are reliable at the **1% level** in the solar neutrino energy range (Eν ≤ 20 MeV)
- SNPA calculations are reliable at the **5% level** up to the pion-production threshold energy (Eν ≤ 130 MeV)
- The LSND experiment measured the inclusive cross section for ¹²C(νμ,μ)¹²N* for **123.7 MeV < Eν < 280 MeV**, finding a value more than a factor of 2 lower than Fermi-gas model predictions
- Haxton's calculation for ν-¹⁶O reactions used **2ħω** shell-model wave functions
- Kuramoto et al.'s relativistic Fermi gas model results and Haxton's shell-model results can be smoothly extrapolated into each other at around **Eν = 60 MeV**
- Average electron-neutrino energy in stellar collapse ranges typically from **10 to 50 MeV**

## Context

This invited talk at NuInt05 (2005) reviews the status of low-energy neutrino-nucleus reaction calculations, extending the work of Nakamura et al. (2002) on ν-d reactions and addressing the long-standing "anomaly" in the ¹²C(νμ,μ)¹²N* reaction first reported by the LSND collaboration (Auerbach et al., 2002). The paper discusses how recent calculations by Krmpotić et al. (2002), Oset and collaborators (Nieves et al., 2005), and Hayes et al. (2003) using no-core shell model with three-body interactions have resolved this anomaly. The work enables improved cross section estimates for supernova neutrino detection and nucleosynthesis studies, and points toward future experimental projects at the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory that aim to measure neutrino-nucleus cross sections with unprecedented precision.
