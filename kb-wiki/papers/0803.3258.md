# Strong dineutron correlation in 8He and 18C

> We study the spatial structure of four valence neutrons in the ground state of $^8$He and $^{18}$C nuclei using a core+4$n$ model. For this purpose, we employ a density-dependent contact interaction among the valence neutrons, and solve the five-body Hamiltonian in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation. We show that two neutrons with the coupled spin of $S$=0 exhibit a strong dineutron correlation around the surface of these nuclei, whereas the correlation between the two dineutrons is much weaker. Our calculation indicates that the probability of the (1p$_{3/2})^4$ and [(1p$_{3/2})^2$ (p$_{1/2})^2$] configurations in the ground state wave function of $^8$He nucleus is 34.9% and 23.7%, respectively. This is consistent with the recent experimental finding with the $^8$He($p,t)^6$He reaction, that is, the ground state wave function of $^8$He deviates significantly from the pure (1p$_{3/2})^4$ structure.

# Strong dineutron correlation in 8He and 18C

## Key claim

The authors claim that a strong dineutron correlation (two neutrons with coupled spin S=0) exists around the surface of 8He and 18C, but the correlation between the two separate dineutrons is much weaker. This conclusion is drawn from a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation to a core+4n model, which the authors acknowledge is an approximate method that they validate against exact three-body calculations for 6He.

## Method

The authors employed a core+4n model with a density-dependent contact interaction among valence neutrons, solving the five-body Hamiltonian using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation in coordinate space. They expanded HFB wave functions on eigenfunctions of the mean-field Hamiltonian, respecting the Pauli principle by excluding states occupied by core neutrons. Two- and four-particle densities were computed using Wick's theorem and the canonical basis representation of the HFB state, with particle number projection applied for the four-particle density.

## Key numbers

- For 8He: probability of the (1p3/2)^4 configuration in the ground state wave function is 34.9%; probability of the [(1p3/2)^2 (p1/2)^2] configuration is 23.7%
- For 8He: ground state energy relative to core+4n threshold is -3.112 MeV; Fermi energy is -0.0715 MeV; root-mean-square radius is 3.23 fm (experimental: 2.49 ± 0.04 fm)
- For 18C: ground state energy relative to core+4n threshold is -10.514 MeV (experimental -10.385 MeV); Fermi energy is -2.522 MeV; root-mean-square radius is 2.92 fm (experimental: 2.90 ± 0.19 fm)
- For 18C: probability of the (1d5/2)^4 configuration is 32.2%; probability of the [(1d5/2)^2 (2s1/2)^2] configuration is 26.2%
- Pairing interaction strength: V0 = -502 MeV fm^3 for 8He; V0 = -538 MeV fm^3 for 18C (with ρ0 = 0.32 fm^-3)
- For 6He test case: exact (p3/2)^2 occupation probability is 83.0%; HFB gives 57.4%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of dineutron correlation in the two-neutron halo nucleus 11Li (Hagino, Sagawa, and collaborators, 2007) to nuclei with four valence neutrons. It contrasts with an earlier study by Zhukov et al. (1994) that assumed pure (1p3/2)^4 occupation in a harmonic oscillator potential for 8He, which found no dineutron correlation. The results are consistent with experimental findings from the 8He(p,t)6He reaction (KSL07) showing deviation from a pure (1p3/2)^4 structure. The work enables future analysis of that transfer reaction data using the correlated wave functions obtained here, and provides a bridge between studies of two-neutron halo nuclei and heavier neutron-skin nuclei.
