# Hydrodynamic predictions for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions

> We argue that relativistic hydrodynamics is able to make robust predictions for soft particle production in Xe+Xe collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The change of system size from Pb+Pb to Xe+Xe provides a unique opportunity to test the scaling laws inherent to fluid dynamics. Using event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations, we make quantitative predictions for several observables: mean transverse momentum, anisotropic flow coefficients, and their fluctuations. Results are shown as function of collision centrality.

# Hydrodynamic predictions for 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions

## Key claim

Relativistic hydrodynamics can make robust predictions for soft particle production in Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC, even without precise knowledge of the initial conditions or transport coefficients, because uncertainties in the modeling (e.g., overestimating both eccentricity and fluctuations) are expected to affect Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe systems by the same fraction, with only a small 17% increase in viscous damping from the smaller system size.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the TRENTo initial condition model with p=0 and the V-USPHYDRO code, evolving approximately 2200 events per centrality bin for both Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV. The hydrodynamic evolution started at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c with shear viscosity over entropy ratio eta/s = 0.047 and zero bulk viscosity, using a lattice QCD equation of state with three quark flavors, and the fluid was converted to hadrons below 150 MeV with strong decays of all hadronic resonances.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity over entropy ratio used in the simulations is eta/s = 0.047.
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c after the time of collision.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for the Xe nucleus are: A=129, R=5.42 fm, a=0.57 fm, beta_2=0.162, beta_4=-0.003.
- The centrality of the knee in the V0M amplitude distribution is predicted to be c_knee = 0.51 +/- 0.04% for Xe+Xe collisions, compared to 0.39 +/- 0.02% for Pb+Pb collisions from the TRENTo model.
- The ratio of initial triangularities between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb is close to 1.27 for most centralities, consistent with A^{-1/2} scaling.
- The ratio of initial eccentricities in central collisions is increased by nearly 20% due to the prolate deformation of the Xe nucleus.
- The mean transverse momentum in Xe+Xe differs from Pb+Pb by at most 2% in mid-central collisions.
- Elliptic flow is larger by 25% in Xe+Xe than in Pb+Pb in the 0-5% centrality window (15% from system size change, 10% from nuclear deformation), but smaller by 10% above 30% centrality.
- The maximum value of v_2{4}/v_2{2} is around 0.8 in Xe+Xe collisions, while it reaches 0.9 in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The ratio v_3{4}/v_3{2} between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb is close to 1.13, consistent with A^{-1/4} scaling.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (Heinz 2013, Bernhard 2016, Niemi 2015) to the newly available Xe+Xe system, providing a direct test of scaling laws inherent to fluid dynamics. The change in system size from 208Pb to 129Xe allows testing predictions about the A^{-1/2} scaling of fluctuations (Bhalerao 2011) and the A^{-1/3} scaling of viscous corrections (Baier 2007), while the prolate deformation of the Xe nucleus (Moller 2015) introduces additional effects on eccentricity. The results enable experimental verification of whether hydrodynamics correctly captures the transition from fluctuation-dominated flow in central collisions to geometry-dominated flow in mid-central collisions, and can help constrain the initial conditions and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
