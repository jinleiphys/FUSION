# Three-hadron angular correlations from pQCD at RHIC and LHC

> We study three-hadron azimuthal angular correlations in high energy proton-proton and central nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC at mid-rapidity. We use the LO parton matrix elements for 2 \rightarrow 3 processes and include the effect of parton energy loss in the Quark-Gluon Plasma using the modified fragmentation function approach. For the case when the produced hadrons have either same or not too different momenta, we observe two away side peaks at 2pi/3 and 4pi/3. We consider the dependence of the angular correlations on energy loss parameters that have been used in studies of single inclusive hadron production at RHIC. Our results on the angular dependence of the cross section agree well with preliminary data by the PHENIX collaboration. We comment on the possible contribution of 2 \rightarrow 3 processes to di-hadron angular correlations and how a comparison of the two processes may help characterize the plasma further.

## Key claim

The authors show that leading-order 2→3 partonic processes, combined with a modified fragmentation function approach for parton energy loss, can reproduce the two away-side peaks at 2π/3 and 4π/3 observed in three-hadron azimuthal correlations, but only for the case where the produced hadrons have either the same or not too different momenta.

## Method

The authors compute the three-hadron production cross section at mid-rapidity using leading-order 2→3 parton matrix elements with CTEQ6 parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, they incorporate parton energy loss in the Quark-Gluon Plasma using modified fragmentation functions that depend on the gluon density profile from a Woods-Saxon nuclear density, with the medium evolving via longitudinal expansion only. They apply angular cuts to avoid collinear and soft singularities in the matrix elements and integrate over the transverse plane location and emission angle of the hard scattering to obtain the per-nucleon yield.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC at √s_NN = 200 GeV with symmetric hadron momenta 2 GeV ≤ h₁, h₂, h₃ ≤ 3 GeV, the two away-side peaks appear at 2π/3 and 4π/3 rad.
- For LHC at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with symmetric hadron momenta 2 GeV ≤ h₁, h₂, h₃ ≤ 3 GeV, the same peak positions at 2π/3 and 4π/3 rad are observed.
- The energy loss parameter ε₀ is varied from 0.1 to 2 GeV/fm for RHIC and from 2 to 4 GeV/fm for LHC calculations.
- The average number of scatterings for ε₀ = 1.5 GeV/fm has a maximum of about 2.5.
- The calculation with ε₀ = 2 GeV/fm best describes preliminary PHENIX data for leading hadron momentum 2.5 GeV ≤ h₁ ≤ 4 GeV and away-side hadron momenta 1 GeV ≤ h₂, h₃ ≤ 2.5 GeV, integrated over Δθ = θ₂ᴴ in the range 1.65 ≤ Δθ ≤ 2.2 rad.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ayala et al., 2010) that connected two-particle correlations to three-hadron production by considering different path lengths of away-side partons in 2→3 versus 2→2 processes. The results agree with preliminary PHENIX data (Ajitanand et al.) and suggest that comparing 2→3 contributions to di-hadron correlations from genuine 2→2 processes may provide additional information for tomographic studies of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, particularly regarding the transverse plane location of hard scatterings.
