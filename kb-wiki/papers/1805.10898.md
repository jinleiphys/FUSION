# Exploring the generalized loosely bound Skyrme model

> The Skyrme model is extended with a sextic derivative term - called the BPS-Skyrme term - and a repulsive potential term - called the loosely bound potential. A large part of the model's parameter space is studied for the 4-Skyrmion which corresponds to the Helium-4 nucleus and emphasis is put on preserving as much of the platonic symmetries as possible whilst reducing the binding energies. We reach classical binding energies for Helium-4 as low as 0.2%, while retaining the cubic symmetry of the 4-Skyrmion, and after taking into account the quantum mass correction to the nucleon due to spin/isospin quantization, we get total binding energies as low as 3.6% - still with the cubic symmetry intact.

# Exploring the generalized loosely bound Skyrme model

## Key claim

Classical binding energies for Helium-4 as low as 0.2% can be reached while retaining cubic symmetry of the 4-Skyrmion, but after including the quantum mass correction to the nucleon due to spin/isospin quantization, the total binding energy rises to 3.6%, still with cubic symmetry intact, which is about a factor of 4.5 too large compared to experimental values, suggesting that further quantum corrections from massive (vibrational) modes cannot be neglected.

## Method

The generalized Skyrme model was extended with a sextic BPS-Skyrme term and a repulsive loosely bound potential (the pion mass term squared). Full numerical PDE calculations on cubic lattices of size 121³ with a spatial lattice constant of about 0.08 were performed for the 4-Skyrmion, using nonlinear conjugate gradients with a Newton-Raphson and quadratic line search switching algorithm. The parameter space was scanned over c₆ ∈ [0,1] and m₂ ∈ [0,6] in a square grid with steps of 0.1, yielding 671 numerical solutions, with the rational map Ansatz as initial condition at (m₂,c₆)=(0,0).

## Key numbers

- Classical relative binding energy (CRBE) of the 4-Skyrmion as low as 0.2%, about a factor of four smaller than the experimental value for Helium-4.
- Total (quantum-corrected) relative binding energy (QRBE) as low as 3.6%, about a factor of 4.5 too large compared to experiment.
- Parameter space scanned: c₆ ∈ [0,1] and m₂ ∈ [0,6]; note that m₂² enters the Lagrangian, so 6² = 36 is much larger than other coefficients.
- Numerical precision: baryon charge captured to within 0.15%; local equation of motion precision better than 1.7×10⁻⁶.
- At (m₂,c₆)=(0,0) compared to (m₂,c₆)=(6,1), the energy increases by a factor of 5.42 for the 1-Skyrmion and 5.95 for the 4-Skyrmion.
- Pion mass parameter fixed at m₁ = 1/4 throughout.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model combining the BPS-Skyrme-type models and the loosely bound model first studied in Gudnason (2016), which used the rational map Ansatz for the 4-Skyrmion only in the regime where c₆ and m₂ were both small (≤ 1). The present full PDE calculations explore much larger m₂ values (up to 6) and c₆ up to 1, enabling detection of symmetry changes that the earlier restricted study could not capture. The results enable future inclusion of quantum corrections due to massive (vibrational) modes, following the approach of Barnes (1997) and Halcrow (2015, 2016), which will be necessary to bring the binding energies closer to experimental values while preserving the cubic symmetry essential for phenomenological successes such as the Hoyle state rotational band and the Oxygen-16 spectrum.
