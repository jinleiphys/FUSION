# Electromagnetic form factors of ${}^6$Li, ${}^7$Li, and ${}^7$Be in cluster effective field theory

> Effective field theory (EFT) provides a powerful model-independent theoretical framework for illuminating complicated interactions across a wide range of physics areas and subfields. In this work, we consider the low-energy deuteron-Helium-4, triton-Helium-4, and helion-Helium-4 systems at low energies in cluster EFT. In particular, we focus on the deuteron + Helium-4 cluster configuration of the Lithium-6 nucleus, the triton + Helium-4 cluster configuration of the Lithium-7 nucleus, and the Helium-3-Helium-4 configuration of the Beryllium-7 nucleus, respectively. We illustrate how to directly extract the asymptotic normalization coefficient and several observables using experimental measurement of the electromagnetic form factors of these nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that cluster effective field theory (cluster EFT) can be extended to describe the electromagnetic form factors of ${}^6$Li, ${}^7$Li, and ${}^7$Be, but this description is valid only for low-momentum probes that cannot resolve the internal structure of the constituent clusters, with a breakdown scale set by the three-body breakup energy.

## Method

The author constructs a cluster EFT treating the nuclei as two-body systems: deuteron + ${}^4$He for ${}^6$Li, triton + ${}^4$He for ${}^7$Li, and ${}^3$He + ${}^4$He for ${}^7$Be. The Coulomb interaction between charged clusters is treated non-perturbatively using Coulomb Green's functions and Coulomb-modified effective range expansion. The electromagnetic form factors are calculated from Feynman diagrams where photons couple to the constituent clusters and to auxiliary fields, with the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) extracted from the bound-state wavefunction renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ${}^6$Li is bound by $B = 1.47$ MeV with respect to the $d + \alpha$ threshold, corresponding to a bound-state momentum of $60.6676$ MeV.
- The three-body breakup of ${}^6$Li into $\alpha + n + p$ requires $E_\text{3B} = 3.7$ MeV, setting the breakdown scale $\Lambda = \sqrt{2m_R E_{3B}} \approx 96$ MeV or $0.5$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The experimental charge radius of the deuteron is $\langle r_C\rangle_d = 2.12799(74)$ fm and of ${}^4$He is $\langle r_C\rangle_\alpha = 1.67824(83)$ fm.
- The calculated charge radius of ${}^6$Li is $2.589(39)$ fm, yielding an intrinsic charge radius $\sqrt{\langle r_C^2\rangle_\text{int}} = 3.86(12)$ fm.
- The numerical integral $\xi_0 \approx 3.99131$ fm$^3$ appears in the charge radius calculation.

## Context

This work extends cluster EFT (also known as Halo EFT) from halo nuclei and $\alpha$-$\alpha$ scattering to non-halo systems with a separation of scales, specifically the $\alpha$-$d$, $\alpha$-triton, and $\alpha$-${}^3$He systems. It builds on prior work by Rupak (2018) on cluster EFT for three-nucleon systems and by Hammer (2017, 2019) on Halo EFT reviews. The approach enables model-independent extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients and electromagnetic observables for light nuclei, complementing ab initio methods and experimental measurements.
