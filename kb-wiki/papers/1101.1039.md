# The extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials associated with a special LMG model

> New polynomials associated with a special Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model corresponding to the standard two-site Bose-Hubbard model are derived based on the Stieltjes correspondence. It is shown that there is a one-to-one correspondence between zeros of this new polynomial and solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations for the LMG model.A one-dimensional classical electrostatic analogue corresponding to the special LMG model is established according to Stieltjes early work. It shows that any possible configuration of equilibrium positions of the charges in the electrostatic problem corresponds uniquely to one set of roots of the Bethe ansatz equations for the LMG model, and the number of possible configurations of equilibrium positions of the charges equals exactly to the number of energy levels in the LMG model. Some relations of sums of powers and inverse powers of zeros of the new polynomials related to the eigenenergies of the LMG model are derived.

# The extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials associated with a special LMG model

## Key claim

The paper derives new polynomials (extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials) whose zeros correspond one-to-one with solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations for a special Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model equivalent to the standard two-site Bose-Hubbard model, but only for the case where the parameters are transformed to ensure positivity of the coefficients α, β, and γ in the Bethe ansatz equations.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe ansatz equations (BAEs) for the special LMG model, which take the form of coupled nonlinear equations for spectral parameters. They map these BAEs onto a second-order Fuchsian differential equation (Eq. 18) by identifying the BAEs as the condition for the zeros of a polynomial solution. The polynomial coefficients are determined by solving a four-term recurrence relation (Eq. 21), which is cast as an eigenvalue problem for a tridiagonal-like matrix F (Eq. 23). The eigenvalues f^(ζ) of this matrix correspond to different eigenstates, and the eigenvectors yield the polynomial coefficients. A classical electrostatic analogue is also constructed with fixed charges at ±1 and a uniform electric field.

## Key numbers

- For the example with k=2, α=β=γ=1/2 (corresponding to ν₁=ν₂=0 and t/U=1/2), three polynomial solutions are obtained with zeros: (−7.2904, −1.7379), (−5.7052, 0.5612), and (−0.6036, 0.7759)
- The corresponding sums of zeros (x₁+x₂) are −9.0283, −5.1440, and 0.1723 respectively
- The eigenvalues f for these three solutions are 0.5141, −1.4280, and −4.0861 respectively
- The total number of possible configurations of equilibrium positions of charges equals k+1 for m=2, given by the binomial coefficient (k+m-1 choose k) = k+1

## Context

This work extends the Stieltjes correspondence, previously applied to Richardson's BCS pairing model for nuclei by Gaudin and clarified by subsequent authors (around 1976-2000), to the special LMG model equivalent to the two-site Bose-Hubbard model studied by various authors (2001-2009). The polynomial approach provides an alternative to solving the nonlinear Bethe ansatz equations directly, which becomes difficult for large systems. The method is similar to that used for quasi-exactly solvable sextic anharmonic oscillator problems and PT-symmetric quantum mechanics (studied around 2000-2005), though the resulting polynomials are of a different type. The work enables easier determination of eigenenergies and eigenstates for this class of models and suggests applicability to other quantum many-body systems such as atomic-molecular Bose-Einstein condensates and nuclear pairing problems.
