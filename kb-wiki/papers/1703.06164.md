# Dilepton radiation and bulk viscosity in heavy-ion collisions

> Starting from IP-Glasma initial conditions, we investigate the effects of bulk pressure on thermal dilepton production at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. Though results of the thermal dilepton $v_2$ under the influence of both bulk and shear viscosity is presented for top RHIC energy, more emphasis is put on LHC energy where such a calculation is computed for the first time. The effects of the bulk pressure on thermal dilepton $v_2$ at the LHC are explored through bulk-induced modifications on the dilepton yield.

# Dilepton radiation and bulk viscosity in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The study shows that thermal dilepton elliptic flow ($v_2$) is sensitive to bulk viscosity effects at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the results are exploratory because dilepton emission from the post-hydrodynamical hadronic transport phase is not yet included. The bulk-induced modification to $v_2$ at the LHC is driven by increased hadronic medium yield, while the behavior at RHIC requires further investigation.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations with IP-Glasma initial conditions, evolving the fluid using energy-momentum conservation and relaxation equations for bulk pressure and shear tensor. Thermal dilepton rates were computed from both partonic (quark-antiquark annihilation with viscous corrections) and hadronic (vector meson in-medium decay via Vector meson Dominance Model) sources, with shear viscous corrections from the 14-moment approximation and bulk viscous corrections from the relaxation time approximation. The hydrodynamic evolution was run until a switching temperature ($T_{sw}$), after which UrQMD simulations described hadronic dynamics, though dilepton production was only evaluated above $T_{sw}$ and not yet from the hadronic transport phase.

## Key numbers
- At LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV), the medium with both bulk and shear viscosity used $\eta/s=0.095$ to fit hadronic observables, while the shear-only baseline used $\eta/s=0.16$.
- At top RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV), $\eta/s$ was reduced to 0.06 to reproduce hadronic observables.
- The switching temperature was $T_{sw}=165$ MeV at RHIC and $T_{sw}=145$ MeV at LHC energy.
- The bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio $\zeta/s$ has a sharp peak at $T\sim 180$ MeV.
- The linear interpolation between hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom was performed within the interval $184<T<220$ MeV.
- Thermal dilepton $v_2$ shows bulk-induced modifications for invariant mass $M>0.85$ GeV at the LHC, where the yield transitions from hadronic-medium-dominated to having significant QGP contribution.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Vujanovic et al. (2016, 2015, 2014) on thermal dilepton sensitivity to transport coefficients, and builds on hydrodynamic simulations by Ryu et al. (2015) and Paquet et al. (2015) that established the temperature-dependent bulk viscosity parametrization. The calculations use the equation of state from Huovinen (2009) and lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014) for the speed of sound. The results enable future comparisons with experimental dilepton data at RHIC and LHC, provided that dilepton emission from the hadronic transport phase is included in upcoming calculations.
