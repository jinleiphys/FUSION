# Enhanced effect of temporal variation of the fine structure constant and strong interaction in 229Th

> The relative effects of variation of the fine structure constant alpha=e^2/hc and dimensionless strong interaction parameter m_q/Lambda_{QCD} are enhanced 5-6 orders of magnitude in very narrow ultraviolet transition between the ground and first excited state in 229Th nucleus (energy (3.5 +/- 1) eV). Corresponding experiment has potential of improving sensitivity to the variation of the fundamental constants by 7-10 orders of magnitude (up to 10^{-23} per year).

# Enhanced effect of temporal variation of the fine structure constant and strong interaction in 229Th

## Key claim

The relative effects of variation of the fine structure constant α and the dimensionless strong interaction parameter m_q/Λ_QCD are enhanced by 5–6 orders of magnitude in the very narrow ultraviolet transition between the ground and first excited state in 229Th, but this enhancement depends on the cancellation of large terms in the nuclear potential and the specific deformation mechanism linking β₂ to α.

## Method

The author uses the deformed nuclear shell model to describe the ground state (5/2⁺[633]) and first excited state (3/2⁺[631]) of 229Th, with their energy difference given by ω = 2C − 8D where C is the spin-orbit interaction constant and D is the orbit-axis interaction constant. The relative variation δω/ω is expressed in terms of δD/D and δC/C, with the large factor 2C/ω ≈ 8D/ω ≈ −0.4 × 10⁶ arising because ω is 5 orders of magnitude smaller than C and D. The dependence of D on α is derived by assuming nuclear deformation β₂ ∝ α, and the dependence of the strong potential depth V₀ on quark masses is estimated using the Walecka model, where V₀ is a highly tuned small difference of two large meson-exchange terms.

## Key numbers

- Transition energy: (3.5 ± 1) eV between ground and first excited state in 229Th
- Estimated width of the excited state: about 10⁻⁴ Hz
- Enhancement factor from ratio 2C/ω ≈ 8D/ω ≈ −0.4 × 10⁶ for ω = 3.5 eV
- Relative variation of transition frequency: δω/ω ≈ 10⁵ (4 δα/α + δX_q/X_q − 10 δX_s/X_s) × (3.5 eV / ω), where X_q = m_q/Λ_QCD and X_s = m_s/Λ_QCD
- Potential improvement in sensitivity to variation of fundamental constants: 7–10 orders of magnitude, up to 10⁻²³ per year

## Context

This work extends the concept of enhanced sensitivity to α variation previously identified in nearly degenerate levels of dysprosium (Dzuba 1999) and in narrow microwave molecular transitions, but applies it to a nuclear transition with a width several orders of magnitude smaller than typical atomic clock transitions (∼Hz). The 229Th nuclear clock transition was proposed earlier as a high-accuracy optical clock reference (Th4), and this paper adds the crucial advantage of enhanced sensitivity to fundamental constant variation. The result enables laboratory searches for variation of α, m_q/Λ_QCD, and m_s/Λ_QCD with sensitivity potentially 7–10 orders of magnitude beyond current atomic clock limits, which are approaching 10⁻¹⁵ per year.
