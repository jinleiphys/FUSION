# Toward Equilibration in the Early Stages After a High Energy Heavy Ion Collision

> The early stages in the evolution of the gluons produced in the central region of a head-on high-energy heavy ion collision is studied. An equation is given for the rate of change of transverse momentum into longitudinal momentum where the longitudinal direction is along the collision axis. We are able to follow the system up to the time where equilibration seems to be setting in, but we are unable to actually follow the system as it reaches equilibrium.

# Toward Equilibration in the Early Stages After a High Energy Heavy Ion Collision

## Key claim

The gluon system produced in the central region of a head-on high-energy heavy ion collision can be followed analytically up to the time when equilibration appears to begin, but the formalism breaks down at the point where equilibrating interactions (scatterings that change longitudinal momentum into transverse momentum and interactions between gluons of different rapidities) start to become important, so the paper cannot actually demonstrate that equilibrium is reached.

## Method

The author derives an analytic equation (Eq. 49) for the rate of change of the squared transverse momentum of gluons at zero rapidity, based on small-angle gluon-gluon elastic scattering in the McLerran-Venugopalan model of saturated gluon distributions. The minimum scattering angle is determined by requiring that the soft gluon field from hard gluons be limited to magnitude 1/g, giving an infrared cutoff that evolves with time. The equation is then extended to gluons of arbitrary rapidity by boost invariance, and the time at which the system's expansion rate matches that of an equilibrated ideal gas is identified as the onset of equilibration.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum: \( Q_s \approx 1 \) GeV for RHIC energies, \( Q_s \approx 2-3 \) GeV for LHC energies (from Section 2).
- The time when equilibration begins to set in for zero rapidity gluons: \( t_1 \sim \frac{1}{Q_s} e^{\sqrt{2\pi / (c \alpha N_c)}} \) (Eq. 53), where \( c \) is an order-one constant.
- The squared transverse momentum at the onset of equilibration: \( (\ell_\perp / Q_s)^2 \sim \sqrt{c \alpha N_c / (2\pi)} \) (Eq. 59).
- The equilibration time in terms of the parameter \( \xi_2 \): \( \xi_2 = \sqrt{2\pi / (c \alpha N_c)} + \xi_0 - 3/2 \) (Eq. 58), where \( \xi_0 = \ln(c \alpha N_c / \pi) \).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) for saturated gluon distributions in heavy ion wavefunctions to the time evolution of freed gluons after a collision. It provides an analytic complement to Monte Carlo calculations that have given evidence for equilibration (Kincaid and Zhang, 1998; Krasnitz and Venugopalan, 1998). The paper enables a theoretical estimate of the time and momentum scale at which equilibration begins in the early stages of a heavy ion collision, suggesting that equilibrium can set in while the system is still undergoing one-dimensional expansion, well before three-dimensional expansion begins.
