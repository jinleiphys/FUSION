# Hadronic Reaction Zones in Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> On the basis of the proposed algorithm for calculation of the hadron reaction rates, the space-time structure of the relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is studied. The reaction zones and the reaction frequencies for various types of reactions are calculated for AGS and SPS energies within the microscopic transport model. The relation of the reaction zones to the kinetic and chemical freeze-out processes is discussed. It is shown that the space-time freeze-out layer is most extended in time in the central region, while, especially for higher collision energies, the layer becomes very narrow at the sides. The parametrization of freeze-out hypersurface in the form of specific hyperbola of constant proper time was confirmed. The specific characteristic time moments of the fireball evolution are introduced. It is found that the time of the division of a reaction zone into two separate parts does not depend on the collision energy. Calculations of the hadronic reaction frequency show that the evolution of nucleus-nucleus collision can be divided into two hadronic stages.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the space-time freeze-out layer is most extended in time in the central region and becomes very narrow at the sides for higher collision energies, but this conclusion is conditioned on the specific choice of a global parameter alpha (fraction of total reactions) rather than a local critical reaction density, and the results are obtained within the UrQMD v2.3 microscopic transport model.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v2.3 microscopic transport model to calculate the four-density of hadronic reactions Gamma(t,r,z) in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS and SPS energies. They divided the space-time volume into pixels of size 1 fm in each dimension and sorted pixels by reaction density, defining the reaction zone as the region containing a given fraction alpha of all reactions. The reaction zone projections onto the z-t plane were constructed by integrating over transverse coordinates, and the temporal structure was analyzed through the reaction frequency nu_i(t) for different reaction types (elastic scattering, inelastic reactions, fusion, and decay).

## Key numbers

- For inelastic reactions at alpha=0.99, the critical reaction density Gamma_c ranges from 0.51 x 10^{-3} fm^{-3} (fm/c)^{-1} at AGS energy 10.8A GeV to 0.11 x 10^{-3} fm^{-3} (fm/c)^{-1} at SPS energy 158A GeV
- The division time t_d for the inelastic reaction zone at alpha=0.99 is 17.5 fm/c at AGS 10.8A GeV and 19.5 fm/c at SPS 158A GeV, showing weak dependence on collision energy
- The fireball formation time t_c is 3.30 fm/c at AGS 10.8A GeV and 0.87 fm/c at SPS 158A GeV
- The first maximum of reaction frequency t_m1 occurs at 3.9 fm/c at AGS 10.8A GeV and 0.88 fm/c at SPS 158A GeV
- The hyperbola parameters for the cold fireball space-like boundary at AGS 10.8A GeV are t^0_RZ = -30 fm/c and tau_RZ = 60.5 fm/c; at SPS 158A GeV they are t^0_RZ = -5 fm/c and tau_RZ = 43.5 fm/c
- The average number of pions per nucleon is approximately 0.6-1.6 at AGS energies and 2-6 at SPS energies

## Context

This work extends the concept of reaction zones introduced by Anchishkin (2010) to study the space-time structure of relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results confirm the parametrization of freeze-out hypersurfaces as hyperbolae of constant proper time, similar to findings from studies of constant pion density hypersurfaces by Anchishkin (2012). The weak dependence of fireball division time on collision energy is identified as a universal feature, and the approach enables quantitative comparison between reaction zones and kinetic/chemical freeze-out processes in hybrid models that use the Cooper-Frye prescription.
