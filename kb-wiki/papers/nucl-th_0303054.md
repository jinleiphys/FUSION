# The algebraic solution of RPA equations for the charged current quasielastic neutrino - nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic expressions for four independent RPA corrections to the charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section using an Abelian matrix algebra to solve the Dyson equation, but the numerical results are only expected to reproduce the known reduction of the differential cross section maximum by about 10% and are presented primarily to demonstrate consistency with other approaches rather than to claim new quantitative predictions.

## Method

The nucleus is modeled as a relativistic Fermi gas with effective nucleon mass M* and Fermi momentum k_F, including interactions via contact terms, pion exchange, and rho meson exchange with a Landau-Migdal parameter g'. The polarization tensor is split into a free part and an RPA correction, where the RPA correction is obtained by solving an 8x8 Dyson equation for the propagator using a closed algebra of four 4x4 matrices (e_L, e_T, e_A, e_VA) that satisfy simple multiplication relations. The solution yields separate analytic expressions for longitudinal, transverse, V-A, and axial contributions to the RPA-corrected polarization tensor, which are then combined with the leptonic tensor to compute cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The RPA corrections typically reduce the maximum in the energy transfer differential cross section by a factor of about 10%, as known from prior work (Langancke).
- Numerical calculations assume axial mass M_A = 1.03 GeV, Fermi momentum k_F = 225 MeV for oxygen-16, and effective mass M* = 638 MeV (derived from the self-consistency equation of mean field theory).
- For neutrino energy of 1 GeV, differential cross sections are compared for free nucleon mass (939 MeV) and effective mass 638 MeV, with Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7 (except for comparison plots where g' = 0.6 is used).
- The Marteau model gives a contribution II that is smaller by about 25% at energy transfer of approximately 50 MeV compared to the present model.

## Context

This work extends the RPA treatment of neutrino-nucleus scattering developed by Horowitz (1993) and Marteau (1999) by providing an algebraic solution that separates four independent correction terms, enabling systematic study of parameter uncertainties. The approach is designed to be extended to neutral-current reactions and Delta excitation, and it allows numerical integration over Fermi momenta with a density profile to incorporate local density effects, which is more difficult in the Marteau model that combines nonrelativistic potential approaches with relativistic Lindhard functions. The results enable future analysis of uncertainties in nucleon form factors, coupling constants, and effective mass in quasi-elastic charged-current processes.
