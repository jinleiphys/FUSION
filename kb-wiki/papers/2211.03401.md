# Revealing the Signal of QCD Phase Transition in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We propose a novel method to construct the Landau thermodynamic potential directly from the fluctuations measured in heavy-ion collisions. The potential is capable of revealing the signal of the critical end-point (CEP) and the first order phase transition (FOPT) of QCD in the system even away from the phase transition region. With the available experimental data, we show that the criterion of the FOPT is negative for most of the collision energies which indicates no signal of FOPT. The data at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7$ GeV with 0-5% centrality shows a different behavior and the mean value of the data satisfies the criterion. However, the uncertainty is still too large to make a certain conclusion. The higher order fluctuations are also required for confirming the signal. We emphasize therefore that new measurements with higher precision for the $C_{1,...,6}$ within 0-5% centrality in the vicinity of $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7$ GeV are in demand which may finally reveal the signal of QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the Landau thermodynamic potential, constructed directly from experimentally measured baryon number fluctuations, can reveal signals of the QCD critical end-point (CEP) and first-order phase transition (FOPT) even when the system at freeze-out is far from the phase boundary. The mean value of the data at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7\) GeV with 0-5% centrality satisfies the FOPT criterion, but the uncertainty is too large to make a certain conclusion.

## Method

The Landau thermodynamic potential is expressed as a Taylor expansion in the baryon number density (the order parameter), with coefficients determined by ratios of cumulants \(C_k\) of the net-proton multiplicity distribution measured in heavy-ion collisions. The criterion for a FOPT is that the discriminant \(\Delta\) of the polynomial derived from the potential's first derivative is non-negative, which for a quartic potential reduces to \(\Delta = \frac{1}{4}\omega_3^2 - \frac{2}{3}\omega_2\omega_4 \geq 0\), where \(\omega_k\) are dimensionless Taylor coefficients constructed from cumulant ratios \(R_{ij} \equiv C_i/C_j\). The potential is then constructed from STAR experimental data for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 3\)–200 GeV, and the criterion is evaluated at each energy.

## Key numbers

* At \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7\) GeV with 0-5% centrality, the mean value of the experimental data satisfies the FOPT criterion \(\Delta \geq 0\), but the uncertainty is too large for a conclusive assertion.
* For \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \geq 11.5\) GeV with 0-5% centrality, the potential shows only one minimum (monotonous for \(\tilde{n} > 0\)), indicating a smooth crossover.
* At \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3\) GeV with 0-5% centrality, the signature of FOPT disappears.
* For peripheral 70-80% centrality collisions at all energies, the criterion is negative, indicating no signal of FOPT.
* The discriminant \(\Delta\) is close to zero for \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} > 39\) GeV because the potential is mostly quadratic with \(R_{21}\) dominant.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of QCD phase structure using baryon number fluctuations as probes (Stephanov 1999, Asakawa 2000, Stephanov 2009) and the beam energy scan program at RHIC (Luo 2015, Luo 2017). The method addresses the difficulty that fluctuations measured at the chemical freeze-out line may not directly reflect the phase boundary, as the system evolves away from it. The constructed potential provides a more holistic observable than individual cumulant ratios, and the results call for higher-precision measurements of \(C_1\) through \(C_6\) in 0-5% centrality near \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7\) GeV to confirm or rule out the FOPT signal. The approach is validated using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which shows distinct potential shapes for crossover and FOPT trajectories.
