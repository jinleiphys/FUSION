# Nonperturbative renormalization in light-front dynamics and applications

> We present a general framework to calculate the properties of relativistic compound systems from the knowledge of an elementary Hamiltonian. Our framework provides a well-controlled nonperturbative calculational scheme which can be systematically improved. The state vector of a physical system is calculated in light-front dynamics. From the general properties of this form of dynamics, the state vector can be further decomposed in well-defined Fock components. In order to control the convergence of this expansion, we advocate the use of the covariant formulation of light-front dynamics. In this formulation, the state vector is projected on an arbitrary light-front plane $ω\cd x=0$ defined by a light-like four-vector $ω$. This enables us to control any violation of rotational invariance due to the truncation of the Fock expansion. We then present a general nonperturbative renormalization scheme in order to avoid field-theoretical divergences which may remain uncancelled due to this truncation. This general framework has been applied to a large variety of models. As a starting point, we consider QED for the two-body Fock space truncation and calculate the anomalous magnetic moment of the electron. We show that it coincides, in this approximation, with the well-known Schwinger term. Then we investigate the properties of a purely scalar system in the three-body approximation, where we highlight the role of antiparticle degrees of freedom. As a non-trivial example of our framework, we calculate the structure of a physical fermion in the Yukawa model, for the three-body Fock space truncation (but still without antifermion contributions). We finally show why our approach is also well-suited to describe effective field theories like chiral perturbation theory in the baryonic sector.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general nonperturbative calculational framework for relativistic compound systems using covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) combined with a Fock-sector-dependent renormalization (FSDR) scheme; the key hedge is that the framework is systematically improvable but relies on truncating the Fock expansion to a finite number of components, which introduces violations of rotational invariance and uncancelled divergences that must be controlled through explicit \(\omega\)-dependent counterterms and sector-dependent bare parameters.

## Method

The state vector of a physical system is defined on an arbitrary light-front plane \(\omega \cdot x = 0\) and decomposed into Fock components, with the eigenstate equation derived from the operator \(\hat{P}^2\). The Fock expansion is truncated to a maximal number of particles \(N\), and a Fock-sector-dependent renormalization scheme is applied where mass counterterms and bare coupling constants are assigned labels \(l\) such that the total number of particles in flight plus \(l\) equals \(N\). Renormalization conditions are imposed by requiring the physical mass \(M\) to equal the constituent fermion mass \(m\) and by relating the on-energy-shell two-body vertex function \(\Gamma_2^{(N)}(s_2=M^2)\) to the physical coupling constant \(g\) via the one-body norm factor \(I_1^{(N-1)}\).

## Key numbers

* Anomalous magnetic moment of the electron: calculated in QED with two-body Fock space truncation, it coincides with the well-known Schwinger term (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
* For the Yukawa model with three-body Fock space truncation (without antifermion contributions), the renormalization condition requires one additional \(\omega\)-dependent counterterm of the form \(\delta H^{int}_\omega = -Z_\omega \bar \psi \frac{m \sla \omega}{i \omega \cd}\) (explicit form given but no numerical value for \(Z_\omega\)).
* The two-body vertex function \(\Gamma_2\) on the energy shell (\(s_2=M^2\)) must be independent of \(\omega\); the component \(\psi_2^\prime\) at \(s_2=M^2\) should be identically zero.

## Context

This work extends the covariant formulation of light-front dynamics developed by Karmanov (1976) and collaborators (cdkm) to include a systematic nonperturbative renormalization scheme, addressing the problem of uncancelled divergences that arise when the Fock expansion is truncated. The approach is applied to QED (recovering the Schwinger term), a scalar model (highlighting antiparticle degrees of freedom), and the Yukawa model (three-body truncation without antifermions). The framework is also argued to be well-suited for effective field theories like chiral perturbation theory in the baryonic sector, enabling controlled calculations of hadron properties from an underlying Hamiltonian.
