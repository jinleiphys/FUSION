# Nuclear vorticity and the low-energy nuclear response - Towards the neutron drip line

> The transition density and current provide valuable insight into the nature of nuclear vibrations. Nuclear vorticity is a quantity related to the transverse transition current. In this work, we study the evolution of the strength distribution, related to density fluctuations, and the vorticity strength distribution, as the neutron drip line is approached. Our results on the isoscalar, natural-parity multipole response of Ni isotopes, obtained by using a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + Continuum RPA model, indicate that, close to the drip line, the low-energy response is dominated by L>1 vortical transitions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for isoscalar natural-parity multipole response of Ni isotopes calculated with a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus Continuum RPA model, the low-energy response near the neutron drip line is dominated by L>1 vortical transitions, but this conclusion is restricted to spherical, closed-(sub)shell nuclei without pairing correlations and is based on the specific example of 110Ni as an academic case.

## Method

The authors used a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Continuum RPA model formulated in coordinate space to calculate the isoscalar natural-parity response of the doubly closed Ni isotopes 56Ni, 78Ni, and 110Ni for multipolarities up to L=4. From the RPA Green function, they extracted the radial components of the transition density and transition current, then computed the vorticity density from the transverse part of the current using the curl operator. The strength distributions S_rho(E) (related to density fluctuations) and S_omega(E) (vorticity strength) were compared to discriminate between collective states of longitudinal zero-sound character and highly vortical excitation modes.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar quadrupole giant resonance in all three Ni isotopes carries a large fraction of S_rho,tot and little vorticity, with its width increasing and energy decreasing as the neutron drip line is approached.
- For 110Ni, a highly vortical threshold strength appears at low energy, exemplified by a 3.4 MeV transition (compared to a 9.3 MeV state in 56Ni).
- The SkM* Skyrme force (effective mass m*/m = 0.786) predicts the last occupied neutron state of 110Ni is bound, while the MSk7 force (m*/m = 1.05) predicts it as a resonant state at positive energy, placing the nucleus beyond the neutron drip line.
- The strength distributions are systematically shifted towards lower energies when MSk7 is used compared to SkM*.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear convection currents and vorticity in stable nuclei (RaW1987) and predictions of threshold strength in neutron-rich nuclei (HSZ1999, HSZ1996, HaS1996b, HSZ1997b). The results indicate that near the neutron drip line, low-energy transitions become predominantly vortical rather than irrotational, which contrasts with the nearly irrotational character of giant resonances in stable nuclei. The study enables future work using Quasiparticle-RPA methods including the continuum (Mat2001) to account for pairing correlations, and suggests examining the magnetic response and the proton drip line for a more complete picture.
