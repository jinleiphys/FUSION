# Beam energy and centrality dependence of the statistical moments of the net-charge and net-kaon multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at STAR

> In part to search for a possible critical point (CP) in the phase diagram of hot nuclear matter, a Beam Energy Scan was performed at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The STAR experiment collected significant Au+Au data sets at beam energies, $\sqrt{{\rm s}_{\rm NN}}$, of 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. Lattice and phenomenological calculations suggest that the presence of a CP might result in divergences of the thermodynamic susceptibilities and correlation length. The statistical moments of the multiplicity distributions of particles reflecting conserved quantities, such as net-charge and net-strangeness, are expected to depend sensitively on these correlation lengths, making them attractive tools in the search for a possible critical point. The centrality and beam-energy dependence of the statistical moments of the net-charge multiplicity distributions will be discussed. The observables studied include the lowest four statistical moments (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) and the products of these moments. The measured moments of the net-kaon multiplicity distributions will also be presented. These will be compared to the predictions from approaches lacking critical behavior, such as the Hadron Resonance Gas model and Poisson statistics.

## Key claim

The statistical moments products \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of net-charge and net-kaon multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at STAR show no significant, beam-energy-localized enhancement that would indicate a critical point in the QCD phase diagram, contrary to predictions of 1–2 order-of-magnitude enhancements from some models.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured event-by-event identified particle multiplicities for net-charge (all charged tracks with \(0.2 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\)) and net-kaons (\(K^+ - K^-\)) within \(|\eta| < 0.5\) and full azimuthal coverage, using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detector for particle identification. Centrality was defined using charged tracks with \(0.5 < |\eta| < 1.0\) to avoid mathematical autocorrelations, and a centrality bin-width correction was applied. The lowest four statistical moments (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) and their products \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) were computed for seven beam energies and compared to Poisson statistics and Hadron Resonance Gas model expectations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4,\) and 200 GeV, corresponding to baryochemical potentials of approximately 20 to 420 MeV.
- Transverse momentum range for charged tracks: \(0.2 < p_{\rm T} < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\).
- For net-charge in 0–5% central collisions, \(S\sigma\) is greater than the Poisson expectation but less than the Hadron Resonance Gas expectation; \(\kappa\sigma^2\) is generally above the Poisson expectation (which equals 1) at all beam energies.
- For net-kaons in 0–5% central collisions, the Poisson expectation describes the moments products well.
- No significant, \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\)-localized enhancement of either \(S\sigma\) or \(\kappa\sigma^2\) is observed for net-charge or net-kaons.

## Context

This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, searching for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram as suggested by lattice QCD calculations (Aoki, 2006) and phenomenological models. The results contradict predictions from a Polyakov loop extended quark-meson model (Skokov, 2012) that predicted a ~50% enhancement of \(\kappa\sigma^2\), and an ansatz based on universality arguments (MIT group) that predicted 1–2 order-of-magnitude enhancements of kurtosis for pions and protons. The absence of non-monotonic behavior in the moments products constrains the location of a possible critical point and enables future higher-statistics measurements at lower beam energies.
