# Double $Λ$ and the $Λ-Λ$ Interaction

> The $Λ$-$Λ$ effective interaction, in the channel $L=S=0$, in the nuclear medium is fitted to the available binding energies, $B_{ΛΛ}$, of double $Λ$ hypernuclei: $^{6}_{ΛΛ}$He, $^{10}_{ΛΛ}$Be and $^{13}_{ΛΛ}$B. The mesonic decay of these hypernuclei is also investigated. Finally, this effective interaction is used to predict the binding energies and mesonic decays widths of heavier double $Λ$ hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper fits an effective Λ-Λ interaction in the L=S=0 channel to the binding energies of three known double-Λ hypernuclei, but finds that many different potentials (varying in shape and magnitude) give equally good fits, making it impossible to select a unique interaction from binding energies alone.

## Method

The authors model double-Λ hypernuclei as two interacting Λ hyperons moving in a mean-field potential from the nuclear core, with core potentials adjusted to reproduce single-Λ hypernucleus binding energies. They use a σ-ω meson exchange potential for the Λ-Λ interaction, fixing the ω-Λ-Λ coupling and cutoffs from prior work, and fitting only the σ-Λ-Λ coupling via χ² minimization to the three known B_ΛΛ values. Both Hartree-Fock (HF) and variational approximations are applied, with the HF results presented in detail.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of three double-Λ hypernuclei fitted: ⁶_ΛΛHe, ¹⁰_ΛΛBe, and ¹³_ΛΛB (explicit numerical values not given in text).
- χ² per degree of freedom: ~0.1 for HF fits, ~0.5 for variational fits.
- For g_ωΛΛ/g_ωNN = 2/3 and Λ_σΛΛ = 1 GeV: g_σΛΛ/√(4π) = 3.11 ± 0.04.
- For g_ωΛΛ/g_ωNN = 2/3 and Λ_σΛΛ = 2 GeV: g_σΛΛ/√(4π) = 2.28 ± 0.02.
- Linear correlation found: g²_σΛΛ/(4π) = (2.24 ± 0.17) + (0.825 ± 0.009) × g²_ωΛΛ/(4π) for Λ_σΛΛ = 1 GeV; and (2.21 ± 0.18) + (0.836 ± 0.010) × g²_ωΛΛ/(4π) for Λ_σΛΛ = 2 GeV.
- Predicted mesonic decay ratios for ¹³_ΛΛB: Γ(→ X + Λ + π⁰)/Γ_Λ = 0.076 ± 0.004; Γ(→ X + Λ + π⁻)/Γ_Λ = 0.37 ± 0.02.
- Mesonic decay width varies at most 6% across the ten different HF potentials.
- Predicted ratios of mesonic decay widths for heavier hypernuclei are around 0.7 (not the naively expected 1), with errors of order 5%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Λ-Λ interactions (referenced as "ll" in the text) that analyzed N-N and N-hyperon scattering data, and uses Λ-core potentials from prior single-Λ hypernucleus work (referenced as "l-core"). The results enable predictions of binding energies and mesonic decay widths for heavier double-Λ hypernuclei (⁴²_ΛΛCa, ⁹²_ΛΛZr, ²¹⁰_ΛΛPb), and show that mesonic decay rates cannot distinguish between different Λ-Λ potential families, but can be predicted accurately.
