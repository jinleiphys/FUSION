# Hadrons and direct photon in pp and pA collisions at LHC and saturation effects

> We investigate hadrons and direct photon production in pp and pA collisions at the energies of RHIC and LHC within the color-dipole approach employing various saturation models. We show that greatest sensitivity to saturation effects is reached at very forward rapidities for pp collisions at LHC (\sqrt{s}=14 TeV). The ratio of direct-photon to pion production can be about 20-10 (at η=7-8). Therefore, direct photon production at forward rapidities should provide a rather clean probe. We calculate the rapidity dependence of the invariant cross-section and find some peculiar enhancement at forward rapidities which is more pronounced for direct photon production. We show that this peak is further enhanced by saturation effects. We provide predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_{pA} for pions and direct photon production in pA collisions at LHC energy at midrapidity. We show within various saturation models that the pion Cronin enhancement at RHIC is replaced by a moderate suppression at LHC energy at midrapidity due to gluon shadowing effects. Cronin enhancement of direct photons can survive at LHC energy within models with a larger saturation scale.

## Key claim

The greatest sensitivity to saturation effects in pp collisions at LHC (√s = 14 TeV) is reached at very forward rapidities, where the ratio of direct-photon to pion production can be about 20–10 (at η = 7–8), making direct photons a clean probe; however, the authors note that their formulation at midrapidity in pp collisions is not well justified and may not be valid there.

## Method

The authors employ the light-cone color-dipole approach, calculating gluon bremsstrahlung from projectile gluons (gN → g₁g₂X) and quarks (qN → qgX), and photon bremsstrahlung from quarks (qN → γX), using universal qq̅ dipole cross-sections fitted to DIS data. Hadron production cross-sections are obtained by convolving parton distribution functions (PDFs) at the hard scale Q = k_T with the dipole cross-sections and fragmentation functions, while direct-photon production uses the proton structure function F₂^p. Nuclear targets are treated via eikonalization of the dipole amplitudes with the nuclear thickness function T_A(b).

## Key numbers

- Ratio of direct-photon to pion production γ/π⁰ in pp collisions at LHC (√s = 14 TeV): about 20–10 at pseudorapidity η = 7–8.
- For pion production at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) and intermediate p_T, the average momentum fraction ⟨z⟩ in fragmentation functions is about 0.4–0.6 in the range 2 ≤ p_T (GeV) ≤ 8.
- The nonperturbative parameter r₀ = 0.3 fm characterizes the mean quark-gluon separation, fitted to soft diffraction pp → pX data.
- The effective quark mass used as a cutoff in photon radiation is m_q = 0.2 GeV.
- The gluon bremsstrahlung cross-section from projectile quarks is 6 times smaller than that from projectile gluons due to the color factor.

## Context

This work extends the color-dipole approach previously applied to DIS and hadron production at RHIC (e.g., by Kopeliovich et al. (2002) and Rezaeian and Schaefer (2008)) to LHC energies, providing predictions for both pp and pA collisions. It contrasts with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach of Jalilian-Marian and Kovchegov (2004), which predicts stronger suppression of the nuclear modification factor R_pA for pions at LHC. The results enable experimental tests of saturation physics at forward rapidities at LHC, where direct photons offer a cleaner probe than hadrons due to reduced background from radiative hadronic decays.
