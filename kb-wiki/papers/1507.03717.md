# Towards exotic hidden-charm pentaquarks in QCD

> Inspired by $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ recently observed by LHCb, a QCD sum rule investigation is performed, by which they can be identified as exotic hidden-charm pentaquarks composed of an anti-charmed meson and a charmed baryon. Our results suggest that $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ have quantum numbers $J^P=3/2^-$ and $5/2^+$, respectively. Furthermore, two extra hidden-charm pentaqurks with configurations $\bar{D}Σ_c^*$ and $\bar{D}^*Σ_c^*$ are predicted, which have spin-parity quantum numbers $J^P=3/2^-$ and $J^P=5/2^+$, respectively. As an important extension, the mass predictions of hidden-bottom pentaquarks are also given. Searches for these partners of $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ are especially accessible at future experiments like LHCb and BelleII.

## Key claim
The paper uses QCD sum rules to identify the LHCb-observed $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ as hidden-charm pentaquarks with specific quantum numbers, but the identification depends on the choice of interpolating currents and the mixing angle for $P_c(4450)$; the results are consistent with the experimental masses only when using currents of the $[\bar c_d q_d][\epsilon^{abc}c_a q_b q_c]$ color configuration, not the $[\bar c_d c_d][\epsilon^{abc}q_a q_b q_c]$ configuration.

## Method
The authors constructed local pentaquark interpolating currents with spin $J=3/2$ and $J=5/2$ in two color configurations: $[\bar c_d c_d][\epsilon^{abc}q_a q_b q_c]$ and $[\bar c_d q_d][\epsilon^{abc}c_a q_b q_c]$. They performed QCD sum rule analyses up to dimension-eight condensates, using the Borel sum rule to extract masses from two-point correlation functions, with criteria of OPE convergence (dimension-eight term < 10%) and pole contribution (> 10%) to define the Borel window. For $P_c(4450)$, a mixed current of $J^{\bar D\Sigma_c^*}_{\{\mu\nu\}}$ and $J^{\bar D^*\Lambda_c}_{\{\mu\nu\}}$ with a fine-tuned mixing angle was required to obtain a reliable mass sum rule.

## Key numbers
- $M_{[\bar D^*\Sigma_c],3/2^-} = 4.37^{+0.19}_{-0.12}$ GeV, consistent with $P_c(4380)$ mass $4380 \pm 8 \pm 29$ MeV from LHCb.
- $M_{[\bar D\Sigma_c^*\&\bar D^*\Lambda_c],{5/2^+}} = 4.47^{+0.20}_{-0.13}$ GeV, consistent with $P_c(4450)$ mass $4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5$ MeV from LHCb.
- Predicted $[\bar D\Sigma_c^*]$ hidden-charm pentaquark mass: $4.45^{+0.17}_{-0.13}$ GeV with $J^P=3/2^-$.
- Predicted $[\bar D^*\Sigma_c^*]$ hidden-charm pentaquark mass: $4.59^{+0.17}_{-0.12}$ GeV with $J^P=5/2^+$.
- Predicted hidden-bottom pentaquark masses: $M_{[\bar B^*\Sigma_b],3/2^-} = 11.55^{+0.23}_{-0.14}$ GeV and $M_{[\bar B\Sigma_b^*\&\bar B^*\Lambda_b],{5/2^+}} = 11.66^{+0.28}_{-0.27}$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule method to hidden-charm pentaquarks, following the LHCb observation of $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ (LHCb, 2015). It builds on earlier studies of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks using the one-boson-exchange model (Yang, 2011) and the boson exchange model (Chen, 2015), as well as the topological soliton model (Scoccola, 2015). The predictions of additional hidden-charm and hidden-bottom pentaquarks provide targets for experimental searches at LHCb and BelleII, and the methodology can be extended to test other color configurations such as the antiquark-diquark-diquark structure.
