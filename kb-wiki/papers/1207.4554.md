# New Generation of the Monte Carlo Shell Model for the K Computer Era

> We present a newly enhanced version of the Monte Carlo Shell Model method by incorporating the conjugate gradient method and energy-variance extrapolation. This new method enables us to perform large-scale shell-model calculations that the direct diagonalization method cannot reach. This new generation framework of the MCSM provides us with a powerful tool to perform most-advanced large-scale shell-model calculations on current massively parallel computers such as the K computer. We discuss the validity of this method in ab initio calculations of light nuclei, and propose a new method to describe the intrinsic wave function in terms of the shell-model picture. We also apply this new MCSM to the study of neutron-rich Cr and Ni isotopes using the conventional shell-model calculations with an inert 40Ca core and discuss how the magicity of N = 28, 40, 50 remains or is broken.

## Key claim

The enhanced Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) method, incorporating the conjugate gradient method and energy-variance extrapolation, enables large-scale shell-model calculations that direct diagonalization cannot reach, but the method's accuracy depends on the number of basis states and the stability of the energy-variance extrapolation, which can fail for certain states (e.g., the 8+ state of 56Ni) without a reordering technique.

## Method

The new-generation MCSM uses a variational procedure to construct an approximated wave function as a linear combination of angular-momentum- and parity-projected deformed Slater determinants, optimized sequentially (SCG) or simultaneously (FCG) using the conjugate gradient method. The energy expectation values and energy variances of these wave functions are then used in an energy-variance extrapolation, fitting a first- or second-order polynomial to estimate the exact eigenvalue at zero variance. A reordering technique for the basis states is applied to stabilize the extrapolation when anomalous behavior occurs.

## Key numbers

- The M-scheme dimension for 72Ge in the f5pg9 shell is 140,050,484.
- For 56Ni in the pf shell with the FPD6 interaction, the minimum variance of the approximated wave function using the SCG method is ⟨ΔH²⟩_{Nb=100} = 0.89 MeV², which is smaller than the original MCSM result of ⟨ΔH²⟩_{Nb=150} = 1.05 MeV².
- For the 8+ state of 56Ni, the energy-variance plot showed anomalous behavior with variance around 4 MeV², which was resolved by the reordering technique.
- The number of mesh points for the angular-momentum projection is 26² × 16 × 2 = 21,632 components.

## Context

This work extends the original MCSM method developed since 1996 (Shimizu et al., 1996) and the energy-variance extrapolation method introduced in shell-model calculations by Mizusaki and Imada (2002). The new method enables calculations beyond the reach of direct diagonalization, such as the full pf-shell calculation of 56Ni (with ~10⁹ M-scheme dimension) which was previously performed with the original MCSM (Shimizu et al., 1998). It is designed for massively parallel computers like the K computer (with more than 700,000 cores) and enables future studies of neutron-rich Cr and Ni isotopes and ab initio calculations of light nuclei.
