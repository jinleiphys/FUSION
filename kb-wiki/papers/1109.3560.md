# EMC effect, short-range nuclear correlations, neutron stars

> The recent x>1 (e,e') and correlation experiments at momentum transfer Q^2 \ge 2 GeV^2 confirm presence of short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei mostly build of nucleons. Recently we evaluated in a model independent way the dominant photon contribution to the nuclear structure. Taking into account this effect and using definition of x consistent with the exact kinematics of eA scattering (with exact sum rules) results in the significant reduction of R_A(x,Q^2)=F_{2A}(x,Q^2)/F_{2N}(x,Q^2) ratio which explains \sim 50% of the EMC effect for x\le 0.55 where Fermi motion effects are small. The remaining part of the EMC effect at $x\ge 0.5$ is consistent with dominance of the contribution of SRCs. Implications for extraction of the F_{2n}/F_{2p} ratio are discussed. Smallness of the non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei matches well the recent observation of a two-solar mass neutron star, and while large pn SRCs lead to enhancement of the neutron star cooling rate for kT\le 0.01 MeV.

# EMC effect, short-range nuclear correlations, neutron stars

## Key claim
Taking into account the Coulomb field of the nucleus (via equivalent photons) and using a kinematically consistent definition of the Bjorken variable x explains approximately 50% of the EMC effect for x ≤ 0.55, where Fermi motion effects are small; the remaining EMC effect at x ≥ 0.5 is consistent with dominance of short-range correlations (SRCs), and the small probability of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom matches observations of two-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Method
The author evaluates the photon contribution to nuclear structure in a model-independent way using the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams approximation for the Coulomb field of a fast nucleus, and corrects the definition of the Bjorken variable x from the experimental x_p = Q²/2q₀m_p to the theoretical x = AQ²/2q₀m_A that enters the convolution formula for F₂A. The remaining "hadronic EMC effect" is compared to the A-dependence of the SRC probability parameter a₂(A) extracted from x > 1 (e,e') scaling measurements at JLab and SLAC.

## Key numbers
- The photon light-cone momentum fraction λ_γ is 0.11% for ¹²C, 0.35% for ⁵⁶Fe, and 0.65% for ¹⁹⁷Au.
- The combined Coulomb and x-rescaling effects explain approximately 50% of the EMC effect for x ≤ 0.55.
- The "hadronic EMC effect" for A ≥ 50 is approximately 4% at x = 0.5, growing to about 15% at x = 0.6 and about 25% at x = 0.7.
- The probability of exotic (non-nucleonic) components in nuclei is ≤ 2%, with the component relevant for large-x EMC effect occurring with probability ∼ 0.2%.
- For the deuteron, the EMC effect at x = 0.5 is approximately 1 − R_D(0.5, Q²) ≈ 0.01.
- The neutron star cooling enhancement factor R relative to the URCA process is R ≈ 0.1 (MeV/kT)^(3/2) for x = 0.1 and kT ≪ 1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's long-standing program with Frankfurt (1980s–2010) on short-range correlations and hard nuclear processes, and directly builds on the JLab measurements of a₂(A) by Fomin (2011) and the earlier SLAC analysis by Frankfurt (1993). The model-independent Coulomb correction resolves a long-standing puzzle in the EMC effect, reducing the required role of exotic degrees of freedom. The results enable improved extraction of the F₂n/F₂p ratio at large x and connect nuclear structure at SRC scales to neutron star properties, particularly cooling rates and the stiffness of the equation of state consistent with the two-solar-mass neutron star observed by Demorest (2010).
