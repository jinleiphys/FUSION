# Quasielastic Charged Current Neutrino-nucleus Scattering

> We provide integrated cross sections for quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering. Results evaluated using the phenomenological scaling function extracted from the analysis of experimental $(e,e')$ data are compared with those obtained within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. We show that very reasonable agreement is reached when a description of final-state interactions based on the relativistic mean field is included. This is consistent with previous studies of differential cross sections which are in accord with the universality property of the superscaling function.

## Key claim

Integrated quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections computed using the phenomenological superscaling function (SuSA) extracted from electron scattering data agree very closely with those from the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) when final-state interactions are described by a relativistic mean field (RMF) potential, provided the neutrino energy is high enough (above about 1.2 GeV) for scaling arguments to be valid.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current muon-neutrino cross sections on carbon-12 within the RIA framework using an RMF potential for final-state interactions, and compared these to results obtained by folding the phenomenological superscaling function extracted from world (e,e') data (SuSA approach). They also included results from the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model and a semi-relativistic Woods-Saxon (SRWS) approach for reference. Cross sections were integrated over the muon scattering angle as a function of muon kinetic energy, over muon kinetic energy as a function of scattering angle, and fully integrated over both variables as a function of incident neutrino energy.

## Key numbers

- For incident neutrino energies of 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 GeV, the RMF and SuSA approaches both produce a reduction of about 20-25% in the cross section integrated over angle near the maximum of the muon kinetic energy distribution, compared to the RFG result.
- At neutrino energies above 1.2 GeV, the reduction of the fully integrated cross section due to final-state interactions in RMF and SuSA stabilizes at approximately 15%.
- The contribution of the discrete spectrum of nitrogen-12 obtained with the Woods-Saxon potential (SRWS-tot) to the total cross section is below 2%.
- The total cross section increases with neutrino energy up to about 1-1.2 GeV and then saturates to an almost constant value.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA approach, previously applied to differential cross sections by the same authors (Amaro et al., 2005; PRL, 2005), to integrated cross sections relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments. It confirms that the phenomenological scaling function extracted from electron scattering data (Donnelly et al., 1999, 2002) provides predictions consistent with the RIA+RMF framework, which reproduces the asymmetric shape of the experimental superscaling function. The results enable reliable predictions for quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections without requiring detailed nuclear structure models, though caution is advised for low neutrino energies where scaling arguments become less valid.
