# Non-Newtonian gravity in finite nuclei

> In this talk, we report our recent study of constraining the non-Newtonian gravity at femtometer scale. We incorporate the Yukawa-type non-Newtonian gravitational potential consistently to the Skyrme functional form using the exact treatment for the direct contribution and density-matrix expansion method for the exchange contribution. The effects from the non-Newtonian potential on finite nuclei properties are then studied together with a well-tested Skyrme force. Assuming that the framework without non-Newtonian gravity can explain the binding energies and charge radii of medium to heavy nuclei within 2% error, we set an upper limit for the strength of the non-Newtonian gravitational potential at femtometer scale.

## Key claim

The authors constrain the strength of a Yukawa-type non-Newtonian gravitational potential at femtometer scale (1–10 fm) by requiring that its addition to the well-tested MSL0 Skyrme force does not change the calculated binding energies and charge radii of medium-to-heavy nuclei by more than 2%, which is the uncertainty of the nuclear-physics framework without the non-Newtonian term.

## Method

The Yukawa-type non-Newtonian gravitational potential was incorporated into a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation for finite nuclei. The direct (Hartree) contribution was treated exactly, while the exchange (Fock) contribution was approximated using the density-matrix expansion method to obtain a Skyrme-like energy density functional. The MSL0 Skyrme force was used as the baseline nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the charge radii and binding energies of ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹²⁰Sn, and ⁴⁰Ca were computed for various values of the strength parameter α and length scale λ.

## Key numbers

- The constraint from binding energy is given by log(|α|) < 1.75 / [λ(fm)]^0.54 + 33.6 for λ = 1–10 fm.
- The constraint from charge radius is given by log(|α|) < 1.18 / [λ(fm)]^0.79 + 35.0 for λ = 1–10 fm.
- The binding-energy constraint translates to an upper limit on the boson-nucleon coupling constant: log(g²) < 0.10 [μ(MeV)]^0.54 – 3.53 for boson masses μ between 20 MeV and 200 MeV.
- For comparison, a Yukawa potential with strength α = –1.24×10³⁶ corresponds to a coupling constant g²/4π = 1/137, equal to the Coulomb coupling strength.

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on non-Newtonian gravity from neutron scattering experiments (e.g., Nesvizhevsky 2004, Pokotilovski 2006, Kamiya 2008) down to the previously unexplored femtometer scale. The results provide a nuclear-physics-based upper limit on the strength of a Yukawa-type non-Newtonian potential, which can serve as a reference for constraining weakly-coupled gauge bosons and exploring possible extra dimensions at femtometer scale. The approach uses the MSL0 Skyrme force of Chen (2010) as the baseline interaction.
