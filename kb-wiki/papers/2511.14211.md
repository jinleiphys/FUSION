# Mass-imbalance effect on the cluster formation in a one-dimensional Fermi gas with coexistent $s$- and $p$-wave interactions

> We consider the mass-imbalance effect on the clustering in a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas with coexistent even- and odd-wave interactions resulting in different configurations of clustering phases. We obtain the solutions of both stable two- and three-body cluster states with different mass ratios and configurations by solving the corresponding variational equations. We numerically map out phase diagrams consisting of the $s$- and $p$-wave pairing phases, and {trimer} phase with different configurations, in a plane of $s$- and $p$-wave pairing strengths. Within the explored ranges of $s$- and $p$-wave pairing strengths, the in-vacuum three-body states are always more deeply bound than the two-body ones. While for the in-medium case, the Cooper {trimer} phase dominates over the pairing phases when both $s$- and $p$-wave interactions are moderately strong. There is also a competition between different clustering configurations of three-body clustering.

## Key claim

Within the explored ranges of $s$- and $p$-wave pairing strengths, the in-vacuum three-body states are always more deeply bound than the two-body ones; for the in-medium case, the Cooper trimer phase dominates over the pairing phases only when both interactions are moderately strong, and there is a competition between different three-body clustering configurations.

## Method

The author solves variational equations for in-vacuum and in-medium two- and three-body cluster states in a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas with coexistent interspecies $s$-wave and intraspecies $p$-wave interactions. The trial wave functions for $s$-wave pairing, $p$-wave pairing (in both $aa$ and $bb$ configurations), and Cooper trimers (in $aab$ and $abb$ configurations) are constructed on top of the Fermi sea, and the lowest-lying cluster energies are obtained by minimizing the expectation value of the Hamiltonian. The coupled integral equations for the auxiliary amplitudes are discretized on uniform momentum grids and solved as a linear eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

* For a mass ratio $m_a/m_b = 2$, the in-vacuum three-body $aab$ configuration is dominant for $1/(\Lambda a_p)\times10 \lesssim 0.25$, while the $abb$ configuration becomes more stable when $1/(\Lambda a_p)\times10$ exceeds $0.25$, at a fixed $s$-wave interaction strength $1/(\Lambda a_s)\times10 = 2.7$.
* For the in-medium case with $m_a/m_b = 2$, the phase diagram is mapped in the range $0.5 \leq 1/(k_{\rm F}a_s) \leq 3$ and $-1.5 \leq 1/(k_{\rm F}a_p) \leq 1.5$.
* For a fixed $1/(k_{\rm F}a_s) = 2.8$, the boundary point $1/(k_{\rm F}a_p)$ between $s$-wave pairing and Cooper trimer ($aab$ configuration) shifts from $0.021$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=8$ to $-0.386$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=10$ and $-0.522$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=12$.
* For a fixed $1/(k_{\rm F}a_s) = 3.0$, the same boundary point shifts from $-0.175$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=8$ to $-0.500$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=10$ and $-0.850$ at $\Lambda/k_{\rm F}=12$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of mass-balanced two-component fermions with coexistent interspecies and intraspecies interactions (Guo 2023) by introducing mass imbalance and multiple clustering configurations. The model setup is motivated by experimental realizations of competing odd- and even-parity pairing channels in systems such as UTe$_2$, UPt$_3$, and Sr$_2$RuO$_4$, as well as by ultracold atomic gas experiments with overlapped Feshbach resonances. The results enable future investigations of unconventional superfluids and superconductors, and may be applied to studies of hypernuclei containing the lightest hyperon $\Lambda$.
