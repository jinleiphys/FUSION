# Properties of charmed and bottom hadrons in nuclear medium: Results for $Λ_c^+$ and $Λ_b$ hypernuclei

> This reports our recent studies on changes in properties of heavy hadrons containing at least a charm or a bottom quark in nuclear matter, and that the results for the $Λ^+_c$ and $Λ_b$ hypernuclei are studied quantitatively. Comparisons are made with the results for the $Λ$ hypernuclei studied previously in the same approach. It is shown that although the scalar and vector potentials for the $Λ$, $Λ_c^+$ and $Λ_b$ in the hypernuclei multiplet with the same baryon numbers are quite similar, the wave functions obtained, e.g., for $1s_{1/2}$ state, are very different. The $Λ^+_c$ probability density distribution in $^{209}_{Λ^+_c}$Pb is much more pushed away from the center than that for the $Λ$ in $^{209}_Λ$Pb due to the Coulomb force. On the contrary, the $Λ_b$ probability density distributions in $Λ_b$ hypernuclei are much larger near the origin than those for the $Λ$ in the $Λ$ hypernuclei due to its heavy mass. A possibility of $B^-$ nuclear bound (atomic) states is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The scalar and vector potentials for the Λ, Λc⁺, and Λb in hypernuclei with the same baryon numbers are quite similar, yet the wave functions obtained for the 1s₁/₂ state are very different due to the Coulomb force for Λc⁺ and the heavy mass for Λb.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in a Hartree, mean-field approximation to study static, spherically symmetric charmed and bottom hypernuclei (closed shell plus one heavy baryon configuration). The MIT bag model was employed to calculate the in-medium properties of the baryons self-consistently, with the scalar and vector potentials derived from quark-meson coupling constants. Single-particle energies were computed by solving Dirac equations with perturbative spin-orbit corrections based on the underlying quark structure.

## Key numbers

- Bag radii in free space: (RΛ, RΛc⁺, RΛb) = (0.806, 0.846, 0.930) fm
- Current quark masses used: (mu,d, ms, mc, mb) = (5, 250, 1300, 4200) MeV
- Bag pressure B = (170.0 MeV)⁴, with nucleon bag radius RN = 0.8 fm
- Single-particle energy for 1s₁/₂ state in ²⁰⁹_ΛPb: -27.0 MeV; in ²⁰⁹_Λc⁺Pb: -5.2 MeV; in ²⁰⁹_ΛbPb: -27.4 MeV
- Single-particle energy for 1s₁/₂ state in ⁴¹_ΛCa: -19.5 MeV; in ⁴¹_Λc⁺Ca: -12.8 MeV; in ⁴¹_ΛbCa: -23.0 MeV
- Meson masses used: mσ = 418 MeV, mω = 783 MeV, mρ = 770 MeV
- Coupling constants: g²σ/4π = 3.12, g²ω/4π = 5.31, g²ρ/4π = 6.93

## Context

This work extends the QMC model previously applied to Λ hypernuclei by Tsushima (2002) to the study of charmed and bottom hypernuclei, which had not been quantitatively studied before. The results enable predictions for future experiments at the Japan Hadron Facility (JHF), where charmed hadrons will be produced profusely with a beam energy of 50 GeV. The paper also briefly discusses the possibility of B⁻ nuclear bound (atomic) states, analogous to kaonic atoms studied by earlier authors, which would probe nuclear density at smaller radii than D⁻ bound states.
