# Constraining neutrino electromagnetic properties by germanium detectors

> The electromagnetic properties of neutrinos, which are either trivial or negligible in the context of the Standard Model, can probe new physics and have significant implications in astrophysics and cosmology. The current best direct limits on the neutrino millicharges and magnetic moments are both derived from data taken with germanium detectors with low thresholds at keV levels. In this paper, we discuss in detail a robust, ab initio method: the multiconfiguration relativistic random phase approximation, that enables us to reliably understand the germanium detector response at the sub-keV level, where atomic many-body physics matters. Using existing data with sub-keV thresholds, limits on reactor antineutrino's millicharge, magnetic moment, and charge radius squared are derived. The projected sensitivities for next generation experiments are also given and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the multiconfiguration relativistic random phase approximation (MCRRPA) provides a robust, ab initio method for reliably understanding germanium detector response at sub-keV levels, where atomic many-body physics matters, and that using this method with existing sub-keV threshold data yields improved limits on reactor antineutrino electromagnetic properties.

## Method

The authors applied the multiconfiguration relativistic random phase approximation (MCRRPA) theory to calculate atomic ionization cross sections for neutrino scattering off germanium atoms, treating the atomic many-body dynamics beyond the free electron approximation. They benchmarked their method by computing germanium atomic structure via multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock (MCDF) calculations and comparing photoabsorption results with experimental data. Using these calculated cross sections together with realistic reactor antineutrino spectra and existing TEXONO experimental data with sub-keV thresholds, they derived constraints on neutrino millicharge, magnetic moment, and charge radius squared.

## Key numbers

- The MCDF calculation yields binding energies for germanium orbitals: 4p1/2 at 7.8 eV, 4p3/2 at 8.0 eV, 4s1/2 at 15.4 eV, 3d5/2 at 43.1 eV, 3d3/2 at 43.8 eV, 3p3/2 at 140.1 eV, 3p1/2 at 144.8 eV, 3s1/2 at 201.5 eV, 2p3/2 at 1255.6 eV, 2p1/2 at 1287.9 eV, 2s1/2 at 1454.4 eV, and 1s1/2 at 11185.5 eV.
- The ground state of germanium (J^pi = 0^+) has configuration weights: 72.15% from (4p1/2)^2 and 27.85% from (4p3/2)^2.
- The current best direct limits on neutrino millicharges and magnetic moments are both derived from data taken with germanium detectors with low thresholds at keV levels.
- New generations of Ge detectors capable of measuring events as low as 100 eV are expected to further expand sensitivities.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous partial results reported in Chen (2013) and Chen (2014) on neutrino magnetic moment and millicharge constraints, respectively, by providing the full theoretical formulation and benchmark calculations. The MCRRPA approach improves upon earlier treatments such as the free electron approximation (FEA) and equivalent photon approximation (EPA) discussed in Fayans (1992), Kopeikin (1997), and Voloshin (2010), which have limited validity at sub-keV energies. The results enable next-generation germanium detector experiments with thresholds as low as 100 eV to probe neutrino electromagnetic properties with greater sensitivity, building on existing TEXONO and GEMMA experimental data.
