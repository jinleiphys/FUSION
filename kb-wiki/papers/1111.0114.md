# Strange quarks and lattice QCD

> The last few years have seen a dramatic improvement in our knowledge of the strange form factors of the nucleon. With regard to the vector from factors the level of agreement between theory and experiment gives us considerable confidence in our ability to calculate with non-perturbative QCD. The calculation of the strange scalar form factor has moved significantly in the last two years, with the application of new techniques which yield values considerably smaller than believed for the past 20 years. These new values turn out to have important consequences for the detection of neutralinos, a favourite dark matter candidate. Finally, very recent lattice studies have resurrected interest in the famed H-dibaryon, with modern chiral extrapolation of lattice data suggesting that it may be only slightly unbound. We review some of the major sources of uncertainty in that chiral extrapolation.

## Key claim

The strange scalar form factor (sigma commutator) of the nucleon is now known to be between 20 and 50 MeV, almost an order of magnitude smaller than previously believed for 20 years, with the consequence that neutralino dark matter detection cross sections are reduced by an order of magnitude relative to earlier optimistic expectations.

## Method

The authors review lattice QCD calculations of strange nucleon form factors, focusing on finite-range regularization (FRR) chiral extrapolation techniques applied to lattice data for the baryon octet and the H-dibaryon. For the H-dibaryon, they perform simultaneous chiral fits to octet baryon masses and H-dibaryon binding energies from the NPLQCD and HAL QCD collaborations, testing sensitivity to regulator form, regulator mass, phenomenological input parameters, and the chiral coefficient.

## Key numbers

- Strange scalar form factor (sigma_s) is between 20 and 50 MeV, based on multiple modern lattice simulations (Young 2009, Ohki 2008, Toussaint 2009)
- Strange magnetic form factor is at most a few percent of the proton magnetic form factor at low Q^2
- Strange electric radius is at most a few percent of the proton charge radius
- H-dibaryon mass relative to Lambda-Lambda threshold: m_H - 2 m_Lambda = 13 +/- 14 MeV (slightly unbound) from the primary fit using a dipole regulator
- With monopole and exponential regulators: H unbound by 13 +/- 14 MeV; with sharp cutoff: 14 +/- 16 MeV
- With Lambda_H 20% smaller than Lambda_Lambda: H bound by 2 +/- 14 MeV
- With Lambda_H 20% larger than Lambda_Lambda: H unbound by 23 +/- 13 MeV
- With C_H fixed at the Mulders-Thomas value: H unbound by 30 +/- 9 MeV
- Chi^2 per degree of freedom for regulator variation fits: between 0.48 and 0.49
- Varying f, F, and C by +/- 10% yields H unbound by 9 to 17 MeV

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of calculating strange nucleon form factors using lattice QCD, building on the indirect techniques of Leinweber and Thomas (1999, 2004) and direct calculations by the Kentucky group (Doi 2009). The new smaller value of the strange sigma commutator (Young 2009) contradicts the previous 20-year consensus and has immediate implications for neutralino dark matter searches (Giedt 2009, Ellis 2008). The H-dibaryon analysis (Shanahan 2011) uses the FRR chiral extrapolation method with the chiral coefficient calculated by Mulders and Thomas (1982), and the results suggest the H-dibaryon may be only slightly unbound, motivating experimental searches at J-PARC.
