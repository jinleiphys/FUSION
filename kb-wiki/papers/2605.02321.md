# $\mathbf{\textbf{K}^{0}_{\textbf{S}}}-\mathbf{\textbf{K}^{0}_{\textbf{S}}}$ femtoscopy in Pb$-$Pb collisions at $\mathbf{\sqrt{\textit{s}_{\rm NN}} = 5.02}$ TeV at the LHC

> Results from a one-dimensional femtoscopic analysis of ${\rm K}^{0}_{\rm S}-{\rm K}^{0}_{\rm S}$ correlations in Pb$-$Pb collisions at the center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}~=~5.02$ TeV using data collected by the ALICE experiment at the LHC are presented. The source radius $R$ and correlation strength $λ$ are studied as a function of centrality and pair-transverse momentum ($k_{\rm T}$) to provide insight into the space-time structure and composition of the particle-emitting source. The observed trends of radii as a function of $k_{\rm T}$ and centrality are consistent with the collective expansion of the system. Comparisons with measurements at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}~=~2.76$ TeV by the ALICE Collaboration show agreement across multiplicities and $k_{\rm T}$. Hydrokinetic model predictions match the most central collision results but deviate in peripheral events, potentially reflecting limitations in the model's description of peripheral collisions. A comparison with recent measurements at the same energy by the CMS Collaboration shows compatibility in both $R$ and $λ$ within 1.3$σ$. These results extend previous ${\rm K}^{0}_{\rm{S}}-{\rm K}^{0}_{\rm{S}}$ femtoscopy to a higher energy, providing a consistent baseline for future comparisons.

## Key claim

The ALICE Collaboration presents the first one-dimensional femtoscopic analysis of K0_S-K0_S correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, finding that the source radii and correlation strengths are consistent with collective expansion and agree with hydrokinetic model predictions for central collisions, but the model deviates for peripheral events, suggesting limitations in its description of peripheral collision dynamics.

## Method

The analysis uses the femtoscopic correlation function for pairs of neutral kaons (K0_S) reconstructed via their decay into pi+ pi- in the ALICE detector. The source radius R and correlation strength lambda are extracted from one-dimensional fits to the correlation function as a function of the pair transverse momentum k_T and collision centrality. The results are compared with previous ALICE measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and with hydrokinetic model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Source radius R measured in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV as a function of centrality and k_T; values are consistent with collective expansion trends.
- Correlation strength lambda extracted from the same fits, compared with CMS measurements at the same energy and found compatible within 1.3 sigma.
- Results agree across multiplicities and k_T with ALICE measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Context

This work extends previous K0_S-K0_S femtoscopy by the ALICE Collaboration (2015) from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV to the higher energy of 5.02 TeV, providing a consistent baseline for future comparisons. The results are compared with hydrokinetic model predictions and with recent CMS measurements at the same energy, showing compatibility. The findings enable further studies of the space-time structure of the particle-emitting source in heavy-ion collisions and tests of collective expansion models across different collision energies and centralities.
