# Forward antiproton-deuteron elastic scattering and total spin-dependent antiproton-deuteron cross sections at intermediate energies

> Spin-dependent total pbar-d cross sections are considered using the optical theorem. For this aim the full spin dependence of the forward pbar-d elastic scattering amplitude is considered in a model independent way. The single-scattering approximation is used to relate this amplitude to the elementary amplitudes of pbar-p and pbar-n scattering and the deuteron form factor. A formalism allowing to take into account Coulomb-nuclear interference effects in polarized pbar-d cross sections is developed. Numerical calculations for the polarized total pbar-d cross sections are performed at beam energies 20-300 MeV using the Nbar-N interaction models developed by the Julich group. Double-scattering effects are estimated within the Glauber approach and found to be in the order of 10-20%. Existing experimental data on differential pbar-d cross sections are in good agreement with the performed Glauber calculations. It is found that for the used Nbar-N models the total longitudinal and transversal pbar-d cross sections are comparable in absolute value to those for pbar-p scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin-dependent total antiproton-deuteron cross sections can be calculated using the optical theorem and single-scattering approximation, with double-scattering corrections estimated at 10-20%, and that the resulting longitudinal and transversal cross sections are comparable in absolute value to those for antiproton-proton scattering, but only for the specific antinucleon-nucleon interaction models used (the Julich group models).

## Method

The authors used the optical theorem to relate forward antiproton-deuteron elastic scattering amplitudes to total spin-dependent cross sections, expressing the forward amplitude in a model-independent way with four complex invariant amplitudes. They employed the single-scattering (impulse) approximation within Glauber-Sitenko theory to relate the antiproton-deuteron amplitude to elementary antiproton-proton and antiproton-neutron scattering amplitudes and the deuteron form factor, and developed a formalism for Coulomb-nuclear interference effects in polarized cross sections. Numerical calculations were performed at beam energies 20-300 MeV using two Julich group antinucleon-nucleon interaction models (models A and D), with double-scattering effects estimated within the Glauber approach.

## Key numbers

- Double-scattering effects were found to be in the order of 10-20% for the total unpolarized cross section at energies 50-300 MeV
- The total unpolarized antiproton-deuteron cross section is reduced by around 15% at energies 50-300 MeV when double-scattering is included compared to single-scattering approximation
- Beam energy range considered: 20-300 MeV
- Model A reproduces measured antiproton-proton polarizations quantitatively up to laboratory momentum approximately 800 MeV/c (corresponding to approximately 300 MeV kinetic energy)
- The Coulomb-nuclear interference cross sections depend on the beam acceptance angle θ_acc through the parameter Ψ = -(α/v) ln(sin(θ_acc/2)), where α is the fine structure constant and v is the antiproton velocity

## Context

This work extends the spin-filtering mechanism previously studied for proton-proton scattering (FILTEX experiment, 1993) and antiproton-proton scattering (Dmitriev, Milstein, and Strakhovenko, 2005) to the antiproton-deuteron system, motivated by the PAX collaboration's goal to produce polarized antiprotons at FAIR. The calculations use the Julich group antinucleon-nucleon interaction models (Hippchen, 1991; Mull, 1992) and compare favorably with existing experimental data on unpolarized antiproton-deuteron differential cross sections. The results enable future experimental tests of antiproton polarization buildup using polarized deuteron targets and provide predictions for spin-dependent cross sections that could constrain antinucleon-nucleon interaction models.
