# Possibility of synthesizing doubly closed superheavy nucleus

> The possibility of synthesizing a doubly magic superheavy nucleus, $^{298}114_{184}$, is investigated on the basis of fluctuation-dissipation dynamics. In order to synthesize this nucleus, we must generate more neutron-rich compound nuclei because of the neutron emissions from excited compound nuclei. The compound nucleus $^{304}114$ has two advantages to achieving a high survival probability. First, because of small neutron separation energy and rapid cooling, the shell correction energy recovers quickly. Secondly, owing to neutron emissions, the neutron number of the nucleus approaches that of the double closed shell and the nucleus obtains a large fission barrier. Because of these two effects, the survival probability of $^{304}114$ does not decrease until the excitation energy $E^{*}= 50$ MeV. These properties lead to a rather high evaporation reside cross section.

## Key claim
The doubly magic superheavy nucleus ²⁹⁸114₁₈₄ may be synthesizable via the neutron-rich compound nucleus ³⁰⁴114, whose survival probability does not decrease until an excitation energy of 50 MeV due to rapid cooling and the approach of its neutron number to the closed shell N=184 during de-excitation.

## Method
The author used a fluctuation-dissipation model combining a three-dimensional Langevin equation for the fusion process (with two-center parameterization of nuclear shapes) and a one-dimensional Smoluchowski equation for the survival probability during de-excitation. The temperature-dependent shell correction energy was included, and neutron evaporation was simulated using the statistical code SIMDEC. The evaporation residue cross section was calculated by multiplying capture probability, compound-nucleus formation probability, and survival probability.

## Key numbers
- The average neutron separation energy ⟨Bₙ⟩ for A=304 (Z=114) is 4.06 MeV, compared to 5.25 MeV for A=298 and 6.43 MeV for A=292.
- The survival probability of ³⁰⁴114 has a flat region from excitation energy E* = 20 to 50 MeV.
- At E* = 50 MeV, the survival probability of ³⁰⁴114 is three orders of magnitude larger than that of ²⁹⁸114.
- The shell-damping energy E_d is chosen as 20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author’s previous studies (Aritomo 1997, 1999) on isotope dependence of evaporation residue cross sections for Z=114 with N ≤ 184, now investigating neutron-rich compound nuclei with N > 184. It builds on macroscopic-microscopic calculations (Myers and Swiatecki 1966) predicting a magic island of stability at Z=114, N=184, and on experimental efforts by the FLNR group (Oganessian 1999) using reactions like ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu. The mechanism described suggests that neutron-rich superheavy nuclei may be accessible with future secondary beams, and could apply to any system with small neutron separation energy and neutron number slightly above a closed shell.
