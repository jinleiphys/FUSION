# A lattice theory for low energy fermions at finite chemical potential

> We construct a lattice theory describing a system of interacting nonrelativistic spin s=1/2 fermions at nonzero chemical potential. The theory is applicable whenever the interparticle separation is large compared to the range of the two-body potential, and does not suffer from a sign problem. In particular, the theory could be useful in studying the thermodynamic limit of fermion systems for which the scattering length is much larger than the interparticle spacing, with applications to realistic atomic systems and dilute neutron gases.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a lattice formulation of nonrelativistic spin-1/2 fermions at nonzero chemical potential that avoids the sign problem, applicable when the interparticle separation is large compared to the range of the two-body potential. The method is intended to study systems where the scattering length is much larger than the interparticle spacing, but the authors note that a first-order phase transition at strong attraction may limit the accessible parameter region, and a full-scale simulation beyond mean-field is needed to confirm that a useful continuum limit persists.

## Method

The authors discretize Euclidean spacetime on an \(N_\tau \times N_s^3\) lattice with equal spacing in space and time, using a free fermion action with chemical potential \(\mu\) treated as an imaginary gauge field. Interactions are introduced via a non-propagating scalar auxiliary field \(\varphi\) coupled to the fermions through a four-fermion interaction, with the coupling \(m^2\) tuned to reproduce the physical two-body scattering length \(a\) by summing ladder diagrams at zero momentum. A constant complex source \(J\) for fermion pairs is included to study pairing transitions; after integrating out the fermions, the remaining path integral over \(\varphi\) has a positive semidefinite measure, enabling Monte Carlo methods. The phase structure is analyzed in the mean-field approximation by computing the effective potential for constant \(\varphi\) and determining the fermion density \(n\) from \(\langle \varphi \rangle\).

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless parameter \(\eta = a n^{1/3}\) characterizes the system, where \(a\) is the two-body scattering length and \(n\) is the fermion density.
* The relation between the lattice parameter \(m^2\) and the scattering length is \(\frac{m^2}{M} = -\frac{1}{4\pi a} + L(M)\), where \(L(M) = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{d^3\mathbf{p}}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{1}{\Delta_\mathbf{p} + \Delta_\mathbf{p}^2/2M}\) integrated over the Brillouin zone \(|p_i| \le \pi\).
* In the mean-field approximation at \(\mu=0\), a first-order phase transition separates a region with fermion density \(n=0\) from a region with \(n=O(1)\) in lattice units; the phase diagram is shown in the \((M, a^{-1})\) plane for \(M \ge 0.1\).
* For fixed \(M=1\) and \(a^{-1}=0\), the density \(n\) as a function of \(\mu\) exhibits a discontinuous jump at a critical chemical potential, with a small-\(\mu\) phase where a continuum limit can be defined.

## Context

This work extends lattice techniques for nonrelativistic fermions at finite density, addressing the sign problem that plagues most such formulations. It is motivated by experimental progress in ultracold fermionic atoms near Feshbach resonances (Inouye 1998, Courteille 1998, Roberts 1998) and by the need for non-variational numerical methods to complement the fixed-node Diffusion Monte Carlo calculation of Carlson (2003), which obtained \(\xi = 0.44 \pm 0.01\) for the energy per particle relative to a noninteracting Fermi gas at large \(|\eta|\). The proposed lattice theory enables future studies of thermodynamic properties such as the critical temperature for the pairing transition, and can be extended to include kinetic terms for the auxiliary field, derivative couplings, or multiple flavors to address three-body interactions and renormalization group limit cycles (Bedaque 1998, Beane 2000, Glazek 2002, Bawin 2003).
