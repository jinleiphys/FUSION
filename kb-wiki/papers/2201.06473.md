# Electromagnetic spectral functions in hot and dense chirally imbalanced quark matter

> The photon self-energy from chirally imbalanced quark matter is evaluated at finite temperature and density using the real time formulation of thermal field theory. The analytic structure is explored in detail exposing the cut structure which corresponds to a variety of physical scattering and decay processes in the medium and their thresholds. The mass of the quarks in the chiral symmetry broken phase are obtained from the gap equation of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. It is found that, in presence of finite chiral chemical potential, the chiral condensate tends to get stronger at low temperature while the opposite is observed at high values of temperature. A continuous spectrum is obtained for the electromagnetic spectral function and this is purely a finite chiral chemical potential effect.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic spectral function in hot and dense quark matter acquires a continuous spectrum that is purely a finite chiral chemical potential effect, and the chiral condensate exhibits "chiral catalysis" (strengthening at low temperature) and "inverse chiral catalysis" (weakening at high temperature) in the presence of a chiral chemical potential, as obtained from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model gap equation.

## Method

The authors derived the real-time fermion propagator at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and chiral chemical potential using the real time formalism of thermal field theory. They obtained the constituent quark mass by solving the gap equation of the 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation with a smooth three-momentum cutoff regularization. The one-loop photon self-energy was then evaluated using this propagator, and its imaginary part was decomposed into longitudinal and transverse (right- and left-circularly polarized) electromagnetic spectral functions via Lorentz projection tensors.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of electromagnetic spectral functions in hot and dense matter (McLerran 1984, Kajantie 1986, Weldon 1990, Alam 1996, Rapp 1999, Aurenche 2000, Arnold 2001) by incorporating chiral imbalance via a chiral chemical potential. It builds on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model treatments of chirally imbalanced matter (Fukushima 2010, Ruggieri 2011, Chao 2013, Yu 2014, Farias 2016, Chaudhuri 2021) and provides the first derivation of the massive fermion propagator with chiral chemical potential in the real time formalism. The results enable calculation of photon and dilepton emission rates from chirally imbalanced quark matter, relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments searching for the chiral magnetic effect.
