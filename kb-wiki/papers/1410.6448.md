# A semi-holographic model for heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a semi-holographic model for the out-of-equilibrium dynamics during the partonic stages of an ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision. The model combines a weakly-coupled hard sector, involving gluon modes with energy and momenta of the order of the saturation momentum and relatively large occupation numbers, with a strongly-coupled soft sector, which physically represents the soft gluons radiated by the hard partons. The hard sector is described by perturbative QCD, more precisely, by its semi-classical approximation (the classical Yang-Mills equations) which becomes appropriate when the occupation numbers are large. The soft sector is described by a marginally deformed conformal field theory, which in turn admits a holographic description in terms of classical Einstein's equations in $AdS_5$ with a minimally coupled massless `dilaton'. The model involve two free parameters which characterize the gauge-invariant couplings between the hard and soft sectors. Via these couplings, the hard modes provide dynamical sources for the gravitational equations at the boundary of $AdS_5$ and feel the feedback of the latter as additional soft sources in the classical Yang-Mills equations. Importantly, the initial conditions for this coupled dynamics are fully determined by the hard sector alone, i.e. by perturbative QCD, and are conveniently given by the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory. We also develop a new semi-holographic picture of jets in the QGP by attaching a non-Abelian charge to the endpoint of the trailing string in $AdS_5$ representing a heavy quark. This leads to modified Nambu-Goto equations for the string which govern the (collisional and radiative) energy loss by the heavy quark towards both hard and soft modes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semi-holographic model for heavy-ion collisions that simultaneously describes weakly-coupled hard gluon modes (via classical Yang-Mills equations) and strongly-coupled soft gluon modes (via a holographic dual of an infrared conformal field theory), with the caveat that the model involves two free parameters characterizing the gauge-invariant couplings between the hard and soft sectors, and the initial conditions are fully determined by the hard sector alone through the color glass condensate effective theory.

## Method

The authors couple classical Yang-Mills fields representing the hard gluon modes (the "glasma") to two gauge-invariant operators of a strongly-coupled infrared conformal field theory (IR-CFT) that describes the soft gluon sector: the energy-momentum tensor and a scalar glueball operator. The IR-CFT dynamics are computed holographically via classical Einstein's equations in AdS₅ with a minimally coupled massless dilaton, where the hard modes provide dynamical boundary sources for the graviton and dilaton fields, and the soft sector feeds back as additional sources in the classical Yang-Mills equations. The coupled system must be solved self-consistently through numerical iterations, with initial conditions given by the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory from perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum Qₛ sets the hard scale; gluon modes have transverse and longitudinal momenta of order Qₛ at proper time τ ∼ 1/Qₛ
- Gluon occupation numbers in the hard sector are of order 1/α(Qₛ), where α(Qₛ) ≪ 1 is the QCD running coupling at the saturation scale
- The model involves two dimensionless parameters β and γ for the hard-soft couplings, which at large N_c must scale like 1/N_c²
- The AdS/CFT dictionary identifies 4πG₅/l³ = 2π²/N_c², where G₅ is the five-dimensional Newton's constant and l is the AdS radius
- The boundary metric for the IR-CFT is g_(0)μν(x) = η_μν + (γ/Qₛ⁴) t_μν(x), where t_μν is the energy-momentum tensor of the Yang-Mills fields

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model for jet quenching by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2014), which combined perturbative QCD fragmentation with AdS/CFT descriptions of the plasma, by developing a unified framework that treats both hard and soft degrees of freedom in the medium itself rather than only for probes. The semi-holographic approach was previously applied to condensed matter systems by Faulkner and Polchinski (2010) and Mukhopadhyay et al. (2013) for non-Fermi liquids, but the present work differs because in the QGP context the densities of hard and soft modes scale similarly with N, requiring inclusion of back-reaction from hard modes on the soft sector. The model enables future numerical studies of the full out-of-equilibrium dynamics from CGC initial conditions through thermalization and jet energy loss.
