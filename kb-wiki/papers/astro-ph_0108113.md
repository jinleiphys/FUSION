# Supernova SN1987A Bound on Neutrino Spectra for R-Process Nucleosynthesis

> The neutrino driven wind during a core collapse supernova is an attractive site for r-process nucleosynthesis. The electron fraction $Y_e$ in the wind depends on observable neutrino energies and luminosities. The mean antineutrino energy is limited by supernova SN1987A data while lepton number conservation constrains the ratio of antineutrino to neutrino luminosities. If $Y_e$, in the wind, is to be suitable for rapid neutron capture nucleosynthesis, then the mean electron neutrino energy may be significantly lower then that predicted in present supernova simulations, or there may be new neutrino physics such as oscillations to sterile neutrinos.

# Supernova SN1987A Bound on Neutrino Spectra for R-Process Nucleosynthesis

## Key claim

If the neutrino-driven wind in a core collapse supernova is to produce a suitable electron fraction \(Y_e\) for r-process nucleosynthesis, the mean electron neutrino energy must be significantly lower than predicted in present supernova simulations, or there must be new neutrino physics such as oscillations to sterile neutrinos; alternatively, a rapid increase of the mean antineutrino energy at late times could evade the limits, but this is not seen in the SN1987A data.

## Method

The author derives the initial electron fraction \(Y_e\) in the neutrino-driven wind from the ratio of neutrino capture rates on neutrons and antineutrino capture rates on protons, including weak magnetism and recoil corrections often neglected in simulations. The mean antineutrino energy \(\bar\epsilon\) is constrained by the 95% upper limit on the antineutrino temperature from SN1987A data (Jegerlehner, Neubig and Raffelt 1996), and the ratio of antineutrino to neutrino luminosities is bounded by lepton number conservation. These constraints are then used to set upper limits on the mean electron neutrino energy \(\epsilon\) required for the wind to be neutron rich (\(Y_e < 0.5\)) or significantly neutron rich (\(Y_e < 0.4\)).

## Key numbers

- 95% upper limit on time-averaged antineutrino temperature from SN1987A (no oscillations): \(T_{\bar\nu_e} < 4.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), corresponding to \(\bar\epsilon < 18.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- 95% upper limit including oscillations (large mixing angle solar solution, \(\bar\nu_\mu/\bar\nu_\tau\) temperature 1.7 times \(\bar\nu_e\)): \(T_{\bar\nu_e} < 4.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\), corresponding to \(\bar\epsilon < 16.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(Y_e < 0.5\) (minimum for r-process), without oscillations: \(\epsilon < 11.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(T_{\nu_e} < 2.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(Y_e < 0.5\), with oscillations: \(\epsilon < 10.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(T_{\nu_e} < 2.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(Y_e < 0.4\) (significantly neutron rich, longer expansion timescale), without oscillations: \(\epsilon < 6.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(T_{\nu_e} < 1.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(Y_e < 0.4\), with oscillations: \(\epsilon < 5.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(T_{\nu_e} < 1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Assumed ratio of antineutrino to neutrino luminosities: \(\bar L / L \leq 1.1\).

## Context

This work extends constraints from SN1987A data, originally analyzed by Jegerlehner, Neubig and Raffelt (1996) for antineutrino temperatures, to directly limit the electron neutrino energy required for r-process nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven wind. The paper contrasts with many detailed supernova simulations (e.g., Bruenn 1991, Rampp and Janka 2000, Buras et al. 2001) that predict higher electron neutrino temperatures than the limits derived here. It also discusses two r-process scenarios: a short expansion timescale (Thompson et al. 2001, Cardall and Fuller 1997) requiring \(Y_e < 0.5\), and a longer timescale (Hoffman et al. 1997) requiring \(Y_e < 0.4\), both of which appear difficult to satisfy with current simulations. The results motivate either colder neutrino spectra in supernova models, new neutrino physics such as oscillations to sterile neutrinos, or alternative r-process sites such as prompt explosions, peculiar supernovae, jets, or colliding neutron stars.
