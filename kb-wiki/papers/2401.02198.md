# Hybrid star models in the light of new multi-messenger data

> Recent astrophysical mass inferences of compact stars HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0952-0607, with extremely small and large masses respectively, as well as the measurement of the neutron skin of Ca in the CREX experiment challenge and constrain the models of dense matter. We examine the concept of hybrid stars - objects containing quark cores surrounded by nucleonic envelopes - as models that account for these new data along with other inferences. We employ a family of 81 nucleonic equations of state (EoSs) with variable skewness and slope of symmetry energy at saturation density and a constant speed-of-sound EoS for quark matter. For each nucleonic EoS, a family of hybrid EoSs is generated by varying the transition density, the energy jump, and the speed of sound. These models are tested against the data from GW170817 and J1731-347, which favor low-density soft EoS and J0592-0607 and J0740+6620, which require high-density stiff EoS. The addition of J0592-0607's mass measurement to the constraints has no significant impact on the parameter space of the admissible EoS, but allows us to explore the potential effect of pulsars more massive than J0740+6620, if such exists. We then examine the occurrence of twin configurations and quantify the ranges of masses and radii that they can possess. It is shown that including J1731-347 data favors EoSs that predict low-mass twins with $M \lesssim 1.3\,M_{\odot}$ that can be realized if the deconfinement transition density is low. If combined with large speed of sound in quark matter such models allow for maximum masses of hybrid stars in $2.0$--$2.6\,M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hybrid stars (objects with quark cores surrounded by nucleonic envelopes) remain viable models for compact stars even after including the extreme mass inferences from HESS J1731-347 (very low mass) and PSR J0952-0607 (very high mass), but only if the nucleonic equation of state is soft at low densities and stiff at high densities, and the deconfinement transition density is low enough to accommodate the low-mass object.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of 81 nucleonic equations of state (EoSs) using covariant density functional theory with variable skewness parameter Q_sat (range -600 to 1000 MeV) and symmetry energy slope L_sym (range 20 to 100 MeV) at saturation density. They combined these with a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parameterization for quark matter, varying the transition density (1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0 times nuclear saturation density rho_0), the energy density jump at transition, and the squared sound speed (c_s^2 = 1, 2/3, 1/2). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each hybrid EoS and filtered the resulting mass-radius sequences against observational constraints from GW170817, NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the new mass inferences for HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0952-0607.

## Key numbers

- The skewness coefficient Q_sat varies in the range -600 to 1000 MeV; the slope parameter L_sym varies in the range 20 to 100 MeV.
- Transition densities considered: 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0 times nuclear saturation density rho_0 (0.152 fm^{-3}).
- HESS J1731-347 has mass M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_sun and radius R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km (68% credible interval).
- PSR J0952-0607 has mass M = 2.35^{+0.17}_{-0.17} M_sun (68% credible interval), with spin frequency 707 Hz.
- Including J1731-347 data favors low-mass twin configurations with masses M less than or equal to 1.3 M_sun.
- Maximum masses of hybrid stars range from 2.0 to 2.6 M_sun depending on the sound speed in quark matter.
- Ultra-compact configurations have radii in the range 6 to 10 km, appearing for models with nucleonic branch maximum mass around 2.0 to 2.15 M_sun.
- The largest radius difference between twin configurations Delta R_twin is approximately 4 to 5 km for low-mass hybrid stars relevant to HESS J1731-347.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars and twin configurations by Alford (2013), Benic (2015), and Alvarez-Castillo (2019) by incorporating the newly reported mass inferences for HESS J1731-347 from Doroshenko (2022) and PSR J0952-0607 from Romani (2022), as well as the CREX experiment measurement of the neutron skin of Calcium. The authors use a family of nucleonic EoSs from Li (2023c) that systematically vary the high-order nuclear matter parameters Q_sat and L_sym, which control the high-density and intermediate-density behavior respectively. The results enable future searches for observational signatures that could distinguish hybrid stars from purely hadronic compact stars, particularly through the identification of twin configurations with identical masses but different radii and tidal deformabilities.
