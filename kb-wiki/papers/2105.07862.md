# Heavy quark potential in quark-gluon Plasma: Deep neural network meets lattice quantum chromodynamics

> Bottomonium states are key probes for experimental studies of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in high-energy nuclear collisions. Theoretical models of bottomonium productions in high-energy nuclear collisions rely on the in-medium interactions between the bottom and antibottom quarks. The latter can be characterized by the temperature ($T$) dependent potential, with real ($V_R(T,r)$) and imaginary ($V_I(T,r)$) parts, as a function of the spatial separation ($r$). Recently, the masses and thermal widths of up to $3S$ and $2P$ bottomonium states in QGP were calculated using lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD). Starting from these LQCD results and through a novel application of deep neural network, here, we obtain $V_R(T,r)$ and $V_I(T,r)$ in a model-independent fashion. The temperature dependence of $V_R(T,r)$ was found to be very mild between $T\approx0-334$~MeV. For $T=151-334$~MeV, $V_I(T,r)$ shows a rapid increase with $T$ and $r$, which is much larger than the perturbation-theory-based expectations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the in-medium heavy quark potential can be extracted from lattice QCD results for bottomonium masses and thermal widths using a deep neural network approach, finding that the real part of the potential shows very mild temperature dependence between approximately 0 and 334 MeV, while the imaginary part increases rapidly with temperature and spatial separation, much larger than perturbation-theory-based expectations.

## Method

The authors represent the real and imaginary parts of the temperature-dependent heavy quark potential as deep neural networks with two-dimensional input layers (temperature T and spatial separation r) and one-dimensional output layers. They optimize the network parameters by minimizing a chi-square function comparing masses and thermal widths of up to 3S and 2P bottomonium states from the Schrödinger equation (solved with the DNN-represented potentials) against lattice QCD results from Larsen et al. (2019) at six temperature points (0, 151, 173, 199, 251, 334 MeV). The gradient of the cost function with respect to network parameters is computed via perturbation theory and the Hellmann-Feynman theorem, enabling back-propagation through the Schrödinger equation solver, and Bayesian inference is used for uncertainty quantification.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: bottom quark mass m_b = 6.00 GeV, strong coupling alpha = 0.406, string tension sigma = 0.221 GeV^2, bag constant B = -2.53 GeV
- Vacuum bottomonium mass predictions from the potential model match experiment with maximum absolute difference of 14 MeV (for the 2S state)
- Temperature range studied: T = 0 to 334 MeV for the real part V_R(T,r); T = 151 to 334 MeV for the imaginary part V_I(T,r)
- The DNN networks used: 4-layer architecture with {32, 16, 32} neurons for V_R and {16, 16, 16} neurons for V_I, using elu activation functions
- Regularizer values: lambda_R = 10^{-3} for V_R network, lambda_I = 10^{-2} for V_I network

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of bottomonium properties by Larsen et al. (2019, 2020) by providing a model-independent extraction of the in-medium heavy quark potential, which is a critical input for effective field theory descriptions of quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma. The method contrasts with previous perturbative approaches such as one-loop hard thermal loop calculations by Laine (2006) and Beraudo (2007), and with static quark potential extractions from thermal Wilson loop spectral functions by Rothkopf (2011) and Burnier (2014, 2015). The results enable more reliable theoretical modeling of bottomonium suppression patterns observed in heavy ion collision experiments at the LHC.
