# Relativistic Viscous Hydrodynamics with Angular Momentum

> Hydrodynamics is a general theoretical framework for describing the long-time large-distance behaviors of various macroscopic physical systems, with its equations based on conservation laws such as energy-momentum conservation and charge conservation. Recently there has been significant interest in understanding the implications of angular momentum conservation for a corresponding hydrodynamic theory. In this work, we examine the key conceptual issues for such a theory in the relativistic regime where the orbital and spin components get entangled. We derive the equations for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with angular momentum through Navier-Stokes type of gradient expansion analysis and find five new transport coefficients for angular momentum diffusion modes.

## Key claim

The authors derive a consistent framework for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics that includes angular momentum conservation via a Navier-Stokes-type gradient expansion, but they note that the separation of spin and orbital components within a fluid cell may not be uniquely defined and that the power counting of the angular momentum chemical potential as a zeroth-order term (as they adopt) is not the only possible choice.

## Method

The authors start from the conservation laws for energy-momentum, charge, and angular momentum, and decompose the angular momentum current into orbital and internal (spin) parts. They then perform a systematic gradient expansion to first order in derivatives (Navier-Stokes level) in the Eckart frame, writing constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor, charge current, and angular momentum current in terms of hydrodynamic variables and their gradients. The second law of thermodynamics is enforced via the entropy current to fix the signs and forms of the dissipative terms.

## Key numbers

* Five new transport coefficients are identified: χ₁, χ₂, χ₃, χ₄, and χ₅, which quantify angular momentum diffusion in various modes.
* The ratio of angular momentum J to baryon number B in a heavy ion collision is estimated to be J/B ∼ 10²–10³, several orders of magnitude larger than the proton's J/B = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics (which only includes energy-momentum and charge conservation) by incorporating angular momentum conservation, motivated by experimental observations of spin polarization in rotating matter such as heavy ion collisions (STAR 2017, ALICE 2019). It builds on earlier efforts to develop spin hydrodynamics (Becattini 2009, Florkowski 2017, Hattori 2019, Fukushima 2020) and provides a foundation for second-order causal frameworks (Li 2020, Gallegos 2021) and for microscopic computations of the new transport coefficients from quantum transport theories (Bhadury 2020, Shi 2020, Weickgenannt 2020). The framework is intended to enable phenomenological simulations of angular momentum transport and spin polarization observables in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR.
