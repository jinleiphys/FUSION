# Aspects of the phase diagram in (P)NJL-like models

> We discuss three applications of NJL- and PNJL-like models to assess aspects of the QCD phase diagram: First, we study the effect of mesonic correlations on the pressure below and above the finite temperature phase transition within a nonlocal PNJL model beyond the mean-field approximation. Second, we reconstruct the phase boundary of an NJL model from a Taylor expansion of the chiral susceptibility about $μ= 0$ and compare the result with the exact phase boundary. Finally, we demonstrate the realization of the "non-standard scenario" for the critical surface in a three-flavor PNJL model with a $μ$-dependent determinant interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-standard scenario for the QCD critical surface (curved towards smaller masses) can be realized in a three-flavor PNJL model by introducing a chemical-potential-dependent determinant interaction, but only if the coupling drops sufficiently fast with \(\mu\); the authors do not claim this is the actual QCD behavior, only that it is possible in a simple model.

## Method

The authors perform three separate analyses within NJL and PNJL models: (1) a two-flavor nonlocal PNJL model with a Gaussian form factor, where the thermodynamic potential is computed beyond mean-field via a \(1/N_c\) expansion to next-to-leading order, including ring-sum RPA-like polarization loops for pion and sigma mesons; (2) a two-flavor NJL model in mean-field approximation, where the chiral susceptibility is Taylor expanded in powers of \(\mu/T\) up to 6th order and compared to the exact phase boundary; (3) a three-flavor PNJL model with local 4-point and 6-point (determinant) interactions, where the six-point coupling \(g_D\) is made \(\mu\)-dependent to explore the critical surface.

## Key numbers

* Pressure from the nonlocal PNJL model with physical pion mass (140 MeV) shows that at low temperatures the pressure is well described by a free pion gas, with the sigma meson giving an additional visible contribution near \(T_c\) and negligible contributions above \(T > 1.5 T_c\).
* For the Taylor expansion study in the two-flavor NJL model, the exact phase diagram has a critical point at \(\mu/T \approx 4\); the reconstructed crossover line from the Taylor expansion deviates from the exact result already below \(\mu/T \approx 0.6\) even with 6th-order terms included.
* In the three-flavor PNJL model with unphysically small quark masses \(m_u = m_d = m_s = 0.5\) MeV, the non-standard scenario is realized using a \(\mu\)-dependent coupling \(g_D(\mu) = g_D^{(0)} \exp(-\mu^2/\mu_0^2)\) with \(\mu_0 = 168\) MeV, producing critical lines in the \(m_{ud}-m_s\) plane at \(\mu = 0\), 100 MeV, and 200 MeV that are curved towards lower masses.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model approach of Ratti (2005) by including mesonic correlations beyond mean-field, which were absent in that earlier comparison with lattice data. The Taylor expansion study addresses the reliability of the method used by lattice groups such as Allton (2005) and Schmidt (2008) to reconstruct the phase boundary at non-zero chemical potential. The non-standard critical surface scenario follows a suggestion by Fukushima (2008) and is demonstrated explicitly in a three-flavor PNJL model, providing a simple framework to test ideas about the QCD phase diagram that are relevant for interpreting lattice results from deForcrand (2006).
