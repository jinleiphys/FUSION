# Collision Energy Dependence of Particle Ratios and Freeze-out Parameters in Ultra Relativistic Nucleus Nucleus Collisions

> This work investigates the thermo-chemical freeze-out condition of the multi-component hot and dense hadron resonance gas (HRG) formed in the ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (URNNC). The van der Waals (VDW) type model used in the present analysis incorporates the repulsive as well as attractive interactions among the hadrons. The baryons (antibaryons) are treated as incompressible objects. Using this theoretical approach the values of the model freeze-out parameters of the system are extracted over a wide range of collision energy by analyzing experimental data on like-mass antibaryon to baryon ratios. The same set of parameters is found to explain the energy dependence of several other particle ratios quite satisfactorily. We find that the horn-like structures seen in the ratios of strange particles to pions as a function of the collision energy cannot be explained by the VDW-HRG model alone without considering the strangeness imbalance effect in the system. We have compared our freeze-out line with those obtained earlier. The correlation between the $\bar{p}/p$ and $K^-/K^+$ ratios is also examined.

## Key claim

The van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDW-HRG) model, which includes both repulsive and attractive interactions among hadrons, can describe the energy dependence of many particle ratios in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, but it cannot explain the horn-like structures seen in strange particle to pion ratios without additionally introducing a strangeness imbalance factor.

## Method

The authors used a van der Waals-type grand canonical ensemble formulation where baryons and antibaryons are treated as incompressible spheres with a hard-core radius, while mesons are treated as point-like. The freeze-out parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B) were extracted by fitting the model to experimental data on like-mass antibaryon-to-baryon ratios (p̄/p, Λ̄/Λ, Ξ̄/Ξ, Ω̄/Ω) across a wide range of collision energies using a chi-squared minimization procedure. The same parameter set was then used to predict other particle ratios, and a strangeness imbalance factor ξ_s was introduced to address discrepancies in strange-to-non-strange particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic hard-core radius: r = 0.73 ± 0.02 fm, extracted from the best fit to antibaryon-to-baryon ratios.
- Attractive interaction parameter: a = 329 MeV fm³, chosen from previous works.
- Fit parameters for μ_B = c / (1 + d√s_NN): c = 1398 ± 29 MeV, d = 0.28 ± 0.008 GeV⁻¹.
- Fit parameters for T = e - fμ_B² - gμ_B⁴: e = 164 ± 2.5 MeV, f = 0.14 ± 0.021 MeV⁻¹, g = 0.015 ± 0.04 MeV⁻³.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for antibaryon-to-baryon ratios: χ²/dof = 0.54 (p̄/p), 0.79 (Λ̄/Λ), 3.92 (Ξ̄/Ξ), 0.68 (Ω̄/Ω).
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for K⁻/K⁺ ratio: χ²/dof = 1.07.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for π⁻/π⁺ ratio: χ²/dof = 1.25.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for p/π⁺ ratio: χ²/dof = 0.71.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for p̄/π⁻ ratio: χ²/dof = 3.09.

## Context

This work extends the VDW-HRG model approach previously used by Vovchenko (2015) and Samanta (2017) to describe lattice QCD thermodynamics, applying it to particle ratio systematics across a wide energy range from AGS to LHC. The study confirms earlier findings by Andronic (2006) and Cleymans (2006) regarding the energy dependence of freeze-out parameters, while specifically addressing the long-standing discrepancy in K/π ratios that had been noted by Beutler (2008) and Tawfik (2016). The introduction of a strangeness imbalance factor ξ_s enables future work to reconcile the VDW-HRG model with the horn-like structures observed in strange particle to pion ratios, particularly the K⁺/π⁺ and K⁻/π⁻ ratios at SPS and RHIC energies.
