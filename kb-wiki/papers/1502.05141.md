# Deuteron form factors in a phenomenological approach

> The electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron, particularly the quadrupole form factor, are studied with a help of a phenomenological Lagrangian approach where the vertex of the deuteron-proton-neutron with $D$-state contribution is explicitly taken into account. The result shows the importance of this contribution to the quadrupole form factor in the approach.

## Key claim

The inclusion of both \(S\)- and \(D\)-state contributions to the deuteron-proton-neutron vertex in a phenomenological Lagrangian approach can reasonably reproduce the deuteron charge, magnetic, and quadrupole form factors simultaneously, but the model still cannot correctly reproduce the crossing points of the charge and magnetic form factors at \(Q^2 = 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\), respectively.

## Method

The authors treat the deuteron as a weakly bound hadronic molecule of a proton and neutron, using a phenomenological effective Lagrangian with a vertex that includes both \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-state contributions, following the work of Blankenbecler, Goldberger, and Halpern. The coupling constant \(g_D\) is fixed by the compositeness condition \(Z=0\) (Weinberg, Salam), and a Gaussian correlation function with a size parameter \(\Lambda\) is used to render Feynman diagrams ultraviolet finite. The deuteron electromagnetic form factors \(G_C\), \(G_M\), and \(G_Q\) are then extracted from the one-photon-exchange matrix element for electron-deuteron elastic scattering, using the nucleon form factors of Blunden.

## Key numbers

- The three model parameters are fixed by fitting to experimental data: \(\Lambda_S = 0.10\) GeV, \(\Lambda_D = 0.08\) GeV, and \(\rho = 0.03\).
- The estimated magnetic moment at zero recoil is \(G_M(0) = 1.53\), compared to the normalization condition of 1.714.
- The estimated quadrupole moment at zero recoil is \(G_Q(0) = 21.38\), compared to the normalization condition of 25.83.
- The experimental data show a zero in the charge form factor at \(Q_{0C}^2 = 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) and in the magnetic form factor at \(Q_{0M}^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\), which the present calculation does not reproduce.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological Lagrangian approach (Liang and Dong, earlier works), which only considered the \(S\)-wave contribution and produced a negligibly small quadrupole moment. By explicitly including the \(D\)-state contribution to the deuteron-proton-neutron vertex, as discussed by Blankenbecler, Goldberger, and Halpern, the quadrupole moment is significantly improved. The approach is fully relativistic and differs from three-dimensional potential model calculations (e.g., Mau, Gross1, Carbonell); future work aims to apply this method to deuteron generalized parton distribution functions.
