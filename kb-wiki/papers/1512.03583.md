# Effect of $Z_b$ states on $Υ(3S)\toΥ(1S)ππ$ decays

> Within the framework of dispersion theory, we analyze the dipion transitions between the lightest $Υ$ states, $Υ(nS) \rightarrow Υ(mS) ππ$ with $m < n \leq 3$. In particular, we consider the possible effects of two intermediate bottomoniumlike exotic states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$. The $ππ$ rescattering effects are taken into account in a model-independent way using dispersion theory. We confirm that matching the dispersive representation to the leading chiral amplitude alone cannot reproduce the peculiar two-peak $ππ$ mass spectrum of the decay $Υ(3S) \rightarrow Υ(1S) ππ$. The existence of the bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states can naturally explain this anomaly. We also point out the necessity of a proper extraction of the coupling strengths for the $Z_b$ states to $Υ(nS)π$, which is only possible if a Flatté-like parametrization is used in the data analysis for the $Z_b$ states.

## Key claim
The two-peak structure in the $\pi\pi$ mass spectrum of $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$ cannot be reproduced by matching the dispersive representation to the leading chiral amplitude alone; the existence of the bottomoniumlike $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ states can naturally explain this anomaly, but a proper extraction of their coupling strengths to $\Upsilon(nS)\pi$ requires a Flatté-like parametrization rather than a Breit-Wigner form.

## Method
The authors use dispersion theory with modified Omnès solutions to incorporate $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions in a model-independent way. The $Z_b$-exchange amplitudes provide left-hand-cut contributions to the dispersion integral, and the decay amplitude is determined up to subtraction functions matched to the leading chiral tree-level amplitude. The formalism is applied to fit the $\pi\pi$ invariant mass distributions and helicity angular distributions for $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$, $\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$, and $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(2S)\pi\pi$ using CLEO data.

## Key numbers
- $M(Z_b(10610)) = (10607.4 \pm 2.0)$ MeV, $\Gamma(Z_b(10610)) = (18.4 \pm 2.4)$ MeV
- $M(Z_b(10650)) = (10652.2 \pm 1.5)$ MeV, $\Gamma(Z_b(10650)) = (11.5 \pm 2.2)$ MeV
- Naive coupling products (from Breit-Wigner branching fractions): $|C_{31,1}^\text{naive}| = (0.014 \pm 0.004)$ GeV$^2$ for $Z_b(10610)$, $|C_{31,2}^\text{naive}| = (0.004 \pm 0.001)$ GeV$^2$ for $Z_b(10650)$
- Expected magnitude of $|C_{31,1}|$ from Flatté-like extraction: $\mathcal{O}(0.1$ GeV$^2)$
- Fitted low-energy constants for $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$: $c_1 = -0.025 \pm 0.001$, $c_2 = 0.026 \pm 0.001$, $C_{nm,1} = 0.10 \pm 0.01$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of dipion transitions between heavy quarkonia, where the QCD multipole expansion with soft-pion theorems (Cornell group, 1978; Orsay group, 1973) could not explain the two-hump anomaly in $\Upsilon(3S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$. Previous proposed mechanisms included coupled-channel effects (Lipkin, 1988; Kuang, 1991), a hypothetical $\Upsilon\pi$ resonance (Voloshin, 1983; Zou, 1995; Guo, 2005), and $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions (Komada; Uehara; Moxhay, 1989). The discovery of the $Z_b$ states by Belle (2011, 2012) provides a concrete realization of the resonance mechanism. The results enable future extraction of $Z_b$ coupling constants using Flatté parametrizations, as recently proposed by Hanhart (2015).
