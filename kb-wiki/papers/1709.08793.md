# Proton pairing in neutron stars from chiral effective field theory

> We study the ${}^{1}S_0$ proton pairing gap in beta-equilibrated neutron star matter within the framework of chiral effective field theory. We focus on the role of three-body forces, which strongly modify the effective proton-proton spin-singlet interaction in dense matter. We find that three-body forces generically reduce both the size of the pairing gap and the maximum density at which proton pairing may occur. The pairing gap is computed within BCS theory, and model uncertainties are estimated by varying the nuclear potential and the choice of single-particle spectrum in the gap equation. We find that a second-order perturbative treatment of the single-particle spectrum suppresses the proton ${}^{1}S_0$ pairing gap relative to the use of a free spectrum. We estimate the critical temperature for the onset of proton superconductivity to be $T_c = (3.7 - 6.0)\times 10^{9} $ K, which is consistent with previous theoretical results in the literature and marginally within the range deduced from a recent Bayesian analysis of neutron star cooling observations.

## Key claim

Three-body forces generically reduce both the size of the proton pairing gap and the maximum density at which proton pairing may occur in beta-equilibrated neutron star matter, but the magnitude of this reduction depends on the treatment of the single-particle spectrum and the chiral potential cutoff.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for the \(^{1}S_0\) proton pairing gap using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N2LO and N3LO, including N2LO three-body forces whose low-energy constants are fitted to A=3 nuclei. The proton single-particle energy was computed at three levels of approximation: free spectrum, Hartree-Fock (first-order), and self-consistent second-order perturbation theory, with the effective in-medium NN interaction derived by normal-ordering the three-body force. The proton fraction in beta-equilibrated matter was determined self-consistently from the equation of state computed at second order in perturbation theory, and the gap equation was solved using a modified Broyden method.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the onset of proton superconductivity is estimated to be \(T_c = (3.2 - 5.1)\times 10^{9}\) K.
- The peak in the pairing gap occurs within a narrow window of proton Fermi momentum \(0.35\,{\rm fm}^{-1} < k_F^p < 0.43\,{\rm fm}^{-1}\) for all chiral potentials considered.
- The maximum pairing gap from the n3lo450 potential including three-body forces is roughly 1/3 the \(^{1}S_0\) neutron pairing gap in neutron star inner crusts.
- For the n3lo450 potential with a free spectrum and three-body forces, the maximum pairing gap is reduced by a factor of four compared to the case without three-body forces.
- Proton effective masses at \(k_F^p=0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) range from \(M_p^*/M_p = 0.76\) to 0.82 in the Hartree-Fock approximation and from 0.87 to 0.98 in the second-order approximation across the five chiral potentials.
- The proton fraction at twice saturation density ranges from \(Y_p = 4-6\%\) for N3LO potentials and \(Y_p = 7.5-8.5\%\) for N2LO potentials.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton pairing in neutron star cores (Chao 1972, Takatsuka 1973, Amundsen 1984, Baldo 1992, Chen 1993, Elgaroy 1996) by employing chiral effective field theory potentials with consistent three-body forces, rather than phenomenological interactions like the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential used by Zuo (2004). The results are consistent with the range deduced from a recent Bayesian analysis of neutron star cooling observations and enable more consistent modeling of the equation of state and nucleonic pairing for neutron star cooling calculations.
