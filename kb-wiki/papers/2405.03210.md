# Magnetic dipole transition in proton-deuteron radiative capture at BBN energies within potential model

> The $pd$ radiative capture reaction plays a vital role in Big Bang nucleosynthesis and stellar proton-proton chain. The study of the low-energy reaction is challenging in both experiments and theories. Using the framework of potential model, we analyze $pd$ radiative capture below 1 MeV for both electric dipole ($E1$) and magnetic dipole ($M1$) transitions. The obtained astrophysical $S$ factors agree well with recent results, especially at energies relevant to sensitive deuterium abundance. The calculated reaction rate shows good agreement, with less than a 5\% difference compared to recent works. The extrapolated value for $S(0)$ including both transitions is determined to be $0.211 \pm 0.016$ eV b. A comparison with experimental data using the $χ^2$ test reveals the sensitivity of the $M1$ cross section at low energies to the scattering potential depth.

## Key claim
Using a potential model with Woods-Saxon potentials, the authors find that the magnetic dipole (M1) transition in proton-deuteron radiative capture is highly sensitive to the depth of the scattering potential, and that including both E1 and M1 transitions yields an extrapolated S(0) of 0.211 ± 0.016 eV b, which is about 4% lower than recent experimental values from Casella (2002) and Turkat (2021).

## Method
The authors model the pd system as a proton captured by a deuteron core using single-particle Woods-Saxon potentials for both bound and scattering states, with parameters derived from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (SLy5 interaction). They solve the radial Schrödinger equation for the bound state (1s₁/₂ proton in ³He with binding energy -5.49 MeV) and for scattering states (p-waves for E1, s-waves for M1), then compute the electromagnetic dipole transition matrix elements and astrophysical S-factors. The potential depth for the scattering states is optimized by χ² minimization against experimental S-factor data from multiple experiments below 1 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Extrapolated S(0) including both E1 and M1 transitions: 0.211 ± 0.016 eV b
- S_E1(0) = 0.098 eV b (with spectroscopic factor S_F = 0.9)
- S_M1(0) = 0.113 eV b (with S_F = 0.9)
- Optimal scattering potential depth V₀ˢ = -26.3 MeV for both E1 and M1 transitions
- Best-fit spectroscopic factor S_F = 0.9
- s-wave asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) for ³He: 1.79 fm⁻¹/² (squared value 3.2 fm⁻¹)
- At 66.7 keV: calculated S = 0.602 ± 0.042 eV b (experimental: 0.627 ± 0.025 eV b from Mossa (2020))
- At 99.5 keV: calculated S = 0.821 ± 0.073 eV b (experimental: 0.850 ± 0.029 eV b from Mossa (2020))
- M1 contribution at 80-100 keV: approximately 11% to 16% of total S-factor
- Calculated reaction rates differ by less than 5% from recent works (Mossa 2020, Moscoso 2021, Iliadis 2016, Coc 2015)

## Context
This work extends the potential model approach of Huang (2010), Xu (2013), and Ghasemi (2018) to specifically analyze the M1 transition sensitivity in pd radiative capture at BBN energies. The results are compared with ab initio calculations (Marcucci 2016, Viviani 2000), R-matrix analysis (Descouvemont 2004), and experimental data from LUNA (Casella 2002, Mossa 2020) and other groups. The study enables improved reaction rate inputs for BBN calculations, particularly at the 80 keV energy region where primordial deuterium abundance is most sensitive, and demonstrates that a simple two-body potential model can reproduce results from more complex few-body approaches.
