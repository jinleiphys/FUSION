# A Subleading Operator Basis and Matching for $gg \to H$

> The Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) is a powerful framework for studying factorization of amplitudes and cross sections in QCD. While factorization at leading power has been well studied, much less is known at subleading powers in the $λ\ll 1$ expansion. In SCET subleading soft and collinear corrections to a hard scattering process are described by power suppressed operators, which must be fixed case by case, and by well established power suppressed Lagrangians, which correct the leading power dynamics of soft and collinear radiation. Here we present a complete basis of power suppressed operators for $gg \to H$, classifying all operators which contribute to the cross section at $\mathcal{O}(λ^2)$, and showing how helicity selection rules significantly simplify the construction of the operator basis. We perform matching calculations to determine the tree level Wilson coefficients of our operators. These results are useful for studies of power corrections in both resummed and fixed order perturbation theory, and for understanding the factorization properties of gauge theory amplitudes and cross sections at subleading power. As one example, our basis of operators can be used to analytically compute power corrections for $N$-jettiness subtractions for $gg$ induced color singlet production at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete basis of power-suppressed hard scattering operators for the gluon-initiated production of a color singlet scalar (specifically the Higgs boson) at next-to-leading power in the Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) expansion, classifying all operators contributing to the cross section at order O(λ²) and performing tree-level matching to determine their Wilson coefficients, with the caveat that the basis is complete only within the SCET_I framework and does not include additional soft operators required in SCET_II.

## Method

The authors construct a complete basis of subleading power hard scattering operators for the process gg → H by using helicity operators in SCET, which simplifies the construction through helicity selection rules and makes symmetries manifest. They classify all operators that contribute to the cross section at O(λ²) in the SCET power expansion, including those with multiple collinear fields in the same collinear sector and insertions of the perpendicular label momentum operator P_⟂. Tree-level matching calculations are performed to determine the Wilson coefficients of the operators, using reparametrization invariance (RPI) symmetries to relate subleading power coefficients to the leading power Wilson coefficient.

## Key numbers

- The power expansion parameter λ scales such that λ² ∼ τ_B, where τ_B is the beam thrust event shape variable.
- The Higgs effective theory coupling is C₁(m_t, α_s)/(12π v) G^{μν} G_{μν} H, with v = (√2 G_F)^{-1/2} = 246 GeV.
- The matching coefficient C₁(m_t, α_s) is known to O(α_s³) from Chetyrkin (1997).
- The cross section expansion in powers of √τ_B is given as dσ/dτ_B = dσ⁽⁰⁾/dτ_B + dσ⁽¹⁾/dτ_B + dσ⁽²⁾/dτ_B + dσ⁽³⁾/dτ_B + O(τ), where odd orders (including dσ⁽¹⁾/dτ_B) are expected to vanish.
- The first non-vanishing power correction arises from dσ⁽²⁾/dτ_B, which contains all terms scaling as O(τ_B⁰).

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based analysis of subleading power corrections from the qq̅ initiated Drell-Yan case studied by Feige, Moult, Stewart, and Vita (2017) to the gluon-initiated production of a color singlet scalar (gg → H). The operator basis provides the groundwork for deriving factorization theorems at subleading power for gluon fusion processes, enabling analytic calculations of power corrections for N-jettiness subtractions at the LHC, which can improve the stability and numerical accuracy of NNLO fixed-order subtraction schemes as demonstrated by Moult, Stewart, and Vita (2016) for the qq̅ case.
