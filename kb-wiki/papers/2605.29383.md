# Bayesian constraints on the transport coefficients $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ from spin polarization in relativisitic heavy-ion collisions

> Bayesian analyses in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collisions have so far relied almost exclusively on bulk hadronic observables constructed from momentum degrees of freedom to constrain the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma. In this work, we perform the Bayesian inference after incorporating the longitudinal spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons alongside conventional bulk measurements in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV to constrain the shear and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratios, $η/s$ and $ζ/s$. We demonstrate that the inclusion of spin polarization, which provides complementary sensitivity to the space-time structure and vorticity of the medium, shifts the posterior distribution of $ζ/s$ toward larger values, although current uncertainties do not allow a statistically significant separation at the 68% credibility level. Nevertheless, the results establish spin polarization as a valuable probe in quantitative studies of QGP transport properties and indicate that it should be incorporated in comprehensive and systematically constrained Bayesian extractions of the medium's dynamical parameters.

## Key claim

Including longitudinal spin polarization of Λ hyperons alongside conventional bulk observables in a Bayesian analysis shifts the posterior distribution of the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ζ/s toward larger values, but current uncertainties prevent a statistically significant separation at the 68% credibility level.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with event-averaged initial profiles for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, using a lattice-QCD-based equation of state and a parameterized temperature dependence for η/s and ζ/s with three free parameters each. They constructed a Gaussian process emulator trained on 793 design points from a 13-dimensional parameter space, then used Markov Chain Monte Carlo to extract posterior distributions both with and without inclusion of longitudinal spin polarization data from the ALICE collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The analysis included 9 observables evaluated across 7 centrality classes, yielding an observable vector of dimension nobs = 63
- 3 principal components were retained, accounting for 97.06% of total variance when including spin polarization and 98.19% when excluding it
- The design consisted of 800 parameter sets, with 7 excluded leaving Ntot = 793 valid samples
- The maximum a posteriori (MAP) value for (ζ/s)max was 0.16 when including Pz, compared to 0.12 without Pz
- The MAP value for (ζ/s)width was 0.19 with Pz versus 0.07 without Pz
- The MAP value for (η/s)min was 0.07 with Pz and 0.06 without Pz
- The switching energy density εsw had MAP values of 0.54 GeV fm⁻³ with Pz and 0.48 GeV fm⁻³ without Pz

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of QGP transport coefficients (Bernhard 2016, Everett 2021, Parkkila 2021) that relied exclusively on momentum-space observables such as multiplicities, mean transverse momenta, and anisotropic flow coefficients. It builds on theoretical developments showing that longitudinal Λ polarization is particularly sensitive to bulk viscosity at LHC energies (Palermo 2024) and that spin polarization can modify effective transport coefficients (Wei 2025). The results establish spin polarization as a complementary probe for quantitative studies of QGP transport properties and motivate its inclusion in future comprehensive Bayesian extractions with event-by-event treatments and pre-equilibrium dynamics.
