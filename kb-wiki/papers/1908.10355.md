# Probing up-down quark matter via gravitational waves

> Recently, it was shown that quark matter with only $u$ and $d$ quarks ($ud$QM) can be the ground state of matter for baryon numbers $A>A_\textrm{min}$ with $A_{\rm min}\gtrsim 300$. In this paper, we explore $ud$ quark stars ($ud$QSs) that are composed of $ud$QM, in the context of the two-families scenario in which $ud$QSs and hadronic stars (HSs) can coexist. Distinct signatures are discussed compared to the conventional study regarding strange quark stars (SQSs). We show that the requirements of $A_{\rm min}\gtrsim 300$ and the most massive compact star observed being a $ud$QS together may put stringent constraints on the allowed parameter space of $ud$QSs. Then, we study the related gravitational-wave probe of the tidal deformability in binary star mergers, including the $ud$QS-$ud$QS and $ud$QS-HS cases. The obtained values of the tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses and the average tidal deformability are all in good compatibility with the experimental constraints of GW170817. This study points to a new possible interpretation of the GW170817 binary merger event, where $ud$QS may be at least one component of the binary system detected.

## Key claim

The paper argues that up-down quark stars (udQSs) can be viable components of binary mergers like GW170817, but only if the effective bag constant is tightly constrained to approximately 50 MeV/fm³, with a conservative range of 45–55 MeV/fm³, based on the dual requirements that udQM be absolutely stable only for baryon numbers above 300 and that the most massive observed compact star be a udQS.

## Method

The author uses a linear equation of state p = 1/3 (ρ − ρ_s) for udQM, with the effective bag constant B_eff as the key parameter. The Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved with a dimensionless rescaling method to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for udQSs. For the two-families scenario, the average tidal deformability of binary mergers is computed for both udQS–udQS and udQS–hadronic star (HS) cases, using three benchmark hadronic equations of state (SLy, Bsk19, Bsk21) for the HS branch.

## Key numbers

- The minimum baryon number for absolute stability of udQM is A_min ≳ 300, corresponding to E/A ≳ 903 MeV or B_eff ≳ 50 MeV/fm³.
- The maximum mass constraint from the observed pulsar J0740+6620 (M ≈ 2.14 M_⊙) gives B_eff ≲ 50.3 MeV/fm³, with a central upper bound of 50.3 MeV/fm³ and uncertainties of +4.5/−4.4 MeV/fm³.
- The conservative allowed window for B_eff in udQSs is [45, 55] MeV/fm³.
- For a 1.4 M_⊙ udQS with B_eff in the allowed range, the tidal deformability Λ(1.4 M_⊙) lies in [530, 857], with a central value of 670 for B_eff = 50 MeV/fm³.
- For the udQS–udQS merger case with GW170817 chirp mass M_c = 1.186 M_⊙, the average tidal deformability constraint Λ̃ = 300^{+420}_{-230} translates to a mass ratio q in 0.4–1.0, and specifically q = 0.74 for B_eff = 50 MeV/fm³.
- The upper bound on Λ̃ and the mass ratio q ≳ 0.73 further constrain B_eff to [49.5, 55] MeV/fm³ for the udQS–udQS case.

## Context

This work extends the two-families scenario previously developed for strange quark stars (SQSs) by Drago (2013) and others, but focuses on udQM which is more stable than SQM in the bulk limit according to the quark-meson model of Holdom, Ren, and Zhang (2017). The study provides a new interpretation of the GW170817 event, suggesting that at least one component could be a udQS rather than a neutron star, which differs from conventional one-family analyses (Annala 2017, Fattoyev 2017, De 2018) that assume all compact stars follow hadronic equations of state. The results enable future gravitational-wave events to be used to further constrain the udQS parameter space and distinguish between the udQS–udQS and udQS–HS merger scenarios.
