# Ambiguities in Partial Wave Analysis of Two Spinless Meson Photoproduction

> We describe the formalism to analyze the mathematical ambiguities arising in partial-wave analysis of two spinless mesons produced with a linearly polarized photon beam. We show that partial waves are uniquely defined when all accessible observables are considered, for a wave set which includes $S$ and $D$ waves. The inclusion of higher partial waves does not affect our results, and we conclude that there are no mathematical ambiguities in partial-wave analysis of two mesons produced with a linearly polarized photon beam. We present Monte Carlo simulations to illustrate our results.

## Key claim
The paper claims that there are no mathematical ambiguities in partial-wave analysis of two spinless mesons produced with a linearly polarized photon beam, but this result is conditional on using all accessible observables (including polarized moments) and is explicitly demonstrated only for wave sets containing S and D waves; the authors argue the conclusion generalizes to higher partial waves but do not provide a formal proof for all possible wave sets.

## Method
The authors develop a formalism based on Barrelet zeros to analyze ambiguities in the partial-wave expansion of photoproduction amplitudes for two spinless mesons. They express the angular intensity distributions as polynomials in tan(theta/2), factorize these polynomials into roots, and test whether complex conjugation of subsets of these roots can produce different partial-wave sets that yield identical observables. The analysis is performed separately for each reflectivity component, using the unpolarized and polarized moments accessible with a linearly polarized photon beam.

## Key numbers
- For the wave set {S0, D0, D1} with positive reflectivity, there are three linearly independent moments (h0^0, h1^0, h0^1) and two Barrelet zeros (r1, r2) from the polynomial g0; the polarized moment h0^1 depends on a separate polynomial g0-g1 with one independent Barrelet zero (r3 = sqrt(2)B/A), which breaks the ambiguity.
- For the wave set {S0, D0, D1, D-1} with positive reflectivity, there are three unpolarized moments (h0^0, h1^0, h2^0) and the matrices H0^0, H1^0, H2^0 are not simultaneously diagonalizable, preventing ambiguous solutions.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of mathematical ambiguities in partial-wave analysis, particularly the treatment of Barrelet zeros by Barrelet (1971) and the analysis of pion-beam production processes by Chung (1997). The formalism is developed specifically for the GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab, which uses linearly polarized photon beams to study light exotic mesons in final states such as eta pi. The paper enables unambiguous extraction of D-wave components (e.g., from the a2(1320) resonance) before isolating weaker exotic P-wave signals, addressing a problem noted by the COMPASS collaboration (2014) that no ambiguities are found once m=2 components are included.
