# Non-Global Logarithms, Factorization, and the Soft Substructure of Jets

> An outstanding problem in QCD and jet physics is the factorization and resummation of logarithms that arise due to phase space constraints, so-called non-global logarithms (NGLs). In this paper, we show that NGLs can be factorized and resummed down to an unresolved infrared scale by making sufficiently many measurements on a jet or other restricted phase space region. Resummation is accomplished by renormalization group evolution of the objects in the factorization theorem and anomalous dimensions can be calculated to any perturbative accuracy and with any number of colors. To connect with the NGLs of more inclusive measurements, we present a novel perturbative expansion which is controlled by the volume of the allowed phase space for unresolved emissions. Arbitrary accuracy can be obtained by making more and more measurements so to resolve lower and lower scales. We find that even a minimal number of measurements produces agreement with Monte Carlo methods for leading-logarithmic resummation of NGLs at the sub-percent level over the full dynamical range relevant for the Large Hadron Collider. We also discuss other applications of our factorization theorem to soft jet dynamics and how to extend to higher-order accuracy.

## Key claim

Non-global logarithms (NGLs) can be factorized and resummed down to an unresolved infrared scale by making sufficiently many measurements on a jet, with even a minimal number of measurements producing agreement with Monte Carlo methods for leading-logarithmic resummation at the sub-percent level over the full dynamical range relevant for the Large Hadron Collider.

## Method

The authors identify a jet in e+e- collisions using a broadening axis cone algorithm with fixed radius R, then measure the two-point energy correlation functions e2^(alpha) and e2^(beta) and the three-point energy correlation function e3^(alpha) on the jet to isolate the "soft subjet" region of phase space where a single soft subjet lies near the jet boundary. They construct a factorization theorem in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) that separates in-jet scales from out-of-jet scales, enabling resummation of NGLs via renormalization group evolution of the factorized soft function. The "dressed gluon approximation" is introduced, where each dressed gluon corresponds to resolving one additional soft subjet, and the expansion is controlled by the volume of allowed phase space for unresolved emissions.

## Key numbers

* Even a minimal number of measurements produces agreement with Monte Carlo methods for leading-logarithmic resummation of NGLs at the sub-percent level over the full dynamical range relevant for the Large Hadron Collider.
* The soft subjet region is parametrically defined by e3^(alpha) << (e2^(alpha))^3, where e2^(alpha) ~ e2^(beta) << 1.
* The jet radius R is taken as an order-1 number, with the specific case R = pi/2 dividing the event into hemispheres.
* The out-of-jet observable B is required to satisfy B << 1, enforcing soft and collinear dynamics to dominate the out-of-jet region.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-global logarithms first identified by Dasgupta and Salam (2001), which had previously resisted systematic factorization and resummation beyond the large-Nc limit using the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye (BMS) equation. The approach enables resummation to any perturbative accuracy and for any number of colors Nc, and provides an analytic understanding of the "buffer region" near the jet boundary where emissions are forbidden. The factorization theorem can be incorporated into existing multi-jet factorization theorems and extended to higher-order accuracy.
