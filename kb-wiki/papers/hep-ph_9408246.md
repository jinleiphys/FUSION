# Scatttering of Weakly Interacting Massive Particles from Ge73

> We use a "hybrid" method, mixing variationally-determined triaxial nuclear Slater determinants, to calculate the response of Ge73 to hypothetical dark-matter particles such as neutralinos. The method is a hybrid in that rotational invariance is approximately restored prior to variation and then fully restored before the mixing of intrinsic states. We discuss such features of Ge73 as shape coexistence and triaxiality, and their effects on spin-dependent neutralino cross sections. Our calculations yield a satisfactory quadrupole moment and an accurate magnetic moment in this very complicated nucleus, suggesting that the spin structure and thus the axial--vector response to dark matter particles is modeled well.

## Key claim

The authors calculate spin-dependent neutralino cross sections for Ge73 using a "hybrid" variational method that mixes triaxial Slater determinants, and they claim their results are the most reliable yet obtained because their calculation reproduces the experimental magnetic moment to within 10% without requiring ad-hoc quenching, unlike the shell-model calculation of Ressell et al. (1993) which overestimates the magnetic moment by roughly 30%.

## Method

The authors use a "hybrid" method that mixes variationally-determined triaxial nuclear Slater determinants, where rotational invariance is approximately restored via a Kamlah-type projection before variation and then fully restored before mixing the intrinsic states. The single-particle space includes the full 0f,1p shell plus the 0g9/2 and 0g7/2 orbitals for both protons and neutrons. The two-body interaction is derived from a Paris-potential G-matrix with all monopole components removed and their effects shifted to single-particle energies, which are determined from BCS fits to spherical quasi-particle energies in mass-71 isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Ground state magnetic moment of Ge73: -0.920 n.m. (calculated) versus -0.88 n.m. (experimental), compared to -1.24 n.m. from Ressell et al. (1993) and -1.91 n.m. from the Schmidt value
- Ground state quadrupole moment: -40.98 e-fm2 (calculated) versus -21 e-fm2 (experimental)
- Neutron spin expectation value: 0.378 (present work) versus 0.491 (Ressell et al. 1993)
- Proton spin expectation value: 0.030 (present work) versus 0.011 (Ressell et al. 1993)
- Mixing of the two higher intrinsic states lowers the ground state energy by 0.95 MeV
- Polynomial fits for spin structure functions S00(y), S11(y), and S01(y) are provided as sixth-order polynomials in y = (bq/2)2 with b = 2.04 fm

## Context

This work extends the EXCITED VAMPIR method studies of even germanium isotopes by Petrovici et al. (1988, 1990) to the odd-mass system Ge73, which VAMPIR cannot treat due to its BCS-like pairing treatment. The results contradict the large-basis shell-model calculation of Ressell et al. (1993), which obtained a magnetic moment of -1.24 n.m. and required quenching of the isovector spin only; the present work achieves the full experimental quenching without arbitrary assumptions. The polynomial fits to the spin structure functions enable calculation of spin-dependent neutralino cross sections at any momentum transfer, and the authors note that future improvements should include explicit incorporation of the remaining 2s,1d,0g shell orbits.
