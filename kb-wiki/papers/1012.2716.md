# Pions are neither perturbative nor nonperturbative: Wilsonian renormalization group analysis of nuclear effective field theory including pions

> Pionful nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) in the two-nucleon sector is examined from the Wilsonian renormalization group point of view. The pion exchange is cut off at the floating cutoff scale, $Λ$, with the short-distance part being represented as contact interactions in accordance with the general principle of renormalization. We derive the nonperturbative renormalization group equations in the leading order of the nonrelativistic approximation in the operator space including up to $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ and find the nontrivial fixed points in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$--$^3D_1$ channels which are identified with those in the pionless NEFT. The scaling dimensions, which determine the power counting, of the contact interactions at the nontrivial fixed points are also identified with those in the pionless NEFT. We emphasize the importance of the separation of the pion exchange into the short-distance and the long-distance parts, since a part of the former is nonperturbative while the latter is perturbative.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the question of whether pions should be treated perturbatively or nonperturbatively in nuclear effective field theory is ill-posed, because the short-distance part of the one-pion exchange should be represented as contact interactions while only the long-distance part remains as explicit pion exchange; at the nontrivial fixed points of the Wilsonian renormalization group, the long-distance part of the pion exchange is perturbative while a relevant contact operator (which includes the short-distance part of the pion exchange) must be resummed nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors perform a Wilsonian renormalization group analysis of pionful nuclear effective field theory in the two-nucleon sector, imposing a sharp cutoff on the relative three-momentum of the two nucleons to preserve Galilean invariance. They derive nonperturbative renormalization group equations at leading order in the nonrelativistic approximation, truncating the operator space to include contact interactions up to order p^2 in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels, with the pion exchange separated into short-distance and long-distance parts relative to the floating cutoff scale Lambda.

## Key numbers

- The nontrivial fixed point in the spin-singlet channel is (x*, y*, z*) = (-1, -1/2, 1/2), where x, y, z are dimensionless couplings for the leading contact interaction, the derivative contact interaction, and the redundant operator respectively.
- The nontrivial fixed point in the spin-triplet channel is (x'*, y'*, z'*, w'*) = (-1, -1/2, 1/2, 0), where w' is the dimensionless coupling for the tensor operator.
- At these nontrivial fixed points, there is exactly one relevant operator in each channel, with the scaling dimension of the relevant coupling being one.
- The physical cutoff Lambda_0 is taken to be of order 400 MeV, larger than the pion mass m_pi.

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian renormalization group analysis of pionless nuclear effective field theory (Harada 2006, Harada 2007) to include pions as dynamical degrees of freedom, and addresses the controversy over power counting in pionful NEFT raised by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996, 1998) and the failure of the KSW scheme in tensor channels found by Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (1999). The approach is closely related to the Pauli-Villars regularization prescription of Beane, Kaplan, and Vuorinen (2008) for separating the short-distance part of the tensor interaction, but uses the Wilsonian RG framework to provide a systematic treatment. The results enable a smooth transition from pionful to pionless NEFT as the cutoff is lowered below the pion mass, and contradict the claim by Birse (2005) that pions do not decouple at very low momenta.
