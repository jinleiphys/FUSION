# Jastrow Two-nucleon Overlap Functions and Cross Sections of $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}NN)^{14}$C Reactions

> Using the relationship between the two-particle overlap functions (TOF's) and the two-body density matrix (TDM), the TOF's for $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C reaction are calculated on the basis of TDM obtained with a Jastrow-type approach. The main contributions of the removal of $^1S$ and $^3P$ $pp$-pairs from $^{16}$O are taken into account in the calculations of the cross sections of the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C reaction using the Jastrow TOF's. The contributions of the one-body and two-body delta currents are considered. The results are compared with the calculations using TOF's from other approaches.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that two-nucleon overlap functions (TOFs) extracted from a Jastrow-type two-body density matrix (TDM) that includes only short-range correlations (SRC) can reproduce the main qualitative features of cross sections for the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C reaction, though the Jastrow TDM is not "rich" enough to explain transitions to all excited states of $^{14}$C realistically.

## Method

The authors calculate two-proton overlap functions for $^{16}$O using the relationship between TOFs and the ground-state two-body density matrix, applying the asymptotic procedure of ADS+99 to extract TOFs from a Jastrow TDM obtained via the low-order approximation (LOA) of the Jastrow correlation method. These TOFs are then used as input to compute differential cross sections of the $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime}pp)^{14}$C knockout reaction in the one-photon exchange approximation, considering contributions from both one-body and two-body delta currents. The results are compared with cross sections calculated using TOFs from a spectral function approach (GAD+96, GPA+98) that includes both SRC and long-range correlations (LRC), and with a simpler shell-model-plus-correlation approach (SM+CORR).

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the $^1S_0$ overlap function: 0.958 (Jastrow case).
- Spectroscopic factor for the $^3P_1$ overlap function: 0.957 (Jastrow case).
- Spectroscopic factor for the total TOF: 0.965 (Jastrow case), compared to unity in the uncorrelated case.
- Spectroscopic factor for the $^3P_1$ state in the transition to the $1^+$ excited state of $^{14}$C: 0.967 (Jastrow case), compared to unity in the uncorrelated case.
- Kinematics for the super-parallel setting: incident electron energy $E_0 = 855$ MeV, energy transfer $\omega = 215$ MeV, momentum transfer $q = 316$ MeV/$c$.
- The $^1S_0$ component dominates at low recoil momentum up to $\sim 150-200$ MeV/$c$; the $^3P_1$ component dominates for $p_\mathrm{B} \geq 200$ MeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the method of ADS+99 for extracting TOFs from the TDM, previously applied to one-nucleon overlap functions, to the two-nucleon case for $^{16}$O. It contrasts with the more refined spectral function approach of GAD+96 and GPA+98, which includes both SRC and LRC, and with the simpler SM+CORR approach of GP97. The results enable future application of the TDM-based method to a wider range of residual nuclear states and to more sophisticated TDM calculations, such as those from PWP92, Hei+99, Co+00, and Mut+00.
