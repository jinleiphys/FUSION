# The $^4$He$(e,e^\prime p)^3$H Reaction with Full Final--State Interaction

> An {\it ab initio} calculation of the $^4$He$(e,e^\prime p)^3$H longitudinal response is presented. The use of the integral transform method with a Lorentz kernel has allowed to take into account the full four--body final state interaction (FSI). The semirealistic nucleon-nucleon potential MTI--III and the Coulomb force are the only ingredients of the calculation. The reliability of the direct knock--out hypothesis is discussed both in parallel and in non parallel kinematics. In the former case it is found that lower missing momenta and higher momentum transfers are preferable to minimize effects beyond the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA). Also for non parallel kinematics the role of antisymmetrization and final state interaction become very important with increasing missing momentum, raising doubts about the possibility of extracting momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors. The comparison with experimental results in parallel kinematics, where the Rosenbluth separation has been possible, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an *ab initio* calculation of the longitudinal response for the ⁴He(e,e′p)³H reaction, finding that final-state interaction (FSI) enhances the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results at higher missing momenta, contrary to previous estimates based on approximate approaches. The authors conclude that the reliability of extracting momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors is questionable, especially at higher missing momenta, because both antisymmetrization and FSI effects become very important.

## Method

The authors used the integral transform method with a Lorentz kernel (LIT) to calculate the longitudinal response function for the ⁴He(e,e′p)³H reaction, treating the full four-body final-state interaction. They employed the semirealistic nucleon-nucleon potential MTI–III and the Coulomb force, expanding the wave functions over a basis of correlated hyperspherical harmonics multiplied by hyperradial functions. The transition matrix elements were separated into a Born term (PWIAS) and an FSI-dependent term, with the latter obtained by solving two inhomogeneous Schrödinger-like equations using the Lanczos technique and subsequent inversion of the Lorentz integral transform.

## Key numbers

*   The spectroscopic factor for the S-shell of ⁴He with the MTI–III potential is 0.88, compared to 0.84 obtained with the realistic AV18 potential and Urbana IX.
*   For kinematics close to the ω = q²/(2mₚ) ridge, FSI effects decrease for increasing q; at higher momenta and lower energy side of the ridge, FSI enhances PWIA results.
*   The comparison with experimental data shows that theoretical results are systematically higher than data, with differences ranging from about 30% for kinematics near the quasi-elastic ridge to 70–100% for other kinematics.
*   For non-parallel kinematics, antisymmetrization effects prevent interpretation of the response in terms of momentum distribution beyond missing momenta of around 1 fm⁻¹ for lower momentum transfer kinematics and up to 2 fm⁻¹ for higher q kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to total photoabsorption in ⁴He (Belyaev et al., 2001) and two-body breakup of the four-body system (Quaglioni et al., 2004), to the exclusive (e,e′p) reaction with full final-state interaction. The results contradict earlier estimates based on optical potentials and orthogonalization procedures (Schiavilla, 1990) or diagrammatic expansions (Laget, 1989), which had predicted FSI effects in the opposite direction. The findings are consistent with previous *ab initio* calculations of inclusive longitudinal response in ²H (Alt et al., 2005), ³He (Golak et al., 2005), and ⁴He (Efros et al., 1997). The work enables future studies using realistic potentials and raises questions about the validity of extracting spectroscopic factors and momentum distributions from (e,e′p) reactions in heavier nuclei.
