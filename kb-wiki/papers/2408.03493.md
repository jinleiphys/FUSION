# Effect of neutrino electromagnetic properties on the quasielastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering

> In the quasielastic region, we investigate the effect of neutrino electromagnetic properties constrained from the recent experiments on the electroweak neutral current reaction process of the neutrino-$^{12}$C scattering. For a relativistic description of the nuclear dynamics, we employ the relativistic mean-field model, which has been proven to describe the data nicely in the quasielastic region. In the present work, we analyze the influence beyond the Standard Model by considering the neutrino magnetic and electric dipole form factors and charge radius on the neutrino electroweak interactions within $^{12}$C. To this end, we use the values of the neutrino charge radius and the magnetic moment at the squared four momentum transfer $Q^2=0$ obtained from the recent experiments and calculate the neutrino differential cross section of the neutrino-$^{12}$C scattering. We find that the effect of the charge radius and the electric dipole form factor is very small, but the role of the magnetic dipole form factor is sensitive to $Q^2$ and becomes sizable at small momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The neutrino magnetic dipole form factor has a sizable effect on the quasielastic neutral-current neutrino-\(^{12}\)C scattering cross section at low momentum transfer, while the charge radius and electric dipole form factor effects are very small; however, the magnetic moment's enhancement is large enough that with the largest experimental upper limit it overestimates MiniBooNE data at low \(Q^2\), whereas the smallest experimental upper limit reduces but does not fully close the gap between theory and experiment.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean-field (quantum hadrodynamics) model to describe the nuclear dynamics of \(^{12}\)C in the quasielastic region. They computed the single differential cross section for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering by including weak, electromagnetic, and interference contributions to the lepton and hadron tensors, using experimental values for the neutrino charge radius and magnetic moment at \(Q^2=0\) from the CE\(\nu\)NS (Dresden-II/COHERENT) and GEMMA experiments, and from BNL-E734. The cross section was evaluated as a function of the outgoing nucleon kinetic energy and of the squared four-momentum transfer, and compared with MiniBooNE flux-averaged data.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino magnetic moment values used: \(\mu_{\nu_e} = 2.13 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B\) (CE\(\nu\)NS/Dresden-II) and \(\mu_{\nu_e} = 2.9 \times 10^{-11} \mu_B\) (GEMMA, smallest upper limit).
- Neutrino charge radius values used: \(\langle R^2 \rangle = 5 \times 10^{-32}\) cm\(^2\) (CE\(\nu\)NS/Dresden-II) and \(\langle R^2 \rangle = 0.48 \times 10^{-32}\) cm\(^2\) (BNL-E734, smallest value).
- With the large magnetic moment (\(2.13 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B\)), the proton cross section increases by a factor larger than 10 relative to the small magnetic moment case at \(E_i = 250\) MeV and low \(T_N\).
- With the small magnetic moment (\(2.9 \times 10^{-11} \mu_B\)), the electromagnetic contribution amounts to about 60% of the weak contribution at small momentum transfer.
- The effect of the charge radius on the total cross section is less than 1%.
- The ratio of the two magnetic moment values is about 7.3, and the EM contribution enhancement scales approximately as the square of that ratio (\(\sim 53\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering using the relativistic mean-field model (Horowitz, 1998; Kim et al., 2021, 2022) by incorporating neutrino electromagnetic properties beyond the Standard Model. It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical predictions using the standard axial mass \(M_A = 1.032\) GeV and MiniBooNE data (MiniBooNE Collaboration, 2010), which had previously required consideration of meson-exchange currents, 2p-2h excitations, and final-state interactions (Sobczyk, 2013; Kim et al., 2013). The results show that neutrino magnetic moments at current experimental limits can partially close the gap at low momentum transfer, but inelastic processes remain necessary at high \(Q^2\). This enables future work to combine electromagnetic properties with inelastic channels for a more complete description.
