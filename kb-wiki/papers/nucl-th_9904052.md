# Perturbative Pions and the Effective Range Expansion

> In this talk the $Q$ counting scheme to implement effective field theory is discussed. It is pointed out that there are two small mass scales in the problem $m_π$ and $1/a$ with $1/a \ll m_π$. It is argued that while the expansion based on $1/a$ being small compared to the underlying short distance scales works well, the chiral expansion may not. The coefficients of the effective range expansion are sensitive to the chiral physics and are very poorly described in $Q$ counting at lowest nontrivial order. A ``shape function'' is introduced which again is sensitive to pionic physics and insensitive to fitting procedures. It is also poorly described in $Q$ counting.

## Key claim

The Q counting scheme for effective field theory in nuclear physics involves two small mass scales (1/a and m_π), and while the expansion based on 1/a being small compared to short-distance scales works well, the chiral expansion may not converge because m_π may be comparable to the short-distance scale Λ rather than well separated from it.

## Method

The author analyzes the Q counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW) for nucleon-nucleon scattering, focusing on the effective range expansion (ERE) of p cot(δ) as a power series in energy. The calculation is performed using a cutoff method in configuration space, where the potential is divided into a short-distance part (vanishing beyond a matching radius R) and a long-distance part (vanishing inside R), with the one-pion-exchange potential treated explicitly. The resulting expression for p cot(δ) at order Q² is expanded to obtain low-energy theorems for the ERE coefficients v₂, v₃, v₄, and a "shape function" S(p) is introduced by subtracting the first two terms of the ERE to isolate pion-dominated contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹S₀ channel: predicted v₂ = -3.3 fm³, v₃ = 17.8 fm⁵, v₄ = -108 fm⁷; from Nijmegen partial wave analysis: v₂ = -0.48 fm³, v₃ = 3.8 fm⁵, v₄ = -17 fm⁷
- For the ³S₁ channel: predicted v₂ = -0.95 fm³, v₃ = 4.6 fm⁵, v₄ = -25 fm⁷; from Nijmegen partial wave analysis: v₂ = 0.04 fm³, v₃ = 0.67 fm⁵, v₄ = -4.0 fm⁷
- Empirical values: m_π a ≈ 4 for triplet channel, m_π a ≈ 16 for singlet channel; 1/m_π ≈ 1.5 fm; singlet scattering length a^s ≈ -23 fm; triplet scattering length a^t ≈ 6 fm; singlet effective range r_e^s ≈ 2.7 fm; triplet effective range r_e^t ≈ 1.6 fm
- Shape function S(p) for ³S₁ at lab energy 50 MeV: extracted value 11.03 ± 0.24 MeV versus low-energy theorem prediction -20.10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Q counting scheme introduced by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the cutoff implementation by Cohen and Hansen, while challenging the assumption that the chiral expansion converges at the same rate as the 1/a expansion. The analysis shows that the low-energy theorems for ERE coefficients disagree with results from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and potential models (Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, Reid 93) by factors of roughly 5, suggesting the chiral part of Q counting may not be under control. The shape function provides a cleaner test than the ERE coefficients themselves, and the results indicate that higher-order calculations are needed to determine whether the chiral expansion converges or fails systematically.
