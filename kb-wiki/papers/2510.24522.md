# Spectrum and electromagnetic properties of $^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ in the Geometric $α$-cluster Model with $\mathcal{D}_{4h}$ symmetry at leading order

> The relevance of the point-symmetry group $\mathcal{D}_{4h}$ for the prediction of spectrum and electromagnetic properties of the $^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ nucleus is discussed in the framework of the geometric $α$-cluster model at leading order. The latter represents a macroscopic $α$-cluster framework wherein nuclear excitations are described in terms of rotations and vibrations of $^4\mathrm{He}$ clusters about their equilibrium positions, at the vertices of a square bipyramid. The finite group associated with the latter regulates the composition of the rotational bands as well as the transitions between the energy levels, by means of additional selection rules, of molecular nature. A sample of reduced electric multipole transition probabilities of intraband nature is provided.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the point-symmetry group D4h, associated with a square bipyramidal alpha-cluster equilibrium configuration, provides a valid leading-order description of the spectrum and electromagnetic properties of 24Mg, but only under the condition that rotation-vibration coupling is neglected and the rigid-rotor limit is taken; higher-order corrections will break this symmetry and induce triaxiality.

## Method

The geometric alpha-cluster model (GalphaCM) is applied to 24Mg, treating the nucleus as six 4He clusters rotating and vibrating about equilibrium positions at the vertices of a square bipyramid. The leading-order Hamiltonian decouples rotations from vibrations, yielding eigenstates that factorize into rotational Wigner D-matrices and harmonic oscillator vibrational wavefunctions, with the D4h point group imposing additional selection rules on allowed angular momenta and band compositions. Reduced electric multipole transition probabilities and form factors are calculated in the static limit, evaluating the transition operators at the equilibrium cluster positions.

## Key numbers

- Structure parameters (beta1, beta2) evaluated at (2.380, 3.857) fm, corresponding to a prolate shape consistent with the measured charge radius of the 0_1+ state and electric quadrupole moment of the 2_1+ state
- Static inertia tensor components: I_xx^stat = I_yy^stat = 2m(beta1^2 + beta2^2) and I_zz^stat = 4m(beta1^2), where m is approximately 3727.4 MeV (the alpha-particle mass)
- For a spherical Gaussian charge profile, the form factor uses alpha1 = 0.53 fm^{-2}
- The LO energy eigenvalues are given by E_LO(J,K,[n]) = (hbar^2/(2I_xx^stat))[J(J+1) - K^2] + (hbar^2 K^2)/(2I_zz^stat) - (hbar^2/8) sum_alpha I_alphaalpha^stat^{-1} + sum_i hbar omega_i (n_i + 1/2) + sum_i hbar omega_i (n_i + 1) for degenerate modes

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic alpha-cluster models applied to light alpha-conjugate nuclei such as 12C and 16O, and builds on the Bloch-Brink alpha-cluster model (Brink 1965) which first identified the square bipyramidal configuration for 24Mg. The D4h symmetry has roots in the literature (Bou62, NTD66) and appears in early applications of the Bloch-Brink model (BFW70). The paper contrasts with alternative proposals such as the D2h symmetry associated with a bitetrahedron (HWD71, Hau71), and complements recent ab-initio approaches including nuclear lattice effective field theory (EBM24) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations (KFT12, KiC14, KEO21). The results enable systematic improvement by including rotation-vibration coupling at next-to-leading order, which will break the D4h symmetry and induce triaxiality.
