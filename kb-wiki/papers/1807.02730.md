# Model-Independent Bounds on $R(J/ψ)$

> We present a model-independent bound on $R(J/ψ) \! \equiv \! \mathcal{BR} (B_c^+ \rightarrow J/ψ\, τ^+ν_τ)/ \mathcal{BR} (B_c^+ \rightarrow J/ψ\, μ^+ν_μ)$. This bound is constructed by constraining the form factors through a combination of dispersive relations, heavy-quark relations at zero-recoil, and the limited existing determinations from lattice QCD. The resulting 95\% confidence-level bound, $0.20\leq R(J/ψ)\leq0.39$, agrees with the recent LHCb result at $1.3 \, σ$, and rules out some previously suggested model form factors.

## Key claim
The paper derives a model-independent 95% confidence-level bound on the ratio \(R(J/\psi) = \mathcal{BR}(B_c^+ \rightarrow J/\psi\, \tau^+\nu_\tau)/\mathcal{BR}(B_c^+ \rightarrow J/\psi\, \mu^+\nu_\mu)\) within the Standard Model, finding \(0.20 \leq R(J/\psi) \leq 0.39\). This bound relies on combining dispersive relations, heavy-quark spin-symmetry relations at zero recoil, and limited lattice QCD data, but the authors note that the uncertainty is dominated by the unknown form factor \(\mathcal{F}_2(t)\) (related to \(A_0\)), and the result agrees with the LHCb measurement at \(1.3\,\sigma\).

## Method
The authors constrain the four transition form factors (\(V, A_0, A_1, A_2\) or equivalently \(g, f, \mathcal{F}_1, \mathcal{F}_2\)) for the decay \(B_c^+ \rightarrow J/\psi\) using a dispersive analysis based on the Boyd-Grinstein-Lebed (BGL) formalism, which enforces analyticity and unitarity via a conformal mapping to a variable \(z\). They incorporate heavy-quark spin-symmetry relations at the zero-recoil point (\(t = t_-\)) to relate \(g\), \(\mathcal{F}_1\), and \(\mathcal{F}_2\) to \(f\), allowing up to 50% violations of these relations, and use preliminary lattice QCD results from the HPQCD Collaboration for \(V(q^2)\) at two \(q^2\) values and \(A_1(q^2)\) at three \(q^2\) values. The maximum and minimum values of \(R(J/\psi)\) are then obtained by optimizing over the unknown form factors \(\mathcal{F}_1\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2\) subject to these constraints, without assuming a prior distribution.

## Key numbers
- The 95% confidence-level bound on \(R(J/\psi)\) is \(0.20 \leq R(J/\psi) \leq 0.39\), obtained with a 20% systematic uncertainty (\(f_{\rm lat}\)) on lattice data and truncation at \(n=2\) in the dispersive expansion.
- The semileptonic decay region in the conformal variable \(z\) has \(z_{\rm max,\tau} \approx 0.019\) and \(z_{\rm max,\mu} \approx 0.027\), with a 1.5% variation depending on the threshold used.
- The LHCb measurement of \(R(J/\psi) = 0.71(17)(18)\) (Aaij 2017) agrees with the bound at \(1.3\,\sigma\).
- The zero-recoil point corresponds to \(w=1\) (where \(w = v \cdot v'\)), and the maximum \(w\) values are \(w_{\rm max} \approx 1.28\) for muons and \(1.20\) for taus.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent dispersive approach previously applied to heavy-light decays (e.g., \(B \rightarrow D^{(*)}\ell\bar{\nu}\) by Boyd, Grinstein, and Lebed 1997) to the heavy-heavy decay \(B_c^+ \rightarrow J/\psi\), where heavy-quark flavor symmetry is lost but spin symmetry remains. The bound rules out some earlier model predictions (e.g., from nonrelativistic QCD by Qiao 2013 giving \(0.07^{+0.06}_{-0.04}\)) and provides a rigorous Standard-Model reference for the LHCb measurement (Aaij 2017). Future lattice QCD calculations of \(A_0(0)\) could reduce the uncertainty by half, enabling tighter tests of lepton universality.
