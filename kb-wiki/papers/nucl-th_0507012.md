# Improved quark mass density- dependent model with quark and non-linear scalar field coupling

> The improved quark mass density- dependent model which includes the coupling between the quarks and a non-linear scalar field is presented. Numerical analysis of solutions of the model is performed over a wide range of parameters. The wave functions of ground state and the lowest one-particle excited states with even and odd parity are given. The root-mean squared radius, the magnetic moment and the ratio between the axial-vector and the vector beta-decay coupling constants of the nucleon are calculated. We found that the present model is successful to describe the properties of nucleon.

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model, which couples quarks to a non-linear scalar field, can successfully describe nucleon properties (rms charge radius, magnetic moment, and axial-vector coupling ratio) without requiring the MIT bag boundary condition, but only for specific ranges of the phenomenological parameters \(c_4\) and coupling constant \(f\); for many parameter choices no self-consistent solution exists.

## Method

The authors extend the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model by introducing a coupling between \(u\) and \(d\) quarks and a non-linear scalar \(\sigma\) field with a self-interaction potential \(U(\sigma) = \frac{c_2}{2}\sigma^2 + \frac{c_3}{6}\sigma^3 + \frac{c_4}{24}\sigma^4 + B\). They solve the coupled Dirac equation for the quarks and the Klein-Gordon equation for the \(\sigma\) field self-consistently using an iterative method, with boundary conditions that the quark wavefunctions vanish at infinity and the \(\sigma\) field approaches its vacuum value \(\sigma_{vac}\) at infinity. The ground state and lowest one-particle excited states (even-parity and odd-parity) are computed for two families of parameters: one with \(B^{1/4} = 145\) MeV and one with \(c_2 = 0\).

## Key numbers

*   Proton rms charge radius fixed to \(r_p = 0.83\) fm as input constraint.
*   For \(B^{1/4} = 145\) MeV, \(c_4 = 8\times10^5\), and \(f = 15\): magnetic moment \(\mu_p = 1.87\) (in Bohr magnetons of the proton) and \(g_A/g_V = 1.21\).
*   For \(B^{1/4} = 145\) MeV, \(c_4 = 8\times10^5\), and \(f = 200\): magnetic moment \(\mu_p = 2.61\) and \(g_A/g_V = 0.618\).
*   For \(c_2 = 0\), \(c_4 = 1\times10^4\), and \(f = 15\): total ground state energy \(E = 7.00\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_p = 2.31\), \(g_A/g_V = 1.00\).
*   For \(c_2 = 0\), \(c_4 = 1\times10^4\), and \(f = 150\): total ground state energy \(E = 5.31\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_p = 2.63\), \(g_A/g_V = 0.583\).
*   For \(B^{1/4} = 145\) MeV, \(f = 30\), \(c_4 = 8\times10^5\): ground state energy \(E = 7.26\) fm\(^{-1}\), even-parity excited state energy \(E_+ = 9.11\) fm\(^{-1}\), odd-parity excited state energy \(E_- = 8.70\) fm\(^{-1}\).
*   Experimental values quoted for comparison: proton mass \(E = 4.69\) fm\(^{-1}\), proton magnetic moment \(\mu = 2.79\) nuclear magnetons, \(g_A/g_V = 1.25\), and \(r_p = 0.83\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model of Fowler, Raha, and Weiner (1981) by adding a non-linear scalar field coupling, following the approach of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and Thomas, Saito, and Jennings (1994, 1996). Unlike the QMC model, the IQMDD model does not require a MIT bag boundary condition, which the authors argue provides a better starting point for many-body calculations. The results are compared to the Friedberg-Lee soliton bag model calculations of Saly and Sundaresan (1984), and the authors claim better agreement with experimental nucleon properties. The work enables future extensions to finite temperature and inclusion of strange quarks for hyperon studies.
