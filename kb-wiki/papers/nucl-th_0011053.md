# The Isospin Asymmetry in the Pseudo-spin Dynamical Symmetry

> Pseudo-spin symmetry in nuclei is investigated considering the Dirac equation with a Lorentz structured Woods-Saxon potential. The isospin correlation of the energy splittings of pseudo-spin partners with the nuclear potential parameters is studied. We show that in an isotopic chain, the pseudo-spin symmetry is better realized for neutrons than for protons. This behavior comes from balance effects amongst the central nuclear potential parameters. In general, we found an isospin asymmetry of the nuclear pseudo-spin interaction, opposed to the nuclear spin-orbit interaction which is quasi isospin symmetric.

## Key claim

The paper shows that pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is isospin asymmetric: it is better realized for neutrons than for protons in an isotopic chain, which is opposite to the nuclear spin-orbit interaction that is quasi isospin symmetric. This asymmetry arises from balance effects among the central nuclear potential parameters (depth, radius, and surface diffuseness) that differ between protons and neutrons due to the isovector rho meson interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation with a Lorentz-structured Woods-Saxon potential for the scalar (S) and vector (V) mean fields, using six parameters (central depths, radii, and diffuseness for the sum Σ = S+V and the difference Δ = V-S). They fitted these parameters to the neutron spectra of 208Pb, then systematically varied the depth, radius, and diffuseness of the Σ potential to study how pseudospin energy splittings change. The analysis was extended to Sn isotopes (A=100 to A=170) using published mean-field parameters for both neutrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the fitted Woods-Saxon parameters were: Σ₀ = -66 MeV, Δ₀ = 650 MeV, R = 7 fm, and a = 0.6 fm.
- For Sn isotopes, the neutron Σ₀ varied from -68.6 MeV to -55.7 MeV, R from 5.6 fm to 6.7 fm, and a from 0.6 fm to 0.76 fm as A increased from 100 to 170.
- For Sn isotopes, the proton Σ₀ varied from -50 MeV to -60 MeV, R from 5 fm to 6.5 fm, with surface diffuseness increasing slightly more than for neutrons.
- The product |Σ₀|R² was found to be roughly constant for neutrons across the Sn isotopic chain, so that varying |Σ₀| and R while keeping this product fixed left pseudospin doublet splittings almost unchanged.
- The rho meson potential V_ρ was in the range 4-8 MeV, negligible compared to Δ₀ (650-750 MeV) but significant compared to |Σ₀| (50-70 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic understanding of pseudospin symmetry first identified by Ginocchio (1997) as a symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian when S+V=0, and the later condition dΣ/dr=0 from Meng et al. (1998). It provides a systematic parameter study that explains the isospin asymmetry observed in relativistic mean-field calculations by Lalazissis et al. (1998) and the nonrelativistic helicity transformation approach of Blokhin et al. (1995). The results enable predictions for pseudospin splitting in neutron-rich nuclei accessible at radioactive beam facilities, and clarify why the spin-orbit interaction remains roughly isospin symmetric while the pseudospin-orbit interaction does not.
