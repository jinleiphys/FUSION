# $^{21}$Ne level structure in the resonance $^{17}$O+$α$ elastic scattering

> The first study of resonances in $^{17}$O+$α$ elastic scattering was carried out using the Thick Target Inverse Kinematics (TTIK) method. The data were analyzed in the framework of an $\textit{R}$-matrix approach. Many $α$-cluster states were found in the $^{21}$Ne excitation region of the 9-13 MeV excitation energy including the first observation of a broad $\textit{l}$=0 state in an odd-even nucleus, which is likely the analog of the broad 0$^+$ at 8 MeV in $^{20}$Ne. The observed structure in $^{21}$Ne appeared to be strikingly similar to that in $^{20}$Ne populated in the resonance $^{16}$O+$α$ scattering. The results are also useful for refinement of data on an $^{17}$O($α$,$\textit{n}$) reaction important for astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of a broad _l_=0 α-cluster state in an odd-even nucleus (²¹Ne), which is likely the analog of the broad 0⁺ state at 8 MeV in ²⁰Ne; however, the identification of this state is qualified by the authors' statement that they did not aim for the "best" fit but were satisfied with a "good" one, and the χ² criterion for the R-matrix description up to 4.1 MeV c.m. energy is 1.4.

## Method

The Thick Target Inverse Kinematics (TTIK) method was used with a 32 MeV ¹⁷O beam incident on a helium gas target at the DC-60 cyclotron. Fifteen Si detectors at forward laboratory angles (starting from zero degrees) detected α recoils, and the excitation functions were analyzed using a multilevel multichannel R-matrix code. The analysis assumed a single _l_ value for α capture and decay, and spins were randomly selected from those allowed by conservation laws, with the influence on cross sections compensated by varying elastic scattering widths and total widths.

## Key numbers

- The lowest resonance observed is at E_c.m. = 1.75 MeV with _l_=0 (5/2⁺), Γ_α = 8.4 keV, Γ_total = 83.4 keV, and reduced width γ_α = 0.61.
- A very broad _l_=0 resonance at E_c.m. = 3.70 MeV (5/2⁺) has Γ_α = 932 keV, Γ_total = 1103 keV, and γ_α = 0.24.
- The experimental energy resolution was ∼25 keV (FWHM) at zero degrees, deteriorating to ∼60 keV at other angles.
- Uncertainties in absolute cross section are less than 9%.
- The 5/2⁺ resonance at 9.10 MeV excitation energy (E_c.m. = 1.75 MeV) agrees in total width with the data from Best et al. within ±20%, but the α width (8.4 keV) differs from the 16.8 keV reported by Best et al.
- The average reduced width for _l_=2 states in ²¹Ne is γ_α = 0.27, in fair agreement with γ_α = 0.2 for the 2.69 MeV 2⁺ level in ²⁰Ne.

## Context

This work extends the study of α-cluster states to the N≠Z nucleus ²¹Ne, where data are scarce due to experimental difficulties, and follows the TTIK method previously applied to ¹⁶O+α scattering (Goldberg et al.) and ¹⁸O+α scattering. The results enable refinement of the ¹⁷O(α,n) reaction rate, important for the s-process in massive stars at low metallicity, and challenge the shell-model description of α-cluster states (as discussed by Volya and collaborators). The observed broad _l_=0 state in an odd-even nucleus, previously seen only in even-even nuclei like ¹²C, ¹⁸O, and ²⁰Ne, suggests such states may be a necessary element of developed cluster structure in many light nuclei.
