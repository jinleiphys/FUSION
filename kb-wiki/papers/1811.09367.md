# Shell model results for nuclear $ОІ^-$-decay properties of $sd$ shell nuclei

> We evaluate the allowed $ОІ^-$-decay properties of nuclei with $Z = 8 - 15$ systematically under the framework of the nuclear shell model with the use of the valence space Hamiltonians derived from modern $ab~intio$ methods, such as in-medium similarity renormalization group and coupled-cluster theory. For comparison we also show results obtained with fitted interaction derived from the chiral effective field theory and phenomenological USDB interaction. We have performed calculations for O $\rightarrow$ F, F $\rightarrow$ Ne, Ne $\rightarrow$ Na, Na $\rightarrow$ Mg, Mg $\rightarrow$ Al, Al $\rightarrow$ Si, Si $\rightarrow$ P and P $\rightarrow$ S transitions. Theoretical results of $B(GT)$, log$ft$ values and half-lives, are discussed and compared with the available experimental data.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the sd model space using the NuShellX code with four interactions: two ab initio interactions (in-medium similarity renormalization group, IM-SRG, and coupled-cluster effective interactions, CCEI) derived from chiral N3LO NN and N2LO 3N forces, a fitted chiral EFT interaction (CEFT) with LECs fitted to 441 ground- and excited-state energies, and the phenomenological USDB interaction. ## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of ОІ-decay in the sd shell by Brown and Wildenthal (1988) and Wildenthal et al. (1983), which used phenomenological effective interactions, by providing the first comprehensive comparison of ОІвЃ»-decay properties using modern ab initio interactions (IM-SRG and CCEI) and a fitted chiral EFT interaction. The results enable systematic evaluation of quenching factors for the axial-vector coupling strength in the sd shell and provide benchmarks for future ab initio calculations of weak nuclear response, relevant for astrophysical applications and neutrinoless double-beta decay studies as discussed by Menendez (2011).
