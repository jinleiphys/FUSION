# Particle species dependence of elliptic flow fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies in a multiphase transport model

> The fluctuations of elliptic flow (\vtwo) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions offer a powerful tool to probe the collective behavior and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The dependence of these fluctuations on particle species further sheds light on the hadronization mechanism. At LHC energies, the ALICE experiment has measured $v_2$ fluctuations for charged pions, kaons, and (anti-)protons via the ratio of \vtwo measured with respect to the spectator plane (\vtwosp) and from the four-particle cumulants (\vtwofour). However, the observed dependencies on transverse momentum and particle type remain not fully understood. In this study, we perform a phenomenological investigation using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, which allows us to trace the full evolution of flow fluctuations intertwined with the quark coalescence. The results qualitatively reproduce the ALICE measurements and offer deeper insights into the transport dynamics and hadronization of the QGP.

# Particle species dependence of elliptic flow fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies in a multiphase transport model

## Key claim

The AMPT model qualitatively reproduces the ALICE measurements of particle-species-dependent elliptic flow fluctuations (ratio v2{SP}/v2{4}) for pions, kaons, and protons, and demonstrates that the observed species dependence emerges directly from quark coalescence hadronization rather than from the partonic interaction stage, where no quark flavor dependence is present.

## Method

The authors use the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, tracking the full evolution from initial partons through partonic scattering (ZPC), quark coalescence hadronization, and hadronic rescattering (ART). Elliptic flow is calculated using three methods: v2 with respect to the spectator plane (v2{SP}), two-particle cumulants (v2{2}), and four-particle cumulants (v2{4}), with a pseudorapidity gap of 0.3 applied for v2{2} to suppress non-flow effects, and all particles selected within |eta| < 1 and transverse momentum 0.1 < pT < 4.0 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The ratio v2{SP}/v2{4} for quarks after ZPC rises monotonically from unity to approximately 1.5 with increasing pT in 30-40% centrality Pb-Pb collisions.
- Centrality-averaged double ratios of intercepts for identified hadrons to inclusive hadrons: pi:K:p = 0.98:0.96:0.70, compared to ALICE preliminary results of pi:K:p = 1:0.98:0.96.
- The eccentricity ratio epsilon{RP}/epsilon{4} calculated from initial-state partons ranges from 0.9927 in 10-20% centrality to 0.9406 in 50-60% centrality.
- The v2{SP}/v2{4} ratio for hadrons shows a particle species hierarchy pi >= K > p across all centrality classes.

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies of flow fluctuations (Wan et al., previous work) that found no particle-species dependence, and addresses the open question raised by ALICE measurements (ALICE Collaboration, 2023) of identified particle v2 fluctuations. The results are compared with initial-state eccentricity ratios from the T_R_ENTo and EPM models, and the analysis demonstrates that the event-mixing technique causes v2{2}, v2{4}, and v2{SP} to converge to the same value, confirming that observed fluctuations originate from intra-event correlations. The findings enable future comparisons with higher-statistics ALICE Run 3 data to further constrain QGP transport properties and hadronization dynamics.
