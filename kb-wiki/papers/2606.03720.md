# Nuclear Reaction Data for Fission Products Off Stability

> Neutron cross sections on fission products are relevant to a wide range of applications, including nuclear nonproliferation and forensics, spent-fuel assay, reactor burnup and design, as well as astrophysics. Evaluated nuclear data libraries generally fulfill application needs for isotopes on or near stability, however, for unstable fission products, theoretical descriptions of neutron-induced reactions often constitute the only available source of information. These models often make use of simplified assumptions, leading to unquantified impacts on predicted cross sections. In this work, we discuss possible approaches to addressing these issues, particularly by leveraging machine-learning methods, improved predictive reaction modeling, and experimental data to better constrain model parameters. Our goal is to eventually produce evaluated files for the most-produced nuclei off stability in the fission process of $^{235}$U and submit them to the ENDF/B for consideration in the future ENDF/B-IX.0 release. Here we present the methodology and discuss preliminary results comparing usual simplified approaches with a more realistic one accounting for nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a methodology to produce evaluated neutron cross-section files for unstable fission products, but the actual results are preliminary and limited to comparing a spherical optical model with an adiabatic coupled-channels approach for one nucleus (98Zr), showing that deformation effects produce substantially different cross sections than spherical approximations.

## Method

The project (RREFPOS) uses the EMPIRE reaction code with an adiabatic coupled-channels optical model that incorporates nuclear deformation parameters, rather than the standard spherical optical model potential. For the resonance region, the authors plan to use machine learning to predict cross-section probability distributions from average resonance parameters, while for the fast region they will combine microscopic models with a generative neural network trained on systematic trends across the nuclear chart. Experimental nuclear level density data are being obtained through particle evaporation measurements at the Edwards Accelerator Lab for nuclei near stability to constrain model parameters.

## Key numbers

- 98Zr is expected to have a strong dynamic quadrupole deformation of the order of 0.33, based on RIPL-3 data
- The ratio E4+/E2+ is used as an indicator of deformation, with a value near 3.3 indicating a perfect rotor
- Two experimental campaigns completed: one measuring NLD of 92,96Nb via 92+96Zr(p,n) reactions, and one constraining 94Nb NLD via 94Zr(p,n)
- Neutron evaporation measurements of 94Zr(d,n) reaction are underway to obtain data for 95Nb NLD

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic coupled-channels approach developed by Nobre (2014, 2015) and Herman (2014), which has demonstrated success for statically-deformed stable nuclei, to unstable fission products where experimental data are scarce or nonexistent. The project aims to produce evaluated files for submission to the ENDF/B library for the future ENDF/B-IX.0 release, improving upon the TENDL library approach which stochastically generates resonances without including uncertainties in average resonance parameters. The work enables more realistic neutron cross-section evaluations for the most-produced nuclei off stability in 235U spontaneous fission, with potential extension to 239Pu and 252Cf fission products.
