# Competition of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin ordering in nuclear matter

> In the framework of a Fermi liquid theory it is considered the possibility of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic phase transitions in symmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme effective interaction. The zero temperature dependence of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin polarization parameters as functions of density is found for SkM$^*$, SGII effective forces. It is shown that in the density domain, where both type of solutions of self--consistent equations exist, ferromagnetic spin state is more preferable than antiferromagnetic one.

## Key claim

In symmetric nuclear matter with Skyrme effective interactions (SkM* and SGII), both ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin ordering can occur at sufficiently high densities, but where both solutions exist the ferromagnetic state is thermodynamically more preferable than the antiferromagnetic one.

## Method

The author uses a Fermi liquid theory framework with Skyrme effective interactions to derive self-consistent equations for the ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin polarization parameters at zero temperature. The normal distribution function of nucleons is expanded in Pauli matrices in spin and isospin space, and the energy functional is specified with Skyrme forces to obtain the single-particle energies and self-consistent equations for the order parameters. Numerical solutions are then obtained for the SkM* and SGII parametrizations of the Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers

- Ferromagnetic spin order parameter appears at density ≈ 2ρ₀ for SkM* force and at ≈ 2.75ρ₀ for SGII force.
- Antiferromagnetic spin order parameter appears at density ≈ 3.3ρ₀ for SkM* force and at ≈ 5ρ₀ for SGII force.
- Nuclear matter becomes totally ferromagnetically polarized at density ≈ 2.7ρ₀ for SkM* force and at ≈ 3.9ρ₀ for SGII force.
- Totally antiferromagnetically polarized state is formed at density ≈ 4.5ρ₀ for SkM* potential and at ≈ 7.2ρ₀ for SGII potential.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ferromagnetic phase transitions in nuclear matter that used magnetic susceptibility calculations with Skyrme forces (Vidana et al., 2001; Rios et al., 2005) by directly computing the spin polarization parameters rather than just locating instability thresholds. It also introduces the possibility of antiferromagnetic ordering in nuclear matter, which had not been previously explored in this context. The results enable further investigation of spin-polarized matter at densities relevant for neutron star interiors, though the author notes that a pure nucleonic description may be incomplete at the highest densities considered.
