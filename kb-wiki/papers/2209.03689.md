# Spin polarization measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The hot and dense matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is termed quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The evolution of the medium is characterized by non-trivial velocity and vorticity fields, resulting in the polarization of the produced particles. The spin polarization, being sensitive to the hydrothermal (flow velocity and temperature) gradients, is unique compared to conventional observables that are sensitive to the hydrothermal fields only. Hence, the recent measurements of global and local hyperon spin polarization and vector meson spin alignment by the LHC and STAR collaborations provide a unique opportunity to probe the QGP substructure with finer details.

## Key claim

Spin polarization measurements provide a unique probe of the quark-gluon plasma substructure because they are sensitive to gradients of flow velocity and temperature fields, unlike conventional observables that are sensitive only to the fields themselves; however, the exact nature of the spin-orbit interaction and spin relaxation times remain unknown, and several pressing issues such as the discrepancy between measured and predicted sign of the longitudinal polarization component remain unresolved.

## Method

The global polarization of hyperons (Λ, Ξ, Ω) is reconstructed from the parity-violating weak decay daughter angular distribution in the parent rest frame, using the event plane method with spectator neutrons to define the orbital angular momentum direction. Local polarization is measured relative to the second harmonic symmetry plane to capture the quadrupole structure of vorticity from elliptic flow. Vector meson (K*0, φ) spin alignment is quantified via the spin density matrix element ρ00 from the parity-conserving strong decay angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- Λ global polarization P_Λ decreases monotonically from a few percent at √s_NN = 3 GeV to a fraction of a percent at √s_NN = 200 GeV, and is consistent with zero at LHC energies (2.76 and 5.02 TeV)
- P_Λ at √s_NN = 3 GeV in Au+Au collisions is the largest ever measured experimentally
- P_Ξ = 0.47 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.23 (syst.) % and ⟨P_Ω⟩ = 1.11 ± 0.87 (stat.) ± 1.97 (syst.) % for 20%-80% centrality in Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- ρ00 for K*0 and φ at low pT (< 2 GeV) in semi-central Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV shows ρ00 < 1/3, while ρ00 for φ at intermediate pT in Au-Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV shows ρ00 > 1/3
- Local polarization P_z,s2 measured at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (ALICE) and 200 GeV (STAR) has similar magnitude and phase, with no significant rapidity dependence

## Context

This work reviews the experimental program at RHIC and LHC that extends the earlier theoretical framework of Becattini et al. (2013) and Voloshin (2018) connecting thermal vorticity to particle polarization. The measurements challenge the standard thermal vorticity picture: while global polarization is reasonably described by hydrodynamic (3FD) and transport (AMPT) models for √s_NN ≥ 7.7 GeV, the same models fail to explain the azimuthal angle dependence of polarization and the sign of the longitudinal component. The introduction of shear-induced polarization by Becattini et al. (2021) and Fu et al. (2021) with additional assumptions on hadronization temperature or spin carrier mass can reproduce the observed positive local polarization, but a detailed theoretical understanding of quark spin polarization in QGP, spin transfer at hadronization, and hadronic scattering effects on spin remains needed.
