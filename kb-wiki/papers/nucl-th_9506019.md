# The Isovector Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction Used in Shell Model Calculations

> An interaction $-χQ\cdot Q(1+B\vecτ(1)\cdot \vecτ(2))$ is used in a shell model calculation for $^{10}Be$. Whereas for $B=0$ the $2_1^+$ state is two-fold degenerate, introducing a negative $B$ causes an `isovector' $2^+$ state to come down to zero energy at $B=-0.67$ and an $S=1~L=1$ triplet ($J=0^+,~1^+,~2^+$) to come down to zero energy at $B=-0.73$. These are undesirable properties, but a large negative $B$ is apparently needed to fit the energy of the isovector giant quadrupole resonance.

# The Isovector Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction Used in Shell Model Calculations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction with a large negative isovector parameter $B$ (needed to fit the splitting of isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances) causes unphysical behavior in $p$-shell calculations for $^{10}\text{Be}$, including states collapsing to zero energy; however, introducing an effective mass $m^*/m = 0.7$–$0.8$ reduces the required $B$ magnitude to values between $-0.41$ and $-0.97$, which may resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for $^{10}\text{Be}$ in the $p$ shell using the interaction $V = -\chi Q \cdot Q (1 + B \vec{\tau}(1) \cdot \vec{\tau}(2))$ with $\chi = 0.36146$, studying the energies and transition rates of selected states as a function of the isovector parameter $B$. They classified states by orbital symmetry $[f_1 f_2 f_3]$, $L$, $S$, and $T$ using the Elliott SU(3) scheme, and computed $B(E2)$ and $B(M1)$ strengths for both isoscalar and isovector operators.

## Key numbers

- For $B=0$, the first excited $2^+$ state in $^{10}\text{Be}$ is two-fold degenerate at excitation energy $18\bar{\chi}$ (with $\bar{\chi}=0.1286$).
- An isovector $2^+$ state collapses to zero energy at $B = -0.668$ (linear fit: $E_A/\bar{\chi} = 18 - 26.935|B|$).
- An $S=1$, $L=1$ triplet ($J=0^+, 1^+, 2^+$) collapses to zero energy at $B = -0.729$ (linear fit: $E_C/\bar{\chi} = 30 - 41.160|B|$).
- At $B=-0.6$, the isovector orbital $M1$ strength to $T=1$ scissors mode states is $0.2314\ \mu_N^2$, up from $0.0895\ \mu_N^2$ at $B=0$.
- At $B=-1.0$ (beyond crossover), the isoscalar orbital $M1$ strength to a $J=1^+$ state at zero energy is $0.1194\ \mu_N^2$, and the isovector scissors mode strength fragments into $0.1368\ \mu_N^2$ at $0.96$ MeV and $0.2672\ \mu_N^2$ at $13.3$ MeV.
- Using effective mass $m^*/m = 0.8$ gives $B = -0.97$; with $m^*/m = 0.7$ gives $B = -0.41$, compared to $B = -3.1$ without effective mass.

## Context

This work extends the use of the schematic quadrupole-quadrupole interaction from RPA treatments of giant resonances (Bohr and Mottelson 1975; Suzuki and Rowe 1977) to shell model calculations in the $p$ shell, specifically for the deformed nucleus $^{10}\text{Be}$. The authors identify a conflict: the large negative $B$ values ($-3.6$ to $-0.6$) required to fit the isoscalar–isovector giant quadrupole resonance splitting cause unphysical ground-state collapses in the $p$-shell, whereas Elliott’s SU(3) model (Elliott 1958) uses $B=0$ successfully. The paper suggests that introducing effective mass or momentum-dependent quadrupole terms (as Elliott did) may resolve this dilemma, and notes that Nojarov, Faessler, and Dingfelder (1995) also argued for smaller $|B|$ values.
