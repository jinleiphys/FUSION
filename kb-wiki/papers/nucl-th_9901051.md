# Many-particle nucleon-nucleon forces from nuclear single-particle states

> As follows from the energies of single-particle states in ^{40}Ca, ^{90}Zr and ^{208}Pb nuclei the contribution of many-particle NN forces to the nuclear single-particle potential is at least the sum of repulsive and attractive parts resulting from three-particle and four-particle forces respectively. In addition the specified nucleon density distributions in the above nuclei are determined from both the 1 GeV proton-nucleus elastic scattering and the single-particle energies.

## Key claim

The contribution of many-particle nucleon-nucleon forces to the nuclear single-particle potential is at least the sum of a repulsive part from three-particle forces and an attractive part from four-particle forces, as inferred from single-particle state energies in ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb. The authors cannot guarantee the absence of contributions from higher many-particle forces, but the three-plus-four-particle combination corresponds to the least number of free parameters.

## Method

The authors used the Baranger approach (Baranger, 1970) to extract single-particle energies from experimental data on quasielastic knockout reactions, independent of any nuclear model. They calculated the two-particle contribution to the single-particle potential using the Bonn B potential (Machleidt, 1989) and the nucleon density distributions from combined electron and 1 GeV proton elastic scattering data. The many-particle contribution was modeled as an expansion in powers of the nucleon density, with a repulsive ρ² term (three-particle forces) and an attractive ρ³ term (four-particle forces), and the parameters were fitted to reproduce the observed single-particle energies in ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle contribution to the isoscalar single-particle potential in nuclear matter at equilibrium density (ρ_eq = 0.17 fm⁻³) is U_pair = -83 MeV, with scalar field S_pair = -367 MeV and vector field V_pair = +284 MeV.
- The three-particle contribution to the isoscalar potential in nuclear matter is U₃ = 78 MeV (with the Eq. (34) parameter set) or 86 MeV (with the Eq. (35) set).
- The four-particle contribution to the isoscalar potential in nuclear matter is U₄ = -78 MeV (Eq. (34)) or -88 MeV (Eq. (35)).
- The best-fit strength parameters for all three nuclei (Eq. (34)) are a₃ = 13.6608 fm⁵, a₄ = -80.2568 fm⁸, a₃⁻ = 28.7531 fm⁵, a₄⁻ = -144.3534 fm⁸.
- The average discrepancy between observed single-particle energies and the "tot1" calculations for ⁹⁰Zr and ⁴⁰Ca, and "tot2" for ²⁰⁸Pb, is 1.65 MeV, with the largest discrepancy less than 3 MeV.
- The isovector part of the many-particle potential is U⁻_m = 24 (N-Z)/A MeV, compared to the two-particle value U⁻_pair = 7 (N-Z)/A MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baranger (1970) for model-independent determination of single-particle energies and applies it to extract information about many-particle forces, which are usually studied only in few-nucleon systems. The results contradict the assumption that two-particle forces alone can describe nuclear single-particle states, showing that three-particle repulsion and four-particle attraction are necessary. The findings enable future tests with higher-energy quasielastic knockout experiments (e.g., (p,p'N) or (e,e'N) at about 1 GeV) to reduce ambiguities from final-state interactions, and provide constraints for meson-exchange theories of the strong interaction.
