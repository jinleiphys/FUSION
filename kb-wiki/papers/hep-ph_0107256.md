# Diffractive Gluon Production in Proton-Nucleus Collisions and in DIS

> We derive expressions for the gluon production cross sections in the single diffractive proton-nucleus scattering and DIS processes in a quasi-classical approximation. The resulting cross sections include the effects of all multiple rescatterings in the classical background field of the target proton or nucleus, which remains intact after the scattering. We also write down an expression for the inclusive gluon production cross section in DIS in the quasi-classical approximation.

## Key claim

The paper derives expressions for diffractive gluon production cross sections in proton-nucleus (pA) scattering and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in a quasi-classical approximation, where the target nucleus remains intact and all multiple rescatterings in its classical background field are included, but the calculation assumes the projectile's valence quarks form a color singlet state with no soft non-perturbative partons present.

## Method

The author uses time-ordered light cone perturbation theory in the light cone gauge of the projectile hadron (A+ = 0) to calculate diffractive gluon production cross sections. For quarkonium-nucleus scattering, the propagators of the q-qbar and q-qbar-G states through the nucleus are derived by exponentiating single-nucleon interactions, yielding expressions in terms of the saturation scale Q_s. For proton-nucleus scattering, the three-valence-quark color structure requires diagonalizing a 2x2 color matrix in the (u2, u3) basis to obtain the qqqG propagator, leading to a more complex cross section formula.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s is defined implicitly by Q_s^2(b) = (8 pi^2 alpha_s N_c sqrt(R^2 - b^2))/(N_c^2 - 1) * rho * xG(x, Q_s^2(b)), where rho = A/[(4/3) pi R^3] is the nuclear density and R is the nuclear radius.
- The gluon distribution function at the two-gluon level is xG(x, 1/x^2) = (alpha_s C_F/pi) * ln(1/(x^2 mu^2)), with mu an infrared cutoff.
- The q-qbar propagator through the nucleus is exp(-(C_F/(4 N_c)) * (x - y)^2 Q_s^2).
- The q-qbar-G propagator through the nucleus is exp(-(1/8)(x - z)^2 Q_s^2 - (1/8)(y - z)^2 Q_s^2 + (1/(8 N_c^2))(x - y)^2 Q_s^2).
- For N_c = 3, the proton (qqq) propagator through the nucleus is exp(-(1/18)[(x1 - x2)^2 Q_s^2 + (x2 - x3)^2 Q_s^2 + (x1 - x3)^2 Q_s^2]).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical saturation approach previously applied to total diffractive cross sections in DIS by Hebecker (1997), Kovchegov and McLerran (1999), and Kovner and Wiedemann (2001), as well as to inclusive gluon production in pA collisions by Kovchegov and Mueller (1998). The diffractive gluon production cross section had not been addressed in the literature prior to this paper. The results enable calculations relevant for proton-nucleus collision experiments at RHIC and DIS at HERA, and provide a foundation for future inclusion of quantum corrections (leading logarithmic contributions resumming multiple BFKL pomeron exchanges) to the diffractive gluon production cross section.
