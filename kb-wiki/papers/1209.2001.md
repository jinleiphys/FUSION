# CGC predictions for p+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We present predictions for total multiplicities and single inclusive particle production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC. The main dynamical input in our calculations is the use of solutions of the running coupling BK equation tested in e+p data. We use a Monte-Carlo treatment of the nuclear geometry and either $k_t$-factorization or the hybrid formalisms to describe particle production in the central and forward rapidity regions, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors present predictions for total multiplicities and single inclusive particle production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC, using solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation as the main dynamical input, but note that the precise value of x at which one should switch between the k_t-factorization and hybrid formalisms has only been estimated on an empirical basis, and that no smooth matching between the two formalisms is known.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) from nucleon UGDs extracted by the AAMQS collaboration through global fits to inclusive e+p data on structure functions, using solutions of the rcBK equation. They employ a Monte Carlo treatment of the nuclear geometry with Woods-Saxon distributions and nucleon position fluctuations, and compute particle production via k_t-factorization for central rapidities and the hybrid formalism for forward rapidities, using either the "natural" prescription where Q_s0^2 scales linearly with the number of overlapping nucleons or a "modified" prescription where it scales as N(R)^(1/gamma).

## Key numbers

- The expected center-of-mass collision energy for the p+Pb run is 5 TeV, compared to a maximal energy of 200 GeV for RHIC d+Au collisions.
- The initial evolution scale for the rcBK equation is set at x_0 = 10^(-2).
- Two AAMQS parameter sets are used: (Q_s0,proton^2, gamma) = (0.168 GeV^2, 1.119) and (0.157 GeV^2, 1.101), with the MV model corresponding to gamma=1 and Q_s0,proton^2 = 0.2 GeV^2.
- The strong coupling is frozen to a constant value alpha_fr = 0.7 in the infrared, with Lambda = 0.241 GeV.
- The transverse area sigma_0 for large-x partons of a nucleon is taken as approximately 42 mb, given by the inelastic cross-section at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- The saturation scale at the LHC is expected to be a factor of 2 to 4 times larger than at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the rcBK-MC model previously presented in Albacete (2010) as an upgrade of the original KLN-MC code from Drescher (2007), and builds upon global fits by the AAMQS collaboration (Albacete 2009, 2010, 2012) that demonstrated the rcBK equation successfully accounts for the x-dependence of inclusive structure functions measured in e+p collisions at HERA. The predictions are intended to provide baseline results for testing the Color Glass Condensate framework against upcoming LHC p+Pb data, and to help distinguish among different approaches to describe high energy scattering in nuclear collisions, including leading twist shadowing, string fusion processes, and energy-dependent cut-offs in event generators like HIJING or HYDJET.
