# The effect of three-body nucleon-nucleon interaction on the ground state binding energy of the light nuclei

> We calculate the ground state binding energies of the light nuclei such as 4He, 6Li, 12C and 14N by considering the effect of three-body nucleon-nucleon interaction. We use the effective twobody potential obtained from the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) calculations of the nuclear matter for the Reid68, AV14, UV14, and AV18 nuclear potentials in different channels. To calculate the ground state binding energy, we implement the local density approximation by using the harmonic oscillator wave functions while the effect of three-body interaction is considered by employing the UIX potential. We compare the obtained two-body ground state binding energy with the energy related to the three-body effect. We also compare the obtained values with the experimental data and also work of others, and show that the results are relatively acceptable. We compute the root mean-square radius Rrms of the above nuclei for the Reid68, AV14, UV14, and AV18 potentials and compare the results with the experiment. We also obtain the contribution of different channels by matching to the experimental values of the quadrupole moments and magnetic dipole moments. Furthermore, we calculate the three-body cluster energy of the above nuclei and compare the results with that of nuclear matter. According to the obtained results, we see that the three-body cluster energy contribution is small. For example, for 4He nuclide, this value is 0.079 MeV with the Reid68 potential.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the three-body UIX potential improves the agreement of their LOCV-based binding energy calculations with experimental data for light nuclei, but the improvement is modest and the results remain "relatively acceptable" rather than highly precise; the three-body cluster energy contribution is explicitly stated to be small (e.g., 0.079 MeV for 4He with the Reid68 potential).

## Method

The authors use the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to compute effective two-body potentials from the Reid68, AV14, UV14, and AV18 nuclear potentials in different channels for nuclear matter. They then apply the local density approximation with harmonic oscillator wave functions to calculate the ground state binding energies of 4He, 6Li, 12C, and 14N, and incorporate the three-body interaction via the UIX potential (comprising a two-pion-exchange term and a repulsive term). The three-body cluster energy is computed separately using the correlation functions and effective potentials from the LOCV calculations.

## Context

This work extends the LOCV method combined with local density approximation, previously applied to nuclear matter and heavy closed-shell nuclei (e.g., Bordbar et al. 2005, 2011), to light nuclei including three-body forces via the UIX potential. It builds on the Fujita-Miyazawa (1957) three-body interaction and the Urbana-Argonne (UA) model, and compares results with Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations using AV18+IL7 (Wiringa et al.) and other approaches such as CD-Bonn+TM, AV18+Urb IX, and N2LO(AFDMC). The small three-body cluster energy found for finite nuclei mirrors earlier findings for nuclear matter (Bordbar et al. 1998).
