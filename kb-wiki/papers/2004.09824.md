# Extraction of $ND$ scattering lengths from the $Λ_b\rightarrowπ^-pD^0$ decay and properties of the $Σ_c(2800)^+$

> The isovector and isoscalar $ND$ $s$-wave scattering lengths are extracted by fitting to the LHCb data of the $pD^0$ invariant-mass distribution in the decay $Λ_b\rightarrowπ^-pD^0$, making use of the cusp effect at the $nD^+$ threshold. The analysis is based on a coupled-channel nonrelativistic effective field theory. We find that the real part of the isovector $ND$ scattering length is unnaturally large due to the existence of a near-threshold state with a mass around 2.8 GeV. The state is consistent with the $Σ_c(2800)^+$ resonance observed at Belle. Our results suggest that it couples strongly to the $ND$ channel in an $s$-wave, and that its quantum numbers are $J^P=1/2^-$. The strong cusp behavior at the $nD^+$ threshold can be verified using updated LHCb data.

## Key claim

The isovector $ND$ scattering length has a large negative real part, indicating a near-threshold bound state consistent with the $\Sigma_c(2800)^+$ resonance, but this conclusion is hedged by the large statistical uncertainties of the LHCb data; the isoscalar scattering length is not well constrained and could correspond to either repulsive interaction or a bound state too far from threshold to be reliably described by the nonrelativistic effective field theory.

## Method

The authors fit a coupled-channel nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) to the $1/2^-$ partial wave of the $pD^0$ invariant-mass distribution from the decay $\Lambda_b\rightarrow\pi^-pD^0$, using LHCb data (Aaij 2017). The fit includes the $pD^0$ and $nD^+$ channels and exploits the cusp at the $nD^+$ threshold; the $1/2^+$ and $3/2^\pm$ contributions are subtracted from the data to isolate the $s$-wave $ND$ component. The $T$-matrix is obtained from the Lippmann–Schwinger equation with constant contact interactions, and inelasticities from lower channels (such as $\pi\Lambda_c$ and $\pi\Sigma_c$) are incorporated via imaginary parts of the scattering-length parameters.

## Key numbers

* Real part of the isovector $ND$ scattering length: $\mathrm{Re}\,a_{ND(I=1)} = -3.8^{+1.4}_{-2.0}$ fm (from fit to LHCb data).
* Imaginary part of the isovector $ND$ scattering length: $\mathrm{Im}\,a_{ND(I=1)} = 2.7^{+1.6}_{-2.7}$ fm.
* Real part of the isoscalar $ND$ scattering length: $\mathrm{Re}\,a_{ND(I=0)} = -0.79^{+0.66}_{-0.61}$ fm.
* Pole on the first Riemann sheet (RS-I): $2801.8^{+1.0}_{-4.0} - i\,(2.6\pm2.6)$ MeV.
* Pole on the second Riemann sheet (RS-II): $2807.0^{+4.5}_{-17.1} - i\left(9.4^{+10.2}_{-9.4}\right)$ MeV.
* Best-fit reduced chi-square: $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 4.9/10$ for 14 data points below 2.83 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of extracting scattering lengths from threshold cusps, previously applied to $\pi\pi$ scattering in decays such as $K^\pm\rightarrow\pi^\pm\pi^0\pi^0$ (Cabibbo 2004, 2005; Batley 2005), to the $ND$ system. The results are compared with phenomenological models: the SU(4) model (Lutz 2005; Mizutani 2006), the SU(8) model (Garcia-Recio 2008), and a meson-exchange model (Haidenbauer 2010). The extracted isovector pole supports the interpretation of the $\Sigma_c(2800)^+$ (discovered by Belle, Mizuk 2004) as a $J^P=1/2^-$ $ND$ molecular state, and the analysis provides input for future chiral effective field theory studies of $D^{(*)}N$ interactions (Wang 2020). Improved LHCb data could verify the predicted cusp and further constrain the scattering lengths.
