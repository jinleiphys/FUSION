# Neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering and strange quark effects

> A relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation model is applied to quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. The bound state is obtained in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory and the final state interaction is taken into account in the scattering state. Results for the charged- and neutral-current neutrino (antineutrino) reactions on $^{12}$C target nucleus are presented and the sensitivity of various quantities to the strange quark content of the nucleon weak current is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) model, which includes final state interactions (FSI) via a complex optical potential, can describe quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on 12C, and that the sensitivity of various cross sections and ratios to strange quark contributions depends strongly on the specific observable and on whether the knockout is proton or neutron.

## Method

The authors apply a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) model to charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino and antineutrino quasi-elastic scattering on a 12C target. The bound nucleon states are obtained from relativistic mean field theory Dirac-Hartree solutions, and the final state interaction (FSI) of the ejected nucleon is treated via a relativistic optical potential with real and imaginary parts (the EDAD1 potential). The cross sections are computed semi-exclusively, summing over all one-nucleon knockout channels, and the sensitivity to strange quark contributions is explored by varying the axial-vector strangeness parameter g_s_A, the strange magnetic moment μ_s, and the strange radius ρ_s in the nucleon weak current.

## Key numbers

- CC cross sections on 12C at E_ν = 500 and 1000 MeV: FSI reduce the cross section by approximately 50% compared to the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) results.
- NC cross sections on 12C: FSI also reduce the cross section by up to approximately 50% relative to RPWIA.
- Axial mass used: M_A = (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV, taken as the weighted average from (quasi-)elastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering experiments.
- For NC scattering with g_s_A = -0.10, the cross section is enhanced for proton knockout and reduced for neutron knockout by approximately 10% relative to the case with no strangeness.
- The ratio of neutrino-to-antineutrino NC cross sections shows a maximum at outgoing nucleon kinetic energy T_N ≈ 0.6 E_ν.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic models for quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, such as the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model used by Alberico and Barbaro (2002) and the RPWIA approach of Van der Ventel (2004), by including a more complete treatment of FSI via a relativistic optical potential. The calculations are relevant for interpreting data from the FINeSSE experiment at Fermilab, which aims to extract the strange quark contribution to the proton spin via neutral-current elastic scattering on 12C, and complement parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as SAMPLE, HAPPEX, and G0. The results enable more reliable extraction of strange form factors from future neutrino-nucleus measurements by quantifying the role of nuclear structure and FSI effects.
