# On Ruby's solid angle formula and some of its generalizations

> Using the Mellin-Barnes representation, we show that Ruby's solid angle formula and some of its generalizations may be expressed in a compact way in terms of the Appell F4 and Lauricella Fc functions.

## Key claim

Using the Mellin-Barnes representation, Ruby's solid angle formula and its generalizations can be expressed in closed form in terms of the Appell \(F_4\) and Lauricella \(F_C\) functions, but the resulting series representations converge only in restricted parameter regions (e.g., \(R_S + R_D < d\) for the simplest case), with analytic continuation formulas needed for other regions.

## Method

The author inserted Mellin-Barnes integral representations for each Bessel function in the integrand, performed the \(k\)-integral analytically using the gamma function, and then identified the resulting multiple Mellin-Barnes integral as the standard representation of the Appell \(F_4\) function (for two Bessel functions) or the Lauricella \(F_C^{(N)}\) function (for \(N\) Bessel functions). The duplication formula for the Euler gamma function was used to bring the integrals into the canonical form of these special functions.

## Key numbers

- Ruby's formula: \(G = \left(\frac{R_D}{2d}\right)^2 F_4\left(1,\frac{3}{2},2,2; -\left(\frac{R_S}{d}\right)^2, -\left(\frac{R_D}{d}\right)^2\right)\), where \(R_S\) and \(R_D\) are source and detector radii and \(d\) is the distance between them.
- The double series representation for Ruby's formula converges in the region \(R_S + R_D < d\).
- For the non-coaxial disk source with constant surface emissivity: \(G = \left(\frac{R_D}{2d}\right)^2 F_C\left(1,\frac{3}{2},1,2,2; -\left(\frac{a}{d}\right)^2, -\left(\frac{R_S}{d}\right)^2, -\left(\frac{R_D}{d}\right)^2\right)\), where \(a\) is the offset distance of the disk axes.
- For the non-coaxial ring source case: \(G = \left(\frac{R_D}{2d}\right)^2 F_C\left(1,\frac{3}{2},1,1,2; -\left(\frac{a}{d}\right)^2, -\left(\frac{R_S}{d}\right)^2, -\left(\frac{R_D}{d}\right)^2\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Ruby's solid angle formula beyond the closed-form expression in terms of complete and incomplete elliptic integrals given by Conway (2006). It also generalizes the double series representation previously mentioned by Ruby (1994) and studied by Pomme (2004), which had restricted convergence. The results enable analytic computation of integrals involving products of Bessel functions that appear in detector geometry calculations, including non-coaxial configurations and non-constant surface emissivity cases that Conway (2006) had not expressed in closed form.
