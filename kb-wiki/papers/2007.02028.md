# A field theoretical model for quarkyonic matter

> The possibility that nuclear matter at a density relevant to the interior of massive neutron stars may be a quarkynoic matter has attracted considerable recent interest. In this work, we construct a field theoretical model to describe the quarkyonic matter, that would allow quantitative and systematic calculations of its various properties. This is implemented by synthesizing the Walecka model together with the quark-meson model, where both quark and nucleon degrees of freedom are present based on the quarkyonic scenario. With this model we compute at mean-field level the thermodynamic properties of the symmetric nuclear matter and calibrate model parameters through well-known nuclear physics measurements. We find this model gives a very good description of the symmetric nuclear matter from moderate to high baryon density and demonstrates a continuous transition from nucleon-dominance to quark-dominance for the system.

# A field theoretical model for quarkyonic matter

## Key claim
The authors construct a field theoretical model for quarkyonic matter that, at mean-field level, gives a good description of symmetric nuclear matter from moderate to high baryon density and demonstrates a continuous transition from nucleon-dominance to quark-dominance, but only after calibrating parameters to nuclear matter saturation properties and with the caveat that the prominent peak structure in the speed of sound seen in some prior models is absent in their results.

## Method
The model synthesizes the Walecka model (for nucleons interacting via scalar sigma and vector omega mesons) with the quark-meson model (for quarks interacting with scalar and pseudoscalar mesons), incorporating both quark and nucleon degrees of freedom. The quarkyonic picture is implemented by defining a Pauli-blocked nucleon Fermi sphere: nucleons are excluded from the interior of the Fermi sea (filled by quarks) and reside only in an outer shell, characterized by an effective chemical potential μ_B'. The thermodynamic potential is computed at mean-field level for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, and model parameters are fixed by fitting vacuum hadron properties and empirical nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: E/N = -16 MeV
- Critical chemical potential at saturation: μ_Bc = 923 MeV
- Best-fit vacuum quark mass: m_q^v = 370.4 ± 0.8 MeV
- Nucleon fraction at saturation density: R_N ≈ 85.8%
- Coupling constants at best fit: g_Nv ≈ 7.2, g_sv ≈ 81
- Chiral condensate at the new minimum at μ_Bc: ⟨σ⟩ = 69.2 MeV
- Compressibility constraint from experiments: K_0 = 240 ± 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007, 2008) by providing a field theoretical framework that goes beyond simple picture implementations. It improves upon prior studies that lacked dynamical treatment of chiral symmetry restoration and did not carefully check constraints from nuclear matter saturation properties. The model enables systematic calculations of quarkyonic matter properties relevant to neutron star interiors, and the authors plan to explore implications for compact stars in future work, following the approach of McLerran (2018).
