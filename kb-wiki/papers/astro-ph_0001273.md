# Thermodynamic properties of nuclear "pasta" in neutron star crusts

> Equilibrium phase diagrams for neutron star matter at subnuclear densities are obtained at zero temperature. Spherical, rod-like and slab-like nuclei as well as spherical and rod-like nuclear bubbles are taken into account by using a compressible liquid-drop model. This model is designed to incorporate uncertainties in the nuclear surface tension and in the proton chemical potential in a gas of dripped neutrons. The resultant phase diagrams show that for typical values of these quantities, the phases with rod-like nuclei and with slab-like nuclei occur in the form of Coulomb lattice at densities below a point where the system becomes uniform. Thermal fluctuations leading to displacements of such nuclei from their equilibrium positions are considered through explicit evaluations of their elastic constants; these fluctuations can be effective at destroying the layered lattice of slab-like nuclei in the temperature region typical of matter in the neutron star crust.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for typical values of the nuclear surface tension and proton chemical potential in the neutron gas, only the phases with rod-like nuclei and slab-like nuclei survive in the equilibrium phase diagram, while the phases with rod-like and spherical bubbles exist only for unrealistically low surface tension, a qualification that the abstract does not explicitly state.

## Method

The authors constructed a compressible liquid-drop model, generalizing the approach of Baym, Bethe and Pethick (1971), to compute the energy of neutron star matter at subnuclear densities for five nuclear shapes: spherical nuclei, cylindrical (rod-like) nuclei, planar (slab-like) nuclei, cylindrical bubbles, and spherical bubbles. They minimized the total energy density with respect to nucleon density, proton fraction, neutron gas density, nuclear size, and volume fraction under fixed baryon density and charge neutrality, then compared the resulting energies to identify the ground-state phase. Thermal fluctuations were evaluated by computing elastic constants for the rod-like and slab-like phases following Pethick and Potekhin (1999) and estimating mean-square displacements at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip density is approximately \(4 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- The normal saturation density \(\rho_{\rm s}\) is approximately \(3 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- The system dissolves into uniform nuclear matter at a density of about \(\rho_{\rm s}/2\).
- The energy difference between two successive phases is generally of order 0.1–1 keV per nucleon.
- For the typical nuclear model (\(C_1 = 400\) MeV fm\(^2\) and \(C_2 = 1.0\)), curvature effects lower the transition density from sphere to cylinder and from cylinder to slab by 10% or so.
- The quantum-classical crossover temperature \(T_0\) is of order \(10^7\) K for planar nuclei and of order \(10^9\) K for cylindrical nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of nuclear "pasta" phases by Ravenhall et al. (1983) and Hashimoto et al. (1984) by systematically incorporating uncertainties in the nuclear surface tension and proton chemical potential that previous authors such as Lorenz et al. (1993), Oyamatsu (1993), and Sumiyoshi et al. (1995) had treated as fixed. The phase diagrams show a simpler shape sequence (sphere → cylinder → slab → uniform matter) than those earlier works, which the authors attribute to differences in the treatment of uniform nuclear matter at intermediate proton fractions. The thermal fluctuation analysis, using elastic constants from Pethick and Potekhin (1999), enables estimates of the temperatures at which the ordered pasta structures are destroyed, relevant for understanding neutron star crust properties such as vortex pinning and cooling.
