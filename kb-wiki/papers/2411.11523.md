# New multinucleon knockout model in NuWro Monte Carlo generator

> We present the implementation and results of a new model for the n-particle n-hole ($\it{np-nh}$) contribution in the NuWro event generator, grounded in the theoretical framework established by the Valencia group in 2020. For the $\it{2p2h}$ component, we introduce a novel nucleon sampling function with tunable parameters to approximate correlations in the momenta of outgoing nucleons. These parameters are calibrated by comparing our results to those of the Valencia model across a range of incoming neutrino energies. In addition, our model incorporates a distinct contribution from the $\it{3p3h}$ mechanism. We discuss the differences between the new NuWro implementation, the original Valencia model, and the previous NuWro version, focusing on the distribution of outgoing nucleon momenta. Finally, we assess the impact of the hadronic model on experimental analyses involving hadronic observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new model for multinucleon knockout (np-nh) contributions in the NuWro event generator, grounded in the 2020 Valencia model, but introduces a tunable nucleon sampling function with parameters (P, l) to approximate correlations in outgoing nucleon momenta rather than reproducing the exact theoretical correlations, justified by the argument that the hadronic model only needs to exceed the precision of the final-state interaction (FSI) model.

## Method

The authors implemented a new scheme for meson-exchange current (MEC) events in NuWro that separates 2p2h and 3p3h contributions, using a Monte Carlo algorithm to select event topology (pp, np, pn, or 3p3h) based on weights from the inclusive cross-section. For 2p2h, they developed a two-parameter nucleon sampling function f(cosθ*) that controls the angular distribution of outgoing nucleons in the hadronic center-of-mass frame, with parameters (P, l) calibrated by minimizing a χ²-like quantity comparing two-dimensional momentum distributions from NuWro to those from the 2020 Valencia model across five neutrino energies. For 3p3h, the isospin of final-state nucleons is determined by a combinatorial model based on the number of possible nucleon sets, and kinematics are modeled using a three-body phase-space approach.

## Key numbers

* Best-fit parameters for the pp nucleon pair: (P, l)_pp = (0.77, 4), obtained from a global fit across neutrino energies E_ν ∈ {0.5, 0.7, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0} GeV.
* Best-fit parameters for np and pn nucleon pairs: (P, l)_np = (0.7, 3) and (P, l)_pn = (0.8, 4), obtained from a simultaneous global fit.
* The 2020 Valencia model imposes a constraint on momentum transfer |q| < 1.2 GeV, consistent with the older model.
* The distribution of energy transfer in the new Valencia model has two peaks: one at ω ≈ 0.15 GeV and another at ω ≈ 0.45 GeV.
* The boundary between two kinematic regions in the (ω, |q|) plane was tested at ω [GeV] = (0.65/1.2) · |q| [GeV/c], but no significant improvement was found from splitting the parameter space.

## Context

This work extends the 2020 Valencia model (Sobczyk, 2020) by implementing its 2p2h and 3p3h contributions into the NuWro event generator, which has been developed since 2004 (Juszczak, 2005; Golan, 2012). The new model replaces the earlier NuWro MEC implementation (Sobczyk, 2012) that used a uniform nucleon sampling function, and it is motivated by the need for more precise modeling of hadronic observables in neutrino-nucleus experiments, following the excess of CC0π events observed by the MiniBooNE collaboration (2010). The approach enables future experimental analyses to assess the impact of hadronic modeling on observables involving outgoing nucleons, while acknowledging that FSI carries significant uncertainty (Niewczas, 2019).
