# Low-energy dipole strength in 112,120Sn

> The 112,120Sn(gamma,gamma') reactions below the neutron separation energies have been studied at the superconducting Darmstadt electron linear accelerator S-DALINAC for different endpoint energies of the incident bremsstrahlung spectrum. Dipole strength distributions are extracted for 112Sn up to 9.5 MeV and for 120Sn up to 9.1 MeV. A concentration of dipole excitations is observed between 5 and 8 MeV in both nuclei. Missing strength due to unobserved decays to excited states is estimated in a statistical model. A fluctuation analysis is applied to the photon scattering spectra to extract the amount of the unresolved strength hidden in background due to fragmentation. The strength distributions are discussed within different model approaches such as the quasiparticle-phonon model and the relativistic time blocking approximation allowing for an inclusion of complex configurations beyond the initial particle-hole states. While a satisfactory description of the fragmentation can be achieved for sufficently large model spaces, the predicted centroids and total electric dipole strengths for stable tin isotopes strongly depend on the assumptions about the underlying mean field.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the summed electric dipole (E1) strength below the neutron separation energy in stable tin isotopes does not increase monotonically with neutron number as theoretical models predict; instead, 120Sn shows the lowest summed strength despite having more neutrons than 112Sn, and the predicted centroids and total E1 strengths strongly depend on the assumptions about the underlying mean field.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments using the (gamma,gamma') reaction at the S-DALINAC facility with bremsstrahlung endpoint energies of 5.5, 7.0, and 9.5 MeV for 112Sn and 7.5 and 9.1 MeV for 120Sn. Dipole strength distributions were extracted from resolved transitions, and a fluctuation analysis was applied to the photon scattering spectra to extract unresolved strength hidden in the background due to fragmentation. Missing strength from unobserved decays to excited states was estimated using a statistical model approach.

## Key numbers

- Summed B(E1) transition strength in 112Sn: 0.187(25) e2fm2 up to 9.5 MeV, corresponding to 0.25% of the E1 energy-weighted sum rule
- Summed B(E1) transition strength in 120Sn: 0.163(31) e2fm2 up to 9.1 MeV, corresponding to 0.22% of the EWSR
- Centroid energy of low-energy E1 strength: 6.74 MeV for 112Sn and 6.60 MeV for 120Sn
- 91 dipole transitions observed in 112Sn up to 9.5 MeV endpoint energy
- 72 dipole transitions observed in 120Sn up to 9.1 MeV endpoint energy
- Statistical model correction increases summed strength by 23% (20%) for 112Sn and 39% (29%) for 120Sn using the level density parameterizations of Rauscher (1997) and von Egidy and Bucurescu (2005), respectively
- Averaged branching ratios to excited states rise from about 10% at Ex = 5 MeV to about 40% at Ex = 9 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous NRF measurements on 116,124Sn by Gov98 to 112Sn and 120Sn, establishing systematics of low-energy E1 strength across the stable even-mass tin isotope chain. The results are compared with theoretical predictions from the quasiparticle-phonon model and the relativistic time blocking approximation, which include complex configurations beyond initial particle-hole states. The findings enable future comparisons with experiments on exotic tin isotopes such as 130,132Sn reported by Kli07 and Adr05, and with new GSI experiments on 124-134Sn, providing a benchmark for models of the pygmy dipole resonance as a function of neutron excess.
