# Finite-nuclear-size effect for hydrogenlike ions under high external pressure

> The influence of pressure on finite-nuclear-size corrections to atomic energy levels and electron-capture decay rate is investigated in confined hydrogenlike ions. The ions are modeled inside an impenetrable spherical cavity, with a Gaussian distribution used to represent the nuclear charge distribution. For each confinement radius used to simulate external pressure, the energies and wave functions of the lowest-lying bound states are determined by numerically solving the Dirac equation via the kinetically balanced generalized pseudospectral method. In contrast to unconfined ions, both the FNS corrections and electron-capture decay rates increase markedly under pressure and exhibit parallel trends with increasing confinement. Pressure also removes level degeneracies and alters the relative magnitudes of FNS corrections across different bound states. Moreover, the nuclear charge radius is found to significantly affect the pressure-enhanced electron-capture decay rate.

## Key claim

Under high external pressure, both the finite-nuclear-size (FNS) corrections to atomic energy levels and the electron-capture decay rate in hydrogenlike ions increase markedly compared to unconfined ions, with the effect becoming significant only as pressure approaches a system-dependent critical pressure where the ground-state energy reaches zero.

## Method

The authors modeled hydrogenlike ions inside an impenetrable spherical cavity of variable confinement radius to simulate external pressure, using a Gaussian charge distribution for the nucleus. They solved the Dirac equation numerically via the kinetically balanced generalized pseudospectral (KBGPS) method to obtain energies and wave functions for the lowest-lying bound states, from which FNS corrections and electron densities at the nucleus were computed. The hydrostatic pressure was estimated from the derivative of the ground-state energy with respect to the confinement volume, and the fractional increase in electron-capture decay rate was taken as proportional to the fractional increase in electron density at the nucleus.

## Key numbers

* For the $1s_{1/2}$ state of $^{7}\mathrm{Be}^{3+}$, the critical pressure (where the energy level reaches zero) is $7\times 10^{5}$ GPa; for $^{56}\mathrm{Fe}^{25+}$ it is $8\times 10^{9}$ GPa; for $^{120}\mathrm{Sn}^{49+}$ it is $2\times 10^{11}$ GPa; and for $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}^{81+}$ it is $4\times 10^{12}$ GPa.
* At approximately 100 GPa, the FNS correction $\Delta E_{\mathrm{FNS}}$ in $^{7}\mathrm{Be}^{3+}$ is about seven orders of magnitude smaller than in $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}^{81+}$; as pressure approaches $10^{16}$ GPa, this difference reduces to three orders of magnitude.
* For the $1s_{1/2}$ state of $^{7}\mathrm{Be}^{3+}$, the ratio of the electron-capture decay rate under pressure to that at zero pressure increases by approximately seven orders of magnitude over the pressure range studied.
* At a pressure of $10^{6}$ GPa (roughly the solar core pressure), the fractional increase in electron-capture decay rate $(\lambda - \lambda_0)/\lambda_0$ for Pr isotopes rises from 0.002 to 0.003 as the nuclear charge radius $r_c$ increases from 4.823 to 4.899 fm.

## Context

This work extends the confined hydrogen atom model, originally proposed by Michels (1937), to relativistic hydrogenlike ions under pressure, building on prior nonrelativistic studies of FNS corrections in confined hydrogen by Aquino (2018). The results show that pressure can significantly alter both FNS energy corrections and electron-capture decay rates, with implications for stellar nucleosynthesis processes such as hydrogen burning in the Sun, where $^{7}\mathrm{Be}^{3+}$ ions are present. The findings also motivate future studies of pressure effects on electron-capture decay in multi-electron systems and in high-pressure solid phases.
