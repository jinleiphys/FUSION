# Landau-Migdal vs. Skyrme

> The magnitude and density-dependence of the non-spin dependent Landau-Migdal parameters are derived from Skyrme energy functionals and compared with the phenomenological ones. We perform RPA calculations with various approximations for the Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction and compare them with the results obtained with the full Skyrme interaction. For the first time the next to leading order in the Landau-Migdal approach is considered in nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Landau-Migdal approximation, which neglects momentum-dependent (kinetic) terms in the particle-hole interaction, can be seriously inadequate for describing isoscalar giant resonances in Skyrme-based RPA calculations, although it performs better for the isovector giant dipole resonance when the effective mass is near unity and the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule enhancement is small.

## Method

The authors derived Landau-Migdal parameters from Skyrme energy-density functionals using nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, symmetry energy, effective mass, and TRK sum rule enhancement) and compared them with phenomenological values. They then performed RPA calculations for giant resonances in 208Pb using two Skyrme parametrizations (SLy4 and SkT6) with different effective masses, comparing the full Skyrme residual interaction (including momentum-dependent terms) against the Landau approximation where the direct momentum-dependent term is omitted and the exchange term is expanded in Legendre polynomials up to p-wave order.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor C0 is typically 150 MeV fm3 for phenomenological shell models with effective mass m*/m = 1
- For SV-min at bulk equilibrium density (rho = 0.16 fm-3): f1 = -0.144 +/- 0.215 and f'1 = 0.071 +/- 0.781
- For SV-bas at bulk equilibrium density (rho = 0.16 fm-3): f1 = -0.302 +/- 0.000 and f'1 = 0.778 +/- 0.010
- Empirical Landau-Migdal parameters: |f1| approximately 0.1-0.2 and |f'1| less than or equal to 0.1
- The SLy4 parametrization has effective mass m*/m = 0.68 and TRK sum rule enhancement kappa_TRK = 0.25
- The SkT6 parametrization has effective mass m*/m = 1.0 and vanishing kappa_TRK
- The RPA calculations used a computational box of 18 fm radius and single-particle states below 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing comparison between Landau-Migdal theory and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approaches, following earlier studies by Jackson (1975) and the Munich-Julich group (Zamick, 1974; Review, 1977). The paper uses modern Skyrme parametrizations SV-min and SV-bas from Klupfel et al. (2009) that incorporate systematic uncertainty propagation from least-squares fits. The results show that while the density dependence of Landau-Migdal parameters from Skyrme functionals agrees with phenomenological values in the nuclear surface region (rho approximately 0.1 fm-3), the Landau approximation for the residual interaction fails for isoscalar modes, particularly for parametrizations with low effective mass. This enables future work to incorporate next-to-leading order terms in Landau-Migdal calculations for finite nuclei, especially for medium-mass and light nuclei where effective mass effects are more significant.
