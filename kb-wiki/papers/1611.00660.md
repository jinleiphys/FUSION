# Dissipationless Hall Current in Dense Quark Matter in a Magnetic Field

> We show the realization of axion electrodynamics within the Dual Chiral Density Wave phase of dense quark matter in the presence of a magnetic field. The system exhibits an anomalous dissipantionless Hall current perpendicular to the magnetic field and an anomalous electric charge density. Connection to topological insulators and 3D optical lattices, as well as possible implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Dual Chiral Density Wave (DCDW) phase of dense quark matter in a magnetic field realizes axion electrodynamics, producing a dissipationless Hall current and an anomalous electric charge density, but only when both the DCDW condensate (with modulation parameter q) and the magnetic field are present; the effect vanishes if either B=0 or m=0.

## Method

The authors model cold, dense quark matter in a constant magnetic field using an NJL-QED Lagrangian with a four-fermion interaction. They analyze the mean-field DCDW condensate, perform a local chiral transformation to eliminate the z-dependent mass term, and then apply Fujikawa's regularization to account for the non-invariance of the fermion measure, which generates an axion term in the effective Lagrangian. The resulting equations of motion are those of axion electrodynamics, and the LLL quark propagator is computed to verify that the ordinary current does not cancel the anomalous contributions.

## Key numbers

- Anomalous baryon number density: $\rho^A_B = 3\frac{|e|}{4\pi^2} q B$ for the case $q < 2m$.
- Anomalous electric charge density: $J^{anom}_0 = \frac{e^2}{4\pi^2} q B$.
- Anomalous Hall current density: $\mathbf{J}^{anom} = -\frac{e^2}{4\pi^2} \mathbf{q} \times \mathbf{E}$.
- Hall conductance: $\sigma_H = e^2 q / 4\pi^2$.
- Inhomogeneity length scale of the DCDW phase: $\Delta x = \hbar / q \sim 0.6$ fm for $q \sim \mu = 300$ MeV.
- Ordinary LLL electric charge density at zero temperature: $J_{LLL}^0 = \frac{e^2 B}{2\pi^2} \sqrt{(\mu - q/2)^2 - m^2} \, \Theta(|\mu - q/2| - m)$.

## Context

This work extends the study of axion electrodynamics from condensed matter systems (topological insulators, Weyl semimetals) to dense QCD, specifically the magnetic DCDW phase. It contrasts with the high-temperature Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) mechanism, where the magnetic field serves only as a probe of QCD vacuum topology; here the magnetic field is essential for producing the nontrivial topology via LLL spectral asymmetry. The results connect to topological insulators (Qi et al., 2008) and 3D optical lattices (PRL 105), and have potential implications for magnetar cores and future heavy-ion collision experiments at NICA and FAIR. The paper also notes that the anomalous Hall current differs from the quantized Hall conductance found at the surface of topological insulators.
