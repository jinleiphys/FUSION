# New computational methods in lattice gauge theory -- quantum computation and tensor networks

> Lattice QCD calculations have been conducted using large-scale classical computers based on the Lagrangian formalism of field theory for the past 40 years. On the other hand, the advent of quantum computers has brought increasing attention to first-principles computational methods based on the Hamiltonian formalism compatible with these machines. In this talk, we discuss recent results on how to calculate hadronic properties using the Hamiltonian formalism.

## Key claim

The Hamiltonian formalism, combined with tensor network methods (DMRG), can compute hadron mass spectra in the 2-flavor Schwinger model even in the presence of a topological theta-term, where conventional Lagrangian Monte Carlo methods suffer from a sign problem; the dispersion-relation scheme works without modification for nonzero theta, unlike correlation-function or one-point-function schemes that require prior knowledge of mixing angles.

## Method

The authors discretize the 2-flavor Schwinger model on an open interval using staggered fermions and apply the Jordan-Wigner transformation to obtain a spin Hamiltonian with finite-dimensional Hilbert space. They then use the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) method with matrix product states (MPS) to compute excited states up to the 23rd level, identifying meson species by measuring isospin, parity, and G-parity quantum numbers, and extracting masses via the dispersion relation E = sqrt(M^2 + K^2). For nonzero theta, they apply the same dispersion-relation scheme without modification, while also using a modified one-point-function scheme for cross-check.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size N=100, lattice spacing a=0.20, cutoff parameter epsilon=10^{-10}, number of DMRG sweeps N_sweep = 50 or 80
- At theta=0, the mass ordering is m_pi < m_sigma < m_eta, with m_sigma/m_pi = sqrt(3) and m_eta ~ sqrt(2/pi) g ~ 0.8
- The theta-dependence of pion mass follows M_pi(0) |cos(theta/2)|^{2/3} and sigma mass follows sqrt(3) M_pi(0) |cos(theta/2)|^{2/3}, as predicted by bosonization analyses by Coleman (1976)
- At theta=pi, the theory becomes nearly conformal (SU(2) level-1 Wess-Zumino-Witten model), with a tiny mass gap M_pi ~ exp(-# g^2/m^2)

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian formalism for lattice gauge theory originally proposed by Kogut and Susskind (1975) to compute hadron spectra in the 2-flavor Schwinger model, a testbed for QCD-like phenomena. It contrasts with conventional Lagrangian Monte Carlo methods that suffer from the sign problem at nonzero theta, as seen in the work of Fukaya and Onogi (2003) who could only obtain clear data for small theta and could not resolve sigma and eta mesons. The results enable future quantum computations of gauge theories with around 40 logical qubits, as the DMRG-based methods can be straightforwardly ported to quantum algorithms.
