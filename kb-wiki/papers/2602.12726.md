# Phase diagrams of BCS-BEC crossover in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The phase structure of the BCS-BEC crossover for neutron-proton superfluid in asymmetric nuclear matter is systematically investigated, with particular focus on the roles of the angle-dependent gap (ADG) and the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) states. Phase diagrams in the T-alpha, alpha-rho, and T-rho planes are constructed using both angle-averaged and angle-dependent gap treatments, enabling a unified analysis of the interplay between the FFLO pairing, ADG, and normal-superfluid phase separation (PS). The results confirm that the crossover is primarily density-driven. In the weak-coupling BCS regime, isospin asymmetry suppresses the stability of the homogeneous superfluid phase and drives the system toward PS, while the FFLO and ADG mechanisms partially alleviate this suppression. Although the ADG itself does not extend the asymmetry window for superfluidity, in combination with the FFLO state it enlarges the asymmetry range over which superfluidity survives and significantly reduces PS. At high density, these combined effects can nearly eliminate PS. However, as density decreases, ADG-induced suppression of PS is progressively weakened, due to both the reduced destructive effect of isospin asymmetry and the decreasing D-wave fraction in the 3SD1 channel. In general, the system evolves smoothly from a D-wave-dominated superfluid at high density to an S-wave superfluid at low density, with a corresponding weakening of ADG effects. Furthermore, the ADG lifts the orientational degeneracy of the FFLO state, resulting in two distinct FFLO-ADG phases separated by a first-order transition. In contrast, in the BEC regime, the FFLO and ADG states vanish, while the PS persists, leading to an inhomogeneous mixed phase at low temperatures and large asymmetries, where the superfluid component forms a BEC of deuterons.

## Key claim

The paper systematically maps the phase structure of the BCS-BEC crossover for neutron-proton superfluid in asymmetric nuclear matter, showing that while the angle-dependent gap (ADG) alone does not extend the asymmetry window for superfluidity, its combination with the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state enlarges the asymmetry range over which superfluidity survives and significantly reduces normal-superfluid phase separation (PS), with these combined effects nearly eliminating PS at high density.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature gap equation and density constraints self-consistently using the Argonne V18 (Av18) nucleon-nucleon potential in the mixed \(^3SD_1\) channel, treating both angle-averaged (AAG) and angle-dependent (ADG) pairing gaps. For the FFLO state, the magnitude \(q\) and orientation \(\theta_0\) of the Cooper pair momentum are determined by minimizing the free energy, and thermodynamic stability against phase separation is evaluated via the eigenvalues of the matrix \(\partial^2 \mathcal{F} / \partial \rho_\tau \partial \rho_{\tau'}\). Phase diagrams are constructed in the \(T-\alpha\), \(\alpha-\rho\), and \(T-\rho\) planes over wide ranges of density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.17~\text{fm}^{-3}\) is used as the reference density throughout.
- At saturation density \(\rho_0\), the ADG and AAG phases vanish at the same critical asymmetry \(\alpha_c\), with no extension of the asymmetry window by ADG alone.
- The critical asymmetry \(\alpha_c\) approaches unity for \(\rho_0/1000\) at low temperature in the BEC regime.
- Phase diagrams are computed at temperature \(T = 0.1\) MeV for the \(\alpha-\rho\) plane, covering densities from \(\rho_0\) down to \(\rho_0/1000\).
- Near (\(\alpha = 0.3\), \(\rho = 2.5\rho_0\)), a stable region of the ADG state is identified that satisfies the uniform superfluid phase criterion.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter by Baldo (1995), Lombardo (2001), and others, which used angle-averaged gap treatments, and builds on the finding by Shang (2013) and Duan (2025) that angular dependence of the pairing gap can mitigate asymmetry suppression in the weak-coupling BCS regime. The results enable future investigations of neutron-proton pairing in neutron star crusts and supernova matter, where density-driven crossover and isospin asymmetry are relevant, and provide a framework for comparing with ultracold atomic Fermi gas experiments by Zwierlein (2006) and Partridge (2006) that exhibit analogous FFLO and phase separation phenomena.
