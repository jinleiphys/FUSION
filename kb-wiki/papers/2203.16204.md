# Geometrical Scaling of Direct Photons in Relativistic Heavy Ion and d+Au Collisions

> In this paper, we show that multiplicity spectra of direct photons in A+A and d+Au collisions at different centrality classes and different energies exhibit geometrical scaling, {\em i.e.}, they depend on a specific combination of number of participants $N_{\rm part}$, collisions energy $W$, and transverse momentum $p_{T}$ -- called saturation scale -- rather than on all these three variables separately. In particular, the dependence on the geometry of collisions encoded in the dependence on $N_{\rm part}$ is in agreement with the expectations based on the Color Glass Condensate theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that direct photon transverse momentum spectra in heavy ion and d+Au collisions exhibit geometrical scaling, meaning they depend on a specific combination of number of participants, collision energy, and transverse momentum rather than on these variables separately; however, the energy-scaling exponent lambda is found to be substantially lower (approximately 0.2) than the canonical value of approximately 0.33 from deep inelastic scattering, suggesting scaling violations or contributions not directly related to initial state physics.

## Method

The authors analyzed direct photon invariant yield data from PHENIX (Au+Au at 62.4 and 200 GeV, Cu+Cu at 200 GeV, d+Au at 200 GeV) and ALICE (Pb+Pb at 2760 GeV) across multiple centrality classes. They tested geometrical scaling by plotting N_part^delta-scaled spectra against the scaling variable tau = p_T/Q_sat, where the saturation scale Q_sat depends on N_part, collision energy, and p_T, and examined whether ratios of scaled spectra from different centrality classes and energies equal unity over extended tau intervals, without assuming any specific functional form for the scaling function.

## Key numbers

- The best value for the centrality-scaling exponent delta is 2/3, determined by comparing scaled PHENIX Au+Au 200 GeV and ALICE Pb+Pb 2760 GeV data across centrality classes
- The best value for the energy-scaling exponent lambda is between 0.1 and 0.2, with lambda = 0.2 used in the final analysis; the canonical value of 0.33 is excluded
- For d+Au collisions, the number of participants in the deuteron is taken as n_part = 1.62, and the number of participants in the Gold nucleus is taken as N_part = cube root of 197, approximately 5.8, giving a total volume of 8.03, close to the minimum bias value of 8.6 plus or minus 0.4
- The data sets cover beam energies from 62.4 GeV to 2760 GeV, with N_part values ranging from 12.5 (Au+Au 60-92% centrality) to 308.0 (Pb+Pb 0-20% centrality)

## Context

This work extends the initial study of Klein-Bosing (2014) on geometrical scaling in direct photon production, using newer experimental data and a model-independent method that does not assume a specific functional form like p_T^{-n} for the photon spectra. The analysis is grounded in the Color Glass Condensate theory (McLerran, 2010) and follows earlier observations of geometrical scaling in deep inelastic scattering (Stasto, 2000) and charged particle spectra in hadronic collisions (McLerran, 2010; Praszalowicz, 2015). The finding that direct photons in heavy ion collisions exhibit scaling originating from the initial gluon wave function is remarkable because photons are produced from quarks, not directly from gluons. The results enable further studies of small-large collision systems and may help resolve the "thermal photon puzzle" by establishing initial-state contributions to direct photon production.
