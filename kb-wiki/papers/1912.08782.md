# Accretion-induced collapse to third family compact stars as trigger for eccentric orbits of millisecond pulsars in binaries

> A numerical rotating neutron star solver is used to study the temporal evolution of accreting neutron stars using a multi-polytrope model for the nuclear equation of state named ACB5. The solver is based on a quadrupole expansion of the metric, but confirms the results of previous works, revealing the possibility of an abrupt transition of a neutron star from a purely hadronic branch to a third-family branch of stable hybrid stars, passing through an unstable intermediate branch. The accretion is described through a sequence of stationary rotating {stellar} configurations which lose angular momentum through magnetic dipole emission while, at the same time, gaining angular momentum through mass accretion. The model has several free parameters which are inferred from observations. The mass accretion scenario is studied in dependence on the effectiveness of angular momentum transfer which determines at which spin frequency the neutron star will become unstable against gravitational collapse to the corresponding hybrid star on the stable third-family branch. It is conceivable that the neutrino burst which accompanies the deconfinement transition may trigger a pulsar kick which results in the eccentric orbit. A consequence of the present model is the prediction of a correlation between the spin frequency of the millisecond pulsar in the eccentric orbit and its mass at birth.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that accretion-induced collapse of a neutron star to a hybrid star on a third-family branch, accompanied by a neutrino burst that triggers a pulsar kick, can explain the observed eccentric orbits of millisecond pulsars in binaries with white dwarf companions, but only if the collapse occurs after a spin-down phase (delayed collapse) rather than promptly during accretion, to avoid rapid orbital re-circularization.

## Method

The authors used a numerical rotating neutron star solver based on the Hartle-Thorne perturbation method (quadrupole expansion of the metric) to compute stationary rotating configurations of compact stars with the ACB5 multi-polytrope equation of state. They modeled accretion as a sequence of such configurations where the star gains angular momentum from infalling matter and loses angular momentum through magnetic dipole emission, governed by an evolution equation for angular momentum as a function of baryon mass. The magnetic field decay during accretion was described by a phenomenological burial model with a free parameter \(m_B\) in the range \(10^{-5} - 10^{-4} M_\odot\).

## Key numbers

- The ACB5 equation of state produces a hadronic branch maximum mass of \(1.40 M_\odot\) and a hybrid branch maximum mass of \(2.00 M_\odot\), with the minimal mass on the hybrid branch at \(1.39 M_\odot\).
- The onset mass for the strong phase transition in the static case is at or below \(1.353^{+0.014}_{-0.017} M_\odot\), the lowest well-measured mass for eccentric millisecond pulsars (PSR J2234+0611).
- The accretion rate used is \(\dot{M}_b \cong 10^{-9} M_\odot\) with initial magnetic field \(B_i \cong 10^8\) G.
- The observed eccentricities of the millisecond pulsars in binaries are \(e \simeq 0.01 - 0.15\) for orbital periods between 15 and 50 days.

## Context

This work extends the scenario of rotationally delayed accretion-induced collapse proposed by Freire and Tauris (2013) and the suggestion by Jiang et al. (2015) of an accretion-induced phase transition to a strange quark star as an origin for eccentric orbits. It contrasts with earlier work by Glendenning (1997) where quark deconfinement in accreting low-mass binaries would take a secular time scale of \(10^6 - 10^8\) years, whereas the present scenario allows for a prompt collapse at the free-fall time scale. The model predicts a correlation between spin frequency and birth mass of the millisecond pulsar that could serve as a diagnostic tool for the equation of state of dense matter, and it awaits testing by precise mass measurements of highly spun-up pulsars like PSR J0955-6150.
