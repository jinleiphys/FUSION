# Neutron-skin effect in direct-photon and charged hadron-production in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> A well-established observation in nuclear physics is that in neutron-rich spherical nuclei the distribution of neutrons extends farther than the distribution of protons. In this work, we scrutinize the influence of this so called neutron-skin effect on the centrality dependence of high-$p_{\rm T}$ direct-photon and charged-hadron production. We find that due to the estimated spatial dependence of the nuclear parton distribution functions, it will be demanding to unambiguously expose the neutron-skin effect with direct photons. However, when taking a ratio between the cross sections for negatively and positively charged high-$p_{\rm T}$ hadrons, even centrality-dependent nuclear-PDF effects cancel, making this observable a better handle on the neutron skin. Up to 20~\% effects can be expected for the most peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The neutron-skin effect can produce up to 10% effects in the ratio of negatively to positively charged high-pT hadrons in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, but for direct-photon production the effect is of the same order as the uncertainties from nuclear parton distribution functions and their spatial dependence, making unambiguous exposure of the neutron-skin effect demanding.

## Method

The authors used an optical Glauber model with separate two-parameter Fermi distributions for protons and neutrons in lead nuclei, taking neutron parameters from Tarbert (2013). They calculated centrality-dependent hard-process cross sections at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD using the Incnlo program, with EPS09s nuclear modifications for the spatial dependence of nuclear parton distribution functions and CT10NLO free-nucleon PDFs. For hadron production they used three different parton-to-hadron fragmentation function sets (DSS, Kretzer, AKK08) and for direct photons the BFG set II fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron 2pF parameters for lead: d_n,Pb = 6.70 ± 0.03 fm and a_n,Pb = 0.55 ± 0.03 fm; proton parameters fixed at d_p,Pb = 6.680 fm and a_p,Pb = 0.447 fm
- For direct-photon production in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV, the neutron-skin effect gives a 5-10% impact on R_PbPb^gamma in peripheral collisions
- For the ratio h-/h+ in charged-hadron production at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV, up to 10% effects are expected for the most peripheral collisions in the measurable region
- The realistically measurable pT region is pT < 200 GeV/c for the 70-80% centrality bin and pT < 100 GeV/c for the 90-100% bin with 1 nb^-1 nucleon-nucleon luminosity

## Context

This work extends the study of the neutron-skin effect from Paukkunen (2015) on W± production to direct-photon and charged-hadron production in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The results show that while direct-photon production is challenging due to competing nPDF effects, the charged-hadron charge ratio provides a cleaner observable, potentially enabling future calibration of centrality classification in heavy-ion collisions and providing constraints for fragmentation function analyses. The authors plan to study the neutron-skin effect in future high-luminosity lepton-ion colliders.
