# Low and moderate $x$ gluon contribution to exclusive Compton scattering processes

> We revisit the high energy semi-classical description of the exclusive processes DVCS, TCS, and Double DVCS by explicitly keeping track of the Feynman $x$ dependence in both the hard and the hadronic matrix elements. This is achieved by a modification of the standard shock wave approximation to derive the effective Feynman rules, which leads to a generic expression on which we then perform a partial twist expansion to get rid of quantities suppressed by the proper physical scales. We obtain a compact factorized master formula that can be used to investigate the Bjorken limit at leading twist. In particular, we recover the full one-loop result in the collinear limit for pure gluon exchange with the target. Finally, we discuss the subtleties in taking the simultaneous collinear and small $x$ limit.

## Key claim

The authors derive a unified factorized master formula for exclusive Compton scattering processes (DVCS, TCS, DDVCS) that interpolates between the Bjorken limit and the Regge limit by explicitly keeping the Feynman \(x\) dependence in both the hard and hadronic matrix elements, rather than setting \(x=0\) as in the standard shock wave approximation.

## Method

The authors modify the standard shock wave approximation to derive effective Feynman rules that retain the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) dependence in the background field. They then perform a Partial Twist Expansion (PTE) on the resulting amplitude to eliminate terms suppressed by the physical scales, obtaining a compact factorized expression involving unintegrated gluon distributions \(G(x,k_\perp)\) and hard subamplitudes \(H(x,k_\perp)\). The method is applied to the gluon-mediated amplitude for \(\gamma^{(\ast)}P\rightarrow\gamma^{(\ast)}P\) scattering, and the Bjorken limit is recovered by neglecting transverse momentum in the hard subamplitude while the Regge limit is recovered by setting \(x=0\) in the distribution.

## Key numbers

* The energy denominators for the photon wave functions take the form \(-[(\boldsymbol{k}_1-z_1\boldsymbol{q})^{2}+z_1\overline{z}_1 Q^{2}-i0]/(2z_1\overline{z}_1 q^{+})\) for the incoming photon and \(-[(\boldsymbol{k}_2-z_2\boldsymbol{q}^{\prime})^{2}-z_2\overline{z}_2 Q^{\prime2}-i0]/(2z_2\overline{z}_2 q^{\prime+})\) for the outgoing photon, where \(Q^{2}=-q^{2}\) and \(Q^{\prime2}=q^{\prime2}\).
* The longitudinal photon wave functions are \(\psi_{L}(z_{1},\boldsymbol{k}_1-z_{1}\boldsymbol{q})\equiv-2z_{1}\overline{z}_{1}Q\) for the incoming photon and \(\psi_{L}^{\prime\ast}(z_{2},\boldsymbol{k}_2-z_{2}\boldsymbol{q}^{\prime})\equiv2z_{2}\overline{z}_{2}Q^{\prime}\) for the outgoing photon.
* The Gaussian phase for a single propagator scales as \((\boldsymbol{x}_{2}-\boldsymbol{x}_{1})^{2}\sim 1/[(p^{\prime-}+p^{-})q^{+}]\), which is suppressed in both Regge and Bjorken regimes.

## Context

This work extends the effective framework previously developed by the same authors for inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (Boussarie 2020, Boussarie 2021) to the exclusive processes DVCS, TCS, and DDVCS. It generalizes the earlier study of DVCS at small \(x\) by Hatta (2017) and addresses inconsistencies in the collinear corner of phase space that arise when parton distributions are evaluated at the strict \(x=0\) limit, as noted in prior work by Bialas (2000) and Kutak (2004). The resulting formalism enables a consistent treatment of both the Bjorken limit (where QCD factorization with GPDs applies) and the Regge limit (where semi-classical effective approaches with Wilson lines dominate), and provides a framework for investigating the interplay between these two kinematic regimes.
