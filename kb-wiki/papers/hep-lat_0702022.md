# Infrared properties of propagators in Landau-gauge pure Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature

> The finite-temperature behavior of gluon and of Faddeev-Popov-ghost propagators is investigated for pure SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge. We present nonperturbative results, obtained using lattice simulations and Dyson-Schwinger equations. Possible limitations of these two approaches, such as finite-volume effects and truncation artifacts, are extensively discussed. Both methods suggest a very different temperature dependence for the magnetic sector when compared to the electric one. In particular, a clear thermodynamic transition seems to affect only the electric sector. These results imply in particular the confinement of transverse gluons at all temperatures and they can be understood inside the framework of the so-called Gribov-Zwanziger scenario of confinement.

## Key claim

Both lattice simulations and Dyson-Schwinger equations indicate that the magnetic (3d-transverse) sector of pure SU(2) Yang-Mills theory is essentially unaffected by temperature and the thermodynamic transition, while the electric (3d-longitudinal) sector shows clear sensitivity to the transition; however, the authors extensively discuss possible limitations of both approaches, including finite-volume effects and truncation artifacts, and note that the DSE results suggest the difference between the two sectors occurs for any non-zero temperature, not only above the phase transition.

## Method

The gluon and Faddeev-Popov ghost propagators were computed using lattice gauge theory with asymmetric lattices (reduced temporal extent) at four temperatures (T=0, 119, 298, and 597 MeV) for pure SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge, with multiple spatial volumes at each temperature to assess finite-volume effects. Separately, Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) were solved in the continuum to study the propagators in the limit of vanishing momentum, using the decomposition of the gluon propagator into 3d-transverse (magnetic) and 3d-longitudinal (electric) tensor structures.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for time extension N_t=4 is beta=2.299, corresponding to T_c approximately 295 MeV (Fingberg 1992)
- The highest temperature studied is T approximately 597 MeV, about twice the critical temperature T_c
- In the 3d case, the gluon propagator shows a maximum at p_max approximately 400 MeV on the largest lattices
- The DSE calculation for 3d yields a gluon propagator suppressed as p^0.59 in the IR limit and a ghost propagator enhanced as p^{-2.8} in the IR limit
- The DSE prediction for the 4d gluon propagator IR exponent is p^0.38 (weaker suppression than the 3d case)
- Lattice spacings used range from a^{-1}=1.193 GeV (beta=2.3) to a^{-1}=2.309 GeV (beta=2.5)
- Spatial volumes range from approximately 1.98 fm to 6.93 fm per side in the 4d finite-temperature runs

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by Cucchieri (2005) that focused on the high-temperature phase and by Cucchieri (2003) on the infinite-temperature (3d) limit, and it provides a systematic comparison between lattice and DSE methods across the thermodynamic transition. The results support the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario (Zwanziger 1994, Gribov 1978) and the Kugo-Ojima scenario (Kugo 1995), showing that transverse gluons remain confined at all temperatures while the electric sector undergoes a change across the transition. The findings enable future studies of the dimensionally-reduced 3d theory (Maas 2005, Appelquist 1981) and help reconcile the confining properties of that theory with the vanishing of the conventional string tension at high temperature.
