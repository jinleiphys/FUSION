# Evolution of Nuclear Shell Structure due to the Pion Exchange Potential

> The evolution of nuclear shell structure is investigated for the first time within density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory and the role of $π$-exchange potential is studied in detail. The energy differences between the neutron orbits $\Lrb{\nu1h_{9/2},ν1i_{13/2}}$ in the N=82 isotones and between the proton ones $\Lrb{\pi1g_{7/2},\pi1h_{11/2}}$ in the Z=50 isotopes are extracted as a function of neutron excess $N-Z$. A kink around $Z = 58$ for the N=82 isotones is found as an effect resulting from pion correlations. It is shown that the inclusion of $π$-coupling plays a central role to provide realistic isospin dependence of the energy differences. In particular, the tensor part of the $π$-coupling has an important effect on the characteristic isospin dependence observed in recent experiments.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the pion-exchange potential, particularly its tensor component, within density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory is shown to produce a kink in the energy difference between neutron orbits \(\nu 1h_{9/2}\) and \(\nu 1i_{13/2}\) in N=82 isotones around Z=58, and a similar improvement in the isospin dependence of proton orbit energy differences in Z=50 isotopes, although substantial discrepancies with experimental data remain.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent DDRHF calculations using three parameter sets (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) and compared them with relativistic mean field (RMF) results using the parameter set PKDD. The energy differences between the neutron orbits \(\nu 1h_{9/2}\) and \(\nu 1i_{13/2}\) in N=82 isotones from \(^{132}\)Sn to \(^{156}\)W, and between the proton orbits \(\pi 1g_{7/2}\) and \(\pi 1h_{11/2}\) in Z=50 isotopes from \(^{104}\)Sn to \(^{134}\)Sn, were extracted as a function of neutron excess \(N-Z\). Pairing correlations were treated by the BCS method with a density-dependent, zero-range pairing force.

## Key numbers

- A kink in the energy difference \(\Delta E(Z) = E_{\nu 1i_{13/2}} - E_{\nu 1h_{9/2}}\) for N=82 isotones is found at Z = 58 (N-Z = 24, corresponding to \(^{140}\)Ce) in the pion-dependent models PKO1 and PKO3.
- The nuclei studied along the N=82 isotones range from \(^{132}\)Sn to \(^{156}\)W, and the Sn isotopes with Z=50 range from \(^{104}\)Sn to \(^{134}\)Sn.
- The tensor \(\pi\)-coupling matrix elements between \(\nu 1h_{9/2}\) and \(\pi 1g_{7/2}\) or \(\pi 2d_{3/2}\) are repulsive, while those between \(\nu 1h_{9/2}\) and \(\pi 1h_{11/2}\) or \(\pi 2d_{5/2}\) are attractive, with opposite signs for \(\nu 1i_{13/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of shell evolution by Schiffer (2004) by applying for the first time the DDRHF theory to investigate the role of the one-pion exchange potential in single-particle spectra. The results contrast with the monotonous isospin dependence predicted by pion-independent models (PKO2 and PKDD) and show that the tensor part of the pion coupling provides a mechanism similar to that discussed by Otsuka (2005) in non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations. The findings enable future inclusion of dynamical coupling to RPA vibrations and \(\rho\)-tensor coupling to resolve remaining discrepancies with experiment.
