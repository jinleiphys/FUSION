# Kinetic freeze-out in central heavy-ion collisions between 7.7 and 2760 GeV per nucleon pair

> We fit the single-particle $p_t$ spectra of identified pions, kaons, and (anti)protons from central collisions of gold or lead nuclei at energies between 7.7 and 2760~GeV per nucleon pair. Blast wave model with included resonance production and with an assumption of partial chemical equilibrium is used and the fits are performed with the help of a Gaussian emulator process. A kinetic freeze-out temperature is found about 100~MeV for the lowest collision energies and 80~MeV at the LHC. The average transverse expansion velocity grows with increasing $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ from 0.45 to 0.65. Due to partial chemical equilibrium, the influence of resonance decays on the shape of the $p_t$ spectra for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ above 27~GeV is small.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature decreases from about 100 MeV at the lowest collision energies (7.7 GeV per nucleon pair) to about 80 MeV at the LHC (2760 GeV per nucleon pair), but this result depends on the inclusion of resonance decays and the assumption of partial chemical equilibrium; the effect of resonance decays on the extracted temperature is negligible at high collision energies but causes an upward shift of about 10 MeV at the lowest energies.

## Method

The authors fit single-particle transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and (anti)protons from central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions using a blast-wave model that includes resonance production and assumes partial chemical equilibrium. The chemical potentials for each hadron species are evolved from chemical freeze-out temperatures (taken from prior analyses by STAR and ALICE collaborations) down to the kinetic freeze-out temperature using the partial chemical equilibrium framework. Theoretical spectra are generated with the DRAGON Monte Carlo event generator, and Bayesian fits are performed using a Gaussian process emulator within the MADAI statistical analysis package to explore the three-parameter space of freeze-out temperature, transverse flow gradient, and transverse velocity profile exponent.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV: 102.0 ± 2.0 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV: 78.3 ± 1.6 MeV
- Mean transverse velocity increases from 0.45 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to 0.65 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV
- Transverse flow gradient increases from eta_f = 0.620 ± 0.016 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to eta_f = 0.903 ± 0.005 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom values range from 0.25 (at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) to 1.07 (at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV)
- Chemical freeze-out temperatures used as starting points range from 144.3 MeV (at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV) to 164.3 MeV (at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV)

## Context

This work extends prior blast-wave fits by STAR (2007, 2014) and ALICE (2013) that did not include resonance decays, and improves upon the authors' own earlier analysis of ALICE data (Melo, 2015) by incorporating partial chemical equilibrium. The results are consistent with the Cracow single freeze-out model fits by Begun (2013, 2014) and the two-component blast-wave model by Li (2018), but differ from the chemical equilibrium treatment by Rode (2018). The finding that resonance contributions become negligible at high energies enables simpler future analyses of LHC data using only directly produced hadrons, while the systematic energy dependence of freeze-out parameters provides constraints for models of the QCD phase diagram being explored by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
