# Production of $K^-$-mesons in proton-proton and proton-nucleus interactions at various energies

> The experimental data on the production of $K^-$-mesons in pp-collisions are analyzed and a method of the unified description of these data in a broad energy range for primary protons is proposed. The $K^-$-mesons production in pA-collisions is considered. The simple formulas for inclusive cross sections of the $K^-$ production in these collisions are given. The results of the calculations by these formulas are compared with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes simple analytical formulas for the inclusive cross sections of K⁻-meson production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions that describe the existing experimental data with an accuracy of 30–40%, but the authors note that the maximum uncertainty in the data on K⁻-meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions refers to the energy region below 10 GeV, where experimental measurements are scarce.

## Method

The authors parameterize the total cross section for K⁻ production in pp collisions using a phase-space-inspired formula (Eq. 9) that interpolates between threshold behavior and high-energy scaling, with coefficients fitted to mean multiplicity data from Table 2 and partial cross sections from Table 1. For the invariant inclusive cross section, they adopt an empirical parameterization (Eqs. 10–12) with energy-dependent coefficients determined from experimental data at beam momenta from 12.5 to 1500 GeV/c. For proton-nucleus collisions, they use a single-interaction model (Eqs. 13–16) where the nuclear cross section is obtained by multiplying the pp cross section by an effective nuclear thickness factor I_V(A), incorporating absorption of the incident proton and the produced kaon via inelastic nucleon-nucleon and kaon-nucleon cross sections.

- Total cross section for K⁻ production in pp collisions at ε₀ = 2.85 GeV: σ = 4 ± 2 μb (from Ref. [1])
- Total cross section for the reaction pp → ppK⁺K⁻ at ε₀ = 5.7 GeV: σ = 34 ± 12 μb (from Ref. [19])
- Partial cross section for pp → ppK⁻K⁰π⁺ at ε₀ = 6.99 GeV: σ = 13.8 ± 5.2 μb (from Table 1)
- Estimated σ_{pp→K⁻X} at ε₀ = 7 GeV: approximately 0.08 mb from partial cross sections; 85 μb from the fitted formula (Eq. 9)
- σ_{pp→K⁻X} at ε₀ = 4.1 GeV: 15.0 ± 3.2 μb (derived from K⁰ production channels)
- σ_{pp→K⁻X} at ε₀ = 4.6 GeV: 25.8 ± 11.1 μb (derived from K⁰ production channels)
- Mean multiplicity ⟨n_{K⁻}⟩ in pp collisions at s = 25.3 GeV²: 0.008; at s = 2810 GeV²: 0.37 (from Table 2)
- Fitted parameters for the total cross section formula (Eq. 9): k = 3, σ₃⁻ = 3.90 ± 0.85 mb, σ₂⁻ = 7.7 ± 1.5 mb
- Inelastic pp cross section assumed: 30 mb for s < 100 GeV², 32 mb for s ≥ 100 GeV²
- Parameter b₀ for Gaussian nuclear density: 0.24 fm⁻²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis for K⁺-meson production (Efremov and Paryev, 1995, Ref. [22]) to the case of K⁻-mesons in pp and pA interactions, providing a unified description across a broad energy range relevant to kaon factory projects. The paper addresses the lack of systematic parameterizations for K⁻ production at low energies (below 10 GeV), where direct experimental data are practically absent, and provides formulas that can be used to interpret experiments on K⁻ production in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Refs. [10, 23, 24]). The approach contrasts with earlier threshold models (Refs. [19–21]) and high-energy scaling descriptions (Refs. [15–18]) by providing a single parameterization covering both regimes.
