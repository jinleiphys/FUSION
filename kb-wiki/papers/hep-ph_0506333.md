# Modelling Drell-Yan pair production in pbar-p

> We predict the triple differential cross section of the unpolarized Drell-Yan process at sqrt(S)=6 GeV. The model incorporates primordial parton transverse momentum and quark off-shellness effects caused by the initial state interaction.

## Key claim
The authors predict the triple differential cross section for unpolarized Drell-Yan production in antiproton-proton collisions at sqrt(S)=6 GeV, but note that the uncertainty in the quark off-shellness width Gamma (100-250 MeV) reflects the lack of data in this low-energy region, meaning the prediction is extrapolated from higher-energy fits rather than directly constrained.

## Method
The authors model Drell-Yan production using k_T-unintegrated parton distributions with a Breit-Wigner spectral function for quark off-shellness, parametrizing initial-state interaction effects including primordial parton transverse momentum and off-shell kinematics. They fit the two model parameters (k_T dispersion and quark width Gamma) to existing Drell-Yan data from Fermilab at 800 GeV/c and 125 GeV/c projectile energies, then extrapolate to sqrt(S)=6 GeV and M<6 GeV. The triple differential cross section dσ/dM^2 dp_T^2 dx_F is computed for several lepton-pair masses at fixed x_F=0.1.

## Key numbers
- sqrt(S)=6 GeV: center-of-mass energy for the predicted Drell-Yan cross sections
- M=1, 2, 3, 5 GeV: lepton-pair masses for which the p_T-distribution is shown (M=4 GeV excluded due to charmonium contamination)
- Average quark intrinsic transverse momentum = 1 GeV: value used in the calculations
- Gamma = 100-250 MeV: range of the Breit-Wigner width for quark off-shellness, determined from fitting higher-energy Drell-Yan data
- x_F=0.1: fixed Feynman variable for all plotted predictions
- The cross section scale changes from nb (for M=1 GeV) to pb (for M=2, 3, 5 GeV)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous model (Linnyk, Leupold, Mosel, 2005) that successfully described Drell-Yan data from Fermilab at 800 GeV/c and 125 GeV/c, as well as deep inelastic scattering data. The prediction targets the PANDA experiment at the future GSI facility, which will use the pbar-p to mu+mu- X reaction to study polarized quark distributions. The model addresses the failure of next-to-leading order pQCD to reproduce the triple differential cross section, particularly at low sqrt(S) and high M^2/S where nonperturbative effects from initial-state interactions become sizable.
