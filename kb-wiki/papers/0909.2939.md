# The chiral and angular momentum content of the rho-meson

> It is possible to define and calculate in a gauge-invariant manner the chiral as well as the partial wave content of the quark-antiquark Fock component of a meson in the infrared, where mass is generated. Using the variational method and a set of interpolators that span a complete chiral basis we extract in a lattice QCD Monte Carlo simulation with two dynamical light quarks the orbital angular momentum and spin content of the rho-meson. We obtain in the infrared a simple 3S1 component as a leading component of the rho-meson with a small admixture of the 3D1 partial wave, in agreement with the SU(6) flavor-spin symmetry.

# The chiral and angular momentum content of the rho-meson

## Key claim

The rho-meson's quark-antiquark Fock component in the infrared consists predominantly of a simple $^3S_1$ partial wave with a small admixture of the $^3D_1$ wave, in agreement with SU(6) flavor-spin symmetry. This result is obtained at a resolution scale defined by the smearing size of the interpolating operators, not in the continuum limit, and applies only to the quark-antiquark component of the rho-meson as extracted from a variational analysis of lattice correlation matrices.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD Monte Carlo simulations with two dynamical light quarks (n_f = 2) using chirally improved fermions and a tadpole-improved Luscher-Weisz gauge action. They constructed a complete set of interpolating operators spanning the chiral basis for the rho-meson channel (the vector current $O_\rho^V$ and the pseudotensor operator $O_\rho^T$) and applied the variational method to extract the ground state eigenvector of the cross-correlation matrix. Using a unitary transformation relating the chiral basis to the $^{2S+1}L_J$ basis, they decomposed the eigenvector coefficients into S-wave and D-wave contributions at three different resolution scales defined by Jacobi smearing widths of 0.27 fm (narrow), 0.41 fm (quenched) or 0.55 fm (dynamical, wide), and point-like (no smearing) sources.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $a_\rho^V / a_\rho^T$ for narrow sources (0.27 fm smearing) was extracted from plateau fits in the time range $\Delta t = 3$ to 8 for dynamical ensembles with pion masses ranging from 318(5) MeV to 526(7) MeV.
- The dynamical ensemble C had $m_\pi = 318(5)$ MeV and $m_\rho = 810(28)$ MeV at lattice spacing $a = 0.1440(12)$ fm.
- The dynamical ensemble A had $m_\pi = 526(7)$ MeV and $m_\rho = 922(17)$ MeV at $a = 0.1507(17)$ fm.
- The quenched data covered pion masses from 475 to 1053 MeV and rho masses from 912 to 1251 MeV at $a = 0.1480(10)$ fm.
- The unitary transformation matrix relating chiral to angular momentum basis has elements $\sqrt{2/3}$ and $\sqrt{1/3}$ for the $(0,1)\oplus(1,0)$ representation and $\sqrt{1/3}$ and $-\sqrt{2/3}$ for the $(1/2,1/2)_b$ representation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier lattice studies of hadron structure using chirally improved fermions in both quenched (Gattringer et al., 2004; Bruckmann et al., 2006) and dynamical (Gattringer et al., 2008) settings. It provides a gauge-invariant, model-independent bridge between QCD in the infrared and the constituent quark model language, building on the chiral classification of quark-antiquark interpolators developed by Glozman (2003, 2007) and the unitary transformation to the partial wave basis derived by Glozman and Negele (2007). The method enables future studies of the scale dependence of hadron structure and can be extended to excited states and other meson channels.
