# Anderson Metal-to-Critical Transition in QCD

> A picture of thermal QCD phase change based on the analogy with metal-to-insulator transition of Anderson type was proposed in the past. In this picture, a low-$T$ thermal state is akin to a metal with deeply infrared (IR) Dirac modes abundant and extended, while a high-$T$ state is akin to an insulator with IR modes depleted and localized below a mobility edge $λ_{\text A} > 0$. Here we argue that, while $λ_{\text A}$ exists in QCD, a high-$T$ state is not an insulator in such an analogy. Rather, it is a critical state arising due to a new singular mobility edge at $λ_{\text IR}=0$. This new mobility edge appears upon the transition into the recently proposed IR phase. As a key part of such a metal-to-critical scenario, we present evidence using pure-glue QCD that deeply infrared Dirac modes in the IR phase extend to arbitrarily long distances. This is consistent with our previous suggestion that the IR phase supports scale invariance in the infrared. We discuss the role of Anderson-like aspects in this thermal regime and emphasize that the combination of gauge field topology and disorder plays a key role in shaping its IR physics. Our conclusions are conveyed by the structure of Dirac spectral non-analyticities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the high-temperature state of QCD is not an Anderson insulator (as previously proposed), but rather a critical state arising from a new singular mobility edge at $\lambda_{\text{IR}} = 0$, leading to a "metal-to-critical" transition rather than a metal-to-insulator one. This claim is conditional on evidence from pure-glue QCD that deeply infrared Dirac modes in the IR phase extend to arbitrarily long distances.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of pure-glue QCD at temperature $T = 1.12\,T_{\text{IR}}$ using the Wilson action with $\beta = 6.054$ ($a = 0.085$ fm) and analyzed eigenmodes of the overlap Dirac operator on systems with $L/a = 24, 28, 32, 40, 48, 56, 64$ and $1/(Ta) = 7$. They computed the effective distance $\ell[\psi]$ of each mode (the average separation from the probability maximum) and extracted the index $\gamma$ from the scaling $\langle \ell \rangle \propto L^{\gamma}$ as the lattice size $L$ increases, where $\gamma = 0$ indicates exponential localization and $\gamma = 1$ indicates plane-wave extended states.

## Key numbers

- The mobility edge $\lambda_{\text{A}} \approx 750$ MeV was determined for the pure-glue setup at $T = 1.12\,T_{\text{IR}}$.
- The left rise in $\gamma$ appears below $\lambda \approx 50$ MeV and the right rise above $\lambda \approx 550$ MeV.
- The limiting value $\lim_{\lambda \to \lambda_{\text{IR}}^+} \gamma(\lambda) \lessapprox 0.8$ for near-zero modes.
- The crossover temperature range is approximately $150-180$ MeV, with the IR phase transition occurring at $200 < T_{\text{IR}} < 250$ MeV.
- Lattice spacing $a = 0.085$ fm with $r_0 = 0.5$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the Anderson localization analogy for QCD previously proposed by Garcia-Garcia and Osborn (2005, 2006) and studied by Kovacs (2010), Giordano (2013), and Ujfalusi (2015), who found the mobility edge $\lambda_{\text{A}} > 0$ in hot QCD. The paper contradicts the metal-to-insulator interpretation by showing that the high-temperature state instead exhibits critical behavior, consistent with the authors' earlier proposal of an IR phase (Alexandru 2019) supporting scale invariance in the infrared. The results enable a new understanding of the IR phase where the combination of gauge field topology and disorder shapes the infrared physics, and suggest that modes in the critical region near $\lambda_{\text{IR}}$ may encode zero glueball screening masses conjectured for the IR phase.
