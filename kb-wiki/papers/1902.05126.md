# Constraints on nuclear parton distributions from dijet photoproduction at the LHC

> Using QCD calculations of the cross section of inclusive dijet photoproduction in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions in the LHC kinematics as pseudo-data, we study the effect of including these data using the Bayesian reweighting technique on nCTEQ15, nCTEQ15np, and EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs). We find that, depending on the assumed error of the pseudo-data, it leads to a significant reduction of the nPDF uncertainties at small values of the momentum fraction $x_A$. Taking the error to be 5\%, the uncertainty of nCTEQ15 and nCTEQ15np nPDFs reduces approximately by a factor of two at $x_A=10^{-3}$. At the same time, the reweighting effect on EPPS16 nPDFs is much smaller due to the higher value of the tolerance and a more flexible parametrization form.

## Key claim

Using pseudo-data from NLO QCD calculations of inclusive dijet photoproduction in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, the Bayesian reweighting technique can significantly reduce the uncertainties of nCTEQ15 and nCTEQ15np nuclear parton distribution functions at small momentum fraction x_A, but the effect on EPPS16 nPDFs is much smaller due to its higher tolerance value and more flexible parametrization form.

## Method

The authors used NLO QCD calculations of the inclusive dijet photoproduction cross section in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions at LHC kinematics as pseudo-data, with the central nCTEQ15 fit as the reference. They applied the Bayesian reweighting technique to 10,000 replicas generated from the error sets of nCTEQ15 (16 error sets), nCTEQ15np (16 error sets), and EPPS16 (20 error sets) nPDFs, calculating statistical weights based on chi-squared values with assumed experimental errors of 5%, 10%, 15%, and 20% of the central cross section value.

## Key numbers

- With an assumed error of 5%, the uncertainty of nCTEQ15 and nCTEQ15np nPDFs reduces approximately by a factor of two at x_A = 10^{-3}
- The effective number of replicas N_eff for nCTEQ15 ranges from 4407 (epsilon=0.05) to 9464 (epsilon=0.20)
- The effective number of replicas N_eff for nCTEQ15np ranges from 3982 (epsilon=0.05) to 9607 (epsilon=0.20)
- The effective number of replicas N_eff for EPPS16 ranges from 5982 (epsilon=0.05) to 9818 (epsilon=0.20)
- The analysis used 9 pseudo-data bins in x_A and a characteristic scale Q^2 = 400 GeV^2
- The tolerance value used was T=35 for nCTEQ15 and nCTEQ15np, and T=52 for EPPS16

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear parton distribution functions using hard processes in proton-nucleus scattering at the LHC (Eskola 2013, Helenius 2014, Armesto 2015, d'Enterria 2015), which provided only modest constraints at small x. It builds on the NLO QCD calculations of dijet photoproduction in Pb-Pb UPCs by Guzey and Klasen (2018) and the Bayesian reweighting methodology developed by Armesto (2013), Paukkunen (2014), and Kusina (2016). The results suggest that actual LHC measurements of dijet photoproduction could provide constraints on nCTEQ15 nPDFs comparable to those from RHIC inclusive pion production data, but without the additional uncertainty from pion fragmentation functions.
