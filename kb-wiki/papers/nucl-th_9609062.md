# Tagging nucleon structure functions by heavy particle detection in deep inelastic electron-ion scattering

> It is shown that in deep inelastic electron - ion collisions the detection, in coincidence with the scattered electron, of a nucleus A-1 in the ground state, as well as a nucleon and a nucleus A-2 also in the ground state, may provide unique information on several long standing problems, such as the origin of the EMC effect, the possible medium modifications of the nucleon structure functions, and the nature of Nucleon-Nucleon correlations.

# Tagging nucleon structure functions by heavy particle detection in deep inelastic electron-ion scattering

## Key claim

The paper proposes that detecting a nucleus A-1 in the ground state, or a nucleon plus a nucleus A-2 in the ground state, in coincidence with the scattered electron in deep inelastic electron-ion collisions could distinguish between different models of the EMC effect (such as x-rescaling versus Q²-rescaling) and separately probe structure functions of weakly bound versus deeply bound nucleons, provided the spectator mechanism can be experimentally validated through predicted ratios of momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors derive differential cross sections in the Impulse Approximation for two semi-inclusive processes: A(e,e'(A-1))X, where the detected A-1 nucleus has low momentum and is in the ground state or a hole state, and A(e,e' N₂ (A-2))X, where a high-momentum nucleon and a low-momentum A-2 nucleus in the ground state are detected. They construct ratios of cross sections between different target nuclei (e.g., A and deuterium) to isolate the nuclear momentum distribution from the nucleon structure function, and propose kinematic conditions (Q² = 20 GeV², x_Bj = 0.05) where the kinematic factor K^A equals the free nucleon factor K^N to within a few percent.

## Key numbers

- For the A(e,e'(A-1))X process, the ratio of cross sections between different nuclei at fixed x_Bj and fixed angle α (so that z_A = z_A') reduces to the ratio of momentum distributions n₀^A(|P_{A-1}|)/n₀^A'(|P_{A-1}|), with the kinematic factor K^A = K^N to within a few percent accuracy.
- For the A(e,e' N₂ (A-2))X process at Q² = 20 GeV² and x_Bj = 0.05, the ratio K^A/K^N varies from 0.99 at |p₂| = 350 MeV to 0.96 at |p₂| = 1 GeV.
- For the (A-1) process, fixing |P_{A-1}| = 50 MeV and Q² = 20 GeV², varying x_Bj from 0 to 0.5 and the angle of P_{A-1} from 0° to 180°, K^A = K^N within ±3%, allowing study of F₂^N in the interval x from 0 to 0.6.
- For the (A-2) process, choosing |P_{A-2}| = 50 MeV, |p₂| = 700 MeV, Q² = 20 GeV², and x_Bj = 0.05, with the angle between p₂ and P_{A-2} fixed at 180°, the structure function of a strongly bound nucleon can be obtained across the whole interval of x.
- In a proposed collider with electron beam energy E_k ≈ 5 GeV and nucleon kinetic energy T_N ≈ 25 GeV, the chosen Q² = 20 GeV² and x_Bj = 0.05 correspond to E_k' ≈ 2 GeV and scattering angle θ ≈ 90° in the laboratory system.

## Context

This work extends the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering approach previously applied to deuteron targets (Frankfurt and Strikman; Ciofi degli Atti and Simula) to heavier nuclei, proposing detection of heavy nuclear fragments rather than just nucleons. It directly addresses the EMC effect, whose origin remains debated between models such as the Q²-rescaling model (Close, 1988) and x-rescaling models, and the possible medium modification of nucleon structure functions (Thomas, 1983; Kulagin and Kukharenko). The proposed measurements would enable separate extraction of structure functions for weakly bound (shell-model) nucleons and deeply bound nucleons from short-range correlated pairs, which has not been possible with inclusive measurements alone.
