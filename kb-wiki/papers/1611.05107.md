# Rearrangement of valence neutrons in the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{136}$Xe

> A quantitative description of the change in ground-state neutron occupancies between $^{136}$Xe and $^{136}$Ba, the initial and final state in the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{136}$Xe, has been extracted from precision measurements of the cross sections of single-neutron adding and -removing reactions. Comparisons are made to recent theoretical calculations of the same properties using various nuclear-structure models. These are the same calculations used to determine the magnitude of the nuclear matrix elements for the process, which at present disagree with each other by factors of 2 or 3. The experimental neutron occupancies show some disagreement with the theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The change in ground-state neutron occupancies between $^{136}$Xe and $^{136}$Ba has been extracted from precision transfer-reaction measurements, and these experimental occupancies show some disagreement with theoretical calculations used to determine nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, which currently disagree with each other by factors of 2 or 3.

## Method

Single-neutron adding reactions (($d$,$p$) and ($\alpha$,$^3$He)) and neutron-removing reactions (($p$,$d$) and ($^3$He,$\alpha$)) were performed on $^{134,136}$Ba targets at the Tandem-ALTO facility at IPN Orsay. The outgoing ions were momentum-analyzed using an Enge split-pole spectrometer, and spectroscopic factors were extracted by dividing experimental cross sections by distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations using the code Ptolemy. The Macfarlane and French sum rules were used to normalize the spectroscopic factors and extract ground-state neutron vacancies for the $\nu0g_{7/2}$, $\nu1d$, $\nu2s_{1/2}$, and $\nu0h_{11/2}$ orbitals.

## Key numbers

- Neutron vacancy in $^{136}$Ba: $\nu0h_{11/2}$ = 1.68 $\pm$ 0.13 nucleons; $\nu1d$ = 0.24 $\pm$ 0.05 nucleons; $\nu2s_{1/2}$ = 0.08 $\pm$ 0.02 nucleons; $\nu0g_{7/2}$ = 0.00$^{+0.15}_{-0.00}$ nucleons (upper limit)
- Neutron vacancy in $^{134}$Ba: $\nu0h_{11/2}$ = 2.38 $\pm$ 0.15 nucleons; $\nu1d$ = 1.12 $\pm$ 0.15 nucleons; $\nu2s_{1/2}$ = 0.50 $\pm$ 0.15 nucleons; $\nu0g_{7/2}$ = 0.00$^{+0.15}_{-0.00}$ nucleons
- Total neutron vacancy for $^{136}$Ba is 2.00 nucleons (two neutrons above $N=82$); for $^{134}$Ba it is 4.00 nucleons
- The $Q$-value resolution was approximately 50 keV FWHM for ($d$,$p$), 40 keV for ($p$,$d$), 100 keV for ($^3$He,$\alpha$), and 70 keV for ($\alpha$,$^3$He) reactions
- The normalization factor $N_j$ for the $\nu0h_{11/2}$ orbital was determined to be 0.58

## Context

This work completes a systematic study of ground-state nucleon occupancies for three of the most promising neutrinoless double-beta decay candidates: $^{76}$Ge (Schiffer 2008, Kay 2009), $^{130}$Te (Kay 2013), and $^{136}$Xe (present work), building on the proton-occupancy measurements for the $A=136$ system by Entwisle (2016). The experimental neutron occupancies show significant discrepancies with theoretical calculations (shell-model calculations SM1 and SM2, and interacting boson model calculations), particularly for the $\nu0h_{11/2}$ orbital where all calculations underestimate the vacancy. These results provide constraints for new theoretical calculations of nuclear matrix elements, as the discrepancies highlight deficiencies in how calculations describe the initial and final states of the decay.
