# Best Values for the CP-odd Meson-Nucleon Couplings from Supersymmetry

> In the supersymmetric models, the dominant sources of the hadronic flavor-diagonal CP violation at low energy are the theta term and the chromoelectric dipole moments of quarks. Using QCD sum rules, we estimate the preferred range and the best values for the CP-odd meson-nucleon coupling constants induced by these operators. When the theta term is removed by the axion mechanism, the size of the most important isospin triplet pion-nucleon coupling is estimated to be \bar g_{πNN}^{(1)} = 2 times 10^{-12}(tilde d_u - tilde d_d), where chromoelectric dipole moments are given in units of 10^{-26} cm.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules, the paper estimates that when the theta term is removed by the axion mechanism, the isospin triplet pion-nucleon coupling is given by \(\bar g_{\pi NN}^{(1)} = 2 \times 10^{-12} (\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d)\), where the chromoelectric dipole moments are in units of \(10^{-26}\) cm; this best value is about 1.5 times smaller than a naive estimate and carries an uncertainty reflected in a preferred range of \((1 \text{ to } 6) \times 10^{-12} d_{-,26}\).

## Method

The author uses QCD sum rules to compute the matrix elements of the operators \(H_q = \bar q g_s G \sigma q - m_0^2 \bar q q\) over the nucleon state, which arise from the reduction of the pion field via PCAC and from pion-pole diagrams. The operator product expansion for the nucleon correlator in the presence of external chromoelectric dipole moment sources is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order, and the sum rule is matched to a phenomenological double-pole and continuum parametrization. The Borel-transformed sum rule is then analyzed over a range of parameters (interpolating current parameter \(\beta\), condensates, continuum threshold) to extract the preferred range and best value for the couplings.

## Key numbers

* \(\bar g_{\pi NN}^{(1)} = 2 \times 10^{-12} (\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d)\) (best value), with preferred range \((1 \text{ to } 6) \times 10^{-12} d_{-,26}\), where \(d_{-,26}\) is \((\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d)\) in units of \(10^{-26}\) cm.
* \(\bar g_{\pi NN}^{(0)}\) preferred range: \((-0.5 \text{ to } 1.5) \times 10^{-12} d_{+,26}\) (no best value given).
* \(\bar g_{\rho NN}^{(0)}\) best value: \(1.4 d_{+}\) (units of \(10^{-26}\) cm), preferred range \((0.7 \text{ to } 2) d_{+}\).
* \(\bar g_{\rho NN}^{(1)}\) best value: \(0.9 d_{-}\), preferred range \((0.5 \text{ to } 1.5) d_{-}\).
* \(\bar g_{\omega NN}^{(0)}\) best value: \(-0.9 d_{-}\), preferred range \((-1.5 \text{ to } -0.5) d_{+}\).
* \(\bar g_{\omega NN}^{(1)}\) best value: \(-1.4 d_{-}\), preferred range \((-2 \text{ to } -0.7) d_{+}\).
* All values carry an overall multiplier \(|\langle \bar q q \rangle|/(225\ \text{MeV})^3\).
* The naive estimate from leading-order terms gives \(\bar g_{\pi NN}^{(1)} = 3 \times 10^{-12} (\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d) / 10^{-26}\ \text{cm}\) for \(\beta = 0\), \(M = 1\ \text{GeV}\), \(|\langle \bar q q \rangle| = (225\ \text{MeV})^3\), \(|m_0^2| = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of K. K. Yazaki et al. (1990) and P. Herczeg et al. (1997) by providing a dedicated QCD sum-rule calculation of the matrix elements that determine the CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings, addressing the potential cancellation between the \(\bar q g_s G \sigma q\) and \(-m_0^2 \bar q q\) contributions. The results enable a more reliable interpretation of experimental limits on the electric dipole moments of diamagnetic atoms (e.g., mercury from the Seattle group, 2001) in terms of constraints on supersymmetric models, translating the mercury EDM bound into \(|\tilde d_u - \tilde d_d| < 2 \times 10^{-26}\) cm and probing SUSY masses up to a few TeV.
