# Fission widths of hot nuclei from Langevin dynamics

> Fission dynamics of excited nuclei is studied in the framework of Langevin equation. The one body wall-and-window friction is used as the dissipative force in the Langevin equation. In addition to the usual wall formula friction, the chaos weighted wall formula developed earlier to account for nonintegrability of single-particle motion within the nuclear volume is also considered here. The fission rate calculated with the chaos weighted wall formula is found to be faster by about a factor of two than that obtained with the usual wall friction. The systematic dependence of fission width on temperature and spin of the fissioning nucleus is investigated and a simple parametric form of fission width is obtained.

## Key claim

The fission rate calculated with the chaos weighted wall formula (CWWF) friction is faster by about a factor of two than that obtained with the usual wall formula (WF) friction, but this enhancement factor remains almost the same across different choices of the window friction onset parameter and across a wide range of compound nuclear spin and temperature.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated a one-dimensional Langevin equation for the elongation coordinate \(c\) of a fissioning nucleus, using the \(c,h,\alpha\) shape parametrization restricted to symmetric fission with no neck degree of freedom. They employed the chaos weighted wall formula friction, which multiplies the standard wall formula friction by a shape-dependent chaoticity factor \(\mu(c)\) that accounts for nonintegrability of single-particle motion, combined with a window friction switched on at a critical elongation \(c_{win}=1.9\). Stationary fission widths were extracted from the plateau region of the time-dependent fission rate obtained from ensembles of typically 100,000 or more Langevin trajectories for the compound nucleus \(^{200}\text{Pb}\).

## Key numbers

* The fission rate with CWWF friction is faster by about a factor of two than that with WF friction for \(^{200}\text{Pb}\) at various spins and temperatures.
* A time step of \(0.005\ \hbar/\text{MeV}\) was used for numerical integration of the Langevin equation.
* The scission configuration occurs at elongation \(c=2.08\) where the neck radius becomes zero.
* The window friction is switched on at \(c_{win}=1.9\), where the neck radius is half of the fragment radius.
* The stationary fission widths follow the form \(\Gamma_f(l,T) = A_l \exp(-b_f/T)\) for a given spin \(l\), where \(b_f\) is the fission barrier height in the free energy profile.

## Context

This work extends the chaos weighted wall formula approach previously developed by Pal and Mukhopadhyay (1996, 1997, 1998) for collective energy damping to the specific case of fission dynamics, motivated by the phenomenological shape-dependent friction of Frobrich, Gontchar, and Mavlitov (1993) which required a strong suppression of wall friction for compact nuclear shapes. The parametric representation of fission widths obtained here enables future statistical model calculations of compound nucleus decay that require fission widths at numerous temperature and spin values, which cannot be obtained analytically when the friction has strong shape dependence as in the CWWF case.
