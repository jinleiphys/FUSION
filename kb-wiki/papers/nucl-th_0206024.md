# $η$ bound states in nuclei

> The energies and widths of bound states of the $η$ meson in different nuclei are obtained using the results for its selfenergy in a nuclear medium, which is evaluated in a selfconsistent manner using techniques of unitarized chiral perturbation theory. We find bound states in all studied nuclei (from $^{12}{C}$ on) and the half widths obtained are larger than the separation of the levels, what makes the experimental observation of peaks unlikely. We have paid a special attention to the region of nuclei where only the $1s$ state appears and the binding energies are of the order of magnitude of the half width, which would magnify the chances that some broad peak could be observed. This is found in the region of $^{24}{Mg}$ with a binding energy around 12.6 MeV and half width of 16.7 MeV. In heavy nuclei like $^{208}{Pb}$ there are many bound states which would be difficult to disentangle and the deepest state has a binding energy about 21 MeV and half width around 16 MeV. Such an overlapping accumulation of states could be seen as an extension of the continuum of $η$ strength into the bound region in $η$ production experiments.

## Key claim

The authors predict that η meson bound states exist in all nuclei from ¹²C upward, but the half-widths of these states are larger than the level separation, making experimental observation of distinct peaks unlikely; the best chance for observing a broad bump is in the region of ²⁴Mg, where the binding energy (≈12.6 MeV) is comparable to the half-width (≈16.7 MeV) and only one bound state exists.

## Method

The η self-energy in nuclear matter was evaluated self-consistently using unitarized chiral perturbation theory with a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation that dynamically generates the N*(1535) resonance. Medium effects included Pauli blocking, meson self-energies (with the η self-energy treated self-consistently), and baryon self-energies. The resulting energy-dependent η-nucleus optical potential was applied to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, and the Klein-Gordon equation was solved to find bound-state energies and widths.

## Key numbers

- ²⁴Mg 1s state: binding energy ≈ 12.6 MeV, half-width ≈ 16.7 MeV (energy-dependent potential)
- ²⁰⁸Pb deepest (1s) state: binding energy ≈ 21.3 MeV, half-width ≈ 15.9 MeV (energy-dependent potential)
- ¹²C 1s state: binding energy ≈ 9.7 MeV, half-width ≈ 17.5 MeV (energy-dependent potential)
- ²⁷Al 1s state: binding energy ≈ 16.7 MeV, half-width ≈ 18.0 MeV (energy-dependent potential)
- ⁴⁰Ca 1s state: binding energy ≈ 17.9 MeV, half-width ≈ 17.2 MeV (energy-dependent potential)
- Optical potential at normal nuclear matter density (k⁰ = m_η, zero momentum): U_η = (−54 − i29) MeV
- Energy-independent potential (k⁰ = m_η) gave binding energies roughly double those from the energy-dependent potential, e.g., ²⁴Mg 1s: binding ≈ 22.7 MeV, half-width ≈ 25.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent η-nucleus optical potential developed by Inoue et al. (2002) to finite nuclei, using the same unitarized chiral perturbation theory framework that dynamically generates the N*(1535) resonance. Earlier studies by Haider and others had predicted η bound states but with large uncertainties tied to the unknown binding potential of the N*(1535) in the nucleus. The present results contradict the expectation that light nuclei are optimal for searching for η bound states, instead identifying medium-mass nuclei around ²⁴Mg as the best candidates. The findings enable experimental searches at GSI and other laboratories to focus on the most promising nuclear targets and to interpret broad strength distributions below threshold rather than narrow peaks.
