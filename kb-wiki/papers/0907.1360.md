# Interference of thermal photons from quark and hadronic phases in relativistic collisions of heavy nuclei

> We explore the intensity correlations for thermal photons having $K_T\leq$ 2 GeV/$c$, for central collisions of heavy nuclei at RHIC and LHC energies. These photons get competing contributions from the quark and the hadronic phases. This competition gives rise to a unique structure, especially in the outward correlation function, due to the interference between the photons from the two sources. The temporal separation of the two sources provides the life time of the system and their strengths provide the relative contribution of the two phases. The results are found to be quite sensitive to the quark-hadron phase transition temperature and the formation time of the plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that intensity correlations of thermal photons with transverse momenta up to 2 GeV/c in central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC exhibit a distinctive interference pattern between photons emitted from the quark-gluon plasma phase and those from the hadronic phase, which is most clearly visible in the outward correlation function; this interference provides a quantitative method to determine the lifetime of the system, the relative contributions of the two phases, and is sensitive to the quark-hadron phase transition temperature and the formation time of the plasma.

## Method

The authors model the space-time evolution of the collision system using relativistic hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion, assuming an isentropic expansion to relate initial temperature to particle rapidity density. They compute the photon emission function from the quark phase using complete leading-order results and from the hadronic phase using results from Turbide et al., then evaluate the two-photon intensity correlation function defined by the spin-averaged expression involving the space-time emission function. The correlation function is analyzed in terms of outward, sideward, and longitudinal momentum differences, and the source is decomposed into quark and hadronic components to extract the interference term and the corresponding source radii and temporal separation.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC (200A GeV Au+Au, dN/dy = 1260, tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c, T_C = 180 MeV, freeze-out at 100 MeV): at K_T approx 2 GeV/c, the extracted radii are R_o,Q = 2.8 fm, R_o,H = 7.0 fm, Delta R_o = 12.3 fm; R_s,Q approx R_s,H = 2.8 fm, Delta R_s approx 0; R_l,Q = 0.3 fm, R_l,H = 1.8 fm, Delta R_l approx 0.
- For LHC (5.5A TeV Pb+Pb, dN/dy = 5625, tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c, T_C = 180 MeV): at K_T approx 1.5 GeV/c, the temporal separation of the two sources is about 19 fm/c.
- The particle rapidity density at y=0 is taken as 1260 for RHIC and 5625 for LHC.
- The initial formation time is mostly taken as tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c, with variations studied from 0.2 to 1.0 fm/c.
- The quark-hadron phase transition temperature is assumed to be 180 MeV, with sensitivity studies at 160 and 200 MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature is taken as 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of photon intensity interferometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by authors such as Srivastava (DKS) and collaborators, who had previously found that a simple Gaussian parametrization of the correlation function was inadequate. The paper builds on the recognition that thermal photons with intermediate transverse momenta (0.2-2 GeV/c) receive competing contributions from the quark and hadronic phases, an effect noted qualitatively in prior work but not previously studied in terms of their interference. The results enable a quantitative determination of the system lifetime, the relative strength of quark and hadronic contributions, and the thermalization time of the plasma, and open the possibility of measuring the quark-hadron phase transition temperature through the transverse momentum at which the two contributions become equal.
