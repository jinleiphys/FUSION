# Systematic studies of charmonium-, bottomonium-, and $B_c$-like tetraquark states

> We study the mass splittings of $Q_1q_2\bar{Q}_3\bar{q}_4$ ($Q=c,b$, $q=u,d,s$) tetraquark states with chromomagnetic interactions between their quark components. Assuming that $X(4140)$ is the lowest $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark, we estimate the masses of the other tetraquark states. From the obtained masses and defined measure reflecting effective quark interactions, we find the following assignments for several exotic states: (1) both $X(3860)$ and the newly observed $Z_c(4100)$ seem to be $0^{++}$ $cn\bar{c}\bar{n}$ tetraquarks; (2) $Z_c(4200)$ is probably a $1^{+-}$ $cn\bar{c}\bar{n}$ tetraquark; (3) $Z_c(3900)$, $X(3940)$, and $X(4160)$ are unlikely compact tetraquarks; (4) $Z_c(4020)$ is unlikely a compact tetraquark, but seems the hidden-charm correspondence of $Z_b(10650)$ with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$; and (5) $Z_c(4250)$ can be a tetraquark candidate but the quantum numbers cannot be assigned at present. We hope further studies may check the predictions and assignments given here.

## Key claim

The paper uses the assumption that X(4140) is the lowest JPC=1++ cs¯c¯s tetraquark state as a reference input to estimate masses of other tetraquark states, with the hedge that the obtained masses are reasonable only if the quark mass difference between ms in the cs¯c¯s system and mn in the cn¯c¯n system is close to that between Ds and D, and that further studies are required to test this method.

## Method

The authors employ a chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model with an effective Hamiltonian H = Σ mi − Σ Cij λ̃i·λ̃j σi·σj, using diquark-antidiquark basis wave functions for S-wave Q1q2Q̄3q̄4 systems. They extract 14 coupling constants Cij from measured masses of low-lying conventional hadrons, then estimate tetraquark masses using a modified formula M = (Mref − ⟨HCM⟩ref) + ⟨HCM⟩, where X(4140) serves as the reference system to evade uncertainties from effective quark masses. The analysis also introduces dimensionless measures Kij = ∂M/∂Cij to characterize effective attractive or repulsive interactions between quark components.

## Context

This work extends previous CMI model studies of cs¯c¯s tetraquarks by Wu et al. (2016) to systematically cover charmonium-like, bottomonium-like, and Bc-like tetraquark systems. It builds on the observation by LHCb (2016) that two 1++ states X(4140) and X(4274) exist in the J/ψφ channel, which disfavors molecule interpretations and supports the tetraquark picture originally proposed by Stancu (2009). The analysis enables future experimental checks of predicted tetraquark masses and quantum number assignments for states such as X(3860), Zc(4100), Zc(4200), and Zc(4020), and provides a framework for distinguishing compact tetraquarks from meson-meson molecules.
