# Effects of Negative Energy Components in the Constituent Quark Model

> Relativistic covariance requires that in the constituent quark model for mesons the positive energy states as well as the negative energy states are included. Using relativistic quasi-potential equations the contribution of the negative energy states is studied for the light and charmonium mesons. It is found that these states change the meson mass spectrum significantly but leave its global structure untouched.

## Key claim

The inclusion of negative energy states in relativistic quasi-potential equations significantly changes the meson mass spectrum but leaves its global structure (level ordering, relative sizes of splittings) untouched, confirming that nonrelativistic quark models can mimic most relativistic effects using effective parameters.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic quasi-potential equations (Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze and equal-time approximations) for equal-mass quark-antiquark systems, using a potential consisting of a Coulomb-like one-gluon-exchange part and a linearly rising confining part with both scalar and vector contributions. They systematically switched off the coupling to negative energy plane wave states by projecting them out of the interaction, solving the resulting coupled integral-differential equations in coordinate space by expanding the wave function on a set of spline functions.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium, replacing the kinetic energy with the relativistic form \(\omega = \sqrt{p^2 + m^2}\) shifts masses downward by 0.05–0.13 GeV.
- For light mesons, the same kinetic energy replacement shifts masses downward by 0.5–1.5 GeV.
- Replacing the nonrelativistic potential with the full projection onto positive energy states shifts masses by 0.00–0.16 GeV for charmonium and 0.0–0.8 GeV for light mesons.
- Introducing single negative energy states (\(|+-\rangle_{pw}\) and \(|-+\rangle_{pw}\)) increases the charmonium spectrum by up to 0.10 GeV.
- Including double negative energy states (\(|--\rangle_{pw}\)) changes charmonium masses by less than 1 MeV, except for the lowest lying mesons.
- The equal-time model shows single negative energy state shifts approximately twice as large as in the BSLT model, due to the propagator \(S_{ET}^{++} = -1/2\omega\) versus \(S_{BSLT}^{++} = -1/4\omega\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quasi-potential model studies (Tiemeijer and Tjon, 1992, 1993) by isolating the specific contributions of negative energy states to the meson mass spectrum. It confirms the success of nonrelativistic quark models in reproducing global spectral features with effective parameters, while showing that the inclusion of negative energy states is essential for determining the confinement strength \(\kappa\). The results enable future studies of whether relativistic modifications to meson wave functions produce sizeable changes in cross sections for electromagnetic processes involving mesons.
