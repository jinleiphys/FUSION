# Symmetry energy dependence of the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter

> We clarify how the weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity $ζ$ and the bulk relaxation time $τ_Π$ of neutrino-transparent $npe$ matter depend on the nuclear symmetry energy. We show that, at saturation density, the equation-of-state dependence of these transport quantities is fully determined by the experimentally constrained nuclear symmetry energy $S$ and its slope $L$. Variations of $L$ can change the bulk viscosity by orders of magnitude, which can affect both the dissipative and the conservative tidal response of neutron stars. This suggests that both conservative and dissipative effects encoded in the gravitational-wave signatures of binary neutron star inspirals may help constrain nuclear symmetry energy properties.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at saturation density, the bulk viscosity and bulk relaxation time of neutrino-transparent npe matter are fully determined by the nuclear symmetry energy S and its slope L, with variations in L changing the bulk viscosity by orders of magnitude; this suggests that both conservative and dissipative effects in gravitational-wave signals from binary neutron star inspirals may help constrain symmetry energy properties.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the bulk viscosity ζ₀ and relaxation time τΠ of npe matter in the low-temperature, neutrino-transparent regime, using the Israel-Stewart formalism for out-of-beta-equilibrium dynamics. They connect these transport coefficients to the symmetry energy expansion by expressing the reaction affinity δμ in terms of the symmetry energy E_sym(n_B) and the electron contribution, then evaluate the coefficients at saturation density using experimentally constrained values of S and L. To test the parabolic approximation in the isospin asymmetry parameter, they also compute ζ₀ and τΠ using chiral effective field theory (EFT) parameterizations from Tews (2024), fitting the energy per nucleon to a phenomenological form and extracting S and L for each parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry slope L = 121 ± 47 MeV from PREX-I and PREX-II measurements (68% confidence), and L = -5 ± 40 MeV from CREX measurements (68% confidence), with optimal combined value L = 53 ± 13 MeV from Lattimer (2023).
- The dimensionless bulk modulus ζ₀/(τΠ n_sat^(4/3)) = 0.004 for L ~ 50 MeV and ζ₀/(τΠ n_sat^(4/3)) = 0.056 for L ~ 100 MeV at saturation density.
- The upper bound on the density-averaged bulk viscosity from GW170817 is ⟨ζ⟩_A ≲ 10^31 g cm^(-1) s^(-1) from Ripley (2023).
- The saturation density used is n_sat = 0.16 fm^(-3).
- The range of S explored is [30, 40] MeV and L explored is [30, 150] MeV in the parameter scan.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Yang 2023) that showed bulk viscosity in npe matter is sensitive to S and L for relativistic mean-field models, by providing a general analytic derivation valid for any equation of state that satisfies the parabolic approximation in isospin asymmetry. It builds on constraints from chiral EFT calculations (Tews 2024) and experimental measurements of neutron skin thickness from PREX and CREX (Lattimer 2023). The results enable future work connecting the dissipative tidal deformability (Ripley 2023) and conservative dynamical Love numbers (Hegade 2024) to nuclear symmetry energy parameters, potentially allowing gravitational-wave observations to constrain L.
