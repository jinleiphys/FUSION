# Magnetic field induced corrections to the NJL model coupling constant from vacuum polarization

> Magnetic field dependent corrections for the coupling constant of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model are calculated by considering the one-loop background field method. These coupling constants turn out to break chiral and flavor symmetries and they lead to a slight improvement of the numerical values of the up and down quark condensates when compared to results from lattice QCD. The corresponding magnetic field dependencies of the neutral pion and kaon masses are also presented and compared with available lattice QCD calculations. The resulting magnetic field correction to the $η-η'$ mixing angle is also estimated.

## Key claim

The magnetic field dependent corrections to the NJL coupling constant, calculated from one-loop vacuum polarization, break chiral and flavor symmetries and lead to a slight improvement in the numerical values of up and down quark condensates compared to lattice QCD results, but only when the scalar coupling constants are used and the difference between scalar and pseudoscalar couplings is neglected.

## Method

The authors employed the background field method (BFM) to expand the sea-quark determinant in a large quark mass expansion at zero order derivative expansion, extracting magnetic field dependent corrections to the NJL coupling constant from the second-order terms. They used the proper-time representation for the quark propagator in a uniform magnetic field, separated the UV-divergent vacuum contribution from the UV-finite magnetic field contribution, and adopted a four-momentum cutoff regularization in Euclidean space. The corrected gap equations and Bethe-Salpeter equations were then solved self-consistently with the B-dependent coupling constants to compute quark effective masses, chiral condensates, and neutral meson masses.

## Key numbers

- The NJL coupling constant at zero magnetic field was fixed to G0 = 9.76 GeV^-2 to reproduce vacuum observables.
- The model parameters were fitted to reproduce m_pi = 135.0 MeV, m_K = 498.0 MeV, f_pi = 93.0 MeV, and f_K = 111.0 MeV at B=0.
- The eta meson mass at B=0 was taken as M_eta = 548 MeV and the eta-prime mass as M_eta-prime = 958 MeV from the Particle Data Group.
- For the eta-eta-prime mixing angle estimation, three scenarios for the mass difference were considered: constant at 786 MeV, increasing as (1 + eB/b0) with b0 = 2 GeV^2, and decreasing as (1 - eB/b0) with b0 = 2 GeV^2.
- The magnetic field strengths considered range from eB ~ 0.04-0.3 GeV^2 (RHIC to LHC heavy ion collisions) up to eB ~ 10^21 G in the early Universe.

## Context

This work extends a previous restricted calculation for weak magnetic fields and SU(N_f=2) presented in PRD (2016) to the full SU(N_f=3) case with strong magnetic fields. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations from Bali et al. (2018) and Endrodi and Marko, and with earlier NJL model studies that assumed either constant or B-dependent coupling constants by Gastao et al. (PRC), Xu et al., Hattori et al., Avancini et al., and others. The work enables improved predictions for neutral pion and kaon masses in strong magnetic fields and provides an estimate for the magnetic field correction to the eta-eta-prime mixing angle, which remains largely unknown.
