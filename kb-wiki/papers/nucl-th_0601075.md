# Testing nuclear forces by polarization transfer coefficients in d(\vec p, \vec p)d and d(\vec p,\vec d)p reactions at E^{lab}_p = 22.7 MeV

> The proton to proton polarization transfer coefficients K_x^{x'}, K_y^{y'}, K_z^{x'} and the proton to deuteron polarization transfer coefficients K_x^{x'}, K_y^{y'}, K_z^{x'}, K_x^{y'z'}, K_y^{z'z'}, K_z^{y'z'}, K_y^{x'z'} and K_y^{x'x'-y'y'} have been measured in d(\vec p, \vec p)d and d(\vec p, \vec d)p reactions at E^{lab}_p = 22.7 MeV, respectively. The data have been compared to predictions of modern nuclear forces obtained by solving the three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum space. Realistic (semi) phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials combined with model three-nucleon forces and modern chiral nuclear forces have been used. The AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm I and II nucleon-nucleon interactions have been applied alone or combined with the Tucson-Melbourne 99 three-nucleon force, adjusted separately for each potential to reproduce the triton binding energy. For the AV18 potential also the Urbana IX three-nucleon force have been used. In addition chiral NN potentials in the next-to-leading-order and chiral two- and three-nucleon forces in the next-to-next-to-leading-order have been applied. Only when three-nucleon forces are included a satisfactory description of all data results. For the chiral approach the restriction to the forces in the next-to-leading order is insufficient. Only when going over to the next-to-next-to-leading order one gets a satisfactory description of the data, similar to the one obtained with the (semi) phenomenological forces.

## Key claim

Only when three-nucleon forces are included do the theoretical predictions provide a satisfactory description of all measured polarization transfer coefficients; for chiral forces, the next-to-leading-order (NLO) approximation is insufficient, and only at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) does the description become satisfactory, comparable to that obtained with (semi)phenomenological forces.

## Method

The authors measured proton-to-proton and proton-to-deuteron polarization transfer coefficients in d(p,p)d and d(p,d)p reactions at a proton laboratory energy of 22.7 MeV. The data were compared to predictions from solving the three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum space, using realistic (semi)phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm I and II) alone or combined with the Tucson-Melbourne 99 (TM99) or Urbana IX three-nucleon forces, as well as chiral nuclear forces at NLO and NNLO. Coulomb force effects were corrected by evaluating the shifts between pd and nd calculations using the Kohn variational principle with a pair correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis, then applying those shifts to the pd data to generate "nd" data for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Proton laboratory energy: E_p^{lab} = 22.7 MeV
- Cut-off parameters for TM99 three-nucleon force adjusted to reproduce triton binding energy: 5.215 (AV18), 4.856 (CD Bonn), 5.120 (Nijm I), 5.072 (Nijm II), in units of the pion mass m_pi
- Faddeev calculations included all 3N partial wave states with total two-body angular momenta up to j=5; when 3NF was switched off, calculations went up to J = 25/2; with 3NF active, up to J = 13/2
- Coulomb force effects were significant for K_y^{y'} and K_z^{x'} around theta_cm approximately 120 degrees, where the differential cross section has its minimum

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of polarization transfer coefficients in nucleon-deuteron scattering, which were restricted to few experiments in the pd system at E_p^{lab}=10 MeV (Sperisen), 19 MeV (Sydow), 250 MeV (Hatano), and E_d^{lab}=270 MeV (Sekiguchi), and one nd measurement at E_p^{lab}=19 MeV (von Witsch). The results confirm earlier indications from studies of few-nucleon bound states and 3N reactions (Friar 1993, Nogga 1997) that three-nucleon forces are an important component of the potential energy. The findings enable further testing of chiral effective field theory predictions, particularly at NNNLO where NN forces have already been worked out and parameter-free 3NFs including first relativistic effects are expected to contribute.
