# Global Spin Alignment of (Anti-)$^4$Li in Non-Central Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Non-central heavy-ion collisions produce hot and dense nuclear matter with significant fluid vorticity, which can induce global polarizations or alignments of particles with non-zero spins along the direction of the total orbital angular momentum. This phenomenon has been observed for hyperons and vector mesons in experiments. In the present study, we demonstrate that polarized nucleons lead to global spin alignment of the unstable nucleus $^4$Li, which can be measured through its strong decays via $^4\text{Li} \rightarrow {^3\text{He}} + p$. Assuming that $^4$Li is formed through the coalescence of polarized nucleons at kinetic freeze-out, we obtain the angular distribution of the daughter particle $^3$He in the rest frame of the polarized $^4$Li. Taking kinetically freeze-out nucleons from an isotropic and thermalized fireball of constant vorticity and including quantum corrections up to $\hbar^2$ in the coalescence calculation through the Moyal star product, we find that the angular distribution of $^3$He has a $\cos(2θ^*)$ dependence with $θ^*$ being its angle with respect to the quantization axis of $^4$Li. We also find that the $^3$He angular distribution depends on both the vorticity and the polarization of kinetically freeze-out nucleons. Future measurements on the spin alignment of $^4$Li in heavy-ion collisions thus offer a promising method to probe the spin dynamics, vortical structure, and spin-dependent equation-of-state of the nuclear matter produced in these collisions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that global spin alignment of the unstable nucleus $^4$Li can arise from the coalescence of polarized nucleons at kinetic freeze-out in non-central heavy-ion collisions, and that this alignment can be measured via the angular distribution of its decay daughter $^3$He. The key qualifier is that the result assumes polarized nucleons from an isotropic, thermalized fireball of constant vorticity, with quantum corrections included up to $\hbar^2$ via the Moyal star product, and neglects spin correlations among nucleons.

## Method
The authors used a spin-dependent coalescence model to compute the spin density matrix of $^4$Li formed from three protons and one neutron at kinetic freeze-out, assuming the nucleons are taken from an isotropic thermalized fireball with constant vorticity. The internal Wigner function of the $^4$Li state was constructed using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions, and the Moyal star product was employed to include quantum corrections up to $\hbar^2$ in the coalescence integral. The angular distribution of the decay product $^3$He in the $^4$Li rest frame was then derived from the density matrix elements for the ground state ($J^\pi=2^-$) and three excited states ($^3P_1$, $^1P_0$, $^1P_1$), and averaged over the spin degeneracies of these states.

## Key numbers
- The ground state $^4$Li($^3P_2$) has spin $J=2$, decays via $^4\text{Li} \rightarrow {^3\text{He}} + p$ with 100% branching ratio and a width of 6.03 MeV.
- The first excited state $^4$Li($^3P_1$) lies at 0.32 MeV above ground state, with decay width 7.35 MeV.
- The second excited state $^4$Li($^1P_0$) lies at 2.08 MeV, with decay width 9.35 MeV.
- The third excited state $^4$Li($^1P_1$) lies at 2.85 MeV, with decay width 13.51 MeV.
- The ground state energy of $^4$Li lies 4.07 MeV above the $^3$He + p threshold masses.
- The averaged angular distribution of the decay particle, weighted by spin degeneracies (5:3:1:3 for the four states), is approximately $\frac{dN}{\sin \theta^* d\theta^*} = \frac{1}{2} [1 - \frac{1}{36}(5 \mathcal{P}_N^2 + 8 \mathcal{P}_N \mathcal{P}_L + 11 \mathcal{P}_L^2)(3\cos^2\theta^* - 1)]$, where $\mathcal{P}_N$ is nucleon polarization and $\mathcal{P}_L \approx \frac{\hbar\omega}{2T}$ characterizes orbital polarization.
- Under the assumption $\mathcal{P}_L = \mathcal{P}_N$, this simplifies to $\frac{dN}{\sin \theta^* d\theta^*} = \frac{1}{2} [1 - \frac{2}{3}\mathcal{P}_N^2 (3\cos^2\theta^* - 1)]$.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on global polarization of hyperons (STAR Collaboration, 2017) and vector mesons, as well as spin polarization of hypertriton and anti-hypertriton (Sun, 2025; Liu, 2025), to the case of the unstable light nucleus $^4$Li. The results are consistent with the thermal model prediction (Becattini, 2016) for a thermalized medium, but the coalescence model is more general and can be applied to non-equilibrium spin-dependent transport models (Xu, 2025). Future measurements of $^4$Li spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions could provide a probe of nucleon spin polarization, vortical structure, and the spin-dependent equation of state of nuclear matter, and may help understand phenomena such as the hadronic spin Hall effect (Liu, 2020).
