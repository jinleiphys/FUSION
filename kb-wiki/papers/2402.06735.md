# Flavor Factorization at Two-Loops

> Recently, a factorization theorem was proposed for partonic flavor evolution as defined by the net flavor of the Winner-Take-All axis of a jet. We validate the factorization theorem through explicit calculation at two-loop order, and in the process extract all anomalous dimensions and renormalization factors for any ultraviolet-to-infrared flavor transition at this order. These results can then be used to extract the renormalized hard function for flavored jet production at next-to-next-to-leading order for any process of interest.

## Key claim

The paper validates a factorization theorem for partonic flavor evolution defined by the net flavor of the Winner-Take-All (WTA) axis of a jet through explicit two-loop calculation, but only for the specific case where the flavor is defined as the sum of all parton flavors within a transverse momentum cutoff \(k_\perp^2\) of the WTA axis, and the validation relies on explicit cancellation of soft divergences and vanishing of scaleless rapidity-divergent integrals in certain flavor channels.

## Method

The author calculated bare jet functions for all flavor transitions at two-loop order by evaluating tree-level \(1\to 3\) splitting functions and one-loop \(1\to 2\) splitting functions, then extracted renormalized jet functions by absorbing ultraviolet divergences into multiplicative renormalization factors \(Z_{j\to k}\). The anomalous dimensions \(\gamma_{j\to k}\) were obtained from the renormalization group equations satisfied by the jet and hard functions, with consistency checked through sum rules enforcing probability conservation.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop validation and partial two-loop results of Larkoski (2023) for the WTA flavor factorization theorem, providing complete two-loop anomalous dimensions for all flavor transitions. The results enable extraction of renormalized hard functions for flavored jet production at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) resummation for any process, interfacing with recent high-precision flavored calculations by Czakon (2020), Gauld (2020), Catani (2020), Hartanto (2022), and Gauld (2023). The factorization theorem itself was originally proposed by Caletti (2022) and Larkoski (2023), building on earlier IRC-safe jet flavor definitions by Banfi (2006) and others.
