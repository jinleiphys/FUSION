# Renormalization-group consistent treatment of color superconductivity in the NJL model

> The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and specifically its extension to color superconductivity (CSC) is a popular effective model for investigating dense quark matter. However, the reliability of its results is challenged by cutoff artifacts, which emerge if temperature or chemical potential are of the order of the cutoff energy scales. In this work, we generalize an idea from [Braun et al. SciPost Phys., 6:056, 2019], which is based on the requirement of renormalization-group (RG) consistency and has successfully been applied to the two-flavor Quark-Meson-Diquark model, to the NJL model for electrically and color-neutral three-flavor color-superconducting quark matter. To this end, we analyze the medium divergences of the model and eliminate them by appropriate counterterms, introducing three different schemes. We show that the RG-consistent treatment removes the cutoff artifacts of the conventional regularization and enables the investigation of CSC matter at higher densities by the model. Our studies reveal the emergence of a so-called d-quark superconducting (dSC) phase within the melting pattern of the Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) phase at high chemical potentials, consistent with earlier Ginzburg-Landau analyses.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that applying a renormalization-group (RG) consistent regularization scheme to the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for color-superconducting quark matter removes cutoff artifacts that plague conventional regularization, enabling reliable investigations at chemical potentials up to and beyond the original cutoff scale. The authors show that this treatment reveals a d-quark superconducting (dSC) phase in the melting pattern of the Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) phase at high chemical potentials, consistent with earlier Ginzburg-Landau analyses, which was previously obscured by cutoff artifacts.

## Method

The authors generalize the RG-consistency approach of Braun et al. (2019) from the two-flavor Quark-Meson-Diquark model to the three-flavor NJL model with electric and color neutrality constraints. They analyze medium divergences that emerge in the presence of diquark condensates and eliminate them by introducing counterterms in three different schemes, constructing a new UV effective action at an arbitrarily high scale from the original cutoff-regularized action by integrating the Wetterich flow equation in vacuum. The resulting RG-consistent effective potential is then minimized to obtain the phase diagram, which is compared with results from conventional sharp three-momentum cutoff regularization at 602.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The conventional cutoff used in the reference model (Ruester et al., 2005) is 602.3 MeV, fitted to vacuum observables (pion decay constant and pseudoscalar meson spectrum) following Rehberg et al. (1995).
* The onset chemical potential of the color-superconducting regime is approximately 350 MeV, with conventional investigations performed up to 500 MeV or 550 MeV, where cutoff artifacts become severe.
* The RG-consistent treatment removes the unphysical decrease and vanishing of pairing gaps as chemical potential approaches the cutoff, which previously occurred in conventional regularization (Farias et al., 2005).
* The dSC phase emerges within the melting pattern of the CFL phase at high chemical potentials, consistent with Ginzburg-Landau analyses by Iida et al. (2003, 2004) for small M_s^2/mu^2.

## Context

This work extends the RG-consistency concept introduced by Braun et al. (2019) for the two-flavor Quark-Meson-Diquark model to the three-flavor NJL model with color superconductivity, addressing cutoff artifacts previously identified in studies by Ruester et al. (2005), Blaschke et al. (2005), and Abuki et al. (2005). The approach resolves discrepancies between NJL model predictions and Ginzburg-Landau analyses by Iida et al. (2003, 2004) regarding the sequential melting pattern of diquark condensates in the CFL phase, and enables future investigations of the equation of state for neutron star interiors and mergers at densities where conventional NJL calculations were unreliable.
