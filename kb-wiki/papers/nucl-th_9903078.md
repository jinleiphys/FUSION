# The three-body Coulomb scattering problem in discrete Hilbert-space basis representation

> For solving the $2\to 2,3$ three-body Coulomb scattering problem the Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations in discrete Hilbert-space basis representation are considered. It is shown that as far as scattering amplitudes are considered the error caused by truncating the basis can be made arbitrarily small. By this truncation also the Coulomb Green's operator is confined onto the two-body sector of the three-body configuration space and in leading order can be constructed with the help of convolution integrals of two-body Green's operators. For performing the convolution integral an integration contour is proposed that is valid for all energies, including bound-state as well as scattering energies below and above the three-body breakup threshold.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations for three-body Coulomb scattering can be solved in a discrete Hilbert-space basis representation with arbitrarily small error in the scattering amplitudes when the basis truncation parameters are taken to infinity, and this approach works for all energies (below and above breakup threshold) and for both repulsive and attractive Coulomb interactions.

## Method

The authors expand the square-integrable terms \(v_\alpha^{(s)}|\psi_\gamma\rangle\) appearing in the Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations in a discrete basis of Coulomb-Sturmian functions, truncating the expansion at finite \(N\) and \(M\). They then show that the residual terms contribute negligibly to the scattering amplitudes, reducing the problem to solving matrix equations with degenerate kernels. The channel long-ranged Green's operator \(G_\alpha^{(l)}\) is approximated by the channel-distorted Coulomb Green's operator \(\widetilde{G}_\alpha\), which is constructed via a contour integral convolution of two-body Green's operators, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for \(G_\alpha^{(l)}\) is further reduced to an algebraic matrix equation using the same basis expansion.

## Key numbers

* The splitting function \(\zeta_\alpha\) defining the asymptotic two-body sector \(\Omega_\alpha\) uses parameters \(a > 0\) and \(0 < \nu < 1/2\) in the condition \(|x_\alpha| < a (1 + |y_\alpha|/a)^\nu\).
* The auxiliary potential \(U^\alpha\) decays as \(\mathcal{O}(|y_\alpha|^{-2+\nu})\) as \(|y_\alpha| \to \infty\), ensuring square integrability for \(\nu < 1/2\).
* The asymptotic form of the Coulomb spherical wave function \(Q_0(X,E)\) scales as \(|X|^{-5/2}\) as \(|X| \to \infty\), while \(Q_{\alpha j}(y_\alpha,E)\) scales as \(|y_\alpha|^{-1}\) as \(|y_\alpha| \to \infty\).
* The error bound on the scattering amplitudes satisfies \(\sum_{\alpha j} |A_{\alpha j,\beta i} - A_{\alpha j,\beta i}^{NM}|^2 + \sum_{\alpha} |A_{\alpha 0,\beta i} - A_{\alpha 0,\beta i}^{NM}|^2 < 3\varepsilon^2\) for sufficiently large \(N\) and \(M\).

## Context

This work extends the discrete Hilbert-space basis method previously applied to Faddeev-Noble equations (Noble, 1967) for bound-state problems (Papp et al., 1997) and \(p-d\) scattering below breakup threshold (Papp et al., 1998) to the more general Faddeev-Merkuriev theory (Merkuriev, 1980; Merkuriev and Faddeev, 1985). The Faddeev-Noble approach was restricted to repulsive Coulomb interactions without energy-range limitations, whereas the Faddeev-Merkuriev formalism handles both repulsive and attractive Coulomb forces at all energies. This enables practical numerical calculations of \(2 \to 2\) and \(2 \to 3\) three-body Coulomb scattering processes using matrix equations with finite basis sets.
