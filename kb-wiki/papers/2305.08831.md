# Neural-network quantum states for ultra-cold Fermi gases

> Ultra-cold Fermi gases display diverse quantum mechanical properties, including the transition from a fermionic superfluid BCS state to a bosonic superfluid BEC state, which can be probed experimentally with high precision. However, the theoretical description of these properties is challenging due to the onset of strong pairing correlations and the non-perturbative nature of the interaction among the constituent particles. This work introduces a novel Pfaffian-Jastrow neural-network quantum state that includes backflow transformation based on message-passing architecture to efficiently encode pairing, and other quantum mechanical correlations. Our approach offers substantial improvements over comparable ansätze constructed within the Slater-Jastrow framework and outperforms state-of-the-art diffusion Monte Carlo methods, as indicated by our lower ground-state energies. We observe the emergence of strong pairing correlations through the opposite-spin pair distribution functions. Moreover, we demonstrate that transfer learning stabilizes and accelerates the training of the neural-network wave function, enabling the exploration of the BCS-BEC crossover region near unitarity. Our findings suggest that neural-network quantum states provide a promising strategy for studying ultra-cold Fermi gases.

## Key claim

The Pfaffian-Jastrow neural-network quantum state with backflow transformations achieves lower ground-state energies than state-of-the-art fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo methods for ultra-cold Fermi gases across the BCS-BEC crossover, though the improvement depends on the effective range and the specific interaction parameters used.

## Method

The authors perform variational Monte Carlo calculations using a neural-network quantum state where the antisymmetric part is a Pfaffian of pairing orbitals parameterized by a feedforward neural network, combined with a Jastrow correlator based on a Deep-Set architecture. Backflow transformations are generated by a permutation-equivariant message-passing neural network that iteratively builds correlations into one-body and two-body features from the original particle coordinates and spins. The wave function is trained by minimizing the energy using stochastic reconfiguration with RMSprop regularization, and transfer learning is employed by pretraining on softer interactions before proceeding to harder ones.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy per particle for the Pfaffian-Jastrow with backflow (PJ-BF) ansatz at effective range $r_e k_F = 0.4$ (interaction parameters $v_0 = 1$, $\mu = 5$) is lower than the DMC-BCS benchmark, with the PJ-BF ansatz using $T=2$ MPNN layers containing about 8500 variational parameters
- The single-layer SJ-BF ansatz contains approximately 6200 parameters, while the single-layer PJ ansatz contains approximately 5600 parameters
- The training requires $\mathcal{O}(10^3)$ to $\mathcal{O}(10^4)$ optimization steps depending on whether the neural quantum states were pretrained or initialized with random parameters
- The effective range is tuned by varying $\mu$ with values of 5, 10, 20, and 40, corresponding to $r_e = 2/\mu$ at unitarity where $v_0=1$

## Context

This work extends neural-network quantum state methods previously applied to bosonic quantum gases (Pescia, 2022), the homogeneous electron gas (Wilson, 2022; Cassella, 2023), and dilute neutron matter (Fore, 2022) to ultra-cold Fermi gases. The Pfaffian-Jastrow approach generalizes earlier geminal wave functions (Casula, 2003; Casula, 2004) and STU Pfaffian wave functions (Bajdich, 2006; Bajdich, 2008) by using a fully trainable neural-network pairing orbital that can discover both singlet and triplet correlations without requiring fixed spin ordering. The method enables systematic improvement over fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo calculations that rely on BCS-like trial wave functions (Carlson, 2011; Forbes, 2011), and the transfer learning strategy demonstrated here can be applied to study larger systems and other strongly-interacting fermionic systems with the same symmetries and boundary conditions.
