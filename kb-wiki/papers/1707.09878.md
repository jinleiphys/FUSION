# Event-plane dependent di-hadron correlations with harmonic $v_n$ subtraction in a hydrodynamic model

> In this work, a hydrodynamic study of the di-hadron azimuthal correlations for the Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is carried out. The correlations are evaluated using the ZYAM method for the centrality windows as well as the transverse momentum range in accordance with the existing data. Event-plane dependence of the correlation is obtained after the subtraction of contributions from the most dominant harmonic coefficients. In particular, the contribution from the triangular flow, $v_3$, is removed from the proper correlations following the procedure implemented by the STAR collaboration. The resultant structure observed in the correlations was sometimes attributed to the mini-jet dynamics, but the present calculations show that a pure hydrodynamic model gives a reasonable agreement with the main feature of the published data. A brief discussion on the physical content of the present findings is presented.

## Key claim

A pure hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions can reproduce the observed double-peak structure of two-particle correlations on the away-side for out-of-plane triggers, even after subtracting triangular flow \(v_3\) via the ZYAM method, suggesting that the observed correlations are mostly of hydrodynamic origin rather than requiring mini-jet dynamics.

## Method

The authors used NeXSPheRIO, an ideal hydrodynamic model with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from NeXuS, to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They generated 1300–4600 events per 10% centrality bin in the 20–60% centrality range, with 200 Monte Carlo hadron generator samplings per event. They applied the ZYAM method to subtract flow backgrounds using Eq. (1) for \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) subtraction (matching STAR 2014 data) and Eq. (2) for only \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) subtraction (matching STAR 2010 data), with trigger particles at \(3 \le p_T^t \le 4\) GeV and associated particles at \(1 \le p_T^a \le 2\) GeV, and a pseudorapidity cut \(|\Delta\eta| < 0.7\) between trigger and associated particles.

## Key numbers

- Trigger particle \(v_2\) values for 20–30% centrality range from 0.1899 (in-plane, \(\phi_s = 0-\pi/12\)) to -0.0649 (out-of-plane, \(\phi_s = 5\pi/12-\pi/2\)) for \(3 < p_T^t < 4\) GeV/c.
- Trigger particle \(v_3\) values for 20–30% centrality range from 0.0304 (in-plane) to 0.0059 (out-of-plane) for the same \(p_T\) range.
- Associated particle \(v_2\) values for 20–30% centrality: 0.1028 for \(1 < p_T^a < 2\) GeV/c.
- Associated particle \(v_3\) values for 20–30% centrality: 0.0399 for the same \(p_T\) range.
- The hydrodynamic model used 1300 events for 20–30% centrality, 2400 for 30–40%, 4400 for 40–50%, and 4600 for 50–60%.

## Context

This work extends the peripheral-tube model (Castilho et al., 2015) and addresses the STAR collaboration's 2014 finding that after subtracting elliptic, triangular, and quadrangular flow, the away-side double-peak structure persists, which STAR attributed to pathlength-dependent jet-quenching. The authors show that a pure hydrodynamic model reproduces this feature, contradicting the need for jet-based explanations. The results also relate to Luzum's 2011 analysis suggesting flow entirely generates the correlations, but extend it by showing that even with \(v_3\) subtraction, the structure remains due to event-by-event fluctuations of flow harmonics. This enables further study of correlations between different flow harmonics, as explored by ATLAS (2014) and ALICE (2015).
