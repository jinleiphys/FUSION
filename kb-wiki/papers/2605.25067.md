# Thermal Spin Polarization Driven by Nuclear Spin-Orbit Coupling in Neutron Star Pasta

> We discuss anomalous spin polarization on the surface of nuclear pasta in a neutron star, driven by a nuclear spin-orbit interaction. We present an effective two-band model of surface-localized neutrons near the nuclear pasta. The central point is the emergence of a Rashba-type spin-orbit hybridization generated by the neutron--nucleus spin-orbit force in the presence of the strong density gradient normal to the pasta surface. Starting from a single-particle Hamiltonian with a central potential and a standard nuclear spin-orbit interaction, we show that the surface spin polarization occurs due to the thermal inhomogeneity even in the absence of a magnetic field. Our study links neutron-star physics and solid-state spintronics and would contribute to understanding the interplay between spin dynamics and strong magnetic fields.

# Thermal Spin Polarization Driven by Nuclear Spin-Orbit Coupling in Neutron Star Pasta

## Key claim

Surface-localized neutrons on nuclear pasta in neutron star crusts can develop a spin polarization through a thermal Rashba-Edelstein effect driven by a temperature gradient, even in the complete absence of a magnetic field. The effect relies on a Rashba-type spin-orbit hybridization that emerges from the standard nuclear spin-orbit force combined with the strong density gradient normal to the pasta surface.

## Method

The authors construct an effective two-band model for neutrons localized near a slab nuclear pasta surface, starting from a single-particle Hamiltonian with a central potential and a standard Skyrme-type nuclear spin-orbit interaction. They decompose the Hilbert space into surface-localized modes (described by a single-mode approximation in the z-direction with plane waves in the x-y plane) and extended bulk modes, then compute the linear response of the surface spin density to an in-plane temperature gradient using a semiclassical Boltzmann approach with a momentum relaxation time tau.

## Key numbers

- Effective Rashba spin-orbit coupling strength: alpha_s = -2 MeV·fm (used in numerical calculations)
- Surface band bottom shift: for B_z = 0, the band minimum shifts to k_perp = m* alpha_s
- Zeeman energy at B_z = 10^17 G: 2|mu_n B_z| ≈ 1.2 MeV
- Surface neutron density: rho_perp = O(10^-2) fm^-2 in the present setup
- Condition for non-negligible spin polarization P = O(10^-2): tau |nabla_perp T| = O(0.1) fm^-1
- For tau = O(1) MeV^-1, this requires |nabla_perp T| = O(0.1) MeV/fm
- Lower bound on tau: tau >> 1/|Delta epsilon| ~ O(1) MeV^-1 from the effective model validity condition
- Typical superfluid critical temperature: ~1 MeV
- Magnetic field range considered: up to 10^17 G, with the effect persisting even at vanishing or weak fields (e.g., B_z ~ 10^12-10^15 G)

## Context

This work bridges neutron-star physics and solid-state spintronics by applying the Rashba-Edelstein effect (Edelstein 1990, Johansson 2016), previously studied in semiconductor heterostructures and metallic interfaces (Yang 2006, Rojas-Sanchez 2013, Lesne 2016), to nuclear pasta surfaces in neutron star crusts. The nuclear spin-orbit coupling, essential for shell structure in finite nuclei (Mayer 1949, Haxel 1949), is usually negligible in homogeneous nuclear matter but remains strong near nuclear surfaces (PhysRevC.102.015802). The paper extends prior work on spin dynamics in neutron stars (Funaki 2025 on gyromagnetic effects, Sedrakian 2026 on spin currents) and on pasta phase structure (PhysRevC.77.035806, PhysRevC.87.055805, PhysRevD.106.063020). The results may contribute to understanding spin-triplet pairing in neutron-star crusts (PhysRevC.108.L052802, Yoshimura 2026) and connect to the Kibble-Zurek mechanism for vortex generation during superfluid transitions (Ko 2019, 2024).
