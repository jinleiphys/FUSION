# Shear viscosity, cavitation and hydrodynamics at LHC

> We study evolution of quark-gluon matter in the ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions within the frame work of relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics. In particular, by using the various prescriptions of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity to the entropy ratio, we show that the hydrodynamic description of the relativistic fluid become invalid due to the phenomenon of cavitation. For most of the initial conditions relevant for LHC, the cavitation sets in very early during the evolution of the hydrodynamics in time $\lesssim 2 $fm/c. The cavitation in this case is entirely driven by the large values of shear viscosity. Moreover we also demonstrate that the conformal term used in equations of the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic can influence the cavitation time.

# Shear viscosity, cavitation and hydrodynamics at LHC

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic description of quark-gluon matter at LHC energies becomes invalid due to cavitation driven entirely by large shear viscosity, setting in very early (less than 2 fm/c) for most LHC-relevant initial conditions. The conformal terms in the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equations can influence the cavitation time, and for some prescriptions of temperature-dependent η/s, inclusion of these terms can prevent cavitation entirely.

## Method
The authors solved 1+1 dimensional boost-invariant causal viscous hydrodynamics equations of Israel-Stewart type, using temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy ratio (η/s) from three sources: lattice QCD parametrization, virial expansion techniques, and the analytical forms of Shen et al. (2011). They evolved the equations for various initial proper times (0.3, 0.6, and 1.0 fm/c) and initial temperatures, with the equation of state taken from recent lattice QCD results, and monitored the longitudinal pressure P_z = P - Φ to determine when cavitation (negative longitudinal pressure) occurs.

## Key numbers
- For lQCD η/s with τ₀=0.6 fm/c and T₀=0.405 GeV, cavitation occurs at τ_cav=1.20 fm/c with T_cav=0.333 GeV (IS case without conformal terms)
- For lQCD η/s with τ₀=0.6 fm/c and T₀=0.405 GeV, cavitation occurs at τ_cav=1.53 fm/c with T_cav=0.316 GeV (IS+C case with conformal terms)
- For virial η/s with τ₀=0.6 fm/c and T₀=0.450 GeV, cavitation occurs at τ_cav=1.43 fm/c with T_cav=0.353 GeV (IS case); no cavitation occurs with conformal terms included
- For lQCD η/s with τ₀=0.3 fm/c and T₀=0.560 GeV, cavitation occurs at τ_cav=0.57 fm/c with T_cav=0.465 GeV (IS case)
- For virial η/s with τ₀=1.0 fm/c and T₀=0.350 GeV, cavitation occurs at τ_cav=2.16 fm/c with T_cav=0.283 GeV (IS case)
- For the η/s prescriptions of Shen et al. (2011) with T₀=0.419 GeV and τ₀=0.6 fm/c, cavitation sets in at τ_cav approximately 1 fm/c for all cases
- With η/s=1/4π and temperature-dependent ζ/s as in Rajagopal and collaborators (2009), no cavitation occurs at LHC energies

## Context
This work extends prior studies of cavitation in heavy-ion collisions, which had focused on bulk viscosity-driven cavitation near the critical temperature at RHIC energies (Rajagopal and collaborators, 2009; Bhatt and collaborators, 2010). The authors demonstrate that at LHC energies, where temperatures are well above T_c, shear viscosity alone can drive cavitation, unlike the RHIC case. The results indicate that hydrodynamic descriptions at LHC are valid only up to approximately 2 fm/c, after which the fluid may fragment or form inhomogeneous clusters, motivating hybrid approaches coupling viscous hydrodynamics to microscopic kinetic evolution as attempted by Song and collaborators (2010).
