# Transport parameters in neutron stars from in-medium NN cross sections

> We present a numerical study of shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter and $β$-stable nuclear matter, in the framework of the Brueckner theory. The calculation of in-medium cross sections and nucleon effective masses is performed with a consistent two and three body interaction. The investigation covers a wide baryon density range as requested in the applications to neutron stars. The results for the transport coefficients in $β$-stable nuclear matter are used to make preliminary predictions on the damping time scales of non radial modes in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, calculated consistently within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation with two- and three-body forces, produce a strong enhancement of shear viscosity and thermal conductivity in neutron star matter compared to calculations using free-space cross sections; this enhancement is mainly driven by the reduction of the effective mass and the associated level density, and the results are preliminary because they rely on a constant-density neutron star model that may underestimate the damping time scales by about a factor of ten.

## Method

The authors compute the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and beta-stable nuclear matter using the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism with Brooker-Sykes corrections. The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and effective masses are obtained self-consistently from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation using the Bonn B potential for the two-body force and a density-dependent effective three-body force derived from the same meson-exchange parameters. The transport coefficients are then integrated over the density profile of a constant-density neutron star model to estimate damping time scales for non-radial oscillation modes.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in beta-stable nuclear matter: at low total density, the difference between neutron and proton viscosity is about three orders of magnitude; at high density, where the proton fraction reaches 30% of the total density, the difference reduces to about one order of magnitude.
- Damping time scale for the l=m=2 non-radial mode due to neutron shear viscosity in cold neutron stars: of the same order of magnitude as the gravitational wave time scale (10-100 seconds), based on the constant-density approximation.
- At high temperature (T = 3 × 10^11 K), the viscosity damping time scale is about 10^13 to 10^14 seconds.
- Thermal conductivity damping time scale at T = 10^6 K is about 10^3 times larger than the viscosity damping time scale.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of transport coefficients in dense matter, specifically those by Benhar et al. (2005) using the correlated basis function approach and by Yakovlev et al. (2001) using a constant effective mass approximation, by providing a consistent BHF treatment with both two- and three-body forces over a wider density range. The results enable preliminary estimates of damping times for neutron star oscillations, which are relevant for interpreting gravitational wave signals, but the authors note that more accurate calculations beyond the constant-density approximation are needed, and that future work should also include superfluid effects and contributions from strange particles and quarks in the inner core.
