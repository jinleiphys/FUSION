# Exclusive QCD Factorization and Single Transverse Polarization Phenomena at High-Energy Colliders

> This Ph.D.~thesis is divided into two distinct parts. The first part focuses on hard exclusive scattering processes in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at high energies, while the second part delves into spin phenomena at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Hard exclusive scattering processes play a crucial role in QCD at high energies, providing unique insights into the confined partonic dynamics within hadrons, complementing inclusive processes. Studying these processes within the QCD factorization approach yields the generalized parton distribution (GPD), a nonperturbative parton correlation function that offers a three-dimensional tomographic parton image within a hadron. However, the experimental measurement of these processes poses significant challenges. This thesis will review the factorization formalism for related processes, examine the limitations of some widely used processes, and introduce two novel processes that enhance the sensitivity to GPD, particularly its dependence on the parton momentum fraction $x$. The second part of the thesis centers on spin phenomena, specifically single spin production, at the LHC. Noting that a single transverse polarization can be generated even in an unpolarized collision, this research proposes two new jet substructure observables: one for boosted top quark jets and another for high-energy gluon jets. The observation of these phenomena paves the way for innovative tools in LHC phenomenology, enabling both precision measurements and the search for new physics.

## Key claim

This Ph.D. thesis proposes that hard exclusive scattering processes can provide enhanced sensitivity to the x-dependence of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through two novel processes, and that single transverse polarization can be generated even in unpolarized collisions at the LHC, enabling new jet substructure observables for boosted top quark jets and high-energy gluon jets.

## Method

The thesis is divided into two parts. The first part reviews the QCD factorization formalism for single-diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs), examines limitations of widely used processes like deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), and introduces two new processes that enhance sensitivity to the x-dependence of GPDs. The second part proposes two new jet substructure observables for single transverse polarization phenomena at the LHC, one for boosted top quark jets and another for high-energy gluon jets, based on the observation that single transverse polarization can arise even in unpolarized collisions.

## Context

This thesis extends the QCD factorization approach for exclusive processes, building on the framework of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that provide three-dimensional tomographic parton images within hadrons. The work addresses limitations of standard processes like DVCS, which can only probe GPDs up to a few moments, and proposes new processes with enhanced x-sensitivity. The second part on single transverse polarization phenomena at the LHC enables new tools for precision measurements and new physics searches, complementing existing spin physics studies at high-energy colliders.
