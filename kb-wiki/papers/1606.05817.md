> 3 pages, 2 figures (note that due to size limitations Fig. 2 has reduced resolution as compared with journal submission), contribution to the proceedings of the 11th International conference on Clustering Aspects of Nuclear Structure and Dynamics, Napoli, May 23-27, 2016, which will be published in Journal of Physics: Conference series

# High spin structures in the $A\approx 40$ mass region: from superdeformation to extreme deformation and clusterization (an example of $^{28}$Si)

## Key claim

Cranked relativistic mean field calculations for $^{28}$Si up to spin $I=20\hbar$ show that clusterization signatures are present in superdeformed configurations and become especially pronounced in hyperdeformed configurations, but the paper hedges that the [0,0]-prolate configuration, while showing indications of neck development, cannot be described as a cluster of two nuclei because the density maximum remains in the central part of the nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) calculations using the NL3* functional, neglecting pairing correlations (justified by the Coriolis anti-pairing effect, shell gaps, and blocking), and considering only reflection-symmetric shapes. Configurations were labeled by shorthand [$n$,$p$] notation indicating the number of neutrons and protons in $N=3$ intruder orbitals, and the calculated energies were presented relative to a smooth liquid drop reference $AI(I+1)$ with inertia parameter $A=0.05$.

## Key numbers

- The [0,0]-oblate configuration is yrast up to $I\sim 10\hbar$ and terminates at $I=12\hbar$.
- The superdeformed [1,1]b configuration terminates at spin $I=18\hbar$.
- The hyperdeformed [2,2] configuration becomes yrast at higher spin above the termination of the [1,1]b band.
- The experimental prolate band has kinematic moment of inertia $J^{(1)}\sim 4$ $\hbar^2$/MeV, matching the calculated [0,0]-prolate configuration.
- The calculated kinematic moment of inertia for the [2,2] configuration is $J^{(1)}\sim 6.68$ $\hbar^2$/MeV, nearly constant over a large spin range, compared to the experimental value of $J^{(1)}\sim 6$ $\hbar^2$/MeV for the observed $4^+$ and $6^+$ superdeformed states.
- The maximum spin considered in the calculations is $I=20\hbar$.

## Context

This work extends the systematic search for extremely deformed structures in $N=Z$ and $N=Z+2$ nuclei from S, Ar, Ca, Ti, and Cr (reported in Ray and Afanasjev (2016)) to the specific case of $^{28}$Si. The calculations build on experimental indications from Jenkins et al. (2012) that the $4^+$ and $6^+$ states at 10.946 and 12.865 MeV are superdeformed, and show large similarities with predictions from the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model of Taniguchi, Kimura, and Kanada-En'yo (2009). The results identify configurations whose observation could become possible with future $\gamma$-tracking detectors such as AGATA and GRETA.
