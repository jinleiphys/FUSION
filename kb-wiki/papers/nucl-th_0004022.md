# On the $Λ$ to $Σ^0$ ratio from proton-proton collisions

> We compare the recent COSY data on the total $pp{\to}pΛ{K^+}$ and $pp{\to}pΣ^0{K^+}$ cross sections with the predictions from two boson exchange models that either are based solely on $π$ and $K$ exchange or include $π$, $η$ and $ρ$ exchange as well as the virtual excitation of intermediate baryon resonances. Both models are found to reproduce the data after the inclusion of final state hyperon-nucleon interactions. Thus, within the experimental uncertainties, both models also roughly reproduce the strong dependence of the $Λ$ to $Σ^0$ ratio as a function of the excess energy near threshold, as well as at higher energies.

## Key claim
Both a simple pi+K-exchange model and a more complex resonance model (including pi, eta, rho exchange and baryon resonances) can reproduce the COSY data on the Lambda to Sigma0 ratio from proton-proton collisions, provided final-state hyperon-nucleon interactions are included. The models were fixed prior to the COSY data, so the agreement constitutes a test of predictive power rather than a fit.

## Method
The authors compared two boson exchange models for pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> p Sigma0 K+ cross sections against COSY data as a function of excess energy. The pi+K-exchange model uses only pion and kaon exchange with amplitudes from scattering data, while the resonance model includes pi, eta, and rho exchange with excitation of intermediate baryon resonances (N(1650), N(1710), N(1710), Delta(1920)). Both models were supplemented with a Jost-approximation final-state interaction correction using hyperon-proton scattering lengths and effective ranges from the literature.

## Key numbers
- The Lambda to Sigma0 ratio R varies from about 20-30 at threshold (excess energy epsilon < 13 MeV) to about 2.5 at high energies (epsilon >= 1 GeV).
- The pi+K-exchange model with FSI describes the p K+ Lambda channel "remarkably well" and slightly overestimates the p K+ Sigma0 cross section very close to threshold.
- Both models reproduce the data to better than a factor of 2 without parameter adjustment to the COSY data.
- The model parameters for the pi+K-exchange model were fixed in 1995, and for the resonance model in 1997, both before the COSY data appeared.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Sibirtsev 1995, Tsushima 1997) that fixed the model parameters using high-energy data, and directly addresses the COSY measurements by Balewski, Bilger, and Sewerin (1999-2000). The paper contradicts the suggestion that the strong threshold suppression of Sigma0 production requires a special Sigma-to-Lambda conversion mechanism, showing instead that standard boson exchange models with FSI can reproduce the effect. The results enable future differential cross section measurements and polarization studies to isolate the kaon exchange contribution and determine NYK coupling constants.
