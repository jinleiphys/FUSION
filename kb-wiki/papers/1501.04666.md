# Unresolved issues in the search for eta-mesic nuclei

> Even if the theoretical definition of an unstable state is straightforward, its experimental identification often depends on the method used in the analysis and extraction of data. A good example is the case of eta mesic nuclei where strong hints of their existence led to about three decades of extensive theoretical and experimental searches. Considering the still undecided status of these states and the limitations in the understanding of the eta-nucleon as well as the eta-nucleus interaction, the present article tries to look back at some unresolved problems in the production mechanism and final state interaction of the eta mesons and nuclei. An unconventional perspective which provides a physical insight into the nature of the eta-nucleus interaction is also presented using quantum time concepts.

# Unresolved issues in the search for eta-mesic nuclei

## Key claim

Despite three decades of theoretical and experimental searches, there is still no general agreement on the existence of eta-mesic nuclei, and the strength of the eta-nucleon and eta-nucleus interaction remains undetermined. The identification of these unstable states depends critically on the theoretical model used for the production mechanism and the treatment of final state interactions, with different approaches leading to contradictory conclusions.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of eta-mesic nuclei by examining three key ingredients: models for the reaction mechanism producing eta mesons in the final state, frameworks for incorporating the eta-nucleus final state interaction (FSI), and the determination of the eta-nucleon interaction strength. The author presents a time delay analysis based on Wigner's concept, applied to eta-helium systems, and compares results from few-body calculations with on-shell approximations for extracting eta-nucleus scattering lengths from production data.

## Key numbers

- The eta meson mass is approximately 547 MeV, and its lifetime is about 10^-18 s
- For the pd → ^3He η reaction, a pole near threshold was found at Q0 = (-0.30 ± 0.15_stat ± 0.04_syst) ± i(0.21 ± 0.29_stat ± 0.06_syst) MeV
- The eta-nucleon scattering length a_ηN varies over a wide range: 0.18 ≤ Re a_ηN ≤ 1.03 fm and 0.16 ≤ Im a_ηN ≤ 0.49 fm
- For increasing a_ηN values of (0.28 + i0.19) fm, (0.51 + i0.26) fm, and (0.88 + i0.41) fm, the pole moves from a quasibound state at (-5 - i8) MeV to near threshold at (0 - i1.95) MeV and then to a resonance at (0.5 + i0.65) MeV
- The eta-^3He scattering length was determined as a_η^3He ≈ (-2.3 + i3.2) fm and eta-^4He as a_η^4He ≈ (-2.2 + i1.1) fm from a simultaneous fit to dd → ^4He η and pd → ^3He η data
- The first prediction of a_ηN = (0.28 + i0.19) fm (Bhalerao, 1985) is in close agreement with a more recent calculation giving a_ηN = (0.3 + i0.18) fm
- The latest coupled channel analysis (Roenchen, 2015) yields a_ηN = (0.49 + i0.24) fm and (0.55 + i0.24) fm from different fits

## Context

This review extends the long-standing search for eta-mesic nuclei initiated by Haider and Liu (1986) and follows earlier reviews by Wilkin (1997), Mach (2001), and the author's own group. The paper highlights discrepancies between few-body calculations (Weinberg, 2004; Garcilazo, 2007) that include off-shell rescattering and simpler on-shell approximations for extracting scattering lengths. The unresolved issues point to the need for more accurate determination of the eta-nucleon interaction from elementary eta production reactions, which would guide experimental searches by the WASA collaboration at COSY toward specific nuclei and reactions.
