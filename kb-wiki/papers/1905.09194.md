# Constraining level densities using spectral data

> Several models of level densities exist and they often make simplified assumptions regarding the overall behavior of the total level densities (LD) and the intrinsic spin and parity distributions of the excited states. Normally, such LD models are constrained only by the measured $D_0$, i.e. the density of levels at the neutron separation energy of the compound nucleus (target plus neutron), and the sometimes subjective extrapolation of discrete levels. In this work we use microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) level densities, which intrinsically provide more realistic spin and parity distributions, and associate variations predicted by the HFB model with the observed double-differential cross sections at low outgoing neutron energy, region that is dominated by the LD input. With this approach we are able to perform fits of the LD based on actual experimental data, constraining the model and ensuring its consistency. This approach can be particularly useful in extrapolating the LD to nuclei for which high-excited discrete levels and/or values of $D_0$ are unknown. It also predicts inelastic gamma (n,n$^{\prime}γ$) cross sections that in some cases can differ significantly from more standard LD models such as Gilbert-Cameron.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that neutron double-differential spectra data can be used to directly constrain microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) level densities in excitation energy regions where no other experimental information is available, but they note that the resulting smoothed HFB level densities do not necessarily represent the optimal or unique solution, only that the method improves consistency between calculated cross sections and experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach and pre-equilibrium reaction calculations for n + 56Fe using the EMPIRE code, comparing five different level density approaches: Gilbert-Cameron (GC) as used in the ENDF/B-VIII.0 evaluation, unmodified HFB from RIPL-3, HFB with fitted 56Mn parameters, HFB with smoothed 56Fe structures to improve double-differential spectra agreement, and HFB with both smoothed 56Fe and smoothed and refitted 56Mn. They used the Kalman filter utility within EMPIRE to vary the two HFB parameters (ROHFBA and ROHFBP) for 56Mn, minimizing chi-squared relative to experimental (n,p) cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- The HFB level densities for 56Fe show fluctuations in the excitation energy range 5 to 9 MeV that are not present in the smooth Gilbert-Cameron model.
- After fitting 56Mn HFB parameters to (n,p) data, the optimal parametrization increased the ROHFBA parameter by 45% and the ROHFBP parameter by 49% from default values.
- After smoothing 56Fe structures and refitting 56Mn, the final parameter deviations were only 33% for ROHFBA and 18% for ROHFBP, indicating less deviation from the original HFB predictions.
- Double-differential spectra comparisons are shown at incident neutron energies of 14.1 MeV and 8.17 MeV at 150 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the use of microscopic HFB level densities from the RIPL-3 library (Goriely et al.) beyond their standard application by introducing experimental constraints from double-differential spectra, addressing a limitation noted in prior work (WONDER2015) where phenomenological models like Gilbert-Cameron (1965) were preferred for their fitting flexibility. The approach enables more reliable extrapolation of level densities to nuclei without measured D0 values or discrete level information, and provides a method to improve predictions for inelastic gamma cross sections, particularly for transitions involving states with parity opposite to the ground state where the HFB model's realistic parity distributions become important.
