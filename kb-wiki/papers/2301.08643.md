# Multiphonon structure of high-spin states in $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb

> The method of description of the high-spin states, which was previously developed and applied for the states of this type in $^{208}$Pb, is generalized for the case of the states having more complex multiphonon structure. In this method, the harmonic approximation with the renormalized phonons is used in which the phonons themselves are determined within the non-linear version of the model based on the random-phase approximation (RPA) and including both the RPA correlations and the beyond-RPA ones. The mean field and the residual interaction are derived within the framework of the self-consistent RPA from the energy-density functional of the Skyrme type. The method is applied for the analysis of the available experimental data in doubly magic $^{208}$Pb and $^{40}$Ca and in semi-magic $^{90}$Zr.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed method for describing high-spin states in $^{208}$Pb to nuclei with more complex multiphonon structure, using a harmonic approximation with renormalized phonons determined from a non-linear version of the random-phase approximation (RPA) that includes both RPA and beyond-RPA correlations, with the caveat that the harmonic approximation neglects phonon-phonon interactions in the multiphonon configurations at higher energies.

## Method

The authors employ the renormalized time-blocking approximation (RenTBA), a non-linear extension of RPA that includes 1p1h and 1p1h⊗phonon configurations, where phonons are determined self-consistently from non-linear RenTBA equations. The mean field and residual interaction are derived from the Skyrme-type energy-density functional (SV-bas$_{-0.44}$ parametrization) using a spherical coordinate-space grid with box radius 18 fm and particle energies limited to 100 MeV. Multiphonon states are constructed as simple sums of renormalized phonon energies, with incompatible phonon combinations excluded by numerical analysis of the 1p1h structure of the RPA phonon components.

## Key numbers

- In $^{208}$Pb, phonon renormalization changes phonon energies by 0.3–1.4 MeV, with especially large renormalization for high-spin phonons.
- In $^{40}$Ca, phonon renormalization changes phonon energies by 0.2–0.6 MeV, but many states have 3-, 4-, and 5-phonon configurations, so renormalization can change state energies by 1–1.5 MeV.
- For $^{40}$Ca, the theoretical proton separation energy is $S_\mathrm{theor.}(p)=7.565$ MeV, compared to the experimental value $S_\mathrm{exp.}(p)=8328.17$ keV.
- In $^{208}$Pb, the $13^-_1$ and $14^-_1$ states are one-phonon configurations; states with spins $13 \le I \le 26$ (except $26^-$) are two-phonon configurations; states with $I=27$ and higher require three-phonon configurations.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the $18^+_1$ state has experimental energy 9.1030 MeV and theoretical energy 9.44 MeV; the $20^+_1$ state has experimental energy 10.1959 MeV and theoretical energy 9.51 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RenTBA approach of Lyutorovich (2022) for $^{208}$Pb to include multiphonon configurations in $^{40}$Ca and $^{90}$Zr, building on the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev and co-workers and the equation of motion phonon method (EMPM) of Andreozzi (2007) and Bianco (2012), which did not apply multiphonon models to high-spin states. The results enable systematic self-consistent description of high-spin states in magic and semi-magic nuclei without parameter refitting, and allow refinement of level assignments in $^{40}$Ca where previous calculations overestimated band-head energies by 2 MeV or more.
