# Axial-vector weak coupling at medium momentum for astro neutrinos and double beta decays

> Neutrino nuclear responses associated with medium momentum transfer of q=20-80 MeV for astro neutrinos and double beta decays were studied by using charge exchange reactions on Te128 and Te130. Gamow-Teller and spin dipole nuclear matrix elements are found to be reduced with respect to the pnQRPA matrix elements by the coefficient of around 0.35. The reduction is discussed in terms of the quenching of axial vector coupling (g_A).

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole (SD) nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for tellurium isotopes are uniformly reduced with respect to pnQRPA NMEs by a coefficient of approximately 0.35 across the wide momentum transfer region of q = 20–80 MeV/c, suggesting a universal quenching of the axial-vector weak coupling (g_A) for astro-neutrino and double-beta-decay responses.

## Method

The author analyzed charge exchange (³He,t) reactions on ¹²⁸Te and ¹³⁰Te at the RCNP Osaka University with a beam energy of 0.42 GeV, measuring differential cross sections as a function of momentum transfer q over an angular range of θ = 0–4 degrees (q = 5–120 MeV/c). Experimental cross sections for the Fermi (IAS), GT (first excited state), and SD (ground state) transitions were compared to DWBA calculations to extract the effective q-dependent coupling coefficient κ^eff(q). The ratios of experimental to DWBA cross sections were examined to determine the constancy of the quenching coefficient across the momentum region.

## Key numbers

- The reduction (quenching) coefficient for GT and SD NMEs relative to pnQRPA NMEs is k_NM ≈ 0.35 over the momentum region q = 20–80 MeV/c.
- For ¹²⁸Te → ¹²⁸I: experimental SD NME = 3.22 (in 10⁻³ n.u.), pnQRPA SD NME = 9.62, giving k^eff_NM = 0.33.
- For ¹³⁰Te → ¹³⁰I: experimental GT NME = 0.32, pnQRPA GT NME = 0.90, giving k^eff_NM = 0.34.
- For ¹²⁴Te → ¹²⁴I: experimental GT NME = 0.36, pnQRPA GT NME = 0.99, giving k^eff_NM = 0.36.
- For ¹²⁶Te → ¹²⁶I: experimental SD NME = 2.743 (in 10⁻³ n.u.), pnQRPA SD NME = 8.38, giving k^eff_NM = 0.33.
- The vector (Fermi) NME for the isobaric analog state is given by the sum rule limit in the momentum transfer region, with no reduction observed.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of β-decay and electron-capture NMEs at low momentum transfer (q ≤ a few MeV/c) by Ejiri and collaborators (2000, 2015, 2014), which found reduction factors of order 0.4–0.6, to the medium momentum region relevant for supernova neutrinos and neutrinoless double beta decay. The results are consistent with recent ordinary muon capture (OMC) measurements by Haselsberger (2018), Zinatulina (2019), and Jokiniemi (2019), which found a reduction coefficient of k_NM ≈ 0.4 at q = 50–100 MeV/c. The universal quenching implies that neutrinoless double-beta-decay NMEs are reduced by a factor of (k^eff_NM)² ≈ 0.1–0.2 relative to pnQRPA predictions, requiring 2–3 orders of magnitude more detector mass for a given neutrino-mass sensitivity.
