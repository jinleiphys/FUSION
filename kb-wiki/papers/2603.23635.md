# Equation of state and cumulants of proton multiplicity in equilibrium near critical point from Pade estimates

> The fluctuations of proton multiplicity in heavy-ion collisions are the key observables in the search for the QCD critical point. In this work we present an approach to constraining the cumulants of proton number based on the analytical properties of the QCD equation of state in the vicinity of the critical point. We show that, under the assumption of local equilibrium, the features of the collision energy dependence, such as the peaks and the dips of the cumulants, are significantly constrained by the data on the Lee-Yang singularity structure available via Pade resummation of the lattice QCD data. Furthermore, we identify four topologically distinct scenarios, all within the uncertainty range of the Pade estimates for the non-universal mapping parameters, classified based on the location of the critical point and the slope of the chiral crossover curve with respect to the freeze-out curve. These different scenarios result in qualitatively different critical signatures, especially for the third factorial cumulant and thus could be potentially discriminated using the experimental data.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of local equilibrium, the collision-energy dependence of proton-number cumulants near the QCD critical point is significantly constrained by the Lee-Yang singularity structure extracted via Padé resummation of lattice QCD data, but the qualitative features (peaks and dips) depend sensitively on the non-universal mapping parameters, leading to four topologically distinct scenarios that could be discriminated by experimental data.

## Method

The authors used Padé approximants (ordinary and with two different conformal maps) to extrapolate lattice QCD Taylor coefficients (up to order \(\mu_B^8\) from the HotQCD collaboration) to extract the Lee-Yang edge singularity trajectory in the complex \(\mu_B\) plane as a function of temperature. From this trajectory they determined the critical point location \((T_c, \mu_c)\) and the non-universal mapping parameters \(c_1\) and \(c_2\), which fix the slope of the crossover line and the shape of the critical region. They then applied the maximum-entropy freeze-out framework (Pradeep 2022) to convert the critical contribution to the QCD equation of state (mapped from the 3D Ising model via a linear map) into factorial cumulants of proton multiplicity along the freeze-out curve.

## Key numbers

* Critical point from uniformizing-map Padé: \(T_c = 97^{+18}_{-18}\) MeV, \(\mu_c = 579^{+172}_{-160}\) MeV, crossover slope \(\alpha_1 = 9.40^\circ\,^{+3.89}_{-3.81}\), \(c_2 = 2.22^{+0.52}_{-0.86}\) MeV\(^{1-\beta\delta}\).
* Critical point from two-cut-map Padé: \(T_c = 100^{+18}_{-18}\) MeV, \(\mu_c = 557^{+175}_{-150}\) MeV, crossover slope \(\alpha_1 = 8.69^\circ\,^{+3.91}_{-3.83}\), \(c_2 = 2.56^{+0.58}_{-1.21}\) MeV\(^{1-\beta\delta}\).
* Critical point from ordinary Padé (no conformal map): \(T_c = 108^{+21}_{-21}\) MeV, \(\mu_c = 437^{+114}_{-50}\) MeV, crossover slope \(\alpha_1 = 4.55^\circ\,^{+3.41}_{-3.37}\), \(c_2 = 3.35^{+0.82}_{-1.37}\) MeV\(^{1-\beta\delta}\).
* Lattice QCD indicates no critical point for \(\mu_B \lesssim 450\) MeV (Borsanyi 2025).
* Chiral crossover width approximately 30 MeV; deconfinement crossover width approximately 80 MeV (Borsanyi 2024).
* Lee-Yang edge exponent \(\sigma = 0.0742(56)\) in d=3 (An 2016); numerical constant \(x_{\rm LY} \approx 0.246\).

## Context

This work extends the program of using Lee-Yang singularities extracted from lattice QCD via Padé resummation (Basar 2021, Basar 2023) to constrain the QCD critical point and the non-universal mapping parameters. It builds on the maximum-entropy freeze-out framework (Pradeep 2022, Karthein 2025) that relates hydrodynamic fluctuations to hadronic observables. The results enable experimental discrimination between four topologically distinct scenarios for the critical point location and the slope of the chiral crossover relative to the freeze-out curve, which could be tested by the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future experiments such as CBM at FAIR.
