# Holographic QCD in the NICER era

> We analyze families of hybrid equations of state of cold QCD matter, which combine input from gauge/gravity duality and from various ab initio methods for nuclear matter at low density, and predict that all neutron stars are fully hadronic without quark matter cores. We focus on constraints from recent measurements by the NICER telescope on the radius and mass of the millisecond pulsar PSR J0740+6620. These results are found to be consistent with our approach: they set only mild constraints on the hybrid equations of state, and favor the most natural models which are relatively stiff at low density. Adding an upper bound on the maximal mass of neutron stars, as suggested by the analysis of the GW170817 neutron star merger event, tightens the constraints considerably. We discuss updated predictions on observables such as the transition density and latent heat of the nuclear to quark matter transition as well as the masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that all neutron stars are fully hadronic without quark matter cores, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific holographic V-QCD model used and holds only for the hybrid equations of state that satisfy the combined astrophysical constraints from NICER, LIGO/Virgo, and maximum mass limits.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state for cold QCD matter by combining low-density nuclear matter models (HLPSs, APR, SLy, HLPSi, IUF) with the V-QCD holographic model for dense nuclear matter and quark matter, matching them at a transition density parameter n_tr ranging from 1.2 to 2.6 times nuclear saturation density. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and apply constraints from NICER radius measurements of PSR J0740+6620, the tidal deformability bound from GW170817, and the maximum mass limit of 2.19 solar masses from the merger remnant analysis.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: M = 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (68% confidence)
- Riley et al. equatorial radius for PSR J0740+6620: 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km (68% credibility), giving a lower limit of R > 11.4 km
- Miller et al. equatorial radius for PSR J0740+6620: 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km (68% credibility), giving a lower limit of R > 12.2 km
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: 580 ≥ Λ(1.4 solar masses) ≥ 70 (90% confidence)
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass neutron stars constrained to 12.0 km ≤ R(1.4 solar masses) ≤ 12.8 km
- Transition density range for nuclear to quark matter: 4.25 ≤ n_b/n_s ≤ 6.83 (where n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3})
- Latent heat of nuclear to quark matter transition: always in excess of 750 MeV/fm^3 (minimum 747 MeV/fm^3)
- Maximum speed of sound squared stays below c_s^2 ≈ 0.6 for all constrained equations of state

## Context

This work extends the hybrid equation of state framework developed by Jokela et al. (2020) and Ecker et al. (2019) by incorporating the latest NICER radius constraints from Miller et al. (2021) and Riley et al. (2021) for PSR J0740+6620. The results are consistent with the interpolative approach of Annala et al. (2021) but provide a theoretically motivated holographic description that rules out quark matter cores in stable neutron stars, in contrast to some interpolation schemes that allow for such cores. The findings enable more precise predictions for gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers and constrain the parameter space for future multimessenger observations.
