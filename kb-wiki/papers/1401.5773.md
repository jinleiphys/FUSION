# Ghosts in Keldysh-Schwinger Formalism

> We discuss how to introduce Faddeev-Popov ghosts to the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism describing equilibrium and non-equilibrium statistical systems of quantum fields such as the quark-gluon plasma which is considered. The plasma is assumed to be homogeneous in a coordinate space but the momentum distribution of plasma constituents is arbitrary. Using the technique of generating functional, we derive the Slavnov-Taylor identities and one of them expresses the ghost Green's function, which we look for, through the gluon one. As an application, the Green's function of ghosts is used to compute the gluon polarization tensor in the hard loop approximation which appears to be automatically transverse, as required by the gauge invariance.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that in the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism for out-of-equilibrium quantum chromodynamics, the Faddeev-Popov ghost Green's function is not an independent input but is fully determined by the gluon Green's function through a Slavnov-Taylor identity, provided the system is homogeneous in coordinate space and the Feynman gauge is used.

## Method

The authors construct the generating functional for QCD in the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism, including ghost fields and external sources, with the system assumed translationally invariant but with arbitrary momentum distribution. They derive the general Slavnov-Taylor identities by requiring invariance of the generating functional under local gauge transformations that vanish at the initial time, adapting Slavnov's original method rather than using BRST symmetry. From these identities, they extract a specific relation that expresses the contour-ordered ghost Green's function in terms of the gluon Green's function, and they verify the consistency of this relation by computing the gluon polarization tensor in the hard loop approximation.

## Key numbers

* The gluon density is given as \(\rho_g = 2 (N_c^2 -1) \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}\, n_g ({\bf p})\), where the factor of 2 accounts for two gluon spin states and \(N_c\) is the number of colors.
* The free gluon Feynman propagator in the medium is \(D^c_{\mu\nu}^{ab}(p) = -g_{\mu\nu} \delta^{ab} \big[ \frac{1}{p^2+i0^+} - \frac{i\pi}{E_p}\big(\delta(p_0-E_p) n_g({\bf p}) + \delta(p_0+E_p) n_g(-{\bf p}) \big)\big]\), where \(E_p = |{\bf p}|\) and \(n_g({\bf p})\) is the gluon distribution function.
* The Fourier-transformed Slavnov-Taylor identity relating gluon and ghost contour Green's functions is \(- p^\mu \mathcal{D}_{\mu\nu}^{ab}(p) = p_\nu \Delta_{ab}(-p)\).

## Context

This work extends the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism for non-equilibrium gauge theories by resolving the long-standing ambiguity of how to treat Faddeev-Popov ghosts in this framework, where the generating functional cannot be computed in closed form even for free theories. It improves upon an earlier attempt by Okano (2001) to derive Slavnov-Taylor identities in this formalism, which the authors characterize as unsatisfactory. The result enables consistent perturbative calculations in out-of-equilibrium quark-gluon plasmas, as demonstrated by the automatic transversality of the gluon polarization tensor in the hard loop approximation when the ghost contribution is included via the derived identity.
