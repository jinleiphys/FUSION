# Vector Meson production in ep -> epV

## Key claim
The diffractive production of vector mesons in ep interactions at low x is a subject of heated debates, and this chapter presents four distinct theoretical scenarios from the original authors that must be resolved experimentally; the paper does not claim a single correct model but rather lays out competing frameworks whose predictions differ on key observables such as energy dependence, t-slope behavior, and the role of shadowing corrections.

## Method
The paper compiles four separate theoretical contributions: (1) Frankfurt, Koepf, and Strikman derive QCD factorization formulas for hard diffractive vector meson production using light-cone wave functions and the gluon distribution in the target; (2) Landshoff compares models coupling the virtual photon to the vector meson via a quark loop with two gluons attached to the proton, examining soft-pomeron versus BFKL-pomeron energy dependence; (3) Gotsman, Levin, and Maor compute shadowing corrections within the DGLAP evolution framework at low x, defining a damping factor D^2 for the differential cross section; (4) Nikolaev and Zakharov use the color dipole formalism with a scanning radius r_S = 6/sqrt(Q^2 + m_V^2) to relate the production amplitude to the gluon density at a low factorization scale.

## Key numbers
- For longitudinal polarization, the end point contribution is suppressed by 1/Q^2, supporting application of the factorization theorem.
- For transverse polarizations, large distance effects are suppressed only by the square of a Sudakov type form factor, exp(-(4*alpha_s/(3*pi)) * ln^2(Q^2/k_t^2)).
- For Q^2 ~ 5 GeV^2, the increase of the cross section with decreasing x is expected to slow down at x ~ 10^{-3} due to b-space unitarity.
- The ratio sigma_L/sigma_T is proportional to Q^2/m_V^2 (equation 1).
- For the soft pomeron, epsilon ≈ 0.08 and alpha' = 0.25 GeV^{-2} in the W-dependence formula dσ/dt = f(t,Q^2) * exp(4(epsilon - alpha'|t|) log W).
- Shadowing corrections generate a damping factor D^2 of order 0.5 for J/Ψ production at W = 100-200 GeV for Q^2 = 0-6 GeV^2.
- The scanning radius r_S = 6/sqrt(Q^2 + m_V^2) makes vector meson production at best semiperturbative unless Q^2 + m_V^2 ≥ 20-40 GeV^2 (i.e., r_S ≤ R_c = 0.3 fm).
- The effective intercept Δ from gBFKL is predicted to rise with Q^2 and flatten at a Q^2-independent Δ ≈ 0.4 at very large W.
- The Q^2 dependence follows the law ∝ (Q^2 + m_V^2)^n with n typically ~ -2.2 at HERA, approximately flavor independent.
- The rise of the diffraction slope by ~1.5 GeV^{-2} is predicted to be approximately universal for all vector mesons and at all Q^2 from CERN/FNAL to HERA energies.

## Context
This paper extends the ongoing debate on diffractive vector meson production at HERA, where multiple theoretical approaches, including the QCD factorization approach of Brodsky et al. (1994), the soft-pomeron model of Donnachie and Landshoff, and the BFKL-based color dipole formalism of Nikolaev and Zakharov, compete to describe the same experimental data. The work enables experimental tests at HERA to distinguish between these scenarios, particularly through measurements of the energy dependence of the cross section, the t-slope behavior, the ratio of longitudinal to transverse cross sections, and the production ratios of different vector meson states and their radial excitations.
