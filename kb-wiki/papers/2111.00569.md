# Gliding down the QCD transition line, from $N_f=2$ till the onset of conformality

> We review the hot QCD transition with varying number of flavors, from two till the onset of the conformal window. We discuss the universality class for $N_f=2$, along the critical line for two massless light flavors, and a third flavor whose mass serves as an interpolator between $N_f = 2$ and $N_f=3$. We identify a possible scaling window for the 3D $O(4)$ universality class transition, and its crossover to a mean field behaviour. We follow the transition from $N_f=3$ to larger $N_f$, when it remains of first order, with an increasing coupling strength; we summarize its known properties, including possible cosmological applications as a model for a strong electroweak transition. The first order transition, and its accompanying second order endpoint, finally morphs into the essential singularity at the onset of the conformal window, following the singular behaviour predicted by the Functional Renormalization Group.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the hot QCD transition as the number of light quark flavors is varied from Nf=2 up to the onset of the conformal window, identifying a possible scaling window for the 3D O(4) universality class transition and its crossover to mean field behavior, while noting that the first-order transition and its second-order endpoint eventually morph into an essential singularity at the conformal onset following the singular behavior predicted by the Functional Renormalization Group.

## Method

The authors survey lattice QCD results and functional renormalization group analyses across varying numbers of flavors, examining the universality class for Nf=2 along the critical line with two massless light flavors and a third flavor whose mass serves as an interpolator between Nf=2 and Nf=3. They trace the transition from Nf=3 to larger Nf, where it remains first order with increasing coupling strength, and follow the first-order transition and its accompanying second-order endpoint as they morph into the essential singularity at the onset of the conformal window.

## Key numbers

- For Nf=2, the transition is expected to belong to the 3D O(4) universality class, with a possible scaling window identified.
- For Nf=3 and larger, the transition remains first order with an increasing coupling strength as Nf increases.
- The first-order transition and its second-order endpoint eventually morph into an essential singularity at the onset of the conformal window, following the singular behavior predicted by the Functional Renormalization Group.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the QCD phase diagram by examining the flavor dependence of the chiral transition, building on studies of the Nf=2 O(4) universality class and the Nf=3 first-order transition. The paper synthesizes lattice results and functional renormalization group predictions to map the transition line from the chiral limit through the conformal window, enabling future work on the nature of the conformal onset and potential cosmological applications as a model for a strong electroweak transition.
