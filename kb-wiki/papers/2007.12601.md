# Pseudoscalar and vector open-charm mesons at finite temperature

> Vacuum and thermal properties of pseudoscalar and vector charm mesons are analyzed within a self-consistent many-body approach, employing a chiral effective field theory that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. Upon unitarization of the vacuum interaction amplitudes for the scattering of charm mesons off light mesons in a fully coupled-channel basis, new dynamically generated states are searched. The imaginary-time formalism is employed to extend the calculation to finite temperatures up to $T=150$ MeV. Medium-modified spectral shapes of the $D$, $D^*$, $D_s$ and $D_s^*$ mesons are provided. The temperature dependence of the masses and decay widths of the nonstrange $D_0^*$ (2300) and $D_1^*$(2430) mesons, both showing a double-pole structure in the complex-energy plane, is also reported, as well as that of the $D_{s0}^*$(2317) and $D_{s1}^*$(2460) resonances and other states not yet identified experimentally. Being the first calculation incorporating open-charm vector mesons at finite temperature in a self-consistent fashion, it brings up the opportunity to discuss the medium effects on the open charm sector under the perspective of chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first self-consistent calculation of open-charm vector mesons at finite temperature, showing that the medium modifications of both pseudoscalar and vector charm mesons can be described within a chiral effective field theory that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry, with the caveat that the calculation is limited to temperatures up to 150 MeV and does not include contributions from baryonic matter.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral effective field theory expanded up to next-to-leading order in chiral counting and leading order in heavy-quark mass counting, with low-energy constants fixed from lattice-QCD calculations. The tree-level scattering amplitudes for charm mesons scattering off light mesons (pions, kaons, antikaons, and eta mesons) are unitarized by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation in a fully coupled-channel basis using a hard momentum cutoff of 800 MeV. For finite-temperature calculations, the imaginary-time formalism is used with self-consistent dressing of the heavy-meson propagators, and the temperature dependence is studied up to 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff value for regularization is taken as Lambda = 800 MeV, of the order of the rho meson mass
- The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- The calculation extends to finite temperatures up to T = 150 MeV
- The D and D* mesons acquire a substantial width in the pionic bath, reaching 30-40 MeV at T = 150 MeV (referencing prior work by Cleven et al. 2017)
- The D meson mass is 1867.24 MeV, the D* meson mass is 2008.56 MeV, the D_s meson mass is 1968.34 MeV, and the D_s* meson mass is 2112.20 MeV (isospin-averaged vacuum values)
- The D_0*(2300) and D_1*(2430) mesons both show a double-pole structure in the complex-energy plane

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature unitarized approach of Cleven et al. (2017) by using a chiral effective Lagrangian expanded to next-to-leading order rather than leading order, and extends the authors' previous work (Montana et al. 2020) beyond the analysis of D_0*(2300) and D_s(2317) states to include all strange-isospin channels in the scalar and axial vector open-charm sectors. The calculation enables future studies of open-charm meson properties in heavy-ion collision environments at facilities such as FAIR with the CBM experiment, and provides a framework for discussing medium effects under the perspective of chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries.
