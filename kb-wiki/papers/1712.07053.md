# Illuminating the photon content of the proton within a global PDF analysis

> Precision phenomenology at the LHC requires accounting for both higher-order QCD and electroweak corrections as well as for photon-initiated subprocesses. Building upon the recent NNPDF3.1 fit, in this work the photon content of the proton is determined within a global analysis supplemented by the LUXqed constraint relating the photon PDF to lepton-proton scattering structure functions: NNPDF3.1luxQED. The uncertainties on the resulting photon PDF are at the level of a few percent, with photons carrying up to 0.5% of the proton's momentum. We study the phenomenological implications of NNPDF3.1luxQED at the LHC for Drell-Yan, vector boson pair, top quark pair, and Higgs plus vector boson production. We find that photon-initiated contributions can be significant for many processes, leading to corrections of up to 20%. Our results represent a state-of-the-art determination of the partonic structure of the proton including its photon component.

## Key claim

The photon content of the proton is determined within a global PDF analysis supplemented by the LUXqed theoretical constraint, reducing photon PDF uncertainties to the level of a few percent, with the caveat that the NNPDF3.1 dataset and kinematic cuts were deliberately designed to minimize sensitivity to photon-initiated contributions, so those effects are not directly fitted but rather imposed via the LUXqed relation.

## Method

The authors performed an iterative global PDF analysis starting from the NNPDF3.1 quark and gluon PDFs, computing the photon PDF at Q=100 GeV using the LUXqed formalism (which expresses the photon PDF in terms of lepton-proton scattering structure functions F2 and FL), then evolving the photon PDF down to the parametrization scale Q0=1.65 GeV and using it as a fixed input in a refit of quark and gluon PDFs. The DGLAP evolution equations included QED corrections up to O(α^2) and O(αα_s), and the momentum sum rule was modified to include the photon contribution. The procedure was iterated until convergence, with additional systematic uncertainties from elastic, resonance, and low-Q^2 inelastic contributions added in the final iteration.

## Key numbers

- Photon PDF uncertainties are at the level of a few percent
- Photons carry up to approximately 0.5% of the proton's momentum
- The total dataset χ²/N_dat = 1.148 for both NNPDF3.1 and NNPDF3.1luxQED fits
- Photon-initiated corrections can reach up to approximately 10% for high-mass Drell-Yan production
- Photon-initiated corrections can reach up to approximately 20% for W⁺W⁻ production
- The cumulative effect of O(α²) and O(αα_s) corrections to DGLAP splitting functions on the γγ luminosity ranges between approximately 10% at low invariant masses and approximately 5% at high invariant masses
- QED effects on DIS structure functions are at the permille level, except at large x where they can be up to 2%

## Context

This work extends the NNPDF3.1 global fit (Ball 2017) by incorporating the LUXqed formalism (Manohar 2016, 2017), which provides a theoretical constraint relating the photon PDF to lepton-proton scattering structure functions. It supersedes earlier data-driven NNPDF QED determinations (NNPDF2.3/3.0QED, Ball 2013, 2014) that had large photon PDF uncertainties due to limited experimental sensitivity, and is related to parallel work in the MMHT framework (Harland-Lang 2017). The resulting NNPDF3.1luxQED set enables precision calculations of LHC processes requiring both QCD and electroweak corrections, particularly for Drell-Yan, vector boson pair, top quark pair, and Higgs plus vector boson production where photon-initiated contributions can be significant.
