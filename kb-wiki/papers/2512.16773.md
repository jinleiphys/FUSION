# Comparing invariant-mass spectroscopy of 8B with ab initio predictions

> Levels in 8B have been investigated experimentally using the invariant-mass technique and compared to ab initio calculations. Data sets obtained using E/A=69-MeV 9C and 13O beams on a Be target have been further analyzed to extend the level scheme of 8B for Ex<10 MeV. New levels were observed in the 2p+6Li, p+3He+alpha, and the p+7Be+gamma exit channels. Momentum correlations between the decay fragments were also investigated in order to deduce the decay pathways and whether the decays are prompt or sequential. This nucleus and its mirror were also investigated in the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model. Correspondence between the newly observed and predicted levels were made based on the level energy and the decay modes. For positive parity levels with J<=3, all predicted levels can be connected to an experimental counter part (as least tentatively) for Ex<8.4 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for positive-parity levels with spin J <= 3, all predicted levels from the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) can be connected to an experimental counterpart, at least tentatively, for excitation energies below 8.4 MeV, but this correspondence relies on matching both level energy and decay modes, and the assignment is qualified as tentative.

## Method

The authors used the invariant-mass technique with two datasets from E/A = 69-MeV 9C beams on a Be target (single-proton knockout) and one dataset from an E/A = 69.5-MeV 13O beam (projectile fragmentation) to study resonances in 8B. They detected decay fragments using the HiRA Si-CsI(Tl) telescope array and the CAESAR gamma-ray detector array, reconstructing excitation energies from invariant masses of 2p+6Li, p+3He+alpha, and p+7Be+gamma exit channels. Momentum correlations between decay fragments were analyzed to distinguish prompt from sequential decay pathways, and the experimental results were compared to ab initio SA-NCSM calculations using the NNLO_opt chiral potential with model spaces up to N_max = 12 and extrapolation to infinite model space.

## Key numbers

- The 0+2 isobaric analog state in 8B has a real excitation energy of 10.619 MeV (from ENSDF), observed as a narrow peak at E*_n-gamma approximately 7.1 MeV in the 2p+6Li channel due to the accompanying 3.562-MeV gamma ray from 6Li decay.
- A new state in the 2p+6Li channel was observed at E* = 8.38(6) MeV with width Gamma = 0.94(10) MeV from the 9C dataset, and at E* = 8.41(5) MeV with Gamma = 0.82(10) MeV from the 13O dataset.
- A new state in the p+7Be+gamma channel was observed at E* = 5.095(20) MeV with width Gamma = 1.15(7) MeV, corresponding to a centroid of E*_n-gamma = 4.666(20) MeV for decay to the 7Be(1/2-) first excited state.
- The 8.2-MeV state in the p+3He+alpha channel has a fitted branching ratio of 73(7)% for sequential decay through 5Li(g.s.) and 27(7)% through 7Be(5/2-1).
- The 2p branching ratio of the 8.4-MeV state is at least 30% (or at least 23% if the p+3He+alpha branch is included).

## Context

This work extends the level scheme of 8B for excitation energies below 10 MeV using invariant-mass spectroscopy, building on prior studies of 8B resonances by Charity (2011), Brown (2014, 2017), and others. The experimental results are compared to ab initio SA-NCSM calculations using the NNLO_opt chiral potential, which are also benchmarked against Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations with the AV18+IL7 interaction by Wiringa (1995) and Pieper (2008). The study enables future investigations of isospin breaking effects in the mirror pair 8B and 8Li, and the newly observed 2p-emitting state is the focus of a companion paper comparing it to other known A=8 2p emitters.
