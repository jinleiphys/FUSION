# Spectrum of hadrons with strangeness

> We describe a calculation of the spectrum of strange and nonstrange hadrons that simultaneously correlates the dressed-quark-core masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states within a single framework. The foundation for this analysis is a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction. Our results exemplify and highlight the deep impact of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the hadron spectrum: an accurate description of the meson spectrum entails a similarly successful prediction of the spectrum of baryons, including those with strangeness. The analysis also provides numerous insights into baryon structure. For example, that baryon structure is largely flavour-blind, the first radial excitation of ground-state baryons is constituted almost entirely from axial-vector diquark correlations, and DCSB is the foundation for the ordering of low-lying baryon levels; viz., (1/2)^+, (1/2)^+, (1/2)^-.

# Spectrum of hadrons with strangeness

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction can simultaneously correlate the dressed-quark-core masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states, including those with strangeness, but only after introducing a phenomenological spin-orbit repulsion factor (g_SO = 0.24) to correct for missing nonperturbative DCSB effects in scalar and axial-vector channels.

## Method
The authors employ a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations using a vector-vector contact interaction with a gluon mass-scale m_G = 0.8 GeV and coupling alpha_IR = 0.93 pi. Meson bound-states are solved via the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and baryon bound-states are described using a Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation whose key elements are dressed-quark and diquark propagators and diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The regularisation procedure uses infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs (Lambda_ir = 0.24 GeV, Lambda_uv = 0.905 GeV) that implement confinement and set the scale of all dimensioned quantities.

## Key numbers

- Dressed quark masses: M_u = 0.37 GeV, M_s = 0.53 GeV, with M_s/M_u = 1.43, obtained with current-quark masses m_u = 0.007 GeV and m_s = 0.17 GeV (ratio m_s/m_u = 24.3)
- Chiral-limit dressed-quark mass: M_0 = 0.36 GeV
- Ground-state meson masses (DSE vs. experiment): m_pi = 0.14 GeV (both); m_K = 0.50 GeV (both); m_rho = 0.93 GeV (expt. 0.78 GeV); m_K* = 1.03 GeV (expt. 0.89 GeV); m_phi = 1.13 GeV (expt. 1.02 GeV); m_sigma = 1.29 GeV (expt. 1.0-1.2 GeV); m_a1 = 1.38 GeV (expt. 1.23 GeV)
- First radial excitation meson masses: m_pi(1) = 1.33 +/- 0.06 GeV; m_K(1) = 1.33 +/- 0.07 GeV; m_rho(1) = 1.29 +/- 0.05 GeV; m_K*(1) = 1.40 +/- 0.05 GeV; m_phi(1) = 1.51 +/- 0.05 GeV
- Spin-orbit repulsion factor: g_SO = 0.24, chosen to reproduce the experimental a1-rho mass splitting
- In-hadron condensates: kappa_0^(1/3) = 0.241 GeV (chiral limit), kappa_pi^(1/3) = 0.243 GeV, kappa_K^(1/3) = 0.246 GeV

## Context
This work extends the unified DSE description of light-quark meson and baryon spectra from Roberts (2011) to include strange hadrons, using the same contact interaction framework. The computed dressed-quark-core masses are systematically larger than empirical values, consistent with the expectation that bound-state kernels omitting type-2 meson-cloud corrections (such as those computed by Pichowsky (1999) showing a 0.13 GeV reduction for the rho meson) should overestimate ground-state masses. The results provide a necessary step toward comprehensive analyses of form factors involving hadrons with strangeness and offer a bridge between QCD and dynamical coupled-channels reaction models.
