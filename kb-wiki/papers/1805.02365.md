# Systematics of the HAL QCD Potential at Low Energies in Lattice QCD

> The $ΞΞ$ interaction in the $^1$S$_0$ channel is studied to examine the convergence of the derivative expansion of the non-local HAL QCD potential at the next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). We find that (i) the leading order potential from the N$^2$LO analysis gives the scattering phase shifts accurately at low energies, (ii) the full N$^2$LO potential gives only small correction to the phase shifts even at higher energies below the inelastic threshold, and (iii) the potential determined from the wall quark source at the leading order analysis agrees with the one at the N$^2$LO analysis except at short distances, and thus, it gives correct phase shifts at low energies. We also study the possible systematic uncertainties in the HAL QCD potential such as the inelastic state contaminations and the finite volume artifact for the potential and find that they are well under control for this particular system.

## Key claim

The derivative expansion of the non-local HAL QCD potential converges well for the ΞΞ interaction in the ¹S₀ channel, with the leading-order potential from the wall source giving accurate scattering phase shifts at low energies and the next-to-next-to-leading-order correction remaining small even at higher energies below the inelastic threshold.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations at a lattice spacing of a = 0.08995(40) fm on 40³×48, 48³×48, and 64³×64 volumes, using both wall and smeared quark sources to extract the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. The time-dependent HAL QCD method was employed to derive the energy-independent non-local potential for the ΞΞ system in the ¹S₀ channel, with the derivative expansion truncated at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) to examine convergence. The potentials were extracted from the R-correlator, defined as the ratio of the two-baryon correlation function to the square of the single-baryon correlation function, and the scattering phase shifts were calculated by solving the Schrödinger-type equation with fitted potential forms.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 0.51 GeV, kaon mass mK = 0.62 GeV, nucleon mass mN = 1.32 GeV, and Ξ mass mΞ = 1.46 GeV at lattice spacing a = 0.08995(40) fm
- The inverse scattering length (a0 mπ)⁻¹ from the LO wall-source potential at t=13 is 0.341(36)(⁺⁷⁰₋₀), from the N²LO central potential is 0.368(39)(⁺⁶⁵₋₀), and from the full N²LO potential including the V₂ term is 0.352(36)(⁺⁸⁰₋₀)
- The N²LO correction to the phase shift becomes non-negligible only at high energies where (k/mπ)² = 0.5, corresponding to ΔE ≈ 90 MeV from threshold
- The χ²/dof values for the fitted potentials at t=13 are 1.14 for V₀^LO(wall), 1.01 for V₀^N²LO, and 0.0019 for V₂^N²LO

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method studies of baryon-baryon interactions by systematically examining the convergence of the derivative expansion, which had previously been studied for NN systems in quenched QCD by Murano (2011) and for I=2 ππ systems by Kawai (2017). The paper addresses the discrepancy between the direct method and the HAL QCD method for two-nucleon systems, as discussed in a series of papers by Iritani (2016, 2017, 2018) and Aoki (2017), where the direct method showed bound states for heavy pion masses while the HAL QCD method did not. The results enable reliable extraction of baryon-baryon interactions from lattice QCD using the time-dependent HAL QCD method with controlled systematic uncertainties, and demonstrate that the wall source at leading order is sufficient for low-energy physics in this channel.
