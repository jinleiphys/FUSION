# Flow in heavy-ion collisions - Theory Perspective

> I review recent developments in the field of relativistic hydrodynamics and its application to the bulk dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy- Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In particular, I report on progress in going beyond second order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for conformal fluids, including temperature dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratios, as well as coupling hydrodynamic calculations to microscopic hadronic rescattering models. I describe event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations and their ability to compute higher harmonic flow coefficients. Combined comparisons of all harmonics to recent experimental data from both RHIC and LHC will potentially allow to determine the desired details of the initial state and the medium properties of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that systematic comparison of all harmonic flow coefficients (v_n up to n=6) from event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations with experimental data from both RHIC and LHC can potentially determine the details of the initial state and the medium properties of the quark-gluon plasma, but the largest uncertainty remains the limited knowledge of the initial conditions.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics applied to heavy-ion collisions, starting from the conservation equations for energy-momentum and baryon current, and progressing from ideal hydrodynamics through first-order Navier-Stokes to second-order Israel-Stewart formalism for conformal fluids. It describes event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations that compute higher harmonic flow coefficients, and discusses coupling hydrodynamic calculations to microscopic hadronic rescattering models such as UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.05 yields negligible numerical differences between second and third order gradient expansions; differences become significant for η/s ≥ 0.2
- The new equations of motion from Denicol et al. (2010) show agreement with microscopic transport calculations up to η/s ~ 3, while Israel-Stewart equations show large differences for η/s ≥ 0.2
- In hybrid models, the hadronic η/s rises to approximately 0.4 when emulating the dissipation of UrQMD in the hadronic stage
- The viscous correction to distribution functions has χ(p) ∝ p^α where α can range from 1 to 2, with radiative and elastic energy loss giving χ(p) ∝ p^1.38
- The switching temperature between hydrodynamics and hadronic afterburner is approximately 160 MeV
- Flow harmonics are studied up to n=6 in dihadron correlation analyses

## Context

This review extends the work of Baier et al. (2007) on second-order viscous hydrodynamics for conformal fluids and the Israel-Stewart formalism (Israel 1976, Stewart 1977). It builds on earlier 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Romatschke (2007), Luzum (2008), Heinz (2005), and Song (2007), and more recent 3+1 dimensional calculations by Schenke (2010, 2011). The paper enables systematic extraction of temperature-dependent η/s(T) and initial state properties through combined analysis of all harmonic flow coefficients, while identifying open questions about pre-equilibrium dynamics, viscous corrections to distribution functions, and the transition to hydrodynamics.
