# A schematic model for fragmentation and phase transition in nuclear collisions

> We develop here a simple yet versatile model for nuclear fragmentation in heavy ion collisions. The model allows us to calculate thermodynamic properties such as phase transitions as well as the distribution of fragments at disassembly. In spite of its simplicity the model gives very good fit to recent data taken at the Michigan National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The model is an extension of a lattice gas model which itself has strong overlaps with percolation models which have been used in the past to compare with nuclear fragmentation data.

# A schematic model for fragmentation and phase transition in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The authors develop a lattice-gas-based model that can simultaneously describe nuclear fragment distributions and thermodynamic phase transitions, and they claim that at the critical density (ρ_c = 0.5 ρ_0) the percolation point coincides with the thermal critical point, though they note it is "not clear if there is any a priori reason for this remarkable result to happen."

## Method

The model places nucleons on a simple cubic lattice with nearest-neighbor attractive interactions of strength -ε, using Monte Carlo sampling to populate lattice sites with a Boltzmann-weighted probability. After spatial configuration is set, momenta are assigned from a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, and two neighboring nucleons are considered bonded only if their relative kinetic energy is less than the binding energy (p_r²/2μ - ε < 0). The model has a single free parameter, the freeze-out density, and the authors compare calculated fragment mass distributions (characterized by the power-law exponent τ) against experimental data from the Michigan National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory.

## Key numbers

- Critical density: ρ_c = 0.5 ρ_0, where ρ_0 is normal nuclear density (ρ_0 = 1/6.25 fm⁻³)
- Critical temperature: kT_c = γε/4 in Bragg-Williams approximation; improved calculation gives T_c = 1.1275ε
- Lattice spacing: a³ = 1/ρ_0 = 6.25 fm³
- For n=85 nucleons (simulating ⁴⁰Ar+⁴⁵Sc collisions), simulations used lattice sizes N = 5³, 6³, and 7³, corresponding to densities ρ = 0.68 ρ_0, 0.39 ρ_0, and 0.25 ρ_0
- At ρ = ρ_0, the percolation point occurs at temperature 1.55 T_c
- Best fit to experimental data was obtained at freeze-out density ρ/ρ_0 = 0.39

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice gas model (Pan and Das Gupta, 1994) and is closely related to percolation models used for nuclear fragmentation by Bauer (1988) and Campi (1988). The model addresses the same experimental data from Li et al. (1993, 1994) and Ogilvie et al. (1991) that showed a minimum in the power-law exponent τ as a function of beam energy, which had been interpreted as evidence for a liquid-gas phase transition. The model enables simultaneous study of thermal critical points and percolation points within a single framework, and the authors suggest future work should measure critical exponents to more firmly establish the phase transition interpretation.
