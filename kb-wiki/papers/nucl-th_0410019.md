# A microscopic calculation of neutrino neutral reaction on 4He

## Key claim
This ab-initio calculation of the inelastic neutral reaction of neutrino on 4He, using realistic nuclear force and full final state interaction, predicts an increased reaction cross-section by 10%–30% for neutrino temperatures up to 15 MeV compared to previous calculations. The calculation is performed in the impulse approximation, which neglects two-body axial currents expected to give small contributions, and the authors estimate a resulting error of about 10%.

## Method
The calculation combines the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with the hyperspherical-harmonic effective interaction approach (EIHH) to solve the final state interaction problem without continuum wave functions. The neutrino-nucleus interaction is treated in the impulse approximation using one-body weak currents, and the response functions for Coulomb, longitudinal, transverse electric, and transverse magnetic multipole operators are computed. The differential cross-section is obtained via Fermi's golden rule, and the results are averaged over neutrino flavor and temperature.

## Key numbers
- Flavor- and temperature-averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T = 10 MeV: 7.85 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (this work) versus 6.78 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Woosley et al. (1990).
- Flavor- and temperature-averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T = 12 MeV: 2.05 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (this work) versus 1.63 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Woosley et al. (1990).
- Energy transfer cross-section at T = 10 MeV: 2.30 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV (this work).
- Energy transfer cross-section at T = 16 MeV: 2.84 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV (this work).
- Fit parameters for energy transfer cross-section: α = 0.64 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV, T₀ = 2.05 MeV, β = 4.46 (this work); compared to α = 0.62 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV, T₀ = 2.54 MeV, β = 3.82 from Haxton (1988).
- Numerical accuracy of the calculation: about 1%; estimated total error: about 10%, mainly due to neglected many-body currents.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Woosley et al. (1990) and Haxton (1988) by providing the first realistic microscopic calculation of the neutrino-4He inelastic neutral reaction cross-section, including full final state interaction among the four nucleons. The results show a 10%–30% enhancement in cross-section and a steeper temperature dependence compared to these prior estimates, which may help resolve the problem of shock revival in core-collapse supernova simulations, where hydrodynamic simulations currently fail (Liebendörfer 2001, Buras 2003). The calculation enables more accurate modeling of neutrino-matter coupling in supernova environments, particularly for heavy-flavor neutrinos (muon and tau) which decouple at higher temperatures (∼10 MeV) and can dissociate 4He through neutral reactions.
