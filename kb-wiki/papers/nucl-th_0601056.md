# Microscopic theory of the two-proton radioactivity

> We formulate theory of the two-proton radioactivity based on the real-energy continuum shell model. This microscopic approach is applied to describe the two-proton decay of the $1^-_2$ state in $^{18}$Ne.

# Microscopic theory of the two-proton radioactivity

## Key claim

The authors extend the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) to include couplings to two-nucleon decay channels, enabling a microscopic description of two-proton radioactivity; however, the direct di-proton decay width is found to be small (1–2 eV) compared to the sequential decay width (13–89 eV), and the experimental data are compatible with sequential 2p-decay through the correlated scattering continuum of ¹⁷F with possibly only a weak di-proton branch.

## Method

The Hilbert space is divided into three subspaces: Q (A nucleons in quasi-bound single-particle orbits), P (one nucleon in the non-resonant continuum), and T (two nucleons in the non-resonant continuum). The effective Hamiltonian in Q is constructed by coupling to P and T subspaces via Green's functions, and two limits are considered: indirect (sequential) 2p-emission and direct di-proton emission. For the direct case, the 2p-decay is approximated by emission of a (2p)-cluster with final-state interaction between the two protons described by the s-wave phase shift, and the decay width is computed from the imaginary part of the matrix element involving the source term and its continuation in the T subspace.

## Key numbers

- The experimental width for 2p-emission from the 1⁻₂ state in ¹⁸Ne is 21±3 eV assuming a di-proton model, and 57±6 eV assuming a sequential decay model.
- The calculated sequential 2p-emission width Γ⁽ˢᵉᵠ⁾ with coupling to 1p-decay channels included is 88.8 eV for the psdfp effective interaction and 13.6 eV for the WBT interaction.
- The calculated direct 2p-emission width Γ⁽ᵈⁱʳ⁾ is 1.89 eV for psdfp and 1.01 eV for WBT interactions.
- The 1p-decay width of the 1⁻₂ state saturates at large coupling strength V₀; the adopted value is V₀ = 900 MeV·fm³.
- The branching ratio B₁/₂⁺ for 2p-decay through the {1/2⁺}-continuum in ¹⁷F is 93% for psdfp and 80% for WBT interactions when 1p-decay coupling is included.

## Context

This work extends the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) formalism, previously limited to one-particle continuum couplings (Smirnov and collaborators, 2000; Okolowicz and collaborators, 2003), to include two-particle decay channels for the first time. The approach is applied to the 1⁻₂ state in ¹⁸Ne, where experimental data from the Oak Ridge National Laboratory group (2005) had suggested both di-proton and sequential decay branches. Compared to earlier Shell Model plus R-matrix calculations (Barker, 2001; Barker, 2003), the SMEC approach yields systematically larger sequential two-proton decay widths and smaller direct di-proton widths, primarily due to the inclusion of external mixing between the shell model states and the continuum, which is absent in the R-matrix treatment. The strong coupling between 1p- and 2p-emission sectors found here invalidates a simple picture of direct 2p-decay as an independent decay mode when 1p-decay channels are open.
