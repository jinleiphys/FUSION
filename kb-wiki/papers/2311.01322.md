# Lattice QCD Constraints on the Fourth Mellin Moment of the Pion Light Cone Distribution Amplitude using the HOPE method

> The light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) of the pion contains information about the parton momentum carried by the quarks and is an important theoretical input for various predictions of exclusive processes at high energy, including the pion electromagnetic form factor. Progress towards constraining the fourth Mellin moment of the LCDA using the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method is presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents progress toward the first continuum-limit determination of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude using the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method, but the quoted result is preliminary with large statistical uncertainty and is obtained in the quenched approximation at an unphysically heavy pion mass of 550 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute two-point and three-point correlation functions in lattice QCD using quenched gauge field ensembles at three lattice spacings (0.0813 fm, 0.0600 fm, 0.0502 fm) with a fixed physical volume of 1.92 fm and pion mass of approximately 0.55 GeV. Momentum smearing and the variational method with a two-operator set are employed to construct an optimized interpolating operator for the pion ground state. The hadronic matrix element entering the HOPE is extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point correlators, and the Mellin moments are obtained by fitting the Fourier-transformed HOPE expression truncated to the second and fourth moments, followed by a combined continuum and twist-two extrapolation using a parameterization that includes lattice spacing and heavy quark mass dependence.

## Key numbers

* Second Mellin moment at the renormalization scale mu^2 = 4 GeV^2: <xi^2> = 0.245 +/- 0.014 (statistical error only, quenched approximation, m_pi = 550 MeV)
* Fourth Mellin moment at the renormalization scale mu^2 = 4 GeV^2: <xi^4> = 0.075 +/- 0.070 (statistical error only, quenched approximation, m_pi = 550 MeV)
* Lattice spacings used: a = 0.0813 fm (24^3 x 48), a = 0.0600 fm (32^3 x 64), a = 0.0502 fm (40^3 x 80)
* Heavy quark masses in the MSbar scheme range from 1.2 GeV to 3.4 GeV across ensembles
* Pion mass: m_pi ~ 0.55 GeV; physical volume: L = 1.92 fm; exp(-m_pi L) ~ 0.5% for finite volume effects

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method previously applied to the second Mellin moment of the pion LCDA by Detmold et al. (2021) to the fourth Mellin moment, about which little is known. The only existing lattice QCD determination of <xi^4> = 0.124(11)(20) at mu = 2 GeV was reported by Gao et al. (2022) at a single lattice spacing of a = 0.076 fm. The present analysis provides the first continuum limit determination of the fourth Mellin moment from lattice QCD, though with large statistical uncertainty; data taking is ongoing with the aim of reducing errors and including a fourth lattice spacing closer to the continuum limit.
