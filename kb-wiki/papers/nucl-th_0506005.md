# Renormalization of One-Pion Exchange and Power Counting

> The renormalization of the chiral nuclear interactions is studied. In leading order, the cutoff dependence is related to the singular tensor interaction of the one-pion exchange potential. In S waves and in higher partial waves where the tensor force is repulsive this cutoff dependence can be absorbed by counterterms expected at that order. In the other partial waves additional contact interactions are necessary. The implications of this finding for the effective-field-theory program in nuclear physics are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Weinberg's power counting for chiral nuclear interactions is inconsistent in leading order for spin-triplet partial waves where the tensor force from one-pion exchange is attractive, because the singular $1/r^3$ behavior generates cutoff-dependent spurious bound states that cannot be absorbed by the contact interactions expected at that order; additional counterterms beyond those predicted by naive dimensional analysis are required in these channels.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering using a momentum-space regulator with cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 2 fm$^{-1}$ and 20 fm$^{-1}$, employing the leading-order chiral potential consisting of one-pion exchange and two S-wave contact interactions. They extracted phase shifts and mixing angles for partial waves up to total angular momentum $j \le 4$, and extended the analysis to the three-nucleon bound state by solving Faddeev equations for the triton.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff range studied: $\Lambda$ between 2 fm$^{-1}$ and 20 fm$^{-1}$
- One-pion exchange parameters: $g_A = 1.26$, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $m_\pi$ the pion mass
- $^3$D$_2$ phase shift at 50 MeV in the plateau region: $\simeq 9^\circ$, compared to Nijmegen PWA value of $8.97^\circ$
- Deuteron binding energy predicted in leading order: $1.92$ MeV
- Counterterms fitted at: $^3$P$_0$ phase shift at 50 MeV, $^3$P$_2$ phase shift at 50 MeV, $^3$D$_2$ phase shift at 100 MeV
- Spurious bound states appear in $^3$P$_0$ and $^3$D$_2$ channels for $\Lambda$ between 2 and 20 fm$^{-1}$, and in $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ just above $\Lambda = 20$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends earlier non-perturbative renormalization studies of one-pion exchange in S-waves by Frederico (1999), Beane (2002), and Valderrama (2004, 2005) to higher partial waves, and directly addresses the criticism by Kaplan (1996, 1998) regarding ultraviolet divergences in the two-nucleon system. The authors show that Weinberg's power counting (1990, 1991) requires modification: attractive triplet channels need additional contact interactions at leading order, not just at next-to-leading order as previously assumed. This finding enables a consistent renormalization framework for chiral nuclear forces that can be extended to three-nucleon systems and beyond, with the triton binding energy shown to be cutoff-independent without a leading-order three-nucleon force.
