# Neutrino cross sections and nuclear structure

> The effects of the theoretical uncertainties in the description of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova neutrino energies are investigated.

## Key claim
The theoretical uncertainty in neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova neutrino energies is too large for reliable astrophysical predictions in the giant resonance region, but the use of ratios of observables reduces the effects of nuclear structure uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using four different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions that reproduce excitation energies and electromagnetic properties of double magic nuclei with similar accuracy. They calculated neutrino-nucleus cross sections for discrete states in \(^{12}\)C, focusing on the isovector 1\(^{+}\) triplet, and rescaled the neutrino cross sections using momentum-dependent quenching functions derived from experimental electron scattering form factors below \(q=1\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers
- Average supernova neutrino energies are about 15–30 MeV, with electron neutrino and antineutrino fluences having lower temperatures than \(\mu\) and \(\tau\) neutrinos.
- The total energy transported by neutrinos used in the estimate is \(E_B \sim 5.0 \times 10^{52}\) erg = \(8.01 \times 10^{60}\) MeV, with distance from Earth D = 10 kpc and energy fraction per neutrino type \(f_i = 1/6\).
- The neutral current event ratio \(R = N^{T}_{NC} / N_{NC}\) was calculated for characteristic temperatures of electron neutrinos (4 MeV) and electron antineutrinos (5 MeV), with \(\mu\) and \(\tau\) neutrinos and antineutrinos all having the same temperature \(T\).

## Context
This work extends studies of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova detection, specifically addressing uncertainties from nuclear structure models. The authors compare their RPA calculations with electron scattering data from Hicks (1984) and with cross sections used by Fukugita (1988). The results enable the use of observable ratios to disentangle the temperatures of different neutrino flavors, reducing model dependence in future supernova neutrino detection experiments such as the Large Volume Detector at Gran Sasso.
