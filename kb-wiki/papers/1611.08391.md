# Extracting kinetic freeze-out temperature and radial flow velocity from an improved Tsallis distribution

> We analyze the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of identified particles ($π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar p$) produced in gold-gold (Au-Au) and lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions over a $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair) range from 14.5 GeV [one of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies] to 2.76 TeV [one of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies]. For the spectra with a narrow $p_T$ range, an improved Tsallis distribution which is in fact the Tsallis distribution with radial flow is used. For the spectra with a wide $p_T$ range, a superposition of the improved Tsallis distribution and an inverse power-law is used. Both the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_0$) and radial flow velocity ($β_T$) increase with the increase of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, which indicates a higher excitation and larger expansion of the interesting system at the LHC. Both the values of $T_0$ and $β_T$ in central collisions are slightly larger than those in peripheral collisions, and they are independent of isospin and slightly dependent on mass.

# Extracting kinetic freeze-out temperature and radial flow velocity from an improved Tsallis distribution

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature (T0) and radial flow velocity (βT) extracted from an improved Tsallis distribution increase with collision energy from RHIC to LHC, but this trend is inconsistent with the original blast-wave model which gives lower T0 at higher energy; the authors note that the higher T0 at LHC energies can be explained by a higher excitation degree rather than a longer QGP lifetime.

## Method

The authors fit transverse momentum (pT) spectra of π±, K±, p, and p̄ from Au-Au collisions at RHIC (√sNN = 14.5, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) using an improved Tsallis distribution that incorporates radial flow via a first-order Taylor expansion in (q-1). For spectra covering a wide pT range, they use a superposition of the improved Tsallis distribution (soft component) and an inverse power-law (hard component) from QCD calculus, with a free parameter k controlling the relative contribution of each component.

## Key numbers

- T0 values for central (0-5%) Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 14.5 GeV: π+ = 0.102 ± 0.004 GeV, K+ = 0.109 ± 0.011 GeV, p = 0.118 ± 0.009 GeV
- T0 values for central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: π± = 0.161 ± 0.009 GeV, K± = 0.162 ± 0.009 GeV, p+p̄ = 0.192 ± 0.012 GeV
- βT values for central (0-5%) Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 14.5 GeV: π+ = 0.602 ± 0.023 c, K+ = 0.574 ± 0.032 c, p = 0.538 ± 0.023 c
- βT values for central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: π± = 0.612 ± 0.034 c, K± = 0.612 ± 0.033 c, p+p̄ = 0.612 ± 0.023 c
- Entropy index q values for central Au-Au at 14.5 GeV: π+ = 1.011 ± 0.005, K+ = 1.002 ± 0.001, p = 1.003 ± 0.002
- T0 dependence on √sNN fitted as T0 = a(√sNN)^b + c, with exponent b ≈ 0.45-0.52 across particle species and centralities

## Context

This work extends the improved Tsallis distribution approach of Sahoo and colleagues (2013, 2014) to a systematic analysis across multiple RHIC and LHC energies, comparing results with the traditional blast-wave model of Schnedermann, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1993) and the improved blast-wave model using Tsallis-distributed sources. The extracted T0 values increase with energy, contradicting the original blast-wave model which yields lower T0 at higher energies, but the authors note this is consistent with the improved blast-wave model. The results enable further study of mass-dependent differential kinetic freeze-out scenarios and suggest that non-zero radial flow exists even in peripheral collisions, contrary to the original blast-wave model assumption of zero flow in peripheral events.
