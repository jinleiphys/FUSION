# Neural Network Solutions of Bosonic Quantum Systems in One Dimension

> Neural networks have been proposed as efficient numerical wavefunction ansatze which can be used to variationally search a wide range of functional forms for ground state solutions. These neural network methods are also advantageous in that more variational parameters and system degrees of freedom can be easily added. We benchmark the methodology by using neural networks to study several different integrable bosonic quantum systems in one dimension and compare our results to the exact solutions. While testing the scalability of the procedure to systems with many particles, we also introduce using symmetric function inputs to the neural network to enforce exchange symmetries of indistinguishable particles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neural networks using symmetric power-sum inputs can accurately reproduce ground state energies and wavefunctions for several integrable bosonic quantum systems in one dimension, but the method's accuracy is explicitly benchmarked only against exactly-solvable models and the authors note that the optimal number of neural network layers and nodes remains an unsolved systematic question.

## Method

The authors construct a variational wavefunction ansatz as a neural network acting on normalized power-sum symmetric functions of the boson coordinates, multiplied by a Gaussian factor to enforce proper boundary conditions. They minimize the energy expectation value using the Adam optimizer with gradients estimated via Metropolis Monte Carlo importance sampling, and systematically increase the number of neural network parameters until the ground state energy converges within Monte Carlo error bars.

## Key numbers

- For two bosons in a harmonic trap with contact interactions, neural network ground state energies agree with the exact Busch et al. solution to approximately one to two percent precision across a wide range of coupling strengths g, using roughly 5×10⁴ variational parameters in a four-layer network.
- For fifty bosons in the exactly-solvable regime (σ = -g/2) of the long-range interaction model, the neural network computed E₀^NN = 12.083(72) for g = 0.05 compared to exact E₀ = 11.984375, and E₀^NN = 20.367(76) for g = -0.03 compared to exact E₀ = 20.314375, using β ≈ 2×10⁵ parameters.
- The neural network calculations with N = 50 and β ≈ 2×10⁵ took roughly 2 minutes per training step CPU run-time using 8×10⁵ Monte Carlo steps per training step on a 32-core workstation.

## Context

This work extends the neural network variational approach of Carleo and Troyer (2017) from spin systems to continuous-space bosonic systems, and builds on the symmetric-input technique used by Saito (2018) for the Calogero-Sutherland model. The method enables systematic study of both integrable systems (such as the Beau et al. (2020) exactly-solvable model with short and long-range interactions) and non-integrable regimes where no analytic solution exists, and demonstrates scalability to systems with dozens of particles.
