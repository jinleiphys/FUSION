# Decoding the riddle of Y(4260) and $Z_c(3900)$

> The observation of $Z_c(3900)$ by the BESIII collaboration in the invariant mass spectrum of $J/ψπ^\pm$ in $e^+e^-\to J/ψπ^+π^-$ at the center of mass 4.260 GeV suggests the existence of a charged $\bar D D^*+D\bar D^*$ molecular state with $I(J^P)=1(1^+)$, which could be an isovector brother of the famous X(3872) and an analogue of $Z_b(10610)$ claimed by the Belle Collaboration. We demonstrate that this observation provides strong evidence that the mysterious Y(4260) is a $\bar{D}D_1(2420) +D\bar{D}_1(2420)$ molecular state. Especially, we show that the decay of this molecule naturally populates low momentum $\bar DD^*$ pairs and leads unavoidably to a cusp at the $\bar DD^*$ threshold. We discuss the signatures that distinguish such a $\bar DD^*$ cusp from the presence of a true resonance.

## Key claim

The observation of the charged $Z_c(3900)$ enhancement near the $\bar D D^*$ threshold in $Y(4260)$ decays provides strong evidence that $Y(4260)$ is a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+D\bar{D}_1(2420)$ molecular state, but the data may require an explicit $Z_c(3900)$ pole in addition to the unavoidable cusp from the $\bar D D^*$ threshold.

## Method

The authors assume $Y(4260)$ is dominantly a bound $\bar{D}D_1(2420)$ $S$-wave system and calculate the decay $Y(4260)\to J/\psi\pi\pi$ and $h_c\pi\pi$ via triangle and box diagrams with intermediate $\bar{D}D_1$ and $\bar D D^*$ states. They include $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions using a parametrization with coupled-channel unitarity and the $K\bar{K}$ threshold, and treat the $Z_c(3900)$ with a Breit-Wigner propagator. The $YDD_1$ coupling is estimated via Weinberg's compositeness theorem.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{D}D_1(2420)$ threshold lies about 29 MeV above the $Y(4260)$ mass of 4.260 GeV.
- The $D_1(2420)$ width is $\Gamma=27\pm 3$ MeV.
- The $YDD_1$ coupling squared is estimated as $y^2/4\pi \simeq 17$ GeV$^2$, predicting a partial width for $Y(4260)\to \bar{D}D^*\pi$ larger than 40 MeV.
- The $\bar D D^*$ threshold is at $M_D+M_{D^*}\simeq 3.876$ GeV.
- The $Z_c(3900)DD^*$ coupling is set to $g_Z=1$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, giving a $Z_c\to DD^*$ branching ratio of about 20\%.
- The $\pi\pi$ $S$-wave FSI parameters are $c_1=0.23$ GeV$^2$, $c_2=-1.07$, $c_3=1.15$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $X(3872)$ and the $Z_b(10610)$ states observed by Belle to the charmonium sector, proposing that $Y(4260)$ is a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)$ molecule analogous to the $\bar{B}B_1$ component possibly present in $\Upsilon(5S)$. It contradicts the conclusion of Dai et al. (2012) that the $\chi_{c0}\omega$ channel dominates $Y(4260)$ decays. The mechanism predicts a cusp at the $\bar D D^*$ threshold that can be distinguished from a true resonance by measuring $Y(4260)\to h_c\pi\pi$, and suggests that $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays may produce $Z_b$ states through a similar two-cut mechanism.
