# Two-loop study of the deconfinement transition in Yang-Mills theories: SU(3) and beyond

> We study the confinement-deconfinement phase transition of pure Yang-Mills theories at finite temperature using a simple massive extension of standard background field methods. We generalize our recent next-to-leading-order perturbative calculation of the Polyakov loop and of the related background field effective potential for the SU(2) theory to any compact and connex Lie group with a simple Lie algebra. We discuss in detail the SU(3) theory, where the two-loop corrections yield improved values for the first-order transition temperature as compared to the one-loop result. We also show that certain one-loop artifacts of thermodynamical observables disappear at two-loop order, as was already the case for the SU(2) theory. In particular, the entropy and the pressure are positive for all temperatures. Finally, we discuss the groups SU(4) and Sp(2) which shed interesting light, respectively, on the relation between the (de)confinement of static matter sources in the various representations of the gauge group and on the use of the background field itself as an order parameter for confinement. In both cases, we obtain first-order transitions, in agreement with lattice simulations and other continuum approaches.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative calculation of the Polyakov loop and background field effective potential from SU(2) to any compact and connex Lie group with a simple Lie algebra, finding that two-loop corrections improve the first-order transition temperature for SU(3) compared to the one-loop result, but the approach still relies on a massive extension of the Faddeev-Popov action whose justification from first principles (Gribov ambiguities, BRST breaking) remains an open issue requiring further investigation.

## Method

The authors use a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge, where a gluon mass term is added to the Faddeev-Popov action, motivated by Gribov ambiguities and lattice observations of massive gluon behavior at low momentum. They compute the background field effective potential and Polyakov loop at two-loop (NLO) order for SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature, using canonical (Cartan-Weyl) bases to diagonalize the action in the presence of a homogeneous temporal background field. For SU(4) and Sp(2), the analysis is performed only at leading order (one-loop).

## Key numbers

* For SU(3), the two-loop (NLO) corrections yield improved values for the first-order transition temperature compared to the one-loop result (the paper does not quote a specific numerical temperature value in the provided text).
* The entropy and pressure become positive for all temperatures at two-loop order, unlike at one-loop where unphysical negative values appear near the transition.
* For both SU(4) and Sp(2), the authors obtain first-order phase transitions at leading order, in agreement with lattice simulations and other continuum approaches.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculation for SU(2) (Reinosa et al., 2014) to SU(3) and other gauge groups, building on the massive extension approach developed by Tissier and Wschebor (2010, 2011) that successfully describes lattice gluon propagators. The results are compared to lattice simulations of pure Yang-Mills theories (Engels, 1981; Boyd, 1995; Bazavov, 2014; Borsanyi, 2013) and to other continuum approaches such as the functional renormalization group (Braun, 2007; Fister, 2013) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Fischer, 2009). The work enables future studies of the deconfinement transition in QCD with dynamical quarks and clarifies the role of center symmetry and Polyakov loops in various representations for groups beyond SU(3).
