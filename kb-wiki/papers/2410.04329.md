# Azimuthal Anisotropy Scaling for Identified Mesons and Baryons: Insights into Medium Transport Properties, Equation of State and Hadronic Re-scattering

> Scaling functions for the centrality and transverse momentum dependence of \(v_2(p_T,\text{cent})\) and \(v_3(p_T,\text{cent})\) are constructed for identified mesons and baryons in Pb+Pb (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76,\ 5.02\)~TeV), Xe+Xe (5.44~TeV), and Au+Au (0.2~TeV) collisions. These species-resolved functions capture the interplay of initial geometry, viscous attenuation, radial flow, partonic energy loss, and hadronic re-scattering across both flow- and quenching-dominated regimes. The systematic growth of the radial-flow parameter \(ζ_{\rm rf}\) with multiplicity and beam energy provides direct empirical constraints on the equation of state (EOS) of hot QCD matter. The extracted parameters also yield differential constraints on the specific shear viscosity \(η/s\), the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\), and late-stage hadronic dynamics. LHC systems exhibit low \(η/s\), strong radial flow, and negligible re-scattering at high energy density, whereas Au+Au at RHIC energy shows even lower \(η/s\), weaker radial flow, finite re-scattering, and reduced energy density. The coexistence of strong radial flow at low \(p_T\) and significant jet suppression at high \(p_T\) emerges as a defining hallmark of QGP formation, establishing the framework as a quantitative probe of QGP transport properties and EOS stiffness.

## Key claim

The paper constructs species-resolved scaling functions for azimuthal anisotropy that disentangle initial geometry, viscous attenuation, radial flow, partonic energy loss, and hadronic re-scattering across both flow-dominated and quenching-dominated regimes, but the scaling collapse depends on an empirical baryon-number-dependent radial-flow parameter \(\zeta_b = (1-\zeta_{\rm rf})^{|n_B|}\) and a hadronic re-scattering parameter \(\zeta_{\rm hs}\) that are extracted from the data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The author extends a previously developed anisotropy scaling methodology (Lacey 2024) to identified mesons (\(\pi^{\pm}, K^{\pm}, K^0_S, \phi\)) and baryons (\(p, d, {^3\text{He}}, \Lambda^0, \Xi^-, \Omega^-\)) using published \(v_2(p_T,\text{cent})\) and \(v_3(p_T,\text{cent})\) data from ALICE, PHENIX, and STAR for Pb+Pb at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV, and Au+Au at 0.2 TeV. The scaling relations incorporate eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) from Monte Carlo quark-Glauber simulations, a system size scale \(\mathbb{R} \propto \langle N_{\text{chg}} \rangle^{1/3}\), a viscous correction \(\delta f = \kappa p_T^2\) with \(\kappa = 0.17~(\mathrm{GeV}/c)^{-2}\), and species-dependent parameters for radial flow (\(\zeta_{\rm rf}\)) and hadronic re-scattering (\(\zeta_{\rm hs}\)). The scaling variable is transverse kinetic energy \(\text{KE}_T = m_T - m_0\), and the reference baseline is kaon \(v_{2,3}(p_T)\) in ultra-central 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.

- \(\zeta_{\rm hs} = 0.00\) for all Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe systems; \(\zeta_{\rm hs} = 0.08\) for Au+Au at 0.2 TeV

- Quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 \approx 0.18 \pm 0.01\) for \({}^{129}\)Xe

## Context

This work extends the charged-particle anisotropy scaling framework of Lacey (2024) to identified species, recovering information about radial flow and hadronic re-scattering that is averaged over in inclusive measurements. The species-resolved functions provide differential constraints on the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\), the jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\), and the equation of state stiffness, complementing hydrodynamic models such as IP-Glasma+MUSIC (Schenke 2010, Gale 2012) that successfully describe low-\(p_T\) flow but have diminished predictive power at high \(p_T\) where partonic energy loss dominates. The framework enables simultaneous extraction of QGP transport properties and nuclear deformation parameters (e.g., Xe quadrupole deformation) without the assumptions that complicate conventional \(v_n\) analyses.
