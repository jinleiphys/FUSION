# The Effect of Bound Dineutrons upon BBN

> We have examined the effects of a bound dineutron, n2, upon big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) as a function of its binding energy B_n2. We find a weakly bound dineutron has little impact but as B_n2 increases its presence begins to alter the flow of free nucleons to helium-4. Due to this disruption, and in the absence of changes to other binding energies or fundamental constants, BBN sets a reliable upper limit of B_n2 <~ 2.5 MeV in order to maintain the agreement with the observations of the primordial helium-4 mass fraction and D/H abundance.

## Key claim

Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) sets a reliable upper limit on the dineutron binding energy of B_n2 less than about 2.5 MeV, but only in the absence of changes to other binding energies or fundamental constants; a weakly bound dineutron has little impact on BBN.

## Method

The authors modified a standard BBN reaction network to include a bound dineutron (n2) and seven new reactions involving it, with cross sections estimated from analogous known nuclear reactions by correcting for Coulomb barrier penetrability, phase space factors, and Q-values that depend on the dineutron binding energy. They calculated primordial abundances of deuterium, helium-3, and helium-4 as a function of B_n2 up to 4 MeV at a fixed baryon-to-photon ratio of eta = 6.14 x 10^-10, then compared with observed primordial abundances to derive constraints.

## Key numbers

- The dineutron is currently unbound by approximately 70 keV, with a negative neutron-neutron scattering length.
- For B_n2 less than or equal to the deuteron binding energy (B_D = 2.22 MeV), the dineutron abundance in nuclear statistical equilibrium is smaller than the deuteron abundance by roughly a factor of 1/20.
- The dineutron abundance surpasses the deuteron abundance at B_n2 approximately 2.5 MeV, based on the condition exp((B_n2 - B_D)/T) ~ 20 at T ~ 100 keV.
- BBN sets an upper limit of B_n2 less than about 2.5 MeV to maintain agreement with observed primordial helium-4 mass fraction and D/H abundance.
- For B_n2 = 3 MeV, tritium and helium-3 depart from nuclear statistical equilibrium at T ~ 170 keV rather than T ~ 200 keV in standard BBN.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kneller and McLaughlin (2003) who studied variations of Lambda_QCD and its effects on deuteron binding energy and the neutron-proton mass difference in BBN. The paper considers the possibility that changes in fundamental constants (such as alpha, Lambda_QCD, or the Higgs vev) could stabilize the dineutron, which is currently unbound, and uses BBN as a probe to constrain such scenarios. The results enable future studies of how variations of fundamental constants might affect nuclear stability and primordial nucleosynthesis, while also providing a constraint that must be satisfied by any theory predicting a bound dineutron.
