# $η^{\prime}\toηππ$ decays in unitarized resonance chiral theory

> We study the hadronic $η^{\prime}\toηππ$ decays within the framework of $U(3)_{L}\otimes U(3)_{R}$ Chiral Perturbation Theory including resonance states and the complete one-loop corrections. The amplitude is projected in partial waves and unitarized by means of the $N/D$ method resumming both the important $S$-and $D$-wave $ππ$ and the subleading $S$-wave $πη$ final-state interactions. The participating scalar multiplet mass and coupling strengths are determined from fits to the Dalitz plot experimental data recently released by the A2 collaboration. As a byproduct of our analysis, the associated Dalitz-plot slope parameters are found to be $a=-0.072(7)_{\rm{stat}}(8)_{\rm{syst}}\,, b=-0.052(1)_{\rm{stat}}(2)_{\rm{syst}}\,, d=-0.051(8)_{\rm{stat}}(6)_{\rm{syst}}$, which lie in the ballpark of the current experimental and theoretical determinations.

## Key claim

The authors show that a unitarized resonance chiral theory framework, including complete one-loop corrections and final-state interactions, can describe the $\eta^{\prime}\to\eta\pi\pi$ Dalitz plot data from the A2 collaboration, but the extracted parameters depend on which unitarization scheme is used and whether $\pi\eta$ rescattering is included.

## Method

The decay amplitude was computed within $U(3)_L\otimes U(3)_R$ Chiral Perturbation Theory including scalar resonance exchanges ($\sigma/f_0(550)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$) and complete one-loop corrections at $\mathcal{O}(\delta^2)$ in the combined chiral and large-$N_C$ expansion. The amplitude was projected into partial waves and unitarized using the $N/D$ method to resum $S$- and $D$-wave $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions and subleading $S$-wave $\pi\eta$ rescattering. The scalar multiplet mass and coupling strengths were determined from fits to the A2 collaboration Dalitz plot data for the neutral $\eta^{\prime}\to\eta\pi^0\pi^0$ channel, which contained approximately $1.23\times10^5$ events.

## Key numbers

- Dalitz slope parameters from the fit to A2 data: $a = -0.072(7)_{\rm stat}(8)_{\rm syst}$, $b = -0.052(1)_{\rm stat}(2)_{\rm syst}$, $d = -0.051(8)_{\rm stat}(6)_{\rm syst}$
- The $\eta^{\prime}\to\eta\pi\pi$ decay width represents approximately $65\%$ of the total $\eta^{\prime}$ width; PDG branching ratios are $42.6(7)\%$ for the charged channel and $22.8(8)\%$ for the neutral channel
- The A2 data set contains $\sim1.23\times10^5$ events for the neutral mode; the BESIII data set contains $351016$ events for the charged channel and $56249$ for the neutral channel
- The $\eta^{\prime}$ mass is approximately $958$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analyses of Escribano et al. (2010) and Guo et al. (2011) by including complete one-loop corrections within $U(3)$ ChPT and accounting for $\pi\pi$ and $\pi\eta$ final-state interactions via the $N/D$ unitarization method. The study is motivated by discrepancies among experimental Dalitz parameter measurements from VES (2006), GAMS-4$\pi$ (2009), BESIII (2010, 2017), and A2 (2017), as well as among theoretical predictions from Borasoy et al. (2005), Escribano et al. (2010), and the dispersive analysis of Isken et al. (2017). The results enable predictions for the charged $\eta^{\prime}\to\eta\pi^+\pi^-$ channel and extraction of information about the $I=1$ $\pi\eta$ scattering phase shift within the physical decay region.
