# The Solar Proton Burning Process Revisited In Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The proton burning process p + p -> d + e(+) + ν(e), important for the stellar evolution of main-sequence stars of mass equal to or less than that of the Sun, is computed in effective field theory using chiral perturbation expansion to the next-to-next-to leading chiral order. This represents a model-independent calculation consistent with low-energy effective theory of QCD comparable in accuracy to the radiative np capture at thermal energy previously calculated by first using very accurate two-nucleon wavefunctions backed up by an effective field theory technique with a finite cut-off. The result obtained thereby is found to support within theoretical uncertainties the previous calculation of the same process by Bahcall and his co-workers.

## Key claim

The authors compute the solar proton burning process p + p → d + e⁺ + νₑ using chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and find that their result supports the previous calculation by Bahcall and collaborators within theoretical uncertainties, contradicting the much larger rate claimed by Ivanov et al. (1997).

## Method

The authors apply chiral perturbation theory to compute the weak hadronic current for the pp reaction to O(Q³) relative to the leading-order Gamow-Teller term, using the Argonne v₁₈ potential to generate accurate two-nucleon wavefunctions for both the initial pp scattering state and the final deuteron state. The initial pp wavefunction includes the nuclear potential, pure Coulomb potential, two-photon-exchange Coulomb potential, and vacuum-polarization potential, with the latter two treated perturbatively. The two-body meson-exchange Gamow-Teller operator is evaluated at O(Q³), and the low-energy constants c₃ and c₄ are taken from pion-nucleon experiments while the contact-term constants d₁ and d₂ are argued to be negligible due to short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

* Reduced matrix element squared from chiral perturbation theory: Λ²_χPT = (1 ± 0.003) × 6.93, where the uncertainty is due to experimental errors and the theoretical uncertainty is estimated at about 0.1%
* Reduced matrix element squared from Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994): Λ²_KB = (1 ± 0.002⁺⁰·⁰¹⁴₋₀·₀₀₉) × 6.92
* The Argonne v₁₈ potential gives deuteron binding energy B_d = 2.22460 MeV, asymptotic normalization A_S = 0.88506 fm¹/², D/S ratio η_d = 0.02504, and effective range ρ_d = 1.7660 fm
* For the pp channel, the Argonne potential yields scattering length a^C = -7.8202 fm and effective range r^C = 2.782 fm, in agreement with experimental values a^C_exp = -7.8196(26) fm and r^C_exp = 2.790(14) fm
* The low-energy constants from pion-nucleon experiments: c₃ = -5.29 ± 0.25 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.63 ± 0.10 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied successfully to thermal np capture (Park, Min, and Rho, 1996; Park et al., 1997) to the weak-interaction sector of the pp reaction, providing a model-independent calculation consistent with low-energy QCD. The result supports the canonical value from Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994) and contradicts the claim by Ivanov et al. (1997) that the pp rate could be 2.9 times larger, a claim that Degl'Innocenti et al. (1997) argued would be inconsistent with helioseismology. The calculation enables a first-principles check of the solar neutrino flux predictions and demonstrates that chiral perturbation theory can achieve comparable accuracy for weak processes as for electromagnetic ones.
