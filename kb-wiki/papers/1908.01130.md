# Modeling of Charged-Neutral Kaon Fluctuations as a Signature of DCC Production in A--A Collisions

> Anomalous event-by-event fluctuations of the relative yields of neutral (K$^0_s$) and charged kaon (K$^\pm$) have been predicted to yield a signature for the formation of Disoriented Chiral Condensate (DCC) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we model the production and decay of DCCs in the context of heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, and estimate the sensitivity of large acceptance detectors, such as the ALICE detector, towards the identification of such anomalous decays. Our study is based on the robust statistical observable, $ν_{\rm dyn}$, known for its sensitivity to dynamical fluctuations. We first present simulations without DCCs, based on the HIJING and AMPT models, in order to establish an approximate reference for the magnitude of $ν_{\rm dyn}({\rm K}^{\pm},{\rm K}^{0}_{s})$ and its centrality evolution in Pb--Pb collisions at the TeV energy scale. We next introduce simple phenomenological models of K$^0_s$ vs. K$^\pm$ event-by-event yield fluctuations, which we use to study the feasibility and sensitivity of detection of the production of DCCs in heavy-ion collisions. Although the precision of models such as HIJING and AMPT limit their use as absolute references and thus render anomalous fluctuations difficult to define precisely, our studies demonstrate that the magnitude of $ν_{\rm dyn}({\rm K}^{\pm},{\rm K}^{0}_{s})$ is in fact very sensitive to the presence of small admixture of DCCs in normal non-DCC events. Consequently, while large values of $ν_{\rm dyn}({\rm K}^{\pm},{\rm K}^{0}_{s})$ may not be sufficient to identify the existence of DCCs, nonetheless they constitute a first and necessary condition to signal their possible production in heavy-ion collisions.

# Modeling of Charged-Neutral Kaon Fluctuations as a Signature of DCC Production in A--A Collisions

## Key claim

The magnitude of the statistical observable νdyn(K±,K0s) is very sensitive to the presence of even a small admixture (as low as 1%) of Disoriented Chiral Condensate (DCC) events in normal non-DCC events in Pb--Pb collisions at LHC energies; however, large values of νdyn alone may not be sufficient to definitively identify DCC production, but they constitute a first and necessary condition to signal possible DCC production.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING and AMPT event generators to establish a baseline for νdyn(K±,K0s) in Pb--Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV across five centrality classes, selecting K± and K0s in the transverse momentum range 0.2 < pT < 1.5 GeV/c and pseudorapidity range |η| < 0.5. They then developed a simple phenomenological model that mixes binomial-distributed "normal" particle production with DCC particle production governed by the probability densities P(fπ) = 1/(2√fπ) for neutral pions and P(fK) = 1 for neutral kaons, varying the DCC fraction fDCC and the probability of DCC occurrence pDCC to study the sensitivity of νdyn.

## Key numbers

- νdyn(K±,K0s) values from HIJING and AMPT models show a monotonic increase from 0-10% most central collisions to 70% most peripheral collisions in Pb--Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- 3 million HIJING events and 39, 53, and 39 million AMPT events (for three different dynamical modes) were generated for the baseline study
- The kaon selection used transverse momentum range 0.2 < pT < 1.5 GeV/c and pseudorapidity range |η| < 0.5
- Scaled values νdyn√(NK±NK0) from HIJING and AMPT/RON are essentially invariant with collision centrality, confirming approximate 1/N dilution
- In Scenario 2, sizeable νdyn magnitudes and deviations from binomial expectations occur for DCC occurrence probabilities as small as pDCC = 0.01 (1%)
- In Scenario 3, significant deviations from the HIJING baseline are found when the fraction of DCC events amounts to only one percent

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions from the SU(3) linear sigma model (Randrup, Kapusta) that DCC domains could produce kaon isospin fluctuations, building on earlier inconclusive experimental searches for DCC signals based on pion fluctuations (WA98, Minimax, STAR). The study provides a baseline for an ongoing ALICE collaboration measurement of νdyn(K±,K0s) in Pb--Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, and demonstrates that the observable is sensitive enough to detect DCC admixtures as small as 1%, enabling future experimental searches for strange DCC production at LHC energies.
