# Effect of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section on proton-proton momentum correlation in intermediate energy heavy-ion collision

> The proton-proton momentum correlation function from different rapidity regions are systematically investigated for the Au + Au collisions at different impact parameters and different energies from 400$A$ MeV to 1500$A$ MeV in the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model complemented by the $Lednick\acute{y}$ and $Lyuboshitz$ analytical method. In particular, in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section dependence of the correlation function is brought into focus, while the impact parameter and energy dependence of the momentum correlation function are also explored. The sizes of the emission source are extracted by fitting the momentum correlation functions using the Gaussian source method. We find that the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section obviously influence the proton-proton momentum correlation function which is from the whole rapidity or projectile/target rapidity region at smaller impact parameters, but there is no effect on the mid-rapidity proton-proton momentum correlation function, which indicates that the emission mechanism differs between projectile/target rapidity and mid-rapidity protons.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section significantly influences the proton-proton momentum correlation function for protons from the whole rapidity or projectile/target rapidity region at smaller impact parameters, but has no effect on the mid-rapidity proton-proton momentum correlation function, indicating different emission mechanisms between these rapidity regions.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) transport model to generate phase-space distributions at freeze-out for Au + Au collisions at beam energies from 400A MeV to 1500A MeV. These phase-space points were then processed using the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical formalism to reconstruct the proton-proton momentum correlation function, with the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section parameterized as σ_NN^med = (1 - η ρ/ρ₀) σ_NN^free where η is the in-medium reduction factor. The effective source sizes were extracted by fitting the correlation functions using a Gaussian source method, and the Blast-Wave model was used to fit proton transverse momentum spectra to extract radial flow parameters.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 400A MeV to 1500A MeV for Au + Au collisions
- Impact parameters studied: b = 3, 6, 9, and 12 fm
- In-medium reduction factor η values compared: 0.0, 0.2, 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9
- The proton-proton correlation function exhibits a peak at relative momentum q = 20 MeV/c
- Bohr radius for two protons: a_c = 57.5 fm
- Gaussian wave packet width L = 2.16 fm² for Au + Au system
- The exponent α in the Blast-Wave model was fixed to 1/3 for central collisions
- Minimum spanning tree cluster distance criterion: 3.5 fm with relative momentum smaller than 0.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-proton correlation functions in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly building on earlier work by Ma (2006) that briefly studied in-medium NN cross section effects using the CRAB code within IQMD. The study uses the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical method rather than the CRAB code, providing more detailed investigation of how the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section affects correlation functions across different rapidity regions. The results enable better understanding of emission mechanisms for mid-rapidity versus projectile/target rapidity protons, and the extracted source sizes and radial flow parameters can inform future studies of nuclear matter properties and collision dynamics in intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions.
