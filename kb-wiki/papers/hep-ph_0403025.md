# Does HBT Measure the Freeze-out Source Distribution?

> It is generally assumed that as a result of multiple scattering, the source distribution measured in HBT interferometry corresponds to a chaotic source at freeze-out. This assumption is subject to question as effects of multiple scattering in HBT measurements must be investigated within a quantum-mechanical framework. Applying the Glauber multiple scattering theory at high energies and the optical model at lower energies, we find that multiple scattering leads to an effective HBT density distribution that depends on the initial chaotic source distribution with an absorption.

## Key claim

The paper argues that HBT interferometry does not measure the freeze-out source distribution as commonly assumed; instead, multiple scattering leads to an effective HBT density distribution that depends on the initial chaotic source distribution with an absorption, with the real parts of the multiple-scattering phase differences approximately canceling and only the imaginary absorptive parts remaining.

## Method

The author applies the Glauber multiple scattering theory at high energies and the optical model at lower energies to compute the phase shifts accumulated by pions propagating from their production points to the detectors. The HBT correlation function for a chaotic source is then derived by taking the difference of the phases from two distinct particle trajectories (histories), showing that the real parts of the multiple-scattering phase shifts cancel approximately while the imaginary parts survive as an absorption factor. This modifies the effective source distribution in the HBT correlation function from the freeze-out distribution to the initial chaotic source distribution multiplied by an absorption factor.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \sim 1\) is cited from experimental measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, representing the "HBT puzzles" that motivate the theoretical investigation.
- No other explicit numerical results are presented in the paper; the work is a theoretical derivation without new experimental data or computed numerical values.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier quantum-mechanical treatment of multiple scattering in HBT (Wong 2003) and addresses the "HBT puzzles" discussed by many authors, where theoretical models fail to explain the small changes in extracted HBT radii with collision energy. The findings are qualitatively supported by new results from Kapusta and Li (2004). The paper enables future inclusion of longitudinal momentum loss effects using the Blankenbecler-Drell extension of Glauber theory, and suggests that the effective HBT source is closer to the initial chaotic source configuration and nuclear geometrical overlap than to the freeze-out configuration.
