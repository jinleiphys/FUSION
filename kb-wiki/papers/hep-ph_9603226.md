# A covariant diquark-quark model of the nucleon in the Salpeter approach

> We develop a rather simple, formally covariant quark-diquark model of the nucleon. The nucleon is treated as a bound state of a constituent quark and a diquark interacting via a quark exchange. We include both scalar and axial-vector diquarks. The underlying Bethe-Salpeter equation is transformed into a pair of coupled Salpeter equations. The electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated in the Mandelstam formalism. We obtain a very good description of all electromagnetic form factors for momentum transfers up to -3 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a formally covariant quark-diquark model of the nucleon, including both scalar and axial-vector diquarks interacting via quark exchange, can describe all electromagnetic form factors very well for momentum transfers up to -3 (GeV/c)^2, but only in the most symmetric case where the scalar and axial-vector diquark masses and coupling constants are set equal, and with the introduction of a Gaussian form factor for the diquarks and an anomalous magnetic moment parameter for the axial-vector diquark.

## Method

The nucleon is treated as a bound state of a constituent quark and a diquark (scalar and axial-vector) interacting via an instantaneous quark exchange. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is reduced to a pair of coupled Salpeter equations in the instantaneous approximation, which are solved using the Ritz variational principle with six radial basis functions. Electromagnetic form factors are then calculated using the Mandelstam formalism, with the axial-vector diquark propagator approximated by neglecting the term proportional to p_mu p_nu / m^2.

## Key numbers

*   Bound state mass M = 939 MeV obtained from the Ritz variational principle with oscillator parameter alpha = 1.35 fm.
*   Constituent quark mass m_q = 350 MeV/c^2; scalar and axial-vector diquark masses m_s = m_v = 650 MeV/c^2.
*   Diquark-quark coupling constant g = 8.10; diquark form factor parameter lambda = 0.24 fm; anomalous magnetic moment of axial-vector diquark kappa = 1.6.
*   Proton rms charge radius sqrt(<r^2>_p) = 0.84 fm, compared to experimental value (0.862 +/- 0.012) fm.
*   Neutron mean square charge radius <r^2>_n = -0.118 fm^2, compared to experimental value (-0.119 +/- 0.002) fm^2.
*   Proton magnetic moment mu_p = 2.78 mu_N (experimental: 2.793 mu_N); neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -1.51 mu_N (experimental: -1.913 mu_N); ratio mu_n/mu_p = -0.543 (experimental: -0.685).
*   Contributions to the normalization from the three channels: scalar diquark 0.63, zero-component of axial-vector diquark -0.23, vector-component of axial-vector diquark 0.60.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier scalar-diquark-only model (Keiner, 1995) which failed to describe the neutron electric form factor (overestimating it by a factor of more than three) and the magnetic form factors. By including axial-vector diquarks, the model achieves a remarkable improvement, particularly for the neutron electric form factor. The approach follows earlier studies using quark exchange interactions in diquark models (Lichtenberg, Huang, Reinhardt, Meyer, Hellstern, Hanhart, Ishii) and the Salpeter approach (Salpeter, 1952) combined with the Mandelstam formalism (Mandelstam, 1955). The model enables calculation of N-Delta transition form factors as a further test, which would involve only the axial-vector diquark channel.
