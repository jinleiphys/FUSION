# Dihadron production in DIS at NLO: the real corrections

> By using the formalism of the light-cone wave function along with the colour glass condensate effective theory, we consider next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to the production of a pair of hadrons in electron-proton, or electron-nucleus, collisions at small Bjorken $x$. To the order of interest, the process involves the fluctuation of a virtual photon into a quark-antiquark pair, followed by the emission of a gluon from either the quark, or the antiquark. For the case of a virtual photon with transverse polarization, we compute the real NLO corrections, where the emitted gluon is present in the final state. We first compute the tree-level cross-section for the production of the quark-antiquark-gluon system and then deduce the real NLO corrections to dihadron production by integrating out the kinematics of the gluon. We verify in detail that, in the limit where the gluon is soft, our calculation reproduces the (real piece of the) B-JIMWLK evolution of the leading-order cross-section for quark-antiquark production. Similarly, in the limit where the gluon is collinear with its emitter, we recover the real terms in the DGLAP evolution of the fragmentation function. The virtual NLO corrections to dihadron production will be presented by one of us in a subsequent publication.

## Key claim

The paper computes the real next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to dihadron production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken x, specifically for a transversely polarized virtual photon, using the light-cone wave function formalism within the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory; the calculation is restricted to the case where the emitted gluon is present in the final state (real corrections), with the virtual corrections deferred to a subsequent publication.

## Method

The authors use light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT) to construct the outgoing light-cone wavefunction of the virtual photon, expanded to include the quark-antiquark-gluon Fock component. They compute the tree-level cross-section for producing a quark-antiquark-gluon system and then integrate out the kinematics of the unmeasured gluon to obtain the real NLO corrections to dihadron production. The calculation is performed in the transverse coordinate representation, with the hadronic target treated as a shockwave in the eikonal approximation, and the results are verified against the B-JIMWLK evolution in the soft gluon limit and against DGLAP evolution in the collinear limit.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed to order α_em (QED coupling) and to order α_s (QCD coupling), corresponding to NLO in the strong coupling.
- The saturation momentum scales as Q_s^2(x,A) ∝ A^{1/3} (1/x^λ) with λ ≃ 0.2, providing the hard scale for perturbative calculations.
- The NLO accuracy targets perturbative precision of about 10% for phenomenological predictions at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
- The soft gluon limit reproduces the real piece of the B-JIMWLK evolution for the LO quark-antiquark production cross-section, involving 16 Feynman graphs in one-to-one correspondence with the real terms of the B-JIMWLK equation for the quadrupole.

## Context

This work extends previous NLO calculations for dihadron production in DIS at small x, complementing the results of Bergabo and Jalilian-Marian (2022) which focused on longitudinally polarized virtual photons, while agreeing with the dijet calculations of Caucal, Salazar, and Venugopalan (2021) and Taels, Altinoluk, and Marquet (2022). The calculation enables more accurate theoretical predictions for azimuthal correlations in dihadron production at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), where gluon saturation effects can be distinguished from final-state Sudakov radiation.
