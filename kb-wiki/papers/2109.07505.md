# Light Cone Distribution Amplitudes of Heavy-Light Mesons and Quarkonia

> In this work we use the framework of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations to compute Light-Cone Distribution Amplitudes of heavy-light mesons and quarkonia. In studying the meson properties, we introduce a flavor dependence in the heavy-quark sector of the Bethe-Salpeter ladder kernel which yields improved numerical results for masses and leptonic decay constants of the pseudoscalar $D$, $D_s$, $B$ and $B_s$ mesons. Finally, the corresponding heavy-light Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are projected onto the light front and we reconstruct the distribution amplitudes of the mesons in the full theory.

## Key claim
The authors compute light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) of heavy-light mesons and quarkonia using a modified rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, where the key improvement is introducing flavor dependence in the heavy-quark sector of the Bethe-Salpeter ladder kernel. This flavor-dependent kernel yields improved numerical results for masses and leptonic decay constants, but the reconstructed LCDAs are only valid at a fixed renormalization scale of mu = 2 GeV and rely on a specific parametrization of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes using Nakanishi representations.

## Method
The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar mesons together with the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation, but with a flavor-dependent interaction ansatz for the heavy-quark sector. They obtain the meson masses, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and leptonic decay constants, then project the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions onto the light front using Mellin moments to reconstruct the leading-twist LCDAs at a scale mu = 2 GeV. The quark propagators are parametrized with a complex conjugate pole representation and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes with a Nakanishi representation to enable analytic continuation to complex momenta.

## Context
This work extends earlier continuum studies of heavy-light mesons and quarkonia using Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations (Rojas 2014, El-Bennich 2016, Mojica 2017, Bedolla 2015, Raya 2017, Fischer 2014, Hilger 2017) by introducing a flavor-dependent interaction in the ladder truncation that accounts for the different impact of quark-gluon vertex dressing on light versus heavy quarks. The computed LCDAs provide nonperturbative input for QCD factorization calculations of non-leptonic heavy meson decays, which are needed to reduce theoretical uncertainties in extracting CKM parameters and CP-violating asymmetries (Beneke 1999, Charles 2004). The results enable more reliable predictions for weak decays of D and B mesons by replacing the asymptotic or simple model distributions previously used in such calculations.
