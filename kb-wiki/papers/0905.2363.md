# Thermal Effects for Quark and Gluon Distributions in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In-medium effects for distributions of quarks and gluons in central A+A collisions are considered. We suggest a duality principle, which means similarity of thermal spectra of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions and inclusive spectra which can be obtained within the dynamic quantum scattering theory. Within the suggested approach we show that the mean square of the transverse momentum for these partons grows and then saturates when the initial energy increases. It leads to the energy dependence of hadron transverse mass spectra which is similar to that observed in heavy ion collisions.

# Thermal Effects for Quark and Gluon Distributions in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes a "duality principle" whereby thermal spectra of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions can be dynamically generated from quantum scattering theory without introducing a thermodynamic temperature, and this approach predicts that the mean transverse momentum squared of partons grows with incident energy and then saturates, in contrast to thermodynamic models which predict continued growth.

## Method
The authors model parton distributions in central A+A collisions by assuming that mini-jets of quark-antiquark pairs are produced in the first N-N interaction, with a distribution following the dynamic ansatz (DA) of Eq. (4): f ∼ (1−x_r)^{d_A} ≈ exp(−E_h^* / T_s^A), where T_s^A = √s / 2d_A and d_A is proportional to the mean pion multiplicity in NN collisions. They then compute the mean transverse momentum squared of valence quarks in a medium using a convolution of parton distributions with either a local thermodynamic equilibrium (LE) distribution or the dynamic ansatz distribution, and extend this to meson transverse momentum spectra in central A+A collisions.

## Key numbers
- The parameter d_A is taken as d_A ≈ d_0 ⟨n⟩_π^{NN} with d_0 = 0.5, using the energy dependence for ⟨n⟩_π^{NN} from Meannpi (1993).
- The mean transverse momentum squared of K+ mesons in A-A collisions increases with incident energy up to AGS energies (√s ≤ 20–30 GeV) and then saturates at higher energies, as shown in Fig. 2.
- For the LE case, the temperature T = 150 MeV is used as a constant value.
- The effective slope T_eff^{pp} for kaons in p-p collisions is taken from Braun-Munzinger (2006) and depends on √s.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Lykasov, Sissakian, and Sorin (2008) on parton distributions in a medium under local thermodynamic equilibrium, and contrasts with thermodynamic models such as those by Sinyukov which predict continued increase of the effective temperature T_eff with √s_{NN} even at very high energies. The duality principle provides a dynamical interpretation of thermal-like spectra observed in heavy-ion experiments at AGS (Ah00, 2000), SPS (NA49), and RHIC, and the predicted saturation of transverse momentum can be tested by more precise measurements at SPS energies and future LHC experiments.
