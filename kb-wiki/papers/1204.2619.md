# Pairing fluctuations and gauge symmetry restoration in rotating superfluid nuclei

> Rapidly rotating nuclei provide us good testing grounds to study the pairing correlations; in fact, the transition from the superfluid to the normal phase is realized at high-spin states. The role played by the pairing correlations is quite different in these two phases: The static (BCS like mean-field) contribution is dominant in the superfluid phase, while the dynamic fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation are important in the normal phase. The influence of the pairing fluctuations on the high-spin rotational spectra and moments of inertia is discussed.

## Key claim

Pairing fluctuations (dynamic motions of the pairing gap beyond the static BCS mean-field) remain significant even after the static pairing gap vanishes at high rotational frequencies, and their inclusion is necessary to reproduce experimental rotational spectra and moments of inertia in both normal-deformed and superdeformed nuclei.

## Method

The author employs the random phase approximation (RPA) to evaluate the correlation energy from pairing fluctuations, using a monopole pairing force and the cranking model for rotating nuclei. The RPA correlation energy is calculated via a response function technique that avoids explicit diagonalization of the RPA matrix, using an integral along the imaginary axis. Results are compared with the variation after number projection (NP) method, which restores gauge symmetry broken by the BCS mean-field.

## Key numbers

- The static BCS pairing gap for ground states of deformed nuclei is approximately Δ ≈ 1 MeV.
- The critical rotational frequency for the super-to-normal phase transition in ¹⁶⁴Er is ω_c ≈ 0.33 MeV.
- The dealignment correction from pairing fluctuations amounts to 2–3 ħ.
- In ¹⁶⁸Yb, the mean-field pairing gap for the (+,0) yrast band quenches around ω_rot ≈ 0.4 MeV.
- For the negative parity bands in ¹⁶⁸Yb, pairing gaps are about 60% of the (+,0) band at the lowest frequency.
- The unpaired phase for neutrons in the rare-earth region is realized at spins I ≈ 20–30 ħ.

## Context

This work extends the cranked shell model (BFM86) and BCS mean-field treatments of rotating nuclei by incorporating dynamic pairing fluctuations via RPA, following earlier studies by Shimizu et al. (SGB89, SB88, SVB90, SB90, YRS90, SDB00). It addresses the recognized problem that effective pairing gaps remain finite after static gaps vanish (ERI85, BDF86). The method enables improved description of rotational spectra and moments of inertia in both normal-deformed and superdeformed nuclei, and the NP method (Egido, this volume) provides a smooth alternative across the phase transition.
