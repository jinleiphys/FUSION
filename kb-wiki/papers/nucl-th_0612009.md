# Inclusive electron scattering off 4He

> Inclusive electron scattering off 4He is calculated exactly with a complete treatment of the final state interaction within a simple semirealistic potential model. We discuss results for both the longitudinal and the transverse response functions, at various momentum transfers. A consistent meson exchange current is implemented. Good agreement with available experimental data is found for the longitudinal response function, while some strength is still missing in the transverse response function.

# Inclusive electron scattering off 4He

## Key claim
The authors achieve good agreement with experimental data for the longitudinal response function of 4He using a semirealistic potential model with full final state interaction, but some strength is still missing in the transverse response function, likely due to the absence of explicit pionic degrees of freedom in their model.

## Method
The authors use the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method to avoid explicit calculation of all final states, solving a bound-state-like Schrödinger equation. They expand the charge and current operators into multipoles and solve using the effective interaction in hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) basis with the semirealistic Malfliet-Tjon (MTI-III) nucleon-nucleon interaction. A consistent meson exchange current (MEC) is derived for the transverse response, though the center-of-mass phase factor is approximated as unity for low momentum transfer.

## Key numbers
- At momentum transfer q=300 MeV/c, five isovector Coulomb multipoles are sufficient for convergence of the longitudinal response; at q=500 MeV/c, two additional multipoles are needed.
- At q=300 MeV/c, the two-body current (MEC) contributes only about 2-3% at the peak of the transverse response function.
- The transverse response is shown for momentum transfers q=100 and 300 MeV/c, with the spin current strongly dominating at the higher momentum transfer.
- At q=100 MeV/c, the convection current contribution remains important, and MEC plays an important role.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of the longitudinal response of 4He using the Trento (TN) potential (el, year not specified) by adding a consistent treatment of the transverse response with meson exchange currents. The results contradict the stronger two-body current effects found at q=300 MeV/c by Carl_Schiav (year not specified), which the authors attribute to the missing explicit pionic degrees of freedom in their semirealistic model. The calculation enables future comparisons with experimental data from Bates and Saclay, and highlights the need for more realistic potentials with explicit pion exchange to fully describe the transverse response.
