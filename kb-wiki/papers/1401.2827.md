# Physics input for modelling superfluid neutron stars with hyperon cores

> Observations of massive ($M \approx 2.0~M_\odot$) neutron stars (NSs), PSRs J1614-2230 and J0348+0432, rule out most of the models of nucleon-hyperon matter employed in NS simulations. Here we construct three possible models of nucleon-hyperon matter consistent with the existence of $2~M_\odot$ pulsars as well as with semi-empirical nuclear matter parameters at saturation, and semi-empirical hypernuclear data. Our aim is to calculate for these models all the parameters necessary for modelling dynamics of hyperon stars (such as equation of state, adiabatic indices, thermodynamic derivatives, relativistic entrainment matrix, etc.), making them available for a potential user. To this aim a general non-linear hadronic Lagrangian involving $σωρϕσ^\ast$ meson fields, as well as quartic terms in vector-meson fields, is considered. A universal scheme for calculation of the $\ell=0,1$ Landau Fermi-liquid parameters and relativistic entrainment matrix is formulated in the mean-field approximation. Use of this scheme allow us to obtain numerical tables with the equation of state, Landau quasiparticle effective masses, adiabatic indices, the $\ell=0,1$ Landau Fermi-liquid parameters, and the relativistic entrainment matrix for the selected models of nucleon-hyperon matter. These data are available on-line and suitable for numerical implementation in computer codes modelling various dynamical processes in NSs, in particular, oscillations of superfluid NSs and their cooling.

## Key claim

The paper constructs three specific models of nucleon-hyperon matter that are consistent with the existence of 2 solar mass neutron stars (PSRs J1614-2230 and J0348+0432), but only by introducing additional hidden-strangeness meson fields (phi and sigma*) and/or allowing breaking of SU(6) symmetry in vector-meson couplings to hyperons, which the authors acknowledge is necessary to simultaneously satisfy hypernuclear constraints.

## Method

The authors employ a general non-linear hadronic Lagrangian with sigma, omega, rho, phi, and sigma* meson fields including quartic vector-meson terms, solved in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation. They formulate a universal scheme to calculate the l=0 and l=1 Landau Fermi-liquid parameters and the relativistic entrainment matrix for a mixture of baryon superfluids at zero temperature, extending previous work by Gusakov et al. (2009a, 2009b) that used a simpler sigma-omega-rho model. Numerical tables of the equation of state, Landau effective masses, adiabatic indices, Landau parameters, and entrainment matrix are produced for three selected models and made available online.

## Key numbers

* Maximum neutron star mass M_max > 2 solar masses is required by observations of PSRs J1614-2230 and J0348+0432, and all three models satisfy this constraint.
* Nuclear saturation density is n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}, corresponding to mass density ρ_0 ≈ 2.8 × 10^{14} g cm^{-3}.
* The outer core extends up to 2-3 ρ_0, while the inner core reaches up to ~10 ρ_0 at the center of the most massive stars.
* The Landau effective mass m*_i is defined by m*_i = sqrt(p_{Fi}^2 + (m_i - g_{σi} σ - g_{σ*i} σ*)^2), and the dimensionless Landau parameters F^{ij}_ℓ are defined via F^{ij}_ℓ ≡ sqrt(N_{Fi} N_{Fj}) f^{ij}_ℓ with N_{Fi} = m*_i p_{Fi} / π^2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Gusakov et al. (2009a, 2009b) by replacing their simple sigma-omega-rho model with a more general Lagrangian including phi and sigma* mesons, motivated by the discovery of 2 solar mass pulsars (Demorest et al. 2010; Antoniadis et al. 2013) which ruled out most previous nucleon-hyperon matter models. The paper builds on the relativistic mean-field approach of Glendenning (1985, 2000) and the relativistic extension of Landau Fermi-liquid theory by Baym and Chin (1976), and it provides the microphysical input needed for modeling superfluid neutron star dynamics such as oscillations and cooling.
