# Nuclear liquid-gas phase transition with machine learning

> The machine-learning techniques have shown their capability for studying phase transitions in condensed matter physics. Here, we employ the machine-learning techniques to study the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. We adopt an unsupervised learning and classify the liquid and gas phases of nuclei directly from the final state raw experimental data of heavy-ion reactions. Based on a confusion scheme which combines the supervised and unsupervised learning, we obtain the limiting temperature of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. Its value $9.24\pm0.04~\rm MeV$ is consistent with that obtained by the traditional caloric curve method. Our study explores the paradigm of combining the machine-learning techniques with heavy-ion experimental data, and it is also instructive for studying the phase transition of other uncontrollable systems, like QCD matter.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that machine-learning techniques can classify liquid and gas phases of nuclei directly from raw experimental heavy-ion data and determine the limiting temperature of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, but they note that the nucleus is an uncontrollable system where events cannot come from separate phases, so the phase change is gradual through a liquid-gas phase coexistence rather than a sharp transition.

## Method

The authors used experimental data from 40Ar on 27Al and 48Ti reactions at 47 MeV/nucleon, measured with the NIMROD 4π detector. They reconstructed quasi-projectile (QP) events with ZQP = 12 using a three-source fit method, yielding 40081 valid events. An autoencoder neural network was trained on the event-by-event charge-weighted charge multiplicity distributions ZMc(Z) to classify liquid and gas phases in an unsupervised manner, and a Bayesian neural network with a confusion scheme was used to determine the limiting temperature by proposing a transition temperature and measuring testing accuracy.

## Key numbers

- Limiting temperature from confusion scheme: 9.24 ± 0.04 MeV, consistent with 9.0 ± 0.4 MeV from traditional caloric curve analysis
- Analogical characteristic excitation energy per nucleon: 5.79 ± 0.02 MeV
- 40081 valid QP events with ZQP = 12 were used, with 2/3 for training and 1/3 for testing
- Events were selected with ZQP ≥ 12, focusing on ZQP = 12 events which had the largest statistics
- Low excitation bin: 0.9 – 2.8 MeV; intermediate excitation bin: 5.3 – 5.4 MeV; high excitation bin: 8.1 – 13.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends machine-learning techniques from condensed matter physics phase transitions (Carleo and Troyer 2017, Nieuwenburg et al. 2017) to nuclear physics, specifically the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition traditionally studied via caloric curves (Pochodzalla et al. 1995, Ma et al. 1997). The approach enables studying phase transitions in uncontrollable systems like nuclei and QCD matter, where equilibrium thermodynamics is not directly applicable. The method uses the same experimental data previously analyzed by Wada et al. (1999) with the traditional caloric curve method, and the results are consistent with that earlier work.
