# The role of the g9/2 orbital in the development of collectivity in the A = 60 region: The case of 61Co

> An extensive study of the level structure of 61Co has been performed following the complex 26Mg(48Ca, 2a4npg)61Co reaction at beam energies of 275, 290 and 320 MeV using Gammasphere and the Fragment Mass Analyzer (FMA). The low-spin structure is discussed within the framework of shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A effective interaction. Two quasi-rotational bands consisting of stretched-E2 transitions have been established up to spins I = 41/2 and (43/2), and excitation energies of 17 and 20 MeV, respectively. These are interpreted as signature partners built on a neutron ν(g9/2)2 configuration coupled to a proton πp3/2 state, based on Cranked Shell Model (CSM) calculations and comparisons with observations in neighboring nuclei. In addition, four I = 1 bands were populated to high spin, with the yrast dipole band interpreted as a possible candidate for the shears mechanism, a process seldom observed thus far in this mass region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two quasi-rotational bands of stretched-E2 transitions in 61Co, extending to spins I = 41/2 and (43/2) at excitation energies of approximately 17 and 20 MeV, are signature partners built on a neutron ν(g9/2)2 configuration coupled to a proton πp3/2 state, based on Cranked Shell Model calculations and comparisons with neighboring nuclei; the yrast dipole band is interpreted as a possible candidate for the shears mechanism, a process seldom observed in this mass region, though definitive parity assignments could not be made for all bands since neither linear polarization nor internal conversion was measured.

## Method

Excited states in 61Co were populated via the multi-nucleon transfer reaction 26Mg(48Ca, 2α4npγ)61Co in inverse kinematics at beam energies of 275, 290, and 320 MeV using Gammasphere and the Fragment Mass Analyzer (FMA). Gamma-ray multipolarities were deduced from angular distribution measurements and two-point angular-correlation ratios, and a transition quadrupole moment for band QB1 was extracted using the Doppler-shift attenuation method (DSAM) with the Monte Carlo simulation code wlife4 and stopping powers from SRIM-2010. Low-spin structure was interpreted within the shell-model framework using the GXPF1A effective interaction, while high-spin bands were compared with Cranked Shell Model (CSM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Two quasi-rotational bands were established up to spins I = 41/2 and (43/2) at excitation energies of approximately 17 and 20 MeV, respectively.
- Band QB1 extends from a 17/2- state at 5814 keV up to a tentative (41/2-) state at 17170 keV, with a cascade of 1008-, 1267-, 1703-, 2053-, 2442-, and 2880-keV transitions.
- The transition quadrupole moment for band QB1 was measured as Qt = 1.9+0.8-1.1 eb, corresponding to a quadrupole deformation of |β2| = 0.4(2).
- Band QB2 was assigned a tentative excitation energy greater than or equal to 8.4 MeV and spin-parity greater than or equal to 23/2-.
- The yrast dipole band DB1 extends up to a tentative (23/2-) state at 7174 keV, with in-band transitions including 207-, 270-, and 1108-keV gamma rays.
- Band DB2 extends up to a 27/2- state at 9672 keV, with the 1030-keV transition depopulating the 21/2- level at 5832 keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of high-spin collective excitations involving the νg9/2 orbital in the A ≈ 60 region, following observations of deformed rotational bands in 57Co (PhysRevC.65.034309) and in neutron-rich Ni isotopes including 62Ni (ni62-albers) and 63Ni (PhysRevC.88.054314-albers). The results provide evidence for the development of collectivity in 61Co near N = 40 and enable further investigation of the shears mechanism in this mass region, as well as comparisons with Cranked Shell Model calculations and shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A effective interaction.
