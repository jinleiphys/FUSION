# Temperature fluctuations and Tsallis statistics in Relativistic Heavy Ion collisions

> We study temperature fluctuations in the initial stages of the relativistic heavy ion collision using a multiphase transport model. We consider the plasma in the initial stages after collision before it has a chance to equilibrate. We have considered Au + Au collision with a center of mass energy of 200 GeV. We use the non extensive Tsallis statistics to find the entropic index in the partonic stages of the relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find that the temperature and the entropic index have a linear relationship during the partonic stages of the heavy ion collision. This has already been observed in the hadronic phase. A detailed analysis of the dependence of the entropic index on the system shows that for increasing space time rapidity, the entropic index of the partonic system increases. The entropic index also depends on the beam collision energy. The calculation of the entropic index from the experimental data fitting of the transverse momenta deals with the hadronic phase. However, our current study shows that the behaviour of the entropic index in the initial non-equilibrium stage of the collision is very similar to the behaviour of the entropic index in the hadronic stage.

## Key claim

The authors show that the linear relationship between effective temperature and the Tsallis entropic index, previously observed in the hadronic phase, also holds in the partonic (pre-equilibrium) stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, though with a different slope. The entropic index values in the partonic stage are systematically higher (mostly greater than 1.2) than those obtained from hadronic transverse momentum fits (q ~ 1.12), because the system is further from equilibrium before hadronization.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multiphase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They divided the transverse plane into grid cells of size dx = dy = 0.3 fm, assumed local thermal equilibrium in each cell, and calculated the temperature from the energy density using the ideal gas relation epsilon = 12 (4 + 3 N_f) (T^4 / pi^2) with N_f = 3. They then constructed the probability distribution of the inverse temperature beta = 1/T across all cells, fitted it with a chi-squared distribution, and extracted the Tsallis entropic index q from the fit parameter.

## Key numbers

- Entropic index q = 1.51158 with asymptotic standard error 3.5% and beta_0 = 0.968718 GeV^{-1} with asymptotic standard error 0.33% for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, |eta| < 3, at tau = 1 fm/c.
- Entropic index values from the partonic stage are mostly greater than 1.2, compared to q ~ 1.12 from hadronic transverse momentum fits.
- Temperature fluctuations were studied at proper times tau = 1, 2, 3, and 5 fm/c, with the entropic index peaking around tau = 3 fm/c.
- The entropic index increases with space-time rapidity, plateauing for higher collision energies, and increases with collision energy in the range 20 GeV to 200 GeV.
- For central collisions (0-10%), the q value is nearly constant across 20-200 GeV; for peripheral collisions (60-80%), the q value increases more strongly over the same energy range.

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis statistics from the hadronic phase, where it has been used to fit transverse momentum spectra (e.g., by Cleymans et al. and Wilk et al.), into the partonic pre-equilibrium stage of heavy ion collisions. The authors compare their results to experimental data analyses by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations and to theoretical work by Biro et al. and Deppman et al. on Tsallis statistics in quark matter and the MIT bag model. The study enables future comparisons between partonic and hadronic non-equilibrium properties and suggests that the entropic index's dependence on collision energy and centrality observed in hadronic data has a counterpart in the initial partonic stage.
