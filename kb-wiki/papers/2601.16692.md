# Compatibility of recent ${\cal S}=-2$ emulsion events

> We question the compatibility of recent ${\cal S}=-2$ hypernuclear assignments of J-PARC E07 $Ξ^-$-capture emulsion events with assignments deduced from other experiments.

# Compatibility of recent ${\cal S}=-2$ emulsion events

## Key claim
The recent J-PARC E07 assignments of $\Xi^-_{1s}$ nuclear bound states in $^{14}$N from $\Xi^-$-capture emulsion events are questionable, because radiative $E1$ de-excitation from atomic $\Xi^-_{3D}$ states preferentially populates $\Xi^-_{1p}$ and $\Xi^0_{1p}$ states rather than $\Xi^-_{1s}$ states, and the claimed $\Xi^-_{1s}-^{14}{\rm N}$ binding energy of 6.27$\pm$0.27 MeV is incompatible with the $\Xi^-_{1s}-^{11}{\rm B}$ binding energy of 8.9$\pm$1.4$^{+3.8}_{-3.1}$ MeV from J-PARC E05, unless an abnormally strong repulsive $\Xi NN$ three-body force is invoked.

## Method
The paper examines the compatibility of $\Xi^-$ capture events in emulsion (C,N,O nuclei) from KEK-PS E373 and J-PARC E07 with $\Xi^-$ nuclear spectra from the J-PARC E05 ${^{12}}{\rm C}(K^-,K^+){^{12}_{\Xi}}{\rm Be}$ reaction. It analyzes the atomic cascade and radiative $E1$ transition rates from $\Xi^-_{3D}$ atomic states to nuclear bound states, and compares $\Lambda\Lambda$ binding energies derived from the new J-PARC E07 ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}}{\rm B}$ event with those from the KEK-PS E373 ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He}$ NAGARA event and a KEK-E176 candidate event.

## Key numbers
- $\Xi^-_{1s}-^{11}{\rm B}$ binding energy from J-PARC E05: $B_{\Xi^-}=8.9\pm 1.4{^{+3.8}_{-3.1}}$ MeV
- $\Xi^-_{1s}-^{14}{\rm N}$ binding energy from J-PARC E07 event IRRAWADDY: $6.27\pm 0.27$ MeV
- $\Xi^-_{1p}$ Coulomb-assisted nuclear states bound by $\approx$1 MeV in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N
- $\Xi$-nuclear attractive potential depth at nuclear-matter density $\rho_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$: $\approx$21 MeV, within the range $17\pm 6$ MeV from BNL-AGS E906
- $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}({_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}}{\rm B})$ from J-PARC E07: $25.57 \pm 1.18 \pm 0.07$ MeV
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}}{\rm B}$ from J-PARC E07: $2.83 \pm 1.18 \pm 0.14$ MeV
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}}{\rm B}$ from KEK-E176 event #15-03-37: $0.9 \pm 0.8$ MeV
- $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ for ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He}$ from KEK-PS E373 NAGARA event: $0.67 \pm 0.17$ MeV

## Context
This work questions the $\Xi^-_{1s}$ assignments of J-PARC E07 emulsion events (E07, 2025) and proposes reinterpretation as $\Xi^0_{1p}-^{14}{\rm C}$ states following the mechanism of Friedman and Gal (FG23). It extends the discussion of $\Xi$-nuclear potential depths from earlier experiments including KEK-E224, BNL-AGS E885, and BNL-AGS E906, and compares with theoretical evaluations from HAL-QCD (Inoue, 2019), $\chi$EFT@NLO (Kohno, 2019), SU(6) (Gal and Friedman, 2021), and the Nijmegen ESC16* model (Nij20). The new ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}}{\rm B}$ event from J-PARC E07 (He, 2025) is shown to disagree with the KEK-E176 candidate and with the well-established NAGARA event value for $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$, which remains the unique benchmark for $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction strength.
