# Low energy doubly-virtual Compton scattering from di-lepton electroproduction on a nucleon

> We propose a new way to experimentally determine the subleading low-energy structure constant of doubly-virtual Compton scattering on a proton. Such empirical determination will reduce the theoretical model error in estimates of the hadronic correction to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift. We demonstrate that the di-lepton forward-backward asymmetry in the $e^- p \to e^- p \, e^- e^+$ process, which can be accessed at electron scattering facilities, yields a large sensitivity to this so far unknown low-energy constant.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the forward-backward asymmetry in the $e^- p \to e^- p \, e^- e^+$ process can experimentally determine the previously unconstrained low-energy constant $b_{3,0}$ appearing in the fourth-order term of the subtraction function for doubly-virtual Compton scattering, thereby reducing the theoretical model uncertainty in the hadronic correction to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift.

## Method

The authors analyze the helicity-averaged doubly-virtual Compton scattering (dVCS) amplitude off a proton, expanding the non-Born amplitudes $\bar{B}_1$ and $\bar{B}_3$ at low photon virtualities to isolate the low-energy constant $b_{3,0}$. They compute the differential cross section and forward-backward asymmetry $A_{FB}$ for the $e^- p \to e^- p \, e^- e^+$ reaction, including Bethe-Heitler (spacelike and timelike) and dVCS diagrams, using the electromagnetic form factor parameterization of Bernauer (2010) and a $\Delta(1232)$-pole model for the inelastic dVCS amplitude. The asymmetry is evaluated at $W = 1.25$ GeV with kinematics $Q^2 = q^{\prime 2} = -t = 0.075$ GeV$^2$, comparing results across different theoretical estimates for $b_{3,0}$.

## Key numbers

- The $Q^4$ term of the subtraction function $\frac{1}{2}\bar{T}_1^{\prime\prime}(0)$ ranges from $[-1.01, -0.35] \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^5$ in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Birse 2012) to $-2.14 \pm 0.98 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^5$ in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (Lensky 2017), and $-0.47 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^5$ from superconvergence sum rules (Tomalak 2015).
- The low-energy constant $\alpha_\mathrm{em} b_{3,0}$ is $0.11 \pm 0.05 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^5$ in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory and $3.96 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^5$ from superconvergence sum rules.
- At $W = 1.25$ GeV with $q^{\prime 2} = -t = 0.075$ GeV$^2$, the $\gamma p \to e^- e^+ p$ asymmetry $A_{FB}$ reaches values between -40% and +30%.
- For the $e^- p \to e^- p \, e^- e^+$ process at $W = 1.25$ GeV and $Q^2 = q^{\prime 2} = -t = 0.075$ GeV$^2$, the spread in $b_{3,0}$ values corresponds to a change in $A_{FB}$ from 20% to 50% at forward and backward angles.

## Context

This work addresses the dominant theoretical uncertainty in the hadronic correction to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, which currently limits the precision of the proton charge radius extraction from muonic atom spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013). The paper extends prior dispersive analyses of the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering subtraction function (Carlson 2011, Birse 2012) and chiral perturbation theory calculations (Alarcon 2013, Lensky 2017) by proposing an experimental observable sensitive to the previously unconstrained $b_{3,0}$ constant. The measurement can be performed at existing electron scattering facilities such as MAMI, MESA, and JLab, and builds on a pioneering forward-backward asymmetry measurement in $\gamma p \to e^- e^+ p$ at DESY (Alvensleben 1973).
