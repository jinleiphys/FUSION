# Magnetic dipole transition in $^{48}$Ca

> The magnetic dipole transition strength $B(M1)$ of $^{48}$Ca is dominated by a single resonant state at an excitation energy of 10.23 MeV. Experiments disagree about $B(M1)$ and this impacts our understanding of spin flips in nuclei. We performed ab initio computations based on chiral effective field theory and found that $B(M1:0^+\rightarrow1^+)$ lies in the range from $7.0$ to $10.2~μ_N^2$. This is consistent with a $(γ,n)$ experiment but larger than results from $(e,e^\prime)$ and $(p,p')$ scattering. Two-body currents yield no quenching of the $B(M1)$ strength and continuum effects reduce it by about 10%. For a validation of our approach, we computed magnetic moments in $^{47,49}$Ca and performed benchmark calculations in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors compute the $B(M1)$ strength for the $1^+$ state in $^{48}$Ca using ab initio methods and find a range of $7.0$ to $10.2~\mu_N^2$, which is consistent with the $(\gamma,n)$ experiment but larger than results from $(e,e^\prime)$ and $(p,p')$ scattering; the key qualifier is that two-body currents yield no quenching of the $B(M1)$ strength, contrary to expectations from Gamow-Teller transitions, and continuum effects reduce it by about 10%.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled-cluster method (CCSDT-3 and EOM-CCSDT-3 approximations) with Hamiltonians and currents from chiral effective field theory, using four different chiral interactions (1.8/2.0(EM), NNLO$_{\rm sat}$, $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}(394)$, and $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}(450)$). They computed the ground state and excited $1^+$ state of $^{48}$Ca in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with $N_{\rm max}=12$ and $\hbar\omega=16$ MeV, and included continuum effects by using a Berggren basis for the $\nu f_{5/2}$ partial wave. Two-body currents were normal-ordered with respect to the Hartree-Fock reference state and included in the transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- $B(M1:0^+\rightarrow1^+)$ for $^{48}$Ca lies in the range from $7.0$ to $10.2~\mu_N^2$, with a conservative uncertainty estimate of 15%.
- The extreme single-particle model estimate is $12~\mu_N^2$ from a neutron particle-hole excitation $\nu f_{5/2} (\nu f_{7/2})^{-1}$.
- The $1^+$ state is at excitation energy $E_x=10.23$ MeV, which is $0.28$ MeV above the neutron-emission threshold experimentally.
- For the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction, the computed resonance width $\Gamma$ is $4$ keV, compared to the experimental upper limit of $\leq 17$ keV.
- Two-body currents increase the $M1$ strength by about 5%, while continuum effects reduce it by about 10%.
- The $B(M1)$ predictions for the four different chiral interactions agree within 7%.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations by Holt (2014) that reported $B(M1)\approx 5.3~\mu_N^2$ and earlier calculations by Brown (1998) giving about $7.4~\mu_N^2$, and directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between $(e,e^\prime)$ experiments by Steffen (1980) reporting $B(M1)=4.0\pm 0.3~\mu_N^2$ and the $(\gamma,n)$ experiment by Tompkins (2011) reporting $B(M1)=6.8\pm 0.5~\mu_N^2$. The results enable improved estimates for neutrino-nucleus cross sections and electron capture rates in supernova cores, and demonstrate that two-body currents for $M1$ transitions differ fundamentally from those in Gamow-Teller decays, contrary to the expectation of quenching factors used in shell-model calculations.
