# Information entropy in fragmenting systems

> The possibility of facing critical phenomena in nuclear fragmentation is a topic of great interest. Different observables have been proposed to identify such a behavior, in particular, some related to the use of information entropy as a possible signal of critical behavior. In this work we critically examine some of the most widespread used ones comparing its performance in bond percolation and in the analysis of fragmenting Lennard Jones Drops.

# Information entropy in fragmenting systems

## Key claim

The information entropy $S_1$ is not a reliable signature of critical behavior in fragmenting systems, contrary to claims by Ma (1999). The maximum of $S_1$ is systematically shifted away from the true critical point in systems where finite-size effects are negligible, and only appears close to other signatures in small systems where all signals are smeared by finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors tested five proposed criticality signatures, information entropy ($S_1$), normalized variance of the maximum fragment size ($NVM$), second moment of the cluster distribution ($M_2$), best-fitting power law with one-parameter fit (minimum $\chi^2$), and minimum $\tau$ from two-parameter power law fits, on bond percolation on simple cubic lattices of sizes $30\times30\times30$ and $6\times6\times6$. They then applied the same analysis to molecular dynamics simulations of Lennard-Jones drops of 147 particles undergoing multifragmentation, using excitation energy as the control parameter.

## Key numbers

- For the $30\times30\times30$ percolation lattice, $NVM$, $M_2$, and $\chi^2$ all give the same critical probability $p_c = 0.25 \pm 0.01$, while the information entropy maximum is shifted to $p = 0.225 \pm 0.015$.
- For the $6\times6\times6$ percolation lattice, the best-fitted mass spectra (minimum $\chi^2$) is at $p = 0.31 \pm 0.01$, $NVM$ maximum at $p = 0.30 \pm 0.01$, $M_2$ at $p = 0.29 \pm 0.01$, and $S_1$ at $p = 0.27 \pm 0.05$.
- For the Lennard-Jones drops, $M_2$ and $NVM$ peak at excitation energy $E = 0.3 \pm 0.2 \epsilon$, while the minimum $\chi^2$ is at $E = 0.15 \pm 0.15 \epsilon$; the information entropy monotonically increases over the energy range analyzed and shows no maximum.
- The derivative of $S_1$ evaluated at the critical point for the theoretical cluster distribution yields $(dS_1/d\epsilon)_{\epsilon=0} \approx -1.6$, confirming the entropy does not have an extremum at the critical point.

## Context

This work critically tests the information entropy proposed by Ma (1999) as a signature of critical behavior in nuclear fragmentation, comparing it against established signatures from Bauer (1986), Campi (1986), Dorso, Latora and Bonasera (1999), Stauffer and Aharoni (1992), and Elliott et al. (2000). The results contradict Ma (1999) and show that $S_1$ should be discarded in favor of the one-parameter power law fit (minimum $\chi^2$), the normalized variance of the maximum fragment ($NVM$), or the second moment of the cluster distribution ($M_2$) when searching for critical behavior in multifragmentation.
