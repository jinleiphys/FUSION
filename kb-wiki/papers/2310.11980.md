# Shape polarization in the tin isotopes near $N=60$ from precision $g$-factor measurements on short-lived $11/2^-$ isomers

> The $g$ factors of $11/2^-$ isomers in semimagic $^{109}$Sn and $^{111}$Sn (isomeric lifetimes $τ= 2.9(3)$ ns and $τ= 14.4(7)$ ns, respectively) were measured by an extension of the Time Differential Perturbed Angular Distribution technique, which uses \LaBr detectors and the hyperfine fields of a gadolinium host to achieve precise measurements in a new regime of short-lived isomers. The results, $g(11/2^-; {^{109}\textrm{Sn}}) = -0.186(8)$ and $g(11/2^-; {^{111}\textrm{Sn}}) = -0.214(4)$, are significantly lower in magnitude than those of the $11/2^-$ isomers in the heavier isotopes and depart from the value expected for a near pure neutron $h_{11/2}$ configuration. Broken-symmetry density functional theory calculations applied to the sequence of $11/2^-$ states reproduce the magnitude and location of this deviation. The $g(11/2^-)$ values are affected by shape core polarization; the odd $0h_{11/2}$ neutron couples to $J^π=2^+,4^+,6^+...$ configurations in the weakly-deformed effective core, causing a decrease in the $g$-factor magnitudes.

## Key claim
The g-factors of the 11/2- isomers in 109Sn and 111Sn deviate significantly from the near-constant value of approximately -0.243 observed in heavier tin isotopes, indicating that shape core polarization from the odd neutron coupling to even-spin core configurations causes this departure, not just the usual spin polarization.

## Method
The g-factors were measured using an extension of the Time Differential Perturbed Angular Distribution (TDPAD) technique with LaBr3 detectors and the hyperfine fields of a gadolinium host after in-beam implantation. Excited states in 109,111Sn were populated by 58-MeV 16O induced reactions on 96,98Mo targets, and the precession frequencies were measured relative to the known g-factor of the 11/2- isomer in 113Sn. Broken-symmetry density functional theory (DFT) calculations using the Skyrme force UNEDF1 were performed with time-odd mean fields included to compute spectroscopic magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers
- g(11/2-; 109Sn) = -0.186(8), measured relative to 113Sn with isomeric lifetime tau = 2.9(3) ns
- g(11/2-; 111Sn) = -0.214(4), measured relative to 113Sn with isomeric lifetime tau = 14.4(7) ns
- g(11/2-; 113Sn) = -0.235(2), used as the reference value from prior work by Brenn (1974)
- Mean hyperfine field strength for 111Sn measurement: <Bhf> = -30.2(5) T with width Delta Bhf = 4.0(7) T
- Mean hyperfine field strength for 109Sn measurement: <Bhf> = -31.6(6) T with width Delta Bhf = 4.6(6) T

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of 11/2- isomer g-factors across the tin isotopic chain from 109Sn to 131Sn, where prior measurements by Dimmling (1974), Brenn (1974), and Yordanov (2020) showed near-constant values for isotopes 113Sn through 131Sn. The observed deviation near N=60 coincides with enhanced B(E2; 0+ to 2+) strengths in neighboring even-even Sn isotopes reported by Banu (2005), Cederkall (2007), and others, which have been interpreted as evidence for breaking of the Z=50 shell closure. The DFT calculations reproduce the g-factor variations through core polarization effects that include excitations beyond the valence spaces available to shell-model calculations, and the results call for comparative analysis of effective cores in odd indium, antimony, and tin isotopes.
