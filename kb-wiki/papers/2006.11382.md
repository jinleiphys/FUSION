# Drell-Yan $q_T$ Resummation of Fiducial Power Corrections at N$^3$LL

> We consider Drell-Yan production $pp\to V^* X \to L X$ at small $q_T \ll Q$. Experimental measurements require fiducial cuts on the leptonic state $L$, which introduce enhanced, linear power corrections in $q_T/Q$. We show that they can be unambiguously predicted from factorization, and resummed to the same order as the leading-power contribution. We thus obtain predictions for the fiducial $q_T$ spectrum to N3LL and next-to-leading-power in $q_T/Q$. Matching to full NNLO ($α_s^2$), we find that the linear power corrections are indeed the dominant ones, and the remaining fixed-order corrections become almost negligible below $q_T \lesssim 40$ GeV. We also discuss the implications for more complicated observables, and provide predictions for the fiducial $ϕ^*$ spectrum at N3LL+NNLO. We find excellent agreement with ATLAS and CMS measurements of $q_T$ and $ϕ^*$. We also consider the $p_T^\ell$ spectrum. We show that it develops leptonic power corrections in $q_T/(Q - 2p_T^\ell)$, which diverge near the Jacobian peak $p_T^\ell \sim Q/2$ and must be kept to all powers to obtain a meaningful result there. Doing so, we obtain for the first time an analytically resummed result for the $p_T^\ell$ spectrum around the Jacobian peak at N3LL+NNLO. Our method is based on performing a complete tensor decomposition for hadronic and leptonic tensors. In practice this is equivalent to often-used recoil prescriptions, for which our results now provide rigorous, formal justification. Our tensor decomposition yields nine Lorentz-scalar hadronic structure functions, which directly map onto the commonly used angular coefficients, but also holds for arbitrary leptonic final states. In particular, for suitably defined Born-projected leptons it still yields a LO-like angular decomposition even when including QED final-state radiation. We also discuss the application to $q_T$ subtractions.

## Key claim

The authors show that fiducial cuts on the leptonic final state in Drell-Yan production generically introduce linear power corrections in \(q_T/Q\) that can be unambiguously predicted from factorization and resummed to the same logarithmic order as the leading-power contribution, contrary to the inclusive case where such linear corrections are absent.

## Method

The authors perform a complete Lorentz tensor decomposition of the hadronic and leptonic tensors for \(pp\to V^* X \to L X\), yielding nine Lorentz-scalar hadronic structure functions that map onto the standard angular coefficients. They implement the factorization of fiducial power corrections using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and perform \(q_T\) resummation up to N\(^3\)LL, matched to fixed-order NNLO (\(\alpha_s^2\)) calculations. The method is applied to the \(q_T\) spectrum, the \(\phi^*\) observable, and the lepton \(p_T^\ell\) spectrum near the Jacobian peak.

## Key numbers

- For the fiducial \(q_T\) spectrum at N\(^3\)LL+NNLO, the remaining fixed-order corrections become almost negligible below \(q_T \lesssim 40\) GeV after including the linear power corrections via factorization.
- The lepton \(p_T^\ell\) spectrum develops leptonic power corrections in \(q_T/(Q - 2p_T^\ell)\) that diverge near the Jacobian peak at \(p_T^\ell \sim Q/2\), requiring all-power resummation.
- The tensor decomposition yields nine Lorentz-scalar hadronic structure functions for the Drell-Yan process.
- The authors provide predictions for the fiducial \(\phi^*\) spectrum at N\(^3\)LL+NNLO and find excellent agreement with ATLAS and CMS measurements.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of power corrections in Drell-Yan production from Ebert (2018), which showed that linear NLP corrections are absent for the inclusive \(q_T\) spectrum, and from Ebert (2019), which demonstrated that linear corrections generically arise once fiducial cuts are applied. The results provide rigorous formal justification for existing recoil prescriptions used in the literature by Balazs (1995), Ellis (1997), Catani (2015), and others. The method enables improved \(q_T\) subtraction techniques for fixed-order calculations, particularly near kinematic edges where leptonic power corrections previously caused breakdowns.
