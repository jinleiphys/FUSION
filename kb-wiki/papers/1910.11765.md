# The properties of neutron star from realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction within relativistic Hartree-Fock model

> The properties of neutron star are studied in the framework of relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) model with realistic nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions, i.e. Bonn potentials. The strong repulsion of $NN$ interaction at short range is properly removed by the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Meanwhile, the tensor correlation is neglected due to the very rich neutron environment in neutron star, where the total isospin of two nucleons can be approximately regarded as $T=1$. The equations of state of neutron star matter are calculated in $β$ equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. The properties of neutron star, such as mass, radius, and tidal deformability are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation and tidal equation. The maximum masses of neutron from Bonn A, B, C potentials are around $2.2M_\odot$. The radius are $12.40-12.91$ km at $1.4M_\odot$, respectively. The corresponding tidal deformabilities are $Λ_{1.4}=293-355$. All of these properties are satisfied the recent observables from the astronomical and gravitational wave devices and are consistent with the results from the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model.

## Key claim
The relativistic Hartree-Fock model with unitary correlation operator method (RHFU), using Bonn potentials and neglecting tensor correlation due to the neutron-rich environment, produces neutron star properties consistent with observational constraints from GW170817 and pulsar measurements, with maximum masses around 2.2 solar masses.

## Method
The authors applied the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) to remove the short-range repulsion of realistic Bonn A, B, and C nucleon-nucleon potentials within a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) framework. They calculated equations of state for neutron star matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation and tidal equations to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability. Tensor correlation was neglected because the neutron-rich environment suppresses isospin T=0 contributions.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star masses from Bonn A, B, C potentials: around 2.2 solar masses
- Radii at 1.4 solar masses: 12.40 km (Bonn A), 12.62 km (Bonn B), 12.90 km (Bonn C)
- Tidal deformabilities at 1.4 solar masses: 292 (Bonn A), 318 (Bonn B), 354 (Bonn C)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density (Bonn A): 26.85 MeV (compared to empirical 31.7 +/- 3.2 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at 2 times saturation density (Bonn A): 54.00 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L: 96.48 MeV (Bonn A), 76.86 MeV (Bonn B), 47.19 MeV (Bonn C)
- Maximum second Love numbers: 0.142 (Bonn A), 0.131 (Bonn B), 0.123 (Bonn C)
- Central density at maximum mass: around 0.95 fm^{-3}
- Central density at 1.4 solar masses: 0.40 fm^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous relativistic central variational method (Hu 2011, Hu 2017) and RHF with UCOM (Hu 2010) to compute tidal deformabilities, connecting to the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott 2017). The results are consistent with the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model (Brockmann 1990, Krastev 2006) and satisfy constraints from pulsar measurements (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2019) and GW170817 (Abbott 2018). The approach enables systematic study of neutron star properties using realistic potentials while avoiding the computational complexity of full Brueckner calculations.
