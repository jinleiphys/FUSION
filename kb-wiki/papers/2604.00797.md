# Emergent Weyl Nodes and Berry Curvature in Bose Polarons via $p$-Wave Feshbach Coupling

> We show that an impurity quasiparticle immersed in a Bose-Einstein condensate, known as a Bose polaron, exhibits topological properties characterized by a nonzero Berry curvature, which is induced by Weyl nodes that emerge via interspecies $p$-wave Feshbach resonance. Such nodes occur even in the absence of spin degrees of freedom and spin-orbit coupling. For charged impurities, the corresponding $p$-wave polarons are shown to be accompanied by chiral anomaly. The above predictions can be tested in a cold atomic environment by observing the Hall transport of the atomic or ionic impurity cloud.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bose polaron (an impurity quasiparticle in a Bose-Einstein condensate) can exhibit topological properties, specifically nonzero Berry curvature and Weyl nodes, when the impurity-medium interaction is mediated by a $p$-wave Feshbach resonance, even without spin degrees of freedom, spin-orbit coupling, or a topologically nontrivial medium. The hedge is that this result holds only near a specific $\ell_z = +1$ resonance, with the off-resonant $\ell_z = 0$ and $-1$ channels neglected as higher-order corrections.

## Method

The authors construct a two-channel Hamiltonian for an impurity atom immersed in a Bose-Einstein condensate with $p$-wave Feshbach coupling that depends on the $z$-component of the orbital angular momentum ($\ell_z = 0, \pm 1$). They treat the condensate via a macroscopic occupation of the zero-momentum state and derive a leading-order effective Hamiltonian that reduces to a two-level system near the $\ell_z = +1$ resonance, expressed as a $2\times2$ matrix with a $d$-vector structure. From this effective Hamiltonian, they compute the Berry connection and Berry curvature, derive semiclassical equations of motion including an anomalous velocity, and calculate the mass Hall conductivity.

## Key numbers

- The Weyl point momentum is given by $p_{\rm W} = \sqrt{\frac{\nu_{+1} - \mu_b}{\frac{1}{2M_c} - \frac{1}{2M_A}}} \, \theta(\nu_{+1} - \mu_b)$, where $\nu_{+1}$ is the closed-channel molecular energy level for $\ell_z = +1$, $\mu_b$ is the boson chemical potential, and $M_c$, $M_A$ are impurity and molecule masses.
- The anomalous Hall velocity for small momenta is $v_{{\rm H},y} \simeq \kappa \frac{\zeta^2}{(\nu_{+1} - \mu_b)^2} F_x$, where $\zeta = \frac{M_b \sqrt{n_0} g_{+1}}{\sqrt{2} M_A}$ and $F_x$ is an applied force.
- For fermionic impurities at $T=0$ and $\mu_c = E_{\rm W}$, the mass Hall conductivity is $\sigma_{yx} = p_{\rm W} / 2\pi^2$.
- The numerical example uses $M_c/M_b = 6/52$ (relevant to a $^6$Li-$^{52}$Cr mixture) and $2M_c \zeta^2 / E_{\rm W} = 0.1$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $p$-wave Bose polarons (previously examined in PhysRevC.111.025802, PhysRevLett.109.075302, PhysRevA.100.062712) by revealing their topological properties, which were not explored in those earlier studies. It contrasts with prior approaches that required topological media (PhysRevA.99.033613, PhysRevB.99.081105, vashisht2025chiral) or artificial gauge fields (RevModPhys.83.1523) to induce topology in polaron systems. The results enable experimental tests via Hall transport of atomic or ionic impurity clouds in cold atom setups, and suggest potential for simulating chiral magnetic effects and topological flat-band superconductors.
