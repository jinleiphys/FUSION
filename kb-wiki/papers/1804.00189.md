# Non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes and equilibration of a baryon dense holographic QGP with a critical point

> We compute the homogeneous limit of non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes (QNM's) of a phenomenologically realistic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model for the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) that is able to: i) {\it quantitatively} describe state-of-the-art lattice results for the QCD equation of state and higher order baryon susceptibilities with $2+1$ flavors and physical quark masses up to highest values of the baryon chemical potential currently reached in lattice simulations; ii) describe the nearly perfect fluidity of the strongly coupled QGP produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions; iii) give a very good description of the bulk viscosity extracted via some recent Bayesian analyzes of hydrodynamical descriptions of heavy ion experimental data. This EMD model has been recently used to predict the location of the QCD critical point in the QCD phase diagram, which was found to be within the reach of upcoming low energy heavy ion collisions. The lowest quasinormal modes of the $SO(3)$ rotationally invariant quintuplet, triplet, and singlet channels evaluated in the present work provide upper bounds for characteristic equilibration times describing how fast the dense medium returns to thermal equilibrium after being subjected to small disturbances. We find that the equilibration times in the different channels come closer to each other at high temperatures, although being well separated at the critical point. Moreover, in most cases, these equilibration times decrease with increasing baryon chemical potential while keeping temperature fixed.

## Key claim

The lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes of the EMD holographic model provide upper bounds for characteristic equilibration times of the baryon-dense QGP after small disturbances, but these times do not account for earlier nonlinear stages of the evolution and should be seen as lower bounds for full relaxation times of the medium.

## Method

The authors computed the homogeneous (zero wavenumber) limit of non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes in the SO(3) quintuplet, triplet, and singlet channels by solving linearized perturbation equations on charged black hole backgrounds generated by the EMD model of Critelli et al. (2017). They used a shooting method with Dirichlet boundary conditions at the asymptotic boundary and regularity at the horizon to find the complex eigenfrequencies, then extracted equilibration times as minus the inverse of the imaginary part of the lowest mode in each channel.

## Key numbers

- The QCD critical point was predicted at (T^CEP, mu_B^CEP) ~ (89, 724) MeV, with a corresponding center-of-mass energy window sqrt(s_NN^CEP) ~ 2.5 to 4.1 GeV.
- At (T, mu_B) ~ (400, 0) MeV, (145, 0) MeV, and (89, 724) MeV, the equilibration time in the quintuplet channel was computed (specific numerical values from the text are not provided for these points; the text states reference values can be read from the figure but does not give explicit numbers in the visible portion).
- The EMD model parameters include kappa_5^2 = 8 pi (0.46) and Lambda = 1058.83 MeV.
- The sixth and eighth order baryon susceptibilities predicted by the EMD model were compared to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2018), showing good quantitative agreement.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes in holographic models from the conformal case studied by Critelli et al. (2017) to a phenomenologically realistic non-conformal EMD model that simultaneously describes lattice QCD thermodynamics, nearly perfect fluidity, and bulk viscosity. The results enable future studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics in the baryon-dense region of the QCD phase diagram, though the authors caution that the dynamical universality class of large N_c theories (type B) differs from that expected for QCD (type H), as discussed by Natsuume (2010) and Son (2004).
