# Nucleon to $Δ$ and $Δ$ form factors in Lattice QCD

> We present recent lattice QCD results on the electroweak nucleon to $Δ$ transition and $Δ$ form factors using dynamical fermion gauge configurations with a lowest pion mass of about 300 MeV, with special emphasis in the determination of the sub-dominant quadrupole $Nγ^*\rightarrow Δ$ and $Δ$ electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon to Delta transition and Delta form factors using dynamical fermion configurations with a lowest pion mass of about 300 MeV show that the sub-dominant quadrupole form factors can be extracted, but the results still underestimate experimental values at low momentum transfer, with the electric quadrupole ratio (EMR) showing better agreement with experiment than the Coulomb quadrupole ratio (CMR).

## Method

The authors computed two-point and three-point correlation functions on dynamical fermion gauge configurations using a hybrid action of staggered sea quarks and domain wall valence quarks, as well as Nf=2+1 dynamical domain wall fermions from the RBC-UKQCD collaboration. To extract the sub-dominant quadrupole form factors, they constructed optimized sources to isolate them from the dominant dipole contribution and used the coherent sink technique to increase statistics. The matrix elements were obtained by taking appropriate ratios of three-point to two-point functions at large source-sink separations.

## Key numbers

- Lowest pion mass used in the simulations: about 300 MeV
- The ratio G_pi N Delta(Q^2) f_pi / (2 m_N C_5^A(Q^2)) approaches unity for Q^2 greater than 0.5 GeV^2, indicating the Goldberger-Treiman relation holds in that region
- The slope of G*_M1 at low Q^2 remains smaller than experiment, underestimating G*_M1(0)
- Lattice results on the Delta electric quadrupole form factor G_E2 were obtained and used to compute transverse charge densities

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors (Alexandrou, 2010) to the nucleon to Delta transition and Delta resonance form factors, which are difficult to measure experimentally due to the Delta's short lifetime. The results enable combined chiral fits using chiral perturbation theory to extract the axial couplings g_A, c_A, and g_Delta from lattice data on nucleon and Delta axial charges and the axial N to Delta form factor C_5(0). The paper notes that current simulations reaching pion masses below 200 MeV are becoming available and are expected to help resolve discrepancies with experimental values.
