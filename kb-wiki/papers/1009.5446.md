# Effect of nuclear compressibility on the fragmentation in peripheral Au+Au collisions at 35 AMeV

> We studied the fragmentation in Au(35 AMeV)+Au collisions at reduced impact parameters in the range b/b_max=0.55 and 0.95 using soft and hard equations of state. The comparison of of QMD simulations at 100 fm/c as a function of reduced impact parameter $b/b_{max}$ with Multics Miniball data showed that soft EoS accurately reproduces the experimental trend of declining fragment multiplicity with impact parameter. The hard EoS on the contrary, seems too explosive to explain the data.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a soft equation of state (EoS) with compressibility κ = 200 MeV accurately reproduces the experimental trend of declining fragment multiplicity with impact parameter in peripheral Au+Au collisions at 35 AMeV, while a hard EoS (κ = 380 MeV) is too explosive to explain the data. This conclusion is conditional on the use of the QMD model with the SACA (2.1) clustering algorithm and comparison to preliminary experimental data from the Miniball-Multics array.

## Method
Quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations were performed for Au+Au collisions at 35 AMeV at reduced impact parameters b/b_max = 0.55 and 0.95, using both a soft EoS (κ = 200 MeV) and a hard EoS (κ = 380 MeV). The phase space of nucleons was generated via Hamilton's equations of motion and then clusterized using the SACA (2.1) algorithm, which checks the binding energy of each cluster against a liquid-drop model formula. Fragment multiplicities for clusters with charge 3 ≤ Z ≤ 80 were calculated at 100 fm/c and compared to experimental data from the Miniball-Multics array.

## Key numbers
- Soft EoS compressibility: κ = 200 MeV; hard EoS compressibility: κ = 380 MeV.
- Incident energy: T_lab = 35 AMeV for Au+Au collisions.
- Reduced impact parameters studied: b/b_max = 0.55 and 0.95.
- Fragment charge range analyzed: 3 ≤ Z ≤ 80.
- Simulation time for fragment observables: 100 fm/c.
- Saturation density of cold nuclei: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends previous QMD studies of fragmentation in the Fermi energy domain, such as those by Vermani et al. (earlier work) and the study of ¹⁶O+⁸⁰Br fragmentation at 50-200 AMeV by R.K. Puri and collaborators. The paper uses the SACA (2.1) clustering algorithm, an improved version of the simulated annealing clusterization method from earlier work by the same group. The results favor a soft nuclear EoS and enable further constraints on nuclear compressibility from peripheral heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.
