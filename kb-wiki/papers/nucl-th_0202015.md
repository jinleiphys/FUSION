# Toward a global description of the nucleus-nucleus interaction

> Extensive systematization of theoretical and experimental nuclear densities and of optical potential strengths exctracted from heavy-ion elastic scattering data analyses at low and intermediate energies are presented.The energy-dependence of the nuclear potential is accounted for within a model based on the nonlocal nature of the interaction.The systematics indicate that the heavy-ion nuclear potential can be described in a simple global way through a double-folding shape,which basically depends only on the density of nucleons of the partners in the collision.The poissibility of extracting information about the nucleon-nucleon interaction from the heavy-ion potential is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the heavy-ion nuclear potential can be described globally through a double-folding shape depending only on the nucleon densities of the collision partners, but this description requires accounting for the energy-dependence of the potential via a nonlocal model based on Pauli exchange effects, and the authors neglect the real part of the polarization potential in their analysis.

## Method

The authors performed extensive systematizations of theoretical (Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov) and experimental (electron scattering) nuclear densities, and of optical potential strengths extracted from heavy-ion elastic scattering data at low and intermediate energies. They used a nonlocal model for the bare interaction where the local-equivalent potential is related to the folding potential through an exponential factor depending on the kinetic energy and the range of Pauli nonlocality, and they fitted the reduced potential strengths to analytical expressions derived from a zero-range approximation of the double-folding model.

## Key numbers

- Average experimental charge distribution diffuseness: \(\bar{a}_c = 0.53\) fm with standard deviation 0.04 fm
- Average nucleon distribution diffuseness from DHB calculations: \(\bar{a}_N = 0.48\) fm with standard deviation 0.025 fm; more realistic average adopted: 0.50 fm
- Average matter density diffuseness: 0.56 fm (from DHB calculations, with dispersion \(\sigma_a \approx 0.025\) fm)
- Linear fit for nucleon/matter density radius: \(R_0 = 1.31 A^{1/3} - 0.84\) fm, with dispersion \(\sigma_{R_0} = 0.07\) fm
- Best-fit parameters from reduced potential systematization: \(V_0 = -456\) MeV fm\(^3\) and diffuseness \(a = 0.56\) fm
- Standard deviation of reduced potential data around best fit: 25%
- Without nonlocality correction: \(V_0 = -274\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(a = 0.61\) fm
- Empirical nucleon-nucleon interaction volume integral: \(V_0 \approx -430\) MeV fm\(^3\) and RMS radius \(\approx 1.5\) fm (from nucleon-nucleus scattering at \(E_{\text{nucleon}} = 10\) MeV)
- Pauli nonlocality range parameter for nucleon-nucleus: \(b_0 = 0.85\) fm

## Context

This work extends previous systematizations of heavy-ion potential strengths at the surface region by Christensen and Winther (1981) and others, which found an exponential shape with diffuseness \(\alpha \approx 0.62\) fm, smaller than the proximity potential prediction of \(\alpha \approx 0.75\) fm from Blocki et al. (1977). The paper provides a unified description covering both surface and inner distances by incorporating the nonlocal model of Refs. [ref11,ref12,ref13] to account for energy-dependence. This enables a global parametrization of the nucleus-nucleus interaction that can be used in DWBA or coupled-channel calculations, and also allows investigation of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction from heavy-ion potential data.
