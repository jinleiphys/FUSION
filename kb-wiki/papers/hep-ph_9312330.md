# Quark-Gluon Plasma Freeze-out from a Supercooled State?

> We consider time-scales of first-order deconfinement or chiral-symmetry restoring phase transition in high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Recently it was shown that the system must supercool below $T_c$ before the nucleation of hadronic bubbles is sufficiently rapid to overcome the expansion rate. It is shown here that the expected time-scales of high energy heavy ion reactions are sufficiently short to prevent the reheating of the system to near $T_c$. If quark-gluon plasma is produced in these collisions, it may have to hadronize from a supercooled state and the hadrons produced during rehadronization may freeze-out almost immediately.

## Key claim

If a quark-gluon plasma is produced in high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, the expected time-scales are sufficiently short to prevent reheating of the system to near the critical temperature \(T_c\), so the plasma may have to hadronize from a supercooled state and the hadrons produced may freeze-out almost immediately.

## Method

The authors compare the time-scales of first-order deconfinement or chiral-symmetry restoring phase transitions, as calculated from homogeneous nucleation theory for a longitudinally expanding Bjorken tube, with freeze-out times estimated from extrapolations of Bose-Einstein correlation function (HBT) data at CERN SPS energies to RHIC and LHC. They also analyze relativistic timelike deflagrations using the Taub adiabat, Rayleigh-line, and Poisson-adiabat to show that a sudden transition from a supercooled quark-gluon plasma to a hadron gas can satisfy energy-momentum conservation with non-decreasing entropy.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic nucleation time for a longitudinally expanding gas of gluons and massless quarks rehadronizing into a massless pion gas is about 100 fm/c.
- For an initial temperature \(T_0 = 2 T_c\) at time \(t_0 = 3/8\) fm/c, the plasma cools to \(T_c\) at \(t_c = 3\) fm/c, reaches a bottom temperature \(T_b = 0.8 T_c\) at \(t_b = 7\) fm/c, and the transition completes at \(t_{compl} = 50\) fm/c.
- In spherically expanding systems, the bottom temperature is \(T_b = 0.7-0.9 T_c\) achieved at \(t_b = 7\) to 10 fm/c.
- The freeze-out proper-time at CERN SPS for \(^{32}S + ^{238}U\) collisions is \(t_f = 4.5 - 6.5\) fm/c.
- Extrapolated freeze-out times are 6-10 fm/c for CERN SPS lead on lead, 8-13 fm/c for RHIC gold on gold, and 11-16 fm/c for LHC lead on lead collisions.
- The bag constant is \(B^{1/4} = 235\) MeV, the critical temperature is \(T_c = 169\) MeV, and the pressure of the supercooled QGP vanishes at \(T = 0.98 T_c\).
- The temperature of the system in the supercooled phase reaches \(T = 0.7-0.9 T_c \simeq 120 - 150\) MeV.
- For a pure gluonic plasma, the ratio of degrees of freedom is \(r = 16/3\); for a quark-gluon plasma with three quark flavors it is \(r = 37/3\).

## Context

This work extends the nucleation calculations of the authors' previous work (Csorgo and Csernai, 1993) and the detailed dynamical simulations of bubble formation and growth in expanding systems (Csernai and Kapusta, 1992; Zsenya, 1993). It contradicts the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann equilibrium phase transition picture by arguing that the finite nucleation rate and short expansion time-scales prevent reheating, leading to a supercooled state. The paper enables future studies of sudden rehadronization mechanisms such as timelike deflagrations, and suggests that experimental signatures like vanishing side-out HBT radius differences, free resonance masses in dilepton spectra, and enhanced strange antibaryon production (as observed by the WA85 collaboration at CERN SPS) could indicate this scenario.
