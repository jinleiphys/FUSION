# Neutrino collective excitations in the Standard Model at high temperature

> Neutrino collective excitations are studied in the Standard Model at high temperatures below the symmetry breaking scale. Two parameters determine the properties of the collective excitations: a mass scale $m_ν=gT/4$ which determines the \emph{chirally symmetric} gaps in the spectrum and $Δ=M^2_W(T)/2m_νT$. The spectrum consists of left handed negative helicity quasiparticles, left handed positive helicity quasiholes and their respective antiparticles. For $Δ< Δ_c = 1.275...$ there are two gapped quasiparticle branches and one gapless and two gapped quasihole branches, all but the higher gapped quasiparticle branches terminate at end points. For $Δ_c < Δ< π/2$ the quasiparticle spectrum features a pitchfork bifurcation and for $Δ>π/2$ the collective modes are gapless quasiparticles with dispersion relation below the light cone for $k\ll m_ν$ approaching the free field limit for $k\gg m_ν$ with a rapid crossover between the soft non-perturbative to the hard perturbative regimes for $k\sim m_ν$.The \emph{decay} of the vector bosons leads to a \emph{width} of the collective excitations of order $g^2$ which is explicitly obtained in the limits $k =0$ and $k\gg m_νΔ$. At high temperature this damping rate is shown to be competitive with or larger than the collisional damping rate of order $G^2_F$ for a wide range of neutrino energy.

## Key claim

Neutrino collective excitations at high temperatures below the electroweak scale exhibit a spectrum that depends on two parameters (a mass scale \(m_\nu = gT/4\) and \(\Delta = M_W^2(T)/2m_\nu T\)) and features qualitatively different branches, including gapped and gapless quasiparticles and quasiholes, depending on whether \(\Delta\) is below, between, or above the critical values \(\Delta_c = 1.275\ldots\) and \(\pi/2\); the damping rate from vector boson decay is of order \(g^2\) and can exceed the collisional damping rate of order \(G_F^2\) for a wide range of neutrino energy.

## Method

The author computed the one-loop retarded neutrino self-energy in the Standard Model at high temperature (below the symmetry breaking scale) using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, including both charged and neutral current contributions from vector boson exchange in unitary gauge. The effective Dirac equation for the neutrino field expectation value was derived via a real-time linear response approach, and the dispersion relations and damping rates of collective excitations were obtained from the poles of the propagator for left-handed fields. The analysis assumed a CP-symmetric thermal bath, neglected fermion masses, and considered the regime \(T \gg M_{W,Z}(T) \gg gT\) where perturbation theory and the HTL approximation are reliable.

## Key numbers

* The mass scale that determines chirally symmetric gaps is \(m_\nu = gT/4\).
* The dimensionless parameter controlling the spectrum is \(\Delta = M_W^2(T) / (2 m_\nu T)\).
* The critical value separating different spectral regimes is \(\Delta_c = 1.275\ldots\).
* For \(\Delta < \Delta_c\) there are two gapped quasiparticle branches and one gapless plus two gapped quasihole branches.
* For \(\Delta_c < \Delta < \pi/2\) the quasiparticle spectrum features a pitchfork bifurcation.
* For \(\Delta > \pi/2\) only a gapless quasiparticle branch remains, with dispersion below the light cone for \(k \ll m_\nu\) and approaching the free field limit for \(k \gg m_\nu \Delta\).
* The damping rate from vector boson decay is of order \(g^2\) and is explicitly obtained in the limits \(k = 0\) and \(k \gg m_\nu \Delta\).
* At high temperature this damping rate is competitive with or larger than the collisional damping rate of order \(G_F^2\) for a wide range of neutrino energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino dispersion relations and damping rates, previously carried out for temperatures up to about 1–10 MeV relevant for stellar evolution and big bang nucleosynthesis (Notzold, 1988; Barbieri, 1990; Enqvist, 1991; Dolgov, 1997), to the high-temperature regime near the electroweak scale, where vector bosons are abundant and their decay provides a novel damping mechanism. The results differ qualitatively from the fermionic collective excitations in high-temperature QCD and QED (Klimov, 1981; Weldon, 1982; Pisarski, 1989; Le Bellac, 1996) because of the presence of the vector boson mass scale \(M_W(T)\). The findings enable more refined studies of thermal leptogenesis and non-equilibrium neutrino dynamics in the early universe, including the possibility that the one-loop damping rate dominates over the collisional damping rate for soft momenta.
