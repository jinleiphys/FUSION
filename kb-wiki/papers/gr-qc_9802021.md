# Singular shells of quark-gluon matter

> The spherically symmetric thin shells of the macroscopically stable quark-gluon matter are considered within the frameworks of the bag model and theory of discontinuities in general relativity. The equation of state for the two-dimensional matter is suggested, and its features are discussed. The exact equations of motion of such shells are obtained. Distinguishing the two cases, circumstellar and microscopical shells, we calculate the parameters of equilibrium configurations, including the conditions of decay (deconfinement).

## Key claim

Spherically symmetric thin shells of macroscopically stable quark-gluon matter can exist as independent relativistic objects, but only if the bag constant exceeds a critical value \(B_c \approx 83.33\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\); below this threshold the shells decay (deconfine), and the paper's quantitative estimates for microscopic shells are acknowledged as "sufficiently conditional" because the classical spherical shell model neglects spins, charges, quantum fluctuations, and pair creation.

## Method

The author models a thin shell of quark-gluon matter as a singular hypersurface in general relativity, using the Lichnerowicz-Darmois-Israel junction conditions to match interior and exterior spherically symmetric spacetimes. An effective two-dimensional equation of state is derived from the MIT bag model's three-dimensional equation of state via dimensional reduction, yielding surface energy density and pressure as functions of radius. The equations of motion are obtained and solved in two regimes: the M-approximation for microscopic shells (radius much larger than horizon radius) and the A-approximation for astronomical shells (large mass and radius, neglecting the quasicharge term).

## Key numbers

- Critical bag constant: \(B_c \approx 83.33\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) (derived from QCD vacuum energy density \(\varepsilon_v = -500\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) under full screening of the QCD vacuum)
- For \(B = 83.334\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\): equilibrium radius \(R_0 = 18.8\) (dimensionless units), mass amplification \(\Delta M = 0.48\, m_p\) (proton masses)
- For \(B = 86.7\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\): \(R_0 = 2.25\), \(\Delta M = 4.13\, m_p\)
- For \(B = 91.5\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\): \(R_0 = 1.83\), \(\Delta M = 5.37\, m_p\)
- For an astronomical shell around a strange star of mass \(M_{\text{star}} = 1.63 M_\odot\) and bag constant \(B = 86.7\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\): equilibrium shell radius \(R_0 \approx 32\ \text{km}\) (more than 3.6 times the stellar radius of \(8.82\ \text{km}\))
- MIT bag constant reference value: \(B_{\text{MIT}} = 67\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), corresponding to QGP energy density \(\varepsilon_{\text{QGP}} = 4.3 \times 10^{35}\ \text{erg}\,\text{cm}^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the bag model of quark-gluon matter (MIT bag model by Chodos et al. (1974), Witten (1984)) into general relativity by treating the bag surface as a singular thin shell rather than a boundary of first kind, building on the theory of surface layers by Darmois (1927) and Israel (1966). The paper introduces a critical bag constant \(B_c\) that determines whether such shells can exist in equilibrium, and it enables future study of quantum properties of these shells and their possible role in the quark-hadron phase transition in the early Universe (as discussed by Witten (1984) and others).
