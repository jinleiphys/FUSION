# Recent ALICE results from light-ion collision systems

> This article presents recent measurements by the ALICE Collaboration in proton--oxygen (pO), oxygen--oxygen (OO), and neon--neon (Ne--Ne) collisions delivered by the LHC in July 2025. Measurements of the primary charged-particle pseudorapidity density and the elliptic and triangular flow coefficients of charged particles are reported. Experimental evidence of the suppression of neutral pion yields in OO collisions relative to the proton--proton baseline is also discussed. Comparisons of these new data with theoretical models provide key input to understand particle production, collective phenomena, and parton energy loss in small collision systems.

# Recent ALICE results from light-ion collision systems

## Key claim
The paper reports that neutral pion yields in oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions are suppressed relative to the proton-proton baseline with a significance of 2.4 sigma when compared to the most suppressed cold nuclear matter prediction (EPPS21), suggesting parton energy loss effects beyond cold nuclear matter contributions, but the interpretation depends on the assumed nuclear parton distribution function baseline.

## Method
The ALICE Collaboration measured primary charged-particle pseudorapidity density using the Inner Tracking System and Time Projection Chamber at midrapidity in proton-oxygen (pO), oxygen-oxygen (OO), and neon-neon (Ne-Ne) collisions at the LHC. Elliptic and triangular flow coefficients were extracted from two- and multiparticle cumulants with a pseudorapidity separation of |Delta-eta|>1.4 to suppress nonflow correlations. Neutral pion yields were reconstructed via the two-photon decay channel using the Electromagnetic Calorimeter, and the nuclear modification factor R_OO was computed by comparing OO yields to the proton-proton baseline scaled by the nuclear overlap function.

## Key numbers
- Charged-particle pseudorapidity density averaged over |eta|<0.5 in OO collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.36 TeV: 129.7 +/- 4.1 (syst) for 0-5% central collisions, 11.7 +/- 1.2 (syst) for 60-90% peripheral collisions
- Systematic uncertainty on dN_ch/deta: 3.5% for central collisions, 10% for peripheral collisions across all colliding systems
- Systematic uncertainties on flow coefficients v_n{2} and v_n{4}: up to 6.5% for both OO and Ne-Ne collisions
- Neutral pion suppression significance: 2.4 sigma when compared with the most suppressed cold nuclear matter prediction (EPPS21)
- Neutral pion pT range measured: 1.2 < pT < 20 GeV/c at midrapidity |y|<0.8
- Normalised multiplicity (2/<N_part>)<dN_ch/deta> decreases by a factor of approximately 1.7 from central to peripheral collisions

## Context
This work extends ALICE measurements from proton-proton and proton-lead collisions (ALICE Collaboration, 2022) to light-ion systems (oxygen and neon) at the LHC, addressing the open question of whether jet quenching occurs in small collision systems. The flow measurements are compared with hydrodynamic simulations from the Trajectum framework using nuclear structure inputs from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (Lee, 2025) and the ab initio Projected Generator Coordinate Method (Frosini, 2021). The neutral pion suppression results provide the first experimental evidence of parton energy loss in light-ion collisions, and future measurements in pO collisions will help disentangle cold nuclear matter effects from hot-medium energy loss.
