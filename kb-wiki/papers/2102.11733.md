# alpha clustering and neutron-skin thickness of carbon isotopes

> The interplay between the formation of neutron skin and alpha cluster at the dilute surface of neutron-rich nuclei is one of the interesting subjects in the study of neutron-rich nuclei and nuclear clustering. A theoretical model has predicted that the growth of neutron skin will prevent the alpha clustering at the nuclear surface. Quite recently, this theoretical perspective; the suppression of alpha clustering by the neutron-skin formation was first confirmed experimentally in Sn isotopes as the reduction of the (p, p alpha) reaction cross-section. Motivated by the novel discovery, in this work, we have investigated the relationship between the neutron-skin thickness and alpha clustering in C isotopes. Based on the analysis by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, we show that the alpha spectroscopic factor at nuclear exterior decreases in neutron-rich C isotopes, and the clustering suppression looks correlated with the growth of the neutron-skin thickness.

## Key claim

The authors show that the alpha spectroscopic factor at the nuclear exterior decreases in neutron-rich carbon isotopes and that this suppression appears correlated with the growth of neutron-skin thickness, but they also note that the neutron shell effect may be the real cause of the suppression rather than the neutron skin itself.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S density functional to calculate the wave functions of Be and C isotopes. They then applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) using the deformation parameter beta as the generator coordinate to obtain the ground and excited states. The alpha reduced width amplitudes (RWA) and spectroscopic factors were evaluated from the GCM wave functions using the Laplace expansion method, with the exterior spectroscopic factor defined as the integral of the RWA beyond the root-mean-square matter radius.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness Delta r for carbon isotopes: 0.00 fm (12C), 0.05 fm (14C), 0.23 fm (16C), 0.33 fm (18C)
- Total alpha spectroscopic factor S_alpha(0+_1 x 0) for carbon isotopes: 0.30 (12C), 0.10 (14C), 0.05 (16C), 0.04 (18C)
- Exterior alpha spectroscopic factor S>_alpha(0+_1 x 0) for carbon isotopes: 0.24 (12C), 0.08 (14C), 0.04 (16C), 0.04 (18C)
- Matter distribution radii sqrt(<r_m^2>) for carbon isotopes: 2.52 fm (12C), 2.57 fm (14C), 2.74 fm (16C), 2.87 fm (18C)
- B(E2 uparrow) values for carbon isotopes: 11.2 e^2 fm^4 (12C), 1.4 e^2 fm^4 (14C), 5.6 e^2 fm^4 (16C), 4.7 e^2 fm^4 (18C)

## Context

This work extends the experimental discovery by Tanaka (2021) that the (p,p alpha) knockout cross section monotonically decreases in neutron-rich Sn isotopes, indicating suppression of alpha clustering by neutron-skin formation. The authors apply the same question to light carbon isotopes, building on prior AMD studies of Be and C isotopes by Kanada-Enyo (2012) and Kimura (2016), as well as detailed alpha clustering studies of 12C by Tohsaki (2001) and Funaki (2003). The results enable future systematic studies of other isotope chains such as O, Ne, and Mg to disentangle whether the suppression is caused by neutron skin or by neutron shell effects.
