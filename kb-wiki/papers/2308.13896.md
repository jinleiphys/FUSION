# Establishing connection between neutron star properties and nuclear matter parameters through a comprehensive multivariate analysis

> We have attempted to mitigate the challenge of connecting the neutron star (NS) properties with the nuclear matter parameters that describe equations of state (EoSs). The efforts to correlate various neutron star properties with individual nuclear matter parameters have been inconclusive. A Principal Component Analysis is employed as a tool to uncover the connection between multiple nuclear matter parameters and the tidal deformability as well as the radius of neutron stars within the mass range of $1.2-1.8M_\odot$. The essential EOSs for neutron star matter at low densities have been derived using both uncorrelated uniform distributions and minimally constrained joint posterior distributions of nuclear matter parameters. For higher densities ($ρ> 0.32$fm$^{-3}$), the EOSs have been established through a suitable parameterization of the speed of sound, which consistently maintains causality and gradually approaches the conformal limit. Our analysis reveals that in order to account for over 90\% of the variability in NS properties, it is crucial to consider two or more principal components, emphasizing the significance of employing multivariate analysis. To explain the variability in tidal deformability needs a greater number of principal components compared to those for the radius at a given NS mass. The contributions from iso-vector nuclear matter parameters to the tidal deformability and radius of NS decrease by $\sim$ 25\% with the increase in mass of NS from 1.2$M_\odot$ to 1.8$M_\odot$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multivariate approach (Principal Component Analysis) is necessary to connect neutron star properties with nuclear matter parameters, because single-parameter correlations are inconclusive; specifically, to account for over 90% of the variability in neutron star properties, two or more principal components are required, and the number needed depends on the neutron star mass and whether tidal deformability or radius is considered.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state (EoSs) for neutron star matter using two different distributions of nuclear matter parameters: uncorrelated uniform distributions (D1) and minimally constrained joint posterior distributions (D2) obtained from Bayesian inference incorporating experimental nuclear data and chiral effective field theory pure neutron matter EoS. For densities below 0.32 fm$^{-3}$ (approximately twice saturation density), the EoS was built using a Taylor expansion in terms of six nuclear matter parameters; for higher densities, a parameterized speed-of-sound model was used that maintains causality and approaches the conformal limit. Principal Component Analysis was then applied to the linear fits of tidal deformability and radius as functions of the nuclear matter parameters for neutron stars in the mass range 1.2–1.8 $M_\odot$.

## Key numbers

* The analysis used 10,000 samples of nuclear matter parameters for each distribution (D1 and D2), with about 2,500 samples surviving filters for thermodynamic stability, causality, and maximum mass above 2 $M_\odot$.
* The correlation coefficient between $L_0$ and $K_{\rm sym,0}$ in the D2 distribution was r = 0.80.
* The $\mathcal{R}^2$ values for fitted neutron star properties at 2 $M_\odot$ fell below 0.9, motivating the mass range limit of 1.2–1.8 $M_\odot$ for the multivariate analysis.
* For tidal deformability at 1.2 $M_\odot$ in both D1 and D2 cases, at least four principal components were needed to achieve over 94% and 97% variance, respectively; for radius at 1.2 $M_\odot$, only three principal components were sufficient to achieve more than 94% and 98% variance.
* The contributions from iso-vector nuclear matter parameters to tidal deformability and radius decreased by approximately 25% as neutron star mass increased from 1.2 $M_\odot$ to 1.8 $M_\odot$.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts that found only weak or moderate correlations between individual nuclear matter parameters and neutron star properties such as tidal deformability and radius (Carson 2018, Malik 2018, Tsang 2019, Pradhan 2022). It builds on studies that established empirical relationships between radius and tidal deformability with multiple nuclear matter parameters (Imam 2023, Richter 2023). The multivariate approach enables a more complete understanding of how different nuclear matter parameters collectively determine neutron star observables, which is essential for interpreting gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers (GW170817) and X-ray observations from NICER.
