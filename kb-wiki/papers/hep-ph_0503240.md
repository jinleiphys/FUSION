# Nonperturbative renormalization for 2PI effective action techniques

> Nonperturbative approximation schemes based on two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective actions provide an important means for our current understanding of (non-)equilibrium quantum field theory. A remarkable property is their renormalizability, since these approximations involve selective summations to infinite perturbative orders. In this paper we show how to renormalize all $n$-point functions of the theory, which are given by derivatives of the 2PI-resummed effective action $Γ[ϕ]$ for scalar fields $ϕ$. This provides a complete description in terms of the generating functional for renormalized proper vertices, which extends previous prescriptions in the literature on the renormalization for 2PI effective actions. The importance of the 2PI-resummed generating functional for proper vertices stems from the fact that the latter respect all symmetry properties of the theory and, in particular, Goldstone's theorem in the phase with spontaneous symmetry breaking. This is important in view of the application of these techniques to gauge theories, where Ward identities play a crucial role.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that all n-point functions derived from the 2PI-resummed effective action for scalar field theories can be renormalized using a finite number of local counterterms, extending previous prescriptions that only addressed the renormalization of the two-point function. The renormalization procedure is medium-independent, meaning no temperature, chemical potential, or time-dependence is hidden in the counterterms.

## Method

The authors construct all n-point functions of the theory as functional derivatives of the 2PI-resummed effective action, expressing them in terms of 2PI kernels and a Bethe-Salpeter-type vertex function that resums infinite series of ladder diagrams. They show that when expressed in terms of this vertex function, the n-point functions involve only a finite number of contributions, which simplifies the analysis of ultraviolet singularities. The renormalization is performed by adjusting a finite number of local counterterms in the symmetric phase with vanishing field expectation value, and they demonstrate that no new divergences appear for non-vanishing field expectation values.

## Key numbers

- The paper considers a four-dimensional Z2-symmetric scalar field theory with phi^4 interaction, using a 2PI effective action framework
- The Bethe-Salpeter-type equation for the vertex function V_{12,34} is given as: V_{12,34} = Lambda_{12,34} + (i/2) Lambda_{12,ab} G_{ac} G_{db} V_{cd,34}
- The self-energy is defined as Sigma_{12} = 2i delta(Gamma_inter)/delta(G_{21}) evaluated at the physical propagator G

## Context

This work extends the renormalization prescriptions of van Hees and Knoll (2001) and Blaizot, Iancu, and Reinosa (2003), which only addressed the renormalization of the two-point function in 2PI approximations for scalar phi^4 theories. The paper provides the missing prescription for renormalizing all proper vertices encoded in the 2PI-resummed effective action, which is crucial for applications to gauge theories where Ward identities play a central role. The techniques demonstrated here for scalar theories can be applied to more complicated systems including fermionic or gauge degrees of freedom, and to higher nPI effective actions.
