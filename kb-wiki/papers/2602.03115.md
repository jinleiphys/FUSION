# Extraction of the pion-nucleon coupling constant using the effective-range expansion with the left-hand cut

> We apply the generalized effective-range expansion of Phys. Rev. Lett. 135, 011903(2025), which incorporates the left-hand cut from one-pion exchange, to low-energy neutron-proton scattering in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels. The amplitude zero for the center-of-mass momentum near 0.35 GeV in the $^1S_0$ channel is naturally accommodated within this framework. We extract the pole position, scattering length, effective range, and the pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{πN}^2/(4π)$ at different expansion orders. The low-energy parameters are stable and consistent with established values, while $g_{πN}^2/(4π)$ exhibits larger uncertainties. The extraction of $g_{πN}^2/(4π)$ is data-driven, relying on the analytic constraints from the left-hand cut and phase-shift data within the one-pion-exchange approximation. Despite larger uncertainties compared to high-precision extractions, the consistency with established values demonstrates that this framework can probe the left-hand-cut singularity.

# Extraction of the pion-nucleon coupling constant using the effective-range expansion with the left-hand cut

## Key claim
The generalized effective-range expansion that incorporates the left-hand cut from one-pion exchange can extract the pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling constant from neutron-proton scattering phase-shift data, but the extracted coupling exhibits larger uncertainties than high-precision determinations and its central values tend to be smaller than several recent high-precision results around 14, indicating residual contributions beyond the one-pion-exchange left-hand cut are needed.

## Method
The authors applied the generalized effective-range expansion of Du et al. (2025), which incorporates the left-hand cut from one-pion exchange via an N/D method, to low-energy neutron-proton scattering phase shifts from Stoks (1993) in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels. They performed fits at different polynomial expansion orders, including separate channel-by-channel fits and a joint analysis constrained by a common pion-nucleon coupling constant and the bound coherent scattering length from Schoen (2003). The pseudoscalar coupling constant was extracted from the relation between the generalized ERE parameter $\tilde{g}$ and the one-pion-exchange potential.

## Key numbers
- Virtual pole in $^1S_0$ channel: $-66.33(4)$ keV (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- Bound pole in $^3S_1$ channel: $-2208(4)$ keV (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- $^1S_0$ scattering length: $23.73(1)$ fm (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- $^3S_1$ scattering length: $-5.418(2)$ fm (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- $^1S_0$ effective range: $2.68(1)$ fm (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- $^3S_1$ effective range: $1.741(3)$ fm (joint fit, $f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- Pseudoscalar coupling constant from joint fit: $g_{\pi N}^2/(4\pi) = 12.2(1.4)$ ($f_{[2,1]}$/$f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- Pseudoscalar coupling constant from $^1S_0$ only fit: $g_{\pi N}^2/(4\pi) = 12.4_{-2.3}^{+2.2}$ ($f_{[1,2]}$ parametrization)
- Pseudoscalar coupling constant from $^3S_1$ only fit: $g_{\pi N}^2/(4\pi) = 18.3_{-8.6}^{+8.4}$ ($f_{[2,1]}$ parametrization)
- Amplitude zero in $^1S_0$ channel occurs at center-of-mass momentum near 0.35 GeV
- Bound coherent scattering length used as constraint: $a_c = 3.7384(20)$ fm from Schoen (2003)

## Context
This work extends the generalized effective-range expansion framework of Du et al. (2025), previously applied to $DD^*$ scattering at unphysical pion masses, to physical neutron-proton scattering. It provides a data-driven extraction of the pion-nucleon coupling constant that relies on analytic constraints from the one-pion-exchange left-hand cut, in contrast to high-precision determinations from partial-wave analyses using the Nijmegen potential (Stoks 1993, Klomp 1991), chiral effective-field-theory (Reinert 2020), and dispersion relations (Arndt 2006, Baru 2011). The results enable future matching of the generalized ERE to chiral effective field theory in the region before the opening of the two-pion-exchange left-hand cut.
