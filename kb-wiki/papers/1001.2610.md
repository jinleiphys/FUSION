# Particle Ratios and the QCD Critical Temperature

> We show how the measured particle ratios at RHIC can be used to provide non-trivial information about the critical temperature of the QCD phase transition. This is obtained by including the effects of highly massive Hagedorn resonances on statistical models, which are used to describe hadronic yields. Hagedorn states are relevant close to $T_c$ and have been shown to decrease $η/s$ to the KSS limit and allow for quick chemical equilibrium times in dynamical calculations of hadrons. The inclusion of Hagedorn states creates a dependence of the thermal fits on the Hagedorn temperature, $T_H$, which is assumed to be equal to $T_c$, and leads to an overall improvement of thermal fits. We find that for Au+Au collisions at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV the best square fit measure, $χ^2$, occurs at $T_c \sim 176$ MeV and produces a chemical freeze-out temperature of 170.4 MeV and a baryon chemical potential of 27.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The measured particle ratios at RHIC can be used to infer the QCD critical temperature by including Hagedorn resonances in thermal fits, under the assumption that the Hagedorn temperature equals the critical temperature. The best fit occurs at a critical temperature of approximately 176 MeV, but this result depends on which lattice QCD critical temperature is used as input.

## Method

The authors used a grand-canonical statistical model including an exponentially increasing density of Hagedorn states above 2 GeV, with volume corrections for repulsive interactions. They varied the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential to minimize chi-squared for six hadron yield ratios measured by STAR and PHENIX in Au+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, comparing fits with and without Hagedorn states for two different lattice QCD critical temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Best chi-squared of 17.8 obtained for T_c = 176 MeV (BMW lattice results), yielding chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch = 172.6 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_b = 39.7 MeV
- For T_c = 196 MeV (RBC-Bielefeld lattice results), chi-squared = 20.9, T_ch = 165.9 MeV, mu_b = 25.3 MeV
- Without Hagedorn states, chi-squared = 21.2, T_ch = 160.4 MeV, mu_b = 22.9 MeV
- Hagedorn state contribution to particle yields was 30-40% for T_H = 176 MeV versus 4-11% for T_H = 196 MeV

## Context

This work extends statistical model fits to hadron yields, previously performed by Andronic (2005) and Manninen (2008), by incorporating Hagedorn states that had been shown to reduce eta/s to the KSS bound and enable rapid chemical equilibration (Noronha-Hostler 2008, Greiner 2004). The results enable using thermal fits to discriminate between different lattice QCD predictions for the critical temperature, and demonstrate that extracted chemical freeze-out parameters depend significantly on whether Hagedorn states are included.
