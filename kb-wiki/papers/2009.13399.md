# Chemical freeze-out systematics of thermal model analysis using hadron yield ratios

> We provide a framework to estimate the systematic uncertainties in chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from $χ^2$ analysis of thermal model, using hadron multiplicity ratios in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. Using a well known technique of graph theory, we construct all possible sets of independent ratios from available hadron yields and perform $χ^2$ minimization on each set. We show that even for ten hadron yields, one obtains a large number ($10^8$) of independent sets which results in a distribution of extracted freeze-out parameters. We analyze these distributions and compare our results for chemical freeze-out parameters and associated systematic uncertainties with previous results available in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper provides a framework to estimate systematic uncertainties in chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature and chemical potentials) extracted from thermal model fits to hadron yield ratios, showing that even for ten hadron yields there are about 10^8 independent ratio sets, and that the choice of which set to use introduces a bias that can be quantified by considering all possible independent sets.

## Method

The authors used graph theory to enumerate all possible independent sets of hadron yield ratios, establishing a one-to-one correspondence between independent ratio sets and spanning trees on N labeled vertices (Cayley's formula gives N^(N-2) such trees). For N=10 hadron yields, they generated all 10^8 independent sets of nine ratios and performed chi-squared minimization on each set using the Hadron Resonance Gas model, fitting to experimental data from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and LHC at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They constructed histograms of the extracted freeze-out parameters and fitted Gaussian distributions to obtain central values and systematic uncertainties from the full width at half maximum.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), Model-I freeze-out temperature T = 0.169 +/- 0.003 GeV
- For RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), Model-I baryon chemical potential mu_B = 0.028 +/- 0.010 GeV
- For LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), Model-I freeze-out temperature T = 0.155 +/- 0.003 GeV
- For LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), Model-I baryon chemical potential mu_B = 0.002 +/- 0.009 GeV
- For RHIC, the reduced chi-squared distribution peaks at chi^2/NDF approximately 1
- For LHC, the reduced chi-squared distribution peaks at chi^2/NDF approximately 2.2
- The total number of independent sets for N=10 hadron yields is 10^8

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Bhattacharyya et al. (2019) which considered only nine independent sets for freeze-out parameter extraction, by now enumerating all possible independent sets to remove residual bias from set selection. The results are compared with previous thermal model analyses by Andronic et al. (2005, 2017) and the STAR Beam Energy Scan collaboration (2017), showing generally consistent central values but providing quantitative systematic uncertainties from the fitting procedure itself. The framework enables future studies to properly account for systematic uncertainties in chemical freeze-out parameter extraction from hadron yield ratios, and the authors note that the relatively large reduced chi-squared at LHC (approximately 2.2) suggests the thermal model with conserved charge constraints may need further refinement for LHC data.
