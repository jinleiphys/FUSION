# Forward-backward correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions: baseline contributions from geometrical fluctuations

> We discuss the effects of initial collision geometry and centrality bin definition on correlation and fluctuation observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We focus on the forward-backward correlation coefficient recently measured by the STAR Collaboration in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Our study is carried out within two models: the Glauber Monte Carlo code with a `toy' wounded nucleon model and the hadron-string dynamics (HSD) transport approach. We show that strong correlations can arise due to averaging over events in one centrality bin. We, furthermore, argue that a study of the dependence of correlations on the centrality bin definition as well as the bin size may distinguish between these `trivial' correlations and correlations arising from `new physics'.

## Key claim

The forward-backward correlation coefficient measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be largely explained by "trivial" geometrical fluctuations, specifically, averaging over events with different system sizes within a single centrality bin, without invoking new physics such as a quark-gluon plasma or color glass condensate. The authors argue that the dependence of correlations on centrality bin definition and bin size may distinguish these geometrical baseline contributions from genuine dynamical correlations.

## Method

The study uses two models: a Glauber Monte Carlo (GMC) code with a "toy" wounded nucleon model where particles are distributed independently in pseudorapidity, and the hadron-string dynamics (HSD) transport approach which includes hadronic re-interactions and string fragmentation but no new physics. For each model, the forward-backward correlation coefficient ρ is computed for charged particle multiplicities in two symmetric pseudorapidity intervals separated by a gap η_gap, using centrality bins defined via impact parameter b, number of participating nucleons N_P, or reference charged multiplicity N_ch^ref in |η|<1.

## Key numbers

- The GMC model uses a nucleon-nucleon cross section of σ_NN = 42 mb for Au+Au collisions.
- The mean number of charged particles per participating nucleon in GMC is set to n_ch = 10, with a Gaussian pseudorapidity width σ_η = 3, to roughly correspond to charged particle production at √s = 200 GeV.
- The HSD model uses a threshold energy √s_th ≈ 2.6 GeV for inelastic baryon–baryon collisions and √s_th ≈ 2.3 GeV for meson–baryon collisions.
- For 10% centrality bins defined via N_P, the scaled variance ω_P of the participant distribution increases with centrality in both GMC and HSD models.
- For 2% centrality bins, the participant number fluctuations become smaller and more strongly dependent on the bin definition compared to 10% bins.
- The HSD results systematically exceed the STAR data for ρ but reproduce the qualitative features: approximate independence of η_gap and strong increase with centrality.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of baseline contributions to correlation observables in heavy-ion collisions, following the STAR measurements of forward-backward correlations (STAR Collaboration, 2006 and 2007). The authors contrast their geometrical fluctuation mechanism with interpretations invoking a quark-gluon plasma transition (QGP1, QGP2), percolation (Perc1, Perc2), or color glass condensate (Correlation_CGC1, Correlation_CGC2). The paper enables future experimental analyses to distinguish geometrical from dynamical correlations by studying the dependence on centrality bin size and definition, and notes that earlier work by Broniowski and Florkowski (2002) argued that any centrality selection is equivalent to geometrical selection via impact parameter, but only when fluctuations at fixed b are neglected.
