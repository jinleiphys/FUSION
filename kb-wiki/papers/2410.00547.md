# Molecular states with charm: insights from vacuum and finite-temperature analyses

> This contribution to the SQM2024 conference covers the molecular hypothesis for the internal structure of some open and hidden charm states. The use of effective theories that incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry, combined with unitarization techniques, has provided strong evidences supporting this interpretation. In the heavy-light sector, we discuss the double pole structure of the $D_0^*(2300)$ and the generation of the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$. In the hidden charm sector, we focus on the exotic $X(3872)$ and its heavy-quark partner, the $X(4014)$. Furthermore, we emphasize the benefits of femtoscopic measurements in $p+p$ collisions to establish the nature of these states, as well as the potential role of temperature to discern their internal structure.

## Key claim

The molecular hypothesis for open- and hidden-charm hadrons is supported by effective field theory calculations, but the interpretation is not exclusive, other configurations such as tetraquarks may also play a role, and the molecular nature is inferred from proximity to two-particle thresholds and small decay widths rather than proven.

## Method

The author reviews effective field theories incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, combined with unitarization techniques (such as coupled-channel T-matrix calculations), to dynamically generate molecular states from meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions. For finite-temperature studies, the imaginary-time formalism is used to extend the vacuum EFT calculations, extracting spectral functions and monitoring pole trajectories as a function of temperature. Femtoscopic correlation functions in p+p collisions are analyzed to test the molecular interpretation through final-state interactions of the detected hadron pairs.

## Key numbers

- The two poles of the D0*(2300) are separated by 250 MeV in mass, both with decay widths greater than 200 MeV (Albaladejo 2016).
- For the D0*(2300) and Ds0*(2317) at finite temperature up to T = 150 MeV, the mass shift is approximately Δm ≈ −20 MeV, while thermal widths acquire sizable values.
- The X(3872) and X(4014) masses and widths vary only slightly between T = 0 and T ≈ 100 MeV, but for T > 150 MeV the masses exceed the thermal masses of their constituents and decay widths increase largely due to phase-space opening.

## Context

This conference contribution reviews and extends the molecular interpretation of charm hadrons previously developed using SU(4) chiral theory (Gamermann 2007) and the local hidden gauge formalism (Molina 2009). It builds on the double-pole structure hypothesis for the D0*(2300) proposed by Albaladejo (2016) and Meissner (2020), and on finite-temperature calculations for D mesons by Fuchs (2004), He (2011), and Montana (2020, 2022). The work enables future discrimination between molecular and tetraquark configurations through femtoscopic measurements in p+p collisions (ALICE 2022, 2024) and through temperature-dependent production rates in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as suggested by Zhang (2020).
