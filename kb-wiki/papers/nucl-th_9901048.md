# Collisional relaxation of collective motion in a finite Fermi liquid

> Finite size effects in the equilibrium phase space density distribution function are taken into account for alculations of the relaxation of collective motion in finite nuclei. Memory effects in the collision integral and the diffusivity and the quantum oscillations of the equilibrium distribution function in momentum space are considered. It is shown that a smooth diffuse (Fermi-type) equilibrium distribution function leads to a spurious contribution to the relaxation time. The residual quantum oscillations of the equilibrium distribution function eliminates the spurious contribution. It ensures the disappearance of the gain and loss terms in the collision integral in the ground state of the system and strongly reduces the internal collisional width of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances.

# Collisional relaxation of collective motion in a finite Fermi liquid

## Key claim
The paper shows that using a smooth Fermi-type equilibrium distribution function in the collision integral for finite nuclei introduces a spurious nonzero contribution to the relaxation time because the gain and loss terms in the collision integral do not vanish separately in the ground state. The residual quantum oscillations of the equilibrium Wigner distribution function eliminate this spurious contribution and strongly reduce the internal collisional width of isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances compared to predictions using a simple Fermi distribution.

## Method
The authors start from the collisional Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation and derive expressions for the width of giant multipole resonances using a nuclear fluid dynamic approach that includes dynamic Fermi-surface distortions up to multipolarity l=2. They evaluate the collision integral using the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov method, incorporating memory effects through energy-conserving delta functions with the collective frequency. The equilibrium distribution function is taken as the smeared-out quantum Wigner distribution function for a spherical harmonic oscillator potential and for a Woods-Saxon potential, and the local relaxation time and total width of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance are computed numerically.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic theory approach to nuclear collective motion damping developed by Landau (1957), Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1959), and later authors such as Nix and Sierk (1980), Kohno and collaborators (1986), and Kolomietz et al. (1995-1998). The paper addresses a conceptual problem in earlier treatments that used a smooth Fermi distribution function for the equilibrium phase-space density: such treatments produce nonzero gain and loss terms in the collision integral for the ground state. By using the quantum Wigner distribution function with residual oscillations, the authors obtain results similar to the sharp Thomas-Fermi approximation but with a proper physical foundation. This enables more reliable calculations of giant resonance widths in finite nuclei and clarifies the role of finite-size effects and quantum oscillations in the relaxation of collective motion.
