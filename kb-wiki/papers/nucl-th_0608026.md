# On neutron number dependence of B(E1;0+ --> 1-) reduced transition probability

> A neutron number dependence of the E1 0+ --> 1- reduced transition probability in spherical even--even nuclei is analysed within the Q--phonon approach in the fermionic space to describe the structure of collective states. Microscopic calculations of the E1 0+ --> 1- transition matrix elements are carried out for the Xe isotopes based on the RPA for the ground state wave function. A satisfactory description of the experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The neutron number dependence of the E1 \(0^+_1 \rightarrow 1^-_1\) reduced transition probability in spherical even–even nuclei can be explained by the cancellation of proton and neutron contributions to the transition matrix element, but the neutron number at which the minimum occurs varies from element to element; for Xe isotopes the minimum is predicted at \(A=128\) (eight neutron holes), whereas in Nd and Sm isotopes the minimum occurs at four valence neutrons or holes.

## Method

The authors used the fermionic Q-phonon approach, in which the \(1^-_1\) state is constructed as a product of the quadrupole (\(\hat{Q}_2\)) and octupole (\(\hat{Q}_3\)) operators acting on the RPA ground state. Microscopic calculations of the E1 transition matrix elements were performed for Xe isotopes, with the ground state described by the RPA wave function. The reduced matrix element was expressed as the difference between proton (\(B_p\)) and neutron (\(B_n\)) contributions, and their separate neutron-number dependences were computed.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{136}\)Xe (closed neutron shell \(N=82\)), the neutron contribution to the E1 transition matrix element is sufficiently smaller than the proton contribution.
- The minimum of \(|\langle 0^+_1 \parallel \mathcal{M} \parallel 1^-_1 \rangle|\) in Xe isotopes occurs at \(A=128\).
- For \(^{124}\)Xe, the experimental value of \(|\langle 0^+_1 \parallel \mathcal{M} \parallel 1^-_1 \rangle|\) is 0.051 \(e\cdot fm\); for \(^{126}\)Xe, it is 0.041 \(e\cdot fm\).
- In Nd and Sm isotopes, the minimum occurs when the number of valence neutrons or neutron holes is equal to four.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA-based calculations by Voronov (year not given) and schematic IBA-based analyses by Pietralla (year not given), Smirnova (year not given), and Jolos (year not given), which showed that the minimum arises from cancellation of proton and neutron contributions but could not determine the exact neutron number. The present microscopic calculation predicts the minimum at \(A=128\) for Xe isotopes, whereas recent experimental data for Xe isotopes by Kneissl (year not given) showed no minimum at four valence neutron holes, leaving open the question of whether a minimum exists at larger hole numbers. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted minimum in lighter Xe isotopes and similar analyses in other isotopic chains.
