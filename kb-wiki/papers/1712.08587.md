# New color-magnetic defects in dense quark matter

> Color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter expels color-magnetic fields due to the Meissner effect. One of these fields carries an admixture of the ordinary abelian magnetic field and therefore flux tubes may form if CFL matter is exposed to a magnetic field, possibly in the interior of neutron stars or in quark stars. We employ a Ginzburg-Landau approach for three massless quark flavors, which takes into account the multi-component nature of color superconductivity. Based on the weak-coupling expressions for the Ginzburg-Landau parameters, we identify the regime where CFL is a type-II color superconductor and compute the radial profiles of different color-magnetic flux tubes. Among the configurations without baryon circulation we find a new solution that is energetically preferred over the flux tubes previously discussed in the literature in the parameter regime relevant for compact stars. Within the same setup, we also find a new defect in the 2SC phase, namely magnetic domain walls, which emerge naturally from the previously studied flux tubes if a more general ansatz for the order parameter is used. Color-magnetic defects in the interior of compact stars allow for sustained deformations of the star, potentially strong enough to produce detectable gravitational waves.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new type of color-magnetic flux tube in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase that is energetically preferred over previously known configurations in the parameter regime relevant for compact stars, and also discovers magnetic domain walls in the 2SC phase, but these results rely on a Ginzburg-Landau approach valid only near the critical temperature and assume massless quarks.

## Method

The authors employed a Ginzburg-Landau approach for three massless quark flavors, using a diagonal order parameter with three independent complex scalar fields (more general than the two-component ansatz used in prior work). They computed the Gibbs free energy for homogeneous phases to construct the phase diagram at nonzero external magnetic field, then numerically solved the equations of motion to obtain radial profiles of flux tubes and calculated the critical fields H_c1 and H_c2 to determine the type-I/type-II regime.

## Key numbers

- The weak-coupling Ginzburg-Landau parameters are given by: mu^2 = [48 pi^2 / (7 zeta(3))] T_c (T_c - T), lambda = [72 pi^4 / (7 zeta(3))] (T_c^2 / mu_q^2), and h = -[36 pi^4 / (7 zeta(3))] (T_c^2 / mu_q^2), yielding eta = h/lambda = -0.5.
- The homogeneous CFL condensate is rho_CFL^2 = mu^2 / [lambda (1 - 2 eta)], and the homogeneous 2SC condensate is rho_2SC^2 = mu^2 / lambda.
- The mixing angle between the photon and eighth gluon is given by cos theta = sqrt(3) g / sqrt(3 g^2 + 4 e^2) and sin theta = -2e / sqrt(3 g^2 + 4 e^2).
- The Gibbs free energy density of the homogeneous CFL phase is G_CFL/V = -H^2 cos^2 theta / 2 - 3 mu^4 / [4 lambda (1 - 2 eta)].
- The Gibbs free energy density of the homogeneous 2SC phase is G_2SC/V = -H^2 cos^2 theta / 2 - (H^2 / 2) [3 sin^2 theta cos^2 theta / (1 + 3 cos^2 theta)] - mu^4 / (4 lambda).

## Context

This work extends previous Ginzburg-Landau studies of CFL flux tubes by Iida (2002, 2004) and Giannakis (2003), which treated CFL as an effective two-component superconductor, by allowing all three diagonal order parameter components to differ. The new flux tube configuration has a 2SC-like core and is energetically preferred over previously discussed CFL flux tubes with an unpaired core in the parameter regime relevant for compact stars. The results enable more quantitative estimates of color-magnetic mountain deformations in quark stars or neutron stars with quark cores, potentially producing detectable gravitational waves as argued by Glampedakis (2012).
