# Fermion propagator in a nontrivial background field

> We study the fermion propagator in a spatially varying classical background field, and show that, contrary to common wisdom, it may get nontrivial gradient corrections already at the first order in derivative expansion. This occurs whenever the fermion self-energy acquires a spatially (or temporally) varying pseudoscalar term, a simple example of which is given by a complex mass term m(x)= m_R + i gamma_5 m_I. Such effective mass terms arise for example in extensions of the Standard Model during the electroweak transition, and they are crucial in providing the CP-violation necessary for electroweak baryogenesis.

## Key claim
The fermion propagator in a spatially varying classical background field receives nontrivial gradient corrections already at first order in the derivative expansion, contrary to a well-known no-go theorem by Henning (1995). This occurs specifically when the fermion self-energy contains a spatially (or temporally) varying pseudoscalar term, such as a complex mass term of the form \(\hat m(x) = m_R + i\gamma_5 m_I\).

## Method
The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the retarded and advanced fermion propagators in the Keldysh closed time contour formalism, working in the weak coupling limit (on-shell limit with \(\Gamma \to 0\)) and expanding to first order in gradients of the complex mass. They then computed spectral integrals over test functions using contour integration and residue calculus, extracting the physical energy shell and the associated semiclassical dispersion relation. The current was derived from the Wigner function and compared to the WKB wave-packet result.

## Key numbers
- The first-order gradient correction to the propagator contains a term proportional to \(\ABS{m}^2 \theta' / z^2\), where \(z \equiv k^2 - \ABS{m}^2\) and \(\theta'\) is the gradient of the complex phase of the mass.
- The semiclassical energy shell is shifted to \(\omega_{\rm sc} = \omega_0 - (s s_\omega \ABS{m}^2 \theta')/(2\omega_0^2)\), where \(\omega_0 = (k_3^2 + |m|^2)^{1/2}\) and \(s = \pm 1\) is the spin eigenvalue.
- The semiclassical momentum is \(k_{\rm sc} = k_0 \left(1 + (s s_\omega \ABS{m}^2 \theta')/(2k_0 \omega^2)\right)\), with \(k_0 = \sqrt{\omega^2 - \ABS{m}^2}\).
- The current density for spin state \(s\) is \(j^0_s = 2 \int d^3k/(2\pi)^3 \, (\omega_0/\omega_{\rm sc}) f_s^{\rm sc}\) and \(j^3_s = 2 \int d^3k/(2\pi)^3 \, (k_3/\omega_0) f_s^0\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior series of papers (Joyce et al., 1999-2000) on semiclassical transport equations for quantum fields in varying backgrounds beyond the trivial order in gradients. It contradicts the no-go theorem of Henning (1995), which claimed first nontrivial corrections arise only at second order, by showing that a pseudoscalar self-energy term evades that restriction. The results are directly relevant to electroweak baryogenesis mechanisms, specifically the classical force mechanism (CFM) studied by Joyce et al. (1995) and Cline et al. (1998), where CP-violating backgrounds induce particle-antiparticle asymmetries. The formalism enables a consistent derivation of quantum transport equations for fermions in a plasma, including the role of quantum coherence effects, which will be addressed in a forthcoming work (Joyce et al., 2000).
