# Quantum Vacuum in Hot Nuclear Matter - A Nonperturbative Treatment

> We derive the equation of state for hot nuclear matter using Walecka model in a nonperturbative formalism. We include here the vacuum polarisation effects arising from the nucleon and scalar mesons through a realignment of the vacuum. A ground state structure with baryon-antibaryon condensates yields the results obtained through the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) of summing baryonic tadpole diagrams. Generalization of such a state to include the quantum effects for the scalar meson fields through the $σ$-meson condensates amounts to summing over a class of multiloop diagrams. The techniques of thermofield dynamics (TFD) method are used for the finite temperature and finite density calculations. The in-medium nucleon and sigma meson masses are also calculated in a self consistent manner. We examine the liquid-gas phase transition at low temperatures ($\approx$ 20 MeV), as well as apply the formalism to high temperatures to examine for a possible chiral symmetry restoration phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper derives the equation of state for hot nuclear matter using a nonperturbative variational approach that includes vacuum polarization effects from nucleon and scalar meson fields, but the abstract's suggestion of examining chiral symmetry restoration is qualified: the authors find that the decrease in nucleon mass with temperature is gradual and "need not be indicative of a chiral symmetry restoration phase transition."

## Method

The authors use the Walecka model (QHD-I) with nucleons, scalar sigma mesons, and vector omega mesons, constructing a trial ground state with baryon-antibaryon condensates and sigma-meson condensates via a squeezed coherent state ansatz. They generalize this zero-temperature vacuum to finite temperature and density using thermofield dynamics (TFD), determining the condensate functions and thermal Bogoliubov parameters through minimization of the thermodynamic potential. The resulting self-consistent equations yield the effective nucleon mass, effective sigma meson mass, and equation of state, with renormalization performed by subtracting vacuum contributions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.193 fm⁻³, binding energy: −15.75 MeV (used to fit coupling constants)
- For RHA: C_s² = 183.3, C_v² = 114.7; for λ_R = 1.8: C_s² = 167.5, C_v² = 96.45; for λ_R = 5: C_s² = 137.9, C_v² = 63.7
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition: approximately 23 MeV for RHA, lowered to 22 MeV for λ_R = 1.8, and to 21 MeV for λ_R = 5
- At zero baryon density, the sigma field becomes nonzero at around 160 MeV due to thermal effects
- The effective nucleon mass becomes about half of its vacuum value at T = 250 MeV (compared to M*/M ≈ 0.2 at T = 200 MeV in mean field calculations of Furnstahl (1997))

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative variational approach previously applied to nuclear matter at zero temperature by Mishra (2000) to finite temperature, using thermofield dynamics. It contrasts with the mean field calculations of Furnstahl (1997) which used the no-sea approximation and found a rapid drop in effective nucleon mass suggestive of chiral symmetry restoration; the present results show a more gradual decrease. The formalism enables self-consistent calculation of in-medium sigma meson masses and provides a framework for studying neutron star matter including strange baryons, as previously done by Panda (2000).
