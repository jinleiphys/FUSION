# Collisional time and shear relaxation time for interacting QGP

> We have studied the collisional time and relaxation time of a QGP(Quark-Gluon Plasma) by parameterizing them by temperature. From this parameterization we have obtained the decay rate parameterized by temperature which further helps us to calculate and compare the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a QGP with the KSS(Kovtun-Son-Starinets) result.

# Collisional time and shear relaxation time for interacting QGP

## Key claim
The authors parameterize the thermal width of quarks and gluons as a function of temperature by matching lattice QCD entropy density data, then use the KSS bound (η/s = 1/(4π)) to extract the shear relaxation time, finding that the collisional time and relaxation time are of the same order of magnitude near and above the transition temperature.

## Method
The thermal width Γ_c(T) is parameterized by fitting a sigmoid-like function to lattice QCD entropy density data, using a spectral function approach where the entropy density is expressed as an integral over off-shell masses with a Breit-Wigner distribution. The shear relaxation time τ is then obtained by imposing the KSS bound η/s = 1/(4π) on the shear viscosity expression, which also uses the same spectral function and thermal width parameterization.

## Key numbers
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for entropy density is s ≈ 20.8 T³, derived from the massless non-interacting limit with gluon and quark degeneracy factors.
- The thermal width parameterization Γ_c(T) uses coefficients: a₀ = 6.76802, a₁ = 88.6265, a₂ = −37.3715, a₃ = 0.170, a₄ = 14.0653.
- Near and above the transition temperature, τ_c ≈ 2 fm and τ ≈ 1 fm, indicating order-of-magnitude agreement between the two time scales.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Muronga (no year given) by providing explicit numerical estimates for the collisional time and shear relaxation time in an interacting QGP, where previously no general formula linking the two time scales existed. The thermal width parameterization is based on lattice QCD entropy density data (LQCD1, no year given), and the shear viscosity result is compared to the KSS bound (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, no year given). The findings enable more precise modeling of dissipative hydrodynamics for QGP by providing temperature-dependent time scales that can be used in transport calculations.
