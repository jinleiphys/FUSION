# Axial inverse magnetic catalysis

> We find that the inverse magnetic catalysis for $U(1)$ axial symmetry (AIMC: axial inverse magnetic catalysis) can emerge around the chiral crossover regime in the thermomagnetic QCD with 2 + 1 flavors at physical point. This phenomenon can be correlated with the IMC for the chiral $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ symmetry (CIMC: chiral IMC). We explicitly observe the AIMC based on a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with 2 + 1 quark flavors, where introduced anomalous magnetic moments of the quarks play the essential role to drive both the CIMC and AIMC. Our finding is shortly testable on lattices. Possible phenomenological and cosmological implications are also briefly addressed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that inverse magnetic catalysis for the U(1) axial symmetry (AIMC) can emerge around the chiral crossover regime in thermomagnetic QCD with 2+1 flavors at the physical point, but this finding is conditional on the presence of sufficiently large anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of quarks and is demonstrated only within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model framework, not yet confirmed by lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors employed a 2+1 flavor NJL model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant term to mimic the U(1)_A anomaly, and introduced anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) for the quarks parameterized as κ_f(eB,T) = v_f · σ_f(eB,T). They solved the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark masses in the mean-field approximation using the imaginary time formalism with a constant magnetic field applied along the z-direction, and evaluated the axial susceptibility χ_{π-δ} = χ_π - χ_δ as a function of temperature and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters fixed at vacuum with eB=T=0: m_0 = 0.0055 GeV, m_{0s} = 0.1407 GeV, Λ = 0.6023 GeV, GΛ^2 = 1.835, and KΛ^5 = 12.36, quoted from Rehberg (1995).
- The magnetic field strength was taken as √(eB) ~ (0.5 - 0.6) GeV for a temperature range of (0.05 - 0.25) GeV.
- A reference AMM parameter set v = v_s = 1.4 GeV^{-2} was used to demonstrate the CIMC phenomenon.
- The lower bound of magnetic field strength for the analysis was set at eB ≥ 0.25 GeV^2 to maintain quantitative agreement with lattice QCD results on the subtracted quark condensates at T=0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inverse magnetic catalysis for chiral symmetry (CIMC) in two-flavor NJL models with AMMs by Ferrer (2014), Fayazbakhsh (2014), Chaudhuri (2019, 2020), Ghosh (2020), Xu (2020), and Farias (2021) to the 2+1 flavor case including the KMT determinant U(1)_A anomaly contribution. The finding enables future lattice QCD simulations to test the existence of AIMC and determine whether the remnant of U(1)_A breaking in the origin of mass might be comparable with chiral breaking in the early thermomagnetic universe, in contrast to pure-thermal QCD where the former may dominate as suggested by Aoki (2021).
