# The wake in mid-central nuclear collisions

> It is argued that PHENIX collaboration observed for the first time the radiation of the longitudinal wake oscillations formed behind the parton penetrating the quark-gluon medium. It shifts the maximum of a hump in two-particle correlations and changes its width in the case of some special orientation of the trigger particle.

# The wake in mid-central nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The PHENIX collaboration's observation of a shifted maximum and altered width in two-particle correlations for mid-central nuclear collisions is argued to be the first detection of longitudinal wake oscillations radiated behind a parton traversing the quark-gluon medium, but only for a specific trigger orientation near π/4 where the wake gluons can escape the overlap region more easily than for in-plane or out-of-plane orientations.

## Method
The author derives the energy-angular spectrum of emitted gluons from classical chromodynamic equations analogous to electrodynamics, separating the transverse Cherenkov term and the longitudinal wake term. The wake radiation is compared to the Cherenkov radiation at the peak of the latter, and the geometry of mid-central collisions (modeled as an ellipsoidal "rugby ball" with principal axis ratio 3/4) is used to argue that trajectories at about π/4 orientation provide optimal escape conditions for wake radiation emitted perpendicular to the parton trajectory.

## Key numbers
- The two-hump peaks in central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV are positioned at Δφ ≈ π ± 1.1 rad, with approximate chromopermittivity values ε₁t ≈ 6 and ε₂t ≈ 0.8.
- The ratio of wake radiation to Cherenkov radiation at the Cherenkov peak is approximately 4 × 10⁻³, making wake radiation negligible at that peak.
- Wake and Cherenkov contributions become comparable at xₑ ≈ x₀²/(2x₀ + 1), corresponding to π − Δφ_L ≈ 1.43 rad.
- The combined maximum shifts to π − Δφ_L ≈ 1.3 rad.
- For 20-25% centrality events, the ratio of principal axes of the overlap region can be as small as 3/4.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous interpretation of the two-hump structure in central collisions as color Cherenkov radiation (Dremin, 2005), where the chromopermittivity was extracted by comparing theory to STAR and PHENIX data (Dremin et al., 2005). The new PHENIX results (Holzmann, 2009) on mid-central collisions show an additional contribution near Δφ = π/2 that cannot be explained by Cherenkov radiation alone. The wake effect, known from electrodynamics (Ryazanov, 1984), provides a qualitative explanation, and the geometry-dependent escape probability for wake gluons explains why the effect appears only at specific trigger orientations. Quantitative comparison requires full computation including collision geometry and damping factors.
