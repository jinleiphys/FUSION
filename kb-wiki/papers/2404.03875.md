# Photo-production of $η_{c,b}$ near Threshold

> We analyze the photo-production of $η_{c,b}$ off a proton in the threshold region, in terms of C-odd gluonic correlations in the off-forward proton matrix element. Near threshold, the skewness is large leading to a production amplitude that is dominated by four C-odd twist-3 gluon GPDs. We use the QCD instanton vacuum to estimate these C-odd contributions in the proton. The results are used to estimate the differential cross sections for coherent photo-production of $η_{c,b}$ in the threshold region, at current electron facilities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that near-threshold photoproduction of $\eta_{c,b}$ mesons is dominated by C-odd twist-3 gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the proton, and that these contributions can be estimated using the QCD instanton vacuum, yielding predictions for differential cross sections at current electron facilities. The authors hedge that this analysis holds specifically in the large skewness limit near threshold, where the production amplitude is dominated by four C-odd twist-3 gluon GPDs.

## Method

The authors factorize the photoproduction amplitude into a hard scattering kernel computed using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the heavy meson, and soft gluon correlations in the proton described by generalized parton distributions. For $\eta_{c,b}$ production, the dominant contribution comes from three-gluon exchange (C-odd), which is parameterized by four twist-3 gluon GPDs. The authors evaluate the relevant gluonic matrix elements in the proton using the QCD instanton vacuum at next-to-leading order in the pseudoparticle density, then use tripole parameterizations for the resulting form factors to compute differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- $A_g(0) = 0.391$ at resolution $\mu = 2$ GeV, the gluonic gravitational form factor from the instanton vacuum, close to the CTEQ global analysis value $\langle x \rangle_g = 0.414$
- $C_g(0) = -0.4563$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV, the gluonic D-term form factor
- $f_g(0) = 3.4809 \times 10^{-5}$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV, the C-odd gluonic magnetic form factor, substantially smaller than the C-even contribution
- Tripole masses: $m_A = 2.02$ GeV, $m_C = 1.226$ GeV, $m_{3g} = 1.49$ GeV for the form factor parameterizations
- Threshold momentum transfer for $\eta_c$: $t_{th} = -M_N M_X^2 / (M_N + M_X)$ with $M_{\eta_c} = 2.982$ GeV and $M_{\eta_b} = 9.388$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of near-threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction from C-even vector mesons ($J/\Psi$, $\Upsilon$) to C-odd pseudoscalar mesons ($\eta_c$, $\eta_b$). The C-even case, which probes the gluonic gravitational form factors $A_g$ and $C_g$ via $2^{++}$ glueball exchange, has been studied by Hatta (2018), Mamo (2019), Kharzeev (2021), and others following JLAB data from GlueX (2019) and Meziani (2020). The present work focuses on C-odd $1^{+-}$ glueball exchange (Odderon at higher energies), using the QCD instanton vacuum framework previously developed by Shuryak (2018) and Zahed (2021). The results enable comparison of C-even and C-odd gluonic contributions in the proton at current electron facilities like JLAB.
