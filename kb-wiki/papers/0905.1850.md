# Charm at FAIR

> Charmed mesons in hot and dense matter are studied within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach for the experimental conditions of density and temperature expected at the CBM experiment at FAIR/GSI. The $D$ meson spectral function broadens with increasing density with an extended tail towards lower energies due to $Λ_c(2593) N^{-1}$ and $Σ_c(2800) N^{-1}$ excitations. The in-medium $\bar D$ meson mass increases with density. We also discuss the consequences for the renormalized properties in nuclear matter of the charm scalar $D_{s0}(2317)$ and D(2400), and the predicted hidden charm X(3700) resonances at FAIR energies.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of charmed mesons at FAIR conditions is studied, but the abstract's implication that this explains J/Psi suppression is explicitly qualified: the authors state that the lower-energy tail of the D meson spectral function is unlikely to extend far enough (by 600 MeV) with sufficient strength to enable D-Dbar decay into J/Psi, and instead suggest suppression via excited charmonia decay channels.

## Method

The authors perform a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation for D and Dbar mesons in hot nuclear matter, using a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term. The in-medium solution at finite temperature incorporates Pauli blocking effects, baryon mean-field bindings via a temperature-dependent sigma-omega model, and pion and open-charm meson self-energies in the intermediate propagators. The transition matrix T is solved using cutoff regularization, fixed by reproducing the position and width of the Lambda_c(2593) resonance in the I=0 channel while generating the Sigma_c(2800) resonance in the I=1 channel.

## Key numbers

- The D meson optical potential at T=0 is attractive: -12 MeV for model A and -18 MeV for model B
- The Dbar meson optical potential at T=0 is repulsive: 11 MeV for model A and 20 MeV for model B
- The Dbar mass shift in cold nuclear matter is repulsive, and the low-density approximation (T-rho) breaks down at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- The Dbar mass increases by about 10-20 MeV in the medium
- The D_{s0}(2317) resonance develops a width of about 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- The X(3700) resonance develops a width of about 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- The Dbar-N scattering lengths for model A: a^{I=0}=0.61 fm and a^{I=1}=-0.26 fm
- The Dbar-N scattering lengths for model B: a^{I=0}=0 fm and a^{I=1}=-0.29 fm
- The lower peak from the Lambda_c N^{-1} excitation moves up by about 50 MeV when mean-field binding is included

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent coupled-channel approach of Mizutani (2006) and Tolos (2006, 2007) to finite temperature conditions relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR. The results for Dbar scattering lengths are consistent with Haidenbauer (2007) but differ from Lutz (2006) regarding the I=0 scattering length. The study enables experimental investigation of charm and hidden charm scalar resonances (D_{s0}(2317), D_0(2400), X(3700)) at FAIR, where the charm degree of freedom will be thoroughly investigated, and suggests transparency ratio measurements as a sensitive probe of in-medium absorption rates.
