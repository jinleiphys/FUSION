# The ${\bar K}N$ interaction in higher partial waves

> We present a chiral ${\bar K}N$ interaction model that has been developed and optimized in order to account for the experimental data of inelastic ${\bar K}N$ reaction channels that open at higher energies. In particular, we study the effect of the higher partial waves which originate directly from the chiral Lagrangian, as they could supersede the role of high-spin resonances employed in earlier phenomenological models to describe meson-baryon cross sections in the 2~GeV region. We present a detailed derivation of the partial wave amplitudes that emerge from the chiral SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian up to the d-waves and next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. We implement a nonperturbative unitarization in coupled channels and optimize the model parameters to a large pool of experimental data in the relevant energy range where these new contributions are expected to be important. The obtained results are encouraging. They indicate the ability of the chiral higher partial waves to extend the description of the scattering data to higher energies and to account for structures in the reaction cross sections that cannot be accommodated by theoretical models limited to the s-waves.

## Key claim

The authors present a chiral Kbar-N interaction model that includes higher partial waves (up to d-waves) derived from the chiral SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian at next-to-leading order, and they claim that these chiral higher partial waves can account for structures in reaction cross sections in the 2 GeV region that cannot be described by s-wave-only models, but the results are described only as "encouraging" rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors derived partial wave amplitudes from the chiral SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian up to d-waves and next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. They implemented a nonperturbative unitarization in coupled channels and optimized the model parameters to a large pool of experimental data for inelastic Kbar-N reaction channels at higher energies. The model was then tested against scattering data in the energy range where higher partial wave contributions are expected to be important.

## Key numbers

* The study focuses on the energy region around 2 GeV for meson-baryon cross sections.
* The chiral Lagrangian is expanded up to d-waves and next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral Kbar-N interaction models that were limited to s-waves, which could not describe structures in reaction cross sections at higher energies. The approach aims to supersede earlier phenomenological models that employed high-spin resonances to describe meson-baryon cross sections in the 2 GeV region. The results enable future studies of Kbar-nucleus interactions at higher energies using a chiral framework without introducing ad hoc resonances.
