# Polarized e-p Elastic Scattering in the Collider Frame

> Double polarization elastic e-p cross sections and asymmetries are considered in collider kinematics. Covariant expressions are derived for the general situation involving crossed beams; these are checked against the well-known results obtained when the proton is at rest. Results are given using modern models for the proton electromagnetic form factors for kinematics of interest in e-p colliders such as the EIC facility which is in its planning stage. In context, parity-violating elastic e-p scattering is compared and contrasted with these double-polarization (parity-conserving) results.

## Key claim

The paper derives covariant expressions for double-polarization parity-conserving elastic electron-proton scattering in collider kinematics, showing that the asymmetries are rather small and that the sideways (transverse) proton polarization contribution arises specifically from the proton's anomalous magnetic moment (Pauli form factor), vanishing for point Dirac particles.

## Method

The authors develop the full covariant formalism for polarized e-p elastic scattering in both collinear and crossed-beam collider kinematics, retaining both electron and proton masses throughout. They contract the leptonic and hadronic tensors for longitudinally polarized electrons with longitudinally (L) and sideways (S) polarized protons, expressing results in terms of Sachs form factors G_E^p and G_M^p. The formalism is checked against the well-known laboratory-frame results and then used to compute cross sections, asymmetries, and figures-of-merit for two specific EIC-relevant kinematic choices using modern proton electromagnetic form factor models.

## Key numbers

* Kinematics case I: electron momentum k = 10 GeV/c, proton momentum p = 250 GeV/c, giving gamma_p ≈ 250 and equivalent rest-frame electron beam of 5.3 TeV/c for 1° collider scattering angle
* Kinematics case II: electron momentum k = 2 GeV/c, proton momentum p = 50 GeV/c, giving equivalent rest-frame electron beam of 213 GeV/c for 1° collider scattering angle
* For 1° scattering in the collider frame, the equivalent rest-frame scattering angle is 0.002° for case I and 0.009° for case II
* For 5° scattering in the collider frame, the equivalent rest-frame scattering angle is 0.01° for case I and 0.047° for case II
* The parity-violating single-polarization asymmetry is only about one order of magnitude smaller than the double-polarization parity-conserving asymmetries

## Context

This work extends the standard formalism for polarized e-p elastic scattering from the laboratory frame (where the proton is at rest) to collider kinematics relevant for future facilities like the EIC. The authors build upon the tensor formalism of Donnelly (1986) and Bjorken and Drell (1964) for the flux factor generalization. The results enable planning for double-polarization measurements at e-p colliders, where the well-known double-polarization asymmetry can also be used to determine the product of electron and proton beam polarizations for other reactions such as deep inelastic scattering.
