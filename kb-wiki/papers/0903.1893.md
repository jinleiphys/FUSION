# Hyperons in nuclear matter

> The chiral version of the QMC model, in which the effect of gluon and pion exchanges is included self-consistently, is applied to the hyperons in a nuclear medium. The hyperfine interaction due to the gluon exchange plays an important role in the in-medium baryon spectra, while the pion-cloud effect is relatively small. At the quark mean-field level, the $Λ$ feels more attractive force than the Σor Ξin matter.

## Key claim

The chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) model predicts that, at the quark mean-field level, the Λ hyperon feels a more attractive force in nuclear matter than the Σ or Ξ hyperon, with the hyperfine interaction due to one-gluon exchange playing an important role while the pion-cloud effect is relatively small.

## Method

The authors applied the CQMC model, an extension of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model that incorporates chiral symmetry via the cloudy bag model (CBM), to study hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) in symmetric nuclear matter. They calculated the in-medium baryon masses by self-consistently solving the coupled equations for the nuclear matter, including the effects of gluon exchange (color magnetic interaction) and pion exchange (pseudovector coupling) on the quark wave functions inside the bag, with the σ and ω mesons providing the intermediate-range attraction and short-range repulsion.

## Key numbers

- The N-Δ mass difference (about 300 MeV) is mainly reproduced by the one-gluon exchange contribution (about 240 MeV), while the pion-exchange contribution is about 60 MeV.
- At normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the effective nucleon mass M_N* is 717 MeV (case 1, with pion) or 740 MeV (case 2, without pion).
- The nuclear incompressibility K is 337 MeV (case 1) or 299 MeV (case 2).
- The root-mean-square radius of the nucleon swells by about 3% at ρ₀.
- The quark eigenvalue x decreases by about 20% at ρ₀.
- The pion corrections to N, Δ, Λ, Σ, and Ξ are in the ratio 15 : 3 : 9 : 1 : 0.
- The bag radius of the Δ is calculated to be 0.88 fm (case 1).
- The bag constant B^{1/4} is 168.8 MeV (case 1) or 169.9 MeV (case 2); the strange quark mass m_s is 275.6 MeV (case 1) or 286.6 MeV (case 2).

## Context

This work extends the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and the earlier work of Miyatsu and Saito on finite nuclei to include hyperons in nuclear matter, incorporating chiral symmetry via the cloudy bag model of Thomas (1981). The results contradict the simple scaling law predicted by the QMC model without gluon and pion exchanges, where the hadron mass reduction is proportional only to the number of light quarks. The finding that the Λ feels more attraction than the Σ or Ξ is consistent with the experimental difficulty in discovering Σ or Ξ hypernuclei, and the authors note that future studies at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR will provide important tests.
