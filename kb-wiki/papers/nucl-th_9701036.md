# Variational Field Theoretic Approach to Relativistic Scattering

> Nonperturbative polaron variational methods are applied, within the so-called particle or worldline representation of relativistic field theory, to study scattering in the context of the scalar Wick - Cutkosky model. Important features of the variational calculation are that it is a controlled approximation scheme valid for arbitrary coupling strengths, the Green functions have all the cuts and poles expected for the exact result at any order in perturbation theory and that the variational parameters are simultaneously sensitive to the infrared as well as the ultraviolet behaviour of the theory. We generalize the previously used quadratic trial action by allowing more freedom for off-shell propagation without a change in the on-shell variational equations and evaluate the scattering amplitude at first order in the variational scheme. Particular attention is paid to the $s$-channel scattering near threshold because here non-perturbative effects can be large. We check the unitarity of a our numerical calculation and find it greatly improved compared to perturbation theory and to the zeroth order variational results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first-order variational calculation (beyond the zeroth-order Gaussian approximation) for meson-nucleon scattering in the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model yields significantly improved unitarity compared to both perturbation theory and the zeroth-order variational result, particularly near the s-channel threshold where non-perturbative effects can be large.

## Method

The authors apply Feynman's polaron variational method within the worldline (particle) representation of relativistic field theory, using a quadratic trial action with a retardation function that captures both infrared and ultraviolet behavior. They generalize the trial action by allowing different variational parameters for each nucleon line (λ_i) to account for off-shell propagation of intermediate nucleons, while keeping the on-shell variational equations unchanged. The scattering amplitude is evaluated at first order in the variational scheme by computing the averaged difference between the true and trial actions (⟨ΔS⟩) and performing the necessary analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski space.

## Key numbers

- The forward scattering amplitude at zeroth order satisfied unitarity to within a factor of two; the first-order calculation improves this result significantly.
- The pseudotime μ²(σ) grows linearly with σ for both σ → 0 and σ → ∞, with no poles or cuts in the half-plane where Re(σ) is positive.
- The variational parameters C₁, C₂, w₁, and w₂ are determined from the variational equations for the 2-point function G₂(p), with the profile function A(E) having branch points at E = i w₂ and E = i w₁.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers (I–IV, 1994–1996) on variational treatments of the Wick-Cutkosky model, where the zeroth-order approximation was applied to scattering (III) and the vertex function was examined near pair production threshold (IV). The method is a relativistic generalization of Feynman's highly successful polaron variational approach from condensed matter physics (Feynman, 1955; Feynman and Hibbs, 1965), which yields results within a few percent of exact Monte Carlo calculations (Alexandrou and Rosenfelder, 1992). The present first-order calculation enables more accurate treatment of scattering near thresholds where non-perturbative effects dominate, and the authors note that a generalization to QED will appear in a forthcoming publication.
