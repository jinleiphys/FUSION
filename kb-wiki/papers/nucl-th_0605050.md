# Hyperon-nucleon interactions - a chiral effective field theory approach

> We construct the leading order hyperon-nucleon potential in chiral effective field theory. We show that a good description of the available data is possible and discuss briefly further improvements of this scheme.

## Key claim

The authors construct the leading-order hyperon-nucleon potential in chiral effective field theory and show that a good description of the available low-energy data is possible, but the fit requires five free parameters and the $\Lambda N$ scattering lengths cannot be well determined from the scattering data alone.

## Method

The authors apply the Weinberg power counting scheme to the hyperon-nucleon system, constructing a leading-order potential consisting of non-derivative four-baryon contact terms (with SU(3)$_f$ symmetry) and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges (pions and kaons). They solve a regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space for the partial-wave projected potential, fitting five low-energy constants to 35 low-energy total cross sections and the inelastic capture ratio at rest, while treating the cutoff mass and the pseudoscalar $F/(F+D)$ ratio $\alpha$ as fixed parameters.

## Key numbers

* The weak pion decay constant is taken as $F_\pi = 92.4$ MeV.
* The axial-vector coupling satisfies $F+D = g_A \simeq 1.26$.
* The fit to 35 low-energy $YN$ data yields $\chi^2$ values ranging from 27.8 (for cutoff $\Lambda = 550$ MeV) to 42.8 (for $\Lambda = 700$ MeV).
* The $\Lambda N$ $S$-wave contact term $C^{\Lambda\Lambda}_{1S0}$ ranges from $-0.0467 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$ (at $\Lambda = 550$ MeV) to $-0.0516 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$ (at $\Lambda = 700$ MeV).
* The $\Lambda N$ $S$-wave contact term $C^{\Lambda\Lambda}_{3S1}$ ranges from $-0.0214 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$ (at $\Lambda = 550$ MeV) to $-0.0024 \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$ (at $\Lambda = 700$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions by Epelbaum et al. (2005) to the hyperon-nucleon system, where the data are less complete and of lower precision. It differs from the next-to-leading order analysis by Korpa et al. (2001), who used a tree-level amplitude with contact terms and the Kaplan-Savage-Wise resummation scheme without explicit meson exchanges. The results enable systematic improvements to higher orders in the chiral expansion and provide predictions for hyperon-nucleon phase shifts and the hypertriton binding energy that can be tested against future experiments and lattice QCD calculations.
