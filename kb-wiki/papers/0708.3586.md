# Calculations of $^{6}$He+p elastic scattering cross sections using folding approach and high-energy approximation for the optical potential

> Calculations of microscopic optical potentials (OP's) (their real and imaginary parts) are performed to analyze the $^6$He+p elastic scattering data at a few tens of MeV/nucleon (MeV/N). The OP's and the cross sections are calculated using three model densities of $^6$He. Effects of the regularization of the NN forces and their dependence on nuclear density are investigated. Also, the role of the spin-orbit terms and of the non-linearity in the calculations of the OP's, as well as effects of their renormalization are studied. The sensitivity of the cross sections to the nuclear densities was tested and one of them that gives a better agreement with the data was chosen.

## Key claim

The high-energy approximation (HEA) optical potential, when used with a spin-orbit term and LSSM densities, can describe $^{6}$He+p elastic scattering data at a few tens of MeV/N without free fitting parameters, but only for scattering angles smaller than approximately 40 degrees; at larger angles the agreement degrades.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was calculated via single folding of the CDM3Y6 Paris effective NN interaction with three model densities of $^{6}$He (Tanihata, COSMA, and LSSM), including exchange terms and density dependence. The imaginary part was obtained from the high-energy approximation (HEA) using eikonal phases reconstructed from NN scattering amplitudes and total cross sections, with in-medium corrections. Differential cross sections were computed by numerical integration of the Schrodinger equation using the DWUCK4 code, not by the HEA scheme itself.

## Key numbers

- At $E=41.6$ MeV/N, the HEA optical potential with spin-orbit term agrees with experimental data up to $\vartheta \simeq 40^\circ$ (fig. 4).
- For case (A) (both real and imaginary parts from HEA) at $E=41.6$ MeV/N, good agreement with data is obtained with $N_R=1.00$ and $N_I=1$ (no renormalization needed) for angles below $40^\circ$ (fig. 5).
- For case (B) (folded real part $V^F$ + HEA imaginary part $W^H$) at $E=41.6$ MeV/N, good agreement is obtained with $N_R=1.00$ and $N_I=1$ (fig. 5).
- For case (C) (both real and imaginary parts from folded $V^F$) at $E=41.6$ MeV/N, good agreement requires $N_R=0.85$ with $N_I=1$ (fig. 5).
- The rms radius of the point-proton density of $^{6}$He is 1.72 fm for the Tanihata model and 1.89 fm for the COSMA model (section 2.4).
- The regularization of the M3Y effective NN interaction has a weak effect on cross sections, becoming noticeable only at higher energies and larger angles (fig. 2).
- The spin-orbit term is important at angles larger than $60^\circ$ at $E=41.6$ MeV/N (fig. 3).
- The applicability region of the HEA approach for $^{6}$He+p at $E_{lab}=40$ MeV/N is estimated as $\vartheta < 50^\circ$ (section 3).

## Context

This work extends microscopic folding model analyses of $^{6}$He+p elastic scattering previously performed using M3Y-Paris interactions with Tanihata and COSMA densities (Lukyanov et al., 2004; 2006) and the coordinate-space g-matrix folding method (Amos et al., 2000; 2006; Deb et al., 2001; 2003; 2005). It also tests the HEA optical potential approach, previously applied to $^{16,17}$O+A scattering at higher energies (Lukyanov et al., 2001; 2004), at lower energies (tens of MeV/N) for the exotic halo nucleus $^{6}$He. The results enable future use of the HEA imaginary potential with minimal fitting parameters for proton scattering on unstable nuclei at intermediate energies, and demonstrate that the LSSM density with realistic exponential asymptotic behavior gives the best description of the data.
