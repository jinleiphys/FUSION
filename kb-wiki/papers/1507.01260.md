# Investigation of the $nnΛ$ bound state in pionless effective theory

> The possibility of an $nnΛ$ bound state is investigated in the framework of pionless effective field theory at leading order. A system of coupled integral equations are constructed in the spin-isospin basis, of which numerical solutions are investigated. In particular, we make use of the limit cycle behavior, i.e., cyclic singularities of coupled integral equations of the system, which would be associated with the formation of a three-body bound state, so-called the Efimov state, in the unitary limit. Furthermore, we find that, when the sharp momentum cutoff introduced in the integral equations is taken significantly larger than the hard scale of the effective theory, the coupling of a three-body contact interaction becomes cyclically singular indicating the onset of Efimov-like bound state formation. However, the paucity of empirical information to determine the parameters of the theory precludes a definitive conclusion on the existence of such a bound state. As a simple test of the feasibility of the $nnΛ$ bound system in nature, we explore the cutoff dependence of the theory, and uncertainties of the present study are discussed as well.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether an $nn\Lambda$ bound state can exist using pionless effective field theory at leading order, but the lack of empirical data to determine the theory's parameters precludes a definitive conclusion on the existence of such a bound state.

## Method

The authors constructed a system of coupled integral equations in the spin-isospin basis for the $nn\Lambda$ three-body system using pionless effective field theory at leading order, with $S$-wave two-body interactions described by dibaryon fields. They introduced a sharp momentum cutoff $\Lambda_c$ in the loop integrations and examined the cutoff dependence of the three-body contact interaction coupling $g(\Lambda_c)$ and the binding energy, looking for cyclic singularities (limit cycle behavior) that would indicate Efimov-like bound state formation.

## Key numbers

- The $nn$ scattering length used was $a_{nn} \approx -18.5$ fm, taken from standard experimental extraction
- The $n\Lambda$ spin singlet scattering length was $a_{s(n\Lambda)} \simeq -2.90$ fm and the spin triplet scattering length was $a_{t(n\Lambda)} \simeq -1.60$ fm, obtained from chiral effective theory calculations invoking isospin symmetry
- The first critical cutoff where the three-body coupling $g(\Lambda_c)$ changes sign (indicating bound state formation from two-body dynamics alone) was found at $\Lambda_c \sim 1.5$ GeV
- The second critical cutoff appeared at $\Lambda_c \sim 80$ GeV, indicating the onset of a second bound state
- The ratio of critical cutoffs gave $s_0 = \pi/\ln(\Lambda_2/\Lambda_1) \approx 0.801$, in agreement with the asymptotic analysis value $s_0 = 0.80339$

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory approach previously applied to the hypertriton ($pn\Lambda$ system) by Hammer (2001) to the $nn\Lambda$ system, and follows earlier investigations by two of the authors (Ando and Oh, 2013, 2014) on $\Lambda\Lambda d$ and $\Lambda\Lambda\alpha$ systems. The study was motivated by the HypHI Collaboration's (2013) suggestion of an $nn\Lambda$ bound state, which had been questioned by subsequent potential model studies (Golak et al., 2014; Hiyama et al., 2014; Gal and Garcilazo, 2014; Rijken et al., 2014) due to inconsistencies with $N\Lambda$ scattering data and hypertriton binding energy. The results suggest that if the cutoff is taken significantly larger than the hard scale ($\Lambda_c \gtrsim 1.5$ GeV), two-body interactions alone could generate an $nn\Lambda$ bound state, but definitive conclusions require empirical data to fix the three-body coupling.
