# QCD sum rules for ρ, ω, ϕmeson-nucleon scattering lengths and the mass shifts in nuclear medium

> A new QCD sum rule analysis on the spin-isospin averaged $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson-nucleon scattering lengths is presented. By introducing the constraint relation on the the low energy limit of the vector-current nucleon forward scattering amplitude, we get $a_ρ=-0.47\pm 0.05$ fm, $a_ω=-0.41\pm 0.05$ fm and $a_ϕ=-0.15\pm 0.02$ fm, which suggests that these V-N interactions are attractive. It is also proved that the previous studies on the mass shift of these vector mesons in the nuclear medium are essentially the ones obtained from these scattering lengths in the linear density approximation.

## Key claim

The spin-isospin averaged ρ, ω, and φ meson-nucleon scattering lengths are extracted from QCD sum rules by introducing a constraint relation from the low energy theorem for the vector-current nucleon forward scattering amplitude, yielding attractive interactions for all three mesons. The authors prove that previous studies of vector meson mass shifts in nuclear medium are essentially equivalent to the mass shifts obtained from these scattering lengths in the linear density approximation.

## Method

The authors performed a QCD sum rule analysis of the vector current-nucleon forward scattering amplitude at zero nucleon momentum, using operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 operators. They introduced a constraint relation from the low energy theorem requiring the phenomenological side of the sum rule to match the Born term contribution at zero energy, then applied both derivative Borel sum rule (DBSR) and fitting Borel sum rule (FBSR) methods to extract the scattering lengths. The mass shifts in nuclear medium were then obtained from the scattering lengths via the linear density approximation formula δm_V = 2π(M_N + m_V)/(M_N m_V) a_V ρ_N.

## Key numbers

- a_ρ = -0.47 ± 0.05 fm (spin-isospin averaged ρ meson-nucleon scattering length)
- a_ω = -0.41 ± 0.05 fm (spin-isospin averaged ω meson-nucleon scattering length)
- a_φ = -0.15 ± 0.02 fm (spin-isospin averaged φ meson-nucleon scattering length)
- δm_ρ = -45 to -55 MeV (6-7% decrease) at nuclear matter density ρ_N = 0.17 fm⁻³
- δm_ω = -40 to -50 MeV (5-6% decrease) at nuclear matter density ρ_N = 0.17 fm⁻³
- δm_φ = -10 to -20 MeV (1-2% decrease) at nuclear matter density ρ_N = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Continuum threshold s_0 = 1.75 GeV² for ρ and ω mesons, s_0 = 2.0 GeV² for φ meson
- OPE parameters for ρ and ω: α = 0.39 GeV², β = -0.23 ± 0.07 GeV⁴ (ρ) and -0.16 ± 0.10 GeV⁴ (ω)
- OPE parameters for φ: α = 0.24 GeV², β = -0.12 GeV⁴

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) for vector meson mass shifts in nuclear matter, and reexamines the earlier analysis by Koike (1995) which had found slight mass increases in contradiction to Hatsuda-Lee. The authors show that by properly incorporating the low energy theorem constraint, their results agree with the decreasing mass shifts found by Hatsuda-Lee (1992), while clarifying that the interpretation in terms of vector meson-nucleon scattering lengths essentially persists. The method parallels the QCD sum rule calculation of πN and KN scattering lengths by Kondo, Morimatsu and Nishino (1995), and provides a framework for estimating vector meson-nucleon interactions where no current algebra technique exists.
