# Electromagnetic Processes in Three-Nucleon Systems

> Results gained for electron scattering on $^3$He in a nonrelativistic framework are reviewed. The electromagnetic current is truncated to a single nucleon operator, but the interaction among the three nucleons is treated exactly. Thus initial and final 3N states are evaluated consistently as solutions of Faddeev equations based on realistic NN forces. The correct inclusion of the final state interaction turned out to be important. The agreement to data is reasonably good, but the neglection of MEC's can be seen. That inconsistency should be removed. For pd capture processes we included some MEC's via the Siegert theorem, which dramatically improves the description of the pd capture data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) in three-nucleon (3N) systems, via Faddeev equations based on realistic NN forces, is crucial for describing electron scattering and photodisintegration data, but the restriction to a single-nucleon current operator (neglecting meson-exchange currents, MECs) causes visible discrepancies that must be removed for a fully consistent picture.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev-type integral equations for 3N continuum states, using realistic NN potentials (Bonn B, Paris, AV18, Nijmegen, MT I-III) to generate initial and final 3N wavefunctions. For electron scattering on 3He and 3H, the electromagnetic current was truncated to a single-nucleon operator with standard nucleon form factors; for pd capture, some MECs were included via the Siegert theorem in the electric multipoles. Observables were computed for momentum transfers up to 400 MeV/c, where relativity is expected to be minor.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: |Q| ≤ 400 MeV/c, where relativity is expected to play a minor role.
- For inclusive scattering, longitudinal and transverse response functions were computed at Q = 174, 250, and 300 MeV/c.
- The exponent α = 4 was favored for extrapolating RL(ω) tails, with tail contributions to the Coulomb sum ranging from 6 to 23% for one data set and up to 40% for another.
- For pd capture, calculations were performed at Ed = 45 MeV, comparing to data for the tensor analyzing power Ayy and cross section.
- The pp correlation function C(Q)/Z in 3He was found to be roughly an order of magnitude smaller than [F1p(Q)]^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electron scattering on 3He/3H (e.g., by the same group in Ref. [REF2]) by systematically including exact FSI via Faddeev equations, going beyond plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and simple rescattering expansions. It contradicts the adequacy of single-nucleon currents alone, showing that MECs (via Siegert's theorem) dramatically improve pd capture predictions. The results enable future inclusion of consistent two-body currents and relativistic frameworks, as already partially addressed by Ref. [REF11] for the Coulomb sum.
