# Enhancement of low-mass dileptons in SPS heavy-ion collisions: possible evidence for dropping rho meson mass in medium

> Dilepton production in proton- and nucleus-induced reactions at SPS energies is studied in the relativistic transport model using initial conditions determined by the string dynamics from RQMD. It is found that both the CERES and HELIOS-3 data for dilepton spectra in proton-nucleus reactions can be well described by the conventional mechanism of Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay. However, to provide a quantitative explanation of the observed dilepton spectra in central S+Au and S+W collisions requires contributions other than these direct decays. Introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium, we find that these heavy-ion data can also be satisfactorily explained. We also give predictions for Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon using current CERES mass resolution and acceptance.

# Enhancement of low-mass dileptons in SPS heavy-ion collisions: possible evidence for dropping rho meson mass in medium

## Key claim
The observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons in central S+Au and S+W collisions at SPS energies can be quantitatively explained by introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium, but only after conventional mechanisms (Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay) fail to reproduce the data; the conventional mechanisms alone are sufficient for proton-nucleus reactions.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the Walecka model, with initial conditions determined by string fragmentation from the RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) model. They extended the Walecka model to couple light quarks to scalar and vector fields using the meson-quark coupling model and constituent quark model, calculating in-medium rho, omega, eta, and a1 meson masses self-consistently from the scalar field. Dilepton yields were computed by integrating over the time evolution of the collision system, including contributions from Dalitz decays, direct vector meson decays, and pion-pion annihilation.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Freeze-out time: about 20 fm/c
- In S+Au collisions, the in-medium meson masses produce a factor of 2-3 enhancement of the dilepton yield in the mass region from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV compared to free meson masses
- The initial nuclear density in Pb+Au collisions is about 4 rho_0, compared to about 2.5 rho_0 in S+Au collisions
- The average charged-particle pseudo-rapidity density for Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon is about 440 in the pseudo-rapidity range of 2 to 3
- The scalar coupling constant used is g_sigma = 9.58 and the sigma meson mass is m_sigma = 550 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) using a thermally equilibrated fire-cylinder model, and is supported by calculations by Cassing et al. (1995) using a dropping in-medium rho meson mass predicted from QCD sum rules. The results contradict conventional mechanisms that successfully describe proton-nucleus data but fail for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The paper enables predictions for Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon, which were being measured by the CERES collaboration at the time, and suggests that medium effects may become stronger in heavy-ion collisions at lower incident energies where the system expands more slowly.
