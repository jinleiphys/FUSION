# Shell-model calculations for neutron-rich carbon isotopes with a chiral nucleon-nucleon potential

> We have studied neutron-rich carbon isotopes in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective hamiltonian derived from the chiral N3LOW nucleon-nucleon potential. The single-particle energies and effective two-body interaction have been both determined within the framework of the time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory. The calculated results are in very good agreement with the available experimental data, providing a sound description of this isotopic chain toward the neutron dripline. The correct location of the dripline is reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully microscopic shell-model calculation using the chiral N3LOW nucleon-nucleon potential, with no adjustable parameters, can reproduce the experimental properties of neutron-rich carbon isotopes and correctly locate the neutron dripline at 22C, though the calculated ground-state energies systematically underestimate experimental values.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for carbon isotopes from A=16 to 24 using valence neutrons in the 0d5/2, 0d3/2, and 1s1/2 orbitals above a 14C inert core. The effective Hamiltonian was derived from the chiral N3LOW potential (momentum cutoff at 2.1 fm^-1) using time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory through third order, with both single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements obtained from the Q-box plus folded-diagram method.

## Key numbers

- Calculated two-neutron separation energy S2n for 22C: 601 keV, compared to experimental evaluation of 420 keV (Audi 2003)
- Calculated one-neutron separation energy Sn for 21C: -1.6 MeV, confirming particle instability
- Single-particle energies (MeV): 0d5/2 = 0.601, 0d3/2 = 5.121, 1s1/2 = -0.793
- Magnetic dipole moments: mu(1/2+_1) = -1.920 n.m. (experiment: +/-1.720 +/- 0.009 n.m.), mu(5/2+_1) = -1.803 n.m. (experiment: +/-1.758 +/- 0.030 n.m.), mu(3/2+_1) = -0.807 n.m. (experiment: +/-0.758 +/- 0.004 n.m.)
- B(M1) transition rates in 17C: calculated 0.0941 mu_N^2 for 1/2+ -> 3/2+ (experiment: 0.0010 +/- 0.0001 mu_N^2), calculated 0.095 mu_N^2 for 5/2+ -> 3/2+ (experiment: 0.082 +0.032/-0.018 mu_N^2)
- B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) for 16C: calculated 1.8 e^2fm^4 (experiment: 2.6 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.7 e^2fm^4)
- B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) for 18C: calculated 3.0 e^2fm^4 (experiment: 4.3 +/- 0.2 +/- 1.0 e^2fm^4)
- B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) for 20C: calculated 3.7 e^2fm^4 (experiment: < 3.7 e^2fm^4)
- Predicted excitation energy for unbound 2+_1 state in 22C: 4.661 MeV
- Neutron effective charge: approximately 0.4e with harmonic-oscillator parameter b = 1.72 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fully microscopic shell-model approach, which successfully reproduced the neutron dripline for oxygen isotopes (Coraggio 2007) and properties of p-shell nuclei (Coraggio 2005) and N=82 isotones (Coraggio 2009), to the carbon isotopic chain for the first time. The calculations confirm the disappearance of the N=14 subshell closure present in oxygen isotopes and predict a new N=16 subshell closure, while reproducing the Borromean nature of 22C recently observed by Tanaka (2010). The results enable future studies of nuclei far from stability using a parameter-free approach based on chiral effective field theory.
