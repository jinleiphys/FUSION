# The cross section minima in elastic Nd scattering: a ``smoking gun'' for three nucleon force effects

> Neutron-deuteron elastic scattering cross sections are calculated at different energies using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions and the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force adjusted to the triton binding energy. Predictions based on NN forces only underestimate nucleon-deuteron data in the minima at higher energies starting around 60 MeV. Adding the three-nucleon forces fills up those minima and reduces the discrepancies significantly.

# The cross section minima in elastic Nd scattering: a "smoking gun" for three nucleon force effects

## Key claim

The minima in elastic neutron-deuteron (nd) and proton-deuteron (pd) scattering cross sections at nucleon laboratory energies starting around 60 MeV and above are identified as a "smoking gun" for three-nucleon force (3NF) effects, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the calculations including 3NFs are not fully converged with respect to partial waves at higher energies, and that relativistic effects and Coulomb force uncertainties (for pd comparisons) have not been fully accounted for.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for elastic Nd scattering at nucleon laboratory energies of 12, 65, 140, and 200 MeV using four modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm I, Nijm II) and the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) 2π-exchange three-nucleon force, with the TM cutoff parameter Λ adjusted individually for each NN force to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy. Calculations including 3NFs included all partial wave states with total two-nucleon subsystem angular momenta up to j_max = 3, while NN-only calculations included states up to j_max = 5 for convergence.

## Key numbers

- At 12 MeV, the pure 3NF contribution to the cross section minimum is "totally negligible"
- At 65 MeV, the 3NF signal becomes "sufficiently large to be seen in the minimum region"
- At 200 MeV, the pure 3NF contribution overshoots the minimum value of the cross section by a factor of approximately 6
- Modern NN interactions underbind ³H by about 800 keV (local forces) out of 8.48 MeV total binding energy, while the nonlocal CD Bonn interaction underbinds by approximately 500 keV
- The calculations with 3NFs included partial waves up to j_max = 3; NN-only calculations included states up to j_max = 5

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of three-nucleon continuum observables that might reveal 3NF effects, following the identification of the low-energy vector analyzing power A_y discrepancy (Witala et al., 1990s) and the space star configuration puzzle in breakup at 13 MeV as other possible signatures. The authors note that the bulk of 3N scattering observables below about 100 MeV can be described well by NN forces alone, as shown for the total nd cross section, but the cross section minima at higher energies provide a new, clearer signal. The results enable precise experimental tests: the authors call for accurate nd elastic scattering data at the minima for energies from 65 MeV upward to confirm the 3NF interpretation and to avoid the theoretical uncertainty of pp Coulomb force effects that complicate pd comparisons.
