# Fully coupled-channel study of $K^-pp$ resonance in a chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$ potential

> We have investigated the most essential kaonic nucleus "$K^-pp$" as a resonant state of the $\bar{K}NN$-$πΣN$-$πΛN$ coupled channel system using a chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$ potential. We treat the "$K^-pp$" resonance adequately with a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (full ccCSM). Self-consistency needs to be considered for the energy dependence of the chiral SU(3)-based potential. In the present study, we propose a simple prescription for the treatment of self-consistency, considering the {\it averaged threshold} and {\it averaged binding energy of mesons}. With this prescription, we have successfully found the self-consistent solutions of the "$K^-pp$" three-body resonance. The results indicate that the "$K^-pp$" system is bound rather shallowly. In particular, when the potential parameters are constrained with the latest $\bar{K}N$ scattering length, the binding energy and half of the mesonic decay width are obtained as $14-50$ MeV and $8-19$ MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $K^-pp$ system is bound rather shallowly, with a binding energy of at most 50 MeV when the chiral SU(3)-based potential is constrained by the latest $\bar{K}N$ scattering length from the SIDDHARTA experiment; this result is conditional on the choice of the "field picture" ansatz for treating the energy dependence of the potential, which the authors consider the more reasonable prescription.

## Method

The authors performed a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (full ccCSM) calculation of the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi\Sigma N$-$\pi\Lambda N$ three-body system, treating $K^-pp$ as a resonant state. They employed a chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$(-$\pi Y$) potential (the NRv2c and NRv2-S versions) with a local Gaussian form factor, derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, and used the Argonne v18 potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. To handle the energy dependence of the chiral potential, they proposed a self-consistency prescription using the averaged threshold and averaged binding energy of mesons, iterating the calculation until the input and output meson-baryon energies matched.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of $K^-pp$ ($B_{K^-pp}$) = 14-50 MeV and half of the mesonic decay width ($\Gamma_{\pi YN}/2$) = 8-19 MeV, obtained with the NRv2-S potential (constrained by the latest $\bar{K}N$ scattering length).
* With the NRv2c potential (constrained by the old $\bar{K}N$ scattering length), $B_{K^-pp}$ = 19-59 MeV and $\Gamma_{\pi YN}/2$ = 8-14 MeV.
* Under the preferred "field picture" ansatz with the NRv2-S potential, $B_{K^-pp}$ = 14-28 MeV and $\Gamma_{\pi YN}/2$ = 8-15 MeV.
* Mean distance between two nucleons in the $K^-pp$ system = 2.10-0.06i fm (NRv2c, field picture) and 2.14-0.16i fm (NRv2-S, field picture), comparable to the 2.2 fm distance in normal nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $K^-pp$ using chiral SU(3)-based potentials (Dote et al. 2016, Barnea et al. 2012, Ikeda et al. 2012) by employing a fully coupled-channel treatment that correctly handles both the resonance nature and the coupled-channel aspects, unlike earlier variational methods that treated $K^-pp$ as a bound state. The results contradict predictions from phenomenological energy-independent potentials (Akaishi and Yamazaki 2002, Shevchenko 2012) which yield deeper binding energies above 50 MeV. The findings enable direct comparison with the high-statistics data from the J-PARC E15 experiment, which is currently analyzing a signal for the $K^-pp$ bound state.
