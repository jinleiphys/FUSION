# Thermal photon production from gluon fusion induced by magnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the production of thermal photons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by gluon fusion in the presence of an intense magnetic field, and during the early stages of the reaction. This photon yield is an excess over calculations that do not consider magnetic field effects. We add this excess to recent hydrodynamic calculations that are close to describing the experimental transverse momentum distribution in RHIC and LHC. We then show that with reasonable values for the temperature, magnetic field strength, and strong coupling constant, our results provide a very good description of such excess. These results support the idea that the origin of at least some of the photon excess observed in heavy-ion experiments may arise from magnetic field induced processes.

## Key claim
The excess thermal photon yield observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can be explained by gluon fusion processes induced by intense magnetic fields during the early stages of the reaction, provided the magnetic field strength, temperature, and strong coupling constant take "reasonable values" (the authors do not perform an exhaustive parameter search but select representative values).

## Method
The authors compute the amplitude for photon production from gluon fusion via a quark loop in the presence of a homogeneous magnetic field, using the Schwinger proper-time formalism for the fermion propagator. They work in the lowest Landau level approximation for the quark propagator, valid for intense magnetic fields, and assume massless quarks since gluons dominate the early-stage dynamics before quarks thermalize. The squared matrix element is integrated over thermal Bose-Einstein distributions for the initial gluons to obtain the invariant photon yield, which is then normalized and compared to experimental excess data relative to a hydrodynamic baseline calculation.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the magnetic field strength at early times (\(\tau\sim 0.05\) fm) is in the range \(0.5 \times 10^4 < eB/(\text{MeV})^2 < 10^5\); the temperature used is \(T=300\) MeV.
- For LHC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, the magnetic field strength is \(eB/(\text{MeV})^2\simeq 10^4\); the temperature used is \(T=350\) MeV.
- The strong coupling constant is taken as \(g=1\).
- The photon emission probability is proportional to \(g^2\) (not \(g^4\)) and to \(B^2\), and the normalized yield is independent of the space-time volume of the reaction.
- The matrix element squared contains the numerical factor \(6256/2187 \sim 2.86\) from the longitudinal piece.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of photon production in magnetic fields, including the QCD trace anomaly mechanism (Skokov, Shuryak) and synchrotron radiation (Tuchin), by computing for the first time the perturbative gluon fusion channel. It adds the computed excess to the hydrodynamic calculations of Ref. [hydro-photons1], which already approach the experimental data from PHENIX and ALICE within uncertainties, and supports the idea that magnetic field effects may explain part of the observed photon excess and the large elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) of photons. The results enable future quantification of the azimuthal asymmetry from magnetic field induced processes.
