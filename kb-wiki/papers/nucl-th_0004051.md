# Foundations of self-consistent particle-rotor models and of self-consistent cranking models

> The Kerman-Klein formulation of the equations of motion for a nuclear shell model and its associated variational principle are reviewed briefly. It is then applied to the derivation of the self-consistent particle-rotor model and of the self-consistent cranking model, for both axially symmetric and triaxial nuclei. Two derivations of the particle-rotor model are given. One of these is of a form that lends itself to an expansion of the result in powers of the ratio of single-particle angular momentum to collective angular momentum, that is essentual to reach the cranking limit. The derivation also requires a distinct, angular-momentum violating, step. The structure of the result implies the possibility of tilted-axis cranking for the axial case and full three-dimensional cranking for the triaxial one. The final equations remain number conserving. In an appendix, the Kerman-Klein method is developed in more detail, and the outlines of several algorithms for obtaining solutions of the associated non-linear formalism are suggested.

## Key claim

The paper provides a microscopic derivation of the self-consistent particle-rotor model (PRM) and the self-consistent cranking model from the Kerman-Klein (KK) equations of motion and associated trace variational principle, but the derivation of the cranking limit requires an expansion in powers of the ratio of single-particle angular momentum to collective angular momentum and a distinct angular-momentum-violating step.

## Method

The author starts from a shell-model Hamiltonian with multipole and pairing forces and uses the Kerman-Klein method to derive exact equations of motion for coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP). These equations are shown to follow from a trace variational principle. The formalism is then applied to axially symmetric nuclei by assuming the even (core) nuclei occupy a single rotational band, expressing the CFP in terms of intrinsic amplitudes and Wigner D-functions, and evaluating the variational functional to obtain the self-consistent PRM equations. A second derivation of the PRM is given in a form that allows expansion in powers of the ratio of single-particle to collective angular momentum, which leads to the cranking limit; this derivation is extended to triaxial nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle energies are measured relative to the chemical potential λ = (1/2)(Ē₀ − E̱₀), where Ē₀ and E̱₀ are ground-state energies of heavier and lighter neighboring even nuclei.
- The normalization condition for the CFP involves Ω = Σⱼₐ (2jₐ + 1), the total degeneracy of the shell-model space.
- The Coriolis coupling term in the PRM equations involves the function f(JK) defined by ∂E*/∂Jᵢ ≡ f(J² − K²)Jᵢ, which in the simplest case is the reciprocal of the moment of inertia.
- The interaction terms in the final variational sum contain factors of (2L+1)/(2J'+1) and 6-j symbols with arguments jₐ, jₐ', j_b, j_b', L, and I''.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic foundation of the particle-rotor model previously studied using the Kerman-Klein method by Klein and collaborators (Klein 1980s), and provides a derivation of the cranking model that connects to the cranked shell model of Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979) and the tilted-axis cranking generalization of Frauendorf (1990s). The paper also references the standard textbook treatments of the PRM by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and by Ring and Schuck (1980), and the generator-coordinate-based derivation of the cranking model by Kamlah (1968) and by Reinhard, Bhaduri, and Maruhn (1970s). The results enable systematic microscopic calculations for both axially symmetric and triaxial deformed nuclei, including the possibility of tilted-axis cranking for axial cases and full three-dimensional cranking for triaxial cases.
