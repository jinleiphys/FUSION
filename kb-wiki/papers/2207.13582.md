# Production of proton-rich nuclei in the vicinity of 100Sn via multinucleon transfer reactions

> The production of new proton-rich nuclei in the vicinity of 100Sn is investigated via multinucleon transfer reactions within the framework of microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) and statistical model GEMINI++. The TDHF+GEMINI method has demonstrated the reliable description in the multinucleon transfer dynamics and the agreement between theoretical results and experimental data is quite satisfactory in the observed transfer reactions. We reveal the production cross sections of proton-rich nuclei in 100Sn region via multinucleon transfer reactions to be several orders of magnitude higher than those measured via fusion-evaporation and projectile fragmentation experiments. About 19 new proton-rich isotopes with cross sections of larger than 1 nb are predicted to be produced in multinucleon transfer reaction of 58Ni with 112Sn. The reaction mechanisms are discussed to lead the experimental production of these previously unreported nuclei. Multinucleon transfer reactions provide a fascinating possibility to reach the proton drip-line in 100Sn region and beyond.

## Key claim

Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions in low-energy collisions of 58Ni with 112Sn can produce proton-rich nuclei near 100Sn with cross sections several orders of magnitude higher than those from fusion-evaporation or projectile fragmentation, but the predicted advantage depends on the specific reaction system and incident energy, with the optimal energy lying between 1.4 and 2.0 times the fusion barrier.

## Method

The authors combined the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach with the statistical model GEMINI++ to describe the multinucleon transfer process. TDHF was used to simulate the initial transfer stage, with particle-number projection applied to extract transfer probabilities for each channel, and GEMINI++ modeled the subsequent deexcitation of primary fragments via light-particle emission and fission. The calculations were performed for tip and side collisions of deformed projectile and target nuclei using the Skyrme effective interaction SLy5 on a three-dimensional grid.

## Key numbers

- The fusion barrier for 58Ni+112Sn is 158.40 MeV; for 58Ni+106Cd it is 151.89 MeV; for 58Ni+124Xe it is 163.96 MeV.
- About 19 new proton-rich isotopes with cross sections larger than 1 nb are predicted to be produced in the 58Ni+112Sn MNT reaction.
- The cross section of 100Sn via MNT reaction is predicted to be between 10^-4 and 10^-6 mb, compared to around 10^-9 mb via projectile fragmentation of 124Xe.
- The cross section of 97In via MNT reaction is predicted to be around 10^-6 mb, compared to around 10^-10 mb via fragmentation of a 345A MeV 124Xe beam on a Be target.
- For 104Sb, the MNT reaction predicts a cross section of 10^-3 mb, compared to 10^-7 mb in fragmentation.
- The cross section of 104Te via MNT reaction is predicted to be 10^-4 mb, compared to less than 10^-6 mb via fusion-evaporation.
- For 103Sb, the MNT reaction predicts a production cross section on the order of 100 nb.
- The cross sections of 100Sn are predicted to be 10^-3 mb with both 106Cd and 112Sn targets, but only 10^-7 mb with a 124Xe target.

## Context

This work extends the application of the TDHF+GEMINI method, previously used to describe neutron-rich nuclei around N=126 (Barrett 2015, Vogt 2015, Watanabe 2015) and superheavy element production (Wuenschel 2018), to the proton-rich side of the nuclear chart near the doubly magic 100Sn. The results contradict the conventional wisdom that fusion-evaporation and projectile fragmentation are the primary routes to proton-rich nuclei in this region, as demonstrated by the work of Karny (2005), Suzuki (2013), and Celikovic (2016). The predictions enable experimental searches for about 19 new proton-rich isotopes at current facilities, potentially reaching the proton drip-line beyond 100Sn.
