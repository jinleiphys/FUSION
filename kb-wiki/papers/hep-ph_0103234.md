# Centrality dependence of multiplicity, transverse energy, and elliptic flow from hydrodynamics

> The centrality dependence of the charged multiplicity, transverse energy, and elliptic flow coefficient is studied in a hydrodynamic model, using a variety of different initializations which model the initial energy or entropy production process as a hard or soft process, respectively. While the charged multiplicity depends strongly on the chosen initialization, the p_t-integrated elliptic flow for charged particles as a function of charged particle multiplicity and the p_t-differential elliptic flow for charged particles in minimum bias events turn out to be almost independent of the initial energy density profile.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of charged multiplicity depends strongly on whether the initial energy or entropy density is assumed to scale with wounded nucleons or binary collisions, but the pT-integrated elliptic flow as a function of charged particle multiplicity and the pT-differential elliptic flow for minimum bias events are almost independent of the initial transverse energy density profile.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 A GeV using five different models for the initial transverse energy or entropy density profile: two where the initial entropy or energy density scales with the number of wounded nucleons (sWN, eWN), two where it scales with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (sBC, eBC), and one saturation model based on perturbative QCD with gluon shadowing and saturation. The hydrodynamic evolution assumed boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and used an equation of state with a first-order phase transition from hadron resonance gas to non-interacting quark-gluon gas at Tc=164 MeV, with freeze-out at Tf=120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged multiplicity density at midrapidity for central collisions normalized to dNch/dy=670 at y=0 for b=0, corresponding to dNch/deta=545 for the 6% most central collisions in model eWN
- Initial time for four models set to tau0=0.6 fm/c; saturation model uses tau_sat(b) ranging from (0.75 GeV)^{-1}=0.26 fm/c to larger values depending on impact parameter
- Initial spatial anisotropy epsilon_x varies by up to 20% between models at fixed impact parameter
- For the saturation model, the tail of the energy density profile (computed with p_sat=0.75 GeV) contributes only 7% to the total multiplicity in central collisions
- The saturation model yields dNch/dy values about 10% lower than those in Eskola et al. (2001) due to inclusion of quark degrees of freedom, realistic hadron resonance gas entropy per particle of 4.87 units, and charged particle fraction of approximately 0.62

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies by Kolb et al. (1999, 2000) and Heinz et al. (2000) that assumed initial energy density scaling with wounded nucleons, and by Ollitrault (1992) and Teaney and Shuryak (1999) who used entropy density scaling with wounded nucleons. The paper systematically investigates the sensitivity of flow observables to the initial transverse density profile, which had not been done before. The finding that pT-integrated elliptic flow versus multiplicity and minimum-bias differential elliptic flow are robust against initialization choices enables more reliable extraction of the equation of state from collective flow measurements at RHIC, and provides a baseline for identifying the transition to hard physics at high pT where quark energy loss dominates.
