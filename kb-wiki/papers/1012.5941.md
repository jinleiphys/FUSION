# Correspondence between HBT radii and the emission zone in non-central heavy ion collisions

> In non-central collisions between ultra-relativistic heavy ions, the freeze-out distribution is anisotropic, and its major longitudinal axis may be tilted away from the beam direction. The shape and orientation of this distribution are particularly interesting, as they provide a snapshot of the evolving source and reflect the space-time aspect of anisotropic flow. Experimentally, this information is extracted by measuring pion HBT radii as a function of angle with respect to the reaction plane. Existing formulae relating the oscillations of the radii and the freezeout anisotropy are in principle only valid for Gaussian sources with no collective flow. With a realistic transport model of the collision, which generates flow and non-Gaussian sources, we find that these formulae approximately reflect the anisotropy of the freezeout distribution.

## Key claim

The existing formulae that connect HBT radius oscillations to freezeout anisotropy are strictly valid only for Gaussian sources with no collective flow, but when tested with a realistic transport model (UrQMD) that generates flow and non-Gaussian sources, these formulae approximately reflect the anisotropy of the freezeout distribution, with discrepancies providing an estimate of systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD 3.3) transport model to generate freezeout distributions for Au+Au collisions at various energies. They constructed two-pion correlation functions using the weighting method, fitting them with the Gaussian functional form to extract the six HBT radii as a function of azimuthal angle phi relative to the reaction plane. Fourier coefficients of the HBT radii oscillations were then used to extract source eccentricity and tilt angle via formulae derived for static Gaussian sources, and these extracted values were compared to the actual source geometry obtained directly from the UrQMD freezeout coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=3.84 GeV with impact parameter b=4-8 fm, the freezeout distribution shows a tilt structure relative to the beam axis, with the tilt angle varying depending on the fit range in coordinate space from 10 fm to 40 fm
- The binning correction factor for 45-degree-wide bins is 1.026 for first-order Fourier coefficients and 1.111 for second-order Fourier coefficients
- Correlation functions were fitted over the range -q_max to q_max for q_max values from 60 to 150 MeV/c to study fit-range dependence
- Predictions are given for heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=30 GeV, relevant for FAIR and the RHIC energy scan

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hardtke and Voloshin (1999) that showed correspondence between HBT radii and source curvatures in central collisions, and by Retiere and Lisa (2003) who proposed formulae connecting azimuthal oscillations of HBT radii to transverse anisotropic source shape. The paper tests these formulae in a realistic transport model (UrQMD) that includes non-Gaussian sources and collective flow, unlike the idealized Gaussian, non-flowing sources for which the formulae were derived. The results enable experimental extraction of source tilt and eccentricity from azimuthally-differential femtoscopic measurements, with quantified systematic uncertainties, and are relevant for ongoing measurements at RHIC and future FAIR experiments.
