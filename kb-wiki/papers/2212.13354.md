# Spin polarization and correlation of quarks from glasma

> We investigate the interaction of strong color fields in the glasma stage of high-energy nuclear collisions with the spins of quarks and antiquarks. We employ the perturbative solution of the quantum kinetic theory for the spin transport of (massive) quarks in a background color field governed by the linearized Yang-Mills equation and derive expressions for the quark-spin polarization and quark-antiquark spin correlation at small momentum in terms of field correlators. For the Golec-Biernat Wüsthoff dipole distribution the quark-spin polarization vanishes, but the out-of-plane spin correlation of quarks and antiquarks is nonzero. Our order-of-magnitude estimate of the correlation far exceeds that caused by vorticity effects, but does not fully explain the data for vector meson alignment. We identify possible mechanisms that could further increase the predicted spin correlation.

## Key claim
The strong color fields in the glasma phase of high-energy nuclear collisions can generate a nonzero out-of-plane spin correlation between quarks and antiquarks even when the quark-spin polarization itself vanishes, but this effect does not fully explain the experimental data for vector meson alignment, suggesting additional mechanisms are needed.

## Method
The authors employed the perturbative solution of quantum kinetic theory for spin transport of massive quarks in a background color field governed by the linearized Yang-Mills equation. They derived expressions for quark-spin polarization and quark-antiquark spin correlation at small momentum in terms of field correlators, then evaluated these using the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff dipole distribution for the glasma color fields.

## Context
This work extends the quantum kinetic theory framework developed by Müller and Yang (2021) for spin polarization from turbulent color fields in anisotropic QGP, now applied to the glasma phase described by the color glass condensate effective theory of McLerran and Venugopalan (1993-1994). It contrasts with thermal vorticity approaches of Becattini (2013) that successfully describe global spin polarization of Λ hyperons but fail to explain local spin polarization and vector meson alignment data from STAR and ALICE. The results enable future studies of how early-time color fields in glasma contribute to spin observables measured at freeze-out, and identify possible mechanisms that could further increase the predicted spin correlation.
