# Medium Effects in Neutrino Cooling of Neutron Stars

> This review demonstrates that neutrino emission from dense hadronic component in neutron stars is subject of strong modifications due to collective effects in the nuclear matter. With the most important in-medium processes incorporated in the cooling code an overall agreement with available soft $X$ ray data can be easily achieved. With these findings so called "standard" and "non-standard" cooling scenarios are replaced by one general "nuclear medium cooling scenario" which relates slow and rapid neutron star coolings to the star masses (interior densities). In-medium effects take important part also at early hot stage of neutron star evolution decreasing the neutrino opacity for less massive and increasing for more massive neutron stars. A formalism for calculation of neutrino radiation from nuclear matter is presented that treats on equal footing one-nucleon and multiple-nucleon processes as well as reactions with resonance bosons and condensates.

## Key claim

This review argues that neutrino emission from neutron stars is strongly modified by collective nuclear-matter effects, and that incorporating these in-medium processes into cooling codes can achieve overall agreement with available soft X-ray data, replacing the separate "standard" and "non-standard" cooling scenarios with a single "nuclear medium cooling scenario" that relates cooling rates to star masses (interior densities). The key hedge is that this unification depends on treating one-nucleon and multiple-nucleon processes, as well as reactions with resonance bosons and condensates, on equal footing within a formalism that avoids double-counting problems present in earlier approaches.

## Method

The paper presents a formalism (the "optic theorem formalism in non-equilibrium diagram technique") that calculates neutrino emissivities using closed diagrams with full non-equilibrium Green functions, treating nucleons within the quasiparticle approximation for temperatures where the nucleon particle width is small compared to typical lepton energies. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is treated within the Fermi liquid Landau-Migdal approach, incorporating softening of the medium one-pion exchange mode, nucleon-nucleon correlations in vertices, renormalization of the local part of the interaction by loops, and contributions from neutrino emission from intermediate reaction states and resonance processes on zero sounds and boson condensates.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for pion condensation: $\varrho_{c\pi} \simeq (1\div3)\varrho_0$ depending on condensation type (neutral or charged) and model
- Critical density for kaon condensation: $\varrho_{cK} \simeq (2\div6)\varrho_0$ depending on type ($K^-$ or $\bar{K}^0$, $S$ or $P$ wave) and model
- Critical density for direct Urca process: $\varrho_{cU} \simeq (2\div6)\varrho_0$ depending on equation of state model
- Recent estimate for neutral pion condensation critical density: $2.5\varrho_0$; for charged pion condensation: $1.7\varrho_0$ (SST99)
- Variational calculations (APR98) give critical density for $\pi^0$ condensation as $\simeq 1.3\varrho_0$, but allow direct Urca only at $\varrho > 5\varrho_0$
- Asymptotic emissivity for neutron pair breaking and formation (nPBF) processes: $\varepsilon [\mbox{nPBF}]\sim 10^{20} T^7_{9}\mbox{exp}({-2\Delta/T})$ for $T\ll \Delta$, where $T_9 = T/10^9 \mbox{K}$ (from FRS76)
- Corrected asymptotic emissivity for nPBF and pPBF: $\varepsilon [\mbox{nPFB}, \mbox{pPFB}]\sim 10^{28} (\Delta /\mbox{MeV})^{7}(T/\Delta )^{1/2} \mbox{exp}({-2\Delta/T})$ for $T\ll \Delta$ (from VS87)
- Pion condensate rates are suppressed by factors $\sim 10$ to $100$ compared to first estimate (MBCDM77), due to nucleon-nucleon correlations

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of modified Urca process by Friman and Maxwell (1979) using free one-pion exchange, and the direct Urca process by Lattimer, Pethick, Prakash, and Haensel (1991), by incorporating in-medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction that were previously ignored. The formalism builds on the earlier work of Voskresensky and Senatorov (1984, 1986, 1987) and Migdal, Saperstein, Troitsky, and Voskresensky (1990) to treat the softening of pion modes and other polarization effects. The resulting "nuclear medium cooling scenario" enables a smooth transition between slow and rapid cooling regimes as a function of neutron star mass, and was implemented in cooling code calculations by Schaab, Voskresensky, Sedrakian, Weber, and Weigel (1997). The paper also addresses the early hot stage of neutron star evolution, where medium effects modify neutrino opacity, with implications for supernova explosion mechanisms discussed in Voskresensky, Senatorov, Kholodov, and Haensel (1987) and Haensel, Kholodov, Senatorov, and Voskresensky (1988).
