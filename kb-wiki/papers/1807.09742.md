# Radiative quark $p_T$-broadening in a quark-gluon plasma beyond the soft gluon approximation

> We study the radiative correction to $p_T$-broadening of a fast quark in a quark-gluon plasma beyond the soft gluon approximation. We find that the radiative processes can suppress considerably $p_T$-broadening. This differs dramatically from previous calculations to logarithmic accuracy in the soft gluon approximation, predicting a considerable enhancement of $p_T$-broadening.

## Key claim

Radiative processes can suppress, rather than enhance, the transverse momentum broadening of a fast quark in a quark-gluon plasma, contradicting previous calculations that used the soft gluon approximation and predicted a considerable enhancement. The suppression arises because negative contributions from terms not included in prior analyses (specifically the \(I_2\) and \(I_3\) terms) are comparable in magnitude to the positive \(I_1\) term.

## Method

The analysis uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to compute the radiative correction to \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle\) for a fast quark traversing a uniform medium of thickness \(L\), accounting for single gluon emission beyond the soft gluon approximation. The calculation includes both real splitting (\(q\to qg\)) and virtual (\(q\to qg\to q\)) processes, using an oscillator (quadratic) approximation for the dipole cross section \(\sigma_2(\rho)=C\rho^2\) with \(C=\hat{q}C_F/2C_An\). The contributions are separated into three terms (\(I_1\), \(I_2\), \(I_3\)) based on their dependence on the initial and final state Glauber factors and the medium-modified Green function.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC conditions (\(E=30\) GeV, \(\hat{q}\approx 0.27\) GeV\(^3\), \(L=5\) fm, \(\alpha_s=1/3\)): \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.632\) when accounting for the energy dependence of \(\hat{q}\) (\(r=2.4\)).
- For LHC conditions (\(E=100\) GeV, \(\hat{q}\approx 0.32\) GeV\(^3\), \(L=5\) fm, \(\alpha_s=1/3\)): \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.692\) when accounting for the energy dependence of \(\hat{q}\) (\(r=2.63\)).
- If the difference between \(\hat{q}'\) (transport coefficient at the quark energy) and \(\hat{q}\) (transport coefficient at the gluon energy) is ignored (\(r=1\)): \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{rad}/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0} \approx -0.378\) at \(E=30\) GeV and \(\approx -0.165\) at \(E=100\) GeV.
- The ratio of transport coefficients for the quark to that for soft gluons is \(r\approx 2.4\) at \(E=30\) GeV (RHIC) and \(r\approx 2.63\) at \(E=100\) GeV (LHC).
- The individual term contributions at \(E=30\) GeV: \([I_1,I_2,I_3]/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0}\approx [0.436/r,-0.213,-0.601]\).
- The individual term contributions at \(E=100\) GeV: \([I_1,I_2,I_3]/\langle p_\perp^2\rangle_{0}\approx [0.85/r,-0.107,-0.908]\).

## Context

This work extends the calculations of radiative \(p_T\)-broadening by Mueller (2017) and Blaizot (2017), which were performed in the soft gluon approximation and predicted a large enhancement dominated by a double logarithmic term \(\ln^2(L/l_0)\). The present analysis goes beyond both the soft gluon and logarithmic approximations, finding that the previously neglected \(I_2\) and \(I_3\) terms (which are negative) drastically change the result, leading to suppression rather than enhancement. The negative radiative contribution appears consistent with the STAR measurement of hadron-jet correlations (STAR Collaboration, 2017), which found no evidence for large-angle jet scattering in the medium. Future work should extend the calculation to an expanding QGP and beyond the oscillator approximation, as well as study higher-order effects.
