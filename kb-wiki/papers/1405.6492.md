# Reviving quark nuggets as a candidate for dark matter

> We discuss a novel mechanism for segregation of baryons and anti-baryons in the quark-gluon plasma phase which can lead to formation of quark and antiquark nuggets in the early universe, irrespective of the order of the quark-hadron phase transition. This happens due to CP violating scattering of quarks and antiquarks from moving $Z(3)$ domain walls. CP violation here is spontaneous in nature and arises from the nontrivial profile of the background gauge field ($A_{0}$ ) between different $Z(3)$ vacua. We study the effect of this spontaneous CP violation on the baryon transport across the collapsing large $Z(3)$ domain walls (which can arise in the context of certain low energy scale inflationary models). Our results show that this CP violation can lead to large concentrations of baryons and anti-baryons in the early universe. The quark and antiquark nuggets, formed by this alternate mechanism, can provide a viable dark matter candidate within standard model without violating any observational constraints.

## Key claim

The authors propose that spontaneous CP violation arising from the nontrivial profile of the background gauge field \(A_0\) between different \(Z(3)\) vacua can lead to different reflection coefficients for quarks and antiquarks from moving \(Z(3)\) domain walls, thereby generating baryon and antibaryon segregation in the early universe and enabling quark nugget formation without requiring a first-order quark-hadron phase transition.

## Method

The authors model the Polyakov loop order parameter \(l(x)\) between different \(Z(3)\) vacua using an effective Landau-Ginzburg potential with parameters fixed from lattice QCD results for pure SU(3) gauge theory at \(T = 400\) MeV. They invert the relation between the Polyakov loop and the background gauge field \(A_0\) in the Polyakov gauge to obtain the \(A_0\) profile across the domain wall, then compute reflection and transmission coefficients for quarks and antiquarks scattering from this background. These coefficients are inserted into baryon transport equations for collapsing spherical \(Z(3)\) domain walls, assuming constant temperature, instantaneous homogenization of baryons, and wall velocity equal to the sound speed \(v_w = 1/\sqrt{3}\).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the confinement-deconfinement transition is \(T_c \sim 182\) MeV.
- The Polyakov loop profile is calculated at \(T = 400\) MeV.
- The horizon size at initial time is \(r_H \simeq t_0 \simeq 30 \left( \frac{150}{T(\text{MeV})} \right)^2\) in units of microseconds.
- The wall velocity is taken as \(v_w = 1/\sqrt{3}\) (the sound velocity).
- Baryon number in quark nuggets can range from a few thousand to \(\sim 10^{50}\), with sizes varying from fm to meters.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Layek et al. (2005) on baryon inhomogeneity generation from \(Z(3)\) walls by incorporating spontaneous CP violation from the background gauge field, which was first discussed qualitatively by Korthals-Altes et al. (1994). It provides an alternative to Witten's (1984) original mechanism for quark nugget formation, which required a strong first-order quark-hadron phase transition that lattice QCD now indicates is unlikely; the present mechanism works regardless of the order of the transition. The results suggest that quark nuggets formed this way could serve as a viable dark matter candidate entirely within the Standard Model, motivating further studies of \(Z(3)\) domain wall dynamics including friction from quark-gluon scattering.
