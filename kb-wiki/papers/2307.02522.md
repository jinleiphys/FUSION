# High-Energy Collision of Quarks and Mesons in the Schwinger Model: From Tensor Networks to Circuit QED

> With the aim of studying nonperturbative out-of-equilibrium dynamics of high-energy particle collisions on quantum simulators, we investigate the scattering dynamics of lattice quantum electrodynamics in 1+1 dimensions. Working in the bosonized formulation of the model and in the thermodynamic limit, we use uniform-matrix-product-state tensor networks to construct multi-particle wave-packet states, evolve them in time, and detect outgoing particles post collision. This facilitates the numerical simulation of scattering experiments in both confined and deconfined regimes of the model at different energies, giving rise to rich phenomenology, including inelastic production of quark and meson states, meson disintegration, and dynamical string formation and breaking. We obtain elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections, together with time-resolved momentum and position distributions of the outgoing particles. Furthermore, we propose an analog circuit-QED implementation of the scattering process that is native to the platform, requires minimal ingredients and approximations, and enables practical schemes for particle wave-packet preparation and evolution. This study highlights the role of classical and quantum simulation in enhancing our understanding of scattering processes in quantum field theories in real time.

## Key claim

Using uniform-matrix-product-state tensor networks in the bosonized formulation of the Schwinger model, the authors numerically simulate high-energy quark-antiquark and meson-meson scattering in 1+1 dimensions, obtaining elastic and inelastic cross sections and time-resolved momentum distributions, while also proposing an analog circuit-QED implementation of the scattering process that requires minimal ingredients and approximations.

## Method

The authors work with the bosonic dual of the lattice Schwinger model (a massive Sine-Gordon model) in the thermodynamic limit, using uniform matrix product states (uMPS) to construct multi-particle wave-packet states, evolve them in time, and detect outgoing particles post collision. They construct single-particle energy-momentum eigenstates using the uMPS quasiparticle ansatz, form Gaussian wave packets centered at opposite momenta, and time-evolve the state under the lattice Hamiltonian while dynamically expanding the nonuniform region from 150-300 sites up to 600-1300 sites. They project the post-collision state onto a basis of asymptotic two-particle states to obtain momentum-resolved S-matrix elements and elastic scattering probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Quark-antiquark scattering in the deconfined phase: center-of-mass energies up to 30 times the quark mass (E_cm/m_q = 11.4, 23.0, and 28.8 are shown explicitly)
- Elastic scattering probability falls below 0.5 around E_cm/m_q greater than or approximately equal to 28
- Meson-meson scattering in the confined phase: wave packets centered at p_0 = plus or minus 0.6 with E_cm/m_pi1 = 6.84 (for epsilon = 0.04) and 5.95 (for epsilon = 0.07)
- At late time t = 700, asymptotic two-particle states account for about 90 percent (epsilon = 0.04) and 76 percent (epsilon = 0.07) of the state
- Lattice parameters: mu^2 = 0.1 and lambda = 0.5 for all scattering simulations

## Context

This work extends previous tensor-network studies of the Schwinger model that were limited to elastic scattering at either weak coupling (nearly free fermions, Rigobello et al. 2021) or strong coupling (nearly free bosons, Pichler et al. 2016), and advances beyond earlier quantum-simulation proposals based on quantum-link models (Marcos et al. 2013). The results enable future quantum simulation of scattering processes in regimes inaccessible to classical tensor-network methods due to entanglement growth, and the circuit-QED implementation can be generalized to other bosonic theories including the phi^4 theory and multi-flavor fermion generalizations of the Schwinger model.
