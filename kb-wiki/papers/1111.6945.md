# Gluon-gluon elastic scattering amplitude in classical color field of colliding protons

> We present a formalism for gluon-gluon elastic scattering in the presence of the classical color field of the protons in high energy collision. The classical field is obtained by solving the classical Yang-Mills equation in the covariant gauge and treated as a prescribed background for the quantum gluons involved in the scattering process. The interaction between the classical field and the quantum gluon modifies the gluon propagator, and, in turn, the $gg\rightarrow gg$ amplitude. The modified gluon propagator is derived to the first non-zero order of the classical field using the Gaussian approximation in Color Glass Condensate and shown to satisfy the generalized Slavnov-Taylor identity. This formalism is the theoretical basis for our recently proposed classical color field modified minijet model where we show that the $pp$ and $\pbar p$ cross section data from $\sqrt{s}=5$ GeV to 30 TeV can be satisfactorily fitted and the model predicts a $(\ln s)^2$ behavior for large $s$, which saturates the asymptotic behaviour of Froissart bound.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for gluon-gluon elastic scattering where the classical color field of the protons is treated as a prescribed background for the quantum gluons, and the modified gluon propagator is derived to the first non-zero order of the classical field using the Gaussian approximation in Color Glass Condensate; the authors claim this formalism provides the theoretical basis for their classical color field modified minijet model which can fit pp and pbar-p cross section data from sqrt(s)=5 GeV to 30 TeV and predicts a (ln s)^2 behavior for large s.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equation in covariant gauge for the classical color field of each proton, treating the source as static and the total field as a superposition of the two proton fields to leading order. They then introduce the quantum gluon field B on top of the classical background A, work in background gauge, and derive the modified gluon propagator by calculating the two-point Green's function to leading order in the classical field using the IIM Gaussian Ansatz for averaging over random color sources. The calculation shows that vertex corrections vanish due to delta functions in the classical field's momentum dependence, leaving only propagator corrections from diagrams with two interactions with the classical field.

## Key numbers

- The pp and pbar-p cross section data are fitted from sqrt(s)=5 GeV to 30 TeV
- The model predicts a (ln s)^2 behavior for large s, which saturates the asymptotic behaviour of the Froissart bound
- The propagator correction from the 1-1 term (two interactions of type L_int_1) yields a factor proportional to alpha_s N_c / (4 pi) times an integral I(Q^2,x) over k_perp from 1/R_p^2 to Q^2
- The color factor from the 1-1 term gives -N_c delta^{ab} after summing over color indices

## Context

This work extends the QCD-inspired minijet model first introduced in the 1970s (Cline 1973) and later incorporated into eikonal minijet models by various authors (Pancheri 1984, Gaisser 1985, Durand 1987, Capella 1987, Godbole 2005, Achilli 2011) to tame the rapid rise of the minijet cross section that would violate the Froissart bound. The formalism uses the Gaussian approximation from Color Glass Condensate (Iancu 2002b) and the IIM model to describe small-x gluons as a classical field, and it enables the calculation of gluon-gluon scattering amplitudes in high-density gluon regions where collinear factorization may break down, suggesting that un-integrated gluon densities in the k_perp factorization scheme should be used instead.
