# Amending the Vafa-Witten Theorem

> The strong version of the Vafa-Witten theorem is shown may not to hold because the zero condensate from a direct computation of the order parameter is found to be a result on the symmetric vacuum. The validity of the Vafa-Witten theorem relies then on its weak version, that the Goldstone boson is absent in vector-like gauge theories with vanishing θ-angle. The existence of a charged ρ-meson condensate, which violates electromagnetic gauge symmetry, is consistent with this weak version of the Vafa-Witten theorem when applied to strong magnetic fields in QCD.

## Key claim

The strong version of the Vafa-Witten theorem, which asserts that vector-like global symmetries cannot be spontaneously broken in vector-like gauge theories with zero theta angle, may not hold because the zero condensate obtained from a direct computation of the order parameter is only a result on the symmetric vacuum; the theorem's validity then reduces to its weak version, that no Goldstone boson appears.

## Method

The authors examine the direct computation of the charged rho meson condensate in 2-flavor QCD with an external magnetic field, showing that the standard expression for the condensate vanishes due to the absence of tau-plus or tau-minus components in the quark propagator. They then reformulate the path integral by integrating out quark and gluon fields and integrating in bilocal colorless meson fields, which introduces isospin-dependent fields into the propagator and prevents the trivial zero. Using a 0-dimensional toy model and a D-dimensional O(N) lambda-phi^4 model in the large N limit, they demonstrate that the generating functional can have multiple branches (symmetric and non-symmetric), and that choosing the symmetric branch from the outset yields a vanishing condensate while the effective action formalism reveals nonzero physical condensates.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass of the charged rho meson in an external magnetic field is given by m^2_{rho^pm}(B_ext) = m^2_{rho^pm} - e B_ext, with the critical field value B_crit = m^2_{rho^pm}/e where the effective mass becomes imaginary.
- In the 0-dimensional toy model, the effective action is Gamma = 6 lambda r^(2/3) phi^2 - lambda phi^4, yielding nonzero solutions phi = +/- sqrt(3) r^(1/3) for the physical condensate.
- In the D-dimensional O(N) lambda-phi^4 model in the large N limit, the translational invariant generating functional yields two branches: one symmetric branch with 3 Theta = arccos(J/(8 N^(1/2) r tilde_lambda)) and one non-symmetric branch with 3 Theta = 2pi - arccos(J/(8 N^(1/2) r tilde_lambda)).

## Context

This work extends the debate between QCDnegative (which claimed charged vector mesons cannot condense in a magnetic field based on the Vafa-Witten theorem) and QCDpositive (which argued condensation is possible via the Higgs mechanism). The authors show that the direct computation of the condensate in Vafa-Witten (1984) and QCDnegative only probes the symmetric vacuum, and that the effective action formalism or the introduction of meson fields as in Li and Wang (2000) can yield nonzero condensates. This enables a resolution of the controversy over charged rho meson condensation in strong magnetic fields in QCD, consistent with the weak version of the Vafa-Witten theorem (no Goldstone boson) but not the strong version (no condensate).
