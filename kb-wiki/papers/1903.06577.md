# Sigma models on quantum computers

> We formulate a discretization of sigma models suitable for simulation by quantum computers. Space is substituted by a lattice, as usually done in lattice field theory, while the target space (a sphere) is replaced by the "fuzzy sphere", a construction well known from non-commutative geometry. Contrary to more naive discretizations of the sphere, in this construction the exact $O(3)$ symmetry is maintained, which suggests that the discretized model is in the same universality class as the continuum model. That would allow for continuum results to be obtained for very rough discretizations of the target space as long as the space discretization is made fine enough. The cost of performing time-evolution, measured as the number of CNOT operations necessary, is $12 L T/Δt $, where $L$ is the number of spatial sites, $T$ the maximum time extent and $Δt$ the time spacing.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that discretizing the target space of the O(3) sigma model using the "fuzzy sphere" construction preserves the exact O(3) symmetry, which suggests the discretized model is in the same universality class as the continuum model, allowing continuum results to be obtained even with very rough target-space discretizations as long as the spatial lattice is fine enough.

## Method

The authors replace the continuous sphere target space of the O(3) sigma model with the fuzzy sphere, a non-commutative geometry construction where functions on the sphere are replaced by matrices in a finite-dimensional representation of SU(2). For the minimal case of spin j=1/2, each spatial site has a 4-dimensional Hilbert space encoded by two qubits. The Hamiltonian is split into a kinetic term (diagonal in the chosen basis) and three interaction terms from nearest-neighbor couplings, and time evolution is implemented via a Suzuki-Trotter decomposition with each term implemented as a quantum circuit using CNOT and single-qubit gates.

## Key numbers

* The cost of performing time evolution is 12 L T / Δt CNOT operations, where L is the number of spatial sites, T the maximum time extent, and Δt the time spacing.
* For the j=1/2 fuzzy sphere, the single-site Hilbert space dimension is 4, corresponding to 2 qubits per spatial site.
* The kinetic term eigenvalues are 0 for the a=0 state and g^2 for the a=1,2,3 states, with the authors setting g^2=1 for simplicity.
* The simulation results shown used Δt=0.2, Δx=1, g^2=1 with L=3 spatial sites (6 qubits total) and 4000 measurements per data point.

## Context

This work addresses the challenge of implementing bosonic fields on quantum computers, where the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space per site must be truncated. It extends prior attempts to discretize bosonic fields using finite sets of values, such as the platonic solid discretizations of the sphere studied by Hasenfratz (2001) and Caracciolo (2001), which broke the O(3) symmetry and raised questions about universality. The fuzzy sphere approach, originally developed by Hoppe (1989) and Madore (1991) for non-commutative geometry and membrane theory, maintains exact O(3) symmetry and can be generalized to principal chiral models and gauge theories.
