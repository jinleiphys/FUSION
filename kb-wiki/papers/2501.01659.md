# Modification of single-hole-like states by configuration mixing in the $^{99-131}$In

> Large-scale shell-model calculations are performed for the $9/2^+_{\rm g.s.}$, $1/2^-_1$, $3/2^-_1$, and $5/2^-_1$ states in the odd-$A$ indium isotopes with $N=50-82$. The calculated energy levels, electromagnetic moments, and spectroscopic factors exhibit remarkable agreement with the experimental data due to significant configuration mixing for the neutron numbers away from the closed shells. The $1/2^-_1$ energy levels closely follow the trend of effective single-particle energies, which are determined using the fractional occupancies of neutron orbitals. However, configuration mixing with the proton $p_{3/2}$ and $f_{5/2}$ orbitals in the actual shell-model calculations plays a crucial role in accurately reproducing the positions of the $1/2^-_1$ levels, ensuring better agreement with the experimental data across the entire isotopic chain.

# Modification of single-hole-like states by configuration mixing in the \(^{99-131}\)In

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations reproduce the experimental energy levels, electromagnetic moments, and spectroscopic factors of the \(9/2^+_{\text{g.s.}}\), \(1/2^-_1\), \(3/2^-_1\), and \(5/2^-_1\) states in odd-\(A\) indium isotopes across \(N=50-82\), but the agreement for the \(1/2^-_1\) levels depends critically on configuration mixing with the proton \(p_{3/2}\) and \(f_{5/2}\) orbitals in the full model space, not merely on the effective single-particle energies derived from neutron fractional occupancies.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the code kshell with a valence space comprising four proton orbitals (\(0f_{5/2}\), \(1p_{3/2}\), \(1p_{1/2}\), \(0g_{9/2}\)) and five neutron orbitals (\(1d_{5/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\), \(0g_{7/2}\), \(0h_{11/2}\)) above an inert \(^{78}\)Ni core. The neutron-neutron interaction was taken from the SNBG1 interaction, the proton-neutron interaction from a version of the \(V_{\text{MU}}\) interaction (with overall central force scaled by 0.84), and the proton single-particle energies were adjusted to reproduce the single-hole states in \(^{131}\)In. No truncation was applied; the largest \(M\)-scheme dimension was \(3.4 \times 10^8\) for \(^{115}\)In.

## Key numbers

- The largest \(M\)-scheme dimension in the calculations was \(3.4 \times 10^8\) for \(^{115}\)In.
- For \(^{107}\)In, the shell-model calculation predicts the \(5/2^-_1\) state at 1770 keV; experimentally an unconfirmed state at 1519 keV is assigned as \(5/2^-_1\).
- For \(^{109}\)In, the experimental \(5/2^-\) state at 1441 keV is assigned as \(5/2^-_1\); for \(^{113}\)In, the \((3/2^-,5/2^-)\) state at 1106 keV; for \(^{123}\)In, the \((3/2^-,5/2^-)\) state at 1138 keV; for \(^{125}\)In, the \((1/2,3/2,5/2^-)\) state at 1220 keV.
- The rms deviation for quadrupole moments with effective charges \((1.6, 0.8)e\) is \(0.117^{+0.020}_{-0.018}\) eb; with \((1.65, 1.04)e\) it is \(0.061^{+0.009}_{-0.001}\) eb.
- The rms deviation for magnetic moments is \(0.10669^{+0.00044}_{-0.00003}\) \(\mu_N\).
- For \(^{115}\)In, the calculated \(B(E2; 3/2^-_1 \rightarrow 1/2^-_1)\) is 14.5 W.u. with \((1.6,0.8)e\) and 21.3 W.u. with \((1.65,1.04)e\); the experimental \(B(M1)\) limit is \(\ge 0.0047\) W.u.
- For \(^{109}\)In, the calculated \(B(E2; 3/2^-_1 \rightarrow 1/2^-_1)\) is 12.2 W.u. with \((1.6,0.8)e\) and 17.3 W.u. with \((1.65,1.04)e\); the experimental \(B(M1)\) is \(0.96^{+30}_{-31}\) W.u.
- For \(^{111}\)In, the calculated \(B(E2; 5/2^-_1 \rightarrow 1/2^-_1)\) is 14.6 W.u. with \((1.6,0.8)e\) and 22.2 W.u. with \((1.65,1.04)e\); the experimental limit is \(>0.47\) W.u.

## Context

This work extends large-scale shell-model studies of proton-hole states in the \(Z=50\) region, following earlier calculations for Sb isotopes by Utsuno et al. using the same \(V_{\text{MU}}\)-based proton-neutron interaction. It addresses the long-standing debate between tensor-force-driven shell evolution (Otsuka et al.) and particle-core coupling (Sorlin and Porquet) by demonstrating that configuration mixing involving both neutron excitations (class 2) and proton excitations to \(p_{3/2}\) and \(f_{5/2}\) orbitals (class 3) is essential to reproduce the \(1/2^-_1\) level systematics. The results enable a unified description of In and Sb isotopes and provide a benchmark for future studies of shell evolution in the \(^{100-132}\)Sn region.
