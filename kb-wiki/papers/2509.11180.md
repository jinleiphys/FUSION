# Few-Nucleon Systems within Finite-Cutoff Pionless EFT

> We investigate pionless effective field theory (\nopieft) with finite-cutoff regularization as a framework for describing few-nucleon systems. This formulation incorporates effective-range effects already at leading order (LO), thereby reaching next-to-leading-order (NLO) accuracy while maintaining computational efficiency. Using correlated-Gaussian stochastic variational methods in a weak harmonic-oscillator trap, together with neutral and Coulomb-modified quantization conditions, we calculate binding energies and low-energy $S$-wave scattering parameters for systems with up to five nucleons. At an optimal cutoff, the computed binding energies of the deuteron, triton, helion, and alpha particle reproduce experimental values at the percent level once a three-body force is included. Scattering parameters for proton--proton, nucleon--deuteron, nucleon--triton, proton--helion, deuteron--deuteron, and nucleon--alpha channels are obtained and found to be consistent with both experimental data and existing NLO \nopieft\ calculations. These results demonstrate that finite-cutoff \nopieft\ offers a robust and predictive framework for few-body nuclear physics.

## Key claim

Finite-cutoff pionless EFT, where the cutoff is chosen so that leading-order interactions already reproduce the empirical effective range, yields binding energies and scattering parameters for few-nucleon systems up to A=5 that match experimental data and next-to-leading-order calculations at the percent level, provided a three-body force is included for A=3 and A=4.

## Method

The authors used a leading-order pionless EFT with Gaussian-regulated contact interactions at a finite momentum cutoff, with two-body low-energy constants fitted to reproduce the singlet and triplet scattering lengths and effective ranges. They solved the few-body Schrodinger equation using the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions in a weak harmonic-oscillator trap, then extracted S-wave scattering parameters from the trapped spectra using neutral and Coulomb-modified quantization conditions. The three-body force was adjusted to reproduce the triton, helion, and alpha-particle binding energies simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.213 MeV, compared to experimental 2.225 MeV (less than 1% deviation)
- Triton binding energy: 8.421 MeV, compared to experimental 8.482 MeV (less than 1% deviation)
- Helion binding energy: 7.685 MeV, compared to experimental 7.718 MeV (less than 1% deviation)
- Alpha-particle binding energy: 28.423 MeV, compared to experimental 28.296 MeV (0.5% deviation)
- Proton-proton scattering length: -7.98 +/- 0.03 fm, effective range: 2.69 +/- 0.09 fm
- Neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length: 0.71 +/- 0.01 fm, effective range: 1.45 +/- 0.02 fm
- Neutron-deuteron quartet scattering length: 6.29 +/- 0.01 fm, effective range: 2.14 +/- 0.02 fm
- Neutron-alpha doublet scattering length: 2.31 +/- 0.01 fm, effective range: 1.58 +/- 0.05 fm
- Two-body LECs: C_s = -31.246 MeV, C_t = -67.599 MeV, D = 16.4 MeV
- Cutoffs: Lambda_s = 1.11479 fm^{-1}, Lambda_t = 1.29624 fm^{-1}, Lambda_3 = 1.78885 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the finite-cutoff pionless EFT approach previously explored in Refs. KirGriShu10, KieGat16, and SchGirGne21, and is closely related to the strategy of ConSchGne25 where an artificial range is introduced at LO and removed perturbatively at NLO. The method reproduces NLO pionless EFT results from SchBaz23 for A=3 systems and from RojSch25 for A=4 systems, while also matching experimental data from Dil71, SchJacAri03, and HauWieAri20. This framework enables systematic few-body calculations at NLO accuracy with LO computational cost, and provides benchmarks for future studies of five-nucleon scattering, which remains one of the least explored frontiers of low-energy nuclear theory.
