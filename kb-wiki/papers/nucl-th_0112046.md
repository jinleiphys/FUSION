# Strangeness Production in Microscopic Transport Models

> Strangeness production in microscopic transport models for relativistic heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC is reviewed: after a brief introduction into elementary strangeness production processes, the main emphasis is put on strangeness as indicator of the nuclear equation of state, the excitation function of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio and strangeness as a deconfinement indicator.

## Key claim
Strangeness production in microscopic transport models is reviewed as a tool for studying relativistic heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC energies, with the main emphasis on using strangeness as an indicator of the nuclear equation of state, the excitation function of the K+/π+ ratio, and as a deconfinement indicator; however, the paper notes that the K+/π+ ratio as a deconfinement indicator remains ambiguous at best, and that while non-hadronic medium effects like color-ropes can enhance strangeness yields to near-compatibility with data, this does not prove their validity.

## Method
The paper reviews microscopic transport models based on the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) equation, which describes the time-evolution of the one-particle distribution function including mean field interactions and binary scattering. Strangeness production mechanisms are examined through direct parametrization of exclusive channels (e.g., p+p → p+Λ+K+) and resonance models (e.g., N*1710 → Y+K+), with string excitation and fragmentation dominating at higher energies. The review compares predictions from multiple models including UrQMD, RQMD, HSD, and hybrid hydrodynamical+microscopic approaches against experimental data from SIS to RHIC energies.

## Key numbers
- The string-tension κ is increased from its vacuum value of κ=1 GeV/fm to κ=3 GeV/fm for strings fragmenting in high energy-density regions, producing a drastic enhancement of Ω- formation probability by more than one order of magnitude.
- The K+/π+ ratio is expected to reach its maximum value around a beam energy of 30 GeV/u, based on statistical model calculations.
- In UrQMD calculations for Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/u, the standard calculation strongly underestimates (multi-)strange particle yields in central collisions, particularly for the Ω-.
- Inverse slope parameters T from exponential fits to dN_i/d^2m_Tdy in the range m_T-m_i<1 GeV show that Ω's suffer on average only one hadronic interaction, while N's and Λ's suffer far more collisions before freeze-out.
- The specific entropy increases by a factor of 4-5 when going from SPS to RHIC energy in hybrid models, yet inverse slopes increase only slightly due to the first-order phase transition softening transverse expansion.

## Context
This review extends prior work on microscopic transport models including (I)QMD, VUU/BUU, ART, BEM, ARC, LUZIFER, RQMD, UrQMD, HSD, VNI/BMS, and AMPT, covering their range of applicability from SIS to RHIC energies. The paper discusses how strangeness production relates to the nuclear equation of state, building on findings by Hartnack (2000) showing K- yield sensitivity to K+ potentials via the flavor-exchange reaction K-+N ↔ Y+π, and by other authors (1999) showing K+ yield sensitivity to the equation of state. The work enables future studies of deconfinement through combined macro+micro models coupling hydrodynamical descriptions of the deconfined phase with microscopic calculations for the hadronic phase, particularly for using Ξ and Ω spectra as probes of QGP expansion prior to hadronization.
