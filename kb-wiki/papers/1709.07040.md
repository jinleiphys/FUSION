# Phase transition in compact stars: nucleation mechanism and $γ$-ray bursts revisited

> We have revisited the nucleation process based on the Lifshitz-Kagan theory, which is the underlying mechanism of conversion of a pulsar constituted of hadronic matter to a quark star. We have selected appropriate models that have been tested against experimental and observational constraints to restrict the model arbitrariness present in previous investigations. The phase transition pressures and chemical potentials have been identified and afterwards, the tunneling probabilities and the nucleation time were computed. The critical pressures for which the half life of the metastable hadronic phase is one year were obtained. Even with the restrictions imposed to the selection of models, the results remained model dependent, but we found that the tunneling that makes possible the appearance of stable matter requires an overpressure that is practically independent of the quark matter bag constant. Finally, we have confirmed that the nucleation process can be one of the causes of gamma-ray bursts.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the quantum nucleation mechanism for converting a hadronic pulsar into a quark star, finding that even after restricting hadronic models to those satisfying nuclear matter and observational constraints, the results remain model-dependent; however, the overpressure required for tunneling to stable quark matter is practically independent of the quark matter bag constant.

## Method

The authors used the nonlinear Walecka model (NLWM) with IU-FSU and NL3ωρ parameterizations for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model for quark matter, restricting the bag constant to values within the stability window (148 MeV ≤ B^(1/4) ≤ 168 MeV). They computed the Gibbs phase coexistence conditions at zero temperature, then applied the relativistic Lifshitz-Kagan theory of quantum nucleation to calculate tunneling probabilities and nucleation times for the formation of quark matter bubbles in metastable hadronic matter. The surface tension σ was treated as a free parameter in the range 10–50 MeV/fm².

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational masses for hadronic stars: 1.97 M⊙ (IU-FSU, nucleons only), 1.56 M⊙ (IU-FSU with 8 hyperons), 2.76 M⊙ (NL3ωρ, nucleons only), 2.25 M⊙ (NL3ωρ with 8 hyperons)
- Central pressures of maximum-mass stars: 350 MeV/fm³ (IU-FSU, nucleons), 235 MeV/fm³ (IU-FSU, hyperons), 454 MeV/fm³ (NL3ωρ, nucleons), 206 MeV/fm³ (NL3ωρ, hyperons)
- Phase coexistence pressures P0 for IU-FSU with MIT* bag model: 12.4 MeV/fm³ (B^(1/4)=153 MeV), 31.0 MeV/fm³ (158 MeV), 60.0 MeV/fm³ (163 MeV), 97.3 MeV/fm³ (168 MeV)
- Phase coexistence pressures P0 for NL3ωρ with MIT* bag model: 2.8 MeV/fm³ (B^(1/4)=153 MeV), 7.4 MeV/fm³ (158 MeV), 12.6 MeV/fm³ (163 MeV), 19.1 MeV/fm³ (168 MeV)
- Nucleation time τ varies from many orders of magnitude larger than the universe age to almost zero in less than one percent increase of pressure P

## Context

This work extends the pure energetic approach of Deb et al. (2016) by also accounting for the phase conversion conditions, lifetime of the metastable star, and the nucleation mechanism. It restricts model choices to those tested against nuclear matter constraints from Dutra et al. (2014, 2016) and the observational discovery of two massive pulsars (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432) with masses around 2 M⊙. The results confirm that the nucleation process can be one of the causes of gamma-ray bursts, as previously proposed in the literature.
