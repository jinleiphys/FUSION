# Cold Quark Matter, Quadratic Corrections and Gauge/String Duality

## Key claim
The paper estimates that the pressure of cold quark matter contains a quadratic correction term proportional to μ², which is strongly enhanced in the intermediate chemical potential region compared to perturbative QCD predictions, but the result depends on a deformation parameter c fixed from meson Regge trajectories rather than derived from first principles.

## Method
The author uses a deformed AdS/CFT correspondence, starting from an extremal Reissner-Nordström black hole in Euclidean AdS₅ with a z-dependent exponential factor H = exp(½ c z²) inserted into the metric. The gauge potential A₀(z) is obtained from the condition of Weyl invariance on a string world-sheet, and the baryon chemical potential μ and baryon number density ρ are identified via the AdS/CFT dictionary. The pressure is then derived by integrating ρ(μ), with the parameter b normalized to match perturbative QCD at one-loop order for non-interacting quarks.

## Key numbers
- Quadratic correction coefficient: μ⋆² ≈ 4.1 GeV² for light (u,d) quarks, derived from c ≈ 0.9 GeV² fixed by the ρ(n) meson Regge trajectory slope.
- Perturbative QCD estimate for Nf=2: (27/2)(m_u² + m_d²) ≈ 6 × 10⁻⁴ GeV², using m_u = 3 MeV and m_d = 6 MeV.
- For Nf=3, the perturbative estimate is 9(m_u² + m_d² + m_s²) ≈ 0.1 GeV², with m_s ≈ 0.1 GeV.
- Lower limit estimate: μ_min ~ 1.5 μ_c, giving μ_c ≈ 1.3 GeV.
- Upper limit estimate: μ_max ~ 3.3 μ⋆ ~ 5 μ_c.
- The baryon number density in the non-deformed model (c=0) is ρ(μ) = 4b μ³, with b = Nf/(12π² Nc³).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies on quadratic corrections in gauge/string duality, specifically following the same deformation approach used to find the T² term in the pressure at finite temperature (Andreev, 2009) and the quadratic correction in the two-current correlator (Andreev, 2007). The resulting equation of state p(μ) = b(μ⁴ - μ⋆² μ²) matches the phenomenological "fuzzy bag model" parametrization proposed by Kapusta (2001) and later discussed by Alford (2005) and Pisarski and McLerran (2007). The interpretation differs from Pisarski and McLerran (2007), who attribute the μ² term to nonperturbative corrections, while this work treats it as a power correction from the string theory deformation. The model enables predictions for the trace anomaly and speed of sound in cold quark matter, analogous to the finite-temperature results of Andreev (2009).
