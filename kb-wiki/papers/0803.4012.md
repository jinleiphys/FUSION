# Color Transparency in Semi-Inclusive Electroproduction of rho Mesons

> We study the electroproduction of rho mesons in nuclei at intermediate energies, deriving a treatment of the energy lost by the rho in each step of multiple-scattering. This enables a close match between calculations and the experimental kinematic conditions. A standard Glauber calculation is presented, and then the effects of color-transparency are included. The influence of poor experimental resolution on the extracted transparency is assessed. The effects of $ρ$ meson decay inside the nucleus are examined, and are typically about 5 percent at most. This effect disappears rapidly as $Q^2$ increases from about 1 to 3 GeV$^2$, causing a rise in the transparency that is not attributable to color transparency. The size of color transparency effects for C and Fe nuclei is studied for values of $Q^2$ up to 10 GeV$^2$. The detailed results depend strongly on the assumed value of the $ρN$ cross section. The overall effects of color transparency are greater than about 10 % for both nuclear targets if $Q^2$ is greater than about 5 GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observed rise in transparency for rho meson electroproduction as Q² increases from about 1 to 3 GeV² is partly caused by the disappearance of rho meson decay effects inside the nucleus (about 5% at most), not solely by color transparency; genuine color transparency effects become greater than about 10% only when Q² exceeds about 5 GeV², and the detailed results depend strongly on the assumed value of the rho-nucleon total cross section (taken between 25 and 30 mb).

## Method

The authors developed a Glauber multiple-scattering treatment that accounts for the energy lost by the rho meson in each rescattering step, matching the experimental kinematic conditions of the JLab experiment led by Hafidi. They computed the transparency as the ratio of the nuclear cross section to A times the free-nucleon cross section, summing contributions from zero, one, and two elastic rescatterings, and then incorporated color transparency using the quantum diffusion model where the effective cross section grows linearly with propagation distance up to a coherence length l_h = 2p_rho / (0.7 GeV²). The effects of rho meson decay into two pions inside the nucleus were included by replacing the total cross section with an effective cross section that accounts for the decaying rho component and the growing two-pion component.

## Key numbers

- Rho meson decay effects inside the nucleus are typically about 5% at most, and this effect disappears rapidly as Q² increases from about 1 to 3 GeV²
- Color transparency effects are greater than about 10% for both carbon and iron targets if Q² is greater than about 5 GeV²
- The coherence length l_c = 2nu/(M_rho² + Q²) ranges between 0.4 and 0.9 fm for the JLab kinematics studied
- The rho-nucleon total cross section sigma_tot was varied between 25 and 30 mb
- The coherence length for PLC expansion l_h = 2p_rho / (0.7 GeV²), with Delta M² = 0.7 GeV² (varied to 0.8 GeV² with negligible effect except at Q² ≈ 10 GeV²)
- The initial PLC cross section is sigma_PLC = sigma_tot * (n² ⟨k_t²⟩ / Q²) with n=2 and ⟨k_t²⟩^{1/2} ≈ 0.35 GeV
- The rho meson width Gamma = 149 MeV
- The momentum transfer variable t was restricted to -0.4 GeV² ≥ -(t-t_0) ≥ -0.1 GeV²
- The virtual photon-proton center-of-mass energy W was taken as W > 2 GeV, with a range between 2 and 3.1 GeV
- Q² varied between 0.7 and 3.2 GeV² in the JLab experiment, with the paper studying Q² up to 10 GeV²
- The photon energy nu varies from 2.2 to 4.8 GeV for Q² between 0.7 and 1.5 GeV², and from 2.4 to 4.6 GeV for Q² greater than 1.5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for color transparency in meson production, following earlier studies of pion production at Jefferson Laboratory (2007) that reported evidence for the onset of color transparency, and prior calculations by Falter (2002) and by Kopeliovich (2001). The paper was stimulated by the imminent publication of a JLab experiment led by Hafidi using deuteron, carbon, and iron targets, and provides calculations that distinguish the genuine color transparency signal from the kinematic rise caused by rho decay effects, enabling experimentalists to properly extract the color transparency signal at higher Q² values.
