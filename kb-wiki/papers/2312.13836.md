# Effect of the equation of state for dilute neutron matter on the composition of the inner crust of neutron stars

> The composition of the inner crust of neutron stars is usually studied using phenomenological interactions such as Skyrme energy-density functionals. But most of these functionals do not agree well with ab-initio calculations of very dilute neutron matter. In this work, we study the inner crust of neutron stars in the model of phase coexistence of dense neutron-rich nuclear clusters and dilute neutron gas, and we investigate how employing a realistic microscopic equation of state to the neutron gas alters the composition. Our results indicate that with a functional that reproduces the correct equation of state of neutron matter at moderate densities, one can obtain a good description of the crust even if the functional does not have the correct behavior at extremely low density.

## Key claim

Using a simple phase coexistence model, the authors show that a Skyrme energy-density functional (BSk22) which reproduces the correct equation of state of neutron matter at moderate densities (above about 0.02 fm\(^{-3}\)) can give a good description of the inner crust composition even if it does not have the correct behavior at extremely low density, whereas a functional (SLy4) that deviates more strongly from ab-initio results at low density yields significantly different predictions.

## Method

The authors model the inner crust as a phase coexistence of dense neutron-rich nuclear clusters (liquid phase) and a dilute neutron gas (gas phase), neglecting surface energy, Coulomb energy, and shell effects. They minimize the thermodynamic potential using a Skyrme energy-density functional (SLy4 or BSk22) for the liquid phase, while for the gas phase they either use the same Skyrme functional or replace it with a realistic microscopic equation of state from third-order Bogoliubov Many-Body Perturbation Theory (BMBPT3) based on realistic neutron-neutron interactions. The resulting neutron and proton densities in each phase, the volume fraction of clusters, the proton fraction, and the total pressure are compared as functions of total baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-drip density obtained with the simple model is \(n_{B}^{\text{(ND)}} \approx 6.6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), approximately three times higher than the expected value.
- The transition from inner crust to outer core occurs at \(n_B \approx 0.09\) fm\(^{-3}\) when using SLy4 for both phases.
- The BSk22 functional almost coincides with BMBPT3 at Fermi momenta \(k_F \gtrsim 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The discrepancy between BSk22 and ab-initio results is essentially limited to densities below \(\sim 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the phase coexistence model of Avancini (2008), Martin (2015), and Grams (2017) by replacing the phenomenological Skyrme equation of state for the dilute neutron gas with a realistic microscopic one from BMBPT3 (Palaniappan 2023). It directly addresses the known failure of Skyrme functionals to reproduce ab-initio neutron matter results at very low densities, noted by Yang (2016). The results enable future studies using the compressible liquid-drop model (Baym 1971, Vinas 2017, Carreau 2019) that can include surface and Coulomb energies for more reliable predictions of inner crust composition.
