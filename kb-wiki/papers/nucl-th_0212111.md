# High-p_T Pion Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at RHIC energies

> Perturbative QCD results on pion production are presented in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions from CERN SPS up to RHIC energy. A K_{jet}(s, p_T, Q) factor obtained from jet production is applied to perform next-to-leading order calculations. Using the intrinsic transverse momentum (k_T) we determined transverse momentum spectra for pions in wide energy region. We have investigated nuclear multiscattering and the Cronin effect at RHIC energies.

# High-p_T Pion Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at RHIC energies

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD calculations using a K_jet(s, p_T, Q) factor derived from jet production, combined with intrinsic transverse momentum (k_T) broadening from nuclear multiscattering, can reproduce preliminary PHENIX data for pion production in peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but central collisions show a suppression factor of approximately 4-5 that the model cannot explain without invoking jet quenching.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD calculations for pion production by applying a K_jet factor obtained from jet production in proton-proton collisions to the leading-order Born partonic cross section, then incorporating nuclear effects through modified parton distribution functions with shadowing and a two-dimensional Gaussian intrinsic k_T distribution. The Gaussian width was parameterized as <k_T^2>_pA = <k_T^2>_pp + C * h_pA(b), where C represents the average increase in transverse momentum width per nucleon-nucleon collision and h_pA(b) is a geometrical effectivity function that can be saturated. Fragmentation was handled using the KKP parametrization with scale Q' = p_T/2.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian width <k_T^2>_pp = 1.8 GeV^2 at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV and 1.5 GeV^2 at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV, found to be p_T-independent in the window 2 GeV <= p_T <= 6 GeV
- Saturation parameter C_sat = 0.4 and maximum number of collisions nu_max = 3 for the nuclear broadening
- Number of binary collisions in peripheral Au+Au: N_bin,p = 19 for 60-80% centrality at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV; N_bin,p = 10 for 70-80% centrality at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV
- Number of binary collisions in central Au+Au: N_bin,c = 857 for top 10% centrality at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV; N_bin,c = 940 at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV
- Suppression factor of approximately 4-5 between central Au+Au data and theoretical spectra
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA shows a difference of approximately 5 between central data and calculations
- Cronin peak maximum located at p_T approximately 3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier pQCD calculations by Barnafoldi et al. (2001) and Levai et al. (2000) that established the dependence of nuclear effects (specifically the Cronin effect first observed by Cronin et al. in 1975) on the choice of renormalization, factorization, and fragmentation scales. The results enable quantitative separation of initial-state effects (Cronin enhancement from nuclear multiscattering) from final-state effects (jet quenching) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, with the observed suppression in central collisions pointing to the need for jet quenching mechanisms as discussed at Quark Matter 2001.
