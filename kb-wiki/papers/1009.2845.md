# Mixing of fermion fields of opposite parities and baryon resonances

> We consider a loop mixing of two fermion fields of opposite parities whereas the parity is conserved in a Lagrangian. Such kind of mixing is specific for fermions and has no analogy in boson case. Possible applications of this effect may be related with physics of baryon resonances. The obtained matrix propagator defines a pair of unitary partial amplitudes which describe the production of resonances of spin $J$ and different parity ${1/2}^{\pm}$ or ${3/2}^{\pm}$. The use of our amplitudes for joint description of $πN$ partial waves $P_{13}$ and $D_{13}$ shows that the discussed effect is clearly seen in these partial waves as the specific form of interference between resonance and background. Another interesting application of this effect may be a pair of partial waves $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ where the picture is more complicated due to presence of several resonance states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that loop mixing of two fermion fields with opposite parities, even when parity is conserved in the Lagrangian, produces a specific interference pattern between resonance and background in partial waves that is clearly visible in πN scattering data for the P13 and D13 partial waves; the effect is specific to fermions and has no boson analogue.

## Method

The authors derive a matrix dressed propagator for two fermion fields of opposite parities using an off-shell projection operator basis (Λ± and Λ±γ5) to solve the Dyson–Schwinger equation. They then construct two-channel partial wave amplitudes for πN → πN, πN → ηN, and ηN → ηN for both spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances, incorporating self-energy loops via dispersion relations and on-mass-subtraction renormalization. The resulting amplitudes are applied to fit the P13 and D13 partial waves from the George Washington University partial wave analysis (Arndt et al., 2006) using a three-channel approach (πN, ηN, σN) with an effective σN channel for the ππN state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier observation of opposite-parity fermion mixing (Kaloshin, 2006) and provides a field-theoretic framework for describing overlapping baryon resonances of opposite parity, going beyond simplified quantum-mechanical mixing models reviewed by Beuthe (2003) and developed by Esposito et al. (2002), Blaschke et al. (2004), and others. The resulting amplitudes are shown to be equivalent to a specific variant of the analytical K-matrix parametrization (Babelon et al., 1976; Arndt et al., 1985). The approach enables joint description of partial wave pairs such as P13/D13 and S11/P11, with the latter pair noted as more complicated due to multiple resonance states and the ongoing issues with the Roper resonance interpretation discussed by Capstick et al. (2000), Krewald et al. (2000), and Sarantsev et al. (2008).
