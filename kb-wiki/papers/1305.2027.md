# Sum rule approach to a soft dipole mode in $Λ$ hypernuclei

> Applying the sum rule approach, we investigate the energy of a soft dipole motion in $Λ$ hypernuclei, which results from a dipole oscillation of a $Λ$ hyperon against the core nucleus. To this end, we systematically study single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, from $^{16}_{\;\,Λ}$O to $^{208}_{\;\;\;Λ}$Pb, for which the ground state wave function is obtained in the framework of Hartree-Fock method with several Skyrme-type $ΛN$ interactions. Our results indicate that the excitation energy of the soft dipole $Λ$ mode, $E_{sdΛ}$, decreases as the mass number increases. We find that the excitation energy is well parametrized as $E_{sdΛ}=26.6A^{-1/3}+11.2A^{-2/3}$ MeV as a function of mass number $A$.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of the soft dipole Λ mode (E_sdΛ) in single-Λ hypernuclei decreases with mass number and can be parametrized as E_sdΛ = 26.6A^{-1/3} + 11.2A^{-2/3} MeV, though this formula somewhat overestimates the energy for ^{208}_ΛPb.

## Method

The authors employed the sum rule approach, computing the energy-weighted (m_1^Λ) and non-energy-weighted (m_0^Λ) sum rules for the dipole operator acting between the Λ hyperon and the core nucleus. Ground state wave functions for nine single-Λ hypernuclei from ^{16}_ΛO to ^{208}_ΛPb were obtained using the Hartree-Fock method with the SkM* NN interaction and six different Skyrme-type ΛN interaction parameter sets (No. 1-6), assuming spherical symmetry and using the filling approximation for odd-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The empirical formula for the soft dipole Λ mode excitation energy is E_sdΛ = 26.6A^{-1/3} + 11.2A^{-2/3} MeV, obtained by least square fit to all data points from six ΛN interactions
- The Λ hyperon mass is m_Λ = 1115.68 MeV/c^2 and the nucleon mass is m = 938.92 MeV/c^2
- The coefficient a_1 = (t_1^Λ + t_2^Λ)/4 is 26.3 MeV·fm^5 for parameter sets No. 1-4, 0 for No. 5, and 45.0 MeV·fm^5 for No. 6
- The factor (Zm_Λ/M)^2 takes values 0.591, 0.534, and 0.518 for ^{40}_ΛCa, ^{51}_ΛV, and ^{64}_ΛNi respectively
- The soft dipole Λ mode appears at around 10 MeV in ^{16}_ΛO and at around 4 MeV in ^{208}_ΛPb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RPA study of a novel soft dipole Λ mode in the double-Λ hypernucleus ^{18}_{ΛΛ}O (Minato and Hagino, 2012) to single-Λ hypernuclei across a wide mass range. The sum rule approach provides a simpler alternative to RPA for single-Λ hypernuclei, where broken time-reversal symmetry complicates RPA calculations. The results are compared with shell model calculations using meson-exchange YN interactions for ^{16}_ΛO (Tzeng et al., 2000) and ^{40}_ΛCa (Tzeng et al., 2002), showing reasonable agreement. The work enables experimental searches for this mode, particularly in heavier hypernuclei where the lower excitation energy (around 4 MeV in ^{208}_ΛPb) may allow discrimination from core excitations.
