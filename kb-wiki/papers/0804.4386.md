# Early dynamics of transversally thermalized matter

> We argue that the idea that the parton system created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is formed in a state with transverse momenta close to thermodynamic equilibrium and its subsequent dynamics at early times is dominated by pure transverse hydrodynamics of the perfect fluid is compatible with the data collected at RHIC. This scenario of early parton dynamics may help to solve the problem of early equilibration.

# Early dynamics of transversally thermalized matter

## Key claim
The authors argue that the parton system formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC may be created in a state where only the transverse momenta are close to thermodynamic equilibrium, with early-time dynamics governed by pure transverse hydrodynamics of a perfect fluid. This scenario is presented as a possible solution to the early thermalization puzzle, but the claim is conditional: the model is shown to be compatible with existing data, not proven as the unique explanation.

## Method
The authors model the early stage of heavy-ion collisions as a superposition of independent transverse clusters at fixed rapidity, with longitudinal dynamics treated as free-streaming. They solve the transverse hydrodynamic equations based on energy-momentum conservation using an energy-momentum tensor appropriate for a gluon-dominated system (degeneracy factor ν_g = 16), with initial transverse energy density profiles from the Glauber model of wounded nucleons. Parton spectra and elliptic flow v_2 are computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription on a constant-temperature freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T_i ≈ 250 MeV at the origin (x_⊥ = 0) yields transverse momentum spectra matching the slope of π⁺ spectra measured by PHENIX at √s_NN = 200 GeV for centrality 30-40%.
- Final temperature T_f = 180 MeV gives the best agreement with PHENIX elliptic flow v_2 data for pions and kaons at √s_NN = 200 GeV for centrality 20-40%.
- The evolution time from T_i ≈ 250 MeV to T_f = 180 MeV is approximately 4 fm.
- The impact parameter for centrality class 20-40% is b ≈ 7.9 fm.

## Context
This work extends the approach of transverse hydrodynamics introduced by Bialas (2007) and differs from the earlier model by Heinz and Wong, where entropy is not conserved and the system is treated as dissipative. The paper is presented at Quark Matter 2008 and builds on the success of perfect-fluid hydrodynamics in describing RHIC data (Shuryak 2004, Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003), while addressing the puzzle of early thermalization. The results are consistent with a recent model by Broniowski (2008) that assumes initial free-streaming in all three directions followed by a sudden transition to standard hydrodynamics, suggesting that complete early thermalization may not be necessary.
