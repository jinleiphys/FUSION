# Elliptic flow of deuterons from simulations with hybrid model

> Elliptic flow of deuterons is measured on simulated collisions events of Pb+Pb at CMS energy of 2.76 TeV per colliding nucleon pair. We use hybrid model that includes hydrodynamics for the deconfined phase and hadron transport as an afterburner. For deuterons, two production mechanisms are examined: coalescence, and direct thermal production during hadronisation and subsequent transport through the hadronic phase. Differential elliptic flow of deuterons in different centrality bins is evaluated for both implemented production models. In this approach, coalescence describes the experimental data better than direct deuteron production.

## Key claim
In a hybrid model combining hydrodynamics and hadronic transport, coalescence describes the experimental deuteron elliptic flow data better than direct thermal production, but the ordering of the two mechanisms' predictions for \(v_2(p_t)\) is opposite to that found in a previous blast-wave study (Vozabova 2024), preventing a simple qualitative discrimination.

## Method
The authors simulated Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV using a chain of TRENTo 3D initial conditions, vHLLE hydrodynamic evolution of the deconfined phase, and SMASH v2.2 as a hadronic afterburner. For deuterons, two production mechanisms were implemented: coalescence of protons and neutrons after their last scattering, using phase-space cuts \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{max}} = 3.5\) fm and \(\Delta p_{\mathrm{max}} = 0.310\) GeV/\(c\); and direct thermal production, where deuterons were generated statistically at the particlisation hypersurface and then propagated through the hadronic transport. Differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_t)\) was measured with the scalar product method for centrality bins from 0-5% to 60-70%.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV for Pb+Pb.
- Coalescence parameters: \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{max}} = 3.5\) fm, \(\Delta p_{\mathrm{max}} = 0.310\) GeV/\(c\).
- Simulation statistics: \(1.5 \times 10^6\) events per centrality bin, composed of 3000 hydrodynamic events each oversampled by 500 SMASH runs.
- Maximum simulation time in SMASH: \(t_{\mathrm{max}} = 100\) fm/\(c\) (results stable between 100 and 150 fm/\(c\)).
- Shear viscosity: \(\eta/s = 0.12\); bulk viscosity peak: \(\zeta/s = 0.4\).
- Critical energy density for particlisation: \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{crit}} = 0.32\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends a previous study by Vozabova (2024) that used an extended blast-wave model and found coalescence deuterons had higher \(v_2\) than thermal ones; here, using a more realistic hybrid model (TRENTo+vHLLE+SMASH), the ordering is reversed, with thermal deuterons showing higher \(v_2\). The results enable future studies of larger clusters (e.g., tritons, \(^4\)He) but note that such simulations are statistically prohibitive due to suppression factors of order 300 relative to deuterons (ALICE 2015). The paper concludes that differential elliptic flow alone is not a robust discriminator between the two production mechanisms.
