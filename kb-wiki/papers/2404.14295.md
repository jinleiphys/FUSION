# $Λ$ polarization in very high energy heavy ion collisions as a probe of the Quark-Gluon Plasma formation and properties

> We have studied the spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in heavy ion collisions at center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV carried out at RHIC and LHC colliders. We have calculated the mean spin vector at local thermodynamic equilibrium, including all known first-order terms in the gradients of the thermo-hydrodynamic fields, assuming that the hadronization hypersurface has a uniform temperature. We have also included the feed-down contributions to the polarization of $Λ$ stemming from the decays of polarized $Σ^*$ and $Σ^0$ hyperons. The obtained results are in good agreement with the data. In general, the component of the spin vector along the global angular momentum, orthogonal to the reaction plane, shows strong sensitivity to the initial longitudinal flow velocity. Furthermore, the longitudinal component of the spin vector turns out to be very sensitive to the bulk viscosity of the plasma at the highest LHC energy. Therefore, the azimuthal dependence of spin polarization can effectively constrain the initial hydrodynamic conditions and the transport coefficients of the Quark Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the azimuthal dependence of Λ hyperon spin polarization in heavy ion collisions is sensitive to both the initial longitudinal flow velocity and the bulk viscosity of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, but this sensitivity is conditional on using the correct theoretical formula for the mean spin vector at local thermodynamic equilibrium, specifically the isothermal hadronization hypersurface formula with the energy defined as p·t̂ rather than p·u.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the vHLLE code with two different initial state models (superMC and 3D GLISSANDO) for Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. They calculated the Λ mean spin vector including all known first-order gradient terms (thermal vorticity and thermal shear tensor) assuming an isothermal hadronization hypersurface, and incorporated feed-down corrections from polarized Σ* and Σ0 hyperon decays using spin-transfer formulas. The hydrodynamic evolution was coupled to hadronic rescattering via SMASH, and polarization was computed with the publicly available hydro-foil code.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energies studied: √sNN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and √sNN = 5.02 TeV (LHC)
- Hadronization hypersurface defined at constant energy density e = 0.4 GeV/fm³
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 for superMC initial state at both energies; for GLISSANDO initial state η/s = 0.08 at RHIC and η/s = 0.1 at LHC
- Feed-down fractions used: primary Λ fraction fP = 0.243, Σ* fraction fΣ* = 0.359, Σ0 fraction fΣ0 = 0.165
- Feed-down correction reduces longitudinal polarization by approximately 10% and transverse polarization by approximately 3% relative to the pure primary assumption
- Λ decay constant set to αΛ = 0.732 for comparison with experiments
- Longitudinal polarization at LHC is smaller than at RHIC by a factor of approximately 2

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of Λ polarization that included only thermal vorticity (Wu 2019, Florkowski 2019, Huang 2020) or also shear tensor contributions (Becattini 2021, Fu 2021, Alzhrani 2022) by incorporating feed-down corrections from Σ* and Σ0 decays for the first time in realistic simulations. The paper confirms the earlier finding of Becattini (2021) that the isothermal hadronization formula with p·t̂ resolves the polarization sign puzzle, and shows that the transverse polarization component is sensitive to initial longitudinal flow conditions, as previously studied by Jiang (2023) at √sNN = 27 GeV. The results enable future experimental measurements of the azimuthal dependence of spin polarization to constrain the initial hydrodynamic conditions and transport coefficients of the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
