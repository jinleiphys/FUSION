# Localized form of Fock terms in nuclear covariant density functional theory

> In most of the successful versions of covariant density functional theory in nuclei, the Fock terms are not included explicitly, which leads to local functionals and forms the basis of their widespread applicability at present. However, it has serious consequences for the description of Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) and spin-dipole resonances (SDR) which can only be cured by adding further phenomenological parameters. Relativistic Hartree-Fock models do not suffer from these problems. They can successfully describe the GTR and SDR as well as the isovector part of the Dirac effective mass without any additional parameters. However, they are non-local and require considerable numerical efforts. By the zero-range reduction and the Fierz transformation, a new method is proposed to take into account the Fock terms in local functionals, which retains the simplicity of conventional models and provides proper descriptions of the spin-isospin channels and the Dirac masses.

## Key claim

By applying zero-range reduction and Fierz transformation to the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) parameterization PKO2, the non-local Fock terms can be expressed as local Hartree terms, producing a local covariant density functional (PKO2-H) that retains the simplicity of conventional relativistic Hartree (RH) models while properly describing spin-isospin channels and Dirac masses without additional phenomenological parameters.

## Method

The authors start from the RHF parameterization PKO2, which includes only three heavy mesons (σ, ω, ρ). They apply a zero-range (heavy-meson) approximation to the Yukawa propagators, then use the Fierz transformation to re-express the exchange (Fock) terms as linear superpositions of direct (Hartree) terms in ten coupling channels. The resulting local functional, called PKO2-H, is tested in nuclear matter for Dirac mass splitting and in finite nuclei (²⁰⁸Pb and ⁹⁰Zr) for Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) and spin-dipole resonances (SDR) using proton-neutron random phase approximation (RPA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The zero-range coupling strengths α for PKO2-H are determined by three density-dependent RHF parameters from PKO2 via the Fierz transformation matrix C, whose transpose is given explicitly in the paper.
- In ²⁰⁸Pb, the GTR peak energy from RHF-loc (the local approximation) reproduces the experimental peak energy from Akimune (1995) very well, matching the full RHF result; the RH calculation requires a fitted Migdal term to match the same data.
- For the SDR in ⁹⁰Zr, the RHF-loc calculation reproduces the experimental peak energy from Yako (2006) and yields the energy hierarchy E(2⁻) < E(1⁻) < E(0⁻), identical to the full RHF result, while the conventional RH calculation shows a more fragmented structure.
- The Dirac mass splitting between neutron and proton in neutron matter from RHF-loc agrees well with microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations from Dalen (2007), whereas it vanishes identically in conventional RH models.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock approach of Long (2006, 2008) by showing that its Fock terms can be localized, contradicting the common practice in most covariant density functional theories (e.g., DD-ME2 from Lalazissis 2005) that omit Fock terms and require additional phenomenological parameters to describe spin-isospin excitations. The method enables future development of local covariant density functionals with proper isovector properties, following the Kohn-Sham scheme, and opens the door to simpler calculations for deformed nuclei and beyond-mean-field effects that are currently impractical with full RHF.
