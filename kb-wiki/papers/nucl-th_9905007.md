# Hadronic Scatterings of Charm Mesons and Enhancement of Intermediate Mass Dileptons

> The scattering effects of charm mesons by hadrons such as the pion, rho meson and nucleon are studied in an effective Lagrangian, and are found to be important in heavy ion collisions. At the CERN-SPS energies, hadronic rescatterings are shown to harden the charm meson $m_T$ spectra, leading to a significant enhancement of the yield of intermediate-mass dimuons from charm meson decays.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescatterings of charm mesons with pions, rho mesons, and nucleons in heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS energies can harden the charm meson transverse mass spectra, leading to a significant enhancement of intermediate-mass dimuon yields from charm meson decays; however, the estimates are based on a simplified analytical treatment of the time evolution of the hadronic system and do not include all possible scattering partners (kaons, omega, eta, delta, higher baryon resonances).

## Method

The authors computed scattering cross sections for charm mesons (D and D*) with pions, rho mesons, and nucleons using an effective Lagrangian based on SU(4) symmetry, with coupling constants taken from empirical values and monopole form factors at t-channel vertices. They then estimated the total squared momentum transfer to charm mesons from hadronic scatterings in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies by assuming a 1/t time evolution of hadron densities and using thermal averages of σv p₀² at T=150 MeV, summing contributions from π, ρ, and nucleon scatterings.

## Key numbers

- Thermal average <σv p₀²> at T=150 MeV: 1.1 mb·GeV² for pion-charm meson scatterings, 1.5 mb·GeV² for rho-charm meson scatterings, and 2.7 mb·GeV² for nucleon-charm meson scatterings
- Initial particle numbers for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS: 500 pions, 220 rho mesons, 180 nucleons
- Product of initial density and formation time ρ₀τ₀: 0.79 fm⁻² (pions), 0.35 fm⁻² (rho mesons), 0.28 fm⁻² (nucleons), with τ₀=1 fm
- Total squared momentum transfer from hadronic scatterings: <p_S²> ≈ 0.61 GeV², corresponding to a scattering strength parameter T_S = 96 MeV
- Charm meson effective inverse slope T_eff increases from 160 MeV to about 235 MeV due to rescatterings
- Dimuon enhancement factor of about 2.1 for the NA50 acceptance

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Lin, Ko, and Li (1998) that charm mesons develop a transverse flow from hadronic rescatterings, which could explain the intermediate-mass dimuon enhancement observed by the NA50 experiment at CERN-SPS. The authors provide explicit cross section calculations using an effective Lagrangian approach, whereas the earlier work had assumed such rescattering effects phenomenologically. An alternative explanation based on dilepton production from secondary meson-meson interactions had also been proposed by Gale and collaborators. The results motivate more quantitative studies using partonic and hadronic cascade programs, and the authors note that for RHIC energies, partonic rescattering and radiative energy loss of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma must also be considered.
