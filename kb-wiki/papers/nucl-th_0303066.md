# In-medium meson properties and field transformations

> Since the existing calculations of the effective meson mass in nuclear medium involve approximations, it is important to examine whether they satisfy the general requirement of the equivalence theorem that the physical observables should be independent of the choice of field variables. We study here consequences of nucleon field transformations. As an illustrative case we consider the in-medium effective pion mass calculated for the s-wave pion-nucleon interaction in the linear density approximation. We demonstrate that it is necessary to include the Born term explicitly in order that the effective pion mass should obey the equivalence theorem.

## Key claim

The effective in-medium pion mass calculated in the linear density approximation is invariant under nucleon field transformations only if both the Born (nucleon-exchange) term and the contact interaction term are explicitly included; omitting or absorbing the Born term into the contact term leads to a representation-dependent (and therefore unphysical) result.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic chiral Lagrangian at tree level, including terms up to second order in small momenta or the pion mass. They calculate the s-wave pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in the linear density (mean-field) approximation, where the pion self-energy is proportional to the forward scattering amplitude in the nucleon rest frame. They then apply a chiral rotation of the nucleon field (parameterized by a real parameter λ) and compute the effective pion mass in both the original and transformed representations, comparing the Born and contact contributions separately.

## Key numbers

- The Born contribution to the squared effective pion mass is \( M_{eff, Born}^2 = \rho \, g_A^2 M^2 / (4 m f^2) \), estimated as \( 0.066 \, M^2 (\rho/\rho_0) \) using \( g_A=1.27 \), \( f=92.4 \, \text{MeV} \), \( M=139 \, \text{MeV} \), \( m=938 \, \text{MeV} \), and normal nuclear matter density \( \rho_0 = 0.17 \, \text{fm}^{-3} \).
- The contact contribution to the squared effective pion mass is \( M_{eff, c}^2 = -(\rho / f^2) [ \sigma + 2(c_2 + c_3) M^2 ] \), where \( \sigma = -4 c_1 M^2 \) is the nucleon sigma-term.
- The transformed Born contribution acquires an additional term \( 4 m \lambda^2 / f^2 \), and the transformed contact term acquires an identical term \( -4 m \lambda^2 / f^2 \), which cancel exactly in the sum, leaving the total effective mass unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the studies of Thorsson and Wirzba (1995) and Lee et al. (1995), who demonstrated the invariance of the in-medium meson mass under transformations of the meson field variables. The present paper addresses the complementary case of nucleon field transformations, which had not been previously examined. The results imply that future calculations of in-medium meson properties must explicitly retain the Born term to maintain field-redefinition invariance, and suggest that analogous studies should be carried out within non-relativistic chiral perturbation theory and for simultaneous transformations of both meson and nucleon fields.
