# Linear response of light deformed nuclei investigated by self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase-approximation

> We present a calculation of the properties of vibrational states in deformed, axially--symmetric even--even nuclei, within the framework of a fully self--consistent Quasparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). The same Skyrme energy density and density-dependent pairing functionals are used to calculate the mean field and the residual interaction in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. We have tested our software in the case of spherical nuclei against fully self consistent calculations published in the literature, finding excellent agreement. We investigate the consequences of neglecting the spin-orbit and Coulomb residual interactions in QRPA. Furthermore we discuss the improvement obtained in the QRPA result associated with the removal of spurious modes. Isoscalar and isovector responses in the deformed ${}^{24-26}$Mg, $^{34}$Mg isotopes are presented and compared to experimental findings.

## Key claim

The paper presents fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations of vibrational states in deformed, axially-symmetric even-even nuclei, but the abstract's claim of "excellent agreement" with spherical benchmarks is hedged by the fact that the deformed code was tested only on the spherical nucleus \({}^{48}\)Ca, and the results for deformed nuclei show notable discrepancies with experimental data, particularly for \({}^{26}\)Mg.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in a finite harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis using the Skyrme functional SkM* and a density-dependent delta pairing interaction. The QRPA equations are then solved in the canonical basis, with matrix elements expanded respecting selection rules for the projection of angular momentum \(\Omega\) and parity \(\pi\). The strength functions are calculated using a Lorentzian smearing of \(\Gamma = 1\) MeV for isoscalar monopole, quadrupole, and isovector dipole operators.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{24}\)Mg, the HFB ground state has a prolate deformation \(\beta = 0.39\) (HO and THO basis).
- For \({}^{26}\)Mg, the HFB ground state is oblate with \(\beta = -0.18\) (HO and THO basis).
- For \({}^{34}\)Mg, the HFB ground state is prolate with \(\beta = 0.36\) (HO and THO basis).
- The spurious state energies in fully consistent QRPA for \({}^{24}\)Mg are below 500 keV (real or imaginary), compared to about 3 MeV when both spin-orbit and Coulomb residual interactions are neglected.
- In \({}^{24}\)Mg, the calculated energy of the lowest \(\Omega^\pi = 2^+\) state is 4.14 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.23 MeV for the gamma-band head.
- In \({}^{26}\)Mg, the calculated energy of the lowest \(\Omega^\pi = 2^+\) state is 1.31 MeV, compared to the experimental \(2^+_2\) state at 3.0 MeV.
- The mean energy of the IS giant quadrupole resonance in \({}^{24}\)Mg (SkM*, THO basis) is 19.3 MeV in the interval [9,41] MeV, compared to the experimental value of 16.9 \(\pm\) 0.6 MeV from Youngblood et al.
- The mean energy of the IV giant dipole resonance in \({}^{24}\)Mg (SkM*, THO basis) is 19.8 MeV in the interval [10,29] MeV, compared to the experimental value of 22.1 MeV from Irgashev et al.

## Context

This work extends fully self-consistent HFB+QRPA calculations to deformed nuclei, building on spherical QRPA codes by Terasaki et al. (2005) and deformed HFB codes by Stoitsov et al. (2005). It contrasts with the Gogny-force QRPA calculations by Peru et al. (2008), which achieve better results for the \(\Omega^\pi = 0^+\) spurious mode but worse results for translational invariance modes. The calculations enable future studies of vibrational modes in exotic neutron-rich nuclei, where coupling to the continuum and pygmy resonances become important, and provide a baseline for comparing Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions in deformed systems.
