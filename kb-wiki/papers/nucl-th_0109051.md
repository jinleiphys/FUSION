# Optical Potentials in Kaonic Atoms

> A microscopic optical potential based on a chiral model is used as a starting point for studying kaonic atoms levels. We add to this potential a phenomenological part fitted to the experimentally known shifts and widths of kaonic levels. This fitted potential is used to predict deeply bound atomic levels, as well as nuclear levels. Comparison with the predictions of other optical models found in the literature is done. Also the effects on the kaonic atoms levels of certain known non-local contributions to the optical potential are also analyzed.

## Key claim
A microscopic optical potential based on a chiral model, when supplemented with a fitted phenomenological correction, can describe kaonic atom data with a chi-squared per data point of about 1.3, but the purely theoretical potential without free parameters already achieves a chi-squared per data point of 2.9, indicating that the chiral model captures the dominant physics.

## Method
The authors started from a self-consistent s-wave K- optical potential in nuclear matter, calculated using an in-medium effective Kbar-N interaction derived from the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian via the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation (Ramos-Oset model). They added a phenomenological correction term, either proportional to the nuclear density with a complex parameter delta b0 or proportional to density times (density/rho0)^(1/3) with a real parameter delta Im B0, and fitted these parameters to 63 experimentally known shifts and widths of kaonic atomic levels. They also evaluated the effects of p-wave contributions and non-local s-wave terms (momentum and energy dependence) on the calculated shifts and widths.

## Key numbers
- The purely theoretical s-wave potential (1) gave chi-squared per data point of 3.76 for 63 experimental data points.
- The improved theoretical s-wave potential including Sigma*-h excitations (1_Sigma*) gave chi-squared per data point of 2.89.
- The fitted potential (1_Sigma*b0) achieved chi-squared per data point of 1.30, with fitted parameter delta b0 = (0.0750 - i0.200) fm.
- The fitted potential (1m) gave chi-squared per data point of 1.52, with fitted parameter delta b0 = (0.078 - i0.25) fm.
- For the 4f level in Cu-63, the experimental shift was -370 eV with width 1370 eV; the (1_Sigma*b0) potential gave shift -309 eV and width 1368 eV.
- For the 7i level in U-238, the experimental shift was -260 eV with width 1500 eV; the (1_Sigma*b0) potential gave shift -255 eV and width 1241 eV.

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent microscopic approach of Ramos-Oset (2000) for the K- self-energy in nuclear matter to the calculation of kaonic atom levels. It provides a theoretically founded alternative to purely phenomenological optical potentials such as those used by Friedman (1999) and Baca (2000). The fitted potentials enable predictions for deeply bound atomic states in heavy nuclei like Pb-208, which are narrow and separable, making them candidates for experimental observation via nuclear reactions.
