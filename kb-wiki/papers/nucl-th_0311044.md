# Clusters of matter and antimatter

> In this talk I first give a short overview of antinuclei production in recent experiments at RHIC. Then I discuss the possibility of producing new types of nuclear systems by implanting an antibaryon into ordinary nuclei. The structure of nuclei containing one antiproton or antilambda is investigated within the framework of a relativistic mean-field model. Self-consistent calculations predict an enhanced binding and considerable compression in such systems as compared with normal nuclei. I present arguments that the life time of such nuclei with respect to the antibaryon annihilation might be long enough for their observation. Few experimental signatures are suggested. Possible formation of multi-quark-antiquark clusters is also discussed.

# Clusters of matter and antimatter

## Key claim
Self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations predict that implanting a single antiproton or antilambda into a normal nucleus creates a "super bound nucleus" with enhanced binding energy and central compression by a factor of 2–4, and that the annihilation lifetime of such systems (5–25 fm/c) may be long enough for experimental observation, provided the available annihilation energy is reduced by about half compared to vacuum.

## Method
The author performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the TM1, NL3, and NL-Z2 parametrizations, solving coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and an antibaryon together with differential equations for mean meson (sigma, omega, rho) and Coulomb fields under axial symmetry. Antibaryon coupling constants were set by G-parity transformation (sign-flipped vector coupling for antiprotons) and SU(3) flavor symmetry (factor 2/3 for antilambdas), with a "no-sea" approximation. Annihilation rates were estimated by scaling to available phase space, considering channels with pions, rho, omega, eta mesons, and multi-pion final states.

## Key numbers
- Rapidity densities at y≈0 in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV: dN/dy = 32 (p), 24 (antiprotons), 2.7×10⁻³ (antideuterons), 6.3×10⁻⁶ (³He-bar); antiproton-to-proton ratio ≈ 0.75, antideuteron-to-antiproton ratio ≈ 10⁻⁴, ³He-bar-to-antideuteron ratio ≈ 2×10⁻³.
- Binding energies for the antiproton + ¹⁶O system: 830 MeV (NL-Z2), 1050 MeV (NL3), 1160 MeV (TM1), compared to 130 MeV for normal ¹⁶O.
- Binding energy for antilambda + ¹⁶O system: 560–700 MeV.
- Binding energy for antiproton + ⁸Be system: about 700 MeV, compared to 53 MeV for normal ⁸Be; ground state deformation changes from β₂ ≈ 1.20 to β₂ ≈ 0.23.
- Central nucleon density increase by a factor of 2–4 when inserting an antiproton into ¹⁶O, depending on RMF parametrization.
- Estimated SBN lifetimes: 5–25 fm/c; reduction of Q value from 2 GeV to 1 GeV reduces total annihilation rate by factor 20–30.
- Estimated SBN formation probability in central antiproton-nucleus collisions: 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶; with planned GSI luminosity of 2×10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹, reaction rate of tens to hundreds events per second.
- Local baryon densities up to 5 times normal nuclear density may be reached during compression.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions of strongly bound antinucleon-nucleus states (Bue) and the Dirac picture of antinucleon potentials in nuclear matter (Aue) by performing self-consistent calculations that include nuclear rearrangement. It contrasts with previous studies that did not account for structure changes due to the antibaryon. The predicted super bound nuclei are analogous to but stronger than the effects predicted for kaon-bound states in ⁸Be (Akaishi 2002). The results suggest that future antiproton beam facilities (e.g., GSI) could produce these systems, and that multi-quark-antiquark clusters might form via inertial compression, building on earlier work showing that antiquark admixture in cold quark matter is energetically favorable (Mishustin).
