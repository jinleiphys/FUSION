# Neutrino Non-Standard Interactions: A Status Report

> This report summarizes the present status of neutrino non-standard interactions (NSI). After a brief overview, several aspects of NSIs are discussed, including connection to neutrino mass models, model-building and phenomenology of large NSI with both light and heavy mediators, NSI phenomenology in both short- and long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments, neutrino cross-sections, complementarity of NSI with other low- and high-energy experiments, fits with neutrino oscillation and scattering data, DUNE sensitivity to NSI, effective field theory of NSI, as well as the relevance of NSI to dark matter and cosmology. We also discuss the open questions and interesting future directions that can be pursued by the community at large. This report is based on talks and discussions during the Neutrino Theory Network NSI workshop held at Washington University in St. Louis from May 29-31, 2019 (https://indico.cern.ch/event/812851/)

## Key claim

This report summarizes the present status of neutrino non-standard interactions (NSI), finding that while global oscillation data show a preference for the 3ν+NSI scenario at approximately 2σ for any value of the quark coupling parameter η, the addition of COHERENT scattering data significantly disfavors the LMA-Dark solution, restricting it to a narrower range of η at 3σ.

## Method

The authors performed a CP-conserving global fit of neutrino oscillation data available until January 2018, combining solar experiments with KamLAND, atmospheric neutrinos, long-baseline accelerator experiments, and reactor data. They also included coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering data from COHERENT, and analyzed the compatibility of the three-neutrino scenario in the presence of neutral-current NSI that modify the neutrino matter potential into a non-diagonal matrix parameterized by εαβ. The NSI couplings to quarks were parameterized in terms of an angle η describing the relative coupling to protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Global oscillation data show a preference for the 3ν+NSI scenario at approximately 2σ for any value of η (LMA solution)
- The LMA-Dark solution is allowed at 3σ in the range -38° ≤ η ≤ 87° from global oscillation data alone
- After adding COHERENT data, the LMA-Dark solution is allowed at 3σ only for -38° ≤ η ≤ 14°
- Atmospheric neutrino energies span from approximately 100 MeV to 100 TeV, covering more than six orders of magnitude
- Matter effects in long-baseline experiments can be as large as approximately 30%
- In the Zee model, the maximum allowed tree-level NSI values are: |εee| = 0.08, |εμμ| = 0.038, |εττ| = 0.43, |εeμ| = O(10⁻⁵), |εeτ| = 0.0056, |εμτ| = 0.0034
- In the leptoquark variant of the Zee model, the maximum allowed |εττ| is 34.3%, which can be probed down to 14.5% (9.5%) at DUNE in the 300 (850) kt·MW·yr configuration
- IceCube atmospheric neutrino data requires |εττ - εμμ| < 9.3%

## Context

This report is based on talks and discussions during the Neutrino Theory Network NSI workshop held at Washington University in St. Louis in May 2019. It extends prior global fit analyses by Esteban (2018) and Coloma (2017) by incorporating both oscillation and scattering data to constrain NSI parameters, and examines specific radiative neutrino mass models including the Zee model (Zee, 1980) and its leptoquark variants. The work enables future experimental searches at DUNE and other long-baseline experiments to probe NSI at the few-percent level, and connects NSI phenomenology to dark matter and cosmology.
