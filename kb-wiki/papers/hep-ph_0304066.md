# Sliding Vacua in Dense Skyrmion Matter

> In continuation of our systematic effort to understand hadronic matter at high density, we study dense skyrmion matter and its chiral phase structure in an effective field theory implemented with the trace anomaly of QCD applicable in the large $N_c$ limit. By incorporating a dilaton field $χ$ associated with broken conformal symmetry of QCD into the simplest form of skyrmion Lagrangian, we simulate the effect of "sliding vacua" influenced by the presence of matter and obtain what could correspond to the ``intrinsic dependence" on the background of the system, i.e., matter density or temperature, that results when a generic chiral effective field theory of strong interactions is matched to QCD at a matching scale near the chiral scale $Λ_χ\sim 4πf_π\sim 1$ GeV. The properties of the Goldstone pions and the dilaton scalar near the chiral phase transition are studied by looking at the pertinent excitations of given quantum numbers on top of a skyrmion matter and their behavior in the vicinity of the phase transition from Goldstone mode to Wigner mode characterized by the changeover from the FCC crystal to the half-skyrmion CC crystal. We recover from the model certain features that are connected to Brown-Rho scaling and that suggest how to give a precise meaning to the latter in the framework of an effective field theory that is matched to QCD .

## Key claim

By incorporating a dilaton field associated with the trace anomaly of QCD into the simplest Skyrme Lagrangian, the authors simulate the effect of "sliding vacua" in dense matter and obtain what could correspond to the intrinsic density dependence of effective field theory parameters, thereby giving a more rigorous formulation of Brown-Rho scaling within a large \(N_c\) framework.

## Method

The authors start from a Skyrme-type Lagrangian with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry and scale symmetry, implemented via a dilaton field \(\chi\) with scale dimension 1. They construct FCC skyrmion crystal configurations for dense matter, then minimize the energy per baryon using Fourier expansions of the chiral and dilaton fields as variational parameters. The phase transition from an FCC crystal (single skyrmions) to a half-skyrmion CC crystal is studied as a function of the lattice size parameter \(L\), and the behavior of the pion and dilaton excitations is examined near the chiral restoration point.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV and the pion mass as \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV.
* The Skyrme parameter \(e\) is fixed to 4.75 from the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\).
* Three parameter sets for the dilaton are used: Set A (\(m_\chi = 550\) MeV, \(f_\chi = 240\) MeV, \(G_0 = 0.004\) GeV\(^4\)), Set B (\(m_\chi = 720\) MeV, \(f_\chi = 240\) MeV, \(G_0 = 0.007\) GeV\(^4\)), and Set C (\(m_\chi = 1000\) MeV, \(f_\chi = 240\) MeV, \(G_0 = 0.012\) GeV\(^4\)).
* The single skyrmion mass in the original model (without dilaton) is 1479 MeV and the root mean square radius is 0.43 fm; with the dilaton, the soliton mass decreases by up to 7% for Set A.
* Normal nuclear matter density is \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to \(L \sim 1.43\) fm.
* The energy per baryon in the half-skyrmion phase drops to approximately 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unified approach to dense skyrmion matter (Lee, Park, Rho, Vento 2003) by incorporating the trace anomaly of QCD, which was missing in the standard Skyrme model. It provides a more rigorous formulation of Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho 1991) and offers a scheme to understand the intrinsic density dependence needed in the hidden local symmetry / vector manifestation theory of Harada and Yamawaki. The results enable future studies of how effective field theory parameters behave at arbitrary densities away from the vacuum and the critical point.
