# Regge Trajectories of Radial Meson Excitations: Exploring the Dyson-Schwinger-- Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> The combined Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations in rainbow-ladder approximation are used to search for Regge trajectories of mesons in the pseudo-scalar and vector channels. We focus on the often employed Alkofer-Watson-Weigel kernel which is known to deliver good results for the ground state meson spectra; it provides linear Regge trajectories in the $J^P= 0^-$ channel.

# Regge Trajectories of Radial Meson Excitations: Exploring the Dyson-Schwinger--Bethe-Salpeter Approach

## Key claim

The combined Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations in rainbow-ladder approximation with the Alkofer-Watson-Weigel kernel can produce linear Regge trajectories for radial excitations in the pseudo-scalar ($J^P = 0^-$) channel, but this linearity is restricted to a certain parameter range and does not hold reliably for the vector ($J^P = 1^-$) channel. The authors acknowledge that some calculated excited states have improper C-parity and that the method cannot strictly ensure a given C-parity for the bound states determined by zeros of the determinant.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equation for the dressed quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, both in rainbow-ladder approximation using the Alkofer-Watson-Weigel (AWW) kernel. The AWW kernel is defined as $D^{\mathrm{AWW}}(k^2) = (4\pi^2 D k^2 / \omega^2) e^{-k^2/\omega^2}$ with interaction strength $D$ and range $\omega$. Meson masses are found by locating zeros of the determinant $\det |\hat S - \mathbb{1}| = 0$ as a function of the meson mass $M_{12}$, where each zero above the ground state is associated with a radial excitation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fischer (2014) and Kubrak (2014) who found linear orbital Regge trajectories ($M_J^2 = M^2(0) + \hat \beta J$) for $J^{PC} = 1^{--}, 2^{++}, 3^{--}$ states using similar functional methods, but here the focus is on radial excitations in the $0^-$ and $1^-$ channels. The results contradict earlier remarks in the literature (Alkofer 2002, Binosi 2016, Mojica 2017, El-Bennich 2016) that the AWW kernel is less appropriate for excitations due to sensitivity to long-range interactions. The study enables future extension to non-zero temperatures and baryon densities, which is relevant for understanding sequential meson melting in heavy-ion collisions as observed by the CMS and ATLAS collaborations at LHC.
