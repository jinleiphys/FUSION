# Chiral extrapolation and finite-volume dependence of the hyperon vector couplings

> The hyperon vector form factors at zero momentum transfer, $f_1(0)$, play an important role in a precise determination of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element $V_{us}$. Recent studies based on lattice chromodynamics (LQCD) simulations and covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory yield contradicting results. In this work, we study chiral extrapolation of and finite-volume corrections to the latest $n_f=2+1$ LQCD simulations. Our results show that finite-volume corrections are relatively small and can be safely ignored at the present LQCD setup of $m_πL=4.6$ but chiral extrapolation needs to be performed more carefully. Nevertheless, the discrepancy remains and further studies are needed to fully understand it.

## Key claim
The paper finds that finite-volume corrections to the hyperon vector couplings are negligible at the current lattice QCD setup (mπL = 4.6), but chiral extrapolation must be performed more carefully; nevertheless, the discrepancy between lattice QCD results and covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) predictions persists and cannot be resolved by either effect without invoking large O(p⁵) contributions.

## Method
The authors compute the hyperon vector form factors f₁(0) up to O(p⁴) in covariant BChPT, including both octet and decuplet baryon degrees of freedom, and derive the finite-volume corrections by discretizing the spatial components of loop integrals in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They apply these calculations to the n_f = 2+1 lattice QCD data from Sasaki (2012) for the ΣN and ΞΣ channels, using the meson and baryon masses from the lattice ensembles to determine the quark-mass dependence. They also perform fits adding an analytical O(p⁵) term to quantify the size of higher-order corrections needed to match the lattice results.

## Key numbers
- For the ΣN channel at the lightest pion mass (mπ = 0.330 GeV), the BChPT O(p⁴) result including octet and decuplet contributions is δf₁ = +5.9% (with finite-volume correction -0.16%), while the lattice QCD result is -3.44 ± 1.4%.
- For the ΞΣ channel at the same pion mass, the BChPT O(p⁴) result including octet and decuplet contributions is δf₁ = +2.5% (with finite-volume correction +0.06%), while the lattice QCD result is -1.92 ± 0.79%.
- The finite-volume corrections are given in parentheses for each channel and pion mass; for example, at mπ = 0.330 GeV, the correction for ΣN is -0.16% and for ΞΣ is +0.06%, both negligible relative to the total corrections.
- At the physical point (mπ = 0.14 GeV) with L ≈ 3 fm, finite-volume corrections can be as large as 30% of the SU(3)-breaking correction in some channels, requiring L = 5–6 fm to keep them below 10%.
- The O(p⁵) analytical term needed to fit the lattice data is c₁₂ = -1.40(12) for ΣN and c₁₂ = -1.16(8) for ΞΣ, leading to physical-point corrections of -0.6(0.7)(3.5)% and -6.6(0.4)(1.4)%, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the covariant BChPT calculation of hyperon vector couplings by Geng (2009) to include finite-volume effects and a detailed chiral extrapolation, addressing the contradiction between BChPT predictions and lattice QCD results from Sasaki (2012). The study shows that the discrepancy cannot be explained by finite-volume corrections or chiral extrapolation alone, requiring either large O(p⁵) contributions or further lattice simulations at lighter quark masses and larger volumes. This enables future lattice QCD studies to use the provided finite-volume correction formulas for more accurate extrapolations to the physical point.
