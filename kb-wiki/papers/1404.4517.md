# Hadronic vs. partonic J/psi production in the Statistical Hadronization Model

> Assuming the Statistical Hadronization Model and charm flavour conservation, one can quantify the deviation of charm and anti-charm quarks from chemical equilibrium both in the hadronic phase and in the quark-gluon plasma. By linking the hadronic fireball volume and the corresponding plasma source volume via entropy conservation as proposed by Grandchamp et al., a direct comparison between the $J/ψ$ yields in both pictures can be used to obtain limits for the charm quark mass in the medium.

## Key claim

By comparing J/ψ yields calculated in the hadronic Statistical Hadronization Model with those from a partonic formulation assuming entropy conservation across hadronization, the authors find that the effective dressed charm quark mass must lie in a narrow interval centered at about 1550 MeV to obtain compatible results, updating the earlier estimate of 1.6–1.7 GeV from Grandchamp et al. (2003).

## Method

The authors extended the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) to describe charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma phase by introducing a charm fugacity λ_c, analogous to the charm fugacity g_c used in the hadronic phase. They linked the hadronic fireball volume V_SHM to the plasma source volume V_QGP via entropy conservation, using the entropy density of a three-flavor QGP. They then compared J/ψ multiplicities calculated from both the hadronic formulation (using g_c) and the partonic formulation (using λ_c) as functions of freeze-out temperature and charm quark mass, fixing N_ch = 730 and N_c\bar{c}^dir = 1.92 to reproduce RHIC data from Andronic et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

- The QGP volume is smaller than the hadronic fireball volume by a factor of 2.5 at T = 160 MeV.
- The favored effective dressed charm quark mass is m_c ≃ 1550 MeV, updating the earlier range of m_c ≃ 1.6–1.7 GeV from Grandchamp et al. (2003).
- The vacuum charm quark mass is 1275 MeV (PDG), and lattice QCD results suggest m_c = 1348 MeV (Carrasco et al., 2014).
- The D meson mass used in some approaches is m_D = 1870 MeV (Liu et al., 2009; Zhuang et al., 2007; Zhou et al., 2014).
- The hadronic SHM uses T = 162 MeV and μ_B = 24 MeV to reproduce particle ratios from PHENIX and STAR (Andronic et al., 2012).
- The excluded volume correction uses a hard-sphere radius R = 0.3 fm for all hadron species.

## Context

This work extends the Statistical Hadronization Model of Andronic et al. (2003, 2007) by adapting the charm fugacity formalism to the partonic phase, following the proposal of Grandchamp et al. (2003). It contradicts the earlier estimate of m_c = 1.6–1.7 GeV from Grandchamp et al. (2003) and the approach of substituting m_c with the D meson mass (1870 MeV) used by Liu et al. (2009), Zhuang et al. (2007), and Zhou et al. (2014). The result enables future tests using kinetic processes in the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) to compare with the temporal evolution calculations of Zhou et al. (2014).
