# Reducing nonideal to ideal coupling in random matrix description of chaotic scattering: Application to the time-delay problem

> We write explicitly a transformation of the scattering phases reducing the problem of quantum chaotic scattering for systems with M statistically equivalent channels at nonideal coupling to that for ideal coupling. Unfolding the phases by their local density leads to universality of their local fluctuations for large M. A relation between the partial time delays and diagonal matrix elements of the Wigner-Smith matrix is revealed for ideal coupling. This helped us in deriving the joint probability distribution of partial time delays and the distribution of the Wigner time delay.

# Reducing nonideal to ideal coupling in random matrix description of chaotic scattering: Application to the time-delay problem

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for quantum chaotic scattering with M statistically equivalent channels, a specific transformation of the scattering eigenphases maps the problem at nonideal coupling (nonzero average S-matrix) to the simpler ideal coupling case (circular ensemble), provided the average S-matrix is proportional to the unit matrix. This mapping is exact for the eigenphases and their joint distribution, and the eigenvectors of the S-matrix remain unaffected by the transformation.

## Method

The authors derive an explicit transformation (Eq. 4) relating the eigenphases δ_c at nonideal coupling to eigenphases φ_c distributed according to Dyson's circular ensemble, using the average S-matrix parameterized by coupling constants γ and the mean density of states ν(E). They compute the Jacobian of this transformation and verify that the joint probability density of the transformed phases matches the Poisson kernel distribution. For the time-delay problem, they relate partial time delays τ_c = 2ħ ∂δ_c/∂E at nonideal coupling to those at ideal coupling via the local density of phases, and derive the joint probability distribution of partial time delays and the distribution of the Wigner time delay using integration over the unitary group and the Itzykson-Zuber formula.

## Key numbers

- The transformation Jacobian is ∂φ_c/∂δ_c = T / |1 - \overline{S}^* e^{2iδ_c}|^2, where T(E) = 1 - |\overline{S}(E)|^2 = 4γπν(E) / [1 + γ² + 2γπν(E)] is the energy-dependent transmission coefficient.
- For ideal coupling (T=1), the mean density of partial time delays is P_0(t = τ/t_H) = (β/2)^{βM/2} / Γ(βM/2) * e^{-β/(2t)} / t^{βM/2+2}, with t_H = 2πħ/Δ the Heisenberg time.
- For M=2 channels, the Wigner time delay distribution is P_2(t_w) ∝ t_w^{-3(β+1)} e^{-β/t_w} U((β+1)/2, 2β+2, β/t_w), where U is the confluent hypergeometric function, with asymptotic tail P_2(t_w) ∝ t_w^{-β-2} for t_w ≫ 1.
- The mean density of scattering eigenphases at nonideal coupling is ρ(δ) = T / |1 - \overline{S}^* e^{2iδ}|², independent of symmetry class.

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian random matrix approach to chaotic scattering (Verbaarschot, Weidenmüller, Zirnbauer 1985; Fyodorov, Sommers 1997) by providing an explicit mapping between nonideal and ideal coupling that was previously only suspected. The transformation enables analytical results for time-delay statistics that were previously accessible only via supersymmetry calculations (Fyodorov, Savin, Sommers 1997). The results enable calculation of the joint distribution of partial time delays and the Wigner time delay for arbitrary channel number M, generalizing earlier work for M=1 (Fyodorov, Sommers 1997; Gopar, Mello, Büttiker 1996) and connecting to the circular ensemble approach of Brouwer, Frahm, and Beenakker (1997) for the time-delay matrix.
