# Energy densities and equilibration in heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with the quark-gluon string model

> We study thermodynamic characteristica of ultra-relativistic Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 200 GeV within the framework of a microscopic transport model, namely the quark-gluon string model (QGSM). The temporal evolution of the local energy density, transverse and longitudinal pressure and equilibration times are considered. In contrast to complete equilibration which is even in central reactions hardly achieved, pre-equilibrium stages with energy densities well above the critical energy density predicted by lattice QCD are established at short time scales. Corresponding energy density profiles at proper time tau = 1 fm/c compare well with hydrodynamical assumptions for initial energy density distributions.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon string model (QGSM) shows that in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, complete local equilibrium is hardly achieved even in central reactions, but pre-equilibrium stages with energy densities well above the lattice QCD critical energy density (0.7-1.3 GeV/fm^3) are established at short time scales; the energy density profiles at proper time tau = 1 fm/c compare well with hydrodynamical assumptions for initial energy density distributions.

## Method

The authors used the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), a microscopic transport model based on Gribov Regge theory, to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for impact parameters b = 0 fm (central) and b = 8 fm (semi-peripheral). They computed the local energy density in cells of 1 fm^3 volume from the scalar mass density of all formed and pre-formed hadrons, and evaluated the diagonal elements of the pressure tensor via the virial theorem to define an equilibration ratio R_LE = (P_x + P_y)/(2 P_z) as a measure of local kinetic equilibrium. Results were averaged over 200 central and 600 semi-peripheral events.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy density in the central overlap zone: approximately 15 GeV/fm^3 for central (b = 0 fm) collisions and approximately 10 GeV/fm^3 for semi-peripheral (b = 8 fm) collisions.
- Energy density in the central cell stays nearly constant during the first 2 fm/c of evolution.
- For particles with pseudorapidity |eta| <= 1, energy densities of at least 4 GeV/fm^3 (central) and at least 2 GeV/fm^3 (semi-peripheral) are obtained after 2 fm/c.
- The critical energy density for the QCD phase transition from lattice QCD is 0.7-1.3 GeV/fm^3 (depending on number of degenerate quark flavours).
- Local equilibrium (R_LE approximately 1) is approached after 12-15 fm/c in central collisions and 15-20 fm/c in semi-peripheral collisions.
- Preconditions for a phase transition (epsilon > epsilon_crit) exist for a duration of approximately 8 fm/c in a space-time volume of about 500 fm^4.

## Context

This work extends microscopic transport studies of heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies, using the QGSM which had previously been shown to describe elliptic flow at SPS and RHIC energies (Bleibel et al., earlier QGSM papers). The results contradict the usual hydrodynamical assumption of fast thermal equilibration (less than 1 fm/c) as argued by Heinz and others, and instead support the picture from Bahl et al. (2005) that thermalization may not be reached so quickly. The energy density profiles at tau = 1 fm/c are compared directly with initial conditions used in hydrodynamical calculations by Hirano et al. for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, and with estimates from the PHOBOS Collaboration (Back et al., 2004). The work enables future studies of how multi-string and multi-particle fusion processes might reduce the relaxation times toward local equilibrium.
