# Proton Production in d+Au Collisions and the Cronin Effect

> Proton production in the intermediate p_T region in d+Au collisions is studied in the parton recombination model. The recombination of soft and shower partons is shown to be important in central collisions, but negligible in peripheral collisions. It is found that the large nuclear modification factor for proton production can be well reproduced by a calculation of the 3-quark recombination process.

# Proton Production in d+Au Collisions and the Cronin Effect

## Key claim
The large nuclear modification factor for proton production in d+Au collisions can be reproduced by 3-quark recombination without requiring transverse momentum broadening from initial-state multiple scattering, but only if the recombination of soft and shower partons is included for central collisions; this mechanism is negligible in peripheral collisions.

## Method
The authors apply the parton recombination model, decomposing the joint quark distribution into four components: thermal-thermal-thermal (TTT), thermal-thermal-shower (TTS), thermal-shower-shower (TSS), and shower-shower-shower (SSS). They fit the thermal parton parameters C and T to low-pT proton data from PHENIX in the range 0.5 < pT < 1.5 GeV/c for 0-20% and 60-90% centralities, then compute the intermediate and high-pT spectra from the TTS, TSS, and SSS terms without free parameters, using shower parton distributions determined previously.

## Key numbers
- For 0-20% centrality: C = 11.5 (GeV/c)^(-1), T = 0.24 GeV/c
- For 60-90% centrality: C = 8.0 (GeV/c)^(-1), T = 0.21 GeV/c
- The nuclear modification factor R^p_CP reaches a value of roughly 2 for 2 < pT < 3 GeV/c
- The crossover between thermal-shower (TTS+TSS) and fragmentation (SSS) contributions occurs at pT ≈ 2.5 GeV/c for 0-20% centrality and at pT ≈ 1 GeV/c for 60-90% centrality
- For comparison, pion parameters were C_π = 12 (5.65) (GeV/c)^(-1) and T_π = 0.21 (0.21) GeV/c for 0-20% (60-90%) centrality

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous treatment of the Cronin effect for pion production in d+Au collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2004) to proton production, using the same recombination formalism developed for Au+Au collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2003). The approach contrasts with fragmentation-based explanations that would predict R_CP ≈ 1 for both pions and protons in d+Au collisions. The finding that the thermal TTT contribution dominates over a wider pT range for protons than for pions is consistent with earlier work on the p/π ratio in Au+Au collisions (Greco et al., 2003; Fries et al., 2003). The results enable predictions for proton production at pT > 3 GeV/c that can be tested against future data.
