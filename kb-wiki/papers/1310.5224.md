# Off-shell effects on the interaction of Nambu-Goldstone bosons and $D$ mesons

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation in unitarized chiral perturbation theory is usually solved with the so-called on-shell approximation. The underlying argument is that the off-shell effects can be absorbed by the corresponding coupling constants and physical masses, which has been corroborated by the success of unitarized chiral perturbation theory in describing a variety of physical phenomena. Such an approximation needs to be scrutinized when applied to study the light-quark mass evolution of physical observables, as routinely performed nowadays. In the present work, we propose to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the full off-shell terms of the chiral potentials and apply this formalism to the description of the latest $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD (LQCD) data on the scattering lengths of Nambu-Goldstone bosons off $D$ mesons. It is shown that the LQCD data can be better described in this formalism than in the widely used on-shell approximation. On the other hand, no qualitative difference between the on-shell and off-shell approaches is observed for the light-quark mass evolution of the scattering lengths, given the limited LQCD data and their relatively large uncertainties. We also show that the light-quark mass dependence of the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ remains essentially the same in both approaches.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the full off-shell terms of the chiral potentials in the Bethe-Salpeter equation improves the description of lattice QCD scattering lengths of Nambu-Goldstone bosons off D mesons compared to the widely used on-shell approximation, but they also find that the on-shell and off-shell approaches yield no qualitative difference for the light-quark mass evolution of these observables given the limited precision of the lattice data.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UChPT) including the full off-shell terms of the leading-order (Weinberg-Tomozawa) and next-to-leading-order contact chiral potentials, rather than using the standard on-shell approximation. They represented the chiral potentials and the loop functions as matrices in a basis that separates the dependence on loop momenta, reducing the integral equation to an algebraic matrix equation. The loop functions were regularized in the modified minimal subtraction scheme with a single subtraction constant, and the unknown low-energy constants were fitted to the 15 lightest lattice QCD data points from Liu et al. (2012) for scattering lengths in five isospin-strangeness channels at four pion masses.

## Key numbers

- The NLO off-shell fit yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.79, compared to 1.23 for the on-shell approximation (Altenbuchinger and Geng, 2013).
- At leading order, the off-shell fit gives a chi-squared per degree of freedom of approximately 17.9 with f0 = 92.21 MeV, which reduces to about 3.9 when using the SU(3) average f0 = 1.15 f_pi.
- The off-shell approach produces a bound state pole for the D*_{s0}(2317) at sqrt(s) = 2.295 GeV, which is 22 MeV below the on-shell result of 2.317 GeV (the experimental value).
- The lattice data used are from n_f = 2+1 simulations at four pion masses: m_pi = 301, 364, 511, and 617 MeV (Liu et al., 2012).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of off-shell effects in UChPT for pion-pion interactions (Nieves, 1998; Nieves, 1999) and pion-nucleon interactions (Nieves, 2000; Nieves, 2001; Djukanovic, 2006; Borasoy, 2007; Bruns, 2010; Mai, 2012) to the heavy-light sector of Nambu-Goldstone bosons scattering off D mesons. It builds on the on-shell UChPT studies of the same system by Guo (2009), Liu (2012), Wang (2012), and Altenbuchinger (2013), and shows that including off-shell effects can improve the description of lattice QCD data while supporting the validity of the simpler on-shell approximation for studying the light-quark mass evolution of hadronic observables.
