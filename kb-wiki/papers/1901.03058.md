# The pseudoscalar meson and baryon octet interaction with strangeness zero in the unitary coupled-channel approximation

> The interaction of the pseudoscalar meson and the baryon octet is investigated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the unitary coupled-channel approximation, In addition to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, the contribution of the $s-$ and $u-$ channel potentials in the S-wave approximation are taken into account. In the sector of isospin $I=1/2$ and strangeness $S=0$, a pole is detected in the reasonable region on the complex energy plane of $\sqrt{s}$ in the center of mass frame by analyzing the behavior of the scattering amplitude, which is higher than the $О·N$ threshold and lies on the third Riemann sheet. Thus it can be regarded as a resonance state and might correspond to the $N(1535)$ particle in the review of the Particle Data Group(PDG). The coupling constants of this resonance state to the $ПЂN$, $О·N$, $K О›$ and $K ОЈ$ channels are calculated, and it is found that this resonance state couples strongly to the hidden strange channels. Apparently, the hidden strange channels play an important role in the generation of the resonance state with strangeness zero. The interaction of the pseudoscalar meson and the baryon octet is repulsive in the sector of isospin $I=3/2$ and strangeness $S=0$, therefore, no resonance state can be generated dynamically.

## Key claim

By solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the Weinberg-Tomozawa term plus s- and u-channel potentials in the S-wave approximation, a pole at 1518-i46 MeV on the complex energy plane is detected in the I=1/2, S=0 sector, which the authors propose might correspond to the N(1535) particle from the Particle Data Group, but only after adjusting subtraction constants across 39 parameter sets to place the pole within the PDG range.

## Method

The authors constructed the S-wave potential for pseudoscalar meson (ПЂ, О·, K) and baryon octet (N, О›, ОЈ) interactions using a nonlinear realized chiral Lagrangian, including the Weinberg-Tomozawa contact term, s-channel, and u-channel contributions. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the unitary coupled-channel approximation with a revised loop function that incorporates relativistic corrections, using dimensional regularization with a fixed scale Ој=630 MeV. The subtraction constants a_ПЂN, a_О·N, a_KО›, and a_KОЈ were varied systematically from -3.2 to -0.5 in steps of 0.3 to search for poles in the complex energy plane consistent with the N(1535) resonance.

* 12 sets of subtraction constants generated a resonance with mass about 1520 MeV and decay width about 90 MeV; the other 27 sets gave slightly larger mass and width.
* The subtraction constant a_KО› was -3.2 in 38 of the 39 parameter sets (the exception was -2.9 in the eighth set), indicating its stability and importance.
* The ПЂN s-channel potential was repulsive; the u-channel potentials in the S-wave approximation were attractive; the Weinberg-Tomozawa term was dominant.
* In the I=3/2, S=0 sector, all coefficients C_ij were negative (-1 for ПЂN and KОЈ), making the total potential repulsive and producing no resonance.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach of Kaiser (1995) and Inoue (2002) by including s- and u-channel potentials alongside the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, using a revised loop function with relativistic corrections from Dongsun (2015). It contrasts with the Hamiltonian effective field theory of Liuzw (2016) and the next-to-leading-order Lagrangian study of Bruns (2011), which found different coupling strengths for the N(1535) to the KО› channel. The results enable further investigation of the role of hidden strange channels (О·N, KО›, KОЈ) in dynamically generating the N(1535) resonance, and suggest that the subtraction constant a_KО› is a critical parameter for reproducing the PDG pole position.
