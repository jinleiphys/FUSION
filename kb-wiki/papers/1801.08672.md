# Nuclear fourth-order symmetry energy and its effects on neutron star properties in the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> Adopting the density dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approaches, the properties of the nuclear fourth-order symmetry energy $S_4$ are studied within the covariant density functional (CDF) theory. It is found that the fourth-order symmetry energies are suppressed in RHF at both saturation and supranuclear densities, where the extra contribution from the Fock terms is demonstrated, specifically via the isoscalar meson-nucleon coupling channels. The reservation of $S_4$ and higher-order symmetry energies in the nuclear equation of state then affects essentially the prediction of neutron star properties, which is illustrated in the quantities such as the proton fraction, the core-crust transition density as well as the fraction of crustal moment of inertia. Since the Fock terms enhance the density dependence of the thermodynamical potential, the RHF calculations predict systematically smaller values of density, proton fraction and pressure at the core-crust transition boundary of neutron stars than density dependent RMF ones. In addition, a linear anti-correlation between the core-crust transition density $ρ_t$ and the density slope of symmetry energy $L$ is found which is then utilized to constrain the core-crust transition density as $ρ_t\thicksim[0.069, 0.098]~\rm{fm}^{-3}$ with the recent empirical information on $L$. The study clarifies the important role of the fourth-order symmetry energy in determining the properties of nuclear matter at extreme isospin or density conditions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the fourth-order symmetry energy $S_4$ is systematically suppressed in relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations compared to relativistic mean-field (RMF) ones, due to extra Fock-term contributions from isoscalar meson-nucleon coupling channels, and this suppression affects neutron star properties such as proton fraction, core-crust transition density, and crustal moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors used density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approaches within covariant density functional (CDF) theory to calculate the nuclear equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter. They extracted the fourth-order symmetry energy $S_4$ by expanding the binding energy per nucleon in a Taylor series with respect to isospin asymmetry $\delta$, using a seven-point finite difference method with step size $\delta=0.01$. The calculations employed RHF functionals PKA1, PKO1, PKO2, PKO3 and RMF functionals PKDD, TW99, DD-ME1, DD-ME2.

## Key numbers

- $S_4(\rho_0)$ in RHF functionals ranges from $0.352$ MeV (PKA1) to $0.583$ MeV (PKO2), while in RMF functionals it is around $0.645$-$0.661$ MeV.
- The kinetic part $S_{4,kin}(\rho_0)$ in RHF is $1.09$-$2.12$ MeV, systematically larger than RMF values of about $0.477$-$0.494$ MeV.
- The Fock term contribution $S_{4,pot}^E$ gives a maximum suppression of approximately $-4.7$ MeV to the potential part of $S_4$ over a broad density region.
- The core-crust transition density $\rho_t$ is constrained to $[0.069, 0.098]~\rm{fm}^{-3}$ using a linear anti-correlation with the density slope of symmetry energy $L$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of higher-order symmetry energy effects in neutron stars, such as those by Xu (2009) and Cai (2012), by incorporating Fock terms that were neglected in earlier RMF calculations. The results contradict the large $S_4(\rho_0)$ values (e.g., $20.0\pm4.6$ MeV) extracted from an extended nuclear mass formula by Wang (2017), while agreeing with predictions from density functional theory and chiral pion-nucleon dynamics that find $S_4(\rho_0)$ generally below 2 MeV. The study enables more accurate predictions of neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process and pulsar glitch properties by clarifying the role of fourth-order symmetry energy in the equation of state.
