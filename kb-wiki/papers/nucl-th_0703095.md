# Rapidity dependence of hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 200$ GeV

> The rapidity and transverse momentum spectra for identified hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 200$ GeV are computed in a quark combination model. The data of rapidity distributions for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p(\bar{p})$ and net protons $(p-\bar{p})$ are well described. We also predict rapidity distributions for $K^{0}_{s}$, $Λ(\barΛ)$, $\mathrm{Ξ^{-}}$ ($\mathrm{\barΞ^{_+}}$) and $\mathrm{Ω^{-}}+\mathrm{\barΩ}^{_+}$. The multiplicity ratios of charged antihadrons to hadrons as a function of rapidity are reproduced. The results for ${p}_{T}$ spectra of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p(\bar{p})$ and for the $p/π$ ratios in a broader ${p}_{T}$ range agree well with the data. Finally the rapidity dependence of transverse momentum distributions for hadrons are given.

## Key claim
The quark combination model can describe hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV across a wide range of rapidities and transverse momenta, but the model fails to reproduce proton and antiproton transverse momentum spectra at forward rapidity $y\approx 3.2$, likely because the transverse momentum distribution of net-quarks varies with rapidity and is not fully thermalized at large rapidity.

## Method
The authors used a quark combination model where constituent quarks and antiquarks are arranged in one-dimensional order in phase space and combine into ground-state mesons (36-plets) and baryons (56-plets), followed by resonance decays simulated with PYTHIA 6.1. The momentum distributions of newborn quarks were described using a thermal model with nonuniform longitudinal flow (parameterized by an expansion function $w(y)$) and transverse flow (with surface velocity $\beta_s$ and radial exponent $n$), while minijet partons at high $p_T$ were parameterized by a power-law function. The rapidity distribution of net-quarks was obtained from a three-sources relativistic diffusion model (RDM) solving a Fokker-Planck equation, with parameters fitted to BRAHMS net-baryon data.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of thermal source: $T=170$ MeV, consistent with lattice QCD phase transition temperature ($T\sim 165-185$ MeV).
- Constituent quark masses: $m=0.34$ GeV for light quarks/antiquarks, $m=0.5$ GeV for strange quarks/antiquarks.
- Transition point between thermal and minijet partons: $p_0=1.8$ GeV/c.
- Transverse flow parameters: $n=0.5$ for light and strange quarks; $\beta_s=0.45c$ for light quarks/antiquarks, $\beta_s=0.56c$ for strange quarks/antiquarks.
- RDM parameters: $\tau_{int}/\tau_y=0.263$, $D_y\tau_y=0.9$; net-quark source weights $N_1=N_2=214$, $N_{mid}=607$ for central collisions.
- Strangeness suppression factor: $\lambda_s=0.55$; parameter $\beta=0.36$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- Rapidity densities at midrapidity: $K^0_s$: data $45.28^*$, model $44.1$; $\Lambda$: data $16.7\pm0.2\pm1.1$, model $15.9$; $\overline{\Lambda}$: data $12.7\pm0.2\pm0.9$, model $12.5$; $\Xi^-$: data $2.17\pm0.06\pm0.19$, model $2.13$; $\overline{\Xi}^+$: data $1.83\pm0.05\pm0.2$, model $1.76$; $\Omega^-+\overline{\Omega}^+$: data $0.53\pm0.04\pm0.04$, model $0.59$.
- Transverse momentum difference limits: $\Delta p_T=0.66$ GeV for mesons, $\Delta p_T=1.0$ GeV for baryons.
- $p/\pi^+$ and $\overline{p}/\pi^-$ ratios peak at $p_T\sim 2-3$ GeV with values near unity, then approach $p/\pi^+\approx 0.4$, $\overline{p}/\pi^-\approx 0.25$ at $p_T\gtrsim 7$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' quark combination model, previously applied to midrapidity hadron production (Shao 2004, Yao 2006, Shao 2006), to describe rapidity-dependent hadron spectra measured by the BRAHMS and STAR collaborations at RHIC. The model successfully reproduces BRAHMS data for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons across rapidity, and predicts rapidity distributions for strange hadrons ($K^0_s$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$). The failure at $y\approx 3.2$ for protons and antiprotons suggests that net-quark thermalization decreases at forward rapidity, providing a constraint for future models of parton dynamics and nuclear stopping at RHIC.
