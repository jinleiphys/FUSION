# Elastic scattering, polarization and absorption of relativistic antiprotons on nuclei

> We perform Glauber model calculations of the antiproton-nucleus elastic and quasielastic scattering and absorption in the beam momentum range $\sim 0.5\div10$ GeV/c. A good agreement of our calculations with available LEAR data and with earlier Glauber model studies of the $\bar p A$ elastic scattering allows us to make predictions at the beam momenta of $\sim 10$ GeV/c, i.e. at the regime of the PANDA experiment at FAIR. The comparison with the proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross sections shows that the diffractive minima are much deeper in the $\bar p A$ case due to smaller absolute value of the ratio of the real-to-imaginary part of the elementary elastic amplitude. Significant polarization signal for $\bar p A$ elastic scattering at 10 GeV/c is expected. We have also revealed a strong dependence of the $\bar p A$ absorption cross section on the slope parameter of the transverse momentum dependence of the elementary $\bar pN$ amplitude. The $\bar p A$ optical potential is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors use the Glauber model to predict antiproton-nucleus elastic scattering, polarization, and absorption cross sections at beam momenta up to 10 GeV/c, finding that the diffractive minima are much deeper than in proton-nucleus scattering due to the smaller absolute value of the ratio of the real-to-imaginary part of the elementary elastic amplitude, and that a significant polarization signal is expected at 10 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber model calculations for antiproton-nucleus elastic and quasielastic scattering and absorption across a beam momentum range of approximately 0.5 to 10 GeV/c. They used parameterized elementary antiproton-nucleon amplitudes with central and spin-orbit components, including total cross sections, real-to-imaginary ratios, and slope parameters, and incorporated Coulomb corrections and center-of-mass corrections for the nuclear wave functions. The absorption cross section was computed from the difference between the total cross section (via the optical theorem) and the integrated elastic diffraction cross section.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range studied: approximately 0.5 to 10 GeV/c
- The antiproton-proton total cross section parameterization uses a high-momentum formula: 38.4 + 77.6 * p_lab^(-0.64) + 0.26 * ln^2(p_lab) - 1.2 * ln(p_lab) (in mb, with p_lab in GeV/c) for p_lab >= 5.92 GeV/c
- The absorption cross section formula: sigma_abs = integral d^2 b (1 - exp(-2 * Im[chi_N(b)]))
- The semiclassical absorption cross section formula: sigma_abs = integral d^2 b (1 - exp(-sum_{tau=n,p} sigma_tau * T_tau(b)))
- The c.m. correction factor: H_cm(q) = exp(<R^2> q^2 / 6), with <R^2> = 6 a_0^2 / (4A) for harmonic oscillator densities

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber model studies of antiproton-nucleus elastic scattering that successfully described LEAR data at low beam momenta (e.g., Dalkarov (1984, 1986)), and provides predictions for the PANDA experiment at FAIR at approximately 10 GeV/c. The results enable tests of the antiproton-nucleon elastic amplitude parameters, including the real-to-imaginary ratio and spin-orbit components, and provide absorption cross sections relevant for cosmic antiproton flux calculations (e.g., Moskalenko (2001)) and potential antiproton radiotherapy applications (e.g., Holzscheiter (2006)).
