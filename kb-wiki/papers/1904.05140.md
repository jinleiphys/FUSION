# Role of consistent parameter sets in an assessment of the alpha-particle optical potential below the Coulomb barrier

> Background: Further studies of high-precision measurements of alpha-induced reaction data below the Coulomb barrier have still raised questions about the alpha-particle optical model potential (OMP) within various mass ranges, i.e. for 64Zn, 108Cd, 113,115In, 121,123Sb, and 191,193Ir target nuclei. Purpose: The accuracy as well as eventual uncertainties and/or systematic errors of using a previous optical potential are much better considered by analysis of such accurate data. Method: Statistical model (SM) calculations of the (alpha,x) reaction cross sections have been carried out using model parameters which were previously obtained by analyzing independent data, particularly gamma-ray strength functions, and taking into account their uncertainties and questions of extrapolation for nuclei without similar data. Results: Consistent description of the recent alpha-induced reaction data is provided by the above-mentioned optical potential with no empirical rescaling factors of either its own parameters or the gamma and/or nucleon widths. Effects of still uncertain SM parameters on calculated alpha-induced reaction cross sections and conclusions on the alpha-OMP can be now discussed due to unprecedented precision of the new data. Conclusions: The alpha-particle optical potential has been confirmed at incident energies below the Coulomb barrier using statistical-model parameters validated through a former analysis of independent data.

## Key claim

The alpha-particle optical potential previously developed by the authors (Avrigeanu, 2014) is confirmed for incident energies below the Coulomb barrier, but only when statistical-model parameters are used that have themselves been validated through independent data analysis (particularly gamma-ray strength functions), without any empirical rescaling factors for the potential parameters or the gamma and nucleon widths.

## Method

Statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) calculations of (alpha,x) reaction cross sections were carried out using the code STAPRE-H95, with the alpha-particle optical potential of Avrigeanu (2014). The nucleon and gamma-ray transmission coefficients, back-shifted Fermi gas nuclear level densities, and gamma-ray strength functions were established or validated using independently measured data including neutron total cross sections, (p,n) reaction cross sections, gamma-ray strength functions, (p,gamma) reaction cross sections, low-lying levels, and resonance data. Direct-interaction distorted-wave Born approximation calculations using DWUCK4 were also performed for collective inelastic-scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{64}$Zn target, the calculated (alpha,p) reaction cross sections show good agreement with new data from Ornelas et al. (2016), with changes due to different nucleon optical model potentials and gamma-ray strength function models within experimental error bars
- For $^{64}$Zn, the measured (alpha,n) reaction cross section at the higher incident energy is described within the limit of 2-sigma uncertainty
- For $^{64}$Zn, the calculated (alpha,gamma) cross section using the EGLO model for electric-dipole gamma-ray strength functions agrees entirely with the newly-measured data from Ornelas et al. (2016)
- For $^{64}$Zn, inclusion of the M1 upbend gamma-ray strength function component increases the calculated (alpha,gamma) cross sections by no more than about 12% around 8 MeV incident energy, and less than 3% below about 5.5 MeV and above 12.5 MeV
- For $^{64}$Zn, the uncertainty band from gamma-ray strength function and nuclear level density effects on (alpha,gamma) cross sections ranges from 3% around 5 MeV incident energy to 48% at 16.5 MeV
- For $^{108}$Cd, the alpha-particle optical potential of Avrigeanu (2014) with only the EGLO model provides good agreement with measured (alpha,gamma) excitation functions from Palumbo et al. (2016)
- For $^{108}$Cd, the optical potential of McFadden and Satchler (1966) overestimates (alpha,gamma) cross sections at lower energies
- For $^{113,115}$In, the (alpha,gamma) data are underestimated just above the (alpha,n) threshold even when including uncertainties from nuclear level density and gamma-ray strength function parameters
- For $^{113,115}$In, the ground-state and isomeric (alpha,n) cross sections are well described within error bars or 2-sigma uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Avrigeanu, 2016) of alpha-induced reaction data to new high-precision measurements on $^{64}$Zn (Ornelas et al., 2016), $^{108}$Cd (Palumbo et al., 2016), $^{113,115}$In (Gyurky et al., 2018), $^{121,123}$Sb (Zilges et al., 2018), and $^{191,193}$Ir (Tsinganis et al., 2018). It contradicts the conclusion of Gyurky et al. (2018) that further efforts are needed to establish an optical potential simultaneously describing elastic scattering and reaction data, by showing that consistent parameter sets from independent data analysis resolve the apparent discrepancies. The work enables future use of the validated optical potential for alpha-particle emission calculations in neutron-induced reactions, a question still open since the work of Avrigeanu (1994) and McFadden and Satchler (1966).
