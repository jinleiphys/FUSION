# Delta Decay in Nuclear Medium

> Proton-nucleus collisions, where the beam proton gets excited to the delta resonance and then decays to p$π^+$, either inside or outside the nuclear medium, are studied. Cross-sections for various kinematics for the (p,p$' π^+$) reaction between 500 MeV and 1 GeV beam energy are calculated to see the effects of the nuclear medium on the propagation and decay of the resonance. The cross-sections studied include proton energy spectra in coincidence with the pion, four momentum transfer distributions, and the invariant p$π^+$ mass distributions. We find that the effect of the nuclear medium on these cross-sections mainly reduces their magnitudes. Comparing these cross-sections with those considering the decay of the delta outside the nucleus only, we further find that at 500 MeV the two sets of cross-sections have large differences, while by 1 GeV the differences between them become much smaller.

## Key claim

The effect of the nuclear medium on (p,p'π⁺) cross-sections is primarily to reduce their magnitudes; at 500 MeV beam energy the cross-sections differ significantly from those assuming delta decay only outside the nucleus, while by 1 GeV the differences become much smaller, especially for lighter target nuclei like ¹²C.

## Method

The authors developed a formalism for the A(p,p'π⁺)B reaction where the beam proton is excited to a Δ⁺⁺ resonance via one-pion-exchange, which then propagates through the nucleus and decays to pπ⁺ either inside or outside the nuclear medium. All continuum particles (proton, delta, pion) were treated in the eikonal approximation with absorptive optical potentials, and the delta propagator included a spreading width from ΔN→NN collisions. Cross-sections were calculated for ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb targets at 500 MeV and 1 GeV beam energies, comparing results with and without nuclear distortions of the delta and its decay products.

## Key numbers

- Free delta resonance parameters: m_Δ = 1232 MeV, Γ₀ = 120 MeV, γ = 200 MeV
- Coupling constants: f_π* = 2.156 (πNΔ), f_π = 1.008 (πNN)
- At 1 GeV on ¹²C, the peak four-momentum transfer cross-section is reduced by a factor of about 6 due to beam proton and delta distortions, and by an additional factor of 3/2 due to p' and π⁺ distortions
- At 500 MeV on ¹²C, the corresponding reduction factors are about 2 (beam and delta) and 3 (p' and π⁺)
- Invariant mass peak for ¹²C at 1 GeV appears around 1200 MeV (shift of ≈30 MeV from 1232 MeV); at 500 MeV it appears around 1150 MeV (shift of ≈80 MeV)
- Delta-nucleus interaction suppresses the peak invariant mass cross-section by about 30% at 1 GeV and 50% at 500 MeV for ¹²C
- Spreading width Γ_s = 70 MeV taken from the delta-hole model of Hirata et al. (1977)
- Form factor cutoff Λ = 1.2 GeV/c for both πNΔ and πNN vertices

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of delta dynamics in nuclei, particularly the (p,Δ⁺⁺) reaction studied by Jain (1983, 1985) and the delta-hole model of Hirata, Lenz, and Yazaki (1977). It provides a formalism that incorporates the unstable nature of the delta and nuclear medium effects on its propagation and decay, unlike earlier DWBA treatments that treated the delta as stable. The calculations are motivated by planned experiments at TRIUMF using dual magnetic spectrometers (Yen et al., 1994 proposal). The results enable identification of beam energies and target masses where the simpler "decay outside nucleus only" model is adequate versus where full in-medium treatment is required.
