# Generalization of the Bloch-Messiah-Zumino theorem

> It is shown how to construct a basis in which two arbitrary complex antisymmetric matrices C and C' acquire simultaneously canonical forms. The present construction is not restricted by any conditions on properties of the C^+C' matrix. Canonical bases pertaining to the generator-coordinate-method treatment of many-fermion systems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proves that two arbitrary complex antisymmetric matrices \(C\) and \(C'\) can be simultaneously brought to canonical forms using a common (generally non-unitary) basis, without any restrictions on the properties of the matrix \(C^+C'\); this generalizes earlier results that required \(C^+C'\) to be fully diagonalizable.

## Method

The author constructs the canonical basis by analyzing the Jordan canonical form of the matrix \(C^+C'\). For each pair of degenerate Jordan blocks (same eigenvalue and same block length), the antisymmetry of \(C\) forces the basis vectors in the two blocks to be related by specific transformation matrices, leading to block-off-diagonal canonical forms for both \(C\) and \(C'\). The construction handles non-degenerate blocks by showing they can only exist in the null-eigenvalue subspace, which is separated and treated separately.

## Key numbers

- For a pair of degenerate Jordan blocks of length \(L\), the canonical form of \(C^+\) is given by \((W^{-1}C^+W^{-1T})_{ji} = s_{I_j}C^{I_j+}_{k_jk_i}\delta_{I_j\tilde{I}_i}\), where the symmetric matrix \(C^I\) satisfies \(C^I_{k,L-k+1}>0\).
- The condition linking the two matrices in a degenerate pair is \(\beta\beta'^* = D\), where \(D\) contains the Jordan-block eigenvalues on its diagonal.
- An explicit counterexample uses \(4\times4\) matrices with \(A = \begin{pmatrix}1 & a \\ a^* & 0\end{pmatrix}\) and \(A' = \begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}\); for real \(a\) the matrix \(C^+C'\) cannot be fully diagonalized, but for any non-zero imaginary part of \(a\) it can.

## Context

This work extends the classic Bloch-Messiah-Zumino theorem (Bloch 1962, Zumino 1962) and the results of Burzynski (1995), which required \(C^+C'\) to be fully diagonalizable. It corrects an incorrect conjecture in Burzynski (1995) that the simple canonical forms could always be achieved, as noted by Robledo (1999). The construction is directly applicable to generator-coordinate-method (GCM) calculations of overlaps and transition densities between fermion-pair condensates, where the matrix \(C^+C'\) appears naturally. The paper also shows that for typical nuclear physics applications with time-even states and attractive pairing forces, the simpler diagonalizable case remains the most common.
