# Double distributions: Loose ends

## Key claim

Double distributions (DDs) need not vanish at their boundary, contrary to assumptions in earlier treatments; boundary terms do not change the inherent ambiguity in defining DDs, but boundary conditions must be satisfied to switch between different decompositions, and phenomenological parameterizations of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) must generally include such terms.

## Method

The paper analyzes the ambiguity in defining double distributions for both spin-zero (pion) and spin-one-half (proton) cases by examining the transformation properties of DDs under a potential function χ(β,α). The author derives the boundary conditions that the potential must satisfy to preserve the bilocal matrix element, and demonstrates explicit transformations to the Polyakov-Weiss gauge, the Drell-Yan gauge, and a minimal gauge that reduces the number of independent DDs.

## Key numbers

- The support region for DDs is defined by integration limits: β ∈ [-1,1] and α ∈ [-1+|β|, 1-|β|]
- DDs must vanish only at the corners of support: δ(α)δ(|β|-1) and δ(β)δ(|α|-1)
- The D-term for the pion is defined as D(α) = ∫_{-1+|α|}^{1-|α|} dβ G⁺(β,α)
- The Drell-Yan gauge yields the reduction relation f₁(x) = H(x,0,0) = D̄(x,0) for the quark distribution
- The form factor relation: F(t) = ∫ dx H(x,ξ,t) = ∫ dx D̄(x;t)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Teryaev (2001) on the gauge ambiguity of DDs for the pion to the proton case, and addresses boundary issues left unresolved in earlier treatments by Radyushkin (1997) and Polyakov (1999). The paper enables the inclusion of boundary terms in phenomenological parameterizations of GPDs, which is necessary for modeling hard exclusive reactions such as deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson electroproduction.
