# Effects of density-dependent spin-orbit interactions in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of the charge radii and densities of Pb isotopes

> I have investigated the $N$-dependence of the charge radii $r_c$ across $N=126$ in the Pb isotopes and the surface densities of $^{208}$Pb using new Skyrme interactions that contain a density-dependent spin-orbit term, which I have named the Skyrme-ddso interactions. I have compared the results obtained using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations that employ the Skyrme-ddso interactions with the original Skyrme results and have discussed the effects of including the density-dependent spin-orbit term. The results for the kink behavior of $r_c$ at $N=126$ in the Pb isotopes were improved by the inclusion of the density-dependent spin--orbit term. Moreover, the new Skyrme calculations yield better results for the inner part of the surface proton density of $^{208}$Pb at $r\sim 5$ fm. The change in the $\ell s$ potentials from the original Skyrme calculation contributes to the kink behavior through its effects on the $\ell s$ splitting of the neutron $2g_{7/2,9/2}$ orbitals. It also affects the inner part of the surface proton density through its effect on the proton $1h_{11/2}$ orbitals. I have further demonstrated that the change in the isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of the spin--orbit term makes only a minor contribution to the single-particle energies and to the kink behavior in the Pb isotopes. In addition, I have investigated $^{48}$Ca using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with the new Skyrme interactions and have determined the effects of the density-dependent spin--orbit term on its radii and densities.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a density-dependent spin-orbit term in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations improves the description of the kink behavior in charge radii of Pb isotopes at N=126 and the inner part of the surface proton density of 208Pb at r~5 fm, though the change in the isoscalar-to-isovector ratio of the spin-orbit term makes only a minor contribution to the single-particle energies and the kink behavior.

## Method

Spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) calculations were performed using the HFBRAD code for even-even Pb isotopes and 48Ca, with new Skyrme interactions (named Skyrme-ddso) that incorporate a density-dependent spin-orbit term of the form D(ρ) = -w4 ρ^γ4 with an isoscalar-type ratio (x4=1). The original spin-orbit term was reduced to 33% and the density-dependent term was appended, with the strength w4 adjusted to reproduce the same spin-orbit energy for 208Pb as the original Skyrme interaction. Results were compared with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 interactions, and with conventional Skyrme parametrizations including SLy4, SkM*, SAMi, SkO', SkI series, SkT series, and Skxs20.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass m*/m in nuclear matter at normal density is 0.572 for DD-ME2, 0.69 for SLy4, and 0.79 for SkM*
- The SLy4-IS interaction uses b4=93 MeV and b'4=0 for the IS-type spin-orbit term, approximately equal to b4=3W0/4=92.5 MeV derived from the ansatz ρp=(Z/N)ρn
- The SLy4mod interaction uses a 20% smaller value b4=75 MeV for the IS-type spin-orbit term compared to the original SLy4 interaction
- The inner-surface region of 208Pb where proton densities are underestimated by Skyrme interactions is at r~5 fm, and for 48Ca at r~2.5 fm

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the kink behavior in charge radii of Pb isotopes at N=126, which was successfully described by relativistic mean-field calculations but not by conventional Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. Reinhard (1995) demonstrated that the small energy difference between neutron 1h11/2 and 2g9/2 orbitals in 208Pb is essential for reproducing the kink, while Goddard (2012) showed that a modified SLy4 interaction (SLy4mod) with 20% weaker spin-orbit strength reproduced the kink. Nakada (2014, 2015) employed density-dependent spin-orbit interactions in finite-range calculations and obtained improved isotope shifts. The present work enables systematic improvement of Skyrme energy-density functionals for describing both charge radii systematics and detailed surface density profiles, which are important for testing nuclear models against high-precision electron scattering data.
