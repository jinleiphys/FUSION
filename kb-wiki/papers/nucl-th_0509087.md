# Centrality dependence of global variables in relativistic heavy ion collisions: Final $p_{T}$ data analysis in the framework of a statistical model

> The global variables like the transverse energy at midrapidity, the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity and the total multiplicity of charged particles are evaluated in the single-freeze-out statistical model for different centrality bins at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ and 200 GeV. Full description of decays of hadron resonances is applied in these estimations. The geometric parameters of the model are obtained from the fit to the final data on the $p_{T}$ spectra. The predicted values of the global variables agree qualitatively well with the experimental data. The centrality independence of the total number of charged particles per participant pair has been also reproduced.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out statistical model, with geometric parameters obtained from fits to final $p_{T}$ spectra of identified charged hadrons, reproduces the centrality dependence of global variables (transverse energy density, charged particle multiplicity density, and total charged particle multiplicity) only qualitatively for RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV, but achieves much better agreement for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, including the centrality independence of total charged particles per participant pair.

## Method

Geometric parameters $\tau$ (freeze-out time) and $\rho_{max}$ (maximal transverse radius) were determined from $\chi^{2}$ fits to final $p_{T}$ spectra of $\pi^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ measured by PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS collaborations at RHIC for various centrality bins at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ and 200 GeV, with thermal parameters taken from independent fits to particle abundance ratios. Using these geometric parameters and the full resonance decay treatment (including weak decays), the model then computed the pseudorapidity densities of transverse energy $dE_{T}/d\eta|_{mid}$ and charged particles $dN_{ch}/d\eta|_{mid}$, as well as total charged particle multiplicity, for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For PHENIX at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, most central bin (0-5%): $\rho_{max} = 8.46 \pm 0.10$ fm, $\tau = 8.84 \pm 0.08$ fm, $\chi^{2}/NDF = 0.80$
- For STAR at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, most central bin (0-5%): $\rho_{max} = 9.22 \pm 0.18$ fm, $\tau = 7.13 \pm 0.06$ fm, $\chi^{2}/NDF = 0.29$
- For PHENIX at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV, most central bin (0-5%): $\rho_{max} = 6.50 \pm 0.27$ fm, $\tau = 8.23 \pm 0.23$ fm, $\chi^{2}/NDF = 0.52$
- Scaling exponents for geometric parameters: $\rho_{max} \sim N_{part}^{\kappa}$ with $\kappa = 0.40-0.43$, $\tau \sim N_{part}^{\kappa}$ with $\kappa = 0.23-0.33$
- For most central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, freeze-out volume at beginning $V_{f.o.}^{(1)} \approx 3V_{Au}$ (where $V_{Au} \approx 1160$ fm$^{3}$), and at central slice cessation $V_{f.o.}^{(2)} \approx 5V_{Au}$
- Model underprediction of $dN_{ch}/d\eta$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV is about 15%, attributed to low-$p_{T}$ pion yields being 29% below data on average
- For PHENIX at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, low-$p_{T}$ pion underestimation is about 15% on average

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out statistical model analysis of Prorok (2004) by using final (rather than preliminary) $p_{T}$ spectra data from PHENIX (Adler 2003), STAR (Adams 2003), and BRAHMS (Arsene 2005), and by fitting additional centrality bins not covered in earlier work by Baran (2003) and Broniowski (2002). The improved fits now reproduce the centrality independence of total charged particles per participant pair reported by the PHOBOS Collaboration (Back 2003), which was not achieved with preliminary data. The results enable further tests of statistical hadronization models against global observables that are independent of particle identification, and suggest that the model's low-$p_{T}$ extrapolations can be validated against PHOBOS data (Back 2004).
