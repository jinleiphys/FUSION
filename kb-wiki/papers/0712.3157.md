# Dynamical instabilities in density-dependent hadronic relativistic models

> Unstable modes in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) at subsaturation densities are studied in the framework of relativistic mean-field density-dependent hadron models. The size of the instabilities that drive the system are calculated and a comparison with results obtained within the non-linear Walecka model is presented. The distillation and anti-distillation effects are discussed.

## Key claim

Density-dependent relativistic hadron models (DDRM) predict larger instability regions and weaker distillation effects than the non-linear Walecka model (NL3) at subsaturation densities, but only for highly asymmetric matter; for nearly symmetric matter the three models give similar results.

## Method

The authors linearize the Vlasov equation for asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) including electrons and the electromagnetic field, within the relativistic mean-field framework with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (TW and DD-ME2 parametrizations). They solve the resulting dispersion relation for longitudinal normal modes and identify unstable modes by looking for solutions with imaginary frequencies. The dynamical spinodals, direction of instability (ratio of proton to neutron density fluctuations), and most unstable modes (growth rates and associated cluster sizes) are computed and compared with results from the NL3 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for TW: 0.153 fm\(^{-3}\); for DD-ME2: 0.152 fm\(^{-3}\); for NL3: 0.148 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32.0 MeV (TW), 32.3 MeV (DD-ME2), 37.4 MeV (NL3).
- Incompressibility: 240 MeV (TW), 250.89 MeV (DD-ME2), 272 MeV (NL3).
- Effective mass ratio \(M^*/M\): 0.56 (TW), 0.572 (DD-ME2), 0.60 (NL3).
- Density at the inner edge of the crust for neutrino-free matter (\(Y_\nu=0\)): 0.050 fm\(^{-3}\) (NL3), 0.076 fm\(^{-3}\) (TW), 0.073 fm\(^{-3}\) (DD-ME2).
- Density at the inner edge of the crust for trapped neutrinos (\(Y_L=0.4\)): 0.082 fm\(^{-3}\) (NL3), 0.084 fm\(^{-3}\) (TW), 0.083 fm\(^{-3}\) (DD-ME2).
- Maximum momentum transfer \(k\) (in MeV) for anti-distillation at \(y_p=0.35\) and \(\rho=0.15\rho_0\): 25 MeV (NL3), 22.4 MeV (TW), 21.8 MeV (DD-ME2).
- Size of clusters formed by the most unstable modes: 4 to 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of unstable modes in npe matter within the NL3 parametrization (Santos et al., 2006) to density-dependent relativistic models (DDRM) such as TW (Typel and Wolter, 1999) and DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al., 2005). It complements thermodynamical instability calculations (Providencia et al., 2006) by analyzing dynamical instabilities via the Vlasov formalism. The results enable more realistic predictions for the crust properties of compact stars and for neutrino opacities in supernova simulations, where the distillation and anti-distillation effects influence the clustering of neutrons and thus the neutrino mean free path.
