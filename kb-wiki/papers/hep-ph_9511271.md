# Master Formula Approach to $γγ\to ππ$ processes

> We analyze the $γγ\to π^0π^0$ and $γγ\to π^+π^-$ reactions using the master formula approach to chiral symmetry breaking. The pertinent vacuum correlators are estimated at tree level, and the results are compared with one- and two-loop chiral perturbation theory. The Compton scattering amplitude and the pion polarizabilities are also discussed.

## Key claim

The master formula approach to chiral symmetry breaking, when the vacuum correlation functions are saturated by low-mass physical states at tree level, yields results for γγ → ππ that are comparable to two-loop chiral perturbation theory, but the analysis explicitly relies on a broad scalar-isoscalar resonance (σ) with parameters fitted to the data rather than predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors express the γγ → ππ amplitude as a sum of vacuum correlation functions (vector-vector-vector, axial-axial-vector-vector, and vector-vector-scalar) using the master formula approach. They saturate these correlation functions at tree level with physical intermediate states: the ρ meson, the a₁ axial meson, and a scalar σ meson, using parametrized matrix elements for the unknown vertices. The free parameters (F_A for the ρ-a₁ transition and γ_{ρρσ} for the σ-ρ coupling) are fixed by fitting the calculated cross sections to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For γγ → π⁰π⁰, the fit uses m_σ = 500 MeV and Γ₁ = 550 MeV, yielding γ_{ρρσ} ≈ 6.1 m_π²/^m (where ^m is the average light quark mass).
- The neutral pion polarizability is calculated as α_π⁰(0) ≈ −2.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, compared to the one-loop chiral perturbation theory result of −0.49 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and experimental values of (0.69 ± 0.07 ± 0.04) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and (0.8 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The charged pion polarizability is calculated as α_π^±(0) ≈ +2.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ for F_A = 1, compared to the chiral perturbation theory value of 2.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and the experimental value of (2.2 ± 1.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- Input constants used: m_π = 135 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, f_ρ = 144 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the master formula approach of Yamagishi and Zahed (1994) to γγ → ππ processes, providing an alternative to one-loop chiral perturbation theory (Bijnens, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1988; Donoghue, Holstein, and Lin, 1988) and two-loop chiral perturbation theory (Bellucci, Gasser, and Sainio, 1994), which requires resonance saturation parameters. The results are similar in spirit to dispersion theory analyses (Pennington, 1990) and enable future lattice QCD calculations of the two vacuum correlation functions that govern these fusion processes.
