# Multiparticle azimuthal cumulants from transverse momentum conservation and collective flow

> We calculate the $n$th order of $2k$-particle azimuthal cumulants $c_n\{2k\}$ based on transverse momentum conservation (TMC) and collective flow $v_n$(n=2,3). We demonstrate that the TMC effect only leads to a nonzero $c_n\{2k\}$ with the sign of $(-1)^{nk}$ and the magnitude inversely proportional to $(N-2k)^{nk}$. The interplay between TMC and collective flow can change the signs of $c_2\{4\}$, $c_3\{2\}$ and $c_3\{4\}$ at some values of multiplicity $N$, which could provide a good probe to study the onset of collectivity and search for the substructure of proton in small colliding systems.

# Multiparticle azimuthal cumulants from transverse momentum conservation and collective flow

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transverse momentum conservation (TMC) alone produces nonzero multiparticle azimuthal cumulants $c_n\{2k\}$ with sign $(-1)^{nk}$ and magnitude inversely proportional to $(N-2k)^{nk}$, and that the interplay between TMC and collective flow ($v_2$ and $v_3$) can change the signs of $c_2\{4\}$, $c_3\{2\}$, and $c_3\{4\}$ at certain multiplicities $N$, providing a probe for the onset of collectivity and proton substructure in small colliding systems. The authors note that their analytic investigation should be viewed as a first approximation, assuming all momenta are fixed and identical.

## Method

The authors analytically calculate the $n$th order $2k$-particle azimuthal cumulants $c_n\{2k\}$ by expanding the $2k$-particle probability distribution under transverse momentum conservation, using the central limit theorem approximation. They then incorporate collective flow by modifying the probability distribution to include elliptic flow $v_2$ and triangular flow $v_3$, expanding the exponential term in powers of $X$ (which scales inversely with $N-2k$), and keeping terms up to the leading TMC contribution. The resulting expressions are evaluated at fixed transverse momentum $p$ and compared with experimental data from ATLAS and ALICE for p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- TMC-induced $c_n\{2k\}$ magnitude is inversely proportional to $(N-2k)^{nk}$, where $N$ is total particle multiplicity and $2k$ is the cumulant order
- For $c_2\{4\}$ from TMC only: $\frac{p_1^2 p_2^2 p_3^2 p_4^2}{[(N-4)\langle p^2\rangle_F]^4}$
- For $c_3\{2\}$ from TMC only: $-\frac{1}{6}\frac{p_1^3 p_2^3}{[(N-2)\langle p^2\rangle_F]^3}$
- For $c_3\{4\}$ from TMC only: $\frac{1}{2}\frac{p_1^3 p_2^3 p_3^3 p_4^3}{[(N-4)\langle p^2\rangle_F]^6}$
- Flow parameters used for calculations: $v_2=0.05$, $v_3=0.0175$, $v_{2F}=0.025$
- $\langle p^2\rangle_F$ taken as $0.135$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- Experimental data compared: ATLAS p+Pb 5.02 TeV for $c_2\{4\}$ and $c_3\{4\}$; ALICE p+Pb 5.02 TeV for $c_3\{2\}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on TMC effects on elliptic flow cumulants (Bzdak et al., 2017; Bzdak et al., 2018) by generalizing to higher-order cumulants and including triangular flow $v_3$. It builds on the framework of multiparticle azimuthal cumulants developed by Borghini et al. (2000) for reducing non-flow contributions. The results are relevant to understanding the onset of collectivity in small colliding systems (p+p, p+Pb), where recent experiments show strong collective flow similar to large A+A systems. The sign changes of $c_2\{4\}$, $c_3\{2\}$, and $c_3\{4\}$ with multiplicity could help distinguish between final-state hydrodynamic explanations and initial-state color glass condensate approaches, and may be sensitive to the three-hot-spot substructure of the proton as studied by Mantysaari et al. (2016) and Schenke et al. (2021).
