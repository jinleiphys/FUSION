# Photon Scattering off Nuclei

> The study of nuclear and subnuclear structure by means of photon scattering is outlined. Besides a brief exposition of the formalism a few illustrative examples are discussed.

# Photon Scattering off Nuclei

## Key claim

Photon scattering provides a particularly interesting source of information on off-shell properties of nuclear constituents, but genuine microscopic calculations of photon scattering cross sections are rather complicated since in principle the complete excitation spectrum must be taken into account. The paper presents a personal account of the author's own work and that of his collaborators, rather than a general review.

## Method

The scattering amplitude is expanded to second order in the electromagnetic field, yielding a two-photon amplitude (seagull term) and a resonance amplitude from iterated linear interactions. The amplitude is further expanded in terms of generalized polarizabilities classified by total angular momentum transfer J and multipole type (electric or magnetic), separating geometrical angular-momentum aspects from dynamical strength content. For the resonance amplitude, a subtracted dispersion relation is used to relate the real part of scalar polarizabilities to the imaginary part obtained from partial photoabsorption cross sections via the optical theorem.

## Key numbers

- Classical Thomson limit for the total nuclear scattering amplitude: T^{ii}_{\lambda'\lambda}(0,0) = - (Ze)^2/(A M) times the polarization dot product, with M_A approximated as A M.
- For elastic scattering off a spin-zero nucleus, only scalar polarizabilities (J=0) contribute.
- In the low-energy limit k=0, only the scalar E1 polarizability is nonvanishing: P^J(E1,E1)|_{k=0} = -delta_{J0} * sqrt(3) * (e^2 Z^2)/M_A.
- For elastic E1 scattering, the angular distribution is symmetric around 90 degrees in the center-of-mass system, with angular functions g_0^{E1}(theta) = (1/6)(1+cos^2 theta), g_1^{E1}(theta) = (1/4)(2+sin^2 theta), and g_2^{E1}(theta) = (1/12)(13+cos^2 theta).
- For photon scattering off complex nuclei in the Delta(1232) region at about 300 MeV, the static M1-scattering approach underestimates experimental cross sections for 12C and 208Pb by orders of magnitude at theta=115 degrees.
- For deuteron photon scattering, calculations were performed at photon energies of 50, 70, and 100 MeV using the Reid soft core potential with inclusion of meson exchange currents and NDelta and DeltaDelta isobar configurations.

## Context

This work extends the dynamic collective model of Danos and Greiner (1964) for giant dipole resonances in deformed nuclei, which was applied to nuclei such as 166Er and 165Ho. The formalism of generalized polarizabilities was first introduced by Fano (1960) for pure E1 transitions and later generalized by Arenhövel (1965), Arenhövel, Danos, and Greiner (1967), and others. The paper enables further microscopic calculations of photon scattering that include subnucleon degrees of freedom such as meson exchange currents and isobar configurations, particularly for light nuclei like the deuteron where such effects can be studied in detail.
