# Electromagnetic Dipole Strength in Transitional Nuclei

> Electromagnetic dipole absorption cross-sections of transitional nuclei with large-amplitude shape fluctuations are calculated in a microscopic way by introducing the concept of Instantaneous Shape Sampling. The concept bases on the slow shape dynamics as compared to the fast dipole vibrations. The elctromagnetic dipole strength is calculated by means of RPA for the instantaneous shapes, the probability of which is obtained by means of IBA. Very good agreement with the experimental absorption cross sections near the nucleon emission threshold is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper introduces Instantaneous Shape Sampling (ISS) as a new method to calculate dipole strength in transitional nuclei, claiming very good agreement with experimental absorption cross sections near the nucleon emission threshold. The key condition is that the method assumes the quadrupole deformation does not change during photon absorption, treating the shape dynamics as slow compared to the fast dipole vibrations.

## Method

The authors combine the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA-1) to generate probability distributions of instantaneous nuclear shapes over the β-γ plane with the quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) to calculate dipole absorption cross sections for each instantaneous deformation. The total cross section is obtained as the incoherent sum of instantaneous cross sections weighted by their probabilities, and the result is further folded with a Lorentzian of energy-dependent width Γ = αE² to account for collisional damping. The method is applied to the nuclides ⁸⁸Sr, ⁹⁰Zr, and ⁹²⁻¹⁰⁰Mo.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁴Mo, the collisional damping width Γ_CD = 4.0 MeV, the Landau fragmentation contribution Γ_LF = 1.1 MeV, and the ISS contribution Γ_ISS = 0.6 MeV, compared to the experimental total width Γ_exp = 5.7 MeV
- The coefficient α for the collisional damping Lorentzian is 0.0105 MeV⁻¹ for neutron number N=50 and 0.014 MeV⁻¹ for N>50
- For the Mo isotopes with N=94-100, the collisional damping widths Γ_CD are (4.0, 3.6, 3.5, 3.9) MeV respectively
- The combined contribution from fluctuating shape and Landau fragmentation Γ_LF+ISS for Mo isotopes with N=94-100 are (1.7, 2.7, 2.5, 4.0) MeV respectively
- The neutron separation energies S_n for the Mo isotopes are 9.68, 9.15, 8.64, 8.29 MeV for N=52, 54, 56, 58 respectively

## Context

This work extends the traditional Lorentzian parameterization of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) by Axel (1962) and Kadmenskii (1983) by providing a microscopic treatment of shape fluctuations in transitional nuclei. It addresses the limitations of particle-phonon coupling models such as QPM (Soloviev, 1992) and QTBA, which encounter principle problems in transitional nuclei. The method enables prediction of dipole strength functions for unstable nuclei needed in astrophysical simulations of supernovae and gamma-ray bursts, and can be extended by replacing the simple RPA with modern density functionals or the IBA-1 with any large-amplitude description of collective shape degrees of freedom.
