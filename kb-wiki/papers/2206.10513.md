# Fundamental-mode eigenfrequencies of neutral and charged twin neutron stars

> We investigate the effects of rapid and slow conversions on the fundamental-mode eigenfrequencies of hybrid neutron stars having highly discontinuous transitions between hadronic and quark matter, the so-called twin stars. We analyze some characteristic cases in the available parameter space of the equations of state for the hadronic and quark phases. Furthermore, we also consider the possibility that these stellar configurations are electrically charged. Our results indicate that for neutral configurations under rapid conversions the stability window coincides with the usual stability criterion, i.e. $\partial M/\partial p_c > 0$ and that the two branches are disconnected. This discontinuity remains when electric charge is considered, but the usual criterion is not sufficient to determine the star's stability. On the other hand, slow conversions connect initially disconnected branches and increase the stability window of the hybrid configurations. For both conversion speeds, the presence of electric charge diminishes the magnitude of the eigenfrequencies and its stability window.

## Key claim

The stability window and fundamental-mode eigenfrequencies of twin neutron stars depend critically on whether the phase conversion between hadronic and quark matter is rapid or slow, and on the presence of electric charge; for neutral stars under rapid conversions the usual stability criterion (∂M/∂p_c > 0) suffices and branches are disconnected, but for slow conversions this criterion fails and initially disconnected branches become connected, while electric charge reduces both the eigenfrequency magnitudes and the stability window for both conversion speeds.

## Method

The authors solved the Gondek-Rosińska et al. radial oscillation equations in general relativity for hybrid neutron stars with a sharp first-order phase transition, implementing distinct junction conditions at the hadron-quark interface for rapid and slow phase conversions. They modeled the hadronic phase with a generalized piecewise polytropic EoS matching the chiral-effective stiffest EoS of Hebeler et al. (2013) at 1.1n₀, and the quark phase using either the constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization (with sound speed set to the speed of light) or the multipolytrope (MP) approach of Alvarez-Castillo et al. (2017). Electric charge was included via an α-distribution where charge density is proportional to energy density (ρ_e = α ε), with α = 0.25 and 0.5 considered.

## Key numbers

- For neutral twin stars under rapid conversions, the maximum fundamental-mode frequency of the hadronic branch is larger than that of the twin branch for CSS Categories I, II, and III, but for Category IV the opposite holds: max(f_had) < max(f_twin).
- For neutral twin stars under slow conversions, only CSS Category I fails to satisfy max(f_had) < max(f_twin).
- For the MP approach under rapid conversions, the hadronic branch frequency is almost 4 times larger than the twin branch frequency.
- For charged twin stars (CSS Categories III and IV) under rapid conversions, increasing α from 0 to 0.5 diminishes both the magnitude of the eigenfrequencies and the stability window.
- For charged twin stars (CSS Categories I and II) under rapid conversions, no stable configurations exist; stable configurations only arise for slow conversions.
- For the MP approach, Sets 1 and 2 show no stable configurations under rapid conversions for any charge value, but do show stable configurations under slow conversions.

## Context

This work extends the static stability analysis of charged twin stars by Goncalves et al. (2021) by incorporating the dynamical effects of phase conversion speed on radial oscillations, following the formalism of Pereira et al. (2017) which showed that the usual ∂M/∂p_c ≥ 0 criterion is insufficient for hybrid stars with sharp phase transitions. The results complement studies of twin star stability by Lugones et al. (2021) and Lugones et al. (2021), and the CSS categorization scheme follows Christian et al. (2020). The findings enable future work to constrain the degree of discontinuity in the equation of state and the conversion dynamics from potential gravitational-wave observations of radial modes, and suggest that charged twin stars with masses exceeding 2.5 M⊙ (as hinted by the GW190814 event) may be possible but require careful dynamical stability analysis beyond the usual criterion.
