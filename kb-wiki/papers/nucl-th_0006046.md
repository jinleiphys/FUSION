# Nuclear Shell Effects near the r-Process Path at N=82 in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory

> Evolution of the shell structure of nuclei near the neutron drip line is investigated in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. By introducing the vector self-coupling of $ω$ meson in the RHB theory, we reproduce successfully the experimental data on the shell effects about the waiting-point nucleus $^{80}$Zn. With this basis, it is shown that the shell effects at N=82 in the inaccessible region of the r-process path remain strong. In contrast, a quenching exhibited by the HFB+SkP approach is shown to be incompatible with the available data. Consequently, the neutrino-driven mechanism of the nucleosynthesis is supported.

# Nuclear Shell Effects near the r-Process Path at N=82 in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory

## Key claim
The shell effects at N=82 in neutron-rich nuclei along the r-process path remain strong when calculated with the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory including vector self-coupling of the omega meson, contrary to the quenching predicted by the HFB+SkP approach. This conclusion depends on the RHB force NL-SV1 being able to reproduce experimental shell effects at the waiting-point nucleus 80Zn (N=50), which the authors use as a benchmark to validate their method before extrapolating to inaccessible regions.

## Method
The authors performed spherical RHB calculations using the NL-SH force (scalar self-coupling only) and the NL-SV1 and NL-SV2 forces (including both scalar and vector self-couplings of the omega meson), with finite-range Gogny D1S pairing. They expanded fermionic and bosonic wavefunctions in 20 oscillator shells and computed two-neutron separation energies (S2n) for isotopic chains of Zn, Kr, Sr, Zr, and Mo to evaluate shell gaps at N=50 and N=82. The results were compared with experimental data from Wapstra and Audi (1995) and with predictions from HFB+SkP and the Extended Thomas-Fermi with Strutinsky Integral (ETF-SI) mass formula.

## Key numbers
- The shell gap at N=50 in Zn isotopes with NL-SV1 agrees well with the experimental value, while NL-SH shows a gap much larger than experiment and HFB+SkP shows a strongly quenched gap.
- For Mo (Z=42) and Zr (Z=40) isotopes at N=82, the shell gap with NL-SV1 is significantly larger than with HFB+SkP; these nuclei are where the r-process path is assumed to pass.
- For 118Kr (N=82), the Fermi energy is close to zero and the shell gap diminishes significantly, with all forces except NL-SH showing complete washing out of shell effects.
- The binding energies of 80Zn and 82Zn from the compilation by Wapstra and Audi (1995) were used as experimental benchmarks.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Sharma, Farhan, and collaborators, 2000) on shell effects at the stability line in Ni and Sn isotopes, where they introduced vector self-coupling of the omega meson to reproduce experimental data. It directly contradicts the quenching predicted by the HFB+SkP approach (Dobaczewski, Flocard, and Treiner, 1984), which has been used to argue for shell quenching in r-process abundance calculations. The results support the neutrino-driven mechanism of nucleosynthesis proposed by Haxton (1997) and Qian (1997), suggesting that neutrino-induced reactions can break through waiting-point nuclei without requiring shell quenching. The paper also shows that the quenched version of ETF-SI (Pearson, 1996) worsens agreement with experimental data compared to the standard ETF-SI (Aboussir, 1995).
