# Renormalized Quarkonium

> We extend our previous study of the vector charmonium states within a renormalization approach with boundary conditions to the full spectrum of charmonium and bottomonium. On the light of the predicted spectrum we comply to assignments suggested in the literature. A comparison with the regularized quark model is also included.

## Key claim

The paper extends a renormalization approach with boundary conditions, previously applied only to vector charmonium, to the full charmonium and bottomonium spectra, showing that the short-range regulators in standard constituent quark models effectively only determine the ground state masses, while the excited states are predicted from orthogonality with the ground state and agree with experiment to within a few MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation for quarkonium using a constituent quark model with a screened linear confinement potential and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) spin-dependent terms (Coulomb, tensor, spin-orbit). Instead of introducing ad hoc form factors to regularize the singular \(1/r^3\) tensor and spin-orbit potentials, they impose boundary conditions at a cutoff radius \(r_c \sim 0.01\) fm, using the experimental ground state mass as the single input parameter; excited states are then determined by requiring orthogonality to the ground state. The procedure is applied to all \(J^{PC}\) channels of charmonium and bottomonium, including singlet, triplet uncoupled, and triplet coupled channels, with the number of free parameters determined by the short-distance behavior of the potential.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium \(\eta_c(2S)\): predicted mass 3634 MeV, compared to experimental 3637 ± 4 MeV
- Charmonium \(\psi(2S)\): predicted mass 3704 MeV, compared to experimental 3686.093 ± 0.034 MeV
- Charmonium \(\psi(1D)\): predicted mass 3796 MeV, compared to experimental 3775.2 ± 1.7 MeV
- Charmonium \(\chi_{c2}(2P)\) (Z(3930)): predicted mass 3974 MeV, compared to experimental 3929 ± 5 ± 2 MeV; predicted two-photon width \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}B = 0.15\) keV, compared to experimental \(0.18 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.03\) keV
- Charmonium \(\eta_{c2}(2D)\): predicted mass 4166 MeV, compared to Belle measurement 4156\(^{+25}_{-20}\) ± 15 MeV
- Bottomonium \(\eta_b(2S)\): predicted mass 9957 MeV, compared to Belle measurement 9999.0 ± 3.5\(^{+2.8}_{-1.9}\) MeV
- Bottomonium hyperfine splitting \(\Delta m_{hf}[\eta_b(1S)] = 69.6 \pm 2.9\) MeV (experimental, used as input)
- Charmonium hyperfine splitting \(\Delta m_{hf}[h_c(1P)]\): predicted compatible with zero; experimental centroid of \(1^3P_J\) states is 3525.30 ± 0.04 MeV, with CLEO measurement +0.02 ± 0.23 MeV and BES III measurement −0.10 ± 0.22 MeV

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach of Segovia (2011) from vector charmonium to the full charmonium and bottomonium spectra, building on the constituent quark model of Vijande (2005) which uses screened confinement and OGE spin-dependent terms. The method avoids the ad hoc regulators of standard potential models by treating the ground state mass as input, and shows that the excited spectrum is insensitive to the short-range regulator details. The results enable a systematic assignment of conventional quarkonium states among the XYZ mesons discovered at B-factories, and the authors identify the Z(3930) as \(\chi_{c2}(2P)\) and the X(4160) as \(\eta_{c2}(2D)\), while noting that states like X(3872), X(3915), Y(3940), and X(3940) do not appear as pure \(c\bar{c}\) states in their calculation, consistent with molecular interpretations by Ortega (2012).
