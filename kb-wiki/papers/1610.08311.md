# Overview of recent azimuthal correlation measurements from ALICE

> Azimuthal correlations are a powerful tool to probe the properties and the evolution of the collision system. In this proceedings, we will review the recent azimuthal correlation measurements from ALICE at the LHC. The comparison to other experimental measurements and various theoretical calculations will be discussed as well.

## Key claim

Azimuthal correlation measurements from ALICE provide crucial constraints on the initial conditions and transport properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, but the paper shows that current hydrodynamic models cannot simultaneously describe all measured correlations, indicating that further tuning of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio and initial conditions is necessary.

## Method

The paper reviews ALICE measurements of anisotropic flow harmonics using Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal angle distribution of final-state particles in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Flow harmonics v2, v3, v4, and v5 were measured using two-particle correlations with pseudorapidity gaps, multi-particle cumulants (2-, 4-, 6-, and 8-particle), and symmetric cumulants SC(m,n) to study correlations between different flow harmonics. Measurements were performed over a wide pseudorapidity range (-3.5 < eta < 5) and for identified particles (pions, kaons, protons) using ALICE's particle identification capabilities.

## Key numbers

- v2 increases from 0-5% to 40-50% centrality classes in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with v2{2} decreasing slightly in more peripheral collisions over the entire pseudorapidity range
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, v2 reaches a maximum value of about 0.10 for two-particle correlations and about 0.09 for multi-particle cumulants in the 40-50% centrality class
- v2 increases by (3.0 +/- 0.6)% and v3 increases by (4.3 +/- 1.4)% from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV over the centrality range 0-50%
- v4 increases by (10.2 +/- 3.8)% from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV over the centrality range 0-50%
- iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model over-predicts v2 by 10% for pions and 10-15% for kaons, while being compatible with protons within 10% at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Number of constituent quark scaling holds at the level of +/- 20% for v2, v3, v4, and v5 within current statistical and systematic uncertainties
- SC(4,2) is positive and SC(3,2) is negative for all centralities in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

## Context

This review extends the anisotropic flow program from RHIC, where measurements by PHOBOS (Back 2002, Alver 2010) established extended longitudinal scaling of v2, to the LHC energy regime. The work builds on previous ALICE measurements (Aamodt 2010, Abelev 2014) and comparisons with ATLAS (Aad 2014) and CMS (Chatrchyan 2012) results. The new symmetric cumulant measurements provide stronger constraints on the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio than individual vn measurements alone, and enable future tuning of hydrodynamic models such as iEBE-VISHNU (Xu 2016) and the EKRT framework (Niemi 2015).
