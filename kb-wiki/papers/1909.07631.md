# Production of $ϕ(2170)$ and $η(2225)$ in a kaon induced reaction

> In this work, we study the production of strange quarkoniums, the $ϕ(2170)$, also named $Y(2175)$, and the $η(2225)$, via a kaon induced reaction on a proton target in an effective Lagrangian approach. The total and differential cross sections of the reactions $K^{-}p\rightarrow ϕ(2170)Λ$ and $K^{-}p\rightarrow η(2225)Λ$ are calculated by the Reggeized $t $-channel Born term under an assumption that the $ϕ(2170)$ and $η(2225)$ are $Λ\barΛ$ molecular states. At the center of mass energies of about 4.2 GeV, the total cross section for the $ϕ(2170)$ production is predicted to be about 1 $μ$b. The numerical results indicate that it is feasible to produce the $ϕ(2170)$ via kaon beam scattering at the best energy window near 4.2 GeV. The total cross section for the $η(2225)$ production is smaller than that for the $ϕ(2170)$ production and it may reach an order of the magnitude of 0.1 $μ$b. The differential cross sections for both reactions at different center of mass energies are also presented. It is found that the Reggeized $t$ channel gives a considerable contribution at forward angles. As the energy increases, the contribution from the $t$-channel almost concentrates at extreme forward angles. From these theoretical predictions, the relevant experimental research is suggested, which could provide important information to clarify the internal structure and production mechanism of these two strange quarkoniums.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that the phi(2170) and eta(2225) are Lambda-antilambda molecular states, the total cross section for phi(2170) production via kaon-induced reaction can reach about 1 microbarn at center-of-mass energies near 4.2 GeV, making it feasible to observe at existing facilities; the eta(2225) production cross section is smaller, reaching about 0.1 microbarn.

## Method

The authors calculated the total and differential cross sections for the reactions K- p -> phi(2170) Lambda and K- p -> eta(2225) Lambda using an effective Lagrangian approach with Reggeized t-channel K and K* exchange, respectively. The coupling constants were determined from SU(3) flavor symmetry and from the partial decay widths of the two states into K K and K* K, assuming they are Lambda-antilambda molecular states as computed by Dong (2017). The only free parameter, a cutoff in the form factor, was set to 1.6 GeV based on previous fits to similar reactions.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for K- p -> phi(2170) Lambda at center-of-mass energy W = 4.2 GeV: about 1.9 microbarn (with cutoff 1.6 GeV).
* Total cross section for K- p -> eta(2225) Lambda: about one order of magnitude smaller than for phi(2170) production, reaching about 0.1 microbarn.
* Coupling constant g_{phi* K K} = 2.81, determined from a partial decay width Gamma_{phi* -> K K} of about 81 MeV.
* Coupling constant g_{eta* K* K} = 1.39, determined from a partial decay width Gamma_{eta* -> K* K} of about 79.2 MeV.
* Coupling constants for the nucleon vertices: g_{K N Lambda} = -13.24, g_{K* N Lambda} = -4.26, and kappa_{K* N Lambda} = 2.66.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of strange quarkonium production beyond the J/psi decay and e+e- collision channels where phi(2170) and eta(2225) were previously observed, proposing kaon-induced reactions as an alternative production mechanism. The calculation builds on the molecular interpretation of these states as Lambda-antilambda bound states from Dong (2017) and applies the Reggeized t-channel formalism previously used for similar reactions by Ozaki (2010) and Wang (2015). The predictions are intended to guide experimental searches at J-PARC, JLab, COMPASS at CERN, and OKA at U-70, and could be complemented by photoproduction studies at GlueX at CEBAF.
