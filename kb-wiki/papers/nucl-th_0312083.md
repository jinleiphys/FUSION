# Analyses of two and three pion Bose-Einstein Correlations using Coulomb wave functions

> Using effective formulas we analyze the Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) data corrected for Coulomb interactions provided by STAR Collaboration and the quasi-corrected data (raw data with acceptance correction etc) on 2πand 3πBEC by using Coulomb wave function with coherence parameter included. The corresponding magnitudes of the interaction regions turn out to be almost the same: R_{Coul}(2π) \simeq \frac 32R_{Coul}(3π). R_{Coul} means the size of interaction region obtained in terms of Coulomb wave function. This approximate relation is also confirmed by the core-halo model. Moreover, the genuine 3rd order term of BEC has also been investigated in this framework and its magnitude has been estimated both in the fully corrected data and in the quasi-corrected data.

## Key claim

Using Coulomb wave functions with a coherence parameter, the authors find that the interaction region sizes extracted from two-pion and three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations satisfy the approximate relation \(R_{\rm Coul}(2\pi) \simeq \frac{3}{2}R_{\rm Coul}(3\pi)\), a result also confirmed by the core-halo model; this relation holds for both Coulomb-corrected and quasi-corrected data from the STAR Collaboration, but the analysis of quasi-corrected data requires skipping the first two data points to reproduce the STAR-reported radius of about 6.3 fm.

## Method

The authors analyzed STAR Collaboration data on two-pion and three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) using two approaches: (i) a conventional method applying effective plane-wave formulas to Coulomb-corrected data, and (ii) a method using Coulomb wave functions integrated over a Gaussian source distribution applied to quasi-corrected data (raw data with acceptance corrections). For three-pion BEC, they employed a theoretical formula involving products of three Coulomb wave functions with a coherence parameter \(\lambda\), decomposing the six permutation amplitudes into groups \(F_1\), \(F_2\), and \(F_3\) representing the constant term, two-particle BEC, and genuine three-particle BEC, respectively. They also applied the core-halo model with parameters \(f_c\) (core fraction) and \(p\) (coherent fraction) to further test the relation between the two-pion and three-pion interaction radii.

## Key numbers

* For Coulomb-corrected \(2\pi\) BEC data (STAR, Ref. [star01]): \(R_{\rm eff} = 7.84 \pm 0.20\) fm, \(\lambda_2 = 0.62 \pm 0.02\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 30.8/24\) (all data points); skipping first two points gives \(R_{\rm eff} = 6.52 \pm 0.31\) fm, \(\lambda_2 = 0.43 \pm 0.03\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 8.32/22\).
* For Coulomb-corrected \(3\pi\) BEC data (STAR, Ref. [star03]): \(R_{\rm eff} = 7.83 \pm 0.26\) fm, \(\lambda_3 = 0.57 \pm 0.02\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 5.32/35\).
* For quasi-corrected \(3\pi\) BEC data analyzed with Coulomb wave functions (Eq. 7): \(R_{\rm Coul} = 5.34 \pm 0.24\) fm, \(\lambda = 0.56 \pm 0.02\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 2.80/35\).
* For quasi-corrected \(2\pi\) BEC data analyzed with Coulomb wave functions (Eq. 9): \(R_{\rm Coul} = 8.75 \pm 0.31\) fm, \(\lambda_2 = 0.58 \pm 0.02\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 23.0/25\).
* Core-halo model results for \(3\pi\) BEC at \(p=1.0\): \(f_c = 0.75 \pm 0.02\), \(R = 5.34 \pm 0.24\) fm; at \(p=0.6\): \(f_c = 0.85 \pm 0.02\), \(R = 6.48 \pm 0.32\) fm.
* Core-halo model results for \(2\pi\) BEC at \(p=1.0\): \(f_c = 0.76 \pm 0.01\), \(R = 8.75 \pm 0.31\) fm; at \(p=0.6\): \(f_c = 0.83 \pm 0.02\), \(R = 11.12 \pm 0.42\) fm.
* Analysis including a phase factor \(W = \cos(g \times Q_3)\) for quasi-corrected \(3\pi\) BEC (Eq. 16): \(R = 4.91 \pm 0.36\) fm, \(\lambda = 0.61 \pm 0.04\), \(g = 31.9 \pm 8.8\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dof} = 0.57/34\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of higher-order Bose-Einstein correlations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly following the STAR Collaboration's measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\) GeV (STAR, 2003; Wilson, 2003). The authors build on their own previous formulation using Coulomb wave functions (Mizoguchi et al., 2002) and compare with the conventional plane-wave approach used by Bowler (1991) and others. The core-halo model analysis follows the framework of Csorgo (1999) and Alt et al. (2000). The results enable future studies of the phase structure in three-pion correlations, as suggested by Heinz (1996) and Van Hove (1989), and can be applied to other data from SPS and RHIC energies.
