# Impact of octupole correlation on the inverse quasifission in ${}^{160}\text{Gd}+{}^{186}\text{W}$ collisions

> Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions offer a promising pathway to synthesize neutron-rich heavy nuclei, but the mechanism of inverse quasifission, as a key reaction channel of MNT, still remains not well understood. We employ time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory to investigate the reaction mechanism, especially the role of the octupole deformed shell in the MNT reaction of ${}^{160}\text{Gd}+{}^{186}\text{W}$. The results show that inverse quasifission occurs when the deformed projectile and target collide in near tip-tip and tip-side orientations, which favors production of neutron-rich transtarget nuclei. Interestingly, the distributions and single-particle spectra of primary products reveal that the $N=88$ octupole deformed shell in light fragments dominates inverse quasifission instead of the spherical shells of $^{208}\text{Pb}$ at a center-of-mass energy of $502.6~\text{MeV}$, thus explaining the experimental observation that the yields of the transtarget products are enhanced in the Au region. Further exploration finds that quantum shell effects in inverse quasifission exhibit energy dependence. These results demonstrate that the octupole deformed shell plays a crucial role in the inverse quasifission dynamics, significantly advancing the understanding of the MNT reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the ${}^{160}\text{Gd}+{}^{186}\text{W}$ multinucleon transfer reaction at a center-of-mass energy of 502.6 MeV, the $N=88$ octupole deformed shell in light fragments, rather than the spherical shells of $^{208}\text{Pb}$, drives inverse quasifission and explains the experimentally observed yield enhancement of transtarget products in the Au region. This claim is hedged by the energy dependence of the shell effects: at higher incident energies (700-780 MeV), the $Z=82$ spherical shell competes with the $Z=56$ octupole deformed shell.

## Method

The authors employed the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with the SLy5 Skyrme effective interaction to simulate collisions of ${}^{160}\text{Gd}+{}^{186}\text{W}$ at a center-of-mass energy of 502.6 MeV. They performed simulations for 18 distinct initial orientations of the deformed projectile and target, with projectile angles from $0^\circ$ to $180^\circ$ in $22.5^\circ$ increments and target angles of $0^\circ$ and $90^\circ$, and varied the impact parameter from 0 to 7 fm. The particle-number projection technique was applied to the TDHF wave functions to obtain primary fragment distributions and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barriers for the four extreme orientations are 422.47 MeV (tip-tip), 446.07 MeV (tip-side), 460.86 MeV (side-tip), and 486.75 MeV (side-side), calculated by the frozen Hartree-Fock method.
- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV in tip-tip central collisions, the maximum transfer is about 11 neutrons and 7 protons from ${}^{160}\text{Gd}$ to ${}^{186}\text{W}$.
- Inverse quasifission occurs in tip-tip and tip-side collisions for impact parameters $b \leq 3.5$ fm at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV.
- The primary fragment distribution peaks at ${}_{59}^{147}\text{Pr}_{88}$ for light fragments and ${}_{79}^{199}\text{Au}_{120}$ for heavy fragments at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 502.6$ MeV.
- The ground-state deformation parameters are $\beta_2 \approx 0.32$, $\gamma \approx 7.9^\circ$, and $\beta_3 \approx 0.03$ for ${}^{160}\text{Gd}$, and $\beta_2 \approx 0.24$ for ${}^{186}\text{W}$.

## Context

This work extends microscopic TDHF studies of inverse quasifission, previously applied to systems like ${}^{232}\text{Th}+{}^{250}\text{Cf}$ by Kedziora (2010), and addresses a discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements for the ${}^{160}\text{Gd}+{}^{186}\text{W}$ reaction. The theoretical model of Zagrebaev (2007, 2013) predicted enhanced yields near $^{208}\text{Pb}$ due to spherical shells, but experiments by Loveland (2011) and Kozulin (2017) found yield enhancement in the Au region instead. The stochastic mean-field calculations by Ayik (2023) reproduced the mass distribution but did not reveal the shell effects. This work demonstrates that octupole deformed shells, previously identified in asymmetric fission by Scamps (2018) and in quasifission by Godbey (2019) and McGlynn (2023), also dominate inverse quasifission dynamics, and shows that the dominant shell effects are energy dependent.
