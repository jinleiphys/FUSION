# Systematic studies of binding energy dependence of neutron - proton momentum correlation function

> Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) results of the neutron-proton correlation function have been systematically investigated for a series nuclear reactions with light projectiles with help of Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. The relationship between the binding energy per nucleon of the projectiles and the strength of the neutron-proton HBT at small relative momentum has been obtained. Results show that neutron-proton HBT results are sensitive to the binding energy per nucleon.

# Systematic studies of binding energy dependence of neutron - proton momentum correlation function

## Key claim

The strength of the neutron-proton HBT correlation function at small relative momentum (5 MeV/c) shows a nearly linear increase with the binding energy per nucleon of the projectile, for light isotopes of Li, B, C, and N at 800 MeV/n incident energy. This relationship is demonstrated in a transport-model simulation (IDQMD) rather than in experimental data, and the authors note it would be "of particular interest if one can explore experimentally."

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate head-on collisions of various light projectiles (Li, B, C, N isotopes) with a 12C target at 800 MeV/n. Initial density distributions for the projectile nuclei were taken from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations with the SKM* parameter set. The Koonin-Pratt formalism, implemented via Pratt's Correlation After Burner code, was used to construct neutron-proton correlation functions from the emission phase space at freeze-out, with emitted nucleons identified by a coalescence criterion (r_min ≤ 3.5 fm, p_min ≤ 300 MeV/c).

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton correlation function strength C_PN was evaluated at a relative momentum of **5 MeV/c** for all isotope chains.
- The incident energy for all simulated collisions was **800 MeV/n**.
- The soft potential used an incompressibility of **K = 200 MeV** and an asymmetry strength **C_sym = 32 MeV** between neutron and proton.
- Correlation functions were extracted at an evolution time of **200 fm/c**, which is later than the freeze-out time (less than **100 fm/c** for these systems at 800 MeV/n).
- The coalescence parameters for identifying emitted nucleons were **r_min ≤ 3.5 fm** in coordinate space and **p_min ≤ 300 MeV/c** in momentum space.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Wei, 2004) which reproduced halo neutron-neutron correlation functions and predicted a binding-energy dependence for Be isotopes. It systematically tests the same relationship across four isotope chains (Li, B, C, N) using the IDQMD model, complementing earlier integral measurements such as total reaction cross sections (Shen, 1989; Ma, 1993; Ozawa, 2001) that are only sensitive to overall nuclear size. The results suggest that neutron-proton HBT interferometry could serve as a probe of nuclear compactness and binding energy, potentially enabling experimental studies of nuclear structure for unstable nuclei where traditional methods are difficult.
