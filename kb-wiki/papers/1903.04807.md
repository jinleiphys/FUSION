# Gauge ambiguity of the quark spectrum in the Color Glass Condensate

> In the Color Glass Condensate, the inclusive spectrum of produced quarks in a heavy ion collision is obtained as the Fourier transform of a $2$-fermion correlation function. Due to its non-locality, the two points of this function must be linked by a Wilson line in order to have a gauge invariant result, but when the quark spectrum is evaluated in a background that has a non-zero chromo-magnetic field, this procedure suffers from an ambiguity related to the choice of the contour defining the Wilson line. In this paper, we use an analytically tractable toy model of the background field in order to study this contour dependence. We show that for a straight contour, unphysical contributions to the spectrum in $p_\perp^{-2}$ and $p_\perp^{-3}$ cancel, leading to a spectrum with a tail in $p_\perp^{-4}$. If the contour defining the Wilson line deviates from a straight line, the path dependence is at most of order $p_\perp^{-5}$ if its curvature is bounded, and of order $p_\perp^{-4}$ otherwise. When the contour is forced to go through a fixed point, the path dependence is even larger, of order $p_\perp^{-2}$.

## Key claim
The inclusive quark spectrum in the Color Glass Condensate, when evaluated in a background with non-zero chromo-magnetic field, acquires a gauge-fixing ambiguity from the choice of contour defining the Wilson line that links the two fermion fields; the authors show that a straight contour cancels unphysical power-law contributions, while curved contours introduce path dependence at various orders in transverse momentum.

## Method
The authors construct an analytically tractable toy model using an SU(2) gauge group with homogeneous color fields before the collision, achieved by choosing Wilson lines with linear arguments in the transverse coordinates. They compute the inclusive quark spectrum at proper time τ=0⁺ by evaluating the inner product between free positive-energy spinors and dressed negative-energy spinors that have evolved through the background field, then insert a Wilson line between the two spinor points to restore gauge invariance. The asymptotic expansion of the spectrum at large transverse momentum is obtained by expanding the Wilson line in powers of the separation between the two points, and the non-Abelian Stokes theorem is used to relate curved-path Wilson lines to the straight-path reference.

## Key numbers
- For a straight Wilson line, unphysical contributions to the spectrum at orders p⊥⁻² and p⊥⁻³ cancel, leading to a spectrum with a tail in p⊥⁻⁴.
- If the contour deviates from a straight line, the path dependence is at most of order p⊥⁻⁵ when the curvature is bounded, and of order p⊥⁻⁴ otherwise.
- When the contour is forced to go through a fixed point, the path dependence is of order p⊥⁻².
- The leading p⊥⁻⁴ term for a straight Wilson line is given by an expression involving Q₁²Q₂², (Q₁·Q₂)², and dot products with the unit transverse momentum vector, with numerical coefficients such as 27, 12, 8, 4, and 2 (from the explicit formula in the paper).
- The quark spectrum without a Wilson line has a leading term proportional to Q₁²/p⊥² and Q₂²/p⊥².

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework for quark production in heavy ion collisions, specifically addressing the gauge-invariant definition of the inclusive quark spectrum when the background field at the evaluation time is not a pure gauge. The authors build on the classical Yang-Mills description of the collision (Kovner 1995, Blaizot 2008) and the Dirac equation approach to quark production (Gelis 2015, Tanji 2017). The toy model uses a configuration of color sources introduced by Tanji (2018) to enable semi-analytic calculations. The results enable systematic treatment of gauge ambiguities in quark spectra from CGC calculations, particularly for future numerical studies where the background field has non-zero chromo-magnetic components.
