# Comprehensive analysis of the $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$, $γn \to K^+ Σ^-(1385)$, and $π^+ p \to K^+ Σ^+(1385)$ reactions

> A comprehensive analysis of all available data on cross sections and spin-dependent observables for the $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$, $γn \to K^+ Σ^-(1385)$, and $π^+ p \to K^+ Σ^+(1385)$ reactions is performed within an effective Lagrangian framework. In addition to the $s$-channel $N$ exchange, $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges, $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Σ^\ast$ exchanges, and the interaction current, the $s$-channel contributions from a minimal set of $N$ and $Δ$ resonances are included in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the data. The analysis reveals that all available data for both photon- and hadron-induced $KΣ(1385)$ production reactions can be simultaneously and satisfactorily reproduced when the contribution of the $s$-channel $Δ(1930){5/2}^-$ resonance is taken into account. The reaction mechanisms, particularly the resonance contributions in each considered reaction, are thoroughly analyzed and discussed in detail.

## Key claim
All available data for both photon- and hadron-induced KΣ(1385) production reactions can be simultaneously and satisfactorily reproduced only when the s-channel Δ(1930)5/2⁻ resonance contribution is included, ruling out other N and Δ resonance candidates that individually described subsets of the data in prior analyses.

## Method
The authors performed a combined analysis within an effective Lagrangian framework, constructing reaction amplitudes from s-channel N exchange, t-channel K and K* exchanges, u-channel Λ, Σ, and Σ* exchanges, an interaction current, and s-channel contributions from a minimal set of N and Δ resonances. They systematically tested all eight Δ resonances and the N(1895)1/2⁻ resonance rated three-star or four-star in the Review of Particle Physics within the energy region below W=2200 MeV, fitting the hadronic and electromagnetic coupling constants as free parameters to simultaneously describe differential cross sections and spin-dependent observables for all three reactions.

## Key numbers
- The Δ(1930)5/2⁻ resonance was the only resonance capable of simultaneously describing all data; fitted parameters include g^{(1)}_{Δ*Nγ}=0.0±0.2, g^{(2)}_{Δ*Nγ}=-7.90±0.6, g^{(1)}_{Δ*Σ*K}=-2.22±0.2, and g^{(2)}_{Δ*Σ*K}=-7.71±0.3
- Cutoff parameters: Λ_t=860±3 MeV, Λ_u=1132±3 MeV, Λ_s=878±4 MeV
- The coupling constant g_{Σ*NK}=-3.22 was fixed by SU(3) flavor symmetry using g_{ΔNπ}=2.23
- The coupling constant g_{γK±K*±}=0.413 was determined from the K*(892)→Kγ radiative decay width
- The Σ(1385)→Λγ decay width of 0.45 MeV constrained the electromagnetic coupling constants g^{(1)}_{Σ*Λγ} and g^{(2)}_{Σ*Λγ}, with their ratio fitted as -0.57±0.2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of γp→K+Σ0(1385) (Wang 2020) and γn→K+Σ-(1385) (Wang 2022) by performing a self-consistent combined analysis where hadronic coupling constants, cutoff parameters, and electromagnetic couplings for isospin-3/2 resonances are uniquely determined across all three reactions. The inclusion of π+p→K+Σ+(1385) differential cross-section data from Hanson (1972) provides additional constraints that were absent in the prior separate analyses. The results demonstrate that the Δ(1930)5/2⁻ resonance is essential for describing all available KΣ(1385) production data, enabling more reliable extraction of resonance properties and providing constraints for quark model predictions of missing N* and Δ* states.
