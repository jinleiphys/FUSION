# Multipion correlations induced by isospin conservation of coherent emission

> Recent measurements have revealed a significant suppression of multipion Bose-Einstein correlations in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The suppression may be explained by postulating coherent pion emission. Typically, the suppression of Bose-Einstein correlations due to coherence is taken into account with the coherent state formalism in quantum optics. However, since charged pion correlations are most often measured, the additional constraint of isospin conservation, which is absent in quantum optics, needs to be taken into account. As a consequence, correlations emerge between pions of opposite charge. A calculation of the correlations induced by isospin conservation of coherent emission is made for two, three- and four-pion correlation functions and compared to the data from the LHC.

## Key claim

The suppression of multipion Bose-Einstein correlations observed at the LHC may be explained by coherent pion emission, but the additional constraint of isospin conservation (absent in standard quantum optics) induces correlations between pions of opposite charge that must be accounted for when comparing calculations to charged-pion data.

## Method

The author derived expressions for three- and four-pion correlation functions by extending the coherent state formalism of quantum optics with a super-selection rule for isospin conservation, averaging over all orientations of the isospin vector. The coherent fraction \(G(Q_n)\) was parametrized as a Gaussian function of the relative momentum \(Q_n\), with parameters extracted from five types of same-charge correlation functions measured by ALICE. Calculations of mixed-charge cumulant correlation functions were then compared to LHC data for \(\pi^-\pi^-\pi^+\), \(\pi^-\pi^-\pi^-\pi^+\), and \(\pi^-\pi^-\pi^+\pi^+\) combinations, considering two extreme assumptions for the coherent source radius (\(R_{\rm coh}=R_{\rm ch}\) and \(R_{\rm coh}=0\)).

## Key numbers

* Coherent fraction Gaussian parameters from \(C_3^{\rm QS}\): \(\alpha = 71\) (74 for \(R_{\rm coh}=0\)), \(\beta = 20\) (27 for \(R_{\rm coh}=0\)) in units of (GeV/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)
* Coherent fraction Gaussian parameters from \(c_3^{\rm QS}\): \(\alpha = 49\) (46), \(\beta = 16\) (23)
* Coherent fraction Gaussian parameters from \(C_4^{\rm QS}\): \(\alpha = 37\) (29), \(\beta = 6\) (11)
* Coherent fraction Gaussian parameters from \(b_4^{\rm QS}\): \(\alpha = 59\) (59), \(\beta = 20\) (24)
* Chaotic source radius \(R_{\rm ch} = 10.5\) fm, with Edgeworth coefficients \(\kappa_3 = 0.14\) and \(\kappa_4 = 0.29\) for \(0.2 < K_{\rm T2} < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\)
* Default correlated fraction parameter \(f_c = 0.837\) with a variation of 0.03 in the ALICE analysis
* Estimated required adjustments: increase \(f_c\) by 10% and decrease the FSI cumulant \((K_2-1)\) by 30% to bring data in line with isospin calculations

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Biyajima (1998) and Akkelin (2001) for two-pion correlations with isospin conservation to three- and four-pion correlations, and compares to the ALICE measurement of Adam (2015) which found a 6\(\sigma\) suppression of four-pion Bose-Einstein correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The results suggest that an overcorrection of final-state interactions in the experimental analysis may explain the negative values observed in \(\pi^-\pi^-\pi^+\pi^+\) cumulant correlations, and that future measurements with reduced systematic uncertainties could test whether isospin conservation of coherent emission is indeed responsible.
