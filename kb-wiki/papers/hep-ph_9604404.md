# Propagation of Cool Pions

> For an exact chiral symmetry which is spontaneously broken at zero temperature, we show that at nonzero temperature, generally pions travel at {\it less} than the speed of light. This effect first appears at next to leading order in an expansion about low temperature. When the chiral symmetry is approximate we obtain two formulas, like that of Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner, for the static and dynamic pion masses.

# Propagation of Cool Pions

## Key claim

For an exact chiral symmetry that is spontaneously broken at zero temperature, pions at nonzero temperature travel at less than the speed of light, with this effect first appearing at next-to-leading order in a low-temperature expansion. When chiral symmetry is approximate, the paper derives two formulas analogous to the Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner relation for both the static and dynamic pion masses, with the condition that the pion velocity is less than or equal to the speed of light.

## Method

The authors use chiral Ward identities of QCD to relate the pion decay constants (timelike and spacelike) to the pion dispersion relation at nonzero temperature. They perform explicit one-loop calculations in a linear sigma model to extract the real and imaginary parts of the two distinct pion decay constants, and from these compute the pion velocity, damping rate, and mass. The analysis is restricted to "cool" pions where momentum components are small relative to the decay constants.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant at zero temperature is given as f_pi ~ 93 MeV.
- To leading order in low temperature, f_pi^t(T) = f_pi^s(T) = (1 - T^2/(12 f_pi^2)) f_pi, so pions move at the speed of light and are undamped at this order.
- At next-to-leading order in the linear sigma model, the pion velocity squared is v^2 ~ 1 - 8 t_2, where t_2 = (pi^2/45) * (T^4/(f_pi^2 m_sigma^2)).
- For T = 150 MeV, the authors cite Schenk's result that v ~ 0.87.
- The damping rate gamma ~ p * t_3, where t_3 = (1/(32 pi)) * (m_sigma^4/(f_pi^2 p^2)) * exp(-m_sigma^2/(4 p T)).
- The dynamic pion mass at nonzero temperature is m_pi^2(T) ~ m_pi^2 * (1 + t_1/2 - 6 t_2), with t_1 = T^2/(12 f_pi^2).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of pion propagation in a thermal medium, building on the Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (1968) relation and the chiral Ward identity approach of Shore and Veneziano (1992). The results generalize previous calculations by Itoyama and Mueller (1983) in the linear sigma model and by Schenk (1991, 1993) in the nonlinear sigma model, showing that the flattening of the pion dispersion relation found in many models arises on very general grounds from chiral symmetry breaking in a medium. The paper also notes exact analogies with spin waves in antiferromagnets, as discussed by Leutwyler (1994), and suggests that the deviation of the pion velocity squared from unity may be proportional to the free energy density for all temperatures.
