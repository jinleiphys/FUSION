# Axial anomaly and the interplay of quark loops with pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the gamma* --> pi+ pi0 pi- process

> Motivated by the ongoing measurements of the Primakoff process pi- gamma* --> pi- pi0 by COMPASS collaboration at CERN, the transition form factor for the canonical anomalous process gamma* --> pi+ pi0 pi- is calculated in a constituent quark loop model. The simplest contribution to this process is the quark "box" amplitude. In the present paper we also explicitly include the vector meson degrees of freedom, i.e., the rho and the omega, thus giving rise to additional, resonant contributions. We find that in order to satisfy the axial anomaly result, a further subtraction in the resonant part is needed. The results are then compared with the vector meson dominance model as well as the Dyson--Schwinger calculations, the chiral perturbation theory result, and the available data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including vector mesons (rho and omega) in a constituent quark loop model for the gamma* → π+ π0 π- process produces a total amplitude 5/2 times larger than the correct axial anomaly result in the chiral and soft-point limit, requiring a subtraction in the resonant part to restore consistency with the anomaly.

## Method

The authors calculate the transition form factor for gamma* → π+ π0 π- using a constituent quark loop model that includes both the quark "box" amplitude and additional resonant contributions from rho and omega vector mesons via a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD2) approach. They compute the contributions from resonant triangle diagrams and box diagrams separately, then evaluate the total amplitude in the chiral and soft-point limits using the Goldberger-Treiman relation and the Kawarabayashi-Suzuki-Fayyazuddin-Riazuddin (KSFR) relation. They compare their results with the axial anomaly prediction, the vector meson dominance model, Dyson-Schwinger calculations, chiral perturbation theory, and available experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The axial anomaly prediction for the amplitude in the chiral and soft-point limit is A_γ^(3π) = (9.72 ± 0.09) GeV^(-3), derived from the formula e N_c / (12 π^2 f_π^3) with f_π = (92.42 ± 0.33) MeV.
* The only published experimental value for the form factor at finite momenta is F_γ^(3π)(expt) = 12.9 ± 0.9 ± 0.5 GeV^(-3), extracted from the Serpukhov Primakoff experiment.
* In the soft-point and chiral limit, the resonant triangle contribution alone gives 3/2 times the correct anomalous amplitude, and the box contribution gives 1 times the correct anomalous amplitude, yielding a total of 5/2 times the correct value.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark loop study of Bistrovic (1999) by including vector meson degrees of freedom, and addresses the same problem that Rudaz (1974) encountered in pure VMD approaches to the gamma* → 3π process, where the amplitude was too large by a factor of 3/2. The paper connects to Dyson-Schwinger approach calculations by Cotanch (2003) and Maris and Tandy (1999), which show that vector meson contributions vanish in the soft limit when treated at the quark substructure level, unlike the naive CQL-VMD model. The results enable future comparisons with COMPASS collaboration measurements of the Primakoff process π- γ* → π- π0 at CERN.
