# Quark Matter in the Chiral Color Dielectric Model

> We study the equation of state (EOS) of quark matter at zero temperature, using the Color Dielectric Model (CDM) to describe confinement. Sensible results are obtained in the version of the CDM for which confinement is imposed {\it smoothly}. The two--phases version of the model turns out to give unrealistic results for the EOS. Chiral symmetry plays a marginal rôle and the quarks are massive till high densities. The deconfinement phase transition is smooth and unlikely to be first order. Instabilities of the quark matter and the gap equation are discussed.

## Key claim

The Color Dielectric Model (CDM) produces a sensible equation of state for quark matter only when confinement is imposed smoothly (the Single Minimum version with p=1); the Double Minimum version and the Single Minimum with p=2 give unrealistically low energy per baryon, and the deconfinement phase transition is smooth and unlikely to be first order.

## Method

The authors solved the mean-field equations for homogeneous infinite quark matter using the chiral CDM Lagrangian, treating quarks as plane waves with constant scalar fields (chi, sigma, and pion). They fixed model parameters by reproducing the average nucleon-delta mass and isoscalar radius of the nucleon after performing double projection on linear and angular momentum eigenstates from the hedgehog ansatz. The equation of state was computed from the total energy density including the kinetic energy of quarks and the potential energies of the chi and sigma fields, and instabilities were studied via the compressibility and the RPA polarization propagator.

## Key numbers

* The SM (p=1) parameters g=0.02 GeV and M=1.7 GeV reproduce (E_N+E_Delta)/2 = 1.112 GeV (experimental 1.085 GeV) and isoscalar radius 0.82 fm (experimental 0.79 fm).
* The SM (p=2) parameters g=0.02 GeV and M=1.10 GeV give (E_N+E_Delta)/2 = 1.102 GeV and isoscalar radius 0.78 fm.
* The equilibrium density of nuclear matter is rho_eq = 0.17 N/fm^3.
* Quark matter becomes unstable at densities below approximately rho_eq/2.
* At a density of about 3 rho_eq, the effective quark mass is reduced from its value at rho_eq by a factor of approximately 0.8.
* The energy difference between nuclear matter and quark matter in the range rho_eq to 2 rho_eq is of the order of 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the deconfinement phase transition using the CDM (Mitia et al.) and uses the same model parameters previously fixed for nucleon structure functions (Fiolhais et al.). The results contrast with the MIT bag model (Rafelski, Giacopuzzi), which predicts a first-order deconfinement transition coinciding with chiral restoration; here the transition is smooth and chiral symmetry is restored only at much higher densities. The findings enable studies of neutron star cooling via the URCA mechanism, supernova explosion energetics, and the EMC effect, and are consistent with lattice indications (Blaizot) that non-perturbative effects persist above the critical temperature.
