# Three-body hadron systems with strangeness

> Recently, many efforts are being put in studying three-hadron systems made of mesons and baryons and interesting results are being found. In this talk, I summarize the main features of the formalism used to study such three hadron systems with strangeness $S=-1,0$ within a framework built on the basis of unitary chiral theories and solution of the Faddeev equations. In particular, I present the results obtained for the $π\bar{K}N$, $K\bar{K}N$ and $KK\bar{K}$ systems and their respective coupled channels. In the first case, we find four $Σ$'s and two $Λ$'s with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$, in the 1500-1800 MeV region, as two meson-one baryon s-wave resonances. In the second case, a $1/2^+$ $N^*$ around 1900 MeV is found. For the last one a kaon close to 1420 MeV is formed, which can be identified with K(1460).

# Three-body hadron systems with strangeness

## Key claim
The authors claim that several low-lying hyperon and nucleon resonances with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$ in the 1500-1900 MeV region can be dynamically generated as three-hadron $s$-wave resonances from two-meson-one-baryon systems, but note that the results depend on using on-shell two-body $t$ matrices and neglecting three-body forces from chiral Lagrangians, which is justified only when the off-shell contributions are found to be small compared to the on-shell part.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev equations for three-hadron systems using two-body $t$ matrices obtained from unitary chiral dynamics via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in its on-shell factorization form. They exploit a cancellation between the off-shell part of the two-body amplitudes and three-body contact terms from chiral Lagrangians, allowing the use of on-shell two-body $t$ matrices as input. The resulting amplitudes are projected onto isospin basis and searched for peaks in the squared amplitude to identify resonances.

## Key numbers
- Four $\Sigma$ resonances found in the $\pi\overline{K}N$ system: $\Sigma(1590)$ with width 70 MeV, $\Sigma(1630)$ with width 39 MeV, $\Sigma(1656)$ with width 30 MeV, and $\Sigma(1790)$ with width 2 MeV.
- Two $\Lambda$ resonances found in the $\pi\overline{K}N$ system: $\Lambda(1568)$ with width 60 MeV and $\Lambda(1700)$ with width 136 MeV, plus $\Lambda(1740)$ with width 20 MeV.
- A $N^*$ resonance with $J^P=1/2^+$ found in the $K\overline{K}N$ system at $\sqrt{s}\sim1922$ MeV, appearing when the $\overline{K}N$ subsystem has invariant mass near 1428 MeV and the $K\overline{K}$ subsystem has invariant mass near 987 MeV.
- A kaonic state found in the $KK\overline{K}$ system at $\sqrt{s}\sim1420$ MeV with width $\sim50$ MeV from the Faddeev calculation, and a quasibound state with 21 MeV binding energy and 110 MeV width from a potential model.
- The root mean squared radius of the $KK\overline{K}$ quasibound state is 1.6 fm; the average $K$-$K$ distance is 2.8 fm and the distance between the $KK$ cluster and $\overline{K}$ is 1.7 fm.

## Context
This work extends the well-studied two-body chiral unitary approach (Oller et al., Jido et al., Nieves et al., Borasoy et al.) to three-body systems, building on prior Faddeev equation studies of the $K\overline{K}N$ system by Jido et al. (2010) and the $KK\overline{K}$ system by Jido et al. (2010). The results suggest that many poorly understood $1/2^+$ hyperon resonances in the 1500-1800 MeV region, such as $\Sigma(1560)$, $\Sigma(1620)$, $\Sigma(1660)$, $\Sigma(1770)$, $\Lambda(1600)$, and $\Lambda(1810)$, may have significant three-hadron molecular components. The predicted $N^*$ near 1920 MeV could correspond to a peak observed in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ reactions (SAPHIR, Jefferson Lab, Mizuki), and the kaonic state near 1420 MeV may be identified with the $K(1460)$ listed by the Particle Data Group. Future work should check the contribution of higher-order terms in the chiral Lagrangians to the three-body calculation.
