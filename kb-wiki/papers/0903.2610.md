# Quantum Monte Carlo calculation of the equation of state of neutron matter

> We calculate the equation of state of neutron matter at zero temperature by means of the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method (AFDMC) combined with a fixed-phase approximation. The calculation of the energy is carried out by simulating up to 114 neutrons in a periodic box. Special attention was made to reduce finite size effects at the energy evaluation by adding to the interaction the effect due to the truncation of the simulation box, and by performing several simulations using different number of neutrons. The finite size effects due to the kinetic energy were also checked by employing the twist--averaged boundary conditions. We considered a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian containing modern two-- and three--body interactions of the Argonne and Urbana family. The equation of state can be used to compare and to calibrate other many-body calculations and to predict properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper presents an accurate equation of state for neutron matter at zero temperature using the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method with a fixed-phase approximation, but the results depend on the specific choice of two- and three-nucleon interactions (Argonne and Urbana family) and the fixed-phase approximation does not guarantee an upper bound on the energy.

## Method

The authors simulated up to 114 neutrons in a periodic box using the AFDMC method, which employs a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to linearize quadratic spin and isospin operators, reducing the computational cost from exponential to linear in the number of nucleons. They used the Argonne \(v_8^\prime\) potential in the propagator and added the difference between Argonne \(v_{18}\) and \(v_8^\prime\) via mixed expectation values, along with the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. Finite-size effects were reduced by adding corrections for the truncation of the simulation box, performing simulations with different neutron numbers, and applying twist-averaged boundary conditions to check kinetic energy finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- Simulations were performed with up to 114 neutrons in a periodic box.
- The nuclear saturation density is given as 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The Argonne \(v_8^\prime\) potential is more attractive than Argonne \(v_{18}\) in light nuclei by about 0.5 MeV per nucleon, and in neutron drops the difference is about 0.06 MeV per neutron.
- The healing distance \(d\) for the Jastrow function was set to 2 fm or \(L/2\) if \(L/2 < 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous AFDMC calculations of nuclei (Gandolfi 2007) and addresses limitations of other many-body techniques such as Fermi Hyper Netted Chain (FHNC) with the Single Operator Chain (SOC) approximation (Pandharipande 1979) and Skyrme effective forces (Brown 2000, Stone 2003). The computed equation of state can serve as a benchmark for calibrating other many-body calculations and for predicting properties of neutron stars, where densities range from a small fraction up to several times nuclear saturation density.
