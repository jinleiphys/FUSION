# Different seniority states of $^{119-126}$Sn isotopes: shell model description

> In the present work available experimental data up to high-spin states of $^{119-126}$Sn isotopes with different seniority ($v$), including $v$ = 4, 5, 6, and 7 have been interpreted with shell model, by performing full-fledged shell model calculations in the 50-82 valence shell composed of $1g_{7/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$, $1h_{11/2}$, $3s_{1/2}$, and $2d_{3/2}$ orbitals. The results have been compared with the available experimental data. These states are described in terms of broken neutron pairs occupying the $h_{11/2}$ orbital. Possible configurations of seniority isomers in these nuclei are discussed. The breaking of three neutron pairs have been responsible for generating high-spin states. The isomeric states $5^-$, $7^-$, $10^+$ and $15^-$ of even Sn isotopes, and isomeric states $19/2^+$, $23/2^+$, $27/2^-$ and $35/2^+$ of odd Sn isotopes, are described in terms of different seniority. For even-Sn isotopes, the isomeric states $5^-$, $7^-$, and $10^+$ are due to seniority $v$ = 2; the isomeric state $15^-$ is due to seniority $v$ = 4, and in the case of odd-Sn isotopes, the isomeric states $19/2^+$, $23/2^+$, and $27/2^-$ are due to seniority $v$ = 3, and the isomeric state $35/2^+$ in $^{123}$Sn is due to seniority $v$ = 5. These are maximally-aligned spin, which involve successive pair breakings in the $ν(h_{11/2})$ orbit.

# Different seniority states of $^{119-126}$Sn isotopes: shell model description

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that high-spin isomeric states in $^{119-126}$Sn isotopes can be described by shell model calculations using the SN100PN interaction in the full 50-82 valence space, with the interpretation that these states arise from successive breaking of neutron pairs occupying the $\nu h_{11/2}$ orbital. The authors claim that the seniority assignments proposed by Astier et al. (2012) are confirmed, but note that the wave functions are highly fragmented, with the largest configuration component reaching only 10-37% depending on the isotope and state.

## Method

Full-fledged shell model calculations were performed without truncation in the 50-82 valence shell comprising the $1g_{7/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$, $1h_{11/2}$, $3s_{1/2}$, and $2d_{3/2}$ orbitals, using the SN100PN interaction derived from the CD-Bonn G-matrix with the $\hat{Q}$-box folded-diagram method. The neutron-neutron part of the interaction was multiplied by a factor of 0.90 to improve agreement with experiment for $^{130}$Sn. Calculations were performed with the code Antoine, and results were compared with experimental data from Astier et al. (2012), Iskra et al. (2014), and Iskra et al. (2015) for even $^{120,122,124,126}$Sn and odd $^{119,121,123,125}$Sn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for neutrons: -10.609 MeV ($1g_{7/2}$), -10.289 MeV ($2d_{5/2}$), -8.717 MeV ($2d_{3/2}$), -8.694 MeV ($3s_{1/2}$), and -8.816 MeV ($1h_{11/2}$)
- For $^{120}$Sn: calculated $2^+$ level is 67 keV lower than experiment; $5^-$ and $7^-$ levels are 118 keV and 95 keV higher than experiment, respectively; calculated $9^-$ level is 537 keV lower than experiment
- For $^{122}$Sn: $2^+$ level predicted 46 keV lower and $4^+$ level 26 keV higher than experiment; $5^-$ and $7^-$ levels are 85 and 73 keV higher than experiment
- For $^{124}$Sn: $2^+$ and $4^+$ levels are 35 keV and 15 keV lower than experiment; experimental energy gaps between $4^+$ and $6^+$ are 352 keV (calculated: 339 keV)
- For $^{126}$Sn: $2^+$ and $4^+$ levels are 17 keV and 38 keV lower than experiment; gaps between $4^+$ and $6^+$ are 324 keV (experiment) and 333 keV (calculation)
- For $^{119}$Sn: calculated $11/2^-$ is ground state, while experiment places it at 89 keV excitation; calculated $27/2^-$ is 285 keV lower than experiment
- For $^{121}$Sn: calculated $11/2^-$ is ground state, experiment places it at 6 keV; calculated $27/2^-$ is 116 keV lower than experiment
- For $^{123}$Sn: calculated $15/2^-_1$ is 111 keV lower, $19/2^-_1$ is 252 keV lower, $27/2^-$ is 98 keV lower than experiment; $13/2^-$ and $19/2^-_2$ are only 1 keV higher than experiment
- For $^{125}$Sn: calculated $15/2^-$ is 94 keV lower, $27/2^-$ is 108 keV lower, $39/2^-_1$ is only 57 keV higher than experiment
- Average occupancy of $h_{11/2}$ orbital: 5.17 in $^{119}$Sn, 7.79 in $^{125}$Sn
- Largest configuration probability for $10^+$ state: 10.13% in $^{120}$Sn, increasing to 37.24% in $^{126}$Sn
- Largest configuration probability for $27/2^-$ state: 13.80% in $^{119}$Sn, increasing to 34.55% in $^{125}$Sn
- $B(E2; 10^+ \rightarrow 8^+)$ in $^{126}$Sn: 10.23 $e^2fm^4$ (with $e_n=0.5e$)
- $B(E2; 27/2^- \rightarrow 23/2^-)$ in $^{119}$Sn: experimental 73(7) $e^2fm^4$, calculated 21.09 $e^2fm^4$ (with $e_n=0.5e$)
- $B(E2; 23/2^+ \rightarrow 19/2^+)$ in $^{119}$Sn: experimental 6.7(8) $e^2fm^4$, calculated 59.12 $e^2fm^4$ (with $e_n=0.5e$)

## Context

This work extends the shell model interpretation of seniority isomers in Sn isotopes previously studied experimentally by Astier et al. (2012), Fotiades et al. (2001), and Iskra et al. (2014, 2015). The calculations use the SN100PN interaction developed by Brown et al. (2005), which the authors have previously applied to structural properties of nuclei in this region (Srivastava et al., various years). The paper provides more complete shell model results than the selected states reported by Iskra et al. (2014, 2015), and presents calculated $B(E2)$ transition probabilities that the authors state will be useful for future experiments. The linear decrease in $\sqrt{B(E2)}$ values for $10^+ \rightarrow 8^+$ and $27/2^- \rightarrow 23/2^-$ transitions as a function of mass number is used to determine the filling of the $\nu h_{11/2}$ orbital, following the formalism of McNeill et al. (1989).
