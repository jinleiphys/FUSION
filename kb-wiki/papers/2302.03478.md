# Universality Classes of Relativistic Fluid Dynamics: Foundations

> A general organizing principle is proposed that can be used to derive the equations of motion describing the near-equilibrium dynamics of causal and thermodynamically stable relativistic systems. The latter are found to display some new type of universal behavior near equilibrium that allows them to be grouped into universality classes defined by their degrees of freedom, information content, and conservation laws. The universality classes expose a number of surprising equivalences between different theories, shedding new light on the near-equilibrium behavior of relativistic systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that any causal and thermodynamically stable relativistic fluid theory, when linearized around homogeneous equilibrium, can be grouped into universality classes defined solely by the degrees of freedom, information content, and conservation laws, with the caveat that this equivalence holds only in the linear regime and that some transport coefficients may be constrained to zero in a specific theory while being nonzero in the most general theory of the class.

## Method

The authors prove a theorem showing that for any linear system of first-order partial differential equations describing perturbations about equilibrium, if an information current exists that is future-directed timelike and satisfies the second law, the equations can be rewritten in symmetric hyperbolic form using the information current and entropy production rate. They then prove a second theorem establishing that two such theories are equivalent if and only if there exists an invertible change of variables mapping the information current and entropy production rate of one theory into those of the other. Finally, they show that any such theory can be rearranged into an Israel-Stewart representation, where the fields are decomposed into conserved quantities and dissipative fields, and further classified by their transformation properties under the SO(3) rotation group.

## Key numbers

* The paper defines universality classes by two lists of integers: (g0, g1, g2, ...) for the geometric character of degrees of freedom and (g0_bar, g1_bar, g2_bar, ...) for the conservation laws, where gn_bar ≤ gn for each n.
* A perfect fluid at finite chemical potential is of class (2,1)-(2,1) and does not dissipate.
* A bulk-viscous fluid at zero chemical potential is of class (2,1)-(1,1) and dissipates.
* The theory is non-dissipative if and only if gn_bar = gn for all n.

## Context

This work extends the known result that second-order viscous hydrodynamic theories from different approaches (Israel-Stewart 1979, Baier 2008, Denicol 2012) are mathematically equivalent near equilibrium, as previously shown by Liu (1986), Priou (1991), and Gavassino (2022). The paper provides a rigorous foundation for this phenomenon by proving that all causal and thermodynamically stable relativistic theories linearized about equilibrium can be classified by their degrees of freedom and conservation laws. This enables practitioners to determine stability and causality conditions for an entire class by analyzing a single representative theory, and reveals unexpected equivalences such as a fluid mixture undergoing a chemical reaction being indistinguishable from Israel-Stewart bulk viscosity theory near equilibrium.
