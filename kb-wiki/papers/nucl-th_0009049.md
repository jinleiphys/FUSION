# The source of elliptic flow and initial conditions for hydrodynamical calculations

## Key claim

The authors present a model for the initial energy, pressure, and flow velocity distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions, showing that the quark-gluon plasma forms a tilted disk whose largest pressure gradient lies in the reaction plane but deviates from both the beam axis and the usual transverse flow direction. This initial condition may produce an "antiflow" or "third flow component," but the model relies on effective parameters (string tension, bag constant) that must be fixed by comparison with experimental data, and the homogeneous final streak assumption is the simplest choice that may be modified.

## Method

The authors construct a streak-by-streak collision model in the center-of-rapidity frame, treating each nucleus-nucleus collision as independent collisions of streaks at the same transverse coordinates. They use an effective abelian Yang-Mills field to describe chromoelectric flux tubes (string ropes) between projectile and target streaks, with the string tension parameterized as σ = A (ε₀/m)² ρ₀ √(l₁ l₂) where A ≈ 0.05–0.06. The model solves conservation equations for baryon current and energy-momentum tensor analytically in light-cone variables, then stops the oscillatory "yo-yo" motion at the point where each streak reaches the final rapidity y_f, assuming string-string interactions and decays produce a single homogeneous final streak.

## Key numbers

- String tension σ takes typical values of 8–15 GeV/fm for beam energy ε₀ = 100 GeV/nucleon, compared to standard string tensions of 1 GeV/fm.
- The free parameter A in the string tension parameterization has typical values around 0.05–0.06.
- The model is applied to Au+Au collisions at ε₀ = 100 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 0.5(R₁+R₂).
- The final rapidity y_f is determined from conservation laws using the ratio M = (l₂+l₁)/(l₂−l₁), where l₁ and l₂ are initial streak lengths.

## Context

This work extends the coherent Yang-Mills field model of Glendenning and Csernai (1986) by strictly enforcing energy and momentum conservation (which the earlier work neglected by assuming infinite projectile energy) and by generalizing it to two heavy ions. The model contradicts earlier expectations based on standard string tensions of 1 GeV/fm that led to Bjorken-type initial states; instead, collective effects in massive heavy ion collisions produce effective string tensions of order 10 GeV/fm and much less transparency. The resulting tilted disk initial condition is proposed as the source of the "antiflow" or "third flow component" predicted by Csernai and Röhlich (1999), and the authors call for detailed fluid dynamical calculations and flow experiments at SPS and RHIC energies to connect this initial state with observables.
