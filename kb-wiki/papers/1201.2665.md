# Local P Violation Effects and Thermalization in QCD: Views from Quantum Field Theory and Holography

> We argue that the local violation of P and CP invariance in heavy ion collisions and the universal thermal aspects observed in high energy collisions are in fact two sides of the same coin, and both are related to quantum anomalies of QCD. We argue that the low energy relations representing the quantum anomalies of QCD are saturated by coherent low dimensional vacuum configurations as observed in Monte Carlo lattice studies. The thermal spectrum and approximate universality of the temperature with no dependence on energy of colliding particles in this framework is due to the fact that the emission results from the distortion of these low dimensional vacuum sheets rather than from the colliding particles themselves. The emergence of the long- range correlations of P odd domains (a feature which is apparently required for explanation of the asymmetry observed at RHIC and LHC) is also a result of the same distortion of the QCD vacuum configurations. We formulate the corresponding physics using the effective low energy effective Lagrangian. We also formulate the same physics in terms of the dual holographic picture when low-dimensional sheets of topological charge embedded in 4d space, as observed in Monte Carlo simulations, are identified with D2 branes. Finally, we argue that study of these long range correlations in heavy ion collisions could serve as a perfect test of a proposal that the observed dark energy in present epoch is a result of a tiny deviation of the QCD vacuum energy in expanding universe from its conventional value in Minkowski spacetime.

## Key claim

Local P and CP violation in heavy ion collisions and the universal thermal hadronization temperature are both consequences of the same QCD vacuum dynamics, specifically the saturation of quantum anomaly relations by coherent low-dimensional vacuum configurations; the long-range correlations of P-odd domains required to explain the observed asymmetry at RHIC and LHC arise from the distortion of these vacuum sheets rather than from the colliding particles themselves.

## Method

The paper constructs a low-energy effective Lagrangian for gluodynamics that reproduces the low-energy theorems for scalar (gluon condensate) and pseudoscalar (topological charge density) correlation functions, including all multi-point functions. The effective Lagrangian is formulated in terms of a complex field combining a dilaton field η and an axion field a, where the axion field encodes the dynamics of the topological charge density and exhibits 2π periodicity that leads to a discrete set of degenerate vacuum states. The same physics is then reformulated in a dual holographic picture where low-dimensional sheets of topological charge observed in Monte Carlo simulations are identified with D2 branes.

## Key numbers

- The universal hadronization temperature is TH ∼ (150–200) MeV, observed across e+e− annihilation, pp, p¯p, and heavy ion collisions from a few GeV to TeV energies.
- The topological susceptibility in Euclidean space is χEucl = (1/Nc²) |Evac(θ)| > 0, where Nc is the number of colors.
- The vacuum energy density is given by 4Evac ≡ ⟨σ⟩ = ⟨(−bαs)/(8π) G²⟩, with b = (11/3) Nc for the one-loop β-function.
- The effective potential minimum yields Fmin(η=0) = −|Evac|, with the axion field minimum at amin = (θ + 2πl)/Nc.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Zhitnitsky (2010) that local P violation and thermalization share a common origin, but now provides a microscopic picture in terms of specific gauge configurations rather than an effective acceleration parameter. The paper connects the charge separation effect (chiral magnetic effect) of Kharzeev (2004, 2007) with the universal hadronization temperature of Hagedorn and the conformal anomaly approach of Castorina (2007). The analysis is supported by lattice results from Horvath (2005), Ilgenfritz (2007–2008), and Bruckmann (2011), and by the "deformed QCD" model of Yaffe (2008) where computations are under weak-coupling control. The work enables future tests of a proposal linking QCD vacuum energy to dark energy in an expanding universe.
