# Machine learning the single-$Λ$ hypernuclei with neural-network quantum states

> Single-$Λ$ hypernuclei are the most straightforward extension of atomic nuclei. A thorough description of baryonic system beyond first-generation quark sector is indispensable for the maturation of nuclear $ab$ $initio$ methods. This study pioneers the application of neural-network quantum states to hypernuclei, with trainable parameters determined by variational Monte Carlo approach (VMC-NQS). In order to reduce the numerical uncertainty and treat the nucleons and hyperons in a unified manner, spinor grouping (SG) method is proposed to analytically integrate out isospin degrees of freedom. A novel spin purification scheme is developed to address the severe spin contamination occurring in standard energy minimization due to the weakly bound characteristic of light single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. The energy spectrum of $s$-shell hypernuclei is computed with one-thousandth level accuracy and benchmarked against existing stochastic variational results, showing superior performance. By comparing two different sets of Hamiltonian based on pionless effective field theory (pionless EFT), we choose an optimal model and further carry out calculations of selected $p$-shell charge-symmetric hypernuclei with mass number up to 13, exhibiting satisfactory consistency with experimental results. Our findings underscore the potential of VMC-NQS family in approaching exact solution of few-body systems and the accuracy of pionless EFT in modeling hypernuclei. This is crucial for understanding hyperon-nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon-nucleon interactions, providing a powerful tool for precisely predicting the properties of multi-strangeness hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neural-network quantum states (NQS) with variational Monte Carlo (VMC-NQS) can match or exceed the accuracy of the stochastic variational method (SVM) for single-Λ hypernuclei, but only when augmented with the proposed spinor grouping (SG) method and spin purification scheme; without these additions, standard VMC-NQS fails to improve upon SVM for the ground energy of ⁵ΛHe.

## Method

The authors employ a Slater-Jastrow ansatz enhanced by a message-passing neural-network (MPNN) backflow transformation, with parameters optimized via variational Monte Carlo with stochastic reconfiguration. They introduce the spinor grouping (SG) method to analytically integrate out isospin degrees of freedom, reducing statistical noise, and develop a spin purification scheme using spin projection operators for A≤4 systems and a penalty method for heavier nuclei. The Hamiltonian uses leading-order pionless effective field theory (πEFT) with ΛN and ΛNN contact interactions, with two model variants: model "s" using an s-shell-specific nuclear force and model "o" using a nuclear force calibrated for nuclei up to mass 40.

## Context

This work extends the VMC-NQS approach, previously applied to normal nuclei by Yang (2022) and the FeynmanNet group, to single-Λ hypernuclei for the first time. It directly benchmarks against SVM results from PhysRevC.106.L031001, which had resolved the ⁵ΛHe overbinding problem using πEFT. The paper also compares with Faddeev calculations by Cobis (1997), cluster model work by Hiyama (2012), and recent NLEFT and NCSM approaches. The results enable more precise determination of ΛNN interaction low-energy constants and provide a tool for predicting multi-strangeness hypernuclei properties, with the authors noting that a very recent preprint by Di Donna (2025) using standard VMC-NQS failed to improve upon SVM for ⁵ΛHe, highlighting the necessity of the SG method.
