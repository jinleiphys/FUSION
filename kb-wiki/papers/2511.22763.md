# Saturation effects in exclusive vector meson production in DIS

> We investigate saturation effects in exclusive vector meson production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS), where we model fluctuations within the target protons as localized color-charge hotspots. Based on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and the dipole picture for vector meson production, we examine the dependencies of coherent and incoherent scattering cross sections on the momentum transfer. We draw conclusions on the effectiveness of our hot spot model and the strength of the suppression of the scattering cross sections caused by saturation effects. We find that saturation has mild effects in the given energy and charge-density ranges, but can also show that suppression becomes more prominent as the color-charge density inside the proton increases.

## Key claim

Saturation effects in exclusive vector meson production are mild at the energy and color-charge density ranges studied, but the suppression of scattering cross sections becomes more prominent as the color-charge density inside the proton increases.

## Method

The authors model the proton target as a set of localized color-charge hotspots, using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory and the dipole picture for vector meson production. They compute coherent and incoherent scattering cross sections for J/psi and Upsilon production in both the dense (full Wilson line) and dilute (leading-twist) limits, averaging over color-charge configurations analytically and over hotspot positions via Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

*   The best-fit value for the dimensionless combination of the gluon coupling and color-charge density is \(g^2\mu_0^2 = 6.574\), determined from comparison with HERA H1 data.
*   The charm quark mass is fixed to \(m_c = 1.275\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and the bottom quark mass to \(m_b = 4.18\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
*   The photon virtuality is set to \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
*   The hotspot radius is \(r_h = 0.165\ \mathrm{fm}\), the proton radius is \(R = 0.358\ \mathrm{fm}\), and the number of hotspots per proton is \(N_h = 3\), yielding a coherent radius \(R_C = 0.336\ \mathrm{fm}\).
*   The IR regulator in the Green's function is \(m = 0.22\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
*   The decay width normalization constant for charmed states is \(A_c = 0.211\ \mathrm{GeV}^{3/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the hotspot model approach of Albacete (2016) and Mantysaari (2016) by incorporating full CGC multiple-scattering effects beyond the leading-twist approximation previously studied by Demirci (2021, 2022). The framework enables systematic exploration of how high gluon densities affect both coherent and incoherent cross sections, and provides a benchmark for interpreting future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
