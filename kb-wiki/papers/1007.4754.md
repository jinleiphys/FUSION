# Entropy development in ideal relativistic fluid dynamics with the Bag Model equation of state

> We consider an idealized situation where the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is described by a perfect, 3+1 dimensional fluid dynamic model starting from an initial state and expanding until a final state where freeze-out and/or hadronization takes place. We study the entropy production with attention to effects of (i) numerical viscosity, (ii) late stages of flow where the Bag Constant and the partonic pressure are becoming similar, (iii) and the consequences of final freeze-out and constituent quark matter formation.

## Key claim

The entropy production in ideal relativistic fluid dynamics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma is dominated by numerical viscosity (yielding a 5-6% increase) rather than physical processes, and the late-stage behavior depends critically on assumptions about non-mechanical energy transfer between the Bag field and the parton gas when the total pressure drops to zero.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional perfect fluid dynamical simulations using the Particle in Cell (PIC) method with the MIT Bag Model equation of state, applied to Au + Au central collisions at 65+65 A·GeV. They decomposed the total energy density into parton gas and Bag field components, and examined three different assumptions for energy exchange between these components after the total pressure reaches zero: adiabatic expansion of the parton gas (dS_p = 0), constant Bag energy density (e_B = B), and constant parton gas energy (E_p = const). The entropy evolution was tracked as a function of time.

## Key numbers

- The numerical viscosity causes a weak entropy increase of 5-6% in the perfect fluid simulation, with the increase being smaller when computational cell size is smaller.
- The Bag constant used in the calculations is B = 0.396 GeV/fm³.
- The computational cell size for the main simulation is dx = dy = dz = 0.575 fm.
- Zero pressure fluid cells first appear after approximately 3-4 fm/c of evolution time.
- The interaction measure from the Bag Model shows relatively good agreement with lattice QCD calculations above a temperature of 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fluid dynamical approach of Csernai et al. (2010, 2009) and Bravina et al. (1998) by scrutinizing entropy production in the late stages of QGP expansion, where the Bag constant and partonic pressure become comparable. The entropy increase from numerical viscosity (5-6%) is compared to the 6-24% increase obtained in lower-dimensional models with physical viscosity (SHB, 2008). The study enables a more detailed description of the Bag field break-up and subsequent hadronization, potentially connecting to Quarkyonic matter as proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), and to Constituent Quark Scaling observed by Adams (2004) and Adare (2007).
