# Dense QCD : Overhauser or BCS Pairing ?

> We discuss the Overhauser effect (particle-hole pairing) versus the BCS effect (particle-particle or hole-hole pairing) in QCD at large quark density. In weak coupling and to leading logarithm accuracy, the pairing energies can be estimated exactly. For a small number of colors, the BCS effect overtakes the Overhauser effect, while for a large number of colors the opposite takes place, in agreement with a recent renormalization group argument. In strong coupling with large pairing energies, the Overhauser effect may be dominant for any number of colors, suggesting that QCD may crystallize into an insulator at a few times nuclear matter density, a situation reminiscent of dense Skyrmions. The Overhauser effect is dominant in QCD in 1+1 dimensions, although susceptible to quantum effects. It is sensitive to temperature in all dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in dense QCD, the Overhauser effect (particle-hole pairing) can dominate over BCS pairing (particle-particle or hole-hole pairing) for a large number of colors in weak coupling, and may dominate for any number of colors in strong coupling, suggesting QCD could crystallize into an insulator at a few times nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors construct a Wilsonian effective action around the Fermi surface by integrating out quark modes in the presence of smooth bilocal fields, then Fierz-rearrange the quark-gluon interaction to isolate the Overhauser (particle-hole) and BCS (particle-particle) channels. They derive gap equations for both pairings in the one-loop approximation with screened gluon propagators, solving them to leading logarithm accuracy using one-dimensional reduction of the integral equations.

## Key numbers

- Overhauser gap (unscreened, large N_c): F_0 ~ 2μ exp{-π/(√2 h)} where h² = (g²N_c/8π²)(1-1/N_c)
- BCS gap (unscreened, large N_c): G_0 ~ 2μ exp{-π/(2√2 h_*)} where h_*² = (g²/8π²)(2/N_c)[min(N_f,N_c)-1]/2
- Energy densities in weak coupling: S_Σ/V_4 ≈ -μ F_0³ (Overhauser) and S_Γ/V_4 ≈ -μ² G_0² (BCS)
- BCS phase energetically favored up to N_c ~ 10 in unscreened case for one standing wave with M_C Fierzing
- With total nesting of Fermi surface, Overhauser energy density becomes S_Σ/V_4 ≈ -μ² F_0², making BCS and Overhauser comparable for N_c ~ 4
- Screened BCS gap: G_0 ≈ (4Λ_⊥⁶/πm_E⁵) exp{-√3π/(2h_*)}

## Context

This work extends the early variational study by Deryagin, Grigoriev and Rubakov (1992) for large N_c and the renormalization group argument by Son (1999), providing a unified framework to compare Overhauser and BCS pairing in QCD at high density. The analysis generalizes previous BCS calculations by Bailin and Love, Alford, Rajagopal and Wilczek, and Pisarski and Rischke to include the particle-hole channel, and enables future studies of crystalline color superconducting phases at densities relevant to compact stars and relativistic heavy ion collisions.
