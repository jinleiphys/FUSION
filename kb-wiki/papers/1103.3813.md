# Electron capture rates in a plasma

> A new general expression is derived for nuclear electron capture rates within dense plasmas. Its qualitative nature leads us to question some widely accepted assumptions about how to calculate the effects of the plasma on the rates. A perturbative evaluation, though not directly applicable to the strongly interacting case, appears to bear out these suspicions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new general expression for nuclear electron capture rates in dense plasmas and argues that widely used "ionic energy shift" corrections to the effective Q-value are not supported by perturbative calculations, even though such corrections are commonly applied in the literature for strongly coupled or degenerate plasmas.

## Method

The author derives a formal expression for the electron capture rate using linear response theory and thermal averages of field operators in the Heisenberg picture, treating the weak interaction to second order. A perturbative evaluation is then carried out for the weakly coupled case, retaining terms of relative order \(e^2 Z \kappa_D/T\) (the "Salpeter" order), using an expansion of the time-ordered exponentials and the infrared part of the Coulomb potential-potential correlation function. The calculation is performed for fixed chemical potential, with a subsequent correction to fixed electron density.

## Key numbers

- The Salpeter screening factor \(\exp(-e^2 Z \kappa_D/T) \approx (1 - e^2 Z \kappa_D/T)\) gives a 16% rate reduction for electron capture on \(^7\)Be in the solar core.
- In the perturbative result, the rate kernel is given by \(M(t) = M_0(t) - e e_a \kappa_D \beta (M_0(t) - M_A(t))\), where \(M_0\) and \(M_A\) are integrals over the Fermi distribution.
- For supernova infall conditions, screening corrections range from a 25% decrease in rates at density \(10^{10}~\text{g cm}^{-3}\) and temperature \(0.75~\text{MeV}\) (plasma parameter \(\Gamma \approx 1\)) to a factor of two at density \(10^{12}~\text{g cm}^{-3}\) and temperature \(1.5~\text{MeV}\) (\(\Gamma \approx 3\)).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of electron capture rates beyond the weak-screening Salpeter factor (Salpeter, 1954) and the approaches of Iben (1978), Bahcall and collaborators, and Brown and Sawyer (1997), which are not applicable to cases with strong plasma coupling or small/negative Q-values. The paper directly contradicts the "ionic energy correction" procedure used by Gutierrez (1996), Itoh (2002), Bravo (2005), and Langanke (2003) for O-Ne-Mg stellar core collapse, showing that such corrections do not appear in the perturbative result. The author suggests that for strongly coupled plasmas (e.g., neutron star accretion), the unknown plasma effects could be large and strongly temperature-dependent, making current rate estimates unreliable.
