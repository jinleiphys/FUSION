# The Quantum Liquid of Alpha Clusters

> Within the variational approach of Bose liquids we analyze the g.s. energy of charge neutral alpha matter at $T$=0. As a prerequisite for such calculation we take from the literature or propose new $\alp-\alp$ potentials that are particularly suitable for this task, i.e. posses a repulsive core and/or reproduce the low energy scattering data and the resonance properties of the $\alp-\alp$ system. The alpha matter EOS is then obtained with the HNC method using Pandharipande-Bethe correlation derived variationally in the lowest order expansion of the energy functional or a simple gaussian function with a healing range determined by the normalization of the radial distribution function in the lowest order. We show that saturation is achieved only via repulsive and shallow potentials that are not consistent with the scattering and resonance constraints.

## Key claim

The authors show that alpha matter (a Bose liquid of alpha particles) does not saturate at physically reasonable densities when using alpha-alpha potentials that reproduce low-energy scattering data and the first resonant state in 8Be, contradicting the expectation that such potentials should yield a stable equation of state.

## Method

The ground-state energy of charge-neutral alpha matter at zero temperature was computed using the variational approach for Bose liquids, employing the Hypernetted Chain (HNC) approximation with either Pandharipande-Bethe or Gaussian two-body correlation functions. Several alpha-alpha potentials were tested: the phenomenological Ali-Bodmer (AB) and Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley (BFW) potentials, double-folded potentials derived from Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (D1, D1S, D1N), and supersymmetric (SUSY) transformations of the deep potentials that remove Pauli-forbidden bound states while preserving phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* The Ali-Bodmer potential has a short-range repulsive part with range 1.43 fm and a long-range attractive part with range 2.50 fm.
* The BFW potential has depth V0 = -122.6225 MeV and range μ = 0.469 fm^{-1}.
* The first two bound states in the BFW potential are located at -72.62 MeV and -25.61 MeV (obtained via Runge-Kutta-Nyström integration).
* The minimum of the shallow BFW-SUSY potential is at 2.85 fm with a depth of -7.56 MeV.
* The first resonant state in 8Be has energy E_res = 92.12 ± 0.05 keV and width Γ0 = 6.8 ± 1.7 eV.
* The alpha particle root-mean-square radius is 1.58 ± 0.002 fm.
* The saturation density of normal nuclear matter is ρ_NM = 4ρ_α ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends earlier alpha-matter calculations by Clark (1980) using the paired-phonon analysis and HNC method, which showed that hard-core potentials saturate near nuclear matter density while the soft-core Ali-Bodmer potential almost fails to saturate. The authors previously argued (Misicu and Carstoiu, 2009) that very shallow potentials with strong repulsive cores are needed to avoid collapse. The present paper demonstrates that potentials constrained by scattering data and the 8Be resonance (including supersymmetric partners of deep potentials as proposed by Baye, 1987) still lead to collapsing equations of state, indicating that the Pandharipande-Bethe correlation prescription itself is inadequate for alpha matter.
