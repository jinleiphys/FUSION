# Secondary Charmonium Production at LHC Energy

> We consider the production of charmonium by $D\bar D$ annihilation during the mixed and hadronic phase of Pb-Pb collision at LHC energy. The calculations for secondary $J/ψ$ and $ψ^,$ production are performed within a kinetic model taking into account the space-time evolution of a longitudinally and transversely expanding medium. It is shown that the yield of secondary $J/ψ$ mesons depends strongly on the $J/ψ$ dissociation cross section with co-moving hadrons. Within the most likely scenario for the dissociation cross section it will be negligible. The secondary production of $ψ^,$ mesons, however, due to their large cross section above the threshold, can substantially exceed the primary yield.

## Key claim

The yield of secondary \(J/\psi\) mesons from \(D\bar D\) annihilation in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy depends strongly on the \(J/\psi\) dissociation cross section with co-moving hadrons, and within the most likely scenario (short distance QCD approach) it will be negligible; however, secondary \(\psi'\) production can substantially exceed the primary yield due to its large geometric cross section of 10 mb.

## Method

The authors used a kinetic model incorporating the space-time evolution of a longitudinally and transversely expanding medium, assuming an initial quark-gluon plasma at temperature \(T_0 \sim 1\) GeV and time \(\tau_0 \sim 0.1\) fm, undergoing isentropic expansion. The rate of charmonium production from \(D\bar D\) annihilation was calculated via detailed balance from the hadronic absorption cross section, using four different models for the \(J/\psi\)-pion dissociation cross section and a constant geometric cross section of 10 mb for \(\psi'\) absorption. The initial number of \(c\bar c\) pairs was obtained by scaling PYTHIA \(p-p\) calculations with the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions, yielding 50 \(c\bar c\) pairs per unit rapidity.

## Key numbers

- 50 \(c\bar c\) pairs per unit rapidity at midrapidity in Au-Au collisions at LHC, leading to about 50 \(D\) and \(\bar D\) mesons and 0.5 primary \(J/\psi\).
- Initial temperature \(T_0 \sim 1\) GeV and initial time \(\tau_0 \sim 0.1\) fm for the quark-gluon plasma.
- Critical temperature \(T_c \sim 0.17\) GeV used in the numerical calculation, with chemical freezeout temperature in the range \(0.16 < T_f < 0.17\) GeV.
- \(\psi'\) absorption cross section taken as energy independent and equal to 10 mb (its geometric value at threshold).
- Secondary yield of \(\psi'\) reaches almost 1/5 of the initial number of primary \(J/\psi\).
- Thermal \(c\bar c\) pairs increase secondary charmonium yield by 40% with equilibrium initial conditions and by 20% with initial conditions from SSPC for a plasma out of chemical equilibrium.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Matsui and Satz (1986) on charmonium suppression in a quark-gluon plasma and by subsequent authors (e.g., the comover model and effective Lagrangian calculations) on secondary charmonium production via \(D\bar D\) annihilation. It provides a quantitative kinetic treatment for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy, showing that while secondary \(J/\psi\) production is negligible under the most realistic cross section models, \(\psi'\) regeneration can be large enough to be observable. This enables future experimental searches for \(\psi'\) at LHC and clarifies that \(J/\psi\) suppression may be complete, making the \(\psi'\) a more promising probe of the hadronic phase.
