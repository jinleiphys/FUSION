# Ab initio calculation of the contact operator contribution in the standard mechanism for neutrinoless double beta decay

> Starting from chiral nuclear interactions, we evaluate the contribution of the leading-order contact transition operator to the nuclear matrix element (NME) of neutrinoless double-beta decay, assuming a light Majorana neutrino-exchange mechanism. The corresponding low-energy constant (LEC) is determined by fitting the transition amplitude of the $nn\to ppe^-e^-$ process to a recently proposed synthetic datum. We examine the dependence of the amplitude on similarity renormalization group (SRG) scale and chiral expansion order of the nuclear interaction, finding that both dependences can be compensated to a large extent by readjusting the LEC. We evaluate the contribution of both the leading-order contact operator and standard long-range operator to the neutrinoless double-beta decays in the light nuclei $^{6,8}$He and the candidate nucleus $^{48}$Ca. Our results provide the first clear demonstration that the contact term enhances the NME by 43(7)% in $^{48}$Ca, where the uncertainty is propagated from the synthetic datum.

# Ab initio calculation of the contact operator contribution in the standard mechanism for neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim
The leading-order contact transition operator enhances the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) by 43(7)% in 48Ca, where the uncertainty is propagated from a synthetic datum for the nn → ppe−e− transition amplitude rather than from direct experimental measurement. The enhancement is robust against changes in similarity renormalization group (SRG) scale and chiral expansion order when the low-energy constant (LEC) is readjusted.

## Method
The authors compute the nn → ppe−e− transition amplitude in the 1S0 channel using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and determine the LEC of the leading-order contact operator by fitting to a synthetic datum from Cirigliano et al. (2020, 2021). They evaluate the long-range neutrino potentials (Fermi and Gamow-Teller parts) and the short-range contact operator using R-matrix scattering wavefunctions, then apply the resulting operator in importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) and in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM) calculations for finite nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The synthetic datum for the nn → ppe−e− transition amplitude at kinematic point p = 25 MeV/c, p' = 30 MeV/c is −0.0195(5) per MeV squared.
- The contact term enhances the NME by 43(7)% in 48Ca, yielding a total NME of 0.875(40) from IM-GCM calculations.
- For the ΔT=0 transition in 6He, the contact operator increases the NME by 11% to 17% depending on the interaction.
- For the ΔT=2 transition in 8He, the contact increases the NME by 92% to 172%.
- The LEC g for the EM interaction at infinite SRG scale is 0.597 with uncertainty 0.083.
- The LEC g for the ΔN2LOGO(394) interaction at infinite SRG scale is 0.566 with uncertainty 0.080.

## Context
This work extends the ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs by Yao et al. (2020) and Belley et al. (2021), which previously included only the standard long-range transition operator. It implements the contact operator formalism proposed by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019) to ensure renormalizability of the chiral EFT description, using the synthetic datum from Cirigliano et al. (2020, 2021) to fix the LEC. The results enable future ab initio calculations of heavier candidate nuclei with the contact contribution included, and the separate short- and long-range amplitudes provided allow matching to improved synthetic data from future lattice QCD calculations.
