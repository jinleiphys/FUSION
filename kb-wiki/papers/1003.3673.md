# Penetrating intrinsic charm: evidence in data

> Nuclei are transparent for a heavy intrinsic charm (IC) component of the beam hadrons, what leads to an enhanced nuclear dependence of open charm production at large Feynman x_F. Indeed, such an effect was observed recently in the SELEX experiment. Our calculations reproduce well the data, providing strong support for the presence of IC in hadrons in amount less than 1%. Moreover, we performed an analysis of nuclear effects in J/Psi production and found a similar, although weaker effect

## Key claim

The anomalous rise of the nuclear ratio for charmed hadron production at large Feynman \(x_F\) observed in the SELEX experiment can be explained by the presence of an intrinsic charm (IC) component in the projectile hadron, provided the IC probability is less than 1%. The IC component does not attenuate in nuclear matter, so it is filtered out and relatively enhanced by the nucleus.

## Method

The authors calculate the effective atomic number \(A_{eff}(x_F)\) for conventional light-quark fragmentation using a modified Glauber expansion with a suppression factor \(S(x_F)=1-x_F\) and an effective absorption cross section \(\sigma_{abs}\) evaluated from the dipole approach. They then add an IC contribution weighted by a parameter \(\delta\) (fitted to \(\delta=0.8\)) and modulated by a factor \(x_F^m\) where \(m = \frac{3}{5}n\) and \(n\) is the exponent controlling the \((1-x_F)^n\) behavior at \(x_F\to1\) for each beam type. For \(J/\Psi\) production, they fit the data at \(0.3<x_F<0.7\) with a combination of quark annihilation and \(\bar cc\) mechanisms, then add an IC term proportional to \(x_F^{3n^{\bar cc}_\psi}\).

## Key numbers

* For copper at \(p_{lab}=300\ \text{GeV}\), the effective absorption cross sections are \(\sigma^{p}_{abs}=20.4\ \text{mb}\), \(\sigma^{\pi}_{abs}=13.1\ \text{mb}\), and \(\sigma^{\Sigma^-}_{abs}=18.8\ \text{mb}\).
* The exponent \(n\) controlling the asymptotic \((1-x_F)^n\) behavior at \(x_F\to1\) is \(n_\pi=4\) for pion beams, \(n_p=5.5\) for proton beams, and \(n_\Sigma=6.5\) for \(\Sigma^-\) beams.
* The IC contribution parameter \(\delta=0.8\) provides a fair description of the SELEX data within the range \(\delta=0.5-0.9\).
* The IC probability \(P_{IC}\) is estimated to be in the range \(0.0002-0.008\) depending on whether the IC is of perturbative origin (\(\sigma_{IC}\sim 1\ \text{mb}\), \(P_{IC}\sim 0.0009-0.008\)) or nonperturbative origin (\(\sigma_{IC}\sim 5\ \text{mb}\), \(P_{IC}\sim 0.0002-0.002\)).
* For \(J/\Psi\) production, the endpoint exponents are \(n_\psi^{q}=4\) for \(q\bar q\) annihilation and \(n_\psi^{\bar cc}=5\) for the \(\bar cc\) mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear suppression in forward particle production (Kopeliovich et al., 2005) to the case of open charm, where the SELEX experiment (2009) observed an anomalous rise in the nuclear ratio at large \(x_F\) contrary to the usual suppression seen for light hadrons. The authors interpret this as evidence for intrinsic charm, a concept previously studied by Brodsky et al. (1980s) and others, and estimate the IC probability to be much smaller than the earlier evaluation by Vogt (1995) based on EMC measurements. The analysis enables future experiments to search for IC signals in charmonium production and provides a framework for distinguishing perturbative and nonperturbative origins of intrinsic heavy quarks.
