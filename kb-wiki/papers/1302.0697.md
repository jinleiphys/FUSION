# Hydrodynamic gradient expansion in gauge theory plasmas

> We utilize the fluid-gravity duality to investigate the large order behavior of hydrodynamic gradient expansion of the dynamics of a gauge theory plasma system. This corresponds to the inclusion of dissipative terms and transport coefficients of very high order. Using the dual gravity description, we calculate numerically the form of the stress tensor for a boost-invariant flow in a hydrodynamic expansion up to terms with 240 derivatives. We observe a factorial growth of gradient contributions at large orders, which indicates a zero radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic series. Furthermore, we identify the leading singularity in the Borel transform of the hydrodynamic energy density with the lowest nonhydrodynamic excitation corresponding to a `nonhydrodynamic' quasinormal mode on the gravity side.

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic gradient expansion for boost-invariant flow in strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory is an asymptotic series with zero radius of convergence, and the leading singularity in its Borel transform corresponds to the lowest nonhydrodynamic quasinormal mode on the gravity side.

## Method
The authors used the fluid-gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) to compute the stress tensor of a boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow in a strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) super Yang-Mills plasma at large \(N_c\) and strong coupling. They solved Einstein's equations with a negative cosmological constant iteratively in a gradient expansion in \(\tau^{-2/3}\), using spectral methods to reconstruct the energy density up to order 240 (i.e., up to the term \(\epsilon_{242}\) in the series). The large-order coefficients were then analyzed via factorial growth and Borel transform with Padé approximants.

## Key numbers
- The hydrodynamic series for the energy density was computed up to 240 derivatives (order \(\epsilon_{242}\)).
- The frequency of the lowest nonhydrodynamic quasinormal mode extracted from the Borel transform is \(\omega_{\mathrm{Borel}} = 3.1193 - 2.7471\,i\) (with magnitude \(|\omega_{\mathrm{Borel}}| = 4.1565\)), matching the gravity calculation \(\omega_{qnm} = 3.1195 - 2.7467\,i\).
- The powerlike preexponential factor from the Borel transform is \(\alpha_{\mathrm{Borel}} = -1.5426 + 0.5192\,i\), matching the gravity result \(\alpha_{qnm} = -1.5422 + 0.5199\,i\).
- The radius of convergence of the Borel transformed series is estimated to be \(6.37\) (in units of the expansion variable \(u \equiv \tau^{-2/3}\)), corresponding to \(2/3 \times 6.37 = 4.25\) for the quasinormal frequency.

## Context
This work extends the study of hydrodynamic gradient expansions beyond the first few orders, which had previously been limited by computational complexity. It builds on the fluid-gravity duality framework of Bhattacharyya (2008) and the Bjorken flow analysis of Janik (2005). The results show that the hydrodynamic series is asymptotic, analogous to perturbative expansions in quantum field theories where factorial growth arises from instanton contributions (Suslov, 2005). The identification of nonhydrodynamic quasinormal modes from the Borel transform enables future resummation of the hydrodynamic series, potentially refining criteria for the applicability of hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions as explored by Heller (2011, 2012) and Lublinsky (2007, 2009).
