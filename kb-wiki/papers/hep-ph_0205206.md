# Nuclear Data in Oklo and Time-Variability of Fundamental Coupling Constants

> We re-examined Shlyakhter's analysis of the Sm data in Oklo. With a special care of minimizing contamination due to the inflow of the isotope after the end of the reactor activity, we confirmed that his result on the time-variability of the fine-structure constant, $|\dotα/α|\lsim 10^{-17}{\rm y}^{-1}$, was basically correct. In addition to this upper bound, however, we obtained another result that indicates a different value of $α$ 2 billion years ago. We add comments on the recent result from QSO's.

## Key claim

The authors confirm Shlyakhter's earlier upper bound on the time-variability of the fine-structure constant, but also obtain a second, nonzero result that indicates a different value of alpha 2 billion years ago, with the caveat that contamination from isotope inflow after the reactor ended may affect the interpretation.

## Method

The authors analyzed five deep-underground samples from the Oklo natural reactor site to minimize contamination from post-reactor isotope migration. They set up and solved differential equations for the evolution of the system of U-235, Sm-147, Sm-148, and Sm-149, determining the effective neutron-capture cross section for the reaction n + Sm-149 -> Sm-150 + gamma. Using improved temperature estimates of 200-400 degrees Celsius, they compared the observed effective cross section to calculated values as a function of resonance energy shift to extract possible changes in the fine-structure constant.

## Key numbers

- Effective cross section for n + Sm-149 -> Sm-150 + gamma: (91 +/- 6) kb (kilobarns)
- Resonance energy of the reaction: E_r = 97.3 meV (millielectronvolts)
- Temperature estimate for the reactor zone: T = (200-400) degrees Celsius
- Right-branch result for resonance energy shift: Delta E_r = 9 +/- 11 meV, corresponding to Delta alpha/alpha = -(0.8 +/- 1.0) x 10^-8 and dot-alpha/alpha = (0.4 +/- 0.5) x 10^-17 y^-1
- Left-branch result for resonance energy shift: Delta E_r = -97 +/- 8 meV, corresponding to Delta alpha/alpha = (0.88 +/- 0.07) x 10^-7 and dot-alpha/alpha = -(0.44 +/- 0.04) x 10^-16 y^-1
- With 4% contamination assumed, Delta E_r becomes 2 +/- 12 meV, bringing Delta alpha = 0 inside the 1-sigma allowed range
- Damour and Dyson's estimate for the conversion factor between resonance energy shift and alpha change: M_c approximately -1.1 MeV

## Context

This work re-examines Shlyakhter's (1976) original analysis of Oklo samarium data, which had provided the most stringent upper bound on time variation of the fine-structure constant. The authors extend the analysis of Damour and Dyson (1996) by using cleaner samples and improved temperature estimates, obtaining both a null result consistent with Shlyakhter and a second nonzero solution. The results are compared with Webb et al.'s (2001) QSO absorption-line studies, which found Delta alpha/alpha = (-0.78 +/- 0.18) x 10^-5 at larger look-back times, suggesting the possibility of non-monotonic time dependence that could be explored in unified theories.
