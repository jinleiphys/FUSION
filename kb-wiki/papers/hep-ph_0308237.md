# The Pion Velocity at Chiral Restoration and the Vector Manifestation

> We study the effects of Lorentz non-invariance on the physical pion velocity at the critical temperature $T_c$ in an effective theory of hidden local symmetry (HLS) with the "vector manifestation" fixed point. We match at a "matching scale" $Λ_M$ the axial-vector current correlator in the HLS with the one in the operator product expansion for QCD, and present the matching condition to determine the bare pion velocity. We find that the physical pion velocity, which is found to be one at $T=T_c$ when starting from the Lorentz invariant bare HLS, remains close to one with the Lorentz non-invariance, $v_π(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99$. This result is quite similar to the pion velocity in dense matter.

## Key claim

The physical pion velocity at the critical temperature \(T_c\) remains close to the speed of light even when Lorentz non-invariance is included in the bare hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian, with a value \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\), rather than exactly one as found when starting from a Lorentz-invariant bare HLS.

## Method

The authors constructed a Lorentz non-invariant HLS Lagrangian with separate temporal and spatial decay constants for the pion and sigma fields. They performed a Wilsonian matching at a scale \(\Lambda_M\) between the axial-vector current correlator derived from this HLS Lagrangian and the correlator from the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD, including twist-2 operators. The bare pion velocity was extracted from the matching conditions for the longitudinal part of the correlator, and a non-renormalization theorem was used to argue that this bare velocity equals the physical velocity at the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* The physical pion velocity at the critical temperature is \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\).
* The deviation from the speed of light at low temperature is given by \(\delta_{\rm bare} \equiv 1 - V_{\pi,{\rm bare}}^2 = \frac{1}{G_0} \frac{32}{105}\pi^4\frac{T^6}{\Lambda_M^6} A_4^{\pi (u+d)}\), where the intrinsic temperature dependence starts at order \(T^6\).
* The matching scale is taken as \(\Lambda_M = 1.1\) GeV, based on the success of Wilsonian matching at zero temperature.
* The bare pion velocity satisfies \(V_{\pi,{\rm bare}} < 1\), consistent with causality.

## Context

This work extends the HLS/vector manifestation (VM) framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and the finite-temperature analysis of Harada and Sasaki (2003) by lifting the assumption of Lorentz invariance in the bare Lagrangian. It contradicts the standard chiral perturbation theory prediction of Son and Stephanov (2002) that the pion velocity goes to zero at chiral restoration. The result enables further study of pion propagation in hot matter and is noted to be quite similar to the pion velocity in dense matter.
