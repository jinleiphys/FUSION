# The coset construction for particles of arbitrary spin

> When a Poincaré-invariant system spontaneously breaks continuous internal symmetries, Goldstones's theorem demands the existence of massless, spin-zero excitations in a one-to-one correspondence with the broken symmetry generators. When a system spontaneously breaks Poincaré symmetry, however, the kinds of excitations that satisfy Goldstone's theorem can be quite unusual. In particular, they may have any spin and need not be particles or even quasiparticles. The standard coset construction used to formulate effective actions of Goldstones, however, is rather restrictive and is incapable of generating the full spectrum of possibilities allowed by Goldstone's theorem. We propose a (partial) remedy to this problem by postulating a novel coset construction for systems that spontaneously break Poincaré symmetry. This new construction is capable of generating effective actions with a wide range of Goldstone excitations---including fermionic degrees of freedom---even when all symmetries are bosonic. To demonstrate it's utility, we focus on constructing effective actions for point particles of various spins. We recover the known result that a particle of spin $s$ requires an $\mathcal N=2s$ supersymmetric worldline reparameterization gauge symmetry, which we implement at the level of the coset construction. In the process, we discover that massless particles require a novel kind of inverse Higgs constraint that bears some resemblance to the dynamical inverse Higgs constraints that appear in certain fermi liquid effective field theories. We then consider particles that, in addition to quantum spin, have finite spatial extent and are free to rotate. We derive a novel action for such particles and find a `spin-orbital' coupling between the intrinsic quantum spin and the physical-rotation degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel coset construction for systems that spontaneously break Poincare symmetry, which can generate effective actions with Goldstone excitations of arbitrary spin, including fermionic degrees of freedom, even when all global symmetries are bosonic, partially remedying the inability of the standard coset construction to capture the full spectrum of possibilities allowed by Goldstone's theorem.

## Method

The authors parameterize the full symmetry group with Goldstone fields associated with every symmetry generator (both broken and unbroken), introduce worldvolume coordinates that may include fermionic dimensions, and impose gauge symmetries via right-multiplication on the group element to distinguish broken from unbroken Goldstones. They apply this framework to point particles by imposing an N=2s supersymmetric worldline reparameterization gauge symmetry at the level of the coset construction, using the Maurer-Cartan form to generate invariant building blocks and imposing inverse Higgs constraints to remove extraneous Goldstones.

## Key numbers

- A particle of spin s requires an N=2s supersymmetric worldline reparameterization gauge symmetry, where N is the number of supercharges.
- For a massive spin-0 point particle, the leading-order effective action is S = -m ∫ dt √(1 - ẊⁱẊⁱ), where m is the mass.
- For the Polyakov-style action of a spin-0 particle, the action takes the form S = -∫ dτ [ (1/e) Ẋ^μẊ_μ - e m² ], where e is the worldline einbein.
- The inverse Higgs constraint for spin-0 particles gives (ηⁱ/η) tanh η = Ẋⁱ/Ẋ⁰, where η = √(ηⁱηⁱ).

## Context

This work extends the coset construction framework developed by Ogievetsky (1974), Volkov (1973), Weinberg (1968), and Ivanov and Ogievetsky (1975) to accommodate a wider range of Goldstone excitations than previously possible. The paper builds on the approach of Landry (2019) for finite-temperature systems, where the coset is parameterized as if every symmetry were broken, and addresses limitations noted in prior attempts to include fermionic degrees of freedom in coset constructions by Ira (2018) and Ira (2019), which added fermions by hand rather than generating them from the coset itself. The work enables the construction of effective actions for particles of arbitrary spin using the coset method and introduces a novel kind of inverse Higgs constraint for massless particles that resembles the dynamical inverse Higgs constraints appearing in Fermi liquid effective field theories.
