# Cluster-decay of $^{56}$Ni$^{\ast}$} compound system using different Fermi density parameters

> We have calculated the half-lives for $^{56}$Ni$^{\ast}$ within PCM and UFM for clusters $\ge ^{16}$O. For PCM, the order of magnitude of cluster decay constants for different density parameters is nearly same, except for SM parameters. Similar trends are observed for UFM. The percentage variation in the half-lives for the PCM lies within $\pm$5% excluding SM parameters, whereas including SM parameters it lies within $\pm$13%. In the case of UFM, half-lives lie within $\pm$1.5% for all density parameters except of SM. For SM parameters these variations lie within $\pm$9%.

## Key claim

The half-lives for cluster decay of the excited compound nucleus \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\) into clusters \(\ge ^{16}\text{O}\) are largely insensitive to the choice of Fermi density parameters, except for the SM parameter set, which produces larger deviations. The abstract’s statement of “nearly same” order of magnitude is qualified by the exclusion of SM parameters in the tighter variation ranges.

## Method

The author calculated cluster decay half-lives using the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) and the Unified Fission Model (UFM) for the compound system \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\). Five different two-parameter Fermi density parameter sets (labeled DV, Ngo, SM, EW, and HS) were taken from the literature and used to compute the nuclear potential via the Skyrme force SIII (without surface correction factor, \(\lambda = 0\)). The decay constant \(\lambda\) was obtained from the product of preformation probability \(P_0\), assault frequency \(\nu_0\), and penetrability \(P\), with the effective Q-value \(Q_{\text{eff}}\) set to \(0.4(28 - |Q_{\text{out}}|)\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- For PCM, the percentage variation in half-lives across all density parameters except SM is within \(\pm 5\%\); including SM parameters, the variation is within \(\pm 13\%\).
- For UFM, the half-life variation across all density parameters except SM is within \(\pm 1.5\%\); for SM parameters, the variation is within \(\pm 9\%\).
- The fragmentation potential and fractional mass distribution yields were calculated at a temperature \(T = 3.0\) MeV for \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\) using PCM.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of cluster decay half-lives by systematically testing the sensitivity to different Fermi density parameter sets from the literature (e.g., DV, Ngo, SM, EW, HS). The results show that the choice of density parameters has only a minor effect on half-lives, except for the SM set, which suggests that future calculations can safely use most standard parameterizations without large uncertainty. The study enables more reliable predictions for cluster decay of neutron-deficient compound systems like \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\) formed in heavy-ion collisions.
