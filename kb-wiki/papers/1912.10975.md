# Dense 2-color QCD towards continuum and chiral limits

> We study two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermion as a function of quark chemical potential mu and temperature T, for two different lattice spacings and two different quark masses. We find that the quarkyonic region, where the behaviour of the quark number density and the diquark condensate are described by a Fermi sphere of almost free quarks distorted by a BCS gap, extends to larger chemical potentials with decreasing lattice spacing or quark mass. In both cases, the quark number density also approaches its non-interacting value. The pressure at low temperature is found to approach the Stefan-Boltzmann limit from below.

## Key claim

The quarkyonic region, where the quark number density and diquark condensate behave as a Fermi sphere of almost free quarks distorted by a BCS gap, extends to larger chemical potentials as the lattice spacing decreases or the quark mass decreases, but the authors note that the diquark source extrapolation remains a significant systematic uncertainty, particularly for the light ensemble where power-law fits can yield results consistent with BCS scaling at all chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermion on three ensembles: a coarse ensemble (a=0.178 fm, m_pi/m_rho=0.805), a fine ensemble (a=0.138 fm, m_pi/m_rho=0.810), and a light ensemble (a=0.189 fm, m_pi/m_rho=0.61). They computed the diquark condensate, Polyakov loop, and quark number density as functions of chemical potential mu and temperature T, extrapolating results to zero diquark source j using linear, quadratic, and power-law forms.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing on the fine ensemble: a = 0.138(6) fm, determined from the string tension sqrt(sigma)=440 MeV
- Pion mass on the fine ensemble: am_pi = 0.446(3), with m_pi/m_rho = 0.810(7)
- Pion mass on the light ensemble: am_pi = 0.438(15), with m_pi/m_rho = 0.61(5)
- Superfluid critical temperature T_s on the fine ensemble: 90(10) MeV at mu a=0.3, 107(18) MeV at mu a=0.4, 86(10) MeV at mu a=0.5
- Deconfinement temperature T_d on the fine ensemble at mu=0: 194(4) MeV (Scheme A) and 234(10) MeV (Scheme B)
- Quark number density on the fine ensemble at low temperature (T=45 MeV) approaches the non-interacting Stefan-Boltzmann limit from below, with n_q/n_SB reaching a plateau near unity for mu greater than approximately 500 MeV
- Pressure p/p_SB at low temperature (T approximately 45 MeV) reaches a plateau at mu/mu_o approximately 2, approaching the Stefan-Boltzmann limit from below

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-color QCD by Hands et al. (2010), Cotter et al. (2012), and Boz et al. (2013) that used coarser lattices and heavier quarks (m_pi/m_rho=0.8). The new results show that the high-mu increase in n_q/n_SB seen on coarse lattices is a lattice artefact, and that the quarkyonic phase persists to larger chemical potentials without a deconfinement transition at low temperature. The light ensemble results suggest a possible BEC window near onset where chiral perturbation theory predictions by Kogut et al. (2000) may apply, but the diquark source extrapolation remains uncontrolled. The findings enable future studies of the continuum and chiral limits of two-color QCD and provide benchmarks for effective models such as Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models and Dyson-Schwinger approaches.
