# Multijet topology in high-energy nuclear collisions: jet broadening

> This work presents the first theoretical investigation of the medium modification of jet broadening as an event-shape observable in multijet final states due to jet quenching in high-energy nuclear collisions. The partonic spectrum of $pp$ collisions with next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV is provided by the POWHEG$+$PYTHIA8 event generator, while the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model is utilized to investigate the energy loss of fast partons as they traverse through the hot and dense QCD medium. We present the jet broadening distributions in multijet final states for both $pp$ and PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV, then observe an enhancement at the small jet broadening region and suppression at the large jet broadening region in PbPb collisions relative to that in $pp$. This suggests that medium modification with parton energy loss in the QGP leads to a more concentrated energy flow in all observed multijet events in PbPb reactions. We also demonstrate that the intertwining of two effects, the jet number reduction and the restructured contribution, results in the novel behavior of nuclear modification of the jet broadening observable in PbPb collisions.

## Key claim

This work presents the first theoretical investigation of how jet broadening, an event-shape observable, is modified in multijet final states by jet quenching in PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The authors find that the nuclear modification arises from the intertwining of two effects: jet number reduction and a restructured contribution, not solely from energy loss.

## Method

The authors generated a pp baseline using the POWHEG event generator matched with PYTHIA8 for parton showering, providing NLO-accurate partonic spectra. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-\(k_t\) algorithm (radius \(R=0.5\)) using FastJet, requiring at least three jets with \(p_T > 30\) GeV and leading jet \(110 < p_{T,1} < 170\) GeV in \(|\eta_{\mathrm{jet}}| < 2.4\). The linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model was then used to simulate both elastic and inelastic parton energy loss as the partons traverse the QGP medium described by the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamic model.

## Key numbers

* The normalized jet broadening distribution in 0-30% central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV shows a peak at \(\Btot = 0.082\) (\(\ln B_{tot} = -2.5\)).
* In pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV, the fraction of three-jet events is 76.98% and events with four or more jets is 23.02%; in PbPb collisions, the three-jet fraction increases to 81.17% and the multijet fraction decreases to 18.83%.
* The relative contributions to the selected quenched events in PbPb are: "Survival" events 57.85%, "Falldown" events 27.53%, and "Restructured" events 14.63%.
* The pp baseline calculation at 7 TeV using POWHEG+PYTHIA8 provides a "pretty good description" of CMS experimental data for the jet broadening distribution.

## Context

This work extends the study of event-shape variables in heavy-ion collisions beyond transverse sphericity, which was previously investigated by Chen (2020) and Mallick (2020). Unlike transverse sphericity, jet broadening is zero for two-jet events and therefore provides more complete information on the geometric properties of multijet events with \(N_{\mathrm{jet}} \geq 3\). The results enable future experimental measurements at the LHC to test whether the QGP makes multijet energy flow more concentrated, and suggest that the "Restructured" events may involve contributions from Moliere scattering, which could serve as a probe of short-distance quark and gluon interactions within the strongly coupled QGP.
