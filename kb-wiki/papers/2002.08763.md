# Heavy-flavour in relativistic nuclear collisions: recent developments

> Transport calculations represent the major tool to simulate the modifications induced by the presence of a hot-deconfined medium on the production of heavy-flavour particles in high-energy nuclear collisions. After a brief description of the approach and of the major achievements in its phenomenological applications we discuss some recent developments. In particular we focus on observables arising from event-by-event fluctuations in the distribution of deposited energy (odd flow harmonics, event-shape-engineering) and from the tilting of the initial geometry with respect to the beam axis (directed flow), with a possible role played by the strong magnetic field generated by the spectator nucleons.

# Heavy-flavour in relativistic nuclear collisions: recent developments

## Key claim
Transport calculations are the primary tool for simulating heavy-flavour modifications in nuclear collisions, but because the fireball has a finite lifetime and undergoes rapid expansion, charm and beauty quarks are not expected to reach full kinetic equilibrium with the medium, this fact may instead provide an estimate of the medium’s transport coefficients, for which otherwise one would obtain only a lower or upper bound.

## Method
The authors review the use of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, approximated in most numerical implementations as a Langevin equation, to model heavy-quark interactions with the quark-gluon plasma via multiple uncorrelated soft scatterings. The heavy-quark momentum is updated in each time step using a deterministic friction force and a random noise term, requiring knowledge of three transport coefficients (transverse and longitudinal momentum broadening and drag), which are related by the Einstein fluctuation-dissipation relation. Various transport calculations differ in their choice of these coefficients and are compared to experimental data on the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of D-mesons.

## Key numbers
- The directed flow \(v_1\) of \(D^0/\overline D^0\) mesons in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV is measured by the STAR collaboration as a function of rapidity.
- The triangular flow \(v_3\) of D-mesons is studied for 0-10% most central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, comparing transport simulations to CMS data.
- The orbital angular momentum in non-central collisions is of order 1000\(\hbar\).
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of D-mesons in the 30-50% centrality class is analyzed for the 0-20% highest and 0-60% lowest-eccentricity subsamples, with the ratio to the unbiased result found to be independent of the choice of transport coefficients.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on heavy-flavour transport in nuclear collisions, comparing results from multiple calculations (Beraudo 2014, He 2014, Cao 2017, Nahrgang 2013) to ALICE data (Acharya 2018). It discusses recent developments in observables from event-by-event fluctuations (odd flow harmonics, event-shape-engineering) and from the tilting of the initial geometry (directed flow), with a possible role for the strong magnetic field generated by spectator nucleons. The work enables future extraction of charm and beauty transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma, particularly once measurements of beauty at low transverse momentum become available.
