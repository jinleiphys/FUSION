# High-energy direct reactions with exotic nuclei and low-energy nuclear astrophysics

> Indirect methods in nuclear astrophysics are discussed. Recent work on Coulomb dissociation and an effective-range theory of low-lying electromagnetic strength of halo nuclei is presented. Coulomb dissociation of a halo nucleus bound by a zero-range potential is proposed as a homework problem (for further references see G. Baur and S. Typel, nucl-th/0504068). It is pointed out that the Trojan-Horse method (G. Baur, F. Rösel, D. Trautmann and R. Shyam, Phys. Rep. 111 (1984) 333) is a suitable tool to investigate subthreshold resonances.

## Key claim

Indirect methods (Coulomb dissociation and the Trojan-Horse method) can extract low-energy nuclear astrophysics cross sections from high-energy direct reactions with exotic nuclei, but the Trojan-Horse method requires more phenomenological input (optical model parameters and effective nuclear interactions) than Coulomb dissociation, which relies essentially only on QED.

## Method

The authors present a minireview of indirect methods for nuclear astrophysics, focusing on Coulomb dissociation of halo nuclei and the Trojan-Horse method for transfer reactions. They develop an effective-range theory for the electromagnetic strength distribution in neutron halo nuclei, applying it to the single neutron halo nucleus ¹¹Be and to ²³O, and propose a solvable model for Coulomb dissociation of a neutron halo nucleus bound by a zero-range potential in the Coulomb field of a target nucleus. The Trojan-Horse method is described as a special case of transfer reactions to the continuum under quasi-free scattering conditions, where a two-body astrophysical reaction A + x → C + c is replaced by a three-body reaction A + a → C + c + b with a = (x+b).

## Key numbers

- The reduced transition probability for ¹¹Be was deduced from high-energy Coulomb dissociation at GSI; using a cutoff radius R = 2.78 fm and an inverse bound-state decay length q = 0.1486 fm⁻¹, an ANC of C₀ = 0.724(8) fm⁻¹/² was extracted, corresponding to a spectroscopic factor C²S = 0.704(15)
- The halo expansion parameter γ = qR = 0.4132 < 1 for ¹¹Be
- The p₁/₂ channel in ¹¹Be has a binding energy of 184 keV, corresponding to q = 0.094 fm; with R = 2.78 fm, γ² = 0.068 and a₁ = 210 fm³, giving c₁ = 2.14
- For the ¹¹Be fit, c³/²₁ = -0.41(86,-20) and c¹/²₁ = 2.77(13,-14)
- The ⁸B Coulomb dissociation experiment at GSI used a beam energy of 254 A MeV
- The ⁶Li → α + d Coulomb dissociation experiment used E_⁶Li = 150 A MeV at the KaoS spectrometer at GSI, reaching α-d relative energies down to 50 keV
- The Trojan Horse reaction ²H(⁶Li,α³He)n was measured at E_⁶Li = 25 MeV, extracting cross sections for ⁶Li(p,α)³He from E_cm = 2.4 MeV down to astrophysical energies

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach for electromagnetic strength in halo nuclei introduced by Typel and Baur (2005) and applies it to ¹¹Be and ²³O, building on the textbook case of the deuteron as the prime example of a halo nucleus. The Coulomb dissociation method is related to earlier work by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986) and the Trojan-Horse method was introduced by Baur, Rösel, Trautmann, and Shyam (1984). The paper enables future studies of neutron halo nuclei for intermediate masses at RIKEN, GSI, and RIA, relevant for the astrophysical r-process, and extends the approach to the transition from bound to unbound states in exotic nuclei near the dripline.
