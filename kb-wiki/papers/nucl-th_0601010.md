# Can a Hagedorn system have a temperature other than $T_C$ or can a thermostat have a temperature other than its own?

> This is a note intended to complement our paper (nucl-th/0504010) and addressed to the attention of QGP workers interested in bag models, Hagedorn spectra, and the like. It tries to show that with a Hagedorn-like experimental spectrum the partition function can not be calculated and that a canonical description derived for the microcanonical ensemble exists only for a single, fixed temperature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for systems with an exponential level density (such as Hagedorn or bag model spectra), the canonical partition function \( Z(T) = T_C T / (T_C - T) \) is mathematically incorrect and physically meaningless for any temperature other than the system's intrinsic temperature \( T_C \), because such systems act as thermostats that cannot be forced into thermal equilibrium at a different temperature.

## Method

The authors analyze the conditions under which a canonical partition function can be meaningfully defined by considering two thermally coupled systems with level densities \( \rho_A \) and \( \rho_B \). They examine the distribution of energies between the systems and the condition for thermal equilibrium, which requires matching logarithmic derivatives \( 1/T_A = 1/T_B \). They then test this condition for the special case where one or both systems have a linear entropy \( S(E) = \ln \rho(E) \propto E \), which implies a constant temperature independent of energy.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn spectrum is characterized by \( \rho \propto \exp(aE) \) with \( T_C = 1/a \).
- The ice-water coexistence thermostat at standard pressure has \( T_A = 273 \) K, used as an analog to illustrate the exponential level density \( \rho(Q) = S_0 e^{Q/T_A} \approx K e^{E/T_A} \).
- The partition function derived from the exponential spectrum is \( Z(T) = T_A T / (T_A - T) \), which the paper argues is only valid at \( T = T_A \).

## Context

This note complements the authors' earlier work (Moretto et al., 2005) which showed that systems with exponential spectra act as thermostats at a fixed temperature \( T_C \). It directly contradicts the standard wisdom in bag model and resonance gas literature that such systems admit any temperature \( T < T_C \). The paper enables a critical re-examination of canonical ensemble treatments of Hagedorn spectra and quark-gluon plasma models, and it raises the question of whether thermal equilibrium can be achieved at all when two such systems are coupled.
