# Gamow-Teller strength and the spin-isospin coupling constants of the Skyrme energy functional

> We investigate the effects of the spin-isospin channel of the Skyrme energy functional on predictions for Gamow-Teller distributions and superdeformed rotational bands. We use the generalized Skyrme interaction SkO' to describe even-even ground states and then analyze the effects of time-odd spin-isospin couplings, first term by term and then together via linear regression. Some terms affect the strength and energy of the Gamow-Teller resonance in finite nuclei without altering the Landau parameter g_0' that to leading order determines spin-isospin properties of nuclear matter. Though the existing data are not sufficient to uniquely determine all the spin-isospin couplings, we are able to fit them locally. Altering these coupling constants does not change the quality with which the Skyrme functional describes rotational bands.

## Key claim

The spin-isospin coupling constants of the Skyrme energy functional can be fitted locally to Gamow-Teller (GT) resonance data, but the existing experimental data are not sufficient to uniquely determine all of them; some terms affect the GT strength and energy without altering the Landau parameter \(g_0'\) that to leading order determines spin-isospin properties of nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors used the generalized Skyrme interaction SkO' to describe even-even ground states, then analyzed the effects of time-odd spin-isospin couplings on GT distributions, first term by term and then together via linear regression. Excited states were treated in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), with the residual interaction taken from the second derivative of the energy functional with respect to the density matrix, equivalent to the small-amplitude limit of time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{208}\)Pb, the experimental GT resonance energy is 15.5 MeV; forces SGII, SLy4, SkO, and SkO' produce the resonance at about the right energy, while SkP and SLy5 give it too low.
- For \(^{124}\)Sn, the experimental GT resonance energy is 13.7 MeV; SkP, SLy5, SkO, and SkO' give similar resonance energies close to experiment, while SGII and SLy4 push the resonance too high.
- For \(^{90}\)Zr, the experimental GT resonance energy is 9.4 MeV; the nucleus shows sensitivity to proton spin-orbit splittings and does not respond in a collective way like \(^{124}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The Landau parameter \(g_0'\) for SkO' is 0.79, with \(g_1' = 0.19\); for SGII, \(g_0' = 0.93\) and \(g_1' = 0\); for SkP, \(g_0' = 0.06\) and \(g_1' = 0.97\); for SLy5, \(g_0' = -0.15\) and \(g_1' = 1.05\).
- The effective mass \(m^*/m\) is approximately 1 for SkP, about 0.9 for SkO and SkO', and about 0.7 for SGII and SLy4/SLy5.

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent mean-field studies of GT resonances, particularly the authors' previous study (Engel et al., 1999), and addresses the need for a better description of the GT response to enable more reliable predictions for beta-decay half-lives of very neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis. It contrasts with non-self-consistent approaches that use the Landau-Migdal interaction with a single parameter \(g_0'\) (e.g., Bertsch et al., 1981; Gaarde et al., 1981) or separable interactions (e.g., Moller et al., 1990; Homma et al.), and with earlier Skyrme parameterizations like SGII (Van Giai and Sagawa, 1981) that omitted the \(\tensor{J}^2\) term in the mean-field fit. The results enable future work to fix the spin-isospin part of the Skyrme functional more precisely as more GT data become available.
