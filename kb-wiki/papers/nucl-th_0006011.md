# The Reaction $pp\to ppη$ and the eta-Nucleon and Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> The $π$, $η$ and $ρ$ exchange contributions to the cross-section for the reaction $pp\to ppη$ near threshold are calculated with a phenomenological description of the intermediate S11 (N(1535)) resonance for all exchange mechanisms. The final state interaction in the $pp$ system is described by realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models. The sensitivity of the results to the phenomenological models for the $η$-nucleon transition amplitude and the nucleon-nucleon interaction is explored. The $η$ exchange mechanism is found to play a dominant role. The off-shell behavior of the $ηN \to ηN$ amplitude within the $η-$exchange amplitude leads to a result significantly different from that obtained with the constant scattering length approximation. The two-nucleon amplitudes that are associated with the short range components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction contribute significantly to the cross section.

# The Reaction $pp\to pp\eta$ and the eta-Nucleon and Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

## Key claim

The $\eta$ exchange mechanism dominates the $pp\to pp\eta$ cross section near threshold, but only if the $\eta$NN coupling constant is reduced to about one-third of the value used in realistic nucleon-nucleon potential models (such as the Bonn B model). The off-shell behavior of the $\eta N \to \eta N$ amplitude produces results that differ by a factor of 6 from the constant scattering length approximation, which the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors calculated $\pi$, $\eta$, and $\rho$ exchange contributions to the $pp\to pp\eta$ cross section near threshold using a phenomenological description of the intermediate S11 (N(1535)) resonance for all exchange mechanisms. They employed realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models (Bonn B and Paris potentials) to describe the final state interaction in the $pp$ system, and used the variable-mass isobar model of Batini\'c et al. (1995) for the $\eta N \to \eta N$ and $\pi N \to \eta N$ transition amplitudes. The short-range two-nucleon amplitudes associated with the scalar and vector exchange components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction were also included, and the initial state distortion was treated using a reduction factor of 0.3 from the model of Lee and Matsuyama (1987).

## Key numbers

- The $\eta$ exchange cross section calculated with the energy-dependent off-shell $\eta N \to \eta N$ amplitude is a factor of 6 smaller than the result obtained with the constant scattering length approximation (Fig. 3).
- The $\eta$NN coupling constant used to obtain agreement with data is $g^2_{\eta NN}/4\pi = 0.4$, derived from $\eta$ photoproduction analysis by Tiator (1996), compared to the Bonn B model value of 6.14.
- The $\pi$ exchange contribution increases the cross section by approximately 15% when added to the impulse plus $\eta$-rescattering diagrams (Fig. 4).
- Adding the short-range amplitudes (isospin independent scalar and vector exchange) to the impulse and rescattering diagrams increases the cross section by a factor of 9 (Fig. 5).
- The initial state interaction reduction factor is 0.3, as determined from the model of Lee and Matsuyama (1987).
- The $\rho$NN(1535) coupling constant from the constituent quark model is $g_{\rho NN^*}^{(1535)} = -0.88 g_{\rho NN}$ (with constituent quark mass $m_q = 340$ MeV and oscillator parameter $\omega = 311$ MeV).
- The $\eta$NN pseudoscalar coupling constant derived from chiral symmetry and the Goldberger-Treiman relation is 7.23, which is too large to describe the data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $\eta$ production in $pp$ collisions by Wilkin (1993), Moalem (1994), and Meissner (1994), which primarily considered pion-exchange excitation of the N(1535) resonance. The authors confirm the dominance of $\eta$ exchange found by Lee and Svec (1995) using the same $\eta N \to \eta N$ amplitude model of Batini\'c et al. (1995), but go beyond that work by including $\rho$ exchange, short-range two-nucleon amplitudes, and testing sensitivity to different nucleon-nucleon interaction models. The results enable discrimination between phenomenological models for $\eta$N scattering and constrain the $\eta$NN coupling constant, which is relevant for understanding the $\eta$d system and the possible existence of $\eta$NN quasibound states as studied by Pe\~na et al. (1999).
