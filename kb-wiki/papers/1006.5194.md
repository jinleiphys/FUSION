# A Relativistic Generalization of Fowler-Nordheim Cold Emission in Presence of Strong Magnetic Field

> A relativistic version of cold emission of electrons in presence of strong magnetic field, relevant for strongly magnetized neutron stars is obtained. It is found that in this scenario, a scalar type potential barrier does not allow quantum tunneling through the surface. Whereas, in presence of a vector type surface barrier, the probability of electron emission is much larger compared to the original Fowler-Nordheim cold emission of electrons. It is found that the relativistic version in presence of strong magnetic field does not follow exponential decay.

# A Relativistic Generalization of Fowler-Nordheim Cold Emission in Presence of Strong Magnetic Field

## Key claim

The paper claims that for relativistic cold emission of electrons from strongly magnetized neutron star surfaces, a scalar-type potential barrier completely prohibits quantum tunneling regardless of electron energy or external electric field strength, whereas a vector-type surface barrier yields a non-zero transmission coefficient that does not follow the exponential decay characteristic of the original Fowler-Nordheim model.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac equation with scalar and vector interactions in the framework of relativistic quantum hadro-dynamics, then reduce it to an effectively one-dimensional form under the assumption that the magnetic field strength exceeds the critical value \(B_c^{(e)} \approx 4.43 \times 10^{13}\) G, where Landau quantization restricts electron motion along the field direction. A triangular potential barrier of the form \(V(x) = C - eFx\) is introduced at the matter-vacuum interface, and the Dirac equation is solved analytically for both scalar and vector potential cases using Hermite differential equations and Coulomb functions respectively. The transmission coefficient is obtained by matching wavefunctions and their derivatives at the interface.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for electron Landau quantization: \(B_c^{(e)} \approx 4.43 \times 10^{13}\) G
- Typical magnetic field strengths for neutron stars: \(\geq 10^{12}\) G for conventional radio pulsars and \(\geq 10^{15}\) G for magnetars
- Electric field at neutron star poles: \(F \sim 2 \times 10^8 P^{-1} B_{12}\) volt cm\(^{-1}\), where \(P\) is the period in seconds and \(B_{12}\) is the magnetic field in units of \(10^{12}\) G
- Numerically fitted cold emission current: \(R = 0.26 F_{24}^{1/2} \exp(-9.8 \times F_{14})\), where \(F_{14} = 10^{-14}F\) and \(F_{24} = 10^{-24}F\)
- Work function magnetic field dependence: \(W_f = W_c (B/B_c^{(e)})^{0.5}\) in eV, with \(W_c \approx 82.93\) eV

## Context

This work extends the original non-relativistic Fowler-Nordheim cold emission model (Fowler and Nordheim, 1928) to the relativistic regime relevant for strongly magnetized neutron stars, where previous treatments using the Schrodinger equation are inadequate. The study is motivated by the unresolved problem of magnetospheric plasma formation in pulsars and magnetars, where cold emission from polar caps provides the initial electron flux that ultimately produces synchrotron radiation. The finding that scalar-type barriers completely suppress tunneling has analogy with quark confinement in hadronic bag models with scalar linear potentials, while the vector-type result enables future calculations of magnetospheric charge density and synchrotron emission intensity from magnetars.
