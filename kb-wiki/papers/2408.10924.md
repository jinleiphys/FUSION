# Unveiling the jet angular broadening with photon-tagged jets in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The medium modification of jet substructure in hot and dense nuclear matter has garnered significant interest from the heavy-ion physics community in recent years. Measurements of inclusive jets show an angular narrowing in nucleus-nucleus collisions, while recent CMS results for photon-tagged jets ($γ$+jets) suggest evidence of broadening. In this study, we conduct a theoretical analysis of the angular structure of inclusive jets and $γ$+jets using a transport approach that accounts for jet energy loss and the medium response in the quark-gluon plasma. We examine the girth modification of $γ$+jets in $0-30\%$ PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, achieving satisfactory agreement with recent CMS measurements. We explore the relationship between selection bias and jet kinematics by varying the threshold for $x_{jγ} = p_T^{\rm jet}/p_T^γ$. Notably, we quantitatively demonstrate that $γ$+jets significantly reduce selection bias and can effectively select jets that have been sufficiently quenched in PbPb collisions, which is crucial for capture the jet angular broadening. Additionally, we estimate the contributions of medium-induced gluon radiation and the medium response to the broadening of the jet angular substructure. Lastly, we analyze the modification patterns of jet $R_g$ and $ΔR_{\rm axis}$ in PbPb collisions, which indicate slight broadening for $γ$+jets and noticeable narrowing for inclusive jets compared to pp collisions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that photon-tagged jets ($\gamma$+jets) significantly reduce selection bias compared to inclusive jets in PbPb collisions, enabling the observation of genuine jet angular broadening that is otherwise masked in inclusive jet measurements. The authors show that the apparent narrowing of inclusive jets is an artifact of selection bias, while jets intrinsically become broader due to jet-medium interactions.

## Method
The authors employ PYTHIA8 with the Monash Tune to generate pp event baselines, then simulate jet evolution in the quark-gluon plasma using a transport approach that incorporates both radiative energy loss via the higher-twist formalism and collisional energy loss via Hard-Thermal-Loop calculations. Medium response is included using a Cooper-Frye based perturbation approach, and a Jet-by-Jet matching procedure is used to track individual jets before and after quenching in Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers
- Jet transport parameter: $q_0 = 1.2 \pm 0.2$ GeV$^2$/fm, extracted from $\chi^2$ fitting to identified hadron production in PbPb collisions at LHC
- Fraction of jets rejected by selection bias: $23.9\%$ for $\gamma$+jets with $x_{j\gamma} > 0.4$, compared to $58.4\%$ for $x_{j\gamma} > 0.8$
- Fraction of jets falling below $p_T > 40$ GeV threshold after quenching: $20.3\%$ for $\gamma$+jets versus $63.4\%$ for inclusive jets
- Initial gluon jet fraction: approximately $60\%$ in inclusive jet sample versus $10-20\%$ in $\gamma$+jet sample
- Survival rate: approximately $80\%$ of $\gamma$+jets pass selection criteria in PbPb, while less than $40\%$ of inclusive jets do
- All calculations performed for $0-30\%$ PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV with jet $p_T > 40$ GeV, $|\eta_{\rm jet}| < 2$, and jet radius $R=0.2$

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet substructure modifications in heavy-ion collisions, addressing the apparent contradiction between inclusive jet narrowing observed by ALICE (2018) and ATLAS (2022) and the broadening hints seen in CMS $\gamma$+jet measurements (2024). The study builds on prior work by Brewer (2021) and Wang (2021) on selection bias in jet measurements, and provides quantitative support for using vector boson tagged jets as a cleaner probe of jet-medium interactions. The results enable future experimental searches for more pronounced angular broadening effects by optimizing $x_{j\gamma}$ and $p_T^{\gamma}$ selection cuts.
