# Symmetry and Asymmetry in Bosonic Gaussian Systems: A Resource-Theoretic Framework

> We study the interplay of symmetries and Gaussianity in bosonic systems, under closed and open dynamics, and develop a resource theory of Gaussian asymmetry. Specifically, we focus on Gaussian symmetry-respecting (covariant) operations, which serve as the free operations in this framework. We prove that any such operation can be realized via Gaussian Hamiltonians that respect the symmetry under consideration, coupled to an environment prepared in a symmetry-respecting pure Gaussian state. We further identify a family of tractable monotone functions of states that remain non-increasing under Gaussian symmetry-respecting dynamics, and are exactly conserved in closed systems. We demonstrate that these monotones are not generally respected under non-Gaussian symmetry-respecting dynamics. Along the way, we provide several technical results of independent interest to the quantum information and optics communities, including a new approach to the Stinespring dilation theorem, and an extension of Williamson's theorem for the simultaneous normal mode decomposition of Gaussian systems and conserved charges.

## Key claim

The paper develops a resource theory of Gaussian asymmetry for bosonic continuous-variable systems, proving that Gaussian symmetry-respecting (covariant) operations can always be realized via Gaussian Hamiltonians that respect the symmetry coupled to a symmetry-respecting pure Gaussian environment, and identifying tractable monotones that remain non-increasing under such dynamics but are not generally conserved under non-Gaussian symmetry-respecting dynamics.

## Method

The authors construct a resource-theoretic framework where the free operations are Gaussian channels that are covariant under a given symmetry group representation, characterized by invariance conditions on the channel's phase-space parameters (displacement vector, gain matrix X, and noise matrix Y). They derive families of monotone functions for Gaussian asymmetry by analyzing how the displacement vector and covariance matrix transform under symmetry, using techniques including an extension of Williamson's theorem for simultaneous normal mode decomposition of Gaussian systems and conserved charges, and a new approach to the Stinespring dilation theorem for Gaussian covariant channels.

## Context

This work bridges the resource theory of asymmetry, previously developed primarily for finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces (Bartlett 2007, Gour 2008, Marvian 2013-2014), with the Gaussian formalism that is ubiquitous in quantum optics and continuous-variable quantum information (Weedbrook 2012, Adesso 2014, Serafini 2017). The framework extends Gaussian resource theories (Lami 2018, Takagi 2018) by incorporating symmetry constraints, and connects to quantum thermodynamics where Gaussian thermal operations have been studied (Serafini 2020) as a restriction of energy-conserving operations. The results enable systematic quantification of asymmetry in experimentally relevant bosonic systems and provide conservation laws for closed Gaussian dynamics that do not hold under non-Gaussian dynamics.
