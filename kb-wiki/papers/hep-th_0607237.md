# Photon and dilepton production in supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

> By weakly gauging one of the U(1) subgroups of the R-symmetry group, N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory can be coupled to electromagnetism, thus allowing a computation of photon production and related phenomena in a QCD-like non-Abelian plasma at both weak and strong coupling. We compute photon and dilepton emission rates from finite temperature N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma both perturbatively at weak coupling to leading order, and non-perturbatively at strong coupling using the AdS/CFT duality conjecture. Comparison of the photo-emission spectra for N=4 plasma at weak coupling, N=4 plasma at strong coupling, and QCD at weak coupling reveals several systematic trends which we discuss. We also evaluate the electric conductivity of N=4 plasma in the strong coupling limit, and to leading-log order at weak coupling. Current-current spectral functions in the strongly coupled theory exhibit hydrodynamic peaks at small frequency, but otherwise show no structure which could be interpreted as well-defined thermal resonances in the high-temperature phase.

## Key claim
Photon and dilepton emission rates from N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma can be computed at both weak and strong coupling by weakly gauging a U(1) subgroup of the R-symmetry group, with the strong-coupling results relying on the AdS/CFT duality conjecture rather than on direct non-perturbative QCD methods.

## Method
The authors couple N=4 SYM theory to electromagnetism by gauging a specific U(1) subgroup of the SU(4) R-symmetry, choosing an embedding that gives two Weyl fermions charge ±1/2 and two complex scalars charge 1/2. They compute the retarded current-current correlation function at strong coupling by solving Maxwell's equations for U(1) gauge field perturbations on a five-dimensional AdS-Schwarzschild background, imposing incoming-wave boundary conditions at the horizon. At weak coupling, they perform perturbative calculations to leading order in the 't Hooft coupling for frequencies large compared to λ²T.

## Key numbers
- Electric conductivity at strong coupling: σ = e² Nc² T / (16π)
- Trace of spectral function for lightlike momenta at small frequency: χμμ(wn=qn) ~ (1/2) Nc² T² [wn - (π²/12) wn³ + O(wn⁵)] for wn ≪ 1
- Trace of spectral function for lightlike momenta at large frequency: χμμ(wn=qn) ~ (1/4) Nc² T² wn^(2/3) × 3^(5/6) Γ(2/3)/Γ(1/3) + O(1) for wn ≫ 1
- The spectral function at zero temperature has the form χμν(K) = Pμν(K) × (Nc²/16π) × |K²| × Θ(-K²) × sgn(k⁰)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of thermal spectral functions in strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory, which were performed for the energy-momentum tensor by Teaney (2006) and Kovtun and Starinets (2006), to the case of R-current correlators relevant for photon and dilepton production. The weak-coupling analysis generalizes the corresponding QCD calculation by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2001-2003) to the SYM case. The results enable comparison of photo-emission spectra between weakly coupled SYM, strongly coupled SYM, and weakly coupled QCD, revealing that strongly coupled SYM has greater photon production at large momentum but less at small momenta (k ≪ λ^(2/3) T), while dilepton production for large masses is essentially identical between weak and strong coupling.
