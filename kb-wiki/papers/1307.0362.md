# Determination of exotic hadron structure by constituent-counting rule for hard exclusive processes

> We propose to use hard exclusive production of an exotic hadron for finding its internal quark-gluon configuration by the constituent-counting rule in perturbative QCD. In particular, the cross section for the exclusive process $π^- + p \to K^0 + Λ(1405)$ is estimated at the scattering angle $θ=90^\circ$ in the center-of-mass frame by using current experimental data. In comparison, the cross section for the ground-state $Λ$ production $π^- + p \to K^0 + Λ$ is also shown. We suggest that the internal quark configuration of $Λ(1405)$ should be determined by the asymptotic scaling behavior of the cross section. If it is an ordinary three-quark baryon, the scaling of the cross section is $s^{8} dσ/dt=$constant, whereas it is $s^{10} dσ/dt=$constant if $Λ(1405)$ is a five-quark hadron, where $s$ and $t$ are Mandelstam variables. Such a measurement will be possible, for example, by using the high-momentum beamline at J-PARC. In addition, another exclusive process $γ+ p \to K^+ + Λ(1405)$ could be investigated at LEPS and JLab for finding the nature of $Λ(1405)$. We indicate that the constituent-counting rule could be used as a valuable quantity in determining internal structure of exotic hadrons by high-energy exclusive processes, where quark-gluon degrees of freedom explicitly appear. Furthermore, it is interesting to investigate the transition from hadron degrees of freedom to quark-gluon ones for exclusive exotic-hadron production processes.

## Key claim
The internal quark-gluon configuration of the exotic hadron candidate Λ(1405) can be determined by measuring the asymptotic scaling behavior of the exclusive cross section for π⁻ + p → K⁰ + Λ(1405) at large momentum transfer, provided the measurement is performed at sufficiently high energy where the constituent-counting rule of perturbative QCD applies. The authors hedge that their cross section estimates are only order-of-magnitude and that precise theoretical predictions are not possible at this stage.

## Method
The authors apply the constituent-counting rule from perturbative QCD, which predicts that for a hard exclusive 2→2 scattering process a+b→c+d, the differential cross section scales as dσ/dt ∼ s^(2−n) f(θ_cm), where n = n_a + n_b + n_c + n_d is the total number of elementary constituents (quarks and antiquarks) in the four hadrons. They estimate the cross section for π⁻ + p → K⁰ + Λ(1405) at θ=90° in the center-of-mass frame by using existing experimental data for the ground-state Λ production π⁻ + p → K⁰ + Λ and scaling arguments, without performing a full perturbative QCD calculation.

## Key numbers
- For Λ(1405) as an ordinary three-quark baryon (n=3+3+2+3=11), the predicted scaling is s⁸ dσ/dt = constant.
- For Λ(1405) as a five-quark hadron (n=3+3+2+5=13), the predicted scaling is s¹⁰ dσ/dt = constant.
- The Λ(1405) has mass 1405.1 MeV and width 50 MeV (from PDG 2012).
- The mass difference M_Λ(1405) − M_N(1535) ≈ −130 MeV, showing the anomalously light mass of Λ(1405) compared to the SU(6) quark model expectation.
- The high-momentum beamline at J-PARC will provide unseparated hadron (pion) beam with momentum up to 15–20 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the constituent-counting rule, previously confirmed for reactions such as γ + p → π⁺ + n at JLab (JLab 2009), to the study of exotic hadron candidates. It proposes a new method to distinguish between the three-quark (uds) interpretation and the five-quark (meson-baryon molecule or pentaquark) interpretation of Λ(1405), which has been debated since the 1960s with proposals including a K⁻N molecule (Dalitz 1960s) and a dynamically generated resonance in chiral unitary models (Hyodo 2008). The measurement would be enabled by future experiments at J-PARC, LEPS, and JLab, and could also investigate the transition from hadron degrees of freedom to quark-gluon degrees of freedom in exclusive exotic-hadron production.
