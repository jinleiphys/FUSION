# Gauge-invariant tree-level photoproduction amplitudes with form factors

> We show how the gauge-invariance formulation given by Haberzettl is implemented in practice for photoproduction amplitudes at the tree level with form factors describing composite nucleons. We demonstrate that, in contrast to Ohta's gauge-invariance prescription, this formalism allows electric current contributions to be multiplied by a form factor, i.e., it does not require that they be treated like bare currents. While different in detail, this nevertheless lends support to previous ad hoc approaches which multiply the Born amplitudes by an overall form factor. Numerical results for kaon photoproduction off the nucleon are given. They show that the gauge procedure by Haberzettl leads to much improved $χ^2$ values as compared to Ohta's prescription.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariance restoration method by Haberzettl (1997) allows hadronic form factors to multiply electric current contributions in tree-level photoproduction amplitudes, unlike Ohta's (1989) prescription which forces these terms to be treated as bare currents; this provides a formal foundation for previous ad hoc approaches that multiply Born amplitudes by an overall form factor.

## Method

The authors implement Haberzettl's gauge-invariance formalism for tree-level photoproduction amplitudes with hadronic form factors, using the reaction gamma p -> n pi+ with pseudoscalar piNN coupling as a pedagogical example. They then apply the formalism to kaon photoproduction reactions gamma p -> Lambda K+ and gamma p -> Sigma0 K+, using a phenomenological Born-plus-resonance model that includes K* exchange in the t-channel and S11(1650) and P11(1710) resonances in the s-channel, with all resonances multiplied by the same hadronic form factor. The subtraction function in the contact current is taken as a linear combination of the three vertex form factors with coefficients a_i that sum to unity.

## Key numbers

- For fixed SU(3) values of the coupling constants (g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -3.8 and g_{K Sigma N}/sqrt(4 pi) = 1.2), the model without hadronic form factors yields chi^2 = 55.8 for the (gamma, K) reactions
- When the two couplings are allowed to vary freely without form factors, the fit gives g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -1.89 and g_{K Sigma N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -0.37
- For g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -4.2, Ohta's prescription gives chi^2 = 32.2, while Haberzettl's method keeps chi^2 approximately constant
- At g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi) = -3.4, the chi^2 obtained with Ohta's method is almost a factor of 2 larger than with Haberzettl's method

## Context

This work extends the general gauge-invariance treatment of Haberzettl (1997) to practical tree-level calculations, directly contrasting it with the widely used Ohta (1989) prescription. The authors demonstrate that Ohta's method cannot accommodate hadronic form factors in electric current contributions, which forces extracted coupling constants far from SU(3) values, while Haberzettl's approach allows coupling constants close to SU(3) predictions. This enables more reliable separation of resonance and background contributions in meson photoproduction studies in the 1-2 GeV region, which is the main purpose of such measurements.
