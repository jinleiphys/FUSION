# Constraining non-dissipative transport coefficients in global equilibrium

> The fluid in global equilibrium must fulfill some constraints. These constraints can be derived from quantum statistical theory or kinetic theory. In this paper we will show that how these constraints can be applied to determine the non-dissipative transport coefficients for chiral systems along with the energy-momentum conservation, chiral anomaly for charge current and trace anomaly in energy-momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the constraints a fluid must satisfy in global equilibrium, combined with conservation laws and anomalies, can determine or constrain non-dissipative transport coefficients for chiral systems up to second order in the expansion in electromagnetic field and vorticity, without requiring the principle of entropy increase or Kubo formulas.

## Method

The authors start from the global equilibrium constraints for a fluid in a homogeneous external electromagnetic field: the symmetrized derivative of the thermal velocity β_μ = u_μ/T vanishes, and the gradient of the thermal potential μ̄ = μ/T is balanced by the electromagnetic field. They expand the energy-momentum tensor and charge current in powers of the field strength tensor F_μν and the thermal vorticity tensor Ω_μν, then impose energy-momentum conservation, the chiral anomaly for the charge current, and the trace anomaly for the energy-momentum tensor order by order to derive differential equations for the transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly coefficient C appears in the first-order charge current coefficients: ξ^B = -C μ + b T and ξ = -C μ² + 2b T μ + a T², where a and b are integration constants.
- The first-order energy-momentum tensor coefficient λ^B equals half of ξ: λ^B = (1/2)(-C μ̄² + 2b μ̄ + a)T².
- The first-order energy-momentum tensor coefficient λ equals (2/3)(-C μ̄³ + 3b μ̄² + a μ̄ + c)T³, where c is another integration constant.
- At second order, the trace anomaly introduces the constant C̃ through the relations λ^{EE} + 3λ̄^{EE} + λ̃^{EE} = C̃ and λ^{BB} + 3λ̄^{BB} + λ̃^{BB} = -C̃.

## Context

This work extends the program of determining anomalous transport coefficients from global equilibrium constraints, previously studied using the principle of entropy increase by Son and Surowka (2009), Sadofyev et al. (2010), Pu et al. (2010), and Kharzeev and Yee (2011). The method presented here involves fewer calculations than the entropy principle approach. The results enable systematic determination of second-order non-dissipative transport coefficients in chiral fluids, relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact stars where chiral magnetic and vortical effects occur.
