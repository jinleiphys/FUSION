# QCD Equation of State From a Chiral Hadronic Model Including Quark Degrees of Freedom

> This work presents an effective model for strongly interacting matter and the QCD equation of state (EoS). The model includes both hadron and quark degrees of freedom and takes into account the transition of chiral symmetry restoration as well as the deconfinement phase transition. At low temperatures $T$ and baryonic densities $ρ_B$ a hadron resonance gas is described using a SU(3)-flavor sigma-omega model and a quark phase is introduced in analogy to PNJL models for higher $T$ and $ρ_B$. In this way, the correct asymptotic degrees of freedom are used in a wide range of $T$ and $ρ_B$. Here, results of this model concerning the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions and thermodynamic model properties are presented. Large hadron resonance multiplicities in the transition region emphasize the importance of heavy-mass resonance states in this region and their impact on the chiral transition behavior. The resulting phase diagram of QCD matter at small chemical potentials is in line with latest lattice QCD and thermal model results.

## Key claim

The chiral effective model produces a smooth cross-over transition in the entire temperature–chemical potential plane when the resonance vector coupling parameter is set to \(r_v \approx 1\), and the resulting phase transition line at small chemical potentials lies at the upper boundary of lattice QCD extrapolations, not exactly in the center of the lattice band.

## Method

The authors construct a unified SU(3)-flavor chiral mean-field model that couples a hadron resonance gas (including baryon octet, decuplet, and heavy-mass resonances up to 2.6 GeV) to a PNJL-like quark phase via scalar (\(\sigma\), \(\zeta\)) and vector (\(\omega\), \(\phi\)) meson fields. Excluded volume effects for finite-size hadrons and point-like quarks, together with a Polyakov loop potential, shift the degrees of freedom from hadrons to quarks and gluons at high temperature and density. All thermodynamic quantities are derived from the grand canonical potential, and the model parameters are fixed to reproduce vacuum masses, nuclear saturation properties, and the additive quark model.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for the chiral transition: \(T_c = 164\) MeV at \(\mu = 0\), determined from the full interacting model including quarks.
* Critical Polyakov temperature: \(T_0 = 175\) MeV, above which quarks become abundant.
* Heavy-mass baryon resonances included up to mass \(m \le 2.6\) GeV.
* Dip in energy density \(e/T^4\) occurs at \(T = (155 \pm 15)\) MeV, caused by hard-core hadron repulsion before quark density rises.
* At \(\mu = 0\), heavy-mass meson resonances become the most abundant mesons at \(T \approx 210\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) sigma-omega model of hadronic matter by incorporating a PNJL-like quark phase and explicit excluded volume effects, building on prior effective model approaches (e.g., the original chiral model and PNJL formulations). The results are compared directly to continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD data (lattice collaborations, 2013) and to thermal model freeze-out curves from SPS to LHC energies. The extracted equation of state is intended for use in dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions, enabling more realistic studies of nuclear matter properties across a wide range of temperatures and baryon densities.
