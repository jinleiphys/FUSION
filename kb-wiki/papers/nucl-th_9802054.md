# A study of nuclei of astrophysical interest in the continuum shell model

> We present here the first application of realistic shell model (SM) including coupling between many-particle (quasi-)bound states and the continuum of one-particle scattering states to the spectroscopy of 8B and to the calculation of astrophysical factors in the reaction 7Be(p,gamma)8B.

# A study of nuclei of astrophysical interest in the continuum shell model

## Key claim

The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), which couples realistic shell-model (quasi-)bound states to the one-particle scattering continuum via the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction, can describe the spectroscopy of 8B and the astrophysical S-factor for 7Be(p,gamma)8B, but the calculated S-factor at 20 keV (22.58-22.70 eV·b) agrees with older experiments while disagreeing with the most recent measurement by Hammache (1998).

## Method

The authors construct the SMEC by solving the shell-model problem for A-particle (quasi-)bound states in the Q-subspace using the code ANTOINE with realistic effective interactions, while treating the one-particle continuum in the P-subspace via coupled-channel equations with a finite-depth Saxon-Woods average potential. The coupling between subspaces is mediated by a zero-range residual interaction V = -V0 (a + b P12^sigma) delta(r1 - r2) with a = 0.73 and a + b = 1, and the effective Hamiltonian H_QQ^eff = H_QQ + H_QP G_P^(+) H_PQ is diagonalized to yield complex eigenvalues giving resonance energies and widths. For the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, the initial scattering wavefunction and final bound-state wavefunction are used to compute E1, E2, and M1 transition amplitudes and the resulting capture cross section and astrophysical S-factor.

## Key numbers

* Width of the 1_1^+ resonance in 8B: 17 keV for V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3 and 29 keV for V0 = 1200 MeV·fm^3, compared to the experimental width of 37 ± 5 keV.
* Astrophysical S-factor at center-of-mass energy 20 keV: S = 22.58 eV·b for V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3 and S = 22.70 eV·b for V0 = 1200 MeV·fm^3.
* Ratio of S-factors at different energies for V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3: S(20)/S(100) = 1.07 and S(20)/S(500) = 1.05.
* Ratio of M1 to E1 contribution at V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3: sigma^M1/sigma^E1 = 8.13 × 10^(-6) at 20 keV, 4.55 × 10^(-5) at 100 keV, and 4.3 × 10^(-3) at 500 keV.
* Resonant M1 contribution to S-factor at the 1_1^+ resonance: S^M1 = 24.72 eV·b for V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3 and S^M1 = 18.23 eV·b for V0 = 1200 MeV·fm^3.
* Total S-factor at the 1^+ resonance: S = 47.05 eV·b for V0 = 650 MeV·fm^3 and S = 41.97 eV·b for V0 = 1200 MeV·fm^3.
* Overlap of the first 1_1^+ resonance wavefunction with unperturbed SM states for V0 = 1200 MeV·fm^3: 0.992 with 1_1^+, 0.1 + 0.005i with 1_2^+, and 0.07 + 0.003i with 1_3^+.

## Context

This work extends the Continuum Shell Model (CSM) approach of Fano (1961), Mahaux and Weidenmuller (1969), and Philpott (1977) by using a realistic shell-model description of the bound-state subspace rather than the restricted configuration spaces previously employed for giant resonances and radiative capture by Barz (1977, 1978) and Fladt (1988). The SMEC enables microscopic calculation of astrophysical S-factors for reactions involving weakly bound exotic nuclei near the drip line, where coupling to the continuum is essential; the authors note ongoing applications to reactions such as 14C(n,gamma)15C, 16O(p,gamma)17F, and 18O(n,gamma)19O.
