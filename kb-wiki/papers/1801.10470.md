# Hyperfine interaction with the $^{229}$Th nucleus and its low lying isomeric state

> The thorium nucleus with mass number $A=229$ has attracted much interest because its extremely low lying first excited isomeric state at about $8$eV opens the possibility for the development of a nuclear clock. However, neither the exact energy of this nuclear isomer nor properties, such as nuclear magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moment are known to a high precision so far. The latter can be determined by investigating the hyperfine structure of thorium atoms or ions. Due to its electronic structure and the long lifetime of the nuclear isomeric state, Th$^{2+}$ is especially suitable for such kind of studies. In this letter we present a combined experimental and theoretical investigation of the hyperfine structure of the $^{229}$Th$^{2+}$ ion in the nuclear ground and isomeric state. A very good agreement between theory and experiment is found for the nuclear ground state. Moreover, we use our calculations to confirm the recently presented experimental value for the nuclear magnetic dipole moment of the thorium nuclear isomer, which was in contradiction to previous theoretical studies.

## Key claim
The paper confirms the recently measured experimental value of the nuclear magnetic dipole moment of the thorium-229 isomer (mu_iso = -0.37 +/- 0.06 mu_N) using two independent theoretical methods, resolving a contradiction with earlier theoretical studies that predicted a much smaller value of mu_iso = -0.076 mu_N.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale calculations of hyperfine constants A and B for Th2+ ions using two independent relativistic many-body methods: a combination of configuration interaction with second-order many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) and the multi-configurational Dirac-Fock (MCDF) approach. These theoretical results were compared with experimental hyperfine spectra obtained from Th2+ ions stored in a radio-frequency linear Paul trap, using both single-step and Doppler-free two-step laser excitation schemes.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic dipole moment of the 229Th nuclear ground state: mu_gr = 0.36 mu_N (from prior work by Safronova (2013))
- Electric quadrupole moment of the 229Th nuclear ground state: Q_gr = 3.11 eb (from prior work by Safronova (2013))
- Magnetic dipole moment of the 229mTh nuclear isomer: mu_iso = -0.37 +/- 0.06 mu_N (experimental value from Thielking (2017))
- Average theoretical value for mu_iso from the two methods: -0.35 mu_N
- Electric quadrupole moment of the 229mTh nuclear isomer: Q_iso = 1.74 +/- 0.08 eb (experimental value)
- Hyperfine constant A for the 6d2(J=2) state at 63 cm-1: experimental value 151(8) MHz, MCDF value 162(8) MHz
- Hyperfine constant A for the 5f6d(J=1) state at 20711 cm-1: experimental value 88(5) MHz, MCDF value 90(4) MHz
- Hyperfine constant B for the 5f6d(J=1) state at 20711 cm-1: experimental value 901(18) MHz, MCDF value 689(110) MHz

## Context
This work extends the hyperfine structure studies of thorium from Th3+ (Safronova 2013) to the more complex Th2+ system, which has 45 levels in the energy range up to 25000 cm-1. The results resolve a contradiction between the experimental measurement of the isomer magnetic dipole moment (Thielking 2017) and earlier theoretical predictions (Dykhne 1998, Litvinova 2009). The precise knowledge of hyperfine structure in Th2+ is essential for future nuclear clock development and for tests of temporal variations of fundamental constants such as the fine structure constant.
