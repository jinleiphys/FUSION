# Comparisons and Predictions for Collisions of deformed $^{238}$U nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 193$ GeV

> We present comparisons to experimental data along with predictions of observables for U+U collisions at 193 GeV using a multistage theoretical and computational framework consisting of boost-invariant IP-Glasma initial state, MUSIC hydrodynamics, and a hadronic transport cascade generated by iS3D \& SMASH. Our results show great agreement with existing anisotropic flow measurements from RHIC [ arXiv:1505.07812 ; arXiv:1901.08155 ] . We provide predictions for differential flow observables as well as multiparticle correlations and transverse-momentum-flow correlations. When possible, we compare our predictions to results from Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV to properly outline the effects of deformation in the initial state on final state observables.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a multistage theoretical framework (IP-Glasma + MUSIC + iS3D/SMASH) reproduces existing anisotropic flow measurements for U+U collisions at 193 GeV, and predict that central collisions of prolate-shaped uranium nuclei should produce a negative correlation between elliptic flow $v_2$ and average transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$, in contrast to the positive correlation seen in spherical nuclei. This prediction is conditional on the use of deformed Woods-Saxon parametrizations and carries systematic uncertainty from the model-dependent extraction of nuclear deformation parameters.

## Method

The authors simulate U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 193$ GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV using a multistage framework: IP-Glasma initial state (based on the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory with classical Yang-Mills evolution), MUSIC relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (with constant shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.136$ and temperature-dependent bulk viscosity), and hadronic transport via iS3D (Cooper-Frye particlization with 14-moment viscous corrections) coupled to SMASH (hadronic cascade, version 1.8). Two different Woods-Saxon parametrizations are used for each nuclear species, including quadrupole ($\beta_2^0$) and hexadecapole ($\beta_4^0$) deformation parameters for uranium, and additional triaxial parameters for deformed gold. Centrality selection is calibrated by matching charged-particle multiplicity distributions to experimental data, and Zero-Degree Calorimeter binning is emulated by sampling spectator neutron counts from a binomial distribution.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 193$ GeV for U+U, 200 GeV for Au+Au
- Woods-Saxon parameters for "New $^{238}$U": $R_0 = 7.068$ fm, $a = 0.538$ fm, $\beta_2^0 = 0.247$, $\beta_4^0 = 0.081$
- Woods-Saxon parameters for "Prev $^{238}$U": $R_0 = 6.874$ fm, $a = 0.556$ fm, $\beta_2^0 = 0.2802$, $\beta_4^0 = -0.0035$
- Woods-Saxon parameters for "Spher $^{197}$Au": $R_0 = 6.37$ fm, $a = 0.535$ fm, all $\beta = 0$
- Woods-Saxon parameters for "Def $^{197}$Au": $R_0 = 6.62$ fm, $a = 0.519$ fm, $\beta_2^0 = 0.098$, $\beta_2^2 = 0.076$, $\beta_4^0 = -0.025$, $\beta_4^2 = -0.018$, $\beta_4^4 = -0.018$
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta/s = 0.136$ (constant)
- Bulk viscosity peak parameters: $N = 0.13$, $T_p = 0.18$ GeV, with asymmetric widths $B_L = 0.02$ GeV (below peak) and $B_G = 0.12$ GeV (above peak)
- Switching temperature for hydrodynamics to hadronic transport: $T_{\rm sw} = 155$ MeV
- Hydrodynamic starting time: $\tau_{\rm hyd} = 0.52$ fm
- Impact parameter sampling range: $b_{\rm min} = 0$ fm to $b_{\rm max} = 8$ fm
- Color charge fluctuation constant: $C = 0.505$ (calibrated to reproduce U+U multiplicity)
- Transverse momentum acceptance for flow analysis: $0.2$ GeV $< p_T < 2.0$ GeV (matching STAR acceptance)
- Rapidity windows for differential flow: $|\eta| < 0.5$ for particles of interest, $0.5 < \eta < 2$ for reference flow vector

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma+MUSIC framework previously applied to spherical nuclei (Schenke et al., 2020; McDonald et al., 2017; Heffernan et al., 2023) to collisions of deformed uranium nuclei, comparing with experimental data from STAR (Adamczyk et al., 2015; Adam et al., 2019). The study uses updated nuclear structure parameters from Ryssens (2023) alongside earlier parametrizations from the Woods-Saxon literature (osti_6477756). The predictions for negative $v_2$-$p_T$ correlations in central U+U collisions test phenomenological hypotheses about how prolate nuclear deformation affects initial-state eccentricities and energy densities, and the framework enables future studies of deformed nuclei including the possibility that gold nuclei may also be deformed (Giacalone et al., 2021; Ryssens, 2023).
