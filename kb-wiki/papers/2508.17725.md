# Impact of a Reflecting Material on a Search for Neutron--Antineutron Oscillations using Ultracold Neutrons

> We investigate neutron--antineutron oscillations of ultracold neutrons in a storage bottle represented by a one-dimensional potential. The experimental sensitivity is determined by the annihilation rate of antineutrons. Its dependence on the antineutron reflectivity and the relative phase shift between the neutron and the antineutron wavefunctions by a reflection from the wall is derived. Optimization of the antineutron pseudopotential was found crucial to maximize the sensitivity of the experiment. Furthermore, methods are discussed for determining the antineutron pseudopotential, which has only been studied indirectly thus far.

## Key claim

The experimental sensitivity of a neutron-antineutron oscillation search using ultracold neutrons in a storage bottle depends critically on matching the real part of the antineutron pseudopotential to that of the neutron pseudopotential, which minimizes the relative phase shift between the two wavefunctions upon reflection; the paper derives this condition analytically and shows it is feasible despite the large antineutron annihilation cross section.

## Method

The authors model neutron-antineutron oscillations in a one-dimensional storage bottle potential using a semi-classical plane-wave treatment, solving the Schrödinger equation for the antineutron wavefunction with reflections at the walls described by complex reflection coefficients. They derive the antineutron annihilation rate as a function of the antineutron reflectivity and the relative phase shift between neutron and antineutron wavefunctions, assuming the quasi-free condition holds only between successive reflections (ΔE·T ≪ ħ) rather than for the total storage time. The figure of merit is defined as the enhancement factor of the annihilation probability relative to free-neutron measurements.

## Key numbers

- Current limit on free neutron-antineutron oscillation time: τ_n-ñ > 0.86 × 10⁸ sec (90% C.L.) from the ILL experiment (Baldo-Ceolin, 1994)
- Current limit for bound neutrons from ¹⁶O half-life at Super-Kamiokande: τ_n-ñ > 4.7 × 10⁸ sec (90% C.L.) (Abe, 2021)
- Theoretically motivated oscillation time in post-sphaleron baryogenesis models: 5 × 10¹⁰ sec (Babu, 2013)
- Antineutron-nucleus scattering length formula: a_ñA = (1.54 ± 0.03)·A^(0.311 ± 0.005) − i(1.00 ± 0.04) fm (Batty, 2001)
- For a baseline pseudopotential U_ñ = 100 − 10i neV with U_n = 100 neV and 50 neV UCNs, the figure of merit is 20; a ±10% variation in the imaginary part changes the FoM by ∓10%, while a ±10% variation in the real part halves the FoM to 10
- Antineutron reflectivity exceeding 0.8 is achievable for kinetic energies below 50 neV with U_ñ = 100 − 10i neV
- The phase shift difference between neutron and antineutron for U_ñ = 100 − 10i neV and U_n = 100 neV is less than 0.01 rad below 50 neV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kerbikov (2004), who treated neutron-antineutron oscillations of trapped UCNs under an idealized assumption of zero magnetic field (ΔE = 0), by deriving the antineutron wavefunction and annihilation rate including finite magnetic field effects and without requiring the quasi-free condition for the total elapsed time. The paper enables optimization of reflecting materials for UCN storage bottles in future oscillation searches, motivated by recent advances in superthermal UCN sources (Golub, 1977; Masuda, 2012; Ito, 2018) that offer orders-of-magnitude higher UCN densities. The authors propose two experimental approaches to determine antineutron scattering lengths: precision X-ray spectroscopy of antiprotonic atoms using transition-edge sensor microcalorimeters (Higuchi, 2025) and low-energy antineutron scattering measurements using antiproton beams at CERN's Antiproton Decelerator (Filippi, 2025).
