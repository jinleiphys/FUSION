# Jet charge modification in dense QCD matter

> In these proceedings we report a recent calculation of the jet charge modification in heavy-ion relative to proton collisions at the LHC. Jets have played an essential role in constraining theories of in-medium parton shower evolution and in determining the properties of the quark-gluon plasma created in ultra-relativistic nuclear reactions. It is important to extend these studies to flavor-tagged jets and explore observables that are sensitive to their partonic origin. The average jet charge, introduced early on in the history of quantum chromodynamics, is a proxy for the electric charge of the quark or gluon that initiates the jet. In the framework of soft-collinear effective theory, we show how to evaluate the jet charge in a dense strongly-interacting matter environments. We identify observables that can isolate the contribution of in-medium branching from isospin effects and present predictions for the transverse momentum dependence of the jet charge distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions and its modification relative to the proton case.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of jet charge modification in heavy-ion collisions using soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET$_{\rm G}$), showing that the observable can isolate in-medium parton shower effects from isospin contributions, but only at transverse momenta below 200 GeV where medium-induced evolution becomes visible.

## Method

The authors evaluate the average jet charge in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC using SCET$_{\rm G}$, where medium corrections to jet functions, matching coefficients, and fragmentation function evolution are constructed from medium-induced splitting kernels. The jet charge is defined as the transverse momentum-weighted sum of particle charges within the jet cone, and its modification is computed by comparing the ratio of flavor-separated jet charges in heavy-ion versus proton collisions. The theoretical framework is validated against ATLAS data for p+p collisions at 8 TeV before extending to nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- Average jet charge in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV is shown for $\kappa=0.3$, 0.5, and 0.7 as a function of jet $p_T$ for more central jets
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, isospin effects dominate the jet charge at very high transverse momenta, while in-medium evolution effects become observable for $p_T < 200$ GeV
- The coupling $g$ between the jet and the medium is varied in the interval (1.8, 2.0) to produce uncertainty bands
- Calculations are presented for $\kappa=0.3$, 1, and 2 in the Pb+Pb results

## Context

This work extends earlier jet charge calculations using Monte Carlo approaches (Chen 2019) and SCET (Li 2019) to provide a systematic effective field theory treatment of medium effects. The results build on first measurements of jet charge in heavy-ion collisions by CMS (Sirunyan 2020) that were used to isolate gluon jet fractions. The formalism enables future studies of jet charge modification in e+A collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider, where recent calculations (Li 2020) have shown observable jet quenching effects in cold nuclear matter.
