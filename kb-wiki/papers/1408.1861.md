# Comparative study of high-spin isomers in semi-magic $Z$=50 isotopic and $N$=82 isotonic chains

> We present a comparative study of the high-spin ${11/2}^-$, ${10}^+$ and ${27/2}^-$ nuclear isomers, observed commonly in the full semi-magic $Z=50$ isotopic and $N=82$ isotonic chains, which are found to display nearly identical systematics in excitation energy and half-lives. A constant and particle number independent energy gap of $\sim$ 4 MeV between the $0^+$ ground states and ${10}^+$ isomers and, the ${11/2}^-$ and ${27/2}^-$ isomers exists before the mid-shell, which declines near the mid-shell, and then becomes constant at $\sim$ 3 MeV after the mid-shell. Large scale shell model calculations are carried out for the full chains of Sn-isotopes and $N=82$ isotones, which reproduce the observed energy systematics reasonably well. The role of seniority, generalized seniority and alignment in the high-spin isomeric states as well as in the states populating the high spin isomers has been discussed. The empirical features suggest that the generalized seniority, known to be valid in the low lying states of even-even nuclei before the mid-shell, remains good up to the ${10}^+$ isomeric states; we further extend this interpretation to the odd-A nuclei before the mid-shell, particularly the ${27/2}^-$ isomers. The seniority becomes good after the mid-shell. The change in energy gap around the mid-shell, therefore, marks a transition from the generalized seniority regime to the seniority regime. From the alignment considerations, the high-spin isomers after the mid-shell emerge as maximally aligned decoupled states. We also note that the similar features of the isomeric half-lives in both the chains may be understood in terms of their similar seniorities and configurations. An overview of the $0^+$ to ${10}^+$ yrast states in the full chain of even-even Sn-isotopes has also been presented. We also make some predictions of possible new isomers based on the empirical systematics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the high-spin isomers in the Z=50 isotopic and N=82 isotonic chains display nearly identical systematics in excitation energy and half-lives, with a constant energy gap of approximately 4 MeV before the mid-shell that declines to approximately 3 MeV after the mid-shell, marking a transition from a generalized seniority regime to a seniority regime.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations using the Nushell code with the SN100PN interaction were performed for the full chains of Sn-isotopes (N=51 to 81) and N=82 isotones (up to Z=76). The calculations used the valence space consisting of the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals between magic numbers 50 and 82, with truncations imposed for valence nucleon numbers 59 to 73 to keep dimensions tractable. The experimental energy systematics of the 11/2-, 10+, and 27/2- isomers were plotted and compared with the calculated results, and the wave functions were analyzed to determine seniority and generalized seniority assignments.

## Key numbers

- Energy gap of approximately 4 MeV between the 0+ ground states and 10+ isomers before the mid-shell, declining to approximately 3 MeV after the mid-shell
- Energy gap of approximately 4 MeV between the 11/2- and 27/2- isomers before the mid-shell, declining to approximately 3 MeV after the mid-shell
- The 11/2- isomeric state declines from approximately 1.5 MeV to approximately 0 MeV as the valence nucleon number crosses the mid-shell at 59
- For Sn-isotopes, the 2+ to 0+ transition energy ranges from 1.257 MeV in 112Sn to 1.132 MeV in 124Sn
- For N=82 isotones, the 2+ to 0+ transition energy ranges from 1.972 MeV in 146Gd to 1.531 MeV in 152Yb
- The ratio R(15:2) (15/2- to 11/2- transition divided by 2+ to 0+ transition) ranges from 0.9295 to 1.0089 for Sn-isotopes with N greater than or equal to 64
- The ratio R(31:12) (31/2- to 27/2- transition divided by 12+ to 10+ transition) ranges from 0.883 to 0.953 for Sn-isotopes with N greater than or equal to 64

## Context

This work extends the study of seniority isomers in semi-magic nuclei, building on the concept of seniority introduced by Racah (1942) and generalized seniority developed by Talmi (1971). The paper compares the behavior of high-spin isomers in the Z=50 isotopic chain with the N=82 isotonic chain, where the same valence orbitals (50-82) are occupied by neutrons in one case and protons in the other. The findings confirm and extend previous seniority assignments by Astier et al. (2013, 2012) for Sn-isotopes and N=82 isotones, and by McNeill et al. (1989) for the Z=66-72 range of N=82 isotones. The work enables predictions of new isomers based on empirical systematics and provides a framework for understanding the transition between generalized seniority and seniority regimes near the mid-shell.
