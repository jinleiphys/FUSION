# Difference between stable and exotic nuclei: medium polarization effects

> The bare NN-potential, parametrized so as to reproduce the nuclear phase shifts leads to a sizable Cooper pair binding energy in nuclei along the stability valley. It is a much debated matter whether this value accounts for the "empirical" value of the pairing gap or whether a similarly important contribution arises from the exchange of collective vibrations between Cooper pair partners. In keeping with the fact that two-particle transfer reactions are the specific probe of pairing in nuclei, and that exotic halo nuclei like 11Li are extremely polarizable, we find that the recent studied reaction, namely 11Li+p -> 9Li+t, provides direct evidence of phonon mediated pairing in nuclei.

## Key claim
The bare NN-potential alone cannot bind the halo neutrons in exotic nuclei like 11Li; the exchange of collective vibrations (phonons) between Cooper pair partners provides the essential pairing interaction, and the reaction 11Li(p,t)9Li offers direct experimental evidence for this phonon-mediated pairing.

## Method
The authors analyze the two-particle transfer reaction 11Li + p → 9Li + t, using microscopic ground-state wavefunctions of 11Li that incorporate both bare and medium-polarization pairing interactions. They calculate absolute cross sections including successive, simultaneous, and non-orthogonality contributions, and compare the ratio between the 2+ excited state and ground state cross sections to test the role of the quadrupole phonon in the effective pairing force.

## Key numbers
- The amplitude of the configuration |((s,p)2+ ⊗ 2+)0+ p3/2(π); 3/2−⟩ in the 11Li ground state wavefunction is 0.1, consistent with predictions from Barranco (2001).
- The first excited state of 9Li populated in the reaction lies at Ex = 2.69 MeV (1/2− state).
- In stable nuclei, the relative contribution to pairing is approximately 50% from bare NN-potential and 50% from exchange of collective vibrations; in light exotic halo nuclei like 11Li and 12Be, the split is about 20% bare and 80% induced.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Barranco (2001), which predicted that medium polarization effects dominate pairing in halo nuclei, and directly addresses the debate over whether bare NN-interactions alone can account for empirical pairing gaps. The analysis of the Tanihata (2008) experiment on 11Li(p,t)9Li provides the first direct evidence for phonon-mediated pairing in nuclei, analogous to the role of phonon exchange in metallic superconductivity. The results enable future systematic studies of two-particle transfer reactions on drip-line nuclei to probe pairing correlations.
