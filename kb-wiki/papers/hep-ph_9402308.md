# On The $Q^2$ Dependence of The Spin Structure Function In The Resonance Region

> In this paper, we show what we can learn from the CEBAF experiments on spin-structure functions, and the transition from the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule in the real photon limit to the spin dependent sum rules in the deep inelastic scattering, and how the asymmetry $A_1(x,Q^2)$ approaches the scaling limit in the resonance region. The spin structure function in the resonance region alone can not determine the spin-dependent sum rule due to the kinematic restriction of the resonance region. The integral $\int_0^1 \frac {A_1(x,Q^2)F_2(x,Q^2)}{2x(1+R(x,Q^2))}dx$ is estimated from $Q^2=0$ to $2.5$ GeV$^2$. The result shows that there is a region where both contributions from the baryon resonances and the deep inelastic scattering are important, thus provides important information on the high twist effects on the spin dependent sum rule.

## Key claim

The spin structure function in the resonance region alone cannot determine the spin-dependent sum rule due to kinematic restrictions; the integral \(I(Q^2)=\int_0^1 \frac {A_1(x,Q^2)F_2(x,Q^2)}{2x(1+R(x,Q^2))}dx\) is estimated from \(Q^2=0\) to \(2.5\) GeV\(^2\), showing a region where both baryon resonance and deep inelastic contributions are important, providing information on high twist effects.

## Method

The authors define a variable \(x = \nu_{th}/\nu\) that reduces to the Bjorken scaling variable at large \(Q^2\) and use it to connect the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov (DHG) sum rule at the real photon limit to spin-dependent sum rules in deep inelastic scattering. They extract transverse photo-coupling amplitudes \(A_{1/2}(Q^2)\) and \(A_{3/2}(Q^2)\) for prominent baryon resonances from existing pion and \(\eta\) electroproduction data in the range \(0 \le Q^2 \le 3.0\) GeV\(^2\), and use a single quark transition model for weaker resonances. The integral \(I(Q^2)\) is then computed by combining resonance contributions with non-resonance contributions from deep inelastic scattering parameterizations for \(Q^2 \ge 0.75\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The DHG sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(\int_0^1 g_1(x,Q^2=0)dx = -0.123\) for protons and \(-0.14\) for neutrons, using anomalous magnetic moments \(\kappa_p=1.79\) and \(\kappa_n=-1.91\).
- The experimental extraction for protons at \(Q^2=0\) yields \(\int^1_0 g_1^p(x,Q^2=0)dx = -0.126 \pm 0.011\).
- In the region \(1.0 \le Q^2 \le 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), the \(Q^2\) dependence of \(I_p(Q^2)\) is parameterized as \(I_p(Q^2)=a+b/Q^2\) with \(a \approx 0.139 \sim 0.144\) and \(b \approx -0.03 \sim -0.04\) GeV\(^2\).
- The resonance region covers \(x = 0.11 \sim 1\) at \(Q^2=0\), but only \(x = 0.57 \sim 1\) at \(Q^2=2.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The non-resonance contribution accounts for 15 to 20 percent of \(I(Q^2)\) in the region \(1.0 \le Q^2 \le 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the \(Q^2\) extension of the DHG sum rule by Burkert and collaborators, and addresses the transition from the negative DHG sum rule at the real photon limit to the positive spin-dependent sum rules in deep inelastic scattering. The authors show that the resonance region alone is insufficient for accurate sum rule extraction, and that for \(1.0 \le Q^2 \le 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) both resonance and deep inelastic contributions are important, enabling reliable study of leading high twist effects. The results are consistent with the analysis of Close and Roberts (1993) and the QCD sum rule prediction by Balitsky et al., and the framework is designed to be tested by future CEBAF experiments.
