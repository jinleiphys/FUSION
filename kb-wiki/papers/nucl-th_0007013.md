# Spin Structure of Many-Body Systems with Two-Body Random Interactions

> We investigate the spin structure of many-fermion systems with a spin-conserving two-body random interaction. We find a strong dominance of spin-0 ground states and considerable correlations between energies and wave functions of low-lying states with different spin, but no indication of pairing. The spectral densities exhibit spin-dependent shapes and widths, and depend on the relative strengths of the spin-0 and spin-1 couplings in the two-body random matrix. The spin structure of low-lying states can largely be explained analytically.

## Key claim

The paper finds that spin-0 ground states dominate in many-fermion systems with spin-conserving two-body random interactions, but this dominance is conditional on the relative strengths of the spin-0 and spin-1 couplings and on the particle density; for sufficiently strong spin-1 coupling in the dilute limit, higher-spin ground states can become favored.

## Method

The authors study systems of N spin-1/2 fermions distributed over M orbitals, interacting via a random two-body Hamiltonian that conserves total spin. They consider two models: one where the spin-0 and spin-1 two-body matrix elements are independent GOE ensembles with relative strengths C0 and C1, and another where the interaction is separated into spin-independent and spin-dependent parts with random Gaussian matrix elements. They compute ensemble-averaged spectral widths and fourth moments analytically for sectors of definite total many-body spin S, and perform numerical diagonalizations for systems up to M=12 orbitals and N=4-6 particles.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 fermions on M=4 sites with pure spin-0 coupling (C0=1, C1=0), the ground state has total spin S=0 in 99.8% of 2000 ensemble members.
- For N=4 fermions on M=4 sites with pure spin-1 coupling (C1=1, C0=0), the ground state has total spin S=0 in 99.75% of the ensemble.
- For N=4 particles on M=5 sites, all 2000 computed ground states had total spin S=0, independent of whether pure J=0 or J=1 two-body coupling was used.
- For N=4 particles with pure J=1 coupling, the width ratio sqrt(Tr_0 H^2)/sqrt(Tr_1 H^2) crosses over near M=11, with larger S=0 width for M<11 and larger S>0 widths for M >= 11.
- For N=4 particles with pure J=1 coupling, the crossover between S=1 and S=2 width dominance occurs at M=9.
- For M=10 orbitals with pure J=1 coupling, the ratio <Tr H^4>/<Tr H^2>^2 equals 2.42, 2.18, and 2.15 for total many-body spin S=0, 1, and 2 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of regular structures in low-lying states of nuclear models with random two-body interactions, previously observed in shell model calculations (Johnson et al., 1999) and random interacting boson models (Bijker et al., 1999). It provides an analytical explanation for the dominance of spin-0 ground states seen in those studies, using a simpler spin-1/2 fermion model that allows exact counting of spectral moments. The analysis complements work on mesoscopic systems (Jacquod, 1999) and enables understanding of when the spin-0 ground state dominance breaks down, particularly in the dilute limit with strong spin-1 coupling. The paper finds no evidence for pairing correlations in the wave functions, in contrast to some earlier random-interaction studies.
