# Remarks on the non-equilibrium effects and collision dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the beam energy dependence of equilibration process and space-time characteristics of participant and spectator matter. For this, we simulated the semi-central collisions of $^{40}Ca+ ^{40}Ca$ at incident energies of 400, 600 and 1000 AMeV within the \emph{quantum molecular dynamics} (QMD) approach. Our numerical calculations based on the molecular dynamics approach show that incident energy of the projectile influences the reaction observables drastically. The effect is more visible for transverse expansion of the nuclear matter and transparency behavior. The degree of thermalization of participant matter, however, remains independent of the incident energy. The characteristics of the trajectories followed by the nucleons suffering maximal and minimal binary collisions are also analyzed.

## Key claim

The degree of thermalization (equilibrium) achieved by the participant zone in semicentral Ca+Ca collisions is independent of incident energy across 400, 600, and 1000 AMeV, but full equilibrium is never attained even at the highest energy, with approximately 25% anisotropy remaining in the momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors performed quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of semicentral collisions of 40Ca+40Ca at incident energies of 400, 600, and 1000 AMeV at a reduced impact parameter b/b_max=0.3. They used a soft equation of state with incompressibility K=200 MeV and analyzed the time evolution of average nucleon density, collision rate, directed transverse momentum, and momentum anisotropy ratio for the participant zone. They also tracked individual nucleons facing maximal and minimal numbers of binary collisions to study their trajectories in phase space.

## Key numbers

- Average nucleon density reaches maximum at 1000 AMeV, with the highest central density among the three energies studied (400, 600, 1000 AMeV)
- Momentum anisotropy ratio ⟨R_iso⟩ converges to the same value at all three incident energies (400, 600, and 1000 AMeV), indicating approximately 25% anisotropy in momentum distribution
- A participant nucleon suffering 14 collisions (n_coll=14) is stopped around mid-rapidity with y/y_beam=0.25
- A spectator nucleon with zero collisions (n_coll=0) remains at projectile beam rapidity with y/y_beam=1
- Nuclear matter at density ρ>1.5ρ_0 lasts for less than 20 fm/c (cited from prior work)

## Context

This work extends QMD-based studies of heavy-ion collision dynamics at intermediate energies, building on prior simulations of collective flow, multifragment emission, and thermalization by Vermani, Puri, and others. The finding that equilibration of participant matter is independent of incident energy in semicentral Ca+Ca collisions contradicts the assumption of thermal equilibrium used in earlier theoretical predictions by Braun and Andronic. The results enable further study of spectator-participant coupling and the limitations of single-fireball models and hydrodynamical prescriptions for describing non-equilibrium dynamics.
