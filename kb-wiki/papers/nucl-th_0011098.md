# Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance and Nuclear Matter Incompressibility Coefficient

> We present results of microscopic calculations of the strength function, S(E), and alpha-particle excitation cross sections sigma(E) for the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR). An accurate and a general method to eliminate the contributions of spurious state mixing is presented and used in the calculations. Our results provide a resolution to the long standing problem that the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient, K, deduced from sigma(E) data for the ISGDR is significantly smaller than that deduced from data for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR).

## Key claim

The authors resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient \(K\) deduced from the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) and that from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) by showing that the missing high-energy ISGDR strength (above the experimental sensitivity threshold) leads to an apparent reduction of more than 2.5 MeV in the ISGDR centroid energy, thereby reconciling theory and experiment.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) based random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations using the SL1 Skyrme interaction (associated with \(K=230\) MeV) in a spherical box with a Green's function approach, including particle-hole states up to 150 MeV. They developed an accurate projection method that replaces the scattering operator \(F\) with a modified operator \(F_{\eta}=F-\eta F_1\) to eliminate contributions from spurious state mixing (SSM) in the strength function and transition density. They then calculated \(\alpha\)-particle excitation cross sections \(\sigma(E)\) for the ISGDR using the folding model (FM) distorted-wave-Born-approximation (DWBA) with transition densities from the HF-RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

* The ISGMR centroid energies calculated with the SL1 interaction are 17.09 MeV for \(^{116}\)Sn and 14.48 MeV for \(^{208}\)Pb; the corresponding experimental values are \(16.07 \pm 0.12\) MeV and \(14.17 \pm 0.28\) MeV, respectively.
* The spurious state mixing correction parameter for the ISGDR operator \(F_3\) is \(\eta = \frac{5}{3}\langle r^2\rangle\), giving \(\eta = 35.6\) fm\(^2\) for \(^{116}\)Sn and \(\eta = 52.1\) fm\(^2\) for \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The low-energy component of the ISGDR strength function contains close to 30% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for energies up to 23 MeV in \(^{116}\)Sn and 19 MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The maximum cross section for the ISGDR drops below the experimental sensitivity of 2 mb/sr/MeV for excitation energies above 35 MeV in \(^{116}\)Sn and above 30 MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb, where about 20% of the EWSR resides.
* The missing high-energy strength leads to a reduction of more than 2.5 MeV in the ISGDR centroid energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier HF-RPA calculations for the ISGMR (Shlomo 1993) and addresses the discrepancy first pointed out by Dumitrescu and Serr (1983) that HF-RPA results for the ISGDR centroid energy were higher than experimental values by more than 3 MeV. The authors improve upon the SSM correction method of Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1998) and provide a framework that enables consistent extraction of \(K\) from both ISGDR and ISGMR data, suggesting that more sensitive experiments or higher \(\alpha\)-particle energies are needed to observe the missing high-energy strength.
