# Applications of a nonlinear evolution equation I: the parton distributions in the proton

> The parton distributions in the proton are evaluated dynamically using a nonlinear QCD evolution equation - the DGLAP equation with twist-4 (the GLR-MQ-ZSR) corrections - starting from a low scale $μ^2$, where the nucleon consists of valence quarks. We find that the negative nonlinear corrections can improve the perturbative stability of the QCD evolution equation at low $Q^2$. Our resulting parton distributions of the proton with four free parameters are compatible with the existing databases. We show that the sea quark distributions exhibit a positive and flattish behavior at small $x$ and low $Q^2$. This approach provides a powerful tool to connect the quark models of the hadron and various non-perturbative effects on them at scale $μ^2$ with the measured structure functions at high scale $Q^2>> μ^2$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that using the DGLAP equation with the ZRS (Zhu-Ruan-Shen) nonlinear parton recombination corrections, starting from a natural input of only valence quarks at a low scale, produces parton distributions compatible with existing databases, but they note that their predicted structure functions are generally lower than experimental data at Bjorken x below 10^{-4}, implying that BFKL corrections are not negligible in that very small x range.

## Method

The authors solved the DGLAP evolution equations modified by the ZRS twist-4 parton recombination corrections at leading logarithmic (LL(Q^2)) approximation, using time-ordered perturbative theory (TOPT) cutting rules rather than the AGK cutting rule used in the earlier GLR-MQ approach. They started from a low scale mu^2 = 0.064 GeV^2 with only valence quark distributions (u and d) parameterized by four free parameters, with gluon and sea quark distributions set to zero at the input scale, and evolved the equations to higher Q^2 to generate the full parton distributions dynamically.

## Key numbers

- Starting scale: mu^2 = 0.064 GeV^2, with Lambda_QCD = 0.204 GeV for f=3 flavors; the inverse scale 1/mu ~ 0.8 fm is compatible with the proton radius.
- Valence quark input parameters: A_u = 24.30, B_u = 1.98, C_u = 2.06; A_d = 9.10, B_d = 1.31, C_d = 3.80.
- Correlation length parameter: R = 4.24 GeV^{-1} for best fit to data.
- The GRV model starting point is Q^2 ~ 0.26 GeV^2, compared to the authors' mu^2 = 0.064 GeV^2.
- Comparisons with GRV(98LO) and CJ12 databases are shown at Q^2 = 1.4 GeV^2 and other scales.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical parton model approach originally proposed in 1977 by Gluck, Reya, and Vogt (GRV), who found that natural valence-only inputs failed due to overly steep small-x behavior and instead used valence-like sea and gluon inputs. The authors replace those valence-like inputs with the ZRS nonlinear corrections (Zhu, Ruan, and Shen, 2000s) to the DGLAP equation, which include both shadowing and antishadowing terms and conserve momentum, unlike the earlier GLR-MQ corrections (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin; Mueller and Qiu) which violated momentum conservation. This approach enables connecting quark model predictions at low scales (e.g., the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) to measured structure functions at high Q^2, and provides a framework for studying nuclear shadowing effects in future work.
