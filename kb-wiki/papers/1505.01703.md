# Effective Field Theory of Emergent Symmetry Breaking in Deformed Atomic Nuclei

> Spontaneous symmetry breaking in non-relativistic quantum systems has previously been addressed in the framework of effective field theory. Low-lying excitations are constructed from Nambu-Goldstone modes using symmetry arguments only. We extend that approach to finite systems. The approach is very general. To be specific, however, we consider atomic nuclei with intrinsically deformed ground states. The emergent symmetry breaking in such systems requires the introduction of additional degrees of freedom on top of the Nambu-Goldstone modes. Symmetry arguments suffice to construct the low-lying states of the system. In deformed nuclei these are vibrational modes each of which serves as band head of a rotational band.

## Key claim

The paper extends effective field theory (EFT) treatments of spontaneous symmetry breaking from infinite non-relativistic quantum systems to finite systems such as deformed atomic nuclei, where strict spontaneous symmetry breaking does not occur and one must instead speak of "emergent symmetry breaking." The key technical novelty is that finite systems require the introduction of additional degrees of freedom (purely time-dependent rotational modes) on top of the standard Nambu-Goldstone modes, and these rotational modes cannot be treated in a small-amplitude approximation.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian based on the nonlinear Nambu-Goldstone realization of the broken SO(3) symmetry (reduced to SO(2) axial symmetry), parameterizing the coset space SO(3)/SO(2) with two complex fields π±. They decompose the fields into purely time-dependent components (ω₀, ζ₀) describing global rotations and angle-dependent components (ω₁, ζ₁) describing surface vibrations, then expand the Lagrangian in powers of the small-amplitude vibrational fields while treating rotational degrees of freedom exactly. A second parameterization using product-form variables (g(ζ,ω) for rotations and u(x,y) for vibrations) is introduced to obtain a clearer physical interpretation and simpler quantization.

## Key numbers

- The energy scales satisfy ξ ≪ Ω ≪ Λ, where ξ is the scale of rotational motion, Ω is the scale of vibrational motion, and Λ is the cutoff beyond which other modes (e.g., fermionic excitations) appear.
- The vibrational amplitudes scale as ω_L, ζ_L ∼ √(ξ/Ω) for L ≥ 2, making them much smaller than unity when ξ ≪ Ω.
- The time derivatives of vibrational modes scale as ω̇_L, ζ̇_L ∼ √(ξΩ) for L ≥ 2.
- The coefficient C (moment of inertia) scales as C ∼ 1/ξ, and the coefficient D scales as D ∼ 1/ξ.
- For purely rotational motion, the Hamiltonian reduces to H = (1/2C) Σ_k Q²_k, where C is the moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach to spontaneous symmetry breaking developed for infinite systems by Leutwyler (1994), Weinberg, and Brauner (2010), and for non-relativistic systems such as ferromagnets by Roman and Baer (1999, 2004). It builds upon the authors' own short summary (Papenbrock 2014) and their earlier effective theory of deformed nuclei (Papenbrock 2011). The approach is designed to systematically generate higher-order corrections beyond the leading-order descriptions of the collective geometric model (Bohr 1975) and the algebraic model (Iachello), which fail to account quantitatively for next-to-leading order effects such as the change of moment of inertia with vibrational band head or weak E2 inter-band transitions. The paper establishes a field-theoretical foundation for these effective theories and enables systematic inclusion of higher-order terms.
