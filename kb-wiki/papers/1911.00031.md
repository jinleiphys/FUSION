# Table of electronic factors for E0 electron and electron-positron pair conversion transitions

> A new tabulation of electronic factors is reported for electron conversion for elements of Z from 5 to 126 and electronic factors for electron-positron pair conversion for elements of even Z from 4 to 100. The electronic factors for electron conversion, $Ω_{CE}$(E0), were calculated using a modified version of the CATAR program developed by Pauli and Raff with a relativistic-Hartree-Fock-Slater approach (Pauli and Raff, 1975). The electronic factors for electron-positron pair conversion, $Ω_{IPF}$(E0), were calculated using the model developed by Wilkinson (1969). The data tables presented here cover all atomic shells up to R2 and transition energies from 1 keV to 6000 keV and from 1100 keV to 8000 keV for pair conversion. A comparison with previous electronic factor tabulations is presented. Ratios of experimental Ω(E0) values for 83 E0 transitions in 8$\leq$Z$\leq$98 are compared to this tabulation. Two examples of how to use the tabulation to extract E0 strengths are also included.

## Key claim
This paper provides a new, comprehensive tabulation of electronic factors for E0 electron conversion (for Z=5 to 126) and electron-positron pair conversion (for even Z=4 to 100), calculated using a relativistic-Hartree-Fock-Slater approach and the Wilkinson model, respectively. The authors claim improved accuracy over previous tabulations, but the comparison with 83 experimental E0 transitions shows that the theoretical ratios often differ from experiment by more than 10%, with some discrepancies exceeding 30%.

## Method
Electronic factors for electron conversion, $\Omega_{CE}$(E0), were calculated using a modified version of the CATAR program (Pauli and Raff, 1975) with a relativistic-Hartree-Fock-Slater approach. Electronic factors for electron-positron pair conversion, $\Omega_{IPF}$(E0), were calculated using the model developed by Wilkinson (1969). The resulting data tables cover all atomic shells up to R2 for transition energies from 1 keV to 6000 keV (electron conversion) and from 1100 keV to 8000 keV (pair conversion).

## Key numbers
- Electron conversion electronic factors are tabulated for elements with atomic numbers Z from 5 to 126.
- Electron-positron pair conversion electronic factors are tabulated for elements with even Z from 4 to 100.
- The data tables cover transition energies from 1 keV to 6000 keV for electron conversion.
- The data tables cover transition energies from 1100 keV to 8000 keV for pair conversion.
- The tabulation was compared to experimental $\Omega$(E0) values for 83 E0 transitions in nuclei with $8 \leq Z \leq 98$.

## Context
This work extends the earlier tabulations of electronic factors for E0 transitions, notably those by Pauli and Raff (1975) and Wilkinson (1969), by providing a more modern and comprehensive dataset covering a wider range of Z and transition energies. The new tabulation enables more accurate extraction of E0 transition strengths from experimental conversion electron and pair conversion data, which is critical for studying nuclear shapes and monopole transitions. The comparison with experimental data from sources such as Eriksen (2018) and others highlights that while the new calculations are generally reliable, significant discrepancies remain for some nuclei, pointing to the need for further theoretical or experimental investigation.
