# Constraint on parity-violating muonic forces

> Using the nonobservance of missing mass events in the leptonic kaon decay $K \to μX$, we place a strong constraint on exotic parity-violating gauge interactions of the right-handed muon. By way of illustration, we apply it to an explanation of the proton size anomaly that invokes such a new force; scenarios in which the gauge boson decays invisibly or is long-lived are constrained.

## Key claim
The paper places a strong constraint on parity-violating gauge interactions coupling to the right-handed muon, specifically for gauge boson masses below about 100 MeV, using the nonobservation of missing mass events in leptonic kaon decay. The constraint applies to scenarios where the gauge boson decays invisibly or is long-lived, but does not apply to the minimal version of the model where the boson decays promptly to electron-positron pairs.

## Method
The authors calculated the differential decay rate for the process K- → μ- V ν̅μ, where V is a light vector boson coupled to the right-handed muon via bremsstrahlung from the muon line. They normalized this rate to the standard two-body muonic kaon decay rate and compared the resulting missing mass distribution to the 90% confidence level upper limit from a null search for missing mass events in K → μX decays by Pang et al. (1989). The constraint was derived by integrating over the experimental acceptance range for muon kinetic energy (60 MeV to 100 MeV) and determining the excluded region in the (mV, gR) parameter space.

## Key numbers
- The experimental 90% CL upper limit on the branching fraction for missing mass events in the mass interval 227.6 MeV to 302.2 MeV is 3.5 × 10^{-6}, corresponding to a normalized differential fraction of 4.7 × 10^{-8} MeV^{-1}.
- The muon kinetic energy acceptance range is 60 MeV to 100 MeV, corresponding to a missing mass range from 302.2 MeV down to 227.6 MeV, a mass interval of 74.6 MeV.
- The experimental sensitivity for missing mass events is 10^{-7} MeV^{-1}.
- The kaon decay constant fK used in the calculation is 156.1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends constraints on new gauge interactions coupled to the right-handed muon, specifically targeting a model proposed by Batell et al. (2011) to explain the proton radius anomaly from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements by Pohl et al. (2010). The constraint is complementary to searches by the KLOE-2 collaboration for V → e+e- decays and to measurements of muonic X-ray transitions in 24Mg and 28Si by Beltrami et al. The result enables further discrimination between viable and excluded parameter space for models attempting to explain the proton size anomaly with new muonic forces.
