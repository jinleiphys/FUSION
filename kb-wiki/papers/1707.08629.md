# Quarkonium Polarization and the Long Distance Matrix Elements Hierarchies using Jet Substructure

> We investigate the quarkonium production mechanisms in jets at the LHC, using the Fragmenting Jet Functions (FJF) approach. Specifically, we discuss the jet energy dependence of the $J/ψ$ production cross section at the LHC. By comparing the cross sections for the different NRQCD production channels ($^1S_0^{[8]}$,$^3S_1^{[8]}$,$^3P_J^{[8]}$, and $^3S_1^{[1]}$), we find that at fixed values of energy fraction $z$ carried by the $J/ψ$, if the normalized cross section is a decreasing function of the jet energy, in particular for $z > 0.5$, then the depolarizing $^1S_0^{[8]}$ must be the dominant channel. This makes the prediction made in [Baumgart et al., JHEP 1411, 003 (2014)] for the FJF's also true for the cross section. We also make comparisons between the long distance matrix elements extracted by various groups. This analysis could potentially shed light on the polarization properties of the $J/ψ$ production in high $p_T$ region.

## Key claim

If the normalized cross section for J/ψ production in jets is a decreasing function of the jet energy for energy fraction z > 0.5, then the depolarizing ¹S₀⁽⁸⁾ channel must be the dominant production channel at high pT; this prediction, originally made for Fragmenting Jet Functions by Baumgart et al. (2014), also holds for the total cross section after including hard scattering effects.

## Method

The authors calculate the total cross section for J/ψ production in jets at the LHC using the Fragmenting Jet Function (FJF) approach, including both gluon and charm quark initiated jets. They compare the cross sections for the four NRQCD production channels (¹S₀⁽⁸⁾, ³S₁⁽⁸⁾, ³P_J⁽⁸⁾, and ³S₁⁽¹⁾) using long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) extracted by three different groups (Bodwin et al., Butenschoen et al., and Chao et al.). The cross section is normalized in two ways: by summing over all channels and integrating over z, and by using the 1-jet inclusive cross section as denominator.

## Context

This work extends the diagnostic tool proposed by Baumgart et al. (2014) for Fragmenting Jet Functions to the total cross section, making the prediction directly testable with LHC data. The analysis addresses the J/ψ polarization puzzle, where NRQCD predictions of significant transverse polarization at high pT contradict measurements from the Tevatron (Abulencia, 2007) and LHC (Chatrchyan, 2013; Aaij, 2013). The method can distinguish between different LDME extractions, particularly those from global fits (Butenschoen et al., 2011, 2012) versus high-pT fits (Bodwin et al., 2014; Chao et al., 2012), and enables experimental verification of the dominant production mechanism.
