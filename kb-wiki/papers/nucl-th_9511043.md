# Spectral Decorrelation of Nuclear Levels in the Presence of Continuum Decay

> The fluctuation properties of nuclear giant resonance spectra are studied in the presence of continuum decay. The subspace of quasi-bound states is specified by one-particle one-hole and two-particle two-hole excitations and the continuum coupling is generated by a scattering ensemble. It is found that, with increasing number of open channels, the real parts of the complex eigenvalues quickly decorrelate. This appears to be related to the transition from power-law to exponential time behavior of the survival probability of an initially non-stationary state.

# Spectral Decorrelation of Nuclear Levels in the Presence of Continuum Decay

## Key claim
The real parts of complex eigenvalues of quasi-bound nuclear states quickly decorrelate as the number of open decay channels increases, transitioning from GOE (Wigner) to Poissonian fluctuation statistics. This decorrelation occurs even when the ratio of open channels to quasi-bound states is only a few percent, and is linked to a transition from power-law to exponential decay of the survival probability.

## Method
The authors constructed a 1661×1661 Hamiltonian matrix for quadrupole excitations (J^π=2^+) in ^40Ca, using a basis of one-particle one-hole (1p1h) and two-particle two-hole (2p2h) states up to 40 MeV excitation energy. The hermitian part was generated from a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian with mean-field and two-body interactions, while the anti-hermitian part was modeled by a Gaussian ensemble of real amplitudes A^a_i with equal channel strengths (γ^a=1). The complex eigenvalues were computed for varying numbers of open channels k, and the nearest-neighbor-spacing distribution (NNSD) and Δ_3-statistics were analyzed for the real parts of the energies.

## Key numbers
- Matrix dimension: 1661 × 1661 (all 1p1h and 2p2h states up to 40 MeV in ^40Ca)
- For k=10 open channels (k/N ≈ 6×10^{-3}), visible deviation from the Wigner distribution is already observed in the NNSD of the real parts
- For k=1 open channel, the survival probability decays as a power law P(t) ~ t^{-z} with z ≈ -1/2
- For k > 100 open channels, the survival probability becomes exponential: P(t) ~ exp(-η t), with η growing rapidly with k
- The transition region L_max for the Δ_3-statistics from GOE to Poissonian characteristics is restricted to about 10 normalized distance units

## Context
This work extends the random-matrix treatment of open quantum systems developed by Verbaarschot, Weidenmüller, and collaborators (1985) and by Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989), who predicted that continuum coupling could wash out level repulsion. The authors use a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian (based on the embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble approach) rather than a pure GOE, following their earlier work (Drożdż et al., 1994) which showed that the spectral fluctuations of the closed system coincide with GOE. The results enable quantitative understanding of how the transition from isolated to overlapping resonances (Ericson fluctuations) modifies fluctuation properties in nuclear giant resonances, and connect to semiclassical relations between survival probability and resonance structure (Blümel and Smilansky, 1990).
