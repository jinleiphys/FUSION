# Double beta decay transition mechanism

> After briefly reviewing $ββ$ decay as a test of the neutrino mass, I examine the nuclear structure involved in this process. Simple formulas (à la Padé) are designed for the transition amplitudes and the general behavior of $ββ$ decay amplitudes in the quasiparticle random phase approximation are discussed. Results of a calculation for $^{48}Ca$, $^{76}Ge $, $^{82}Se$, $^{100}Mo$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$ nuclei are presented, in which the particle-particle interaction strengths have been fixed by invoking the partial restoration of the isospin and Wigner SU(4) symmetries. An upper limit of $ <m_ν>\approx 1$ eV is obtained for the effective neutrino mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the particle-particle (PP) interaction strength in QRPA calculations for double beta decay can be fixed by invoking the partial restoration of isospin and Wigner SU(4) symmetries, rather than by fitting individual beta-decay data; this yields an upper limit for the effective neutrino mass of approximately 1 eV, but the author notes that the calculated two-neutrino matrix elements vary abruptly near the chosen symmetry-restoration point and that the procedure remains somewhat arbitrary, especially for heavier nuclei where SU(4) is badly broken.

## Method

The author uses the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a delta-force residual interaction to compute the two-neutrino (2ν) and neutrinoless (0ν) double beta decay matrix elements for six nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹²⁸Te, ¹³⁰Te). A single-mode model (SMM) is introduced to derive analytic Padé-type formulas for the transition amplitudes as functions of the particle-particle strength parameter t (or g^pp). The PP strength is fixed by requiring minimal GT β⁺ strength, interpreted as maximal restoration of the Wigner SU(4) symmetry, and the resulting matrix elements are used to extract limits on the effective neutrino mass from experimental half-lives.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on effective neutrino mass: ⟨m_ν⟩ ≈ 1 eV, obtained from the calculated matrix elements and measured 0ν half-lives.
- For ⁸²Se, the calculated 2ν matrix element at t = t_sym "does not contradict the experimental limit" and the 2ν measurement is "well accounted for by the theory."
- The calculated 2ν matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge and ¹⁰⁰Mo are "too small" by a factor of ≈3, and for ¹²⁸Te and ¹³⁰Te "too large" by a factor of ≈3, relative to experiment.
- The 0ν half-life sensitivity improved from ~5×10¹⁵ y in 1948 to ~5×10²³ y in 1987.
- The energy difference between GT and F resonances is given by E_GT − E_F = (26 A^(−1/3) − 18.5 (N−Z)/A) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA treatment of double beta decay by Krmpotić, Hirata, and collaborators (Krm92, Krm93, Hir90, Krm94) and addresses the long-standing problem of fixing the particle-particle interaction strength, which had previously been calibrated using individual GT positron decays (e.g., Grotz and Klapdor, 1992). The paper contrasts its symmetry-restoration approach with the Pasadena, Tübingen, and Heidelberg groups' calculations, noting a factor of 2–3 spread in extracted neutrino mass limits. It enables future work by providing analytic Padé formulas that capture the universal behavior of QRPA matrix elements, and by showing that the SU(4) symmetry broken by the spin-orbit field can be partially restored by the residual interaction.
