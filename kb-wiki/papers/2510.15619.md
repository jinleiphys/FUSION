# Faddeev-type calculation of nonelastic breakup in deuteron-nucleus scattering

> The nonelastic breakup (NEB), one of channels in $(d,p)$ inclusive reactions, is studied using the Faddeev-type scattering theory. The NEB differential cross section is obtained in terms of the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus optical potential sandwiched between the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas three-body transition operators. The momentum-space calculations including the Coulomb force are extended to higher charge numbers. Well converged numerical results are obtained for the energy distribution of the NEB cross section, being roughly consistent with previous works. The spin-dependent interaction terms do not play a significant role. The optical potential nonlocality effect shows up at higher proton energies, but is comparable to local potential uncertainties.

# Faddeev-type calculation of nonelastic breakup in deuteron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross section can be calculated using Faddeev-type three-body scattering theory via the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) transition operators, with the NEB differential cross section expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus optical potential. The results are roughly consistent with previous works, though the Faddeev-type approach predicts cross sections 10 to 35% higher at the peak than prior distorted-wave Born-approximation (DWBA) and Glauber model calculations.

## Method

The author solves the AGS integral equations for three-body transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, including the Coulomb force via the screening and renormalization method. The NEB differential cross section is derived by applying the spectator assumption for the detected proton, closure over final states of the undetected neutron-nucleus subsystem, and projection onto the ground state of the target nucleus, yielding an expression involving the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus optical potential sandwiched between half-shell breakup operators. Calculations use a simple Gaussian neutron-proton potential and several nucleon-nucleus optical potential parametrizations (Koning-Delaroche, Weppner, Watson, Chapel Hill CH89, and Giannini-Ricco), with orbital angular momenta up to 3, 12, and 22 for the neutron-proton, neutron-nucleus, and proton-nucleus pairs respectively, and total angular momentum up to 35.

## Key numbers

- For 80 MeV deuteron scattering from ⁵⁸Ni, the Faddeev-type NEB cross section at the peak is 10% higher than the results of Moro and Lei (2015) and 35% higher than those of Torabi and Carlson (2023)
- Coulomb screening convergence requires R = 6 fm for ¹²C (Z=6) at 50 MeV, but at least R = 15 fm for ⁹⁰Zr (Z=40) at 80 MeV
- Calculations are extended to nuclei with charge numbers up to Z=40 (⁹⁰Zr), compared to previous Faddeev-type calculations limited to ⁵⁸Ni with Z=28
- The NEB cross section for 80 MeV deuteron scattering shows increase with Z across ⁵⁸Ni, ⁷⁴Ge, ⁸⁴Kr, and ⁹⁰Zr, though not strictly linear

## Context

This work extends rigorous Faddeev-type three-body calculations, previously applied to elastic, inelastic, transfer, and breakup reactions by Deltuva (2007, 2013), to the nonelastic breakup channel in deuteron-nucleus inclusive reactions. It contrasts with prior NEB approaches that used distorted-wave Born-approximation (DWBA) or Glauber model wave functions, as in the works of Jia et al. (2011), Moro and Lei (2015), Potel et al. (2017), Liu et al. (2023), and Torabi and Carlson (2023), as well as the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method of Lei et al. (2015). The formalism enables future benchmark comparisons between different NEB methods and provides a pathway toward applying Faddeev-type momentum-space equations to medium-mass and heavy nuclei, with preliminary studies suggesting machine learning methods could further extend the reach to higher charge numbers.
