# Flavour symmetry breaking and meson masses

> The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is used to derive mass formulae for neutral pseudoscalar mesons. Flavour symmetry breaking entails non-ideal flavour content for these states. Adding that the η^\prime is not a Goldstone mode, exact chiral-limit relations are developed from the identity. They connect the dressed-quark propagator to the topological susceptibility. It is confirmed that in the chiral limit the η^\prime mass is proportional to the matrix element which connects this state to the vacuum via the topological susceptibility. The implications of the mass formulae are illustrated using an elementary dynamical model, which includes an Ansatz for that part of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel related to the non-Abelian anomaly. In addition to the current-quark masses, the model involves two parameters, one of which is a mass-scale. It is employed in an analysis of pseudoscalar- and vector-meson bound-states. While the effects of SU(N_f=2) and SU(N_f=3) flavour symmetry breaking are emphasised, the five-flavour spectra are described. Despite its simplicity, the model is elucidative and phenomenologically efficacious; e.g., it predicts η-η^\prime mixing angles of ~ (-15 degrees) and π^0-ηangles of ~ 1 degree.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact mass formulae for neutral pseudoscalar mesons from the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, showing that the eta-prime meson is split from the Goldstone modes by a term proportional to the topological susceptibility; this splitting persists in the chiral limit only if the topological susceptibility contribution is nonzero, and the authors illustrate these exact results using a simple dynamical model that includes an Ansatz for the part of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel related to the non-Abelian anomaly.

## Method

The authors use the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity to derive mass formulae for neutral pseudoscalar mesons, incorporating the non-Abelian anomaly term. They then implement a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach with a rainbow-ladder truncation for the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using a simple delta-function effective interaction (Munczek 1983). To model anomaly effects beyond leading-order truncation, they add a novel contact interaction term to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel that has a "hairpin" structure representing purely gluonic intermediate states, with a nonperturbative mass-dependence from dynamical constituent-quark masses.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to meson bound-states beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation used in earlier studies such as Jain (1993) and Maris (1997), by incorporating a model for the non-Abelian anomaly contribution to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The exact mass formulae derived from the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity reproduce current algebra results of Gasser (1982) in the small quark-mass domain and confirm relationships between the eta-prime mass and topological susceptibility that have been observed in lattice QCD simulations by Bardeen (2000) and Ahmad (2005). The model enables predictions for the masses of B* mesons and estimates of the non-electromagnetic component of the neutron-proton mass difference, and provides a framework for studying how flavour symmetry breaking affects the full hadron spectrum including heavy-quark systems.
