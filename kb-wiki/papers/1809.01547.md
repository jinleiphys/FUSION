# Chiral anomaly in Weyl systems: no violation of classical conservation laws

> The anomalous term $\sim\mathbf{EB}$ in the balance of the chiral density can be rewritten as quantum current in the classical balance of density. Therefore it does not violate classical conservation laws as it is claimed to be caused by quantum fluctuations.

## Key claim

The anomalous term proportional to **E**·**B** in the chiral density balance can be exactly rewritten as the divergence of a quantum current, so it does not violate classical conservation laws despite being commonly interpreted as evidence of symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author starts from the standard heuristic derivation of the chiral anomaly rate \(dn_5/dt = (e^2/2\pi^2\hbar^2) \mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\) and then performs an algebraic manipulation using vector identities to express \(\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\) as a pure divergence. Specifically, using the vector potential representation \(\mathbf{B} = \nabla \times \mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathbf{E} = -\nabla\Phi - \dot{\mathbf{A}}\), the term is rewritten as \(-\nabla \cdot \mathbf{j}_{\text{anom}}\) with an explicit anomalous current \(\mathbf{j}_{\text{anom}}\). This shows the anomaly fits into the classical continuity equation \(\partial_t n_5 + \nabla \cdot (\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{j}_{\text{anom}}) = 0\).

## Key numbers

* The chiral anomaly rate is \(dn_5/dt = (e^2/2\pi^2\hbar^2) \mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\), derived from the product of longitudinal phase-space density \(p_F/2\pi\hbar\) and transverse Landau level density \(eB/2\pi\hbar\).
* The anomalous current is \(\mathbf{j}_{\text{anom}} = (e^2/2\pi^2\hbar^2) \left( \frac{1}{2} \dot{\mathbf{A}} \times \mathbf{A} - \Phi \nabla \times \mathbf{A} \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the author's own conserving chiral kinetic theory (Morawetz 2018) and contradicts the widespread interpretation that the \(\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\) term observed in Weyl semimetals (e.g., Xu 2015, Gooth 2017) signals violation of conservation laws or Lorentz symmetry. The result aligns with earlier formal work showing that the chiral anomaly can be expressed in a conserved form (Bardeen and Zumino 1984) and with Del Cima (2017) showing that infrared subtraction removes claimed Lorentz-violating terms. It enables a reinterpretation of experimental observations in Weyl semimetals as anomalous transport rather than fundamental symmetry breaking.
