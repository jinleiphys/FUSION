# Low Mass Thermal Dilepton Production at NLO in a Weakly Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We present a computation, within weakly-coupled thermal QCD, of the production rate of low invariant mass ($M^2 \sim g^2 T^2$) dileptons, at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the coupling (which is $O(g^3 e^2 T^2)$). This involves extending the NLO calculation of the photon rate which we recently presented to the case of small nonzero photon invariant mass. Numerical results are discussed and tabulated forms and code are provided for inclusion in hydrodynamical models. We find that NLO corrections can increase the dilepton rate by up to 30-40% relative to leading order. We find that the electromagnetic response of the plasma for real photons and for small invariant mass but high energy dilepton pairs (e.g., $M^2 < (300\:\mathrm{MeV})^2$ but $p_T > 1 \: \mathrm{GeV}$) are close enough that dilepton pair measurements really can serve as Ersatz photon measurements. We also present a matching a la Ghisoiu and Laine between our results and results at larger invariant masses.

## Key claim

The paper computes the next-to-leading order (NLO) thermal dilepton production rate for low invariant mass ($M^2 \sim g^2 T^2$) in weakly-coupled QCD, finding that NLO corrections can increase the rate by up to 30-40% relative to leading order, and that high-energy but low-mass dilepton pairs (e.g., $M^2 < (300\:\mathrm{MeV})^2$ with $p_T > 1\:\mathrm{GeV}$) probe essentially the same electromagnetic response as real photons, validating their use as ersatz photon measurements.

## Method

The authors extend their previous NLO calculation of the real photon production rate to the case of small nonzero photon invariant mass, working within weakly-coupled thermal QCD at a scale where $K^2 \sim g^2 T^2$. They treat the two distinct kinematic regions (elastic 2-to-2 scattering processes and collinear splitting processes with LPM resummation) separately, incorporating NLO corrections from soft gluon exchange, thermal mass shifts, and the new longitudinal photon contribution $g(\mathbf{p})$ that appears for timelike virtuality. The results are matched onto the large-invariant-mass NLO calculation of Laine and Ghisoiu using a matching procedure.

## Key numbers

* NLO corrections increase the dilepton rate by up to 30-40% relative to leading order.
* The calculation applies for invariant masses $M^2 < (300\:\mathrm{MeV})^2$ and transverse momenta $p_T > 1\:\mathrm{GeV}$.
* The Debye mass at leading order is $\md^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + n_f/6)$.
* The asymptotic quark mass is $\mm = g^2 T^2 C_F/4$.
* The NLO correction to the asymptotic quark mass is $\delta \mm = -2\md \mm / (\pi T)$.
* The leading-log coefficient $\Bb(k)$ for QCD with $uds$ quarks is $\Bb(k) = 8 \alpha_\mathrm{EM} n_f(k) g^2 T^2 / 3$.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order dilepton calculation of Aurenche et al. (2002) to NLO, building on the authors' own NLO real photon calculation (Ghiglieri and Moore, 2013) and the NLO large-invariant-mass dilepton calculation of Laine and Ghisoiu (2013-2014). The results enable more precise inclusion of dilepton rates in hydrodynamical models of heavy ion collisions and provide theoretical support for using low-mass dilepton measurements as substitutes for direct photon measurements, as done by the PHENIX collaboration. The calculation also informs lattice QCD efforts to reconstruct the real-frequency spectral function from Euclidean correlators by showing whether a sharp cusp at the lightlike point is expected.
