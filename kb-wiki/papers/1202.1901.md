# Neutrino and anti-neutrino transport in accretion disks

> We numerically solve the one dimensional Boltzmann equation of the neutrino and anti-neutrino transport in accretion disks and obtain the fully energy dependent and direction dependent neutrino and anti-neutrino emitting spectra, under condition that the distribution of the mass density,temperature and chemical components are given. Then, we apply the resulting neutrino and anti-neutrino emitting spectra to calculate the corresponding annihilation rate of neutrino pairs above the neutrino dominated accretion disk and find that the released energy resulting from the annihilation of neutrino pairs can not provide sufficient energy for the most energetic short gamma ray bursts whose isotropic luminosity can be as high as $10^{52}$ ergs/s unless the high temperature zone where the temperature is beyond 10 MeV can stretch over 200 km in the disk. We also compare the resulting luminosity of neutrinos and anti-neutrinos with the results from the two commonly used approximate treatment of the neutrino and anti-neutrino luminosity: the Fermi-Dirac black body limit and a simplified model of neutrino transport, i.e., the gray body model, and find that both of them overestimate the neutrino/anti-neutrino luminosity and their annihilation rate greatly. Additionally, as did in Sawyer (2003), we also check the validity of the two stream approximation, and find that it is a good approximation to high accuracy.

# Neutrino and anti-neutrino transport in accretion disks

## Key claim
The authors numerically solve the full one-dimensional Boltzmann equation for neutrino and anti-neutrino transport in neutrino-dominated accretion flows (NDAFs) and find that the commonly used Fermi-Dirac black body and gray body approximations overestimate the neutrino/anti-neutrino luminosity and their annihilation rate by factors of 5–7 and 10–49, respectively. The paper concludes that neutrino pair annihilation cannot power the most energetic short gamma-ray bursts (with isotropic luminosity up to $10^{52}$ ergs/s) unless the disk region with temperature above 10 MeV extends over at least 200 km.

## Method
The authors solve the one-dimensional Boltzmann equation for neutrino and anti-neutrino transport in the vertical direction of an infinite, homogeneous accretion disk on the equatorial plane, assuming an exponentially decreasing mass density profile, constant temperature, and constant electron fraction. They include absorption via charged-current processes ($\nu_e + n \leftrightarrow e^- + p$ and $\overline{\nu}_e + p \leftrightarrow e^+ + n$) and isotropic elastic scattering off neutrons and protons, using an iterative numerical method to obtain the full energy-dependent and direction-dependent spectra. The resulting spectra are then used to compute the neutrino pair annihilation rate above the disk.

## Key numbers
- For disk temperature $T=6$ MeV: total neutrino luminosity $L_{\nu_e} = 2.0 \times 10^{53}$ ergs/s, total anti-neutrino luminosity $L_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 0.7 \times 10^{53}$ ergs/s, annihilation luminosity $L_{\nu_e \overline{\nu}_e} = 2.3 \times 10^{50}$ ergs/s, annihilation efficiency $\eta \sim 0.1\%$.
- For disk temperature $T=10$ MeV: total neutrino luminosity $L_{\nu_e} = 7.2 \times 10^{53}$ ergs/s, total anti-neutrino luminosity $L_{\overline{\nu}_e} = 8.8 \times 10^{53}$ ergs/s, annihilation luminosity $L_{\nu_e \overline{\nu}_e} = 1.5 \times 10^{52}$ ergs/s, annihilation efficiency $\eta \sim 1\%$.
- The Fermi black body spectrum overestimates total neutrino/anti-neutrino luminosity by a factor of 5.4 at $T=6$ MeV and 7 at $T=10$ MeV; it overestimates the annihilation rate by factors of 29 and 49, respectively.
- The gray body model overestimates total luminosity by a factor of 3.2 at $T=6$ MeV and 5.8 at $T=10$ MeV; it overestimates the annihilation rate by factors of 10 and 34, respectively.
- The characteristic mass density at the disk midplane is $\rho_c = 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$, with scale height $z_0 = 1$ km and electron fraction $Y_e = 0.4$.
- The absorption optical depth at $T=6$ MeV is $\tau_a = 0.22$ and total optical depth $\tau = 0.35$; at $T=10$ MeV, $\tau_a = 0.6$ and $\tau = 0.96$.

## Context
This work extends the neutrino transport calculations of Sawyer (2003), who checked the validity of the two-stream and energy-averaged approximations but did not provide explicit neutrino spectra and neglected the driving chemical potential that distinguishes neutrinos from anti-neutrinos. The authors show that the two-stream approximation is accurate for direction-averaged quantities, confirming Sawyer (2003), but that the Fermi-Dirac black body limit used by Popham (1999) and the gray body model used by Di Matteo (2002) and Janiuk (2007) both significantly overestimate neutrino luminosity and annihilation rates. The results enable more realistic modeling of the central engine of short gamma-ray bursts and motivate future self-consistent calculations that include chemical evolution and thermal balance in NDAFs.
