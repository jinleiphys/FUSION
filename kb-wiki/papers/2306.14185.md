# Baryonic models of ultra-low-mass compact stars for the central compact object in HESS J1731-347

> The recent attempt on mass and radius inference of the central compact object within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 suggests for this object an unusually low mass of $M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}\,M_{\odot}$ and a small radius of $R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$\,km. We explore the ways such a result can be accommodated within models of dense matter with heavy baryonic degrees of freedom which are constrained by the multi-messenger observations. We find that to do so using only purely nucleonic models, one needs to assume a rather small value of the slope of symmetry energy $L_{\rm sym}$. Once heavy baryons are included higher values of the slope $L_{\rm sym}$ become acceptable at the cost of a slightly reduced maximum mass of static configuration. These two scenarios are distinguished by the particle composition and will undergo different cooling scenarios. In addition, we show that the universalities of the $I$-Love-$Q$ relations for static configurations can be extended to very low masses without loss in their accuracy.

## Key claim

Purely nucleonic models can accommodate the unusually low mass (0.77 solar masses) and small radius (10.4 km) inferred for the central compact object in HESS J1731-347, but only if the slope of symmetry energy L_sym is fairly small (less than about 60 MeV for some models); including heavy baryons relaxes this constraint by about 30-40 MeV, allowing higher L_sym values at the cost of slightly reduced maximum mass.

## Method

The authors used a covariant density functional (CDF) approach with density-dependent baryon-meson couplings to model dense matter containing nucleons, hyperons (Lambda, Xi, Sigma), and Delta resonances. They varied two key nuclear matter parameters, the skewness coefficient Q_sat of symmetric nuclear matter and the slope L_sym of the symmetry energy, and computed mass-radius relations for static compact stars, matching the core equation of state to a crust EoS at about half nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the central compact object in HESS J1731-347: M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} solar masses (68.3% CI) from parallax priors and X-ray data alone
- Radius of the central compact object: R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km (68.3% CI) from the same data
- Improved estimates including full distance and EoS priors: M = 0.83^{+0.17}_{-0.13} solar masses, R = 11.25^{+0.53}_{-0.37} km (68.3% CI)
- For purely nucleonic models with Q_sat = -600 MeV, consistency at 95.4% CI requires L_sym less than about 60 MeV
- For purely nucleonic models with Q_sat = 600 MeV, consistency at 95.4% CI requires L_sym less than about 30 MeV
- Including heavy baryons relaxes the L_sym constraint by about 30-40 MeV, allowing L_sym up to about 80 MeV for models with Q_sat = -200 MeV
- Maximum masses of heavy baryon models lie in the range 2.0 to 2.3 solar masses
- The I-Love-Q universal relations hold for low-mass stars down to about 0.2 solar masses with fractional errors less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Doroshenko (2022), who inferred the mass and radius of the CCO in HESS J1731-347 and suggested it could be a "strange star." The authors provide a baryonic alternative using covariant density functionals, contrasting with Brodie (2023) who found nucleonic CDF models inconsistent with the 68.3% CI ellipse, a difference arising because the present work includes models with smaller L_sym values. The results enable future discrimination between nucleonic and heavy-baryon compositions through cooling observations, since the two scenarios predict different neutrino emission processes (direct Urca processes are suppressed in nucleonic models but allowed in Delta-admixed hyperonic matter).
