# Hadrons from a hard wall AdS/QCD model

> We review a recent work on masses of mesons and nucleons within a hard wall model of holographic QCD in a unified approach. In order to treat meson and nucleon properties on the same footing, we introduced the same infrared (IR) cut for both sectors. In framework of present approach, the best fit to the experimental data is achieved introducing the anomalous dimension for baryons.

# Hadrons from a hard wall AdS/QCD model

## Key claim

The hard wall AdS/QCD model, using the same infrared cutoff for both meson and baryon sectors, can reproduce nucleon masses only when an anomalous dimension is introduced for baryons, effectively treating the 5D Dirac mass as a free parameter; without this anomalous dimension, the predicted nucleon masses are far too high (2050–2196 MeV versus the experimental 940 MeV).

## Method

The authors construct a 5D holographic model in anti-de Sitter space with a hard wall (IR cutoff at z = z_m), including 5D gauge fields A_L and A_R for the chiral symmetry, a bi-fundamental scalar field X to encode the quark mass and condensate, and two 5D Dirac spinors N_1 and N_2 to represent nucleons. They fix mesonic parameters (m_q, sigma, z_m) from the meson sector following Erlich et al. (2005) and DaRold and Pomarol (2005), then introduce a Yukawa coupling g between the scalar X and the nucleon fields, and allow the 5D Dirac mass m_5 to vary from its conformal value of 5/2 to account for anomalous dimensions.

## Key numbers

- Without anomalous dimension, the lowest-lying nucleon mass is 2050 MeV (Model A) and 2196 MeV (Model B), compared to the experimental value of 940 MeV.
- Best fit to experimental data in the chiral limit uses z_m^{-1} = 285 MeV, sigma^{1/3} = 227 MeV, and g = -9.6, with the 5D mass set to zero due to large anomalous dimension.
- The model reproduces the nucleon (p,n)+ mass as 930 MeV (experiment: 939 MeV), the N+ resonance as 1826 MeV (experiment: 1440 MeV), and the N- resonance as 1856 MeV (experiment: 1535 MeV).
- The rho meson mass is reproduced as 685 MeV (experiment: 776 MeV) and the rho' as 1573 MeV (experiment: 1475 MeV).
- Nonstrange baryon masses are reproduced within 10–20% deviations from experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the hard wall AdS/QCD approach of Erlich et al. (2005) and DaRold and Pomarol (2005), which successfully described mesons, to include nucleons following the baryon formulation of Hong et al. (2006). The key finding is that the conformal 5D mass m_5 = 5/2, derived from AdS/CFT, fails for baryons, requiring an anomalous dimension that effectively renormalizes m_5 to zero. This enables future work to explore holographic descriptions of nuclear matter and nucleon properties such as the neutron electric dipole moment, as previously studied by Hong et al. (2007) and Kim et al. (2007).
