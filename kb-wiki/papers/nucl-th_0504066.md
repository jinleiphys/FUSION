# Gapless color-flavor locked phase in quark and hybrid stars

> We study the effects of the gapless color-flavor locked (gCFL) phase on the equation of state of strongly interacting matter in the range of baryonic chemical potential involved in a compact star. We analyze the possibility of a phase transition from hadronic matter to gCFL quark matter and we discuss, for different values of the strange quark mass and diquark coupling strength, the existence of a gCFL phase in quark or hybrid stars. The mass-radius relation and the structure of compact stars containing the gCFL phase are shown and the physical relevance of this superconducting phase inside a stellar object is also discussed.

## Key claim

The gapless color-flavor locked (gCFL) phase may occupy a wide region inside both quark and hybrid stars, but it does not sensibly modify the global mass-radius relation; its main physical relevance lies in its transport properties (cooling, viscosity, r-mode stability) rather than in a distinctive stellar-structure signature.

## Method

The authors compute the equation of state (EOS) of the gCFL phase using an NJL-like model with three superconducting gaps, a form factor cutoff at 800 MeV, and a bag constant of B^(1/4)=150 MeV. They impose chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and color neutrality, then minimize the thermodynamic potential with respect to the gap parameters. The hadronic EOS is obtained from a relativistic mean-field model (GM3 parameter set) with nucleons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho mesons. Phase transitions between hadronic matter and gCFL quark matter are studied using both the Gibbs construction (for mixed phases) and the Maxwell construction (for discontinuous transitions), and the resulting EOSs are used to solve the Tolman-Volkoff-Oppenheimer equations for stellar structure.

## Key numbers

- For diquark couplings G1 and G2, the corresponding CFL gaps at mu = 500 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV are Delta_CFL ~ 40 MeV and Delta_CFL ~ 100 MeV, respectively.
- The gCFL phase is energetically favored over unpaired quark matter (UQM) for a window of about Delta_mu ~ 30-50 MeV in quark chemical potential.
- The transition from gCFL to CFL occurs when m_s^2/mu is approximately 2 Delta_CFL.
- Quark stars with masses of 1.2 M_sun and 1.4 M_sun are studied; the 1.2 M_sun star is almost entirely gCFL with a small CFL core, while the 1.4 M_sun star has a larger CFL fraction.
- Hybrid stars with masses of 1.3 M_sun and 1.4 M_sun are studied; the 1.3 M_sun star develops a gCFL core, while the 1.4 M_sun star has only a narrow gCFL layer.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the gCFL phase by Alford et al. (2004) and Fukushima et al. (2004), who identified the phase and computed its properties, and by Alford et al. (2004), who first estimated its effect on compact-star cooling at a fixed chemical potential of 500 MeV. The present paper provides the first detailed investigation of the gCFL phase across the full range of baryonic chemical potential relevant to compact stars, including the hadron-to-quark phase transition and the resulting mass-radius relations. It enables future work on the cooling, bulk and shear viscosity, and r-mode instability of stars containing gCFL matter, and suggests that a first-order transition from unpaired quark matter to gCFL matter could be relevant for gamma-ray burst mechanisms.
