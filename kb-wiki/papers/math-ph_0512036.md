> We derive a general expression for the transformation brackets between the chains U(n+1) -> U(n) -> SO(n) and U(n+1) -> SO(n+1) -> SO(n).

# Transformation brackets between U(n+1) -> U(n) -> SO(n) and U(n+1) -> SO(n+1) -> SO(n)

## Key claim

The authors derive a closed-form general expression for the transformation brackets between the two group chains U(nu+1) -> U(nu) -> SO(nu) and U(nu+1) -> SO(nu+1) -> SO(nu) for any dimension nu >= 2, extending previous results that existed only for the specific cases nu=3 and nu=5. The derivation relies on boson realizations and SU(1,1) quasi-spin algebra, with the explicit formula given in terms of Pochhammer symbols and double factorial coefficients.

## Method

The authors construct basis states for both group chains using nu+1 boson creation and annihilation operators, where the U(nu+1) algebra is realized by bilinear products b-dagger_j b_k. For the first chain U(nu+1) -> U(nu) -> SO(nu), states are labeled by total boson number N, boson number n in the nu-dimensional subspace, and seniority tau; for the second chain U(nu+1) -> SO(nu+1) -> SO(nu), states are labeled by N, the SO(nu+1) quantum number sigma, and tau. The transformation brackets are obtained by taking the overlap between the two sets of basis states, both expressed in terms of the common SO(nu) highest-weight states |[tau],tau,tau>, using normalization coefficients derived from SU(1,1) algebra and expansion coefficients from the SO(nu+1) -> SO(nu) reduction via hyperspherical coordinates and Gegenbauer polynomials.

## Key numbers

* The general transformation bracket formula is given for arbitrary nu >= 2, with the explicit expression in Eq. (bra1) containing double factorial factors such as (2sigma+nu-1)!!, (N+sigma+nu-1)!!, and (n+tau+nu-2)!!.
* For the special case sigma = N (the lowest SO(nu+1) representation), the bracket simplifies to c^tau_{nN} = [(N-tau)!(N+tau+nu-2)! / ((N-n)!(n+tau+nu-2)!!(n-tau)!!(2N+nu-3)!!)]^{1/2}.
* The normalization coefficient for the first chain is B_{ntau} = (-1)^{(n-tau)/2} sqrt[(2tau+nu-2)!! / ((n+tau+nu-2)!!(n-tau)!!)].
* The normalization coefficient for the second chain is A_{Nsigma} = (-1)^{(N-sigma)/2} sqrt[(2sigma+nu-1)!! / ((N+sigma+nu-1)!!(N-sigma)!!)].
* The expansion coefficients F_k(sigma,tau) for the SO(nu+1) -> SO(nu) reduction contain factors (2sigma+nu-3-2k)!! and (-1/2)^k.

## Context

This work generalizes the transformation brackets previously derived for the specific cases nu=5 (the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello, 1979) and nu=3 (the vibron model of Iachello and Levine, 1995) to arbitrary dimension nu >= 2. The results are directly applicable to the algebraic treatment of bound-state problems where U(nu+1) serves as the spectrum generating algebra, including the U(6) model for nuclear quadrupole degrees of freedom, the U(4) model for molecular dipole vibrations, and the U(7) model for baryon structure. The transformation brackets enable evaluation of matrix elements in the physically important deformed (SO(nu+1)) chain by first computing them in the spherical (U(nu)) chain and then transforming, with specific applications noted for nu=2 (bending vibrations of linear molecules) and nu=9 (rotations and vibrations of non-planar tetratomic molecules).
