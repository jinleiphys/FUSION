# Riemann's theorem for quantum tilted rotors

> The angular momentum, angular velocity, Kelvin circulation, and vortex velocity vectors of a quantum Riemann rotor are proven to be either (1) aligned with a principal axis or (2) lie in a principal plane of the inertia ellipsoid. In the second case, the ratios of the components of the Kelvin circulation to the corresponding components of the angular momentum, and the ratios of the components of the angular velocity to those of the vortex velocity are analytic functions of the axes lengths.

# Riemann's theorem for quantum tilted rotors

## Key claim
The authors prove that the classical Riemann theorem for ellipsoidal fluid rotors also holds for quantum cranked Riemann rotors: the angular momentum, angular velocity, Kelvin circulation, and vortex velocity vectors must either be aligned with a principal axis or lie in a principal plane of the inertia ellipsoid. The proof relies on the condition that the intrinsic energy is minimized with respect to orientation of both the angular velocity and vortex velocity vectors, and the result holds only when the self-consistency condition between the mean field and density distribution is satisfied.

## Method
The authors start from a quantum theory of Riemann rotors formulated by simultaneous angular and vortex cranking of a nuclear mean-field Hamiltonian with an anisotropic oscillator potential. Using Inglis's cranking formula for the collective energy of an A-nucleon system, they derive semiclassical expressions for the expectations of angular momentum and Kelvin circulation. They then impose parallelism conditions (angular velocity parallel to angular momentum, vortex velocity parallel to Kelvin circulation) derived from minimization of the intrinsic energy with respect to orientation, and substitute the explicit expectation values to prove that the only consistent solutions are those where the vectors are either aligned with a principal axis or lie in a principal plane.

## Key numbers
- The product of dimensionless axes lengths must equal unity: a1*a2*a3 = 1, when the nuclear volume is set equal to the spherical value 4πR^3/3.
- For tilted rotors in a principal plane, the ratios λ2/ω2 and λ3/ω3 are given by analytic expressions involving q, where q^2 = (4a1^2 - (a3 + a2)^2)(4a1^2 - (a3 - a2)^2).
- Type II and Type III tilted rotors require the ratio of the longest to the shortest axis to be at least three to one (ultradeformed prolate-like solutions).
- The moment of inertia of a sphere of radius R and total mass M = mA is I0 = (2/5)MR^2.

## Context
This work extends Riemann's classical theorem (Riemann, 1860) for ellipsoidal fluid figures of equilibrium to the quantum domain of cranked nuclear mean-field models. The quantum Riemann rotor formalism builds on earlier work by Rosensteel (1988, 1992) and generalizes the Inglis cranking approach (Inglis, 1954) to include both angular velocity and vortex velocity cranking. The results are relevant to tilted nuclear rotors, which have been studied by Frisk and Bengtsson (1987), Nazarewicz and Szymanski (1992), and Goodman (1992), and provide a rigorous classification of possible equilibrium orientations for quantum collective rotation. The classification into Type I (oblate-like), Type II, and Type III (ultradeformed prolate-like) tilted rotors enables systematic searches for such configurations in nuclear structure calculations.
