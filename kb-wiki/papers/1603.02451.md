# $K^+$-nucleus potentials from $K^+$-nucleon amplitudes

> Optical potentials for $K^+$-nucleus interactions are constructed from $K^+$-nucleon amplitudes using recently developed algorithm based on $K^+$-N kinematics in the nuclear medium. With the deep penetration of $K^+$ mesons into the nucleus at momenta below 800~MeV/c it is possible to test this approach with greater sensitivity than hitherto done with $K^-$ and pions. The energy-dependence of experimental reaction and total cross sections on nuclei is better reproduced with this approach compared to fixed-energy amplitudes. The inclusion of Pauli correlations in the medium also improves the agreement between calculation and experiment. The absolute scale of the cross sections is reproduced very well for $^6$Li but for C, Si and Ca calculated cross sections are (23$\pm4$)\% smaller than experiment, in agreement with earlier analyses. Two phenomenological models that produce such missing strength suggest that the imaginary part of the potential needs about 40\% enhancement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a recently developed algorithm using in-medium kinematics (variable $\sqrt s$) for constructing $K^+$-nucleus optical potentials from $K^+$-nucleon amplitudes reproduces the energy dependence of experimental reaction and total cross sections on nuclei better than fixed-energy amplitudes, but for C, Si, and Ca the calculated cross sections are (23±4)% smaller than experiment, requiring a phenomenological enhancement of the imaginary part of the potential by about 40%.

## Method

Optical potentials for $K^+$-nucleus interactions were constructed from $K^+$-nucleon forward scattering amplitudes using a self-consistent algorithm where the Mandelstam variable $s$ is evaluated locally in the nuclear medium, accounting for nucleon binding energy, Fermi motion, and the real part of the optical potential itself. The free $K^+$-nucleon amplitudes (including $S$, $P$, and $D$ partial waves from the SAID software package) were corrected for Pauli correlations using the Waas-Rho-Weise (WRW) formalism. Reaction and total cross sections were calculated for $K^+$ on $^6$Li, C, Si, and Ca at four momenta below 800 MeV/c (488, 531, 656, and 714 MeV/c) and compared to experimental data from earlier transmission measurements.

## Key numbers

- Calculated cross sections for C, Si, and Ca are (23±4)% smaller than experiment, consistent with earlier analyses.
- The imaginary part of the potential needs about 40% enhancement to reproduce the missing strength for C, Si, and Ca.
- For $^6$Li, calculation and experiment agree to better than 3% uniformly over the energy range studied.
- Scaling factors from a two-parameter fit to 24 data points for C, Si, and Ca: $F_{\rm R}=0.87\pm0.10$ for the real part and $F_{\rm I}=1.41\pm0.02$ for the imaginary part of the potential.
- For $^6$Li, scaling factors from a separate fit: $F_{\rm R}=0.71\pm0.10$ and $F_{\rm I}=1.02\pm0.02$.
- Total cross sections on $^6$Li at 488 MeV/c: 77.5±1.1 mb; at 714 MeV/c: 88.5±0.6 mb; three times the deuteron cross sections at the same momenta: 76±1.8 mb and 86.0±0.6 mb, respectively.
- The in-medium energy shift for Ca at 488 MeV/c reaches up to 30 MeV at full nuclear density.
- A cut-off density $\rho_c = 0.088\pm0.005$ fm$^{-3}$ was found, larger than the average density of $^6$Li, allowing a three-parameter fit to all 32 data points.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium kinematics algorithm previously applied to kaonic atoms, pionic atoms, low-energy pion scattering, and $\eta$-nucleus interactions (Friedman and Gal, 2012; Friedman, Gal, and Mareš, 2013; Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, and Mareš, 2014) to $K^+$-nucleus scattering below 800 MeV/c, where kaons penetrate deeply into nuclei. It builds on earlier analyses by Friedman, Gal, and Mareš (1997) and Friedman, Gal, and Weiss (1997) that found 15-20% enhancement of cross sections on C, Si, and Ca relative to $^6$Li and deuteron, and on the work of Waas, Rho, and Weise (1997) on Pauli correlation corrections. The results enable direct comparisons with models of in-medium modifications such as density-dependent effective masses of vector mesons (Brown, Dooher, and Saito, 1988), meson exchange currents (Jiang and Koltun, 1992), and pion cloud contributions (García-Recio, Nieves, and Oset, 1995).
