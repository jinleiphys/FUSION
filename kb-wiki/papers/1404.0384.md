# The tricky azimuthal dependence of jet quenching at RHIC and LHC via CUJET2.0

> High transverse momentum neutral pion and charged hadron suppression pattern with respect to reaction plane at RHIC and LHC energies in central and semi-peripheral AA collisions are studied in a perturbative QCD based model, CUJET2.0. CUJET2.0 has dynamical DGLV radiation kernel and Thoma-Gyulassy elastic energy loss, with both being generalized to including multi-scale running strong coupling as well as energy loss probability fluctuations, and the full jet path integration is performed in a low $p_T$ flow data constrained medium which has 2+1D viscous hydrodynamical expanding profile. We find that in CUJET2.0, with only one control parameter, $α_{max}$, the maximum coupling strength, fixed to be 0.26, the computed nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ in central and semi-peripheral AA collisions are consistent with RHIC and LHC data at average $χ^2/d.o.f.<1.5$ level. Simultaneous agreements with high $p_T$ azimuthal anisotropy $v_2$ data are acquired given average $α_{max}$ over in-plane and out-of-plane paths varying as little as 10\%, suggesting a non-trivial dependence of the high $p_T$ single particle $v_2$ on the azimuthally varied strong coupling.

## Key claim

The CUJET2.0 model can simultaneously reproduce both the nuclear modification factor R_AA and the high-pT azimuthal anisotropy v_2 at RHIC and LHC, but only if the maximum strong coupling parameter α_max is allowed to vary by about 10% between in-plane and out-of-plane jet paths, indicating a non-trivial azimuthal dependence of the coupling strength that the abstract's "varying as little as 10%" glosses over as a necessary condition rather than a minor adjustment.

## Method

The authors used the CUJET2.0 model, which incorporates the dynamical DGLV opacity series for radiative energy loss and the Thoma-Gyulassy formula for elastic energy loss, both generalized to include multi-scale running strong coupling and energy loss probability fluctuations. Full jet path integration was performed in a 2+1D viscous hydrodynamical expanding medium constrained by low-pT flow data from the VISH2+1 code, with the HTL deformation parameters fixed to (f_E,f_M)=(1,0) so that α_max was the only adjustable parameter. The model was applied to central (b=2.4 fm) and semi-peripheral (b=7.5 fm) Au+Au collisions at √s_NN=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

* α_max = 0.25–0.27 yields simultaneous agreement with π^0/h^± R_AA data at RHIC and LHC for both central and semi-peripheral collisions at average χ²/d.o.f. < 1.5
* α_max = 0.23–0.30 yields agreement at average χ²/d.o.f. < 2 level
* For b=2.4 fm collisions, best-fit α_max values are 0.26 for in-plane (R_AA^in) and 0.29 for out-of-plane (R_AA^out) paths
* For b=7.5 fm collisions, best-fit α_max values are 0.23 for in-plane and 0.26 for out-of-plane paths
* The 10% azimuthal variation of path-averaged α_max produces consistency with both R_AA and v_2 data at average χ²/d.o.f. < 2
* The minimal running scale Q_min ≈ 2.9 GeV for α_max = 0.26

## Context

This work extends the CUJET framework (CUJET1.0 used fixed coupling) to address the long-standing difficulty that pQCD-based models such as AMY, ASW, and HT (as noted by Barbara) could explain leading hadron R_AA but significantly underestimated high-pT elliptic flow v_2. The CUJET2.0 results show that the azimuthal dependence of jet quenching requires a path-length-dependent effective coupling, which the authors suggest could arise from non-perturbative near-T_c enhancement of α_s or from uncertainties in the hydrodynamical evolution profile from VISH2+1. The extracted effective jet transport coefficient q̂ is consistent with values from HT-BW, HT-M, MARTINI, and McGill-AMY models. This work enables future studies to disentangle whether the azimuthal coupling variation reflects genuine medium properties or limitations in the current smooth hydrodynamical profiles.
