# Lee-Yang-inspired functional including contributions to fourth order in effective field theory

> In the last years, a new family of energy density functionals directly inspired by effective field theory (EFT) has been developed, among which the ELYO (extended Lee-Yang Orsay) functional. In this paper, a new extension of ELYO that includes the recently calculated fourth-order EFT terms is presented. Compared to the previous version, the description of neutron-drop energies slightly degrades for a harmonic trap $\hbarω=10$ MeV -- yet remaining reasonable -- , but improves for $\hbarω=5$ MeV. Furthermore, the obtained neutron effective mass agrees significantly better with \textit{ab-initio} estimates.

## Key claim

The new ELYO4 functional, which includes fourth-order EFT terms, slightly degrades the description of neutron-drop energies for a harmonic trap of ℏω=10 MeV compared to the previous ELYO-s+p version, but improves them for ℏω=5 MeV, while the neutron effective mass agrees significantly better with ab-initio estimates.

## Method

The author extends the ELYO functional by incorporating fourth-order contributions to the equation of state of dilute Fermi gas recently obtained in EFT, following the same method used previously for p-wave terms. The PNM EOS is constructed by making the s-wave scattering length density-dependent to broaden the validity condition to any density scale, while the effective range is fitted to the SLy5 Skyrme parameterization and the p-wave scattering length keeps its physical value. The PNM EOS is then mapped onto a Skyrme-like EDF through term-by-term identification, introducing two splitting parameters W1 and W2 that modulate contributions from velocity-dependent terms and are adjusted together with spin-orbit and pairing parameters to reproduce ab-initio results for neutron drops with N≤20 in a ℏω=10 MeV trap.

## Key numbers

- The optimal value of the tunable effective range is r_s^o = 6.831 fm, fitted to the SLy5 PNM EOS
- The neutron-neutron s-wave scattering length a_s = -18.9 fm, effective range r_s = 2.75 fm, and p-wave scattering length a_p = 0.63 fm
- The fourth-order EFT coefficients are γ1 = 0.181853, γ2 = 0.0644872, γ3 = 0.0425
- The ELYO4 functional yields saturation density ρ_c = 0.162 fm^{-3}, energy per nucleon E/A|_c = -15.979 MeV, incompressibility K_c = 229.499 MeV, isoscalar effective mass m_s^*/m|_c = 0.700, symmetry energy J = 33.142 MeV, slope L = 52.625 MeV, and curvature K_sym = -113.930 MeV
- The pairing strength V_pp = -150.00 MeV.fm^3, which is the minimum value allowed in the fitting protocol

## Context

This work extends the ELYO functional family previously developed by the author and collaborators, which began with s-wave contributions only (ELYO-s) and was then generalized to include p-wave terms (ELYO-s+p) encompassing the full third-order Lee-Yang formula. The new ELYO4 functional incorporates fourth-order EFT terms recently calculated by other authors (EFT4l, EFT4), and is compared against the SLy5 Skyrme parameterization and multiple ab-initio results from various groups. The paper highlights that the number of adjustable parameters has now reached 10 (equal to standard Skyrme EDFs), reducing the appeal of EFT-inspired functionals, and notes that finite-size instabilities previously identified in ELYO-s+p remain an open question to be addressed in future work using linear response theory.
