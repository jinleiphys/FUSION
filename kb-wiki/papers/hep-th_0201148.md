# Casimir Effects in Renormalizable Quantum Field Theories

> We review the framework we and our collaborators have developed for the study of one-loop quantum corrections to extended field configurations in renormalizable quantum field theories. We work in the continuum, transforming the standard Casimir sum over modes into a sum over bound states and an integral over scattering states weighted by the density of states. We express the density of states in terms of phase shifts, allowing us to extract divergences by identifying Born approximations to the phase shifts with low order Feynman diagrams. Once isolated in Feynman diagrams, the divergences are canceled against standard counterterms. Thus regulated, the Casimir sum is highly convergent and amenable to numerical computation. Our methods have numerous applications to the theory of solitons, membranes, and quantum field theories in strong external fields or subject to boundary conditions.

## Key claim

The authors present a framework for computing one-loop quantum corrections to extended field configurations in renormalizable quantum field theories, claiming that their method resolves longstanding ambiguities in Casimir calculations by unambiguously identifying divergent terms in the phase shift expansion with standard Feynman diagrams, provided the regularization is performed consistently (e.g., by analytically continuing the space dimension before taking the physical limit).

## Method

The method transforms the standard Casimir sum over modes into a sum over bound states plus an integral over scattering states weighted by the density of states, which is expressed in terms of phase shifts. Divergences are extracted by identifying Born approximations to the phase shifts with low-order Feynman diagrams; the Born-subtracted phase shifts are then combined with renormalized Feynman diagrams and counterterms to yield a finite, numerically tractable expression for the one-loop effective energy. The approach is applied to static, spherically symmetric background fields using partial wave expansions and the variable phase method for computing phase shifts and their Born approximations.

## Key numbers

- The degeneracy factor for bosons in three dimensions is given as \(D^{\ell}=2\ell+1\).
- For the charged scalar field model, the mass of the \(\phi\) field is \(M=Gv^2\).
- The renormalized two-point Feynman diagram \(\overline\Gamma^{(2)}_{\rm FD}[\chi]\) is given explicitly in terms of Fourier transforms and integrals over Feynman parameters, with the \(h\) field mass \(m=\sqrt{\lambda v^2/3}\).
- The first Born contribution to the Casimir energy in \(n\) dimensions is \(\Delta E^{(1)}_{n}[\chi] = \frac{\langle V \rangle m^{n-1}}{(4\pi)^\frac{n+1}{2}} \Gamma\Bigl(\frac{1-n}{2}\Bigr)\), which converges for \(0<n<1\).
- For the one-dimensional electrostatic potential hole, the total phase shift as \(\omega\to\pm\infty\) approaches \(\pm 2e\varphi L\) when the limit \(n\to 1\) is taken prematurely, but vanishes when the proper dimensional regularization prescription (taking \(n\to 1\) after computing in \(n<1\)) is followed.

## Context

This work extends the approach introduced by Schwinger (1950s) for QED in strong fields, providing a systematic method for renormalizing Casimir energies in renormalizable quantum field theories with background fields. The framework enables variational searches for quantum-stabilized solitons (e.g., nontopological solitons in theories with no classical soliton solutions), computation of induced charges on background configurations, and analysis of vacuum energies in the presence of boundaries. The authors note that Blankenbecler and Boyanovsky (1980s) first developed related techniques for charge calculations, which the present work generalizes to more complex systems.
