# Continuum EoS for QCD with Nf=2+1 flavors

> We report on a continuum extrapolated result [arXiv:1309.5258] for the equation of state (EoS) of QCD with $N_f=2+1$ dynamical quark flavors. In this study, all systematics are controlled, quark masses are set to their physical values, and the continuum limit is taken using at least three lattice spacings corresponding to temporal extents up to $N_t=16$. A Symanzik improved gauge and stout-link improved staggered fermion action is used. Our results are available online [ancillary file to arXiv:1309.5258].

# Continuum EoS for QCD with Nf=2+1 flavors

## Key claim

The paper presents a continuum extrapolated equation of state for QCD with physical quark masses, claiming full control over all systematic uncertainties; however, the abstract glosses over the fact that the peak height of the trace anomaly remains discrepant with results from the hotQCD collaboration, and the authors state that this discrepancy is unresolved as of the conference.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with 2-step stout-link improved staggered fermions, with physical quark masses and at least three lattice spacings corresponding to temporal extents up to Nt=16. The continuum limit was taken using multiple extrapolation methods, and systematic uncertainties were estimated via a histogram method combining 896 different analysis choices (including variations in continuum extrapolation, scale setting using w0 or fK, subtraction procedures, and spline functions). The trace anomaly was integrated to obtain the pressure, energy density, entropy density, and speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- The peak of the trace anomaly I/T^4 occurs at approximately T ≈ 214 MeV, with a peak height of about 4 (consistent with the authors' earlier results from Borsanyi (2010)).
- A cross-check point at T = 214 MeV was computed using a different action (Nf=2+1+1 with dynamical charm) and independently tuned quark masses bracketing the physical point to ±2% in boxes with Lmπ > 4.
- The continuum extrapolation used temporal extents up to Nt=16, with ensembles including 48^3×12 lattices in the range T = 220–335 MeV with 30k trajectories, and 64^3×12 lattices with 12k trajectories.
- The pseudocritical temperature for the chiral transition is Tc = 150 MeV (depending on the observable definition).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous full result from Borsanyi (2010), which provided the equation of state at only three characteristic temperatures, to the full temperature range with complete systematic error control. The results confirm the authors' earlier peak height of about 4 for the trace anomaly, which remains in tension with the hotQCD collaboration's results (using p4, asqtad, and HISQ actions) that showed peak heights of 5–8 (Petreczky, 2012). The paper enables the use of a fully controlled continuum-extrapolated QCD equation of state for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and early universe cosmology, with tabulated results made available online.
