# Non-Perturbative Time-Dependent Quantum Field Evolution

> We present a nonperturbative, first-principles numerical approach for time-dependent problems in the framework of quantum field theory. In this approach the time evolution of quantum field systems is treated in real time and at the amplitude level. As a test application, we apply this method to QED and study photon emission from an electron in a strong, time-dependent external field. Coherent superposition of electron acceleration and photon emission is observed in the nonperturbative regime.

## Key claim

The authors present a nonperturbative, first-principles numerical method for time-dependent quantum field theory that treats evolution in real time at the amplitude level, but the demonstration is limited to a simplified model of nonlinear Compton scattering in which the laser background field does not obey Maxwell's equations and back-reaction on the laser is neglected.

## Method

The method, called time-dependent Basis Light-front Quantization (tBLFQ), first constructs stationary eigenstates of the light-front QED Hamiltonian using Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ), which diagonalizes the Hamiltonian in a basis of Fock states with longitudinal plane waves and transverse two-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenstates. The time evolution of quantum field amplitudes under an external time-dependent background field is then computed by solving the light-front Schrodinger equation in an interaction picture, using time-step discretization and basis truncation as the only approximations. For the nonlinear Compton scattering test case, the external field is a classical longitudinal periodic electric field with finite light-front time duration, and the Fock space is truncated to the lowest two sectors (electron and electron-photon).

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal periodic electric field has peak amplitude \(E^3_0\) and frequency \(l_\LCm\), with the gauge potential given by \(\mathcal{A}^-(x^+,x^-)=\frac{E^3_0}{l_\LCm} \cos{(l_\LCm x^-)}\Theta(x^+)\Theta(\Delta x^+-x^+)\).
* The longitudinal box length is \(L=2\pi\) MeV\(^{-1}\), so the longitudinal momentum quantum number \(k\) can be read as longitudinal momentum in units of MeV.
* The 2D-harmonic oscillator scale parameter is \(b=0.511\) MeV, matching the physical electron mass \(m_e\).
* The basis truncation parameter \(N_{\text{max}}\) sets the maximum total number of oscillator quanta \(2n+|m|\) for the Fock states.

## Context

This work extends the stationary-state Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) method developed by Vary (2009) and Honkanen (2010) into the time-dependent regime, enabling first-principles treatment of scattering processes with time-dependent background fields. The approach is intended for applications in strong-field laser physics and relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where time-dependent electromagnetic fields are present, and it enables future studies of more realistic background fields and processes beyond the simplified nonlinear Compton scattering test case presented here.
