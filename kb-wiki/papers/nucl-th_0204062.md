# The Decoupling Problem at RHIC

> We investigate whether it is possible to dynamically generate from classical transport theory the observed surprising Rout ~ Rside in Au+Au at s^1/2 = 130A GeV at RHIC. We obtained covariant solutions to the Boltzmann transport equation via the MPC technique, for a wide range of partonic initial conditions and opacities. We demonstrate that there exist transport solutions that yield a freezeout distribution with Rout < Rside for Kperp > ~ 1.5 GeV. These solutions correspond to continuous evaporation-like freezeout, where the emission duration is comparable to the source size. Naively this would mean Rout > Rside. Nevertheless, our sources exhibit Rout < Rside because they are narrower in the 'out' than in the 'side' direction and, in addition, a positive x_out-t correlation develops reducing Rout further.

# The Decoupling Problem at RHIC

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that classical transport theory can dynamically produce freezeout distributions with Rout < Rside for Kperp > ~1.5 GeV in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130A GeV, contrary to naive expectations that continuous evaporation-like freezeout would yield Rout > Rside. This result depends on the source being narrower in the 'out' than in the 'side' direction combined with a positive x_out-t correlation that further reduces Rout.

## Method
The authors solved the Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann transport equation with elastic 2-to-2 gluon scattering using the MPC parton cascade algorithm with parton subdivision, for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130A GeV. They varied the transport opacity across three classes of initial conditions (minijet from HIJING, gluon saturation with Qsat = 1.2 GeV, and a combination of both) and computed freezeout distributions defined as the space-time point of last interaction of test particles, with hadronization via parton-hadron duality or independent fragmentation.

## Key numbers
- The observed ratio Rout / Rside ≈ 0.9-1.1 for average pair momenta Kperp < 0.7 GeV at RHIC is the experimental puzzle addressed
- Transport solutions yield Rout < Rside for Kperp > ~1.5 GeV, with a turning point Kc ≈ 1.3-2 GeV depending on hadronization scheme
- Initial parton density dNg(tau0)/deta = 1000 for minijet and saturation initial conditions; minijet part with original HIJING normalization gave dN/deta = 210
- For zero opacity (chi = 0), both Rout and Rside ≈ 3 fm determined by nuclear size, giving Rout/Rside = 1
- For chi = 0, RL < ≈ 0.5 fm for all Kperp bins studied due to formation time tau0 = 0.1 fm/c
- The time spread sqrt(<Delta t_tilde^2>) is comparable to the spatial width of the source ~3 fm
- Transport theory results are smaller than observed Rout and RL at RHIC for chi ~ 0-8; better agreement requires chi > ~10
- Rside(Kperp) ≈ constant ≈ 3 fm independent of transport opacity

## Context
This work extends classical transport theory studies of elliptic flow (Molnar and Gyulassy, 2002) to HBT interferometry at RHIC, addressing the puzzle that Rout ≈ Rside observed by STAR and PHENIX contradicts ideal hydrodynamics predictions of Rout/Rside significantly larger than one (Heinz, 1999; Kolb et al., 2000). The results contradict earlier hybrid hydrodynamics+hadronic cascade calculations (Teaney et al., 2001) that gave Rout/Rside ≈ 1.2-1.4, and demonstrate that nonequilibrium partonic evolution can produce the observed ratio. The work enables systematic studies of how finite opacity and continuous freezeout affect HBT radii, though it leaves open the puzzle of why Rside remains constant at ~3 fm independent of collective dynamics.
