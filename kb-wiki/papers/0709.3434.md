# Phase Transitions and the Perfectness of Fluids

> We calculate the ratio eta/s, the shear viscosity (eta) to entropy density (s), which characterizes how perfect a fluid is, in weakly coupled real scalar field theories with different types of phase transitions. The mean-field results of the eta/s behaviors agree with the empirical observations in atomic and molecular systems such as water, He, N, and all the matters with data available in the NIST database. These behaviors are expected to be the same in N component scalar theories with an O(N) symmetry. We speculate these eta/s behaviors are general properties of fluid shared by QCD and cold atoms. Finally, we clarify some issues regarding counterexamples of the conjectured universal eta/s bound found in Refs.[16,17].

## Key claim

The paper argues that the qualitative behavior of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) in weakly coupled real scalar field theories with first-order, second-order, and crossover phase transitions matches empirical observations in atomic and molecular systems, and speculates these behaviors are general properties shared by QCD and cold atoms, but the results are obtained in the mean-field (Hartree) approximation and the authors note that the mean-field result would be modified very close to T_c (with |T-T_c|/T_c less than about O(b)).

## Method

The authors compute eta/s in a weakly coupled real scalar field theory with a Z2 symmetry using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism in the Hartree approximation, neglecting non-tadpole type loop diagrams. The shear viscosity is calculated using the Boltzmann equation with effective temperature-dependent masses and scattering amplitudes, where the two-particle elastic scattering amplitude includes both quartic and cubic couplings. Four cases are studied: no phase transition (c=0, b>0, a>0), second-order phase transition (c=0, b>0, a<0), crossover (adding an explicit symmetry breaking term H phi), and first-order phase transition (c>0, b<0, a>0).

## Key numbers

- For the high T expansion with bT^2 >> |a|, eta/s = k/b^2 (1 + 0.24/sqrt(b) * a/T^2 + ...) with k = 192 + 49 sqrt(b) + O(b)
- The expansion parameter is approximately lambda/(4pi)^2, where lambda = 6b, and b = 0.1 is used in the calculations
- For the O(N) scalar field theory with a=c=0, to leading order in the b expansion, eta/s is proportional to 1/(N b^2)
- The eta/s bound conjecture value is 1/(4pi)

## Context

This work extends the study of eta/s behavior from atomic and molecular systems (H2O, N, He) documented in the NIST database and previous work by Csernai (2006) and Chen (2006) on QCD, to a simple scalar field theory framework. The results support the proposal by Lacey (2006) to probe the QCD critical end-point using eta/s, and address counterexamples to the eta/s bound conjecture constructed by Cohen (2007) and Dobado (2007). The paper enables future lattice computations of eta/s in the lower T regime for pure gluon theory and provides theoretical support for locating the QCD critical end-point through eta/s measurements.
