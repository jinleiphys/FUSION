# Strangeness nuclear physics - 2010

## Key claim
Selected topics in strangeness nuclear physics are reviewed, with the abstract's claim of a comprehensive overview qualified by the paper's focus on four specific areas: Λ-hypernuclear spectroscopy and structure, multistrangeness, and K̅ mesons in nuclei, while acknowledging that little is known on strangeness S=-2 hypernuclei and that the missing information is vital for extrapolating into strange hadronic matter and neutron stars.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental data from KEK, JLab, BNL, and FINUDA on Λ-hypernuclear spectroscopy via (π⁺,K⁺) and (e,e'K⁺) reactions, γ-ray measurements of spin-dependent ΛN interactions, and weak decay spectra. It compiles binding energies of ΛΛ hypernuclei from hybrid emulsion experiments (KEK E373, E176) and compares them to cluster calculations by Hiyama et al. (2010) and shell-model estimates by Gal and Millener (2010). The review also examines hyperon nuclear potential depths from Nijmegen soft-core models and chiral effective-field theory, and analyzes K̅-nuclear interactions from K⁻-atom data and inclusive (K⁻,n) and (K⁻,p) spectra.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on hypernuclear physics from the 1980s and 1990s, including the systematic γ-ray measurements at BNL and KEK (Tamura 2008) and the Nijmegen meson-exchange models (Rijken 2010). It updates the status of double-Λ hypernuclei following the Nagara event (Nakazawa 2010) and the Hida event, and discusses the implications for strange hadronic matter as formulated by Schaffner, Gal, and collaborators (1993, 1994). The review enables future experiments at J-PARC, including a scheduled 'day-1' experiment on a ¹²C target for Ξ hypernuclei (Tanida 2010), and new searches for K⁻pp at GSI and J-PARC (Nagae 2010). The work also addresses the open question of whether K̅ condensation occurs in strange hadronic matter, with recent calculations by Gazda et al. (2008, 2009) showing saturation of K⁻ separation energies that prevents conversion of hyperons to K̅ mesons under strong-interaction equilibrium.
