# Nonlinear evolution of density and flow perturbations on a Bjorken background

> Density perturbations and their dynamic evolution from early to late times can be used for an improved understanding of interesting physical phenomena both in cosmology and in the context of heavy-ion collisions. We discuss the spectrum and bispectrum of these perturbations around a longitudinally expanding fireball after a heavy-ion collision. The time-evolution equations couple the spectrum and bispectrum to each other, as well as to higher-order correlation functions through nonlinear terms. A non-trivial bispectrum is thus always generated, even if absent initially. For initial conditions corresponding to a model of independent sources, we discuss the linear and nonlinear evolution is detail. We show that, if the initial conditions are sufficiently smooth for fluid dynamics to be applicable, the nonlinear effects are relatively small.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for density and flow perturbations on a Bjorken background in heavy-ion collisions, nonlinear effects are relatively small if the initial conditions are sufficiently smooth for fluid dynamics to be applicable, but a non-trivial bispectrum is always generated even if absent initially due to coupling between the spectrum and bispectrum through nonlinear terms.

## Method

The authors apply the time-renormalization-group (time-RG) formulation from cosmology to study the time evolution of two-point and three-point correlation functions (spectrum and bispectrum) of density and flow fluctuations on top of a Bjorken-expanding background. They derive coupled evolution equations for the spectrum and bispectrum in Fourier space, assuming an irrotational fluid with no rapidity dependence, and solve these equations perturbatively for initial conditions corresponding to a model of independent sources.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken background energy density evolves as $\epsilon_\text{Bj}(\tau) = \epsilon_\text{Bj}(\tau_0) \left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{4/3}$, with the background temperature scaling as $T_\text{Bj}(\tau) = T_\text{Bj}(\tau_0) \left({\tau_0}/{\tau}\right)^{1/3}$.
- The ratio $\nu_\text{Bj}(\tau)/\tau$ decreases as $\left( \frac{\eta}{s} \right) \frac{1}{T_\text{Bj}(\tau_0)\, \tau_0} \left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{2/3}$, where $\eta/s$ is the viscosity to entropy ratio.
- The rescaled time variable is defined as $t = \frac{3 \,\tau^{4/3}}{4\, \tau_0^{1/3}}$, with $\partial_t \equiv \left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{1/3} \partial_\tau$.
- The transverse extension of the system is taken to be around 8 fm, serving as an effective IR cutoff for the initial power spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the time-renormalization-group formulation developed for cosmological large-scale structure (LSS) by Bernardeau et al. (2008) to the heavy-ion collision context, specifically to perturbations on a Bjorken background. It differs from the approach of Teaney and Yan (2012), who used numerical hydro-solvers to determine linear and nonlinear response coefficients, and from Floerchinger et al. (2013), who used a hydro-solver to show that fluid dynamic response is close to linear. The paper enables a direct analytical treatment of correlation function evolution in heavy-ion collisions without requiring numerical hydro-solvers, analogous to methods used in cosmology for studying the bispectrum and higher-order spectra.
