# Neutrino (Antineutrino)-Nucleus Interactions in the Shallow- and Deep-Inelastic Scattering Regions

> In $ν/\barν$-N/A interactions SIS is technically defined in terms of the four-momentum transfer to the hadronic system as non-resonant meson production with $Q^2 \lessapprox 1~GeV^2$. This non-resonant meson production intermixes with resonant meson production in a regime of similar effective hadronic mass W of the interaction. As $Q^2$ grows and surpasses this $\approx 1~GeV^2$ limit, non-resonant interactions begin to take place with quarks within the nucleon indicating the start of DIS region. SIS and DIS regions have received varying degrees of attention from the community. While the theoretical / phenomenological study of $ν$-nucleon and $ν$-nucleus DIS scattering is advanced, such studies of a large portion of the SIS region, particularly the SIS to DIS transition region, have hardly begun. Experimentally, the SIS and the DIS regions for $ν$-nucleon scattering have minimal results and only in the experimental study of the $ν$-nucleus DIS region are there significant results for some nuclei. Since current and future neutrino oscillation experiments have contributions from both higher W SIS and DIS kinematic regions and these regions are in need of both considerable theoretical and experimental study, this review will concentrate on these SIS to DIS transition and DIS kinematic regions surveying our knowledge and the current challenges.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shallow inelastic scattering (SIS) region, particularly the transition from SIS to deep inelastic scattering (DIS), has been seriously neglected in both theoretical and experimental studies of neutrino-nucleus interactions, despite being essential for current and future oscillation experiments. The authors define SIS practically as inclusive meson production with effective hadronic mass W between approximately 1.1 GeV and 2 GeV at all Q², and DIS as W greater than 2 GeV with Q² greater than 1 GeV², noting that resonant and non-resonant meson production cannot be experimentally separated.

## Method

This is a review paper that surveys the theoretical formalism for neutrino-nucleon scattering cross sections including QCD corrections, the nuclear medium effects in weak interaction processes (comparing results from the Aligarh-Valencia group with experimental data), and the phenomenological and experimental treatment of both the SIS region (including quark-hadron duality) and the DIS region. The authors also compare theoretical and phenomenological predictions (nuclear PDFs) with existing higher-energy experimental results from bubble chamber experiments of the 1970s, iron and lead experiments of the 1990s (CDHSW, CCFR, NuTeV), and the current MINERvA experiment at Fermilab.

## Key numbers

* SIS is technically defined as non-resonant meson production with Q² less than approximately 1 GeV², while DIS requires Q² greater than or equal to 1 GeV² and W greater than or equal to 2 GeV.
* A cut of 2 GeV in the effective hadronic mass W is introduced to separate resonant plus non-resonant meson production from DIS quark-fragmented meson production.
* The MINERvA experiment uses the medium energy NuMI beam with average neutrino energy of approximately 6 GeV, taking data with nuclear targets including ¹²C, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb.
* For the planned DUNE experiment using an argon target, more than 30% of events are expected from the DIS region and more than 50% from the SIS (W greater than or equal to M_Δ) plus DIS regions.
* The EMC effect was discovered using a muon beam in the energy region of 120-280 GeV, finding that the ratio 2σ_Fe/(Aσ_D) is not unity in the DIS region.

## Context

This review extends and updates relevant sections of the NuSTEC White Paper (Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2017) on neutrino-nucleus scattering. It surveys the theoretical work of the Aligarh-Valencia group (Sajjad Athar et al., 2009-2019) and others on nuclear medium effects, and discusses the Rein-Sehgal model (1980) and Berger-Sehgal model (2007) for resonance production, as well as the Bodek-Yang model (2002) used in Monte Carlo generators like GENIE, NuWro, NEUT, and GiBUU. The review enables improved understanding of nuclear medium effects needed for next-generation oscillation experiments including DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, and highlights the need for both theoretical and experimental study of the SIS-to-DIS transition region.
