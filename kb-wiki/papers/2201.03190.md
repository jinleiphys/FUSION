# Moments and Radii of exotic Na and Mg isotopes

> The ground-state properties of neutron-rich exotic Na and Mg isotopes with even numbers of neutrons, N, are studied up to driplines. The shell-model calculations with an ab initio effective nucleon-nucleon interaction reported in [Tsunoda, Otsuka, Takayanagi et al., Nature 587, 66 (2020)] are extended to observables such as magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments, and charge and matter radii. Good agreements with experimental data are found, and predictions are shown up to driplines. A prescription to extract the deformation parameters for the eigenstates of Monte Carlo Shell Model is presented, and the obtained values are used to calculate charge and matter radii. The increase of these radii from the Droplet model is described as the consequences of the varying deformation of the surface and the growing neutron excitations or occupations in the pf shell, consistently with the dripline mechanism presented in the above reference. The neutron skin thickness is shown to be about 0.1 fm for N=20, which can be compared to the value for 208Pb in an A1/3 scaling. The relation of the neutron skin thickness to the electromagnetic moments is discussed for an exotic nucleus, 31Na.

## Key claim

The paper extends shell-model calculations using an *ab initio* effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (EEdf1) to predict ground-state magnetic dipole moments, electric quadrupole moments, and charge/matter radii for neutron-rich Na and Mg isotopes up to the driplines, with the hedge that the spin g-factor quenching is reduced compared to standard shell-model calculations because the full sd+pf model space already includes two-particle-two-hole excitations.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) calculations using the EEdf1 interaction, derived from chiral effective field theory with in-medium renormalization via the extended Krenciglowa-Kuo method, in a model space comprising all single-particle orbits in the sd and pf shells above a 16O inert core. They extracted deformation parameters (beta2 and gamma) from the MCSM eigenstates using a new prescription based on diagonalizing the norm matrix of basis vectors, then used these deformation parameters in the standard formula relating mean-square charge radius to the droplet model value and beta2. The matter radii were computed by adding a correction for neutrons occupying the pf shell, estimated from harmonic oscillator potential wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness for 31Na: 0.10 fm; for 27Na: 0.02 fm; predicted for 39Na (dripline): 0.19 fm
- Neutron skin thickness for N=20 Na isotopes: about 0.1 fm, compared to 0.156-0.283 fm for 208Pb with an A^(1/3) scaling factor of (31/208)^(1/3) = 0.53
- Beta2 values for Na and Mg isotopes are nearly identical for the same N when N >= 20
- Experimental beta2 for the 2+_1 state of 32Mg: 0.43(3) from one measurement, and 0.501(30) or 0.0484(38) from B(E2) values
- Excitation energy errors for wrong ground-state spin/parity ordering: 30 keV for 21Na and 24 keV for 27Na
- Orbital g-factor isovector shift used: 0.2 (gl = 1.2 for protons, -0.2 for neutrons)
- Effective charges used: 1.25e for protons, 0.25e for neutrons
- Droplet model parameter R0: 1.28 fm

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations reported in Tsunoda, Otsuka, Takayanagi et al. (Nature 587, 66, 2020) which established the deformation-driven dripline mechanism for F, Ne, Na, and Mg isotopes. The present results contrast with the Coupled-Cluster calculation by Ohayon (2021) which yielded a smaller RMS charge radius (<= 3.1 fm) for 31Na. The paper enables future studies of the relation between neutron skin thickness and electromagnetic moments in exotic nuclei, and provides predictions for radii and moments of Na and Mg isotopes up to the driplines that can be tested by future experiments.
