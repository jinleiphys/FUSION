# The pion quasiparticle in the low-temperature phase of QCD

> We investigate the properties of the pion quasiparticle in the low-temperature phase of two-flavor QCD on the lattice with support from chiral effective theory. We find that the pion quasiparticle mass is significantly reduced compared to its value in the vacuum, in contrast to the static screening mass, which increases with temperature. By a simple argument, the two masses are expected to determine the quasiparticle dispersion relation near the chiral limit. Analyzing two-point functions of the axial charge density at non-vanishing spatial momentum, we find that the predicted dispersion relation and the residue of the pion pole are simultaneously consistent with the lattice data at low momentum. The test, based on fits to the correlation functions, is confirmed by a second analysis using the Backus-Gilbert method.

# The pion quasiparticle in the low-temperature phase of QCD

## Key claim
The pion quasiparticle mass is significantly reduced compared to its vacuum value, while the static screening mass increases with temperature; the two masses together determine the quasiparticle dispersion relation near the chiral limit, and this predicted dispersion relation is consistent with lattice data at low momentum, but only when the quark mass is small and the momentum is not too large.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two dynamical light quarks on a 24×64³ thermal ensemble at T=169 MeV and a 128×64³ zero-temperature ensemble with the same bare parameters. They computed two-point correlation functions of the axial charge density at zero and non-zero spatial momentum, and extracted the pion quasiparticle parameters using two independent lattice estimators derived from chiral effective theory and Ward identities. A second analysis using the Backus-Gilbert method, applied for the first time in QCD to obtain model-independent estimators for spectral functions, confirmed the fit results.

## Key numbers
- Pion screening mass: m_π/T = 1.79(2) at T=169 MeV
- Pion screening decay constant: f_π/T = 0.46(1)
- Parameter u_f = 0.76(1) and u_m = 0.74(1), extracted from two independent lattice estimators
- Quasiparticle pole mass at zero momentum: ω_0/T = 1.32(2), compared to the screening mass m_π/T = 1.79(2)
- Temporal pion decay constant: f_π^t/T = 0.62(1)
- For momentum n=1 (p_1 = 2π/L), the residue parameter b = -0.08(3) indicates consistency with the chiral prediction
- The dispersion relation with u=u_m=0.74(1) describes the data up to p ≈ 400 MeV (n=1) with χ²/d.o.f. = 0.06

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice study (Brandt et al., 2015) where the modified pion dispersion relation was derived from chiral effective theory. The results are in qualitative agreement with two-loop calculations in chiral perturbation theory by Schenk (1993) and Toublan (1997), all showing a violation of boost invariance due to the thermal medium. The findings enable future simulations at the physical light quark mass and with inclusion of the strange quark, as well as further tests of the Backus-Gilbert method for extracting spectral functions in QCD.
