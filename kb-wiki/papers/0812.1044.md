# Crossover transition in bag-like models

> We formulate a simple model for a gas of extended hadrons at zero chemical potential by taking inspiration from the compressible bag model. We show that a crossover transition qualitatively similar to lattice QCD can be reproduced by such a system by including some appropriate additional dynamics. Under certain conditions, at high temperature, the system consist of a finite number of infinitely extended bags, which occupy the entire space. In this situation the system behaves as an ideal gas of quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

A gas of compressible bag-like hadrons with an exponential mass spectrum can reproduce a crossover transition qualitatively similar to lattice QCD, provided the hadrons are allowed to shrink under the kinetic pressure of their own thermal motion; the behavior depends sensitively on the exponent \(\alpha\) of the mass spectrum, and only for \(\alpha \ge 1\) do the thermodynamic quantities approach finite asymptotic values.

## Method

The authors construct a grand-canonical partition function for a nonrelativistic Boltzmann gas of extended hadrons at zero chemical potential, using an exponential mass spectrum \(\rho(m)=c_0 e^{m/T_0}/m^\alpha\) inspired by the MIT bag model. They introduce an excluded-volume correction to prevent overlapping bags and, crucially, allow each bag's volume to vary self-consistently with the kinetic pressure \(p_k\) generated by the thermal motion of all other bags, modifying the bag stability condition to \(p_r = B + p_k\). The isobaric (Laplace-transformed) partition function is solved analytically to obtain the pressure \(p_k\) from the pole condition \(s_0(T)=f(T,s_0(T))\), and the energy density is computed numerically via \(\varepsilon = T\,\partial p_k/\partial T - p_k\).

## Key numbers

* The Hagedorn temperature is set to \(T_0 = 0.17\) GeV, corresponding to a bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 250\) MeV (i.e. \(B = 3.9\times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^4\)).
* The dimensionless constant \(k\) relating bag temperature to bag pressure is fixed at \(k=0.68\), chosen to match the large-\(T\) limit of \(\varepsilon/T^4\) from lattice QCD.
* For \(\alpha = 2\) and \(\alpha = 5/2\), the ratio \(p_k/T^4\) converges asymptotically to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit \(1/k^4 \approx 4.67\), and \(\varepsilon/T^4\) converges to \(3/k^4 \approx 14\).
* A lower mass cutoff \(m_c = m_\pi = 0.139\) GeV is imposed for \(\alpha > 0\) to avoid a pole at \(m=0\).
* The parameter \(c_0\) is fitted to the hadron mass spectrum in the range \(1-2\) GeV; values range from \(c_0 = 0.157\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for \(\alpha=0\) to \(c_0 = 0.502\) GeV\(^{4/2}\) for \(\alpha=5/2\).

## Context

This work extends the compressible-bag approach of Gorenstein (1981, 1998), who showed that a gas of extended hadrons can produce first-order, second-order, or crossover transitions, by explicitly including the kinetic pressure as a self-consistent squeezing mechanism. It contrasts with percolation models (Fortunato, 1999–2000) where bag sizes and numbers are independent and no kinetic-pressure feedback is present. The model enables a unified description of both the hadron-resonance-gas regime below \(T_c\) and the ideal quark-gluon plasma above \(T_c\) without introducing a separate deconfined phase, and it provides a schematic framework for understanding why the entropy density from lattice QCD (Aoki, 2006; Cheng, 2007) can be reproduced by a single partition function.
