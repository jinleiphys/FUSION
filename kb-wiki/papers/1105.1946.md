# On nucleon exchange mechanism in heavy-ion collisions at near-barrier energies

> Nucleon drift and diffusion mechanisms in central collisions of asymmetric heavy-ions at near-barrier energies are investigated in the framework of a stochastic mean-field approach. Expressions for diffusion and drift coefficients for nucleon transfer deduced from the stochastic mean-field approach in the semiclassical approximation have similar forms familiar from the phenomenological nucleon exchange model. The variance of fragment mass distribution agrees with the empirical formula $σ^2_{AA}(t)= N_{\rm exc}(t)$. The comparison with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations shows that, below barrier energies, the drift coefficient in the semiclassical approximation underestimates the mean number of nucleon transfer obtained in the quantal framework. Motion of the window in the dinuclear system has a significant effect on the nucleon transfer in asymmetric collisions.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach provides a semiclassical description of nucleon exchange in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions, but at energies below the Coulomb barrier the semiclassical drift coefficient underestimates the quantal nucleon drift obtained from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations by more than 50%, primarily because barrier penetration of single particles is not included in the semiclassical description.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional TDHF calculations for central collisions of the asymmetric system $^{40}$Ca + $^{90}$Zr at near-barrier energies using the SLy4d Skyrme effective force. They extracted the quantal nucleon drift coefficient from the net nucleon flux through a moving window between the colliding nuclei, and compared it with the semiclassical drift coefficient derived from the SMF approach. The diffusion coefficient for nucleon exchange was obtained from the SMF approach in the semiclassical approximation, and the variance of the fragment mass distribution was computed by integrating the diffusion coefficient over time.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{90}$Zr system is $V_B = 97.7$ MeV.
- At $E_{\rm cm}=97$ MeV (below barrier), the semiclassical calculation underestimates the quantal value of nucleon drift by more than 50%.
- At $E_{\rm cm}=93$ MeV (below barrier), the drift in the semiclassical calculation goes in the opposite direction compared to the quantal result.
- Neglecting window motion can introduce an error in the order of 50% of the mean number of transferred nucleons.
- The variance of the fragment mass distribution $\sigma^2_{AA}(t)$ agrees with the empirical formula $\sigma^2_{AA}(t) = N_{\rm exc}(t)$, where $N_{\rm exc}(t)$ is the total number of exchanged nucleons up to time $t$.

## Context

This work extends previous SMF studies of symmetric collisions (Ayik 2009, Washiyama 2009) to the asymmetric system $^{40}$Ca + $^{90}$Zr, and compares the semiclassical transport coefficients with quantal TDHF results. The empirical relation $\sigma^2_{AA}(t) = N_{\rm exc}(t)$, widely used to analyze deep-inelastic collision data (Freiesleben, Adamian), is shown to hold for asymmetric systems at both below- and above-barrier energies in the semiclassical SMF framework. The results raise the open question of whether quantal effects on nucleon diffusion coefficients become important below the Coulomb barrier, which cannot be addressed by the present semiclassical calculations.
