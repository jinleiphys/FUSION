# Role of diproton correlation in two-proton emission decay of the $^6$Be nucleus

> We discuss a role of diproton correlation in two-proton emission from the ground state of a proton-rich nucleus, $^6$Be. Assuming the three-body structure of $α+ p + p$ configuration, we develop a time-dependent approach, in which the two-proton emission is described as a time-evolution of a three-body metastable state. With this method, the dynamics of the two-proton emission can be intuitively discussed by monitoring the time-dependence of the two-particle density distribution. With a model Hamiltonian which well reproduces the experimental two-proton decay width, we show that a strongly correlated diproton emission is a dominant process in the early stage of the two-proton emission. When the diproton correlation is absent, the sequential two-proton emission competes with the diproton emission, and the decay width is underestimated. These results suggest that the two-proton emission decays provide a good opportunity to probe the diproton correlation in proton-rich nuclei beyond the proton drip-line.

## Key claim

The authors show that a strongly correlated diproton emission is the dominant process in the early stage of two-proton emission from the ground state of $^6$Be, but this conclusion depends on the inclusion of opposite-parity configuration mixing; when the diproton correlation is absent (i.e., only odd-\(l\) partial waves are included), sequential two-proton emission competes with diproton emission and the decay width is significantly underestimated.

## Method

The authors develop a time-dependent three-body model of $^6$Be assuming an $\alpha + p + p$ configuration, using the V-coordinate system. They construct an initial metastable state by applying a confining potential method to the core-proton Woods-Saxon potential, then suddenly switch to the original potential at \(t=0\) and evolve the wave packet in time using the three-body Hamiltonian with the Minnesota potential for the proton-proton interaction. The decay width is extracted from the time-evolution of the survival probability, and partial decay widths are computed for spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Q-value for two-proton emission from $^6$Be is 1.37 MeV, which the model reproduces by adjusting the repulsive term of the Minnesota potential to \(v_0 = 156.0\) MeV (full calculation) or \(v_0 = 88.98\) MeV (odd-\(l\) only case).
- The calculated total decay width with full configuration mixing is \(\Gamma \cong 88.2\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(\Gamma = 92 \pm 6\) keV.
- With only odd-\(l\) partial waves, the total decay width is \(\Gamma = 12.5\) keV, severely underestimating the experimental value.
- The spin-singlet partial width dominates in the full calculation: \(\Gamma_{S=0} = 87.1\) keV versus \(\Gamma_{S=1} = 1.1\) keV (evaluated at \(ct = 1200\) fm).
- The $(p_{3/2})^2$ configuration comprises 88.9% of the initial state in the full calculation, and the spin-singlet probability is 82.2%.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent approach previously applied to one-proton emission (Serot 1994, Talou 1998-2000) and to one-dimensional two-proton emission (Maruyama 2012) to a realistic three-dimensional calculation for the lightest two-proton emitter, $^6$Be. The results support the concept of diproton correlation originally proposed by Goldansky (1960, 1961) and contradict the notion that two-proton decays are always dominated by sequential emission; the authors show that the mixing of opposite-parity configurations (which generates the diproton correlation) is indispensable for reproducing both the Q-value and the decay width simultaneously. The initial state of $^6$Be is compared to the bound ground state of its mirror nucleus $^6$He, showing similar dinucleon correlation patterns. This work enables future studies of diproton correlation in heavier proton-rich nuclei beyond the proton drip-line.
