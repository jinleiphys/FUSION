# String-Theory-Based Predictions for Nonhydrodynamic Collective Modes in Strongly Interacting Fermi Gases

> Very different strongly interacting quantum systems such as Fermi gases, quark-gluon plasmas formed in high energy ion collisions and black holes studied theoretically in string theory are known to exhibit quantitatively similar damping of hydrodynamic modes. It is not known if such similarities extend beyond the hydrodynamic limit. Do non-hydrodynamic collective modes in Fermi gases with strong interactions also match those from string theory calculations? In order to answer this question, we use calculations based on string theory to make predictions for novel types of modes outside the hydrodynamic regime in trapped Fermi gases. These predictions are amenable to direct testing with current state-of-the-art cold atom experiments.

# String-Theory-Based Predictions for Nonhydrodynamic Collective Modes in Strongly Interacting Fermi Gases

## Key claim
String-theory calculations using Lifshitz black hole duals predict the existence of an infinite number of non-hydrodynamic collective modes in strongly interacting Fermi gases, with frequencies and damping rates that are independent of the trapping frequency and should be detectable with current cold atom experiments, provided the approximate universality of transport properties among strongly interacting quantum fluids holds.

## Method
The authors compute quasinormal modes of a probe scalar field propagating in a fixed Lifshitz black brane background with scaling parameter z=2, chosen to reproduce the equation of state of a strongly interacting Fermi gas (epsilon = P d/2). For d=2 dimensions, analytic expressions are used; for d=3 dimensions, numerical results are obtained following the approach of Starinets (2002). The black hole results, which scale as 4 pi k_B T/hbar, are converted to experimentally accessible quantities using the replacement 4 pi k_B T/hbar -> (epsilon+P)/eta, and the untrapped results are applied directly to trapped Fermi gases based on kinetic theory guidance that non-hydrodynamic modes do not change in a trap.

## Key numbers
- For d=2, the first non-hydrodynamic mode (n=1) has frequency omega_1 = 0 and damping rate Gamma_1 = 1.25(25) x P/eta
- For d=2, the second non-hydrodynamic mode (n=2) has Gamma_2 = 3.25(25) x P/eta
- For d=3, the first non-hydrodynamic mode (n=1) has omega_1 = 0.8(1) x P/eta and Gamma_1 = 1.6(3) x P/eta
- For d=3, the second non-hydrodynamic mode (n=2) has omega_2 = 1.5(1) x P/eta and Gamma_2 = 3.9(3) x P/eta
- The predicted amplitude ratio of the first non-hydrodynamic mode to the hydrodynamic mode is approximately 40 percent for the two-dimensional quadrupole mode and 20 percent for the three-dimensional breathing mode
- For the d=2 quadrupole mode, using the experimentally determined Gamma_Q = 0.30 omega_perp from Vogt (2011), the predicted Gamma_1 becomes approximately 4.17(83) omega_perp

## Context
This work extends the observation that strongly interacting Fermi gases, quark-gluon plasmas, and black holes exhibit quantitatively similar damping of hydrodynamic modes (with eta k_B/hbar s values around 0.2-0.4 for Fermi gases and 0.08 for black holes) into the non-hydrodynamic regime. It builds on the approximate transport universality conjecture discussed by Brewer (2015) and uses the Lifshitz black hole framework of Hoyos (2013) and Sybesma (2015). The predictions are directly testable by re-analyzing existing experimental data from Vogt (2011) with higher time resolution, or by new experiments with improved sampling rates. A previous re-analysis by Brewer (2015) of the Vogt (2011) data found constraints on the first non-hydrodynamic mode damping rate that are broadly consistent with these predictions, though with low statistical significance.
