# Kinetic theory of chiral relativistic plasmas and energy density of their gauge collective excitations

> We use the recently developed kinetic theory with Berry curvature to describe the fermions and antifermions of a chiral relativistic plasma. We check that this transport approach allows to reproduce the chiral anomaly equation of relativistic quantum field theory at finite temperature. We also check that it allows to describe the anomalous gauge polarization tensor that appears in the Hard Thermal (and/or Dense) effective field theory. We also construct an energy density associated to the gauge collective modes of the chiral relativistic plasma, valid in the case of small couplings or weak fields, which can be the basis for the study of their dynamical evolution.

## Key claim

The authors show that a semiclassical kinetic theory incorporating Berry curvature can reproduce the chiral anomaly equation at any finite temperature (not just at zero temperature with a well-defined Fermi surface) and also yields the anomalous gauge polarization tensor of the Hard Thermal Loop effective field theory, provided that both fermions and antifermions are described on equal footing.

## Method

The authors used the recently developed kinetic transport equation with Berry curvature corrections for chiral fermions, including a modified phase-space measure and a dispersion law that depends on the magnetic field. They performed a linear response analysis, expanding the distribution function in powers of the gauge field amplitude and its derivatives, and computed the induced electromagnetic current up to order O(epsilon delta) for isotropic configurations. They then compared the resulting current and polarization tensor with those obtained from quantum field theory via the Hard Thermal Loop effective action, and constructed an energy density for the collective gauge modes by introducing auxiliary functions W_+ and W_- that satisfy local transport equations.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass: m_D^2 = e^2 (T^2/3 + (mu_R^2 + mu_L^2)/(2 pi^2))
* Anomaly coefficient: c_E = -mu_5/2, where mu_5 = mu_R - mu_L
* Chiral magnetic effect current: J(x) = (e^2 mu_5)/(4 pi^2) B(x)
* Free energy density at zeroth order: epsilon^(0) = (7 pi^2 T^4)/60 + (mu_R^2 + mu_L^2)/4 T^2 + (mu_R^4 + mu_L^4)/(8 pi^2)
* Chiral anomaly equation at finite temperature: partial_mu j_A^mu = (e^2)/(2 pi^2) E dot B (no thermal corrections)

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical kinetic theory with Berry curvature, previously applied at zero temperature by Son and Yamamoto (2012) and Stephanov and Yin (2012), to finite temperature by including antifermions. It reproduces the anomalous Hard Thermal Loop effective action derived by Laine (2005) from Feynman diagrams, and generalizes the energy density construction for collective modes of a quark-gluon plasma by Blaizot and Iancu (1994) to the chiral case. The results enable numerical studies of the dynamical evolution of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions for small couplings or weak fields.
