# One fewer solution to the cosmological lithium problem

> Data from a recent 9Be(3He,t)9B measurement are used to rule out a possible solution to the cosmological lithium problem based on conventional nuclear physics.

# One fewer solution to the cosmological lithium problem

## Key claim

The 16.8 MeV state in ⁹B cannot enhance the ⁷Be(d,p) reaction rate enough to resolve the cosmological lithium problem, reducing the ⁷Li abundance by at most 3.5(8)% rather than the factor of ~3 needed, based on a new precise energy determination of 16.800(10) MeV that places the resonance 310(10) keV above the d+⁷Be threshold, outside the 170–220 keV range required for a viable solution.

## Method

The authors used the standard Kawano/Wagoner BBN code and added a resonant contribution to the ⁷Be(d,p) reaction rate via a single-channel, single-level R-matrix formula, parameterized by the resonance energy, total width, and partial widths for deuteron and proton emission. They adopted the new experimental values from a ⁹Be(³He,t)⁹B measurement at 140 MeV/nucleon with 30 keV resolution: E_r = 310(10) keV and Γ⁰ = 81(5) keV, and maximized the rate by setting Γ_d⁰ = Γ_p⁰ = 0.5 Γ⁰ with physically plausible channel radii (a₂₇ = 7 fm, a₁₈ = 5 fm) and reduced widths within the Wigner limit.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy of the 16.8 MeV state in ⁹B: 16.800(10) MeV, corresponding to E_r = 310(10) keV above the d+⁷Be threshold at S_d = 16.4901(10) MeV (Scholl 2011)
- Total width of the 16.8 MeV state: 81(5) keV (Scholl 2011)
- Maximum reduction in ⁷Li abundance from the resonant contribution: 3.5(8)%, essentially independent of channel radius choice
- Required resonance energy for a viable solution per Cyburt (2009): E_r ≃ 170–220 keV
- Required deuteron width for a viable solution per Cyburt (2009): Γ_d ≃ 10–40 keV
- Required total width minus deuteron width for a viable solution per Cyburt (2009): Γ − Γ_d ≃ Γ_d
- Significance of the cosmological lithium problem: 4.2σ–5.3σ (Cyburt 2008)
- Baryon-to-photon ratio: η = 6.19(15) × 10⁻¹⁰ (WMAP)
- Uncertainty on calculated ⁷Li abundance from standard BBN: 13% (Cyburt 2008)

## Context

This work rules out a specific nuclear-physics solution to the cosmological lithium problem proposed by Cyburt (2009), which suggested that the 16.8 MeV state in ⁹B could enhance ⁷Be destruction via d+⁷Be → ⁹B* → p+⁸Be* at BBN temperatures. The new precise energy measurement from Scholl (2011) places the resonance 310 keV above threshold, well outside the 170–220 keV window required for the mechanism to work. The authors conclude, in agreement with Boyd (2010), that all possibilities for solving the cosmological lithium problem by conventional nuclear physics now appear exhausted, leaving explanations involving new physics beyond the Standard Model or errors in the observationally inferred primordial lithium abundance as remaining options.
