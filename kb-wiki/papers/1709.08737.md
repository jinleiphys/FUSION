# A meson-baryon molecular interpretation for some $öˋ_c$ excited baryons

> We explore the possibility that some of the five narrow $öˋ_c$ resonances recently observed at LHCb could correspond to pentaquark states, structured as meson-baryon bound states or molecules. The interaction of the low-lying pseudoscalar mesons with the ground-state baryons in the charm $+1$, strangeness $-2$ and isospin 0 sector is built from t-channel vector meson exchange, using effective Lagrangians. The resulting s-wave coupled-channel unitarized amplitudes show the presence of two structures with similar masses and widths to those of the observed $öˋ_c(3050)^0$ and $öˋ_c(3090)^0$. The identification of these resonances with the meson-baryon bound states found in this work would also imply assigning the values $1/2^-$ for their spin-parity. An experimental determination of the spin-parity of the $öˋ_c(3090)^0$ would help in disentangling its structure, as the quark-based models predict its spin-parity to be either $3/2^-$ or $5/2^-$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that two of the five narrow Omega_c resonances observed by LHCb, specifically Omega_c(3050)^0 and Omega_c(3090)^0, can be interpreted as meson-baryon molecular states (pentaquarks) rather than conventional three-quark excitations. This identification would imply a spin-parity assignment of 1/2^- for both states, which differs from quark model predictions that favor 3/2^- or 5/2^- for Omega_c(3090)^0.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel unitarized scattering amplitude for pseudoscalar mesons and ground-state baryons in the charm +1, strangeness -2, isospin 0 sector, using t-channel vector meson exchange derived from effective Lagrangians under SU(4) symmetry. The interaction kernel is reduced to a contact term via the zero-range approximation, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved algebraically with on-shell factorization, using dimensional regularization with subtraction constants tuned to reproduce the experimental masses and widths.

## Key numbers

- Two poles are generated in the pseudoscalar-baryon scattering amplitude: one at 3051.6 MeV with width 0.45 MeV (Model 1) and 3050.3 MeV with width 0.44 MeV (Model 2)
- The second pole appears at 3103.3 MeV with width 17 MeV (Model 1) and 3090.8 MeV with width 12 MeV (Model 2)
- The experimental values from LHCb are: Omega_c(3050)^0 mass = 3050.2 +/- 0.1 +/- 0.1 (+0.3/-0.5) MeV, width = 0.8 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.1 MeV; Omega_c(3090)^0 mass = 3090.2 +/- 0.3 +/- 0.5 (+0.3/-0.5) MeV, width = 8.7 +/- 1.0 +/- 0.8 MeV
- The higher resonance at 3090.8 MeV has a compositeness of 0.91 in the D Xi channel, qualifying it as a D Xi bound state
- The lower resonance at 3050.3 MeV couples most strongly to the anti-K Xi'_c channel with compositeness 0.61

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation previously applied to the Lambda(1405) resonance in the strange sector and to the hidden-charm P_c pentaquarks discovered by LHCb in 2015. It revisits the SU(4) vector-meson exchange model of Hofmann (2005) and Jimenez-Tejero (2009), which predicted Omega_c states below 3 GeV, and finds that with appropriate regularization parameters two states appear in the 3.05-3.09 GeV region matching the new LHCb observations. The results enable experimental tests: a spin-parity measurement of Omega_c(3090)^0 would distinguish this molecular interpretation from quark model predictions.
