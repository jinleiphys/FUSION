# Axial form factors of the octet baryons in a covariant quark model

> We study the weak interaction axial form factors of the octet baryons, within the covariant spectator quark model, focusing on the dependence of four-momentum transfer squared, Q^2. In our model the axial form factors G_A(Q^2) (axial-vector form factor) and G_P(Q^2) (induced pseudoscalar form factor), are calculated based on the constituent quark axial form factors and the octet baryon wave functions. The quark axial current is parametrized by the two constituent quark form factors, the axial-vector form factor g_A^q(Q^2), and the induced pseudoscalar form factor g_P^q(Q^2). The baryon wave functions are composed of a dominant S-state and a P-state mixture for the relative angular momentum of the quarks. First, we study in detail the nucleon case. We assume that the quark axial-vector form factor g_A^q(Q^2) has the same function form as that of the quark electromagnetic isovector form factor. The remaining parameters of the model, the P-state mixture and the Q^2-dependence of g_P^q(Q^2), are determined by a fit to the nucleon axial form factor data obtained by lattice QCD simulations with large pion masses. In this lattice QCD regime the meson cloud effects are small, and the physics associated with the valence quarks can be better calibrated. Once the valence quark model is calibrated, we extend the model to the physical regime, and use the low Q^2 experimental data to estimate the meson cloud contributions for G_A(Q^2) and G_P(Q^2). Using the calibrated quark axial form factors, and the generalization of the nucleon wave function for the other octet baryon members, we make predictions for all the possible weak interaction axial form factors G_A(Q^2) and G_P(Q^2) of the octet baryons. The results are compared with the corresponding experimental data for G_A(0), and with the estimates of baryon-meson models based on SU(6) symmetry.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, calibrated using lattice QCD data at large pion masses (where meson cloud effects are small), can describe the valence quark contributions to nucleon axial form factors and then be extrapolated to the physical regime to estimate meson cloud contributions, with predictions extended to all octet baryons via SU(3)-flavor symmetry.

## Method

The authors constructed a covariant spectator quark model where baryon wave functions include both S-state and P-state components, with the P-state mixing coefficient determined by fitting lattice QCD data for the nucleon axial-vector form factor at large pion masses. The quark axial-vector form factor was assumed to have the same functional form as the quark electromagnetic isovector form factor, while the quark induced pseudoscalar form factor was parametrized by a vector meson dominance form with two parameters fit to lattice QCD data. After calibrating the valence quark contributions, the model was extrapolated to the physical regime using a normalization factor Z_N to account for meson cloud effects, and then extended to other octet baryons using SU(3)-flavor symmetry at the quark level.

## Key numbers

- The quark axial-vector form factor at Q^2=0 satisfies g_A^q(0)=1
- The Cabibbo angle for ΔI=1 transitions is cos θ_C ≃ 0.97, and for ΔS=1 transitions is sin θ_C ≃ 0.23
- The effective heavy meson mass used in the parametrization is M_h = 2M (twice the nucleon mass)
- The parameters for the quark electromagnetic isovector form factors (model II) are: λ=1.21, κ_-=1.823, c_-=1.16, d_-=-0.686
- The lattice QCD data used for calibration correspond to pion masses m_π > 350 MeV (the "large pion masses" regime)
- The physical pion mass used for extrapolation is m_π^phys ≃ 138 MeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to electromagnetic structure of nucleons and baryons (Ramalho and Tsushima, various works), to the weak interaction axial structure of octet baryons. It builds upon lattice QCD simulations for nucleon axial form factors (Alexandrou 2011, 2013; Bratt 2010) and octet baryons (Lin 2009; Sasaki 2009; Erkol 2010; Alexandrou 2014), and compares results with SU(3) baryon-meson models and SU(6) symmetry predictions. The approach enables separation of valence quark and meson cloud contributions to axial form factors, and provides predictions for all octet baryon weak transitions where experimental data are limited to G_A(0) values for a few transitions.
