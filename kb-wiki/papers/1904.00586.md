# Charmed dibaryon resonances in the potential quark model

> Charmed dibaryon states with the spin-parity $J^π=0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$are predicted for the two-body $Y_cN$ ($=Λ_c$, $Σ_c$, or $Σ^*_c$) systems. We employ the complex scaling method for the coupled channel Hamiltonian with the $Y_cN$-CTNN potentials, which were proposed in our previous study. We find four sharp resonance states near the $Σ_c N$ and $Σ^*_c N$ thresholds. From the analysis of the binding energies of partial channel systems, we conclude that these resonance states are Feshbach resonances. We compare the results with the $Y_c N$ resonance states in the heavy quark limit, where the $Σ_c N$ and $Σ^*_c N$ thresholds are degenerate, and find that they form two pairs of the heavy-quark doublets in agreement with the heavy quark spin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper predicts four sharp Feshbach-type resonance states in the charmed dibaryon systems near the ΣcN and Σc*N thresholds, but the existence of these resonances does not depend on whether a bound ΛcN state exists; the resonances persist even in less attractive potential variations that yield no ΛcN bound state.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Schrödinger equations for the YcN (Λc, Σc, or Σc*) two-body systems with spin-parity 0+, 1+, and 2+, using the CTNN potentials from their previous study. These potentials combine meson exchange (pion and sigma) with short-range repulsion from a quark exchange model, including D-wave mixings from the pion-exchange tensor force. They applied the complex scaling method to identify resonance states as complex-energy eigenstates that remain stationary under variation of the scaling angle θ.

## Key numbers

- ΣcN Jπ=0+ resonance: energy from ΛcN threshold ER = 163 MeV, energy from ΣcN threshold ΔE = -4 MeV, width Γ = 1 MeV
- ΣcN Jπ=1+ resonance: ER = 144 MeV, ΔE = -23 MeV, Γ = 12 MeV
- Σc*N Jπ=1+ resonance: ER = 225 MeV, ΔE = -7 MeV, Γ = 2 MeV
- Σc*N Jπ=2+ resonance: ER = 206 MeV, ΔE = -25 MeV, Γ = 14 MeV
- In the heavy quark limit (degenerate ΣcN/Σc*N threshold at 189 MeV above ΛcN): narrow doublet (0+ and 1+) has ΔE = -5 MeV, Γ = 1 MeV; broad doublet (1+ and 2+) has ΔE = -27 to -28 MeV, Γ = 13-14 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous potential model study of ΛcN bound states (Oka, Maeda, and Liu, 2018) to the resonance region above the ΛcN threshold. The results contrast with the strangeness sector, where such pairing of resonances is not seen. The predicted Feshbach resonances near ΣcN and Σc*N thresholds provide experimental targets even if no ΛcN bound state exists, as suggested by recent lattice QCD calculations (Miyamoto, 2018) that find less attractive ΛcN interactions.
