# Interaction and Identification of the Meson-Baryon molecules

> The challenges with the molecular model of the multiquark systems are the identification of the hadronic molecules and the interaction between two color neutral hadrons. We study the di-hadronic molecular systems with proposed interaction potential as s-wave one boson exchange potential along with Screen Yukawa-like potential, and arrived with the proposal that within hadronic molecule the two color neutral hadrons experience the dipole-like interaction. The present study is the continuation of our previous study \cite{arxiv-Rathaud-penta}. With the proposed interaction potential, the mass spectra of $Σ_{s}K^{*}$, $Σ_{c}K^{*}$, $Σ_{b}K^{*}$, $Σ_{s}D^{*}$, $Σ_{c}D^{*}$, $Σ_{b}D^{*}$, $Σ_{s}B^{*}$, $Σ_{c}B^{*}$, $Σ_{b}B^{*}$, $Ξ_{s}K^{*}$, $Ξ_{c}K^{*}$, $Ξ_{b}K^{*}$, $Ξ_{s}D^{*}$, $Ξ_{c}D^{*}$, $Ξ_{b}D^{*}$, $Ξ_{s}B^{*}$, $Ξ_{c}B^{*}$, $Ξ_{b}B^{*}$ meson-baryon molecules are predicted. The Weinberg compositeness theorem which provides clue for the compositeness of the state is used for determination of the scattering length and effective range. The present study predict $P_{c}(4450)$ pentaquark sate as $Σ_{c}D^{*}$ molecule with $I(J^{P})=\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})$. The formalism also predicts some very interesting open as well as hidden flavour near threshold molecular pentaquark states.

## Key claim

The authors propose that within a hadronic molecule, the two color-neutral hadrons experience a dipole-like interaction, modeled by adding a screened Yukawa-like potential to an s-wave one-boson exchange potential (OBEP), and that this combined potential can predict the mass spectra of meson-baryon molecules and identify the LHCb pentaquark candidate \(P_{c}(4450)\) as a \(\Sigma_{c}D^{*}\) molecule with quantum numbers \(I(J^{P})=\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})\).

## Method

The authors used a variational method with a hydrogenic trial wave function to solve the Schrödinger equation for a di-hadronic Hamiltonian. The interaction potential combined an s-wave one-boson exchange potential (OBEP), including pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector meson exchanges with finite-size cutoff parameters, and an additional screened Yukawa-like potential to provide sufficient attraction. The model's free parameter (the screening parameter \(c\)) was fitted to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the deuteron and then kept fixed for all other systems; the Weinberg compositeness theorem was then applied to extract the scattering length and effective range for each predicted molecular state.

## Key numbers

*   The screening parameter \(c = 0.0686\) GeV was fitted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy of \(-2.2211\) MeV (calculated value), with a calculated root-mean-square radius of 3.13 fm.
*   The \(P_{c}(4450)\) pentaquark state is predicted as a \(\Sigma_{c}D^{*}\) molecule with \(I(J^{P}) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})\) and a binding energy of \(-2.5205\) MeV, yielding a mass of 3.347 GeV.
*   For the \(\Sigma_{s}K^{*}\) system in the \(I(J^{P}) = \frac{3}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})\) channel, the binding energy is \(-1.5888\) MeV, giving a mass of 2.086 GeV.
*   For the \(\Sigma_{c}K^{*}\) system in the \(I(J^{P}) = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})\) channel, the binding energy is \(-2.5205\) MeV, giving a mass of 3.347 GeV.
*   The neutron-neutron system was found to be unbound, with a positive energy of \(+0.9528\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of dimesonic molecules (Rathaud and Rai, 2017) to meson-baryon systems, using a similar interaction potential. It aims to address two challenges of the molecular model for multiquark states: the nature of the interaction between color-neutral hadrons and the identification of hadronic molecules. The results predict mass spectra for 18 meson-baryon systems and identify specific spin-isospin channels that yield bound states, enabling future experimental searches for these near-threshold molecular pentaquark states, particularly in the hidden-charm sector where the LHCb Collaboration (2015) reported the \(P_{c}(4380)\) and \(P_{c}(4450)\) states.
