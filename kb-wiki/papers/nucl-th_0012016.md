# Evidence for Three Nucleon Force Effects in p-d Elastic Scattering

> A new measurement of the p-d differential cross section at Ep= 1 MeV has been performed. These new data and older data sets at energies below the deuteron breakup are compared to calculations using the two-nucleon Argonne v18 and the three-nucleon Urbana IX potentials. A quantitative estimate of the capability of these interactions to describe the data is given in terms of a chi^2 analysis. The chi^2 per datum drastically improves when the three-nucleon interaction is included in the Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The p-d differential cross section at low energies (1, 2, and 3 MeV) cannot be correctly described using only the modern two-nucleon Argonne v18 potential; the chi-squared per datum improves drastically only when the three-nucleon Urbana IX force is included in the Hamiltonian, with the qualification that the analysis is limited to energies below the deuteron breakup threshold.

## Method

The authors measured the p-d differential cross section at Ep = 1 MeV using a deuteron beam accelerated to 2.0 MeV at TUNL, with hydrogenated carbon foil targets and silicon surface-barrier detectors covering theta_lab = 7° to 64° (theta_cm = 21° to 166°). Absolute normalization was obtained by relating the measured p-d cross sections to the well-known p-p cross section using beams of the same magnetic rigidity. The theoretical calculations solved the p-d scattering problem using the Pair Correlated Harmonic basis and the Kohn variational principle, comparing results from the Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential alone and with the addition of the Urbana IX three-nucleon force (AV18+UR).

## Key numbers

- The new measurement at Ep = 1 MeV achieved statistical accuracy less than 0.5% and systematic error less than 0.8%.
- For the AV18+UR calculation compared to the present data (N=56), the minimum chi-squared per datum was 1.03 after lowering the data normalization by 0.2%.
- For the AV18-only calculation with the present data, chi-squared per datum was approximately 50 with c=1, reducing to approximately 8 after a 3% normalization change (far outside the 0.8% systematic error).
- At Ep = 2 and 3 MeV, the AV18+UR calculation gave chi-squared per datum less than 1 for the data of Sagara et al., with less than 1% normalization change.
- The AV18 potential underbinds 3He by 0.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantitative chi-squared analysis of three-nucleon scattering data that had previously been performed for total n-d cross sections (Bochum-Cracow group, 1996) and for d-p elastic observables at 270 MeV (Sakai et al., 1999), but now applied to low-energy p-d differential cross sections. The results confirm the known underbinding problem of modern two-nucleon potentials (such as AV18, Nijmegen, CD-Bonn) in the three-nucleon system and demonstrate that including the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, which was adjusted to reproduce the 3H binding energy, also brings the elastic scattering data into agreement. The analysis enables future extension to higher energies and to polarization observables (vector and tensor analyzing powers) to search for three-nucleon force effects beyond those tied to binding energy corrections.
