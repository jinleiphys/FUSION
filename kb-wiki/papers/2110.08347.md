# Shapes, Softness and Non-Yrast Collectivity in 186W

> Non-yrast, excited states in neutron-rich $^{186}$W were populated via inelastic-scattering reactions using beams of $^{136}$Xe nuclei accelerated to 725 and 800 MeV. Levels populated in the reactions were investigated via particle-$ γ$ coincidence techniques using the Gammasphere array of High-Purity Germanium detectors and the compact heavy-ion counter, CHICO2. The $ K^π = 2 ^{+} $ ($γ$), $ K^π = 0^{+}$ and $ K^π = 2^{-} $ (octupole) rotational side bands were extended to spins $ 14\hbar $, $ 12\hbar $, and $ 13\hbar $, respectively. A staggering pattern observed in the energies of levels in the $ K^π = 2^{+} $ band was found to be consistent with a potential that gets softer to vibration in the $ γ$ degree of freedom with increasing spin. The odd-even staggering of states in the $ K^π = 2^{-}$ band was found to exhibit a phase opposite to that seen in the $ γ$ band; an effect most probably associated with Coriolis coupling to other, unobserved octupole vibrational bands in $^{186}$W.

## Key claim

The staggering pattern in the $K^\pi = 2^+$ ($\gamma$) band of $^{186}$W is consistent with a potential that becomes softer to vibration in the $\gamma$ degree of freedom as spin increases, while the opposite-phase staggering observed in the $K^\pi = 2^-$ octupole band is most probably associated with Coriolis coupling to other, unobserved octupole vibrational bands.

## Method

Non-yrast excited states in neutron-rich $^{186}$W were populated via inelastic scattering of $^{136}$Xe beams at 725 and 800 MeV (10% and 20% above the Coulomb barrier) delivered by the ATLAS accelerator at Argonne National Laboratory. Prompt $\gamma$ rays were detected with the Gammasphere array of 91 Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors in coincidence with scattered beam- and target-like ions identified by the CHICO2 compact heavy-ion counter, and particle-$\gamma$ coincidence techniques were used to reconstruct the level scheme.

## Key numbers

- The $K^\pi = 2^+$ ($\gamma$) band was extended to spin $14\hbar$, the $K^\pi = 0^+$ band to $12\hbar$, and the $K^\pi = 2^-$ (octupole) band to $13\hbar$.
- The $\gamma$-band head is located at 738 keV; the ratio $E(2^+_2)/E(2^+_1)$ is 6.0, roughly half that seen in $^{180}$W where the same band head is at 1117 keV.
- A triaxiality parameter of $\gamma = 16.0^\circ$ was deduced from the rigid geometrical model, while a mean value of $\gamma = 17.0(7)^\circ$ was inferred from prior Coulomb-excitation measurements (Wu 1996).
- The ground-state band was confirmed up to $J^\pi = 14^+$; relative yields of ground-state band transitions normalized to the $2^+ \to 0^+$ transition drop from 1.00 at spin $2\hbar$ to 0.01 at spin $14\hbar$, with the $16^+$ yield below 0.001.
- Uncertainties in energies of new $\gamma$ rays are approximately $\pm 0.3$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-yrast rotational side bands in neutron-rich tungsten isotopes, building on prior Coulomb excitation studies of $^{186}$W (McGowan 1977, Milner 1971, Kulessa 1989) and following a similar heavy-ion inelastic scattering approach that successfully accessed high-spin states in $^{180}$Hf (Ngijoi-Yogo 2007, Tandel 2008). The results enable investigation of shape softness and evolution with angular momentum in $^{186}$W, which lies beyond the maximum axial deformation in the tungsten chain and is predicted to be softening in shape, and provide data to test models of triaxiality and octupole correlations in this mass region.
