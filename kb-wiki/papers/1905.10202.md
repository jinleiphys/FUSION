# Pion scattering in the isospin I=2 channel from elongated lattices

> Pion-pion elastic scattering in the isospin I=2 channel is investigated in two-flavor dynamical lattice QCD. Six ensembles are used with lattices elongated in one of the spatial dimensions at two quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 315 MeV and 226 MeV. The energy of the low-lying states below the inelastic threshold are extracted in each case using the standard variational method.The extracted finite-volume spectrum is fitted by the inverse amplitude method simultaneously for both quark masses and extrapolated thereafter to the physical point. The resulting phase-shifts and scattering length are compared with those from experiment, leading-order chiral perturbation theory and other lattice studies. Our calculations match the experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their lattice QCD calculations of pion-pion scattering in the isospin I=2 channel match experimental results, but this agreement is achieved only after extrapolating from two unphysical pion masses (315 MeV and 226 MeV) to the physical point using the inverse amplitude method, and the extrapolation relies on chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The study uses two-flavor dynamical lattice QCD with nHYP fermions on six ensembles, including two cubic lattices and four lattices elongated in one spatial dimension. The finite-volume energy spectrum below the inelastic threshold is extracted using the variational method with two-pion interpolating fields projected to the A1+ irreducible representation, and the energies are then related to phase-shifts via Luscher's formula extended to elongated boxes and boosted frames. The extracted finite-volume spectrum is fitted simultaneously for both quark masses using the inverse amplitude method, which is unitary and matches the chiral pion-pion amplitude up to next-to-leading order, and then extrapolated to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses used: 315 MeV and 226 MeV, corresponding to two different sea quark masses
* Lattice spacing: 0.1210(2)(24) fm for the 315 MeV ensembles and 0.1215(3)(24) fm for the 226 MeV ensembles
* Scattering length at 315 MeV: m_pi a_0 = -0.20(3) with chi^2/dof = 5.6/(6-2) from effective range expansion fit
* Scattering length at 226 MeV: m_pi a_0 = -0.10(2) with chi^2/dof = 3.3/(6-2) from effective range expansion fit
* Extrapolated scattering length at physical pion mass: m_pi a_0 = -0.0455(16) from chiral perturbation theory fit with l_pi_pi = -1.09(2.52) and chi^2/dof = 0.74/(2-1)
* Number of LapH modes used: N_v = 100, corresponding to a smearing radius of roughly 0.5 fm
* Number of gauge configurations: 300-400 per ensemble

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of pion-pion scattering in the I=2 channel, including calculations of the scattering length by Sharpe (1992), Kuramashi (1993), Gupta (1993), Yamazaki (2004), Aoki (2005), Beane (2005, 2007), Feng (2009), Yagi (2011), Fu (2013), Sasaki (2013), and Kawai (2017), as well as higher-energy studies by Dudek (2012), Bulava (2016), and Akahoshi (2019). The use of elongated boxes follows the approach of Feng (2004) and Lee (2017) to generate additional phase-shift points. The results enable a future global analysis of all three pion-pion scattering channels (I=0,1,2) using the inverse amplitude method, and provide necessary input for three-body calculations of the 3pi+ system above threshold as developed by Mai (2018).
