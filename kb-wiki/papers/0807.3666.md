# Perspectives on heavy-quarkonium production at the LHC

> We summarise the perspectives on heavy-quarkonium production at the LHC, both for proton-proton and heavy-ion runs, as emanating from the round table held at the HLPW 2008 Conference. The main topics are: present experimental and theoretical knowledge, experimental capabilities, open questions, recent theoretical advances and potentialities linked to some new observables.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the perspectives on heavy-quarkonium production at the LHC from a round-table discussion, emphasizing that while heavy quarkonia are proposed as probes of the quark-gluon plasma, a consistent theoretical picture predicting both cross sections and polarization for charmonium at the Tevatron and RHIC is not yet available, and that bottomonium predictions appear more reliable than those for charmonium.

## Method

The authors convened a round table at the HLPW 2008 Conference to review the experimental capabilities of the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb detectors for quarkonium measurements in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions. They surveyed the current theoretical status of non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and color-singlet calculations, and discussed open issues including cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, absorption, Cronin effect) and the interpretation of J/psi suppression as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for J/psi and Upsilon production in pp collisions at 14 TeV are expected to be around 0.4 mb and 7 microbarn, respectively.
- ALICE can detect charmonia down to about 100 MeV/c in the dimuon channel at forward rapidity (-4 < eta < -2.5), with mass resolution of about 30 (70) MeV/c^2 for J/psi in the dielectron (dimuon) channel.
- ATLAS and CMS muon trigger thresholds are P_T(mu1) > 6 GeV/c, P_T(mu2) > 4 GeV/c (ATLAS) and P_T(mu1) > 3 GeV/c, P_T(mu2) > 3 GeV/c (CMS); reconstructed events in 10 pb^-1 are 2 x 10^5 (ATLAS) and 3 x 10^5 (CMS) for J/psi, and 5 x 10^4 (ATLAS) and 1 x 10^5 (CMS) for Upsilon.
- The dissociation temperature pattern is T_d[psi'] approx T_d[chi_c] < T_d[Upsilon(3S)] < T_d[J/psi] approx T_d[Upsilon(2S)] < T_d[Upsilon(1S)].
- About 100 c-cbar pairs are expected in central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, while only about 5 b-bbar pairs are expected.
- Feed-down from B decays is expected to account for about 20% of the total J/psi yield if no cold nuclear matter effect is considered.
- The effective break-up cross-section from NA50 is sigma_abs(J/psi) = 4.2 +/- 0.5 mb and sigma_abs(psi') = 7.7 +/- 0.9 mb at SPS; at RHIC, sigma_abs(J/psi) = 2.8^{+1.7}_{-1.4} mb (or 2.2^{+1.6}_{-1.5} mb depending on nPDF parametrization).
- LO parametrizations EKS98 and EPS08 give nPDF/PDF ratios for gluon shadowing in Pb that differ by about a factor of ten at x = 10^{-4} and Q^2 = 1.69 GeV^2.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of heavy-quarkonium production from earlier reviews (Brambilla, Lansberg 2006, Bedjidian 2004, Vogt 1999, Kopeliovich 2003) and addresses the long-standing puzzle from CDF measurements (Abe 1997) that NRQCD predictions of transverse polarization for J/psi and psi' at high P_T are not supported by data. It builds on recent NLO and NNLO QCD corrections (Campbell 2007, Artoisenet 2007, NNLO) that improve agreement for Upsilon production, and on s-channel cut approaches (schannelcut) that reproduce J/psi spectra at intermediate P_T. The work enables future LHC measurements to test cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, absorption) and quark-gluon plasma signatures, particularly through bottomonium suppression and regeneration studies.
