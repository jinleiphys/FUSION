# On the consistency of the spindown behavior of young and old pulsars

> We study the spindown of pulsars due to gravitational wave emission and show that r-modes in neutron stars provide a quantitative explanation for the observed low rotation frequencies of young pulsars if the r-mode saturation amplitude is sufficiently large. In contrast for such large saturation amplitudes old hadronic millisecond pulsars would spin down much faster than observed. We discuss resolutions of this apparent discrepancy that could make this mechanism consistent with the observational data.

# On the consistency of the spindown behavior of young and old pulsars

## Key claim
R-mode oscillations in neutron stars can quantitatively explain the low rotation frequencies of young pulsars if the r-mode saturation amplitude is sufficiently large (greater than about 10^-2), but the same large saturation amplitudes would cause old hadronic millisecond pulsars to spin down much faster than observed, creating an apparent discrepancy that may be resolved if neutron stars transform into quark stars during their evolution.

## Method
The authors derived semi-analytic power-law expressions for the r-mode spindown evolution of neutron stars, encoding microphysical material properties (shear and bulk viscosity, specific heat, neutrino emissivity) into dimensionless constants. They solved the coupled evolution equations for temperature, rotation frequency, and r-mode amplitude along the steady-state curve where heating from viscous dissipation balances neutrino cooling, and compared the resulting spindown paths in frequency-spindown rate space to observational data from the ATNF pulsar catalog.

## Key numbers
- Final spin frequency of a standard 1.4 solar mass neutron star after r-mode spindown: approximately 61.4 Hz, with extremely weak dependence on microphysical parameters and saturation amplitude (scaling as alpha_sat^(-5/92))
- Spindown time for a neutron star: approximately 12.3 years, with strong dependence on saturation amplitude (scaling as alpha_sat^(-77/46))
- For young pulsars, required saturation amplitudes are in the range 0.01 to 0.1 at core temperatures of 10^9 K to 10^10 K
- For old millisecond pulsars, r-mode saturation amplitudes must be less than 10^-7 to be compatible with observed spindown rates
- The ratio of young to old saturation amplitudes required is approximately 10^6 over a temperature ratio of approximately 50, requiring a temperature exponent beta of about 3.5

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous semi-analytic analysis (Alford 2012) of r-mode spindown in young pulsars to address the apparent conflict with observations of old millisecond pulsars. The r-mode instability mechanism was originally proposed by Andersson (1997) as an explanation for the low spin frequencies of young neutron stars. The paper shows that standard neutron star models with constant saturation amplitudes cannot simultaneously explain both young and old pulsar data, and proposes that deconfined quark matter (following the strange matter hypothesis of Witten 1984) could resolve the discrepancy, as quark stars have a stability window that allows high spin frequencies. This scenario will be developed in a forthcoming work.
