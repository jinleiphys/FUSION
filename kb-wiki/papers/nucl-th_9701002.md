# Mapping the proton drip-line up to A=70

> Coulomb energy differences between mirror nuclei with $A \le 70$ are calculated within the framework of the nuclear shell model using an effective Coulomb plus isovector and isotensor interaction. Absolute binding energies for proton-rich nuclei are predicted by adding the calculated Coulomb shifts to experimentally measured binding energies for the neutron-rich mirror. The location of the proton drip-line is investigated, as well as candidates for the exotic decay mode known as di-proton emission. Taking into account the lifetimes of competing decay modes and limits imposed by experimental setups, it is concluded that the best candidates for the observation of correlated di-proton emission are $^{45}$Fe, $^{48}$Ni, and $^{63}$Se.

## Key claim

The paper predicts absolute binding energies for proton-rich nuclei up to A=70 by adding shell-model Coulomb shifts to experimentally measured binding energies of their neutron-rich mirror nuclei, with the hedge that the theoretical uncertainty is estimated at 50–200 keV and that the Thomas-Ehrman shift near the drip line may lie within this uncertainty but is not explicitly accounted for in the empirical isospin-nonconserving interaction.

## Method

Coulomb energy differences between mirror nuclei in the fp shell (0f7/2, 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2 orbitals) were computed using the shell-model code OXBASH with the FPD6 Hamiltonian plus an empirical isovector and isotensor (isospin-nonconserving) interaction fitted to experimental b-coefficients. For A ≤ 59, truncations allowed up to four particles excited out of the 0f7/2 and 1p3/2 orbits into the 0f5/2 and 1p1/2 orbits, keeping dimensions under 14,000; for A > 60, the 0f7/2 orbit was closed and single-particle energies were modified to reproduce levels of 57Ni. Absolute binding energies for proton-rich nuclei were obtained by adding the calculated Coulomb shift (2bT) to the experimental binding energy of the neutron-rich mirror from the 1993 Audi–Wapstra compilation.

## Key numbers

- Predicted binding energies for proton-rich nuclei with 46 ≤ A ≤ 69 are given with theoretical uncertainties of 50–200 keV; the rms deviation of the shell-model b-coefficients from experimental data is approximately 33 keV for fp-shell nuclei, increased to 45 keV for A > 60.
- The liquid-drop parameterization of Möller and Nix (1988) yields b-coefficients that are 100–200 keV smaller than the shell-model values in the mass region 52 ≤ A ≤ 64; for the 59Zn–59Cu pair, the Möller–Nix b-coefficient is 9.683 MeV, which is 200 keV smaller than the experimental value of 9.881(40) MeV.
- 65As is predicted to be unbound to proton emission by 0.428(254) MeV; the uncertainty in the one-proton separation energy leads to a range of 16 orders of magnitude in the partial half-life for proton emission (1.4 × 10⁻¹² s to 1.6 × 10⁴ s).
- The best candidates for correlated di-proton emission are identified as 45Fe, 48Ni, and 63Se, based on two-proton separation energies between 0.9 and 1.4 MeV and practical lifetime constraints of 10⁻⁸–10⁻³ s.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach of Ormand (1996) and Brown (1991) for A ≤ 55 to the mass range 46 ≤ A ≤ 70, and agrees with the binding energies of Cole (1996) for A ≤ 52. It contradicts the macroscopic-microscopic model of Möller and Nix (1988), which underestimates Coulomb displacement energies by 100–200 keV in the A = 52–64 region and poorly reproduces the mass of the neutron-rich analog 59Cu. The predicted binding energies and two-proton separation energies enable identification of the best experimental candidates for di-proton decay (45Fe, 48Ni, 63Se) and inform the rp-process path, particularly regarding whether 65As is a waiting point at 64Ge.
