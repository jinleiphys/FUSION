# Implications of the Oklo phenomenon in a chiral approach to nuclear matter

> It has been customary to use data from the Oklo natural nuclear reactor to place bounds on the change that has occurred in the electromagnetic fine structure constant $α$ over the last 2 billion years. Alternatively, an analysis could be based on a recently proposed expression for shifts in resonance energies which relates them to changes in both $α$ and the average $m_q$ of the $u$ and $d$ current quark masses, and which makes explicit the dependence on mass number $A$ and atomic number $Z$. (Recent model independent results on hadronic $σ$-terms suggest sensitivity to the strange quark mass is negligible.) The most sophisticated analysis, to date, of the quark mass term invokes a calculation of the nuclear mean-field within the Walecka model of quantum hadrodynamics. We comment on this study and consider an alternative in which the link to low-energy quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and its pattern of chiral symmetry-breaking is more readily discernible. Specifically, we investigate the sensitivity to changes in the pion mass $M_π$ of a single nucleon potential determined by an in-medium chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) calculation which includes virtual $\mathrmΔ$-excitations. Subject to some reasonable assumptions about low-energy constants (LECs), we confirm that the $m_q$-contribution to resonance shifts is enhanced by a factor of 10 or so relative to the $α$-term and deduce that the Oklo data for Sm imply that $|m_q(\mathrm{Oklo})- m_q(\mathrm{now})| \lesssim 10^{-9}m_q(\mathrm{now})$.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the quark-mass contribution to resonance energy shifts in the Oklo neutron capture reaction on Sm-149 is enhanced by a factor of about 10 relative to the electromagnetic fine-structure constant term, but the analysis relies on reasonable assumptions about low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory and the bound on quark mass variation is derived subject to the condition that the radius-parameter term in the shift formula is negligible.

## Method

The author uses an in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculation of the single-nucleon potential in symmetric nuclear matter, including virtual Delta-excitations, to determine the sensitivity of the potential depth U0 to changes in the pion mass M_pi. The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation then connects changes in M_pi to changes in the average light quark mass m_q. The resulting sensitivity coefficient for the quark-mass contribution to resonance shifts is compared with the electromagnetic contribution using the recently proposed expression for resonance energy shifts that depends on mass number A and atomic number Z.

## Key numbers

- The bound on the change in the average light quark mass from Oklo Sm data is |m_q(Oklo) - m_q(now)| less than or approximately equal to 10^{-9} m_q(now)
- The coefficient a for the quark-mass term in the resonance shift formula is estimated to be approximately -40 MeV
- The coefficient b for the electromagnetic term is approximately 0.5 MeV (to within a factor of 2)
- For Sm-149, the factor Z^2/A^{4/3} equals 4.87
- The current resonance energy for n + Sm-149 is E_r = 97.3 meV
- The bound on the resonance energy shift is |Delta E_r| < 11 meV
- The sensitivity coefficient K_{M_pi}^q = 0.49 (to two significant figures)
- The fractional change in the potential depth from long- and intermediate-range interactions is delta U_0/U_0 = -0.28 delta m_q/m_q
- The fractional change including contact interactions gives delta U_0/U_0 approximately 0.8 delta m_q/m_q

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Flambaum (2009) who used variational Monte Carlo calculations with the Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX interactions to estimate the quark-mass sensitivity, but whose results were sensitive to the properties of an exchange boson with no first-principles determination of the sensitivity coefficient K_V^q. The paper also comments on the Walecka model approach of Flambaum (2009) and notes that the strong scalar and vector mean fields may be induced by changes of QCD vacuum condensates rather than exchange bosons, following the work of Finelli, Kaiser, Vretenar, and Weise (2004, 2006). The chiral effective field theory approach used here enables a more direct link to low-energy QCD and its pattern of chiral symmetry breaking, and the results enable improved bounds on time variation of fundamental constants from Oklo data.
