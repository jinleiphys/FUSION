# Extracting shear viscosity of the Quark Gluon Plasma in the presence of bulk viscosity

> One of the most remarkable features of the Quark Gluon Plasma is its nearly perfect fluidity behavior indicated by the small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio obtained from fitting relativistic viscous hydrodynamics flow harmonics to experimental data. In recent years, bulk viscosity has also been considered in the context of event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamics and it has been found to have a non-trivial interplay with shear viscosity. In this paper some of the issues are discussed that require further work when extracting the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in the presence of a non-zero bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

Extracting the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from experimental flow harmonics is strongly dependent on the choice of bulk viscosity temperature dependence and the out-of-equilibrium corrections at freeze-out, meaning that the commonly quoted small shear viscosity values are not unique unless bulk viscosity is simultaneously constrained.

## Method

The author performed 2+1 event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamical simulations using the v-USPhydro code with NeXus initial conditions, solving the equations of motion with both shear and bulk viscosity effects via Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics. Two different theoretical parametrizations of the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio as a function of temperature were tested: one from a hadron resonance gas model with Hagedorn states and one from non-conformal holography. The elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum were computed for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the Hagedorn-state-based bulk viscosity parametrization (zeta/s^(1) with peak zeta/s^(1)_max approx 0.35), the required shear viscosity to entropy density ratio increased from eta/s=0.1 (with zeta/s=0) to eta/s=0.24.
- For the non-conformal holography bulk viscosity parametrization (zeta/s^(2)), the required shear viscosity increased only modestly to eta/s=0.145.
- The percentage change in mean transverse momentum from shear-only to shear-plus-bulk, including out-of-equilibrium corrections, was -12% for zeta/s^(1) and -1.5% for zeta/s^(2).
- Without out-of-equilibrium corrections (local equilibrium only), the percentage change in mean transverse momentum was -3.6% for zeta/s^(1) and -2% for zeta/s^(2).
- A freeze-out temperature of T_FO=120 MeV was used, and hadrons with masses up to 1.7 GeV were included in the decay treatment.

## Context

This work extends the study of bulk viscosity effects in event-by-event hydrodynamics previously reported by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2013, 2014) and Gardim et al. (2014), and directly compares two theoretical bulk viscosity parametrizations: one from Ryu et al. (2015) using Hagedorn states and one from Finazzo et al. (2014) using non-conformal holography. The results show that extracting a unique shear viscosity from data requires simultaneously constraining bulk viscosity and its out-of-equilibrium corrections, and that mean transverse momentum may serve as a distinguishing observable for different bulk viscosity models.
