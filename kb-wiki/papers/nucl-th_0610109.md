# Relativistic Calculation of Hadronic Baryon Decays

> The description of baryon resonance decays represents a major challenge of strong interaction physics. We will report on a relativistic approach to mesonic decays of light and strange baryon resonances within constituent quark models. The calculations are performed in the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics, specifically focussing on the strange sector. It is found that the relativistic predictions generally underestimate the experimental data. The nonrelativistic approximation of the approach leads to the decay operator of the elementary emission model. It is seen that the nonrelativistic reduction has considerable effects on the decay widths.

## Key claim

The relativistic point-form spectator model (PFSM) predictions for pion decay widths of strange baryon resonances generally underestimate experimental data, with only the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1670)\) showing overpredictions under certain conditions (the former due to an overly high theoretical mass, the latter possibly due to resonance coupling).

## Method

The authors calculated mesonic decay widths of light and strange baryon resonances using the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics within constituent quark models (CQMs), employing both the Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) and one-gluon exchange (OGE) hyperfine interactions. The decay operator was constructed via the point-form spectator model (PFSM), where only one quark directly couples to the emitted meson, and the nonrelativistic reduction of this operator yields the elementary emission model (EEM). Decay widths were computed using both theoretical and experimental resonance masses for comparison.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda(1405)\) \(\rightarrow \Sigma\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 55 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((50\pm2)\) MeV; nonrelativistic EEM gives 320 MeV (GBE, theoretical mass)
- \(\Lambda(1520)\) \(\rightarrow \Sigma\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 5 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((6.55\pm0.16)^{+0.04}_{-0.04}\) MeV
- \(\Lambda(1670)\) \(\rightarrow \Sigma\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 69 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((14.0\pm5.3)^{+8.3}_{-2.5}\) MeV; nonrelativistic EEM gives 620 MeV (GBE, theoretical mass)
- \(\Sigma(1385)\) \(\rightarrow \Sigma\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 3.1 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((4.2\pm0.5)^{+0.7}_{-0.5}\) MeV
- \(\Sigma(1385)\) \(\rightarrow \Lambda\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 11 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((31.3\pm0.5)^{+4.4}_{-4.3}\) MeV
- \(\Xi(1530)\) \(\rightarrow \Xi\pi\): relativistic GBE prediction 2.2 MeV (theoretical mass) vs. experimental \((9.9)^{+1.7}_{-1.9}\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the Graz group's previous relativistic CQM calculations for \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) decays of \(N\) and \(\Delta\) resonances (Melde 2005) into the strange baryon sector, providing the first covariant results for \(\pi\) decays of strange baryon resonances. It contrasts with earlier nonrelativistic or relativised studies (Stancu 1989, Capstick 1994, Geiger 1994, Ackleh 1996, Theussl 2000) and complements a Bethe-Salpeter approach by the Bonn group (Metsch 2003, 2004). The results enable future coupled-channel formulations and studies of additional Fock components, as already explored in meson decays (Krassnigg 2003, 2004) and baryon resonances (Li 2005, 2006).
