# Collective longitudinal polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at very high energy

> We study the polarization of particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at very high energy along the beam direction within a relativistic hydrodynamic framework. We show that this component of the polarization decreases much slower with center-of-mass energy compared to the transverse component, even in the ideal longitudinal boost-invariant scenario with non-fluctuating initial state, and that it can be measured by taking advantage of its quadrupole structure in the transverse momentum plane. In the ideal longitudinal boost-invariant scenario, the polarization is proportional to the gradient of temperature at the hadronization and its measurement can provide important information about the cooling rate of the Quark Gluon Plasma around the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The longitudinal (beam-direction) component of particle polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions decreases much more slowly with increasing center-of-mass energy than the transverse component, and remains measurable even at LHC energies in the TeV range, even under the idealized assumptions of longitudinal boost invariance, ideal fluid dynamics, and no initial-state fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of non-central heavy-ion collisions using averaged initial states from the Monte Carlo Glauber model (for 200 and 2760 GeV) and the UrQMD cascade (for 7.7 and 19.6 GeV). They calculated the mean spin vector of primary Lambda hyperons at mid-rapidity using the thermal vorticity formalism, assuming local thermodynamic equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom, and extracted the second harmonic coefficient f2 of the longitudinal polarization component as a function of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The transverse polarization component P^y decreases by about a factor of 10 between sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV and 200 GeV, while the longitudinal second harmonic coefficient f2 decreases by only 35% over the same energy range.
- The p_T-integrated mean value of f2 stays around 0.2% at all collision energies studied (7.7, 19.6, 200, and 2760 GeV).
- The longitudinal polarization pattern at mid-rapidity follows a sin(2*phi) dependence, with the second harmonic coefficient f2(p_T) = 2 * (dT/d_tau) / (m*T) * v2(p_T) under simplifying assumptions.
- The Lambda weak decay constant used is alpha = 0.642.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic polarization framework previously applied to global polarization along the angular momentum direction (Becattini 2013, Becattini 2015, Karpenko 2016) and the STAR experimental observations (STAR 2017). It contrasts with the rapid energy decrease of the transverse polarization component measured by STAR and ALICE, and provides a specific observable that survives the "minimal vorticity" scenario of Bjorken longitudinal boost invariance. The predicted quadrupole pattern enables experimental measurement without requiring identification of the reaction plane orientation, and the results enable direct extraction of the temperature gradient at hadronization around the QCD critical temperature.
