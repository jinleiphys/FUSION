# Heavy-ion Physics at a Fixed-Target Experiment Using the LHC Proton and Lead Beams (AFTER@LHC): Feasibility Studies for Quarkonium and Drell-Yan Production

> We outline the case for heavy-ion-physics studies using the multi-TeV lead LHC beams in the fixed-target mode. After a brief contextual reminder, we detail the possible contributions of AFTER@LHC to heavy-ion physics with a specific emphasis on quarkonia. We then present performance simulations for a selection of observables. These show that $Υ(nS)$, $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ production in heavy-ion collisions can be studied in new energy and rapidity domains with the LHCb and ALICE detectors. We also discuss the relevance to analyse the Drell-Yan pair production in asymmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions to study the factorisation of the nuclear modification of partonic densities and of further quarkonia to restore their status of golden probes of the quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Key claim

AFTER@LHC can study quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 72 GeV, a region where charmonium recombination is not expected to play an important role, thereby providing a cleaner baseline for quark-gluon plasma diagnostics than is available at higher collider energies.

## Method

The authors performed feasibility simulations for quarkonium (Υ(nS), J/ψ, ψ(2S)) and Drell-Yan production using the LHCb and ALICE detectors in fixed-target mode with LHC proton and lead beams. They evaluated the accessible kinematic ranges and statistical precision for these observables in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and compared model predictions for elliptic flow from a pure cascade (UrQMD) versus a hybrid model with hydrodynamic evolution (vHLLE+UrQMD).

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair for AFTER@LHC heavy-ion collisions: √sNN = 72 GeV
- Energy density in heavy nucleus-nucleus collisions at similar energy per nucleon: ε ∼ 2 GeV·fm⁻³
- Initial volume before expansion in heavy nucleus-nucleus collisions: approximately 150 fm³
- Energy density in proton-proton collisions at Ec.m.s. ∼ 20 GeV: ε ∼ 0.4 GeV·fm⁻³
- Average multiplicity in pp collisions at Ec.m.s. ∼ 20 GeV: approximately 3 per unit rapidity
- Average momentum in pp collisions at Ec.m.s. ∼ 20 GeV: approximately 0.5 GeV/c
- Lattice QCD transition temperature: 160 MeV
- Pseudorapidity coverage for Drell-Yan studies with LHCb-like detector: 2 ≲ ylab ≲ 5
- Bjorken-x range for target partons in Drell-Yan: x₂ ∈ [0.05:0.2] (antishadowing region)
- Transverse momentum range for elliptic flow studies: pT range = [0.2, 2.0] GeV
- Centrality for elliptic flow predictions: 20-30% (impact parameter b = [6.8, 8.3] fm)
- NA60 mass resolution at ω and φ peaks: 20 MeV

## Context

This paper extends the fixed-target heavy-ion program from earlier SPS experiments (NA38, NA50, NA60) to LHC energies, complementing the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and the NA61 experiment at SPS energies. The work builds on previous feasibility studies for pp and pA collisions at AFTER@LHC (Massacrier 2015) and aims to resolve open questions about the interplay between cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, energy loss, comovers) and quark-gluon plasma suppression of quarkonia, as well as to test the factorisation of nuclear parton distribution function effects via Drell-Yan measurements.
