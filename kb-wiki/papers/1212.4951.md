# Mesonic and non-mesonic branching ratios of K^- absorption in the nuclear medium

> The branching ratios of K^- absorption at rest in nuclear matter are evaluated from the K^- self-energy by using the chiral unitary approach for the s-wave \bar{K} N amplitude. We find that both the mesonic and non-mesonic absorption potentials are dominated by the Λ(1405) contributions. We also observe that the mesonic absorption ratio [π^{-} Σ^{+}] / [π^{+} Σ^{-}] increases as a function of nuclear density due to the interference between Λ(1405) and the I=1 non-resonant background, which is consistent with experimental results. The fraction of the non-mesonic absorption is evaluated to be about 30 % at the saturation density. The branching ratios of the K^- absorption at rest into deuteron and 4He are also calculated.

## Key claim

The branching ratios of stopped K- absorption in nuclear matter are evaluated from the K- self-energy using the chiral unitary approach, with the hedge that the results are for a simplified condition of stopped K- absorption and that a realistic treatment of the bound kaon with finite nuclei is necessary for quantitative discussions.

## Method

The mesonic and non-mesonic absorption potentials for K- in nuclear matter were calculated as the imaginary part of the K- self-energy as a function of nuclear density, using the chiral unitary approach for the s-wave antikaon-nucleon amplitude. The mesonic absorption was obtained from a one-body self-energy process, while the non-mesonic absorption was calculated using a two-body absorption model with one-meson exchange of Nambu-Goldstone bosons between baryons. Nuclear matter was described using the Thomas-Fermi approximation.

## Key numbers

* Non-mesonic absorption fraction evaluated to be about 30% at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3)
* Mesonic absorption ratio R_+- = [pi- Sigma+] / [pi+ Sigma-] becomes about 1.6 at saturation density
* R_+- equals unity at relatively lower density, rho_N approximately 0.08 fm^-3
* For deuteron target: R_+- = 0.84, non-mesonic fraction = 3.4%
* For 4He target: R_+- = 1.32, non-mesonic fraction = 18.6%
* Non-mesonic absorption ratios: [Lambda p] / [Sigma0 p] approximately 1, [Sigma+ n] / [Sigma0 p] approximately 2, with marginal density dependence

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on in-medium K- self-energy by Waas (1997) and provides theoretical interpretation for experimental branching ratios measured by Tovee (1971), Veirs (1970), and Katz (1970) for stopped K- absorption on various nuclei. The results enable comparison with recent experimental data from Agnello (2011) on p-shell nuclei and support the interpretation that the Lambda(1405) resonance acts as a doorway state for both mesonic and non-mesonic K- absorption in the nuclear medium.
