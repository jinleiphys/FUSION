# Shell effects in nuclear charge radii based on Skyrme density functionals

> A unified description of the charge radii throughout the entire nuclide chart plays an essential role for our understanding of nuclear structure and fundamental nuclear interactions. In this work, the influence of new term, which catches the spirit of neutron and proton pairs condensation around Fermi surface, on the charge radii has been investigated based on the Skyrme density functionals with the effective forces SLy5 and SkM$^{*}$. The differential charge radii of even-even Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes are employed to evaluate the validity of this theoretical model. Meanwhile, the results obtained by the relativistic density functional with the effective Lagrangian NL3 are also shown for the quantitative comparison. The calculated results suggest that the modified model can improve the trend of changes of the differential charge radii along Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains, especially the shell closure effect at the neutron numbers $N=28$, 82 and 126. The shell quenching phenomena of charge radii can also be predicted at the neutron number $N=50$ along the corresponding Ni and Sn isotopes, respectively. The inverted parabolic-like shapes between the two fully filled shells can also be observed, but the amplitude is gradually weakened from Ca to Pb isotopic chains. Combining the existing literatures, it suggests that the discontinuous behavior in nuclear charge radii can be described well by considering the influence of neutron Cooper pairs condensation around Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The modified Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model, which incorporates a term representing the difference between neutron-pair and proton-pair condensation around the Fermi surface, can improve the description of differential charge radii trends in even-even Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes, particularly at shell closures N=28, 82, and 126; however, systematic overestimations persist for some calcium isotopes (e.g., 46-50Ca) and deviations remain for lead isotopes near 208Pb, indicating the correction is not fully quantitative across all isotopic chains.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-HFB calculations using the SLy5 and SkM* effective forces with mixed-type density-dependent zero-range pairing forces, adjusting pairing strengths to empirical gaps of 44Ca, 64Ni, 122Sn, and 198Pb. They added a correction term a0/√A * |Dn - Dp| to the standard charge-radius formula, where Dn and Dp measure neutron and proton Cooper-pair condensation around the Fermi surface, with a0=0.561 fm² fixed from prior work on potassium and calcium isotopes. Differential mean-square charge radii relative to reference nuclei (40Ca, 58Ni, 108Sn, 182Pb) and three-point differences Δr^(2N) were computed for even-even isotopes and compared to experimental data and to results from the relativistic RHB model with the NL3 Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths V0 (MeV fm³) for SLy5: Ca 245.2, Ni 362.8, Sn 410.0, Pb 468.0; for SkM*: Ca 262.8, Ni 313.5, Sn 366.2, Pb 396.0.
- The correction parameter a0 = 0.561 fm² was fixed from prior work on potassium and calcium isotopes.
- The proton finite-size contribution added to the charge-radius formula is 0.7056 fm².
- Nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is used in the pairing force definition.
- The modified HFB* model reproduces the inverted parabolic-like shape of charge radii between N=20 and N=28 for calcium isotopes and the shell closure at N=28, though 46,48,50Ca values are systematically overestimated.
- Shell quenching at N=50 is predicted by HFB* for both Ni and Sn isotopes; the kink at N=82 in Sn and at N=126 in Pb is reproduced by HFB* but not by standard HFB.
- For lead isotopes, the RHB* model shows large deviations for 202-206Pb and fails to describe the shell effect at 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the approach of An et al. (2024, Phys. Rev. C 109.064302), which introduced the neutron-pair condensation correction in the relativistic mean-field model, to the non-relativistic Skyrme EDF framework using SLy5 and SkM* forces. It addresses the known failure of standard Skyrme functionals to reproduce shell quenching and kink phenomena in charge radii, as documented by Sharma (1993) and more recent work (Phys. Rev. Lett. 128.022502). The results enable systematic comparison between relativistic (NL3) and non-relativistic (Skyrme) density functionals for charge radii across multiple isotopic chains, though the overestimations in neutron-deficient calcium and lead regions indicate that further refinements or additional mechanisms (e.g., deformation effects) are needed for a fully unified description.
