# Theoretical Overview on (Hidden) Charm in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Recent developments in the theoretical evaluation of charmonium production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHIC's) are discussed. In particular, the consequences of equilibrium properties of open and hidden charm states -- accessible, {\it e.g.}, in QCD lattice gauge calculations -- are assessed. These include abundances as well as formation and dissociation rates of charmonia in both hadronic and quark-gluon matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at RHIC energies, the equilibrium abundance of J/psi mesons in the quark-gluon plasma becomes comparable to primordial production, making regeneration via the backward reaction in charmonium dissociation reactions potentially significant; this contrasts with SPS energies where the equilibrium level is much smaller than primordial production and regeneration can be neglected.

## Method

The authors use rate equations for J/psi abundances in a fireball, incorporating chemical off-equilibrium via a charm-quark fugacity matched to the total number of c-cbar pairs from hard nucleon-nucleon collisions. They evaluate equilibrium densities and relaxation times using parton-induced dissociation cross sections (gluodissociation and quasifree dissociation) and compare with lattice QCD results for heavy-quark free energies and spectral functions. For the hadronic phase, they consider inelastic reactions such as pi J/psi to D Dbar* and assess the impact of in-medium D-meson mass reductions.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass m_c approximately 1.3 GeV/c^2, large enough for production to be dominated by primordial nucleon-nucleon collisions
- In Pb-Pb at SPS energy sqrt(s)=17.3 AGeV, intermediate-mass dimuon excess (M_mu mu=1.5-2.5 GeV) increases with centrality up to a factor of about 2.5 in most central events
- Thermal dimuon radiation from a fireball with initial temperatures T_i=200-250 MeV can account for the intermediate-mass dimuon excess
- Lattice QCD results show J/psi spectral functions exhibit well-defined states up to T approximately 1.5 T_c, with widths Gamma_eta_c,psi increasing to about 0.2-0.5 GeV slightly above T_c
- Using standard gluodissociation cross section leads to relaxation times tau_psi greater than or equal to 10 fm/c for temperatures T less than or equal to 200 MeV, falling to below 1 fm/c at T greater than or equal to 300 MeV
- For hadronic phase, assuming average cross section of 1 mb and total hadron density n_had=3 n_0 approximately 0.5 fm^{-3}, chemical relaxation time tau_psi approximately 30-40 fm/c
- A reduction of D-meson masses by 80 MeV at T_c=170 MeV lowers the J/psi equilibrium level by a factor of 2

## Context

This theoretical overview extends the work of Matsui and Satz (1986) on charmonium suppression as a deconfinement probe, and engages with statistical hadronization models proposed by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) and Gorenstein et al. (2001). The paper synthesizes lattice QCD results from Karsch, Laermann, and Peikert (2001) and Datta et al. (2002) showing charmonia survive as resonances above T_c, and discusses implications for rate-equation approaches at SPS and RHIC energies. It enables future work on distinguishing between suppression-only and regeneration scenarios using upcoming RHIC data on open charm observables and excited charmonia.
