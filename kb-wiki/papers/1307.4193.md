# Statistical pairing fluctuation and phase transition in $^{94}Mo$

> In the framework of BCS model, we have applied the isothermal probability distribution to take into account the statistical fluctuations in calculation of thermodynamical properties of nuclei. The energy and the heat capacity are calculated in $^{94}Mo$ nucleus using the mean gap parameter. The results are compared with the values obtained based on the most probable values, experimental data as well as some other theoretical models. We have shown that heat capacity versus temperature behaves smoothly instead of singular behaviour predicted by the standard BCS model. Also a smooth peak in heat capacity is observed which is a signature of transition from normal to super fluid phase.

# Statistical pairing fluctuation and phase transition in $^{94}Mo$

## Key claim

Including statistical fluctuations via the isothermal probability distribution removes the unphysical singularities in heat capacity predicted by standard BCS theory for finite nuclei, producing a smooth S-shaped heat capacity curve with a peak that signals the normal-to-superfluid phase transition; however, the agreement with experimental data is limited to temperatures above 0.4 MeV, and below 0.5 MeV the results coincide with SPA and NPSPA methods rather than providing a distinct improvement.

## Method

The authors applied the BCS model with an isothermal probability distribution $P(\Delta) \propto \exp(\Omega(\beta,\Delta))$ to compute the mean gap parameter $\bar{\Delta}$ via integration over all gap values, rather than using the most probable gap from the standard BCS gap equation. Single-particle energies from the Nilsson potential with quadrupole deformation $\beta=0.15$ were used for $^{94}Mo$, treating neutrons and protons as two independent non-interacting thermodynamic systems. The mean gap parameter and its partial derivatives were then used to calculate energy, entropy, and heat capacity from modified thermodynamic relations that include fluctuation terms absent in standard BCS.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature pairing gaps extracted via the three-point method: $\Delta_{0p} = 1.7$ MeV for protons and $\Delta_{0n} = 1.15$ MeV for neutrons in $^{94}Mo$
- Critical temperature for neutrons: $T_c = 0.72$ MeV, defined as the temperature where the gap parameter becomes zero
- Deformation parameter used: $\beta = 0.15$ for $^{94}Mo$
- Spin-orbit and centrifugal parameters: $k_n = 0.06385$, $\mu_n = 0.508004$ for neutrons; $k_p = 0.06928$, $\mu_p = 0.554006$ for protons
- Reference zero-temperature gaps from prior work: $\Delta_{0p} = 1.6$ MeV and $\Delta_{0n} = 1.2$ MeV (Kaneko et al.)

## Context

This work extends the BCS treatment of nuclear pairing by incorporating thermal fluctuations through the Landau isothermal probability distribution, addressing the known failure of standard BCS to reproduce the smooth heat capacity observed experimentally by the Oslo Group (Kaneko et al.). The results are compared with the Static-Path Approximation (SPA) and Random-Phase Approximation plus SPA (RPA+SPA) methods from prior work (Kaneko et al.), showing agreement with those approaches below 0.5 MeV. The method enables a fluctuation-corrected description of the thermal pairing phase transition in medium-mass nuclei without the singularities of standard BCS.
