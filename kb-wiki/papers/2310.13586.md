# The impact of nuclear reactions on the neutron-star g-mode spectrum

> Mature neutron stars are expected exhibit gravity g-modes due to stratification caused by varying composition. These modes are affected by nuclear reactions, leading to complex (damped) mode frequencies and the suppression of high order g-modes, in contrast with the common non-dissipative analysis which leads to an infinite g-mode spectrum. Focusing on the transition between the fast and slow reaction regimes, we examine the impact of nuclear reactions on the g-mode spectrum. The general framework for the analysis is presented along with sample numerical results for the BSk21 equation of state and the standard Urca reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear reactions (specifically Urca processes) cause neutron-star g-modes to become damped, with the mode spectrum becoming finite rather than infinite as in the standard non-dissipative analysis; the key condition is that sufficiently high-order g-modes become overdamped (purely imaginary frequency) when the reaction timescale is shorter than the mode period.

## Method

The authors formulate the linear perturbation problem for a non-rotating Newtonian neutron star with composition stratification, introducing a complex equation of state that accounts for deviations from beta equilibrium via the chemical potential imbalance β = μ_n − μ_p − μ_e. They derive a set of four coupled ordinary differential equations for the radial eigenfunctions (y₁–y₄) in dimensionless form, incorporating a parameterised reaction rate A = −1/t_R. The system is solved numerically for the BSk21 equation of state with standard Urca reactions, using boundary conditions of regularity at the centre and vanishing Lagrangian pressure perturbation at the surface.

## Key numbers

- The background neutron star model has central density ρ = 5.10 × 10¹⁵ g cm⁻³, radius R = 13.49 km, and mass M = 1.43 M⊙ (BSk21 equation of state).
- For a polytropic test case (Γ = 2, n = 1 polytrope with R = 10 km and M = 1.4 M⊙), the dimensionless g-mode frequencies scale as ω̃_n ∝ (Γ₁ − Γ)^(0.46–0.47) and the dimensionless overlap integrals scale as Q̃_n ∝ (Γ₁ − Γ)^(0.95–0.98) for the first six g-modes.
- The critical reaction timescale separating oscillatory from overdamped behaviour is t_R = 1/(2ω₀), where ω₀ is the mode frequency in the slow-reaction limit.

## Context

This work extends the proof-of-principle analysis of Gittins, Andersson and Jones (2019) by providing a full numerical treatment of the g-mode spectrum across the transition between fast and slow reaction regimes, rather than focusing on either limit separately. The results contradict the common assumption of an infinite undamped g-mode spectrum from standard stellar oscillation theory (Unno et al., 1989) and show that realistic neutron star models have a finite number of oscillatory g-modes. The findings are relevant for understanding the p-g instability in neutron star binaries (Weinberg, 2013) and the nonlinear saturation of modes driven unstable by gravitational-wave emission (Schenk et al., 2001).
