# Repulsion and absorption of the $Σ$-nucleus potential for $Σ^-$-$^5$He in the $^6$Li($π^-$, $K^+$) reaction

> We study phenomenologically inclusive spectra of the $^6$Li($π^-$, $K^+$) reaction at 1.2 GeV/$c$ within a distorted-wave impulse approximation with the optimal Fermi-averaging $π^- p \to K^+ Σ^-$ $t$ matrix. We attempt to clarify the property of a $Σ$-nucleus potential for $Σ^-$-$^5$He by comparing the calculated spectra with the data of the J-PARC E10 experiment. The result shows that the repulsive and absorptive components of the $Σ^-$-$^5$He potential provide the ability to explain the data of the continuum spectra in $Σ$ and $Λ$ regions; the strengths of $V_Σ\simeq$ +30 MeV and $W_Σ\simeq$ $-$26 MeV are favored within the Woods-Saxon potential, consistent with analyses for heavier nuclei. Effects of the size and potential range for $Σ^-$-$^5$He in the neutron excess of $(N-Z)/(N+Z)=$ 0.2 are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Σ-nucleus potential for Σ⁻-⁵He has a repulsive real part and an absorptive imaginary part, with favored strengths of V_Σ ≃ +30 MeV and W_Σ ≃ -26 MeV within a Woods-Saxon potential, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of potential geometry (r₀ = 0.835 fm, a = 0.706 fm) and the use of a renormalization factor f_s = 1.23 to match the absolute magnitude of the data.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations with optimal Fermi-averaging of the π⁻p → K⁺Σ⁻ t matrix to compute inclusive K⁺ spectra for the ⁶Li(π⁻, K⁺) reaction at 1.2 GeV/c. They used a Green's function method to describe Σ⁻⊗⁵He doorway states with a Woods-Saxon optical potential, and fitted the calculated average cross sections over θ_lab = 2°–14° to the J-PARC E10 experimental data by varying the real (V_Σ) and imaginary (W_Σ) potential strengths and a renormalization factor f_s.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit potential strengths: V_Σ = +30 ± 10 MeV and W_Σ = -26 ± 2 MeV in a Woods-Saxon potential with r₀ = 0.835 fm and a = 0.706 fm for Σ⁻-⁵He.
- Minimum χ² value: χ²_min = 84.5 for N = 66 data points, giving χ²/N = 1.28, at V_Σ = +30 MeV, W_Σ = -26 MeV, and f_s = 1.23.
- Total χ² for angular distributions: χ²_tot/N_tot = 459.6/396 = 1.16 over six angles (θ_lab = 3°, 5°, 7°, 9°, 11°, 13°) with f_s = 1.19.
- Matter rms radius of ⁵He(3/2⁻_g.s.) used in folding model: ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 2.43 fm.
- Potential rms radius from Woods-Saxon fit: ⟨r²⟩^{1/2}_U = 2.84 fm.
- Threshold energy difference between Σ⁻-⁵He and Λ-⁵H channels: ΔM = 56.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DWIA analyses of Harada and Hirabayashi (2005, 2006) for (π⁻, K⁺) reactions on heavier nuclei (²⁸Si and ²⁰⁹Bi) to the light system ⁶Li, using data from the J-PARC E10 experiment (Sugimura 2014, Honda 2016). The extracted repulsive Σ-nucleus potential is consistent with earlier analyses for heavier nuclei and with studies of Σ⁻ atomic x-ray data by Batty (1994) and Friedman (2007). The results enable improved modeling of Σ⁻ behavior in neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron star structure, and provide constraints for modern YN potentials and lattice QCD calculations.
