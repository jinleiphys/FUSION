# Quark mass effects in quark number susceptibilities

> The quark degrees of freedom of the QGP with special focus on mass effects are investigated. A next-to-leading-order perturbation theory approach with quark mass dependence is applied and compared to lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative calculation that includes quark mass dependence can reproduce the trend of lattice QCD results for quark number susceptibilities, but only for temperatures above roughly 300–350 MeV; at lower temperatures deviations appear, and a small maximum in the lattice data near 580 MeV is not captured by the perturbative setup.

## Method

The authors performed a NLO perturbative calculation of the thermodynamic potential for QCD, incorporating running quark masses and a running coupling constant, with the renormalization scale set to Λ = 2πT. They computed the pressure and quark number susceptibilities (second, fourth, and sixth order) for light (u, d), strange, and charm quarks, and compared these results to lattice QCD data from Petreczky (2009), Bazavov (2013), and Ding (2015). The perturbative calculation was restricted to order g², and the coupling constant was run using a two-loop expression with the scale fixed by requiring α_s ≈ 0.336 at Λ = 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Light quark masses were set to m_u = 2.3 MeV and m_d = 4.8 MeV for all numerical perturbative calculations.
- The strange quark mass was fixed to m_s = 93.6 MeV at Λ = 2 GeV, leading to a parameter value of m̂_s = 252 MeV.
- The Λ_MSbar parameter was determined as 343 MeV for 2+1 flavors and 285 MeV for 2+1+1 flavors.
- The charm quark pole mass was taken as m_c^pole ≈ 1.7 GeV, with the matching between 2+1 and 2+1+1 flavor running occurring at roughly T = 271 MeV.
- For temperatures T ≳ 300 MeV, the perturbative and lattice results for the pressure were in good agreement.
- The ratio χ_s²/χ_u² showed good agreement between perturbative and lattice results down to T = 350 MeV, with slight deviations at lower temperatures.
- The ratio χ_s⁴/χ_u⁴ showed increasing deviation for T < 400 MeV, and the lattice maximum near T = 580 MeV was not reproduced by perturbation theory.
- The sixth-order charm-quark susceptibility χ_c⁶ showed a maximum around T = 450 MeV in the perturbative calculation, consistent with lattice data at N_t = 8 within error bars.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of quark number susceptibilities by incorporating quark mass effects, which were absent in previous calculations that treated all quarks as massless and thus predicted ratios like χ_s²/χ_u² = 1. The results are compared to lattice QCD data from Petreczky (2009), Bazavov (2013), and Ding (2015), and to HTLpt results from Haque (2014). The finding that the NLO mass-dependent calculation reproduces the bump in the sixth-order charm susceptibility resolves a previous ambiguity about whether that feature arose from quark masses or higher-order effects. The authors suggest that including higher orders in the perturbative setup is the natural next step.
