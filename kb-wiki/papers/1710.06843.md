# Neutron-star radius constraints from GW170817 and future detections

> We introduce a new, powerful method to constrain properties of neutron stars (NSs). We show that the total mass of GW170817 provides a reliable constraint on the stellar radius if the merger did not result in a prompt collapse as suggested by the interpretation of associated electromagnetic emission. The radius R_1.6 of nonrotating NSs with a mass of 1.6 M_sun can be constrained to be larger than 10.68_{-0.04}^{+0.15} km, and the radius R_max of the nonrotating maximum mass configuration must be larger than 9.60_{-0.03}^{+0.14} km. We point out that detections of future events will further improve these constraints. Moreover, we show that a future event with a signature of a prompt collapse of the merger remnant will establish even stronger constraints on the NS radius from above and the maximum mass M_max of NSs from above. These constraints are particularly robust because they only require a measurement of the chirp mass and a distinction between prompt and delayed collapse of the merger remnant, which may be inferred from the electromagnetic signal or even from the presence/absence of a ringdown gravitational-wave (GW) signal. This prospect strengthens the case of our novel method of constraining NS properties, which is directly applicable to future GW events with accompanying electromagnetic counterpart observations. We emphasize that this procedure is a new way of constraining NS radii from GW detections independent of existing efforts to infer radius information from the late inspiral phase or postmerger oscillations, and it does not require particularly loud GW events.

# Neutron-star radius constraints from GW170817 and future detections

## Key claim

The total binary mass of GW170817 provides a reliable lower bound on neutron-star radii *if* the merger did not result in a prompt collapse, as suggested by the interpretation of the associated electromagnetic emission. The method does not require a high signal-to-noise ratio of the gravitational-wave measurement, unlike existing approaches that rely on finite-size effects during the late inspiral or postmerger oscillations.

## Method

The authors use the measured total binary mass of GW170817 (2.74 solar masses) as a lower limit on the threshold binary mass for prompt black-hole formation, under the assumption that the merger resulted in a delayed or no collapse. They apply empirical relations derived from hydrodynamical simulations that connect the threshold binary mass to the radius of a 1.6-solar-mass neutron star (R_1.6) and to the radius of the maximum-mass configuration (R_max), combined with causality constraints. The analysis propagates the measurement errors from the low-spin prior results of Abbott (2017) and considers both a conservative lower bound from the total mass alone and a "realistic" bound assuming the remnant survived at least 10 milliseconds.

## Key numbers

- Total binary mass of GW170817: 2.74_{-0.01}^{+0.04} solar masses (low-spin prior)
- Conservative lower limit on R_1.6 (radius of 1.6-solar-mass nonrotating neutron star): larger than 10.30_{-0.03}^{+0.15} km
- "Realistic" lower limit on R_1.6 (assuming remnant lifetime at least 10 ms): larger than 10.68_{-0.04}^{+0.15} km
- Conservative lower limit on R_max (radius of nonrotating maximum-mass configuration): larger than 9.26_{-0.03}^{+0.17} km
- "Realistic" lower limit on R_max: larger than 9.60_{-0.03}^{+0.14} km
- Threshold binary mass for prompt collapse follows the relation M_thres = (-3.606 G M_max / c^2 R_1.6 + 2.38) * M_max, accurate to better than 0.1 solar masses
- Causality requires M_thres >= 1.22 M_max for the R_1.6 relation and M_thres >= 1.23 M_max for the R_max relation

## Context

This work introduces a new method for constraining neutron-star radii that is independent of existing efforts to infer radius information from the late inspiral phase (Faber 2002, Flanagan 2008, Read 2013, Del Pozzo 2013, Abbott 2017) or from postmerger oscillations (Bauswein 2012, Bauswein 2014, Takami 2014, Clark 2014, Chatziioannou 2017). The method extends earlier work by Bauswein (2013) that established the relation between threshold binary mass and neutron-star properties. Future detections of events with prompt collapse signatures could establish upper bounds on neutron-star radii and the maximum mass, while additional events with delayed collapse would tighten the lower bounds. The approach is directly applicable to any new gravitational-wave event with an electromagnetic counterpart that allows classification of the collapse behavior.
