# Isoscalar dipole excitations in $^{16}$O

> Isoscalar (IS) monopole and dipole excitations in $^{16}$O were investigated by the method of shifted basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method. Significant strengths of the IS monopole and dipole transitions were obtained in the low-energy region below the giant resonances. In addition to the compressive mode, which mainly contributes to the high-energy strengths for the IS dipole giant resonance, we obtained a variety of low-energy dipole modes such as the vortical dipole mode in the $1^-_1$ state of the vibrating tetrahedral $4α$ and the $^{12}$C+$α$ cluster structure in the $1^-_2$ state. The $1^-_1$ state contributes to the significant low-energy strength of the IS dipole transition as 5\% of the energy-weighted sum rule, which describes well the experimental data observed by the $α$ inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The low-energy isoscalar dipole strength in $^{16}$O, observed experimentally as approximately 5% of the energy-weighted sum rule, originates primarily from the $1^-_1$ state, which exhibits both a vortical dipole mode from the vibrating tetrahedral $4\alpha$ structure and a compressive component from the $3\alpha$-$\alpha$ relative motion, rather than from the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster state ($1^-_2$).

## Method

The authors applied the shifted basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (sAMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to $^{16}$O, superposing 256 shifted AMD basis states (describing 1p-1h excitations) with VAP-optimized AMD wave functions and $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster wave functions at inter-cluster distances of 1.2 to 7.2 fm. They calculated isoscalar monopole (IS0) and dipole (IS1) transition strengths, form factors, transition densities, and compressive dipole (CD) and toroidal dipole (TD) strength functions for $0^+$ and $1^-$ states up to 60 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{16}$O ground state: 125.6 MeV (sAMD+GCM), compared to experimental 127.62 MeV
- $1^-_1$ state excitation energy: 9.6 MeV (sAMD+GCM), compared to experimental 7.12 MeV
- $1^-_2$ state excitation energy: 14.4 MeV (sAMD+GCM), compared to experimental 9.59 MeV
- $B(\textrm{IS1};0^+_1\to 1^-_1)$ = 169.8 (units not explicitly given in text; context implies fm$^6$)
- Energy-weighted sum rule ratio $P_\textrm{IS1}(1^-_1)$ = 0.051 (5.1%) from sAMD+GCM, compared to experimental value 0.42 (42%) from Harakeh (1981) $\alpha$ inelastic scattering analysis
- $B(\textrm{IS1};0^+_1\to 1^-_2)$ = 10.2, with $P_\textrm{IS1}(1^-_2)$ = 0.0045 (0.45%)
- $M(E0;0^+_1\to0^+_2)$ = 3.8 $e$ fm$^2$ (sAMD+GCM), compared to experimental 3.55(0.21) $e$ fm$^2$
- $M(E0;0^+_1\to0^+_3)$ = 3.9 $e$ fm$^2$, compared to experimental 4.03(0.09) $e$ fm$^2$
- Ground state rms matter radius: 2.72 fm (sAMD+GCM), compared to experimental 2.55 fm from Angeli (2013)
- $1^-_1$ state rms radius: 2.80 fm; $1^-_2$ state rms radius: 3.37 fm
- IS GDR peak around 40 MeV excitation energy

## Context

This work extends the sAMD+GCM approach previously applied to $^{12}$C by Kanada-En'yo (2015, 2017) to $^{16}$O, providing a unified microscopic description of both low-energy cluster states and the giant resonance region. It builds on earlier VAP+GCM calculations by Kanada-En'yo (2017) that described cluster states but not the IS GDR, and on semi-microscopic $4\alpha$-cluster model calculations by Yamada (2011) that described low-energy IS monopole strengths. The results confirm the vortical dipole mode predicted by hydrodynamical models (Semenko, 1981; Ravenhall, 1987) and microscopic approaches (Paar, 2007; Kvasil, 2011), and demonstrate that the $1^-_1$ state in $^{16}$O combines both vortical and compressive characters due to the tetrahedral $4\alpha$ structure, enabling future studies of the interplay between cluster and vortical modes in light nuclei.
