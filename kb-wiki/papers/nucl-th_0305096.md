# Quest for the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The paper argues that data from fixed-target experiments at AGS and SPS "strongly suggest a picture that a quark-gluon plasma is formed at SPS at least for a short while," but this conclusion remains conditional because alternative explanations (such as nuclear absorption models for J/ψ suppression) can account for some observations without invoking plasma formation.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical expectations for quark-gluon plasma formation in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, then compares these predictions against experimental data from AGS (Brookhaven) and SPS (CERN) fixed-target experiments, as well as preliminary results from RHIC. Specific probes examined include flavor composition (strange hadron enhancement), penetrating probes (dileptons and photons), quarkonium suppression (J/ψ), and jet quenching (high-pT hadron suppression).

## Key numbers
- RHIC collision energy: up to 200A GeV with gold nuclei (A = 197)
- Nucleon rest mass energy: m_N c^2 = 0.94 GeV
- Estimated transition temperature from lattice QCD: T ≃ 150 MeV
- J/ψ resonance mass: M = 3.1 GeV
- Maximum nuclear size constrained by Coulomb repulsion: Z_Max ≃ 114
- Lorentz contraction factor at RHIC: γ_cm ≃ 100, giving longitudinal nuclear thickness 2R/γ_cm ≃ 0.015 fm
- Expansion time scale: τ_exp = R/c_s ≃ (1-2)R fm/c
- Plasma lifetime: 10^{-21} to 10^{-20} seconds
- High momentum tail suppression at RHIC observed at E > 5 GeV
- Temperature of universe at ten microseconds after Big Bang: 10^{12} K (equivalent to ~100 MeV)

## Context
This invited talk (Matsui, 2003) reviews the status of quark-gluon plasma searches following the RHIC startup, extending the theoretical framework established by Bjorken (1983) for boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion and by Matsui and Satz (1986) for J/ψ suppression as a deconfinement signal. The paper contrasts earlier expectations from perturbative QCD calculations (Rafelski and Müller, 1982) with experimental results from NA38, NA50, CERES, and WA97 collaborations at CERN, and notes that preliminary RHIC data from the PHENIX experiment exclude thermal recombination models for J/ψ production at collider energies. The work enables future searches at LHC energies (8000Z/A GeV) and identifies bottomonium (Υ) as a potential probe for very high temperature plasma.
