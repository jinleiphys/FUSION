# General equilibrium second-order hydrodynamic coefficients for free quantum fields

> We present a systematic calculation of the corrections of the stress-energy tensor and currents of the free boson and Dirac fields up to second order in thermal vorticity, which is relevant for relativistic hydrodynamics. These corrections are non-dissipative because they survive at general thermodynamic equilibrium with non vanishing mean values of the conserved generators of the Lorentz group, i.e. angular momenta and boosts. Their equilibrium nature makes it possible to express the relevant coefficients by means of correlators of the angular-momentum and boost operators with stress-energy tensor and current, thus making simpler to determine their so-called "Kubo formulae". We show that, at least for free fields, the corrections are of quantum origin and we study several limiting cases and compare our results with previous calculations. We find that the axial current of the free Dirac field receives corrections proportional to the vorticity independently of the anomalous term.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic calculation of second-order corrections to the stress-energy tensor and currents for free boson and Dirac fields in thermal vorticity, showing that these non-dissipative corrections are of quantum origin (vanishing in the \(\hbar \to 0\) limit) and can be expressed via correlators of angular-momentum and boost operators with the stress-energy tensor, simplifying the determination of their Kubo formulae.

## Method

The authors expand the global thermodynamic equilibrium density operator in powers of thermal vorticity \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\), using an operator formalism where the small term is \(\frac{1}{2}\varpi:\hat{J}_x\) and the leading order is the homogeneous grand-canonical ensemble. They decompose thermal vorticity into acceleration \(\alpha^\mu\) and vorticity \(w^\mu\) vectors, then express the second-order corrections to the mean stress-energy tensor and currents in terms of Euclidean three-point functions of the stress-energy tensor with imaginary-time evolved boost and angular momentum operators. The coefficients are computed explicitly for free charged scalar and Dirac fields using the imaginary time formalism.

## Key numbers

- For the free charged scalar field at zero chemical potential, the coefficient \(U_w\) (multiplying \(-w^2 u^\mu u^\nu\) in the stress-energy tensor) is given by \(U_w = \frac{\pi^2 T^4}{90}\) in the high-temperature limit.
- For the free Dirac field at zero chemical potential, the coefficient \(W\) (multiplying \(w^\mu w^\nu\)) is \(W = \frac{7\pi^2 T^4}{360}\) in the high-temperature limit.
- The axial current of the free Dirac field receives a correction proportional to vorticity: \(\langle j^\mu_A \rangle = \frac{T^2}{6} \omega^\mu\) at zero chemical potential, independent of the anomalous term.
- The dimensionless parameters \(\alpha\), \(w\), and \(\gamma\) scale as \(\hbar |\boldsymbol{a}|/(c k_B T)\), \(\hbar |\boldsymbol{\omega}|/(k_B T)\), and \(\hbar^2 |\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{\omega}|/(c k_B^2 T^2)\) respectively, demonstrating their quantum nature.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of second-order non-dissipative hydrodynamic coefficients from the real scalar field (Becattini 2015) to charged scalar and Dirac fields, confirming that these coefficients are of quantum origin as argued by Romatschke (2009) and missing in classical kinetic theory expansions (Jaiswal). The results enable systematic computation of equilibrium second-order coefficients for free fields and provide a foundation for comparing with Kubo formulae from three-point stress-energy tensor correlators (Moore 2010) and with relations from the functional approach (Kovtun, Banerjee 2012).
