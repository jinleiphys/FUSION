# A pseudo-conformal structure in dense baryonic matter

> We clarify and extend further the idea developed in \cite{PKLMR,MLPR} that baryonic matter at high density has an emergent "pseudo-conformal symmetry." It is argued that as baryonic density exceeds $n \simeq n_{1/2} \gtrsim 2n_0$, a topology change mimicking the baryon-quark continuity takes place at $n_{1/2}$. In terms of skyrmions, this corresponds to the transition from skyrmions to half-skyrmions and impacts on the equation of state of dense baryonic matter. The emergence in medium at $n_{1/2}$ of parity-doublet symmetry -- which is invisible in QCD in matter-free vacuum -- plays the crucial role. The consequence of the topology change is that massive compact stars carry the "pseudo-conformal sound velocity" $v_s^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ at $n\gsim n_{1/2}$ signaling a precursor to precocious emergence of scale symmetry as well as {a} local symmetry hidden in QCD in the matter-free vacuum. A highly significant prediction of this work is that the topology change density from normal matter to half-skyrmion matter, up-to-date inaccessible either by QCD proper or by terrestrial experiments, could possibly be pinned down within the range $2 < n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4$, commensurate with the range expected for a continuous hadrons-to-quarks/gluons transition.

## Key claim
The paper argues that dense baryonic matter above a topology-change density \(n_{1/2} \gtrsim 2n_0\) exhibits an emergent "pseudo-conformal symmetry," where the sound velocity approaches \(v_s^2/c^2 \approx 1/3\) even though the trace of the energy-momentum tensor is not zero. The key hedge is that this symmetry is not fundamental but emerges from a topology change (skyrmion to half-skyrmion transition) that mimics hadron-quark continuity, and the transition density itself is predicted to lie in the range \(2 < n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4\), a range currently inaccessible by QCD or terrestrial experiments.

## Method
The authors construct an effective field theory called \(bs\)HLS (baryons as skyrmions, scalar dilaton, hidden local symmetry) that incorporates scale symmetry, hidden local flavor symmetry for vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)), and a dilaton field \(\chi\). The parameters of the Lagrangian are matched to QCD via operator product expansion at the chiral scale, endowing them with intrinsic density dependence (IDD) through QCD condensates. The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at density \(n_{1/2}\) is implemented via a change in the behavior of the space-averaged quark condensate \(\overline{\Sigma}\) (which vanishes at \(n_{1/2}\)) while the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) remains nonzero, and the resulting equation of state is used to compute compact-star properties.

## Key numbers
- The topology change density is predicted to lie in the range \(2 < n_{1/2}/n_0 < 4\), where \(n_0 \sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear equilibrium density.
- The pseudo-conformal sound velocity in massive compact stars is \(v_s^2/c^2 \approx 1/3\) for densities \(n \gtrsim n_{1/2}\).
- The in-medium pion decay constant \(f_\pi^\ast\) in the half-skyrmion phase (region II) remains constant at approximately \(60-80\%\) of its free-space value.
- The vector meson mass formula is \(m_V^2 = a_V f_\pi^2 g_V^2\), with \(a_\rho = 2\) giving the KSRF formula for the \(\rho\) meson.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous framework (Ma, Rho, 2018; PKLMR, MLPR) that uses topology change and hidden symmetries to describe dense baryonic matter, contrasting with standard approaches that explicitly introduce quark degrees of freedom via phase transitions (e.g., Baym et al., 2018; Kojo et al., 2015; McLerran and Reddy, 2019). The predicted pseudo-conformal sound velocity and the range for \(n_{1/2}\) are testable against gravitational-wave observations (GW170817) and heavy-ion collision data, and the framework enables a unified hadronic description of compact stars up to densities \(\sim 5-7 n_0\) without invoking explicit quark matter.
