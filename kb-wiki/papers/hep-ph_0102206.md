# Chiral multiplets versus parity doublets in highly excited baryons

> It has recently been suggested that the parity doublet structure seen in the spectrum of highly excited baryons may be due to effective chiral restoration for these states. We argue how the idea of chiral symmetry restoration high in the spectrum is consistent with the concept of quark-hadron duality. If chiral symmetry is effectively restored for highly-lying states, then the baryons should fall into representations of $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ that are compatible with the given parity of the states - the parity-chiral multiplets. We classify all possible parity-chiral multiplets: (i) $(1/2,0)\oplus(0, 1/2)$ that contain parity doublet for nucleon spectrum;(ii) $(3/2,0) \oplus (0, 3/2)$ consists of the parity doublet for delta spectrum; (iii) $(1/2,1) \oplus (1, 1/2)$ contains one parity doublet in the nucleon spectrum and one parity doublet in the delta spectrum of the same spin that are degenerate in mass. Here we show that the available spectroscopic data for nonstrange baryons in the $\sim$ 2 GeV range is consistent with all possibilities, but the approximate degeneracy of parity doublets in nucleon and delta spectra support the latter possibility with excited baryons approximately falling into $(1/2,1) \oplus (1, 1/2)$ representation of $SU(2)_L\timesSU(2)_R$ with approximate degeneracy between positive and negative parity $N$ and $Δ$ resonances of the same spin.

# Chiral multiplets versus parity doublets in highly excited baryons

## Key claim
The parity doublet structure observed in highly excited baryons around 2 GeV can be explained by effective chiral symmetry restoration, but the data are consistent with three possible chiral multiplet assignments; the approximate degeneracy between nucleon and delta parity doublets of the same spin favors the specific representation (1/2,1) ⊕ (1,1/2) over the alternatives (1/2,0) ⊕ (0,1/2) or (3/2,0) ⊕ (0,3/2), though accidental degeneracy cannot be ruled out.

## Method
The authors classify all possible parity-chiral multiplets of SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R compatible with baryon quantum numbers and parity, then compare the predicted multiplet structures against the Particle Data Group listings of nonstrange baryon resonances in the ~2 GeV region. They also argue from the operator product expansion of the Ioffe current correlator that at asymptotically high momentum the chirally-odd part of the correlator is suppressed relative to the chirally-even part, implying chiral symmetry must become manifest in the high-lying spectrum via quark-hadron duality.

## Key numbers
- The typical mass splitting within parity doublets is ~200 MeV or less, which is within the decay widths of those states.
- Extracted resonance masses have uncertainties typically of order 100 MeV.
- The "asymptotic regime" for e+e- → hadrons sets in at s ~ 2-3 GeV^2.
- Specific resonance masses listed include: N(1900) J=3/2+, N(2080) J=3/2-, Δ(1920) J=3/2+, Δ(1940) J=3/2-; N(2000) J=5/2+, N(2200) J=5/2-, Δ(1905) J=5/2+, Δ(1930) J=5/2-; N(1990) J=7/2+, N(2190) J=7/2-, Δ(1950) J=7/2+, Δ(2200) J=7/2-; N(2220) J=9/2+, N(2250) J=9/2-, Δ(2300) J=9/2+, Δ(2400) J=9/2-; N(2600) J=11/2-, Δ(2420) J=11/2+; N(2700) J=13/2+, Δ(2750) J=13/2-; Δ(2950) J=15/2+.

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by Glozman (2000) that parity doublets in highly excited baryons arise from effective chiral restoration, and provides a rigorous group-theoretic classification of the possible chiral multiplets. The authors contrast their approach with the scheme of Jido, Hatsuda and Kunihiro (2000), who organized low-lying baryon fields into (1/2,1) ⊕ (1,1/2) representations; the present work argues that low-lying states do not form chiral multiplets due to strong spontaneous symmetry breaking, while high-lying states can. The classification enables future tests: discovery of states beyond the parity-doublet systematics would be strong evidence against the conjecture, while filling the vacancies marked in the PDG listings (e.g., missing J=11/2+ nucleon or J=13/2- nucleon states) would support it.
