# The role of the boundary conditions in the Wigner-Seitz approximation applied to the neutron star inner crust

> The influence of the boundary conditions used in the Wigner-Seitz approximation applied to the neutron star inner crust is examined. The generalized energy functional method which includes neutron and proton pairing correlations is used. Predictions of two versions of the boundary conditions are compared with each other. The uncertainties in the equilibrium configuration (Z,R_c) of the crust, where Z is the proton charge and R_c the radius of the Wigner-Seitz cell, correspond to variation of Z by 2 -- 6 units and of R_c, by 1 -- 2 fm. The effect of the boundary conditions is enhanced at increasing density. These uncertainties are smaller than the variation of Z and R_c coming from the inclusion of pairing. The value of the pairing gap itself, especially at high density, can depend on the boundary condition used.

# The role of the boundary conditions in the Wigner-Seitz approximation applied to the neutron star inner crust

## Key claim

The choice of boundary condition in the Wigner-Seitz approximation introduces uncertainties in the equilibrium configuration (proton number Z and cell radius R_c) of the neutron star inner crust that are smaller than the changes induced by including pairing correlations, but at high densities the neutron pairing gap itself can depend significantly on which boundary condition is used.

## Method

The authors applied the generalized energy functional method, which includes both neutron and proton pairing correlations self-consistently, to spherical Wigner-Seitz cells in the neutron star inner crust. They compared two versions of boundary conditions (BC1, where the radial wavefunction vanishes at the cell boundary for odd orbital angular momentum and its derivative vanishes for even l; BC2, where the opposite assignment is used) for average baryon densities corresponding to Fermi wave numbers k_F from 0.2 to 1.2 fm^{-1}. The energy functional combined a phenomenological nuclear functional by Fayans et al. for the cluster region with a microscopic functional for neutron matter based on the Argonne v18 potential within Brueckner theory and BCS approximation.

## Key numbers

- For k_F = 0.8 fm^{-1}, the absolute minimum in binding energy occurs at Z = 52 for BC1 and Z = 54 for BC2, a shift of 2 units.
- The uncertainty in equilibrium Z across all densities (k_F >= 0.6 fm^{-1}) ranges from 2 to 6 units, growing with increasing density.
- The uncertainty in the Wigner-Seitz cell radius R_c is typically about 1 fm, reaching about 2 fm at k_F = 1.1 fm^{-1}.
- At k_F = 0.2 fm^{-1} (near the neutron drip point), the two boundary conditions give practically identical results, with Z = 52 and R_c = 57.18 fm (BC1) versus 57.10 fm (BC2).
- At k_F = 1.1 fm^{-1} with Z = 20, the neutron pairing gap at the Fermi surface Delta_F is 1.32 MeV for BC1 and 2.32 MeV for BC2.
- At k_F = 1.2 fm^{-1} with Z = 20, Delta_F is 1.21 MeV for BC1 and 1.56 MeV for BC2.
- The difference between neutron and proton chemical potentials (mu_n - mu_p) is approximately 50 to 70 MeV for average values of k_F.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-Seitz approach originally developed by Negele and Vautherin (1973) for the neutron star inner crust, and builds on the authors' own previous studies (Baldo, Saperstein, Tolokonnikov, 2004-2005) that incorporated pairing correlations into the generalized energy functional method. The paper quantifies an inherent uncertainty in the Wigner-Seitz approximation that had not been systematically examined before. The results enable more reliable predictions for crust properties relevant to pulsar glitch models, but also indicate that at high densities (k_F >= 1 fm^{-1}) a more consistent band theory approach, such as that developed by Chamel, Carter, and Haensel (2003) for deep layers, may be necessary to resolve the boundary condition dependence of the pairing gap.
