# J/ΨProduction in pp Collisions at \sqrt s = 200 GeV at RHIC

> We study J/ψproduction in pp collisions at RHIC within the PHENIX detector acceptance range using the color singlet and color octet mechanism which are based on pQCD and NRQCD. Here we show that the color octet mechanism reproduces the RHIC data for J/ψproduction in pp collisions with respect to the p_T distribution, the rapidity distribution and the total cross section at \sqrt s = 200 GeV. The color singlet mechanism leads to a relatively small contribution to the total cross section when compared to the octet contribution.

# J/Ψ Production in pp Collisions at √s = 200 GeV at RHIC

## Key claim

The color octet mechanism within NRQCD, using only parton fusion processes (without fragmentation), reproduces the PHENIX data for J/ψ production in pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV across pT distributions, rapidity distributions, and total cross section, while the color singlet mechanism contributes negligibly. This agreement holds for pT ≤ 5 GeV without requiring fragmentation contributions, though the authors note that pQCD calculations are not valid below pT = 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed J/ψ production cross sections in pp collisions using both the color singlet and color octet mechanisms based on pQCD and NRQCD, incorporating parton fusion processes at leading order (αs² for total cross section and rapidity distributions, αs³ for pT distributions). They applied PHENIX detector acceptance cuts, used GRV98 and MRST99 parton distribution functions with factorization scales set to Q = 2m for total cross section and Q = √(pT² + M²) for pT distributions, and employed non-perturbative matrix elements previously extracted from Tevatron data by Cho and Leibovich (1996).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for J/ψ production in pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV: B σ_{J/ψ} = 159 ± 6 nb (color octet mechanism with MRST99 PDFs, charm quark mass = 1.45 GeV), in agreement with the PHENIX experimental value of 159 nb
- Color singlet contribution is more than an order of magnitude smaller than the color octet contribution for the pT distribution
- Charm quark mass used in calculations: m = 1.45 GeV
- Radial wave function at origin for color singlet: |R(0)|² = 0.81 GeV³ (Buchmuller-Tye wave function)
- Derivative of radial wave function: (9/2π)(|R′(0)|²/Mc⁴) = 15 MeV
- PHENIX rapidity coverage: −3 < y < 3
- pT range of validity: pT ≥ 2 GeV (pQCD not valid below this); PHENIX expected sensitivity up to pT = 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends the color octet mechanism, previously successful at Tevatron energies for J/ψ production (Braaten, 1995; Cho and Leibovich, 1996), to RHIC energies by comparing with the first PHENIX pp data at √s = 200 GeV. The authors confirm that the same non-perturbative matrix elements extracted from Tevatron data describe RHIC data without renormalization, establishing the pp baseline needed for interpreting J/ψ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and for extracting polarized gluon distribution functions from polarized pp collisions.
