# Extracting nuclear matter properties from the neutron star matter equation of state using deep neural networks

> The extraction of the nuclear matter properties from neutron star (NS) observations is nowadays an important issue, in particular, the properties that characterize the symmetry energy which are essential to describe correctly asymmetric nuclear matter. We use deep neural networks (DNNs) to map the relation between cold $β$-equilibrium NS matter and the nuclear matter properties. Assuming a quadratic dependence on the isospin asymmetry for the energy per particle of homogeneous nuclear matter and using a Taylor expansion up to fourth order in the iso-scalar and iso-vector contributions, we generate a dataset of different realizations of $β$-equilibrium NS matter and the corresponding nuclear matter properties. The DNN model was successfully trained, attaining great accuracy in the test set. Finally, a real case scenario was used to test the DNN model, where a set of 33 nuclear models, obtained within a relativistic mean field approach or a Skyrme force description, were fed into the DNN model and the corresponding nuclear matter parameters recovered with considerable accuracy, in particular, the standard deviations $σ(L_{\text{sym}})= 12.85$ MeV and $σ(K_{\text{sat}})= 41.02$ MeV were obtained, respectively, for the slope of the symmetry energy and the nuclear matter incompressibility at saturation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a deep neural network (DNN) can map the cold beta-equilibrium neutron star matter equation of state to the underlying nuclear matter properties, but only under the assumptions of a quadratic dependence on isospin asymmetry and a Taylor expansion up to fourth order around saturation density; the model recovers the lowest-order parameters with considerable accuracy even when applied to realistic nuclear models, though higher-order terms are treated as effective.

## Method

The authors generated a dataset of 89,567,940 beta-equilibrium equations of state by assuming a quadratic dependence on isospin asymmetry for the energy per particle of homogeneous nuclear matter and Taylor expanding both the symmetric nuclear matter energy and the symmetry energy up to fourth order around saturation density. A densely connected feed-forward DNN with 5 hidden layers (sizes 80, 80, 80, 40, 15) and sigmoid activation functions was trained to map the 41-element energy density vector (from 0.08 to 0.30 fm^-3) to 8 nuclear matter parameters (E_sat, K_sat, J_sat, E_sym, L_sym, K_sym, J_sym, n_0). The model was tested on 8,956,794 held-out EoS and then applied to 33 unified EoS from relativistic mean-field and Skyrme models from Fortin (2016).

## Key numbers

* Test set standard deviation for saturation density n_0: sigma = 0.0010 fm^-3
* Test set standard deviation for symmetry energy slope L_sym: sigma = 0.9798 MeV
* Test set standard deviation for incompressibility K_sat: sigma = 10.9070 MeV
* Test set standard deviation for symmetry energy E_sym: sigma = 0.2036 MeV
* Real-case application (33 nuclear models) standard deviation for L_sym: sigma = 12.85 MeV
* Real-case application standard deviation for K_sat: sigma = 41.02 MeV
* Real-case application standard deviation for symmetry energy E_sym: sigma = 2.1487 MeV
* Real-case application standard deviation for saturation density n_0: sigma = 0.0067 fm^-3
* Dataset size: 89,567,940 beta-equilibrium EoS generated from a grid with 6 points for 9 parameters and 16 points for n_0

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to extract nuclear matter parameters from the neutron star equation of state, which had faced difficulties: Imam (2021) using Bayesian methods could not recover nuclear matter parameters, and Mondal (2021) showed it was impossible to determine neutron star composition from the beta-equilibrium EoS due to multiple solutions. The DNN approach enables instantaneous inference of nuclear matter properties once a candidate EoS is available, and the authors plan to apply it to agnostic Gaussian-process representations of the EoS from Landry (2018) and Essick (2019) to extract nuclear matter properties from those descriptions.
