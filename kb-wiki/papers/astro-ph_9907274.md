# Shell-model half-lives for the N=82 nuclei and their implications for the r-process

> We have performed large-scale shell-model calculations of the half-lives and neutron-branching probabilties of the r-process waiting point nuclei at the magic neutron number N=82. We find good agreement with the measured half-lives of 129Ag and 130Cd. Our shell-model half-lives are noticeably shorter than those currently adopted in r-process simulations. Our calculation suggests that 130Cd is not produced in beta-flow equilibrium with the other N=82 isotones on the r-process path.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations produce half-lives for the N=82 r-process waiting-point nuclei that are noticeably shorter than those from the FRDM and ETFSI models currently used in r-process simulations, and the results suggest that 130Cd is not produced in beta-flow equilibrium with the other N=82 isotones on the r-process path.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the code ANTOINE with a model space of the 0g7/2, 1d3/2,5/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2 orbitals outside the N=50 core for neutrons and the 1p1/2, 0g9/2,7/2, 1d3/2,5/2, 2s1/2 orbitals for protons, allowing up to 2 (3) proton excitations in the parent (daughter) nucleus. The residual interaction combined a renormalized G-matrix component with a monopole part fine-tuned to reproduce known spectra around the N=82 shell closure, and the Gamow-Teller strength was scaled by the universal quenching factor (0.74)^2. Q-beta values were taken from experiment (131In) or the Duflo-Zuker mass compilation.

## Key numbers

- 129Ag experimental half-life: 46 +/- 9 ms; shell-model prediction: 35.1 ms
- 130Cd experimental half-life: 195 +/- 35 ms; shell-model prediction: 146 ms
- 131In ground state experimental half-life: 280 +/- 30 ms; shell-model prediction: 177 ms
- 131In isomeric state experimental half-life: 350 +/- 50 ms; shell-model prediction: 274 ms
- ETFSI half-lives are longer than shell-model results by average factors of 4-5
- FRDM half-lives for even N=82 isotones overestimate shell-model results by an order of magnitude
- Isomeric state excitation energy decreases from 500 keV for 123Nb to 375 keV for 131In (experimental value 360 keV)
- Beta-flow equilibrium fails for 130Cd and 131In by more than a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends previous half-life predictions for N=82 r-process waiting-point nuclei beyond the semi-empirical global models, quasiparticle random phase approximation, and the FRDM model of Moller (1997) and ETFSI approach of Borzov, Goriely and Pearson (1997). The results support the recent HFB calculations of Engel et al. (1999) which also found shorter half-lives than previously assumed. The finding that 130Cd is not produced in beta-flow equilibrium is consistent with the expectation from Thielemann (1993), Wasserburg (1996), and Qian (1998) that the N=82 and N=126 r-process abundance peaks are made under different conditions, and helps resolve potential conflicts between the required r-process duration and the expansion timescale of the neutrino-driven wind scenario from type II supernovae.
