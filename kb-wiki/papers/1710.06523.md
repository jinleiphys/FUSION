# Nucleon axial coupling from Lattice QCD

> We present state-of-the-art results from a lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon axial coupling, $g_A$, using Möbius Domain-Wall fermions solved on the dynamical $N_f = 2 + 1 + 1$ HISQ ensembles after they are smeared using the gradient-flow algorithm. Relevant three-point correlation functions are calculated using a method inspired by the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, and demonstrate significant improvement in signal for fixed stochastic samples. The calculation is performed at five pion masses of $m_π\sim \{400, 350, 310, 220, 130\}$~MeV, three lattice spacings of $a\sim\{0.15, 0.12, 0.09\}$~fm, and we do a dedicated volume study with $m_πL\sim\{3.22, 4.29, 5.36\}$. Control over all relevant sources of systematic uncertainty are demonstrated and quantified. We achieve a preliminary value of $g_A = 1.285(17)$, with a relative uncertainty of 1.33\%.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon axial coupling with a preliminary value of \(g_A = 1.285(17)\) at 1.33% relative uncertainty, which is commensurate with the experimental value \(g_A^{\text{PDG}} = 1.2723(23)\) but does not yet reach the sub-percent precision of experiment; the result is presented as preliminary and the paper emphasizes demonstrated control over all relevant systematic uncertainties rather than claiming a final definitive value.

## Method

The calculation uses Möbius Domain-Wall valence fermions on dynamical \(N_f = 2+1+1\) HISQ gauge ensembles from the MILC collaboration, after applying gradient-flow smearing to the gauge fields. Three-point correlation functions for the axial and vector currents are computed using a method inspired by the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, which algebraically resolves the derivative of the two-point correlator and gives access to the full source-sink time dependence at the cost of a single fixed-sink time. Bare couplings are extracted from simultaneous correlated two-state fits to the two-point correlator and the effective mass derivatives for both currents with smeared and point sinks, with fit stability verified by varying fit regions and requiring p-values above 0.05.

## Key numbers

*   Preliminary value of the nucleon axial coupling: \(g_A = 1.285(17)\), with a relative uncertainty of 1.33%.
*   Experimental comparison value: \(g_A^{\text{PDG}} = 1.2723(23)\).
*   Five pion masses used: \(m_\pi \sim \{400, 350, 310, 220, 130\}\) MeV.
*   Three lattice spacings used: \(a \sim \{0.15, 0.12, 0.09\}\) fm.
*   Volume study performed at \(a \sim 0.12\) fm, \(m_\pi \sim 220\) MeV with three spatial volumes: \(m_\pi L \sim \{3.22, 4.29, 5.36\}\).
*   Residual chiral symmetry breaking kept to less than 10% of the input light quark mass.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of \(g_A\) (e.g., Edwards (2005), Capitani (2012), Horsley (2013), Bali (2014), Abdel-Rehim (2015), Bhattacharya (2016)) by addressing two main challenges: the exponential signal-to-noise problem for nucleons and the dense excited-state spectrum. The Feynman-Hellmann inspired method improves signal for fixed stochastic samples compared to the fixed-sink method, and the use of gradient-flow smearing on HISQ ensembles with multiple lattice spacings, pion masses, and volumes enables a controlled chiral-continuum extrapolation. This work enables future precision calculations of multi-body corrections to \(g_A\) relevant for neutrinoless double-beta decay and the nucleon axial form factor for neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and T2HK.
