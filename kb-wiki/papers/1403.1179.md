# Higher order quark-mesonic scattering processes and the phase structure of QCD

> We study the impact of higher order quark-meson scattering processes on the chiral phase structure of two-flavour QCD at finite temperature and quark density. Thermal, density and quantum fluctuations are included within a functional renormalisation group approach to the quark-meson model. We present results on the chiral phase boundary, the critical endpoint, and the curvature of the phase transition line at vanishing density.

## Key claim

The inclusion of higher-order quark–anti-quark multi-meson interactions (a meson-field-dependent Yukawa coupling) leads to quantitatively significant changes in the chiral phase structure of two-flavor QCD, but the expansion in the order of mesonic fields converges rapidly, meaning that the systematic inclusion of these higher-order couplings is a controlled improvement over the standard quark-meson model.

## Method

The authors employed a functional renormalisation group (FRG) approach to the two-flavor quark-meson model, including thermal, density, and quantum fluctuations. They expanded the effective mesonic potential in powers of the O(4)-invariant field \(\rho\) and the Yukawa coupling in powers of \((\rho-\kappa)\) about a scale-independent expansion point \(\kappa\), thereby systematically incorporating higher-order meson-meson and quark–multi-meson scattering processes. The flow equations for the effective potential and the renormalised couplings were derived and solved, with wave function renormalisations treated as locally constant.

## Key numbers

* The ultraviolet cutoff scale was set to \(\Lambda = 700\ \text{MeV}\), below which the low-energy effective action of the quark-meson model was used.
* The initial scale for the RG flow was \(\Lambda = 700\ \text{MeV}\), chosen because composite degrees of freedom have already formed at this scale.
* The number of degenerate quark flavours was \(N_f = 2\) and the number of colors was \(N_c = 3\).
* The explicit symmetry breaking term \(-c\sigma\) is related to the current quark mass via \(m_q^c = \frac{h_\Lambda}{\lambda_{1,\Lambda}} c\), where \(\lambda_{1,\Lambda}\) is the squared meson mass in the UV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral phase transition within the quark-meson model that used a full mesonic effective potential but neglected field-dependent Yukawa couplings (Berges 2000, Schaefer 2006, Braun 2011). It also builds on the systematic embedding of low-energy effective models in first-principle QCD via functional methods (Braun 2009, Pawlowski 2010, Herbst 2013). The results enable quantitative precision in the matter sector of QCD and can be combined with Polyakov-loop extensions and first-principle QCD flows to achieve a fully quantitative description of the QCD phase diagram.
