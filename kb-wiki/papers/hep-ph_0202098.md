# Color-Flavor (Un)locking

> The structure of the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter at moderate densities is calculated within a 3-flavor NJL-type quark model with realistic quark masses. We focus on the influence of the selfconsistently determined effective strange quark mass on the color-flavor unlocking phase transition.

# Color-Flavor (Un)locking

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the color-flavor unlocking transition between the two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase and the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase is primarily driven by a discontinuous change in the selfconsistently determined strange quark mass, and that including a flavor-mixing 't Hooft interaction lowers the critical chemical potential for the transition by about 50 MeV compared to the case without mixing.

## Method
The authors employ a 3-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type quark model with realistic quark masses, including both quark-antiquark (scalar and pseudoscalar) four-point interactions and a 't Hooft six-point interaction that breaks U_A(1) symmetry, plus a quark-quark interaction in the scalar color-antisymmetric channel. They compute the mean-field thermodynamic potential at temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, then minimize it selfconsistently to determine the chiral condensates φ_u, φ_s and the diquark condensates Δ_2 and Δ_5 (where Δ_2 corresponds to pairing of up and down quarks only, and Δ_5 to pairing involving strange quarks). The coupling constants are fixed by fitting vacuum meson properties from Ref. Rehberg, with the diquark coupling H set to 3/4 G based on single-gluon exchange arguments, and all loops regularized by a sharp three-momentum cutoff Λ.

## Key numbers
- At the first-order transition from hadronic phase to 2SC phase (μ = μ_1), the light quark mass drops from its vacuum value to about 50 MeV, and the baryon number density jumps from zero to about 2.5 times nuclear matter density.
- For the case with flavor mixing (KΛ^5 = 20), a nonzero density of strange quarks appears above μ ≈ 415 MeV within the 2SC phase.
- The critical chemical potential μ_2 for the 2SC-to-CFL transition is about 50 MeV lower for KΛ^5 = 20 than for K = 0.
- The critical temperature for the 2SC-to-QGP transition follows the BCS relation T_c = 0.57 Δ_2(T = 0).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Buballa and Oertel (2001) by including a flavor-mixing 't Hooft interaction that was absent in that reference. The results show that the 2SC phase persists up to rather high densities before the CFL phase becomes favored, contradicting the expectation that CFL should appear immediately at moderate densities. The paper enables future studies of β-equilibrated quark matter relevant for neutron star cores, and calls for more systematic examination of the sensitivity to the choice of effective interaction.
