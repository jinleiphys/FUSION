# Comprehensive Effective Field Theory Analysis for Baryon Number Violating Processes

> Baryon number-violating processes arise generically in many extensions of the Standard Model, with Grand Unified Theories providing the most compelling realizations. Ongoing experimental searches at JUNO, Hyper-K, and DUNE motivate a more precise and model-independent analysis utilizing the effective field theory (EFT) framework capable of connecting ultraviolet dynamics to low-energy hadronic observables. In this work, we perform a pipeline analysis that connects baryon number-violating (BNV) new physics to its low-energy description through the Standard Model EFT (SMEFT) up to dimension nine and the Low-Energy EFT (LEFT) up to dimension eight, and subsequently matches onto chiral perturbation theory using a systematic spurion method. We show that the complete set of dimension-eight LEFT operators enables the inclusion of the full set of chiral representations for three-quark operators with derivatives, which are also embedded in the chiral Lagrangian after quark-hadron matching. By contrast, the conventional dimension-six operators generate only the $(\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{3})$ and $(\mathbf{8},\mathbf{1})$ representations. The higher dimensional LEFT operators should be matched from higher order SMEFT operators, and thus a wider class of UV completions are allowed. We consider the complete tree-level UV resonances for dimension 6 and 7 SMEFT operators, and representative UVs for higher dimension operators.

# Comprehensive Effective Field Theory Analysis for Baryon Number Violating Processes

## Key claim

The paper claims that including dimension-eight operators in the Low-Energy EFT (LEFT) enables access to the complete set of chiral representations for three-quark operators with derivatives, specifically the $(\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{3})$, $(\mathbf{8},\mathbf{1})$, $(\bar{\mathbf{6}},\mathbf{3})$, and $(\mathbf{10},\mathbf{1})$ representations, whereas conventional dimension-six operators generate only the $(\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{3})$ and $(\mathbf{8},\mathbf{1})$ representations. This extension allows a wider class of ultraviolet completions to be connected to low-energy baryon number violating observables.

## Method

The authors perform a pipeline analysis connecting baryon number violating (BNV) new physics to low-energy hadronic observables through the Standard Model EFT (SMEFT) up to dimension nine and the Low-Energy EFT (LEFT) up to dimension eight, subsequently matching onto chiral perturbation theory using a systematic spurion method. They consider the complete tree-level UV resonances for dimension-six and dimension-seven SMEFT operators, and representative UV completions for higher-dimensional operators. The matching between LEFT operators and the chiral Lagrangian is organized using naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to assess the relative dominance of different operator contributions.

## Key numbers

- Dimension-six LEFT operators generate only the $(\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{3})$ and $(\mathbf{8},\mathbf{1})$ chiral representations for three-quark operators, while dimension-eight operators enable the full set including $(\bar{\mathbf{6}},\mathbf{3})$ and $(\mathbf{10},\mathbf{1})$ representations.
- The NDA master formula for LEFT operators is normalized as $\frac{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^4}{16\pi^2} \left[\frac{\partial}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}}\right]^{N_p} \left[\frac{4\pi F}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^2}\right]^{N_F} \left[\frac{4\pi \psi}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^{3/2}}\right]^{N_\psi}$, with 4-fermion operators of dimension-6, -7, and -8 normalized as $\frac{{(4\pi)}^2}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^2}\psi^4$, $\frac{{(4\pi)}^2}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^2}\frac{\partial}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}}\psi^4$, and $\frac{{(4\pi)}^2}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^2}\frac{\partial^2}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}^2}\psi^4$ respectively.
- The NDA master formula for chiral perturbation theory is $f^2\Lambda_\chi^2 \left[\frac{\partial}{\Lambda_\chi}\right]^{N_p} \left[\frac{\psi}{f\sqrt{\Lambda_\chi}}\right]^{N_\psi} \left[\frac{F}{\Lambda_\chi f}\right]^{N_A}$, where $4\pi f\sim \Lambda_\chi$.
- The matching NDA formula introduces a factor $\left[\frac{\Lambda_\chi}{\Lambda_{\text{EW}}}\right]^{\mathcal{D}}$ with $\mathcal{D}=N_p+2N_F + \frac{3}{2}N_\psi-4$.

## Context

This work extends previous EFT analyses of baryon number violation that predominantly focused on dimension-six operators, such as the foundational chiral Lagrangian derivation by Claudson (1981) and subsequent lattice QCD determinations of low-energy constants by JLQCD (1999), Aoki (2006, 2008, 2017), QCDSF (2008), and Yoo (2021). The systematic spurion method builds on recent work by Li (2025) and Song (2025). The framework is motivated by next-generation experimental searches at JUNO, Hyper-Kamiokande, and DUNE, which will probe nucleon decay with improved sensitivity. The analysis enables a model-independent connection between ultraviolet dynamics and low-energy baryon decay observables, accommodating both $(B-L)$-conserving and $(B-L)$-violating processes, and can be extended to other BNV processes such as neutron-antineutron oscillations.
