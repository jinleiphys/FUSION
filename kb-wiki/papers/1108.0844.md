# Charmed-Meson Decay Constants from Improved QCD Sum Rules

> The decay constants of the charmed heavy pseudoscalar D and D_s mesons are revisited within a recently developed novel approach to dispersive QCD sum rules which relies on an unprejudiced implementation of quark-hadron duality. The proposed modification of the conventional sum-rule techniques is assessed by applying our prescriptions to quantum mechanics, where exact solutions may be easily obtained by simply solving the Schrödinger equation. The very striking similarity of the extraction procedures of bound-state parameters in potential models and in QCD gives us great confidence in the reliability of our improvements of the sum-rule formalism and their applicability to hadron phenomenology. The implications of one's chosen definition of the heavy-quark masses are scrutinized and the MS-bar quark-mass scheme is identified as the optimal choice for our purposes. Our ideas turn out to reconcile QCD sum-rule predictions for the charmed-meson decay constants (which before tended to be markedly too low) with the findings of both lattice QCD and experiment.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their improved QCD sum-rule approach, which treats the effective continuum threshold as a function of the Borel parameter rather than a constant, reconciles sum-rule predictions for charmed-meson decay constants with lattice QCD and experimental results, whereas conventional sum rules had tended to give values that were markedly too low.

## Method

The authors first test their improved sum-rule formalism in quantum mechanics by applying it to the Schrödinger equation with a funnel potential, where exact bound-state solutions are known. They then extend the method to QCD, using the MS-bar quark-mass scheme for the charm quark and fixing the behavior of the effective threshold by fitting predicted ground-state masses to experimental values. The decay constants are extracted from dispersive QCD sum rules for the pseudoscalar mesons D and D_s.

## Key numbers

*   $f_D = \left(206.2 \pm 7.3_{\rm QCD} \pm 5.1_{\rm syst}\right) \text{MeV}$, using $\overline{m}_c(\overline{m}_c) = (1.279 \pm 0.013) \text{ GeV}$ for the MS-bar charm-quark mass.
*   $f_{D_s} = \left(245.3 \pm 15.7_{\rm QCD} \pm 4.5_{\rm syst}\right) \text{MeV}$, using the same charm-quark mass input.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own prior development of an improved QCD sum-rule formalism (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2010-2011) that treats the effective continuum threshold as a function of the Borel parameter. The method is validated by comparison to exact solutions in quantum mechanics for the funnel potential (Lucha, Schöberl, Gromes, 1998). The results bring QCD sum-rule predictions for charmed-meson decay constants into agreement with lattice QCD and experiment, resolving a long-standing discrepancy where conventional sum rules gave values that were too low. The authors suggest future applications to pseudoscalar-meson form factors.
