# Strangeness in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes a purely hadronic model that can describe the sharp decrease of the K+/π+ ratio above 30 AGeV at the CERN SPS, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation. The amount of produced strangeness is mainly controlled by the total lifetime of the fireball, which is conjectured to decrease with increasing collision energy due to weakening stopping.

## Method
The model evolves kaon densities in time using a master equation that includes expansion rate, production rates from momentum-averaged cross-sections, and annihilation rates. Energy density and baryon density are parametrized as functions of time with an accelerating phase followed by a power-law expansion, with parameters tuned to match final-state chemical composition from fits by Becattini et al. (2004). Non-strange species are assumed in chemical equilibrium, while the S<0 sector (K−, Λ) is in relative chemical equilibrium with ratios given by temperature and chemical potentials, with total strangeness constrained to zero.

## Key numbers
- The lowest threshold for strangeness production in a hadronic system is about 530 MeV above incoming masses, realized in the πN → ΛK channel, compared to about 300 MeV for s s̄ pair production in QGP.
- The peak of the K+/π+ ratio occurs at projectile energy of 30 AGeV at the CERN SPS.
- Calculations are performed for Pb+Pb collisions at projectile energies including 30 AGeV and 11.6 AGeV (AGS energy).
- The parametrization for baryon density uses an exponent δ with value 3/4 < δ < 1 depending on the equation of state.

## Context
This work extends the search for alternative models that make no plasma assumptions, which must be falsified if QGP identification is to be claimed. The sharp decrease of the K+/π+ ratio above 30 AGeV was previously interpreted only within the Statistical Model of Early Stage (Smes, 2004), which connects the decrease to a mixture of hadronic and QGP phases. The proposed hadronic model can describe the data, but the authors note that applicability must be decided after cross-check with freeze-out analysis of spectra, HBT, abundances, and dilepton spectra.
