# Explosion of a Minimum-Mass Neutron Star within Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The relativistic hydrodynamics equations are adapted for the spherically symmetric case and the Lagrangian form. They are used to model the explosive disruption of a minimum-mass neutron star: a key ingredient of the stripping model for short gamma-ray bursts. The shock breakout from the neutron star surface accompanied by the acceleration of matter to ultrarelativistic velocities is studied. A comparison with the results of previously published nonrelativistic calculations is made.

# Explosion of a Minimum-Mass Neutron Star within Relativistic Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The relativistic hydrodynamics equations adapted for spherically symmetric Lagrangian form can model the explosive disruption of a minimum-mass neutron star (MMNS) without producing unphysical superluminal velocities that arise in nonrelativistic calculations; however, the gravitational field is weak enough that general relativity effects are a factor of 100 smaller than for an ordinary neutron star, making a weak-field relativistic approximation appropriate.

## Method

The author adapted the relativistic hydrodynamics equations from Hwang and Noh (2016) to spherically symmetric Lagrangian form, including artificial viscosity following Liebendoerfer et al. (2001) and a Poisson equation generalized for weak-field relativity. A minimum-mass neutron star was constructed using the BSk22 equation of state fit from Haensel and Potekhin (2004), and the explosion was initiated by imposing a small initial velocity perturbation proportional to radius, then evolved numerically with 3899 mass zones.

## Key numbers

- Minimum neutron star mass in the relativistic calculation: 0.084 solar masses
- Minimum neutron star mass in the nonrelativistic calculation: 0.089 solar masses
- Minimum neutron star mass from Blinnikov et al. (1990): 0.095 solar masses
- Explosion kinetic energy in the relativistic calculation: 8.7 × 10^50 erg
- Explosion kinetic energy in the nonrelativistic calculation: 9.1 × 10^50 erg
- Explosion kinetic energy from Blinnikov et al. (1990): 8.8 × 10^50 erg
- Mass accelerated to v/c greater than 0.1: approximately 0.047 solar masses (nearly half the stellar mass)
- Mass accelerated to v/c greater than 0.2: 3 × 10^{-4} solar masses
- Mass with v/c greater than 1 in the nonrelativistic computation: approximately 3 × 10^{-9} solar masses
- Shock breakout temperature: approximately 10^10 K (about 1 MeV)
- Number of zones in the relativistic calculation: 3899
- Number of zones in the nonrelativistic calculation: 2609
- Number of zones in Blinnikov et al. (1990): 151

## Context

This work extends the stripping model for short gamma-ray bursts originally proposed by Blinnikov et al. (1984) and the nonrelativistic MMNS explosion calculations of Blinnikov et al. (1990) and Sumiyoshi et al. (1998). The relativistic treatment resolves the problem that nonrelativistic calculations produce velocities exceeding the speed of light during shock breakout due to the cumulation effect along the descending density profile. The results enable future studies of neutrino energy losses from electron-positron pair annihilation at temperatures near 10^10 K, r-process nucleosynthesis during explosive decompression as studied by Panov and Yudin (2020), and three-dimensional calculations including tidal heating and asymmetric mass transfer in binary systems as discussed by Manukovskii (2010).
