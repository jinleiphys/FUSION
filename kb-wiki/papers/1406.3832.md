# Nucleon properties inside compressed nuclear matter

> In this work we show the modifications of nucleon mass and nucleon radius with the help of the extended Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model. We argue that even small departures above nuclear equilibrium density with constant nucleon mass require an energy transfer from the repulsive mean field to the quarks forming nucleon massive bags in Nuclear Matter (NM), together with the decrease in the nucleon volume. The transfer, which is proportional to pressure and absent in a standard RMF approach, provides good values for nuclear compressibility, symmetry energy and its slope. Different courses of the Equation of State (EOS), which depend on the energy transfer, are considered.

## Key claim
The paper argues that even small compressions of nuclear matter above equilibrium density require an energy transfer from the repulsive mean field to the quarks inside nucleon bags to keep the nucleon mass constant, a mechanism absent in standard Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models. This energy transfer, proportional to pressure, provides good values for nuclear compressibility, symmetry energy, and its slope, but the specific numerical results depend on the chosen scenario (constant mass vs. constant radius) and the initial nucleon radius.

## Method
The author extends the standard Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model by treating nucleons as MIT bag spheres of finite volume, whose radius and mass become density-dependent via a balance between internal quark pressure and external nuclear pressure. Two extreme scenarios are compared: (A) constant nucleon mass with a decreasing radius and an energy transfer \(\Delta E = p_H V_N\) to the bag, and (B) constant nucleon radius with a decreasing mass. The model is solved self-consistently using four coupled equations for the single-particle energy, effective mass, Fermi energy, and radius, with parameters fitted to saturation density (\(\varrho_0 \approx 0.193\) fm\(^{-3}\)) and binding energy (15.6 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: \(\varrho_0 \approx 0.193\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Binding energy at saturation: \(\varepsilon^q_N = 15.6\) MeV.
- For scenario (A) with initial nucleon radius \(R_0 = 0.7\) fm, the symmetry energy \(E_s = 24.8\) MeV (without \(\rho\) meson coupling) and \(E_s = 31\) MeV (with \((g_\rho/m_\rho)^2 = 1.34\) fm\(^2\)).
- The slope of the symmetry energy \(L\) in scenario (A) is 88 MeV without \(\rho\) meson coupling and 108 MeV with it; an additional energy transfer \(\Delta M = C_m p_H\) with \(C_m \simeq 40-50\) fm\(^3\) reduces \(L\) to 61 MeV.
- Nuclear compressibility \(K_q^{-1}\) in scenario (A) is in the range 250–350 MeV for \(R_0 \sim 0.7\) fm.
- Critical densities for deconfinement (bag constant vanishing) in scenario (B) are \(\varrho \simeq (0.38, 0.43)\) fm\(^{-3}\) for initial bag constants \(B(\varrho_0) = (60, 100)\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).
- Alignment density (where nuclear energy density equals nucleon energy density) in scenario (B) for \(R_0 = 0.75\) fm is \(\varrho_{al} = 0.44\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The derivative \(dM^*_{pr}/d\varrho\) at saturation is approximately \(-2000\) MeV fm\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Rozynek, 2014, not cited in text but referenced as "ja") by including the energy transfer \(\Delta E\) that was absent in the earlier constant-mass scenario. It contrasts with standard RMF models (Walecka, 1974) that treat nucleons as point particles and with other finite-volume approaches (Costa, 2010; Benic, 2011; Typel, 2012) that assume fixed size and mass without energy transfer. The model provides an alternative to RMF models with nonlinear scalar potentials (Boguta, 1977) or density-dependent couplings, and its predictions for the equation of state are compared to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Bonn A interaction, 1987). The results enable future inclusion of decreasing nucleon radius in ab-initio calculations of dense nuclear and neutron matter.
