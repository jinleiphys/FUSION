# Upsilon production in p(d)A collisions at RHIC and the LHC

> We study the effect of nuclear matter in Upsilon production in dAu collisions at RHIC and pPb collisions at the LHC. We find that the nuclear modification factor, R^Upsilon_dAu, measured at RHIC is not satisfactorily reproduced by the conventional effects used in the literature, namely the modification of the gluon distribution in bound nucleons and an --effective-- survival probability for a bound state to escape the nucleus. In particular, we argue that this probability should be close to 1 as opposed to the J/psi case. We note that, at backward rapidities, the unexpected suppression of R^Upsilon_dAu observed by PHENIX hints at the presence of a gluon EMC effect, analogous to the quark EMC effect -- but likely stronger. Further nuclear matter effects, such as saturation and fractional energy loss, are discussed, but none of them fit in a more global picture of quarkonium production. Predictions for Upsilon(nS) for the forthcoming pPb run at 5 TeV at the LHC are also presented.

## Key claim

The conventional cold nuclear matter effects (gluon shadowing/antishadowing and nuclear break-up) cannot satisfactorily reproduce the PHENIX measurement of the Upsilon nuclear modification factor in dAu collisions at backward rapidities; the data hint at a gluon EMC effect stronger than the quark EMC effect, but the authors note that current experimental uncertainties are large and confirmation requires reduced errors.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo framework (JIN) based on the probabilistic Glauber model with 2-to-2 partonic kinematics (g+g to b bbar + g) to compute Upsilon production in dAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and pPb collisions at 5 TeV. They compared three nuclear parton distribution function sets (EKS98, EPS08, nDSg) and varied the effective absorption cross section between 0 and 1 mb, then tested additional effects including Fermi motion, a gluon EMC suppression, gluon saturation, and fractional energy loss.

## Key numbers

- Formation time of Upsilon in the Au rest frame at RHIC: 5.8 fm at y=-2.0, 42 fm at y=0.0, 310 fm at y=+2.0 (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV)
- Formation time of Upsilon in the Pb rest frame at the LHC: 8 fm at y=-4.0, 700 fm at y=0.0, 6.0e4 fm at y=+4.5 (sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV)
- Saturation scale Q_sAu at RHIC forward rapidity (y=+2.0): 1.1-1.2 GeV, ratio to m_Upsilon approximately 0.1
- Saturation scale Q_sPb at LHC forward rapidity (y=+4.0): 1.9-2.5 GeV, ratio to m_Upsilon approximately 0.2-0.25
- Upsilon broadening from E772 with W target: Delta <P_T^2> = 0.410 GeV^2, corresponding to 0.072 GeV^2 A^{1/3}
- Maximum fractional energy loss estimate: Delta E_max/E approximately 4% (for N_c=3, alpha_s=0.2)
- Forward-to-backward asymmetry prediction for pPb at LHC: up to 45%
- Shadowing suppression in minimum bias PbPb at mid rapidity: approximately 20%

## Context

This work extends the study of cold nuclear matter effects on quarkonium production from J/psi to Upsilon states, using the same JIN Monte Carlo framework previously applied to J/psi at RHIC (Ferreiro 2009). The authors argue that the Upsilon survival probability should be close to 1, contrary to the J/psi case, due to the smaller b-bbar size and longer formation times. The paper provides predictions for the upcoming LHC pPb run at 5 TeV and suggests that a forward-to-backward ratio measurement could reveal nuclear PDF effects without requiring a pp reference. The fractional energy loss scenario (Arleo 2010) is discussed but noted to conflict with the successful description of low-P_T pp data by the Colour-Singlet Model (Lansberg 2012).
