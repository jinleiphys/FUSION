# QCD in Extreme Conditions and the Wilsonian `Exact Renormalization Group'

> This is an introduction to the use of nonperturbative flow equations in strong interaction physics at nonzero temperature and baryon density. We investigate the QCD phase diagram as a function of temperature, chemical potential for baryon number and quark mass within the linear quark meson model for two flavors. Whereas the renormalization group flow leads to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in vacuum, the symmetry is restored in a second order phase transition at high temperature and vanishing quark mass. We explicitly connect the physics at zero temperature and realistic quark mass with the universal behavior near the critical temperature $T_c$ and the chiral limit. At high density we find a chiral symmetry restoring first order transition. The results imply the presence of a tricritical point with long-range correlations in the phase diagram. We end with an outlook to densities above the chiral transition, where QCD is expected to behave as a color superconductor at low temperature.

# QCD in Extreme Conditions and the Wilsonian `Exact Renormalization Group'

## Key claim

The QCD phase diagram as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential, computed within the linear quark meson model for two flavors using nonperturbative flow equations, exhibits a chiral symmetry restoring second order phase transition at high temperature for vanishing quark mass and a first order transition at high density, implying the presence of a tricritical point with long-range correlations; however, the quantitative relevance for realistic quark masses depends on whether the correlation length near the critical temperature is large enough to produce observable signatures in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author employs the exact nonperturbative flow equation for the scale-dependent effective action (average action) \(\Gamma_k\), which interpolates between the classical action at the ultraviolet cutoff and the standard effective action at \(k=0\), to study the QCD phase diagram within the linear quark meson model for two quark flavors at nonzero temperature and chemical potential. Truncated flow equations are derived that incorporate both the low-temperature chiral perturbation theory domain and the high-temperature critical phenomena domain, and the renormalization group flow is followed from short to long distance scales to determine the behavior of the chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: \(n_0 = 0.153 \, \text{fm}^{-3} = (105 \, \text{MeV})^3\)
- Critical temperature for the chiral phase transition at vanishing quark mass: \(T_c \simeq 150 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Conversion factor: \((1 \, \text{MeV})^{-1} \hbar c = 197.33 \, \text{fm}\)
- Nucleon size: \(r_N \simeq 1 \, \text{fm} \simeq (200 \, \text{MeV})^{-1}\)
- Phenomenologically significant gaps from color superconductivity at intermediate densities: of order \(100 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Temperature of the nuclear gas-liquid critical endpoint: about \(10 \, \text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the application of the Wilsonian exact renormalization group to QCD phase structure, building on earlier realizations by Collins and Perry (1975) that QCD at high temperature or density differs from vacuum properties, and on the use of the average action formalism developed by Wetterich (1993). The approach connects to prior studies of critical phenomena in QCD by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), and Rajagopal (1995), and to investigations of color superconductivity by Bailin and Love (1984) and more recent effective model studies. The results enable quantitative predictions for the universal equation of state near critical points and provide a framework for determining whether the correlation length near the critical temperature is sufficient to produce observable signatures such as disoriented chiral condensates in heavy-ion collisions at AGS, CERN SPS, RHIC, and future LHC experiments.
