# Fluctuations in the canonical ensemble of an Abelian charge

> We study fluctuations in the canonical ensemble, where the net baryon number is exactly conserved. The focus is on cumulants and factorial cumulants linked to the baryon and antibaryon multiplicities and their sum or difference in full phase-space as well as in subsystems. In particular, we connect the fluctuations of the net baryon number in a subsystem, relevant for fluctuation studies in nucleus-nucleus collisions, with fluctuations of the baryon and antibaryon numbers of the total system. We derive analytic expressions for factorial cumulants of arbitrary order. Compact results are obtained in terms of cumulants of the baryon number of the total system. Moreover, we derive the asymptotic forms of the factorial cumulants of baryon and antibaryon multiplicities in the high- and low-temperature limits and discuss the results in the context of heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper derives general analytic expressions for factorial cumulants of baryon and antibaryon multiplicity distributions in a subsystem of a canonical ensemble where net baryon number is exactly conserved, extending previous results up to sixth order to arbitrary order; the results are compactly expressed in terms of cumulants of the baryon number of the total system, but the derivation assumes uncorrelated baryons in the Boltzmann approximation with baryon-baryon interactions neglected.

## Method

The authors start from a canonical partition function for uncorrelated baryons and antibaryons with exact net baryon number conservation, formulated using the S-matrix thermodynamic approach where meson-baryon interactions are subsumed into single-particle partition functions. They compute factorial cumulants of baryon and antibaryon numbers in a subsystem by differentiating the generating function using the multivariate Faà di Bruno formula, expressing the results in terms of cumulants of the total baryon number and generalized Stirling numbers of the first kind. The cumulants of net baryon number in the subsystem are then obtained from the factorial cumulants via relations involving Stirling numbers of the second kind.

## Key numbers

* The first few baryon number cumulants in the full system are given as: \(c_1 = S/2\), \(c_2 = Q\), \(c_3 = Q - W\), \(c_4 = Q - W + W S - 2 Q^2\), where \(S = \langle N_B + N_{\bar B}\rangle\), \(P = \langle N_B\rangle \langle N_{\bar B}\rangle\), \(Q = z^2 - P\), and \(W = Q S - P\).
* The factorial cumulants in a subsystem for baryons and antibaryons are expressed as \(F^{(n,m)} = -\frac{B}{2}\big((-\alpha_B)^n (n-1)! \delta_{m,0} - (-\alpha_{\bar B})^m (m-1)! \delta_{n,0}\big) + (\alpha_B)^n (\alpha_{\bar B})^m \sum_{k=1}^{n+m} c_k \sum_{l=0}^k s(n,k-l) s(m,l)\), where \(\alpha_B\) and \(\alpha_{\bar B}\) are acceptance probabilities and \(s(n,k)\) are Stirling numbers of the first kind.
* The net baryon number cumulants in a subsystem are given by \(\kappa_n = B k_+^{(n)} + \sum_{k=1}^n C_k B_{n,k}(k_-^{(1)},\dots,k_-^{(n-k+1)})\), where \(C_k = 2^k c_k\) are cumulants of \(N_B+N_{\bar B}\) in the full system and \(B_{n,k}\) are partial Bell polynomials.

## Context

This work extends the previous findings by Barej and Bzdak (2020, 2022), who derived analytic results for factorial cumulants up to sixth order in a subsystem with exact baryon number conservation. The paper provides closed-form expressions for factorial cumulants of arbitrary order, enabling a systematic baseline for fluctuation studies in heavy-ion collisions. The results are directly applicable to interpreting net-proton number fluctuation data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration at LHC, where exact conservation of net baryon number in full phase space provides a non-critical baseline that must be accounted for before searching for signals of chiral criticality.
