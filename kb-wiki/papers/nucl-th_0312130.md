# Cluster structures within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> The many-body states in an extended Fermionic Molecular Dynamics approach are flexible enough to allow the description of nuclei with shell model nature as well as nuclei with cluster and halo structures. Different many-body configurations are obtained by minimizing the energy under constraints on collective variables like radius, dipole, quadrupole and octupole deformations. In the sense of the Generator Coordinate Method we perform variation after projection and multiconfiguration calculations. The same effective interaction derived from realistic interactions by means of the Unitary Correlation Operator Method is used for all nuclei. Aspects of the shell model and cluster nature of the ground and excited states of C12 are discussed. To understand energies and radii of neutron-rich He isotopes the soft-dipole mode is found to be important.

## Key claim
The Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) approach, when extended with two Gaussians per single-particle state and variation-after-projection (VAP) plus multiconfiguration mixing, can simultaneously describe shell-model and cluster aspects of light nuclei, but the effective interaction requires a 15% correction to the ab-initio correlated two-body interaction to reproduce binding energies and radii of selected doubly-magic nuclei.

## Method
The authors construct A-body Slater determinants from single-particle Gaussian wave packets with complex width parameters and spin orientations as variational parameters. They minimize the intrinsic energy under constraints on collective variables (radius, dipole, quadrupole, octupole moments), then perform parity and angular momentum projection in the Generator Coordinate Method framework, followed by multiconfiguration diagonalization. The effective interaction is derived from realistic Bonn or Argonne potentials via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), with an additional two-body correction term fitted to binding energies and radii of 4He, 16O, 40Ca, 24O, and 48Ca.

## Key numbers
- 16O and 40Ca tetrahedral α-cluster configurations are favored over spherical shell model configurations by about 5 MeV after angular momentum projection.
- For 12C, the VAP calculation with two Gaussians yields 91.9 MeV binding energy (Exp: 92.2 MeV), charge radius 2.38 fm (Exp: 2.47 fm), and B(E2) 24.7 e²fm⁴ (Exp: 39.7 ± 3.3 e²fm⁴); the multiconfiguration calculation with 4 states gives 93.4 MeV, 2.50 fm, and 40.0 e²fm⁴.
- For 6He, the dipole-deformed VAP minimum is more bound than the quadrupole-deformed minimum by about 1.1 MeV.
- Multiconfiguration matter radii: 6He 2.42 fm (Exp: 2.48 ± 0.03 fm), 8He 2.53 fm (Exp: 2.52 ± 0.03 fm).
- Multiconfiguration binding energies: 4He 28.4 MeV (Exp: 28.3 MeV), 6He 29.1 MeV (Exp: 29.3 MeV), 8He 31.7 MeV (Exp: 31.4 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the FMD model (Neff and Feldmeier, 2000) by introducing two Gaussians per single-particle state and systematic VAP/multiconfiguration calculations, building on earlier AMD studies of 12C clustering (Enyo, 1998; Itagaki, 2003) and the UCOM effective interaction (Feldmeier et al., 1998, 2003). The results for 12C demonstrate that polarized α-clusters intermediate between pure shell-model and pure α-cluster configurations are needed, while for neutron-rich He isotopes the soft-dipole mode from zero-point motion of paired neutrons is essential. This enables future studies of excited 0⁺ and 2⁺ states in 12C, including possible Bose-condensed three-α states near threshold (Horiuchi, 2003), and suggests the soft-dipole mode is relevant for other neutron halo nuclei.
