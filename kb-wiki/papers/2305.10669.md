# Thermal photon production in Gubser inviscid relativistic fluid dynamics

> The Gubser solution to inviscid relativistic fluid dynamics is used to examine the role of transverse expansion on the energy spectrum of photons radiated by quark-gluon plasma. Transverse flow is shown to be a modest effect on the energy spectrum of photons as a whole, despite its large effect on rare high-energy photons produced at low temperatures. An exact expression is derived for the volume of the plasma as a function of its temperature. A simple formula is obtained for the energy spectrum of high-energy thermal photons, which is used to relate the inverse slope $T_{\textrm{eff}}$ of the photon spectrum at energy $E$ to the maximum temperature of the plasma $T_0$, finding $T_{\textrm{eff}} \approx T_0/(1+\frac{5}{2} \frac{T_0}{E})$.

# Thermal photon production in Gubser inviscid relativistic fluid dynamics

## Key claim

Transverse flow has only a modest effect on the overall photon energy spectrum (less than 30% change for most energies), despite producing enormous Doppler shifts of up to five orders of magnitude for rare high-energy photons emitted at low temperatures. The inverse slope of the photon spectrum relates to the maximum plasma temperature as T_eff ≈ T_0/(1 + (5/2)(T_0/E)), showing that the effective temperature depends on the photon energy range used for fitting.

## Method

The Gubser exact solution to inviscid relativistic fluid dynamics with a conformal equation of state (c_s^2 = 1/3) was used to obtain spacetime temperature and flow velocity profiles for quark-gluon plasma. Thermal photon emission was computed by convolving the equilibrium photon emission rate from Arnold et al. (2001) with the Gubser temperature and flow profiles, using a minimum temperature cutoff T_min = 100 MeV to account for hadronization. The photon energy spectrum was evaluated numerically with and without the Doppler shift from transverse flow, and an analytic approximation was derived for high-energy photons.

## Key numbers

- Transverse flow changes the overall photon spectrum by less than 30% for most of the energy range
- For 4 GeV photons produced at low temperatures (100-200 MeV), the Doppler shift enhances the spectrum by three orders of magnitude; for 6 GeV photons, by five orders of magnitude
- The transverse volume per unit temperature at low temperatures scales as dV_⟂/dT ∝ T^{-4}, compared to T^{-7} when transverse expansion is neglected
- For T_0 = 0.5 GeV and E = 2 GeV, the effective temperature is T_eff = 0.307 GeV
- The approximate formula for high-energy photon production (E ≳ 2 GeV) is valid for parameters T_0 = 0.5 GeV, q = 0.25 fm^{-1}, τ_0 = 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken solution (Bjorken, 1982) for longitudinal expansion by using the Gubser solution (Gubser, 2010) to include transverse expansion in an analytically tractable setting. It provides a simple analytic framework to understand the relationship between the measured effective temperature of thermal photons and the actual maximum plasma temperature, complementing numerical studies by Shen et al. (2013) and Paquet et al. (2016) that include viscosity, non-conformal equations of state, and fluctuating initial conditions. The results enable more precise interpretation of photon excess measurements from PHENIX (2014), ALICE (2015), and STAR (2016), and can be extended to include shear viscosity effects in future work.
