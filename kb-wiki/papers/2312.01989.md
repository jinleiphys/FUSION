# Strange hidden-charm pentaquark poles from $B^-\to J/ψΛ\bar{p}$

> Recent LHCb data for $B^-\to J/ψΛ\bar{p}$ show a clear peak structure at the $Ξ_c\bar{D}$ threshold in the $J/ψΛ$ invariant mass ($M_{J/ψΛ}$) distribution. The LHCb's amplitude analysis identified the peak with the first hidden-charm pentaquark with strangeness $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$. We conduct a coupled-channel amplitude analysis of the LHCb data by simultaneously fitting the $M_{J/ψΛ}$, $M_{J/ψ\bar{p}}$, $M_{Λ\bar{p}}$, and $\cosθ_{K^*}$ distributions. Rather than the Breit-Wigner fit employed in the LHCb analysis, we consider relevant threshold effects and a unitary $Ξ_c\bar{D}$-$Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel scattering amplitude from which $P_{ψs}^Λ$ poles are extracted for the first time. In our default fit, the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ pole is almost a $Ξ_c \bar{D}$ bound state at $( 4338.2\pm 1.4)-( 1.9\pm 0.5 )\,i$ MeV. Our default model also fits a large fluctuation at the $Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ threshold, giving a $Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ virtual state, $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$, at $4254.7\pm 0.4$ MeV. We also found that the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ peak cannot solely be a kinematical effect, and a nearby pole is needed.

## Key claim

The LHCb's Breit-Wigner fit for the $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ peak is unsuitable because the peak sits right on the $\Xi_c\bar{D}$ threshold, where a kinematical threshold cusp may cause the resonancelike structure; the authors instead extract poles from a unitary coupled-channel amplitude and find that the $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ peak cannot solely be a kinematical effect and requires a nearby pole.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel amplitude analysis of LHCb data for $B^-\to J/\psi\Lambda\bar{p}$ by simultaneously fitting the $M_{J/\psi\Lambda}$, $M_{J/\psi\bar{p}}$, $M_{\Lambda\bar{p}}$, and $\cos\theta_{K^*}$ distributions. Instead of Breit-Wigner amplitudes, they used a unitary $\Xi_c\bar{D}$-$\Lambda_c\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel scattering amplitude with contact separable hadron interactions, respecting coupled-channel unitarity, and included diagrams for hadronic rescatterings at the $\Xi_c\bar{D}$, $\Lambda_c\bar{D}_s$, and $\bar{\Lambda}_c D$ thresholds. The scattering amplitudes were analytically continued to extract pole positions.

## Key numbers

- $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ pole at $(4338.2 \pm 1.4) - (1.9 \pm 0.5)\,i$ MeV, with $J^P = 1/2^-$, identified as almost a $\Xi_c\bar{D}$ bound state.
- $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4255)$ pole at $4254.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV, identified as a $\Lambda_c\bar{D}_s$ virtual state.
- Fit quality: $\chi^2/\text{ndf} \simeq 1.21$ with 9 fitting parameters in the default model, compared to 16 parameters in the LHCb model.
- The LHCb analysis reported $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ mass $4338.2 \pm 0.7$ MeV, width $7.0 \pm 1.2$ MeV, and $J^P = 1/2^-$ using a Breit-Wigner fit.

## Context

This work extends the LHCb analysis (2023) of $B^-\to J/\psi\Lambda\bar{p}$ by addressing the inadequacy of the Breit-Wigner fit for a peak at threshold, following the authors' previous analysis of $B^0_s\to J/\psi p\bar{p}$ (Nakamura and Wu, 2023) which found a repulsive $\bar{\Lambda}_c D$ interaction. The results enable future studies of the $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ and $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4255)$ poles using more precise data from $B^-\to \Lambda_c \bar{D}_s \bar{p}$ and $\Xi^-_b \to J/\psi\Lambda K^-$ decays.
