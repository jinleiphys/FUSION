# Color-Singlet and Color-Octet Quark Matters

> Quarks and antiquarks carry color and electric charges and belong to the color-triplet $3$ group and the color-antitriplet $\bar 3$ group respectively. The product groups of $3$ and $\bar 3$ consist of the color-singlet $1$ and the color-octet $8$ subgroups. Therefore, quarks and antiquarks combine to form color-singlet $[q \bar q]^1$ quark matter and color-octet $[q \bar q]^8$ quark matter. The color-octet quark matter corresponds to the $q\bar q$ quark matter as envisaged in the realm of present knowledge but the color-singlet quark matter is as yet unexplored and now submitted for exploration. The color-singlet quark matter with two flavors can be separated into charged and neutral color-singlet quark matters. In the neutral color-singlet quark matter, the quark and the antiquark interacting only in the QED interaction may form stable and confined colorless QED mesons non-perturbatively at about 17 MeV and 38 MeV (PRC81,064903(2010) and JHEP(2020(8),165). It is proposed that the possible existence of the QED mesons may be a signature of the neutral color-singlet quark matter at $T=0$. The observations of the anomalous soft photons at CERN, and the anomalous bosons with mass about 17 MeV at ATOMKI, DUBNA, and HUS, and mass about 38 MeV at DUBNA hold promising experimental evidence for the existence of such QED mesons, pending further confirmations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quarks and antiquarks can form not only the well-studied color-octet quark matter (conventional QCD-based quark matter) but also a previously unexplored color-singlet quark matter, in which quark-antiquark pairs interact only via QED and may form stable, confined "QED mesons" at zero temperature, potentially explaining several experimental anomalies, though this remains pending further confirmation.

## Method

The author applies group theory (color-triplet 3 and color-antitriplet 3̅ product yielding color-singlet 1 and color-octet 8 subgroups) to classify qq̅ quark matter into color-singlet and color-octet types. For neutral color-singlet quark matter with two light flavors, the Schwinger confinement mechanism in (1+1)D QED is used to derive QED meson masses, with the (1+1)D open string treated as an approximate compactification of a (3+1)D flux tube. The mass formula incorporates a flux tube radius R_T ~ 0.4 fm and a QED quark condensate contribution proportional to the QCD quark condensate scaled by the ratio of coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Predicted QED meson masses (including quark condensate contribution): m_{I=0}^{QED} = 17.9 MeV, m_{I=1}^{QED} = 36.4 MeV
- Without quark condensate: m_{I=0}^{QED} = 11.4 MeV, m_{I=1}^{QED} = 33.6 MeV
- Flux tube radius assumed: R_T ~ 0.4 fm
- DELPHI anomalous soft photon yields: dy_asp/dN_ch = (6.9 ± 1.8 ± 1.8) × 10^{-3} γ/jet and dy_asp/dN_neu = (37.7 ± 3.0 ± 3.6) × 10^{-3} γ/jet
- ATOMKI X17 signal observed at ~17 MeV; energy sum peak at ~20.6 MeV for the p + ³H reaction at E_p^{lab} = 0.9 MeV
- QCD meson mass formula (with strange quark) gives m_η = 498.4 MeV and m_η' = 948.2 MeV, in approximate agreement with experiment

## Context

This work extends the conventional treatment of quark matter (Wong 1993, Yagi 2005, Vogt 2007) by considering color-singlet qq̅ configurations that interact only via QED, a sector previously unexplored. The QED meson predictions (Wong 2010, Wong 2020) are proposed as explanations for the anomalous soft photon excess observed at CERN (Chliapnikov 1984, Botterweck 1991, Banerjee 1993, Belogianni 1997, 2002, DELPHI 2006, 2008, 2010) and for the X17 (~17 MeV) and E38 (~38 MeV) neutral boson anomalies observed at ATOMKI (Krasznahorkay 2016, 2019, 2021, 2022, 2023), Dubna (Abrashkin 2009, 2012, 2019, 2023), and HUS (Tran 2024). If confirmed, this would open a new frontier in quark matter physics, including possible QED meson gas and quark-photon plasma phases at finite temperature, and could resolve the puzzling DELPHI anomaly where anomalous soft photons are produced more readily with neutral hadrons than with charged hadrons.
