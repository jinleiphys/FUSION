# Likelihood of a zero in the proton elastic electric form factor

> Working with the $29$ available data on the ratio of proton electric and magnetic form factors, $μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/ G_M^p(Q^2)$, and independent of any model or theory of strong interactions, we use the Schlessinger point method to objectively address the question of whether the ratio possesses a zero and, if so, its location. Our analysis predicts that, with 50% confidence, the data are consistent with the existence of a zero in the ratio on $Q^2 \leq 10.37\,$GeV$^2$. The level of confidence increases to $99.9$\% on $Q^2 \leq 13.06\,$GeV$^2$. Significantly, the likelihood that existing data are consistent with the absence of a zero in the ratio on $Q^2 \leq 14.49\,$GeV$^2$ is $1/1$-million.

## Key claim
Using a model-independent statistical method (the Schlessinger point method) on the 29 available polarization transfer data points, the authors predict that the data are consistent with a zero in the ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) at Q^2 = 10.37 GeV^2, but only with 50% confidence; the confidence increases to 99.9% for a zero on Q^2 ≤ 13.06 GeV^2.

## Method
The authors applied the Schlessinger point method (SPM), a continued-fraction interpolation technique, to the 29 available data points on the ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) obtained from polarization transfer experiments. They generated 15,000 Gaussian-distributed replicas of the data, selected M-point subsets (M = 9, 8, 7, 5, 4) at random from each replica, constructed SPM interpolators, and retained only those that were C^1 and monotonically decreasing on 0 < Q^2/GeV^2 < 20. For each of 5,000 independent replicas, 25 acceptable interpolators were averaged to produce a representative curve, and the zero-crossing locations were extracted.

## Key numbers
- The combined SPM prediction for the zero location in μ_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2) is Q_z^2 = 10.37_{-0.68}^{+0.87} GeV^2.
- With 50% confidence, the data are consistent with a zero on Q^2 ≤ 10.37 GeV^2.
- With 99.9% confidence, the data are consistent with a zero on Q^2 ≤ 13.06 GeV^2.
- The likelihood that existing data are consistent with the absence of a zero on Q^2 ≤ 14.49 GeV^2 is 1/1-million.
- For M=9 interpolators, 92% of individual interpolators crossed zero on Q^2 < 15 GeV^2; for M=7, 95%; for M=5, 97%.
- The M=9 result from one replica set gave Q_z^2 = 9.86^{+4.45}_{-1.77} GeV^2; from an independent set, 9.87^{+4.33}_{-1.75} GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing debate over whether the proton electric form factor ratio possesses a zero, following the first polarization transfer measurements by Jones (1999) which showed the ratio falls with Q^2. The SPM analysis provides a model-independent complement to parameter-dependent predictions from Faddeev equation studies such as Yao (2024), which predicted a zero at Q_z^2 = 8.86_{-0.86}^{+1.93} GeV^2, and to phenomenological fits like Ye (2017). The results enable objective benchmarking for future experiments planned to reach Q^2 = 12 GeV^2, and challenge models that predict no zero, such as those by Anikin (2013), Giannini (2015), Sufian (2016), Kallidonis (2018), and Xu (2021).
