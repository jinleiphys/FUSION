# Binding two and three $α$ particles in cold neutron matter

> We elucidate the fate of neighboring two and three-$α$ particles in cold neutron matter by focusing on an analogy between such $α$ systems and Fermi polarons realized in ultracold atoms. We describe in-medium excitation properties of an $α$ particle and neutron-mediated two- and three-$α$ interactions using theoretical approaches developed for studies of cold atomic systems. We numerically solve the few-body Schrödinger equation of $α$ particles within standard $α$ cluster models combined with in-medium properties of $α$ particles. We point out that the resultant two-$α$ ground state and three-$α$ first excited state, which correspond to $^8$Be and the Hoyle state, respectively, known as main components in the triple-$α$ reaction, can become bound states in such a many-neutron background although these states are unstable in vacuum. Our results suggest a significance of these in-medium cluster states not only in astrophysical environments such as core-collapsed supernova explosions and neutron star mergers but also in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The normally unbound ground state of $^8$Be and the first excited Hoyle state of $^{12}$C can become bound in cold neutron matter at densities below about 0.01 times nuclear saturation density, provided the neutron Fermi momentum exceeds certain thresholds that depend on the choice of $\alpha$-cluster model.

## Method

The authors treat each $\alpha$ particle as a mobile impurity (Fermi polaron) in a dilute neutron Fermi sea at zero temperature, using Chevy's variational ansatz from ultracold-atom physics to obtain the in-medium effective mass of a single $\alpha$ particle. They derive medium-induced two-body (RKKY-type) and three-body interactions among $\alpha$ particles via a diagrammatic approach, then solve the few-body Schrödinger equation for two- and three-$\alpha$ systems using a correlated Gaussian basis expansion with two different $\alpha$-$\alpha$ potential models (Ali-Bodmer shallow repulsive potential and orthogonality condition model with deep attractive potential).

## Key numbers

- The $s$-wave neutron-$\alpha$ scattering length is $a = 2.64$ fm.
- The effective range of the neutron-$\alpha$ interaction is $r_0 = 1.43$ fm, giving a cutoff energy scale $\hbar^2/(m_r r_0^2) \simeq 25$ MeV.
- The ground state of $^8$Be becomes bound at neutron Fermi momentum $k_F \gtrsim 0.11$ fm$^{-1}$ for the Ali-Bodmer potential and $k_F \gtrsim 0.08$ fm$^{-1}$ for the orthogonality condition model.
- The Hoyle state becomes bound at $k_F \gtrsim 0.22$ fm$^{-1}$ for the Ali-Bodmer potential and $k_F \gtrsim 0.16$ fm$^{-1}$ for the orthogonality condition model.
- The Ali-Bodmer potential reproduces the $^8$Be resonance at 0.086 MeV (empirical value 0.092 MeV) and the Hoyle state at 0.38 MeV above the three-$\alpha$ threshold.
- The orthogonality condition model reproduces the $^8$Be resonance at 0.095 MeV and the Hoyle state at 0.34 MeV above the three-$\alpha$ threshold.
- The induced three-$\alpha$ interaction is repulsive and its strength is inversely proportional to $k_F$.

## Context

This work extends the polaron-based treatment of a single $\alpha$ particle in neutron matter by Nakano, Iida, and Horiuchi (2020) to two- and three-$\alpha$ systems, providing the first quantitative evaluation of their in-medium binding. The results suggest that molecular-like $\alpha$ cluster states should be incorporated into simulations of astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, complementing the microscopic $\alpha+\alpha+n$ cluster model calculations by Lyu et al. (2015) that showed $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance shrinkage in $^9$Be due to neutron mediation.
