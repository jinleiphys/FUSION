# Isotope Shifts of Radium Monofluoride Molecules

> Isotope shifts of $^{223-226,228}$Ra$^{19}$F were measured for different vibrational levels in the electronic transition $A^{2}{}Π_{1/2}\leftarrow X^{2}{}Σ^{+}$. The observed isotope shifts demonstrate the particularly high sensitivity of radium monofluoride to nuclear size effects, offering a stringent test of models describing the electronic density within the radium nucleus. Ab initio quantum chemical calculations are in excellent agreement with experimental observations. These results highlight some of the unique opportunities that short-lived molecules could offer in nuclear structure and in fundamental symmetry studies.

## Key claim

The isotope shifts of radium monofluoride molecules exhibit a particularly high sensitivity to nuclear size effects, and ab initio quantum chemical calculations are in excellent agreement with experimental observations, but the theoretical values carry an estimated overall uncertainty of about 10% due to basis set limitations, active space size, nuclear model choice, and neglected higher-order electron correlation effects.

## Method

Isotope shifts of the electronic transition A2Π1/2 ← X2Σ+ were measured for five isotopologues (223-226,228Ra19F) using the Collinear Resonance Ionization Spectroscopy (CRIS) experiment at the ISOLDE facility at CERN. Radium isotopes were produced by impinging 1.4-GeV protons onto a uranium-carbide target, reacted with CF4 gas to form RaF molecules, mass-selected, neutralized in-flight by collisions with sodium vapor, and resonantly ionized using two pulsed laser beams. Relativistic Fock-Space Coupled Cluster including Singles and Doubles amplitudes (FSCCSD) calculations were performed to compute electronic transition wavenumbers for different nuclear charge radii, with vibrational corrections calculated using a one-dimensional discrete variable representation (DVR) approach.

## Key numbers

- Isotope shift constant F for the 0 ← 0 transition of A2Π1/2 ← X2Σ+ in RaF: experimental value -0.845(24) cm-1/fm2
- FSCCSD(17e) calculation of F at bond length 4.3 a0: -0.797 cm-1/fm2; FSCCSD(27e) calculation: -0.825 cm-1/fm2
- Isotope shift constant in the 7p2P1/2 ← 7s2S1/2 transition in Ra+ calculated at FSCCSD(27e) level: F = -1.283(3) cm-1/fm2
- Correction to the Y00 Dunham parameter ΔV00A,Π-Σ from experiment: -0.839(33) cm-1/fm2
- Change in effective electron density between Π and Σ states from experiment: 392(15) Å-3
- Measured isotope shifts for 223Ra19F 0 ← 0 transition relative to 226Ra19F: 0.269(9) cm-1; for 228Ra19F: -0.184(17) cm-1

## Context

This work provides the first isotope shift measurements in molecules containing short-lived isotopes and the first such measurements in molecules containing elements heavier than lead (Z=82), extending prior work on atomic isotope shifts by groups such as Garcia Ruiz (2016) and Campbell (2016). The results establish an important benchmark for quantum chemical calculations of heavy-element molecules, which are essential for extracting nuclear properties and fundamental physics observables from molecular spectroscopy, and enable future studies of nuclear structure in exotic actinide nuclei that are challenging to study in atomic or ionic form.
