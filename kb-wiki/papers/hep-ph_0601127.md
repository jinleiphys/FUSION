# Hadronic Parity Violation: a New View through the Looking Glass

> Studies of the strangeness changing hadronic weak interaction have produced a number of puzzles that have so far evaded a complete explanation within the Standard Model. Their origin may lie either in dynamics peculiar to weak interactions involving strange quarks or in more general aspects of the interplay between strong and weak interactions. In principle, studies of the strangeness conserving hadronic weak interaction using parity violating hadronic and nuclear observables provide a complementary window on this question. However, progress in this direction has been hampered by the lack of a suitable theoretical framework for interpreting hadronic parity violation measurements in a model-independent way. Recent work involving effective field theory ideas has led to the formulation of such a framework while motivating the development of a number of new hadronic parity violation experiments in few-body systems. In this article, we review these recent developments and discuss the prospects and opportunities for further experimental and theoretical progress.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the conventional meson-exchange framework for interpreting hadronic parity violation (PV) has reached its limits due to model-dependent ambiguities, and that a new effective field theory (EFT) approach, combined with precise experiments in few-body systems, offers a more systematic path to connecting PV observables with the Standard Model, potentially shedding light on unresolved puzzles in the strangeness-changing sector.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical development of a parity-violating effective field theory (PV EFT) for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, which at energies well below the pion mass contains only four-nucleon operators characterized by five low-energy constants (λ⁰,¹,²_s, λ_t, ρ_t), and at higher energies includes three additional constants associated with pion-exchange effects (h_π¹, k_π^{1a}, and C̅_π). They compare this EFT framework to the older Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange model, which parameterizes the PV NN interaction using seven PV meson-nucleon couplings (h_π¹, h_ρ⁰,¹,², h_ρ^{1′}, h_ω⁰,¹). The review also summarizes experimental progress in few-body PV measurements, including polarized proton-proton scattering, neutron spin rotation, and polarized neutron capture on hydrogen.

## Key numbers

* The natural strength for weak ΔS=1 baryon couplings is g_π = 3.8 × 10⁻⁸ ≈ G_F F_π²/(2√2), where F_π = 92.4 MeV is the pion decay constant.
* The DDH reasonable range for the PV πNN coupling h_π¹ is 0 to 30 (in units of g_π), with a best value of +12; the DDH best value for h_ρ⁰ is -30, with a range of 30 to -81.
* The expected magnitude of PV effects in few-body systems is O(10⁻⁷), while nuclear structure amplification in many-body nuclei (e.g., ¹³⁹La) can produce PV effects of order 10%.
* The SAMPLE Collaboration measured the PV asymmetry in quasielastic scattering from the deuteron; after reanalysis of pion-production background, the extracted effective axial coupling G_A^e agreed with predictions from heavy baryon χPT and earlier work.

## Context

This review extends and updates the earlier comprehensive review by Adelberger and Haxton (1985), which documented the state of hadronic PV experiments and theory at that time. The paper contrasts the DDH meson-exchange framework (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1979) with the newer PV EFT approach formulated by Zhu et al. (2004), which builds on developments in NN EFT and chiral perturbation theory. The work enables a program of precise few-body PV experiments (at NIST, LANSCE, the SNS, and IASA) whose interpretation can be made with ab initio few-body calculations, free from the nuclear structure uncertainties that plague many-body PV measurements such as those in ¹⁸F and ¹³³Cs.
