# Probing the nucleon structure with CLAS

## Key claim
The CLAS results on nucleon resonance transitions, spin structure, and generalized parton distributions provide new constraints on the underlying degrees of freedom in the nucleon, but many of the observed behaviors, such as the N→Δ quadrupole ratios failing to approach asymptotic predictions and the Roper resonance amplitude showing a sign change, present serious challenges to existing theoretical models, including constituent quark models and light-cone approaches.

## Method
The CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab used electron beams with varying momentum transfer to measure exclusive and inclusive electroproduction reactions, including π⁰, π⁺, π⁻, η, K⁺Λ, and K⁺Σ final states, across a broad kinematic range. Resonance transition form factors were extracted using unitary isobar models and dispersion relation analyses of single- and double-pion electroproduction data. Spin structure functions were obtained from inclusive double-polarization scattering on proton and deuteron targets, and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) beam spin asymmetries were measured to access generalized parton distributions.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic transition form factor G*_M for the N→Δ(1232) transition measured up to Q² = 6 GeV², with pion contributions making up more than 30% of the total amplitude at the photon point
- Electric quadrupole ratio R_EM remains in the range from -2% to -4% at the highest Q², showing no indication of approaching the predicted asymptotic behavior of R_EM → +100% as Q² → ∞
- The Roper P₁₁(1440) transverse amplitude A₁/₂ shows a zero-crossing near Q² = 2 GeV², followed by a smooth falloff at higher Q²
- Helicity asymmetry for the D₁₃(1520) state shows the transition from helicity 3/2 dominance to helicity 1/2 dominance occurring in the range Q² = 0.5–1.0 GeV², with asymptotic value approached at Q² > 3 GeV²
- Photocoupling amplitude for the P₁₃(1720) resonance found as A^(GWU)₁/₂(0) = 96.6 ± 3.4, compared to the RPP average of A^(RPP)₁/₂(0) = 18 ± 30
- The ratio A₁/A₀ in η electroproduction changes sign near W = 1.65 GeV, consistent with a rapid change in the relative phase of E₀₊ and M₁₋ multipoles
- The uncertainty in the polarized gluon distribution from LSS QCD analysis is reduced by a factor of 3 at x_B = 0.4 using CLAS data on g₁d(x,Q²)

## Context
This paper reviews CLAS results that extend and challenge prior work on nucleon resonance structure, including the long-standing problem of "missing states" predicted by the SU(6)⊗O(3) constituent quark model but not observed experimentally. The N→Δ transition results build on benchmark measurements from JLab (Joo 2002, Ungaro 2006, Kelly 2005, Frolov 1999), MIT-Bates (Sparveris 2003), and MAMI (Sparveris 2007), while the Roper resonance analysis provides the first systematic extraction of transition form factors that discriminate between light-cone quark models, hybrid baryon models (Li and Burkert), and meson-cloud approaches. The spin structure results enable improved QCD fits to extract polarized parton distributions, and the DVCS measurements provide the first broad-kinematic constraints on generalized parton distributions that will be extended by planned 12 GeV experiments with CLAS12.
