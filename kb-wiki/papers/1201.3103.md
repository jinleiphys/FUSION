# Neutrino and antineutrino oscillations from decay of Z boson

> The two-particle wave function of neutrino and antineutrino after the decay of a Z boson is found as a solution of an initial value problem. The wave packet of this state involves the coherent superposition of massive neutrinos and antineutrinos resulting in independent oscillations of their flavor counterparts. Possible experiments to observe oscillations of a single lepton or both leptons from the same decay and the three independently emitted pairs are considered.

# Neutrino and antineutrino oscillations from decay of Z boson

## Key claim
Neutrino and antineutrino produced in Z boson decay are entangled and oscillate independently of each other, with the two-particle wave packet having a fixed energy equal to the decay energy, but the claim is conditional on the far-zone approximation and the Weisskopf-Wigner treatment of the decay dynamics.

## Method
The author solves the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the Z boson decay into a neutrino-antineutrino pair, using the Weisskopf-Wigner theory of spontaneous emission with the initial Z boson wave function taken as a finite-size wave packet in its rest frame. The two-particle wave function is obtained in the far-zone approximation by integrating over momentum variables using the residue method, yielding a product of center-of-mass and relative-motion wave functions that does not factorize in the neutrino and antineutrino spatial coordinates.

## Key numbers
- The decay width of the Z boson is Γ = 86 MeV.
- The width of the relative-motion wave packet is D_ij ≈ 2/Γ ~ 10^(-13) cm.
- The spreading time of the relative-motion wave packet is t_RM ~ 100 s.
- The oscillation length is L_ij = 2π M / |Δm²_ij|, with neutrino energy equal to M/2.
- The coherence length is L_coh = L_osc Q/Γ ~ 10³ L_osc.
- The condition for negligible spatial separation of massive neutrino wave packets is π Γ/Q ≪ 1.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous formalism developed for electron capture decay (Pavlichenkov, 2011) to the case of Z boson decay, where the small parameter α = Q/M equals 1 rather than being small. The problem of neutrino oscillations in invisible Z decay was previously considered by Bilenky and Pontecorvo and by Smirnov and Zatsepin, but with no consensus among them. The paper enables future experimental consideration of coincidence measurements detecting both leptons from a single Z decay, and shows that for an incoherent flux of Z bosons, neutrinos do not oscillate, in agreement with Bilenky and Pontecorvo.
