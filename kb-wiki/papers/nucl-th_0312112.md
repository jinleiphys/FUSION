# Stellar matter in the Quark-Meson-Coupling Model with neutrino trapping

> The properties of hybrid stars formed by hadronic and quark matter in $β$-equilibrium are described by appropriate equations of state (EoS) in the framework of the quark meson coupling (QMC) model. In the present work we include the possibility of trapped neutrinos in the equation of state and obtain the properties of the related hybrid stars. We use the quark meson coupling model for the hadron matter and two possibilities for the quark matter phase, namely, the unpaired quark phase and the color-flavor locked phase. The differences are discussed and a comparison with other relativistic EoS is done.

## Key claim

The inclusion of trapped neutrinos (with fixed total lepton fraction \(Y_L=0.4\)) stiffens the equation of state for hybrid stars in the quark-meson-coupling model, but the resulting increase in maximum gravitational mass is smaller (about \(0.2\,M_\odot\)) than the increase seen in the non-linear Walecka model (about \(0.4\,M_\odot\)), implying that fewer stars would decay into black holes during deleptonization in the QMC framework.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for hybrid (hadronic+quark) matter in beta equilibrium at zero temperature, using the quark-meson-coupling model with hyperons for the hadron phase and either the MIT bag model (unpaired quark matter) or the color-flavor locked (CFL) superconducting phase for the quark phase. Neutrino trapping is imposed by fixing the total lepton fraction to \(Y_L=0.4\) and modifying the chemical equilibrium condition to include the electron-neutrino chemical potential; the mixed phase is treated via Gibbs conditions with global charge neutrality. Stellar properties are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for QMC+UQM with \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, and neutrino trapping: \(1.94\,M_\odot\) (radius 12.09 km, central energy density \(5.47\) fm\(^{-4}\)).
- Maximum gravitational mass for the same model without neutrinos (\(Y_{\nu_e}=0\)): \(1.73\,M_\odot\) (radius 12.44 km, central energy density \(4.89\) fm\(^{-4}\)).
- Maximum gravitational mass for QMC+CFL with \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, and neutrino trapping: \(1.80\,M_\odot\) (radius 10.86 km, central energy density \(7.32\) fm\(^{-4}\)).
- Maximum baryonic mass for QMC+UQM with \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, and neutrino trapping: \(2.15\,M_\odot\).
- Maximum baryonic mass for QMC+CFL with \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, and neutrino trapping: \(1.99\,M_\odot\).
- For comparison, NLWM+UQM with \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV, \(m_s=150\) MeV, and neutrino trapping gives maximum gravitational mass \(2.00\,M_\odot\) and baryonic mass \(2.22\,M_\odot\).
- The difference in maximum gravitational mass between trapped and untrapped matter is about \(0.2\,M_\odot\) for QMC+UQM and about \(0.4\,M_\odot\) for NLWM+UQM.
- The neutrino fraction in the mixed and quark phases reaches up to about 0.16 for the UQM case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study of hybrid stars in the QMC model (Panda, Menezes, Providencia, 2003) by adding neutrino trapping, following the approach used for the non-linear Walecka model by Menezes and Providencia (2003). It also builds on earlier work by Prakash et al. (1997) and by Gourgoulhon and Bonazzola (1993) on the role of trapped neutrinos in proto-neutron star evolution. The results enable comparisons between the QMC and NLWM frameworks for the fate of proto-neutron stars, showing that the mass reduction during deleptonization is model-dependent and that the CFL phase would produce a much larger neutrino flux than the unpaired quark phase, though finite-temperature effects are expected to weaken this difference.
