# The effect of $T$-invariance violation in scattering of polarized $^3$He nuclei on tensor-polarized deuterons

> In the interaction of a transversely polarized nuclear beam with a tensor-polarized deuteron target, a nonzero value of the component of the total cross section of the process corresponding to this combination of polarizations is an unambiguous signal of $T$-invariance violation while $P$-parity is preserved. The method developed earlier for calculating this component of the total cross section for $pd$ scattering based on the Glauber theory has been generalized by us to the case of $^3$He$d$ scattering, and its energy dependence in the range of beam energies 0.1--1 GeV/nucleon has been calculated. It is found that in $^3$He$d$ collisions, in contrast to $pd$ scattering, the contribution of only one type of $T$-violating nucleon-nucleon forces dominates, which is essential for extraction of the unknown constant of this interaction from the corresponding data.

## Key claim

In $^3$He$d$ scattering, unlike in $pd$ scattering, the TVPC signal is dominated by only one type of $T$-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction (the $h_N$-type), with contributions from $g_N$-type and $g'$-type interactions being suppressed or vanishing entirely, which simplifies extraction of the unknown constant of this interaction from experimental data.

## Method

The authors generalized their earlier Glauber theory approach for calculating the TVPC signal in $pd$ scattering to the case of $^3$He$d$ scattering. They constructed $T$-even and $T$-odd spin-dependent amplitudes for elastic $p^3$He and $n^3$He scattering using elementary $pN$ amplitudes from the SAID database, accounting for single-, double-, and triple-scattering mechanisms within the $S$-wave approximation for the $^3$He wave function. The TVPC signal was then computed from the imaginary part of the forward $^3$He$d$ scattering amplitude using the double-scattering mechanism with $^3$He$N$ amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The TVPC signal in $^3$He$d$ scattering was calculated over the beam energy range 0.1--1 GeV/nucleon.
* For the $g_N$-type interaction, the contribution to the TVPC signal is suppressed by 3--4 orders of magnitude compared to the $h_N$-type interaction (assuming equal $h$ and $g$ constants).
* The $p^3$He elastic scattering calculations were tested against experimental data at proton energies of 156, 200, 300, 415, and 515 MeV, and at 1 GeV.
* The $^3$He wave function parameter used was $c^{-1}=1.56\sqrt{2}$ fm.
* The planned experimental accuracy for the TVPC signal in $pd$ scattering at 135 MeV is $10^{-6}$, requiring suppression of deuteron vector polarization to $p_y^d < 2\cdot 10^{-6}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Glauber theory calculations of the TVPC signal in $pd$ scattering (Uzikov 2015, Uzikov 2016) to the $^3$He$d$ system, which is considered for the first time. The study is motivated by the search for $T$-invariance violation while $P$-parity is conserved (TVPC effects), which are much less constrained experimentally than electric dipole moments and could indicate physics beyond the Standard Model. The results enable experimental searches for TVPC signals using polarized $^3$He beams at facilities such as RHIC, the future EIC, and the NICA complex, where polarized $^3$He beams are being developed (Zelenski 2023).
