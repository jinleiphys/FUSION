# Probing quark gluon plasma with jets

> We study multiple scatterings of jets on constituents of quark gluon plasma and introduce energy--energy correlations to quantify their effects. The effects from a longitudinally expanding plasma on medium as well as high energy jets are found to be significant at both RHIC and LHC energies. Because jets escape from the plasma long before the completion of mixed phase, these effects are free from complications of final state hadronic interactions and decays. These suggest that jets can be used to probe the plasma that might be created in future high energy heavy ion collisions.

# Probing quark gluon plasma with jets

## Key claim

Multiple scatterings of jets on quark gluon plasma constituents produce significant effects on transverse energy–energy correlations at both RHIC and LHC energies, provided the plasma is formed and undergoes a phase transition; the effects are free from final state hadronic interactions because jets escape the plasma before the mixed phase completes.

## Method

The authors derived an analytic expression for the transverse energy–energy correlation function for back-to-back jets propagating through a longitudinally expanding quark gluon plasma, incorporating multiple independent scatterings on plasma constituents. They modeled the plasma evolution using the Bjorken isentropic expansion model, computed the average number of scatterings per jet from the plasma density and geometry, and added the QCD perturbative contribution from 2→2 and 2→3 processes as well as a hadronization correction. The final correlation function was evaluated for central Au–Au collisions at RHIC (200 GeV per nucleon) and LHC (6300 GeV per nucleon) energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: 250 MeV for RHIC, 500 MeV for LHC.
- Jet energies studied: 10, 20 GeV (RHIC); 40, 100 GeV (LHC).
- Quark–quark cross section: σ_qq ≈ 4 mb; averaged plasma cross section σ_pl ≈ 12 mb.
- Average transverse momentum per scattering: ⟨k_T⟩ = 0.36 GeV; average hadron transverse momentum ⟨h_T⟩ = 0.36 GeV.
- Integration of energy–energy correlation (φ from π/6 to 5π/6) for T_i = 250 MeV, Q = 10 GeV: QCD only = 1.29, QCD+hadronization = 1.54, QCD+hadronization+plasma = 2.27.
- Integration of asymmetric correlation (φ from π/6 to π/2) for T_i = 500 MeV, Q = 40 GeV: QCD only = 0.54, QCD+hadronization = 0.54, QCD+hadronization+plasma = 0.80.
- Enhancement factor from plasma: 1.2 to 1.6 for the integrated correlations.

## Context

This work extends the energy–energy correlation technique introduced by Basham, Brown, Ellis and Love (1978) for e+e− collisions and adapted by Ali, Pietarinen and Stirling (1984) for hadronic collisions, applying it to heavy ion collisions to probe the quark gluon plasma. It builds on the Bjorken hydrodynamical model (1983) for longitudinal expansion and on earlier studies of jet quenching and multiple scattering in plasma by Appel (1986) and Blaizot and McLerran (1987). The results enable experimental searches for plasma signatures using high-transverse-energy jets at RHIC and LHC, with the asymmetric correlation A(φ) proposed as a clean observable that cancels hadronization effects.
