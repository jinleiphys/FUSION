# Solution to new sign problems with Hamiltonian Lattice Fermions

> We present a solution to the sign problem in a class of particle-hole symmetric Hamiltonian lattice fermion models on bipartite lattices using the idea of fermion bags. The solution remains valid when the particle-hole symmetry is broken through a staggered chemical potential term. This solution allows, for the first time, simulations of some massless four-fermion models with minimal fermion doubling and with an odd number of fermion flavors using ultra-local actions. One can thus study a variety of quantum phase transitions that have remained unexplored so far due to sign problems.

# Solution to new sign problems with Hamiltonian Lattice Fermions

## Key claim
The fermion bag technique solves the sign problem for a class of particle-hole symmetric Hamiltonian lattice fermion models on bipartite lattices, including cases where particle-hole symmetry is broken by a staggered chemical potential, but only when the free Hamiltonian satisfies a specific symmetry property (M^T = -D M D) and interaction vertices respect a staggered reference configuration.

## Method
The authors use the continuous-time Hamiltonian formalism with staggered fermions on a bipartite lattice, expanding the partition function as a series of bond interactions. They apply the fermion bag technique by treating the diagonal elements of the fermion matrix (which depend on the particle-hole operator labels s_q) as fermion bags, summing over all s_q configurations analytically to obtain a determinant of the off-diagonal matrix A([b,t]). They then prove that this determinant multiplied by (-1)^k is positive because A times a diagonal matrix of staggered signs is real anti-symmetric.

## Key numbers
- On an 8x8 square lattice at β = 10 with matrices of size 250x250, a sample of 10,000 randomly generated configurations yielded 5004 positive determinants and 4996 negative determinants, demonstrating the severity of the sign problem before the solution.
- The sum over s_q configurations yields a factor of 4^k, where k is the number of interaction bonds.
- The final partition function is expressed as a sum over positive terms for V > 0, with the weight V^k Det(A([b,t]) D̃).

## Context
This work extends the fermion bag technique developed by Chandrasekharan (2013) to Hamiltonian lattice fermions, solving sign problems that remained unsolvable with traditional determinant methods (Hirsch, 1983) and the meron cluster approach (Chandrasekharan, 1999), which could only handle the t-V model for V ≥ 2t. The solution enables quantum Monte Carlo studies of quantum phase transitions in massless four-fermion models with minimal fermion doubling and odd numbers of fermion flavors, including graphene-inspired systems on honeycomb lattices (Wang, 2014) and three-flavor nuclear-inspired models (Huffman, 2013).
