# Directed flow from parton spin-orbit coupling in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

> We point out a novel mechanism to generate $\cos ϕ$ two-particle azimuthal correlation (`directed flow') in unpolarized proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions in the forward rapidity region of the projectile proton. This is a direct consequence of the recently discovered strong spin-orbit coupling in gluons at small-$x$. The observable simultaneously serves as a unique probe into the double helicity parton distribution functions of the proton.

# Directed flow from parton spin-orbit coupling in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

## Key claim
The authors propose that a $\cos\phi$ two-particle azimuthal correlation (directed flow $v_1$) in unpolarized proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at forward rapidity arises from the recently discovered strong spin-orbit coupling in gluons at small-$x$, rather than from hydrodynamic evolution or odderon exchange. The observable simultaneously probes the double helicity parton distribution functions of the projectile proton, which are nonvanishing even when the proton is unpolarized.

## Method
The authors compute double parton scattering cross sections in the hybrid factorization approach appropriate for forward rapidity kinematics, where collinear quarks from the projectile scatter off small-$x$ gluons from the target. They square the polarized term in the quark-gluon scattering amplitude (involving $\bar{q}\gamma^+\gamma_5 q$) to obtain a contribution proportional to the product of the quark double helicity PDF $F_{\Delta q\Delta q}$ and the gluon helicity generalized transverse momentum dependent distribution $\tilde{f}_g$, which encodes the gluon spin-orbit correlation $C_g$. Using the relation $C_g(x) \approx -g(x)$ valid at small-$x$, they express the cross section in terms of the dipole $S$-matrix and evaluate $v_1$ numerically with Gaussian models for double PDFs and two models (GBW and MV$^e$) for the dipole $S$-matrix.

## Key numbers
- The spin-orbit correlation at small-$x$ satisfies $C_g(x) \approx -g(x)$, meaning gluon helicity and orbital angular momentum are maximally anti-aligned.
- For $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13.6$ TeV with $y_{\rm ref}=2$ and $|{\bm P}_{\rm ref}|=4$ GeV, $|v_1|$ at $|{\bm P}|\sim 1$ GeV is at the percent level under the maximal scenario $F_{\Delta u\Delta u}/F_{uu}=-1$.
- The analytic estimate neglecting fragmentation gives $v_1 = 0.098/|{\bm P}| \times {\rm sgn}(F_{\Delta q\Delta q}) \sqrt{|F_{\Delta q\Delta q}(x,x_{\rm ref})|/F_{qq}(x,x_{\rm ref})}$ for $A=1$, with $|{\bm P}|$ in GeV.
- The atomic number dependence scales as $v_1 \sim A^{-1/3}$ from the target radius $R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3}$ fm.
- The Gaussian width for double PDFs is taken as $\sigma = 0.26$ GeV following Chang (2012).

## Context
This work extends the study of spin-orbit correlations in QCD, previously formulated for partons inside a nucleon by Lorce (2011), Kanazawa (2014), and Mukherjee (2014), and recently shown to be maximal at small-$x$ by Bhattacharya (2024) and Hatta (2024). It contrasts with the odderon-exchange mechanism for $v_1$ proposed by Boer (2018), which is expected to be significantly smaller. The observable enables direct access to the double helicity PDF $F_{\Delta q\Delta q}$, which has received limited phenomenological attention compared to the double unpolarized PDF $F_{qq}$ studied by Gaunt (2009) and Diehl (2011). The authors suggest that LHCb data at forward rapidity $2<|y|<5$ could be reanalyzed to extract $v_1$ and its transverse momentum dependence.
