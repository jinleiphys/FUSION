# Magnetic Color Flavor Locking Phase in High Density QCD

> We investigate the effects of an external magnetic field in the gap structure of a color superconductor with three massless quark flavors. Using an effective theory with four-fermion interactions, inspired by one-gluon exchange, we show that the long-range component $\widetilde{B}$ of the external magnetic field that penetrates the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase modifies its gap structure, producing a new phase of lower symmetry. A main outcome of our study is that the $\widetilde{B}$ field tends to strengthen the gaps formed by $\widetilde{Q}$-charged and $\widetilde{Q}$-neutral quarks that coupled among themselves through tree-level vertices. These gaps are enhanced by the field-dependent density of states of the $\widetilde{Q}$-charged quarks on the Fermi surface. Our considerations are relevant for the study of highly magnetized compact stars.

## Key claim

An external magnetic field that penetrates the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of dense three-flavor quark matter modifies the gap structure, producing a new "magnetic CFL" (MCFL) phase of lower symmetry; the field strengthens gaps formed by charged and certain neutral quarks that couple through tree-level vertices, but the analytic solution is only valid for strong fields where the lowest Landau level dominates.

## Method

The authors used a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) four-fermion interaction abstracted from one-gluon exchange, with a coupling constant \(g\) and ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\), to compute the gap equations in coordinate space. They employed Ritus' method to diagonalize the charged fermion propagators in a uniform background magnetic field \(\widetilde{B}\), and solved the coupled gap equations analytically in the strong-field regime \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B} \gtrsim \mu^2/2\) where only the lowest Landau level contributes.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mu \sim 350-400\) MeV, the lower bound of the strong-field regime is \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B} \sim (0.8 - 1.1) \cdot 10^{18}\) G.
- The antisymmetric gap of the charged-quark condensate is \(\Delta^B_A \sim 2 \sqrt{\delta \mu} \, \exp{\Big( - \frac{3 \Lambda^2 \pi^2} {g^2 \left(\mu^2 + \widetilde{e} \widetilde{B} \right)} \Big) }\), compared to the CFL antisymmetric gap \(\Delta^{\rm CFL}_A \sim 2 \sqrt{\delta \mu} \, \exp{\Big( -\frac{3 \Lambda^2 \pi^2} {2 g^2 \mu^2} \Big) }\).
- For NJL parameter \(x \sim 0.3\) and \(y = \widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}/\mu^2 = 3/2\), \(\Delta_A \sim 0.2 \, \Delta^B_A\); for \(x \sim 1\), \(\Delta_A \sim 0.5 \, \Delta^B_A\).
- The density of states around the Fermi surface for a charged quark in each Landau level is \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}/2 \pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity in high-density QCD, specifically the CFL phase (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1999), by incorporating the effect of a penetrating rotated magnetic field \(\widetilde{B}\) that previous analyses (e.g., Iida 1999, and others in the "magnetic-orig" references) had disregarded. The MCFL phase has a smaller vector symmetry than CFL, with 13 broken generators versus the CFL pattern, and its low-energy physics differs: the MCFL superconductor is transparent and behaves as an anisotropic dielectric at zero temperature, unlike the isotropic dielectric behavior of CFL (Litim and Manuel 2001; Manuel 2001). The results are relevant for highly magnetized compact stars, and the authors note that the critical field for destroying CFL pairing, previously estimated at \(\sim 10^{20}\) G, is expected to be even larger in the MCFL phase.
