# Formal comparison of SUSY in the nuclear U(6/2) model and in quantum field theory

> A nuclear physics example of the U(6/2) supersymmetry group is considered. It is shown that this group contains a supersymmetric subgroup with a structure similar to the SUSY model of the quantum field theory (QFT). A comparison of two models help to clarify the relation between the supersymmetry schemes of QFT and of nuclear physics. Using this similarity a relation between the numbers of the bosonic and fermionic states similar to the fundamental relation in QFT is obtained. For those supermultiplets with at least two fermions the number of the bosonic and fermionic states are equal as in QFT.

## Key claim

The U(6/2) supersymmetry group in nuclear physics contains a U(1/2) graded subalgebra whose anticommutation relations are structurally similar to those of the supercharges in quantum field theory, but with a difference in the right-hand side of the anticommutator that leads to a modified relation between bosonic and fermionic state counts: for supermultiplets with at least two s-bosons the numbers are equal as in QFT, but for supermultiplets with exactly one s-boson the number of fermionic substates is twice the number of bosonic substates.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian for the U(6/2) model in the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit, containing s-bosons, d-bosons, and fermions occupying a j=1/2 single-particle state. They identified the supercharge operators P_{1/2m} = a⁺_{1/2m} s and their Hermitian conjugates, which commute with the Hamiltonian, and derived the full graded algebra (anticommutators and commutators) of these operators with the number operators N_s, N_F, and the fermion spin operator S_{1η}. They then compared these algebraic relations term-by-term with the corresponding relations of the supercharges Q^L_α in QFT, and used a trace over (-1)^{N_F} times the anticommutator to derive a counting relation for bosonic and fermionic magnetic substates in each supermultiplet.

## Key numbers

- For supermultiplets with N_s + N_F ≥ 2: the number of bosonic magnetic substates equals the number of fermionic magnetic substates (n_B = n_F).
- For supermultiplets with N_s + N_F = 1: the number of fermionic magnetic substates equals twice the number of bosonic magnetic substates (n_F = 2 n_B).
- The example supermultiplet contains the nuclei ¹⁰²Ru (N_s+N_d=7, N_F=0), ¹⁰³Rh (N_s+N_d=6, N_F=1), and ¹⁰⁴Pd (N_s+N_d=5, N_F=2); a candidate for the lowest ¹⁰⁴Pd state in the supermultiplet is the 0⁺ state at 1793 keV seen in β⁻ decay of ¹⁰⁴Rh with log ft = 5.5.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical supersymmetry program initiated by Iachello (summarized in Iachello 1988) by explicitly constructing the supercharge operators that transform bosons into fermions and vice versa, which had been absent in most previous nuclear-structure subgroup chains. It contrasts with the approach of Balantekin (1983) and Morrison (1986), where the supergroup structure differed from that of QFT. The formal correspondence established here enables nuclear-structure supersymmetry to be compared directly with the SUSY models of Wess (1974), Fayet (1977), and Soff (1998), and provides a nuclear-physics testing ground for the fundamental relation n_B = n_F that is central to QFT supersymmetry.
