# Momentum distribution of fragments in heavy ion reactions: Dependence on the stochastic collision process

> Momentum distribution of fragments produced in 12C+12C reaction at 28.7 MeV/nucleon is analyzed with antisymmetrized version of molecular dynamics (AMD). Calculations are made for several cases of stochastic collisions and the projectile fragmentation peak in momentum distribution is reproduced by the incorporation of many-body nature in stochastic collision process. Furthermore the value of the cross section is found to be reflected in the low momentum part of fragments such as alpha particles and 9Be.

## Key claim

The projectile fragmentation peak in the momentum distribution of fragments from the 12C+12C reaction at 28.7 MeV/nucleon is reproduced by the antisymmetrized version of molecular dynamics (AMD) only when many-body stochastic collisions (such as nucleon-alpha collisions) are incorporated in addition to the usual two-nucleon collisions; the calculation with only two-nucleon collisions fails to produce this peak at this relatively low incident energy.

## Method

AMD simulations were performed for the 12C+12C reaction at 28.7 MeV/nucleon, using a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets with a width parameter of 0.16 fm^-2 and the Volkov No.1 effective interaction. Three types of stochastic collision processes were compared: (a) only two-nucleon collisions with no medium-effect reduction (C=0), (b) both two-nucleon and nucleon-alpha collisions with medium-effect parameter C=1, and (c) both collision types with C=2. After the AMD calculation (truncated at about 200 fm/c), statistical cascade decay of excited fragments was calculated using a code similar to CASCADE.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 28.7 MeV/nucleon for the 12C+12C reaction.
- Width parameter of Gaussian wave packets: nu = 0.16 fm^-2.
- Two-nucleon collision cross section parametrization: sigma_NN = 100 mb / (1 + E/200 MeV + C * min((rho/rho_0)^2, 1)), where E is the laboratory energy of the two-nucleon scattering and C controls medium reduction.
- Nucleon-alpha total cross section parametrization: sigma_Nalpha,tot = 571 mb / (1 + E/200 MeV + C * min((rho/rho_0)^2, 1)).
- Nucleon-alpha inelastic cross section parametrization: sigma_Nalpha,inel = max(120 - 162 * exp(-(E - 20 MeV)/10 MeV), 0) mb.
- Impact parameter range for peripheral collisions: b > 5 fm; for central collisions: b <= 5 fm.
- Critical distance for alpha cluster identification: |Delta W| = 0.25.
- Deflection angle of carbon fragments from mean field effect: about -45 degrees for central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD studies (Ono 1992a, 1992b) which reproduced mass distributions of fragments, by now analyzing momentum distributions which are more sensitive to reaction mechanisms. It contrasts with the abrasion-ablation model, which assumes the projectile fragmentation mechanism, and with statistical models that cannot describe collective momentum flow. The paper shows that momentum distributions of fragments like alpha particles and 9Be can serve as indicators of the stochastic collision cross section, and that the collective flow of fragments reflects the effective interaction, suggesting future work with other interactions such as the Gogny force (Decharge and Gogny 1980).
