# Momentum dependent flow correlations in deformed nuclei at collision energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Flow fluctuations in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision can be probed by studying the momentum dependent correlations or the factorization-breaking coefficients between flow harmonics in separate kinematic bins (transverse momentum or pseudorapidity). We study such factorization-breaking coefficients for collisions of deformed U+U nuclei to see the effect of the nuclear deformation on momentum dependent coefficients. We also study momentum dependent mixed-flow correlations for the isobar collision system : Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr, which have the same mass number but different nuclear structure, thus providing the ideal scenario to study nuclear deformation effect on such observables. We use the TRENTO + MUSIC model for simulations and event-by-event analysis of those observables. We find that these momentum dependent correlation coefficients are not only excellent candidates to probe the fluctuation in heavy-ion collision, but also show significant sensitivity to the nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

The momentum-dependent factorization-breaking coefficients and nonlinear mixed-flow correlations show significant sensitivity to nuclear deformation in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the effect is much more pronounced for the larger uranium system than for the smaller isobar systems, and for the isobars the triangular-flow factorization-breaking coefficients show a small effect from octupole deformation while the elliptic-flow coefficients show almost no difference between Ru and Zr despite their different deformed structures.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the TRENTO initial-state model coupled with 2+1-dimensional MUSIC viscous hydrodynamic evolution (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12) for U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV and for isobar Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They calculated factorization-breaking coefficients for flow vector squares, flow magnitude squares, and flow angle decorrelations as functions of transverse momentum, as well as momentum-dependent nonlinear mixed-flow correlations V₂²-V₄(p) and V₂V₃-V₅(p), for central (0-5%) and ultra-central (0-1%) collisions using Woods-Saxon density distributions with deformation parameters from Table I.

## Key numbers

- U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV; 7000 minimum-bias events simulated
- Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV; 20000 minimum-bias events each
- Woods-Saxon parameters for ²³⁸U: R₀ = 6.86 fm, a₀ = 0.42 fm, β₂ = 0.265, β₃ = 0
- Woods-Saxon parameters for ⁹⁶Ru: R₀ = 5.09 fm, a₀ = 0.46 fm, β₂ = 0.162, β₃ = 0
- Woods-Saxon parameters for ⁹⁶Zr: R₀ = 5.02 fm, a₀ = 0.52 fm, β₂ = 0.06, β₃ = 0.20
- Ratio of quadrupole deformation parameters squared: β²₂,U / β²₂,Ru = 2.676
- Harmonic flow coefficients calculated for charged particles with transverse momenta between 0.2 and 3.0 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature: 135 MeV; chemical freeze-out temperature: 155 MeV
- Flow magnitude decorrelation is approximately one half of the flow vector decorrelation: [1 - r_{n;2}(p)] ≈ 2 [1 - r_n^{v_n²}(p)]

## Context

This work extends previous studies of factorization-breaking coefficients for spherical Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies (Bozek 2021, Bozek 2022) to deformed nuclei at RHIC energies, and builds on theoretical studies of nuclear deformation effects on flow harmonics (Giacalone 2018, Jia 2021, Zhang 2021) and on nonlinear correlations between global flow vectors for isobar collisions (Jia 2022). The results enable experimental tests at RHIC using U+U and isobar collision data to extract nuclear structure information, particularly for the octupole deformation of ⁹⁶Zr, and demonstrate that momentum-dependent correlations provide a complementary probe to global flow measurements.
