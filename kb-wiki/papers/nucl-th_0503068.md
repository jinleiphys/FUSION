# The Coester Line in Relativistic Mean Field Nuclear Matter

> The Walecka model contains essentially two parameters that are associated with the Lorentz scalar (S) and vector (V) interactions. These parameters are related to a two-body interaction consisting of S and V, imposing the condition that the two-body binding energy is fixed. We have obtained a set of different values for the nuclear matter binding energies at equilibrium densities. We investigated the existence of a linear correlation between $B_N$ and $ρ_0$, claimed to be universal for nonrelativistic systems and usually known as the Coester line, and found an approximate linear correlation only if $V?S$ remains constant. It is shown that the relativistic content of the model, which is related to the strength of $V?S$, is responsible for the shift of the Coester line to the empirical region of nuclear matter saturation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the Coester line, a linear correlation between nuclear matter binding energy and saturation density previously claimed to be universal for nonrelativistic systems, appears in the relativistic Walecka model only approximately and only when the difference between the vector and scalar potentials (V minus S) remains constant. The relativistic content of the model, related to the strength of V minus S, is responsible for shifting the Coester line toward the empirical region of nuclear matter saturation.

## Method

The authors model the two-nucleon system using a Dirac equation with constant scalar (S) and vector (V) square-well potentials of radius R_o, fixing the two-body binding energy B_2 at 2 MeV. They then use the resulting S and V values as inputs to the linear Walecka mean-field model for infinite nuclear matter, solving for the saturation density ρ_o and binding energy per nucleon B_N. By varying R_o from 1.4 fm to 2.1 fm and allowing different combinations of S and V, they generate sets of (B_N, ρ_o) pairs and examine the correlation between them.

## Key numbers

- Two-body binding energy B_2 is fixed at 2 MeV for all calculations.
- The radius R_o of the square-well potential is varied from 1.4 fm to 2.1 fm.
- For the reference nuclear matter saturation point, the experimental binding energy per nucleon B_N is taken as 16 MeV and the saturation density ρ_o as 0.15 fm^{-3}.
- The Fermi momentum k_f at saturation is taken as 1.3 fm^{-1} for the reference calculation.
- The bare nucleon mass M is 939 MeV.
- The effective nucleon mass m* required to reproduce empirical spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei lies between 0.58 and 0.64 (dimensionless, in units of M).
- The quantity Δ = V - S is constrained to lie in a narrow band (from 643 MeV to 715 MeV) to reproduce good spin-orbit splitting results in finite nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of Coester (1970) that first identified the linear correlation between binding energy and saturation density, which failed to reach the empirical saturation point. The authors connect their simple two-body model to the Walecka mean-field approach and find that the relativistic Coester line shifts toward the empirical region, consistent with recent relativistic Brueckner calculations using Bonn potentials (GFF, 2004). The paper enables future work using the non-linear Walecka model of Boguta and Bodmer (1977) to obtain more realistic values for incompressibility and effective mass while preserving the Coester line correlation.
