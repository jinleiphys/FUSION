# Hyperon Matter and Black Hole Formation in Failed Supernovae

> We investigate the emergence of hyperons in black-hole-forming failed supernovae, which are caused by the dynamical collapse of nonrotating massive stars. We perform neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations in general relativity adopting realistic hyperonic equation-of-state (EOS). Attractive and repulsive cases are examined for the potential of Σ hyperons. Since hyperons soften the EOS, they shorten the time interval from the bounce to black hole formation, which corresponds to the duration of neutrino emission. This effect is larger for the attractive case than the repulsive case because Σ hyperons appear more easily. In addition, we investigate the impacts of pions to find that they also promotes the recollapse towards the black hole formation.

## Key claim

Hyperons and pions soften the equation of state (EOS) and shorten the time interval from core bounce to black hole formation in failed supernovae, with the effect being larger for an attractive Σ hyperon potential than a repulsive one because Σ⁻ hyperons appear more easily; the authors note that the hyperonic EOS models they adopt cannot satisfy the observed neutron star mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al. 2010), but argue the systematic differences they find should hold for future improved EOS models.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general relativistic neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations using a Boltzmann solver for neutrino transport, adopting a 40 M⊙ progenitor model from Woosley (1995). They used the Ishizuka et al. (2008) hyperonic EOS tables based on an SU_f(3) extended relativistic mean field model, examining both attractive (U_Σ = −30 MeV) and repulsive (U_Σ = +30 MeV) Σ-nucleon potentials, with and without thermal pions, and identified black hole formation by finding the apparent horizon.

## Key numbers

- Time interval from bounce to black hole formation: 682 ms (repulsive, no pions), 653 ms (repulsive with pions), 587 ms (attractive, no pions), 575 ms (attractive with pions), 1345 ms (nucleonic without pions), 1145 ms (nucleonic with pions)
- Central density at bounce: 3.2 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³, which did not differ significantly among models
- Maximum masses of neutron stars: 2.2 M⊙ (nucleonic without pions), 2.0 M⊙ (nucleonic with pions), approximately 1.6 M⊙ for all hyperonic models (repulsive and attractive, with and without pions)
- Initial location of apparent horizon: 1.1–1.2 M⊙ in baryon mass coordinate, independent of EOS
- Pion rest mass used in the model: m_π⁻ = 135 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier simulations of failed supernovae by Sumiyoshi et al. (2009) using the repulsive Σ potential case, and by Fischer et al. (2009) and O'Connor & Ott (2010) who studied black hole formation thresholds with simplified neutrino transport. The authors build on the hyperonic EOS tables of Ishizuka et al. (2008) and the Shen et al. (1998a, 1998b) EOS framework, and their results enable future statistical analyses of neutrino signals from Galactic failed supernovae to diagnose the emergence of hyperonic matter, as proposed by Suwa et al. (2009).
