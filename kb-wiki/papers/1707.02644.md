# Neutron stars in the large-$N_{c}$ limit

> We study the phase transition from dense baryonic matter to dense quark matter within the large-$N_{c}$ limit. By using simple constant speed of sound equations of state for the two phases, we derive the scaling with $N_{c}$ of the critical quark chemical potential $μ_{c}^{\mathrm{crit}}$ for this phase transition. While quark matter is strongly suppressed at large $N_{c}$, the phase transition at a large but finite density could nevertheless be important to determine the maximum mass of compact stars. In particular, in the range $3\leq N_{c}\lesssim5.5$ the quark phase would take place in compact stars and would lead to the formation of an unstable branch of hybrid stars. As a consequence, the maximum mass is restricted to the range $2.1M_{\odot}<M_{max}<3M_{\odot}$. For larger value of $N_{c}$, the phase transition would occur at densities too high to be reached in the core of compact stars. However, the very requirement that it occurs (although at a very large density) translates into interesting constraints on the stiffness of the baryonic phase: its speed of sound must exceed $\sqrt{1/3}$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that requiring a first-order phase transition from baryonic matter to deconfined quark matter at some high density (even if that density is too high to be reached in compact stars) imposes the condition that the speed of sound in the baryonic phase must exceed \(\sqrt{1/3}\), which in turn allows neutron star masses above \(2M_{\odot}\). The actual occurrence of the quark phase inside compact stars is restricted to a narrow range of the number of colors, \(3 \leq N_c \lesssim 5.5\).

## Method

The authors model the quark phase as a free gas of massless quarks with a nonperturbative vacuum pressure (bag constant) that scales as \(N_c^2\), and the baryonic phase at high density using a constant speed-of-sound equation of state parametrized as \(p_b \propto \mu_q^\alpha\), where \(\alpha\) is related to the speed of sound by \(v_b = 1/\sqrt{\alpha-1}\). They match the two phases via a Maxwell construction, derive the scaling of the critical quark chemical potential \(\mu_c^{\mathrm{crit}}\) with \(N_c\), and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations for different values of \(\alpha\) and \(N_c\).

## Key numbers

*   For \(N_c = 3\), the maximum mass of neutron stars is restricted to the range \(2.1 M_{\odot} < M_{\mathrm{max}} < 3 M_{\odot}\) when the quark phase transition occurs.
*   For \(N_c = 3\) and \(\alpha = 3.5\), the phase transition would occur within stars with masses above \(1.9 M_{\odot}\); for \(\alpha = 2\), it occurs for masses above \(2.1 M_{\odot}\).
*   For \(\alpha = 2\), the phase transition occurs at the center of the maximum mass configuration for \(N_c \sim 5.5\); for \(\alpha = 3.5\), this happens for \(N_c \sim 3.4\).
*   For \(N_c = 4\) and the smallest vacuum pressure constant considered (\(\sqrt{3}B^{1/4} \sim 165\) MeV), the phase transition would occur for masses larger than about \(2.4 M_{\odot}\).

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of the QCD phase diagram initiated by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), who introduced the concept of a quarkyonic phase, and applies it to the astrophysical context of neutron stars. It builds on the constant-speed-of-sound approach used by Bedaque and Steiner (2014) to study the maximum mass of neutron stars, and it complements studies of hyperon and delta resonance formation in dense matter (e.g., Weissenborn et al. 2011, Drago et al. 2014). The results enable future tests of quark deconfinement in compact stars through gravitational wave observations of binary neutron star mergers, as the threshold mass for prompt collapse depends on the maximum mass.
