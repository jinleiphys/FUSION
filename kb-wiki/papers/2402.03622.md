# Charmed hadron chemistry and flow in heavy and light ion collisions at the LHC

> We study the charmed meson and baryon production and elliptic flow in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC energies. The space-time evolution of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in these energetic collisions is obtained via the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics model, the heavy quark dynamics inside the QGP is simulated using an improved Langevin model that incorporates both elastic and inelastic parton energy loss processes, and the heavy quark hadronization is simulated utilizing a comprehensive coalescence-fragmentation model. Using our combined approach, we first calculate charmed hadron ratios, $Λ_c/D^0$ and $D_s/D^0$, as well as their elliptic flow ($v_2$) as a function of transverse momentum ($p_T$) for different centralities in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$~TeV. Due to strangeness enhancement and parton coalescence effects, $D_s/D^0$ and $Λ_c/D^0$ ratios increase from peripheral to central collisions, and such centrality dependence for $Λ_c/D^0$ is stronger than $D_s/D^0$. We further predict the $p_T$ and centrality dependences of charmed hadron chemistry and $v_2$ in smaller Xe+Xe, Ar+Ar and O+O collisions at the LHC energies. Strong centrality and system size dependences for $Λ_c/D^0$ and $D_s/D^0$ ratios are observed across four collision systems. As for charmed hadron flow, both system size and collision geometry are important to understand the centrality dependence of $v_2$ in different collision systems. Our study provides a significant reference for studying heavy quark evolution and hadronizaiton in large and small systems in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of charmed hadron ratios (Λc/D0 and Ds/D0) and elliptic flow (v2) across four collision systems (Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, Ar+Ar, O+O) is predicted to serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, with the caveat that system size effects become significant only when comparing heavy (Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe) to light (Ar+Ar, O+O) systems, while between Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe the system size plays a minor role.

## Method

The space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma was simulated using the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics model with smooth initial conditions averaged over 5000 TRENTo events per centrality class. Heavy quark dynamics inside the QGP was modeled using an improved Langevin approach incorporating both elastic scattering and medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung via the higher-twist energy loss formalism, with a single free parameter Ds(2πT)=4. Heavy quark hadronization was simulated using a hybrid coalescence-fragmentation model that includes both s-wave and p-wave hadron states, with charm quark mass mc=1.8 GeV and light quark thermal masses mu=md=0.3 GeV and ms=0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Ds/D0 and Λc/D0 ratios increase from peripheral to central collisions in Pb+Pb at √sNN=5.02 TeV, with stronger centrality dependence for Λc/D0 than Ds/D0
- Ds(2πT)=4 used as the dimensionless spatial diffusion coefficient for all four collision systems
- Switching temperature for hydrodynamics to hadrons: Tsw=137 MeV
- Transition temperature for hadronization: Tc=160 MeV
- Specific shear viscosity: ηv/s=0.16
- Charm quark mass in coalescence model: mc=1.8 GeV; light quark thermal masses: mu=md=0.3 GeV, ms=0.4 GeV
- Woods-Saxon parameters: PbPb R0=6.62 fm, d=0.546 fm; XeXe R0=5.4 fm, d=0.59 fm, β2=0.18; ArAr R0=3.53 fm, d=0.542 fm; OO R0=2.608 fm, d=0.513 fm, w=-0.051
- Initial time for hydrodynamics: τ0=0.6 fm for all systems
- Normalization factors K: PbPb=154, XeXe=155, ArAr=160, OO=180

## Context

This work extends previous heavy flavor studies in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (Li 2020, Li 2021) by systematically scanning collision systems of different sizes, including predictions for future O+O runs proposed at the LHC. The approach combines the CLVisc hydrodynamics model (Pang 2009, Pang 2018, Wu 2021) with an improved Langevin model (Cao 2013, Cao 2015, Li 2019) and a coalescence-fragmentation hadronization model (Cao 2019). The results provide predictions for charmed hadron chemistry and flow that can be tested against ALICE data on Ds/D0 and Λc/D0 ratios (Vermunt 2019, ALICE 2021) and serve as evidence for QGP formation in small systems if the predicted strong centrality dependence is observed in future O+O collisions.
