# $α$-nucleus optical potentials from chiral effective field theory $NN$ interactions

> We present a determination of optical potentials for $^4$He-target collisions using the double-folding method. We use chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order combined with state-of-the-art nucleonic densities. The imaginary part of the optical potential is obtained from the real double-folding interaction either through a proportionality constant or applying Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations. With these potentials, we compute the elastic scattering of $^4$He off various targets, from $^4$He to $^{120}$Sn. We study the sensitivity of our predicted cross sections to the choice of nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear densities. Very good agreement is obtained with existing elastic-scattering data for $^4$He energies between 100 and 400 MeV.

# α-nucleus optical potentials from chiral effective field theory NN interactions

## Key claim

The double-folding method using chiral EFT NN interactions at N²LO combined with Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations can produce α-nucleus optical potentials that reproduce elastic-scattering data without any free parameters, but only for energies between 100 and 400 MeV; the approach works best for lighter targets and shows limitations for heavy targets like ¹²⁰Sn where additional absorption from excited states may be needed.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential is obtained by double-folding local chiral EFT NN potentials at N²LO (with coordinate-space cutoffs R₀ = 1.2 and 1.6 fm) over nucleonic densities from electron-scattering data, quantum Monte Carlo calculations, or relativistic mean-field models. The imaginary part is constructed either by assuming proportionality to the real potential (W = N_W V_F with N_W = 0.6–0.8) or by applying Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations to the energy-dependent exchange term of the real potential. Elastic-scattering cross sections are then computed for ⁴He projectiles on targets from ⁴He to ¹²⁰Sn and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering of ⁴He-⁴He computed at laboratory energies 198.8 and 280 MeV, compared to data from Woo85, Cow94, and Rao00.
- For ⁴He-⁴⁰Ca at E_α = 240 MeV, results using SG_p density for ⁴He show best agreement with data from Youn97.
- For ⁴He-¹²C and ⁴He-¹⁶O at E_α = 104 and 240 MeV, results are compared to data from Haus69 and John03.
- For ⁴He-⁴⁸Ca at E_α = 104 and 240 MeV, results use RMF densities from Chen15 and data from Lui11.
- For ⁴He-¹²⁰Sn at E_α = 386 MeV, the QMC density for ⁴He gives the best overall description of data from Li10.
- The NN interaction cutoffs used are R₀ = 1.2 and 1.6 fm.
- The proportionality constant for the simple imaginary potential is N_W = 0.6–0.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous double-folding studies of ¹²C-¹²C, ¹⁶O-¹⁶O, and ¹²C-¹⁶O collisions (Durant 2017, 2020) to α-particle projectiles, which are important in nuclear astrophysics. It builds on chiral EFT NN interactions developed by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) and uses nucleonic densities from electron scattering (De Vries 1987), quantum Monte Carlo (Lynn 2017), and relativistic mean-field (Chen 2015) calculations. The Kramers-Kronig approach for the imaginary part was previously applied to symmetric systems (Durant 2020); this work extends it to asymmetric projectile-target combinations. The results enable parameter-free predictions of α-nucleus scattering that can be tested against phenomenological optical potentials (Woo85, Rao00) and global optical potentials (Nolte 1987). Future improvements include going beyond leading-order density matrix expansion, including three-nucleon forces, and accounting for coupling to excited states at lower energies.
