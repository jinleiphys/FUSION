# Flow decorrelation in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$=27 and 200 GeV with 3D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics

> We present the first calculation of longitudinal decorrelation of anisotropic flow at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies using event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic model (vHLLE), with two different initial states (GLISSANDO2 and UrQMD) and hadronic cascade. We investigate the origin of the observed decorrelation by checking separately flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelation and by calculating decorrelation in the initial state eccentricity.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first hydrodynamic-model calculation of longitudinal flow decorrelation at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, showing that the GLISSANDO2 initial state reproduces preliminary STAR data within uncertainties while the UrQMD initial state overestimates the decorrelation, and that the decorrelation originates primarily from the initial-state eccentricity rather than from hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method

The authors used a 3D event-by-event hybrid model combining two initial-state generators (GLISSANDO2 Monte Carlo Glauber and UrQMD string-based model) with the vHLLE viscous hydrodynamic code, followed by Monte Carlo hadron sampling via the Cooper-Frye formula and final-state rescatterings with the UrQMD cascade. They computed the factorization ratio r_n for elliptic flow (n=2) as a function of pseudorapidity, and separately decomposed it into contributions from flow magnitude decorrelation (r_n^v) and flow angle decorrelation (r_n^Psi). They also calculated an analogous factorization ratio for initial-state spatial eccentricities to trace the origin of the decorrelation.

## Key numbers

- Factorization ratio r_2 for 10-40% Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=27 GeV: calculations with GLISSANDO initial state reproduce STAR preliminary data within uncertainties; UrQMD initial state overestimates the decorrelation.
- Factorization ratio r_2 for 10-40% Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: same qualitative behavior as at 27 GeV, with GLISSANDO matching data and UrQMD overestimating.
- Flow angle decorrelation (r_2^Psi) surpasses flow magnitude decorrelation (r_2^v) at sqrt(s_NN)=27 GeV, independent of initial-state model.
- Initial-state eccentricity decorrelation (r_2^epsilon) agrees quantitatively with the final-state flow decorrelation (r_2) at both sqrt(s_NN)=27 and 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of longitudinal flow decorrelation performed at LHC energies by CMS (2015) and ATLAS (2017, 2020) to the lower RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, where only preliminary STAR results existed (Nie 2019, 2020). The finding that flow angle decorrelation dominates over magnitude decorrelation agrees with the same conclusion at LHC energies from Bozek (2017). The results enable systematic comparison of initial-state models (Glauber vs. string-based) and demonstrate that the decorrelation pattern is imprinted already in the initial-state eccentricity, constraining future models of the initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions.
