# Spectral Content of Isoscalar Nucleon Form Factors

> The nucleon strange vector and isoscalar electromagnetic form factors are studied using a spectral decomposition. The K\bar{K} contribution to the electric and magnetic radii as well as the magnetic moment is evaluated to all orders in the strong interaction using an analytic continuation of experimental KN scattering amplitudes and bounds from unitarity. The relationship between non-resonant and resonant K\bar{K} contributions to the form factors is demonstrated, and values for the vector and tensor ϕN\bar{N} couplings are derived. The K\bar{K} spectral functions are used to evaluate the credibility of model calculations for the strange quark vector current form factors.

## Key claim

The paper uses dispersion relations to evaluate the $K\bar{K}$ contribution to nucleon strange and isoscalar electromagnetic form factors to all orders in the strong interaction, demonstrating that the scale of this contribution depends critically on effects beyond second order in the strong hadronic coupling $g$, contrary to the truncation used in many meson cloud calculations.

## Method

The authors perform a spectral decomposition of the nucleon strange vector and isoscalar electromagnetic form factors, focusing on the $K\bar{K}$ intermediate state. They use analytic continuation of experimental $KN$ scattering amplitudes via backward dispersion relations to obtain the $K\bar{K} \to N\bar{N}$ partial wave amplitudes in the unphysical region between $4m_K^2$ and $4m_N^2$, and combine these with timelike kaon form factors from $e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}$ data to compute the spectral functions. Unitarity bounds are imposed to constrain the physical region contributions.

## Key numbers

- The SAMPLE collaboration result for the strange magnetic form factor at $q^2 = -0.1$ GeV$^2$: $G_M^s = 0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19$ (statistical, systematic, radiative correction uncertainties)
- The HAPPEX collaboration result at $q^2 = -0.48$ GeV$^2$: $G_E^s + 0.39 G_M^s = 0.023 \pm 0.034 \pm 0.022 \pm 0.026$
- The $K\bar{K}$ threshold is at $t = 4 m_K^2 \approx 0.98$ GeV$^2$; the $N\bar{N}$ threshold is at $t = 4 m_N^2 \approx 3.53$ GeV$^2$
- The $3\pi$ threshold is at $t = 0.18$ GeV$^2$, significantly below the $K\bar{K}$ threshold
- The $\phi$ meson has roughly a 15% branch to multi-pion final states
- The VMD parametrization of the EM kaon form factor gives residues: $C_\rho \approx 1/2$, $C_\omega \approx 1/6$, $C_\phi \approx 1/3$, with coupling constants $f_\rho \approx 5.1$, $f_\omega \approx 17$, $f_\phi \approx 13$
- The flavor-rotated residues for the strangeness form factor are $C_\omega^{(s)}/C_\omega \approx -0.2$ and $C_\phi^{(s)}/C_\phi \approx -3$
- Using different parametrizations for the kaon strangeness form factor leads to less than 10% variation in the leading strangeness moments

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach previously applied to nucleon isovector form factors (Hoehler, 1975; Mergell et al., 1996) to the isoscalar and strange channels, establishing for the first time the relationship between non-resonant continuum and resonant contributions. The analysis evaluates the credibility of model calculations for strange quark form factors, including vector meson dominance approaches (Jaffe, 1989; Hammer et al., 1996; Forkel, 1996) and kaon cloud calculations truncated at second order in the strong coupling, as well as the ${\cal O}(p^3)$ chiral perturbation theory prediction for the magnetic radius (Hammer et al., 1998). The results enable improved interpretation of parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as SAMPLE and HAPPEX.
