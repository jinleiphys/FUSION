# The nucleon properties in finite temperature and density with vector meson

> We introduce the vector meson $ω$ into the Quark Meson model, and study the impact of vector interactions on the properties of static hadrons using the mean-field approximation. The short-range repulsive force associated with vector interactions leads to an expansion of the root mean square radius of nucleons. While the mass of hadrons increases, the gap between this mass and the energy of the three free constituent quarks decreases, resulting in the instability of hadrons. Our study of nucleon mass and radius at finite temperature and density has potential applications for particle yield in heavy ion collisions and the mass-radius relationship in compact stars.

# The nucleon properties in finite temperature and density with vector meson

## Key claim

The inclusion of the vector meson ω in the Quark Meson model increases both the nucleon mass and root mean square radius, but the gap between the hadron mass and the energy of three free constituent quarks decreases, indicating that hadrons become increasingly unstable at high temperatures and densities due to the repulsive force from vector interactions. This behavior is a competitive effect between short-range repulsion (vector fields) and long-range attraction (scalar fields), with the dominant contribution depending on the specific temperature and chemical potential regime.

## Method

The authors introduce the ω meson into the chiral Quark Meson model Lagrangian and solve the coupled radial equations of motion for quark fields (u(r), v(r)) and meson fields (σ(r), π(r), ω(r)) under the mean-field approximation and the hedgehog ansatz. For finite temperature and density, they embed the soliton into a uniform hot dense quark medium by replacing the zero-temperature potential U with the thermal effective potential Ω(σ, π, ω, T, μ) derived from finite-temperature field theory, then solve the modified equations with boundary conditions set by the thermal vacuum expectation values. The nucleon mass E_B and root mean square radius R are computed from the soliton solutions for various values of the vector coupling constant g_ω/m_ω, temperature T, and chemical potential μ.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass set to 500 MeV, σ meson mass m_σ = 1200 MeV, giving g ≈ 5.28 and λ ≈ 82.1, with pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and pion mass m_π = 138 MeV.
- Vector coupling constant g_ω/m_ω varied over {0, 1, 2, 3, 4} × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}, with m_ω ≈ 1 GeV and g_ω ranging from 1 to 10.
- Thermal effective potential studied at T = 140 MeV, μ = 300 MeV for g_ω/m_ω = {1, 2, 3, 4} × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}, showing that the phase transition is postponed as the vector coupling increases.
- RMS radius R plotted as a function of temperature T with μ = {0, 200, 300} MeV and as a function of chemical potential μ with T = {50, 100, 150} MeV for g_ω/m_ω = {0, 2, 4} × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}.
- RMS radius R as a function of g_ω/m_ω at T = 150 MeV, μ = {150, 200} MeV shows opposite trends: radius increases with coupling at μ = 150 MeV (repulsion dominates) but decreases at μ = 200 MeV (attraction dominates).
- Nucleon mass E_B plotted as a function of T with μ = {0, 200, 300} MeV and as a function of μ with T = {50, 100, 150} MeV for g_ω/m_ω = {0, 2, 4} × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the classical linear Sigma model (LSM) or chiral soliton model of Birse (1984) and Cohen (1986), which originally included only scalar σ and pseudoscalar π mesons providing long-range attraction, by adding the vector ω meson to incorporate short-range repulsive forces. The study connects to earlier work by Wu (2007) and Zhang (2017) who incorporated ω and ρ mesons into the Quark Meson model for phase transition studies, and to Jin (2003) who observed a similar competitive effect between scalar and vector fields in a nucleonic system. The results have potential applications for understanding particle yields in heavy-ion collisions via the thermal-statistical model (where yield Y ∝ exp(-(√(m²+p²)-μ)/T)) and for constraining the mass-radius relationship in compact stars.
