# Tidal Deformabilities and Neutron Star Mergers

> Finite size effects in a neutron star merger are manifested, at leading order, through the tidal deformabilities (Lambdas) of the stars. If strong first-order phase transitions do not exist within neutron stars, both neutron stars are described by the same equation of state, and their Lambdas are highly correlated through their masses even if the equation of state is unknown. If, however, a strong phase transition exists between the central densities of the two stars, so that the more massive star has a phase transition and the least massive star does not, this correlation will be weakened. In all cases, a minimum Lambda for each neutron star mass is imposed by causality, and a less conservative limit is imposed by the unitary gas constraint, both of which we compute. In order to make the best use of gravitational wave data from mergers, it is important to include the correlations relating the Lambdas and the masses as well as lower limits to the Lambdas as a function of mass. Focusing on the case without strong phase transitions, and for mergers where the chirp mass M_chirp<1.4M_sun, which is the case for all observed double neutron star systems where a total mass has been accurately measured, we show that the dimensionless Lambdas satisfy Lambda_1/Lambda_2= q^6, where q=M_2/M_1 is the binary mass ratio; $M$ is mass of each star, respectively. Moreover, they are bounded by q^{n_-}>Lambda_1/Lambda_2> q^{n_{0+}+qn_{1+}}, where n_-<n_{0+}+qn_{1+}; the parameters depend only on M_chirp, which is accurately determined from the gravitational-wave signal. We also provide analytic expressions for the wider bounds that exist in the case of a strong phase transition. We argue that bounded ranges for Lambda_1/Lambda_2, tuned to M_chirp, together with lower bounds to Lambda(M), will be more useful in gravitational waveform modeling than other suggested approaches.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for neutron star mergers without strong first-order phase transitions and with chirp mass \( \mathcal{M} \leq 1.4 M_\odot \), the ratio of dimensionless tidal deformabilities satisfies \( \Lambda_1/\Lambda_2 = q^6 \) as an approximate relation, but more importantly that this ratio is bounded by analytic expressions \( q^{n_-} \geq \Lambda_1/\Lambda_2 \geq q^{n_{0+} + q n_{1+}} \) where the parameters depend only on the chirp mass, and that these bounded ranges together with lower bounds on \( \Lambda(M) \) will be more useful for gravitational waveform modeling than other approaches.

## Method

The authors used a piecewise-polytrope parameterization of the neutron star equation of state with three polytropic segments coupled to a crust, sampling thousands of equations of state constrained by causality, thermodynamic stability, and a minimum neutron star maximum mass. They integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the first-order differential equation for the tidal Love number to compute masses, radii, and dimensionless tidal deformabilities \( \Lambda \) for individual stars, then constructed binary systems to study the correlations between \( \Lambda_1 \), \( \Lambda_2 \), and the binary mass ratio \( q \).

## Key numbers

- For the mass range \( 1.1 M_\odot < M < 1.6 M_\odot \) and \( M_{max} > 2 M_\odot \), \( \Lambda \beta^6 \) is constant to about \( \pm 12\% \), with \( a = 0.0085 \pm 0.0010 \), where \( \beta = GM/Rc^2 \) is the compactness parameter.
- The first polytropic segment pressure \( p_1 \) is constrained between 8.4 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) and 30 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) for the main analysis, with a lower limit of 3.74 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) from the unitary gas constraint.
- For \( p_{1,min} = 8.4 \) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) and \( M_{max} = 1.90 M_\odot \), radii of \( 1.4 M_\odot \) stars as low as 10.4 km may be achieved.
- The polytropic indices satisfy \( 2.27 \leq \gamma_1 \leq 3.06 \), giving symmetry energy \( 33.4 \) MeV \( < S_V < 37.5 \) MeV and slope parameter \( 38.9 \) MeV \( < L < 85.3 \) MeV.
- For \( M_{max} \leq 2.2 M_\odot \) and \( M = M_{max} \), \( \Lambda \) can be reduced by a factor of 2 and \( \Lambda \beta^6 \) can be reduced by about 0.001.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GW170817 by De et al. (2018), who adopted the correlation \( \Lambda_1/\Lambda_2 = q^6 \) and showed that models with deformability correlations were favored over uncorrelated models by odds ratio greater than 100. The paper replaces the approximate \( q^6 \) relation with analytic bounds derived from causality and the minimum neutron star maximum mass, and provides lower limits on \( \Lambda(M) \) from the maximally compact equation of state and the unitary gas constraint. It also addresses modifications to deformability correlations if a strong first-order phase transition exists, comparing with the statistical approach of Yagi and Yunes (2017) used by the LIGO/VIRGO collaboration.
