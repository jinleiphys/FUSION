# Radiative corrections of order $O(αE_e/m_N)$ to Sirlin's radiative corrections of order $O(α/π)$, induced by the hadronic structure of the neutron

> We investigate the contributions of the hadronic structure of the neutron to radiative $O(αE_e/m_N)$ corrections (or the inner $O(αE_e/m_N)$ RC) to the neutron beta decay, where $α$, $E_e$ and $m_N$ are the fine-structure constant, the electron energy and the nucleon mass, respectively. We perform the calculation within the effective quantum field theory of strong low-energy pion-nucleon interactions described by the linear $σ$-model with chiral $SU(2) \times SU(2)$ symmetry and electroweak hadron-hadron, hadron-lepton and lepton-lepton interactions for the electron-lepton family with $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ symmetry of the Standard Electroweak Theory (Ivanov et al., Phys. Rev. D99, 093006 (2019)). We show that after renormalization, carried out in accordance with Sirlin's prescription (Sirlin, Phys. Rev. 164, 1767 (1967)), the inner $O(αE_e/m_N)$ RC are of the order of a few parts of $10^{-5} - 10^{-4}$. This agrees well with the results obtained in (Ivanov et al., Phys. Rev. D99, 093006 (2019)).

## Key claim

The hadronic structure of the neutron contributes inner radiative corrections of order O(αEe/mN) to neutron beta decay that, after renormalization following Sirlin's prescription, are of the order of a few parts of 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴, confirming the results previously obtained by Ivanov et al. (2019) using the same effective field theory framework.

## Method

The authors calculated two-loop Feynman diagrams within the effective quantum field theory LσM&SET, which combines the linear sigma model (LσM) with chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry for strong low-energy pion-nucleon interactions and the Standard Electroweak Theory with SU(2)L×U(1)Y symmetry for electroweak interactions. They applied the leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) to compute the divergent contributions of the Feynman diagrams using n-dimensional regularization, keeping only terms that preserve the chiral symmetry and the V−A structure of the amplitude. The inner O(α/π) corrections were removed by renormalization of the Fermi coupling constant GV and the axial coupling constant gA following Sirlin (1967), leaving the next-to-leading order O(αEe/mN) terms.

## Key numbers

- The structure constants for the inner O(αEe/mN) RC to the amplitude are: G(V)st = −70.71, H(V)st = 67.75, G(W)st = 8.94, G(A)st = 41.95, H(A)st = −40.78, H(W)st = 2.10, and F(W)st = −1.64
- The functions describing the corrections to the electron-energy distribution are: ḡst(Ee) = 0.098 (1 + 0.95 Ee/E0) and f̄st(Ee) = 0.057 (1 + Ee/E0), where E0 = 1.2926 MeV is the end-point energy
- The correction to the neutron lifetime rate is hst(Ee) = 0.053 (1 + Ee/E0), giving a relative contribution of 1.92 × 10⁻⁴ to the neutron decay rate
- The function (α/π)hst(Ee) varies over the region 1.72 × 10⁻⁴ ≤ (α/π)hst(Ee) ≤ 2.46 × 10⁻⁴ for electron energies me ≤ Ee ≤ E0
- The integrated contribution of the inner O(αEe/mN) RC to the neutron decay rate is 1.18 × 10⁻⁵

## Context

This work extends the calculation of inner radiative corrections to neutron beta decay beyond the leading order O(α/π) corrections established by Sirlin (1967) and Sirlin (1978), which were calculated to leading order in the large nucleon mass expansion. The authors previously calculated O(αEe/mN) corrections as next-to-leading order terms in Ivanov et al. (2019) using the same LσM&SET framework, and the present paper confirms those results by computing the contributions specifically from the hadronic structure of the neutron. The results are consistent with analyses by Ando et al. (2004) within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), which found that no low-energy constants proportional to 1/mN appear in the radiative corrections to the neutron lifetime and correlation coefficients. The work enables Standard Model predictions of neutron beta decay at the 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ level, which is important for experimental searches for physics beyond the Standard Model with uncertainties of a few parts in 10⁻⁵.
