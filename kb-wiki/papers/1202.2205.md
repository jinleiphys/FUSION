# Is $1^-+$ Meson a Hybrid?

> We calculate the vacuum to meson matrix elements of the dimension-4 operator $\barψγ_4\nblr_i ψ$ and dimension-5 operator $\barψ\epsγ_jψB_k$ of the $1^{-+}$ meson on the lattice and compare them to the corresponding matrix elements of the ordinary mesons to discern if it is a hybrid. For the charmoniums and strange quarkoniums, we find that the matrix elements of $1^{-+}$ are comparable in size as compared to other known $q\bar{q}$ mesons. They are particularly similar to those of the $2^{++}$ meson, since their dimension-4 operators are in the same Lorentz multiplet. Based on these observations, we find no evidence to support the notion that the lowest $1^{-+}$ mesons in the $c\bar{c}$ and $s\bar{s}$ regions are hybrids. As for the exotic quantum number is concerned, the non-relativistic reduction reveals that the leading terms in the dimension-4 and dimension-5 operators of $1^{-+}$ are identical up to a proportional constant and it involves a center-of-mass momentum operator of the quark-antiquark pair. This explains why $1^{-+}$ is an exotic quantum number in the constituent quark model where the center of mass of the $q\bar{q}$ is not a dynamical degree of freedom. Since QCD has gluon fields in the context of the flux-tube which is appropriate for heavy quarkoniums to allow the valence $q\bar{q}$ to recoil against them, it can accommodate such states as $1^{-+}$. By the same token, hadronic models with additional constituents besides the quarks can also accommodate the $q\bar{q}$ center-of-mass motion. To account for the quantum numbers of these $q\bar{q}$ mesons in QCD and hadron models in the non-relativistic case, the parity and total angular momentum should be modified to $P = (-)^{L + l +1}$ and $\vec{J} = \vec{L} + \vec{l} + \vec{S}$, where $L$ is the orbital angular momentum of the $q\bar{q}$ pair in the meson.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence that the lowest \(1^{-+}\) mesons in the \(c\bar{c}\) and \(s\bar{s}\) regions are hybrids, based on comparing their vacuum-to-meson matrix elements of dimension-4 and dimension-5 operators with those of ordinary mesons; the matrix elements of \(1^{-+}\) are comparable in size to other known \(q\bar{q}\) mesons, particularly similar to those of the \(2^{++}\) meson.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations in the quenched approximation using an anisotropic Wilson gauge action and improved anisotropic Wilson fermion action on a \(12^3 \times 96\) lattice with \(a_s = 0.138\) fm and aspect ratio \(\xi = a_s/a_t = 5\). They computed two-point correlation functions using wall sources for dimension-3 (\(\Gamma\)-type), dimension-4 (\(D\)-type), and dimension-5 (\(B\)-type) interpolation operators, extracting ground-state masses and vacuum-to-meson transition matrix elements for charmonium and strange quarkonium states.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium \(1^{-+}\) mass: \(4205 \pm 84\) MeV from \(B_w \to B_p\) correlator and \(4234 \pm 42\) MeV from \(B_w \to D_p\) correlator.
- Charmonium \(2^{++}\) mass: \(3529 \pm 40\) MeV from \(B_w \to B_p\) and \(3501 \pm 13\) MeV from \(B_w \to D_p\).
- Charmonium \(0^{-+}\) mass: \(3000 \pm 3\) MeV from \(\Gamma_w \to \Gamma_p\), consistent across all source-sink combinations and with PDG value \(2980.3 \pm 1.2\) MeV.
- Charmonium \(1^{--}\) mass: \(3096 \pm 3\) MeV from \(\Gamma_w \to \Gamma_p\), consistent with PDG value \(3096.916 \pm 0.011\) MeV.
- Lattice parameters: \(\beta = 2.8\), \(a_s = 0.138\) fm, \(\xi = 5\); charm quark \(\kappa = 0.060325\) with \(\nu = 0.74\); strange quark \(\kappa = 0.0615\).

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of exotic \(1^{-+}\) mesons (e.g., Dudek (2011), Lacock and Michael (2002)) by directly comparing matrix elements of operators of the same dimension across different meson channels, rather than comparing normalized overlaps within a single \(J^{PC}\) channel. The authors argue that previous criteria for identifying hybrids based on strong coupling to dimension-5 \(B\)-type operators are insufficient, using the example of the topological charge operator coupling to \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) (Feldmann, Kroll, Stech (1998); Cheng, Li, Liu (2009)) to illustrate that large matrix elements of higher-dimensional operators do not necessarily indicate hybrid composition. The results enable future studies of whether excited \(1^{-+}\) states or other exotic quantum numbers show hybrid character, and suggest that the \(1^{-+}\) quantum number can be accommodated in QCD and hadronic models with additional constituents beyond the \(q\bar{q}\) pair.
