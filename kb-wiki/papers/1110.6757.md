# Transversity studies with a polarized 3He target

> A realistic study of the SiDIS process 3He(e,e'π)X in the Bjorken limit is briefly reviewed, showing that the nuclear effects, present in the extraction of the neutron information, are largely under theoretical control, within an Impulse-Approximation approach. In view of the forthcoming experimental data, we shortly present a novel Poincare' covariant description of the nuclear target, implementing a Light-Front analysis at finite Q^2, within the Bakamijan-Thomas construction of the Poincare' generators. Furthermore, as a by-product of the introduction of a Light-Front spin-dependent spectral function for a J=1/2 system, we straightforwardly extend our analysis to the quark-quark correlator, obtaining three new exact relations between the six leading-twist Transverse-Momentum Dependent distributions.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear effects in extracting neutron transversity information from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SiDIS) off a polarized ³He target are largely under theoretical control within an impulse-approximation approach, but only when using a specific extraction procedure based on effective nucleon polarizations and dilution factors. They further claim that a novel Light-Front covariant description yields three new exact relations between the six leading-twist T-even transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), reducing them to only three independent functions within that framework.

## Method

The authors performed a realistic calculation of the SiDIS process ³He(e,e'π)X in the Bjorken limit using a non-relativistic spin-dependent spectral function for ³He based on the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, combined with parametrizations of parton distributions and fragmentation functions. They then extended the analysis to finite Q² by introducing a Poincaré-covariant Light-Front description of the nuclear target using the Bakamijan-Thomas construction, defining a Light-Front spin-dependent spectral function for a J=1/2 system. This spectral function was applied to the quark-quark correlator to derive relations between the six leading-twist TMDs.

## Key numbers

- The extraction procedure for the neutron single spin asymmetry (SSA) uses effective nucleon polarizations p_p and p_n, with the formula A^i_n ≃ (1/(p_n d_n)) (A^{exp,i}_3 - 2 p_p d_p A^{exp,i}_p), where d_n and d_p are dilution factors.
- For the JLab experiments, the pion energy fraction z was chosen in the range 0.45 < z < 0.6, meaning only high-energy pions are observed.
- The preliminary calculations indicate that the effect of using integration limits evaluated at the actual JLab kinematics at 6 GeV, rather than in the Bjorken limit, is negligible.
- The Light-Front spin-dependent spectral function for a J=1/2 system is expressed in terms of only three independent functions (B_0, B_1, B_2), from which three exact relations between the six leading-twist T-even TMDs (f_1, g_{1L}, g_{1T}, h_1, h_{1L}^⊥, h_{1T}^⊥) are obtained.

## Context

This work extends the realistic analysis of SiDIS off transversely polarized ³He presented in Del Dotto et al. (2011), which used a non-relativistic spectral function from Kievsky et al. (1997) based on the AV18 interaction. The new Light-Front covariant description improves upon that earlier framework by implementing a fully Poincaré-covariant treatment of nuclear effects at finite Q². The results are directly relevant to the JLab 6 GeV experiment (completed) and the planned 12 GeV experiments, providing a theoretical tool for extracting neutron information from ³He data. The three new relations between TMDs, derived within the Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics framework, may be tested against other theoretical approaches and experimental results.
