# Binding of charmonium with two- and three-body nuclei

> The energies of the (eta_c d) and (eta_c 3He) bound states are calculated on the basis of exact three- and four-body AGS equations. For the eta_c N interaction a Yukawa-type potential has been adopted. The calculations are done for a certain range of its strength parameter. The results obtained are quite different from calculations based on the folding model.

# Binding of charmonium with two- and three-body nuclei

## Key claim

The binding energies of ηc d and ηc 3He systems, calculated using exact three- and four-body AGS equations with a Yukawa-type ηcN potential, are substantially different from predictions of the folding model and variational calculations, yielding only one bound state in the region -2 MeV to -5 MeV for ηc d when the strength parameter a is varied between 0.5 and 0.6 GeV, in contrast to earlier work.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body equations for the ηcNN system and the corresponding four-body equations for the ηc 3He system, using separable representations of the ηcN and NN potentials. For the ηcN interaction, a Yukawa-type potential V(r) = -a exp(-αr)/r with α = 0.6 GeV was adopted, with the strength parameter a varied between 0.4 and 0.6; the potential was represented separably using both the Bateman method and the Hilbert-Schmidt method with up to six terms. For the NN subsystem in the 3S1 triplet state, a separable rank-one version of the Paris potential was employed, and only s-wave parts of the scattering amplitudes were retained.

## Key numbers

- The ηcN scattering length from the rank-one Bateman representation is aηcN ≈ 0.97 fm, with an antibound (virtual) state at ε = -6.1 MeV.
- For the ηc d system with a = 0.55, the binding energy converges to Eb = -3.196 MeV when using six separable terms in the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion; the first term alone gives -3.429 MeV, and going from N=1 to N=6 changes |Eb| by only 1.7%.
- For the ηc 3He system, the four-body AGS calculation yields a binding energy of -1.3 MeV for a = 0.4 and -14.5 MeV for a = 0.6.
- In contrast, the variational calculation of Brodsky (1990) gives -3.0 MeV for a = 0.4 and -19.0 MeV for a = 0.6, while the folding model of Wasson (1991) gives -0.8 MeV for a = 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the study of charmonium-nucleus bound states, previously addressed by Brodsky (1990) using variational methods and by Wasson (1991) using the folding model, by applying the exact AGS few-body formalism. The results differ significantly from those earlier approaches, indicating that multiple scattering effects not captured by folding or variational models are important. The calculations are motivated by planned experiments at the PANDA project and ongoing measurements at the CLIO collaboration, and the precise knowledge of binding energies is considered crucial for experimental observation of such mesic nuclei.
