# Beyond mean-field boson-fermion description of odd nuclei

> We develop a novel theoretical method for calculating spectroscopic properties of those nuclei with odd number of nucleons, that is based on the nuclear density functional theory and the particle-boson coupling scheme. Self-consistent mean-field calculation based on the DFT is performed to provide microscopic inputs to build the Hamiltonian of the interacting boson-fermion systems, which gives excitation spectra and transition rates of odd-mass nuclei. The method is successfully applied to identify the quantum shape phase transitions and the role of octupole correlations in odd-mass nuclei, and is extended further to odd-odd nuclear systems.

# Beyond mean-field boson-fermion description of odd nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents a method that combines nuclear density functional theory (DFT) with the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) to describe odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, but the boson-fermion coupling strengths remain phenomenological parameters that must be fixed empirically to reproduce experimental low-lying levels for each nucleus.

## Method
Self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations based on nuclear DFT are performed to compute potential energy surfaces (PES) in collective coordinates for the even-even core nucleus, along with spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the odd nucleon. These microscopic inputs are then mapped onto the Hamiltonian of the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM), which includes boson-core, single-particle, and boson-fermion interaction terms. For octupole correlations, negative-parity f bosons are added to the standard sd-boson space. For odd-odd nuclei, the framework is extended to the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM) with an additional residual neutron-proton interaction.

## Key numbers
- Energy difference between neighboring contours in the Sm PES is 250 keV.
- For the odd-A Ba nuclei, predicted B(E3) values are approximately 20–30 W.u. for transitions from octupole to ground state bands.
- The calculated B(E3; 3^-_1 → 0^+_1) for the even-even neighbor is 23 W.u.
- A kink in differentials of calculated mean-field and spectroscopic properties is observed around neutron number N ≈ 90 for the Sm and Eu isotopes.

## Context
This work extends the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) framework, originally developed by Iachello and collaborators, by deriving its Hamiltonian parameters from nuclear DFT rather than fitting them entirely to data. It builds on prior constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations with the DD-PC1 energy density functional (Nikšić et al., 2005) and the mapping procedure for even-even nuclei developed by Nomura et al. (2008, 2011). The method enables systematic studies of quantum shape phase transitions in odd-A nuclei (previously studied mainly in even-even systems by Cejnar et al., 2010), octupole correlations in neutron-rich odd-A Ba isotopes, and chiral band structures in odd-odd Cs isotopes. Future work can apply this framework to other mass regions and to odd-odd systems where experimental data are limited.
