# Chiral EFT calculation of neutrino reactions in warm neutron-rich matter

> Neutrino scattering and absorption rates of relevance to supernovae and neutron star mergers are obtained from nuclear matter dynamical structure functions that encode many-body effects from nuclear mean fields and correlations. We employ nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory to calculate the density, spin, isospin, and spin-isospin response functions of warm beta-equilibrium nuclear matter. We include corrections to the single-particle energies in the mean field approximation as well as vertex corrections resummed in the random phase approximation (RPA), including, for the first time, both direct and exchange diagrams. We find that correlations included through the RPA redistribute the strength of the response to higher energy for neutrino absorption and lower energy for antineutrino absorption. This tends to suppress the absorption rate of electron neutrinos across all relevant energy scales. In contrast, the inclusion of RPA correlations enhances the electron antineutrino absorption rate at low energy and supresses the rate at high energy. These effects are especially important at high-density and in the vicinity of the neutrino decoupling region. Implications for heavy element nucleosynthesis, electromagnetic signatures of compact object mergers, supernova dynamics, and neutrino detection from galactic supernovae are discussed briefly.

## Key claim

The authors show that random phase approximation (RPA) correlations, including both direct and exchange diagrams for the first time, redistribute the strength of the nuclear response to higher energy for neutrino absorption and lower energy for antineutrino absorption, which tends to suppress electron neutrino absorption rates across all relevant energy scales while enhancing electron antineutrino absorption at low energy and suppressing it at high energy, with these effects being especially important at high density and near the neutrino decoupling region.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) to calculate the density, spin, isospin, and spin-isospin response functions of warm beta-equilibrium nuclear matter at densities of 10^11-10^13 g/cm^3 and temperatures of 5-10 MeV. They included Hartree-Fock mean field corrections to single-particle energies and resummed particle-hole vertex corrections in the random phase approximation using a matrix eigenvalue method that incorporates both direct and exchange diagrams, with the ChEFT potentials using cutoff scales of 414, 450, and 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon number density range: n = 10^11-10^13 g/cm^3; temperature range: T = 5-10 MeV (conditions typical of supernova and neutron star merger neutrinospheres)
* Proton fraction in beta equilibrium: Y_p ≈ 0.05-0.10
* ChEFT potential cutoff scales: Λ = 414, 450, and 500 MeV with smoothness parameters n = 10, 3, and 2 respectively
* Benchmark calculations performed at density n = 50 × 10^11 g/cm^3, temperature T = 7 MeV, and momentum transfer q = 21 MeV
* Smearing length used for delta function approximation: ε = 70 × 10^(-4) fm^(-1)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino reaction rates in hot dense matter that used nonrelativistic and relativistic mean field models (Sawyer 1989, Reddy 1998, 1999), Fermi liquid theory (Iwamoto 1982, Burrows 1998, 1999), and pseudopotentials (Rrapaj 2015). The inclusion of RPA correlations with both direct and exchange diagrams from chiral EFT interactions goes beyond earlier work that focused primarily on mean field effects (Martinez-Pinedo 2012, Roberts 2012), and the results can be implemented in numerical simulations of supernovae, neutron star mergers, and proto-neutron star cooling.
