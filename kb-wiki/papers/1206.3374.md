# Results of Nucleon Resonance Extraction via Dynamical Coupled-Channels Analysis from Collaboration@EBAC

> We review a global analysis of meson production reactions off the nucleons by a collaboration at Excited Baryon Analysis Center of Jefferson Lab. The analysis is pursued with a dynamical coupled-channels approach, within which the dynamics of multi-channel reaction processes are taken into account in a fully consistent way with the two-body as well as three-body unitarity of the S-matrix. With this approach, new features of nucleon excitations are revealed as resonant particles originating from the non-trivial multi-channel reaction dynamics, which cannot be addressed by static hadron models where the nucleon excitations are treated as stable particles.

# Results of Nucleon Resonance Extraction via Dynamical Coupled-Channels Analysis from Collaboration@EBAC

## Key claim
The analysis reveals that nucleon resonances (N*) can arise from non-trivial multi-channel reaction dynamics, meaning that a naive one-to-one correspondence between bare N* states (as defined in static hadron models) and physical resonance poles does not generally exist; the Roper resonance, for example, appears as two almost degenerate poles rather than a single resonance.

## Method
A dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach was employed, solving coupled-channels integral equations for partial wave amplitudes of meson-baryon reactions (including πN, ηN, πΔ, ρN, σN, KΛ, KΣ channels). The model incorporates both two-body and three-body (ππN) unitarity through self-energies in the meson-baryon Green functions and Z-potentials derived from Feshbach projection techniques. Bare N* states are defined as eigenstates of the Hamiltonian with couplings to meson-baryon continuum turned off, and their propagation is included via a sum over bare poles in the transition potential.

## Key numbers
- The analysis covers meson production reactions up to center-of-mass energy W = 2.0–2.2 GeV, depending on the channel.
- For πN → πN and πN → ηN, data up to W = 2.0 GeV were analyzed in the early (2006–2009) stage; the extended analysis (2010 onward) includes πN → KΛ and πN → KΣ up to W = 2.1 GeV, and γN → KΛ and γN → KΣ up to W = 2.2 GeV.
- The Roper resonance region yields two almost degenerate poles at E = 1357 − 76i MeV and E = 1364 − 103i MeV.
- The early analysis fitted data up to Q² = 1.5 (GeV/c)² for N(e,e'π)N reactions.
- The extended analysis fits more than 20,000 data points.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach developed by Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (2007) and applies it to a global analysis of meson production reactions at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) of Jefferson Lab. The double-pole structure of the Roper resonance had been reported by other groups using different approaches, including VPI (1985), SAID (2006), and the Jülich group (2009). The analysis enables extraction of N* mass spectra, decay widths, and N-N* electromagnetic transition form factors, including their complex nature, which cannot be obtained from static hadron models such as constituent quark models or Dyson-Schwinger approaches.
