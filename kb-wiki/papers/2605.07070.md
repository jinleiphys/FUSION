# Analysis of the energy and angular distributions of photoneutrons from natPb, 197Au, natSn, natCu, natFe, and natTi using resonance direct theory

> Photoneutron double-differential cross sections in the giant dipole resonance (GDR) region were calculated to investigate the underlying nuclear reaction mechanisms, with particular emphasis on the role of the direct process. Contributions from direct, pre-equilibrium, and compound processes were all taken into account. Wilkinson's resonance direct (RD) theory, based on the independent particle model, was applied to describe high-energy neutron emission from the direct process. The angular distribution of neutrons emitted via the RD mechanism was formulated using the Agodi and Courant formalism, which was incorporated into the RD framework. Neutron emission from the pre-equilibrium and compound processes was calculated using the two-component exciton model and the Hauser-Feshbach formalism, respectively. The calculated results were compared with experimental data obtained at NewSUBARU using 16.6-MeV quasi-monochromatic linearly-polarized photon beams. Good agreement between calculations and measurements was observed for Pb, Au, and Sn, confirming the validity of the proposed model. Furthermore, the angular anisotropies of photoneutrons emitted from these elements were investigated, revealing considerable contributions from the RD process at high neutron energies. This study provides a deeper understanding of photoneutron emission mechanisms in the GDR energy region.

# Analysis of the energy and angular distributions of photoneutrons from natPb, 197Au, natSn, natCu, natFe, and natTi using resonance direct theory

## Key claim

The resonance direct (RD) process, described by Wilkinson's theory with angular distributions formulated via the Agodi and Courant formalisms, successfully reproduces the measured photoneutron double-differential cross sections for heavy nuclei (Pb, Au, Sn) but overestimates those for lighter nuclei (Cu, Fe, Ti) when proton transitions are neglected in the branching ratio calculation.

## Method

The authors calculated photoneutron double-differential cross sections by combining three reaction mechanisms: the direct process described by Wilkinson's resonance direct theory using an infinite square-well potential and energy-dependent optical potential parameters; the pre-equilibrium process calculated with the two-component exciton model; and the compound process calculated with the Hauser-Feshbach formalism using TALYS with default parameters. The angular distribution of directly emitted neutrons was formulated using the Agodi formalism for linearly polarized photons, with the b_l/a_l ratio derived by equating Agodi's expression to Courant's formula for circularly polarized photons. Calculated results were smeared using the experimental incident photon energy distribution and detector energy resolution (5% at 2 MeV to 7.5% at 8 MeV) before comparison with NewSUBARU data obtained with 16.6-MeV quasi-monochromatic linearly-polarized photon beams.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy: 16.6 MeV quasi-monochromatic linearly-polarized photon beams from NewSUBARU
- The RD process primarily contributes to neutron emission in the 4–8 MeV energy range for Pb, Au, and Sn
- For 197Au at emission angle (θ,φ) = (90°,0°), the RD component shows discrete transitions corresponding to neutron energies E_n = E_γ − B_i, with binding energies B_i from numerical calculations using a diffuse nuclear potential
- For 63Cu, the 2p_{3/2} → 2d_{5/2} transition contributes at E_γ − 13.1 MeV ≈ 3.5 MeV
- For 56Fe, only transitions from the 2p_{3/2} orbit are allowed for E_γ < 15.8 MeV, producing a peak around 4.5 MeV
- For 48Ti, only neutron emission from the 1f_{7/2} orbit is allowed for E_γ < 15.6 MeV
- The imaginary part of the neutron optical potential used: W = 0.4E_n + 2.5 MeV (Lane and Wandel, 1955)
- Nuclear radius: R_N = 1.3A^{1/3} fm
- Diffuseness parameter: α = 1.45 fm^{−1}; spin-orbit coupling constant: λ = 39.5

## Context

This work extends the resonance direct theory of Wilkinson (1956) and the angular distribution formalisms of Agodi (1957) and Courant (1951) to provide a complete model of photoneutron emission including direct, pre-equilibrium, and compound processes. Previous studies by Zatsepina et al. (1963) and Mutchler (1966) calculated photoneutron spectra using RD and statistical decay but systematically underestimated the direct component due to neglecting pre-equilibrium processes and using transmission coefficients instead of penetrability. The NewSUBARU group (Kirihara et al., 2020; Kim Tuyet et al., 2021; Hong Thuong et al., 2024; Thuong et al., 2025) measured double-differential cross sections with linearly-polarized photons and observed direct components in the 4–8 MeV region but could not quantitatively discuss the energy dependence of angular anisotropy. This work enables quantitative prediction of both energy and angular distributions of photoneutrons in the GDR region, including the energy-dependent angular anisotropy b/a ratio for heavy nuclei.
