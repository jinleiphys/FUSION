# Local well-posedness and singularity formation in non-Newtonian compressible fluids

> We investigate the initial value problem of a very general class of $3+1$ non-Newtonian compressible fluids in which the viscous stress tensor with shear and bulk viscosity relaxes to its Navier-Stokes values. These fluids correspond to the non-relativistic limit of well-known Israel-Stewart-like theories used in the relativistic fluid dynamic simulations of high-energy nuclear and astrophysical systems. After establishing the local well-posedness of the Cauchy problem, we show for the first time in the literature that there exists a large class of initial data for which the corresponding evolution breaks down in finite time due to the formation of singularities. This implies that a large class of non-Newtonian fluids do not have finite solutions defined at all times.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for a large class of non-Newtonian compressible fluids with bulk and shear viscosity, smooth initial data can lead to finite-time singularity formation (loss of $C^1$ regularity), even though the system is locally well-posed and linearly stable; this is the first such result for fluids with both bulk and shear viscosity, not just bulk alone.

## Method

The authors derive the non-relativistic limit of Israel-Stewart-like relativistic viscous fluid theories, obtaining a system of PDEs for density, velocity, and a bulk viscous scalar (and later including shear stress). They rewrite the system in first-order symmetric hyperbolic (FOSH) form to prove local well-posedness, then adapt Sideris-type arguments (originally for ideal fluids) to construct initial data for which the evolution must break down in finite time, using integral estimates and the relaxation-type equation for the viscous stress.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic speed for the bulk-viscous system is $c_v = \sqrt{c_s^2 + \zeta/(\rho \tau_\Pi)}$, where $c_s$ is the speed of sound, $\zeta$ the bulk viscosity, $\rho$ the density, and $\tau_\Pi$ the relaxation time.
* Local well-posedness holds in Sobolev spaces $H^{5/2+}$ (or uniformly local $H^N_{\text{u.l.}}$) when $\tau_\Pi > 0$, $\zeta > 0$, $\rho > 0$, and $c_s$ is nonvanishing and finite.
* Linear stability around equilibrium requires $\tau_\Pi > 0$ and $\zeta > 0$ (with $\rho_0 > 0$), as shown by the Routh-Hurwitz criterion applied to the cubic dispersion relation.

## Context

This work extends the finite-time singularity result of Disconzi, Noronha, and Speranza (2020) for Israel-Stewart-like theories with only bulk viscosity to the case including both bulk and shear viscosity, in the non-relativistic regime. It also connects to the BDNK formulation of Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2019-2020) and to the relativistic Israel-Stewart simulations used in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers. The results imply that non-relativistic regions of quark-gluon plasmas and neutron star merger matter modeled by Israel-Stewart-like theories can lose regularity in finite time.
