# Spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions

> Polarized quarks and antiquarks in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can lead to the spin alignment of vector mesons formed by quark coalescence. Using the relativistic spin Boltzmann equation for vector mesons derived from Kadanoff-Baym equations with an effective quark-meson model for strong interaction and quark coalescence model for hadronizaton, we calculate the spin density matrix element $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons and show that anisotropies of local field correlations or fluctuations with respect to the spin quantization direction lead to $ϕ$ meson's spin alignment. We propose that the local correlation or fluctuation of $ϕ$ fields is the dominant mechanism for the observed the $ϕ$ meson's spin alignment and its strength can be extracted from experimental data as functions of collision energies. The calculated transverse momentum dependence of $ρ_{00}$ agrees with STAR's data. We further predict the azimuthal angle dependence of $ρ_{00}$ which can be tested in future experiments.

## Key claim

The observed spin alignment of $\phi$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions, which is orders of magnitude larger than predictions from vorticity-driven mechanisms, can be explained by local fluctuations or correlations of the $\phi$ meson fields during hadronization, with the strength of these field fluctuations extracted from experimental data as functions of collision energy.

## Method

The authors derive a relativistic spin Boltzmann equation for vector mesons from Kadanoff-Baym equations using an effective quark-meson model for strong interactions and a quark coalescence model for hadronization. They calculate the spin density matrix element $\rho_{00}$ for $\phi$ mesons by solving this equation in the dilute gas limit, expressing $\rho_{00}$ in terms of local correlations of the $\phi$ field, vorticity field, and their electric and magnetic components in the meson rest frame. The two parameters characterizing transverse and longitudinal $\phi$ field fluctuations are extracted by fitting STAR data on momentum-integrated $\rho_{00}^y$ (out-of-plane) and $\rho_{00}^x$ (in-plane) at multiple collision energies.

## Key numbers

- The extracted transverse field fluctuation parameter $F_T^2$ and longitudinal parameter $F_z^2$ follow $\ln (F_{T,z}^2/m_\pi^2)=a_{T,z} - b_{T,z} \ln (\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}/\mathrm{GeV})$ with $a_T=3.90\pm 1.11$, $b_T=0.924\pm 0.234$, $a_z=3.33\pm 0.917$, $b_z=0.760\pm 0.189$.
- The constituent strange quark mass is set to $m_s=419$ MeV with $m_\phi=1020$ MeV; varying $m_s$ from 419 to 486 MeV increases extracted $F_T^2$ and $F_z^2$ by about 37%.
- The calculation uses $\phi$ meson transverse momentum spectra and $v_2(k_T)$ from STAR experiments at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=11.5$ to 200 GeV in the range $k_T=1.2$ to 5.4 GeV and rapidity $|y|<1$.
- The coefficients $C_1$ and $C_2$ in the $\rho_{00}$ expression are $C_1 = (8m_s^4+16m_s^2 m_\phi^2+3m_\phi^4)/(120m_s^2(m_\phi^2+2m_s^2))$ and $C_2 = (8m_s^4-14m_s^2 m_\phi^2+3m_\phi^4)/(120m_s^2(m_\phi^2+2m_s^2))$.

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence model for vector meson spin alignment originally proposed by Liang and Wang (2004) and addresses the discrepancy where STAR's measured $\phi$ meson $\rho_{00}$ deviation from 1/3 is orders of magnitude larger than predictions from vorticity fields that explain $\Lambda$ hyperon polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017, 2018). The authors propose that local $\phi$ field fluctuations, possibly related to non-perturbative QCD properties such as gluon condensates (Shifman, 1978; Shuryak, 1981), are the dominant mechanism. The extracted field fluctuation parameters and their energy dependence can be used to predict the transverse momentum and azimuthal angle dependence of $\rho_{00}$ for future experimental tests, and the method can be extended to heavy quarkonia spin alignment and hyperon spin correlations.
