# On the scalar nonet in the extended Nambu Jona-Lasinio model

> We discuss the lightest scalar resonances, $f_0(600)$, $κ(800)$, $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ in the extended Nambu Jona-Lasinio model. We find that the model parameters can be tuned, but unnaturally, to accommodate for those scalars except the $f_0(980)$. We also discuss problems encountered in the K Matrix unitarization approximation by using $N_c$ counting technique.

## Key claim

The extended Nambu Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model can be tuned, but only unnaturally, to accommodate the masses and widths of the lightest scalar resonances $f_0(600)$, $\kappa(800)$, and $a_0(980)$ simultaneously; however, the $f_0(980)$ cannot be described as a member of the scalar octet because its mass is too small, and the parameter tuning required makes the model unable to simultaneously explain the vector meson spectrum.

## Method

The authors bosonize the ENJL model with four-quark interactions in scalar-pseudoscalar and vector-axial-vector channels, add the 't Hooft instanton interaction to incorporate $U_A(1)$ anomaly effects, and derive an effective meson Lagrangian using proper time regularization and heat-kernel expansion. They compute tree-level scalar mass relations and decay widths, then apply single-channel K-matrix unitarization to the tree-level scattering amplitudes for $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$, $\pi K\to\pi K$, and $\pi\eta\to\pi\eta$ to extract pole positions of the scalar resonances. The $N_c$ dependence of pole trajectories is examined using $N_c$ counting techniques to assess the reliability of the K-matrix unitarization approximation.

## Key numbers

- The $\sigma$ pole from K-matrix unitarization for fit 2 ($g_A=0.6$) is at $985\pm133i$ MeV; for fit 3 ($g_A=0.7$) at $1161\pm229i$ MeV; for fit 4 ($g_A=0.8$) at $544\pm306i$ MeV (from $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ channel).
- The $\kappa$ pole for fit 2 ($g_A=0.6$) is at $1423\pm153i$ MeV; for fit 3 ($g_A=0.7$) at $1629\pm202i$ MeV; for fit 4 ($g_A=0.8$) at $801\pm360i$ MeV (from $\pi K\to\pi K$ channel).
- The $a_0$ pole for fit 2 ($g_A=0.6$) is at $1030\pm31i$ MeV; for fit 3 ($g_A=0.7$) at $1016\pm45i$ MeV; for fit 4 ($g_A=0.8$) at $1000\pm60i$ MeV (from $\pi\eta\to\pi\eta$ channel).
- The bare mass of $\sigma$ is set to approximately 930 MeV; the bare mass of $\kappa$ is approximately 1400 MeV.
- The $f_\pi$ is fitted to 92.4 MeV; $f_K$ ranges from 102.0 MeV (fit 1) to 136.1 MeV (fit 5).
- The $M_{th}^2$ (from $m_\eta^2+m_{\eta'}^2-2m_K^2$) is 0.727 GeV$^2$ experimentally; fitted values range from 0.645 GeV$^2$ (fit 1) to 0.16 GeV$^2$ (fit 5).
- The constituent quark mass $M_Q$ is approximately 393-397 MeV across fits.
- The $g_A$ parameter is fixed at values 0.5, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9 in fits 1-5 respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier ENJL studies by Volkov (1990s) and Osipov (2000s) that attempted to describe the light scalar nonet but gave small $\sigma$ masses around 500-550 MeV, which could not explain the large $\sigma$ width. The authors build on their own earlier work (Xiao, 2006) emphasizing that bare mass parameters must be distinguished from pole masses when widths are large. The paper identifies a fundamental tension: the ENJL model correlates scalar and vector sector parameters too strongly, unlike resonance chiral theory (Ecker, 1989; Pich, 1990s), and cannot accommodate $f_0(980)$ as a member of the scalar octet. The results suggest that a more general framework such as resonance chiral theory may be needed, and that $f_0(980)$ might be a $\bar K K$ molecule (Locher, 1990s; Baru, 2000s) rather than a simple $q\bar q$ state.
