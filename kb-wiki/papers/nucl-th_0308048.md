# Chiral solitons in nuclei: Saturation, EMC effect and Drell-Yan experiments

> The Chiral Quark-Soliton model of the nucleon contains a mechanism for an attractive interaction between nucleons. This, along with the exchange of vector mesons between nucleons, is used to compute the saturation properties of infinite nuclear matter. This provides a new way to asses the effects of the nuclear medium on a nucleon that includes valence and sea quarks. We show that the model simultaneously describes the nuclear EMC effect and the related Drell-Yan experiments.

## Key claim

The Chiral Quark-Soliton model can simultaneously describe nuclear saturation properties, the EMC effect, and the Drell-Yan data on nuclear antiquark enhancement, but only with two free parameters (the vacuum chiral condensate and the vector meson coupling) and working at leading order in the number of colors, which introduces a ~30% theoretical uncertainty in the free nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors embed the Chiral Quark-Soliton (CQS) model of the nucleon into infinite symmetric nuclear matter by adding a constant scalar density contribution from other nucleons to the quark scalar density in the soliton field equation, modeling the scalar interaction from multiple pion exchange. They solve coupled self-consistency equations for the soliton profile and the nucleon scalar density as a function of Fermi momentum using the Kahana-Ripka basis, and add a phenomenological vector meson exchange (mass 770 MeV) between nucleons to provide short-range repulsion. The resulting medium-modified quark and antiquark distributions are computed at leading order in N_C, then evolved via perturbative QCD to the relevant experimental scales (5 GeV for EMC, 10 GeV for Drell-Yan) and folded with a light-cone nucleon momentum distribution to obtain nuclear structure function ratios.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon mass computed as M_N(k_F=0) = 1209 MeV (approximately 30% above the physical value, as expected at leading order in N_C)
- Constituent quark mass M = 420 MeV; Pauli-Villars regulator mass M_PV ~ 580 MeV (fixed by reproducing f_pi = 93 MeV)
- Best-fit parameters: vacuum condensate -<psi-bar psi>^(1/3) = 200 MeV, vector coupling g_v^2/4pi = 10.55
- Resulting saturation Fermi momentum k_F = 1.38 fm^-1 (consistent with known value 1.35 +/- 0.05 fm^-1)
- Binding energy per nucleon B = -15.75 MeV at the minimum
- Nuclear matter compressibility K = 348.5 MeV (above experimental 210 +/- 30 MeV but below Walecka model value of 560 MeV)
- Effective chiral condensate falls ~30% at nuclear density (consistent with model-independent prediction of 25-50% reduction from Cohen (1992))
- Root mean square radius of baryon density increases by 2.4% in the medium (consistent with <6% constraint from McKeown (1986))

## Context

This work extends the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model approach of Saito and Thomas (1994) by explicitly computing medium modifications to both valence and sea quark distributions within the CQS model, enabling simultaneous verification against both EMC and Drell-Yan data. It contradicts the "binding" explanation of the EMC effect (Bickerstaff, Birse, and Miller 1984; Ericson and Thomas 1984) which predicted enhanced nuclear antiquark content not seen in Drell-Yan experiments (Alde et al. 1990), and improves upon relativistic mean-field treatments (Birse 1993; Frankfurt and Strikman 1987) that failed with structureless hadrons. The model enables future calculations of medium modifications for other observables measurable in experiments, with fewer input parameters and model assumptions than previous approaches.
