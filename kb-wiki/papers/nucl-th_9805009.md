# Meson-Baryon Form Factors in Chiral Colour Dielectric Model

> The renormalised form factors for pseudoscalar meson-baryon coupling are computed in chiral colour dielectric model. This has been done by rearranging the Lippmann-Schwinger series for the meson baryon scattering matrix so that it can be expressed as a baryon pole term with renormalized form factors and baryon masses and the rest of the terms which arise from the crossed diagrams. Thus we are able to obtain an integral equation for the renormalized meson-baryon form factors in terms of the bare form factors as well as an expression for the meson self energy. This integral equation is solved and renormalized meson baryon form factors and renormalized baryon masses are computed. The parameters of the model are adjusted to obtain a best fit to the physical baryon masses. The calculations show that the renormalized form factors are energy-dependent and differ from the bare form factors primarily at momentum transfers smaller than 1 GeV. At nucleon mass, the change in the form factors is about 10% at zero momentum transfer. The computed form factors are soft with the equivalent monopole cut-off mass of about 500 MeV. The renormalized coupling constants are obtained by comparing the chiral colour dielectric model interaction Hamiltonian with the standard form of meson-nucleon interaction Hamiltonian. The ratio of $ΔNπ$ and $NNπ$ coupling constants is found to be about 2.15. This value is very close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The renormalized meson-baryon form factors computed in the chiral colour dielectric model are energy-dependent and differ from the bare form factors primarily at momentum transfers smaller than 1 GeV, with the renormalization effects yielding a ratio of ΔNπ to NNπ coupling constants of about 2.15, close to the experimental value of 2.14, whereas the bare coupling constant ratio from SU(3) flavour symmetry is 1.697.

## Method

The author rearranged the Lippmann-Schwinger series for the meson-baryon scattering matrix to isolate a baryon pole term with renormalized form factors and baryon masses, obtaining an integral equation for the renormalized form factors in terms of the bare form factors and an expression for the meson self energy. The coupled quark and colour dielectric field equations were solved self-consistently to construct bare baryon states and compute bare form factors, then the integral equation was solved iteratively (10-15 iterations) to obtain renormalized form factors and masses, with the CCD model parameters (glueball mass, strong coupling constant, and strange quark mass) adjusted to fit physical baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- The change in the form factor at zero momentum transfer at nucleon mass is about 10%
- The ratio of ΔNπ and NNπ coupling constants is about 2.15, compared to the experimental value of 2.14
- The equivalent monopole cut-off mass is about 500 MeV
- The computed πNN coupling constant is 0.254, about 9% smaller than the experimental value of 0.279
- The ratio of renormalized to bare form factors at zero momentum transfer varies from about 1.1 to about 1.3 as energy increases from nucleon mass to Δ mass
- The bare coupling constant ratio from SU(3) flavour symmetry is 1.697
- The glueball mass parameter used in the representative calculation is 978.6 MeV, with α_s = 0.438, B^{1/4} = 122.3 MeV, quark mass m = 122.3 MeV, and m_0 = 210.1 MeV
- The pion decay constant f was fixed at 93 MeV (physical value)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations using the chiral colour dielectric model for πN scattering phase shifts (Phatak, Lu, and Landau, 1995) by computing the renormalized vertex functions that were not obtained in that prior work. The results support the need for softer form factors (Λ ~ 500 MeV) indicated by calculations of Δ production on nuclei (Jain, 1995), and the computed ratio of coupling constants resolves a known discrepancy between the quark model prediction (1.697) and experiment (2.14). The approach enables future nuclear physics calculations (NN potentials, photo- and electro-production of mesons) to use renormalized rather than bare form factors, avoiding double counting of meson loops.
