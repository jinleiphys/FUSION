# The two-nucleon electromagnetic charge operator in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to one loop

> The electromagnetic charge operator in a two-nucleon system is derived in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to order $e\, Q$ (or N4LO), where $Q$ denotes the low-momentum scale and $e$ is the electric charge. The specific form of the N3LO and N4LO corrections from, respectively, one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange depends on the off-the-energy-shell prescriptions adopted for the non-static terms in the corresponding potentials. We show that different prescriptions lead to unitarily equivalent potentials and accompanying charge operators. Thus, provided a consistent set is adopted, predictions for physical observables will remain unaffected by the non-uniqueness associated with these off-the-energy-shell effects.

## Key claim

The paper derives the two-nucleon electromagnetic charge operator in chiral effective field theory up to order e Q (N4LO), and demonstrates that while the specific form of the N3LO and N4LO corrections from one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange depends on the off-the-energy-shell prescription adopted for the non-static terms in the corresponding potentials, different prescriptions lead to unitarily equivalent potentials and charge operators, so predictions for physical observables remain unaffected.

## Method

The authors used time-ordered perturbation theory to derive the two-nucleon electromagnetic charge operator from chiral Lagrangians, expanding the scattering amplitude and extracting the charge operator order by order in the power counting scheme of chiral effective field theory. They constructed the charge operator up to one-loop order (N4LO) by evaluating irreducible and recoil-corrected reducible diagrams, and demonstrated unitary equivalence between charge operators derived with different off-the-energy-shell prescriptions for the one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange potentials (labeled by a parameter nu taking values 0 and 1).

## Key numbers

- The charge operator is derived up to order e Q (N4LO), where Q denotes the low-momentum scale and e is the electric charge
- The leading-order (LO) charge operator is of order e Q^{-3}, given by rho^{(-3)}(q) = e e_{N,1} (2pi)^3 delta(p_1 + q - p_1') + 1 <-> 2
- The N2LO relativistic correction (order e Q^{-1}) contains terms proportional to (q^2 + 2 i q . sigma_1 x K_1) with coefficient -e/(8 m_N^2) (2 mu_{N,1} - e_{N,1})
- The N3LO OPE charge operator (order e Q^0) from the gamma-pi-N coupling is rho^{(0)}_e = (e/(2 m_N)) (g_A^2/F_pi^2) (tau_1 . tau_2 + tau_{2z}) (sigma_1 . q sigma_2 . k_2)/omega^2_{k_2} + 1 <-> 2
- The N4LO triangle pion-in-flight operator (order e Q) is rho^{(1)}_b = e (2 g_A^2/F_pi^4) tau_{2,z} integral (q_1 . q_2)/(omega_1^2 omega_2^2) + 1 <-> 2
- The N4LO charge operator satisfies rho^{(1)}(q=0)=0 and is free of ultraviolet divergences, with no counterterms needed at this order

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of the two-nucleon electromagnetic current by Pastore et al. (2009) and Kolling et al. (2009) to the charge operator, and extends Friar's (1977) original considerations of unitary equivalence for off-the-energy-shell effects from the one-pion-exchange sector to the two-pion-exchange sector. The results enable consistent calculations of electromagnetic observables in few-nucleon systems using chiral effective field theory potentials and charge operators, and complement earlier studies of the isoscalar charge operator by Walzl et al. (2001) and Phillips (2003, 2007) which were limited to tree-level corrections.
