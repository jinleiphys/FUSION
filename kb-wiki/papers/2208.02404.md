# The $S$ matrices of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies in effective field theory

> The elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies for $l=0,1,2,3,4,5,6$ is studied in effective field theory. We discuss the construction of the $S$ matrices of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering in terms of the amplitudes of sub-threshold bound and resonant states of $^{16}$O, which are calculated from the effective Lagrangian. The parameters appearing in the $S$ matrices are fitted to the phase shift data below the $p$-$^{15}$N breakup threshold energy, and we find that the phase shifts are well described within the theory.

## Key claim

The elastic α-¹²C scattering S-matrices for partial waves l=0 through 6 can be constructed from amplitudes of sub-threshold bound and resonant states of ¹⁶O derived from an effective Lagrangian, and the phase shifts below the p-¹⁵N breakup threshold are well described within the theory, though the fit quality varies by partial wave (χ²/N ranges from 0.013 for l=0 to 0.87 for l=3).

## Method

The author constructs S-matrices for each partial wave (l=0–6) by decomposing the phase shift into contributions from sub-threshold bound states and resonant states of ¹⁶O, with amplitudes derived from an effective field theory Lagrangian that includes composite fields for ¹⁶O states. The bound-state amplitudes are expressed in terms of effective range parameters (up to p⁶ or p⁸ order), with one parameter fixed by the binding energy of the sub-threshold state; resonant-state amplitudes are expanded around resonant energies using a Breit-Wigner-like form with additional quadratic and cubic energy-dependent corrections. All parameters are fitted to the phase shift data of Tischhauser et al. (2009) for α energies 2.6 MeV ≤ E_α ≤ 6.62 MeV using a Markov chain Monte Carlo method.

## Key numbers

- For l=0: χ²/N = 0.013 with 7 fitted parameters; fitted resonant energy E_R(03) = 4.8884(1) MeV and width Γ_R(03) = 1.34(3) keV for the 0₃⁺ state, compared to experimental values E_R(03)^(exp) = 4.887(2) MeV and Γ_R(03)^(exp) = 1.5(5) keV
- For l=1: χ²/N = 0.089 with 5 fitted parameters; ANC for the 1₁⁻ sub-threshold state |C_b| = 1.727(3)×10¹⁴ fm⁻¹/²
- For l=2: χ²/N = 0.66; fitted resonant energy E_R(22) = 2.68308(5) MeV and width Γ_R(22) = 0.75(2) keV for the 2₂⁺ state; E_R(23) = 4.3545(2) MeV and Γ_R(23) = 74.61(3) keV for the 2₃⁺ state
- For l=3: χ²/N = 0.87; effective range parameters r₃ = 0.0335(2) fm⁻⁵, P₃ = -0.446(9) fm⁻³, Q₃ = 0.311(5) fm⁻¹, R₃ = -0.152(3) fm
- For l=4: χ²/N = 0.47; fitted resonant energy E_R(41) = 3.19606(1) MeV and width Γ_R(41) = 25.91(1) keV for the 4₁⁺ state; E_R(42) = 3.93655(2) MeV and width Γ_R(42) = 0.425(4) keV for the 4₂⁺ state

## Context

This work extends the author's previous EFT studies of elastic α-¹²C scattering for l=0,1,2,3 (Ando 2018, 2016) and the l=2 case including multiple resonances (Ando 2022) to all partial waves l=0 through 6. The method follows the approach of Gelman (2009) and Habashi, Fleming, and van Kolck (2021) for including resonant states in EFT by summing bubble diagrams to infinite order near resonances. The fitted phase shifts below the p-¹⁵N breakup threshold enable future application of this EFT framework to the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O radiative capture reaction at the Gamow peak energy (0.3 MeV), which is critical for determining the C/O ratio in stellar helium burning.
