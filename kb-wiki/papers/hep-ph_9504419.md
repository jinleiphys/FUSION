# Neutron-antineutron transitions

> We confirm our previous limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time t>4.7*10**31 y. The approach is verified by the example of exactly solvable model. We also discuss the all existing remarks. The approach with finite time interval includes potential model as an extremely simplified special case. It is shown that potential model is inapplicable to the problem in hand because for antineutron-nucleus decay probability it gives W=2. In particular, as we transparently show, this "decay law" (normalized by factor 2) is used by Dover, Gal and Richard for refutation of our results.

# Neutron-antineutron transitions

## Key claim
The paper confirms a lower bound on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time of τ_min > 4.7×10³¹ years, which is 31 orders of magnitude larger than previous limits. This result depends critically on the assumption that the energy gap q₀ in the n-n̄ transition vertex is zero; if q₀ ≠ 0, the bound collapses to approximately 1 year.

## Method
The author calculates the probability W(t) of n-n̄ transitions in nuclear matter using a finite-time-interval approach beyond the potential model, decomposing the process into a two-step sequence: n → n̄ mixing followed by n̄-nucleus decay. The n̄-nucleus decay probability W_n̄(τ) is taken from the exponential decay law W_exp(τ) = 1 − e^{−Γt} with Γ ≈ 100 MeV, rather than from the optical potential model which gives W_n̄^{pot} = 2 in the strong absorption limit. The approach is verified by showing that the exactly solvable potential model emerges as a special case of the finite-time formalism.

## Key numbers
- Free-space n-n̄ oscillation time lower bound: τ_min = 4.7×10³¹ years, derived from T = 6.5×10³¹ years (the experimental limit from free neutron searches) divided by √2.
- Annihilation width of n̄-nucleus: Γ ≈ 100 MeV, corresponding to a characteristic time of approximately 10⁻²⁴ seconds.
- In the strong absorption region (Γt >> 1), the potential model gives W_n̄^{pot} = 2, which the author argues is unphysical for a decay probability.
- The ratio W(T)/W_exp(T) between the author's result and the S-matrix potential model result is approximately ΓT ∼ 10⁶².
- When the energy gap q₀ = δU ≠ 0, the process probability becomes linear in time (W ∼ t) rather than quadratic, yielding a previous bound τ_min ∼ 1 year.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculation (Nazaruk, 1995) of n-n̄ mixing in nuclear matter beyond the potential model, claiming a dramatic increase in the oscillation time limit that would rule out many grand unified theories. The paper directly contradicts the results of Dover, Gal and Richard (1995), who used the potential model to refute the author's earlier findings; the author argues that their approach uses an incorrect decay law W_n̄ = 2. The paper also criticizes a preprint by Krivoruchenko (1995) for fundamental misunderstandings of T-matrix probability interpretation. The result enables a model-independent method for calculating n-n̄ transitions that relies only on unitarity, with implications for experimental searches at underground facilities.
