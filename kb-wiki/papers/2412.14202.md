# Higher-order dissipative anisotropic magnetohydrodynamics from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation

> We apply the method of moments to the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation and derive the equations of motion for the irreducible moments of arbitrary tensor-rank of the invariant single-particle distribution function. We study two cases, in the first of which the moments are taken to be irreducible with respect to the little group associated with the time-like fluid four-velocity, while in the second case they are assumed to be also irreducible with respect to a space-like four-vector orthogonal to the fluid four-velocity, which breaks the spatial isotropy to a rotational symmetry in the plane transverse to this vector. A systematic truncation and closure of the general moment equations leads, in the first case, to a theory of relativistic higher-order dissipative resistive magnetohydrodynamics. In the second case, we obtain a novel theory of dissipative resistive anisotropic magnetohydrodynamics, where the momentum anisotropy is in principle independent from that introduced by the external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper derives general equations of motion for irreducible moments of arbitrary tensor-rank from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, extending previous work by including coupling to electromagnetic fields; the authors claim that in the second case (anisotropic irreducible moments) they obtain a novel theory of dissipative resistive anisotropic magnetohydrodynamics where the momentum anisotropy is in principle independent from that introduced by the external magnetic field, though this independence is a formal feature of the derivation rather than a proven physical result.

## Method

The authors apply the method of moments to the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, deriving equations of motion for irreducible moments of the single-particle distribution function in two cases: first, moments irreducible with respect to the little group associated with the time-like fluid four-velocity (isotropic irreducible moments, IIMs); second, moments also irreducible with respect to a space-like four-vector orthogonal to the fluid four-velocity (anisotropic irreducible moments, AIMs). They perform a systematic truncation and closure of the general moment equations to obtain theories of higher-order dissipative resistive magnetohydrodynamics in the first case and dissipative resistive anisotropic magnetohydrodynamics in the second case.

## Key numbers

- The rank-two projection operator orthogonal to the fluid four-velocity, $\Delta^{\mu\nu}$, satisfies $\Delta^{\mu\nu}g_{\mu\nu}=3$
- The rank-two projection operator orthogonal to both $u^\mu$ and $l^\mu$, $\Xi^{\mu\nu}$, satisfies $\Xi^{\mu\nu}g_{\mu\nu}=2$
- The paper adopts natural Heaviside-Lorentz units with $\hbar=c=\varepsilon_0=\mu_0=k_B=1$
- The metric tensor is $g^{\mu\nu}=\text{diag}(1,-1,-1,-1)$

## Context

This work extends the recent derivation of equations of motion for isotropic irreducible moments of arbitrary tensor-rank without electromagnetic fields by de Brito and Denicol (2024), adding the coupling of IIMs to electromagnetic fields for electrically conducting fluids. It also generalizes earlier dissipative fluid-dynamical and magnetohydrodynamical theories by Denicol (2018, 2019) and Panda (2020, 2021), and builds upon the framework of leading-order anisotropic fluid dynamics developed by Florkowski (2010), Ryblewski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Molnar (2016). The results enable the construction of higher-order resistive and dissipative MHD theories as well as dissipative and resistive anisotropic MHD for systems such as heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical plasmas.
