# Formation and Decay of Chiral Condensates in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss the various physics aspects of hypothetical, coherent excitations of the pion field caused by spontaneously generated local distortions of the chiral order parameter. Such distortions, which may occur in the expansion of a hot fireball created in relativistic nuclear collisions, may be influenced or even triggered by the interaction with strong electromagnetic fields through the axial anomaly. We suggest that semiperipheral collisions may provide the best environment for the formation of these disoriented chiral condensates.

# Formation and Decay of Chiral Condensates in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs), coherent excitations of the pion field arising from local distortions of the chiral order parameter, may form during the expansion of a hot fireball in relativistic nuclear collisions, and their formation may be influenced or triggered by strong electromagnetic fields through the axial anomaly. The paper suggests that semiperipheral collisions provide the best environment for DCC formation, but notes that the effect of the anomaly is numerically small and that the ideal scenario for domain growth lies between full quenching and annealing.

## Method
The author analyzes DCC formation using the linear sigma model, where the chiral order parameter field follows an equation of motion with exponential growth for long-wavelength modes when fluctuations are small compared to the pion decay constant. The decay of the coherent state is studied via the Lame equation for pion modes coupled to a coherent radial (sigma) oscillation, leading to a squeezed state of coherent pion pair excitations. Numerical simulations of the linear sigma model with a schematic initial kick from the axial anomaly are performed to study the effect on neutral pion production.

## Key numbers
- Domain size from sigma model phenomenology: approximately 0.5 fm for lambda = 20.
- Anomaly kick strength parameter a_n in the range 0.05–0.1 for gamma = 100, corresponding to nuclear collisions at RHIC.
- The probability distribution for the neutral pion fraction R = N(pi^0)/N(pi) from a coherent I=0 state is P(R) = 1/(2*sqrt(R)), with average value <R> = 1/3.
- Charge correlations in the large-N limit: C_00 → 4/5, C_0+ → -2/5, C_++ → 1/5.
- The enhancement of neutral pions from the anomaly effect is concentrated at small momenta (k < 250 MeV, as shown in the momentum integration limit used in simulations).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for DCCs developed by Bjorken (1992) and Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), who proposed that coherent isosinglet pion excitations could occur spontaneously during the de-excitation of a region where chiral symmetry has been restored. The paper builds on numerical calculations by Asakawa, Huang, and Wang (1995) showing that three-dimensional expansion of a hot region is close to ideal conditions for DCC formation. The new contribution is the inclusion of the axial anomaly effect, following work by Muller and Minakata (1996), which provides a bias toward neutral pion production in semiperipheral collisions. The results enable experimental searches for DCC signatures at RHIC and LHC by suggesting that the neutral-to-charged pion ratio may exceed 1/3 at low momenta when averaged over many events.
