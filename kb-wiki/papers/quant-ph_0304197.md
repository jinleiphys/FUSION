# Effective Hamiltonian and unitarity of the S matrix

> The properties of open quantum systems are described well by an effective Hamiltonian ${\cal H}$ that consists of two parts: the Hamiltonian $H$ of the closed system with discrete eigenstates and the coupling matrix $W$ between discrete states and continuum. The eigenvalues of ${\cal H}$ determine the poles of the $S$ matrix. The coupling matrix elements $\tilde W_k^{cc'}$ between the eigenstates $k$ of ${\cal H}$ and the continuum may be very different from the coupling matrix elements $W_k^{cc'}$ between the eigenstates of $H$ and the continuum. Due to the unitarity of the $S$ matrix, the $\TW_k^{cc'}$ depend on energy in a non-trivial manner, that conflicts with the assumptions of some approaches to reactions in the overlapping regime. Explicit expressions for the wave functions of the resonance states and for their phases in the neighbourhood of, respectively, avoided level crossings in the complex plane and double poles of the $S$ matrix are given.

# Effective Hamiltonian and unitarity of the S matrix

## Key claim

The coupling matrix elements between resonance states and the continuum are generically energy dependent, not constant as assumed in some statistical approaches to nuclear reactions in the overlapping regime; this energy dependence follows directly from the unitarity of the S matrix and becomes important when the distance in energy between resonance states is smaller than the sum of their widths.

## Method

The author derives the energy dependence of the coupling coefficients $\tilde W_k^{cc'}$ between resonance states and continuum by starting from a unitary S matrix in the one-channel case, first for two resonance states and then generalized to N states. The wave functions of resonance states near avoided level crossings in the complex plane are analyzed using the bi-orthogonal eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian $\mathcal{H} = H_{QQ} + H_{QP} G_P^{(+)} H_{PQ}$, which describes the open quantum system after embedding it into the continuum of decay channels.

## Key numbers

- For two resonance states with equal widths $\tilde\Gamma_1 = \tilde\Gamma_2$, the coupling coefficients $\tilde W_k$ vanish at the critical energy $E^{\rm cr} = (\tilde E_1 + \tilde E_2)/2$, where the S matrix has a double pole.
- At a double pole of the S matrix ($\tilde E_1 = \tilde E_2 \equiv \tilde E_d$, $\tilde\Gamma_1 = \tilde\Gamma_2 \equiv \tilde\Gamma_d$), the S matrix contains a quadratic term: $S = 1 - 2i\tilde\Gamma_d/(E - \tilde E_d + \frac{i}{2}\tilde\Gamma_d) - \tilde\Gamma_d^2/(E - \tilde E_d + \frac{i}{2}\tilde\Gamma_d)^2$.
- At a triple pole of the S matrix, the coupling coefficients are $\tilde W_n = \tilde\Gamma_n/3$, in contrast to $\tilde W_n = 0$ at a double pole.
- For isolated resonances, $A_k = 1$ in the normalization condition $\langle \tilde\Phi_k|\tilde\Phi_k\rangle = A_k \ge 1$, and the standard relation $\Gamma_k = \sum_c |\gamma_k^c|^2$ holds.
- In the overlapping regime, the partial widths lose their physical meaning since $A_k > 1$.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach (1958) formulation of nuclear reactions with two subspaces (Q for discrete states, P for scattering states) and the unified description of nuclear structure and reactions reviewed in Rotter (2001). It directly contradicts the assumption of energy-independent coupling parameters made in statistical approaches to nuclear reactions such as Guhr et al. (1998), Schanz et al. (2001), and Fedorov et al. (1998). The results enable understanding of nonlinear terms in the S matrix at high level density, the phenomenon of resonance trapping (where $N-K$ states decouple from the continuum while $K$ become short-lived), and the interpretation of phase jumps observed in quantum dot experiments by Heiblum et al. (2002).
