# Axial anomaly effect on three-quark and five-quark singly heavy baryons

> Effects of the $U(1)_A$ axial anomaly on the mass spectrum of singly heavy baryons (SHBs) is studied in terms of the chiral effective theory based on the chiral linear representation for light flavors. We consider SHBs made of both three quarks ($Qqq$) and five quarks ($Qqqq\bar{q}$). For the three-quark SHBs we prove that the inverse mass hierarchy for the negative-parity $Λ_c$ and $Ξ_c$ is realized only when the $U(1)_A$ anomaly is present. For the five-quark SHBs, in contrast, it is found that the $U(1)_A$ anomaly does not change the mass spectrum at the leading order, and accordingly their decay properties induced by emitting a pseudoscalar meson are not affected by the anomaly. Moreover, taking into account small mixings between the three-quark and five-quark SHBs, we find that the observed $Ξ_c$ excited state, either $Ξ_c(2923)$ or $Ξ_c(2930)$, can be consistently regarded as a negative-parity SHB that is dominated by the five-quark component. We also predict a new negative-parity five-quark dominant $Λ_c$, whose mass is around $2700$ MeV and the decay width is of order a few MeV, which provides useful information for future experiments to check our description.

## Key claim

The inverse mass hierarchy for negative-parity three-quark singly heavy baryons (where $\Xi_c$ becomes lighter than $\Lambda_c$) is realized only when the $U(1)_A$ axial anomaly is present; for five-quark singly heavy baryons, the anomaly does not change the mass spectrum at leading order.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian based on the linear representation of $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ chiral symmetry for light flavors, incorporating both three-quark ($Qqq$) and five-quark ($Qqqq\bar{q}$) singly heavy baryons. They included terms that break the $U(1)_A$ axial symmetry (the $g'_1$, $g'_2$, and $\mu'_1$ terms) to represent the anomaly, and analyzed mass spectra and one-pseudoscalar-meson decay widths by expanding the meson field $\Sigma$ around its vacuum expectation value. Mixing between three-quark and five-quark states was then introduced to describe observed excited baryons.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state masses used as inputs: $M[\Lambda_c^{[3]}(+)] = 2286$ MeV and $M[\Xi_c^{[3]}(+)] = 2470$ MeV
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV; kaon decay constant: $f_K = 111$ MeV; flavor symmetry breaking parameter: $A = 1.38$
- Predicted mass of a new negative-parity five-quark dominant $\Lambda_c$: around $2700$ MeV, with decay width of order a few MeV
- Theoretical predictions from other models cited: $M[\Lambda_c^{[3]}(-)] = 2890^*$ MeV (quark model, Yoshida 2015) and $M[\Xi_c^{[3]}(-)] = 2765^*$ MeV (diquark-heavy-quark potential model, Kim 2020)
- For the three-quark scenario at those theoretical masses: partial decay width of $120$ MeV for $\Lambda_c^{[3]}(-) \to \Lambda_c^{[3]}(+)\eta$ and $264$ MeV for $\Xi_c^{[3]}(-) \to \Xi_c^{[3]}(+)\pi$
- Experimental total decay widths of $\Xi_c(2930)^+$: $15\pm9$ MeV; $\Xi_c(2930)^0$: $10.2\pm1.4$ MeV
- $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle: $\theta_P = -11.3^\circ$

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory approach for singly heavy baryons previously developed by Harada et al. (2019), who first identified the inverse mass hierarchy for three-quark states, and by Suenaga et al. (2021), who proposed the five-quark picture for Roper-like baryons such as $\Lambda_c(2765)$ and $\Xi_c(2970)$. The analysis shows that the observed $\Xi_c(2923)$ or $\Xi_c(2930)$ cannot be a pure three-quark negative-parity state (which would have decay widths of hundreds of MeV) but can be consistently described as a five-quark dominant state with small three-quark mixing. The prediction of a new five-quark dominant $\Lambda_c$ near 2700 MeV with a narrow width provides a target for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHC, KEK, and SLAC.
