# Weak interaction axial form factors of the octet baryons in nuclear medium

> We study the axial-vector and the induced pseudoscalar form factors associated with the weak transitions between the octet baryon members in nuclear medium, using a covariant constituent quark model. We extend previous calculations of the axial transition form factors from the vacuum (free space) to the nuclear medium (symmetric nuclear matter). The extension of the model to the nuclear medium takes into account the modifications of the properties of hadrons in the medium (masses and coupling constants), as determined by the quark-meson coupling model. The axial-vector ($G_A$) and the induced pseudoscalar ($G_P$) form factors are evaluated for different values of the nuclear density $ρ$ in terms of the square transfer momentum $q^2= -Q^2$. We conclude that, in general, the $G_A$ and $G_P$ form factors are reduced in the nuclear medium. The reduction is stronger for light baryons and high densities. The medium modifications are milder for the heavier octet baryons, particularly at large $Q^2$. The calculations presented here can be used to estimate the cross sections of neutrino and antineutrino scattering with nucleus, and neutrino and antineutrino scattering with hyperons bound to a nucleus, as well as those in the cores of compact stars.

## Key claim

The axial-vector (\(G_A\)) and induced pseudoscalar (\(G_P\)) form factors for weak transitions between octet baryon members are reduced in symmetric nuclear matter compared to vacuum, with the reduction being stronger for lighter baryons and higher nuclear densities, while heavier octet baryons show milder modifications, particularly at large momentum transfer \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors extended a covariant constituent quark model, previously calibrated for vacuum using lattice QCD data and experimental nucleon axial form factors, to symmetric nuclear matter by incorporating medium modifications of hadron masses and baryon-meson couplings via the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The axial-vector and induced pseudoscalar form factors were calculated for various nuclear densities \(\rho\) as functions of the squared momentum transfer \(q^2 = -Q^2\), using a decomposition into bare valence quark contributions and a phenomenological SU(3) parametrization of the meson cloud contribution.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of normal nuclear matter is \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The experimental nucleon axial-vector coupling in vacuum is \(G_A(0) = 1.2723 \pm 0.0023\).
- The dipole mass parameter \(M_A\) for the nucleon axial form factor varies between 1.03 and 1.07 GeV depending on the method, with a central value of \(M_A \simeq 1.05\) GeV.
- Experimental values for \(G_A(0)\) for other octet transitions include: \(\Lambda \to p\) at \(-0.879 \pm 0.018\), \(\Sigma^- \to n\) at \(0.340 \pm 0.017\), \(\Xi^- \to \Lambda\) at \(-0.306 \pm 0.061\), and \(\Xi^0 \to \Sigma^+\) at \(1.22 \pm 0.05\).
- The probability of the \(\ket{N \pi}\) meson cloud component in the nucleon is estimated as \(1 - Z_N \approx 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of axial transition form factors for octet baryons in vacuum by Ramalho et al. (2023) to the nuclear medium, using the QMC model framework developed by Guichon et al. and applied to electromagnetic form factors by Tsushima et al. (2022) and Cheoun et al. (2013). The results enable estimates of cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering with nuclei and with hyperons bound in nuclear matter, as well as applications to the cores of compact stars.
