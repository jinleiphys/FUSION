# Helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions in constituent quark models

> Helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions of the nucleon are derived from the overlap representation of generalized parton distributions using light-cone wave functions obtained in constituent quark models. Results from two different quark models are used also to study the angular momentum sum rule and the spin asymmetry in polarized electron scattering.

## Key claim

Helicity-dependent generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be derived from the overlap representation using light-cone wave functions obtained from constituent quark models, but the approach is limited to the valence-quark region (ξ ≤ x̄ ≤ 1) and therefore results are only reliable where valence quarks dominate.

## Method

The authors compute helicity-dependent GPDs H̃^q and Ẽ^q from the overlap representation of light-cone wave functions, which are obtained by transforming instant-form constituent quark model wave functions via Melosh rotations. Two different constituent quark models are employed: the relativistic hypercentral (HYP) model and the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) model, both restricted to the three-quark Fock state. The resulting GPDs are used to evaluate the angular momentum sum rule and the spin asymmetry A₁ in polarized electron scattering, with Q² evolution applied to compare with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- In the GBE model at ξ=0 and t=0, the integrated quark spin contributions are Σ^u = 0.79 and Σ^d = -0.18
- In the HYP model at ξ=0 and t=0, the integrated quark spin contributions are Σ^u = 0.61 and Σ^d = -0.15
- The integrated orbital angular momentum contributions in the GBE model are L^u = 0.065 and L^d = 0.13
- The integrated orbital angular momentum contributions in the HYP model are L^u = 0.13 and L^d = 0.14
- The axial coupling constants from the GBE model are g^u_A = 0.79 and g^d_A = -0.18
- The axial coupling constants from the HYP model are g^u_A = 0.61 and g^d_A = -0.15
- After Q² evolution to Q² = 1-10 GeV², the hypercentral model predicts L_q ranging from 0.04 to 0.014, while the GBE model predicts L_q from -0.03 to -0.06
- The gluon angular momentum J_g after evolution ranges from 0.20 to 0.25 for both potential models

## Context

This work extends the method of Boffi, Pasquini, and Traini (2003) from unpolarized GPDs to helicity-dependent GPDs, completing the analysis of observable GPDs within constituent quark models. The results are in qualitative agreement with the chiral quark-soliton model of Penttinen et al. (2000), and the evolved angular momentum predictions agree with QCD sum rules (J_g ~ 0.25) and lattice calculations (J_g = 0.20 ± 0.07). The approach enables comparison with polarized electron scattering data and provides a framework for understanding the quark orbital angular momentum contribution to the nucleon spin, though it is limited by the neglect of higher Fock states and sea quarks.
