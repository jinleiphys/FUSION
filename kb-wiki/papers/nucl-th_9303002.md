# Generalized $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$ Annihilation Potentials for $\bar{p}p$ Decay into Two Mesons based on a Simple Quark Model

> Within the quark model a generalization is proposed of the commonly used annihilation potential to describe antiproton-proton annihilation into two mesons, the so-called $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$ mechanisms. This generalized potential treats the two mechanisms in a more symmetric way, has additional angular dependence, and results in an expanded set of selection rules.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized form of the commonly used \(^3P_0\) and \(^3S_1\) annihilation potentials for antiproton-proton decay into two mesons, which treats both mechanisms more symmetrically by allowing momentum transfer in the \(^3P_0\) mechanism (previously neglected), resulting in additional angular dependence and an expanded set of selection rules compared to standard treatments.

## Method

The authors construct a non-local transition potential for \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+\) within a simple quark model, using Gaussian wavefunctions for nucleons and mesons with size parameters \(\alpha = 2.8\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\beta = 3.23\ \text{fm}^{-2}\). They derive analytic expressions for the \(^3P_0\) (vacuum) and \(^3S_1\) (one-gluon) mechanisms by integrating out unconstrained quark and antiquark coordinates, obtaining potentials that depend on the relative \(\bar{p}p\) coordinate \(\vec{r}\), the relative two-meson coordinate \(\vec{r}'\), and the spin operator \(\vec{\sigma}\). The total annihilation potential is taken as \(V_{\text{ann}} = V(^3P_0) + \lambda V(^3S_1)\), where \(\lambda\) is a free parameter for the relative strength of the two mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon size parameter \(\alpha = 2.8\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), giving a nucleon radius of 0.60 fm.
- Meson size parameter \(\beta = 3.23\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), giving a meson radius of 0.48 fm.
- For the generalized \(^3P_0\) potential: \(A = -1.80\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(B = -5.59\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(C = -1.21\ \text{fm}^{-2}\).
- For the standard \(^3P_0\) potential (without momentum transfer): \(A' = -1.40\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(B' = -4.68\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(C' = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the standard quark-model treatment of \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation into two mesons, which previously used asymmetric forms of the \(^3P_0\) and \(^3S_1\) potentials as reviewed by Dover, Gutsche, Maruyama, and Faessler (1992). The generalized potential allows the \(^3P_0\) mechanism to contribute to multiple angular momentum states (\(\ell_{\pi\pi} = 0, 2, 4, \ldots\) and \(\ell_{pp} = 1, 3, 5, \ldots\)) rather than only \(\ell_{\pi\pi}=0\) and \(\ell_{pp}=1\) as in earlier work by Le Yaouanc, Oliver, Pene, and Raynal (1973). This enables future calculations of branching ratios for various \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation channels and can be extended to include strange mesons or baryons such as \(\bar{\Lambda}\Lambda\).
