# The eta-photon transition form factor

> The eta-photon transition form factor is evaluated in a formalism based on a phenomenological description at low values of the photon virtuality, and a QCD-based description at high photon virtualities, matching at a scale $Q_{0}^{2}$. The high photon virtuality description makes use of a Distribution Amplitude calculated in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Pauli-Villars regularization at the matching scale $Q_{0}^{2}$, and QCD evolution from $Q_{0}^{2}$ to higher values of $Q^{2}$. A good description of the available data is obtained. The analysis indicates that the recent data from the BaBar collaboration on pion and eta transition form factor can be well reproduced, if a small contribution of twist three at the matching scale $Q_{0}^{2}$ is included.

## Key claim

The eta-photon transition form factor can be well reproduced using a formalism that matches a low-virtuality phenomenological description to a high-virtuality QCD-based description, provided a small contribution of higher twist (twist three) is included at the matching scale \(Q_{0}^{2}\).

## Method

The authors calculate the eta transition form factor using a low-energy description from the CLEO parametrization for \(Q^{2} < Q_{0}^{2}\) and a QCD-based description for \(Q^{2} > Q_{0}^{2}\), matched at \(Q_{0}^{2} = 1\ \text{GeV}^{2}\). The high-virtuality description uses distribution amplitudes for the \(q\) and \(s\) flavors computed in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Pauli-Villars regularization, evolved via QCD evolution equations, and includes a Radyushkin-type mass term \(M\) and a higher-twist term \(C_{3}\) in the form factor expression.

## Key numbers

* The matching scale is set to \(Q_{0}^{2} = 1\ \text{GeV}^{2}\) with \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 0.226\ \text{GeV}\).
* The eta decay constants used are \(f_{\eta}^{q} = (0.828 \pm 0.019) f_{\pi}\) and \(f_{\eta}^{s} = (-0.848 \pm 0.042) f_{\pi}\), with \(f_{\pi} = 131\ \text{MeV}\).
* The NJL model parameters used are \(\Lambda = 740\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_{u} = 275\ \text{MeV}\), and \(m_{s} = 435\ \text{MeV}\).
* The Gegenbauer coefficients at \(Q_{0}^{2} = 1\ \text{GeV}^{2}\) are \(a_{2}^{q} = 0.134\), \(a_{4}^{q} = 0.352\), \(a_{2}^{s} = 0.377\), and \(a_{4}^{s} = 0.245\).
* The higher-twist parameter \(C_{3}\) takes values \(1.02 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{GeV}^{3}\), \(2.04 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{GeV}^{3}\), and \(3.06 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{GeV}^{3}\), corresponding to 10%, 20%, and 30% contributions at \(Q_{0}^{2}\).
* The mass parameter \(M\) varies between 487 MeV and 638 MeV depending on the \(C_{3}\) value.

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously applied to the pion transition form factor in Noguera (2010) to the eta meson, using an SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Pauli-Villars regularization. The results are compared with data from the BaBar collaboration (2011) and CLEO collaboration (Gronberg 1997), and the Gegenbauer coefficients are compared with those from Kroll (2010), Agaev (2010), and Brodsky (2011). The approach enables a unified description of pseudoscalar meson transition form factors and tests the role of higher-twist contributions in reproducing high-virtuality data.
