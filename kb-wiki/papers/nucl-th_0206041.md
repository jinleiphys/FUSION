# Many-body Systems Interacting via a Two-body Random Ensemble: average energy of each angular momentum

> In this paper, we discuss the regularities of energy of each angular momentum $I$ averaged over all the states for a fixed angular momentum (denoted as $\bar{E}_I$'s) in many-body systems interacting via a two-body random ensemble. It is found that $\bar{E}_I$'s with $I \sim I_{min}$ (minimum of $I$) or $I_{max}$ have large probabilities (denoted as ${\cal P}(I)$) to be the lowest, and that ${\cal P}(I)$ is close to zero elsewhere. A simple argument based on the randomness of the two-particle cfp's is given. A compact trajectory of the energy $\bar{E}_I$ vs. $I(I+1)$ is found to be robust. Regular fluctuations of the $P(I)$ (the probability of finding $I$ to be the ground state) and ${\cal P}(I)$ of even fermions in a single-$j$ shell and boson systems are found to be reverse, and argued by the dimension fluctuation of the model space. Other regularities, such as why there are 2 or 3 sizable ${\cal P}(I)$'s with $I\sim I_{min}$ and ${\cal P}(I) \ll {\cal P}(I_{max})$'s with $I\sim I_{max}$, why the coefficients $C$ defined by $<\bar{E}_I >$=$CI(I+1)$ is sensitive to the orbits and not sensitive to particle number, are found and studied for the first time.

# Many-body Systems Interacting via a Two-body Random Ensemble: average energy of each angular momentum

## Key claim

The probabilities that the average energy of a given angular momentum I (denoted as $\bar{E}_I$) is the lowest among all I values are large only when I is near the minimum or maximum possible angular momentum, and are close to zero elsewhere; this regularity is robust across fermion and boson systems, but the authors note that the relationship between these probabilities (${\cal P}(I)$) and the ground-state probabilities ($P(I)$) is not straightforward, particularly for odd-A systems where the two quantities behave very differently.

## Method

The authors computed $\bar{E}_I$ by averaging over all states of fixed angular momentum I for many-body systems interacting via a two-body random ensemble (TBRE), using 1000 random runs for each system. They studied fermions in single-j shells (up to j=31/2) and two-j shells, as well as d-boson, sd-boson, and sdg-boson systems, and analyzed the coefficients $\bar{\alpha}_I^J$ derived from two-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp's) to predict ${\cal P}(I)$ without diagonalizing the TBRE Hamiltonian. They also introduced a "moment of inertia" ${\cal J}$ defined by $\langle \bar{E}_I \rangle = C I(I+1)$ with ${\cal J} = (2C)^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- For 4 fermions in a j=9/2 shell, the predicted ${\cal P}(I)$ values from the simple approach (${\cal P}(I)={\cal N}_I/N_m$ with $N_m=2N-1$) are "reasonably consistent" with those obtained by diagonalizing a TBRE Hamiltonian over 1000 runs.
- The ${\cal P}(I_{max})$ values are "quite stable" at approximately 28-35% across systems.
- For fermions in a single-j shell, ${\cal P}(I_{max}-2)$ of fermions and boson systems, and ${\cal P}(I_{max}-1)$ of fermions in a many-j shell, are "drastically smaller" than those of the $I_{max}$ state(s), though still sizable at approximately 5-15%.
- For fermions in a single-j shell, d-boson and sd-boson systems, $\sqrt{{\cal J}}$ is fitted by $\sqrt{{\cal J}} = 1.42 j$ (taking j=2 for d-boson and sd-boson systems).
- For 4 fermions in a j=15/2 shell, the ${\cal J}$ obtained from the random cfp approach is 9.96, from the simplified approximation is 11.18, and from diagonalizing a TBRE Hamiltonian is 10.30.
- For d- and sd-boson systems, the estimated ${\cal J} \sim 7/(c_4 - c_2) \sim 7.95$, giving $\sqrt{{\cal J}} \sim 2.82$, consistent with the fitted value of 2.67.

## Context

This work extends the study of random interactions in many-body systems following the discovery by Johnson et al. (Johnson1) that 0+ ground states dominate in even fermion systems with a two-body random ensemble. The authors build on their own previous work (Zhao et al., Arima et al.) where they studied $P(I)$ probabilities in two steps, and on the geometric chaoticity approach of Mulhall, Zelevinsky, and Volya (zelevinsky), who proposed an $I(I+1)$ formula for energies. The present paper shows that the $I(I+1)$ behavior applies to $\langle \bar{E}_I \rangle_{min}$ and $\langle \bar{E}_I \rangle_{max}$ rather than to yrast energies, and that the "moment of inertia" ${\cal J}$ is nearly independent of particle number but sensitive to the orbital angular momentum j, a regularity not captured by previous schemes. The results enable a statistical understanding of angular momentum distributions in random-interaction many-body systems without full diagonalization.
