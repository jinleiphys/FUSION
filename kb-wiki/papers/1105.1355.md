# Shock waves in strongly coupled plasmas II

> In a recent paper we have analyzed the AdS/CFT duals to shock waves propagating in the N=4 plasma. Here we study further properties of the system. In the gravity description we consider the properties of the dual black holes, showing in particular that they are stationary black holes with expanding horizons. This is possible because the horizon is not compact; in the fluid, this corresponds to the situation when entropy is being produced and carried away to infinity. We also consider shocks in dimensionalities d other than four and find that, for plasmas whose duals are given by asymptotically AdS spaces, the exponential tail of the shock on the supersonic side shrinks as gamma^(-2/d) as the velocity approaches the speed of light (the Lorentz factor gamma goes to infinity). This generalizes the behavior gamma^(-1/2) we have found previously for d=4. Finally, we consider corrugations of the shock front and show that the shock is stable under such perturbations. There are, however, long lived modes, excitations of which describe generation of sound by the shock wave, the energy for this being provided by the incoming fluid.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' previous AdS/CFT analysis of shock waves in strongly coupled N=4 plasma to general spacetime dimensions d, finding that the exponential tail of the shock on the supersonic side scales as gamma^(-2/d) as velocity approaches the speed of light, generalizing the previously found gamma^(-1/2) behavior for d=4.

## Method

The authors study shock waves in conformal plasmas using the AdS/CFT correspondence, analyzing the dual gravity description of boosted black holes in asymptotically AdS spaces. For weak shocks, they use first-order hydrodynamics with viscous corrections to resolve the shock profile. For strong shocks, they perform linearized analysis around uniformly boosted black holes by solving the linearized Einstein equations for sound channel perturbations, imposing infalling boundary conditions at the horizon and Dirichlet conditions at the AdS boundary to determine the quasinormal mode eigenvalues q(v) that give the penetration depth.

## Key numbers

- For d=3, the quasinormal mode eigenvalue at v=0 is iq = -2.1486, corresponding to the lowest glueball mass in the dimensionally reduced theory
- For d=3 at the speed of sound v=1/sqrt(2), iq=0; for small deviations iq = 4.24264 * delta_v = 3*sqrt(2) * delta_v
- For d=3 at v_f = sqrt(3/4), iq = 0.86602 = sqrt(3)/2, matching the analytical result
- For d=3 in the ultrarelativistic limit, iq = 1.019 * gamma^(2/3)
- For d=4 in the ultrarelativistic limit, iq = 1.895 * gamma^(1/2)
- For d=6 at v=0, the lowest glueball mass is iq = -2.703
- For d=6 at the speed of sound v=1/sqrt(5), iq=0; for small deviations iq = 6.7082 * delta_v = 3*sqrt(5) * delta_v
- For d=6 in the ultrarelativistic limit, iq = 3.62 * gamma^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of shock waves in N=4 plasma (Khlebnikov 2010) to general dimensions d, including the physically relevant cases d=3 (ABJM Chern-Simons theory dual to M-theory on AdS4 x S7) and d=6 (theory on M5 branes). The results for transport coefficients build on the work of Bhattacharyya (2008) for conformal fluid dynamics in general dimensions. The analysis enables determination of shock penetration depths for strongly coupled plasmas with AdS duals, and shows that the scaling in the ultrarelativistic limit is universal for all such theories, determined by the near-boundary region of the dual geometry.
