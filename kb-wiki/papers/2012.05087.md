# A new boson approach for the wobbling motion in even-odd nuclei

> A triaxial core rotating around the middle axis, i.e. 2-axis, is cranked around the 1-axis, due to the coupling of an odd proton from a high j orbital. Using the Bargmann representation of a new and complex boson expansion of the angular momentum components, the eigenvalue equation of the model Hamiltonian acquires a Schrödinger form with a fully separated kinetic energy. From a critical angular momentum, the potential energy term exhibits three minima, two of them being degenerate. Spectra of the deepest wells reflects a chiral-like structure. Energies corresponding to the deepest and local minima respectively, are analytically expressed within a harmonic approximation. Based on a classical analysis, a phase diagram is constructed. It is also shown that the transverse wobbling mode is unstable. The wobbling frequencies corresponding to the deepest minimum are used to quantitatively describe the wobbling properties in $^{135}$Pr. Both energies and e.m. transition probabilities are described.

# A new boson approach for the wobbling motion in even-odd nuclei

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new boson expansion of angular momentum components, using the Bargmann representation, allows the eigenvalue equation of a particle-triaxial rotor Hamiltonian to be brought into a Schrödinger form with fully separated kinetic energy, and that from a critical angular momentum the potential energy exhibits three minima, two of which are degenerate, with the deepest wells reflecting a chiral-like structure. The hedge is that the transverse wobbling mode is shown to be unstable despite the assumption that the middle axis has the maximal moment of inertia, and the formalism is applied only to a single nucleus, $^{135}$Pr.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian describing a triaxial core with an odd proton rigidly coupled in the principal plane, assuming the maximal moment of inertia is along the 2-axis and expanding the linear term in the angular momentum component $I_2$ to first order. They introduce a new boson representation of the angular momentum components using Jacobi elliptic functions and the Bargmann mapping to boson operators, which transforms the eigenvalue equation into a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with a potential that depends on the total angular momentum I and the coupling parameters. A harmonic approximation around the potential minima yields analytic expressions for the wobbling frequencies, and a classical dequantization procedure provides a phase diagram based on the Hessian of the classical energy function.

## Key numbers

- The potential energy V(q) exhibits three deep symmetric wells with degenerate minima and two local minima at $q = \pm 2K$; the local minima appear only for total angular momentum $I > 19/2$.
- The phonon energy $\omega$ for the deepest well shows a maximum at approximately $\theta = -\pi/6$, while the phonon energy $\omega'$ for the local minimum shows a maximum at approximately $\theta = -5\pi/6$; the two frequencies are equal for about $\theta = \pi/2$.
- The harmonic frequency for the deepest minimum (case A1) is $\omega = 2\sqrt{(1+v)(u+v)I^2}$ with $v = v_0/I$, and for the local minimum (case A2) is $\omega = 2\sqrt{(1-v)(u-v)I^2}$.
- The phase diagram separatrices are given by the equation $(1-u)(1-v^2)(v^2-u^2)(v^2-u) = 0$, which defines boundaries between different nuclear phases in the parameter space (u, v).
- The numerical application uses moments of inertia ${\cal J}_1:{\cal J}_2:{\cal J}_3 = 95:100:85\ \hbar^2\text{MeV}^{-1}$ for $^{135}$Pr with odd particle angular momentum $j = 13/2$ and angles $\theta = -80^\circ$ and $\theta = 100^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the study of wobbling motion in even-odd nuclei, which has been observed experimentally in several triaxial nuclei including $^{161,163,165,167}$Lu, $^{167}$Ta, $^{135}$Pr, $^{187}$Au, $^{133}$La, $^{105}$Pd, and $^{133}$Ba. The formalism differs from previous boson descriptions using Holstein-Primakoff expansions (Holstein and Primakoff, 1940) and Dyson expansions (Dyson, 1956) by introducing a new boson representation based on Jacobi elliptic functions and the Bargmann mapping, which avoids the truncation and normal-ordering issues of the Holstein-Primakoff approach. The paper also addresses the ongoing debate about the existence of transverse wobbling (Frauendorf, 2018; Raduta et al., 2018) by showing that the transverse mode is unstable in this framework. The results enable future quantitative descriptions of wobbling bands and electromagnetic transitions in even-odd triaxial nuclei, with the formalism applied here specifically to $^{135}$Pr.
