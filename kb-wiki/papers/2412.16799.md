# The southern shore of the island of inversion studied via quasi-free scattering

> Neutron-rich nuclei exhibit a variety of intriguing features associated with nuclear structure evolution, deformation, and other phenomena. Particularly interesting is the region in the chart of nuclides around Z = 12 and N = 20, commonly referred to as "Island of Inversion", which is profoundly influenced by these features. Recent cutting-edge experiments performed at SAMURAI/RIBF have investigated the structure of the most neutron-rich O and F isotopes, including 27,28O and 28-30F, utilizing quasi-free scattering and invariant-mass spectroscopy techniques. This experimental campaign manifests the breakdown of the N = 20 magicity for O and F isotopes, placing them within the "Island of Inversion", as is discussed in this review article. The results are further supported by theoretical analyses employing state-of-the-art shell-model and ab-initio calculations. These nuclei serve as corner stones for the study of weak binding and continuum coupling, deformation, and halo formation. Signatures for the establishment of a superfluid regime in 28O and 29F are found. Future experimental and theoretical studies are needed to examine details.

## Key claim

This review argues that quasi-free scattering experiments at SAMURAI/RIBF have unequivocally demonstrated that the neutron N=20 magic number is not restored in the most neutron-rich fluorine and oxygen isotopes, extending the "Island of Inversion" down to Z=8, though the author notes that future experimental and theoretical studies are needed to examine details.

## Method

The experimental campaign employed quasi-free nucleon knockout reactions (p,pN) on radioactive-ion beams at the SAMURAI setup at RIKEN's RIBF, using the MINOS liquid hydrogen target coupled with a time-projection chamber for vertex tracking, combined with the NeuLAND demonstrator and NEBULA neutron detector arrays for invariant-mass spectroscopy. The reactions populated unbound nuclear systems including 27,28O and 28-30F, which were reconstructed via their neutron decay channels, with the 28O measurement requiring detection of four coincident fast neutrons in the five-body decay channel 29F(p,2p)28O → 24O + 4n.

## Key numbers

- 28F ground state at 198(6) keV with width Γ = 180 ± 40 keV, exhibiting ℓ=1 character with spectroscopic strength C²S = 0.40(6)
- 30F ground state resonance at E_rel = 472 ± 58(stat.) ± 33(sys.) keV, with single-neutron separation energy S_n = -472 keV
- 28O ground state resonance at 0.46^{+0.05}_{-0.04}(stat.) ± 0.02(sys.) MeV, with single-particle cross section 1.36^{+0.16}_{-0.14}(stat.) ± 0.13(sys.) mb and C²S = 0.48^{+0.05}_{-0.06}(stat.) ± 0.05(sys.)
- 27O ground state at 1.09 ± 0.04(stat.) ± 0.02(sys.) MeV from four-body decay analysis
- Single-neutron detection efficiency of the NeuLAND demonstrator: 27% (for ΔE > 5 MeVee) at 250 MeV
- Two-neutron reconstruction efficiency: no more than 15%; three-neutron: no more than 2%; four-neutron: no more than 0.4%
- Relative energy resolution σ ~ 100 keV at E_rel = 1 MeV, better than 30 keV at 0.1 MeV
- 29F secondary beam intensity: approximately 90 counts per second from 48Ca primary beam at ~600 pnA
- Improvement in resolution by factor of about four and luminosity by about factor of 1,000 compared to similar past experiments (Christian 2012)

## Context

This work extends the Island of Inversion concept, previously established for Mg (Z=12) and Al (Z=13) isotopes, down to Z=9 (fluorine) and Z=8 (oxygen), contradicting earlier measurements of 28F by Christian (2012) that suggested traditional shell structure. The results enable benchmarking of state-of-the-art shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U-MIX interaction and ab-initio approaches including chiral Effective Field Theory, Gamow Shell Model, and Coupled Cluster methods, with the 27,28O nuclei serving as anchors for theoretical approaches based on χEFT. The findings also support the interpretation of 29F as a two-neutron halo nucleus with extended root-mean-square halo radius of 6.6 fm (Bagchi 2020) and reveal signatures for establishment of a superfluid regime in 28O and 29F.
