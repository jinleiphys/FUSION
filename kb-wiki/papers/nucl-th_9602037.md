# Many Body Corrections to Nuclear Anapole Moment

> The many body contributions to the nuclear anapole moment of $^{133}$Cs, $^{205}$Tl, $^{207,209}$PB, and $^{209}$Bi from the core polarization are calculated in the random-phase approximation with the effective residual interaction. Strong reduction of a valence nucleon contribution was found provided by the core polarization effects. The contribution of the core particles to the anapole moment compensates this reduction to large extent keeping the magnitude of nuclear anapole moment close to its initial single particle value.

# Many Body Corrections to Nuclear Anapole Moment

## Key claim

The many-body corrections from core polarization, calculated in the random-phase approximation, reduce the valence nucleon contribution to the nuclear anapole moment by approximately a factor of 2, but the contribution from parity-nonconserving effects in the core states largely compensates this reduction, keeping the total anapole moment close to its initial single-particle value. The compensation is not exact, with the overall renormalization within approximately 10% of the independent-particle value.

## Method

The authors calculated the core polarization contributions to the nuclear anapole moment using the random-phase approximation (RPA) with an effective residual spin-spin interaction. They solved integral equations for dressed effective vertices describing the renormalization of the bare anapole operators, treating the weak nucleon-nucleon interaction in first-order perturbation theory. The calculations were performed for the spin part of the anapole moment, as well as for the convection current, spin-orbit, and contact current contributions, using a harmonic oscillator basis with spin-orbit potentials for the nuclei 133Cs, 205Tl, 207Pb, 209Pb, and 209Bi.

## Key numbers

- For 205Tl, the total dimensionless anapole moment constant κtot varies between 0.313 and 0.380 when the isoscalar spin-spin constant g0 is varied in the interval 0.2 ≤ g0 ≤ 0.8.
- For 205Tl, κtot varies between 0.450 and 0.327 when the isovector spin-spin constant g0' is varied in the interval 0.5 ≤ g0' ≤ 1.5.
- The analytical estimate for the ratio of the induced weak interaction contribution to the single-particle anapole moment for a valence proton is δas/as ≈ 4.6 Z/A.
- The analytical estimate for the first-order core polarization correction to the single-particle spin anapole moment gives a ratio of approximately -2 relative to the zero-order term.
- The spin-orbit residual interaction constant used is Uls^{pn} = Uls^{np} = 134 MeV·fm^5.
- The normalization constant for the spin-spin interaction is C = 300 MeV·fm^3, with constants g' = 1.01 and g = 0.63.

## Context

This work extends the independent-particle model calculations of the nuclear anapole moment performed by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984) and by Bouchiat and Piketty (1988, 1991), which did not include the dominant core polarization effects. The authors note that previous work by Bouchiat and Piketty accounted for configuration mixing but only partially, and the contribution from induced parity-nonconserving interaction in the nuclear core was not discussed before. The results enable more reliable predictions for atomic parity-nonconservation experiments sensitive to nuclear spin, particularly for 133Cs, 205Tl, and 209Bi, where the anapole moment dominates the spin-dependent parity-violating electron-nucleus interaction.
