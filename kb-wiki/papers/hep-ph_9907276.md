# One-loop analysis of the reaction pi N to pi pi N

> Single pion production off nucleons is studied in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in the chiral expansion. Using total and some older differential cross section data to pin down the low-energy constants, most of the recent differential cross sections and angular correlation functions can be described as well as total cross sections at higher energies. We show that the contributions from the one loop graphs are essentially negligible and that the dominant terms at second and third order are related to pion-nucleon scattering graphs with one pion added. We also discuss the possibility of extracting the pion-pion S-wave scattering lengths from the unpolarized data.

# One-loop analysis of the reaction πN → ππN

## Key claim

The one-loop contributions to single pion production off nucleons in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory are essentially negligible at third order in the chiral expansion, and the dominant terms at second and third order are related to pion-nucleon scattering graphs with one pion added. The authors show that most recent differential cross sections and angular correlation functions can be described without invoking large loop effects, contrary to earlier claims by Beringer (1992) and Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) that third-order contributions were large and essential.

## Method

The authors performed a complete third-order calculation of the reaction πN → ππN using the minimal effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), including all one-loop graphs and tree graphs from the dimension one, two, and three Lagrangians. They fixed the low-energy constants (LECs) using total cross section data and older differential cross section data, with additional constraints from elastic pion-nucleon scattering as determined by Fettes, Meißner, and Steininger (1998), and compared their results to recent TRIUMF and CHAOS data for five physical channels: π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n, π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p, π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n, π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n, and π⁻p → π⁰π⁻p.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-symmetric threshold kinetic energy for the incoming pion is T_π^{thr,iso} = 170.71 MeV, with channel-dependent shifts of +1.68, -5.95, +1.68, -10.21, and -5.95 MeV for the five channels respectively.
- The charged pion mass used is M_π = 139.57 MeV and the proton mass is m = 938.27 MeV.
- The threshold normalization factor is N_1^{thr} = 1.006.
- The low-energy constants ℓ_i from the meson Lagrangian are related to the conventional \bar{ℓ}_i via \bar{ℓ}_i = 16 α_i π² ℓ_i^r(λ) - 2 ln(M_π/λ), with α_1 = 6, α_2 = 3, α_3 = -4, α_4 = 1.
- Four combinations of counterterms (\bar{d}_1 + \bar{d}_2, \bar{d}_3, \bar{d}_5, \bar{d}_{14} - \bar{d}_{15}) were already fixed from pion-nucleon scattering data, and \bar{d}_{18} from the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends the threshold analysis of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1997) to energies above threshold, and improves upon the relativistic tree-level calculation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) by including loop effects and a consistent power counting scheme. It contradicts the earlier third-order calculation by Bernard, Moinester, and Zylberstejn (1995), which claimed large third-order contributions but did not fix the LECs properly and did not provide explicit formulae for the amplitudes. The calculation enables a systematic test of chiral perturbation theory convergence in pion production and allows extraction of the pion-pion S-wave scattering lengths from unpolarized data, addressing the long-standing question of whether the ππ interaction can be accessed via the Chew-Low technique (Kermani, 1998) rather than directly from threshold amplitudes.
