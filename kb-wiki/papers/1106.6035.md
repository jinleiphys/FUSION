# Current response, structure factor and hydrodynamic quantities of a two- and three-dimensional Fermi gas from the operator-product expansion

> We apply the operator-product expansion to determine the asymptotic form of the current response of a Fermi gas in two and three dimensions. The leading-order term away from the one-particle peak is proportional to a quantity known as the contact, the coefficient of which is determined exactly. We also calculate the dynamic structure factor and the high-frequency tails of the spectral viscosities as a function of the scattering length. Our results are used to derive certain sum rules for the viscosities.

## Key claim

The operator-product expansion (OPE) determines the asymptotic form of the current response of a Fermi gas in two and three dimensions, with the leading-order term away from the one-particle peak proportional to the contact; the result holds for any value of the scattering length, not only at unitarity.

## Method

The OPE was applied to the time-ordered current correlator, matching Wilson coefficients using one-particle states (for bilinear operators) and two-particle states with zero energy and momentum (for the contact operator). The Feynman rules included the two-particle scattering amplitude in both 2D and 3D, and the calculation accounted for infrared divergences that cancel between diagrams. The resulting expressions for the spin-symmetric and spin-antisymmetric current response were evaluated in closed form for large momentum q and frequency ω, with the scaling variables x = q²/2mω and a⁻¹/√(mω) kept fixed.

## Key numbers

- In 3D, the spin-symmetric transverse response: Im G_T^s(q,ω)/C = - (3/(4π m q²)) √(mω - q²/4) + [2(mω)² + (mω - q²/2)²]/(4π m² ω q³) ln[(mω + q√(mω - q²/4))/|mω - q²/2|]
- In 3D, the spin-antisymmetric transverse response: Im G_T^a(q,ω)/C = - (1/(4π m q²)) √(mω - q²/4) + [2(mω)² - (mω - q²/2)²]/(4π m² ω q³) ln[(mω + q√(mω - q²/4))/|mω - q²/2|]
- The imaginary part of the response functions vanishes for x > 2, corresponding to the two-particle threshold ω > q²/4m
- The contact operator scaling dimension is Δ_C = 4 in both 2D and 3D

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach of Braaten, Kang, and Platter (2008, 2010) for universal relations in Fermi gases, and generalizes the asymptotic dynamic structure factor results of Son and Thompson (2010) and Goldberger and Rothstein (2010) to finite scattering length and to two dimensions. The results for the high-frequency tails of the spectral shear and bulk viscosities reproduce and generalize the work of Taylor and Randeria (2010) to finite scattering length and to 2D. The calculated current response functions are directly relevant to proposed experiments using artificial gauge fields to probe the transverse current response in ultracold Fermi gases.
