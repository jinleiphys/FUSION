# Hidden-charm pentaquark states $qqqc\bar{c}$ $(q = u,d)$ in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> In this work, we systematically calculate the spectrum of hidden-charm pentaquark states $qqqc\bar{c}$ $(q = u,d)$ in the chiral SU(3) quark model, which has been quite successful in reproducing consistently the energies of octet and decuplet baryon ground states, the binding energy of deuteron, and the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering phase shifts and mixing parameters for partial waves with total angular momentum up to $J=6$. The Hamiltonian contains the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential, the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) potential, the confinement potential, and the kinetic energy of the system. We solve the Schrödinger equation by use of the variational method. It is found that the masses of all the experimentally observed $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4380)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ states are much overestimated, indicating that these states are not compact pentaquark states in the chiral SU(3) quark model. All other $qqqc\bar{c}$ $(q = u,d)$ states are found to lie much above the corresponding baryon-meson thresholds, and thus are not suggested as stable pentaquark states due to their fall-apart decays. A detailed comparison of the results with those obtained in the OGE model and the chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model is further given.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, which successfully describes light-baryon and nucleon-nucleon scattering data, overestimates the masses of all experimentally observed \(P_c\) states (\(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4380)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\)), indicating that these states are not compact pentaquark states within this model; all other \(qqqc\bar{c}\) states lie above their baryon-meson thresholds and are therefore not stable against fall-apart decays.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the \(qqqc\bar{c}\) (\(q = u,d\)) system using a variational method with Gaussian trial wave functions. The Hamiltonian included kinetic energy, a linear confinement potential, a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential, and a Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) potential between light quarks only; parameters for light quarks were taken from a previous fit to baryon masses and \(NN\) scattering (Huang 2018), while parameters involving the charm quark were fixed by fitting masses of known charmed mesons and baryons from the Particle Data Group (2022). Total wave functions were constructed as products of color, spin, flavor, and spatial components, with the spatial part built from four Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

* The calculated masses of the lowest-lying states with color configuration \(|{\bf 1}_c{\bf 1}_c\rangle\) lie exactly at the corresponding baryon-meson thresholds; for example, the \(IJ^P = \frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}^-\) state with configuration \(\{(qqq)^{{\bf 1}_c}_{1/2}[c\bar{c}]^{{\bf 1}_c}_0\}^{{\bf 1}_c}_{1/2}\) has a mass of 3916 MeV.
* The masses of all states with color configuration \(|{\bf 8}_c{\bf 8}_c\rangle\) are much higher than the experimental \(P_c\) masses; for instance, the lowest \(|{\bf 8}_c{\bf 8}_c\rangle\) state with \(IJ^P = \frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}^-\) has a mass of 4517 MeV, compared to the experimental \(P_c(4312)\) at 4311.9 MeV.
* The root mean square radius \(\sqrt{\langle \xi_4^2 \rangle}\) (distance between the \(qqq\) and \(c\bar{c}\) clusters) for \(|{\bf 1}_c{\bf 1}_c\rangle\) states is infinite, confirming they are free scattering states; for \(|{\bf 8}_c{\bf 8}_c\rangle\) states this radius is finite, e.g., 0.465 fm for the \(\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}^-\) state with configuration \(\{(qqq)^{{\bf 8}_c}_{3/2}[c\bar{c}]^{{\bf 8}_c}_1\}^{{\bf 1}_c}_{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously applied to light baryons and \(NN\) scattering by Huang (2018), to hidden-charm pentaquark systems. The results contradict earlier quark-model studies (Weng 2019, Deng 2022, Li 2023) that supported a compact pentaquark interpretation of the \(P_c\) states, and instead agree with the conclusion of Hiyama (2018) that no compact pentaquark states exist in the 4300–4500 MeV region. The findings also differ from the chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model and the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model, which the authors compare explicitly; the chiral SU(3) model predicts higher masses for the \(|{\bf 8}_c{\bf 8}_c\rangle\) states than those models. This work suggests that the observed \(P_c\) states are more likely molecular states, as proposed in one-boson-exchange model studies by Chen (2019).
