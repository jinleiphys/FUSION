# Microscopic description of fission in superheavy nuclei with the parametrization D1M$^{*}$ of the Gogny energy density functional

> The constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation, based on the recent parametrization D1M$^{*}$ of the Gogny energy density functional, is used to describe fission in 435 superheavy nuclei. The Gogny-D1M$^{*}$ parametrization is benchmarked against available experimental data on inner and second barrier heights, excitation energies of the fission isomers and half-lives in a selected set of Pu, Cm, Cf, Fm, No, Rf, Sg, Hs and Fl nuclei. Results are also compared with those obtained with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional. A detailed study of the minimal energy fission paths is carried out for isotopic chains with atomic numbers 100 $\le$ Z $\le$ 126 including very neutron-rich sectors up to around 4 MeV from the two-neutron driplines. Single-particle energies, ground state deformations, pairing correlations, two-nucleon separation energies and barrier heights are also discussed. In addition to fission paths, the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework provides collective masses and zero-point quantum rotational and vibrational energies. Those quantities are building blocks within the Wentzel-Kramer-Brillouin formalism employed to evaluate the systematic of the spontaneous fission half-lives t$_\mathrm{SF}$. The competition between spontaneous fission and $α$-decay is studied, through the computation of the $α$-decay half-lives t$_\mathrmα$ using a parametrization of the Viola-Seaborg formula. From the comparison with the available experimental data and the results obtained with other theoretical approaches, it is concluded that D1M$^{*}$ represents a reasonable starting point to describe fission in heavy and superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The D1M$^{*}$ parametrization of the Gogny energy density functional, which modifies the symmetry energy slope to achieve neutron star masses of 2 solar masses, provides a reasonable starting point for describing fission in heavy and superheavy nuclei, though the calculated inner barrier heights systematically overestimate experimental values by up to 4.87 MeV even after accounting for triaxial effects.

## Method

The constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation with the Gogny-D1M$^{*}$ energy density functional was used to compute fission paths for 435 even-even superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers 100 ≤ Z ≤ 126, extending to neutron-rich nuclei with two-neutron separation energies S₂ₙ ∼ 4 MeV. The HFB equations were solved in an axially symmetric deformed harmonic oscillator basis with optimized oscillator lengths, and the spontaneous fission half-lives were computed using the Wentzel-Kramer-Brillouin (WKB) formalism with collective masses from both the adiabatic time-dependent HFB (ATDHFB) and generator coordinate method (GCM) schemes. Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using the Viola-Seaborg formula with parameters from Dong (2005).

## Key numbers

- Inner barrier heights B_I for D1M$^{*}$ range from 7.47 MeV (²³⁴U) to 11.02 MeV (²⁴⁴Cm), compared to experimental values of 4.80 MeV and 6.18 MeV respectively
- The largest deviation between D1M$^{*}$ inner barrier height and experiment is 4.87 MeV for ²⁴⁶Cm (calculated 10.87 MeV vs experimental 6.00 MeV)
- Triaxial reduction of inner barriers ranges from a few hundred keV to approximately 3 MeV, with values in parentheses such as 7.79 MeV (triaxial) vs 9.14 MeV (axial) for ²³⁸U
- Excitation energies of fission isomers E_II for D1M$^{*}$ range from 2.18 MeV (²⁵²Cf) to 3.15 MeV (²⁴⁴Pu), compared to experimental values of 2.55 MeV and 2.20 MeV respectively
- Second barrier heights B_II for D1M$^{*}$ range from 5.79 MeV (²⁴⁰Cm) to 9.03 MeV (²⁴⁴Pu), compared to experimental values of 5.00 MeV and 4.85 MeV respectively
- The D1M$^{*}$ parametrization has a symmetry energy slope L = 43.18 MeV, compared to L = 24.83 MeV in D1M
- The Gogny-D1M functional achieves an rms for binding energies of 0.798 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous fission studies using the Gogny-D1M functional (Rodriguez-Guzman 2015, 2016) to the new D1M$^{*}$ parametrization, which was introduced to address the inability of earlier Gogny parametrizations to reproduce neutron star masses of about 2 solar masses as required by astrophysical observations. The study covers a wider range of superheavy nuclei than previous Gogny-based calculations, including very neutron-rich regions relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star merger scenarios, and enables systematic comparisons between spontaneous fission and alpha-decay half-lives for nuclei near the limits of nuclear stability.
