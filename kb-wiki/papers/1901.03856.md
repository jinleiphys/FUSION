# First results of EPOS-HQ model for open heavy flavor production in A-A collission at RHIC and LHC

> The EPOS-HQ model is a framework designed for predicting heavy-flavor observables both in p-p, p-A and A-A collisions. It results from the integration of the MC$@_s$HQ and EPOS3 codes and should supersede predictions made with each of these two. In this contribution, we briefly present the new ingredients of the EPOS-HQ model as well as some preliminary results. From the comparison with experimental data, we derive some direction of improvement for the near future.

# First results of EPOS-HQ model for open heavy flavor production in A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The EPOS-HQ model, integrating MC@sHQ and EPOS3 codes, produces preliminary results for open heavy flavor observables that show good agreement with experimental elliptic flow data but reveal a pronounced bump in the nuclear modification factor around 3 GeV that was not present in the previous MC@sHQ+EPOS2 framework, indicating that the initial charm-quark spectrum from EPOS3 is much softer than the previous FONLL fit for transverse momenta below 5 GeV.

## Method
The authors integrated the MC@sHQ heavy quark energy loss model (based on pQCD with running alpha_s, including both elastic and radiative processes) with the EPOS3 event generator, which provides a 3D+1 viscous fluid dynamics description of the bulk medium and a semi-hard pomeron evolution scheme for heavy quark production including cold nuclear matter effects. Heavy flavor hadrons are produced through a mixed hadronization mechanism (fragmentation plus coalescence) and are subsequently propagated through the UrQMD afterburner where they can undergo elastic collisions.

## Key numbers
- D-meson nuclear modification factor R_pPb in p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5 TeV: approximately 0.8 for p_T greater than 5 GeV, attributed to cold nuclear matter effects
- D-meson R_AA in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5 TeV (0%-10% centrality): calibrated to ALICE data at p_T=10 GeV for both pure elastic and elastic plus radiative energy loss scenarios
- D-meson v_2 in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions: the UrQMD afterburner contributes up to a 3% increase in the elliptic flow
- Spatial diffusion coefficient D_s from the new EPOS-HQ calibration: found to be 50% larger compared to values extracted from the previous MC@sHQ+EPOS2 framework (Andronic 2016)
- Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: the flow bump from mixed hadronization is positioned at too large p_T compared to the corrected STAR data

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous MC@sHQ+EPOS2 framework (Gossiaux 2008, Andronic 2016) by integrating heavy quark production directly into the EPOS3 initial state and including viscous corrections to the fluid dynamics. The results reveal tensions with approaches that include heavy flavor hadrons in nPDF extraction procedures, and the authors identify the need to correct the initial charm-quark spectrum from EPOS3, which is much softer than their previous FONLL fit for p_T below 5 GeV. The framework enables future calibration of the energy loss model and improved predictions for heavy flavor observables at both RHIC and LHC energies.
