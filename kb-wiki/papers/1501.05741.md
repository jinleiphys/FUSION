# Extraction of Neutrino Flux from the Inclusive Muon Cross Section

> We have studied a method to extract neutrino flux from the data of neutrino-nucleus reaction by using maximum entropy method. We demonstrate a promising example to extract neutrino flux from the inclusive cross section of muon production without selecting a particular reaction process such as quasi-elastic nucleon knockout.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the maximum entropy method (MEM) can extract the neutrino flux from inclusive muon production cross section data without requiring selection of quasi-elastic events or using the free-nucleon kinematic reconstruction formula, provided an accurate theoretical cross section is known.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo data for the double differential cross section of muon production from neutrino-induced inclusive reactions on carbon-12, using a neutrino flux based on the T2K near detector spectrum modified by two-flavor oscillation parameters (Δm²₂₃ = 2.5×10⁻³ eV², sin²2θ₂₃ = 1.0, L = 295 km). They applied the maximum entropy method to invert the flux-averaged cross section relation, using the Shannon-Jaynes entropy with a default model for the neutrino flux, and integrated over the hyperparameter α to obtain the most probable flux. The theoretical cross section combined a modified Fermi-gas model with spectral function for quasi-elastic scattering and a model for pion production through Δ(1232) excitation.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino oscillation parameters used: Δm²₂₃ = 2.5×10⁻³ eV², sin²2θ₂₃ = 1.0, baseline L = 295 km
- Assumed muon energy resolution: 100 MeV
- Assumed Gaussian standard deviation for pseudo data: 10% of the flux-averaged double differential cross section value at Eμ = 1 GeV
- Pseudo data consisted of 19 points of muon energy distribution at scattering angle θμ = 10°
- Extracted flux averaged in peak region (0.28–0.42 GeV): MEM = 56.5 ± 6.5 (exact = 53.5)
- Extracted flux averaged in dip region (0.52–0.70 GeV): MEM = 5.1 ± 1.7 (exact = 3.9)
- Extracted flux averaged in region 0.72–0.82 GeV: MEM = 14.0 ± 3.7 (exact = 10.5)
- Extracted flux averaged in region 0.86–0.94 GeV: MEM = 11.6 ± 3.7 (exact = 10.9)
- Extracted flux averaged in region 1.00–1.08 GeV: MEM = 10.7 ± 1.9 (exact = 8.7)

## Context

This work extends the application of the maximum entropy method, previously used in condensed matter physics and lattice QCD (Silver, Gubernatis, Asakawa 2001), to the problem of neutrino flux extraction in long-baseline experiments. It addresses the central issue identified by Alvarez-Ruso (2014) and Garvey (2014) regarding the need to improve accuracy of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus reactions. The method offers an alternative to the standard quasi-elastic reconstruction approach and enables future work to find optimal observables for efficient flux extraction with refined neutrino-nucleus reaction models.
