# Integral identities and universal relations for solitons

> We show that any nonlinear field theory giving rise to static solutions with finite energy like, e.g., topological solitons, allows us to derive an infinite number of integral identities which any such solution has to obey. These integral identities can always be understood as being generated by field transformations and their related Noether currents. We also explain why all integral identities generated by coordinate transformations become trivial for Bogomolnyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) solitons, i.e., topological solitons which saturate a topological energy bound. Finally, we consider applications of these identities to a broad class of nonlinear scalar theories, including the Skyrme model. More concretely, we find nontrivial integral identities that can be seen as model-independent relations between certain physical properties of the solitons in such theories, and we comment on the possible connection between these new relations and those already found in the context of astrophysical compact objects. We also demonstrate the usefulness of said identities to estimate the precision of the numerical calculation of soliton observables.

## Key claim

Any nonlinear field theory that supports static, finite-energy solutions (such as topological solitons) admits an infinite family of integral identities parametrized by arbitrary functions, which can be derived from field transformations and their associated Noether currents; for BPS solitons, all identities generated by coordinate transformations become trivial.

## Method

The authors start from the static Euler-Lagrange equations for Poincare-invariant scalar field theories in d+1 dimensions, multiply these equations by arbitrary field transformations, and integrate to obtain integral identities. They focus on two classes of transformations: coordinate transformations (translations, rotations, dilatations) and target-space transformations (chiral rotations). For the Skyrme model and its generalizations, they apply these identities to derive constraints on the spin and mixed moments of inertia of skyrmions in the rigid-body approximation.

## Key numbers

- The virial (Derrick) identity derived from dilatations: \(\sum_n (n-d)E_n = 0\), where \(E_n \equiv \int d^d x \mathcal{E}_n\) and \(d\) is the spatial dimension.
- For the trace of the spin moment of inertia in the Skyrme model: \(U = 2\int d^3 x \, r^2 \left( \mathcal{E}_2 +2 \mathcal{E}_4 +3 \mathcal{E}_6 - \mathcal{F}_2 - 4 \mathcal{F}_4 -3 \mathcal{F}_6 \right)\), where \(\mathcal{E}_2, \mathcal{E}_4, \mathcal{E}_6\) are the energy density terms and \(\mathcal{F}_2, \mathcal{F}_4, \mathcal{F}_6\) are defined in equations (F246) of the paper.
- The integral identity for the spin moment of inertia is derived from the dilatation Noether current multiplied by \(x_k x_l\), leading to equation (rel-D): \(\int d^d x \left( r^2 T + 2 T_{ij}x_ix_j \right) = 0\), where \(T = \delta_{ij}T_{ij}\) is the trace of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Context

This work extends the integral identities previously derived by Bjarke (2015) for effectively one-dimensional systems to the full multi-dimensional case, and generalizes the coordinate-transformation-based identities considered by Manton (2019). The authors also note connections to the "deformation constraints" introduced by Hoyos (2020) and to similar identities for self-gravitating systems studied by Herdeiro (2021, 2022). The results enable model-independent checks of numerical precision for soliton calculations and provide universal relations between observables such as the spin moment of inertia, the rms radius of the mass distribution, and the D-term in any Lorentz-invariant scalar field theory in 3+1 dimensions.
