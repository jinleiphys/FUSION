# Squeezed back-to-back correlation between bosons and antibosons with different in-medium masses in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We derive the formulas of the squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) between a boson and antiboson with different in-medium masses in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The influence of the in-medium mass difference between a boson and antiboson on the SBBC is investigated. We calculate the SBBC functions of $D$-meson pairs for the hydrodynamic sources described by the VISH2+1 code for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =200$~GeV. Our results indicate that the SBBC strengths of $D^+D^-$ and $D^0{\bar D}^0$ are different if there are charge-dependent in-medium interactions.

## Key claim

The squeezed back-to-back correlation (SBBC) between a boson and antiboson depends on both the in-medium interaction that is the same for particle and antiparticle (mass shift \(\Delta m_1\)) and the interaction that is different for them (energy split \(2\delta'\)); the SBBC strengths of \(D^+D^-\) and \(D^0\bar D^0\) pairs are different if charge-dependent in-medium interactions exist, but the sign and magnitude of the difference depend on the specific values of \(\Delta m_1\) and \(\delta'\).

## Method

The authors derived general formulas for the SBBC function of a boson-antiboson pair with different in-medium masses using a Bogoliubov transformation that diagonalizes the Hamiltonian of a complex scalar field with an energy split \(2\delta'\) between the boson and antiboson. They then calculated the SBBC functions for \(D\)-meson pairs using both a simple analytic source model (Gaussian spatial distribution, exponential temporal decay, fixed freeze-out temperature) and the VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamic code with MC-Glb fluctuating initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the simple source model with freeze-out temperature 150 MeV and \(\Delta t = 2\) fm, the mass of the \(D\)-meson in a hadronic medium is approximately \(3 \sim 5\) MeV/\(c^2\) smaller than its free value (based on prior FMFK framework results).
- For \(D^+D^-\) pairs with momentum 0.8 GeV/\(c\) and \(\Delta m_1 = -3\) MeV/\(c^2\), the SBBC strength factor \(B(\mathbf{k})\) decreases with increasing \(\delta'\) (energy split); for momentum 1.2 GeV/\(c\) and \(\Delta m_1 = -5\) MeV/\(c^2\), \(B(\mathbf{k})\) increases with increasing \(\delta'\).
- For the VISH2+1 hydrodynamic sources at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV Au+Au collisions with 0%–80% centrality, the SBBC function for nonzero \(\delta'\) is smaller than for zero \(\delta'\) when \(\Delta m_1 = -3\) MeV, and slightly larger when \(\Delta m_1 = -5\) MeV.
- The SBBC functions for peripheral collisions (40%–80% centrality) are higher than those for central collisions (0%–80% centrality), because the averaged source lifetime is smaller for peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous SBBC studies (Asakawa and Csorgo, 1996; Padula, 2006; Zhang, 2015) that assumed equal in-medium masses for boson and antiboson, by deriving formulas that allow for different masses. The results enable using SBBC of heavy-meson pairs (specifically \(D^+D^-\) vs. \(D^0\bar D^0\)) as a probe to distinguish charge-dependent from charge-independent in-medium interactions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior calculations of \(D\)-meson in-medium masses in the FMFK framework (Furnstahl, 2004; 2006) and hydrodynamic simulations with VISH2+1 (Shen, 2011).
