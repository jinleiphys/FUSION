# Medium modifications of baryon properties in nuclear matter and hypernuclei

> We study the medium modifications of baryon properties in nuclear many-body systems, especially in $Λ$ hypernuclei. The nucleon and the $Λ$ hyperon are described in the Friedberg-Lee model as nontopological solitons which interact through the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons. The quark degrees of freedom are explicitly considered in the model, so that the medium effects on baryons could be investigated. It is found that the model can provide reasonable descriptions for nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and $Λ$ hypernuclei. The present model predicts a significant increase of the baryon radius in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The Friedberg-Lee (FL) model, when applied to nuclear matter and Λ hypernuclei, predicts a significant increase in baryon radius in the nuclear medium, with the magnitude of the swelling depending strongly on the chosen parameter set (set A yields much larger expansion than set B). The model provides a reasonable description of Λ hypernuclei without adjustable parameters, but its simple treatment of spin-dependent interactions fails to reproduce the repulsive Σ-nucleus potential suggested by experiment.

## Method

The nucleon and Λ hyperon are described as nontopological solitons in the Friedberg-Lee model, where three quarks couple to a phenomenological scalar field φ. The baryons interact through self-consistent exchange of σ, ω, and ρ mesons treated as classical mean fields, with mesons coupling directly to quarks inside the baryons. A hybrid treatment is used: effective baryon masses are obtained at the quark level by solving coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations, while baryon Fermi motion is treated at the hadron level. For finite Λ hypernuclei, the local density approximation replaces meson mean fields by their value at the baryon center.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon rms radius in free space: r_N = 0.83 fm (used to constrain both parameter sets)
- Nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV; Λ mass: M_Λ = 1115.7 MeV (used to constrain both parameter sets)
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 190 MeV
- At normal nuclear matter density with parameter set B: r_N*/r_N ~ 1.13 (13% increase in nucleon radius); r_Λ*/r_Λ ~ 1.09 (9% increase in Λ radius)
- At normal nuclear matter density with parameter set A: r_N*/r_N ~ 1.30 (30% increase); r_Λ*/r_Λ ~ 1.23 (23% increase)
- Λ potential depth in saturated nuclear matter: around -24 MeV for set A, around -30 MeV for set B
- Σ potential depth in saturated nuclear matter with set B: around -28 MeV
- Ξ potential depth in saturated nuclear matter with set B: around -14 MeV
- At center of 209_ΛPb with set B: difference between scalar and vector potentials is about -56 MeV for neutron, about -29 MeV for Λ hyperon

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the FL model to nuclear matter and finite nuclei (Wen 2008) by incorporating flavor SU(3) to study Λ hypernuclei. The approach is similar in spirit to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and the quark mean-field (QMF) model, but uses the FL model's dynamical soliton description rather than the MIT bag or constituent quark models. The predicted baryon radius increase (13-30% for nucleons) is much larger than the 1-3% enhancement in the QMC model (Guichon 1996) or the 5-9% increase in the QMF model (Shen 2000). The model enables self-consistent calculation of Λ hypernuclei without adjustable meson-hyperon couplings, unlike RMF models which treat these as phenomenological parameters. The failure to reproduce repulsive Σ potentials points to the need to include quark-quark hyperfine interactions, as done in recent QMC developments (Guichon 2008).
