# Kaon-Deuteron Scattering at Low Energies

> We review the experimental information on the K^+d reaction for K-meson momenta below 800 MeV/c. The data are analysed within the single scattering impulse approximation -- utilizing the Juelich kaon-nucleon model -- that allows to take into account effects due to the Fermi motion of the nucleons in the deuteron and the final three-body kinematics for the break-up and charge exchange reaction. We discuss the consistency between the data available for the K^+d -> K^+np, K^+d -> K^0pp and K^+d -> K^+d reactions and the calculations based on the spectator model formalism.

## Key claim

The paper reviews whether available $K^+d$ reaction data (for kaon momenta below 800 MeV/c) are consistent with each other and can be described by a $KN$ model that reproduces up-to-date partial wave analyses, using the single scattering impulse approximation; the authors acknowledge that multiple scattering corrections may play a role for specific kinematics, but question whether such effects are manifest given the large error bars of the data.

## Method

The authors analyzed $K^+d$ scattering data for the reactions $K^+d \to K^+np$, $K^+d \to K^0pp$, and $K^+d \to K^+d$ using the single scattering impulse approximation, which accounts for Fermi motion of nucleons in the deuteron and final three-body kinematics. The elementary $KN$ amplitudes were taken from the Jülich meson-exchange $KN$ model I, and the deuteron wave function from the charge-dependent Bonn $NN$ potential. The calculations integrated the two-body amplitude over the full three-body phase space without factorizing the reaction amplitude, performed in the deuteron rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers $K^+$-meson momenta below 800 MeV/c, where inelastic channels ($KN \to KN\pi$) contribute only about 10% at the upper end of this momentum range.
- The lowest energy $K^+d \to K^0pp$ data available are at $p_K = 252$ MeV/c, which the model calculation reproduces well within the impulse approximation.
- For $K^+d$ coherent scattering, Faddeev-type calculations at kaon momenta of 110-230 MeV/c suggested multiple scattering corrections of order 10-20%, while total and elastic $K^+d$ cross sections from full Faddeev calculation and impulse approximation practically coincide for kaon momenta from 400 MeV/c onwards.
- For charge-exchange $K^+d$ scattering, double-scattering terms had no observable effect on the cross section above 252 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the $K^+d \to K^0pp$ reaction (Sibirtsev et al., 2005) to the $K^+d \to K^+np$ and $K^+d \to K^+d$ channels, using the same Jülich $KN$ model. The study is motivated by interest in the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark, as the $I=0$ $K^+N$ channel can only be accessed via kaon-deuteron scattering. The paper enables future partial wave analyses of $KN$ scattering by testing the consistency of available $K^+d$ data with the spectator model formalism, and by identifying discrepancies (such as forward-angle suppression in Glasser et al. data) that may signal failures of the impulse approximation or data evaluation ambiguities.
