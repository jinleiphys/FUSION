# Efficacy of the SU(3) scheme for ab initio large-scale calculations beyond the lightest nuclei

> We report on the computational characteristics of ab initio nuclear structure calculations in a symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) framework. We examine the computational complexity of the current implementation of the SA-NCSM approach, dubbed LSU3shell, by analyzing ab initio results for 6Li and 12C in large harmonic oscillator model spaces and SU(3)-selected subspaces. We demonstrate LSU3shell's strong-scaling properties achieved with highly-parallel methods for computing the many-body matrix elements. Results compare favorably with complete model space calculations and significant memory savings are achieved in physically important applications. In particular, a well-chosen symmetry-adapted basis affords memory savings in calculations of states with a fixed total angular momentum in large model spaces while exactly preserving translational invariance.

## Key claim

The symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) using SU(3)-selected subspaces can reproduce 98% of the complete-space NCSM binding energy for 12C at Nmax=8 while using only 0.67% of the corresponding complete model space, provided the selection is based on high-deformation and low-spin dominance along with symplectic Sp(3,R) excitations.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model calculations for 6Li and 12C using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction without renormalization and without three-nucleon forces, comparing complete M-scheme calculations (using the MFDn code) and J-scheme calculations against SA-NCSM calculations (using the LSU3shell code) in SU(3)-selected subspaces. The SA-NCSM basis states are labeled by SU(3) quantum numbers (λ,μ), orbital momentum L, proton/neutron/total intrinsic spins, and total angular momentum J, with model spaces denoted as ⟨N⊥max⟩Nmax that include all configurations up through N⊥max and a restricted subspace beyond that up through Nmax. For 12C, two selection schemes ("A" and "B") were tested at ⟨6⟩8, where the 8ħω subspace was restricted to selected deformations and spin values based on symmetry considerations.

## Key numbers

- SA-NCSM binding energy of 12C in ⟨6⟩8-B model space: 85.951 MeV, compared to complete Nmax=8 NCSM result of 87.902 MeV (98% reproduction) at ħΩ=20 MeV
- SA-NCSM ⟨6⟩8-B model space uses only 0.67% of the corresponding complete Nmax=8 model space for J=0 states
- SA-NCSM ⟨4⟩8-B model space already reproduces 96% of the Nmax=8 NCSM binding energy
- Excitation energies for 2+1, 1+1, and 4+1 states in ⟨6⟩8-B deviate from complete Nmax=8 results by 0.9%, 0.3%, and 0.4% respectively
- Ground state binding energy at Nmax=8: 87.902 MeV (complete space) vs 85.951 MeV (⟨6⟩8-B selection)
- B(E2; 2+1 → 0+gs) = 3.342 e²fm⁴ (complete Nmax=8) vs 3.301 e²fm⁴ (⟨6⟩8-B)
- Ground state point-particle matter rms radius: 2.003 fm (complete Nmax=8) vs 2.005 fm (⟨6⟩8-B)

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-adapted no-core shell method previously demonstrated for 6Li, 6He, and 8Be (Dytrych et al., 2013) to 12C, and builds on earlier findings that NCSM wavefunctions of 12C and 16O project strongly onto symmetry-adapted subspaces (Dytrych et al., 2007). The approach contrasts with the Importance-Truncated NCSM (Roth, 2007) and the no-core Monte Carlo Shell Model (Otsuka et al., 2001-2003), which use different selection strategies. The results enable access to heavier nuclei and larger model spaces essential for describing collective, deformed, and cluster substructures, while exactly preserving translational invariance within the selected subspace.
