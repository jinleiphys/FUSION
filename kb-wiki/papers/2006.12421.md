# Spin Hall effect in heavy ion collisions

> Spin Hall effect (SHE) is the generation of spin current due to an electric field, and has been observed in a variety of materials. The analogous spin Hall current can be induced by chemical potential and temperature gradient, both of which are present in hot and dense nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions. In this letter, we investigate the perspective of detecting spin Hall current experimentally. We propose to measure "directed spin flow", the first Fourier coefficients of local spin polarization of $Λ$ ($\barΛ$) hyperon, at central collisions to probe spin Hall current. To quantify induced spin current, we evaluate relevant transport coefficients using thermal field theory. We benchmark the magnitude of the induced "directed spin flow" at two representatively collisions energies, namely $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200~{\rm GeV}$ and $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=19.6~{\rm GeV}$, by employing a phenomenologically motivated freeze-out prescription. At both beam energies, the resulting "directed spin flow" ranges from $10^{-4}$ to $10^{-3}$, and is very sensitive to the rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that spin Hall current induced by temperature and chemical potential gradients in heavy-ion collisions can be detected experimentally via the "directed spin flow" of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons, but the magnitude of this signal is small (10^{-4} to 10^{-3}) and depends sensitively on the rapidity and on the details of the temperature and chemical potential profile.

## Method

The authors used linear response theory and one-loop thermal field theory to compute the transport coefficients relating spin polarization to temperature and chemical potential gradients. They then employed a phenomenologically motivated freeze-out prescription with a blastwave model, using parameters from prior measurements, to estimate the resulting azimuthal-angle-dependent spin polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons at two collision energies.

## Key numbers

* At both sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 19.6 GeV, the resulting "directed spin flow" ranges from 10^{-4} to 10^{-3}
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the kinetic freezeout temperature T_f = 89 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B,f = 22 MeV
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, T_f = 113 MeV and mu_B,f = 196 MeV
* The freezeout time was benchmarked at tau_f = 10 fm
* The transverse momentum integration used p_T,max = 5 GeV
* The rapidity integration range was 0 < y < 0.8 for the azimuthal angle dependence plots

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the well-studied vorticity-induced polarization (STAR Collaboration 2017, Becattini 2013) to include the thermally-induced spin Hall effect. The authors note that while thermal vorticity (Becattini 2007, 2013) includes temperature gradient effects, it does not account for mu/T gradient contributions. The proposed "directed spin flow" observable complements existing measurements and could provide a new probe of quantum transport in QCD matter, particularly with the planned forward rapidity programs at RHIC and LHC.
