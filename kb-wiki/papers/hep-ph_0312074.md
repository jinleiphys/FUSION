# Pentaquark Magnetic Moments In Different Models

> We calculate the magnetic moments of the pentaquark states from different models and compare our results with predictions of other groups.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of pentaquark states are calculated within four different constituent quark models (Jaffe-Wilczek, Shuryak-Zahed, Karliner-Lipkin, and Strottman), and the resulting values are highly sensitive to the assumed diquark masses and internal cluster structure, meaning that experimental measurement of pentaquark magnetic moments could help distinguish between competing theoretical models.

## Method

The authors computed pentaquark magnetic moments by treating each model's cluster decomposition (scalar diquark pairs, scalar-plus-tensor diquark pairs, diquark-triquark systems, or four-quark-plus-antiquark clusters) as a few-body system and summing the spin and orbital contributions of the constituents using standard angular-momentum coupling. For the Jaffe-Wilczek model, the two scalar diquarks carry no spin but contribute an orbital magnetic moment from their relative P-wave; for the Shuryak-Zahed model, a tensor diquark with spin 1 replaces one scalar diquark; for the Karliner-Lipkin model, a diquark and a triquark are separated by a P-wave barrier; and for the Strottman model, all four quarks and the antiquark are treated as a single cluster. Quark masses were taken as \(m_u=m_d=360\) MeV and \(m_s=500\) MeV, with diquark masses from the original model papers.

## Key numbers

* \(\Theta^+\) (udud\(\bar{s}\)) magnetic moment in Jaffe-Wilczek model: 0.08 \(\mu_N\) (set I: \(m_{ud}=720\) MeV, \(m_{us}=m_{ds}=900\) MeV) and 0.29 \(\mu_N\) (set II: \(m_{ud}=420\) MeV, \(m_{us}=m_{ds}=600\) MeV)
* \(\Theta^+\) magnetic moment in Shuryak-Zahed model: 0.23 \(\mu_N\) (using tensor diquark masses \(m_{ud}^T=570\) MeV, \(m_{us}^T=m_{ds}^T=720\) MeV)
* \(\Theta^+\) magnetic moment in Karliner-Lipkin model: 0.19 \(\mu_N\)
* \(\Xi_5^{--}\) (ddss\(\bar{u}\)) magnetic moment in Jaffe-Wilczek model: 0.116 \(\mu_N\) (set I) and -0.12 \(\mu_N\) (set II)
* \(\Xi_5^{--}\) magnetic moment in Shuryak-Zahed model: -0.17 \(\mu_N\)
* \(\Xi_5^{--}\) magnetic moment in Karliner-Lipkin model: -0.43 \(\mu_N\)
* \(\Theta^+\) mass from QCD sum rules by the same group: \((1.55\pm 0.15)\) GeV (Zhu, 2003)
* \(\Xi_5^{--}\) mass reported by NA49 Collaboration: \((1.862\pm 0.002)\) GeV/c\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier QCD sum rule calculation of the \(\Theta^+\) mass (Zhu, 2003) and light-cone QCD sum rule extraction of its magnetic moment (Huang, 2003) by providing model-dependent predictions that can be compared with future photo-production and electro-production experiments. The calculations directly contrast the predictions of the Jaffe-Wilczek (2003), Shuryak-Zahed (2003), Karliner-Lipkin (2003), and Strottman models, showing that the magnetic moment values differ significantly between models and even between different parameter choices within the same model, which could help resolve the ongoing debate about the \(\Theta^+\) parity and internal structure.
