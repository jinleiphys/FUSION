# Gluon splitting in a shockwave

> The study of azimuthal correlations in particle production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions provides direct information about high gluon density effects, like gluon saturation, in the nuclear wavefunction. In the kinematical conditions for proton-lead collisions at the LHC, the forward di-hadron production is dominated by partonic processes in which a gluon from the proton splits into a pair of gluons, while undergoing multiple scattering off the dense gluon system in the nucleus. We compute the corresponding cross-section using the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory, which enables us to include the effects of multiple scattering and gluon saturation in the leading logarithmic approximation at high energy. This opens the way towards systematic studies of angular correlations in two-gluon production, similar to previous studies for quark-gluon production in the literature. We consider in more detail two special kinematical limits: the "back-to-back correlation limit", where the transverse momenta of the produced gluons are much larger than the nuclear saturation momentum, and the "double parton scattering limit", where the two gluons are produced by a nearly collinear splitting occurring prior to the collision. We argue that saturation effects remain important even for relatively high transverse momenta (i.e. for nearly back-to-back configurations), leading to geometric scaling in the azimuthal distribution.

## Key claim

The paper computes the cross-section for gluon splitting into two gluons in the background of a dense nuclear target (a "shockwave") using the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory, and argues that saturation effects remain important even for relatively high transverse momenta where the produced gluons are nearly back-to-back, leading to geometric scaling in the azimuthal distribution.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for the partonic process \(gA \rightarrow ggX\) using Feynman rules in the projectile light-cone gauge \(A^+_a=0\), where the target field is treated as a classical colour field (shockwave) and parton scattering is described by eikonal Wilson lines in the adjoint representation. The cross-section is expressed in terms of target correlators built from up to 8 fundamental Wilson lines, which are then simplified in the large-\(N_c\) limit to involve only colour dipoles and quadrupoles, and analyzed in two special kinematical limits: the back-to-back correlation limit and the double parton scattering limit.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation momentum scales as \(Q_s^2(A,x) \propto A^{1/3}/x^\lambda\) with \(\lambda \simeq 0.25\), and is expected to be of order a few GeV for the kinematics of interest.
* For p–Pb collisions at the LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV) with semi-hard transverse momenta \(k_{i\perp} \sim 2\) GeV and rapidities \(y_i \simeq 4\), one finds \(x_p \simeq 0.02\) and \(x_A \simeq 5 \times 10^{-5}\).
* The transverse momentum imbalance between the final gluons in the back-to-back limit scales as \(|\mathbf{k}_1 + \mathbf{k}_2| \sim Q_s \ll k_{i\perp}\).
* The width of the azimuthal distribution around \(\Delta\Phi = \pi\) in the high-momentum regime (\(k_{1\perp} \simeq k_{2\perp} \gg Q_s\)) exhibits geometric scaling, depending only on the ratio \(Q_s(A,x_A)/k_{1\perp}\).

## Context

This work extends previous CGC calculations for forward di-hadron production in p–A collisions, which focused on quark-initiated processes (\(qA \rightarrow qgX\)) by Jalilian-Marian (2004), Marquet (2007), and Albacete (2010), to the gluon-initiated channel (\(gA \rightarrow ggX\)) that becomes dominant at the smaller \(x_p\) values accessible at the LHC. The result generalizes the corresponding formula for quark splitting obtained by Marquet (2007) and provides the theoretical foundation for systematic studies of angular correlations in two-gluon production, enabling comparison with LHC data from the first p–Pb run and with the TMD factorization approach at small \(x\) as developed by Dominguez (2011).
