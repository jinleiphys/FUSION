# The Perturbed Static Path Approximation at Finite Temperature: Observables and Strength Functions

> We present an approximation scheme for calculating observables and strength functions of finite fermionic systems at finite temperature such as hot nuclei. The approach is formulated within the framework of the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and goes beyond the static path approximation and the RPA by taking into account small amplitude time-dependent fluctuations around each static value of the auxiliary fields. We show that this perturbed static path approach can be used systematically to obtain good approximations for observable expectation values and for low moments of the strength function. The approximation for the strength function itself, extracted by an analytic continuation from the imaginary-time response function, is not always reliable, and we discuss the origin of the discrepancies and possible improvements. Our results are tested in a solvable many-body model.

## Key claim

The perturbed static path approximation (PSPA) improves upon the static path approximation (SPA) for calculating observables and low moments of strength functions at finite temperature by incorporating small-amplitude time-dependent fluctuations around each static auxiliary field configuration, but the approximation for the strength function itself via analytic continuation from the imaginary-time response function is not always reliable.

## Method

The authors formulate the PSPA within the Hubbard-Stratonovich path integral framework, expanding the correction factor from time-dependent fluctuations to second order in the Fourier components of the auxiliary fields around each static configuration. They compute the resulting Gaussian integrals analytically, obtaining closed-form expressions for the partition function, thermal expectation values of one- and two-body observables, and the first two moments of the strength function. The method is tested against exact solutions of a solvable U(2) many-body model (a variant of the Lipkin model).

## Key numbers

- For the U(2) model with parameters ε=1 and g=10, the mean-field transition temperatures are β_c=1.61 for κ=1.5 and β_c=0.693 for κ=3.0, where κ=gχ/ε.
- The RPA frequencies Ω in the model satisfy Ω²=4ε̄²−(4gε²χ/ε̄)tanh(βε̄/2), where ε̄=√(ε²+χ²σ₀²).
- The PSPA breaks down when the magnitude of an imaginary RPA frequency crosses 2πT, i.e., when Ω²=−(2π/β)² for some static field configuration.
- For κ=0.5 there is no mean-field transition; for κ=1.5 and κ=3.0 the transition occurs at β_c=1.61 and β_c=0.693 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the perturbed static path approximation previously developed for partition functions (PSPA, introduced in works by Alhassid and collaborators in the mid-1990s) to the calculation of observables and strength functions at finite temperature. The approach goes beyond both the static path approximation (SPA) and the finite-temperature random phase approximation (RPA) by treating large-amplitude static fluctuations exactly while adding perturbative time-dependent fluctuations. The method is tested on a solvable U(2) model (a variant of the Lipkin model from Lipkin (1965)) and shows significant improvement over SPA for free energies, especially below the mean-field transition temperature. The results enable more accurate calculations of nuclear properties such as level densities and giant dipole resonance strength functions in hot nuclei, though the direct extraction of strength functions via analytic continuation remains problematic.
