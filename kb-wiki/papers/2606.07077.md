# External-Field-Assisted Muon Reactivation in Muon-Catalyzed Fusion: A Rate-Network Criterion for Reducing Alpha Sticking

> Alpha sticking is a major loss channel in deuterium--tritium muon-catalyzed fusion. We study whether an additional external-field-assisted stripping channel can reduce the residual sticking loss after conventional collisional reactivation. The external contribution is written as $R_X=f_XP_Xη_X$, where $f_X$ is the space--time overlap between the external field and the residual stuck $(αμ)^+$ population, $P_X$ is the microscopic stripping probability, and $η_X$ is the probability that the stripped $μ^-$ is returned to the $dμ/tμ\to dtμ$ fusion cycle before escape or decay. This gives $ω_S^{\rm eff}=ω_S^0(1-R_{\rm col})(1-R_X)$ and leads directly to a probability-level no-go condition, $η_X^{\rm crit}>1$, for any target improvement requiring more recycling than is probabilistically available. We construct an energy-resolved post-stripping rate network including slowing down, atomic capture, free escape, muon decay, atomic-stage loss, ordinary molecular formation, and an effective resonant $dtμ$ channel. Benchmark scans show that the useful regime is a transport window: the stripped muon must be confined and recycled efficiently. With the reference inputs used here, the best-performing scenario increases the cycle yield from $N_{\rm fus,μ}=112.6$ in the collision-only case to $N_{\rm fus,μ}=156.5$. Resonant molecular formation suppresses atomic-stage loss and broadens the high-recycling region, but it cannot compensate for prompt escape or poor field--population overlap. The rate network therefore identifies the transport and overlap conditions required for external-field-assisted reactivation to reduce residual alpha sticking.

## Key claim
External-field-assisted muon reactivation can reduce residual alpha sticking in muon-catalyzed fusion, but only if the stripped muon is confined and recycled efficiently; a probability-level no-go condition, $\eta_X^{\rm crit}>1$, excludes any target improvement requiring more recycling than is probabilistically available.

## Method
The authors constructed an energy-resolved post-stripping rate network that includes slowing down, atomic capture, free escape, muon decay, atomic-stage loss, ordinary molecular formation, and an effective resonant $dt\mu$ channel. They benchmarked this network across four transport scenarios (conservative, baseline, baseline + resonant, and optimistic) to compute the post-stripping recycling probability $\eta_X$ and the resulting cycle yield $N_{\rm fus,\mu}$.

## Key numbers
- The collision-only reference case yields an effective sticking probability $\omega_{S,{\rm col}}^{\rm eff} = 0.557\%$ and a cycle yield $N_{\rm fus,\mu} = 112.6$.
- The best-performing (optimistic) scenario increases the cycle yield to $N_{\rm fus,\mu} = 156.5$.
- The initial sticking probability used is $\omega_S^0 = 8.57 \times 10^{-3}$, and the intramolecular fusion rate is $\lambda_f = 1.15 \times 10^{12}~{\rm s}^{-1}$.
- The reference photon-field parameters include a photon energy $\hbar\omega_X = 15~{\rm keV}$ and a fluence $\Phi_X = 3.0 \times 10^{22}~{\rm photons\,cm}^{-2}$, giving a microscopic stripping probability $P_X \simeq 0.449$.

## Context
This work extends the study of alpha sticking in deuterium-tritium muon-catalyzed fusion, building on the few-body calculations of Kamimura, Kino, and Yamashita (2021) and Wu and Kamimura (2024) for microscopic inputs. It formalizes a kinetic closure criterion for external-field-assisted reactivation, a concept previously explored for x-ray lasers by Kimura and Bonasera (2008) and for radio-frequency acceleration by Mori (2021). The rate network identifies the specific transport and overlap conditions required for such an external channel to be effective, enabling future work to focus on designing fields and target geometries that satisfy these constraints.
