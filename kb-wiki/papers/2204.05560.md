# Dark matter admixed neutron star properties in the light of X-ray pulse profile observations

> The distribution of the dark matter (DM) in DM-admixed-neutron stars (DANSs) is supposed to be either a dense dark core or an extended dark halo, which is subject to the DM fraction of DANS ($f_χ$) and the DM properties, such as the mass ($m_χ$) and the strength of the self-interaction ($y$). In this paper, we perform an in-depth analysis of the formation criterion for dark core/dark halo and point out that the relative distribution of these two components is essentially determined by the ratio of the central enthalpy of the DM component to that of the baryonic matter component inside DANSs. For the critical case where the radii of DM and baryonic matter are the same, we further derive an analytical formula to describe the dependence of $f^{\rm crit}_χ$ on $m_χ$ and $y$ for given DANS mass. The relative distribution of the two components in DANSs can lead to different observational effects. We here focus on the modification of the pulsar pulse profile due to the extra light-bending effect in the case of a dark-halo existence and conduct the first investigation of the dark-halo effects on the pulse profile. We find that the peak flux deviation is strongly dependent on the ratio of the halo mass to the radius of the DM component. Lastly, we perform Bayesian parameter estimation on the DM particle properties based on the recent X-ray observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 by the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer.

# Dark matter admixed neutron star properties in the light of X-ray pulse profile observations

## Key claim

The relative distribution of dark matter and baryonic matter inside a dark-matter-admixed neutron star (DANS) is determined by the ratio of the central enthalpies of the two components, not simply by the dark matter particle mass or self-interaction strength alone. The authors derive an analytical formula for the critical dark matter fraction at which the radii of the two components coincide, and show that the modification of X-ray pulse profiles due to a dark halo depends sensitively on the ratio of halo mass to dark matter component radius.

## Method

The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for a system composed of baryonic matter (described by the NL3ωρ equation of state) and self-interacting fermionic dark matter with particle masses from MeV to GeV. They compute the mass-radius relation and density profiles for DANSs, then derive a criterion for dark core versus dark halo formation based on the central enthalpies of the two components. For the pulse profile analysis, they model radiation from two point-like spots on the baryon surface, including Doppler boost, relativistic aberration, gravitational redshift, and light bending, and compute the normalized bolometric flux as a function of rotational phase.

## Key numbers

- For a DANS with total mass 1.4 solar masses and DM fraction 0.05, light DM particles (0.1 GeV) form a dark halo while heavy DM particles (1 GeV) form a dark core, with the transition depending on the self-interaction strength parameter y.
- The critical DM fraction scales as f_crit proportional to m_chi^4 for fixed interaction strength, and as f_crit proportional to y^{-2} for y >> 1.
- For a DANS model with M_halo/R_D = 7.7 x 10^{-3}, the peak flux deviation is about 6%; for a model with M_halo/R_D = 8.0 x 10^{-5}, the deviation is only 0.05%.
- The empirical relation between peak flux deviation and M_halo/R_D is log(|ΔF_peak|/F_peak) = 0.998 log(M_halo/R_D) + 0.854 with R^2 = 0.995.
- For compact dark halos with M_halo/R_D approximately 0.01, the peak flux deviation can reach about 10%.
- Bayesian analysis using NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 yields a 90% upper limit of m_chi ≤ 1.5 GeV when assuming M_halo/R_D ≤ 10^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on DANS structure by Leung et al. (2012), Ellis et al. (2018), and Nelson et al. (2019) by providing a general criterion for dark core versus dark halo formation based on central enthalpy, rather than relying on qualitative arguments about particle mass. It also conducts the first detailed numerical investigation of dark halo effects on pulsar pulse profiles, following the suggestion by Diedrichs et al. (2022) that such effects could be important. The results enable future NICER analyses to incorporate dark matter effects when constraining neutron star masses and radii, and provide a framework for using X-ray observations to constrain dark matter particle properties.
