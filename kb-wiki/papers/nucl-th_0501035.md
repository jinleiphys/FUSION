# Ferromagnetic instabilities in neutron matter at finite temperature with the Skyrme interaction

> The properties of spin polarized neutron matter are studied both at zero and finite temperature using Skyrme-type interactions. It is shown that the critical density at which ferromagnetism takes place decreases with temperature. This unexpected behaviour is associated to an anomalous behaviour of the entropy which becomes larger for the polarized phase than for the unpolarized one above a certain critical density. This fact is a consequence of the dependence of the entropy on the effective mass of the neutrons with different third spin component and a new constraint on the parameters of the effective Skyrme force is derived in order to avoid such a behaviour.

## Key claim

The critical density for ferromagnetism in neutron matter decreases with increasing temperature when using Skyrme-type interactions, contrary to the intuitive expectation that thermal disorder would suppress magnetic order. This unexpected behavior is traced to an anomalous entropy increase in the polarized phase relative to the unpolarized phase above a certain density, arising from the dependence of entropy on the effective mass of neutrons with different spin components.

## Method

The authors study spin-polarized neutron matter at zero and finite temperature within the Hartree-Fock approximation using Skyrme effective interactions (specifically SLy4 and SkI3 parametrizations). They compute the free energy, entropy, and inverse magnetic susceptibility as functions of density, temperature, and spin polarization parameter Δ, using Fermi integrals to extend the zero-temperature formalism to finite temperature. The critical density for the ferromagnetic instability is identified from the vanishing of the inverse magnetic susceptibility and from the free energy minimum as a function of polarization.

## Key numbers

- For SLy4 at zero temperature, the critical density for magnetic instability (from susceptibility) is ρ_c = 0.60 fm^{-3}; for SkI3, ρ_c = 0.37 fm^{-3}.
- For SLy4 at zero temperature, the density where fully polarized phase has lower free energy than unpolarized is ρ_c^F = 0.71 fm^{-3}; for SkI3, ρ_c^F = 0.44 fm^{-3}.
- The entropy critical density (where polarized entropy exceeds unpolarized entropy) is ρ_c^S = 0.15 fm^{-3} for SLy4 and ρ_c^S = 0.08 fm^{-3} for SkI3, independent of temperature.
- In the classical limit, the condition for normal entropy behavior is m*(Δ=1)/m*(Δ=0) < 2^{2/3}; for SLy4 this gives ρ_c^{cla} = 0.15 fm^{-3}, for SkI3 ρ_c^{cla} = 0.08 fm^{-3}.
- Eleven of the twenty-seven Skyrme forces that passed neutron-star tests have a vanishing denominator in the effective mass condition below 0.5 fm^{-3}, indicating they were not designed for highly spin-asymmetric matter.

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature studies of ferromagnetic instabilities in neutron matter with Skyrme forces (Navarro and collaborators, 1984; Kutschera and Wojcik, 1994) to finite temperature, relevant for protoneutron star evolution. It contrasts with microscopic calculations using realistic interactions: Monte Carlo simulations (Fantoni and collaborators, 2001) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Vidaña and collaborators, 2002) exclude ferromagnetic transitions up to five or six times saturation density. The results enable a new constraint on Skyrme force parameters to avoid the anomalous entropy behavior, and have implications for neutrino transport in protoneutron stars, where spin correlations affect mean free paths (Navarro and collaborators, 1999).
