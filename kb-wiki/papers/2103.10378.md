# Revisit the Chiral Magnetic Effect Expectation in Isobaric Collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200$ GeV have been conducted at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider to circumvent the large flow-induced background in searching for the chiral magnetic effect (CME), predicted by the topological feature of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Considering that the background in isobar collisions is approximately twice that in Au+Au collisions (due to the smaller multiplicity) and the CME signal is approximately half (due to the weaker magnetic field), we caution that the CME may not be detectable with the collected isobar data statistics, within $\sim$2$σ$ significance, if the axial charge per entropy density ($n_5/s$) and the QCD vacuum transition probability are system independent. This expectation is generally verified by the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model. While our estimate provides an approximate "experimental" baseline, theoretical uncertainties on the CME remain large.

## Key claim

The authors caution that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) may not be detectable with the collected isobar collision data at RHIC, with only approximately 2σ significance, if the axial charge per entropy density (n₅/s) and the QCD vacuum transition probability are system-independent between isobar and Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors performed a back-of-the-envelope estimation of the CME signal significance in isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using Au+Au collisions as a baseline. They compared the expected flow-induced background (which scales inversely with mass number A) and the CME signal (which scales with magnetic field strength B ∝ A¹/³) between the systems. They then validated their estimates using the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model, which implements anomalous fluid dynamics to describe fermion current evolution in the quark-gluon plasma, using the VISH2+1 hydrodynamics framework with event-wise fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Background in isobar collisions is approximately twice that in Au+Au collisions (factor of ~1.9 from AVFD), due to the smaller multiplicity (NPOI ≈ 160 in isobar vs. ~320 in Au+Au for 20-60% centrality)
- CME signal in isobar collisions is approximately half that in Au+Au collisions (factor of ~1/1.9 from AVFD), due to the weaker magnetic field (B scales as A¹/³)
- The inclusive Δγ measured in 20-60% central Au+Au collisions is on the order of Δγ^AuAu ≈ 2×10⁻⁴
- The statistical uncertainty on the Δγ difference between isobar systems is estimated as σ ≈ 10⁻⁶ (with Nev^iso ≈ 10⁹ events per species in 20-60% centrality)
- The CME signal difference between isobar systems is estimated as Δ(Δγ_CME^iso) ≈ 6×10⁻⁶, assuming a CME fraction f_CME ≈ 10%
- At the same n₅/s value, the ratio r = (r_bkg / r_CME) ≈ 3.6 from AVFD, implying a 1.7σ measurement
- If n₅/s in isobar collisions is 1.5 times that in Au+Au collisions (as AVFD indicates with glasma estimate), the significance becomes 3.8σ

## Context

This work extends the isobar collision program proposed by Voloshin (2010) and conducted at RHIC by the STAR experiment, which collected approximately 2×10⁹ minimum bias events per species. The authors challenge the general expectation that CME fractions are similar in Au+Au and isobar collisions, showing instead that f_CME ∝ A^(5/3) based on simple scaling arguments. The AVFD model results by Shi et al. (2017) are used to validate the estimates. This analysis provides an approximate experimental baseline for interpreting the upcoming isobar collision data from STAR, while noting that theoretical uncertainties on the CME remain large.
