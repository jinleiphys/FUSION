# On a possible origin of a resonance-like structure in the two-photon invariant mass spectrum of the reaction pp->ppgg

> We show that the resonance-like structure found by the \emph{CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration} in the two-photon invariant mass spectrum of the reaction $pp \to pp γγ$ is rather a signature of the $NN$-decoupled dibaryon resonance $d^\star_1$(1956) that is produced in the radiative process $pp \to γd^\star_1$ and then undergoes radiative decay into two protons $d^\star_1 \to pp γ$. It is found that a contribution of the dibaryon mechanism $pp \to γd^\star_1 \to pp γγ$ of the reaction $pp \to pp γγ$ to the invariant mass spectrum of its photon pairs can reasonably well reproduce the experimentally observed spectrum in the vicinity of the resonance-like structure.

## Key claim

The resonance-like structure observed by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration in the two-photon invariant mass spectrum of the reaction \(pp \to pp \gamma\gamma\) is likely a signature of the \(NN\)-decoupled dibaryon resonance \(d^\star_1(1956)\) produced via \(pp \to \gamma d^\star_1\) followed by radiative decay \(d^\star_1 \to pp\gamma\), rather than evidence for the S-wave dipion resonance \(\sigma\) as originally proposed.

## Method

The authors calculated the invariant mass spectrum of photon pairs from the dibaryon mechanism \(pp \to \gamma d^\star_1 \to pp\gamma\gamma\) using a Monte Carlo method based on the GENBOD event generator, with the matrix element factorized into formation and decay parts. The decay matrix element was derived assuming the \(d^\star_1\) is a bound \(p\Delta(1232)\) state with spin-parity \(J^P=0^-\) and isospin \(I=2\), using either a Gaussian or Fermi-type radial wave function for the bound state, while the formation matrix element was parameterized phenomenologically with an exponential transverse-momentum dependence. Final-state interactions between decay protons in the \(^3P_1\)-state were included via a phenomenological correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The dibaryon resonance mass is \(M_R = 1956\) MeV/\(c^2\).
- The binding energy of the \((p\Delta)\)-system is \(E_b = M_\Delta + m_N - M_R \approx 80\) MeV.
- The parameter \(b\) for the formation matrix element exponential \(|\mathcal{M}_F|^2 \propto \exp(-k_\perp/b)\) was set to \(b = 0.6\) GeV/\(c\).
- The parameter \(\kappa\) in the final-state interaction correlation function is \(\kappa = 3.93\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The calculations were performed for an incident proton energy of 1.36 GeV.

## Context

This work proposes an alternative to the interpretation by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration (2007), which attributed the structure to the \(\sigma\) meson via the process \(pp \to pp\sigma \to pp\gamma\gamma\) interfering with double \(pp\)-bremsstrahlung. The dibaryon mechanism extends earlier theoretical work by Khrykin and Gerasimov (PRC64, NPA721) on two-photon emission in \(NN\) collisions via the \(d^\star_1\) resonance. The results suggest that further experimental and theoretical studies are needed to confirm the existence of the dibaryon \(d^\star_1\) and clarify the origin of the observed structure.
