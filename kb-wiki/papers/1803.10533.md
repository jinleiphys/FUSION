# Study of Isolated-photon and Jet Momentum Imbalance in $pp$ and $PbPb$ collisions

> In this paper, we study the production of isolated-photon plus a jet in $pp$ and $PbPb$ collisions, which can be used as an important probe to the jet transport property in quark gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions. Normally, there are two types of observables associated with the production of isolated-photon plus a jet, namely, the azimuthal angular correlation and the transverse momentum imbalance. To understand both observables in the full kinematical region, we need to employ the perturbative QCD calculation, which takes into account the hard splitting of partons, together with the Sudakov resummation formalism, which resums soft gluon splittings. Furthermore, by introducing energy-loss into the system, we calculate the enhancement of the momentum imbalance distribution for $AA$ as compared to $pp$ collisions and make predictions for future unfolded experimental data. In addition, in order to extract the jet transport coefficient more precisely in our numerical calculation, we also distinguish quark jets from gluon jets, since they interact with quark gluon plasma with different strengths. This work provides a reliable theoretical tool for the calculation of the gamma-jet correlation, which can lead us to a more precise extraction of the jet transport coefficient in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a combined perturbative QCD plus Sudakov resummation framework can describe isolated-photon and jet correlations in both pp and PbPb collisions, but the momentum imbalance distribution (x_Jγ) in pp collisions only agrees with ATLAS data after applying a detector smearing function; the authors argue that the raw experimental data have not been fully unfolded for detector effects, and their unsmeared results should be considered predictions for future unfolded data.

## Method

The authors compute the azimuthal angular correlation Δφ and the transverse momentum imbalance x_Jγ = p_TJ / p_Tγ for isolated-photon plus jet production using a two-pronged approach: perturbative QCD (pQCD) with 2→3 matrix elements for large-angle configurations, and Sudakov resummation at next-to-leading-log (NLL) accuracy for the back-to-back region, matched at a phase-space boundary. For PbPb collisions, they incorporate medium-induced transverse momentum broadening via an additional term (q̂_R L b²/4) in the Sudakov factor, distinguishing quark and gluon jets using the Casimir ratio q̂_g / q̂_q = C_A / C_F.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient extracted for PbPb collisions at the LHC at 5.02 TeV is q̂₀ ~ 2 – 8 GeV²/fm.
- The matching point between pQCD and Sudakov regions is set at φ_m = 2.9 rad; the Sudakov region is further restricted to q_perp < q_cut = p_Tγ / 4.
- The detector smearing function used to compare with ATLAS pp data uses a jet energy scale shift of 3% (r̄ = 0.97) and a jet energy resolution of σ = 0.13.
- The non-perturbative Sudakov separation scale is b_max = 0.5 GeV⁻¹.
- The jet cone sizes used are R = 0.3 (CMS comparison) and R = 0.4 (ATLAS comparison), with jet p_T > 30 GeV and rapidity cuts |y| < 1.6 (CMS) or |y| < 2.1 (ATLAS).

## Context

This work extends the Sudakov resummation improved pQCD formalism previously developed for dijet asymmetries by Chen et al. (2016) to the isolated-photon plus jet channel, which is considered a cleaner probe of jet energy loss since the photon does not interact strongly with the QGP. The results for the angular correlation agree with ATLAS and CMS data, while the x_Jγ distribution matches the NLO Monte Carlo generator POWHEG+PYTHIA as reported by Klasen et al. (2017). The authors note that at LHC kinematics, vacuum Sudakov effects dominate over medium broadening in the angular correlation, consistent with the observation by Mueller et al. (2016) that no significant difference is seen between pp and PbPb Δφ distributions. The framework enables a more precise extraction of the jet transport coefficient q̂ from future unfolded experimental data.
