# Classical model for diffusion and thermalization of heavy quarks in a hot medium: memory and out-of-equilibrium effects

> We consider a simple model for the diffusion of heavy quarks in a hot bath, modeling the latter by an ensemble of oscillators distributed accorded to either a thermal distribution or to an out-of-equilibrium distribution with a saturation scale. Within this model it is easy to introduce memory effects by changing the distribution of oscillators: we model these by introducing a gaussian distribution, $dN/dω$, which can be deformed continuously from a $δ-$function giving a Markov dissipation to a broad kernel with memory. Deriving the equation of motion of the heavy quark in the bath we remark how dissipation comes out naturally as an effect of the back-reaction on the bath of oscillators. Moreover, the exact solution of this equation allows for the definition of a thermalization time as the time necessary to remove any memory of the initial condition. We find that the broadening the dissipative kernel while keeping the coupling fixed lowers the thermalization time. We also derive the fluctuation-dissipation theorem for the bath, and use this to estimate the kinematic regime in which momentum diffusion of the heavy quark dominates over drift: we find that diffusion is more important as long as $K_0/{\cal E}$ is small, where $K_0$ and ${\cal E}$ denote the initial energy of the heavy quark and the average energy of the bath respectively.

# Classical model for diffusion and thermalization of heavy quarks in a hot medium: memory and out-of-equilibrium effects

## Key claim

The authors show that broadening the dissipative kernel (introducing memory effects) while keeping the coupling fixed lowers the thermalization time of heavy quarks in a hot bath, and that momentum diffusion dominates over drift when the ratio of the heavy quark's initial energy to the bath's average energy is small. This result is derived within a classical oscillator model where the bath can be either in thermal equilibrium or out-of-equilibrium with a saturation scale.

## Method

The authors model the hot medium as an ensemble of N harmonic oscillators coupled linearly to a heavy quark via an interaction Hamiltonian H_int proportional to Q times the sum of oscillator positions. The oscillators are distributed according to either a thermal Boltzmann distribution or a Fermi-Dirac-like distribution with a saturation scale (CGC-like distribution) to mimic out-of-equilibrium conditions. Memory effects are introduced by choosing a Gaussian distribution dN/dω for the oscillator frequencies, which can be continuously deformed from a delta-function (Markov dissipation) to a broad kernel with memory. The equation of motion for the heavy quark momentum P is derived exactly, including the back-reaction of the heavy quark on the bath, and solved analytically using Laplace transforms.

## Key numbers

- Formation time of heavy quarks: tau_form ≤ 0.1 fm/c for both charm and beauty quarks
- For a flat out-of-equilibrium distribution (CGC-like), the effective temperature is T_eff = 2M/3, where M is the parameter playing the role of the saturation scale Q_s
- The equilibration time for the Gaussian kernel is tau_eq = M/alpha^2 * (1 - 2*alpha^2/(a*sqrt(pi)*M) + O(alpha^4/a^2)), where alpha^2 = g^2*N/(a^3*m)*sqrt(pi) and a regulates the kernel shape
- For a Markov process, the diffusion coefficient D = pi*g^2*E/(2*m*M)*(1/omega^2*dN/dω) and the drag coefficient gamma = pi*g^2/(2*m*M)*(1/omega^2*dN/dω), satisfying D/gamma = E where E is the bath energy scale

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in the Glasma using Fokker-Planck equations (Mrowczynski 2017) and Wong equations coupled to evolving Glasma backgrounds (Ruggieri 2018a, 2018b), which found diffusion similar to thermal media but no substantial drift. The model builds on classical mechanics approaches to quantum Brownian motion (Russo, references therein) and addresses memory effects that have attracted interest in heavy ion collisions (Greiner 1994, Murase 2013, Kapusta 2014, Schmidt 2014, Wesp 2014). The results enable future implementation of dissipative terms in field theories and provide a framework for studying heavy quark propagation in out-of-equilibrium media like the Glasma.
