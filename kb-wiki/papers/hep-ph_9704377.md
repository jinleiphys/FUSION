# Virtual photons in SU(2) chiral perturbation theory and electromagnetic corrections to $ππ$ scattering

> We construct the generating functional of two flavor chiral perturbation theory including the effects of virtual photons in the one loop approximation. As an application, we calculate the electromagnetic corrections to the elastic $ππ$ scattering amplitude, in particular to its S--wave threshold parameters. Numerical estimates are given for the reaction $π^0 π^0 \to π^0 π^0$. These electromagnetic effects are found to be smaller than the hadronic two-loop corrections for the scattering length $a_0$. The effective range $b_0$ increases by 36% due to the unitary cusp.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic corrections to the S-wave scattering length \(a_0\) for \(\pi^0\pi^0 \to \pi^0\pi^0\) are smaller than the hadronic two-loop corrections, while the effective range \(b_0\) increases by 36% due to the unitary cusp from the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference.

## Method

The authors constructed the generating functional for two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) including virtual photons at one-loop order, counting the electric charge as \(\mathcal{O}(q)\). They derived the dimension-four electromagnetic counterterm Lagrangian with 13 operators and their \(\beta\)-functions, then applied this formalism to calculate the elastic \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitude for \(\pi^0\pi^0 \to \pi^0\pi^0\) using modified pion propagators that incorporate the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length \(a_0(00;00)\) with electromagnetic corrections is 0.0340 (in units of the inverse neutral pion mass), compared to 0.0360 without electromagnetism (\(e=0\)).
- The effective range \(b_0(00;00)\) with electromagnetic corrections is 0.0412 (in units of the inverse neutral pion mass), compared to 0.0302 without electromagnetism (\(e=0\)), an increase of 36%.
- The charged-to-neutral pion mass difference is \((M_{\pi^+} - M_{\pi^0}) / M_{\pi^+} \sim 4\%\).
- The dimensionless constant \(Z = C / F_0^4 = 0.89\), with \(F_0 = 88\) MeV.
- The neutral pion mass is \(M_{\pi^0} = 134.97\) MeV and the physical pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor CHPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) to include virtual photons, following the three-flavor approach of Urech (1995) and Neufeld and Rupertsberger (1996). The results enable systematic inclusion of electromagnetic effects in pion-pion scattering, with the dominant isospin-violating effect arising from the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference, similar to the electric dipole amplitude in neutral pion photoproduction off nucleons (Bernard, Kaiser, Meißner, and Schmidt, 1993).
