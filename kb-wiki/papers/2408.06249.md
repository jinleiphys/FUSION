# On sensitivity of nucleus deformation on final-state flow harmonics

> In this work, we explore the effect of deformation of the nuclei on collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The parameter associated with the geometrical deformation in the Glauber model is tuned to reproduce the empirical multiplicity probability distributions correctly. Subsequently, the particle spectra and collective flows for Au+Au and U+U collisions are evaluated using a hybrid hydrodynamic code CHESS. We analyze the effects of the degrees of freedom associated with the IC on the final-state flow harmonics by exploring the parameter space of the former. The connection between the deformation parameters, specifically $β_2$ and $β_4$, and the flow anisotropies is scrutinized. In particular, deviations in elliptic flow at $p_{\mathrm{T}}\sim 2$ GeV are observed at smaller values of $β_2$ in Au+Au collisions. On the other hand, for U+U collisions, the averaged overall flow harmonics are found to be less sensitive to the geometrical parameters. Despite the difference in the model's specifications, our findings largely confirm those obtained in the literature employing different approaches, which indicate that flow harmonics can be used as a sensible probe for the initial geometry fluctuations and to discriminate between different theoretical models.

## Key claim

The paper finds that while variations in the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ and hexadecapole deformation parameter β₄ do affect the final-state elliptic flow v₂, the averaged overall flow harmonics for U+U collisions are less sensitive to these geometrical parameters than for Au+Au collisions, with the results largely confirming prior literature despite differences in model specifications.

## Method

The authors used the CHESS hybrid hydrodynamic code, which combines the T_RENT_o initial condition generator (with deformed Woods-Saxon profiles including β₂ and β₄ parameters), the vHLLE relativistic hydrodynamic solver, and the THERMINATOR 2 hadronization code. They tuned the T_RENT_o parameters to reproduce the empirical multiplicity probability distributions from STAR data for Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV and U+U collisions at √sNN=193 GeV, then systematically varied β₂ and β₄ across multiple parameter sets for both collision systems while keeping other Woods-Saxon parameters (radius R and skin depth a) fixed.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV: deviations in elliptic flow at pT∼2 GeV are observed at smaller values of β₂
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Au: R=6.37 fm, a=0.535 fm, with β₂ varied from 0.0 to -0.262 and β₄ varied from -0.015 to -0.062
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for U: R=6.81 fm, a=0.60 fm, with β₂ varied from 0.0 to 0.560 and β₄ varied from 0.046 to 0.186
- The optimized T_RENT_o parameters for Au: p=-0.4, k=1.3, w=0.4 with χ²=0.156; for U: p=-0.7, k=1.7, w=0.2 with χ²=0.287
- Numerical convergence for differential flows achieved for NE ≥ 1000 events; the study adopted NE=1600 events per collision setup

## Context

This work extends studies by Filip (2009) on Glauber Monte Carlo eccentricities for deformed nuclei, and by Schenke and collaborators (2014) on initial state geometry using Glauber and IP-Glasma approaches for Au+Au, Cu+Au, and U+U collisions. The results confirm findings by Giacalone and collaborators (2018-2021) that flow harmonics can probe initial geometry fluctuations, and the methodology builds on recent isobar collision studies (Jia and collaborators, 2022-2023) that demonstrated the importance of multipole deformations including β₂ and β₄ for discriminating between theoretical models.
