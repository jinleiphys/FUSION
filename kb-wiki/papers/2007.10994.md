# Heavy meson tomography of cold nuclear matter at the electron-ion collider

> An important part of the physics program at the future electron-ion collider is to understand the nature of hadronization and the transport of energy and matter in large nuclei. Open heavy flavor production in deep inelastic scattering provides a new tool to address these critical questions. We present the first calculation of D-mesons and B-meson cross sections in electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC by including both next-to-leading order QCD corrections and cold nuclear matter effects. Our formalism employs generalized DGLAP evolution to include the contribution of in-medium parton showers, and is based on methods developed in soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons that describe inclusive hadron production in reactions with nucleons and nuclei. The comprehensive study summarized here allows us to identify the optimal observables, center-of-mass energies, and kinematic regions most sensitive to the physics of energy loss and hadronization at the EIC.

# Heavy meson tomography of cold nuclear matter at the electron-ion collider

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of D-meson and B-meson cross sections in electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC including both NLO QCD corrections and cold nuclear matter effects, but the nuclear modification for heavy flavor is often close to unity ($R_{eA}^{D} \approx R_{eA}^{B} \approx 1$) for most energy and rapidity combinations, making the momentum fraction $z$ distribution a more suitable observable than transverse momentum distributions for discriminating between models of hadronization.

## Method

The formalism employs generalized DGLAP evolution equations that include medium-induced splitting kernels derived in soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET$_{\rm G}$) to describe in-medium parton showers in cold nuclear matter. The authors compute semi-inclusive open heavy meson cross sections in e+p and e+A collisions at three benchmark energy combinations (5 GeV x 40 GeV, 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 18 GeV x 275 GeV) using CT10nlo PDFs, heavy quark effective theory boundary conditions for heavy meson fragmentation functions, and the Weizsacker-Williams approximation for quasi-real photon scattering. The nuclear modification ratio $R_{eA}^{h}$ is defined using inclusive jet production for normalization to minimize nuclear PDF effects.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficients extracted from HERMES data: $\langle q^2_\perp \rangle / \lambda_g = 0.12$ GeV$^2$/fm for gluons and $\langle q^2_\perp \rangle / \lambda_q = 0.05$ GeV$^2$/fm for quarks, with bands varying by a factor of two
- NLO QCD corrections yield K-factors in the range of 1.5 to 2.5 for hadron production at the EIC
- For 5 GeV x 40 GeV e+p collisions at NLO, expected $D^0$ multiplicity in $p_T$ bin [2,3] GeV is $3.7 \times 10^6$ events per 10 fb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity; for $B^0$ in same bin it is $1.1 \times 10^6$ events
- For 10 GeV x 100 GeV e+p collisions at NLO, expected $D^0$ multiplicity in $p_T$ bin [2,3] GeV is $2.1 \times 10^7$ events per 10 fb$^{-1}$; for $B^0$ it is $6.2 \times 10^6$ events
- For 18 GeV x 275 GeV e+p collisions at NLO, expected $D^0$ multiplicity in $p_T$ bin [2,3] GeV is $7.2 \times 10^7$ events per 10 fb$^{-1}$; for $B^0$ it is $2.1 \times 10^7$ events
- For $c \to D^0$, in-medium corrections enhance FFs for $z < 0.6$; for ${\bar b} \to B^0$, enhancement occurs for $z < 0.85$
- Quasi-real photon scattering contributes 40% to 50% of NLO corrections for pions, and is even more dominant for D-mesons and B-mesons

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of medium-modified DGLAP evolution previously applied to light hadron production in cold nuclear matter at HERMES (Airapetian 2007) and heavy ion collisions (Kang 2014, Chien 2015) to open heavy flavor production at the EIC. The authors validate their approach by comparing with HERMES measurements of pion suppression on Kr and Xe targets, showing good agreement. The study enables future EIC measurements to discriminate between competing models of hadronization (parton energy loss versus hadron absorption, as debated in Arleo 2003, Chang 2014, Kopeliovich 2003) by exploiting the distinctly different fragmentation patterns of heavy quarks into D-mesons and B-mesons compared to light partons into pions.
