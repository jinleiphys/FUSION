# Equation of state and Taylor expansions at nonzero isospin chemical potential

> We compute the equation of state of isospin asymmetric QCD at zero and non-zero temperatures using direct simulations of lattice QCD with three dynamical flavors at physical quark masses. In addition to the pressure and the trace anomaly and their behavior towards the continuum limit, we will particularly discuss the extraction of the speed of sound. Furthermore, we discuss first steps towards the extension of the EoS to small non-zero baryon chemical potentials via Taylor expansion.

## Key claim
The authors compute the equation of state for isospin-asymmetric QCD from lattice simulations, finding that the speed of sound exceeds the conformal bound of \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\) at high isospin chemical potential. This result is presented as a lower bound for the continuum limit, with the peak height and position increasing as the lattice spacing decreases.

## Method
The study uses \(N_f = 2+1\) flavors of improved rooted staggered quarks at physical quark masses with tree-level Symanzik improved gluon action. The equation of state is extracted from the isospin density via model-independent spline interpolations, with residual temperature effects at \(T \approx 30\) MeV corrected using chiral perturbation theory near the transition to the Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) phase. For nonzero temperature, the modifications to pressure and interaction measure are computed from two-dimensional spline interpolations and combined with \(\mu_I = 0\) data from Borsanyi et al. (2013). The speed of sound is obtained from directional derivatives along isentropes.

## Key numbers
- The interaction measure at \(T=0\) reaches a maximum around \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.63\) to 0.65 and becomes negative around \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.9\).
- At \(T=0\), the squared speed of sound crosses the conformal bound at \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.64\) and peaks at \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.76\) to 0.80, with peak values of \(c_s^2 \approx 0.53\) to 0.6.
- Lattice spacings used include \(a \approx 0.15\) fm (\(32^3 \times 48\)), \(a \approx 0.22\) fm (\(24^3 \times 32\)), and \(a \approx 0.29\) fm (from Brandt 2018).
- Nonzero temperature ensembles use \(N_t = 8, 10, 12\) with aspect ratio \(N_s/N_t \approx 3\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies of the QCD phase diagram at nonzero isospin chemical potential (Brandt 2017, 2018) and the equation of state at zero temperature (Brandt 2018). The observation that the speed of sound exceeds the conformal bound in full QCD is new and agrees with recent results in two-color QCD (Iida 2022, Itou 2022). The Taylor expansion method (Gottlieb 1988) is applied to extend the equation of state to small nonzero baryon chemical potentials, using the isospin-asymmetric results as expansion points. This enables access to previously inaccessible regions of the three-dimensional parameter space relevant for heavy-ion collisions and early Universe physics.
