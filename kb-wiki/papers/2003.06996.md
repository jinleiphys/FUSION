# Non-zero tensor condensates in cold quark matter within the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction

> The possible formation of tensor condensates originated from a tensor-type interaction between quarks is investigated in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction, which leads to flavor mixing. It is shown that independent two tensor condensates appear and a tensor condensate related to the strange quark easily occurs by the effect of the flavor mixing compared with one related to light quarks. Also, it is shown that the tensor condensate related to the strange quark appears at a slightly smaller chemical potential if the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction is included, due to the flavor mixing effect. It is also shown that the two kinds of tensor condensates may coexist in a certain quark chemical potential due to the flavor mixing.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two independent tensor condensates, \(F_3\) and \(F_8\), can form in three-flavor cold quark matter, and that the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (determinant) interaction causes the strange-quark tensor condensate to appear at a slightly smaller chemical potential than in the no-mixing case, while also enabling a region where both condensates coexist. The abstract's claim of coexistence is qualified: it occurs only in a narrow window of quark chemical potential (approximately 0.508 to 0.52 GeV) and only when flavor mixing is included.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model extended with a tensor-type four-point interaction and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft six-point determinant interaction. They apply the mean-field approximation, retaining only diagonal flavor components, and introduce two independent tensor condensates \(F_3 = -G_T \langle \bar{\psi} \Sigma_3 \lambda_3 \psi \rangle\) and \(F_8 = -G_T \langle \bar{\psi} \Sigma_3 \lambda_8 \psi \rangle\). The thermodynamic potential is evaluated at zero temperature with finite quark chemical potential, and the gap equations for the dynamical quark masses and the two tensor condensates are solved self-consistently using a three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

* Tensor condensate \(F_3\) appears at \(\mu \approx 0.41\) GeV in Model O (no flavor mixing) and at \(\mu \approx 0.48\) GeV in Model I (with flavor mixing).
* Tensor condensate \(F_8\) appears at \(\mu \approx 0.532\) GeV in Model O and at \(\mu \approx 0.508\) GeV in Model I.
* In Model I, \(F_3\) and \(F_8\) coexist in the range \(\mu \approx 0.508\) to 0.52 GeV; above \(\mu \approx 0.52\) GeV only \(F_8\) remains.
* The constituent strange quark mass shows a small gap at \(\mu \approx 0.508\) GeV in Model I, while light quark masses decrease at \(\mu \approx 0.34\) GeV in both models.
* Model parameters: current quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 0.0055\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.1375\) GeV; scalar coupling \(G_s = 5.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (Model O) or 4.6 GeV\(^{-2}\) (Model I); tensor coupling \(G_T = 2G_s\); cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.6314\) GeV; determinant coupling \(G_D = 9.288/\Lambda^5\) GeV\(^{-5}\) (Model I).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor NJL studies of tensor condensates (Kagawa et al., 2019; 2020) to the three-flavor case, and corrects the common approximation \(F_8 = F_3/\sqrt{3}\) used in earlier work by the same group (Kagawa et al., 2018) and by others (India, 2019). The inclusion of the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction, which implements the \(U_A(1)\) anomaly, is shown to qualitatively change the coexistence behavior of the two condensates. The results enable future studies of spontaneous magnetization in quark matter and its implications for the structure of compact stars such as neutron stars and magnetars.
