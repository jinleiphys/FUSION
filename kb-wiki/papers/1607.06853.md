# Constraining Anomalous Forces with Pseudoscalar and Axial Couplings Employing a Spin-Independent Analysis

> Present laboratory limits on the coupling strength of anomalous pseudoscalar and axial interactions are many orders of magnitude weaker than their scalar and vector analogs. Here we investigate two mechanisms which can circumvent this suppression and thereby lead to improved limits.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that existing limits on pseudoscalar and axial couplings, which are many orders of magnitude weaker than scalar and vector analogs, can be improved by analyzing spin-independent experimental data through two specific mechanisms, rather than by performing new spin-dependent experiments.

## Method

The authors calculate the long-range potentials arising from two-boson exchange processes: exchange of two pseudoscalars between spin-1/2 particles, and exchange of one scalar and one pseudoscalar boson. They also analyze the effective potential from spin-dependent interactions between bound state systems (nuclei), where parity-violating suppression factors are absorbed by the nuclear mean field. A 2012 data set from the short-range gravity experiment at Indiana University is analyzed for the two-pseudoscalar exchange interaction.

## Key numbers

- The present constraint on spin-independent Yukawa interactions from ultralight scalar or vector bosons is a dimensionless coupling constant of ~10^-40 for a force range of ~1 mm (Heckel 2007).
- The constraint on pseudoscalar coupling to electrons at the same range (~1 mm) is ~10^-16 (Heckel 2015).
- The axial coupling to nucleons is constrained to ~10^-13 (PSI).
- Preliminary results from the Indiana University short-range gravity experiment suggest an improved constraint on (g^N_P)^2, the pseudoscalar coupling to nucleons, in the range between 40 and 200 micrometers by about a factor of 5 compared to previous limits (Klimchitskaya).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of spin-independent experimental data to constrain spin-dependent interactions, addressing the large disparity between limits on pseudoscalar/axial couplings and scalar/vector couplings. The two-boson exchange mechanism builds on prior calculations by Drell and Ferrer, while the bound state approach was first identified by Krause. The analysis enables improved constraints on pseudoscalar and axial couplings at both long and short distance scales using existing experimental data from searches for long-range forces between bound state systems.
