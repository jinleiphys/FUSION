# Progress on tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory for nuclear magnetic and antimagnetic rotation

> Magnetic rotation and antimagnetic rotation are exotic rotational phenomena observed in weakly deformed or near-spherical nuclei, which are respectivelyinterpreted in terms of the shears mecha-nism and two shearslike mechanism. Since their observations, magnetic rotation and antimagnetic rotation phenomena have been mainly investigated in the framework of tilted axis cranking based on the pairing plus quadrupole model. For the last decades, the covariant density functional theory and its extension have been proved to be successful in describing series of nuclear ground-states and excited states properties, including the binding energies, radii, single-particle spectra, resonance states, halo phenomena, magnetic moments, magnetic rotation, low-lying excitations, shape phase transitions, collective rotation and vibrations, etc. This review will mainly focus on the tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory and its application for the magnetic rotation and antimagnetic rotation phenomena.

## Key claim

This review argues that tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) provides a fully self-consistent and microscopic description of magnetic rotation and antimagnetic rotation phenomena, overcoming limitations of earlier models that neglected polarization effects and nuclear currents or treated them only partially.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of tilted axis cranking combined with covariant density functional theory using point-coupling interactions, which avoids the explicit Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields and reduces computing time compared to three-dimensional cranking. They summarize applications of this TAC-CDFT approach to magnetic rotation bands across multiple mass regions (A~60, 80, 100, 140, 190) and to antimagnetic rotation bands in Cd isotopes, using the examples of 198Pb for MR and 105Cd for AMR.

## Key numbers

- More than 195 magnetic dipole bands observed in 85 nuclides across mass regions A~60, 80, 110, 140, and 190
- Experimental indicator for MR: B(M1) ~ a few μ_N^2, with B(M1)/B(E2) ratios ≥ 20 μ_N^2/(eb)^2
- Experimental indicator for MR and AMR: deformation parameter |β| ≤ 0.15
- Experimental indicator for MR and AMR: J^(2)/B(E2) ≥ 100 MeV^(-1)(eb)^(-2), compared to ~10 MeV^(-1)(eb)^(-2) in well-deformed bands and ~5 MeV^(-1)(eb)^(-2) in superdeformed bands
- Lifetime measurements in 106Sn suggested J^(2)/B(E2) > 1000 MeV^(-1)(eb)^(-2), indicating almost pure magnetic rotation in a spherical nucleus
- AMR observed in Cd isotopes: 105Cd, 106Cd, 108Cd, 109Cd, 110Cd, and 112Cd, plus candidates 100Pd and 144Dy

## Context

This review extends the earlier tilted axis cranking work based on the pairing plus quadrupole model (Frauendorf, 1993; Frauendorf, 2001) and the shell correction method by incorporating covariant density functional theory, which treats polarization effects and nuclear currents self-consistently. The TAC-CDFT approach builds on prior principal-axis cranking relativistic Hartree calculations (Koepf and Ring, 1989) and three-dimensional cranking CDFT (Madokoro, 2000), while the point-coupling version (Zhao, 2011) enables systematic studies from light nuclei like 60Ni to heavy nuclei like 198,199Pb. The review enables future microscopic investigations of the competition and transition between electric and magnetic rotation, and further exploration of nuclear chirality in triaxially deformed nuclei.
