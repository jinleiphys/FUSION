# Hypergeometric type operators and their supersymmetric partners

> The generalization of the factorization method performed by Mielnik [J. Math. Phys. {\bf 25}, 3387 (1984)] opened new ways to generate exactly solvable potentials in quantum mechanics. We present an application of Mielnik's method to hypergeometric type operators. It is based on some solvable Riccati equations and leads to a unitary description of the quantum systems exactly solvable in terms of orthogonal polynomials or associated special functions.

# Hypergeometric type operators and their supersymmetric partners

## Key claim

The paper applies Mielnik's (1984) generalization of the factorization method directly to hypergeometric type operators, rather than to individual Schrödinger equations, by solving Riccati equations whose particular solutions are known from the standard factorization of these operators. This yields a unified description of supersymmetric partners for essentially all quantum systems exactly solvable in terms of orthogonal polynomials or associated special functions, provided the constant gamma in the general Riccati solution is chosen so that the resulting superpotential has no singularity.

## Method

The authors start from the hypergeometric type equation sigma(s) y'' + tau(s) y' + lambda y = 0 and its associated self-adjoint form. They define hypergeometric type operators H_m acting on associated special functions Phi_{ell,m}(s) = kappa^m(s) d^m/ds^m Phi_ell(s), where kappa(s) = sqrt(sigma(s)). Using the known raising and lowering operators a_m and a_m^+ that factorize H_m, they write the general solution of the Riccati equation for the superpotential psi in Mielnik's method as the particular solution plus a term involving sigma^m(s) rho(s) divided by gamma plus an integral of sigma^m(t) rho(t) dt. They then construct the operators b_m and b_m^+ and the supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians H_{m,gamma}, and finally transform to Schrödinger form via the change of variable ds/dx = +/- kappa(s) and the definition Psi_{ell,m}(x) = sqrt(kappa(s(x)) rho(s(x))) Phi_{ell,m}(s(x)).

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalues lambda_ell for the hypergeometric type equation are given by lambda_ell = -(sigma''(s)/2) ell(ell-1) - tau'(s) ell, with lambda_0 = 0 and lambda_ell increasing with ell.
- For sigma(s) in {1, s, 1-s^2}, the index ell can go to infinity (Lambda = infinity); for sigma(s) in {s^2-1, s^2, s^2+1}, the maximum index is Lambda = (1-alpha)/2.
- The norm relation between associated special functions is ||Phi_{ell,m+1}|| = sqrt(lambda_ell - lambda_m) ||Phi_{ell,m}||.
- For the shifted oscillator example with sigma=1, tau=alpha s+beta, the eigenvalue is lambda_m = -alpha m; for the special case alpha=-2, beta=0, the superpotential is W_{m,gamma}(x) = x + exp(-x^2)/(gamma + integral_0^x exp(-t^2) dt).
- For the three-dimensional oscillator with sigma=s, tau=alpha s+beta, the eigenvalue is lambda_m = -alpha m and the superpotential contains a term (1/2^(2m+2beta-1)) x^(2m+2beta-1) exp(alpha x^2/4) divided by the integral denominator.
- For the Poschl-Teller type potential with sigma=1-s^2, tau=alpha s+beta, the eigenvalue is lambda_m = m(m-alpha-1).
- For the generalized Poschl-Teller, Morse, Scarf hyperbolic, and Coulomb type potentials, the eigenvalues are lambda_m = -m(m+alpha-1) or, for the Coulomb case with tau=beta, tilde{lambda}_m = -delta^2/(2m+2beta-1)^2.
- For the trigonometric Rosen-Morse, Eckart, and hyperbolic Rosen-Morse type potentials, the eigenvalues are tilde{lambda}_m = m(m-alpha-1) - delta^2/(2m-alpha-1)^2 or tilde{lambda}_m = -m(m+alpha-1) - delta^2/(2m+alpha-1)^2.

## Context

This work extends Mielnik's (1984) generalization of the factorization method from individual quantum systems to the entire class of hypergeometric type operators, building on earlier factorizations by Cotfas (2002, 2004, 2006) and Jafarov (no year given). It unifies the treatment of all exactly solvable quantum systems whose wavefunctions are expressed in terms of Hermite, Laguerre, or Jacobi polynomials and their associated special functions. The results enable the systematic construction of supersymmetric partner potentials for the shifted oscillator, three-dimensional oscillator, Poschl-Teller, generalized Poschl-Teller, Morse, Scarf hyperbolic, Coulomb, trigonometric Rosen-Morse, Eckart, and hyperbolic Rosen-Morse potentials, all from a single formalism.
