# Finite System Size Correction to NLO Scattering in $ϕ^4$ Theory

> We compute $2\rightarrow2$ scattering in massive $ϕ^4$ theory on $\mathbb R^{1,m}\times T^n$ to NLO. We perform the calculations using "denominator regularization" instead of the usual dimensional regularization, which allows for asymmetric configurations of the $T^n$. We give a transparent derivation of and equation for the analytic continuation of the generalized Epstein zeta function. We show that the Optical Theorem is satisfied and generalize a conjecture by Hardy on square counting functions. We comment on the implications.

## Key claim

The finite-size correction to the NLO 2→2 scattering amplitude in massive φ⁴ theory on ℝ^(1,m)×T^n is computed using "denominator regularization" instead of dimensional regularization, and the authors show that the ultraviolet divergence is unaffected by the finite system size, meaning that the running coupling from the Callan-Symanzik equation is insensitive to finite-size effects; the finite-size corrections only appear at subleading order and must be obtained by explicit resummation of 1PI diagrams.

## Method

The authors compute the 2→2 scattering amplitude at next-to-leading order (NLO) in massive φ⁴ theory on a spacetime with m infinite spatial dimensions and n compactified dimensions (T^n) of arbitrary side lengths L_i. They introduce "denominator regularization," where the power of the denominator in the loop integral is analytically continued instead of the number of spacetime dimensions, and derive an analytic continuation of the generalized Epstein zeta function using Poisson summation. The renormalized amplitude is obtained by absorbing the 1/ε pole and the -1 from denominator regularization into a modified MSbar counterterm, and the result is checked for consistency with the Optical Theorem.

## Key numbers

- The finite-size correction to the renormalized NLO amplitude is given by an integral over the Feynman parameter x of a term containing a sum over nonzero integer vectors m: 2∑'_{m∈ℤ^3} exp(-2π i x ∑ m_i p^i L_i) K₀(2π √(Δ² ∑ m_i² L_i²)), where Δ² = -x(1-x)p² + m² - iε.
- For the s-channel in the center-of-mass frame (p^i = 0), the imaginary part of the amplitude yields 2 Im M = (λ²/(16π)) Q̃ θ(Q̃²) ∑_{m̃∈Λ^n} sinc(π √s Q̃ |m̃|), where Q̃ = √(1 - 4m²/s).
- The total cross section is σ_tot = (λ²/(2(4π)²√s)) θ(Q̃²) Ω_(2-n) (1/∏ L_i) ∑_{k̃∈Λ*ⁿ} (s Q̃²/4 - k̃²)^((1-n)/2) θ(s Q̃²/4 - k̃²), with Ω_(2-n) the solid angle in 2-n dimensions.
- For systems of size comparable to p+p collisions, previous work (Mogliacci 2018) found 40%+ corrections to thermodynamic quantities; for mid-central A+A collisions, ~10% corrections were found.

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-size effects in quantum field theory, motivated by the observation that quark-gluon plasma signatures appear in small collision systems at RHIC and LHC. It builds on prior calculations of finite-size corrections to thermodynamic quantities in free massless scalar theory with Dirichlet boundary conditions (Mogliacci 2018) and quenched lattice QCD with periodic boundary conditions (Kitazawa 2019). The authors' technique of denominator regularization provides an alternative to dimensional regularization on a hypercube (Zinn-Justin 2002) and to heat kernel or zeta function regularization in curved spacetimes (Hawking 1976). The results enable future computation of finite-size effects on the QCD trace anomaly and the running coupling, and the generalization of Hardy's conjecture on square counting functions is noted.
