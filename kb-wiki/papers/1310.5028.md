# Sivers Function in the Quasi-Classical Approximation

> We calculate the Sivers function in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and in the Drell-Yan process (DY) by employing the quasi-classical Glauber-Mueller/ McLerran-Venugopalan approximation. Modeling the hadron as a large "nucleus" with non-zero orbital angular momentum (OAM), we find that its Sivers function receives two dominant contributions: one contribution is due to the OAM, while another one is due to the local Sivers function density in the nucleus. While the latter mechanism, being due to the "lensing" interactions, dominates at large transverse momentum of the produced hadron in SIDIS or of the di-lepton pair in DY, the former (OAM) mechanism is leading in saturation power counting and dominates when the above transverse momenta become of the order of the saturation scale. We show that the OAM channel allows for a particularly simple and intuitive interpretation of the celebrated sign flip between the Sivers functions in SIDIS and DY.

## Key claim

The Sivers function receives two dominant contributions in the quasi-classical approximation: one from the orbital angular momentum (OAM) of the nucleus and another from the local Sivers function density (lensing mechanism). The OAM mechanism dominates when transverse momenta are of order the saturation scale, while the lensing mechanism dominates at large transverse momenta, with the OAM channel providing a simple interpretation of the sign flip between SIDIS and Drell-Yan.

## Method

The authors model the hadron as a large "nucleus" with non-zero orbital angular momentum using the quasi-classical Glauber-Mueller/McLerran-Venugopalan approximation, generalized to include rotational motion of nucleons via Wigner functions that specify both positions and momenta. They calculate the Sivers function in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and the Drell-Yan process (DY) by computing quark production cross sections with multiple rescatterings resummed in the saturation framework, identifying two distinct physical mechanisms for single transverse spin asymmetry generation.

## Key numbers

- The OAM mechanism contribution to the Sivers function at large produced quark transverse momentum \(k_T\) scales as \(A \, \alpha_s \, m_N \, p_T \, Q_s^2 / k_T^6\), where \(p_T\) is the typical transverse momentum of valence quarks due to orbital motion and \(m_N\) is the nucleon mass
- The Sivers function density contribution scales as \(A \, \alpha_s^2 \, m_N^2 / k_T^4\)
- The OAM mechanism dominates for \(k_T < Q_s/\sqrt{\alpha_s}\), while the Sivers function density mechanism dominates for \(k_T > Q_s/\sqrt{\alpha_s}\)
- The OAM mechanism is order-one in the saturation power counting (for \(\alpha_s^2 A^{1/3} \sim 1\)), while the lensing mechanism is order-\(\alpha_s\) in the same regime

## Context

This work extends the understanding of single transverse spin asymmetries beyond the quark-diquark model of the proton used by Brodsky, Hwang and Schmidt (2002) and Brodsky et al. (2013), and beyond the lensing interaction mechanism described by Burkardt (2008, 2010). The quasi-classical approximation employed here represents a limiting behavior of strong interactions at high energy, with the saturation scale providing a hard scale that justifies perturbative QCD calculations. The results enable a controlled approximation for the Sivers function in both SIDIS and DY processes, explicitly demonstrating the mechanism for the sign reversal predicted by Collins (2002).
