# Symmetry energy coefficients for asymmetric nuclear matter

> Symmetry energy coefficients of asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated as the inverse of nuclear matter polarizabilities with two different approaches. Firstly a general calculation shows they may depend on the neutron-proton asymmetry itself. The choice of particular prescriptions for the density fluctuations lead to certain isospin (n-p asymmetry) dependences of the polarizabilities. Secondly, with Skyrme type interactions, the static limit of the dynamical polarizability is investigated corresponding to the inverse symmetry energy coefficient which assumes different values at different asymmetries (and densities and temperatures). The symmetry energy coefficient (in the isovector channel) is found to increase as n-p asymmetries increase. The spin symmetry energy coefficient is also briefly investigated.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient in the isovector channel is found to increase as neutron-proton asymmetry increases, but this dependence is model-dependent and arises from specific prescriptions for how density fluctuations relate to the underlying neutron-proton asymmetry.

## Method

The symmetry energy coefficients are derived as the inverse of nuclear matter static polarizabilities, first via a general stability condition and then explicitly using Skyrme-type effective interactions (SLyb, SkSC4, SkSC6, SkSC10). The calculation relates the density fluctuation parameter β to the neutron-proton asymmetry parameter b through a prescription where δρ_n/β = ρ_n/ρ, and then solves for the asymmetry dependence of the symmetry coefficient A_{0,1}.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter (b=0), different Skyrme forces yield symmetry energy coefficients between 27 and 34 MeV.
- For b=2 (neutron density three times proton density), the isovector symmetry coefficient becomes A = 1.5 × A_sym (where A_sym ≈ 30 MeV).
- In the limit of neutron matter (b → ∞), the isovector symmetry coefficient approaches A = 2 × A_sym.
- One calculation at dense nuclear matter ρ = 2ρ_0 with force SLy(b) shows a much smaller increase of A_{0,1} with asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous results (Braghin, 1999) and a related study (Braghin, 2002) on the asymmetry dependence of symmetry energy coefficients, which had been treated as constant in standard macroscopic mass formulas. The results enable improved equations of state for asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to proto-neutron star and neutron star modeling, and the spin symmetry energy results can be compared to Monte Carlo calculations of spin susceptibility in neutron matter by Fantoni, Sarsa, and Schmidt (2001).
