# Nuclear Matter Studies with Density-dependent Meson-Nucleon Coupling Constants

> Due to the internal structure of the nucleon, we should expect, in general, that the effective meson nucleon parameters may change in nuclear medium. We study such changes by using a chiral confining model of the nucleon. We use density-dependent masses for all mesons except the pion. Within a Dirac-Brueckner analysis, based on the relativistic covariant structure of the NN amplitude, we show that the effect of such a density dependence in the NN interaction on the saturation properties of nuclear matter, while not large, is quite significant. Due to the density dependence of the $g_{σNN}$, as predicted by the chiral confining model, we find, in particular, a looping behavior of the binding energy at saturation as a function of the saturation density. A simple model is described, which exhibits looping and which is shown to be mainly caused by the presence of a peak in the density dependence of the medium modified $σN$ coupling constant at low density. The effect of density dependence of the coupling constants and the meson masses tends to improve the results for $E/A$ and density of nuclear matter at saturation. From the present study we see that the relationship between binding energy and saturation density may not be as universal as found in nonrelativistic studies and that more model dependence is exhibited once medium modifications of the basic nuclear interactions are considered.

## Key claim

The density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling constants predicted by the chiral confining model produce a "looping" behavior of the binding energy at saturation as a function of saturation density, and the relationship between binding energy and saturation density may not be as universal as found in nonrelativistic studies; the effects on saturation properties, while not large, are quite significant.

## Method

The authors used the chiral confining model (CCM) in its simplified TOY version to compute the field-dependence of meson-nucleon coupling constants (gσNN, gωNN, gπNN, gρNN, fρNN) and meson masses (except the pion) as functions of external scalar and vector fields. They then converted these field-dependences into density-dependences by solving coupled nonlinear self-consistency equations relating the scalar and vector fields to the nuclear density, incorporating a reduction factor ξ (0 ≤ ξ ≤ 0.5) for the effective density of polarizing nucleons due to the Pauli principle and short-range correlations. Finally, they performed relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations using the one-boson-exchange model of Fleischer and Tjon, modifying the free-space T-matrix to include Pauli-blocking and the density-dependent coupling constants and meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Free-space values from the PUREMASS case: gωNN = 11.02, gσNN(q) = 2.34, (gπNN/2M) = 0.83 (in units of mπ⁻¹); from the QUARTIC case: gωNN = 13.72, gσNN(q) = 4.92, (gπNN/2M) = 0.92 (in units of mπ⁻¹)
- The meson masses are taken to be linearly dependent on the scalar field x, with the coefficient bmeson chosen to give m*/m = 0.92 at normal nuclear density (bmeson = 0.4769 for PUREMASS, 0.4622 for QUARTIC)
- The scalar field vacuum value is ⟨σ⟩vac = -Fπ = -93 MeV; the range of Fπ* studied is 63 MeV to 93 MeV in steps of 6 MeV, and the ω field range is 0 to 40 MeV in steps of 8 MeV
- The effective density of polarizing nucleons is ξρ, with ξ expected in the range 0 ≤ ξ ≤ 0.5, and the error from using vector density instead of scalar density is estimated to be ∼ ⟨p²⟩/4M² ≈ 0.05

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Banerjee and collaborators (1994) on extracting density dependence of nucleon properties using the chiral confining model, with two significant improvements: inclusion of the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction and the pion cloud contributions to gσNN, gρNN, and fρNN coupling constants. The density dependence of meson masses was first stressed by Brown and collaborators (1991). The relativistic many-body approach follows the Dirac-Brueckner method of Tjon and collaborators (1992), using the one-boson-exchange model of Fleischer and Tjon (1980). The results suggest that medium modifications of basic nuclear interactions introduce more model dependence than found in nonrelativistic studies, and the work enables future full self-consistent many-body treatments to determine the reduction factor ξ.
