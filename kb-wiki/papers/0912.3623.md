# Are eccentricity fluctuations able to explain the centrality dependence of $v_4$?

> The fourth harmonic of the azimuthal distribution of particles $v_4$ has been measured for Au-Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The centrality dependence of $v_4$ does not agree with the prediction from hydrodynamics. In particular, the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$, where $v_2$ denotes the second harmonic of the azimuthal distribution of particles, is significantly larger than predicted by hydrodynamics. We argue that this discrepancy is mostly due to elliptic flow ($v_2$) fluctuations. We evaluate these fluctuations on the basis of a Monte Carlo Glauber calculation. The effect of deviations from local thermal equilibrium is also studied, but appears to be only a small correction. Combining these two effects allows us to reproduce experimental data for peripheral and midcentral collisions. However, we are unable to explain the large magnitude of $v_4/(v_2)^2$ observed for the most central collisions.

## Key claim

Eccentricity fluctuations, modeled via a Monte Carlo Glauber calculation, can explain most of the discrepancy between ideal hydrodynamics predictions and experimental data for the ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, except for the most central collisions where the large magnitude of this ratio remains unexplained.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber calculation to evaluate event-by-event fluctuations of the participant plane eccentricity \(\epsilon_{PP}\), with each participant nucleon weighted according to a two-component model (\(x=0.13\)) that reproduces charged hadron multiplicity. They then computed the effect of these fluctuations on the measured ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) by assuming \(v_2\) scales linearly with \(\epsilon_{PP}\) and using the relation \(\langle v_4 \rangle / \langle v_2^2 \rangle^2 = \frac{1}{2} \langle \epsilon_{PP}^4 \rangle / \langle \epsilon_{PP}^2 \rangle^2\). Deviations from local thermal equilibrium were studied using a 2+1-dimensional solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation parameterized by the Knudsen number \(K\).

## Key numbers

- The ideal hydrodynamics prediction for the ratio is \(v_4 = \frac{1}{2}(v_2)^2\).
- In the limit \(K \rightarrow 0\) (local thermal equilibrium), transport results give \(v_4/(v_2)^2 = 0.52\), close to \(1/2\).
- The two-component model parameter used is \(x = 0.13\), which best describes the charged hadron multiplicity observed experimentally.
- Centrality classes are defined as bins containing \(5\%\) of the total number of events.

## Context

This work extends the study of higher-order flow harmonics at RHIC, where experiments by STAR (Abelev 2007) and PHENIX (Huang 2008) observed that \(v_4 \simeq (v_2)^2\) at midrapidity, contradicting the ideal hydrodynamics prediction of \(v_4 = \frac{1}{2}(v_2)^2\) from Borghini (2005). The authors build on prior work by Drescher (2007) on partial thermalization effects in elliptic flow and by Alver (2008) on participant plane eccentricity fluctuations. The results enable future extraction of flow fluctuations directly from experimental data by comparing \(v_2\) from two-particle and four-particle cumulants, but the persistent discrepancy for central collisions points to the need for improved modeling of initial conditions or dynamics in the most central events.
