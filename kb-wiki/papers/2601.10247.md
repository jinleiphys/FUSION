# Updated Results for Kinematic Factors in Double Beta Decays

> Accurate calculations of phase space factors (PSFs), electron energy spectra and angular correlations are essential for designing and interpreting double-beta decay (DBD) experiments. These quantities help maximize sensitivity to potential signals, distinguish between different decay modes and interpret the data. In this work we provide updated results for these kinematic factors for two-neutrino ($2νββ$) and neutrinoless ($0νββ$) decay modes, including electron-emission, positron-emission and electron capture transitions. The calculations are performed with an adapted Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater method which allows for orthogonality of the wave functions of electrons and positrons in bound and continuum states and incorporates relevant atomic features such us screening, finite nuclear size, exchange corrections and phase shift effects. We provide tables with updated PSFs calculated both in the closure approximation and using the Taylor expansion method, for a large number of DBD isotopes. We discuss the impact of individual atomic corrections and find that our results are in line with predictions reported in recent literature. In some specific cases we find differences between our PSF values and those previously reported which are worth considering for better prediction and interpretation of DBD data. Then, we provide numerical values for $^{76}\text{Ge}$, $^{100}\text{Mo}$, $^{130}\text{Te}$ and $^{136}\text{Xe}$, which are most investigated in current DBD experiments. Similar data for other isotopes are available upon request.

## Key claim

The authors provide updated phase space factors (PSFs), electron energy spectra, and angular correlations for double-beta decay (DBD) using an improved Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater method that ensures orthogonality of electron and positron wave functions; while their results generally agree with recent literature, they find "differences between our PSF values and those previously reported" for specific cases, which they argue are "worth considering for better prediction and interpretation of DBD data."

## Method

The authors computed PSFs, single-electron and summed-energy electron spectra, and angular correlations for all nuclei that can undergo double-beta decay, covering \(2\nu\beta\beta\), \(0\nu\beta\beta\), \(\beta^+\beta^+\), EC\(\beta^+\), and ECEC decay modes to ground and first excited states. They used an adapted Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) method to obtain leptonic wave functions, incorporating screening, finite nuclear size, exchange corrections, and phase shift effects, with calculations performed both in the closure approximation and using a Taylor expansion method that accounts for lepton-energy dependence of the decay rate. The authors provided numerical values for \(^{76}\text{Ge}\), \(^{100}\text{Mo}\), \(^{130}\text{Te}\), and \(^{136}\text{Xe}\), with data for other isotopes available upon request.

## Key numbers

*   Current experimental lower bounds on the \(0\nu\beta^-\beta^-\) half-life are at the level of \(10^{25}\)–\(10^{26}\) yr, corresponding to effective neutrino mass in the range of a few tens of meV.
*   The closure energy approximation uses \(\tilde{A} = \frac{1}{2}W_0 + \langle E_n \rangle - E_I \approx 1.12 A^{1/2}\) MeV.
*   The nuclear radius is taken as \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm for computing electron capture probabilities.
*   The Taylor expansion method for \(2\nu\beta^-\beta^-\) decay includes terms up to fourth order in lepton energies, with factors \(\mathcal{A}_N\) defined using \(\epsilon_K\) and \(\epsilon_L\) scaled by \((2m_e)^2\), \((2m_e)^4\), and \((2m_e)^6\).

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Kotila (2012) and Stoica (2013), who computed Fermi functions using exact Dirac solutions in Coulomb potentials, by adopting an improved DHFS method that enforces orthogonality of electron and positron wave functions and includes additional atomic corrections. The updated PSFs and differential distributions, provided for the isotopes most studied in current experiments (GERDA, EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen, CUORE), enable more reliable decay-rate estimates and discrimination between DBD mechanisms, and support tests of the single-state dominance and higher-state dominance hypotheses.
