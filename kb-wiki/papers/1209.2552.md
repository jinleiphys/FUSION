# Near Threshold Proton-Proton Fusion in Effective Field Theory

> The astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton fusion, S_11(E), is obtained with the nuclear matrix element analytically calculated in pionless effective field theory. To the third order, the zero-energy result S_11(0) and the first energy derivative S'_11(0) are found to be (3.99 \pm 0.14)* 10^-25 MeV b and S_11(0)*(11.3 \pm 0.1) MeV^-1, respectively; both consistent with the current adopted values. The second energy derivative is also calculated for the first time, and the result S"_11(0) = S_11(0)*(170 \pm 2) MeV^-2 contributes at the level of 0.5% to the fusion rate at the solar center, which is smaller than 1% as previously estimated.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton fusion, S_11(E), is calculated analytically in pionless effective field theory to third order, yielding a zero-energy result S_11(0) = (3.99 ± 0.14) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b and a first energy derivative S'_11(0) = S_11(0) × (11.3 ± 0.1) MeV⁻¹ that are both consistent with currently adopted values, while the second energy derivative S''_11(0) = S_11(0) × (170 ± 2) MeV⁻² is computed for the first time and contributes at 0.5% to the fusion rate at the solar center, smaller than the previously estimated 1%.

## Method

The orbital matrix element Λ²(E) for the pp → d e⁺ νₑ reaction was extracted from the analytically computed cross section of the analogue process νₑ + d → p + p + e⁻ in pionless effective field theory (EFT), where pions are integrated out and nucleon-nucleon interactions are described by point-like contact interactions. The S-factor was constructed from Λ²(E) combined with the phase space factor f_pp^R(E) including outer radiative corrections, using the two-body axial current coupling L_1,A determined from reactor antineutrino and solar neutrino deuteron breakup data. The expansion of Λ²(E) in powers of energy was carried out to order ε² (next-to-next-to-leading order) with 3% higher-order corrections assigned from the ε³ terms.

## Key numbers

- S_11(0) = (3.99 ± 0.14) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b
- S'_11(0) = S_11(0) × (11.3 ± 0.1) MeV⁻¹
- S''_11(0) = S_11(0) × (170 ± 2) MeV⁻²
- The second derivative contributes 0.5% to the fusion rate at the solar center (temperature ~1.55 × 10⁷ K)
- Λ²(0) = 7.01 (1 ± 3% ± 3%) (first error from L_1,A uncertainty, second from higher-order corrections)
- c₁ = 2.24 (1 ± 0.7% ± 3%) and c₂ = 34.2 (1 ± 0.1% ± 3%) for the expansion Λ²(E) = Λ²(0) (1 + c₁ (E/MeV) + c₂ (E/MeV)²)
- L_1,A = (3.4 ± 3.7) fm³ from combined fits to two-nucleon data
- f_pp^R(E) = 0.144 (1 + 9.04 (E/MeV) + 30.7 (E/MeV)²) with outer radiative correction δ_pp^out = 1.62%
- pp scattering length a = -7.82 fm and effective range r₀ = 2.79 fm for the ¹S₀ channel
- Deuteron binding momentum γ = 45.70 MeV, binding energy E_d = 2.224573 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of solar fusion reactions previously applied to neutrino-deuteron breakup processes by Butler, Chen, and collaborators (2000-2002), and provides the first analytic calculation of the second energy derivative of the pp fusion S-factor. The results are consistent with the recommended values from the SFII review by Adelberger et al. (2010) and the earlier work of Bahcall and May (1968), but the second derivative contribution of 0.5% is smaller than the ~1% previously estimated by Bahcall and May (1968) and recommended for calculation in SFII. This enables more precise solar model predictions and reduces the theoretical uncertainty in the pp chain reaction rate at solar energies.
