# A simple relation between the gamma N -> N(1535) helicity amplitudes

> It is shown that the helicity amplitudes A_1/2 and S_1/2 in the gamma N -> N(1535) reaction, can be well related by S_1/2 = -\frac{\sqrt{1+τ}}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{M_S^2-M^2}{2 M_S Q} A_1/2 in the region Q^2 > 2 GeV^2, where M and M_S are the nucleon and N(1535) masses, q^2=-Q^2 the four-momentum transfer squared, and tau=\sfrac{Q^2}{(M_S+M)^2}. The observed relation is tested by the experimental data and the MAID parametrization. A direct consequence of the relation is that the assumption,|A_1/2| >> |S_1/2|, is not valid for high Q^2. Instead, both amplitudes A_1/2 and S_1/2 have the same Q^2 dependence in the high Q^2 region, aside from that S_{1/2} has an extra factor,-\sfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sfrac{M_S-M}{2M_S}. The origin of this relation is interpreted in a perspective of a quark model.

# A simple relation between the gamma N -> N(1535) helicity amplitudes

## Key claim

The authors show that the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} for the gamma N -> N(1535) reaction satisfy a simple scaling relation, S_{1/2} = -((sqrt(1+tau))/sqrt(2)) * ((M_S^2 - M^2)/(2 M_S Q)) * A_{1/2}, in the region Q^2 > 2 GeV^2, with the caveat that this relation follows from the empirical observation that the Pauli-type transition form factor F_2^* is approximately zero for Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2, a condition that has not been observed in similar reactions like gamma N -> N or gamma N -> N(1440).

## Method

The authors analyze the gamma N -> N(1535) reaction using a covariant form factor representation of the transition current, expressing the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} in terms of the Dirac-type form factor F_1^* and Pauli-type form factor F_2^*. They extract the condition F_2^* approximately 0 from experimental data and the MAID parametrization, then derive the scaling relation between the helicity amplitudes. The relation is tested against the MAID parametrization and interpreted within a covariant spectator quark model developed in a prior work (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2011).

## Key numbers

- The scaling relation holds for Q^2 > 2 GeV^2 (derived from the condition F_2^* approximately 0 for Q^2 > 1.5 GeV^2).
- The approximation |q| approximately sqrt(1+tau) * ((M_S+M)/(2 M_S)) * Q has a precision better than 10% for Q^2 > 1.8 GeV^2.
- The ratio S_{1/2}/A_{1/2} converges to -1/sqrt(2) * (M_S - M)/(2 M_S) approximately -0.13 in the limit Q^2 -> infinity.
- The spectator quark model gives an excellent description of the F_1^* data for Q^2 > 2.3 GeV^2.
- The condition Q^2 >> (M_S + M)^2 = 6.1 GeV^2 is required for the asymptotic convergence, meaning convergence is very slow.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the gamma N -> N(1535) reaction beyond the common assumption that |A_{1/2}| >> |S_{1/2}|, which the authors show is not valid at high Q^2. The scaling relation is tested against the MAID parametrization and CLAS data from Jefferson Lab (Dalton, 2009). The result is interpreted using the covariant spectator quark model of Ramalho and Tsushima (2011), where the cancellation between valence quark and meson cloud effects explains the vanishing of F_2^*. The work enables future experimental tests of the scaling relation at high Q^2, particularly for the S_{1/2} amplitude, and clarifies the role of constituent quark models versus dynamically generated resonance descriptions of the N(1535) (Kaiser, 1995; Jido, 2008).
