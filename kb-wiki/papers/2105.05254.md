# Neutron star structure with a new force between quarks

> The discovery of nondiffuse sources of gravitational waves through compact-object mergers opens new prospects for the study of physics beyond the Standard Model. In this paper, we study the effects of a new force between quarks, suggested by the gauging of baryon number, on pure neutron matter at supranuclear densities. This leads to a stiffening of the equation of state, allowing neutron stars to be both larger and heavier and possibly accommodating the light progenitor of GW190814 as a neutron star. The role of conventional three-body forces in neutron star structure is still poorly understood, though they can act in a similar way, implying that the mass and radius do not in themselves resolve whether new physics is coming into play. However, a crucial feature of the scenario we propose is that the regions of the new physics parameter space that induce observable changes to neutron star structure are testable at low-energy accelerator facilities. This testability distinguishes our scenario from other classes of new phenomena in dense matter.

# Neutron star structure with a new force between quarks

## Key claim
A new repulsive force between first-generation quarks, arising from a gauged U(1)\(_{B_1}\) symmetry with a mediator mass of roughly 0.2–1 GeV and coupling \(g_B\) up to about 0.4, can stiffen the neutron matter equation of state enough to increase neutron star maximum masses by 0.1–0.5 \(M_\odot\) relative to the APR baseline, but the effect is degenerate with conventional three-body forces; the distinguishing feature is that the relevant parameter space is testable at low-energy accelerator facilities through rare \(\eta^{(\prime)}\) decays and \(X\) photoproduction.

## Method
The authors compute the pure neutron matter equation of state using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with the AV18 two-nucleon potential plus a Yukawa repulsive potential \(V_\text{NP} = (\alpha_{B_1}/r) e^{-M_X r}\) from the new gauge boson. They then add the NP-induced energy-per-nucleon difference to the APR equation of state (which includes the Urbana IX three-body force) and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations. Constraints on the \(\alpha_{B_1}\)–\(M_X\) parameter space are derived from rare \(\eta^{(\prime)}\) decay widths using a vector meson dominance model.

## Key numbers
- Maximum stable causal neutron star mass without new physics (AV18 only, BHF): \(\approx 1.8 M_\odot\)
- Maximum TOV mass with APR equation of state plus new physics: \(\sim 2.1 M_\odot\), consistent with PSR J0740+6620 at \(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\)
- Energy per nucleon at saturation density \(n_\text{sat} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with AV18 only: \(E/A = 13.7\) MeV (compared to 13.4 MeV from literature)
- Energy per nucleon at \(n = 0.3\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with AV18 only: 26.0 MeV
- Change to the \(nn\) \(^1S_0\) scattering length in the Born limit: \(\Delta a_{^1S_0} \approx (0.5\ \text{fm}) \times \alpha_{B_1} (600\ \text{MeV}/M_X)^2\)
- New physics increases \(M_\text{TOV}\) by \(0.1\)–\(0.5 M_\odot\) relative to APR baseline for the explored parameter range
- Constraint from \(\eta \to \pi^0 \gamma \gamma\) requires NP contribution not exceed \(3 \times 10^{-4}\) in branching ratio

## Context
This work extends the study of new baryonic forces (Lee 1955, Okun 2006, Dobrescu 2014) to neutron star structure, complementing earlier work on new forces for strange quarks (Krivoruchenko 2009). The authors contrast their mechanism with the quarkyonic matter equation of state of McLerran (2018) and with relativistic mean-field models (Fattoyev 2020) that can reach 2.6 \(M_\odot\) but face challenges from heavy-ion collision constraints (Danielewicz 2002). The paper enables future searches for light gauge bosons at the JLab Eta Factory and REDTOP experiments, and it identifies that mass-radius measurements alone cannot distinguish new physics from conventional three-body forces, but accelerator tests can break this degeneracy.
