# Narrow exotic hadrons in the heavy quark limit of QCD

> This paper focuses on tetraquarks containing two heavy quarks in the formal limit where the heavy quark masses are taken to be arbitrarily large. There are well-established model-independent arguments for the existence of deeply bound exotic $\bar{q}\bar{q}'QQ$ tetraquark in the formal limit of arbitrarily large heavy quark masses. However, these previous arguments did not address the question whether tetraquark states exist close to the threshold for breaking up into two heavy mesons. Such states are not stable under strong interactions---they can emit pions and decay to lower-lying tetraquark states. This raises the issue of whether the nature of the heavy quark limit requires such states to exist as resonances that are narrow. Here we present a model-independent argument that establishes the existence of parametrically narrow tetraquark states. The argument here is based on Born-Oppenheimer and semi-classical considerations. The results derived here are only valid in a formal limit that is well outside the regime occurring for charm quarks in nature. However the analysis may give some insight in the experimentally observed narrow near-threshold tetraquark states containing a heavy quark and antiquark.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent argument, based on Born-Oppenheimer and semi-classical considerations, that establishes the existence of parametrically narrow tetraquark states in the formal limit where heavy quark masses are taken to be arbitrarily large; however, the results are only valid in this formal limit, which is well outside the regime occurring for charm quarks in nature.

## Method

The authors analyze tetraquarks containing two heavy quarks (QQ) in the limit m_Q → ∞ by exploiting scale separation between slow heavy-quark motion and hadronic-scale dynamics. They use Born-Oppenheimer and semi-classical analysis combined with heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory to describe the long-distance interaction between two heavy mesons via one-pion exchange, and then show that the full QCD Hamiltonian can be decomposed into a Schrödinger-equation piece and a remainder, where the remainder converts bound states of the Schrödinger equation into narrow resonances whose width goes to zero as m_Q → ∞.

## Key numbers

- The long-range one-pion exchange potential for I=1, J=0, positive parity states is given by Ṽ_long(r) = −(I(I+1) − 3/4) (g² m_π²)/(π f_π²) e^(−m_π r)/r, where f_π ≈ 93 MeV is the pion decay constant.
- The number of bound states with binding energy less than B grows at least as n_B > (√(M_Q B))/(2√2 m_π) ~ √(M_Q B)/Λ, where Λ is a characteristic hadronic scale.
- The total number of bound doubly heavy tetraquark states in any energy range grows as √(M_Q) as M_Q → ∞.

## Context

This work extends previous model-independent arguments for deeply bound exotic q̄q̄'QQ tetraquarks in the heavy quark limit by Cohen (2006) and Manohar (1993), which did not address near-threshold states or their widths. The paper addresses the theoretical gap left by those earlier analyses, which treated tetraquarks as non-relativistic bound states of two heavy mesons but became problematic for near-threshold states unstable against pion emission. The analysis may give insight into experimentally observed narrow near-threshold tetraquark states containing a heavy quark and antiquark, such as the X, Y, and Z mesons discovered over the past decade and a half.
