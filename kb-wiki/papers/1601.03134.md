# A systematic approach to sketch Bethe-Salpeter equation

> To study meson properties, one needs to solve the gap equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation for the meson wavefunction, self-consistently. The gluon propagator, the quark-gluon vertex, and the quark--anti-quark scattering kernel are key pieces to solve those equations. Predicted by lattice-QCD and Dyson-Schwinger analyses of QCD's gauge sector, gluons are non-perturbatively massive. In the matter sector, the modeled gluon propagator which can produce a veracious description of meson properties needs to possess a mass scale, accordingly. Solving the well-known longitudinal Ward-Green-Takahashi identities (WGTIs) and the less-known transverse counterparts together, one obtains a nontrivial solution which can shed light on the structure of the quark-gluon vertex. It is highlighted that the phenomenologically proposed anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM) vertex originates from the QCD Lagrangian symmetries and its strength is proportional to the magnitude of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB). The color-singlet vector and axial-vector WGTIs can relate the BS kernel and the dressed quark-gluon vertex to each other. Using the relation, one can truncate the gap equation and the BS equation, systematically, without violating crucial symmetries, e.g., gauge symmetry and chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons can be truncated systematically without violating gauge or chiral symmetry by using the relation between the quark–anti-quark scattering kernel and the dressed quark-gluon vertex derived from color-singlet vector and axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identities, but the approach relies on an Abelian approximation (ignoring ghost fields) and a specific parameterized gluon propagator.

## Method

The author models the gluon propagator with a massive-type form fitted to lattice QCD data, then solves the longitudinal and transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi identities together to obtain the full quark-gluon vertex structure, including the anomalous chromomagnetic moment terms. The color-singlet vector and axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identities for the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations are then used to relate the quark–anti-quark scattering kernel to the dressed quark-gluon vertex, enabling a symmetry-preserving truncation of the gap equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key numbers

- Gluon mass scale from lattice QCD fits: \(M_g \sim 700\) MeV, from fitting the parameterized gluon propagator to lattice data (Oliveira 2010).
- Infrared coupling strength in the simplest rainbow-ladder approximation: \(\alpha_{\rm RL}(0)/\pi \sim 10\).
- Infrared coupling strength in the DCSB-improved approximation: \(\alpha_{\rm DB}(0)/\pi \sim 2\).
- Parameters for the interaction model: \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.234\) GeV; \(\gamma_m = 12/25\); \(\tau = e^2 - 1\).
- The product \(D\omega\) (interaction strength times interaction width) is fixed to obtain a uniformly good description of pseudoscalar and vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD, building on earlier studies of the gluon propagator (Cornwall 1981; Aguilar 2002; Oliveira 2010) and the quark-gluon vertex (Ball 1980; Chang 2010). It addresses the failure of the bare-vertex, one-gluon-exchange approximation for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel to describe meson spectra above 1 GeV, such as \(a_1\) and \(b_1\) mesons and radial excitations of pion and rho meson (Qin 2011; Chang 2011). The systematic truncation enables future calculations of meson form factors and baryon properties in the diquark picture.
