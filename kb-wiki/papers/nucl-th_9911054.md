# Spin-dependent correlations and the semi-exclusive ^{16}O(e,e'p) reaction

> The effect of central, tensor and spin-isospin nucleon-nucleon correlations upon semi-exclusive ^{16}O(e,e'p) reactions is studied for Q^2 and Bjorken x values in the range $0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1.1 (GeV/c)^2$ and 0.15 $\lesssim$ x $\lesssim$ 2. The fully unfactorized calculations are performed in a framework that accounts not only for the dynamical coupling of virtual photons to correlated nucleon pairs but also for meson-exchange and $Δ_{33}$-isobar currents. Tensor correlations are observed to produce substantially larger amounts of semi-exclusive ^{16}O(e,e'p) strength than central correlations do and are predominantly manifest in the proton-neutron knockout channel. With the exception of the $x \approx 2$ case, in all kinematical situations studied the meson-exchange and isobar currents are a strong source of A(e,e'p) strength at deep missing energies. This feature gives the A(e,e'p) strength at deep missing energies a pronounced transverse character.

# Spin-dependent correlations and the semi-exclusive \(^{16}\)O(e,e'p) reaction

## Key claim

Tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations produce substantially larger semi-exclusive \(^{16}\)O(e,e'p) strength than central correlations do, and this tensor-driven strength is predominantly manifest in the proton-neutron knockout channel; however, with the exception of the \(x \approx 2\) case, meson-exchange and isobar currents are a strong source of A(e,e'p) strength at deep missing energies, giving that strength a pronounced transverse character.

## Method

The authors performed fully unfactorized calculations of semi-exclusive \(^{16}\)O(e,e'p) cross sections by explicitly evaluating two-nucleon knockout cross sections and integrating over the phase space of the undetected nucleon. They implemented central, spin-isospin, and tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations via an effective operator approach (Unitary Correlation Operator Method) applied to mean-field wave functions from a Hartree-Fock calculation with Skyrme force, and included meson-exchange and \(\Delta_{33}\)-isobar two-body currents. The calculations were carried out for three kinematic regions corresponding to Bjorken \(x \approx 0.15\), \(x \approx 1\) (quasi-elastic), and \(x \approx 2\), with momentum transfers in the range \(0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: \(0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)
- Bjorken \(x\) range studied: \(0.15 \lesssim x \lesssim 2\)
- The central correlation function heals to zero at \(r_{12} \geq 2.5\) fm
- In momentum space, spin-dependent correlation functions become comparable in magnitude to the central correlation function for momenta in the range 200–400 MeV/c
- The hadronic form factor cutoff used for regularizing two-body currents: \(\Lambda_\pi = 1250\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the unfactorized model of Ryckebusch et al. (1997) for semi-exclusive A(e,e'p) reactions, which previously included only central (Jastrow) correlations, to incorporate spin-dependent (tensor and spin-isospin) nucleon-nucleon correlations. It contrasts with factorized approaches (Benhar et al., 1999; Morita et al., 1999; Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1999) that separate the electronuclear part from nuclear structure information and cannot account for competing multi-body mechanisms like meson-exchange currents. The results enable identification of kinematic conditions at TJNAF where meson-exchange and isobar current contributions to semi-exclusive (e,e'p) strength can be minimized, allowing cleaner access to short-range correlation signals.
