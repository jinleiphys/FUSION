# Three-body Effect in Short-range Correlations

> Short-range correlations (SRCs) provide the link between low- and high-energy nuclear physics and can be quantified by two-nucleon densities. We present calculations of the two-nucleon densities using free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG)-evolved operators and in-medium SRG (IMSRG) ground states with softend chiral interaction. Our calculations benchmark well against no-core shell model (NCSM) results with unevolved oparetors and Hamiltonians in $^4\mathrm{He}$. We explicitly include the induced three-body (3b) density operators for the first time which, together with the 3b Hamiltonians, provide the full 3b effects. We show pronounced 3b effects in the $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ two-nucleon densities. Combined with valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) method, we extend the calculation to the oxygen isotopic chain. This approach enables a consistent \textit{ab initio} description of low-energy properties and SRCs within one framework and offers predictions for the upcoming SRC measurements in unstable nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that induced three-body (3b) density operators, included for the first time alongside 3b Hamiltonians, produce pronounced effects on two-nucleon densities in $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ that are negligible in $^4\mathrm{He}$, with the 3b Hamiltonian contribution mainly suppressing short-range coordinate-space densities and the 3b operator contribution primarily modifying the medium/high-momentum tail of momentum-space distributions.

## Method

The authors employ free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to soften chiral NN and 3N interactions and consistently evolve the two-nucleon density operator, retaining induced terms up to the three-body level. The evolved Hamiltonians and operators are then used in in-medium SRG (IMSRG) calculations for closed-shell nuclei and valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) for open-shell nuclei, with normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation applied to the 3b Hamiltonians. The approach is benchmarked against no-core shell model (NCSM) results in $^4\mathrm{He}$ using unevolved operators and Hamiltonians, then extended to $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and the oxygen isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- Two-nucleon densities in $^4\mathrm{He}$ benchmarked against J-NCSM with unevolved operators at $\hbar\omega = 36$ MeV and $N_{\mathrm{max}}=18$, showing close agreement between NN-only and NN+3N results
- For $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, results presented as bands spanning $\lambda = 2.0$–$2.4~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ resolution scale, with 3b Hamiltonian contribution $\Delta_H$ suppressing $\rho_2(r)$ at short distances and 3b operator contribution $\Delta_O$ modifying $\rho_2(k)$ around $k\approx2$ fm$^{-1}$
- Ground-state energy of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ shown as function of $\lambda$ from 2.0 to 2.4 fm$^{-1}$, with inclusion of induced 3N terms reducing $\lambda$ dependence and improving agreement with experimental value
- Calculations performed in HO basis with $\hbar\omega = 20$ MeV, truncating single-particle excitations at $e_{\mathrm{max}}=10$ and 3b Hamiltonians/operators at $E_{3\mathrm{max}}=12$

## Context

This work extends previous SRG-evolved operator calculations that were mostly confined to two-body truncations (Neff 2015, Tropiano 2024) or limited to $^4\mathrm{He}$ for explicit 3b operator studies (Schuster 2014). The consistent inclusion of induced 3b density operators with 3b Hamiltonians enables ab initio calculations of short-range correlations in medium-mass nuclei, addressing the need for theoretical predictions motivated by recent experimental advances in probing SRCs in unstable nuclei (Patsyuk 2021, Ye 2024). The approach provides a framework for connecting low-energy nuclear properties and SRC observables across the nuclear chart.
