# S-shell $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei based on chiral interactions

> We generalize the Jacobi no-core shell model (J-NCSM) to study double-strangeness hypernuclei. All particle conversions in the strangeness $S=-1,-2$ sectors are explicitly taken into account. In two-body space, such transitions may lead to the coupling between states of identical particles and of non-identical ones. Therefore, a careful consideration is required when determining the combinatorial factors that connect the many-body potential matrix elements and the free-space two-body potentials. Using second quantization, we systematically derive the combinatorial factors in question for $S=0,-1,-2$ sectors. As a first application, we use the J-NCSM to investigate $ΛΛ$ s-shell hypernuclei based on hyperon-hyperon (YY) potentials derived within chiral effective field theory at leading order (LO) and up to next-to-leading order (NLO). We find that the LO potential overbinds $^{\text{ }\text{ }\text{ } \text{}6}_{ΛΛ}\text{He}$ while the prediction of the NLO interaction is close to experiment. Both interactions also yield a bound state for $^{\text{ }\text{ }\text{ } \text{}5}_{ΛΛ}\text{He}$. The $^{\text{}\text{ }\text{ }\text{}4}_{ΛΛ}\text{H}$ system is predicted to be unbound.

## Key claim

The chiral next-to-leading order (NLO) hyperon-hyperon interaction predicts a binding energy for \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{He}\) close to experiment, while the leading order (LO) potential overbinds this nucleus; both interactions predict a bound \(^{5}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{He}\) and an unbound \(^{4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{H}\), but these predictions depend on the explicit inclusion of all particle conversions in the strangeness \(S=-1,-2\) sectors and on the extrapolation of the Jacobi no-core shell model results to infinite model space.

## Method

The authors generalized the Jacobi no-core shell model (J-NCSM) to double-strangeness hypernuclei by constructing two orthogonal sets of many-body basis states: one for systems with two singly-strange hyperons (\(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Lambda\Sigma\), \(\Sigma\Sigma\)) and one for systems containing a \(\Xi\) hyperon. They derived the combinatorial factors connecting many-body potential matrix elements to free-space two-body potentials using second quantization, explicitly accounting for all particle conversions (\(\Lambda N \leftrightarrow \Sigma N\), \(\Lambda\Lambda \leftrightarrow \Xi N\), \(\Lambda\Sigma \leftrightarrow \Xi N\), \(\Sigma\Sigma \leftrightarrow \Xi N\)). The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis, and binding energies were extrapolated to infinite model space using a two-step procedure (energy minimization with respect to oscillator frequency followed by exponential fit in \(\mathcal{N}_{max}\)).

## Key numbers

- \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{He}\): the LO chiral potential overbinds this nucleus, while the NLO prediction is close to experiment (no explicit numerical binding energy given in the truncated text).
- \(^{5}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{He}\): both LO and NLO interactions yield a bound state (no explicit numerical binding energy given in the truncated text).
- \(^{4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{H}\): predicted to be unbound by both LO and NLO interactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous J-NCSM studies of single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei up to \(A=7\) (Le, 2019; Le, 2020) to the double-strangeness sector, incorporating the full complexity of tensor forces and channel coupling. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the stochastic variational method of Nemura (1999, 2004) which used phenomenological central potentials, the cluster-model calculations of Hiyama (2001, 2002, 2010) which employed simulated G-matrix potentials, and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations of Filikhin and Gal (2002, 2003) which restricted \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interactions mainly to s-wave. The results enable direct comparison of chiral effective field theory predictions for hyperon-hyperon interactions with experimental double-strangeness hypernuclei, providing constraints for the \(S=-2\) baryon-baryon interaction where direct scattering experiments are impossible.
