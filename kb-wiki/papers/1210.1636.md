# Cluster properties in Poincare invariant quantum mechanics

> Using a simple model we provide a quantitative study of the size of the corrections needed to restore cluster properties to the construction of Poincare invariant dynamical models with kinematic spins, first provided by B. Bakamjian and L. H. Thomas. Our model calculations suggest that these corrections are too small to have a quantitative impact on nuclear physics observables calculated using models with meson and nucleon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Using a simple four-body model with nuclear-physics scales, the authors provide a quantitative evaluation of the operators that restore cluster properties in Bakamjian-Thomas formulations of relativistic few-body quantum mechanics, and find that the corrections are too small to have a quantitative impact on nuclear physics observables at current levels of experimental precision.

## Method

The authors constructed a four-body model consisting of a three-particle system where two particles form a bound state via a relativistic Malfliet-Tjon type potential, plus an external probe that interacts weakly with the third particle. They formulated the three-body system both as a 2+1 tensor-product representation and as a 2+1 Bakamjian-Thomas representation, then computed current matrix elements in both formulations for Dirac's front-form, instant-form, and point-form dynamics. The difference between these matrix elements provides a measure of how much the Sokolov unitary operators differ from the identity, which quantifies the size of the cluster property violations.

## Key numbers

- The violations of cluster properties are a few parts in a thousand, which is well within the size of both theoretical and experimental uncertainties in relativistic nuclear physics observables.
- The quantity F(q, p12), which measures the violation of cluster properties, was computed for p12 perpendicular and parallel to q = p3' - p3 in all three Dirac forms (front-form, instant-form, point-form), with all six plots showing violations of comparable size.
- The violations increase with stronger binding or for wave functions with higher mean momentum, but for scales associated with realistic nuclear-nucleon interactions the violations remain small.

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) for Poincare invariant quantum mechanics, which satisfied cluster properties at the two-body level but not for systems of more than two particles. Coester (1965) applied the construction to three-body systems and showed the S-matrix satisfied cluster properties, but his result did not extend to the unitary representation of the Poincare group or to systems of four or more particles. Sokolov (1977) provided a complete solution using unitary operators, but these operators had never been computed in any applications. This paper provides the first quantitative calculation of Sokolov's operators and demonstrates that the corrections needed to restore cluster properties are negligible for current nuclear physics applications, enabling practitioners to continue using the simpler Bakamjian-Thomas construction without computing the cluster property corrections.
