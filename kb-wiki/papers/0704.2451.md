# Tilting instability in negative-γrotating nuclei

> Based on the cranking model and the random phase approximation, we point out that the wobbling excitation on top of the s band in ^{182}Os is stable against angular momentum tilting. This is consistent with the general trend that the wobbling excitations in γ<0 rotating nuclei are more stable than those in γ>0 ones found in our previous studies. In higher N isotopes known to be γsoft, however, a different type of tilting instability is expected. Its possible correspondence to the experimental data is also discussed.

## Key claim

The wobbling excitation on the $s$ band of $^{182}$Os is stable against angular momentum tilting, consistent with the general trend that wobbling excitations in $\gamma<0$ rotating nuclei are more stable than those in $\gamma>0$ ones. However, in higher $N$ isotopes known to be $\gamma$ soft, a different type of tilting instability is expected, characterized by $\mathcal{J}_x = \mathcal{J}_z$ rather than $\mathcal{J}_x = \mathcal{J}_y$.

## Method

The author uses the cranking model with a Nilsson Hamiltonian plus pairing, treating quadrupole deformation parameters $\epsilon_2$ and $\gamma$ as fixed inputs, and performs random phase approximation (RPA) calculations with a doubly stretched quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction. The RPA dispersion relation is solved to obtain the wobbling excitation energy and the dynamical moments of inertia for the $s$ band of $^{186}$Os, assuming a $(\nu i_{13/2})^2$ quasiparticle configuration.

## Key numbers

- For $^{186}$Os, the adopted mean field parameters are $\epsilon_2 = 0.205$, $\gamma = -32^\circ$, and pairing gaps $\Delta_n = \Delta_p = 0.4$ MeV.
- The wobbling angle $\theta_\mathrm{wob}$ is approximately $15^\circ$ up to just below the instability point.
- At the instability point, the wobbling angles reach $\theta_\mathrm{wob} > 45^\circ$ and $\varphi_\mathrm{wob} = 90^\circ$, indicating the angular momentum vector tilts to the $x$-$z$ plane.
- The instability occurs at around $\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.310$ MeV in the calculation, while the observed crossing in $^{186}$Os takes place between the $12^+$ and $14^+$ members of the ground state band, corresponding to $\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.356$ MeV and $0.389$ MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous RPA studies of wobbling motion in $^{182}$Os (Matsuzaki, 1990; Shimizu and Matsuzaki, 1995) and positive-$\gamma$ nuclei like $^{147}$Gd (Matsuzaki et al., 2002; Matsuzaki and Ohtsubo, 2004). It directly addresses a contradiction with Hashimoto and Horibata (2006), who concluded no wobbling excitation exists on the $s$ band of $^{182}$Os; the author attributes this to their use of a quasiproton alignment leading to positive $\gamma$, whereas the present work uses quasineutron alignment leading to negative $\gamma$. The predicted instability in $^{186}$Os may correspond to the observed yrast band anomaly at $14^+$ reported by Balabanski et al. (2001), though the quantitative agreement is insufficient.
