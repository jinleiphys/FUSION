# Beam-normal single-spin asymmetry in elastic scattering of electrons from a spin-0 nucleus

> We study the beam-normal single-spin asymmetry (BNSSA) in high-energy elastic electron scattering from several spin-0 nuclei. Existing theoretical approaches work in the plane-wave formalism and predict the BNSSA to scale as $\sim A/Z$ with the atomic number $Z$ and nuclear mass number $A$. While this prediction holds for light and intermediate nuclei, a striking disagreement in both the sign and the magnitude of BNSSA was observed by the PREX collaboration for $^{208}$Pb, coined the "PREX puzzle". To shed light on this disagreement, we go beyond the plane-wave approach which neglects Coulomb distortions known to be significant for heavy nuclei. We explicitly investigate the dependence of BNSSA on $A$ and $Z$ by i) including inelastic intermediate states' contributions into the Coulomb problem in the form of an optical potential, ii) by accounting for the experimental information on the $A$-dependence of the Compton slope parameter, and iii) giving a thorough account of the uncertainties of the calculation. Despite of these improvements, the PREX puzzle remains unexplained. We discuss further strategies to resolve this riddle.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even after including Coulomb distortions, inelastic intermediate states via an optical potential, and experimental information on the A-dependence of the Compton slope parameter, the "PREX puzzle", a striking disagreement in both sign and magnitude of the beam-normal single-spin asymmetry for ²⁰⁸Pb, remains unexplained.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for electrons scattering from spin-0 nuclei, including both a Coulomb potential from the nuclear charge distribution and an absorptive potential representing inelastic hadronic excitations in two-photon exchange. The absorptive potential was constructed from the imaginary part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, using experimental Compton scattering data to determine the t-dependence of the relevant amplitudes via a Regge model, and the partial-wave expansion was solved numerically using a modified version of the ELSEPA package.

## Key numbers

- The beam-normal single-spin asymmetry was computed for spin-0 nuclei including ⁴He, ¹²C, ²⁸Si, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb at electron beam energies above 1 GeV.
- The Compton slope parameter B was extracted from fits to data at ω ≈ 3 GeV and ω = 5 GeV; for ¹²C at ω = 5 GeV, B = 10.0 ± 2.1 GeV⁻²; for ¹⁹⁷Au at ω = 5 GeV, B = 56.7 ± 8.2 GeV⁻².
- The master integrals for the collinear photon singularity yield I₀ ≈ ln(|t|/m²) which is of order 10 for |t| ≈ 0.01 GeV², and I₁ ≈ ln(4E²/m²) which is of order 25 for E in the GeV range.
- Removing the effect of the real part of the Compton amplitude from the differential cross section corresponds to a change in the slope parameter B of approximately 2.2 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the plane-wave formalism of Gorchtein (2008) which successfully described BNSSA for light and intermediate-mass nuclei (⁴He, ¹²C, ²⁸Si, ⁹⁰Zr) but failed for ²⁰⁸Pb in the PREX measurement by Abrahamyan (2012). The authors join the distorted-wave Born approximation approach of Cooper (2005) with the two-photon exchange formalism, but the PREX puzzle persists. Future resolution may require including multi-photon exchange effects beyond the two-photon approximation or improved treatment of nuclear structure for very heavy nuclei.
