# Adiabatic model of $(d,p)$ reactions with explicitly energy-dependent non-local potentials

> We have developed an approximate way of dealing with explicit energy-dependence of non-local nucleon optical potentials as used to predict the $(d,p)$ cross sections within the adiabatic theory. Within this approximation, the non-local optical potentials have to be evaluated at an energy shifted from half the incident deuteron energy by the $n-p$ kinetic energy averaged over the range of the $n-p$ interaction and then treated as an energy-independent non-local potential. Thus the evaluation of the distorting potential in the incident channel is reduced to a problem solved in our previous work in [{\it Phys. Rev. Lett. 110, 112501(2013) and Phys. Rev. C 87, 064610 (2013)}]. We have demonstrated how our new model works for the case of $^{16}$O$(d,p)^{17}$O, $^{36}$Ar($d,p)^{37}$Ar and $^{40}$Ca$(d,p)^{41}$Ca reactions and highlighted the need for a detailed understanding of energy-dependence of non-local potentials. We have also suggested a simple way of correcting the $d-A$ effective potentials for non-locality when the underlying energy-dependent non-local nucleon potentials are unknown but energy-dependent local phenomenological nucleon potentials are available.

## Key claim

The paper develops an approximate method to incorporate explicit energy dependence of non-local nucleon optical potentials into the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) for \((d,p)\) reactions, where the non-local potentials must be evaluated at an energy shifted from half the incident deuteron energy by half the average \(n-p\) kinetic energy (approximately 57 MeV) rather than the intuitively assumed \(E_d/2\) value.

## Method

The authors use Feshbach projection operator techniques to derive the effective three-body Hamiltonian for the \(n+p+A\) system, then apply the ADWA to isolate the first Weinberg component of the incident wavefunction. They replace the operators \(T_p+U^0_p+V_{np}\) and \(T_n+U^0_n+V_{np}\) in the energy denominators by average values consistent with the dominance of the first Weinberg component, showing that the neutron and proton optical potentials should be evaluated at energy \(\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{1}{2}E_d + \frac{1}{2}\langle T_r \rangle\). The resulting energy-independent non-local potential problem is then solved using the approach from their previous work (Johnson and Timofeyuk, 2013).

## Key numbers

- The energy shift from half the incident deuteron energy is \(\frac{1}{2}\langle T_r \rangle\), approximately 57 MeV, coming from the average \(n-p\) kinetic energy within the range of the \(n-p\) potential.
- The \(n-p\) relative coordinate range restricted by the first Weinberg component \(\phi_1\) is about 0.45 fm.
- The new prescription is applied to \(^{16}\mathrm{O}(d,p)^{17}\mathrm{O}\) at \(E_d = 15\) MeV, \(^{36}\mathrm{Ar}(d,p)^{37}\mathrm{Ar}\) at \(E_d = 9\) MeV, and \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}(d,p)^{41}\mathrm{Ca}\) at \(E_d = 11.8\) MeV.
- The energy shift of approximately 40 MeV identified in the authors' previous work (Johnson and Timofeyuk, 2013) for energy-independent non-local potentials is close to but not equal to the 57 MeV shift derived here for explicitly energy-dependent potentials.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous adiabatic model for \((d,p)\) reactions with energy-independent non-local potentials (Johnson and Timofeyuk, 2013) to handle the explicit energy dependence that arises from Feshbach's formal theory of optical potentials. It addresses the limitation that global energy-dependent local potentials like CH89 (Varner et al., 1991) do not follow the Perey-Buck non-locality form, particularly for their imaginary parts. The method enables practical ADWA calculations using phenomenological energy-dependent non-local potentials when available, and provides a framework for understanding how to correct \(d-A\) effective potentials for non-locality when only local phenomenological potentials are known.
