# (Hybrid) Baryons: Quantum Numbers and Adiabatic Potentials

> We construct (hybrid) baryons in the flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton. In the limit of adiabatic quark motion, we build proper eigenstates of orbital angular momentum and indicate the flavour, spin, chirality and J^P of (hybrid) baryons. The adiabatic potential is calculated as a function of the quark positions.

# (Hybrid) Baryons: Quantum Numbers and Adiabatic Potentials

## Key claim

Hybrid baryons can be constructed in the flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton by treating the lowest flux excitation as a vibrating junction with an effective mass, and the adiabatic approximation (where flux motion adjusts instantaneously to quark motion) yields a simple model where the junction is connected via a linear potential to the three quarks. The ground state of the junction motion corresponds to a conventional baryon and the various excited states to hybrid baryons, with the lowest flux excitation simulated to about 5% accuracy by neglecting all flux-tube motions except the junction vibration.

## Method

The authors constructed (hybrid) baryons in the non-relativistic flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton, working in the adiabatic limit where quark motion is slow relative to flux motion. They solved the Schrodinger equation for the junction hamiltonian variationally using simple harmonic oscillator wave functions as ansatze: a ground state wave function for the conventional baryon potential and a first excited state wave function for the hybrid baryon H1 potential. The junction effective mass was taken in the limit where the flux-tubes between the junction and quarks are continuous strings, and the linear potential between the junction and the three quarks was included.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the hybrid to baryon potential is found to be 1.44 to 1.6 for all values of rho, lambda, and cos(theta_{rho lambda}).
- A preliminary estimate of the ground state H1 hybrid baryon mass is approximately 2 GeV.
- The lowest flux excitation can be simulated to about 5% accuracy by neglecting all flux-tube motions except the vibration of a junction.
- The difference of the hybrid and conventional baryon potentials was plotted for rho = 2.881 GeV^{-1} as a function of lambda and cos(theta_{rho lambda}).

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton to construct hybrid baryons with proper eigenstates of orbital angular momentum, building on the detailed flux dynamics and flux hamiltonian studied in Page (1997). The quantum number assignments differ from those in the bag model, where low-lying hybrid baryons with transverse electric gluon yield different N and Delta flavor and spin combinations. The preliminary mass estimate of approximately 2 GeV for the ground state H1 hybrid baryon was obtained by adding the potential difference to the phenomenologically successful baryon potential of Capstick and Isgur (1986), enabling future experimental searches for these states.
