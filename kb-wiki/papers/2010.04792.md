# Impact of Three-Body Forces on Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering Observables

> In a previous series of papers we investigated the domain of applicability of chiral potentials to the construction of a microscopic optical potential (OP) for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering. The final expression of the OP was a folding integral between the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) $t$ matrix and the nuclear density of the target. In these calculations $NN$ and three-nucleon ($3N$) chiral interactions were used for the target density and only the $NN$ interaction for the $NN$ $t$ matrix. The purpose of this work is to achieve another step towards the calculation of a more consistent OP introducing the $3N$ force also in the dynamic part of the OP. In the present work this is approximated with a density dependent $NN$ interaction obtained after the averaging over the Fermi sphere. In practice, in our model the $3N$ force acts as a medium correction of the bare $NN$ interaction used to calculate the $NN$ $t$ matrix. Even if the $3N$ force is treated in an approximate way, this method naturally extends our previous model of the OP and allows a direct comparison of our present and previous results. We consider as case studies the elastic scattering of nucleons off $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O. We present results for the differential cross section and the spin observables for different values of the projectile energy. From the comparison with the experimental data and with the results of our previous model we assess the importance of the $3N$ interaction in the dynamic part of the OP. Our analysis indicates that the contribution of the $3N$ force in the $t$ matrix is small for the differential cross section and it is sizable for the spin observables, in particular, for the analyzing power. A chiral expansion order-by-order analysis of the scattering observables confirms the convergence of our results at the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order, as already established in our previous work.

## Key claim

The authors introduce three-nucleon (3N) forces into the dynamic part of a microscopic optical potential for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering, but only in an approximate way by averaging the 3N force over the Fermi sphere to produce a density-dependent NN interaction; the contribution of the 3N force to the t matrix is small for differential cross sections but sizable for spin observables, particularly the analyzing power.

## Method

The optical potential was computed as a folding integral between a nucleon-nucleon (NN) t matrix and a one-body nuclear density matrix obtained from ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using NN and 3N chiral interactions. The 3N force was approximated as a density-dependent NN interaction by averaging the third nucleon momenta over the Fermi sphere, which introduced medium corrections to the bare NN interaction used to calculate the t matrix. The authors applied this model to elastic proton scattering off 12C and 16O at several projectile energies, computing differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and spin rotations.

## Key numbers

- For 16O at 200 MeV laboratory energy, the matter density was varied in the range 0.0 fm^-3 ≤ ρ ≤ 1.6 fm^-3 to show the impact of 3N forces.
- For 16O at 100, 135, and 318 MeV, the density range was restricted to 0.08 fm^-3 ≤ ρ ≤ 0.13 fm^-3 for the band calculations.
- The NCSM densities were computed with harmonic oscillator frequency ℏΩ = 16 MeV and Nmax = 8 for both 12C and 16O.
- The NN chiral interaction was used up to fifth order (N4LO) with a cutoff Λ = 500 MeV.
- The 3N chiral interaction was derived up to third order (N2LO) with cutoff values of 650 MeV and 500 MeV for local and nonlocal regularization, respectively.
- The low-energy constants used were cD = -1.8 and cE = -0.31 with the NN interaction at N4LO.
- The similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution scale was λSRG = 2.0 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers (Vorabbi et al., 2015-2020; Gennari et al., 2017) where microscopic optical potentials were constructed from chiral NN interactions only in the dynamic part, while NN and 3N interactions were used only for the target density. The present study introduces 3N forces into the dynamic part for the first time, following the density-dependent NN force method of Holt et al. (2010). This enables a more consistent treatment of the optical potential and allows assessment of 3N force contributions to scattering observables, with the next step being a full treatment of medium corrections beyond the impulse approximation.
