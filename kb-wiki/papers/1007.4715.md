# Holographic QCD Integrated back to Hidden Local Symmetry

> We develop a previously proposed gauge-invariant method to integrate out infinite tower of Kaluza-Klein (KK) modes of vector and axialvector mesons in a class of models of holographic QCD (HQCD). The HQCD is reduced by our method to the chiral perturbation theory with the hidden local symmetry (HLS) having only the lowest KK mode identified as the HLS gauge boson. We take the Sakai-Sugimoto model as a concrete HQCD, and completely determine the ${\cal O} (p^4)$ terms as well as the ${\cal O}(p^2)$ terms from the DBI part and the anomaly-related (intrinsic parity odd) gauge-invariant terms from the CS part. Effects of higher KK modes are fully included in these terms. To demonstrate power of our method, we compute momentum-dependences of several form factors such as the pion electromagnetic form factors, the $ü^0$-$ö°$ and $ü$-$ü^0$ transition form factors compared with experiment, which was not achieved before due to complication to handle infinite sums. We also study other anomaly-related quantities like $ö°^*$-$ü^0$-$ü^+$-$ü^-$ and $ü$-$ü^0$-$ü^+$-$ü^-$ vertex functions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a gauge-invariant method to integrate out the infinite tower of Kaluza-Klein (KK) modes of vector and axialvector mesons in holographic QCD models, reducing them to a hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective theory containing only the lowest KK mode (identified as the ü meson), with the crucial condition that the method preserves HLS and chiral symmetry only when the infinite sum over higher KK modes is properly accounted for, a naive truncation that simply neglects higher modes violates these symmetries.

## Method

The authors start with the five-dimensional Sakai-Sugimoto model, which contains a DBI action and a Chern-Simons (CS) action, and perform a KK decomposition of the bulk gauge fields into vector and axialvector meson fields. They then integrate out all higher KK modes (except the lowest vector meson) by setting the mass-eigenstate fields \(B_\mu^{(n)} = 0\) for \(n \neq 1\) via their equations of motion, rather than simply truncating the HLS gauge fields to zero, and substitute the resulting expression back into the five-dimensional action to obtain a four-dimensional HLS Lagrangian with fully determined \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\) terms.

## Key numbers

- The overall coupling \(G\) in the SS model is expressed as \(G = N_c g_{\rm YM}^2/(108 \pi^3)\), where \(g_{\rm YM}\) is the gauge coupling of the \(U(N_c)\) gauge symmetry on the \(N_c\) D4-branes.
- The mass scale \(M_{KK}\) is related to the compactification scale of the \(N_c\) D4-branes onto \(S^1\), and the paper notes the SS model may not be reliable beyond \(M_{KK} \sim 1\) GeV.
- The induced metric function is \(K(z) = 1 + z^2\), which appears in the DBI action and the eigenvalue equation \(-K^{1/3}(z) \partial_z (K(z) \partial_z \psi_n) = \lambda_n \psi_n\).
- The CS action is given by \(S_{\rm SS}^{CS}(A) = \frac{N_c}{24\pi^2} \int_{M^4 \times R} w_5(A)\), where \(w_5(A)\) is the CS five-form.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed method (Harada 2006) for integrating out KK modes in holographic QCD models, now applied in full detail to the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto 2004, 2005) including both the DBI and CS parts. The method enables computation of momentum-dependent form factors (pion electromagnetic form factor, \(\pi^0\)-\(\gamma\) and \(\omega\)-\(\pi^0\) transition form factors) that were previously unachievable due to the complexity of handling infinite sums over KK modes, and it provides a framework for calculating \(1/N_c\)-subleading corrections via HLS-ChPT loop expansions in future work.
