# Effective Field Theory for the Bound States and Scattering of a Heavy Charged Particle and a Neutral Atom

> We show the system of a heavy charged particle and a neutral atom can be described by a low-energy effective field theory where the attractive $1/r^4$ induced dipole potential determines the long-distance/low-energy wave functions. The $1/r^4$ interaction is renormalized by a contact interaction at leading order. Derivative corrections to that contact interaction give rise to higher-order terms. We show that this ``Induced-dipole EFT'' (ID-EFT) reproduces the $π^+$-hydrogen phase shifts of a more microscopic potential, the Temkin-Lamkin potential, over a wide range of energies. Already at leading order it also describes the highest-lying excited bound states of the pionic-hydrogen ion. Lower-lying bound states receive substantial corrections at next-to-leading order, with the size of the correction proportional to their distance from the scattering threshold. Our next-to-leading order calculation shows that the three highest-lying bound states of the Temkin-Lamkin potential are well-described in ID-EFT.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an effective field theory (ID-EFT) based on the attractive $1/r^4$ induced-dipole potential, renormalized by a contact interaction, can reproduce the phase shifts and highest-lying bound states of the Temkin-Lamkin potential for the $\pi^+$-hydrogen system, but only for states whose characteristic size is larger than the atom's size; deeper bound states receive substantial corrections and the EFT series does not converge for the three deepest states.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory (ID-EFT) where the long-range interaction is the $1/r^4$ induced-dipole potential with strength fixed by the hydrogen polarizability $\alpha_{\rm H}=9a_B^3/2$, and short-distance physics is captured by a contact interaction regulated at a radius $R$. At leading order (LO), the contact strength $g_{\rm LO}(R)$ is tuned to reproduce the scattering length $a_0 = -65$ a.u. from the Temkin-Lamkin potential. At next-to-leading order (NLO), an energy-dependent contact term with strength $g_{\rm NLO}(R)$ is added and fitted to the shallowest bound-state energy $B^{(6)} = 1.2\times10^{-4}$ a.u. The $S$-wave phase shifts are computed from the $T$-matrix at LO and via distorted-wave Born approximation at NLO, while bound-state energies are obtained from the radial Schrodinger equation with perturbative NLO corrections.

## Key numbers

* $\beta_4 = 32.7$ a.u.: the analog of the van der Waals length scale for the $\pi^+$-hydrogen system, defined as $\beta_4 \equiv \sqrt{\mu \alpha_{\rm H}}$
* $a_0 = -65$ a.u.: the $S$-wave scattering length obtained from the Temkin-Lamkin potential for $\pi^+$-hydrogen
* $B^{(6)} = 1.20\times10^{-4}$ a.u.: the shallowest bound-state energy from the Temkin-Lamkin potential, used to fix the NLO contact term
* $B^{(5)} = 1.56\times10^{-3}$ a.u.: the fifth excited state binding energy from the Temkin-Lamkin potential, reproduced to within approximately 1% at NLO
* $B^{(4)} = 6.12\times10^{-3}$ a.u.: the fourth excited state binding energy from the Temkin-Lamkin potential, reproduced to within approximately 12% at NLO
* $B^{(3)} = 1.47\times10^{-2}$ a.u.: the third excited state binding energy from the Temkin-Lamkin potential, where both LO and NLO are off by approximately 50%
* $\Lambda_b \sim 100/\beta_4 \approx \pi/a_B$: the estimated breakdown scale of ID-EFT from the phase-shift analysis

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for singular potentials, previously applied to $1/r^6$ van der Waals systems (Odell 2021) and $1/r^3$ potentials relevant in chiral EFT (Beane 2001, Hammer 2019), to the $1/r^4$ induced-dipole interaction. It systematizes and improves upon the semi-classical treatment of Flambaum (1999) and the modified effective-range expansion of O'Malley (1961), which are limited to low momenta or high energies respectively. The ID-EFT framework enables systematically improvable predictions for the $\pi^+$-hydrogen system, and the authors intend to extend the approach to full three-body treatments of pionic hydrogen (Lazauskas 2002, Carbonell 2011) in future work.
