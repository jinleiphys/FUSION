# Deep-learning quasi-particle masses from QCD equation of state

> The interactions of quarks and gluons are strong at non-perturbative region. The equation of state (EoS) of a strongly-interacting quantum chromodynamics (QCD) medium can only be studied using the first-principle lattice QCD calculations. However, the complicated QCD EoS can be reproduced using simple statistical formula by treating the medium as a free parton gas whose fundamental degree of freedoms are dressed quarks and gluons called quasi-particles, with temperature-dependent masses. We use deep neural network and auto differentiation to solve this variational problem in which the masses of quasi gluons, up/down and strange quarks are three unknown functions, whose forms are represented by deep neural network. We reproduce the QCD EoS using these machine learned quasi-particle masses, and calculate the shear viscosity over entropy density ($η/s$) as a function of temperature of the hot QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that deep neural networks can extract temperature-dependent quasi-particle masses (for gluons, up/down quarks, and strange quarks) from lattice QCD equation of state data without assuming a fixed functional form, thereby avoiding the bias introduced by traditional parameterized approaches. The authors hedge that while the method reproduces the QCD equation of state, different random initializations sometimes lead to worse solutions, and they only pick solutions with small loss.

## Method

Three separate residual neural networks (each with 8 hidden layers of 32 neurons) represent the unknown temperature-dependent masses of gluons, up/down quarks, and strange quarks. These masses are fed into the Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistical formulae to compute the partition function, from which entropy density and trace anomaly are obtained via automatic differentiation. The networks are trained by minimizing the mean-square-error between the predicted entropy density and trace anomaly and the corresponding lattice QCD results from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations, with additional physical constraints at high temperature from perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

* The minimum of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s) occurs at approximately T ≈ 1.25 Tc, where Tc is 0.155 GeV for HotQCD and 0.150 GeV for Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD.
* At high temperature, the eta/s value lies in the range between 0.1 and 0.2.
* The physical constraint at high temperature uses a cutoff Tcut = 0.150 GeV, with the ratio Rg/q = Mg/Mq constrained to 3/2 for temperatures above 2.5 Tcut.
* The constraint for strange quark mass uses current quark masses: ms ≈ 95 MeV and mu/d ≈ 5 MeV.
* The loss function uses constraint weights beta1 = 0.01 and beta2 = 0.0006.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach to QCD equation of state, where previous studies such as Peshier (1995) and Levai (1997) used parameterized mass functions with fixed functional forms that introduce bias. The method enables the construction of quasi-parton cascade programs that can reproduce lattice QCD equation of state with elastic scatterings only, which traditional parton cascade programs failed to achieve (Zhang 1997, Xu 2004). The extracted eta/s values are compared with Bayesian analysis results from Bernhard (2019) and JETSCAPE (2020), showing consistency with the JETSCAPE 90% confidence region for temperatures above 0.17 GeV.
