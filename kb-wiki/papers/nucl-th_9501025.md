# The giant resonances in hot nuclei: linear response calculations

> We calculate the isovector response function of hot nuclear matter using various effective Skyrme interactions. For Skyrme forces with a small effective mass the strength distribution is found to be nearly independent of temperature, and shows little collective effects. In contrast effective forces with an effective mass close to unity produce sizeable collective effects at zero temperature which disappear at temperatures of a few MeV. We discuss the relevance of these results for the saturation of the multiplicity of photons emitted by the giant dipole resonance in hot nuclei beyond $T$=3 MeV observed in recent experiments.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence and collectivity of the isovector giant dipole resonance in hot nuclear matter depend critically on the effective mass of the Skyrme interaction used, with forces having an effective mass close to unity showing strong collective effects at zero temperature that disappear by a few MeV, while forces with small effective mass show little temperature dependence and weak collectivity throughout.

## Method

The authors calculated the retarded isovector response function of hot nuclear matter using the random phase approximation (RPA) with full Skyrme interactions, generalizing the zero-temperature Lindhard function to finite temperature. They derived analytical expressions for the generalized Lindhard functions \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) that account for momentum-dependent terms in the Skyrme force, and computed the strength distribution \(S(\omega)\) for the dipole mode in lead-208 using the Steinwedel-Jenssen model with momentum transfer \(q = 0.23\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- Skyrme force SV has effective mass \(m^* = 0.36\) (in units of bare nucleon mass) and gives a dipole resonance energy in lead-208 of about 25–30 MeV, much higher than the observed 15 MeV.
- Skyrme force SI has effective mass \(m^* = 0.91\) and gives a sharper resonance at zero temperature with good agreement to the observed position.
- Skyrme force SVI has effective mass \(m^* = 0.95\).
- Skyrme force SII has effective mass \(m^* = 0.58\) and produces a response function close to the bare Lindhard function.
- The momentum transfer for the dipole mode in lead-208 is \(q = 0.23\) fm\(^{-1}\), derived from the nuclear radius \(R = 6.7\) fm.
- The saturation of photon multiplicity from the giant dipole resonance in hot nuclei is observed experimentally beyond \(T = 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations of giant resonances in hot nuclei by Vautherin and Vinh Mau (unreferenced in text) that predicted a sharp peak at zero temperature and increasing width with temperature, in contrast to self-consistent RPA calculations by Sagawa (unreferenced in text) that gave a broad resonance with little temperature evolution. The authors build on a previous schematic calculation by Braghin (unreferenced in text) that used a momentum-independent Skyrme-type interaction, now using full Skyrme forces to obtain more reliable results. The findings help explain the saturation of photon multiplicity from the giant dipole resonance in hot nuclei above \(T = 3\) MeV observed in experiments by Lefaou (unreferenced in text), and point to the need for further theoretical studies of effects beyond RPA, such as coupling to two particle–two hole configurations as studied by Adachi (unreferenced in text).
