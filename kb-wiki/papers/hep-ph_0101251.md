# Direct photons: a nonequilibrium signal of the expanding quark-gluon plasma

> Direct photon production from a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies is studied with a real-time kinetic description that is consistently incorporated with hydrodynamics. Within Bjorken's hydrodynamical model, energy nonconserving (anti)quark bremsstrahlung q(\bar{q})\to q(\bar{q})γand quark-antiquark annihilation q\bar{q}\to γare shown to be the dominant nonequilibrium effects during the transient lifetime of the QGP. For central collisions we find a significant excess of direct photons in the range of transverse momentum 1-2 \lesssim p_T \lesssim 5 GeV/c as compared to equilibrium results. The photon rapidity distribution exhibits a central plateau. The transverse momentum distribution at midrapidity falls off with a {\em power law} p^{-ν}_T with 2.5 \lesssim ν\lesssim 3 as a consequence of these energy nonconserving processes, providing a distinct experimental {\em nonequilibrium signature}. The power law exponent νincreases with the initial temperature of the QGP and hence with the total multiplicity rapidity distribution dN_π/dy.

## Key claim

Direct photon production from a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma is significantly enhanced by energy nonconserving (anti)quark bremsstrahlung and quark-antiquark annihilation during the finite QGP lifetime, but this enhancement is only predicted for the transient nonequilibrium stage and does not apply to an equilibrium plasma of infinite lifetime.

## Method

The authors combined a real-time kinetic description of photon production, formulated as an initial value problem, with Bjorken's hydrodynamical model of longitudinal expansion. The initial state was taken as a thermal density matrix for quarks and gluons at a given initial temperature with no initial photons, and the photon yield was computed to lowest order in the electromagnetic coupling by integrating the time-dependent production rate over the finite space-time history of the expanding QGP from the initial thermalization proper time to the hadronization proper time.

## Key numbers

- The QGP lifetime is of order less than or equal to 10-30 fm/c for central collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
- The initial thermalization proper time is taken as tau_i ~ 1 fm/c.
- The quark-hadron transition temperature is taken as T_c ~ 160 MeV.
- At RHIC energies the initial thermalization temperature is estimated to be T_i ~ 200-300 MeV.
- The QGP phase ends at a proper time tau_f = tau_i (T_i/T_c)^3, which at RHIC energies gives a QGP lifetime of order less than or equal to 10 fm/c.
- The transverse momentum distribution at midrapidity falls off as p_T^{-nu} with 2.5 less than or approximately equal to nu less than or approximately equal to 3 for transverse momentum in the range 1-2 less than or approximately equal to p_T less than or approximately equal to 5 GeV/c.
- The power law exponent nu increases with the initial temperature of the QGP and hence with the total multiplicity rapidity distribution dN_pi/dy.

## Context

This work extends the real-time kinetic approach previously developed by Boyanovsky and collaborators for hot QED and scalar theories to direct photon production from an expanding QGP, directly addressing the conceptual inconsistency between the S-matrix approach (which assumes infinite time and energy conservation) and the finite-lifetime hydrodynamic evolution. The authors compare their nonequilibrium results to the equilibrium rate calculations of Kapusta et al. (1991), Baier et al. (1992), and Aurenche et al. (1998), showing that the nonequilibrium yield from the QGP will stand out over that from the hadronic phase. This enables experimental searches for a distinct power-law signature of nonequilibrium effects in direct photon spectra at RHIC and LHC.
