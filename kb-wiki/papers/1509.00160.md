# Glueballs amass at RHIC and LHC Colliders! - The early quarkless 1st order phase transition at $T=270$ MeV - from pure Yang-Mills glue plasma to GlueBall-Hagedorn states

> The early stage of high multiplicity pp, pA and AA collider is represented by a nearly quarkless, hot, deconfined pure gluon plasma. According to pure Yang-Mills Lattice Gauge Theory, this hot pure glue matter undergoes, at a high temperature, $T_c = 270$ MeV, a first order phase transition into a confined Hagedorn-GlueBall fluid. These new scenario should be characterized by a suppression of high $p_T$ photons and dileptons, baryon suppression and enhanced strange meson production. We propose to observe this newly predicted class of events at LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the early stage of high-multiplicity pp, pA, and AA collisions may be dominated by a nearly quarkless pure gluon plasma, which undergoes a first-order phase transition at T_c = 270 MeV into a confined Hagedorn-GlueBall fluid, rather than the standard crossover transition at approximately 155 MeV. This scenario is conditional on the suppression of soft quark production during the first 3 fm/c of the collision evolution.

## Method

The authors combine pure SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice gauge theory results with transport calculations (BAMPS) to model the time evolution of gluon and quark fugacities in the early stage of relativistic collisions. They use the Columbia plot framework to qualitatively map the effective quark mass and flavor number dependence of the QCD phase structure, and propose interpolating between lattice equations of state for small effective flavor numbers (N_f^effective = 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 1.2, 1.8) to model the time-dependent system. Pure glue hydrodynamic simulations are also presented to illustrate supercooling and bubble formation during the first-order phase transition.

## Key numbers

- T_c = 270 MeV: critical temperature for the first-order phase transition in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice gauge theory, from pure glue plasma to confined GlueBall fluid
- T_{2nd order} ≈ 204 MeV: critical temperature where the phase transition changes from first to second order, about 10% below the pure gauge T_c = 270 MeV (Borsanyi 2012)
- T_{cr} = 155 ± 20 MeV: crossover temperature of full 2+1 flavor lattice QCD at the physical point for a fully equilibrated quark-gluon system
- t < 3 fm/c: time window during which soft quark production is suppressed and the effective number of flavors N_f is much less than 1
- t_{eq} < 0.3 fm/c: timescale for the standard paradigm of extremely rapid thermalization and chemical saturation

## Context

This work extends the "hot glue scenario" previously discussed by Shuryak (1992), and others (1994, 2001), and builds on the prediction by Svetitsky and Yaffe (1982) of a sharp first-order phase transition in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory. It contrasts with the standard paradigm of rapid thermalization and chemical saturation of soft gluons and light quarks, and with the crossover transition at approximately 155 MeV found in full 2+1 flavor lattice QCD by Borsanyi et al. (2014) and Bazavov et al. (2014). The paper enables experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for a new class of events characterized by suppressed high-p_T photons and dileptons, baryon suppression, and enhanced strange meson production, and suggests that the pure glue scenario may also be relevant for ultrahigh-energy cosmic ray interactions and early universe cosmology.
