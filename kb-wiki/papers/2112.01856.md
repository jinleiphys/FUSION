# Spin polarization dynamics in the non-boost-invariant background

> Space-time evolution of spin polarization within the framework of hydrodynamics with spin based on de Groot - van Leeuwen - van Weert forms of energy-momentum and spin tensors is studied. Due to the non-boost invariant flow in the system the spin polarization components couple to each other implying some effects on the spin polarization observables. We study transverse-momentum and rapidity dependence of mean spin polarization vector for $Λ$ hyperons. Our results show qualitative agreement for rapidity dependence of the global spin polarization with the experiments and other models. The quadrupole structure of the longitudinal component at midrapidity is not found, however, as compared to the results for Bjorken expansion, some non-trivial signal at forward rapidities is observed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that breaking boost invariance in a spin-hydrodynamic framework causes coupling between the electric-like and magnetic-like spin polarization components, producing non-trivial rapidity signals for Lambda hyperon polarization at forward rapidities, but it does not reproduce the quadrupole structure of the longitudinal spin polarization component at midrapidity that appears in Bjorken-expansion models.

## Method
The authors solve perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin using the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors for a transversely homogeneous system undergoing non-boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They evolve the background hydrodynamic variables (temperature, baryon chemical potential, fluid rapidity) and six spin polarization coefficients (three electric-like C_kappa and three magnetic-like C_omega) from an initial condition at proper time tau = 0.25 fm/c to a freeze-out hypersurface, then compute the mean spin polarization vector for Lambda hyperons as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time: tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c
- Initial temperature: T_0 = 150 MeV
- Initial baryon chemical potential: mu_B = 0 (central rapidity region)
- Particle mass: m = 1115.68 GeV (Lambda hyperon mass, used in the equation of state)
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 150 MeV (same as initial, isothermal freeze-out assumed)

## Context
This work extends the spin-hydrodynamic framework previously applied to Bjorken-expanding systems by Florkowski et al. (2019) to include non-boost-invariant longitudinal flow. It contrasts with spin-thermal models (Becattini et al., 2013-2021) that use thermal vorticity to predict polarization, which fail to reproduce the sign of the transverse-momentum dependence of longitudinal polarization (the "sign problem"). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from STAR (2019) and ALICE (2021) on differential spin polarization measurements, particularly at forward rapidities.
