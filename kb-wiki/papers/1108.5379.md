# Energy dependent growth of the nucleon and hydrodynamic initial conditions

> Due to gluon saturation, the growth of the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section with increasing collision energy sqrt(s) results in a broadening of the nucleon's density distribution in position space. This leads to a natural smoothing of the initial energy density distribution in the transverse plane of the matter created near midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions. We study this effect for fluctuating initial conditions generated with the Monte Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) model for Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We argue that at the LHC viscous hydrodynamics is applicable at earlier times than at RHIC, not only because of the higher temperature but also since the length scale over which the initial pressure fluctuates increases with collision energy.

## Key claim

The growth of the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section with collision energy, driven by gluon saturation, causes the nucleon's density distribution to broaden in position space, which naturally smooths the initial energy density fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions; this smoothing effect implies that at the LHC viscous hydrodynamics is applicable at earlier times than at RHIC, not only due to higher temperature but also because the length scale over which initial pressure fluctuates increases with collision energy.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) model to generate fluctuating initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. They replaced the traditional disk-like nucleon thickness function with a Gaussian profile whose width \( \sqrt{B} \) grows with collision energy, determined by relating the Gaussian width to the measured inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section \( \sigma_\mathrm{NN} \) via a Glauber-like ansatz with an energy-independent constant \( \lambda = \sigma_{gg}/(4\pi B) \). The nucleon centers were distributed inside the nucleus using a Woods-Saxon distribution, and the resulting nuclear thickness function was used to compute the gluon saturation momentum and initial entropy density.

## Key numbers

- At \( \sqrt{s} = 23.5 \) GeV, the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is \( \sigma_\mathrm{NN} = 32 \) mb and the Gaussian width is \( \sqrt{B} = 0.473 \) fm.
- At \( \sqrt{s} = 200 \) GeV (RHIC), \( \sigma_\mathrm{NN} = 42 \) mb and \( \sqrt{B} = 0.544 \) fm.
- At \( \sqrt{s} = 2.76 \) TeV (LHC), \( \sigma_\mathrm{NN} = 62.1 \) mb and \( \sqrt{B} = 0.661 \) fm.
- At \( \sqrt{s} = 7 \) TeV (LHC), \( \sigma_\mathrm{NN} = 71 \) mb and \( \sqrt{B} = 0.707 \) fm.
- The ratio of ensemble-averaged harmonic eccentricity coefficients \( \langle \varepsilon_n(200\ \mathrm{GeV}) \rangle / \langle \varepsilon_n(2.76\ \mathrm{TeV}) \rangle \) for Au+Au collisions increases with harmonic order \( n \), reaching about 1.5 to 1.6 for \( n = 15 \), based on 95,000 events binned into 10 equal \( N_\mathrm{part} \) bins.

## Context

This work extends the MC-KLN model of Drescher and Nara (2006) and Hirano et al. (2009) by introducing an energy-dependent Gaussian nucleon width, in contrast to the constant-width Gaussian nucleons used by Albacete et al. (2011). It also builds on the earlier demonstration by Miller and Snellings (2003) of the importance of event-by-event fluctuations using a Monte Carlo Glauber model. The results enable more realistic predictions for higher-order anisotropic flow coefficients \( v_n \) at the LHC and improve the applicability of viscous hydrodynamics at early times, with consequences for extracting the QGP shear viscosity from flow data.
