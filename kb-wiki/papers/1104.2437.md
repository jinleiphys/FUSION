# Heavy quarks and charmonium at RHIC and LHC within a partonic transport model

> Heavy quark and charmonium production as well as their space-time evolution are studied in transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. In the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) heavy quarks can be produced in initial hard parton scatterings or during the evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. Subsequently, they interact with the medium via binary scatterings with a running coupling and a more precise Debye screening which is derived from hard thermal loop calculations, participate in the flow and lose energy. We present results of the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor of heavy quarks and compare them to available data. Furthermore, preliminary results on J/psi suppression at forward and mid-rapidity are reported for central and non-central collisions at RHIC. For this, we study cold nuclear matter effects and the dissociation as well as regeneration of J/psi in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
Heavy quark energy loss and charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can be described by a partonic transport model (BAMPS) using elastic scatterings with a running coupling and improved Debye screening, but a phenomenological K-factor of 4 is still needed to match experimental data, indicating that higher-order (radiative) contributions are not yet fully captured.

## Method
The Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) solves the Boltzmann equation for on-shell partons (gluons, charm, bottom, J/psi) using a stochastic transport algorithm with pQCD interactions. Heavy quarks are produced via initial hard parton scatterings (PYTHIA) or secondary production in the QGP; they interact via binary scatterings with a running coupling and Debye screening derived from hard thermal loop calculations. For J/psi, cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing via EPS08, nuclear absorption with sigma_abs = 2.8 mb, Cronin effect) are applied to the initial production, followed by dissociation (J/psi + g -> c + cbar) and regeneration (c + cbar -> J/psi + g) during the QGP evolution, with a lattice-inspired dissociation temperature T_d = 2T_c.

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), between 0.3 and 3.4 charm pairs are produced in the QGP, depending on initial gluon model and charm mass.
- In Pb+Pb at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), between 11 and 55 charm pairs are produced in the QGP; at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, between 5 and 28 charm pairs.
- The elastic gQ -> gQ cross section increases by about a factor of 10 when using running coupling and improved Debye screening (kappa ≈ 0.2).
- A K-factor of 4 is required for the leading-order elastic cross section to match heavy flavor electron v2 and R_AA data at RHIC.
- The nuclear absorption cross section for J/psi is sigma_abs = 2.8 mb.
- The Cronin effect parameter a_gN = 0.1 GeV^2/fm is used for p_T broadening.
- The J/psi dissociation temperature is set to T_d = 2T_c with T_c = 165 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the BAMPS transport model (Xu 2004, Xu 2007) to heavy quarks and charmonium, building on earlier studies of gluon energy loss and flow (Xu 2008, Bouras 2008). The running coupling treatment follows Gossiaux (2008) and Peshier (2008), while the improved Debye screening is compared to hard thermal loop calculations. For J/psi, the study addresses the long-standing problem of disentangling cold and hot nuclear matter effects (Matsui 1986, Brambilla 2010), incorporating regeneration mechanisms discussed by Grandchamp (2002), Thews (2005), and Andronic (2006). The preliminary J/psi results at RHIC show systematic underestimation of R_AA compared to PHENIX data (Adare 2006), suggesting either too strong cold nuclear matter suppression or too small regeneration cross section; future work will extend to LHC energies and include next-to-leading order cross sections.
