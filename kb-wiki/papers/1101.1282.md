# Initial temperature of the strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma created at RHIC

> A 1+3 dimensional solution of relativistic hydrodynamics is analyzed in this paper. Momentum distribution and other observables are calculated from the solution and compared to hadronic measurements from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The solution is compatible with the data, but only the freeze-out point of the evolution is determined. Many equation of states and initial states (initial temperatures) are valid with the same freeze-out distribution, thus the same hadronic observables. The observable that would distinguish between these initial temperatures is momentum distribution of photons, as photons are created throughout the evolution of the fireball created in RHIC collisions. The PHENIX experiment at RHIC measures such data via low invariant mass e+e- pairs. Average temperature from this data is T=221+-23+-18 MeV, while a model calculation with initial temperature 370 MeV agree with the data.

# Initial temperature of the strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma created at RHIC

## Key claim
The 1+3 dimensional exact hydrodynamic solution is compatible with RHIC hadronic data, but hadronic observables alone cannot determine the initial temperature or equation of state because many combinations yield the same freeze-out distribution. The initial temperature can only be constrained by comparing model calculations to direct photon measurements, where an initial temperature of 370 MeV at 1 fm/c agrees with PHENIX data, but the time-averaged temperature from the photon spectrum is only a lower limit.

## Method
A 1+3 dimensional exact, analytic solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics with ellipsoidally symmetric expansion and Hubble-type flow was used to calculate hadronic observables: invariant transverse momentum distributions, elliptic flow, and Bose-Einstein correlation radii. These were fitted to PHENIX data from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The time evolution of the central temperature was then used together with the freeze-out parameters to relate initial temperature to the equation of state parameter κ, and the resulting model was compared to PHENIX direct photon measurements obtained via low invariant mass e+e- pair internal conversion.

## Key numbers
- Central freeze-out temperature T0 = 200 ± 7 MeV from fits to PHENIX hadronic data
- Freeze-out proper time τ0 = 7.7 ± 0.8 fm/c
- Initial central temperature Tinit = 370 MeV at τinitial = 1 fm/c, assuming an average κ = 10
- Time-averaged temperature from PHENIX direct photon spectrum: T = 221 ± 23 (stat) ± 18 (sys) MeV
- PHENIX concluded Tinit = 300 MeV is the lowest possible initial temperature at 1 fm/c consistent with the average slope parameter

## Context
This work extends the exact 1+3 dimensional hydrodynamic solution of Csorgo (2003) by computing hadronic observables and comparing them to PHENIX data, following earlier calculations by Csanad (2009). It builds on the Landau-Khalatnikov (1953) and Hwa-Bjorken (1974, 1982) 1+1 dimensional solutions, and the accelerating solutions of Csorgo (2006) and Bialas (2007). The key finding that hadronic observables do not constrain the equation of state or initial temperature independently motivates using direct photon measurements from PHENIX (Adare 2008) as a probe of the full time evolution. Future work will compare hadron and photon observables simultaneously to this analytic solution.
