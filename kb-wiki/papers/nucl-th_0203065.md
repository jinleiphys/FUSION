# Statistical nature of cluster emission in nuclear liquid phase

> The emission of nuclear clusters is investigated within the framework of isospin dependent lattice gas model and classical molecular dynamics model. It is found that the emission of individual cluster which is heavier than proton is almost Poissonian except near the liquid gas phase transition point and the thermal scaling is observed by the linear Arrhenius plots which is made from the average multiplicity of each cluster versus the inverse of temperature in the nuclear liquid phase. It indicates of a statistical nature of such cluster emission in the models. The "emission barriers" which are the slopes of the Arrhenius plots are extracted as a function of the mass or charge number and fitted by the formula embodied with the contributions of the surface energy and Coulomb interaction. The possible influences of the source size, Coulomb interaction and "freeze-out" density and related physical implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that cluster emission in nuclear disassembly is statistical in nature, as demonstrated by Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling (linear Arrhenius plots) in the isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) and classical molecular dynamics model (I-CMD), but only in the nuclear liquid phase (below the liquid-gas phase transition temperature) and not near the phase transition point where large fluctuations break the Poissonian behavior.

## Method

The authors simulated the disassembly of medium-size nuclei (primarily ¹²⁹Xe) using the isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) and the isospin-dependent classical molecular dynamics model (I-CMD), with and without Coulomb interactions. For each temperature, 1000 events were generated at a fixed freeze-out density of approximately 0.38ρ₀, and clusters were identified using the Coniglio-Klein prescription (I-LGM) or by propagating particles under the chosen force until asymptotic times (I-CMD). The multiplicity distributions of individual cluster species (classified by mass A, charge Z, or isotope) were analyzed for Poissonian behavior and their average yields were plotted as Arrhenius plots (ln⟨n⟩ vs. 1/T) to extract emission barriers from the slopes in the low-temperature (liquid phase) region.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density used for most calculations: 0.38 ρ₀, corresponding to a 7³ cubic lattice for ¹²⁹Xe, 6³ for A_sys = 80, and 9³ for A_sys = 274.
- Additional freeze-out densities tested: 0.177 ρ₀ (9³ lattice) and 0.597 ρ₀ (6³ lattice) for ¹²⁹Xe.
- Interaction constant for unlike nucleons: ε_pn = -5.33 MeV; for like nucleons: ε_nn = ε_pp = 0 MeV.
- In the I-CMD potential, the minimum is at r₀ = 1.842 fm with value -5.33 MeV, and the potential is zero beyond 1.3 r₀.
- For the surface-energy-only fit to emission barriers (I-LGM and I-CMD without Coulomb): c₁ = 8.469 and (A/Z)_fit = 1.866.
- For the fit including Coulomb (I-CMD with Coulomb): c₂ = 12.921, r_Coul = 1.22 fm, and Z_res ≈ 41 for ¹²⁹Xe.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Moretto et al. (1997) on binomial reducibility and thermal scaling in multifragmentation, and the later work of Beaulieu and Moretto (1999) on Poissonian reducibility for individual fragment species, by testing these concepts in thermal-equilibrium models (I-LGM and I-CMD) where the true temperature is known rather than inferred from transverse energy. It addresses criticisms by Tsang and Danielewicz (2000) regarding the validity of the T ∝ √(E_t) assumption, and shows that the Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling hold in the liquid phase of these models, supporting a statistical interpretation. The results enable future studies to use these models to extract emission barriers and investigate the roles of surface energy and Coulomb interaction in cluster emission, and to compare with experimental data from the Moretto/Wozniak group.
