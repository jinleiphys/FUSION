# Hunting for an EMC-like effect for antiquarks

> We argue that the Drell-Yan process in the $x_A \ge 0.15$ kinematics recently studied at FNAL by the E906/SeaQuest experiment may allow to observe an analogous of the EMC effect for antiquarks. The effects of Fermi motion and energy loss are considered. The preliminary E906/SeaQuest data are inconsistent with the growth of the $σ_A/Aσ_N$ ratio expected in the Fermi motion scenario at $x_A \ge 0.25$. The pattern of the $x_A$ dependence of the ratio seems also inconsistent with a scenario in which the dominant nuclear effect is a suppression of the cross section due to the energy loss experienced by a quark of the projectile proton involved in the Drell-Yan process. All together the data suggest the possibility of a modification of the antiquark parton distributions in nuclei, with a pattern similar to the one observed in the EMC effect. We argue that optimal kinematics to look for an antiquark EMC-like effect would be to measure the $σ_A^{DY}/Aσ_N^{DY}$ ratios for $x_A$ = 0.2-0.4 and $x_p$ $\approx$ constant.

## Key claim

The Drell-Yan process data from the E906/SeaQuest experiment at FNAL may allow observation of an EMC-like effect for antiquarks in nuclei, because the preliminary data are inconsistent with both the Fermi motion scenario (which predicts a growth of the ratio at x_A >= 0.25) and the energy loss scenario (which predicts suppression from quark energy loss in the nuclear medium).

## Method

The authors performed a theoretical analysis comparing two nuclear effects on the Drell-Yan cross section ratio sigma_A/(A*sigma_N): Fermi motion of nucleons in the target nucleus, calculated using a light-cone convolution formalism with a piecewise momentum distribution including short-range correlations, and quark energy loss as the projectile quark propagates through the nuclear medium, modeled with a Monte Carlo event-by-event approach using nucleon configurations and gluon density distributions. They compared these model predictions with preliminary E906/SeaQuest data for carbon, iron, and tungsten targets at 120 GeV incident proton energy.

## Key numbers

- For antiquarks, the Fermi motion crossover point where R_A = 1 occurs at x_cr = 0.25 (using n = 7 for the (1-x)^n parametrization), compared to x_cr = 0.5 for quarks (n = 3)
- The deviation from unity of R_A for antiquarks from Fermi motion is about 1.5% at x_A ~ 0.1, assuming T_A = 40 MeV
- The ratio of EMC slopes for copper over carbon, G_Cu/C = dR_Cu/dx / dR_C/dx = 1.35, based on a linear fit of DIS data in the x-range 0.2-0.5
- The energy loss mechanism gives G_Cu/C^(en.loss) = (R_Cu - 1)/(R_C - 1) = 1.86
- For x_A = 0.1, x_p = 0.8 versus x_A = 0.25, x_p = 0.32, the energy loss suppression differs by a factor of 3.4

## Context

This work extends the search for nuclear modifications of parton distributions beyond the classic EMC effect for quarks discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (1983) and the valence quark enhancement observed by the New Muon Collaboration (1996). It builds on the E906/SeaQuest experiment (Reimer 2007, Tadepalli 2019) which measured Drell-Yan production at 120 GeV and found flavor asymmetry in antiquark distributions (Dove 2021). The analysis distinguishes between Fermi motion effects, energy loss mechanisms, and genuine modifications of antiquark parton distributions in nuclei, proposing optimal kinematics (x_A = 0.2-0.4 with x_p approximately constant) to isolate an antiquark EMC-like effect. Future final results from E906/SeaQuest and measurements at multiple incident proton energies could resolve the mechanism.
