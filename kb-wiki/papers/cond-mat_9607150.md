# Microcanonical Thermodynamics of First Order Phase Transitions studied in the Potts Model

> Phase transitions of first and second order can easily be distinguished in small systems in the microcanonical ensemble. Configurations of phase coexistence, which are suppressed in the canonical formulation, carry important information about the main characteristics of first order phase transitions like the transition temperature, the latent heat, and the interphase surface tension. The characterisitc backbending of the micro- canonical caloric equation of state T(E) (not to be confused with the well known Van der Waals loops in ordinary thermodynamics) leading to a negative specific heat is intimatly linked to the interphase surface entropy.

## Key claim
First-order phase transitions in finite systems can be identified by a characteristic S-bend (backbending) in the microcanonical caloric equation of state T(E), which produces a negative specific heat; this backbending is intimately linked to the interphase surface entropy and is not an artifact of long-range forces or mean-field approximations.

## Method
The authors performed microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo (M³C) simulations of the two-dimensional q=10 Potts model on square lattices of size N = L×L with periodic boundary conditions. They computed the microcanonical density of states Ω_N(E) by sampling configurations within a narrow energy band, from which they extracted the specific entropy s(ε) and the microcanonical temperature T(ε) = 1/(∂s/∂ε). The surface entropy Δs_surf was obtained from the area under the S-shaped oscillation of β_micro(ε) relative to the Maxwell construction line β_tr.

## Key numbers
- For the largest lattice (L=100), the simulation performed 3.2×10⁶ sweeps.
- The coexistence region in energy per spin ε extends from approximately 0.33 to 1.05 for the q=10 Potts model.
- The specific latent heat q_lat = ε₃ - ε₁ at L=∞ agrees with the analytically known value from Baxter (1973); microcanonical results for L up to 100 extrapolate linearly in 1/L to this infinite-size limit.
- The surface entropy Δs_surf scales with lattice size such that L×Δs_surf approaches the analytically known asymptotic limit for the surface tension, with microcanonical results showing weaker finite-size dependence than canonical results.

## Context
This work extends the microcanonical thermodynamics approach of Gross (1995) and others to the Potts model, demonstrating that the S-bend in T(E) and negative specific heat are generic features of first-order phase transitions in finite systems with short-range interactions. The results contradict the canonical expectation that such features are absent, and they build on prior work by Hüller (1994) on convex intruders in the entropy, by Binder (1982) and Borgs and Janke (1992) on surface tension from canonical simulations, and by Billoire (1993) on multi-canonical methods. The findings enable more reliable extraction of transition temperatures, latent heats, and surface tensions from small-system simulations, and open the question of how to interpret the surface entropy in systems that cannot be extrapolated to infinite size, such as fragmenting nuclei and atomic clusters.
