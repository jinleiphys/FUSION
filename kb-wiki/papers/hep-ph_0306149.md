# Structure of Proton

> Electron--proton scattering in elastic and highly inelastic region is reviewed in a unified approach. The importance of parity--violating scattering due to electro--weak interference in probing the structure of proton is emphasized. The importance of longitudnal spin--spin asymmetry as well as parity violating longitudnal asymmetry to extract the structure functions of proton in both regions are discussed. The recoil polarization of proton in the elastic scattering is also discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that parity-violating electron-proton scattering, arising from electro-weak interference, provides a direct experimental route to determine the isosinglet axial-vector form factor \(g_A^0\) and the isosinglet anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_0\) of the proton, quantities that are essential for establishing the strange quark content of the proton. The abstract's claim that such scattering "can give information" is hedged by the paper's own conclusion that the singlet form factors \(g_A^0\) and \(\kappa_0\) are not yet fixed experimentally and must be extracted from these parity-violating measurements.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism for electron-proton scattering in both the elastic and deep inelastic regimes, presenting the cross-section in terms of structure functions \(W_1\) and \(W_2\) for unpolarized targets and introducing spin-dependent structure functions for polarized targets. It then derives the parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry for unpolarized electrons on polarized protons and the recoil polarization of the proton, expressing these observables in terms of the electromagnetic Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) and the weak neutral-current form factors \(F_1^Z\), \(F_2^Z\), and \(G_A^Z\). The analysis uses the standard electroweak current couplings and SU(3) flavor decomposition to relate the weak form factors at zero momentum transfer to the isosinglet parameters \(g_A^0\) and \(\kappa_0\).

## Key numbers

- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: \(\kappa_p = 1.792\); neutron anomalous magnetic moment: \(\kappa_n = -1.913\) (normalization of Pauli form factors at \(q^2=0\)).
- Proton magnetic moment: \(\mu_p = 2.792\); neutron magnetic moment: \(\mu_n = -1.913\) (normalization of Sachs form factors at \(q^2=0\)).
- The \(\Sigma\) term from low-energy pion-nucleon scattering: \(\sum_{\pi N}(0) = 51 \pm 5\) MeV, compared to a naive SU(3) estimate of about 25 MeV, implying a strange-quark content parameter \(C_s = 3/2\).
- From EMC polarized deep inelastic scattering data: \(\Delta \tilde{u} = 0.78 \pm 0.07\), \(\Delta \tilde{d} = -0.48 \pm 0.08\), \(\Delta \tilde{s} = -0.14 \pm 0.07\), giving \(\Delta \tilde{u} + \Delta \tilde{d} + \Delta \tilde{s} = 0.16 \pm 0.22\) (consistent with zero).
- Axial-vector coupling constants: \(g_A = 1.2670 \pm 0.0035\), \(F = 0.463 \pm 0.023\), \(D = 0.803 \pm 0.040\).
- Elastic neutrino-proton scattering gives \(g_A^0 = 0.12 \pm 0.23\).
- The dipole form factor parameterization: \(G_D = (1 + q^2 / 0.71)^{-2}\) (with \(q^2\) in GeV\(^2\)).
- Recent recoil polarization measurements show the ratio \(G_M/G_E\) systematically decreases as \(q^2\) increases from 0.5 to 3.5 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on proton structure from elastic form factor measurements (Rosenbluth separation, Sachs form factors) to deep inelastic scattering and the spin crisis revealed by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) data. It builds on the suggestion by Kaplan and Manohar (1988) and later by Ellis and Karliner (1990) that parity-violating electron scattering can determine the isosinglet form factors \(g_A^0\) and \(F_2^0(0)\), which are inaccessible from hyperon beta decay alone. The paper enables future experimental programs (e.g., at Jefferson Lab) to measure these singlet form factors directly, thereby resolving the strange-quark content of the proton and the origin of its spin.
