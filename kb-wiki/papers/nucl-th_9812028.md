# e+e- yields in Pb+Au collisions at 158 AGeV/c: Assessment of baryonic contributions

> Using a hydrodynamic approach to describe Pb+Au collisions at 158 AGeV/c, we analyze e+e- yields from matter containing baryons in addition to mesons. We employ e+e- production rates from two independent calculations, which differ both in their input physics and in their absolute magnitudes, especially in the mass range where significant enhancements over expected backgrounds exist in the CERES data. Although the presence of baryons leads to significant enhancement of e+e- emission relative to that from mesons-only matter, the calculated results fall below the data in the range 400 < M_ee/MeV < 600, by a factor of 2-3. Since the calculated e+e- spectra are relatively insensitive to the equation of state for initial conditions that fit the observed hadronic spectra, either in-medium modifications of the e+e- sources more significant than so far considered, or the presence of hitherto unidentified additional sources of e+e- is indicated.

## Key claim

Although including baryons in the calculation significantly enhances e+e- emission relative to mesons-only matter, the calculated results still fall below the CERES data in the invariant mass range 400 < M_ee/MeV < 600 by a factor of 2-3, indicating that either more significant in-medium modifications or additional unidentified sources of e+e- are required.

## Method

The authors used a hydrodynamic model to describe the space-time evolution of Pb+Au collisions at 158 AGeV/c, with initial conditions tuned to reproduce measured hadron spectra from NA49 Pb+Pb data. They integrated e+e- production rates from two independent calculations, those of Steele, Yamagishi, and Zahed (SYZ) and Rapp, Urban, Buballa, and Wambach (RUBW), over the hydrodynamic evolution, applying the experimental cuts and resolution of the CERES detector, and compared the resulting mass spectra to the '95 and '96 CERES data.

## Key numbers

- The average enhancement reported by CERES for Pb+Au collisions in the '95 data was 3.5 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 0.9 (syst.) in the range 0.2 < M_ee/GeV < 2; for the '96 data, the average enhancement was 2.6 ± 0.5 (stat.) ± 0.6 (syst.) in the range 0.25 < M_ee/GeV < 0.7.
- The calculated results fall below the data in the range 400 < M_ee/MeV < 600 by a factor of 2-3.
- For the baseline model with T_c = 200 MeV and T_f = 140 MeV, the average baryon density in the hadron gas phase was n_b = 0.08 fm^-3 (0.5 times nuclear equilibrium density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3); for T_c = 165 MeV and T_f = 120 MeV, it was n_b = 0.03 fm^-3 (≈ 0.2 n_0).
- The total lifetimes of the center of the fireball were ≈ 11 fm/c for T_c(T_f) = 200(140) MeV and ≈ 14 fm/c for T_c(T_f) = 165(120) MeV.
- The SYZ rates including baryons are about a factor of 2 larger than those without baryons, mostly below M_ee = 400 MeV; the RUBW rates result in enhancements of the thermal yield up to M_ee = 300-600 MeV by a factor of about three relative to mesons-only matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic analyses of S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV/c by Sollfrank et al. (1997) and addresses the persistent discrepancy between theoretical calculations and the CERES data on e+e- yields. Prior transport models by Li et al. (1995), Cassing et al. (1995, 1998), which incorporated substantial in-medium modifications such as a decreasing rho-meson mass or a widening of its width, had been able to account for the data, whereas standard rates in both transport and hydrodynamic approaches had fallen short. The results indicate that baryonic contributions alone, even with the larger rates of RUBW, cannot explain the observed excess, and that either stronger in-medium effects or new sources are needed, motivating further theoretical work and improved experimental data, particularly in the omega and phi mass regions, to distinguish between competing scenarios.
