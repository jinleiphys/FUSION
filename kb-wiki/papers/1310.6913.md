# Study of two-proton radioactivity within the relativstic mean-field plus bcs approach

> Encouraged by the success of RMF+BCS approach for the description of the ground state properties of the chains of isotopes of proton magic nuclei with proton number \textit{Z}=8, 20, 28, 50 and 82 as well as those of proton sub-magic nuclei with \textit{Z}=40, we have further employed it, in an analogous manner, for a detailed calculations of the ground state properties of the neutron magic isotones with neutron number \textit{N} = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82 and 126 as well as those of neutron sub-magic isotones with \textit{N} = 40 using the TMA force parameterizations in order to explore low lying resonance and other exotic phenomenon near drip-lines. The results of these calculations for wave-function, single particle pairing gaps etc. are presented here to demonstrate the general validity of our RMF+BCS approach. It is found that, in some of the proton-rich nuclei in the vicinity of the proton drip-line, the main contribution to the pairing correlations is provided by the low-lying resonant states, in addition to the contributions coming from the states close to the Fermi surface, which results extended proton drip-line for isotonic chain.

# Study of Two-proton Radioactivity Within the Relativistic Mean-Field Plus BCS Approach

## Key claim
The RMF+BCS approach predicts that several even-Z nuclei in the region 20 ≤ Z ≤ 40 with small negative two-proton separation energy may still exhibit two-proton radioactivity because the effective potential barrier from Coulomb and centrifugal forces can cause a long delay in decay, extending the proton drip-line beyond what simple separation energy criteria would suggest.

## Method
The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with state-dependent BCS pairing, using the TMA force parameterization, to calculate ground state properties of even-Z nuclei in the isotopic chains from Z=20 to Z=40. Both spherical and axially deformed calculations were performed, with pairing treated using a delta-force interaction of strength V₀ = 350 MeV·fm³. One-proton separation energy (Sₚ) and two-proton separation energy (S₂ₚ) were computed from the binding energies to identify nuclei satisfying the criteria Sₚ > 0 and S₂ₚ < 0, which are candidates for simultaneous two-proton emission.

## Key numbers
- Nucleus ⁴⁵Fe: calculated S₂ₚ = -0.90 MeV, Sₚ = 0.43 MeV (experimental S₂ₚ = -1.12 ± 0.49 MeV)
- Nucleus ⁴⁸Ni: calculated S₂ₚ = -1.49 MeV, Sₚ = 0.08 MeV (no experimental data available)
- Nucleus ⁵⁴Zn: calculated S₂ₚ = -1.97 MeV (both Sₚ and S₂ₚ negative, thus not a two-proton emitter candidate)
- Nucleus ³⁸Ti: calculated S₂ₚ = -1.86 MeV, Sₚ = 0.25 MeV (experimental S₂ₚ = -0.96 ± 0.29 MeV)
- Nucleus ⁴²Cr: calculated S₂ₚ = -0.05 MeV, Sₚ = 0.41 MeV (experimental S₂ₚ = -0.26 ± 0.46 MeV)
- Maximum difference between calculated and experimental binding energies: 3 MeV (about 1 percent of total binding energy), occurring near Z=26 (Fe) isotopic chain
- Pairing interaction strength: V₀ = 350 MeV·fm³, determined by best fit to binding energies of Ni isotopes

## Context
This work extends the RMF+BCS approach previously applied by the same group to proton magic and sub-magic nuclei (Yadav et al., various years) into the study of two-proton radioactivity, a phenomenon first theoretically proposed by Goldansky (1960s) and experimentally verified in ⁴⁵Fe by Pfutzner (2002) and Giovinazzo (2002), and subsequently in ⁵⁴Zn by Blank (2005) and Ascher (2011), and in ⁴⁸Ni by Dossat (2005) and Pomorski (2011). The predictions enable experimental searches for new two-proton emitters in the medium-mass region, particularly the eleven candidate nuclei identified here, most of which have no experimental data available.
