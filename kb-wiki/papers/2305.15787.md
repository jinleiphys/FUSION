# $Z_{cs}$, $Z_c$ and $Z_b$ states under the complex scaling method

> We investigate the $Z_b$, $Z_c$ and $Z_{cs}$ states within the chiral effective field theory framework and the $S$-wave single channel molecule picture. With the complex scaling method, we accurately solve the Schrödinger equation in momentum space. Our analysis reveals that the $Z_b(10610)$, $Z_b(10650)$, $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ states are the resonances composed of the $S-$wave $(B\bar{B}^{*}+B^{*}\bar{B})/\sqrt{2}$, $B^{*}\bar{B}^*$, $(D\bar{D}^{*}+D^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ and $D^{*}\bar{D}^*$, respectively. Furthermore, although the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ states exhibit a significant difference in width, these two resonances may originate from the same channel, the $S-$wave $(D_{s}\bar{D}^{*}+D_{s}^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$. Additionally, we find two resonances in the $S-$wave $D_s^*\bar{D}^*$ channel, corresponding to the $Z_{cs}(4123)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$ states that await experimental confirmation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ states, despite their significantly different widths, may originate from the same $S$-wave $(D_{s}\bar{D}^{*}+D_{s}^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ molecular channel as two distinct resonances (one narrow and one broad), rather than being distinct hadrons or the same entity as proposed in prior literature.

## Method

The authors employ chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) to construct $S$-wave single-channel molecular potentials for the $Z_b$, $Z_c$, and $Z_{cs}$ systems, including both contact terms and one-pion-exchange (OPE) contributions. They solve the Schrödinger equation in momentum space using the complex scaling method (CSM), which treats bound states and resonances on equal footing by rotating the radial coordinate and momentum into the complex plane. The low-energy constants (LECs) are fitted to experimental masses and widths of the $Z_b(10610)$, $Z_b(10650)$, $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$, $Z_{cs}(3985)$, and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ states, and the same parameters are then used to predict resonances in the $D_s^*\bar{D}^*$ channel.

## Key numbers

- $Z_b(10610)$: pole mass $10606.9_{-1.5}^{+1.8}$ MeV, width $15.0_{-3.2}^{+3.4}$ MeV from the $(B\bar{B}^{*}+B^{*}\bar{B})/\sqrt{2}$ channel; experimental values $10607.2_{-2.0}^{+2.0}$ MeV and $18.4_{-2.4}^{+2.4}$ MeV.
- $Z_b(10650)$: pole mass $10652.2_{-1.6}^{+1.8}$ MeV, width $14.8_{-3.2}^{+3.4}$ MeV from the $B^{*}\bar{B}^*$ channel; experimental values $10652.2_{-1.5}^{+1.5}$ MeV and $11.5_{-2.2}^{+2.2}$ MeV.
- $Z_c(3900)$: pole mass $3884.3_{-0.6}^{+0.6}$ MeV, width $26.0_{-1.4}^{+1.4}$ MeV from the $(D\bar{D}^{*}+D^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ channel; experimental values $3881.7_{-2.3}^{+2.3}$ MeV and $26.6_{-3.4}^{+3.0}$ MeV.
- $Z_c(4020)$: pole mass $4025.8_{-0.6}^{+0.6}$ MeV, width $24.0_{-1.4}^{+1.3}$ MeV from the $D^{*}\bar{D}^*$ channel; experimental values $4025.5_{-5.6}^{+3.7}$ MeV and $26.0_{-6.0}^{+6.0}$ MeV.
- $Z_{cs}(3985)$: pole mass $3982.4_{-2.1}^{+2.2}$ MeV, width $14.1_{-3.6}^{+3.7}$ MeV from the $(D_{s}\bar{D}^{*}+D_{s}^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ channel; experimental values $3982.5_{-3.3}^{+2.8}$ MeV and $12.8_{-5.3}^{+6.1}$ MeV.
- $Z_{cs}(4000)$: pole mass $4010.7_{-6.2}^{+6.3}$ MeV, width $119.6_{-14.7}^{+14.5}$ MeV from the same $(D_{s}\bar{D}^{*}+D_{s}^{*}\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ channel; experimental values $4003_{-15.2}^{+7.2}$ MeV and $131_{-30.0}^{+30.0}$ MeV.
- $Z_{cs}(4123)$ prediction: pole mass $4125.2_{-2.1}^{+2.2}$ MeV, width $13.2_{-3.4}^{+3.5}$ MeV from the $D_s^*\bar{D}^*$ channel.
- $Z_{cs}(4220)$ prediction: pole mass $4152.7_{-6.0}^{+6.1}$ MeV, width $115.0_{-14.2}^{+14.0}$ MeV from the $D_s^*\bar{D}^*$ channel; experimental values $4216_{-38}^{+49}$ MeV and $233_{-90}^{+110}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $Z_b$, $Z_c$, and $Z_{cs}$ states previously studied by Wang (2020) using ChEFT, but introduces the complex scaling method to consistently treat these states as resonances rather than bound states. The authors note that prior works by Maiani (2021) and Meng (2021) considered the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and $Z_{cs}(4000)$ as SU(3)-flavor partners of $Z_c(3900)$ with opposite C parity, while other groups such as Chen (2022) and Han (2022) treated them as distinct hadrons. The present work enables a unified description where both the narrow $Z_{cs}(3985)$ and broad $Z_{cs}(4000)$ arise from the same molecular channel, and predicts two new $D_s^*\bar{D}^*$ resonances ($Z_{cs}(4123)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$) awaiting experimental confirmation, with the former potentially corresponding to a state recently reported by the BESIII collaboration (2023).
