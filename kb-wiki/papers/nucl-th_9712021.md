# Can one learn anything from proton-proton bremsstrahlung? Comment on H. W. Fearing preprint nucl-th/9710061

> Recently, Fearing (nucl-th/9710061) has argued that, as a matter of principle, proton-proton bremsstrahlung can yield no more information about the off-shell properties of the nucleon-nucleon interaction than can already be deduced from the on-shell properties. In this note we challenge Fearing's conclusion.

# Can one learn anything from proton-proton bremsstrahlung? Comment on H. W. Fearing preprint nucl-th/9710061

## Key claim

Fearing (1997) argued that proton-proton bremsstrahlung can yield no more information about off-shell properties of the nucleon-nucleon interaction than can already be deduced from on-shell properties; Pursey, Shirokov, and Weber challenge this conclusion, arguing that Fearing's arguments are inconclusive and do not apply generally, particularly for potential models based on effective field theories with different symmetry properties such as quarks versus nucleons.

## Method

The authors respond to Fearing's two main arguments: first, that off-shell contributions to the bremsstrahlung amplitude are inextricably mixed with irreducible contact terms that cannot be computed in a potential model; second, that field transformations can eliminate any additional off-shell contributions. They argue that irreducible contact terms are suppressed in the kinematical region where final-state protons are nonrelativistic, making a distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) appropriate. They further argue that Fearing's field transformation argument fails because the set of invariant functions under field transformations need not be empty for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, unlike the simple pion model Fearing analyzed, and because the cancellation theorem does not hold for nucleons interacting through pion exchange due to isospin.

## Key numbers

- Distorted wave calculations of the pp → ppγ cross section comparing Paris, Hamada-Johnston, and Moscow potentials show dramatic differences for incident laboratory frame energies of 350 MeV and 450 MeV in kinematic regions corresponding to emission of hard photons.
- The authors reference Neudatchin et al. (1997) for these calculations, but no explicit cross section values are given in the present text.

## Context

This comment directly challenges Fearing (1997, nucl-th/9710061), who argued that proton-proton bremsstrahlung cannot yield information about off-shell NN interaction properties beyond on-shell constraints. The authors extend the discussion by referencing the Moscow potential (Neudatchin, Obukhovsky, and Smirnov, 1973; Kukulin and Pomerantsev, 1992), which is inspired by a quark-gluon-Goldstone boson field theory and assumes a 6-quark configuration with s⁴p² orbital structure, and recent work by Stancu, Pepin, and Glozman (1997) using a quark-Goldstone boson model. The comment enables further debate on whether off-shell information can be extracted from bremsstrahlung experiments, with work in progress by Khokhlov, Neudatchin, Shirokov, and Knyr (1997) expected to resolve ambiguities in reference frames.
