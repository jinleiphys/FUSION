# No-Core MCSM calculation for $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$Be low-lying spectra

> The low-lying excited states of $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$Be are investigated within a no-core Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) framework employing a realistic potential obtained via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method. The excitation energies of the 2$^+_1$ and 2$^+_2$ states and the B(E2;$\,2^+_{1,2}\rightarrow$ 0$^+_{g.s.}$) for $^{10}$Be in the MCSM with a standard treatment of spurious center-of-mass motion show good agreement with experimental data. Some properties of low-lying states of $^{10}$Be are studied in terms of quadrupole moments, E2 transitions and single-particle occupation numbers. The E2 transition probability of $^{10}$C, the mirror nucleus of $^{10}$Be, is also presented with a good agreement to experiment. The triaxial deformation of $^{10}$Be and $^{10}$C is discussed in terms of the B(E2) values. The removal of the spurious center-of-mass motion affects differently on various states: for instance, negligible effects on the 2$^+_1$ and 2$^+_2$ levels of $^{10}$Be, while significant and favorable shift for the 1$^-_1$ level. It is suggested that the description of $^{12}$Be needs a larger model space as well as some other higher excited states of $^{10}$Be, as an indicator that these are dominated by intruder configurations.

# No-Core MCSM calculation for $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$Be low-lying spectra

## Key claim
The no-core Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) with a UCOM-transformed realistic potential reproduces the low-lying excitation energies and B(E2) values for $^{10}$Be and its mirror nucleus $^{10}$C in reasonable agreement with experiment, but only for states that are not dominated by intruder configurations; the description of $^{12}$Be and higher excited states of $^{10}$Be requires a larger model space than the $e_{\rm max}=3$ space used here.

## Method
The authors performed no-core MCSM calculations using a harmonic oscillator basis truncated at $e_{\rm max}=3$, employing UCOM-transformed realistic two-nucleon interactions derived from the chiral N$^3$LO potential. Basis states were generated stochastically via imaginary-time evolution with the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, then projected onto good parity and angular momentum quantum numbers before diagonalization. Spurious center-of-mass motion was suppressed using Lawson's prescription with $\beta_{c.m.}\cdot\hbar\omega/A = 10$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of $^{4}$He: about -35 MeV in the $e_{\rm max}=3$ MCSM calculation, compared to the experimental value of about -65 MeV (after Coulomb correction of about 5 MeV, the difference is about 35 MeV, attributed partly to missing three-body forces)
- Excitation energy convergence for $^{10}$Be in $e_{\rm max}=3$: energy differences between last two consecutive MCSM dimensions were 18 keV for 2$^+_1$, 15 keV for 2$^+_2$, and 37 keV for 0$^+_2$, giving relative accuracy of about 0.3% for 2$^+$ states and about 0.7% for the 0$^+_2$ state
- B(E2;$\,2^{+}_{1}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{g.s.}$) for $^{10}$Be: MCSM value 9.29 e$^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental 9.2(3) e$^2$fm$^4$
- B(E2;$\,2^{+}_{2}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{g.s.}$) for $^{10}$Be: MCSM value 0.32 e$^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental 0.11(2) e$^2$fm$^4$
- B(E2;$\,2^{+}_{1}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{g.s.}$) for $^{10}$C: MCSM value 9.30 e$^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental 8.8(3) e$^2$fm$^4$
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment of $^{10}$Be from spectroscopic quadrupole moment: 20.5 e fm$^2$, consistent with 21.6 e fm$^2$ extracted from B(E2;$\,2^{+}_{1}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{1}$)
- Triaxial deformation parameter $\gamma$ for $^{10}$Be: 11.4$^\circ$ from the Davidov-Fillipov model using B(E2;$\,2^{+}_{2}\rightarrow 0^{+}_{1}$)=0.32 e$^2$fm$^4$
- Occupation number of the 0$s_{1/2}$ orbit in $^{10}$Be: about 1.8 for both protons and neutrons (about 90% occupancy), implying about 2/3 probability of $^4$He-core formation
- $sd$ shell occupation in $^{10}$Be: about 0.7 for protons and neutrons combined
- For the 1$^-_1$ state of $^{10}$Be, changing $\beta_{c.m.}\hbar\omega/A$ from 0 to 10 MeV increased the excitation energy by about 4 MeV

## Context
This work extends no-core shell model techniques to the MCSM framework, which had previously been used primarily for shell-model calculations with an inert core (Otsuka 2001, Utsuno 1999). The results for $^{10}$Be are compared with NCSM calculations using CD-Bonn and CD-Bonn 2000 potentials (Caurier 2002), GFMC calculations with AV18 potential (Pieper 2001, 2002), and AMD calculations (Kanada-En'yo 1995). The MCSM reproduces the 2$^+_1$ and 2$^+_2$ states of $^{10}$Be similarly to NCSM, but can include full $sd$ shell configurations that NCSM cannot at 8$\hbar\omega$ truncation. The results enable future systematic no-core MCSM calculations with larger model spaces and improved methods, and suggest that the triaxial deformation of $^{10}$Be and $^{10}$C warrants experimental investigation.
