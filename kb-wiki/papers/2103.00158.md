# Hybrid model of proton structure functions

> We develop a "hybrid" model of the proton inelastic structure functions applicable in a wide region of invariant mass of produced states $W$ and invariant momentum transfer $Q$ including deep inelastic scattering (DIS), nucleon resonance production as well as the region close to inelastic threshold. DIS is described in terms of the parton distributions together with higher-twist corrections from an available global QCD fit. The resonant part is addressed in terms of the Breit-Wiegner contributions from five states including the $Δ(1232)$ resonance, the $N(1440)$ Roper resonance, and three effective resonances describing the second and third resonance regions. The couplings of the nucleon resonances to photon are described in terms of helicity amplitudes. The nonresonant background is addressed in terms of DIS structure functions smoothly extrapolated to low-$W$ and low-$Q$ values with the proper behavior at the real photon limit $Q^2=0$ as well as near the inelastic threshold. We independently treat the transverse $F_T$ and the longitudinal $F_L$ structure function and fix the model parameters from a global analysis of the world hydrogen electroproduction and photoproduction cross-section data. We demonstrate a very good performance of the model by comparing our predictions with data on differential cross sections and the structure functions $F_2$ and $R=F_L/F_T$.

## Key claim

The authors develop a "hybrid" model of proton inelastic structure functions that spans both the resonance production region and deep inelastic scattering, with the nonresonant background modeled by extrapolating DIS structure functions to low invariant mass \(W\) and low momentum transfer \(Q\) while respecting the real photon limit at \(Q^2=0\) and the inelastic threshold behavior.

## Method

The model separately treats the transverse \(F_T\) and longitudinal \(F_L\) structure functions. DIS contributions are computed using parton distribution functions from a global NNLO QCD fit (Alekhin 2007, Alekhin 2008) with higher-twist and target mass corrections. The resonant part uses Breit-Wigner contributions from five states: the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, the \(N(1440)\) Roper resonance, and three effective resonances (\(R_1\), \(R_2\), \(R_3\)) describing the second and third resonance regions, with photon couplings described by helicity amplitudes. The nonresonant background is obtained by smoothly extrapolating DIS structure functions to low \(W\) and low \(Q\) with proper behavior at \(Q^2=0\) and at the inelastic threshold. Model parameters are fixed from a global fit to world hydrogen electroproduction and photoproduction cross-section data.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological resonance analyses such as Walker (1968), Drechsel (1998), Christy (2007), and Hiller Blin (2019) by providing a unified model that smoothly connects the resonance region to DIS. It enables studies of Bloom-Gilman quark-hadron duality, evaluation of the photon content in the proton (Manohar 2016), nuclear effects in the resonance-DIS transition region (Kulagin 2018), and calculation of total neutrino cross sections (Kulagin 2016) needed for modern long-baseline neutrino experiments like DUNE.
