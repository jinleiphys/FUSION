# Thermal behaviors of light scalar resonances at low temperatures

> We study the thermal properties of the lowest multiplet of the QCD light-flavor scalar resonances, including the $f_0(500)/σ$, $K_{0}^{*}(700)/κ$, $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$, in the framework of unitarized $U(3)$ chiral perturbation theory. After the successful fits to the meson-meson scattering inputs, such as the phase shifts and inelasticities, we obtain the unknown parameters and further calculate the resonance poles and their residues at zero temperature. By including the finite-temperature effects in the unitarized meson-meson scattering amplitudes, the thermal behaviors of the scalar resonance poles in the complex energy plane are studied. The masses of $σ$ and $κ$ are found to considerably decrease when increasing the temperatures, while their widths turn out to be still large when the temperatures reach around $200$ MeV. In contrast, both the masses and widths of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ are only slightly changed.

## Key claim

The thermal behaviors of the light scalar resonances are studied by unitarizing only the leading-order chiral perturbation theory amplitudes, with finite-temperature effects entering solely through the unitarization loop function; the authors assume this on-shell approximation may give a qualitatively correct description, though they acknowledge that complete finite-temperature calculations in three-flavor chiral perturbation theory would require an independent work.

## Method

The authors fit the unknown subtraction constants in the unitarized leading-order U(3) chiral perturbation theory amplitudes to experimental phase shifts and inelasticities for pi-pi scattering with (I,J)=(0,0) and K-pi scattering with (I,J)=(1/2,0), using data up to 1100 MeV and 1000 MeV respectively. They then incorporate finite-temperature effects by replacing the zero-temperature two-point loop function G(s) with its thermal counterpart using the imaginary time formalism, and analytically continue the unitarized amplitudes to the second Riemann sheet to track the resonance pole trajectories as functions of temperature from 0 to 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Mass of sigma resonance at zero temperature: 465 (+1/-2) MeV, with half-width 234 (+8/-8) MeV
- Mass of kappa resonance at zero temperature: 738 (+8/-9) MeV, with half-width 274 (+8/-9) MeV
- Mass of f0(980) at zero temperature: 977 (+6/-9) MeV, with half-width 15 (+5/-3) MeV
- Mass of a0(980) at zero temperature: 1037 (+17/-14) MeV, with half-width 44 (+6/-9) MeV
- When temperature increases from 0 to 200 MeV, the sigma mass decreases by approximately 300 MeV and the kappa mass decreases by approximately 100 MeV
- When temperature increases from 0 to 200 MeV, the f0(980) mass decreases by approximately 15 MeV and the a0(980) mass decreases by approximately 30 MeV
- The subtraction constant for K-pi scattering with (I,J)=(1/2,0) is -0.42 (+0.16/-0.16), with chi-squared/d.o.f. = 16.2/(36-1)

## Context

This work extends the study of thermal properties of the sigma resonance previously investigated using the inverse amplitude method up to one-loop level by Dobado (2002), Fernandez-Fraile (2007), Cabrera (2008), and Cortes (2015), but simultaneously treats all members of the lowest scalar multiplet (sigma, kappa, f0(980), a0(980)) within a unitarized U(3) chiral perturbation theory framework. The results provide thermal pole trajectories that can serve as input for hadron-resonance-gas models used in heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and the finding that sigma and kappa masses decrease significantly while f0(980) and a0(980) remain nearly unchanged offers insights into the pattern of chiral symmetry restoration. The authors note that their on-shell unitarization approach gives qualitatively similar sigma pole trajectories to the more complete one-loop IAM study of Cabrera (2008), suggesting that thermal corrections to the unitarity loop function capture the dominant finite-temperature effects for these resonances.
