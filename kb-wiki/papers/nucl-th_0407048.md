# Momentum distribution in heavy deformed nuclei: role of effective mass

> The impact of nuclear deformation on the momentum distributions (MD) of occupied proton states in $^{238}$U is studied with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon (WS) shell model and the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) scheme. Four Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) with different effective masses are used. The calculations reveal significant deformation effects in the low-momentum domain of $K^π=1/2^{\pm}$ states, mainly of those lying near the Fermi surface. For other states, the deformation effect on MD is rather small and may be neglected. The most remarkable result is that the very different Skyrme parameterizations and the WS potential give about identical MD. This means that the value of effective mass, being crucial for the description of the spectra, is not important for the spatial shape of the wave functions and thus for the MD. In general, it seems that, for the description of MD at $0\le k \le 300$ MeV/c, one may use any single-particle scheme (phenomenological or self-consistent) fitted properly to the global ground state properties.

# Momentum distribution in heavy deformed nuclei: role of effective mass

## Key claim
The value of the effective mass, which is crucial for describing single-particle spectra, does not significantly influence the momentum distributions of occupied proton states in heavy deformed nuclei; any single-particle scheme (phenomenological or self-consistent) that is properly fitted to global ground state properties yields about identical momentum distributions in the momentum domain 0 ≤ k ≤ 300 MeV/c, with the exception of states near level crossings.

## Method
The momentum distributions of occupied proton states in axially deformed ²³⁸U were calculated using a phenomenological Woods-Saxon (WS) potential and the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with four Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) covering effective masses from m*/m = 1.00 to 0.58. The single-particle wave functions were Fourier-transformed to momentum space, and the momentum distributions were averaged over the nuclear symmetry axis direction; pairing was treated in the BCS approximation.

## Key numbers
- Effective masses used: m*/m = 1.00 (SkT6), 0.79 (SkM*), 0.69 (SLy6), 0.58 (SkI3)
- Quadrupole moments for ²³⁸U: WS = 11.66 b, SkT6 = 11.10 b, SkM* = 11.11 b, SLy6 = 11.06 b, SkI3 = 10.89 b (experimental estimates: 11.1–11.3 b)
- Fermi energies for protons in ²³⁸U: WS = -6.63 MeV, SkT6 = -6.48 MeV, SkM* = -6.17 MeV, SLy6 = -7.25 MeV, SkI3 = -7.19 MeV
- Energy of lowest proton single-particle level (E₀): WS = -33.69 MeV, SkT6 = -32.75 MeV, SkM* = -39.80 MeV, SLy6 = -43.12 MeV, SkI3 = -48.53 MeV
- Momentum range studied: 0 ≤ k ≤ 300 MeV/c
- Spectral stretching (|E_F - E₀|) ranges from 26 to 41 MeV across the different models

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of deformation effects in momentum distributions for light and rare-earth nuclei using the Nilsson model and SHF with SIII forces (Guerin et al., 1990s), and a preliminary letter on ²³⁸U using WS and SkM* (Nesterenko et al., 2003). The study addresses the need for theoretical analysis of modern (e,e'p) experiments on heavy actinide nuclei, particularly ²³⁸U. The finding that effective mass does not affect momentum distributions enables the use of any well-fitted single-particle potential for calculating inputs to knockout reactions, while clarifying that the effective mass remains critical for spectral properties as established by Mahaux (1980s) and Brown (1990s).
