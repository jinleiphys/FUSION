# Nuclear shell structures in terms of classical periodic orbits

> Semiclassical periodic-orbit theory (POT) is applied to the physics of nuclear structures, with the use of a realistic nuclear mean-field model given by the radial power-law potential. Evolution of deformed shell structures, which are responsible for various nuclear deformations, are clearly understood from the contribution of short classical periodic orbits (POs). Bifurcations of short POs, which imply underlying local dynamical symmetry, play significant role there. The effect of the spin degree of freedom is also investigated in relevance to the pseudospin symmetry in spherical nuclei and the prolate-oblate asymmetry in shell structures of nuclei with quadrupole-type deformations.

## Key claim

Semiclassical periodic-orbit theory can explain the evolution of deformed nuclear shell structures and the prolate-oblate asymmetry in shell energies, but only when spin-orbit coupling is included and the bifurcations of short classical periodic orbits are taken into account; the power-law potential model with exponent α ≈ 4–7 provides a realistic approximation to the Woods-Saxon potential for medium to heavy nuclei.

## Method

The author applies the semiclassical trace formula for the level density and shell energy, expressing the oscillating part as a sum over classical periodic orbits (POs) in a radial power-law potential model V(r) ∝ r^α. The scaling property of this Hamiltonian is exploited to relate POs at different energies, and the Strutinsky shell correction method is used to extract the shell energy from the single-particle spectrum. Bifurcations of short POs are analyzed via the monodromy matrix and catastrophe theory, and the spin-orbit coupling term is incorporated to study pseudospin symmetry and deformation effects.

## Key numbers

- The power-law exponent α = 4–7 corresponds to medium to heavy nuclei with mass numbers A = 50–200, fitted to the Buck-Pilt potential with depth V₀ = 50 MeV, radius R_A = 1.3A^(1/3) fm, and surface diffuseness a = 0.7 fm.
- The smoothing width γ for the Strutinsky method uses curvature corrections up to order 2M = 6 (Laguerre polynomial L_M^(1/2)).
- The energy spacing between successive minima from a PO is Δe = 2πħ / T_β, where T_β is the orbit period; shorter POs give larger Δe and determine the gross shell structure.
- The shell energy expression includes a factor (ħ / T_β)^2, which suppresses contributions from longer POs relative to the level density formula.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical periodic-orbit approach previously applied to metallic clusters by Nishioka et al. and to the spherical cavity model by Balian and Bloch, now adapted to nuclear mean-field potentials. The radial power-law model interpolates between the harmonic oscillator (α = 2) and infinite square well (α → ∞), and the analysis of PO bifurcations connects to local dynamical symmetries relevant for superdeformations and octupole deformations. The results enable a semiclassical understanding of nuclear magic numbers, pseudospin symmetry, and the observed prolate dominance in ground-state deformations, building on the Nilsson model and the Woods-Saxon potential parameterization of Cwiok et al.
