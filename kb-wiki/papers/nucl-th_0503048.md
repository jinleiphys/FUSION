# Covariant description of kinetic freeze out through a finite time-like layer

> The Freeze Out (FO) problem is addressed for a covariant FO probability and a finite FO layer with a time-like normal vector continuing the line of studies introduced in Ref. [1]. The resulting post FO momentum distribution functions are presented and discussed. We show that in general the post FO distributions are non-thermal and asymmetric distributions even for time-like FO situations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that freeze out through a finite time-like layer produces post-FO momentum distributions that are non-thermal and asymmetric, even when the FO normal vector is time-like; this contradicts the common assumption that a boosted Jüttner (equilibrium) distribution can describe the post-FO state at time-like hypersurface points where the flow velocity is not parallel to the FO normal.

## Method

The authors model kinetic freeze out by splitting the full distribution function into interacting and free components, then evolve both using coupled transport equations with a covariant escape rate and a relaxation-time approximation for rethermalization. They solve these equations for a massless pion gas with zero chemical potential, considering both an infinitely long FO and a finite FO layer of proper thickness \(L = 10\tau_0\), and compute the resulting post-FO momentum distributions, temperature evolution, and flow velocity evolution in the Rest Frame of the Front (RFF).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of the interacting component: \(T_0 = 170\) MeV.
- For a finite FO layer, the proper thickness is set to \(L = 10\tau_0\) (where \(\tau_0\) is the initial characteristic FO time).
- The post-FO distributions deviate strongly from thermal distributions for transverse momenta \(p_x < 300\) MeV.
- For FO durations less than \(2\tau_0\), the final momentum spectrum approaches a constant-temperature equilibrium distribution; below this threshold the gradual FO does not have enough time to significantly change the spectrum shape.
- In the special case \(u^\mu = d\sigma^\mu\) (flow parallel to FO normal), the temperature decreases exponentially as \(T(t) = T(t_0) \exp\left(-\frac{k}{\tau_0}(t-t_0)\right)\) with \(k = 3/(4\pi^2\sigma_{SB})\).
- The post-FO distribution for this special case is given by \(f^f = \frac{k^{-1}}{(2\pi^3)} \operatorname{Ei}\left(-\frac{p^\mu u_\mu(t_0)}{T(t_0)}\right)\), where Ei is the exponential integral function.

## Context

This work extends the gradual FO description introduced for space-like FO layers in Molnar et al. (2004) to the time-like case, and generalizes earlier time-like FO models by Csernai et al. (2004) that assumed \(u^\mu = d\sigma^\mu\) and infinitely long FO. The results show that the common Cooper-Frye sudden FO prescription and the practice of boosting a Jüttner distribution at time-like hypersurface points are generally invalid; instead, the post-FO distribution is a superposition of sources with decreasing temperature and flow speed. This enables more realistic modeling of kinetic freeze out in heavy-ion collisions, where the low-\(p_t\) enhancement of pion spectra may be partly explained by gradual FO with rethermalization.
