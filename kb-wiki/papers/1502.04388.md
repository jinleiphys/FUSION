# Detailed study of parton energy loss via measurement of fractional momentum loss of high $p_T$ hadrons in heavy ion collisions

> PHENIX measurement of the fractional momentum loss ($d p_T/p_T$) of high $p_T$ identified hadrons are presented. The $d p_T/p_T$ of high $p_T$ pizero which are computed from 39GeV Au+Au over to 2.76TeV Pb+Pb are found to vary by a factor of six. We plotted the $d p_T/p_T$ against several global variables, $N_{part}$, $N_{qp}$ and $dN/deta$, and found global features. It was found that 200GeV Au+Au points are merging into the central 2.76TeV Pb+Pb points when plotting $d p_T/p_T$ against $dN/deta$.

## Key claim

The fractional momentum loss (δpT/pT) of high-pT π0 mesons varies by a factor of six across collision energies from 39 GeV Au+Au to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb, even though the nuclear modification factors (RAA) appear similar across these systems; this variation is revealed only by directly measuring the momentum shift rather than the yield suppression.

## Method

The fractional momentum loss δpT/pT was extracted by comparing the high-pT hadron spectra from p+p and A+A collisions: the p+p yield was scaled by the nuclear overlap function TAA for each centrality bin, then the p+p spectrum was shifted horizontally until it matched the A+A invariant yield at a given pT, and the momentum difference was calculated. The uncertainty on δpT/pT was obtained by inverting the quadratic sum of the statistical and systematic uncertainties on the A+A and p+p yields using the p+p fit function. The analysis used π0 mesons from PHENIX at RHIC (39, 62.4, and 200 GeV Au+Au and 200 GeV Cu+Cu) and charged hadrons from ALICE at LHC (2.76 TeV Pb+Pb), with all comparisons made at a reference pT(p+p) = 7 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- δpT/pT for high-pT π0 varies by a factor of six from 39 GeV Au+Au to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- δpT/pT changes by a factor of three from 39 to 200 GeV Au+Au collisions
- δpT/pT changes by a factor of approximately 1.5 from 200 GeV Au+Au to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- Power-law fit of δpT/pT vs dNch/dη for 200 GeV Au+Au yields α = 0.64 ± 0.07, assuming dNch/dη ∝ (Npart)^1.19
- Power α obtained from fit to integrated RAA as a function of Npart is 0.57 ± 0.13, consistent with the δpT/pT result

## Context

This work extends the traditional approach of using the nuclear modification factor RAA to study parton energy loss, which was pioneered by Wang (1998) and first observed by PHENIX (Adler, 2003). By directly measuring the fractional momentum shift rather than the yield suppression, the analysis reveals that RAA alone masks a factor-of-six variation in energy loss across collision energies from RHIC to LHC. The scaling of δpT/pT with charged multiplicity dNch/dη shows that 200 GeV Au+Au points merge with 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb points at high multiplicity, suggesting a common energy-density dependence; this enables future systematic studies of parton energy loss using δpT/pT as a more sensitive observable than RAA.
