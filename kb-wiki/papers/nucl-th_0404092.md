# WKB approximation for multi-channel barrier penetrability

> Using a method of local transmission matrix, we generalize the well-known WKB formula for a barrier penetrability to multi-channel systems. We compare the WKB penetrability with a solution of the coupled-channels equations, and show that the WKB formula works well at energies well below the lowest adiabatic barrier. We also discuss the eigen-channel approach to a multi-channel tunneling, which may improve the performance of the WKB formula near and above the barrier.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the single-channel WKB barrier penetrability formula to multi-channel systems using a local transmission matrix method, and shows that this WKB formula works well at energies well below the lowest adiabatic barrier, but requires additional prescriptions (dynamical norm method or eigen-channel approach) for energies near and above the barrier.

## Method

The authors derive a WKB approximation for the local transmission matrix by neglecting the local reflection matrix in the coupled-channels equations, then discretize the coordinate and iterate the resulting equation backward from infinity to obtain the transmission matrix at minus infinity. They apply this multi-channel WKB formula to a three-channel problem with a Gaussian barrier and coupling potentials that mimic the fusion reaction between two 58Ni nuclei, and compare the results with exact numerical solutions of the coupled-channels equations.

## Key numbers

- The three eigen-barriers obtained by diagonalizing the coupling matrix have heights of 97.31 MeV, 102.0 MeV, and 106.7 MeV, while the uncoupled barrier height is 100 MeV.
- The uncoupled barrier parameters are V0 = 100 MeV, F0 = 3 MeV, and s = sf = 3 fm.
- The excitation energy epsilon is 2 MeV and the mass m is 29 mN, where mN = 938 MeV is the nucleon mass.
- The WKB integration was performed from x = -15 fm to x = 15 fm with a mesh spacing of Delta x = 0.05 fm.
- The weight factors for the eigen-channel approach were evaluated as 0.5914, 0.3543, and 0.0543 for the lowest, second lowest, and highest eigen-barriers, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the well-known single-channel WKB formula for barrier penetrability to systems with intrinsic degrees of freedom, building on the local transmission matrix method originally developed by Brenig and Russ (1993). The paper improves upon the dynamical norm method of Takigawa, Hagino, and Abe (1995) by not assuming a classical path from the beginning when evaluating the non-adiabatic factor. The multi-channel WKB formula enables applications to heavy-ion fusion reactions at extremely low energies, electron screening effects in nuclear astrophysical reactions, and quantum scattering problems with coupling to a heat bath, where standard coupled-channels calculations may be computationally demanding or numerically unstable.
