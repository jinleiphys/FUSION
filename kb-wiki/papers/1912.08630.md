# $ΛΛ$ and N$Ξ$ interactions from Lattice QCD near the physical point

> The $S$-wave $ΛΛ$ and $N Ξ$ interactions are studied on the basis of the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations close to the physical point ($m_π\simeq 146{\rm{MeV}}$ and $m_K \simeq 525{\rm{MeV}}$). Lattice QCD potentials in four different spin-isospin channels are extracted by using the coupled-channel HAL QCD method and are parametrized by analytic functions to calculate the scattering phase shifts. The $ΛΛ$ interaction at low energies shows only a weak attraction, which does not provide a bound or resonant dihyperon. The $NΞ$ interaction in the spin-singlet and isospin-singlet channel is most attractive and lead the $NΞ$ system near unitarity. Relevance to the strangeness=$-2$ hypernuclei as well as to two-baryon correlations in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim
The (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses (pion mass about 146 MeV and kaon mass about 525 MeV) show that the Lambda-Lambda interaction is weakly attractive and does not produce a bound or resonant dihyperon, while the N-Xi interaction in the spin-singlet and isospin-singlet channel is sufficiently attractive to bring the system near unitarity.

## Method
The authors used the coupled-channel HAL QCD method to extract energy-independent non-local potentials for the S-wave Lambda-Lambda and N-Xi systems from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations on a 96^4 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.0846(7) fm (L about 8.1 fm). The potentials in four spin-isospin channels (11S0, 31S0, 13S1, 33S1) were parametrized by analytic functions combining Gaussian and Yukawa forms, then used to solve the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation and compute scattering phase shifts and inelasticities.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass m_pi = 146 MeV and kaon mass m_K = 525 MeV, from the lattice QCD simulation
- Lattice cutoff a^{-1} = 2.333(18) GeV, corresponding to a = 0.0846(7) fm and spatial volume L about 8.1 fm
- Lambda-Lambda scattering length a_0^(Lambda-Lambda) = -0.81 +/- 0.23 (+0.00/-0.13) fm
- Lambda-Lambda effective range r_eff^(Lambda-Lambda) = 5.47 +/- 0.78 (+0.09/-0.55) fm
- N-Xi phase shift in the 11S0 channel rises to about 60 degrees just above the N-Xi threshold, indicating strong attraction
- Baryon masses: N = 0.9553(18)(12)(74) GeV, Lambda = 1.1398(11)(2)(88) GeV, Sigma = 1.2217(9)(15)(94) GeV, Xi = 1.3552(12)(1)(105) GeV

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of hyperon interactions by the HAL QCD Collaboration, including their earlier work on the H-dibaryon in 3-flavor QCD at heavy quark masses (Inoue 2011, Beane 2010), and complements recent results on the N-Omega system (Iritani 2018) and Omega-Omega system (Gongyo 2017) using the same lattice setup. The Lambda-Lambda scattering parameters were subsequently confirmed to be consistent with experimental constraints from ALICE at the LHC (Acharya 2018). The results enable improved calculations of strangeness -2 hypernuclei and two-baryon correlations in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, with the fitted analytic potentials being used for phenomenological applications such as binding energy calculations of Xi hypernuclei (Hiyama 2019).
