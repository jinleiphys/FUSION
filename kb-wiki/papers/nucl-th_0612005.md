# Behaviour of the potentials due to strangeness degree of freedom in $_{ΛΛ}^6$He hypernucleus

> Fully correlated study of $_{ΛΛ} ^6$He hypernucleus has been performed with two- and three- baryon potentials. For the S=-2 sector, various simulations of Nijmegen $ΛΛ$ potential models have been used. We investigate the role of every strength of the strange sector potentials on the energy breakdown and present a crystal clear understanding of their interplay. Consistency between $_Λ^5$He and $_{ΛΛ}^6$He depends on the $ΛN$ space-exchange strength only. Investigation limits the strength of simulated Nijmegen $ΛΛ$ potential models. The study is a step forward to determine all the strengths, to reslove A=5 anomaly and to search for $_{ΛΛ}^4$H in an authentic way.

## Key claim

The study finds that consistency between the calculated binding energies of $_\Lambda^5$He and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He depends only on the $\Lambda N$ space-exchange strength $\varepsilon$, with a value of $\varepsilon \approx 0.18$ being the unique condition for consistency, and that the simulated Nijmegen $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential strength parameter $\gamma$ is constrained to $0.85 < \gamma < 0.95$ when reproducing the experimental double-$\Lambda$ separation energy.

## Method

The authors performed a fully correlated variational Monte Carlo (FCVMC) study of the $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He hypernucleus using a Hamiltonian with two- and three-baryon potentials. For the $S=-2$ sector, they used simulated Nijmegen $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential models distinguished by a dimensionless parameter $\gamma$ (with values 0.5463 for NSC97e, 1.0 for ND, and 1.2044 for NEC00), while for the $S=-1$ sector they used a charge symmetric $\Lambda N$ potential with space-exchange strength $\varepsilon$, and for the $S=0$ sector the Argonne $v_{18}$ $NN$ potential and Urbana $NNN$ potential. They systematically varied the strengths $\overline{v}$, $\varepsilon$, $C^P$, and $W^D$ to reproduce the experimental $\Lambda$-separation energy of $_\Lambda^5$He, then computed the double-$\Lambda$ separation energy $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ as a function of $\gamma$.

## Key numbers

* The experimental $\Lambda$-separation energy for $_\Lambda^5$He is $B_{\Lambda}^{exp} = 3.12(2)$ MeV.
* The experimental double-$\Lambda$ separation energy for $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He is $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{exp} = 7.25(19)$ MeV.
* The condition for consistency between $_\Lambda^5$He and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He calculations is $\varepsilon \approx 0.18$.
* The simulated Nijmegen $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential parameter $\gamma$ is constrained to $0.85 < \gamma < 0.95$ when reproducing $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{exp}$ at $\varepsilon \approx 0.18$.
* For $\overline{v} = 6.10$ MeV and $\varepsilon = 0.2$, the total ground-state energy of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He is $E = -35.47(2)$ MeV for $C^P = 0.75$ MeV and $E = -35.43(3)$ MeV for $C^P = 1.5$ MeV, yielding $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 7.74(4)$ MeV and $7.70(4)$ MeV respectively.
* The expectation value of the $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential for $\gamma = 1.0$ is $-5.74(5)$ MeV for $C^P = 0.75$ MeV and $-5.44(4)$ MeV for $C^P = 1.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FCVMC studies of $_\Lambda^5$He (Usmani and Hasan, 2006) and $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He (Usmani and Hasan, 2006) by systematically investigating how variations in all strange-sector potential strengths affect the energy breakdown and consistency between single- and double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei. The study is motivated by the NAGARA event observation of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^6$He (KEK E373) and the conjectured $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4$H from BNL-AGS E906, and it addresses discrepancies between Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations (Nogga, 2002) and fully coupled channel stochastic variational calculations (Nemura, 2005) regarding the binding of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4$H. The results enable a systematic determination of strange-sector potential strengths and provide a pathway to resolve the $A=5$ anomaly and search for $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^4$H.
