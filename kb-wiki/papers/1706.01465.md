# Extracting the sigma-term from low-energy pion-nucleon scattering

> We present an extraction of the pion-nucleon ($πN$) scattering lengths from low-energy $πN$ scattering, by fitting a representation based on Roy-Steiner equations to the low-energy data base. We show that the resulting values confirm the scattering-length determination from pionic atoms, and discuss the stability of the fit results regarding electromagnetic corrections and experimental normalization uncertainties in detail. Our results provide further evidence for a large $πN$ $σ$-term, $σ_{πN}=58(5)$ MeV, in agreement with, albeit less precise than, the determination from pionic atoms.

## Key claim

The paper extracts pion-nucleon scattering lengths from low-energy scattering data using a Roy-Steiner representation, finding values that confirm the pionic-atom determination and support a large sigma-term of σπN = 58(5) MeV, though with less precision than the pionic-atom result.

## Method

The authors fit a Roy-Steiner representation of the πN amplitude to low-energy differential cross section data for π⁺p → π⁺p, π⁻p → π⁻p, and π⁻p → π⁰n, treating both the scattering lengths and experimental normalizations as free fit parameters. The analysis is restricted to energies where the sensitivity to scattering lengths exceeds uncertainties from other sources (Tπmax = 51.5 MeV for π⁺p, 32.7 MeV for π⁻p, 45.6 MeV for charge-exchange), and electromagnetic corrections beyond the Coulomb part are included as a systematic uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- π⁺p → π⁺p scattering length: a = −84.4(4.2) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹ (full electromagnetic corrections, statistical plus systematic uncertainty)
- π⁻p → π⁻p scattering length: a = 82.5(2.1) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹
- π⁻p → π⁰n scattering length: a = −122.3(3.4) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹ (statistical only)
- Resulting sigma-term: σπN = 58(5) MeV
- Maximum kinetic energies for fit: Tπmax = 51.5 MeV (π⁺p), 32.7 MeV (π⁻p), 45.6 MeV (charge-exchange)
- Reduced χ² values: 0.88 (π⁺p), 0.79 (π⁻p), 0.62 (charge-exchange) for full electromagnetic corrections

## Context

This work extends the Roy-Steiner equation framework developed by Hoferichter et al. (2015) for πN scattering, which previously used pionic-atom scattering lengths as external input. The fit provides an independent determination of the scattering lengths from scattering data, analogous to how Kℓ4 decays provide a second determination of ππ scattering lengths (Colangelo, 2001; Garcia-Martin, 2011). The results confirm the pionic-atom determination from Baru et al. (2011) and disagree with the older KH80 partial-wave analysis (Koch, 1980; Hoehler), which gave smaller scattering lengths and a smaller sigma-term. The tension with lattice QCD calculations (BMW, χQCD, ETMC, RQCD) at the 1.5σ to 3.8σ level motivates this independent check.
