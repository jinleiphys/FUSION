# Linear combinations of two-body energies in matrix elements of jn states

> Matrix elements of a two-body interaction between states of the jn configutation (n identical nucleons in the j-orbit) are functions of two-body energies. In some cases, diagonal matrix elements are linear combinations of two-body energies. The coefficients of these linear combinations are rational and non-negative numbers, independent of the two-body interaction. It is shown that if in the jn configuration there is only one state with given spin J, its eigenvalue (the diagonal matrix element) is equal to a linear combination of two-body energies with rational and non-negative coefficients. These coefficients have the same values for any two-body interaction (solvable eigenvalues). If there are several J-states in the jn configuration, they define a sub-matrix of the interaction which should be diagonalized to yield eigenvalues and eigenstates. Bases of these states are constructed from which the sub-matrix characterized by J may be obtained for any two-body interaction. The diagonal elements are linear combinations of two-body energies whose coefficients are independent of the two-body interaction and the non-vanishing ones are rational and positive. Aslo in the non-diagonal elements the coefficients have a simple form. States in the seniority scheme are shown to form such bases. If one of them is an eigenstate of any two-body interaciton its eigenvalue is shown to be solvable.

## Key claim

Diagonal matrix elements of a two-body interaction in $j^n$ configurations are linear combinations of two-body energies with rational and non-negative coefficients only for states constructed by antisymmetrization of principal parents and subsequent orthogonalization; eigenstates obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian sub-matrix when multiple states share the same spin $J$ generally do not have this property, as demonstrated for the $J=9/2$ states in the $j=9/2$ three-particle configuration.

## Method

The paper uses coefficients of fractional parentage (c.f.p.) to expand antisymmetric wave functions of $n$ identical fermions in a single $j$-orbit, reducing matrix elements of two-body interactions to sums over two-body energies $V(J) = \langle j^2 J M | V | j^2 J M \rangle$. For $n=3$, states are constructed by antisymmetrizing principal parents from the $j^2$ configuration, and orthogonalization is performed using Gram-Schmidt procedures. The analysis is extended to general $n$ by induction, showing that c.f.p. of states built from principal parents are square roots of rational functions of angular momenta, and that this property is preserved under orthogonalization.

## Key numbers

- For the $j^3$ configuration with $J=j$ and seniority $v=1$, the c.f.p. for the principal parent with $J_0=0$ is $[j^2(0)j J=j|\}j^3 v=1 J=j] = [(2j-1)/3(2j+1)]^{1/2}$
- For the same state, c.f.p. for $J_1 \neq 0$ are $[j^2(J_1)j J=j|\}j^3 v=1 J=j] = -2[(2J_1+1)/3(2j-1)(2j+1)]^{1/2}$
- For $j < 9/2$, there is only one state with $J=j$ in the $j^3$ configuration; for higher $j$, the number of additional $v=3$ states with $J=j$ is $[(2j-3)/6]$ where $[x]$ is the largest integer not exceeding $x$
- The normalization factor for $n=3$ states is $N(J_0,J) = [3+6(2J_0+1)\{jjJ_0/JjJ_0\}]^{-1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the observation by Zamick and Van Isacker (2008) that coefficients in energy expressions for certain $j^n$ states must be rational numbers, providing a general proof and systematic construction for all $j^n$ configurations. The paper builds on the formalism of coefficients of fractional parentage introduced by Bacher and Goudsmit (1934) and developed by Racah (1942-1949), as well as the $m$-scheme trace method from de-Shalit and Talmi (1963). The results enable identification of which states in the seniority scheme have solvable eigenvalues independent of the specific two-body interaction, and clarify that eigenstates obtained by diagonalization generally do not share this property.
