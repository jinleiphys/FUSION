# Test of J-matrix inverse scattering potentials on electromagnetic reactions of few-nucleon systems

> The J-matrix inverse scattering nucleon-nucleon potentials (JISP), describing both two-nucleon data and bound and resonant states of light nuclei to high accuracy, are tested on the total photoabsorption cross sections of Deuteron, Triton, 3He and 4He. The calculations in the three- and four-body systems are carried out via the Lorentz integral transform method and the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) technique. To this end the HH formalism has been adapted to accommodate non-local potentials. The cross sections calculated with the JISP are compared to those obtained with more traditional realistic interactions, which include two- and three-nucleon forces. While the results of the two kinds of potential models do not differ significantly at lower energies, beyond the resonance peak they show fairly large discrepancies, which increase with the nuclear mass. We argue that these discrepancies may be due to a probably incorrect long range behavior of the JISP, since the one pion exchange is not manifestly implemented there.

## Key claim

The J-matrix inverse scattering nucleon-nucleon potentials (JISP) produce total photoabsorption cross sections for deuteron, triton, 3He, and 4He that agree with results from traditional realistic potentials at lower energies, but show "fairly large discrepancies" beyond the resonance peak that increase with nuclear mass; the authors argue these discrepancies "may be due to a probably incorrect long range behavior of the JISP, since the one pion exchange is not manifestly implemented there."

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross sections for A=2, 3, and 4 nuclei were calculated using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansions for the three- and four-body systems, with the HH formalism adapted to accommodate non-local potentials. For the deuteron, the LIT method was used with harmonic oscillator expansions for JISP, while conventional calculations with explicit NN final state wave functions were carried out for other NN potentials. The JISP results were compared to those from category A potentials (such as AV18 with UIX three-body force) that include explicit one-pion exchange and three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

- JISP calculations for three- and four-body binding energies converged with hyperspherical momentum K ≤ 20, while for potentials like AV18 one needs K larger than 100
- The LIT equation was solved with Γ = 10 and 20 MeV for a few hundred ω₀ values in the interval from -10 MeV to 200 MeV
- For the deuteron longitudinal elastic form factor, JISP6 results are similar to AV14 and BonnRA up to momentum transfer q² = 2 fm⁻², but considerably lower for higher q²
- The JISP6 potential leads to a good description of deuteron form factor data only up to q² = 2 fm⁻²

## Context

This work extends the calculation of total photodisintegration cross sections of 4He from the previous study by the same group (PRLNOI) using the AV18+UIX potential to include non-local potentials of category C, specifically the JISP potentials from Refs. JISP6, JISPlong, and JISP16. The paper enables testing whether potential models that reproduce few-body binding energies without three-body operators (category C) predict the same electromagnetic observables as traditional potentials with three-body forces (category A), and suggests that the missing one-pion exchange in JISP may cause discrepancies at higher energies.
