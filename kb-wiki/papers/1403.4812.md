# A microscopic benchmark-study of triaxiality in low-lying states of 76Kr

> We report on a seven-dimensional generator coordinate calculation in the two deformation parameters $β$ and $γ$ together with projection on three-dimensional angular momentum and two particle numbers for the low-lying states in $^{76}$Kr. These calculations are based on covariant density functional theory. Excellent agreement is found with the data for the spectrum and the electric multipole transition strengths. This answers the important question of dynamic correlations and triaxiality in a fully microscopic way. We find that triaxial configurations dominate both the ground state and the quasi $γ$-band. This yields a different picture from the simple interpretation in terms of "coexistence of a prolate ground state with an oblate low-lying excited state", which is based on the measured sign of spectroscopic quadrupole moments. This study also provides for the first time a benchmark for the collective Hamiltonian in five dimensions. Moreover, we point out that the staggering phase of the $γ$-band is not a safe signature for rigid triaxiality of the low-energy structure.

## Key claim

Triaxial configurations dominate both the ground state and the quasi-γ-band of ⁷⁶Kr, contradicting the earlier interpretation of "coexistence of a prolate ground state with an oblate low-lying excited state" that was based on the measured sign of spectroscopic quadrupole moments. The staggering phase of the γ-band is not a safe signature for rigid triaxiality of the low-energy structure.

## Method

The authors performed a seven-dimensional generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation in the two deformation parameters β and γ, combined with three-dimensional angular momentum projection and two particle-number projections (for neutrons and protons), starting from constrained relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) states based on the covariant density functional PC-PK1. They also carried out a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) calculation using the same energy density functional with cranking inertia parameters and no scaling factor, providing a direct benchmark between the full GCM approach and the 5DCH method.

## Key numbers

- The GCM calculation reproduces the experimental 2⁺₁ state at 0.424 MeV with a calculated value of 0.441 MeV, giving a stretching factor of only 1.04 that stays roughly constant with spin.
- The 5DCH calculation gives the 2⁺₁ state at 0.508 MeV, with a stretching factor of 1.2 that increases to 1.38 for the 8⁺₁ state (experimental 2.879 MeV, 5DCH 3.936 MeV).
- The GCM calculation yields the 0⁺₂ state at 0.876 MeV (experimental 0.770 MeV) and the 2⁺₂ state at 1.036 MeV (experimental 1.222 MeV).
- The constrained RMF+BCS energy surface shows a ridge of roughly 0.5 MeV between the two axial minima; after three-dimensional angular momentum projection a shallow triaxial minimum with a depth of roughly 0.5 MeV develops at β ≈ 0.4 and γ ≈ 20°.
- Computing each matrix element in the collective coordinate space takes about 200 CPU hours with one processor of 2.5 GHz Intel Xeon E5-2640.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field calculations for ⁷⁶Kr that previously were restricted to axial symmetry (Bender 2006, Fu 2013) and failed to reproduce the low-energy structure, and it provides the first fully microscopic benchmark for the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) method. The results enable future studies of triaxiality in the A ≈ 80 mass region and clarify that the 5DCH method, despite using unprojected energy surfaces with zero-point corrections, produces spectra and wave functions qualitatively similar to the full GCM calculation, though with stretched energy spacings.
