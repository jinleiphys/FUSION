# Prospects for neutrino oscillation parameters

> In this contribution we discuss the future of the global long-baseline neutrino oscillation program. The case is made that our current lack of understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions is a serious challenge which will need to be met with new experimental initiatives in neutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the future of long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments lies in precision measurements rather than discovery, but that our current lack of understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions presents a serious challenge that may not be resolvable even with a capable near detector complex.

## Method

The author reviews the current and planned long-baseline neutrino oscillation program, including T2K, NOvA, and DUNE, and analyzes the systematic uncertainties that limit precision measurements. The paper examines the limitations of near detectors in constraining the product of beam flux and cross section, and discusses the difficulties in neutrino energy reconstruction due to incomplete understanding of neutrino-nucleus interaction micro-physics.

## Key numbers

- T2K plans to increase beam power to 1.2 MW on a timescale of about a decade
- Current NOvA data represent only about 1/6 of the approved number of protons on target
- DUNE will need to run for about 3 years to double the global event sample in the appearance channel
- By the time DUNE data dominates the global data set, the global data set will comprise about 1500 events with a corresponding statistical accuracy of 2.6%
- The hint for leptonic CP violation is currently at 1–2 sigma level
- T2K could reach 3 sigma rejection of leptonic CP conservation assuming the current best-fit value is close to the true value of delta

## Context

This paper extends the argument from Huber (2007) that near detectors provide fewer observables than unknowns when trying to separate beam flux from cross section. It contrasts the long-baseline approach with reactor experiments like Daya Bay (An 2016), which succeeded by measuring identical cross sections in near and far detectors. The paper calls for a dedicated experimental neutrino scattering program, referencing proposals by Adey (2013) and Bhadra (2014), to accompany the long-baseline oscillation program.
