# Effects of mutual excitations in the fusion of carbon isotopes

> Fusion data for $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C are analyzed by coupled-channels calculations that are based on the M3Y+repulsion, double-folding potential. The fusion is determined by ingoing-wave-boundary conditions (IWBC) that are imposed at the minimum of the pocket in the entrance channel potential. Quadrupole and octupole transitions to low-lying states in projectile and target are included in the calculations, as well as mutual excitations of these states. The effect of one-neutron transfer is also considered but the effect is small in the measured energy regime. It is shown that mutual excitations to high-lying states play a very important role in developing a comprehensive and consistent description of the measurements. Thus the shapes of the calculated cross sections for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C and $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C are in good agreement with the data. The fusion cross sections for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C determined by the IWBC are generally larger than the measured cross sections but they are consistent with the maxima of some of the observed peak cross sections. They are therefore expected to provide an upper limit for the extrapolation into the low-energy regime of interest to astrophysics.

# Effects of mutual excitations in the fusion of carbon isotopes

## Key claim

Mutual excitations to high-lying states play a very important role in developing a comprehensive and consistent description of fusion measurements for carbon isotopes, but the calculated fusion cross sections for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C are generally larger than the measured cross sections and are therefore expected to provide only an upper limit for the extrapolation into the low-energy regime of interest to astrophysics.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed using the M3Y+repulsion double-folding potential with ingoing-wave-boundary conditions imposed at the minimum of the pocket in the entrance channel potential. Quadrupole and octupole transitions to low-lying states in projectile and target were included, as well as mutual excitations of these states, and the effect of one-neutron transfer was also considered. The calculations were performed in the rotating frame approximation, and the parameters of the ion-ion potential (the $^{13}$C matter radius and the diffuseness associated with the repulsive term) were optimized by fitting to Trentalange's $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C fusion data using $\chi^2$ minimization with an adjustable overall normalization factor $S_c$.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to Trentalange's $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C fusion data gave $\chi^2/N = 1.0$ with parameters: $^{13}$C matter radius $R = 2.28$ fm, repulsion diffuseness $a_r = 0.31$ fm, and scaling factor $S_c = 0.843$.
- The rms matter radius of $^{13}$C from the best fit is 2.407 fm, compared to an estimated value of 2.378 fm from a single-particle model (a discrepancy of 1.2%).
- The Coulomb barrier height for all three carbon isotope systems is approximately $V_{CB} \approx 6$ MeV.
- The enhancement of fusion compared to the no-coupling limit is typically a factor of 2 at energies far below the Coulomb barrier.
- The systematic error of the absolute cross sections quoted in Trentalange's data is $\pm 15\%$.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter for $^{12}$C is $\beta_2 = 0.57(2)$ from the measured $0_1^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ transition strength.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter for $^{13}$C is $\beta_2 = 0.495$ (assuming an oblate shape), producing an intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_0 = -17.9$ fm$^2$.
- The one-neutron transfer from $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C to $^{12}$C+$^{14}$C has a Q-value of +3.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of carbon fusion reactions beyond earlier studies by Trentalange et al. (1980s) and Charvet et al. (1980s) for $^{13}$C+$^{13}$C, and Dayras et al. (1970s) and Notani et al. (2000s) for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C, by applying the M3Y+repulsion potential previously introduced by Misicu and Esbensen (2006) to explain fusion hindrance in heavier systems. The analysis supports the idea of a fusion hindrance and the existence of a shallow pocket in the entrance channel potential for light-ion systems, which is relevant for extrapolating $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion cross sections to the low energies needed for modeling type Ia supernovae and superbursts in accreting neutron stars. The results enable future work to use the calculated IWBC cross sections as an upper limit for astrophysical extrapolations, though the structure of high-lying states remains uncertain.
