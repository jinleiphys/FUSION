# Recent results from the UrQMD hybrid model for heavy ion collisions

> These proceedings present recent results from transport-hydrodynamics-hybrid models for heavy ion collisions at relativistic energies. The main focus is on the absorption of (anti-)protons in the hadronic afterburner stage of the reaction, di-lepton production at SPS and heavy quark dynamics.

## Key claim

The UrQMD hybrid model, which couples a hydrodynamic phase to a hadronic cascade, can reproduce experimental data from SPS to LHC energies, but the absorption of (anti-)protons in the hadronic afterburner reduces their yields by approximately 50% at realistic transition energy densities, and neglecting the back reaction introduces a systematic error of about 10%.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model v3.3 in hybrid mode, coupling an event-by-event fluctuating initial state from hadron and string dynamics to an ideal hydrodynamic evolution with different equations of state (hadron gas EoS, chiral EoS, bag model EoS). At a chosen transition energy density, the hydrodynamic cells were converted to particles via a Cooper-Frye prescription, and the subsequent decoupling stage was handled by the UrQMD hadronic cascade. The model was applied to study (anti-)proton absorption, Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations, dilepton production, and heavy quark dynamics in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Proton and anti-proton yields are reduced by approximately 50% for a transition energy density of epsilon_CF = 5 epsilon_0 in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The systematic error from neglecting the back reaction 5 pi -> p pbar is on the order of 10%.
- Di-muon spectra for In+In collisions at 160 AGeV from hybrid-model simulations show a slight overestimation of the yield around the rho peak compared to NA60 data.
- The bag model EoS clearly overshoots the HBT correlation data, while the hadron gas EoS and chiral EoS provide a reasonable description.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Petersen (2008) and the UrQMD transport framework of Bass (1998) and Bleicher (1999) to study multiple observables across SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The results on (anti-)proton absorption confirm findings by Steinheimer (2012) and Pan (2014), while the dilepton studies compare coarse-grained transport simulations by Endres (2014) with hybrid-model calculations using spectral functions from Rapp and Wambach (1999) and Eletsky. The heavy quark dynamics results are compared with parton cascade calculations by Uphoff (2012), suggesting that substantial local equilibrium is achieved in these collisions.
