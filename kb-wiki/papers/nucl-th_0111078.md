# Enhanced excitation of Giant Pairing Vibrations in heavy-ion reactions induced by weakly-bound projectiles

> The use of radioactive ion beams is shown to offer the possibility to study collective pairing states at high excitation energy, which are not usually accessible with stable projectiles because of large energy mismatch. In the case of two-neutron stripping reactions induced by 6He, we predict a population of the Giant Pairing Vibration in 208Pb or 116Sn with cross sections of the order of a millibarn, dominating over the mismatched transition to the ground state.

# Enhanced excitation of Giant Pairing Vibrations in heavy-ion reactions induced by weakly-bound projectiles

## Key claim

The use of weakly-bound radioactive ion beams such as 6He can enable the population of Giant Pairing Vibrations (GPV) in heavy targets via two-neutron stripping reactions, with cross sections of the order of a millibarn that dominate over the ground-state transition, *provided* the bombarding energy is chosen around 50% above the Coulomb barrier where the semi-classical matching conditions favor the high-lying states over the mismatched low-lying transitions.

## Method

The authors performed Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations of the pairing response for both normal (208Pb) and superfluid (116Sn) target nuclei, using a monopole pairing Hamiltonian with single-particle levels from a spherical harmonic oscillator basis including spin-orbit corrections. They constructed macroscopic two-neutron transfer form factors following the collective model for pair-transfer, where the form factor is proportional to the derivative of the ion-ion optical potential with respect to mass number, scaled by the pairing strength parameter βP from the RPA. Cross sections were then estimated using standard Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations with the code Ptolemy, using the Akyuz-Winther parameterization for the optical potential at bombarding energies corresponding to 50% above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb(6He,4He)210Pb at Ecm=41 MeV: total cross section to the GPV is 3.1 mb, compared to 1.8 mb for the ground-state transition
- For 116Sn(6He,4He)118Sn at Ecm=40 MeV: total cross section to the GPV is 2.4 mb, compared to 0.4 mb for the ground-state transition
- Q-value for 208Pb(6He,4He)210Pb ground-state transition: 8.148 MeV; for the GPV transition: -3.662 MeV
- Q-value for 116Sn(6He,4He)118Sn ground-state transition: 15.298 MeV; for the GPV transition: 5.402 MeV
- Q-value for 208Pb(14C,12C)210Pb ground-state transition: -4 MeV; for the GPV transition: -15.81 MeV
- Q-value for 116Sn(14C,12C)118Sn ground-state transition: 3.15 MeV; for the GPV transition: -6.746 MeV
- The GPV in 208Pb is predicted at approximately 12 MeV excitation energy (addition mode) and 16 MeV (removal mode)
- The GPV in 116Sn appears at high excitation energy with minor fragmentation compared to 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of Giant Pairing Vibrations by Broglia and Bortignon (predicted to have strong collective features) and addresses the experimental failure to observe them in reactions induced by protons or tritons as noted by von Oertzen, due to large energy mismatch. The paper contrasts the use of weakly-bound 6He projectiles with stable beams such as 14C, showing that the Q-value modification from weak binding enhances GPV population. This enables future experimental searches using radioactive ion beams with large-scale particle-gamma detection systems, building on the successful study of low-lying pairing vibrations around closed-shell nuclei via two-particle transfer reactions as established by Broglia and Mottelson.
