# Pseudospin symmetry in single particle resonant states

> The pseudospin symmetry is a relativistic dynamical symmetry connected with the small component of the Dirac spinor. The origin of pseudospin symmetry in single particle bound states in atomic nuclei has been revealed and studied extensively. By examining the zeros of Jost functions corresponding to the small components of Dirac wave functions and phase shifts of continuum states, we show that the pseudospin symmetry in single particle resonant states in nuclei is conserved when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign. The exact conservation and the breaking of pseudospin symmetry are illustrated for single particle resonances in spherical square-well and Woods-Saxon potentials.

# Pseudospin symmetry in single particle resonant states

## Key claim

The pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in single particle resonant states in nuclei is exactly conserved when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign (Σ(r) = 0) or when dΣ(r)/dr = 0, conditions that are never met in realistic finite nuclei, meaning the symmetry is always broken in practice. The authors demonstrate that in the PSS limit, not only the energies but also the widths of pseudospin doublet resonances are identical, contradicting earlier numerical expectations that centrifugal barrier differences would cause width splittings.

## Method

The authors examine the zeros of Jost functions corresponding to the small components of Dirac wave functions and the phase shifts of continuum states within a relativistic framework. They analytically continue the Jost functions into the complex momentum plane to treat bound and resonant states on the same footing, and illustrate the exact conservation and breaking of PSS using spherical square-well potentials (where the Jost function can be derived analytically) and Woods-Saxon potentials (solved numerically with the real stabilization method).

## Key numbers

- Square-well potential parameters: D = 650 MeV, R = 7 fm; C = 0 MeV for the PSS limit and C = -66 MeV for the broken PSS case
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for 208Pb: V₀ - S₀ = 650 MeV, V₀ + S₀ = -66 MeV, diffusivity a = 0.6 fm, R = 7 fm
- Pseudospin doublet studied: h₁₁/₂ (κ = -6) and j₁₃/₂ (κ' = 7) with pseudoorbital angular momentum \tilde{l} = 6
- Three pairs of resonant partners are shown in the complex energy plane for the square-well potential in the PSS limit (C = 0)

## Context

This work extends the rigorous understanding of pseudospin symmetry from bound states, established by Ginocchio (1997) who showed PSS is exact when Σ(r) = 0, and by Meng (1998, 1999) who identified the condition dΣ(r)/dr = 0, to single particle resonant states. Previous numerical studies of PSS in resonances by Guo (2005, 2006) and Zhang (2006, 2007) investigated splittings in 208Pb but lacked a rigorous justification. This paper provides that justification via Jost function analysis and enables future studies of symmetries in exotic nuclei where the Fermi surface approaches the particle continuum and resonant contributions become important.
