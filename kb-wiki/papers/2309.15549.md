# Dynamical mechanism of fusion hindrance in heavy ion collisions

> In the fusion process, the investigation of the reaction dynamics in the time evolution of the nuclear configuration is necessary. The neck parameter $ε$ which is one of the parameters representing the nuclear configuration in the two center shell model is important in fusion owing to the nucleons transferring through the neck. The time evolution of the neck has not been discussed in detail, but is crucial for fusion cross section in the assessment of new elements synthesis. The dynamical analysis for the fusion hindrance under the neck formation on the nuclear deformation space has been done. The fusion probability $P_\text{CN}$ considering the different denecking motion and the fusion hindrance are discussed. The calculations were performed using the dynamical model of nucleus-nucleus collisions based on the multidimensional Langevin equations.The formation of the neck bridge at the approaching stage is found to be crucial to the fusion hindrance. It is clarified that the inner barrier appears owing to the change in the degree of mass asymmetry $α$ with the relaxation of $ε$.The fusion hindrance occurs because the inner barrier is formed by the early neck formation. The role of the neck parameter $ε$ is critically important for the fusion dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that fusion hindrance in heavy ion collisions occurs because early neck formation between the colliding nuclei creates an inner barrier on the potential energy surface, but this mechanism depends on the specific relaxation mode of the neck parameter epsilon; the hindrance is not universal but rather conditional on the neck forming before the nuclei make contact.

## Method

The authors performed trajectory calculations using a multidimensional Langevin equation with three collective coordinates (distance between centers z, fragment deformation delta, and mass asymmetry alpha) plus the neck parameter epsilon, for the 48Ca + 208Pb system at center-of-mass energy 180.0 MeV. The potential energy surface was constructed from a two-center shell model with a time-dependent weighting function that interpolates between diabatic and adiabatic potentials, and the neck parameter epsilon was treated either as fixed (1.00 or 0.65) or with a time-dependent relaxation using a Fermi function with characteristic relaxation time t0 and width Delta_epsilon.

## Key numbers

- The system studied is 48Ca + 208Pb at E_c.m. = 180.0 MeV.
- For fixed epsilon = 1.00, the characteristic time for dynamical variation is 6.845 x 10^(-21) s; for fixed epsilon = 0.65, it is 9.873 x 10^(-21) s.
- The mass asymmetry alpha at the characteristic dynamical change point is 0.46 for fixed epsilon = 1.00 and 0.13 for fixed epsilon = 0.65.
- For the relaxation mode with t0 = 0 s ("contact relaxation"), the fusion-fission percentage is 0.57 percent; for t0 = 5.0 x 10^(-21) s ("delayed relaxation"), it is 14.23 percent.
- The relaxation width parameter Delta_epsilon used is 1.0 x 10^(-22) s.
- The friction model parameters are gamma_t^0 = 0.1 x 10^(-22) MeV s fm^(-2) and gamma_R^0 = 100 x 10^(-22) MeV s fm^(-2).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical treatment of fusion hindrance by focusing on the time evolution of the neck parameter epsilon, which had not been discussed in detail previously. It builds on the early macroscopic dynamical model by Swiatecki (1981) and the dinuclear system (DNS) model by Adamian and collaborators (1999, 2000), who pointed out the importance of neck behavior and showed that theoretical overestimation of fusion probability in heavier systems could be refined by proper treatment of mass parameters for the neck degree of freedom. The results enable more precise predictions for superheavy element synthesis by clarifying how the timing of neck formation relative to contact controls the competition between fusion and quasifission.
