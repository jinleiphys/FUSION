# The Extraordinary Glow

> In this contribution I discuss some recent progress in understanding the evolution of the pre-thermal quark-gluon matter, known as the glasma, during the early stage in heavy ion collisions, and the implication for early time photon and dilepton emissions.

## Key claim

The pre-thermal glasma stage in heavy ion collisions, if it is highly overpopulated with gluon occupation numbers of order \(1/\alpha_s\), may produce a dynamical Bose-Einstein condensate and generate significant photon and dilepton emissions that could explain the PHENIX excesses, though the author notes the estimates are "rough" and the study is "only a first step."

## Method

The author uses a kinetic theory framework with a Fokker-Planck equation derived from elastic gluon scatterings in the small-angle approximation to model the evolution of the overpopulated glasma. Scaling solutions for the soft and hard momentum scales are derived under both static-box and boost-invariant expanding conditions. The resulting time-dependent scales are then used to parametrically estimate photon yields (via Compton scattering \(qg\to q\gamma\)) and dilepton yields (via gluon annihilation from a condensate into a quark loop), which are compared to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial glasma distribution has occupation \(f_0 \sim 1/\alpha_s\) up to the saturation scale \(Q_s\) of a few GeV.
- The critical occupation for overpopulation (exceeding the equilibrium Bose-Einstein capacity) is \(f_0^c \approx 0.154\).
- For the photon fit, the best description of PHENIX data uses \(\lambda = 0.29\), \(\eta = 6.65\) (corresponding to anisotropy \(\delta = 0.144\)), and coefficient \(C_\gamma = 0.0234\ \text{fm}^2\).
- For the dilepton fit, the optimal transverse momentum width parameter is \(\mu = 0.2\ \text{GeV}\), and the normalization coefficient is \(C_{ll} \approx 3.5 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The parametric thermalization time in the static-box case is \(t_{\text{th}} \sim (1/Q_s) (1/\alpha_s)^{7/4}\).
- The scaling exponent for the local chemical potential approaching zero is \(\eta \approx 1\) (universal).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory studies of overpopulated glasma by Blaizot et al. (2011, 2013) and Huang et al. (2013), which predicted dynamical Bose-Einstein condensation from elastic scatterings alone. It applies these ideas to the phenomenological problem of the PHENIX excesses in direct photons (Adare et al., 2009) and low-to-intermediate mass dileptons (Adare et al., 2011), offering an alternative to late-stage hadronic explanations such as the "sticky-pion" picture of Gerald Brown. The results enable future tests of geometric scaling predictions for centrality dependence of dilepton spectra and motivate more quantitative studies of glasma electromagnetic emissions.
