# No Lambda oscillations

> We examine a recently published calculation which predicts an oscillatory behaviour for the decay of Lambdas produced together with a neutral kaon, and proposes a new expression for the wavelength of kaon strangeness oscillations. We modify the calculation by imposing the requirement that the interference of the K_L and K_S components of the kaon wave function occurs at a specific space-time point. With this requirement, the unusual results predicted vanish, and the conventional results are recovered.

# No Lambda oscillations

## Key claim

The authors argue that a recent calculation by Srivastava, Widom and Sassaroli (SWS, 1995) predicting oscillatory behavior in the decay of Lambdas produced together with neutral kaons is based on an error in principle: SWS calculate interference between wave function components at different space-time points. When the requirement that interference occurs at the same space-time point is imposed, the predicted Lambda oscillations vanish and the conventional kaon strangeness oscillation wavelength is recovered.

## Method

The authors re-derive the relation between proper times in the rest frames of the K_L, K_S, Lambda_L, and Lambda_S particles produced in the reaction pi- p -> Lambda K0. They impose the condition that the observation time in the overall center-of-mass frame is the same for both K_L and K_S components, using the mean velocity of the two kaon wave packets to define this common time. They then compute the joint probability distribution for detecting a Lambda and a K0, evaluating the phase difference between the two amplitude components using a derivative expansion in the K_L-K_S mass difference.

## Key numbers

* The K_L - K_S mass difference is delta m = 3.522 x 10^{-12} MeV.
* The wave number for kaon strangeness oscillations is k = m * delta m / p, where m is the kaon mass and p is the kaon momentum, recovering the conventional expression.
* The velocity difference between K_S and K_L wave packets is approximately 10^{-15}, ensuring they do not separate significantly before detection.
* The measurement by Camerini et al. (1966) gives delta m = (0.50 +/- 0.15) tau_S^{-1}, consistent with the regeneration value of delta m = 0.476 tau_S^{-1}.

## Context

This paper directly addresses the work of Srivastava, Widom and Sassaroli (1995), who claimed that the joint probability distribution for Lambda and K0 detection would show oscillations in both particles and that existing measurements of delta m from strangeness oscillations were in error by a factor C(s) of at least 2. The authors argue that experimental data from Gjesdal et al. (1974) and Camerini et al. (1966) support the conventional description rather than the SWS predictions. The work relates to broader discussions of quantum oscillations in two-body final states, including B-Bbar mixing discussed by Kayser (1995) and neutrino oscillations discussed by Grimus and Stockinger (1996) and Goldman (1996), though none of those papers directly address the same questions.
