# p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV in the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics transport approach

> The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model is employed for p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV and compared to recent experimental data from the LHC as well as to alternative models. We focus on the question of initial state dynamics, i.e. if the initial state might be approximated by a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions or should be considered as a coherent gluon field as predicted within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework. We find that the PHSD approach provides correlations between the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity and the number of participant nucleons close to results from the CGC and differs substantially from results calculated with independent Glauber initial conditions. However, a sizeable difference is found between the PHSD approach and CGC models with respect to the rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum. Accordingly, related measurements at LHC should allow to prove or disprove the presence of coherent colour fields in the initial phase of the collisions.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model produces correlations between charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity and the number of participant nucleons that are close to results from the color glass condensate (CGC) framework and differ substantially from independent Glauber initial conditions; however, a sizeable difference between PHSD and CGC models is found in the rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum, which could allow experimental discrimination of coherent colour fields in the initial state.

## Method

The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model, a covariant dynamical approach based on Kadanoff-Baym off-shell transport equations, was extended to LHC energies by implementing the PYTHIA 6.4 generator with the Innsbruck pp tune (390) for initial nucleon collisions. For p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the model was run both with and without additional Glauber-Gribov fluctuations of the nucleon-nucleon cross section, and results were compared to ALICE and ATLAS data as well as to CGC saturation model predictions from the rcBK approach.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity distributions for p-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV are compared to ALICE data; the laboratory system is shifted by y_cm = -0.465 relative to the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass.
- For the most central events (0-1% centrality), the number of charged particles at the maximum of the distribution is about 75.
- The transverse momentum spectrum in the measured range 0.5 < p_T < 10 GeV/c changes by 7 orders of magnitude; deviations from ALICE data of up to a factor of three are observed for p_T greater than about 1.5 GeV/c.
- The maximal energy density in a single p-Pb event at t = 0.002 fm/c is comparable to that in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energy, with a spatial volume of about 5 x 10^{-3} fm^3.
- For N_part greater than about 15, Gribov-Glauber distributions increasingly overshoot the standard Glauber result, reaching an order of magnitude difference for N_part greater than about 30.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD transport approach, previously tested from SPS to RHIC energies, to LHC energies for p-Pb collisions. It directly tests the proposal by Bzdak and Skokov (2013) that the correlation between multiplicity and participant number could distinguish CGC from wounded-nucleon model initial conditions, and the proposal by Bzdak, Bzdak, and Skokov (2013) that the rapidity dependence of average transverse momentum could discriminate CGC from hydrodynamic models. The study finds that the multiplicity-based test is inconclusive because energy-momentum conservation in PHSD suppresses the high-multiplicity tail that the wounded-nucleon model overestimates, but confirms that the average transverse momentum versus rapidity measurement should provide a clear distinction between CGC and PHSD/hydrodynamic approaches.
