# Towards a microscopic description of 12C+12C fusion at stellar energies

> I present a fully microscopic description of the 12C+12C fusion reaction at stellar energies. Utilizing the multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM), my model explicitly includes 12C+12C and alpha+20Ne reaction channels (with excited states). This approach provides a consistent, simultaneous, description of fusion, elastic scattering, and 24Mg spectroscopy. Results for 12C+12C elastic scattering show excellent agreement with experimental data, significantly improving the single-channel approximations. Spectroscopic analysis reveals that 24Mg states and resonances are highly mixed configurations, contradicting the concept of pure "molecular states." The calculated fusion S-factor is consistent with available experimental data and predicts both narrow and broad resonances near the Coulomb barrier. Main resonance widths originate primarily from the alpha+20Ne exit channels. The S-factor exhibits a decrease at low energies, providing a microscopic support for the hypothesis of fusion hindrance. This work is a first step towards a reliable theoretical extrapolation of the 12C+12C reaction to deep stellar burning temperatures. Future works should include the neutron and proton channels to provide a complete description of the 12C+12C fusion.

## Key claim

The multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculation of 12C+12C fusion, which explicitly includes alpha+20Ne reaction channels, provides a fully microscopic description that is consistent with experimental data and supports the hypothesis of fusion hindrance at low energies, but this conclusion is limited to the alpha channel and could be affected by the omitted proton and neutron channels.

## Method

The author performed a multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculation, equivalent to the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), using a nucleon-nucleon interaction (Volkov V2 with Bartlett and Heisenberg terms) as the only input. The model explicitly includes 12C+12C and alpha+20Ne reaction channels (with excited states of both 12C and 20Ne described in the shell model), and solves the Schrodinger equation for the 24-nucleon system to simultaneously obtain fusion cross sections, elastic scattering, and 24Mg spectroscopy. The fusion cross section is computed from the scattering matrix elements for transfer to the alpha+20Ne channels, with no phenomenological imaginary potential.

## Key numbers

* The 12C+12C system involves 225^2 = 50625 Slater determinants for the carbon-carbon partition; for J=0+ there are 150 channels in the C+C partition and 88 channels in the alpha+Ne partition.
* The Q-value for the alpha+20Ne channel is 4.63 MeV; the calculation uses oscillator parameter b=1.6 fm and generator coordinates R_n from 1.5 fm to 10.5 fm with 1 fm step.
* The multichannel model with b=0 and m=0.70 provides a 24Mg binding energy of 11.9 MeV with respect to the C+C threshold (experimental value is 13.9 MeV).
* For the lowest 0+ state of 24Mg, the C(0+_1)+C(0+_1) channel represents 12.0% of the wave function (amplitude N_gamma) and 5.6% spectroscopic factor; the alpha+Ne(0+_1) channel contributes 18.9% and 13.9% respectively.
* Five resonances are found in the fusion cross section: at E_cm = 0.30 MeV (4+), 1.14 MeV (0+), 2.6 MeV (0+), 2.9 MeV (2+), and 5.1 MeV (4+).
* The total widths of these resonances are dominated by alpha+Ne channels; for the 0+ resonance at 2.6 MeV, the C+C partial width is 3.9x10^{-9} MeV while the alpha+Ne(0+) width is 0.33 MeV and alpha+Ne(2+) width is 0.58 MeV.
* The S-factor uses coefficient g=0.46 MeV^{-1} to compensate the energy dependence of the cross section.

## Context

This work extends previous single-channel microscopic calculations of the 12C+12C system by Baye and Descouvemont (1982, 1984) and Suzuki and Hecht (1982), which did not include fusion or multiple reaction channels. It contradicts the concept of pure "molecular states" found in those single-channel calculations, showing instead that 24Mg states are highly mixed configurations. The approach enables a first step toward reliable theoretical extrapolation of the 12C+12C reaction to stellar burning temperatures (around 2.4 MeV at T=10^9 K), and future work should include the neutron and proton channels (23Na+p and 23Mg+n) to provide a complete description.
