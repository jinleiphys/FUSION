# Fluctuation properties of strength functions associated with giant resonances

> We performed fluctuation analysis by means of the local scaling dimension for the strength function of the isoscalar (IS) and the isovector (IV) giant quadrupole resonances (GQR) in $^{40}$Ca, where the strength functions are obtained by the shell model calculation within up to the 2p2h configurations. It is found that at small energy scale, fluctuation of the strength function almost obeys the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) random matrix theory limit. On the other hand, we found a deviation from the GOE limit at the intermediate energy scale about 1.7MeV for the IS and at 0.9MeV for the IV. The results imply that different types of fluctuations coexist at different energy scales. Detailed analysis strongly suggests that GOE fluctuation at small energy scale is due to the complicated nature of 2p2h states and that fluctuation at the intermediate energy scale is associated with the spreading width of the Tamm-Dancoff 1p1h states.

## Key claim

The strength functions of the isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole resonances in $^{40}$Ca, obtained from shell model calculations including up to 2p2h configurations, exhibit Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) random matrix theory fluctuation at small energy scales but deviate from the GOE limit at intermediate energy scales of about 1.7 MeV for the isoscalar and 0.9 MeV for the isovector case, implying that different types of fluctuations coexist at different energy scales.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations within the second Tamm-Dancoff approximation including 1p1h and 2p2h excitations for $^{40}$Ca, using a Woods-Saxon potential with Coulomb interaction and a Landau-Migdal-type residual interaction. They analyzed the resulting strength functions using the local scaling dimension (LSD), a measure that characterizes how the partition function of binned strengths scales with bin width, and compared the results to the GOE random matrix theory limit. To identify the origin of deviations, they rescaled the interaction strengths $V_{12}$ (between 1p1h and 2p2h states) and $V_{22}$ (among 2p2h states) by factors $\lambda_{12}$ and $\lambda_{22}$ and examined the dependence of the LSD on these parameters.

## Key numbers

- Average level spacing $d \simeq 12$ keV for the energy range 20–30 MeV in $^{40}$Ca.
- Characteristic energy scale of deviation from GOE for isoscalar GQR: $\epsilon_{\rm f} \simeq 140d \simeq 1.7$ MeV.
- Characteristic energy scale of deviation from GOE for isovector GQR: $\epsilon_{\rm f} \simeq 70d \simeq 0.9$ MeV.
- Spreading width of 1p1h states (direct method): $\gamma_{12}^{\rm dm} = 1.5$ MeV.
- Spreading width of 2p2h states (direct method): $\gamma_{22}^{\rm dm} = 5.2$ MeV.
- Fermi golden rule estimates: $\gamma_{12}^{\rm FG} \simeq 5.3$ MeV, $\gamma_{22}^{\rm FG} \simeq 6.0$ MeV.
- Root mean square matrix elements: $\overline{\bra{1p1h}V_{12}\ket{2p2h}} = 98$ keV, $\overline{\bra{2p2h}V_{22}\ket{2p2h}} = 104$ keV.
- Mean energy and total width: ISGQR 23 MeV and 4.0 MeV; IVGQR 31 MeV and 7.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis using the local scaling dimension method applied to a schematic doorway damping model (Aiba, 2002) to a realistic shell model calculation of giant resonances in $^{40}$Ca. The results confirm that the spreading width of the 1p1h Tamm-Dancoff doorway states determines the intermediate energy scale where strength function fluctuations deviate from GOE behavior, consistent with the doorway damping mechanism. The analysis enables future studies of fine structure in giant resonances and provides a quantitative tool to distinguish between different damping mechanisms in nuclear spectroscopy.
