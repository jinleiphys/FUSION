# Three-body scattering in Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics

> The relativistic three-nucleon problem is formulated by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. Two-body interactions are included that preserve the Poincaré symmetry, lead to the same invariant two-body S-matrix as the corresponding non-relativistic problem, and result in a three-body S-matrix satisfying cluster properties. The resulting Faddeev equations are solved by direct integration, without partial waves for both elastic and breakup reactions at laboratory energies up to 2 Gev.

# Three-body scattering in Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Poincaré-invariant Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering can be solved by direct integration (without partial waves) at laboratory energies up to 2 GeV, using a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction that lacks spin complexities. The relativistic and non-relativistic calculations are compared using interactions fit to the same two-body data, so all differences arise solely from how two-body dynamics enters the three-body problem.

## Method

A dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group is constructed on the three-nucleon Hilbert space by adding interactions to the mass Casimir operator of the non-interacting irreducible representation. Two-body interactions are chosen to preserve Poincaré symmetry and yield the same invariant two-body S-matrix as the corresponding non-relativistic problem. The resulting Faddeev equations for the mass operator are solved by direct integration (avoiding partial-wave expansion) for both elastic and breakup reactions.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energies up to **2 GeV** are reached with converged calculations using a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction.
- For elastic scattering, the largest difference between relativistic and non-relativistic calculations occurs at angles above **60 degrees** at **1 GeV** beam energy.
- The first-order term of the multiple scattering series is inadequate even at **2 GeV** for large-angle elastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-body formalism of Polyzou (previous work) by demonstrating numerical feasibility of solving Poincaré-invariant Faddeev equations without partial waves at GeV-scale energies. It contrasts with lower-energy partial-wave calculations using realistic interactions (Glöckle et al., earlier work). The method enables future modeling of few-nucleon reactions in the few-GeV range where baryon resonances and meson production channels become relevant, though realistic spin-dependent interactions remain to be incorporated.
