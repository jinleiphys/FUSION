# Size fluctuations of the initial source and the event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show that the event-by-event fluctuations of the transverse size of the initial source, which follow directly from the Glauber treatment of the earliest stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, cause, after hydrodynamic evolution, fluctuations of the transverse flow velocity at hadronic freeze-out. This in turn leads to event-by-event fluctuations of the average transverse momentum, p_T. Simulations with GLISSANDO for the Glauber phase, followed by a realistic hydrodynamic evolution and statistical hadronization carried out with THERMINATOR, lead to agreement with the RHIC data. In particular, the magnitude of the effect, its centrality dependence, and the weak dependence on the incident energy are properly reproduced. Our results show that bulk of the observed event-by-event p_T fluctuations may be explained by the fluctuations of the size of the initial source.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the bulk of observed event-by-event fluctuations of the average transverse momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be explained by fluctuations in the initial transverse size of the source, which arise naturally from the Glauber Monte Carlo treatment of the earliest collision stage and are then amplified by hydrodynamic evolution into fluctuations of transverse flow velocity at freezeout.

## Method

The authors used GLISSANDO for Glauber Monte Carlo simulations of the initial source, generating events with fluctuating nucleon positions from Woods-Saxon distributions. They then ran perfect boost-invariant hydrodynamics with Gaussian transverse initial conditions matched to the Glauber spatial rms radii, followed by statistical hadronization with THERMINATOR using the Cooper-Frye formalism. Rather than performing full event-by-event hydrodynamics, they rescaled the width parameters a and b by the scaled standard deviation of the size fluctuations (e.g., 4.4% for 20-30% centrality) to produce squeezed and stretched initial conditions, then computed the dynamical fluctuation measure σ_dyn from the resulting difference in average transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Scaled standard deviation of the size parameter ⟨r⟩ is about 2.5-3.5% for central collisions, growing toward peripheral collisions approximately as 1/√N_w
- For centrality class 20-30% (N_w ≃ 165), the scaled standard deviation of size fluctuations is 4.4% for the wounded nucleon model
- The mixed model overshoots the STAR data by about 20%
- Freezeout temperature used: T_f = 145 MeV
- Initial temperature T_i values range from 500 MeV (0-5% centrality) down to 279 MeV (60-70% centrality) at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Maximum expansion velocities are about 10% larger for the squeezed case compared to the stretched case for c=20-30%
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used: 32 mb (SPS), 42 mb (RHIC), 63 mb (LHC)

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations from the elliptic component (studied by Aguiar (2000), Miller (2003), Bhalerao (2005), and others) to the size component, showing that size fluctuations provide a new mechanism for generating transverse-momentum fluctuations. The result bears similarity to a formula derived by Ollitrault (1991) relating dynamical fluctuations to entropy density fluctuations. The analysis indicates that with realistic hydrodynamics previously used to describe soft RHIC data, there may be little room for other dynamical sources of fluctuations such as mini-jets or cluster formation at freezeout, though global momentum conservation, resonance decays, and correlations from elementary NN collisions in the corona remain as background mechanisms.
