# Sensitivity of D meson azimuthal anisotropies to system size and nuclear structure

> Recent experimental data at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) confirmed that the soft sector azimuthal anisotropies in central XeXe collisions are sensitive to deformations in the wave function of the Xenon nucleus. Additionally, the CMS experiment found that D meson flow is slightly suppressed compared to other particle species when considering quark number scaling in small systems of pPb compared to PbPb. In this talk we used the D and B Mesons Modular (DAB-MOD) code coupled to Trento+v-USPhydro to calculate the $R_{AA}$ and $v_n$'s of D mesons using different energy loss models and Langevin techniques. Comparing D mesons $R_{AA}$ and $v_n$ in PbPb to XeXe collisions it is found that in central collisions D meson azimuthal anisotropies are sensitive to details in the nuclear structure wave function of Xenon. Additionally, we find that in mid-central collisions the smaller system size of XeXe suppresses D meson flow.

# Sensitivity of D meson azimuthal anisotropies to system size and nuclear structure

## Key claim
D meson azimuthal anisotropies in XeXe collisions are sensitive to the deformed nuclear structure of Xenon in the 0-10% centrality class, with the effect enhanced at intermediate transverse momentum (p_T ~ 3-5 GeV), while in mid-central collisions (30-50%) the smaller system size of XeXe suppresses D meson flow relative to PbPb beyond what eccentricity scaling alone would predict.

## Method
The authors coupled the D and B Mesons Modular (DAB-MOD) code to Trento initial conditions and v-USPhydro relativistic viscous hydrodynamics to compute D meson nuclear modification factors (R_AA) and azimuthal anisotropy coefficients (v_n) on an event-by-event basis. They compared PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV to XeXe collisions at 5.44 TeV, using both spherical and prolate (deformed) Wood-Saxon parameters for the Xenon nucleus, while keeping all other model parameters fixed to those previously validated against PbPb data.

## Key numbers
- D meson v_2{2} in 0-10% central XeXe collisions shows a clear enhancement for a prolate Xenon nucleus compared to a spherical one, with the effect most pronounced at intermediate p_T = 2-5 GeV
- In 30-50% mid-central collisions, D meson v_2{2} in XeXe is suppressed by over 20% compared to PbPb, which is significantly larger than the few percent suppression expected from eccentricity scaling alone
- The ratio v_2^{Xe}{2}/v_2^{Pb}{2} in central collisions for a spherical Xenon nucleus significantly underpredicts experimental data from CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS, while a deformed nucleus brings predictions closer to experiment
- Central XeXe collisions have N_part approximately 200-250 compared to PbPb's N_part approximately 310-415

## Context
This work extends the finding from Giacalone (2017) that soft-sector azimuthal anisotropies in central XeXe collisions are sensitive to Xenon nuclear deformation, and the CMS observation (Sirunyan 2018) that D meson flow is suppressed in small pPb systems compared to PbPb. The authors make the first event-by-event azimuthal anisotropy predictions for D mesons in XeXe collisions at 5.44 TeV, going beyond previous XeXe predictions that used non-fluctuating backgrounds with Bjorken expansion (Zigic 2018, Djordjevic 2018). The results suggest that experimental comparisons of D meson anisotropies in deformed heavy-ion collisions (such as Uranium or the upcoming isobar run) would be valuable, and that the heavy flavor sector can provide new information on energy loss in small systems.
