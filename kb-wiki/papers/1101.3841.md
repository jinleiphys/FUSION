# Holographic Flavored Quark-Gluon Plasmas

> Holography provides a novel method to study the physics of Quark Gluon Plasmas, complementary to the ordinary field theory and lattice approaches. In this context, we analyze the informations that can be obtained for strongly coupled Plasmas containing dynamical flavors, also in the presence of a finite baryon chemical potential. In particular, we discuss the jet quenching and the hydrodynamic transport coefficients.

## Key claim

Holography provides a complementary method to study strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasmas containing dynamical flavors, also at finite baryon chemical potential, but the results apply to a theory that is not QCD but one of its relatives, valid only in the planar limit and at very large 't Hooft coupling.

## Method

The authors construct a backreacted D3-D7 brane system in the AdS black hole background, using a "smearing technique" that homogeneously distributes many flavor D7-branes in their transverse space to reduce the system to ordinary differential equations. They obtain a perturbative analytic solution for massless flavors up to order \(\epsilon_h^2\) and \(\hat\delta^2\), where \(\epsilon_h \sim \lambda_h N_f/N_c\) and \(\hat\delta \sim \mu/(\sqrt{\lambda_h} T)\). From this gravity solution, they compute thermodynamic quantities, the jet quenching parameter via a partially light-like Wilson loop, and hydrodynamic transport coefficients up to second order in the derivative expansion.

## Key numbers

- Entropy density: \(s = \frac12 \pi^2 N_c^2 T^3 [1 + \frac12 \epsilon_h (1 + \hat\delta^2) + \frac{7}{24} \epsilon_h^2 (1 + \hat\delta^2)]\)
- Energy density: \(\varepsilon = \frac38 \pi^2 N_c^2 T^4 [1 + \frac12 \epsilon_h (1 + 2\hat\delta^2) + \frac13 \epsilon_h^2 (1 + \frac74 \hat\delta^2)]\)
- Speed of sound: \(c_s^2 = \frac13 [1 - \frac16 \epsilon_h^2]\)
- Jet quenching parameter: \(\hat q = \frac{\pi^{3/2} \Gamma(3/4)}{\Gamma(5/4)} \sqrt{\lambda_h} T^3 [1 + \frac{2+\pi}{8} \epsilon_h (1 + 0.8675 \hat\delta^2)]\)
- Shear viscosity over entropy density: \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\)
- Naive extrapolation to RHIC parameters (\(N_c=N_f=3\), \(T=300\) MeV, \(\lambda_h = 6\pi\), \(\mu=10\) MeV) gives \(\hat q \sim 5.3\) (GeV)^2/fm, compared to \(\hat q \sim 4.5\) (GeV)^2/fm for the unflavored theory

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of strongly coupled plasmas beyond the quenched approximation of Karch and Katz (2002) by using the smearing technique introduced in non-critical string models and developed by Casero, Nunez, and Paredes (2006). The results enable estimates of transport coefficients for RHIC and LHC phenomenology, complementing lattice methods which struggle with real-time physics and the sign problem at finite chemical potential. The authors note that their computed jet quenching enhancement from dynamical flavors challenges the common phenomenological practice of discarding flavor effects as subleading, as done in the work of Muller (2007).
