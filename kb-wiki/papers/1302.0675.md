# Two-flavour lattice QCD correlation functions in the deconfinement transition region

> We report on a lattice QCD calculation with two dynamical flavors of the isovector vector correlator in the high-temperature phase. We analyze the correlator in terms of the associated spectral function by performing a fit for the difference of the thermal and vacuum spectral functions, using also an exact sum rule that constrains this difference. Additonally we carry out a direct fit for the thermal spectral function, and obtain good agreement between the two analyses for frequencies below the two-pion threshold. Under the assumption that the spectral function is smooth in that region, we give an estimate of the electrical conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of the isovector vector correlator using dynamical (unquenched) light quarks in the high-temperature phase, and under the assumption that the spectral function is smooth in the low-frequency region, it estimates the electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma at T approximately 250 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed Euclidean vector current correlators on two ensembles of dynamical gauge configurations with two light, mass-degenerate O(a)-improved Wilson quark flavors: a zero-temperature ensemble on a 64^3 x 128 lattice and a finite-temperature ensemble on a 64^3 x 16 lattice at T approximately 250 MeV (T/T_c approximately 1.2). They analyzed the correlator by fitting the difference of the thermal and vacuum spectral functions, using an exact sum rule to constrain this difference, and separately performed a direct fit to the thermal spectral function using a weak-coupling-inspired Ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the finite-temperature ensemble: T = 253(4) MeV, corresponding to T/T_c approximately 1.2
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0486(4)(5) fm
- Pion mass: m_pi = 270 MeV
- Quark number susceptibility: chi_s/T^2 = 0.871(1)
- Difference of thermal and reconstructed correlator at the midpoint: Delta G(tau = beta/2, T)/T^3 = 0.291(55)
- Estimated electrical conductivity: sigma/(C_em T) = 0.40(12), where C_em = 5/9

## Context

This work extends previous quenched (quenched) lattice QCD studies of the vector spectral function and electrical conductivity, such as those by Gupta (2003), Aarts (2007), Ding (2010), Francis (2011), and Ding (2013), which used staggered or Wilson-Clover fermions without dynamical quarks. The results are compatible with the recent quenched results and represent the first dynamical (unquenched) calculation in this context, enabling future comparisons between quenched and dynamical calculations of transport properties in the quark-gluon plasma.
