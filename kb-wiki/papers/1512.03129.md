# Compositeness of Hadron Resonances and Quasi-Bound States

> We discuss the composite nature of hadrons appearing near the s-wave two-hadron threshold. Generalizing the Weinberg's weak-binding relation for stable bound states, we show that the compositeness of near-threshold resonances and quasi-bound states can be determined by observable quantities.

## Key claim

The compositeness of near-threshold resonances and quasi-bound states can be determined from observable quantities by generalizing Weinberg's weak-binding relation for stable bound states, but the compositeness becomes complex for unstable states and cannot be directly interpreted as a probability.

## Method

The author generalizes the Weinberg weak-binding relation for stable bound states to near-threshold resonances using effective range expansion of the s-wave two-body scattering amplitude, and to quasi-bound states using effective field theory. For resonances, the compositeness is derived from the pole position of the scattering amplitude, while for quasi-bound states, the relation connects the scattering length, eigenenergy, and compositeness with correction terms that become negligible when the state is sufficiently close to threshold.

## Key numbers

- For the Λc(2595) resonance in πΣc scattering: excitation energy E = 0.67 MeV, decay width Γ = 2.59 MeV, compositeness X = 0.608i, effective range re = -19.5 fm
- For the Λ(1405) resonance: K̅N scattering length a0 = 1.39 - i0.85 fm, eigenenergy EQB = -10 - i26 MeV, K̅N compositeness XK̅N = 1.2 + i0.1, with reinterpreted probability X̃K̅N = 1.0 and uncertainty U = 0.5

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's weak-binding relation (Weinberg, 1965) for stable bound states to unstable resonances and quasi-bound states, building on prior studies of compositeness by Baru (2003), Hyodo (2011, 2013, 2014), Sekihara (2014), and Guo (2015). The formalism enables model-independent determination of hadron structure for near-threshold exotic hadrons such as the XYZ states and Λ(1405), and provides criteria to distinguish composite-dominated states from other configurations using observable scattering parameters.
