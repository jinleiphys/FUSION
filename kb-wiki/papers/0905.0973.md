# Dynamically generated resonances from the vector octet-baryon octet interaction

> We study the interaction of vector mesons with the octet of stable baryons in the framework of the local hidden gauge formalism using a coupled channels unitary approach. We examine the scattering amplitudes and their poles, which can be associated to known J^P=1/2^-,3/2^- baryon resonances, in some cases, or give predictions in other ones. The formalism employed produces doublets of degenerate J^P=1/2^-,3/2^- states, a pattern which is observed experimentally in several cases. The findings of this work should also be useful to guide present experimental programs searching for new resonances, in particular in the strange sector where the current information is very poor.

# Dynamically generated resonances from the vector octet-baryon octet interaction

## Key claim

The interaction of vector mesons with the octet of stable baryons, treated within the local hidden gauge formalism with a coupled channels unitary approach, dynamically generates baryon resonances that can be associated with known $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-$ states; however, the formalism produces degenerate doublets of $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-$ states as a consequence of neglecting the three-momentum of the particles versus their mass, a pattern that is only approximately observed in the experimental spectrum.

## Method

The authors construct the vector meson-baryon interaction using the hidden gauge formalism, deriving the transition potential from vector exchange diagrams under the approximation that the three-momentum of external particles is negligible compared to their masses. They solve the coupled channels Bethe-Salpeter equation in on-shell factorization, using dimensional regularization with a scale of 630 MeV and a subtraction constant of -2, and search for poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitudes. For channels involving $\rho$ and $K^*$ mesons, the loop function is convoluted with the mass distributions of these broad vector mesons to account for their finite widths.

## Key numbers

- The $(I,S)=(1/2,0)$ sector produces a resonance at 1696 MeV (determined from real-axis amplitudes) with a width of 92 MeV, coupling most strongly to $\rho N$ ($|g|=3.2$); a second resonance appears at $1977 + i53$ MeV coupling most strongly to $K^*\Sigma$ ($|g|=3.9$)
- The $(I,S)=(0,-1)$ sector yields three poles: $1784 + i4$ MeV (coupling strongest to $\bar K^* N$ with $|g|=3.3$), $1906 + i70$ MeV (coupling strongest to $\rho\Sigma$ with $|g|=3.2$), and $2158 + i13$ MeV (coupling strongest to $K^*\Xi$ with $|g|=3.2$)
- The $(I,S)=(1,-1)$ sector produces a resonance at 1830 MeV (real-axis determination) with width 42 MeV coupling strongest to $\bar K^* N$ ($|g|=2.1$), and a second at 1987 MeV with width 240 MeV coupling strongest to $\rho\Sigma$ ($|g|=2.6$)
- The $(I,S)=(1/2,-2)$ sector yields poles at $2039 + i67$ MeV (coupling strongest to $\rho\Xi$ with $|g|=2.5$) and $2082 + i31$ MeV (coupling strongest to $\bar K^*\Sigma$ with $|g|=1.9$)
- The $\rho$ meson width used for convolution is 149.4 MeV; the $K^*$ meson width is 50.5 MeV
- The regularization scale $\mu$ is 630 MeV with subtraction constant $a_l(\mu) = -2$

## Context

This work extends the hidden gauge formalism approach previously applied to vector meson-vector meson interactions (which generated the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_0(1370)$ resonances) and to vector meson-decuplet baryon interactions (which produced degenerate $N^*$ and $\Delta$ states around 1800-1900 MeV). It contrasts with the SU(6) symmetry approach, which does not produce the spin degeneracy predicted here. The results enable experimental searches for missing spin partners of known resonances, particularly in the strange sector where the Jefferson Lab program is actively searching for $\Xi$ resonances. The approach differs from the Juelich model, which found a $\rho N$ pole around 1700 MeV that moved when the $N^*(1520)$ was introduced as a genuine resonance.
