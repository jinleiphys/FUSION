# Solving the heat-flow problem with transient relativistic fluid dynamics

> Israel-Stewart theory is a causal, stable formulation of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. This theory has been shown to give a decent description of the dynamical behavior of a relativistic fluid in cases where shear stress becomes important. In principle, it should also be applicable to situations where heat flow becomes important. However, it has been shown that there are cases where Israel-Stewart theory cannot reproduce phenomena associated with heat flow. In this paper, we derive a relativistic dissipative fluid-dynamical theory from kinetic theory which provides a good description of all dissipative phenomena, including heat flow. We explicitly demonstrate this by comparing this theory with numerical solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Israel-Stewart theory fails to describe heat flow in certain regimes because its truncation of the single-particle distribution function expansion in terms of non-orthogonal Lorentz tensors is uncontrolled, and that a resummed transient relativistic fluid-dynamical theory (RTRFD) which keeps additional irreducible moments as dynamical variables resolves this discrepancy, at least when the Knudsen number is not too large.

## Method

The authors derive fluid-dynamical equations from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a massless, classical single-component gas with elastic two-body collisions and constant cross section, using an expansion of the distribution function in a complete orthonormal basis of irreducible tensors and orthogonal polynomials in energy. They diagonalize the linearized collision operator to identify the slowest microscopic eigenmodes, then construct closed equations of motion for the particle diffusion current and shear-stress tensor by keeping the first two eigenmodes (corresponding to irreducible moments \(n^\mu\), \(\rho_2^\mu\), \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\), and \(\rho_1^{\mu\nu}\)) as independent dynamical variables, rather than replacing the faster eigenmodes by their Navier-Stokes values as done in Israel-Stewart theory. The resulting hyperbolic equations are compared with numerical solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation computed using the BAMPS code.

## Key numbers

- 37 moments were found sufficient for the purposes of this work to compute transport coefficients in the RTRFD framework.
- The QGP created at RHIC and LHC has space-time extension \(\sim 10\) fm and gradients \(|\partial_\mu \varepsilon|/\varepsilon \sim 1/\text{fm}\).
- The Knudsen number \(\mathrm{Kn} = \lambda_\mathrm{mfp}/L\) and inverse Reynolds number \(\mathrm{R}^{-1} \sim |n^\mu|/n \sim |\pi^{\mu\nu}|/P_0\) are used as the power-counting parameters for truncation.

## Context

This work extends the systematic derivation of relativistic fluid dynamics from kinetic theory introduced by Denicol, Niemi, Molnar, and Rischke (DNMR, 2012), which resums contributions from all moments of the distribution function rather than truncating at 14 moments as in Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979). The paper resolves discrepancies previously observed by some of the same authors (Bouras et al., 2009) between Israel-Stewart solutions and Boltzmann equation solutions for heat-flow problems. The resulting hyperbolic formulation enables causal, stable descriptions of relativistic dissipative fluids with heat flow, relevant for modeling the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
