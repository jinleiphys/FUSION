# Decay Process Sigma to 2 Pions and Chiral Phase Transition

> The decay process sigma to 2 pions at high temperature and density and its relation with chiral phase transition are discussed in the framework of the NJL model. The decay rate for the process sigma to 2 pions is calculated in the whole temperature and density region. The contribution of the final state pion statistics to the decay rate is discussed. The maximum decay rate at different chemical potential is computed. Finally, we investigate the relation between the starting point of the decay process and the critical point of the first-order chiral phase transition.

# Decay Process Sigma to 2 Pions and Chiral Phase Transition

## Key claim
The decay process sigma to 2 pions near the chiral phase transition point produces a strong enhancement of final state pions, but the enhancement remains finite when the correct statistical factor (1+2f_B) for the final state pion statistics is used, rather than becoming infinite as with the previously used factor (1+f_B)^2. The threshold point for the decay does not always coincide with the critical point of the first-order chiral phase transition when explicit chiral symmetry breaking is included.

## Method
The authors calculated the decay rate for sigma to 2 pions in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using the mean field approximation for the quark propagator and the random phase approximation (RPA) for the meson propagator. They evaluated the triangle diagram for the sigma-pi-pi vertex at finite temperature T and baryonic chemical potential mu, including the Bose-Einstein statistical factor for the final state pions. The decay rate was computed across the full T-mu plane for both the chiral limit and the case of explicit chiral symmetry breaking with a finite current quark mass.

## Key numbers
- In the chiral limit at mu=0, the critical temperature for the second-order chiral phase transition is T_c = 190 MeV.
- In the vacuum (T=0, mu=0) with explicit chiral symmetry breaking, the decay rate is approximately 0.09 GeV.
- The NJL model parameters in the chiral limit are coupling constant G = 5.02 GeV^{-2} and quark momentum cutoff Lambda = 0.653 GeV, determined from quark condensate density <u-bar u> = (-0.25 GeV)^3 and pion decay constant f_pi = 0.093 GeV.
- With explicit chiral symmetry breaking, the parameters are G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}, Lambda = 0.6535 GeV, and current quark mass m_0 = 0.005 GeV, determined from the vacuum pion mass m_pi = 0.134 GeV.
- The first-order chiral phase transition exists for chemical potentials above mu = 0.327 GeV.
- Only about 1% of sigma mesons decay before the phase transition around the endpoint E.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by Zhuang (2001) on the sigma decay process, correcting the treatment of final state pion statistics. The authors use the NJL model, originally developed by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961), which is standard for studying chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and density as reviewed by Vogl (1992) and Quack (1995). The results are relevant for relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the sigma meson becomes light near the chiral phase transition point as discussed by Stephanov (1999). The work enables experimental searches for chiral phase transition signatures through enhanced pion production, with the maximum decay rate being approximately independent of chemical potential, suggesting observability at different collision energies.
