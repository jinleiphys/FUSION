# Studies on a complete experiment for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction

> The complete experiment problem for photoproduction of single pseudoscalar mesons is reviewed briefly. If this problem is investigated in the context of a truncated partial wave analysis, the chance emerges to obtain a unique multipole solution measuring less polarization observables than demanded by the classical solution of the problem that is formulated in terms of spin amplitudes.

## Key claim

If the complete experiment problem for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction is investigated in the context of a truncated partial wave analysis (TPWA), it becomes possible to obtain a unique multipole solution by measuring fewer polarization observables than the 8 demanded by the classical solution formulated in terms of spin amplitudes.

## Method

The author reviews the formal inversion of bilinear Hermitean forms relating the 16 polarization observables to bilinear products of helicity amplitudes, following Chiang and Tabakin. For a TPWA, the multipole expansion is truncated at a finite angular momentum ℓmax, and the resulting angular parametrization of observables is expressed in terms of energy-dependent complex multipoles. Using a Bonn-Gatchina multipole solution as numerical input, an ambiguity diagram following Omelaenko is constructed for ℓmax=1 to identify ambiguous solutions for the Group S observables, and the corresponding beam-target observables {E, F, G, H} are plotted to test distinguishability.

## Key numbers

* For ℓmax=1, the occurring multipoles are {E0+, E1+, M1+, M1-}.
* For every finite order in ℓmax ≥ 1, 4ℓmax complex multipoles appear in the expansion.
* The beam-target observables {E, F, G, H} are plotted at a laboratory photon energy Eγ = 253 MeV for all four ambiguous solutions.

## Context

This work extends the classical complete experiment formulation of Chiang and Tabakin by addressing the problem that an undetermined overall phase in helicity amplitudes extracted from a complete experiment denies access to partial waves. The TPWA approach revisits earlier work by Omelaenko and Grushin on ambiguities in the Group S observables, and the author states that first studies suggest multipoles can be obtained using even fewer than 8 observables. The reformulation of the TPWA first worked out by Omelaenko is planned to be revisited in a forthcoming publication.
