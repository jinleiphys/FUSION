# Reply to K. Amos et al. (nucl-th/0401055)

> An expression for the spin-orbit interaction coupling between different levels, which was shown to be aberrant more than thirty years ago persists in the literature without clear indication of what is used. It leads to expressions quite simpler than they should be. After an attempt to warn the community of the nuclear physicists on this strange situation (nucl-th/0312038), the authors of the publication in which the "aberrant" interaction is described and used, try to justify their work (nucl-th/0401055), by a very strange "symmetrization" of something already symmetric. They claim also that their method allows to solve some problem related to the Pauli principle and give some references, among which a book which reports the solution of such problem almost forty years ago, with a very small effect. An examination of their own results shows that their optimism is not completely justified. Nevertheless, any user of ECIS, sensitive to their arguments, is requested to ask their opinion to these five coauthors before publishing.

# Reply to K. Amos et al. (nucl-th/0401055)

## Key claim
The "aberrant" spin-orbit interaction expression used by Amos et al. for nucleon-nucleus inelastic scattering is mathematically incorrect and leads to oversimplified results; their attempted "symmetrization" of an already symmetric operator does not resolve the error. The authors' claim that their method solves Pauli principle problems is not supported by their own numerical results, which show only partial and inconsistent improvements.

## Method
Raynal derives the correct "normal" deformed spin-orbit interaction from the full Thomas term by starting from the two-body spin-orbit interaction and reducing it to a one-body form through a series of angular momentum and operator identities. He then compares this correct expression (Eq. E6) with the simplified form used by Amos et al., showing that the latter omits essential derivative and angular momentum coupling terms. He also examines the Pauli principle treatment by reviewing prior calculations on 16O(γ,n) and 16O(γ,p) reactions and analyzing the numerical results presented in Table I of Amos et al.

## Key numbers
- For 16O(γ,n) calculations, the Pauli principle effect showed no noticeable effect up to 20 MeV (4.5 MeV above threshold) but a shift of the maximum around 22 MeV.
- In Table I of Amos et al., for the first case the energy was shifted by 33% of what was needed (in the correct direction) but the width increased by 20% instead of 148%.
- For the second case, the energy shifted by only 4% of what was needed and the width was unchanged.
- For the third case, the energy shifted by 82% of what was needed (in the correct direction) but the width increased by 15% instead of being divided by 4.33.
- For 12C scattering lengths, Normand (1975) found a decrease of 8% to 21% for the scattering length and 4% to 7% for the effective range across 14 calculations with 4 different interactions.
- For 40Ca, the 1s1/2 state had no effect (less than 0.3%) but the 2s1/2 state gave 21% to 42% effect on scattering length and 15% to 31% on effective range.

## Context
This reply extends Raynal's earlier warning (nucl-th/0312038) about the persistent use of an incorrect spin-orbit interaction expression in the nuclear physics literature, first identified as aberrant over thirty years ago. The work directly contradicts the claims of Amos et al. (nucl-th/0401055) regarding both the spin-orbit interaction and Pauli principle effects. The correct derivation follows from the two-body spin-orbit interaction formalism developed in earlier works by Raynal and others (Raynal 1967, 1968, 1969), and the Pauli principle discussion references prior calculations on 16O by Raynal, Melkanoff, and Sawada (1967) and the thesis work of Normand (1975). This enables future users of the ECIS code to properly implement the correct spin-orbit interaction and to critically evaluate claims about Pauli principle effects in coupled-channels calculations.
