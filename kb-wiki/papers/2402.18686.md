# Observational Probes of the Neutron Star Equation of State with Hyperons, Bosonic Dark Matter, and Quark Matter

> Context. The presence of dark matter in neutron stars is of growing interest due to its potential impact on the structure and observable properties of these objects. Among the various candidates, the hypothetical sexaquark has emerged as a promising bosonic dark matter particle, potentially forming under extreme conditions in neutron star cores. Aims. We investigate whether a hybrid neutron star model that includes hyperons, bosonic dark matter (in the form of sexaquarks), and deconfined quark matter can satisfy all current observational constraints. We particularly focus on identifying the range of sexaquark masses consistent with mass-radius measurements and the tidal deformability limit. Methods. We used the DD2Y-T model for the hadronic phase, which includes hyperons, and a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for the deconfined quark phase. The phase transition was modeled as a smooth crossover using the replacement interpolation construction method. Sexaquark-baryon interactions were introduced via an effective mass shift representing repulsion. We incorporated the full set of current observational data, including NICER measurements of PSRs J0437-4715 and newly published J0614-3329 data, and performed a Bayesian analysis to constrain the sexaquark mass. Results. Our results show that the presence of the sexaquark softens the equation of state, enabling the hybrid model to satisfy both the radius and tidal deformability constraints around the canonical 1.4 M_\odot neutron stars. We find that hybrid EOSs with a sexaquark mass around 1900 MeV are in agreement with all available constraints, including those from HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0952-0607, which represent the lowest and highest mass neutron stars observed to date. The Bayesian analysis favors a sexaquark mass range of 1885-1935 MeV, supporting the potential relevance of this exotic particle in neutron star interiors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hybrid neutron star model simultaneously incorporating hyperons, bosonic dark matter in the form of sexaquarks, and deconfined quark matter can satisfy all current observational constraints, but only if the sexaquark mass lies in a narrow range around 1900 MeV, with the Bayesian analysis favoring 1885–1935 MeV. The hedge is that this agreement depends on assuming the minimal value of the sexaquark-baryon interaction slope parameter, which keeps the sexaquark weakly interacting while still producing a significant mass shift at high densities.

## Method

The authors constructed a unified equation of state using the DD2Y-T relativistic density functional model for the hadronic phase (including all J=1/2 octet baryons: neutron, proton, Λ, Σ, and Ξ hyperons) and a nonlocal Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model for the deconfined quark phase. The hadron-quark phase transition was modeled as a smooth crossover using the replacement interpolation construction method with a polynomial pressure interpolation and a fixed offset parameter Δ_P = -0.02. Sexaquark-baryon interactions were introduced via a linear density-dependent effective mass shift with a slope parameter set to its minimal value, and the sexaquark was treated as a Bose-Einstein condensate at zero temperature. The authors performed a Bayesian analysis incorporating NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0437-4715 and PSR J0614-3329, the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817, and the extreme mass measurements of HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0952-0607.

## Key numbers

- Sexaquark mass range favored by Bayesian analysis: 1885–1935 MeV, with best agreement around 1900 MeV
- PSR J0437-4715: mass M = 1.418 ± 0.037 M⊙, radius R = 11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63} km (NICER measurement)
- PSR J0614-3329: radius R = 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86} km for mass M = 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} M⊙ (NICER measurement)
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Λ_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Sexaquark mass threshold for absolute stability: m_S < 1878 MeV (two protons plus two electrons decay channel)
- Upper bound on sexaquark mass from experimental exclusion: 2054–2223.7 MeV ruled out (Takahashi 2001)
- Quark model parameter ranges from prior Bayesian analysis: 0.10 < η_V < 0.12 and 0.70 < η_D < 0.74

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Shahrbaf 2022) which established the lower mass limit for the sexaquark (m_S = 1885 MeV) by ensuring it does not appear at sub-saturation densities, and now determines the upper limit using observational constraints. The study addresses the "hyperon puzzle" (the problem that hyperons soften the equation of state too much to support 2 M⊙ neutron stars) using the DD2Y-T model which successfully resolves this issue (Stone 2019). The results support the hypothesis of hadron-quark continuity (Kojo 2021) and the idea that a smooth crossover phase transition, rather than a sharp first-order transition, is consistent with similar radii observed for 1.4 M⊙ and 2.1 M⊙ neutron stars. The work enables future studies to incorporate sexaquark dark matter into neutron star models with a well-constrained mass range, and suggests that post-merger gravitational wave signals (Hensh 2024) could distinguish between crossover and first-order phase transitions in such hybrid stars.
