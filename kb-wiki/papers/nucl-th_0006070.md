# The one-pion-exchange three-nucleon force and the $A_y$ puzzle

> We consider a new three-nucleon force generated by the exchange of one pion in the presence of a 2N correlation. The underlying irreducible diagram has been recently suggested by the authors as a possible candidate to explain the puzzle of the vector analyzing powers $A_y$ and $iT_{11}$ for nucleon-deuteron scattering. Herein, we have calculated the elastic neutron-deuteron differential cross section, $A_y$, $iT_{11}$, $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$, and $T_{22}$ below break-up threshold by accurately solving the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations with realistic interactions. We have also studied how $A_y$ evolves below 30 MeV. The results indicate that this new 3NF diagram provides one possible additional contribution, with the correct spin-isospin structure, for the explanation of the origin of this puzzle.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a new three-nucleon force (3NF) generated by one-pion exchange in the presence of a two-nucleon correlation provides one possible additional contribution, with the correct spin-isospin structure, toward explaining the vector analyzing power puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering, but note that the force contains an adjustable parameter \(c(Z)\) because existing two-nucleon \(t\)-matrices cannot guarantee reliable extrapolation to the far-off-shell energies required.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering below the break-up threshold using a high-rank separable representation (BBEST) of the Bonn B potential as the two-nucleon input. They incorporated the new one-pion-exchange three-nucleon force into the one-dimensional Lovelace form of the AGS equation and calculated the differential cross section and the analyzing powers \(A_y\), \(iT_{11}\), \(T_{20}\), \(T_{21}\), and \(T_{22}\). The force includes a single adjustable parameter \(c(Z)\) that multiplies the far-off-shell two-nucleon \(t\)-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was set to \(f_{\pi NN}^2/(4\pi) = 0.078\).
- For the Bonn B potential, the pion-nucleon cut-off was \(\Lambda_\pi = 1.7\) GeV.
- The subtracted \(t\)-matrix in the 3NF involves energies down to approximately \(Z \approx -160\) MeV.
- Calculations were performed at 3 MeV laboratory energy for neutron-deuteron scattering, with results shown for \(A_y\) and other observables at that energy.
- The authors studied how \(A_y\) evolves below 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for the origin of the \(A_y\) puzzle, which was identified at the Few-Body XI conference in 1986 (Koike, 1986) and confirmed by later Faddeev calculations with realistic two-nucleon potentials from the Graz group and others (Gloeckle, 1996; Witala, 1994; Witala, 1998). The existing \(2\pi\)-exchange three-nucleon forces (Tucson-Melbourne, Brazil, and Urbana-Argonne types) provided too small an effect on \(A_y\) and not always in the right direction. The new diagram was derived from a formalism for explicit pion dynamics in the three-nucleon system (Canton, 1998; Canton, Schadow, 2000) and is related to earlier work on such diagrams by Brueckner, Pask, and Yang, which had been discarded due to a cancellation effect observed by Yang (1986) and Coon (1986) and discussed from chiral effective Lagrangians by van Kolck (1994). The authors argue this cancellation is incomplete, generating a residual three-nucleon force. This work enables future calculations that could test whether the force resolves the puzzle across a range of energies and with different two-nucleon potentials.
