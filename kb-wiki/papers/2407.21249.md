# A framework for semi-universality: Semi-universality of 3-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates

> Quantum circuits with symmetry-respecting gates have attracted broad interest in quantum information science. While recent work has developed a theory for circuits with Abelian symmetries, revealing important distinctions between Abelian and non-Abelian cases, a comprehensive framework for non-Abelian symmetries has been lacking. In this work, we develop novel techniques and a powerful framework that is particularly useful for understanding circuits with non-Abelian symmetries. Using this framework we settle an open question on quantum circuits with SU(d) symmetry. We show that 3-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates are semi-universal, i.e., generate all SU(d)-invariant unitaries, up to certain constraints on the relative phases between sectors with inequivalent representation of symmetry. Furthermore, we prove that these gates achieve full universality when supplemented with 3 ancilla qudits. Interestingly, we find that studying circuits with 3-qudit gates is also useful for a better understanding of circuits with 2-qudit gates. In particular, we establish that even though 2-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates are not themselves semi-universal, they become universal with at most 11 ancilla qudits. Additionally, we investigate the statistical properties of circuits composed of random SU(d)-invariant gates. Our findings reveal that while circuits with 2-qudit gates do not form a 2-design for the Haar measure over SU(d)-invariant unitaries, circuits with 3-qudit gates generate a t-design, with t that is quadratic in the number of qudits.

## Key claim

The paper proves that 3-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates are semi-universal for d ≥ 3, meaning they generate all SU(d)-invariant unitaries up to constraints on relative phases between sectors with inequivalent irreducible representations, settling an open question about the minimum locality needed to achieve semi-universality for non-Abelian symmetries.

## Method

The authors develop a general framework for analyzing semi-universality in quantum circuits with arbitrary symmetries, then apply it specifically to SU(d) symmetry using Schur-Weyl duality and the representation theory of the symmetric group. They prove a necessary and sufficient condition for semi-universality (Lemma 1) requiring subsystem universality in all charge sectors and pairwise independence between sectors, then verify these conditions for 3-qudit gates using induction starting from n=3 and n=4 qudits as base cases.

## Key numbers

* For d ≥ 3, 2-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates are not semi-universal, while 3-qudit gates are semi-universal
* With at most 8 ancilla qudits, semi-universality can be achieved using only 2-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates when d > 2
* With at most 11 ancilla qudits, full universality can be achieved using 2-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates when d > 2
* With 3 ancilla qudits, 3-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates achieve full universality
* For n > d, random circuits with 3-qudit gates generate a t-design with t approximately n²/2
* The asymptotic difference in Lie group dimension between k-qudit and 3-qudit generated groups is approximately k^(d-1) / (d!(d-1)!) for n ≥ k ≫ d ≥ 2

## Context

This work extends the theory of symmetric quantum circuits from Abelian symmetries (Marvian 2024) to non-Abelian symmetries, specifically addressing the open question of whether 3-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates achieve semi-universality. Prior work by Marvian (2022) showed that 2-qudit SU(d)-invariant gates are not semi-universal for d > 2, while Zheng (2023) argued that 4-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries are semi-universal using Young-Jucys-Murphy elements. The results enable characterization of the group generated by k-qudit SU(d)-invariant unitaries and provide a simpler understanding of computational universality for 2-qudit gates with ancilla qudits, building on work by Marin (2007) on Lie algebras generated by transpositions.
