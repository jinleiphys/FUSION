# Extending Israel-Stewart theory: Causal bulk viscosity at large gradients

> We present a class of relativistic fluid models for cold and dense matter with bulk viscosity, whose equilibrium equation of state is polytropic. These models reduce to Israel-Stewart theory for small values of the viscous stress $Π$. However, when $Π$ becomes comparable to the equilibrium pressure $P$, the evolution equations "adjust" to prevent the onset of far-from-equilibrium pathologies that would otherwise plague Israel-Stewart. Specifically, the equations of motion remain symmetric hyperbolic and causal at all times along any continuously differentiable flow, and across the whole thermodynamic state space. This means that, no matter how fast the fluid expands or contracts, the hydrodynamic equations are always well-behaved (away from singularities). The second law of thermodynamics is enforced exactly. Near equilibrium, these models can accommodate an arbitrarily complicated dependence of the bulk viscosity coefficient $ζ$ on both density and temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents a class of relativistic bulk-viscous fluid models that reduce to Israel-Stewart theory for small viscous stress Pi, but remain symmetric hyperbolic and causal for arbitrarily large Pi, provided the total pressure P+Pi stays positive and below the energy density. The key hedge is that the equations remain well-behaved only "away from singularities" and along continuously differentiable flows, and the parameters must fall within a specific triangular region in the Gamma-a plane.

## Method

The author constructs a polytropic fluid model with four fields (four-velocity, specific entropy, baryon density, and bulk viscous stress Pi) and four constant parameters (mass m, polytropic constant K, adiabatic index Gamma, viscosity parameter a). The constitutive relations for pressure, energy density, relaxation time, and bulk viscosity are defined such that the energy density contains a non-equilibrium term PQ(Pi/P) whose functional form is forced by thermodynamic consistency. The equations of motion consist of the relativistic Euler equation, a second-law entropy evolution equation, baryon continuity, and an Israel-Stewart-type relaxation equation for Pi, which are then recast into a symmetric hyperbolic form using dimensionless variables.

* The derivative d(P+Pi)/dt at the point where P+Pi=0 equals P/tau, which is positive since tau is positive by construction.

## Context

This work extends Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979) by constructing models that avoid the causality violations that occur when Pi becomes large and negative, which are known to arise in heavy-ion collision simulations (Plumberg, 2022) and in viscous cosmology near singularities (Maartens, 1996). The models are designed to be safe for numerical implementation in neutron star merger simulations, heavy-ion collisions, and cosmological contexts, and they can accommodate an arbitrarily complicated dependence of the bulk viscosity coefficient zeta on density and temperature near equilibrium. The approach builds on earlier work by Gavassino (2023) on far-from-equilibrium bulk viscosity and on the universality class classification of relativistic fluids.
