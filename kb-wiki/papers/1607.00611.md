# Excitation functions of parameters extracted from three-source (net-)proton rapidity distributions in Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions over an energy range from AGS to RHIC

> Experimental results of the rapidity spectra of protons and net-protons (protons minus antiprotons) emitted in gold-gold (Au-Au) and lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions, measured by a few collaborations at the alternating gradient synchrotron (AGS), super proton synchrotron (SPS), and relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC), are described by a three-source distribution. The values of the distribution width $σ_C$ and contribution ratio (relative contribution) $k_C$ of the central rapidity region, and the distribution width $σ_F$ and rapidity shift $Δy$ of the forward/backward rapidity regions, are then obtained. The excitation function of $σ_C$ increases generally with increase of the center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. The excitation function of $σ_F$ shows a saturation at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV. The excitation function of $k_C$ shows a minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV and a saturation at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 17$ GeV. The excitation function of $Δy$ increase monotonously with $\ln \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ in the considered energy range.

# Excitation functions of parameters extracted from three-source (net-)proton rapidity distributions in Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions over an energy range from AGS to RHIC

## Key claim

The excitation function of the central-region fraction parameter $k_C$ extracted from a three-source fit to (net-)proton rapidity distributions shows a minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV and a saturation at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 17$ GeV, which the authors interpret as possible signatures of changes in the interacting system's state, though they caution that the three-source model does not incorporate system evolution or the equation of state, making a direct connection to the "softest point" of the EoS difficult.

## Method

The authors fitted a three-Gaussian distribution function to experimental rapidity spectra of protons and net-protons (protons minus antiprotons) from Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions measured by the E895, E802/E877/E917, NA49, and BRAHMS collaborations at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. The fit yields four main parameters: the width $\sigma_C$ and fraction $k_C$ of the central rapidity region, and the width $\sigma_F$ and rapidity shift $\Delta y$ of the forward/backward rapidity regions. Parameter values were determined by least-squares minimization, and their excitation functions (dependence on $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$) were studied over the range 2.4 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma_C$ increases generally with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$; for 0-5% central Au-Au collisions, $\sigma_C$ rises from $0.583\pm0.008$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV to $2.255\pm0.072$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV (protons)
- $\sigma_F$ saturates at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV; for 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions at 8.8 GeV, $\sigma_F = 0.596\pm0.016$ (protons)
- $k_C$ shows a minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV, decreasing from $0.98$ at 2.1 GeV to $0.55$ at 8.8 GeV, then saturating to about $0.70$ at above 17 GeV
- $\Delta y$ increases linearly with $\ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}})$; the fitted relation for central collisions is $\Delta y = (0.080\pm0.021) + (0.470\pm0.067)\ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}})$ with $\chi^2$/dof = 6.862
- For 0-5% central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, net-proton parameters: $\sigma_C = 2.402\pm0.068$, $\sigma_F = 0.727\pm0.041$, $k_C = 0.552\pm0.010$, $\Delta y = 2.580\pm0.078$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-source analyses of charged-particle rapidity distributions (Gao et al., 2016) to (net-)protons specifically, and confirms their earlier observation of a minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV from squared speed-of-sound analysis of negative pion widths (Liu et al., 2016) and a minimum at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV from string tension analysis of transverse momentum distributions (Sun et al., 2016). The results are consistent with other studies locating a minimum in the 4-9 GeV range using Landau hydrodynamic models and ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics hybrid approaches (Steinheimer et al., 2014; Petersen et al., 2014). The excitation functions of $\sigma_F$ and $k_C$ showing saturation at higher energies are interpreted as evidence for nuclear limiting fragmentation. The authors note that the three-source model does not incorporate the equation of state, so the minimums cannot be directly identified with the softest point of the EoS without further analysis of many other observables.
