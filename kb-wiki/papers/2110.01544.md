# DREENA-A framework as a QGP tomography tool

> We present a fully optimised framework DREENA-A based on a state-of-the-art energy loss model. The framework can include any, in principle arbitrary, temperature profile within the dynamical energy loss formalism. Thus, 'DREENA' stands for Dynamical Radiative and Elastic ENergy loss Approach, while 'A' stands for Adaptive. DREENA-A does not use fitting parameters within the energy loss model, allowing it to fully exploit differences in temperature profiles which are the only input in the framework. The framework applies to light and heavy flavor observables, different collision energies, and large and smaller systems. This, together with the ability to systematically compare data and predictions within the same formalism and parameter set, makes DREENA-A a unique multipurpose QGP tomography tool.

## Key claim
DREENA-A is a fully optimized QGP tomography framework that can accept any arbitrary spatio-temporal temperature profile as input, without using fitting parameters within the energy loss model, allowing systematic comparison of data and predictions across light and heavy flavor observables at different collision energies and system sizes.

## Method
The framework implements the dynamical energy loss formalism, which includes both radiative and collisional energy losses calculated within the same theoretical framework, with temperature as a natural variable. For each parton trajectory defined by its origin coordinates (x₀, y₀) and direction angle φ, the algorithm numerically integrates the energy loss expressions along the path, using the temperature profile T(x₀ + τ cos φ, y₀ + τ sin φ, τ) as input. The final hadron suppression R_AA and elliptic flow v₂ are obtained by averaging over trajectories using equidistant sampling of production points and direction angles, with tabulation and interpolation of computationally expensive functions for optimization.

## Key numbers
- Integration step size of 0.1 fm is sufficiently small for most temperature profiles
- Integration is terminated when medium temperature drops below T_c = 155 MeV
- For convergence of the Poisson expansion in Eq. (probability), n=5 is sufficient for quark jets and n=7 for gluon jets
- Equidistant sampling of ~10,000 trajectories converges within 1% of the estimated proper value for both R_AA and v₂
- The equidistant sampling method reduced execution time by more than two orders of magnitude compared to Monte Carlo sampling
- For v₂ computation, ~10⁶ trajectories are needed with Monte Carlo sampling to reduce relative deviation below 1%
- Charm quark mass M = 1.2 GeV, bottom quark mass M = 4.75 GeV
- Magnetic-to-electric screening ratio μ_M/μ_E = 0.5

## Context
This work extends the DREENA-C framework (constant temperature, Zigic et al., 2019) and DREENA-B framework (Bjorken expansion, Zigic et al., 2020) by removing approximations of medium evolution and allowing arbitrary temperature profiles. The dynamical energy loss formalism was previously shown by Djordjevic (2006-2015) to provide a reliable tomography tool with properties not simultaneously present in other models such as BDMPS, BZ, ASW, GLV, and AMY. DREENA-A enables systematic comparison of high-p⊥ data with predictions from different hydrodynamic simulations, complementing low-p⊥ constraints on QGP properties.
