# Fluctuating flow angles and anisotropic flow measurements

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the initial density distributions of the fireballs created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions lead to event-by-event fluctuations of the final anisotropic flow angles, and density inhomogeneities in the initial state cause these flow angles to vary with the transverse momentum of the emitted particles. It is shown that these effects lead to characteristically different transverse momentum dependencies for anisotropic flow coefficients extracted from different experimental methods. These differences can be used to experimentally constrain flow angle fluctuations in the final state of heavy-ion collisions which, in turn, are sensitive to the initial state density fluctuations and the shear viscosity of the expanding fireball medium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that event-by-event fluctuations of the anisotropic flow angles, which depend on transverse momentum, cause characteristically different transverse momentum dependencies for anisotropic flow coefficients extracted from different experimental methods, and these differences can be used to experimentally constrain flow angle fluctuations that are sensitive to initial state density fluctuations and shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors performed 22,000 viscous hydrodynamic simulations per centrality bin of 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC using the VISH2+1 code, with 11,000 events each using MC-Glauber initial conditions evolved with specific shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and MC-KLN initial conditions evolved with η/s=0.2. They computed single-particle distributions from the hydrodynamic output on the freeze-out surface using the Cooper-Frye prescription, including all resonance decays, and compared four different differential flow measures: the rms flow v_n[2](pT), the two-particle cumulant flow v_n{2}(pT), the event-plane flow v_n{EP}(pT), and the average magnitude ⟨v_n(pT)⟩.

## Key numbers

- For central (0-5% centrality) Pb+Pb collisions, the ratio v_{2,3}[2](pT)/⟨v_{2,3}⟩(pT) was found to be constant at 2/√π ≈ 1.13 for both pions and protons, independent of transverse momentum
- For protons with transverse momenta below about 1 GeV, flow angle fluctuations cause significant suppression of v_n{2}(pT) and v_n{EP}(pT), in some cases even leading to negative elliptic flow values
- The variance of the cosine of flow angle fluctuations is minimal around pT=1 GeV for pions and around pT=1.5-2 GeV for protons, except for the dipolar flow angle which flips by π in that momentum region
- For peripheral collisions at 60-70% centrality, the flow angle fluctuation effects for protons gradually disappear compared to central collisions
- Resonance decays dilute the sensitivity of the proposed observables to flow angle fluctuations by about 50% for protons

## Context

This work extends the study of flow angle fluctuations initiated by Gardim et al. (2012), who pointed out that fluctuating flow angles cause slight breaking of factorization in two-particle angular correlations. The paper builds on earlier work by Qiu et al. (2012) that generated the hydrodynamic event samples used here, and relates to studies of v_n fluctuations by Voloshin (2008) and Heinz (2013). The results enable experimental separation of flow magnitude fluctuations from flow angle fluctuations, providing new constraints on initial state density fluctuations and the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
