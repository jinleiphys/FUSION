# Proton and deuteron distributions as signatures for collective particle dynamics and event shape geometries at the RHIC

> We present predictions for the formation of (anti)nuclear bound states in nucleus-nucleus reactions at RHIC energies. he phase space coalescence method is used in combination with RQMD-v2.4 transport calculations to demonstrate the relevance of particle production as well as the longitudinal and transverse flow components. The formation of deuterons follows an approximate scaling law proportional to the relative freeze-out densities of nucleons and produced secondaries. For antideuterons, an additional suppression appears that is proportional to the number of nucleons, pointing toward multiple rescattering and absorption prior to freeze-out.

## Key claim

Deuteron and antideuteron production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies can be predicted using the RQMD-v2.4 transport model combined with a phase-space coalescence afterburner, but the predictions depend strongly on the presence of longitudinal and transverse flow components; antideuteron yields show an additional suppression from nuclear absorption that is not present for deuterons.

## Method

The authors used the RQMD-v2.4 semiclassical transport model, which includes color strings, hadronic resonances, and rope formation, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 100 A·GeV per beam. They then applied a phase-space coalescence afterburner that projects the two-body phase-space density from the transport calculation onto bound-state wave functions in Wigner space to compute deuteron and antideuteron yields. To isolate the effect of collective flow, they compared the full calculation to a randomized version where freeze-out correlations of positions and momenta were removed by hand.

## Key numbers

- In central Au+Au collisions at 100 A·GeV per beam (impact parameters 0 ≤ b ≤ 3 fm), the average transverse momenta of deuterons and antideuterons are approximately a factor of two higher than those of protons and antiprotons.
- Randomization of freeze-out correlations suppresses the total number of clusters at midrapidity by orders of magnitude and reduces average transverse momenta at midrapidity by approximately 30%.
- The effective nuclear volume in the centrality dependence analysis differs by factors of 3–5 between different reaction centralities.
- The total number of pions predicted at midrapidity varies by factors ≈2 between parton cascade and RQMD-type calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence studies at lower beam energies (e.g., Nagle, Panitkin) and addresses the large model uncertainties in RHIC predictions, where different transport models give pion yields differing by factors ≈2. The paper suggests that cluster analyses, combined with HBT-type two-particle correlation analyses, can constrain collective flow, freeze-out densities, and event shape geometries, and it points to future studies of elliptic flow (Olitreau, Sorge2) and directed flow (PRL, NA49) as more sensitive probes of early and late event shapes.
