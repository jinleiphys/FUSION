# Deformed Boson Scheme including Conventional q-Deformation in Time-Dependent Variational Method. III

> Basic idea presented in Parts (I) and (II) for the deformed boson scheme is applied to the case of the su(2,1)-algebra for describing many-body systems consisting of three kinds of boson operators. A possible form of the coherent state for the su(2,1)-algebra is generalized and the deformed su(2,1)-algebra is presented. As an application, the time-evolution of the statistically mixed state in a certain boson system, which has been already investigated by the present authors, is reinvestigated.

## Key claim

The paper extends the deformed boson scheme, previously developed for one and two kinds of boson operators in Parts (I) and (II), to the su(2,1)-algebra for systems with three kinds of boson operators, but the deformation is performed only for a specific choice of the coherent state that is suitable for describing the time-evolution of a statistically mixed state, rather than for the most general possible deformation.

## Method

The authors construct a deformed coherent state for the su(2,1)-algebra by applying function-dependent deformations to the three boson operators (a, b1, b2) via a factor Omega(N_a, N_b1, N_b2) = f(N_a) g1(N_b1) g2(N_b2)^{-1}. They then define deformed generators T_plus, T_minus, S_plus, S_minus from the transformed boson operators and derive the corresponding deformed commutation relations, obtaining explicit forms for the q-deformed quantities [2T_0]_q, [2S_0]_q, and [R_plus]_q. The time-dependent variational method is applied using the deformed coherent state as a trial state, and the formalism is used to reinvestigate the time-evolution of a statistically mixed state in a boson system interacting with an external harmonic oscillator, which was previously studied in Ref. (B) (Kuriyama et al., 2000).

## Key numbers

- The expectation value of the operator K for the coherent state |c^0> is given by k = |y|^2/2 + 1, where y is a canonical variable.
- The classical Casimir operator Gamma_{ab1b2} evaluates to k^2 - (3/2)k + 3/4, matching the quantum form Gamma = K^2 - (3/2)K.
- The expectation values of the number operators for the deformed state |c> are N_a = |x_1|^2, N_b1 = |x_1|^2 + |x_2|^2, and N_b2 = |y|^2 - |x_2|^2, where x_1, x_2, and y are boson-type canonical variables.
- The normalization constant for the deformed state is Gamma = Gamma_gamma * sum_{l=0}^{infinity} (|delta|^2)^l / (l! * g_2(l)^2).

## Context

This work extends the deformed boson scheme from Parts (I) and (II) (Kuriyama et al., 2000) to the su(2,1)-algebra, which was previously formulated in terms of three boson operators in Ref. (A) (Kuriyama et al., 1999). The paper specifically reformulates the time-evolution of a statistically mixed state for a boson system interacting with an external harmonic oscillator, originally investigated in Ref. (B) (Kuriyama et al., 2000), within the new deformed framework. The authors note that the su(2,1)_q-algebra has not been investigated by anyone else, and their approach enables the study of q-deformed algebras for systems with three boson species, with potential applications to many-body boson systems.
