# Effective Theory for Color-Flavor Locking in high Density QCD

> We describe the low energy excitations of the diquark condensates in the color-flavor locked phase of QCD with three massless flavors, at high baryon densities, in terms of a non-linear effective lagrangian. Such a lagrangian is formally seen to correspond to the lagrangian for the hidden gauge symmetry description of the effective low energy chiral lagrangian, the role of the hidden symmetry being played by color. In particular, this agrees with the conjecture that the light degrees of freedom of the two phases are in correspondence to each other (complementarity). The discussion includes consideration of the breaking of Lorentz invariance in presence of matter density.

## Key claim

The low-energy effective Lagrangian for the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD with three massless flavors at high baryon density is formally identical to the hidden gauge symmetry description of the chiral Lagrangian for the low-density chirality-breaking phase, with the role of the hidden symmetry played by the color gauge group. This identification holds despite the breaking of Lorentz invariance in the presence of matter density, provided one rescales spatial coordinates by the appropriate Goldstone boson velocity.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-linear effective Lagrangian for the 17 Goldstone bosons arising from the symmetry breaking pattern \(SU(3)_c \otimes SU(3)_L \otimes SU(3)_R \otimes U(1) \to SU(3)_{c+L+R}\) in the CFL phase, using coset fields \(X\) and \(Y\) transforming under color and flavor, plus a \(U(1)\) field for baryon number. They then gauged the \(SU(3)_c\) color symmetry by introducing covariant derivatives, and in the limit where gluon masses are large compared to the gap, eliminated the gluon fields via their equations of motion to obtain a Lagrangian for the field \(\Sigma = Y^\dagger X\) that matches the standard chiral Lagrangian. The analysis was extended to include the breaking of Lorentz invariance by treating time and spatial derivatives with different coefficients, and the gauging of \(U(1)_{\rm em}\) was performed to study the photon-gluon mixing.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap for the CFL phase is estimated to lie between 10 and 100 MeV.
- The gluon mass in the unitary gauge is given by \(m_g^2 = \alpha g_s^2 F^2 / 4\), where \(F\) is a decay constant and \(\alpha\) a parameter from the effective Lagrangian.
- The mixing angle between the photon and the color gluon field associated with the generator \(Q_{SU(3)_c} = \text{diag}(-2/3, +1/3, +1/3)\) is \(\tan\psi = \sqrt{8/3} \, e / g_s\).
- The three types of Goldstone bosons (from \(\Pi_X\), \(\Pi_Y\), and \(\phi\)) have different velocities: \(v_X^2 = \alpha_S F_S^2 / (\alpha_T F_T^2)\), \(v_Y^2 = F_S^2 / F_T^2\), and \(v_\phi^2 = f_S^2 / f_T^2\), all expected to be of order 1.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of color-flavor locking by Wilczek (1998) and the complementarity conjecture between the CFL phase and the chirally broken phase. It provides an explicit effective field theory framework that formalizes the correspondence, showing that the massive gluons in the CFL phase map onto the octet of vector mesons in the hidden gauge symmetry description of the chiral Lagrangian (BESS model). The construction enables further study of the low-energy excitations in dense QCD, including the dibaryon \(H\) state proposed by Jaffe (1977) as the Goldstone boson of broken baryon number, and can be extended to more realistic cases with a finite strange quark mass.
