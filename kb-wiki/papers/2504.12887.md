# On the Inner Crusts of Neo-Neutron Stars: exotic light nuclei, diffusional and thermodynamical stability

> Based on an extended nuclear statistical equilibrium model, we investigate the properties of non-accreted crusts of young and warm neo-neutron stars, i.e., of finite-temperature inhomogeneous dense matter in beta equilibrium. An interesting feature is the appearance, in the deep inner crust, of an extensive and almost pure layer of neutron-rich light nuclei that extends up to the density of the transition to homogeneous matter. Most probably, this layer emerges due to translational degrees of freedom of the nuclei. If confirmed, it will significantly impact the transport and elastic properties of the crust and its crystallization process. Then, we demonstrate that our inner crust is stable with respect to the diffusion of ions, which is in contrast with some of the predictions made in the literature for cold crusts. Finally, we show that clusterization completely exhausts the density instabilities that affect sub-saturated nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inner crusts of warm neo-neutron stars (temperatures around 0.1-0.4 MeV) likely contain an extensive, almost pure layer of neutron-rich light nuclei (specifically 14He or 7H) that extends up to the transition to homogeneous matter, but this conclusion is hedged by the caveat that the exact composition depends on the pool of nuclei used and that the result may be an artifact of the excluded volume approximation rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors employed an extended nuclear statistical equilibrium (eNSE) model that treats nuclei as an ideal gas with excluded volume corrections, unbound nucleons via the mean-field theory of nuclear matter using BSk25 and BBSk1 Skyrme interactions, and electrons as an ideal Fermi gas. Beta equilibrium was imposed at temperatures of 0.1 MeV and 0.4 MeV, and the composition was computed using experimental masses from AME2020 supplemented by the DZ10 mass table, with thermal population of excited states for nuclei with A >= 16. The stability with respect to ion diffusion was assessed by examining the average mass-to-charge ratio (A/Z) as a function of baryon density, and thermodynamic stability was evaluated by checking whether the baryon chemical potential increases monotonically with density.

## Key numbers

- The transition density to homogeneous matter for the eNSE reference run is n_tr = 0.0742 fm^-3, compared to 0.0839 fm^-3 for the zero-temperature model of Pearson (2018)
- The 14He nucleus has a binding energy of 19.3 MeV and a negative neutron separation energy S_n = -1.29 MeV
- The 7H nucleus has a binding energy of 6.58 MeV and a neutron separation energy S_n = 0.81 MeV
- The light nucleus layer becomes abundant at n_B ≈ 0.006 fm^-3 and comprises almost 40% (for 14He) or 30% (for 7H) of the mass
- The impurity parameter Q_imp is almost density independent and very low (Q_imp ≤ 0.01) in the region where 14He is dominant at T < 0.5 MeV
- The average A/Z ratio jumps to 7 in the light nucleus layer
- The crust composition was computed at T = 0.4 MeV and T = 0.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nuclear statistical equilibrium approach of Raduta (2025) and the finite-temperature crust studies of Dinh Thi (2023) to investigate neo-neutron star crusts, which form during the first day after the proto-neutron star phase. The finding of light nuclei in the deep crust qualitatively agrees with Dinh Thi (2023), who showed that the zero-permeability assumption (adopted here) produces the highest light cluster abundances. The results contradict the zero-temperature predictions of Pearson (2018) for cold catalyzed matter, which do not show such light nuclei layers. The paper enables future studies of crust crystallization, thermal and electrical conductivity in warm neutron stars, and the interpretation of crustal cooling in low-mass X-ray binaries, though the authors note that their results apply to non-accreted crusts while accreting systems may differ.
