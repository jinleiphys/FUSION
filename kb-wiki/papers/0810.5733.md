# Quasiparticle random phase approximation uncertainties and their correlations in the analysis of neutrinoless double beta decay

> The variances and covariances associated to the nuclear matrix elements (NME) of neutrinoless double beta decay are estimated within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). It is shown that correlated NME uncertainties play an important role in the comparison of neutrinoless double beta decay rates for different nuclei, and that they are degenerate with the uncertainty in the reconstructed Majorana neutrino mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlated uncertainties in nuclear matrix elements (NME) within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) are large and positively correlated across different nuclei, and that these correlations are degenerate with the uncertainty in the reconstructed Majorana neutrino mass, meaning a common shift of all NME can be compensated by an inverse shift in the neutrino mass parameter.

## Method

The authors performed 24 variants of QRPA calculations for eight candidate neutrinoless double beta decay nuclei, varying the axial coupling (g_A = 1.25 or 1.00), short-range correlation model (Jastrow or unitary correlation operator method), model basis size (small, intermediate, large), and many-body model (QRPA or renormalized QRPA). They defined the 1-sigma range for each nucleus as the interval embracing the minimum and maximum calculated NME value, and calculated the correlation coefficients between joint NME values from the same QRPA sample, including uncertainties from the particle-particle strength parameter g_pp fixed via two-neutrino double beta decay rates.

## Key numbers

* For 76Ge: central NME value eta^0 = 0.635, sigma = 0.122, with phase space factor gamma = 25.517
* For 130Te: central NME value eta^0 = 0.498, sigma = 0.158, with phase space factor gamma = 24.674
* Correlation coefficient between 76Ge and 82Se: rho = 0.978
* Correlation coefficient between 76Ge and 96Zr: rho = 0.518
* Correlation coefficient between 76Ge and 136Xe: rho = 0.805
* Correlation coefficient between 130Te and 136Xe: rho = 0.916
* The Klapdor et al. claim for 76Ge half-life: tau = 25.355 +/- 0.072 (1-sigma), corresponding to m_betabeta = [0.17, 0.45] eV at 90% C.L.
* Best current 90% C.L. half-life limit for 76Ge (IGEX): T > 1.6 x 10^25 y
* Best current 90% C.L. half-life limit for 130Te (CUORICINO): T > 3.0 x 10^24 y

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations by Faessler and collaborators (2008) and Rodin and collaborators (2007) that reduced NME uncertainties by fixing the g_pp parameter via two-neutrino double beta decay rates. The paper explicitly addresses the previously implicit recognition of NME covariances in works by Pena (2004) and Deppisch and collaborators (2007), who observed that the dispersion of NME ratios may be smaller than for individual NME. The results enable statistical comparisons of neutrinoless double beta decay signals across different nuclei using least-squares methods, and show that existing limits from IGEX and CUORICINO do not exclude the Klapdor et al. claim range at comparable confidence levels when correlations are properly included.
