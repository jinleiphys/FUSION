# Implications of PREX-2 on the electric dipole polarizability of neutron rich nuclei

> The recent announcement by the PREX collaboration of an unanticipated thick neutron skin in 208Pb (Rskin208) has challenged our understanding of neutron rich matter in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density. Whereas earlier constraints indicate that the symmetry energy is relatively soft, the PREX-2 result seems to suggest the opposite. We confront constraints on the symmetry energy obtained from measurements of the electric dipole polarizability against those informed by the PREX-2 measurement of Rskin208 and by the correlations that it entails. Covariant energy density functionals informed by the properties of finite nuclei are used to compute the electric dipole response of 48Ca, 68Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb. The set of functionals used in this work are consistent with experimental data, yet are flexible enough in that they span a wide range of values of Rskin208. It is found that theoretical predictions of the electric dipole polarizability that are consistent with the PREX-2 measurement systematically overestimate the corresponding values extracted from the direct measurements of the distribution of electric dipole strength. The neutron skin thickness of 208Pb extracted from parity violating electron scattering and the electric dipole polarizability measured in photoabsorption experiments are two of the cleanest experimental tools used to constrain the symmetry energy around nuclear saturation density. However, the recent value of Rskin208 that suggests a fairly stiff symmetry energy stands in stark contrast to the conclusions derived from the electric dipole polarizability. At present, we offer no solution to this dilemma.

# Implications of PREX-2 on the electric dipole polarizability of neutron rich nuclei

## Key claim

The PREX-2 measurement of a thick neutron skin in 208Pb implies a stiff symmetry energy that is in tension with earlier constraints from electric dipole polarizability measurements; the paper finds that theoretical predictions of the electric dipole polarizability consistent with PREX-2 systematically overestimate values extracted from direct photoabsorption experiments, but offers no resolution to this dilemma.

## Method

Covariant energy density functionals (EDFs) calibrated to finite nuclei properties were used to compute the electric dipole response of 48Ca, 68Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb within a relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) framework. The set of 13 functionals, including FSUGold2 and systematically varied interactions, were chosen to span a wide range of neutron skin thickness values for 208Pb while remaining consistent with experimental ground-state data. The electric dipole polarizability was computed from the inverse-energy-weighted sum rule of the dipole strength distribution.

## Key numbers

- PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm (Adhikari 2021)
- Symmetry energy J and slope L from PREX-2: J = (38.1 ± 4.7) MeV, L = (106 ± 37) MeV (Reed 2021)
- Electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb from RCNP experiment (minus quasi-deuteron contribution): (19.6 ± 0.6) fm^3 (Roca-Maza 2015)
- PREX-2-informed electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb: (21.8^{+1.1}_{-1.4}) fm^3 (median and 16%/84% limits)
- Electric dipole polarizability of 48Ca from RCNP: (2.07 ± 0.22) fm^3 (Birkhan 2016)
- PREX-2-informed electric dipole polarizability of 48Ca: (2.32^{+0.10}_{-0.13}) fm^3
- Electric dipole polarizability of 68Ni from GSI (with tails): (3.88 ± 0.31) fm^3 (Rossi 2013)
- Data-to-data predictions for neutron skin thickness: 48Ca = (0.229 ± 0.035) fm, 68Ni = (0.267 ± 0.050) fm, 132Sn = (0.350 ± 0.074) fm
- Product J times electric dipole polarizability for 208Pb: (830 ± 150) MeV fm^3

## Context

This work confronts constraints on the symmetry energy from electric dipole polarizability measurements against those from the PREX-2 parity-violating electron scattering result (Adhikari 2021). Earlier compilations (Drischler 2020) recommended J = (31.7 ± 1.1) MeV and L = (59.8 ± 4.1) MeV, indicating a soft symmetry energy, while PREX-2 suggests a much stiffer symmetry energy with L = (106 ± 37) MeV. The paper extends prior work by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010) and Roca-Maza (2013, 2015) on correlations between neutron skin thickness and electric dipole polarizability, and provides data-to-data relations that can be tested by the upcoming CREX experiment on 48Ca and by future measurements at radioactive beam facilities such as FRIB.
