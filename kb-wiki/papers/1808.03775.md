# On the peripheral-tube description of the two-particle correlations in nuclear collisions

> In this work, we study the two-particle correlations regarding a peripheral tube model. From our perspective, the main characteristics of the observed two-particle correlations are attributed to the multiplicity fluctuations and the locally disturbed one-particle distribution associated with hydrodynamic response to the geometric fluctuations in the initial conditions. We investigate the properties of the initial conditions and collective flow concerning the proposed model. It is shown that the experimental data can be reproduced by hydrodynamical simulations using appropriately constructed initial conditions. Besides, instead of numerical calibration, we extract the model parameters according to their respective physical interpretations and show that the obtained numerical values are indeed qualitatively in agreement with the observed data. Possible implications of the present approach are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed two-particle correlations in nuclear collisions can be explained by a peripheral tube model where the main characteristics are attributed to multiplicity fluctuations and locally disturbed one-particle distributions from hydrodynamic response to geometric fluctuations, rather than solely by event-averaged flow harmonics. The authors claim that model parameters extracted according to their physical interpretations (without numerical calibration) produce results that are qualitatively in agreement with experimental data, but they hedge that the model serves as a complementary effort and may or may not be proven correct.

## Method

The authors constructed initial conditions (IC) for 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions by combining a smooth elliptic background energy distribution (obtained by averaging over 343 NeXuS events for 20%-40% centrality) with a peripheral high-energy tube placed near the surface. They performed 2000 event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the SPheRIO code (Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics) with Bjorken scaling on the transverse plane, followed by Monte-Carlo hadronization (200 times per event). The two-particle correlations were evaluated using the cumulant method and compared to PHENIX data, and model parameters (flow coefficients, multiplicity fluctuations) were extracted from separate simulations of background-only and tube-containing IC.

## Key numbers

* Background energy distribution parametrization: epsilon_bgd = (9.33 + 7r^2 + 2r^4) e^{-r^{1.8}} fm^{-3}, with r = sqrt(0.41x^2 + 0.186y^2) fm.
* Peripheral tube energy profile: epsilon_tube = 12 e^{-(x - x_tube)^2 - (y - y_tube)^2} fm^{-3}.
* Tube radial parameter r_tube = 2.3 fm used in the hydrodynamic simulations.
* Background elliptic flow coefficients v2^bgd: 0.11 for 0.4 < p_T < 1 GeV, 0.21 for 1 < p_T < 2 GeV, 0.36 for 2 < p_T < 3 GeV.
* Overall elliptic flow coefficients v2^all: 0.09 for 0.4 < p_T < 1 GeV, 0.17 for 1 < p_T < 2 GeV, 0.26 for 2 < p_T < 3 GeV.
* Multiplicity fluctuation for background (0.4 < p_T^A < 1, 2 < p_T^T < 3): <N_bgd^T N_bgd^A> - <N_bgd^T><N_bgd^A> = 14.67.
* Multiplicity fluctuation for background (1 < p_T^A < 2, 2 < p_T^T < 3): <N_bgd^T N_bgd^A> - <N_bgd^T><N_bgd^A> = 5.07.
* Tube-related product for 0.4 < p_T^A < 1, 2 < p_T^T < 3: <N_tube^T N_tube^A> v2^tube,T v2^tube,A = 1.62; <N_tube^T N_tube^A> v3^tube,T v3^tube,A = 1.63.
* Tube-related product for 1 < p_T^A < 2, 2 < p_T^T < 3: <N_tube^T N_tube^A> v2^tube,T v2^tube,A = 1.36; <N_tube^T N_tube^A> v3^tube,T v3^tube,A = 1.48.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous peripheral tube model (Wen et al., 2015-2017) as an alternative to the standard flow harmonics interpretation where triangular flow v3 arises from octupole deformation of the initial energy distribution. The model contrasts with the event-averaged linear correlation approach between epsilon_n and v_n (Heinz, Voloshin, and others, 2000s-2010s), which becomes less evident for harmonics n > 2. The paper enables further investigation of nonlinear hydrodynamic signals through event plane correlations (ATLAS, 2014) and 2+1 correlations (STAR), and suggests that the model can be used to study the genuine nonlinear regime of flow dynamics beyond event-averaged analyses.
