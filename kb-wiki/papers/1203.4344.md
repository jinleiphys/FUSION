# Neutrino cooling rates due to $^{54,55,56}$Fe for presupernova evolution of massive stars

> Accurate estimate of neutrino energy loss rates are needed for the study of the late stages of the stellar evolution, in particular for cooling of neutron stars and white dwarfs. Proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory has recently being used for a microscopic calculation of stellar weak interaction rates of iron isotopes with success. Here I present the detailed calculation of neutrino and antineutrino cooling rates due to key iron isotopes in stellar matter using the pn-QRPA theory. The rates are calculated on a fine grid of temperature-density scale suitable for core-collapse simulators. The calculated rates are compared against earlier calculations. The neutrino cooling rates due to isotopes of iron are in overall good agreement with the rates calculated using the large-scale shell model. During the presupernova evolution of massive stars, from oxygen shell burning till around end of convective core silicon burning phases, the calculated neutrino cooling rates due to $^{54}$Fe are three to four times larger than the corresponding shell model rates. The Brink's hypothesis used in previous calculations can at times lead to erroneous results. The Brink's hypothesis assumes that the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for all excited states are the same. It is, however, shown by the present calculation that both the centroid and total strength for excited states differ appreciably from the ground state distribution. These changes in the strength distributions of thermally populated excited states can alter the total weak interaction rates rather significantly. The calculated antineutrino cooling rates, due to positron capture and $β$-decay of iron isotopes, are orders of magnitude smaller than the corresponding neutrino cooling rates and can safely be neglected specially at low temperatures and high stellar densities.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA calculation of neutrino cooling rates due to iron isotopes shows overall good agreement with large-scale shell model (LSSM) rates, but during the presupernova evolution of massive stars from oxygen shell burning to the end of convective core silicon burning, the calculated neutrino cooling rates due to ⁵⁴Fe are three to four times larger than the corresponding shell model rates. The Brink's hypothesis, which assumes Gamow-Teller strength distributions for all excited states are the same as the ground state, is shown to be a poor approximation because both the centroid and total strength for excited states differ appreciably from the ground state distribution.

## Method

The proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory was used to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions for ground and excited states of ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe in a model space of 7ħω. Neutrino and antineutrino energy loss rates were computed via four weak-interaction channels (electron capture, positron decay, positron capture, and β-decay) using a fine grid of temperature and density values, with a quenching factor of 0.6 applied to the axial-vector coupling constant. The calculation incorporated experimental deformations for even-even iron isotopes and performed a microscopic state-by-state summation over all initial and final states until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino cooling rates due to ⁵⁴Fe are enhanced by a factor of three compared to LSSM rates at temperatures T₉ ∼ 3 and densities ρ ∼ 10⁷ g cm⁻³, corresponding to the oxygen shell burning phase of massive stars.
- For ⁵⁴Fe at T₉ = 30, the calculated antineutrino cooling rates are around a factor of six bigger than LSSM rates.
- The total B(GT₋) strength for the first excited state of ⁵⁴Fe changed from 7.56 to 6.97 relative to the ground state; for ⁵⁵Fe it increased from 6.87 to 8.87; for ⁵⁶Fe it decreased from 10.74 to 8.04.
- For ⁵⁵Fe at temperatures around T₉ ∼ 3 and densities ρ ∼ 10⁷ g cm⁻³, the calculated antineutrino energy loss rates are suppressed by around two orders of magnitude compared with LSSM and FFN rates.
- At T₉ = 1 and ρ = 10¹¹ g cm⁻³, all three calculations reported antineutrino cooling rates less than 10⁻¹⁰⁰ MeV/s.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak-interaction rates for iron isotopes (Nabi 2009, Nabi 2010) to detailed neutrino and antineutrino cooling rates, and compares them against the earlier FFN (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman 1980) and large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations (Langanke et al. 2000). The results contradict the Brink's hypothesis used in previous calculations and show that excited-state Gamow-Teller strength distributions differ significantly from ground-state distributions, which can alter total weak interaction rates. The calculated rates on a fine temperature-density grid are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for determining the temperature, entropy, and lepton-to-baryon ratio during the presupernova evolution of massive stars.
