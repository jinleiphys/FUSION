# Strangeness-changing response functions:an alternative approach to hypernuclear structure

> We study strangeness-changing response functions as an alternative description of hypernuclear structure. Hypernuclear states are treated in the same manner as any other conventional nuclear excitation that emerges from the response of the nuclear ground state to an external probe. The nuclear response is calculated using a random-phase approximation to an effective relativistic mean-field model that reproduces bulk properties of normal nuclei. The relevant meson-baryon vertices are constrained by imposing SU(3)-flavor symmetry, while the residual hyperon-particle - nucleon-hole interaction is assumed to be mediated by the two lightest S = -1 mesons: the pseudoscalar kaon and its vector partner the K*(892). We use this model to analyze the spectra of oxygen and calcium hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The authors propose that hypernuclear states can be treated as conventional nuclear excitations arising from the response of a strangeness-zero (S=0) nuclear ground state to an external probe, rather than as a separate particle-hole system with a hyperon added to a closed-shell core. This approach relies on the assumption that at or near nuclear-matter saturation density, hyperons are suppressed in the ground state by their larger mass, so the normal nuclear ground state is recovered and hypernuclear excitations emerge consistently from the random-phase approximation (RPA) response of that ground state.

## Method

The nuclear ground state is described using a relativistic mean-field model (parameter set G1) that reproduces bulk properties of normal nuclei. Strangeness-changing response functions are calculated in a random-phase approximation (RPA) built on this mean-field ground state, with the residual hyperon-particle–nucleon-hole interaction mediated by the two lightest S = -1 mesons: the pseudoscalar kaon and its vector partner the K*(892). The meson-baryon vertices are constrained by imposing SU(3)-flavor symmetry, with the kaon coupling taken as pseudovector and the K* coupling including both vector and tensor terms. The response is computed via a multipole decomposition of the polarization insertion, reducing the three-dimensional RPA integral equation to one-dimensional equations for each total angular momentum J.

## Key numbers

* The kaon decay constant used for vertices involving kaons is f ≈ 114.4 MeV.
* The Λ magnetic moment is taken from the Particle Data Group as μ_Λ = -0.613 (in nuclear magnetons).
* The Σ^0 magnetic moment is taken from a chiral perturbation theory calculation as μ_Σ^0 = 0.65 (in nuclear magnetons).
* The Σ^0 potential in nuclear matter is set to U^Σ = g^φ_Σ φ - g^ω_Σ ω^0 ≈ 25 MeV.
* The mixing angle for ω-φ mesons is set to the ideal mixing value sin(θ) = 1/√3.
* The F and D coupling constants for the pseudovector kaon-baryon vertices are F = 3/4 and D = 1/2, constrained by F + D = g_A.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Mueller (1999) and the FST97 model (1997) to treat hypernuclear spectra via strangeness-changing response functions, in contrast to earlier particle-hole models (Brueckner, 1976) that approximated hypernuclei as a core of nucleons with one strange baryon added. The approach is motivated by the advent of continuous electron beam facilities such as TJNAF, where K+ photoproduction is relatively insensitive to distortion effects, enabling a cleaner decomposition into nuclear structure and production operator. The method enables the consistent inclusion of many-body correlations beyond the simple particle-hole picture, which were recognized as essential after precise hypernuclear measurements became available (Brueckner, 1976; Chiang, 1979).
