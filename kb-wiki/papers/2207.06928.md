# Condensed dark matter with a Yukawa interaction

> We explore the possible phases of a condensed dark matter (DM) candidate taken to be in the form of a fermion with a Yukawa coupling to a scalar particle, at zero temperature but at finite density. This theory essentially depends on only four parameters, the Yukawa coupling, the fermion mass, the scalar mediator mass, and the DM density. At low fermion densities we delimit the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS), Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) and crossover phases as a function of model parameters using the notion of scattering length. We further study the BCS phase by consistently including emergent effects such as the scalar density condensate and superfluid gaps. Within the mean field approximation, we derive the consistent set of gap equations, retaining their momentum dependence, and valid in both the non-relativistic and relativistic regimes. We present numerical solutions to the set of gap equations, in particular when the mediator mass is smaller and larger than the DM mass. Finally, we discuss the equation of state (EoS) and possible astrophysical implications for asymmetric DM.

## Key claim
The paper maps the possible superfluid phases (BCS, BEC, or crossover) of a fermionic dark matter candidate with a Yukawa interaction to a scalar mediator, but the phase at low densities depends sensitively on the scattering length sign and magnitude, which alternates as the coupling-to-mass ratio changes; at high densities the system always enters a relativistic BCS phase regardless of the low-density behavior.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for the Yukawa potential to compute the s-wave scattering length as a function of the dimensionless parameters \(\beta = \alpha m/m_\phi\) and \(k_F/m_\phi\), using this to delineate BCS, BEC, and crossover phases. They then derive the full set of mean-field gap equations for the BCS phase, retaining momentum dependence of the gap functions and consistently including the scalar density condensate that modifies the effective fermion mass. The coupled gap equations and scalar condensate equation are solved numerically using a matrix inversion method.

## Key numbers
- The phase boundaries are defined by \((k_F a)^{-1} < -1\) for BCS, \((k_F a)^{-1} > 1\) for BEC, and \(-1 < (k_F a)^{-1} < 1\) for crossover.
- For a heavy mediator with \(m_\phi = 5\,m\) and coupling \(g=3\), the gap shows exponential falloff typical of BCS solutions at moderate densities.
- For a light mediator with \(m_\phi = 0.13\,m\) and \(g=2\) (corresponding to \(\beta=2.4\)), the system is in BEC phase at low densities with a gap constant and much larger than \(k_F^2/2m\).
- For the same light mediator with \(g=3\) (\(\beta=5.5\)), the system is in BCS phase at low densities, with crossover occurring at \(k_F \approx |a^{-1}| = 0.07\,m\).
- The effective fermion mass \(m_\ast\) decreases with density and tends to zero in the high-density limit.

## Context
This work extends the study of superfluid dark matter, which has typically focused on fundamental scalar DM (Berezhiani 2015, Fan 2016, Ferreira 2020), to the less-studied case of fermionic DM with Yukawa interactions. It builds on the scattering-length approach used by Chu (2019) for interacting DM and on the mean-field treatment of relativistic BCS pairing by Pisarski (1999) and Alford (2003, 2004). The results enable future phenomenological studies of asymmetric DM accumulation in neutron stars (Kouvaris 2018, Gresham 2018) and the equation of state for compact DM objects, including the possibility of dissipationless heat transport and vortex formation during DM halo mergers.
