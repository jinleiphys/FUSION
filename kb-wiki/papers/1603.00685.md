# Tagged spectator deep-inelastic scattering off the deuteron as a tool to study neutron structure

> We give an overview of a model to describe deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off the deuteron with a spectator proton, based on the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA). The model accounts for the final-state interactions (FSI) of the DIS debris with the spectator proton. Values of the rescattering cross section are obtained by fits to high-momentum spectator data. By using the so-called "pole extrapolation method", free neutron structure functions can be obtained by extrapolating low-momentum spectator proton data to the on-shell neutron pole. We apply this method to the BONuS data set and find a surprising Bjorken $x$ dependence, indicating a possible rise of the neutron to proton structure function ratio at high $x$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that applying the pole extrapolation method to the BONuS data set reveals a surprising rise of the neutron to proton structure function ratio at high Bjorken x, but notes that the highest x values correspond to sub-DIS invariant masses (W approximately 1.18 GeV) where the rise cannot be directly related to quark pdf properties without using duality arguments.

## Method

The authors model deep-inelastic scattering off the deuteron with a spectator proton using the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) and account for final-state interactions (FSI) through a generalized eikonal approximation, where the rescattering amplitude is parametrized by total cross section sigma, slope factor B, and real part epsilon. They fit the sigma parameter to high-momentum spectator data from the Deeps experiment, then apply the pole extrapolation method to renormalized low-momentum spectator data from the BONuS experiment to extract free neutron structure functions by extrapolating to the on-shell neutron pole.

## Key numbers

- Fitted sigma rescattering parameter has a value of about 60 mb at the Delta-resonance region and increases with increasing invariant mass W of hadronic products
- At Q^2 = 1.8 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 2.8 GeV^2, the fitted sigma parameter shows a significant decrease with increasing Q^2, suggesting a possible color transparency signal
- BONuS data measured down to proton momenta of 70 MeV, enabling short extrapolation lengths for the pole extrapolation method
- The highest Bjorken x values correspond to W approximately 1.18 GeV, placing them in the sub-DIS regime

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical models of tagged spectator DIS by Simula (1996), Melnitchouk (1996), and Sargsian (2005) by including FSI effects and applying the pole extrapolation method first proposed by Chew and Low (1958) for hadron-proton scattering. The results contradict earlier expectations for the F2n/F2p ratio at high x and enable future measurements at Jefferson Lab's BONuS12 experiment and at a future electron-ion collider, while also suggesting possible isosinglet quark-quark short-range correlations in the nucleon at x approaching 1.
