# Sensitivity of elastic electron scattering off the 3He to the nucleon form factors

> Elastic electron-3He scattering is studied in the relativistic impulse approximation. The amplitudes for the three-nucleon system - 3He - are obtained by solving the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equation. The charge and magnetic form factor are calculated and compared with the experimental data for the momentum transfer squared up to 100 fm-2. The influence of the various nucleon form factors is investigated.

## Key claim

Elastic electron-3He scattering calculations using a relativistic impulse approximation with a simple one-rank separable kernel for the NN interaction describe experimental data well only at low and medium momentum transfer, with significant deviations at higher Q² that depend crucially on the chosen nucleon form factor model.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equation (Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation) for the three-nucleon system using one-rank separable kernels of NN interaction, considering only the main S partial wave states (¹S₀ and ³S₁). The charge and magnetic form factors of ³He were calculated using a straightforward relativistic generalization of nonrelativistic expressions, with multiple integration performed via the Vegas Monte Carlo algorithm. Three nucleon electromagnetic form factor models were tested: the dipole fit (DIPOLE), vector-dominance model (VDM), and relativistic harmonic oscillator model (RHOM).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer squared range studied: up to 100 fm⁻²
- The Lorentz transformation parameter η = -Q²/4s equals 0.12 at -Q² = 100 fm⁻², justifying a Taylor series expansion for the boosted momenta
- For the charge form factor, the difference between calculations using different nucleon form factor models reaches half an order of magnitude at -Q² = 100 fm⁻²
- For the magnetic form factor, the VDM curve differs from DIPOLE and RHOM by about half an order of magnitude at -Q² = 60 fm⁻²
- The magnetic form factor experimental data show an interference minimum at Q² about 16-18 fm⁻², which none of the three models reproduce

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of elastic electron scattering off ³He by Pinto (2009) and Rupp (1987), using vertex functions obtained from earlier Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation solutions by Bondarenko (2015, 2017). The study demonstrates that the simple one-rank separable kernel for NN interaction is insufficient for high momentum transfer, and the authors suggest that multirank kernels should be considered to improve agreement with experimental data from Stanford (1977), Orsay (1972), SLAC (1978), Bates (2001), and JLab (2016, 2017).
