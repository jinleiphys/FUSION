# QCD Structure of Nuclear Interactions

> This dissertation investigated selected processes involving baryons and nuclei in hard scattering reactions. Through these processes, this work explored the constituent structure of baryons and the mechanisms through which the interactions between these constituents ultimately control the selected reactions. First, hard nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering was studied considering the quark exchange (QE) between the nucleons to be the dominant mechanism of interaction in the constituent picture. It was found that an angular asymmetry exhibited by proton-neutron (pn) elastic scattering data is explained within this framework if a quark-diquark picture dominates the nucleon's structure instead of a more traditional SU(6) model. The latter yields an asymmetry around 90 deg center of mass scattering with a sign opposite to what is experimentally observed. The second process is the hard breakup by a photon of a nucleon-nucleon system in light nuclei. Proton-proton (pp) and pn breakup in 3He, and double Delta-isobars production in deuteron breakup were analyzed in the hard rescattering model (HRM), which in conjunction with the QE mechanism provides a QCD description of the reaction. Cross sections for both channels in 3He photodisintegration were computed without the need of a fitting parameter. The results presented here for pp breakup show excellent agreement with recent experimental data. In double Delta-isobars production in deuteron breakup, HRM angular distributions for the two double Delta channels were compared to the pn channel and to each other. An important prediction from this study is that the Delta++ Delta- channel consistently dominates Delta+Delta0, which is in contrast with models that unlike the HRM consider a double Delta system in the initial state of the interaction. For such models both channels should have the same strength.

## Key claim

The angular asymmetry observed in proton-neutron (pn) hard elastic scattering data is explained by a quark-diquark picture of the nucleon's structure, while a traditional SU(6) three-quark model yields an asymmetry around 90 degrees center-of-mass scattering with a sign opposite to experimental observations.

## Method

Hard nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering was analyzed using the quark exchange (QE) mechanism as the dominant interaction in the constituent quark picture. For photodisintegration processes, the hard rescattering model (HRM) was applied to compute cross sections for proton-proton and proton-neutron breakup in 3He, and for double Delta-isobar production in deuteron breakup, using helicity amplitudes derived from quark interchange.

## Key numbers

- The quark-diquark parameter rho determines the relative amplitude of vector diquark to scalar diquark sectors, with extreme values rho=1 and rho=0 corresponding to SU(6) and good diquark symmetries respectively
- For pn elastic scattering, out of 16 possible helicity amplitudes only 5 are independent, labeled phi_1 through phi_5
- For pn to Delta-Delta scattering, 32 independent helicity amplitudes exist, with 7 helicity-conserving amplitudes labeled phi_1, phi_3, phi_4, phi_6, phi_7, phi_8, and phi_9
- The normalization factor N_DeltaDelta = (1+rho)^2/(1+rho^2) determines the relative strength of the two Delta-Delta channels

## Context

This dissertation extends the quark interchange model (QIM) approach previously applied in works by Sivers (1975) and others to baryon-baryon scattering. The work enables a QCD-based description of nuclear interactions at high momentum transfer, with specific predictions for Delta++ Delta- dominance over Delta+ Delta0 in deuteron photodisintegration, contradicting models that assume a Delta-Delta system in the initial state. The results for pp breakup in 3He show excellent agreement with recent experimental data without fitting parameters.
