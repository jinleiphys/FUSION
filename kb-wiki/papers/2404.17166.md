# Distinguishing charged lepton flavor violation scenarios with inelastic $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion

> The Mu2e and COMET experiments are expected to improve existing limits on charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) by roughly four orders of magnitude. $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion experiments are typically optimized for electrons produced without nuclear excitation, as this maximizes the electron energy and minimizes backgrounds from the free decay of the muon. Here we argue that Mu2e and COMET will be able to extract additional constraints on CLFV from inelastic $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion, given the $^{27}$Al target they have chosen and backgrounds they anticipate. We describe CLFV scenarios in which inelastic CLFV can induce measurable distortions in the near-endpoint spectrum of conversion electrons, including cases where certain contributing operators cannot be probed in elastic $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion. We extend the nonrelativistic EFT treatment of elastic $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion to include the new nuclear operators needed for the inelastic process, evaluate the associated nuclear response functions, and describe several new-physics scenarios where the inelastic process can provide additional information on CLFV.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Mu2e and COMET, despite being optimized for elastic (no nuclear excitation) muon-to-electron conversion, can extract additional constraints on charged lepton flavor violation from inelastic transitions to low-lying excited states of the ²⁷Al target, including cases where certain contributing operators cannot be probed in elastic conversion at all.

## Method

The authors extend the nonrelativistic effective field theory (NRET) treatment of elastic muon-to-electron conversion to include the new nuclear operators needed for inelastic processes, increasing the number of independent nuclear response functions from six to eleven. They evaluate the associated nuclear response functions using shell-model calculations for ²⁷Al, focusing on transitions to the ground state (Jπ=5/2⁺) and three low-lying excited states (1/2⁺ at 0.844 MeV, 3/2⁺ at 1.015 MeV, and 7/2⁺ at 2.212 MeV) that produce conversion electrons above the dominant decay-in-orbit and radiative muon capture backgrounds. The sensitivity to different CLFV scenarios is studied by computing ratios of inelastic-to-elastic conversion rates in the limit where a single nuclear response function dominates.

## Key numbers

- The current best limit on muon-to-electron conversion is Rμe < 7.0×10⁻¹³ at 90% confidence level from SINDRUM II with a gold target.
- Mu2e and COMET are expected to reach a single-event sensitivity Rμe ≲ 10⁻¹⁷ for a ²⁷Al target, improving existing limits by roughly four orders of magnitude.
- The muon binding energy for ²⁷Al is Eμᵇⁱⁿᵈ ≈ 0.463 MeV.
- The three low-lying excited states of ²⁷Al considered have excitation energies ΔEnuc = 0.844 MeV (1/2⁺), 1.015 MeV (3/2⁺), and 2.212 MeV (7/2⁺).
- The endpoint for radiative muon capture electrons in ²⁷Al is 3.62 MeV below the maximum conversion electron energy.
- The typical shift in reconstructed momentum from energy losses in the Mu2e detector is δq₀ = −0.5497(26) MeV/c for electrons near 105 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the NRET formalism for elastic muon-to-electron conversion developed by Rule and Haxton (2021) and Haxton (2022) to inelastic transitions, where parity and time-reversal selection rules no longer restrict the contributing operators. Previous studies of "incoherent" muon-to-electron conversion (Kosmas, 1990s; Chiang, 1993; PhysRevC, 2000) estimated total response to all excited states for selected CLFV operators, whereas this work focuses on experimentally accessible low-lying transitions and operators for which inelastic transitions provide unique information. The results enable Mu2e and COMET to extract multiple independent constraints on CLFV from a single nuclear target, potentially distinguishing between new physics scenarios without requiring additional experiments with different target nuclei.
