# Compton Scattering on the Charged Pion and the Process γγ\to π^0 π^0

> The Compton scattering on a charged pion and the process γγ\to π^0π^0 are studied using the dispersion relations. Unknown parameters of the S-wave ππinteraction, represented as a board Breit-Wignar resonance, and a sum and a difference of the π^0 meson polarizabilities are found from a fit to the experimental data for the process γ+γ\to π^0+π^0. This fit gives α+β=0.98\pm 0.03 and α-β=-1.6\pm 2.2. The found parameters of the S-wave ππinteraction are used for the calculation of the cross section of the Compton scattering on π^{\pm} meson. The analysis of the obtained results shows that the experimental data for the elastic γπscattering in the energy region up to 1 GeV together with the data for the process γ+γ\to π^0+π^0 could be used both for a determination of the pion polarizability values with quite high accuracy and for study of the S-wave ππinteraction.

# Compton Scattering on the Charged Pion and the Process γγ → π⁰π⁰

## Key claim

The dispersion-relation framework with fixed-t subtractions can simultaneously describe both Compton scattering on charged pions and the γγ → π⁰π⁰ process, but the extracted S-wave ππ interaction parameters depend strongly on the assumed value of the sum of π⁰ polarizabilities, and the broad Breit-Wigner resonance used to represent this interaction may be an effective description of non-resonant S-wave ππ interaction rather than a genuine particle.

## Method

Dispersion relations at fixed t with one subtraction were constructed for the helicity amplitudes M₊₊ and M₊₋ of γπ scattering, with subtraction functions determined through dispersion relations in the cross channels and expressed via the difference and sum of pion polarizabilities. The amplitudes were saturated by contributions from ρ(770), b₁(1235), a₁(1260), and a₂(1320) mesons in the s and u channels, and σ, f₀(980), and f₂(1270) mesons in the t channel; for the γγ → π⁰π⁰ process, ρ(770), ω(782), and φ(1020) mesons replaced the axial-vector and tensor contributions. A fit to experimental data for γγ → π⁰π⁰ was performed using five free parameters: the mass, full width, and γγ decay width of the σ meson (representing S-wave ππ interaction via a broad Breit-Wigner), plus the sum and difference of π⁰ polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- Sum of π⁰ polarizabilities from fit (variant a): α+β = 0.98 ± 0.03 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), obtained from fit to γγ → π⁰π⁰ data up to √t = 2 GeV
- Difference of π⁰ polarizabilities from fit (variant a): α−β = −1.6 ± 2.2 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- σ meson parameters from variant a: mass = 547 ± 45 MeV, full width = 1204 ± 362 MeV, γγ decay width = 0.62 ± 0.19 keV (χ² = 0.30)
- σ meson parameters from variant b (ChPT two-loop fixed polarizabilities): mass = 471 ± 23 MeV, full width = 706 ± 164 MeV, γγ decay width = 0.33 ± 0.07 keV (χ² = 0.42)
- σ meson parameters from variant c (Kaloshin et al. fixed polarizabilities): mass = 584 ± 32 MeV, full width = 1378 ± 277 MeV, γγ decay width = 0.83 ± 0.16 keV (χ² = 0.31)
- Dispersion sum rule calculation of α−β for π⁰: −3.01 ± 2.06 (integration up to 25 GeV²)
- Dispersion sum rule calculation of α−β for π±: 10.3 ± 1.9
- At √s = 350 MeV, correction to low-energy expression for total cross section of γπ± scattering is about 0.6%
- At √s = 1 GeV, relative contribution of α (difference) into back scattering exceeds 100%, and contribution of β (sum) into forward scattering exceeds 300%

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-relation approach of Morgan and Pennington (1991), Kaloshin et al. (1995), and Donoghue and Holstein (1993) for γγ → ππ by constructing fixed-t subtracted dispersion relations that simultaneously describe Compton scattering on charged pions. It improves upon the two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation by Bellucci et al. (1994), which disagreed with experimental data for γγ → π⁰π⁰ at low energies, and upon the coupled-channel approach of Oller et al. (1997), which overestimated the cross section for this process between 0.4 and 0.6 GeV. The results enable extraction of pion polarizabilities from planned experiments at Mainz (A2 Collaboration), CEBAF, and CERN in the energy region up to 750–850 MeV, where model dependence is minimized, and also provide a method to study S-wave ππ interaction and determine a₁ meson decay widths from higher-energy data.
