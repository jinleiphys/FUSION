# Dynamical coupled-channel model of kaon-hyperon interactions

> The pi N --> KY and KY --> KY reactions are studied using a dynamical coupled-channel model of meson-baryon interactions at energies where the baryon resonances are strongly excited. The channels included are: pi N, K Λ, and KΣ. The resonances considered are: N^* [S_{11}(1650), P_{11}(1710), P_{13}(1720),D_{13}(1700)]; Δ^* [S_{31}(1900), P_{31}(1910), P_{33}(1920)]; Λ^* [S_{01}(1670), P_{01}(1810)] Σ^* [P_{11}(1660), D_{13}(1670)]; and K^*(892). The basic non-resonant πN --> KY and KY --> KY transition potentials are derived from effective Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method. The dynamical coupled-channel equations are simplified by parametrizing the pi N -->pi N amplitudes in terms of empirical pi N partial-wave amplitudes and a phenomenological off-shell function. Two models have been constructed. Model A is built by fixing all coupling constants and resonance parameters using SU(3) symmetry, the Particle Data Group values, and results from a constituent quark model. Model B is obtained by allowing most of the parameters to vary around the values of model A in fitting the data. Good fits to the available data for pi^- p to K^0 Λ, K^0 Σ^0 have been achieved. The investigated kinematics region in the center-of-mass frame goes from threshold to 2.5 GeV. The constructed models can be imbedded into associated dynamical coupled-channel studies of kaon photo- and electro-production reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamical coupled-channel model for πN → KY and KY → KY reactions that includes off-shell effects, unlike on-shell K-matrix approaches. The authors qualify that while Model A (fixed SU(3) and PDG parameters) gives only a qualitative description of data with a reduced χ² of 3.28, Model B (allowing parameters to vary within ±2δ of PDG ranges) achieves significantly better fits, though the extracted coupling constants sometimes deviate substantially from literature central values.

## Method

The authors extended the Sato-Lee dynamical coupled-channel formalism (1996) for πN scattering to include KΛ and KΣ channels, deriving non-resonant transition potentials from effective Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method. The πN → πN amplitudes were parametrized using empirical partial-wave amplitudes from SAID with a phenomenological off-shell function, while resonant amplitudes for N*, Δ*, Λ*, and Σ* resonances were included using Breit-Wigner forms with parameters from PDG and constituent quark models. Two models were constructed: Model A fixed all coupling constants using SU(3) symmetry, PDG values, and constituent quark model predictions; Model B allowed most parameters to vary within ±2δ of their uncertainties while fitting π⁻p → K⁰Λ and K⁰Σ⁰ data from threshold to W ≈ 2.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Reduced χ² of 3.28 for Model A, reduced by roughly a factor of 2 for Model B
- Cutoff parameters in Model A: Λ_s = 500.0 MeV/c, Λ_u = 730.1 MeV/c, Λ_t = 1200.0 MeV/c, Λ_πN = 1017.8 MeV/c
- Cutoff parameters in Model B: Λ_s = 500.0 MeV/c, Λ_u = 1200.0 MeV/c, Λ_t = 1199.6 MeV/c, Λ_πN = 1199.9 MeV/c
- K*NY vector coupling f^V_{K* N Λ} = 0.437 (Model A), 0.367 (Model B); tensor coupling f^T_{K* N Λ} = -2.161 (Model A), -2.676 (Model B)
- K*NY vector coupling f^V_{K* N Σ} = -0.286 (Model A), -0.291 (Model B); tensor coupling f^T_{K* N Σ} = 0.031 (Model A), 0.186 (Model B)
- Investigated center-of-mass energy range from threshold to 2.5 GeV (W ≈ 1.3 to 2.3 GeV for data fitting)
- Data base of about 500 points for differential cross-sections and polarization asymmetries used in fitting

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channel approach of Sato and Lee (1996) from the pion sector to the kaon sector, addressing limitations of two existing approaches: chiral SU(3) coupled-channel models (Kaiser et al., 1997; Hyodo et al., 2003) which cannot provide higher partial waves needed above threshold, and K-matrix coupled-channel models from the Giessen Group (Feuster et al., 1998; Penner and Mosel, 2002) which neglect off-shell dynamical effects. The constructed models can be embedded into dynamical coupled-channel studies of kaon photo- and electro-production reactions at facilities such as JLab, ELSA, Spring-8, and GRAAL, enabling proper extraction of baryon resonance properties and searches for missing resonances.
