# Recursion Method for Deriving Energy-Independent Effective Interaction

> The effective-interaction theory has been one of the useful and practical methods for solving nuclear many-body problems based on the shell model. Various approaches have been proposed which are constructed in terms of the so-called $\widehat{Q}$ box and its energy derivatives introduced by Kuo {\it et al}. In order to find out a method of calculating them we make decomposition of a full Hilbert space into subspaces (the Krylov subspaces) and transform a Hamiltonian to a block-tridiagonal form. This transformation brings about much simplification of the calculation of the $\widehat{Q}$ box. In the previous work a recursion method has been derived for calculating the $\widehat{Q}$ box analytically on the basis of such transformation of the Hamiltonian. In the present study, by extending the recursion method for the $\widehat{Q}$ box, we derive another recursion relation to calculate the derivatives of the $\widehat{Q}$ box of arbitrary order. With the $\widehat{Q}$ box and its derivatives thus determined we apply them to the calculation of the $E$-independent effective interaction given in the so-called Lee-Suzuki (LS) method for a system with a degenerate unperturbed energy. We show that the recursion method can also be applied to the generalized LS scheme for a system with non-degenerate unperturbed energies. If the Hilbert space is taken to be sufficiently large, the theory provides an exact way of calculating the $\widehat{Q}$ box and its derivatives. This approach enables us to perform recursive calculations for the effective interaction to arbitrary order for both systems with degenerate and non-degenerate unperturbed energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical, non-perturbative recursion method for calculating the energy derivatives of the \(\widehat{Q}\) box to arbitrary order, which enables the construction of energy-independent effective interactions via the Lee-Suzuki (LS) method for systems with degenerate unperturbed energies and its generalization (GLS) for non-degenerate cases, provided the Hilbert space is taken sufficiently large to make the approach exact.

## Method

The authors decompose the full Hilbert space into Krylov subspaces, transforming the Hamiltonian into a block-tridiagonal form. They then define a descending recursion relation for operators \(\widetilde{e}_n(E)\) acting in these subspaces, starting from a truncation point \(N\) where the norm of the coupling to the next subspace is negligible. The \(\widehat{Q}\) box is expressed as \(PVP + PHQ_1 [\widetilde{e}_1(E)]^{-1} Q_1 HP\), and its energy derivatives are obtained by differentiating this expression, using a recursion for the derivatives of the inverse operators \(\widetilde{\xi}_n(E) = [\widetilde{e}_n(E)]^{-1}\) derived from the Leibnitz formula. The resulting \(\widehat{Q}\) box and its derivatives are then inserted into the recursive LS and GLS formulas for the effective interaction.

## Key numbers

* The recursion for the effective interaction \(R_n\) in the degenerate case is given by \(R_n = [1 - \widehat{Q}_1 - \widehat{Q}_2 R_{n-1} - \cdots - \widehat{Q}_{n-1} R_2 R_3 \cdots R_{n-1}]^{-1} \cdot \widehat{Q}\) for \(n \geq 3\), with initial values \(R_1 = \widehat{Q}(E_0)\) and \(R_2 = [1 - \widehat{Q}_1(E_0)]^{-1} \cdot \widehat{Q}(E_0)\).
* The multi-energy \(\widehat{Q}_m\) box for non-degenerate energies is expressed as a linear combination \(\widehat{Q}_m(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,\cdots,\varepsilon_{m+1}) = \sum_{k=1}^{m+1} C_k(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,\cdots,\varepsilon_{m+1}) \widehat{Q}(\varepsilon_k)\) with coefficients \(C_k = \prod_{i=1 (i \ne k)}^{m+1} 1/(\varepsilon_k - \varepsilon_i)\).
* The recursion for \(\widetilde{e}_n(E)\) is \(\widetilde{e}_n(E) = e_n(E) - H_{n,n+1} \widetilde{\xi}_{n+1}(E) H_{n+1,n}\), and its \(k\)-th derivative satisfies \(\widetilde{e}_n^{(k)}(E) = \delta_{k0} e_n(E) + \delta_{k1} Q_n - H_{n,n+1} \widetilde{\xi}_{n+1}^{(k)}(E) H_{n+1,n}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative recursion method for the \(\widehat{Q}\) box (Suzuki et al., 2013) to include its energy derivatives, which are required for the Lee-Suzuki (LS) method (Lee and Suzuki, 1980; Suzuki and Lee, 1980) and its generalization to non-degenerate unperturbed energies (GLS) (Suzuki, Okamoto, Ellis, and Kuo, 1994). It provides an alternative to perturbative diagrammatic calculations, which have typically been limited to third order (Coraggio et al., 2009, 2012) or partially fourth order (Barrett et al., 1970), and to numerical differentiation methods. The approach enables recursive calculation of the effective interaction to arbitrary order for both degenerate and non-degenerate systems, with the GLS method offering potentially faster convergence through the choice of multiple initial energy parameters.
