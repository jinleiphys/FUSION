# Probing the QGP through $p_T$-differential radial flow of heavy quarks

> We introduce the $p_T$-differential radial flow $v_0(p_T)$ in the heavy-quark sector. Within an event-by-event Langevin framework, we show that this observable exhibits a strong sensitivity to the heavy quark-bulk interaction. It provides a powerful and novel tool to constrain the transport coefficients of heavy quarks in the QGP and, more generally, to assess the strength of the interaction of a Brownian particle in an expanding bulk medium. The results further indicate that heavy quarks exhibit collective behavior driven by the isotropic expansion of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions and, at low $p_T$, it offers a marked signature of the heavy quark hadronization mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the $p_T$-differential radial flow $v_0(p_T)$ for heavy quarks as a novel observable that is highly sensitive to the heavy quark–bulk interaction strength, but this sensitivity is conditional: at low $p_T$ the observable is marginally dependent on the diffusion coefficient $D_s$ and instead provides a marked signature of the hadronization mechanism, while at intermediate $p_T$ it constrains transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors employ an event-by-event relativistic transport framework coupling a Boltzmann equation for the bulk medium (gluons and light quarks) at fixed shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio with a Langevin equation for charm quarks. Three interaction scenarios are compared: a weakly coupled regime from leading-order pQCD, a strongly coupled regime fitted to unquenched lattice QCD data, and a Quasi-Particle Model (QPM_p) with momentum-dependent parton masses. Hadronization of charm quarks into D mesons and $\Lambda_c$ baryons uses a hybrid model combining coalescence and fragmentation, with the coalescence contribution approximated via a delta-function Wigner function.

## Key numbers

- In the 0–10% centrality class at intermediate $p_T$, the $v_0(p_T)$ from the QPM_p framework is approximately three times larger than in the weak interaction (pQCD) scenario; the lQCD case gives values about four times higher than pQCD.
- The lQCD data suggest a thermalization time for charm quark of $\tau_{th} \approx 1.5 \, fm/c$ at $T=1.2 \, T_c$.
- At low $p_T \simeq 1 \, \text{GeV}$, the ratio $v_0(\Lambda_c) \sim 2 \, v_0(D)$, while pure fragmentation would give $v_0(\Lambda_c) \sim v_0(D)$.
- The difference between weakly coupled and strongly coupled HQ-bulk interactions yields about a factor of 5 for $v_0(p_T)$ at intermediate $p_T$.

## Context

This work extends the recently proposed $v_0(p_T)$ observable, previously studied for light hadrons by Schenke (2020) and measured by the ATLAS (2025) and ALICE (2025) Collaborations in Pb–Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, into the heavy-quark sector for the first time. It builds on prior heavy-quark transport studies using Langevin frameworks (Plumari 2019, Sambataro 2025) and the QPM_p model (Sambataro 2024) that successfully describes D meson $R_{AA}$ and $v_{2,3}(p_T)$. The results enable future global Bayesian analyses to quantitatively constrain the spatial diffusion coefficient $D_s$ and provide a new probe of heavy-quark hadronization mechanisms, with planned extensions to pA and OO collisions at the HL-LHC and to bottom quarks.
