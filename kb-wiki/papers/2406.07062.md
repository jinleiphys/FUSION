# Anomalous propagators and the particle-particle channel: Hedin's equations

> Hedin's equations provide an elegant route to compute the exact one-body Green's function (or propagator) via the self-consistent iteration of a set of non-linear equations. Its first-order approximation, known as $GW$, corresponds to a resummation of ring diagrams and has shown to be extremely successful in physics and chemistry. Systematic improvement is possible, although challenging, via the introduction of vertex corrections. Considering anomalous propagators and an external pairing potential, we derive a new self-consistent set of closed equations equivalent to the famous Hedin equations but having as a first-order approximation the particle-particle (pp) $T$-matrix approximation where one performs a resummation of the ladder diagrams. This pp version of Hedin's equations offers a way to go systematically beyond the $T$-matrix approximation by accounting for low-order pp vertex corrections.

## Key claim

By introducing anomalous propagators and an external pairing potential, the authors derive a new self-consistent set of closed equations, analogous to Hedin's equations, whose first-order approximation yields the particle-particle \(T\)-matrix approximation (a resummation of ladder diagrams), thereby providing a systematic path to include vertex corrections beyond the \(T\)-matrix approximation, though the resulting equations are not closed in the number-conserving limit because the pp kernel depends on the anomalous self-energy.

## Method

The authors start from the equation of motion for the one-body Green's function and use a Schwinger relation that expresses the two-body Green's function as the derivative of an anomalous propagator with respect to an external pairing potential. They then employ Nambu's matrix formalism to gather normal and anomalous propagators into a Gorkov propagator, derive a Dyson equation for it, and express the self-energy in terms of effective interactions and vertex functions. By taking the limit of vanishing pairing potential, they recover a set of equations for the normal propagator whose lowest-order vertex approximation yields the particle-particle \(T\)-matrix approximation at the pp-RPA level.

## Key numbers

* The first non-zero self-energy terms beyond the \(T\)-matrix approximation are of third order in the effective interaction \(T\), as the \(T\)-matrix self-energy is exact up to second order in the Coulomb interaction.
* The lowest-order vertex correction to the self-energy in the number-conserving limit yields a third-order self-energy term (Eq. 3rdorder_selfnrj) that is diagrammatically equivalent to the third-order \(GW\) bubble diagram with the Coulomb interaction replaced by the effective interaction \(T\).

## Context

This work extends the functional-derivative framework of Hedin (1965) to the particle-particle channel, providing a first-principles derivation of the \(T\)-matrix approximation that was previously introduced diagrammatically by Bozek (2002) for superfluid nuclear matter. It contrasts with the conventional Hedin equations, where the \(GW\) approximation (resummation of ring diagrams) emerges naturally, and where vertex corrections to \(GW\) are an active field of research (Schindlmayr 1998, Mejuto-Zaera 2022). The new formalism enables systematic inclusion of pp vertex corrections, analogous to how Hedin's equations allow systematic improvement beyond \(GW\), and may offer insights for superconducting DFT and nuclear structure calculations.
