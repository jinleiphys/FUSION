# The role of the nucleon recoil in low--energy meson--nucleus reactions

> The role of the nucleon recoil corrections in low--energy meson--nucleus interactions is examined. We demonstrate explicitly when calculations within the static approximation are justified and when the recoil terms need to be kept explictly in the propagators, depending on whether the S-wave two--nucleon intermediate state is Pauli blocked or not, while the meson is in flight. While the effect is studied in detail for $πd$ scattering only, a large class of other reactions is discussed for which the findings are relevant as well.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the role of nucleon recoil corrections in low-energy meson-nucleus reactions depends critically on whether the S-wave two-nucleon intermediate state (while the meson is in flight) is Pauli allowed or forbidden; contrary to earlier claims by Rekalo et al., the static approximation is justified for Pauli-forbidden intermediate states, while for Pauli-allowed states the recoil corrections strongly suppress the rescattering contribution.

## Method

The authors analyze πd scattering near threshold using a simplified πN interaction potential with isoscalar (g₊) and isovector (g₋) components, evaluating Feynman diagrams for one-body and two-body (rescattering) contributions in time-ordered perturbation theory. They decompose the πd scattering length into a one-body piece, a leading-order static term, a recoil correction (containing the three-body singularity), and an NLO static correction, then numerically evaluate the relevant integrals using deuteron wave functions from the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- I₊ = -0.88 and I₋ = -0.19, both given in units of I₀ (the leading-order static integral), reflecting the interference pattern where the three-body recoil correction is much smaller for Pauli-forbidden intermediate states (I₋ ≪ I₊)
- ΔI = -0.15 (in units of I₀) for the NLO static correction to the πd scattering length
- The total rescattering contribution is (-0.03 g₊² - 1.04 g₋²)I₀, meaning the isoscalar part is essentially negligible while the isovector part nearly equals the static term
- The recoil corrections to the static term are of order 15-20% of the static term for the Pauli-forbidden case

## Context

This work extends the static approximation framework of Weinberg (1990) and subsequent chiral effective field theory calculations by BBLM and BBEMP, while critically reexamining claims by Rekalo et al. that rescattering contributions vanish for Pauli-forbidden intermediate states. The findings enable proper extraction of single-nucleon amplitudes from deuteron observables and have implications for reactions such as coherent π⁰ photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron, incoherent γd → π⁺nn production, and η-nucleus scattering (e.g., pd → η³He), where the recoil corrections may explain discrepancies between static-approximation calculations and experimental data.
