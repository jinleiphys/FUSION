# Supercritically charged objects and electron-positron pair creation

> We investigate the stability and $e^+e^-$ pair creation of supercritically charged superheavy nuclei, $ud$QM nuggets, strangelets, and strangeon nuggets based on Thomas-Fermi approximation. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing their masses and charge properties reported in earlier publications. It is found that $ud$QM nuggets, strangelets, and strangeon nuggets may be more stable than ${}^{56}$Fe at $A\gtrsim 315$, $5\times10^4$, and $1.2\times10^8$, respectively. For those stable against neutron emission, the most massive superheavy element has a baryon number $\sim$965, while $ud$QM nuggets, strangelets, and strangeon nuggets need to have baryon numbers larger than $39$, 433, and $2.7\times10^5$. The $e^+e^-$ pair creation will inevitably start for superheavy nuclei with charge numbers $Z\geq177$, $ud$QM nuggets with $Z\geq163$, strangelets with $Z\geq 192$, and strangeon nuggets with $Z\geq 212$. A universal relation $Q/R_e = \left(m_e - \barμ_e\right)/α$ is obtained at a given electron chemical potential $\barμ_e$, where $Q$ is the total charge and $R_e$ the radius of electron cloud. This predicts the maximum charge number by taking $\barμ_e=-m_e$. For supercritically charged objects with $\barμ_e<-m_e$, the decay rate for $e^+e^-$ pair production is estimated based on the JWKB approximation. It is found that most positrons are emitted at $t\lesssim 10^{-15}$ s, while a long lasting positron emission is observed for large objects with $R\gtrsim 1000$ fm. The emission and annihilation of positrons from supercritically charged objects may be partially responsible for the short $γ$-ray burst during the merger of binary compact stars, the 511 keV continuum emission, as well as the narrow faint emission lines in X-ray spectra from galaxies and galaxy clusters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that supercritically charged objects (superheavy nuclei, $ud$QM nuggets, strangelets, and strangeon nuggets) can become more stable than ${}^{56}$Fe above certain baryon numbers, and that electron-positron pair creation inevitably starts at specific critical charge numbers, but these stability thresholds depend on the adopted surface tension values and the specific model parameters for each type of strong matter.

## Method

The authors model the properties of nuclear matter, $ud$ quark matter, strange quark matter, and strangeon matter using an expansion of energy per baryon to second order with parameters fixed from earlier publications. They then apply the Thomas-Fermi approximation to spherically symmetric finite-sized objects consisting of a strong-matter core surrounded by an electron cloud, solving the Poisson equation for the electric potential and minimizing the mass to obtain density profiles, charge distributions, and stability conditions. The decay rate for electron-positron pair production in supercritical electric fields is estimated using the Jeffreys-Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (JWKB) approximation.

## Key numbers

* $ud$QM nuggets become more stable than ${}^{56}$Fe at baryon number $A\gtrsim 315$, strangelets at $A\gtrsim 5\times10^4$, and strangeon nuggets at $A\gtrsim 1.2\times10^8$.
* The most massive superheavy element stable against neutron emission has baryon number $\sim 965$; $ud$QM nuggets require $A>39$, strangelets require $A>433$, and strangeon nuggets require $A>2.7\times10^5$ for stability against neutron emission.
* Electron-positron pair creation starts at charge number $Z\geq177$ for superheavy nuclei, $Z\geq163$ for $ud$QM nuggets, $Z\geq192$ for strangelets, and $Z\geq212$ for strangeon nuggets.
* Most positrons are emitted at times $t\lesssim 10^{-15}$ s, while long-lasting positron emission occurs for objects with radius $R\gtrsim 1000$ fm.
* A universal relation $Q/R_e = (m_e - \bar{\mu}_e)/\alpha$ is obtained, where $Q$ is total charge and $R_e$ the electron cloud radius; at $\bar{\mu}_e=-m_e$ this gives $Q = 0.71 R_e$ (with $R_e$ in fm).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on superheavy nuclei stability (e.g., Moller 1976, Lalazissis 1996, Ren 2003) and on strange quark matter objects (Farhi 1984, Madsen 1993, Alford 2006) by providing a unified Thomas-Fermi treatment across four types of strong matter. It also builds on the authors' earlier UDS model for strangelets (Xia 2016, Xia 2017) and on the Schwinger mechanism for pair production (Schwinger 1951). The results enable predictions for astrophysical signatures of supercritically charged objects, including short gamma-ray bursts from binary compact star mergers, the 511 keV continuum emission, and narrow faint X-ray emission lines from galaxies and galaxy clusters.
