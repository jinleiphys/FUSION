# The Gauge Theory Bootstrap: Predicting pion dynamics from QCD

> The Gauge Theory Bootstrap [arXiv:2309.12402, arXiv:2403.10772] computes the strongly coupled pion dynamics by considering the most general scattering matrix, form factors and spectral densities and matching them with perturbative QCD at high energy and with weakly coupled pions at low energy. In this work, we show that further constraints on the spectral densities significantly reduce the possible solutions to a small set of qualitatively similar ones. Quantitatively, the precise solution is controlled by the asymptotic value of the form factors and SVZ sum rules. We also introduce an iterative procedure that, starting from a generic feasible point, converges to a unique solution parameterized by the UV input. For the converged solution we compute masses and widths of resonances that appear, scattering lengths and effective ranges of partial waves, low energy coefficients in the effective action. Additionally, we use these results to discuss the thermodynamics of a pion gas including pair correlations of pions with same and opposite charge.

## Key claim

The Gauge Theory Bootstrap can predict strongly coupled pion dynamics from QCD without experimental input, but the precise solution is controlled by the asymptotic value of the form factors at energies around 2 GeV, where Brodsky-Lepage calculations provide only a factor-of-order-one uncertainty, meaning the results depend on having accurate values of the form factors at that energy rather than on the precise value of the pion decay constant fπ.

## Method

The authors construct the most general S-matrix, form factors, and spectral densities for pion scattering, imposing unitarity, crossing symmetry, and positivity constraints encoded in a 3x3 positive semidefinite matrix of inner products between in-states, out-states, and QCD operator states. They match these quantities to perturbative QCD at high energy via SVZ sum rules and to weakly coupled pions at low energy via chiral perturbation theory, then apply an iterative "Watsonian" procedure that replaces the phase of the partial wave with the phase of the form factor to converge to a unique solution parameterized by the UV input.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass mπ sets the units for the computation, with the intermediate energy region spanning 2mπ ≤ E ≲ 2 GeV.
- The spectral density for the scalar current at low energy is ρ₀⁰(s) ≃ (mπ⁴/(2π)⁴) × (3/(16π)) × (1 - 4mπ²/s)^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the Gauge Theory Bootstrap approach introduced by the same authors in PhysRevLett.133.191601, PhysRevD.110.096001, and He:2024nwd, which itself builds on the modern S-matrix bootstrap revival by Paulos (2016), Kruczenski (2022), and others, and on form factor bootstrap methods in 2d by Karateev (2019) and Correia (2022). The new iterative procedure goes beyond the standard S-matrix bootstrap approach that uses kinematic constraints to put bounds on theory parameters, instead introducing dynamical information from the UV and IR to compute the S-matrix, returning to the original bootstrap philosophy of Chew (1966) but augmented with microscopic QCD information. This enables computation of low energy constants, partial waves, form factors, and resonance spectrum without experimental input, and has been used in Zahed (2024) to study the g-2 factor contribution from hadrons.
