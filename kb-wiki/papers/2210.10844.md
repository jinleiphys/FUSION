# J/$ψ$ yields in low energy nuclear collisions at SPS and FAIR: A baseline estimation

> The yield of $J/ψ$ mesons, produced in proton-nucleus ($p+A$) and nucleus-nucleus ($A+A$) collisions are estimated within a Glauber model ansatz for the upcoming low energy heavy-ion collision experiments at SPS and FAIR. A data driven parametrization is employed to incorporate the effects of Cold Nuclear Matter (CNM) on the $J/ψ$ production cross-section.

## Key claim

The authors provide a baseline estimation of J/ψ yields in low-energy p+A and A+A collisions at SPS and FAIR using a purely data-driven Glauber model approach, explicitly excluding any hot-medium (QGP) effects even for A+A collisions, so that the results serve as a cold-nuclear-matter reference against which future measurements can isolate genuine secondary-medium effects.

## Method

The authors adopt the optical Glauber model and the "ρL" parametrization (an exponential approximation to the full Glauber formalism) to compute J/ψ production cross-sections. The elementary p+p cross-section is obtained by fitting a four-parameter function to world data on inclusive J/ψ production in p+p, p+Li, and p+Be collisions, excluding heavier targets to avoid nuclear effects. The effective J/ψ absorption cross-section σ_abs is extracted by fitting the ρL model to heavy-to-light target ratios of J/ψ production cross-sections from NA60 and NA50 p+A data at four beam energies (158, 400, 450 GeV and a 450 GeV high-intensity sample).

## Key numbers

* Extrapolated inclusive J/ψ production cross-section in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 7.6 GeV: 0.08 ± 0.12 nb/nucleon.
* Extrapolated inclusive J/ψ production cross-section in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 9.8 GeV: 8.61 ± 6.13 nb/nucleon.
* Extrapolated inclusive J/ψ production cross-section in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 12.3 GeV: 31.30 ± 15.33 nb/nucleon.
* Extrapolated inclusive J/ψ production cross-section in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 17.0 GeV: 87.44 ± 27.38 nb/nucleon.
* Effective J/ψ absorption cross-section σ_abs extracted from NA60 data at √s_NN = 17.27 GeV: 7.6 ± 0.92 mb.
* Effective J/ψ absorption cross-section σ_abs extracted from NA60 data at √s_NN = 27.42 GeV: 4.3 ± 1.0 mb.
* Effective J/ψ absorption cross-section σ_abs extracted from NA50 data at √s_NN = 27.42 GeV: 4.1 ± 0.5 mb.
* Effective J/ψ absorption cross-section σ_abs extracted from NA50 data at √s_NN = 29.08 GeV: 4.6 ± 0.8 mb.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-model analysis of J/ψ production in p+A collisions performed by the NA50 and NA60 collaborations at SPS energies (Alessandro et al., Arnaldi et al.) down to the lower energies relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR (√s_NN ~ 2.7–5.5 GeV) and the NA60+ experiment at SPS (√s_NN ~ 6–17 GeV). By providing a cold-nuclear-matter baseline that excludes any QGP or regeneration effects, the results enable future experiments to isolate genuine hot-medium effects on charmonium production in a previously unexplored energy domain, and to test pQCD-based models of charmonium production near threshold.
