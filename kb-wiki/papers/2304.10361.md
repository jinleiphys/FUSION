# Study of S, Cl and Ar isotopes with $N \geq Z$ using microscopic effective $sd$-shell interactions

> In the present work, newly developed microscopic effective $sd$-valence shell interactions such as chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), $J$-matrix inverse scattering potential (JISP16), Daejeon16 (DJ16), and monopole-modified DJ16 (DJ16A) are employed to study the nuclear structural properties of sulphur, chlorine, and argon isotopes with $N \geq Z$. These interactions are derived using the \textit{ab initio} no-core shell-model and the OLS unitary transformation method. We calculate energy spectra and electromagnetic properties to test the predictive strength of the effective interactions for these heavier $sd$-shell nuclei. For a complete systematic study, we compare the microscopic results with the phenomenological USDB results and experimental data. By looking at the excitation energies of these nuclei, the DJ16A interaction is found to be {the} most suitable for these $sd$-shell nuclei among all microscopic interactions. The electric quadrupole transition strength and excitation energy of the first $2^+$ state data of even-even sulphur isotopes indicate the presence of the $N=20$ shell closure. Quadrupole moment predictions are also made using these interactions where experimental data are unknown. Magnetic moments are in excellent agreement with the experimental values. The root-mean-square deviations are also calculated to provide an idea of how accurate the interactions are.

# Study of S, Cl and Ar isotopes with N ≥ Z using microscopic effective sd-shell interactions

## Key claim

The monopole-modified DJ16A interaction is found to be the most suitable among all microscopic interactions for describing excitation energies of these heavier sd-shell nuclei, but the authors acknowledge that even DJ16A is insufficient to fully explain the energy spectra and that further modifications beyond monopole corrections, such as pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole components, are needed, along with possible inclusion of three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for A=18 (¹⁸F) at Nmax=4 and ħΩ=14 MeV to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, then applied a second Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) unitary transformation to construct effective sd-valence shell interactions (N3LO, JISP16, DJ16, DJ16A). These microscopic interactions were used in shell-model calculations with the KSHELL code to compute energy spectra, B(E2) transition strengths, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments for sulphur (³²⁻³⁶S), chlorine (³⁴⁻³⁷Cl), and argon (³⁶⁻³⁸Ar) isotopes, with results compared to phenomenological USDB calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For ³⁶S (N=20), the first 2⁺ state excitation energy is 3.291 MeV experimentally; DJ16A gives 2.986 MeV, while N3LO gives 3.585 MeV, JISP16 gives 3.878 MeV, DJ16 gives 3.540 MeV, and USDB gives 3.382 MeV
- The rms deviation for DJ16A across 72 energy levels of 12 nuclei is 735 keV, compared to 997 keV (N3LO), 976 keV (JISP16), 966 keV (DJ16), and 437 keV (USDB)
- For ³⁶S, the rms deviations between experimental and theoretical results are: N3LO 1.392, JISP16 1.742, DJ16 1.382, DJ16A 0.666, and USDB 0.965
- B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) for ³²S with standard effective charges (ep=1.5e, en=0.5e): N3LO 117.3 e²fm⁴, JISP16 107.7 e²fm⁴, DJ16 107.7 e²fm⁴, DJ16A 84.4 e²fm⁴, USDB 60.1 e²fm⁴, experimental 59.6(12) e²fm⁴
- B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) for ³⁶S: N3LO 21.5 e²fm⁴, JISP16 22.6 e²fm⁴, DJ16 21.1 e²fm⁴, DJ16A 23.3 e²fm⁴, USDB 26.2 e²fm⁴, experimental 17.6(15) e²fm⁴
- For ³⁸Ar, the first excited 2⁺ state excitation energy is 2.167 MeV experimentally; N3LO gives 0.987 MeV, JISP16 gives 1.003 MeV, DJ16 gives 1.025 MeV, DJ16A gives 1.040 MeV
- The lifetime of the 1/2₁⁺ state in ³⁵S calculated with DJ16A is 0.67 ps, while the experimental lifetime is 3.3(2) ps

## Context

This work extends previous studies of microscopic effective sd-shell interactions that were tested primarily on lighter sd-shell nuclei (A=18-19) by Dikmen et al. (2015) using N3LO and JISP16, and by Smirnova et al. (2019) who developed DJ16 and DJ16A for oxygen isotopes and N=14 isotones. The present study applies these interactions to heavier sd-shell nuclei (S, Cl, Ar) where the phenomenological USDB interaction of Brown and Richter (2006, 2008) has been the standard. The results enable systematic comparison of ab initio-derived interactions with phenomenology for these nuclei, and the discrepancies, particularly at N=20 for S and Ar isotopes, motivate future inclusion of three-nucleon forces in NCSM calculations or extension of the model space beyond the sd-shell, as already suggested by the work of Grocutt et al. (2022) showing the importance of pf-shell orbitals for high-spin states in ³⁶S.
