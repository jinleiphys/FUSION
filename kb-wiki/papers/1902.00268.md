# Exclusive dilepton production in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC

> In this paper we perform a systematic study of the exclusive dilepton production by $γγ$ interactions in $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC Run 2 energies considering different levels of precision for the treatment of the absorptive corrections and for the nuclear form factor. The rapidity and invariant mass distributions are estimated taking into account the experimental cutoffs and a comparison with the recent ALICE and ATLAS data for the $e^+ e^-$ and $μ^+ μ^-$ production is presented.

# Exclusive dilepton production in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim

The systematic study shows that the treatment of the nuclear form factor is fundamental for precise predictions of dilepton rapidity and invariant mass distributions at large invariant mass and large rapidities, while different treatments of the absorptive factor have only a small impact on predictions within the kinematical ranges probed by ALICE and ATLAS.

## Method

The authors compute the exclusive dilepton production cross section using the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) in impact parameter representation, with the Breit-Wheeler formula for the elementary gamma-gamma to lepton-pair cross section. They compare three models for the nuclear form factor (point-like, monopole, and realistic Wood-Saxon) and two models for the absorptive factor (hard-sphere step function and Glauber-based smooth transition), then confront the resulting rapidity and invariant mass distributions with ALICE and ATLAS data for e+e- and mu+mu- production.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the realistic form factor prediction for the rapidity distribution at midrapidity is 3% smaller than the monopole prediction.
- Assuming b_i > R (b_i_min = R) together with the realistic form factor suppresses the rapidity distribution by 17% relative to the full realistic calculation.
- The Glauber absorptive factor uses sigma_nn = 88 mb for the total hadronic interaction cross section at LHC energies.
- The Wood-Saxon form factor parameters are a = 0.549 fm and R_A = 6.63 fm; the monopole form factor uses Lambda = 0.088 GeV.
- ATLAS data for dimuon production are compared with cuts on muon pseudorapidity (-2.4 < eta_i_mu < 2.4) and muon transverse momentum p_t > 4.0 GeV.
- ALICE data for dielectron production at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV are compared with cuts on dielectron pair rapidity (|Y_ee| < 0.9), electron pseudorapidity (-0.9 < eta_i_e < 0.9), and electron p_t > 1.0 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for exclusive dilepton production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, building on the formalism developed by Baur and Ferreira-Filho (hard-sphere absorptive factor), Baltz and Klein (Glabuer-based absorptive factor), and the photon flux studies by Greiner and by Klusek-Gawenda (form factor dependence). The paper provides a systematic comparison of different precision levels for absorptive corrections and nuclear form factors, enabling more reliable interpretation of data from STAR, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS Collaborations. The results show that the assumption b_i > R (used in STARlight Monte Carlo) is not a good approximation for dilepton production, and that future measurements at large invariant mass and large rapidities will discriminate between form factor models.
