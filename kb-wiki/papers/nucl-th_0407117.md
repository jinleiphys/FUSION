# Impact of baryon resonances on the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and density

> We study the phase diagram of a generalized chiral SU(3)-flavor model in mean-field approximation. In particular, the influence of the baryon resonances, and their couplings to the scalar and vector fields, on the characteristics of the chiral phase transition as a function of temperature and baryon-chemical potential is investigated. Present and future finite-density lattice calculations might constrain the couplings of the fields to the baryons. The results are compared to recent lattice QCD calculations and it is shown that it is non-trivial to obtain, simultaneously, stable cold nuclear matter.

# Impact of baryon resonances on the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and density

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) model can produce a critical point around T_c ≈ 150 MeV and finite μ_q,c in qualitative agreement with lattice QCD, but only if the scalar and vector couplings of the baryon decuplet are chosen such that normal nuclear matter is not simultaneously stable; demanding stable cold nuclear matter reduces the critical temperature to T_c ≲ 50 MeV, which is far below lattice predictions.

## Method

The authors study the phase diagram of a generalized chiral SU(3)-flavor model in mean-field approximation, including both the baryon octet (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and the lightest baryon decuplet (Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω). They systematically vary two parameters controlling the decuplet contributions: the explicit symmetry breaking mass term m_Dec (which determines the scalar coupling strength) and the relative vector coupling r_v = g_10^V / g_8^V. The grand canonical thermodynamic potential is extremized to obtain self-consistent field equations for the scalar σ and ζ fields and the vector ω and φ fields, with the strange chemical potential fixed to enforce zero net strangeness.

## Key numbers

- For m_Dec < 260 MeV (i.e., g_Δσ ≳ g_Nσ), a first-order phase transition occurs at T_c ≈ 155 MeV at μ_q = 0.
- For m_Dec ≥ 260 MeV, a crossover transition occurs at μ_q = 0.
- With a crossover at μ_q = 0 and minimal vector coupling r_v = 0, the critical endpoint is at T_c ≈ 155 MeV and μ_q,c ≈ 70 MeV.
- When nuclear matter stability is enforced, the maximum critical temperature is T_c ≈ 50 MeV with μ_q,c ≈ 290 MeV (for m_Dec = 300 MeV and r_v = 0.5).
- For m_Dec = 0 and r_v = 0.7, the first-order line starting from μ = 0 ends at a critical point T_c ≈ 150 MeV and μ_q,c ≈ 100 MeV, with a second first-order line reappearing at higher chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) model previously developed by the same group (Zschiesche et al., 2000; paper3) which successfully described hadronic vacuum properties, nuclear matter saturation, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei. It directly addresses the discrepancy between lattice QCD results (Fodor et al., 2001-2004; Karsch et al., 2001-2004) which predict a crossover at μ=0 with T_c around 150-170 MeV, and simpler linear sigma models (Dumitru et al., 2003) that predict significantly lower critical temperatures in baryon-dense matter. The results show that including baryon resonances can raise the critical temperature toward lattice values, but the simultaneous requirement of stable nuclear matter imposes severe constraints that reduce T_c dramatically, suggesting that additional degrees of freedom or different coupling schemes are needed.
