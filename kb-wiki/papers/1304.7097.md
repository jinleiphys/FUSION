# A New Model of Holographic QCD and Chiral Condensate in Dense Matter

> We consider the model of holographic QCD with asymptotic freedom and gluon condensation in its vacuum. It consists of the color D4-branes and D0-branes as a background and the flavor D8-branes as a probe. By taking a specific field theory limit, the effective coupling decreases to vanish in UV region. We then introduce the uniformly distributed baryons in terms of the baryon vertices and study the density dependence of chiral condensate, which is evaluated using the worldsheet instanton method. In the confined phase, the chiral condensate as a function of density monotonically decreases in high baryon density. Such behavior is in agreement with the expectation, while in extremely low density it increases. We attribute this anomaly to the incorrect approximation of uniformity in very low density. In the deconfined phase the chiral condensate monotonically decreases in the whole region of density.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new holographic QCD model incorporating D0-branes smeared on D4-branes to achieve asymptotic freedom in the UV, and they find that in the confined phase the chiral condensate monotonically decreases at high baryon density (in agreement with expectations) but anomalously increases at extremely low density, which they attribute to the incorrect approximation of uniform baryon distribution in that regime.

## Method

The model consists of color D4-branes with smeared D0-branes as the background and flavor D8-branes as probes, with a specific field theory limit taken to make the effective coupling vanish in the UV. Uniformly distributed baryons are introduced via baryon vertices (D4-branes wrapping S^4) attached to the D8-branes, and the chiral condensate is evaluated using the worldsheet instanton method, computing the area of the minimal open string worldsheet bounded by the D8-branes. The force balance condition between the D8-branes and baryonic D4-branes is solved to determine the D8-brane embedding, and the chiral condensate is calculated in both confined and deconfined phases.

## Key numbers

- The Kaluza-Klein mass is given by M_KK = 3r_M^2 / (2 sqrt(nu) R), where r_M is the horizon radius in dimensionless coordinates.
- The four-dimensional Yang-Mills coupling is g_YM^2 = 2 pi g_s l_s M_KK.
- The scalar curvature of the confined phase background is R(r) = 9 (r_M^3 + r^3) / (sqrt(nu) R^2 r^2).
- The Hawking temperature in the deconfined phase is T = 3 r_T^2 / (4 pi sqrt(nu) R).
- The effective dilaton coupling in the confined phase is e^phi = g_s nu^{3/4} / r^{3/2}, showing a negative power (-3/2) indicating UV asymptotic freedom.

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) and the D4/D8 holographic QCD framework by adding D0-branes to improve the UV behavior, which previously had asymptotically non-free coupling (e^phi ~ r^{+3/4}). The method for computing chiral condensate via open Wilson lines follows Aharony and Kutasov (2008), and the baryon vertex treatment follows Bergman, Lifschytz, and Lippert (2007). The authors previously studied chiral condensate in dense matter (Seki and Sin, 2012) and found that the condensate decreased at low density but increased at high density; the present model reverses this problem, yielding monotonic decrease at high density but an anomaly at very low density. The work enables further study of dense hadronic matter, neutron stars, and chiral symmetry restoration using a holographic model with improved UV properties.
