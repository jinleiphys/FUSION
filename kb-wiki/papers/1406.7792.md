# Effect of initial-state nucleon-nucleon correlations on collective flow in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the effect of nucleon-nucleon correlations on the initial condition of ultra-central heavy ion collisions at LHC energies. We calculate the eccentricities of the MC-Glauber and IP-Glasma models in the 0--1% centrality class and show that they are considerably affected by the inclusion of such type of correlations. For an IP-Glasma initial condition, we further demonstrate that this effect survives the fluid-dynamical evolution of the system and can be observed in its final state azimuthal momentum anisotropy.

# Effect of initial-state nucleon-nucleon correlations on collective flow in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Nucleon-nucleon correlations in the initial nuclear wavefunction produce a large reduction of the eccentricities and final-state flow harmonics in ultra-central (0-1% centrality) Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but the effect is only partially captured because short-range attractive interactions and 3-nucleon correlations were not included in the nuclear configurations used.

## Method
The authors used Monte-Carlo nucleon configurations for Pb nuclei generated by Alvioli et al. (2009) that include effective central nucleon-nucleon correlations, while preserving the Woods-Saxon single-nucleon distribution. They computed eccentricities for both the MC-Glauber model (with entropy density weighting, binary collision fraction f=0.14, and negative binomial multiplicity distribution) and the IP-Glasma model (with energy density weighting from classical Yang-Mills evolution). For IP-Glasma initial conditions, they evolved the system using the MUSIC fluid-dynamical simulation with Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s=0.22, and the s95p-PCE-v1 lattice QCD equation of state, then computed final-state flow harmonics v_n{2} via the Cooper-Frye formalism.

## Key numbers
- For IP-Glasma initial conditions in 0-1% centrality, ε₂ changes by almost 20% with the inclusion of nucleon-nucleon correlations.
- For the MC-Glauber model in 0-1% centrality, ε₂ is reduced by 10% with the inclusion of correlations.
- The MC-Glauber model used parameters: f=0.14, σ_NN=62 mb, negative binomial parameters r=18.4 and κ=1.25.
- The IP-Glasma thermalization time was τ₀=0.4 fm.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was η/s=0.22, with relaxation time τ_π=5η/(ε+P).
- The freezeout temperature was T=103 MeV, with chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem=150 MeV.
- A lower transverse momentum cut-off of 0.3 GeV was applied when comparing to CMS data.

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon-nucleon correlations in heavy-ion collisions by Alvioli et al. (2009) and Alvioli et al. (2011), who found small effects in central and peripheral collisions, by focusing on the ultra-central 0-1% centrality class where the effect becomes large. It contradicts the previous conclusion that correlations have negligible impact on initial collision geometry. The results enable more precise extraction of QGP transport properties from ultra-central collision data, and establish a connection between relativistic heavy-ion physics and nuclear structure studies. The authors note that the agreement with CMS data (2013) for flow harmonics is improved but not yet satisfactory, motivating future inclusion of short-range attractive interactions and 3-nucleon correlations, as well as bulk viscosity and higher-order fluid-dynamical terms.
