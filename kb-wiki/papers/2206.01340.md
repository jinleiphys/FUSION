# Information field based global Bayesian inference of the jet transport coefficient

> Bayesian statistical inference is a powerful tool for model-data comparisons and extractions of physical parameters that are often unknown functions of system variables. Existing Bayesian analyses often rely on explicit parametrizations of the unknown function. It can introduce long-range correlations that impose fictitious constraints on physical parameters in regions of the variable space that are not probed by the experimental data. We develop an information field (IF) approach to modeling the prior distribution of the unknown function that is free of long-range correlations. We apply the IF approach to the first global Bayesian inference of the jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ as a function of temperature ($T$) from all existing experimental data on single-inclusive hadron, di-hadron and $γ$-hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The extracted $\hat q/T^3$ exhibits a strong $T$-dependence as a result of the progressive constraining power when data from more central collisions and at higher colliding energies are incrementally included. The IF method guarantees that the extracted $T$-dependence is not biased by a specific functional form.

## Key claim

The paper develops an information field (IF) approach to Bayesian inference that eliminates long-range correlations in the prior distribution of unknown functions, and applies it to extract the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q(T)\) from combined experimental data on single-inclusive hadron, di-hadron, and \(\gamma\)-hadron spectra. The extracted \(\hat q/T^3\) exhibits a stronger temperature dependence than previous studies because the IF method guarantees that constraints at high temperature are imposed only by data from more central collisions and higher colliding energies, not by the functional form of the prior.

## Method

The authors model the unknown function \(F(x) = \ln(\hat q/T^3)\) as a Gaussian random field with a mean \(\mu\), variance \(\sigma^2\), and a Gaussian correlation function \(C(x,x') = \sigma^2 \exp[-(x-x')^2/2l^2]\) where \(x = \ln(T/{\rm GeV})\) and the correlation length \(l = \ln(2)\) ensures decorrelation beyond a factor of two in temperature. They generate 100 prior random function samples, compute model predictions for \(R_{\rm AA}^h\), \(I_{\rm AA}^{hh}\), and \(I_{\rm AA}^{\gamma h}\) using a next-to-leading-order (NLO) parton model with higher-twist radiative energy loss and CLVisc 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations, train Gaussian Process emulators on these samples, and perform Bayesian inference against all existing experimental data at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The correlation length is fixed to \(l = \ln(2)\), so that the random function is smooth when temperature varies within a factor of two and correlations beyond that are suppressed.
- The prior mean is \(\mu = \langle \ln \hat q/T^3 \rangle = 1.36\) and the prior standard deviation is \(\sigma = 0.7\), chosen so that the 95% credible interval of the prior covers \(0.8 \lesssim \hat q/T^3 \lesssim 15\) from past analyses.
- The time integral for radiative energy loss starts at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and the critical temperature is \(T_c = 165\) MeV, below which \(\hat q\) is assumed negligible.
- The jet transport coefficient of a gluon \(\hat q_A\) is \(9/4\) times that of a quark \(\hat q_F\).
- The analysis finds only mild momentum dependence of \(\hat q\) within \(8 < p_{\rm T} < 100\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends Bayesian inference methods previously applied to the QGP equation of state (Pratt, 2015), shear and bulk viscosity (Bernhard, 2015; JETSCAPE, 2020), heavy quark diffusion (Xu, 2017; Liu, 2021), and jet transport coefficient (Ke, 2020; JETSCAPE, 2021). It directly addresses the problem of long-range correlations in explicit parametrizations that can cause data from peripheral collisions to fictitiously constrain high-temperature behavior. The IF approach enables progressive constraints on \(\hat q\) from low to high temperature as data from more central collisions and higher colliding energies are included, and can be extended to include energy dependence of \(\hat q\) and analyses of other QGP properties such as bulk transport coefficients and the equation of state.
