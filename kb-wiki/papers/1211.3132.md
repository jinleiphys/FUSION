# Double Parton Correlations in the Bag Model

> Double parton scattering is sensitive to correlations between the two partons in the hadron, including correlations in flavor, spin, color, momentum fractions and transverse separation. We obtain a first estimate of the size of these correlations by calculating the corresponding double parton distribution functions in a bag model of the proton. We find significant correlations between momentum fractions, spin and flavor, but negligible correlations with transverse separation. The model estimates of the relative importance of these correlations will help experimental studies disentangle them.

## Key claim

The authors provide a first estimate of double parton correlations in flavor, spin, color, momentum fractions, and transverse separation using a bag model of the proton, finding significant correlations between momentum fractions, spin, and flavor but negligible correlations with transverse separation, though they note that the bag model only describes valence quarks and is only meaningful for momentum fractions \(x \gtrsim 0.1\).

## Method

The authors calculated double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) using the MIT bag model with a spherical cavity of radius \(R = 1.6\) fm, solving the massless Dirac equation for the ground state and constructing the proton state via the Peierls-Yoccoz projection. They computed spin-averaged and spin-correlated dPDFs (including unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, and transversely polarized structures) for \(uu\) and \(ud\) quark combinations, using a flexible bag treatment (bag overlap equal to 1) as their default, and compared with a rigid bag treatment (bag overlap equal to a delta function) to assess factorization assumptions.

## Key numbers

* Bag model ground state frequency \(\Omega \approx 2.043\) from the boundary condition \(j_0(\Omega) = j_1(\Omega)\).
* Bag radius \(R = 1.6\) fm used in numerical analysis.
* The peak of the single PDF occurs at \(x = \Omega/(MR)\), independent of quark flavor, where \(M\) is the proton mass.
* For the rigid bag at \(\mathbf{k}_\perp = 0\), the dPDF factorizes as \(F_{q_1 q_2}(x_1, x_2, \mathbf{k}_\perp = \mathbf{0}) = (c_{q_1 q_2} / (n_{q_1} n_{q_2})) f_{q_1}(x_1) f_{q_2}(x_2)\) with coefficients \(c_{uu} = c_{ud} = 2\).
* Normalization integrals for both rigid and flexible bag treatments yield \(\int dx\, f_q(x) = n_q\) and \(\int dx_1 dx_2 d^2\mathbf{z}_\perp F_{q_1 q_2}(x_1, x_2, \mathbf{z}_\perp) = c_{q_1 q_2}\), with the flexible bag giving a correction of order less than 1\%.
* 2\% of the single PDF integral comes from the unphysical region in the flexible bag, compared to 11\% in the rigid bag.

## Context

This work extends earlier bag model calculations of single parton distribution functions by Jaffe (1974), Benesh (1987), and Schreiber (1991) to the double parton case, providing the first model estimate of diparton correlations. It tests the common factorization ansatz \(F_{ij}(x_1, x_2, \mathbf{z}_\perp) = F_{ij}(x_1, x_2) G(\mathbf{z}_\perp)\) (finding it holds reasonably well) and the factorized ansatz \(F_{ij}(x_1, x_2) = f_i(x_1) f_j(x_2) \theta(1-x_1-x_2)(1-x_1-x_2)^n\) (finding it badly violated), consistent with sum-rule arguments by Gaunt (2009). The results enable experimental studies at the LHC to disentangle different correlation effects in double parton scattering, complementing work by Kasemets (2012) on angular correlations in double Drell-Yan production.
