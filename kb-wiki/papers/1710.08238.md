# Microscopic analysis of octupole shape transitions in neutron-rich actinides with relativistic energy density functional

> Quadrupole and octupole deformation energy surfaces, low-energy excitation spectra, and electric transition rates in eight neutron-rich isotopic chains -- Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, Fm, and No -- are systematically analyzed using a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian model, with parameters determined by constrained reflection-asymmetric and axially-symmetric relativistic mean-field calculations based on the PC-PK1 energy density functional. The theoretical results of low-lying negative-parity bands, odd-even staggering, average octupole deformations $\langleβ_3\rangle$, and $B(E3; 3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ show evidence of a shape transition from nearly spherical to stable octupole-deformed, and finally octupole-soft equilibrium shapes in the neutron-rich actinides. A microscopic mechanism for the onset of stable octupole deformation is also discussed in terms of the evolution of single-nucleon orbitals with deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims evidence for a shape transition from nearly spherical to stable octupole-deformed, and finally octupole-soft equilibrium shapes in neutron-rich actinides, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the Ra isotopic chain shows only weak octupole deformation with very shallow energy surfaces, and the exact location of non-zero equilibrium octupole deformation depends on details of single-particle spectra and pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed constrained reflection-asymmetric and axially-symmetric relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) calculations using the PC-PK1 energy density functional with a density-independent delta-force pairing, then used the resulting collective parameters to construct a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH) for 96 even-even nuclei across eight isotopic chains (Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, Fm, No) with neutron numbers 190 to 212. The collective Hamiltonian was solved to obtain excitation spectra, wave functions, and electric transition rates for both positive- and negative-parity states.

## Key numbers

- Maximum octupole deformation energy Delta_E_oct of approximately 1.8 MeV, observed in Pu-290 and Cm-292
- Ground-state octupole deformation <beta_3> greater than or equal to 0.10 predicted for octupole-deformed nuclei, with peaks at N approximately 198
- Lowest 1^-_1 state excitation energy of 0.086 MeV observed in Cf-296
- B(E3; 3^-_1 to 0^+_1) greater than 60 Weisskopf units predicted for pronounced octupole-deformed nuclei, with peaks at N approximately 198
- B(E2; 2^+_1 to 0^+_1) values increase to more than 200 Weisskopf units with increasing neutron number
- Stable equilibrium octupole deformations calculated in Th-286 to 292, U-286 to 294, Pu-286 to 294, Cm-288 to 296, Cf-290 to 298, Fm-292 to 302, and No-294 to 302

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of octupole collectivity using the same EDF-based QOCH model applied to lighter mass regions (Xe, Ba, Ce, Nd, Sm, Gd, Rn, Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, Fm) by Li and collaborators (2013, 2016) and Xia and collaborators (2017). It also follows a recent mean-field search for axial octupole deformation in actinides and superheavy nuclei by Agbemava and collaborators (2017) using relativistic density functional theory, which predicted octupole-deformed minima around Z approximately 96, N approximately 196. The present work enables future experimental searches for octupole-deformed nuclei in the neutron-rich actinide region using radioactive beam facilities.
