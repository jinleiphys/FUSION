# High $p_T$ Azimuthal Asymmetry in Non-central A+A at RHIC

> The high $p_{\rm T}>3$ GeV azimuthal asymmetry, $v_2(p_{\rm T})$, in non-central nuclear collisions at RHIC is shown to be a sensitive measure of the initial parton density distribution of the produced quark-gluon plasma. A generalization of the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) non-abelian energy loss formalism including Bjorken 1+1D expansion as well as important kinematic constraints is used.

## Key claim

The high $p_{\rm T}>3$ GeV azimuthal asymmetry $v_2(p_{\rm T})$ in non-central nuclear collisions at RHIC is a sensitive measure of the initial parton density distribution of the produced quark-gluon plasma, but the paper's analysis relies on a linearized approximation for the energy loss modulation and includes a phenomenological soft hydrodynamic component to interpolate between low and high $p_{\rm T}$ regimes.

## Method

The authors generalized the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) non-abelian energy loss formalism to include Bjorken 1+1D longitudinal expansion of the gluon plasma and finite kinematic constraints important for intermediate jet energies. They computed the fractional energy loss of gluon jets as a function of path length through the expanding medium, then used a modulation function $R({\bf b},\phi)$ to relate the energy loss at non-zero impact parameter to the central collision case. The final hadron distributions were obtained by combining a soft hydrodynamic component with a quenched pQCD hard component, using an effective fragmentation function that accounts for both the reduced energy of the leading jet and the feedback from radiated gluons.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon rapidity densities considered: $dN^g/dy \simeq 200, 500, 1000$ for central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ AGeV
- Effective radius for sharp cylinder equivalent to Wood-Saxon geometry in $Au+Au$: $R_{\rm eff}\approx 6$ fm (for Bjorken expansion $\alpha=1$)
- Soft component temperature: $T_0\approx 0.25$ GeV
- Hydrodynamic elliptic flow parametrization: $v_{2s}(p_{\rm T}) \approx {\rm tanh}(p_{\rm T}/(10\pm 2\;{\rm GeV}))$
- Transverse expansion with $v_\perp = 0.5c$ reduces jet quenching effects by $<$20\% at high $p_{\rm T}$
- Jet quenching becomes disentangled from soft component only for $p_{\rm T}>4$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the GLV energy loss formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2001) by incorporating Bjorken expansion and finite kinematic constraints, building on the proposal by Wang (2000) to use azimuthal asymmetry in non-central collisions as a probe of energy loss. The paper contrasts two classes of initial condition models: HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy, 1992) with $\delta=1/3$ scaling and EKRT saturation model (Eskola et al., 2000) with $\delta\approx 0$, which differ by an order of magnitude in initial gluon density. The results enable experimental determination of both the density and spatial distribution of the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC by combining measurements of single inclusive spectra suppression and differential elliptic flow at high $p_{\rm T}$.
