# ESC NN-Potentials in Momentum Space. II. Meson-Pair Exchange Potentials

> The partial wave projection of the Nijmegen soft-core potential model for Meson-Pair-Exchange (MPE) for NN-scattering in momentum space is presented. Here, nucleon-nucleon momentum space MPE-potentials are NN-interactions where either one or both nucleons contains a meson-pair vertex. Dynamically, the meson-pair vertices can be viewed as describing in an effective way (part of) the effects of heavy-meson exchange and meson-nucleon resonances. From the point of view of ``duality,'' these two kinds of contribution are roughly equivalent. Part of the MPE-vertices can be found in the chiral-invariant phenomenological Lagrangians that have a basis in spontaneous broken chiral symmetry. It is shown that the MPE-interactions are a very important component of the nuclear force, which indeed enables a very succesful description of the low and medium energy NN-data. Here we present a precise fit to the NN-data with the extended-soft-core (ESC) model containing OBE-, PS-PS-, and MPE-potentials. An excellent description of the NN-data for $T_{Lab} \leq 350$ MeV is presented and discussed. Phase shifts are given and a $χ^2_{p.d.p.} = 1.15$ is reached.

# ESC NN-Potentials in Momentum Space. II. Meson-Pair Exchange Potentials

## Key claim

The meson-pair-exchange (MPE) potentials, which describe NN-interactions where one or both nucleons contain a meson-pair vertex, are a very important component of the nuclear force and enable a successful description of low and medium energy NN-data when combined with one-boson-exchange (OBE) and pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PS-PS) potentials in the extended-soft-core (ESC) model, achieving a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.15 for laboratory energies up to 350 MeV.

## Method

The authors derived momentum-space representations for meson-pair-exchange (MPE) potentials by projecting the Nijmegen soft-core potential model onto Pauli-spinor rotational invariants and performing partial wave analysis. The MPE potentials were combined with OBE- and PS-PS-potentials into the extended-soft-core (ESC) model, and a fit to NN-scattering data was performed using configuration-space methods, with numerical checks in momentum space using the derived formulas.

## Key numbers

- Chi-squared per degree of freedom (χ²_{p.d.p.}) = 1.15 for the fit to NN-data
- The fit covers laboratory energies T_{Lab} ≤ 350 MeV
- The MPE potentials include one-pair (n=1) and two-pair (n=2) exchange diagrams, with coupling constants such as g_{(ππ)₀}, g_{(ππ)₁}, g_{(πρ)₁}, g_{(πσ)}, g_{(πω)}, g_{(πP)}, g'_{(ππ)₀}, and g_{(σσ)} expressed in terms of m_π and m_π²
- The momentum-space representation uses Gaussian form factors with cutoff parameters Λ₁ and Λ₂, and meson masses m₁ and m₂ in the modified Yukawa-type propagators

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange potential (NRS78, MRS89, Rij85) by adding meson-pair-exchange contributions, continuing the program to develop the extended-soft-core (ESC) potential (Rij93, RS96b, SR97, Rij99). The paper provides the momentum-space counterpart to the configuration-space MPE potentials derived in RS96b, and complements the two-meson-exchange (TME) potentials presented in paper I (RN00a). The results enable partial wave analysis of NN-scattering in momentum space and allow extension to hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon systems via SU_f(3) symmetry.
