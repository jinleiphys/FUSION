# Unnatural Acts: Unphysical Consequences of Imposing Boundary Conditions on Quantum Fields

> I examine the effect of trying to impose a Dirichlet boundary condition on a scalar field by coupling it to a static background. The zero point -- or Casimir -- energy of the field diverges in the limit that the background forces the field to vanish. This divergence cannot be absorbed into a renormalization of the parameters of the theory. As a result, the Casimir energy of a surface on which a Dirichlet boundary condition is imposed, and other quantities like the surface tension, which are obtained by deforming the surface, depend on the physical cutoffs that characterize the coupling between the field and the matter on the surface. In contrast, the energy density away from the surface and forces between rigid surfaces are finite and independent of these complications

## Key claim

The Casimir energy of a surface on which a Dirichlet boundary condition is imposed cannot be defined independently of the physical properties of the material that enforces the boundary condition; the renormalized vacuum fluctuation energy diverges when the coupling to a background field is taken strong enough to impose the boundary condition on all modes, and this divergence cannot be absorbed into renormalization of the theory's parameters.

## Method

The author replaces the Dirichlet boundary condition on a scalar field by coupling the field to a static, non-dynamical background field sigma(x) via a renormalizable interaction lambda sigma(x) phi^2, then examines the limit where sigma becomes a delta function (sharp) and lambda goes to infinity (strong). The renormalized one-loop Casimir energy is computed using phase-shift methods and dimensional regularization for one-dimensional point sources and for spherical geometries in two and three dimensions, with the background field providing a physical ultraviolet cutoff.

## Key numbers

- For a single Dirichlet point in one dimension, the renormalized Casimir energy diverges like -lambda ln(lambda) as lambda goes to infinity.
- For two Dirichlet points at separation 2a in one dimension, the force between them in the strong-coupling limit agrees with the standard boundary-condition result: -pi/(48 a^2) for massless fields.
- For the Dirichlet circle (D=2), the Casimir energy diverges like (lambda^2 / R) log(Delta) as the thickness Delta of the background goes to zero at fixed lambda.
- For the Dirichlet sphere (D=3), the leading divergence in the Casimir energy goes like (lambda^2 / R^2) log(Delta)/Delta as the thickness Delta goes to zero.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier collaborative studies (Graham et al., 2002) on vacuum fluctuation energies and challenges the standard textbook treatment of Casimir energies with boundary conditions imposed ab initio (Milton, 2002). The results contradict calculations that assume boundary conditions from the start, which claim finite Casimir energies for Dirichlet spheres in odd dimensions. The work builds on Symanzik's (1981) demonstration that boundary divergences can be cancelled by boundary counterterms, but shows that such counterterms are not available in the underlying renormalizable field theory without boundaries. The findings imply that Casimir pressures on deformable surfaces depend on the detailed dynamical properties of the material, not just on geometry.
