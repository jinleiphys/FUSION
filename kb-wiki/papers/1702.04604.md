# Quark model study of the $πN\to πN$ reactions up to the $N(1440)$ resonance region

> A combined analysis of the reactions $π^+p\to π^+p$, $π^-p\to π^-p$ and $π^-p\to π^0n$ is carried out with a chiral quark model. The observations are reasonably described from the $Δ(1232)$ resonance region up to the $N(1440)$ resonance region. Besides the $Δ(1232)P_{33}$, a confirmed role of $N(1440)P_{11}$ is found in the polarizations of the $π^-p\to π^-p$ and $π^-p\toπ^0n$ reactions. It is found that the $N(1440)Nπ$ and $Δ(1232)Nπ$ couplings are about $1.7$ and $4.8$ times larger than the expectations from the simple quark model, respectively, which may suggest the unusual property of $N(1440)P_{11}$ and deficiency of the simple quark model in the description of $N(1440)P_{11}$ and $Δ(1232)P_{33}$. The $t$- and $u$-channel backgrounds have notable contributions to the $π^+ p\rightarrowπ^+ p$ reaction, while in the $π^-p\rightarrow π^- p,π^0 n$ reactions, the $s$-channel nucleon and $t$- and $u$-channel backgrounds play crucial roles.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model can reasonably describe πN → πN reactions from the Δ(1232) resonance region up to the N(1440) resonance region, but the N(1440)Nπ and Δ(1232)Nπ couplings are found to be about 1.7 and 4.8 times larger than simple quark model expectations, suggesting that both resonances may not be pure three-quark states and that SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry is seriously broken for these states.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of π⁺p → π⁺p, π⁻p → π⁻p, and π⁻p → π⁰n reactions using a chiral quark model with an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling. They included s-channel resonance contributions (with masses, widths, and coupling strength parameters C_R determined by fitting data), u-channel nucleon and resonance contributions, t-channel ρ- and σ-meson exchanges, Coulomb interactions, and a contact term. The partial wave amplitudes were decomposed into isospin-1/2 and isospin-3/2 components to separate the contributions of N and Δ resonances.

## Key numbers

- The N(1440)Nπ coupling is about 1.7 times larger than simple quark model expectations
- The Δ(1232)Nπ coupling is about 4.8 times larger than simple quark model expectations
- The Δ(1232)P33 mass is M ≃ 1212 MeV with width Γ ≃ 100 MeV, extracted from all three reactions
- The N(1440)P11 width is Γ ≃ 200 MeV, extracted from π⁻p → π⁻p and π⁻p → π⁰n reactions
- The Δ(1232)P33 coupling strength parameter C_R ≃ 3.0 (indicating enhancement factor of ~√3 in the amplitude)
- The N(1440)P11 coupling strength parameter C_R = 23 ± 6 for π⁻p → π⁻p and 23⁺⁶₋₄ for π⁻p → π⁰n
- Reduced χ² per data point: 3.21 for π⁺p → π⁺p, 2.48 for π⁻p → π⁻p, 3.60 for π⁻p → π⁰n
- Ratio of total cross sections at Δ(1232) threshold: σ(π⁺p → π⁺p) : σ(π⁻p → π⁻p) : σ(π⁻p → π⁰n) ≃ 205 : 25 : 48

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model previously applied to π⁻p → ηn, K⁺Λ reactions (Xiao and Zhong, 2016) and γN → ηN, π⁰N reactions (Xiao and Zhong, 2015) to the πN elastic and charge-exchange channels. It contradicts the interpretation of N(1440)P11 as a purely dynamically generated resonance from coupled-channel interactions (Krehl, 1999; Doring, 2009) and instead supports a three-quark core dressed by meson cloud. The extracted Δ(1232)P33 parameters are consistent with the authors' previous photoproduction analysis and with PDG pole parametrization values. The results enable future studies of higher resonances and provide constraints for understanding the internal structure of the Roper resonance and Δ(1232).
