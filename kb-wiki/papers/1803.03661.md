# Competition between delta isobars and hyperons and properties of compact stars

> The $Δ$-isobar degrees of freedom are included in the covariant density functional (CDF) theory to study the equation of state (EoS) and composition of dense matter in compact stars. In addition to $Δ$'s we include the full octet of baryons, which allows us to study the interplay between the onset of delta isobars and hyperonic degrees of freedom. Using both the Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximation we find that $Δ$'s appear already at densities slightly above the saturation density of nuclear matter for a wide range of the meson-$Δ$ coupling constants. This delays the appearance of hyperons and significantly affects the gross properties of compact stars. Specifically, $Δ$'s soften the EoS at low densities but stiffen it at high densities. This softening reduces the radius of a canonical $1.4 M_\odot$ star by up to 2~km for a reasonably attractive $Δ$ potential in matter, while the stiffening results in larger maximum masses of compact stars. We conclude that the hypernuclear CDF parametrizations that satisfy the 2$M_\odot$ maximum mass constraint remain valid when $Δ$ isobars are included, with the important consequence that the resulting stellar radii are shifted toward lower values, which is in agreement with the analysis of neutron star radii.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including Δ-isobar degrees of freedom in covariant density functional theory softens the equation of state at low densities and stiffens it at high densities, with the hedge that the hypernuclear parametrizations that already satisfy the 2 solar mass maximum mass constraint remain valid when Δ isobars are included, but the resulting stellar radii are shifted toward lower values.

## Method

The authors extended covariant density functional theory in both the Hartree (relativistic mean-field) and Hartree-Fock approximations to include the full baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) together with the spin-3/2 Δ-isobar decuplet. They solved the equations of motion for baryons and mesons self-consistently under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions at zero temperature, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for compact stars. The meson-Δ coupling constants were treated as free parameters, varied systematically within constraints from electron and pion scattering data and heavy-ion collision analyses.

## Key numbers

- The radius of a canonical 1.4 solar mass star is reduced by up to 2 km for a reasonably attractive Δ potential in matter, compared to purely hyperonic or nucleonic matter.
- For the PKO3 parametrization, the threshold density for Λ hyperons in purely hyperonic matter is 0.33 fm⁻³; with Δ isobars included, this threshold shifts to higher densities.
- The maximum mass of compact stars with Δ isobars reaches 2.00 solar masses for both PKO3 and DD-ME2 parametrizations, matching the purely hyperonic case.
- For the PKO3 parametrization, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass star in purely hyperonic matter is 13.96 km; with Δ isobars and appropriate couplings, this radius decreases by up to about 2.5 km.
- The nucleonic direct Urca process threshold density shifts from 0.282 fm⁻³ in npY matter to 0.271 fm⁻³ in npYΔ matter for the PKO3 parametrization.
- The Δ⁻ → Λ + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ direct Urca process operates at densities of 0.431 fm⁻³ (PKO3) and 0.358 fm⁻³ (DD-ME2) when Δ isobars are included.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Δ isobars in neutron star matter by Chen (2009), Drago (2014), and Zhu (2016) that either ignored hyperons or used fixed coupling schemes, and it builds on the hypernuclear CDF parametrizations of Weissenborn (2012), Fortin (2017), and Spinella (PhD thesis). The simultaneous treatment of Δ isobars and the full baryon octet enables a systematic exploration of the meson-Δ coupling parameter space, showing that the radius reduction from Δ isobars can bring theoretical predictions into better agreement with the 13.2 km upper bound from Lattimer (2013, 2014), which purely hyperonic models with radii near 14 km fail to satisfy.
