# Pion dispersion relation at finite density and temperature

> We study the behavior of the pion dispersion relation in a pion medium at finite density and temperature. We introduce a pion chemical potential to describe the finite pion number density and argue that such description is valid during the hadronic phase of a relativistic heavy-ion collision between chemical and thermal freeze-out. We make use of an effective Lagrangian that explicitly respects chiral symmetry through the enforcement of the chiral Ward identities. The pion dispersion relation is computed through the computation of the pion self-energy in a non-perturbative fashion by giving an approximate solution to the Schwinger-Dyson equation for this self-energy. The dispersion relation is described in terms of a density and temperature dependent mass and an index of refraction which is also temperature, density as well as momentum dependent. The index of refraction is larger than unity for all values of the momentum for finite $μ$ and $T$. We conclude by exploring some of the possible consequences for the propagation of pions through the boundary between the medium and vacuum.

## Key claim

The pion dispersion relation in a dense, finite-temperature pion medium can be described by a density- and temperature-dependent mass and an index of refraction that is larger than unity for all momenta, but this result relies on an approximate solution to the Schwinger-Dyson equation where the momentum-dependent part of the self-energy is treated as a small correction (order α²) relative to the momentum-independent part (order α).

## Method

The authors start from the linear sigma model and integrate out the heavy sigma modes to obtain an effective φ⁴ Lagrangian for pions. They then write the pion self-energy as Π(P) ≈ Π₀ + Π̃(P), where Π₀ is the momentum-independent "superdaisy" resummation (solved via a transcendental equation) and Π̃(P) is the momentum-dependent piece computed from a two-loop diagram. The dispersion relation is obtained by solving p₀² − [p² + m_π² + Π₀ − (α²/6) Re 𝒮ʳ(p₀,p)] = 0, where Re 𝒮ʳ involves the analytically continued thermal function with a finite chemical potential μ introduced via the substitution iω → iω + μ in the internal lines.

## Context

This work extends prior chiral perturbation theory calculations of the pion dispersion relation at finite temperature (Gasser, 1987; Schenk, 1993) by adding a finite pion chemical potential to describe the fixed pion number between chemical and thermal freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions, as argued by Bebie (1992) and Braun-Munzinger (1999). The result is more general than Pisarski (1995), who obtained a similar index of refraction using a linear sigma model without chemical potential but only in the weak-coupling regime. The findings enable future studies of pion transmission and reflection at the boundary between the hadronic medium and vacuum, which may affect transverse pion spectra from SIS to RHIC energies.
