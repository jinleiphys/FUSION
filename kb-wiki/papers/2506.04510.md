# Reflections on Noether's second theorem and the energy-momentum tensor

> Through symmetry of the action under global spacetime translations, Noether's first theorem infamously entails an energy-momentum tensor (EMT) that is neither symmetric nor gauge-invariant. In a prior work [Phys. Rev. D 106, 125012 (2022)], I had obtained a symmetric and gauge-invariant EMT by using Noether's second theorem instead, with local spacetime translations as the symmetry group. However, the derivation therein was flawed, containing a faulty assumption about the transformation rule for spinor fields. In this work, I revisit the derivation of [Phys. Rev. D 106, 125012 (2022)], both correcting the faulty step and simplifying the derivation for broader accessibility. The end result is an EMT for quantum chromodynamics that is gauge-invariant, but not symmetric.

## Key claim

Using Noether's second theorem with local spacetime translations as the symmetry group, the author derives an energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for quantum chromodynamics that is gauge-invariant but not symmetric, correcting a prior flawed derivation (Freese 2022) that had erroneously claimed a symmetric result due to an incorrect transformation rule for spinor fields.

## Method

The author applies Noether's second theorem to local translations of matter fields, where the transformation is defined by \(x^\mu \mapsto x^\mu + \xi^\mu(x)\) with \(\xi^\mu(x)\) having compact support. The change in each field is computed as minus the Lie derivative for tensor fields, while spinor fields are treated as scalars under general coordinate transformations (contradicting the prior work's use of the Kosmann spinor Lie derivative). The coefficients \(\mathscr{A}^\nu\) and \(\mathscr{B}^{\mu\nu}\) in the expansion of the Lagrangian variation are extracted, and the conserved current \(T^{\mu\nu} = -\mathscr{B}^{\mu\nu} - \eta^{\mu\nu}\mathcal{L}\) is identified as the EMT, calculated explicitly for both QED and QCD Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- For QED, the derived EMT is \(T^{\mu\nu}_{\mathrm{QED}} = \frac{i}{2} \bar\psi \gamma^\mu \overleftrightarrow{\D}^\nu \psi + F^{\mu\rho} F_{\rho}^{\ \nu} + \mu A^\mu A^\nu - \lambda (\partial^{\{\mu} A^{\nu\}})(\partial_\rho A^\rho) - \eta^{\mu\nu} \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{QED}}\), which is gauge-invariant but not symmetric when the photon mass \(\mu\) and gauge-fixing parameter \(\lambda\) are removed.
- For QCD, the author uses the Kugo-Ojima Lagrangian (1979) with gauge-fixing fields \(B_a\), Faddeev-Popov ghosts \(c^a, \bar{c}^a\), and the gluon field strength tensor \(F_{\mu\nu}^a = \partial_\mu A_\nu^a - \partial_\nu A_\mu^a + g f_{abc} A_\mu^b A_\nu^c\), obtaining an asymmetric, gauge-invariant EMT.

## Context

This work corrects the author's own prior paper (Freese 2022) in Physical Review D, which had claimed a symmetric Belinfante EMT from Noether's second theorem but contained a faulty assumption that spinor fields transform via the Kosmann Lie derivative. The corrected result aligns with the standard canonical EMT derived from Noether's first theorem, which is asymmetric and gauge-invariant only after adding a superpotential (Leader 2013). The asymmetric EMT has implications for decomposing hadron spin into quark orbital and intrinsic spin contributions, and for interpreting the light-front energy density and momentum density versus energy flux density in systems with fermion constituents.
