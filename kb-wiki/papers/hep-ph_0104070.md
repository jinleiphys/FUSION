# Evidence for the flavor singlet axial anomaly related effects in $ϕ$ meson electromagnetic production at large momentum transfers

> The gluonic contributions to the conventional PCAC formulas due to flavor singlet axial anomaly have been instrumental in explaining the mass of the $η^{\prime}$ and providing a plausible explanation for solving the spin crisis. We show that they also play an important role in the description of photo- and electroproduction of vector mesons at low energy and high momentum transfers. We calculate the contributions of this type to $ϕ$ meson electromagnetic production in a model, which contains also a soft pomeron, and find agreement with recent CLAS data.

# Evidence for the flavor singlet axial anomaly related effects in φ meson electromagnetic production at large momentum transfers

## Key claim

The flavor singlet axial anomaly (FSAA) mechanism, mediated by a point-like gluonic G-pole exchange, provides the dominant contribution to φ meson photo- and electroproduction at large momentum transfers (|t| ≥ 1 GeV²) and low energy, where the soft pomeron model alone fails to describe the CLAS data.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for φ meson electromagnetic production using a model that combines the Donnachie-Landshoff (DL) soft pomeron exchange with a G-pole exchange arising from the flavor singlet axial anomaly. The G-pole contribution is derived from the topological susceptibility of the QCD vacuum and its couplings to the nucleon, photon, and φ meson are estimated using the Veneziano-Witten formula, the Goldberger-Treiman relation for the isosinglet axial-vector nucleon form factor, and the φ → η′γ decay width. Three different models for the φ-strange quark coupling are employed: naive quark model (NQM), vector meson dominance (VMD), and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL).

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility of the QCD vacuum: χ(0)|_YM ≈ (180 MeV)^4, from the Veneziano-Witten formula
- G-nucleon coupling constant: g_GNN ≈ -89.35 GeV^{-3}, estimated from the flavor singlet axial charge G_A^0 ≈ 0.3
- φ → η′γ decay width: Γ = 2.99 × 10^{-4} MeV, yielding |g_{η′γφ}| = 0.233 GeV^{-1}
- G-γ-φ coupling constants: |g_{Gγφ}^{NQM}| = 2.88 GeV^{-4}, |g_{Gγφ}^{VDM}| = 3.08 GeV^{-4}, |g_{Gγφ}^{NJL}| = 1.91 GeV^{-4}
- Pomeron trajectory parameters: α_P(0) = 1.08, α_P′ = 0.25 GeV^{-1}
- Flavor singlet axial form factor mass: M_{f1} = 1.285 GeV
- Photoproduction data at W = 2.76 GeV; electroproduction data at ⟨Q²⟩ = 1.45 GeV² and W = 2.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the flavor singlet axial anomaly, previously used to explain the η′ mass (Witten 1979, Veneziano 1979) and the proton spin crisis (Shore and Veneziano 1989), to vector meson electromagnetic production. It contradicts the expectation that the DL soft pomeron model (Donnachie and Landshoff 1987) alone describes φ production at all momentum transfers, and addresses the failure of u-channel exchange models (Laget 2000, Zhao 2001) to explain electroproduction data. The results enable future investigation of the G-pole contribution in ρ, ω, and J/ψ production, particularly near threshold where minimal momentum transfer is large, and suggest that angular distributions of φ decay can separate the unnatural parity G-exchange from natural parity hard pomeron exchange.
