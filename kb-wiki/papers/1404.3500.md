# On the intersection of the shell, collective and cluster models of atomic nuclei I: Multi-shell excitations

> The relation of the shell, collective and cluster models of the atomic nuclei is discussed from the viewpoint of symmetries. In the fifties the U(3) symmetry was found as their common part for a single shell problem. For multi major-shell excitations, considered here, the U(3)$\otimes$U(3) dynamical symmetry turns out to be their intersection.

# On the intersection of the shell, collective and cluster models of atomic nuclei I: Multi-shell excitations

## Key claim
For multi major-shell excitations, the U(3)⊗U(3) dynamical symmetry is the common intersection of the shell, collective, and cluster models, extending the historical SU(3) connection that was established in the 1950s for single-shell problems. This intersection holds when the Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of the invariant operators of the U_x(3)⊗U_y(3)⊃U(3) group chain, but the overlap of wavefunctions between models is not necessarily 100% even when they share the same SU(3) basis.

## Method
The paper examines the algebraic group structures of the symplectic shell model (Sp(3,R)), the contracted symplectic collective model (U_s(3)⊗U_b(6)), and microscopic and semimicroscopic algebraic cluster models (SACM). For each model, the basis states are characterized by irreps of the U_x(3)⊗U_y(3)⊃U(3)⊃SU(3)⊃SO(3) group chain, where x denotes the band-head or internal cluster structure and y denotes major-shell excitations. The overlap of wavefunctions between the symplectic shell model and microscopic cluster model is studied for the ²⁰Ne nucleus using the strong SU(3) and SU^ST(4) coupled scheme, following the methods of Hecht and Suzuki.

## Context
This work extends the historical SU(3) connection established by Elliott (1958) between the shell model and collective rotation, and by Wildermuth and Kanellopoulos (1958) between the shell and cluster models, from single major-shell problems to multi-shell excitations. The paper builds on the symplectic shell model formalism and the contracted symplectic model, and relates to the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM). The results enable the identification of collective and cluster bands from the microscopic shell model basis according to their U_x(3)⊗U_y(3)⊃U(3) symmetry, and provide a framework for comparing experimental spectra (shown for ²⁰Ne) with the common dynamical symmetry of the three fundamental nuclear structure models.
