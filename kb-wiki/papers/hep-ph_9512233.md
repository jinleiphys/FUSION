# The Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term and the Goldberger-Treiman Discrepancy

> The pion-nucleon sigma term is shown to be equal to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy at tree level. Its value estimated this way is very sensitive to the pion-nucleon coupling constant g_{pi NN}. This relation, when combined with the pion-nucleon S-wave scattering lengths, yields a new determination of g_{pi NN} at tree level. The results of a one-loop analysis are also summarized determining an allowed range for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P.

# The Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term and the Goldberger-Treiman Discrepancy

## Key claim
The pion-nucleon sigma term is exactly equal to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy at tree level, but this relation is extremely sensitive to the value of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi NN}\). The claimed equality holds only at tree level; one-loop corrections introduce a new subtraction constant and modify the relation.

## Method
The authors derive the tree-level relation between the sigma term and the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy using an effective Lagrangian approach with chiral \(SU(2)\times SU(2)\) symmetry, including a scalar-isoscalar symmetry-breaking term that keeps nucleons on shell. They then combine this relation with Weinberg's formula for pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes to extract the scattering lengths \(a^+\) and \(a^-\), and extend the analysis to one-loop order using power counting in \(1/f_\pi\), fixing the subtraction constant with muon capture data.

## Key numbers
- \(\sigma_{\pi N} = -62\) MeV for \(g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 14.6(3)\) (high coupling value), using \((m_N, f_\pi) = (940, 92.4)\) MeV and \(g_A = 1.2650(16)\).
- \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 17\) MeV for \(g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 12.80(36)\) (low coupling value).
- Using \(a^- = (9.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-2}/m_\pi\) yields \(g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 14.4\).
- Using \(a^+ = -(8 \pm 4) \times 10^{-3}/m_\pi\) with the above \(a^-\) gives \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 2\) MeV, corresponding to \(g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 13.1\).
- One-loop analysis yields allowed ranges: \(12.4 \leq g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi \leq 13.15\) and \(8.30 \leq g_p \leq 8.55\), with \(0 \leq \sigma_{\pi N} \leq 70\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous extended S-matrix analysis (Yamagishi and Zahed, 1994a,b) that enforces both chiral symmetry and unitarity for processes with arbitrary numbers of on-shell pions and nucleons. It provides a tree-level relation that directly connects the sigma term to the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, in contrast to earlier approaches that relied on unphysical limits such as the soft-pion limit (Adler, 1965) or the chiral limit (Dashen, 1969). The result enables a new determination of \(g_{\pi NN}\) from scattering length data and constrains the induced pseudoscalar coupling \(g_P\) from muon capture, with the one-loop range for \(g_P\) comparable to the value \(8.44 \pm 0.16\) from Meissner (1995).
