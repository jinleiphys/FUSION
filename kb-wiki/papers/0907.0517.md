# Cluster structures and superdeformation in $^{28}$Si

> We have studied positive-parity states of $^{28}$Si using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and multi-configuration mixing (MCM) with constrained variation. Applying constraints to the cluster distance and the quadrupole deformation of the variational calculation, we have obtained basis wave functions that have various structures such as $α$-$^{24}$Mg and $^{12}$C-$^{16}$O cluster structures as well as deformed structures. Superposing those basis wave functions, we have obtained a oblate ground state band, a $β$ vibration band, a normal-deformed prolate band, and a superdeformed band. It is found that the normal-deformed and superdeformed bands contain large amounts of the $^{12}$C-$^{16}$O and $α$-$^{24}$Mg cluster components, respectively. The results also suggest the presence of two excited bands with the developed $α$-$^{24}$Mg cluster structure, where the inter-cluster motion and the $^{24}$Mg-cluster deformation play important roles.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a superdeformed (SD) band exists in \({}^{28}\)Si starting from a \(J^\pi = 0^+\) state at 13.8 MeV excitation energy, but they hedge that this band has not been clearly identified experimentally and that the previously proposed "excited prolate" band assignment remains unconfirmed because intra-band electromagnetic transitions have not been observed.

## Method

The authors used deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with multi-configuration mixing (MCM) and constrained energy variation. They imposed constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta\) and on the inter-cluster distances \(d\) for \(\alpha\)-\({}^{24}\)Mg and \({}^{12}\)C-\({}^{16}\)O cluster configurations, then superposed the optimized basis wave functions after angular momentum projection to solve the Hill-Wheeler equation. The Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was employed throughout.

## Key numbers

* The ground state band has a peak amplitude at quadrupole deformation \(\beta = -0.4\) (oblate shape), with a rather large deformation.
* The normal-deformed (ND) prolate band has a local minimum around \(\beta = 0.5\) and the superdeformed (SD) band around \(\beta = 0.79\).
* The SD band head is predicted at \(J^\pi = 0^+\) with excitation energy 13.8 MeV.
* Both theoretical and experimental moments of inertia for the ND band are approximately 4 \(\hbar^2\)/MeV and nearly constant as functions of angular momentum.
* The \(\beta\) vibration band is built on the \(0_2^+\) state at 4.98 MeV experimentally, and the prolate ND band is built on the \(0_3^+\) state at 6.69 MeV experimentally.
* The SD minimum involves a \(4p4h\) configuration where four nucleons are promoted into the \(pf\)-shell across the \(N = 20\) shell gap.

## Context

This work extends earlier AMD studies of clustering and deformation in \({}^{32}\)S, \({}^{40}\)Ca, and \({}^{44}\)Ti (Taniguchi 2005, Kimura 2006) to the \({}^{28}\)Si system, where shape coexistence between oblate and prolate deformations has been studied experimentally by Sheline (1982) and Das Gupta (1967). The results also connect to experimental work on \(\alpha\)-\({}^{24}\)Mg cluster states observed in reactions by AGG (1990) and on \({}^{12}\)C-\({}^{16}\)O molecular resonances studied by Baye (1977) and Ohkubo (2004). The calculation enables future experimental searches for the predicted superdeformed band and for the two excited \(\alpha\)-\({}^{24}\)Mg cluster bands where inter-cluster motion and \({}^{24}\)Mg-cluster deformation play important roles.
