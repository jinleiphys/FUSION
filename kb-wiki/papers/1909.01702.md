# Longitudinal correlations from fluctuating strings in Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p collisions

> In a framework of a semi-analytic model with longitudinally extended strings of fluctuating end-points, we demonstrate that the rapidity spectra and two-particle correlations in collisions of Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p at the energies of the Large Hadron Collider can be universally reproduced. In our approach, the strings are pulled by wounded constituents appearing in the Glauber modeling at the partonic level. The obtained rapidity profile for the emission of hadrons from a string yields bounds for the distributions of the end-point fluctuations. Then, limits for the two-particle-correlations in pseudorapidity can be obtained. Our results are favorably compared to recent experimental data from the ATLAS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a semi-analytic model with longitudinally extended strings of fluctuating end-points can universally reproduce rapidity spectra and two-particle correlations in Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p collisions at LHC energies, but only when using 4 or 5 wounded constituent partons per nucleon rather than the 3 partons that work at RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors combine a wounded parton model (using GLISSANDO 3) with longitudinally extended strings whose end-points fluctuate, where hadron emission from each string is uniform between the end-points. They jointly fit the pseudorapidity spectra from ALICE Pb-Pb data and ATLAS p-Pb data at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV to extract universal emission profiles f(eta), then use these profiles to constrain the possible distributions of string end-points. Two extreme cases for the string end-point distributions are considered: identical distributions for both ends (g1=g2 case) and disjoint supports (disjoint case), which yield different predictions for two-particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- Optimum negative binomial parameter q = 0.905 for the 4-parton model and q = 0.785 for the 5-parton model, with corresponding least squares values L-tilde = 140 and 137 respectively
- The p-Pb experimental data are shifted by 0.465 units of rapidity relative to the NN center-of-mass frame
- The Jacobian deta/dy for converting pseudorapidity to rapidity is obtained from the 5% most central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV
- For the 5-parton model: <N_ch> = 5.76 <N_+> for p-Pb and <N_ch> = 5.43 <N_+> for Pb-Pb collisions
- For the 4-parton model: <N_ch> = 6.97 <N_+> for p-Pb and <N_ch> = 6.64 <N_+> for Pb-Pb collisions
- The a_11 Legendre coefficient for the disjoint case is close to ATLAS data, while the g1=g2 case overestimates data by about a factor of 4
- For Pb-Pb collisions in the disjoint case, the ratio a*_11/a_11 (intrinsic vs total correlations) is around 0.4
- For p-Pb collisions in the disjoint case, the ratio a*_11/a_11 is above 0.8

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Rohrmoser and Broniowski, 2018) from RHIC energies at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to LHC energies at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, where the wounded parton model requires 4 or 5 constituents per nucleon instead of 3. The finding that higher collision energies prefer more partons per nucleon is consistent with analyses by Loizides (2016) and Bozek (2016) of particle multiplicities in A-A collisions. The model results are compared to ATLAS data (Aaboud, 2016) for two-particle correlations, and the approach enables understanding of how intrinsic string end-point fluctuations versus fluctuations in the number of strings contribute to observed correlations.
