# Constraining of Nuclear Matter Equations of State With Rotating Neutron Stars

> Neutron stars can be regarded as natural laboratories that enable us to investigate nuclear matter properties under extreme conditions that are otherwise impossible to access in terrestrial experiments. Astrophysical observations of neutron stars provide invaluable information on existing nuclear interaction models and equations of state (EoSs) at various densities. Most studies of neutron star structure employ the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation which describes spherically symmetric, non-rotating stars in hydrostatic equilibrium. However, since neutron stars rotate fast, they could experience significant centrifugal deformation, and axially-symmetric calculations are required for accurate description of internal structure. The Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method is well known for modeling rapidly-rotating compact objects in a fully general relativistic manner. In this contribution, we report results of KEH calculations for rapidly-rotating neutron stars using EoSs based on Gogny-type finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. Our results show that the mass-radius relation systematically changes with increasing angular velocity, highlighting the importance of including rotational effects when confronting theoretical EoSs with observational data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that rotational effects systematically alter the mass-radius relation of neutron stars when using Gogny-type equations of state, with the D1M* parameter set being the only one among seven examined that avoids unphysical predictions of pure neutron matter at high density; the authors emphasize that for rotational frequencies above 400 Hz, spherical (TOV) models become inadequate for constraining nuclear matter equations of state against observational data.

## Method

The authors performed fully general relativistic calculations of rotating neutron star structure using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method, which solves the Einstein field equations for axisymmetric spacetimes via self-consistent field iterations. Seven Gogny-type finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (D1, D1S, D280, D1M, D1M*, D1N, D1P) were used to generate equations of state for npeμ matter in β-equilibrium, with the BPS-BBP EoS applied for the neutron star crust. The KEH integral equations were solved iteratively to obtain equilibrium configurations at fixed rotational frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Six of seven Gogny EoSs (D1, D1M, D1N, D1S, D1P, D280) produce negative symmetry energy at certain high density regions, predicting pure neutron matter
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with D1M* EoS: central density is 9.7 × 10^14 g/cm^3 at 200 Hz, decreasing to 8.8 × 10^14 g/cm^3 at 800 Hz
- Non-rotating TOV solution for 1.4 M⊙ gives central density 9.8 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- Polar-to-equatorial radius ratio R_ratio = 0.99 at 200 Hz, decreasing to R_ratio = 0.82 at 800 Hz for 1.4 M⊙
- Rotational frequencies examined: 200 Hz, 400 Hz, 600 Hz, and 800 Hz
- Observed pulsar frequencies referenced: PSR J0030+0451 at 205 Hz, PSR J0740+6620 at 346 Hz, PSR J1748-2446ad at 716 Hz

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of a KEH-based computational code (Kwon 2025a) to Gogny-type interactions, which are finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon forces (Gogny 1980) widely used in nuclear structure calculations. The results contrast with most nuclear physics studies that rely on the non-rotating Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation (Oppenheimer 1939), showing that rotational deformation becomes significant above 400 Hz. The findings enable more accurate comparisons between theoretical EoSs and observational data from rapidly rotating pulsars, with planned future work (Kwon 2025b) investigating the relationship between the symmetry energy slope L, neutron star radius, and rotation-induced radius changes.
