# Bulk viscosity of two-flavor quark matter from the Kubo formalism

> We study the bulk viscosity of quark matter in the strong coupling regime within the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The dispersive effects that lead to non-zero bulk viscosity arise from quark-meson fluctuations above the Mott transition temperature, where meson decay into two quarks is kinematically allowed. We adopt the Kubo-Zubarev formalism and compute the equilibrium imaginary-time correlation function for pressure in the $O(1/N_c)$ power counting scheme. The bulk viscosity of matter is expressed in terms of the Lorentz components of the quark spectral function and includes multi-loop contributions which arise via re-summation of infinite geometrical series of loop diagrams. We show that the multi-loop contributions dominate the single-loop contribution close to the Mott line, whereas at high temperatures the one-loop contribution is dominant. The multi-loop bulk viscosity dominates the shear viscosity close to the Mott temperature by factors 5 to 20, but with increasing temperature the shear viscosity becomes the dominant dissipation mechanism of stresses as the one-loop contribution becomes the main source of bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of two-flavor quark matter near the Mott temperature is dominated by multi-loop contributions from resummed diagrams, not the single-loop approximation, and exceeds the shear viscosity by factors of 5 to 20; however, this dominance is conditional on being close to the Mott line, and at higher temperatures the one-loop contribution becomes dominant and shear viscosity becomes the main dissipation mechanism.

## Method

The authors used the Kubo-Zubarev formalism to compute the equilibrium imaginary-time correlation function for pressure in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors, employing an O(1/N_c) power counting scheme. The bulk viscosity was expressed in terms of the Lorentz components (scalar, temporal, vector) of the quark spectral function, which were derived from the quark self-energy arising from quark-meson fluctuations above the Mott transition temperature, where meson decay into two quarks is kinematically allowed. Multi-loop contributions were included via resummation of infinite geometrical series of loop diagrams, going beyond the single-loop approximation.

## Key numbers

- The multi-loop bulk viscosity dominates the shear viscosity close to the Mott temperature by factors of 5 to 20.
- The constituent quark mass was computed using a current-quark mass m0 = 5.5 MeV, coupling constant G = 10.1 GeV^{-2}, and a 3-momentum ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 0.65 GeV.
- The maximal chemical potential for meson existence at T = 0 is mu_max = Lambda = 0.65 GeV, where the meson mass mM = 2*Lambda.
- The spectral functions were evaluated at three energies: epsilon1 = 0.1 GeV, epsilon2 = 0.3 GeV, and epsilon3 = 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in two-flavor quark matter within the Kubo-Zubarev formalism and NJL model, specifically building on the authors' prior work (Harutyunyan and Sedrakian, 2017) for the quark spectral function. It contrasts with earlier one-loop computations of bulk viscosity in this regime (e.g., Xu and collaborators, 2014; Ghosh and collaborators, 2016) by showing that the one-loop result is insufficient and a resummation of infinite series is required. The results enable improved hydrodynamical descriptions of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, particularly near the chiral phase transition where bulk viscosity can become large and affect elliptic flow data.
