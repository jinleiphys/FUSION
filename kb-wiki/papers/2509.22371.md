# Recent progress in modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments

> The analysis of accelerator-based neutrino experiments crucially depends on the good control of nuclear effects in the neutrino interactions with the detectors. Recently the focus of both experimental measurements and theoretical studies has moved from the inclusive channel, where only the final lepton is detected, to the more challenging semi-inclusive reactions, where one or more hadrons are detected in coincidence with the lepton. In this contribution we present our recent research of the semi-inclusive $(ν_μ,μp)$ reaction on carbon for the quasielastic and the two-particle-two-hole channels. We show some representative results and their comparison with experimental data, illustrating the robustness of the theoretical approach and discussing possible improvements to the model.

# Recent progress in modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments

## Key claim
The semi-inclusive $(\nu_\mu,\mu p)$ reaction on carbon can be modeled for both quasielastic and two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) channels, but the 2p2h contribution remains based on an inclusive calculation whose extrapolation to semi-inclusive data is questionable, and the conclusions about model performance are strongly dependent on the GENIE simulation of that 2p2h contribution.

## Method
The authors compute the semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model for the initial nuclear state, with final state interactions (FSI) treated either through an energy-dependent RMF (ED-RMF) potential or a relativistic optical potential (ROP) fitted to elastic proton-nucleus scattering data. For the 2p2h channel, they develop a fully microscopic model based on meson-exchange currents (MEC) within a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) framework, with an energy shift of 40 MeV to account for binding and FSI effects, and compare the results to T2K data and GENIE Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers
- The 2p2h $pp$ emission channel dominates over $pn$ emission with a ratio $pp/pn$ of approximately 4 for the kinematics studied at $E_{\nu_\mu}=750$ MeV, transferred energy $\omega=200$ MeV, and muon scattering angle $\theta_\mu=15^\circ$.
- An energy shift $E_{\rm shift}=40$ MeV is introduced in the RFG model to account for nucleon binding energy and some FSI effects.
- The T2K semi-inclusive CC0$\pi$Np cross section data used for comparison require protons in the final state with momenta above 0.5 GeV.
- The 2p2h cross section is computed at fixed neutrino energy $E_{\nu_\mu}=750$ MeV and displayed as a function of proton polar angle and kinetic energy, with a peak near $\theta_p=0^\circ$ (parallel kinematics).

## Context
This work extends the electromagnetic $(e,e'p)$ framework of Belocchi (2024) to the weak reaction $(\nu_\mu,\mu p)$, filling a gap where semi-inclusive 2p2h neutrino-nucleus interactions remain almost unexplored despite several existing calculations for the inclusive 2p2h cross section. The study builds on the SuSAv2 model of Amaro (2004) and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014), which connects electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering through inclusive scaling functions, and on the inclusive 2p2h calculation of Ruiz Simo (2016). The results enable future work to include initial and final state interactions in the 2p2h calculation and to extend into the inelastic domain relevant for the DUNE experiment, as addressed by Gonzalez-Rosa (2024).
