# Exploring the effects of electromagnetic fields and tilted bulk distribution on directed flow of D mesons in small systems

> We studied the directed flow of heavy quarks in small systems produced in p-Pb collisions due to both the impact of initial vorticity and electromagnetic fields. We employed a relativistic transport code to model the bulk evolution of the small systems and studied the heavy quark momentum evolution using Langevin dynamics. For the heavy quarks interaction with the bulk, we employed a quasiparticle model (QPM). We observed a large directed flow splitting ($Δv_1$) of charm quarks due to electromagnetic fields, which is comparable to the directed flow splitting of charm quarks in nucleus-nucleus collisions. However, the magnitude of the directed flow due to the initial tilted matter distribution in p-nucleus collisions is not substantial. The observed directed flow is not rapidity odd due to the asymmetry in the colliding system. The results presented in this manuscript provide an independent way to quantify the initial electromagnetic field produced and the matter distributed in small systems.

## Key claim
The directed flow splitting ($\Delta v_1$) of charm quarks due to electromagnetic fields in p-Pb collisions is comparable to that in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the magnitude of directed flow from the initial tilted matter distribution in p-nucleus collisions is not substantial; the observed directed flow is not rapidity-odd due to the asymmetry of the colliding system.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic transport Boltzmann equation solved at a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio to model the bulk evolution of the fireball in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Heavy quark momentum evolution was simulated via Langevin dynamics including an external Lorentz force from electromagnetic fields, with heavy quark transport coefficients computed using a quasiparticle model (QPM). Electromagnetic fields were calculated by summing contributions from all protons in the proton and lead nucleus, using a constant electric conductivity of 0.023 fm$^{-1}$ from lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers
- Mean impact parameter for 0-10% centrality p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV: $\langle b\rangle = 3.137$ fm
- Beam rapidity for 5.02 TeV p-Pb collisions: $Y_0 = 8.58$
- Electric conductivity used: $\sigma_{el} = 0.023$ fm$^{-1}$ from lattice QCD calculations
- The magnitude of directed flow splitting due to electromagnetic fields in p-Pb collisions is larger by about a factor of 2 than theoretical calculations in Pb-Pb collisions (Oliva 2020)
- Directed flow magnitude is larger for the $p_T$ range $3 < p_T < 6$ GeV/$c$ compared to $p_T > 1.5$ GeV/$c$
- Quark-hadron transition temperature: $T_c = 155$ MeV

## Context
This work extends studies of heavy quark directed flow from nucleus-nucleus collisions (Das 2016, Chatterjee 2017) to small systems (p-Pb collisions), where the influence of electromagnetic fields and fireball tilt had not been previously explored. The results provide an independent way to quantify initial electromagnetic fields and matter distribution in small systems, complementing recent experimental measurements of $D^0$ meson directed flow by STAR (2019) and ALICE (2019) collaborations. The study enables future investigations of the pre-equilibrium phase in small systems and the role of medium conductivity in directed flow splitting.
