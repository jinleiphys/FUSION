# Correlations between bulk parameters in relativistic and nonrelativistic hadronic mean-field models

> In this work, we study the arising of correlations among some isoscalar ($K_o$, $Q_o$, and $I_o$) and isovector ($J$, $L_o$, $K_{sym}^o$, $Q_{sym}^o$, and $I_{sym}^o$) bulk parameters in nonrelativistic and relativistic hadronic mean-field models. For the former, we investigate correlations in Skyrme and Gogny parametrizations, as well as in the nonrelativistic (NR) limit of relativistic point-coupling models. We provide analytical correlations among bulk parameters for the NR limit, discussing the conditions in which they are linear ones. Based on a recent study [B. M. Santos et al., Phys. Rev. C 90, 035203 (2014)], we also show that some correlations presented in the NR limit are reproduced for relativistic models presenting cubic and quartic self-interactions in the scalar field $σ$, mostly studied in this work in the context of the relativistic framework. We also discuss how the crossing points, observed in the density dependence of some bulk parameters, can be seen as a signature of linear correlations between the specific bulk quantity presenting the crossing, and its immediately next order parameter.

# Correlations between bulk parameters in relativistic and nonrelativistic hadronic mean-field models

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that crossing points observed in the density dependence of bulk parameters (such as the symmetry energy slope) serve as signatures of linear correlations between that bulk parameter and its immediately next-order derivative, but only under the condition that specific lower-order bulk parameters (such as the symmetry energy J and incompressibility K_o) are held fixed. The authors show this connection holds for both nonrelativistic models (Skyrme, Gogny, and the NR limit of relativistic point-coupling models) and relativistic models with cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions, generalizing a previous analysis that had only been applied to the incompressibility function in Skyrme models.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions for isovector bulk parameters (L_o, K_sym^o, Q_sym^o, I_sym^o) in terms of J, K_o, and other fixed quantities using the nonrelativistic limit of nonlinear point-coupling (NLPC) models, then verify these linear correlations numerically for Skyrme and Gogny parametrizations. They extend the analysis to finite-range relativistic mean-field (FR-RMF) Boguta-Bodmer models by checking whether the same linear correlations predicted by the NR limit are reproduced. The connection between crossing densities and linear correlations is established by expanding the function F^(m)(rho)/(3x+1)^m in a power series around saturation density and showing that if higher-order bulk parameters are linearly correlated with F^(m)(rho_o), then a common crossing density exists where f(x_c)=0.

## Key numbers

- Crossing density for the symmetry energy slope in the NR limit: rho_c^L/rho_0 = 0.47, with L(rho_c^L) overlapping 48% with the constraint L(rho_c^L) = 47.3 ± 7.8 MeV from Ref. [slope]
- For the NR limit, the expansion of L^(NR)/(3x+1) to order x^2 gives rho_c^L/rho_0 = 0.46, while expansion to order x^3 gives the exact value rho_c^L/rho_0 = 0.47 (using r(0.15) = 14.17)
- For specific Skyrme parametrizations (SV, SkO, SkO', SkT3, SkRA, Ska35s15), a crossing density in the symmetry energy slope occurs at rho_c^L/rho_0 = 0.38
- The effective mass range studied for the NR limit is 0.50 ≤ m* ≤ 0.80, with fixed values rho_o = 0.15 fm^{-3}, B_o = 16 MeV, and K_o = 270 MeV
- The ranges explored for fixed parameters are 25 ≤ J ≤ 35 MeV and 250 ≤ K_o ≤ 315 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Santos et al., Phys. Rev. C 90, 035203, 2014) which provided analytical linear correlations between J, L_o, and K_sym^o in the NR limit of NLPC models. It generalizes the crossing-point analysis of Margueron et al. (2012) and the associated Physical Review Letter, which had identified a crossing in the incompressibility function at rho_c ≈ 0.7 rho_o ≈ 0.11 fm^{-3} for Skyrme models but not for FR-RMF models. The paper enables systematic identification of linear correlations among bulk parameters by searching for crossing densities, potentially reducing the number of independent parameters needed in nuclear model construction and allowing predictions of higher-order isovector properties from experimentally constrained lower-order ones.
