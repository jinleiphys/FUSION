# Prediction of super-heavy $N^*$ and $Λ^*$ resonances with hidden beauty

> The meson-baryon coupled channel unitary approach with the local hidden gauge formalism is extended to the hidden beauty sector. A few narrow $N^*$ and $Λ^*$ resonances around 11 GeV are predicted as dynamically generated states from the interactions of heavy beauty mesons and baryons. Production cross sections of these predicted resonances in $pp$ and $ep$ collisions are estimated as a guide for the possible experimental search at relevant facilities.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of narrow $N^*$ and $\Lambda^*$ resonances with hidden beauty at masses around 11 GeV, but the authors note that the uncertainty for the concrete binding energies is quite large, of the order of tens to a hundred MeV, depending on the regularization scheme and matching point chosen for the loop functions.

## Method

The authors extend the meson-baryon coupled channel unitary approach with the local hidden gauge formalism from the hidden charm sector to the hidden beauty sector. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation using transition potentials derived from effective Lagrangians for pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon interactions via t-channel vector meson exchange, with the loop functions regularized using dimensional regularization or a three-momentum cutoff. The poles in the T-matrix are identified as bound states or resonances, and their decay widths are estimated through box diagrams involving heavy beauty meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- Two $N^*$ states predicted: one at 11052 MeV (width 1.38 MeV) from $B\Sigma_b$ channel, and one at 11100 MeV (width 1.33 MeV) from $B^*\Sigma_b$ channel, both with (I, S) = (1/2, 0)
- Four $\Lambda^*$ states predicted in (I, S) = (0, -1) sector: at 11021 MeV (width 2.21 MeV) from coupled $B_s\Lambda_b$ and $B\Xi_b$ channels; at 11191 MeV (width 1.24 MeV) from $B\Xi'_b$ channel; at 11070 MeV (width 2.17 MeV) from $B^*_s\Lambda_b$ and $B^*\Xi_b$ channels; at 11239 MeV (width 1.19 MeV) from $B^*\Xi'_b$ channel
- Binding energy for $B\Sigma_b$ channel is about 35 MeV with dimensional regularization (a_mu = -3.71), but varies from 35 to 145 MeV depending on regularization choice
- Production cross section for $pp \to pp\eta_b$ through $N^*_{b\bar{b}}(11052)$ is estimated for center-of-mass energies from 13 to 25 GeV

## Context

This work extends the previous study of hidden charm $N^*$ and $\Lambda^*$ resonances by the same group (Wu, Zhao, Zou, 2010) to the hidden beauty sector, where the much heavier beauty quark mass (about 5 GeV) leads to predicted states around 11 GeV rather than around 4 GeV. The predicted states, if confirmed experimentally, would provide clear evidence for the meson-baryon molecular excitation mechanism as opposed to the conventional three-quark description of baryons, similar to the ongoing debate about the nature of $N^*(1535)$ which has been proposed as a $K\Sigma$ quasi-bound state by Siegel (1995), Inoue (2002), and Nieves (2002). The production cross sections estimated for $pp$ and $ep$ collisions serve as guidance for experimental searches at facilities such as LHC and possible future colliders.
