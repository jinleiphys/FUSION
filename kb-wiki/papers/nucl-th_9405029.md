# Photon absorption on a neutron-proton pair in $^3$He

> The cross section of the reaction $^3$He$(γ,pn)p_{spec}$ in the energy range 150 - 450 MeV has been calculated in a quasifree two-body model and compared with a recent kinematically complete experiment. In comparison with free deuteron disintegration the cross section is increased by a factor of about 1.5 in reasonable agreement with the data for photon energies above 200 MeV. At lower energies the data deviate from the two-body behaviour and thus suggest a more important role of the spectator requiring a more exact treatment.

# Photon absorption on a neutron-proton pair in $^3$He

## Key claim
The cross section for photoabsorption on a neutron-proton pair in $^3$He is enhanced by a factor of about 1.5 compared to free deuteron photodisintegration, and this enhancement is primarily due to the compressed wave function of the quasideuteron in $^3$He rather than a simple statistical factor from the number of quasideuteron pairs. The model agrees with experimental data for photon energies above 200 MeV, but fails to reproduce the decrease in cross section observed below 200 MeV.

## Method
The authors calculated the cross section of the reaction $^3$He$(γ,pn)p_{spec}$ in a quasifree two-body model using a modified deuteron wave function for the quasideuteron in $^3$He, taken from a Faddeev calculation with the Reid soft core potential. The model includes one-nucleon current, Siegert operators, explicit pion and ρ-meson exchange currents, and a coupled-channel treatment of the Δ(1232)-isobar excitation. The absorption on the $^1S_0$ (np) pair was also included coherently, weighted by the appropriate spin-isospin probabilities.

## Key numbers
- The calculated cross section for $^3$He$(γ,pn)p_{spec}$ is increased by a factor of about 1.5 compared to free deuteron photodisintegration, compared with the experimental observation of $1.24 \pm 0.26$ (Emura et al., 1994).
- The D-state probability of the quasideuteron in $^3$He is $P_D (^3{\text{He}}) = 10.5\%$.
- The pure wave function effect (without the probability factor) would give an enhancement factor of about 3 over the free reaction.
- The ratio $\sigma_{\text{He}} / \sigma_d = 1.68 \pm 0.07$ was obtained from the proton excitation function in $(γ,p)$ reactions (D'Hose et al., 1992).
- The calculation covers the photon energy range 150 – 450 MeV, with angular distributions compared at 245 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the quasifree two-body model previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration (Leidemann and Arenhövel, 1991; Wilhelm et al., 1993) to the $^3$He system, and compares with kinematically complete data from the TAGX collaboration (Emura et al., 1994). The results support the quasifree assumption for photon energies above 200 MeV, but indicate that at lower energies the spectator nucleon plays a more important role, requiring a more exact treatment. The findings contradict the interpretation that the enhancement is simply a statistical factor from having 1.5 quasideuteron pairs in $^3$He, as suggested by Emura et al. (1994). A forthcoming paper (Wilhelm et al., 1994) will discuss the interplay of one- and two-body current contributions in more detail.
