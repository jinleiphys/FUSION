# Jet conversions in a quark-gluon plasma

> Quark and gluon jets traversing through a quark-gluon plasma not only lose their energies but also can undergo flavor conversions. The conversion rates via the elastic $q(\bar q)g\to gq(\bar q)$ and the inelastic $q\bar q\leftrightarrow gg$ scatterings are evaluated in the lowest order in QCD. Including both jet energy loss and conversions in the expanding quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, we have found a net conversion of quark to gluon jets. This reduces the difference between the nuclear modification factors for quark and gluon jets in central heavy ion collisions and thus enhances the $p/π^+$ and ${\bar p}/π^-$ ratios at high transverse momentum. However, a much larger net quark to gluon jet conversion rate than the one given by the lowest-order QCD is needed to account for the observed similar ratios in central Au+Au and p+p collisions at same energy. Implications of our results are discussed.

## Key claim

Including lowest-order QCD jet conversion rates (elastic and inelastic) in an expanding quark-gluon plasma yields a net conversion of quark to gluon jets, which reduces the difference between quark and gluon nuclear modification factors and enhances the \(p/\pi^+\) and \(\bar p/\pi^-\) ratios at high transverse momentum; however, a factor of four or larger increase in the net quark-to-gluon conversion rate beyond the lowest-order QCD result is needed to match the observed ratios in central Au+Au collisions with those in p+p collisions at the same energy.

## Method

The authors evaluated the collisional widths for quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark jet conversions via elastic \(q(\bar q)g \to gq(\bar q)\) and inelastic \(q\bar q \leftrightarrow gg\) scatterings in a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma, using the lowest-order QCD scattering amplitudes with thermal masses \(m_q = m_g/\sqrt{3} = gT/\sqrt{6}\) and \(\alpha_s = 0.3\). They modeled the expanding QGP with a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and an accelerated transverse expansion, starting at formation time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) with initial temperature \(T_i = 350\) MeV, and followed test jets through straight trajectories with momentum loss from drag coefficients (enhanced by a factor \(K_E = 4\) to mimic radiative energy loss) and stochastic conversion events based on the calculated collisional widths.

## Key numbers

- Collisional widths for quark-to-gluon and gluon-to-quark conversions are shown for jet momenta of 6, 8, and 10 GeV/\(c\) as functions of temperature (Fig. 1); the total conversion rate is larger for quark jets than for gluon jets, especially at high transverse momentum.
- Drag coefficients due to non-conversion two-body scattering are about a factor of two larger for gluon jets than for quark jets; drag coefficients due to conversion scattering are similar for both (Fig. 2, at \(T = 300\) MeV and \(T = 200\) MeV).
- Initial jet spectra for central Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV are parameterized with parameters: gluons \(A = 1440\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^{-2}\), \(B = 1.5\) GeV/\(c\), \(\alpha = 8.0\); quarks \(A = 670\), \(B = 1.6\), \(\alpha = 7.9\); antiquarks \(A = 190\), \(B = 1.9\), \(\alpha = 8.9\) (Table I).
- The QGP formation time is \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\), initial transverse radius \(R_0 = 7\) fm, transverse acceleration \(a = 0.1c^2/\)fm, initial temperature \(T_i = 350\) MeV, critical temperature \(T_c = 175\) MeV at proper time \(\tau_c = 5\) fm/\(c\).
- With \(K_C = 4\) (enhancement factor for conversion widths) the quark and gluon nuclear modification factors become closer and the \(p/\pi^+\) and \(\bar p/\pi^-\) ratios approach those in p+p collisions; a factor \(K_C = 6\) brings them even closer (Figs. 3 and 4).
- Using a starting time of 0.05 fm/\(c\) instead of 0.5 fm/\(c\) reduces the required enhancement factors by about 20%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of jet energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma (Wang, Gyulassy, Wiedemann; Mustafa; Djordjevic) by adding the effect of flavor conversions between quark and gluon jets, which had previously been considered for deeply inelastic scattering (Wang). The results show that lowest-order QCD conversion rates are insufficient to explain the observed similarity of \(p/\pi^+\) and \(\bar p/\pi^-\) ratios in central Au+Au and p+p collisions at RHIC (STAR collaboration), suggesting the need for a strongly coupled QGP. The required large enhancement factor is consistent with findings from the AMPT model (Lin, Chen, Zhang) that a much larger parton scattering cross section than lowest-order QCD is needed to describe elliptic flow and two-pion correlations at RHIC.
