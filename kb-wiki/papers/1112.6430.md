# Strangeness in the cores of neutron stars

> The measurement of the mass 1.97 +/- 0.04 M_sun for PSR J1614-2230 provides a new constraint on the equation of state and composition of matter at high densities. In this contribution we investigate the possibility that the dense cores of neutron stars could contain strange quarks either in a confined state (hyperonic matter) or in a deconfined one (strange quark matter) while fulfilling a set of constraints including the new maximum mass constraint. We account for the possible appearance of hyperons within an extended version of the density-dependent relativistic mean-field model, including the phi-meson interaction channel. Deconfined quark matter is described by the color superconducting three-flavor NJL model.

# Strangeness in the cores of neutron stars

## Key claim
The authors show that hybrid stars containing both hyperonic matter and deconfined quark matter can satisfy the new 1.97 ± 0.04 solar mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230, but only if the quark matter core is in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase rather than the strange quark matter (CFL) phase; straightforward three-phase hybrid stars with strange quark matter fail to meet the mass constraint.

## Method
The hadronic equation of state was computed using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the DD2 parametrization, extended to include all hyperons of the baryon octet with repulsive phi-meson interactions. Quark matter was described by the color superconducting three-flavor NJL model with scalar, diquark, and vector interactions, augmented by a chemical-potential-dependent bag pressure to account for gluon condensate melting. The hadronic-to-quark-matter phase transition was treated via a Maxwell construction, and the resulting hybrid equations of state were used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass constraint: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses for PSR J1614-2230, from Demorest (2010).
- Hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: U_Lambda = U_Sigma = -30 MeV, U_Xi = -21 MeV.
- Critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration: mu_c = 1047 MeV.
- Bag pressure parameters: B_0 = 40 MeV/fm^3, delta_mu = 100 MeV.
- Maximum mass for purely hadronic hyperonic star: 1.94 solar masses.
- Central density in maximum mass hyperonic star exceeds 6 n_0, where n_0 is saturation density.
- Hybrid star configurations with 2SC quark matter core reach masses above the purely hadronic maximum, with quark cores extending up to 2/3 of the star's radius.

## Context
This work extends the debate over whether neutron stars can contain exotic matter, following the 2-solar-mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest (2010). It builds on prior RMF studies by Hofmann (2000) and Weissenborn (2011) that showed hyperonic stars could reach 2 solar masses with repulsive phi-meson interactions, and on NJL-model hybrid star studies by Klahn (2006, 2011) and Blaschke (2010). The paper contradicts earlier claims from Baldo (1999) that hyperonic matter could not even reach 1.4 solar masses in Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory, and addresses the "masquerade problem" of multiple pressure-curve crossings discussed by Alford (2004). The results enable future work on a microscopic description of hadron dissociation at high densities to replace the traditional Maxwell construction.
