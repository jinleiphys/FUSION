# Correlations among discontinuities in QCD phase diagram

> We show, in general, that when a discontinuity of either zeroth-order or first-order takes place in an order parameter such as the chiral condensate, discontinuities of the same order emerge in other order parameters such as the Polyakov loop. A condition for the coexistence theorem to be valid is clarified. Consequently, only when the condition breaks down, zeroth-order and first-order discontinuities can coexist on a phase boundary. We show with the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model that such a type of coexistence is realized in the imaginary chemical potential region of the QCD phase diagram. We also present examples of coexistence of the same-order discontinuities in the real chemical potential region.

## Key claim

The authors prove that when a discontinuity of either zeroth-order (first-order phase transition) or first-order (second-order phase transition) appears in one order parameter such as the chiral condensate, discontinuities of the same order necessarily emerge in other order parameters such as the Polyakov loop, but only when the phase boundary shifts in both temperature and chemical potential directions under variation of external parameters; when that condition fails, zeroth-order and first-order discontinuities can coexist on a single phase boundary.

## Method

The authors extend the Barducci-Casalbuoni-Pettini-Gatto (BCPG) coexistence theorem by deriving generalized Clausius-Clapeyron relations for both zeroth-order and first-order discontinuities of order parameters, using thermodynamic differentiation of the Gibbs condition with respect to external parameters. They then demonstrate the coexistence phenomena numerically using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the chiral limit, calculating the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, and quark number density across the phase diagram for both real and imaginary chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- At μ = 280 MeV, the chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, and conjugate Polyakov loop all become discontinuous at the same temperature T = 154 MeV (zeroth-order discontinuities).
- At μ = 50 MeV, the chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, and conjugate Polyakov loop show first-order discontinuities (kinks) at the same critical temperature T_c = 260 MeV.
- At μ = 0 and m_0 = 0, the chiral susceptibility χ_σ diverges at T = 261.4 MeV, while ∂s/∂T has a finite gap at that temperature.
- The Roberge-Weiss transition lines occur at fixed values θ = (2k+1)π/3, where θ = -iμ/T, with the quark number density showing a zeroth-order discontinuity while the chiral condensate shows only a first-order discontinuity.

## Context

This work extends the BCPG theorem (Barducci, Casalbuoni, Pettini, and Gatto, 1990) on simultaneous first-order phase transitions to the case of second-order phase transitions, clarifying the condition under which discontinuities of different orders can coexist. The results enable a unified understanding of the Roberge-Weiss transition (Roberge and Weiss, 1986) as a family of mixed-order discontinuities, and provide a framework for testing effective models like the PNJL model against lattice QCD results in the imaginary chemical potential region where the sign problem is absent.
