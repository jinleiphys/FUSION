# Mesonic correlations and quark deconfinement

> A simple confining separable interaction Ansatz for the rainbow-ladder truncated QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to implement chiral restoration and quark deconfinement in a study of $\bar q q$ meson states at finite temperature. The model is fixed at T=0 by reproducing selected pi and rho properties. Deconfinement and chiral restoration are found to both occur at T_c=146 MeV. In the pion sector, we investigate M_pi(T) and f_pi(T) along with the exact QCD mass relation and the GMOR relation. For the vector mode, we investigate the 3-space transverse and longitudinal masses M_rho^T(T) and M_ρ^L(T), along with the widthfor the decay rho^0 --> e^+e^-. The equation of state (EOS) for the model is investigated in the T-mu plane, a tri-critical point is identified and the relationship to a bag model is discussed. The deconfinement transition in rapidly rotating neutron stars is considered and a new signal from the pulsar timing in binary systems with mass accretion is suggested. The model is used to discuss the possibility of a superconducting quark matter phase.

## Key claim

Deconfinement and chiral restoration both occur at the same critical temperature T_c = 146 MeV in this confining separable interaction model, but this result depends on the specific rank-1 Gaussian separable Ansatz and the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations; the model is fixed at T=0 by reproducing selected pion and rho properties.

## Method

The authors use a rank-1 Gaussian separable interaction Ansatz for the rainbow-ladder truncated QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) at finite temperature, implemented via the Matsubara formalism. The quark propagator DSE reduces to a nonlinear equation for the mass gap parameter Δm(T), and meson bound states are described by the ladder-approximated Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) with the same separable interaction. The model is extended to finite chemical potential μ for the equation of state, and the phase diagram is constructed from the grand canonical thermodynamic potential in a mean-field approximation.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement and chiral restoration both occur at T_c = 146 MeV
- At T=0: M_π = 0.14 GeV, M_ρ = M_ω = 0.783 GeV, f_π = 0.104 GeV, chiral quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} = -0.248 GeV, ρ-γ coupling constant g_ρ = 5.04
- The electromagnetic decay width Γ_{ρ⁰→e⁺e⁻}(T) increases with T and reaches a maximum of 1.72 times the T=0 width (experimental value 6.77 keV) at about 0.9 T_c
- The tricritical point in the T-μ plane is located at T ≈ 127 MeV, μ ≈ 120 MeV
- At T=0, the equation of state yields a bag constant B = 150 MeV/fm³ for the primary parameter set (D₀Λ₀² = 128) and B = 75 MeV/fm³ for a second confining parameter set (D₀Λ₀² = 97)
- The deconfinement transition in neutron star matter occurs in the density interval 1.39 ≤ n/n₀ ≤ 2.37, where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density
- The critical exponent for the vanishing of Δm(T) and ⟨q̄q⟩⁰ in the chiral limit is β = 1/2 (mean field value)

## Context

This work extends the confining separable Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Blaschke et al. (1997) to finite temperature and chemical potential, building on the finite-T DSE framework developed by Bender et al. (1996), Roberts and Schmidt (1998), and Maris et al. (1998). The model reproduces the qualitative behavior of M_π(T) obtained from more realistic ladder-rainbow DSE-BSE calculations by Bender et al. (1996), and the spatial screening masses M_ρ^T(T) and M_ρ^L(T) agree with results from the zero-momentum-range (infrared dominant) model of Maris et al. (1998). The equation of state at T=0 reduces to a bag model form, enabling applications to deconfinement in rotating neutron stars and suggesting observable signals in pulsar timing from binary systems with mass accretion. The model also provides a framework for discussing color superconductivity in high-density quark matter, with critical temperatures around 50 MeV as found by Alford et al. (1998), Rapp et al. (1998), and Carter and Diakonov (1999).
