# Strangeness -2 hypertriton

> We have solved for the first time the Faddeev equations for the bound state problem of the coupled $ΛΛN$--$ΞNN$ system to study whether an hypertriton with strangeness -2 may exist or not. We make use of the interactions obtained from a chiral quark model describing the low-energy observables of the two-baryon systems with strangeness 0, -1, and -2 and three-baryon systems with strangeness 0 and -1. The $ΛΛN$ system alone is unbound. However, when the full coupling to $ΞNN$ is considered, the strangeness -2 three-baryon system with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(1/2,1/2^+)$ becomes bound, with a binding energy of about 0.5 MeV. This result is compatible with the non-existence of a stable $^3_Λ$H with isospin one.

## Key claim
The strangeness -2 three-baryon system with quantum numbers (I,J^P) = (1/2,1/2^+) becomes bound only when the full coupling between the ΛΛN and ΞNN channels is included; the ΛΛN system alone is unbound, consistent with the non-existence of an isospin-one ³_ΛH bound state.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the bound-state problem of the coupled ΛΛN–ΞNN system, including all S-wave two-body channels (ℓ_i = λ_i = 0) and using the two-body t-matrices expanded in Legendre polynomials. The interactions were taken from a chiral quark model that describes low-energy observables of two-baryon systems with strangeness 0, -1, and -2, and three-baryon systems with strangeness 0 and -1. The calculation used only S-wave two-body amplitudes, but these were obtained from a full model including D waves in spin-triplet channels and coupling to higher mass states where quantum numbers allow.

- Binding energy of the strangeness -2 hypertriton: varies between 0.416 and 0.577 MeV across the range of NΛ scattering length models considered (Table 2).
- The H dibaryon appears in the model as a bound state of the coupled ΛΛ–NΞ–ΣΣ system with binding energy 6.928 MeV.
- The NΛ spin-triplet scattering lengths used: 1.41 ≤ a^{ΛN}_{1/2,1} ≤ 1.58 fm; spin-singlet: 2.33 ≤ a^{ΛN}_{1/2,0} ≤ 2.48 fm, constrained from earlier analysis of hyperon-deuteron scattering lengths and hypertriton binding energy.
- The Nagara event gave B_{ΛΛ} = 7.13 ± 0.87 MeV for ⁶_{ΛΛ}He, with ΔB_{ΛΛ} = 1.0 ± 0.2 MeV, providing the most stringent constraint M_H > 2223.7 MeV at 90% confidence level.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of strangeness -2 three-baryon systems beyond the early symmetry-based argument of Tan (1965) that discarded ΛΛN binding without considering the ΞNN coupling. It builds on the Faddeev formalism developed by Miyagawa (1997, 2001) and applies the chiral quark model of Valcarce (2005) that previously described two-baryon systems with strangeness 0, -1, and -2 and three-baryon systems with strangeness 0 and -1. The result enables experimental searches at J-PARC E07, which expects to detect more than 10³ ΛΛ-nuclei via Ξ-capture reactions on C, N, and O targets, and provides a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of light hypernuclei at physical quark masses.
