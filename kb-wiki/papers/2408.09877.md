# Collisional corrections to spin polarization from quantum kinetic theory using Chapman-Enskog expansion

> We have investigated the collisional corrections to the spin polarization pseudo-vector, $δ\mathcal{P}^μ$, using quantum kinetic theory in Chapman-Enskog expansion. We derive the spin Boltzmann equation incorporating Møller scattering process. We further consider two distinct scenarios using hard thermal loop approximations for simplification. In scenario (I), the vector charge distribution function is treated as off-equilibrium under the validity domain of gradient expansion. Remarkably, the polarization induced by gradients of thermal chemical potential and shear viscous tensors are modified, but $δ\mathcal{P}_{\textrm{ }}^μ$ in this scenario does not depend on the coupling constant. In scenario (II), the vector charge distribution function is assumed to be in local thermal equilibrium. Then collisional corrections $δ\mathcal{P}_{\textrm{ }}^μ$ in this scenario are at $\mathcal{O}(\hbar^{2}\partial^{2})$. Additionally, we evaluate the $δ\mathcal{P}^μ$ using relaxation time approach for comparative analysis. Our results establish the theoretical framework necessary for the future numerical investigations on the interaction corrections to spin polarization.

## Method

The authors derive the spin Boltzmann equation incorporating Møller scattering (2-2 fermion elastic scattering) for massless fermions with quantum electrodynamics interactions, using the hard thermal loop approximation. They apply the Chapman-Enskog expansion to solve for the off-equilibrium correction to the axial distribution function δf_A and the axial Wigner function δ𝒜^<,μ from a Boltzmann-type axial transport equation, considering two distinct scenarios for the vector charge distribution function. The collisional corrections to the spin polarization pseudo-vector δ𝒫^μ are also evaluated using the relaxation time approach for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum kinetic theory calculations (Fang, 2022) where vector and axial collision kernels of 𝒪(ℏ) were computed up to leading logarithm in 2-2 scattering for real quantum electrodynamics interactions under the hard thermal loop approximation. It addresses the "sign problem" in local spin polarization observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (STAR, 2019), where theoretical predictions disagree with experimental results. The results establish a theoretical framework for future numerical investigations of interaction corrections to spin polarization, relevant to spin hydrodynamics developments (Florkowski, 2017; Hattori, 2019; Hongo, 2021) and the understanding of Λ and Λ̅ hyperon polarization measurements at RHIC-STAR and LHC-ALICE.
