# Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions from the Quark Model

> We report on investigations of the applicability of non-relativistic constituent quark models to the low-energy nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. The major innovations of a resulting NN potential are the use of the $^3$P$_0$ decay model and quark model wave functions to derive nucleon-nucleon-meson form-factors, and the use of a colored spin-spin contact hyperfine interaction to model the repulsive core rather than the phenomenological treatment common in other NN potentials. We present the results of the model for experimental free NN scattering phase shifts, S-wave scattering lengths and effective ranges and deuteron properties. Plans for future study are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a non-relativistic constituent quark model, incorporating the $^3$P$_0$ decay model for meson-exchange form-factors and a colored spin-spin contact hyperfine interaction for the repulsive core, can reproduce low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data and deuteron properties to within about 1%, but they explicitly state this is a "proof of principle" potential with no attempt to perform a full fit to NN scattering data.

## Method

The Oxford potential combines one-pion exchange (OPE), one-sigma exchange (OSE), and a one-gluon exchange with constituent interchange (OGE+CI) contact spin-spin interaction, with meson-nucleon form-factors derived from the $^3$P$_0$ quark model using simple harmonic oscillator wave functions. Additional omega exchange with pure vector, isovector coupling was added in P and F partial waves to provide needed repulsion. The potential was fitted to single-energy NN scattering phase shifts from the SAID analysis program up to $T_{\rm Lab} = 400$ MeV and to deuteron properties, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for scattering and the Schrodinger equation for the deuteron bound state.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2246 MeV (Oxford), compared to experimental 2.224575 $\pm$ 0.000009 MeV
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.2871 fm$^2$ (Oxford), compared to experimental 0.28590 $\pm$ 0.00030 fm$^2$; the Oxford value is closer to experiment than CD-Bonn (0.270 fm$^2$), Nijmegen I (0.2719 fm$^2$), Argonne v18 (0.270 fm$^2$), or N$^3$LO (0.275 fm$^2$)
- Deuteron D-state probability: 5.604% (Oxford), compared to 4.85% (CD-Bonn), 5.664% (Nijmegen I), 5.76% (Argonne v18), and 4.51% (N$^3$LO)
- $^1$S$_0$ neutron-proton scattering length: -23.85 fm (Oxford), compared to experimental -23.74 $\pm$ 0.020 fm
- $^3$S$_1$ scattering length: 5.46 fm (Oxford), compared to experimental 5.419 $\pm$ 0.007 fm
- $^3$S$_1$ effective range: 1.77 fm (Oxford), compared to experimental 1.753 $\pm$ 0.008 fm

## Context

This work extends the quark-model approach to NN interactions pioneered by Liberman (1977), Shimizu and collaborators, and Valcarce and collaborators, using the "quark Born diagram" formalism of Barnes and collaborators for the OGE+CI interaction. The Oxford potential is compared against the highly accurate phenomenological potentials Nijm I (Stoks and collaborators, 1994), Argonne v18 (Wiringa and collaborators, 1995), CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2000), and the chiral N$^3$LO potential (Entem and Machleidt, 2003). The potential's non-local Gaussian form-factors produce a rapid high-momentum falloff similar to chiral potentials, which the authors argue is physically preferred over the behavior of potentials like Nijm I, and this property enables more straightforward application in many-body calculations using techniques such as the similarity renormalization group (Bogner and collaborators, 2006).
