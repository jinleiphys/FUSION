# QED-corrected Lellouch-Lüscher formula for $K \rightarrow ππ$ decay

> A precise SM prediction for the direct CP violation in the $K \rightarrow ππ$ decay process is of great importance in confronting experiments and constraining new physics. The state-of-art lattice QCD study of this process will soon achieve a precision that QED effects can no longer be neglected. The inclusion of QED in such calculations is planned, and the formalism to relate the finite-volume matrix element obtained from these calculations to the physical amplitude is underway. Here, we report on the progress towards an extension of the Lellouch-Lüscher formalism in presence of QED, with the goal of enabling the extraction of physical amplitudes for the $K\rightarrow ππ$ process with charged initial and/or final states.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress toward extending the Lellouch-Lüscher formalism to include QED corrections for \(K \to \pi\pi\) decay, enabling extraction of physical infinite-volume amplitudes from finite-volume lattice QCD+QED calculations; the derivation is schematic and qualitative, with full technical details and final results reserved for an upcoming publication.

## Method

The authors consider finite-volume correlation functions for \(K \to K\) in the presence of both QCD and QED, below the inelastic threshold for producing more than two pions, with the photon spatial zero mode removed to regulate infrared divergences. They perform a diagrammatic expansion of the momentum-space correlation function to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\), keeping power-law finite-volume corrections up to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha/L^2)\), and equate two expressions for the same correlation function to obtain the modified Lellouch-Lüscher formula. The derivation treats the two decay channels \(K^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0\) and \(K^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-\) separately, using chiral perturbation theory for the vertices where needed.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of \(\text{Re}(\epsilon'/\epsilon) = 16.6(2.3) \times 10^{-4}\) (Batley 2002, Abouzaid 2010, Patrignani 2016).
- The current theoretical value from the RBC and UK collaboration is \(\text{Re}(\epsilon'/\epsilon) = 1.38(5.15)(4.59) \times 10^{-4}\) (Bai 2015), obtained in the limit of electrically neutral quarks and equal up/down quark masses.
- Naive QED corrections are \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha) \sim 1\%\), but QED effects in the \(A_0\) amplitude could be as large as \(10\%-20\%\) (Christ 2017).
- The finite-volume constraint to avoid on-shell photons is \(m_K - 2m_\pi < 2\pi/L\), which for physical masses gives \(L \lesssim 5.5\ \text{fm}\).
- The perturbative regime for photon exchanges requires \(L \ll 2\pi/(\alpha m_\pi)\).
- The leading finite-volume correction to hadron masses from QED is \(m_V - m = \alpha Q^2 \left( \frac{c_1}{2L} + \frac{c_1}{mL^2} \right) + \mathcal{O}(1/L^3)\), with \(c_1 = -2.83729\cdots\).

## Context

This work extends the Lellouch-Lüscher formalism (Lellouch 2000) for \(1 \to 2\) transition amplitudes to include QED, building on the finite-volume formalism for \(2 \to 2\) scattering with QED developed by Beane (2014) and on the approach of removing the photon zero mode (Hayakawa 2008, Borsanyi 2014, Davoudi 2014). It enables first-principles lattice QCD+QED calculations of \(K \to \pi\pi\) decay amplitudes with charged initial and/or final states, which are necessary to improve the Standard Model prediction for \(\text{Re}(\epsilon'/\epsilon)\) to the \(\sim 10\%\) precision level. Alternative strategies are being pursued by Christ (2017) and Feng (2018).
