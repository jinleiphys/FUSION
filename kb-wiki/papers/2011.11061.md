# Confirmation of $g_{\rm A}$ quenching using the revised spectrum-shape method for the analysis of the $^{113}$Cd $β$-decay as measured with the COBRA demonstrator

> In this article we present an updated spectrum-shape analysis of the $^{113}$Cd fourfold forbidden non-unique $β$-decay transition in order to address the quenching of the weak axial-vector coupling $g_{\rm A}$ in low-momentum exchange nuclear processes. The experimental data were collected in a dedicated low-threshold run with the COBRA demonstrator at the LNGS and resulted in 44 individual $^{113}$Cd spectra. These data are evaluated in the context of three nuclear model frameworks based on a revised version of the spectrum-shape method and the conserved vector current hypothesis. The novel idea devised in the present work is to fit the value of the small relativistic nuclear matrix element (s-NME) driving the nuclear model calculations, which remained essentially as a free parameter in previous studies. This is done by tuning the nuclear structure calculations and making use of the interplay of $g_{\rm A}$ and the s-NME such that the experimentally known $^{113}$Cd half-life gets reproducible by the different frameworks. In this way, a best fit s-NME value can be derived for each of the considered nuclear models, which finally enters the template calculations used to perform the spectrum-shape analysis for each of the obtained $^{113}$Cd spectra. The primary analysis strategy results in significantly quenched values of the axial-vector coupling for all three nuclear models: $\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{ISM}) = 0.907 \pm 0.064$, $\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{MQPM}) = 0.993 \pm 0.063$ and $\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{IBFM-2}) = 0.828 \pm 0.140$. Moreover, with our data-driven approach one of the main shortcomings of the spectrum-shape method has been resolved. This achievement is a milestone in the description of strongly forbidden $β$-decays and adds to the indications for the existence of a quenching of $g_{\rm A}$ in low-momentum exchange nuclear processes.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the quenching (reduction) of the weak axial-vector coupling constant \(g_{\rm A}\) in low-momentum exchange nuclear processes by analyzing the \(\beta\)-decay spectrum of \(^{113}\)Cd, but the result is conditional on treating the small relativistic nuclear matrix element (s-NME) as a free parameter fitted to reproduce the experimental half-life, rather than relying on direct nuclear model calculations alone.

## Method

The authors used a revised spectrum-shape method (SSM) to analyze 44 individual \(^{113}\)Cd \(\beta\)-decay spectra collected by the COBRA demonstrator at the Laboratori Nazionali del Gran Sasso (LNGS). They fitted the value of the small relativistic nuclear matrix element (s-NME) by tuning nuclear structure calculations within three frameworks, the interacting shell model (ISM), the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM), and the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2), such that the experimentally known half-life of \(^{113}\)Cd was reproduced. The best-fit s-NME values were then used to generate template spectra for each nuclear model, and a \(\chi^2\) comparison between the normalized experimental and template spectra yielded effective \(g_{\rm A}\) values for each model.

## Key numbers

*   Effective axial-vector coupling for the ISM: \(\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{ISM}) = 0.907 \pm 0.064\).
*   Effective axial-vector coupling for the MQPM: \(\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{MQPM}) = 0.993 \pm 0.063\).
*   Effective axial-vector coupling for the IBFM-2: \(\overline{g}_{\rm A}(\text{IBFM-2}) = 0.828 \pm 0.140\).
*   Best-fit s-NME for the ISM: \(1.97 \pm 0.21\) fm\(^3\).
*   Best-fit s-NME for the MQPM: \(1.85 \pm 0.19\) fm\(^3\).
*   Best-fit s-NME for the IBFM-2: \(2.10 \pm 0.17\) fm\(^3\).
*   Reference half-life of \(^{113}\)Cd used: \(T_{1/2} = (8.04 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{15}\) yr (Belli 2007).
*   Isotopic exposure collected: 2.89 kg d, with an average energy threshold of 92 keV.

## Context

This work extends the spectrum-shape method previously applied to \(^{113}\)Cd by Haaranen (2016, 2017) and the initial COBRA study (Cobra_Cd113_2020), which could not simultaneously reproduce the experimental half-life and spectral shape. By treating the s-NME as a free parameter, following the approach of Kumar (2020), the authors resolved this shortcoming and obtained consistent quenched \(g_{\rm A}\) values across three distinct nuclear models. The results add to the growing evidence for \(g_{\rm A}\) quenching in low-momentum processes (reviewed by Suhonen 2017) and are a milestone for describing strongly forbidden \(\beta\)-decays, with implications for neutrinoless double beta decay calculations.
