# An efficient method for computing the Thouless-Valatin inertia parameters

> Starting from the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation (ATDHF), we propose an efficient method to calculate the Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia for the nuclear system. The method is based on the rapid convergence of the expansion of the inertia matrix. The accuracy of the proposed method is verified in the rotational case by comparing the results with the exact Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia calculated using the self-consistent cranking model. The proposed method is computationally much more efficient than the full ATDHF calculation, yet it retains a high accuracy of the order of 1%.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an efficient method for computing Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia that achieves accuracy of order 1% compared to exact self-consistent cranking calculations, but only when the time-odd components of the residual interaction are small relative to particle-hole energies; the method may encounter problems in regions of level crossings where this condition fails.

## Method

Starting from the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) formalism, the authors expand the inertia matrix as a series in the residual interaction, using the fact that the diagonal part of the inverse inertia matrix contains the particle-hole energies and the off-diagonal part contains the residual interaction. The expansion is truncated at second order, and the zeroth-order term yields the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula, while the first- and second-order terms provide corrections from the time-odd components of the residual interaction. The method is tested for rotational moments of inertia using the relativistic mean-field density functional PC-F1, with the residual interaction approximated by only the linear isoscalar-vector term.

## Key numbers

* The Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia for 154Sm is 55.53 (units not specified in text, but context implies MeV^-1 or similar); adding the linear time-odd vector term gives 18.99, the nonlinear vector term gives -0.31, and the derivative term gives 1.44, for a cranking frequency of Omega = 0.001 MeV.
* The expansion method truncated to second order yields moments of inertia for the 152-164Sm isotopic chain with relative deviation of approximately 1% from the exact Thouless-Valatin values.
* The enhancement of the moment of inertia relative to the Inglis-Belyaev value ranges from 1.28 to 1.39 across the 152-164Sm isotopic chain.
* Including only the linear time-odd term in the isoscalar-vector channel enhances the moment of inertia by 34%, while the nonlinear and derivative terms together contribute less than 3%.

## Context

This work extends the standard cranking approach (Inglis 1956, Belyaev) by providing a systematic expansion that recovers the full Thouless-Valatin inertia (Thouless and Valatin 1962) without the computational cost of a full ATDHF calculation. The method avoids the technical complications of inverting the RPA matrix in the presence of Goldstone modes, which arise at stationary points of the energy surface. The approach is demonstrated for rotational moments of inertia using a relativistic energy density functional, and the authors plan to extend it to vibrational masses and to include pairing correlations via the QRPA matrix.
