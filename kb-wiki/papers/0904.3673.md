# Isospin corrections for superallowed Fermi beta decay in self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation approaches

> Self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) approaches in the relativistic framework are applied to calculate the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections $δ_c$ for the $0^+\to0^+$ superallowed transitions. It is found that the corrections $δ_c$ are sensitive to the proper treatments of the Coulomb mean field, but not so much to specific effective interactions. With these corrections $δ_c$, the nucleus-independent $\mathcal{F}t$ values are obtained in combination with the experimental $ft$ values in the most recent survey and the improved radiative corrections. It is found that the constancy of the $\mathcal{F}t$ values is satisfied for all effective interactions employed. Furthermore, the element $V_{ud}$ and unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix are discussed.

## Key claim

Self-consistent relativistic RPA calculations show that isospin symmetry-breaking corrections δ_c for superallowed Fermi beta decays are sensitive to the proper treatment of the Coulomb mean field (especially its exchange terms) but not to the specific effective interaction used, and that the resulting nucleus-independent ℱt values satisfy constancy for all employed interactions, though the CKM unitarity test deviates from the shell-model conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) calculations in both relativistic Hartree (RH) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) frameworks, using density-dependent effective interactions (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3 for RHF; DD-ME1, DD-ME2 for RH) and nonlinear effective interactions (NL3, TM1 for RH). The RPA equations were solved in coordinate space with spherical symmetry, and the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections δ_c were extracted from the transition strengths for 0+→0+ superallowed Fermi beta decays across 16 nuclei from 10C to 74Rb.

## Key numbers

- δ_c values range from about 0.1% for 10C to about 1.2% for 74Rb, which are 2-3 times smaller than the shell model results of Towner and Hardy (2008).
- The average nucleus-independent ℱt value from RHF+RPA with PKO1 is 3081.4(7) s; from RH+RPA with DD-ME2 it is 3079.0(7) s.
- The chi-square per degree of freedom χ²/ν is 1.0-1.1 for all relativistic RPA calculations, indicating constancy of ℱt values.
- The element |V_ud| from RHF+RPA with PKO1 is 0.97273(27); from RH+RPA with DD-ME2 it is 0.97311(26).
- The sum of squared top-row CKM matrix elements from PKO1 is 0.9971(10), deviating from unitarity.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations by Sagawa (1996) and provides a relativistic alternative to the shell-model calculations of Towner and Hardy (2008) for isospin symmetry-breaking corrections. The results enable a comparison of CKM matrix element V_ud extracted from superallowed decays with values from neutron decay, pion beta decay, and nuclear mirror transitions, while the observed deviation from CKM unitarity calls for future investigations including proper treatment of pairing, deformation, and particle number projection.
