# Isospin Splittings of Baryons

> We discuss the isospin-breaking mass differences among baryons, with particular attention in the charm sector to the $Σ_c^{+}-Σ_c^0$, $Σ_c^{++}-Σ_c^0$, and $Ξ_c^+-Ξ_c^0$ splittings. Simple potential models cannot accommodate the trend of the available data on charm baryons. More precise measurements would offer the possibility of testing how well potential models describe the non-perturbative limit of QCD.

## Key claim
Simple potential models based on one-gluon-exchange cannot simultaneously reproduce the observed isospin splittings of charmed baryons, particularly the ordering of the Σ_c multiplet, even when including three-body terms, running coupling, or instanton effects.

## Method
The authors performed variational calculations using three potential models (BCN, AL1, AP1) supplemented by electrostatic interactions between quarks. The quark-mass difference m_d - m_u was adjusted to reproduce the neutron-proton and Σ⁻ - Σ⁺ mass splittings, and the resulting predictions for charmed baryon electromagnetic splittings were compared to experimental data from the Particle Data Group and CLEO.

## Key numbers
- Σ_c⁺⁺ - Σ_c⁰: experiment 0.8 ± 0.4 MeV; model predictions range from -2 to 6.5 MeV across different models
- Σ_c⁺ - Σ_c⁰: experiment 1.4 ± 0.6 MeV; model predictions range from -2.24 to 2.4 MeV, with BCN giving -0.96 MeV, AL1 giving -0.55 MeV, and AP1 giving 0.55 MeV
- Ξ_c⁰ - Ξ_c⁺: PDG average 6.3 ± 2.1 MeV; CLEO preliminary result 2.5 ± 1.7 ± 1.1 MeV; model predictions range from 0 to 12 MeV
- n-p splitting: experiment 1.293318 ± 0.0000009 MeV; BCN 1.38 MeV, AL1 1.16 MeV, AP1 1.29 MeV
- Σ⁻ - Σ⁺: experiment 8.09 ± 0.16 MeV; BCN 11.96 MeV, AL1 8.25 MeV, AP1 10.57 MeV

## Context
This work extends the long-standing effort to describe baryon isospin splittings using non-relativistic potential models that simulate the low-energy limit of QCD. While previous work by Wright, Deshpande et al., Itoh, Ono, Lane and Weinberg, Chan, Lichtenberg, Kalman and Jakimow, Isgur, and Richard and Taxil had produced widely varying predictions for charmed baryons, the authors show that even state-of-the-art variational calculations with realistic interactions fail to reproduce the Σ_c⁺ - Σ_c⁰ splitting. The results call for more precise experimental measurements and suggest that new contributions, such as electromagnetic penguins, may be needed to describe the confining regime of QCD.
