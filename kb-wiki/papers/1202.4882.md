# The ratio of decay widths of X(3872) to $ ψ^{\prime}γ$ and $ J/ψγ$ as a test of the X(3872) dynamical structure

> Radiative decays of X(3872) with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ are studied in the coupled-channel approach, where the $c\bar c$ states are described by relativistic string Hamiltonian, while for the decay channels $DD^*$ a string breaking mechanism is used. Within this method a sharp peak and correct mass shift of the $2 {}^3P_1$ charmonium state just to the $D^0D^{*0}$ threshold was already obtained for a prescribed channel coupling to the $DD^*$ decay channels. For the same value of coupling the normalized wave function (w.f.) of X(3872) acquires admixture of the $1 {}^3P_1$ component with the w.f. fraction $c_1=0.153 (θ=8.8^\circ$), which increases the transition rate $Γ(X(3872)\rightarrow J/ψγ)$ up to 50-70 keV, making the ratio $R=\frac{\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow ψ^{\prime}γ)}{\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/ψγ)}=0.8\pm 0.20 (th)$ significantly smaller, as compared to $R\simeq 5$ for X(3872) as a purely $2 {}^3P_1$ state.

## Key claim

The ratio of branching fractions R = B(X(3872) → ψ′γ) / B(X(3872) → J/ψγ) is predicted to be 0.8 ± 0.2 (theoretical), significantly smaller than the value R ≈ 5 expected for a pure 2³P₁ charmonium state, due to a 15.3% admixture of the 1³P₁ component in the X(3872) wave function induced by coupling to the DD* decay channels.

## Method

The authors use a coupled-channel approach where the c\(\bar c\) states are described by a relativistic string Hamiltonian, and the coupling to the DD* decay channels is treated via a string-breaking mechanism of the ³P₀ type. The unperturbed 2³P₁ charmonium state (mass 3948 ± 10 MeV) is shifted down to the D⁰D*⁰ threshold by the channel coupling, and the resulting X(3872) wave function is obtained by solving the coupled-channel equations beyond perturbation theory, yielding a mixing coefficient c₁ = 0.153 for the 1³P₁ component. Electric dipole transition rates to J/ψγ and ψ′γ are then computed using the mixed wave function and a relativistic potential model with fine-structure splittings.

## Key numbers

- Mixing coefficient of the 1³P₁ component in the X(3872) wave function: c₁ = 0.153, corresponding to a mixing angle θ = 8.8°.
- Unperturbed mass of the 2³P₁ charmonium state: E₂ = 3948 ± 10 MeV.
- Mass difference between the 2P and 1P centroids: M_cog(2P) − M_cog(1P) = 425 MeV.
- Predicted ratio R = B(X(3872) → ψ′γ) / B(X(3872) → J/ψγ) = 0.8 ± 0.2 (theoretical), with a lower bound of 0.53 when spin-orbit interaction is suppressed.
- Partial width Γ(X(3872) → J/ψγ) reaches 76.6 keV with fine-structure coupling α_fs = 0.37, and has a lower limit of 41.7 keV when fine-structure interaction is suppressed.
- Partial width Γ(X(3872) → ψ′γ) = 62.8 keV with α_fs = 0.37.
- For X(3872) as a pure 2³P₁ state, the ratio R₀ = 3.75 (with suppressed fine-structure) and Γ₁⁰(2³P₁ → J/ψγ) = 22.8 keV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel treatment of X(3872) previously developed by the same group (Badalian, Orlovsky, Simonov, Bakker, 2010-2011), which explained the state as a 2³P₁ charmonium shifted to the D⁰D*⁰ threshold. It contradicts the predictions of earlier many-channel calculations by Barnes and Godfrey (2004) and by Eichten, Lane, and Quigg (2004), which gave R ≈ 5.8 and R ≈ 5.0 respectively, and instead supports the Belle experimental upper limit R < 2.1 (2010) over the BaBar measurement R = 3.4 ± 1.4 (2009). The results enable future precise measurements of R to determine the 1³P₁ admixture and the role of fine-structure effects in charmonium states above open-charm threshold.
