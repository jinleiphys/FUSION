# Analytical model on mass limit of strange stars

> In this paper, we present a new kind of stellar model using the Nariai IV metric. This model can be used to study the strange/quark stars(which is our present interest, though it can also be applicable to neutron stars). We present a mass-radius region where all the regularity conditions, energy conditions, the TOV equation, and stability conditions are satisfied. According to our model, strange stars having mass up to $1.9165M_{\odot}(=2.81 km)$ is stable. A strange star having a mass greater than $1.9165M_{\odot}$ violates the stability conditions. This model can be very useful to predict the radius of strange stars of mass greater than $1 M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that strange stars modeled with the Nariai IV metric are stable only up to a mass of 1.9165 solar masses, and that any strange star exceeding this mass violates the stability conditions derived from the model. This upper limit depends on imposing a minimum central density of at least nuclear density (2.7 × 10^14 g/cc).

## Method
The authors construct an analytical stellar model using the Nariai IV metric in isotropic coordinates, solving Einstein's field equations for a static, spherically symmetric perfect fluid. They match the interior Nariai IV metric to the exterior Schwarzschild metric at the stellar boundary, obtaining metric constants expressed in terms of the star's mass and radius. The model is then tested against regularity conditions, energy conditions (NEC, WEC, SEC, DEC), the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equilibrium equation, causality (sound speed less than light speed), and stability via the adiabatic index.

## Key numbers
- Maximum stable strange star mass: 1.9165 solar masses (equivalent to 2.81 km in geometric units)
- Minimum radius for a given mass m: R_min = 4.4857 m km (where m is in km)
- Maximum compactness (u_max = M/R) for the minimum radius: 0.222841
- Maximum surface redshift (Z_max) for the minimum radius: 0.343188
- For a 1 solar mass strange star: maximum radius 11.2366 km, minimum central pressure 14.5567 × 10^(-6) km^(-2), minimum compactness 0.131409, minimum redshift 0.164696
- For a 1.91675 solar mass strange star: maximum radius 12.6957 km, minimum central pressure 27.9050 × 10^(-6) km^(-2), minimum compactness 0.222930, minimum redshift 0.343354
- Maximum central energy density at minimum radius: 0.00160562 / m^2 km^(-2)
- Maximum central pressure at minimum radius: 0.000223533 / m^2 km^(-2)

## Context
This work extends prior studies using the Nariai IV metric, which Moustakidis (2017) previously applied to neutron stars. The authors compare their equation of state to the SQM (strange quark matter) equations of state from Prakash (1995) and Lattimer (2001), noting that their maximum mass of 1.9165 solar masses is similar to the approximately 2 solar mass maximum found for the SQM3 equation of state. The model enables prediction of strange star radii for masses above 1 solar mass, where the allowed radius range becomes narrow, and can describe both strongly coupled quark matter (sound speed squared greater than 1/3) and hybrid stars with quark and baryonic matter (sound speed squared less than 1/3), as discussed by Alford (2013) and Chakraborty (2017).
