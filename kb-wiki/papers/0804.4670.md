# The $N^*(1710)$ as a resonance in the $ππN$ system

> We study the $ππN$ system by solving the Faddeev equations, for which the input two-body $t$-matrices are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the coupled channel formalism. The potentials for the $ππ$, $πN$ sub-systems and their coupled channels are obtained from chiral Lagrangians, which have been earlier used to study resonances in these systems successfully. In this work, we find a resonance in the $ππN$ system with a mass of $1704 - i 375/2$ MeV and with quantum numbers $I=1/2$, $J^π=1/2^+$. We identify this state with the $N^*(1710)$. This peak is found where the energies of the $ππ$ sub-system fall in the region of the $σ$ resonance. We do not find evidence for the Roper resonance in our study indicating a more complex structure for this resonance, nor for any state with total isospin $I=3/2$ or $5/2$.

## Key claim

The authors find a resonance in the ππN system with mass 1704 MeV and width 375 MeV, which they identify with the N*(1710), but they do not find evidence for the Roper resonance (N*(1440)) in their s-wave three-body calculation, indicating that the Roper has a more complex structure than what can be described by ππN dynamics alone.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the ππN system in s-wave, using two-body t-matrices obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel formalism with chiral Lagrangians. The πN and ππ sub-system amplitudes are calculated with coupled channels (πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ for the meson-baryon sector; ππ, πη, KK̅ for the meson-meson sector), and the three-body scattering amplitude is summed over all diagrams with two or more t-matrices using a factorization scheme that reduces the integral equations to algebraic ones.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass: 1704 MeV (pole position at 1704 − i 375/2 MeV) for the ππN system with quantum numbers I=1/2, J^π=1/2^+
- Full width at half maximum: 375 MeV, compared to the PDG range of 90-500 MeV for the N*(1710)
- The ππ sub-system invariant mass at the peak falls in the region of the σ resonance (500 − i 200 MeV)
- No resonant structure found for total isospin I=3/2 or I=5/2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev studies of two-meson-one-baryon systems (Khemchandani et al. 2007, 2008) which successfully generated low-lying Σ and Λ resonances, and of the φKK̅ system (Khemchandani et al. 2008) which produced the X(2175). The study addresses the ongoing controversy about the N*(1710) existence, where some partial wave analyses (Arndt et al.) find no pole while others (Cutkosky et al., Manley et al., Chiang et al., Batinic et al.) claim clear evidence. The failure to generate the Roper resonance indicates that additional channels beyond s-wave ππN (such as πΔ, ρN, and σN forces as in the Juelich model) are needed for that state. The results enable further studies of the N*(1710) decay properties and its possible relation to pentaquark candidates.
