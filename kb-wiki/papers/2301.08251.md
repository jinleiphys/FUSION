# Collision geometry effect on free spectator nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the deformed nucleon distributions obtained from the constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculation using different nuclear symmetry energies, we have investigated the effects of the neutron skin and the collision geometry on the yield of free spectator nucleons as well as the yield ratio $N_n/N_p$ of free spectator neutrons to protons in collisions of deformed nuclei at RHIC energies. We found that tip-tip (body-body) collisions with prolate (oblate) nuclei lead to fewest free spectator nucleons, compared to other collision configurations. While the $N_n/N_p$ ratio is sensitive to the average neutron-skin thickness of colliding nuclei and the symmetry energy, it is affected by the polar angular distribution of the neutron skin in different collision configurations. We also found that the collision geometry effect can be as large as 50% the symmetry energy effect in some collision systems. Due to the particular deformed neutron skin in $^{238}$U and $^{96}$Zr, the symmetry energy effect on the $N_n/N_p$ ratio is enhanced in tip-tip $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions and body-body $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions compared to other collision orientations in the same collision system. Our study may shed light on probing deformed neutron skin by selecting desired configurations in high-energy collisions with deformed nuclei.

# Collision geometry effect on free spectator nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The yield ratio N_n/N_p of free spectator neutrons to protons is sensitive to both the average neutron-skin thickness and the polar angular distribution of the neutron skin in deformed nuclei; the collision geometry effect on this ratio can be as large as 50% the symmetry energy effect, but this enhancement depends on selecting specific collision orientations (tip-tip for 238U, body-body for 96Zr) due to the particular deformed neutron skin shapes.

## Method
Deformed nucleon density distributions for 96Zr, 197Au, and 238U were obtained from constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations using two values of the symmetry energy slope parameter L (30 MeV and 120 MeV). The Monte-Carlo Glauber model was used to simulate central collisions (impact parameter b=0) at RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for 96Zr and 197Au, 193 GeV for 238U), with spectator nucleons identified and then processed through a coalescence model (minimum spanning tree for heavy clusters, Wigner function for light clusters) and the GEMINI deexcitation code to determine the final free spectator nucleon yields.

## Key numbers
- For 96Zr+96Zr at 200 GeV: N_n/N_p = 2.214 +/- 0.002 (L=30 MeV, random orientation) vs 2.478 +/- 0.003 (L=120 MeV, random orientation); difference Delta = 0.263 +/- 0.004
- For 96Zr+96Zr body-body collisions: N_n/N_p = 2.234 +/- 0.002 (L=30 MeV) vs 2.518 +/- 0.003 (L=120 MeV); Delta = 0.284 +/- 0.004 (about 8% larger than random orientation)
- For 238U+238U at 193 GeV with beta_4=0, tip-tip collisions: N_n/N_p = 3.355 +/- 0.004 (L=30 MeV) vs 4.202 +/- 0.005 (L=120 MeV); Delta = 0.847 +/- 0.006 (about 13% larger than random orientation)
- For 238U+238U with beta_4=0.17, tip-tip collisions: N_n/N_p = 3.167 +/- 0.003 (L=30 MeV) vs 3.987 +/- 0.004 (L=120 MeV); Delta = 0.818 +/- 0.005
- For 197Au+197Au at 200 GeV: N_n/N_p differences between L values are similar across all collision configurations (Delta ~0.52-0.54)
- Average neutron-skin thickness for 238U (beta_2=0.28, beta_4=0): 0.156 fm (L=30 MeV) and 0.291 fm (L=120 MeV)
- Average neutron-skin thickness for 96Zr (beta_2=0.06, beta_3=0.2): 0.145 fm (L=30 MeV) and 0.227 fm (L=120 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous proposals that free spectator neutrons in ultracentral collisions (Liu 2022) and the N_n/N_p ratio (Liu 2022) are robust probes of neutron-skin thickness, by incorporating the effects of nuclear deformation and collision geometry. It builds on studies of deformed nuclei in heavy-ion collisions, including the quadrupole deformation of 238U (Moller 2015) and the octupole deformation of 96Zr (Zhang 2021), as well as isobaric collision proposals (Li 2019, Jia 2021, Xu 2021). The results suggest that selecting tip-tip 238U+238U or body-body 96Zr+96Zr collisions could provide enhanced sensitivity to the symmetry energy slope parameter L compared to random-orientation collisions, motivating experimental triggers for specific collision geometries at RHIC.
