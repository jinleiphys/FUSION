# Probing nucleon strange asymmetry from charm production in neutrino deep inelastic scattering

> We propose a means to detect the nucleon strange quark-antiquark asymmetry, which is predicted as a non-perturbative effect, but still unchecked directly by available experiments. The difference for the $D(c\bar{q})$ and $\bar{D}(\bar{c}q)$ meson production cross sections in neutrino and antineutrino induced charged current deep inelastic scattering is illustrated to be sensitive to the nucleon strange asymmetry. Prospect is given and the effect due to the light quark fragmentation is also discussed for the extraction of the strange asymmetry in future experiments.

# Probing nucleon strange asymmetry from charm production in neutrino deep inelastic scattering

## Key claim
The difference between $D(c\bar{q})$ and $\bar{D}(\bar{c}q)$ meson production cross sections in neutrino and antineutrino charged current deep inelastic scattering is sensitive to the nucleon strange quark-antiquark asymmetry, but the extraction of this asymmetry may be complicated by light quark fragmentation effects which contribute in opposite directions for charged and neutral charm meson production.

## Method
The authors perform a leading-order analysis of charged current charm production in neutrino and antineutrino deep inelastic scattering on isoscalar targets, using the slow-rescaling prescription to account for the charm quark mass. They compute the cross section difference $f_{H^+}-f_{H^-}$ between $D^+$ (or $D^0$) production in neutrino scattering and $D^-$ (or $\bar D^0$) production in antineutrino scattering, and estimate the fractional contribution from strange asymmetry using model predictions from Ding-Ma (2004), Alwall-Ingelman (2004), Ding-Xu-Ma (2004), and Wakamatsu (2004). They also estimate the light quark fragmentation contribution using prompt like-sign dimuon data from the CDHSW experiment.

## Key numbers
- The strange asymmetry contributes $12\%\sim 40\%$ to the cross section difference $f_{H^+}-f_{H^-}$, based on model calculations from Ding-Ma (2004), Alwall-Ingelman (2004), Ding-Xu-Ma (2004), and Wakamatsu (2004) that can explain the NuTeV anomaly.
- The ratio $R\equiv\int d\xi(f_{H^+}-f_{H^-})/\int d\xi(f_{H^+}+f_{H^-})$ is estimated to be about $20\%$ for $2S^-/Q_V=0.007$ and $25\%$ for $2S^-/Q_V=0.022$.
- The light quark fragmentation contribution to the opposite-sign dimuon cross section difference is estimated as $P_{\mathrm{LQF}}=-(6.1^{+3.5}_{-3.1})\%$, using CDHSW data with $\sigma_{\mu^{-}\mu^{-}}/\sigma_{\mu^{-}\mu^{+}}=(3.5\pm 1.6)\%$ for $p_\mu>6$ GeV and $100<E_{\mathrm{vis}}<200$ GeV.
- The CCFR experiment had 5030 $\nu_\mu$ induced dimuon events and 1060 $\bar\nu_\mu$ induced events; the NuTeV experiment had 5102 $\nu_\mu$ induced events and 1458 $\bar\nu_\mu$ induced events.
- The CHORUS experiment reported about 94000 neutrino CC events with about 2000 charm events observed.

## Context
This work extends previous efforts to measure the strange quark-antiquark asymmetry, which has been suggested as a mechanism to explain the NuTeV anomaly (NuTeV Collaboration, 2001, 2002). Prior approaches include global analysis of DIS data (Olness et al., 2003; Barone et al., 2000) which favor an asymmetric strange sea, and the CCFR next-to-leading-order analysis of neutrino-induced dimuon production (CCFR Collaboration, 1995) which favors a symmetric strange sea. The authors propose that inclusive measurement of charged and neutral charm production in neutrino and antineutrino charged current DIS with emulsion targets (such as CHORUS) can separate the strange asymmetry from light quark fragmentation effects, which contribute oppositely for $D^\pm$ and $D^0$ ($\bar D^0$) productions.
