# Characterizing Proton-Proton Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider with Thermal Properties

> High-multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies have created a new domain of research to look for a possible formation of quark-gluon plasma in these events. In this paper, we estimate various thermal properties of the matter formed in pp collisions at the LHC energies, such as mean free path, isobaric expansivity, thermal pressure, and heat capacity using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution function. Particle species-dependent mean free path and isobaric expansivity are studied as functions of final state charged particle multiplicity for pp collisions at the center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV. The effects of degree of non-extensivity, baryochemical potential, and temperature on these thermal properties are studied. The findings are compared with the theoretical expectations.

## Key claim
The paper estimates thermal properties (mean free path, isobaric expansivity, thermal pressure, heat capacity) of matter formed in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution function, but the physical interpretation of these thermodynamic quantities for low-multiplicity systems must be treated with caution.

## Method
The authors used a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution function to describe identified particle transverse momentum spectra from pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV. From fits to experimental data, they extracted temperature (T) and non-extensive parameter (q) values, which were then used as inputs to calculate mean free path (via number density and a hard-core hadron scattering cross section of 11.3 mb), isobaric expansivity, thermal pressure, and heat capacity using standard thermodynamic relations. The calculations included all hadrons up to a mass cutoff of 1.5 GeV, with baryochemical potential μB = 0 for LHC conditions except when studying its effects.

## Key numbers
- Mean free path for high-multiplicity pp collisions attains a lower asymptotic value between (1–10) fm for higher event multiplicities.
- A threshold in final state event multiplicity of ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ∼ (10–15) is found, after which the mean free path becomes independent of particle species.
- For isobaric expansivity, a threshold of ⟨dNch/dη⟩ > 10 is observed, after which the expansivity converges to the same value for all identified particle species.
- The hard-core hadron scattering cross section used is σ = 11.3 mb, derived from a hard-core hadron radius rh = 0.3 fm.
- Water at 30 °C has an isobaric expansivity of about 3.515 × 10^9 GeV^{-1}, given for comparison with the hadron gas system.

## Context
This work extends previous studies using Tsallis non-extensive statistics to characterize small collision systems (Deb 2019, Azmi 2019) and builds on observations of heavy-ion-like signatures in high-multiplicity pp collisions at the LHC (Nature 2017). The results are compared with theoretical expectations from models like PYTHIA8 (with color reconnection and rope hadronization) and EPOS (with hydrodynamic evolution). The findings enable future investigations of the QCD phase transition and thermalization in small systems, particularly the role of event multiplicity as a classifier for possible QGP droplet formation.
