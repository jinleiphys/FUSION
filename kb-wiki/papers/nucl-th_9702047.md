# Neutron-proton pairing in the BCS approach

> We investigate the BCS treatment of neutron-proton pairing involving time-reversed orbits. We conclude that an isospin-symmetric hamiltonian, treated with the help of the generalized Bogolyubov transformation, fails to describe the ground state pairing properties correctly. In order for the np isovector pairs to coexist with the like-particle pairs, one has to break the isospin symmetry of the hamiltonian by artificially increasing the strength of np pairing interaction above its isospin symmetric value. We conjecture that the np isovector pairing represents part (or most) of the congruence energy (Wigner term) in nuclear masses.

# Neutron-proton pairing in the BCS approach

## Key claim

The generalized Bogolyubov transformation fails to describe isovector neutron-proton pairing when an isospin-symmetric Hamiltonian is used, because the isospin-symmetric value of the np pairing strength lies below the critical value needed for the phase transition from pure like-particle pairing to coexistence of like-particle and np pairs. To obtain nonvanishing np pairing in the BCS approach, one must break isospin symmetry by artificially increasing the np pairing strength above its isospin-symmetric value, and the resulting extra binding may represent part or most of the congruence energy (Wigner term) in nuclear masses.

## Method

The authors solve the monopole pairing Hamiltonian using the generalized Bogolyubov transformation with a 4x4 transformation matrix that mixes neutron and proton creation and annihilation operators into two quasiparticle types. They minimize the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the quasiparticle vacuum while conserving neutron and proton numbers on average, using the Newton-Raphson method, and compare the BCS results to exact diagonalizations in the zero-seniority subspace for one-level and two-level models. The extra binding energy from np pairing is computed as the difference between ground state energies with and without the np pairing interaction, and compared to the empirical congruence energy formula of Myers and Swiatecki (1997).

## Key numbers

- The critical point for the BCS phase transition occurs at G_np/G_pair = 1.05 for the one-level case with Omega = 11, N = 6, Z = 4, and G_pair = 0.242 MeV.
- The binding energy gain from np pairing in the one-level system with pure isovector interaction is Delta E = -G_pair * Z/2, compared to the leading term -G_pair * Omega * (N+Z)/2.
- In the BCS approach for real nuclei with the ratio G_np/G_pair fixed so that Delta_np is reasonable at N approx Z, all traces of isovector np pairing disappear for N - Z >= 6.
- The empirical congruence energy formula is C(I) = -10 * exp(-4.2 * |I|) MeV, where I = (N - Z)/A (Myers and Swiatecki 1997).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of np pairing in the BCS framework by Goswami and Kisslinger (1965) and the exact SO(5) model solutions by Engel, Langanke, and Vogel (1996). It directly addresses the controversy between exact solutions showing np pairing in isospin-symmetric Hamiltonians and the BCS method's failure to reproduce this behavior, as previously noted by Camiz, Covello, and Jean (1966) for the one-level case and by Engel, Pittel, Stoitsov, Vogel, and Dukelsky (1997) and Satula and Wyss (1996) more generally. The paper proposes that isovector T=1 np pairing provides an alternative microscopic origin for the Wigner term (congruence energy) to the T=0 pairing mechanism suggested by Satula and Wyss (1996), and enables further investigation of whether this contribution is the dominant one.
