# Beta decay of halo nuclei

> Beta decay is a well-established and efficient probe of nuclear structure and provides important information also for halo nuclei. Detailed decay studies of near-dripline nuclei are challenging, but are now feasible due to the technical progress during the last decades in isotope production as well as detection capabilities. The halo structure can change the dynamics of the decay process, most noticably when decays proceed directly to continuum states as seems to be the case in beta-delayed deuteron emission, a process only observed until now for the halo nuclei $^6$He and $^{11}$Li. The pronounced clustering in halo states also leaves an imprint on the decay patterns, although the isospin symmetry remains important; in several cases the isobaric analogue states of halo states have also been established. Beta-delayed particle emission channels can be expected to dominate the decays of heavier halo nuclei, if not in terms of branching ratio than certainly in terms of beta strength. A remaining challenge is to find experimental as well as theoretical tools to study the multi-neutron final states that appear for most neutron dripline nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper notes that beta-delayed particle emission channels can be expected to dominate the decays of heavier halo nuclei, if not in terms of branching ratio then certainly in terms of beta strength.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical studies of beta decay in halo nuclei, focusing on how the halo structure affects decay patterns. It examines beta-delayed particle emission channels, the role of isospin symmetry and isobaric analogue states, and the influence of cluster structure and spatial extension on transition rates. The review covers specific halo nuclei including \(^6\)He, \(^8\)He, and \(^{11}\)Li, discussing experimental data from implantation studies, optical TPC measurements, and R-matrix analyses.

## Key numbers

- The beta-delayed deuteron branching ratio for \(^6\)He above 350 keV laboratory deuteron energy is \((1.65 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-6}\) (Raa09) and above 100 keV is \((2.78 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.17) \times 10^{-6}\) (Pfu15)
- The observed integral beta strength for the beta-delayed deuteron channel in \(^6\)He is \(1.6 \times 10^{-3}\)
- The total observed beta strength for the beta-delayed deuteron channel in \(^{11}\)Li is 0.73
- The \(B(GT)\) for the \(^6\)He ground state transition to \(^6\)Li is 4.67(2) (Gne21)
- The experimental value for \(\mathcal{T}\) in the ft-relation is 6144(4) s (Har20)
- The experimental ratio \(g_A/g_V = -1.2754(13)\) (PDG20)
- The Q-value relations for beta-delayed deuteron emission from neutron halos: \(Q_{\beta d} = 3007 \,\mathrm{keV} - S_{2n}\) and \(Q_{\beta p} = 782 \,\mathrm{keV} - S_n\)
- For EC decays of proton halos: \(Q_{EC d} = 1442 \,\mathrm{keV} - S_{2p}\)

## Context

This review extends earlier work on halo nuclei summarized in reviews by Fre12, Han95, Jen04, Rii94, Rii13, Tan96, and Tan13. The paper builds on the suggestion by Lindhard (Han87a) that two-neutron halos may beta decay into a deuteron with the core as a spectator. The work enables future studies of multi-neutron final states that appear for most neutron dripline nuclei, and identifies the need for experimental and theoretical tools to study these channels. The review also connects to earlier calculations by Suzuki (Suz94, Suz97) on the decay rate of \(^{11}\)Li and to the R-matrix framework developed by Barker (Bar69, Bar88) for treating beta-delayed particle emission through broad or overlapping resonances.
