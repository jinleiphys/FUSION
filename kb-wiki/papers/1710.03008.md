# $\mathcalα$ decay properties of nucleus $^{297}$Og within two-potential approach

> The $\mathcalα$ decay half-life of unknown nucleus $^{297}$Og is predicted within two-potential approach, and $\mathcalα$ preformation probabilities of 64 odd-$A$ nuclei in the region of proton numbers $82<Z<126$ and neutron numbers $152<N<184$ from $^{251}$Cf to $^{295}$Og are extracted. In addition, based on the latest experimental data, a new set of parameters for $\mathcalα$ preformation probabilities considering the shell effect and proton-neutron interaction are obtained. The predicted $\mathcalα$ decay half-life of $^{297}$Og is 0.16 ms within a factor of 4.97 as well as the spin and parity of ground states for nuclei $^{269}$Sg, $^{285}$Fl and $^{293}$Lv are $3/2^+$, $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$, respectively.

## Key claim

The alpha decay half-life of the unknown superheavy nucleus 297Og is predicted to be 0.16 ms, but this value is uncertain within a factor of 4.97 based on the standard deviation of the model's predictions for 64 known odd-A nuclei in the same region.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) to calculate alpha decay half-lives, with the nuclear potential described by a cosh parametrized form and isospin-dependent parameters. They extracted alpha preformation probabilities for 64 odd-A nuclei from 251Cf to 295Og by comparing calculated half-lives (assuming a constant preformation factor P0=0.35) with experimental data from NUBASE2016 and AME2016. A new set of parameters for an analytic formula describing the preformation probability as a function of proton number, neutron number, and mass number was fitted, incorporating shell effects and proton-neutron interactions.

## Key numbers

- Predicted alpha decay half-life of 297Og: 0.16 ms, within a factor of 4.97
- Standard deviation of predicted half-lives for 64 odd-A nuclei improved from 0.739 (using previous parameters) to 0.696 (using new parameters), a 5.82% improvement
- Standard deviation reduced by 40.87% when using the new preformation probability formula compared to assuming constant P0=0.35 (from 1.177 to 0.696)
- Predicted spin and parity of ground states: 269Sg (3/2+), 285Fl (3/2+), 293Lv (5/2+)
- Qalpha value for 297Og taken from the WS3+ mass model: 12.00 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of the two-potential approach to even-even, odd-A, and doubly-odd nuclei (Deng et al., 2016, 2017) by focusing on the superheavy region near the predicted N=184 neutron shell closure. The prediction for 297Og, which would be the nucleus with the largest neutron number (N=179) observed, is intended as a reference for future synthesis experiments via the 249Cf+48Ca reaction at the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions in Dubna. The study also builds on earlier work by Bao et al. (2017) who predicted that 297Og decays primarily by alpha emission rather than spontaneous fission.
