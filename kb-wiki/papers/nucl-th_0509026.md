# Description of the 17F(p,gamma)18Ne radiative capture reaction in the continuum shell model

> The shell model embedded in the continuum is applied to calculate the astrophysical S-factor and the reaction rate for the radiative proton capture reaction 17F(p,gamma)18Ne. The dominant contribution to the cross-section at very low energies is due to M1 transitions J_i^pi = 2^+ --> J_f^pi = 2_1^+ whose magnitude is controlled by a weakly bound 2_2^+ state at the excitation energy E_x = 3.62 MeV.

## Key claim

The shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) can describe the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate for the 17F(p,γ)18Ne radiative capture, with the dominant contribution at very low energies coming from M1 transitions (J_i^π = 2^+ → J_f^π = 2_1^+) whose magnitude is controlled by a weakly bound 2_2^+ state at excitation energy E_x = 3.62 MeV, but the calculations require shifting the negative parity states to their experimental positions because the SMEC predictions for those states are somewhat lower than observed.

## Method

The authors applied the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) formalism, which treats the nucleus as an open quantum system coupled to the environment of decay channels, using a projection operator technique to decompose the Hilbert space into a Q subspace of localized A-particle states and a P subspace containing (A-1)-particle states plus one nucleon in a scattering state. They constructed the Q subspace using the Zuker-Buck-McGrory (ZBM) interaction with modified two-body matrix elements, solved coupled-channel equations with a Woods-Saxon potential and density-dependent residual interaction, and calculated the radiative capture cross sections from both the ground state and first excited state of the target 17F using fully antisymmetrized wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The self-consistent potential parameters used were: V_0 = 56.8853 MeV, V_SO = 4.9014 MeV, R_0 = 3.276 fm, a = 0.58 fm, with r_0 = 1.27 fm
- The density-dependent residual interaction parameters were: v̂_00^in = 53.6, v̂_00^ex = -438.1, v̂_01^in = 160.9, v̂_01^ex = 169.9 (all in MeV·fm^3), ν = 1.374, r_n = 2.64 fm, d = 0.58 fm
- The 3_1^+ resonance in 18Ne was placed at E_x = 4.524 MeV (center-of-mass energy E_r = 600 keV) with width Γ = 18 keV, reproducing the experimental measurement of Bardayan et al.
- The S-factor at zero energy, S(0), was calculated to be 2130.29 eV·b
- The 1s_1/2 proton single-particle orbit was placed at the experimental binding energy of the 2_1^+ level (-2.034 MeV), and the 0d_5/2 orbit at the experimental binding energy of the 0_1^+ ground state (-3.92 MeV)
- The 0p_1/2 and 0f_7/2 single-particle orbits were placed at -15.3 MeV and 8.52 MeV, respectively

## Context

This work extends the SMEC formalism previously applied to mirror nuclei 8B/8Li and capture reactions 7Be(p,γ)8B and 7Li(n,γ)8Li (Chatterjee, 2000; Karataglidis, 2000) as well as 16O(p,γ)17F (Nicolas, 2002) to the astrophysically important 17F(p,γ)18Ne reaction relevant to nova explosions and x-ray bursts. The calculation improves upon earlier potential model approaches (Rolfs, 1973) and the extended two-cluster model (Descouvemont, 1993) by providing a unified microscopic description of both resonant and non-resonant parts without separate treatment, and it enables more reliable reaction rate predictions for stellar nucleosynthesis modeling where the competition between 17F proton capture and beta-decay determines nucleosynthesis paths toward 15O enrichment or rp-process activation.
