# Shell-model calculation with density-dependent interaction for $pf$-shell nuclei

> Shell-model calculations with density-dependent interactions are performed to investigate $pf$-shell nuclei, examining the ground-state energies, low-lying spectra, and $E2$ transition probabilities. The density-dependent terms in the interaction are self-consistently determined using the shell-model wave function for the ground state. We test three density-dependent interactions adapted from density functionals of Gogny-D1S, Gogny-GT2, and M3Y-P6. The shell-model results satisfactorily agree with the experimental data. However, the Gogny-D1S and Gogny-GT2 fail to reproduce the magicity of $N=28$, while it is properly described by the M3Y-P6 functional.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using density-dependent interactions adapted from three different density functionals (Gogny-D1S, Gogny-GT2, and M3Y-P6) can reproduce ground-state energies, low-lying spectra, and E2 transition probabilities for pf-shell nuclei, but only the M3Y-P6 functional correctly describes the N=28 magicity in Ni isotopes, while Gogny-D1S and Gogny-GT2 fail to do so.

## Method

The authors constructed shell-model Hamiltonians for the pf-shell model space (0f7/2, 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2 orbits) assuming 40Ca as an inert core, using two-body matrix elements computed from three density-dependent interactions: Gogny-D1S, Gogny-GT2, and M3Y-P6. The density was determined self-consistently by iteratively updating the ground-state wave function from the shell-model calculation and recomputing the Hamiltonian until convergence, typically requiring three iterations for even-even nuclei and ten for others. Shell-model calculations were performed using the KSHELL code, and results were compared with experimental data and conventional shell-model results from the GXPF1A interaction.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of 44Ti: Gogny-D1S and Gogny-GT2 results agree well with experiment, while M3Y-P6 underestimates binding by about 20 MeV.
* Ground-state spin agreement ratios across 100 pf-shell nuclei: 63.0% for Gogny-D1S, 72.0% for Gogny-GT2, and 69.0% for M3Y-P6.
* For odd-mass pf-shell nuclei, ground-state spin agreement ratios: 75.0% (Gogny-D1S), 81.2% (Gogny-GT2), and 79.7% (M3Y-P6); for odd-odd pf-shell nuclei: 41.7% (Gogny-D1S), 55.6% (Gogny-GT2), and 50.0% (M3Y-P6).
* For comparison, the GXPF1A interaction gives 72.0% agreement for pf-shell nuclei, and the USDB interaction gives 88.5% for sd-shell nuclei.
* The largest TBME deviation from GXPF1A is -4.056 MeV for the matrix element ⟨0f7/2,0f7/2|V|1p3/2,1p3/2⟩J=0,T=1 of Gogny-GT2, and -3.255 MeV for the same element in M3Y-P6.
* The 2+1 excitation energy of 56Ni is underestimated by Gogny-D1S and Gogny-GT2, while M3Y-P6 reproduces the experimental magicity.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid DFT-shell-model approach previously demonstrated for p- and sd-shell nuclei by PhysRevC.98.044320 and particles8020061 to the heavier pf-shell region. The method contrasts with conventional phenomenological shell-model interactions like GXPF1A (Honma 2005) and USDB (PhysRevC.74.034315), which require mass-region-specific fitting, and with mean-field approaches using the Gogny-D1S interaction (Hilaire 2007) that show lower accuracy for ground-state spins (56.2% for pf-shell odd-mass nuclei versus 75.0% in the present approach). The results enable future applications to heavier nuclei and suggest that the M3Y-P6 functional is preferable for describing shell structure in the pf-shell region, particularly for N=28 magicity.
