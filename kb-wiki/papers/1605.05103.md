# Shape Coexistence in 78 Ni and the new Island of Inversion

> Large Scale Shell Model calculations (SM-CI) predict that the region of deformation which comprises the heaviest Chromium and Iron isotopes at and beyond N=40 will merge with a new one at N=50 in an astonishing parallel to the N=20 and N=28 case in the Neon and Magnesium isotopes. We propose a valence space including the full pf-shell for the protons and the full sdg shell for the neutrons; which represents a comeback of the the harmonic oscillator shells in the very neutron rich regime. The onset of deformation is understood in the framework of the algebraic SU3-like structures linked to quadrupole dominance. Our calculations preserve the doubly magic nature of the ground state of 78 Ni, which, however, exhibits a well deformed prolate band at low excitation energy, providing a striking example of shape coexistence far from stability.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations predict that shape coexistence in doubly magic 78Ni, with a well-deformed prolate intruder band at low excitation energy, serves as a portal to a new Island of Inversion at N=50 that merges with the established one at N=40 for isotopes with Z ≤ 26, though the doubly magic nature of the 78Ni ground state is preserved.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model configuration-interaction (SM-CI) calculations using a valence space comprising the full pf-shell for protons and the full sdg-shell for neutrons, with an inert 60Ca core. They constructed a new effective interaction (pfsdg-u) by modifying the monopole part of a G-matrix derived from the free nucleon-nucleon interaction, adjusting neutron effective single-particle energies to reproduce experimental binding energies of nickel and zirconium isotopes and the N=50 isotone gaps, and constraining the Z=28 proton gap using B(E2) values in zinc and germanium isotopes. Constrained Hartree-Fock calculations in the same valence space were used to produce projected energy surfaces, and full diagonalizations were performed with truncation on the sum of neutron excitations across N=50 and proton excitations across Z=28.

- 76Fe: yrast 2+ at 0.43 MeV; first excited 0+ at 0.36 MeV; ground state contains 21% 0p-0h and 33% neutron 2p-2h configurations
- 74Cr: yrast 2+ at 0.27 MeV (2p-2h band) and 0.17 MeV (4p-4h band); B(E2)(2+1 → 0+2) = 360 e2fm4 (2p-2h) and 555 e2fm4 (4p-4h); E(4+)/E(2+) ≈ 3; βmass ≈ 0.32 and βcharge ≈ 0.35
- 74Cr: B(E2) for 2+ → 0+ = 630 e2fm4; spectroscopic quadrupole moment Qs(2+) = -51 efm2
- 78Ni: B(E2)(2+ → 0+) = 32 e2fm4; Qs(2+) = -39 efm2
- Quadrupole correlation energies for 4p-4h neutron configurations: 78Ni = 9.3 MeV, 76Fe = 10.9 MeV, 74Cr = 11.3 MeV, 72Ti = 9.1 MeV, 70Ca = 4.8 MeV
- Matrix dimensions reached 2×10^10 Slater determinants for some calculations

## Context

This work extends the understanding of shape coexistence and Islands of Inversion previously established at N=8, 20, 28, and 40, drawing a direct parallel to the N=20-28 case in Neon and Magnesium isotopes studied by the same group (Nowacki et al., 2016). The calculations predict a merging of the N=40 and N=50 Islands of Inversion for chromium and iron isotopes, complementing experimental measurements on chromium and iron isotopes up to N=42 and N=46 respectively (Santamaria et al., 2015). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted deformed intruder bands in 78Ni and the rapid shape transition from spherical 78Ni to fully rotational 74Cr, and provide a framework for extending shell-model descriptions from 48Ni and 44Cr at N=20 to 80Ni and 76Cr at N=52.
