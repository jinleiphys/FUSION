# Pair term in the Electromagnetic Current within the Front-Form Dynamics: Spin-0 Case

> The frame and scale dependence of the pair-term contribution to the electromagnetic form factor of a spin-zero composite system of two-fermions is studied within the Light Front. The form factor is evaluated from the plus-component of the current in the Breit frame, using for the first time a nonconstant, symmetric ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The frame dependence is analyzed by allowing a nonvanishing plus component of the momentum transfer, while the dynamical scale is set by the masses of the constituents and by mass and size of the composite system. A transverse momentum distribution, associated with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, is introduced which allows to define strongly and weakly relativistic systems. In particular, for strongly relativistic systems, the pair term vanishes for the Drell-Yan condition, while is dominant for momentum transfer along the light-front direction. For a weakly relativistic system, fitted to the deuteron scale, the pair term is negligible up to momentum transfers of 1(GeV/c)$^2$. A comparison with results obtained within the Front-Form Hamiltonian dynamics with a fixed number of constituents is also presented.

## Key claim

The pair-term contribution to the electromagnetic form factor of a spin-zero two-fermion composite system depends strongly on the direction of the momentum transfer in the Breit frame and on the relativistic nature of the system; for strongly relativistic systems the pair term vanishes under the Drell-Yan condition (q⁺=0) but dominates when momentum transfer is along the light-front direction, while for weakly relativistic systems fitted to the deuteron scale the pair term is negligible up to momentum transfers of 1 (GeV/c)².

## Method

The authors evaluated the plus-component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame for a pion-like system modeled as a q-anti-q bound state with pseudoscalar coupling, using a symmetric, nonconstant Bethe-Salpeter vertex function with two poles (regulator mass m_R). The form factor was separated into a valence contribution (F^(I)_π) from the light-front time-ordered diagram with 0 ≤ k⁺ < P⁺ and a pair-term contribution (F^(II)_π) from the interval P⁺ ≤ k⁺ ≤ P'⁺, with the momentum transfer direction varied via an angle α (α=0° gives Drell-Yan condition q⁺=0, α=90° gives q⁺=√(-q²)). A transverse momentum probability density f(k_⊥) was introduced to classify systems as strongly or weakly relativistic.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m = 0.220 GeV, regulator mass m_R = 0.6 GeV, fitted to reproduce the experimental pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Pion charge radius from the model: 0.74 fm, about 10% larger than the experimental value r_exp = 0.67 ± 0.02 fm
- Probability of the valence q-anti-q Fock-state component in the pion: η = 0.77
- For the pion model, the momentum transfer where valence and pair-term contributions become equal (α=90°): -q²_(I/II) = 0.03 (GeV/c)²
- For a weakly relativistic system fitted to the deuteron scale, the pair term is negligible up to momentum transfers of 1 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pair-term contributions in light-front dynamics, particularly the work of Sawicki (1991) for two-boson systems and the pion calculations with pseudoscalar coupling by de Melo, Frederico, Pace, and Salmè (1998, 1999) which used a nonsymmetric vertex function. The symmetric vertex ansatz allows simultaneous reproduction of the pion form factor and decay constant, which was not possible with the nonsymmetric regulator. The results enable comparison between field-theoretical approaches and Front-Form Hamiltonian dynamics with a fixed number of constituents, and provide a framework for understanding when effective two-body currents are needed in fixed-particle-number calculations.
