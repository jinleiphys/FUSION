# Unitary Correlation Operator Method and Similarity Renormalization Group: Connections and Differences

> We discuss relations and differences between two methods for the construction of unitarily transformed effective interactions, the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) and Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM). The aim of both methods is to construct a soft phase-shift equivalent effective interaction which is well suited for many-body calculations in limited model spaces. After contrasting the two conceptual frameworks, we establish a formal connection between the initial SRG-generator and the static generators of the UCOM transformation. Furthermore we propose a mapping procedure to extract UCOM correlation functions from the SRG evolution. We compare the effective interactions resulting from the UCOM-transformation and the SRG-evolution on the level of matrix elements, in no-core shell model calculations of light nuclei, and in Hartree-Fock calculations up to 208-Pb. Both interactions exhibit very similar convergence properties in light nuclei but show a different systematic behavior as function of particle number.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a formal connection between the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) and the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) by showing that the initial SRG generator has the same operator structure as the UCOM generators, but notes that a single UCOM transformation encapsulates a whole SRG flow evolution up to a certain flow parameter, so the initial SRG generator alone does not provide the UCOM correlation functions directly.

## Method

The authors compare the SRG and UCOM frameworks by first reviewing the SRG flow equation using the relative kinetic energy as the generator, then contrasting it with the UCOM approach which uses explicit ansatze for central and tensor correlation operators. They evaluate the commutator defining the initial SRG generator for a general interaction containing central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms, showing it yields the same operator structure as the UCOM generators. They then propose a mapping procedure to extract UCOM correlation functions from the SRG evolution by solving the flow equation with the dynamical SRG generator. The resulting effective interactions are compared on the level of matrix elements, in no-core shell model calculations for light nuclei, and in Hartree-Fock calculations for closed-shell nuclei up to Pb-208.

## Key numbers

- The SRG evolution preserves the two-body spectrum to a relative accuracy of better than 10^-6
- Flow parameter values shown for illustrations range from bar-alpha = 0 fm^4 (initial AV18 potential) to bar-alpha = 0.04 fm^4 (typical for applications)
- The deuteron D-wave component is systematically eliminated for flow parameters bar-alpha approximately 0.04 fm^4, with only a weak contribution remaining around r approximately 2 fm
- Hartree-Fock calculations are performed for closed shell nuclei up to Pb-208

## Context

This work extends the comparison between SRG and UCOM approaches for constructing soft phase-shift equivalent effective interactions, building on the SRG formulation of Wegner (1994, 2000) and the UCOM formulation of Feldmeier, Neff, and Roth (1998, 2003, 2004). The paper establishes formal connections between the two methods that were previously treated as separate frameworks, and enables future work to use SRG-evolved interactions to extract UCOM correlation functions. The comparison with no-core shell model calculations for H-3 and He-4 and Hartree-Fock calculations up to Pb-208 shows that both interactions exhibit very similar convergence properties in light nuclei but show different systematic behavior as a function of particle number.
