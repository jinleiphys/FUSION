# Heavy deformed nuclei in the shell model Monte Carlo method

> We extend the shell model Monte Carlo approach to heavy deformed nuclei using a new proton-neutron formalism. The low excitation energies of such nuclei necessitate calculations at low temperatures for which a stabilization method is implemented in the canonical ensemble. We apply the method to study a well deformed rare-earth nucleus, 162Dy. The single-particle model space includes the 50-82 shell plus 1f_{7/2} orbital for protons and the 82-126 shell plus 0h_{11/2}, 1g_{9/2} orbitals for neutrons. We show that the spherical shell model reproduces well the rotational character of 162Dy within this model space. We also calculate the level density of 162Dy and find it to be in excellent agreement with the experimental level density, which we extract from several experiments.

## Key claim

The spherical shell model, when implemented with a sufficiently large model space and an appropriate effective interaction, can reproduce the rotational character of a strongly deformed rare-earth nucleus like 162Dy, contrary to the common assumption that such deformed nuclei require a deformed basis. The agreement with experimental level density is achieved only after the pairing strengths are reduced by a factor of 0.77 from values determined by odd-even mass differences in neighboring spherical nuclei.

## Method

The authors extended the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method to a proton-neutron formalism, treating protons and neutrons explicitly rather than using isospin. They implemented a stabilization method in the canonical ensemble using modified Gram-Schmidt decomposition to handle the ill-conditioned propagator at large inverse temperatures (up to β=20 MeV⁻¹). The single-particle model space included orbitals from the 50-82 shell plus 1f7/2 for protons and the 82-126 shell plus 0h11/2, 1g9/2 for neutrons, determined by a Woods-Saxon criterion of spherical occupations between 0.1 and 0.9 at deformation β2=0.35. The effective interaction used monopole pairing and multipole-multipole (quadrupole, octupole, hexadecupole) terms.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 162Dy: E0 = -375.387 ± 0.019 MeV, extracted by fitting a straight line of slope 1 to the thermal energy at low temperatures
- Correlation energy (HFB minus SMMC ground state): 3.124 ± 0.019 MeV
- Moment of inertia of the ground-state band: Ig/ħ² = 35.8 ± 1.5 MeV⁻¹, compared with experimental value of 37.2 MeV⁻¹ from the first 2⁺ state at 80.7 keV
- Pairing strength reduction factor: 0.77 applied to gp = 10.9 MeV/Z and gn = 10.9 MeV/N to reproduce the moment of inertia
- Quadrupole renormalization factor: k2 = 2.12, close to the value k2 = 2 used in prior work
- Inverse temperature range: up to β = 20 MeV⁻¹, with time slices Δβ = 1/32 and 1/64 MeV⁻¹
- Experimental level density parameters from composite formula fit: a = 18.28 ± 0.15 MeV⁻¹, Δ = 0.421 ± 0.014 MeV, EM = 1.752 ± 0.036 MeV, T1 = 0.36 MeV, E1 = -0.73 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SMMC approach, previously limited to medium-mass nuclei with moderate deformation (Alhassid 1994, Nakada 1997), to heavy deformed rare-earth nuclei for the first time. The method enables calculations of statistical nuclear properties like level densities in nuclei where the first excitation energy is ~100 keV, requiring propagation to much larger β values than earlier applications. The results enable future SMMC studies across the nuclear chart, particularly in the rare-earth region, and demonstrate that spherical shell model calculations can reproduce rotational behavior when a sufficiently large model space is used, in contrast to the approach of Wiedeking et al. (2000) which used a smaller model space.
