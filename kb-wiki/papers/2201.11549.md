# The Role of Baryon Structure in Neutron Stars

> Understanding the equation of state of dense nuclear matter is a fundamental challenge for nuclear physics. It is especially timely and interesting challenge as we have reached a period where neutron stars, which contain the most dense nuclear matter in the Universe, are now being studied in completely new ways, from gravitational waves to satellite based telescopes. We review the theoretical approaches to calculating this equation of state which involve a change in the structure of the baryons.

## Key claim

The hyperon puzzle, the apparent contradiction between the existence of massive neutron stars and the expectation that hyperons soften the equation of state, can be naturally resolved by models that incorporate the change in baryon structure in dense matter, where the resulting repulsive many-body forces emerge from the quark-level dynamics without introducing new free parameters.

## Method

The authors review relativistic mean-field (RMF) models built upon quark-meson phenomenology, specifically the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model and its variants (Bogoliubov-QMC, Quark-Mean-Field, Cloudy Bag QMC, and NJL-based QMC). In these models, baryons are treated as confined three-quark systems (using MIT bag, linear, or harmonic oscillator confinement) whose internal structure responds self-consistently to the scalar mean fields in the nuclear medium, producing a density-dependent effective baryon mass and an effective many-body force with no extra parameters.

## Key numbers

- The conservative lower estimate for the maximum mass of neutron stars is that of PSR J1614-2230: 1.908 ± 0.016 M⊙.
- NICER measured PSR J0030+0451 with radius 12.71 (+1.14, -1.19) km and mass 1.34 (+0.15, -0.16) M⊙ (68% credibility).
- NICER measured PSR J0740+6620 with radius 12.39 (+1.3, -0.98) km for a mass of 2.07 ± 0.06 M⊙.
- The gravitational wave measurement of the binary neutron star merger gave radii of both stars within R = 11.9 (+1.4, -1.4) km at 90% confidence.
- The latest QMC-based energy density functional reproduces binding energies of all known even-even nuclei with an rms deviation of 0.29% using just 5 parameters.
- The Cloudy Bag QMC model gives maximum neutron star masses almost 0.2 M⊙ higher than the standard QMC model, but yields nuclear incompressibility K0 ≈ 310 MeV.
- The central density for a typical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star is of order 3 n0 (three times saturation density).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the hyperon puzzle initiated by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), who measured massive neutron stars, and by Rikovska-Stone (2006), who showed hyperonic cores could support such masses. It contrasts with models that introduce extra repulsive three-body forces phenomenologically, and instead builds on the QMC model of Guichon (1987, 1995) and its extensions to finite nuclei by Stone (2016, 2017) and Martinez (2018, 2020). The review enables future tests of in-medium baryon structure changes via the EMC effect (Thomas 1989, Cloet 2005) and the Coulomb sum rule (Morgenstern 2001, Cloet 2015), with upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments on 7Li.
