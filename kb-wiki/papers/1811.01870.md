# Fluid dynamics of heavy ion collisions with Mode expansion (FluiduM)

> The fluid dynamics of a relativistic fireball with longitudinal and transverse expansion is described using a background-fluctuation splitting. Symmetry representations of azimuthal rotations and longitudinal boosts are used for a classification of initial state configurations and their fluid dynamic propagation in terms of a mode expansion. We develop an accurate and efficient numerical scheme based on the pseudo-spectral method to solve the resulting hyperbolic partial differential equations. Comparison to the analytically known Gubser solution underlines the high accuracy of this technique. We also present first applications of FluiduM to central heavy ion collisions at the LHC energies featuring a realistic thermodynamic equations of state as well as shear and bulk viscous dissipation.

## Key claim

The paper presents FluiduM, a numerical framework that solves relativistic viscous fluid dynamics for heavy-ion collisions by decomposing the fireball evolution into a background and fluctuations expanded in modes classified by azimuthal rotation and longitudinal boost symmetries, achieving high accuracy when benchmarked against the analytically known Gubser solution.

## Method

The authors split the fluid-dynamic fields into a boost-invariant, azimuthally symmetric background and small perturbations, then expand the perturbations in Fourier modes with respect to the azimuthal angle and longitudinal rapidity. They implement a pseudo-spectral method using discrete cosine and sine transforms to solve the resulting hyperbolic partial differential equations in the radial direction, while time evolution is handled by a standard integrator. The code incorporates a realistic equation of state from lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2016, Bazavov 2014) and includes both shear and bulk viscous dissipation with relaxation-type equations for the dissipative currents.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-spectral method is validated against the Gubser solution, which provides an analytic benchmark for conformal, boost-invariant flow with transverse expansion; the paper states the comparison "underlines the high accuracy of this technique" but does not quote a specific numerical error.
- First applications are presented for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies of sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, using a realistic equation of state and shear viscosity over entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.08 (the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound value, Kovtun 2005) and bulk viscosity parameters from Karsch (2008).
- The radial grid uses N points with a mapping r = R * tan(theta) where R is a scale parameter; the discrete cosine/sine transforms are defined for sequences of N elements, with specific formulas for derivative operators involving factors of (2n-2) and (2n-1) in mode space.

## Context

This work extends the mode-by-mode fluid dynamics approach previously developed by Floerchinger and Wiedemann (2013, 2014) from a purely analytical framework to a fully numerical implementation capable of handling realistic equations of state and viscous effects. It provides an alternative to existing 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic codes such as those by Schenke, Jeon, and Gale (2010), Bozek (2012), and Karpenko, Huovinen, and Bleicher (2014), with the advantage of a systematic mode expansion that can be used for efficient computation of initial-state fluctuation responses. The code enables future Bayesian parameter estimation studies (similar to Bernhard 2016) and systematic investigations of nonlinear mode coupling in heavy-ion collisions.
