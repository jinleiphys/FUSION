# Deuteron-Nucleus Collisions in a Multi-Phase Transport Model

> Using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, we study pseudo-rapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC. We find that final-state partonic and hadronic interactions affect the transverse momentum spectrum of protons more than those of kaons or pions. Relative to p+p collisions at same center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair, the effect of final-state interactions on the charged particle transverse momentum spectra in d+Au collisions is much smaller than observed in experimental data, indicating that initial-state effects such as the Cronin effect are important.

## Key claim
Final-state partonic and hadronic interactions in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC produce a much weaker modification of charged particle transverse momentum spectra relative to p+p collisions than is observed experimentally, indicating that initial-state effects such as the Cronin effect (parton momentum broadening) are the dominant cause of the observed spectral shape.

## Method
The authors used the default AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, a hybrid model combining initial conditions from HIJING (version 1.383), Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) for minijet parton interactions, Lund string fragmentation for hadronization, and the ART hadronic cascade. The deuteron was modeled with a Hulthen wave function (neglecting the D-wave component), and collisions were simulated at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV with both beams at 100 GeV per nucleon. Centrality was determined primarily by the number of participating nucleons from primary collisions.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius: 2.0 fm, from Hulthen wave function parameters \(\alpha=(4.38\ \text{fm})^{-1}\) and \(\mu=(1.05\ \text{fm})^{-1}\).
- Average number of partonic collisions per parton in minimum-bias d+Au: about 0.06 (with 3 mb parton scattering cross section).
- Average number of hadronic collisions per produced hadron in minimum-bias d+Au: about 1, compared with about 8 in central Au+Au at 200A GeV.
- For events with \(N^{\rm d}_{\rm part}=2\): average \(N^{\rm Au}_{\rm part}=10.4\), mean impact parameter 4.7 fm; for \(N^{\rm d}_{\rm part}=1\): average \(N^{\rm Au}_{\rm part}=2.8\), mean impact parameter 7.8 fm.
- Final-state interactions increase the number of participating nucleons by more than 40% for the most central events (0-10% centrality bin).

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model, previously applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb at SPS (Lin 2001, Lin 2002), to deuteron-gold collisions to isolate initial-state nuclear effects from final-state interactions. The results contradict the possibility that final-state interactions alone explain the observed transverse momentum spectra in d+Au collisions at RHIC, and instead support the importance of the Cronin effect (Cronin 1975) and parton momentum broadening (Vitev 2003). The study enables future comparisons with experimental d+Au data to constrain nuclear shadowing and string fragmentation modifications in small collision systems.
