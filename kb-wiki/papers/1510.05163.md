# Statistical theory of light nucleus reaction and application to $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction

> A statistical theory of light nucleus reaction (STLN) is proposed to describe both neutron and light charged particle induced nuclear reactions with 1p-shell light nuclei involved. The dynamic of STLN is described by the unified Hauser-Feshbach and exciton model, of which the angular momentum and parity conservations are considered in equilibrium and pre-equilibrium processes. The Coulomb barriers of the incident and outgoing charged particles, which seriously influence the open reaction channels, could be reasonably considered in the incident channel and the different outgoing channels. In kinematics, the recoiling effects in various emission processes are taken strictly into account. Taking $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction as an example, we calculate the double-differential cross sections of outgoing neutrons and charged particles using PUNF code in the frame of STLN. The calculated results agree very well with the existing experimental neutron double-differential cross sections at $E_p=18$ MeV, and indicate that PUNF code is a powerful tool to set up file-6 in the reaction data library for the light charged particle induced nuclear reactions with 1p-shell light nuclei involved.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a statistical theory of light nucleus reaction (STLN) that can describe both sequential and simultaneous particle emission processes between discrete levels while conserving energy, angular momentum, and parity, claiming that the PUNF code based on this theory reproduces experimental neutron double-differential cross sections for the $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction at $E_p=18$ MeV, though the abstract's claim of "very well" agreement applies specifically to this single incident energy and only to neutron emission.

## Method

The authors developed the STLN framework by combining the unified Hauser-Feshbach and exciton model with angular momentum and parity conservation in both equilibrium and pre-equilibrium processes, incorporating Coulomb barriers for charged particles and strict recoil effects in kinematics. They implemented this theory in the PUNF code and applied it to calculate double-differential cross sections for the $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction at 18 MeV proton incident energy, comparing the results with existing experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Neutron double-differential cross sections calculated at $E_p=18$ MeV for the $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction show agreement with experimental data
- The Coulomb barrier for charged particles is approximated as $V_{Coul}\approx e^2 Z_{m_1} Z_{M_1} / R_c$ with $R_c \approx r_c (A_{m_1}^{1/3} + A_{M_1}^{1/3})$ and $r_c \approx 1.2 \sim 1.5$ fm
- The kinetic energy of the first emitted particle in the center-of-mass system is given by $\varepsilon_{m_1}^c = (M_1 / M_C)(E^* - B_1 - E_{k_1})$
- The maximum and minimum kinetic energies of secondary emitted particles in CMS are $\varepsilon_{m_2,max}^c = \varepsilon_{m_2}^r (1+\gamma)^2$ and $\varepsilon_{m_2,min}^c = \varepsilon_{m_2}^r (1-\gamma)^2$

## Context

This work extends the unified Hauser-Feshbach and exciton model previously applied successfully to neutron-induced reactions on 1p-shell nuclei such as $^6$Li (Zhang 2001), $^7$Li (Zhang 2002), $^9$Be (Sun 2009, Duan 2010), $^{10}$B (Zhang 2003), $^{11}$B (Zhang 2003), $^{12}$C (Zhang 1999, Sun 2007), $^{14}$N (Yan 2005), $^{16}$O (Zhang 2001, Duan 2005), and $^{19}$F (Duan 2007). The paper addresses the lack of satisfactory double-differential cross sections for light charged particle induced reactions, where previous approaches such as the Intranuclear-Cascade-Evaporation model used in ENDF/B-VII.1 (Chadwick 2011), the TALYS-based TENDL-2012 (Koning 2012), and the PHITS code calculations (Iwamoto 2009) could not reproduce experimental peaks for the $^9$Be(p, xn) reaction. The STLN framework enables the establishment of file-6 in reaction data libraries for light charged particle induced reactions with 1p-shell light nuclei.
