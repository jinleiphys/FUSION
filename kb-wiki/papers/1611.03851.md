# Constraining gravity with hadron physics: neutron stars, modified gravity and gravitational waves

> The finding of Gravitational Waves by the aLIGO scientific and VIRGO collaborations opens opportunities to better test and understand strong interactions, both nuclear-hadronic and gravitational. Assuming General Relativity holds, one can constrain hadron physics at a neutron star. But precise knowledge of the Equation of State and transport properties in hadron matter can also be used to constrain the theory of gravity itself. I review a couple of these opportunities in the context of modified f(R) gravity, the maximum mass of neutron stars, and progress in the Equation of State of neutron matter from the chiral effective field theory of QCD.

## Key claim

Assuming General Relativity holds, gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers can constrain hadron physics (the equation of state and transport properties of neutron matter), but conversely, precise knowledge of hadron physics can be used to constrain the theory of gravity itself, including modified \(f(R)\) gravity models.

## Method

The paper reviews how the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for hydrostatic equilibrium in neutron stars, combined with an equation of state from chiral effective field theory of QCD, yields mass-radius relations that can be compared to observations. For modified gravity, the author solves the equivalent first-order differential system (including an additional equation for the scalar curvature \(R(r)\)) using Runge-Kutta integration from the star center until the pressure vanishes, then continues in vacuum to match asymptotically flat spacetime. The tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) is extracted from the induced quadrupole moment of a neutron star in response to tidal stress from a binary companion.

## Key numbers

- GW150914: black hole masses \(\sim 36 M_\odot\) and \(29 M_\odot\) merging to \(\sim 62 M_\odot\), with \(\sim 3 M_\odot\) radiated as gravitational energy
- GW151226: two objects of masses \(\sim 7 M_\odot\) and \(\sim 14 M_\odot\)
- Ringdown attenuation time for GW150914: \(\tau \simeq 4\) milliseconds
- Distance to GW source: \(440 \pm 190\) Megaparsec
- Neutron star radius from quiescent X-ray sources: \(R_{\rm NS} = 9.4 \pm 1.2\) km
- Neutron star radius from thermonuclear-burst sources: \(R_{\rm NS} \in (10.4, 12.9)\) km
- Viscosity estimate for GW150914 if treated as Newtonian fluid: \(\eta \sim 4 \times 10^{28}\) Poise, compared to \(\sim 10^{15}\) Poise expected for neutron matter at T=10 MeV
- Estimated NS-NS merger detection rate: 2.5-11 yr\(^{-1}\) from Europium r-process production of \(O(10^{-5} M_\odot)\) per merger
- Two-solar mass neutron stars reported by Shapiro delay method (Demorest 2010) and binary orbital data (Antoniadis 2013)
- In \(f(R)=aR^2\) modified gravity with \(a=-0.05\), neutron stars with masses larger than \(2.4 M_\odot\) are easily allowed

## Context

This review extends the discussion of gravitational wave astrophysics opened by the aLIGO detections (Abbott 2016) to the interplay between hadron physics and tests of gravity. It builds on prior work constraining the neutron star equation of state from mass and radius measurements (Hebeler 2013, Guillot 2014) and on modified gravity studies in neutron stars (Dobado 2011, Resco 2016). The paper enables future joint analyses where gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers simultaneously constrain both the QCD equation of state and modifications to General Relativity, particularly \(f(R)\) theories that are also used to model inflation.
