# Nature of $X(3872)$ in $B^0 \to K^0 X(3872)$ and $B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)$ decays

> We investigate the decays of $B^0 \to K^0 X(3872)$ and $B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)$ based on the picture where the $X(3872)$ resonance is strongly coupled to the $D\bar{D}^* + c.c.$ channel. In addition to the decay mechanism where the $X(3872)$ resonance is formed from the $c\bar{c}$ pair hadronization with the short-distance interaction, we have also considered the $D\bar{D}^*$ rescattering diagrams in the long-distance scale, where $D$ and $\bar{D}^*$ are formed from $c$ and $\bar{c}$ separately. Because of the difference of the mass thresholds of charged and neutral $D\bar{D}^*$ channels, and the rather narrow width of the $X(3872)$ resonance, at the $X(3872)$ mass, the loop functions of $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ and $D^+\bar{D}^{*-}$ are much different. Taking this difference into account, the ratio of $\mathcal{B}[B^0\to K^0X(3872)]/\mathcal{B}[B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)] \simeq 0.5$ can be naturally obtained. Based on this result, we also evaluate the decay widths of $B_s^0 \to η(η') X(3872)$. It is expected that future experimental measurements of these decays can be used to elucidate the nature of the $X(3872)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The ratio of branching fractions \( \mathcal{B}[B^0\to K^0X(3872)]/\mathcal{B}[B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)] \simeq 0.5 \) can be naturally explained in the molecular picture where the \(X(3872)\) resonance is strongly coupled to the \(D\bar{D}^*\) channel, provided one includes both short-distance hadronization of the \(c\bar{c}\) pair and long-distance \(D\bar{D}^*\) rescattering diagrams. The key condition is that the loop functions for charged and neutral \(D\bar{D}^*\) channels differ significantly at the \(X(3872)\) mass because of the different mass thresholds and the narrow width of the resonance.

## Method

The authors computed the decay amplitudes for \(B^0 \to K^0 X(3872)\) and \(B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)\) using two mechanisms: (1) direct production of \(X(3872)\) from hadronization of the \(c\bar{c}\) pair (short-distance), parameterized by a common coupling \(g_{BKX}\); and (2) production via rescattering of \(D\) and \(\bar{D}^*\) mesons formed in the weak decay (long-distance), involving loop functions \(G\) for the charged (\(D^+D^{*-}\)) and neutral (\(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\)) channels. The loop functions were evaluated using a standard dimensional regularization formula with a subtraction constant \(\alpha\) at scale \(\mu = 1000\) MeV. The unknown parameters \(g_{BKX}\), \(g_B\) (absorbing the \(X(3872)\)-\(D\bar{D}^*\) couplings and production factor), and \(\alpha\) were fitted to the experimental branching fractions. Using the fitted \(g_{BKX}\), the branching fractions of \(B_s^0 \to \eta X(3872)\) and \(B_s^0 \to \eta' X(3872)\) were then predicted under SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Ratio \(R_K = \mathcal{B}[B^0\to K^0 X(3872)] / \mathcal{B}[B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)] = 0.50 \pm 0.14(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.04(\text{syst.})\) from Belle (2011), which the model reproduces.
- Experimental branching fractions used as input: \(\mathcal{B}[B^0 \to K^0 X(3872)] = (1.1 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-4}\) and \(\mathcal{B}[B^+ \to K^+ X(3872)] = (2.1 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-4}\) from the Particle Data Group (2020).
- Mass of \(X(3872)\): \(M_{X(3872)} = 3871.65 \pm 0.06\) MeV; width \(\Gamma_{X(3872)} = 1.19 \pm 0.21\) MeV.
- Mass thresholds: \(M_{D^0\bar{D}^{*0}} = 3871.69 \pm 0.11\) MeV; \(m_{D^0} = 1864.84\) MeV, \(m_{D^{*0}} = 2006.85\) MeV, \(m_{D^+} = 1869.66\) MeV, \(m_{D^{*-}} = 2010.26\) MeV.
- For \(\alpha = -1.91\) (corresponding to a cutoff of 567 MeV from Montana (2022)), the fitted parameters are \(g_{BKX} = (6.61 \pm 1.57) \times 10^{-8}\) and \(g_B = (3.59 \pm 0.85) \times 10^{-5}\); the predicted branching fractions are \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \eta X(3872)] = (1.52 \pm 0.72) \times 10^{-5}\) and \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \eta' X(3872)] = (1.70 \pm 0.81) \times 10^{-5}\).
- For \(\alpha = -1.86\), the predicted branching fractions are \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \eta X(3872)] = (1.94 \pm 0.92) \times 10^{-5}\) and \(\mathcal{B}[B_s^0 \to \eta' X(3872)] = (2.16 \pm 1.02) \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the \(X(3872)\) resonance, previously studied in the context of its decays and production in \(B\) decays by Braaten (2004) and in the dynamical generation from \(D\bar{D}^*\) interactions by Montana (2022). It directly addresses the Belle (2011) measurement of the ratio \(R_K = 0.50\), which had been explained within a tetraquark picture by Maiani (2020). The results support the view that \(X(3872)\) is not a pure charmonium but contains a large \(D\bar{D}^*\) component. The predictions for \(B_s^0 \to \eta(\eta') X(3872)\) decays provide testable targets for future experiments at Belle II and LHCb, which could help constrain the subtraction constant \(\alpha\) and further elucidate the nature of the resonance.
