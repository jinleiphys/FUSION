# Hydrodynamic Modeling for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and the LHC

> In this article, we briefly review recent progress on hydrodynamic modeling and its implementations to relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The related topics include: 1) initial state fluctuations, final state correlations and event-by-event hydrodynamics, 2) extracting the QGP shear viscosity from flow data, 3) flow and hydrodynamics in 5.02 A TeV p+Pb collisions.

# Hydrodynamic Modeling for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and the LHC

## Key claim

The specific QGP shear viscosity (η/s)QGP is small, not more than several times of 1/(4π), but the effective averaged value is slightly larger at the LHC than at RHIC; however, the extracted value carries large uncertainties from undetermined initial conditions, with MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models giving results differing by roughly a factor of two.

## Method

The paper reviews viscous (2+1)-d and (3+1)-d relativistic hydrodynamics and hybrid models that couple viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP fluid expansion with a hadron cascade (UrQMD) to describe the microscopic evolution and decoupling of hadronic matter. Initial conditions are provided by fluctuating models such as MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and IP-Glasma, and the simulations are run either as single-shot (event-averaged) or event-by-event to compute flow harmonics vn and compare with experimental data from RHIC and the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Specific QGP shear viscosity extracted from integrated elliptic flow in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions using VISHNU hybrid model: 1/(4π) < (η/s)QGP < 2.5 × 1/(4π), with main uncertainties from the two undetermined initial conditions (MC-Glauber vs. MC-KLN).
- For MC-Glauber initial conditions, (η/s)QGP ≈ 1/(4π); for MC-KLN, (η/s)QGP ≈ 2/(4π) at RHIC.
- For 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, (η/s)QGP increased to ≈ 2.5/(4π) for MC-KLN to better describe elliptic flow.
- Using IP-Glasma initial conditions with event-by-event (3+1)-d viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC): (η/s)RHIC = 0.12 for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions and (η/s)LHC = 0.2 for 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions.
- Hadronic B-B̅ annihilations in the hybrid model reduce proton and antiproton multiplicity by O(30%).
- Late hadronic evolution in pure ideal hydrodynamics leads to an O(30%) reduction of elliptic flow compared to hybrid model results.
- Initial eccentricity ε2 from MC-KLN is about 30% larger than from MC-Glauber, leading to ~30% larger elliptic flow after hydrodynamic evolution.
- For lower collision energies (7.7, 27, 39 A GeV Au+Au), (η/s)QGP ≥ 0.2 from 3+1-d viscous hydrodynamics + UrQMD hybrid model fits.

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Heinz and Gale (2013) and Teaney (2009) on viscous hydrodynamic descriptions of the QGP as an almost perfect liquid, and by Romatschke (2007) and Song (2007) on early viscous hydrodynamic calculations. It summarizes progress from the OSU-LBL group's VISHNU hybrid model (Song 2010) and the McGill group's MUSIC event-by-event simulations (Schenke 2012, Gale 2012) that enabled extraction of the QGP shear viscosity from flow data. The paper enables future extraction of a temperature-dependent (η/s)QGP(T) by combining flow data across multiple collision energies, and motivates systematic evaluation of initial condition models and development of massive data evaluating algorithms such as CHIMERA (Soltz 2012).
