# Update and Comparison of Nuclear Parton Distribution Functions and Neutrino DIS

> We compare the nuclear corrections factors from neutrino deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) with the ones coming from a standard analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDF). We focus on a discrepancy between the most precise neutrino DIS data from NuTeV and the nuclear PDF coming from the analysis of charged lepton DIS and Drell-Yan data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear correction factors derived from a global analysis of charged lepton deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan data cannot simultaneously describe the most precise neutrino DIS data from NuTeV, even when compromise fits with weighted neutrino data are attempted; the discrepancy persists at the 90% confidence level, particularly for iron-target data.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDF) using charged lepton DIS and Drell-Yan data (708 data points) with 32 free parameters, then extended the fit to include 3134 neutrino DIS cross-section data points from NuTeV and Chorus. To prevent the neutrino data from dominating the fit, they introduced a weight parameter \(w\) and produced a series of compromise fits, evaluating goodness-of-fit using the \(\chi^2\) criterion with 90% confidence level bands defined by the chi-squared probability distribution.

## Key numbers

- The fit to only charged lepton data (weight \(w=0\)) achieved \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.946\) for 708 data points with 32 free parameters.
- The fit with weight \(w=1/2\) gave total \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.32\) (5085 total points), with \(\chi^2_{l^\pm A} = 680\) (0.96 per point) and \(\chi^2_{\nu A} = 4405\) (1.40 per point).
- The fit with weight \(w=1\) gave total \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.30\) (5014 total points), with \(\chi^2_{l^\pm A} = 736\) (1.04 per point) and \(\chi^2_{\nu A} = 4277\) (1.36 per point).
- The fit using only neutrino data (weight \(w=\infty\)) gave \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.33\) (4192 total points).
- Standard kinematic cuts applied were \(Q > 2\) GeV and \(W > 3.5\) GeV, following Pumplin (2002).

## Context

This work extends the nPDF analysis framework of Schienbein (2009) and Kovarik (2010) by explicitly testing compatibility between charged lepton and neutrino DIS data. The discrepancy first noted by Schienbein (2007) is confirmed: NuTeV data show weaker shadowing at low and intermediate \(x\) than predicted by charged-lepton-based nPDFs. The inability to find a consistent description poses problems for including NuTeV data in proton PDF analyses, and the paper suggests that uncorrelated systematic errors in NuTeV data would allow a compatible fit, pointing to the need for careful treatment of systematic uncertainties in future global analyses.
