# On the Implications of Energy and Momentum Conservation for Particle Emission in A+A Collisions at SPS Energies

> We construct a simple model of heavy ion collisions, local in the impact parameter plane, and appropriate for the SPS energy range. This model can be regarded as a new realization of the "fire-streak" approach, originally applied to studies of lower energy nucleus-nucleus reactions. Starting from local energy and momentum conservation, we nicely describe the broadening of the pion rapidity distribution when going from central to peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=17.3 GeV. The results of our calculations are compared with SPS experimental data. We discuss the resulting implications on the role of energy and momentum conservation for the dynamics of particle production in heavy ion collisions. A specific space-time picture emerges, where the longitudinal evolution of the system strongly depends on the position in the impact parameter (${b_x}$, ${b_y}$) plane. This picture is consistent with our earlier findings on the longitudinal evolution of the system as deduced from electromagnetic effects on charged pion directed flow, and can provide an explanation for specific low-$p_T$ phenomena seen in the fragmentation region of Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the centrality-dependent broadening of pion rapidity distributions in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies can be explained solely by local energy and momentum conservation in the initial state, without invoking detailed microscopic dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma or hadronization, provided the hadronization function is universal across impact parameter and transverse position.

## Method

The authors construct a "fire-streak" model where the transverse plane perpendicular to the collision axis is divided into 1 fm × 1 fm bins (strips), and for each strip the masses from nucleus A and B are calculated from a realistic nuclear density distribution. Energy and momentum conservation is applied locally to each strip to determine its rapidity and excitation energy, and pions are produced from each strip via a universal modified exponential function F(ξ) = A exp(−|ξ|^n / (n σ_y^n)) that is independent of strip position and collision centrality, with parameters σ_y = 1.475 and n = 2.55 fitted globally to NA49 data.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 17.3 GeV (top SPS energy)
- Beam rapidity: ±2.9 units at top SPS energy
- Strip bin size: 1 fm × 1 fm in the transverse plane
- Impact parameters used: b = 9.72 fm (most peripheral, "C4"), 8.41 fm, 6.64 fm, 4.74 fm, 2.55 fm (most central, "C0")
- Maximum excitation energy density in peripheral collisions: 25 GeV/fm² in the (b̃_x, b̃_y) plane
- Maximum excitation energy density in central collisions: 35 GeV/fm² in the centre of the (b̃_x, b̃_y) plane
- Fitted parameters: σ_y = 1.475, n = 2.55
- Normalization constant A varies by ±2% across centrality classes, compared to a >300% increase in absolute dn/dy density from peripheral to central collisions

## Context

This work extends the "fire-streak" approach originally developed by Myers (1978) and applied by Gosset, Kapusta, and Westfall (1978) to lower-energy nuclear collisions, now adapted to SPS energies where quark-gluon plasma formation is expected. The model is consistent with the authors' earlier findings on electromagnetic effects on charged pion directed flow (Rybicki et al., various years), which suggested that faster pions are produced closer to the spectator system. The approach enables future studies of electromagnetic effects on multiparticle production and provides a natural explanation for low-p_T phenomena in the fragmentation region of Pb+Pb collisions.
