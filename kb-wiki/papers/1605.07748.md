# Freezeout conditions in proton-proton collisions at the top RHIC and LHC energies

> The freezeout conditions in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}= 200$, $900$ and $7000$ GeV have been extracted by fits to the mean hadron yields at mid-rapidity within the framework of the statistical model of an ideal gas of hadrons and resonances in the grand canonical ensemble. The variation of the extracted freezeout thermal parameters and the goodness of the fits with $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}$ are discussed. We find the extracted temperature and baryon chemical potential of the freezeout surface to be similar in p+p and heavy ion collisions. On the other hand, the thermal behaviour of the strange hadrons is qualitatively different in p+p as compared to A+A. We find an additional parameter accounting for non-equilibrium strangeness production is essential for describing the p+p data. This is in contrast to A+A where the non-equilibrium framework could be successfully replaced by a sequential and complete equilibrium model with an early freezeout of the strange hadrons.

## Key claim

The extracted freezeout temperature and baryon chemical potential in proton-proton collisions are similar to those in heavy ion collisions, but the thermal behavior of strange hadrons is qualitatively different, requiring an additional non-equilibrium strangeness suppression parameter (gamma_S) that is not needed for heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed fits of mid-rapidity hadron yields from proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 900, and 7000 GeV using the statistical model of an ideal gas of hadrons and resonances in the grand canonical ensemble, implemented via the THERMUS code. They extracted the chemical freezeout temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, fireball volume V (expressed as radius R), and the strangeness undersaturation factor gamma_S by minimizing chi-squared between model predictions and experimental data for pions, kaons, protons, Lambdas, Xis, Omegas, and phi mesons. At LHC energies, mu_B was fixed to zero.

## Key numbers

* Freezeout temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: T = 162.2 +/- 3.6 MeV
* Freezeout temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 900 GeV: T = 155.4 +/- 2.4 MeV
* Freezeout temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 7000 GeV: T = 152.9 +/- 2.0 MeV
* Strangeness suppression factor at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: gamma_S = 0.54 +/- 0.03
* Strangeness suppression factor at sqrt(s_NN) = 900 GeV: gamma_S = 0.73 +/- 0.03
* Strangeness suppression factor at sqrt(s_NN) = 7000 GeV: gamma_S = 0.75 +/- 0.02
* Fireball radius at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: R = 1.3 +/- 0.08 fm
* Fireball radius at sqrt(s_NN) = 7000 GeV: R = 1.69 +/- 0.05 fm
* Baryon chemical potential at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: mu_B = 14.4 +/- 8.5 MeV
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 1.8
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom at sqrt(s_NN) = 7000 GeV: 2.8

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model analyses of proton-proton collisions, which had mostly used a canonical treatment of strangeness (as in Cleymans et al. (2011)), by applying the grand canonical ensemble to mid-rapidity data. The results are compared to heavy ion collision analyses from Andronic et al. (2012) and show that while temperature and baryon chemical potential agree between the two systems, the strangeness suppression factor gamma_S is significantly below unity in proton-proton collisions (around 0.8 at LHC energies) versus consistent with unity in heavy ion collisions. The paper finds that a single freezeout scheme with gamma_S works better for proton-proton collisions than the two-freezeout scheme that successfully describes the "proton anomaly" in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. This suggests that the shorter fireball lifetime in proton-proton collisions prevents sequential freezeout of strange hadrons.
