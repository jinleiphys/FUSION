# Production of soft $e^+e^-$ Pairs in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC by Semi-coherent Two Photon Processes

> We calculate the contribution of the two photon production process into $e^+e^-$ spectra, and compare the results with experimental data from the PHENIX detector at RHIC. We study the contribution given by ``semi-coherent" kinematics, in which one photon is relatively hard and is incoherently emitted by participating protons, while another can be soft enough to be in a coherent domain.

## Key claim

The semi-coherent two-photon production mechanism, in which one photon is emitted coherently by the entire nucleus while the other resolves individual protons or quarks, contributes to the soft e+e- pair yield in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, but this contribution is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the experimental data from PHENIX and therefore cannot explain the observed "cold" dilepton component.

## Method

The authors used the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) to calculate the differential cross section for dilepton production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC. They introduced a "semi-coherent" kinematics where one photon has small transverse momentum and sees the nucleus as a coherent Woods-Saxon charge distribution, while the other photon has larger transverse momentum and resolves individual protons (using a frozen-nucleon form factor) or, in an extension, individual quarks (using parton distribution functions from CTEQ). The calculated invariant yield was compared to PHENIX data after applying the same single-track acceptance condition (p_perp > 0.2 GeV) and restricting to impact parameters b < 2R to match the experimental centrality selection.

## Key numbers

- The semi-coherent two-photon contribution to the e+e- yield is about **two orders of magnitude smaller** than the experimental PHENIX data for invariant mass ranges 300-500 MeV, 500-750 MeV, and 810-990 MeV, for total transverse momenta up to about 0.7 GeV.
- For the mass range 300-500 MeV, the single-track acceptance condition was relaxed from p_perp > 0.2 GeV to **p_perp > 0.1 GeV** in the calculation.
- The total cross section normalization uses sigma_total ~ 4 pi R^2 ~ **1.4 x 10^4 GeV^{-2}** for Au, with N_part = 109 for the 0-92% centrality bin.
- For the LHC energy range (gamma ~ 3400) with ALICE acceptance (|eta| < 0.9, p_perp > 0.1 GeV), the unrestricted semi-coherent yield shows an enhancement of **one order of magnitude** compared to the RHIC/PHENIX result, but when ultraperipheral collisions are excluded the yields are similar within a factor of about **2**.
- Resolving down to quarks (using x_min = 0.0025) increases the yield by about **one order of magnitude** compared to resolving only protons, for transverse momenta above 0.1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the classic Landau-Lifshitz (1934) theory of coherent two-photon dilepton production in nuclear collisions to the semi-coherent regime relevant for near-central heavy ion collisions, which had not been previously considered. The authors were motivated by the "cold" dilepton component observed by PHENIX (2009) at small transverse momentum (p_t < 500 MeV) with an effective slope T_eff ~ 100 MeV, which could not be explained by the standard hadronic cocktail plus charm decays. Although the two-photon mechanism cannot explain the PHENIX puzzles, the paper suggests that with reduced magnetic field at RHIC the semi-coherent dileptons could become detectable, and it proposes that similar semi-coherent processes may exist in QCD, particularly for the "ridge" phenomenon observed by STAR, analogous to the color glass condensate framework of McLerran (1993).
