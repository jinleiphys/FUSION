# Probing the QCD phase diagram with fluctuations

> The relevance of higher order cumulants of conserved charges for the analysis of freeze-out and critical conditions in heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC is discussed. Using properties of $O(4)$ scaling functions, the generic structure of these higher cumulants at vanishing baryon chemical potential is discussed. Chiral model calculations are then used to study their properties at non-zero baryon chemical potential. It is argued that the rapid variation of sixth and higher order cumulants at the phase boundary may be used to explore the QCD phase diagram in experiment. Moreover, results for the Polyakov loop susceptibilities in SU(3) lattice gauge theory as well as in (2+1) flavor lattice QCD are discussed. An analysis of the ratios of susceptibilities indicates that the deconfinement transition is reflected in characteristic modifications of these ratios.

## Key claim

The rapid variation of sixth and higher order cumulants of net baryon number at the phase boundary, which stems from the singular part of the free energy in the chiral limit, may be used to experimentally explore the QCD phase diagram, provided freeze-out occurs close to the chiral crossover temperature.

## Method

The generic structure of higher-order cumulants of net baryon number at vanishing baryon chemical potential was derived using O(4) scaling functions of the 3-dimensional spin model. Chiral model calculations using the Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model within the functional renormalization group framework were then employed to study these cumulants at non-zero baryon chemical potential. Additionally, Polyakov loop susceptibilities and their ratios were computed in SU(3) lattice gauge theory and in (2+1) flavor lattice QCD using the HISQ action.

## Key numbers

* The specific-heat critical exponent for O(4) spin systems in 3 dimensions is α = -0.2131, which implies that all cumulants of net baryon number of order n ≤ 4 remain finite at the second-order transition in the chiral limit, while the first divergent cumulant is obtained for n = 6.
* With O(4) critical exponents α = -0.2131 and ν = 0.7377, the sixth order cumulant scales as χ₆ᴮ ∼ -ξ¹·¹ and the eighth order cumulant as χ₈ᴮ ∼ -ξ²·⁴ at the critical temperature t = 0, where ξ is the correlation length.
* The curvature parameter κ_q ≈ 0.06 relates the dependence of the reduced temperature on the quark chemical potential.
* In the Polyakov loop susceptibility analysis, lattice calculations were performed on N_σ³ × N_τ lattices with temporal lattice sizes N_τ = 4, 6, 8 and spatial extensions N_σ varying from 16 to 64.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical fluctuations at the QCD phase boundary using higher-order cumulants of conserved charges, building on prior lattice calculations by the HotQCD collaboration and others (Ejiri, 2010; Allton, 2005; Schmidt, 2011) that showed the magnetic equation of state is in the O(N) scaling regime for physical light quark masses. The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model serves as a baseline for comparison, as it describes bulk observables but does not exhibit critical behavior. The results enable experimental searches for critical fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC, with preliminary STAR data (Chen, 2013; Adamczyk, 2013) already showing suppression of net-proton cumulants relative to the HRG result.
