# Systematic investigations of deep sub-barrier fusion reactions using an adiabatic approach

> To describe fusion hindrance observed in fusion reactions at extremely low incident energies, I propose a novel extension of the standard CC model by introducing a damping factor that describes a smooth transition from sudden to adiabatic processes. I demonstrate the performance of this model by systematically investigating various deep sub-barrier fusion reactions. I extend the standard CC model by introducing a damping factor into the coupling matrix elements in the standard CC model. I adopt the Yukawa-plus-exponential (YPE) model as a basic heavy ion-ion potential, which is advantageous for a unified description of the one- and two-body potentials. For the purpose of these systematic investigations, I approximate the one-body potential with a third-order polynomial function based on the YPE model. Calculated fusion cross sections for the medium-heavy mass systems of $^{64}$Ni + $^{64}$Ni, $^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni, and $^{58}$Ni + $^{54}$Fe, the medium-light mass systems of $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca, and $^{24}$Mg + $^{30}$Si, and the mass-asymmetric systems of $^{48}$Ca + $^{96}$Zr and $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb are consistent with the experimental data. The astrophysical S factor and logarithmic derivative representations of these are also in good agreement with the experimental data. Since the results calculated with the damping factor are in excellent agreement with the experimental data in all systems, I conclude that the smooth transition from the sudden to adiabatic processes occurs and that a coordinate-dependent coupling strength is responsible for the fusion hindrance. In all systems, the potential energies at the touching point $V_{\rm Touch}$ strongly correlate with the incident threshold energies for which the fusion hindrance starts to emerge, except for the medium-light mass systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies arises from a smooth transition from sudden to adiabatic processes, modeled by introducing a coordinate-dependent damping factor into the coupling matrix elements of the standard coupled-channels (CC) model, rather than from the Pauli principle effects assumed in the sudden approach.

## Method

The author extends the standard CC model by inserting a Gaussian damping factor Φ(r,λ_α) into the nuclear coupling form factor, which gradually reduces the excitation strength of vibrational states when the two colliding nuclei overlap (r < R_d + λ_α). The Yukawa-plus-exponential (YPE) model is adopted as the basic heavy ion-ion potential, and the one-body potential in the overlapping region is approximated by a third-order polynomial function based on the YPE model, smoothly joined to the two-body potential at the touching point and to the compound nucleus ground state energy from experimental masses. CC calculations with the damping factor are performed using a modified version of the CCFULL code (CCFULLYPE) for eight reaction systems.

## Key numbers

- For the medium-heavy systems, the damping radius parameter r_d values are: 1.298 fm (⁶⁴Ni + ⁶⁴Ni), 1.310 fm (⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni), and 1.330 fm (⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁴Fe); the damping diffuseness a_d values are: 1.05 fm, 1.32 fm, and 1.25 fm, respectively.
- The potential energy at the touching point V_Touch for the medium-heavy systems is: 88.6 MeV (⁶⁴Ni + ⁶⁴Ni), 95.3 MeV (⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni), and 86.8 MeV (⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁴Fe).
- The YPE potential radius parameter r_0 is 1.205 fm for ⁶⁴Ni + ⁶⁴Ni, 1.180 fm for ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni, and 1.198 fm for ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁴Fe; the diffuseness a_0 is 0.68 fm for all three.
- The compound state curvature ℏω_GS is estimated by the systematic formula ℏω_GS = 0.0047x² − 0.4586x + 9.125 MeV, where x = A_T^(1/3)·A_P^(1/3).
- For the ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni system, the nuclear deformation parameter β_λ^(Nucl) for the 2⁺ state is 0.226, which is 20% larger than the Coulomb deformation parameter β_λ^(Coul) = 0.187.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic approach previously proposed by Ichikawa et al. (2007, 2009) for describing fusion hindrance, and contrasts with the sudden approach of Misicu and Esbensen (2006) which attributes hindrance to Pauli blocking effects using a frozen-density potential. The paper systematically tests the adiabatic model across medium-heavy, medium-light, and mass-asymmetric systems, showing that the damping factor reproduces experimental data where standard CC calculations fail. The results enable future investigations into the average angular momentum of compound nuclei as a distinguishing observable between the adiabatic and sudden models, and support the correlation between V_Touch and the threshold energy for fusion hindrance previously found by Ichikawa (2007).
