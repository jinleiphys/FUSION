# Combinatorial nuclear level-density model

> A microscopic nuclear level-density model is presented. The model is a completely combinatorial (micro-canonical) model based on the folded-Yukawa single-particle potential and includes explicit treatment of pairing, rotational and vibrational states. The microscopic character of all states enables extraction of level distribution functions with respect to pairing gaps, parity and angular momentum. The results of the model are compared to available experimental data: neutron separation energy level spacings, data on total level-density functions from the Oslo method, cumulative level densities from low-lying discrete states, and data on parity ratios.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully combinatorial (micro-canonical) nuclear level-density model based on the folded-Yukawa single-particle potential that includes explicit treatment of pairing, rotational, and vibrational states, with the hedge that no additional parameters are introduced beyond those already fixed in the FRLDM mass model, and that the model is limited to the lowest ten million or so states, covering excitation energies from the ground state to well above the neutron resonance region.

## Method

The model constructs all possible many-particle-many-hole excitations (up to n<10 for both protons and neutrons) on the folded-Yukawa single-particle potential ground state, solving BCS equations individually for each of approximately 10^7 configurations per nucleus to obtain pairing gaps and energies. Rotational bands are added combinatorially on each intrinsic band-head for deformed nuclei (|ε₂| ≥ 0.05) using a pairing-dependent moment of inertia, while vibrational states are included microscopically via the Quasi-particle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) with a double-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction. A many-body damping width is applied as a Gaussian smearing with width Γ = 0.039 (A/160)^(-1/2) E^(3/2) MeV to account for residual interactions.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength parameter for the particle-number-projected BCS model is 4.5 MeV, determined from the folded-Yukawa macroscopic-microscopic mass model.
- For ¹⁶²Dy at 8.4 MeV excitation energy, 36% of levels still have a non-zero proton pairing gap, with mean pairing gap ⟨Δ⟩ = 0.2 MeV.
- The rotational enhancement factor for ¹⁶²Dy is approximately a factor of 5 at the neutron separation energy.
- The vibrational enhancement factor for ¹⁶²Dy is close to 1% at 7 MeV excitation energy.
- The spreading width is given by Γ = 0.039 (A/160)^(-1/2) E^(3/2) MeV.
- The typical bin size for level-density binning is ΔE = 30–50 keV.
- For ⁶⁸Zn, the neutron separation energy S_n = 6.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic-macroscopic FRLDM model (Möller et al., various years), which previously described nuclear masses, fission barriers, ground-state spins, and beta-decay properties, to now also calculate nuclear level densities globally for all nuclei heavier than ¹⁶O. The combinatorial approach contrasts with simpler Fermi-gas models (Bethe, Ericson) and phenomenological extensions (Rauscher, Gilbert-Cameron) by providing microscopic distributions of pairing gaps, parity, and angular momentum without adjustable parameters. The model enables comparison with Oslo-method experimental data and parity-ratio measurements (Richter et al.), and can be used in Hauser-Feshbach reaction calculations for astrophysical processes and nuclear fission.
