# Jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be used to probe properties of hot and dense quark-gluon plasma. We provide a brief introduction to the concept and framework for the study of jet quenching. Different approaches and implementation of multiple scattering and parton energy loss are discussed. Recent progresses in the theoretical and phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC are reviewed.

# Jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be used to probe properties of hot and dense quark-gluon plasma, but the review notes that there is currently no formal proof of factorization for the widely used phenomenological formula that separates initial hard scattering from medium-induced parton energy loss. The assumption of factorization is motivated by consistency with various phenomenological studies rather than rigorous derivation.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical frameworks for jet quenching, including the BDMPS-Z, GLV, ASW, AMY, and higher-twist (HT) formalisms for radiative energy loss, as well as collisional energy loss calculations. It describes how single gluon emission kernels are computed under common approximations (eikonal, collinear, and localized momentum transfer), and how multiple gluon emission is treated via Poisson ansatz, transport models (including the Linear Boltzmann Transport model), and modified DGLAP evolution equations. The review also covers the factorized cross section formula for inclusive hadron production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, incorporating cold nuclear matter effects through nuclear PDF modifications and hot nuclear matter effects through parton-medium interaction functions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons in most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (LHC) shows strong suppression, while R_AA for high p_T photons is consistent with unity
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for identified hadrons in most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC) shows strong suppression
- The jet transport coefficient q_hat = d<Delta p_T^2>/dL characterizes the mean transverse momentum squared exchanged per unit path length in the BDMPS-Z and ASW-MS formalisms
- The formation time for medium-induced radiation is tau_f ~ 2*omega/k_perp^2, where omega is the gluon energy and k_perp is its transverse momentum
- Nuclear PDF modification factors R_i^A(x, Q^2) are shown at Q^2 = 1.69 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 100 GeV^2 for lead nucleus from EPS09NLO, HKN07, and nDS parameterizations

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework established by Bjorken (1982) on collisional energy loss and by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (1999-2000), Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, and Zakharov (1996-1997), and others on radiative energy loss formalisms. It summarizes recent phenomenological studies comparing model calculations to experimental data from RHIC and LHC collaborations including PHENIX and CMS. The review enables future work on reconciling different formalisms, incorporating finite formation time effects consistently, and extending calculations beyond the single-scattering and eikonal approximations.
