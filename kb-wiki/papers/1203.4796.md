# Interaction of vector mesons with baryons and vectors in the nuclear medium

> In this talk we present a short review of recent developments concerning the interaction of vector mesons with baryons and with nuclei. We begin with the hidden gauge formalism for the interaction of vector mesons, then review results for vector baryon interaction and in particular the resonances which appear as composite states, dynamically generated from the interaction of vector mesons with baryons. New developments concerning the mixing of these states with pseudoscalars and baryons are also reported. We include some discussion on the $5/2^+$ $Δ$ resonances around 2000 MeV, where we suggest that the $Δ(2000)5/2^+$ resonance, which comes in the PDG from averaging a set of resonances appearing around 1700 MeV and another one around 2200 MeV, corresponds indeed to two distinct resonances. We also report on a hidden charm baryon state around 4400 MeV coming from the interaction of vector mesons and baryons with charm, and how this state has some repercussion in the $J/ψ$ suppression in nuclei. The interaction of $K^*$ in nuclei is also reported and suggestions are made to measure by means of the transparency ratio the huge width in the medium that the theoretical calculations predict. The formalism is extended to $J/ψ$ interaction with nuclei and the transparency ratio for $J/ψ$ photoproduction in nuclei is studied and shown to be a good tool to find possible baryon states which couple to $J/ψN$.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the PDG's single entry for the Δ(2000)5/2⁺ resonance, which averages states around 1700 MeV and 2200 MeV, actually corresponds to two distinct resonances, and they provide quantitative theoretical support for this interpretation from a three-body Δρπ calculation.

## Method

The authors use the hidden gauge formalism for vector meson interactions, constructing scattering amplitudes for vector meson–baryon (VB) and vector–vector (VV) systems via vector-meson exchange diagrams. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe–Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, incorporating unitarity, and for the VB case they also include intermediate pseudoscalar–baryon (PB) states via box diagrams with Kroll–Ruderman contact terms required by gauge invariance. For the Δρπ system they apply the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations, and for the K* in nuclei they compute the self-energy self-consistently from both the K*→Kπ decay in medium and the K*N interaction in the Fermi sea.

## Key numbers

- The K*⁻ width in vacuum from the calculation is 42 MeV, close to the experimental value 50.8 ± 0.9 MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ and zero momentum, the K*⁻ acquires a width of about 260 MeV, roughly five times its vacuum width.
- A hidden charm baryon state is found at a pole position of 4415 − 9.5i MeV (mass 4412 MeV, total width 47.3 MeV) coupling to J/ψN with a partial width of 19.2 MeV.
- Two Δ* states from the Δρπ system are predicted at masses 1770 MeV and 1875 MeV, with a hint of a third around 2200 MeV.
- The VB interaction generates nine dynamically generated resonances; for example, the N(1650) 1/2⁻ is found at 1658 MeV with width 98 MeV, and the N(1700) 3/2⁻ at 1705 MeV with width 103 MeV after including PB channels.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to pseudoscalar–baryon interactions (Kaiser 1995, Oller 2000, Jido 2003) into the vector meson sector, building on the hidden gauge formalism (Bando 1985, Harada 2003). It also extends earlier work on ρΔ interactions (Vijande 2009) and ρρ interactions (Molina 2008) to the full SU(3) vector–baryon and vector–vector systems. The predictions for K* in nuclei enable experimental tests via transparency ratios in photoproduction reactions, and the hidden charm baryon states provide a mechanism for J/ψ suppression in nuclei that can be studied in future experiments.
