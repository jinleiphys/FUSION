# Expressions for the number of J=0 pairs in even-even Ti isotopes

> We count the number of pairs in the single j-shell model of {44}Ti for various interactions. For a state of total angular momentum I, the wave function can be written in terms of the probability amplitude D(Jp Jn) that the protons couple to Jp and the neutrons to Jn. For I=0 there are three states with (I=0,T=0) and one with (I=0,T=2). The latter is the double analog of {44}Ca. In that case (T=2), the magnitude of D(JJ) is the same as that of a corresponding two-particle fractional parentage coefficient. In counting the number of pairs with an even angular momentum J, we find a new relationship obtained by diagonalizing a unitary nine-j symbol. We are also able to get results for the `no-interaction' case for T=0 states, for which it is found, e.g., that there are less (J=1,T=0) pairs than on the average. Relative to this `no-interaction case', we find for the most realistic interaction used that there is an enhancement of pairs with angular momentum J=0,2,1 and 7, and a depletion for the others. Also considered are interactions in which only the (J=0,T=1) pair state is at lower energy, only the (J=1,T=0) pair state is lowered and where both are equally lowered, as well as the QQ interaction. We are also able to obtain simplified formulae for the number of J=0 pairs for the I=0 states in {46}Ti and {48}Ti by noting that the unique state with isospin |Tz|+2 is orthogonal to all the states with isospin |Tz|.

## Key claim

The paper derives simplified analytic expressions for the number of pairs with a given angular momentum in even-even Ti isotopes within the single \(j\)-shell model, but the results are restricted to the \(f_{7/2}\) shell and to states with total angular momentum \(I=0\); the relationship for even \(J_{12}\) pairs in \(^{44}\)Ti holds for any isospin-conserving interaction, while the simplified formulae for \(J=0\) pairs in \(^{46}\)Ti and \(^{48}\)Ti rely on the orthogonality of the unique \(T_{max}\) state to all \(T_{min}\) states.

## Method

The authors work in the single \(j\)-shell model with valence nucleons in the \(f_{7/2}\) orbital. For \(^{44}\)Ti (two valence protons and two valence neutrons), they write the \(I=0\) wave function as a sum over proton-neutron coupling amplitudes \(D(JJ)\) and count the number of \(nn\), \(pp\), and \(np\) pairs with angular momentum \(J_{12}\) using unitary nine-\(j\) symbols and coefficients of fractional parentage. For \(^{46}\)Ti and \(^{48}\)Ti, they exploit the orthogonality between the unique state with isospin \(T_{max}=|T_z|+2\) and all states with isospin \(T_{min}=|T_z|\) to obtain simplified expressions for the number of \(J=0\) pairs.

## Key numbers

* For the \((I=0,T=2)\) state of \(^{44}\)Ti, the number of \(J_{12}=0\) pairs is 1.5 and the number of \(J_{12}=6\) pairs is \((2\times6+1)/6 = 13/6 \approx 2.167\).
* For the \((I=0,T=2)\) state of \(^{44}\)Ti, the amplitudes are \(D(00)=0.5\), \(D(22)=-0.3727\), \(D(44)=-0.5\), \(D(66)=-0.600\).
* For the \(T=0\) ground state of \(^{44}\)Ti with the realistic \(^{42}\)Sc spectrum interaction (case E), the number of \(J_{12}=0\) pairs is 1.8617, \(J_{12}=1\) pairs is 0.6752, \(J_{12}=2\) pairs is 0.9458, and \(J_{12}=7\) pairs is 1.8945.
* In the no-interaction case for \(^{44}\)Ti \(T=0\) states, the number of \(J_{12}=1\) pairs is 0.432, compared to an equal-distribution average of 0.75.
* For the \((J=0,T=1)\) pairing interaction in \(^{44}\)Ti, the number of \(J_{12}=0\) pairs is 2.25 and the number of \(J_{12}=1\) pairs is 0.250.
* For \(^{46}\)Ti with \(T=T_{min}\), the number of \(np\) pairs with \(J_{12}=0\) is \(|D(00)|^2/2\); for \(^{48}\)Ti it is \(|D(00)|^2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the pair-counting formalism of Zamick and collaborators (2004) by deriving simplified expressions that were not obtained in that earlier study. The results for the \(T=2\) state connect to the double analog state in \(^{44}\)Ca, and the orthogonality method for \(^{46}\)Ti and \(^{48}\)Ti generalizes the approach of Engel, Langanke, and Vogel (1996), who treated only isovector pairing interactions. The findings enable future comparisons with two-nucleon transfer experiments and with number-projected generalized BCS calculations by Raduta and Moya de Guerra.
