# Possible $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ in the initial-state polarized $γN\rightarrow K^{+} Σ^*(1385) \rightarrow K^{+} πΛ$ reaction near threshold

> By using an effective Lagrangian method, we study the effects of a newly proposed $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ state with mass around 1380 MeV in the initial-state polarized $γN\rightarrow K^{+} Σ^*(1385) \rightarrow K^{+} πΛ$ process near threshold. The theoretical predictions for the helicity cross sections $σ_{3/2}$, $σ_{1/2}$ as well as their ratios, and the angular distributions of $π$ in the $πΛ$ center-of-mass system are given. It is found that assuming $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ exists or not, these physical quantities are distinctly different. So our results could be useful for the investigation of the existence of $Σ^*(1/2^-)$ when the experimental data are available in the future.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the ratio of helicity cross sections and the angular distribution of the final pion in the initial-state polarized γN → K⁺ Σ*(1385) → K⁺ πΛ reaction near threshold will be distinctly different depending on whether a proposed Σ*(1/2⁻) state with mass around 1380 MeV exists or not, but these predictions are conditional on future experimental data being available for verification.

## Method
The authors used a gauge-invariant effective Lagrangian approach to compute helicity cross sections σ₃/₂ and σ₁/₂ for the γN → K⁺ Σ* → K⁺ πΛ reaction near threshold, considering t-channel K meson exchange, s-channel N and Δ resonances exchange, u-channel Λ and Σ* exchange, and contact terms. They performed calculations for two scenarios: assuming only the known Σ*(3/2⁺) exists, and assuming both Σ*(3/2⁺) and the proposed Σ*(1/2⁻) exist, using coupling constants and form factors from previous work by Nakayama (2008) and Gao (2010). The angular distributions of the pion in the πΛ center-of-mass system were also computed at an excess energy Q = 20 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The ratio σ₃/₂/σ₁/₂ for the γp → K⁺ Σ*⁰ → K⁺ π⁰ Λ process is about 40 assuming only Σ*(3/2⁺) exists, and about 3 assuming both Σ*(3/2⁺) and Σ*(1/2⁻) exist.
- For the γn → K⁺ Σ*⁻ → K⁺ π⁻ Λ process, the ratio σ₃/₂/σ₁/₂ from pure Σ*(1/2⁻) is zero at threshold and reaches about one when Q = 10 MeV.
- The coupling constant for KNΣ*(1/2⁻) is g_{KNΣ*_{1/2}} = 1.34 ± 0.07.
- The coupling constant for ΛπΣ*(1/2⁻) is g_{ΛπΣ*_{1/2}} = 2.12 ± 0.33.
- The decay width of Σ*(1/2⁻) is Γ_{Σ*(1/2⁻)} = 0.119^{+0.055}_{-0.035} GeV.
- The cutoff parameter Λ_M = 1.6 GeV is used for Σ*(1/2⁻) in the t-channel form factor, compared to Λ_M = 0.83 GeV for Σ*(3/2⁺).

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Gao (2010) and Nakayama (2008) on KΣ* photoproduction, where including a Σ*(1/2⁻) state resolved discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental beam asymmetry data from the LEPS Collaboration. The paper also builds on evidence for Σ*(1/2⁻) near 1380 MeV found in K⁻p → Λπ⁺π⁻ by Wu (2009, 2010) and in KΛπ and KΣπ photoproduction by Gao (2010) and Moriya. The predictions enable future experiments with polarized photons and polarized nucleon targets to test the existence of this penta-quark-predicted state, which would help validate penta-quark models proposed by Helminen and Zhu.
