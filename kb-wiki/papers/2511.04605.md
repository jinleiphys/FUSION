# The size of the quark-gluon plasma in ultracentral collisions: impact of initial density fluctuations on the average transverse momentum

> Recent experiments have shown that the mean transverse momentum $\langle p_T\rangle$ of outgoing particles increases as a function of the particle multiplicity in ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collisions at collider energies. This increase was originally predicted on the basis of simulations where the multiplicity increase occurred at constant volume, so that it implied a larger density and temperature. However, recent state-of-the-art simulations have shown that, for some models of initial condition, the volume may vary with the multiplicity in ultracentral collisions. We elucidate this effect by analytically relating the variation of the volume to the radial distribution of the one- and two-point functions of the fluctuating density field. We show that the volume variation is small if the total entropy of the ultracentral collisions scales with the mass number of the colliding isotopes. We argue that probing detailed transverse distributions of initial-state fluctuations through the ultracentral $\langle p_T\rangle$ has nontrivial implications for models of nuclear structure and of the pre-equilibrium stages.

# The size of the quark-gluon plasma in ultracentral collisions: impact of initial density fluctuations on the average transverse momentum

## Key claim

The variation of the quark-gluon plasma transverse size with multiplicity in ultracentral collisions depends on the radial distribution of entropy density fluctuations, and this variation is small if the total entropy scales with the mass number of the colliding isotopes; the increase of mean transverse momentum with multiplicity can therefore constrain the spatial dependence of the two-point function of the initial entropy density field.

## Method

The authors use the TRENTO model of initial conditions with a generalized exponent nu in the entropy density parametrization s proportional to (t_A t_B)^nu, where t_A and t_B are nuclear thickness functions. They simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for various nu values, tuning the gamma fluctuation parameter k to match the tail of the measured multiplicity distribution. They then analytically relate the variation of the rms transverse size R^2 with total entropy S to the one-point and two-point functions of the fluctuating density field, deriving a linearized expression for the conditional average of R^2 at fixed S.

## Key numbers

- The relative variance of the entropy at b=0 is constrained to (sigma_S / S_knee)^2 approximately 2 x 10^(-3), inferred from experimental multiplicity distributions.
- The nucleon width is set to w_p = 0.5 fm in the TRENTO model simulations.
- The effective volume relation from hydrodynamic simulations is V_eff approximately 0.9 pi (R sqrt(2))^3.
- The difference between the left and right sides of the constant-size relation d ln<p_T> = c_s^2 d ln N_ch is smaller than 0.01 for a broad range of collision energies, freeze-out temperatures, and equations of state.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Gardim et al. (2019, 2024) and Gavassino et al. (2025), who established that the mean transverse momentum increase with multiplicity in ultracentral collisions is related to the speed of sound under the assumption of constant transverse size. It addresses the critique by Nijs and van der Schee (2023) that the volume may vary with multiplicity depending on the initial-state model. The results enable future quantitative constraints on the spatial distribution of initial-state fluctuations from precision measurements of the mean transverse momentum slope in ultracentral collisions, with implications for models of nuclear structure and pre-equilibrium dynamics.
