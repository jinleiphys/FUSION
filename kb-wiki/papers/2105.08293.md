# Helicity polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the helicity polarization which can be locally induced from both vorticity and helicity charge in non-central heavy ion collisions. Helicity charge redistribution can be generated in viscous fluid and contributes to azimuthal asymmetry of the polarization along global angular momentum or beam momentum. We also discuss on detecting the initial net helicity charge from topological charge fluctuation or initial color longitudinal field by the helicity polarization correlation of two hyperons and the helicity alignment of vector mesons in central heavy ion collisions.

# Helicity polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Helicity polarization, spin polarization along a hadron's own momentum, can be induced locally from both thermal vorticity and helicity charge redistribution in non-central heavy ion collisions, and the contribution from helicity charge redistribution requires viscous fluid dynamics to occur from an initially zero axial charge.

## Method
The author derives the helicity polarization from the single-particle mean spin vector formula using thermal vorticity, then extends the analysis to include axial charge redistribution via chiral kinetic theory and relativistic hydrodynamics with dissipative terms. The helicity polarization correlation for two hyperons and the helicity alignment (spin-density matrix element ρ₀₀) for vector mesons are formulated as observables to detect initial net helicity charge from topological charge fluctuations or color longitudinal fields.

## Key numbers
- Helicity polarization S^h ≈ (Y/mT) (dT/dτ) v₂(p_T) sin 2φ at small rapidity, where Y is rapidity, T is temperature, τ is proper time, and v₂ is elliptic flow.
- The axial charge density n₅ = (1/3) μ₅ T² and anomalous transport coefficient ξ₅ = (1/6) T² for a free fermion system.
- The deviation of the vector meson spin-density matrix element from 1/3 is Δ ≡ ρ₀₀ − 1/3 ≈ −(4/9) P_q^h P_ȳ^h for small quark/antiquark helicity polarization.
- At Y=0, the averaged spin-density matrix elements are: ρ̄ˣ₀₀ = 1/3 + (1/4)Δ + (3/4)v₂Δ, ρ̄ʸ₀₀ = 1/3 + (1/4)Δ − (3/4)v₂Δ, and ρ̄ᶻ₀₀ = 1/3 − (1/2)Δ.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of spin polarization in heavy ion collisions beyond the transverse polarization along global angular momentum and longitudinal polarization along beam momentum, which were the focus of most prior studies (Becattini 2013, Becattini and Karpenko 2017). The helicity polarization is distinguished from longitudinal polarization by being rapidity-odd and vanishing at Y=0. The axial charge redistribution mechanism builds on the chiral separation effect (Gao 2012) and the anomalous transport formalism (Landsteiner 2011). The proposed two-hyperon correlation method extends earlier work by Pang (2016) and Becattini (2020), while the helicity alignment analysis follows the recombination model of Liang (2004). The results enable experimental searches for initial net helicity charge in central collisions, where the strong spin alignment observed by STAR and ALICE, inconsistent with hyperon polarization, may indicate large local polarization fluctuations rather than global helicity charge.
