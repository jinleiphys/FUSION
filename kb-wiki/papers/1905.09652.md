# Kramers's escape rate problem within a non-Markovian description

> We compare the thermal escape rates of a Brownian particle, initially trapped into one of the two wells of an asymmetric double-well potential, for thermal Markovian and non-Markovian noise. The Markovian treatment of this problem goes originally back to the studies of Kramers in 1940 and is therefore often referred to as "Kramers's escape rate problem". We solve the generalized Langevin equation for the trajectories of the particles numerically and analytically for both limiting cases, Markovian and non-Markovian thermal noise. We compute the escape rate and work out the fundamental differences arising from finite correlation times of the thermal noise.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-Markovian (colored) thermal noise with finite correlation times produces escape rates that differ fundamentally from the classical Markovian Kramers result, with the magnitude and shape of the rate as a function of damping depending on the specific form of the noise correlation function.

## Method

The authors solve the generalized Langevin equation (GLE) numerically for an asymmetric double-well potential using a pulse-shape method to generate stationary Gaussian colored noise with three different correlation functions: exponential (C1), Gaussian (C2), and a quantum-field-theory-inspired kernel (C3) that becomes negative at past times. They compute the escape rate by averaging over ensembles of particle trajectories and compare the results to analytical Markovian predictions from Kramers (1940) for both the weak-friction and intermediate-to-strong-friction regimes.

## Key numbers

- Barrier height used in simulations: \(E_{\text{b}} = 2.5 \, \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Temperature: \(T = 1 \, \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Mass: \(m = 1.11 \, \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Barrier frequency: \(\omega_b = 5 \, \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Noise strength for C1 and C2: \(D = 20 \, \mathrm{GeV}^3\)
- Coupling constant for C3: \(g = 20\)
- Damping rates tested: \(\beta = 0.03375 \, \mathrm{GeV}\) (weak friction) and \(\beta = 9 \, \mathrm{GeV}\) (strong friction)
- Numerical correlations averaged over \(8 \times 10^5\) runs for validation of noise generation

## Context

This work extends Kramers's (1940) classical Markovian escape rate problem to non-Markovian noise, building on the colored-noise generation method developed by Xu (1999) and previously employed by Schmidt (2014). The results are motivated by applications in high-energy nuclear physics, including the dynamics of disoriented chiral condensates, heavy quarks, and the chiral phase transition in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables future studies of how finite correlation times from quantum field theoretical settings modify thermally activated escape processes.
