# Light Goldstone boson and domain walls in the $K^0$-condensed phase of high density quark matter

> It is pointed out that $K^0$ condensation in high density matter gives rise to an extremely light Goldstone boson whose mass comes entirely from weak interactions. This implies the existence of metastable non-topological domain walls with a long lifetime. We comment on the mass of the superfluid mode if baryon number is violated.

## Key claim

The K⁰-condensed phase of high-density quark matter contains an extremely light pseudo-Goldstone boson whose mass arises entirely from weak interactions, leading to metastable non-topological domain walls; the claim is conditional on the existence of K⁰ condensation in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase and on the neglect of instanton effects that could modify the mass formula.

## Method

The author uses symmetry arguments and effective field theory to analyze the K⁰-condensed CFL phase, identifying the spontaneously broken U(1) strangeness symmetry and its explicit violation by weak interactions. The mass of the resulting pseudo-Goldstone boson is computed by averaging the weak-interaction four-fermion Lagrangian over the kaon-condensed ground state, using the CFL order parameter Σ and the known decay constant f of pseudoscalar mesons in the CFL phase. The effective Lagrangian for the Goldstone phase variable φ is shown to be of sine-Gordon form, from which domain wall solutions and their properties (tension, decay rate) are derived.

## Key numbers

- Goldstone boson mass estimate: m ∼ 50 keV, using α_s = 0.3, m_u = 4 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, Δ = 100 MeV.
- Mass formula: m² = [162√2 π / (21 − 8 ln 2)] (G_F / α_s) cosθ_c sinθ_c m_u m_s Δ².
- Decay constant of the Goldstone boson: f² = [(21 − 8 ln 2) / 18] (μ² / 2π²).
- Domain wall tension: σ = 8 u f² m, where u = 1/√3.
- Domain wall decay rate exponent: Γ ∼ exp[ − (π⁴ u³ / 12) (f² / m²) ln³(m_K / m) ], with m_K the mass scale of other pseudoscalar mesons.
- For baryon number violation: superfluid mode mass m < 10⁻⁷ eV from experimental bound τ_{n↔n̄} > 10⁸ s.

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon condensation in the CFL phase, previously investigated by Schäfer (2000), Bedaque and Schäfer (2001), and Kaplan and Reddy (2001), and builds on the inverse meson mass ordering found by Son and Stephanov (2000). The predicted light boson and domain walls are analogous to axion domain walls (Vilenkin and Shellard, 1994), the U(1)_A domain walls of high-density QCD (Son, Stephanov, and Zhitnitsky, 2001), and domain walls in two-component Bose-Einstein condensates (Son and Stephanov, 2001). The results enable future exploration of observable consequences in neutron star cores, where such domain walls could form during cooling below the kaon condensation temperature.
