# Superfluid Pairing in Neutrons and Cold Atoms

> Ultracold atomic gases and low-density neutron matter are unique in that they exhibit pairing gaps comparable to the Fermi energy which in this sense are the largest in the laboratory and in nature, respectively. This strong pairing regime, or the crossover between BCS and BEC regimes, requires non-perturbative treatments. We describe Quantum Monte Carlo results useful to understand the properties of these systems, including infinite homogeneous matter and trapped inhomogeneous gases.

# Superfluid Pairing in Neutrons and Cold Atoms

## Key claim

Ultracold atomic gases and low-density neutron matter exhibit pairing gaps comparable to the Fermi energy, making them the strongest superfluids in the laboratory and in nature respectively, but this claim is conditional on the systems being in the strongly paired regime where the pairing gap is large enough that Quantum Monte Carlo methods become applicable, unlike in weakly paired systems where the energy scale separation prevents reliable gap calculations.

## Method

The authors employ Green's Function Monte Carlo and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo methods to compute ground-state energies and pairing gaps for both homogeneous infinite matter and trapped inhomogeneous systems. For cold atoms, the interaction is treated as a contact interaction with zero effective range, while for neutron matter the Argonne v18 two-body potential is used, with three-body forces included at higher densities. The pairing gap is extracted from odd-even staggering of ground-state energies across particle numbers N=66-68, using Jastrow-BCS trial wave functions with fixed particle number and periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy ratio at unitarity for cold atoms: ξ = 0.372(5) from auxiliary field QMC calculations and ξ = 0.375(5) from experiments
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a ≈ -18 fm; effective range: r_e = 2.7 fm
- Pairing gap at unitarity for cold atoms: Δ = 0.45(0.05) E_F from experiments, with ratio Δ/Δ_BCS ≈ 0.65
- Weak-coupling polarization correction factor: Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov reduction factor of 1/(4e)^(1/3) ≈ 0.45 relative to mean-field BCS
- Harmonic oscillator frequencies for neutron drops: ω = 5 MeV and ω = 10 MeV

## Context

This review extends the comparison between cold atomic gases and neutron matter pioneered by Giorgini (2008) and builds on Quantum Monte Carlo developments by Carlson (2003, 2005) and Gezerlis (2008). The results provide constraints for Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov treatments of neutron-rich nuclei as used by Chamel (2008), and inform neutron-star crust physics including superfluid phonon cooling mechanisms proposed by Aguilera (2009) and Cirigliano (2011). The work enables future studies of polarized neutron matter relevant to magnetars and optical lattice simulations of neutron star crusts.
