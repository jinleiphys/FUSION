# Opportunities and open questions in modern $β$ decay

> For well over half a century, precision studies of neutron and nuclear $β$ decays have been at the forefront of searches for exotic electroweak physics. Recent advances in nuclear ab initio theory and the widespread use of effective field theories means that its modern understanding is going through a transitional phase. This has been propelled by current tensions in the global data set leading to renewed scrutiny of its theoretical ingredients. In parallel, a host of novel techniques and methods are being investigated that are able to sidestep many traditional systematic uncertainties and require a diverse palette of skills and collaboration with material science and condensed matter physics. We highlight the current opportunities and open questions with the aim of facilitating the transition to a more modern understanding of $β$ decay.

## Key claim
Precision studies of neutron and nuclear β decay are in a transitional phase driven by tensions in the global data set (the "Cabibbo Angle Anomaly") and by recent advances in ab initio nuclear theory and effective field theories; the paper argues that resolving these tensions requires a modernized theoretical framework that consistently treats radiative corrections, isospin breaking, and final-state interactions to 0.01%-level precision.

## Method
The paper reviews the effective field theory (EFT) tower connecting quark-level, nucleon-level, and nuclear-level descriptions of β decay, emphasizing matching procedures and renormalization-group running of Wilson coefficients. It surveys experimental systems (neutron, superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ decays, mirror nuclei, T=1 Gamow-Teller decays) and observables (total decay rates, β spectrum shapes, angular correlations) that constrain exotic couplings, and discusses recent theoretical developments in lattice QCD, chiral EFT, and multipole decompositions.

## Key numbers
- The Standard Model vector coupling gives √2 C_V ≈ 1×10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², corresponding to a scale ~100 GeV (the W boson mass).
- Constraining exotic Wilson coefficients C_X at the 0.01% level probes new physics at Λ_BSM ≥ 10 TeV.
- The CKM unitarity test Δ_CKM = |V_ud|² + |V_us|² – 1 is sensitive to (M_W/Λ_BSM)²; |V_ub|² ~ 10⁻⁵ is neglected.
- The Fierz interference term b_F appears as a 1/E_e distortion to the β spectrum; its statistical sensitivity is optimal for endpoints around 2 MeV.
- The inner radiative correction Δ_R^V is given by Δ_R^V = [g_V(μ)]² (1 + 5α(μ)/(8π)) – 1, where μ is either m_N or m_e depending on the approach.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on precision β decay tests of the Standard Model, following the Lee-Yang Hamiltonian (Lee 1956) and the development of the Low-energy EFT (LEFT) by Bhattacharya (2012) and Gonzalez-Alonso (2018). It addresses current tensions in the global data set, notably the "Cabibbo Angle Anomaly" where superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ V_ud determinations disagree with unitarity and with neutron-based extractions. The paper enables future work by identifying open questions, such as consistent inclusion of final-state interactions, strong isospin breaking effects on g_V and g_A, and nuclear structure corrections to O(α) radiative corrections, and by highlighting novel experimental techniques (Cyclotron Radiation Emission Spectroscopy, Superconducting Tunnel Junctions) that can sidestep traditional systematic uncertainties.
