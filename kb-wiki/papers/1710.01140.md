# Consistent optical potential for incident and emitted low-energy $α$ particles. II. $α$-emission in fast-neutron induced reactions on Zr isotopes

> Background: Challenging questions of the $α$-particle optical-model potential (OMP) are still pointed out by recent high-precision measurements of $α$-induced reaction data below the Coulomb barrier. Moreover, the reliability of a previous OMP for $α$-particles on nuclei within the mass number range 45$\leq$$A$$\leq$209 has been recently proved for emitted $α$ particles as well, but only in the case of proton-induced reactions on Zn isotopes [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 91}, 064611 (2015), Paper I]. Purpose: Analysis of most recent $(α,γ)$ reaction data for Ge and Zr isotopes, which provides an additional validation of the above-mentioned potential, is related to a further account of $α$-particle emission in neutron-induced reactions on Zr isotopes, at the same time with a suitable description of all competitive processes. Methods: A consistent parameter set, established or validated by independent analysis of recent various data, particularly $γ$-ray strength functions, have been involved within model calculation of the $(α,γ)$ as well as $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections. The latter are part of the whole analysis of the neutron activation of Zr isotopes, in order to avoid any error compensation or latent ambiguity. Results: The aforesaid potential provides a consistent description of recent $α$-induced reaction data with no empirical rescaling factors of the $γ$ and/or nucleon widths. On the other hand, its use leads to underestimated predictions of the pre-equilibrium emission and statistical models for the $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections. Conclusions: An optical potential with a volume imaginary component seems to be needed to describe the low-energy $α$-particle evaporation, while only surface absorption occurs in $α$-induced reactions at similar energies.

# Consistent optical potential for incident and emitted low-energy α particles. II. α-emission in fast-neutron induced reactions on Zr isotopes

## Key claim

The previously established α-particle optical-model potential (OMP) for nuclei with mass number 45 ≤ A ≤ 209 provides a consistent description of recent (α,γ) reaction data on Ge and Zr isotopes without empirical rescaling factors, but its use leads to underestimated predictions for (n,α) reaction cross sections, suggesting that a volume imaginary component is needed for low-energy α-particle evaporation while only surface absorption occurs in α-induced reactions at similar energies.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical model and pre-equilibrium Geometry-Dependent Hybrid model calculations using the STAPRE-H95 code, combined with distorted-wave Born approximation calculations for collective inelastic scattering. A consistent parameter set was employed, including back-shifted Fermi gas nuclear level densities fitted to low-lying levels and resonance data, nucleon optical potentials (Koning and Delaroche 2003 for neutrons; Johnson et al. 1968 for low-energy protons), and γ-ray strength functions using the enhanced generalized Lorentzian model with a small resonance contribution, validated against independent (p,n), (p,γ), and radiative strength function measurements.

## Key numbers

- The pre-equilibrium emission fraction varies in the incident energy range 5–21 MeV from 2 to 30% for 90Zr, from 6 to 36% for 91Zr, from 4 to 31% for 92Zr, from 5 to 36% for 94Zr, and from 6 to 37% for 96Zr.
- The small resonance (SR) contribution to the s-wave average radiation width Γγ is approximately 30% for the odd nucleus 89Y but only approximately 10% for the even-even nucleus 90Zr, with similar odd-even dependence for Mo isotopes (approximately 32% for odd-mass 95Mo versus approximately 16% to approximately 8% for even-even 94,96Mo).
- The global proton optical potential of Koning and Delaroche (2003) overestimates measured (p,n) excitation functions by approximately 45%, and their local parameter set for protons on 90Zr overestimates by approximately 80%, in the first 2–3 MeV above threshold.
- The McFadden and Satchler (1966) α-particle OMP overpredicts the 70Ge(α,γ)74Se cross section by a factor of approximately 2 at the lowest incident energies below the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends the validation of the α-particle OMP developed by Avrigeanu and Avrigeanu (2014) for the mass range 45 ≤ A ≤ 209, previously demonstrated for α-emission in proton-induced reactions on Zn isotopes (Paper I, 2015), to (α,γ) reactions on Ge and Zr isotopes and to (n,α) reactions on Zr isotopes. The analysis addresses problems identified in earlier fast-neutron reaction studies on Zr isotopes by Simakov and van der Marck (2010), who found significant difficulties describing (n,p) and (n,α) reactions using TALYS-1.0 with global parameters. The consistent parameter approach avoids the error compensation and latent ambiguity that can arise from fitting single reaction channels, following the methodology of the modern nuclear data evaluation tradition. The results enable improved modeling of α-particle emission in neutron-induced reactions relevant to radiation damage calculations and nuclear astrophysics applications, while identifying the need for a volume imaginary component in the α-particle OMP for evaporation processes.
