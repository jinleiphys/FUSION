# New exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs

> We present new, exact, finite solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs for arbitrary constant value of the speed of sound. These new solutions generalize earlier, longitudinally finite, exact solutions, from an unrealistic to a reasonable equation of state, characterized by a temperature independent (average) value of the speed of sound. Observables like the rapidity density and the pseudorapidity density are evaluated analytically, resulting in simple and easy to fit formulae that can be matched to the high energy proton-proton and heavy ion collision data at RHIC and LHC. In the longitudinally boost-invariant limit, these new solutions approach the Hwa-Bjorken solution and the corresponding rapidity distributions approach a rapidity plateaux.

## Key claim

The paper presents new exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs that work for any constant value of the speed of sound, generalizing earlier solutions that were limited to an unrealistic equation of state where the speed of sound equals the speed of light. The solutions are finite in longitudinal extent and accelerating, with the hedge that they assume a temperature-independent (average) speed of sound rather than a fully realistic temperature-dependent one.

## Method

The authors solve the 1+1 dimensional relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics equations (energy-momentum conservation and entropy conservation) in Rindler coordinates (proper time τ and space-time rapidity η_x), using an equation of state ε = κ p where κ is a temperature-independent constant related to the speed of sound as c_s = 1/√κ. They search for self-similar solutions with a scaling variable s that has vanishing co-moving derivative, and derive analytic expressions for the temperature, entropy density, and fluid rapidity Ω as functions of τ and η_x. The observables (rapidity density dN/dy and pseudorapidity density dN/dη_p) are then evaluated analytically near midrapidity.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound is taken as c_s = 1/√κ, with the realistic range given as κ ≈ 6 to 11, corresponding to c_s = 0.35 ± 0.05 (citing Adare 2006).
- The paper reports that the CNC solution (Csörgő 2006, Nagy 2007) with the superhard equation of state (κ = 1, c_s = 1) gave corrections to Bjorken's initial energy density estimate of a factor of 10 at RHIC energies, and conjectured corrections of order 15.
- The new solutions are compared to proton-proton collision data at √s = 7 TeV and 8 TeV at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the CNC solution (Csörgő, Nagy, Csanád, 2006-2007) which had an unrealistic superhard equation of state (ε = p, speed of sound = speed of light) to a more realistic equation of state with arbitrary constant speed of sound. It also generalizes earlier longitudinally finite solutions (CGHK and CCHK solutions by Csörgő, Grassi, Hama, Kodama, 2002-2003) that had non-accelerating Hubble flow profiles. The new solutions approach the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) in the longitudinally boost-invariant limit, and enable exact calculation of the equation-of-state-dependent corrections to Bjorken's initial energy density estimate, which previously could only be conjectured.
