# Four-dimensional QCD equation of state with multiple chemical potentials

> We construct a four-dimensional version of the equation of state (EoS) model NEOS, NEOS-4D, as a function of the temperature and chemical potentials of baryon, electric charge, and strangeness for the hot and dense QCD matter created in relativistic nuclear collisions. This EoS enables multiple conserved charge current evolution in a relativistic fluid. Input from Lattice QCD simulations and a hadron resonance gas model is considered for constructing the equation of state. We investigate its applicability to the relativistic hydrodynamic description of nuclear collisions and present a method for efficient numerical implementation.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a four-dimensional QCD equation of state (NEOS-4D) as a function of temperature and three chemical potentials (baryon, electric charge, strangeness) without imposing the usual constraints on charge ratios, but the authors note that results involving regions with large chemical potential-to-temperature ratios (μ_B/T > 3, μ_S/T, or μ_Q/T) should be considered extrapolations because the Taylor expansion method is not well-defined there.

## Method

The authors extend the NEOS model by smoothly connecting the pressure from lattice QCD simulations (using Taylor expansion with susceptibilities up to fourth order, plus selected sixth-order susceptibilities) to the hadron resonance gas model pressure in the crossover region, using a hyperbolic tangent switching function with a μ_B-dependent connecting temperature. They then tabulate the equation of state using pseudo-temperature and pseudo-chemical potentials derived from a parton gas model to enable efficient numerical implementation in hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- Connecting temperature parameters: a = 0.16 GeV, b = 0.139 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.053 GeV^{-3}, d = 0.4, with connecting width ΔT_c = 0.1 × T_c(0)
- The squared sound velocity c_s^2 is computed at μ_B = 0.2 GeV, μ_Q = 0.2 GeV, and μ_S = 0.2 GeV, showing non-trivial corrections near the crossover
- Trajectories in the phase diagram are shown for s/n_B = 420, 144, 51, and 30, corresponding to collision energies √s_NN = 200, 62.4, 19.6, and 14.5 GeV
- At μ_B = 0.6 GeV (lowest considered collision energies), trajectory bands extend up to |μ_Q| ∼ 0.1 GeV and μ_S ∼ 0.2 GeV
- Pressure difference between NEOS-4D and hadron resonance gas is within 3% at switching energy density e = 0.36 GeV/fm^3 and within 1% at e = 0.16 and 0.26 GeV/fm^3
- Deviation of conserved charges from hadron resonance gas values at e = 0.36 GeV/fm^3: 3-5% for net baryon, 5-10% for net charge and strangeness; within 3% at e = 0.16 GeV/fm^3
- Ratio of UrQMD pressure to hadron resonance gas pressure shows 1-5% difference along switching energy densities e = 0.36, 0.26, and 0.16 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the NEOS equation of state model (Monnai 2019) from two dimensions (T, μ_B) to four dimensions (T, μ_B, μ_Q, μ_S), enabling hydrodynamic simulations without the traditional constraint n_Q/n_B ∼ 0.4 that holds for heavy nuclei like gold or lead. The approach uses lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2014, 2017) and Ding (2015) for the high-temperature phase, and enables studies of collisions involving light nuclei, local fluctuations, and diffusion of conserved charges. A similar model using different methods was presented by Plumberg (2024). The inclusion of a critical point is left for future work.
