# Boson-Fermion pairing in Bose-Fermi mixtures on 1D optical lattices

> Boson-fermion pairing is considered in a discrete environment of bosons and fully spin-polarized fermions, coupled via an attractive Bose-Fermi Hubbard Hamiltonian in one dimension. The results of the T-matrix approximation for particles of equal mass and at double half filling are compared with the results of exact diagonalization and with Quantum Monte Carlo results. Satisfactory agreement for most quantities is found. The appearance of a stable, weak-coupling pairing mode is also confirmed.

## Key claim

The T-matrix (RPA) approximation for boson-fermion pairing in a one-dimensional optical lattice reproduces most quantities from exact diagonalization and Quantum Monte Carlo with satisfactory qualitative and semi-quantitative agreement, even though one-dimensional systems are an unfavorable case for such a ladder approximation. The second stable boson-fermion branch found for arbitrarily weak attractive interaction is confirmed, but in one dimension this branch is likely an artefact of the approximation, whereas the authors argue it should be real in two or three dimensions.

## Method

The authors solve the Bose-Fermi Hubbard model on a one-dimensional lattice at zero temperature using a T-matrix (ladder) approximation, which reduces to a boson-fermion random phase approximation (RPA) in the particle-particle channel. They compare the RPA results with exact diagonalization for a 6-site system (3 bosons and 3 fermions) and with Quantum Monte Carlo simulations for larger systems up to 70 sites, all at double half-filling with equal boson and fermion masses and equal tunneling amplitudes. The boson-boson interaction is repulsive and the boson-fermion interaction attractive, with the ratio fixed to \(U_{bb} = \frac{3}{4}|U_{bf}|\) for most calculations.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy per site for a 70-site system at \(U_{bf} = -6\): RPA gives approximately \(-2.5\), QMC gives approximately \(-2.4\) (read from Fig. 2); agreement is reasonable up to \(|U_{bf}| \approx 8\).
* For a 30-site system with \(t_f = 4 t_b\) and \((U_{bf}, U_{bb}) = (-40, 6)\): RPA ground state energy per site = \(-24.30\), QMC = \(-20.78\), HF = \(-12.85\).
* For the same 30-site system with \((U_{bf}, U_{bb}) = (-50, 10)\): RPA = \(-29.71\), QMC = \(-25.54\), HF = \(-14.88\).
* For the same 30-site system with \((U_{bf}, U_{bb}) = (-60, 12)\): RPA = \(-35.69\), QMC = \(-30.33\), HF = \(-17.15\).
* The second stable boson-fermion branch appears for arbitrarily small \(|U_{bf}|\) and lies just below the continuum; for the example shown, \(U_{bf} = -10\) and \(k_F = \pi/4\).

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix approach for in-medium boson-fermion scattering developed by Storozhenko et al. (2005) from the continuum to a discrete one-dimensional optical lattice, providing a test of the method against exact results from Quantum Monte Carlo by Pollet et al. (2006) and exact diagonalization. The study confirms the existence of a second stable boson-fermion branch analogous to Cooper pairing in pure Fermi systems, but notes that in one dimension this branch is an artefact of the approximation, unlike in higher dimensions where it should be physical. The approach is intended for future application to two- and three-dimensional systems where mean-field and RPA theories perform better and where no Quantum Monte Carlo data are available.
