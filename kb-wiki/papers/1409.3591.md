# Neutrino-antineutrino correlations in dense anisotropic media

> We derive the most general evolution equations describing in-medium (anti)neutrino propagation in the mean-field approximation. In particular, we consider various types of neutrino-antineutrino mixing, for both Dirac and Majorana fields, resulting either from nontrivial pair correlations or from helicity coherence due to the nonvanishing neutrino masses. We show that, unless the medium is spatially homogeneous and isotropic, these correlations are sourced by the usual neutrino and antineutrino densities. This may be of importance in astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The paper shows that neutrino-antineutrino pair correlations, which are usually neglected because they average to zero on typical flavor-conversion timescales, are sourced by the usual neutrino and antineutrino densities whenever the medium is spatially inhomogeneous or anisotropic, meaning they cannot be dismissed in realistic astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae.

## Method

The authors derive the most general mean-field evolution equations for Dirac and Majorana neutrinos using a quantum field-theoretical approach based on the BBGKY hierarchy, including all possible two-point correlators (normal densities, helicity coherence, and pairing correlations). They then specialize to spatially homogeneous systems in the ultrarelativistic limit to isolate the role of pairing correlations, and separately treat helicity coherence terms arising from nonvanishing neutrino masses.

## Key numbers

- The rapid oscillation timescale for free-theory pair correlations is approximately \(1/[\epsilon_{i,q}+\epsilon_{j,q}]\), where \(\epsilon_{i,q}=(q^2+m_i^2)^{1/2}\) is the (anti)particle energy.
- The evolution equations are expressed in terms of \(2N_f \times 2N_f\) matrices for the extended Hamiltonian and density matrix, where \(N_f\) is the number of neutrino flavors.
- For spatially homogeneous systems, the momentum-space delta functions take the form \((2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}(\vec q - \vec q')\) for normal densities and \((2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}(\vec q + \vec q')\) for pairing correlations.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field equations of Volpe (2013) and Vlasenko (2013) by systematically including all possible neutrino-antineutrino two-point correlations, both from pairing and from helicity coherence due to nonzero masses. It establishes a formal connection to the BCS theory of superconductivity, as previously noted by Pehlivan (2011), and provides the foundation for studying collective flavor instabilities in anisotropic supernova environments, where the standard assumption of isotropy fails.
