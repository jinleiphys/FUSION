# Quenching effects in the cumulative jet spectrum

> The steeply falling jet spectrum induces bias on the medium modifications of jet observables in heavy-ion collisions. To explore this bias, we develop a novel analytic framework to study the quenched jet spectrum, and its cumulative. We include many energy-loss-related effects, such as soft and hard medium induced emissions, broadening, elastic scattering, jet fragmentation, cone size, coherence effects, etc. We show that, different jet spectrum-based observables are connected, e.g., the nuclear modification, spectrum shift, and the quantile procedure. We present the first predictions for the nuclear modification factor and the quantile procedure with cone size dependence. As an example, we compare dijet and boson+jet events to unfold the spectrum bias effects. We improve quark-, and gluon-jet classification using arguments based on the cumulative. Besides pointing out its flexibility, we apply our framework with other energy loss models such as the hybrid weak-, strong-coupling.

## Key claim

The steeply falling jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions biases medium-modification observables because equal-\(p_T\) selection compares jet populations that originated at different initial transverse momenta. The paper develops an analytic framework to study this bias and shows that different spectrum-based observables, such as the nuclear modification factor, the spectrum shift, and the quantile procedure, are connected, with the quantile procedure reducing the bias by using the tail-cumulative of the jet spectrum.

## Method

The authors construct a quenching factor \(\mathcal{Q}_>(\nu)\) (the Laplace transform of the energy-loss distribution \(\mathcal{P}_>(\varepsilon)\)) for a jet by first deriving the out-of-cone medium-induced gluon emission spectrum, matching the harmonic-oscillator (multiple-soft-scattering) regime at low gluon energies with the \(N=1\) (single-hard-scattering) regime at high energies using an improved opacity expansion (IOE) at NNLO. They then account for multiple independent emissions via a Poisson process to obtain the single-parton quenching factor, and incorporate jet fragmentation and multiple partons via a collimator function to obtain the full jet quenching factor, including elastic energy loss and coherence effects.

## Key numbers

* Medium parameters for 0–10% central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV: \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(T_0 = 0.45\) GeV, \(\hat q_0 = 0.095\) GeV\(^3\), \(L = 4\) fm.
* The matching scale for the medium-induced spectrum is \(\omega_\star = \min(\omega_c, \omega_R)\), where \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q L^2/2\) and \(\omega_R \equiv \sqrt{\hat q L}/R\).
* The thermalization scale for soft gluons is \(\omega_s \approx 8.5\) GeV for the chosen parameters, given by \(\omega_s \equiv \pi (N_c \alpha_s/\pi)^2 \hat q_0 L^2\).
* The Bethe–Heitler cutoff is set to \(\omega_{\rm BH} = T_0 = 0.45\) GeV; emissions below this are neglected.
* The approximation \(R_{\rm med} \approx \mathcal{Q}_>(\nu)\) with \(\nu = n/p_T\) is precise within at most a few percent for \(10 < p_T < 1000\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the quenching-weight formalism of Baier (2001) and Salgado (2003) from single-hadron spectra to fully reconstructed jets, incorporating cone-size dependence and a matched treatment of soft and hard medium-induced emissions. It connects to recent efforts on bias mitigation, such as the quantile procedure of Brewer (2018) and quark-gluon discrimination via cumulative arguments of Brewer (2020), and provides a flexible framework that can accommodate other energy-loss models (e.g., hybrid weak/strong coupling). The results enable first analytic predictions for the cone-size dependence of the nuclear modification factor and the quantile procedure, and can be used to unfold spectrum-bias effects in dijet versus boson+jet events.
