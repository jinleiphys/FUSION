# Supercritical instability in graphene with two charged impurities

> We study the supercritical instability in gapped graphene with two charged impurities separated by distance R using the two-dimensional Dirac equation for electron quasiparticles. Attention is paid to a situation when charges of impurities are subcritical, whereas their total charge exceeds a critical one. The critical distance R_{cr} in the system of two charged centers is defined as that at which the electron bound state with the lowest energy reaches the boundary of the lower continuum. A variational calculation of the critical distance R_{cr} separating the supercritical (R<R_{cr}) and subcritical (R>R_{cr}) regimes is carried out. It is shown that the critical distance R_{cr} increases as the quasiparticle gap decreases. The energy and width of a quasistationary state as functions of the distance between two impurities are derived in the quasiclassical approximation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two subcritical charged impurities in gapped graphene can collectively become supercritical when placed closer than a critical distance \(R_{\rm cr}\), even though each impurity individually is below the critical charge threshold. The critical distance increases as the quasiparticle gap decreases, and in the gapless limit the two impurities are always supercritical if their total charge exceeds the critical value.

## Method

The authors model electron quasiparticles using the two-dimensional Dirac equation in the field of two identical Coulomb impurities separated by distance \(R\), including a quasiparticle gap \(\Delta\). They derive the asymptotic behavior of the bound state wave function at the boundary of the lower continuum (\(E=-\Delta\)) using a monopole approximation and matching of solutions near the impurities and at infinity. A variational calculation employing the Kantorovich method with a trial function \(\psi = \sum_k \psi_k(\mu) \nu^{k-1}\) in elliptic coordinates is performed to determine the critical distance \(R_{\rm cr}\) numerically. For the supercritical regime (\(R < R_{\rm cr}\)), the energy and width of the quasistationary state are obtained using the Wentzel–Kramers–Brillouin (WKB) approximation in the monopole approximation.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling constant for a single impurity is \(\beta_c = 1/2 + \pi^2 / \log^2(c \Delta R_0 / \hbar v_F)\) with \(c \approx 0.21\), where \(\Delta\) is the quasiparticle gap and \(R_0\) is the regularization cutoff of order the graphene lattice spacing.
* The dimensionless charge parameter is \(\zeta = 2Z\alpha/\kappa\), where \(\alpha = e^2/\hbar v_F \simeq 2.19\) is the graphene fine-structure constant and \(v_F \approx 10^6\) m/s is the Fermi velocity.
* The critical distance for the onset of supercriticality in the monopole approximation is given by \(m R_{\rm cr} = (1/\zeta) \exp(-2\pi / \sqrt{4\zeta^2 - 1})\) for \(\sqrt{4\zeta^2 - 1} \ll 1\), where \(m = \Delta / v_F^2\).
* For the lowest-energy state with total angular momentum \(j = 1/2\), the critical distance from the WKB approximation is \(R_{\rm cr} \Delta / \hbar v_F = [4(\zeta^2 - j^2) / (\zeta \varkappa)] \exp(-\pi / \sqrt{\zeta^2 - j^2} - 2)\), with \(\varkappa \sim 1\) a numerical factor.
* The width of the quasistationary state near the critical point scales as \(\Gamma \propto \exp[-2\pi(\tilde\beta \sqrt{R/(R_{\rm cr} - R)} - \sqrt{\zeta^2 - j^2})]\), with \(\tilde\beta = \sqrt{(8\zeta^2 + 4j^2 + 6j + 3)/24}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of supercritical atomic collapse in graphene from single multivalent impurities (Shytov et al., Pereira et al.) to the two-center problem, motivated by the experimental observation of collapse in clusters of charged Ca dimers on graphene (Wang et al., 2013). The two-impurity problem is the condensed-matter analog of heavy-ion collisions in QED, where supercritical fields are created temporarily by bringing together subcritical nuclei (Gershtein, Rafelski, Zeldovich, 1970s). The results enable quantitative predictions for future experiments with controlled impurity clusters in suspended graphene, where substrate screening is absent and the gapless limit can be probed.
