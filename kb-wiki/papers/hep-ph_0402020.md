# Hadron Liquid with a Small Baryon Chemical Potential at Finite Temperature

> First, within one diagram of $Φ$ we discuss general properties of a system of heavy fermions of one kind (including antiparticles) interacting with rather light bosons of one kind. Fermion chemical potential is assumed to be small, $μ_f \lsim T$. Already for the low temperature, $T\ll {min} (T_{\rm bl.f}, m_{b})$, the fermion mass shell proves to be partially blurred due to multiple fermion rescatterings on virtual bosons, $m_{b}$ is the boson mass, $T_{\rm bl.f}$ $(\ll m_f)$ is the typical temperature corresponding to a complete blurring of the gap between fermion-antifermion continua, $m_f$ is the fermion mass. As the result, the ratio of the number of fermion-antifermion pairs to the number provided by the ordinary Boltzmann distribution becomes larger than unit ($R_N >1$). For $T\gsim m_{b}^* (T)$ (hot hadron liquid, blurred boson continuum), $m_{b}^* (T)$ is the effective boson mass, the abundance of all particles dramatically increases. The effective fermion mass $m_f^* (T)$ decreases with the temperature increase. For $T\gsim T_{\rm bl.f}$ fermions are essentially relativistic particles. Due to the interaction of the boson with fermion-antifermion pairs, $m_{b}^* (T)$ decreases leading to the possibility of the ``hot Bose condensation'' for $T>T_{cb}$. The phase transition might be of the second order or of the first order depending on the species under consideration. We estimate $R_N \sim 1.5$ for $T\sim m_π/2$; $T_{\rm bl.f}$ proves to be near $T_{cb}$; both values are in the vicinity of the pion mass $m_π$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a hot hadron liquid with small baryon chemical potential (μ_f ≲ T), the fermion mass shell becomes partially blurred due to multiple rescatterings on virtual bosons even at low temperatures, leading to an enhancement of fermion-antifermion pair production beyond the ordinary Boltzmann distribution; this effect is conditional on the fermion-boson coupling being sufficiently strong and the temperature approaching characteristic scales near the pion mass.

## Method

The author employs a Φ-derivable self-consistent approach (Baym 1962) within the simplest one-loop diagram of the Φ functional, solving coupled Dyson equations for fermion and boson Green functions. The analysis uses the soft thermal loop approximation, treating heavy fermions as hard particles and lighter bosons as soft quasi-static impurities, and separates the problem into low-temperature (T ≪ min(m_b, T_bl.f)) and high-temperature (T ≳ m_b*(T)) regimes for a system of one fermion species interacting with one boson species via Yukawa, vector, or pseudo-vector couplings.

## Key numbers

- R_N ∼ 1.5 for T ∼ m_π/2, where R_N is the ratio of fermion-antifermion pairs to the Boltzmann distribution prediction
- T_bl.f (temperature for complete blurring of the fermion-antifermion continuum gap) is near T_cb (critical temperature for hot Bose condensation); both values are in the vicinity of the pion mass m_π = 140 MeV
- For the Nσ system, the fermion-boson coupling constant g ∼ 10; for pions, g ∼ 1
- The resulting density of fermion-antifermion pairs at T ∼ T_bl.f is estimated as ≲ ρ_0, where ρ_0 ≃ 0.5 m_π^3 is the nuclear saturation density
- Typical baryon chemical potential values used to fit RHIC data vary in the range μ_bar ≃ 20–40 MeV, with break-up temperature T ≃ 140–170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the intuitive picture of Dyugaev (1993), who considered nucleons and antinucleons as non-relativistic particles interacting with pions via non-relativistic pion-nucleon coupling and conjectured an ending temperature for the hadron world. The paper develops a general relativistic approach to describe the heated quasi-equilibrium hadron liquid with small or zero total baryon charge, matching it to the non-relativistic treatment. It enables future study of the hadron-quark-gluon continuity region, where strong thermal fluctuations blur the distinction between hadron and quark-gluon phases, and suggests that enhanced dilepton production from softened vector meson spectra could be measured in heavy ion collisions.
