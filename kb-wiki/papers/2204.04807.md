# Spurious poles in a finite volume

> Using effective-range expansion for the two-body amplitudes may generate spurious sub-threshold poles outside of the convergence range of the expansion. In the infinite volume, the emergence of such poles leads to the inconsistencies in the three-body equations, e.g., to the breakdown of unitarity. We investigate the effect of the spurious poles on the three-body quantization condition in a finite volume and show that it leads to a peculiar dependence of the energy levels on the box size $L$. Furthermore, within a simple model, it is demonstrated that the procedure for the removal of these poles, which was recently proposed in Ref.[1] in the infinite volume, can be adapted to the finite-volume calculations. The structure of the exact energy levels is reproduced with an accuracy that systematically improves order by order in the EFT expansion.

## Key claim
Using effective-range expansion for two-body amplitudes in three-body finite-volume calculations generates spurious sub-threshold poles that cause unphysical merging and disappearance of energy levels as a function of box size L, but the subtraction method of Ebert (2021) can be adapted to finite volume to systematically remove these poles order by order in the EFT expansion.

## Method
The authors study a model of identical spinless bosons with mass m = 939.565 MeV interacting via a separable Yamaguchi potential, whose two-body scattering length a = 5.4194 fm and effective range r = 1.7536 fm match the triplet np scattering channel. They solve the three-body quantization condition in a finite volume using the particle-dimer formalism, replacing integrals over three-momenta by sums over discrete momenta p = 2πn/L, and compute the finite-volume energy spectrum by finding poles of the Green functions. The spurious pole removal follows the E-scheme of Ebert (2021), which expands only the spurious pole contribution in the two-body energy variable rather than expanding the full amplitude perturbatively.

## Key numbers
- Two-body scattering length a = 5.4194 fm and effective range r = 1.7536 fm, chosen to match triplet np scattering
- Two-body bound state (dimer) at E_2 = -2.209 MeV
- Three-body bound states in the Yamaguchi model: shallow at E_s = -2.356 MeV and deep at E_d = -24.797 MeV
- Ultraviolet cutoff on spectator momentum set to Λ = 1.5 GeV
- Exponential decay constants for finite-volume corrections: κ_s ≈ 26 MeV for shallow state, κ_d ≈ 189 MeV for deep state
- Matching points for particle-dimer couplings at relative momenta p = 10^{-3} MeV and p = 10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the spurious pole removal method of Ebert (2021) from infinite volume to finite-volume three-body calculations, addressing a problem that arises in the RFT, NREFT, and FVU approaches to three-body quantization conditions (Hansen 2014, Hammer 2017, Mai 2017). The spurious poles appear because effective-range expansion parameterizations of two-body amplitudes become inconsistent at large negative energies, violating unitarity even at low energies. The method enables reliable analysis of lattice QCD data in the three-particle sector by providing a systematic EFT expansion that converges to the exact finite-volume spectrum, with natural next steps being application to actual lattice data and extension beyond N2LO.
