# Charge and Magnetic Properties of Three-Nucleon Systems in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> A method to calculate the form factor for an external current with non-derivative coupling for the three-body system in an effective field theory (EFT) of short-range interactions is shown. Using this method the point charge radius of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ is calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pionless EFT ($\mathrm{EFT}(\not{\!π})$), and the magnetic moment and magnetic radius of ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ and ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ are calculated to next-to-leading order (NLO). For the ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ charge and magnetic form factors Coulomb interactions are ignored. The ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ point charge radius is given by 1.74(4) fm at NNLO. This agrees well with the experimental ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ point charge radius of 1.7753(54) fm [Angeli and Marinova, At. Data Nucl. Data Tables 99, 69 (2013)]. The ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ (${}^3\mathrm{He}$) magnetic moment in units of nuclear magnetons is found to be 2.92(35) (-2.08(25)) at NLO in agreement with the experimental value of 2.979 (-2.127). For ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ (${}^3\mathrm{He}$) the NLO magnetic radius is 1.78(11) fm (1.85(11) fm) which agrees with the experimental value of 1.840(182) fm (1.965(154) fm) [I. Sick, Prog. Part. Nucl. Phys. 47, 245 (2001)]. The fitting of the low-energy constant $L_{1}$ of the isovector two-body magnetic current and the consequences of Wigner-SU(4) symmetry for the three-nucleon magnetic moments are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the charge and magnetic form factors of three-nucleon systems can be calculated in pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) using a unified method for any external current with non-derivative coupling, with the caveat that Coulomb interactions are ignored for ³He and the theory is only valid for momentum transfers Q ≲ 0.7 fm⁻¹.

## Method

The author calculates the three-nucleon form factors by solving coupled-channel integral equations for the three-nucleon vertex function in cluster-configuration space, using dibaryon fields to represent the spin-triplet and spin-singlet nucleon-nucleon channels. The charge form factor of ³He is computed to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), while the magnetic form factors and magnetic radii of ³H and ³He are computed to next-to-leading order (NLO), using the Z-parametrization to fix two-body low-energy constants from the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ poles and fitting three-body forces to the triton binding energy and doublet S-wave nd scattering length.

## Key numbers

- ³He point charge radius at NNLO: 1.74(4) fm, compared to experimental value 1.7753(54) fm from Angeli and Marinova (2013)
- ³H magnetic moment at NLO: 2.92(35) nuclear magnetons, compared to experimental value 2.979
- ³He magnetic moment at NLO: -2.08(25) nuclear magnetons, compared to experimental value -2.127
- ³H magnetic radius at NLO: 1.78(11) fm, compared to experimental value 1.840(182) fm from Sick (2001)
- ³He magnetic radius at NLO: 1.85(11) fm, compared to experimental value 1.965(154) fm from Sick (2001)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative methods for three-body bound states developed by Vanasse (2015) from the triton charge radius to the full set of charge and magnetic properties of both ³H and ³He. The results agree with experimental data within uncertainties and complement potential model calculations by Schiavilla (1990) and Marcucci (1998) and chiral EFT calculations by Piarulli (2012), while providing a systematically improvable EFT framework that can isolate the role of two- and three-body currents. The paper also explores the Wigner-SU(4) symmetric limit, showing that at leading order it reproduces the Schmidt limit for magnetic moments, and enables future calculations of weak properties and capture reactions in three-nucleon systems.
