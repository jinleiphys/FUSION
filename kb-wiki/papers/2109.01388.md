# Bayesian evaluation of residual production cross sections in proton induced spallation reactions

> The Bayesian neural network (BNN) method is used to construct a predictive model for fragment prediction of proton induced spallation reactions with the guidance of a simplified EPAX formula. Compared to the experimental data, it is found that the BNN + sEPAX model can reasonably extrapolate with less information compared with BNN method. The BNN + sEPAX method provides a new approach to predict the energy-dependent residual cross sections produced in proton-induced spallation reactions from tens of MeV/u up to several GeV/u.

## Key claim

The Bayesian neural network (BNN) method, when guided by a simplified EPAX (sEPAX) empirical formula, can reasonably extrapolate residual production cross sections in proton-induced spallation reactions with less training information compared to the BNN method alone, but the model's reliable predictions are limited to systems smaller than 136Xe and incident energies from tens of MeV/u to a few GeV/u.

## Method

The authors constructed a 5-32-1 BNN architecture (5 input nodes, 32 hidden units, 1 output) with inputs being projectile mass and charge, incident energy, and fragment mass and charge. They introduced a simplified EPAX formula (sEPAX) that omits the incident energy dependence and uses fixed exponent U=2.0 for both neutron-deficient and neutron-rich sides. Two datasets were used: D1 (about 10,000 isotopic cross sections from GSI, LBL, and RIKEN for reactions from 105 MeV/u to 1.5 GeV/u) and D2 (about 3,000 excitation function data from Michel (2014) for reactions from 30 MeV/u to 2.6 GeV/u). The BNN model predicted log(sigma_exp) directly, while the BNN + sEPAX model predicted the residual log(sigma_exp) - log(sigma_sEPAX).

## Key numbers

- The learning set D1 + D2 contained 13,786 total data points after merging, with D1 contributing about 10,000 data and D2 contributing about 3,000 data.
- Only reactions with incident energy higher than 30 MeV/u and measuring uncertainty less than 30 percent were adopted in the learning set.
- The sEPAX formula uses a fixed exponent U = 2.0 for both neutron-deficient and neutron-rich sides, with the width parameter R approximated as R = exp(-0.015*A + 3.2e-5*A^2).
- The model validation used the empirical relation sigma = C*exp[(B' - 8)/tau] with pairing energy parameter epsilon_0 = 30 MeV taken from Tsang (2007).
- The BNN + sEPAX model with N_l = 13,786 can provide predictions for systems smaller than 136Xe within incident energy from tens of MeV/u to a few GeV/u.

## Context

This work extends prior BNN and BNN + SPACS approaches (Dan et al., previous works) that used about 4,000 data points from GSI and LBL but showed poor extrapolation for light fragments and energies below 300 MeV/u. The authors incorporate additional data from RIKEN and excitation functions from Michel (2014) to improve model accuracy and generalization. The BNN + sEPAX method enables prediction of energy-dependent residual cross sections for applications in proton therapy (tens of MeV/u to 1 GeV/u), accelerator-driven systems (below 1 GeV/u), and cosmic ray physics (sub-GeV region and higher), and suggests similar Bayesian approaches could be applied to projectile fragmentation reactions for rare isotope production.
