# Baryon form factors: Model-independent results

> Baryon form factors can be analyzed in a largely model-independent fashion in terms of two complementary approaches. These are chiral perturbation theory and dispersion relations. I review the status of dispersive calculations of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the light of new data. Then, I present the leading one-loop chiral perturbation theory analysis of the hyperon and the strange nucleon form factors. Open problems and challenges are also discussed.

# Baryon form factors: Model-independent results

## Key claim
Dispersion relations and chiral perturbation theory provide largely model-independent frameworks for analyzing baryon form factors, but the dispersive analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors fails to reproduce new Jefferson Lab data on the ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p, indicating that an essential physics ingredient is missing from the spectral function ansatz.

## Method
The author reviews dispersive calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors that reconstruct spectral functions from unitarity constraints on the isovector two-pion continuum up to about 1 GeV^2, combined with vector meson poles and perturbative QCD constraints. For hyperon and strange nucleon form factors, a leading one-loop (third order) chiral perturbation theory analysis in SU(3) is performed using dimensional regularization with a scale of 1 GeV, with low-energy constants fixed from proton and neutron properties to make parameter-free predictions for hyperons.

## Key numbers
- Σ^- charge radius from WA89: 0.92 ± 0.32 ± 0.40 fm^2; from SELEX: 0.60 ± 0.08 ± 0.08 fm^2
- Predicted Σ^- charge radius from chiral perturbation theory: 0.77 to 0.80 fm^2
- Predicted Σ^+ charge radius: 0.64 to 0.66 fm^2; predicted Ξ^- charge radius: 0.61 to 0.65 fm^2
- Strange magnetic form factor at Q_S^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 from SAMPLE: G_M^(s) = +0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19 (nuclear magnetons)
- HAPPEX measurement at Q_H^2 = 0.48 GeV^2: G_E^(s) + 0.39 G_M^(s) = 0.023 ± 0.034 ± 0.022 ± 0.026
- Strange magnetic moment from chiral perturbation theory: μ_s = 0.18 ± 0.44 nuclear magnetons
- Strange electric radius: ⟨r^2_{E,s}⟩^{1/2} = (0.05 ± 0.09) fm^2; strange magnetic radius: ⟨r^2_{M,s}⟩^{1/2} = -0.14 fm^2

## Context
This plenary talk from the "Nucleon '99" conference reviews the status of two complementary model-independent approaches to baryon form factors. The dispersive calculations extend earlier work by Meißner (1996) and others (Mergell et al., Hohler et al.) and are confronted with new polarization transfer data from Jefferson Lab. The chiral perturbation theory analysis of hyperon form factors was triggered by recent Σ^- radius measurements from the WA89 and SELEX collaborations, and extends previous SU(2) calculations by Gasser et al., Bernard et al., and others to the three-flavor case. The strange form factor analysis uses data from SAMPLE and HAPPEX to determine low-energy constants, building on earlier work by Hemmert et al. and Musolf et al. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming MAMI A4 measurements and improved Jefferson Lab data, while open problems include the need for fourth-order chiral calculations and resolution of discrepancies between chiral predictions and dispersive analyses of the strange magnetic radius.
