# Dilepton and Photon Emission Rates from a Hadronic Gas II

> We extend our recent analysis of the dilepton and photon emission rates to the case of finite temperature and baryon density, within the context of a density expansion. To leading order, the effects of the baryon density are assessed using data (photon emission) or constraints from broken chiral symmetry (dilepton emission). Next to leading order effects are worked out, and their contribution qualitatively assessed. The opening of the $πN$ cut causes the photon rate to saturate the empirical photon yield of WA80, but may not be enough to fully account for the excess low mass dileptons seen at CERES.

## Key claim

The inclusion of nucleons at finite baryon density enhances the dilepton and photon emission rates from a hadronic gas, but the enhancement is insufficient to fully account for the excess low-mass dileptons observed at CERES, even though the photon rate saturates the empirical upper bound from WA80.

## Method

The authors extend their previous density expansion for a baryon-free hadronic gas to finite temperature and baryon density, using on-shell chiral reduction formulas to organize the calculation. The forward Compton scattering amplitude on nucleons is evaluated using experimental data via the optical theorem for photons, and using one-loop chiral perturbation theory plus a Delta-resonance contribution for dileptons, with the Delta parameters constrained by chiral symmetry and experimental polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless expansion parameters are $\kappa_\pi = n_\pi/2m_\pi f_\pi^2 \lesssim 0.3$ and $\kappa_N = n_N g_A^2/2m_N f_\pi^2 \lesssim 0.3$ for $T \lesssim m_\pi$ and $n_N \lesssim 3\rho_0$, with $f_\pi = 93$ MeV and $g_A = 1.26$.
- The Delta decay width is $\Gamma_{\Delta\to N\gamma} \simeq 0.72$ MeV, fixing $S(0)=1.2/m_N^2$.
- The sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities is fixed to $\overline{\alpha}+\overline{\beta} = 10\times10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, giving $Q(0)=2.75/m_N$.
- At $M=400$ MeV and $T=150$ MeV with $n_N=\rho_0$, the inclusion of nucleons enhances the dielectron rate by a factor of three.
- For the space-time evolved photon rate with $n_N=2.5\rho_0$, the result saturates the WA80 upper bound.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Steele, Yamagishi, Zahed, 1996, referred to as "I") from a baryon-free hadronic gas to finite baryon density, using the same on-shell chiral reduction formalism developed in earlier work (Yamagishi and Zahed, "Master" paper). The results are compared with Rapp et al. (1996) and Brown et al. (1996), who found larger enhancements using effective models with in-medium effects; the present analysis finds smaller enhancements and no shift of the rho peak. The space-time evolution follows the transport results of Brown et al. (1996) and Sorge (1996). The findings imply that the CERES low-mass dilepton excess cannot be explained by nucleon contributions alone without overshooting the WA80 photon upper bound.
