# Analysis of quasielastic $(e,e')$ electromagnetic responses and scaling for nuclear matter in the relativistic mean field model

> A detailed study of the electromagnetic responses for quasielastic $(e,e')$ reactions from nuclear matter is presented within the framework of a relativistic model including momentum dependent scalar and vector mean field potentials in both the initial and final nucleon wave function states. The effects ascribed to the use of energy-dependent potentials are carefully analyzed by comparing their predictions with the responses obtained in the case of constant, i.e., energy-independent, potentials, as well as with the plane wave limit in the final state. The study is extended to the scaling phenomenon. Results are provided for the scaling functions corresponding to different nuclear systems evaluated at several kinematics ranging from intermediate to high values of the transfer momentum. Emphasis is placed on scaling of the first and second kinds and the role played by the relativistic scalar and vector mean field potentials.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the failure of the pure Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model to describe electromagnetic responses at high momentum transfer is linked to the use of energy-independent scalar and vector potentials in the final nucleon states, which produce Final State Interactions that do not diminish as momentum transfer increases; the authors propose that using energy-dependent potentials may remedy this shortfall.

## Method

The authors compute the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions for quasielastic (e,e') reactions in nuclear matter using a relativistic mean field model with momentum-dependent scalar and vector potentials in both initial and final nucleon states. They evaluate the polarization propagator via a local density approximation, comparing results for energy-dependent potentials, constant (energy-independent) potentials, and the plane wave limit in the final state. The study also examines scaling functions of the first and second kinds across different kinematics and nuclear systems.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Caballero et al. (2006, 2007) and Maieron et al. (2001) on scaling and superscaling in quasielastic electron scattering, and by Horowitz (1993) and Kim (1994) on relativistic mean field models for nuclear matter. The analysis aims to provide a unified framework that can describe both low-to-intermediate and high momentum transfer regimes, which is needed to improve the SuperScaling Approach (SuSAv2) model that currently blends RMF and Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation results using a q-dependent blending function.
