# Dynamical meson-baryon resonances with chiral Lagrangians

> The s-wave meson-baryon interaction is studied using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian in a unitary coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation. In the strangeness $S=-1$ sector the low-energy $K^- p$ dynamics leads to the dynamical generation of the $Λ(1405)$ as a ${\bar K}N$ state, along with a good description of the $K^- p$ scattering observables. At higher energies, the $Λ(1670)$ is also found to be generated dynamically as a $K Ξ$ quasibound state for the first time. For strangeness S=0, it is the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance that emerges from the coupled-channels equations, leading to a satisfactory description of meson-baryon scattering observables in the energy region around the $S_{11}(1535)$. We speculate on the possible dynamical generation of $Ξ$ resonances within the chiral $S=-2$ sector as ${\bar K} Λ$ or ${\bar K} Σ$ quasibound states.

## Key claim

The s-wave meson-baryon interaction, studied using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian in a unitary coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation, dynamically generates the Λ(1405) as a K̅N state and, for the first time, the Λ(1670) as a KΞ quasibound state; the S11(1535) resonance also emerges from the coupled-channels equations, but the description in the S=0 sector requires more parameters than the S=-1 sector and is only moderately successful around the resonance energy.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) using amplitudes from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for the octet of pseudoscalar mesons and the octet of 1/2+ baryons, including all ten possible meson-baryon states. The loop integral in the BSE was regularized either by a cut-off (qmax = 630 MeV for S=-1) or by dimensional regularization with subtraction constants a_l(μ) adjusted to reproduce the cut-off results at threshold. For the S=0 sector, a fixed physical cut-off of 1 GeV was used with separate decay constants for πN, K Y, and η N channels, and four subtraction constants were fitted to πN phase-shifts and inelastic cross sections around the N(1535) resonance.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off value qmax = 630 MeV used to reproduce K−p threshold branching ratios γ, Rc, and Rn in the S=-1 sector
- Decay constant f = 1.15 fπ (with fπ = 93 MeV) chosen for the S=-1 calculation
- Subtraction constants for S=-1: a_K̅N = -1.84, a_πΣ = -2.00, a_πΛ = -1.83, a_ηΛ = -2.25, a_ηΣ = -2.38, a_KΞ = -2.52 (with μ = 630 MeV)
- Two poles found in the I=0 amplitude: (1426 + i 16) MeV corresponding to Λ(1405) and (1708 + i 21) MeV corresponding to Λ(1670)
- One pole found in the I=1 amplitude: (1579 + i 296) MeV, corresponding to Σ(1620)
- For the S=0 sector, the N(1535) resonance has total decay width Γ ≃ 110 MeV, divided into Γπ ≃ 43 MeV and Γη ≃ 67 MeV
- Decay constants for S=0: fπ = 93 MeV, fK = 1.22 fπ, fη = 1.3 fπ

## Context

This work extends the S=-1 coupled-channels approach of Oset and Ramos (1998) to higher energies (up to 2 GeV), revealing the dynamical generation of the Λ(1670) and Σ(1620) as members of the 1/2− octet. It contrasts with the earlier approach of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995), which used next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangians and five parameters, while the present work uses only the lowest-order Lagrangian with one parameter (the cut-off) for S=-1. The S=0 description is less complete than the S=-1 one, but a recent work by another group (2001) achieved a satisfactory description from πN threshold to 2 GeV by fitting 12 parameters, generating both the N(1535) and N(1650) S11 resonances. The results enable predictions for the S=-2 sector, suggesting that a Ξ resonance in the 1600-1700 MeV region (possibly the Ξ(1690)) should be dynamically generated as the missing octet member.
