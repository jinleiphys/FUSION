# Quark-meson coupling model and heavy-ion collision

> We implement the quark-meson coupling model in Daejeon Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (DJBUU) transport model and perform Au+Au collision simulations at intermediate energies. Results are compared with simulations using a conventional quantum hadrodynamics model. Differences in the maximum density reached during the collisions are interpreted in terms of nuclear matter properties predicted by each model.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, when implemented in the Daejeon Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (DJBUU) transport model, produces a slightly larger maximum central density in Au+Au collisions at 400 A MeV than the conventional quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model with the Liuρ parameter set, despite QMC having a larger incompressibility and symmetry energy that would normally stiffen the equation of state and reduce density.

## Method

The authors implemented the QMC model, a relativistic mean-field approach where nucleons are treated as bags containing three quarks that interact directly with meson mean fields, into the DJBUU transport model. They performed simulations of 197Au+197Au collisions at a beam energy of 400 A MeV with impact parameter b = 4.7 fm, comparing results from the QMC parameter set against two QHD parameter sets (Liuρ and NL3). The time evolution of central baryon density was extracted from the simulations and interpreted in terms of nuclear matter properties including incompressibility, symmetry energy, and effective nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ0 = 0.15 fm−3 for QMC model, compared to 0.16 fm−3 for Liuρ and 0.15 fm−3 for NL3
- Effective mass ratio m*/m = 0.8 for QMC, compared to 0.75 for Liuρ and 0.6 for NL3
- Incompressibility K0 = 280 MeV for QMC, compared to 240 MeV for Liuρ and 272 MeV for NL3
- Symmetry energy S = 35 MeV for QMC, compared to 30.5 MeV for Liuρ and 37.3 MeV for NL3
- Binding energy per nucleon E0 = −15.7 MeV for QMC, compared to −16 MeV for both Liuρ and NL3
- QMC parameter aN = 0.181 fm, obtained from quark-level calculation with quark mass mq = 5 MeV and bag constant B = (170 MeV)4

## Context

This work extends the application of the QMC model, previously used for nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron stars (Guichon 1988, Saito 1996, Guichon 2018), into heavy-ion collision simulations via the DJBUU transport model developed for the RAON facility in Korea (Kim 2020). The results are compared with the conventional QHD approach using the Liuρ parameter set (Liu 2001) and the NL3 set (Lalazissis 1996), which is known to predict a stiff equation of state (Danielewicz 2002). This enables future studies of dense nuclear matter properties using rare-isotope beams at facilities such as RAON, RIBF, and FRIB.
