# Spin relaxation rate for baryons in thermal pion gas

> We study the relaxation dynamics of the spin polarization of baryons (nucleon and $Λ$-baryon), in a thermal pion gas as a simple model of the hadronic phase of the QCD plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. For this purpose, we formulate the quantum kinetic theory for the spin density matrix of baryons in the leading order of the gradient expansion. Considering the baryon-pion elastic scattering processes as the dominant interaction between baryons and thermal pions, we compute the spin relaxation rate of nucleons and $Λ$-baryons in a pion gas up to temperature 200 MeV. In the case of nucleons, we evaluate the spin relaxation rate in the $s$-channel resonance approximation, based on the known experimental data on $Δ$-resonances. We also estimate the spin relaxation rate for $Λ$-baryons, based on experimental inputs and theoretical models for the low-energy $Λ$$π$ scattering, including the chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The spin relaxation rate for baryons in a thermal pion gas is computed up to temperature 200 MeV, but the authors note that their approach works well only for low temperatures and eventually breaks down as the temperature approaches the crossover temperature \(T_c\sim 200\) MeV from the hadronic to quark-gluon plasma phases.

## Method

The authors formulate a quantum kinetic theory for the spin density matrix of baryons in the leading order of the gradient expansion, treating baryon-pion elastic scattering as the dominant interaction. For nucleons, they evaluate the spin relaxation rate using the \(s\)-channel resonance approximation based on experimental data on \(\Delta\)-resonances, while for \(\Lambda\)-baryons they use experimental inputs and theoretical models for low-energy \(\Lambda\pi\) scattering, including chiral perturbation theory and known hadron spectrum data for \(\Sigma\)-baryon (average mass 1190 MeV) and \(\Sigma^*(1385)\) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The spin relaxation rate for nucleons and \(\Lambda\)-baryons is computed up to temperature \(T=200\) MeV.
- The width of \(\Sigma^*(1385)\) used for \(\Lambda\)-baryon spin relaxation evaluation is \(\Gamma_{\Sigma^*}=36\) MeV from its dominant decay to \(\Lambda+\pi\).
- The crossover temperature from hadronic to quark-gluon plasma phases is \(T_c\sim 200\) MeV.
- The chiral perturbation theory description of nucleon-pion scattering becomes inadequate above \(T=100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin relaxation in QCD plasma from the high-temperature weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma regime, where the spin relaxation rate for heavy quarks was computed by Hongo (2022), to the low-temperature hadronic phase relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results are directly motivated by experimental observations of spin-polarized \(\Lambda\)-baryons at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2019, 2020) and ALICE (2019). The computed spin relaxation rates enable the development of spin hydrodynamics, where spin polarization can be treated as an additional quasi-hydrodynamic variable characterized by the rotational viscosity transport coefficient.
