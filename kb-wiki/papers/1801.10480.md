# N* Experiments and their Impact on Strong QCD Physics

> I give a brief overview of experimental studies of the spectrum and the structure of the excited states of the nucleon and what we learn about their internal structure. The focus is on the effort to obtain a more complete picture of the light-quark baryon excitation spectrum employing electromagnetic beams, and on the study of the transition form factors and helicity amplitudes and their dependence on the magnitude of the photon virtuality $Q^2$, especially for some of the most prominent resonances. The results were obtained in pion and eta electroproduction experiments off proton targets. They strengthen the connection of experiment and new results from modeling sQCD in DSE and Light Cone SR approaches. They also point to the nature of these states as 3-quark excitations at the core.

## Key claim
The paper argues that high-precision photoproduction and electroproduction data, particularly in open strangeness channels with polarization observables, have enabled the discovery or confirmation of several new light-quark baryon states, and that the Q² dependence of transition helicity amplitudes reveals the dominance of a three-quark core at high momentum transfer, with meson-baryon contributions important at low Q².

## Method
The author reviews experimental results from the CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab, focusing on exclusive meson photoproduction (γp → K⁺Λ, K⁺Σ⁰, pω, pφ) and electroproduction (ep → e'π⁺n, ep → e'p'π⁰, ep → e'p'π⁺π⁻) off proton targets. Multi-channel partial wave analyses by the Bonn-Gatchina, JLab, Bonn-Jülich, and Argonne-Osaka groups are used to extract resonance parameters. Transition helicity amplitudes and form factors are extracted as functions of photon virtuality Q² and compared to light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM), Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE), and light-cone sum rule (LCSR) calculations.

## Key numbers
- The Roper resonance N(1440)1/2⁺ pole mass is shifted to ≈ 1360 MeV downward from its bare core mass of 1736 MeV in dynamical coupled channel calculations (EBAC group).
- Resonances in the light-quark baryon spectrum have widths often 200 MeV to 350 MeV.
- New nucleon states and a Δ state have been discovered in the mass range 1.85 to 2.25 GeV, with five nucleon states showing nearly degenerate masses near 1.9 GeV.
- The Δ(2200)7/2⁻ state was confirmed with a branching ratio to Nπ of just 3.5%.
- For the N(1675)5/2⁻ state, the difference between data and quark model for the proton A₁/₂ amplitude is ΔAᵖ₁/₂(0) = 16 ± 8 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹, and for the neutron ΔAⁿ₁/₂(0) = -13 ± 5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹.
- For the N(1675)5/2⁻ A₃/₂ amplitudes: ΔAᵖ₃/₂(0) = 15 ± 5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹ and ΔAⁿ₃/₂(0) = -23 ± 10 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹.
- Lattice QCD predictions for the nucleon spectrum are made with pion masses of 396 MeV (Dudek 2012).

## Context
This work extends the decades-long effort to map the light-quark baryon excitation spectrum, which began with hadronic probes in the 1950s (Anderson 1952) and was framed by the constituent quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986). The results strengthen the connection between experiment and QCD-based approaches including DSE calculations (Segovia 2015) and light-cone sum rules (Anikin 2015), while also confirming the Moorhouse selection rule (Moorhouse 1966) for the N(1675)5/2⁻ state. The findings enable future searches for hybrid baryon states with gluonic admixtures and motivate higher-Q² measurements with CLAS12 to probe the transition to bare quark core contributions.
