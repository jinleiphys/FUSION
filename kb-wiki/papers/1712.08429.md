# Fluctuation and Dissipation of Axial Charge from Massive Quarks

> In quantum chromodynamics (QCD), axial charge is known to be non-conserved due to chiral anomaly and non-vanishing quark mass. In this paper, we explore the role of quark mass in axial charge fluctuation and dissipation. We present two separate calculations of axial charge correlator, which describe dynamics of axial charge. The first is free quarks at finite temperature. We find that axial charge can be generated through effective quantum fluctuations in free theory. However the fluctuation does not follow a random walk behavior. Due to the presence of axial symmetry breaking mass term, the axial charge also does not settle asymptotically to the thermodynamic limit given by susceptibility. The second calculation is in weakly coupled quark gluon plasma (QGP). We find in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, the quark-gluon interaction leads to random walk growth of axial charge, but dissipation is not visible. We estimate relaxation time scale for axial charge, finding it lies beyond the HTL regime.

## Key claim

Quark mass generates axial charge fluctuations in both free theory and weakly coupled QGP, but the dynamics differ qualitatively: in free theory the fluctuation does not follow a random walk and does not approach the thermodynamic susceptibility limit, while in weakly coupled QGP the fluctuation exhibits random walk growth with a rate that is numerically much smaller than the topological fluctuation rate for physically relevant strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors compute the Wightman correlator of total axial charge \(N_5\) in two settings: (1) free massive quarks at finite temperature, evaluating the one-loop quark diagram analytically; (2) weakly coupled QGP using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation at one-loop order, with quark mass taken as soft (\(m \sim gT\)). In the HTL calculation, they evaluate three one-loop diagrams (quark-antiquark, two-pseudophoton-two-gluon, and two-pseudophoton-two-quark) and use Ward identities to simplify the sum, extracting the low-frequency behavior of the correlator.

## Key numbers

* Free theory: the fluctuation \(\langle \Delta N_5(t)^2 \rangle_G\) scales as \(m^2\) and shows an initial rise followed by oscillatory decay to an asymptotic value; the spectral function vanishes for \(|q_0| < 2m\).
* Weakly coupled QGP: the mass diffusion rate \(\Gamma_m \simeq 0.013 \, m^2 m_f^2\), where \(m_f^2 = \frac{1}{8} C_F g^2 T^2\) is the thermal quark mass.
* For strange quark mass \(m = 100\) MeV, \(T = 400\) MeV, \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), and \(m_f \simeq 1.0 T\): \(\Gamma_{\text{CS}} \simeq 0.24 \, T^4\) (topological fluctuation rate) versus \(\Gamma_m \simeq 0.001 \, T^4\).
* The relaxation time scale \(\tau_m = \chi T / (2\Gamma_m)\) lies beyond the HTL regime, with \(\tau_m \sim T/(m^2 g^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of axial charge dynamics beyond the chiral anomaly and topological fluctuations of gluons (Moore 2010, Akamatsu 2015) by including the explicit breaking from quark mass. It contrasts with the free-theory result where no random walk occurs, and with the HTL calculation where random walk growth appears but dissipation is not visible. The mass diffusion rate is compared to the Chern-Simons diffusion rate (Moore and Tassler 2010). The results enable modeling of axial charge evolution in heavy-ion collisions and provide a baseline for future kinetic theory calculations (Moore 2006) needed to access the damping regime.
