# Shock waves from heavy-quark mesons in AdS/CFT

> We calculate the far-field stress-energy tensor of a quark gluon plasma due to a heavy-quark meson moving through it, using a semi-classical description in string theory of the meson as a string hanging into anti-de Sitter space. We find that these mesons create a shock wave but no diffusion wake, in contrast to single heavy quarks described via a trailing string, where the diffusion wake is strong. If thermal QCD responds similarly to heavy quarks and heavy quarkonia, the presence or suppression of the diffusion wake constitutes a prediction which can in principle be checked in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Heavy-quark mesons moving through a quark-gluon plasma create a shock wave but no diffusion wake, in contrast to single heavy quarks where the diffusion wake is strong; this result is derived in the holographic AdS/CFT setting for a meson described as a string with both ends attached to the boundary, and the absence of a diffusion wake is associated with the dipole structure of the source and the fact that such mesons experience no drag force.

## Method

The authors model a heavy-quark meson as a string hanging into AdS-Schwarzschild with both ends attached to the boundary, using the Nambu-Goto action to describe the string configuration. They compute the five-dimensional stress-energy tensor of the string, then solve the linearized five-dimensional Einstein equations sourced by this tensor to leading order in small wave-numbers, extracting the far-field gauge theory stress-energy tensor via the holographic dictionary. The analysis is performed for two orientations: the quark-antiquark separation parallel to the direction of motion and perpendicular to it.

## Key numbers

- The background thermal stress-energy tensor of the bath is \(\langle T_{mn} \rangle_{\rm bath} = \frac{\pi^2}{8} N^2 T^4 \operatorname{diag}\{3,1,1,1\}\).
- The meson contribution to the stress-energy tensor is \(\langle \delta T_{mn} \rangle = 2L^3 Q_{mn}\), where \(Q_{mn}\) are coefficients from the near-boundary expansion of metric perturbations.
- For the perpendicular configuration, the stress-energy tensor contains a pole at \(k_1^2 = k_{\bot}^2/(3v^2-1)\) for \(v^2 > 1/3\), indicating a shock wave; no pole at \(k_1=0\) indicates no diffusion wake.
- For the parallel configuration, the stress-energy tensor similarly shows a pole at \(k_1^2 = k_{\bot}^2/(3v^2-1)\) and no diffusion wake.
- The figure of merit \(\gamma_1\) for jet-splitting is given by \(\gamma_1 = \frac{1}{3}\left(1 + \frac{\ell}{2 v^2 \sigma}\right)\) for the perpendicular case, where \(\ell\) is the quark separation and \(\sigma\) is a function of velocity and turning point.

## Context

This work extends holographic calculations of energy loss from single heavy quarks (trailing string configurations) studied by Herzog (2006), Casalderrey-Solana (2006), Gubser (2006, 2007), and others, where a strong diffusion wake was found. The authors contrast their result with the phenomenological model of Casalderrey-Solana (2004) used to explain jet-splitting, which assumed suppressed diffusion wake for single quarks. The finding that composite colorless objects produce no diffusion wake provides a first hint that jet-splitting in heavy-ion collisions may involve motion of composite particles through the QGP, and could in principle be checked by constructing di-hadron correlators tagged with a \(J/\psi\) meson.
