# Measurement of Spin-Density Matrix Elements for $ϕ$-Meson Photoproduction from Protons and Deuterons Near Threshold

> The LEPS/SPring-8 experiment made a comprehensive measurement of the spin-density matrix elements for $γp \to ϕp$, $γd \to ϕp n$ and $γd \to ϕd$ at forward production angles. A linearly polarized photon beam at $E_γ$=1.6-2.4 GeV was used for the production of $ϕ$ mesons. The natural-parity Pomeron exchange processes remains dominant nearthreshold. The unnatural-parity processes of pseudoscalar exchange is visible in the production from nucleons but is greatly reduced in the coherent production from deuterons. There is no strong $E_γ$-dependence, but some dependence on momentum-transfer. A small but finite value of the spin-density matrix elements reflecting helicity-nonconserving amplitudes in the $t$-channel is observed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that natural-parity Pomeron exchange remains the dominant mechanism for phi-meson photoproduction near threshold (E_gamma = 1.6-2.4 GeV), but a small but finite contribution from helicity-nonconserving amplitudes is observed, contrary to the expectation of pure s-channel helicity conservation at higher energies.

## Method

The LEPS/SPring-8 experiment used a linearly polarized photon beam (E_gamma = 1.6-2.4 GeV) incident on liquid hydrogen and deuterium targets. The phi mesons were identified via their charged-kaon decay mode (K+K-), and a binless extended maximum likelihood fit was applied to the full three-dimensional decay angular distributions to extract all nine spin-density matrix elements simultaneously for the reactions gamma p -> phi p, gamma d -> phi p n, and gamma d -> phi d. For deuterium data, the coherent and incoherent contributions were disentangled using a weighted likelihood approach based on missing-mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Photon beam energy range: E_gamma = 1.6-2.4 GeV
- Integrated tagged photons: 5.6 x 10^12 for hydrogen runs, 4.6 x 10^12 for deuterium runs
- Phi meson selection cut: |M(K+K-) - M_phi| < 0.01 GeV/c^2
- Missing mass cut for gamma p -> phi p: |MM_p(gamma,phi)| < 0.03 GeV/c^2
- Missing mass division between coherent and incoherent deuterium events: MM_div = 1.89 GeV/c^2
- The matrix element rho^1_{1-1} (reflecting natural-parity vs unnatural-parity asymmetry) was found to be approximately 0.2 for proton targets, deviating from the pure natural-parity limit of 0.5
- For coherent deuterium production, rho^1_{1-1} was observed to be close to 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by the LEPS collaboration (2005, 2006) and others (CLAS at Jefferson Lab, SAPHIR at Bonn) that had only determined limited numbers of spin-density matrix elements from one-dimensional decay angular distributions. The complete set of nine spin-density matrix elements provides comprehensive constraints for theoretical models attempting to explain the narrow bump structure around E_gamma = 2 GeV seen in earlier cross-section measurements (LEPS 2005). The results enable tests of models involving Pomeron exchange, pseudoscalar meson exchange (pi, eta), and possible contributions from N* resonances with hidden strangeness as proposed by Titov (2003, 2004) and Hosaka (2005).
