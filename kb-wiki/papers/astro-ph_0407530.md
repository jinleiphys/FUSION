# The diffusive instability of kaon condensate in neutron star matter

> The beta equilibrated dense matter with kaon condensate is analyzed with respect to extended stability conditions including charge fluctuations. This kind of the diffusive instability, appeared to be common property in the kaon condensation case. Results for three different nuclear models are presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that kaon condensate in neutron star matter, when described by chiral effective field theory, is intrinsically unstable with respect to charge fluctuations (diffusive instability) for any value of the kaon-nucleon coupling, contrary to the assumption that only mechanical stability (positive compressibility) need be checked. This instability is not a consequence of the coupling strength being too strong but rather of the manner in which kaon-nucleon interactions are realized in the Lagrangian.

## Method

The author analyzes the thermodynamic stability of beta-equilibrated dense matter with kaon condensate by extending the usual stability conditions to include charge fluctuations as a second conserved quantity alongside baryon number. The intrinsic stability of a single phase is tested via the positivity of both the compressibility (mechanical stability) and the electrical capacitance (diffusive stability), expressed as derivatives of pressure and chemical potential. The analysis is performed for three nuclear models: a chiral effective theory model, the Glendenning-Schaffner (GS) meson exchange model, and the Knorren-Prakash-Ellis (KPE) meson exchange model, by computing pressure isobars in the charge-per-baryon versus electron chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density used is \(n_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for the chiral model and \(n_0 = 0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for the meson exchange models.
- The compression modulus at saturation is set to \(K_0 = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for both the chiral model and the meson exchange models.
- The nuclear symmetry energy at saturation is \(E_s(n_0) = 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the chiral model and \(E_s(n_0) = 32.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the meson exchange models.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term parameter \(a_3 m_s\) is varied in the range \(-310\ \mathrm{MeV} < a_3 m_s < -134\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the chiral model.
- The kaon optical potential values used for the meson exchange models are \(U_K = -80\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(U_K = -120\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (and \(-90\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the KPE model).
- For the GS model with \(U_K = -120\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the isobar \(P = 89\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^3\) corresponds to the critical density where both compressibility and electrical capacitance become negative.

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of kaon-condensed matter beyond the standard mechanical stability condition (positive compressibility) used in earlier studies such as Thorsson (1994) and Glendenning (1997). It directly contradicts the assumption that the Maxwell construction (which preserves only pressure and neutron chemical potential) is sufficient for neutron star matter with kaon condensate, as was applied in Thorsson (1994). The paper shows that the chiral model of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) leads to a completely unstable equation of state, while the GS meson exchange model of Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1997) can satisfy both stability conditions and form a mixed phase via the Gibbs construction. The KPE model of Knorren, Prakash and Ellis (1995) exhibits the same diffusive instability as the chiral model, indicating that the stability depends on the specific form of the kaon-nucleon coupling in the Lagrangian. This result enables future work to identify which classes of effective Lagrangians can produce thermodynamically stable kaon-condensed phases in neutron stars.
