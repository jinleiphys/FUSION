# Charmonium dissociation in hadronic matter

> Production of J/$ψ$ from nucleus-nucleus reactions depends sensitively on the dissociation cross section with light hadrons. Effective lagrangian methods are used to describe the hadronic degrees of freedom, including strangeness and charm. Cross sections with pions, rho mesons, kaons and nucleons having magnitudes 4--8 mb are found, and with steep thresholds. This, folded with thermal momentum distributions for the scattering partners, suggests a mean dissociation lifetime $\simeq$ 20 fm/c. Therefore, the ``abnormal'' J/$ψ$ suppression seen in recent Pb+Pb experiments seems to owe to expected hadron kinetics.

# Charmonium dissociation in hadronic matter

## Key claim

Effective lagrangian calculations including gauge-invariant contact terms (missing in prior analyses) yield J/ψ dissociation cross sections with pions, rho mesons, kaons, and nucleons of 4–8 mb, which when folded with thermal momentum distributions produce a mean dissociation lifetime of approximately 20 fm/c; this suggests that the "abnormal" J/ψ suppression seen in Pb+Pb experiments at CERN may be explained by ordinary hadronic kinetics rather than requiring quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The author constructs an SU(4) effective lagrangian for pseudoscalar and vector mesons including charm, with gauge covariant derivatives that generate both three-point vertices and four-point contact terms necessary for gauge invariance. Cross sections are computed for the reactions π + J/ψ → D + D̅*, ρ + J/ψ → D + D̅ and D̅* + D*, K + J/ψ → D_s + D̅ and related channels, and N + J/ψ → Λ_c + D̅, with coupling constants constrained by measured D* widths, vector meson dominance, and QCD sum rules. These cross sections are then folded with thermal Boltzmann distributions to obtain dissociation rates in a hadronic fireball.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ dissociation cross sections with pions, rho mesons, kaons, and nucleons have magnitudes 4–8 mb
- Mean dissociation lifetime in hadronic matter is approximately 20 fm/c
- D*± width upper limit from measurement is 131 keV; model calculation gives 46 keV, corresponding to g_{π D D*} = 8.8
- Vector meson dominance gives g_{ρ DD} = g_{ρ D* D*} = 5.6 and g_{ψ DD} = g_{ψ D* D*} = 7.7
- SU(4) symmetry gives g_{K D_s D*} = √2 g_{ρππ} = 8.5, with ρππ coupling adjusted to give ρ width of 151 MeV
- QCD sum rules predict g_{D N Λ_c} = 6.7 ± 2.1
- J/ψ dissociation rate in resonance matter is approximately 0.03 (fm/c)⁻¹; at temperatures approaching 200 MeV, the rate approaches 0.1 (fm/c)⁻¹
- Proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus experiments show A^α dependence with α ≈ 0.92

## Context

This work extends the effective lagrangian approach of Matinyan and Müller (1998), who found cross sections of order 1 mb, by including contact terms and interference effects that produce significantly larger cross sections (4–8 mb). It contrasts with the perturbative gluonic approach of Peskin (1979) and Bhanot (1979), applied by Kharzeev and Satz (1994) to pions, which gave cross sections below 0.1 mb, and with the nonperturbative quark-exchange calculation of Martins, Blaschke, and Quack (1995) which gave several mb. The results enable baseline estimates for J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions that must be understood before invoking deconfinement as an explanation for the "abnormal" suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration in Pb+Pb reactions at 158 GeV/nucleon.
