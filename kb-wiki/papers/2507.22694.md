# Entanglement suppression and emergent symmetries in hadron scatterings

> Recently entanglement suppression was proposed to be one potential origin of emergent symmetries. In this work, we extend this theoretical framework to accommodate particles with arbitrary spins and/or arbitrary group representations. As case studies, we discuss recent efforts to test the entanglement-suppression conjecture in two hadron systems that exhibit possible emergent symmetries. The first concerns interactions involving spin-3/2 baryons, where entanglement suppression gives rise to symmetries such as $\text{SU}(40)$ spin-flavor symmetry. The second system involves low-energy scattering of heavy mesons, where entanglement suppression leads to an enhancement of the inherent heavy-quark spin symmetry to a light-quark spin symmetry, predicting additional siblings for the prominent exotic double-charm meson $T_{cc}(3875)^+$. These predictions should be confronted against experimental data and lattice results to further test the minimal-entanglement conjecture.

# Entanglement suppression and emergent symmetries in hadron scatterings

## Key claim
Entanglement suppression in two-particle scattering, requiring the S-matrix to act as either the Identity or SWAP gate in spin space, can generate emergent symmetries such as SU(40) spin-flavor symmetry for spin-3/2 baryons and a light-quark spin symmetry for heavy mesons, but these predictions remain conjectural and must be tested against experimental data and lattice results.

## Method
The authors develop a general framework for entanglement suppression in scattering of two distinguishable particles with arbitrary spins and group representations, reformulating the condition that the S-matrix must be proportional to either the Identity or SWAP gate to avoid generating entanglement. They apply this condition to two hadron systems: non-relativistic S-wave scattering of spin-3/2 baryons (treated as four-dimensional qudits) and low-energy S-wave scattering of heavy mesons (D*D and D*D*), incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry and the near-threshold unitary limit of the Tcc(3875)+ state.

## Key numbers
- For spin-3/2 baryon scattering, entanglement suppression requires either all eight phase shifts equal (Identity case) or phase shifts for odd total spins equal and for even total spins equal with a difference of 0 or π/2 (SWAP case), leading to SU(40) spin-flavor symmetry or SU(4)spin × SU(10)flavor symmetry.
- For D*D* scattering, the vanishing-entanglement solution requires δ0* = δ2* and |δ0* − δ1*| = 0 (Identity) or π/2 (SWAP).
- For D*D scattering, the vanishing-entanglement solution requires |δ0 − δ1| = 0 (Identity) or π/2 (SWAP).
- The near-threshold Tcc(3875)+ state implies δ0 = π/2 in the I=0 D*D channel, corresponding to the unitary limit.
- Two possible solutions emerge: (i) all phase shifts equal to π/2; (ii) all isoscalar phase shifts equal to π/2 and all isovector phase shifts vanish.

## Context
This work extends the entanglement-suppression conjecture first proposed by Beane (2018) as an origin of emergent SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry in nuclear forces, and subsequent studies on pionic scattering (Beane, 2021), light octet baryon scattering (Liu, 2022; Liu, 2023), and Higgs doublet scattering (Carena, 2023; Chang, 2024; Busoni, 2025). The framework generalizes previous analyses confined to low-spin systems to arbitrary spins and group representations. The predictions for heavy meson scattering, specifically the enhancement of heavy-quark spin symmetry to light-quark spin symmetry and the prediction of isovector partners for Tcc(3875)+, should be tested against experimental data from LHCb and lattice QCD calculations to validate the minimal-entanglement conjecture.
