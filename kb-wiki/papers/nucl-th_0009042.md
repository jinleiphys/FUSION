# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Proton Number Z=126 for Translead Elements

> A highly extrapolatable semiempirical shell model mass equation applicable to translead elements up to Z=126 is presented. The equation is applied to the recently discovered superheavy nuclei with Z=118 and A=293 and with Z=114 and A=289 and their decay products.

# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Proton Number Z=126 for Translead Elements

## Key claim
The authors present a semiempirical shell model mass equation (SSME) that assumes Z=126 as the next spherical proton magic number after lead, rather than Z=114 as in earlier versions, and show it reproduces measured masses and alpha-decay energies of superheavy nuclei including 293118 and 289114. However, they explicitly caution that the results "are not a proof of superior magicity of Z = 126 as compared to the recently predicted Z = 120 or 124, because no comparative mass studies of this kind were made."

## Method
The total nuclear energy is written as a sum of pairing, deformation, and Coulomb energies, with separate parameterizations for two shell regions: region A (Z >= 82, N <= 126) and region B (Z >= 82, 126 <= N <= 184). The coefficients were determined by least-squares adjustment to experimental masses, with the deformation terms including particle-hole symmetric configuration interaction functions. For region A, a readjustment was performed allowing only four parameters (alpha, epsilon, Theta, kappa) to vary while holding the other seven coefficients fixed, in order to preserve the quality of Q_alpha predictions.

## Key numbers
- For region B: average deviation of mass predictions for 56 new masses measured after the original adjustment is 53 keV, with an rms deviation of 236 keV; for Q_alpha values the average deviation is -3 keV and rms is 220 keV.
- For the 49 nuclei in region B with N >= 129 (interior of the shell, not on the N=126 boundary), the average deviation is -1 keV and rms is 155 keV.
- For region A after readjustment: average deviation of mass predictions for all 150 known masses is 2 keV, rms is 246 keV; for Q_alpha values the average deviation is 2 keV and rms is 99 keV.
- For the 293118 decay chain: average deviation of predicted Q_alpha values from data is -197 keV, rms is 308 keV for the Z=126 equation; for the Smolanczuk (1999) predictions the average deviation is -154 keV and rms is 357 keV.
- For the 289114 decay chain: average deviation of predicted Q_alpha values from measured energies is 847 keV, rms is 905 keV.

## Context
This work extends the semiempirical shell model mass equation (SSME) originally developed by Liran and Zeldes (1976), which assumed Z=114 as the next proton magic number and showed deteriorating agreement beyond Z=108. The authors build on their own earlier studies (Liran, Marinov, Zeldes 2000) that established predictive power for the Z=126 results in both region A and region B. The paper contrasts its approach with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Smolanczuk (1999), Moller et al. (1997), and self-consistent mean field calculations by Cwiok et al. (1996), Benhar et al. (1999), and Kruppa et al. (2000), which variously predict proton magicities at Z=114, 120, 124, or 126. The results enable improved mass predictions for superheavy element research and support the phenomenological evidence from B(E2) systematics (Zamfir 1995) and Wigner term studies (Zeldes 1998) favoring Z=126 as a spherical proton magic number.
