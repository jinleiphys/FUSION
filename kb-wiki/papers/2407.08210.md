# Chiral dynamics in soft triaxial nuclei

> The chirality in soft triaxial nuclei is investigated for the first time with the time-dependent and tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theories on a three-dimensional space lattice in a microscopic and self-consistent way. Taking the puzzling chiral nucleus $^{106}$Ag as an example, the experimental energies of the observed nearly degenerate bands are well reproduced without any free parameters beyond the well-defined density functional. A novel chiral mode in soft triaxial nuclei is newly revealed from the microscopic dynamics of the total angular momentum. This opens a new research area for the study of chirality, particularly in relation to soft nuclear shapes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the first time, chirality in soft triaxial nuclei has been investigated microscopically and self-consistently using time-dependent and tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theories, and that a novel chiral mode distinct from the precession dynamics seen in rigid chiral nuclei is revealed; the key qualifier is that this is demonstrated for the specific case of the puzzling chiral nucleus \(^{106}\)Ag, where the experimental energies of the observed nearly degenerate bands are well reproduced without any free parameters beyond the well-defined density functional.

## Method

The authors combined the tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) to obtain the lower band of the chiral doublet bands and the time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) on a three-dimensional space lattice to describe the chiral excitations and obtain the upper band. For \(^{106}\)Ag, they used the density functional PC-PK1 with no additional parameters, solving the Kohn-Sham equations in a 22.4 fm cubic box with 0.8 fm mesh, and extracted normal-mode frequencies from Fourier transforms of the time evolution of the azimuth angle \(\varphi_J\) of the total angular momentum in the body-fixed frame.

## Key numbers

* The potential energy curves for the four-quasiparticle configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}\otimes\nu h_{11/2}(gd)^2\) in \(^{106}\)Ag are soft: the energy rise against the minimum is generally less than 500 keV when the triaxial deformation reaches \(\gamma \simeq 20^\circ\).
* At total angular momentum \(I=10\hbar\), the chiral excitation energy (one-phonon energy) extracted from the higher-frequency normal mode is 0.584 MeV, identical for two different initial states (10 keV and 100 keV above equilibrium).
* At total angular momentum \(I=18\hbar\), the chiral excitation energy is 0.700 MeV, identical for two different initial states.
* The TDCDFT calculations were performed for angular momenta from \(I=11\hbar\) to \(I=20\hbar\) to build the full energy spectra.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear chirality from rigid triaxial shapes, which has been the focus of prior work such as the particle rotor model (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997) and the interacting boson fermion-fermion model (Tonev et al., 2006), to soft triaxial nuclei, where most realistic nuclei lie. It directly addresses the puzzling chiral candidate \(^{106}\)Ag, for which previous phenomenological models (e.g., particle rotor model, interacting boson fermion-fermion model) gave contrasting interpretations, and for which a prior TAC-CDFT study (Zhao et al., 2019) showed a soft potential energy surface. The results enable a new research area for studying chirality in relation to soft nuclear shapes and provide a microscopic framework for interpreting future experiments on soft chiral nuclei.
