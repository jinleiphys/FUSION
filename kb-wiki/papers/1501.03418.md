# Dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma using (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic dissipative hydrodynamics

> We compute dilepton production from the deconfined phase of the quark-gluon plasma using leading-order (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. The anisotropic hydrodynamics equa- tions employed describe the full spatiotemporal evolution of the transverse temperature, spheroidal momentum-space anisotropy parameter, and the associated three-dimensional collective flow of the matter. The momentum-space anisotropy is also taken into account in the computation of the dilepton production rate, allowing for a self-consistent description of dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma. For our final results, we present predictions for high-energy dilepton yields as a function of invariant mass, transverse momentum, and pair rapidity. We demonstrate that high- energy dilepton production is extremely sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma. As a result, it may be possible to experimentally constrain the early-time momentum-space anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma generated in relativistic heavy ion collisions using high-energy dilepton yields.

## Key claim

High-energy dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma is extremely sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy, which may allow experimental constraints on early-time anisotropy, but only if the full spatiotemporal evolution of both the anisotropy parameter and transverse temperature is self-consistently modeled using (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics rather than standard viscous hydrodynamics linearized around an isotropic background.

## Method

The authors compute dilepton production rates using leading-order (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics, which evolves the transverse temperature Λ, the spheroidal momentum-space anisotropy parameter ξ, and three-dimensional collective flow by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation. The dilepton emission rate is derived from relativistic kinetic theory using a Romatschke-Strickland form for the quark and antiquark distribution functions, and the rate is then integrated over the full space-time history of the QGP phase (defined by effective temperature T > Tc = 175 MeV) after boosting each fluid cell's momentum to its local rest frame. The initial conditions use an optical Glauber model with a mixed wounded-nucleon and binary-collision source density, a Gaussian longitudinal profile, and a homogeneous initial anisotropy parameter ξ0.

* Initial temperature fixed to T0 = 567 MeV for the shear viscosity scan (Figure 2).

* Longitudinal profile parameters: Δς = 2.5, σς = 1.4.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dilepton production using viscous hydrodynamics (Dusling 2008, Vujanovic 2013) by linearizing around an anisotropic background rather than an isotropic thermal background, enabling better description of early-time and edge-of-plasma emissions. It builds on earlier anisotropic rate calculations (Martinez 2008) and the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski, Ryblewski, Martinez, and Strickland (2010–2014). The results suggest that high-energy dilepton yields could serve as an experimental probe of early-time momentum-space anisotropy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, complementing studies of real photon and hadron observables.
