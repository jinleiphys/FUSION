# Color Molecular-Dynamics for High Density Matter

> We propose a microscopic simulation for quark many-body system based on molecular dynamics. Using color confinement and one-gluon exchange potentials together with the meson exchange potentials between quarks, we construct nucleons and nuclear/quark matter. Statistical feature and the dynamical change between confinement and deconfinement phases are studied with this molecular dynamics simulation.

## Key claim

The authors propose a color molecular dynamics (CMD) simulation as a microscopic approach to study the transition between confined nuclear matter and deconfined quark matter, but note that the results are still at the qualitative level and require refinement of interaction parameters and inclusion of additional degrees of freedom for more quantitative discussions.

## Method

The authors simulate a many-quark system using classical molecular dynamics with SU(3) color degrees of freedom, representing each quark as a Gaussian wave packet with a fixed width and a coherent color state parametrized by four angles. The time evolution of spatial and color coordinates is governed by equations of motion derived from a time-dependent variational principle, using an effective Hamiltonian that includes a confining potential, one-gluon exchange, and sigma-plus-omega meson exchange potentials between quarks. Infinite matter is simulated under periodic boundary conditions, with nucleons constructed as color-neutral three-quark clusters that are randomly distributed in a box and then cooled to their minimum energy state before the internal quark motion is turned on.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- Constituent quark mass: $m = 350$ MeV
- QCD fine structure constant: $\alpha_s = 1.25$
- String tension: $K = 0.75$ GeV/fm
- Wave packet width for gluonic interactions: $L = 0.35$ fm (corresponding to r.m.s. radius of 0.43 fm)
- Effective wave packet width for meson interactions: $L^{\rm eff} = 0.7$ fm (corresponding to r.m.s. radius of 0.86 fm)
- Nucleon total energy after initial kick: 1269 MeV, with time-averaged kinetic energy of 74 MeV
- Nucleon r.m.s. radius: 0.46 fm (quark-core size) or 0.87 fm (physical nucleon size for meson-exchange interaction)
- Cluster distance criterion for confinement: $d_{\rm cluster} = 1$ fm
- Color accuracy criterion: $\varepsilon = 0.05$
- For excitation energy $E^* > 200$ MeV/q, confined ratio $R$ is less than 20% for all densities studied
- For $E^* > 300$ MeV/q, effective temperature $T^*$ depends almost linearly on $E^*$ irrespective of baryon density

## Context

This work extends molecular dynamics simulations previously applied to many-nucleon systems (Aichelin and Stocker, 1986; Maruyama et al., 1998) to the quark level, and is similar in spirit to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model developed by Guichon (1988) and Saito and Thomas (1994). The method provides a starting point for studying the statistical features of the hadron-quark transition, with potential applications to finite nuclei and heavy-ion collision dynamics, though the authors note that future work must include antisymmetrization of quark wave functions, flavor and spin degrees of freedom, anti-quarks, and medium modification of the constituent-quark mass.
