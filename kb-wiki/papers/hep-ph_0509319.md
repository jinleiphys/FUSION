# Vector Manifestation and the Hidden Local Symmetry

> In this write-up, I summarize the key ingredients of the vector manifestaion formulated in the hidden local symmetry theory, in which the $ρ$ meson becomes massless degenerated with the pion at the chiral phase transition point.

## Key claim

The vector manifestation (VM) is a specific Wigner realization of chiral symmetry in which the ρ meson becomes massless and degenerate with the pion at the chiral phase transition point, in contrast to the conventional Ginzburg-Landau (GL) manifestation where the scalar meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion. The VM is formulated under the assumptions that only π and ρ are relevant degrees of freedom near the critical temperature, that other mesons remain heavy, and that partial chiral symmetry restoration already occurs at Tc − ε.

## Method

The author formulates the VM within the hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory by introducing intrinsic temperature dependences of the bare parameters through Wilsonian matching to QCD. The bare gauge coupling g and parameter a are required to approach g → 0 and a → 1 as T → Tc, which are fixed points of the renormalization group equations, ensuring that the ρ meson pole mass vanishes at the critical temperature. The chiral representations of the π, ρ, scalar meson S, and axial-vector meson A1 are analyzed under SU(3)L × SU(3)R to distinguish the VM from the GL manifestation.

## Key numbers

- At the chiral phase transition point, the VM predicts fπ² → 0, mρ² → mπ² = 0, and fρ²/fπ² → 1, where fρ is the decay constant of the longitudinal ρ at on-shell.
- For Nf = 2, the VM predicts the vector and axial-vector susceptibilities satisfy χA = χV = (2/3) Tc².
- The predicted pion velocity at the critical temperature is vπ(Tc) = 0.83 – 0.99, consistent with the value vπ(T) = 0.65 extracted from STAR data by Cramer et al.
- The predicted splitting of chiral doublers of D mesons is 0.31 ± 0.12 GeV, in agreement with experiments from BABAR, CLEO, and Belle.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) which predicted that light-quark hadron masses drop in proportion to the quark condensate, and provides a theoretical foundation for explaining dilepton spectra observed in KEK-PS E325, CBELSA/TAPS, and CERN/SPS experiments. The VM contrasts with the conventional GL manifestation based on the linear sigma model, as analyzed by Gilman, Harari, and Weinberg (1969) for two-flavor QCD. The formulation enables predictions for vector dominance violation near Tc, which helps explain NA60 dimuon data, and provides a framework for extending the VM to dense matter through intrinsic density dependence.
