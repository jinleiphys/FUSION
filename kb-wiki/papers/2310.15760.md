# Improved action for contact effective field theory

> We present an improved action for renormalizable effective field theories (EFTs) of systems near the two-body unitarity limit. The ordering of EFT interactions is constrained, but not entirely fixed, by the renormalization group. The remaining freedom can be used to improve the theory's convergence, to simplify its applications, and to connect it to phenomenological models. We exemplify the method on a contact theory applied to systems of up to five $^4$He atoms. We solve the EFT at leading order including a subleading interaction that accounts for part of the two-body effective range. We show that the effects of such fake range can be compensated in perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, as long as the fake range is smaller or comparable to the experimental effective range. These results open the possibility of using similar improved actions for other many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a subleading interaction introducing a "fake range" can be included at leading order (LO) in contact effective field theory for systems near the two-body unitarity limit, and its effects can be compensated perturbatively at next-to-leading order (NLO) as long as the fake range is smaller than or comparable to the experimental effective range, preserving renormalization group invariance and the hierarchy of interactions.

## Method

The authors modify the LO interaction by adding a finite-range auxiliary two-body potential (and optionally a three-body potential) that accounts for part of the two-body effective range, using Gaussian smearing for regularization. They solve the EFT at LO with this improved action for systems of up to five $^4$He atoms using the stochastic variational method, then include the standard NLO interactions (two-body effective range, three-body force, and four-body force) in first-order distorted-wave Born approximation perturbation theory. The method is benchmarked against potential-model results from LM2M2 and PCKLJS potentials for $^4$He clusters.

## Key numbers

- The two-body scattering length for $^4$He is $a_2 = 100.23$ Å (LM2M2) or $90.42(92)$ Å (PCKLJS); the two-body effective range is $r_2 = 7.326$ Å (LM2M2) or $7.27$ Å (PCKLJS)
- The three-body scale is $Q_3 \simeq 0.08$ Å$^{-1}$, giving $|a_2| Q_3 \simeq 8$
- The three-body ground-state binding energy $B_3$ is 126.50 mK (LM2M2) or 131.84 mK (PCKLJS)
- The tetramer ground-state binding energy $B_4$ is 559.22 mK (LM2M2) or 573.90 mK (PCKLJS)
- The pentamer ground-state binding energy $B_5$ is 1306.7 mK (LM2M2)
- At LO with the standard potential, extrapolated binding energy ratios are $B_3/B_3^*(\Lambda\to\infty)=59.089$, $B_4(\Lambda\to\infty)/B_3 = 4.150(6)$, and $B_5(\Lambda\to\infty)/B_3 = 9.27(5)$
- The fake range is varied in the range $6 \leq (Q_3 \tilde{R})^{-1} \leq 24$, corresponding to inverse fake ranges from $6 Q_3$ to $24 Q_3$
- The dimer binding energy ratio from the standard LO potential converges to $B_2(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty )/B_2^{\rm LM2M2}\simeq 0.93$

## Context

This work extends the contact EFT approach for systems near unitarity previously applied to $^4$He clusters by Bazak (2016, 2018) and to nuclear systems by various authors. The method is inspired by Symanzik's improved actions from lattice quantum field theory (Symanzik, 1983) and addresses the problem of LO instability in multi-fermion systems observed by Contessi (2017) and Bansal (2017). The approach offers an alternative to resumming higher-order interactions into truncated potentials (as done by Machleidt, 2020) which can violate the Wigner bound (Wigner, 1955) and produce regulator-dependent results. The improved action enables systematic perturbation theory while preserving renormalization, and could be applied to other many-body systems including nuclear physics.
