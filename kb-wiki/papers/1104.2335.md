# Relation between measurable and principal characteristics of radiation-induced shape-change of graphite

> On the basis of studies of radiation-induced shape-change of reactor graphite GR-280, through the series of measurements of samples with different orientation of cutting with respect to the direction of extrusion, a conclusion is made about the existence of polycrystal substructural elements - domains. Domains, like graphite as a whole, possess the property of transverse isotropy, but have different amplitudes of shape-change and random orientations of the axes of axial symmetry. The model of graphite, constructed on the basis of the concept of domains allowed to explain from a unified point of view most of existing experimental data. It is shown that the presence of the disoriented domain structure leads to the development of radiation-induced stresses and to the dependence of the shape-change on the size of graphite samples. We derive the relation between the shape-change of finite size samples and the actual shape-change of macro-graphite.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the radiation-induced shape-change of reactor graphite cannot be fully described by a homogeneous texture model; instead, the existence of mesoscale "domains" (regions of intermediate size between the grain and macro-graphite) with random orientations of their anisotropy axes explains the observed elliptical cross-sections and the discrepancy between conventional and directly measured volume changes, provided that the domain structure and resulting internal stresses are accounted for.

## Method

The authors irradiated cylindrical samples of reactor graphite GR-280 (cut parallel and perpendicular to the extrusion direction, diameters 6 mm and 8 mm, length 50 mm) in the BOR-60 fast reactor at three temperatures (460, 550, and 640 degrees Celsius) up to a fluence of about 3 times 10 to the 22 neutrons per square centimeter (energies above 0.18 MeV). They measured length changes and cross-sectional diameters at 32 points on the circle for each of many sections along the sample, then used Fourier analysis of the squared diameters to extract the maximal and minimal diameters of the elliptical cross-sections and the orientation angles of the ellipses. From these data they derived the principal strains of the domains and the average squared sine of the polar angle of domain orientation.

## Key numbers

- Samples were irradiated at temperatures 460 plus or minus 25 degrees Celsius, 550 plus or minus 25 degrees Celsius, and 640 plus or minus 25 degrees Celsius.
- Maximum neutron fluence reached was about 3 times 10 to the 22 neutrons per square centimeter at energies above 0.18 MeV.
- Measurements were taken with dose intervals of about 0.6 times 10 to the 21 neutrons per square centimeter.
- Sample diameters used were 6 mm and 8 mm; sample length was 50 mm.
- The distance between measured cross-sections along the sample was 4.5 mm.
- The typical domain size was estimated as about 6 mm from the ratio r of sums of squared angle differences.
- The coefficient C in the asymptotic volume change formula C times [(Delta l/l)_perpendicular minus (Delta l/l)_parallel] squared was approximately 7 for all curves at large fluence.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of radiation-induced dimensional changes in reactor graphites beyond the homogeneous transversely isotropic model commonly used in the literature (e.g., in earlier studies of GR-280 and other extruded graphites). The authors introduce a domain concept to explain the observed elliptical cross-sections and size-dependent shape-change, which previous models could not account for. The results enable improved extrapolation of graphite behavior to higher neutron doses and provide a framework for relating laboratory measurements on small samples to the behavior of bulk graphite in reactor cores.
