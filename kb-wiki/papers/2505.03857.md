# Parameterizations of Electron Scattering Form Factors for Elastic Scattering and Electro-Excitation of Nuclear States for $\rm ^{27}Al$ and $\rm ^{40}Ca$

> We report on empirical parameterizations of longitudinal (${\cal R}_L$) and transverse (${\cal R}_T$) nuclear electromagnetic form factors for elastic scattering and the excitations of nuclear states in ${\rm ^{27}Al}$ and ${\rm ^{40}Ca}$. The parameterizations are needed for the calculations of radiative corrections in measurements of electron scattering cross sections on ${\rm ^{27}Al}$ and ${\rm ^{40}Ca}$ in the quasi-elastic, resonance and inelastic continuum regions, provide the contribution of nuclear excitations in investigations of the Coulomb Sum Rule, and test theoretical model predictions for excitation of nuclear states in electron and neutrino interactions on nuclear targets at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper provides empirical parameterizations of longitudinal and transverse nuclear electromagnetic form factors for elastic scattering and nuclear state excitations (below 10 MeV) in ²⁷Al and ⁴⁰Ca, but these parameterizations are explicitly needed for radiative correction calculations and are not derived from first-principles theory; they are fits to existing experimental data, with many form factor measurements requiring digitization from published figures.

## Method

The authors parameterize the squared longitudinal and transverse form factors for elastic scattering and nuclear excited states (excitation energy < 10 MeV) using sums of Gaussian functions of the effective three-momentum transfer q_eff, with separate functional forms for electric (charge) and magnetic elastic form factors versus excitation form factors. For ²⁷Al, the elastic electric form factor includes a harmonic well shape plus four Gaussian terms, while the elastic magnetic form factor uses three Gaussians with a multiplicative q_eff/(q_eff + A_M) factor; for ⁴⁰Ca, the elastic electric form factor uses a harmonic well shape plus six Gaussians. Excitation form factors for both nuclei are parameterized as q_eff² times a sum of Gaussians for longitudinal and q_eff times a sum of Gaussians for transverse, with parameters tabulated for each resolved nuclear state. The fits are compared to experimental cross-section data from Li (1974), Itoh (1970), Oguro (1984), and others, with Gaussian smearing applied to match experimental resolution.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parameterization for ¹²C (Bodek 2023) to ²⁷Al and ⁴⁰Ca, providing form factors needed for radiative corrections in precision electron scattering measurements by the Qweak collaboration at Jefferson Lab and the JUPITER collaboration. The parameterizations enable calculations of the Coulomb Sum Rule contributions from nuclear excitations and provide inputs for testing theoretical models of electron and neutrino scattering on nuclear targets at low energies, complementing quasi-elastic response function measurements. A recent analysis of only the elastic form factors for these nuclei was reported by Noel (2024).
