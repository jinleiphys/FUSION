# Anomalous transport coefficients from Kubo formulas in Holography

> In the presence of dense matter quantum anomalies give rise to two new transport phenomena. An anomalous current is generated either by an external magnetic field or through vortices in the fluid carrying the anomalous charge. The associated transport coefficients are the anomalous magnetic and vortical conductivities. Whereas a Kubo formula for the anomalous magnetic conductivity is well known we develop a new Kubo type formula that allows the calculation of the vortical conductivity through a two point function of the anomalous current and the momentum density. We also point out that the anomalous vortical conductivity can be understood as a response to a gravitomagnetic field. We apply these Kubo formulas to a simple Holographic system, the R-charged black hole.

## Key claim
The paper derives new Kubo formulas for the anomalous vortical conductivity, allowing its calculation via a two-point function of the anomalous current and the energy current, and shows that the chiral vortical effect can be understood as a response to a gravitomagnetic field. The results are applied to a holographic system (the R-charged black hole) and reproduce known results for the magnetic conductivity, while the vortical conductivity result is a genuine new finding.

## Method
The authors derive Kubo formulas for the anomalous magnetic and vortical conductivities from the constitutive relations of relativistic hydrodynamics in the Landau frame, accounting for an ambiguity in the definition of fluid velocity. They then compute the relevant retarded two-point functions analytically in the hydrodynamic limit using a holographic model: a 5-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory dual to a 4-dimensional chiral U(1) gauge theory, specifically the asymptotically AdS Reissner-Nordstrom black brane (R-charged black hole). The calculation involves expanding the bulk action to second order in metric and gauge field perturbations and solving the resulting equations of motion.

## Key numbers
* The anomalous magnetic conductivity from the Kubo formula is given by $\xi_B = \lim_{k_n\rightarrow 0} \frac{-i}{2 k_n} \sum_{k.l} \epsilon_{nkl} \left(\left. \left\langle J^{k} J^{l} \right\rangle - \frac{n}{\epsilon+P}\left\langle T^{tk} J^{l} \right\rangle \right)\right|_{\omega=0, A_0=0}$.
* The anomalous vortical conductivity from the Kubo formula is given by $\xi_V = \lim_{k_n\rightarrow 0} \frac{-i}{2 k_n} \sum_{k.l} \epsilon_{nkl} \left.\left( \left\langle J^k T^{tl} \right\rangle - \frac{n}{\epsilon+P}\left\langle T^{tk} T^{tl} \right\rangle \right) \right|_{\omega=0}$.
* The frequency-dependent chiral magnetic conductivity $\sigma_B$ shows that at very small temperatures, the conductivity drops to $1/3$ of its zero frequency value as soon as one moves to $\omega\neq0$.
* The pressure of the dual gauge theory is given by $P = \frac{M}{16\pi G L^3}$, and the theory is conformal, obeying $\epsilon = 3P$.

## Context
This work extends the holographic derivation of anomalous transport coefficients for a single anomalous U(1) current, building on the constitutive relations established by Son and Surowka (2009) and the holographic calculations of Erdmenger et al. (2008) and Banerjee et al. (2008). It resolves a discrepancy in earlier holographic calculations of the chiral magnetic conductivity (Rebhan 2009, Yee 2009) by following the approach of Gynther et al. (2010), which distinguishes between the chemical potential and a background gauge field. The derived Kubo formulas enable first-principle field-theoretical computations of the chiral vortical effect in weakly coupled theories, analogous to the calculation for the chiral magnetic effect by Kharzeev and Warringa (2009), and are argued to be relevant for explaining charge asymmetry fluctuations observed at RHIC for non-central heavy ion collisions.
