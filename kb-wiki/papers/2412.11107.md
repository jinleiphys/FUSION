# Quantum-Corrected Holographic Wilson Loop Expectation Values and Super-Yang-Mills Confinement

> Confinement is a well-known phenomenon in the infrared regime of (supersymmetric) Yang-Mills theory. While both experimental observations and numerical simulations have robustly confirmed its existence, the underlying physical mechanism remains elusive. Unraveling the theoretical origin of confinement continues to be a profound and longstanding challenge in both physics and mathematics. Motivated by recent advances in quantum Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity, we investigate the Wilson loop expectation values in the large-$N$ limit of $\mathscr{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory at finite chemical potential, employing a holographic approach within the background of an extremal AdS$_5$ Reissner-Nordström black brane. Our results reveal that quantum gravitational fluctuations in the near-horizon region significantly modify the holographic Wilson loop expectation values. These values exhibit an area-law behavior, indicative of a confining quark-antiquark potential. Within this framework, our findings suggest that confinement in the super-Yang-Mills theory arises as a consequence of near-horizon quantum gravity fluctuations in the bulk extremal AdS$_5$ black brane geometry.

# Quantum-Corrected Holographic Wilson Loop Expectation Values and Super-Yang-Mills Confinement

## Key claim
The authors show that quantum gravitational fluctuations in the near-horizon AdS$_2$ region of an extremal AdS$_5$ Reissner-Nordström black brane, modeled via Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity, produce an area-law behavior for holographic Wilson loop expectation values and a linear term in the quark-antiquark potential at zero temperature. This suggests that confinement in large-$N$ $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory at finite chemical potential arises from near-horizon quantum gravity effects, rather than from finite-temperature or phenomenological metric deformations.

## Method
The authors start with an extremal Reissner-Nordström AdS$_5$ black brane, whose near-horizon region is AdS$_2 \times \mathbb{T}^3$. They introduce quantum gravity fluctuations in the near-horizon AdS$_2$ region using the Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity approach, treating the metric factor as a bilocal operator and averaging over Schwarzian modes. The quantum-corrected metric is then matched to the full AdS$_5$ geometry via continuity and smoothness conditions. Using this quantum-corrected metric, they compute the Nambu-Goto action for both rectangular temporal Wilson loops and circular spatial Wilson loops, extracting the quark-antiquark potential and the Wilson loop expectation values analytically and numerically.

## Key numbers
- The quark-antiquark potential from the rectangular temporal Wilson loop is $E = - \frac{4 \pi^2}{\Gamma(1/4)^4} \frac{R^2}{L} + \frac{5\, \Gamma(1/4)^2}{4608\, \pi^5\, \Gamma(3/4)^2} \frac{R^2}{C^2} L + \mathcal{O}(C^{-3}, U_T/U_0)$, where the first term is the Coulomb potential and the second term is the confining linear term.
- For the circular spatial Wilson loop, the Nambu-Goto action is $S_{\text{NG}} = \frac{\pi R^2}{12} \left(- {_3F_2}(1/2,1/2,3/4;1,7/4;1) - \frac{24 \pi^2}{\Gamma(1/4)^4}\right) + \frac{B R^2\, \Gamma(1/4)^4}{2\pi^3} \frac{L^2}{C^2} + \mathcal{O}(C^{-3}, U_T/U_0)$, with $B = [\pi - 4 + 4\log(2)]/(2304 \pi^4)$, showing area-law behavior.
- The quantum correction factor $h(\zeta)$ in the near-horizon AdS$_2$ metric expands as $h(\zeta) = 1 + \frac{3}{2 \pi^4} (\zeta/C)^2 - \frac{15}{2 \pi^6} (\zeta/C)^3 + \frac{315}{8 \pi^8} (\zeta/C)^4 + \cdots$.
- Fitting the quark-antiquark potential to the Cornell potential yields $C \approx 0.09986$ fm and $R = L_{\text{AdS}}/\sqrt{\alpha'} \approx 1.50863$.

## Context
This work extends earlier holographic studies of Wilson loops in AdS/CFT, where Rey and Maldacena (1998) found only a Coulomb potential in pure AdS, and Witten (1998), Rey (1998), Brandhuber (1998), and Greensite (1999) showed that finite-temperature black holes produce confining terms. The authors build on recent advances in quantum Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity by Almheiri (2014) and Maldacena (2016), applying the Schwarzian mode averaging technique of Blommaert (2019) to the near-horizon region. Unlike phenomenological metric deformations (Andreev 2006, Karch 2006, Pirner 2009, Nian 2009, Trawinski 2014), this provides a first-principles holographic mechanism for confinement at zero temperature. The results enable further investigation of quantum-gravity-induced phase transitions in the super-Yang-Mills phase diagram and may provide a holographic interpretation for the mass gap.
