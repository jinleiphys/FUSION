# Testing the Mutually Enhanced Magicity Effect in Nuclear Incompressibility via the Giant Monopole Resonance in the $^{204,206,208}$Pb Isotopes

> Using inelastic $α$-scattering at extremely forward angles, including $0^\circ$, the strength distributions of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) have been measured in the $^{204,206,208}$Pb isotopes in order to examine the proposed mutually enhanced magicity (MEM) effect on the nuclear incompressibility. The MEM effect had been suggested as a likely explanation of the "softness" of nuclear incompressibility observed in the ISGMR measurements in the Sn and Cd isotopes. Our experimental results rule out any manifestation of the MEM effect in nuclear incompressibility and leave the question of the softness of the open-shell nuclei unresolved still.

# Testing the Mutually Enhanced Magicity Effect in Nuclear Incompressibility via the Giant Monopole Resonance in the $^{204,206,208}$Pb Isotopes

## Key claim

The mutually enhanced magicity (MEM) effect, which had been proposed to explain the unexpectedly low nuclear incompressibility ("softness") observed in open-shell Sn and Cd isotopes, is ruled out as a measurable influence on the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid energies in Pb isotopes. The ISGMR centroid energies in $^{204,206,208}$Pb are found to be practically identical, contradicting the predicted $\sim$600 keV difference between $^{204}$Pb and $^{208}$Pb that would arise from the MEM effect.

## Method

Inelastic $\alpha$-scattering at 100 MeV/u was performed at the RCNP ring cyclotron facility using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer, with measurements covering $0^\circ$ to $9.5^\circ$ to access the ISGMR which peaks at $0^\circ$. Elastic scattering data were used to extract optical model parameters via a hybrid potential (density-dependent single-folding model for the real part, Woods-Saxon for the imaginary part). Multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) was applied to the excitation-energy spectra (8.5–35 MeV) to separate the ISGMR ($L=0$) contribution from other multipoles up to $\Delta L=7$, using distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR centroid energies from Lorentzian fits: $^{204}$Pb = 13.8 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV, $^{206}$Pb = 13.8 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV, $^{208}$Pb = 13.7 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV
- ISGMR widths: $^{204}$Pb = 3.3 $\pm$ 0.2 MeV, $^{206}$Pb = 2.8 $\pm$ 0.2 MeV, $^{208}$Pb = 3.3 $\pm$ 0.2 MeV
- Moment ratios $\sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}$ (9.5–19.5 MeV): $^{204}$Pb = 13.7 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV, $^{206}$Pb = 13.6 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV, $^{208}$Pb = 13.5 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV
- Product $E_{ISGMR}A^{1/3}$: $^{204}$Pb = 81.2 $\pm$ 0.6 MeV, $^{206}$Pb = 81.5 $\pm$ 0.6 MeV, $^{208}$Pb = 81.2 $\pm$ 0.6 MeV (practically identical across isotopes)
- The predicted difference in $E_{ISGMR}$ between $^{204}$Pb and $^{208}$Pb from the MEM effect was $\sim$600 keV; the measured difference is consistent with zero
- The difference expected from the $K_{\tau}$ value obtained from Sn and Cd measurements (Tao 2007, Tao 2010, Patel 2012) would be 0.19 $\pm$ 0.03 MeV, consistent with the current measurements

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the "softness" puzzle in nuclear incompressibility, previously observed in ISGMR measurements on Sn isotopes (Tao 2007, Tao 2010) and Cd isotopes (Patel 2012), where standard relativistic and non-relativistic models (which reproduce ISGMR centroid energies in $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb) overestimate the centroid energies by up to 1 MeV. The MEM effect had been proposed by Khan (2009) as a possible explanation, predicting that doubly-magic $^{208}$Pb would be stiffer than open-shell $^{204,206}$Pb. The present results rule out the MEM effect as the cause, leaving the question of the softness of open-shell nuclei unresolved. The data also provide new ISGMR centroid energies for $^{204}$Pb (previously unmeasured) and $^{206}$Pb, with improved precision compared to earlier work at KVI (Harakeh 1979) and Texas A&M (Youngblood 1999).
