# Thermal conductivity due to phonons in the core of superfluid neutron stars

> We compute the contribution of phonons to the thermal conductivity in the core of superfluid neutron stars. We use effective field theory techniques to extract the phonon scattering rates, written as a function of the equation of state of the system. We also calculate the phonon dispersion law beyond linear order, which depends on the gap of superfluid neutron matter. With all these ingredients, we solve the Boltzmann equation numerically using a variational approach. We find that the thermal conductivity $κ$ is dominated by combined small and large angle binary collisions. As in the color-flavor-locked superfluid, we find that our result can be well approximated by $κ\propto 1/ Δ^6$, where $Δ$ is the neutron gap, the constant of proportionality depending on the density. We further comment on the possible relevance of electron and superfluid phonon collisions in obtaining the total contribution to the thermal conductivity in the core of superfluid neutron stars.

## Key claim

The phonon contribution to the thermal conductivity in superfluid neutron star cores is dominated by combined small and large angle binary collisions, and at low temperatures the result can be well approximated by κ ∝ 1/Δ⁶, where Δ is the neutron gap, with the constant of proportionality depending on density. However, the authors note that a careful study of electron-phonon interactions is still needed to fully determine the total thermal conductivity.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory techniques to extract phonon scattering rates from the equation of state of superfluid neutron matter, employing a causal parametrization of the Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (APR) equation of state for β-stable nuclear matter. They computed the phonon dispersion law beyond linear order, which depends on the neutron gap, and solved the Boltzmann equation numerically using a variational approach with a basis of orthogonal polynomials up to order N=6. The phonon propagators in scattering amplitudes were regulated using the non-linear phonon dispersion law, as the imaginary part of the phonon self-energy vanishes on-shell for the negative γ found in superfluid neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The phonon dispersion law beyond linear order is E_k = c_s k (1 + γ k²) with γ = -v_F²/(45 Δ²), where c_s = v_F/√3
- For nuclear matter saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ with the ¹S₀(A)+³P₂(i) gap model, the critical temperature is T_c = 0.57 Δ(n₀) = 3.4 × 10⁹ K
- A fit to numerical results at n₀ with N=6 gives κ ∼ (7.02 × 10²⁹ + 9.28 × 10¹⁹ T + 9.08 × 10¹⁰ T²) erg cm⁻¹ s⁻¹ K⁻¹, with T in Kelvin
- For the ¹S₀(A)+³P₂(i) model, critical temperatures at different densities are: T_c = 5.8 × 10⁹ K at 0.5 n₀, T_c = 3.4 × 10⁹ K at n₀, and T_c = 6.3 × 10⁹ K at 2 n₀
- For the ¹S₀(c)+³P₂(k) model, critical temperatures are: T_c = 1.6 × 10⁸ K at 0.5 n₀, T_c = 5.8 × 10⁸ K at n₀, and T_c = 5.8 × 10⁸ K at 2 n₀
- The ¹S₀(A) gap model has a maximum neutron gap of about 3 MeV at p_F ≈ 0.85 fm⁻¹, while the ³P₂(i) model has a maximum gap of approximately 1 MeV
- The ¹S₀(c)+³P₂(k) model has maximum gap values of the order of 0.1 MeV for the ³P₂ channel

## Context

This work extends previous computations by the same authors of superfluid phonon contributions to shear viscosity (Manuel, 2011) and bulk viscosity (Manuel, 2013) in neutron stars, with the aim of assessing effects on hydrodynamical modes. The computation is technically analogous to studies of phonon thermal conductivity in color-flavor-locked quark matter (Braby, 2009) and cold Fermi atoms in the unitarity limit (Braby, 2010), but differs in the phonon couplings and dispersion laws due to the different system. The results enable future studies of electron-phonon interactions in neutron star cores, which are needed to determine the total thermal conductivity, and can inform models of neutron star cooling that previously assumed thermal conductivity was dominated by electron and muon collisions (Flowers, Baiko, 2001; Shternin, 2007).
