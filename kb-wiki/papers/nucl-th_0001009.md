# Collisional Quenching of the 2S State of Muonic Hydrogen

> We have calculated differential, total and transport cross sections for muonic hydrogen in the n=2 state scattering from hydrogen. The metastable fraction of the 2S state that slows down below the 2P threshold without undergoing collisional quenching has been calculated as a function of the initial kinetic energy using a Monte Carlo kinetics program. Contrary to earlier estimates, the metastable fraction in the kinetic energy range of 2-5 eV cannot be neglected.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier estimates by Carboni and Fiorentini (1990), the metastable fraction of the 2S state of muonic hydrogen that slows down below the 2P threshold without undergoing collisional quenching cannot be neglected in the kinetic energy range of 2–5 eV, with the effect being significantly larger than previously thought due to backward scattering in elastic collisions.

## Method

The authors performed fully quantum mechanical coupled-channel calculations of differential, total, and transport cross sections for muonic hydrogen in the n=2 state scattering from hydrogen, using exact matrix elements for the muon-proton-proton interaction with electron screening included. These cross sections were then used as input to a Monte Carlo kinetics program that simulated the slowing-down process, tracking the competition between elastic 2S→2S collisions, Stark mixing 2S→2P transitions, and radiative 2P→1S decays.

## Key numbers

- The 2S–2P energy splitting in muonic hydrogen is ΔE = 0.21 eV
- The inelastic threshold T0 for 2S→2P transitions is 0.44 eV for atomic hydrogen target and 0.33 eV for molecular hydrogen target
- The radiative 2P→1S transition rate is λ = 1.2 × 10^11 s^-1
- At initial kinetic energy 1 eV, the surviving metastable fraction is larger than 50%, in agreement with earlier estimates by Carboni and Fiorentini (1990)
- At initial kinetic energy 5 eV, the surviving metastable fraction is about 4%
- At 2 eV initial kinetic energy, the surviving fraction is in the range 15–20% for atomic target mass and 10–16% for molecular target mass

## Context

This work extends the earlier quantum mechanical approach of Carboni and Fiorentini (1990) by solving the four coupled differential equations exactly rather than perturbatively, including full angular coupling, and using exact matrix elements for the muon-proton-proton interaction instead of the dipole approximation. The results enable improved estimates of the metastable 2S fraction for the planned Lamb-shift experiment at PSI, and provide a significantly improved basis for understanding the kinetics of muonic hydrogen in the n=2 state.
