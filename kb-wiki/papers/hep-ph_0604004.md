# New nonperturbative approach to the Debye mass in hot QCD

> The Debye mass m_D is computed nonperturbatively in the deconfined phase of QCD, where chromomagnetic confinement is known to be present. The latter defines m_D to be m_D=c_D\sqrt{σ_s}, where c_D \cong 2.06 and σ_s=σ_s(T) is the spatial string tension. The resulting magnitude of m_D(T) and temperature dependence are in good agreement with lattice calculations. Background perturbation theory expansion for m_D(T) is discussed in comparison to standard perturbative results and recent gauge-invariant definitions.

## Key claim

The Debye mass \(m_D\) in the deconfined phase of QCD is determined nonperturbatively by chromomagnetic confinement, yielding \(m_D = c_D \sqrt{\sigma_s(T)}\) with \(c_D \cong 2.06\), where \(\sigma_s(T)\) is the spatial string tension; the result agrees with lattice data without the need for a multiplicative fitting constant required by perturbative approaches.

## Method

The authors use the Field Correlator Method (FCM) and Background Perturbation Theory (BPTh) to compute the thermal Wilson loop for a static \(Q\bar Q\) pair in the singlet color state. The gluon propagator in the deconfined phase is expressed as a gluelump Green's function, where the spatial string tension \(\sigma_s(T)\) provides confinement in the spatial directions. An effective Hamiltonian in the einbein formalism yields the gluelump mass spectrum, and the lowest eigenvalue gives the Debye mass after including a small correction from the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential with \(\alpha_s^{\rm eff} = 0.15\).

## Key numbers

- \(c_D \cong 2.06\) for \(N_c=3\), obtained as \(m_D = (2.82 - 5.08 \alpha_s^{\rm eff}) \sqrt{\sigma_s}\) with \(\alpha_s^{\rm eff} = 0.15\).
- The spatial string tension is parameterized as \(\sqrt{\sigma_s(T)} = c_\sigma g^2(T) T\) with \(c_\sigma = 0.566 \pm 0.013\) and \(L_\sigma \equiv \Lambda_\sigma / T_c = 0.104 \pm 0.009\) for pure glue QCD.
- For quenched QCD (\(N_f=0\)), the lattice data require a multiplicative coefficient \(A_{N_f=0} = 1.51\) in the perturbative ansatz; for 2-flavor QCD, \(A_{N_f=2} = 1.42\).
- The magnetic Debye mass is \(\sqrt{2}\) times heavier than the electric one, giving \(\varepsilon_1 \approx 4 \sqrt{\sigma_s}\).
- The lightest two-gluon gluelump mass is 1.75 times heavier than the one-gluon gluelump mass.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative Field Correlator Method (FCM) of Simonov and collaborators to finite temperature, providing a gauge-invariant alternative to standard perturbative expansions and effective 3d theories (e.g., by Braaten and Nieto, 1996). The result agrees with lattice calculations of the Debye mass by Karsch et al. (2001, 2002) and by Petreczky et al. (2004) for both quenched and 2-flavor QCD. The approach enables analytic computation of screening masses in hot QCD and can be extended to include higher-order BPTh contributions and magnetic screening.
