# Production of high-orbital kaon excited states in the $K^{-}p$ reaction

> In this work, a systematic investigation of the production of high-orbital-excitation kaons in $K^{-}p$ reactions is carried out within an effective Lagrangian approach. The relevant $t$-channel processes are constructed, and the model is calibrated using a single adjustable parameter determined from existing experimental data. With this parameter, the measured production cross sections for the $K_3^*(1780)$, $K_2(1820)$, $K_2(1770)$ and $K_4^*(2045)$ states are successfully reproduced. Employing the same framework, the production cross sections for other high-orbital kaons are predicted. The results indicate that these states possess sizable cross sections and exhibit characteristically forward-peaked angular distributions, which is a typical feature of $t$-channel exchange, highlighting their great potential for observation in future experiments.

# Production of high-orbital kaon excited states in the $K^{-}p$ reaction

## Key claim
The production cross sections for high-orbital-excitation kaons in $K^{-}p$ reactions are predicted to be sizable and exhibit forward-peaked angular distributions characteristic of $t$-channel exchange, but these predictions rely on a single adjustable parameter calibrated to only four experimentally measured states ($K_3^*(1780)$, $K_2(1820)$, $K_2(1770)$, and $K_4^*(2045)$), and the framework assumes that the dominant production mechanism proceeds via $t$-channel meson exchange while $s$- and $u$-channel contributions are negligible.

## Method
The authors construct effective interaction Lagrangians for the $t$-channel production of high-orbital kaon states in $K^{-}p$ reactions, where the exchanged mesons ($\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$) are selected based on the dominant decay channels of each kaon state. The model is calibrated by determining a single adjustable parameter (the cutoff in the phenomenological form factor $F_t(q)$) from existing experimental cross-section data for the $K_3^*(1780)$, $K_2(1820)$, $K_2(1770)$, and $K_4^*(2045)$ states, and then used to predict production cross sections for other high-orbital kaons including $K^*(1680)$, $K_3^{\prime}(2120)$, $K_3(1F)$, $K_2^*(1980)$, $K_5^*(2380)$, $K_4(2210)$, $K_4^{\prime}(1G)$, and $K_3^*(1G)$.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of kaon spectroscopy from mass spectrum and decay calculations (Pang 2017, Wang 2025) into the underexplored area of production dynamics, motivated by the recent COMPASS (2025) observation of $K_3^{\prime}(2120)$ and $K_4(2210)$ and by planned meson-beam facilities at J-PARC and HIAF. The effective Lagrangian approach with $t$-channel exchange follows the methodology used in prior phenomenological studies of meson production in beam experiments (Li 2026, Wang 2019, Wang 2017), and the predicted cross sections and angular distributions provide guidance for future experimental searches for the as-yet-unobserved $K_3(1F)$, $K_4^{\prime}(1G)$, and $K_3^*(1G)$ states.
