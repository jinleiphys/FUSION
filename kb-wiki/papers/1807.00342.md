# Cluster Radioactivity in Super Heavy Nuclei

> Cluster radioactivity is an exotic nuclear decay observed in actinides where a light nucleus is emitted while the remaining heavy mass residue is the doubly magic $^{208}$Pb or a nucleus in its neighborhood. We have investigated this type of decay in heavier nuclei up to Lv $(Z=116)$ within a microscopic theory. It has been found that super asymmetric fission with $^{208}$Pb as heavy fragment may be dominant decay channel in some super heavy nuclei. This reaction is closely related with cluster radioactivity.

## Key claim

The authors claim that super asymmetric fission with doubly magic \({}^{208}\)Pb as the heavy fragment may be the dominant decay channel in some super heavy nuclei around \({}^{284}\)Cn, but this conclusion is hedged by the condition that the effect only becomes measurable for nuclei with mass number \(A>280\), where calculated half-lives fall into a range accessible to contemporary experiments.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with the Gogny D1S interaction to calculate potential energy surfaces (PES) and collective inertias in the axially symmetric quadrupole (\(Q_{20}\)) and octupole (\(Q_{30}\)) collective coordinates. They then applied the WKB approximation with perturbative cranking inertia to compute cluster radioactivity half-lives for a chain of even-even isotopes from \({}^{224}\)Ra to \({}^{294}\)Lv, selecting nuclei with an \(N/Z\) ratio close to 1.537 to match the heavy fragment \({}^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers

* The CR fission barrier height in \({}^{234}\)U is 26.6 MeV at the saddle point, with scission at \(Q_{20}=45\) b and \(Q_{30}=51\;\mathrm{b}^{3/2}\).
* In \({}^{258}\)No the CR fission barrier height is reduced to 14.2 MeV, with scission shifted to larger quadrupole moments.
* In \({}^{284}\)Cn the CR fission barrier height is only 1.5 MeV at the saddle point, and the scission point lies 12.6 MeV below the ground state energy at \(Q_{20}=128\) b and \(Q_{30}=92\;\mathrm{b}^{3/2}\).
* The first symmetric fission barrier in \({}^{234}\)U is 6.6 MeV high.
* The second symmetric fission barrier in \({}^{284}\)Cn is 5.6 MeV high, compared to the asymmetric barrier of 1.5 MeV.
* Half-lives for CR in the \(Z=96-106\) region are longer than \(10^{30}\) s, but for nuclei with \(A>280\) they fall into a range measurable in contemporary super heavy nuclei experiments.
* The tip distance between fragments at scission varies from 2.5 fm to 4.7 fm, with an average constant value of \(d=3.3\) fm used in the Coulomb energy estimate.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of cluster radioactivity, previously applied to light actinides by Warda (2011) and Robledo (2004), into the super heavy region up to Lv (\(Z=116\)). It contradicts the expectation from phenomenological approaches that CR half-lives would remain extremely long in super heavy nuclei, instead finding a dramatic reduction in barrier height for nuclei above \({}^{274}\)Hs. The results enable experimental searches for super asymmetric fission fragments with heavy mass concentrated around \(A=208\) in super heavy elements, and suggest that the dominant decay mode of isotopes around \({}^{284}\)Cn is super asymmetric fission rather than symmetric fission or alpha decay, revising earlier claims by Warda (2012) about asymmetric fission in this region.
