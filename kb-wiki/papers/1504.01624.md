# Solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski space: the zero-energy limit

> For the first time, the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation for an interacting system, composed by two massive scalars exchanging a massive scalar, is numerically investigated in ladder approximation, directly in Minkowski space, by using an approach based on the Nakanishi integral representation. In this paper, the limiting case of zero-energy states is considered, extending the approach successfully applied to bound states. The numerical values of scattering lengths, are calculated for several values of the Yukawa coupling constant, by using two different integral equations that stem within the Nakanishi framework. Those low-energy observables are compared with (i) the analogous quantities recently obtained in literature, within a totally different framework and (ii) the non relativistic evaluations, for illustrating the relevance of a non perturbative, genuine field theoretical treatment in Minkowski space, even in the low-energy regime. Moreover, dynamical functions, like the Nakanishi weight functions and the distorted part of the zero-energy Light-front wave functions are also presented. Interestingly, a highly non trivial issue related to the abrupt change in the width of the support of the Nakanishi weight function, when the zero-energy limit is approached, is elucidated, ensuring a sound basis to the forthcoming evaluation of phase-shifts.

## Key claim

For the first time, the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation for two massive scalars exchanging a massive scalar is numerically solved in Minkowski space in ladder approximation, but only in the limiting case of zero-energy states; the approach relies on assuming the Nakanishi integral representation as an Ansatz for the scattering amplitude, which is formally justified only within perturbative Feynman-diagram frameworks.

## Method

The authors start from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation in ladder approximation for a two-scalar system exchanging a massive scalar, and project it onto the null-plane by integrating over the minus component of the relative four-momentum, obtaining a three-dimensional Light-front equation. They then apply the Nakanishi perturbation-theory integral representation (PTIR) as an Ansatz for the scattering amplitude, deriving two alternative integral equations for the Nakanishi weight function: one from the projected ladder BSE and one from the uniqueness theorem. The zero-energy limit (κ² → 0⁻) is taken, which forces the support of the Nakanishi weight function in its non-compact variable γ' to shrink from (-∞,∞) to [0,∞), decoupling the positive-γ' and negative-γ' components. The resulting inhomogeneous integral equation for the positive-γ' component is solved numerically to obtain scattering lengths for various Yukawa coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths are calculated for several values of the Yukawa coupling constant α = g²/(m² 16π), where g is the coupling constant and m the scalar mass; specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text.
- The zero-energy limit corresponds to κ² → 0⁻, where κ² = m² - M²/4 and M is the total invariant mass of the two-body system.
- The Nakanishi weight function support transitions from γ' ∈ (-∞,∞) for κ² < 0 to γ' ∈ [0,∞) in the zero-energy limit κ² → 0⁻.
- The scattering length a is defined through the zero-energy limit of the s-wave amplitude: f⁽ᵉˡ⁾₀ ≃ δ₀ ≃ -a kᵣ, where kᵣ = √(s/4 - m²) is the relative three-momentum.

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi PTIR approach, previously successfully applied to bound states of the same two-scalar system (Carbonell et al., 2009-2013; Frederico et al., 2014), to scattering states for the first time. The calculated scattering lengths are compared with results from a completely different framework (Carbonell et al., 2014) and with non-relativistic evaluations, demonstrating the relevance of a non-perturbative field-theoretical treatment even at low energies. The results enable future evaluation of phase shifts and the covariant off-shell T-matrix, which is a key ingredient for describing heavy meson decay amplitudes such as D → Kππ processes (Magalhaes et al., 2011; Guimaraes et al., 2014) and final-state-interaction models for CP violation in three-body B decays (Bedolla et al., 2014).
