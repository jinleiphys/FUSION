# Dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential at short distances

> The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potentials for fusion reactions 40Ca+40Ca, 48Ca+208Pb and 126Sn+130Te are studied with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model together with the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation for the kinetic energies of nuclei. The obtained fusion barrier for 40Ca+40Ca is in good agreement with the extracted fusion barrier from the measured fusion excitation function, and the depth of the fusion pockets are close to the results of time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. The energy dependence of fusion barrier is also investigated. For heavy fusion system, the fusion pocket becomes shallow and almost disappears for symmetric systems and the obtained potential at short distances is higher than the adiabatic potential.

## Key claim

The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential at short distances, calculated with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model and an extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation for kinetic energies, shows a strong dependence on incident energy and system mass; for heavy systems the fusion pocket becomes shallow or disappears and the potential at short distances is higher than the adiabatic potential, in contrast to lighter systems.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model, a semi-classical microscopic dynamics model, to simulate head-on fusion reactions for 40Ca+40Ca, 48Ca+208Pb, and 126Sn+130Te. They calculated the kinetic energy of nuclei using an ETF approximation fitted to the ImQMD results, then extracted the dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential from the energy conservation relation by subtracting the relative motion kinetic energy and other collective energies from the center-of-mass energy. For each reaction, 500 head-on events were evolved for 700 fm/c, and the potential was constructed as a smooth function connecting the separated-nuclei and di-nuclear system regimes.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca+40Ca, the dynamical barrier height falls to 52.0 MeV at an incident energy of 55 MeV, and approaches about 50 MeV at lower energies, close to the lowest barrier B0 extracted from the fusion excitation function.
- The depth of the fusion pocket for 40Ca+40Ca is about 25 MeV.
- For 40Ca+40Ca, the barrier height of the entrance channel potential obtained with the Skyrme energy density functional and frozen density approximation is 56.9 MeV.
- For 48Ca+208Pb, the depth of the fusion pocket is about 7 MeV, much shallower than for 40Ca+40Ca.
- For 126Sn+130Te, the fusion pocket almost disappears.
- The Qgg values (mass differences) are 14.3 MeV for 40Ca+40Ca, 153.8 MeV for 48Ca+208Pb, and 261.2 MeV for 126Sn+130Te.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the ImQMD model for heavy-ion fusion (Wang 2002, 2004, 2005; Zhang 2008) and applies an ETF approximation to improve the description of kinetic energies in finite nuclei. The results for 40Ca+40Ca are compared with time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations (Umar 2006, 2009) and with the Skyrme energy density functional approach (Liu 2006). The finding that heavy systems require extra-push energy beyond the Coulomb barrier to form a compound nucleus supports the concept of an extra-push energy (Shen) and the di-nuclear system (DNS) model (Adamian 1998). The work enables systematic studies of potential energy surfaces for heavy fusion systems as a function of mass asymmetry and distance, with shell effects to be included in future work.
