# Aspects of near threshold neutral pion photoproduction off protons

> We investigate near threshold neutral pion photoproduction off protons to fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory in the light of the new data from MAMI. We show that the unitarity cusp at the secondary π^+ n threshold is in agreement with expectations from the final state theorem. We also analyze the fourth order corrections to the P-wave low-energy theorems and show that potentially large Delta-isobar contributions are cancelled by sizeable pion loop effects. This solidifies the parameter free third order predictions, which are in excellent agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The fourth-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) calculation shows that the potentially large Delta-isobar contributions to the P-wave low-energy theorems are cancelled by sizeable pion loop effects, solidifying the parameter-free third-order predictions which agree well with the new MAMI data; the unitarity cusp at the secondary π⁺n threshold agrees with expectations from the final state theorem, contrary to earlier speculation that its strength might indicate isospin violation.

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop (fourth order) calculation of neutral pion photoproduction off protons in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, evaluating the electric dipole amplitude E₀₊ and the three P-wave multipoles P₁, P₂, P₃. They fitted the resulting expressions to 171 differential cross section data, 19 total cross section points, and 7 photon asymmetry data from MAMI (Schmidt et al., 2001) in the energy range E_γ = 145.1 to 165.6 MeV, using low-energy constants (LECs) for the S-wave (a₁, a₂) and P-wave (b_P, ξ₁, ξ₂) sectors, with resonance saturation estimates from Delta-isobar and vector meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The sum of S-wave LECs a₁ + a₂ = 6.04 GeV⁻⁴ (fit to Schmidt et al. data) and 6.60 GeV⁻⁴ (fit to Fuchs et al. data), agreeing within 10%.
- The electric dipole amplitude at threshold: E₀₊(ω_thr) = -1.13 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ (Set I) and -1.12 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺ (Set II), consistent with the experimental value -(1.33 ± 0.08 ± 0.03) × 10⁻³/M_π⁺.
- The cusp strength parameter b = 3.63 × 10⁻³/M_π⁺, in perfect agreement with the final state theorem prediction b = (3.67 ± 0.14) × 10⁻³/M_π⁺.
- The P-wave LET predictions: P̅₁^LET = 0.469 GeV⁻² (range [0.445, 0.492] GeV⁻²) and P̅₂^LET = -0.498 GeV⁻² (range [-0.472, -0.523] GeV⁻²), compared to experimental values P̅₁^exp = (0.441 ± 0.004 ± 0.013) GeV⁻² and P̅₂^exp = (-0.440 ± 0.005 ± 0.013) GeV⁻².
- For κ* = 4.0 (NΔ transition magnetic moment), the fourth-order predictions give P̅₁ = 0.408 GeV⁻² and P̅₂ = -0.475 GeV⁻²; for κ* = 6.0, P̅₁ = 0.450 GeV⁻² and P̅₂ = -0.521 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the third-order HBCHPT calculations of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1996, 1997) for neutral pion photoproduction, completing the analysis to fourth order (one-loop accuracy). It addresses the long-standing concern, raised e.g. by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) in a delta-inclusive effective field theory, that Delta-isobar contributions would spoil the convergence of P-wave low-energy theorems. The results enable precise tests of chiral dynamics in the threshold region and show that the new MAMI data (Schmidt et al., 2001) resolve a previous experimental discrepancy between the Fuchs et al. (1996) and SAL (Bergstrom et al., 1996, 1997) data sets. The analysis also demonstrates that the unitary cusp is not a sensitive probe of isospin violation, contrary to earlier speculation.
