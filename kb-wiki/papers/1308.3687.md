# A semiclassical collective response of heated, asymmetric and rotating nuclei

> The Landau Fermi-liquid and extended Gutzwiller periodic-orbit theories are presented for the semiclassical description of collective excitations in nuclei, which are close to main topics of the fruitful activity of S.T. Belyaev. Static susceptibilities show the ergodicity of Fermi liquids. Transport coefficients (nuclear friction and inertia) as functions of the temperature for the hydrodynamic and zero-sound modes are derived within the response theory by using the Fermi-liquid droplet model, in agreement with the shell model for large temperatures. The surface symmetry binding-energy constants are obtained as functions of Skyrme force parameters in the approximation of a sharp edged proton-neutron asymmetric nucleus.The energies and sum rules of the isovector dipole giant resonances are in fairly good agreement with the experimental data. An analysis of the specific structure of these resonances in terms of a main, and satellite peaks, in comparison with the experimental data and microscopic theoretical models, might turn out to be of importance for a better understanding of the values of the surface symmetry-energy constant. The semiclassical collective moment of inertia is derived analytically beyond the quantum perturbation approximation of the cranking model for any potential well as a mean field. It is shown that this moment of inertia can be approximated by its rigid-body value for the rotation with a given frequency within the ETF and more general periodic orbit theories in the nearly local long-length approximation. Its semiclassical shell-structure components are derived in terms of the periodic-orbit free-energy shell corrections. We obtained good agreement between the semiclassical and quantum shell-structure components of the moment of inertia for several critical bifurcation deformations for the harmonic oscillator mean field.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified semiclassical framework for collective nuclear excitations that extends the Landau Fermi-liquid theory and periodic-orbit theory to heated, asymmetric, and rotating nuclei, showing that transport coefficients (friction and inertia) and the collective moment of inertia can be derived analytically with temperature-dependent corrections, but the agreement with experimental data for the isovector giant dipole resonance is described as "fairly good" rather than exact, and the analysis of satellite peak structure is presented as a potential avenue for future understanding rather than a definitive result.

## Method

The authors apply the linearized Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation with a relaxation-time collision term to describe small deviations from local equilibrium in a heated Fermi liquid, using a moment expansion to derive conservation equations for particle number, momentum, and energy. They then compute density-density response functions, static susceptibilities, and transport coefficients (viscosity, thermal conductivity, friction, and inertia) within the Fermi-liquid droplet model (FLDM) for both hydrodynamic and zero-sound modes. For rotating nuclei, the collective moment of inertia is derived semiclassically using the extended Gutzwiller periodic-orbit theory (POT) beyond the cranking-model perturbation approximation, with shell-structure components expressed as sums over periodic orbits.

## Key numbers

- Isothermal in-compressibility: \( K^T = 9 \rho \mathcal{G}_0 / \mathcal{N}(T) \), where \( \mathcal{G}_0 = 1 + \mathcal{F}_0 \) and \( \mathcal{N}(T) \) is the density of states at the chemical potential
- Effective mass relation: \( m^* = \mathcal{G}_1 m \) with \( \mathcal{G}_1 = 1 + \mathcal{F}_1/3 \), where \( \mathcal{F}_0 \) and \( \mathcal{F}_1 \) are Landau parameters
- Temperature expansion parameter: \( T/\mu \) is assumed small, with corrections of order \( (T/\mu)^2 \) neglected
- Relaxation time \( \tau \) is taken as independent of quasiparticle momentum but allowed to depend on temperature and frequency

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid droplet model previously developed by Kolomietz, Magner, and collaborators (2000s) for isoscalar quadrupole modes to include neutron-proton asymmetry and isovector giant dipole resonances, building on the heated Fermi-liquid theory of Heiselberg, Pethick, and collaborators (1990s). The semiclassical treatment of the moment of inertia goes beyond the cranking model of Inglis (1956) and the Strutinsky shell-correction method, using the periodic-orbit theory of Gutzwiller (1971) and its extensions by Strutinsky, Magner, and collaborators (1980s) to treat shell-structure components at finite temperature. The results enable analytical calculations of transport coefficients and collective response for heated, asymmetric, and rotating nuclei, with potential applications to understanding the nuclear symmetry energy and superdeformed rotational bands.
