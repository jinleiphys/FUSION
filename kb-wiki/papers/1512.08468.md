# Approaches to QCD phase diagram; effective models, strong-coupling lattice QCD, and compact stars

> The outline of the two lectures given in "Dense Matter School 2015" is presented. After an overview on the relevance of the phase diagram to heavy-ion collisions and compact star phenomena, I show some basic formulae to discuss the QCD phase diagram in the mean field treatment of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Next, I introduce the strong-coupling lattice QCD, which is one of the promising methods to access the QCD phase diagram including the first order phase boundary. In the last part, I discuss the QCD phase diagram in asymmetric matter, which should be formed in compact star phenomena.

# Approaches to QCD phase diagram; effective models, strong-coupling lattice QCD, and compact stars

## Key claim

The QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon density can be explored through complementary approaches (chiral effective models, strong-coupling lattice QCD, and compact star phenomenology), but the location of the critical point and the nature of the phase transition at high density remain uncertain because lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations suffer from the sign problem at finite chemical potential, and effective model predictions are sensitive to details such as vector coupling strength and isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The author presents lecture notes covering three approaches: (1) mean-field treatment of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, where the free energy density is expanded in powers of the constituent quark mass to derive the second-order phase boundary and tricritical point condition; (2) strong-coupling lattice QCD with staggered fermions, where the plaquette term is treated as perturbation and the partition function is evaluated via one-link integrals, bosonization, and mean-field or Monte-Carlo methods (Monomer-Dimer-Polymer and auxiliary field Monte-Carlo); (3) the Polyakov-loop extended Quark Meson (PQM) model with vector mesons applied to isospin-asymmetric matter relevant to neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The critical baryon chemical potential from the second-order phase boundary condition is estimated as N_c * mu_c = pi * N_c * T_c / sqrt(3), which amounts to around 925 MeV for T_c ~ 170 MeV.
- In Au+Au collisions at 25 A GeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.1 GeV), the event-averaged density in a small central box of 1 fm^3 may reach 9 rho_0.
- The tricritical point condition in the NJL model is satisfied around |mu/T| = pi for T_c = 170 MeV with Lambda = 600 MeV.
- In the strong-coupling lattice QCD, the Wilson loop follows the area law: <W(C)> -> N_c * (1/(g^2 * N_c))^(L * N_tau) in the strong coupling limit (g^2 -> infinity).
- The critical point temperature in the PQM model decreases with increasing isospin chemical potential, and the critical point disappears at delta_mu = 70-80 MeV.
- In failed supernovae from gravitational collapse of a 40 M_sun star, temperature can reach T ~ 90 MeV at off-center by shock heat, while the center has T < 40 MeV and mu_B ~ 1300 MeV just before black hole formation.

## Context

This paper extends the long history of QCD phase diagram studies using effective models such as the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) and the Quark Meson model, as well as strong-coupling lattice QCD first explored by Kawamoto and Smit (1981) and Wilson (1974). The strong-coupling results with fluctuation effects from Monomer-Dimer-Polymer simulations (MDP) and auxiliary field Monte-Carlo (AFMC) are compared with mean-field treatments. The discussion of isospin-asymmetric matter connects to compact star phenomenology, including the possibility of crossover transitions in neutron star cores that could support 2 M_sun neutron stars (Masuda, 2013) and the critical point sweep scenario during dynamical black hole formation (Sumi, 2006). The work enables future experimental searches for the critical point in beam energy scan programs at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, as well as improved understanding of neutron star interiors.
