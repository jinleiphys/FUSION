# Solitonic modulation and Lifshitz point in an external magnetic field within Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the inhomogeneous solitonic modulation of a chiral condensate within the effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model when a constant external magnetic field is present. The self-consistent Pauli-Villars regularization scheme is adopted to manipulate the ultraviolet divergence encountered in the thermodynamic quantities. In order to determine the chiral restoration lines efficiently, a new kind of Ginzburg-Landau expansion approach is proposed here. At zero temperature, we find that both the upper and lower boundaries of the solitonic modulation oscillate with the magnetic field in the $μ$--$B$ phase diagram which is actually the de Hass-van Alphan (dHvA) oscillation. It is very interesting to find out how the tricritical Lifshitz point $(T_L,μ_L)$ evolves with the magnetic field: There are also dHvA oscillations in the $T_L$--$B$ and $μ_L$--$B$ curves, though the tricritical temperature $T_L$ increases monotonically with the magnetic field.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with a constant external magnetic field, the solitonic modulation phase boundaries and the tricritical Lifshitz point exhibit de Haas–van Alphen oscillations as a function of magnetic field, although the tricritical temperature increases monotonically with the field.

## Method
The authors study the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in the chiral limit with a constant magnetic field along the z-direction, using the Pauli–Villars regularization scheme to handle ultraviolet divergences. They assume a one-dimensional solitonic modulation of the chiral condensate along the magnetic field direction and derive the thermodynamic potential by generalizing the zero-field case, replacing transverse momenta with Landau levels. A new Ginzburg–Landau expansion in the elliptic modulus (small ν) is developed to efficiently locate the chiral restoration lines and the Lifshitz point.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum constituent quark mass set to m = 330 MeV; Pauli–Villars parameters fixed as Λ = 0.786 GeV and GΛ² = 6.24, yielding a chiral condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = ⟨dd⟩ = −(0.20 GeV)³.
- The size of the solitonic modulation region is smallest around √(eB) = 0.33 GeV, which coincides with the vacuum mass.
- For a strong magnetic field √(eB) = 0.6 GeV, the phase diagram is compared with the B = 0 case; the Lifshitz point shifts to upper left.
- The μ_L–B curve shows a minimum around √(eB) = 0.7 GeV.
- The tricritical temperature T_L increases monotonically with magnetic field B, with a flat oscillation in the region √(eB) ∈ [0.2 GeV, 0.5 GeV].

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, particularly the solitonic modulation phase previously examined by Nickel (2009) and Carignano (2014) in the quark-meson model. It also builds on earlier investigations of the dual chiral density wave in magnetic fields by Frolov (2010) and Tatsumi (2013). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD at finite chemical potential and motivate further studies using Dyson–Schwinger equations or functional renormalization group methods to treat more general inhomogeneous order parameters.
