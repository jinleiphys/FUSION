# Asymmetrical and Non-equilibrium Expansion of Pion Gas Created in HIC

> Results of the computer simulation of dilepton production from the expanding pion gas created in ultrarelativistic Pb+Pb 160 GeV/n collisions are presented. Finite size of the expanding pion gas influences invariant mass spectrum of dileptons. Sensitivity of the shape of dilepton mass spectrum to the initial stage of the pion gas is discussed. Second order asymmetry in azimuthal distribution of dileptons is predicted for non-central collisions. Non-equilibrium nature of the pion gas expansion is found to exhibit itself in rapidity distribution of produced dilepton pairs.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the finite size and non-equilibrium expansion of a pion gas created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions produce observable signatures in dilepton spectra, specifically a minimum in the rapidity distribution and a second-order azimuthal asymmetry, which are sensitive to the initial momentum-space correlations and the collision rate per pion.

## Method

The authors performed a computer simulation of dilepton production from an expanding pion gas using a rescattering model, where pions move in time steps of 0.1 fm and collide based on the total elastic ππ cross section. The initial state of pions for Pb+Pb 160 GeV/n collisions was taken from a cascade generator, and two independent methods were used to simulate π⁺π⁻ annihilation into dileptons: one based on the standard rate expression and another using an enhanced cross section to record annihilation events without altering the pion dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The simulation used a time step of ΔT = 0.1 fm for pion propagation.
- For non-central Pb+Pb 160 GeV/n collisions with impact parameter b = 7 fm, the second-order azimuthal asymmetry coefficient was S₂ = 0.093 ± 0.004.
- The corresponding R_p parameter characterizing the squeeze-out effect was R_p = 1.202.
- The minimum in the rapidity distribution of dileptons was strongest when the number of collisions per pion was close to 1.0, and disappeared for collision rates above 10 coll/π.
- The asymmetry in the azimuthal distribution was strongest for a collision rate of 4.0 coll/π.

## Context

This work extends prior simulations of dilepton production from expanding pion gas (Filip, 1997; APS96, APS97) and is inspired by the rescattering model of Humanic (1993). It addresses the excess of dilepton yield observed by NA50 and CERES experiments, which had been attributed to unconventional mechanisms like modified ρ-meson mass (Shuryak, 1993; Song and Nakano, 1992) or in-medium pion dispersion relations (Fuchs, 1995; Shuryak, 1993). The findings enable experimental searches for non-equilibrium effects in dilepton rapidity and azimuthal distributions, which could provide new probes of the early stage of heavy ion collisions.
