# A Density Matrix Renormalization Group Study of Ultrasmall Superconducting Grains

> We apply the DMRG method to the BCS pairing Hamiltonian which describes ultrasmall superconducting grains. Our version of the DMRG uses the particle (hole) states around the Fermi level as the system block (environment). We observe a smooth logarithmic-like crossover between the few electron regime and the BCS-bulk regime.

## Key claim

The DMRG method, using a particle-hole decomposition around the Fermi level, accurately describes the ground state of the BCS pairing Hamiltonian for ultrasmall superconducting grains, revealing a smooth logarithmic-like crossover between the few-electron regime and the BCS-bulk regime, in contrast to the sharp crossover predicted by the projected BCS (PBCS) ansatz of Braun and von Delft (1998).

## Method

The authors apply a momentum-space DMRG to the BCS pairing Hamiltonian at half filling, using the particle states above the Fermi level as the system block and the hole states below as the environment. They start with a system of 4 levels and iteratively grow the system by adding one particle and one hole level at each step, keeping the m most probable eigenstates of the reduced density matrix. An effective coupling constant is used at each DMRG step to maintain the correct bulk gap for the final system size.

## Key numbers

- For Omega = 24 levels and m = 60 kept states, the DMRG condensation energy agrees with exact Lanczos results from Mastellone, Falci and Fazio (1998) to the first 9 digits; the largest DMRG superblock matrix dimension is 3066 compared to the Lanczos matrix dimension of 2,704,156.
- For Omega up to 400 and m = 60, the condensation energy is computed with a relative error less than 10^-4.
- For Omega = 100 and lambda = 0.4, the condensation energy with the effective coupling constant is -40.50075 d (m = 110), with a truncation error 1-P_m = 1.5 x 10^-12 and a maximum superblock dimension of 9720.
- The crossover is fitted by a logarithmic formula with parameters c1 = 1.48, c2 = 3.05, c3 = -1.98, c4 = 0.08 for even grains (b=0) and c1 = 0.36, c2 = 0.86, c3 = -1.95, c4 = 0.16 for odd grains (b=1), with lambda = 0.224.

## Context

This work extends the DMRG method to the BCS pairing problem in ultrasmall metallic grains, a system previously studied by Ralph, Black and Tinkham (1997) experimentally and by von Delft (1996), Braun and von Delft (1998), and Matveev and Larkin (1997) theoretically. The results contradict the sharp crossover predicted by the PBCS ansatz of Braun and von Delft (1998), showing instead a smooth logarithmic crossover with no critical level spacings. The particle-hole DMRG approach enables accurate treatment of systems up to 400 levels and can be generalized to other fermionic systems in condensed matter, atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics.
