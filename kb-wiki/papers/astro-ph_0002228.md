# Coherent neutrino radiation in supernovae at two loops

> We develop a neutrino transport theory, in terms of the real-time non-equilibrium Green's functions, which is applicable to physical conditions arbitrary far from thermal equilibrium. We compute the coherent neutrino radiation in cores of supernovae by evaluating the two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) polarization function with dressed propagators. The propagator dressing is carried out in the particle-particle channel to all orders in the interaction. We show that at two loops there are two distinct sources of coherence effects in the bremsstrahlung. One is the generically off-shell intermediate state propagation, which leads to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal type suppression of radiation. We extend previous perturbative results, obtained in the leading order in quasiparticle width, by deriving the exact non-perturbative expression. A new contribution due to off-shell finial/initial baryon states is treated in the leading order in the quasiparticle width. The latter contribution corresponds to processes of higher order than second order in the virial expansion in the number of quasiparticles. At 2p-2h level, the time component of the polarization tensor for the vector transitions vanishes identically in the soft neutrino limit. Vector current thereby is conserved. The contraction of the neutral axial vector current with tensor interaction among the baryons leads to a non-vanishing contribution to the bremsstrahlung rate. These rates are evaluated numerically for finite temperature pure neutron matter at and above the nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at two-loop order there are two distinct sources of coherence effects in neutrino bremsstrahlung from supernova cores: the previously known Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) suppression from off-shell intermediate state propagation, and a new contribution from off-shell final/initial baryon states treated in leading order in quasiparticle width. The vector current is conserved at the 2p-2h level in the soft neutrino limit, while the axial-vector current contraction with tensor interactions among baryons yields a non-vanishing bremsstrahlung rate.

## Method

The authors develop a neutrino transport theory using real-time non-equilibrium Green's functions (Kadanoff-Baym formalism) applicable far from thermal equilibrium. They compute the coherent neutrino radiation in supernova cores by evaluating the two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) polarization function with dressed propagators, where the propagator dressing in the particle-particle channel is carried out to all orders in the interaction via finite-temperature Brueckner theory, while the particle-hole channel is truncated at two loops. The strong interaction is modeled using one-pion exchange plus a contact interaction for the particle-hole channel, and the short-range correlations in the particle-particle channel are treated non-perturbatively by summing ladder diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung emissivity is evaluated numerically for finite temperature pure neutron matter at and above nuclear saturation density (no specific numerical emissivity values are given in the truncated text).
- The quasiparticle width γ(p) = -2 Im Σ(p) is defined, with the property γ(-ω) = γ(ω) used in the propagator expansion.
- The propagator expansion in the soft-neutrino limit uses the approximation (ω + ε_p) - ε_{p+q} ≈ ω, valid when vq ≪ ω where v ≪ 1 is the characteristic baryon velocity.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative results on LPM suppression of neutrino radiation in supernovae, which were obtained in leading order in quasiparticle width, by deriving the exact non-perturbative expression. It builds on earlier studies of neutrino-nucleon processes using Fermi-liquid theory (Iwamoto and Pethick), one-boson exchange interaction (Sawyer), relativistic random phase approximation (Horowitz), and variational approaches (Haensel), as well as the authors' own prior one-loop computation using the quasiclassical Kadanoff-Baym transport equation (Sedrakian and Dieperink). The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in the first tens of seconds after supernova core collapse, where temperatures of several tens of MeV make collective LPM effects important.
