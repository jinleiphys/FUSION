# Dynamical initial state model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a fully three-dimensional model providing initial conditions for energy and net-baryon density distributions in heavy ion collisions at arbitrary collision energy. The model includes the dynamical deceleration of participating nucleons or valence quarks, depending on the implementation. The duration of the deceleration continues until the string spanned between colliding participants is assumed to thermalize, which is either after a fixed proper time, or a fluctuating time depending on sampled final rapidities. Energy is deposited in space-time along the string, which in general will span a range of space-time rapidities and proper times. We study various observables obtained directly from the initial state model, including net-baryon rapidity distributions, 2-particle rapidity correlations, as well as the rapidity decorrelation of the transverse geometry. Their dependence on the model implementation and parameter values is investigated. We also present the implementation of the model with 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics, which involves the addition of source terms that deposit energy and net-baryon densities produced by the initial state model at proper times greater than the initial time for the hydrodynamic simulation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully three-dimensional dynamical initial state model for heavy-ion collisions that interweaves initial condition generation with hydrodynamic evolution via source terms, enabling simulations at arbitrary collision energies where the finite nuclear overlap time cannot be neglected.

## Method

The model uses a Monte-Carlo Glauber approach to determine binary nucleon-nucleon collision positions in three spatial dimensions, then forms strings between colliding participants whose endpoints decelerate under a constant string tension until thermalization. Energy and net-baryon density are deposited along the strings in space-time, and after a minimal thermalization time the hydrodynamic evolution is started with source terms that add contributions from strings thermalizing later. The model can use either nucleon or valence quark degrees of freedom, and the thermalization time can be either fixed or fluctuating based on sampled rapidity losses from the LEXUS model.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear overlap time for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV is approximately 2 fm or more
- String tension sigma = 1 GeV/fm used as an effective parameter
- Particle mass m = 1 GeV used for deceleration calculations
- Constant deceleration time Delta tau = 0.5 fm used in one model variant
- Gaussian smearing widths: sigma_perp = 0.5 fm in transverse direction, sigma_etas = 0.2 in longitudinal direction
- At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, strings at large |eta_s| thermalize up to tau approximately 3 fm
- For y_in_lrf approaching infinity, mean rapidity loss <y_loss> approaches 1
- Average string deceleration time <Delta tau> = O(1 fm) for sigma = 1 GeV/fm and m = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior three-dimensional initial condition models by Karpenko (2015) using UrQMD and Pang (2012, 2015) using AMPT, which started hydrodynamics after nuclei completely passed through each other. The dynamical source-term approach enables hydrodynamic simulations to begin before the nuclei have fully overlapped, which is essential for Beam Energy Scan energies at RHIC (5-200 GeV) where overlap times are significant. The model enables future studies of baryon transport, longitudinal fluctuations, and the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon chemical potential, and can be extended with explicit string fragmentation mechanisms as in Andersson (1983) or Werner (1994).
