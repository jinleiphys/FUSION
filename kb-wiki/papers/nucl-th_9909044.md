# Two- and Three-Pion Interferometry for a Nonchaotic Source in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Two- and three-pion correlation functions are investigated for a source that is not fully chaotic. Various models are examined to describe the source. The chaoticity and weight factor are evaluated in each model as measures of the strength of correlations and compared to experimental results. A new measure of three-pion correlation is also suggested.

# Two- and Three-Pion Interferometry for a Nonchaotic Source in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Two- and three-pion correlation functions are investigated for sources that are not fully chaotic, and the conventional weight factor used to measure genuine three-pion correlations fails for sources with more complicated structure than the standard partially coherent model. The authors propose a modified weight factor that works for all models examined, but caution that it is not universally applicable.

## Method

The authors introduce several models of pion-emitting sources: a partially coherent source (mixture of one chaotic and one coherent source), a model of multiple coherent sources obeying a Poisson distribution, and a mixture of multiple coherent sources with one chaotic source. For each model, they compute the two- and three-pion correlation functions, the chaoticity parameter lambda, and the weight factor omega, then compare these quantities to experimental data from the CERN NA44 Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Chaoticity and weight factor measured by NA44: chaoticity = 0.4–0.5, weight factor = 0.20 ± 0.19
- For the mixture of multi-coherent sources and one chaotic source, best fit to NA44 data yields alpha = 0.13 and epsilon = 0.60, corresponding to mean number of coherent sources = 0.13 and about 60% of total pions emitted from the chaotic source
- Ratio of pion number from one coherent source to one chaotic source (h11/F11) is about 5 for the best-fit parameters
- For a chaotic source, both chaoticity and weight factor equal unity
- For a coherent source, chaoticity equals zero
- For the partially coherent source, chaoticity = epsilon(2 − epsilon) and weight factor = sqrt(epsilon) × (3 − 2epsilon)/(2 − epsilon)^(3/2), where epsilon ranges from 0 (coherent) to 1 (chaotic)
- For multi-coherent sources, chaoticity = alpha/(alpha + 1) and weight factor = (1/2) × (2alpha^2 + 2alpha + 3)/(alpha^2 + 3alpha + 1) × sqrt((alpha + 1)/alpha)
- For the mixture model, chaoticity = alpha/(alpha + (1 − epsilon)^2) and weight factor = [2alpha^2 + 2alpha(1 − epsilon)^2 + 3(1 − epsilon)^3(1 − 2epsilon)] / [2(alpha^2 + 3alpha(1 − epsilon)^2 + (1 − epsilon)^3)] × sqrt((alpha + (1 − epsilon)^2)/alpha)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of three-pion correlations for chaotic sources (Nakamura and Seki, 1999), where they found extracting phase information was difficult. It addresses the experimental observation that two-pion correlations at zero relative momentum are less than two, indicating sources are not fully chaotic. The partially coherent source model (Heinz, 1994; Biyajima, 1981) is found to poorly reproduce NA44 data, while the novel multi-coherent source plus chaotic source model yields good agreement. The new weight factor proposed enables more reliable extraction of genuine three-pion correlation strength for nonchaotic sources, though the authors note no universal expression exists.
