# Higgs-confinement phase transitions with fundamental representation matter

> We discuss the conditions under which Higgs and confining regimes in gauge theories with fundamental representation matter fields can be sharply distinguished. It is widely believed that these regimes are smoothly connected unless they are distinguished by the realization of global symmetries. However, we show that when a $U(1)$ global symmetry is spontaneously broken in \emph{both} the confining and Higgs regimes, the two phases can be separated by a phase boundary. The phase transition between the two regimes may be detected by a novel topological vortex order parameter. We first illustrate these ideas by explicit calculations in gauge theories in three spacetime dimensions. Then we show how our analysis generalizes to four dimensions, where it implies that nuclear matter and quark matter are sharply distinct phases of QCD with an approximate $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors show that Higgs and confining regimes in gauge theories with fundamental representation matter can be separated by a phase boundary even when both regimes share the same realization of global symmetries, provided a U(1) global symmetry is spontaneously broken in both regimes. This contradicts the widely held expectation, based on the Fradkin-Shenker-Banks-Rabinovici theorem, that such regimes are always smoothly connected when global symmetries do not distinguish them.

## Method

The authors construct a compact U(1) gauge theory in three Euclidean spacetime dimensions with two oppositely-charged scalar fields (phi_+, phi_-) and one neutral scalar (phi_0), where all scalars carry charges under a global U(1) symmetry. They analyze the phase diagram as a function of the charged and neutral scalar mass parameters, using a novel topological vortex order parameter defined as the phase of the gauge field holonomy around U(1) global vortices. The analysis is then generalized to four-dimensional non-Abelian gauge theories including QCD with an approximate SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The photon mass in the confining regime is given by m_gamma^2 = 4 pi^2 (mu_UV^3 / e^2) e^{-S_I}, where S_I is the minimal monopole-instanton action
- The gauge coupling has mass dimension [e^2] = 1 in three spacetime dimensions
- The cubic coupling has mass dimension [epsilon] = 3/2
- The quartic couplings have mass dimension [lambda_c] = [lambda_0] = 1
- The sextic couplings are dimensionless: [g_c] = [g_0] = 0
- In the Higgs regime, the charged scalar expectation value is v_c = O(|m_c lambda_c^{-1/2}|)
- The pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson mass in the Higgs regime scales as m_pNGB proportional to sqrt(epsilon)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of cold dense QCD matter (Cherman 2018) and provides evidence against the Schafer-Wilczek conjecture of quark-hadron continuity in dense QCD (Schafer 1998). The analysis shows that the Fradkin-Shenker-Banks-Rabinovici theorem (Fradkin 1978, Banks 1979) does not apply when Higgs fields carry global U(1) charge, even when the global symmetry realization is identical in both regimes. The results imply that nuclear matter and quark matter are sharply distinct phases of QCD, enabling new approaches to studying phase transitions in dense matter that are not detectable by standard Landau paradigm order parameters.
