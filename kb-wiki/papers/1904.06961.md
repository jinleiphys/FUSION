# Wiedemann-Franz Law For Hot QCD Matter in a Color String Percolation Scenario

> Transport coefficients serve as important probes in characterizing the QCD matter created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Thermal and electrical conductivities as transport coefficients have got special significance in studying the time evolution of the created matter. We have adopted color string percolation approach for the estimation of thermal conductivity ($κ$), electrical conductivity ($σ_{el}$) and their ratio, which is popularly known as Wiedemann-Franz law in condensed matter physics. The ratio $κ/σ_{el}T$, which is also known as Lorenz number ($\mathbb{L}$) is studied as a function of temperature and is compared with various theoretical calculations. We observe that the thermal conductivity for hot QCD medium is almost temperature independent in the present formalism and matches with the results obtained in ideal equation of state (EOS) for quark-gluon plasma with fixed coupling constant ($α_s$). The obtained Lorenz number is compared with the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for an ideal gas. We observe that a hot QCD medium with color degrees of freedom behaves like a free electron gas.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within the color string percolation model (CSPM), the thermal conductivity of hot QCD matter is almost temperature independent, and the Lorenz number (the ratio of thermal to electrical conductivity times temperature) approaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for an ideal gas, suggesting that a hot QCD medium with color degrees of freedom behaves like a free electron gas. This claim is hedged by the fact that the CSPM results are compared with several other theoretical approaches (ideal equation of state, effective fugacity quasiparticle model) and show qualitative agreement only at higher temperatures, while at lower temperatures the other models show a decreasing trend that the CSPM does not capture.

## Method

The authors adopt the color string percolation model (CSPM), in which color flux tubes (strings) stretched between colliding partons overlap and form clusters; the percolation density parameter xi is extracted from a parameterization of pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. Using the Anderson-Witting model of the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, they derive expressions for electrical conductivity (sigma_el) and thermal conductivity (kappa) in terms of the mean free path lambda_mfp = L/(1 - e^{-xi}), where L is the longitudinal string extension (~1 fm). The thermal conductivity is obtained from kinetic theory via kappa = (pi^2/9) * (sum_k n_k L)/(1 - e^{-xi}), and the Lorenz number L = kappa/(sigma_el T) is computed as a function of T/T_c.

## Key numbers

* The thermal conductivity to squared temperature ratio kappa/T^2 is found to be almost independent of T/T_c in CSPM, in contrast to other models (NJL, ideal EOS, EQPM) which show a decrease up to T ~ 1-2 T_c and then saturation.
* The Lorenz number L = kappa/(sigma_el T) in CSPM is approximately constant with T/T_c and is close to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for an ultra-relativistic gas of gluons and quarks.
* The electrical conductivity expression includes a factor 4/9 from fractional quark charge squared (sum_f q_f^2; q_f = 2/3) and uses e^2 = 4 pi alpha with alpha = 1/137.
* The percolation density parameter xi is derived from a fit to pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with parameters p0 = 1.982 and alpha = 12.877.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CSPM studies of electrical conductivity and shear viscosity (Sahoo 2018) to include thermal conductivity and the Wiedemann-Franz law. It compares CSPM results with the ideal equation of state for QGP with fixed coupling (Mitra 2017), the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) with various QCD equations of state (Mitra 2017), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Marty 2013). The study enables future lattice QCD calculations of thermal conductivity to be compared with CSPM predictions, and it suggests that the Lorenz number near the critical temperature may help distinguish between different models of the QCD medium.
