# $σ$ exchange in the one-boson exchange model involving the ground state octet baryons

> Based on the one-boson-exchange framework that the $σ$ meson serves as an effective parameterization for the correlated scalar-isoscalar $ππ$ interaction, we calculate the coupling constants of the $σ$ to the $\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground state light baryon octet ${\mathbb B}$ by matching the amplitude of ${\mathbb B}\bar{\mathbb B}\toππ\to\bar{\mathbb B}{\mathbb B}$ to that of ${\mathbb B}\bar{\mathbb B}\toσ\to\bar{\mathbb B}{\mathbb B}$. The former is calculated using a dispersion relation, supplemented with chiral perturbation theory results for the ${\mathbb B}{\mathbb B}ππ$ couplings and the Muskhelishvili-Omn\` es representation for the $ππ$ rescattering. Explicitly, the coupling constants are obtained as $g_{NNσ}=8.7_{-1.9}^{+1.7}$, $g_{ΣΣσ}=3.5_{-1.3}^{+1.8}$, $g_{ΞΞσ}=2.5_{-1.4}^{+1.5}$, and $g_{ΛΛσ}=6.8_{-1.7}^{+1.5}$. These coupling constants can be used in the one-boson-exchange model calculations of the interaction of light baryons with other hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives effective coupling constants for the sigma meson to ground-state octet baryons by matching the dispersive amplitude for correlated scalar-isoscalar pion-pair exchange to the one-boson-exchange amplitude, under the condition that the matching is performed at large s values where the pion and sigma masses become negligible.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for baryon-antibaryon scattering through correlated S-wave isoscalar pion-pair exchange using a once-subtracted dispersion relation, with the discontinuity obtained from chiral perturbation theory amplitudes up to next-to-leading order and the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation for pion-pion rescattering. They then match this dispersive amplitude to the one-boson-exchange amplitude with an intermediate sigma meson, extracting the coupling constants by equating the two amplitudes in the physical region at large s. The upper limit of the dispersive integral is set to s0 = (0.8 GeV)^2 to isolate the sigma region and avoid interference from other resonances such as the f0(980).

## Key numbers

* g_NNsigma = 8.7 (+1.7, -1.9) for the nucleon-sigma coupling, obtained by matching dispersive and OBE amplitudes in the SU(3) framework
* g_SigmaSigma_sigma = 3.5 (+1.8, -1.3) for the Sigma-sigma coupling
* g_XiXi_sigma = 2.5 (+1.5, -1.4) for the Xi-sigma coupling
* g_LambdaLambda_sigma = 6.8 (+1.5, -1.7) for the Lambda-sigma coupling
* The upper limit of the dispersive integral is set to s0 = (0.8 GeV)^2, following the approach of Donoghue (2006)
* The sigma meson mass is taken as m_sigma = 0.5 GeV in the OBE amplitude

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Donoghue (2006) for the nucleon-sigma coupling to the full ground-state baryon octet, and provides systematic error analyses that were lacking in previous estimates based on the quenched quark model or SU(3) symmetry assumptions (Liu 2018, Zhao 2013). The derived coupling constants enable more reliable one-boson-exchange model calculations of interactions involving light baryons with other hadrons, particularly for studying hadronic molecules and exotic states in the heavy-flavor region.
