# Quantum non-equilibrium approach for fast electron transport in open systems: photosynthetic reaction centers

> Creation of electron or exciton by external fields in a system with initially statistically independent unrelaxed vibrational modes leads to an initial condition term. The contribution of this term in the time convolution generalized master equation approach is studied in second order of the perturbation theory in the path integral formalism. The developed approach, applied for analysis of dynamics in the photosynthetic reaction center, exhibits the key role of the initial condition terms at the primary stage of electron transfer.

# Quantum non-equilibrium approach for fast electron transport in open systems: photosynthetic reaction centers

## Key claim

The initial condition term (IT) in the time-convolution generalized master equation (GME), which arises when the electron and vibrational subsystems are not initially in equilibrium, can activate electron transfer through channels that are predicted to be closed when the IT is neglected, but only when the electron lifetime in the system is shorter than or comparable to the phonon relaxation time.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized master equation for site occupation probabilities using the projection operator technique in the Born (second-order) approximation, retaining the initial condition term that is usually discarded. They apply this formalism to a three-site model of the photosynthetic reaction center of *Rb. sphaeroides*, where the photoexcited dimer (site 1) can transfer an electron to either the M-side bacteriochlorophyll (site 2) or the L-side bacteriochlorophyll (site 3), with the bath described by one high-frequency and one low-frequency vibrational mode. The initial condition term is calculated in the path integral formalism assuming the vibrational subsystem is initially in thermal equilibrium with the electronic ground state of the dimer, while the electron is initially localized on the excited dimer.

## Key numbers

- Energy differences for the reaction center: $\varepsilon_{1}-\varepsilon_{3} \approx 0.05-0.1$ eV and $\varepsilon_{2}-\varepsilon_{1} \approx 0.1-0.2$ eV
- Coupling parameter for L-branch: $V_{13} = 7$ meV
- High-frequency phonon mode: $\hbar\omega_{h} = 187$ meV; low-frequency phonon mode: $\hbar\omega_{v} = 12.5$ meV
- Huang-Rhys factors for L-branch: $S_{13}^{h} = 0.5$, $S_{13}^{v} = 8$
- Energy offsets for asymmetry: $\varepsilon_{3}-\varepsilon_{1} = -56$ meV, $\varepsilon_{2}-\varepsilon_{1} = 110$ meV
- Sink parameters: $2\Gamma_{1}/\hbar = (250\text{ ps})^{-1}$, $2\Gamma_{2}/\hbar = 2\Gamma_{3}/\hbar = (5\text{ ps})^{-1}$ or $(1\text{ ps})^{-1}$
- Phonon relaxation time: $\tau_{\alpha} \approx 3.5$ ps
- Temperature: 300 K

## Context

This work extends the standard GME approach (Nakajima, 1958; Zwanzig, 1960) by including the initial condition term that is typically neglected when the system and bath are assumed to be in equilibrium at the initial time. It builds on prior studies of electron transfer in photosynthetic reaction centers (Jortner, 1976; Tanaka, 1996; Bixon, 1999) and on the treatment of initial system-bath correlations in donor-acceptor pairs (McCutcheon, 2009). The results enable more accurate modeling of ultrafast electron transfer in biological systems and nanoscale devices where the electron transfer time is comparable to or shorter than the vibrational relaxation time, and suggest that the initial condition term can explain transient asymmetry between the L and M branches in reaction centers.
