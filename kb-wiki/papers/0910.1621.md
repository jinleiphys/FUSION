# Pion reactions with few-nucleon systems

> We report about the recent results for s- and p-wave pion production in NN -> NNpi within effective field theory and discuss how the charge symmetry breaking in pn -> d pi^0 can be used to extract the strong contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference.

## Key claim
The paper argues that, contrary to a previous claim by Nakamura (2008), the same low-energy constant (LEC) describing the short-range \( (\bar NN)^2\pi \) contact operator can simultaneously describe p-wave pion production in multiple \( NN \to NN\pi \) channels and the weak proton-proton fusion process, provided the partial wave analysis of Flammang (2005) is corrected.

## Method
The author applied chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with a modified power counting scheme (from Hanhart, 2007) to calculate s-wave and p-wave pion production amplitudes in \( NN \to NN\pi \) reactions. For s-waves, irreducible loop contributions were identified using the concept of reducibility, including energy-dependent Weinberg-Tomozawa vertices. For p-waves, diagrams up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) were computed, and the LEC \( d \) for the \( (\bar NN)^2\pi \) contact term was varied to simultaneously fit observables across the channels \( pp \to d\pi^+ \), \( pn \to pp\pi^- \), and \( pp \to pn\pi^+ \). For charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in \( pn \to d\pi^0 \), a new leading-order rescattering operator proportional to the neutron-proton mass difference was included.

## Key numbers
- The initial nucleon momentum at threshold kinematics is \( p \simeq \sqrt{m_N M_\pi} \simeq 360 \) MeV.
- The s-wave amplitude for \( pp \to d\pi^+ \) from NLO loops is \( A_{pp\to d\pi^+}^{b+c+d} = \frac{g_A^3 |\vec q|}{256 f_\pi^5} \), where \( |\vec q| \) is the final NN relative momentum.
- The theoretical uncertainty for the s-wave cross section is about \( 2 M_\pi / m_N \approx 30\% \).
- The forward-backward asymmetry in \( pn \to d\pi^0 \) at leading order is \( A_{\rm fb}^{\rm LO} = (11.5 \pm 3.5) \times 10^{-4} \times (\delta m_N^{\rm str} / {\rm MeV}) \).
- The extracted strong contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is \( \delta m_N^{\rm str} = (1.5 \pm 0.8\ {\rm (exp.)} \pm 0.5\ {\rm (th.)}) \) MeV.
- The best-fit value for the LEC \( d \) (in units of \( 1/(f_\pi^2 M_N) \)) is \( d = 3 \), preferred by data for analyzing power in \( pp \to d\pi^+ \) and \( pn \to pp\pi^- \).

## Context
This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations for pion production in few-nucleon systems, building on the modified power counting scheme of Hanhart (2007) and the reducibility concept of Lensky (2008). It contradicts the claim by Nakamura (2008) that the \( (\bar NN)^2\pi \) contact term cannot simultaneously describe \( pp \to pn\pi^+ \) and weak proton-proton fusion, attributing the discrepancy to a flawed partial wave analysis by Flammang (2005). The results enable a determination of the strong neutron-proton mass difference from charge symmetry breaking in \( pn \to d\pi^0 \), consistent with the Cottingham sum rule (Gasser, 1982) and lattice QCD calculations (2009). Future measurements at COSY (ANKE collaboration) are expected to allow a quantitative extraction of the LEC \( d \).
