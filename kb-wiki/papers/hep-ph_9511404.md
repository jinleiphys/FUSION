# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Jets and Heavy Ion Reactions

> Bose-Einstein correlations and invariant momentum distributions are analyzed for longitudinally expanding finite systems, like jets in elementary particle collisions or systems created in high energy heavy ion reactions. Cross-term generating hyperbolic mixing angle is introduced for HBT. Temporal or spatial temperature gradients are shown to contribute to thermal length and time scales for HBT. Results are illustrated in three figures.

## Key claim

Bose-Einstein correlation functions for longitudinally expanding finite systems (jets or heavy-ion reactions) do not directly measure the full geometrical sizes of the source, because expansion creates strong correlations between space-time and momentum-space variables; the measured radius parameters instead reflect "lengths of homogeneity" that combine both geometrical and thermal scales.

## Method

The authors model the emission function for a boost-invariant longitudinally expanding system using a Wigner-function formalism, factorizing the source into proper-time, space-time rapidity, and transverse coordinate distributions. They introduce a hyperbolic mixing angle η_s that generates the out-longitudinal cross-term in the Gaussian parameterization of the Bose-Einstein correlation function, and derive analytic expressions for the HBT radius parameters in terms of geometrical sizes (R_G, Δη, Δτ) and thermal length scales (R_T, Δη_T, Δτ_T) that depend on the transverse mass M_t and temperature gradients.

## Key numbers

- For prompt pions from decaying Lund jets (JETSET6.3 default parameters a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV⁻², resonance decays off): Δη_* ≈ 0.5, yielding R_L ≈ τ_0 Δη_* ≈ 0.6 fm and Δτ_* ≈ 0.5 fm/c.
- When resonance decays are switched on, the width of the G_*(η−y) distribution for all pions increases to Δη_* ≈ 1 unit.
- For the Lund jet model (JETSET7.4 default parameters a=0.3, b=0.58 GeV⁻², κ≈1 GeV/fm): τ_s = ⟨τ⟩ = (1/√(bκ²)) Γ(3/2+a)/Γ(1+a) and Δτ_*² = (1/(bκ²))[1+a − Γ²(3/2+a)/Γ²(1+a)].
- The thermal length scales are given by R_T² = (τ_0²/(a²+b²))(T_0/M_t), Δη_T² = T_0/M_t, and Δτ_T² = (τ_0²/d²)(T_0/M_t), where M_t is the transverse mass of the pair.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bose-Einstein correlations for expanding finite systems previously developed by Csörgő (1994) and Csörgő & Lörstad (1995) to include both jets and heavy-ion reactions. It introduces the concept of a hyperbolic mixing angle η_s that generates the out-longitudinal cross-term, building on the work of Wiedemann, Heinz, and collaborators (1995) who first identified this cross-term. The results enable combined analysis of invariant momentum distributions and Bose-Einstein correlation functions to determine jet sizes, and show that temperature gradients in the transverse and temporal directions create transverse-mass-dependent thermal length scales that can cause the effective lifetime parameter to vanish at high transverse mass, potentially leading to M_t-scaling of the HBT radii.
