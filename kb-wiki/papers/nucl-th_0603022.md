# Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonances in Spherical Nuclei - Macroscopic Description

> The properties of the Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance (ISGDR) and its electromagnetic structure are investigated within a semiclassical nuclear Fermi-Fluid dynamical approach. Microscopical calculations pointing to a ISGDR distribution splitted into two main broad structures is confirmed within the presented macroscopic approach by the occurence of a ''low-lying'' and a ''high-lying'' state, that are nothing else than the first two overtones of the same resonance. Macroscopically they are pictured as a combination of compressional and vortical nuclear flows. In the second part of the paper the electromagnetic structure of the ISGDR, relevant for reactions with inelastically scattered electrons, and the relation between the vorticity and the toroidal dipole moment is analyzed. The relative strengths of the compresional and vortical collective currents is evaluated by means of electron-scattering sum-rules.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance (ISGDR) distribution splits into two main broad structures (a "low-lying" and a "high-lying" state) within a semiclassical macroscopic Fermi-fluid dynamical approach, but qualifies that these are the first two overtones of the same resonance rather than distinct modes, and that the high-lying state (around 3ℏω) is not purely compressional but contains significant vortical admixture.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Boltzmann equation for a spherical nucleus with a sharp-edge density distribution, obtaining the Lame equation for the displacement field. The displacement field is decomposed into longitudinal (compressional) and transverse (vortical) components using vector analysis, and the Helmholtz equation is solved for both compressibility and vorticity with free-surface boundary conditions. The resulting eigenfrequencies and the ratio of transverse-to-longitudinal weights are computed for the first four overtones in 208Pb, and the electromagnetic structure is analyzed using multipole parametrization of charges and currents to evaluate Coulomb and transverse electric form factors for electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- First overtone energy: 11.56 MeV (1.67 ℏω) for 208Pb, with vorticity/compressibility ratio r_n = 1.94
- Second overtone energy: 22.19 MeV (3.21 ℏω) for 208Pb, with vorticity/compressibility ratio r_n = -1.19
- Third overtone energy: 28.53 MeV (4.12 ℏω) for 208Pb, with vorticity/compressibility ratio r_n = 6.09
- Fourth overtone energy: 40.03 MeV (5.78 ℏω) for 208Pb, with vorticity/compressibility ratio r_n = -4.03
- Longitudinal wave number for first overtone: k_L = 2.05 fm^-1 (k_L R_0 = 3.05)
- Longitudinal wave number for second overtone: k_L = 3.93 fm^-1 (k_L R_0 = 5.86)
- The low-energy experimental peak for 116Sn, 144Sm and 208Pb falls in the interval (1.71-1.92)ℏω, while the high-energy peak lies between 3ℏω and 3.2ℏω (from cited experimental work by Clark et al. and Young et al.)

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic nuclear-fluid dynamical approach of Semenko (1981), Balbutsev and Mikhailov (1988), and Bastrukov, Misicu, and collaborators (1993) by including the full momentum content in the radial part of the collective field and using distinct compressional and shear elastic constants. It confirms the bimodal ISGDR structure found in microscopic RPA calculations by Dumitrescu and Hamamoto (1983), Colo et al. (2000), and the relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA framework of Vretenar et al. (2000). The paper enables future electron scattering experiments to disentangle compressional and vortical contributions to the ISGDR by comparing Coulomb and transverse electric form factors.
