# Relativistic quantum transport theory of hadronic matter: the coupled nucleon, delta and pion system

> We derive the relativistic quantum transport equation for the pion distribution function based on an effective Lagrangian of the QHD-II model. The closed time-path Green's function technique, the semi-classical, quasi-particle and Born approximation are employed in the derivation. Both the mean field and collision term are derived from the same Lagrangian and presented analytically. The dynamical equation for the pions is consistent with that for the nucleons and deltas which we developed before. Thus, we obtain a relativistic transport model which describes the hadronic matter with $N$, $Δ$ and $π$ degrees of freedom simultaneously. Within this approach, we investigate the medium effects on the pion dispersion relation as well as the pion absorption and pion production channels in cold nuclear matter. In contrast to the results of the non-relativistic model, the pion dispersion relation becomes harder at low momenta and softer at high momenta as compared to the free one, which is mainly caused by the relativistic kinetics. The theoretically predicted free $πN \to Δ$ cross section is in agreement with the experimental data. Medium effects on the $πN \to Δ$ cross section and momentum-dependent $Δ$-decay width are shown to be substantial.

## Key claim

The authors derive a relativistic quantum transport equation for pions from the QHD-II effective Lagrangian, obtaining a unified framework for nucleons, deltas, and pions; however, the treatment is not fully self-consistent because pions are treated explicitly while other mesons (sigma, omega, rho) remain virtual, and the derivation employs the Born approximation rather than a full G-matrix approach.

## Method

The closed time-path Green's function technique was used to derive a Dyson equation for the pion propagator, from which a Kadanoff-Baym equation was obtained. The semi-classical, quasi-particle, and Born approximations were applied to extract a relativistic Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport equation for the pion distribution function, with both mean field and collision terms derived analytically from the same effective Lagrangian. The pion self-energy was computed including Hartree-Fock terms (nucleon-hole, delta-hole, and delta-nucleon loops) and Born terms for elastic scattering channels (πN → πN, πΔ → πΔ, ππ → ππ), with the imaginary part of the loop diagrams restricted to the Δ-formation (Nπ → Δ) and Δ-decay (Δ → Nπ) processes.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave πN scattering length from the pseudovector coupling is −0.010, compared to the empirical value of −0.015 ± 0.015 (Pil73).
- Differences between cross sections calculated in G-matrix theory and in Born approximation for σ*_{NN→NN} and σ*_{NN→NΔ} are on the order of 10–20%.
- More than 30% of nucleons are excited to resonance states, especially Δ-resonance, in Au+Au collisions at 2 GeV/nucleon incident energy (Met93).
- The contribution of the sigma-pion coupling term to πN S-wave scattering lengths is of order m_π²/(M_N m_σ) and can be neglected.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior relativistic transport models for nucleons (ZPA94, PRC94, PRC97) and deltas (PRC96, PLB96) to include explicit pion degrees of freedom, building on the RVUU framework applied to heavy-ion collisions by Elze et al. (1987) and others. It contrasts with non-relativistic Δ-hole model treatments of the pion optical potential (Eri88, Ehe93, Xio93, Fuc97) and with the work of Wang et al. (1991) who treated pions as free particles without explicit collision terms. The derived framework enables self-consistent studies of in-medium pion dispersion relations, absorption, and production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and is a prerequisite for quantitative dilepton production calculations at SIS and SPS energies.
