# The Dirac and Gauge Yang-Mills Fields in Self-Consistent Consideration

> The quasi-classical model in a gauge theory with the Yang-Mills (YM) field is developed. On a basis of the exact solution of the Dirac equation in the SU(N) gauge field, which is in the eikonal approximation, the Yang-Mills (YM) equations containing the external fermion current are solved. The derived solutions are quantized in the quasi-classical approach. The developed model proves to have the self-consistent solutions of the Dirac and Yang-Mills equations at $N\geq 3$. Thereat the solutions take place provided that the fermion and gauge fields exist simultaneously, so that the fermion current completely compensates the current generated by the gauge field due to it self-interaction. The obtained solution are considered in the context of QCD.

# The Dirac and Gauge Yang-Mills Fields in Self-Consistent Consideration

## Key claim

Self-consistent solutions of the Dirac and Yang-Mills equations exist only for gauge group SU(N) with N ≥ 3, and only when the fermion current completely compensates the current generated by the gauge field due to its self-interaction, meaning the Yang-Mills field cannot exist without fermions in this quasi-classical eikonal model.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation exactly in an external SU(N) gauge field under the eikonal approximation, where the gauge field depends on a scalar function φ(x) satisfying (∂_μ φ)(∂^μ φ) = 0. The Yang-Mills equations containing the external fermion current are then solved using these fermion solutions, with the gauge field expanded as a circularly polarized wave on the wave surface. The solutions are quantized in the quasi-classical approximation, and the resulting system is analyzed for equilibrium fermion matter at temperature T.

## Key numbers

- Self-consistent solutions exist only for gauge group dimension N ≥ 3; no solution exists for SU(2) because the convolution C in Eq.(31) is always positive for N=2.
- The effective fermion mass is renormalized as m_*^2 = m^2 + g^2 (N^2 - 1) A^2 / (2N), where A is the gauge field amplitude.
- For Boltzmann-distributed fermions at high temperature (T ≫ m), the dimensionless parameter z = m_*/T satisfies z = (n_0 / (2|C| T^3))^{1/2} when z ≪ 1, and z = (n_0 / (|C| T^3))^{1/3} when z ≫ 1, where n_0 is the fermion density.
- The gauge field amplitude A = [2N / (g(N^2 - 1))] (n_0 / (2|C| T^3))^{1/2} T when (n_0 / T^3) ≪ 1 and m/T ≪ (n_0 / |C| T^3) ≪ 1.

## Context

This work extends the long history of solving Yang-Mills equations that began with Yang and Mills (1954), including plane wave solutions for source-less YM equations and solutions with external sources in (1+3) Minkowski space-time. It specifically addresses the need for simultaneous solution of the Dirac and Yang-Mills equations for strongly interacting matter generated in high-energy heavy ion collisions, as discussed in prior work on quark-gluon plasma. The derived self-consistent solutions enable calculation of renormalized vertex functions and effective fermion masses beyond perturbation theory, and are applied to the hadronization of equilibrium quark-gluon plasma into lightest hadrons via first-order phase transition.
