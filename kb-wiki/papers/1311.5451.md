# Charge-dependent azimuthal correlations from AuAu to UU collisions

> We study the charge-dependent azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions, as motivated by the search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and the investigation of related background contributions. In particular we aim to understand how these correlations induced by various proposed effects evolve from collisions with AuAu system to that with UU system. To do that, we quantify the generation of magnetic field in UU collisions at RHIC energy and its azimuthal correlation to the matter geometry using event-by-event simulations. Taking the experimental data for charge-dependent azimuthal correlations from AuAu collisions and extrapolating to UU with reasonable assumptions, we examine the resulting correlations to be expected in UU collisions and compare them with recent STAR measurements. Based on such analysis we discuss the viability for explaining the data with a combination of the CME-like and flow-induced contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the charge-dependent azimuthal correlations measured in both AuAu and UU collisions can be consistently understood as a combination of flow-driven background contributions (transverse momentum conservation and local charge conservation) and a Chiral Magnetic Effect-like signal, but the decomposition depends on an uncertain factor kappa that relates the flow-driven contributions to the reaction-plane dependent and independent correlations.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations of electromagnetic fields in UU collisions at sqrt(s)=193 GeV using the Lienard-Wiechert potentials for protons sampled from a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution, with random orientations of the uranium nuclei's symmetry axes. They then used STAR data for charge-dependent azimuthal correlations gamma and delta from AuAu collisions, combined with their computed magnetic field properties and participant plane correlations, to decompose the correlations into flow-driven (F) and CME-like (H) components via a two-component model with an adjustable factor kappa. Finally, they extrapolated these components to UU collisions using the computed UU magnetic field strengths and eccentricities.

## Key numbers

- The event-averaged magnetic field component eB_y in UU collisions at t=0 is slightly smaller than in AuAu collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, shown as a function of impact parameter b
- The strongest azimuthal correlation between the B-field orientation and the second harmonic participant plane, <cos[2(psi_B - psi_2)]>, reaches approximately 0.7 at impact parameter b around 10 fm or charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta around 200
- The "projected field strength" <(eB)^2 cos[2(psi_B - psi_2)]> in UU collisions is generally weaker than in AuAu collisions for most centralities
- The proton charge radius used in simulations was R_p = 0.84184(67) fm, with variation from 0.6 fm to 0.9 fm causing about 15% variation in computed field strength
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for U-238 were R = 6.81 fm, a = 0.55 fm, beta_2 = 0.28, and beta_4 = 0.093
- The multiplicity model used n_pp = 2.42 for pp collisions at sqrt(s)=193 GeV and x = 0.13 for the binary collision fraction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of magnetic fields and their correlation with participant planes in AuAu collisions (Bloczynski 2012) to the UU system, motivated by proposals to use UU collisions to separate CME signals from flow-driven backgrounds (Voloshin 2010). The analysis builds on the understanding that transverse momentum conservation (Pratt 2010, Bzdak 2010) and local charge conservation (Schlichting 2010) contribute to charge-dependent correlations in a way that scales with elliptic flow. The results enable quantitative comparison with recent STAR preliminary data for UU collisions and help assess whether a combination of CME-like and flow-induced contributions can consistently explain measurements across both collision systems.
