# Applying Twisted Boundary Conditions for Few-body Nuclear Systems

> We describe and implement twisted boundary conditions for the deuteron and triton systems within finite-volumes using the nuclear lattice EFT formalism. We investigate the finite-volume dependence of these systems with different twists angles. We demonstrate how various finite-volume information can be used to improve calculations of binding energies in such a framework. Our results suggests that with appropriate twisting of boundaries, infinite-volume binding energies can be reliably extracted from calculations using modest volume sizes with cubic length $L\approx8-14$ fm. Of particular importance is our derivation and numerical verification of three-body analogue of `i-periodic' twist angles that eliminate the leading order finite-volume effects to the three-body binding energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that twisted boundary conditions can be used to reliably extract infinite-volume binding energies of few-body nuclear systems from finite-volume calculations with modest box sizes of L ≈ 8–14 fm, and it provides the first derivation and numerical verification of a three-body analogue of "i-periodic" twist angles that exactly cancel the leading-order finite-volume correction to the three-body binding energy.

## Method

The authors implemented twisted boundary conditions within the nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) formalism for the deuteron and triton systems, using a discretized space-time lattice with a cubic volume. They performed direct diagonalization of the transfer matrix (avoiding Monte Carlo statistical uncertainties) for various twist angles while constraining the sum of twist angles to zero to eliminate center-of-mass motion, and extracted ground-state binding energies as a function of volume size. The leading-order chiral contact interactions were fitted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and the ³S₁ scattering length, with parameters given in Table 1 of the paper.

## Key numbers

- The lattice spacing parameters used were 1/a_L = 100 MeV and 1/a_T = 150 MeV, with a box length range of L ≈ 8–14 fm suggested for reliable infinite-volume extrapolations.
- The contact interaction coefficients were c_S = -4.2000 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻² and c_T = -6.0513 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻², fitted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and ³S₁ scattering length.
- The numerical error from the Lanczos diagonalization was estimated as δE_ε ≤ 0.002 MeV, based on eigenvalue precision of ε ≤ 10⁻⁵.
- The pion mass was set to m_π = 134.98 MeV, the nucleon mass to m_N = 938.92 MeV, the pion decay constant to f_π = 92.2 MeV, and the axial coupling to g_A = 1.29.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of twisted boundary conditions for finite-volume corrections from two-body systems, as studied by Briceno et al. (2013), to three-body nuclear systems using the NLEFT formalism. Prior work by Meissner et al. (2014) derived the finite-volume dependence for three identical bosons at the unitary limit with periodic boundary conditions, but no general analytic formula exists for higher A-body systems. The results enable more efficient extraction of infinite-volume binding energies from few-body lattice calculations by using specific twist angles to cancel leading-order finite-volume effects, reducing the need for calculations at multiple large volumes.
