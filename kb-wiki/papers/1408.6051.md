# Production of multi-strangeness hypernuclei and the YN-interaction

> We investigate for the first time the influence of hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction models on the strangeness dynamics of antiproton- and $Ξ$-nucleus interactions. Of particular interest is the formation of bound multi-strangeness hypermatter in reactions relevant for \panda. The main features of two well-established microscopic approaches for YN-scattering are first discussed and their results are then analysed such that they can be applied in transport-theoretical simulations. The transport calculations for reactions induced by antiproton beams on a primary target including also the secondary cascade beams on a secondary target show a strong sensitivity on the underlying YN-interaction. In particular, we predict the formation of $Ξ$-hypernuclei with an observable sensitivity on the underlying $Ξ$N-interaction. We conclude the importance of our studies for the forthcoming research plans at FAIR.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that the production of bound multi-strangeness hypernuclei, particularly $\Xi$-hypernuclei, in reactions relevant for the PANDA experiment at FAIR shows a strong sensitivity on the underlying hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction model in the S=-2 sector, with the caveat that the effect is most pronounced in low-energy $\Xi$-induced reactions and depends on whether the $\Xi$N-interaction is attractive or repulsive.

## Method

The authors implemented parametrizations of elastic and inelastic $\Xi$N scattering cross sections from two microscopic models, the Nijmegen extended soft-core ESC04 model (Rijken et al.) and the Fujiwara-Suzuki quark-cluster FSS model (Fujiwara et al.), into the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model GiBUU. They simulated antiproton-induced reactions on a copper target at beam energies of 2, 3, and 4 GeV, followed by secondary $\Xi$-induced reactions on a second copper target at low beam energies (0.15–0.3 GeV). Hypernuclei formation was identified by applying the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to the freeze-out configuration and then performing momentum coalescence between SMM fragments and hyperons.

## Key numbers

- Total net $\Lambda$ relative yield (normalized to number of GiBUU events) for $\Xi$-induced reactions on Cu at 0.15 GeV beam energy: 0.242 (ESC model) and 0.243 (FSS model)
- Average velocity of bound $\Xi$ particles at capture: $\bar{\beta}_\Xi = \langle v/c \rangle \sim 0.11$, compared to average nucleon velocity at Fermi surface $\bar{\beta}_N \sim 0.25$
- $\Xi$N elastic cross sections show strong energy dependence in ESC model, with inelastic $\Xi N \to \Lambda\Lambda$ channel dominating at low cascade energies; FSS model predicts smoother energy behavior with elastic channels comparable to inelastic ones
- $\Xi$N inelastic cross section for $\Xi N \to \Lambda\Sigma$ in ESC model is in the order of a few mb only
- $\Xi$-beam energies studied: 0.15 GeV (corresponding to $P_{lab}=0.646$ GeV/c) and 0.3 GeV (corresponding to $P_{lab}=0.937$ GeV/c)
- In-medium single-particle $\Lambda$ potential at saturation density: between -38 MeV (ESC) and -45 MeV (FSS)
- $\bar{p}p \to \bar{\Xi}\Xi$ annihilation cross section: in the range of a few microbarns; $\bar{p}p \to \bar{\Lambda}\Lambda$ annihilation cross section: in the range of a few hundred microbarns

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on hypernuclei production in heavy-ion, proton, and antiproton reactions (Gaitanos 2013, Gaitanos 2009) by incorporating for the first time explicit comparisons of two different microscopic YN interaction models in the S=-2 sector. The study is motivated by the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR, which aims to produce multi-strangeness hypernuclei through a two-step process of antiproton-induced primary reactions followed by $\Xi$ capture on secondary targets (PANDA proposals). The results show that the Nijmegen ESC model (Rijken 2004) predicts a repulsive $\Xi$N interaction with strong inelastic absorption, leading to very low bound $\Xi$ production, while the FSS quark-cluster model (Fujiwara 2004) predicts an attractive $\Xi$N interaction with smaller inelastic cross sections, enabling copious $\Xi$-hypernuclei formation. This enables future experiments at FAIR to constrain the still poorly known $\Xi$N interaction by measuring multi-strangeness hypernuclei yields.
