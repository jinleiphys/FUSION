# Extraction of the Parton Momentum-Fraction Dependence of Generalized Parton Distributions from Exclusive Photoproduction

> The $x$ dependence of hadrons' generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $\mathcal{F}(x,ξ,t)$ is the most difficult to extract from the existing known processes, while the $ξ$ and $t$ dependence are uniquely determined by the kinematics of the scattered hadron. We study the single diffractive hard exclusive processes for extracting GPDs in the photoproduction. We demonstrate quantitatively the enhanced sensitivity on extracting the $x$ dependence of various GPDs from the photoproduction cross sections, as well as the asymmetries constructed from photon polarization and hadron spin that could be measured at JLab Hall D by GlueX Collaboration and future facilities.

# Extraction of the Parton Momentum-Fraction Dependence of Generalized Parton Distributions from Exclusive Photoproduction

## Key claim

The single diffractive hard exclusive process (SDHEP) of photoproduction, specifically N(p) + γ(p₂) → N'(p') + π(q₁) + γ(q₂), provides enhanced sensitivity to the x-dependence of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) because the hard scattering coefficients contain terms where the external observable (the pion polar angle θ) is entangled with the parton momentum fraction x, unlike deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) where the x-dependence decouples from the measured hard scale. This enhanced sensitivity is demonstrated quantitatively for the ERBL region (|x| ≤ |ξ|) of GPDs, complementing the DGLAP-region sensitivity of diphoton production in pion-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-order (LO) short-distance hard parts for the exclusive photoproduction process N γ → N' π γ, factorizing the scattering amplitude into GPDs for the hadron transition N→N', a distribution amplitude (DA) for the final-state pion, and perturbatively calculable helicity amplitudes. They construct four observables, the unpolarized differential cross section and three polarization asymmetries (ALL, AUT, ALT), from convolutions of GPDs H and \widetilde{H} with hard coefficients, and evaluate these observables using the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model as a reference GPD, adding various shadow GPDs constructed to give zero contribution to the forward limit and the moment-type integral ∫ dx F(x,ξ,t)/(x-ξ).

## Key numbers

- Unpolarized differential cross section formula: dσ/(d|t| dξ dcosθ) = π (α_e α_s)² (C_F/N_c)² (1-ξ²)/(ξ² s³) Σ_UU
- Kinematic parameters for numerical evaluation: t = -0.2 GeV², ξ = 0.2, E_γ = 9 GeV
- Renormalization and factorization scales fixed at 2 GeV
- The special x-pole in the entangled hard coefficients: x_p(ξ, z, θ) = ξ · [cos²(θ/2)(1-z) - z] / [cos²(θ/2)(1-z) + z], which scans from ξ to -ξ as z goes from 0 to 1, covering the entire ERBL region
- The condition for factorization validity: |q_{1T}| = |q_{2T}| ≡ q_T ≫ √|t|, ensuring suppression of quantum interference between hard scattering and GPD

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of single diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEP) developed by Qiu and Yu (2022) for extracting GPDs, and addresses the well-known limitation of DVCS (Ji, 1996; Radyushkin, 1997) and deeply virtual meson production (Brodsky, 1994; Frankfurt, 1995) which only provide moment-type sensitivity ∫ dx F(x,ξ,t)/(x-ξ) and cannot distinguish shadow GPDs (Bertone, 2021). The process is accessible at JLab Hall D by the GlueX Collaboration and future facilities, and the results enable global analyses combining multiple processes to extract the full x, ξ, and t dependence of GPDs, which is necessary for tomographic imaging of hadrons and understanding emergent hadronic properties such as mass, spin, and internal pressure.
