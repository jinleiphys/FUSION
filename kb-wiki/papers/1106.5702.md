# Second-order quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter with chiral two-nucleon interactions

> We employ Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids to study the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter in the vicinity of saturation density. Realistic low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions evolved from the Idaho N3LO chiral two-body potential are used as input potentials. We derive for the first time exact results for the central part of the quasiparticle interaction computed to second order in perturbation theory, from which we extract the L=0 and L=1 Landau parameters as well as some relevant bulk equilibrium properties of nuclear matter. The accuracy of the intricate numerical calculations is tested with analytical results derived for scalar-isoscalar boson exchange and (modified) pion exchange at second order. The explicit dependence of the Fermi liquid parameters on the low-momentum cutoff scale is studied, which provides important insight into the scale variation of phase-shift equivalent two-body potentials. This leads naturally to explore the role that three-nucleon forces must play in the effective interaction between two quasiparticles.

## Key claim

The authors derive for the first time exact results for the central part of the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter computed to second order in perturbation theory using realistic low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions, but they explicitly note that this treatment does not include three-nucleon forces and therefore seeks to identify deficiencies that remain when only two-nucleon forces are included.

## Method

The quasiparticle interaction is obtained by functionally differentiating the ground-state energy density twice with respect to the quasiparticle distribution function within Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids. The authors compute the first-order and second-order contributions to the quasiparticle interaction using partial-wave matrix elements of low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions evolved from the Idaho N3LO chiral two-body potential. The numerical accuracy of the second-order calculation is tested against exact analytical results derived for scalar-isoscalar boson exchange and modified pion exchange interactions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, corresponding to k_F = 1.33 fm⁻¹
- Free nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV
- Compression modulus of nuclear matter: K = 250 ± 50 MeV (estimated from giant monopole resonance energies)
- Nuclear symmetry energy: β = 33 ± 3 MeV (from semi-empirical binding energy formulas)
- Anomalous orbital g-factor: δg_l ≈ 0.23 ± 0.03 (from experimental results)
- Spin-isospin interaction parameter: g'_NN ≈ 0.6–0.7 (from analysis of giant Gamow-Teller resonances)
- For scalar-isoscalar boson exchange test (m_s = 500 MeV, g_s = 2.5) at k_F = 1.33 fm⁻¹: total f_0 = −0.830 fm² (exact), total g_0 = 0.291 fm² (exact), total f_1 = 0.240 fm² (exact), total g_1 = 0.127 fm² (exact), with agreement between numerical partial-wave expansion and analytical results to within 1% or better

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter that computed only the leading-order contribution exactly while approximately summing certain classes of diagrams to all orders, such as the Brueckner G-matrix approach of Babu (1973) and Sjoberg (1973) and the induced interaction of Babu and Brown (1973). It builds on the order-by-order study of pion-exchange contributions by Kaiser (2006) and on low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction developments by Bogner (2002, 2003). The present framework sets the stage for future calculations including the leading-order chiral three-nucleon interaction, which the authors identify as necessary to resolve deficiencies in the description of nuclear matter bulk properties when only two-nucleon forces are employed.
