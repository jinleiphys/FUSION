# Investigation of the high-spin rotational properties of the proton emitter $^{113}$Cs using a particle-number conserving method

> The recently observed two high-spin rotational bands in the proton emitter $^{113}$Cs are investigated using the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the Pauli blocking effects are taken into account exactly. By using the configuration assignments of band 1 ($π3/2^+[422], α= -1/2$) and band 2 ($π1/2^+[420], α= 1/2$), the experimental moments of inertia and quasiparticle alignments can be well reproduced by the present calculations, which in turn strongly support these configuration assignments. Furthermore, by analyzing the occupation probability $n_μ$ of each cranked Nilsson level near the Fermi surface and the contribution of each orbital to the angular momentum alignments, the backbending mechanism of these two bands is also investigated.

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with a particle-number conserving method can reproduce the experimental moments of inertia and quasiparticle alignments for the two high-spin rotational bands in the proton emitter $^{113}$Cs, but the sharp backbendings at $\hbar\omega \sim 0.35$ MeV are not very well reproduced because the cranking model mixes configurations before and after backbending, and going beyond the cranking model is needed for exact treatment.

## Method

The authors used the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, where the Hamiltonian is diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space of cranked many-particle configurations (CMPC) with dimension about 1000 for both protons and neutrons. The Nilsson parameters ($\kappa$ and $\mu$) were taken from traditional values, deformation parameters $\varepsilon_2 = 0.192$ and $\varepsilon_4 = -0.027$ from Moeller (1995), and effective monopole pairing strengths $G_{\rm p} = 0.5$ MeV and $G_{\rm n} = 0.7$ MeV. The occupation probability $n_\mu$ of each cranked Nilsson level and the contribution of each orbital to angular momentum alignments were analyzed to investigate the backbending mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: $\varepsilon_2 = 0.192$, $\varepsilon_4 = -0.027$ for $^{113}$Cs
- Effective monopole pairing strengths: $G_{\rm p} = 0.5$ MeV, $G_{\rm n} = 0.7$ MeV
- CMPC truncation energies: about $0.9\hbar\omega_0$ for both protons and neutrons; $\hbar\omega_{\rm 0p}=8.406$ MeV for protons, $\hbar\omega_{\rm 0n}=8.556$ MeV for neutrons
- Harris parameters used for alignment definition: $J_0 = 17.0\ \hbar^2$MeV$^{-1}$, $J_1 = 25.8\ \hbar^4$MeV$^{-3}$ from Wady (2015)
- Backbending occurs at rotational frequency $\hbar\omega \sim 0.35$ MeV for both bands
- Experimental half-life of $^{113}$Cs: $T_{1/2}=16.7(7)$ $\mu$s; proton energy $E_{\rm p}=959(6)$ keV from Page (1994)
- Observed spins extend to $45/2\ \hbar$ and $51/2\ \hbar$ for the two bands, with excitation energies over 8 MeV above ground state

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to rare-earth nuclei (Liu 2004, Zhang 2009) and actinide nuclei (He 2009, Zhang 2011), to the proton emitter $^{113}$Cs, which lies beyond the proton drip-line. The configuration assignments of band 1 ($\pi 3/2^+[422], \alpha = -1/2$) and band 2 ($\pi 1/2^+[420], \alpha = 1/2$) from Wady (2015) are supported, contradicting earlier assignments as $\pi h_{11/2}$ from Gross (1998) and Yu (2003). The analysis of occupation probabilities reveals that the backbending in both bands is mainly driven by alignment of $\nu h_{11/2}$ and $\pi h_{11/2}$ orbitals, with the neutron and proton backbending frequencies very close to each other. The work enables future studies incorporating neutron-proton pairing correlations, which may improve the calculated results after backbending.
