# Statistical Gauge Theory for Relativistic Finite Density Problems

> A relativistic quantum field theory is presented for finite density problems based on the principle of locality. It is found that, in addition to the conventional ones, a local approach to the relativistic quantum field theories at both zero and finite density consistent with the violation of Bell like inequalities should contain, and provide solutions to at least three additional problems, namely, 1) the statistical gauge invariance 2) the dark components of the local observables and 3) the fermion statistical blocking effects, base upon an asymptotic non-thermo ensemble. An application to models are presented to show the importance of the discussions.

## Key claim

A local relativistic quantum field theory for finite density systems must incorporate three additional elements beyond the conventional framework: statistical gauge invariance, dark components of local observables, and fermion statistical blocking effects, based on an asymptotic non-thermo ensemble (ANTE) rather than the standard grand canonical ensemble (GCE). The abstract glosses over the condition that the GCE-based approach is unstable against fermion number fluctuations in phases with spontaneous symmetry breaking, requiring the ANTE to cure this pathology.

## Method

The author introduces a primary statistical gauge field μ^α(x) as a local 4-vector field to implement locality in finite-density relativistic quantum field theory. The partition functional is defined as a functional of this field, and the ground state fermion number density is obtained as a functional derivative with respect to μ^0(x). The asymptotic grand canonical ensemble (AGCE) is defined as the GCE of the corresponding free system in the remote past when interactions are adiabatically switched off, but is found to be unstable; the asymptotic non-thermo ensemble (ANTE) is introduced with modified boundary conditions where negative energy states are filled up to E = -ε and positive energy states up to E = ε. The effective potential is evaluated for half-bosonized four-fermion interaction models (Nambu Jona-Lasinio type and chiral Gross-Neveu type) in 3+1 and 2+1 dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For a massless system in the AGCE, the fermion number density is given by ρ̅ = N_g μ^3 / 3π^2, where N_g is the total internal degrees of freedom besides spin.
- The effective potential for the chiral symmetry breaking phase shows ∂²V_eff/∂μ²|_(μ=0) < 0 for σ ≠ 0 and D ≥ 2, indicating instability of the σ ≠ 0 state with ρ̅ = 0.
- In the α-phase (quark-antiquark condensation), the vacuum has ε_vac < σ_vac in all studied cases.
- The effective action for slow-varying μ'^α in the normal phase contains a mass term (N_g/π²) ε̅² μ'·μ', where ε̅ is the ground state value of ε.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous developments (Ying, 1998; Ying, 1997) on local finite-density field theory and the eight-component fermion formalism. It addresses the inadequacy of the standard GCE approach used in lattice simulations (Barbour, Kogut, and collaborators, 1998; 1999) for finite-density problems, particularly the early onset of baryon number before chiral symmetry restoration. The theory enables a framework where dark components of local observables correspond to localized, non-propagating fermion states analogous to Anderson localization (Stern, 1998), and predicts the absence of a long-range force coupling to baryon number in the normal phase of hadronic matter, consistent with null results in fifth-force searches.
