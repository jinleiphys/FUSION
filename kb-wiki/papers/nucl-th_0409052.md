# On Raynal's posting in arXiv (nucl-th/0407060)

> With nucl-th/0407060, Jacques Raynal uses the arXiv in a way which does not conform to standard professional practices. His posting contains many statements that are beyond the borders of acceptable scientific disputes, with the scope to defame colleagues by manifestly false or misleading statements. In this comment we reject the three ``critiques'' expressed by Raynal. 1. The fact that we possibly misquoted our references. 2. The role of the Pauli principle in these kind of calculations. 3. The nature and limits of our coupled-channel potential model. Raynal's postings unfairly detract from the importance of our work, which we published in Nuclear Physics A728, 65 (2003), on a new approach, Multi-Channel-Algebraic-Scattering (MCAS), for coupled-channel calculations. With the MCAS approach we were able to identify systematically all low-energy compound resonances, and to include effectively the Pauli principle in collective, geometrical-type, macroscopic models of multichannel interaction. This represents a clear advantage with respect to the current distribution of the ECIS formulation.

# On Raynal's posting in arXiv (nucl-th/0407060)

## Key claim

The authors reject Raynal's three critiques of their Multi-Channel-Algebraic-Scattering (MCAS) approach, asserting that Raynal's claims about misquoted references, the role of the Pauli principle, and the nature of their coupled-channel potential model are false or misleading; they maintain that their method successfully includes the Pauli principle in collective macroscopic models, which Raynal dismisses as irrelevant at low energies.

## Method

The authors defend their MCAS method published in Nuclear Physics A728, 65 (2003), which uses an Orthogonalizing Pseudo Potential (OPP) method (Eq. 43 of that paper) to incorporate Pauli exclusion effects into coupled-channel calculations with collective, geometrical-type macroscopic models. They compare results obtained "With OPP" (Pauli principle included) versus "Without OPP" (macroscopic form factor calculation of the same type as might be made with the ECIS code) for low-energy neutron-12C scattering, identifying systematic differences in bound and resonance states. The authors also specify page and line numbers in three textbooks (Hodgson, Greiner and Maruhn, Mahaux and Weidenmuller) to substantiate their claims about the known violation of the Pauli principle in collective-model formulations.

## Key numbers

- In the "Without OPP" column of Table 1 of Ref. [7], there are 8 additional spurious excited states and at least 2 spurious resonances compared to the "With OPP" column, demonstrating the importance of Pauli principle treatment.
- The MCAS approach addresses low-energy compound resonances in the energy range 0 to 5 MeV or more, where Raynal dismisses Pauli effects as negligible.
- The authors reference that Raynal's own 1967 microscopic-type calculation (with co-authors) achieved only qualitative agreement with experimental data, as noted on page 108 of Mahaux and Weidenmuller's book.

## Context

This comment responds to Raynal's arXiv posting (nucl-th/0407060) which criticized the authors' MCAS approach and made personal attacks. The work extends the authors' prior publication in Nuclear Physics A728, 65 (2003) and addresses the long-standing problem of Pauli principle violation in collective, geometrical-type macroscopic models, as discussed in textbooks by Hodgson (pages 426-427), Greiner and Maruhn (page 297), and Mahaux and Weidenmuller (pages 103, 113-114). The authors claim their approach represents a clear advantage over the current distribution of the ECIS formulation developed by Raynal, and enables systematic identification of all low-energy compound resonances with proper treatment of anti-symmetrization.
