# The Asymmetric Superfluid Local Density Approximation (ASLDA)

> Here we describe the form of the Asymmetric Superfluid Local Density Approximation (ASLDA), a Density Functional Theory (DFT) used to model the two-component unitary Fermi gas. We give the rational behind the functional, and describe explicitly how we determine the form of the DFT from the to the available numerical and experimental data.

# The Asymmetric Superfluid Local Density Approximation (ASLDA)

## Key claim
The ASLDA functional provides a Density Functional Theory (DFT) description of the two-component unitary Fermi gas that is tuned to reproduce Monte-Carlo thermodynamics of homogeneous normal and symmetric superfluid phases, but the functional's dependence on polarization is constrained by limited data, only data for polarizations x = n_b/n_a < 0.5 were used in fitting the normal state parameters due to suspected contamination from superfluid correlations at smaller polarizations.

## Method
The authors construct a local energy-density functional of the densities n_a, n_b, kinetic densities τ_a, τ_b, and anomalous density ν, expressed in terms of Bogoliubov quasiparticle wavefunctions satisfying generalized Kohn-Sham equations. The functional is parametrized by three dimensionless functions α(x), β(x), and γ(x) of the asymmetry x = n_b/n_a, which are determined by fitting to Monte-Carlo data for the homogeneous normal state energy, the symmetric superfluid energy ξ = 0.40(1), the quasiparticle dispersion, and the effective polaron mass of a spin-down fermion in a spin-up sea. The pairing interaction γ(x) is held constant at its symmetric value, while α(x) and β(x) are interpolated from Monte-Carlo data points using a smooth parametrization in the variable z = tanh(ln x / c).

## Key numbers
- ξ = 0.40(1): the ratio of the symmetric superfluid energy density to the free Fermi gas energy density at equal densities
- η = Δ/ε_F = 0.504(24): the pairing gap in units of the Fermi energy for the symmetric superfluid (from Monte-Carlo); the fit gives η = 0.493(12)
- α(x=1) = 1.094(17): the inverse effective mass (m_eff^{-1}/m^{-1}) at equal densities
- β(x=1) = -0.526(18): the dimensionless Hartree energy parameter at equal densities
- γ^{-1} = -0.0907(77): the inverse pairing coupling constant at equal densities
- m_0 ≈ 1.04(3) m: the effective polaron mass of a single spin-down fermion in a spin-up sea (from multiple Monte-Carlo calculations)
- α(0) = 1.000(00) and α(∞) = 0.962(28): the endpoint inverse effective masses for extreme polarization

## Context
This work extends the Symmetric Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) of Bulgac (2007) to asymmetric systems, incorporating Monte-Carlo data from Lobo et al. (2006), Carlson et al. (2005), and others. The functional enables quantitative exploration of inhomogeneous phases such as the Larkin-Ovchinnikov (LO) supersolid phase predicted in Bulgac and Forbes (2008). The approach is constrained by the fermion sign problem limiting polarized Monte-Carlo data; future ab initio calculations or experiments at large polarizations could test whether qualitatively new physics (e.g., p-wave pairing) appears.
