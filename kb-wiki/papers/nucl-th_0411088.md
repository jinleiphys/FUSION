# Covariant Impulse Approximation for the study of the internal structure of composite particles

> We present a brief review on the Impulse Approximation method to study processes of scattering off composite particles. We first construct the model in a non-relativistic fashion that enables us to extend the model to a covariant Impulse Approximation, which is needed for the study of high momentum transfer processes.

## Key claim

The paper presents a didactic construction of a covariant Impulse Approximation (IA) for scattering off composite particles, showing that a relativistic three-dimensional model can be defined without introducing time components of dynamical quantities, while conserving the main requirements of the IA. The authors explicitly treat only two- and three-hadron composite systems of scalar constituents, noting that generalization to fermionic particles is straightforward.

## Method

The authors first construct a non-relativistic Impulse Approximation (NRIA) where the transition amplitude is expressed as a matrix element of single-particle transition operators between initial and final bound-state wave functions, with intrinsic coordinates separating center-of-mass and internal motion. They then develop a covariant generalization by placing spectator momenta on the mass shell, using Lorentz transformations to boost wave functions from the bound system rest frame to the observation reference frame, and introducing covariant three-dimensional integrations with invariant normalization factors to ensure correct Lorentz transformation properties.

## Key numbers

- Two-body vertex factor: \( A_{FI}^{(2)} = 2 \int d^3p \, \psi_F^\dagger(\vec p - \frac{\vec q}{2}) \hat O_2 \psi_I(\vec p) \) in the non-relativistic case
- Three-body vertex factor: \( A_{FI}^{(3)} = 3 \int d^3p_\rho d^3p_\lambda \, \psi_F^\dagger(\vec p_\rho, p_\lambda - \sqrt{2/3}\vec q) \hat O_3 \psi_I(p_\rho, p_\lambda) \) in the non-relativistic case
- Normalization Jacobian for three-body case: \( J^{(3)} = 3^{-3/2} \)
- Two-body normalization Jacobian: \( J^{(2)} = 1 \)

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Impulse Approximation, which had been successfully applied to elastic and inelastic form factors of atomic nuclei, to a covariant framework needed for lepton scattering off hadrons at high momentum transfer where non-relativistic models fail. The authors note that relativistic corrections up to order \( c^{-2} \) had been included via the Foldy-Wouthuysen expansion (Foldy) and applied to hadron electromagnetic interactions (De Sanctis), but no viable method existed to include relativistic effects to all orders. The covariant model presented realizes the Point Form of Relativistic Quantum Mechanics (Dirac) and enables future derivation of a conserved covariant electromagnetic current for interacting fermions.
