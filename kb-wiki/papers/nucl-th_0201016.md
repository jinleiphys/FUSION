# Final state interactions in omega photoproduction near threshold

> Vector meson photoproduction and electroproduction have been suggested as a tool to find or confirm the nucleon resonances. In order to extract more reliable informations on the nucleon resonances, understanding the non-resonant background is indispensable. We consider final state interactions in omega photoproduction as a background production mechanism. For the intermediate states, we consider nucleon--vector-meson and nucleon-pion channels. The role of the final state interactions is discussed in omega meson photoproduction near threshold.

## Key claim

Final state interactions (FSI) from intermediate nucleon–vector-meson and nucleon–pion channels provide a non-negligible background contribution to omega photoproduction near threshold, and must be understood before nucleon resonance parameters can be reliably extracted from experimental data. The authors caution that their calculation is a preliminary estimate and they do not adjust parameters to fit data.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop FSI diagrams for the reaction γ p → ω p, selecting intermediate ρ⁰ p, π⁰ p, and π⁺ n states based on their large photoproduction cross sections. The intermediate ρ⁰ p amplitude uses a tree-level model for γ p → ρ⁰ p (including Pomeron, π, σ exchange, and nucleon pole terms) combined with a ρ⁰ p → ω p amplitude via vector meson dominance and the ωρπ interaction Lagrangian with coupling g_ωρπ = 12.9 GeV⁻¹. For intermediate pion–nucleon channels, the γ p → π N amplitude is taken from the SAID program (valid up to W ≤ 2 GeV), and the π N → ω p amplitude uses a model with ωρπ, ωNN, and ρNN couplings.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant g_ωρπ = 12.9 GeV⁻¹, fixed by SU(3) flavor symmetry.
- ωNN coupling: g_ωNN = 10.35, κ_ω = 0.
- ρNN coupling: g_ρNN = 6.12, κ_ρ = 3.1.
- Differential cross sections for γ p → ω p are computed at photon energies E_γ = 1.23 GeV and E_γ = 1.68 GeV, with contributions from intermediate ρ⁰ p, π⁰ p, and π⁺ n states shown separately.
- The charged pion intermediate state (π⁺ n) gives the largest FSI contribution among the channels considered.

## Context

This work extends the tree-level background models for ω photoproduction (Oh et al., 2001; Zhao, 2001) by adding one-loop final state interactions, following the approach used in dynamical models for pion photoproduction and pion–nucleon scattering (Nozawa et al., 1990; Sato and Lee, 1996). The results indicate that FSI contributions are comparable to tree-level amplitudes and must be included before nucleon resonance parameters can be reliably extracted from forthcoming experimental data; the authors note that careful analysis of angular distributions is needed to distinguish ρ⁰ p and π N intermediate channels at backward scattering angles.
