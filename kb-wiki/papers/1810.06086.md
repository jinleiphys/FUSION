# Systematic investigation of the high-$K$ isomers and the high-spin rotational bands in the neutron rich Nd and Sm isotopes by a particle-number conserving method

> The rotational properties of the neutron rich Nd and Sm isotopes with mass number $A\approx150$ are systematically investigated using the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the Pauli blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The 2-quasiparticle states in even-even Nd and Sm isotopes with excitation energies lower than 2.5~MeV are systematically calculated. The available data can be well reproduced and some possible 2 and 4-quasiparticle isomers are also suggested for future experiments. The experimentally observed rotational frequency variations of moments of inertia for the even-even and odd-$A$ nuclei are reproduced very well by the calculations. The effects of high-order deformation $\varepsilon_6$ on the 2-quasiparticle excitation energies and moments of inertia of the ground state bands in even-even Nd and Sm isotopes are analyzed in detail. By analyzing the occupation probability $n_μ$ of each cranked Nilsson orbitals near the Fermi surface and the contribution of each major shell to the angular momentum alignments, the alignment mechanism in these nuclei is understood clearly.

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with a particle-number conserving method, which treats Pauli blocking exactly, can systematically reproduce the available experimental data for 2-quasiparticle states and moments of inertia in neutron-rich Nd and Sm isotopes, but the agreement depends on including high-order deformation $\varepsilon_6$ and the calculations suggest that $\varepsilon_6$ may decrease with increasing rotational frequency.

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with Nilsson potential, Coriolis interaction, and monopole plus quadrupole pairing was diagonalized in a truncated cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space of dimension about 1000 for both protons and neutrons. The particle-number conserving method was used to treat pairing correlations, conserving particle number exactly and accounting for Pauli blocking effects without approximation. Deformation parameters ($\varepsilon_2$, $\varepsilon_4$, $\varepsilon_6$) were taken from Moller et al. (1995), and the neutron orbital $\nu5/2^+[642]$ was shifted upward by $0.07\hbar\omega_0$ to reproduce the experimental ground state sequence.

## Key numbers

- Effective pairing strengths: $G_{\rm 0p}=0.25$ MeV and $G_{\rm 2p}=0.01$ MeVfm$^{-4}$ for protons; $G_{\rm 0n}=0.30$ MeV and $G_{\rm 2n}=0.02$ MeVfm$^{-4}$ for neutrons, used globally for all Nd and Sm isotopes.
- CMPC truncation energies: about $0.85\hbar\omega_0$ for protons and $0.80\hbar\omega_0$ for neutrons.
- The inclusion of $\varepsilon_6$ deformation alters 2-quasiparticle excitation energies by about 250 keV, consistent with potential energy surface calculations by Patel et al. (2014).
- Root-mean-square deviation between calculations with and without $\varepsilon_6$ is about 130 keV for Nd isotopes and about 260 keV for Sm isotopes.
- Calculated excitation energy for the $K^\pi=8^-$ 4-quasiparticle state in $^{160}$Nd is about 2899 keV.
- Calculated excitation energy for the $K^\pi=11^-$ 4-quasiparticle isomer in $^{160}$Sm is 2918 keV, compared to the experimental value of 2757 keV.
- The $3^+$ state with configuration $\nu^2 1/2^-[521]\otimes 5/2^-[512]$ in $^{160}$Nd is lowered by 439 keV when $\varepsilon_6$ deformation is neglected.

## Context

This work extends the particle-number conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) approach, previously applied to rare-earth and actinide nuclei by Zeng et al. (1994), Liu et al. (2004), and Zhang et al. (2009, 2011, 2012, 2013), to a systematic study of neutron-rich Nd and Sm isotopes around $A\approx150$. It complements and improves upon earlier calculations using the quasiparticle rotor model by Simpson et al. (2009) and Urban et al. (2009), the projected shell model by Yang et al. (2010, 2011), and potential energy surface calculations by Patel et al. (2014, 2016). The results suggest specific 2- and 4-quasiparticle isomers for future experimental confirmation and indicate that the $\varepsilon_6$ deformation may be rotationally dependent, motivating further studies of deformation evolution at high spin.
