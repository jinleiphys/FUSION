# Large-Scale Calculations of the Double-Beta Decay of 76Ge, 130Te, 136Xe, and 150Nd in the Deformed Self-Consistent Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation

> We use the axially-deformed Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) together with the SkM* energy-density functional, both as originally presented and with the time-odd part adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance energy in 208Pb, to calculate the matrix elements governing the neutrinoless double-beta decay of 76Ge, 130Te, 136Xe, and 150Nd. Our matrix elements in 130Te and 136Xe are significantly smaller than those of previous QRPA calculations, primarily because of the difference in pairing or deformation between the initial and final nuclei. In 76Ge and 150Nd our results are similar to those of less computationally intensive QRPA calculations. We suspect the 76Ge result, however, because we are forced to use a spherical ground-state, even though the HFB indicates a deformed minimum.

## Key claim

The authors find that their self-consistent deformed QRPA matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in 130Te and 136Xe are significantly smaller than those from previous QRPA calculations, primarily due to differences in pairing or deformation between initial and final nuclei that earlier spherical calculations neglected; however, they caution that the QRPA may be inadequate for 76Ge because they were forced to use a spherical ground state despite the HFB indicating a deformed minimum.

## Method

The authors used an axially-deformed Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with the SkM* energy-density functional, both as originally presented and with the time-odd part adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance energy in 208Pb. They performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a B-spline basis in a cylindrical box of dimensions 20 fm with 0.7 fm mesh spacing and 60 MeV energy cutoff, then constructed and diagonalized the QRPA Hamiltonian matrix in a basis of two-quasiparticle states, truncating to about 15,000 states out of roughly half a million. They fitted the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength to reproduce experimental two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements, then used those strengths to compute neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- 76Ge: 0nuββ matrix element M' = 5.09 (with modified SkM* and g_A=1.25); the HFB minimum occurs at prolate deformation β2=0.18 but the calculation used a near-spherical minimum at β2=-0.025
- 130Te: 0nuββ matrix element M' = 1.37 (modified SkM*, g_A=1.25); the daughter 130Xe has prolate deformation β2=0.13
- 136Xe: 0nuββ matrix element M' = 1.89 (modified SkM*, g_A=1.25, with Hartree-Fock + Lipkin-Nogami overlap); the HFB overlap between initial and final mean fields is 0.47, increasing to 0.57 with Lipkin-Nogami
- 150Nd: 0nuββ matrix element M' = 2.71 (modified SkM*, g_A=1.25); initial 150Nd has β2=0.27 and final 150Sm has β2=0.22
- The modification of SkM* suppresses the 0nuββ matrix element by up to 15% in most cases

## Context

This work extends previous spherical QRPA calculations by allowing axial deformation and using fully self-consistent Skyrme-HFB mean fields with all nucleons active and continuum states included via a large cylindrical box. The results for 76Ge and 150Nd are similar to those of the deformed QRPA calculations by Fang et al. (2011), while the significantly smaller matrix elements for 130Te and 136Xe contradict earlier spherical QRPA results from the Tubingen group (Simkovic et al., 2009) and the Jyvaskyla group (Suhonen et al., 2008). The authors conclude that further refinements within the QRPA framework are unlikely to improve results substantially, and that moving beyond the QRPA to methods such as the generator-coordinate method is needed to address shape coexistence in 76Se and 130Xe and pairing effects at closed shells in 136Xe.
