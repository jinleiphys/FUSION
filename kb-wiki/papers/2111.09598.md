# Search for the $α$ + core structure in the ground state bands of $22 \leq Z \leq 42$ even-even nuclei

> A systematic analysis of the $α$ + core structure is performed in the ground state bands of even-even nuclei of the $22 \leq Z \leq 42$ region in terms of the Local Potential Model. The $α$ + core interaction is described by the nuclear potential of (1 + Gaussian)$\times$(W.S. + W.S.$^3$) shape with 2 free parameters. Properties such as energy levels, reduced $α$-widths, $B(E2)$ transition rates and rms charge radii are calculated and compared with experimental data. A good agreement with the experimental data is obtained in general, even for the nuclei without the $α$ + {doubly closed shell core} configuration. The analysis of the selected nuclei in the $22 \leq Z \leq 42$ region indicates that $^{44}$Ti and $^{94}$Mo have a greater $α$-clustering degree in comparison with their respective neighboring nuclei of this set. In addition, the model points to the existence of nuclei without the $α$ + {doubly closed shell core} configuration with a significant $α$-clustering degree compared to $^{44}$Ti, $^{60}$Zn, and $^{94}$Mo. The study shows that the $α$ + core approach is satisfactorily applicable to nuclei other than those with $α$-clustering above double-shell closures.

## Key claim

The Local Potential Model with a (1 + Gaussian)×(W.S. + W.S.³) nuclear potential provides a satisfactory description of ground state bands for even-even nuclei in the 22 ≤ Z ≤ 42 region, including nuclei without the α + doubly closed shell core configuration, with the analysis indicating that ⁴⁴Ti and ⁹⁴Mo show greater α-clustering degree than their respective neighbors, but that several non-doubly-closed-shell nuclei also exhibit significant α-clustering comparable to ⁴⁴Ti, ⁶⁰Zn, and ⁹⁴Mo.

## Method

The authors applied the Local Potential Model (LPM) to 21 even-even nuclei selected from Ti (Z=22) to Mo (Z=42) using a criterion based on the maximum Qα/AT value (binding energy per nucleon from α-core separation) in each isotopic chain, with additional conditions for nuclei having sufficient experimental level data. The α + core interaction was described by a nuclear potential of the form (1 + Gaussian)×(W.S. + W.S.³) with two free parameters (R and σ) fitted to reproduce the 0⁺ and 4⁺ experimental levels of each ground state band, while four parameters (V₀ = 220 MeV, a = 0.65 fm, b = 0.3, λ = 0.14) were fixed. Energy levels, reduced α-widths, B(E2) transition rates, and rms charge radii were calculated from the Schrödinger radial equation and compared with experimental data from the ENSDF database and other sources.

## Key numbers

- 21 even-even nuclei were selected for systematic analysis in the 22 ≤ Z ≤ 42 region
- Standard deviations (S.D.) for calculated ground state bands (0⁺ to 8⁺ levels) ranged from 0.057 MeV (⁶⁸Se) to 1.108 MeV (⁵⁸Ni); ⁴⁶Cr had S.D. = 0.066 MeV, ⁵⁴Cr had S.D. = 0.089 MeV, ⁶⁰Zn had S.D. = 0.155 MeV, ⁴⁴Ti had S.D. = 0.477 MeV, and ⁹⁴Mo had S.D. = 0.155 MeV
- Reduced α-width γα² for the 0⁺ ground state ranged from 0.031 keV (⁷⁸Sr, G=12) to 3.644 keV (⁴⁴Ti); ⁹⁴Mo had γα² = 0.835 keV, ⁶⁰Zn had γα² = 0.591 keV
- The ratio γα²/γα²(⁴⁴Ti) ranged from 0.009 (⁷⁸Sr, G=12) to 1.000 (⁴⁴Ti); ⁶⁰Zn had ratio 0.162, ⁹⁴Mo had ratio 0.229
- The ratio of predicted to experimental rms charge radius (⟨r²⟩T¹/²/⟨r²⟩T exp¹/²) ranged from 0.9874 (⁷²Kr, G=12) to 0.9951 (⁶⁰Ni); ⁴⁴Ti had ratio 0.9929, ⁹⁴Mo had ratio 0.9928
- For ⁷²Kr, S.D. improved from 0.721 MeV (G=12) to 0.284 MeV (G=14); for ⁷⁸Sr, S.D. improved from 1.159 MeV (G=12) to 0.565 MeV (G=14)
- The Qα/AT value for ⁵⁴Cr was −146.8 keV, for ⁴⁶Cr was −147.7 keV; for ⁹²Zr the value was −32.200 keV

## Context

This work extends the Local Potential Model approach previously applied by the same authors to ⁴⁶Cr and ⁵⁴Cr (Souza and Miyake, 2017) and to the set {²⁰Ne, ⁴⁴Ti, ⁹⁴Mo, ²¹²Po} (Souza et al., 2019), and by Buck, Dover, and Vary (1975) for α + doubly closed shell core nuclei. The study systematically covers a broader region (22 ≤ Z ≤ 42) than prior works, including nuclei without doubly magic cores, and enables future comparative studies of α-clustering degree across isotopic chains. The results also provide a basis for testing the model on unstable nuclei such as ¹⁰⁴Te, where experimental data on energy levels and transition rates are still needed.
