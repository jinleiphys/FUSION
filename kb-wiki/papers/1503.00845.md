# $\bar{K} + N \to K + Ξ$ reaction and $S=-1$ hyperon resonances

> The $\bar{K} + N \to K + Ξ$ reaction is studied for center-of-momentum energies ranging from threshold to 3 GeV in an effective Lagrangian approach that includes the hyperon $s$- and $u$-channel contributions as well as a phenomenological contact amplitude. The latter accounts for the rescattering term in the scattering equation and possible short-range dynamics not included explicitly in the model. Existing data are well reproduced and three above-the-threshold resonances were found to be required to describe the data, namely, the $Λ(1890)$, $Σ(2030)$, and $Σ(2250)$. For the latter resonance we have assumed the spin-parity of $J^P=5/2^-$ and a mass of 2265 MeV. The $Σ(2030)$ resonance is crucial in achieving a good reproduction of not only the measured total and differential cross sections, but also the recoil polarization asymmetry. More precise data are required before a more definitive statement can be made about the other two resonances, in particular, about the $Σ(2250)$ resonance that is introduced to describe a small bump structure observed in the total cross section of $K^- + p \to K^+ + Ξ^-$. The present analysis also reveals a peculiar behavior of the total cross section data in the threshold energy region in $K^- + p \to K^+ + Ξ^-$, where the $P$- and $D$-waves dominate instead of the usual $S$-wave. Predictions for the target-recoil asymmetries of the $\bar{K} + N \to K + Ξ$ reaction are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper finds that three above-threshold hyperon resonances, Λ(1890), Σ(2030), and Σ(2250), are required to reproduce the existing data for the \(\bar{K} + N \to K + \Xi\) reaction, but the authors emphasize that more precise data are needed before definitive statements can be made about the Λ(1890) and Σ(2250) resonances; the Σ(2030) resonance is identified as crucial for reproducing not only total and differential cross sections but also the recoil polarization asymmetry.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level \(s\)-channel and \(u\)-channel hyperon exchange amplitudes, plus a phenomenological contact amplitude that accounts for rescattering contributions and short-range dynamics not explicitly included. The model is fitted to existing total cross section, differential cross section, and recoil polarization data for the reactions \(K^- + p \to K^+ + \Xi^-\), \(K^- + p \to K^0 + \Xi^0\), and \(K^- + n \to K^0 + \Xi^-\) from threshold up to a center-of-momentum energy of 3 GeV. Resonances were added one by one to the model, and only those that improved the fit quality by \(\delta\chi^2/N > 0.1\) were retained.

## Key numbers

- The production threshold energy for the \(\bar{K} + N \to K + \Xi\) reaction is approximately 1813 MeV.
- The Σ(2250) resonance was assigned a spin-parity of \(J^P = 5/2^-\) and a mass of 2265 MeV (adjusted from the PDG value of 2250 MeV).
- The Σ(2030) resonance has a mass of 2030 MeV and width of 180 MeV (PDG values); the Λ(1890) has a mass of 1890 MeV and width of 100 MeV (PDG values).
- The momentum transfer at threshold is about 200 MeV.
- The global fit yields \(\chi^2/N = 1.49\) for all data combined, with \(\chi^2/N = 1.65\) for the \(K^- + p \to K^+ + \Xi^-\) channel and \(\chi^2/N = 1.10\) for the \(K^- + p \to K^0 + \Xi^0\) channel.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian analyses by Sharov et al. (2011) and Shyam et al. (2011), which also studied the \(\bar{K} + N \to K + \Xi\) reaction but differed in the number and treatment of intermediate hyperon resonances. The present analysis is a first step toward building a more complete reaction model for extracting hyperon resonance properties from forthcoming experimental data, particularly from the J-PARC \(\Xi\) spectroscopy program and the CLAS program at Jefferson Lab. The results also impact the study of \(\Xi\) hypernuclei, where the elementary reaction serves as input for hypernucleus production models.
