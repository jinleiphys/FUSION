# Transverse momentum distributions of valence quark in light and heavy vector mesons

> We study the leading-twist time-reversal even transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) of light and heavy vector mesons, i.e., the $ρ$, $J/ψ$ and $Υ$. We employ the leading Fock-state light front wave functions (LF-LFWFs) of $ρ$ and $J/ψ$ from our recent study, and supplement with $Υ$'s LF-LFWFs. These LF-LFWFs are extracted from dynamically solved Bethe-Salpeter wave functions. The vector meson TMDs are then studied with the light front overlap representation at leading Fock-state. All the obtained TMDs are non-vanishing and evolve with current quark mass, in particular the tensor polarized TMDs $f_{1LT}$ and $f_{1TT}$ which undergo a sign flip. The $ρ$ TMDs are compared with other model studies and agreement is found, aside from $f_{1LT}$ and $f_{1TT}$. Finally, the collinear PDFs of vector mesons are studied. The $ρ$'s valence PDFs $f_{1,v}(x)$ and $g_{1L,v}(x)$ are evolved to the scale of 2.4 GeV, with their first three moments compared to lattice QCD prediction. The qualitative behavior of tensor polarized PDF $f_{1LL}(x)$ in $ρ$ at large $x$ is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors compute leading-twist T-even transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) for light and heavy vector mesons (ρ, J/ψ, Υ) using leading Fock-state light front wave functions extracted from dynamically solved Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, finding that all nine TMDs are non-vanishing and evolve with current quark mass, with the tensor polarized TMDs f1LT and f1TT undergoing a sign flip between light and heavy mesons; however, the f1LL TMD is excluded from most of the study because its profile is highly sensitive to the rescaling procedure used to normalize the leading Fock-state wave functions, indicating that higher Fock-state effects must be carefully treated for this observable.

## Method

The authors extract leading Fock-state light front wave functions (LF-LFWFs) for ρ, J/ψ, and Υ from covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave functions solved under rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations, projecting the BS wave functions onto the light front. They then compute the nine leading-twist T-even TMDs using the light front overlap representation at leading Fock-state, rescaling the LF-LFWFs to unit normalization separately for helicity Λ=0 and Λ=±1 states. The collinear PDFs are obtained by integrating TMDs over transverse momentum, and the ρ valence PDFs are evolved to 2.4 GeV for comparison with lattice QCD moments.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of vector meson light front wave functions from Bethe-Salpeter equations (Shi 2021) to the calculation of TMDs, and supplements with Υ meson wave functions following their study of η_b (Shi 2021). It compares ρ meson TMDs with results from the NJL model (Ninomiya 2017), the light front holographic model, and the light front quark model (Kaur 2020), finding agreement except for the tensor polarized TMDs f1LT and f1TT where the BSE-based approach yields non-vanishing results unlike the LF quark model. The collinear PDF moments are compared with lattice QCD predictions (Best 1997, Loffler 2021). This work enables future studies of spin-1 TMDs in the relativistic-to-nonrelativistic transition and provides predictions for possible experimental measurements in Drell-Yan or SIDIS processes.
