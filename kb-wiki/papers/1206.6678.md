# Neutrino masses and mixing: a flavour symmetry roadmap

> Over the last ten years tri-bimaximal mixing has played an important role in modeling the flavour problem. We give a short review of the status of flavour symmetry models of neutrino mixing. We concentrate on non-Abelian discrete symmetries, which provide a simple way to account for the TBM pattern. We discuss phenomenological implications such as neutrinoless double beta decay, lepton flavour violation as well as theoretical aspects such as the possibility to explain quarks and leptons within a common framework, such as grand unified models.

## Key claim

The tri-bimaximal (TBM) mixing pattern has been a useful reference ansatz for neutrino mixing models over the last ten years, but the robust measurement of a relatively "large" value of $\theta_{13}$ casts some doubt on its validity as a good first approximation, though it is too early to jump into conclusions since concrete theories may have large corrections to the TBM pattern.

## Method

The authors review non-Abelian discrete flavour symmetry models of neutrino mixing, concentrating on groups such as $A_4$ (the tetrahedral group) and its double covering $T'$, which contain triplet irreducible representations matching the three Standard Model generations. They examine how these symmetries can break into different subgroups in the charged lepton and neutrino sectors to produce the TBM mixing pattern, and discuss the seesaw mechanisms (type-I, type-II, inverse, and linear) that generate neutrino masses, as well as radiative and supersymmetric models.

## Key numbers

- The TBM mixing matrix has entries $2/\sqrt{6}$, $1/\sqrt{3}$, $0$, $-1/\sqrt{6}$, $1/\sqrt{3}$, $1/\sqrt{2}$, $-1/\sqrt{6}$, $1/\sqrt{3}$, $-1/\sqrt{2}$ in its three rows
- In the BMV model, the common neutrino mass $m_0$ must satisfy $m_0 \gsim 0.4$ eV to get large enough mass splittings to account for oscillation data
- The Cabibbo angle governing first-second generation quark mixing is about $\lambda_C\sim 0.22$, with first/third and second/third mixing angles about $\lambda_C^2$ and $\lambda_C^3$ respectively
- The ratio $|U_{e3}|$ is naturally of order $|\Delta m^2_{12}/\Delta m^2_{32}|^{1/2}$ in the limit $\delta',\delta''\ll \delta$

## Context

This review extends the line of flavour symmetry model building that began with the TBM ansatz proposed by Harrison, Perkins and Scott (2002) and the $A_4$ model by Ma and Rajasekaran (2001), followed by the realistic supersymmetric model of Babu, Ma and Valle (2002) and the derivation of TBM from flavour symmetry by Altarelli and Feruglio (2005). The paper discusses how recent measurements of $\theta_{13}$ by reactor experiments Double CHOOZ (2011), Daya Bay (2012), and RENO (2012) challenge the TBM pattern, and surveys possible extensions to include quarks and grand unification, enabling future work on reconciling large $\theta_{13}$ with discrete flavour symmetries and testing low-scale seesaw mechanisms at the LHC.
