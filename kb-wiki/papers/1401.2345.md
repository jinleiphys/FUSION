# Nuclear PDFs in the beginning of the LHC era

> The status of the global fits of nuclear parton distributions (nPDFs) is reviewed. In addition to comparing the contemporary analyses of nPDFs, difficulties and controversies posed by the neutrino-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering data is overviewed. At the end, the first dijet data from the LHC proton+lead collisions is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of global fits of nuclear parton distributions (nPDFs) at the beginning of the LHC era, arguing that while the first LHC proton+lead dijet data support the universality of nPDFs and the collinear factorization approach, significant uncertainties remain among existing parametrizations, and the long-standing tension with neutrino-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering data may be resolved by a normalization procedure.

## Method

The author compares the key characteristics, input data sets, and resulting nuclear modification factors from the four main contemporary NLO nPDF analyses: HKN07, EPS09, DSSZ, and nCTEQ. The comparison focuses on the up valence, sea quark, and gluon nuclear modification factors for lead at Q² = 10 GeV², and examines the treatment of neutrino-nucleus DIS data, including a proposed normalization method that divides cross sections by the total cross section for each neutrino beam energy. The first CMS dijet pseudorapidity distribution data from proton+lead collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV are compared to NLO pQCD predictions using the different nPDF sets.

## Key numbers

- The HKN07 fit uses 1241 data points with 12 free parameters and a Δχ² tolerance of 13.7; EPS09 uses 929 data points with 15 free parameters and Δχ² = 50; DSSZ uses 1579 data points with 25 free parameters and Δχ² = 30; nCTEQ uses 708 data points with 17 free parameters and Δχ² = 35.
- The Q² cut in DIS data is 1 GeV for HKN07 and DSSZ, 1.3 GeV for EPS09, and 2 GeV for nCTEQ.
- The dijet data from CMS use leading jet pT > 120 GeV, subleading jet pT > 30 GeV, and a cone size R = 0.3 in the anti-kT algorithm, at √s = 5.02 TeV.
- The EPS09 prediction agrees well with the preliminary CMS dijet data, while DSSZ nuclear effects are too weak and HKN07 corrects in the wrong direction.
- The inclusive pion data from RHIC at √s = 200 GeV and pT < 20 GeV are compared to the dijet data at √s = 5.02 TeV and pT ≳ 100 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the comparison of nPDF analyses by Eskola (2009), Hirai (2007), de Florian (2011), and the nCTEQ collaboration (Schienbein 2009, Kovarik 2010, 2013), and addresses the controversy over neutrino-nucleus DIS data compatibility initiated by Schienbein (2007) and Kovarik (2010). The proposed normalization method for neutrino data (Paukkunen 2013) and the first LHC dijet results (CMS 2014) enable future global fits with reduced uncertainties and a test of nPDF universality at higher energies.
