# Chiral aspects of hadron structure

> Chiral loop corrections for hadronic properties are considered in a constituent quark model. It is emphasized that the correct implementation of such corrections requires a sum over intermediate hadronic states. The leading non-analytic corrections are very important for baryon magnetic moments and explain the failure of the sum rule $(μ_{Σ^{+}}+2μ_{Σ^{-}})/μ_Λ=-1$ predicted by the constituent quark model.

## Key claim

Chiral loop corrections to hadron properties must be calculated by projecting onto intermediate hadronic states, not by computing loops on individual quark lines; the leading non-analytic (LNA) corrections to baryon magnetic moments are large and explain the failure of the constituent quark model sum rule \((\mu_{\Sigma^{+}}+2\mu_{\Sigma^{-}})/\mu_{\Lambda} = -1\), but the correct LNA behavior cannot be obtained from chiral loops on single quarks independent of their hadronic environment.

## Method

The authors start from a general chiral quark model Hamiltonian with a pion-quark interaction vertex and compute the physical baryon mass via a self-energy that sums over intermediate baryon-pion states. For magnetic moments, they evaluate the magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer, including contributions from intermediate octet and decuplet baryons, and extract the leading non-analytic term proportional to the pion mass. They then apply this formalism to the hyperon magnetic moment sum rule of Lipkin, expressing the ratio as a function of the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark model predicts \(R^{QM}_{\Sigma/\Lambda} = -1\) for the ratio \((\mu_{\Sigma^{+}}+2\mu_{\Sigma^{-}})/\mu_{\Lambda}\); the experimental value is \(R^{Exp}_{\Sigma/\Lambda} = -0.23\).
- The LNA contribution to the nucleon mass is \(\delta M_N = -\frac{3}{32 \pi f_\pi^2} g_A^2 m_\pi^3\).
- The LNA contribution to baryon magnetic moments is \(\mu^{LNA}_B = \frac{m_N m_\pi}{8\pi f^2_{\pi}}\,\beta^{BB\pi} \equiv \alpha_B \, m_\pi\).
- For the \(\Sigma^{+}\) magnetic moment: \(\mu_{\Sigma^+} \simeq 2.80 - 2.46 m_\pi\) (with \(m_\pi\) in GeV).
- For the \(\Sigma^{-}\) magnetic moment: \(\mu_{\Sigma^-} \simeq -1.50 + 2.46 m_\pi\) (with \(m_\pi\) in GeV).
- The ratio as a function of pion mass: \(\frac{\mu_{\Sigma^+}+2\mu_{\Sigma^-}} {\mu_{\Lambda}} \simeq 0.33 - 0.56 \frac{m_\pi}{m_\pi^{\rm phys}} + {\cal O}(m_\pi^2)\); at the physical pion mass the ratio is \(-0.23\), while in the chiral limit it is \(+0.33\).
- A naive treatment ignoring the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) mass difference would give a nucleon self-energy of \(-855\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the cloudy bag model (CBM) approach of Thomas and collaborators to general chiral quark models, emphasizing that the correct implementation of chiral loops requires projection onto hadronic states as previously argued by the same authors. It directly addresses the sum rule problem raised by Lipkin (Lipkin) and contrasts with the approach of Glozman and Riska (Glozman and Riska, 1996) who used a spin-isospin interaction of the form \(\sum_{i<j} \bsigma_i\cdot\bsigma_j \btau_i\cdot\btau_j V_{ij}\). The results enable model-independent extrapolations of lattice QCD data to physical quark masses, following the work of Leinweber et al. (1999) on baryon masses and magnetic moments.
