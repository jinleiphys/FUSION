# Quark magnetar in three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with vector interaction and magnetized gluon potential

> We investigate properties of strange quark matter in the framework of SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio(NJL) model with vector interaction under strong magnetic fields. The effects of vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector interaction on the equation of state of strange quark matter are investigated, and it is found that the equation of state is not sensitive to the vector-isovector interaction, however, a repulsive interaction in the vector-isoscalar channel gives a stiffer equation of state for cold dense quark matter. In the presence of magnetic field, gluons will be magnetized via quark loops, and the contribution from magnetized gluons to the equation of state is also estimated. The sound velocity square is a quantity to measure the hardness or softness of dense quark matter, and in the NJL model without vector interaction at zero magnetic field the sound velocity square is always less than 1/3. It is found that a repulsive vector-isoscalar interaction and a positive pressure contribution from magnetized gluons can enhance the sound velocity square, which can even reach 1. To construct quark magnetars under strong magnetic fields, we consider anisotropic pressures and use a density-dependent magnetic field profile to mimic the magnetic field distribution in a quark star. We also analyze the parameter region for the magnitude of vector-isoscalar interaction and the contribution from magnetized gluons in order to produce 2 solar mass quark magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a repulsive vector-isoscalar interaction combined with a positive pressure contribution from magnetized gluons can produce a stiff enough equation of state to support two-solar-mass quark magnetars, but only within a specific parameter region for the vector coupling constant and the gluon pressure coefficient, and under the condition that the magnetic field profile is density-dependent and the pressure anisotropy is accounted for.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector interactions under a strong external magnetic field, treating the quark matter in mean-field approximation with beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They introduce an ansatz for the magnetized gluon pressure at zero temperature as \( p_g = a_0 (\mu^2 eB + \mu^4) \), where \( a_0 \) is a free parameter, and they adopt a density-dependent magnetic field profile \( B = B_{\text{surf}} + B_0 [1 - \exp(-\beta_0 (n_b/n_0)^\gamma)] \) to mimic the magnetic field distribution inside a quark star. The longitudinal and transverse pressures are computed separately to account for the anisotropy induced by the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The vector-isoscalar coupling constant is varied as \( G_V = 0, 0.4 G_S, 0.8 G_S, 1.1 G_S \), where \( G_S \Lambda^2 = 1.835 \) with cutoff \( \Lambda = 631.4 \) MeV.
- The magnetized gluon pressure coefficient is taken as \( a_0 = -0.01, 0, 0.01 \).
- The density-dependent magnetic field profile uses \( B_0 = 4 \times 10^{18} \) G, \( \beta_0 = 0.003 \), \( \gamma = 3 \), and surface field \( B_{\text{surf}} = 10^{15} \) G.
- The sound velocity square can reach up to 1 with repulsive vector-isoscalar interaction and positive magnetized gluon pressure, whereas in the NJL model without vector interaction at zero magnetic field it is always less than 1/3.
- The vacuum constituent quark masses for u, d, s quarks do not change much for magnetic fields below \( 10^{19} \) G, but u and d quark masses increase drastically above \( 3 \times 10^{19} \) G.
- The chemical potentials for u, d, s quarks at baryon density \( n_b = 10 n_0 \) (with \( n_0 = 0.16 \) fm\(^{-3}\)) remain constant below \( 10^{19} \) G and decrease above that.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter in the NJL model under magnetic fields (Menezes 2009) and the role of vector interactions in producing stiff equations of state for two-solar-mass compact stars (Hell 2014, Menezes 2014). It also builds on the concept of density-dependent magnetic field profiles introduced by Bandyopadhyay (1997, 1998) and anisotropic pressures in magnetars (Ferrer 2010, Isayev 2011-2013). The paper enables future calculations of mass-radius relations for quark magnetars and constrains the parameter space for vector coupling and gluon contributions needed to reach 2 solar masses.
