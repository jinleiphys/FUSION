# Electron- versus neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We illustrate the connection between electron and neutrino scattering off nuclei and show how the former process can be used to constrain the description of the latter. After reviewing some of the nuclear models commonly used to study lepton-nucleus reactions, we describe in detail the SuSAv2 model and show how its predictions compare with the available electron- and neutrino-scattering data over the kinematical range going from the quasi-elastic peak to pion-production and highly inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that electron-nucleus scattering data can be used to constrain nuclear models for neutrino-nucleus scattering, but this validation is necessary yet insufficient because neutrinos probe the axial nuclear response (inaccessible via unpolarized electron scattering) and because neutrino beams have broad energy distributions that mix overlapping reaction channels in ways that electron beams do not.

## Method

The authors review the general formalism for lepton-nucleus scattering in Born approximation, expressing cross sections as contractions of leptonic and hadronic tensors, then decompose the hadronic tensor into nuclear response functions (longitudinal, transverse, and for neutrinos also vector-axial interference). They describe the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model for quasielastic scattering, the superscaling analysis that factorizes cross sections into a single-nucleon term times a scaling function, and the SuSAv2 model which extends this approach to cover the full kinematic range from quasielastic through pion production and deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum \(k_F\) is introduced to remove dependence on the particular nucleus in the scaling function definition (Eq. 1, no explicit numerical value given for \(k_F\) in the truncated text)
* Axial mass \(M_A = 1.032\) GeV is used in the dipole parametrization of the axial form factor
* Scaling of first kind is observed for momentum transfers \(q \gtrsim 300\text{--}400\) MeV/c, where low-energy collective effects become negligible
* Spectroscopic factors extracted from \((e,e'p)\) data are typically of order 0.5--0.7, depending on the orbital and nucleus

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach developed by Donnelly (1999) and collaborators, which factorizes electron-nucleus cross sections into a single-nucleon term times a scaling function that embodies nuclear dynamics. The paper reviews the SuSAv2 model developed by the authors' group, which uses electron scattering data to predict inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections, and compares with available electron and neutrino data from quasielastic through pion-production and highly inelastic scattering. The analysis is motivated by the needs of long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments (MiniBooNE, T2K, NOvA, DUNE, T2HK) which require percent-level control of nuclear uncertainties to extract oscillation parameters, and the paper notes that recent \((e,e')\) data on argon and titanium from JLab (Dai, 2018) were specifically taken to constrain models used in neutrino-nucleus scattering analyses.
