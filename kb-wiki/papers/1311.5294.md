# Atomic ionization of germanium due to neutrino magnetic moments

> An ab initio calculation of atomic ionization of germanium (Ge) by neutrinos was carried out in the framework of multiconfiguration relativistic random phase approximation. The main goal is to provide a more accurate cross section formula than the conventional one, which is based on the free electron approximation, for searches of neutrino magnetic moments with Ge detectors whose threshold is reaching down to the sub-keV regime. Limits derived with both methods are compared, using reactor neutrino data taken with low threshold germanium detectors.

## Key claim

The authors provide an *ab initio* calculation of atomic ionization of germanium by neutrinos using the multiconfiguration relativistic random phase approximation (MCRRPA), showing that atomic binding effects suppress the differential cross section at sub-keV energy transfers by up to a factor of 0.63 for weak scattering and 0.5 for magnetic scattering, contrary to earlier claims of enhancement but consistent with the free electron approximation only for energy transfers above approximately 1 keV.

## Method

The authors compute the response functions for weak and electromagnetic currents in germanium using the MCRRPA method, which extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation by using a multiconfiguration reference state (necessary due to the near degeneracy of the 4p subshells) and including two-body correlations via the random phase approximation. The atomic wave functions are calculated with the multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock (MCDF) package, and the transition matrix elements for neutrino scattering are obtained by solving the MCRRPA equations with spherical multipole expansion of the current operators. The method is benchmarked against photoionization data, reproducing experimental cross sections within 5% for photon energies above 80 eV.

## Key numbers

- Photoionization cross sections from MCRRPA agree with experimental data within 5% for photon energies from 80 eV up to 10 keV.
- The first ionization energy of the Ge atom calculated by MCDF is 7.856 eV, agreeing with the experimental value of 7.899 eV.
- For sub-keV energy transfers, atomic binding suppresses the differential cross section by a factor of 0.63 for weak scattering and 0.5 for magnetic scattering relative to the free electron approximation.
- The 90% CL limits on the neutrino magnetic moment derived with MCRRPA are identical to those from the free electron approximation for existing experiments: less than 7.4 x 10^{-11} mu_B (TEXONO 1 kg HPGe at 12 keV threshold), less than 2.9 x 10^{-11} mu_B (GEMMA 1.5 kg HPGe at 2.8 keV threshold), and less than 1.6 x 10^{-10} mu_B (TEXONO 900 g PPCGe at 0.5 keV threshold).
- For a projected PPCGe experiment with 1500/500 kg-day ON/OFF data at 0.3 keV threshold, the MCRRPA-derived limit is less than 2.6 x 10^{-11} mu_B compared to less than 2.3 x 10^{-11} mu_B from the free electron approximation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of atomic binding effects on neutrino scattering (Fayans 1992, Kopeikin 1997, Fayans 2001, Kopeikin 2003, Gounaris 2001) that showed suppression, and contradicts the claim by Wong (2010) that atomic structure could greatly enhance the magnetic cross section by orders of magnitude at low energy transfers. The MCRRPA method provides a more refined calculation than the relativistic Hartree-Fock approaches of Fayans (2001) and Kopeikin (2003) by using a multiconfiguration reference state, treating the non-local Fock term exactly, and including two-body correlations via RPA. The results enable more reliable constraints on neutrino magnetic moments from next-generation germanium detectors with sub-keV thresholds, such as those being developed for light WIMP searches and neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering.
