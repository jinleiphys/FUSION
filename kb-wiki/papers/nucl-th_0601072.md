# Complete Relativistic Description of the N*(1520)

> A relativistic description of spin 3/2 resonances and their decay channels is presented by calculating their selfenergies and spectral functions. The full vector-spinor structure is taken into account. Special emphasis is put on the N*(1520) and its decay channels pi N, rho N and pi Δ. All interactions are formulated such that only the correct number of degrees of freedom of a spin 3/2 state is propagated. The obtained results are compared with several approximations frequently used to avoid the complicated vector-spinor structure. Since this structure is taken fully into account here, the quality of the approximations can be judged.

## Key claim

The paper provides a fully relativistic description of the spin 3/2 N*(1520) resonance using the Pascalutsa formalism, which ensures that only the correct number of degrees of freedom is propagated; this framework is claimed to be simpler than conventional approaches because the spin 1/2 sector of the selfenergy drops out, reducing the number of required coefficients from ten to two.

## Method

The authors calculate the selfenergies and spectral functions of the N*(1520) resonance by constructing interactions for its three main decay channels (πN, ρN, and πΔ) within the Pascalutsa formalism, which enforces transversality of the selfenergy. The full vector-spinor (Rarita-Schwinger) structure is retained, and the dressed propagator is obtained via the Schwinger-Dyson equation. The spectral functions ρ_v and ρ_s are extracted from the imaginary part of the propagator, and the mass and width of the resonance are defined by expanding the denominator around the physical mass where the real part of the inverse propagator vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1520) resonance mass is defined by the condition that at √s = M_R, the quantity ṡ − M̃² = 0, where ṡ and M̃² are functions of the selfenergy components.
- The width Γ_V is defined as Γ_V = −(c/√s) Im F_v evaluated at s = M_R², where c is the inverse derivative of (ṡ − M̃²) with respect to s.
- The free Rarita-Schwinger propagator contains a pole term (p̸ + M)/(p² − M²) multiplying the spin 3/2 projection operator P^{3/2}, plus spin 1/2 terms proportional to 1/(3M²) and 1/(√3 M).
- The selfenergy in the Pascalutsa formalism reduces to only two independent coefficients (a₁ + p̸ a₂) for the spin 3/2 part and (a₃ + p̸ a₄) for the spin 1/2 part, compared to ten coefficients in conventional approaches.

## Context

This work extends the Pascalutsa formalism (Pascalutsa and Timmermans, 1999), previously applied only to the Δ(1232) isobar, to the negative-parity N*(1520) resonance, which also decays into unstable particles (ρN and πΔ). It provides a fully relativistic vacuum calculation that can serve as a starting point for more involved in-medium computations, such as those by Post et al. (2000, 2003) which used non-relativistic or simplified spin structures. The paper enables future coupled-channel calculations that respect analyticity and unitarity, in contrast to K-matrix approaches (Penner, 2002) that only include on-shell two-particle propagators.
