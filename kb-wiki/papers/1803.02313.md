# Methods for separation of deuterons produced in the medium and in jets in high energy collisions

> Coalescence has long been used to describe the production of light (anti-)nuclei in heavy ion collisions. The same underlying mechanism may also exist in jets when a proton and a neutron are close enough in phase space to form a deuteron. We model deuteron production in jets by applying an afterburner to protons and neutrons produced in PYTHIA for $p$+$p$ collisions at a center of mass energy $\sqrt{s} =$ 7 TeV. PYTHIA provides a reasonable description of the proton spectra and the shape of the deuteron spectrum predicted by the afterburner is in agreement with the data. We show that the rise in the coalescence parameter $B_2$ with momentum observed in data is consistent with coalescence in jets. We show that di-hadron correlations can be used to separate the contributions from the jet and the underlying event. This model predicts that the conditional coalescence parameter in the jet-like correlation should be independent of the trigger momentum.

# Methods for separation of deuterons produced in the medium and in jets in high energy collisions

## Key claim
The rise of the coalescence parameter B2 with transverse momentum observed in proton-proton collision data can be generated by deuteron formation through coalescence in jets, even without flow contributions; however, the PYTHIA+afterburner model overpredicts the absolute deuteron yield by a factor of approximately 20, likely because PYTHIA assigns the primary vertex as the origin of primary particles and therefore overestimates spatial correlations between protons and neutrons.

## Method
The authors used PYTHIA (Perugia 2011 tune) to simulate 1.1 billion proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, then applied a coalescence afterburner that evaluates the Wigner phase-space density derived from the Hulthén wave function (expressed as a sum of 15 Gaussian wave functions) for each proton-neutron pair to determine deuteron formation probability. They then employed di-hadron correlation techniques with trigger particles in various transverse momentum ranges to separate deuterons produced in jet-like correlations from those in the underlying event, using a background subtraction method based on fitting the correlation function in pseudorapidity over the range 1.0 < |Delta-eta| < 1.8.

## Key numbers
- Proton-proton collisions at center of mass energy sqrt(s) = 7 TeV were simulated with 1.1 billion events including diffractive events.
- The deuteron spectrum from PYTHIA+afterburner was scaled by a factor of 20 to match data, indicating the model overpredicts the yield by approximately this factor.
- The coalescence parameter B2 from both data and PYTHIA+afterburner shows similar behavior, increasing as a function of transverse momentum with similar slopes.
- Trigger particle momenta ranges studied: 3-5, 5-7, 7-9, and 9-11 GeV/c, with associated particles within pseudorapidity |eta| < 0.9.
- The ratio of deuteron to proton yields for jet-like correlations is higher than that for the underlying event and decreases as a function of pT^a/A (transverse momentum per nucleon).

## Context
This work extends coalescence models previously applied to heavy ion collisions (Gutbrod 1988, Kapusta 1980) to proton-proton collisions, using the same afterburner approach as Chen (2003). It addresses the open question from ALICE measurements (Sharma 2016) of why the d/p ratio increases with multiplicity from pp to p-Pb collisions but cannot be fully explained by either coalescence or thermal models alone. The proposed di-hadron correlation technique enables future data-driven tests of deuteron production mechanisms in both pp and heavy ion collisions, where correlations between protons and neutrons can arise from both jets and hydrodynamical flow.
