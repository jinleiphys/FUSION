# Efficiency corrections for factorial moments and cumulants of overlapping sets of particles

> In this note we discuss subtleties associated with the efficiency corrections for measurements of off-diagonal cumulants and factorial moments for a situation when one deals with overlapping sets of particles, such as correlations between numbers of protons and positively charged particles. In particular, we discuss the situation commonly encountered in heavy-ion experiments, where first all charges are reconstructed and then protons are selected from these charges by an additional particle identification procedure. We present the efficiency correction formulas for the case when the detection efficiencies follow a binomial distribution.

## Key claim

The standard efficiency correction formula for off-diagonal cumulants (dividing measured factorial moments by the product of efficiencies) fails when the two sets of particles overlap, such as protons and positively charged particles, because the self-correlation of protons within the charge set introduces an extra term that must be subtracted; the correction requires an additional term proportional to the mean number of identified protons times (1 − ε_q)/(ε_p ε_q), which vanishes only in the limit of perfect charge identification efficiency.

## Method

The authors derive efficiency correction formulas for off-diagonal cumulants of overlapping particle sets (e.g., protons and positive charges) assuming binomial detection efficiencies for both charge identification and particle identification. They treat the total positive charge as the sum of protons and other positive charges, fold the binomial efficiency distributions sequentially (charge identification first, then proton identification), and compute the measured factorial moments from the generating function. The resulting expressions are generalized to the case of non-uniform (momentum-dependent) efficiencies by partitioning phase space into bins and applying the appropriate correction separately for same-bin (overlapping) and different-bin (non-overlapping) pairs.

## Key numbers

* For realistic charge identification efficiencies ε_q = 0.6–0.8 in heavy-ion collisions, using the standard efficiency correction (instead of the corrected formula) overestimates the proton-charge covariance Cov(N_p, Q⁺) by 20–50%.
* In a Monte Carlo simulation with ⟨N₁⟩ = 90, ⟨N₂⟩ = 10, ⟨N₃⟩ = 200, proton identification efficiency ε_t = 0.7, and charge identification efficiency varied from 0.5 to 1.0 in steps of 0.05, the corrected formula (Eq. 25) recovers the true value Cov(N_p, Q⁺)/⟨N_p⟩ = 1.1 for all ε_q values, while the standard formula systematically overestimates it.
* The STAR experiment measures off-diagonal cumulants in the transverse momentum range 0.4 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c, split into two bins: 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c and 0.8 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the binomial efficiency correction framework for cumulants developed by Bzdak and Koch (2012, 2013) and Kitazawa and Asakawa (2016) to the previously unaddressed case of overlapping particle sets, such as proton-charge correlations. The results are directly applicable to ongoing measurements by the STAR collaboration (Adam et al., 2019) of off-diagonal cumulants (e.g., net-proton–net-charge correlations) as probes of the phase structure of strongly interacting matter, and enable proper extraction of true correlations from experimental data where particle identification is performed on a subset of reconstructed charged tracks.
