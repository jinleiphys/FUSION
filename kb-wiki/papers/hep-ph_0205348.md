# Magnetic catalysis and anisotropic confinement in QCD

> The expressions for dynamical masses of quarks in the chiral limit in QCD in a strong magnetic field are obtained. A low energy effective action for the corresponding Nambu-Goldstone bosons is derived and the values of their decay constants as well as the velocities are calculated. The existence of a threshold value of the number of colors $N^{thr}_c$, dividing the theories with essentially different dynamics, is established. For the number of colors $N_c \ll N^{thr}_c$, an anisotropic dynamics of confinement with the confinement scale much less than $Λ_{QCD}$ and a rich spectrum of light glueballs is realized. For $N_c$ of order $N^{thr}_c$ or larger, a conventional confinement dynamics takes place. It is found that the threshold value $N^{thr}_c$ grows rapidly with the magnetic field [$N^{thr}_c \gtrsim 100$ for $|eB| \gtrsim (1{GeV})^2$]. In contrast to QCD with a nonzero baryon density, there are no principal obstacles for checking these results and predictions in lattice computer simulations.

## Key claim

In QCD with a strong magnetic field, the dynamics of chiral symmetry breaking and confinement become anisotropic and can be separated, with a threshold number of colors \(N_c^{thr}\) dividing two regimes: for \(N_c \ll N_c^{thr}\) the confinement scale is much smaller than \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) and a rich spectrum of light glueballs appears, while for \(N_c\) of order \(N_c^{thr}\) or larger conventional confinement dynamics is recovered.

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation for the quark propagator in a strong magnetic field using a "strong-magnetic-field-loop improved rainbow approximation," where the vertex is taken as bare and the gluon propagator is computed at one-loop order including only the fermion contribution to the polarization operator. They derived the low-energy effective action for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons using the Pagels-Stokar approximation and the Ward identities for axial currents. The low-energy effective action for gluons was constructed by incorporating the polarization effects from the magnetic field, yielding an anisotropic dielectric constant.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical quark mass squared is given by \(m_q^2 \simeq 2 C_1 |e_q B| (c_q \alpha_s)^{2/3} \exp\left[-\frac{4 N_c \pi}{\alpha_s (N_c^2-1) \ln(C_2 / c_q \alpha_s)}\right]\), with \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) of order 1 in the leading approximation.
- The gluon mass is \(M_g^2 = (2 N_u + N_d) \frac{\alpha_s}{3\pi} |e B|\).
- The decay constants of Nambu-Goldstone bosons are \(f_u^2 = \frac{N_c}{6\pi^2} |e B|\) and \(f_d^2 = \frac{N_c}{12\pi^2} |e B|\).
- The threshold number of colors satisfies \(N_c^{thr} \gtrsim 100\) for \(|e B| \gtrsim (1 \text{ GeV})^2\).
- The dynamical mass of quarks in a wide range of strong magnetic fields \(\Lambda_{QCD}^2 \ll |e B| \lesssim (10 \text{ TeV})^2\) remains smaller than the vacuum dynamical mass \(m_{dyn}^{(0)} \simeq 300 \text{ MeV}\).
- For large \(N_c\), the quark mass is \(m_{q,\infty}^2 = C |e_q B| \exp\left[-\pi \left(\frac{\pi}{\tilde{\alpha}_s}\right)^{1/2}\right]\).
- The new confinement scale is estimated as \(\lambda_{QCD} \simeq m_d \left(\frac{\Lambda_{QCD}}{\sqrt{|e B|}}\right)^{b/b_0}\).

## Context

This work extends the magnetic catalysis phenomenon previously studied in QED by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (2000) and in QCD by other authors, establishing a rigorous first-principles treatment for QCD in strong magnetic fields. It contrasts with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model results of Kawati (1983), where the chiral condensate grows logarithmically with magnetic field, by showing that asymptotic freedom and gluon screening lead to suppression of the chiral condensate in a wide range of fields. The results enable lattice QCD simulations to test these predictions, unlike the case of QCD at nonzero baryon density where sign problems arise.
