# Renormalisation of the low-energy constants of chiral perturbation theory from loops with dynamical vector mesons

> Starting from a relativistic Lagrangian for pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons and vector mesons in the antisymmetric tensor representation, a one-loop calculation is performed to pin down the divergent structures that appear for the effective low-energy action at chiral orders Q^2 and Q^4. The corresponding renormalisation-scale dependences of all low-energy constants up to chiral order Q^4 are determined. Calculations are carried out for both the pseudoscalar octet and the pseudoscalar nonet, the latter in the framework of chiral perturbation theory in the limit of a large number of colours.

## Key claim

Starting from a relativistic Lagrangian for pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons and vector mesons in the antisymmetric tensor representation, the authors perform a one-loop calculation to determine the divergent structures and renormalisation-scale dependences of all low-energy constants up to chiral order Q^4, but only for a limited set of interaction terms (the chiralised three-flavour versions of the phenomenologically known rho-2pi and rho-v couplings), not for a full effective theory of pseudoscalar and vector mesons.

## Method

The authors use non-perturbative path-integral methods to expand the action around classical pseudoscalar fields, treating vector mesons as pure fluctuations. They determine the matrix operator D from the second functional derivative of the combined LO chiral perturbation theory and vector-meson Lagrangian, then compute the one-loop contribution Z_one_loop = (1/2) i log(det D). The divergences are isolated using a slightly modified MS-bar scheme according to Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), and the calculations are carried out for both the pseudoscalar octet and the pseudoscalar nonet (the latter in the large-N_c limit of chiral perturbation theory).

## Key numbers

* Vector-meson mass m_V = 776 MeV is used as a common mass for all vector mesons in the nonet.
* The "intrinsic" high-energy scale of chiral perturbation theory is roughly 4 pi F_pi = 1.2 GeV.
* The "external" high-energy scale is given by the vector-meson mass m_V = 0.77 GeV (the mass of the omega and rho mesons).
* The kaon mass (dynamical scale) is about 500 MeV, while the K* mass (degree of freedom integrated out) is about 900 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculations of Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) for pure chiral perturbation theory by including dynamical vector mesons in the antisymmetric tensor representation. It is complementary to calculations of selected n-point functions by Kampf (2009) for vector-meson properties and by Pich (2008, 2010), Sanz Cillero (2009), and Guo (2014) for some low-energy constants, though those works made additional assumptions about high-energy behaviour. The present feasibility study enables future work (MassPaper, in progress) to address the quantitative importance of non-local logarithmic structures from vector-meson loops for observables like pseudoscalar masses and decay constants.
