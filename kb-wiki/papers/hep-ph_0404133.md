# Enhancement of Critical Slowing Down in Chiral Phase Transition -- Langevin Dynamics Approach --

> We derive the linear Langevin equation that describes the behavior of the fluctuations of the order parameter of the chiral phase transition above the critical temperature by applying the projection operator method to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature and density. The Langevin equation relaxes exhibiting oscillation, reveals thermalization and converges to the equilibrium state consistent with the mean-field approximation as time goes on. With the help of this Langevin equation, we further investigate the relaxation of the critical fluctuations. The relaxation time of the critical fluctuations increases at speed as the temperature approaches toward the critical temperature because of the critical slowing down. The critical slowing down is enhanced as the chemical potential increases because of the Pauli blocking. Furthermore, we find another enhancement of the critical slowing down around the tricritical point.

## Key claim

The relaxation time of critical fluctuations in the chiral phase transition increases more rapidly as the temperature approaches the critical temperature, and this critical slowing down is further enhanced both by increasing chemical potential (due to Pauli blocking) and by the presence of a tricritical point, as derived from a linear Langevin equation obtained via the projection operator method applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model above the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors applied the projection operator method (Nakajima-Zwanzig-Mori formalism) to the two-flavor, three-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit at finite temperature and density, choosing the order parameter (the scalar density fluctuation δσ) as the sole gross variable. They derived the memory function by expanding the coarse-grained time evolution operator in terms of the normal time evolution operator, retaining only the ring diagram contribution (random phase approximation) for the interaction part, and then obtained a linear Langevin equation for the critical fluctuations above the critical temperature. The noise term was determined to satisfy the fluctuation-dissipation theorem of the second kind.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant was set to g = 5.01 GeV⁻² and the three-dimensional cutoff to Λ = 650 MeV to reproduce the pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV and the chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = (−250 MeV)³ in the chiral limit.
* The memory function Γ(0,t) was computed at chemical potential μ = 200 MeV for temperatures T − Tc = 10 MeV, 30 MeV, and 80 MeV, showing convergence to finite values at late time (indicating the need for renormalization).
* The system has Nc = 3 colors and Nf = 2 flavors.

## Context

This work extends the projection operator method to finite temperature and density for the chiral phase transition, building on prior applications at vanishing chemical potential by Koide and Maruyama (2004). It provides a Langevin equation framework for studying critical dynamics near the chiral critical point, complementing hydrodynamic models (which break down near critical points due to diverging correlation lengths) and enabling investigation of critical slowing down enhancement at high chemical potential and around the tricritical point, as previously discussed in mode coupling theory for condensed matter systems by Kawasaki (1970) and Onuki (2002).
