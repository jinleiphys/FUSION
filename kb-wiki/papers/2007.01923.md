# Diffractive two-meson electroproduction with a nucleon and deuteron target

> The diffractive electro- or photo-production of two mesons separated by a large rapidity gap gives access to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in a very specific way. First, these reactions allow to easily access the chiral-odd transversity quark GPDs by selecting one of the produced vector meson to be transversely polarized. Second, they are only sensitive to the so-called ERBL region where GPDs are not much constrained by forward quark distributions. Third, the skewness parameter $ξ$ is not related to the Bjorken $x_\text{Bj}$ variable, but to the size of the rapidity gap. We analyze different channels ($ρ_L^0\,ρ_{L/T}, ρ^0_L\,ω_{L/T}$ and $ρ^0_L\,π$ production) on nucleon and deuteron targets. The analysis is performed in the kinematical domain where a large momentum transfer from the photon to the diffractively produced vector meson introduces a hard scale (the virtuality of the exchanged hard Pomeron). This enables the description of the hadronic part of the process in the framework of collinear factorization of GPDs. We show that the unpolarized cross sections depend very much on the parameterizations of both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark distributions of the nucleon, as well as on the shape of the meson distribution amplitudes. The rates are shown to be in the range of the capacities of a future electron-ion collider.

## Key claim
The diffractive electroproduction of two mesons separated by a large rapidity gap provides access to chiral-odd transversity quark GPDs and probes the ERBL region of GPDs, with the skewness parameter determined by the rapidity gap size rather than Bjorken x, and the unpolarized cross sections depend strongly on the parameterizations of both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark distributions as well as meson distribution amplitudes.

## Method
The authors calculate scattering amplitudes for diffractive two-meson electroproduction (ρ_L^0 ρ_L/T, ρ_L^0 ω_L/T, and ρ_L^0 π) on nucleon and deuteron targets using collinear factorization of GPDs in the hybrid factorization approach, where a hard scale is provided by the virtuality of the exchanged hard Pomeron. The analysis employs different nucleon GPD models (VGG99, GK16, MMS13 for chiral-even; Goloskokov et al. for chiral-odd) and two meson distribution amplitude parameterizations (asymptotic 6z(1-z) and holographic 8/π√(z(1-z))), with deuteron GPDs computed via a convolution model using the AV18 deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers
- Cross sections are calculated at renormalization/factorization scale μ_R = μ_F = 1 GeV, with fixed values of q_ρ^2 = 2 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 for model comparison plots
- The skewness ξ is related to the two-meson invariant mass s₁ via ξ ≈ s₁/(2s - s₁), yielding ξ ≈ 0.5 when s₁ = 2s/3 and ξ ≈ 0.33 when s₁ = s/2
- For the deuteron target, the active nucleon skewness ξ_N relates to deuteron skewness ξ as ξ_N ≈ 2ξ/(1-ξ) due to the convolution model
- The minimum momentum transfer is given by (-t)_min = 4ξ²m_D²/(1-ξ²) for deuteron and (-t)_min = 4ξ²m_N²/(1-ξ²) for nucleon targets
- The cross sections are independent of the total photon-hadron energy s, with the s-dependence canceling between the amplitude and phase space factors

## Context
This work extends the pioneering study of Ivanov et al. (2002) on diffractive ρ_L^0 ρ⁺ production and the transversity-focused analysis of Enberg et al. (2006), revisiting the phenomenology with updated GPD models and adding coherent deuteron target channels. The results enable experimental planning at future electron-ion colliders (EIC, LHeC, EicC) by showing that predicted rates are within the range of these facilities' capacities, and provide a framework for extracting poorly constrained chiral-odd transversity GPDs and probing the ERBL region where GPDs are not constrained by forward quark distributions.
