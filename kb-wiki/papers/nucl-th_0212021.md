# The relativistic nuclear dynamics for the SU(3) Goldstone bosons of chiral QCD

> In this habilitation theses the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian is applied to antikaon and hyperon propagation in cold nuclear matter. The self consistent many-body dynamics relies on a detailed analysis of the pion, kaon and antikaon-nucleon scattering data with particular emphasis on constraints set by unitarity, covariance, causality and crossing symmetry. The work is based in large parts on collaborations with E.E. Kolomeitsev and C.L. Korpa and part of the results are published already. Unpublished results concern a discussion of the nature of baryon resonances in the context of coupled channel dynamics and a discussion of antikaon production in heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies. A new mechanism which drives the system towards equilibrium conditions is proposed. The theses contains also new results on the properties of antikaons and hyperon resonances in asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) Lagrangian can be applied to antikaon and hyperon propagation in cold nuclear matter using a self-consistent many-body dynamics that respects unitarity, covariance, causality, and crossing symmetry, but the quantitative evaluation of the antikaon spectral function in nuclear matter remains an outstanding problem because reliable subthreshold antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes are needed and different analyses may differ by as much as a factor of two in the region of the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Method

The relativistic chiral SU(3) Lagrangian including an explicit baryon decuplet resonance field with J^P = 3/2^+ and a phenomenological baryon nonet d-wave resonance field with J^P = 3/2^- was applied to meson-baryon scattering. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the scattering amplitude was solved with an interaction kernel truncated at chiral order Q^3, using a covariant projector formalism with a subtraction scheme rather than a cutoff scheme. The s-, p- and d-wave contributions with J = 1/2, 3/2 in the scattering amplitude were considered, and the scheme considered the in-medium mixing of s-, p- and d-waves in a self-consistent manner for the first time.

- Pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 495 MeV, nucleon mass m_N = 940 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV
- Empirical isospin averaged pion-nucleon scattering length a_piN = -0.01 fm
- Empirical K^+ nucleon scattering length a_K^+N = -0.3 fm
- Empirical K^- nucleon scattering length a_K^-N = (-0.6 + i 1.1) fm
- Weinberg-Tomozawa prediction for a_K^-N = +0.9 fm
- K^+p scattering length a_K^+p = 0.28-0.34 fm
- K^+n scattering length a_K^+n = 0.1 fm
- Up and down current quark masses m_u,d = 10 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 10 x m_u,d
- Chiral scale of 1 GeV
- SPS energies per nucleon 40 GeV < E/N < 200 GeV, GSI energies up to 2 GeV per nucleon
- Nuclear saturation density reached at GSI up to three times the density of nuclear saturation
- Temperatures at GSI typically smaller than 100 MeV, at SPS exceeding 200 MeV

## Context

This habilitation thesis extends the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel approach previously applied by Kaiser, Siegel and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998) to antikaon-nucleon scattering, but improves upon those works by including both s- and p-waves systematically, using a subtraction scheme rather than channel-dependent cutoff parameters that break SU(3) symmetry. The work enables improved transport model simulations of heavy-ion reactions by providing in-medium reaction rates and spectral functions for antikaons and hyperon resonances, and addresses the open challenge of understanding subthreshold antikaon production observed by the KaoS collaboration at GSI.
