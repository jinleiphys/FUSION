# Multi-Nucleon Transfer in Central Collisions of $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U

> Quantal diffusion mechanism of nucleon exchange is studied in the central collisions of $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U in the framework of the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach. For bombarding energies considered in this work, the di-nuclear structure is maintained during the collision. Hence, it is possible to describe nucleon exchange as a diffusion process for mass and charge asymmetry. Quantal neutron and proton diffusion coefficients, including memory effects, are extracted from the SMF approach and the primary fragment distributions are calculated.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach can describe multi-nucleon transfer in central collisions of \(^{238}\)U + \(^{238}\)U as a quantal diffusion process, but only for bombarding energies where the di-nuclear structure is maintained throughout the collision; the resulting primary fragment distributions are calculated without any fitting parameters.

## Method

The authors employed the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, which extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) description by incorporating mean-field fluctuations through an ensemble of Slater determinants. Quantal neutron and proton diffusion coefficients, including memory effects, were extracted from the SMF approach using the occupied single-particle wave functions from TDHF solutions with the SLy4d Skyrme functional. The coupled differential equations for the variances and co-variance of neutron and proton distributions were solved, and the primary fragment distributions were determined as correlated Gaussians in the N-Z plane.

## Key numbers

- For side-side collisions at \(E_{c.m.}=900\) MeV, the final mass distribution variance is \(\sigma_{AA}(t)=12.9\).
- For tip-tip collisions at \(E_{c.m.}=1050\) MeV, the final mass distribution variance is \(\sigma_{AA}(t)=12.0\).
- The final total kinetic energy (TKE) is 620 MeV for side-side and 634 MeV for tip-tip collisions.
- The excitation energy of each \(^{238}\)U nucleus after symmetric fragmentation is \(E^*=140\) MeV for side-side and \(E^*=208\) MeV for tip-tip collisions.
- The memory time is approximately \(\tau_0 \approx 20\) fm/c, based on an average nucleon flow speed of \(u_0 \approx 0.05c\).
- The angle between the mean one-sided drift path and the N-axis is \(\theta \approx 30^\circ\) in both collision geometries.
- In side-side collisions, the average coefficients are \(\alpha \approx 0.035\) and \(\beta \approx 0.007\) during the time interval from 200 fm/c to 800 fm/c.
- In tip-tip collisions, the average coefficients are \(\alpha \approx 0.039\) and \(\beta \approx 0.009\) during the time interval from 200 fm/c to 700 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the quantal diffusion approach previously applied by Ayik et al. (2016) to several symmetric heavy-ion systems at bombarding energies slightly below fusion barriers, now specifically to the \(^{238}\)U + \(^{238}\)U system in both side-side and tip-tip configurations. The results provide a parameter-free prediction of primary fragment mass and charge distributions that may guide experimental investigations of heavy neutron-rich isotopes, though the authors note that de-excitation calculations of the primary fragments (including particle evaporation and fission) are not performed in this work.
