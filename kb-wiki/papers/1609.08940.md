# Three-body system of $ππΣ_c$

> The existence of near-threshold charmed baryon $Λ_c(2595)^+$ implies that the pion and the lightest, isospin-$1$ charmed baryon $Σ_c$ interact very strongly at extremely low energies. Using the two-flavor version of heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory, I explore the direct consequences of this strong force by investigating whether the $Σ_c$ can trap two very soft pions to form any visible hadronic states. The answer is positive. It is found without tuning any free parameters or ultraviolet cutoff that the state in question, with quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 1({\frac{1}{2}}^+)$, presents itself as a resonance pole only a few MeVs away from the $ππΣ_c$ threshold. Subleading corrections are estimated with power-counting arguments, and the smallness of pion momenta is found to facilitate the reliability of the analysis. Because of its proximity in mass, this excited $Σ_c$ resonance is speculated to be related to the broad resonance labeled as $Λ_c^+(2765)$.

# Three-body system of ππΣ_c

## Key claim

Using two-flavor heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory without tuning any free parameters or ultraviolet cutoff, a Σ_c resonance with quantum numbers I(J^P) = 1(1/2)^+ is predicted to exist as a pole only a few MeV away from the ππΣ_c threshold, and is speculated to be related to the broad Λ_c^+(2765) resonance.

## Method

The author uses a specialized two-flavor version of heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT) to study the ππΣ_c three-body system, approximating the Σ_c as stable due to its small width (~2 MeV). The πΛ_c^* scattering amplitude is computed by resumming u-channel Σ_c-exchange diagrams, leading to an integral equation that is solved numerically after analytic continuation into the complex energy plane. The analysis exploits the small pion momentum scale Q ~ 20 MeV set by the near-threshold Λ_c(2595)^+ resonance, and subleading corrections from pion-pion interactions, Weinberg-Tomozawa terms, and πΛ_c^+ intermediate states are estimated via power counting.

## Key numbers

- The Λ_c(2595)^+ resonance lies δ ≡ Δ - m_π ≃ 1 MeV above the πΣ_c threshold, with Δ ≃ 139 MeV
- The characteristic pion three-momentum around the Λ_c(2595)^+ resonance is Q ~ √(2δ m_π) ≃ 20 MeV
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV
- The small parameter ε ≡ m_π^2/(4π f_π^2) = 0.18
- Using parameters from Chiladze (1997): M_{Λ_c^*} - M_{Λ_c^+} = 308.7 MeV, h^2 = 3/2 × 0.30, the pole position is M_{newΣ} - (M_{Σ_c} + 2m_π) = (4.00 - 5.72i) MeV
- Using parameters from Aaltonen (2011): M_{Λ_c^*} - M_{Λ_c^+} = 305.8 MeV, h^2 = 3/2 × 0.36, the pole position is M_{newΣ} - (M_{Σ_c} + 2m_π) = (-0.45 - 0.02i) MeV
- The isospin-averaged pion mass used is m_π = 138.0 MeV, and M_{Σ_c} - M_{Λ_c^+} = 167.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of the fine-tuned πΣ_c system from Long (2015), which showed that chiral symmetry requires only one fine-tuning (the pion mass) to place Λ_c(2595)^+ so close to threshold. The analysis echoes earlier efforts to investigate pion trapping in finite nuclear matter by Dyson (1964), Gal (2014), Akaishi (2002), and Kanada-En'yo (2008), as well as in infinite nuclear matter by Migdal (1990) and Kaplan (1986). The predicted resonance could be identified with the broad Λ_c^+(2765) peak observed by CLEO in decays into Λ_c^+ π^- π^+ (Artuso, 2000), and the decay mechanism is consistent with that observation. Future work (Lv and Long, ongoing) will confront the invariant mass spectrum data more carefully and determine whether Λ_c^+(2765) is this state or an overlap with its spin-3/2 partner.
