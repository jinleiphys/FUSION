# Electric and magnetic dipole strength in 112,114,116,118,120,124Sn

> Inelastic proton scattering experiments were performed at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics, Osaka, with a 295 MeV beam covering laboratory angles 0°-6° and excitation energies 6-22 MeV. Cross sections due to E1 and M1 excitations were extracted with a multipole decomposition analysis and then converted to reduced transition probabilities with the "virtual photon method" for E1 and the "unit cross section method" for M1 excitations, respectively. Including a theory-aided correction for the high excitation energy region not covered experimentally, the electric dipole polarizability was determined from the E1 strength distributions. Total photoabsorption cross sections derived from the E1 and M1 strength distributions show significant differences compared to those from previous ($γ$,xn) experiments in the energy region of the isocvector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR). The widths of the IVGDR deduced from the present data with a Lorentz parameterization show an approximately constant value of about 4.5 MeV in contrast to the large variations between isotopes observed in previous work. The IVGDR centroid energies are in good correspondence to expectations from systematics of their mass dependence. Furthermore, a study of the dependence of the IVGDR energies on bulk matter properties is presented. The E1 strengths below neutron threshold show fair agreement with results from ($γ$,$γ$') experiments on 112,116,120,124Sn in the energy region between 6 and 7 MeV. At higher excitation energies large differences are observed pointing to a different nature of the excited states with small ground state branching ratios. The isovector spin-M1 strengths exhibit a broad distribution between 6 and 12 MeV in all studied nuclei.

## Key claim

The widths of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in stable tin isotopes, deduced from inelastic proton scattering data with a Lorentz parameterization, show an approximately constant value of about 4.5 MeV, in contrast to the large variations between isotopes observed in previous (γ,xn) experiments.

## Method

Inelastic proton scattering experiments were performed at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics, Osaka, using a 295 MeV unpolarized proton beam covering laboratory angles 0°–6° and excitation energies 6–22 MeV. Cross sections due to E1 and M1 excitations were extracted via a multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) and converted to reduced transition probabilities using the "virtual photon method" for E1 and the "unit cross section method" for M1 excitations. A theory-aided correction was applied for the high excitation energy region not covered experimentally to determine the electric dipole polarizability from the E1 strength distributions.

## Key numbers

- IVGDR widths deduced from a Lorentz parameterization show an approximately constant value of about 4.5 MeV across the tin isotope chain.
- The experiments covered excitation energies from 6 to 22 MeV with an incident proton beam energy of 295 MeV.
- Energy resolution achieved was 30–40 keV (FWHM) across the two experimental campaigns.
- Target areal densities ranged from 3.4 to 7.5 mg/cm² for the main tin targets.
- The enrichment of ¹¹⁸Sn was determined to be 86(7)% by interpolation of IVGDR systematics.
- The enrichment of the thin ¹¹²Sn target was determined to be 90.2(1.4)% by normalization to a thicker calibration target.

## Context

This work provides a systematic study of electric and magnetic dipole strength across the stable even-mass tin isotope chain (¹¹²,¹¹⁴,¹¹⁶,¹¹⁸,¹²⁰,¹²⁴Sn), extending previous (p,p′) measurements on ¹²⁰Sn by Krumbholz (2015) and Hashimoto (2015). The results reveal significant differences in total photoabsorption cross sections compared to prior (γ,xn) experiments by Berman (1975), Fultz (1969), Lepretre (1974), Sorokin (1974, 1975), and Utsunomiya (2009, 2011), particularly in the IVGDR region. The data enable new constraints on energy density functionals (EDFs) for describing dipole polarizability across the nuclear chart, from which properties of the symmetry energy can be derived, and provide systematics of the isovector spin-M1 strength in heavy nuclei where data were previously limited to ⁹⁰Zr by Rusev (2013) and ²⁰⁸Pb by Laszewski (1988) and Birkhan (2016).
