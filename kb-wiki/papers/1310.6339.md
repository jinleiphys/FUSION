# Disconnected quark loop contributions to nucleon observables in lattice QCD

> We perform a high statistics calculation of disconnected fermion loops on Graphics Processing Units for a range of nucleon matrix elements extracted using lattice QCD. The isoscalar electromagnetic and axial vector form factors, the sigma-terms and the momentum fraction and helicity are among the quantities we evaluate. We compare the disconnected contributions to the connected ones and give the physical implications on nucleon observables that probe its structure.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that disconnected quark loop contributions to nucleon observables can be computed with high statistics using GPU-accelerated lattice QCD, but the results are conditional on the specific ensemble used (pion mass ~370 MeV, lattice spacing 0.082 fm) and require source-sink separations of at least 1.5 fm to control excited-state contamination.

## Method
The authors used the truncated solver method (TSM) combined with the one-end trick on a single N_f=2+1+1 twisted mass fermion ensemble with lattice size 32^3×64. They computed disconnected fermion loops for scalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor, and one-derivative operators using N_HP=24 high-precision and N_LP=500 (light) or N_LP=300 (strange/charm) stochastic noise vectors, analyzing 2,300 gauge configurations with ~150,000 effective measurements through both plateau and summation methods.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.082(1)(4) fm (extracted from nucleon mass)
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 370 MeV (a m_π = 0.15518(21)(33))
- Lattice volume: 32^3 × 64, giving m_π L ≈ 5
- Renormalization constants in MS-bar at μ=2 GeV: Z_A = 0.757(3), Z_T = 0.769(1), Z_P = 0.506(4)
- For the isoscalar axial charge g_A^{u+d}: a non-zero disconnected contribution was observed, requiring source-sink separation t_s/a ≥ 8 for convergence
- For the strange sigma term σ_s: non-zero result obtained, requiring source-sink separation of at least 18-20 time slices (~1.5 fm) for plateau stabilization
- For the charm sigma term σ_c and charm axial charge g_A^c: results consistent with zero, only upper bounds obtainable
- For the isoscalar tensor charge g_T^{u+d}: disconnected contribution very small with ~90% statistical error
- For the isoscalar momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_{u+d}: value consistent with zero
- For the isoscalar helicity moment ⟨x⟩_{Δu+Δd}: non-zero value obtained, requiring t_s/a > 8

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies that typically neglected disconnected quark loop contributions, introducing an uncontrolled systematic uncertainty (Alexandrou, 2011). It builds on algorithmic improvements including the one-end trick (Boucaud, 2008; Michael, 2007; Dinter, 2012), dilution techniques (Alexandrou, 2012), and the truncated solver method (Alexandrou, 2012; Collins, 2007; Bali, 2009). The results enable future studies to include disconnected contributions in nucleon structure calculations and to investigate quark mass dependence, with the authors noting that non-zero charm content was reported near the chiral limit by Gong (2013).
