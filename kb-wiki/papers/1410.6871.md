# Multiquark Cluster Form Factors In the Relativistic Harmonic Oscillator Model

> A QCD multiquark cluster system is studied in the relativistic harmonic oscillator potential model (RHOPM), and the electromagnetic form factors of the pion, proton and deuteron in the RHOPM are predicted. The calculated theoretical results are then compared with existing experimental data, finding very good agreement between the theoretical predictions and experimental data for these three target particles. We claim that this model can be applied to study QCD hadronic properties, particularly neutron properties, and to find six-quark cluster and/or nine-quark cluster probabilities in light nuclei such as helium $^{3}He$ and tritium $^{3}H$. This is a problem of particular importance and interest in quark nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The relativistic harmonic oscillator potential model (RHOPM) predicts electromagnetic form factors for the pion, proton, and deuteron that agree very well with experimental data, provided only one arbitrary parameter, \(g_E(q^2)=1.0\), is applied; the model is then claimed to be extendable to study neutron properties and to extract six-quark and nine-quark cluster probabilities in light nuclei such as \(^{3}\text{He}\) and \(^{3}\text{H}\).

## Method

The authors model an N-valence-quark cluster system where quarks move in a relativistic harmonic oscillator potential, solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a Takabayashi condition to remove nonphysical oscillations. They derive an analytical expression for the electromagnetic form factor \(F_{Nq}(Q^2)\) of an N-quark bound state, then specialize it to the two-quark (pion), three-quark (nucleon), and six-quark (deuteron) cases. The theoretical form factors are compared with existing experimental data from Refs. [10-12] for the proton, pion, and deuteron.

## Key numbers

*   The deuteron six-quark cluster probability, \( \sin^2(\theta) \), is estimated to be about \(7\%\), obtained by comparing the calculated \(F_{6q}(Q^2)\sin^2(\theta)\) with the experimental Rosenbluth separation form factor \(A(Q^2)\) at high energies.
*   The nucleon form factor is given by \(F_N(Q^2) = [1 + Q^2/(2M_N^2)]^{-2} \exp[ - (1/(2\alpha_N)) Q^2 / (1 + Q^2/(2M_N^2)) ]\).
*   The pion form factor is given by \(F_\pi(Q^2) = [1 + Q^2/(2M_\pi^2)]^{-1} \exp[ - (1/(4\alpha_\pi)) Q^2 / (1 + Q^2/(2M_\pi^2)) ]\).
*   The deuteron form factor is given by \(F_D(Q^2) = [1 + Q^2/(2M_D^2)]^{-5} \exp[ - (5/(4\alpha_D)) Q^2 / (1 + Q^2/(2M_D^2)) ]\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic harmonic oscillator model, previously applied to hadron form factors in Refs. [5-7], to multiquark clusters including the six-quark system of the deuteron. It provides a simple analytical expression for form factors that can be used to extract the probability of six-quark and nine-quark clusters in light nuclei, a problem of interest in quark nuclear physics. The model enables future studies of neutron properties and the search for multiquark cluster probabilities in nuclei such as \(^{3}\text{He}\) and \(^{3}\text{H}\).
