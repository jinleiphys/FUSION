# Primordial black-hole formation and heavy r-process element synthesis from the cosmological QCD transition. Two aspects of an inhomogeneous early Universe

> We review the role of primordial black holes (PBHs) for illuminating the dark ages of the cosmological evolution and as dark matter candidates. We elucidate the role of phase transitions for primordial black hole formation in the early Universe and focus our attention to the cosmological QCD phase transition within a recent microscopical model. We explore the impact of physics beyond the Standard Model on the cosmic equation of state and the probability distribution for the formation of primordial black holes which serve as dark matter (DM) candidates. We argue that besides primordial black holes also droplet-like quark-gluon plasma inhomogeneities may become gravitationally stabilized for a sufficiently long epoch to distill baryon number and form nuclear matter droplets which upon their evaporation may enrich the cosmos locally with heavy $r$-process elements already in the early Universe.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the cosmological QCD phase transition, modeled with a microscopical approach rather than lattice QCD, can produce primordial black holes (PBHs) with a mass distribution peaked near 2 solar masses, and that adding a hypothetical X17 boson (mass ~17 MeV) beyond the Standard Model enhances the formation of intermediate-mass PBHs in the range 100–10^5 solar masses, which could serve as seeds for supermassive black holes observed at high redshift by JWST.

## Method

The authors compute the cosmic equation of state (EoS) for temperatures 1–1300 MeV using a microscopical model that decomposes the thermodynamic potential into a quark-gluon plasma sector and a Mott hadron resonance gas sector, including quark flavors up to bottom. They then translate the EoS into a PBH mass distribution via the critical density threshold for collapse (δ_c) derived from Musco & Miller (2012), assuming Gaussian inflationary fluctuations with spectral index n_s = 0.97, and normalize the PBH fraction to 10% of dark matter. The same calculation is repeated after adding a scalar X17 boson with mass 17 MeV to the particle inventory.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical QCD transition temperature: T_c = 156.5 MeV (from lattice QCD, HotQCD 2018)
- Temperature range considered: T ∈ [1; 1300] MeV
- Horizon mass range corresponding to this temperature range: M ∈ [3.4×10^−2, 1.5×10^5] M_⊙
- X17 boson mass: m_X17 = 17 MeV; onset temperature for its contribution: T_X17 ∼ m_X17/6 ∼ 3 MeV
- Spectral index: n_s = 0.97 (from Planck 2018 CMB data)
- Target PBH dark matter fraction: f_PBH = 0.1
- In the SM+X17 scenario, the QCD peak (1–10 M_⊙) contains 86.44% of PBH abundance (vs. 91.53% in SM); the X17 mass range (1000–3.73×10^4 M_⊙) contains 0.82% (vs. 0.28% in SM)
- The optimal fluctuation amplitude A_opt differs by approximately 1% between SM and SM+X17 for the same target f_PBH

## Context

This work extends the line of PBH formation studies at the QCD transition by Byrnes (2018), Carr (2019, 2020), Bödeker (2020), Escrivà (2022), and Musco (2023), but uses a microscopical model (Blaschke 2023) instead of lattice QCD data (Borsanyi 2016). It adds for the first time the putative X17 particle (Krasznahorkay 2015, 2021, 2022) to the cosmic EoS and PBH mass distribution. The paper also revives Witten's (1984) scenario of baryon number distillation from quark-gluon plasma droplets to propose an early-Universe site for heavy r-process element synthesis, complementing the sudden freeze-out scenario (2024) and addressing open questions in galactic archeology (2025).
