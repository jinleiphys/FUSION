# Nuclear Reactions and Superfluid Time Dependent Density Functional Theory

## Key claim

The extension of Time Dependent Density Functional Theory (TDDFT) to superfluid systems via the Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) provides a framework for describing nuclear reactions and large amplitude collective motion, but the adiabatic approximation (which neglects memory effects) prevents proper description of dissipation processes except for one-body dissipation, and the formalism cannot easily extract observables requiring knowledge of the many-body wave function.

## Method

The paper reviews the formal extension of static DFT to time-dependent superfluid systems by introducing an anomalous density as the superconducting order parameter and deriving time-dependent Kohn-Sham equations that take the structure of time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (TDBdG) or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations. The SLDA approach uses a local pairing field with renormalized coupling constant and momentum cutoff, solved on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with periodic boundary conditions using fifth-order predictor-corrector-modifier Adams-Bashforth-Milne time propagation. For nuclear systems, the equations are solved for both protons and neutrons coupled through potentials depending on local densities, currents, spin densities, and kinetic energy densities derived from Skyrme-type functionals.

## Key numbers

- Time step of order 0.07–0.08 fm/c ensures numerical accuracy for evolution intervals up to about 10,000 fm/c
- Box sizes up to 50³–60³ with lattice constant of 1 fm allow dynamics of arbitrarily heavy nuclei
- Relative truncation error in the Adams-Bashforth-Milne method is chosen between 10⁻⁷ and 10⁻¹⁵
- In ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U collisions at 700 MeV/nucleon, collision time was of order 10 fm/c for impact parameters of order the nucleus diameter
- For impact parameters 14–20 fm in ²³⁸U Coulomb excitation, approximately half of total energy was transferred to internal degrees of freedom
- Goldhaber-Teller model predicts only 40–60% of the TDSLDA energy transferred to internal degrees of freedom for impact parameters 14–20 fm
- Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance vibrational frequency estimated as ω ≈ 80 MeV A⁻¹/³ for spherical nuclei

## Context

This work extends the SLDA formalism developed in a series of papers (Bulgac and collaborators, 2002–2013) from static to time-dependent phenomena, building on the Runge-Gross theorem (1984) for time-dependent DFT and the resolution of the causality paradox (Rajagopal, 1996; van Leeuwen, 1998, 2001). The approach has been applied to Coulomb excitation of deformed nuclei including ¹⁷²Yb, ¹⁸⁸Os, and ²³⁸U (Stetcu and collaborators, 2011, 2015), showing good agreement with experimental photoabsorption cross sections but inability to reproduce resonance widths due to the adiabatic approximation's neglect of dissipation. The framework enables future studies of large amplitude nuclear motion including induced fission, though it is 2–3 orders of magnitude more computationally demanding than standard TDDFT.
