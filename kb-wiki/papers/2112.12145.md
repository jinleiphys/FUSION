# Neutron stars with crossover to color superconducting quark matter

> We follow the idea that the QCD phase diagram may be described by a crossover from a hadron resonance gas to perturbative QCD using the switch function ansatz of Albright, Kapusta and Young [1]. While the switch function could be calibrated at vanishing baryon chemical potential with data from lattice QCD simulations, it has been suggested recently by Kapusta and Welle [2] that in the zero temperature limit, the switch function parameter $μ_0$ could be constrained by neutron star phenomenology, in particular by massive pulsars like PSR J0740+6620 with a mass exceeding $2~M_\odot$. In this work we demonstrate that this procedure to constrain the QCD phase diagram does crucially depend on the fact that cold dense quark matter is very likely in a color superconducting state.

# Neutron stars with crossover to color superconducting quark matter

## Key claim
The procedure to constrain the QCD phase diagram by using neutron star phenomenology to fix the switch function parameter μ₀ depends crucially on whether cold dense quark matter is in a color superconducting state; only a model with a nonperturbatively enhanced strong coupling (a₄ = 0.3), a diquark pairing gap of Δ = 120 MeV, and a low switch parameter μ₀ = 1200 MeV can simultaneously satisfy both the mass-radius constraint from PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817.

## Method
The authors construct a crossover equation of state (EoS) by interpolating between a nonlinear Walecka (NLW) model for pure neutron matter and a color-flavor-locking (CFL) color superconducting quark matter EoS using the switch function S(μ) = exp[-(μ₀/μ)^r] from Albright, Kapusta and Young (2014). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius and tidal deformability sequences, then compare these to observational constraints from the NICER/XMM-Newton observations of PSR J0740+6620 and the LIGO-Virgo detection of GW170817.

## Key numbers
- The massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620 has a mass exceeding 2 M⊙.
- The switch function parameter μ₀ is varied over values 1400, 1500, 1600, and 1800 MeV.
- The favored crossover model uses a₄ = 0.3, diquark pairing gap Δ = 120 MeV, effective bag constant B_eff^{1/4} = 140 MeV, switch parameter μ₀ = 1200 MeV, and switch exponent r = 6.
- For the NLW hadronic model, the effective mass ratio is m*/m = 0.8, incompressibility K = 250 MeV, and asymmetry energy E_s = 32 MeV at saturation.
- The squared sound speed for color superconducting quark matter can reach c_s² = 1/2 when ζ(μ_c) ≈ 1 for a₄ = 0.3 and Δ ≈ 150 MeV at μ_c ≈ 1150 MeV.
- The lattice QCD chiral transition temperature in the chiral limit is T_c⁰ = 132^{+3}_{-6} MeV.

## Context
This work extends the switch function approach of Albright, Kapusta and Young (2014) and the neutron star constraint method of Kapusta and Welle (2021) by incorporating color superconductivity, which those authors neglected. The results contradict the earlier conclusion that a simple crossover without color superconductivity could satisfy neutron star constraints, and instead show that only a model with nonperturbative strong coupling enhancement (low a₄ parameter) and a diquark pairing gap can simultaneously meet both the NICER mass-radius and GW170817 tidal deformability constraints. This enables future Bayesian analyses of the multi-parameter crossover EoS and motivates comparisons with interpolation methods such as those of Abgaryan et al. (2018) and Ayriyan et al. (2021).
