# One- and two-nucleon transfer in $^{\mathbf{116}}$Sn+$^{\mathbf{60}}$Ni: A coupled reaction channel analysis

> Recent studies of multi-nucleon transfer in heavy ion collisions have employed both macroscopic and microscopic models. Although macroscopic approaches offer useful insights, microscopic analyses of high-precision experimental data provide a more reliable framework for understanding the nucleon transfer mechanisms. The present study aims to carry out a comprehensive theoretical investigation of the $^{116}$Sn+$^{60}$Ni system using microscopic coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations. The calculations employ microscopic double-folding S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potentials, incorporating all relevant inelastic and transfer couplings guided by observed $γ$-ray transitions, wherever available. For the one-nucleon transfer channels, spectroscopic amplitudes are also obtained from large-scale shell-model calculations. In the case of two-nucleon transfer, sequential, microscopic cluster and extreme cluster mechanisms are considered to reproduce the data. Results for quasielastic scattering and one-neutron ($1n$) transfer show excellent agreement with experimental data. Measured one-proton ($1p$) transfer probabilities are best described by incorporating experimental spectroscopic amplitudes in the CRC calculations. For transfer of two-nucleons, the extreme cluster mechanism is found to best reproduce the data. This study highlights that microscopic description of one- and two-nucleon transfer between two heavy ions in the CRC framework, without taking recourse to arbitrary normalization of the cross sections, is quite feasible. Nonetheless, lack of experimental corroboration for all the transitions included in the calculations and practical limits of computational resources, affecting accuracy of shell-model results and causing a cap on the number of states, leave room for further refinement of the results.

## Key claim

Microscopic coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations using double-folding Sao Paulo potentials and shell-model spectroscopic amplitudes can reproduce experimental quasielastic scattering and one-neutron transfer data for the 116Sn+60Ni system without arbitrary normalization, but the description of one-proton transfer requires experimental spectroscopic amplitudes, and for two-nucleon transfer the extreme cluster mechanism works best, though the results remain limited by incomplete experimental corroboration and computational constraints on shell-model accuracy and state count.

## Method

The authors performed CRC calculations using the fresco code with microscopic double-folding Sao Paulo potentials, incorporating inelastic and transfer couplings guided by observed gamma-ray transitions. Spectroscopic amplitudes for one-nucleon transfer were obtained from large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations using the kshell code with multiple interactions (KB3G, JUN45, fpd6npn, JJ44BPN for Ni-region nuclei; SN100PN and Monopole for Sn-region nuclei), and for two-nucleon transfer they compared sequential, microscopic cluster, and extreme cluster mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 60Ni is at 0.000 MeV (experimental), with the first 2+ state at 1.332 MeV (experimental) compared to LSSM values of 1.267 MeV (KB3G), 1.635 MeV (JUN45), 1.335 MeV (fpd6npn), and 1.553 MeV (JJ44BPN).
- The first 2+ state of 116Sn is at 1.293 MeV (experimental), compared to LSSM values of 1.160 MeV (SN100PN) and 1.202 MeV (Monopole).
- For 61Ni, the ground state (3/2-) is at 0.000 MeV, the first excited 5/2- state at 0.067 MeV, and the first 1/2- state at 0.283 MeV (experimental).
- One-neutron spectroscopic amplitude for 60Ni ground state (0+) to 61Ni ground state (3/2-) via 2p3/2 orbital is -0.6701 (KB3G), 0.6688 (fpd6npn), -0.5952 (JUN45), 0.7091 (JJ44BPN).
- One-proton spectroscopic amplitude for 60Ni ground state (0+) to 59Co ground state (7/2-) via 1f7/2 orbital is 2.0995 (KB3G) and -1.8841 (fpd6npn).
- Cluster spectroscopic amplitude for di-neutron transfer from 116Sn ground state (0+) to 114Sn ground state (0+) via (1g7/2)^2 configuration is -0.91777 (SN100PN) and 0.84224 (Monopole), with center-of-mass spectroscopic amplitude of -0.61427 (SN100PN) and 0.58225 (Monopole).

## Context

This work extends microscopic CRC analyses of multi-nucleon transfer in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies that used macroscopic models for such systems. The authors compare their shell-model results with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center (NNDC) for multiple nuclei including 58Fe, 59Co, 60,61,62Ni, 114,115,116Sn, 117Sb, and 118Te. The study demonstrates that microscopic calculations without arbitrary normalization are feasible for one- and two-nucleon transfer, but the lack of experimental corroboration for all included transitions and computational limits on shell-model accuracy and state count leave room for further refinement.
