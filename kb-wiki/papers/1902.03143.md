# Chiral constraints on the isoscalar electromagnetic spectral functions of the nucleon from leading order vector meson couplings

> Using baryon chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons, we analyse various continuum contributions to the isoscalar electromagnetic spectral functions of the nucleon induced by the leading order couplings.

## Key claim

Using baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit vector mesons at leading one-loop order, the authors derive new chiral constraints on the isoscalar electromagnetic spectral functions of the nucleon, finding that the π-ρ loop contribution is the most substantial non-resonant effect, while the K̄K contribution under the φ-meson is less pronounced, though the results cannot be directly compared to dispersion-theoretical calculations without inclusion of next-to-leading order tensor couplings.

## Method

The authors calculate the imaginary parts of the isoscalar electric and magnetic Sachs form factors from π-ρ, K̄K, K*K, and K*K* continuum loops using relativistic two-flavor and SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory with vector mesons, applying Cutkosky rules to one-loop diagrams and reducing the amplitudes to scalar two-point and three-point loop integrals. The vector meson dominance approximation is employed where the photon couples only via vector mesons to hadrons, and the couplings are taken from the hidden symmetry approach with parameter a=2.

## Key numbers

- The π-ρ continuum contribution to the magnetic spectral function shows a peak at t ≈ 1.1 GeV², consistent with earlier modeling in Meissner (1997)
- The φ-meson width is taken as Γ_φ ≈ 4 MeV to account for its finite width in the K̄K loop calculation
- The K*K and K*K* loops only start to have an imaginary part at about 2 GeV² and 3 GeV², respectively
- The three-pion continuum threshold is at t₀ = 9M_π²
- The π-ρ loop threshold is at t ≥ (M_ρ + M_π)²
- Coupling constants used: g_A ≈ 1.27, g_ρN = 5.92, g_ωρπ = 11.6, f_ω = 0.1, F = 0.5, D = 0.8

## Context

This work extends earlier tree-level analyses of vector meson contributions to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Kubis (2000) and Schindler (2005) to leading one-loop order, and re-analyzes continuum contributions previously studied in dispersion relations by Hammer (1999, 1998) and modeling by Meissner (1997). The results provide chiral constraints on the isoscalar spectral functions that can be used in dispersion relation analyses of nucleon form factors, such as those by Belushkin (2006), and the next step is to include tensor coupling effects at next-to-leading order.
