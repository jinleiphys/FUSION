# Alpha-Particle condensation in nuclear systems: present status and perspectives

> Alpha clustering and alpha condensation in lighter nuclei is presently strongly and increasingly discussed in the literature both from the experimental side as from the theoretical one. A discussion of the present status of the theory as well as outlooks for future developements will be presented.

## Key claim

Alpha-particle condensation in nuclear systems exists only in the Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC) phase at low density where alpha particles do not overlap strongly; there is no analogue to the BCS phase of pairing, and quartet condensation breaks down abruptly at positive chemical potential because the three-hole level density goes to zero at the Fermi surface.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework for alpha-particle condensation using the in-medium four-body equation, solved via Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with the Malfliet-Tjohn bare nucleon-nucleon interaction and also via a variational mean-field ansatz for the quartet wave function projected on zero total momentum. For finite nuclei, the THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave function is employed, which is a fully antisymmetrized product of single alpha-particle wave functions, each split into a center-of-mass Gaussian with width parameter B and an intrinsic Gaussian with width parameter b. The nonlinear generalization of the quartet order parameter is written in analogy to BCS theory, with a mass operator containing the quartet order parameter and three-hole propagators.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in 12C at 7.65 MeV excitation energy lies approximately 300 keV above the 3-alpha threshold.
- The density of the Hoyle state is lower than the ground state density of 12C by a factor of 3-4.
- For the alpha condensate in infinite matter near zero chemical potential, the occupation number at zero momentum is approximately n(k=0) ~ 0.30.
- For alpha-particle condensation in finite nuclei, the bosonic occupation numbers in the condensate are around 70%, with all other occupancies lower by at least a factor of ten.
- The critical temperature for alpha-particle condensation in symmetric nuclear matter breaks down at very low density, approximately where alpha particles start to overlap appreciably.

## Context

This work extends the concept of alpha-particle condensation first formulated in the THSR approach (Tohsaki et al., 2001) and builds on earlier studies of in-medium quartet condensation (Röpke et al., 1998; Sogo et al., 2009). The paper contrasts alpha condensation with deuteron pairing, noting that deuteron condensation persists to much higher densities and can cross from negative to positive chemical potentials, forming large Cooper pairs. The review enables future work on extending the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory to alpha-particle condensation, studying mixed gases of nucleons and light clusters (tritons, 3He, deuterons) relevant to compact star physics, and investigating alpha-gas states in heavier nuclei such as 56Ni and alpha preformation in 212Po.
