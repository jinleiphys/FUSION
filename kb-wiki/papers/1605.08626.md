# Light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> We present cross sections for diphoton production in (semi)exclusive $PbPb$ collisions, relevant for the LHC. The calculation is based on equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. The cross sections for elementary elastic scattering $γγ\to γγ$ subprocess are calculated including two mechanisms: box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops and a mechanism based on vector-meson dominance (VDM-Regge) model with virtual intermediate vector-like excitations of the photons. We get measureable cross sections in $PbPb$ collisions. We present many interesting differential distributions which could be measured by the ALICE, CMS or ATLAS Collaborations at the LHC. We study whether a separation of box and VDM-Regge contributions is possible. We find that the cross section for elastic $γγ$ scattering could be measured in the heavy-ion collisions for subprocess energies smaller than $W_{γγ} \approx 15-20$~GeV.

## Key claim

The cross section for elastic light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the LHC is measurable, but only for subprocess energies smaller than Wγγ ≈ 15–20 GeV; the VDM-Regge contribution, while larger than the box contribution at higher energies, produces photons at rapidities that cannot be detected by current LHC zero-degree calorimeters.

## Method

The calculation uses the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space to compute diphoton production in (semi)exclusive PbPb collisions. The elementary γγ → γγ cross section is calculated including two mechanisms: box diagrams with lepton and quark loops, and a VDM-Regge mechanism where both photons fluctuate into virtual vector mesons. The total cross section is obtained via a five-fold integral over photon energies, impact parameters, and the γγ subsystem energy and rapidity, with an absorption factor S²_abs(b) to exclude nuclear breakup.

## Key numbers

- The elementary γγ → γγ cross section from the VDM-Regge mechanism becomes larger than the box contribution for subsystem energies W > 30 GeV.
- The expected number of counts in 1 GeV bins, for an integrated luminosity of 1 nb⁻¹, suggests the invariant mass distribution can be measured up to Mγγ ≈ 15 GeV.
- For the VDM-Regge contribution, the maxima of the cross section occur at |yγ1|, |yγ2| ≈ 5, which is below the ZDC coverage of |η| > 8.3 for ATLAS and |η| > 8.5 for CMS.
- The box mechanism shows a pronounced maximum when both photon energies are small, with a minimum cut of Eγ > 3 GeV applied.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light-by-light scattering, which were previously limited to inelastic processes in e⁺e⁻ collisions (Bohm 1994, Jikia 1993, Bern 2001) and theoretical proposals for laser-based experiments (Homma 2015). It builds on the realization that ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions can test elastic γγ scattering (d'Enterria 2013, KLS2016). The results enable future experimental searches at the LHC by ALICE, CMS, or ATLAS, and suggest that the dominant box contribution is statistically feasible, while the VDM-Regge mechanism requires detectors with coverage at very forward rapidities. Future work should include two-gluon exchange contributions and background from AA → AA e⁺e⁻ events where electrons are misidentified as photons.
