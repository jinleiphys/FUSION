# Constraints of cluster separability and covariance on current operators

> Realistic models of hadronic systems should be defined by a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincare group that is also consistent with cluster properties and a spectral condition. All three of these requirements constrain the structure of the interactions. These conditions can be satisfied in light-front quantum mechanics, maintaining the advantage of having a kinematic subgroup of boosts and translations tangent to a light front. The most straightforward construction of dynamical unitary representations of the Poincare group due to Bakamjian and Thomas fails to satisfy the cluster condition for more than two particles. Cluster properties can be restored, at significant computational expense, using a recursive method due to Sokolov. In this work we report on an investigation of the size of the corrections needed to restore cluster properties in Bakamjian-Thomas models with a light-front kinematic symmetry. Our results suggest that for models based on nucleon and meson degrees of freedom these corrections are too small to be experimentally observed.

## Key claim

For models based on nucleon and meson degrees of freedom, the unitary transformations needed to restore cluster properties in Bakamjian-Thomas light-front models are too small to be experimentally observed, suggesting that the simpler Bakamjian-Thomas construction may be adequate for few-nucleon systems without including Sokolov corrections.

## Method

The authors compared two representations of a three-nucleon system (a deuteron bound state plus a spectator proton) interacting with an electron via a scalar current in the one-photon-exchange approximation: a tensor-product representation that satisfies cluster properties by construction, and an S-matrix equivalent Bakamjian-Thomas representation that violates cluster properties. They computed the difference between the current matrix elements in the two representations as a function of the spectator momentum \(p_{12}\) and momentum transfer \(Q\), using a Malfliet-Tjon nucleon-nucleon interaction that supports a deuteron bound state, while ignoring all particle spins.

## Key numbers

- The fractional difference \((F_{tp} - F_{bt})/F_{tp}\) between the tensor-product and Bakamjian-Thomas current matrix elements was plotted as a function of \(Q = P' - P\) and \(p_{12}\) for two orientations: \(p_{12}\) perpendicular to \(Q\) and \(p_{12}\) parallel to \(Q\).
- The model used a two-body mass Casimir operator of the form \(M_0^2 + 4mV\), where \(V\) is a Malfliet-Tjon nucleon-nucleon interaction and \(m\) is the nucleon mass.
- The calculations were performed in a frame where the \(+\) component of the momentum transfer is zero, with the light front defined by \(z + t = 0\) and momentum transfer \(Q\) in the \(x\) direction.

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) construction for light-front quantum mechanics, which fails to satisfy cluster properties for more than two particles, and the Sokolov (1977) recursive method that restores cluster properties at significant computational expense. The results suggest that for nuclear physics models with meson-exchange interactions, the Sokolov corrections are negligible, enabling practical few-body calculations using the simpler Bakamjian-Thomas representation. The authors note that for systems with sub-nuclear degrees of freedom involving stronger binding and larger internal momenta, the corrections could become observable.
