# Nucleon mass from a covariant three-quark Faddeev equation

> We report the first study of the nucleon where the full Poincare-covariant structure of the three-quark amplitude is implemented in the Faddeev equation. We employ an interaction kernel which is consistent with contemporary studies of meson properties and aspects of chiral symmetry and its dynamical breaking, thus yielding a comprehensive approach to hadron physics. The resulting current-mass evolution of the nucleon mass compares well with lattice data and deviates only by ~5% from the quark-diquark result obtained in previous studies.

## Key claim

The first fully Poincaré-covariant three-quark solution of the nucleon's Faddeev equation yields nucleon masses that deviate by only ~5% from the quark-diquark model result, but this close agreement is obtained under the truncation of omitting the dependence on the angular variable z0 and using a ladder dressed-gluon exchange kernel.

## Method

The authors solved the covariant three-quark Faddeev equation in rainbow-ladder truncation, using a dressed-gluon exchange kernel between any two quarks and dressed quark propagators from the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger equation. The nucleon amplitude was decomposed into 64 Dirac covariants, and the dependence on five Lorentz-invariant momentum variables was reduced by omitting the angular variable z0 = p̂T·q̂T while solving for all 64 dressing functions. Two parameter setups were employed: Setup A with a fixed scale Λ = 0.72 GeV adjusted to experimental pion decay constant, and Setup B with a current-mass dependent scale inflated near the chiral limit to describe a hadronic quark core.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass from Setup A: 0.99 GeV (mixed-antisymmetric solution) and 0.97 GeV (mixed-symmetric solution), compared to 0.94 GeV from the quark-diquark model (Nicmorus 2008)
- Nucleon mass from Setup B: 1.33(2) GeV (mixed-antisymmetric) and 1.31(2) GeV (mixed-symmetric), compared to 1.26(2) GeV from the quark-diquark model (Nicmorus 2008)
- The difference between the two symmetry solutions is ~2%, attributed to the omission of the angular variable z0
- The deviation from the quark-diquark result is only ~5%

## Context

This work extends the quark-diquark model studies of nucleon and Δ properties (Hellstern 1997, Oettel 2000, Eichmann 2008, Nicmorus 2008) by solving the full three-quark Faddeev equation without the diquark approximation, while maintaining consistency with meson studies using the same rainbow-ladder truncation (Maris 1999, Krassnigg 2009). The results enable future investigations of the Δ-baryon, more sophisticated interaction kernels including pionic corrections, and comprehensive studies of baryon resonances.
