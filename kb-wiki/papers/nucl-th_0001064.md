# T-dependence of pseudoscalar and scalar correlations

> Inhomogeneous pseudoscalar and scalar Bethe-Salpeter equations solved using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation exhibit bound state poles below and above T_c, the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration. Above T_c the bound state amplitudes are identical, as are the positions and residues of the pseudoscalar and scalar poles in the vertices. In the chiral limit the pi^0 -> gamma gamma coupling vanishes at T_c, as do f_pi, m_sigma, g_{sigma pi pi}. For light current-quark masses the 2-pi decay channel of the isoscalar-scalar meson remains open until very near T_c, and the widths of the dominant pion decay modes remain significant in the vicinity of the crossover.

## Key claim

In the chiral limit, pseudoscalar and scalar bound states become degenerate and locally identical above the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration, with the pion's coupling to two photons vanishing at T_c along with f_pi, m_sigma, and g_{sigma pi pi}; for realistic light current-quark masses the transition becomes a crossover rather than a phase transition, and the two-pion decay channel of the isoscalar-scalar meson remains open until very near T_c.

## Method

The authors solved inhomogeneous pseudoscalar and scalar Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation, with the gluon propagator modeled by an infrared-dominant term plus one-loop perturbative QCD behavior. Bound state masses and amplitudes were extracted from the associated homogeneous BSEs, and the impulse approximation was used to compute decay matrix elements for sigma -> pi pi and pi^0 -> gamma gamma as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: T_c = 0.15 GeV
- Chiral limit pion decay constant at T=0: f_pi^0 = 0.088 GeV
- Chiral limit scalar meson mass at T=0: m_sigma = 0.56 GeV (0.59 GeV at current-quark mass hat m = 5.7 MeV)
- Renormalisation point invariant light current-quark mass: hat m_{u,d} = 5.7 MeV, giving m_pi = 0.14 GeV and f_pi = 0.092 GeV at T=0
- Chiral limit condensate: -langle q bar q rangle^0_{1 GeV^2} = (0.235 GeV)^3
- Chiral limit pseudoscalar residue: r_pi^0(1 GeV^2) = (0.384 GeV)^2
- At large T, meson screening mass approaches: m_meson / (2 pi T) -> 1^-
- For nonzero current-quark masses, meson masses become indistinguishable at T ~ 1.2 T_c
- For nonzero current-quark masses, the sigma -> pi pi decay channel closes at T ~ 0.98 T_c

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of finite-temperature QCD (Echaya, 1997; Holl, 1999) by using a renormalisable model that incorporates correct one-loop renormalisation group evolution, going beyond simpler models used previously (Blaschke, 1999; Maris, 1998). The results confirm and refine expectations from lattice-QCD simulations (Echaya, 1997) about the persistence of bound states above T_c and the chiral symmetry restoration pattern. The calculation enables quantitative predictions for how pion and sigma meson properties evolve through the QCD crossover, relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data and for understanding the nature of the scalar meson as a chiral partner of the pion.
