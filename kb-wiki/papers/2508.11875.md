# Limitations in constraining neutron star radii and nuclear properties from inspiral gravitational wave detections

> We investigate the constraints on the neutron star equation of state (EoS) and nuclear properties achievable with third-generation gravitational wave detectors using the Fisher information matrix approach within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Assuming an optimistic binary neutron star (BNS) merger rate, we generate simulated inspiral gravitational wave (GW) signals corresponding to one year of observation. From these simulated data, we compute the covariance matrix and posterior distributions for nuclear properties and EoS. Our results show that the EoS can be tightly constrained, particularly in the density range between one and four times nuclear saturation density. However, due to the scarcity of low-mass neutron stars in the GW sample, the EoS at sub-saturation densities remains poorly constrained. Thus, in turn, leads to weaker constraints on neutron star radii, as the radii are sensitive to the low-density EoS. Additionally, we present the expected correlations among nuclear parameters in general and plots of the inferred symmetry energy in particular, which represent degeneracies in their influence on the EoS and make them difficult to be constrained through GW observations alone. These highlights inherent limitations of inspiral GW signals in probing dense matter properties. Therefore, precise radius measurements, post-merger GW observations, and supplementary constraints from terrestrial nuclear experiments remain essential for a comprehensive understanding of dense matter.

# Limitations in constraining neutron star radii and nuclear properties from inspiral gravitational wave detections

## Key claim
Even with an optimistic scenario of ~250,000 binary neutron star merger events detected by third-generation gravitational wave observatories over one year, the equation of state at sub-saturation densities remains poorly constrained due to the scarcity of low-mass neutron stars in the observed population, which in turn limits the precision of neutron star radius measurements. The authors find that inspiral gravitational wave signals alone cannot break the intrinsic degeneracies among nuclear properties, meaning that precise radius measurements, post-merger observations, and terrestrial nuclear experiments remain essential.

## Method
The authors use the Fisher information matrix approach within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory to forecast constraints on the neutron star equation of state and nuclear properties. They generate simulated inspiral gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers detectable by the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer over one year, assuming an optimistic merger rate of 1000 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹ and a realistic neutron star mass distribution. The Fisher matrix is computed with respect to nuclear parameters (rather than tidal deformabilities) using analytical variational formulations of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff and tidal equations reformulated in terms of log-enthalpy, and the total information is accumulated from approximately 250,000 events with signal-to-noise ratio greater than 8.

## Key numbers
- Merger rate adopted: 1000 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹ (optimistic scenario; current estimates range from ~10 to 1700 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹)
- Approximately 250,000 binary neutron star merger events with SNR > 8 are accumulated over one year of observation
- The equation of state is tightly constrained in the density range between 1 and 4 times nuclear saturation density (n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- The radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star shows a spread of up to 500 meters between maximum and minimum posterior samples
- Relative uncertainties in the equation of state remain below 3% between n₀ and 4n₀
- The symmetry energy exhibits uncertainties of 5% to 10% over the same density range, reaching up to 20% near n₀
- Injected nuclear parameters: E_sym = 31 MeV, L_sym = 40 MeV, K₀ = 260 MeV, J₀ = -350 MeV, M*_N/M_N = 0.7
- Prior standard deviations: E_sym = 3 MeV, L_sym = 30 MeV, K₀ = 40 MeV, J₀ = 300 MeV, M*_N/M_N = 0.2

## Context
This work extends previous studies on constraining the neutron star equation of state with gravitational waves, particularly following the first binary neutron star merger detection GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2017). The authors explicitly build on Fisher matrix forecasts for third-generation detectors by Iacovelli et al. (2023) and others, but go further by directly computing derivatives with respect to nuclear parameters rather than tidal deformabilities. The results confirm and quantify degeneracies among nuclear properties previously noted by Iacovelli et al. (2023), Wouters et al. (2025), and Imam et al. (2024), while explicitly demonstrating that the sub-saturation and inner-crust equation of state remains poorly constrained by inspiral gravitational waves alone. This work enables future combined analyses that incorporate precise radius measurements from missions like NICER (Miller et al., 2019; Riley et al., 2019), post-merger gravitational wave observations, and constraints from terrestrial nuclear experiments such as those measuring neutron-skin thickness (Adhikari et al., 2021; Adhikari et al., 2022).
