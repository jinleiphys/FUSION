# eta-Nucleus interactions and in-medium properties of N*(1535) in chiral models

> The properties of eta-nucleus interaction and their experimental consequences are investigated with eta-nucleus optical potentials obtained by postulating the N*(1535) dominance for eta-N system. The N*(1535) properties in nuclear medium are evaluated by two kinds of chiral effective models based on distinct pictures of N*(1535). We find that these two models provide qualitatively different optical potentials of the eta meson, reflecting the in-medium properties of N*(1535) in these models. In order to compare these models in physical observables, we calculate spectra of (d,3He) reactions for the eta mesic nucleus formation with various kinds of target nuclei. We show that the (d,3He) spectra obtained in these models are significantly different and are expected to be distinguishable in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two chiral effective models of N*(1535), the chiral doublet model and the chiral unitary approach, produce qualitatively different η-nucleus optical potentials and correspondingly different (d,³He) reaction spectra for η mesic nucleus formation, and that these differences should be distinguishable in experiments; however, this conclusion depends on the choice of the chiral restoration parameter C in the chiral doublet model, with C=0.0 giving results similar to the chiral unitary approach and C=0.2 giving significantly different results.

## Method

The authors constructed η-nucleus optical potentials assuming N*(1535) dominance in the η-N system, using two chiral models: the chiral doublet model (with both naive and mirror assignments) and the chiral unitary approach. They calculated the in-medium masses and widths of N and N*(1535) as functions of nuclear density, then used these to compute the η self-energy and optical potential. Finally, they evaluated the double differential cross section dσ/dΩ/dE for the (d,³He) reaction at T_d = 3.5 GeV using the Green function method for various target nuclei including ¹²C and ¹²Be.

## Key numbers

- N*(1535) mass in vacuum: 1535 MeV; nucleon mass: 940 MeV; the mass difference lies only 50 MeV above the ηN threshold
- Coupling constant g_η ≈ 2.0, chosen to reproduce the partial width Γ_{N* → ηN} ≈ 75 MeV at tree level
- Chiral doublet model parameters: g₁ = 9.8, g₂ = 16.2, m₀ = 270 MeV, with ⟨σ⟩₀ = f_π = 93 MeV
- The C parameter in the linear density approximation Φ(ρ) = 1 − Cρ/ρ₀ takes values C = 0.1 to 0.3; calculations shown for C = 0.0 and C = 0.2
- The NN* → πNN decay width is estimated to be typically 15 MeV at saturation density
- For the ¹¹Li halo nucleus: experimental matter RMS radius R_mat(¹¹Li) = 3.2 fm; ⁹Li core RMS radius R_mat(⁹Li) = 2.32 fm; range parameters α_1s = 4.88 fm and α_1d = 3.2 fm for halo neutron densities
- ¹²Be target RMS radius R_rms(¹²Be) = 2.59 fm
- Initial deuteron kinetic energy T_d = 3.5 GeV, chosen to satisfy the recoilless condition for η production
- Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters: R = 1.18A^{1/3} − 0.48 fm, a = 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of hadron-nucleus bound systems including pionic atoms (Itahashi, 2000; Geissel, 2002), kaonic atoms/nuclei (Ramos, 2000), and η and ω mesic nuclei (Nagahiro, 2002; Jido, 2002) by focusing specifically on the η meson and its coupling to N*(1535). The chiral doublet model follows the work of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) and Jido (2000) on chiral partners, while the chiral unitary approach follows the Valencia group calculations (Inoue and Oset, 2002). The (d,³He) reaction method was originally developed theoretically for deeply bound pionic states (Toki, 1988; Nieves, 1991) and later proven experimentally (Itahashi, 2000). The paper enables future experimental distinction between competing chiral models of N*(1535) through measurement of (d,³He) spectra at GSI, where an experimental proposal for η and ω-nucleus systems has already been approved.
