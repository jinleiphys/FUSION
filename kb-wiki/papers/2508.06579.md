# Inclusion of the Longitudinal Momentum-Transfer Component and Kinematic Factors in a diffraction approach for H(d,p)X Reactions

> In this work, within the framework of the Glauber-Sitenko approximation, an analysis of the differential cross section for deuteron breakup into a proton in the reaction H(d,p)X is presented. The study is carried out using various parameterizations of the deuteron wave function, including the single-Gaussian parametrization, the multi-Gaussian K2 parametrization, and models based on the Av18 and NijmI nucleon-nucleon potentials. Special attention is given to the effects of small longitudinal components of the transferred momentum (Qz < 0.5 GeV/c) and the transverse momentum of the proton-neutron pair (p_perp < 0.5 GeV/c) in the anti-laboratory reference frame. The results are compared with experimental data, particularly in the region of longitudinal momenta p\_3 = 0.25-0.5 GeV/c, where quark effects are expected to manifest. Preliminary estimates show a decrease in the cross section with increasing transverse momentum, as well as a relatively small shift (and growth) of the cross-section maximum due to the inclusion of the longitudinal component Qz.

## Key claim

Within the Glauber–Sitenko diffraction approximation, including the small longitudinal component of the transferred momentum (Qz < 0.5 GeV/c) and the transverse momentum of the proton–neutron pair (p_perp < 0.5 GeV/c) in the anti-laboratory frame produces a relatively small shift and a significant increase of the differential cross-section maximum for the H(d,p)X reaction, while the cross section decreases with increasing transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors generalized the standard multiple-scattering diffraction theory (MSDT) by incorporating the longitudinal momentum-transfer component Qz into the profile functions and transition form factors for deuteron breakup on a proton target. They computed the differential cross section using several parameterizations of the deuteron wave function: a single-Gaussian parametrization, the multi-Gaussian K2 parametrization, and models based on the Av18 and NijmI nucleon–nucleon potentials, with the profile functions expressed in terms of nucleon–nucleon scattering amplitudes and the optical theorem.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal momentum-transfer component considered: Qz < 0.5 GeV/c
- Transverse momentum of the proton–neutron pair in the anti-laboratory frame: p_perp < 0.5 GeV/c
- Longitudinal proton momentum region compared with experiment: p3 = 0.25–0.5 GeV/c
- Internal momentum region where traditional models show considerable discrepancies with data: k >= 250 MeV/c
- Estimated dimensionless parameter alpha relating longitudinal to transverse deuteron structure: alpha ~ O(1), with range 0.3 less than or similar to alpha less than or similar to 4

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber–Sitenko calculations by Kobushkin (2008) for deuteron breakup on carbon, which showed that Coulomb interaction effects are substantial only for internal momenta k < 50 MeV and do not alter conclusions about quark degrees of freedom at k > 200 MeV. The study addresses unresolved issues in the H(d,p)X reaction, particularly the neglect of the longitudinal momentum-transfer component Qz in classical diffraction theory (Glauber 1959, Akhiezer 1957) and the transverse momenta of nucleons in the anti-laboratory frame, which earlier work by Davydovskyy (2016) and Tartakovsky (2005) suggested could lead to misinterpretation of experimental results. The findings are relevant for testing the quark-origin interpretation of the observed cross-section enhancement in the 300–500 MeV/c region, previously attributed to an S-wave quark component by Ableev (1983) and Kobushkin (1982), and for future studies with polarized deuteron beams.
