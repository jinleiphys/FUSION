# Back-to-back relative-excess observable in search for the chiral magnetic effect

> $\textbf{Background:}$ The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is extensively studied in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. In the commonly used reaction plane (RP) dependent, charge dependent azimuthal correlator ($Δγ$), both the close and back-to-back pairs are included. Many backgrounds contribute to the close pairs (e.g. resonance decays, jet correlations), whereas the back-to-back pairs are relatively free of those backgrounds. $\textbf{Purpose:}$ In order to reduce those backgrounds, we propose a new observable which only focuses on the back-to-back pairs, namely, the relative back-to-back opposite-sign (OS) over same-sign (SS) pair excess ($r_{\text{BB}}$) as a function of the pair azimuthal orientation with respect to the RP ($φ_{\text{BB}}$). $\textbf{Methods:}$ We use analytical calculations and toy model simulations to demonstrate the sensitivity of $r_{\text{BB}}(φ_{\text{BB}})$ to the CME and its insensitivity to backgrounds. $\textbf{Results:}$ With finite CME, the $φ_{\text{BB}}$ distribution of $r_{\text{BB}}$ shows a clear characteristic modulation. Its sensitivity to background is significantly reduced compared to the previous $Δγ$ observable. The simulation results are consistent with our analytical calculations. $\textbf{Conclusions:}$ Our studies demonstrate that the $r_{\text{BB}}(φ_{\text{BB}})$ observable is sensitive to the CME signal and rather insensitive to the resonance backgrounds.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new observable, the back-to-back relative-excess of opposite-sign over same-sign pairs as a function of pair azimuthal orientation with respect to the reaction plane, is sensitive to the chiral magnetic effect signal and significantly less sensitive to resonance backgrounds compared to the standard \(\Delta\gamma\) observable, provided the pair opening angle is restricted to be larger than \(150^\circ\).

## Method

The authors define a new observable \(r_{\text{BB}}(\varphi_{\text{BB}})\) as the ratio of the difference to the sum of opposite-sign and same-sign back-to-back pair counts, where pairs are formed from particles in separate \(\eta\) subevents (east and west) with an opening angle larger than \(150^\circ\). They perform analytical calculations of the Fourier expansion of this observable and use a toy model simulation (previously used in Wang (2016) and Feng (2018)) that includes primordial pions and \(\rho\) meson decays, with parameters tuned to Au+Au collisions at 40-50% centrality, to test the observable's response to an input CME signal of \(a_1 = 0.01\) and to resonance backgrounds.

## Key numbers

*   The Fourier coefficient \(-c_2\) from the \(r_{\text{BB}}(\varphi_{\text{BB}})\) distribution, with no input CME (\(a_1=0\)) and in the invariant mass range \(1.5 < m_{\text{inv}} < 3.0\) GeV, is \(0.07 \pm 0.04 \times 10^{-4}\), indicating near-zero background.
*   With an input CME of \(a_1 = 0.01\) and in the same high invariant mass range (\(1.5 < m_{\text{inv}} < 3.0\) GeV), the extracted \(-c_2\) is \(1.69 \pm 0.04 \times 10^{-4}\), and the extracted \(a_1\) from \(-c_2\) is \(0.85 \pm 0.02 \times 10^{-2}\).
*   For the inclusive \(\Delta\gamma\) observable with the same input CME (\(a_1=0.01\)) in the mass range \(1.5 < m_{\text{inv}} < 3.0\) GeV, the signal is lower than that of the back-to-back observables, demonstrating dilution from close pairs.
*   The analytical calculation shows that for a back-to-back opening angle requirement of \(\Delta = 15^\circ\), the Fourier coefficient \(c_2\) is approximately \(a_1^2 (-2 - 3.648(v_{2,\pi^+} + v_{2,\pi^-})) + 1.267(v_{2,\pi^+} + v_{2,\pi^-})(v_{2,\pi^+} - v_{2,\pi^-})^2\), where the non-CME term is on the order of \(10^{-7}\) for typical flow values.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by proposing a new observable designed to suppress the dominant backgrounds (e.g., resonance decays and jet correlations) that contaminate the standard \(\Delta\gamma\) correlator proposed by Voloshin (2004). The new back-to-back observable is directly related to a modified version of \(\Delta\gamma\) applied only to back-to-back pairs (\(\Delta\gamma_{\text{BB}}\)), and its Fourier coefficient \(-c_2\) is shown to be nearly identical to \(\Delta\gamma_{\text{BB}}\). This enables a cleaner extraction of the CME signal, particularly when combined with an invariant mass cut to further reduce resonance contributions, and provides a method that can be applied to existing experimental data from STAR and ALICE.
