# On the possibility of superradiant neutrino emission by atomic condensates

> In a recent work [B. J. P. Jones and J. A. Formaggio, Phys. Rev. Lett. 135, 111801 (2025)], the possibility of superradiant neutrino emission from atomic condensates has been theoretically proposed. Subsequent analysis by Y. K. Lu, H. Lin, and W. Ketterle [arXiv:2510.21705] questioned this scenario, emphasizing the limiting role of the fermionic nature of the decayed atoms. In this study, we revisit the problem and discuss under which conditions collective emission phenomena might still emerge in cold-atom systems.

## Key claim

Collective (superradiant) neutrino emission from atomic condensates is not ruled out on fundamental grounds, but its realization depends critically on the quantum statistics of the decay products: while boson-to-fermion channels are suppressed by Pauli blocking, cooperative behavior can persist when fermionic atoms in the deep BEC regime decay into bosonic species.

## Method

The authors develop minimal microscopic models for three statistically distinct decay scenarios (boson-to-boson, boson-to-fermion, and fermion-to-boson) using Lindblad dynamics and effective few-mode Hamiltonians derived from a real-space description. They analyze the time evolution of atomic populations and correlation functions under the influence of dissipation, Pauli blocking, bosonic stimulation, and atom-atom interactions, solving the resulting coupled differential equations analytically and numerically.

## Key numbers

* For the boson-to-boson model, the decayed fraction follows the logistic solution $\frac{\langle n_b \rangle}{N} = \frac{e^{\Omega (N+1)t} - 1}{e^{\Omega (N+1)t} + N}$, with $\Omega \equiv \frac{2|g|^2}{\hbar} \frac{\Gamma}{\Delta^2+\Gamma^2} \approx \frac{2|g|^2}{\hbar \Gamma}$, reproducing the superradiant behavior of Jones and Formaggio (2025).
* For the boson-to-fermion model with $N=100$ atoms and $\alpha=80$, the decay dynamics shows no superradiant behavior; with $g_\alpha = \gamma$ the decayed fraction reaches saturation at approximately 80 out of 100 atoms after $\gamma t \approx 10^3$.
* For the boson-to-boson model with atom-atom interactions, the effective decay rate at the process beginning is reduced to $\sim \Omega/\eta$ when $\eta = (UN/\Gamma)^2 \gg 1$, compared to $\Omega$ in the non-interacting case.
* The electron capture in $^{163}$Ho has a Q-value of $Q = 2863.2(6)$ eV and a halflife of about 4570 years; the nuclear recoil of the daughter atom $^{163}$Dy is of order a few $\mu$eV.
* The $^7$Be electron capture has a Q-value of $Q = 861.963(23)$ keV, a halflife of about 52 days, and a recoil energy for the daughter nucleus $^7$Li of about 50 eV; the excited nuclear state in $^7$Li has halflife $\tau_{1/2} = 728(20)$ fs and emits a $\gamma$-ray with energy 477.603(2) keV.

## Context

This work revisits the proposal by Jones and Formaggio (2025) for superradiant neutrino emission from atomic condensates, which was questioned by Lu, Lin, and Ketterle (2025) on the grounds that fermionic statistics of daughter atoms causes Pauli blocking. The authors confirm the suppression for boson-to-fermion channels consistent with Lu, Lin, and Ketterle (2025), but show that cooperative emission can persist in fermion-to-boson scenarios (e.g., $^7$Be decay) and in boson-to-boson channels (e.g., gamma decay of metastable nuclear excited states). The analysis enables future experimental searches for collective neutrino emission using specific radioactive isotopes with suitable decay pathways.
