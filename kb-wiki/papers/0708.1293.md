# Lattice QCD at finite temperature and density

> QCD at finite temperature and density is becoming increasingly important for various experimental programmes, ranging from heavy ion physics to astro-particle physics. The non-perturbative nature of non-abelian quantum field theories at finite temperature leaves lattice QCD as the only tool by which we may hope to come to reliable predictions from first principles. This requires careful extrapolations to the thermodynamic, chiral and continuum limits in order to eliminate systematic effects introduced by the discretization procedure. After an introduction to lattice QCD at finite temperature and density, its possibilities and current systematic limitations, a review of present numerical results is given. In particular, plasma properties such as the equation of state, screening masses, static quark free energies and spectral functions are discussed, as well as the critical temperature and the QCD phase structure at zero and finite density.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD is the only known first-principles method for non-perturbative studies of QCD at finite temperature and density, but its predictions require careful extrapolations to the thermodynamic, chiral, and continuum limits to eliminate systematic effects introduced by the discretization procedure.

## Method
The paper reviews the methodology of lattice QCD at finite temperature and density, focusing on the need for extrapolations to the thermodynamic, chiral, and continuum limits. For finite density, it discusses three methods that circumvent the sign problem: multi-parameter reweighting, Taylor expansion around zero chemical potential, and simulations at imaginary chemical potential followed by analytic continuation or Fourier transformation to the canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature for the QCD transition is approximately $T_c \approx 200$ MeV.
- The methods for finite density are found to be reliable as long as $\mu/T \lesssim 1$, or $\mu_B \lesssim 550$ MeV, which includes the region of interest for heavy ion collisions.

## Context
This review extends the work of Karsch (2002) and Laermann and Philipsen (2003) on the status of lattice QCD at finite temperature. It discusses the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (sQGP) regime, contradicting early hopes that the high-temperature phase would be accessible to perturbation theory. The review enables future work on the QCD phase diagram, equation of state, and properties of the quark-gluon plasma relevant for heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR, as well as for early universe and astro-particle physics.
