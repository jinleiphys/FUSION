# Spin observables in antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda and density-matrix constraints

> The positivity conditions of the spin density matrix constrain the spin observables of the reaction antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda, leading to model-independent, non-trivial inequalities. The formalism is briefly presented and examples of inequalities are provided.

## Key claim

The positivity conditions of the spin density matrix for the reaction antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda produce model-independent, non-trivial inequalities among spin observables, but these inequalities are derived under the assumption of a 100% polarized proton target and the conservation of discrete symmetries such as parity and charge conjugation.

## Method

The authors constructed the spin density matrix for the initial antiproton-proton state with a transversely polarized proton target, then applied the transition matrix for the reaction to obtain the final-state density matrix for the AntiLambda-Lambda system. By imposing positivity conditions on the density matrix elements, specifically that diagonal elements are non-negative and that for any 2x2 submatrix the product of diagonal elements is at least the squared modulus of the off-diagonal element, they derived algebraic inequalities relating the spin observables. The formalism was applied to the 21 independent observables that remain after accounting for parity and charge conjugation conservation.

## Key numbers

- The inequality \(C_{xz}^{2}+P_{n}^{2}+C_{zz}^{2}\leq 1\) constrains the spin correlation \(C_{xz}\), the polarization \(P_{n}\), and the spin correlation \(C_{zz}\) for the reaction antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda.
- The inequality \(D_{nn}^{2}+C_{zz}^{2}+O_{nzx}^{2}\leq 1\) constrains the spin depolarization \(D_{nn}\), the spin correlation \(C_{zz}\), and the observable \(O_{nzx}\).
- The inequality \(D_{nn}^{2}+C_{zz}^{2}\leq 1\) was previously published by Elchikh and Richard (2000) and Richard (1996).
- The inequality \(O_{nxx}^{2}+O_{nzx}^{2}+C_{xz}^{2}+P_{n}^{2}+D_{nn}^{2}+C_{zz}^{2}\leq 1+A_{n}^{2}\) mixes elements from the unpolarized and polarized target blocks of the density matrix.

## Context

This work extends the empirical approach of Elchikh and Richard (2000), who derived inequalities among spin observables by randomly generating complex amplitudes and plotting observables against each other, revealing restricted domains within the naive square \([-1,+1]^{2}\). The density-matrix formalism provides a more powerful and physically interpretable method for deriving these constraints, which can be applied to test data from the PS185 collaboration at CERN's LEAR facility (Bassalleck et al., 2002; Paschke, 2001). The formalism can be extended to inclusive reactions and spin-dependent parton densities, as discussed by Artru and Richard (2004) and Soffer (2003), and a forthcoming review article by Artru, Elchikh, Richard, Soffer, and Teryaev is planned.
