# Relativistic mean-field description of light $Λ$ hypernuclei with large neutron excess

> The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov model in coordinate space, with finite range pairing interaction, is applied to the description of $Λ$-hypernuclei with a large neutron excess. The addition of the $Λ$ hyperon to Ne isotopes with neutron halo can shift the neutron drip by stabilizing an otherwise unbound core nucleus. The additional binding of the halo neutrons to the core originates from the increase in magnitude of the spin-orbit term. Although the $Λ$ produces only a fractional change in the central mean-field potential, through a purely relativistic effect it increases the spin-orbit term which binds the outermost neutrons.

# Relativistic mean-field description of light $\Lambda$ hypernuclei with large neutron excess

## Key claim

The addition of a single $\Lambda$ hyperon to neutron-rich Ne isotopes can shift the neutron drip line by stabilizing an otherwise unbound core nucleus, but this effect is limited to light systems; for heavier cores such as Zr isotopes, the $\Lambda$-N interaction does not have enough strength to stabilize the core. The mechanism is a purely relativistic effect where the $\Lambda$ increases the magnitude of the spin-orbit term in the surface region, even though it produces only a fractional change in the central mean-field potential.

## Method

The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model in coordinate space, with finite range Gogny-type pairing interaction (D1S parameter set), was applied self-consistently to even-even Ne isotopes and their corresponding $\Lambda$-hypernuclei. The NL3 parameter set was used for the mean-field Lagrangian, and the $\Lambda$ coupling constants were taken from Mares and Jennings (1994), with $g_{\omega\Lambda} = \frac{2}{3} g_{\omega N}$ from the naive quark model and $g_{\sigma\Lambda} = 0.621 g_{\sigma N}$ fitted to the binding energy of a $\Lambda$ in the $1s$ state of $^{17}_{\Lambda}$O. The Dirac equation for the $\Lambda$ particle was solved simultaneously with the RHB equations for nucleons, with potentials determined from Klein-Gordon equations for mesons.

## Key numbers

- The neutron $rms$ radii of Ne isotopes are reduced on average by 2% when a $\Lambda$ hyperon is added.
- For $^{30}$Ne, the scalar potential S in the center of the nucleus is -380 MeV and the vector potential V is 308 MeV; for $^{31}_{\Lambda}$Ne, the corresponding values are -412 MeV and 336 MeV, changing the mean-field potential by 4 MeV but the anti-nucleon potential (V-S) by 60 MeV.
- The spin-orbit term in Ne nuclei close to the neutron drip is reduced by approximately 40% in the surface region compared to beta-stable nuclei; for the corresponding $\Lambda$-hypernuclei, the spin-orbit term increases in magnitude by about 10%.
- The last bound Ne isotope without the $\Lambda$ is $^{40}$Ne (N=32); with the $\Lambda$, $^{42+\Lambda}$Ne becomes bound (Fermi level negative), whereas $^{42}$Ne alone was unbound.
- For Zr isotopes, RHB theory predicts the last bound isotope to be $^{138}$Zr (N=98), and the hypernucleus $^{140+\Lambda}$Zr remains unbound.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RHB study of neutron halo formation in Ne and C isotopes (Vretenar et al., 1997) by adding a $\Lambda$ hyperon to investigate its effect on halo nuclei near the drip line. It builds on earlier suggestions by Majling (1995) that neutron-rich $\Lambda$-hypernuclei could display interesting phenomena, and on the early work of Dalitz and Levi Setti (1963) on unusual light hypernuclei. The results enable future investigation of whether multi-$\Lambda$ systems could stabilize even heavier neutron-rich cores, and whether such objects might play a role in nucleosynthesis via the r-process.
