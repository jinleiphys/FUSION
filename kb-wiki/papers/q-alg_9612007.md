# Complex su_q(2) Dynamical Symmetry, Limiting Cases and 1-Dimensional Potential Realisation

> Using a complex deformation q=exp(is) of su(2) we obtain extensions of the finite-dimensional representations towards the infinite-dimensional ones. A generalised q-deformation of su(2), as a Hopf algebra is introduced. We present the corresponding Schrodinger picture, by using a differential realisation, and a large class of potentials is obtained.A connection between the unirreps with q a root of unity and the comensurability of the potentials is investigated. The smooth transition between su(2) and su(1,1) , through E(1) is obtained at different levels: of the unirreps, of the topology, of the commutators and of the potentials in the corresponding Schrodinger equation.

## Key claim

Using a complex deformation parameter \(q = e^{is}\) of the Lie algebra \(su(2)\), the authors show that infinite-dimensional unitary representations can be obtained directly from the compact algebra, not only in the limiting case \(s \to \pi\) (where the algebra resembles \(su(1,1)\)) but also for other values of \(s\), provided the Casimir eigenvalue \(c\) lies above a threshold \(c_0 = 1/\sin^2 s\).

## Method

The paper classifies the unitary representations of \(su_{e^{is}}(2)\) by analysing the positivity condition for the matrix elements of \(J_+J_-\) and \(J_-J_+\) as a function of the Casimir eigenvalue \(c\) and the deformation parameter \(s\). A differential realisation of the algebra is then used to derive a Schrödinger equation, yielding a family of one-dimensional potentials whose shape depends on \(s\). A separate generalised real deformation of \(su(2)\) is also constructed as a Hopf algebra by replacing the usual q-number \([2J_z]\) with a polynomial function \(f(J_z, q)\), and its unitary representations are examined.

## Key numbers

* For \(q = e^{is}\) with \(s \in (0,\pi)\), the threshold for infinite-dimensional continuous series is \(c_0 = 1/\sin^2 s\); the complementary series exists for \(c\) in the interval \((c_1, c_0)\) where \(c_1 = 1/(4\sin^2(s/2))\).
* In the limit \(s \to \pi/2\), the commutator \([J_+, J_-]\) vanishes on the subspace of integer \(m\), yielding an algebra homeomorphic to the Euclidean Lie algebra \(e(2)\).
* For \(s = \pi/(k+1)\) with \(k \in \mathbb{N}\), \(k \neq 0\), the action of \([2J_z]\) produces only \(k\) distinct eigenvalues, and the states \(|c,m\rangle\) have infinite degeneracy for each value of \(m\).
* For the generalised real deformation with \(q = q_1 = (\alpha-1)/\alpha\), the unitarity conditions require the function \(f(J_z, q_1)\) to lie in an interval \((f_{\text{min}}, f_{\text{max}})\) that exists for \(c \geq c_{\text{min}}(q_1) > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic scattering theory (AST) approach of Alhassid et al. (1980s) by replacing the analytic continuation of generators with a continuous q-deformation of the compact algebra \(su(2)\). It builds on prior classifications of unitary representations of \(su_q(2)\) and \(su_q(1,1)\) by Biedenharn (1989), Macfarlane (1989), Ui and Aizawa (1990), and Kulish and Damaskinsky (1990), and on the observation by Ballesteros et al. (1993) that \(su_{-1}(2)\) is equivalent to \(su(1,1)\). The results enable a unified algebraic description of the transition from bound to scattering states in one-dimensional quantum systems, and suggest a connection between the rational/irrational character of \(s/\pi\) and the commensurability of the corresponding exactly solvable potentials.
