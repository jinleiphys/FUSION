# Estrellas híbridas con núcleos superconductores de color

> The aim of this work is the study of hybrid stars interiors from the equations of state describing the matter composing them. We used modern hadronic equations of state to describe the matter in the outer core of these stars. The quark phase is modeled by an extended version of the Field Correlator Method, in which vector interactions among quarks and color superconductivity are included. We analyze the effect of these two contributions on the mass-radius relationship and tidal deformability. This is a relevant quantity for neutron stars after the GW170817 event, allowing to impose additional constraints to the equation of state of these compact objetcs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hybrid stars with color-superconducting quark cores can satisfy the current observational constraints from 2 solar mass pulsars and the GW170817 event, but the hybrid star branch is very short because quark matter tends to destabilize the neutron star.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining modern hadronic models (density-dependent Walecka model) for the outer core with an extended Field Correlator Method for the quark phase, including vector interactions and Color Flavor Locked (CFL) superconductivity. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, using the Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of stable hybrid stars exceeds 2 solar masses, satisfying the pulsar mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432
- For 1.4 solar mass stars, radii fall within the GW170817 constraint of 9.6 km to 13.76 km, as reported by Tews et al. (2018)
- The quark phase parameters used: gluon condensate G2 = 0.001 GeV^4, static quark-antiquark potential V1 = 50 MeV, superconducting gap Delta = 100 MeV, vector coupling constant Gv = 0.007 MeV^{-2}
- Nuclear saturation density rho0 = 2.7 x 10^14 g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the Field Correlator Method by Mariani et al. (2017) and Klahn et al. (2015), incorporating both vector interactions and color superconductivity simultaneously. The results enable future investigations of finite-temperature equations of state for proto-hybrid stars and neutron star mergers, as well as studies of non-radial oscillation modes that could generate gravitational waves. The authors plan to explore the Gibbs formalism for mixed phases and include strong magnetic field effects.
