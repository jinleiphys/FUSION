# QCD phase diagram for nonzero isospin-asymmetry

> The QCD phase diagram is studied in the presence of an isospin asymmetry using continuum extrapolated staggered quarks with physical masses. In particular, we investigate the phase boundary between the normal and the pion condensation phases and the chiral/deconfinement transition. The simulations are performed with a small explicit breaking parameter in order to avoid the accumulation of zero modes and thereby stabilize the algorithm. The limit of vanishing explicit breaking is obtained by means of an extrapolation, which is facilitated by a novel improvement program employing the singular value representation of the Dirac operator. Our findings indicate that no pion condensation takes place above $T\approx 160$ MeV and also suggest that the deconfinement crossover continuously connects to the BEC-BCS crossover at high isospin asymmetries. The results may be directly compared to effective theories and model approaches to QCD.

## Key claim

The paper maps the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-isospin chemical potential plane using continuum-extrapolated lattice simulations with physical quark masses, finding that no pion condensation occurs above approximately 160 MeV and that the deconfinement crossover continuously connects to the BEC-BCS crossover at high isospin asymmetries. The key hedge is that these findings depend on an extrapolation of an explicit symmetry-breaking parameter λ to zero, which is facilitated by a novel singular-value-based improvement program.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1-flavor lattice QCD simulations with dynamical staggered quarks at physical quark masses, using a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action and two steps of stout smearing. They introduced a small explicit pionic source parameter λ to avoid zero-mode accumulation and stabilize the algorithm, then extrapolated to the λ→0 limit using a singular value representation of the Dirac operator combined with leading-order reweighting. The simulations covered various temperatures and isospin chemical potentials on lattices with temporal extent Nt=6 and spatial extent Ns=24, with the strange quark included via the rooting procedure.

## Key numbers

- Pion condensation is absent above T ≈ 160 MeV (the chiral pseudocritical temperature at zero isospin chemical potential is Tpc ≈ 155 MeV)
- The critical isospin chemical potential at zero temperature is μI,c = mπ/2, with mπ ≈ 135 MeV
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is fπ = 86 MeV, used for normalization of condensates
- The renormalization scheme for the Polyakov loop uses T⋆ = 162 MeV as reference temperature

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of QCD with isospin asymmetry by Kogut et al. (2002-2004), Cea et al. (2012), and Endrodi (2014), improving upon them by using physical quark masses, an improved lattice action enabling faster continuum scaling, and inclusion of the strange quark. The results can be directly compared to predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Son, 2000), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations, and other effective approaches. The findings enable future studies of the BEC-BCS crossover region at high isospin asymmetries and may provide insight for addressing the sign problem in simulations with nonzero baryon chemical potential.
