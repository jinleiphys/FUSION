# Parity violation in hot QCD: why it can happen, and how to look for it

> The arguments for the possibility of violation of P and CP symmetries of strong interactions at finite temperature are presented. A new way of observing these effects in heavy ion collisions is proposed -- it is shown that parity violation should manifest itself in the asymmetry between positive and negative pions with respect to the reaction plane. Basing on topological considerations, we derive a {\it lower} bound on the magnitude of the expected asymmetry, which may appear within the reach of the current and/or future heavy ion experiments.

## Key claim

Parity and CP violation can occur in metastable domains of hot QCD matter produced in heavy ion collisions, and this violation should manifest as an asymmetry between positive and negative pions with respect to the reaction plane. The magnitude of the expected asymmetry has a lower bound derived from topological considerations, making it potentially observable in current or future experiments, though the effect depends on the existence of a symmetry axis provided by the angular momentum of the collision.

## Method

The author argues that metastable "P-odd bubbles" with a nonzero topological charge can form in the deconfined phase near the QCD phase transition, where the Vafa-Witten theorem does not apply at finite temperature. The angular momentum of a non-central heavy ion collision provides a symmetry axis, and the interaction of quarks with a space-time dependent "spurion" field (associated with a local CP-violating phase θ) induces a spatial asymmetry in the production of u and anti-u quarks along this axis. This asymmetry is then transferred to charged pions during hadronization, leading to an excess of π⁺ in one hemisphere and π⁻ in the opposite hemisphere relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- The initial angular momentum of a heavy ion collision at √s = 200 GeV (RHIC energy) is estimated as L ≈ A/2 × b[fm] × 10³ units of angular momentum, where A is the mass number and b is the impact parameter.
- The expected asymmetry for charged pions is A_π⁺ = -A_π⁻ ≈ Q/N_π⁺, where Q ≥ 1 is the topological charge of the domain and N_π⁺ is the multiplicity per unit rapidity.
- For RHIC Au-Au collisions, the positive pion multiplicity per unit rapidity varies within 100 ≤ N_π⁺ ≤ 300, yielding an estimated asymmetry A_π⁺ ∼ 10⁻².
- The average angle of π⁺ mesons with respect to the reaction plane is ⟨δχ_π⁺⟩ = 4A_π⁺/3 ∼ 10⁻².

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kharzeev, Pisarski, and Tytgat (1998) that metastable P-odd domains can exist near the deconfinement phase transition, and the earlier suggestion by Lee and Wick (1974) of excited vacuum domains in heavy ion collisions. The paper provides a new observable signature, charge asymmetry with respect to the reaction plane, that is more experimentally accessible than the pion momentum correlations previously proposed by Voloshin (1998) and Sandweiss (2000). The estimate based on topological charge conservation avoids the model-dependent uncertainties of earlier calculations using chiral Lagrangian approaches (Kharzeev and Pisarski, 2000) or quasi-classical color field models (Kharzeev, 2000). Observation of this effect would establish the creation of a new phase of quark-gluon matter and provide the first evidence of P and CP violation in strong interactions.
