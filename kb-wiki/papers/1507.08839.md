# Exact calculations of a quasi-bound state in the $\bar{K} \bar{K} N$ system

> Dynamically exact calculations of a quasi-bound state in the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ three-body system are performed using Faddeev-type AGS equations. As input two phenomenological and one chirally motivated $\bar{K}N$ potentials are used, which describe the experimental information on the $\bar{K}N$ system equally well and produce either a one- or two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. For the $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ interaction separable potentials are employed that are fitted to phase shifts obtained from two theoretical models. The first one is a phenomenological $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ potential based on meson exchange, which is derived by SU(3) symmetry arguments from the Jülich $ππ- \bar{K} K$ coupled-channels model. The other interaction is a variant of the first one, which is adjusted to the $KK$ s-wave scattering length recently determined in lattice QCD simulations. The position and width of the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound state is evaluated in two ways: (i) by a direct pole search in the complex energy plane and (ii) using an "inverse determinant" method, where one needs to calculate the determinant of the AGS system of equations only for real energies. A quasi-bound state is found with binding energy $B_{\bar{K}\bar{K}N} = 12 - 26$ MeV and width $Γ_{\bar{K}\bar{K}N} = 61 - 102$ MeV, which could correspond to the experimentally observed $Ξ(1950)$ state.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first dynamically exact calculation of a quasi-bound state in the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) three-body system, finding that the repulsive \(\bar{K}\bar{K}\) interaction is more than compensated by the attractive \(\bar{K}N\) interaction, allowing a quasi-bound state to form. The authors hedge that the state "could correspond to the experimentally observed \(\Xi(1950)\) state," rather than claiming a definitive identification.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) system with coupled \(\bar{K}\pi\Sigma\) channels, using separable representations of two-body interactions. Three \(\bar{K}N\) potentials (two phenomenological and one chirally motivated) and two \(\bar{K}\bar{K}\) potentials (one from the Julich meson-exchange model and one adjusted to lattice QCD scattering lengths) were employed as input. The pole position of the quasi-bound state was determined by two independent methods: a direct pole search in the complex energy plane and an "inverse determinant" method that fits a Breit-Wigner form to the modulus squared of the inverse determinant of the AGS kernel on the real energy axis.

- Width range: \(\Gamma_{\bar{K}\bar{K}N} = 61 - 102\) MeV (from abstract)

- Two-body threshold: 1434.57 MeV (from Table caption)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quasi-bound states in strangeness \(S=-1\) systems like \(\bar{K}NN\) (Shevchenko et al., 2007) to the \(S=-2\) \(\bar{K}\bar{K}N\) system, which had only been explored previously by Kanada-En'yo (2008) using variational methods with uncontrolled approximations. The results enable future comparisons with experimental candidates such as the \(\Xi(1950)\) state and provide a benchmark for exact three-body calculations with coupled channels, while also highlighting the sensitivity of the predicted binding energy and width to the choice of \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\bar{K}\bar{K}\) interactions.
