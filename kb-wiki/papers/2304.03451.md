# Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons, and Neutrinos (FSNN): Whitepaper for the 2023 NSAC Long Range Plan

> This whitepaper presents the research priorities decided on by attendees of the 2022 Town Meeting for Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons and Neutrinos, which took place December 13-15, 2022 in Chapel Hill, NC, as part of the Nuclear Science Advisory Committee (NSAC) 2023 Long Range Planning process. A total of 275 scientists registered for the meeting. The whitepaper makes a number of explicit recommendations and justifies them in detail.

## Key claim

This whitepaper presents the research priorities and explicit recommendations decided upon by 275 registered attendees of the 2022 Town Meeting for Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons and Neutrinos, held as part of the NSAC 2023 Long Range Planning process; the recommendations are justified in detail but represent the consensus of the meeting participants rather than a binding agency directive.

## Method

The whitepaper was produced by the community through the 2022 Town Meeting for Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons and Neutrinos, which took place December 13-15, 2022 in Chapel Hill, NC, with 275 registered scientists. The document synthesizes input across multiple subfields including lepton number violation, CP-violation via electric dipole moments, precision tests of the Standard Model, neutrino properties, theory, and cross-cutting initiatives, and formulates four main recommendations for the NSAC Long Range Plan.

## Key numbers

- 275 scientists registered for the 2022 Town Meeting in Chapel Hill, NC (December 13-15, 2022).
- The whitepaper references the 2015 Long Range Plan for Nuclear Science ("Reaching for the horizon") as the prior planning document.
- Current experimental limits cited include: neutron EDM searches, atomic EDM limits from Graner (2016) for Hg, Parker (2015) and Bishof (2016) for Ra, ACME (2018) for ThO, Roussy (2022) for HfF+, and Hudson (2011) for YbF.
- Neutrinoless double beta decay half-life limits are cited from KamLAND-Zen (2022), GERDA (Agostini 2020), EXO-200 (Anton 2019), CUORE (Adams 2022), and Majorana Demonstrator (Arnquist 2022).

## Context

This whitepaper extends the 2015 NSAC Long Range Plan ("Reaching for the horizon") and the 2015 NSAC subcommittee report on neutrinoless double beta decay (Geesaman et al. 2015). It updates the community's priorities for fundamental symmetry tests, neutrino physics, and neutron science, and enables coordinated planning for next-generation experiments such as LEGEND, nEXO, CUPID, SNO+, and the nEDM Search at the Spallation Neutron Source (Ahmed 2019). The document also connects to parallel efforts in high energy physics and atomic/molecular physics.
