# Relation between transition density and proton inelastic scattering by $^{12}$C target at $E_p =$ 65 and 200 MeV

> We calculate proton elastic and inelastic scatterings with a microscopic coupled channel (MCC) calculation. The localized diagonal and coupling potentials including the spin-orbit part are obtained by folding a complex $G$-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with a transition density. This is the first time that the present folding prescription for the spin-orbit part is applied to the proton inelastic scattering, while for the monopole transition only. We apply the MCC calculation to the proton elastic and inelastic (0$^+_2$) scatterings by $^{12}$C target at $E_p$ = 65 and 200 MeV. The role of diagonal and coupling potentials for the central and spin-orbit parts is checked. In addition, the relation between the transition density and the proton inelastic scattering is investigated with the modified wave function and the modified transition density. Namely, we perform the investigation with the artificial drastic change rather than fine structural change. The inelastic cross section is sensitive to the strength and shape of the transition density, but the inelastic analyzing power is sensitive only to the shape of that. Finally, we make clear the property of the inelastic analyzing power derived from the transition density without an ambiguity.

## Key claim

The inelastic analyzing power for proton scattering on $^{12}$C is sensitive only to the shape of the transition density, not its strength, while the inelastic cross section is sensitive to both strength and shape. This conclusion is drawn from artificial drastic modifications to the transition density rather than fine structural changes.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic coupled channel (MCC) calculations for proton elastic and inelastic (0$_2^+$) scattering on $^{12}$C at 65 and 200 MeV. Localized diagonal and coupling potentials, including the spin-orbit part, were obtained by folding a complex $G$-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (MPa interaction) with transition densities from the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM). The role of central and spin-orbit parts of the potentials was checked by switching them on and off, and the relation between transition density and inelastic scattering was investigated using artificially modified wave functions and transition densities.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energies: $E_p = 65$ and 200 MeV
- Root-mean-square radii of modified ACM wave functions for the 0$_2^+$ state: (i) 2.97 fm, (ii) 3.55 fm, (iii) 4.38 fm, (iv) 5.65 fm; original ACM value is 3.81 fm
- Renormalization factor for imaginary part of folding potential: $N_W = 0.5 + E_p/1000$
- The calculated results reproduce experimental data for elastic and inelastic cross sections at both energies with no adjustable parameter

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the relation between transition density and inelastic scattering, which were performed for $\alpha$ + $^{12}$C reactions by Takashina and collaborators (2006, 2008). The authors note that while the black sphere model was applied to proton scattering by Iida and collaborators (2012), spin observables were not investigated in that approach. The present study demonstrates that proton inelastic analyzing power provides a clean probe of transition density shape without ambiguity, enabling future extension to other transitions beyond the monopole case.
