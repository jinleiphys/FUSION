# The $η$-3N problem with separable interactions

> The $η$-3N-interaction is studied within the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky theory adopting purely separable forms for the two- and three-body subamplitudes, limiting the basic two-body interactions to s-waves only. The corresponding separable approximation for the integral kernels is obtained by using the Hilbert-Schmidt procedure. Results are presented for the $η$-$^3$H scattering amplitude and for the total elastic cross section for energies below the triton break-up threshold.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky formalism with separable interactions can be applied to the η-3N system, but the authors hedge that the results are limited to energies below the triton break-up threshold and that the oversimplified rank-one Yamaguchi NN potential overbinds the three-nucleon system, so the calculations should be considered a starting point rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body η-3H scattering problem using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky theory in momentum space, adopting purely separable rank-one forms for the NN and ηN two-body subamplitudes and using the Hilbert-Schmidt procedure to construct separable representations for the three-body subamplitudes in the (1+3) and (2+2) partitions. Only s-wave interactions were included, and the resulting coupled integral equations were reduced to effective two-body equations via the quasiparticle method, then solved by direct matrix inversion for energies below the triton break-up threshold.

## Key numbers

- η-3H scattering length: a_η_3H = (4.2 + i 5.7) fm, obtained with ηN input corresponding to a_ηN = (0.75 + i 0.27) fm
- The triton binding energy from the model is approximately -13 MeV, compared to the experimental value of approximately -8.5 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy ε_d is approximately -2.22 MeV, which defines the upper limit of the energy region where the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion is valid
- The six leading terms in the separable expansion for the three-nucleon amplitude accumulate more than 95% of the Schmidt norm of the operator near the two-body threshold

## Context

This work extends earlier nonperturbative calculations of η-3N scattering, specifically the optical model approach of Wilkin (1993) and the multiple scattering series summation of Wycech (1995), as well as the finite-rank-approximation (FRA) model of Belyaev (1995). Unlike those approaches, the present calculation treats the four-body dynamics exactly within the separable approximation without neglecting target excitations or nucleon recoil. The results disagree with the FRA model and optical model predictions of a bound η-3H state, finding instead that the interaction is not strong enough to bind the system. The authors note that using more refined NN potentials would likely further reduce the binding probability, and that the present work enables future more realistic calculations with improved separable representations of the NN interaction.
