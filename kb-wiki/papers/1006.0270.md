# Parton radiative processes and pressure isotropization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The impact of radiative processes on kinetic equilibration is studied via a radiative transport model. The 2 <-> 3 processes can significantly increase the level of thermalization. These processes lead to an approximate coupling constant scaling of the evolution of the pressure anisotropy qualitatively different from the case with only 2 -> 2 partonic processes. Furthermore, thermal and Color Glass Condensate motivated initial conditions are shown to share the same asymptotic evolution when 2 <-> 3 processes are included. This emphasizes the unique role of radiative processes in Quark-Gluon Plasma thermalization.

## Key claim

Radiative 2 ↔ 3 processes can significantly increase the level of thermalization in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, leading to an approximate coupling constant scaling of the pressure anisotropy evolution that is qualitatively different from the case with only 2 → 2 partonic processes. The paper shows that thermal and Color Glass Condensate motivated initial conditions share the same asymptotic evolution when 2 ↔ 3 processes are included, but this convergence depends on the initial fugacity of the system.

## Method

The authors used a radiative transport model for massless gluons undergoing longitudinal Bjorken expansion in a central cell, with a formation proper time of 0.5 fm/c and uniform transverse distribution within a 5 fm radius. They compared simulations with only 2 → 2 elastic gluon collisions (using a perturbative QCD cross section regulated by a dynamically calculated Debye screening mass) against simulations that additionally included 2 ↔ 3 radiative processes, where the 2 → 3 cross section was taken as 1/2 of the 2 → 2 cross section and detailed balance was enforced for the 3 → 2 process.

## Key numbers

- Pressure anisotropy at 2 fm/c with radiative processes: approximately 0.78, compared to 0.65 for elastic-only processes (for initial energy density 38.2 GeV/fm³ and coupling constant α_s = 0.6).
- Initial energy densities used: 38.2 GeV/fm³ (equivalent to initial temperature T₀ = 0.5 GeV) and 76.4 GeV/fm³ (equivalent to T₀ = 1.0 GeV).
- Initial space-time rapidity density: dN/dη_s = 1000, within a space-time rapidity region from -5 to +5.
- By 2 fm/c, differences between pressure anisotropy evolutions starting from different initial anisotropies all go to about or below 1% of the initial difference.
- Final fugacities at 2 fm/c for cases with 2 ↔ 3 processes are all on the 80% to 90% level.

## Context

This work extends the study of kinetic equilibration in heavy ion collisions by Xu and Greiner (2007), who showed that radiative processes are important for parton momentum isotropization, and by the Frankfurt group (El et al., 2009), who extended viscous hydrodynamics to third order for pressure isotropization studies. The paper demonstrates that radiative processes produce a qualitatively different scaling behavior (approximate α_s scaling) compared to elastic-only processes (ε₀ α_s scaling), and that the inclusion of 2 ↔ 3 processes makes pressure isotropization robust against changes in the initial momentum distribution, which is relevant for simulations at different collision energies.
