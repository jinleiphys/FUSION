# A unified mechanism for the origin and evolution of nuclear magicity

> A simple pattern of organisation, the nuclear shell structure, emerges from the complex interactions between nucleons in nuclei and determines, to some significant degree, nuclear structure properties. Recent experimental investigations of exotic nuclei revealed a shortfall in our current understanding of nuclear shell evolution and nuclear magicity. We introduce a novel perspective where the Dirac mass kinetic term, which stems from the singular participation of a spin-0 boson in the nuclear strong force, plays a pivotal role in generating the nuclear shell structure. Namely, the combination of the Dirac mass kinetic Term with the spin-orbit term redefines magic numbers both in stable and exotic nuclei. The identification of this mechanism allows to provide a broad understanding of the origin and evolution of nuclear magic numbers.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the Dirac mass kinetic term (DKT), arising from the spin-0 scalar meson mediating the nuclear strong force, as the overlooked mechanism that, in combination with the spin-orbit term, drives the origin and evolution of nuclear magic numbers across the nuclear chart, including in exotic nuclei far from stability.

## Method

The authors perform relativistic mean-field calculations using covariant energy density functionals (cEDFs) with the DD-MEV parametrization, then apply a non-relativistic reduction via a similarity renormalization group transformation to isolate the DKT contribution. They analyze single-particle energy gaps, particularly pseudo-spin-orbit (PSO) gaps, across isotopic and isotonic chains to demonstrate how the DKT governs the evolution of shell structure and the emergence of new magic numbers.

## Key numbers

- The scalar mean potential S is approximately -400 MeV and the vector mean potential V is approximately 350 MeV, with the DKT arising from the spatial variation of S(r)
- The DKT is derived from a 1/M^2 expansion of the relativistic Hamiltonian, where M is the nucleon mass
- The PSO gap between 2d₅/₂ and 1g₇/₂ neutron orbitals in the N=50 chain changes sign around ¹⁰⁰Sn, a behavior only reproduced when the DKT is explicitly included
- The neutron magic number 16 persists from ²⁴O to ³⁶Ca in the N=16 isotonic chain
- The Z=28 gap is predicted to remain robust across the entire Nickel isotopic chain
- The N=56 subshell closure is predicted to erode approaching ⁷⁸Ni (Z=28), with an N=58 subshell gap appearing instead

## Context

This work extends the standard shell model description originating from Mayer (1949) and the spin-orbit coupling framework, while addressing limitations of the tensor force mechanism proposed by Otsuka (2001, 2005) for shell evolution in exotic nuclei. The authors show that traditional non-relativistic energy density functionals (Skyrme, Gogny) that include only central and spin-orbit terms cannot reproduce the sign change of PSO gaps observed experimentally, whereas the explicit inclusion of the DKT from covariant functionals successfully predicts these changes. This mechanism enables systematic predictions of shell evolution across the nuclear chart, including new subshell closures such as N=58 near ⁷⁸Ni that have not yet been experimentally studied.
