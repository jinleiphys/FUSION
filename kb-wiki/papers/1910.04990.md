# Nuclear incompressibility from spherical and deformed nuclei

> We present an analysis based on the deformed Quasi Particle Random Phase Approximation, on top of a deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov description of the ground state, aimed at studying the isoscalar monopole and quadrupole response in a deformed nucleus. This analysis is motivated by the need of understanding the coupling between the two modes and how it might affect the extraction of the nuclear incompressibility from the monopole distribution. After discussing this motivation, we present the main ingredients of our theoretical framework, and we show some results obtained with the SLy4 and SkM$^{*}$ interactions for the nucleus ${}^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim

The coupling between the isoscalar monopole and the K=0 component of the isoscalar quadrupole resonance in deformed nuclei may affect the extraction of the nuclear matter incompressibility from monopole measurements, but the present work only shows preliminary results for the nucleus ²⁴Mg and does not yet provide a revised value of the incompressibility.

## Method

The authors solved Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for axially deformed ²⁴Mg using the HFBTHO code, with a density-dependent pairing delta interaction and either the SLy4 or SkM* Skyrme functional. The ground-state deformation was determined by minimizing the total energy under a quadrupole-moment constraint. On top of the deformed HFB ground state, the isoscalar monopole and quadrupole response was calculated using the deformed Quasi Particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), and the resulting strength distributions and transition densities were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The deformed ground state of ²⁴Mg has a prolate deformation of β ≈ 0.4 for both the SLy4 and SkM* interactions.
- For the SLy4 interaction, the two main monopole peaks are located at 17.76 MeV and 24.29 MeV.
- For the SkM* interaction, the two main monopole peaks are located at 17.62 MeV and 24.03 MeV.
- The pairing interaction parameters used are V_pair = -280 MeV fm³, ρ_c = 0.16 fm⁻³, and γ = 0.5.
- The Energy Weighted Sum Rules are preserved within less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in closed-shell nuclei, which point to a nuclear matter incompressibility K_∞ ≈ 240 ± 20 MeV (Gar18), by addressing the open question of how deformation affects the monopole strength in open-shell nuclei. The coupling between the ISGMR and the K=0 component of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) in axially deformed nuclei, previously noted by Rag05, is investigated here using a microscopic deformed QRPA framework. The authors plan to implement angular-momentum projection of the QRPA phonons to cleanly decouple the two modes and enable a more reliable extraction of the monopole peak energy.
