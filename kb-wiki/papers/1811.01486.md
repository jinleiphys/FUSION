# Beyond mean-field approach for pear-shaped hypernuclei

> We develop both relativistic mean field and beyond approaches for hypernuclei with possible quadrupole-octupole deformation or pear-like shapes based on relativistic point-coupling energy density functionals. The symmetries broken in the mean-field states are recovered with parity, particle-number, and angular momentum projections. We take $^{21}_Λ$Ne as an example to illustrate the method, where the $Λ$ hyperon is put on one of the two lowest-energy orbits (labeled as $Λ_s, Λ_p$), respectively. We find that the $Λ$ hyperon in both cases disfavors the formation of a reflection-asymmetric molecular-like $^{16}$O$+α$ structure in $^{20}$Ne, which is consistent with the Nilsson diagram for the hyperon in $(β_2, β_3)$ deformation plane. In particular, we show that the negative-parity states with the configuration $^{20}$Ne($K^π=0^-)\otimes Λ_s$ are close in energy to those with the configuration $^{20}$Ne($K^π=0^+)\otimes Λ_p$, even though they have very different structures. The $Λ_s$ ($Λ_p$) becomes more and more concentrated around the bottom (top) of the "pear" with the increase of octupole deformation.

## Key claim

The authors develop a beyond-mean-field framework for hypernuclei with pear-like (quadrupole-octupole) shapes, and find that a Λ hyperon in either the lowest (Λ_s) or second-lowest (Λ_p) orbit disfavors the formation of a reflection-asymmetric molecular-like ¹⁶O+α cluster structure in ²⁰Ne, but the negative-parity states built on ²⁰Ne(K^π=0⁻)⊗Λ_s are close in energy to those built on ²⁰Ne(K^π=0⁺)⊗Λ_p, despite very different intrinsic structures.

## Method

The authors perform relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for the hypernucleus ²¹_ΛNe using the point-coupling energy density functionals PC-F1 for nucleon-nucleon interactions and PCY-S2 for ΛN interactions, with pairing correlations treated in the BCS approximation. They then restore broken symmetries by applying parity, particle-number, and angular-momentum projection to the mean-field states, and compute the projected energies of low-lying states as a function of quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation parameters.

## Key numbers

- The band-head energy of the negative-parity K^π=0⁻ band in ²⁰Ne is around 5.8 MeV, comparable to the typical energy to excite a Λ_s to Λ_p.
- The oscillator frequency used in the spherical harmonic oscillator basis is ℏω = 41A⁻¹/³ MeV, with 12 major shells.
- The quadrupole deformation is fixed at β₂ = 0.60 for the projection calculations shown in Fig. 4.

## Context

This work extends the beyond-RMF approach with parity, particle-number, and angular-momentum projections previously applied to octupole-shaped ordinary nuclei (Yao 2015, Zhou 2016) and to hypernucleus ²¹_ΛNe without octupole degrees of freedom (Mei 2016). It builds on earlier studies of ²¹_ΛNe using a three-cluster model (Yamada 1984), a microscopic cluster model (Hiyama 2009), and the hypernuclear AMD model (Isaka 2011), but the present method is not limited to light hypernuclei and can treat excited hyperon states more easily. The results enable future configuration-mixing calculations via the generator coordinate method and extension to other exotic shapes such as tetrahedral deformations.
