# New structures in the proton-antiproton system

> In the most recent measurements of the reaction $e^+e^- \rightarrow p\bar{p}$ by the BABAR collaboration, new structures have been found with unknown origin. We examine a possible relation of the most distinct peak to the recently observed $Φ(2170)$. Alternatively, we analyse possible explanations due to the nucleon$\,\barΔ$ and $Δ\barΔ$ thresholds. The latter could explain a periodicity found in the data.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether the distinct peak observed in the BABAR data for the reaction \(e^+e^- \rightarrow p\bar{p}\) around 2.2 GeV could be related to the \(\phi(2170)\) vector meson or alternatively explained by threshold cusps from the \(N\bar{\Delta}\) and \(\Delta\bar{\Delta}\) channels, but the authors emphasize that the separation of resonance structures from background is highly ambiguous and the analysis remains conceptual rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors performed fits to the effective proton form factor \(G_{\rm eff}^p\) using Breit-Wigner resonance shapes to test compatibility with the \(\phi(2170)\) parameters, and separately constructed triangle diagram integrals (Passarino-Veltman scalar \(C_0\) functions) for virtual \(N\bar{\Delta}\pi\) and \(\Delta\bar{\Delta}\pi\) states to model threshold cusp effects. They also carried out simultaneous fits to seven data sets including spacelike and timelike proton and neutron form factors, using a dispersion relation framework with effective pole terms and continuum contributions from \(2\pi\), \(K\bar{K}\), and \(\rho\pi\) channels.

## Key numbers

* The \(\phi(2170)\) mass from PDG compilations ranges between 2079 and 2192 MeV across different experiments, with widths from 58 to 192 MeV.
* Test fits to \(G_{\rm eff}^p\) alone yield a resonance mass range \(M \approx (2 - 2.18)\) GeV and width \(\Gamma \approx (0.05 - 0.5)\) GeV.
* A second resonance at \(M = 2.43\) GeV with width \(\Gamma = 0.23\) GeV improves the data description.
* In simultaneous fits to all data, fixing the resonance mass at \(M = 2.125\) GeV gives a width of \(\Gamma = 0.088\) GeV.
* The fit including triangle diagrams uses parameters \(f_{N\Delta} = 0.02\), \(f_{\Delta\Delta} = 0.3\), \(\Lambda_{N\Delta} = 10\) GeV, and \(\Lambda_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.7\) GeV.
* The total data set comprises 1627 points, with 1422 from electron-proton scattering cross sections from MAMI.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of nucleon form factors by groups such as Bianconi (2015) and Haidenbauer (2014), who studied pQCD parametrizations and chiral EFT descriptions of the final-state interaction near threshold. The paper addresses structures in the BABAR data (Lees 2013) that cannot be fully described by conventional dipole or pQCD fits, and suggests that future measurements from the PANDA experiment at FAIR could help distinguish between the \(\phi(2170)\) resonance and threshold cusp explanations by providing polarization data and improved form factor ratio determinations.
