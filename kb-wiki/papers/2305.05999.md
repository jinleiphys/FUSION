# Detectability of a phase transition in neutron star matter with third-generation gravitational wave interferometers

> Possible strong first-order hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron star interiors leave an imprint on gravitational waves, which could be detected with planned third-generation interferometers. Given a signal from the late inspiral of a binary neutron star (BNS) coalescence, %the possibility of assessing the presence of such a phase transition depends on the precision that can be attained in the determination of the tidal deformability parameter, as well as on the model used to describe the hybrid star equation of state. For the latter, we employ here a phenomenological meta-modelling of the equation of state that largely spans the parameter space associated with both the low density phase and the quark high density compatible with current constraints. We show that with a network of third-generation detectors, a single loud BNS event might be sufficient to infer the presence of a phase transition at low baryon densities with an average Bayes factor $B\approx 100$, up to a luminosity distance ($\mathcal{D}_L \lesssim$ 300 Mpc).

## Key claim

With a network of third-generation gravitational wave detectors (Einstein Telescope and two Cosmic Explorer detectors), a single loud binary neutron star coalescence event might be sufficient to infer the presence of a strong first-order hadron-quark phase transition at low baryon densities, but only up to a luminosity distance of approximately 300 Mpc and with an average Bayes factor of about 100, depending on the specific model used to describe the hybrid star equation of state.

## Method

The authors employed a phenomenological meta-modelling approach for the nuclear equation of state, using 16 parameters for purely nucleonic matter and 19 parameters for hybrid matter including a first-order phase transition characterized by the onset density, energy density jump, and sound speed in the quark phase. They used the Fisher information formalism implemented in the public tool gwbench to estimate measurement uncertainties for simulated binary neutron star coalescence events, then applied a Bayesian framework to compute Bayes factors comparing the nucleonic and hybrid hypotheses against simulated observations.

## Key numbers

- Average Bayes factor B ≈ 100 for inferring a phase transition from a single loud binary neutron star event with third-generation detectors
- Luminosity distance threshold D_L ≲ 300 Mpc for detectable phase transition signatures
- Three hybrid model families defined by transition onset densities: n_t = 0.3 fm⁻³ (PT03), 0.4 fm⁻³ (PT04), and 0.5 fm⁻³ (PT05)
- Squared sound speed in quark phase varied randomly between 0.1 c² and 0.9 c²
- Chirp mass fixed at M_c = 1.186 M_sun for the GW170817-informed prior
- Maximum mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432: M_max = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies that compared only a few specific equation of state models with and without phase transitions (Chatziioannou 2019, Chen 2019, Coupechoux 2023) by using a flexible meta-modelling approach that spans a much larger parameter space. It also differs from non-parametric inference studies (Landry 2020, Essick 2020) and Bayesian inference with limited injected models (Pang 2020) by systematically varying both the nucleonic and quark-phase parameters. The results enable future work on distinguishing phase transition signatures using third-generation detectors, though the authors note that a random variation of the transition onset density would systematically favor the purely nucleonic model regardless of observation.
