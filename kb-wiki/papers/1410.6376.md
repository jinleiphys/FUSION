# Fully damped Mott oscillations in sub-barrier elastic scattering of identical heavy ions and the nuclear interaction

> We investigate the possible disappearance of Mott oscillations in the scattering of bosonic nuclei at sub-barrier energies. This effect is universal and happens at a critical value of the Sommerfeld parameter. It is also found that the inclusion of the short-range nuclear interaction has a profound influence on this phenomenon. Thus we suggest that the study of this lack of Mott oscillation, which we call, "transverse isotropy" is a potentially useful mean to study the nuclear interaction.

# Fully damped Mott oscillations in sub-barrier elastic scattering of identical heavy ions and the nuclear interaction

## Key claim
The disappearance of Mott oscillations (termed "transverse isotropy") in the elastic scattering of identical bosonic nuclei is a universal effect occurring at a critical Sommerfeld parameter, but the inclusion of the short-range nuclear interaction shifts this critical value and can produce multiple flat regions in the angular distribution, making the phenomenon a potentially useful probe of the nuclear interaction at sub-barrier energies.

## Method
The authors calculated the second derivative of the symmetrized cross section at theta = 90 degrees for the alpha+alpha system, first for pure Coulomb scattering and then including a nuclear potential composed of two Gaussians (attractive and repulsive) taken from Abdullah et al. (2006). They performed optical model calculations solving the Schrodinger equation exactly to determine the energies at which the second derivative crosses zero, indicating transverse isotropy.

## Key numbers
- The critical Sommerfeld parameter for pure Coulomb Mott scattering is eta_c = sqrt(2)
- For the alpha+alpha system, eta_c = sqrt(2) corresponds to a center-of-mass energy of E_cm = 0.397 MeV
- The Coulomb barrier height for alpha+alpha is V_B = 0.87 MeV
- The nuclear potential parameters used: V_A = 122.62 MeV, R_A = 2.132 fm (attractive Gaussian); V_R = 3.0 MeV, R_R = 2.0 fm (repulsive Gaussian)
- The Coulomb radius used: R_C = 5.8 fm
- With nuclear interaction included, the optical model calculation shows two clear transverse isotropy crossings at E_cm = 0.5 MeV and E_cm = 2.5 MeV, with a near-crossing at approximately E_cm = 1.4 MeV
- The data from Abdullah et al. (2006) show flat regions at E_cm = 1.0 MeV and E_cm = 1.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the theoretical prediction by Canto (2001) that pure Coulomb Mott scattering becomes flat at eta = sqrt(2), and compares with experimental data from Abdullah et al. (2006) for alpha+alpha scattering. The authors find that the nuclear interaction shifts the predicted transverse isotropy from 0.4 MeV to 0.5 MeV and introduces additional flat regions not present in pure Coulomb calculations. The study suggests that measuring elastic scattering of alpha+alpha at around E_cm = 0.4 MeV could verify these predictions and provide a new method to study the nuclear interaction at very low energies.
