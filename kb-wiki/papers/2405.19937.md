# High-accuracy Nuclear Spin Dependent Parity Violating Amplitudes in $^{133}$Cs

> Relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory at the singles and doubles approximation has been implemented to estimate nuclear spin dependent (NSD) parity violating (PV) electric dipole (E1) transition amplitudes ($E1_{PV}^{NSD}$) among hyperfine levels of the $6s ~^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 7s ~^2S_{1/2}$ transition in $^{133}$Cs. To validate our calculations, we reproduce the Dirac-Hartree-Fock values and results from the combined coupled-Dirac-Hartree-Fock and random phase approximation (CPDF-RPA) method reported earlier. Contributions from the double-core-polarization (DCP) effects at the CPDF-RPA method were found to be between 3-12\% among different hyperfine levels. We derived a generalized expression for $E1_{PV}^{NSD}$, which helped incorporate both the NSD PV Hamiltonian and E1 operator simultaneously in the perturbative approach to account for the DCP contributions. The RCC method subsumes the CPDF-RPA and DCP effects in addition to contributions from the Brückner pair-correlations and normalization of the wave functions, and correlations among them. To improve accuracy of the $E1_{PV}^{NSD}$ amplitudes further, we replace the {\it ab initio} values of the E1 matrix elements and energies by their experimental values via a sum-over-states approach.

## Key claim

The authors implement relativistic coupled-cluster theory at the singles and doubles approximation (RCCSD) to compute nuclear spin dependent parity violating electric dipole transition amplitudes among hyperfine levels of the 6s → 7s transition in ¹³³Cs, claiming improved accuracy by including double-core-polarization effects and replacing ab initio E1 matrix elements and energies with experimental values via a sum-over-states approach, though the final results still depend on the nuclear parameter K_W to be inferred from experiment.

## Method

The authors first compute Dirac-Hartree-Fock, coupled-Dirac-Hartree-Fock (CPDF), random phase approximation (RPA), and combined CPDF-RPA results for the NSD PV amplitudes, then apply relativistic coupled-cluster theory at the singles and doubles level (RCCSD) to incorporate Brückner pair-correlations, core-polarization, and double-core-polarization effects to all orders. They derive a generalized expression for the reduced matrix element using 9j-symbols that allows simultaneous treatment of the NSD PV Hamiltonian and E1 operator in the perturbative approach. To improve accuracy, they replace ab initio E1 matrix elements and energies with experimental values in a sum-over-states approach for the RCCSD results.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of NSD PV amplitudes in ¹³³Cs by Johnson (2003) using RPA, Mani (2011) using perturbative RCC, and Safronova (2009) using the SD method, addressing the disagreement between atomic studies and nuclear theory on the nuclear anapole moment. The improved RCCSD results enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear spin-dependent parameter K_W and the anapole moment coupling constant from the existing experimental measurements by Wood (1997), potentially resolving the sign discrepancy with Tl measurements and the variance with shell model predictions from Haxton (2001) and Wilburn (1998).
