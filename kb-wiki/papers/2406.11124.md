# Time-dependent density functional theory study of induced-fission dynamics of $^{226}$Th

> A microscopic finite-temperature model based on time-dependent nuclear density functional theory (TDDFT), is employed to study the induced-fission process of $^{226}$Th. The saddle-to-scission dynamics of this process is explored, starting from various points on the deformation surface of Helmholtz free energy at a temperature that corresponds to the experimental excitation energy, and following self-consistent isentropic fission trajectories as they evolve toward scission. Dissipation effects and the formation of excited fragments are investigated and, in particular, the difference in the evolution of the local temperature along asymmetric and symmetric fission trajectories. The relative entropies and entanglement between fission fragments emerging at scission are analyzed.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature TDDFT model can follow isentropic fission trajectories of $^{226}$Th from saddle to scission, but only for initial points beyond the outer fission barrier because TDDFT cannot be applied in the classically forbidden region of the collective space where the potential energy is higher than the total energy of the initial state.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent covariant density functional theory calculations on a 3D lattice with 1.0 fm mesh spacing and box size $20\times20\times60$ fm$^3$, using the PC-PK1 relativistic energy density functional. Initial states at temperature $T=0.8$ MeV were obtained from deformation-constrained relativistic DFT calculations, and the time-dependent Kohn-Sham equation was solved with a predictor-corrector method using a time step of $6.67\times10^{-4}$ zs. The chemical potential and temperature were adjusted at each time step to conserve particle number and total energy, yielding isentropic trajectories with constant local entropy.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{226}$Th at $T=0$: $-1730.47$ MeV (calculated), compared to experimental value $-1730.51$ MeV
- Initial temperature for induced fission trajectories: $T=0.8$ MeV, corresponding to internal excitation energy $E^*_{FS} = 10.47$ MeV (matching the peak photon energy of 11 MeV from Schmidt (2000))
- For asymmetric trajectory 1: dissipation energy $E^{*,dis}=9.56$ MeV, scission temperature $T_{sci}=0.82$ MeV, prescission kinetic energy $E^{k,pre}=12.19$ MeV
- For asymmetric trajectory 2: dissipation energy $E^{*,dis}=11.00$ MeV, scission temperature $T_{sci}=0.90$ MeV, prescission kinetic energy $E^{k,pre}=12.56$ MeV
- For symmetric trajectory 3: dissipation energy $E^{*,dis}=3.73$ MeV, scission temperature $T_{sci}=0.78$ MeV, prescission kinetic energy $E^{k,pre}=6.23$ MeV
- For symmetric trajectory 4: dissipation energy $E^{*,dis}=3.57$ MeV, scission temperature $T_{sci}=0.74$ MeV, prescission kinetic energy $E^{k,pre}=5.57$ MeV
- For symmetric trajectory 6 (compact scission): dissipation energy $E^{*,dis}=9.91$ MeV, scission temperature $T_{sci}=0.78$ MeV, prescission kinetic energy $E^{k,pre}=16.38$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature TDDFT framework previously applied to asymmetric fission of $^{234}$U, $^{240}$Pu, $^{244}$Cm, and $^{250}$Cf by Li (2023) to the $^{226}$Th system, which exhibits a three-peak structure (both symmetric and asymmetric) in its charge yield distribution as measured by Schmidt (2000) and Chatillon (2019). The study contrasts with the finite-temperature TDGCM approach used by Zhao (2019) for $^{226}$Th, which cannot describe energy dissipation or fragment heating. The analysis of nucleon localization functions in the neck region builds on prior work by Ren (2022) on cluster formation in fission at zero temperature, and the entanglement analysis extends methods from Li (2024) on multinucleon transfer reactions.
