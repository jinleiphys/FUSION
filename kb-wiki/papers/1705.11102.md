# Lorentz Structure of Vector Part of Matrix Elements of Transitions n <-> p, Caused by Strong Low-Energy Interactions and Hypothesis of Conservation of Charged Vector Current

> We analyse the Lorentz structure of the matrix elements of the transitions "neutron <-->proton", induced by the charged hadronic vector current. We show that the term maintaining conservation of the charged hadronic vector current even for different masses of the neutron and proton (see T. Leitner et al., Phys. Rev. C 73, 065502 (2006) and A. M. Ankowski, arXiv:1601.06169 [hep-ph]) has a dynamical origin, related to the G-even first class current contribution. We show that because of invariance of strong low-energy interactions under the G-parity transformations, the G-odd contribution with the Lorentz structure $q_μ$, where $q_μ$ is a momentum transferred, does not appear in the matrix elements of the ``neutron <-> proton' transitions.

## Key claim

The term that enforces conservation of the charged hadronic vector current (CVC) even when neutron and proton masses differ, specifically the Lorentz structure (−q_μ q̸/q²)F₁(q²) used by Leitner et al. (2006) and Ankowski (2016), has a dynamical origin from G-even first class current contributions, not from second class currents. The G-odd contribution with Lorentz structure q_μ is forbidden from appearing in the matrix elements because strong low-energy interactions are invariant under G-parity transformations.

## Method

The author analyzes the Lorentz structure of the n → p transition matrix element induced by the charged hadronic vector current using a strongly coupled πN system with linear pseudoscalar pion-nucleon interaction. The matrix element is computed to order g²_π using path-integral techniques, separating contributions from the nucleon part and the mesonic part of the isovector vector current. The resulting momentum integrals are evaluated in the heavy baryon approximation under the conditions m²_N ≫ m²_π ≫ q², and the Lorentz structures are classified by their G-parity to determine which terms are allowed by first class currents.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient for the q_μ q̸ term from the mesonic part is c = (1/(6 m_N m_π)) arctan(m_N / m_π), where m_N = (m_p + m_n)/2 is the average nucleon mass and m_π is the pion mass.
- The coefficient for the q_μ q̸ term from the nucleon part is c′ = −1/(24 m²_N), which is much smaller than the mesonic contribution.
- The calculation uses the relation m²_N ≫ m²_π ≫ q² to justify the heavy baryon approximation for evaluating the momentum integrals.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the CVC hypothesis for neutron-proton transitions with unequal masses, following the formulation of Leitner et al. (2006) and Ankowski (2016) who introduced a term (−q_μ q̸/q²)F₁(q²) to maintain current conservation. The paper provides a dynamical justification for that term using the πN model, showing it arises from the mesonic part of the isovector vector current rather than from second class currents. The result confirms the Weinberg (1958) and Lee (1956) classification of first class currents and shows that G-parity invariance of strong interactions prohibits the appearance of the q_μ Lorentz structure. This enables a consistent treatment of CVC in neutrino-nucleus scattering calculations where the neutron and proton masses differ.
