# Neutron matter from chiral two- and three-nucleon calculations up to N$^3$LO

> Neutron matter is an ideal laboratory for nuclear interactions derived from chiral effective field theory since all contributions are predicted up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in the chiral expansion. By making use of recent advances in the partial-wave decomposition of three- nucleon (3N) forces, we include for the first time N$^3$LO 3N interactions in many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) up to third order and in self-consistent Green's function theory (SCGF). Using these two complementary many-body frameworks we provide improved predictions for the equation of state of neutron matter at zero temperature and also analyze systematically the many-body convergence for different chiral EFT interactions. Furthermore, we present an extension of the normal-ordering framework to finite temperatures. These developments open the way to improved calculations of neutron-rich matter including estimates of theoretical uncertainties for astrophysical applications.

# Neutron matter from chiral two- and three-nucleon calculations up to N$^3$LO

## Key claim

The paper provides improved predictions for the neutron matter equation of state by including, for the first time, subleading three-nucleon (3N) forces at N$^3$LO beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation in many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) and self-consistent Green's function theory (SCGF), but the net effect of these subleading 3N contributions on the energy per particle at saturation density is relatively small (a few MeV or less) and varies in sign depending on the specific nucleon-nucleon potential used.

## Method

The authors performed many-body perturbation theory calculations up to third order and self-consistent Green's function theory calculations (ladder approximation) for infinite neutron matter at zero temperature, using chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials at N$^3$LO combined with 3N forces at both N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO. The 3N interactions were included via a normal-ordering framework that produces density-dependent effective NN interactions in a partial-wave momentum basis, allowing 3N forces to be treated on the same footing as NN forces in the many-body expansion. The calculations were performed for three different N$^3$LO NN potentials (EM 500 MeV, EGM 450/500 MeV, and EGM 450/700 MeV) with variations of the 3N cutoff and low-energy constants to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The total uncertainty band for the energy per particle in neutron matter at saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm^-3) combining all Hamiltonians is E/N(n0) = (14.7 - 21.1) MeV, compared to the corrected previous band of (14.3 - 21.1) MeV from Kruger et al. (2013).
- For the EM 500 MeV potential with N$^3$LO 3N forces, the many-body uncertainty at saturation density provides contributions of about -2.5 MeV from the variation of spectra and many-body order in MBPT.
- The 4N Hartree-Fock contribution to the energy in neutron matter at saturation density is E_4N/N ~ -180 keV, which is very small compared to the overall uncertainty.
- The normal-ordering framework at finite temperatures was benchmarked up to T = 50 MeV, showing excellent agreement between the partial-wave and operatorial approaches for N$^2$LO 3N Hartree-Fock energies at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous N$^3$LO calculations of neutron matter by Tews et al. (2013) and Kruger et al. (2013), which could only include subleading 3N forces at the Hartree-Fock level due to the lack of partial-wave matrix elements. By utilizing the partial-wave decomposition of 3N forces developed by Hebeler et al. (2015) and the normal-ordering framework of Drischler et al. (2016), the authors systematically include N$^3$LO 3N contributions beyond Hartree-Fock in both MBPT and SCGF. The results enable improved calculations of neutron-rich matter with theoretical uncertainty estimates for astrophysical applications, and the extension to finite temperatures opens the way for future finite-temperature nuclear matter calculations.
