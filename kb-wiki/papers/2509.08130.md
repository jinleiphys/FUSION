# Wave-Breaking Phenomena in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We investigate the onset of wave-breaking in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and the LHC. A nonlinear longitudinal color field is coupled to a three-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic background constrained by experimental conditions: Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV (LHC) and Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV (RHIC). The instantaneous wave-breaking threshold is determined from the in-medium plasma frequency, while the field evolution follows a nonlinear Landau equation with Debye screening and expansion damping. At the LHC, the higher initial temperature and density drive the system above the threshold within $τ\lesssim $ 2.3 fm/$c$, whereas at RHIC the cooler and more dilute medium delays wave-breaking to $τ\simeq 2.8$~fm/$c$. Scans over initial temperature, density, thermalization time, QGP lifetime, and collision system (RHIC vs LHC) confirm the robustness of this instability against parameter variations. These results identify wave-breaking as a universal microscopic mechanism for the early loss of coherence and rapid onset of hydrodynamics in the QGP, providing a common explanation for the fast equilibration observed at both RHIC and the LHC.

# Wave-Breaking Phenomena in Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Wave-breaking in the quark-gluon plasma occurs within the first few fm/c of the plasma lifetime at both RHIC and LHC energies, providing a universal microscopic mechanism for the early loss of coherence and rapid onset of hydrodynamics. The claim is conditional on the specific initial conditions: at the LHC the threshold is crossed within tau less than or approximately 2.3 fm/c, while at RHIC the cooler and more dilute medium delays wave-breaking to tau approximately 2.8 fm/c.

## Method

A nonlinear longitudinal chromoelectric field is coupled to a three-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic background with boost-invariant Bjorken expansion. The instantaneous wave-breaking threshold is determined from the in-medium plasma frequency, while the field evolution follows a nonlinear Landau-type equation incorporating Debye screening, expansion damping, and nonlinear self-interaction. The initial conditions are constrained by global hydrodynamic analyses and bulk hadron spectra for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV (LHC) and Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (RHIC), with parameter scans over initial temperature, density, thermalization time, and QGP lifetime.

## Key numbers

- LHC wave-breaking onset: tau less than or approximately 2.3 fm/c for Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- RHIC wave-breaking onset: tau approximately 2.8 fm/c for Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- LHC initial conditions: T0 approximately 0.50 GeV, n0 approximately 6.5 fm^-3, tau0 approximately 0.4 fm/c
- RHIC initial conditions: T0 approximately 0.32 GeV, n0 approximately 3 fm^-3, tau0 approximately 0.8 fm/c
- LHC QGP lifetime: tau_f approximately 10 fm/c
- RHIC QGP lifetime: tau_f approximately 7 fm/c
- LHC parameter ranges: T0 0.45-0.55 GeV, n0 5-8 fm^-3, tau0 0.2-0.6 fm/c
- RHIC parameter ranges: T0 0.30-0.35 GeV, n0 2.5-4 fm^-3, tau0 0.6-1.0 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the concept of wave-breaking from electromagnetic plasmas (Dawson 1959, Maity 2013) to QCD matter under realistic heavy-ion collision conditions, building on prior work on plasma instabilities in the QGP (Mrowczynski 1993, Romatschke 2003). The predicted wave-breaking timescales coincide with the experimentally observed onset of elliptic and higher-order anisotropic flow measured by ALICE at the LHC and by PHOBOS/PHENIX at RHIC, as well as with quarkonium suppression trends and enhanced early thermal photon and dilepton emission, offering testable signatures for future studies combining these instabilities with full event-by-event hydrodynamics and jet transport.
