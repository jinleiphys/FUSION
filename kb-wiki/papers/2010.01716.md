# Few-neutron systems with the long-range Casimir-Polder force

> In this work we present results of the long-range electromagnetic Casimir-Polder interactions between two neutrons, a neutron and a conducting wall, and a neutron between two walls. As input, we use the dynamic dipole polarizabilities of the neutron fitted to chiral EFT results up to the pion production threshold and at the onset of the Delta resonance. Our work can be relevant to the physics of confined ultracold neutrons inside bottles.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the long-range Casimir-Polder (CP) electromagnetic interactions between two neutrons, a neutron and a conducting wall, and a neutron between two walls, using dynamic dipole polarizabilities fitted to chiral effective field theory results; however, the results are not reliable for distances shorter than about 30 fm, and the asymptotic static limit is only reached beyond 1000 fm.

## Method

The authors parametrized the neutron's dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities using formulas that incorporate the non-analytic threshold behavior of pion photoproduction and a Breit-Wigner form for the Delta resonance, fitted to the chiral EFT predictions of Lensky, McGovern, and Pascalutsa (2015). They then used these frequency-dependent polarizabilities in standard Casimir-Polder integral expressions (e.g., from Spruch and Kelsey (1978)) to compute the CP potentials for the three systems: neutron-neutron, neutron-wall, and neutron between two walls.

## Key numbers

- Static electric dipole polarizability of the neutron: α_n(0) = 13.7 ± 3.1 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from chiral EFT); static magnetic dipole polarizability: β_n(0) = 4.6 ± 2.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The asymptotic CP potential between two neutrons at large separation is V_CP,nn*(r) = -[23(α_n²(0)+β_n²(0)) - 14α_n(0)β_n(0)]/(4π r⁷).
- The asymptotic CP potential between a neutron and a conducting wall is V_CP,nW*(r) = -3α_n(0)/(8π r⁴).
- The CP potential for a neutron between two walls shows an asymptotic L⁻⁴ behavior, with the explicit form given by V_CP,WnW*(z,L) = -π³α_n(0)/(α₀ L⁴) × [(3 - 2cos²(πf/2))/(8cos⁴(πf/2)) - 1/360], where f = 2z/L.
- The 1/r⁶ van der Waals behavior is observed for distances r ≲ 20 fm; the 1/r⁷ asymptotic CP behavior is achieved only beyond 10³ fm.
- The neutron-wall CP interaction at r ∼ 1500 fm is comparable to the repulsive Fermi pseudo-potential energy near nickel (252 neV) and aluminium (54 neV) surfaces.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Arnold (1973), who used only the static polarizability and the asymptotic 1/r⁷ potential for neutron-neutron scattering, by incorporating frequency-dependent polarizabilities from modern chiral EFT. It follows the same approach as Spruch and Kelsey (1978) for atomic systems, applying it to neutron physics. The results are relevant for the physics of confined ultracold neutrons in bottles and may also inform studies of possible three- and four-neutron resonances, where the CP force could alter the nature of such states.
