# Kinetics of chiral phase transition in hot and dense quark matter

> The kinetics of chiral transitions in hot and dense quark matter is studied via a microscopic framework (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and a phenomenological model (Ginzburg-Landau free energy). We focus on the far-from-equilibrium ordering dynamics subsequent to a quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase. The morphology of the ordering system is characterized by the scaling of the order-parameter correlation function. The domain growth process obeys the Allen-Cahn growth law, $L(t)\sim t^{1/2}$. We also study the growth of bubble of the stable massive phase in a background of the metastable massive phase.

## Key claim

The kinetics of chiral phase transitions in hot and dense quark matter obey the Allen-Cahn growth law $L(t)\sim t^{1/2}$ for both second-order and first-order transitions, but only in the late-stage domain growth regime; for shallow quenches through the first-order line, the massless phase is metastable and the transition initially proceeds via nucleation and growth of droplets before entering the same coarsening regime.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to obtain the equilibrium phase diagram of chiral symmetry breaking in the temperature-chemical potential plane, then constructed an equivalent $M^6$ Ginzburg-Landau free energy by fitting coefficients to the NJL thermodynamic potential. They solved the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation numerically on a $256^3$ lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using an Euler-discretization scheme, to simulate the far-from-equilibrium ordering dynamics after a quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum quark mass $M \simeq 321$ MeV, obtained with ultraviolet cutoff $\Lambda = 631$ MeV and four-fermion coupling $G = 5.074 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$.
* At $T=0$, the first-order chiral transition occurs at chemical potential $\mu \simeq 335$ MeV.
* For $\mu=0$, a second-order transition occurs at temperature $T \simeq 195$ MeV.
* The tricritical point (tcp) is located at $(\mu_{tcp}, T_{tcp}) \simeq (285.0, 74.9)$ MeV.
* For $T=15$ MeV, the dimensionless parameter $\lambda$ takes values 0.065, 0.14, 0.23, and 0.34 as $\mu$ takes values 320, 330, 338, and 350 MeV respectively.
* The domain growth obeys the Allen-Cahn law $L(t) \sim t^{1/2}$ in all late-stage regimes.

## Context

This work extends the study of ordering kinetics, previously applied to condensed matter systems like ferromagnets and binary fluids, to the quark-hadron phase transition in high-energy physics. The authors use the NJL model (Klevansky, 1992) to obtain the phase diagram and then map it to a Ginzburg-Landau free energy, following the approach of Ohta et al. (1982) for the scaling function. The results enable predictions for the dynamical evolution of the chiral order parameter in heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly those exploring the high baryon density regime around the critical point (as discussed by the Critical Point of QCD collaboration).
