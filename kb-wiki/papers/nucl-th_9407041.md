# Pion Cloud Contribution to K+ Nucleus Scattering

> A careful reanalysis is done of the contribution to $K^{+}$ nucleus scattering from the interaction of the kaon with the virtual pion cloud. The usual approximations made in the evaluation of the related kaon selfenergy are shown to fail badly. We also find new interaction mechanisms which provide appreciable corrections to the kaon selfenergy. Some of these contribute to the imaginary part below pion creation threshold. The inclusion of these new mechanisms in the inelastic part of the optical potential produces a significant improvement in the differential and total $K^{+}$ nuclear cross sections. Uncertainties remain in the dispersive part of the optical potential.

## Key claim

The standard static approximation used in prior evaluations of the pion cloud contribution to the K+ nucleus optical potential fails badly, and new interaction mechanisms (K+ decaying into K + ph + ph, K + ph + Δh, and K + Δh + Δh) provide appreciable corrections that significantly improve agreement with experimental differential and total cross sections, though uncertainties remain in the dispersive (real) part of the optical potential.

## Method

The authors calculated the K+ selfenergy in infinite nuclear matter using a field-theoretical approach with a renormalized pion propagator (including ph and Δh excitations) and a crossing-symmetric Kπ amplitude from prior work. They evaluated the imaginary part of the selfenergy via Cutkosky rules, subtracting terms linear in density to isolate the genuine meson exchange current (MEC) contribution, and applied the local density approximation to obtain the optical potential for finite nuclei. The resulting optical potential (impulse approximation plus MEC) was used to solve the Klein-Gordon equation numerically for K+ scattering on 12C and 40Ca.

## Key numbers

- The excess number of pions per nucleon in nuclear matter at normal density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³) is 0.33, and in 12C it is 0.17 pions per nucleon.
- The static approximation result for Im δΠ (the "standard" mechanism) is about twice the exact result at normal density.
- Threshold kinetic energies for the new MEC channels: d2 (K → K ph ph) has threshold T_K > 0 MeV; d3 (K → K ph Δh) has threshold T_K ≥ 181 MeV; d4 (K → K Δh Δh) has threshold T_K ≥ 392 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the pion selfenergy from 2p2h absorption uses Im C₀ = 0.096 m_π⁻⁶.
- At T_K = 450 MeV, the d3 diagram contributes more than half of the total MEC effect.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Siegel et al. (1985) and by Brown et al. (1988) on the pion cloud contribution to K+ nucleus scattering, correcting the static approximation used in those works and introducing new reaction channels (K → K ph ph, K → K ph Δh) not previously considered. The results enable improved optical potential calculations for K+ scattering on nuclei, addressing systematic discrepancies between earlier microscopic calculations and experimental data that had led to speculation about increased nucleon size in the nuclear medium. The approach is analogous to methods used for mesonic Λ decay in nuclei by Oset et al. (1992).
