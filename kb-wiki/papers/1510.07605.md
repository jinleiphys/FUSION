# Constraints on the $ηη'$ decay rate of a scalar glueball from gauge/gravity duality

> Predictions of glueball decay rates in the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model for low-energy QCD can be uniquely extended to include finite quark masses up to an as yet undetermined parameter in the coupling of glueballs to the nonanomalous part of the pseudoscalar mass terms. The assumption of a universal coupling of glueballs to mass terms of the full nonet of pseudoscalar mesons leads to flavor asymmetries in the decay rates of scalar glueballs that agree well with experimental data for the glueball candidate $f_0(1710)$ and implies a vanishing decay rate into $ηη'$ pairs, for which only upper bounds for the $f_0(1710)$ meson are known at present from experiment. Relaxing this assumption, the holographic model gives a tight correlation between the decay rates into pairs of pseudo-Goldstone bosons of same type and $ηη'$ pairs. If $Γ(G\to KK)/Γ(G\toππ)$ is kept within the range reported currently by the Particle Data Group for the $f_0(1710)$ meson, the rate $Γ(G\toηη')/Γ(G\toππ)$ is predicted to be $\lesssim 0.04$. The corresponding situation for $f_0(1500)$ is also discussed, which however is found to be much less compatible with the interpretation of a largely unmixed glueball.

## Key claim

The holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model predicts that if the scalar glueball candidate $f_0(1710)$ is a nearly unmixed glueball, the decay rate $\Gamma(G\to\eta\eta')/\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)$ is $\lesssim 0.04$, provided the ratio $\Gamma(G\to KK)/\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)$ is kept within the range reported by the Particle Data Group; this prediction depends on an undetermined parameter $x = d_m/d_0$ that controls the glueball coupling to nonanomalous pseudoscalar mass terms.

## Method

The authors extend the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model to include finite quark masses via mass terms of the form $\mathcal L_m^{\mathcal M} \propto \Tr(\mathcal M U(x) + h.c.)$, with $\mathcal M = \mathrm{diag}(m_u,m_d,m_s)$. They introduce a free parameter $x = d_m/d_0$ that scales the glueball coupling to the nonanomalous mass terms relative to the known coupling $d_0$ to the Witten-Veneziano mass term, and compute the resulting decay rates for scalar glueballs into pairs of pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi\pi$, $KK$, $\eta\eta$, $\eta\eta'$) as functions of $x$ and the Witten-Veneziano mass $m_0$.

## Key numbers

* The Witten-Veneziano mass $m_0$ in the model ranges from 730 to 967 MeV, for $\MKK = 949$ MeV and 't Hooft coupling $\lambda = 12.55$ to 16.63.
* For the glueball candidate $f_0(1710)$, the predicted ratio $\Gamma(G\to\eta\eta')/\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)$ is $\lesssim 0.04$ when $\Gamma(G\to KK)/\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)$ is kept within the Particle Data Group error band.
* The WA102 experiment gives an upper limit of 0.18 for $\Gamma(f_0(1710)\to\eta\eta')/\Gamma(f_0(1710)\to\pi\pi)$.
* For a glueball with mass set to $f_0(1500)$, the model requires $x\approx 0$ (nonchiral suppression) to approximately reproduce experimental ratios, but the absolute $\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)$ is underestimated by a factor of 2.
* The model predicts $\Gamma(G\to4\pi)/\Gamma(G\to2\pi) \approx 2.5$ and $\Gamma(G\to2\omega)/\Gamma(G\to2\pi) \approx 1.1$ for a pure glueball around 1.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic glueball decay calculations (Brunner and Rebhan, 2015) by relaxing the assumption of universal glueball coupling to all pseudoscalar mass terms, which previously gave a vanishing $\eta\eta'$ decay rate. The results support the interpretation of $f_0(1710)$ as a nearly unmixed scalar glueball, consistent with lattice QCD predictions (Morningstar and Peardon, 1999; Bali et al., 2013) and phenomenological studies (Close and Zhao, 2005; Janowski et al., 2014), while disfavoring $f_0(1500)$ as a pure glueball. The predicted $\eta\eta'$ rate is well below the current WA102 upper limit (Barberis et al., 2000) and contrasts with higher predictions from other phenomenological approaches (Frere et al., 2015).
