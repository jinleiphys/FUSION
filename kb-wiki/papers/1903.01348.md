# Systematics of toroidal dipole modes in Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes

> We analyze the relation between isoscalar toroidal modes and so-called pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) which both appear in the same region of low-energy dipole excitations. To this end, we use a theoretical description within the fully self-consistent Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA). Test cases are spherical nuclei $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{58,72}$Ni, $^{90,100}$Zr, and $^{100,120,132}$Sn which cover four different elements and for each element at least two isotopes with different neutron excess, one small and another large. The structure of the modes is investigated in terms of strength functions, transition densities (TD) and transition currents (TC). For all considered nuclei, we see that, independently on whether PDR strength exists or not, the flow pattern in the lower part of the "PDR energy region" is basically the isoscalar vortical toroidal motion with a minor irrotational fraction. A one-to-one correspondence between calculated TD and TC can be established. The toroidal flow appears already in the uncoupled two-quasiparticle (2qp) excitations and becomes definitively strong for the QRPA modes. Altogether, we find that low-lying dipole strength often denoted as isoscalar PDR is actually an oversimplified imitation of the basically toroidal motion in nuclei with a sufficient neutron excess.

## Key claim

The low-lying dipole strength often labeled as isoscalar pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) is actually an oversimplified imitation of a fundamentally toroidal motion in nuclei with sufficient neutron excess; the flow pattern in the lower part of the PDR energy region is basically isoscalar vortical toroidal motion with a minor irrotational fraction, regardless of whether PDR strength exists or not.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations for the spherical nuclei ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸,⁷²Ni, ⁹⁰,¹⁰⁰Zr, and ¹⁰⁰,¹²⁰,¹³²Sn, covering four elements with at least two isotopes each having different neutron excess. They analyzed strength functions, transition densities (TD), and transition currents (TC) for isoscalar toroidal and compressional dipole modes, and compared QRPA results with uncoupled two-quasiparticle (2qp) excitations. Most calculations used the Skyrme force SLy6, with additional checks using SV-bas and SkM* for ¹²⁰Sn.

## Key numbers

- The toroidal dipole mode in ¹²⁰Sn covers a large energy region 8–18 MeV, with the low-energy part analyzed in the 7–11 MeV interval.
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule and isoscalar energy-weighted sum rule are exhausted by 99–103% using a 2qp basis up to 120 MeV in ¹²⁰Sn.
- The Lorentzian folding width parameters are Δ₀ = 0.3 MeV, with E₀ = min{Sₙ, Sₚ} and a = 0.1667.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, the toroidal strength covers a large energy region 8–20 MeV, with a lowest vortical IS TDR peak at 10 MeV.
- The typical 2qp dipole strength energy is E_sp ≈ 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of the relation between isoscalar toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) and pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), which were performed only for a few selected nuclei: ¹²⁰Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb, and ¹⁵⁴Sm (Repko et al., 2013; Nesterenko et al., 2016; Repko et al., 2017). The systematic survey across Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes demonstrates that the toroidal flow is a general feature of nuclear dipole excitations, appearing already in uncoupled 2qp states and becoming definitively strong in QRPA modes. This enables a reinterpretation of low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, suggesting that the PDR picture of neutron-skin oscillations against an N=Z core is a crude approximation of the underlying toroidal motion.
