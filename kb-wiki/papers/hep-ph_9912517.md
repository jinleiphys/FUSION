# Reordering of Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Reordering of the chiral perturbation series, proposed recently by Becher and Leutwyler in the framework of SU(2) baryonic ChPT, is applied to the SU(3) case. This results in improved convergence of the chiral expansion of static properties of the lowest lying baryon octet, which in most cases is quite impressive. Finite renormalization of coupling constants and the role it plays in the interpretation of effective field theories is discussed. Some future tests of the viability of the scheme are proposed too.

## Key claim
Applying the reordering of the chiral perturbation series (proposed by Becher and Leutwyler for SU(2) baryonic ChPT) to the SU(3) case significantly improves the convergence of the chiral expansion for static properties of the baryon octet, but this improvement is achieved by a fitting procedure that prevents higher orders from spoiling agreement achieved at lower orders, and the large corrections are not eliminated but rather hidden in the expansion of the unphysical parameter m.

## Method
The authors generalized the reordering approach of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) from the loop expansion to the chiral expansion by writing each low-energy constant (LEC) as a series of contributions at different chiral orders (e.g., c = c^(0) + δc^(1) + δc^(2) + ...). They then re-expressed the chiral Lagrangian so that results at each order are expressed in terms of physical coupling constants rather than bare ones, with the difference shifted to higher orders. Numerical fits were performed sequentially: first fixing m^(0) = 1150 MeV from first-order baryon mass fits, then determining second-order LECs from second-order fits, and so on, using experimental baryon masses, magnetic moments, and axial couplings.

## Key numbers
- Baryon masses in the reordered expansion (in MeV): m_N = (1150 - 209 + 0 + 3) MeV; m_Λ = (1150 - 39 + 1 + 8) MeV; m_Σ = (1150 + 39 + 0 + 0) MeV; m_Ξ = (1150 + 170 + 0 + 0) MeV
- For comparison, the standard expansion of m_Λ was 767 (1 + 0.69 - 0.77 + 0.54) MeV
- Magnetic moment of Λ in the standard expansion: μ_Λ = -1.31 (0 + 1 - 0.82 + 0.29) μ_N
- Reordered magnetic moments (in μ_N): μ_p = (2.56 + 0.39 - 0.15) μ_N; μ_n = (-1.60 - 0.91 + 0.60) μ_N; μ_Λ = (-0.80 + 0.36 - 0.18) μ_N; μ_Σ+ = (2.56 - 0.33 + 0.22) μ_N; μ_Σ- = (-0.97 - 0.39 + 0.19) μ_N; μ_Σ0 = (0.80 - 0.36 + 0.22) μ_N; μ_Ξ0 = (-1.60 + 0.79 - 0.45) μ_N; μ_Ξ- = (-0.97 + 0.45 - 0.15) μ_N
- Loop corrections for magnetic moments are typically at the level of 20% of the tree result (4% in the best case, 31% in the worst case)
- Reordered axial couplings: g_1[pn](0) = 1.30 - 0.14; g_1[ΛΣ-](0) = 0.65 - 0.01; g_1[pΛ](0) = -0.94 - 0.04; g_1[ΛΞ-](0) = 0.29 + 0.02; g_1[nΣ-](0) = 0.30 + 0.02; g_1[Σ0Ξ-](0) = 0.92 + 0.13
- For comparison, the standard expansion of g_1[pΛ](0) was -0.5 - 0.48
- The πN σ-term shift due to loop corrections was -31 MeV in the standard expansion but only 2 MeV in the reordered series (from Becher and Leutwyler, 1999)

## Context
This work extends the reordering approach of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) from SU(2) to SU(3) baryonic chiral perturbation theory, directly addressing the slow convergence problem highlighted by Donoghue, Holstein, and Borasoy (1998). The reordering resolves the apparent contradiction that tree-level SU(3) results agree well with experiment but one-loop corrections are typically too large, as seen in the work of Borasoy (1998) on axial couplings and the mass and magnetic moment calculations of Borasoy and Meissner (1996) and Meissner and Steininger (1997). The approach enables future tests of convergence in processes such as non-leptonic hyperon decays, hyperon polarizabilities, strange form factors of the nucleon, and kaon photo-production.
