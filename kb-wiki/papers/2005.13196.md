# Quasiparticle properties of a single alpha particle in cold neutron matter

> Light clusters such as alpha particles and deuterons are predicted to occur in hot nuclear matter as encountered in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions and protoneutron stars. To examine the in-medium properties of such light clusters, we consider a much simplified system in which like an impurity, a single alpha particle is embedded in a zero-temperature, dilute gas of non-interacting neutrons. By adopting a non-selfconsistent ladder approximation for the effective interaction between the impurity and the gas, which is often used for analyses of Fermi polarons in a gas of ultracold atoms, we calculate the quasiparticle properties of the impurity, i.e., the energy shift, effective mass, quasiparticle residue, and damping rate.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the quasiparticle properties of a single alpha particle embedded in a zero-temperature, dilute gas of non-interacting neutrons, using a non-selfconsistent ladder approximation; the authors explicitly note that the model is safely applicable only at very low neutron densities below about 0.003 times normal nuclear density, where s-wave scattering dominates.

## Method

The authors treat the alpha particle as an inert point impurity immersed in a zero-temperature normal neutron gas, described by a low-energy effective Hamiltonian with a contact interaction characterized by the s-wave scattering length. They employ a variational method restricted to single particle-hole pair excitations near the neutron Fermi surface, which is equivalent to a non-selfconsistent ladder approximation for the alpha-neutron scattering amplitude. The quasiparticle energy, residue, effective mass, and damping rate are obtained from the self-energy via the spectral peak of the retarded propagator.

## Key numbers

- Reference neutron density: ρ_ref = 0.01 ρ_0 = 0.0016 fm⁻³, corresponding to Fermi momentum k_F_ref = 0.36 fm⁻¹
- Alpha-neutron s-wave scattering length: a_ref = 2.64 fm
- Effective range: r_0 = 1.43 fm
- At reference density and zero momentum: interaction energy E_ref = 0.467 ε_F_ref = 1.26 MeV, where ε_F_ref = 2.69 MeV
- At reference density: quasiparticle residue Z_ref = 0.650
- At reference density: effective mass M*_ref = 1.217 M (where M/m = 4)
- At reference density: decay width Γ_ref = 0.032 ε_F_ref = 0.086 MeV
- Critical neutron density for alpha particle stability estimated from neutron separation energy: about 21 ρ_ref ≃ 0.034 fm⁻³
- s-wave dominance condition: k_F < 0.25 fm⁻¹, corresponding to ρ < 0.003 ρ_0

## Context

This work extends the Fermi polaron picture from ultracold atomic physics (Chevy 2006, Chevy 2007) to nuclear physics, treating an alpha particle as an impurity in neutron matter. It contrasts with earlier approaches using excluded-volume effects (Lalit 2019) and virial expansion (Horowitz 2005), which the authors note are too repulsive and too attractive, respectively, for cold systems. The results enable estimates of alpha particle fractions in supernova matter and possible Bose-Einstein condensation temperatures for dilute alpha gases, and may relate to alpha clustering in heavy nuclei as discussed by Brink (1972) and Brink (1973).
