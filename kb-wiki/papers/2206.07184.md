# Precision tests of the nonlinear mode coupling of anisotropic flow via high-energy collisions of isobars

> Valuable information on the dynamics of expanding fluids can be inferred from the response of such systems to perturbations in their initial geometry. We apply this technique in high-energy $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions to scrutinize the expansion dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma, where the initial geometry perturbations are sourced by the differences in deformations and radial profiles between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr, and the collective response is captured by the change in anisotropic flow $V_n$ between the two collision systems. Using a transport model, we analyze how the nonlinear coupling between lower-order flow harmonics $V_2$ and $V_3$ to the higher-order flow harmonics $V_4$ and $V_5$, expected to scale as $V_{4\mathrm{NL}}=χ_4 V_2^2$ and $V_{5\mathrm{NL}}=χ_5 V_2V_3$, gets modified as one moves from $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru to $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr systems. We find that these scaling relations are valid to high precision: variations of order 20\% in $V_{4\mathrm{NL}}$ and $V_{5\mathrm{NL}}$ due to differences in quadrupole deformation, octupole deformation, and nuclear skin modify $χ_{4}$ and $χ_5$ by about 1--2\%. Percent-level deviations are however larger than the expected experimental uncertainties and could be measured. Therefore, collisions of isobars with different nuclear structures are a unique tool to isolate subtle nonlinear effects in the expansion of the quark-gluon plasma that would be otherwise impossible to access in a single collision system.

# Precision tests of the nonlinear mode coupling of anisotropic flow via high-energy collisions of isobars

## Key claim
The scaling relations \(V_{4\mathrm{NL}}=\chi_4 V_2^2\) and \(V_{5\mathrm{NL}}=\chi_5 V_2V_3\) hold to high precision across isobar systems, with variations of order 20% in \(V_{4\mathrm{NL}}\) and \(V_{5\mathrm{NL}}\) modifying \(\chi_4\) and \(\chi_5\) by only about 1–2%. However, these percent-level deviations are larger than expected experimental uncertainties and could be measured, revealing subleading nonlinear couplings that would be otherwise impossible to access in a single collision system.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT transport model (version v2.26t5 in string-melting mode) to simulate \(^{96}\mathrm{Ru}+^{96}\mathrm{Ru}\) and \(^{96}\mathrm{Zr}+^{96}\mathrm{Zr}\) collisions at RHIC energies. Nuclear structure was modeled via Woods-Saxon densities with parameters for quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\), octupole deformation \(\beta_3\), nuclear diffusivity \(a_0\), and half-width radius \(R_0\). The nonlinear coupling coefficients \(\chi_4\) and \(\chi_5\) were extracted from event-averaged flow vectors using the definitions \(\chi_4 = \langle V_4 (V_2^2)^* \rangle / \langle v_2^4 \rangle\) and \(\chi_5 = \langle V_5 (V_2 V_3)^* \rangle / \langle v_2^2 v_3^2 \rangle\), and isobar ratios of these quantities were computed at fixed charged-particle multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- Variations of order 20% in \(V_{4\mathrm{NL}}\) and \(V_{5\mathrm{NL}}\) due to differences in nuclear structure modify \(\chi_4\) and \(\chi_5\) by about 1–2%.
- The ratio \(\mathcal{R}_{\langle v_2^2 \rangle^2}\) is about 2% larger than \(\mathcal{R}_{\langle v_2^4 \rangle}\) in mid-central collisions, explained by \(x \sim 0.1\) and \(\mathcal{R}_{v_2\{4\}^4} \sim 1.35\).
- About 170 million AMPT events were generated for each of the five nuclear structure cases, approximately 10% of the available real isobar data for one collision system.
- The fraction of nonlinear contribution to \(v_4\{2\}^2\) approaches zero in central collisions, with the nonlinear mode impacting \(\mathcal{R}_{v_4\{2\}}\) mostly in the non-central region.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of nonlinear flow harmonic coupling developed by Yan and Ollitrault (2015) and Gardim et al. (2011–2018), and builds on experimental measurements by ATLAS (2014, 2015), ALICE (2017, 2020), and STAR (2020). The isobar ratio technique exploits the unique capability of the STAR collaboration's isobar data (2021) to cancel measurement systematics and final-state effects, enabling detection of subleading nonlinear couplings such as \(V_1V_3\) and \(V_3^2 V_2^*\) that contribute to \(V_4\). This approach enables data-driven determinations of QGP transport properties that would be impossible using a single collision system.
