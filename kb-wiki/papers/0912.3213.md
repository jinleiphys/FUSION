# Kaons in Dense Half-Skyrmion Matter

> Dense hadronic matter at low temperature is expected to be in crystal and at high density make a transition to a {\em chirally restored but color-confined} state which is a novel phase hitherto unexplored. This phase transition is predicted in both skyrmion matter in 4D and instanton matter in 5D, the former in the form of half-skyrmions and the latter in the form of half-instantons or dyons. We predict that when $K^-$'s are embedded in this half-skyrmion or half-instanton (dyonic) matter which may be reached not far above the normal density, there arises an enhanced attraction from the soft dilaton field figuring for the trace anomaly of QCD and the Wess-Zumino term. This attraction may have relevance for a possible strong binding of anti-kaons in dense nuclear matter and for kaon condensation in neutron-star matter. Such kaon property in the half-skyrmion phase is highly non-perturbarive and may not be accessible by low-order chiral perturbation theory. Relevance of the half-skyrmion or dyonic matter to compact stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors predict that when $K^-$ mesons are embedded in half-skyrmion or half-instanton (dyonic) matter, a phase that may be reached not far above normal nuclear density, there arises an enhanced attraction from the soft dilaton field and the Wess-Zumino term, which may have relevance for strong binding of anti-kaons in dense nuclear matter and for kaon condensation in neutron-star matter. This behavior is highly non-perturbative and may not be accessible by low-order chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors construct dense nuclear matter using the $SU(2)_f$ Skyrme Lagrangian implemented with a soft dilaton field $\chi$ that accounts for the trace anomaly of QCD, and extend it to three flavors with the Wess-Zumino term. They embed kaon fluctuations into the background of skyrmion matter placed on an fcc crystal, following the Callan-Klebanov scheme, and take a space average over the background matter fields in a mean-field approximation to obtain an effective Lagrangian for the kaon. The in-medium effective kaon mass $m_K^*$ is then evaluated from the dispersion relation derived from this Lagrangian, using the background $u_0$ from previously computed crystal configurations.

## Key numbers

* The normal nuclear matter density is $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
* The half-skyrmion phase transition occurs at density $n_{1/2} \sim 1.3 n_0$, with a best-guess range between $1.3 n_0$ and $3 n_0$.
* The kaon binding energy at nuclear matter density is approximately 110 MeV for dilaton mass $m_\chi = 600$ MeV and approximately 80 MeV for $m_\chi = 720$ MeV.
* The critical density $n_c$ at which the kaon mass vanishes (in the chiral limit) is $n_c \approx 2.3 n_0$ for $m_\chi = 600$ MeV and $n_c \approx 4.0 n_0$ for $m_\chi = 720$ MeV.
* The vacuum $\rho$ meson mass is taken as $\sqrt{2} e f_\pi = m_\rho = 780$ MeV, with $f_\pi \approx 93$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Callan-Klebanov model (Callan and Klebanov, 1985) for hyperons as bound states of $K^-$ with an $SU(2)$ soliton to the case of dense matter, and builds on prior studies of half-skyrmion matter in crystal lattices (Park and Vento, 2009) and instanton-to-dyon transitions in holographic QCD (dyons, 2009). The results connect to the "hadronic freedom" regime previously identified in dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions (Brown, Rho, et al., 1991) and to the vector manifestation fixed point of hidden local symmetry (Georgi, 1990; Harada and Yamawaki, 2003). The paper enables future work on kaon condensation in compact stars and suggests that kaon condensation and hyperon condensation are physically equivalent in this framework.
