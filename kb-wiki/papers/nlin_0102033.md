# Structure of wavefunctions in (1+2)-body random matrix ensembles

> Abstrtact: Random matrix ensembles defined by a mean-field one-body plus a chaos generating random two-body interaction (called embedded ensembles of (1+2)-body interactions) predict for wavefunctions, in the chaotic domain, an essentially one parameter Gaussian forms for the energy dependence of the number of principal components NPC and the localization length ${\boldmath $l$}_H$ (defined by information entropy), which are two important measures of chaos in finite interacting many particle systems. Numerical embedded ensemble calculations and nuclear shell model results, for NPC and ${\boldmath $l$}_H$, are compared with the theory. These analysis clearly point out that for realistic finite interacting many particle systems, in the chaotic domain, wavefunction structure is given by (1+2)-body embedded random matrix ensembles.

## Key claim

For wavefunctions in the chaotic domain of interacting many-particle systems described by a mean-field one-body plus a random two-body interaction (EGOE(1+2)), the number of principal components (NPC) and the localization length \(l_H\) follow essentially one-parameter Gaussian forms as functions of excitation energy, but this result holds only in the Gaussian domain where \(\lambda > \lambda_{F_k}\) (the strength functions have Gaussian form), not in the Breit-Wigner domain where \(\lambda_c \leq \lambda < \lambda_{F_k}\).

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas for NPC and \(l_H\) in wavefunctions by exploiting the bivariate Gaussian form of the joint density \(\rho_{biv}(E,E_k)\) between eigenstate energies \(E\) and mean-field basis energies \(E_k\), combined with the Porter-Thomas form of local strength fluctuations and the average-fluctuations separation property of EGOE(1+2). They tested these formulas against numerical EGOE(1+2) ensemble calculations (with 6 fermions in 12 single-particle states, using 25 ensemble members) and against nuclear shell model results for \(^{24}\)Mg, \(^{28}\)Si, \(^{22}\)Na, \(^{50}\)Ca, and \(^{46}\)Sc.

## Key numbers

* For the EGOE(1+2) test with 6 particles in 12 states, the correlation coefficient \(\zeta\) took values 0.16, 0.59, and 0.82 for \(\alpha = 0, 0.5, 1\) respectively (with \(\lambda=0.2\) fixed).
* The variance fluctuation parameter \([(\delta\sigma^2)/\sigma_H^2]^2\) for these three cases was \(0.121 \times 10^{-1}\), \(0.545 \times 10^{-2}\), and \(0.134 \times 10^{-2}\) respectively.
* For the EGOE(2)-S model (6 fermions in 7 doubly-degenerate levels, total \(S_z=0\), dimension \(d=1225\)), \(\zeta = 0.3\) and \([(\delta\sigma^2)/\sigma_H^2]^2 = 0.052\).
* For the \(sd\) shell nucleus \(^{24}\)Mg (325-dimensional \(J=0,T=0\) space), \(\zeta = 0.68\) and \([(\delta\sigma^2)/\sigma_H^2]^2 = 0.024\).
* For the \(pf\) shell nucleus \(^{50}\)Ca (2051-dimensional \(J=6,T=5\) space), \(\zeta = 0.96\) (from shell model results); for \(^{46}\)Sc (2042-dimensional \(J=1,T=2\) space), \(\zeta = 0.92\) (from shell model results) or 0.89 (from \(mT\)-space averages).
* At the spectrum center, the EGOE(1+2) formulas give \(\text{NPC} = (d/3)\sqrt{1-\zeta^4}\) and \(l_H = \sqrt{1-\zeta^2}\exp(\zeta^2/2)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier results of G. Stoitcheva and collaborators (1997) for NPC in the Breit-Wigner domain, and of Kota (1998) who gave the final formulas for NPC and \(l_H\) in transition strength distributions with only brief mention of wavefunction results. The present paper provides the detailed derivation and numerical verification of those wavefunction formulas. The theory enables quantitative prediction of wavefunction structure in realistic finite fermion systems (atomic nuclei, atoms, quantum dots) described by EGOE(1+2), and shows that shell model wavefunctions for \(sd\) and \(pf\) shell nuclei are well described by these one-parameter Gaussian forms rather than by GOE. The work also connects to the "scar theory" results of Kaplan and Papenbrock (2000) for the pure EGOE(2) limit.
