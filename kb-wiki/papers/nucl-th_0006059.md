# Projected shell model study for the yrast-band structure of the proton-rich mass-80 nuclei

> A systematic study of the yrast-band structure for the proton-rich, even-even mass-80 nuclei is carried out using projected shell model approach. We describe the the energy spectra, transition quadrupole moments and gyromagnetic factors. The observed variations in energy spectra and transition quadrupole moments in this mass region are discussed in terms of the configuration mixing of the projected deformed Nilsson states as a function of shell filling.

# Projected shell model study for the yrast-band structure of the proton-rich mass-80 nuclei

## Key claim
The projected shell model (PSM) can systematically describe the yrast-band energy spectra, transition quadrupole moments, and gyromagnetic factors for proton-rich even-even Kr, Sr, and Zr isotopes in the mass-80 region, but the calculations fail to reproduce states at very low spins, particularly in the moment of inertia and transition quadrupole moment plots, because the fixed axial deformation basis may be too crude for the spin region characterized by shape coexistence.

## Method
The authors performed projected shell model calculations using a quadrupole-quadrupole plus monopole and quadrupole pairing Hamiltonian, with basis states constructed from the Nilsson potential including three major shells (N = 2, 3, and 4) for both protons and neutrons. The intrinsic states within an energy window of 3.5 MeV around the Fermi surface were projected to good angular momentum and diagonalized, considering the quasiparticle vacuum, two-quasineutron, two-quasiproton, and four-quasiparticle (two-quasineutron plus two-quasiproton) configurations. Calculations were carried out for Kr, Sr, and Zr isotopes using basis deformations taken from experiment or total routhian surface calculations.

## Key numbers
- For $^{72}$Kr, a prolate basis deformation of $\epsilon_2 = 0.36$ was used; the g-band is crossed by proton and neutron 2-qp bands at about spin I = 10.
- For $^{74}$Kr, a prolate deformation of $\epsilon_2 = 0.37$ was used; neutron and proton 2-qp bands cross the g-band around I = 14.
- For $^{76}$Kr, a prolate deformation of $\epsilon_2 = 0.36$ was used; band crossings occur between I = 12 and 14.
- For $^{76}$Sr, a basis deformation of $\epsilon_2 = 0.36$ was used; band crossings occur at I = 14 and above I = 16.
- For $^{78}$Sr, a prolate deformation of $\epsilon_2 = 0.36$ was used; the proton 2-qp band crosses the g-band at I = 12.
- For $^{80}$Sr, $\epsilon_2 = 0.34$ was used; the proton 2-qp band crosses the g-band between I = 10 and 12.
- For $^{80}$Zr, $\epsilon_2 = 0.36$ was used; proton and neutron 2-qp bands cross the ground band between I = 12 and 14, with a 4-qp band crossing at I = 16.
- For $^{82}$Zr, $\epsilon_2 = 0.29$ was used; the proton 2-qp band crosses the g-band at I = 10, with a 4-qp band crossing at I = 16.
- For $^{84}$Zr, $\epsilon_2 = 0.22$ was used; proton 2-qp bands cross the g-band at I = 8, with a neutron alignment from a 4-qp band between I = 14 and 16.
- The monopole pairing force constants used were $G_M^\nu = [20.25 - 16.20 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1}$ and $G_M^\pi = 20.25 A^{-1}$.
- The ratio $G_Q/G_M$ was adjusted around 0.20 to reproduce observed band crossings.
- Effective charges of 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons were used.
- Standard g-factor values were taken: $g_l^\pi = 1$, $g_l^\nu = 0$, $g_s^\pi = 5.586 \times 0.75$, and $g_s^\nu = -3.826 \times 0.75$.

## Context
This work extends the projected shell model approach, previously applied successfully to rare-earth nuclei (Hara and Sun, 1994) and to $^{74}$Se (Dobaczewski et al., 1998), to a systematic study of the proton-rich mass-80 region. It contrasts with earlier mean-field approaches using the Woods-Saxon method (Nazarewicz, 1985), the Nilsson model (Henderson, 1984), and Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS theory (Bonche, 1985), as well as the more numerically intensive Excited VAMPIR approach (Petrovici, 1996, 2000). The results enable predictions for the structure of N=Z nuclei that could be tested by future experiments with radioactive ion beams, and highlight the need for triaxial projected shell model calculations or generator coordinate method investigations to properly describe low-spin shape coexistence.
