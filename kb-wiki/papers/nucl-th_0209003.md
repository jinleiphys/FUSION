# Faddeev-Yakubovsky search for Lambda-Lambda hydrogen-4

> Evidence for particle stability of Lambda-Lambda hydrogen-4 (4LLH) has been suggested by the BNL-AGS E906 experiment. We report on Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the four-body Lambda-Lambda-p-n system using Lambda-N interactions which reproduce the observed binding energy of Lambda hydrogen-3 (3LH) within a Faddeev calculation for the Lambda-p-n subsystem. No 4LLH bound state is found over a wide range of Lambda-Lambda interaction strengths, although the Faddeev equations for a three-body Lambda-Lambda-d model of 4LLH admit a 1+ bound state for as weak a Lambda-Lambda interaction strength as required to reproduce the binding energy of Lambda-Lambda Helium-6 (6LLHe).

## Key claim

No bound state of ΛΛ hydrogen-4 (⁴_ΛΛH) is found in a full four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation over a wide range of ΛΛ interaction strengths, even though a three-body ΛΛd model of the same system does admit a 1⁺ bound state for ΛΛ interactions as weak as those required to reproduce the binding energy of ΛΛ helium-6 (⁶_ΛΛHe).

## Method

The authors solved differential Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations under the s-wave approximation for the four-body ΛΛpn system, using three-range Gaussian s-wave potentials for the ΛN and ΛΛ interactions. The ΛN interactions were taken from the Nijmegen soft-core models NSC97e and NSC97f, calibrated to reproduce the observed binding energy of ³_ΛH within a Faddeev calculation of the Λpn subsystem, while the ΛΛ interaction was adjusted to reproduce the binding energy of ⁶_ΛΛHe. For comparison, a three-body ΛΛd Faddeev calculation was also performed using Λd interactions derived from the same Λpn Faddeev results.

## Key numbers

- Calculated B_Λ for ³_ΛH(1/2⁺) using model NSC97f: 0.19 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value of 0.13±0.05 MeV.
- ΛN scattering lengths from model NSC97e: singlet a = -2.10 fm, triplet a = -1.84 fm; from model NSC97f: singlet a = -2.50 fm, triplet a = -1.75 fm.
- ΛΛ scattering length from the interaction reproducing ⁶_ΛΛHe: a = -0.77 fm.
- Λd doublet scattering length for model NSC97f: 13.1 fm; doublet effective range: 2.46 fm.
- In the three-body ΛΛd model, the spin-averaged effective B_Λ(³_ΛH) in ⁴_ΛΛH is 0.07 MeV, with B_ΛΛ ∼ 0.3 MeV, giving B_ΛΛ > 2B̄_Λ.
- The three-body ΛΛd model binds ⁴_ΛΛH for ΛΛ scattering lengths with -a_ΛΛ ≥ 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical search for particle-stable ΛΛ hypernuclei following the BNL-AGS E906 experiment, which suggested evidence for ⁴_ΛΛH (Ahn 2001). It contradicts the earlier three-body ΛΛd model calculations that predicted binding, and instead shows that a full four-body treatment with dynamically independent nucleons yields no bound state. The result contrasts with the authors' own Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for ¹⁰_ΛΛBe (Filikhin and Gal 2002), where the four-body model gave higher binding than the three-body cluster model. The non-binding is attributed to the relatively weak ΛN interaction, and the work enables future experimental confirmation or refutation of ⁴_ΛΛH stability in extensions of the E906 experiment.
