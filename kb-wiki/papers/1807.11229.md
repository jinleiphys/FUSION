# Adding vacuum branching to jet evolution in a dense medium

> We study the fragmentation of a jet propagating in a dense quark-gluon plasma. We show that the "vacuum-like" emissions triggered by the parton virtualities can be factorized from the medium-induced radiation responsible for the energy loss within a controlled, "double-logarithmic", approximation in perturbative QCD. We show that the collisions with the plasma constituents modify the vacuum-like parton shower already at leading twist, in two ways: the radiation phase-space is reduced and the first emission outside the medium can violate angular ordering. We compute the jet fragmentation function and find results in qualitative agreement with measurements at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors show that "vacuum-like" emissions (triggered by parton virtualities) and medium-induced radiation (responsible for energy loss) can be factorized from each other within a controlled double-logarithmic approximation in perturbative QCD, but only under the condition that the vacuum-like emissions occur with formation times much shorter than those of medium-induced emissions of the same energy.

## Method

The authors analyze a quark-antiquark antenna in a color singlet state propagating through a static, uniform quark-gluon plasma characterized by the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\). They compute the phase-space constraints for vacuum-like emissions inside the medium, including a "vetoed region" where such emissions cannot occur, and treat color decoherence effects to determine whether angular ordering is preserved or violated for emissions inside versus outside the medium. The jet fragmentation function is then calculated in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) by integrating over the two-dimensional gluon distribution in energy and angle.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q L^2/2\) separates emissions that behave as in vacuum (\(\omega \ge \omega_c\)) from those modified by the medium (\(\omega \le \omega_c\)).
- The special angle \(\theta_c \equiv 2/\sqrt{\hat q L^3}\) is quite small, \(\theta_c < 0.1\), for phenomenologically relevant values of \(\hat q\) and \(L\).
- The suppression of the two-dimensional gluon distribution relative to vacuum reaches up to 15% for intermediate energies and relatively small angles \(\theta \lesssim 0.1\tqq\).
- The fragmentation function shows a slight suppression at intermediate energies from roughly 2 GeV up to \(\omega_c\), and a substantial enhancement at lower energies \(\omega \lesssim 2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical understanding of color decoherence (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2011, 2012) and multiple medium-induced branchings (Blaizot 2012, 2013, 2014; Kurkela 2014; Iancu 2015; Escobedo 2016) by constructing a unified description that consistently encompasses both vacuum-like and medium-induced radiation. The results are in qualitative agreement with LHC measurements for central PbPb collisions (Chatrchyan 2014, Aad 2014), and the probabilistic approach is suitable for Monte-Carlo implementations that could go beyond the double-logarithmic approximation.
