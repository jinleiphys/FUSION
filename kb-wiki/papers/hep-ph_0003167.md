# Large N_c, Constituent Quarks, and N, Delta Charge Radii

> We show how one may define baryon constituent quarks in a rigorous manner, given physical assumptions that hold in the large-N_c limit of QCD. This constituent picture gives rise to an operator expansion that has been used to study large-N_c baryon observables; here we apply it to the case of charge radii of the N and Delta states, using minimal dynamical assumptions. For example, one finds the relation r_p^2 - r_{Delta^+}^2 = r_n^2 - r_{Delta^0}^2 to be broken only by three-body, O(1/N_c^2) effects for any N_c.

# Large N_c, Constituent Quarks, and N, Delta Charge Radii

## Key claim
The paper shows that a rigorous constituent quark picture for baryons follows from the assumption that physical baryons are narrow-width eigenstates of distinct spin-flavor representations, which holds in the large-N_c limit. The relation r_p^2 - r_{Delta^+}^2 = r_n^2 - r_{Delta^0}^2 is broken only by three-body, O(1/N_c^2) effects for any N_c, but this result depends on the "general parametrization" method which imposes mild physical constraints beyond pure symmetry.

## Method
The authors construct a 1/N_c operator expansion for baryon charge radii using the spin-flavor symmetry that emerges in the large-N_c limit of QCD, representing observables as polynomials in one-body operators (spin J, isospin I, and the combined operator G). They then apply the "general parametrization (GP) method" which restricts operators to those coupling to quarks through exactly one factor of quark charge, and evaluate matrix elements for N and Delta states using the completely symmetric spin-flavor wavefunction of the ground-state baryon multiplet.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses extracted from N,Delta masses: m_u = 287.6 MeV, m_d = 289.6 MeV
- Interaction energy terms for nucleons: approximately 73 MeV; for Deltas: approximately 366 MeV
- Experimental values used: r_p^2 = 0.792(24) fm^2, r_n^2 = -0.113(3)(4) fm^2
- Predicted Delta charge radii (with O(1/N_c^2) uncertainty of approximately 0.09 fm^2): r_{Delta^{++}}^2 = 0.85 ± 0.09 fm^2, r_{Delta^+}^2 = 0.79 ± 0.09 fm^2, r_{Delta^0}^2 = -0.11 ± 0.09 fm^2, r_{Delta^-}^2 = 1.02 ± 0.09 fm^2
- The isovector charge radius ratio A/B is approximately 5 when the three-body coefficient C is neglected

## Context
This work extends the large-N_c operator expansion previously applied to baryon masses (Jenkins and Lebed, 1995), magnetic moments (Luty, March-Russell, and White, 1993; Dashen, Dicus, and Jenkins, 1994), and axial-vector couplings (Dashen, Dicus, and Jenkins, 1994; Flores-Mendieta, Jenkins, and Manohar, 1998) to the case of N and Delta charge radii. The general parametrization method was developed by DeGrand and collaborators (1995) and applied to charge radii by Dillon and Morpurgo (1999); the authors extend this to arbitrary N_c. The relation r_p^2 - r_{Delta^+}^2 = r_n^2 - r_{Delta^0}^2 was found previously in an N_c=3 quark model with two-body currents (Buchmann, Hernandez, and Faessler, 1997). The results enable predictions for Delta charge radii that can be tested experimentally, and provide a framework for incorporating baryon strong decay amplitudes and configuration mixing in future large-N_c quark models.
