# Slow Proton Production in Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering off Deuteron and Complex Nuclei: Hadronization and Final State Interaction Effects

> The effects of the final state interaction in slow proton production in semi inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes off nuclei, A(e,e'p)X, are investigated in details within the spectator and target fragmentation mechanisms; in the former mechanism, the hard interaction on a nucleon of a correlated pair leads, by recoil, to the emission of the partner nucleon, whereas in the latter mechanism proton is produced when the diquark, which is formed right after the visrtual photon-quark interaction, captures a quark from the vacuum. Unlike previous papers on the subject, particular attention is paid on the effects of the final state interaction of the hadronizing quark with the nuclear medium within an approach based upon an effective time-dependent cross section which combines the soft and hard parts of hadronization dynamics in terms of the string model and perturbative QCD, respectively. It is shown that the final state interaction of the hadronizing quark with the medium plays a relevant role both in deuteron and complex nuclei; nonetheless, kinematical regions where final state interaction effects are minimized can experimentally be selected, which would allow one to investigate the structure functions of nucleons embedded in the nuclear medium; likewise, regions where the interaction of the struck hadronizing quark with the nuclear medium is maximized can be found, which would make it possible to study non perturbative hadronization mechanisms.

## Key claim

The final state interaction (FSI) of the hadronizing quark with the nuclear medium plays a relevant role in slow proton production from both deuteron and complex nuclei in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering; however, kinematical regions exist where FSI effects are minimized (allowing extraction of bound nucleon structure functions) or maximized (enabling study of nonperturbative hadronization mechanisms), depending on the momentum and emission angle of the detected proton.

## Method

The authors calculated the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross section for the A(e,e'p)X process using both the spectator mechanism (where the detected proton is the recoil partner of a struck nucleon from a correlated pair) and the target fragmentation mechanism (where the proton forms when the diquark captures a quark from the vacuum). They incorporated FSI effects through an effective time-dependent cross section sigma_eff(z,x,Q^2) that combines soft hadronization dynamics from the string model with hard contributions from perturbative QCD, using a Glauber-like eikonal S-matrix with a profile function depending on both transverse and longitudinal separation. The calculations were performed for the deuteron and extended to complex nuclei using a few-nucleon correlation model.

## Key numbers

- The effective cross section sigma_eff reaches a maximum value of approximately 100 mb for the deuteron at kinematics corresponding to JLab experiments with initial electron energy E_e = 5.765 GeV.
- The slope parameter b_0 in the profile function varies in the range 0.35-0.6 fm, and alpha (ratio of real to imaginary forward scattering amplitude) is approximately -0.35.
- The first pion is created at z_0 approximately equal to 0.6 fm from the interaction point, and the average number of pions produced is about two for the kinematics of the experiment considered.
- The transverse momentum distribution parameter beta for the nucleon fragmentation function is 0.38 fm^2, while for complex nuclei the momentum distribution decreases with beta approximately 3.5-5 fm^2.
- The spectator mechanism within PWIA fails to explain experimental data in the whole kinematical range, particularly at high values of |p_2| where the reduced cross section is dominated by eikonal-type FSI.
- At low values of |p_2| approximately 0.2-0.3 GeV/c, the interference between PWIA and FSI mostly cancels out.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to the spectator mechanism in SIDIS, particularly the work of Ciofi degli Atti, Kaptari, and collaborators (2003) which used an asymptotic value of sigma_eff, by implementing a Q^2-dependent effective cross section based on the hadronization model of Kopeliovich and collaborators. The paper is motivated by and compared with experimental data from JLab experiment E-03-012 (Klimenko, 2006) and provides theoretical support for future SIDIS experiments at the upgraded 12 GeV JLab. The results are in qualitative agreement with the approach of Frankfurt, Strikman, and collaborators (2000) who treated the rescattering amplitude parameters as free, and the ratio of target fragmentation to spectator cross sections agrees with the light-front dynamics calculations of Frankfurt and Strikman (1999).
