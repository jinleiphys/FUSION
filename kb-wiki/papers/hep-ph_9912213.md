# Coherence Time Effects on J/psi Production and Suppression in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Using a coherence time extracted from high precision proton-nucleus Drell-Yan measurements and a nuclear absorption cross section extracted from pA charmonium production experiments, we study J/psi production and absorption in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We find that coherence time effects are large enough to affect the measured J/psi-to-Drell-Yan ratio. The S+U data at 200A GeV/c measured by NA38 are reproduced quantitatively without the introduction of any new parameters. However, when compared with recent NA50 measurements for Pb+Pb at 158A GeV/c, the data is not reproduced in trend or in magnitude.

# Coherence Time Effects on J/psi Production and Suppression in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Using a coherence time of 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c extracted from proton-nucleus Drell-Yan data and a nuclear absorption cross section of 3.6 mb from pA charmonium experiments, the model reproduces S+U data at 200A GeV/c quantitatively without new parameters, but fails to reproduce NA50 Pb+Pb data at 158A GeV/c in either trend or magnitude, suggesting that if the Pb+Pb data stand, J/psi suppression cannot be explained by hadronic mechanisms alone.

## Method

The authors used LEXUS, a linear extrapolation model from proton-proton scattering to nucleus-nucleus collisions, incorporating a coherence time (τ0 = 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c) that translates into initial-state energy loss for Drell-Yan pair and J/psi production. They extracted the J/psi absorption cross section on nucleons (3.6 mb) from pA charmonium data and the coherence time from high-precision pA Drell-Yan measurements by E772 at 800 GeV/c, then applied these parameters without adjustment to S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Coherence time τ0 = 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c (in the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass frame), extracted from E772 pA Drell-Yan data at 800 GeV/c
- J/psi absorption cross section on nucleons = 3.6 mb, extracted from pA charmonium experiments and consistent with photoproduction measurements
- For S+U at 200A GeV/c, the model with n = 2 to n = 3 (corresponding to coherence time effects) brackets the experimental data for both absolute cross sections and the J/psi-to-Drell-Yan ratio
- For Pb+Pb at 158A GeV/c, the measured Drell-Yan cross section exceeds the n = 3 prediction by about one standard deviation, while the J/psi value falls within the predicted range
- The parameterization B σ_{NN → J/psi}(x_F > 0) = 37 (1 - m_{J/psi}/√s)^{12} nb was used for elementary nucleon-nucleon J/psi production
- The neutron skin of Pb is 0.19 ± 0.09 fm, too small to affect the calculations

## Context

This work extends the hadronic explanation of J/psi suppression by incorporating coherence time effects (related to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) that had been successful in describing Drell-Yan production in pA collisions. The model successfully reproduces NA38 S+U data without new parameters, but fails for NA50 Pb+Pb data, similar to other hadronic approaches (Kluberg et al., 1999). The authors note that if the Pb+Pb data stand, J/psi suppression may require high energy density explanations, whether from hadronic co-movers or quark-gluon plasma, and call for systematic exploration using the most recent pA measurements (E866) and partonic variables including nuclear shadowing.
