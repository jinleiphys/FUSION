# Ground State Electromagnetic Moments of $^{37}$Ca

> The hyperfine coupling constants of neutron deficient $^{37}$Ca were deduced from the atomic hyperfine spectrum of the $4s~^2S_{1/2}$ $\leftrightarrow$ $4p~^2P_{3/2}$ transition in Ca II, measured using the collinear laser spectroscopy technique. The ground-state magnetic-dipole and spectroscopic electric-quadrupole moments were determined for the first time as $μ= +0.7453(72) μ_N$ and $Q = -15(11)$ $e^2$fm$^2$, respectively. The experimental values agree well with nuclear shell model calculations using the universal sd model-space Hamiltonians versions A and B (USDA/B) in the $sd$-model space with a 95\% probability of the canonical nucleon configuration. It is shown that the magnetic moment of $^{39}$Ca requires a larger non-$sd$-shell component than that of $^{37}$Ca for good agreement with the shell-model calculation, indicating a more robust closed sub-shell structure of $^{36}$Ca at the neutron number $N$ = 16 than $^{40}$Ca. The results are also compared to valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The ground-state magnetic-dipole and spectroscopic electric-quadrupole moments of neutron-deficient 37Ca were measured for the first time, and the magnetic moment agrees well with shell-model calculations using the USDA/B Hamiltonians with a 95% probability of the canonical nucleon configuration; however, the magnetic moment of 39Ca requires a larger non-sd-shell component than that of 37Ca for good agreement, indicating that 36Ca is a more robust closed sub-shell at neutron number N = 16 than 40Ca.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on the 4s 2S1/2 ↔ 4p 2P3/2 transition in Ca II at 393 nm using bunched-beam technique at the BECOLA facility at Michigan State University. The 37Ca beam was produced via projectile fragmentation of a 140 MeV/nucleon 40Ca primary beam on a Be target, thermalized in a He-filled gas cell, extracted at 30 keV, and bunched in an RFQ cooler-buncher. Hyperfine coupling constants were extracted by fitting Voigt profiles to the hyperfine spectra, using 39Ca as a line-shape reference and 43Ca as a reference nucleus for moment extraction.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of 37Ca: μ = +0.7453(72) μN (first determination)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of 37Ca: Q = -15(11) e²fm² (first determination, oblate deformation)
- Magnetic moment of 39Ca: μ = +1.0217(1) μN (from prior work, Minamisono 1976)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of 39Ca: Q = +3.82(27) e²fm² (three times more precise than previous value)
- Hyperfine coupling constant A(2S1/2) for 37Ca: +1064.5(103)(08) MHz
- Hyperfine coupling constant B(2P3/2) for 37Ca: -22.9(163)(05) MHz
- Canonical nucleon configuration probability for 37Ca: 95% (USDA/B calculations)
- Single-particle effective magnetic moment for d3/2 neutron: 0.930 μN (compared to Schmidt value 1.148 μN)

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear shell structure around the N = 16 magic number, following observations of new magic numbers at extreme neutron-to-proton ratios (Ozawa 2000, Kanungo 2009, Hoffman 2009). The results are compared to shell-model calculations using USDA/B Hamiltonians (Brown 2006) and to ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. The finding that 36Ca is a better closed sub-shell than 40Ca enables further investigation of the transition from JJ-type to LS-type closed-shell configurations and motivates studies of heavier calcium isotopes where non-sd-shell components become significant (Garcia Ruiz 2015).
