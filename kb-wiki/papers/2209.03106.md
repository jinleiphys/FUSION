# Free spectator nucleons in ultracentral relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a probe of neutron skin

> Besides the yield ratio of free spectator neutrons produced in ultracentral $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr to $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru collisions, we propose that the yield ratio $N_n/N_p$ of free spectator neutrons to protons in a single collision system at RHIC and LHC can be a more sensitive probe of the neutron-skin thickness $Δr_{\mathrm{np}}$ and the slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy. The idea is demonstrated based on the proton and neutron density distributions of colliding nuclei obtained from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations, and a Glauber model that provides information of spectator matter. The final spectator particles are produced from direct emission, clusterization by a minimum spanning tree algorithm or a Wigner function approach, and deexcitation of heavy clusters by GEMINI. A larger $Δr_{\mathrm{np}}$ associated with a larger $L$ value increases the isospin asymmetry of spectator matter and thus leads to a larger $N_n/N_p$, especially in ultracentral collisions where the multiplicity of free nucleons are free from the uncertainties of cluster formation and deexcitation. We have further shown that the double ratio of $N_n/N_p$ in isobaric collision systems or in collisions by isotopes helps to cancel the detecting efficiency for protons. Effects from nuclear deformation and electromagnetic excitation are studied, and they are found to be subdominant compared to the expected sensitivity to $Δr_{\mathrm{np}}$.

## Key claim

The yield ratio \(N_n/N_p\) of free spectator neutrons to protons in a single ultracentral heavy-ion collision system at RHIC and LHC is proposed as a more sensitive probe of the neutron-skin thickness \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{np}}\) and the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy than the previously proposed yield ratio of free spectator neutrons between isobaric systems, provided that both free spectator neutrons and protons can be accurately measured.

## Method

The authors used Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) calculations to obtain proton and neutron density distributions for various nuclei, varying the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy. A Monte Carlo Glauber model simulated the collisions, identifying spectator nucleons. Final spectator particles were produced via direct emission, clusterization using a minimum spanning tree algorithm or a Wigner function approach for light clusters, and deexcitation of heavy clusters (\(A \geq 4\)) using the GEMINI model. The excitation energy of heavy clusters was calculated using a test-particle method based on a simplified Skyrme-Hartree-Fock energy-density functional.

## Key numbers

*   The neutron-skin thickness \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{np}}\) for \(^{96}\)Zr, \(^{96}\)Ru, \(^{197}\)Au, and \(^{208}\)Pb was calculated for \(L = 30\) MeV and \(L = 120\) MeV; for example, for \(^{96}\)Zr with spherical shape, \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{np}}\) is 0.00 fm for \(L=30\) MeV and 0.00 fm for \(L=120\) MeV (values for other nuclei are tabulated in the paper).
*   The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross sections used in the Glauber model are 42 mb at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV and 68 mb at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV.
*   The clusterization algorithm used \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{max}} = 3\) fm and \(\Delta p_{\mathrm{max}} = 300\) MeV/\(c\) for the minimum spanning tree.
*   The Wigner function widths for light clusters are \(\sigma_d = 2.26\) fm for deuterons, \(\sigma_t = 1.59\) fm for tritons, and \(\sigma_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 1.76\) fm for \(^3\)He.

## Context

This work extends the proposal in Liu (2022) to use the ratio of free spectator neutrons in isobaric \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr to \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru collisions as a probe of the neutron skin. It offers a complementary method to midrapidity observables sensitive to neutron skin, such as multiplicity distributions and elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) in isobaric systems (Li, 2019; Jia, 2021), which are affected by uncertainties from quark-gluon plasma hydrodynamics and hadronization. The proposed observable enables a cleaner probe of \(\Delta r_{\mathrm{np}}\) and the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) for heavy nuclei like \(^{197}\)Au and \(^{208}\)Pb, providing a measurement complementary to parity-violating electron scattering experiments like PREX-II.
