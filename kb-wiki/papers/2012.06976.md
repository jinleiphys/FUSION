# Symmetry energy investigation with pion production from Sn+Sn systems

> In the past two decades, pions created in the high density regions of heavy ion collisions have been predicted to be sensitive at high densities to the symmetry energy term in the nuclear equation of state, a property that is key to our understanding of neutron stars. In a new experiment designed to study the symmetry energy, the multiplicities of negatively and positively charged pions have been measured with high accuracy for central $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, $^{112}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn, and $^{108}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn collisions at $E/A=270~\mathrm{MeV}$ with the S$π$RIT Time Projection Chamber. While the uncertainties of individual pion multiplicities are measured to 4\%, those of the charged pion multiplicity ratios are measured to 2\%. We compare these data to predictions from seven major transport models. The calculations reproduce qualitatively the dependence of the multiplicities and their ratios on the total neutron to proton number in the colliding systems. However, the predictions of the transport models from different codes differ too much to allow extraction of reliable constraints on the symmetry energy from the data. This finding may explain previous contradictory conclusions on symmetry energy constraints obtained from pion data in Au+Au system. These new results call for better understanding of the differences among transport codes, and new observables that are more sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim
The measured charged pion multiplicities and their ratios from Sn+Sn collisions are precise enough (2-4% uncertainties) to constrain the symmetry energy, but the predictions from seven major transport models disagree too much to allow extraction of reliable constraints, which may explain previous contradictory conclusions from Au+Au data.

## Method
Central collisions of 132Sn+124Sn, 112Sn+124Sn, and 108Sn+112Sn at E/A=270 MeV were measured using the SπRIT Time Projection Chamber at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory. Charged pion multiplicities and π−/π+ yield ratios were extracted with high precision, and double ratios were constructed for systems with the same total charge but different isospin asymmetries. The experimental results were compared to predictions from seven transport codes (χBUU, TuQMD, pBUU, AMD+JAM, IQMD-BNU, SMASH, UrQMD) using two choices of symmetry energy stiffness (soft and stiff) without any parameter adjustment.

## Key numbers
- Individual pion multiplicities measured to 4% uncertainty; charged pion multiplicity ratios measured to 2% uncertainty
- For 132Sn+124Sn: π− multiplicity = 0.603(20), π+ multiplicity = 0.131(5), π−/π+ single ratio = 4.60(11)
- For 108Sn+112Sn: π− multiplicity = 0.349(12), π+ multiplicity = 0.185(8), π−/π+ single ratio = 1.89(4)
- Double ratio [Y(π−)/Y(π+)]_{132+124} / [Y(π−)/Y(π+)]_{108+112} = 2.42 ± 0.05 (2% uncertainty)
- Systematic errors: 3% for π− multiplicities, 4% for π+ multiplicities; statistical uncertainties less than 1.2%
- Selected central events correspond to cross section of about 0.3 barns, mean impact parameter ≈3 fm
- Beam energy: E/A = 270 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous pion production studies in Au+Au systems (Reisdorf 2007) that led to contradictory symmetry energy constraints (Feng 2010, Xiao 2009, Xie 2013). The Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP) had previously developed benchmark calculations in a box (Ono 2019, Xu 2016, Zhang 2018) showing good agreement among codes for pion yield ratios when identical input parameters were used. The present results demonstrate that when codes use their own best modeling choices, predictions diverge significantly, calling for better understanding of transport code differences and new observables more sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
