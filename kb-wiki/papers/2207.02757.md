# Constituent quark-model hidden-flavor pentaquarks

> We study hidden-flavor pentaquarks, $Q\bar Q qqq$, based on a constituent quark-model with a standard quark-quark interaction that reproduces the low-energy meson and baryon spectra. We make use of dynamical correlations between the heavy quarks arising from the Coulomb-like nature of the short-range interaction. A detailed comparison is made with other results in the literature and with experimental data. Our results show a different pattern for open-flavor and hidden-flavor pentaquarks, as suggested by the data. Further implications about the existence of quarkonia bound to nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors argue that dynamical correlations between the heavy quark-antiquark pair in hidden-flavor pentaquarks, arising from the Coulomb-like nature of the short-range interaction, freeze the color wave function into a specific configuration, reducing the five-body problem to a three-body problem that can be solved exactly via Faddeev equations; the resulting spectrum shows a different pattern for open-flavor and hidden-flavor pentaquarks, but the existence of bound states depends on selecting substructures that contain the most attractive QCD channels (e.g., spin-zero diquarks).

## Method

The authors used the AL1 constituent quark model (Semay and Silvestre-Brac, 1994), which includes a Coulomb-plus-linear central potential and a smeared chromomagnetic interaction, with parameters fitted to 36 mesons and 53 baryons. They assumed that the color wave function of the hidden-flavor pentaquark is uniquely determined by the requirement that the heavy quark-antiquark pair forms a color singlet, leading to an effective three-body system consisting of a light quark, a heavy quark-antiquark pair, and a light-quark pair. The bound-state problem was solved exactly using Faddeev equations in momentum space for S-wave and P-wave configurations, with the two-body amplitudes obtained from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The AL1 model parameters include: quark masses m_u = m_d = 0.315 GeV, m_c = 1.836 GeV, m_b = 5.227 GeV; string tension λ = 0.1653 GeV²; Coulomb strength κ = 0.5069.
- The mass difference between spin-1 and spin-0 heavy quark-antiquark pairs is taken as ΔM^{Q\bar Q} = 86 MeV, and between spin-1 and spin-0 light-quark pairs as ΔM^{qq} = 146 MeV.
- The calculated masses for hidden-charm pentaquarks (with M_0 = 4321 MeV) are: v₁ (I=1/2, J=1/2) at 4312 MeV, v₂ (I=1/2, J=1/2) at 4390 MeV, w₁ (I=1/2, J=3/2) at 4395 MeV, v₃ (I=1/2, J=1/2) at 4443 MeV, and w₃ (I=3/2, J=3/2) at 4455 MeV.
- These theoretical masses are compared to experimental P_c states: P_c(4312)⁺ at 4311.9 ± 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6} MeV, P_c(4380)⁺ at 4380 ± 8 ± 29 MeV, P_c(4440)⁺ at 4440.3 ± 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7} MeV, and P_c(4457)⁺ at 4457.3 ± 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7} MeV.
- The binding energy varies less than 1.5 MeV for variations up to 10% of the effective masses m₂ and m₃.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark-model approach to multiquarks previously applied to tetraquarks (e.g., the T⁺_{cc} predicted by potential-model calculations in Ade82 and later confirmed experimentally) and to pentaquarks studied under the diquark hypothesis (e.g., Maiani et al. 2015, Giron et al. 2019, Ali et al. 2019, Shi et al. 2021). The authors contrast their approach with uncorrelated multiquark models (Silvestre-Brac 1993, Richard 2018) and lattice QCD studies (Hughes et al. 2018, Hudspith et al. 2020) that find few bound states, and with diquark models that restrict color quantum numbers of pairs. The results enable further investigation of whether quarkonia can bind to nuclei, and provide a framework for analyzing the growing number of states in the quarkonium-nucleon energy region.
