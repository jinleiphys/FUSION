# Neutron gas and pairing

> We study the emergence of neutron gas effects in the description of nuclei with large neutron excess within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approach. We consider Ni and Sn isotopes where, in the literature, these effects have been found. We investigate the role of the single particle states with positive energy generating the neutron gas, and we find that the contribution of these states is numerically irrelevant for the various observables that we evaluate.

## Key claim

The authors find that, contrary to claims in the literature, the contribution of positive-energy single-particle states (the "neutron gas") to observables in neutron-rich nuclei is numerically irrelevant within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach, provided that numerical convergence is properly achieved; the neutron gas problem is therefore a numerical artifact rather than a genuine physical effect.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction to generate single-particle bases for spherical Ni and Sn isotopes, then solved the BCS equations using the same D1S interaction for pairing. They systematically varied the box size (R_box from 10 to 27 fm) and the maximum single-particle energy (epsilon_max from 8 to 12 MeV) to test numerical convergence, and compared their HF+BCS results with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1S interaction (from the AMEDEE database and the HFBAXIAL code) and the Skyrme SLy5 interaction (using the HFBRAD code).

## Key numbers

- Neutron rms radii for Ni isotopes (A=50-90): relative differences between HF(D1S)+BCS(D1S) and HFB^ref(D1S) are well below 1% for R_box=12 fm, and increase to about 4% for ^90Ni with R_box=20 fm (Fig. 1, panel a).
- For ^86Ni, ^88Ni, and ^90Ni, the 2d_{3/2} neutron single-particle energy converges to 0.502, 0.363, and 0.204 MeV respectively when R_box increases from 12 to 26 fm (Table 2).
- Neutron rms radii for Sn isotopes (^140Sn to ^172Sn): relative differences between HF(D1S)+BCS(D1S), HFB(D1S), and HFB(SLy5) are smaller than 2% for R_box >= 20 fm or N_ho >= 14 (Table 3).
- For ^150Sn, the asymptotic behavior of the neutron density follows the expected exponential decay with kappa = sqrt(|E_min - lambda| * 2m_n / hbar^2), where E_min = 0.561 MeV and lambda = -0.304 MeV (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work directly addresses the "neutron gas problem" identified in earlier studies by Dobaczewski et al. (1984, 1996, 1999), who reported that HF+BCS calculations for neutron-rich nuclei produced neutron radii and density tails that were too large compared to HFB results. The authors show that those earlier findings were due to insufficient numerical convergence, not a fundamental flaw in the BCS approach. The present results extend the work of Grasso et al. (who found similar convergence issues with the SIII Skyrme interaction) and demonstrate that the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction naturally cuts off coupling to high-energy states, avoiding the need for additional pairing interactions. This enables continued use of the simpler MF+BCS approach for calculating nuclear excited states via Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA).
