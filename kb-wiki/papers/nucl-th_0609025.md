# Fluctuations due to the nonlocal character of collisions

> It is shown that the collision integral describing the nonlocal character of collisions leads to the same mean-field fluctuations as proposed by Boltzmann-Langevin pictures. It is argued that this appropriate collision integral contains the fluctuation-dissipation theorems in equilibrium itself and therefore there is no need to assume additionally stochasticity. This leads to tremendous simplifications in numerical simulation schemes.

# Fluctuations due to the nonlocal character of collisions

## Key claim

The nonlocal extension of the Boltzmann collision integral deterministically generates the same mean-field fluctuations as those proposed by Boltzmann-Langevin stochastic approaches, provided the collisions are treated with finite spatial and temporal extent. The author argues that no additional ad-hoc stochastic term is needed because the appropriate nonlocal collision integral inherently satisfies fluctuation-dissipation theorems in equilibrium.

## Method

The author derives the zero-angle (zero momentum transfer) channel of the nonlocal kinetic equation, which is absent in standard local Boltzmann equations due to the absence of spatial and temporal shifts. By expanding the delay terms in the nonlocal collision integral and shifting them to the drift (mean-field) side of the kinetic equation, the author shows that the resulting term is proportional to the variance of the distribution function, matching the form used in Boltzmann-Langevin simulations. The derivation uses the non-equilibrium Green's function formalism and the nonlocal quasiparticle kinetic equation previously developed by the author and collaborators (Morawetz et al., 1996–1998).

## Key numbers

- The fluctuation term emerging from the nonlocal collision integral is proportional to $1/\tilde\tau$, where $1/\tilde\tau = \langle (2\pi s / \hbar) \int dp dq / (2\pi\hbar)^6 |\mathcal{T}^r|^2 \delta(k\cdot q/m_a - p\cdot q/m_b) f_p(1-f_p) \rangle$, with $s$ the spin-isospin degeneracy.
- The resulting mean-field fluctuation term has the form $\sigma_n^2(r,t) = (4/V) \int dp/\hbar^3 \langle f\rangle (1-\langle f\rangle)$, matching the density projection used in Boltzmann-Langevin codes (Chomaz et al., 1998).
- The nonlocal kinetic equation conserves density, momentum, and energy including two-particle correlated parts, and requires no more computational power than solving the Boltzmann equation.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal kinetic theory developed by Morawetz and collaborators (1996–1998) and addresses a long-standing problem in nuclear physics: the inability of time-dependent mean-field (TDHF) and standard Boltzmann (BUU) simulations to reproduce experimental fluctuations of observables like mass, charge, and momenta of emitted particles (Müller and Serot, 1985). Prior attempts to include fluctuations include the Balian-Veneroni variational approach (Balian and Veneroni, 1981), the time-dependent generator coordinate method (Reinhard et al., 1980; Reinhard and Stevenson, 1992), and stochastic TDHF (Reinhard and Stevenson, 1992), all of which are too limited for practical applications or require ad-hoc stochastic terms. The Boltzmann-Langevin approach (Aichelin and Gaitanos, 1990; etc.) adds a stochastic Langevin term to the collision integral, but the present work shows this is unnecessary if collisions are treated nonlocally. This enables deterministic numerical simulations with the same practical results as stochastic methods but with much less numerical effort.
