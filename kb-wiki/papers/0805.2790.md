# $N_pN_n$ scheme and the valence proton-neutron interaction

> We examine the common belief that the $N_pN_n$ scheme is manifested as a direct consequence of the valence proton-neutron interaction which has proven to be a dominant factor in developing collectivity in nuclei. We show that the simplification of the $N_pN_n$-plot of the lowest $2^+$ excitation energy is introduced merely because the excitation energy always decreases when the valence nucleon number becomes larger.

## Key claim
The authors argue that the simple pattern observed in $N_pN_n$ plots of nuclear observables (such as the lowest $2^+$ excitation energy) does not necessarily arise from the valence proton-neutron interaction, but instead originates from the trivial property that excitation energies decrease monotonically with valence neutron number for fixed proton number and vice versa. The $N_pN_n$ scheme is therefore not a direct proof of the dominance of the p-n interaction.

## Method
The authors examine the $N_pN_n$ scheme by plotting measured excitation energies $E_x(2_1^+)$ of even-even nuclei against the product $N_pN_n$, the sum $N_p+N_n$, and the Casten factor $P = N_pN_n/(N_p+N_n)$, and comparing the resulting patterns. They also analyze the multiplicity profiles of nuclides as a function of $A$, $N_pN_n$, and $N_p+N_n$ to show that differences in plot breadth arise from counting statistics rather than dynamical effects. The same analysis is extended to the lowest $3^-$ excitation energies.

## Key numbers
- The $N_pN_n$-multiplicity decreases steeply from a maximum value of around 100 at $N_pN_n = 0$, while the $(N_p+N_n)$-multiplicity stays around 50 up to $N_p+N_n = 20$.
- The $A$-multiplicity is more or less constant for all $A$ and has a small value of less than 10.
- The analysis covers isotopes with valence proton number $N_p = 10$ for elements with atomic numbers $Z = 38$, $40$, $60$, $72$, and $92$.

## Context
This work challenges the long-held interpretation by Casten (1985) and subsequent authors (Espino, Gupta, Saha, Zhao) that the $N_pN_n$ scheme is direct evidence for the valence proton-neutron interaction being the dominant factor in nuclear deformation, building on earlier work by de-Shalit and Goldhaber (1953) and Federman and Pittel (1979). The authors instead argue that any plot against a quantity that increases with $N_p$ or $N_n$ shows a similar tendency, and they point to Zamfir et al.'s parametrization of $E_x(3_1^-)$ by $N_p+N_n$ as an analogous case where conclusions about underlying interactions may be premature. The paper enables a more cautious interpretation of $N_pN_n$ plots and suggests that an empirical formula for $E_x(J_1^\pi)$ developed by the same group (Jin, Cha, Yoon, 2008) provides a more appropriate framework for understanding the observed systematics.
