# The $K^- p \rightarrow ηΛ$ reaction in an effective Lagrangian model

> We report on a theoretical study of the $K^- p \to ηΛ$ reaction near threshold by using an effective Lagrangian approach. The role of $s-$channel $Λ(1670)$, $t-$channel $K^*$ and $u-$channel proton pole diagrams are considered. We show that the total cross sections data are well reproduced. However, only including the $s-$wave $Λ(1670)$ state and the background contribution from $t-$ and $u-$channel are not enough to describe the bowl structures in the angular distribution of $K^- p \to ηΛ$ reaction, which indicates that there should be higher partial waves contributing to this reaction in some energy region. Indeed, if we considered the contributions from a $D_{03}$ resonance, we can describe the bowl structures, however, a rather small width ($\sim 2$ MeV) of this resonance is needed.

## Key claim

The total cross sections of the \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) reaction near threshold can be well reproduced using an effective Lagrangian model with \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1670)\), \(t\)-channel \(K^*\), and \(u\)-channel proton pole diagrams, but the bowl structures in the angular distributions require the inclusion of a \(D_{03}\) resonance with a very small width of about 2 MeV, which is not consistent with the conventional \(\Lambda(1690)\) resonance.

## Method

The authors constructed invariant scattering amplitudes from effective Lagrangian densities for the \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1670)\) exchange, \(t\)-channel \(K^*\) exchange, and \(u\)-channel proton pole diagrams. They performed a seven-parameter \(\chi^2\) fit to 155 data points of total and differential cross sections from the Crystal Ball Collaboration, using MINUIT to determine masses, widths, coupling constant products, and relative phases. A second fit additionally included a \(D_{03}\) resonance in the \(s\)-channel to describe the bowl structures in the angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Fitted mass of \(\Lambda(1670)\): \(1671.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV; total decay width: \(23.3 \pm 0.2\) MeV; product of coupling constants \(|g_{\Lambda^*{\bar K}N} g_{\Lambda^*\eta \Lambda}| = 0.28 \pm 0.03\).
- Fitted product \(g_{K^*N\Lambda}g_{K^*K\eta} = 14.8 \pm 1.7\); product \(g_{KN\Lambda}g_{\eta NN} = -5.6 \pm 0.9\); relative phases \(\theta_1 = 2.9 \pm 0.2\) and \(\theta_2 = 2.9 \pm 0.3\).
- Resultant \(\chi^2/dof = 1.3\) for the fit without the \(D_{03}\) resonance.
- Fitted mass of the \(D_{03}\) resonance: \(1668.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV; total decay width: \(1.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV; \(\chi^2/dof = 0.9\) for the fit including the \(D_{03}\) resonance.
- The bowl structures in angular distributions appear in a narrow energy window corresponding to \(K^-\) beam momenta from 730 to 742 MeV/c, which corresponds to an invariant mass range of about \(\pm 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) reaction using new high-precision data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration, which had previously been used to argue that \(\Lambda(1670)\) is a three-quark state, while other authors (Oset et al., 2010) argued it is dynamically generated. The study shows that the conventional \(\Lambda(1690)\) resonance cannot explain the observed bowl structures in angular distributions, suggesting the need for further experimental and theoretical studies to resolve the nature of the contributing hyperon resonances.
