# Longitudinal eccentricity decorrelations in heavy ion collisions

> In heavy-ion collisions, the harmonic flow $V_n$ of final-state particles are driven by the eccentricity vector ${\mathcal{E}}_n$ that describe the shape of the initial fireball projected in the transverse plane. It is realized recently that the structure and shape of the fireball, and consequently the ${\mathcal{E}}_n$, fluctuate in pseudorapidity $η$ in a single event, ${\mathcal{E}}_n(η)$. This leads to eccentricity decorrelation between different $η$, driving the longitudinal flow decorrelations observed in the experiments. Using a Glauber model with a paramerterized longitudinal structure, we have estimated the eccentricity decorrelations and related them to the measured flow decorrelation coefficients for elliptic flow $n=2$ and triangular flow $n=3$. We investigated the dependence of eccentricity decorrelations on the choice of collision system in terms of the size, asymmetry and deformation of the nuclei. We found that these nuclear geometry effects lead to significant and characteristic patterns on the eccentricity decorrelations, which describe the measured ratios of the flow decorrelations between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions. These patterns can be searched for using existing experimental data at RHIC and the LHC, and if confirmed, they will provide a mean to improve our understanding of the initial state of the heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
Using a Glauber model with parameterized longitudinal structure, the eccentricity decorrelations between different pseudorapidities can be related to measured flow decorrelation coefficients, and nuclear geometry effects (size, asymmetry, deformation) produce characteristic patterns in these decorrelations that describe the measured ratios of flow decorrelations between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions, though the quantitative agreement depends on whether results are plotted as a function of number of participants or scaled by atomic number.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo quark Glauber model simulations with three constituent quarks per nucleon, generating participant nucleon and quark positions for various collision systems (U, Pb, Au, Xe, Zr, Cu). The eccentricity vector was calculated from transverse quark positions, and the eccentricity decorrelation parameter A_n was obtained from the difference between forward-going and backward-going eccentricity vectors, using a mixing function f_n(η) to relate these to the pseudorapidity-dependent eccentricity.

## Key numbers
- Quark-quark cross-section chosen as σ_qq = 18 mb, corresponding to nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section σ_nn = 68 mb for √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Woods-Saxon skin depth a = 0.55 fm used for all nuclei
- Deformation parameters: β_2 = 0.28 and β_4 = 0.093 for U^d; β_2 = 0.162 and β_4 = -0.003 for Xe^d
- Nuclear radii: U = 6.81 fm, Pb = 6.62 fm, Au = 6.38 fm, Xe = 5.42 fm, Zr = 5.08 fm, Cu = 4.20 fm
- ε_n-ratios between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb agree with v_n-ratios as a function of N_part, while A_n-ratios agree with F_n-ratios as a function of N_part/2A, with about 10% difference for n=2 and 10-20% for n=3 between ε_n-ratio and v_n-ratio when plotted as a function of N_part/2A

## Context
This work extends the longitudinal flow decorrelation studies by CMS (Khachatryan 2015) and ATLAS (Aaboud 2017) in Pb+Pb collisions, and the recent ATLAS measurement in Xe+Xe (Aad 2020). It builds on the authors' previous work (Jia 2017) relating eccentricity decorrelations to flow decorrelations. The paper shows that current hydrodynamic models (Pang 2018, Wu 2018) reproduce v_n ratios but fail to describe F_n ratios between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb, indicating incorrect initial-state longitudinal geometry. The results enable searches for nuclear deformation effects using existing RHIC isobar data (Ru+Ru vs Zr+Zr) and LHC data.
