# Positivity Constraints on Spin Observables in Exclusive Pseudoscalar Meson Photoproduction

> Positivity constraints have proved to be important for spin observables of exclusive reactions involving polarized initial and final particles. Attention is focused in this note on the photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons from spin 1/2 baryons, more specifically gamma+ N -> K+ Lambda, gamma+ N -> K Sigma, for which new experimental data are becoming available.

## Key claim

Positivity constraints derived from the density matrix of the crossed channel provide model-independent inequalities among spin observables in exclusive pseudoscalar meson photoproduction; these inequalities must be satisfied by experimental data, and some preliminary results from CLAS and LEPS appear to violate them, pending final data.

## Method

The authors derive inequalities among spin observables for the reactions γN → KΛ and γN → KΣ using the positivity of the density matrix in the crossed channel γ+N+Λ̅ → K, which is of rank 1 and 4×4 dimension. They express the transition matrix in the transversity basis, obtaining four independent amplitudes, and then derive quadratic identities and inequalities from the vanishing of 2×2 sub-determinants of the density matrix. The inequalities are also obtained by elementary vector methods and by considering the polarization vectors of the recoil baryon under different photon polarization states.

## Key numbers

- For the LEPS reaction γn → K⁺Σ⁻ at incident photon energy 1.5 to 2.4 GeV, Σ^γ is close to 1 in a wide range of energies for cos(θ_cm) > 0.6.
- For CLAS data on γp → K⁺Y at center-of-mass energies W between 1.6 and 2.53 GeV and cosθ_K^c.m. between -0.85 and +0.95, a preliminary value P_Λ = -0.73 is observed at W = 1.73 GeV and cosθ_K^c.m. = +0.30.
- From the inequality (P^Y)² + (C^Y_x)² + (C^Y_z)² ≤ 1, the value P_Λ = -0.73 implies (C^Λ_x)² + (C^Λ_z)² ≤ 0.5, which appears inconsistent with a reported C^Λ_z ∼ 1.
- For GRAAL π⁰ photoproduction, Σ^γ = 0.990 ± 0.069 at incident energy E_γ = 1344 MeV and center-of-mass angle θ_cm = 45.1°.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of positivity constraints previously applied to reactions such as πN → πN, NN → NN, and NN̅ → ΛΛ̅, as well as to inclusive reactions and structure functions (Artru, Richard, Soffer, earlier work). The paper specifically addresses the reactions γN → KΛ and γN → KΣ, for which new data are becoming available from the SAPHIR, LEPS, CLAS, and GRAAL collaborations. The inequalities provide a model-independent test of data consistency and can be used to check the validity of dynamical assumptions in theoretical models; they also enable the identification of incompatible measurements before undertaking full amplitude reconstruction.
