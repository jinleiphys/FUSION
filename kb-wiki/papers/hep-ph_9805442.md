# Matching Long and Short Distances in Large-Nc QCD

> It is shown, with the example of the experimentally known Adler function, that there is no matching in the intermediate region between the two asymptotic regimes described by perturbative QCD (for the very short-distances) and by chiral perturbation theory (for the very long-distances). We then propose to consider an approximation of large-Nc QCD which consists in restricting the hadronic spectrum in the channels with J^P quantum numbers 0^-, 1^-, 0^+ and 1^+ to the lightest state and treating the rest of the narrow states as a perturbative QCD continuum; the onset of this continuum being fixed by consistency constraints from the operator product expansion. We show how to construct the low-energy effective Lagrangian which describes this approximation. The number of free parameters in the resulting effective Lagrangian can be reduced, in the chiral limit where the light quark masses are set to zero, to just one mass scale and one dimensionless constant to all orders in chiral perturbation theory. A comparison of the corresponding predictions, to O(p^4) in the chiral expansion, with the phenomenologically known couplings is also made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that direct matching between perturbative QCD (short distances) and chiral perturbation theory (long distances) fails in the intermediate region, even when non-perturbative power corrections are included; the authors propose that a "lowest meson dominance" (LMD) approximation to large-Nc QCD, where the hadronic spectrum is restricted to the lightest state per channel plus a perturbative continuum, can successfully interpolate between the two regimes.

## Method

The authors analyze the Adler function as a test case, comparing its behavior from perturbative QCD (up to O(alpha_s^3)), from chiral perturbation theory, and from experimental tau-decay data. They then construct an effective Lagrangian starting from an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) type Lagrangian, but modify it by adding an infinite set of local operators whose couplings are fixed by requiring removal of non-confining Q\bar{Q} discontinuities and by imposing matching conditions from the operator product expansion (OPE). The resulting Lagrangian, when quark fields are integrated out, yields a nonlinear sigma model with Goldstone, vector, axial-vector, and scalar fields, where the number of free parameters reduces to one mass scale and one dimensionless constant in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Onset of the perturbative QCD continuum from experimental data: s0 = (1.60 ± 0.17) GeV^2
- Onset of the continuum for the VMD parameterization: s_rho = (1.53 ± 0.06) GeV^2
- Gluon condensate range: <alpha_s G^{munu}_a G^a_{munu}> = (0.08 ± 0.04) GeV^4
- Quark condensate at 1 GeV^2: <bar{psi}psi>(1 GeV^2) = [(-235 ± 27) MeV]^3
- Lambda_MSbar = (372 ± 76) MeV for three flavors

## Context

This work extends the program of understanding QCD in the large-Nc limit initiated by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979), and addresses the long-standing problem of matching short-distance perturbative QCD with long-distance chiral perturbation theory, as formulated by Weinberg (1967, 1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985). The paper builds on the SVZ operator product expansion approach (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1979) and on previous ENJL model studies by Bijnens, Bruno, and de Rafael (1993) and Bijnens, de Rafael, and Zheng (1994). The LMD approximation enables systematic calculation of low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory to all orders, addressing the problem that at O(p^6) the chiral Lagrangian contains approximately one hundred unknown couplings (Fearing and Scherer, 1996).
