# Simple high-accuracy method for solving bound-state equations with the Cornell potential in momentum space

> The well-known Cornell quark-antiquark potential in momentum space contains singularities both in its one-gluon-exchange (OGE) and linear confining parts, which prevents a direct use of the convenient Nyström method to solve the corresponding bound-state integral equation for the meson masses. While it has been known for a long time how the Coulomb-type singularity in the OGE potential can be treated with a subtraction technique, only very complicated methods have been developed to deal with the stronger singularity in the linear potential. In this work, we present a simple subtraction method to remove this singularity from the kernel, such that the Nyström method becomes applicable. Derivatives of the wave function, that appear as a result of the subtraction, are represented by means of interpolating functions, for which we found Lagrange polynomials to be very efficient. Test calculations show excellent agreement with exactly known energy eigenvalues. By increasing the number of integration points and the order of the Lagrange interpolation polynomials, extremely high accuracy can be achieved. This method can also be extended to relativistic Bethe-Salpeter type equations with singular kernels.

## Key claim

The authors present a simple subtraction method that removes the principal-value singularity of the linear confining potential from the kernel of the momentum-space Schrodinger equation, making the Nystrom method applicable; the method requires representing the first and second derivatives of the wave function via Lagrange interpolation, which the authors claim is very efficient and can achieve extremely high accuracy.

## Method

The authors start from the partial-wave momentum-space Schrodinger equation with the Cornell potential, which contains a Coulomb-type singularity (treatable by a known Lande subtraction) and a stronger principal-value singularity from the linear confining term. They apply a second Lande subtraction to remove the principal-value singularity, which introduces first and second derivatives of the wave function into the kernel. These derivatives are then approximated at the quadrature points using Lagrange polynomial interpolation, allowing the discretized equation to be written as a standard matrix eigenvalue problem solvable by the Nystrom method.

## Key numbers

* For the pure linear potential with l=0, using N=1000 Gauss-Legendre points and 5-point Lagrange interpolation, the ground state energy (n=1) converges to 2.3381074105 in units of (sigma^2/2m_R)^(1/3), matching the exact value to all digits shown.
* For the same system, the n=10 eigenvalue converges to 12.8287765499 with N=1000, compared to the exact value 12.8287767529 in the same units.
* With N=50 points, the ground state energy is 2.3381366586, showing that even a modest grid gives results within about 1e-5 of the exact value.
* The method reproduces the exact Airy-function eigenvalues for the linear potential to at least 10 significant figures for the lowest 10 states when N=1000.

## Context

This work extends the Lande subtraction technique, previously applied to the Coulomb singularity by Kwon (1978), to the linear confining potential of the Cornell model, which had previously required more complicated methods such as the Galerkin approach with B-spline basis functions used by Leitao (2014) or the specialized quadrature method of Andreev (2017). The new method enables straightforward application of the Nystrom method to the Cornell potential in momentum space without needing to choose basis functions with correct asymptotic behavior, and the authors state it can be extended to relativistic Bethe-Salpeter type equations with singular kernels.
