# N-d scattering above the deuteron breakup threshold

> The complex Kohn variational principle and the (correlated) Hyperspherical Harmonics technique are applied to study the N--d scattering above the deuteron breakup threshold. The configuration with three outgoing nucleons is explicitly taken into account by solving a set of differential equations with outgoing boundary conditions. A convenient procedure is used to obtain the correct boundary conditions at values of the hyperradius $\approx 100$ fm. The inclusion of the Coulomb potential is straightforward and does not give additional difficulties. Numerical results have been obtained for a simple s-wave central potential. They are in nice agreement with the benchmarks produced by different groups using the Faddeev technique. Comparisons are also done with experimental elastic N--d cross section at several energies.

# N-d scattering above the deuteron breakup threshold

## Key claim

The complex Kohn variational principle combined with the Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics (PHH) basis can successfully describe N-d scattering above the deuteron breakup threshold, including the three-outgoing-nucleon configuration, but only for a simple s-wave central potential (Malfliet-Tjon I-III) in the results presented here; extension to realistic potentials is deferred to a forthcoming paper.

## Method

The wave function is written as a sum of a core term Ψ_C expanded in the PHH basis and an asymptotic term Ψ_A describing the incident nucleon and deuteron. The complex Kohn variational principle is used to determine both the hyperradial functions and the S-matrix elements by solving a set of coupled second-order differential equations with outgoing boundary conditions. Asymptotic boundary conditions for three outgoing nucleons are imposed at a hyperradius of approximately 100 fm using an expansion in inverse powers of ρ, with Coulomb effects included via a matrix χ term in the asymptotic equations.

## Key numbers

- Eight hyperradial functions per channel are sufficient for four-digit accuracy in the phase shift parameters.
- At E_N = 14.1 MeV, the s-wave phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for n-d and p-d scattering are converged; a constant difference of about 3° is observed between p-d and n-d s-wave phase shifts at the energies considered.
- The matching radius ρ₀ > 80 fm is required for the asymptotic form to be reached; solutions are insensitive to variation of ρ₀ when this condition is met.
- At E_n = 14.1 MeV, (1 − ²S⁺¹η_L) < 10⁻³ for L ≥ 4 in n-d scattering.
- The S-matrix unitarity relation is verified numerically with a precision of 10⁻⁵.
- Elastic cross sections are calculated at neutron energies E_n = 3, 9, 18 MeV and proton energies E_p = 3, 6, 9, 18 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the variational technique previously developed by Kievsky, Viviani, and Rosati (1994) for N-d scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold to the regime above breakup. It provides an alternative to the Faddeev approach used by Kloet and Tjon and by the benchmark calculations of the early 1990s, with the advantage that Coulomb repulsion (important for p-d scattering) is handled straightforwardly, unlike in the Faddeev approach where it poses difficulties as noted by Alt, Merkuriev, and others. The method enables calculation of both elastic and breakup cross sections for n-d and p-d systems, and the authors state that extension to realistic NN interactions will follow in a subsequent paper.
