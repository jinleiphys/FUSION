# Effect of centrality bin width corrections on two-particle number and transverse momentum differential correlation functions

> Two-particle number and transverse momentum differential correlation functions are powerful tools for unveiling the detailed dynamics and particle production mechanisms involved in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Measurements of transverse momentum correlators $P_2$ and $G_2$, in particular, provide added information not readily accessible with better known number correlation functions $R_2$. However, it is found that the $R_2$ and $G_2$ correlators are somewhat sensitive to the details of the experimental procedure used to measure them. They exhibit, in particular, a dependence on the collision centrality bin width, which may have a rather detrimental impact on their physical interpretation. A technique to correct these correlators for collision centrality bin-width averaging is presented. The technique is based on the hypothesis that the shape of single- and pair- probability densities vary slower with collision centrality than the corresponding integrated yields. The technique is tested with Pb-Pb simulations based on the HIJING and ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics models and shown to enable a precision better than 1% for particles in the kinematic range $0.2 \leq p_{\rm T} \leq 2.0$ GeV/$c$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-particle number and transverse momentum differential correlation functions $R_2$ and $G_2$ exhibit a dependence on the width of collision centrality bins used in experimental analyses, which can bias their physical interpretation; the authors present a correction technique based on the hypothesis that the shape of single- and pair-probability densities varies more slowly with collision centrality than the corresponding integrated yields, and show that this correction enables precision better than 1% for particles in the kinematic range $0.2 \leq p_{\rm T} \leq 2.0$ GeV/$c$.

## Method

The authors derive correction formulas for the $R_2$ and $G_2$ correlators by expressing particle densities in terms of probability densities and mean multiplicities, then calculating the ratio of bin-averaged quantities to the desired average of the correlator across the centrality bin. They test the correction procedure using HIJING and ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, comparing corrected results for a 0-5% centrality bin against the weighted average of results from five narrower bins (0-1%, 1-2%, 2-3%, 3-4%, and 4-5%).

## Key numbers

- The correction technique enables precision better than 1% for both $R_2$ and $G_2$ correlators when tested with HIJING and UrQMD simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.
- The analysis uses $6.5 \times 10^4$ Pb-Pb collisions from HIJING and $10^5$ Pb-Pb collisions from UrQMD for the simulation tests.
- Particles are analyzed in the kinematic range $0.2 \leq p_{\rm T} \leq 2.0$ GeV/$c$ and pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 0.8$.
- The $P_2$ correlator is shown to be unaffected by centrality bin width, with a unitary correction factor, under the assumption that probability densities vary only modestly within a bin.

## Context

This work extends prior unpublished correction procedures used by the STAR collaboration for differential two-particle transverse momentum correlations in Au-Au collisions (Adams 2005) and by the ALICE collaboration for $R_2$ and $P_2$ correlators in $p$-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions (Abelev 2014). The paper provides a formal derivation and documentation of these correction methods, and establishes their accuracy through systematic tests with HIJING and UrQMD event generators. The results enable future experimental analyses to apply centrality bin-width corrections with quantified systematic uncertainties, improving the reliability of two-particle correlation measurements in heavy-ion collisions.
