# Inferring the breakdown scales of the chiral expansions for $g_A$ and $m_N$

> We apply Bayesian inference to the order-by-order chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) expansions for the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ and the nucleon mass m_N, and thereby infer the scales at which $χ$PT breaks down for these two observables. Using a pointwise Bayesian analysis, we find that the inferred breakdown scales are notably different for the two observables. For the chiral expansion of $g_A$, we obtain $251^{+20}_{-50}$ MeV and $211^{+20}_{-30}$ MeV using two distinct sets of low-energy constants, while for the chiral expansion of $m_N$ we infer a significantly larger breakdown scale of $491^{+60}_{-90}$ MeV.

## Key claim

The breakdown scale of chiral perturbation theory inferred from the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) is approximately 230 MeV, close to the nucleon-delta mass splitting, while the breakdown scale inferred from the nucleon mass \(m_N\) is approximately 490 MeV, closer to the mass scale of light mesonic degrees of freedom; the authors emphasize that the inferred breakdown scale depends on the observable used for inference.

## Method

The authors applied Bayesian inference to order-by-order chiral perturbation theory expansions for \(g_A\) and \(m_N\), using a pointwise Bayesian analysis that treats the expansion coefficients as natural-sized random variables with a normal prior and a conjugate inverse-chi-squared hyperprior. They extracted expansion coefficients from the chiral expansions evaluated at two pion mass values between 9.85 MeV and 301.84 MeV, and assumed a log-uniform prior for the breakdown scale \(\Lambda_B\) with compact support on the interval \([M_\pi^{\text{(phys)}}/40, 40 M_\pi^{\text{(phys)}}]\).

## Key numbers

- For the chiral expansion of \(g_A\) using Set 1 low-energy constants, the inferred breakdown scale at order \(M^4\) is \(251^{+20}_{-50}\) MeV (median and 68% highest posterior density interval).
- For the chiral expansion of \(g_A\) using Set 2 low-energy constants, the inferred breakdown scale at order \(M^4\) is \(211^{+20}_{-30}\) MeV.
- For the chiral expansion of \(m_N\) using Set \(m_N\) low-energy constants, the inferred breakdown scale at order \(M^4\) is \(491^{+60}_{-90}\) MeV.
- The nucleon-delta mass splitting is approximately 290 MeV, which the authors relate to the lower breakdown scale found for \(g_A\).

## Context

This work extends Bayesian techniques previously applied to chiral effective field theory for two-nucleon scattering, nuclei with mass number \(A=3\) and \(4\), and neutron matter (Melendez 2017, Wesolowski 2021, Drischler 2020, Millican 2024, McClung 2025), where a breakdown scale of approximately 600 MeV was inferred. It also builds on earlier qualitative comparisons of baryon chiral perturbation theory expansions for \(m_N\) and \(g_A\) (Djukanovic 2006, McGovern 2006, Schindler 2006, Bernard 2006) that found expansions become unreliable around pion masses of 300 to 500 MeV. The results enable future work that would estimate low-energy constants from data in the presence of truncation errors and consistently determine the breakdown scale as part of that inference process, and raise the question of whether the breakdown scale inferred from expansions in the pion mass is the same for momentum-dependent observables such as form factors.
