# Truncation scheme of time-dependent density-matrix approach II

> A truncation scheme of the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon hierarchy for reduced density matrices, where a three-body density matrix is approximated by two-body density matrices, is improved to take into account a normalization effect. The truncation scheme is tested for the Lipkin model. It is shown that the obtained results are in good agreement with the exact solutions.

## Key claim

The authors improve a previous truncation scheme for the BBGKY hierarchy by including a normalization factor of the total wavefunction, which suppresses the three-body correlation matrix and yields much better agreement with exact solutions for the Lipkin model, particularly in the strongly interacting regime where the previous scheme (TDDM$_0$) underestimated correlation effects.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent density-matrix (TDDM) equations of motion for the one-body and two-body density matrices, truncating the BBGKY hierarchy by approximating the three-body correlation matrix using products of two-body correlation matrices derived from a coupled-cluster-doubles-like wavefunction. The new truncation scheme (TDDM) includes the normalization factor ${\cal N} = 1 + \frac{1}{4}\sum_{\rm pp'hh'}z_{\rm pp'hh'}z^*_{\rm pp'hh'}$ in the expressions for the three-body correlation matrix, whereas the previous scheme (TDDM$_0$) omitted this factor. The method is tested by calculating ground-state properties of the Lipkin model for systems with $N=8$ and $N=20$ fermions.

## Key numbers

- For $N=8$ at $\chi=5$, the normalization factor ${\cal N}=2.0$.
- For $N=20$ at $\chi=5$, the normalization factor ${\cal N}=6.6$.
- The ground-state energy, occupation probability $n_{-p}$, two-body correlation matrix element $C_{-p-p'pp'}$, and three-body correlation matrix element $C_{-pp'p''p-p'p''}$ are shown as functions of $\chi=|V|(N-1)/\epsilon$ for both $N=8$ and $N=20$, with TDDM results in good agreement with exact solutions across the range shown (approximately $\chi=0$ to $\chi=5$).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous truncation scheme (Tohyama and Schuck, 2014) which used perturbative expressions for the three-body correlation matrix but underestimated correlation effects in strongly interacting regions. The new normalization procedure improves upon both that scheme and the simplest truncation (Wang and Cassing, 1985; Gong and Tohyama, 1990) where the three-body correlation matrix is completely neglected, which overestimates ground-state correlations. The approach enables more accurate treatment of strongly correlated fermion systems using density-matrix methods, and the authors note they have also applied TDDM to the one-dimensional Hubbard model with improved results.
