# Symmetry energy in nuclear density functional theory

> The nuclear symmetry energy represents a response to the neutron-proton asymmetry. In this survey we discuss various aspects of symmetry energy in the framework of nuclear density functional theory, considering both non-relativistic and relativistic self-consistent mean-field realizations side-by-side. Key observables pertaining to bulk nucleonic matter and finite nuclei are reviewed. Constraints on the symmetry energy and correlations between observables and symmetry-energy parameters, using statistical covariance analysis, are investigated. Perspectives for future work are outlined in the context of ongoing experimental efforts.

## Key claim

This survey reviews the nuclear symmetry energy (NSE) within nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using both non-relativistic and relativistic self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) models, but the authors note that large uncertainties remain in the isovector channel because it is poorly constrained by experiment, and the underlying energy density functionals (EDFs) differ in functional form and optimization strategies even within a single family.

## Method

The authors performed a side-by-side comparison of three SCMF models: Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF), relativistic mean-field with point-coupling (RMF-PC), and relativistic mean-field with meson-exchange (RMF-ME). They used a standard least-squares optimization technique with a chi-squared objective function to adjust coupling constants to a common set of experimental data on spherical nuclei (masses, diffraction radii, surface thickness, charge radii, separation energies, isotope shifts, and odd-even mass differences). Statistical covariance analysis was applied to study correlations between symmetry-energy parameters and finite-nuclei observables, including constrained fits with systematically varied symmetry energy values and computation of Pearson correlation coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi-gas model estimate for the symmetry energy coefficient is a_sym approximately 25 MeV, with near-equality of kinetic and potential contributions a_sym,kin approximately a_sym,int.
- SHF has 11 adjustable parameters, RMF-PC has 10, and RMF-ME has 8.
- For RPA calculations in Pb-208, the energy-dependent width parameters are Delta_min = 0.2 MeV, E_thr = 10 MeV, and E_slope = 5 MeV.
- The liquid drop model expansion includes terms a_vol (volume), a_surf (surface), a_curv (curvature), a_sym (symmetry), a_ssym (surface-symmetry), and a_sym^(2) (quartic symmetry).
- The neutron pressure P(rho_n) = rho_n^2 d/drho_n (E/A)_n is identified as an excellent isovector indicator.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on nuclear DFT and symmetry energy, including the Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean-field approaches reviewed in Ben03, Lal04, and Vre05. It builds on the covariance analysis framework developed in Rei10 and the fitting protocol from Klu09 and Kor10. The survey enables future work to better constrain the isovector channel of nuclear EDFs using experimental efforts on neutron radii, neutron skins, dipole polarizability, and neutron star radii, as well as planned extensions involving isospin cranking.
