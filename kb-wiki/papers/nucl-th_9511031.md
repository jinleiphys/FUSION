# New QCD Sum Rules for In-Medium Nucleons

> New QCD sum rules for nucleons in nuclear matter are derived from a mixed correlation function of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields. These sum rules allow a determination of the scalar self-energy of the nucleon independent of the poorly known four-quark condensates. An analysis of these new sum rules in concert with previous nucleon sum rules from spin-1/2 interpolators indicates consistency with the expectations of relativistic mean-field phenomenology. We find $M^* = 0.68 \pm 0.08$ GeV and $Σ_v = 0.31 \pm 0.06$ GeV at nuclear matter saturation density.

## Key claim

The new QCD sum rules derived from a mixed correlation function of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields allow a determination of the nucleon scalar self-energy that is independent of the poorly known four-quark condensates, and the results are consistent with relativistic mean-field phenomenology.

## Method

The authors derived new finite-density QCD sum rules from a mixed correlation function of generalized spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 interpolating fields for nucleons in nuclear matter. They evaluated the correlation function at large space-like momenta using an operator product expansion (OPE) and equated this to a phenomenological Ansatz for the spectral densities using appropriately weighted integrals. The analysis used a Monte-Carlo error analysis with 1000 QCD parameters to estimate uncertainties, and the valid Borel region was defined by limiting continuum model contributions to 50% and highest-dimensional operator contributions to less than 10% of the OPE sum.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass in nuclear matter: M* = 0.68 ± 0.08 GeV at nuclear matter saturation density
- Vector self-energy: Σ_v = 0.31 ± 0.06 GeV at nuclear matter saturation density
- The vector self-energy was initially fixed at Σ_v = 0.25 GeV at saturation density for the analysis

## Context

This work extends the finite-density QCD sum-rule approach of Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992) by deriving new sum rules that avoid the problematic density dependence of four-quark condensates. The results are consistent with relativistic mean-field phenomenology and provide a more robust determination of the nucleon scalar self-energy. The new sum rules enable future studies of in-medium nucleon properties without relying on poorly constrained four-quark condensate modeling.
