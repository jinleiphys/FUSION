# Chiral first order phase transition at finite baryon density and zero temperature from self-consistent pole masses in the linear sigma model with quarks

> We use the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model with quarks as an effective description of QCD to investigate the nature of the chiral phase transition at finite baryon chemical potential and zero temperature. We work at one-loop order to set up and solve the system of self-consistent coupled equations for the particle pole masses. The chemical potential-dependent value of the chiral order parameter is obtained by minimizing the one-loop effective potential. This treatment goes beyond the conventional ring-diagram approximation and provides a description valid for arbitrary values of the chemical potential. We find that the phase transition is of first order, and occurs when the quark chemical potential reaches the value of the vacuum quark mass for the chosen set of parameters. The first order nature of the transition is signaled by the discontinuous behavior of the chiral condensate, the masses and the couplings. The thermodynamics of the system is readily implemented and in particular, we find that the square of the speed of sound exhibits a discontinuity at the phase transition and then smoothly approaches the conformal limit from below.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential is of first order, occurring when the quark chemical potential reaches the vacuum quark mass for the chosen set of parameters, as determined by a self-consistent one-loop treatment that goes beyond the conventional ring-diagram approximation.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model with quarks (LSMq) at one-loop order in perturbation theory, computing the self-consistent coupled equations for the particle pole masses of pions, sigma, and quarks at finite baryon chemical potential and zero temperature. The chiral order parameter (sigma field vacuum expectation value) was obtained by minimizing the one-loop effective potential, while the dynamical masses were solved simultaneously from the self-energy equations derived via the Matsubara formalism in the zero-temperature limit.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition occurs when the quark chemical potential reaches the value of the vacuum quark mass for the chosen set of parameters.
- The square of the speed of sound exhibits a discontinuity at the phase transition and then smoothly approaches the conformal limit from below.
- The fermion matter contribution to the effective potential involves an integral over momentum up to the Fermi momentum \(k_F = \sqrt{\mu^{2}-m_f^{2}}\), with the step function \(\Theta(\mu-M_f)\) controlling the onset of the medium contribution.
- The dynamical pion mass equation includes an integral over \(q\) from 0 to \(\sqrt{\mu^2 - M_f^2}\) with the factor \(\frac{4N_cN_f g^2}{\pi^2} \int dq\,\frac{q^4}{\sqrt{q^2 + M_f^2}}\,\frac{1}{M_\pi^2 - 4(q^2 + M_f^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the LSMq that used the ring-diagram approximation (Ayala 2021, Ayala 2015) and the static, high-temperature limit for meson self-energies, which were limited to marginally good approximations for the chiral transition line. The self-consistent pole mass approach enables a description valid for arbitrary values of the chemical potential, overcoming the limitations of earlier analytical approximations that could only be obtained in the large-temperature limit (Ayala 2017). This method can be extended to finite temperature in future work to explore the critical end point region relevant to the NICA and STAR BES-II experimental programs.
