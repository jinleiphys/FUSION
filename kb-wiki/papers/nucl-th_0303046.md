# Influence of hydrodynamics on the interpretation of the high $p_{T}$ hadron suppression at RHIC

> A hybrid parameterization including contributions of hydrodynamics and of expectations from the spectral shape observed in p+p collisions is introduced. This parameterization can successfully describe identified hadron spectra over a wide range of $p_{T}$ in Au+Au reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV for all centralities. The parameters of the hydrodynamic source compare reasonably well to other attempts to describe the spectra. The description is obtained using one universal suppression factor of the hard scattering component independent of $p_{T}$ and hadron species. For the fit results obtained the observed nuclear modification factor for the different particles converges to a universal suppression behavior for $p_{T} > 6 \mathrm{GeV}/c$.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be described by a universal, \(p_T\)- and species-independent suppression factor of the hard scattering component only when a significant hydrodynamic contribution to the hadron spectra at intermediate \(p_T\) is included; without this hydrodynamic component, the extracted suppression appears weaker and species-dependent.

## Method

The author constructed a hybrid parameterization that adds a hydrodynamic source (with transverse flow, chemical freeze-out, and resonance decays) to a p+p-inspired spectral shape separated into soft and hard components. The soft component was assumed to scale with the number of participant nucleons, while the hard component scaled with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions. Fits were performed simultaneously to PHENIX spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV for all centrality classes, with the kinetic temperature fixed to 120 MeV and the hydrodynamic normalization, chemical temperature, baryochemical potential, strangeness suppression, transverse flow velocity, and the two p+p normalization factors left free.

## Key numbers

- Chemical temperature \(T_{chem} \approx 160\) MeV, nearly independent of centrality, with baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_B/T_{chem} \approx 0.15\)–\(0.2\).
- Strangeness suppression factor \(\lambda_s \approx 0.65\) for peripheral collisions, increasing to \(\lambda_s \approx 0.9\) for central collisions.
- Average transverse flow velocity \(\langle \beta_T \rangle \approx 0.25\) for peripheral reactions, rising to \(\langle \beta_T \rangle \approx 0.5\) for central reactions.
- For central collisions (0–5%), the universal suppression factor of the hard component extracted from the full fit is smaller than 0.2.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for different hadron species converges to a universal suppression behavior for \(p_T > 6\) GeV/\(c\).
- The hydrodynamic contribution remains significant for pions out to at least \(p_T = 3\) GeV/\(c\) and for protons and antiprotons out to \(p_T = 5\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic parameterizations by Peitzmann (2002) and others (Broniowski, Kolb) that described low- and intermediate-\(p_T\) spectra, and addresses the puzzle that the large proton/antiproton yield at intermediate \(p_T\) (observed by PHENIX) appeared inconsistent with a simple jet-quenching picture. By combining hydrodynamics with a p+p-based hard component, the paper shows that the apparent species dependence of the suppression is largely an artifact of neglecting the hydrodynamic yield, and that a single suppression factor can describe all species once that contribution is accounted for. This enables future studies of the interplay between jet energy loss and the hydrodynamic medium, as in Hirano (2002).
