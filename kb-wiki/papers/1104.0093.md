# Finite Pressure Corrections to Nucleon Structure Function Inside a Nuclear Medium

> Our model calculations performed in the frame of the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach show how important are the modifications of nucleon Structure Function (SF) in Nuclear Matter (NM) above the saturation point. They originated from the conservation of a parton longitudinal momenta - essential in the explanation of the EMC effect at the saturation point of NM. For higher density the finite pressure corrections emerge from the Hugenholtz -van Hove (HH) theorem valid for NM and asks to modify the nucleon SF. The density evolution of the nuclear SF seems to be stronger for higher densities. Here we show that the course of Equation o State (EoS) in our modified Walecka model is very close to that obtained from extensive DBHF calculations with a Bonn A potential. The nuclear compressibility decreases. Our model - a nonlinear extension of nuclear RMF, has no additional parameters.

# Finite Pressure Corrections to Nucleon Structure Function Inside a Nuclear Medium

## Key claim
The paper argues that above nuclear saturation density, finite pressure corrections from the Hugenholtz–van Hove theorem break the Momentum Sum Rule by a factor \(E_F/\varepsilon_N > 1\), requiring the nucleon structure function to be squeezed toward smaller Bjorken \(x\) and the nucleon mass to decrease, in order to restore the sum rule without introducing new parameters.

## Method
The author uses a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach based on the Walecka model, extended by a nonlinear modification where the nucleon mass becomes density-dependent via the factor \(\varepsilon_N/E_F\). The nuclear structure function is computed in the convolution model, restricted to nucleons only (pion contributions are argued to be negligible at high density). The equation of state is solved self-consistently with this mass modification and compared to Dirac–Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (DBHF) calculations with the Bonn A potential.

## Key numbers
- At saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.19\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (S1 parametrization) and \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (S2 parametrization), the standard Walecka model gives compressibility \(K^{-1} \simeq 560\ \text{MeV}\), which is too large.
- The nucleon mass in the medium is modified as \(M_{\text{med}} = (\varepsilon_N / E_F) M_N \simeq M_N (1 - p / (\varrho \varepsilon_N))\), decreasing with increasing pressure.
- The nucleon distribution function \(\rho^A(y)\) yields the integral \(\int dy\, y \rho^A(y) = E_F / \varepsilon_N\), which equals 1 at saturation but deviates above saturation.
- The correction factor for the structure function argument is \(E_F / \varepsilon_N > 1\) for positive pressure, squeezing the structure function toward smaller \(x\).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies of the EMC effect at saturation density (Rozynek, 2007; Rozynek and Wilk, 2009) to densities above saturation, where finite pressure becomes significant. It contrasts with the stiff equation of state of the standard Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) and with nonrelativistic expansions in Fermi momentum (Dabrowski and others). The results are compared to DBHF calculations with the Bonn A potential (Bonn group) and to the ZM model (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990). The softened equation of state is relevant for compact star properties and mass–radius constraints (Lattimer and Prakash, 2007).
