# Systematic Uncertainties from Gribov Copies in Lattice Calculation of Parton Distributions in the Coulomb gauge

> Recently, it has been proposed to compute parton distributions from boosted correlators fixed in the Coulomb gauge within the framework of Large-Momentum Effective Theory. This method does not involve Wilson lines and could greatly improve the efficiency and precision of lattice QCD calculations. However, there are concerns about whether the systematic uncertainties from Gribov copies, which correspond to the ambiguity in lattice gauge-fixing, are under control. This work gives an assessment of the Gribov copies' effect in the Coulomb-gauge-fixed quark correlators. We utilize different strategies for the Coulomb-gauge fixing, selecting two different groups of Gribov copies based on the lattice gauge configurations. We test the difference in the resulted spatial quark correlators in the vacuum and a pion state. Our findings indicate that the statistical errors of the matrix elements from both Gribov copies, regardless of the correlation range, decrease proportionally to the square root of the number of gauge configurations. The difference between the strategies does not show statistical significance compared to the gauge noise. This demonstrates that the effect of the Gribov copies can be neglected in the practical lattice calculation of the quark parton distributions.

## Key claim

The systematic uncertainty from Gribov copies in Coulomb-gauge-fixed quark correlators is negligible compared to the statistical noise from averaging over gauge configurations, supporting the viability of the Coulomb gauge method for lattice calculation of parton distributions without Wilson lines.

## Method

The authors generated different Gribov copies on 100 gauge configurations from a 2+1 flavor HISQ ensemble using the mother-daughter approach, applying 8 instances of gauge-fixing per configuration with a precision of delta F/F <= 10^-8. They compared two strategies for selecting Gribov copies: "First instance" (choosing the first copy obtained in the first Gribov region) and "Smallest functional" (choosing the copy with the smallest functional value among all obtained copies). The measurement distortion was estimated by comparing light-quark spatial propagators and pion quasi-distribution matrix elements from the two strategies, with uncertainties evaluated via bootstrap resampling.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.06 fm, volume 48^3 x 64, valence pion mass m_pi = 300 MeV
- 100 gauge configurations measured, with 8 gauge-fixing instances per configuration
- Gauge-fixing precision set to delta F/F <= 10^-8
- Three time separations t_sep = {8, 10, 12}a measured for three-point correlations
- Effective mass of quark propagator evaluated at z = 10a shows errors scaling as 1/sqrt(N) for both strategies
- Ratio of matrix elements from the two strategies shows consistency within 1 sigma at separations z = {-20, -10, 10, 20}a

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb gauge method for parton distributions proposed by Gao (2023) and Zhao (2023), which avoids Wilson lines and their associated signal-to-noise degradation. It addresses concerns about Gribov copy ambiguities raised by Gribov (1977) and Singer (1978), following studies of Gribov effects in gluon propagators by Maas (2008) and Bornyakov (2011), and in quark propagators by Burgio (2012) and Kalusche (2024). The results enable confident application of the Coulomb gauge approach to lattice calculations of PDFs and TMDs within the LaMET framework.
