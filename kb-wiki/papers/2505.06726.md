# Pseudoscalar Meson Parton Distributions Within Gauge-Invariant Nonlocal Chiral Quark Model

> In this paper, I investigate the gluon distributions for the kaon and pion, as well as the improvement of the valence-quark distributions, in the framework of the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model (NL$χ$QM), where the momentum dependence is taken into account. I then compute the gluon distributions for the kaon and pion that are dynamically generated from the splitting functions in the DGLAP QCD evolution. In a comparison with the recent lattice QCD and JAM global analysis results, it is found that the results for the pion gluon distributions at $Q =$ 2 GeV, which is set based on the lattice QCD, have a good agreement with the recent lattice QCD data; this is followed up with the up valence-quark distribution of the pion results at $Q =$ 5.2 GeV in comparison with the reanalysis experimental data. The prediction for the kaon gluon distributions at $Q = 2$ GeV is consistent with the recent lattice QCD calculation.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model (NLχQM), which incorporates momentum-dependent quark masses, produces pion gluon distributions at Q = 2 GeV that agree with recent lattice QCD data and kaon gluon distributions at the same scale that are consistent with lattice QCD calculations, but only when the nonlocal (derivative) interaction terms arising from gauge invariance are included alongside the local contributions.

## Method

The author computed valence-quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the pion and kaon from the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model (NLχQM) using a three-point functional derivative of the effective chiral action with respect to meson fields and a vector field. The gluon distributions were then dynamically generated by evolving the valence-quark PDFs from an initial scale Q₀ = 0.42 GeV to higher scales using next-to-leading-order (NLO) Dokshitzer–Gribov–Lipatov–Altarelli–Parisi (DGLAP) QCD evolution, where the gluon distribution arises from the singlet quark evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- Pion up valence-quark PDF at Q = 5.2 GeV carries momentum fraction 2⟨x⟩_π = 0.41; the nonlocal contribution carries 0.11 and the local contribution carries 0.31
- Pion up valence-quark PDF at Q = 5.2 GeV has large-x power-law behavior (1−x)^(1.85) when fit over x = [0.8, 1.0]
- Gluon carries momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_g^π = 0.62 relative to total pion momentum at Q = 5.2 GeV
- Pion gluon PDF at Q = 5.2 GeV has large-x power-law behavior (1−x)^(3.06)
- Kaon gluon PDF at Q = 5.2 GeV has large-x power-law behavior (1−x)^(3.17)
- Kaon up valence-quark PDF at Q = 5.2 GeV has power-law behavior (1−x)^(2.30); kaon antistrange valence-quark PDF has (1−x)^(1.92)
- Model parameters: constituent quark mass M₀ = 300 MeV, model renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV, pion weak-decay constant F_π = 93.2 MeV, kaon weak-decay constant F_K = 113.4 MeV, current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of meson PDFs within the NLχQM framework (Hutauruk 2023) and contrasts with the Bethe-Salpeter equation Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (BSE-NJL) model approach (Hutauruk 2021), where the BSE-NJL model without momentum dependence agrees with older experimental data (Conway 1989) while the NLχQM with momentum dependence agrees with reanalysis data (Aicher 2010). The results provide predictions for gluon distributions that can be tested by future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC), and COMPASS++/AMBER at CERN, which are needed to resolve the current controversy on the large-x behavior of pion PDFs.
