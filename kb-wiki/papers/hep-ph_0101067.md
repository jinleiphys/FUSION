# A Diagrammatic Approach to Crystalline Color Superconductivity

> We present a derivation of the gap equation for the crystalline color superconducting phase of QCD which begins from a one-loop Schwinger-Dyson equation written using a Nambu-Gorkov propagator modified to describe the spatially varying condensate. Some aspects of previous variational calculations become more straightforward when rephrased beginning from a diagrammatic starting point. This derivation also provides a natural base from which to generalize the analysis to include quark masses, nontrivial crystal structures, gluon propagation at asymptotic densities, and nonzero temperature. In this paper, we analyze the effects of nonzero temperature on the crystalline color superconducting phase.

## Key claim

The paper presents a diagrammatic derivation of the gap equation for crystalline color superconductivity that reproduces the earlier variational result of Bowers et al. (2001) once antiparticle contributions are eliminated, and extends the analysis to nonzero temperature. The derivation is more straightforward than the variational approach and provides a natural framework for generalizations to quark masses, nontrivial crystal structures, and gluon propagation.

## Method

The authors begin from a one-loop Schwinger-Dyson equation written using a Nambu-Gorkov propagator modified to describe a spatially varying condensate of Cooper pairs with total momentum 2q. They replace the gluon propagator with a point-like four-fermion interaction having the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange, then perform a projection onto particle and antiparticle states using projectors P_+ and P_-. After eliminating antiparticle contributions, they evaluate the p_0 integral by contour integration, which naturally reproduces the blocking regions that had to be imposed by hand in the variational approach of Bowers et al. (2001).

## Key numbers

- The BCS state persists while δμ < δμ₁ = Δ₀/√2 in the weak coupling limit
- Crystalline color superconductivity is favored over no pairing for δμ < δμ₂ ≈ 0.754 Δ₀ in the weak coupling limit with no interaction in J=1 channels
- The critical temperature T_c above which the crystalline condensate vanishes satisfies T_c → 0.39Δ as δμ → δμ₂
- The center-of-mass momentum of each Cooper pair satisfies |q| ≈ 1.2 δμ
- Near δμ₂, the gap parameter scales as Δ ∼ (δμ₂ − δμ)^{1/2}

## Context

This work extends the variational analysis of crystalline color superconductivity by Bowers et al. (2001) by providing a diagrammatic derivation that makes the blocking region structure emerge naturally from contour integration rather than requiring an explicit variational ansatz. The formalism enables future generalizations to include quark masses (relevant for the transition from the color-flavor locked phase studied by Alford et al. (2000) and others), nontrivial crystal structures beyond the single plane-wave condensate, and gluon propagation at asymptotic densities as pioneered by Son (1999).
