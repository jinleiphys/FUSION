# A chiral quark model for meson electro-production in the region of D-wave resonances

> The meson scattering and electroproduction amplitudes in the D13, D33 and D15 partial waves are calculated in a coupled-channel formalism incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states, extending our previous studies of the P11, P33 and S11 partial waves. The vertices of the baryon-meson interaction including the s- and d-wave pions and $ρ$-mesons, the s-wave $η$-meson, and the $s$- and p-wave $σ$-mesons are determined in the Cloudy Bag Model, with some changes of the parameters to reproduce the widths of the resonances. The helicity amplitudes and the electroproduction amplitudes exhibit consistent behavior in all channels but tend to be too weak compared to the experiment. We discuss possible origins of this discrepancy which arises also in the constituent quark model calculations.

# A chiral quark model for meson electro-production in the region of D-wave resonances

## Key claim

The authors extend their coupled-channel quark-model approach to D-wave resonances (D13, D33, D15 partial waves) and find that while the calculated helicity amplitudes and electroproduction amplitudes show consistent behavior across all channels, they tend to be too weak compared to experiment, a discrepancy that also arises in constituent quark model calculations and may originate from inadequate description of the peripheral part of the resonance wave-function.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel formalism incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states, where the vertices of baryon-meson interaction (including s- and d-wave pions and ρ-mesons, s-wave η-mesons, and s- and p-wave σ-mesons) are determined in the Cloudy Bag Model. The K-matrix approach is used to compute scattering and electroproduction amplitudes, with the T-matrix obtained by solving the Heitler equation. The quark-model parameters are adjusted to reproduce the widths of the resonances, using renormalization factors for d-wave and s-wave couplings while preserving p-wave couplings.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius R = 0.83 fm (standard) and R = 1.0 fm (preferred for D13); also tested R = 1.4 fm
- For N(1520)D13 at R = 1.0 fm: d-wave coupling increased by 43% relative to quark-model value to reproduce experimental width Γ = 115 MeV; s-wave πΔ coupling reduced to 58% of quark-model value
- For Δ(1700)D33 at R = 0.83 fm: d-wave pion coupling multiplied by factor 2.4 to give total width 288 MeV
- For N(1675)D15 at R = 1.0 fm: πN coupling increased by factor 2.25, πΔ, ηN and ρN couplings increased by factor 1.45 to reproduce experimental width 150 MeV
- Bare-to-pole mass differences: 200 MeV (D13), 240 MeV (D15), 500 MeV (D33)
- Branching fractions for N(1520)D13: πN 59%, πΔ(S) 23%, πΔ(D) 5%, ρN(S) 7%, σ 5% (PDG ranges: πN 55–65%, πΔ(S) 10–20%, πΔ(D) 10–15%, ρN 9±1%, σ <8%)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of P11, P33, and S11 partial waves (Golli et al., 2008, 2009, 2011) to D-wave resonances, using the same coupled-channel formalism with Cloudy Bag Model vertices. The results are compared to experimental data from Jefferson Lab, Mainz, and Bonn, as well as analyses from MAID (2007, 2008, 2011), SAID (Arndt et al., 2006), and the JLab-MSU model (Mokeev et al., 2012). The calculated helicity amplitudes show similar behavior to constituent quark model calculations (Aiello et al., 1998; Santopinto et al., 2012; Metsch et al., 2013), all of which tend to underestimate the experimental amplitudes. The work enables future improvements by suggesting that inclusion of f-wave quark excitations (particularly for N(1675)D15) and more elaborate treatment of the meson cloud (including γρπ and γωπ vertices as in the Dynamical Coupled Channel Approach of Julia-Diaz et al., 2008) may bring calculations into better agreement with experiment.
