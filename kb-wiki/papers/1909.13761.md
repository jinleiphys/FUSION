# Comment on the recent article "Fission dynamics of from saddle to scission and beyond" by Bulgac et al., published in Phys. Rev. {\bf C 100}, 034615 (2019)

> In this note we provide answers to criticisms made the appendix D of the article [Phys. Rev. C100, 034615 (2019)] against to the theory developed recently and known a "Stochastic Mean-Field" (SMF) approach and its applications. Briefly, we list our replies and leave the judgment to the readers.

# Comment on "Fission dynamics from saddle to scission and beyond" by Bulgac et al. (2019)

## Key claim

The Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach is not an exact formulation of the many-body problem but an approximate phase-space method that can treat beyond-mean-field effects in transport theories; the criticisms in Bulgac et al. (2019) are based either on erroneous physics arguments or on misunderstanding of the SMF approach itself.

## Method

The authors provide point-by-point rebuttals to four specific criticisms raised in Appendix D of Bulgac et al. (2019). They argue that: (1) initial fluctuations alone are consistent with Langevin dynamics, as demonstrated by the Caldeira-Leggett model and the Mori formalism; (2) event-by-event one-body densities in SMF should not be interpreted as quantum objects, only the ensemble average has physical meaning; (3) local density fluctuations diverge in SMF because the reference quantum fluctuations of a quasi-particle vacuum also diverge, and a coarse-graining procedure resolves this; (4) the SMF approach treats fluctuations of all collective degrees of freedom without imposing symmetry restrictions, unlike the constrained HFB method used by Bulgac et al. (2019).

## Key numbers

- The SMF approach in the small amplitude limit becomes equivalent to the Balian-Veneroni description for the dispersion of one-body observables (no numerical value given).
- Cross-section calculations for multi-nucleon transfer using SMF give very good description of data without any adjustable parameter (no specific numerical result reported).
- The application of SMF to spontaneous fission of an unspecified nucleus with truncation of the particle-hole space "works very well" (Tan17) (no numerical values provided in the text).

## Context

This comment responds to criticisms made in Bulgac et al. (2019, Phys. Rev. C 100, 034615) regarding the Stochastic Mean-Field approach developed by Ayik and Lacroix. The SMF approach extends earlier work by Ayik (2008) and Lacroix (2014) and has been validated against exact solutions in many situations (Lacroix 2012, 2013, 2014b). The authors note that a recently proposed fermionic-Truncated Wigner Approximation by Davidson (2017) has direct connection with SMF, providing an independent formulation. The comment clarifies that SMF is not intended to be an exact many-body method but a practical approximation for incorporating fluctuations beyond mean-field in low-energy nuclear dynamics, particularly for heavy-ion collisions and fission.
