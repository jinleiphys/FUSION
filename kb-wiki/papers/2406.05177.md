# Neutron-star seismology with realistic, finite-temperature nuclear matter

> The oscillation spectrum of a neutron star is notably rich and intrinsically dependent on the equation of state of nuclear matter. With recent advancements in gravitational-wave and electromagnetic astronomy, we are nearing the capability to perform neutron-star asteroseismology and probe the complex physics of neutron stars. With this in mind, we explore the implementation of three-parameter finite-temperature matter models in the computation of neutron-star oscillations. We consider in detail the thermodynamics of nuclear matter and show how this information enters the problem. Our realistic treatment takes into account entropy and composition gradients that exist in the nuclear matter, giving rise to buoyant g-mode oscillations. To illustrate the implementation, we determine the oscillation spectrum of a low-temperature neutron star. In addition to the expected compositional and thermal g-modes, we find perturbations sourced by phase transitions in the equation of state. We also examine two thermal models, comparing the results for constant redshifted temperature with those for uniform entropy per baryon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that implementing a three-parameter finite-temperature equation of state (with temperature, baryon-number density, and electron fraction as independent variables) is necessary to correctly model neutron-star oscillations, because the barotropic assumption (energy density as a function of pressure only) is insufficient for dynamical contexts where composition and entropy gradients give rise to buoyant g-modes.

## Method

The authors used the finite-temperature APR equation of state from the CompOSE library, which provides pressure, entropy per baryon, deviation from beta equilibrium, and energy density on a grid of temperature, baryon-number density, and electron fraction. They interpolated these quantities using splines to obtain partial derivatives, then computed the adiabatic index Gamma_1 and the barotropic index Gamma from the thermodynamic derivatives. The oscillation modes were calculated by solving the relativistic perturbation equations of Detweiler and Lindblom (1985) for a non-rotating, perfect-fluid neutron star, with the Lagrangian perturbations assuming frozen composition (Delta Y_e = 0) and no heat transfer (Delta s = 0).

## Key numbers

- The equation-of-state grid covers temperatures from 0.00999 MeV to 251 MeV, baryon-number densities from 5.43e-13 per femtometer cubed to 6.32 per femtometer cubed, and electron fractions from 0.005 to 0.655.
- The paper identifies compositional and thermal g-modes, plus interfacial i-modes from phase transitions, in a low-temperature neutron star; specific oscillation frequencies are not reported in the truncated text.
- The comparison between two thermal models (constant redshifted temperature versus uniform entropy per baryon) is performed, but numerical values for mode frequencies are not given in the available text.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of neutron-star g-modes at zero temperature by Jin (2021), Camelio (2021), Zhao (2022), and Chirenti (2025), and moves beyond phenomenological thermal-pressure models used by Kruger (2015) and Loffredo (2022). The authors also note that their numerical implementation was used to support a study on thermal effects in merger simulations by Gittins (2025). The paper enables future asteroseismology efforts by providing a framework to compute oscillation spectra from realistic, finite-temperature nuclear matter, which is relevant for interpreting gravitational-wave observations from instruments like Cosmic Explorer and the Einstein Telescope.
