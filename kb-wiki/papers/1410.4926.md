# Effects of pion potential and nuclear symmetry energy on the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies around the pion production threshold

> Within the framework of the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck(IBUU) transport model, we studied the effects of the pion potential and the symmetry energy on the pion production in the central $^{197}Au+^{197}Au$ collisions around the pion production threshold. It is found that the pion potential has opposite effects on the value of $π^-/π^+$ ratio at low and high pion energies. The effect of the pion potential on the total $π^-/π^+$ ratio becomes larger in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below the pion production threshold. And at beam energies below the pion production threshold, with the pion potential, the effect of the symmetry energy on the $π^-/π^+$ ratio becomes smaller compared with that above the pion production threshold.

## Key claim

The pion potential has opposite effects on the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio at low and high pion energies, and at beam energies below the pion production threshold the effect of the symmetry energy on the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio becomes smaller when the pion potential is included, compared with the case above threshold.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central $^{197}Au+^{197}Au$ collisions at beam energies of 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon. They implemented a density- and momentum-dependent pion potential (including both s-wave and p-wave contributions) and compared results with and without this potential for two parameterizations of the symmetry energy (softer with x=0 and stiffer with x=-1).

## Key numbers

- At $E_{beam}$ = 400 MeV/nucleon, $\pi$ mesons in the compression stage are mainly located around p = 58 MeV/c and p = 215 MeV/c.
- At $E_{beam}$ = 200 MeV/nucleon, $\pi$ mesons in the compression stage are mainly located around p = 51 MeV/c.
- The pion potential used for the momentum-independent (MID) case uses parameters $\gamma$ = 2 and $S_{int0}$ = 20 MeV.
- The momentum-dependent (MD) pion potential uses three momentum regimes: $p_{\pi} <$ 80 MeV, $p_{\pi} >$ 140 MeV, and an interpolating spline for 80 MeV $\leq p_{\pi} \leq$ 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li (2002) who first proposed the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio as a sensitive observable for the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, and by Jun Hong et al. (2014) who constructed an isospin-dependent pion potential. The study addresses the contradictory conclusions from different transport models interpreting FOPI pion data regarding the stiffness of the symmetry energy. The results are relevant for experiments at NSCL/MSU (beam energies below 150 MeV/nucleon) and RIKEN/Japan (200-350 MeV/nucleon), and enable more accurate extraction of symmetry energy information from pion ratios at beam energies near the pion production threshold.
