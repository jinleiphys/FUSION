# The eta Photoproduction of Nucleons and The structure of the Resonance S11(1535) in The Quark Model

> In this paper, we present our study on the $η$ photoproduction based on the chiral quark model. We find that quark model provides a very good description of the $η$ production with much less parameters, and the threshold region is not a reliable source to determine the $ηNN$ coupling constant due to its strong dependence on the properties of the resonance $S_{11}(1535)$. We suggest that the systematic data in $E_{lab}=1.2 \sim 1.4$ GeV region may help us to determine the $ηNN$ coupling constant more precisely. The structure of the resonance $S_{11}(1535)$ is discussed, we find that the recent data from Mainz group bring the helicity amplitude much closer to the quark model prediction. However, more studies need to be done to understand the large $ηN$ branching ratio of the resonance $S_{11}(1535)$. Our results show that the quark model is a very good approach to study the underlying structure of baryon resonances from the meson photoproduction data.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model provides a very good description of eta photoproduction with far fewer parameters than traditional hadronic models, but the threshold region is not a reliable source to determine the eta-NN coupling constant because the extracted value depends strongly on the properties of the S11(1535) resonance.

## Method

The authors applied the chiral quark model to eta photoproduction on nucleons, treating pseudoscalar mesons as Goldstone bosons that interact directly with quarks inside baryons. They performed three separate fits to differential cross section data: Fit 1 assumed SU(6) x O(3) symmetry for baryon wavefunctions with only one free parameter (the eta-NN coupling constant); Fit 2 introduced configuration mixing parameters for the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances plus the S11(1535) decay width as additional free parameters; Fit 3 concentrated specifically on the recent Mainz data in the threshold region with three parameters.

## Key numbers

- The eta-NN coupling constant alpha_eta was found to be 0.465 in Fit 1 (SU(6) symmetry limit), but reduced to approximately 0.14 in Fit 2 when configuration mixing was included, showing strong dependence on resonance properties.
- The total decay width of S11(1535) was extracted as 0.111 GeV in Fit 2 (using Bates data) and 0.198 GeV in Fit 3 (using Mainz data), with the latter in very good agreement with a simple Breit-Wigner fit.
- The quantity xi, which characterizes the S11(1535) resonance structure, was computed as 0.186 GeV^-1 (Fit 1), 0.208 GeV^-1 (Fit 2), and 0.220 GeV^-1 (Fit 3), in good agreement with the RPI group result of 0.22 +/- 0.02 GeV^-1.
- The quark mass was set to 0.34 GeV and the harmonic oscillator parameter alpha^2 to 0.16 GeV^2, both standard values in the quark model.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model framework previously developed by Li (1995) for kaon photoproduction to the eta photoproduction channel. It contrasts with traditional Breit-Wigner parametrizations and coupled-channel isobar models used by Bennhold and collaborators, as well as the effective Lagrangian approach of the RPI group (Mukhopadhyay et al.), by requiring far fewer free parameters. The results enable future studies of baryon resonance structure directly from photoproduction data, particularly for the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances, and highlight the need for systematic data in the E_lab = 1.15 to 1.45 GeV region to better determine the eta-NN coupling constant.
