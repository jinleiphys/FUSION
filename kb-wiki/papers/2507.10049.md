# Composition of scalar mesons and their effects on nuclear matter properties in an extended linear sigma model

> It has been argued that the iso-scalar and iso-vector mesons play significant roles in nuclear matter and neutron star structures. We improve the extended linear sigma model with baryons, proposed in our previous work, by introducing the flavor structures constructed from antisymmetric tensors of chiral representations to study these physics. The parameter space of this model is refined with well-reproduced nuclear matter properties at saturation density by the lowest order Lagrangian, ensuring consistency with vacuum results, such as $f_π\approx 134 \, \text{MeV}$. The anticipated plateau-like behaviors of the symmetry energy are predicted at intermediate densities, which is crucial for the consistency of GW170817 and the neutron skin thickness of $\text{Pb}^{208}$. Subsequently, neutron star structures are calculated using several parameter sets, and the results for the nuclear matter properties at saturation density align with empirical values. It is found that the neutron star structures are sensitive to the couplings between the iso-vector $a_0$ meson and nucleons and the four-vector meson couplings: small values of both are favorable. Meanwhile, nuclear matter properties at saturation density favor larger values of the latter and are not sensitive to the former. This signifies the statistical significance of neutron star observations when obtaining realistic chiral effective field theories or models at various densities. The parameter set favored by neutron star observations also aligns the behavior of the sound velocity with the conformal limit at high densities relevant to cores of massive stars. It is hoped that the results of this work can guide future studies on the relationship between the microscopic symmetry of strong interactions and macroscopic phenomena.

## Key claim
The improved extended linear sigma model with baryons (bELSM) predicts that neutron star structures are sensitive to the couplings between the isovector \(a_0\) meson and nucleons and the four-vector meson couplings, with small values of both being favorable for consistency with observations, while nuclear matter properties at saturation density favor larger values of the latter and are not sensitive to the former.

## Method
The authors extended their previously proposed baryonic extended linear sigma model by introducing flavor structures constructed from antisymmetric tensors of chiral representations, including operators with three-rank Levi-Civita tensors in flavor space that were not present in the original model. The parameter space was refined by fitting to vacuum hadron spectra (nucleon and meson masses) and nuclear matter properties at saturation density using the lowest-order Lagrangian under the relativistic mean field approximation. Neutron star mass-radius relations were then computed using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation with the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density fixed at \(n_0 = 0.155\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) across all parameter sets
- Binding energy at saturation: \(E_0 = -14.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(-15.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) depending on parameter set (empirical: \(-16.0 \pm 1.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\))
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(n_0) = 30.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(31.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (empirical: \(31.7 \pm 3.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\))
- Incompressibility \(K(n_0)\) ranges from 253 MeV (el-g3150eg) to 419 MeV (el-g30e); empirical value is \(250 \pm 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Pion decay constant from the favored parameter set: \(f_\pi \approx 134\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Nucleon mass reproduced as \(m_N = 939\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for all parameter sets
- \(\sigma\) meson mass ranges from 485 MeV to 525 MeV across parameter sets
- \(a_0\) meson mass ranges from 983 MeV to 994 MeV across parameter sets
- \(\rho\) meson mass: 763 MeV; \(\omega\) meson mass: 783 MeV for all parameter sets
- Two-quark component of \(\sigma\) meson: 45.8% to 51.1%; four-quark component: 48.9% to 54.2%
- Two-quark component of \(a_0\) meson: 18.5% to 25.7%; four-quark component: 74.3% to 81.5%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous baryonic extended linear sigma model (Ma, 2023) by including additional flavor structures that split the couplings of singlet and octet vector mesons to baryons, removing the need for two-quark terms that were necessary in the original model. The predicted plateau-like behavior of the symmetry energy at intermediate densities (around \(2n_0\)) is consistent with analyses from Zabari (2018), Miyatsu (2022), and Li (2022), and satisfies constraints from the neutron skin thickness of \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) (PREX collaboration, 2021) and tidal deformations from GW170817 (LIGO Scientific, 2018). The results enable future studies connecting the microscopic chiral symmetry of strong interactions to macroscopic neutron star phenomena, and the authors note that the parameter space can be further improved via statistical analysis including neutron star observations, as discussed in their past work (Guo, 2023).
