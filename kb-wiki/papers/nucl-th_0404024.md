# Nucleon pole contributions in $J/ψ\to N \bar{N} π$, $p \bar{p} η$, $p \bar{p} η^{\prime}$ and $p \bar{p} ω$ decays

> Nucleon pole contributions in $J/ψ\to N \bar N π$, $p \bar p η$, $p \bar p η^{\prime}$ and $p \bar{p} ω$ decays are re-studied. Different contributions due to PS-PS and PS-PV couplings in the $π$-N interaction and the effects of $NNπ$ form factors are investigated in the $J/ψ\to N \bar N π$ decay channel. It is found that when the ratio of $|F_0| /|F_M|$ takes small value, without considering the $NNπ$ form factor, the difference between PS-PS and PS-PV couplings are negligible. However, when the $NNπ$ form factor is included, this difference is greatly enlarged. The resultant decay widths are sensitive to the form factors. As a conclusion, the nucleon-pole contribution as a background is important in the $J/ψ\to N\bar{N}π$ decay and must be accounted. In the $J/ψ\to N\bar{N}η$ and $N\bar{N}η'$ decays, its contribution is less than 0.1% of the data. In the $J/ψ\to N\bar{N}ω$ decay, it provides rather important contribution without considering form factors. But the contribution is suppressed greatly when adding the off-shell form factors. Comparing these results with data would help us to select a proper form factor for such kind of decay.

## Key claim

The nucleon-pole contribution to the branching ratio of \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \pi^0\) is comparable to the experimental data only when no hadronic form factor is applied; once off-shell form factors are included, the contribution is strongly suppressed and becomes much smaller than the data, meaning the nucleon-pole background is important but its size depends critically on the choice of form factor and cutoff parameter.

## Method

The authors calculated the decay amplitudes for nucleon-pole diagrams in \(J/\psi \to N \bar{N} \pi\), \(p \bar{p} \eta\), \(p \bar{p} \eta'\), and \(p \bar{p} \omega\) using both pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PS-PS) and pseudoscalar-pseudovector (PS-PV) couplings for the meson-nucleon vertex. They introduced five different phenomenological form factors (monopole, dipole, exponential, and two others) at the meson-nucleon-nucleon vertices and varied the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) from 0.65 GeV to 2.0 GeV to study the sensitivity of the decay widths. The \(J/\psi \to N \bar{N}\) vertex was parameterized by two form factors \(F_M\) and \(F_0\), whose ratio was extracted from the angular distribution of \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p}\) data.

## Key numbers

* For \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \pi^0\) without form factors, the branching ratio relative to \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p}\) is 0.556 for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 0\) and 0.815 for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 1.04\) in the PS-PS coupling case; in the PS-PV case it is 0.556 for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 0\) and 0.421 for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 1.04\).
* With the monopole form factor \(F_1\) and \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV, the branching ratio for \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \pi^0\) is 3.95% (PS-PS) and 2.79% (PS-PV); with \(\Lambda = 2.0\) GeV it rises to 19.35% (PS-PS) and 15.89% (PS-PV).
* Using form factors with parameters from the literature (\(\Lambda = 1350\) MeV for \(F_1\), 1116.6 MeV for \(F_4\), 1000 MeV for \(F_5\)) and \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 0.12\), the branching ratios are 0.0808, 0.0465, and 0.0967 respectively.
* For \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \eta\) without form factors, the branching ratio is \(5.48 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 0\) and \(8.19 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 1.04\) in PS-PS coupling; the experimental ratio is \(0.98 \pm 0.09\).
* For \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \eta'\) without form factors, the branching ratio is \(1.93 \times 10^{-5}\) for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 0\) and \(2.91 \times 10^{-5}\) for \(|F_0|/|F_M| = 1.04\) in PS-PS coupling.
* The ratio of PS-PV to PS-PS branching ratios for \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \pi^0\) with \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV is 0.74 for \(F_1\), 0.618 for \(F_2\), 0.606 for \(F_3\), 0.621 for \(F_4\), and 0.905 for \(F_5\); without form factors this ratio is 0.940.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Sinha and Okubo (year not given in text) who studied nucleon-pole contributions in \(J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \pi^0\) and related decays, and it provides a systematic study of form-factor effects that were not fully explored previously. The results show that the nucleon-pole background must be accounted for when extracting information about nucleon resonances (\(N^*\)) from \(J/\psi \to N \bar{N} M\) decays, and that comparing these calculations with data from BEPC and other facilities can help select a proper form factor for such processes. The work enables more reliable extraction of \(N^*\) contributions by quantifying the size and shape of the nucleon-pole background under different form-factor assumptions.
