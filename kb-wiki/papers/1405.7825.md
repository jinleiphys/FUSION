# Non-Abelian Bremsstrahlung and Azimuthal Asymmetries in High Energy p+A Reactions

> We apply the GLV reaction operator solution to the Vitev-Gunion-Bertsch (VGB) boundary conditions to compute the all-order in nuclear opacity non-abelian gluon bremsstrahlung of event-by-event fluctuating beam jets in nuclear collisions. We evaluate analytically azimuthal Fourier moments of single gluon, $v_n^M\{1\}$, and even number $2\ell$ gluon, $v_n^M\{2\ell\}$ inclusive distributions in high energy p+A reactions as a function of harmonic $n$, %independent target recoil cluster number, $M$, and gluon number, $2\ell$, at RHIC and LHC. Multiple resolved clusters of recoiling target beam jets together with the projectile beam jet form Color Scintillation Antenna (CSA) arrays that lead to characteristic boost non-invariant trapezoidal rapidity distributions in asymmetric $B+A$ nuclear collisions. The scaling of intrinsically azimuthally anisotropic and long range in $η$ nature of the non-abelian \br leads to $v_n$ moments that are similar to results from hydrodynamic models, but due entirely to non-abelian wave interference phenomena sourced by the fluctuating CSA. Our analytic non-flow solutions are similar to recent numerical saturation model predictions but differ by predicting a simple power-law hierarchy of both even and odd $v_n$ without invoking $k_T$ factorization. A test of CSA mechanism is the predicted nearly linear $η$ rapidity dependence of the $v_n(k_T,η)$. Non-abelian beam jet \br may thus provide a simple analytic solution to Beam Energy Scan (BES) puzzle of the near $\sqrt{s}$ independence of $v_n(p_T)$ moments observed down to 10 AGeV where large $x$ valence quark beam jets dominate inelastic dynamics. Recoil \br from multiple independent CSA clusters could also provide a partial explanation for the unexpected similarity of $v_n$ in $p(D)+A$ and non-central $A+A$ at same $dN/dη$ multiplicity as observed at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that azimuthal anisotropy moments ($v_n$) observed in high-energy p+A and peripheral A+A collisions can be explained entirely by non-abelian wave interference phenomena from fluctuating Color Scintillation Antenna (CSA) arrays, without invoking hydrodynamic flow or final-state interactions, and that this mechanism predicts a simple power-law hierarchy of both even and odd $v_n$ without requiring $k_T$ factorization.

## Method

The authors apply the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) reaction operator solution to the Vitev-Gunion-Bertsch (VGB) boundary conditions to compute all-order-in-nuclear-opacity non-abelian gluon bremsstrahlung from event-by-event fluctuating beam jets in p+A collisions. They evaluate analytically the azimuthal Fourier moments of single-gluon ($v_n^M\{1\}$) and even-number $2\ell$ gluon ($v_n^M\{2\ell\}$) inclusive distributions as functions of harmonic $n$, target recoil cluster number $M$, and gluon number $2\ell$, using a linked cluster theorem that factorizes the multiple scattering into a probability distribution for total accumulated momentum transfer ${\bf Q}$.

## Key numbers

* The cold nuclear matter screening scale is taken as $\mu^2 \approx 0.12$ GeV$^2$, from fits to forward dihadron correlations.
* In the $\mu=0$ limit, the single GB antenna azimuthal moments scale as $v_n^{GB}(k,q,0) = (k/q)^n \; \theta(q-k)$, reaching unity at $k=q$ and vanishing for $k>q$.
* For finite $\mu > 0$, all moments maximize at $k^2 = k_*^2 = q^2 + \mu^2$ with $v_n(k_*) = (\sqrt{1+\mu^2/q^2} - \mu/q)^n$.
* The power-law scaling $[v_n^{GB}(k,q,0)]^{1/n} = [v_m^{GB}(k,q,0)]^{1/m}$ holds for $k/M < 1$ but breaks down at higher $k > M$ when ensemble averaged over $q^2$.

## Context

This work extends the Gunion-Bertsch (1981) first-order-in-opacity perturbative QCD bremsstrahlung formula to all orders in opacity via the Vitev (2007) generalization, providing an alternative to hydrodynamic interpretations of azimuthal anisotropies in small systems. The results are similar to recent numerical saturation model predictions by Dusling and collaborators (2013) but differ by predicting a simple power-law hierarchy without $k_T$ factorization. The mechanism offers a potential explanation for the Beam Energy Scan puzzle of near $\sqrt{s}$ independence of $v_n(p_T)$ moments observed down to 10 AGeV by the STAR collaboration (2013), and for the unexpected similarity of $v_n$ in p(D)+A and non-central A+A at the same $dN/d\eta$ multiplicity observed at RHIC and LHC.
