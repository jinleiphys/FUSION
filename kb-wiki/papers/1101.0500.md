# Domain Growth in Chiral Phase Transitions

> We investigate the kinetics of chiral phase transitions in quark matter. We discuss the phase diagram of this system in both a microscopic framework (using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and a phenomenological framework (using a Landau free energy). Then, we study the far-from-equilibrium coarsening dynamics subsequent to a quench from the chirally-symmetric phase to the massive quark phase. Depending on the nature of the quench, the system evolves via either {\it spinodal decomposition} or {\it nucleation and growth}. The morphology of the ordering system is characterized using the order-parameter correlation function, structure factor, domain growth laws, etc.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the late-stage coarsening dynamics of chiral phase transitions in quark matter, focusing on universal properties such as growth laws and scaling of correlation functions, which are robust features that depend only on general dynamical universality class rather than system-specific details.

## Method

The authors derive a time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two light flavors, using a variational approach with thermo-field dynamics to incorporate temperature and chemical potential effects. They map the NJL phase diagram onto a $\psi^6$-Landau potential and numerically solve the TDGL equation to study domain growth following quenches from the chirally-symmetric phase to the massive quark phase, characterizing morphology through order-parameter correlation functions, structure factors, and domain growth laws.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point in the NJL phase diagram is located at $(\mu_\text{tcp}, T_\text{tcp}) \simeq (282.58, 78.0)$ MeV
- The ultraviolet cut-off is set to $\Lambda = 653.3$ MeV and the four-fermion coupling to $G = 5.0163 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$
- At $\mu = 0$ and $T = 0$, the constituent quark mass is $M \simeq 312$ MeV
- The first-order transition at $T=0$ occurs at $\mu_1 \simeq 326.321$ MeV
- Chiral symmetry is restored at $\mu=0$ for $T \simeq 190$ MeV
- For the Landau potential coefficients at $(\mu,T) = (311.00, 10)$ MeV: $a/\Lambda^2 = -1.306 \times 10^{-3}$, $b = 0.092$, $d\Lambda^2 = 0.439$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics in quark matter by Sasaki et al., Scavenius et al., Fraga and Krein, Bessa et al., Skokov and Voskresensky, and Randrup, which focused primarily on early-time kinetics, linearized regimes, or nucleation rates. The paper is complementary to those studies by investigating the late-stage universal scaling properties of coarsening morphology in chiral transitions, and provides a quantitative mapping between the NJL model and a $\psi^6$-Landau potential that enables identification of relevant time and length scales for chiral transition kinetics.
