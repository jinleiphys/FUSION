# Is X(3872) {\sl Really} a Molecular State?

> After taking into account both the pion and sigma meson exchange potential, we have performed a dynamical calculation of the $D^0\bar{D}^{\ast0}$ system. The $σ$ meson exchange potential is repulsive from heavy quark symmetry and numerically important for a loosely bound system. Our analysis disfavors the interpretation of X(3872) as a loosely bound molecular state if we use the experimental $D^\ast Dπ$ coupling constant $g=0.59$ and a reasonable cutoff around 1 GeV, which is the typical hadronic scale. Bound state solutions with negative eigenvalues for the $D\bar{D}^\ast$ system exist only with either a very large coupling constant (two times of the experimental value) or a large cutoff ($Λ\sim 6$ GeV or $β\sim 6$ GeV$^2$). In contrast, there probably exists a loosely bound S-wave $B\bar{B}^\ast$ molecular state. Once produced, such a molecular state would be rather stable since its dominant decay mode is the radiative decay through $B^\ast\to B γ$. Experimental search of these states will be very interesting.

## Key claim

The paper argues that X(3872) is unlikely to be a loosely bound molecular state of \(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\) when using the experimentally measured \(D^* D\pi\) coupling constant \(g=0.59\) and a reasonable cutoff around 1 GeV, because bound state solutions require either a coupling constant twice the experimental value or a cutoff an order of magnitude larger than the typical hadronic scale.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical calculation of the \(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\) system by solving the radial Schrodinger equation with a potential derived from both one-pion exchange and sigma meson exchange, using effective chiral Lagrangians in the heavy quark and chiral limits. They regulated the short-distance behavior of the potential using two independent approaches: introducing a monopole form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda\), and applying a Gaussian smearing technique with parameter \(\beta\). The coupling constant \(g=0.59\pm0.07\pm0.01\) was taken from experimental \(D^*\) decay width fits, and the sigma coupling \(g_\sigma=0.76\) was derived from heavy quark symmetry relations.

## Key numbers

- The experimental \(D^* D\pi\) coupling constant used is \(g=0.59\pm0.07\pm0.01\), extracted from the precise experimental width of \(D^*\).
- With one-pion exchange alone and \(g=0.59\), bound state solutions with binding energy between -0.1 and -5.0 MeV require \(\Lambda > 5.6\) GeV (form factor case) or \(\beta > 5.3\) GeV\(^2\) (smearing case), far above the reasonable cutoff of \(\sim 1\) GeV.
- With both pion and sigma exchange included and \(g=0.59\), the required cutoff increases to \(\Lambda \sim 6.0\) GeV or \(\beta \sim 6.0\) GeV\(^2\) for binding energies in the same range, and the binding energy decreases by at least 83% compared to the pion-only case.
- For the \(B\bar{B}^*\) system with \(g=0.59\), bound state solutions exist with \(\Lambda \sim 2.3-2.4\) GeV, a much more reasonable cutoff than for the \(D\bar{D}^*\) case.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical study of the molecular interpretation of X(3872) initiated by Swanson (2004) and Wong (2004), who found bound state solutions using one-pion exchange plus short-range quark-gluon forces, and contradicts the conclusion of Suzuki (2005) that X(3872) is not a molecular state. The paper introduces sigma meson exchange as an additional repulsive contribution, motivated by the analogy with nuclear forces where intermediate-range attraction from sigma exchange is crucial for binding the deuteron. The results disfavor the molecular interpretation for X(3872) but predict a possible loosely bound S-wave \(B\bar{B}^*\) molecular state that would decay radiatively through \(B^* \to B\gamma\), providing a target for future experimental searches.
