# The phase transition in QCD with broken SU(2) flavour symmetry

> We report the first investigation of the QCD transition temperature, T_c, for two flavours of staggered quarks with unequal masses at lattice spacings of 1/4T. On changing the u/d quark mass ratio in such a way that m(pi_0)^2/m(pi_+)^2 changes from 1 to 0.78, thus bracketing the physical value of this ratio, we find that T_c remains unchanged in units of both m_rho and Lambda_MSbar.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD investigation of the transition temperature \(T_c\) for two flavours of staggered quarks with unequal \(u\) and \(d\) quark masses, finding that \(T_c\) remains unchanged in units of both \(m_\rho\) and \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\) when the \(u/d\) mass ratio is varied such that \(m_{\pi^0}^2/m_{\pi^+}^2\) changes from 1 to 0.78, which brackets the physical value of this ratio. The authors note that a significant downward shift in \(\beta_c\) is seen only when the ratio \(m_d/m_u\) is made as large as 10, indicating that realistic vector SU(2) flavour symmetry breaking produces a negligible shift in \(T_c\) at the level of accuracy reached.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of QCD with 1+1 flavours of staggered quarks on \(4 \times 8^3\) lattices at several bare average quark masses \(a\bar{m} = (m_u + m_d)/2\) (0.025, 0.05, 0.1, and 0.15) for both degenerate (\(m_d/m_u = 1\)) and non-degenerate (\(m_d/m_u = 2\) and 10) cases. They identified the critical coupling \(\beta_c\) from peaks in the Wilson-line susceptibility \(\chi_L\), the chiral susceptibility \(\chi_F\), and the integrated autocorrelation time \(\tau\), and then measured zero-temperature meson masses on \(8^4\) lattices at each \(\beta_c\) to extract \(T_c/m_\rho\) and \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\).

## Key numbers

- For \(m_d = 2m_u\) at \(a\bar{m} = 0.05\): \(m_{\pi^0}^2/m_{\pi^+}^2 = 0.78(2)\), \(T_c/m_\rho = 0.194(7)\), \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 1.2(2)\)
- For \(m_d = 2m_u\) at \(a\bar{m} = 0.10\): \(m_{\pi^0}^2/m_{\pi^+}^2 = 0.76(1)\), \(T_c/m_\rho = 0.177(6)\), \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 1.3(2)\)
- For \(m_d = 2m_u\) at \(a\bar{m} = 0.15\): \(m_{\pi^0}^2/m_{\pi^+}^2 = 0.72(1)\), \(T_c/m_\rho = 0.172(4)\), \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 1.3(2)\)
- Extrapolated to physical quark masses: \(T_c = 175 \pm 6\) MeV from \(T_c/m_\rho\) and \(T_c = 167 \pm 9^{+15}_{-14}\) MeV from \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\)
- The physical value of \(m_{\pi^0}^2/m_{\pi^+}^2\) is 0.935, which lies within the range 1 to 0.78 studied

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the QCD transition temperature with two degenerate flavours of staggered quarks (Gottlieb et al., 1997; Fukugita et al., 1990) by introducing a 1+1 flavour prescription that allows unequal \(u\) and \(d\) quark masses. The results confirm that the transition temperature is insensitive to realistic SU(2) flavour symmetry breaking, and the extracted \(T_c\) values are compatible with estimates using improved actions. The study leaves open the question of the order of the phase transition, which requires a finite-size scaling analysis, and the extrapolation to zero quark mass and continuum limit.
