# Azimuthal anisotropy in U+U and Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> Collisions between prolate uranium nuclei are used to study how particle production and azimuthal anisotropies depend on initial geometry in heavy-ion collisions. We report the two- and four-particle cumulants, $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$, for charged hadrons from U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Nearly fully overlapping collisions are selected based on the amount of energy deposited by spectators in the STAR Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs). Within this sample, the observed dependence of $v_2\{2\}$ on multiplicity demonstrates that ZDC information combined with multiplicity can preferentially select different overlap configurations in U+U collisions. An initial-state model with gluon saturation describes the slope of $v_2\{2\}$ as a function of multiplicity in central collisions better than one based on Glauber with a two-component multiplicity model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in nearly fully overlapping U+U collisions (impact parameter approximately 0 fm), the anti-correlation between elliptic flow v2 and charged-particle multiplicity can be used to select different geometric configurations (tip-tip versus body-body) of the prolate uranium nuclei, but only if the ZDC selection is stringent enough (top 0.125% most central) to suppress contamination from non-central collisions.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured two- and four-particle cumulants of elliptic flow (v2{2} and v2{4}) for charged hadrons in U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using the STAR Time Projection Chamber within |eta| < 1 and 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c. Nearly fully overlapping collisions were selected by requiring minimal energy deposition in the Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs), and the dependence of v2 on multiplicity was compared to three initial-state models: a Glauber model with two-component multiplicity (Glauber-x_hard), a constituent-quark Glauber model (Glauber-CQ), and the IP-Glasma model based on gluon saturation.

## Key numbers

- U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Approximately 307 million U+U events including 7 million specially triggered central events
- ZDC resolution determined to be 23 +/- 2% from the single neutron peak
- v3{2} in the 0.125% most central collisions: (1.410 +/- 0.006) x 10^-2 for U+U and (1.380 +/- 0.008) x 10^-2 for Au+Au (statistical errors only)
- Slope of v3 vs multiplicity was approximately -0.005 +/- 0.002 in both systems
- NBD parameters for Glauber-CQ model: n=0.76, k=0.34 for |eta|<0.5 and n=2.9, k=0.86 for |eta|<1
- Constituent quark cross-section sigma_qq = 9.36 mb used in Glauber-CQ model

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptic flow fluctuations at RHIC by STAR (Agakishiev 2012) and predictions for U+U collisions by Voloshin (2010) and Heinz and Kuhlman (2005). The results directly contradict the Glauber-x_hard model (Kharzeev and Nardi 2001; Miller et al. 2007) which predicted a "knee" structure in v2 versus multiplicity for central U+U collisions that is not observed. The findings support models with sub-nucleonic structure and coherence, specifically the IP-Glasma model (Schenke, Tribedy, and Venugopalan 2012, 2014) based on gluon saturation (McLerran and Venugopalan 1994) and the constituent-quark Glauber model (Eremin and Voloshin 2003; PHENIX Collaboration 2014). The demonstrated ability to select tip-tip or body-body enriched event samples enables future studies of path-length dependence of jet-quenching and tests of the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2006; Voloshin 2010).
