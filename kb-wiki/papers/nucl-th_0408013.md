# Nuclear matter properties with the re-evaluated coefficients of liquid drop model

> The coefficients of the volume, surface, coulomb, asymmetry and pairing energy terms of the semiempirical liquid drop model mass formula have been determined by furnishing best fit to the observed mass excesses. Slightly different sets of the weighting parameters for liquid drop model mass formula have been obtained from minimizations of chisquare and mean square deviation. The most recent experimental and estimated mass excesses from Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass table have been used for the least square fitting procedure. Equation of state, nuclear incompressibility, nuclear mean free path and the most stable nuclei for corresponding atomic numbers, all are in good agreement with the experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper re-evaluates the coefficients of the Bethe-Weizsäcker liquid drop mass formula by fitting to the latest atomic mass excesses, but the abstract's claim of "good agreement" with experimental results is qualified by the fact that the fits produce slightly different coefficient sets depending on whether the minimization targets the mean square deviation (sigma-squared) or the chi-squared per degree of freedom, and these two quantities cannot be minimized simultaneously with the same set of parameters.

## Method

The authors performed five-parameter least-squares fits of the Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula to 3179 atomic mass excesses (2228 experimentally measured plus 951 extrapolated values) from the Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass table, minimizing either the mean square deviation sigma-squared or the chi-squared per degree of freedom. They then used the extracted volume coefficient a_v as the saturation energy per nucleon for spin- and isospin-symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter, combined with a density-dependent M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, to compute the nuclear matter equation of state, incompressibility, and mean free path.

## Key numbers

- Volume coefficient a_v = 15.260 ± 0.020 MeV (from sigma-squared minimization using all 3179 masses)
- Surface coefficient a_s = 16.267 ± 0.062 MeV (same fit)
- Coulomb coefficient a_c = 0.689 ± 0.001 MeV (same fit)
- Asymmetry coefficient a_sym = 22.209 ± 0.048 MeV (same fit)
- Pairing coefficient a_p = 10.076 ± 0.854 MeV (same fit)
- Mean square deviation sigma-squared = 15.15 (same fit)
- Volume coefficient a_v = 15.777 ± 0.053 MeV (from chi-squared minimization using all 3179 masses)
- Coulomb radius constant r_0 = 1.254 fm (from a_c = 0.689 MeV) and r_0 = 1.217 fm (from a_c = 0.710 MeV)
- Nuclear incompressibility K_0 = 293.4 MeV (using a_v = 15.260 MeV and saturation density 0.1533 fm^{-3})
- Nuclear incompressibility K_0 = 301.1 MeV (using a_v = 15.777 MeV and same saturation density)
- Nuclear mean free path lambda = 3.9 fm (using beta = 1.668 fm^2 and density 0.1533 fm^{-3})
- Velocity of sound becomes superluminal at density rho >= 5.3 rho_0

## Context

This work extends the long line of Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula coefficient determinations (Bethe 1936, Weizsäcker 1935) by using the most recent Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass evaluation and by systematically comparing sigma-squared versus chi-squared minimization. The extracted volume coefficients are then used as input to a density-dependent M3Y effective interaction to compute nuclear matter properties, yielding an incompressibility of 290-300 MeV that agrees with experimental giant monopole resonance data (Youngblood 1999) and hard-photon heavy-ion collision measurements (Garg 2002). The results enable improved predictions for nuclear binding energies, reaction Q-values, and the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, and the authors note that their incompressibility is close to other theoretical estimates from the INM model and relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory.
