# Excited nuclei in neutron star crusts

> The paper considers the chains of successive electron capture reactions by nuclei of the iron group which take place in the crystal structures of neutron star envelopes. It is shown that as a result of such reactions the daughter nuclei in excited states accumulate within certain layers of neutron star crusts. The phonon model of interactions is proposed between the excited nuclei in the crystalline structure, as well as formation of highly excited nuclear states which emit neutrons and higher energy photons.

# Excited nuclei in neutron star crusts

## Key claim
The paper proposes that in overdense crystalline layers of neutron star crusts, daughter nuclei produced by electron capture reactions accumulate in excited states because the compressed lattice prevents normal gamma-ray emission; instead, nonlinear phonon-mediated interactions between excited nuclei and the crystal lattice can lead to highly excited nuclear states that emit neutrons and high-energy photons.

## Method
The authors analyze chains of successive electron capture reactions for stable iron-group isotopes (⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁶Fe, ⁵⁷Fe, ⁵⁸Fe, ⁵⁸Ni) using known threshold energies and nuclear level schemes. They compare the lattice spacing in the overdense Coulomb crystal (for densities between 10⁶ and 10¹² g·cm⁻³) with the wavelengths of gamma rays from excited daughter nuclei, and develop a phonon model describing the conversion of nuclear excitations into crystal lattice phonons and back, enabling energy transfer between neighboring excited nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Threshold density for electron capture by ⁵⁴Fe: ρₜₕ ≈ 1.555×10⁶ g·cm⁻³
- Excited state energies of ⁵⁴Cr: E*₁ = 0.835 MeV and E*₂ = 1.824 MeV
- Threshold density for third reaction in ⁵⁴Fe chain: ρₜₕ₃ ≈ 5.06×10⁹ g·cm⁻³
- Threshold density for first reaction in ⁵⁶Fe chain: ρ ≥ 7.155×10⁹ g·cm⁻³
- Excited state energies of ⁵⁶Cr: E*₁ = 1.007 MeV and E*₂ = 1.832 MeV
- Lattice parameter at threshold density of first ⁵⁴Fe reaction: d ≈ 6.7×10³ fm
- For ρ ≥ 7.155×10⁹ g·cm⁻³, distance between nuclei d ≤ 288 fm and wave number K ≥ 2.18×10¹¹ cm⁻¹, corresponding to gamma energy of 4.3 MeV
- Threshold density for fifth reaction in ⁵⁶Fe chain: ρₜₕ₅ ≈ 3.697×10¹⁰ g·cm⁻³

## Context
This work extends studies of electron capture in neutron star crusts (Takibayev, various years) by focusing on the fate of excited daughter nuclei in the crystalline lattice, rather than assuming immediate de-excitation. The phonon model draws on nuclear collective excitation theory (Takibayev, SVG) and solid-state concepts of excitons and polaritons (BK, KL). The paper suggests that free neutrons may appear in the outer crust at densities as low as ρ ≥ 7.155×10⁹ g·cm⁻³, earlier than the traditional neutron-drip boundary, which could enable resonant neutron-nucleus interactions and enrich the reaction dynamics in neutron star envelopes.
