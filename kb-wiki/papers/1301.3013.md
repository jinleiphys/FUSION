# Symmetries of the Three Heavy-Quark System and the Color-Singlet Static Energy at NNLL

> We study the symmetries of the three heavy-quark system under exchange of the quark fields within the effective field theory framework of potential non-relativistic QCD. The symmetries constrain the form of the matching coefficients in the effective theory. We then focus on the color-singlet sector and determine the so far unknown leading ultrasoft contribution to the static potential, which is of order alpha_s^4 ln(μ), and consequently to the static energy, which is of order alpha_s^4 ln(alpha_s). Finally, in the case of an equilateral geometry, we solve the renormalization group equations and resum the leading ultrasoft logarithms for the static potential of three quarks in a color singlet, octet and decuplet representation.

## Key claim

The paper determines the previously unknown leading ultrasoft contribution to the static potential (of order \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \mu\)) and static energy (of order \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \alpha_s\)) for the three-heavy-quark color-singlet system, but only under the condition that the three quarks are in an equilateral geometry when solving the renormalization group equations to resum logarithms.

## Method

The authors work within potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD), an effective field theory derived from NRQCD by integrating out soft gluons. They construct the three-quark composite fields linked by Wilson strings to the center of mass, decompose them into singlet, two octet, and decuplet color representations, and derive the pNRQCD Lagrangian including an octet mixing potential. They then impose symmetry under exchange of the heavy-quark fields to constrain the potentials, and compute the one-loop color-singlet self-energy from ultrasoft gluon exchange to extract the leading logarithmic divergence, which determines the \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \mu\) term in the static potential and the \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \alpha_s\) term in the static energy.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order singlet static potential is \(V^s = -\frac{2}{3}\alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|}+\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}+\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\) at order \(\alpha_s\).
* The leading-order decuplet static potential is \(V^d = \frac{1}{3}\alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|}+\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}+\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\) at order \(\alpha_s\).
* The leading-order antisymmetric octet potential is \(V^o_A = \alpha_s\left(-\frac{2}{3}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|}+\frac{1}{12}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}+\frac{1}{12}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\) at order \(\alpha_s\).
* The leading-order symmetric octet potential is \(V^o_S = \alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{3}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_1|}-\frac{5}{12}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}-\frac{5}{12}\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\) at order \(\alpha_s\).
* The leading-order octet mixing potential is \(V^o_{AS} = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\alpha_s\left(\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_2|}-\frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}_3|}\right)\) at order \(\alpha_s\).
* The ultrasoft contribution to the singlet static energy is of order \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \alpha_s\), and the corresponding contribution to the static potential is of order \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \mu\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of three-heavy-quark systems previously developed by Brambilla, Vairo, and collaborators (2005, 2009), who derived pNRQCD for \(QQQ\) systems and computed potentials up to next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order. The calculation of the \(\alpha_s^4 \ln \alpha_s\) term in the singlet static energy for three quarks is new, while analogous results for the \(Q\bar{Q}\) system were obtained more than ten years earlier by Brambilla, Pineda, Soto, and Vairo (1999). The symmetry analysis constrains the form of the octet potentials and shows that the octet mixing potential is essential for consistency. The results enable future resummation of ultrasoft logarithms in the three-quark static energy and provide perturbative predictions that can be compared with lattice computations of triple-heavy baryon systems.
