# Strong coupling isotropization of non-Abelian plasmas simplified

> We study the isotropization of a homogeneous, strongly coupled, non-Abelian plasma by means of its gravity dual. We compare the time evolution of a large number of initially anisotropic states as determined, on the one hand, by the full non-linear Einstein's equations and, on the other, by the Einstein's equations linearized around the final equilibrium state. The linear approximation works remarkably well even for states that exhibit large anisotropies. For example, it predicts with a 20% accuracy the isotropization time, which is of the order of t_iso \lesssim 1/T, with T the final equilibrium temperature. We comment on possible extensions to less symmetric situations.

## Key claim

The linear approximation (LA), solving Einstein's equations linearized around the final equilibrium black brane, predicts the isotropization time of a strongly coupled, homogeneous, non-Abelian plasma to within 20% accuracy, even for initial states with large anisotropies where the pressure anisotropy is an order of magnitude larger than the energy density. This accuracy holds despite the fact that the initial states are far from equilibrium and are not guaranteed a priori to be small perturbations of the final state.

## Method

The authors studied isotropization of a homogeneous, strongly coupled conformal field theory (CFT) plasma in flat Minkowski space using its gravity dual in the Poincaré patch of AdS₅. They specified around 1000 anisotropic initial states on an initial-time slice (ITS) by choosing a function B(t=0, r) (dual to the pressure anisotropy) and a constant energy density, then evolved each state both by solving the full non-linear Einstein's equations numerically and by solving the linearized equation for B around the static black brane solution. The time evolution of the CFT stress tensor was extracted from the near-boundary behavior of the metric.

## Key numbers

- The linear approximation predicts the isotropization time with 20% accuracy for most of the 1000 initial states considered.
- The isotropization time is of order t_iso ≲ 1/T, where T is the final equilibrium temperature.
- For the representative initial state shown, the pressure anisotropy ΔP/ε reaches almost an order of magnitude larger than the energy density during the evolution.
- The lowest quasinormal mode frequency has Im ω₀ ≃ 8.5 T; a naive estimate from this single mode gives T t_iso ∼ 1, while the actual isotropization times from the full evolution lie in the range 0.27 ≲ T t_iso ≲ 0.62.
- For states with ΔP/ε ≲ 1, the entropy increase is fairly small (≲ 10%).

## Context

This work extends the "close limit approximation" (CLA) from black hole mergers in four-dimensional general relativity (Price 1994, Anninos 1995) to the isotropization of strongly coupled plasmas via gauge/gravity duality. It contrasts with earlier numerical studies of far-from-equilibrium plasma evolution (Chesler 2008, Heller 2011, Bantilan 2012) by showing that linearization around the final equilibrium works surprisingly well even for large anisotropies. The results enable simplified modeling of isotropization using quasinormal mode expansions and suggest possible extensions to less symmetric situations such as boost-invariant expanding plasmas (Janik 2005).
