# Ising analogue to compact-star matter

> By constructing an Ising analogue of compact-star matter at sub-saturation density we explored the effect of Coulomb frustration on the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. Our conclusions is twofold. First, the range of temperatures where inhomogeneous phases form expands with increasing Coulomb-field strength. Second, within the approximation of uniform electron distribution, the limiting point upon which the phase-coexistence region ends does not exhibit any critical behaviour. Possible astrophysics consequences and thermodynamical connections are discussed.

## Key claim

The Coulomb field, in the presence of a uniform electron background, increases the limiting temperature for phase coexistence in compact-star matter, contrary to the decrease seen in finite nuclei or mean-field calculations; however, within the approximation of a uniform electron distribution, the limiting point does not exhibit critical behavior, meaning the correlation length remains finite.

## Method

The authors constructed a Lattice-Gas model on a cubic lattice with short-range (nuclear-like) and long-range (Coulomb-like) interactions, immersed in a uniform negative background representing the degenerate electron gas. They used a multi-canonical ensemble to treat the two energy components as independent observables with separate Lagrange multipliers, allowing them to explore the phase diagram for both neutral and charged matter. Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations were performed for various lattice sizes to extract the limiting temperature and to test finite-size scaling of the susceptibility.

## Key numbers

* The limiting temperature for phase coexistence increases by about 6% for a proton fraction x=1/3 when the Coulomb field is included.
* The short-range coupling constant was set to epsilon = -5.5 MeV.
* The long-range coupling constant was set to lambda*epsilon = alpha*hbar*c*rho_0^(1/3)*x^2, where rho_0 = 0.17 fm^(-1) is the nuclear saturation density and x=1/3 is the proton fraction.
* The critical temperature of normal symmetric nuclear matter is given as T_c approximately 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of Coulomb frustration in nuclear matter, originally described in condensed-matter physics, to the astrophysical context of neutron-star crusts and supernova matter. It contrasts with the behavior of finite atomic nuclei, where Coulomb repulsion reduces the limiting temperature (Bonche 1985, Gulminelli 2003), and with frustrated Ising ferromagnets (Grousson). The results are consistent with a recent RMF calculation by Maruyama (2005) that found a widening of the mixed-phase region when charge neutrality over Wigner-Seitz cells is enforced. The finding that the limiting point is not a critical point explains why molecular-dynamics simulations (Horowitz 2004) did not observe the expected increase in the static form factor, and suggests that warm stellar matter will show relatively small opacity to neutrino scattering.
