# Non-Markovian Collision Integral in Fermi-systems

> The non-Markovian collision integral is obtained on the base of the Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green functions in a form with allowance for small retardation effects. The collisional relaxation times and damping width of the giant isovector dipole resonances in nuclear matter are calculated. For an infinite Fermi liquid the dependence of the relaxation times on the collective vibration frequency and the temperature corresponds to the Landau's prescription.

# Non-Markovian Collision Integral in Fermi-systems

## Key claim
The authors derive a modified non-Markovian collision integral for the linearized Vlasov-Landau equation that, unlike prior derivations, correctly drives the distribution function toward local equilibrium in the limit of weak time-dependence. The resulting relaxation times for collective vibrations in nuclear matter depend quadratically on both frequency and temperature, consistent with Landau's prescription, but only for small retardation effects where the frequency is not too large.

## Method
Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green functions in the mixed Weyl-Wigner representation, the authors linearize the collision integral in the Born approximation for two-body scattering, assuming periodic time dependence of the nonequilibrium distribution function with real frequency. They modify the expression for the collision integral by replacing derivatives of delta functions with finite differences in energy shifts, yielding a form that depends only on the deviation from local equilibrium and on the occupation probability of 2p-2h states. The resulting collision integral is then used to compute collective relaxation times for isoscalar and isovector modes in two-component nuclear matter, and the damping width of the giant isovector dipole resonance (GDR) is calculated for finite temperatures.

## Key numbers
- The relaxation time formula yields $1/\tau^{(\pm)}_\ell \propto (\hbar\omega)^2 + (2\pi T)^2$ in the low-temperature, low-frequency limit ($T, \hbar\omega \ll \epsilon_F$).
- For isotropic scattering with free-space cross sections, the parameter $\widetilde{\alpha}^{(\pm)}_\ell = 4.18 / [c_\ell + 1.3 d^{(\pm)}_\ell]$ MeV, using adopted free-space cross sections $\sigma^{(f)}_{av} = 3.75$ fm² and $\sigma^{(f)}_{np} = 5$ fm² near Fermi energy.
- The GDR width calculations use $E_0 = 41 A^{-1/3}$ MeV, with calculations performed for $^{208}$Pb and $^{120}$Sn at a GDR energy of $\hbar\omega = 13.43$ MeV.
- In-medium cross sections from effective interactions are approximately 20% of free-space values for Skyrme forces and approximately 60% for Gogny interaction.
- The GDR width follows $\Gamma \propto (\hbar\omega)^2 + 4(\pi T)^2$ in the rare collision regime.

## Context
This work extends prior derivations of the non-Markovian collision integral from Kadanoff-Baym equations by KMP (1992) and KP (1994), correcting an inconsistency where those earlier forms did not reduce to the proper Landau-Vlasov equation in the static limit. The modified collision integral is consistent with the Landau prescription (Landau, 1957) for zero sound attenuation in Fermi liquids. The results enable calculation of collisional damping widths for giant resonances in nuclear matter, connecting to the relaxation time approach used by AB (1992), ABB (1995), and BAB (1995), and to the extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock framework of AS (1998). The temperature dependence of GDR widths is compared with experimental data for $^{208}$Pb and $^{120}$Sn from HBH (1994) and BR (1998).
