# Predicative Ability of QCD Sum Rules for Decuplet Baryons

> QCD sum rules for decuplet baryon two-point functions are investigated using a comprehensive Monte-Carlo based procedure. In this procedure, all uncertainties in the QCD input parameters are incorporated simultaneously, resulting in realistic estimates of the uncertainties in the extracted phenomenological parameters. Correlations between the QCD input parameters and the phenomenological parameters are studied by way of scatter plots. The predicted couplings are useful in evaluating matrix elements of decuplet baryons in the QCD sum rule approach. They are also used to check a cubic scaling law between baryon couplings and masses, as recently found by Dey and coworkers. The results show a significant reduction in the scaling constant and some possible deviations from the cubic law.

# Predicative Ability of QCD Sum Rules for Decuplet Baryons

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule method for decuplet baryon two-point functions, when analyzed with a comprehensive Monte-Carlo procedure that incorporates all QCD input parameter uncertainties simultaneously, yields current couplings with realistic error estimates; however, the chiral-odd sum rules are significantly more reliable than the chiral-even sum rules, and the previously claimed cubic scaling law between baryon couplings and masses (Dey and coworkers, 1997) shows a significant reduction in the scaling constant and possible deviations when using the new results.

## Method

The author re-derives QCD sum rules for all members of the decuplet family (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega) using operator-product-expansion (OPE) consistently including operators up to dimension 8, first-order strange quark mass corrections, flavor symmetry breaking of strange quark condensates, anomalous dimension corrections, and factorization violation of the four-quark condensate. A Monte-Carlo based procedure generates 1000 randomly-selected, Gaussianly-distributed sets of QCD input parameters, then applies chi-squared minimization to match the OPE side to a phenomenological representation within a Borel window determined by OPE convergence (highest-dimension operators contribute no more than 10%) and ground-state dominance (excited states contribute no more than 50%).

## Key numbers

- Delta chiral-odd sum rule: coupling squared = 2.26 ± 0.89 GeV^6, mass = 1.43 ± 0.12 GeV (experimental 1.232 GeV), continuum threshold = 1.65 ± 0.22 GeV
- Sigma* chiral-odd sum rule: coupling squared = 2.83 ± 0.32 GeV^6, mass = 1.394 ± 0.052 GeV (experimental 1.384 GeV), continuum threshold fixed at 1.80 GeV
- Xi* chiral-odd sum rule: coupling squared = 4.32 ± 0.47 GeV^6, mass = 1.505 ± 0.037 GeV (experimental 1.533 GeV), continuum threshold fixed at 2.00 GeV
- Omega chiral-odd sum rule: coupling squared = 7.19 ± 0.75 GeV^6, mass = 1.676 ± 0.031 GeV (experimental 1.672 GeV), continuum threshold fixed at 2.30 GeV
- Delta chiral-even sum rule (without alpha_s corrections): coupling squared = 4.13 ± 0.65 GeV^6, mass = 1.19 ± 0.13 GeV
- Omega chiral-even sum rule (without alpha_s corrections): coupling squared = 9.16 ± 0.43 GeV^6, mass = 1.61 ± 0.12 GeV
- Quark condensate parameter a = 0.52 ± 0.05 GeV^3, corresponding to <u-bar u> = -(236)^3 MeV^3
- Gluon condensate b = 1.2 ± 0.6 GeV^4
- Mixed condensate parameter m_0^2 = 0.72 ± 0.08 GeV^2
- Four-quark condensate violation parameter kappa_v = 2 ± 1
- Strange quark mass m_s = 0.15 ± 0.02 GeV
- Flavor symmetry breaking ratio f_s = 0.83 ± 0.05
- New cubic scaling law fit: lambda_B^2 = 0.71 M_B^6, compared to previous result of lambda_B^2 = 1.35 M_B^6 (Dey and coworkers, 1997)

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of decuplet baryons by Belyaev and Ioffe (1982, 1983), Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1982, 1985), Chung et al. (1984), and Leinweber (1990), which used relatively crude analysis methods and often claimed 10% or better accuracy without rigorous error analysis. The Monte-Carlo procedure follows the approach of Leinweber (1996) for the nucleon. The results enable more reliable evaluation of decuplet baryon matrix elements (magnetic moments, transition moments, axial charges, tensor charges) in the QCD sum rule approach, and provide a quantitative test of the cubic scaling law between baryon couplings and masses proposed by Dey and coworkers (1997), finding significant reduction in the scaling constant and possible deviations that call for re-examination of octet baryon sum rules and further investigations using other methods such as lattice QCD.
