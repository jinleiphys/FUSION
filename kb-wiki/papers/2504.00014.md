# Neutrino Theory in the Precision Era

> This document summarises discussions on future directions in theoretical neutrino physics, which are the outcome of a neutrino theory workshop held at CERN in February 2025. The starting point is the realisation that neutrino physics offers unique opportunities to address some of the most fundamental questions in physics. This motivates a vigorous experimental programme which the theory community fully supports. \textbf{A strong effort in theoretical neutrino physics is paramount to optimally take advantage of upcoming neutrino experiments and to explore the synergies with other areas of particle, astroparticle, and nuclear physics, as well as cosmology.} Progress on the theory side has the potential to significantly boost the physics reach of experiments, as well as go well beyond their original scope. Strong collaboration between theory and experiment is essential in the precision era. To foster such collaboration, \textbf{we propose to establish a CERN Neutrino Physics Centre.} Taking inspiration from the highly successful LHC Physics Center at Fermilab, the CERN Neutrino Physics Centre would be the European hub of the neutrino community, covering experimental and theoretical activities.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a strong, coordinated theoretical neutrino physics effort is paramount to fully exploit upcoming experimental data, and proposes the establishment of a CERN Neutrino Physics Centre as a European hub to foster theory-experiment collaboration. The hedge is that this theoretical effort is presented as necessary to "optimally take advantage" of experiments and to "go well beyond their original scope," rather than claiming that experiments cannot succeed without it.

## Method
The document is the outcome of a neutrino theory workshop (Neutrino Platform Pheno Week 2025) held at CERN in February 2025, where participants discussed future directions in theoretical neutrino physics. The paper synthesizes these discussions into a set of priority research areas and a concrete institutional proposal, drawing on the collective expertise of over 80 authors from institutions worldwide.

## Key numbers
- The paper notes that supernova neutrino densities exceed \(10^{30}\,\text{cm}^{-3}\), which drives highly non-linear flavour evolution.
- Accelerator-based neutrino experiments operate in the energy range from hundreds of MeV to tens of GeV, with distinct interaction regimes: deep-inelastic scattering above several GeV, resonance excitation around 1-3 GeV, and quasi-elastic scattering at lower energies.
- Neutrino telescopes are sensitive at energies from TeV to EeV, probing kinematic regions where parton distribution functions have never been measured.
- The paper states that if the neutrino mass ordering is inverted, the two largest neutrino masses are quasi-degenerate at the "few percent level."

## Context
This white paper extends the discussions from previous Neutrino Platform Pheno Weeks and serves as input to the European Strategy for Particle Physics 2025 Update. It builds on the recognition that neutrino physics has matured into a precision field, following the discovery of neutrino oscillations at the end of the twentieth century. The proposal for a CERN Neutrino Physics Centre is explicitly inspired by the LHC Physics Center at Fermilab, which has been highly successful in fostering theory-experiment collaboration. The document aims to enable the European community to capitalize on investments in upcoming experiments like DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, and JUNO, and to maintain a strong particle physics programme before a new collider becomes available.
