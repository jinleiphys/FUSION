# String Breaking, Baryons, Medium, and Gauge/String Duality

> The string breaking phenomenon in QCD can be studied using the gauge/string duality. In this approach, one can make estimates of some of the string breaking distances at non-zero temperature and baryon chemical potential. These point towards the enhancement of baryon production in strong decays of heavy mesons in dense baryonic medium.

## Key claim

Using gauge/string duality, the author estimates string breaking distances for heavy meson decays at non-zero temperature and baryon chemical potential, finding that the meson-baryon decay mode (Q\bar Q → Qqq + \bar Q q) becomes energetically more favorable as baryon density increases, potentially even dominating over the standard meson mode in dense baryonic medium.

## Method

The author models QCD string breaking using a five-dimensional charged black hole geometry in asymptotically AdS space with a deformation parameter, a tachyon field for light quarks at string endpoints, and a baryon vertex from a five-brane. String breaking distances are defined by equating the free energy of a connected string configuration between two static heavy quarks with the free energies of disconnected configurations representing meson pairs, baryon-meson pairs, or baryon-antibaryon pairs, computed from Nambu-Goto strings, baryon vertex actions, and tachyon boundary terms. The free parameters are fixed using lattice QCD results at zero temperature and chemical potential (set L) or phenomenological quarkonium data (set P).

## Key numbers

- String breaking distance for the meson mode at zero temperature and chemical potential: ℓ_c^(m) = 1.22 fm (set L, fitted to lattice data from Bulava et al.)
- String breaking distance for the baryon mode at zero temperature and chemical potential: ℓ_c^(b) = 2.35 fm (set L)
- Pseudocritical temperature: T_pc = 132 MeV (set L)
- Pseudocritical chemical potential: μ_pc = 538 MeV (set L)
- String breaking distance for the meson mode at zero temperature and chemical potential: ℓ_c^(m) = 1.07 fm (set P, fitted to phenomenological data)
- String breaking distance for the baryon mode at zero temperature and chemical potential: ℓ_c^(b) = 1.99 fm (set P)
- Pseudocritical temperature: T_pc = 76 MeV (set P)
- Pseudocritical chemical potential: μ_pc = 621 MeV (set P)
- String breaking distance for the baryon mode ℓ_c^(b) varies by less than 5% across the allowed range of the parameter κ (from 2.23 fm to 2.35 fm)

## Context

This work extends the gauge/string duality approach to string breaking previously applied at zero temperature and chemical potential (Andreev, 2015) to include finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, building on the model of Andreev and Zakharov for heavy quark potentials. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD simulations (Bulava et al., 2019; Bali et al., 2005) that are limited to zero chemical potential, and suggest that baryon production from heavy meson decays is enhanced in dense baryonic matter, which could be tested by future lattice calculations at non-zero baryon density or by heavy-ion collision experiments.
