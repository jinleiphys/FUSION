# Neutron stars and the transition to color-superconducting quark matter

> We explore the relevance of color superconductivity inside a possible quark matter core for the bulk properties of neutron stars. For the quark phase we use an Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model, extended to include diquark condensates. For the hadronic phase, a microscopic many-body model is adopted, with and without strangeness content. In our calculations, a sharp boundary is assumed between the hadronic and the quark phases. For NJL model parameters fitted to vacuum properties we find that no star with a pure quark core does exist. Nevertheless the presence of color superconducting phases can lower the neutron star maximum mass substantially. In some cases, the transition to quark matter occurs only if color superconductivity is present. Once the quark phase is introduced, the value of the maximum mass stays in any case below the value of two solar masses.

## Key claim
For NJL model parameters fitted to vacuum properties, no star with a pure quark core exists; the presence of color-superconducting phases can lower the neutron star maximum mass substantially, but the value of the maximum mass stays below two solar masses once the quark phase is introduced.

## Method
The hadronic phase was described using a Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with the Paris potential and Urbana three-body force, including cases with and without hyperons. The quark phase was modeled with a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type Lagrangian extended to include diquark condensates, and a sharp first-order boundary was assumed between the hadronic and quark phases. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was solved to obtain mass-radius relations for compact stars.

## Key numbers
- Maximum gravitational mass for hadronic EOS without hyperons (BHF with nucleons and leptons): 2.07 solar masses.
- Maximum mass for hadronic EOS without hyperons combined with NJL quark matter without color superconductivity: 1.97 solar masses.
- Maximum mass for hadronic EOS without hyperons combined with NJL quark matter with color superconductivity: 1.77 solar masses.
- Maximum mass for hadronic EOS with hyperons (BHF with nucleons, hyperons, leptons): 1.25 solar masses.
- Maximum mass for relativistic mean field EOS (G240) with hyperons combined with NJL quark matter with color superconductivity: 1.55 solar masses.
- Baryon chemical potential at the hadron-quark transition from BHF (nucleons, leptons) to CFL phase: 1312 MeV.
- Baryon density in the hadronic phase at that transition: 3.7 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}).
- Average diquark gap at that transition: 115 MeV.
- Effective bag constant at that transition: 208 MeV/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies using NJL models without diquark condensates (SLS99; SPL00) which found quarks exist at most in a small mixed phase regime. It contrasts with bag model results (Alford and Reddy, 2002) where stable hybrid stars with pure quark cores were found for a bag constant of 137 MeV/fm^3 and strange quark mass of 200 MeV. The findings call for a systematic survey of parameter dependence and suggest that observation of a neutron star with mass well above two solar masses would seriously question present views on the quark matter equation of state.
