# Shell Effects in Nuclei Near Neutron Drip

> Shell effects in nuclei close to the neutron-drip lines have been investigated. It has been demonstrated in the relativistic mean-field theory that nuclei very far from stability manifest the shell effects strongly. This behaviour is in accord with the predictions of nuclear masses in the finite-range droplet model including shell corrections. As a consequence we find a large neutron halo in nuclei near the neutron-drip line. The shell effects predicted in the existing Skyrme mean-field theory in comparison are significantly weaker than those of the other approaches.

# Shell Effects in Nuclei Near Neutron Drip

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory predicts strong shell effects at the neutron-drip line for the chain of Zr isotopes, in striking agreement with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM), while the Skyrme mean-field approach (using SkM*) shows significantly weaker shell effects at the same closed shell (N=82). The authors argue that this difference arises from the density-dependent spin-orbit coupling inherent to the RMF theory, which is absent in the Skyrme ansatz.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree calculations using the NL-SH force for even-even Zr isotopes from A=110 to A=136, with pairing added in the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps. They compared the resulting ground-state binding energies, quadrupole deformations, and single-particle spectra against predictions from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) and from spherical Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkM* interaction.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies from RMF (NL-SH) and FRDM agree within 1-2 MeV for A=110 to A=128, and within 2-3 MeV beyond A=130.
- The shell closure occurs at A=122 (N=82), which lies close to the neutron-drip line.
- The force NL1 gives binding energies that differ from NL-SH and FRDM by up to 15 MeV, attributed to its large asymmetry energy of 44 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splittings 1h9/2-1h11/2 and 1g7/2-1g9/2 are systematically larger in the Skyrme approach than in RMF by 30% and 20%, respectively; the 2d3/2-2d5/2 splitting for SkM* is about 2 times larger than for NL-SH.

## Context

This work extends the application of RMF theory to nuclei far from stability, specifically near the neutron-drip line, and directly compares with the phenomenological FRDM of Moller et al. (1992) and with Skyrme mean-field calculations using SkM* from Bartel et al. (1982). The results contradict the findings of a later study (ref. [16] in the paper) which claimed quenching of shell effects in both RMF and Skyrme theories for isobaric chains near A=100; the authors attribute this discrepancy to the absence of a shell closure in that chain. The strong shell effects predicted here are relevant for understanding r-process abundance peaks at A approximately 80, 130, and 195, and for predicting large neutron halos in nuclei near the drip line.
