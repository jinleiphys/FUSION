# Dissecting Jet Modification in the QGP with Multi-Point Energy Correlators

> Energy correlators have recently attracted significant attention in the study of heavy ion collisions due to their potential to robustly connect experimental measurements with an underlying quantum field theoretic description. While theoretical studies have so far primarily focused on the simplest two-point correlator, mapping out the dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) will require developing a theoretical understanding of multi-point energy correlators. In this paper we present a systematic theoretical study of multi-point energy correlators for jets fragmenting in a dense quark-gluon plasma, accounting for both the medium's perturbative modification to the jet, and its hydrodynamical back-reaction. We consider both the scaling behavior of projected correlators, as well as the shape dependent three-point correlator, highlighting how both provide insight into interactions with the QGP. We discuss the parametric dependence of modifications on the medium scales, opening new opportunities to experimentally separate jet modifications from the medium response. Our results open the door to a systematic exploration of multi-point energy correlators in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic theoretical study of multi-point energy correlators for jets in a quark-gluon plasma, showing that both perturbative medium modifications and hydrodynamic back-reaction imprint onto the correlators with distinct parametric dependencies on medium scales, potentially allowing experimental separation of these effects. The authors hedge that their calculation assumes a static and homogeneous QGP, and that the exact functional form of the classical energy flux does not play an important role, with the response rather determined by the magnitude of energy propagating in the matter and its characteristic angular region.

## Method

The authors compute projected energy correlators (PENCs) and the full shape-dependent three-point energy correlator (E3C) for jets fragmenting in a dense QGP, using a factorized cascade approximation for the three-body splitting functions. They account for perturbative medium modifications by replacing vacuum splitting functions with medium-induced ones that depend on the critical gluon frequency and characteristic angle, and model the hydrodynamic back-reaction using a two-parameter classical energy flux with Gaussian angular profile. The results are expanded in the collinear limit using the light-ray operator product expansion and celestial block decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The medium-induced splitting functions depend on two parameters: the critical gluon frequency ω_c ∝ q̂ L² and the characteristic angle θ_c⁻² ∝ q̂ L³, where L is the medium longitudinal size and q̂ is the jet quenching transport coefficient
- For the PE3C/E2C ratio including medium response, the authors use parameters: θ₀ = 0.025, p_t = 100 GeV, θ_cut = Λ_QCD/p_t = 0.002, Δ = 0.01 p_t, a^(P(3))_(2,0) = 0.286, fixing α_s = 0.3
- The cascade approximation deviates from the exact solution by 30% to 50% in flattened or squeezed configurations of the three detectors
- The fit of the E3C/E2C ratio at R_L = 0.2 to celestial block structure yields R² = 0.887, with numerator coefficients for i > 1 approximately an order of magnitude larger than denominator coefficients

## Context

This work extends the study of energy correlators in heavy ion collisions beyond the two-point correlator, which was the focus of earlier theoretical work by Andres (2022) and experimental measurements by CMS (2024) and ALICE (2024). The authors build on the light-ray operator product expansion framework initiated by Andres (2024) and the hydrodynamic treatment of energy correlators by Barata (2024). The results enable a systematic program, analogous to cosmology, of mapping out higher-point correlator shapes to distinguish different properties of QCD matter produced in heavy ion collisions, and provide a theory-driven approach to separate perturbative jet modifications from medium response effects.
