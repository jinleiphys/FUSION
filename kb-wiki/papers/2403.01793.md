# Isospin-forbidden electric dipole transition of the 9.64 MeV state of $^{12}$C

> The electric dipole transition of the 3$^-$ state at 9.64 MeV of $^{12}$C to the 2$^+$ state at 4.44 MeV is speculated to play a key role in the triple-$α$ reaction at high temperatures. A theoretical prediction of its transition width is a challenge to nuclear theory because it belongs to a class of isospin-forbidden transitions. We extend a microscopic 3$α$ cluster-model to include isospin 1 impurity components, and take into account both isovector and isoscalar electirc dipole operators. Several sets of $2^+$ and $3^-$ wave functions are generated by solving a radius-constrained equation of motion with the stochastic variational method, resulting in reproducing very well the electric quadrupole and octupole transition probabilities to the ground state. The electric dipole transition width is found to be 7--31 meV, 16 meV on the average, and more than half of the width is contributed by the isospin mixing of $α$ particles.

## Key claim

The electric dipole transition width of the 9.64 MeV 3⁻ state of ¹²C to the 2⁺ state at 4.44 MeV is predicted to be 7–31 meV (average 16 meV), which is consistent with the upper limit of 19 meV but considerably larger than the 2 meV assumed in the NACRE compilation; more than half of the width arises from isospin mixing of α particles.

## Method

The authors extended a microscopic 3α cluster model to include isospin 1 impurity components in the α-particle wave functions, using a radius-constrained equation of motion with the stochastic variational method to generate wave functions for the 0⁺ ground state, the 2⁺ state, and the 3⁻ state. They constrained the wave functions by reproducing the experimental point proton radius of the ground state, the B(E2) value of the 2⁺ state, and the B(E3) value of the 3⁻ state, then evaluated the E1 transition matrix element using both the isoscalar E1 operator (beyond long-wavelength approximation) and the isovector E1 operator acting on the isospin-mixed components.

## Key numbers

- The E1 radiation width Γ_rad(3⁻ → 2⁺) ranges from 7 to 31 meV, with an average of 16 meV, across seven combinations of 2⁺ and 3⁻ wave functions.
- The "best" combination (λ₃ = 1.1 MeV·fm⁻², λ₂ = 0.2 MeV·fm⁻²) gives Γ_rad = 9.5 meV.
- Without isospin mixing (ε = 0), the average Γ_rad decreases to 4 meV.
- The ratio Γ_rad/Γ_total with Γ_total = 46 ± 3 keV is 0.35 × 10⁻⁶ on average.
- The isospin mixing parameter ε is set to −4.2 × 10⁻³.
- The B(E3) value for the 3⁻ → 0⁺ transition is 107 ± 14 e²·fm⁶; the B(E2) value for the 2⁺ → 0⁺ transition is 7.77 ± 0.43 e²·fm⁴.
- The point proton radius of the ground state is 2.33 fm.

## Context

This work addresses the role of the 9.64 MeV 3⁻ state in the triple-α reaction at high temperatures (T > 2 GK), relevant to supernovae and X-ray bursts. It extends earlier 3α cluster-model calculations by Uegaki, Kamimura, and Descouvemont (various years) by including isospin mixing and the isoscalar E1 operator beyond the long-wavelength approximation. The predicted width is consistent with the experimental upper limit from Ajzenberg-Selove (1990) and Kelley (2017), and falls within the error bars of the measurement by Tsumura (2021) (Γ_rad/Γ_total = 1.3⁺¹·²₋₁·₁ × 10⁻⁶), but is much smaller than the value reported by Cardella (6.4 ± 5.1 × 10⁻⁵). The result enables improved reaction-rate calculations for the triple-α process at high temperatures and suggests a similar approach could be applied to the isospin-forbidden E1 transition in ¹⁶O relevant to the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction.
