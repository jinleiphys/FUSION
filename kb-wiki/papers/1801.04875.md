# Multi Quark Production in p+A collisions: Quantum Interference Effects

> We consider forward inclusive production of several quarks in the high energy p-A collisions in the CGC formalism. For three particle production we provide a complete expression in terms of multipole scattering amplitudes on the nucleus and multi particle generalized TMD's of the proton. We then calculate all the terms that are not suppressed by the factor of the area in four particle production, and generalize this result up to terms of order $1/N_c^2$ for arbitrary number of produced particles. Our results include the contribution of quantum interference effects both in the final state radiation (HBT) and in the initial projectile wave function (Pauli blocking).

## Key claim
The paper provides complete expressions for inclusive multi-quark production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions within the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) formalism, explicitly including quantum interference effects from both final-state Hanbury-Brown–Twiss (HBT) correlations and initial-state Pauli blocking, which the abstract notes are included but does not quantify that the calculation is restricted to terms not suppressed by the target area and, for more than three quarks, only to contributions up to order \(1/N_c^2\).

## Method
The authors extend the hybrid CGC formalism to forward inclusive production of three, four, and arbitrarily many quarks. For three quarks, they derive the full cross-section expression in terms of multipole scattering amplitudes on the nucleus (dipole, quadrupole, sextupole Wilson-line correlators) and multi-particle generalized transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (GTMDs) of the proton, using a single-nucleon intermediate-state dominance approximation. For four and more quarks, they calculate only the terms that are not suppressed by the area of the projectile, retaining contributions down to order \(1/N_c^2\) and neglecting processes where quarks arise from splitting of scattered projectile gluons.

- For three-quark production, the leading term in \(N_c\) describes independent production; the subleading terms encode correlated production and are expressed via six coefficients \(\mathcal{A}_1\) through \(\mathcal{A}_6\) given in Eqs. (an), which depend on \(N_c\) and on averages of dipole, quadrupole, and sextupole Wilson-line correlators.
- The decomposition of the quadrupole amplitude in Eq. (qbar) separates the area-unsuppressed part \(\langle D(1,1')\rangle\langle D(2,2')\rangle + \langle D(1,2')\rangle\langle D(2,1')\rangle\) from the area-suppressed part \(\bar Q\).
- The sextupole decomposition in Eq. (xbar) contains three types of terms: pure dipole terms (leading in area), terms with reduced quadrupole \(\bar Q\) (suppressed by one power of area), and the term \(\bar X\) (suppressed by two powers of area).
- For identical quarks, the 3GTMD approximation in Eq. (3id) includes terms of order \(1/N_c\) and \(1/N_c^2\) relative to the leading product of three single-quark GTMDs.
- When quarks are interchanged in the GTMD, a factor of \(1/2\) per interchange is introduced due to spin averaging (e.g., \(T(\p_1,\p'_2)T(\p_2,\p'_1) \rightarrow \frac{1}{2} T(\p_1,\p'_2)T(\p_2,\p'_1)\)).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of two-quark production (Kovner and Rezaeian, 2017) to three and more quarks, building on the CGC hybrid formalism of Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian (2002) and the JIMWLK/BK evolution equations. It explicitly incorporates quantum interference effects (HBT and Pauli blocking) that were identified as parametrically leading for fundamental quarks in the two-quark case, complementing studies of initial-state correlations by Dumitru et al. (2011) and Dusling and Venugopalan (2012). The results enable future calculations of multi-particle correlations in p-A collisions, potentially relevant for understanding ridge correlations observed at the LHC, and the authors note that for \(n>3\) quarks the calculation is more appropriate for a light-nucleus projectile than for a proton, as corrections from gluon splitting may become significant.
