# Baryon and Antibaryon Production in Hadronic and Nuclear Interactions

> We introduce a new formulation of the diquark breaking mechanism which describes with no free parameters the huge nuclear stopping observed in central nucleus-nucleus collisions. Supplemented, in the dual parton model, with strings originating from diquark--antidiquark pairs from the nucleon sea, it gives a substantial increase of hyperon and antihyperon yields from $pPb$ to central $PbPb$ collisions. Compared to data, this increase is slightly underestimated for $Î›$'s and $Îž$'s but is five times too small for $Î©$'s. Introducing final state interactions, a reasonable description of all baryon and antibaryon yields is achieved.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a new formulation of the diquark breaking mechanism that describes nuclear stopping with no free parameters, assuming the diquark breaking probability is equal to one. However, the model significantly underestimates Omega and anti-Omega production in central PbPb collisions by a factor of five, requiring final state interactions to achieve a reasonable description of all baryon and antibaryon yields.

## Method
The authors implement the dual parton model (DPM) with a diquark breaking mechanism where the net baryon number (string junction) has equal probability of being in any inelastic collision, producing a slower baryon when it joins with sea quarks. They supplement this with strings originating from diquark-antidiquark pairs from the nucleon sea to describe baryon-antibaryon pair production. The resulting baryon densities are used as initial conditions for final state interactions via coupled channel equations involving reactions such as pi N to K Lambda, pi Lambda to K Xi, and pi Xi to K Omega, with a single averaged cross-section parameter of 0.14 fm.

## Key numbers
- Net baryon rapidity density at y* = 0 for central PbPb collisions: dN_DB^{Delta B}/dy = 1.87 x 10^{-1} for protons, 6.22 x 10^{-2} for Lambdas, 3.26 x 10^{-3} for Xi-, and 2.38 x 10^{-4} for Omegas
- Antibaryon rapidity density from sea component at y* = 0 for central PbPb: dN_sea^{anti-B}/dy = 4.36 x 10^{-3} for anti-protons, 2.44 x 10^{-3} for anti-Lambdas, 2.92 x 10^{-4} for anti-Xi+, and 2.91 x 10^{-5} for anti-Omegas
- Conventional DP component for net baryons at y* = 0: dN_DP^{Delta B}/dy = 6.90 x 10^{-2} for protons, 1.40 x 10^{-2} for Lambdas, zero for Xi- and Omegas
- Conventional string component for antibaryons at y* = 0: dN_string^{anti-B}/dy = 8.50 x 10^{-3} for anti-protons, 2.83 x 10^{-3} for anti-Lambdas, 1.65 x 10^{-4} for anti-Xi+, and 5.05 x 10^{-6} for anti-Omegas
- pp net baryon rapidity density at y* = 0: dN_pp^{Delta B}/dy = 3.60 x 10^{-2} for protons, 7.00 x 10^{-3} for Lambdas
- pp antibaryon rapidity density at y* = 0: dN_pp^{anti-B}/dy = 1.70 x 10^{-2} for anti-protons, 5.65 x 10^{-3} for anti-Lambdas, 3.30 x 10^{-4} for anti-Xi+, and 1.01 x 10^{-5} for anti-Omegas
- Central PbPb collisions: average number of participants n_A = 178, average number of collisions n = 858
- Strangeness suppression factor S/L = 0.3 with L = 0.435 and S = 0.13
- Final state interaction averaged cross-section <sigma> = 0.14 fm

## Context
This work extends the dual parton model approach of Capella et al. (1995) by introducing a new diquark breaking formulation that eliminates the need for a dynamical suppression parameter, addressing the lack of nucleon stopping in earlier DPM calculations. The paper builds on the diquark breaking mechanism of Kharzeev (1996) and the baryon stopping studies of Vance et al. (1998), and is motivated by the observed enhancement of strange baryons in central PbPb collisions at the SPS (NA49 and WA97 collaborations). The results enable future comparisons with LHC data, where a flat rapidity behavior of net baryon production has been proposed, and provide a baseline for distinguishing string-based explanations from quark-gluon plasma formation scenarios.
