# Universal features of QCD dynamics in hadrons and nuclei at high energies

> We discuss the empirical evidence for a universal Color Glass Condensate and outline prospects for further studies at future colliders. Some ramifications for initial conditions in heavy ion collisions are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that empirical evidence from HERA, RHIC, and fixed-target experiments supports the existence of a universal Color Glass Condensate (CGC) describing gluon dynamics at high energies, but notes that the saturation scale extracted from HERA data is only \(\leq 1\) GeV\(^2\) even at the lowest \(x\) values, and that geometrical scaling alone is not sufficient evidence of saturation effects.

## Method

The paper reviews phenomenological saturation/CGC models applied to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) data, including the GBW model, the impact parameter saturation (IPsat) model, and the b-CGC model, which express the virtual photon-proton cross-section in terms of a dipole cross-section. For nuclear collisions, the paper discusses classical Yang-Mills solutions with CGC initial conditions, \(k_\perp\)-factorization for gluon production, and next-to-leading order computations of particle production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale energy dependence from HERA data: \(Q_s^2(x) = \exp(\lambda Y)\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\lambda = 0.288\) and \(x_0 = 3 \times 10^{-4}\) (GBW model fit).
- Saturation scale in nuclei scales approximately as \(Q_{s,A}^2(x, b_{\text{med.}}) \approx Q_{s,p}^2(x, b_{\text{med.}}) \cdot (A/x)^{1/3}\), giving a factor of \(200^{1/3} \approx 6\) enhancement for gold relative to a proton at the same \(x\).
- Predicted charged particle multiplicity in central lead+lead collisions at the LHC: 1000–1400.
- Growth rate of maximally unstable modes in Glasma simulations: \(\exp\left(C\sqrt{\Lambda_s \tau}\right)\) with \(C \approx 0.425\).
- Saturation scale in the IPsat model at median impact parameters in the proton is \(\leq 1\) GeV\(^2\) at the lowest \(x\) values at HERA.

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the JIMWLK/BK evolution equations (Balitsky, 1996; Kovchegov, 1999) to interpret data from HERA, RHIC, and fixed-target experiments (E665, NMC). It enables future precision studies at the LHC and proposed electron-ion colliders (EIC, LHeC) to probe the crossover from linear to non-linear QCD dynamics, and to understand the formation and thermalization of the Glasma in heavy ion collisions.
