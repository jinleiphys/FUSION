# In-medium Properties of Hadrons -- Observables

> We first briefly review the theoretical basis for calculations of changes of hadronic properties in dense nuclear matter. These changes have usually been investigated by means of relativistic heavy-ion reactions. Here we discuss that observable consequences of such changes can also be seen in more elementary reactions on nuclei. Particular emphasis is put on a discussion of actual observables in photonuclear reactions; we discuss in detail $η$- and vector-meson production. We show that photoproduction of $η$'s can yield essential information on in-medium properties of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance while the $ϕ$ meson properties will probably not be accessible through the $K^+K^-$ decay channel. However, for $ω$ mesons the $π^0γ$ decay channel, due to its reduced final state interaction, looks more promising in this respect. Completely free of final state interactions is dilepton production in the few GeV range. We show that the sensitivity of this decay channel to changes of hadronic properties in medium in photonuclear reactions on nuclei is as large as in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Finally we discuss that hadron production in nuclei at 10 -- 20 GeV photon energies can give important information on the hadronization process.

## Key claim

The paper argues that observable consequences of in-medium modifications of hadronic properties can be seen not only in relativistic heavy-ion collisions but also in more elementary reactions on nuclei, particularly photonuclear reactions. The authors hedge that while heavy-ion reactions reach higher densities, the theoretical input is calculated for cold equilibrium matter, making photonuclear reactions on stationary nuclei a cleaner testing ground despite probing densities at or below normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors performed semi-classical coupled-channel transport calculations for photon-induced reactions on nuclear targets, treating final-state interactions incoherently and allowing vector mesons with correct in-medium spectral functions to be produced and transported consistently. For photon energies above about 1 GeV, coherent initial-state interactions (shadowing) of the incoming photon were included. The calculations used a low-density expansion for in-medium selfenergies and, for some cases, more refined selfconsistent coupled-channel calculations of meson selfenergies that included baryon resonances such as the Delta(1232), S11(1535), and D13(1520).

## Key numbers

- Collisional broadening of the S11(1535) resonance: about 35 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0).
- For the phi meson, collisional broadening of up to 40 MeV at rho_0 and vanishing phi momentum, compared to a vacuum width of 4.4 MeV; expected mass shift of the phi is -10 MeV to -30 MeV.
- For omega mesons, theoretical predictions for the in-medium mass range from 640 to 765 MeV, and width up to 50 MeV at nuclear matter density.
- The Coulomb potential depth in Ca-40 is 11 MeV.
- Hadron formation times estimated at 0.5-0.8 fm/c in the hadron rest frame.
- Photon energies for hadron formation studies: 10-20 GeV (HERMES) and 2-9 GeV (planned CLAS upgrade).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium hadron properties that were traditionally investigated through relativistic heavy-ion reactions, such as the CERES experiment at CERN SPS which found dilepton excess in the 300-700 MeV invariant mass range. The authors build on their own previous calculations for proton-induced (Bratprot, 1999), pion-induced (Weidmann, 2000; Effepi, 2001), and photon-induced reactions (Effephot, 2003). The paper also references the low-density expansion approach and the more refined selfconsistent calculations of Post and collaborators (Postneu, 2003) that go beyond linear density scaling. The results enable future experiments such as the TAPS/Crystal Barrel measurement at ELSA for omega mesons and a planned JLab experiment for dilepton production, providing a hadronic baseline for interpreting heavy-ion data.
