# Lepton U_l(1) symmetry and interaction of neutrinos with deuterons at high energies

> The following items are discussed: 1. The lepton U_l(1) symmetry and its relation to the presence of the neutrino mass term in the Hamiltonian. 2. The possibility of the measurement of the neutrino-deuteron reactions with high energy neutrinos at SNO by using neutrinos from a neutrino factory. 3. The weak axial one-boson exchange currents for the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that consistent relativistic calculations of neutrino-deuteron cross sections using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with weak axial meson exchange currents are necessary to give more confidence in conclusions about neutrino oscillations, but the formalism is presented only as a theoretical framework; no actual cross-section calculations or numerical predictions for SNO are performed.

## Method

The authors construct weak axial meson exchange currents (MEC) for the π, ρ, a₁, and ω meson ranges within the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation, starting from two chiral Lagrangian schemes: a Yang-Mills type and a hidden local symmetries (HLS) approach. The one- and two-nucleon current operators are derived from Feynman diagrams, and the full current is shown to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities and the PCAC constraint when evaluated between two-body Bethe-Salpeter wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The electron neutrino mass upper bound from direct search is about 2.5 eV.
- The solar neutrino energy from ⁸B decay is ≤ 15 MeV.
- The estimated neutrino flux from a neutrino factory with stored muon momenta p = 20–50 GeV/c is ∼ 10¹⁰–10¹¹ \(\bar \nu_\mu\) m⁻² year⁻¹.
- The atmospheric neutrino flux at the terrestrial surface is ∼ 3 × 10¹⁰ m⁻² year⁻¹.
- The axial coupling constant used is g_A = –1.26.
- Previous calculations of neutrino-deuteron cross sections were performed up to energies of 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier low-energy calculations of neutrino-deuteron reactions by K. Kubodera and S. Nozawa (1990s), which included only the static part of the weak exchange current of the pion range. The formalism is intended to enable consistent covariant calculations of neutrino-deuteron cross sections at high energies (up to 50 GeV) for the SNO detector and for proposed neutrino factories, allowing comparison with standard nuclear physics approaches. The authors note that the constructed currents can immediately improve calculations of the solar proton burning process (p + p → d + e⁺ + ν_e) within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, as previously studied by J. G. Congleton and H. W. Fearing (1990s).
