# Enhanced violation of the Lorentz invariance and Einstein's equivalence principle in nuclei and atoms

> Local Lorentz Invariance violating (LLIV) and Einstein equivalence principle violating (EEPV) effects in atomic experiments are discussed. The EEPV effects are strongly enhanced in the narrow 7.8 eV transition in 229Th nucleus. Nuclear LLIV momentum tensors describing anisotropy in the maximal attainable speed for massive particles (analog of Michelson-Morley experiment for light) are expressed in terms of the experimental values of nuclear quadrupole moments. Calculations for nuclei of experimental interest 133Cs, 85Rb, 87Rb, 201Hg, 131Xe and 21Ne have been performed. The results for 21Ne are used to improve the limits on the proton LLIV constants by 4 orders of magnitude.

# Enhanced violation of the Lorentz invariance and Einstein's equivalence principle in nuclei and atoms

## Key claim
The Einstein equivalence principle violating (EEPV) effect in the narrow 7.8 eV nuclear transition in 229Th is enhanced by approximately 10^5 relative to atomic transitions, because the difference in nuclear kinetic energy between the excited and ground states (~0.5–0.95 MeV) is orders of magnitude larger than the transition energy itself (7.8 eV), while the non-relativistic virial relation that would normally cancel the effect is broken by relativistic corrections.

## Method
The author calculates the difference in kinetic energy between the ground state (5/2+[633]) and the 7.8 eV excited state (3/2+[631]) of 229Th using the deformed oscillator model with spin-orbit coupling, obtaining an analytical estimate of 0.5 MeV. For the tensor (spatial anisotropy) LLIV interaction, the author expresses the nuclear LLIV tensor in terms of the experimental nuclear electric quadrupole moment using a proportionality relation derived from the oscillator model and the giant dipole resonance sum rule, then applies this to nuclei of experimental interest (133Cs, 85Rb, 87Rb, 201Hg, 131Xe, 21Ne). For the deformed nucleus 21Ne, explicit deformed oscillator calculations are performed to extract the proton and neutron contributions to the LLIV shift.

## Key numbers
- The 229Th transition energy is 7.8(5) eV with a width of ~10^-3–10^-4 Hz (Beck; Tkalya 2015).
- The difference in neutron kinetic energy between the excited and ground states in 229Th is 0.954 MeV from Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov calculations (Litvinova), and the proton contribution is 0.233 MeV.
- The analytical estimate for the kinetic energy difference in 229Th is 0.5 MeV.
- The amplitude of the annual oscillation in the Sun's gravitational potential is ΔU_g/c^2 = 1.7 × 10^-10.
- The purely gravitational limit on the proton EEPV constant is c_00 = (0.20 ± 30) × 10^-6 (Hohensee 2013).
- The limit on the electron EEPV constant is c_00 = (0.14 ± 0.28) × 10^-7 (Hohensee 2013c).
- For 21Ne, the LLIV shift is <δH> = (0.54 C^(2)_0,p + 0.57 C^(2)_0,n) MeV.
- The experimental limits on tensor LLIV constants from the 21Ne experiment (Romalis) are: c_X = (4.8 ± 4.4) × 10^-29, c_Y = -(2.8 ± 3.4) × 10^-29, c_Z = -(1.2 ± 1.4) × 10^-29, c_- = (1.4 ± 1.7) × 10^-29.
- The experimental quadrupole moment of 21Ne is Q = 0.103(8) barn.
- The deformed oscillator frequency for 21Ne is ω̅ = 13.03 MeV, with ω̅/ω_z = 1.304 and ω̅/ω_x = 0.896.
- The proton LLIV shift from the Schmidt model for 21Ne is 0 MeV; from the semi-empirical model using experimental Q it is 0.54 MeV.
- The neutron LLIV shift from the Schmidt model for 21Ne is -4 MeV; from the semi-empirical model it is 0.57 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the search for Lorentz and Einstein equivalence principle violations using the Standard Model Extension (SME) framework (Kostelecky and Potting 1995; Kostelecky and Russell 2011). The enhancement mechanism for EEPV in 229Th parallels the approach used for nearly degenerate atomic levels in dysprosium (Hohensee 2013c) and proposed for Yb+ ions (Dzuba 2015) and highly charged ions (Dzuba 2016), but achieves a much larger enhancement factor (~10^5) due to the nuclear energy scale. The reinterpretation of the 21Ne NMR experiment (Romalis; Lamoreaux; Chupp; Prestage) improves limits on proton LLIV constants by 4 orders of magnitude compared to the previous best limits from the Cs fountain experiment (Cs). The results enable new experimental searches using the 229Th nuclear clock transition and measurements of neutron distribution quadrupole moments in molecules such as TaN, ThO, ThF+, HfF+, PbO, and WC (Flambaum 2014).
