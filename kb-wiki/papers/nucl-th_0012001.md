# Shape Transition in Rare-Earth Nuclei in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> A systematic study of the temperature dependence of the shapes and pairing gaps of some isotopes in the rare-earth region is made in the relativistic Hartree-BCS theory. Thermal response to these nuclei is always found to lead to a phase transition from the superfluid to the normal phase at a temperature $T_Δ\sim 0.4 - 0.8$ MeV and a shape transition from prolate to spherical shapes at $T_c\sim 1.0 - 2.5$ MeV. These shape transition temperatures are appreciably higher than the corresponding ones calculated in the non-relativistic framework with the pairing plus quadrupole interaction. Study of nuclei with continued addition of neutron pairs for a given isotope shows that with increased ground state deformation, the transition to the spherical shape is delayed in temperature. A strong linear correlation between $T_Δ$ and the ground state pairing gap $Δ^0$ is observed; a well- marked linear correlation between $T_c$ and the ground state quadrupole defromation $β_2^{0}$ is also seen. The thermal evolution of the hexadecapole deformation is further presented in the paper.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, the shape transition temperature from prolate to spherical shapes in rare-earth nuclei (T_c ~ 1.0 - 2.5 MeV) is appreciably higher than in non-relativistic calculations with the pairing plus quadrupole (P+Q) interaction, but the abstract does not mention that these higher values are found to be compatible with calculations using the realistic Gogny force.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-BCS calculations using the NL3 parameter set for even-even isotopes of Sm, Gd, and Dy nuclei. The single-particle states were calculated in a spherical oscillator basis with twelve shells, and the pairing gaps were determined from experimental odd-even mass differences. The temperature-dependent occupancies were obtained by minimizing the free energy, yielding the quadrupole deformation β₂, hexadecapole deformation β₄, and pairing gaps as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Superfluid-to-normal phase transition temperature T_Δ ~ 0.4 - 0.8 MeV across the studied rare-earth isotopes
- Shape transition temperature T_c ~ 1.0 - 2.5 MeV for prolate-to-spherical transition
- For ¹⁴⁸Sm, shape transition temperature T_c ~ 1.15 MeV; for ¹⁵⁰Sm, T_c ~ 1.6 MeV
- Neutron pairing gaps vanish at T ~ 0.6 - 0.7 MeV; proton pairing gaps vanish at T ~ 0.65 - 0.85 MeV
- Linear correlation found: T_c = 7.75 β₂⁰ (from twelve isotopes of Sm, Gd, Dy, and Er)
- Linear correlations for pairing: T_Δp = 0.56 Δp⁰ and T_Δn = 0.60 Δn⁰
- For ¹⁵⁰Sm with parameter set HS (M*/M = 0.54), T_c = 1.45 MeV; with NL2 (M*/M = 0.67), T_c = 1.75 MeV
- At highest temperature studied (T = 2.7 MeV), occupancy of positive energy states n⁺/A = 0.011

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-temperature non-relativistic Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov calculations with the pairing plus quadrupole interaction by Goodman (1981) and others, which found shape transition temperatures mostly between 1.0 and 1.8 MeV for rare-earth nuclei. The authors had previously studied only ¹⁶⁶Er and ¹⁷⁰Er in Agrawal (2000). The results are compared with calculations using the Gogny force by Egido (2000), which show similar characteristics to the RMF results. The linear correlations found enable estimation of transition temperatures from ground state properties that can be experimentally extracted.
