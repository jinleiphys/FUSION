# Enhancement of nucleon-nucleon cross section in sidewards flowing nuclear matter through zero sound mode excitation

> We investigate the modification induced on the nucleon-nucleon cross section by screening of the interaction inside nuclear matter. The interaction is described by the relativistic one boson exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ mesons. Medium effects enter as loop corrections to the meson propagators and are characterized not only by density, but also by temperature and velocity with respect to the center of mass of the collision. The cross section displays peaks for some specific values of the velocity, corresponding to the excitation of zero-sound modes of the longitudinal $ω$ field. The enhancement factor amounts to about 1.5 under reasonable conditions. It increases with density and is reduced at finite temperature. These findings may have verifiable consequences on the angular dependence of the measurable secondary particle distributions.

# Enhancement of nucleon-nucleon cross section in sidewards flowing nuclear matter through zero sound mode excitation

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon cross section inside nuclear matter is enhanced by a factor of about 1.5 due to screening of the interaction, but only when the background nuclear matter flows with a specific relative velocity with respect to the collision center-of-mass that excites zero-sound modes of the longitudinal omega field; the enhancement is density-dependent and is reduced at finite temperature.

## Method

The authors calculate the elastic proton-proton cross section in the Born approximation using a relativistic one-boson-exchange model with sigma, omega, and pi mesons, where medium effects enter as loop corrections to the meson propagators at the random phase approximation (RPA) level. The meson propagators are dressed by polarization functions that depend on density, temperature, and the relative velocity between the nuclear matter rest frame and the collision center-of-mass frame, with the velocity dependence entering through Lorentz boosts of the momentum transfer and through velocity-dependent projectors in the propagator structure. The free-space coupling constants and cutoff parameters are fitted to experimental elastic p-p scattering data, and the in-medium cross section is evaluated for velocities parallel and perpendicular to the beam axis.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor of about 1.5 under reasonable conditions (no specific density or temperature given for this value in the text)
- Characteristic velocity for the sharp peak at nuclear saturation density n₀: v̂_y ≈ 0.41 c
- Characteristic velocity for the sharp peak at twice saturation density 2 n₀: v̂_y ≈ 0.57 c
- Peak structure begins to develop above density n = 0.5 n₀
- At temperature T = 10 MeV, a broad bump remains visible around v̂_y; at T ≥ 50–100 MeV the peak structure disappears completely
- For velocities orthogonal to the reaction plane (v_x), no appreciable modification is observed up to v_x ≈ 0.8 c
- Meson masses used in the fit: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_π = 550 MeV; pion-nucleon coupling g_π²/4π = 14.4
- Fitted coupling constants (parameter set A): g_σ = 3.80, g_ω = 9.31; cutoffs: Λ_σ = 1298.8 MeV, Λ_ω = 1240.5 MeV, Λ_π = 362.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Diaz-Alonso and Mornas, 1998) which calculated the density and temperature dependence of the in-medium cross section assuming the background nuclear matter was at rest relative to the collision center-of-mass. The present paper releases that assumption and investigates the velocity dependence, finding that zero-sound modes in the mixed sigma-omega channel produce resonant enhancements for specific relative velocities. The results are relevant for interpreting anisotropic flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions, such as those from the FOPI collaboration at GSI and the E877 collaboration at AGS, where the observed anisotropy is not yet fully understood and has been analyzed mainly in terms of the equation of state rather than velocity-dependent cross section modifications. The enhancement mechanism is distinct from that found by Alm et al. (nonrelativistic Brueckner calculation), where a pole in the intermediate two-nucleon state produces an enhancement at low densities (n/n₀ ≈ 0.5) that disappears for nonzero total pair momentum, whereas the present enhancement occurs at densities n/n₀ ≥ 0.5 and for nonzero relative velocities v/c = 0.3–0.7.
