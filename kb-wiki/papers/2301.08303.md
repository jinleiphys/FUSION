# Probing neutron skin and symmetry energy with relativistic isobar collisions

> In these proceedings, we present the three proposed observables to probe the neutron skin and symmetry energy with relativistic isobar collisions, namely, the isobar ratios of the produced hadron multiplicities ($N_{\rm ch}$), the mean transverse momenta ($\langle p_{\perp} \rangle$), and the net charge multiplicities ($ΔQ$). Our findings suggest potentially significant improvement to neutron skin and symmetry energy determination over traditional low energy methods.

# Probing neutron skin and symmetry energy with relativistic isobar collisions

## Key claim
The isobar ratios of hadron multiplicities, mean transverse momenta, and net charge multiplicities in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV can determine the neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy slope parameter with precision potentially comparable to or exceeding traditional low-energy methods, but the systematic uncertainties are dominated by nuclear deformation effects rather than the QCD modeling uncertainties that limit conventional approaches.

## Method
The author uses four nuclear density functionals (eSHF with Lc20, Lc47, Lc70 and SHF with SLy4) to generate nuclear density distributions for Ru and Zr with varying neutron skin thicknesses. These densities are then input into multiple relativistic heavy-ion collision models (Hijing, AMPT with string fragmentation and string melting, UrQMD, and the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model) to simulate particle production, from which the isobar ratios of charged hadron multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and net charge are computed as functions of centrality.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy slope parameter extracted from the multiplicity ratio in top 2% centrality by STAR is L(ρ_c) = 53.8 ± 1.7 (stat.) ± 7.8 (syst.) MeV, where systematic uncertainty is dominated by nuclear deformations.
- The symmetry energy slope parameter extracted from the mean transverse momentum ratio in top 5% centrality by STAR is L(ρ_c) = 56.8 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 10.4 (syst.) MeV, again with systematic uncertainty dominated by nuclear deformations.
- The mean transverse momentum ratio R_pt can reach as large as 0.5% above unity in central collisions.
- The net charge ratio R_ΔQ for grazing collisions (impact parameter b between 7 and 20 fm) with N_ch less than about 10 follows a simple superimposition prediction with α = -0.344 from UrQMD NN interactions.

## Context
This work extends the prediction by Xu (2017) and Li (2018) that relativistic isobar collisions are sensitive to neutron skin, which was confirmed by STAR data (2021). It proposes three complementary observables to determine the symmetry energy slope parameter, complementing traditional low-energy measurements from electron and hadron scattering (Frois, 1987; Tsang, 2012; Tarbert, 2013) and the PREX-II electroweak measurement (Abrahamyan, 2012; Adhikari, 2021) which gave Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm and L = 105 ± 37 MeV. The extracted L(ρ_c) values from isobar collisions are consistent with the constraint L(ρ_c) = 47.3 ± 7.8 MeV from electric dipole polarizability in 208Pb (Zhang, 2014). The method enables future measurements with different systematics than traditional nuclear scattering experiments.
