# Lower and upper bounds on the mass of light quark-antiquark scalar resonance in the SVZ sum rules

> The calculation of the mass of light scalar isosinglet meson within the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov (SVZ) sum rules is revisited. We develop simple analytical methods for estimation of hadron masses in the SVZ approach and try to reveal the origin of their numerical values. The calculations of hadron parameters in the SVZ sum rules are known to be heavily based on a choice of the perturbative threshold. This choice requires some important ad hoc information. We show analytically that the scalar mass under consideration has a lower and upper bound which are independent of this choice: $0.78\lesssim m_s\lesssim1.28$ GeV.

# Lower and upper bounds on the mass of light quark-antiquark scalar resonance in the SVZ sum rules

## Key claim
The mass of the light scalar isosinglet meson (quark-antiquark state) in the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov (SVZ) sum rules has a lower and upper bound that are independent of the perturbative threshold choice, which is normally an ad hoc input: 0.78 GeV ≲ m_s ≲ 1.28 GeV. This result implies that the f_0(500) (σ-meson) cannot be a simple quark-antiquark state within this framework.

## Method
The author revisits the SVZ sum rules for the scalar current j_s = q̅q, using the standard two-point correlation function with operator product expansion (OPE) including gluon and quark condensates up to dimension six. Instead of the usual numerical extraction of the mass from the Borel sum rules with a chosen perturbative threshold s_0, the author develops analytical methods by taking the limit M² → ∞ (large Borel parameter) and by analyzing the minimum of the mass as a function of s_0 and as a function of M² in the limit s_0 → ∞. The lower bound is derived from minimizing the asymptotic mass formula with respect to s_0, and the upper bound from maximizing the mass in the Borel window as s_0 → ∞.

## Key numbers
- Lower bound on scalar mass: m_s|_min = 0.78 ± 0.02 GeV, obtained from the minimum of the asymptotic mass formula with respect to the perturbative threshold s_0, using h_0 ≈ 1.64, h_2 ≈ 0.049 GeV⁴, h_3 ≈ 0.092 GeV⁶.
- Upper bound on scalar mass: m_s|_max ≈ 1.28 GeV, obtained from the minimum of the mass formula in the limit s_0 → ∞, with the Borel fixed point M_0 ≈ 0.78 GeV.
- Standard SVZ prediction from Ref. [rry] (with s_0 ≈ 1.5 GeV²): m_s = 1.00 ± 0.03 GeV.
- The lower bound coincides numerically with the ω-meson mass; the upper bound coincides with the f_1(1285) meson mass.
- The Borel fixed point M_0 ≈ 0.78 GeV is determined mainly by the dimension-6 condensate h_3.

## Context
This work extends the classic SVZ sum rule analysis of the scalar meson by Refs. [svz] and [rry], which required an ad hoc choice of the perturbative threshold s_0 to obtain the mass prediction near 1 GeV. The author shows analytically that the mass is bounded between 0.78 and 1.28 GeV regardless of this choice, confirming the widespread idea that the f_0(500) (σ-meson) is an exotic state rather than a simple quark-antiquark meson. The analysis enables a clearer interpretation of the SVZ results: the scalar state described by the method could correspond to f_0(980) or f_0(1370), with the latter being less likely. The analytical techniques developed here (asymptotic limits, fixed-point analysis) can be applied to other meson channels, as demonstrated for the axial-vector case in the Appendix, where the author re-derives the Weinberg relation m_a² ≈ 2m_ρ².
