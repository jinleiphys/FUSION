# High Energy QCD at NLO: from light-cone wave function to JIMWLK evolution

> Soft components of the light cone wave-function of a fast moving projectile hadron is computed in perturbation theory to third order in QCD coupling constant. At this order, the Fock space of the soft modes consists of one-gluon, two-gluon, and a quark-antiquark states. The hard component of the wave-function acts as a non-Abelian background field for the soft modes and is represented by a valence charge distribution that accounts for non-linear density effects in the projectile. When scattered off a dense target, the diagonal element of the S-matrix reveals the Hamiltonian of high energy evolution, the JIMWLK Hamiltonian. This way we provide a new direct derivation of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian at the Next-to-Leading Order.

## Key claim

The paper provides a direct derivation of the Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) JIMWLK Hamiltonian by computing the soft component of the light-cone wave function of a fast-moving projectile hadron to third order in the QCD coupling constant, without assuming any previous results from the literature; the derivation relies on a Born-Oppenheimer approximation that splits the Hilbert space into valence and soft sectors, with the valence charges acting as non-commuting background fields.

## Method

The authors compute the light-cone wave function of a fast-moving hadron in perturbation theory to third order in the QCD coupling constant, treating the hard (valence) component as a non-Abelian background field represented by a valence charge distribution. The Fock space of soft modes at this order consists of one-gluon, two-gluon, and quark-antiquark states, and the coefficients of the wave function expansion are determined by perturbatively diagonalizing the light-cone QCD Hamiltonian on the soft Hilbert space. The diagonal element of the S-matrix, averaged over soft modes, is then computed using this wave function, and the NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian is extracted from terms linear in the rapidity interval delta Y.

## Key numbers

* The soft component of the light-cone wave function is computed to third order in the QCD coupling constant g_s, with the LO Weizsacker-Williams gluon emission vertex kappa_1 appearing at order g_s.
* The NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian is expanded as H_JIMWLK = H^LO_JIMWLK(alpha_s) + H^NLO_JIMWLK(alpha_s^2) + ..., where alpha_s is the strong coupling constant.
* The rapidity interval delta Y is defined by the longitudinal phase space of soft modes, with the number of new soft modes proportional to delta Y via the integral over k^+ from Lambda to Lambda exp(delta Y).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order JIMWLK framework developed by Jalilian-Marian, Iancu, McLerran, Weigert, Leonidov, and Kovner (1997-2004) and provides an independent cross-check of the NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian previously obtained in Lublinsky, Mulian (2015-2016) using symmetry constraints and matching to known evolution equations for the quark dipole and SU(3) baryon from Balitsky and Chirilli (2008) and Grabovsky (2013). The complete NLO light-cone wave function enables future reformulation of the NLO JIMWLK as a stochastic Langevin equation suitable for numerical simulations and allows simultaneous treatment of NLO corrections to the Hamiltonian and semi-inclusive observables such as single inclusive particle production.
