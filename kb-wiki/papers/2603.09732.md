# Event-by-Event Multiplicity Fluctuations in Heavy-Ion Collisions Using Modified HIJING Monte Carlo Generator

> This work presents an analysis of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations as a sensitive tool for diagnosing the state of matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using a modified version of the HIJING Monte Carlo generator, which integrates various models of partonic energy loss in hot (quark-gluon plasma) and cold media, the connection between fluctuation dynamics and system properties is investigated. It is shown that the nature and magnitude of fluctuations allow for the identification of the created medium type (hot or cold), the testing of the adequacy of energy loss models, and the detection of signatures of a first-order phase transition in different kinematic regions. The results obtained are important for interpreting the data from experiments aimed at mapping the QCD phase diagram and searching for the critical point.

## Key claim

The authors show that event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations of charged hadrons can serve as a sensitive diagnostic tool to distinguish between hot (quark-gluon plasma) and cold hadronic media produced in heavy-ion collisions, and to detect signatures of a first-order phase transition, but only under the specific condition that the modified HIJING generator includes a probabilistic model for the phase transition whose parameters (binodal energy and fluctuation width) must be chosen a priori.

## Method

The authors modified the HIJING v.1.411 Monte Carlo generator by implementing partonic energy loss formalisms for both hot media (BDMPS radiative loss plus collisional loss) and cold media (radiative loss only). They simulated central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 20 to 200 GeV per nucleon pair, generating 3.5 million events per energy point. They then computed the ratios of the second to first cumulant (C2/C1) and third to second cumulant (C3/C2) of charged hadron (K+, K-, pi+, pi-, p, antiproton) multiplicity distributions for different energy loss models and kinematic regions.

## Key numbers

- Simulated Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 20 to 200 GeV, with 3.5 million events per energy point
- Temperature of the hot medium parameterized as T0 = 0.1238294 * (sqrt(s_NN))^0.2224218 GeV
- Transverse length of the medium L = 3 * r0 * A^(1/3) / 4, with r0 = 1.112 fm
- First-order phase transition modeled with binodal energy (sqrt(s_NN))_b = 120 GeV and fluctuation width sigma^2 = 20 GeV (Figures 4 and 5) or sigma^2 = 30 GeV (Figure 1)
- Kinematic cuts used: |y| < 0.5 and 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c (primary); also |y| < 1.0 with 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |y| < 1.0 with 0 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c
- Default HIJING parton energy loss parameter: 2 GeV/fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (Blume 2018, Koch 2017) by implementing specific partonic energy loss models from Baier (1995, 1997) and Piegne (2008) into the HIJING generator framework of Wang (1994). The results are intended to guide experimental searches for the QCD critical endpoint at facilities such as PHENIX (Adare 2008), NA49 (Gazdzicki 2008), STAR (Xu 2014, Luo 2015), MPD, and FAIR, by providing predictions for how multiplicity cumulant ratios behave in the presence of a first-order phase transition versus hot or cold medium effects alone.
