# Role of the Coulomb interaction in the flow and the azimuthal distribution of kaons from heavy ion reactions

> Coulomb final-state interaction of positive charged kaons in heavy ion reactions and its impact on the kaon transverse flow and the kaon azimuthal distribution are investigated within the framework of QMD (Quantum Molecular Dynamics) model. The Coulomb interaction is found to tend to draw the flow of kaons away from that of nucleons and lead to a more isotropic azimuthal distribution of kaons in the target rapidity region. The recent FOPI data have been analyzed by taking into accout both the Coulomb interaction and a kaon in-medium potential of the strong interaction. It is found that both the calculated kaon flows with only the Coulomb interaction and with both the Coulomb interaction and the strong potential agree within the error bars with the data. The kaon azimuthal distribution exhibits asymmetries of similar magnitude in both theoretical approaches. This means, the inclusion of the Coulomb potential makes it more difficult to extract information of the kaon mean field potential in nuclear matter from the kaon flow and azimuthal distribution data.

## Key claim

The Coulomb final-state interaction of positive kaons produces effects on kaon transverse flow and azimuthal distributions that are similar in tendency and magnitude to those caused by the in-medium strong kaon potential, making it difficult to extract unambiguous information about the strong kaon mean field potential from existing FOPI data.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate heavy ion reactions, incorporating a soft Skyrme force with momentum-dependent interactions. Kaon production was treated perturbatively via baryon-baryon and pion-baryon channels, and the Coulomb field from charged nucleons, Delta and N* resonances, and pions was included in the kaon Hamiltonian. The calculations were compared to FOPI data for Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/A, with and without an additional in-medium strong kaon potential based on chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

*   For Au+Au at 1 GeV/A and b = 5 fm, the Coulomb interaction changed the kaon flow parameter F from 66 MeV/c to 15 MeV/c.
*   For Au+Au at 1 GeV/A and b = 5 fm, the asymmetry factor of the kaon azimuthal distribution decreased from about 0.075 to 0.005 due to the Coulomb interaction.
*   For Ni+Ni at 1.93 GeV/A with b < 4 fm, the asymmetry factor of the kaon azimuthal distribution was 0.046 with no potentials, 0.032 with only the Coulomb interaction, and 0.027 with both Coulomb and strong potentials.
*   The expected pi-/pi+ ratio for Au+Au was about 1.95 for pions from Delta decay and 1.75 for pions from N* decay.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li, Ko, and Li (1995) who first suggested kaon flow as a probe of the kaon mean field potential, but did not quantify the Coulomb effect. The paper shows that the Coulomb interaction, which had been noted earlier by Benenson (1979) and others for charged pions, produces distortions that mimic the repulsive strong potential for K+ mesons. This finding implies that future experiments with higher precision are needed to disentangle Coulomb and strong interaction effects, and that theoretical analyses must include both contributions to reliably extract the kaon in-medium potential relevant to neutron star physics.
