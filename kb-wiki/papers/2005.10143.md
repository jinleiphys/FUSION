# Exact sum rules with approximate ground states

> Electromagnetic and weak transitions tell us a great deal about the structure of atomic nuclei. Yet modeling transitions can be difficult: it is often easier to compute the ground state, if only as an approximation, than excited states. One alternative is through transition sum rules, in particular the non-energy-weighted and energy-weighted sum rules, which can be computed as expectation values of operators. We investigate by computing sum rules for a variety of nuclei, comparing the numerically exact full configuration-interaction shell model, as a reference, to Hartree-Fock, projected Hartree-Fock, and the nucleon pair approximation. These approximations yield reasonable agreement, which we explain by prior work on the systematics of transition moments.

## Key claim

Approximate ground states from Hartree-Fock, projected Hartree-Fock, and the nucleon pair approximation yield reasonable agreement with exact full configuration-interaction sum rules, but the agreement is not guaranteed and depends on the approximate state being dominated by eigenstates near the true ground state, as explained by prior work on the systematics of transition moments and the Brink-Axel hypothesis.

## Method

The authors computed non-energy-weighted sum rules (NEWSR) and energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR) for E2, M1, and Gamow-Teller transitions as expectation values of scalar operators in the ground state, using a shell-model framework with the USDB interaction in the sd shell and the GX1A interaction in the pf shell. They compared the numerically exact full configuration-interaction (FCI) shell model results against three approximations: Hartree-Fock (HF) using gradient descent minimization, angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock (PHF) via linear algebra projection, and the nucleon pair approximation (NPA) restricted to S, D, and G pairs in the sd shell and S, D pairs in the pf shell.

## Key numbers

- For E2 transitions in the sd shell, the HF and PHF approximations to $^{32}$S and $^{33}$S severely underestimate the NEWSR relative to FCI; the HF state for $^{32}$S has filled $0d_{5/2}$-$1s_{1/2}$ shells and the $^{33}$S HF minimum is almost entirely (96%) a single $0d_{3/2}$ neutron outside filled shells.
- For M1 transitions in the sd shell, $^{32}$S HF and PHF values are outliers, overestimating the M1 strength; in the pf shell, three NPA cases ($^{59}$Fe, $^{60}$Fe, $^{61}$Fe) provide notable overestimates of the M1 NEWSR.
- For Gamow-Teller transitions, the same pattern of overestimation appears: $^{32}$S HF and PHF in the sd shell, and $^{59,60,61}$Fe NPA in the pf shell.
- In the $^{76}$Sr test case using the JUN45 interaction, the HF and PHF E2 NEWSR values are above the local FCI values, while the NPA E2 value is below; for GT+, the HF and PHF NEWSR are well within the range of FCI values.
- Typical NPA M-scheme dimensions for Fe isotopes range from 350 ($^{52}$Fe) to 1276 ($^{56}$Fe), compared to FCI dimensions from $1.1 \times 10^8$ to $5.0 \times 10^8$.

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of sum rules in nuclear physics, particularly the Brink-Axel hypothesis and the systematics of transition moments studied by Johnson (2015) and Johnson et al. (2018). The authors respond to the call of Lipparini and Stringari (1989) to investigate sum rule methods beyond the mean-field. The results enable using approximate ground states as a computationally efficient cross-check for transition strength calculations in methods that use only a small fraction of the model space, such as the Monte Carlo shell model, the nucleon pair approximation, or the projected shell model.
