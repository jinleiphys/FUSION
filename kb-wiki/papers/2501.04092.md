# A Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield bound with a first-order system in the $2D$ Gross-Pitaevskii equation

> A novel Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) bound for the Gross-Pitaevskii equations in two spatial dimensions is presented. The energy can be bounded from below in terms of the combination of two boundary terms, one related to the vorticity (but ``dressed'' by the condensate profile) and the second to the ``skewness'' of the configurations. The bound is saturated by configurations that satisfy a system of two first-order partial differential equations. When such a BPS system is satisfied, the Gross-Pitaevskii equations are also satisfied. The analytic solutions of this BPS system in the present manuscript represent configurations with fractional vorticity living in an annulus. Using these techniques, we present the first analytic examples of this kind. The hydrodynamical interpretation of the BPS system is discussed, and the implications of these results are outlined.

# A Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield bound with a first-order system in the $2D$ Gross-Pitaevskii equation

## Key claim
The authors derive a novel BPS bound for the two-dimensional Gross-Pitaevskii equation, contrary to the long-held belief that no such bound exists. The bound is saturated by a first-order system of partial differential equations, but the topological charge on the right-hand side is not the obvious vorticity; instead it is a combination of a "dressed" vorticity term and a "skewness" term.

## Method
The authors rewrite the Gross-Pitaevskii energy functional in terms of two real scalar fields (the real and imaginary parts of the condensate wavefunction). By completing the square, they express the energy as a sum of two squared first-order differential operators plus two boundary terms, identifying the conditions under which the first-order system implies the second-order GPE. They then solve this BPS system in polar coordinates to obtain analytic solutions, and extend the technique to a sextic self-interaction potential.

## Key numbers
- The fractional vorticity configuration has phase $S = \theta/3 + \gamma$, so that when $\theta$ goes from $0$ to $2\pi$, the phase variation $\Delta S$ is $2\pi/3$, corresponding to vorticity $1/3$.
- The amplitude profile for the $1/3$-vorticity solution is $\rho(r) = 2\sqrt{2} / (2\sqrt{2} A r^{1/3} - 3\kappa r)$, where $A$ is an integration constant and $\kappa^2 = g_{eff} = 2Mg/\hbar^2$.
- For the sextic interaction potential, the solution has phase $S(\theta) = \theta/4 + S_0$ (fraction $1/4$) and amplitude $\rho(r) = (A r^{1/2} - (4/\sqrt{3})\kappa r)^{-1/2}$.
- The solution is defined on an annulus with inner radius $R_1$ and outer radius $R_2$ ($R_1 < R_2$), where the integration constant $A$ is chosen to keep the fields regular.

## Context
This work provides the first BPS bound and first-order BPS system for the Gross-Pitaevskii equation in two spatial dimensions, a result previously believed impossible. It extends techniques from BPS soliton theory (used in Ginzburg-Landau theory for superconductors and in chiral perturbation theory as discussed by BPSlast1 and BPSlast2) to the theory of superfluids. The analytic solutions with fractional vorticity require no extra fields or interactions beyond the standard GPE, unlike previous fractional vortex constructions in condensed matter physics (FracVort1, FracVort2, FracVort3, FracVort4) and high energy physics (FracVort5, FracVort6, FracVort7, FracVort8). The results enable future study of multi-vortex solutions via zero-curvature representations and analysis of two-dimensional quantum turbulence.
