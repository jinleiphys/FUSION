# Energy Loss of Charm Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma: Collisional vs Radiative

> Considering the collisional energy loss rates of heavy quarks from hard light parton interactions the total energy loss of a charm quark for a static medium has been computed. For the energy range $E\sim (5-10)$ GeV of charm quark, it is found to be almost same order as that of radiative ones estimated to a first order opacity expansion. The collisional energy loss will become much more important for lower energy charm quarks and this feature could be very interesting for phenomenology of hadrons spectra. Using such collisional energy loss rates we estimate the momentum loss distribution employing a Fokker-Planck equation and the total energy loss of a charm quark for an expanding quark-gluon plasma under conditions resembling the RHIC energies. The fractional collisional energy loss is found to be suppressed by a factor of 5 as compared to static case and does not depend linearly on the system size. We also investigate the heavy to light hadrons $D/π$ ratio at moderately large (5-10) GeV/$c$ transverse momenta and comment on its enhancement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for charm quarks with energy in the range of 5-10 GeV in a static quark-gluon plasma, the collisional energy loss is of the same order as the radiative energy loss estimated using first-order opacity expansion, contrary to earlier assumptions that radiative loss dominates. The author qualifies that this equivalence holds specifically for this energy range where charm quarks are not ultra-relativistic (γv ≤ 4), and that collisional loss becomes even more important at lower energies.

## Method

The author computed collisional energy loss rates for charm quarks from hard light parton interactions using leading-order perturbative QCD with Hard-Thermal-Loop resummation, comparing these to radiative energy loss calculations from Djordjevic et al. (2004) that included the Ter-Mikayelian effect. For an expanding plasma under RHIC conditions, the momentum loss distribution was estimated by solving a Fokker-Planck equation with momentum-averaged drag and diffusion coefficients derived from elastic scattering cross sections, assuming Bjorken scaling for the temperature evolution.

## Key numbers

- For a 10 GeV charm quark in a static medium with T=0.225 GeV (μ=0.5 GeV) and α_s=0.3, the differential collisional energy loss is d(ΔE)/dL ∼ 0.36 GeV/fm, compared to d(ΔE)/dL ∼ 0.375 GeV/fm for radiative loss
- The scaled collisional energy loss ΔE/E for a charm quark in a static plasma of length L=4 fm is approximately constant and similar to radiative loss in the energy range E ∼ 5-10 GeV
- The fractional collisional energy loss in an expanding plasma is suppressed by a factor of 5 compared to the static case
- The initial temperature used for RHIC conditions is T_0=0.5 GeV with initial time t_0=0.3 fm/c and α_s=0.3
- The Debye screening mass μ=0.5 GeV corresponds to a temperature T=0.225 GeV for n_f=2 and α_s=0.3
- The drag coefficient approximation is valid up to momentum p ≤ 15 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier collisional energy loss calculations by Braaten and Thoma (1991) and compares directly with the radiative energy loss framework developed by Djordjevic et al. (2004) for heavy quarks including the Ter-Mikayelian effect. The paper challenges the prevailing view from Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) and Djordjevic et al. (2004) that radiative energy loss dominates for heavy quarks, showing instead that collisional loss is equally important in the experimentally relevant momentum range of 5-10 GeV. This enables more complete phenomenological studies of D/π ratios at RHIC energies and suggests that both collisional and radiative mechanisms must be included for accurate modeling of heavy quark energy loss in quark-gluon plasma.
