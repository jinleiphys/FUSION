# Renormalizability of the nuclear many-body problem with the Skyrme interaction beyond mean field

> Phenomenological effective interactions like Skyrme forces are currently used in mean--field calculations in nuclear physics. Mean--field models have strong analogies with the first order of the perturbative many--body problem and the currently used effective interactions are adjusted at the mean--field level. In this work, we analyze the renormalizability of the nuclear many--body problem in the case where the effective Skyrme interaction is employed in its standard form and the perturbative problem is solved up to second order. We focus on symmetric nuclear matter and its equation of state, which can be calculated analytically at this order. It is shown that only by applying specific density dependence and constraints to the interaction parameters could renormalizability be guaranteed in principle. This indicates that the standard Skyrme interaction does not in general lead to a renormalizable theory. For achieving renormalizability, other terms should be added to the interaction and employed perturbatively only at first order.

## Key claim

The standard Skyrme interaction does not in general lead to a renormalizable theory when the nuclear many-body problem is solved up to second order in perturbation theory; renormalizability can only be guaranteed by applying specific density dependence and constraints to the interaction parameters, and even then only for particular values of the density exponent alpha.

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter analytically up to second order in perturbation theory using the standard zero-range Skyrme interaction, omitting the spin-orbit term but retaining the density-dependent term. They employed a momentum cutoff regulator on the outgoing relative momentum and analyzed the asymptotic behavior of the second-order contributions, separating them into finite parts, parts absorbable into renormalized parameters, and genuinely divergent parts that cannot be absorbed. They then imposed renormalizability by requiring that all cutoff-dependent terms be absorbable into first-order parameters, which forced specific constraints on the Skyrme parameters and the density exponent alpha.

## Key numbers

- For the case with alpha = 2/3 and t1^R = t2^R = T3^R = 0, fitting to the SLy5 mean-field EOS gave chi^2 = 346850 with m = 939 MeV, and chi^2 = 15989 with m = -969.55 MeV (negative mass)
- For the case with alpha = -1/6 and t1^R = t2^R = 0, fitting to SLy5 gave chi^2 less than 0.1 with m^R = 591.9 MeV, t0^R = 793.15 MeV fm^3, T3^R = -1570.8 MeV fm^(5/2), x0^R = 1.465, x3^R = -0.1759
- For the case with alpha = 1/3 and t1^R = t2^R = 0, fitting to SLy5 gave chi^2 = 1364 with m = 939 MeV, and chi^2 = 188 with m = 23845 MeV
- The density exponent alpha must take specific values (-1/6, 1/3, or 2/3) for renormalizability to be possible in the t0-t3 model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on ultraviolet divergences in second-order calculations with the Skyrme force (Moghrabi et al., Yang et al.) and addresses the overcounting problem identified in beyond-mean-field methods such as second random-phase approximation (Gambacurta et al.), particle-vibration coupling (Bernard et al., Colo et al.), and multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing (Pillet et al.). The analysis is framed within the context of effective field theories, drawing parallels to Pionless EFT where renormalizability guided the discovery of the three-body force (Bedaque et al., Braaten et al.). The results call for a systematic power-counting analysis in energy-density functional theories based on Skyrme interactions, and suggest that additional interaction terms (such as four-derivative two-body, two-derivative three-body, and no-derivative four-body terms) would be needed for a fully renormalizable theory.
