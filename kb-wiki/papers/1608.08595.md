# Anomalous transport from holography: Part I

> We revisit the transport properties induced by the chiral anomaly in a charged plasma holographically dual to anomalous $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ Maxwell theory in Schwarzschild-$AdS_5$. Off-shell constitutive relations for vector and axial currents are derived using various approximations generalising most of known in the literature anomaly-induced phenomena and revealing some new ones. In a weak external field approximation, the constitutive relations have all-order derivatives resummed into six momenta-dependent transport coefficient functions: the diffusion, the electric/magnetic conductivity, and three anomaly induced functions. The latter generalise the chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects. Nonlinear transport is studied assuming presence of constant background external fields. The chiral magnetic effect, including all order nonlinearity in magnetic field, is proven to be exact when the magnetic field is the only external field that is turned on. Non-linear corrections to the constitutive relations due to electric and axial external fields are computed.

## Key claim

The paper derives off-shell constitutive relations for vector and axial currents in a holographic charged plasma with a chiral anomaly, showing that in a weak external field approximation all-order derivative terms can be resummed into six momentum-dependent transport coefficient functions, and that the chiral magnetic effect remains exact for arbitrary strong constant magnetic fields when no other external fields are present.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality, modeling the boundary theory as anomalous \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) Maxwell theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\) spacetime. They solve the dynamical components of the bulk anomalous Maxwell equations near the conformal boundary, decomposing the bulk gauge fields into basis functions constructed from external fields and charge densities, which transforms the dynamics into ordinary differential equations for the decomposition coefficients. The study is divided into two parts: a linearized regime with weak external fields scaling linearly with a small parameter \(\epsilon\), and a nonlinear regime with constant background external fields plus small inhomogeneous fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The diffusion coefficient in the hydrodynamic limit: \(\mathcal{D} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{8}\pi i\omega + \frac{1}{48}[-\pi^2 \omega^2 + q^2 (6\log 2 - 3\pi)] + \cdots\)
* The electric conductivity in the hydrodynamic limit: \(\sigma_e = 1 + \frac{\log 2}{2}i\omega + \frac{1}{24}[\pi^2\omega^2 - q^2 (3\pi + 6\log 2)] + \cdots\)
* The magnetic conductivity in the hydrodynamic limit: \(\sigma_m = 72\kappa^2(\bar{\mu}^2 + \bar{\mu}_5^2)(2\log 2 - 1) + i\omega[\frac{1}{16}(2\pi - \pi^2 + 4\log 2) + \mathcal{O}(\bar{\mu}^2,\bar{\mu}_5^2)] + \cdots\)
* The chiral magnetic conductivity in the hydrodynamic limit: \(\sigma_\chi = 12\kappa \bar{\mu}_5 \{1 + i\omega \log 2 - \frac{1}{4} \omega^2 \log^2 2 - \frac{q^2}{24}[\pi^2 - 1728\kappa^2(\bar{\mu}^2_5 + 3\bar{\mu}^2)(\log 2 - 1)^2]\} + \cdots\)
* The chiral separation conductivity: \(\sigma_\kappa = 12\kappa \bar{\mu} \{1 + i\omega \log 2 - \frac{1}{4} \omega^2 \log^2 2 - \frac{q^2}{24}[\pi^2 - 1728\kappa^2(\bar{\mu}^2 + 3\bar{\mu}_5^2)(\log 2 - 1)^2]\} + \cdots\)
* The axial analogue of magnetic conductivity: \(\sigma_a = 144\kappa^2\bar{\mu}\bar{\mu}_5(2\log 2 - 1) + \cdots\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Bu, Lublinsky, and Sharon, 2015) which derived off-shell constitutive relations for a globally conserved \(U(1)\) current without anomalies, by now incorporating triangle anomaly effects through a holographic model with Chern-Simons terms. The results generalize known anomaly-induced phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev et al., 2004-2009) and chiral separation effect (Metlitski and Zhitnitsky, 2005; Newman and Son, 2006), providing all-order momentum-dependent transport coefficient functions rather than just constant or low-order derivative terms. The work enables future studies of nonlinear anomalous transport, including derivative corrections to the chiral magnetic effect for spatially-varying magnetic fields and the dependence of AC conductivity on magnetic fields in experimentally realizable condensed matter systems.
