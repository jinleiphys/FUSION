# The Spinless Relativistic Hellmann Problem

> We compile some easily deducible information on the discrete eigenvalue spectra of spinless Salpeter equations encompassing, besides a relativistic kinetic term, interactions which are expressible as superpositions of an attractive Coulomb potential and an either attractive or repulsive Yukawa potential and, hence, generalizations of the Hellmann potential employed in several areas of science. These insights should provide useful guidelines to all attempts of finding appropriate descriptions of bound states by (semi-) relativistic equations of motion.

## Key claim

The paper compiles rigorous constraints on the discrete eigenvalue spectra of spinless Salpeter equations with a generalized Hellmann potential (superposition of an attractive Coulomb term and a Yukawa term), but these constraints are derived only for the case of equal-mass constituents and rely on the assumption that the Hamiltonian can be defined such that its spectrum is bounded from below.

## Method

The authors analyze the spinless Salpeter Hamiltonian \(H = 2\sqrt{\bm{p}^2 + m^2} + V_{\rm H}(r)\) with the generalized Hellmann potential \(V_{\rm H}(r) = -\kappa/r - \ups \exp(-b r)/r\) (Coulomb coupling \(\kappa \ge 0\), Yukawa coupling \(\ups \gtreqqless 0\), range \(b > 0\)). They classify the potential shapes into six regimes based on the relative size of \(\ups\) and \(\kappa\), then derive lower bounds on the Hamiltonian using known results for the spinless relativistic Coulomb problem and variational upper bounds on binding energies via a Rayleigh–Ritz method with a basis of generalized-Laguerre orthogonal polynomials.

## Key numbers

- The critical Coulomb coupling for the spinless Salpeter equation with a pure Coulomb potential is \(\kappa_{\rm c} = 4/\pi = 1.273239\dots\); the spectrum is bounded from below if and only if \(\kappa \le \kappa_{\rm c}\).
- For the Hellmann potential, the sum of Coulomb and Yukawa couplings must satisfy \(\kappa + \ups \le 16/(3\pi) = 1.69765\dots\) to avoid a negative singularity at the origin (for \(\ups > 0\)); for \(-\kappa < \ups < 0\), the condition becomes \(\kappa \le 16/(3\pi) + |\ups|\).
- Variational upper bounds on binding energies \(B_k = E_k - 2m\) (in units of the constituent mass \(m\)) for three representative cases: for \(\kappa = \ups = 1/2\) (case \(\ups = \kappa\)), the ground state (radial \(n_r=0\), orbital \(\ell=0\)) binding energy is \(-0.11673\,m\); for \(\kappa = 1\), \(\ups = -1\) (case \(\ups = -\kappa\)), the ground state binding energy is \(-0.17951\,m\); for \(\kappa = 1\), \(\ups = -2\) (case \(\ups < -\kappa\)), the ground state binding energy is \(-0.14410\,m\).
- The spectral lower bound for the pure Coulomb case with \(\kappa = 1/2\) is \(\sigma(H) \ge 2m\sqrt{(1+\sqrt{1-\kappa^2})/2} = -0.58578\dots\,m\) (from Eq. (SM)); for \(\kappa = 1\), \(\ups = -1\) the lower bound is \(-1\,m\); for \(\kappa = 1\), \(\ups = -2\) the lower bound is \(-0.37336\dots\,m\).

## Context

This work extends prior rigorous analyses of the spinless relativistic Coulomb problem (Lucha and Schöberl, various years; Martin and Roy, 1989) to the generalized Hellmann potential, which has been used in atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics since Hellmann (1930s). The results provide guidelines for future numerical or approximate studies of semirelativistic bound states with such potentials, and the authors explicitly criticize two recent attempts (Arda, 2018; Antia, 2018) that used an expansion of the square-root kinetic term, arguing those approaches produce operators unbounded from below and are flawed due to neglect of a commutator term (Chargui, 2010). The paper enables systematic variational studies of relativistic Hellmann problems and clarifies the conditions under which the Hamiltonian is well-defined.
