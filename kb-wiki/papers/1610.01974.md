# Correlations between critical parameters and bulk properties of nuclear matter

> The present work starts by providing a clear identification of correlations between critical parameters ($T_c$, $P_c$, $ρ_c$) and bulk quantities at zero temperature of relativistic mean-field models (RMF) presenting third and fourth order self-interactions in the scalar field $σ$. Motivated by the nonrelativistic version of this RMF model, we show that effective nucleon mass ($M^*$) and incompressibility ($K_o$), at the saturation density, are correlated with $T_c$, $P_c$, and $ρ_c$, as well as, binding energy and saturation density itself. We verify agreement of results with previous theoretical ones regarding different hadronic models. Concerning recent experimental data of the symmetric nuclear matter critical parameters, our study allows a prediction of $T_c$, $P_c$ and $ρ_c$ compatible with such values, by combining them, through the correlations found, with previous constraints related to $M^*$ and $K_o$. An improved RMF parametrization, that better agrees with experimental values for $T_c$, is also indicated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlations between critical parameters (T_c, P_c, ρ_c) of symmetric nuclear matter and zero-temperature bulk properties (effective nucleon mass M* and incompressibility K_o) emerge in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models only when the effective mass is held fixed, a condition that the abstract does not explicitly state as necessary for the correlation to appear.

## Method

The authors first derived analytical expressions for the critical parameters in the nonrelativistic limit (NRL) of a point-coupling model with σ^3 and σ^4 self-interactions, using a classical ideal gas approximation for the temperature contribution. They then computed the critical parameters for 128 Boguta-Bodmer (BB) RMF parametrizations from the literature, imposing fixed values of saturation density (ρ_o = 0.15 fm^{-3}) and binding energy (B_o = 16 MeV) while varying K_o and m* within constrained ranges (250 ≤ K_o ≤ 315 MeV, 0.58 ≤ m* ≤ 0.64). The critical point was located by solving the simultaneous conditions of vanishing first and second derivatives of pressure with respect to density.

## Key numbers

- Critical density in the NRL model parametrized as ρ_c = α + β K_o, with α = (0.0278 ± 1.34×10^{-4}) fm^{-3} and β = (6.84×10^{-5} ± 4.76×10^{-7}) MeV^{-1} fm^{-3}
- For BB parametrizations with m* = 0.60, the critical temperature follows T_c = 13 - (1.9×10^{-3})K_o + (2.9×10^{-5})K_o^2 (T_c in MeV, K_o in MeV)
- The NRL model with ρ_o = 0.15 fm^{-3}, B_o = 16 MeV, K_o = 250 MeV, and m* = 0.6 yields T_c ≈ 12 MeV
- BB parametrizations with m* = 0.60 show Δρ_o = 0.004 fm^{-3} and ΔB_o = 0.69 MeV; those with m* = 0.70 show Δρ_o = 0.015 fm^{-3} and ΔB_o = 0.72 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of correlations in the isovector sector (Lourenço et al., 2015) to the finite-temperature regime, specifically the liquid-gas phase transition critical point. The results are compared with earlier theoretical correlations from Kapusta (1984), Lattimer and Swesty (1991), Natowitz et al. (2002), and Rios (2010), which all predict T_c increasing with K_o but with different functional forms. The study enables improved RMF parametrizations that better agree with experimental critical parameter values from Natowitz et al. (2002) and Karn et al. (2002-2005), by combining the found correlations with existing constraints on M* and K_o.
