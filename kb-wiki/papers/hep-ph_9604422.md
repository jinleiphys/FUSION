# Parton Sum Rules and Nucleon Structure Functions

> A brief summary on the Wigner rotation effect in the understanding of the proton spin ``crisis" related with the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule violation, and on the proton-neutron isospin symmetry breaking explanation of the Gottfried sum rule violation. The proton spin could be fully provided by the spin sum of quarks if one considers the Wigner rotation effect in the measured quark helicity distributions.

# Parton Sum Rules and Nucleon Structure Functions

## Key claim
The proton spin “crisis” can be resolved without requiring gluon polarization or orbital angular momentum contributions: if one accounts for the relativistic Wigner rotation effect, the vector sum of quark spins in the proton rest frame can still equal the proton spin, even though the measured quantity ΔΣ in the infinite momentum frame appears small. The Gottfried sum rule violation can be explained either by an asymmetric SU(2) sea in the nucleon or by proton–neutron isospin symmetry breaking, and the two alternatives can be distinguished via neutrino deep inelastic scattering.

## Method
The author reinterprets the measured quark helicity distributions Δq by expressing quark wave functions in terms of light-cone Dirac spinors (infinite momentum frame) versus conventional instant-form Dirac spinors (rest frame), showing that Δq includes a factor M_q arising from internal quark transverse motion. For the Gottfried sum rule, the author compares two explanations, flavor-asymmetric sea versus proton–neutron isospin breaking, by examining predictions for neutrino deep inelastic scattering, charged pion Drell-Yan, proton Drell-Yan, and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers
- In the gluonic explanation of the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule violation, the required average Wigner rotation factors are ⟨M_u⟩ ≈ ⟨M_d⟩ ≈ 0.7 to preserve the values of Δu and Δd while maintaining the naive quark model spin sum rule Δu_L + Δd_L + Δs_L = 1.
- For the SU(2) sea asymmetry explanation of the Gottfried sum rule violation: ∫₀¹ [ū(x) – d̄(x)] dx = –0.140 ± 0.024.
- For the proton–neutron isospin symmetry breaking explanation: ∫₀¹ [q̄^p(x) – q̄^n(x)] dx = –0.084 ± 0.014.
- The new sum S = ∫₀¹ [(F₂^{νp} + F₂^{ν̄p}) – ½(F₂^{νD} + F₂^{ν̄D})] dx is zero for the asymmetric sea explanation and equals –0.336 ± 0.058 for the p–n isospin breaking explanation.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the proton spin puzzle triggered by the EMC experiment (1988) and the Gottfried sum rule violation measured by the NMC collaboration. It contrasts with the then-common gluonic and strange sea explanations (e.g., by Altarelli and Ross, or by Ellis and Karliner) that required the quark spin contribution to be only about 70% or negligible. The author’s earlier papers (Ma, 1991–1994) introduced the Wigner rotation mechanism, and the present paper systematically compares the two explanations of the Gottfried sum rule violation, enabling future experiments (particularly neutrino scattering) to decide between them.
