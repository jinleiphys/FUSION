# SU(4) Model of High-Temperature Superconductivity: Manifestation of Dynamical Symmetry in Cuprates

> The mechanism that leads to high-temperature superconductivity in cuprates remains an open question despite intense study for nearly two decades. Here, we introduce an SU(4) model for cuprate systems having many similarities to dynamical symmetries known to play an important role in nuclear structure physics and in elementary particle physics. Analytical solutions in three dynamical symmetry limits of this model are found: an SO(4) limit associated with antiferromagnetic order; an SU(2) limit that may be interpreted as a d-wave pairing condensate; and an SO(5) limit that may be interpreted as a doorway state between the antiferromagnetic order and the superconducting order. It is demonstrated that with a slightly broken SO(5) but under constraint of the parent SU(4) symmetry, the model is capable of describing the rich physics that is crucial in explaining why cuprate systems that are antiferromagnetic Mott insulators at half filling become superconductors through hole doping.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an SU(4) dynamical symmetry model for cuprate high-temperature superconductors, claiming that the three symmetry limits (SO(4), SU(2), and SO(5)) correspond respectively to antiferromagnetic order, d-wave pairing condensate, and a doorway state between them. The hedge is that the model works only with a slightly broken SO(5) symmetry under constraint of the parent SU(4) symmetry, and that the no-double-occupancy constraint is necessarily implied by the SU(4) algebra, limiting the maximum doping fraction to 1/4.

## Method

The authors construct a 16-element operator set from d-wave singlet pairs (p), d-wave triplet pairs (q), particle-hole operators (Q), and spin operators (S), which closes under a U(1) × SU(4) algebra. They identify three dynamical symmetry subgroup chains (SO(4), SU(2), and SO(5)) and construct Hamiltonians as linear combinations of quadratic Casimir operators for each chain. A coherent-state method is employed to obtain energy surfaces and order parameters, with the transformation matrix constrained to preserve SU(4) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Maximum doping fraction consistent with SU(4) symmetry: P_f = 1/4 (Ω_max = 1/4 Ω_e, where Ω_e is total lattice sites)
- Empirical maximum doping fraction for cuprate superconductivity: 0.23 to 0.27
- Dimensionality of the collective subspace for irrep (Ω/2, 0, 0): (1/12)(Ω/2 + 1)(Ω/2 + 2)^2(Ω/2 + 3)
- In the SO(4) limit ground state: staggered magnetization Q = (1/2)Ω(1 - x) and energy gap ΔE = 2χ_eff(1 - x)Ω
- In the SU(2) limit: pairing gap Δ = (1/2)G^0_eff Ω √(1 - x^2) and energy gap ΔE = G^(0)_eff Ω

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry methods developed in nuclear physics (Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model by Wu, Feng, and Guidry; Interacting Boson Model by Arima and Iachello) to condensed matter systems. The SU(4) model contains Zhang's SO(5) model (1997) as a subgroup but implements the full quantum commutator algebra of all 15 generators rather than treating only 10 operators dynamically. The model enables analytical solutions for three competing phases and provides a framework for studying the transition from antiferromagnetic Mott insulator to d-wave superconductor as a function of hole doping.
