# Gluon dipole factorisation for diffractive dijets

> Within the colour dipole picture for deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken $x$, we study the production of a pair of relatively hard jets via coherent diffraction. By "relatively hard" we mean that the transverse momenta of the two jets -- the quark ($q$) and the antiquark ($\bar{q}$) generated by the decay of the virtual photon -- are much larger than the target saturation momentum $Q_s(Y_{\mathbb{P}})$ evaluated at the rapidity gap $Y_{\mathbb{P}}$. We argue that the typical final-state configurations are such that the hard $q\bar q$ dijets are accompanied by a semi-hard gluon jet, with a transverse momentum of the order of $Q_s(Y_{\mathbb{P}})$. The presence of this third jet ensures that the scattering is strong and thus avoids the strong suppression of exclusive (hard) dijet production due to colour transparency. For such "2+1" jet configurations, we demonstrate that both the emission of the semi-hard gluon and its scattering with the hadronic target can be factorised in terms of an effective gluon-gluon dipole. This effective description, originally proposed in [1-4], builds a bridge between the colour dipole picture and collinear factorisation: the cross-section for diffractive 2+1 jets can be written as the product between a hard factor describing the $q\bar{q}$ dijets and a semi-hard factor expressing the unintegrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron. The latter is controlled by gluon dipole scattering in the black disk limit and hence is strongly sensitive to gluon saturation. By integrating out the kinematics of the 3 jets, we obtain the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution to the diffractive structure function in collinearly-factorised form.

## Key claim

The authors argue that diffractive production of a hard quark-antiquark pair in deep inelastic scattering is typically accompanied by a semi-hard gluon jet with transverse momentum of order the target saturation momentum, and that this "2+1 jet" configuration avoids the strong suppression from colour transparency that would affect exclusive dijet production, provided the gluon is soft enough that its longitudinal momentum fraction satisfies ξ ≲ Q_s²/Q².

## Method

Within the colour dipole picture for deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, the authors compute the light-cone wavefunction for the qq̅g Fock component of the virtual photon to leading order in perturbative QCD, first in the eikonal approximation for a very soft gluon and then extending to generic gluon longitudinal momentum fractions. They demonstrate that the diffractive amplitude factorises into a hard component describing the qq̅ pair formation and a semi-hard component describing gluon emission and elastic scattering of an effective gluon-gluon dipole, which they identify with the unintegrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momenta of the quark and antiquark jets are required to satisfy k_{1⊥}, k_{2⊥} ≫ Q_s(Y_ℙ), where Q_s(Y_ℙ) is the target saturation momentum evaluated at the rapidity gap Y_ℙ
- The gluon jet transverse momentum is of order k_{3⊥} ∼ Q_s(Y_ℙ)
- The gluon longitudinal momentum fraction satisfies ξ ≲ Q_s²/Q² ≪ 1
- The transverse size of the qq̅ dipole is limited to r ≲ 1/ Q̅, with Q̅² ≡ ϑ₁ϑ₂Q²

## Context

This work extends the gluon dipole picture originally proposed by Wüsthoff (1997), Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (1999), Hebecker (1997), and Buchmüller, McDermott, and Schäfer (1998) for computing the qq̅g contribution to the diffractive structure function, by establishing the factorisation at the amplitude level and for generic qq̅ configurations beyond the aligned-jet limit. The result builds a bridge between the colour dipole picture and collinear factorisation, enabling the diffractive 2+1 jet cross-section to be expressed as a product of a hard factor (identical to that for inclusive dijets) and a semi-hard factor interpreted as the unintegrated gluon distribution of the Pomeron, which is strongly sensitive to gluon saturation and can be evolved via the combined BK and DGLAP equations.
