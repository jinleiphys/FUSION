# Spin versus helicity in processes involving transversity

> We construct the spin formalism in order to deal in a direct and natural way with processes involving transversity which are now of increasing popularity. The helicity formalism which is more appropriate for collision processes of definite helicity has been so far used also to manage processes with transversity, but at the price of computing numerous helicity amplitudes generally involving unnecessary kinematical variables.In a second step we work out the correspondence between both formalisms and retrieve in another way all results of the helicity formalism but in simpler forms.We then compute certain processes for comparison.A special process:the quark dipole magnetic moment is shown to be exclusively treated within the spin formalism as it is directly related to the transverse spin of the quark inside the baryon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the spin formalism (SF) is more natural and efficient than the helicity formalism (HF) for processes involving transversity (transverse spin polarization), because the HF requires computing numerous helicity amplitudes and unnecessary kinematical variables when dealing with general polarizations, whereas the SF handles them directly; the quark dipole magnetic moment is claimed to be exclusively treatable within the SF as it is directly related to the transverse spin of the quark inside the baryon.

## Method

The author constructs the spin formalism by deriving a generalized spin density matrix rho(k',s',k,s) = u(k',s') u-bar(k,s) from its rest-frame form via Lorentz boosts, expressing it as a trace over Dirac indices. The correspondence between the spin formalism and the helicity formalism is then worked out by projecting transverse spin states onto helicity states in the center-of-mass frame, retrieving the known helicity projector formulas of Reference 1 (Mekhfi, 1992) in simpler forms. The formalism is applied to compute the electromagnetic vertex for e- -> e- + gamma and the process e+ e- -> selectron_R+ selectron_R- via photino exchange as illustrative examples.

## Key numbers

- The helicity-conserving density in the center-of-mass frame is given as rho_{lambda,-lambda} = -lambda gamma_0 ((slash k + m)/(2m)) ((1 - lambda gamma^5 slash s_L)/2) e^{i lambda phi}
- The helicity-non-conserving density is rho_{lambda,lambda} = -lambda gamma_0 ((slash k + m)/(2m)) gamma_5 (slash eta^lambda / 2) e^{i lambda phi}, where slash eta^lambda = slash eta_1 - i lambda slash eta_2
- The spin formalism electromagnetic vertex trace yields 2i Tr(rho_{-s,s} gamma_mu) = -(2s/m) |k| eta_{1 mu} - 2i g_{mu 0}
- The helicity formalism components for the same vertex are Tr(rho_{lambda,lambda} gamma_mu) = -(i lambda/m) |k| eta^lambda_mu and Tr(rho_{-lambda,lambda} gamma_mu) = -i g_{mu 0}

## Context

This work extends the author's own prior introduction of transversity in hadronic physics (Mekhfi, 1992) and provides a systematic alternative to the helicity formalism developed by the same author (Mekhfi, 1992, Reference 1). The spin formalism enables simpler computation of processes with transverse polarizations, such as one-spin transverse asymmetries, and is necessary for the quark dipole magnetic moment which depends directly on transverse spin. The formalism is also connected to a generalization of a formula due to Michel and Bouchiat (Reference 4).
