# Probing Transverse-Momentum Distributions With Groomed Jets

> We present the transverse momentum spectrum of groomed jets in di-jet events for $e^+e^-$ collisions and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS). The jets are groomed using a soft-drop grooming algorithm which helps in mitigating effects of non-global logarithms and underlying event. At the same time, by reducing the final state hadronization effects, it provides a clean access to the non-perturbative part of the evolution of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions. In SIDIS experiments we look at the transverse momentum of the groomed jet measured w.r.t. the incoming hadron in the Breit frame. Because the final state hadronization effects are significantly reduced, the SIDIS case allows to probe the TMD parton distribution functions. We discuss the sources of non-perturbative effects in the low transverse momentum region including novel (but small) effects that arise due to grooming. We derive a factorization theorem within SCET and resum any large logarithm in the measured transverse momentum up to NNLL accuracy using the $ζ$-prescription as implemented in the artemide package and provide a comparison with simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using soft-drop grooming on jets in di-jet events and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) provides clean access to transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions by significantly reducing final state hadronization effects, though the authors note that novel but small non-perturbative effects arise specifically due to the grooming procedure itself.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the transverse momentum spectrum of groomed jets in both e+e- collisions and SIDIS, using the soft-drop grooming algorithm with parameters zcut ~ 0.1 and beta = 0. They resum large logarithms in the measured transverse momentum up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy using the zeta-prescription implemented in the artemide package, and compare their results with Pythia 8 Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- Soft-drop grooming parameter zcut ~ 0.1 is used throughout the analysis
- Center of mass energies Q = 100 GeV and Q = 50 GeV are used for Pythia 8 simulations of hadronization effects
- Jet radius R ~ 1 is considered the relevant case for low energy experiments such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC)
- The mass cutoff parameter ecut = 0.01 is used as a reference value for normalized cross section comparisons
- The factorization theorem resums logarithms up to NNLL accuracy using the zeta-prescription

## Context

This work extends previous studies on jet-TMD definitions by Gutierrez-Reyes, Scimemi, and collaborators (2018, 2019) which used the winner-take-all (WTA) axis to achieve factorization for large jet radii, and by Makris and collaborators (2017, 2018) who proposed groomed jets with identified hadrons as probes of TMD evolution. The use of soft-drop grooming allows factorization theorems for large jet radius (R ~ 1) that were previously problematic, and the universal soft function appearing in the factorization is the same as in traditional hadronic TMD factorization for Drell-Yan and di-hadron production. This enables future experimental tests at facilities like the EIC and LHeC, where the groomed jet observable provides a cleaner probe of TMD parton distribution functions compared to traditional hadron-based measurements.
