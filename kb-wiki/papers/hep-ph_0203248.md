# Magnetic moments of the Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1670) resonances

> By using techniques of unitarized chiral perturbation theory, where the $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1670)$ resonances are dynamically generated, we evaluate the magnetic moments of these resonances and their transition magnetic moment. The results obtained here differ appreciably from those obtained with existing quark models. The width for the $Λ(1670) \to Λ(1405) γ$ transition is also evaluated leading to a branching ratio of the order of $2 \times 10^{-6}$.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) resonances, calculated using unitarized chiral perturbation theory where these states are dynamically generated from meson-baryon multiple scattering, differ appreciably from quark model predictions; the authors hedge that the complex-plane evaluation involves extrapolation uncertainties, and the real-axis results show channel dependence for the Λ(1405) ranging from +0.26 to +0.44 nuclear magnetons depending on the chosen channel.

## Method

The authors compute meson-baryon scattering T-matrices using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization and dimensional regularization, generating the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) as poles in the second Riemann sheet. They then evaluate photon coupling amplitudes by summing Feynman diagrams where the resonance appears on both sides of the photon vertex, extracting magnetic moments from the ratio of the photon amplitude to the scattering amplitude at the resonance pole (complex plane) or on the real axis near the resonance energy. The elementary photon couplings to baryons are derived from the chiral Lagrangian with parameters b6^D = 2.40 and b6^F = 1.82 fitted to ground-state baryon magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1405) magnetic moment on the real axis: +0.44 ± 0.06 μ_N in the Kbar N → γ Kbar N channel, and +0.26 ± 0.07 μ_N in the Kbar N → γ π Σ channel
- Λ(1405) magnetic moment from complex plane (absolute value): 0.41 ± 0.01 μ_N at pole z_R = 1426 + 16i MeV, and 0.30 ± 0.01 μ_N at pole z_R = 1390 + 66i MeV
- Λ(1670) magnetic moment: -0.29 ± 0.01 μ_N from real-axis evaluation with the K Ξ channel
- Transition magnetic moment |μ(Λ(1670)→Λ(1405))|: 0.023 ± 0.009 μ_N from real-axis evaluation
- Branching ratio for Λ(1670) → Λ(1405) γ: 2 × 10^{-6}
- Quark model results (Isgur-Karl parameters): Λ(1405) = -0.13 μ_N, Λ(1670) = 0.01 μ_N, transition = 0.14 μ_N
- Quark model results (Hey-Litchfield-Cashmore parameters): Λ(1405) = -0.15 μ_N, Λ(1670) = -0.23 μ_N, transition = 0.26 μ_N

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to dynamically generate the Λ(1405) by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and the full octet of negative-parity baryon resonances by Oller and Meissner (2001) and by Jido et al. (2001). The method for extracting resonance properties from scattering amplitudes was developed following the technique used for N*N*π couplings by Nacher et al. (2000). The results contradict existing quark model calculations by Isgur and Karl (1978) and by Hey, Litchfield, and Cashmore, which predict opposite sign for the Λ(1405) magnetic moment and transition magnetic moments roughly ten times larger. The small predicted branching ratio of 2 × 10^{-6} for the radiative transition means that even setting an experimental upper bound would discriminate between the dynamically generated and quark-model descriptions of these resonances.
