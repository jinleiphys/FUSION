# Strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon with $N_f = 2 + 1$ $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions

> We present results for the strange contribution to the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon computed on the CLS ensembles with $N_f=2+1$ flavors of $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions and an $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved vector current. Several source-sink separations are investigated in order to estimate the excited-state contamination. We calculate the form factors on six ensembles with lattice spacings in the range of $a=0.049-0.086\,\text{fm}$ and pion masses in the range of $m_π=200-360\,\text{MeV}$, which allows for a controlled chiral and continuum extrapolation. In the computation of the quark-disconnected contributions we employ hierarchical probing as a variance reduction technique.

## Key claim

The strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are determined from lattice QCD with controlled chiral and continuum extrapolations, but the final values for the strange charge radii and magnetic moment still carry large relative statistical errors, and the quoted systematic errors from variations in the fitting procedure are comparable to the statistical uncertainties.

## Method

The calculation uses six CLS ensembles with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of \(\mathcal{O}(a)\)-improved Wilson fermions and an \(\mathcal{O}(a)\)-improved vector current, covering lattice spacings \(a=0.049-0.086\,\text{fm}\) and pion masses \(m_\pi=200-360\,\text{MeV}\). Quark-disconnected contributions are computed via hierarchical probing with 512 Hadamard vectors per noise source, and excited-state contamination is controlled using the summation method over source-sink separations of \(0.5-1.3\,\text{fm}\). The form factors are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point functions, and the radii and magnetic moment are obtained from model-independent \(z\)-expansion fits followed by chiral and continuum extrapolations based on SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Electric strange radius at the physical point: \((r^2_E)_{\text{phys}}^s = -0.0046(12)(7)(1)(9)(3)(6)\,\text{fm}^2\) (first error statistical, remaining errors from fitting variations)
- Strange magnetic moment at the physical point: \(\mu^s_\text{phys} = -0.020(5)(0)(2)(11)(1)(3)\)
- Magnetic strange radius at the physical point: \((r^2_M)_{\text{phys}}^s = -0.010(6)(2)(5)(7)(0)(2)\,\text{fm}^2\)
- Statistical error of the strange quark loop is reduced by a factor of 5 when using 512 Hadamard vectors compared to 512 U(1) noise vectors
- Lattice spacings used: \(a=0.04981\,\text{fm}\) (N302), \(0.06426\,\text{fm}\) (N203, N200, D200), \(0.07634\,\text{fm}\) (N401), \(0.08636\,\text{fm}\) (H105)
- Pion masses on ensembles: 200 MeV (D200), 278 MeV (H105), 283 MeV (N200), 289 MeV (N401), 345 MeV (N203), 354 MeV (N302)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of strange nucleon form factors by using a larger set of ensembles with controlled continuum and chiral extrapolations, and by employing hierarchical probing for variance reduction. The results agree with other lattice determinations from LHPC (Green 2015) and ETMC (Alexandrou 2018), but disagree with the magnetic moment from \(\chi\)QCD (Sufian 2016, Sufian 2017) by more than two standard deviations. The results are also compatible with experimental data from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as SAMPLE, G0, A4, and HAPPEX. The precise determination of these form factors is important for isolating the weak charge of the proton from measurements of parity-violating asymmetries, where the strange form factors constitute a leading uncertainty.
