# Stopped nucleons in configuration space

> In this note, using the colour string model, we study the configuration space distribution of stopped nucleons in heavy-ion collisions. We find that the stopped nucleons from the target and the projectile end up separated from each other by the distance increasing with the collision energy. In consequence, for the center of mass energies larger than 6 or 10 GeV (depending on the details of the model) it appears that the system created is not in thermal and chemical equilibrium, and the net baryon density reached is likely not much higher than that already present in the colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

Using a colour string model, the authors find that stopped nucleons from the target and projectile in heavy-ion collisions become spatially separated along the beam axis by a distance that increases with collision energy, so that for center-of-mass energies above 6 or 10 GeV (depending on model details) the system likely does not reach thermal and chemical equilibrium, and the net baryon density may not exceed that already present in the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors model nucleon energy loss per unit length as a constant \(\sigma\) (following the Lund string model) and derive the configuration-space distribution of nucleons decelerated to mid-rapidity (\(|y|<1\)) by folding the nuclear density profiles (Gaussian with width 6.5 fm) with the collision-point distribution. They consider two values of \(\sigma\): 1 GeV/fm (wounded nucleon model) and 3 GeV/fm (wounded quark model), and compute the longitudinal (\(z\)) distribution of stopped nucleons for various center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- For \(\sigma = 1\) GeV/fm (wounded nucleon model), the left- and right-moving stopped nucleon peaks become clearly separated above \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 10\) GeV.
- For \(\sigma = 3\) GeV/fm (wounded quark model), the peaks become clearly separated above \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 20\) GeV.
- The limiting energy for achieving relatively high baryon density in the central region (\(z\approx 0\)) ranges from \(\sqrt{s} \sim 6\) GeV to \(\sqrt{s} \sim 10\) GeV, depending on model details.
- The nuclear density profiles are taken with width \(R_L = R_R = 6.5\) fm.
- The transverse momentum of final nucleons is taken as \(P_\perp = 1\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the colour string model (Lund model, Anderson; bremsstrahlung mechanism, Stodolsky) to study baryon stopping in configuration space, a topic relevant to the RHIC beam energy scan program (Adamczyk 2013, Alt 2006). The results suggest that at higher energies the spatial separation of stopped baryons prevents the formation of a thermally equilibrated high-density system, contradicting the assumption that lowering beam energy automatically produces high baryon density at mid-rapidity. The authors call for detailed simulations using event generators such as UrQMD (Bleicher 1999) and AMPT (Lin 2004) to study fluctuations of configuration-space baryon density on an event-by-event basis.
