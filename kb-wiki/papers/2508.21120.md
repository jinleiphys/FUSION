# Revisiting extremely high energy QED bremsstrahlung in matter: large modifications to the LPM effect

> Very high energy electrons initiate electromagnetic showers in ordinary matter that branch and multiply through bremsstrahlung and pair production. At extremely high energies, the quantum mechanical duration of these processes becomes longer than the mean free time to elastically scatter from the medium, which leads to a very significant suppression of bremsstrahlung (and pair production) known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We revisit the LPM effect for bremsstrahlung of energy $k_γ$ from an electron of energy $E$. We find that there are very large corrections to the LPM bremsstrahlung rate for certain regions of $(k_γ,E)$ due to quantum overlap of bremsstrahlung and subsequent pair production. This possibility was first raised in the 1960s, when it was argued qualitatively that pair production would significantly decrease the bremsstrahlung rate in those regions of $(k_γ,E)$ compared to the already-suppressed LPM bremsstrahlung rate. We find the opposite -- quantum overlap of bremsstrahlung with pair production significantly *increases* the bremsstrahlung rate compared to the LPM calculation -- and we verify our qualitative arguments with an analytic calculation of the effect.

## Key claim
Quantum overlap of bremsstrahlung with subsequent pair production significantly *increases* the bremsstrahlung rate compared to the already-suppressed LPM calculation, contrary to the 1960s qualitative argument by Galitsky and Gurevich that such overlap would further suppress the rate.

## Method
The authors used a formalism similar to Migdal's quantum mechanical treatment of the LPM effect, generalized to include the effects of pair production during bremsstrahlung. They performed an analytic calculation of the bremsstrahlung rate in the large-Nf limit of QED, focusing on the region where the electron energy E and photon energy k_gamma satisfy E >= k_gamma >> E_LPM (the LPM energy scale), with the additional condition that 1 - k_gamma/E >> E_LPM/E so that the electron mass can be ignored.

## Key numbers
- E_LPM = 2.5 TeV for Gold and E_LPM = 234 PeV for air (standard temperature and pressure), for bremsstrahlung by high-energy electrons
- The transition where the LPMplus rate starts to deviate from ordinary LPM occurs at k_gamma ~ alpha E, where alpha is the fine structure constant (approximately 1/137)
- For x_gamma = k_gamma/E much less than Nf*alpha, the LPMplus rate scales as (Nf*alpha^2)/(2*pi*x_gamma^(3/2)) * sqrt(qhat/E) times a logarithmic factor
- The ratio qhat(R_A) is approximately 2*qhat(m_e^(-1)) for m = m_e, where R_A is the nuclear radius and m_e is the electron mass

## Context
This work extends the original LPM effect calculations by Landau, Pomeranchuk (1953) and Migdal, and contradicts the qualitative argument by Galitsky and Gurevich (1964) that pair production would further suppress bremsstrahlung. The authors use techniques previously applied to partons traversing a quark-gluon plasma, and their results enable future work treating the massive case for analysis of the LPM effect at lower energies.
