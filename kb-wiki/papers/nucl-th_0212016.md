# Shape Coexistence in Pb186: Beyond-mean-field description by configuration mixing of symmetry restored wave functions

> We study shape coexistence in Pb186 using configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected self-consistent mean-field states. The same Skyrme interaction SLy6 is used everywhere in connection with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The model predicts coexisting spherical, prolate and oblate 0+ states at low energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected mean-field states, using the Skyrme interaction SLy6, predicts coexisting spherical, prolate, and oblate 0+ states in Pb186 at low energy, but the calculated excitation energy of the oblate 0+ state is significantly overestimated compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors generated a set of Hartree-Fock+BCS (HFBCS) wave functions with constraints on the axial quadrupole moment, then performed particle-number and angular-momentum projection on each state. They then carried out a variational configuration mixing (generator coordinate method) over the quadrupole moment coordinate to obtain collective states with good angular momentum, using the same Skyrme interaction SLy6 and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force throughout.

## Key numbers

- The experimental excitation energies of the second and third 0+ states in Pb186 are 532 keV (oblate) and 650 keV (prolate); the calculation gives 0.55 MeV for the prolate 0+ state but overestimates the oblate 0+ state.
- The prolate band has a transition quadrupole moment Q0 ≈ 1000 e fm² (β2 ≈ 0.34), the oblate band has Q0 ≈ -600 e fm² (β2 ≈ -0.20), and a third rotational band has Q0 ≈ 1400 e fm² (β2 ≈ 0.49).
- With the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, the prolate and oblate 0+ states are pushed up to 1.05 MeV and 1.39 MeV, respectively.
- A test with reduced pairing strength (-1100 MeV fm³) reduces the prolate and oblate minima of the deformation energy curves to 0.2 MeV and 0.65 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes by Tajima et al. (1993) and Chasman et al. (2001), who performed configuration mixing of mean-field states but without full symmetry restoration. The method improves upon those calculations by including particle-number and angular-momentum projection before the configuration mixing, enabling direct calculation of quadrupole moments and transition probabilities in the laboratory frame. The results support the interpretation of the low-lying 0+ states in Pb186 as shape coexistence, but the overestimation of the oblate band energy points to the need for improved effective interactions or inclusion of triaxial configurations.
