# Inclusive pion double charge exchange on Oxygen(16) at 0.6-1.1 GeV

> The inclusive pion double charge exchange (DCX) on oxygen nuclei has been measured in the region where additional pion production is kinematically forbidden. The experiment was performed at ITEP PS at incident pi- kinetic energies T_0= 0.59, 0.75 and 1.1 GeV. The integrated forward differential cross section was found to decrease with energy slowly. At 1.1 GeV it exceeds the theoretical prediction within the conventional sequential single charge exchange mechanism with a neutral pion in the intermediate state (Glauber elastic rescattering) by about half an order of magnitude. The sequential mechanism with two pions in the intermediate state (Glauber inelastic rescatterings), which was proposed recently, seems to be able to explain the observed slow energy dependence and allows to predict the DCX cross section for higher energies.

## Key claim

The inclusive pion double charge cross section on oxygen at 1.1 GeV exceeds the conventional sequential single charge exchange (SSCX) mechanism prediction by about a factor of five, but a recently proposed sequential mechanism with two pions in the intermediate state (Glauber inelastic rescatterings) appears able to explain the observed slow energy decrease and allows predictions at higher energies.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the ITEP 10-GeV Proton Synchrotron using negative pion beams at momenta 0.72, 0.88, and 1.26 GeV/c (kinetic energies 0.59, 0.75, and 1.1 GeV) incident on H₂O and D₂O targets. Forward-going positive particles were detected in a 3m magnet spectrometer instrumented with multigap optical spark chambers, with pions identified via time-of-flight and Cherenkov counters, and the inclusive double charge exchange cross section was extracted from the high-energy end of the π⁺ spectrum where additional pion production is kinematically forbidden (ΔT ≤ 140 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₈₀ (ΔT = 0–80 MeV) at T₀ = 0.59 GeV: 18.8 ± 2.1 μb/sr
- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₈₀ at T₀ = 0.75 GeV: 11.1 ± 1.8 μb/sr
- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₈₀ at T₀ = 1.1 GeV: 7.3 ± 1.7 μb/sr
- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₁₄₀ (ΔT = 0–140 MeV) at T₀ = 0.59 GeV: 80.2 ± 4.3 μb/sr
- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₁₄₀ at T₀ = 0.75 GeV: 42.6 ± 4.0 μb/sr
- Integrated forward differential cross section ⟨dσ/dΩ⟩₁₄₀ at T₀ = 1.1 GeV: 28.6 ± 3.8 μb/sr
- The DCX cross section decreases by about a factor of six from 0.18 to 1.1 GeV
- At 1.1 GeV the measured cross section exceeds the SSCX prediction by about half an order of magnitude (factor of five)

## Context

This work extends the study of inclusive pion double charge exchange to energies above 0.5 GeV, where no previous data existed, and follows preliminary results reported by the same group (Abramov et al., 2000; 2001). The conventional SSCX mechanism, which successfully described DCX data at lower energies (Oset et al., 1988; Gibbs et al., 1998), predicts a much faster decrease of the cross section with energy than observed. The new mechanism involving Glauber inelastic rescatterings with two pions in the intermediate state, proposed by Kaidalov (2001) and Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2001), offers a potential explanation and enables predictions for DCX cross sections at even higher energies.
