# Heavy-Quark Spectra at RHIC and Resonances in the QGP

> Thermalization and collective flow of charm (c) and bottom (b) quarks are evaluated from elastic parton scattering via "D"- and "B"-meson resonances in an expanding, strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma at RHIC. Pertinent drag and diffusion coefficients are implemented into a relativistic Langevin simulation to compute transverse-momentum spectra and azimuthal flow asymmetries (v_2) of c- and b-quarks. Upon hadronization (including coalescence and fragmentation) and semileptonic D- and B-decays, the resulting electron spectra (R_{AA} and v_2) are compared to recent RHIC data.

# Heavy-Quark Spectra at RHIC and Resonances in the QGP

## Key claim
Elastic scattering of charm and bottom quarks via D- and B-meson resonances in a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma can simultaneously reproduce the observed nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of single electrons at RHIC, provided the resonance cross sections are supplemented with leading-order pQCD scattering and combined with coalescence-plus-fragmentation hadronization. The claim is conditional on the assumed existence of mesonic resonance states surviving up to approximately twice the critical temperature, with resonance masses fixed at 2 GeV (D) and 5 GeV (B) and widths varied between 0.4 and 0.75 GeV.

## Method
The authors compute drag and diffusion coefficients from elastic parton scattering via D- and B-meson resonances (supplemented by leading-order pQCD scattering with Debye mass regularization and α_s = 0.4) and implement them into a relativistic Langevin simulation for semi-central (b = 7 fm) Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The heavy quarks evolve in an isentropically expanding isotropic QGP fireball with initial temperature T_0 = 340 MeV and formation time 1/3 fm/c, and are subsequently hadronized using a coalescence model at low p_T with δ-function fragmentation for unpaired quarks at high p_T, followed by semileptonic D- and B-meson decays to obtain single-electron spectra.

## Key numbers
- Resonance masses: m_D = 2 GeV, m_B = 5 GeV, with heavy-quark masses m_c = 1.5 GeV and m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Resonance widths varied as Γ = 0.4–0.75 GeV
- Strong coupling constant α_s = 0.4 for pQCD scattering
- Initial temperature T_0 = 340 MeV at formation time 1/3 fm/c
- Cross section ratio σ_{b\bar{b}}/σ_{c\bar{c}} = 4.9 × 10^{-3}
- Crossing of D- and B-decay electrons at p_T ≈ 5 GeV
- Charm-quark R_AA down to 0.2 and v_2 up to 10% from resonant interactions alone
- Bottom contributions reduce effects starting from electron momenta of about 3 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier pQCD-based calculations (Svetitsky, 1988; Mustafa and Thoma, 2004; Moore and Teaney, 2004) that found elastic pQCD cross sections insufficient to explain the observed heavy-quark suppression and flow at RHIC. It builds on the authors' prior proposal of D- and B-meson resonances in the QGP (van Hees and Rapp, 2004) and on lattice QCD computations suggesting mesonic bound states survive up to ~2T_c (Asakawa and Hatsuda, 2004; Karsch and Laermann, 2003). The coalescence hadronization scheme follows Greco, Ko, and Rapp (2004). The results enable further microscopic characterization of the strongly interacting QGP and its thermalization mechanisms, and motivate searches for resonance effects in heavy-quark observables at LHC energies.
