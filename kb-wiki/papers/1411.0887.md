# Ballistic protons in incoherent exclusive vector meson production as a measure of rare parton fluctuations at an Electron-Ion Collider

> We argue that the proton multiplicities measured in Roman pot detectors at an electron ion collider can be used to determine centrality classes in incoherent diffractive scattering. Incoherent diffraction probes the fluctuations in the interaction strengths of multi-parton Fock states in the nuclear wavefunctions. In particular, the saturation scale that characterizes this multi-parton dynamics is significantly larger in central events relative to minimum bias events. As an application, we study the centrality dependence of incoherent diffractive vector meson production. We identify an observable which is simultaneously very sensitive to centrality triggered parton fluctuations and insensitive to details of the model.

## Key claim

Ballistic protons measured in Roman pot detectors at an Electron-Ion Collider can serve as a centrality trigger for incoherent diffractive events, enabling the selection of rare parton configurations with enhanced saturation scale \(Q_s\); the double ratio of incoherent diffractive vector meson cross sections in central versus minimum bias events is simultaneously sensitive to centrality-triggered parton fluctuations and insensitive to model details.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent diffractive cross sections for exclusive vector meson (\(J/\Psi\), \(\rho\), \(\phi\)) production off a gold nucleus using the IPsat dipole model, with the dipole-nucleus amplitude obtained via an independent scattering approximation from the dipole-proton amplitude. Central events are defined at impact parameter \(b=0\) and compared to minimum bias events integrated over all impact parameters; the double ratio of cross sections for different vector meson species in central versus minimum bias events is evaluated as a function of \(Q^2\) at fixed \(\xpom = 0.005\).

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at \(\xpom = 0.005\), within EIC kinematic reach, with electron beams of 15 GeV and nuclear beams of 100 GeV/nucleon.
- The \(J/\Psi/\rho\) and \(J/\Psi/\phi\) double ratios show a significant enhancement at low \(Q^2\), reducing to unity only above \(Q^2 \sim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- The \(\phi/\rho\) double ratio remains nearly unity for the entire \(Q^2\) range studied.
- Ballistic protons are identified in the laboratory transverse momentum range \(400\) MeV to \(1\) GeV.
- Incoherent diffraction dominates in the kinematic regime \(1/R_A^2 \lesssim |t| \lesssim 1/R_p^2\), with sensitivity to sub-nucleon scale fluctuations for \(|t| \gtrsim 1/R_p^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event multi-parton spatial fluctuations in protons and nuclei, previously explored in high-multiplicity p+p and p/d+A collisions at LHC and RHIC (Chatrchyan 2013, Abelev 2012, Adare 2013, Aad 2013), to incoherent diffraction in e+A collisions at a future EIC. The approach complements recent work by Zheng (2014) on centrality selection using evaporation neutrons in inclusive scattering, and provides a method to probe rare large-\(Q_s\) configurations that is insensitive to final-state interactions complicating p+A analyses. The double ratio observable enables direct measurement of the nuclear enhancement of nonlinear gluon dynamics, previously quantified for inclusive DIS cross sections by Kowalski (2007).
