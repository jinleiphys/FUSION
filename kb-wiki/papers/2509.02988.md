# A Study on the Triggering of Nucleonic Direct Urca Processes in Neutron Stars of Specific Masses and Their Hyperon Dependence

> This work aims to analyze how hyperons affect neutrino radiation properties in nucleonic direct URCA processes, expecting to provide useful references for finding evidence of the existence of hyperons in astronomical observations. This analysis is carried out using the GM1 and NL3 parameter sets under the SU(6) and SU(3) flavor symmetries in the relativistic mean field theory framework. Combined with the inferred mass and radius values of PSRs J1231-1411, J0030+0451, and J0740+6620, our results show that nucleonic direct Urca processes are absent in PSR J1231-1411 due to momentum conservation violation. In hyperon-containing PSR J0030+0451 (NL3 parameter set), the nucleonic direct Urca processes involving $e^{-}$/ $μ^{-}$ would occur. A large inferred mass span induces hyperon fraction variations, affecting neutrino emissivity. If the inferred mass of PSR J0030+0451 exceeds approximately 1.8 $M_{\odot}$, the neutrino luminosity of the nucleonic direct Urca processes under the SU(3) flavor symmetry remains nearly the same as that in npe$μ$ matter, without depending on hyperons. However, it exhibits an obvious hyperon dependence under the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry. For hyperon-containing J0740+6620, the nucleonic direct Urca processes under the SU(3) flavor symmetry in GM1 parameter set predicts faster neutrino luminosity decline with hyperonic fraction than npe$μ$ matter, and under the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry in NL3 parameter set it shows monotonic decreasing trend. The research shows that hyperonic fraction significantly affect the neutrino radiation properties of the nucleonic direct URCA processes in neutron stars. Different-mass pulsars (e.g., PSRs J1231-1411, J0030+0451, J0740+6620) exhibit the distinct nucleonic direct URCA processes behaviors, dependent on inferred masses/radii, parameter sets, and theoretical models.

## Key claim

The nucleonic direct Urca processes in neutron stars are suppressed or enhanced depending on the hyperon fraction, which itself depends on the pulsar's mass, the chosen relativistic mean-field parameter set (GM1 or NL3), and the flavor symmetry (SU(3) or SU(6)); the abstract's claim of "obvious hyperon dependence" is conditional on the pulsar mass exceeding approximately 1.8 \(M_{\odot}\) and on the symmetry model used.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field theory with the GM1 and NL3 parameter sets, incorporating \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma^{*}\), and \(\phi\) mesons, under SU(3) flavor symmetry and SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars with and without hyperons (\(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^{-}\), \(\Xi^{0}\)), then computed the neutrino emissivity and luminosity of nucleonic direct Urca processes using the Fermi Golden Rule. The inferred masses and radii of PSRs J1231-1411, J0030+0451, and J0740+6620 from NICER observations were used to constrain which theoretical cases apply to each pulsar.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1231-1411: inferred mass \(1.04^{+0.05}_{-0.03}\) \(M_{\odot}\) and radius \(12.6 \pm 0.3\) km; nucleonic direct Urca processes are absent due to momentum conservation violation.
- PSR J0030+0451: inferred mass \(1.70^{+0.18}_{-0.19}\) \(M_{\odot}\) and radius \(14.44^{+0.88}_{-1.05}\) km; if mass exceeds approximately 1.8 \(M_{\odot}\), neutrino luminosity under SU(3) symmetry remains nearly the same as in npe\(\mu\) matter, but shows hyperon dependence under SU(6) symmetry.
- PSR J0740+6620: inferred mass \(2.073^{+0.069}_{-0.069}\) \(M_{\odot}\) and radius \(12.49^{+1.28}_{-0.08}\) km; under GM1 with SU(3) symmetry, neutrino luminosity declines faster with hyperonic fraction than in npe\(\mu\) matter; under NL3 with SU(6) symmetry, it shows a monotonic decreasing trend.
- For a canonical 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) neutron star, neutrino emissivity under GM1 is between \(9.154 \times 10^{26}\) and \(9.457 \times 10^{26}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\); under NL3 it is between \(5.752 \times 10^{26}\) and \(9.803 \times 10^{26}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Threshold density for \(\Lambda\) hyperon appearance under GM1: 0.386 fm\(^{-3}\) (SU3) and 0.349 fm\(^{-3}\) (SU6); under NL3: 0.303 fm\(^{-3}\) (SU3) and 0.285 fm\(^{-3}\) (SU6).
- Neutrino luminosity of PSR J0030+0451 under NL3: \(2.068\)–\(3.573 \times 10^{45}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg s\(^{-1}\) (mass-constrained) and \(1.550\)–\(4.027 \times 10^{45}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg s\(^{-1}\) (compactness-constrained).
- Neutrino luminosity of PSR J0740+6620 under GM1: \(3.376\)–\(5.155 \times 10^{45}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg s\(^{-1}\) (mass-constrained) and \(3.879\)–\(5.307 \times 10^{45}\) T\(_{9}^{6}\) erg s\(^{-1}\) (compactness-constrained).

## Context

This work extends the systematic studies of neutron star cooling via baryonic direct Urca processes by Potekhin et al. (2015, 2018, 2023) and Marino et al. (2024), who suggested that such processes may explain anomalously low surface temperatures in young neutron stars. The authors apply the relativistic mean field framework with hyperonic degrees of freedom to three NICER-observed pulsars (PSRs J1231-1411, J0030+0451, J0740+6620), showing that the hyperon fraction can significantly alter neutrino emissivity and luminosity in a mass-dependent way. The results provide a potential observational pathway to distinguish between neutron stars with and without hyperons, and to constrain nuclear physics parameters, if future observations can measure neutrino emissivity or surface thermal radiation from pulsars.
