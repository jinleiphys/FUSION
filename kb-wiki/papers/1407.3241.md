# Open quantum systems and Random Matrix Theory

> A simple model for open quantum systems is analyzed with Random Matrix Theory. The system is coupled to the continuum in a minimal way. In this paper we see the effect of opening the system on the level statistics, in particular the $Δ_3(L)$ statistic, width distribution and level spacing are examined as a function of the strength of this coupling. A super-radiant transition is observed, and it is seen that as it is formed, the level spacing and $Δ_3(L)$ statistic exhibit the signatures of missed levels.

# Open quantum systems and Random Matrix Theory

## Key claim
Opening a chaotic quantum system by coupling it to the continuum via a minimal non-Hermitian term causes the spectral statistics to mimic those of an incomplete spectrum or one with intruder levels, with the effect peaking at the super-radiant transition (κ ≈ 1) where the apparent fraction of missed levels reaches about 3%, even though the spectrum is complete.

## Method
An N×N Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) matrix is opened by adding an imaginary term −iκ√N to a single diagonal element (or equivalently −iκ/√N to all diagonal elements). The complex eigenvalues ε_n = E_n + iΓ_n are computed as a function of the coupling strength κ. Standard RMT statistics, the Δ₃(L) spectral rigidity, the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution, and the width distribution, are calculated for ensembles of opened matrices and compared to the closed GOE case.

## Key numbers
- The super-radiant transition occurs at κ ≈ 1, where the spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) reaches a maximum increase of about 15% (factor of 1.15) relative to the closed GOE value.
- The apparent fraction of missed levels, as determined by three separate RMT tests, peaks at about 3% when κ = 1.
- For the width distribution, when the largest (super-radiant) width is excluded, the remaining widths follow a Porter-Thomas distribution; the average of the non-largest widths follows ln Γ̄ = ±ln κ − 2.5 over a range from κ = 10⁻³ to 10⁶.
- The GOE average entropy is S = ln(0.48N), which for N = 50 gives S = 3.2; the super-radiant state drops to much lower entropy in the original basis.

## Context
This work extends the effective Hamiltonian approach to open quantum systems developed by Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1988, 1992) and by Zelevinsky and Volya (2003), applying it to a minimal GOE-based model. The finding that open spectra produce false positives for missed levels in RMT tests is relevant to the analysis of neutron resonance data and compound nucleus spectra. The missed-level tests used are based on the maximum likelihood method of Mulhall (2011), the Bohigas-Pato formula (2004) for Δ₃(L) of incomplete spectra, and the nearest-neighbor distribution method of Agvaanluvsan et al. (2003). The results enable more careful interpretation of RMT diagnostics in experimental nuclear physics, where coupling to the continuum is unavoidable.
