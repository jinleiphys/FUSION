# Universal scaling of meson and baryon spectra in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

> We systematically investigate the scaling property of mesons (pions and kaons) and baryons (protons, ${\rm Λ}$, ${\rm Ξ}$ and ${\rm Ω}$) transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra at different centrality classes (0-5$\%$, 5-10$\%$, 10-20$\%$, 20-40$\%$, 40-60$\%$, 60-80$\%$ and 80-100$\%$) in proton-lead collisions with center of mass energy per nucleon pair 5.02 TeV. In the low $p_{\rm T}$ region with $p_{\rm{T}}\leq $ 3.9 (3.1, 2.5, 2.7, 2.4 and 2.8) GeV/c, a universal scaling independent of the centrality is observed in the pion (kaon, proton, ${\rm Λ}$, ${\rm Ξ}$ and ${\rm Ω}$) spectra when a dilatation, $p_{\rm T}\rightarrow p_{\rm T}/K$, is applied. Here $K$ is a scaling parameter depending on the centrality class. We find that the rates at which ln$K$ changes with the logarithmic value of the average value of the number of participating nucleons, ln$\langle N_{\rm{part}}\rangle$, are stronger for baryons than those for mesons. In the high $p_{\rm T}$ region, there is a deviation from the scaling. The more peripheral the collisions are, the more obvious the violation of the scaling is. In the framework of the colour string percolation (CSP) model, we show that mesons and baryons are generated from the decay of clusters formed by strings overlapping in the transverse plane with the same size dispersion but with different mean size. The mean size of clusters for baryons is smaller than that of mesons. For the same hadrons at different centrality classes, the mean size of clusters decreases with the increase of centrality. The fragmentation functions for cluster decay are different for different hadrons, while they are universal for the same hadrons at different centrality classes. The universal scaling of the meson and baryon spectra in the low $p_{\rm T}$ region can be quantitatively understood with the CSP model at the same time.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the low transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) region, with species-dependent upper limits (e.g., $p_{\rm T}\leq 3.9$ GeV/c for pions, $p_{\rm T}\leq 2.5$ GeV/c for protons), the $p_{\rm T}$ spectra of mesons and baryons in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV exhibit a universal scaling independent of collision centrality when a dilatation $p_{\rm T}\rightarrow p_{\rm T}/K$ is applied, but this scaling is violated in the high $p_{\rm T}$ region, with the violation becoming more obvious in more peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors applied a scaling transformation, $p_{\rm T}\rightarrow p_{\rm T}/K$, to the published ALICE data for pion, kaon, proton, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega$ spectra across seven centrality classes (0-5% to 80-100%). They used the quality factor (QF) method to determine the scaling parameters $K$ and $A$ for each centrality class, with $K$ and $A$ set to 1 for the most central (0-5%) class. The scaling functions were parameterized using double-Tsallis distributions for pions, kaons, and protons, and single-Tsallis distributions for $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega$, fitted to the 0-5% centrality data.

## Key numbers

*   Universal scaling holds in the low $p_{\rm T}$ region up to $p_{\rm T}\leq 3.9$ GeV/c for pions, $p_{\rm T}\leq 3.1$ GeV/c for kaons, $p_{\rm T}\leq 2.5$ GeV/c for protons, $p_{\rm T}\leq 2.7$ GeV/c for $\Lambda$, $p_{\rm T}\leq 2.4$ GeV/c for $\Xi$, and $p_{\rm T}\leq 2.8$ GeV/c for $\Omega$, where almost all data points agree with the fitted scaling curve within 20%.
*   The scaling parameter $K$ for pions at the 80-100% centrality class is $0.77\pm0.01$, and for protons at the same centrality is $0.661\pm0.004$.
*   The mean scaled transverse momentum $\langle z \rangle$ for pions is $0.548\pm0.008$ GeV/c, and for $\Omega$ is $1.756\pm0.163$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the observation of a universal scaling of $p_{\rm T}$ spectra, previously seen in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV by the same group (Liu et al., 2018), to the smaller system of p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. It also builds on earlier findings of energy-independent scaling in pp collisions (Liu et al., 2015). The scaling is quantitatively explained within the colour string percolation (CSP) model, where mesons and baryons are generated from the decay of clusters with different mean sizes. The results enable further tests of the CSP model and the role of final state effects in small collision systems.
