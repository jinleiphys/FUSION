# Weak response of nuclei

> We discuss some differences and similarities between electron and neutrino scattering off atomic nuclei. We find that, in the giant resonance region, the two processes excite different nuclear modes, therefore the weak and the electromagnetic nuclear responses are rather different. In any case, the scattering of electrons and photons is the best guide we have to test the validity of our nuclear models and their prediction power. The experience in describing electromagnetic excitations of the nucleus, suggests that, when the nucleus is excited in the continuum, the re-interaction between the emitted nucleon and the remaining nucleus should not be neglected. A simple model taking into account this final state interaction is proposed, and applied to the neutrino scattering off 16O nucleus.

## Key claim

The weak and electromagnetic nuclear responses are rather different in the giant resonance region because they excite different nuclear modes, even for charge-conserving neutrino scattering where the basic particle-hole transitions are the same; this difference arises because neutrino scattering is dominated by the axial transverse operator, which is absent in electromagnetic excitations.

## Method

The authors calculated electron and neutrino double differential cross sections off ¹⁶O in first-order plane wave Born approximation using a Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) with a zero-range Landau-Migdal interaction, summing multipole excitations up to J=6. To account for final state interactions (FSI), they developed a phenomenological folding model where the CRPA response is convolved with an energy-dependent function whose parameters were fixed to reproduce photoabsorption data in the giant resonance region and optical potential values in the quasi-elastic region.

## Key numbers

- For electron scattering off ¹⁶O at εᵢ = 50 MeV and θ = 30°, the 1⁻ excitation contributes 93% of the cross section, with 6% from 2⁺ and about 1% from 0⁺.
- For charge-conserving neutrino scattering (ν,ν') off ¹⁶O at the same kinematics, the multipole decomposition is: 58% 2⁻, 33% 1⁻, 6% 0⁻, 2% 1⁺, and about 1% 3⁺.
- The FSI model parameters for the giant resonance region are A_Γ = 6 MeV and B_Γ = 60 MeV at ω = 10 MeV, evolving linearly to asymptotic values A_Γ = 11 MeV, B_Γ = 20 MeV, and C_Γ = 110 MeV above ω = 40 MeV.
- The relative reduction of the total cross section due to FSI is about 35% for neutrinos of 20 MeV and reaches about 10% above 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA and FSI modeling of electron scattering (Co' 1988, Amaro 1994) to neutrino-nucleus reactions, directly comparing the two probes. The results contradict the naive expectation that electron scattering benchmarks guarantee accurate neutrino cross sections, showing that the axial current operator in weak interactions excites unnatural parity states not accessible to electrons. The findings enable improved predictions for supernova neutrino detection, with consequences currently under investigation.
