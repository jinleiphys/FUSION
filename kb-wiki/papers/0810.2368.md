# Structure of Neutron Star with a Quark Core

> The equation of state of de-confined quark matter within the MIT bag model is calculated. This equation of state is used to compute the structure of a neutron star with quark core. It is found that the limiting mass of the neutron star is affected considerably by this modification of the equation of state. Calculations are carried out for different choices of the bag constant.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a quark core in neutron star models, using the MIT bag model with both constant (B=90) and density-dependent bag constants, reduces the maximum mass compared to traditional neutron star models without a quark core, bringing the theoretical maximum mass closer to observational limits.

## Method
The authors calculated the equation of state for de-confined quark matter within the MIT bag model, assuming massless u and d quarks and a strange quark mass of 150 MeV. They constructed a mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter using the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium, with hadronic matter described by the UV14+TNI potential. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were then integrated numerically to compute the mass and radius of neutron stars with a quark core, using both a constant bag constant B=90 and a density-dependent bag constant parameterized to match the quark-hadron transition at an energy density of 1100 MeV fm^{-3}.

## Key numbers
- Strange quark mass assumed: m = 150 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter energy density: ε₀ = 156 MeV fm⁻³
- Transition energy density for hadron-quark phase transition: ε = 1100 MeV fm⁻³
- Transition baryon density: ρ_t = 0.98 fm⁻³
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³
- Bag constant at zero density: B₀ = 400 (units not specified in text)
- Asymptotic bag constant determined: B_∞ = 8.99 (units not specified in text)
- Constant bag constant used: B = 90 (units not specified in text)
- Pure quark core radius: approximately 8 km
- Mixed phase layer thickness: approximately 1.5 km

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by Bordbar and collaborators on neutron star structure without a quark core, and builds on Glendenning's (no year given) construction for nucleon-quark phase transitions in neutron stars. The authors compare their results for neutron stars with quark cores and strange stars (pure quark matter) against traditional neutron star models, finding that the quark core reduces the maximum mass and brings it closer to the observed maximum mass of neutron stars reported by Thorsett (1999). The density-dependent bag constant parameterization follows attempts by Adami (no year given), Jin (no year given), and Blaschke (no year given) to understand the density dependence of the bag constant.
