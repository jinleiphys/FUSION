# Thermal equation of state of polarized fermions in one dimension via complex chemical potentials

> We present a nonperturbative computation of the equation of state of polarized, attractively interacting, nonrelativistic fermions in one spatial dimension at finite temperature. We show results for the density, spin magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and Tan's contact. We compare with the second-order virial expansion, a next-to-leading-order lattice perturbation theory calculation, and interpret our results in terms of pairing correlations. Our lattice Monte Carlo calculations implement an imaginary chemical potential difference to avoid the sign problem. The thermodynamic results on the imaginary side are analytically continued to obtain results on the real axis. We focus on an intermediate- to strong-coupling regime, and cover a wide range of temperatures and spin imbalances.

## Key claim

The authors present a nonperturbative lattice Monte Carlo computation of the equation of state for polarized, attractively interacting nonrelativistic fermions in one dimension at finite temperature, using an imaginary chemical potential difference to circumvent the sign problem; the results are limited to finite temperatures and cannot reach the zero-temperature limit or arbitrary polarizations at fixed temperature due to the \(2\pi\) periodicity in \(\beta h_{\rm I}\).

## Method

The authors simulate the Gaudin-Yang model on a spatial lattice with \(N_x = 61\) sites and a temporal lattice with \(\tau = 0.05\), fixing the dimensionless coupling \(\lambda = \sqrt{\beta} g = 1.0\) and inverse temperature \(\beta = 8.0\). They introduce an imaginary chemical potential difference \(h_{\rm I} = -i h\) so that the fermion determinants become complex conjugates, yielding a non-negative probability measure, and then analytically continue the Monte Carlo results for density, spin magnetization, and Tan's contact from imaginary to real \(h\) using Padé-type fit functions.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coupling is fixed at \(\lambda = 1.0\), corresponding to an intermediate-to-strong-coupling regime.
- The inverse temperature is \(\beta = 8.0\) and the spatial lattice size is \(N_x = 61\).
- The imaginary asymmetry parameter \(\beta h_{\rm I}\) is varied over the full period \([-\pi, \pi]\), and the chemical potential parameter \(\beta \mu\) is varied in the interval \([-4.0, 4.0]\).
- For each Monte Carlo point, 1000 de-correlated samples were taken, ensuring statistical uncertainty below 10%.
- The noninteracting unpolarized density \(n_0\) is used as the scale for reporting \(n/n_0\) and \(m/n_0\); the contact \(C\) is scaled by \(C_0\), the unpolarized result at \(\beta \mu = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the complex-chemical-potential approach of Braun et al. (2012) to nonrelativistic fermions in one dimension, complementing exact Bethe ansatz solutions (OneDReview, OneDBooks) that are limited to zero temperature. The method serves as a proof of principle for applying imaginary chemical potentials to avoid the sign problem in spin-imbalanced systems, enabling future calculations in higher dimensions where phase transitions (e.g., Yang-Lee zeros) may complicate analytic continuation.
