# Universal strangeness production and size fluctuactions in small and large systems

> Strangeness production in high multiplicity events gives indications on the transverse size fluctuactions in nucleus-nucleus ($AA$), proton-nucleus ($pA$) and proton-proton ($pp$) collisions. In particular the behavior of strange particle hadronization in "small" ($pp,pA$) and "large" ($AA$) initial configurations of the collision can be tested for the specific particle species, for different centralities and for large fluctuations of the transverse size in $pA$ and $pp$ by using the recent ALICE data. A universality of strange hadron production emerges by introducing a dynamical variable proportional to the initial parton density in the transverse plane.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strangeness production in high-energy collisions of small systems (pp, pA) and large systems (AA) follows a universal behavior when the strangeness suppression factor γ_s is plotted not against collision energy, but against a dynamical variable proportional to the initial parton density in the transverse plane (such as initial entropy density or saturation scale Q²_s). The universality holds only when transverse size fluctuations in high-multiplicity events are properly accounted for, and the analysis remains model-dependent for small systems with large gluon field fluctuations.

## Method

The authors use the statistical hadronization model (SHM) with the strangeness suppression factor γ_s to describe hadron multiplicities. They eliminate the collision energy √s by combining the energy-dependent parametrizations of γ_s for pp and AA collisions (Eqs. 1.1, 1.2) with the energy-dependent parametrizations of charged-particle multiplicity dN_ch/dy (Eqs. 2.2, 2.3), and then express γ_s as a function of the initial entropy density s_0 derived from the 1D Bjorken hydrodynamical model. For high-multiplicity events, they incorporate transverse area fluctuations using Glauber Monte Carlo for AA and CGC-based relations for pp and pA (Fig. 3), then compare the resulting γ_s^n suppression factors with ALICE data on multi-strange hadron production.

## Key numbers

- For AA collisions: γ_s^A(s) = 1 - a_A exp(-b_A √(A√s)) with a_A = 0.606, b_A = 0.0209.
- For pp collisions: γ_s^p(s) = 1 - a_p exp(-b_p s^{1/4}) with a_p = 0.5595, b_p = 0.0242.
- For AA charged multiplicity: (2/N_part)(dN/dy)_{y=0}^{AA} = a(√s)^{0.3} + b with a = 0.7613, b = 0.0534.
- For pp charged multiplicity: (dN/dy)_{y=0}^{pp} = a(√s)^{0.22} + b with a = 0.797, b = 0.04123.
- Transverse radii used: R_A = 1.25 A^{1/3} fm for nuclei, R_p = 0.8 fm for proton, R_T = R_p (0.5 N̄_part)^{1/3} for pA with N̄_part ≈ 8 for pPb.
- Thermalization time assumed: τ_0 ≈ 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the observation by the ALICE collaboration (2017) of enhanced multi-strange hadron production in high-multiplicity pp collisions, previously seen only in Pb-Pb collisions. It builds on earlier theoretical suggestions by Castorina et al. (2015, 2016) that small and large systems produce similar final states. The paper contradicts the traditional view that strangeness suppression (γ_s < 1) is intrinsic to elementary collisions, showing instead that the suppression depends on the initial parton density in the transverse plane. The results enable a unified description of strangeness production across all collision systems, but the authors note that a more complete analysis incorporating higher-energy ALICE data is in progress due to model dependence in the transverse size of small systems with large gluon field fluctuations.
