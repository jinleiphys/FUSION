# The 35-plet Baryons from Chiral Soliton Models

> We investigate the 35-plet baryons from the chiral soliton models. We find that the coupling constant for the decay of 35-plet baryons with spin 3/2 to the decuplet baryons is surprisingly small, but that for the decay to 27-plet baryons with spin 3/2 is larger. We give all the masses and widths of 35-plet baryons with spin 5/2 and suggest candidates for all nonexotic members from the available particle listings. We also focus on $Δ_{5/2}$ and $Θ_2$, which are the lightest two baryons of 35-plet with spin 5/2 and with simplest minimal pentaquark configurations. Calculations show that $Γ_{Δ_{5/2}}<$380 MeV, compared with the results from SU(2) Skyrme Model ($Γ_{Δ_{5/2}}>$800 MeV), and $Γ_{Θ_{2}}<100$ MeV if we assume that their widths are dominated by two-body decay and that $Θ^+$ has a width $Γ_{Θ^+}<25$ MeV.

## Key claim

The coupling constant for the decay of 35-plet baryons with spin 3/2 to the decuplet baryons is surprisingly small, but that for the decay to 27-plet baryons with spin 3/2 is larger; the 35-plet with spin 5/2 has coupling constants larger than those of the antidecuplet, enabling broader widths. The authors find that the width of Δ₅/₂ is less than 380 MeV, compared with the SU(2) Skyrme Model prediction of greater than 800 MeV, and the width of Θ₂ is less than 100 MeV, assuming their widths are dominated by two-body decay and that Θ⁺ has a width less than 25 MeV.

## Method

The authors used SU(3) chiral soliton models (Skyrme model and chiral quark-soliton model) with collective coordinate quantization to study the 35-plet baryons. They calculated masses using the Hamiltonian with symmetry breaking terms treated by first-order perturbation theory, and estimated widths via the Goldberger-Treiman relation connecting the axial coupling operator to the Yukawa coupling constants. Parameters were fixed model-independently from experimental data, including the reported narrow Ξ⁻π⁻ baryon resonance with mass 1.862 ± 0.003 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Δ₅/₂ predicted mass: 1.68 GeV (lightest 35-plet baryon with spin 5/2)
- Θ₂ predicted mass: 1.84 GeV (35-plet with spin 5/2)
- Γ(Δ₅/₂) < 380 MeV (compared with SU(2) Skyrme Model prediction Γ > 800 MeV)
- Γ(Θ₂) < 100 MeV (assuming Γ(Θ⁺) < 25 MeV and two-body decay dominance)
- Γ(Θ₂ → ΔK) < 65 MeV
- Γ(Θ₂ → Θ*π) < 35 MeV
- Γ(Δ₅/₂ → Δπ) < 380 MeV
- Δ(1905) candidate for Δ in 35-plet with spin 5/2: mass 1.87 to 1.92 GeV (PDG)
- Σ(2070) candidate for Σ in 35-plet with spin 5/2: mass ≈ 2.07 GeV
- Ξ(2250) candidate for Ξ in 35-plet with spin 5/2: mass ≈ 2.25 GeV
- Ω(2250)⁻ candidate for Ω in 35-plet with spin 5/2: mass 2.252 ± 0.009 GeV
- Parameters used: α = -663 MeV, β = -12 MeV, γ = 185 MeV, 1/I₁ = 154 MeV, 1/I₂ = 399 MeV, M_cl = 798 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model predictions for pentaquark baryons beyond the antidecuplet studied by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and the 27-plet studied by Wu and Ma (2004). It provides the first systematic calculation of masses and widths for the 35-plet baryons with spin 5/2 and 3/2, which are the last SU(3) multiplets with exoticness X=1 that may correspond to pentaquark states. The results enable experimental searches for the predicted Δ₅/₂ and Θ₂ states, which have simpler pentaquark configurations than other 35-plet members, and test the validity of chiral soliton models for exotic baryon multiplets.
