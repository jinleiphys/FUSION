# Strength fragmentation of Gamow-Teller transitions and delayed neutron emission of atomic nuclei

> Starting from a Skyrme interaction with tensor terms, the $β$-decay rates of $^{52}$Ca have been studied within a microscopic model including the $2p-2h$ configuration effects. We observe a redistribution of the strength of Gamow-Teller transitions due to the $2p-2h$ fragmentation. Taking into account this effect results in a satisfactory description of the neutron emission probability of the $β$-decay in $^{52}$Ca.

## Key claim
Including two-phonon (2p-2h) configuration effects in the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) framework, starting from a Skyrme energy-density functional with tensor terms, redistributes the Gamow-Teller strength and reduces the predicted delayed neutron emission probability for \(^{52}\)Ca, yielding a "satisfactory" but not exact description of the experimental neutron emission probability.

## Method
The author used the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) for the residual interaction, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a space of one- and two-phonon QRPA states (the latter built from \(1^+ \otimes 2^+\) configurations). The Skyrme interaction T45, which includes tensor terms, was employed for the mean field, with pairing correlations generated by a zero-range volume force (strength -315 MeV fm\(^3\), smooth cutoff at 10 MeV above Fermi energies). The \(\beta\)-decay half-life and delayed neutron emission probability \(P_n\) were computed from the Gamow-Teller strength within the \(Q_\beta\)-window, using the allowed GT approximation and the statistical independence of \(\beta\)-decay and particle emission.

## Key numbers
- The calculated \(\log ft\) value for the first \(1^+\) state of \(^{52}\)Sc (excitation energy 1.3 MeV in the two-phonon model) is 4.3, compared to the experimental value of \(4.2 \pm 0.1\).
- The two-phonon model yields a \(\log ft\) of 6.4 for the second \(1^+\) state (experiment: \(4.5 \pm 0.2\)) and 9.2 for the third \(1^+\) state (experiment: \(5.3 \pm 0.5\)).
- The fourth \(1^+\) state has a calculated \(\log ft\) of 3.3 (experiment: \(4.0 \pm 0.5\)) and an excitation energy of 4.9 MeV (experiment: 4.27 MeV).
- The delayed neutron emission probability \(P_n\) from the two-phonon calculation is 5%, which is "almost three times less" than the one-phonon (QRPA) approximation value.

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) approach to charge-exchange excitations, building on earlier FRSA developments by Severyukhin et al. (2012, 2013, 2014) and the Skyrme tensor-force parameterization of TIJ (2009). It addresses the observed shell effects in exotic calcium isotopes (Wienholtz 2013, Steppenbeck 2013) and the \(\beta\)-decay data of \(^{52}\)Ca from Huck et al. (1985). The inclusion of two-phonon fragmentation improves agreement with the measured \(P_n\) value compared to the one-phonon QRPA, and the method can be applied to other neutron-rich nuclei relevant for r-process modeling and radioactive beam experiments.
