# Dissociation rates of J/psi's with comoving mesons - thermal vs. nonequilibrium scenario

> We study J/psi dissociation processes in hadronic environments. The validity of a thermal meson gas ansatz is tested by confronting it with an alternative, nonequilibrium scenario. Heavy ion collisions are simulated in the framework of the microscopic transport model UrQMD, taking into account the production of charmonium states through hard parton-parton interactions and subsequent rescattering with hadrons. The thermal gas and microscopic transport scenarios are shown to be very dissimilar. Estimates of J/psi survival probabilities based on thermal models of comover interactions in heavy ion collisions are therefore not reliable.

## Key claim

Estimates of J/psi survival probabilities based on thermal models of comover interactions in heavy ion collisions are not reliable because the thermal gas and microscopic transport scenarios are shown to be very dissimilar, particularly in the early stage of the reaction where most dissociation occurs.

## Method

The authors simulated central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon using the microscopic transport model UrQMD, producing charmonium states through hard parton-parton interactions and inserting them into the evolving hadronic environment at appropriate space-time points. They compared the resulting J/psi dissociation rates and collision energies as functions of time against those calculated from an ideal thermal gas of pions and rho mesons with Bose-Einstein distributions, using a fixed dissociation cross section of 2.41 mb for all meson species based on the additive quark model.

## Key numbers

- In central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon, the total J/psi dissociation rate reaches a maximum of more than 0.1 c/fm at about 1 fm/c after the reaction begins, then drops an order of magnitude within the first 10 fm/c.
- Pions and rho mesons together account for only 37% of the total comover absorption (pions 18.5%, rho mesons 18.4%), with kaons contributing 10.7%, K*(892) 7.4%, eta and omega mesons each 6.4%, and the ten most dominant channels summing to 83.7%.
- The average J/psi-meson collision energies in the early stage (t approximately 1 fm/c) correspond to temperatures greater than 800 MeV in a thermal gas, while the rates at that time roughly correspond to a thermal pion and rho gas with T approximately 220 MeV.
- In the later stage (t greater than 2 fm/c), the rates correspond to a temperature of T = 140 +/- 20 MeV in the equilibrated system.
- The kinematic threshold for J/psi-meson dissociation is assumed to be E_th = 2m_D = 3730 MeV, while the minimum J/psi-rho collision energy in the thermal model is 3870 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of charmonium suppression as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation, following the proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) and experimental reports from the NA50 collaboration on anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb reactions at SPS energies. The authors directly test the assumption of thermal hadronic environments used in prior comover interaction models by Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi and Satz (1997) and by Armesto and Capella (1998), and they contrast their nonequilibrium approach with thermal model calculations of strangeness production by Koch, Muller and Rafelski (1986). The results enable future work using energy-dependent dissociation cross sections from meson exchange models, such as those by Matinian and Muller (1998), within microscopic transport frameworks to obtain more reliable charmonium survival probabilities.
