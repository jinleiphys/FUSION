# Improved determination of Heavy Quarkonium magnetic dipole transitions in pNRQCD

> We compute the magnetic dipole transitions between low lying Heavy Quarkonium states in a model independent way. We use the weak-coupling version of the effective field theory named potential NRQCD with the static potential exactly incorporated in the leading order Hamiltonian. ## Key claim

The authors compute magnetic dipole transitions between low-lying heavy quarkonium states using the weak-coupling version of potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) with the static potential exactly incorporated in the leading-order Hamiltonian, achieving precision of \(k_\gamma^3/m^2 \times \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2, v^2)\) for allowed transitions and \(k_\gamma^3/m^2 \times \mathcal{O}(v^4)\) for forbidden transitions; however, the convergence for the 2S bottomonium decays is less conclusive, and for the \(\Upsilon(2S) \to \eta_b(2S)\,\gamma\) decay they do not find convergence at all.

## Method

The authors solve the SchrĂśdinger equation with the static potential (known up to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^4)\) including leading ultrasoft corrections) exactly in the leading-order Hamiltonian, treating spin-dependent potentials as perturbations. They use the renormalon-subtracted (RS') scheme for the heavy quark mass and static potential to cancel renormalon ambiguities, and resum large logarithms associated with the heavy quark mass scale. Matrix elements of \(\vec{p}^{\,2}\), \(\vec{r}^{\,2}\), and the spin-dependent potential are computed numerically from the exact wavefunctions, and the decay widths are evaluated using formulas from Brambilla (2005) with modifications for the exact static potential, \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) anomalous magnetic moment, and LL-resummed spin-dependent potential.

## Key numbers

- For the bottomonium ground state, the RS' mass is \(m_{b,\mathrm{RS'}}(0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 4902\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_{b,\mathrm{RS'}}(1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 4859\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the charmonium ground state, the RS' mass is \(m_{c,\mathrm{RS'}}(0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 1648\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_{c,\mathrm{RS'}}(1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 1536\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The reference value for the strong coupling is \(\alpha_s^{(n_f=3)}(1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 0.479778\), obtained by running from \(\alpha_s^{(n_f=5)}(M_Z) = 0.118\).
- The renormalon subtraction constant is \(N_m = 0.574974\) (for three light flavors) with a 10% uncertainty.
- The electromagnetic radius \(\langle r^2 \rangle\) is found to be nicely convergent in all cases (1S, 1P, 2S bottomonium and 1S charmonium), while \(\langle p^2 \rangle\) is convergent except for the 2S bottomonium state.

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD analysis of magnetic dipole transitions by Brambilla (2005), which used the strict weak-coupling (Coulomb) approximation and found large errors for ground-state observables and disagreement with experimental bounds for the hindered transition \(\Upsilon(2S) \to \eta_b(1S)\,\gamma\). The new expansion scheme, first applied to inclusive electromagnetic decays by Kiyo (2010), exactly incorporates the static potential into the leading-order Hamiltonian, improving convergence and reducing factorization scale dependence. The results enable solid predictions for ground-state and 1P transitions, resolve the previous disagreement for \(\Upsilon(2S) \to \eta_b(1S)\,\gamma\), and confirm the renormalon dominance picture by showing that \(\langle r^2 \rangle\) and \(\langle p^2 \rangle\) (which are insensitive to a constant shift in the potential) converge well even when the binding energy does not.
