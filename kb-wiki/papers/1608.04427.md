# Ordering of the $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ proton levels in light nuclei

> A survey of the available single-proton data in $A\leq17$ nuclei, along with calculations using a Woods-Saxon potential, show that the ordering of the $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ proton orbitals are determined primarily by the proximity of the $s$-state proton energy to the Coulomb barrier. This is analogous to the dependence of the corresponding neutron orbitals in proximity to the neutron threshold, that was previously discussed.

## Key claim

The ordering of the $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ proton orbitals in light nuclei ($A\leq17$) is determined primarily by the proximity of the $s$-state proton energy to the Coulomb barrier, analogous to the previously observed dependence of neutron orbitals near the neutron threshold; however, the effect for protons occurs when the $s$-state energies are in the continuum near the potential barrier height rather than near the particle threshold itself.

## Method

The authors surveyed available single-proton data for ten nuclei with $Z=5-9$ and $N=4-8$, compiling $1/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ excitation energies and converting them to single-proton energies relative to the proton separation threshold. They performed Woods-Saxon potential calculations with fixed geometry parameters ($r_0=1.25$ fm, $a_0=0.63$ fm) and a spin-orbit strength fixed to reproduce the $^{17}$O neutron $1/2^+$-$5/2^+$ energy difference, then compared calculated and experimental $1s_{1/2}$-$0d_{5/2}$ energy differences after subtracting the Coulomb barrier height from the proton energies.

## Key numbers

- In $^{17}$F, the calculated proton $1/2^+$-$5/2^+$ energy difference is 0.42 MeV, in fair agreement with the experimental value of 0.495 MeV.
- The crossing of the $\pi1s_{1/2}$ and $\pi0d_{5/2}$ orbitals in the Woods-Saxon calculation occurs at around 0.45 MeV, approximately 1.0 MeV below the Coulomb barrier peak (which is $\sim$1.5 MeV for $^{16}$O+$p$).
- The $1/2^+$ proton energies change by $\sim$2 MeV from $N=4$ to $N=8$, while the $5/2^+$ energies change by $\sim$5 MeV over the same neutron number range.
- The neutron $1s_{1/2}$ and $0d_{5/2}$ orbital crossing occurs at around -1.25 MeV in the Woods-Saxon calculation.

## Context

This work extends the finding of Hoffman (2014) that neutron $s$-state energies linger near the one-neutron threshold, showing that the same mechanism operates for protons near the Coulomb barrier peak. The study connects to prior work on proton halo states (Risager 1993, Jensen 2004, Jonson 2004), Coulomb displacement energies and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen 1969), and the Thomas-Ehrman shift (Thomas 1951, Ehrman 1951). The results enable future studies of proton level ordering in heavier nuclei and provide a framework for understanding how the potential geometry, rather than many-body correlations, governs single-particle level sequences near thresholds.
