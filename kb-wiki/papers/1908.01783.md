# The soft drop groomed jet radius at NLL

> We present results for the soft drop groomed jet radius $R_g$ at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. The radius of a groomed jet which corresponds to the angle between the two branches passing the soft drop criterion is one of the characteristic observables relevant for the precise understanding of groomed jet substructure. We establish a factorization formalism that allows for the resummation of all relevant large logarithms, which is based on demonstrating the all order equivalence to a jet veto in the region between the boundaries of the groomed and ungroomed jet. Non-global logarithms including clustering effects due to the Cambridge/Aachen algorithm are resummed to all orders using a suitable Monte Carlo algorithm. We perform numerical calculations and find a very good agreement with Pythia 8 simulations. We provide theoretical predictions for the LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the soft drop groomed jet radius distribution can be resummed to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy by establishing an all-order equivalence between the soft drop declustering algorithm and a jet veto procedure, but only in the limit where both the groomed jet radius parameter \(\theta_g \ll 1\) and the soft threshold \(z_{\rm cut} \ll 1\).

## Method

The authors develop a factorization formalism within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that separates the cross section into hard, collinear, soft, and collinear-soft functions, each describing radiation at distinct momentum scales. They prove that for small \(\theta_g\) and \(z_{\rm cut}\), the soft drop grooming condition on the Cambridge/Aachen (C/A) clustering tree is equivalent to a veto on emissions outside the groomed jet radius. Non-global logarithms (NGLs) and Abelian clustering logarithms arising from the C/A algorithm are resummed to leading logarithmic (LL) accuracy and leading color using a Monte Carlo algorithm following the approach of Dasgupta (2001) and Neill (2018).

## Key numbers

* The collinear function at NLO contains a double logarithm term \(\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} C_i \frac{L^2}{2}\) where \(L = \ln(\mu^2 / (\theta_g^2 p_T^2 R^2))\) and \(C_i = C_F\) for quarks, \(C_A\) for gluons.
* The collinear-soft function at NLO yields a double logarithm \(-\frac{\alpha_s C_i}{2\pi} \frac{1}{1+\beta} \left[\frac{1}{2} \ln^2\left(\frac{\mu^2}{z_{\rm cut}^2 \theta_g^{2(1+\beta)} p_T^2 R^2}\right) - \frac{\pi^2}{12}\right]\).
* The leading NGL coefficient at NNLO without clustering effects is \(S_{i,2} = - C_i C_A \frac{\pi^2}{3}\), corresponding to \(\sim \alpha_s^2 \ln^2(z_{\rm cut} \theta_g^\beta)\).
* The natural collinear scale is \(\mu_C \sim \theta_g \, p_T R\) and the natural collinear-soft scale is \(\mu_S^{\rm gr} \sim z_{\rm cut} \theta_g^{1+\beta} p_T R\).

## Context

This work extends the modified leading-logarithmic (MLL) calculation of the soft drop groomed jet radius by Larkoski (2014) to NLL accuracy, using a factorization framework suitable for higher-order extensions. The equivalence to a jet veto builds on jet veto resummation techniques from Banfi (2010) and Liu (2012), while the treatment of NGLs and clustering logarithms follows the Monte Carlo methods of Dasgupta (2001) and Appleby (2002). The results enable precise theoretical predictions for groomed jet substructure at the LHC and RHIC, including in heavy-ion collisions where grooming isolates jet quenching effects.
