# Testing Dirac-Brueckner models in collective flow of heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate differential in-plane and out-of-plane flow observables in heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies from $0.2÷2$ AGeV within the framework of relativistic BUU transport calculations. The mean field is based on microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations. We apply two different sets of DBHF predictions, those of ter Haar and Malfliet and more recent ones from the Tübingen group, which are similar in general but differ in details. The latter DBHF calculations exclude spurious contributions from the negative energy sector to the mean field which results in a slightly softer equation of state and a less repulsive momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleus potential at high densities and high momenta. For the application to heavy ion collisions in both cases non-equilibrium features of the phase space are taken into account on the level of the effective interaction. The systematic comparison to experimental data favours the less repulsive and softer model. Relative to non-relativistic approaches one obtains larger values of the effective nucleon mass. This produces a sufficient amount of repulsion to describe the differential flow data reasonably well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) mean fields, when applied to heavy-ion collisions via a non-equilibrium colliding nuclear matter (CNM) approximation, can describe differential collective flow observables reasonably well, but only if the spurious contributions from negative energy states are removed from the self-energies; the softer and less repulsive Tübingen DBHF model (DBT) is favored over the ter Haar-Malfliet model (DBHM) by comparison to experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport calculations for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.2 to 2 AGeV, using mean fields from two different Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations: those of ter Haar and Malfliet (DBHM) and those of the Tübingen group (DBT). To account for non-equilibrium phase-space anisotropies during the collision, the mean fields were applied in the Local Configuration Approximation (LCA), where the local momentum distribution is parametrized as two inter-penetrating Fermi spheres (colliding nuclear matter, CNM). The transport results were filtered through detector acceptance routines and compared to experimental data from the FOPI Collaboration.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density for DBHM: 0.164 fm⁻³; for DBT: 0.185 fm⁻³.
* Binding energy per nucleon for DBHM: -13.6 MeV; for DBT: -16.1 MeV.
* Compression modulus for DBHM: 250 MeV; for DBT: 230 MeV.
* Dirac effective mass at saturation for DBHM: 558 MeV; for DBT: 637 MeV.
* Non-relativistic effective mass ratio m*_NR/M at saturation for DBHM: 0.63; for DBT: 0.73.
* The scalar and vector self-energies in DBT are smaller by about 50-100 MeV compared to DBHM across the density range shown.
* The DB models reproduce the empirical optical potential up to laboratory energies of about 0.6-0.8 GeV, but fail to reproduce the saturation behavior at higher momenta.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Gaitanos et al. (1996, 1999) that found qualitative agreement with collective flow using DBHF mean fields, and responds to more detailed differential flow measurements from the FOPI Collaboration (2000). The paper contrasts the two DBHF approaches (ter Haar and Malfliet, 1987; Tübingen group, 1999) and shows that the removal of spurious negative-energy contributions in the DBT model yields a softer equation of state and less repulsive momentum dependence, which is better able to describe the data. The results enable a more rigorous test of microscopic DBHF predictions against non-relativistic approaches such as Skyrme parameterizations (e.g., soft/hard with K=200/380 MeV) and suggest that DBHF mean fields become unreliable above about 1 AGeV, where phenomenological suppression of the vector field is required.
