# Weak Interaction Rates Of sd-Shell Nuclei In Stellar Environment Calculated in the Proton-Neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation

> Allowed weak interaction rates for sd-shell nuclei in stellar environment are calculated using a generalized form of proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces. Twelve different weak rates are calculated for each nucleus as a function of temperature and density. This project consists of calculation of weak rates for a total of 709 nuclei with masses ranging from A = 18 to 100. This paper contains calculated weak rates for sd-shell nuclei. The calculated capture and decay rates take into consideration the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations. The results are also compared with earlier works. Particle emission processes from excited states, previously ignored, are taken into account, and are found to significantly affect some beta decay rates.

## Key claim

The paper calculates allowed weak interaction rates for sd-shell nuclei using a generalized proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces, claiming that including particle emission processes from excited states (previously ignored) significantly affects some beta decay rates, though the results are presented as a function of temperature and density without a single summary number for overall accuracy.

## Method

The authors employed a generalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable Gamow-Teller forces to compute twelve different weak interaction rates (including electron capture, beta decay, neutrino energy loss, gamma energy, proton emission, and particle emission probabilities) for each nucleus as a function of stellar temperature (T9, in units of 10^9 K) and density (Aden, in g/cm^3). The calculations incorporated the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations, and considered particle emission processes from excited states. The paper presents tabulated rates for sd-shell nuclei (A = 18 to 24) as part of a larger project covering 709 nuclei with masses from A = 18 to 100.

## Key numbers

- For the transition 18Ne -> 18Na at density 3.0 g/cm^3 and T9 = 1.0, the electron capture rate (E+Cap) is -100 (meaning negligible or not calculated), while at T9 = 30.0 it is -1.537.
- For 18Ne -> 18F at density 3.0 g/cm^3 and T9 = 1.0, the electron capture rate (E-Cap) is -4.323 and the positron decay rate (E+Dec) is -0.387.
- For 19Ne -> 19Na at density 3.0 g/cm^3 and T9 = 1.0, the electron capture rate (E+Cap) is -60.842 and the proton emission probability (PPEm) is -2.000.
- For 20Mg -> 20Na at density 3.0 g/cm^3 and T9 = 1.0, the electron capture rate (E-Cap) is -3.629 and the positron decay rate (E+Dec) is 1.193.
- For 24Si -> 24Al at density 3.0 g/cm^3 and T9 = 1.0, the electron capture rate (E-Cap) is -4.034 and the positron decay rate (E+Dec) is 0.792.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of weak interaction rates for sd-shell nuclei by using a generalized pn-QRPA model that includes particle emission from excited states, which previous studies (e.g., Fuller, Fowler, and Newman in the 1980s) had largely ignored. The inclusion of these particle emission processes is found to significantly affect some beta decay rates, particularly at higher temperatures where excited states are populated. The results provide a comprehensive set of weak rates for 709 nuclei (A = 18 to 100) that can be used in stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis models, enabling more accurate simulations of processes such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star crust evolution.
