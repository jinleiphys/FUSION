# Generalizing the calculable $R$-matrix theory and eigenvector continuation to the incoming wave boundary condition

> The calculable $R$-matrix theory has been formulated successfully for regular boundary conditions with vanishing radial wave functions at the coordinate origins [P. Descouvemont and D. Baye, Rept. Prog. Phys. 73, 036301 (2010)]. We generalize the calculable $R$-matrix theory to the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC), which is widely used in theoretical studies of low-energy heavy-ion fusion reactions to simulate the strong absorption of incoming flux inside the Coulomb barriers. The generalized calculable $R$-matrix theory also provides a natural starting point to extend eigenvector continuation (EC) [D. Frame et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 032501 (2018)] to fusion observables. The ${}^{14}\text{N}+{}^{12}\text{C}$ fusion reaction is taken as an example to validate these new theoretical tools. Both local and nonlocal potentials are considered in numerical calculations. Our generalizations of the calculable $R$-matrix theory and EC are found to work well for IWBC.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the calculable $R$-matrix theory and eigenvector continuation (EC) from regular boundary conditions to the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC), which is widely used in low-energy heavy-ion fusion reactions; the generalization works well for both local and nonlocal potentials, as validated by the example of the ${}^{14}\text{N}+{}^{12}\text{C}$ fusion reaction.

## Method

The configuration space is divided into three regions: Absorption Region $[0, r_\text{abs}]$, Internal Region $[r_\text{abs}, r_\text{c}]$, and External Region $[r_\text{c}, \infty)$. The Bloch-Schrodinger equation is solved variationally in the Internal Region using Lagrange functions that give nonzero values at the absorption radius, matching the IWBC wave functions in the Absorption and External Regions. For the eigenvector continuation extension, exact internal wave functions at selected training points in parameter space are used as basis functions to construct a variational emulator for fusion observables at test points.

## Key numbers

- The absorption radius is taken as $r_\text{abs} = A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3}$ for all partial waves.
- The channel radius is taken as $r_\text{c} = 20$ fm unless otherwise mentioned.
- For the local potential, $N \geq 20$ Lagrange functions are used; for the nonlocal potential, $N \geq 100$ Lagrange functions are used.
- The maximal partial-wave angular momentum is taken to be $L_\text{max} = 10$.
- The range of nonlocality is $\beta = \beta_0 / A_\text{red}$ with $\beta_0 = 0.85$ fm, giving $\beta \sim 0.13$ fm for the nonlocal potential.
- The local nuclear potential parameters are $V_0 = 60$ MeV, $R_0 = 1.206 (A_T^{1/3} + A_P^{1/3})$ fm, and $a = 0.5$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the calculable $R$-matrix theory of Descouvemont and Baye (2010) from regular boundary conditions to IWBC, which is a standard approach in low-energy heavy-ion fusion studies (e.g., the CCFULL code of Hagino et al., 1999). The generalization also enables the extension of eigenvector continuation, originally developed for bound-state observables by Frame et al. (2018), to fusion observables. This provides a unified framework for treating both local and nonlocal potentials in fusion reactions, filling a gap where previous methods (such as the modified Numerov method in CCFULL) could only handle local potentials.
