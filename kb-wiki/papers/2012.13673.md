# Correlation between the curvature and some properties of the neutron star

> According to the general theory of relativity, a massive body induces curvature in the surrounding spacetime. In this study, the surface curvature (SC) of neutron stars is computed using various curvature quantities derived from the relativistic mean-field, density-dependent RMF, and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations of states. Neutron star properties, including mass, radius, compactness, and central density, are calculated utilizing the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The analysis reveals a significant cubic correlation between the SC and compactness for the canonical 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ neutron star, with a correlation coefficient of 0.99, indicating an almost linear relationship. A similarly significant inverse cubic correlation is observed between the SC and the radius of the canonical star. However, these correlations diminish for the maximum mass NS. Furthermore, a universal relation between the SC and the dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$) for the canonical neutron star is established. Using the tidal deformability constraint of GW170817 ($Λ_{1.4} = 190_{-120}^{+390}$), the surface curvature is limited to SC$_{1.4} (10^{14}) = 2.87^{+0.30}_{-0.78}$ at a confidence level 90\%. Furthermore, the tidal deformability constraint of the secondary component in the GW190814 event ($Λ_{1.4} = 616_{-158}^{+273}$) offers a more stringent limit, with the result of SC$_{1.4} (10^{14}) = 2.03^{+0.27}_{-0.36}$. These findings indicate that the GW190814 event imposes more rigorous constraints on SC compared to GW170817.

## Key claim
The paper claims a significant cubic correlation between the surface curvature (SC) and compactness of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars, with a correlation coefficient of 0.99 indicating an almost linear relationship; however, this correlation diminishes for maximum mass neutron stars, and the universal relation between SC and tidal deformability is established specifically for the canonical star, not for all masses.

## Method
The authors computed neutron star properties (mass, radius, compactness, central density) by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using 30 relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameter sets, 15 Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) parameter sets, and 6 density-dependent RMF (DDRMF) parameter sets. Surface curvature was defined as the ratio of the Kretschmann scalar at the neutron star surface to that of the Sun, and correlations between SC and compactness, radius, and dimensionless tidal deformability were established using least-squares fitting.

## Key numbers
- Correlation coefficient between SC and compactness for canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars: 0.99, with fitting parameters a = 450.770 and b = 2.9806 for the relation SC = a * C^b
- Using the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (Lambda_1.4 = 190 with +390 / -120), the surface curvature is limited to SC_1.4 (10^14) = 2.87 with +0.30 / -0.78 at 90% confidence level
- Using the GW190814 tidal deformability constraint (Lambda_1.4 = 616 with +273 / -158), the surface curvature is limited to SC_1.4 (10^14) = 2.03 with +0.27 / -0.36
- Neutron star curvature is approximately 10^14 times greater than that of the Sun

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron star curvature by Kazim (2014) and Xiao (2015), who quantified curvature using the Kretschmann scalar and Weyl tensor, and by Das (2021) who examined curvature with dark matter inclusion. The paper establishes universal correlations between surface curvature and bulk properties (compactness, radius, tidal deformability) that can serve as auxiliary tools for constraining equations of state using gravitational wave observations from GW170817 and GW190814, complementing earlier correlations between tidal deformability and radius found by Malik (2018), Hebler (2010), and others.
