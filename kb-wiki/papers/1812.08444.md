# Dilute Fermi gas at fourth order in effective field theory

> Using effective field theory methods, we calculate for the first time the complete fourth-order term in the Fermi-momentum or $k_{\rm F} a_s$ expansion for the ground-state energy of a dilute Fermi gas. The convergence behavior of the expansion is examined for the case of spin one-half fermions and compared against quantum Monte-Carlo results, showing that the Fermi-momentum expansion is well-converged at this order for $| k_{\rm F} a_s | \lesssim 0.5$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of the fourth-order term in the Fermi-momentum expansion for the ground-state energy of a dilute Fermi gas using effective field theory methods, showing that the expansion is well-converged at this order for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 0.5\) but diverges for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \gtrsim 1\).

## Method

The authors applied effective field theory (EFT) with short-range contact interactions to many-body perturbation theory (MBPT), calculating all 39 fourth-order Hugenholtz diagrams for the ground-state energy density. They regularized ultraviolet divergences with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\), renormalized by introducing counterterms from the two-body effective-range expansion and the three-body potential, and evaluated the diagrams numerically using Monte-Carlo integration with dimensionless momenta scaled by the Fermi momentum \(k_{\rm F}\).

## Key numbers

* Fourth-order coefficient for spin-one-half fermions with \(r_s = a_p = 0\): \(\mathcal{C}_4(k_{\rm F}) = -0.0425(1) (k_{\rm F} a_s)^4\)
* Fourth-order coefficient for hard-sphere gas (\(a_s = 3r_s/2 = a_p\)): \(X_4 = +0.001\) (from \(X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4 = +0.354, +0.186, +0.384, +0.001\))
* Relative error with respect to quantum Monte-Carlo at \(k_{\rm F} a_s = -0.5\): 4.5% at first order, 0.8% at second, 0.4% at third, and 0.1% at fourth order
* Bertsch parameter range from Padé approximants: \(\xi_{\rm Padé} \in [0.33, 0.54]\), consistent with experimental value \(\xi \approx 0.376\)
* Logarithmic divergence coefficient: \(\beta = M^3 (4\pi - 3\sqrt{3})/(4\pi^3)\)

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-momentum expansion first calculated by Lenz (1929) for the leading term and by Lee and Yang (1957) for the second-order term, and later by Efimov (1966) and Kaiser (2011-2017) for the third-order term. The fourth-order result resolves discrepancies in earlier calculations by Baker (1965, 1971, 1999), showing that no non-ERE parameter is needed for spin-one-half fermions. The results provide benchmarks for cold atomic gas experiments, neutron star crust models, and low-density nuclear many-body calculations, and enable systematic uncertainty estimates for the ground-state energy of dilute Fermi systems.
