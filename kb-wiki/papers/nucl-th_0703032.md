# Hadron-hadron correlations at low and high $p_T$ in heavy-ion collisions

> The modification of two particle correlations within a jet due to its propagation through dense strongly interacting matter is explored. Different properties of the medium may be probed by varying the momentum of the detected hadrons. Very high transverse momentum ($p_T$) correlations sample the gluon density of the medium; the minimal modification on the same side as the trigger is consistent with the picture and parameters of partonic energy loss. Lower momentum hadrons, sensitive to the presence of composite structures in the medium may excite collective modes such as Cherenkov radiation, resulting in conical patterns in the detected correlations.

## Key claim

The modification of two-particle correlations within a jet can probe different properties of the dense strongly interacting medium depending on the momentum of the detected hadrons: very high transverse momentum correlations sample the gluon density and are consistent with partonic energy loss, while lower momentum hadrons may excite collective modes such as Cherenkov radiation, producing conical correlation patterns.

## Method

The author calculates the medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions using a framework similar to that for modified single hadron fragmentation functions, requiring no additional parameters. For hard-hard correlations, the associated hadron distribution is computed as the ratio of dihadron to single hadron fragmentation functions, integrated over initial jet energies weighted by production cross sections. For hard-soft correlations, the possibility of Cherenkov gluon radiation is explored using a scalar \(\Phi^3\) theory at finite temperature with three fields, where a massless field representing the gluon couples to two massive fields representing bound states in the medium.

## Key numbers

- In DIS off a nuclear target (N and Kr), the associated hadron distribution \(R_{2h}(z_2)\) at large \(z_2\) shows small suppression compared to the suppression of single fragmentation functions.
- For heavy-ion collisions, the associated yield is computed as a function of number of participants \(N_{part}\) with trigger \(p_T^{\rm trig}\) ranging from 8-15 GeV and associated momentum from 6 GeV \(< p_T^{\rm assoc} < p_T^{\rm trig}\).
- Theoretical predictions for the associated yield in central Au+Au collisions, using two extreme flavour assumptions (charged pions only versus p, \(\bar{p}\), K\(^+\), K\(^-\) and \(\pi^+\), \(\pi^-\) inclusive of all decays), bracket the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the framework of partonic energy loss in dense matter, building on prior studies of modified fragmentation functions by Guo and Wang and dihadron fragmentation functions by Majumder (2004). The hard-hard correlation analysis is compared to experimental data from the HERMES collaboration at DESY for DIS and from the STAR collaboration at RHIC for Au+Au collisions (Adams 2006). The hard-soft correlation analysis addresses the observed double-humped structure on the away side in central heavy-ion collisions reported by STAR (Adler 2005), proposing Cherenkov gluon radiation as a possible explanation, which builds on earlier work by Dremin and on the existence of coloured bound states in a deconfined plasma as suggested by Shuryak (2004).
