# CT25: Progress toward next-generation PDFs for precision phenomenology at the LHC

> We summarize recent progress toward the next generation of CTEQ-TEA parton distribution functions, CT25, based on a global NNLO analysis that incorporates a significant sample of newly included LHC data. We present a baseline fit within the forthcoming full CT25 fit, which includes new Drell-Yan, top-pair, and inclusive-jet data at 8 and 13 TeV, and exhibits non-trivial pulls on the high-$x$ gluon and the flavor structure of the quark sea. In the context of progress toward CT25, we also summarize several recent and ongoing studies of the interplay between phenomenological PDFs and lattice-QCD calculations, simultaneous extractions of $α_s(M_Z)$ within the CT framework, and an expanded program of uncertainty quantification that treats parametrization dependence as an explicit source of epistemic uncertainty, among other issues. We also briefly highlight CT efforts to understand the effects of partial implementations of N$^3$LO corrections into PDF fits, which include benchmark calculations for Higgs and vector-boson processes. We comment on the implications of recent improvements to the CT analysis for precision phenomenology at the LHC and future facilities.

## Key claim

The CT25 baseline fit, a successor to CT18 (Hou et al., 2019), incorporates new LHC Drell-Yan, top-pair, and inclusive-jet data at 8 and 13 TeV, producing non-trivial pulls on the high-x gluon and the flavor structure of the quark sea, but the presented results are a preliminary baseline within the forthcoming full CT25 release and are determined with one selection of PDF functional forms using a specific dynamical tolerance prescription.

## Method

The CTEQ-TEA collaboration performed a global NNLO QCD analysis, adding new LHC data sets including Drell-Yan pair-production experiments at 5, 8, and 13 TeV from ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb; 13 TeV top-pair production cross sections differential in rapidity and invariant mass from ATLAS and CMS; and 531 data points from inclusive-jet cross-section measurements at 13 TeV from ATLAS and CMS. The analysis used a Hessian approach with a dynamical tolerance prescription for PDF uncertainties, and explored sensitivity to PDF parametrizations using flexible analytic forms including Bezier curves and machine-learning-based approaches. The strong coupling was determined simultaneously with PDFs using multiple methods, including generation of a chi-squared profile via a single global fit and comparison of values from multiple fits employing independent PDF parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant is determined as alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1183 +/- 0.00225 at NNLO, derived by synthesizing multiple extraction methods using different statistical models.
- The CT25 baseline includes 531 data points from inclusive-jet cross-section measurements at 13 TeV from ATLAS and CMS.
- The analysis includes several dozen points on 13 TeV top-pair production cross section, differential in both top-pair rapidity and invariant mass.
- The CT25 PDF ensemble is determined using alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118 and a dynamical tolerance prescription; the impact from approximately 300 alternative PDF parametrizations on the chi-squared profile is small and compatible with the Delta chi-squared = 1 criterion.
- The CT25 gluon PDF at x = 0.01-0.1 is mildly lower than CT18A, relevant for Higgs and other LHC measurements.

## Context

This work extends the CT18 global PDF analysis (Hou et al., 2019) by incorporating new LHC Run-2 data and improved methodology for uncertainty quantification, including treatment of parametrization dependence as an explicit source of epistemic uncertainty. The CT25 baseline is compared with MSHT20 and NNPDF4.0 error ensembles, and the analysis explores synergies between phenomenological PDFs and lattice-QCD calculations (Ablat et al., 2025), as well as partial implementations of N3LO corrections benchmarked against Higgs and vector-boson processes. The results enable improved precision phenomenology at the LHC and future facilities, with the full CT25 PDF set and comprehensive details to appear in a forthcoming long paper.
