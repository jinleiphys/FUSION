# The thermal index of neutron-star matter in the virial approximation

> Motivated by gravitational wave observations of binary neutron-star mergers, we study the thermal index of low-density, high-temperature dense matter. We use the virial expansion to account for nuclear interaction effects. We focus on the region of validity of the expansion, which reaches $10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$ at $T=5$ MeV up to almost saturation density at $T=50$ MeV. In pure neutron matter, we find an analytical expression for the thermal index, and show that it is nearly density- and temperature-independent, within a fraction of a percent of the non-interacting, non-relativistic value of $Γ_\text{th} \approx 5/3$. When we incorporate protons, electrons and photons, we find that the density and temperature dependence of the thermal index changes significantly. We predict a smooth transition between an electron-dominated regime with $Γ_\text{th} \approx 4/3$ at low densities to a neutron-dominated region with $Γ_\text{th} \approx 5/3$ at high densities. This behavior is by and large independent of proton fraction and is not affected by nuclear interactions in the region where the virial expansion converges. We model this smooth transition analytically and provide a simple but accurate parametrization of the inflection point between these regimes. When compared to tabulated realistic models of the thermal index, we find an overall agreement at high temperatures that weakens for colder matter. The discrepancies can be attributed to the missing contributions of nuclear clusters. The virial approximation provides a clear and physically intuitive framework for understanding the thermal properties of dense matter, offering a computationally efficient solution that makes it particularly well-suited for the regimes relevant to neutron star binary remnants.

## Key claim

The thermal index of pure neutron matter in the virial approximation is nearly density- and temperature-independent, remaining within a fraction of a percent of the non-interacting, non-relativistic value of Gamma_th approximately 5/3, but this near-constancy holds only within the low-density, high-temperature regime where the virial expansion converges (fugacity z less than 0.3).

## Method

The authors used the virial expansion for nuclear matter, truncating at second order for pure neutron matter and at second order for isospin-asymmetric npegamma matter, with third-order terms used only to estimate truncation errors. They computed second virial coefficients from neutron-neutron and neutron-proton elastic scattering phase shifts from the Granada database, applying charge-independence breaking corrections to obtain nn phase shifts from np data using effective range parameters. The thermal index Gamma_th was then obtained from the ratio of thermal pressure to thermal energy density in the virial EoS, and the results were compared to tabulated equations of state from the CompOSE database.

## Key numbers

* The virial expansion is valid for densities reaching 10^{-3} fm^{-3} at T=5 MeV up to almost saturation density at T=50 MeV (abstract).
* In pure neutron matter, the maximum deviation of the thermal index from the ideal gas value of Gamma_th = 5/3 is always less than 0.2% across several orders of magnitude in density (Section 3).
* The second virial coefficient for pure neutron matter is approximately 0.3 and remains essentially temperature independent, with its dimensionless temperature derivative less than 0.04 across the relevant temperature domain (Section 2.2).
* The truncation error from neglecting third-order virial coefficients is estimated to be approximately 0.05% as an average across different temperatures (Section 3).
* The thermal index transitions from an electron-dominated regime with Gamma_th approximately 4/3 at low densities to a neutron-dominated region with Gamma_th approximately 5/3 at high densities (abstract).

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion approach of Horowitz and collaborators (2006) to the thermal index of neutron-star matter, providing a model-independent framework for understanding thermal effects in binary neutron-star merger remnants. The results are compared to tabulated equations of state from the CompOSE database, showing overall agreement at high temperatures that weakens for colder matter due to missing contributions from nuclear clusters. The virial approximation offers a computationally efficient alternative to full finite-temperature many-body calculations for the regimes relevant to neutron star binary remnants, and the analytical parametrization of the thermal index transition can be directly used in hydrodynamical simulations.
