# The Ginzburg-Landau Theory and the Surface Energy of a Colour Superconductor

> We apply the Ginzburg-Landau theory to the colour superconducting phase of a lump of dense quark matter. We calculate the surface energy of a domain wall separating the normal phase from the super phase with the bulk equilibrium maintained by a critical external magnetic field. Because of the symmetry of the problem, we are able to simplify the Ginzburg-Landau equations and express them in terms of two components of the di-quark condensate and one component of the gauge potential. The equations also contain two dimensionless parameters: the Ginzburg-Landau parameter $κ$ and $ρ$. The main result of this paper is a set of inequalities obeyed by the critical value of the Ginzburg-Landau parameter--the value of $κ$ for which the surface energy changes sign--and its derivative with respect to $ρ$. In addition we prove a number of inequalities of the functional dependence of the surface energy on the parameters of the problem and obtain a numerical solution of the Ginzburg-Landau equations. Finally a criterion for the types of colour superconductivity (type I or type II) is established in the weak coupling approximation.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the critical Ginzburg-Landau parameter for colour superconductors, at which the surface energy of a normal-superconducting interface changes sign, satisfies the inequality κ_c(ρ) ≤ 1/√2, with the weak coupling approximation yielding κ_c ≈ 0.589 rather than the ordinary superconductor value of 0.707.

## Method

The authors apply Ginzburg-Landau theory to a domain wall separating normal and colour superconducting phases of dense quark matter, maintained in equilibrium by a critical external magnetic field. Using symmetry arguments, they reduce the full set of Ginzburg-Landau equations to a simplified system involving two components of the di-quark condensate (the colour-flavour locked component and the colour-flavour octet component) and one component of the gauge potential. They derive analytical inequalities for the critical Ginzburg-Landau parameter and its derivative with respect to the additional parameter ρ, and obtain numerical solutions by discretizing the field equations on a finite lattice and minimizing the surface energy functional through iteration.

## Key numbers

- The critical Ginzburg-Landau parameter for an ordinary superconductor is κ_c = 1/√2 ≈ 0.707 (Ginzburg and Landau, 1950).
- For colour superconductors in the weak coupling approximation (ρ = -1/2), the critical Ginzburg-Landau parameter is κ_c ≈ 0.589.
- The critical magnetic field for a homogeneous colour superconductor is H_c ≈ 1.47 × 10^20 (k_B T_c / μ)(1 - T_c/T) Gauss, estimated for μ = 400 MeV using the one-loop running coupling constant with Λ = 200 MeV.
- The Weinberg angle for the mixing of electromagnetic and colour gauge fields at μ = 400 MeV is θ ≈ -5.6 degrees.
- The Ginzburg-Landau parameter expressed in terms of microscopic quantities is κ ≈ (16.3/√α_s)(k_B T_c / μ).
- For μ = 400 MeV, the colour superconductor is type I if k_B T_c < 14 MeV and type II otherwise; extrapolating weak coupling formulas gives k_B T_c = 3.5 MeV, implying type I behaviour.

## Context

This work extends the classic Ginzburg-Landau analysis of surface energy in ordinary superconductors (Ginzburg and Landau, 1950) and the subsequent classification into type I and type II superconductors (Abrikosov, 1957) to the colour superconducting phase of dense quark matter. It builds upon earlier Ginzburg-Landau treatments of colour superconductivity by Iida and Baym (2001) and Giannakis and Ren (2002), and provides a criterion for distinguishing type I from type II colour superconductors. The results enable future studies of vortex structures in colour superconductors and their implications for neutron star physics, particularly regarding whether magnetic fields inside neutron stars can penetrate the colour superconducting phase.
