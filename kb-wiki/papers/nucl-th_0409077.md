# The Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation and Its Analog: the Breit-like Equation

> We take ($μ^\pm e^\mp$) systems and consider the states with quantum number $J^P=0^-$ as examples, to explore the different contents of the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation and its analog, Breit equation, by solving them exactly. The results show that the two equations are not equivalent, although they are analogous. Furthermore, we point out that the Breit equation contains extra un-physical solutions, so it should be abandoned if one wishes to have an accurate description of the bound states for the instantaneous interacting binding systems.

## Key claim

The instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation and the Breit equation are not equivalent, despite their formal analogy; the Breit equation contains extra un-physical solutions (type-B solutions with non-zero \(f_3\) and \(f_4\)) that the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation does not, specifically for systems with unequal masses such as (\(\mu^\pm e^\mp\)).

## Method

The authors solved both the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation and the Breit equation exactly for (\(\mu^\pm e^\mp\)) systems with quantum number \(J^P = 0^-\) (S-wave bound states). They expanded the wave functions \(f_i\) (\(i=1,2,3,4\)) in a basis of exact S-wave solutions of the Schrödinger equation (momentum-space Coulomb wavefunctions \(R_{nl}\)), truncated the expansion at \(j=5\) and also at \(j=10\) for stability checks, and diagonalized the resulting matrix eigenvalue problem to obtain eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for both equations.

## Key numbers

- Trivial (type-A) eigenvalues for (\(\mu^\pm e^\mp\)) systems (in eV): \(-13.5410866\), \(-3.3863112\), \(-1.5048207\), \(-0.8467912\), \(-0.5420610\) (from the \(j=5\) truncation).
- Non-trivial (type-B) eigenvalues for (\(\mu^\pm e^\mp\)) systems (in eV): approximately \(-1.022015012 \times 10^6\), \(-1.021999529 \times 10^6\), \(-1.021998308 \times 10^6\), \(-1.021997962 \times 10^6\), \(-1.021997837 \times 10^6\) (from the \(j=5\) truncation).
- The non-trivial eigenvalues have binding energies almost as large as \(2m_e\) (two times the electron mass).
- Overall renormalization constants for the trivial solutions: \(45778.501\), \(45778.730\), \(45778.772\), \(45778.787\), \(45778.794\).
- Overall renormalization constants for the non-trivial solutions: \(1.240240535 \times 10^7\), \(3.652200818 \times 10^7\), \(6.728317282 \times 10^7\), \(1.215161253 \times 10^8\), \(2.757689877 \times 10^8\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier arguments (Chang, 2004) that the Breit equation is an improper replacement for the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, as previously used by Salpeter (1952) and Itzykson and Zuber (1980). By providing exact numerical solutions for a concrete system (\(\mu^\pm e^\mp\)), the paper demonstrates that the Breit equation introduces spurious deep-binding solutions absent from the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. This enables future work to confidently use the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for relativistic bound-state calculations without the contamination of un-physical states, while cautioning against using the Breit equation for complete energy spectra or relativistic corrections.
