# Comparison of Heavy-Ion Transport Simulations: Mean-field Dynamics in a Box

> Within the transport model evaluation project (TMEP) of simulations for heavy-ion collisions, the mean-field response is examined here. Specifically, zero-sound propagation is considered for neutron-proton symmetric matter enclosed in a periodic box, at zero temperature and around normal density. The results of several transport codes belonging to two families (BUU-like and QMD-like) are compared among each other and to exact calculations. For BUU-like codes, employing the test particle method, the results depend on the combination of the number of test particles and the spread of the profile functions that weight integration over space. These parameters can be properly adapted to give a good reproduction of the analytical zero-sound features. QMD-like codes, using molecular dynamics methods, are characterized by large damping effects, attributable to the fluctuations inherent in their phase-space representation. Moreover, for a given nuclear effective interaction, they generally lead to slower density oscillations, as compared to BUU-like codes. The latter problem is mitigated in the more recent lattice formulation of some of the QMD codes. The significance of these results for the description of real heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The mean-field response of transport codes for heavy-ion collisions, tested via zero-sound propagation in a periodic box of symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature and normal density, shows that BUU-like codes can reproduce analytical zero-sound features when test-particle number and profile-function spread are properly tuned, whereas QMD-like codes exhibit larger damping and slower density oscillations due to inherent fluctuations and the localized wave-packet representation.

## Method

The authors initialized a standing-wave density perturbation in neutron-proton symmetric nuclear matter enclosed in a periodic box at zero temperature and normal density, with momenta assigned randomly within the local Fermi sphere. They then propagated the wave using only the Vlasov (mean-field) part of nine BUU-type and five QMD-type transport codes, employing density functionals that yield identical equation-of-state features, and compared the resulting oscillation frequencies, damping, and response functions via spatial and temporal Fourier transforms against each other and against exact results from Landau theory.

## Key numbers

- The number of test particles per nucleon was set to 100 for BUU-type codes.
- The width parameter (Delta x)^2 for Gaussian wavepackets in QMD codes was 2 fm^2; for BUU codes, profile half-base lengths (l) or (Delta x)^2 ranged from 0 (point particles) to 6.25 fm^2 depending on the code.
- The saturation density rho_0 is the reference density around which the box simulations were performed (exact numerical value not given in the text).
- The number of BUU-type codes compared was nine; the number of QMD-type codes compared was five.

## Context

This work is part of the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP), extending earlier comparisons of Au+Au collisions (Xu et al., 2016) and box studies of the collision integral (comp2, comp3) to isolate and test mean-field dynamics separately. The results enable systematic benchmarking of transport codes against exact kinetic-theory limits and identify specific numerical parameters (test-particle number, profile width, lattice formulation) that can be adjusted to improve agreement, thereby increasing the reliability of heavy-ion collision simulations used to extract the nuclear equation of state.
