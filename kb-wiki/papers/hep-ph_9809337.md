# N/D Description of Two Meson Amplitudes and Chiral Symmetry

> The most general structure of an elastic partial wave amplitude when the unphysical cuts are neglected is deduced in terms of the N/D method. This result is then matched to lowest order, ${\mathcal{O}}(p^2)$, Chiral Perturbation Theory($χ$PT) and to the exchange (consistent with chiral symmetry) of resonances in the s-channel. The extension of the method to coupled channels is also given. Making use of the former formalism, the $ππ$ and $Kπ$(I=1/2) P-wave scattering amplitudes are described without free parameters when taking into account relations coming from the 1/$N_c$ expansion and unitarity. Next, the scalar sector is studied and good agreement with experiment up to $\sqrt{s}=1.4$ GeV is found. It is observed that the $a_0(980)$, $σ$ and $κ(900)$ resonances are meson-meson states originating from the unitarization of the ${\mathcal{O}}(p^2)$ $χ$PT amplitudes. On the other hand, the $f_0(980)$ is a combination of a strong S-wave meson-meson unitarity effect and of a preexisting singlet resonance with a mass around 1 GeV. We have also studied the size of the contributions of the unphysical cuts to the $ππ$(I=0) and $Kπ$(I=1/2) elastic S-wave amplitudes from $χ$PT and the exchange of resonances in crossed channels up to $\sqrt{s}\approx 800$ MeV. The loops are calculated as in $χ$PT at next to leading order. We find a small correction from the unphysical cuts to our calculated partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that scalar meson resonances such as the sigma, kappa, and a0(980) arise as meson-meson states generated purely from unitarizing the lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes, without requiring preexisting quark-model states; however, the f0(980) requires a combination of this unitarity effect and a preexisting singlet resonance with mass around 1 GeV, and the entire framework depends on neglecting unphysical cuts, which the authors later show give only small corrections up to about 800 MeV.

## Method

The authors use the N/D method to derive the most general form of an elastic partial wave amplitude when unphysical cuts are neglected, expressing the amplitude as a quotient of a numerator function (bearing unphysical cuts) and a denominator function (bearing the right-hand unitarity cut). They match this general structure to tree-level amplitudes from lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory at O(p^2) and from s-channel resonance exchange consistent with chiral symmetry, then unitarize by including the loop function g_L(s) that accounts for the right-hand cut. The method is extended to coupled channels, and the free parameters (resonance masses, couplings, and subtraction constants) are fitted to experimental data for pi-pi and K-pi scattering up to sqrt(s)=1.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The pi-pi and K-pi (I=1/2) P-wave scattering amplitudes are described without free parameters using the formalism combined with 1/N_c expansion and unitarity.
* Good agreement with experiment is found for the scalar sector up to sqrt(s)=1.4 GeV.
* The contribution of unphysical cuts to the pi-pi (I=0) and K-pi (I=1/2) elastic S-wave amplitudes is studied up to sqrt(s) approximately 800 MeV, and the correction is found to be small.
* The relative suppression factor between P-wave and S-wave loops from O(p^2) Chiral Perturbation Theory is 1/6^(n+1) for n-loop calculations.
* The a0(980), sigma, and kappa(900) resonances are identified as meson-meson states from unitarization of O(p^2) Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes.
* The f0(980) is a combination of a strong S-wave meson-meson unitarity effect and a preexisting singlet resonance with mass around 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oller and Oset's earlier studies (1997) that described I=0,1 S-wave channels with just one free parameter up to sqrt(s) approximately 1.2 GeV, and it contrasts with the well-understood vector channels where the rho(770) and K*(890) resonances dominate via vector meson dominance. The paper enables a systematic treatment of scalar meson spectroscopy within chiral symmetry and unitarity, showing that many scalar states emerge from unitarization rather than requiring explicit q-qbar, q^2-qbar^2, K-Kbar molecule, glueball, or hybrid interpretations as proposed by earlier authors such as Morgan (1974), Tornqvist (1995), Jaffe (1977), Weinstein (1990), and others.
