# Effects of centrality fluctuation and deuteron formation on proton number cumulant in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 3 GeV from JAM model

> We studied the effects of centrality fluctuation and deuteron formation on the cumulants ($C_n$) and correlation functions ($κ_n$) of protons up to sixth order in most central ($b<3$ fm) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 3 GeV from a microscopic transport model (JAM). The results are presented as a function of rapidity acceptance within transverse momentum $0.4<p_{T}<2 $ GeV/$c$. We compared the results obtained by centrality bin width correction (CBWC) using charged reference particle multiplicity with CBWC done using impact parameter bins. It was found that at low energies the centrality resolution for determining the collision centrality using charged particle multiplicities is not good enough to reduce the initial volume fluctuations effect for higher-order cumulant analysis. New methods need to be developed to classify events with high centrality resolution for heavy-ion collisions at low energies. Finally, we observed that the formation of deuteron will suppress the higher-order cumulants and correlation functions of protons and is found to be similar to the efficiency effect. This work can serve as a noncritical baseline for the QCD critical point search at the high baryon density region.

## Key claim

Centrality bin width correction (CBWC) using charged-particle multiplicity bins cannot effectively suppress initial volume fluctuations for higher-order cumulant analysis in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 3 GeV, contrary to its effectiveness at higher collision energies; the effect of deuteron formation on proton cumulants is similar to a binomial efficiency effect from random proton loss.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transport) model to generate approximately 25 million central events (impact parameter b < 3 fm) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 3 GeV. They calculated cumulants \(C_n\) and correlation functions \(\kappa_n\) of protons up to sixth order as a function of rapidity acceptance within transverse momentum \(0.4 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\). Deuterons were produced via an afterburner coalescence code with parameters \(\Delta R = 4\) fm and \(\Delta P = 0.3\) GeV/\(c\), and the results were compared to a 7% random binomial dropping of protons to mimic the deuteron formation effect.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 25 million central events (b < 3 fm) were analyzed for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 3 GeV.
- Deuteron coalescence parameters were set to \(\Delta R = 4\) fm and \(\Delta P = 0.3\) GeV/\(c\).
- About 7% reduction in the mean proton number was observed when deuteron formation was included.
- The beam rapidity at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 3 GeV is \(y_{beam} = 1.039\); cumulants saturate around \(\Delta y \sim 2.2\).
- \(C_4\) values become negative above \(\Delta y \sim 0.6\); \(C_5\) and \(C_6\) are consistent with zero within large statistical uncertainties.
- At large rapidity acceptance, odd-order correlation functions \(\kappa_n\) are positive while even-order are negative up to sixth order.

## Context

This work provides a non-critical baseline for the QCD critical point search at high baryon density, extending prior studies of background effects on cumulant measurements such as those by Luo (2013), Xu (2016), and Chatterjee (2019). The finding that charged-particle multiplicity bins give poor centrality resolution at low energies challenges the standard CBWC method used at higher energies and motivates the development of new centrality determination techniques, such as machine learning approaches proposed by Li (2020) and Kuttan (2020). The deuteron formation effect is shown to mimic binomial efficiency loss, which must be accounted for in experimental fluctuation analyses at low beam energies.
