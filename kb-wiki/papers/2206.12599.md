# Role of the effective range in the density-induced BEC-BCS crossover

> We elucidate the role of the effective range in the Bose-Einstein-condensate (BEC) to Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) crossover regime of two-component fermions in three dimensions. In contrast to ultracold Fermi gases near the broad Feshbach resonance, where the interaction can be characterized by the contact-type interaction, the interaction range in general becomes important in the density-induced BEC-BCS crossover discussed in the context of condensed-matter and nuclear systems. Characterizing the non-local interaction in terms of the low-energy constants such as scattering length $a$ and effective range $r$, we show how the crossover phenomena are affected by nonzero effective ranges. In particular, we show that the superfluid order parameter is strongly suppressed in the high-density regime and the sound velocity exhibits a non-monotonic behavior reflecting mechanical stability of the system. Moreover, we point out that the high-momentum tail associated with the contact parameter can be visible when the magnitude of momentum $k$ is much less than $1/r$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the density-induced BEC-BCS crossover, where the Fermi momentum changes with fixed interaction parameters, the effective range correction strongly suppresses the superfluid order parameter in the high-density regime and can produce a non-monotonic sound velocity, but contrary to some prior work, no mechanical collapse occurs when only the pure effective-range correction is considered without higher-order terms in the phase shift.

## Method

The authors apply the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory to two-component fermions in three dimensions, using a separable s-wave interaction that exactly reproduces the effective-range expansion of the phase shift up to O(k^2) with zero shape parameter. They self-consistently solve the gap equation, number density equation, and the Hartree-Fock self-energy equation to compute the superfluid order parameter, internal energy, momentum distribution, and sound velocity as functions of the dimensionless parameters 1/(k_F a) and k_F r.

## Key numbers

* Superfluid order parameter Δ/E_F is strongly suppressed near 1/(k_F a)=0 as k_F r increases; for example, at 1/(k_F a)=0, Δ/E_F decreases monotonically with increasing k_F r, approaching zero in the limit k_F r → ∞.
* In the dilute BEC regime (1/(k_F a) ≳ 0.5), the two-body binding energy E_b reaches 4/(ma^2) at r = a/2, which is four times the zero-range counterpart 1/(ma^2), leading to enhancement of Δ.
* The molecule-molecule scattering length extracted from the HFB theory is given by a_BB = 2a [1 + 4√(mE_b)/Λ] / [1 + √(mE_b)/Λ]^2, where Λ = (1/r)[1 + √(1 - 2r/a)].
* The Hartree-Fock self-energy at zero momentum Σ(k=0) starts to decrease in magnitude around k_F r = 6.1 at |a| = ∞.

## Context

This work extends the study of effective-range corrections in the BEC-BCS crossover beyond the zero-range contact interaction typical of ultracold Fermi gases near broad Feshbach resonances (Chin 2010). It contrasts with the mechanical collapse reported by Schonenberg (2017) using diffusion Monte Carlo and Hartree-Fock methods, showing that with a pure effective-range interaction (no higher-order phase shift coefficients), no such collapse occurs and the system approaches the ideal gas limit at large k_F r. The results are relevant to density-induced crossovers in neutron matter, electron-hole systems, and two-color QCD, and could be tested in future cold-atom experiments using optical control of the effective range (Wu 2012, Arunkumar 2018).
