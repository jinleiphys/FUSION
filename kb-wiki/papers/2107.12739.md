# On the order of the QCD chiral phase transition for different numbers of quark flavours

> The nature of the QCD chiral phase transition in the limit of vanishing quark masses has remained elusive for a long time, since it cannot be simulated directly on the lattice and is strongly cutoff-dependent. We report on a comprehensive ongoing study using unimproved staggered fermions with $N_\text{f}\in[2,8]$ mass-degenerate flavours on $N_τ\in\{4,6,8\}$ lattices, in which we locate the chiral critical surface separating regions with first-order transitions from crossover regions in the bare parameter space of the lattice theory. Employing the fact that it terminates in a tricritical line, this surface can be extrapolated to the chiral limit using tricritical scaling with known exponents. Knowing the order of the transitions in the lattice parameter space, conclusions for approaching the continuum chiral limit in the proper order can be drawn. While a narrow first-order region cannot be ruled out, we find initial evidence consistent with a second-order chiral transition in all massless theories with $N_\text{f}\leq 6$, and possibly up to the onset of the conformal window at $9\lesssim N_\text{f}^*\lesssim 12$. A reanalysis of already published $\mathcal{O}(a)$-improved $N_\text{f}=3$ Wilson data on $N_τ\in[4,12]$ is also consistent with tricritical scaling, and the associated change from first to second-order on the way to the continuum chiral limit. We discuss a modified Columbia plot and a phase diagram for many-flavour QCD that reflect these possible features.

## Key claim

The authors find initial evidence consistent with a second-order chiral phase transition in the chiral limit for all massless QCD-like theories with up to six quark flavours, and possibly up to the onset of the conformal window near 9 to 12 flavours, though a narrow first-order region cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The study uses unimproved staggered fermions with 2 to 8 mass-degenerate flavours on lattices with temporal extents N_tau = 4, 6, and 8, locating the chiral critical surface in the bare parameter space. The critical surface is extrapolated to the chiral limit using tricritical scaling with known exponents, exploiting the fact that it terminates in a tricritical line. A reanalysis of published O(a)-improved N_f = 3 Wilson data on N_tau from 4 to 12 is also performed using the same tricritical scaling approach.

## Key numbers

* For N_f = 3 Wilson data on N_tau in [4,12], the reanalysis is consistent with tricritical scaling and a change from first-order to second-order on the way to the continuum chiral limit.
* The study covers N_f in the range [2,8] and N_tau in {4,6,8} for the staggered fermion simulations.
* The conformal window onset is estimated at 9 less than or approximately equal to N_f^* less than or approximately equal to 12.
* For N_tau = 8, N_f = 5.0 at am = 0.005, the critical coupling beta_c is 4.9549 with 426k total statistics and aspect ratio 2.
* For N_tau = 8, N_f = 6.0 at am = 0.01, the critical coupling beta_c is 4.8323 with 324k total statistics and aspect ratio 2.
* For N_tau = 6, N_f = 3.0 at am = 0.0025, the critical coupling beta_c is 5.2122 with 464k total statistics and aspect ratio 2.
* For N_tau = 4, N_f = 2.1 at am = 0.0015, the critical coupling beta_c is 5.2324 with 800k total statistics and aspect ratio 3.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to determine the order of the QCD chiral phase transition in the chiral limit, which cannot be simulated directly on the lattice due to strong cutoff dependence. It builds on previous lattice studies such as Cuteri (2017) and provides a systematic scan across many flavour numbers, proposing a modified Columbia plot and a phase diagram for many-flavour QCD. The results enable future continuum extrapolations and help clarify whether the transition is second-order or weakly first-order for the physical case of three light quarks.
