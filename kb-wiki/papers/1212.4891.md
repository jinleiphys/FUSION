# Studies of Nucleon Resonance Structure in Exclusive Meson Electroproduction

> Studies of the structure of excited baryons are key to the N* program at Jefferson Lab. Within the first year of data taking with the Hall B CLAS12 detector following the 12 GeV upgrade, a dedicated experiment will aim to extract the N* electrocouplings at high photon virtualities Q2. This experiment will allow exploration of the structure of N* resonances at the highest photon virtualities ever yet achieved, with a kinematic reach up to Q2 = 12 GeV2. This high-Q2 reach will make it possible to probe the excited nucleon structures at distance scales ranging from where effective degrees of freedom, such as constituent quarks, are dominant through the transition to where nearly massless bare-quark degrees of freedom are relevant. In this document, we present a detailed description of the physics that can be addressed through N* structure studies in exclusive meson electroproduction. The discussion includes recent advances in reaction theory for extracting N* electrocouplings from meson electroproduction off protons, along with QCD-based approaches to the theoretical interpretation of these fundamental quantities. This program will afford access to the dynamics of the non-perturbative strong interaction responsible for resonance formation, and will be crucial in understanding the nature of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in baryons, and how excited nucleons emerge from QCD.

# Studies of Nucleon Resonance Structure in Exclusive Meson Electroproduction

## Key claim

The N* program at Jefferson Lab, using the CLAS12 detector after the 12 GeV upgrade, will extract nucleon resonance electrocouplings at photon virtualities up to Q² = 12 GeV², enabling exploration of the transition between constituent-quark degrees of freedom and nearly massless bare-quark degrees of freedom; however, the paper emphasizes that the success of this program depends critically on combining experimental data with QCD-based theoretical frameworks that respect dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and confinement, as constituent-quark models without quantum field theory foundations cannot veraciously connect meson and baryon properties.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations for extracting N* electrocouplings from exclusive meson electroproduction off protons, focusing on Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approaches and Faddeev equation treatments that treat mesons and baryons within a unified, symmetry-preserving framework. The authors compare DSE-computed baryon masses and transition form factors with results from coupled-channels analyses (Argonne-Osaka, EBAC, Julich) and light-front constituent quark models, using the Faddeev equation with diquark correlations to compute nucleon-to-resonance transition helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic reach up to Q² = 12 GeV² for N* electrocouplings with the CLAS12 detector following the 12 GeV upgrade
- The nucleon sigma-term σ_N ≈ 0.06 m_N, indicating more than 90% of the nucleon mass originates from DCSB rather than current-quark masses
- Dressed-gluon infrared mass M_g = m_g(0) ≈ 0.7 GeV
- Infrared coupling in rainbow-ladder truncation: α_RL(M_g²)/π = 2.2
- DSE-computed Roper resonance mass: 1.82 ± 0.07 GeV, matching the bare mass of 1.83 GeV from the Argonne-Osaka analysis
- Root-mean-square relative error between DSE and Argonne-Osaka bare masses: 9.4 ± 5.7%
- Nucleon-to-Roper helicity amplitude at Q²=0: A_{1/2}(0) = -16.3 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (DSE result), compared to -35.1 (Aznauryan 2007), -32.3 (Cardarelli 1996), and -18.6 (Suzuki 2010/Julia-Diaz 2009/Lee 2011)
- DSE-computed hadron masses uniformly overestimate PDG values with rms relative error ≤ 15%

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for interpreting the N* program at Jefferson Lab, building on DSE studies of meson and baryon spectra (Roberts 2011, Chen 2012) and coupled-channels analyses from EBAC (Suzuki 2009) and Julich (Gasparyan 2003). The paper demonstrates that the Roper resonance, discovered in 1963/64, can now be understood as the proton's first radial excitation with a dense meson cloud obscuring its dressed-quark core, resolving a long-standing puzzle. The results enable future extraction of N* electrocouplings from CLAS12 data to chart the evolution of the dressed-quark mass function on the momentum domain 0.3 to 1.2 GeV, bridging nonperturbative and perturbative QCD, and potentially unfolding the relationship between confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.
