# Entanglement in multinucleon transfer reactions

> Nuclear reactions present an interesting case for studies of the time-evolution of entanglement between complex quantum systems. In this work, the time-dependent nuclear density functional theory is employed to explore entanglement in multinucleon transfer reactions. As an illustrative example, for the reaction $^{40}$Ca $+$ $^{208}$Pb at $E_{\rm lab} = 249$ MeV, in the interval of impact parameters $4.65-7.40$ fm, and the relativistic density functional PC-PK1, we compute the von Neumann entropies, entanglement between fragments, nucleon-number fluctuations, and Shannon entropy for the nucleon-number observable. A simple linear correlation is established between the entanglement and nucleon-number fluctuation of the final fragments. The entanglement between the fragments can be related to the corresponding excitation energies and angular momenta. The relationship between the von Neumann entropy and the Shannon entropy for the nucleon-number observable is analyzed, as well as the time-evolution of the entanglement (nucleon-number fluctuation). The entanglement is also calculated for a range of incident energies and it is shown how, depending on the impact parameter, the entanglement increases with the collision energy.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a simple linear correlation between entanglement (mutual information) and nucleon-number fluctuations of the final fragments in multinucleon transfer reactions, but notes that this linear relation, derived from time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) which includes only one-body dynamics, provides a lower limit on both quantities; experimental fluctuations and entanglement are expected to be considerably larger.

## Method

The authors employed time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT) with the relativistic point-coupling functional PC-PK1 to simulate the multinucleon transfer reaction ⁴⁰Ca + ²⁰⁸Pb. The time-dependent Dirac equation was integrated on a three-dimensional lattice with 0.8 fm mesh spacing and 0.2 fm/c time step, using the predictor-corrector method. After collision, the space was divided into projectile-like and target-like subspaces, and the von Neumann entropy, entanglement (mutual information), nucleon-number fluctuations, Shannon entropy for the nucleon-number observable, excitation energies, and angular momenta were computed from the overlap matrix elements and particle-number projection.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction ⁴⁰Ca + ²⁰⁸Pb at Elab = 249 MeV, impact parameters were considered in the interval 4.65–7.40 fm.
- The largest impact parameter for complete fusion was b = 4.63 fm using the PC-PK1 functional.
- The correlation coefficient between entanglement and particle-number fluctuation was r = 0.9987 for both neutrons and protons.
- At b = 4.65 fm, the average total excitation energy was approximately 90 MeV, with approximately 70 MeV in the target-like fragment and approximately 20 MeV in the projectile-like fragment.
- The total angular momentum varied between approximately 80 ħ at b = 4.65 fm and approximately 140 ħ at b = 7.40 fm.
- The inequality L ≥ (8 ln 2) ΔN² was satisfied for all computed cases, where L is the entanglement and ΔN² is the nucleon-number fluctuation.
- Center-of-mass energies from 156.7 to 185.8 MeV were used for comparison with experimental data at laboratory angle θlab = 115°.

## Context

This work extends previous TD-DFT studies of multinucleon transfer reactions (Sekizawa 2013, 2014, 2016, 2017) and recent TD-CDFT applications (Ren 2020, Zhang 2024) by introducing entanglement measures. The linear correlation between entanglement and nucleon-number fluctuations builds on the inequality derived by Klich (2006) and complements recent studies of entanglement entropies in finite nuclei (Gu 2023). The comparison with experimental data from Cook (2023) shows that TD-DFT provides a lower limit on nucleon-number fluctuations and entanglement, motivating future extensions to include quantum fluctuations beyond mean-field, as developed in recent work by Li (2023, 2024).
