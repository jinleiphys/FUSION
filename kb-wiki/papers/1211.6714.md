# Global variables and correlations: Summary of the results presented at the Quark Matter 2012 conference

> In these proceedings, we highlight recent developments from both theory and experiment related to the global description of matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions as presented during the Quark Matter 2012 conference.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the state of the field as presented at the Quark Matter 2012 conference, claiming that a "standard model" for heavy-ion collision dynamics is emerging, but with the caveat that significant uncertainties remain in initial conditions, the equation of state, and transport coefficients, and that models must be validated across the full energy range from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical results presented at the Quark Matter 2012 conference, covering: (1) constraints on fluid dynamics from measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients v_n at RHIC and LHC; (2) comparisons of initial condition models (Glauber, KLN, IP-Glasma) with event-by-event viscous fluid-dynamical calculations using MUSIC; (3) lattice QCD results for the critical temperature and equation of state from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations; and (4) statistical thermal model analyses of hadron yields and blast-wave fits to pT-spectra for chemical and thermal freeze-out parameters.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.2 reproduces v_{n=2,3,4,5} distributions from ATLAS and ALICE with IP-Sat Glasma initial conditions
- Conservative range for η/s: 0.07 ≤ η/s ≤ 0.43 (Luzum, 2012)
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: Tc = 154 ± 8 (stat.) ± 1 (sys.) MeV (HotQCD) vs. Tc = 155 ± 3 (stat.) ± 3 (sys.) MeV (Wuppertal-Budapest)
- Lattice QCD interaction measure above Tc: HotQCD values systematically larger than Wuppertal-Budapest by about 25%
- STAR v3 increases progressively up to ~30% with beam energy from sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 to 200 GeV
- pT-integrated v2 increases by ~30% from top RHIC to LHC energies
- pT-differential v2 from 4-particle cumulant stays close in range 1 ≤ pT ≤ 3 GeV/c from lower BES RHIC to LHC energies
- Hypertriton lifetime from STAR combined fit: τ = 138 +23/-60 ps
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from ALICE analysis: Tch = 164 MeV
- Radial flow mean transverse velocity increases from ~0.1 to 0.65 while Tkin decreases from ~135 to below 100 MeV across beam energies from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV

## Context

This conference summary extends the line of work on the fluid-dynamical description of heavy-ion collisions that began with elliptic flow measurements at RHIC, which established the Quark-Gluon Plasma as a "most perfect liquid." It reports that IP-Sat Glasma initial conditions (Schenke, 2012) reconcile v2 and v_{n≥3} at LHC energies, unlike MC-Glauber or MC-KLN models. The paper enables future systematic studies of initial-state fluctuations using event-by-event harmonic flow analyses and ultra-central collisions, as well as further development of chiral anomalous fluid dynamics to assess the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev, 2007).
