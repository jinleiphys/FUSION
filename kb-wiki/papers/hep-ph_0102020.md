# Explosive Collisions at RHIC?

> Motivated by experimental results from RHIC, we suggest how a condensate for the Polyakov loop might produce explosive behavior at the QCD phase transition. This is due to a rapid rollover of the condensate field below the transition temperature.

# Explosive Collisions at RHIC?

## Key claim
The authors suggest that a condensate for the Polyakov loop might produce explosive behavior at the QCD phase transition, but only if the potential for the Polyakov loop changes rapidly near the transition temperature and the system is in a damped regime where the condensate field oscillates only a few times rather than many coherent oscillations.

## Method
The authors analyze a condensate model for the Polyakov loop, where the pressure is determined by a potential for the loop field and lattice data for the pressure is used to fit the temperature dependence of the loop expectation value. They study the equation of motion for the Polyakov loop field, including damping and spatial gradient terms, in the Hartree approximation assuming spatial homogeneity, and consider both underdamped and overdamped scenarios for the field's evolution below the transition temperature.

## Key numbers
- Dynamical fluctuations in average transverse momentum at RHIC are almost three times larger than at SPS, with STAR finding fluctuations approximately 8% compared to CERES at SPS finding less than or equal to 3%.
- Observed HBT radii are approximately 5-7 fm, with average transverse boost velocities around 0.6 c.
- The HBT ratio R_out/R_side is less than or equal to 1 as a function of pion pair p_t from 200 to 400 MeV.
- In the pure gauge theory at 2 T_c, the pressure ratio p/p_ideal is approximately 0.8, corresponding to a Polyakov loop expectation value of about 0.95.
- The window of temperature for the potential change is extremely narrow, approximately 2% of T_c.
- Produced pions have large average transverse momentum of about 5 f_pi = 500 MeV.
- Fluctuations about the average transverse momentum are on the order of about 10%, reduced to about 0.6% for 300 domains in one unit of rapidity, and about 1.2% for a bin of Delta y = 0.25.
- Suppression of particles at high p_t from 2 to 6 GeV is observed at RHIC but not at SPS.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous condensate model (Dumitru and Pisarski, 2001) which predicted dynamical fluctuations in average transverse momentum, and is motivated by experimental results from RHIC including HBT radii, flow velocities, and high-p_t suppression. The paper contrasts the explosive scenario with the "burning log" scenario for a strong first order transition (Rischke and collaborators) and the "smoldering log" scenario for a slow crossover, and draws analogies to reheating after inflation and Disoriented Chiral Condensates. The model enables predictions for event-by-event fluctuations, electromagnetic signatures such as direct photons, and energy loss of fast particles, and raises the open question of how these signals change between SPS energies of 17 A GeV and RHIC energies of 130 A GeV.
