# Solving Bethe-Salpeter scattering state equation in Minkowski space

> We present a method to directly solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, both for bound and scattering states. It is based on a proper treatment of the singularities which appear in the kernel, propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude itself. The off-mass shell scattering amplitude for spinless particles interacting by a one boson exchange is computed for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to directly solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for scattering states, not only bound states, by properly treating the singularities in the kernel, propagators, and amplitude itself; the off-mass-shell scattering amplitude for spinless particles interacting by one-boson exchange is computed for the first time, but the method is demonstrated only for the half-off-shell amplitude (not the full off-shell amplitude) and only for S-wave.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two equal-mass spinless particles interacting via a one-boson exchange kernel is transformed to remove the pole singularities of the constituent propagators using principal-value prescriptions and subtraction techniques. The logarithmic singularities of the kernel are handled by splitting the integration domain into intervals with singularities at the borders. The singular Born term is factorized out of the amplitude to obtain a smoother unknown function, and the resulting equation is solved numerically for the S-wave partial amplitude in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

* Coupling constant defined as \(\alpha = g^2/(16\pi m^2)\), where the kernel is \(K(k,k';p) = -16\pi m^2\alpha / [(k-k')^2 - \mu^2 + i\epsilon]\).
* The on-shell amplitude \(F^{on}_L\) satisfies \(i({F^{on}}^*_L - F^{on}_L) = (2k_s/\varepsilon_{k_s}) |F^{on}_L|^2\), leading to phase shifts via \(\delta_L(k_s) = \frac{1}{2i}\log\bigl(1 + \frac{2i k_s}{\varepsilon_{k_s}} F^{on}_L\bigr)\).
* The two-delta-function contribution to the integral term yields a factor \(\frac{i\pi^2 k_s}{8\varepsilon_{k_s}} W_0^S(k_0,k,0,k_s) F_0(0,k_s)\).
* The subtraction for the \(a_-\) singularity uses the identity \(PV\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dx/(x^2-a^2) = \pi^2 \delta(a)/2\), which for \(a = \varepsilon_{k'} - \varepsilon_{k_s}\) gives a contribution \(\frac{i\pi^2 k_s^2}{8\varepsilon_{k_s}^2} W_0(k_0,k,0,k_s) F_0(0,k_s)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for bound-state Bethe-Salpeter solutions in Minkowski space (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013; 2012) to scattering states, which had previously been treated only via Wick rotation (Tjon, 1975) or using the Nakanishi representation (Kusaka and Williams, 1995; 1997). The method enables direct computation of the off-shell Minkowski amplitude needed for electromagnetic form factors and for solving three- and many-body Bethe-Salpeter equations, which cannot be obtained from Euclidean solutions. The approach is tested by comparing the Minkowski solution with a Euclidean version of the equation that includes crossed singularities, exploiting the fact that both amplitudes must coincide on the mass shell.
