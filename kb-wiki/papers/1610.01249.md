# High-density kaonic-proton matter (KPM) composed of Lambda* equiv K-p multiplets and its astrophysical connections

> We propose and examine a new high-density composite of $Λ^* \equiv K^-p = (s \bar{u}) \otimes (uud)$, which may be called {\it Kaonic Proton Matter (KPM)}, or simply, {\it $Λ^* $-Matter}, where substantial shrinkage of baryonic bound systems originating from the strong attraction of the $(\bar{K}N)^{I=0}$ interaction takes place, providing a ground-state neutral baryonic system with a huge energy gap. The mass of an ensemble of $(K^-p)_m$, where $m$, the number of the $K^-p$ pair, is larger than $m \approx 10$, is predicted to drop down below its corresponding neutron ensemble, $(n)_m$, since the attractive interaction is further increased by the Heitler-London type molecular covalency, as well as by chiral symmetry restoration of the QCD vacuum. Since the seed clusters ($K^-p$, $K^-pp$ and $K^-K^-pp$) are short-lived, the formation of such a stabilized relic ensemble, $(K^-p)_m$, may be conceived during the Big-Bang Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) period in the early universe before the hadronization and $quark$-$anti$-$quark$ annihilation proceed. At the final stage of baryogenesis a substantial amount of primordial ($\bar{u},\bar{d}$)'s are transferred and captured into {\it KPM}, where the anti-quarks find places to survive forever. The expected {\it KPM} state may be {\it cold, dense and neutral $\bar{q} q$-hybrid ({\it Quark Gluon Bound (QGB)}) states, $[s(\bar{u} \otimes u) ud]_m$,} to which the relic of the disappearing anti-quarks plays an essential role as hidden components. The {\it KPM} may also be produced during the formation and decay of neutron stars.

# High-density kaonic-proton matter (KPM) composed of Λ* ≡ K⁻p multiplets and its astrophysical connections

## Key claim

The authors propose that ensembles of (K⁻p) pairs, called Kaonic Proton Matter (KPM), can form stable neutral baryonic systems with masses per baryon dropping below that of neutron matter when the number of pairs exceeds m ≈ 10, provided the combined effects of Heitler-London type covalent bonding from bosonic K⁻ migration and chiral symmetry restoration of the QCD vacuum are both operative.

## Method

The authors construct effective Λ*-Λ* interactions by folding the bonding potential from doubly-migrating bosonic K⁻ mesons, a K⁻-K⁻ repulsive potential, and a realistic NN potential with repulsive core, using the internal K⁻p distribution of Λ*. They then apply a variational method (ATMS) to calculate binding energies of multiple (Λ*)ₘ systems for m = 2 through 6 and beyond, and estimate the critical multiplicity for stability against strong decay. The chiral symmetry restoration effect is incorporated via a model of Brown, Kubodera and Rho (BKR) to estimate enhancement factors for the (K̄N)ᴵ⁼⁰ interaction as a function of nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1405) resonance mass: M = 1405 MeV/c², with a precise value from CLAS data of 1405.1⁺¹·³₋₁·₀ MeV/c²
- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³; KNC clusters achieve ρ ≈ 3 ρ₀
- Average effective Λ*-Λ* bonding potential ⟨ΔU⟩ₐᵥ = −135 MeV for m = 4 to 8
- Λ* separation energy S₆(Λ*) from (Λ*)₆ is estimated at 675 MeV, roughly two orders of magnitude larger than typical nucleon separation energy (≈ 8 MeV)
- The critical Λ* multiplicity for stability against strong decay is estimated to be 10 when CSR enhancement is included
- K⁻pp binding energy from DISTO and J-PARC E27 data is about 100 MeV, corresponding to a 17% more attractive effective K̄N interaction than originally predicted

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior theoretical framework of dense anti-kaonic nuclear clusters (KNC) (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, 2004, 2007a, 2007b; Dote 2004; Maeda 2013), where nuclear systems at ρ ≈ 3ρ₀ form spontaneously from strong (K̄N)ᴵ⁼⁰ attraction. It contrasts with the double-pole hypothesis for Λ(1405) (Jido 2003, Mai-Meissner) which claims weaker attraction with M ≈ 1420 MeV/c², and with mean-field models for multi-K̄ in nuclei (Gazda 2008) that lack the multi-bonding mechanism. The proposed KPM state connects to kaon condensation ideas (Kaplan and Nelson 1986, Brown 1994) and suggests new observational signatures from supernova explosions and heavy-ion collisions at LHC-ALICE, as well as a possible hidden anti-quark relic from the Big Bang QGP era.
