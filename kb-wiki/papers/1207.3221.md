# Generalized universality of higher transverse moments of quark transverse momentum dependent correlators

> The color gauge-invariant transverse momentum dependent (TMD) quark correlators contain process dependent gauge links in the bilocal matrix elements. In this paper, we split these process dependent correlators into universal TMD correlators, which in turn can be parametrized in universal TMD distribution functions. The process dependence is contained in gluonic pole factors, of which the value is determined by the gauge link. The operator structures of the universal TMD correlators are identified using transverse moments. In this paper, specific results for double transverse weighting of quark TMDs are given. In particular, we show that for a spin 1/2 target one has three universal time-reversal even leading `pretzelocity distributions', two of which involve double gluonic pole matrix elements and come with process dependent gluonic pole factors. We generalize the results for single and double weighting to TMD correlators of any specific rank, illustrating it for unpolarized, spin 1/2 and spin 1 targets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the pretzelocity TMD PDF \(h_{1T}^{\perp[U]}(x,p_T^2)\), previously considered a single T-even function, is actually a combination of three universal functions with process-dependent coefficients; the separation into T-even and T-odd parts is insufficient to isolate process dependence for higher transverse moments.

## Method

The authors decompose process-dependent TMD quark correlators into universal TMD correlators by analyzing transverse moments (weighting with powers of \(p_T\)). They compute double-weighted transverse moments of the quark correlator for a spin-1/2 target, identifying the operator structures that arise from covariant derivatives and gluonic pole matrix elements, and extract the gauge-link-dependent gluonic pole factors \(C_G^{[U]}\), \(C_{GG,1}^{[U]}\), and \(C_{GG,2}^{[U]}\) for various gauge link configurations. They then generalize the single- and double-weighting analysis to TMD correlators of arbitrary rank, illustrating the expansion for unpolarized, spin-1/2, and spin-1 targets.

## Key numbers

* For the \([+]\) gauge link: \(C_G^{[+]} = +1\), \(C_{GG,1}^{[+]} = 1\), \(C_{GG,2}^{[+]} = 0\).
* For the \([-]\) gauge link: \(C_G^{[-]} = -1\), \(C_{GG,1}^{[-]} = 1\), \(C_{GG,2}^{[-]} = 0\).
* For the \(U^{[+]}\,U^{[\Box]}\) gauge link: \(C_G = 3\), \(C_{GG,1} = 9\), \(C_{GG,2} = 0\).
* For the \(\frac{1}{N_c}\tr_c(U^{[\Box]})\,U^{[+]}\) gauge link: \(C_G = 1\), \(C_{GG,1} = 1\), \(C_{GG,2} = 4\).
* The double-weighted pretzelocity function decomposes as \(h_{1T}^{\perp(2)[U]}(x) = h_{1T}^{\perp(2)(A)}(x) + C_{GG,1}^{[U]} h_{1T}^{\perp(2)(B1)}(x) + C_{GG,2}^{[U]} h_{1T}^{\perp(2)(B2)}(x)\), where the three functions on the right are universal.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of single-weighted TMD correlators by Boer, Mulders, and collaborators (2003), which expressed single-weighted moments in terms of a T-even part and a T-odd gluonic pole matrix element with a process-dependent factor \(C_G^{[U]}\). The present paper shows that for double weighting, the T-even pretzelocity distribution is itself nonuniversal and must be decomposed into three universal functions, generalizing the concept of gluonic pole factors to double gluonic pole matrix elements. This enables a systematic treatment of higher azimuthal asymmetries (e.g., \(\sin(3\varphi-\varphi_S)\) in SIDIS and \(\cos(2\varphi+\varphi_a-\varphi_b)\) in Drell-Yan) and provides a framework for defining universal TMDs of any rank for targets of arbitrary spin.
