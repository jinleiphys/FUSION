# Study of the $\bm{p+^6}$Li$\bm{\toη+^7}$Be reaction 11.3 MeV above threshold

> The cross section for the reaction $p+^6\text{Li}\toη+^7\text{Be}$ was measured at an excess energy of 11.28 MeV above threshold by detecting the recoiling $^7$Be nuclei. A dedicated set of focal plane detectors was built for the magnetic spectrograph Big Karl and was used for identification and four momentum measurement of the $^7$Be. A differential cross section of $\frac{dσ}{dΩ}=(0.69\pm{0.20}\text{(stat.)}\pm 0.20\text{(syst.)})\text{nb/sr}$ for the ground state plus the 1/2$^-$ was measured. The result is compared to model calculations.

## Key claim

The differential cross section for the reaction p+6Li→η+7Be was measured at an excess energy of 11.28 MeV above threshold, but the result includes contributions from both the ground state and the first excited state (1/2− at 0.43 MeV) of 7Be, which could not be separated due to the target thickness limiting energy resolution to 1 MeV.

## Method

A proton beam of 1310 MeV/c momentum (673.1 MeV beam energy) from the COSY synchrotron was directed onto a 100 μm thick isotopically pure 6Li target. Recoiling 7Be nuclei were detected using the magnetic spectrograph Big Karl equipped with a dedicated focal plane detector system consisting of two multi-wire avalanche chambers for position measurement and five plastic scintillators for energy loss, total energy, and time-of-flight measurements, all operating in vacuum. Particle identification was performed via redundant methods (TOF vs momentum, TOF vs energy, and ΔE-E correlations), and the missing mass distribution was extracted after subtracting a multi-pion background fitted to the data outside the peak region.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section: dσ/dΩ = (0.69 ± 0.20 (stat.) ± 0.20 (syst.)) nb/sr for the ground state plus the 1/2− state of 7Be at an excess energy of 11.28 MeV
- Total cross section: (8.6 ± 2.6 (stat.) ± 2.4 (syst.)) nb when isotropic emission is assumed
- Beam momentum: 1310 MeV/c, corresponding to beam energy of 673.1 MeV
- Total incident protons: (6.97 ± 0.70) × 10^13
- Target thickness: 100 μm, corresponding to energy resolution of 1 MeV
- Number of counts in the peak after background subtraction: 12.7 ± 5.0 counts (from Gaussian fit)
- Comparison with earlier measurement at SATURNE (Scomparin 1993): dσ/dΩ = (4.6 ± 3.8) nb/sr at excess energy of 19.13 MeV for four final states (L=1 and L=3)

## Context

This work extends studies of η meson production on light nuclei with two-body final states, following earlier measurements of pd→η3He (Smyrski 2007, Mersmann 2007) and dd→ηα (Budzanowski 2009b) reactions, which were used to extract η-nucleus scattering lengths and search for η-nucleus quasi-bound states. The present result is compared to model calculations by Al-Khalili (1993), which treats the α particle as a spectator in the underlying pd→η3He reaction, and by the Mumbai group (Upadhyay 2009), which includes a rescattering term with a large η-nucleon scattering length. The data show a dramatic decrease of the total cross section with increasing mass number (407±20 nb for pd→η3He, 16±1.6 nb for dd→η4He, and 8.6 nb for p+6Li→η+7Be at similar excess energies), which the authors attribute to the A-dependence of form factors at large momentum transfer. More data closer to threshold are needed to determine the size of the final state interaction.
