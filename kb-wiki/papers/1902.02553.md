# $K^0Λ$ Photoproduction with Nucleon Resonances

> We investigate the reaction mechanism of $K^0 Λ$ photoproduction off the neutron target, i.e., $γn \to K^0 Λ$, in the range of $W\approx 1.6-2.2$ GeV. We employ an effective Lagrangian method at the tree-level Born approximation combining with a Regge approach. As a background, the $K^*$-Reggeon trajectory is taken into account in the $t$ channel and $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperons in the $u$-channel Feynman diagram. In addition, the role of various nucleon resonances listed in the Particle Data Group (PDG) is carefully scrutinized in the $s$ channel where the resonance parameters are extracted from the experimental data and constituent quark model. We present our numerical results of the total and differential cross sections and compare them with the recent CLAS data. The effect of the narrow nucleon resonance $N(1685,1/2^+)$ on cross sections is studied in detail and it turns out that its existence is essential in $K^0 Λ$ photoproduction to reproduce the CLAS data.

## Key claim

The narrow nucleon resonance N(1685,1/2+) is essential for reproducing the CLAS differential cross section data for γn → K0Λ, even though its effect on the total cross section is very small; the authors find that the N(1650,1/2-) and N(1720,3/2+) give the most dominant contributions to the cross section.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian method at tree-level Born approximation combined with a Regge approach, including K*-Reggeon exchange in the t-channel and Λ and Σ hyperons in the u-channel. In the s-channel, they include sixteen nucleon resonances from the Particle Data Group (PDG) plus the N(1685,1/2+), with strong coupling constants derived from quark model predictions and experimental data. The total and differential cross sections are computed and compared with CLAS data for center-of-mass energies W ≈ 1.6–2.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The K*-Reggeon exchange contribution at threshold (W ≤ 1.85 GeV) reaches only about 25% of the CLAS data level.
- The narrow resonance N(1685,1/2+) contributes a magnitude of around 0.25 μb to the total cross section.
- The strong coupling constant g_{KΛN*} for N(1685,1/2+) is determined to be -0.9.
- For N(1650,1/2-), the strong coupling constant is -0.78, with a branching ratio Br_{N*→KΛ} of 5–15%.
- For N(1720,3/2+), the strong coupling constant is -1.1, with a branching ratio of 4–5%.
- The N(1710,1/2+) has a strong coupling constant of -4.2 and branching ratio 5–25%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charged K+Λ photoproduction (MAID, SAID) to the neutral K0Λ channel, where background contributions are reduced due to the absence of charged particle exchanges. The narrow resonance N(1685,1/2+) was previously supported in γn → ηn reactions (Kuznetsov 2006), where it produced a clear bump near W = 1.68 GeV (the "neutron anomaly"). The results enable further investigation of nucleon resonances that couple weakly to πN and ηN channels, and provide evidence for the existence of N(1685,1/2+) in kaon photoproduction.
