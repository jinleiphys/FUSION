# Recent issues in hadron spectroscopy

> A brief survey is presented of recently discovered hadrons, some of them presumably demonstrating a new kind of internal structure. This includes : spin-singlet quarkonium, mesons with unexpected mass or width, baryons with two heavy quarks, and pentaquark candidates. Flavour configurations with a combination of light and heavy quarks appear as particularly promising.

# Recent issues in hadron spectroscopy

## Key claim
The paper surveys recently discovered hadrons that may exhibit new internal structures beyond the simple quark model, but cautions that many candidates require further experimental confirmation and that theoretical interpretations remain uncertain, particularly for the pentaquark states which are seen in some experiments but not in others.

## Method
The author reviews experimental results from multiple collaborations (Belle, BaBar, CLEO, Selex, NA49, Spring8) on newly observed hadrons, including charmonium states, D_s mesons, double-charm baryons, and pentaquark candidates. The paper compares these observations with predictions from potential models, chiral dynamics, diquark models, chromomagnetic interactions, and spin-flavour models, evaluating which theoretical frameworks can explain the observed masses, widths, and decay patterns.

## Key numbers
- D_sJ states at masses 2317 MeV and 2458 MeV, compared to ground-state pseudoscalar at 1968 MeV and vector at 2112 MeV
- D_sJ(2632) state decaying more often into D_s eta than into D K
- Light pentaquark candidate at approximately 1.5 GeV with strangeness S=+1 and charge Q=+1
- X(3872) lies almost exactly at the D0 D*0 threshold
- Chromomagnetic model predicts binding as large as B = -150 MeV for the H dibaryon and heavy pentaquark in the limit m(Q) -> infinity
- Universal spacing of about 300 MeV observed between any hadron and its chiral partner

## Context
This review extends the long-standing program of hadron spectroscopy from the quark model era (Greenberg 1964, Dalitz, Isgur and Karl) through to modern discoveries. It addresses the proliferation of scalar mesons below 2 GeV first noted by Jaffe in the MIT bag model, and the ongoing tension between diquark models and full three-body calculations for baryons. The paper enables future searches for double-charm exotics, heavy pentaquarks, and other multiquark states, while warning against repeating the mistakes of the baryonium episode where postulated diquark structures lacked dynamical justification.
