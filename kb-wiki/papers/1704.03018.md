# 3-D Glasma initial state from small-x evolution

> We present an ab-initio approach to compute the longitudinal dependence of the initial state in heavy-ion collisions by including small-x evolution of the nuclear gluon distributions. Extending the IP-Glasma model by including JIMWLK rapidity evolution, we compute event-by-event rapidity distributions of produced gluons and the early time energy momentum tensor as a function of space-time rapidity and transverse coordinates. We show how the effects of small-x evolution manifest themselves in longitudinal (rapidity) correlations of event-by-event multiplicities and transverse geometry and compare our results to various phenomenological models and experimental observations.

## Key claim
The authors present an ab-initio approach to compute the longitudinal dependence of the initial state in heavy-ion collisions by including small-x evolution of nuclear gluon distributions, but they assume that high-energy factorization holds over the entire range of rapidity separations considered, even though it is expected to break down when the rapidity separation is of order 1/alpha_s.

## Method
The authors extend the IP-Glasma model by including JIMWLK rapidity evolution of Wilson lines, starting from an initial rapidity scale of +/-2.4 and evolving to the rapidity of interest using leading-log JIMWLK evolution. They then compute event-by-event rapidity distributions of produced gluons and the early-time energy-momentum tensor as a function of space-time rapidity and transverse coordinates by solving classical Yang-Mills equations of motion. The strong coupling constant alpha_s is treated as a free parameter to adjust evolution speed, and an infrared regulator is introduced to suppress gluon radiation at large distance scales.

## Key numbers
- For central (b=0) Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the event-averaged multiplicity distribution dN/dy shows good overall agreement with ALICE data for alpha_s = 0.15.
- The evolution speed of the saturation scale is d ln Q_s^2/dy approximately 0.3, matching phenomenological extractions from DIS structure functions.
- The de-correlation ratios r_2 and r_3 for elliptic (n=2) and triangular (n=3) eccentricities are computed across the rapidity range -2.4 < eta < 2.4 and compared to CMS results for central (0-5%) Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma model (Schenke, 2012) by incorporating longitudinal structure via JIMWLK evolution, providing a first-principles alternative to phenomenological models such as the Torque model (Bozek, 2015), AMPT (Pang, 2015), and 3DMCG (Monnai, 2015), which use vastly different degrees of freedom ranging from flux tubes to hadronic degrees of freedom. The results enable future studies of longitudinal correlations and fluctuations in different collision systems (p+p, p+A, A+A) and can be extended by including running coupling effects and proper event selection.
