# Heuristic quantization of the cranking model

> We propose a simple and systematic method to quantize the cranking model and to calculate the matrix elements of intrinsic operators entering the generalized intensity relations. An example of the application is given to show the Coriolis coupling effects for B(E3) values of the 3- octupole states in Gd isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a heuristic "quantization" procedure that replaces classical d-functions with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and angular-momentum operators, enabling the cranking model to describe electromagnetic transition probabilities at low spin where the semiclassical high-spin approximation fails.

## Method

The authors derive a quantization rule by comparing the cranking model's expression for reduced transition amplitudes in the high-spin limit with the unified model's quantum-mechanical intensity relations. They replace the classical d-functions in the cranking formula with reduced matrix elements of the rotation operator D, and replace the rotational frequency ω_rot times d-functions with anticommutators of angular-momentum operators I_± and the D operator divided by the moment of inertia J. This yields explicit intensity formulas including lowest-order Coriolis coupling corrections for both K-allowed and K-forbidden transitions.

## Key numbers

- B(E3; 3^- → 0^+) values are calculated for octupole-vibrational bands (K=0 through 3) in Gd isotopes (¹⁵⁶Gd, ¹⁵⁸Gd, ¹⁶⁰Gd)
- The Coriolis coupling concentrates E3 strength onto the lowest-lying octupole state: K=1 in ¹⁵⁶,¹⁵⁸Gd and K=2 in ¹⁶⁰Gd
- Large differences in B(E3) values between K=0 and K=1 bands observed in ¹⁵⁶,¹⁵⁸Gd are reproduced by the calculation with lowest-order correction terms (solid circles), while the leading-order calculation without Coriolis coupling (open circles) disagrees

## Context

This work extends the cranking model formalism of Marshalek (1970s) by providing a quantization procedure that bridges the semiclassical cranking model and the quantum-mechanical unified model for rotational bands. The method enables microscopic calculation of intrinsic parameters in rotational intensity relations including Coriolis coupling effects, which Neergård and Vogel (1980s) had shown to be important even at low spins for octupole states. The approach can be applied to both intra-band and inter-band transitions in deformed nuclei.
