# Towards quantitative precision in the chiral crossover: masses and fluctuation scales

> We investigate the relation between the physical pion pole and screening masses and the mesonic fluctuation scale in low-energy QCD, which relates to the curvature of the mesonic effective potential. This relation is important for the correct relative weight of quantum, thermal and density fluctuations. Hence, it governs the location of phase boundaries as well as the phase structure of QCD. The identification of the correct physics scales is also primarily important for the correct adjustment of the parameters of effective models for low-energy QCD. It is shown that subject to an appropriate definition of the latter, all these scales agree at vanishing temperature, while they deviate from each other at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that with an appropriate definition of the mesonic fluctuation scale (curvature mass), the pion pole mass, screening mass, and curvature mass all agree at vanishing temperature to within less than one percent, but deviate from each other at finite temperature due to the breaking of Euclidean O(4) invariance by the heat bath.

## Method

The authors use the functional renormalisation group (FRG) to compute fully momentum-dependent mesonic two-point functions and the effective potential in a two-flavor quark-meson model. They employ an iterative procedure that alternates between solving the flow equation for the effective potential and the flow equation for the momentum-dependent part of the two-point function, allowing convergence to a self-consistent solution. The calculation includes momentum- and field-dependent bosonic wavefunction renormalisation factors Z and Y, while the fermionic sector is treated classically.

## Key numbers

* At zero temperature with UV cutoff Lambda_UV = 500 MeV, the converged pion curvature mass is 135.3 MeV and the pion pole mass is 133 +/- 2 MeV, with the minimum of the effective potential at sigma_min = 92.9 MeV (Table I).
* At zero temperature with UV cutoff Lambda_UV = 900 MeV, the converged pion curvature mass is 135.3 MeV and the pion pole mass is 134 +/- 2 MeV, with sigma_min = 93.6 MeV (Table III).
* At zero temperature with UV cutoff Lambda_UV = 700 MeV, the converged pion curvature mass is 134.9 MeV and the pion pole mass is 133 +/- 2 MeV, with sigma_min = 93.2 MeV (Table II).
* The LPA' calculation at Lambda_UV = 500 MeV yields m_pol = m_cur = 135.0 MeV, compared to the fully converged result of 135.3 MeV (curvature) and 133 +/- 2 MeV (pole).
* The thermal range Lambda_T of the model is estimated as Lambda_T less than or approximately 7 T for the regulators used.
* Results are presented for temperatures up to approximately 180 MeV, covered by a UV cutoff of 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous functional RG studies of low-energy QCD effective models, particularly addressing the relation between different mass definitions (pole, screening, curvature) that had been treated inconsistently in earlier LPA calculations such as those by Strodthoff (2011), Kamikado (2012), and Svanes (2010). The results enable more precise determination of phase boundaries in QCD at finite temperature and density by correctly identifying the relevant fluctuation scales for quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations. The iterative method developed here can be extended to complex external momenta to provide direct access to spectral functions within an Euclidean framework, following approaches by Strodthoff (2011), Floerchinger (2011), and Tripolt (2013, 2014).
