# Correspondence between momentum dependent relaxation time and field redefinition of relativistic hydrodynamic theory

> In this article a correspondence has been established between the out of equilibrium system dissipation and the thermodynamic field redefinition of the macroscopic variables through the momentum dependent relaxation time approximation (MDRTA) solution of relativistic transport equation. Here, it has been shown that the out of equilibrium thermodynamic fields are not uniquely defined and are subjected to include dissipative effects from the medium. A second order relativistic hydrodynamic theory has been developed including such dissipative effects. The necessary conditions for developing a hydrodynamic theory has been fulfilled, (i) the thermodynamic identities incorporating such redefined fields have been shown to conserve the energy-momentum tensor perfectly under MDRTA, (ii) the non-negativity of entropy production remains unaffected by the inclusion of such dissipative contributions in hydro fields as long as the independent transport coefficients remain positive.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that out-of-equilibrium thermodynamic fields (energy density, pressure, particle number density, and hydrodynamic four-velocity) are not uniquely defined and necessarily include dissipative corrections from the medium, with these corrections being determined by the momentum dependence of the relaxation time in the collision integral, while the number of independent transport coefficients remains unchanged.

## Method

The author solves the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation using the Chapman-Enskog gradient expansion method with a momentum-dependent relaxation time approximation (MDRTA), where the relaxation time follows a power law in scaled particle energy, τ_R = τ_R^0 τ_p^n. The first-order and second-order corrections to the distribution function are obtained iteratively, and the corresponding dissipative corrections to thermodynamic fields (energy density, pressure, particle number density, energy flow, and particle flow) are extracted. The conservation of energy-momentum tensor and non-negativity of entropy production are verified for all values of the momentum-dependence exponent n.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity ζ, thermal conductivity λ, and shear viscosity η are all positive for all values of n and for various combinations of z (scaled mass), T (temperature), and μ̃ (scaled chemical potential)
- For momentum-independent case n=0, the energy correction c_Λ^1, particle number correction c_Γ^1, and energy flux coefficient c_Σ^1 all vanish
- For n=1, c_Ξ^1 vanishes while c_Λ^1 = 3c_Ω^1
- The relaxation times for bulk viscosity (τ_Π) and shear viscosity (τ_π) equal τ_R^0 only for n=0; for all other n values, τ_Π and τ_π become larger than τ_R^0
- The second-order vector correction coefficients r_π^2 and r_Π^2 are both zero for n=0

## Context

This work extends the program of including dissipative effects into thermodynamic field definitions, previously explored phenomenologically or via renormalization-group techniques by Monnai (2018), Tsumura (2006, 2009), and others. The MDRTA approach addresses two issues with constant relaxation time: it connects to microscopic interaction theory as demonstrated by Dusling (2009), and it separates relaxation time scales between microscopic particle distributions and macroscopic viscous fields. The derived second-order hydrodynamic equations can be applied to hydrodynamic simulations, with the momentum dependence of the relaxation time determining how dissipative fluxes are distributed among scalar and vector thermodynamic fields.
