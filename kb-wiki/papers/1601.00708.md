# Jet Fragmentation via Recombination of Parton Showers

> We propose to model hadronization of parton showers in QCD jets through a hybrid approach involving quark recombination and string fragmentation. This is achieved by allowing gluons at the end of the perturbative shower evolution to undergo a non-perturbative splitting into quark and antiquark pairs, then applying a Monte-Carlo version of instantaneous quark recombination, and finally subjecting remnant quarks (those which have not found a recombination partner) to Lund string fragmentation. When applied to parton showers from the PYTHIA Monte Carlo event generator, the final hadron spectra from our calculation compare quite well to PYTHIA jets that have been hadronized with the default Lund string fragmentation. Our new approach opens up the possibility to generalize hadronization to jets embedded in a quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors propose a hybrid hadronization model that replaces Lund string fragmentation in PYTHIA with a combination of quark recombination (for partons close in phase space) and string fragmentation (for remnant partons), and show that this approach reproduces the results of pure string fragmentation for vacuum jets, with the caveat that the comparison is only qualitative and precision tuning would require fits to data.

## Method

The authors extract perturbative parton showers from PYTHIA 6.3 for 100 GeV and 25 GeV jets from e+e- collisions, then force gluons at the end of the shower to decay non-perturbatively into quark-antiquark pairs. They apply a Monte Carlo version of instantaneous quark recombination using Gaussian-smeared Wigner functions for mesons and baryons (with widths fixed by measured charge radii), including excited states up to n=8 for mesons and n1+n2=8 for baryons, and finally subject remnant quarks that did not find a recombination partner to Lund string fragmentation via PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Jets of energy 100 GeV extracted from e+e- collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, with perturbative cutoff Q0 = 1 GeV
- Constituent quark masses: m_u,d = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV
- Gluon decay mass cutoff parameter m_max = 1.25 GeV
- Average number of quarks and antiquarks in 100 GeV jet showers after gluon decays: about 13
- Phase space density of shower partons in momentum space: about 0.025/GeV^3, compared to about 2.5/GeV^3 for partons in a quark-gluon plasma at phase transition temperature
- Distribution of quark-antiquark pairs peaks at relative spatial distance y ~ 0.5 fm and relative momentum k ~ 0.3 GeV in their common rest frame
- Charge radii used for width parameters: pion 0.67 fm, kaon 0.56 fm, nucleon 0.88 fm
- Recombination dominates hadron production below z ~ 0.1 for 100 GeV jets
- Typical error in total energy of the shower from reintroducing gluon virtuality: less than 1% for 100 GeV jets

## Context

This work extends earlier quark recombination models applied to jets by Das (1977), Chang (1980), Migneron (1981), and Hwa and Yang (2003), but improves upon them by using sophisticated parton Monte Carlo generators (PYTHIA 6.3) rather than fitted or model-determined parton distributions, and by accounting for fluctuations from the small number of partons in each jet. The hybrid approach enables future generalization to jets embedded in a quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where thermal partons from the medium can participate in recombination, as previously studied by Greco (2003) and Fries (2003) for thermal partons.
