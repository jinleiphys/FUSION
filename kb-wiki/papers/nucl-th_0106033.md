# Deformed Boson Scheme in Time-Dependent Variational Method. I

> Various aspects of the deformed boson scheme are investigated with aiming of applying to the time-dependent variational method for many-boson system. The case of the multiboson states is also discussed. In analogy with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory in many-fermion system, the classical aspect of the deformed boson is discussed.

# Deformed Boson Scheme in Time-Dependent Variational Method. I

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized boson coherent state, parametrized by a function f(n) of the boson number, which can be used as a trial state in the time-dependent variational method for many-boson systems; the generalization is equivalent to a deformed boson scheme where the deformation is specified by the choice of f(n), and the classical counterpart of the deformed boson emerges naturally from the canonicity condition.

## Method

The authors introduce a wave packet |c> = (√Γ)^{-1} Σ_n f(n) (√n!)^{-1} γ^n |n> with a well-behaved function f(n) satisfying f(0)=f(1)=1 and f(n)>0 for n≥2. They define deformed boson operators γ̂ = f(N̂) ĉ f(N̂)^{-1} and γ̂* = f(N̂)^{-1} ĉ* f(N̂), which satisfy γ̂|c> = γ|c>. The function [x]_q = x f(x)^{-2} f(x-1)^2 is introduced to connect with the standard deformed boson formalism. The time-dependent variational principle δ ∫ <c| i∂_t - Ĥ |c> dt = 0 is applied, and through the canonicity condition the variables (γ, γ*) are transformed into canonical variables (z, z*) = (γ√(Γ'/Γ), γ*√(Γ'/Γ)) that satisfy Hamilton's equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- For the most popular form with [x]_q = (q^x - q^{-x})/(q - q^{-1}) and 0 < q ≤ 1: f(n) = √(n! / ∏_{k=2}^n (q^k - q^{-k})/(q - q^{-1})) for n ≥ 2; at q→0 the state reduces to |c> = (1+|γ|²)^{-1/2} (1+γĉ*)|0>; at q=1 it reduces to the conventional boson coherent state exp(-|γ|²/2) exp(γĉ*)|0>.
- For the Penson-Solomon form with f(n) = q^{n(n-1)/4}: [n]_q = n q^{-(n-1)} and [n]_q! = q^{-n(n-1)/2} n!; the commutation relation is γ̂γ̂* - q^{-1}γ̂*γ̂ = q^{-N̂}.
- For the product form combining both deformations: [n]_q = (1 - q^{-2n})/(1 - q^{-2}) and the commutation relation is γ̂γ̂* - q^{-2}γ̂*γ̂ = 1.
- For the multiboson state with m bosons: f(n) = √(n!)/√((mn)!); the mapping gives (ĉ*ĉ)_c = m N̂ and (ĉ*)^m_c = √(m^m) ĉ* √(F_m(N̂)) with F_m(N̂) = ∏_{p=1}^{m-1} (N̂ + p/m).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent variational method for many-boson systems previously developed by the same authors (Kuriyama et al., 1998) by placing it in the framework of the deformed boson scheme. It generalizes the conventional boson coherent state approach and connects to the work of Penson and Solomon (1999) on generalized coherent states, as well as to the standard q-deformed boson formalism of Macfarlane (1989) and Arik and Coon (1976). The multiboson treatment uses the MYT boson mapping method (Matsuo, Yamamura, and Tsue, 1994) and is motivated by the problem of anharmonic effects in large amplitude vibrations of metal clusters (Karpeshin, da Providência, and Providência, private communication). The paper enables a systematic treatment of time-dependent variational problems for boson systems with arbitrary deformations, and sets the stage for Part II which will treat the su(2) and su(1,1) algebras in the Schwinger boson representation.
