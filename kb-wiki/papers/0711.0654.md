# Point-form quantum field theory and meson form factors

> We shortly review point-form quantum field theory, i.e. the canonical quantization of a relativistic field theory on a Lorentz-invariant surface of the form $x_μx^μ= τ^2$. As an example of how point-form quantum field theory may enter the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics we discuss the calculation of the electromagnetic form factor of a confined quark-antiquark pair (e.g. the pion).

## Key claim

The authors show that a point-form quantum field theory (PFQFT) approach, combined with a Bakamjian-Thomas framework and the approximation that total 4-velocity is conserved at electromagnetic vertices, can produce a satisfactory fit to pion form-factor data up to momentum transfers of a few GeV², but the extracted form factor depends not only on photon virtuality but also on the meson momentum in the center-of-mass frame, with this dependence vanishing only in the limit of infinite meson momentum.

## Method

The authors treat electron-meson scattering as a quantum mechanical two-channel problem for the mass operator, explicitly including the dynamics of the exchanged photon, and reduce it to a one-channel problem with a one-photon-exchange optical potential. By comparing matrix elements of the optical potential for electron scattering off a confined quark-antiquark pair (with an instantaneous confining potential) to those for electron-meson scattering, they identify the electromagnetic form factor. The calculation uses spinless quarks, a harmonic-oscillator wave function, and the approximation that the total 4-velocity of the system is conserved at electromagnetic vertices.

## Key numbers

* Pion form factor calculated with harmonic-oscillator wave function parameters: oscillator parameter \(a = 0.21\) GeV and constituent-quark mass \(m_\mathrm{q} = 0.2\) GeV.
* The form factor fit is satisfactory up to momentum transfers of a few GeV².
* The form factor depends on the meson center-of-mass momentum \(|\vec{k}_\mathrm{M}|\); this dependence vanishes in the limit \(|\vec{k}_\mathrm{M}| \rightarrow \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the point-form quantum field theory formalism previously developed by the authors (Biernat et al., 2007) and applies it to the Bakamjian-Thomas framework (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) for constructing relativistic quantum mechanical models of hadrons. The approach enables first exploratory calculations of electromagnetic form factors for confined quark-antiquark systems, with planned extensions to include quark spin, vector mesons, baryons, and hadron transition form factors.
