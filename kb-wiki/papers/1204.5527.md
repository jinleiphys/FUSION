# Understanding the different rotational behaviors of $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No

> Total Routhian surface calculations have been performed to investigate rapidly rotating transfermium nuclei, the heaviest nuclei accessible by detailed spectroscopy experiments. The observed fast alignment in $^{252}$No and slow alignment in $^{254}$No are well reproduced by the calculations incorporating high-order deformations. The different rotational behaviors of $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No can be understood for the first time in terms of $β_6$ deformation that decreases the energies of the $νj_{15/2}$ intruder orbitals below the N=152 gap. Our investigations reveal the importance of high-order deformation in describing not only the multi-quasiparticle states but also the rotational spectra, both providing probes of the single-particle structure concerning the expected doubly-magic superheavy nuclei.

# Understanding the different rotational behaviors of $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No

## Key claim
The different rotational behaviors of $^{252}$No (fast alignment) and $^{254}$No (slow alignment) can be understood for the first time in terms of $\beta_6$ deformation, which lowers the energies of the $\nu j_{15/2}$ intruder orbitals below the $N=152$ deformed shell gap. This explanation is conditional on the inclusion of high-order deformations ($\beta_6$ and $\beta_8$) in total Routhian surface calculations that also incorporate pairing correlations.

## Method
Total Routhian surface (TRS) calculations were performed using a cranked shell model with an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, including both monopole and doubly-stretched quadrupole pairings with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection. The deformation space was extended to include $\beta_2$, $\beta_4$, $\beta_6$, and $\beta_8$ degrees of freedom, and the total energy was minimized in this multi-dimensional deformation space at each rotational frequency. The calculations were performed without any adjustment of parameters, using the standard average-gap method for monopole pairing strength.

## Key numbers
- $^{252}$No shows a sharp growth in moment of inertia at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.2$ MeV, while $^{254}$No remains steady until $\hbar\omega \approx 0.3$ MeV
- $\beta_2 \approx 0.27$ has been derived for $^{254}$No from the measured ground-state band
- The yrast spectrum of $^{254}$No has been extended to spins of more than $20\hbar$
- For $^{254}$No, the $\beta_{2,4,6,8}$ deformations remain almost constant until $\hbar\omega \approx 0.3$ MeV, then sharply change; for $^{252}$No, large deformation changes occur at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.2$ MeV
- The calculation restricted to $\beta_{2,4}$ only gives an upbending for $^{254}$No at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.2$ MeV, in contrast to the gradual increase until $\hbar\omega \approx 0.3$ MeV when high-order deformations are included

## Context
This work extends previous TRS calculations by Schunck et al. (2007) that included high-order deformations but did not include pairing correlations, which are necessary for describing upbending and backbending phenomena. The study builds on experimental observations of rotational bands in transfermium nuclei including $^{246}$Fm, $^{248}$Fm, $^{250}$Fm, $^{252}$No, and $^{254}$No, and addresses a question open since 2001 when the rotational band of $^{252}$No was first observed by Herzberg et al. (2001). The results enable predictions for $^{250}$Fm and $^{252}$Fm isotopes, where a more distinct difference in alignment behavior is expected, and establish a consistent relation between high-order deformations, moments of inertia, and excitation energies of multi-quasiparticle states relevant to the expected doubly-magic superheavy nuclei.
