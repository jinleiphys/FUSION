# Strong Upper Limits on Sterile Neutrino Warm Dark Matter

> Sterile neutrinos are attractive dark matter candidates. Their parameter space of mass and mixing angle has not yet been fully tested despite intensive efforts that exploit their gravitational clustering properties and radiative decays. We use the limits on gamma-ray line emission from the Galactic Center region obtained with the SPI spectrometer on the INTEGRAL satellite to set new constraints, which improve on the earlier bounds on mixing by more than two orders of magnitude, and thus strongly restrict a wide and interesting range of models.

## Key claim
The authors use gamma-ray line emission limits from the INTEGRAL satellite's SPI spectrometer to set new constraints on sterile neutrino warm dark matter, improving earlier bounds on the mixing angle by more than two orders of magnitude for masses above 40 keV, assuming sterile neutrinos comprise all of the present-day dark matter.

## Method
The authors calculated the expected gamma-ray flux from radiative decays of sterile neutrinos in the Milky Way halo, using the decay channel ν_s → ν_a + γ which produces mono-energetic photons at E_γ = m_s/2. They compared this predicted flux to the 3.5σ limits on narrow line emission from the Galactic Center region obtained by Teegarden and Watanabe using the SPI spectrometer on INTEGRAL, accounting for the background subtraction procedure that removes flux from angles ψ > 30°. The analysis used a conservative value for the line-of-sight integral over the dark matter distribution, adopting ∫_ΔΩ dΩ [J(ψ) - J(30°)] ≈ 0.5 for the region within 13° of the Galactic Center.

## Key numbers
- The sterile neutrino radiative decay lifetime is τ⁻¹ = (6.8 × 10⁻³³ s⁻¹) × [sin²2θ/10⁻¹⁰] × [m_s/keV]⁵ for Dirac neutrinos
- The prefactor relating flux to sterile neutrino parameters is (4.3 × 10⁻⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ sr⁻¹) × [sin²2θ/10⁻¹⁰] × [m_s/keV]⁴
- The 3.5σ limits on narrow line emission from the Galactic Center region are ≲ 10⁻⁴ photons cm⁻² s⁻¹ for the full energy range 20-8000 keV
- The INTEGRAL constraints apply for sterile neutrino masses above 40 keV, with a sharp cut-off at m_s = 40 keV due to the SPI instrument energy range
- The subtraction correction for background removal is less than a factor of 3 at ψ = 13° for all three dark matter profiles considered (NFW, Moore, Kravtsov)

## Context
This work extends prior constraints on sterile neutrino dark matter from radiative decay searches, specifically improving on the Cosmic X-ray Background (CXB) constraints from Boyarsky et al. by more than two orders of magnitude for masses above 40 keV. The results complement cosmological lower limits from Lyman-α forest clustering data (which give mass limits of about 10-13 keV independent of mixing angle) and X-ray limits from nearby sources summarized by Watson et al. The constraints leave only a narrow gap at m_s ≈ 20-40 keV where mixing limits are substantially weaker, and the authors encourage dedicated analyses of INTEGRAL data to extend constraints to lower masses.
