# Simple, empirical order parameter for a first order quantum phase transition in atomic nuclei

> A simple, empirical signature of a first order phase transition in atomic nuclei is presented, the ratio of the energy of the 6+ level of the ground state band to the energy of the first excited 0+ state. This ratio provides an effective order parameter which is not only easy to measure, but also distinguishes between first and second order phase transitions and takes on a special value in the critical region. Data in the Nd-Dy region show these characteristics. In addition, a repeating degeneracy between alternate yrast states and successive excited 0+ states is found to correspond closely to the line of a first order phase transition in the framework of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model in the large N limit, pointing to a possible underlying symmetry in the critical region.

# Simple, empirical order parameter for a first order quantum phase transition in atomic nuclei

## Key claim

The ratio of the energy of the 6+ level of the ground state band to the energy of the first excited 0+ state, E(6_1+)/E(0_2+), serves as an effective order parameter for first order quantum phase transitions in atomic nuclei that can distinguish first from second order transitions, but this distinction is clearest in the large boson number limit and may be difficult to discern in finite nuclei when the Hamiltonian parameter approaches the second order case.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using the extended consistent Q formalism (ECQF) of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model with a Hamiltonian containing two parameters, zeta and chi, spanning the U(5), SU(3), and O(6) dynamical symmetries. They computed the ratio E(6_1+)/E(0_2+) for boson numbers N_B = 15 and 100 across the U(5)-SU(3) transition (first order) and U(5)-O(6) transition (second order), and compared these results to experimental data for Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, Xe, and Ba isotopes. They also mapped lines of degeneracy between yrast states and excited 0+ states in the IBA triangle for N_B = 10, 40, 100, and 250.

## Key numbers

- The ratio E(6_1+)/E(0_2+) equals 1.5 in the U(5) limit and approaches zero in the large N_B limit of SU(3) as the 0_2+ state rises in energy.
- For the IBA calculation with chi = -sqrt(7)/2 and N_B = 250, the degeneracy between E(0_2+) and E(6_1+) occurs at zeta = 0.4729, which lies very close to the critical point zeta_crit = 0.4721 obtained in the exact infinite N_B limit.
- The N = 90 nuclei (150Nd, 152Sm, 154Gd) have E(6_1+)/E(0_2+) very close to 1.0, consistent with the degeneracy signature of the critical region.
- The B_{4/2} ratio (B(E2;4_1+ -> 2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+)) varies only slightly as a function of structure, typically in the range 1.4-1.7.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quantum phase transitions in nuclei using the IBA model, particularly the intrinsic state formalism of Gilmore (1979) and the critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) proposed by Iachello (2000, 2001). The ratio E(6_1+)/E(0_2+) improves upon earlier order parameters such as nu_2 and nu_2' from Franci and Van Isacker (2004), which do not maintain their distinguishing power in the large N_B thermodynamic limit, and upon the B_{4/2} ratio studied by Rowe (2004), which varies only over a narrow range and is difficult to measure far from stability. The identification of degeneracies between alternate yrast states and successive excited 0+ states along the first order phase transition line points to a possible underlying quasi-dynamical symmetry in the critical region, similar to those discussed by Rowe (2004) for the U(5)-O(6) and U(5)-SU(3) transitions.
