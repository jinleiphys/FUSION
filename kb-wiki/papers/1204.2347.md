# Comparison of results from a 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model to elliptic and hexadecapole flow of charged hadrons measured in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV

> Simulated results from a 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model have been compared to the experimental data on the centrality dependence of invariant yield, elliptic flow ($v_{2}$), and hexadecapole flow ($v_{4}$) as a function of transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) of charged hadrons in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. Results from two types of initial transverse energy density profile, one based on the Glauber model and other based on Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) are presented. We observe no difference in the simulated results on the invariant yield of charged hadrons for the calculations with different initial conditions. The comparison to the experimental data on invariant yield of charged hadrons supports a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$) between 0 to 0.12 for the 0-10% to 40-50% collision centralities. The simulated $v_{2}(p_{T})$ is found to be higher for a fluid with CGC based initial condition compared to Glauber based initial condition for a given collision centrality. Consequently the Glauber based calculations when compared to the experimental data requires a lower value of $η/s$ relative to CGC based calculations. In addition, a centrality dependence of the estimated $η/s$ is observed from the $v_{2}(p_{T})$ study. The $v_{4}(p_{T})$ for the collision centralities 0-10% to 40-50% supports a $η/s$ value between 0 - 0.08 for a CGC based initial condition. While simulated results using the Glauber based initial condition for the ideal fluid evolution under estimates the $v_{4}(p_{T})$ for collision centralities 0-10% to 30-40%.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of the quark-gluon plasma formed in Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV can be constrained by comparing 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations to experimental data on elliptic flow (v2) and hexadecapole flow (v4), but the extracted η/s value depends on the choice of initial condition (Glauber versus Color-Glass-Condensate) and on collision centrality, with no single η/s value simultaneously describing both v2 and v4 data.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code with Israel-Stewart formalism, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance and zero net baryon density at midrapidity. Two initial transverse energy density profiles were used: one based on a two-component optical Glauber model and one based on the CGC/KLN kT-factorization approach, with initial time τi = 0.6 fm and temperature-independent η/s values of 0.0, 0.08, 0.12, 0.16, and 0.18. The equation of state combined a hadron resonance gas at low temperatures with a parametrization of lattice QCD results, with a cross-over transition at Tc = 175 MeV, and freeze-out was performed at Tf = 130 MeV using the Cooper-Frye prescription with shear viscous corrections to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- η/s values tested: 0.0, 0.08, 0.12, 0.16, and 0.18 (temperature-independent)
- Initial time τi = 0.6 fm
- Freeze-out temperature Tf = 130 MeV
- Cross-over transition temperature Tc = 175 MeV
- Central energy density ε0 for Glauber initial condition ranges from 43.4 GeV/fm³ (η/s = 0.0) to 25.4 GeV/fm³ (η/s = 0.18)
- Normalization constant C for CGC initial condition ranges from 0.11 GeV/fm^(1/3) (η/s = 0.0) to 0.065 GeV/fm^(1/3) (η/s = 0.18)
- For invariant yield, 0-10% centrality data is best explained by η/s = 0.0, while 20-30% to 40-50% centralities support η/s between 0.08 and 0.12
- For v2(pT) with Glauber initial condition, 0-10% centrality prefers η/s = 0.0, while 40-50% centrality is closest to η/s = 0.18
- For v2(pT) with CGC initial condition, 0-10% centrality is best explained by η/s between 0.08 and 0.12
- For v4(pT) with CGC initial condition, the preferred η/s lies between 0.0 and 0.08 for centralities 0-10% to 40-50%
- Impact parameter for comparison studies: b = 7.4 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section used in CGC calculation: 42 mb
- Saturation scale growth parameter λ = 0.28

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Glauber and CGC initial conditions to RHIC data, specifically using high-statistics PHENIX measurements of v2(pT) and v4(pT) for charged hadrons across multiple centrality bins. Prior studies by Luzum (2008, 2009) compared similar simulations to multiplicity, average pT, and pT-integrated v2, finding that CGC initial conditions prefer higher η/s than Glauber initial conditions. Song (2010, 2011) used a hybrid approach coupling viscous hydrodynamics to a transport model for the hadronic phase and found η/s ~ 0.16-0.24 for CGC and η/s ~ 0.08-0.16 for Glauber initial conditions. The present work enables future event-by-event hydrodynamic studies with fluctuating initial conditions to simultaneously describe odd and even flow harmonics for a more robust determination of η/s.
