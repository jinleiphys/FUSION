# Nuclear Pasta and Crustal Quasi-Periodic Oscillations in Neutron Star

> We investigate the impact of nuclear pasta on crustal structure and torsional oscillations using a Bayesian ensemble of unified neutron-star equations of state based on relativistic mean-field models constrained by nuclear experiments, empirical saturation properties, chiral effective field theory, and multimessenger observations. For each posterior sample, we compute the pasta sequence within a compressible liquid-drop model and quantify the onset density, thickness, and mass fraction of the pasta layers. We show that the appearance and extent of nuclear pasta are primarily controlled by the symmetry-energy slope parameter $L$. While spherical and rod-like pasta configurations are present for all equations of state, only a small fraction of the posterior supports slab, tube, or bubble geometries. The transition from spherical nuclei to rods is tightly constrained to occur at a density of $ρ_{\rm sr} = 0.0588^{+0.0045}_{-0.0065}\,\mathrm{fm^{-3}}$. We further predict that the nuclear pasta layer occupies a relative radial thickness of $ΔR_{\rm pasta}/ΔR_{\rm c} = 0.140^{+0.025}_{-0.036}$ and contributes a relative mass fraction of $ΔM_{\rm pasta}/ΔM_{\rm c} = 0.475^{+0.071}_{-0.113}$. Using the resulting crust models, we present the first quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) analysis based on a Bayesian posterior ensemble of neutron-star equations of state and systematically assess their compatibility with observed low-frequency quasi-periodic oscillations. We find that the predicted QPO frequencies are strongly correlated with the curvature of the symmetry energy evaluated at sub-saturation density, $K_{\rm sym}(ρ_0/2)$, and that uncertainties in the equation of state translate into a range of angular indices $\ell$ consistent with the observed frequencies.

## Key claim

The appearance and extent of nuclear pasta layers in neutron-star crusts are primarily controlled by the symmetry-energy slope parameter \(L\) evaluated at half saturation density, and the first Bayesian posterior ensemble analysis of quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) shows that predicted frequencies are strongly correlated with the curvature of the symmetry energy \(K_{\rm sym}(\rho_0/2)\), though only a small fraction of the posterior supports slab, tube, or bubble geometries beyond rods.

## Method

Using a Bayesian posterior ensemble of approximately 40,000 relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state constrained by nuclear experiments, empirical saturation properties, chiral effective field theory, and multimessenger observations, the authors computed the nuclear pasta sequence within a compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with surface and curvature terms fitted to the AME2020 atomic mass evaluation. For each posterior sample, they determined the energetically favored pasta geometry (sphere, rod, slab, tube, or bubble) across the inner crust density range and then solved the one-dimensional eigenvalue equation for crustal torsional shear oscillations in a plane-parallel approximation, adopting a phenomenological prescription for shear-modulus softening in the pasta layer.

## Key numbers

* Transition density from spherical nuclei to cylindrical rods: \(\rho_{\rm sr} = 0.0588^{+0.0045}_{-0.0065}\,\mathrm{fm^{-3}}\)
* Relative radial thickness of the pasta layer: \(\Delta R_{\rm pasta}/\Delta R_{\rm c} = 0.140^{+0.025}_{-0.036}\)
* Relative mass fraction of the pasta layer: \(\Delta M_{\rm pasta}/\Delta M_{\rm c} = 0.475^{+0.071}_{-0.113}\)
* Crust-core transition density: \(\rho_t = 0.0808^{+0.0115}_{-0.0175}\,\mathrm{fm^{-3}}\)
* Pressure at the spherical-to-rod transition: \(P_{\rm sr} = 0.252^{+0.055}_{-0.055}\,\mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}}\)
* Chemical potential at the spherical-to-rod transition: \(\mu_{\rm sr} = 11.04^{+1.54}_{-1.89}\,\mathrm{MeV}\)
* Isospin asymmetry at the spherical-to-rod transition: \(\alpha_{\rm sr} = 0.795^{+0.034}_{-0.037}\)
* Relative thickness of the rod phase only: \(\Delta R_{\rm rod}/\Delta R_{\rm c} = 0.085^{+0.023}_{-0.031}\)
* Relative mass fraction of the rod phase only: \(\Delta M_{\rm rod}/\Delta M_{\rm c} = 0.307^{+0.073}_{-0.109}\)
* Feature importance from decision tree classifier: \(L(\rho_0/2) = 0.77\), \(K_{\rm sym}(\rho_0/2) = 0.22\), \(J(\rho_0/2) = 0.01\)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of unified RMF equations of state from Parmar (2026) to include nuclear pasta phases and their impact on crustal torsional oscillations, building on the compressible liquid-drop model frameworks of Newton et al. (2022) and Dinh Thi et al. (2021). The results are consistent with those earlier studies within uncertainties, though the present work finds systematically thicker pasta layers at the posterior median and uses surface parameters constrained by the AME2020 mass evaluation rather than uniform priors. The QPO analysis, following the formalism of Steiner and Watts (2009), provides the first systematic propagation of equation-of-state uncertainties into torsional-mode predictions, enabling future comparisons with observed low-frequency QPOs from magnetar giant flares.
