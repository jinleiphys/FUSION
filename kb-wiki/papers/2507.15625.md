# The role of scalar current coupling along surfaces

> In this paper we propose a coupling between the complex scalar field and an external Dirac delta-like planar potential. The coupling is achieved through the Klein-Gordon current normal to the plane where the potential is concentrated. The results are obtained exactly and exhibit many peculiarities. We show that a complex scalar charge does not interact with the potential, but the potential modifies the interaction between two scalar charges if they are placed on opposite sides of the planar potential. When the coupling constant between the potential and the field goes to infinity, the classical field solutions satisfy a kind of MIT boundary conditions along the plane where the potential is concentrated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a coupling between a complex scalar field and an external Dirac delta-like planar potential via the Klein-Gordon current normal to the plane, and shows that a single scalar charge does not interact with the potential, but the potential modifies the interaction between two scalar charges only when they are placed on opposite sides of the planar potential.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian for a complex scalar field with a coupling term proportional to the Klein-Gordon current projected onto the normal direction of a plane, using a Dirac delta function to localize the potential. They solve the field equations exactly by deriving the Green function in mixed momentum-coordinate representation, using Fourier transforms in the coordinates parallel to the plane and solving the resulting differential equations in the normal coordinate. The interaction energy between stationary point-like scalar sources is then computed from the Green function by evaluating the energy functional for two delta-function sources.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant λ is dimensionless and defined in the range (−∞, ∞), but the paper considers only λ ≥ 0.
- The Green function for the system contains a factor 1/(1 + λ²/16) in intermediate expressions, and the interaction energy denominators contain (16 + λ²).
- The interaction energy between two scalar charges on opposite sides of the plane is proportional to (16 − λ²)/(16 + λ²) for the diagonal part and 8λ/(16 + λ²) for the off-diagonal part.
- The interaction between charges takes the form of a Yukawa potential e^(−m|B−C|)/|B−C|, with the same exponential factor as the free-field case.

## Context

This work extends the study of delta-like potentials coupled to bosonic fields, which have been used to describe anisotropic planar materials (PhysRevD.85.085010, 2016) and planar boundaries with magnetoelectric properties (Physica Scripta 105018, 2023). The model is inspired by MIT boundary conditions for fermionic fields (Chodos, 1974), where the fermionic current plays a central role, and here the Klein-Gordon current takes an analogous role for scalar fields. The results enable future investigation of whether this type of current coupling can describe material boundaries and surfaces, and the authors note that the approach based on boundary conditions of field modes (as in reference "livromodelossoluveis") remains an open question.
