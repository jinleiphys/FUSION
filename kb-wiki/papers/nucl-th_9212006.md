# Poisson and Porter-Thomas Fluctuations in off-Yrast Rotational Transitions

> Fluctuations associated with stretched E2 transitions from high spin levels in nuclei around $^{168}$Yb are investigated by a cranked shell model extended to include residual two-body interactions. It is found that the gamma-ray energies behave like random variables and the energy spectra show the Poisson fluctuation, in the cranked mean field model without the residual interaction. With two-body residual interaction included, discrete transition pattern with unmixed rotational bands is still valid up to around 600 keV above yrast, in good agreement with experiments. At higher excitation energy, a gradual onset of rotational damping emerges. At 1.8 MeV above yrast, complete damping is observed with GOE type fluctuations for both energy levels and transition strengths(Porter-Thomas fluctuations).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the assumption of random gamma-ray energies for stretched E2 transitions in high-spin rotational bands is valid at high rotational frequencies (above approximately 0.3 MeV), but only when the residual two-body interaction is neglected; with the residual interaction included, discrete unmixed rotational bands persist up to around 600 keV above yrast, and complete rotational damping with GOE-type fluctuations for both energy levels and transition strengths (Porter-Thomas fluctuations) is observed only at approximately 1.8 MeV above yrast.

## Method

The authors extended the cranked shell model by including residual two-body interactions (monopole and quadrupole pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and random Gaussian-distributed matrix elements) and diagonalized the Hamiltonian within a truncated basis of independent-particle configurations (np-nh excitations) for nuclei around 168Yb at rotational frequencies around 0.5 MeV. They calculated stretched E2 transition strengths and gamma-ray energies from the overlap of wave functions at neighboring rotational frequencies, and analyzed the statistical fluctuations of both transition energies and strengths using Poisson statistics, Porter-Thomas distributions, and the Delta_3 statistic from random matrix theory.

## Key numbers

- The random (Poisson) limit for gamma-ray energy fluctuations is achieved for rotational frequencies above 0.3 MeV, corresponding to gamma-ray energies above approximately 0.6 MeV.
- Discrete unmixed rotational bands persist up to approximately 600 keV above yrast, in good agreement with experiments.
- Complete rotational damping with Porter-Thomas strength fluctuations and GOE energy level fluctuations is observed at approximately 1.8 MeV above yrast.
- The rotational damping width extracted from the smoothed strength function is approximately 170-200 keV.
- The average branching number in the Porter-Thomas limit is given by approximately 0.5 times the product of the rotational damping width (200 keV) and the level density.
- The root-mean-square value of the random two-body matrix elements is fixed to 16 keV, based on a spreading width of 0.039 times (A/160)^{-1/2} times E^{3/2} MeV.
- The basis of 1000 states is sufficient to describe levels up to 2.0 MeV from yrast, amounting to over 200 levels for each parity and signature combination.

## Context

This work extends the cranked shell model calculations of S. Aberg (1990-1992) by including residual two-body interactions and analyzing both energy level and transition strength fluctuations. It provides theoretical justification for the fluctuation analysis technique developed by the experimental group (Herskind et al., 1992) which assumes random gamma-ray energies in the quasi-continuum spectra. The results enable quantitative extraction of the number of rotational decay paths from experimental fluctuation data and establish the excitation energy threshold (approximately 1.8 MeV above yrast) where the GOE random matrix description becomes valid for rotating deformed nuclei.
