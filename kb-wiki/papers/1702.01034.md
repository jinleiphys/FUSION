# Four-body calculation of ${}^2\mathrm{H}(d,p){}^3\mathrm{H}$ and ${}^2\mathrm{H}(d,n){}^3\mathrm{He}$ reactions above breakup threshold

> Nucleon transfer reactions in deuteron-deuteron collisions at energies above the three- and four-body breakup threshold are described using exact four-body equations for transition operators that are solved in the momentum-space framework. Differential cross sections, analyzing powers, polarizations, and spin transfer coefficients are obtained using realistic two-nucleon potentials and including the Coulomb repulsion between protons. Overall good agreement between predictions and experimental data is found. Most remarkable discrepancies are seen around the minima of the differential cross section at higher energies and in the outgoing nucleon polarization at lower energies.

# Four-body calculation of \(^2\mathrm{H}(d,p)^3\mathrm{H}\) and \(^2\mathrm{H}(d,n)^3\mathrm{He}\) reactions above breakup threshold

## Key claim

Exact four-body AGS equations solved in momentum space with realistic two-nucleon potentials and Coulomb repulsion provide an overall good description of nucleon transfer reactions in deuteron-deuteron collisions up to 25.3 MeV beam energy, but discrepancies persist around the minima of differential cross sections at higher energies and in outgoing nucleon polarization at lower energies, with the magnitude of disagreement sensitive to the force model used.

## Method

The authors solved the symmetrized Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum space, using the complex-energy method with extrapolation to the \(\varepsilon \to +0\) limit. The Coulomb force between protons was included via screening and renormalization with screening radii of 12 to 16 fm. Calculations employed the INOY04 potential as the primary interaction model, with comparisons at selected energies using CD Bonn, CD Bonn + \(\Delta\), and N3LO potentials.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for both transfer reactions were calculated at deuteron beam energies \(E_d\) from 5.0 to 25.3 MeV as a function of center-of-mass scattering angle \(\Theta_\mathrm{cm}\)
- The one-deuteron breakup threshold is at \(E_d = 4.45\) MeV; the two-deuteron breakup threshold is at \(E_d = 8.90\) MeV
- At \(E_d = 10\) MeV, sensitivity to the NN force model in differential cross sections can reach 15%
- At \(E_d = 25.3\) MeV, the total breakup cross section reaches 287 mb, more than 6 times larger than each transfer cross section
- The INOY04 potential yields triton binding energy 8.49 MeV and \(^3\mathrm{He}\) binding energy 7.73 MeV, compared to experimental values of 8.48 MeV and 7.72 MeV respectively
- The outgoing nucleon polarization peak position is shifted to larger angles by 2 to 8 degrees relative to experiment, with the shift more pronounced at lower beam energies

## Context

This work extends previous exact four-body calculations of two-cluster scattering in the four-nucleon system, both below and above breakup threshold, by Deltuva and collaborators (2012-2015) and Lazauskas (2015). It completes the study of all possible two-cluster reactions in the four-nucleon system using realistic pairwise potentials, bringing four-nucleon scattering to the same level of development as three-nucleon scattering. The results enable future calculation of breakup observables, though the authors note that lack of accurate breakup data makes such effort premature. The paper suggests that the low-energy region with four-nucleon resonances may be the best laboratory for studying NN force models, as greater sensitivity to force models is found there compared to the three-nucleon system.
