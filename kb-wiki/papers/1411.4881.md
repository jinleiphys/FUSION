# Recoil corrections in antikaon-deuteron scattering

> The recoil retardation effect in the $K^-d$ scattering length is studied. Using the non-relativistic effective field theory approach, it is demonstrated that a systematic perturbative expansion of the recoil corrections in the parameter $ξ=M_K/m_N$ is possible in spite of the fact that $K^-d$ scattering at low energies is inherently non-perturbative due to the large values of the $\bar KN$ scattering lengths. The first order correction to the $K^-d$ scattering length due to single insertion of the retardation term in the multiple-scattering series is calculated. The recoil effect turns out to be reasonably small even at the physical value of $M_K/m_N\simeq 0.5$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that recoil corrections to the antikaon-deuteron scattering length can be treated perturbatively in the parameter ξ = M_K/m_N, even though the multiple-scattering series itself is non-perturbative due to the large values of the antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths. The first-order recoil correction is found to be reasonably small even at the physical value ξ ≈ 0.5, justifying the static approximation as a useful starting point.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic effective field theory to separate the antikaon-deuteron scattering amplitude into a static part, which is summed to all orders analytically, and a recoil correction that enters perturbatively through insertions of the retarded Green function Δg. They calculate the first-order recoil correction from three classes of diagrams: self-energy type (kaon scattering on the same nucleon), exchange type (kaon scattering on different nucleons), and diagrams with intermediate nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results are expressed in terms of convolutions of the deuteron wave function with re-summed static amplitudes A_i(r) that satisfy algebraic equations in coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is ξ = M_K/m_N, with physical value M_K/m_N ≈ 0.5
- The static scattering length formula (Eq. 13) includes the kinematic prefactor 1/(1+ξ/2)
- The renormalized self-energy graph vanishes as O(ξ^{1/2}) in the vicinity of the static limit
- Each recoil insertion counts as O(ξ^{1/2}) or O(ξ) in the power counting
- The Green functions are defined as G_st(l) = 4π/l² and G(p,l) = 4π/[l²(1+ξ/2) + 2ξ(p²+γ²)]

## Context

This work extends the framework developed in Baru (2009) for recoil corrections in double scattering to the full multiple-scattering series for antikaon-deuteron scattering. It provides a systematic alternative to numerical Faddeev calculations (Shevchenko 2011, 2012, 2014) and improves upon the static approximation used in earlier extraction formulas (Chand, Kamalov, Meissner 2006, Doring 2011). The results enable a controlled extraction of the antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths from future kaonic deuterium measurements at LNF and J-PARC, by providing a perturbative expansion in which the static contributions are summed to all orders while recoil effects can be calculated order by order.
