# Predictions of α-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient nuclei with the aid of artificial neural network

> In recent years, artificial neural network (ANN) has been successfully applied in nuclear physics and some other areas of physics. This study begins with the calculations of α-decay half-lives for some neutron-deficient nuclei using Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM), temperature dependent Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPMT), Royer empirical formula, new Ren B (NRB) formula, and a trained artificial neural network model (TANN ). By comparison with experimental values, the ANN model is found to give very good descriptions of the half-lives of the neutron-deficient nuclei. Moreover CPPMT is found to perform better than CPPM, indicating the importance of employing temperature-dependent nuclear potential. Furthermore, to predict the α-decay half-lives of unmeasured neutron-deficient nuclei, another ANN algorithm is trained to predict the Q α values. The results of the Q α predictions are compared with the Weizsäcker-Skyrme-4+RBF (WS4+RBF) formula. The half-lives of unmeasured neutron-deficient nuclei are then predicted using CPPM, CPPMT, Royer, NRB, and TANN , with Qα values predicted by ANN as inputs. This study concludes that half-lives of α-decay from neutron-deficient nuclei can successfully be predicted using ANN, and this can contribute to the determination of nuclei at the driplines.

## Key claim

The study claims that artificial neural networks (ANN) can successfully predict α-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient nuclei, with the ANN model achieving the lowest root mean square error (0.3843) among all tested models, though this performance is conditional on using Qα values predicted by a separate ANN as inputs for unmeasured nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated α-decay half-lives for 69 neutron-deficient nuclei (Z=80-118) using the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM), temperature-dependent CPPM (CPPMT), Royer empirical formula, new Ren B (NRB) formula, and a trained artificial neural network (ANN) with a [4,50,100,50,1] architecture trained on 549 α emitters from the NUBASE2020 database. New coefficients for the Royer and NRB formulas were obtained via least-square fitting to the same 549 nuclei. To predict half-lives for 126 unmeasured neutron-deficient nuclei, a separate ANN with a [2,120,120,120,1] architecture was trained on 1021 Qα values from NUBASE2020 to predict Qα values, which were then used as inputs to all five half-life models.

## Key numbers

- ANN model achieved the lowest RMSE of 0.3843 for predicting half-lives of 69 neutron-deficient nuclei, compared to CPPM (0.5946), CPPMT (0.4963), Royer (0.4608), and NRB (0.4413)
- For Qα value prediction, ANN achieved RMSE of 0.1475 on 69 neutron-deficient nuclei, slightly better than WS4+RBF (0.1565) and WS4 (0.2038)
- The ANN trained for half-life prediction used 549 nuclei split into 80% training (439 nuclei) and 20% test (110 nuclei), with training RMSE of 0.3876 and test RMSE of 0.5719
- The ANN trained for Qα prediction used 1021 nuclei split into 80% training and 20% test, with training RMSE of 0.1684 and test RMSE of 0.1802
- New coefficients for the Royer formula were fitted using 549 α emitters (189 even-even, 150 even-odd, 117 odd-even, 93 odd-odd nuclei) yielding overall RMSE of 0.5411

## Context

This work extends previous applications of machine learning in nuclear physics, such as predictions of nuclear charge radii by Akkoyun (2013) and Wu (2020), nuclear β-decay half-lives by Niu (2019), and α-decay Qα values by Ubaldo (2019). The study uses the recently released NUBASE2020 database by Kondev (2021) and Wang (2021) to refit coefficients for the Royer formula (originally from Royer, 2010) and the new Ren B formula (from Akrawy, 2019). The predicted half-lives for unmeasured neutron-deficient nuclei are compared with previous theoretical calculations by Cui (2019) using the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM). The work enables improved predictions for nuclei near the proton drip-line, which can contribute to determining the location of nuclear driplines.
