# Dispersive analysis of the $S$-, $P$-, $D$-, and $F$-wave $ππ$ amplitudes

> A reanalysis of $ππ$ amplitudes for all important partial-waves below about 2 GeV is presented. A set of once subtracted dispersion relations with imposed crossing symmetry condition is used to modify unitary multi-channel amplitudes in the $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ waves. So far, these specific amplitudes constructed in our works and many other analyzes have been fitted only to experimental data and therefore do not fulfill the crossing symmetry condition. In the present analysis, the self consistent, i.e. unitary and fulfilling the crossing symmetry, amplitudes for the $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ waves are formed. The proposed very effective and simple method of modification of the $ππ$ amplitudes does not change their previous-original mathematical structure and the method can be easily applied in various other analyzes.

## Key claim

The authors present a reanalysis of ππ scattering amplitudes for S-, P-, D-, and F-waves that simultaneously satisfies unitarity and crossing symmetry, using once-subtracted GKPY dispersion relations. The key hedge is that the method modifies only selected parameters of the original amplitudes without changing their mathematical structure, and that the improvement from crossing symmetry constraints is less effective for higher partial waves, particularly the F1 wave.

## Method

The authors start with multi-channel unitary amplitudes for S0, P1, D0, and F1 waves that were previously fitted only to experimental data. They impose crossing symmetry by fitting selected resonance and background parameters simultaneously to experimental data and to once-subtracted GKPY dispersion relations (Roy-like equations with one subtraction), using a χ² minimization procedure. The analysis proceeds in three steps: first modifying only S0 and P1 amplitudes (SP analysis), then modifying only D0 and F1 amplitudes (DF analysis), and finally simultaneously refitting all four partial waves (SPDF analysis). The S2 and D2 isotensor waves are taken from prior work and kept fixed.

## Key numbers

- Total χ²/n.d.f. = 1061.5/895 = 1.19 for the final SPDF analysis, compared to initial χ²/n.d.f. = 1520.3/541 = 2.81 for the S and P waves alone
- For the D0 wave fit to experimental data alone: χ²/n.d.f. = 242.28/(199-58) = 1.72, with the pseudo-data contribution giving χ²/n.d.f. = 239.28/(183-58) = 1.91
- For the F1 wave two-channel fit: χ²/n.d.f. = 136.20/(108-6) = 1.34, with the pseudo-data contribution giving χ²/n.d.f. = 80.84/(77-6) = 1.38
- The ρ₃(1690) resonance mass in the New F1 amplitude is 1714.0 MeV, compared to the PDG value of 1688.8 ± 2.1 MeV
- The f₂(1270) resonance mass in the New D0 amplitude is 1275.5 MeV, matching the PDG value of 1275.5 ± 0.8 MeV
- The χ² for dispersion relations in the SPDF analysis improved from 313.2 (initial) to 113.9 (re-fitted) for all waves combined

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive analysis of S- and P-wave amplitudes (Bydžovský et al., 2014) to include D- and F-waves, using GKPY dispersion relations for the higher partial waves that were developed in a separate work (Bydžovský et al., 2016). It builds on the framework established by the Madrid-Kraków group (GKPY equations) and the Bern group (Roy equations), which previously constrained only S- and P-waves below 1100 MeV. The resulting self-consistent amplitudes can be used to account for final-state interactions in heavy meson decays and photoproduction processes at experiments such as CLAS12 and GlueX at JLab, and to construct full ππ scattering cross sections at low energies.
