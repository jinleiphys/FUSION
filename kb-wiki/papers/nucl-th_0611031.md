# Pairing correlations beyond the mean field

> We discuss dynamical pairing correlations in the context of configuration mixing of projected self-consistent mean-field states, and the origin of a divergence that might appear when such calculations are done using an energy functional in the spirit of a naive generalized density functional theory.

## Key claim

Dynamical pairing correlations beyond the mean field can be incorporated through configuration mixing of particle-number projected HFB states within the generator coordinate method, but the energy gain from such mixing is small (around 100–200 keV for ¹²⁰Sn), and a divergence in the projected energy appears when using an energy density functional rather than a Hamiltonian, specifically when a single-particle level crosses the Fermi energy with occupation v²_μ = 0.5.

## Method

The authors solve HFB equations with the Skyrme interaction SLy4 and a density-dependent pairing interaction with a soft cutoff at 5 MeV above and below the Fermi energy, using the two-basis method. They complement the HFB equation with the Lipkin-Nogami method to enforce pairing correlations in the weak pairing regime, then project onto exact particle-number eigenstates using a discretized Fomenko integration over the gauge angle φ. For ¹²⁰Sn, they generate a family of HFB states by varying a "generating pairing strength" V_c, recalculate the energy of each state with the physical pairing strength V₀ = −1000 MeV fm³, and then mix the projected states via the generator coordinate method to obtain the ground state.

## Key numbers

- The pairing interaction uses a soft cutoff at 5 MeV above and below the Fermi energy.
- The physical pairing strength for neutrons is V₀ = −1000 MeV fm³.
- The HFB equations without LN corrections break down to HF at values of the generating pairing strength around −500 MeV fm³.
- The additional energy gain from the GCM of projected states is around 100 keV when starting with HFB-LN states, and about 200 keV for pure HFB states in ¹²⁰Sn.
- For ¹⁸O, the integrals over gauge angles are numerically converged for L = 5 discretization points.
- For light nuclei, L ranges from 5; for heavy nuclei, up to 13 discretization points are sufficient for numerical convergence of the integrals over φ.

## Context

This work extends the method of configuration mixing of projected self-consistent mean-field states, previously developed for angular-momentum projection (Bender et al., 2005), to the treatment of dynamical pairing correlations via particle-number projection. It builds on earlier exploratory studies of pairing vibrations within realistic mean-field models (Meyer et al., 1991; Heenen et al., 2001) and addresses the divergence problem in particle-number projection noted by Donau (1998) and Anguiano et al. (2001), as well as the finite step identified by Stoitsov et al. (2005). The analysis enables future systematic studies of dynamical pairing correlations, better constraints on pairing, excited states, and coupling with deformation modes.
