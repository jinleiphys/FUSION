# Anisotropic momentum broadening in the 2+1D Glasma: analytic weak field approximation and lattice simulations

> In heavy ion collisions, transverse momentum broadening quantifies the modification of a hard probe due to interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We calculate momentum broadening in the Glasma, which is the highly non-isotropic precursor stage of the QGP right after the collision. We show that the Glasma leads to anisotropic momentum broadening: high energy partons accumulate more momentum along the beam axis than transverse to it. The physical origin of anisotropic broadening can be traced back to differences in the shapes of chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic flux tubes in the Glasma. We provide semi-analytic results for momentum broadening in the dilute Glasma and numerical results from real-time lattice simulations of the non-perturbative dense Glasma.

## Key claim

The Glasma leads to anisotropic momentum broadening, with high-energy partons accumulating more momentum along the beam axis than transverse to it, and this anisotropy is directly linked to differences in the average spatial shapes of chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic flux tubes (their spatial autocorrelation functions) rather than to longitudinal expansion or flow of the medium.

## Method

The authors compute momentum broadening of an ultrarelativistic test particle traversing the Glasma using a gauge-invariant formalism based on lightlike Wilson loops and classical colored particle dynamics (Wong's equations). They derive semi-analytic results in the weak-field (dilute) approximation using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the nuclear color charge densities, and perform real-time lattice simulations of the classical Yang-Mills equations in 2+1D for the non-perturbative dense Glasma, solving the boost-invariant equations in Milne coordinates.

## Key numbers

- For SU(3), the ratio of accumulated squared transverse momentum for a gluon versus a quark is \(C_A/C_F = 9/4\), following Casimir scaling.
- The infrared regulator \(m\) in the MV model is chosen on the order of \(\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD} \approx 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to enforce color neutrality on transverse length scales of \(\sim 1\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- The MV model parameter \(\mu_A\) is given in units of energy and defines the two-point correlator of color charge densities via \(g^2 \mu_A^2 \lambda_A(x^-) \delta^{ab} \delta(x^- - y^-) \delta^{(2)}(\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{y})\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet quenching in the Glasma, such as the synchrotron-like gluon emission estimate by Aurenche (2012) which relied on a small-angle approximation valid only for high-\(p_T\) hadrons, and the lattice calculation by Schenke (2008) using Wong-Yang-Mills equations that did not use typical Glasma initial conditions or account for longitudinal expansion. It also contrasts with anisotropic broadening mechanisms proposed for the quark-gluon plasma, such as kinetic theory calculations by Romatschke (2006), non-Abelian plasma instabilities by Dumitru (2008), and gauge/gravity duality results by Gubser (2012) and Rebhan (2012). The results enable systematic inclusion of pre-hydrodynamic Glasma effects in jet quenching phenomenology, particularly for the energy regime \(\omega \lesssim 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) where Glasma contributions may be non-negligible.
