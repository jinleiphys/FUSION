# Application of the Kerman-Klein method to the solution of a spherical shell model for a deformed rare-earth nucleus

> Core-particle coupling models are made viable by assuming that core properties such as matrix elements of multipole and pairing operators and excitation spectra are known independently. From the completeness relation, it is seen, however, that these quantities are themselves algebraic functions of the calculated core-particle amplitudes. For the deformed rare-earth nucleus 158Gd, we find that these sum rules are well-satisfied for the ground state band, implying that we have found a self-consistent solution of the non-linear Kerman-Klein equations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a self-consistent solution of the non-linear Kerman-Klein equations can be achieved for the deformed rare-earth nucleus 158Gd, with sum rules satisfied to within 10 percent, but only when contributions from a large number of high-lying solutions of the eigenvalue problem are included, and the normalization of the coefficients of fractional parentage has not yet been properly incorporated into the algorithm.

## Method

The authors start from a spherical shell model with a Wood-Saxon single-particle potential and a Hamiltonian containing single-particle terms, mass quadrupole (L=2) interactions, and monopole pairing interactions. They calculate coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP) for the odd nuclei 157Gd and 157Eu, then use completeness relations to express core matrix elements (quadrupole moments, pairing matrix elements, and excitation spectra) as quadratic sums of these CFP, solving the resulting non-linear Kerman-Klein equations iteratively to check self-consistency for the ground-state band of 158Gd.

## Key numbers

- For 158Gd, the calculated total neutron number is 95.70 compared to the actual value of 94; the calculated total proton number is 65.16 compared to the actual value of 64.
- The neutron quadrupole matrix elements yield a proportionality constant eta = 1.1 relative to the proton quadrupole matrix elements.
- Diagonal neutron pairing matrix elements vary monotonically from 1.859 (I=0) to 1.814 (I=8), compared with the experimental gap parameter of 1.65.
- Diagonal proton pairing matrix elements vary from 1.672 (I=0) to 1.612 (I=8), compared with the experimental value of 1.321.
- The total calculated moment of inertia is 0.011951 (in the paper's units), compared with the experimental value of 0.0124.

## Context

This work extends the Kerman-Klein method, originally proposed by Kerman and Klein (1963) and previously applied with only modest success to semi-magic nuclei by Dreiss et al. (1971) and Li and Klein (1979). The authors build on their own recent core-particle coupling (CPC) calculations for odd deformed nuclei (Protopapas, Klein, and Walet, 1994, 1996; Protopapas and Klein, 1997) and demonstrate a new route from the spherical shell model to deformed rare-earth nuclei. The next steps are to add quadrupole pairing interactions and to properly formulate the normalization of the coefficients of fractional parentage.
