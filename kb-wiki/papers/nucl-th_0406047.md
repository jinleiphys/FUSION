# Chiral dynamics of the two Lambda(1405) states

> The Lambda(1405) resonance is studied by a chiral unitary approach, in which the resonance is dynamically generated in coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering. Investigating the analytic structure of the scattering amplitudes obtained by the chiral unitary approach, we find two poles around the energies of the Lambda(1405) coupling differently to the meson-baryon states. We reach the conclusion that the Lambda(1405) resonance seen in experiments is not just one single resonance, but a superposition of these two states.

## Key claim
The Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single resonance but a superposition of two distinct poles in the scattering amplitude, which couple differently to meson-baryon channels; the lower pole couples strongly to pi-Sigma states while the higher pole couples dominantly to anti-K N states.

## Method
The authors used a chiral unitary approach for coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering with strangeness S=-1 and isospin I=0, respecting flavor SU(3) symmetry with octet mesons (pi, K, eta) and octet baryons (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi). Unitarity was imposed by summing diagrams non-perturbatively using an N/D-like method, taking only s-channel unitarity and limiting the model space to one meson and one baryon states to obtain an analytic solution. The pole positions and coupling strengths were extracted from the scattering amplitude in the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers
- Two pole positions in the second Riemann sheet: 1390 - 66i MeV and 1426 - 16i MeV
- A third pole at 1680 - 20i MeV (corresponding to Lambda(1670))
- Coupling strength of the lower pole (1390 - 66i MeV) to pi-Sigma: |g_i| = 2.9
- Coupling strength of the higher pole (1426 - 16i MeV) to anti-K N: |g_i| = 2.7
- Coupling strength of the lower pole to anti-K N: |g_i| = 2.1
- Coupling strength of the higher pole to pi-Sigma: |g_i| = 1.5

## Context
This work extends earlier findings of two poles near the Lambda(1405) in the cloudy bag model (Fink, year not given) and in prior chiral dynamics studies (Oller, year not given). The results are based on the chiral unitary approach developed in earlier work by the same group (Oller, year not given; Bennhold, year not given). The double pole structure predicts that reactions initiated by anti-K N states will produce a narrower pi-Sigma mass distribution peaking at higher energy than those from pi-Sigma initiated reactions, which can be tested in experiments such as K- p to gamma Lambda(1405) (Nacher, 1999) or photo-induced K* production on protons (Hyodo, 2004).
