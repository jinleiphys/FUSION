# Isoscaling of projectile-like fragments

> The isotopic and isotonic distributions of the projectile fragmentation products have been simulated by a modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model and the isoscaling behavior of projectile-like fragments has been discussed. The isoscaling parameters $α$ and $β$ for hot fragments before evaporation and cold fragments after evaporation have been extracted, respectively. It looks that the evaporation has strong effect on $α$. For cold fragments, a monotonic increase of $α$ and $|β|$ with the increasing of $Z$ and $N$ is observed. The relation between isoscaling parameter and the change of isospin content is demonstrated. In addition, the disappearance of the isospin effect of projectile fragmentation is also discussed in the viewpoint of isoscaling parameter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the isoscaling parameters α and β for projectile-like fragments from the reactions ⁴⁰Ar+⁹Be and ³⁶Ar+⁹Be at 60 MeV/nucleon are strongly affected by evaporation, with α changing dramatically after evaporation while β changes only weakly; the authors caution that the pre-fragments from the abrasion stage are not in equilibrium, so the analysis for them is not strict isoscaling but a useful comparison.

## Method

The authors used a modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model to simulate projectile fragmentation for ⁴⁰Ar+⁹Be and ³⁶Ar+⁹Be at 60 MeV/nucleon. In the abrasion stage, the model describes nucleon-nucleon collisions in the overlap zone using a picture of interacting tubes with binomial distributions for abraded neutrons and protons, and assigns an excitation energy of 13.3 MeV per abraded nucleon. In the ablation stage, the excited pre-fragments deexcite by cascade evaporation of light particles, primarily neutrons. The yield ratios R₂₁(N,Z) were extracted for both hot (pre-evaporation) and cold (post-evaporation) fragments, and the isoscaling parameters α and β were obtained by exponential fits to the ratios as functions of N (for fixed Z) and Z (for fixed N), respectively.

## Key numbers

- The reactions studied are ⁴⁰Ar+⁹Be and ³⁶Ar+⁹Be at 60 MeV/nucleon.
- The excitation energy of the projectile spectator is estimated as E* = 13.3 ⟨ΔA(b)⟩ MeV, where 13.3 is the mean excitation energy per abraded nucleon.
- For hot pre-fragments, α shows a slight decreasing trend with increasing Z (Z from 10 to 17), while |β| increases monotonically with increasing N.
- For cold fragments after evaporation, a monotonic increase of α with increasing Z and of |β| with increasing N is observed.
- After evaporation, ⟨N⟩/Z for cold fragments tends to 1.1 in lower Z and bifurcates in higher Z for the two systems.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscaling, previously observed in multifragmentation events for light particles by Tsang et al. (2001), to projectile-like fragments in projectile fragmentation reactions. It uses the SAA model, which was developed by Brohm (1994) and later modified by the authors' group (Cai et al., 2002; Fang et al., 2000, 2001; Zhong et al., 2003), and compares with empirical parameterizations like EPAX/EPAX II by Summerer et al. (1999, 2000). The results enable a better understanding of how evaporation modifies isoscaling parameters and how the isospin content difference between two systems can serve as a simple measure of the isoscaling parameter α.
