# Review of opportunities at the HESR-Collider with PANDA at FAIR

> Exciting new scientific opportunities are presented for the PANDA detector at the High Energy Storage Ring in the redefined $\overline{\text{p}} \text{p}(A)$ collider mode, HESR-C, at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) in Europe. The high luminosity, $L \sim 10^{31}$ cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$, and a wide range of intermediate and high energies, $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ up to 30 GeV for $\overline{\text{p}} \text{p}(A)$ collisions will allow to explore a wide range of exciting topics in QCD, including the study of the production of excited open charm and bottom states, nuclear bound states containing heavy (anti)quarks, the interplay of hard and soft physics in the dilepton production, probing short-range correlations in nuclei, and the exploration of the early, complete $\overline{\text{p}}$-p-annihilation phase, where an intially pure Yang-Mills gluon plasma is formed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a future collider mode of the HESR ring (HESR-C) with the PANDA detector at FAIR, operating at center-of-mass energies up to 30 GeV and luminosities up to 5 × 10^31 cm^−2 s^−1, would enable unique studies of heavy-quark physics and QCD that are complementary to LHC programs, though the authors acknowledge that many of the most exotic channels (such as double-heavy baryons) would require either higher luminosities or longer running times than the baseline configuration provides.

## Method

The authors present a conceptual physics case for a proton-antiproton and antiproton-nucleus collider mode at the HESR ring, using the existing PANDA detector as a midrapidity detector. They estimate production rates for heavy-quark states using perturbative QCD cross-section calculations (threshold-resummed for bottom pairs) combined with double-parton interaction mechanisms, and extrapolate event yields based on a conservative startup luminosity of 4 × 10^30 cm^−2 s^−1 and a one-year running time of 10^7 seconds. The paper also reviews the accelerator modifications needed, including additional proton injection lines from SIS18, a full-energy electron cooler (8 MeV), and the Recuperated Experimental Storage Ring (RESR) for antiproton stacking.

## Key numbers

* Luminosity in symmetric pbar-p collider mode: up to 5 × 10^31 cm^−2 s^−1 at sqrt(s) ≈ 30 GeV
* Conservative startup luminosity without RESR: 4 × 10^30 cm^−2 s^−1
* Center-of-mass energy reach: sqrt(s_NN) up to 30 GeV for pbar-p, up to 19 GeV for pbar-A
* Bottom pair production cross section at sqrt(s) = 30 GeV: sigma_bbar = 1.8 × 10^−2 microbarn (with ~30% relative uncertainty)
* Charm pair production cross section: sigma_ccbar = 30 microbarn
* Yearly event yields at startup luminosity: N_bbar = 10^6, N_ccbar = 10^9
* Yearly events with two c-cbar pairs: N_ccbar,ccbar = 10^6
* Expected yearly events with b-bbar-c-cbar: about 10^3
* For b-bbar-b-bbar production: ~10^2 events would require a one-year run at luminosity 10^33 cm^−2 s^−1
* For bcc-bbar-cbar-cbar production: ~10^2 events would require luminosity 10^33 cm^−2 s^−1
* Suppression factor for chi_b resonance production relative to chi_c: approximately 10^−7
* Coherent hypernuclei production cross sections: order of a few 10 nb at p_lab ~ 20 GeV/c

## Context

This paper extends the physics case for the PANDA experiment beyond its baseline fixed-target program at sqrt(s) < 5.5 GeV, building on earlier collider design studies by Stocker (2015) and accelerator concepts by Bradamante (2005) and Lehrach (2005). The work is motivated by recent discoveries of exotic heavy-quark states (reviewed by Karliner, 2015) and by TOTEM measurements at the LHC (Antchev, 2017, 2018) suggesting Odderon contributions that would be best studied at lower energies. The proposed collider mode would enable searches for double-heavy baryons and mesons that complement LHC programs, and would allow measurements of color fluctuations in nucleons that have been modeled by Alvioli (2014, 2017) to explain LHC p-Pb and RHIC d-Au data.
