# Inclusive Spectra of (p,xp) and (p,xd) Reactions on $^{90,92}$Zr and $^{92}$Mo Nuclei at E$_{p}$=30.3 MeV

> New experimental data for the inclusive reactions (p,xp) and (p,xd) on isotopes of the nuclei $^{90,92}$Zr and $^{92}$Mo, have been measured at E$_{p}$=30.3 MeV, which has not been investigated in detail so far. We show the extension of the pre-equilibrium reactions to this energy region and interpret the results of these experiments. Moreover, we display the mechanism of the reaction and the level of energy-dependence. The adequacy of the theoretical models in explaining the measured experimental data is also discussed. In our theoretical analysis, the contributions of multi-step direct and compound processes in the formation of cross-sections are determined and we assert that the traditional frameworks are valid for the description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors assert that traditional theoretical frameworks (the exciton model, the multi-step direct/compound theory, and the Hauser-Feshbach model) are adequate for describing the measured inclusive (p,xp) and (p,xd) cross-sections on 90,92Zr and 92Mo at 30.3 MeV, but note that the PRECO-D2 calculation shows a discrepancy in the soft part of the spectra, which they attribute to the neglect of multi-particle emission in that code.

## Method

Double-differential cross-sections for (p,xp) and (p,xd) reactions on 90,92Zr and 92Mo were measured using a three-detector telescope (two silicon surface-barrier detectors and a CsI(Tl) scintillator) on the U-150M cyclotron at 30.3 MeV, covering angles from 15 to 150 degrees in 15-degree steps. The data were analyzed using the Hauser-Feshbach model (with multi-particle emission), the quantum-mechanical multi-step direct (MSD) and multi-step compound (MSC) theory via the EMPIRE II code, and the phenomenological exciton model via the PRECO-D2 code. The contributions of single-step direct processes (knock-out for protons, one-nucleon transfer for deuterons) were also estimated.

## Key numbers

*   Total (p,xp) cross-section on 90Zr: 973.3 ± 8.5 mb (outgoing energy range 3-27 MeV).
*   Total (p,xp) cross-section on 92Zr: 839.6 ± 6.4 mb (outgoing energy range 3-27 MeV).
*   Total (p,xp) cross-section on 92Mo: 1229.0 ± 10.2 mb (outgoing energy range 3-27 MeV).
*   Total (p,xd) cross-section on 90Zr: 47.39 ± 0.59 mb (outgoing energy range 4-17 MeV).
*   Total (p,xd) cross-section on 92Zr: 28.83 ± 0.38 mb (outgoing energy range 4-17 MeV).
*   Total (p,xd) cross-section on 92Mo: 33.25 ± 0.51 mb (outgoing energy range 4-17 MeV).
*   Incident proton energy: 30.3 ± 0.15 MeV.
*   Energy resolution of the spectrometer: approximately 400 keV for the ΔE1xΔE2 matrix and 800 keV for the ΔE2xE3 matrix.
*   Proton spectra were measured from a threshold of approximately 2.5 MeV up to approximately 30 MeV; deuteron spectra from approximately 2.5 to approximately 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends pre-equilibrium reaction studies to 30.3 MeV for 90,92Zr and 92Mo, a region not previously investigated in detail, and addresses a gap in low-energy proton data (below 7 MeV) that was present in earlier measurements by Watanabe et al. on 98Mo at 26 MeV. The results enable tests of the FKK theory and the exciton model at this higher energy, and the data are relevant for applications in hybrid nuclear energy systems where zirconium and molybdenum are construction materials. The analysis builds on prior work by the Kyushu group on 90Zr and other nuclei at 26 MeV, and on the theoretical frameworks of Tamura, Udagawa and Lenske (multi-step direct theory) and Nishioka et al. (multi-step compound theory).
