# Diffusion in superfluid Fermi mixtures: General formalism

> With neutron star applications in mind, we developed a theory of diffusion in mixtures of superfluid, strongly interacting Fermi liquids. By employing the Landau theory of Fermi liquids, we determined matrices that relate the currents of different particle species, their momentum densities, and the partial entropy currents to each other. Using these results, and applying the quasiclassical kinetic equation for the Bogoliubov excitations, we derived general expressions for the diffusion coefficients, which properly incorporate all the Fermi liquid effects and depend on the momentum transfer rates between different particle species. The developed framework can be used as a starting point for systematic calculations of the diffusion coefficients (as well as other kinetic coefficients) in superfluid Fermi mixtures, particularly, in superfluid neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general formalism for calculating diffusion coefficients in superfluid, strongly interacting Fermi mixtures using Landau Fermi-liquid theory, but the derivation is restricted to the linear approximation in small hydrodynamic velocities (where the half Cooper-pair momentum satisfies \(Q_\alpha/p_{F\alpha} \ll 1\)) and assumes isotropic pairing gaps, with the anisotropic neutron gap in neutron star applications replaced by an effective isotropic gap.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid theory to derive expressions for the normal currents (particle currents, momentum densities, and partial entropy currents) in a two-component superfluid Fermi mixture, using the quasiclassical kinetic equation for Bogoliubov excitations. They determine the matrices relating these currents to each other and to the chemical potential gradients, and derive general expressions for the diffusion coefficients that depend on momentum transfer rates between different particle species, incorporating all Fermi-liquid effects through the Landau parameters \(f_l^{\alpha\alpha'}\) and the entrainment matrix.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is given as \(2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), used as the threshold density for neutron star inner layers.
- The neutron star mass is approximately \(M \approx 1.4 M_\odot\) and radius \(R \approx 12\) km.
- The Fermi momentum for particle species \(\alpha\) is expressed as \(p_{F\alpha} = (3 \pi^2 n_\alpha)^{1/3}\).
- The function \(\Phi_\alpha\) changes from \(\Phi_\alpha = 0\) at \(T = 0\) to \(\Phi_\alpha = 1\) at \(T \geq T_{c\alpha}\), where \(T_{c\alpha}\) is the critical temperature for superfluidity of species \(\alpha\).
- The symmetric matrix \(G_{\alpha\alpha'}\) is defined as \(G_{\alpha\alpha'} = \frac{1}{9\pi^4} p_{F\alpha}^2 p_{F\alpha'}^2 f_1^{\alpha\alpha'}\), where \(f_1^{\alpha\alpha'}\) are the \(l=1\) Landau parameters.

## Context

This work extends the theory of diffusion in normal (nonsuperfluid) Fermi mixtures developed by Anderson, Pethick, and Quader (1987) and the subsequent calculations by Yakovlev and Shalybkov (1990, 1991) to the superfluid regime, incorporating Fermi-liquid effects that were previously neglected in superfluid transport studies. The formalism enables systematic calculations of diffusion coefficients and other kinetic coefficients in superfluid neutron stars, where baryon superfluidity (e.g., neutrons and protons) dramatically modifies the hydrodynamics and dissipative properties, and can be used as a starting point for calculations of magnetic field evolution and oscillation damping in neutron stars.
