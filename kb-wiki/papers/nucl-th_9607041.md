# O(4) Expansion of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation

> The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is expanded on a hyperspherical basis, thereby reducing the original 4-dimensional integral equation into an infinite set of coupled 1-dimensional ones. It is shown that this representation offers a highly accurate method to determine numerically the bound state solutions. For generic cases only a few hyperspherical waves are needed to achieve convergence, both for the ground state as well as for radially or orbitally excited states. The wave function is reconstructed for several cases and in particular it is shown that it becomes independent of the relative time in the nonrelativistic regime.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude can be expanded on a hyperspherical O(4) basis to reduce the 4-dimensional integral equation to an infinite set of coupled 1-dimensional integral equations, and for generic cases only a few hyperspherical waves are needed to achieve convergence, particularly when the exchanged mass or binding energy is sizable compared to the constituent masses.

## Method

The authors expand the vertex function of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation in 4-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics Z_{klm}(χ,θ,φ) after a Wick rotation, rather than using the standard O(3) partial wave decomposition. This expansion transforms the original 4-dimensional integral equation into an infinite set of coupled 1-dimensional integral equations that are diagonal in the orbital quantum number l and independent of the magnetic quantum number m. The kernel matrix elements V_k(q,q') and S_{kk'}^l(q) are computed explicitly for the case of two scalar particles interacting via exchange of another scalar particle in the ladder approximation.

## Context

This work extends earlier approximate lowest-order hyperspherical expansion results by Gourdin (1960s) and provides a systematic method to improve upon them by solving the full set of integral equations until desired accuracy is achieved. It contrasts with standard approaches that use O(3) partial wave decomposition (Levine, Zuilhof) which reduce the equation to 2-dimensional form requiring large matrices, and with variational calculations (Zwart, Vosko) which are harder to apply to excited states. The method enables accurate numerical determination of bound state solutions including radially and orbitally excited states, and allows reconstruction of the wave function in both (3+1) and (2+1) dimensions.
