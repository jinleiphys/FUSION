# Deuteron Structure Functions in the Context of Few-Body Physics

> Few-body systems offer a unique challenge to those interested in deep inelastic scattering. Using the deuteron as the simplest and most easily solvable nuclear system we review the main physics issues in the valence and sea quark regimes. For the former the key issue is the understanding of Fermi motion and binding corrections, and our ability to extract the ratio F_2n/F_2p as x -> 1. The most recent analysis of deuteron data for F_2n/F_2p is consistent with perturbative QCD, but inconsistent with the assumptions common to all standard parton distribution fits. This should be taken into account in calculating event rates at HERA --- especially at large x and Q^2. The extraction of g_1n also presents an opportunity to refine the description of nuclear effects in spin-dependent structure functions. In the region of small x one must correct for shadowing and meson exchange current effects in order to explore the flavor structure of sea, which is, in turn, deeply related to the chiral structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The most recent analysis of deuteron data for the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) is consistent with perturbative QCD predictions (which give \(F_2^n/F_2^p \to 3/7\) as \(x \to 1\)) but inconsistent with the assumption common to all standard parton distribution fits that \(d/u \to 0\) as \(x \to 1\); this discrepancy should be taken into account when calculating event rates at HERA, especially at large \(x\) and \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors review the covariant, relativistic impulse approximation for the deuteron structure function, using a fully off-shell formalism that includes a covariant \(DNN\) vertex and an off-shell photon–nucleon scattering amplitude. They then apply a deconvolution procedure (the Bodek smearing method) to SLAC electron–deuteron deep-inelastic scattering data to extract \(F_2^n\) and the \(d/u\) ratio, comparing results from the off-shell model with those from an on-shell, infinite-momentum-frame treatment. For the small-\(x\) region, they model nuclear shadowing using a two-phase approach combining diffractive Pomeron exchange and vector meson dominance.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) extracted using the off-shell deuteron model approaches a value broadly consistent with the perturbative QCD prediction of \(3/7\) as \(x \to 1\), whereas the on-shell formalism gave a ratio tending to \(1/4\).
* The extracted \(d/u\) ratio from the off-shell analysis is consistent with the perturbative QCD prediction \(d/u \to 1/5\) as \(x \to 1\), contrary to the expectation of models in which \(d/u \to 0\).
* The spin-dependent structure function extraction using the simple non-relativistic depolarization formula (with \(\omega_D = 4.7\%\)) is reliable at the 2% level for \(x\) below 0.7, but fails dramatically for \(x > 0.7\).
* A change of the deuteron \(D\)-state probability \(\omega_D\) by 2% (e.g., from 4% to 6%) leads to an error of order 10% or more in the extracted \(g_1^n\).
* The shadowing correction to \(F_2^D\) at small \(x\) is only a few percent in total; relativistic corrections to the deuteron wave function amount to a few percent of this already small correction and can be neglected.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on extracting neutron structure functions from deuteron data, building on the early impulse-approximation treatments of Kuti and Weisskopf (1971), Atwood and West (1973), and Bodek et al. (1979), as well as the on-shell light-front approach of Frankfurt and Strikman (1988). The fully off-shell covariant formalism developed by Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1994) and Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996) is used to reanalyze the SLAC data of Whitlow et al. (1992) and Gomez et al. (1994), yielding a significantly different \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) ratio at large \(x\) that favors the perturbative QCD prediction of Farrar and Jackson (1975) and Brodsky, Burkardt, and Schmidt (1995) over the SU(6)-breaking model of Close (1973) and Carlitz (1975). The work enables improved tests of perturbative QCD at large \(x\), more reliable background estimates for the HERA high-\(Q^2\) anomaly, and a refined framework for extracting spin-dependent neutron structure functions from polarized deuteron targets.
