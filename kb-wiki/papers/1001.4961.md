# Isospin emission and flows at high baryon density: a test of the symmetry potential

> High energy Heavy Ion Collisions (HIC) are studied in order to access nuclear matter properties at high density. Particular attention is paid to the selection of observables sensitive to the poorly known symmetry energy at high baryon density, of large fundamental interest, even for the astrophysics implications. Using fully consistent transport simulations built on effective theories we test isospin observables ranging from nucleon/cluster emissions to collective flows (in particular the elliptic, squeeze out, part). The effects of the competition between stiffness and momentum dependence of the Symmetry Potential on the reaction dynamics are thoroughly analyzed. In this way we try to shed light on the controversial neutron/proton effective mass splitting at high baryon and isospin densities. New, more exclusive, experiments are suggested.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the neutron/proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric nuclear matter at high baryon density can be disentangled from the stiffness of the symmetry energy by measuring isospin ratios and elliptic flows of fast nucleons and light clusters, because these observables are more sensitive to the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential than to its density dependence.

## Method

The authors performed fully consistent Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport simulations of central and semi-central \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at 400\(A\)MeV and 600\(A\)MeV, using a generalized effective interaction with momentum-dependent isoscalar and isovector mean fields. They systematically varied the stiffness of the symmetry energy (Asysoft vs. Asystiff) and the sign of the neutron/proton effective mass splitting (\(m_n^* > m_p^*\) vs. \(m_n^* < m_p^*\)) while keeping the symmetry energy at saturation fixed to 33 MeV, then analyzed the isospin content and elliptic flow of emitted nucleons and light ions at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter \(L\) is about 10–20 MeV for the Asysoft choice and about 70–100 MeV for the Asystiff choice.
* The symmetry energy at saturation is fixed to \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 33\) MeV for all parametrizations.
* In central collisions at 400\(A\)MeV and 600\(A\)MeV, the interacting zone reaches baryon densities of about \(1.7-1.8 \rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)) for a transient time of 15–20 fm/c.
* The isospin yield ratio curves for nucleons and light ions cross at \(p_t \simeq 2.13\) fm\(^{-1}\), corresponding to the Fermi momentum of a source at baryon density \(\rho \simeq 1.6 \rho_0\).
* The stopping power parameter \(Vartl\) (ratio of transverse to longitudinal rapidity variances) is about 0.75 at both beam energies, independent of the isovector contributions.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies of the symmetry energy at high density (Baran et al., 2005; Colonna et al., 1998; Di Toro et al., 2008) by explicitly including momentum-dependent isovector interactions that produce a neutron/proton effective mass splitting, an effect that remains highly controversial in many-body theory (Fuchs et al., 2006). The results suggest that new exclusive experiments with high transverse momentum selection, such as those planned by the ASYEOS Collaboration at SIS-GSI and at RIKEN-Tokyo and CSR-Lanzhou, can disentangle the stiffness of the symmetry potential from the effective mass splitting, which is also relevant for relativistic bosonic models of nuclear matter and chiral symmetry breaking.
