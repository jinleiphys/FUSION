# Muon capture rates from precision measurements of the muon disappearance

> In a typical experiment the nuclear μ-capture rate is determined from measurements of the time constant of the muon decay exponential in a target. We propose a new approach for the evaluation of the μ-capture rate, which is based on direct measurements of the μ^- disappearance rate in the target. Considering, as an example, the reaction of μ-capture on proton, we demonstrate that measurements of the μ^- disappearance at different values of the hydrogen gas pressure in the target could be used to avoid a pressure-dependent correction and determine the "vacuum" μp -> νn reaction rate with accuracy better than 10^{-2} after extrapolation to zero gas density. The proposed method could be used to perform precision measurements of the μ^- capture rate on different types of nuclei in one experiment.

# Muon capture rates from precision measurements of the muon disappearance

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the "vacuum" muon capture rate on protons can be determined with accuracy better than 10^{-2} by directly measuring the muon disappearance rate in a hydrogen gas target at different pressures, then extrapolating to zero gas density, thereby avoiding the pressure-dependent correction from muon capture in pp-mu molecular states that limited previous experiments.

## Method
The method uses a 4-pi hermetic BGO electromagnetic calorimeter (ECAL) surrounding a hydrogen gas target to detect the energy deposited by all muon processes. Negative muons stopped in the target produce either ordinary muon decay (mu -> e nu_e nu_mu) with a detectable electron, or muon capture on proton (mu- p -> nu_mu n) where the neutrino and most recoil neutron energy are invisible, resulting in zero or near-zero ECAL energy deposition. The number of capture signal events is extracted by subtracting the background from low-energy decay electrons, which is determined from the measured ECAL energy spectrum of positive muon decays (where no capture occurs) after correcting for the small difference between electron and positron energy loss in the target vessel material.

## Key numbers
- The MuCap collaboration singlet capture rate: Lambda_S = (714.9 +/- 5.4_stat +/- 5.4_syst) s^{-1}, corresponding to a branching fraction Br(mu- p -> nu_mu n) approximately 10^{-3} determined with 10^{-2} precision
- The proposed method aims for accuracy better than 10^{-2} in the vacuum muon capture rate after extrapolation to zero gas density
- The positive muon lifetime from MuLan collaboration: tau_mu = 2.1969812 +/- 0.0000038 microseconds (precision approximately 1 ppm)
- For 10^7 muon decays, the ratio of background electron events determined from mu+ decay spectrum to true background from mu- decays: n_mu+(E < E_th)/n_mu-(E < E_th) = 0.985 +/- 0.012
- The fraction of muon decay electrons with energy below 5 MeV threshold is approximately 10^{-3}
- Expected approximately 10^6 muon capture signal events per day at beam intensity I_mu approximately 10^4 mu- per second
- The collisional energy loss ratio for positrons versus electrons in aluminum: 0.971 < [(dE/dx)_e+/(dE/dx)_e-]_coll < 0.973 for energies 5 to 50 MeV
- The total energy loss ratio: 0.977 < [(dE/dx)_e+/(dE/dx)_e-]_tot < 0.986

## Context
This work proposes an alternative to the standard method of determining muon capture rates from the time constant of the muon decay exponential, as used by the MuCap collaboration (2012) for the muon capture on proton measurement. The new approach directly measures muon disappearance rather than decay time, and uses pressure-dependent measurements to extrapolate to zero gas density, avoiding the systematic uncertainty from pp-mu molecular state formation that affected MuCap. The technique could be applied to the MuSun experiment's goal of measuring muon capture in cold deuterium gas, and could use a setup similar to that proposed for searching for muonium annihilation into two neutrinos at the Paul Scherrer Institute.
