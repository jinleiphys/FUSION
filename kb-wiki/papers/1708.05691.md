# Strongly-coupled anisotropic gauge theories and holography

> We initiate a non-perturbative study of anisotropic, non-conformal and confining gauge theories that are holographically realized in gravity by generic Einstein-Axion-Dilaton systems. In the vacuum our solutions describe RG flows from a conformal field theory in the UV to generic scaling solutions in the IR with generic hyperscaling violation and dynamical exponents $θ$ and $z$. We formulate a generalization of the holographic c-theorem to the anisotropic case. At finite temperature, we discover that the anisotropic deformation reduces the confinement-deconfinement phase transition temperature suggesting a possible alternative explanation of inverse magnetic catalysis solely based on anisotropy. We also study transport and diffusion properties in anisotropic theories and observe in particular that the butterfly velocity that characterizes both diffusion and growth of chaos transverse to the anisotropic direction, saturates a constant value in the IR which can exceed the bound given by the conformal value.

## Key claim

The paper initiates a non-perturbative study of anisotropic, non-conformal, and confining gauge theories using holography, finding that the anisotropic deformation reduces the confinement-deconfinement phase transition temperature, which suggests a possible alternative explanation of inverse magnetic catalysis based solely on anisotropy rather than charge dynamics.

## Method

The authors study a 4D SU(N) gauge theory in the large-N limit, deformed by a scalar operator with scaling dimension Δ and a marginal operator with a spatially dependent coupling, holographically realized by a 5D Einstein-Axion-Dilaton system with generic potentials V(φ) and Z(φ). They solve the equations of motion for a linear axion ansatz that breaks isotropy while preserving translation invariance, obtaining scaling solutions in the IR characterized by dynamical exponent z and hyperscaling violation exponent θ. Thermodynamic observables and transport coefficients are computed numerically for a specific confining theory with parameters σ = √(2/3) + 1/10, γ = 1/5, and Δ = 3.

## Key numbers

- The confinement-deconfinement transition temperature T_c decreases with increasing anisotropy parameter a; for a/j = 0, 1, and 3 the free energy curves cross F=0 at different temperatures, with a triple point at a ≈ 2.08 and T/j ≈ 0.36.
- The shear viscosity ratio η_∥/s in the IR scales as (a/T)^(2/z - 2), with the exponent independent of θ; for θ=0 the exponent lies in the range (-2,0].
- The transverse butterfly velocity v_{B⊥}^2 in the IR is given by (1-θ+3z)/(2(1-θ+2z)); for θ=0 and z>1 it exceeds the conformal value 2/3 and saturates to 3/4 as z → ∞.
- The entropy density in the IR scales as s = c_IR a^{-2-(1-θ)/z} T^{2+(1-θ)/z}/κ^2, with Hawking temperature T = |3+(1-θ)/z|/(4π r_H).
- The dynamical exponent z and hyperscaling violation exponent θ are given by z = (4γ^2 - 3σ^2 + 2)/(2γ(2γ - 3σ)) and θ = 3σ/(2γ).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of anisotropic systems that focused on scale-invariant or charged plasmas, such as those by Azeyanagi (2009), Mateos (2011), and Gursoy (2016), to uncharged, non-conformal, confining gauge theories. The finding that anisotropy alone reduces T_c resembles the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD by Bali (2011) and D'Elia (2012), but without charged fermionic degrees of freedom. The results enable future studies of anisotropic RG flows via the generalized c-theorem and suggest that anisotropic lattice calculations could test whether anisotropy itself is responsible for inverse magnetic catalysis.
