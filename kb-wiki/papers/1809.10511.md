# Light-Neutrino Exchange and Long-Distance Contributions to $0\nu2β$ Decays: An Exploratory Study on $ππ\to ee$

> We present an exploratory lattice QCD calculation of the neutrinoless double beta decay $ππ\to ee$. Under the mechanism of light-neutrino exchange, the decay amplitude involves significant long-distance contributions. The calculation reported here, with pion masses $m_π=420$ and 140 MeV, demonstrates that the decay amplitude can be computed from first principles using lattice methods. At unphysical and physical pion masses, we obtain that amplitudes are $24\%$ and $9\%$ smaller than the predication from leading order chiral perturbation theory. Our findings provide the lattice QCD inputs and constraints for effective field theory. A follow-on calculation with fully controlled systematic errors will be possible with adequate computational resources.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exploratory lattice QCD calculation of the neutrinoless double beta decay process ππ → ee, demonstrating that the decay amplitude can be computed from first principles, but the authors explicitly note that a follow-on calculation with fully controlled systematic errors will require adequate computational resources. At unphysical and physical pion masses, the lattice results are 24% and 9% smaller than the leading-order chiral perturbation theory prediction, respectively.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on two ensembles with pion masses of 420 MeV and 140 MeV, using Coulomb gauge fixed wall sources for pion interpolating operators. They constructed correlation functions for the ππ → ee process by integrating over Euclidean time windows and extracting the decay amplitude from the coefficient of the term proportional to the integration window size T_box. The ground-state contribution from the eν̅π intermediate state was identified and subtracted to isolate the excited-state contribution, and finite-volume corrections were applied using the Lellouch-Lüscher relation.

## Key numbers

- At m_π = 420 MeV, the total decay amplitude A(ππ → ee) / (F_π² T_lept) = 1.517(13), which is 24% smaller than the leading-order chiral perturbation theory prediction of 2 F_π² T_lept
- At m_π = 140 MeV, the total decay amplitude A(ππ → ee) / (F_π² T_lept) = 1.820(6), which is 9% smaller than the leading-order chiral perturbation theory prediction
- The ground-state contribution at m_π = 420 MeV is A^(g) = 1.462(10) in units of F_π² T_lept, with excited-state contribution A^(e) = 0.055(13)
- The ground-state contribution at m_π = 140 MeV is A^(g) = 1.8864(50) in units of F_π² T_lept, with excited-state contribution A^(e) = -0.0664(70)
- The lattice spacing inverse is a⁻¹ = 1.73 GeV for the 420 MeV ensemble and a⁻¹ = 1.01 GeV for the 140 MeV ensemble
- The finite-volume correction coefficient κ(n_e) = 0.686(3) for m_π = 420 MeV and 0.517(3) for m_π = 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of using lattice QCD to constrain few-body inputs for ab initio many-body calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, as proposed by Cirigliano (2017) and Cirigliano (2018). The calculation provides the first lattice QCD determination of the long-distance contribution to 0ν2β decay via light-neutrino exchange, and the results can serve as inputs and constraints for effective field theory. The techniques developed here can be directly applied to other 0ν2β decay processes such as nπ → pee and nn → pp ee, enabling lattice QCD to provide more low-energy QCD inputs for effective field theory calculations.
