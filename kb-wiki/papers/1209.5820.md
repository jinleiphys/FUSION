# Damping of giant dipole resonance in highly excited nuclei

> The giant dipole resonance's (GDR) width and shape at finite temperature and angular momentum are described within the phonon damping model (PDM), which predicts an overall increase in the GDR's total width at low and moderate temperature T, and its saturation at high T. At T< 1 MeV the GDR width remains nearly constant because of thermal pairing. The PDM description is compared with the experimental systematics obtained from heavy-ion fusion, inelastic scattering of light particles on heavy targets, and alpha induced fusion reactions, as well as with predictions by other theoretical approaches. The results obtained within the PDM and GDR's experimental data are also employed to predict the viscosity of hot medium and heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The phonon damping model (PDM) predicts an overall increase in the GDR's total width at low and moderate temperature T, and its saturation at high T, with the width remaining nearly constant below T < 1 MeV due to thermal pairing; the model claims to be the only one able to describe experimental data across the entire temperature region including T ≤ 1 MeV, where other models fail.

## Method

The PDM Hamiltonian couples independent single-particle (quasiparticle) fields, GDR phonon fields, and the coupling between them, using Woods-Saxon potentials at T = 0 to obtain single-particle energies. The GDR width is calculated as a sum of a quantal width (from coupling to particle-hole configurations) and a thermal width (from coupling to particle-particle and hole-hole configurations), with superfluid pairing included via Bogolyubov transformation and quasiparticle occupation numbers approximated by Fermi-Dirac distributions. For rotating nuclei, the z-projection M of total angular momentum J is added as a term -γM̂ in the Hamiltonian, and the GDR strength function is computed from the phonon damping expression using a Breit-Wigner smoothing of delta functions.

## Key numbers

- GDR width for 120Sn and 208Pb predicted by PDM shows saturation beginning at T > 4 MeV in 106Sn
- GDR width in 201Tl at 0.8 ≤ T < 1.2 MeV reproduced by PDM after including exact canonical pairing gaps
- For 88Mo at M = 41 ħ, PDM predicts FWHM between 9.6 MeV (T = 2.5 MeV) and 11 MeV (T = 3 MeV)
- Experimental GDR width for 88Mo at M = 44 ħ is Γ_ex ≈ 7.5 MeV, with error bars giving Γ_ex^< ≈ 6 MeV and Γ_ex^> ≈ 8.5 MeV
- PDM predicts η/s between (1.3 - 4.0) KSS units for medium and heavy nuclei at T = 5 MeV
- High-T limit of η/s in finite nuclei estimated as (η/s)_min ≈ 2.2^{+0.4}_{-0.9} KSS units
- Using Γ_ex = 7.5 MeV and η(0) = 0.6 × 10^{-23} MeV s fm^{-3} gives η/s = 0.85 KSS units for 88Mo

## Context

This work extends the phonon damping model (PDM) previously developed by the author (Dang, 2012) to describe GDR damping at finite temperature and angular momentum, and compares it with the phenomenological thermal shape fluctuation model (pTSFM), the adiabatic model (AM), and the Fermi liquid drop model (FLDM). The PDM predictions are compared with experimental systematics from heavy-ion fusion (Schiller, 2000), deep inelastic scattering of light particles on heavy targets (Baumann, 1988; Heckman, 2000), and alpha induced fusion reactions (Kolkata, 2005). The results enable predictions of shear viscosity of hot nuclei and testing of the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) conjecture for the lower bound of η/s, with recent preliminary data for 88Mo (Maj, 2012) suggesting either inaccurate data analysis or possible violation of the KSS bound.
