# Yields of neutron-rich nuclei by actinide photofission in giant dipole resonance region

> Photofission of actinides is studied in the region of nuclear excitation energies that covers the entire giant dipole resonance (GDR) region. A comparative analysis of the behavior of the symmetric and asymmetric modes of photon induced fission as a function of the average excitation energy of the fissioning nucleus is performed. The mass distributions of $^{238}$U photofission fragments are obtained at the endpoint bremsstrahlung energy of 29.1 MeV which corresponds to mean photon energy of 13.7$\pm$0.3 MeV that coincides with GDR peak for $^{238}$U photofission. The integrated yield of $^{238}$U photofission as well as charge distribution of photofission products are calculated and its role in the production of neutron-rich nuclei and their exoticity is explored.

## Key claim

The photofission cross sections of actinides in the GDR region can be described by Lorentz line shapes, and the ratio method predictions for photofission are almost as good as direct Lorentz line shape fitting, whereas the evaporation-fission process of the compound nucleus largely overestimates the photofission cross sections.

## Method

The authors calculated total photoabsorption cross sections using a two-component Lorentz line shape for the GDR region and extracted photofission cross sections via a ratio method where the ratios of photofission to photoabsorption peak cross sections scale quadratically with fissility parameter Z^2/A. Mass distributions of photofission fragments were analyzed using a multimode-fission model with five Gaussian functions representing one symmetric mode (SM) and two asymmetric modes (ASMI, ASMII). Charge distributions were modeled as Gaussian functions of charge number Z for each mass number A, and production cross sections for individual fragments were obtained by multiplying the fission cross section by the charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- Endpoint bremsstrahlung energy of 29.1 MeV, corresponding to mean photon energy of 13.7 ± 0.3 MeV, which coincides with the GDR peak for 238U photofission
- For 238U photofission, the symmetric mode (SM) is centered at A = 117, with asymmetric mode displacements D_ASMI = 21 and D_ASMII = 16
- Gaussian fit parameters for 238U photofission mass distribution: C_SM = 0.4929, σ_SM = 4.4732; C_ASMI = 5.8959, σ_ASMI = 5.9612; C_ASMII = 2.2945, σ_ASMII = 1.6223
- Charge distribution parameters: C_p = 0.8 and shift parameter Δ = 3.8, extracted from experimental data
- Ratio method scaling constants: c1 = 0.9978, c2 = 1.4775 for the quadratic dependence of R1 and R2 on fissility
- Production cross sections for most probable isobars range from 0.11562 mb (A=80) to 0.48132 mb (A=101) for 238U photofission at 29.1 MeV endpoint energy
- Three sets of calculations presented: nuclei with largest cross sections (most probable isobars), most neutron-rich isobars with cross sections > 100 nb, and most neutron-rich isobars with cross sections > 100 fb

## Context

This work extends the multimode-fission model approach previously applied by De (2008) at two accelerator energies and builds on qualitative discussions by Pomorski (1993) regarding contributions from various fission modes. The study provides quantitative predictions for the production of neutron-rich nuclei via photofission, which is relevant for radioactive ion beam (RIB) facilities such as TRIUMF (2012). The results enable comparison of photofission with projectile fragmentation methods at high energies (Bhowmick, 1998) and with fragmentation of high-energy radioactive ion beams (Mueller, 1991; Auerbach, 1995) for producing exotic nuclei far from stability.
