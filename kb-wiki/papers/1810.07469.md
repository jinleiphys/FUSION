# Gogny forces in the astrophysical context

> The most successful Gogny interactions of the D1 family, namely D1S, D1N and D1M, suffer the common problem of a too soft neutron matter equation of state at high density, which prevents them from predicting a maximal mass of neutron stars of two solar masses, as required by recent astronomical observations. To cure this deficiency, we have proposed recently a reparametrization of the D1M force by fine tuning the slope of the symmetry energy in such a way that it preserves the ground-state properties of D1M in finite nuclei and also describes successfully the global properties of neutron stars, in particular its maximal mass, in consonance with the observational data. In this contribution we revisit this reparametrization by discussing two modified Gogny forces, dubbed D1M$^*$ and D1M$^{**}$.

# Gogny forces in the astrophysical context

## Key claim
The D1M$^*$ and D1M$^{**}$ reparametrizations of the Gogny D1M force can simultaneously reproduce ground-state properties of finite nuclei with quality similar to the original D1M interaction while predicting neutron star maximum masses of 2 $M_{\odot}$ and 1.9 $M_{\odot}$, respectively, but only if the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation is increased from 24.83 MeV (D1M) to 43.18 MeV (D1M$^*$) or 33.91 MeV (D1M$^{**}$).

## Method
The authors start from the D1M Gogny interaction and modify only the finite-range spin-isospin strength coefficients ($W_i$, $B_i$, $H_i$, $M_i$ for i=1,2) while keeping the Gaussian ranges, zero-range density-dependent term, and spin-orbit parameters fixed. Seven of the eight free parameters are determined by imposing that symmetric nuclear matter properties (saturation density, energy per particle, incompressibility, effective mass) and the symmetry energy at 0.1 fm$^{-3}$ remain identical to D1M, with the eighth parameter $B_1$ used to tune the slope of the symmetry energy. A small refit of the $t_3$ parameter (by about 1 MeV fm$^4$) is performed to recover the mass rms deviation of D1M. The resulting forces are tested by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron stars and performing Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for finite nuclei using the HFBaxial code.

## Key numbers
- D1M$^*$ predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2 $M_{\odot}$, while D1M$^{**}$ predicts about 1.9 $M_{\odot}$.
- Slope of symmetry energy $L$: 43.18 MeV (D1M$^*$), 33.91 MeV (D1M$^{**}$), 24.83 MeV (D1M).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 30.25 MeV (D1M$^*$), 29.37 MeV (D1M$^{**}$), 28.55 MeV (D1M).
- Saturation density $\rho_0$: 0.1650 fm$^{-3}$ (D1M$^*$), 0.1647 fm$^{-3}$ (D1M$^{**}$ and D1M).
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: $-$16.06 MeV (D1M$^*$), $-$16.02 MeV (D1M$^{**}$ and D1M).
- Incompressibility $K$: 225.4 MeV (D1M$^*$), 225.0 MeV (D1M$^{**}$ and D1M).
- Effective mass $m^*/m$: 0.746 for all three D1M family forces.
- rms deviation of binding energies for 620 even-even nuclei: 1.34 MeV (D1M$^*$), 1.36 MeV (D1M).
- Inner fission barrier of $^{240}$Pu: 9.5 MeV for both D1M and D1M$^*$.
- Fission isomer excitation energy $E_{II}$: 3.36 MeV (D1M), 2.80 MeV (D1M$^*$).
- Outer fission barrier $B_{II}$: 8.58 MeV (D1M), 8.00 MeV (D1M$^*$).

## Context
This work extends the Gogny D1 family of interactions (D1S from Berger (1991), D1N from Chappert (2008), and D1M from Goriely (2009)), which successfully describe finite nuclei but produce a neutron matter equation of state too soft to reach the 2 $M_{\odot}$ neutron star maximum mass required by observations from Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The reparametrization follows a strategy similar to families of Skyrme interactions (e.g., SLy4 from Chabanat (1998)) and relativistic mean-field models (SAMi-J, KED0-J, TAMU-FSU) where the symmetry energy slope is varied while preserving bulk nuclear properties. The new forces enable simultaneous description of finite nuclei and neutron stars, with D1M$^*$ matching the performance of the SLy4 Skyrme force in the astrophysical context.
