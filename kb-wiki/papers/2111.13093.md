# Inertial energy dissipation in nuclear dynamics

> We present the TDDFT+Langevin model that incorporates microscopic time-dependent density function theory (TDDFT) with macroscopic Langevin model. By extracting the energy-dependent dissipation effect from the TDDFT dynamics, quantum effects are introduced to the Langevin-type stochastic fission analysis. In this paper, by means of Skyrme nuclear effective interactions, the energy-dependent friction coefficients to be used in Langevin calculations are provided individually for 25 fission nuclei chosen from uranium, plutonium, curium, californium, and fermium isotopes. The validity of the energy-dependent friction coefficients are confirmed by comparing to the existing experimental fission fragment yields. In conclusion, depending on the energy, the transition of energy dissipation mechanism from conventional viscous energy dissipation to inertial energy dissipation is shown.

## Key claim

The paper claims that energy dissipation in nuclear fission transitions from conventional viscous dissipation to inertial dissipation depending on collision energy, with the inertial friction appearing not only at high velocities but also at low velocities due to the nonlinearity and finite-body nature of nucleus-nucleus collisions. The authors hedge that this conclusion is based on a specific model (TDDFT+Langevin) using Skyrme SV-bas interactions and symmetric central collisions, and that the low-energy inertial dissipation is associated with nuclear deformation rather than the ordinary high-velocity mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations for symmetric central collisions of identical nuclei to extract energy-dependent friction coefficients from the TDDFT trajectories, using the Sky3D code with SV-bas Skyrme interactions. These friction coefficients were then incorporated into four-dimensional Langevin calculations by replacing the conventional Wall-and-Window friction coefficient for the center-of-mass relative motion coordinate with the energy-dependent TDDFT-derived friction. The friction coefficients were extrapolated to lower energies using linear regression, and fission fragment yields were calculated from 100,000 Langevin events per nucleus for 25 heavy nuclei from uranium, plutonium, curium, californium, and fermium isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Friction coefficients were calculated for collision energies E/2A = 2, 3, 4, ..., 10 MeV per nucleon for 25 fission nuclei.
* At E/2A = 9 MeV, the friction coefficient is almost constant at 1.10 MeV/(c·fm) across all isotopes.
* For 236U at excitation energy E* = 20 MeV, the friction coefficient γ11(E) = 452 MeV/(fm·c); for 240Pu at the same excitation energy, γ11(E) = 448 MeV/(fm·c).
* The symmetric fission probability excess improved from 0.64% to 0.05% for 236U and from 0.59% to 0.26% for 240Pu compared to conventional Langevin calculations.
* The transition energies between dissipation regimes occur at relative velocities of 0.34c and 0.72c for 236U, and 0.35c and 0.63c for 240Pu.
* The linear relation between slope a and intercept b is b = -0.309a + 249, with slope a values ranging from -1616 to -921 1/(fm·c²) across the 25 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the TDDFT+Langevin approach previously proposed by Sekizawa and Hagino (2019) from three-dimensional to four-dimensional Langevin calculations, and provides the first systematic tabulation of energy-dependent friction coefficients for 25 heavy fission nuclei. The results contradict the conventional assumption of energy-independent friction coefficients used in Wall-and-Window formula calculations (Randrup, 1979; Abe, 1992) and demonstrate that inertial dissipation appears even at low energies where it was not previously expected. The method enables less-phenomenological predictions for fission fragment yields of unknown nuclei, and the authors suggest the approach can be extended to study mass tensor effects and the role of nuclear solitons in light-nucleus reactions.
