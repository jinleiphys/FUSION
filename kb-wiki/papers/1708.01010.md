# Neutron matter within QCD sum rules

> Equation of state (EOS) of pure neutron matter (PNM) is studied in QCD sum rules (QCDSR). It is found that the QCDSR results on EOS of PNM are in good agreement with predictions by current advanced microscopic many-body theories. Moreover, the higher-order density terms in quark condensates are shown to be important to describe the empirical EOS of PNM in the density region around and above nuclear saturation density although they play minor role at subsaturation densities. The chiral condensates in PNM are also studied, and our results indicate that the higher-order density terms in quark condensates, which are introduced to reasonably describe the empirical EOS of PNM at suprasaturation densities, tend to hinder the appearance of chiral symmetry restoration in PNM at high densities.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule (QCDSR) approach, when augmented with an effective higher-order density term (proportional to \(\rho^2\)) in the quark condensates, can reproduce the equation of state (EOS) of pure neutron matter (PNM) in agreement with the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) EOS around and above saturation density, whereas the naive linear-density approximation fails in that region. The authors hedge that the QCDSR method is expected to break down at densities around or above \(2\rho_0\) due to the formation of \(\Delta\) resonances.

## Method

The authors used the QCDSR formalism to relate in-medium quark and gluon condensates (up to mass dimension-6) to the scalar and vector self-energies of a neutron at its Fermi surface, via the operator product expansion (OPE) and Borel transformation. The energy per neutron \(E_n(\rho)\) was then obtained by integrating these self-energies over density. The chiral condensate was parameterized as \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\rho,\delta} \approx \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\text{vac}} + (\sigma_N / 2 m_q)(1 \mp \xi \delta)\rho + \Phi(1 \mp g \delta)\rho^2\), with the parameters \(\Phi\), \(g\), and the four-quark condensate mixing parameter \(f\) fixed by fitting to the APR EOS, the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) result at \(\rho = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\), and the symmetry energy at \(\rho = 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum chiral condensate is taken as \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\text{vac}} = -(252\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- The pion-nucleon sigma term is fixed at \(\sigma_N = 45\ \text{MeV}\), and the average light quark mass at \(m_q = 3.5\ \text{MeV}\).
- The fitted parameters for the \(\rho^2\) term are \(\Phi' \equiv \Phi \times \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\text{vac}} = 3.45\) and \(g = -0.64\), with \(f = 0.43\); for the naive QCDSR (no \(\rho^2\) term), \(f = 0.50\).
- At \(\rho = 0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\), the QCDSR EOS gives \(E_n = 12.9\) MeV, close to the APR value of 13.3 MeV.
- At saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the QCDSR EOS gives \(E_n(\rho_0) \approx 17.1\) MeV; including twist-four four-quark condensates shifts this to 15.9 MeV.
- The density below which the \(\Phi\)-term is negligible is estimated as \(\rho \ll \rho_{\text{es}} \approx 2.13\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The ratio \(\langle \bar{d}d \rangle_{\rho_0} / \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\text{vac}}\) changes from 0.45 (linear approximation) to 0.60 (with \(\Phi\) term); the ratio for u quarks changes from 0.56 to 0.59.

## Context

This work extends the QCDSR method, previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Cohen, Furnstahl, Griegel, and Jin (1995), to pure neutron matter. It shows that the leading-order linear density approximation for the chiral condensate is insufficient above \(\rho \sim 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\), and that an effective higher-order density term is needed to match the APR EOS. The results are consistent with predictions from chiral effective field theory (Tews et al., 2013; Kruger et al., 2013), quantum Monte Carlo simulations (Gandolfi et al., 2013; Tews et al., 2016), and the functional renormalization group approach (Drews and Weise, 2014). The finding that higher-order density terms hinder chiral symmetry restoration at high densities has implications for the QCD phase diagram and the composition of neutron star cores.
