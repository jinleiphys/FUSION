# Viscous effects on the dynamical evolution of QCD matter during the first-order confinement phase transition in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate viscous effects on the dynamical evolution of QCD matter during the first-order phase transition, which may happen in heavy-ion collisions. We first obtain the first-order phase transition line in the QCD phase diagram under the Gibbs condition by using the MIT bag model and the hadron resonance gas model for the equation of state of partons and hadrons. The viscous pressure, which corresponds to the friction in the energy balance, is then derived from the energy and net baryon number conservation during the phase transition. We find that the viscous pressure relates to the thermodynamic change of the two-phase state and thus affects the timescale of the phase transition. Numerical results are presented for demonstrations.

## Key claim

Viscous effects during a first-order QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions cause a stronger decrease of the baryon chemical potential and slow down the hadronization process compared to ideal hydrodynamic expansion, but the quantitative effect depends on the specific expansion geometry and the assumed values of shear and bulk viscosity.

## Method

The authors model the parton phase using the MIT bag model with perturbative QCD corrections up to \(g_s^2\) and the hadron phase using a hadron resonance gas model with excluded-volume corrections for baryons. They derive the viscous pressure from energy and net baryon number conservation during the phase transition, then solve the coupled time evolution of the baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) and the parton fraction \(f_p\) under one-dimensional Bjorken expansion and three-dimensional Gubser expansion, assuming first-order viscous hydrodynamics with constant \(\eta_p T/h_p = \xi_p T/h_p\).

## Key numbers

- Bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and QCD scale \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) used in the parton equation of state.
- Hard-core radius of (anti)baryons set to \(0.6\ \mathrm{fm}\) in the hadron resonance gas model.
- Starting time of the phase transition \(\tau_c = 1.5\ \mathrm{fm/c}\) with initial \(\mu_B(\tau_c) = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and net baryon density \(n_{Bp} = 0.1948\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- Three values of viscosity tested: \(\eta_p T/h_p = \xi_p T/h_p = 0,\ 0.2,\ 0.4\).
- Gubser expansion parameter \(a = 4.5\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Ratio \(s_p/n_{Bp} = 20\) at the start of the phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the ideal-fluid treatment of the first-order QCD phase transition by Lee (1987) to include viscous effects, using the same MIT bag model and hadron resonance gas approach as Kouno (1988) and Singh (2009). The results provide a basis for dynamic transport simulations of the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, as pursued by Feng (2016), and could be refined with more realistic equations of state and transport coefficients at large baryon chemical potential.
