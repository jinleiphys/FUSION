# Dilepton production in high energy heavy ion collisions with 3+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics

> We present a first calculation of the dilepton yield and elliptic flow done with 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical simulations of relativistic heavy ion collisions at the top RHIC energy. A comparison with recent experimental data from the STAR collaboration is made.

## Key claim

This paper presents a first calculation of dilepton yield and elliptic flow using 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical simulations at top RHIC energy, but the authors note that the elliptic flow calculation is approximate because a formal extension of the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger relation to out-of-equilibrium environments is absent.

## Method

The authors use the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamical code MUSIC to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 1/(4 pi). Thermal dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma is computed using the Born approximation with viscous corrections to the thermal distribution functions, while hadronic gas dileptons are obtained via the vector dominance model with in-medium self-energies. Charm quark production is simulated with PYTHIA8, and heavy quarks are evolved through the medium using relativistic Langevin dynamics with a spatial diffusion coefficient D_c = 3/(2 pi T).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio set to eta/s = 1/(4 pi)
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c for the hydrodynamical evolution
- Center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient D_c = 3/(2 pi T)
- Viscous effects on dilepton yields are modest, with shape changes visible at invariant masses M ~ 2-3 GeV in the QGP phase
- Including heavy quark energy loss reduces the intermediate mass dilepton yield by almost an order of magnitude

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamical calculations of electromagnetic probes by incorporating shear viscosity effects on dilepton production, building on the approach used for real photons by Dion et al. (2011). The results are compared with experimental data from the STAR collaboration (Dong), and the authors find that including charm energy loss leads to better agreement with the dilepton yield data, though both scenarios remain consistent within current experimental error bars. Future work will explore alternate vector spectral densities from Rapp et al. (1999) and extend the calculations to LHC conditions.
