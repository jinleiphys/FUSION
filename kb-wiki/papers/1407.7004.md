# On the Search for Time Reversal Invariance Violation in Neutron Transmission

> Time Reversal Invariant Violating (TRIV) effects in neutron transmission through a nuclei target are discussed. We demonstrate the existence of a class of experiments that are free from falls asymmetries. We discuss enhancement of TRIV effects. We analyze a model experiment and show that such tests have a discovery potential of $10^2 - 10^4$ compare to current limits.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a class of neutron transmission experiments exists which are free from false asymmetries arising from final state interactions and instrumental imperfections, and that such tests have a discovery potential of \(10^2\) to \(10^4\) compared to current limits on time reversal invariance violation, provided the apparatus is configured so that all classical fields interacting with the neutron spin are reversed together.

## Method

The authors derive a "TRIV transmission theorem" showing that for zero-angle elastic scattering of polarized neutrons through a polarized target, the forward and reverse transmission probabilities are equal when the time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) amplitude \(d\) is zero, even in the presence of parity-violating and spin-spin interactions. They then analyze a model experiment using a polarized \(^{139}\)La target (exploiting the known large parity-violating asymmetry in the 0.734 eV resonance) and a \(^{3}\)He spin filter as polarizer/analyzer, estimating the statistical sensitivity achievable at the Spallation Neutron Source.

## Key numbers

- The parity-violating asymmetry in the 0.734 eV resonance in \(^{139}\)La has been measured to be \((9.56 \pm 0.35) \times 10^{-2}\).
- The maximal possible parity-violating effect \(P_{max}\) is estimated as \(\sim 10^{-4} \sqrt{\overline{D}(\text{eV})}/\Gamma \leq 10\%\) for medium and heavy nuclei with \(\overline{D} \in (1 - 10^3)\ \text{eV}\) and \(\Gamma \in (0.05 - 0.2)\ \text{eV}\).
- A \(^{139}\)La polarization of 47.5% has been experimentally achieved in Lanthanum Aluminate crystals.
- The estimated neutron flux at the target for the model experiment is \(dN/dt = 7.8 \times 10^7\) neutrons per second, assuming a 10 cm diameter target at 15 meters from the moderator, \(\Delta E = 0.045\) eV (the total width of the resonance), and SNS operating at 1.4 MW with 1.4 mA proton current.
- The discovery potential of the proposed experiments is \(10^2 - 10^4\) compared to current limits.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TRIV searches in neutron optics originally developed by Kabir (1988) and Stodolsky (1982), and builds on the observation of large parity-violating effects in neutron resonances such as the 9.56% asymmetry in \(^{139}\)La measured by Yuan (1991) and others. The paper addresses the practical challenge raised by Lamoreaux and Golub (1994) that systematic effects from instrumental imperfections might make TRIV measurements hopeless, by proving a transmission theorem that eliminates false asymmetries. This enables next-generation experiments using polarized \(^{139}\)La targets (as developed by groups at KEK, Kyoto University, and PSI) and \(^{3}\)He spin filters at high-flux neutron sources like the Spallation Neutron Source.
