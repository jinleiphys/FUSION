# A dressed bag model study of the final-state $NΔ$ interaction in photoproduction processes off the deuteron

> The impact of the short-range $NΔ$ interaction on the pion photoproduction processes off the deuteron in the $Δ$-resonance region is studied in the framework of recently proposed dressed-bag model. A common dressing procedure for bare three- and six-quark states is used to describe both the pion decay widths of baryon resonances and the effective $NN$ (or $NΔ$) interaction at short ranges related to the inner dressed-bag states. It is shown that the effect of short-range $NΔ$ interaction for the forward-angle $π^\mathrm{o}$ photoproduction off the deuteron cannot be neglected. The prospects for further development of the model to describe the short-range $NN$ (or $NΔ$) correlations in the lightest nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The short-range $N\Delta$ final-state interaction (FSI) in forward-angle $\pi^\mathrm{o}$ photoproduction off the deuteron cannot be neglected, contrary to earlier assumptions that only $NN$ FSI matters; however, the quantitative evaluation relies on a simplified model using only the Born term of the $N\Delta$ FSI, and a more complete treatment including half-off-shell t-matrices is still in progress.

## Method

The authors employed the dressed-bag model, which describes short-range $NN$ and $N\Delta$ interactions through a two-component Fock-column representation: a bag-like six-quark state ($s^6$ or $s^5p$) dressed by a $\sigma$-meson field, and a proper cluster-like state with a nodal $2S$ wave function. They derived effective separable potentials for the $N\Delta$ interaction in the $ST=21$ channel from the same quark-shell-model vertices used for the $NN$ system, with algebraic factors differing by spin-isospin quantum numbers. The $\pi^\mathrm{o}$ photoproduction cross section off the deuteron was then calculated including the direct short-range $N\Delta$ FSI via the loop diagram of Fig. 1(a), using the constituent quark model (CQM) for the $\gamma N\Delta$ vertex and the $^3P_0$ model for pion emission.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling constant for the $N\Delta$ short-range interaction is reduced by a factor of approximately 1.7 relative to the $NN$ case, and the effective parameter $\lambda_0$ in the separable potential is reduced by roughly a factor of three, based on algebraic factors $C^{\scriptscriptstyle N}_{\scriptscriptstyle 10} = \frac{-19}{486}\sqrt{\frac{5}{3}}$ and $C^{\scriptscriptstyle \Delta}_{\scriptscriptstyle 21} = \frac{113}{3645}\sqrt{\frac{5}{6}}$.
* The fitted parameters for the $NN$ short-range interaction (used as input for the $N\Delta$ case) are: $g_{\scriptscriptstyle N\sigma q}^2\,\lambda_0 = 328.55$ MeV, $E_0 - 2m_N = 693$ MeV, $a_0 = -0.05$, and the harmonic-oscillator radius $b = 0.5065$ fm.
* The $\Delta$-resonance region studied is at photon energies $E_{\gamma} \approx 250$–350 MeV, where other resonances like $P_{11}(1440)$ or $S_{11}(1535)$ are negligible.
* The $\sigma$-meson is treated as a broad resonance with $\Gamma_{\sigma} \approx m_{\sigma} \approx 500$ MeV and coupling $g_{\pi\pi\sigma} \approx 2$–4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Moscow-Tubingen dressed-bag model (Kukulin et al., 2001) from the $NN$ system to the $N\Delta$ system, providing a consistent microscopic treatment of short-range interactions based on quark degrees of freedom and the Pauli principle. It addresses the long-standing problem that quasifree $\pi^\mathrm{o}$ photoproduction off the deuteron overestimates forward-angle cross sections by a factor of 1.5 to 2 (Laget, 1978; Arenhovel, 1984; Levchuk et al., 1999), and that previous attempts using only $NN$ FSI (Levchuk et al., 2000) or $s$-channel resonance pole mechanisms (Laget, 1978) were insufficient. The results enable future coupled-channel treatments of $NN$ and $N\Delta$ final states in photoproduction and open the possibility to study short-range $N\Delta$ correlations in light nuclei.
