# Sensitivity Studies for Extraction of G_E^n from Inclusive and Semi-inclusive Electron Scattering on Polarized 3He

> The processes \vec{3He}(\vec{e},e') and \vec{3He}(\vec{e},e'n) are theoretically analyzed with the aim to search for sensitivities in the electric form factor of the neutron, G_E^n. Faddeev calculations based on the high precision NN force AV18 and using consistent MEC's are employed. While the inclusive process is too insensitive, the semi-exclusive one appears promising.

## Key claim

The semi-exclusive process \(\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(\vec{e},e'n)\) is promising for extracting the electric form factor of the neutron \(G_E^n\), but only if full Faddeev calculations including final state interactions (FSI) and meson exchange currents (MEC) are used; the inclusive process \(\overrightarrow{^3{\rm He}}(\vec{e},e')\) is too insensitive due to strong proton contributions, at least at the momentum transfers studied (\(q^2 = 0.1\) and \(0.2\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\)).

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations for both inclusive and semi-inclusive electron scattering on polarized \(^3\)He, using the high-precision Argonne AV18 nucleon-nucleon force and consistent \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-like meson exchange currents (MEC) following the Riska prescription. They computed response functions and asymmetries for the inclusive process at \(q^2 = 0.1\) and \(0.2\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), and for the semi-inclusive neutron knockout process at ten \(q^2\) values from \(0.05\) to \(0.5\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), comparing results with and without FSI, with and without MEC, and with and without proton contributions. They also tested the effect of replacing the nonrelativistic single-nucleon current with a fully relativistic one within the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIAS).

## Key numbers

- For the inclusive process at \(q^2 = 0.1\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) and \(\omega = 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\), a \(\pm 60\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced only about \(\pm 8\%\) change in the response function \(R_{TL'}\) in the quasi-elastic peak region.
- For the inclusive process at \(q^2 = 0.2\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) and \(\omega = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\), a \(\pm 60\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced about \(\pm 13\%\) change in \(R_{TL'}\).
- For the semi-inclusive process at \(q^2 = 0.25\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), a \(\pm 25\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced a \(\pm 32\%\) change in the ratio \(A_\perp / A_\parallel\) at the highest neutron energy.
- For the semi-inclusive process at \(q^2 = 0.50\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), a \(\pm 25\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced a \(\pm 27\%\) change in \(A_\perp / A_\parallel\) at the highest neutron energy.
- At \(q^2 = 0.15\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), a \(\pm 25\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced a \(\pm 204\%\) change in \(A_\perp / A_\parallel\), due to the smallness of the ratio itself.
- At \(q^2 = 0.05\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), a \(\pm 25\%\) change in \(G_E^n\) produced only a \(\pm 2\%\) change in \(A_\perp / A_\parallel\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ishikawa (1998) that investigated FSI effects in inclusive scattering, and by Schulze and Sauer (1993) who used PWIA to show the proton contribution overwhelms the \(G_E^n\) signal. It also provides a theoretical framework to correct the simple polarized-neutron assumption used in the experimental analyses by Becker (1999) and Rohe (1999). The results enable future extractions of \(G_E^n\) from semi-inclusive measurements at facilities such as Mainz and Jefferson Lab, provided that full Faddeev calculations with FSI and MEC are employed, and that relativistic effects are eventually included.
