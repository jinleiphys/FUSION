# Eccentricity and elliptic flow at fixed centrality in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200GeV in AMPT

> To reduce the effect of the fluctuations of initial geometrical shape, elliptic flow are studied at fixed centrality in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$=200GeV. It is observed with the participant increasing, elliptic flow has an increase and a decrease at different fixed impact parameter, but not a trivial fluctuation. It is analyzed that the initial space anisotropy dominates the participant dependence of elliptic flow in near-central collisions(b=5fm) and mid-central collisions(b=8fm), while the interaction between particles can mainly answer for the behavior of elliptic flow in peripheral collisions(b=12fm). To distinguish the pure geometrical effect, elliptic flow scaled by initial eccentricity is studied. It is found that the ratio $v_{2}/ε$ increases with participant and reaches a saturation when the participant is large enough, indicating that the collision system reach the local equilibrium.

# Eccentricity and elliptic flow at fixed centrality in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200GeV in AMPT

## Key claim
Elliptic flow at fixed impact parameter shows a monotonic increase or decrease with participant number rather than a trivial fluctuation, and the ratio v2/epsilon saturates at large participant number, indicating that the collision system may reach local equilibrium in near-central collisions.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with string melting (v2.11) with a parton cross section of 10 mb to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. They fixed the impact parameter at b=5 fm (near-central), b=8 fm (mid-central), and b=12 fm (peripheral), and studied the participant number dependence of elliptic flow v2 and initial eccentricity epsilon using the Monte Carlo Glauber model. The ratio v2/epsilon was then examined as a function of participant number at each fixed impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- Elliptic flow reaches its maximum value in mid-central collisions (b=8 fm) and decreases for both more central and more peripheral collisions
- At fixed b=5 fm (near-central), v2 decreases monotonically with increasing participant number
- At fixed b=8 fm (mid-central), v2 decreases monotonically with increasing participant number
- At fixed b=12 fm (peripheral), v2 shows a slow increase with increasing participant number
- Initial eccentricity epsilon decreases with increasing participant number for all three fixed impact parameters (b=5, 8, and 12 fm)
- The ratio v2/epsilon saturates at large participant number for b=5 fm (near-central collisions), indicating possible local equilibrium

## Context
This work extends studies of elliptic flow at RHIC energies by focusing on fixed centrality (fixed impact parameter) rather than the usual centrality bin averaging, which mixes different initial geometries. The authors use the AMPT model with string melting, which was previously shown to describe elliptic flow and two-pion correlation functions (AMPT collaboration, 2005) but not hadron rapidity and transverse momentum spectra. The finding that v2/epsilon saturates in near-central collisions supports hydrodynamic predictions (Heinz, 2005; Shuryak, 2004) that the system reaches local equilibrium when interactions are strong enough. This work enables future studies to separate geometrical effects from dynamical effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding the equation of state of the produced matter.
