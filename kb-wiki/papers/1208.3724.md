# Overlap of QRPA states based on ground states of different nuclei --mathematical properties and test calculations--

> The overlap of the excited states in quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) is calculated in order to simulate the overlap of the intermediate nuclear states of the double-beta decay. Our basic idea is to use the like-particle QRPA with the aid of the closure approximation and calculate the overlap as rigorously as possible by making use of the explicit equation of the QRPA ground state. The formulation is shown in detail, and the mathematical properties of the overlap matrix are investigated. Two test calculations are performed for relatively light nuclei with the Skyrme and volume delta-pairing energy functionals. The validity of the truncations used in the calculation is examined and confirmed.

## Key claim

The overlap of intermediate nuclear states in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) can be calculated more rigorously than previous approximations by explicitly using the equation of the QRPA ground state, but the feasibility of this treatment is demonstrated only for relatively light nuclei and the validity of the truncations used in the calculation must be examined and confirmed.

## Method

The author formulates the overlap matrix elements of QRPA excited states based on the initial and final ground states of double-beta decay using the like-particle QRPA with the closure approximation. The QRPA ground state is expressed explicitly via an exponential operator acting on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state, and the overlap is expanded up to second order in the ground-state correlation operators. Two test calculations are performed for light nuclei using the Skyrme energy density functional and volume delta-pairing energy functionals, with the HFB and QRPA equations solved self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factors of the QRPA ground states include terms up to fourth order in the correlation operators, with the fourth-order terms approximated by quasi-boson terms involving traces of the form Tr(C C^dagger) and Tr((C C^dagger)^2), where C is the matrix of ground-state correlation coefficients.
- The exchange terms in the normalization factors involve sums over eight distinct index permutations of the correlation coefficients C, as shown in Eqs. (exc_v2_nrm_i) and (exc_v2_nrm_f).
- The overlap matrix element is expanded with terms up to second order in the correlation operators v_F and v_I, with the condition that only terms with (K_1 pi_1) = (K_2 pi_2) are included, as the summation over (K_1 pi_1) of the second-order term was found to be negligible in the numerical calculation.

## Context

This work extends the like-particle QRPA approach to calculating nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, building on earlier formulations of the QRPA ground state (Bal69) and previous overlap approximations (Sim04, Kam91, Sim98, Civ86, Gro85). The method differs from the standard proton-neutron QRPA approach (Hal67) and its renormalized versions (Har64, Row68, Cat94) that have been used for double-beta decay calculations by Pac03 and Rod02. The self-consistent use of the Skyrme energy density functional follows the approach of Mor09 but extends it to include pairing correlations and QRPA. The work enables future comparison of nuclear matrix elements between the like-particle QRPA and the pn-QRPA methods, which currently differ by up to a factor of two (Fae12, MED11).
