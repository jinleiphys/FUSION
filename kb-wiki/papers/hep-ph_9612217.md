# A Quantitative Analysis of Charmonium Suppression in Nuclear Collisions

> Data from J/psi and psi' production in p-A collisions are used to determine the cross section for absorption of pre-resonance charmonium in nuclear matter. The J/psi suppression in O-Cu, O-U and S-U collisions is fully reproduced by the corresponding nuclear absorption, while Pb-Pb collisions show an additional suppression increasing with centrality. We study the onset of this change in terms of hadronic comover interactions and conclude that so far no conventional hadronic description can consistently account for all data. Deconfinement, starting at a critical point determined by central S-U collisions, is in accord with the observed suppression pattern.

## Key claim

The paper claims that J/psi suppression in O-Cu, O-U, and S-U collisions is fully explained by pre-resonance absorption in nuclear matter with a cross section of 7.3 ± 0.6 mb, while Pb-Pb collisions show an additional suppression that increases with centrality; the authors conclude that no conventional hadronic description (comover interactions) can consistently account for all data, and that deconfinement starting at a critical point determined by central S-U collisions is in accord with the observed suppression pattern.

## Method

The authors used Glauber theory to model the absorption of a pre-resonance charmonium state (a color octet c-cbar pair coupled to a soft gluon) in nuclear matter, fitting the absorption cross section sigma_abs to p-A collision data from NA38, NA51, and E772 experiments at 200, 450, and 800 GeV/c. They then applied this absorption model to nucleus-nucleus collisions (O-Cu, O-U, S-U, Pb-Pb) using a wounded nucleon model to relate impact parameter to measured transverse energy E_T, and compared the predicted J/psi and psi' survival probabilities to experimental data from NA38 and NA50. They also tested whether hadronic comover interactions could explain the additional suppression in Pb-Pb and psi' data.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross section for pre-resonance charmonium in nuclear matter: sigma_abs = 7.3 ± 0.6 mb (from p-A data fit, 95% confidence level)
- J/psi survival probability in p-W collisions at 800 GeV/c: S_pA_exp = 0.619 ± 0.013 (experimental) vs. S_Gl = 0.650 ± 0.021 (Glauber prediction with 7.3 mb)
- Integrated J/psi survival probability in Pb-Pb collisions: S_psi_exp = 0.27 ± 0.02 (experimental) vs. S_Gl = 0.39 ± 0.03 (Glauber prediction)
- J/psi survival probability in Pb-Pb at highest E_T (149 GeV): S_psi_exp(E_T) = 0.20 ± 0.02 vs. S_Gl(E_T) = 0.35 ± 0.03
- psi' survival probability in S-U collisions at E_T = 82 GeV: S_psi'_exp = 0.09 ± 0.02 vs. S_Gl = 0.43 ± 0.03
- Ratio of psi' to J/psi in p-A collisions: (alpha_psi' - alpha_psi) = 0.00 ± 0.02, with c = (1.74 ± 0.07) × 10^{-2}
- Formation time of c-cbar pair: tau_c ~ 0.07 fm; pre-resonance state formation time: ~0.3 fm (in c-cbar rest frame)

## Context

This work extends the charmonium suppression signal proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986) as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, building on earlier p-A absorption studies by Capella and others and the NA38/NA50 experimental program. The paper provides a quantitative baseline by determining nuclear absorption from p-A data (using the formalism of Kharzeev and Satz (1996) for pre-resonance absorption), then shows that S-U collisions are consistent with this baseline while Pb-Pb collisions show anomalous suppression. This enables future work to use the onset of anomalous suppression as a critical test for deconfinement, and the authors note that hadronic comover models (as attempted by Blaizot and others) cannot simultaneously explain the S-U and Pb-Pb data.
