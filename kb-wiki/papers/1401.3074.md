# High-spin structures of $^{86,87,88,89}$Y: a shell model interpretation

> In this work nuclear structure properties of $^{86,87,88,89}$Y isotopes have been investigated using large-scale shell-model calculations within the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space. The calculations have been performed with JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions that have been proposed for use in the $f_{5/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $g_{9/2}$ model space for both protons and neutrons. Reasonable agreement between experimental and calculated values are obtained. This work will add more information to previous study by projected shell model [Eur. Phys. J. A 48, 138 (2012)] where full-fledged shell model calculations proposed for these nuclei.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations using the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions within the full \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) model space achieve "reasonable agreement" with experimental data for high-spin states in \(^{86,87,88,89}\)Y, but the abstract's claim glosses over that the agreement is interaction-dependent and varies by isotope and spin region, with some levels predicted several hundred keV off from experiment.

## Method

The authors performed full-fledged shell-model calculations for \(^{86,87,88,89}\)Y isotopes using the code ANTOINE, employing the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions in the \(1p_{3/2}\), \(0f_{5/2}\), \(1p_{1/2}\), and \(0g_{9/2}\) model space for both protons and neutrons. They calculated energy levels, reduced electric quadrupole transition strengths \(B(E2)\), electric quadrupole moments \(Q_s\), and magnetic dipole moments \(\mu\), using effective charges \(e_{\text{eff}}^\pi = 1.5e\) and \(e_{\text{eff}}^\nu = 0.5e\) and an effective spin \(g\)-factor \(g_s^{\text{eff}} = 0.7 g_s^{\text{free}}\). Results were compared with experimental data from fusion-evaporation reactions and laser spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{86}\)Y, the \(8^+\) level at 218 keV experimentally is predicted at 181 keV by JUN45 and 74 keV by jj44b.
- For \(^{87}\)Y, the \(15/2^-\) level shows only a 3 keV difference between the experimental value and the jj44b calculation.
- For \(^{88}\)Y, the \(19^+\) level in both calculations is close to the experimental level at 9142 keV.
- For \(^{89}\)Y, the ground state \(1/2^-\) configuration \(\pi(p_{1/2}^1)\) has probability \(\sim 71\%\) (JUN45) and \(\sim 67\%\) (jj44b).
- \(B(E2)\) for \(^{87}\)Y: \(17/2^+ \rightarrow 21/2^+\) transition: experimental 4.6(3) W.u., JUN45 3.99 W.u., jj44b 5.00 W.u.
- \(B(E2)\) for \(^{89}\)Y: \(9/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+\) transition: experimental 4.3(13) W.u., JUN45 2.99 W.u., jj44b 3.22 W.u.
- Quadrupole moment for \(^{87}\)Y \(9/2_1^+\): experimental \(-0.50(6)\) eb, JUN45 \(-0.44\) eb, jj44b \(-0.48\) eb.
- Magnetic moment for \(^{87}\)Y \(9/2_1^+\): experimental \(+6.24(2)\) \(\mu_N\), JUN45 \(+6.59\) \(\mu_N\), jj44b \(+6.42\) \(\mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends earlier truncated shell-model calculations for \(^{86}\)Y (Ref. ref86Y1) and projected shell model calculations for odd Y isotopes (Eur. Phys. J. A 48, 138 (2012)) by performing full-fledged shell-model calculations without truncation in the \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) space. The results enable systematic comparison of the JUN45 and jj44b interactions for yttrium isotopes near \(N=50\), and the authors note that further theoretical development including the \(d_{5/2}\) orbital is needed to study excitations across the \(N=50\) shell closure.
