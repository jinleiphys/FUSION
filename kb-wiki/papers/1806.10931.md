# Bogoliubov Many-Body Perturbation Theory for Open-Shell Nuclei

> A novel Rayleigh-Schrödinger many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) approach is introduced by making use of a particle-number-breaking Bogoliubov reference state to tackle (near-)degenerate open-shell fermionic systems. By choosing a reference state that solves the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov variational problem, the approach reduces to the well-tested Møller-Plesset, i.e., Hartree-Fock based, MBPT when applied to closed-shell systems. Due to its algorithmic simplicity, the newly developed framework provides a computationally simple yet accurate alternative to state-of-the-art non-perturbative many-body approaches. At the price of working in the quasi-particle basis associated with a single-particle basis of sufficient size, the computational scaling of the method is independent of the particle number. This paper presents the first realistic applications of the method ranging from the oxygen to the nickel isotopic chains on the basis of a modern nuclear Hamiltonian derived from chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) provides a computationally simple yet accurate alternative to non-perturbative many-body approaches for open-shell nuclei, but at any finite order it retains a symmetry contamination because particle-number symmetry is only restored in the limit of an all-order resummation.

## Method

The authors introduce a Rayleigh-Schrodinger many-body perturbation theory that uses a particle-number-breaking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) reference state instead of a single Slater determinant. The grand potential is normal-ordered with respect to the HFB vacuum and partitioned into an unperturbed one-body part (diagonal in the quasi-particle basis) and a residual part, enabling a perturbative expansion of the ground-state energy. Calculations are performed for even-even semi-magic nuclei using a chiral Hamiltonian softened by a similarity renormalization group transformation, with the normal-ordered two-body approximation for three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies from second-order BMBPT agree with more sophisticated non-perturbative methods (IT-NCSM, GSCGF-ADC(2), MR-IMSRG(2), CR-CC(2,3)) within a relative deviation not exceeding 2% for O, Ca, and Ni isotopic chains.
- A typical BMBPT run requires up to 15 CPU hours for open-shell nuclei and as little as 6 CPU hours for closed-shell nuclei, compared to roughly 2000 CPU hours per run for MR-IMSRG(2).
- The Hamiltonian uses a chiral two-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to leading order with a cutoff of 500 MeV and a three-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to leading order with a cutoff of 400 MeV, softened with a SRG flow parameter of 0.08 fm^4.
- Calculations use a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with frequency 20 MeV, one- and two-body operators up to e_max = 12, and three-body matrix elements limited to E_3max = 14.

## Context

This work extends the use of symmetry-broken reference states in ab initio nuclear theory, following the Gorkov extension of self-consistent Green's functions (GSCGF) by Soma (2011) and the Bogoliubov coupled-cluster formalism by Signoracci (2015). It provides a perturbative alternative to non-perturbative methods such as MR-IMSRG by Hergert (2013, 2017) and GSCGF, with the computational advantage that it avoids storing large tensors or solving non-linear equations. The method enables systematic low-cost surveys of chiral Hamiltonians across medium-mass isotopic chains and is a stepping stone toward particle-number-projected BMBPT and future extensions to doubly open-shell nuclei via combined SU(2) and U(1) symmetry breaking.
