# Extending pairing energy density functional using pairing rotational moments of inertia

> The pairing energy density functionals (EDFs) that include the spatial derivative and kinetic terms of the pair densities are discussed. The coupling constants of the pairing EDF are adjusted to reproduce the experimental pairing rotational moment of inertia, and the pair-density derivative terms are shown to systematically improve the values of the pairing rotational moments of inertia in Sn and Pb isotopes. It is pointed out that the conventional average pairing gaps overestimate the experimental odd-even mass staggering in the presence of the pair-density derivative terms.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including spatial derivative and kinetic terms of the pair densities in the pairing energy density functional systematically improves the agreement with experimental pairing rotational moments of inertia in Sn and Pb isotopes, but the conventional average pairing gaps overestimate the experimental odd-even mass staggering when these derivative terms are present.

## Method

The author extends the pairing energy density functional by adding pair-density derivative terms (originating from momentum-dependent terms in the Skyrme effective interaction) to the standard zero-range pairing force with density dependence. The coupling constants of the extended pairing EDF are adjusted to reproduce the experimental pairing rotational moment of inertia at 120Sn, and the Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia for neutron pairing rotations are computed using the finite-amplitude method within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation framework, applied to Sn and Pb isotopes with the UNEDF1-HFB functional for the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- The experimental neutron pairing rotational moment of inertia at 120Sn used for fitting is 6.64 MeV^-1
- Pairing coupling constants V_n range from -148.6 to -1499 MeV fm^3 depending on the density dependence type (volume, mixed, surface) and the pair-density derivative coupling constant C_n^DeltaRho (0 to -80 MeV fm^5)
- For 120Sn, the rms radius agrees within 0.03 fm (about 0.6%) across all pairing functionals tested
- For 198Pb, the rms radius agrees within 0.02 fm (about 0.4%) across all pairing functionals tested
- The HFB energy agrees within 1.7 MeV (0.2%) for 120Sn and 5.0 MeV (0.3%) for 198Pb across all pairing functionals

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proposal (Hinohara, 2016) that pairing rotational moments of inertia are excellent pairing observables, and addresses the long-standing problem that pairing gaps derived from Skyrme interactions are unrealistic (as noted by Bender et al., 2003). The approach avoids the difficulties of computing odd-mass systems (which require time-odd functionals with poorly known coupling constants, as discussed by Schunck et al., 2016) by using only even-N ground-state energies. The extended pairing EDF form follows the general bilinear form of pair densities with isoscalar particle-hole density dependence (Perlinska et al., 2004) and local gauge invariance conditions (Engel et al., 1975; Dobaczewski et al., 1995).
