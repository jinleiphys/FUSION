# Alpha-particle condensation: a nuclear quantum phase transition

> When the density of a nuclear system is decreased, homogeneous states undergo the so-called Mott transition towards clusterised states, e.g. alpha clustering, both in nuclei and in nuclear matter. Here we investigate such a quantum phase transition (QPT) by using microscopic energy density functional (EDF) calculations both with the relativistic and the Gogny approaches on the diluted $^{16}$O nucleus. The evolution of the corresponding single-particle spectrum under dilution is studied, and a Mott-like transition is predicted at about 1/3 of the saturation density. Complementary approaches are used in order to understand this QPT. A study of spatial localisation properties as a function of the density allows to derive a value of the Mott density in agreement with the one obtained by fully microscopic calculations in $^{16}$O and in nuclear matter. Moreover a study of the spontaneous symmetry breaking of the rotational group in $^{16}$O, down to the discrete tetrahedral one, provides further insight on the features displayed by the single-particle spectrum obtained within the EDF approach.The content of the tetrahedrally deformed A-nucleon product state in terms of spherical particle-hole configurations is investigated. Finally a study of quartet condensation and the corresponding macroscopic QPT is undertaken in infinite matter.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a Mott-like quantum phase transition from a dilute homogeneous phase to a tetrahedrally arranged four-alpha cluster phase in $^{16}$O at approximately one-third of the nuclear saturation density, based on microscopic energy density functional calculations with both relativistic and Gogny approaches, with the caveat that the transition density depends on the specific functional and basis parameters used.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations on $^{16}$O using both covariant (DD-ME2 parametrization) and non-relativistic Gogny (D1S parametrization) energy density functionals, with the constraint applied to the root-mean-square radius while enforcing zero global quadrupole moment to achieve isotropic inflation. They analyzed the evolution of single-particle spectra, pairing correlations, and spatial localization as functions of density, and classified the tetrahedrally deformed states using the irreducible representations of the double tetrahedral group $T_d$.

## Key numbers

- The Mott transition occurs at a critical radius $R_c = 3.7$ fm in the relativistic calculation, corresponding to a mean density ratio $\rho_\text{Mott}/\rho_0 = (R_\text{eq}/R_c)^3 \sim 0.35$, i.e. approximately $\rho_0/3$
- In the Gogny calculation, the transition occurs at $R_c = 3.9$ fm, giving $\rho_\text{Mott}/\rho_0 \sim 0.33$, again approximately $\rho_0/3$
- The valence neutron gap in the spherical symmetry-restricted calculation drops from 10.71 MeV at the equilibrium radius $R = 2.60$ fm to 4.63 MeV at $R = 3.40$ fm and 2.36 MeV at $R = 6.0$ fm
- At $R = 6.0$ fm, the neutron quasi-particle gap increases from 2.2 MeV in the spherical symmetry-restricted case to 4.1 MeV in the spherical symmetry-restricted Hartree-Bogoliubov case, with a pairing energy of approximately -8 MeV
- In the tetrahedral configuration at $R = 6.0$ fm, the neutron single-particle levels form two bunches of four near-degenerate orbitals separated by a gap of 17.4 MeV
- The confining potential depth decreases from approximately -75 MeV at equilibrium to -10 MeV at $R = 6.0$ fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on dimensionless localization parameters and clustering in nuclei (Ebran 2012, 2013, 2014) by providing explicit microscopic energy density functional calculations of the Mott transition in $^{16}$O. The results support the interpretation of the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C as a finite-size alpha-particle condensate (Schuck 2013) and connect to studies of alpha-cluster structures as manifestations of supersolidity (Ohkubo 2019). The findings enable future investigations of quantum phase transitions in other alpha-conjugate nuclei and provide a microscopic foundation for understanding alpha preformation at the surfaces of heavy nuclei (Typel 2014, Ebran 2018).
