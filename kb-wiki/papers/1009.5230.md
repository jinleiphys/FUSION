# Initial eccentricity fluctuations and their relation to higher-order flow harmonics

> Monte Carlo simulations are used to compute the centrality dependence of the participant eccentricities ($ε_{n}$) in Au+Au collisions, for the two primary models currently employed for eccentricity estimates -- the Glauber and the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) models. They suggest specific testable predictions for the magnitude and centrality dependence of the flow coefficients $v_n$, respectively measured relative to the event planes $Ψ_n$. They also indicate that the ratios of several of these coefficients may provide an additional constraint for distinguishing between the models. Such a constraint could be important for a more precise determination of the specific viscosity of the matter produced in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the centrality dependence of the ratios of higher-order flow harmonics (e.g., \(v_3/(v_2)^{3/2}\), \(v_4/(v_2)^2\), \(v_2/v_3\)) can provide an experimental constraint to distinguish between the Glauber and factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) models for the initial collision geometry, which currently differ by as much as ~25% in their eccentricity predictions and lead to an approximate factor of two uncertainty in the extracted specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\).

## Method
Monte Carlo simulations were used to compute the centrality dependence of participant eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) (for \(n \leq 6\)) in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, using both the MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models. The spatial distribution of nucleons was generated via a Woods-Saxon function with radius \(R_0 = 6.38\) fm and diffuseness \(d = 0.53\) fm; for MC-Glauber, an entropy density weight combining participants and binary collisions (\(\alpha = 0.14\)) was applied. Eccentricities were evaluated from the two-dimensional transverse source density profile using two weighting schemes: \(\omega(\mathbf{r_{\perp}}) = \mathbf{r_{\perp}}^2\) and \(\omega(\mathbf{r_{\perp}}) = \mathbf{r_{\perp}}^n\), and the resulting \(\varepsilon_n\) values were used as proxies for the flow coefficients \(v_n\) under the assumption of perfect fluid hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers
- The Glauber and fKLN models give \(\varepsilon_2\) values that differ by as much as ~25% for a given \(N_{\text{part}}\), leading to an approximate factor of two uncertainty in the extracted \(\eta/s\) value.
- For the \(\omega(\mathbf{r_{\perp}}) = \mathbf{r_{\perp}}^2\) weighting, the ratio \(v_2/v_3\) is predicted to increase by a factor \(\approx 2\) from central to mid-central collisions (over the range \(N_{\text{part}} \sim 350\) to 150) for MC-Glauber-like eccentricities.
- For the \(\omega(\mathbf{r_{\perp}}) = \mathbf{r_{\perp}}^2\) weighting, \(\varepsilon_n\) for \(n \geq 3\) shows an approximate \(1/\sqrt{N_{\text{part}}}\) dependence.
- The MC-Glauber model uses a Woods-Saxon radius \(R_0 = 6.38\) fm and diffuseness \(d = 0.53\) fm for the Au nucleus.
- The entropy density weight for MC-Glauber uses \(\alpha = 0.14\), constrained by multiplicity measurements as a function of \(N_{\text{part}}\) for Au+Au collisions.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of initial geometry fluctuations and their influence on higher-order eccentricities \(\varepsilon_{n, n\geq 3}\) (Broniowski 2007, Lacey 2010, Alver 2010, Holopainen 2010, Petersen 2010, Staig 2010, Qin 2010, Schenke 2010) by providing a systematic comparison of the Glauber and fKLN models across multiple harmonics and weighting schemes. The results enable experimental tests using measured flow harmonic ratios to discriminate between these models, which is critical for reducing the factor-of-two uncertainty in \(\eta/s\) extraction from viscous hydrodynamic simulations (Luzum 2008, Lacey 2010). The paper also notes that the ratios \(v_3/(v_2)^{3/2}\) and \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) have been found to scale with \(p_T\) (Lacey 2011), suggesting reduced sensitivity to viscosity and thus a more direct probe of initial geometry.
