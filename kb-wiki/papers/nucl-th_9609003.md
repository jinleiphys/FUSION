# Nearthreshold Large $Q^2$ Electroproduction off Polarized Deuteron

> The exclusive and inclusive electroproduction off the polarized deuteron is considered at large $Q^2$ and $x \ge 0.5$. It is shown that the use of a polarized target will allow to emphasize smaller than average internucleon distances in the deuteron. As a result, we expect amplification of all the effects (color transparency, relativistic dynamics, etc.) sensitive to small internucleon distances. Numerical estimates are given for the processes $e+ \vec d \rightarrow e +p+n$ and $e+ \vec d \rightarrow e +X$.

## Key claim

Using a polarized deuteron target in large-\(Q^2\) electroproduction at \(x \ge 0.5\) will enhance the contribution of the deuteron’s \(D\)-state, thereby emphasizing smaller-than-average internucleon distances and amplifying effects such as color transparency and relativistic dynamics; the paper provides numerical estimates for both exclusive \(e+\vec d \rightarrow e +p+n\) and inclusive \(e+\vec d \rightarrow e +X\) processes, but the actual magnitude of the amplification depends on the specific kinematic region and the model used for final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for deuteron electrodisintegration including final-state interactions (FSI) via an extended eikonal approximation, using a Feynman-diagram-based formalism that accounts for relativistic kinematics. For color transparency (CT) studies, they replace the elastic eikonal FSI amplitude with reduced PLC-nucleon interactions modeled by the quantum diffusion model (QDM) and a three-state baryonic superposition model. For relativistic effects, they compare the virtual nucleon (VN) approach (lab-frame description with negative-energy states) and the light-cone (LC) approach (non-covariant framework with instantaneous interactions), computing the tensor asymmetry \(T^{20}\) and the asymmetry \(A_d\) for polarized targets.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 \ge 4-6\) GeV\(^2\), CT effects may produce up to 30% changes in the transparency ratio \(T = \sigma^{FSI}_{e,e'p} / \sigma^{PWIA}_{e,e'p}\) for unpolarized targets in perpendicular kinematics (\(\alpha \approx 1\), \(p_t \le 400\) MeV/c).
- For the asymmetry \(A_d\) in polarized deuteron electrodisintegration at \(p_t = 300\) MeV/c, CT effects can change \(A_d\) by as much as a factor of two at \(Q^2 \sim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- In the elastic eikonal (Glauber) approximation at \(Q^2 > 3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), the \(pn\) total cross section is \(\sigma^{pn}_{tot} \approx 40\) mb, with \(a_n \approx -0.2\) and slope parameter \(b_n \approx 6-8\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- For inclusive \(\vec d(e,e')\) scattering at \(x<1\), the VN and LC approaches yield practically the same unpolarized cross section but differ by as much as a factor of two in the tensor polarization cross section.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Frankfurt, Guzey, Sargsian, and Strikman (1995) for intermediate-energy deuteron electrodisintegration at \(Q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x \sim 1\), where higher partial waves converge and relativistic FSI can be treated via covariant Feynman diagrams. It also builds on the CT models of Farrar, Frankfurt, Liu, and Strikman (quantum diffusion model) and Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (1993) (three-state model). The paper enables experimental tests of CT onset at moderate \(Q^2\) and discrimination between VN and LC approaches to relativistic bound-nucleon dynamics, using polarized deuteron targets to enhance sensitivity to small internucleon distances.
