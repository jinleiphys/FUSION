# Relativistic effects in proton-induced deuteron break-up at intermediate energies with forward emission of a fast proton pair

> Recent data on the reaction pD -> (pp) n with a fast forward pp pair with very small excitation energy is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. It is demonstrated that the minimum non-relativistic amplitude is completely masked by relativistic effects, such as Lorentz boost and the negative-energy P components in the 1S_0 Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of the pp pair.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic prediction of a deep minimum in the cross section of the reaction \(pd \to (pp)n\) due to the node of the \(^1S_0\) wave function is completely masked by relativistic effects; the covariant Bethe-Salpeter calculation, including Lorentz boost and negative-energy \(P\) components, reproduces the smooth decline of the measured cross section without any pronounced minimum.

## Method

The authors analyzed the reaction \(p + d \to (p_1 p_2) + n\) at low excitation energy of the \(pp\) pair (\(E_x \sim 0-3\) MeV) and intermediate proton kinetic energies \(T_p \sim 0.6-2.0\) GeV, using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter (BS) formalism. The deuteron BS amplitude was taken from a previous solution with a realistic one-boson exchange kernel, and the final-state interaction of the two protons was treated by solving the BS equation for the \(t\)-matrix within a one-iteration approximation. The cross section was computed including the \(^1S_0^{++}\) component and the negative-energy \(P\) components (\(^3P_0^{+-}\) and \(^3P_0^{-+}\)) of the \(pp\) pair, with the latter obtained via an iterative scheme using a separable potential and the experimental \(^1S_0\) phase shift.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the \(pp\) pair is \(E_x \sim 0-3\) MeV.
- The initial kinetic energy range of the beam proton is \(T_p \sim 0.6-2.0\) GeV.
- The neutron three-momentum in a typical kinematics is \(|\bn| \sim 0.3-0.5\) GeV/c.
- For a specific example with \(|\bp| = 1.22\) GeV/c, \(\theta_1' \sim 4^\circ\), \(E_x = 3\) MeV, and \(|\bp_1| = 0.765\) GeV/c, the neutron three-momentum is \(|\bn| \simeq 0.5\) GeV/c.
- The cross section is integrated over the excitation energy range \(E_x = 0-3\) MeV for the presented numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the deuteron break-up reaction proposed in COSY experiments (Komarov et al., 2002) and addresses the failure of non-relativistic models to describe the measured cross section, which was previously noted in preliminary reports (Komarov et al., 2002; Uzikov et al., 2002). The covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, including negative-energy \(P\) components, provides an excellent description of the data, demonstrating that relativistic effects beyond the one-nucleon exchange mechanism are essential. This enables future studies of off-mass shell effects in \(NN\) interactions and short-distance phenomena in hadron-deuteron processes.
