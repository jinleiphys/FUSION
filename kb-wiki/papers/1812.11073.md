# Testing of quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current and two-body meson exchange current models with the MiniBooNE neutrino data and analysis of these processes at energies available at the NOvA experiment

> The charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) scattering of muon neutrinos on a carbon target is analyzed using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) taking into account the contribution of the two-particle and two-hole meson exchange current ($2p-2h$ MEC) to the weak response functions. A fit the RDWIA+MEC model to the MiniBooNE neutrino data is performed and the best fit value of nucleon axial mass $M_A=1.2 \pm 0.06$ GeV is obtained. We also extract the values of the axial form factor $F_A(Q^2)$ as a function of the squared momentum transfer $Q^2$ from the measured $dσ/dQ^2$ cross section. The flux-integrated CCQE-like differential cross sections for neutrino scattering at energies of the NOvA experiment are estimated within the RDWIA+MEC approach.

## Key claim

The RDWIA+MEC model, which combines relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with two-particle-two-hole meson exchange current contributions, can describe the MiniBooNE CCQE-like neutrino data with a best-fit axial mass of \(M_A = 1.20 \pm 0.06\) GeV, a value lower than the \(M_A \approx 1.35\) GeV previously required by the RFGM alone, but the model slightly overestimates the data in the range \(0.08 < Q^2 < 0.3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and tends to underestimate the data for the most forward muon scattering angles (\(\cos\theta > 0.9\)).

## Method

The authors model charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) neutrino scattering on carbon using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) for the genuine \(1p-1h\) contribution, which includes nuclear shell structure and final-state interactions via a relativistic optical potential. They add the \(2p-2h\) meson exchange current (MEC) contribution using parametrizations of exact MEC calculations based on the relativistic Fermi gas model, without short-range correlations or FSI in the MEC sector. A shape-only fit of the RDWIA+MEC model to the MiniBooNE neutrino data is performed using the \(\chi^2\) statistic on the single-differential \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) and double-differential \(d^2\sigma/dTd\cos\theta\) cross sections, with the nucleon axial mass \(M_A\) as the only free parameter. The axial form factor \(F_A(Q^2)\) is also extracted as a function of \(Q^2\) from the measured flux-integrated \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) cross section.

## Key numbers

* Best-fit axial mass from the combined 1D+2D main fit to MiniBooNE data: \(M_A = 1.20 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/DOF = 111/150\).
* Best-fit axial mass from the 1D fit to \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) alone: \(M_A = 1.17 \pm 0.03\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/DOF = 19/13\).
* Best-fit axial mass from the 2D fit to \(d^2\sigma/dTd\cos\theta\) alone: \(M_A = 1.24 \pm 0.09\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/DOF = 62/136\).
* The \(2p-2h\) MEC contribution is comparable to the genuine QE process, being of order 25% and increasing up to 30% at low \(Q^2\).
* The MiniBooNE data cover the range \(0 < Q^2 < 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with a normalization uncertainty of 10.7% and a CCQE corrected sample purity of 77%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RDWIA-only analysis (Butkevich 2015) which required \(M_A \approx 1.37\) GeV, by including \(2p-2h\) MEC contributions that lower the extracted axial mass. The result \(M_A = 1.20 \pm 0.06\) GeV agrees within errors with the value \(M_A = 1.15 \pm 0.03\) GeV obtained from a global fit of MiniBooNE and MINERvA data by Wilkinson (2016). The study enables predictions of CCQE-like cross sections for the NOvA experiment using the RDWIA+MEC approach with the MiniBooNE-derived axial mass, and demonstrates that MEC contributions are essential for describing the MiniBooNE data across a wide kinematic range.
