# In-medium spectral functions and dilepton rates with the Functional Renormalization Group

> We present recent results on in-medium spectral functions of vector and axial-vector mesons, the electromagnetic (EM) spectral function and dilepton rates using the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach. Our method is based on an analytic continuation procedure that allows us to calculate real-time quantities like spectral functions at finite temperature and chemical potential. As an effective low-energy model for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) we use an extended linear-sigma model including quarks where (axial-)vector mesons as well as the photon are introduced as gauge bosons. In particular, it is shown how the $ρ$ and the $a_1$ spectral function become degenerate at high temperatures or chemical potentials due to the restoration of chiral symmetry. Preliminary results for the EM spectral function and the dilepton production rate are discussed with a focus on the possibility to identify signatures of the chiral crossover and the critical endpoint (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper presents first results for the electromagnetic (EM) spectral function and dilepton production rate using a Functional Renormalization Group framework, but the effects of the critical endpoint (CEP) on the dilepton rate are almost negligible in the present truncation, calling for improved truncations that include baryonic degrees of freedom and 4-particle processes.

## Method

The authors use the Functional Renormalization Group applied to an extended linear-sigma model with quarks, where vector and axial-vector mesons as well as the photon are introduced as gauge bosons via the gauging principle. Flow equations for the rho and a1 2-point functions are analytically continued from Euclidean to real energies and solved numerically. The EM spectral function is extracted by diagonalizing the matrix of 2-point functions for the photon and rho fields under the Vector Meson Dominance assumption, and the dilepton rate is then computed using the Weldon formula.

## Key numbers

- The pion-pion decay threshold for the rho and photon is located at omega approximately 300 MeV at T=10 MeV and mu=0.
- The quark-antiquark decay threshold is located at omega approximately 600 MeV at T=10 MeV and mu=0.
- The critical endpoint (CEP) is located at mu approximately 298 MeV and T approximately 10 MeV.
- The dilepton rate calculation assumes massless dileptons (m=0) and vanishing spatial momentum.

## Context

This work extends the FRG framework for spectral functions developed in Kamikado (2014), Tripolt (2014), and Tripolt, von Smekal, and Wambach (2016), which was previously applied to vector and axial-vector mesons in Jung, Rennecke, Tripolt et al. (2017). The results enable future calculations of dilepton rates with improved truncations including baryonic degrees of freedom and 4-particle processes, which may allow identification of signatures of the chiral crossover and the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
