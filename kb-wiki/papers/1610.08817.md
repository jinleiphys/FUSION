# Meson cloud effects on the pion quark distribution function in the chiral constituent quark model

> We investigate the valence quark distribution function of the pion $v^π(x,Q^2)$ in the framework of the chiral constituent quark model and evaluate the meson cloud effects on $v^π(x,Q^2)$. We explicitly demonstrate how the meson cloud effects affect $v^π(x,Q^2)$ in detail. We find that the meson cloud correction causes an overall 32\% reduction of the valence quark distribution and an enhancement at the small Bjorken $x$ regime. Besides, we also find that the dressing effect of the meson cloud will make the valence quark distribution to be softer in the large $x$ region.

## Key claim

The meson cloud dressing effect on the pion valence quark distribution function is an overall 32% reduction, with an enhancement at small Bjorken x (x ≤ 0.4) and an additional suppression at large x (x ≥ 0.8); the enhancement at small x decreases rapidly as Q² increases, while the suppression at large x becomes more pronounced.

## Method

The authors adopt the chiral constituent quark model and compute the meson cloud dressing corrections to the valence quark distribution of the pion using an effective Lagrangian for the interaction between constituent quarks and Nambu-Goldstone bosons. They first choose a bare valence quark distribution of the form v^π_bare(x,Q₀²) = N x^1.8 (1-x)^1.8 at an initial scale Q₀ = 0.50 GeV, then dress it by including contributions from pion and kaon clouds via convolution integrals with splitting functions and meson quark distributions. The dressed distributions are evolved to higher Q² using next-to-leading-order DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

- The meson cloud correction causes an overall 32% reduction of the valence quark distribution.
- The bare distribution is parameterized as v^π_bare(x,Q₀²) = N x^1.8 (1-x)^1.8 with initial scale Q₀ = 0.50 GeV.
- The constituent quark masses used are m_u = m_d = 360 MeV and m_s = 570 MeV; the pion mass is 140 MeV, the kaon mass is 494 MeV, the pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, and the cutoff parameter Λ = 1.4 GeV.
- The quark axial-vector coupling constant g_A is set to 1.0.
- At Q = 1 GeV and x = 0.2, the enhancement of v^π is about 30%; at Q = 2 GeV the enhancement is reduced to 15%; at Q = 4 GeV the enhancement becomes only 10%.
- The renormalization constant Z is approximately 2/3.

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud dressing scheme previously applied to nucleon parton distribution functions by Suzuki (1997) to the pion, where such studies have been less active due to experimental difficulties. The dressed valence quark distribution is fitted to the phenomenological extraction by Aicher et al. (2010), which included next-to-leading-logarithmic threshold resummation effects and showed a softer large-x behavior than earlier pion PDF sets such as SMRS (Sutton, 1991) and GRS (Gluck, 1999). The results enable future comparisons with data from the COMPASS experiment at CERN and the planned pion-induced Drell-Yan experiment at J-PARC, which will probe the large-x region at lower beam energies.
