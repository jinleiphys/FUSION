# Composition of low-lying $\mathbf{J=\tfrac{3}{2}^\pm \,Δ}$-baryons

> A Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation is used to develop insights into the structure of the four lightest $(I,J^P=\tfrac{3}{2},\tfrac{3}{2}^\pm)$ baryon multiplets. Whilst these systems can contain isovector-axialvector and isovector-vector diquarks, one may neglect the latter and still arrive at a reliable description. The $(\tfrac{3}{2},\tfrac{3}{2}^+)$ states are the simpler systems, with features that bear some resemblance to quark model pictures, \emph{e.g}., their most prominent rest-frame orbital angular momentum component is $\mathsf S$-wave and the $Δ(1600)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$ may reasonably be viewed as a radial excitation of the $Δ(1232)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$. The $(\tfrac{3}{2},\tfrac{3}{2}^-)$ states are more complex: the $Δ(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ expresses little of the character of a radial excitation of the $Δ(1700)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$; and whilst the rest-frame wave function of the latter is predominantly $\mathsf P$-wave, the leading piece in the $Δ(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ wave function is $\mathsf S$-wave, in conflict with quark model expectations. Experiments that can test these predictions, such as large momentum transfer resonance electroexcitation, may shed light on the nature of emergent hadron mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the four lightest $(I,J^P=\tfrac{3}{2},\tfrac{3}{2}^\pm)$ $\Delta$-baryons can be reliably described using a Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation that neglects isovector-vector diquark correlations, but the negative-parity states show unexpected structural features: the $\Delta(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ is predominantly S-wave in its rest-frame wave function, contradicting quark model expectations that it should be a P-wave radial excitation of the $\Delta(1700)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$.

## Method

The authors solved a Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation for the four lowest-lying $(\tfrac{3}{2},\tfrac{3}{2}^\pm)$ $\Delta$-baryons, using only isovector-axialvector $(1,1^+)$ and isovector-vector $(1,1^-)$ diquark correlations. The Faddeev amplitudes were obtained by solving the linear integral equation shown in Fig. 1 of the paper, with dressed-quark and diquark propagators taken from prior work, and the unamputated wave functions were reconstructed by reattaching propagator legs to enable angular momentum decomposition in the baryon rest frame.

## Key numbers

- $\Delta(1232)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$ calculated mass: 1.346 GeV (with both diquark types), after subtracting $\delta_{\rm MB}=0.17$ GeV for comparison with experiment
- $\Delta(1600)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$ calculated mass: 1.786(79) GeV
- $\Delta(1700)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ calculated mass: 1.871(69) GeV
- $\Delta(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ calculated mass: 2.043(50) GeV
- Mean difference between central predicted masses and empirical pole positions: $\delta_{\rm MB}=0.17$ GeV
- Effective diquark masses used: $m_{\{uu\}_{1^+}}=0.9$ GeV, $m_{\{uu\}_{1^-}}=1.4$ GeV
- Canonical normalisation coupling constants: $g_{1^+}=12.7$, $g_{1^-}=1.58$
- For $\Delta(1232)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$: 96.97% of amplitude from $(1,1^+)$ diquarks, 3.03% from $(1,1^-)$ diquarks
- For $\Delta(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$: 88.73% of amplitude from $(1,1^+)$ diquarks, 11.27% from $(1,1^-)$ diquarks
- Single partial-wave lowest masses: $\Delta(1232)\tfrac{3}{2}^+$ S-wave only 1.35 GeV; $\Delta(1700)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ P-wave only 1.80 GeV; $\Delta(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ S-wave only 2.06 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark+diquark Faddeev equation approach previously applied to $(\tfrac{1}{2},\tfrac{1}{2}^\pm)$ baryons by Chen et al. (2017), which found that negative-parity states showed more complex structure than positive-parity ones. The results contradict quark model expectations (Capstick 2000, Crede 2013, Giannini 2015) that the $\Delta(1940)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ should be a P-wave radial excitation of the $\Delta(1700)\tfrac{3}{2}^-$, instead finding it predominantly S-wave. The predictions can be tested by experiments on large momentum transfer resonance electroexcitation, which may shed light on emergent hadron mass.
