# In-Medium Effects in Photo- and Neutrino-Induced Reactions on Nuclei

> In this talk various aspects of in-medium behavior of hadrons are discussed with an emphasis on observable effects. It is stressed that final state interactions can have a major effect on observables and thus have to be considered as part of the theory. This is demonstrated with examples from photo-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that any observable in-medium signal from hadrons produced in photon- or neutrino-nucleus reactions is heavily modified by final state interactions (FSI), and that these FSI must be treated with a full coupled-channel transport calculation rather than simpler Glauber-type approximations to obtain reliable predictions.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical coupled-channel transport theory implemented in the GiBUU code, which propagates spectral phase-space distributions of all particles from the initial probe-nucleus interaction through the nuclear medium to the detector. The model factorizes into three steps: shadowing in the entrance channel, elementary one-body interactions with individual nucleons (including Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and mean-field binding), and propagation of produced particles with rescattering, absorption, and charge-exchange effects. The transport equation includes drift terms for independent hadron propagation and collision terms for elastic and inelastic interactions, with particle production described by a gain term proportional to the spectral function and absorption by a loss term.

## Key numbers

* Inclusive double differential cross section for charged current neutrino scattering on 56Fe at E_nu = 1 GeV and Q^2 = 0.15 GeV^2 shows a left peak from Delta excitation and a right peak from quasielastic scattering.
* For photoproduction of pi0 on Ca and Pb at photon energy 250 MeV, the BUU calculation gives only about 2/3 of the experimental cross section, attributed to coherent pion production not described by transport.
* Neutron knockout in charged current neutrino scattering on 56Fe at E_nu = 1 GeV shows a significant signal at E_mu approximately 0.9 GeV with a long ridge in Q^2 when FSI are included, whereas without FSI essentially no neutron knockout occurs.
* Neutral current pion production on 56Fe shows that the main absorption mechanism for pions above p_pi approximately 0.2 GeV is pi N to Delta followed by Delta N to N N, leading to considerable cross section reduction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous reviews (Mosel et al., 2004; Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2005) on in-medium hadron properties by emphasizing observable effects in photonuclear and neutrino-induced reactions. It directly builds on the GiBUU transport framework originally developed for heavy-ion collisions and subsequently tested against reactions with protons, pions, electrons, photons, and neutrinos. The paper enables more reliable predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE by demonstrating that FSI must be included in calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections, and it provides a benchmark for comparing with experimental data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration on omega meson modifications and the g7 experiment at JLab.
