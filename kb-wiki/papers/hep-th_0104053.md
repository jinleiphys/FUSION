# Perturbation theory for bound states and resonances where potentials and propagators have arbitrary energy dependence

> Standard derivations of ``time-independent perturbation theory'' of quantum mechanics cannot be applied to the general case where potentials are energy dependent or where the inverse free Green function is a non-linear function of energy. Such derivations cannot be used, for example, in the context of relativistic quantum field theory. Here we solve this problem by providing a new, general formulation of perturbation theory for calculating the changes in the energy spectrum and wave function of bound states and resonances induced by perturbations to the Hamiltonian. Although our derivation is valid for energy-dependent potentials and is not restricted to inverse free Green functions that are linear in the energy, the expressions obtained for the energy and wave function corrections are compact, practical, and maximally similar to the ones of quantum mechanics. For the case of relativistic quantum field theory, our approach provides a direct covariant way of obtaining corrections to bound and resonance state masses, as well as to wave functions that are not in the centre of mass frame.

# Perturbation theory for bound states and resonances where potentials and propagators have arbitrary energy dependence

## Key claim

The paper provides a new, general formulation of time-independent perturbation theory that works for bound states and resonances even when the interaction kernel is energy-dependent and the inverse free Green function is a non-linear function of energy, conditions that invalidate standard quantum-mechanical derivations. The authors claim their expressions for energy and wave function corrections are "compact, practical, and maximally similar to the ones of quantum mechanics," but the derivation assumes complete knowledge of the unperturbed Green function (not just the unperturbed spectrum and wave functions), which is a stronger condition than in standard non-relativistic perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter-like equation [G₀⁻¹(P) − K₀(P) − K₁(P)]Ψ = 0, where K₁ is a perturbation to the known kernel K₀, and the unperturbed Green function G_u(P) (including its pole structure) is assumed known. Using a technique adapted from Feshbach's nuclear reaction theory, they derive exact expressions for the perturbed mass M and wave function Ψ by introducing a "background" Green function G^b that satisfies G^b = G_u^b + G_u^b K₁ G^b, where G_u^b is the non-pole part of G_u. From these exact expressions, they generate perturbation series for M² and Ψ to arbitrary order by expanding in powers of K₁ and its derivatives with respect to P².

## Key numbers

- First-order mass shift: δ₁ = i Φ̄ K₁ Φ (evaluated at P² = M_u²)
- Second-order mass shift: δ₂ = i Φ̄ [δ₁ K₁′ + K₁ G_u^b K₁] Φ
- First-order wave function correction factor: η₁ = ½ Δ₁ + G_u^b K₁, where Δ₁ = i Φ̄ K₁′ Φ
- The normalization constant Z = 1 / {1 − i {Φ̄[K₁ + K₁ G^b K₁]Φ}′} evaluated at P² = M²

## Context

This work extends standard time-independent perturbation theory (which requires energy-independent potentials and inverse free Green functions linear in energy) to the covariant field-theoretic context of the Bethe-Salpeter equation and its three-dimensional reductions. It provides an alternative to the contour-integral method of Lepage (1990s), the approach of Bodwin and Yennie (1980s), and the projection-operator formulation of Ivanov et al. (1998). The key advantage claimed over these prior approaches is that the wave function corrections are manifestly Lorentz covariant at each order, avoiding the need to compute corrections in the rest frame and then boost, a procedure that itself requires perturbative treatment of the boost when the mass shifts. The formulation enables direct calculation of bound state mass shifts and wave functions for systems not at rest, relevant for electromagnetic form factors and other scattering observables.
