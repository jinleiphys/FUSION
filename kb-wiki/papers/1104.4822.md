# Diffusion in Coulomb Crystals

> Diffusion in coulomb crystals can be important for the structure of neutron star crusts. We determine diffusion constants $D$ from molecular dynamics simulations. We find that $D$ for coulomb crystals with relatively soft-core $1/r$ interactions may be larger than $D$ for Lennard-Jones or other solids with harder-core interactions. Diffusion, for simulations of nearly perfect body-centered-cubic lattices, involves the exchange of ions in ring-like configurations. Here ions "hop" in unison without the formation of long lived vacancies. Diffusion, for imperfect crystals, involves the motion of defects. Finally, we find that diffusion, for an amorphous system rapidly quenched from coulomb parameter $Γ=175$ to coulomb parameters up to $Γ=1750$, is fast enough so that the system starts to crystallize during long simulation runs. These results strongly suggest that coulomb solids in cold white dwarf stars, and the crust of neutron stars, will be crystalline and not amorphous.

## Key claim

Diffusion in Coulomb crystals with soft-core 1/r interactions may be significantly faster than in solids with harder-core interactions such as Lennard-Jones systems, and this diffusion is fast enough that astrophysical Coulomb solids in white dwarf stars and neutron star crusts are expected to be crystalline rather than amorphous, provided the cooling timescales are long compared to the molecular dynamics simulation timescales used in this work.

## Method

Classical molecular dynamics simulations were performed for one-component plasmas with Yukawa (screened Coulomb) interactions, using the velocity Verlet algorithm with velocity rescaling every ten time steps to maintain approximately constant temperature. The diffusion constant D was calculated from the mean squared displacement of ions using a modified definition D'(t) that excludes ions remaining near their original lattice site via a cutoff radius R_c = 1.07a, where a is the ion sphere radius. Three classes of initial conditions were studied: perfect body-centered-cubic (bcc) lattices, imperfect crystals formed by cooling from liquid configurations, and amorphous systems produced by rapid quenching from Γ=175 to higher Γ values up to Γ=1750.

## Key numbers

- For perfect bcc lattice initial conditions at Γ=175 with λ=1.82a and N=93312 ions, D = 1.0 × 10⁻⁵ ω_p a²
- For perfect bcc lattice initial conditions at Γ=175 with λ=2.70a and N=93312 ions, D = 5.6 × 10⁻⁶ ω_p a²
- For perfect bcc lattice initial conditions at Γ=200 with λ=1.82a, D = 3.9 × 10⁻⁷ ω_p a²
- For imperfect crystal initial conditions at Γ=175 with λ=2.70a and N=27648 ions, D ranges from 8.9 × 10⁻⁶ to 1.9 × 10⁻⁵ ω_p a²
- For imperfect crystal initial conditions at Γ=200 with λ=1.82a, D = 2.1 × 10⁻⁶ ω_p a²
- For amorphous systems quenched from Γ=175 to Γ=500 with N=8192 ions, crystallization time t₀ = 24,000/ω_p
- For amorphous systems quenched from Γ=175 to Γ=1750 with N=8192 ions, no crystallization observed before t₀ = 25,000,000/ω_p
- The diffusion constant for the solid near melting (Γ=175) is about 200 times smaller than D for the liquid phase at the same Γ
- The exponential fit for D versus Γ for λ=1.82a gives D/ω_p a² ≈ 6100 exp(-0.115 Γ) for Γ ≤ 185

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics calculations of diffusion in Coulomb liquids by Hansen et al. (1975) and binary mixtures by Hansen et al. (1985) to the solid phase, where diffusion was often simply assumed to be zero. The results are relevant for sedimentation of impurities in white dwarfs (as studied by Ne_lars and Ne_lars2) and neutron star crusts, and for understanding the structure of multicomponent plasma crystals that determine thermal and electrical conductivity of neutron star crusts. The finding that amorphous systems crystallize relatively quickly at Γ values up to 1500 strongly suggests that astrophysical Coulomb solids will be crystalline, consistent with X-ray observations of rapid neutron star crust cooling after accretion episodes.
