# A HIC Primitive Spinodal Decomposition Signature

> Evidence of a primitive spinodal decomposition has been obtained for central Ni+Ni Heavy Ion Collision, since higher order charge correlations show a peak when four fragments of size equal to 6 are produced with an excitation of 4.75 MeV. This can be considered as a signature of a primitive breakup in equal sized fragments with a privileged fragment size. This computational result confirms other experimental and theoretical evidences about spinodal decompostion in HIC.

## Key claim

A primitive spinodal decomposition signature is observed in computational simulations of central Ni+Ni heavy ion collisions, specifically when higher-order charge correlations show a peak for four fragments of size equal to 6 at an excitation of 4.75 MeV, which the authors describe as a "primitive breakup" rather than a fully developed spinodal decomposition.

## Method

Central Ni+Ni collisions were simulated using the LATINO semiclassical dynamical model with a Pandharipande potential built from linear combinations of Yukawa potentials. Clusters were identified using an Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm that employs simulated annealing to find the most bound partition by minimizing the sum of cluster internal energies. Higher-order charge correlations, defined as the ratio of correlated to uncorrelated fragments, were computed for projectile energies ranging from 600 to 2000 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Four fragments of size equal to 6 were produced at an excitation energy of 4.75 MeV, corresponding to the peak in higher-order charge correlations
- Projectile energy range studied: 600 to 2000 MeV
- The privileged fragment size identified was 6

## Context

This computational result confirms experimental evidence from Borderie et al. (2005) who observed a fossil spinodal decomposition signature in 129Xe+natSn collisions at 32 AMeV, and theoretical work by Guarnera et al. showing that spinodal decomposition produces a primitive breakup with a privileged fragment size related to the most unstable nuclear matter wavelengths. The study extends prior work by Barrañón et al. using the LATINO model that found an inverse relation between entropy and residual size, and supports the interpretation of liquid-gas coexistence and first-order phase transitions in heavy ion collisions previously identified through Campi scatter plots and fragment size distribution power laws.
