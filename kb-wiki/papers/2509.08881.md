# Constraining the Resolution Length of Quark-Gluon Plasma with New Jet Substructure Measurements

> We show that recent measurements of substructure-dependent jet suppression constrain the value of the resolution length of the droplets of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in heavy ion collisions. This resolution length, $L_{\rm res}$, is defined such that the medium can only resolve partons within a jet shower that are separated by more than $L_{\rm res}$. We first use Hybrid Model calculations to reproduce ALICE measurements of the scaled Soft Drop angle $θ_g$ for anti-$k_t$ $R = 0.2$ jets reconstructed from charged-particle tracks. We find that the narrowing of the $θ_g$-distribution in PbPb collisions compared to pp collisions that is seen in the ALICE data rules out a picture of fully coherent energy loss ($L_{\rm res} = \infty$) where each entire parton shower loses energy to the plasma as if it were a single unresolved colored object. We then use Hybrid Model calculations to reproduce ATLAS measurements of $dR_{12}$, the Soft Drop angle obtained by applying the Soft Drop grooming procedure to all charged-particle tracks in $R=1$ jets reconstructed from $R = 0.2$ skinny subjets. Our analysis demonstrates that the ATLAS measurements of $R_{\rm AA}$ for such $R = 1$ jets as a function of $dR_{12}$ are inconsistent with a picture of fully incoherent energy loss ($L_{\rm res} = 0$) in which every splitting in a parton shower is immediately resolved by the plasma. We find that our Hybrid Model calculations agree best with the ATLAS measurements if QGP has a finite, nonzero, resolution length $L_{\rm res}\sim (1-2)/(πT)$. For the first time, jet substructure measurements are constraining the resolution length of QGP from below, as well as from above.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that recent ALICE and ATLAS jet substructure measurements can simultaneously rule out both the fully coherent (L_res = ∞) and fully incoherent (L_res = 0) limits of energy loss, constraining the QGP resolution length to a finite nonzero value L_res ∼ (1-2)/(πT) for the first time from both above and below.

## Method

The authors use the Hybrid Model, which combines perturbative DGLAP parton showers from PYTHIA 8 with a holographic energy loss formula for partons traversing strongly coupled QGP, to compute jet suppression observables. They compare model calculations with four values of L_res (0, 1/(πT), 2/(πT), and ∞) to ALICE measurements of the scaled Soft Drop angle θ_g for anti-k_t R = 0.2 charged-particle jets, and to ATLAS measurements of R_AA for R = 1 jets as a function of dR_12, an observable combining the Hard Group and Soft Drop grooming procedures.

## Key numbers

- L_res ∼ (1-2)/(πT) is the range of resolution lengths that best describes both the ALICE and ATLAS measurements simultaneously
- ALICE measurements use anti-k_t R = 0.2 charged-particle jets with 60 < p_T^ch-jet < 80 GeV and |η^ch-jet| < 0.7, groomed with z_cut = 0.2 and β = 0
- ATLAS measurements use large-radius R = 1.0 jets with 200 < p_T < 251 GeV and |η| < 1.3, constructed from skinny R = 0.2 subjets with p_T > 35 GeV and |η| < 3.0, with Soft Drop parameters z_cut = 0.15 and β = 0
- The Hybrid Model with L_res = 0 uses κ_sc = 0.404, fitted to measurements of jet and high-p_T hadron suppression in Casalderrey-Solana (2018)
- In a weakly coupled plasma with N_c = N_f = 3, λ_D ≃ 1.25/(πT) for α_s = 1/3; in strongly coupled N = 4 SYM theory, λ_D = 0.294/(πT)

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the consequences of varying L_res for jet shape and substructure observables by Hulcher (2017), Casalderrey-Solana (2019), Pablos (2022), and Kudinoor (2025), as well as studies of the related critical coherence angle θ_c by Caucal (2019, 2021), Andres (2022, 2023), Cunqueiro (2023), and Kuzmin (2025). The analysis enables future experimental constraints on the microscopic structure of QGP by demonstrating that jet substructure measurements can now bound the resolution length from below as well as from above, motivating further measurements in the angular range around ΔR_12 ∼ 0.2 where sensitivity to the precise value of L_res is greatest.
