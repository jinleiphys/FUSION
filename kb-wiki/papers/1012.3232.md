# Internal structure of resonant Lambda(1405) state in chiral dynamics

> The internal structure of the resonant Lambda(1405) state is investigated based on meson-baryon coupled-channels chiral dynamics, by evaluating density distributions obtained from the form factors of the Lambda(1405) state. The form factors are defined as an extension of the ordinary stable particles and are directly evaluated from the meson-baryon scattering amplitude, paying attention to the charge conservation of the probe interaction. For the resonant Lambda(1405) state we calculate the density distributions in two ways. One is on the pole position of the Lambda(1405) in the complex energy plane, which evaluates the resonant Lambda(1405) structure without contamination from nonresonant backgrounds, and another on the real energy axis around the Lambda(1405) resonance energy, which may be achieved in experiments. Using sereval probe interactions and channel decomposition, we separate the various contributions to the internal structure of the Lambda(1405). As a result, we find that the resonant Lambda(1405) state is composed of widely spread Kbar around N, which gives dominant component inside the Lambda(1405), with escaping pi Sigma component. Furthermore, we consider Kbar N bound state without decay channels, with which we can observe the internal structure of the bound state within real numbers. We also study the dependence of the form factors on the binding energy and meson mass. This verifies that the form factor defined through the scattering amplitude serves as a natural generalization of the form factor for the resonance state. The relation between the interaction strength and the meson mass shows that the physical kaon mass appears to be within the suitable range to form a molecular bound state with the nucleon through the chiral SU(3) interaction.

## Key claim

The resonant Lambda(1405) state is composed of a widely spread antikaon (Kbar) around a nucleon (N) as the dominant component, with an escaping pi Sigma component, based on form factors and density distributions extracted directly from the meson-baryon scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane, rather than from real-energy measurements that would include nonresonant background contamination.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary model (ChUM) with coupled-channels meson-baryon dynamics, evaluating form factors of the Lambda(1405) resonance by extending the definition used for stable particles. They extracted the form factors from the residue of the double pole in the photon-coupled meson-baryon scattering amplitude at the resonance pole position in the complex energy plane, using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the lowest-order kernel and solving the unitarized scattering amplitude via the N/D method with dimensional regularization. Density distributions were obtained by Fourier transformation of the form factors, and the internal structure was decomposed by channel (Kbar N, pi Sigma) and by probe type (electromagnetic, baryonic, strangeness currents).

## Key numbers

* The Lambda(1405) resonance pole position is located at 1420 MeV (mass) with a width of Gamma_R/2, just below the Kbar N threshold, with the two-pole structure showing one state dominantly coupling to Kbar N at 1420 MeV rather than the nominal 1405 MeV.
* The electromagnetic mean squared radii of the Lambda(1405) were shown to be much larger than those of ground state baryons (Sekihara, 2008).
* The meson decay constant f appears in the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction V_ij = -C_ij/(4f^2) (2 sqrt(s) - M_i - M_j), with the Clebsch-Gordan coefficient C_ij fixed by SU(3) group structure.
* The regularization scale mu_reg and subtraction constant a_i = -(4pi)^2 tilde{a}_i / (2 M_i) are used in the dimensional regularization of the loop function G_i(sqrt(s)).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the Lambda(1405) developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), and Jido (2003), which dynamically generates the resonance without explicit pole terms and reveals a two-pole structure. It builds on the finding by Hyodo (2008) that the Lambda(1405) is predominantly described by meson-baryon dynamics rather than three-quark components, and on the electromagnetic mean squared radii calculation by Sekihara (2008). The results enable future studies of few-body nuclear systems with kaons, such as Kbar NN, Kbar K N, and Kbar Kbar N, and provide input for interpreting the nontrivial energy dependence of Lambda(1405) photoproduction cross sections reported by Niiyama (2008) and for coalescence model estimates in heavy ion collisions.
