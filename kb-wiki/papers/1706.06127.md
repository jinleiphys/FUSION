# Multiple Chirality in Nuclear Rotation: A Microscopic View

> Covariant density functional theory and three-dimensional tilted axis cranking are used to investigate multiple chirality in nuclear rotation for the first time in a fully self-consistent and microscopic way. Two distinct sets of chiral solutions with negative and positive parities, respectively, are found in the nucleus 106Rh. The negative-parity solutions reproduce well the corresponding experimental spectrum as well as the B(M1)/B(E2) ratios of the transition strengths. This indicates that a predicted positive-parity chiral band should also exist. Therefore, it provides a further strong hint that multiple chirality is realized in nuclei.

# Multiple Chirality in Nuclear Rotation: A Microscopic View

## Key claim
Covariant density functional theory combined with three-dimensional tilted axis cranking predicts two distinct sets of chiral solutions (negative and positive parity) in 106Rh, but the positive-parity chiral band has not yet been observed experimentally; the paper provides a "strong hint" rather than a definitive confirmation that multiple chirality is realized in this nucleus.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent three-dimensional tilted axis cranking calculations based on covariant density functional theory with the point-coupling Lagrangian PC-PK1, solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells. For each of the two configurations (πg9/2−1⊗νh11/2^1 and πg9/2−1⊗νh11/2^2(gd)^1), the occupation of valence neutrons in the h11/2 shell was fixed by tracing single-neutron levels with increasing cranking frequency, while other nucleons were treated self-consistently. The orientation of the angular velocity vector was determined by minimizing the total Routhian with respect to the polar and azimuth angles.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters for the negative-parity configuration: (β, γ) = (0.25, 22.8°); for the positive-parity configuration: (0.27, 20.8°)
- Planar-to-chiral transition occurs at ħω ∼ 0.46 MeV for the πg9/2−1⊗νh11/2^1 configuration and at ∼ 0.66 MeV for the πg9/2−1⊗νh11/2^2(gd)^1 configuration
- The kink in rotational frequency vs. spin appears around I = 12 ħ for the negative-parity band
- The observed energy separation between the twin bands is almost constant at ∼ 300 keV
- For the positive-parity band, calculated excitation energies range from 1.69 MeV (I = 14.9 ħ) to 5.45 MeV (I = 22.1 ħ)
- B(M1)/B(E2) ratios for the positive-parity band range from 13.7 μN^2/(e^2 b^2) at ħω = 0.30 MeV to 13.1 μN^2/(e^2 b^2) at ħω = 0.70 MeV
- The barrier of the Routhian at φω = 0 is only several tens of keV for both configurations

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of chiral doublet bands using the particle-rotor model (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997) and previous tilted axis cranking calculations based on Woods-Saxon potentials (Dimitrov et al., 2000) or Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean fields (Olbratowski et al., 2004). The concept of multiple chiral doublet bands (MχD) was introduced by Meng et al. (2006) using constrained relativistic mean-field calculations in Rh isotopes, and experimental evidence for MχD bands has been reported in 133Ce (Ayangeakaa et al., 2013) and 103Rh (Kuti et al., 2014). The present fully self-consistent covariant DFT approach provides a more fundamental test of multiple chirality and enables future systematic investigations of chirality across the Rh isotopic chain, as well as extensions beyond mean field using random phase approximation or collective Hamiltonian methods.
