# Compound and quasi-compound states in low-energy scattering of nucleons from 12C

> A multi-channel algebraic scattering theory has been used to study the properties of nucleon scattering from 12C and of the sub-threshold compound nuclear states, accounting for properties in the compound nuclei to ~10 MeV. All compound and quasi-compound resonances observed in total cross-section data are matched, and on seeking solutions of the method at negative energies, all sub-threshold states in 13C and 13N are predicted with the correct spin-parities and with reasonable values for their energies. A collective-model prescription has been used to define the initiating nucleon-12C interactions and via use of orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials, account is made of the Pauli principle. Information is extracted on the underlying structure of each state in the compound systems by investigating the zero-deformation limit of the results.

## Key claim

The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) theory, using a rotational collective-model prescription for the nucleon-¹²C interaction with orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials to account for the Pauli principle, can simultaneously describe all compound and quasi-compound resonances in low-energy nucleon-¹²C scattering and predict all sub-threshold bound states in ¹³C and ¹³N with correct spin-parities and reasonable energies, though the orthogonalizing pseudo-potential treatment uses a finite strength in place of the infinite value theory presumes, so calculated energies may be affected by about a tenth of an MeV due to small spuriosity.

## Method

The MCAS method solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the nucleon-nucleus system, using a rotational collective-model interaction potential matrix with deformation taken to second order, employing Woods-Saxon form factors with central, spin-orbit (l·s), orbit-orbit (l·l), and spin-spin (s·I) operators that depend on parity. Couplings were included with the ¹²C ground state (0⁺), the first excited 2⁺ state at 4.4389 MeV, and the 0₂⁺ state at 7.6542 MeV, with Pauli blocking handled via orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP). The zero-deformation limit was investigated to identify the underlying single-particle structure of each compound and quasi-compound state by tracking resonance centroids and widths as the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ was reduced from its physical value of -0.52 to zero.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach previously applied to n+¹²C (Amos 2003) to also treat p+¹²C, enabling a comparative analysis of bound and resonance spectra in ¹³C and ¹³N under charge symmetry. The zero-deformation analysis provides a spectroscopic interpretation linking each compound resonance to specific single-particle states (0p₁/₂, 1s₁/₂, 0d₅/₂) coupled to the 2⁺ excited state of ¹²C, clarifying the structure of states previously assumed in the literature (Ajzenberg-Selove 1991). This enables future large-space structure model analyses of the wave functions and extension to higher energies where effects of the 0₂⁺ state and the 3⁻ state at 9.63 MeV would need consideration.
