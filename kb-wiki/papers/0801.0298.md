# Curvature interaction in collective space

> For the Riemannian space, built from the collective coordinates used within nuclear models, an additional interaction with the metric is investigated, using the collective equivalent to Einstein's curvature scalar. The coupling strength is determined using a fit with the AME2003 ground state masses. An extended finite-range droplet model including curvature is introduced, which generates significant improvements for light nuclei and nuclei in the trans-fermium region.

## Key claim

Adding a curvature interaction term (the collective equivalent of Einstein's curvature scalar) to the nuclear collective Hamiltonian improves ground-state mass predictions, but the coupling strength depends on the underlying macroscopic model: it is 7.8 for the finite-range liquid-drop model and 2.5 for the finite-range droplet model, indicating that the droplet model already partially accounts for curvature effects via its empirical exponential term.

## Method

The author extends the collective Schrödinger equation by adding a potential term proportional to the Einstein curvature scalar R of the collective Riemannian space, where the metric is defined by the collective mass parameters. For the symmetric two-center shell model with ellipsoidal deformations, the metric tensor components and curvature scalar are derived analytically using the Werner-Wheeler formalism. The curvature term is then incorporated into two macroscopic mass models (FRLDM and FRDM), and the coupling constant is determined by fitting to the AME2003 experimental ground-state masses while varying a subset of parameters (volume-energy, surface-energy, asymmetry constants, and the new curvature constant).

## Key numbers

* The curvature scalar for a spherical nucleus (Q=1) is R_sphere = -4.3599 / R_0^2, where R_0 = r_0 A^{1/3}
* The curvature energy constant a_R = 529.95850 MeV for the FRLDMC model
* The curvature energy constant a_R = 172.676 MeV for the FRDMC model
* The root-mean-square deviation from AME2003 masses improves from 0.821 MeV (original FRLDM) to 0.764 MeV (FRLDMC)
* The root-mean-square deviation improves from 0.679 MeV (original FRDM) to 0.655 MeV (FRDMC)
* For light nuclei, the curvature term contributes 5.21 MeV to the binding energy of 16O; for heavy nuclei it is 0.07 MeV for 208Pb
* The coupling strength ξ = 7.8 for FRLDMC and ξ = 2.5 for FRDMC (with r_0 = 1.16 fm)

## Context

This work extends the finite-range liquid-drop model (FRLDM) and finite-range droplet model (FRDM) developed by Nix and collaborators by adding a curvature interaction term derived from Riemannian geometry of collective coordinates. The curvature term provides an alternative to the empirical exponential term -C A exp(-γ A^{1/3}) that Nix introduced in the FRDM to correct overestimates of heavy-nucleus masses. The new models (FRLDMC and FRDMC) achieve the best available description of ground-state masses across the full nuclear chart, with particular improvements for light nuclei (Z=8-20) and trans-fermium elements (Z≥100), enabling more precise predictions for super-heavy element properties, cluster radioactivity, and asymmetric fission.
