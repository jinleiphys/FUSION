# A Microscopic T-Violating Optical Potential: Implications for Neutron-Transmission Experiments

> We derive a T-violating P-conserving optical potential for neutron-nucleus scattering, starting from a uniquely determined two-body $ρ$-exchange interaction with the same symmetry. We then obtain limits on the T-violating $ρ$-nucleon coupling $\overline{g}_ρ$ from neutron-transmission experiments in $^{165}$Ho. The limits may soon compete with those from measurements of atomic electric-dipole moments.

## Key claim

The paper derives a microscopic T-violating, P-conserving (TVPC) optical potential for neutron-nucleus scattering from a uniquely determined two-body rho-exchange interaction, and uses it to obtain limits on the TVPC rho-nucleon coupling from neutron-transmission experiments in holmium-165; the limits may soon compete with those from atomic electric-dipole moment measurements, but the authors note that the results are probably correct only to within factors of order unity.

## Method

The authors start from the unique two-body TVPC rho-exchange interaction and evaluate its ground-state expectation value in a nucleus, treating the nucleus as an elementary particle with spin I. They use a zero-range approximation justified by the heavy rho mass, then evaluate the resulting one-body optical potential in a deformed Nilsson model with deformation parameter epsilon = 0.3 for holmium-165. The spin-correlation coefficient A5 is calculated by including the TVPC potential alongside the strong optical potential in a coupled-channels code.

## Key numbers

- The isovector nucleon magnetic moment mu_v = 3.70 nuclear magnetons
- The normal strong rho-nucleon coupling g_rho = 2.79
- The deformation parameter epsilon = 0.3 for the Nilsson model of holmium-165
- The published measurement of A5 at 2 MeV yields an upper limit on the TVPC coupling gbar_rho of about 0.5, which must be increased by a factor of about 3 to account for short-range repulsion
- The resulting limit on alpha_T (ratio of typical T-violating to strong two-body matrix elements) is about 1.5 x 10^-2
- A recently completed experiment improved the bound on A5 by a factor of about 15, potentially yielding bounds on alpha_T of order 10^-4
- The nuclear spin I = 7/2 and its z-projection K = 7/2 for holmium-165

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Hnizdo and Gould (1994), who used a Wood-Saxon shape for the TVPC potential under the assumption it is proportional to nuclear density, and shows that procedure yields limits too small by about two orders of magnitude. The paper connects neutron-transmission experiments to fundamental sources of T-violation through meson-nucleon coupling constants, building on the work of Simonius (1975) who showed that only rho and A1 mesons can contribute at tree level. The results enable neutron-transmission experiments to potentially compete with atomic electric-dipole moment measurements from Haxton, Horing, and Musolf (to appear in Phys. Rev. D), though the authors note that the connection between experimental observables and T-odd forces in compound-nucleus resonances remains unclear.
