# Transition form factors $γ^*\to γf_2(1270)$ and $γ^*\to γa_2(1320)$ in the $e^+e^-$ collisions

> The predictions for transition form factors $γ^*\to γf_2(1270)$ and $γ^*\to γa_2(1320)$ and corresponding $e^+e^-\to γ^*\to f_2γ$ and $e^+e^-\to γ^*\to a_2γ$ cross sections are obtained for the energy region up to $2$ GeV. These predictions are coordinated with the recent Belle data on the $γ^*(Q^2)γ\to f_2$ transition. It is shown that the QCD asymptotics of the amplitudes of the reactions $e^+e^-\to γ^*\to f_2γ$ and $e^+e^-\to γ^*\to a_2γ$ can be reached only by taking into account a compensation of contributions of $ρ(770)$, $ω(782)$ with contributions of their radial excitations. The relation $σ(e^+e^-\to γ^*\to f_2γ)/σ(e^+e^-\to γ^*\to a_2γ)\approx 25/9$, obtained with the help of the $q\bar q$ model of $a_2$ and $f_2$ and QCD, is used to determine the $σ(e^+e^-\to γ^*\to a_2γ)$ at high energies. Recent BABAR measurement of the $e^+e^-\to f_2γ\toπ^+π^-γ$ cross section at $10.58$ GeV gives hope for detailed investigation of the $γ^*(s)\to f_2γ$ and $γ^*(s)\to a_2γ$ transition form factors at high energy region.

## Key claim

The QCD asymptotics of the amplitudes for \(e^+e^-\to \gamma^*\to f_2\gamma\) and \(e^+e^-\to \gamma^*\to a_2\gamma\) can be reached only when the contributions of the ground-state vector mesons \(\rho(770)\) and \(\omega(782)\) are compensated by those of their radial excitations (\(\rho',\rho''\) and \(\omega',\omega''\)); the authors stress that their numerical predictions should be treated as a guide, not precise predictions.

## Method

The authors use a Generalized Vector Dominance Model (GVDM) to compute the transition form factors \(\gamma^*\to \gamma f_2(1270)\) and \(\gamma^*\to \gamma a_2(1320)\) and the corresponding \(e^+e^-\) cross sections up to 2 GeV. They fit the parameters \(a_{f_2}\) and \(b_{f_2}\) (ratios of coupling constants) to Belle data on the space-like \(\gamma^*(Q^2)\gamma\to f_2\) transition form factor, imposing the QCD asymptotic condition that the sum of vector-meson contributions vanishes at high energy. The same parameters are then used for the \(a_2\) case via naive quark-model relations, and the cross sections are evaluated with constant-width propagators for \(\rho,\rho',\rho''\) and \(\omega,\omega',\omega''\).

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma_{f_2\to\gamma\gamma} = 3.03 \pm 0.35\ \text{keV}\) (from PDG 2014).
- \(\Gamma_{a_2\to\gamma\gamma} = 1.00 \pm 0.06\ \text{keV}\) (from PDG 2014).
- For the \(f_2\) case, three parameter sets are considered: Case 1: \((a_{f_2},b_{f_2}) = (-0.393, 0.082)\) with \(\chi^2/\text{n.d.f.}=29.3/28\); Case 2: \((-1.3, 0.74)\) with \(\chi^2/\text{n.d.f.}=49.1/28\); Case 3: \((-0.28, 0)\) with \(\chi^2/\text{n.d.f.}=39.1/29\).
- In Case 1 the peak \(e^+e^-\to f_2\gamma\) cross section is \(\sim 20\ \text{pb}\); in Case 2 it is two orders of magnitude higher (peak \(\sim 3\ \text{nb}\), reducing to \(0.8\ \text{nb}\) if \(\Gamma_{\rho''}\) were 500 MeV).
- The BABAR measurement at 10.58 GeV gives \(\sigma(e^+e^-\to f_2\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma) = 37^{+24}_{-18}\ \text{fb}\); the authors' predictions for this quantity are 1.0 fb (Case 1) and 3.8 fb (Case 2).
- Using the QCD relation \(\sigma(e^+e^-\to f_2\gamma)/\sigma(e^+e^-\to a_2\gamma) \approx 25/9\), the BABAR data imply \(\sigma(e^+e^-\to a_2\gamma,\ 10.58\ \text{GeV}) = 24^{+15}_{-11}\ \text{fb}\).
- Vector meson masses and widths used: \(m_\rho=775\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_{\rho'}=1465\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_{\rho''}=1720\ \text{MeV}\); \(\Gamma_\rho=149\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{\rho'}=400\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{\rho''}=250\ \text{MeV}\); \(\Gamma_\omega=8.5\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{\omega'}=215\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{\omega''}=315\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Achasov and Kiselev (2015) by including the \(\rho''\) and \(\omega''\) contributions in the \(\gamma^*\) leg, which were omitted in their earlier fit to Belle data on \(\gamma^*(Q^2)\gamma\to f_2\) (Achasov and Kiselev 2015). It also improves upon the earlier cross-section estimates of Achasov, Gubin, and Kiselev (2013), which assumed equal couplings of \(f_2\) and \(a_2\) to all vector-meson pairs. The predictions enable experimental searches for \(e^+e^-\to f_2\gamma\) and \(e^+e^-\to a_2\gamma\) at modern colliders such as VEPP-2000, and the BABAR measurement at 10.58 GeV provides a first high-energy constraint on the time-like transition form factors.
