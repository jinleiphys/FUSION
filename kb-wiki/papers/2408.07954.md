# A Complex Scaling Method for Efficient and Accurate Scattering Emulation in Nuclear Reactions

> We present a novel scattering emulator utilizing the complex scaling method to enhance nuclear reaction analysis. This approach leverages a single set of reduced bases, allowing for efficient and simultaneous emulation across multiple channels and potential parameters, significantly reducing computational storage and accelerating calculations. Demonstrated through \(n\)+\(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{11}\)Be+\(^{64}\)Zn elastic scattering, our method achieves high accuracy and efficiency. This emulator exhibits stable and reliable performance without anomalies inherent in other techniques, showcasing its robustness.

# A Complex Scaling Method for Efficient and Accurate Scattering Emulation in Nuclear Reactions

## Key claim
The complex scaling method enables a scattering emulator that uses a single set of reduced bases to simultaneously emulate multiple angular momentum channels and potential parameters, avoiding the Kohn anomalies that plague variational-principle-based emulators. The method achieves high accuracy across the entire parameter space, not just at the best-fit parameter values, with relative errors well below typical experimental uncertainty.

## Method
The authors apply the complex scaling method to transform the oscillatory boundary conditions of scattering wave functions into exponentially decaying ones, then expand the complex-scaled scattered wave function in x-regularized Lagrange-Laguerre basis functions. They perform exact calculations for N_s training parameter sets (generated via Latin hypercube sampling within ±20% of best-fit values), apply principal component analysis to extract reduced bases, and project the linear equations onto these reduced bases for emulation. The same reduced basis set serves all partial-wave channels because only the centrifugal barrier term differs among channels.

## Key numbers
- For n+⁴⁰Ca elastic scattering at E_cm = 20 MeV: with 14 reduced basis states (from 60 Lagrange-Laguerre basis functions), the emulator reproduces the exact cross section; error decreases by approximately an order of magnitude with each two-dimensional increment in reduced basis size (10, 12, 14 dimensions tested).
- For ¹¹Be+⁶⁴Zn elastic scattering at E_cm = 24.5 MeV: with 30 reduced basis states (from 100 Lagrange-Laguerre basis functions), the emulator reproduces the exact cross section; with 20 reduced basis states, relative errors remain around 10⁻³ at scattering angles up to approximately 70°, well below the typical experimental uncertainty of 5%.
- For the d₅/₂ phase shift of n+¹⁰Be scattering across 0.02–10 MeV: with 8 reduced basis states (from 15 training parameter sets), the mean absolute relative error stabilizes around 10⁻⁷; no anomaly is observed at 8.4 MeV where the Kohn variational principle method exhibits an anomaly.
- The emulator reduces time complexity from O(N_r³) to O(n³) for solving the linear equation, where N_r is the number of Lagrange-Laguerre basis functions and n is the number of reduced basis states.

## Context
This work extends the eigenvector continuation method (originally introduced for bound-state eigenvalue problems) to scattering problems, building on prior emulators based on the Kohn variational principle (Furnstahl, 2020; Drischler, 2021) and R-matrix theory. The complex scaling approach directly addresses the "Kohn anomaly" issue, numerical instabilities at certain energies reported in Kohn variational principle emulators (Drischler, 2021; Zhang, 1988). The method enables Bayesian uncertainty quantification for nuclear reactions by providing fast, stable emulation across the full parameter space, and the authors have developed the COLOSS computer code for two-body scattering using complex scaling.
