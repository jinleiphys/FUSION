# Cronin Effect and High Transver Momentum Suppression in D+Au Collisions

> Great interest has attached to recent D+Au, s^(1/2) = 200 A GeV data at RHIC, obtained with the BRAHMS detector. Between pseudorapidity eta=0 and eta=3.2 the appropriately defined ratio R[DAu/PP], comparing transverse momentum spectra of D+Au to P+P exhibits a steady decrease with eta. This diminuition is examined within a two-stage simulation, the last stage being a purely hadronic, reduced energy cascade. The result is an adequate description of the data including the so-called Cronin effect. Additionally there is clear evidence for suppression, in the second stage, of relatively high transverse momentum, eta=0, leading mesons, i.e. the Cronin effect, only near mid-rapidity, is appreciably muted by final state interactions.

# Cronin Effect and High Transverse Momentum Suppression in D+Au Collisions

## Key claim
The observed decrease of the ratio R[DAu/PP] with increasing pseudorapidity in D+Au collisions at RHIC can be adequately described by a two-stage hadronic simulation without invoking partonic degrees of freedom, but the authors concede that a gluon saturation or color-glass-condensate interpretation may be a more fundamental explanation. The Cronin enhancement at mid-rapidity is appreciably muted by final-state interactions in the hadronic cascade, not by initial-state partonic effects.

## Method
The authors used the LUCIFER Monte Carlo code, which operates in two stages: Stage I treats initial nucleon-nucleon interactions in parallel (not as a cascade) using a model fitted to elastic, single diffractive, and non-single diffractive pp data, with no energy loss or transverse momentum creation permitted; Stage II is a purely hadronic cascade of prehadrons (colorless objects with hadron-sized cross-sections) that interact and decay at lower energies. The pp transverse momentum spectrum was used as input to the D+Au simulation, and the ratio R[DAu/PP] was computed using N_coll = 7.0 for minimum-bias events.

## Key numbers
- The D+Au collisions were studied at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV, using data from the BRAHMS detector at RHIC.
- The ratio R[DAu/PP] was examined between pseudorapidities eta = 0 and eta = 3.2, where it exhibits a steady decrease with eta.
- The authors used N_coll = 7.0 for minimum-bias D+Au (impact parameter b <= 16 fm), compared to the BRAHMS value of approximately 7.2.
- More than 90% of the charged rapidity densities result from transverse momentum p_perp <= 0.7 GeV/c.
- The non-strange mesonic prehadrons were assigned a central mass m_0 ~ 500 MeV with width w = m_0/4; strange prehadrons used m_0 ~ 650 MeV.
- Prebaryons were restricted to a mass spectrum from m_N to 2 GeV.
- The prehadron production time was set to the target-projectile crossing time T_AB ~ R_AB/gamma.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LUCIFER simulations applied to SPS energies (sqrt(s) = 17.2 and 20 A GeV) and RHIC energies (sqrt(s) = 56, 130, and 200 A GeV) for Au+Au collisions. The approach is closest in spirit to the RQMD model and parallel work by Gallmeister, Greiner, and Xu, and by Cassing. The paper directly addresses the BRAHMS data and the observed Cronin effect, and contrasts its hadronic explanation with the gluon saturation and color-glass-condensate interpretation advocated by others. The results suggest that final-state interactions in a hadronic cascade can produce jet suppression, with implications for understanding the larger suppression seen in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
