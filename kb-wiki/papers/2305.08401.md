# Resolving phase transition properties of dense matter through tidal-excited g-mode from inspiralling neutron stars

> The investigation of the phase state of dense matter is hindered by complications of first-principle nonperturbative quantum chromodynamics. By performing the first consistent general-relativistic calculations of tidal-excited g-mode of neutron stars with a first-order strong interaction phase transition in the high-density core, we demonstrate that gravitational wave signal during binary neutron star inspiral probes their innermost hadron-quark transition and provides potent constraints from present and future gravitational-wave detectors.

## Key claim

By performing the first consistent general-relativistic calculations of tidal-excited discontinuity g-modes in neutron stars with a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, the authors demonstrate that gravitational wave signals during binary neutron star inspiral can probe the innermost phase transition properties, but only under the assumption of a slow phase transition where the hadron-quark conversion timescale is much longer than the typical oscillation timescale.

## Method

The authors constructed background spherical hybrid stars in hydrostatic equilibrium by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using a constant-speed-of-sound parameterization for the first-order hadron-quark phase transition, with hadronic matter described by the QMF and NL3ωρ models. They then computed the l=2 discontinuity g-modes by solving the linear pulsation equations in full general relativity, using a relativistic definition of the tidal overlap integral that avoids f-mode contamination present in previous hybrid approaches. The resulting mode frequencies and overlap integrals were used to estimate the phase shift in the gravitational waveform during binary neutron star inspiral, and a Bayesian parameter estimation search for mode resonance signals was performed on GW170817 data.

## Key numbers

- g-mode frequencies fall within the range of 100 Hz to 1500 Hz, strongly dependent on the transition strength which varies between approximately 5 MeV/fm³ and 300 MeV/fm³
- For a 1.4 M⊙–1.4 M⊙ system, the threshold frequency for detectability by 3rd generation detectors is f_th ≃ 1200 Hz; for a 1.8 M⊙–1.8 M⊙ system, f_th ≃ 1050 Hz
- The Bayes factor from the GW170817 search was B¹₀ = 0.72, indicating no strong preference between the mode resonance hypothesis and the null hypothesis
- For the QMF+CSS model with p_t/ε_t = 0.11, models with resonant frequency ≤ 485 Hz are ruled out at 95% confidence level
- The difference in mode frequency between c_s = 1/√3 (conformal limit) and c_s = 1 (causal limit) is less than ~10%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of interfacial modes at the crust-core interface (Tsang et al. 2012, Pani et al. 2020, Zhao et al. 2023) and earlier Newtonian treatments of discontinuity g-modes in hybrid stars (Finn 1987). The analysis provides a complementary probe to high-frequency post-merger gravitational wave searches for first-order phase transitions (Bauswein et al. 2019) and to static tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 (Miao et al. 2020). The results enable future gravitational wave detectors to constrain the hadron-quark phase transition parameter space, and the Bayesian framework developed can be applied to discriminate between different physical origins of mode resonance signatures, such as crust-core i-modes or composition g-modes.
