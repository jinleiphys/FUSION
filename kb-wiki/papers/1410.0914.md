# The structure of rotational bands in alpha-cluster nuclei

> In this contribution, I discuss an algebraic treatment of alpha-cluster nuclei based on the introduction of a spectrum generating algebra for the relative motion of the alpha-clusters. Particular attention is paid to the discrete symmetry of the geometric arrangement of the alpha-particles, and the consequences for the structure of the rotational bands in the 12C and 16O nuclei.

# The structure of rotational bands in alpha-cluster nuclei

## Key claim
The Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) predicts that the rotational band structure of alpha-cluster nuclei like 12C and 16O is determined by the discrete symmetry of the geometric arrangement of alpha-particles, specifically, an equilateral triangle (D3h symmetry) for 12C and a regular tetrahedron (Td symmetry) for 16O, and that the existence of parity doublets and specific rotational sequences serves as a fingerprint of that symmetry. The abstract glosses over the fact that the Hoyle band's geometric structure remains an open question, with the ACM predicting an equilateral triangular configuration that can be distinguished from a bent-arm configuration only by identifying the negative parity 3- and 4- states of the Hoyle band.

## Method
The ACM introduces a spectrum generating algebra U(3n-2) for n identical alpha-clusters, using bosonic quantization with dipole bosons for each relative Jacobi coordinate and an auxiliary scalar boson under conservation of total boson number N. For 12C (three clusters) the U(7) model is applied in the oblate top limit, and for 16O (four clusters) the U(10) model is applied in the spherical top limit. Energy eigenvalues are computed including anharmonicities and rotation-vibration couplings, with wave functions characterized by total boson number N, angular momentum and parity LP, and permutation symmetry t, where complete symmetry is required for identical alpha-particles.

## Key numbers
- For 12C ground state band: allowed angular momenta and parity are L^P = 0+, 2+, 4+, ... with K=0 and L^P = 3-, 4-, 5-, ... with K=3; the three-fold symmetry excludes states with K=1 and K=2
- For 12C: a nearly degenerate parity doublet with L^P = 4± is observed in the ground state band
- For 12C Hoyle band: a 3- state was suggested to lie between 11 and 14 MeV, close to the predicted energy from the ACM
- For 16O ground state band and breathing vibration: allowed states have L^P = 0+, 3-, 4+, 6±, ...
- For 16O vibrational excitations with E symmetry: L^P = 2±, 4±, 5±, ...
- For 16O vibrational excitations with F symmetry: L^P = 1-, 2+, 3±, 4±, ...

## Context
This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model previously developed by Bijker, Iachello, and collaborators (2010-2014) for 12C and 16O, building on earlier alpha-cluster models by Wheeler, Brink, and Robson. The ACM provides a framework to distinguish between competing theoretical approaches such as antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD), lattice EFT, and the no-core symplectic model. The key open question is the geometric structure of the Hoyle band in 12C, where the ACM predicts an equilateral triangular configuration that can be tested experimentally by identifying the predicted negative parity 3- and 4- Hoyle states, potentially via 180-degree electron scattering off 12C as suggested by experiments at Darmstadt.
