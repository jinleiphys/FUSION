# Transverse-momentum fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics

> We analyze event-by-event fluctuations of the transverse momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC in the framework based on the fluctuating Glauber-model initial conditions, event-by-event (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics, and statistical hadronization. We use the scaled fluctuation measure < Delta p_Ti Delta p_Tj > / << p_T >>. The identified "geometric" mechanism of generating the transverse-momentum fluctuations from the initial size fluctuations, transmitted to the final statistical-hadronization phase with hydrodynamics, is capable of easily reproducing the magnitude of the effect and explains the basic features of the data. On the other hand, it is somewhat too strong, hinting on modification of the popular Glauber approach to the earliest phase of the collision. We have checked that the considered measure is insensitive of the values of the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients, the freeze-out temperature, and the smoothing parameter for the initial distribution. It remains unaltered in the core-corona picture and is insensitive to the transverse-momentum conservation, approximately imposed in the statistical hadronization.

## Key claim

The geometric mechanism, where event-by-event fluctuations in the initial fireball size lead to fluctuations in transverse flow and thus in the average transverse momentum of produced hadrons, reproduces the magnitude and centrality dependence of the STAR and PHENIX data, but overshoots the measured fluctuation measure by about 50% at low centralities, suggesting that the popular Glauber approach to the earliest phase of the collision may need modification.

## Method

The authors generated fluctuating initial conditions using the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo code with a mixed wounded-nucleon and binary-collision model (parameter alpha = 0.125) at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, then evolved each event with (3+1)-dimensional second-order viscous hydrodynamics (shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08, bulk viscosity zeta/s = 0.04, crossover equation of state), and finally simulated statistical hadronization at freeze-out using THERMINATOR with viscous corrections. The scaled fluctuation measure <Delta p_Ti Delta p_Tj> / <<p_T>> was computed for the STAR and PHENIX experimental cuts and compared to published data.

## Key numbers

- The scaled fluctuation measure <Delta p_Ti Delta p_Tj>^{1/2}/<<p_T>> from the model overshoots STAR data by about 50% at low centralities (e.g., most central bins).
- The relative slope d<<p_T>>/d<r> * <r>/<<p_T>> is approximately 0.31, independent of viscosity, freeze-out temperature, or smearing width.
- The scaled standard deviation of the initial size parameter <r> at fixed N_w = 100 is shown in Fig. 2 (numerical values not given in text, but the curve decreases with N_w).
- The default freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV reproduces the transverse momenta of identified pions, kaons, and protons from PHENIX; T_f = 140 MeV gives results within systematic errors.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is sigma_NN^{inel} = 42 mb.
- The mixed-model parameter alpha = 0.125 for the initial entropy density at top RHIC energy.
- The smearing parameter w = 0.4 fm for the Gaussian profile of sources.

## Context

This work extends the geometric mechanism for transverse-momentum fluctuations proposed by Broniowski (2009) from perfect (2+1)-D hydrodynamics with averaged initial conditions to full event-by-event (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics with fluctuating Glauber initial conditions. The results confirm the earlier finding that the mechanism overshoots the STAR and PHENIX data, and show that the fluctuation measure is insensitive to shear and bulk viscosity, freeze-out temperature, and the initial smearing parameter. This enables future work to focus on modifying the Glauber initial-state model itself, rather than the hydrodynamic or hadronization stages, to bring the predictions into agreement with experiment.
