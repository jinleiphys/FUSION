# Analysis of factorization in (e,e'p) reactions: A survey of the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation

> The issue of factorization within the context of coincidence quasi-elastic electron scattering is reviewed. Using a relativistic formalism for the entire reaction mechanism and restricting ourselves to the case of plane waves for the outgoing proton, we discuss the meaning of factorization in the cross section and the role of the small components of the bound nucleon wave function.

## Key claim

Factorization of the (e,e'p) cross section into an elementary electron-proton cross section and a spectral function, which holds in the non-relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), does not generally hold in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) due to contributions from negative-energy projections of the bound nucleon wave function; the extent of this breakdown depends on the choice of the nucleon current operator and on the missing momentum region.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic analysis of exclusive quasielastic electron scattering within the RPWIA, using plane waves for the outgoing proton and relativistic bound nucleon wave functions obtained from the Walecka mean-field model with scalar-vector potentials (TIMORA code). They decomposed the hadronic tensor and differential cross section into contributions from positive-energy projections (uu), negative-energy projections (vv), and crossed terms (uv) of the bound nucleon wave function, and evaluated these components for three kinematic situations on 16O at momentum transfers q = 500 MeV/c and q = 1 GeV/c. The single-nucleon current operators were studied with different prescriptions, including the CC1, CC2, and CC3 forms.

## Key numbers

- For the 1p1/2 shell in 16O at low missing momentum (p ~ 100 MeV/c), the N_uu component is one order of magnitude larger than N_uv and more than two orders of magnitude larger than N_vv.
- For missing momentum p > 300 MeV/c, the N_uv and N_vv components become similar to or larger than N_uu.
- The non-relativistic normalization factor K for the bound nucleon wave function is 1.025, indicating that the lower component contributes less than 3% to the normalization.
- Three kinematic situations were studied: (I) q = 500 MeV/c, omega = 131.56 MeV (quasielastic peak); (II) q = 1 GeV/c, omega = 432.8 MeV (quasielastic peak); (III) q = 1 GeV/c, omega = 300 MeV (y-scaling region).
- The variation of the outgoing proton momentum p_N is approximately 4% for kinematics I and 1.5% for kinematics II and III over the range 0 ≤ p ≤ 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) studies of Udias et al. (1993-1996) and McNeil et al. (1990) to the plane-wave limit, isolating the effects of negative-energy components from distortion effects. It addresses the discrepancy between non-relativistic DWIA analyses, which extracted spectroscopic factors around 0.5 for 3s1/2 and 2d5/2 orbits in 208Pb, and relativistic RDWIA analyses which obtained values around 0.7 consistent with theoretical predictions from short-range correlation studies by Pandharipande et al. (1984). The analysis enables systematic comparison of different current operator prescriptions (CC1, CC2, CC3) and clarifies under what conditions the factorized PWIA form remains approximately valid in relativistic treatments.
