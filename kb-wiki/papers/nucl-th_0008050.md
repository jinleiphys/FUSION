# Effective hadronic Lagrangian for charm mesons

> An effective hadronic Lagrangian including the charm mesons is introduced to study their interactions in hadronic matter. Using coupling constants that are determined either empirically or by the SU(4) symmetry, we have evaluated the absorption cross sections of $J/ψ$ and the scattering cross sections of $D$ and $D^*$ by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an effective hadronic Lagrangian for charm mesons and find that, after including form factors, the $J/\psi$ absorption cross sections by pions and rho mesons are roughly 7 mb and 3 mb respectively, which are much larger than predictions from earlier meson-exchange models that used only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson couplings.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the SU(4) symmetry limit using minimal substitution, treating vector mesons as gauge particles. Coupling constants are determined empirically from the $D^*$ decay width and from vector meson dominance, or from SU(4) relations for four-point couplings. Monopole form factors with cutoff parameters $\Lambda = 1$ or $2$ GeV are introduced at interaction vertices, and cross sections are computed for $J/\psi$ absorption processes ($\pi\psi \rightarrow D^*\bar D$, $\rho\psi \rightarrow D\bar D$, etc.) and charm meson scattering processes ($\pi D \leftrightarrow \rho D^*$, $\pi D \rightarrow \pi D$, etc.).

## Key numbers

- $g_{\pi DD^*} = 4.4$, determined from the $D^*$ decay width
- $g_{\rho DD} = g_{\rho D^* D^*} = 2.52$, $g_{\psi DD} = g_{\psi D^* D^*} = 7.64$, determined from vector meson dominance
- $\sigma_{\pi \psi} \approx 7$ mb and $\sigma_{\rho \psi} \approx 3$ mb for $J/\psi$ absorption cross sections (with form factors)
- Thermal-averaged $J/\psi$ absorption cross sections at $T = 150$ MeV: $\langle \sigma_{\pi \psi} v \rangle \approx 1$ mb, $\langle \sigma_{\rho \psi} v \rangle \approx 2$ mb
- Charm meson scattering cross sections for $\pi D$, $\pi D^*$, $\rho D$, and $\rho D^*$ are all roughly between 10 and 20 mb

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model approach of Haglin (2000) and earlier studies using only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson couplings by Matinyan and Muller (1998), treating vector-meson couplings and four-point contact terms differently. The results give much larger $J/\psi$ absorption cross sections than the perturbative QCD approach (which predicts about 0.1 mb) and the quark-exchange model of Wong (2000) (which gives about 1 mb), but are comparable to values used in phenomenological studies of $J/\psi$ suppression by comoving hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The calculated charm meson scattering cross sections enable improved modeling of charm meson spectra modification in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and beyond.
