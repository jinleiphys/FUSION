# Possible $Œ£_c^* \barŒ£$ molecular states

> We investigate the possibility of deuteron-like $Œ£_c^*\barŒ£$ bound states within the one-boson-exchange model and systematically analyze the effects of the contact-range $Œ¥^{3}(\vec{r}\,)$ potential, the tensor term from the vector-meson exchange, and nonlocal potentials due to the dependence on the sum of the initial and final state center-of-mass momenta. ## Key claim

The paper investigates whether deuteron-like $\Sigma_c^*\bar{\Sigma}$ bound states can exist, finding that such states are most likely to form with masses around 3.7 GeV in the $I(J^P)=0(2^-)$ and $1(2^-)$ channels, but the authors clarify that this work is not aiming at precisely predicting masses, but rather at exploring the potential existence of such hadronic molecules and formalizing the calculation process of the one-boson-exchange model after considering various factors.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to calculate the effective potential between $\Sigma_c^*$ and $\bar{\Sigma}$, including exchanges of $\sigma$, $\pi$, $\eta$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons with monopole form factors. They systematically analyzed the effects of the contact-range $\delta^{3}(\vec{r}\,)$ potential, the tensor term from vector-meson exchange, and nonlocal potentials arising from dependence on the sum of initial and final state center-of-mass momenta. The Schr√É¬∂dinger equation was solved with the resulting coordinate-space potentials to search for bound state solutions for different quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The $\Sigma_c^*\bar{\Sigma}$ system is most likely to form bound states with mass around 3.7 GeV in the $I(J^P)=0(2^-)$ and $1(2^-)$ channels.
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ was varied from 0.8 GeV to 1.1 GeV to study the dependence of binding energy on the cutoff.
- The coupling constants used include $g_{\Sigma\Sigma\pi}=0.79$, $g_{\Sigma\Sigma v}=7.48$, $g_{B_6^*B_6^* p}=59.50$, and $f_{B_6^*B_6^* v}=95.80$.
- The $\sigma$ meson mass was taken as $m \simeq 519$ MeV, corresponding to the coupling constant $g_{\Sigma\Sigma\sigma}=3.50$.
- The spin factor $\Delta_{S_AS_B}$ equals $5/3$ for total spin $S=1$ and $-1$ for $S=2$.
- The constituent quark mass was taken as $m_q\approx M_N/3\approx 313$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the OBE model approach previously applied to systems such as $\Sigma_c^*\bar{\Sigma}_c^*$, $\Xi_c^*\bar{\Xi}_c^*$, $\Sigma_c\bar{\Sigma}_c$, and $\Xi'_c\bar{\Xi}'_c$ (Yang 2018, Lee 2011) to the $\Sigma_c^*\bar{\Sigma}$ system, which would enrich the excited $D_s$ state spectrum beyond conventional $c\bar s$ mesons. The study addresses contentious issues in the OBE model including the treatment of the $\delta^{3}(\vec{r}\,)$ term, which has been handled differently by various authors (Thomas 2008, Li 2012, Liu 2019), and demonstrates using the quark model that the pion-exchange potential and the tensor term of the $\rho$-exchange potential generally cancel for any $S$-wave baryon-antibaryon system. The results enable future searches for $\Sigma_c^*\bar{\Sigma}$ molecular states in experiments and provide a more formalized framework for OBE calculations in hadronic molecules.
