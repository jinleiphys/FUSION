# Entanglement in the states of the Two-Rotor-Model

> The eigenfunctions of the Two-Rotors Model are superpositions of states corresponding to precessions of the rotors around two orthogonal axes. In Nuclear Physics such an entanglement has not been directly confirmed. We discuss how it might be observed and compare with the application of the TRM to single domain nanoparticles. In the latter case, in addition to the present indirect evidence there is the possibility of direct observation in experiments at temperatures of the order of 1 K.

## Key claim

The eigenfunctions of the Two-Rotors Model (TRM) are necessarily superpositions of states describing precessions of the rotors around two orthogonal axes (the ζ-axis and η-axis of the intrinsic frame), but this entanglement has not been directly confirmed in nuclear physics; the paper shows that measuring electromagnetic transition probabilities for the J=3 member of the scissors mode rotational band and for higher excited states with intrinsic energy twice that of the scissors mode could provide a definite conclusion about its existence.

## Method

The TRM Hamiltonian was solved by separating the intrinsic coordinate θ into two regions (I and II) corresponding to the two minima of the double-well potential, applying a harmonic approximation, and imposing invariance of the wave functions under separate inversions of the rotor axes. The eigenfunctions were constructed as superpositions of states from both regions, with the constraint determining the mixing amplitudes for each total angular momentum I. Transition amplitudes for electromagnetic multipole operators (M1, E2, M3) were then evaluated for the scissors mode rotational band members (J=1,2,3) and for the first overtones (states with intrinsic energy 2ω), and compared to a Reference Model without entanglement.

## Key numbers

- Zero-point oscillation parameter θ₀ ≈ 0.1 for atomic nuclei in the rare earth region, with θ₀² ∼ 0.01
- Scissors mode excitation energy ω = √(C/ℐ), with intrinsic energy eigenvalues ε_{nK} = ω(2n + K + 1)
- Energy splitting between doublet states δE ≈ E exp(−2/θ₀²), negligible for nuclei (θ₀² ∼ 0.01) but different for nanoparticles
- For the J=3 member: E2 transition amplitude to J=2 state is reduced by factor 8 in TRM versus Reference Model; E2 amplitude to J=1 state is reduced by factor 9.7; M3 amplitude to ground state is reduced by factor 8/3
- For the first overtone Ψ_{2M2,0}: B(E2)↑_{overtone} = (1/(32 θ₀²)) × (4 r_{II}²/(r_I + r_{II})²) × B(E2)↑_{scissors}; B(M1; overtone → scissors) = (1/7) × B(M1)↑_{scissors}
- For nanoparticles: |ℐ₁ − ℐ₂|/(4ℐ₁ℐ₂) ∼ 1 and θ₀ ≤ 1, with proposed direct observation possible at temperatures of order 1 K

## Context

This work extends the Two-Rotors Model originally applied to deformed atomic nuclei by LoIudice and Palumbo (1978) and confirmed experimentally in ¹⁵⁶Gd by Bohle et al. (1984), and later in all deformed nuclei. The paper corrects an earlier assumption by Palumbo (2014) that the overtone solution was unique, showing instead a more general solution. The results are relevant to single domain magnetic nanoparticles studied by Hata et al. (2014), where the TRM has shown good agreement with experimental magnetic susceptibility data. The paper enables experimental tests of entanglement in nuclear scissors modes through measurement of the J=3 band member and first overtones, and suggests direct observation in free nanoparticles at cryogenic temperatures.
