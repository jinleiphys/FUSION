# Analysis of the consistency of kaon photoproduction data with Lambda in the final state

> The recent CLAS 2005, SAPHIR 2003, LEPS, and the old, pre-1972, data on K^+ Lambda photoproduction are compared with theoretical calculations in the energy region of E_gamma^lab < 2.6 GeV in order to learn about their mutual consistency. The isobaric models Kaon-Maid and Saclay-Lyon, along with new fits to the CLAS data are utilized in this analysis. The SAPHIR 2003 data are shown to be coherently shifted down with respect to the CLAS, LEPS, and pre-1972 data, especially at forward kaon angles. The CLAS, LEPS, and pre-1972 data in the forward hemisphere can be described satisfactorily by using the isobaric model without hadronic form factors. The inclusion of the hadronic form factors yields a strong suppression of the cross sections at small kaon angles and c.m. energies larger than 1.9 GeV, which is not observed in the existing experimental data. We demonstrate that the discrepancy between the CLAS and SAPHIR data has a significant impact on the predicted values of the mass and width of the ``missing-resonance'' D_{13}(1895) in the Kaon-Maid model.

# Analysis of the consistency of kaon photoproduction data with Lambda in the final state

## Key claim

The SAPHIR 2003 data on K⁺Λ photoproduction are systematically shifted downward relative to the CLAS 2005, LEPS, and pre-1972 data, especially at forward kaon angles, requiring a global scaling factor of about 15% to remove the discrepancy; this inconsistency significantly affects the extracted mass and width of the "missing" D₁₃(1895) resonance in the Kaon-Maid model, with mass values differing by 11 MeV and widths by 337 MeV depending on which data set is used.

## Method

The authors compared four experimental data sets (CLAS 2005, SAPHIR 2003, LEPS, and pre-1972 data) on K⁺Λ photoproduction with predictions from the isobaric models Kaon-Maid and Saclay-Lyon, plus two new fits (fit 1 with hadronic form factors and fit 2 without) tuned to the CLAS data. They computed the relative deviation Rᵢ for each data point from the model predictions, using statistical uncertainties, and evaluated the mean deviation ⟨R⟩, χ²/N, and Student's t-variable z₁ to test the null hypothesis that each model describes each data set consistently, separately for the full angular range (sample A: 0°–180°) and forward angles (sample B: 0°–60°).

## Key numbers

- For the Kaon-Maid model with sample A (full angular range), the z₁ statistic for CLAS data is −1.41 (consistent at 5% level), for SAPHIR data is −11.7 (rejected at 1% level), for LEPS data is 0.09 (consistent), and for OLD data is 5.66 (rejected at 1% level).
- For fit 2 (without hadronic form factors) with sample B (forward angles, 0°–60°), the z₁ statistic for CLAS data is 1.25 (consistent at 5% level), for SAPHIR data is −10.7 (rejected at 1% level), for LEPS data is 0.77 (consistent), and for OLD data is 1.42 (consistent).
- The global scaling factor between SAPHIR and CLAS data is 1.13 (13%) for fit 1 with sample A and 1.15 (15%) for fit 2 with sample B.
- The extracted mass of the missing D₁₃ resonance in the Kaon-Maid model is 1.895 ± 0.004 GeV (original SP98 data), 1.938 ± 0.004 GeV (SP03 data), and 1.927 ± 0.003 GeV (CL05 data).
- The extracted width of the missing D₁₃ resonance is 0.372 ± 0.029 GeV (SP98), 0.233 ± 0.008 GeV (SP03), and 0.570 ± 0.019 GeV (CL05).
- The χ²/n.d.f. for fit 1 (with hadronic form factors) is 3.46, and for fit 2 (without hadronic form factors) is 1.80.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of K⁺Λ photoproduction data from the CLAS collaboration (CL05), SAPHIR collaboration (SP03), LEPS collaboration, and older pre-1972 data compiled by Adelseck and Saghai (1990). It directly addresses the discrepancy between CLAS and SAPHIR data first noted in the CLAS publication (2005), and quantifies its impact on the parameters of the "missing" D₁₃(1895) resonance previously identified in the Kaon-Maid model by Bennhold et al. (1999) using the earlier SAPHIR data (SP98). The results are consistent with a parallel multipole analysis by Mart (2006) that also found different resonance parameters when fitting to CLAS versus SAPHIR data. The finding that models with hadronic form factors produce excessive suppression of forward-angle cross sections above W = 1.9 GeV calls into question the form factor prescription used in the Kaon-Maid model and in the earlier fits by Janssen et al. (2001), and motivates further theoretical work on form factor treatment for hypernuclear photoproduction calculations.
