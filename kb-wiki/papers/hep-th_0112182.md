# Inconsistencies of Massive Charged Gravitating Higher Spins

> We examine the causality and degrees of freedom (DoF) problems encountered by charged, gravitating, massive higher spin fields. For spin s=3/2, making the metric dynamical yields improved causality bounds. These involve only the mass, the product eM_P of the charge and Planck mass and the cosmological constant Λ. The bounds are themselves related to a gauge invariance of the timelike component of the field equation at the onset of acausality. While propagation is causal in arbitrary E/M backgrounds, the allowed mass ranges of parameters are of Planck order. Generically, interacting spins s>3/2 are subject to DoF violations as well as to acausality; the former must be overcome before analysis of the latter can even begin. Here we review both difficulties for charged s=2 and show that while a g-factor of 1/2 solves the DoF problem, acausality persists for any g. Separately we establish that no s=2 theory --DoF preserving or otherwise -- can be tree unitary.

## Key claim

For spin 3/2, making the metric dynamical yields improved causality bounds that involve only the mass, the product eM_P of the charge and Planck mass, and the cosmological constant Lambda; however, the allowed mass ranges of parameters are of Planck order, and for spin 2, no theory can be tree unitary regardless of DoF preservation.

## Method

The authors analyze the characteristic surfaces of the Rarita-Schwinger equation for spin 3/2 by studying shock discontinuities across a wavefront, using the method of Madore (1973, 1975). They compute the determinant of the matrix governing the secondary constraint discontinuity in dynamical Maxwell-Einstein backgrounds, imposing Einstein's equations to relate the Einstein tensor to the Maxwell stress tensor. For spin 2, they review the DoF-preserving g=1/2 coupling of Federbush (1961) and compute the characteristic surfaces for acausality, then analyze tree unitarity of Compton scattering amplitudes as a function of the g-factor.

## Key numbers

- For minimally coupled spin 3/2, the causality bound is m^2 > (2/3)(e^2/kappa^2), where kappa is the gravitational coupling (Planck mass related); this corresponds to Planck-scale masses.
- The onset of acausality for spin 3/2 in pure E/M backgrounds occurs at magnetic field strength B = 3m^2/(2e).
- For spin 3/2 with SUGRA-inspired non-minimal coupling, the supersymmetric tuning is m^2 = 2e^2/kappa^2, and causality is maintained for any cosmological constant at that tuning.
- For spin 3/2 with SUGRA-inspired coupling, the causal large mass regime requires m^2 > 2e^2/kappa^2 and m^2 > -Lambda/3, while the causal small mass regime requires m^2 < 2e^2/kappa^2 and m^2 < -Lambda/3.
- For spin 2, the DoF-preserving gyromagnetic ratio is g = 1/2, but acausality persists for any g.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of neutral higher spins in cosmological backgrounds by Deser (2001) and of spin 3/2 in electromagnetic backgrounds by Deser (2000). The spin 3/2 results are related to the structure of N=2 cosmological supergravity (Freedman 1977) and the earlier causality bound of Velo (1969). For spin 2, the DoF problem was first addressed by Federbush (1961) who found the g=1/2 solution, and acausality was previously noted by Kobayashi (1978, 1979). The paper establishes that spin 3/2 is exceptional due to its supergravity heritage, while higher spins face both DoF violations and acausality that cannot be simultaneously cured, and that no spin 2 theory can achieve tree unitarity.
