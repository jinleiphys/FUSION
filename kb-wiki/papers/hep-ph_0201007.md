# Fluid Dynamics of Relativistic Quantum Dust

> The microscopic transport equations for free fields are solved using the Schwinger function. Thus, for general initial conditions, the evolution of the energy-momentum tensor is obtained, incorporating the quantum effects exactly. The result for relativistic fermions differs from classical hydrodynamics, which is illustrated for Landau and Bjorken type initial conditions in this model of exploding primordial matter. Free fermions behave like classical dust concerning hydrodynamic observables. However, quantum effects which are present in the initial state are preserved.

## Key claim

Free fermions behave like classical dust concerning hydrodynamic observables, but quantum effects present in the initial state are preserved and do not dissipate during the evolution.

## Method

The microscopic transport equations for free Dirac fermions are solved exactly using the Schwinger function, which propagates the Wigner function from one time-like hypersurface to another. The energy-momentum tensor is then expressed in terms of the scalar phase space density, and its evolution is computed analytically for general initial conditions, with explicit results for Landau and Bjorken type initial conditions in (1+1) dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For a Landau type initial condition in (1+1) dimensions with massless particles, the energy density satisfies \( T^{00}(x,t) = \frac{1}{2}\left(T^{00}(x-t,t=0) + T^{00}(x+t,t=0)\right) \), showing wavelike propagation.
- For a Bjorken type initial condition, the energy density in rapidity \( y \) and proper time \( \tau \) is given by \( T^{00}(y,\tau) = 2\int\frac{dp}{2\pi}|p|\left( F(-\tau_0 e^{-y/2+\ln \tau/\tau_0};|p|) + F(\tau_0 e^{y/2+\ln \tau/\tau_0};|p|) \right) \), with \( \tau \geq \tau_0 > 0 \).
- In (1+1) dimensions with massless particles, the equation of state is \( \epsilon = T^{00} = T^{11} \equiv P \), i.e., energy density equals pressure.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic behavior in quantum field theory, following earlier results that a free scalar field behaves like a perfect fluid in the semiclassical WKB regime (Domokos and Levai, 2000). It contrasts with classical hydrodynamic models of high-energy collisions, such as those by Landau and Bjorken, by showing that free fermions exhibit free-streaming classical dust behavior rather than ideal fluid flow. The results enable a testing ground for assessing the importance of quantum effects (e.g., finite size shell effects or matter wave terms involving \( LT/\hbar \)) that are not included in semiclassical transport or classical hydrodynamic models.
