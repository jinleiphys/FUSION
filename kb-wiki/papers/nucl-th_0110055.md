# Coherent Compton scattering on light nuclei in the delta resonance region

> Coherent Compton scattering on light nuclei in the delta resonance region is studied in the impulse approximation and is shown to be a sensitive probe of the in-medium properties of the delta resonance. The elementary amplitude on a single nucleon is calculated from the unitary K-matrix approach developed previously. Modifications of the properties of the delta resonance due to the nuclear medium are accounted for through the self-energy operator of the delta, calculated from the one-pion loop. The dominant medium effects such as the Pauli blocking, mean-field modification of the nucleon and delta masses, and particle-hole excitations in the pion propagator are consistently included in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Coherent Compton scattering on light nuclei in the delta resonance region is shown to be a sensitive probe of in-medium modifications of the delta resonance, but the results depend on the specific model used for the nuclear medium (free Fermi gas versus mean-field models with different parameter sets) and on the local density approximation applied to finite nuclei.

## Method

The elementary Compton scattering amplitude on a single nucleon was calculated using a unitary K-matrix approach that includes all dominant mechanisms up to the delta resonance, with parameters fixed from fits to pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, and Compton scattering on the proton. Medium modifications of the delta resonance were accounted for through the self-energy operator of the delta, calculated from the one-pion loop in nuclear matter, consistently including Pauli blocking, mean-field modifications of nucleon and delta masses via the Walecka sigma-omega model, and particle-hole excitations in the pion propagator. The amplitude on finite nuclei was calculated in the impulse approximation using a factorization approximation for Fermi motion and a local density approximation, with a formfactor accounting for finite size effects.

## Key numbers

- Free delta resonance mass: M_delta = 1232 MeV, with vacuum width Gamma_delta_vac = 115 MeV
- Coupling constant f_pi_N_delta = 2.15, obtained from the vacuum width expression
- Normal nuclear matter density rho_0 corresponds to Fermi momentum k_F^0 = 1.333 fm^-1
- Two parameter sets for the Walecka model: Set I (QHD-I) gives C_s^2 = 267.1, C_v^2 = 195.9, effective nucleon mass M_N*/M_N = 0.56 at rho_0; Set II (relativistic Hartree) gives C_s^2 = 228.2, C_v^2 = 147.8, effective nucleon mass M_N*/M_N = 0.73 at rho_0
- Cut-off parameter for the formfactor in dispersion integrals: Lambda_delta = 1.506 GeV
- Energy range for differential cross sections and photon asymmetries on light nuclei (4He and 12C): 150 to 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the delta-hole model approach developed by Koch, Moniz, and Ohtsuka (1983, 1984) and applied to Compton scattering by Krajewksi (1998), Korchin (1994), and Pascalutsa (1996), by using a full unitary K-matrix amplitude for the elementary Compton scattering on a free nucleon rather than a simplified model with only M1 transition and Kroll-Ruderman background. The medium effects are calculated microscopically from the one-pion loop rather than introduced through parameterized self-energies, following the relativistic framework of Herbert (1998), Kim (1996), and Liu (1994). The results enable future comparison with experimental data on coherent Compton scattering from light nuclei to extract in-medium delta properties, and could be extended by including self-consistent treatment of delta-hole states in the pion self-energy.
