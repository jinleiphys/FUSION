# Crossover transition to quark matter in heavy hybrid stars

> We study the possibility that the transition from hadron matter to quark matter at vanishing temperatures proceeds via crossover, similar to the crossover behavior found with lattice QCD studies at high temperatures. The purpose is to examine astrophysical consequences of this postulate by constructing hybrid star sequences fulfilling current experimental data.

# Crossover transition to quark matter in heavy hybrid stars

## Key claim
The transition from hadron matter to quark matter at zero temperature in hybrid neutron stars may proceed via a smooth crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, and this construction alone can produce stable hybrid stars exceeding 2 solar masses even with small vector coupling strengths, without requiring color superconductivity or strong vector repulsion.

## Method
The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining the DD2 nuclear matter EoS with a non-local Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (nl-NJL) quark matter model, then interpolating the pressure as a function of baryon chemical potential using a Gaussian function to mimic a crossover transition. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for non-rotating hybrid star sequences, varying the vector coupling strength parameter η_V = G_V/G_S while keeping the crossover width Γ at the minimal value consistent with causality.

## Key numbers
- Hybrid star sequences reach and exceed 2 solar masses (2 M_⊙) with vector coupling as small as η_V = 0.05, satisfying the mass constraints for both PSR J0348-0432 and PSR J1614-2230 within their 1σ bands.
- Quark matter appears in stars with masses heavier than approximately M ∼ 1 M_⊙ and central densities above n_c ∼ 2 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For vector couplings η_V > 0.05, sequences with masses above 2 M_⊙ do not contain pure quark matter in their cores, only a mixed phase region.
- The vector coupling parameter range studied is η_V < 0.17, limited to small values.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Masuda (2012) who introduced crossover interpolation in the energy-density versus baryon-density plane, correcting an earlier inappropriate construction in the pressure-density plane. It contrasts with the standard Maxwell construction for first-order phase transitions, which for NJL-type models typically requires large vector couplings or color superconducting states (diquark pairing) to reach 2 M_⊙ as shown by Klähn (2013). The results are similar to those obtained by Orsaria (2012) using Gibbs construction with global charge neutrality. This work enables future microscopically based studies of the hadron-to-quark transition via pasta phases or beyond-mean-field approaches incorporating quark substructure and hadron dissociation in dense matter.
