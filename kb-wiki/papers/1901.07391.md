# Resonance states 0+ of the Boron isotope B8 from the Jost-matrix analysis of experimental data

> The available R-matrix parametrization of experimental data on the excitation functions for the elastic and inelastic p-Be7 scattering at the collision energies up to 3.4 MeV is used to generate the corresponding partial-wave cross sections in the states with J^pi=0+. Thus obtained data are considered as experimental partial cross sections and are fitted using the semi-analytic two-channel Jost matrix with proper analytic structure and some adjustable parameters. Then the spectral points are sought as zeros of the Jost matrix determinant (which correspond to the S-matrix poles) at complex energies. The correct analytic structure makes it possible to calculate the fitted Jost matrix on any sheet of the Riemann surface whose topology involves not only the square-root but also the logarithmic branching caused by the Coulomb interaction. In this way, two overlapping 0+ resonances at the excitation energies ~1.79 MeV and ~1.96 MeV have been found.

## Key claim

Using a semi-analytic two-channel Jost matrix with proper analytic structure including both square-root and logarithmic branching from the Coulomb interaction, the authors identify two overlapping \(0^+\) resonances in \(^8\mathrm{B}\) at excitation energies of approximately 1.79 MeV and 1.96 MeV, based on fitting partial cross sections derived from an existing \(R\)-matrix parametrization of experimental \(p+{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) scattering data.

## Method

The authors used the \(R\)-matrix parametrization of Rogachev (2013) for elastic and inelastic \(p+{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) scattering up to 3.4 MeV to generate partial-wave cross sections for the \(J^\pi=0^+\) state, which involves only \((\ell,s)=(1,1)\) in both channels. They fitted these cross sections with a semi-analytic two-channel Jost matrix, where the unknown single-valued matrices \(\bm{A}(E)\) and \(\bm{B}(E)\) were expanded in Taylor series up to order \(M=4\) around a central energy \(E_0\) and the parameters were optimized using the MINUIT program. After fitting on the real energy axis, the Jost matrix determinant was evaluated at complex energies on the appropriate Riemann sheet to locate the \(S\)-matrix poles corresponding to resonances.

## Key numbers

- Two overlapping \(0^+\) resonances in \(^8\mathrm{B}\) were found at excitation energies of approximately **1.79 MeV** and **1.96 MeV**.
- The analysis used collision energies up to **3.4 MeV** for the \(p+{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) system.
- The second channel threshold corresponds to the first excited state of \(^7\mathrm{Be}\) at **0.4291 MeV**.
- The Taylor expansions for \(\bm{A}(E)\) and \(\bm{B}(E)\) were taken to order \(M=4\), with weight factors \(W_{11}=3\), \(W_{12}=15\), \(W_{21}=0.04\), and \(W_{22}=0.002\) in the \(\chi^2\) minimization.
- Fits were performed with five different central energies \(E_0\): 1.6 MeV, 1.7 MeV, 1.8 MeV, 1.9 MeV, and 2.0 MeV (counted from the \(p+{}^7\mathrm{Be}\) threshold).

## Context

This work extends the \(R\)-matrix analysis of Rogachev (2013), who reported three new low-energy resonances in \(^8\mathrm{B}\) with quantum numbers \(0^+\), \(1^+\), and \(2^+\), by applying a Jost-matrix approach that correctly handles the multi-sheeted Riemann surface including Coulomb-induced logarithmic branching. The method enables reliable analytic continuation to complex energies to locate resonance poles, and it can be applied to other multi-channel nuclear reactions to extract resonance parameters with proper treatment of threshold and Coulomb effects.
