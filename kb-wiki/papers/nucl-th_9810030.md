# Final state interaction in near-threshold meson production

> We address the question what information, other than the Final State Interaction parameters, one can extract from data on near-threshold meson production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that near-threshold meson production data can be used to extract the production amplitude (not just the final-state interaction parameters) by applying FSI corrections based on the known proton-proton scattering amplitude, provided the data cover a wide range of excess energy (0 to 100 MeV) to avoid ambiguity.

## Method

The author analyzes the average reaction amplitude |M|, defined as (F sigma / R_3)^(1/2) where F is the flux factor and R_3 is the three-body phase space, for the reactions pp -> pp pi^0 and pp -> pp eta. The amplitude is factorized into a constant production amplitude and the pp scattering amplitude T_pp from the Nijmegen-93 model, averaged over the available phase space. The same procedure is then applied to the pp -> pp eta' reaction, comparing one-pion exchange calculations with and without FSI to the data.

## Key numbers

- The phase space for n-particle production is proportional to epsilon^((3n-5)/2), where epsilon is the excess energy (difference between invariant collision energy and total mass of produced particles).
- At the range 0 <= epsilon <= 100 MeV, the cross section for 3-particle production might increase 4 orders of magnitude due to phase space alone.
- For the pp -> pp eta' reaction, neglecting FSI gives a constant |M| approximately 70 fm.
- The analysis covers excess energies in the range 0 <= epsilon <= 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Watson-Migdal approximation (Migdal) and the Gell-Mann and Watson (GellMann) prediction that near-threshold energy dependence of the pp -> pp pi^0 cross section is reproduced by phase space and FSI. It also builds on the Gottfried and Jackson (Gottfried) introduction of absorptive corrections. The paper enables extraction of the production amplitude from near-threshold data, using the known pp scattering amplitude from partial wave analyses (Nijmegen-93 model) rather than treating FSI as the only measurable quantity.
