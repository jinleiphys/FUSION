# Radiative pT-broadening of high-energy quarks and gluons in QCD matter

> We study radiative $p_{\perp}$-broadening of high energy quarks passing through hot and cold QCD matter. With $L$ the length of the matter and $l_{0}$ the size of constituents of the matter we find $\avg{p_{\perp}^{2}}$ has both double logarithmic terms, $\ln^{2}(L/l_{0})$, and single logarithmic terms, $\ln(L/l_{0})$, coming from gluon radiation induced by the matter. We use a (slight) extension of a formalism developed by B. Zakharov for studying energy loss, a formalism which, for much of our calculation, reduces to a simple dipole scattering analysis. We estimate the radiative contribution to be a sizable correction to the nonradiative value of $\avg{p_{\perp}^{2}}$. We also carry out a resummation of the double logarithmic terms that we find, and we briefly discuss running coupling effects which appear here in a rather unusual way.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to the transverse momentum broadening of high-energy quarks and gluons in QCD matter produce both double logarithmic terms, proportional to \(\ln^{2}(L/l_{0})\), and single logarithmic terms, proportional to \(\ln(L/l_{0})\), where \(L\) is the medium length and \(l_{0}\) is the size of the medium constituents; these corrections are a sizable addition to the nonradiative value of \(\langle p_{\perp}^{2}\rangle\), but the double logarithmic resummation is only useful for very large medium lengths, and for lengths around 5 fm the first leading double logarithmic term dominates.

## Method

The authors extended the dipole-like formalism developed by B. Zakharov for radiative energy loss to compute radiative \(p_{\perp}\)-broadening, using the elastic scattering of a quark-antiquark-gluon system in the medium. They evaluated the Green function for the propagation of this system through the medium in the large \(N_{c}\) and soft gluon limits, solving a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator equation with an imaginary potential. The calculation was performed by dividing the gluon phase space (lifetime \(t\) and energy \(\omega\)) into kinematic regions where single scattering or multiple scattering dominates, and integrating the contributions from the double logarithmic region and from the boundaries separating these regions.

## Key numbers

* The double logarithmic contribution to \(\langle p_{\perp}^{2}\rangle\) is \(\frac{\alpha_{s} N_{c}}{8\pi}\hat{q}L\ln^{2}(L/l_{0})\), where \(\hat{q}\) is the quark transport coefficient, \(L\) is the medium length, and \(l_{0}\) is the size of the medium constituents.
* The full radiative correction to \(\langle p_{\perp}^{2}\rangle\) contains terms of size \((\alpha_{s} N_{c}\hat{q}L/\pi)\ln^{2}(L/l_{0})\) and \((\alpha_{s} N_{c}\hat{q}L/\pi)\ln(L/l_{0})\).
* For medium lengths of about 5 fm, the first leading double logarithmic term gives the dominant contribution, making resummation unnecessary for such lengths.
* The saturation momentum is given by \(Q_{s}^{2} = \frac{4\pi^{2}\alpha_{s} C_{F}}{N_{c}^{2}-1}L\rho xG(x,1/x_{\perp}^{2})\), with \(\rho\) the nucleon density and \(G\) the gluon distribution of the nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Wu (2013) which used the BDMPS formalism but lacked the single scattering term crucial for broadening; the authors show that the Zakharov formalism is better suited because it does not explicitly eliminate that term. The paper builds on the dipole scattering approach of Kovchegov and Mueller (various years) and the MV model of McLerran and Venugopalan, and it enables future calculations of next-to-leading order corrections to jet quenching observables in heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that the radiative corrections are comparable to the nonradiative multiple scattering values, similar to the conclusion of Wu (2013).
