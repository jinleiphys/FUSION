# Initial fluctuation effect on harmonic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of a multi-phase transport model, harmonic flows $v_n$ (n = 2, 3 and 4) are investigated for Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The event-by-event geometry fluctuations significantly contribute to harmonic flows. Triangular flow ($v_3$) originates from initial triangularity ($ε_3$) and is developed by partonic interactions. The conversion efficiency ($v_n/ε_n$) decreases with harmonic order and increases with partonic interaction cross section. A mass ordering in the low $p_T$ region and number of constitute quark scaling in the middle $p_T$ region seem to work roughly for $n$-th harmonic flows at both energies. All features of harmonic flows show similar qualitative behaviors at RHIC and LHC energies, which implies that the formed partonic matters are similar at the two energies.

## Key claim

Event-by-event initial geometry fluctuations significantly contribute to harmonic flows \(v_2, v_3, v_4\), but the AMPT model cannot simultaneously describe \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) data with the same partonic cross section at either RHIC or LHC energies, only reproducing qualitative trends.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which includes HIJING initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for two-body partonic scatterings, and a quark coalescence model for hadronization, while neglecting final hadronic rescattering. They simulated Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, calculating harmonic flows \(v_n\) (n=2,3,4) with respect to event planes defined from initial coordinate-space eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) and comparing to experimental data from PHENIX and ATLAS.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at 200 GeV (0-80% centrality, 3 mb cross section), replacing \(r^2\) with \(r^n\) in the eccentricity definition changes \(v_3\) by less than 3% and \(v_4\) by less than 12%.
- The conversion efficiency \(v_n/\varepsilon_n\) decreases with harmonic order; for 0-20% centrality and \(p_T < 0.55\) GeV/c at 200 GeV, the \(n\)-dependence is fit by an exponential function.
- The spread between different particle species in the NCQ-scaling is less than 10% for \(v_2\), less than 20% for \(v_3\), and reaches 20-30% for \(v_4\) at RHIC energy.
- At LHC energy, the same NCQ-scaling patterns hold with similar spreads, though \(\phi\) meson shows a slightly larger \(v_n/n_q^{n/2}\) than other hadrons.

## Context

This work extends studies of harmonic flow beyond elliptic flow \(v_2\) to triangular \(v_3\) and quadrangular \(v_4\) flows, building on the recognition by Alver and Roland (2010) that initial-state fluctuations generate odd harmonics. It uses the AMPT model framework previously applied to elliptic flow by Lin et al. (2005) and Xu and Ko (2011), and tests the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling relations established for \(v_2\) by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The results enable future constraints on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) and initial conditions by comparing higher-harmonic flow measurements to model predictions.
