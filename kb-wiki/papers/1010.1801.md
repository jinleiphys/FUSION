# Time-odd mean fields in covariant density functional theory: Rotating systems

> Time-odd mean fields (nuclear magnetism) and their impact on physical observables in rotating nuclei are studied in the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT). It is shown that they have profound effect on the dynamic and kinematic moments of inertia. Particle number, configuration and rotational frequency dependences of their impact on the moments of inertia have been analysed in a systematic way. Nuclear magnetism can also considerably modify the band crossing features such as crossing frequencies and the properties of the kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia in the band crossing region. The impact of time-odd mean fields on the moments of inertia in the regions away from band crossing only weakly depends on the relativistic mean field parametrization, reflecting good localization of the properties of time-odd mean fields in CDFT. The moments of inertia of normal-deformed nuclei considerably deviate from the rigid body value. On the contrary, superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei have the moments of inertia which are close to rigid body value. The structure of the currents in rotating frame, their microscopic origin and the relations to the moments of inertia have been systematically analysed. The phenomenon of signature separation in odd-odd nuclei, induced by time-odd mean fields, has been analysed in detail.

## Key claim

Time-odd mean fields (nuclear magnetism) have a profound effect on the dynamic and kinematic moments of inertia in rotating nuclei, but the magnitude of this effect depends strongly on particle number, configuration, rotational frequency, and deformation; the impact on moments of inertia away from band crossing only weakly depends on the relativistic mean field parametrization.

## Method

The authors performed cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) and cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) calculations for rotating nuclei across the nuclear chart, comparing fully self-consistent calculations that include space-like components of vector mesons (nuclear magnetism, NM) with calculations that omit them (WNM). The NL1 parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian was used for most calculations, with deformation parameters selected to match the local minimum under study for normal-deformed, superdeformed, and hyperdeformed states.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear magnetism increases calculated kinematic moments of inertia by 10-30% for normal-deformed nuclei with Z >= 50, with increases around 40% in N=108,110 W isotopes
- In unpaired CRMF calculations for the lowest superdeformed band in 194Pb, NM shifts the proton band crossing frequency by 120 keV, from 0.465 MeV (WNM) down to 0.345 MeV (NM)
- In CRHB+LN calculations for 194Pb, NM shifts the paired neutron band crossing to lower frequencies by 70 keV, from 0.485 MeV (WNM) to 0.415 MeV (NM)
- The paired proton band crossing in 194Pb lies at Omega_x = 0.535 MeV in NM calculations, while only the beginning of this crossing is seen without NM

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of time-odd mean fields in CDFT begun by the same authors for non-rotating nuclei (Afanasjev and Abusara, 2010). It contrasts with earlier schematic non-self-consistent Skyrme force studies (PWM, 1985) which found that time-odd fields shift neutron alignments to higher frequencies in 158Dy, whereas the present CDFT calculations show shifts to lower frequencies. The results enable systematic predictions for rotating nuclei across the nuclear chart and clarify the role of nuclear magnetism in band crossing phenomena, signature splitting in odd-odd nuclei, and the relationship between moments of inertia and rigid body values for normal-deformed versus superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei.
