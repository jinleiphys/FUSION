# Valence $1s-0d$ proton vacancy of the $^{32}$Si ground state

> The $^{32}$Si($^3$He,$d$)$^{33}$P reaction was studied in inverse kinematics at 6.3~MeV/$u$. States in $^{33}$P corresponding to the proton $1s-0d$ single-particle orbitals were identified up to $\sim$4.5 MeV in excitation energy. The ($^{3}$He,$d$) spectroscopic factors were determined from Distorted Wave Born Approximation calculations. When combined with complementary neutron-adding data, the $1s-0d$ proton vacancies in the $^{32}$Si ground state were extracted. In conjunction with a re-analysis of data from previous single-particle measurements, the trends in proton and neutron vacancy were explored across the $^{28,30,32,34}$Si isotopes. Both proton and neutron vacancy data show gradual changes in their occupancies. The proton $1s_{1/2}$ orbitals in $^{32}$Si and $^{34}$Si are both consistent with being empty. The ground-state nucleon distributions are described by shell-model calculations constrained to the $1s-0d$ model space.

## Key claim

The $^{32}$Si ground state exhibits a robust $Z=14$ proton sub-shell closure, with the proton $1s_{1/2}$ and $0d_{3/2}$ orbitals being nearly empty and the $0d_{5/2}$ orbital containing at most approximately one nucleon vacancy, though the authors note that the $1s_{1/2}$ and $0d_{3/2}$ vacancies are "consistent with being empty" only within the uncertainties of the extracted values.

## Method

The authors studied the $^{32}$Si($^{3}$He,$d$)$^{33}$P single-proton-adding reaction in inverse kinematics at 6.3 MeV/u using the SOLARIS solenoidal spectrometer at NSCL, identifying states in $^{33}$P up to approximately 4.5 MeV excitation energy. Spectroscopic factors were extracted from Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations using the code PTOLEMY, and combined with complementary neutron-adding ($d$,$p$) data from Chen (2024) to determine the $1s-0d$ proton vacancies via isospin coupling relations. The same analysis procedure was applied to reanalyzed literature data for $^{28,30}$Si to explore trends across the isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- The $^{32}$Si($^{3}$He,$d$)$^{33}$P reaction was studied at 6.3 MeV/u beam energy with an excitation-energy resolution of approximately 470 keV FWHM.
- Spectroscopic factor for the $1s_{1/2}$ proton orbital in $^{32}$Si: $S = 1.06(21)$ (ground state of $^{33}$P).
- Spectroscopic factor for the $0d_{3/2}$ proton orbital: $S = 0.67(15)$ (1.43 MeV state) and $S = 0.31(11)$ (2.54 MeV state).
- Spectroscopic factor for the $0d_{5/2}$ proton orbital: $S = 0.16(5)$ (1.85 MeV state).
- Extracted proton vacancy for $^{32}$Si: $0d_{5/2} = 0.8(3)$, $1s_{1/2} = 1.8(3)$, $0d_{3/2} = 3.6(7)$.
- Extracted proton vacancy for $^{34}$Si: $0d_{5/2} = 0.5(5)$, $1s_{1/2} = 1.8(2)$, $0d_{3/2} = 3.7(2)$.
- The $0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1$ B(E2) transition strength in $^{32}$Si: 143(20) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Coulomb excitation (Heery, 2024) and 189(29) $e^2$fm$^4$ from lifetime measurement (Williams, 2025b).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of ground-state nucleon occupancies across the even-A $Z=14$ silicon isotopes from $^{28}$Si to $^{34}$Si, building on prior single-particle transfer measurements by Peterson (1983) and Chen (2024). The results support a smooth evolution of proton and neutron vacancies rather than abrupt structural changes, and provide empirical constraints for shell-model calculations using the USDB effective interaction (Brown, 2006). The findings are relevant to ongoing discussions about the evolution of the neutron $1p_{3/2}-1p_{1/2}$ spin-orbit partner energy differences in the $N\approx20$ region, as debated by Burgunder (2014), Mutschler (2017), Kay (2017), Orlandi (2018), Sorlin (2021), and Chen (2024).
