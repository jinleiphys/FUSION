# Nucleon Form Factors in Point-Form Spectator-Model Constructions

> We discuss electromagnetic currents in the point-form formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics. The construction is along a spectator model and implies that only one quark is explicitly coupled to the photon, but nevertheless many-body contributions are present in the current operator. Such effects are unavoidable in relativistic constructions and resulting ambiguities are notably reduced by imposing charge normalization and time-reversal invariance. The residual theoretical indetermination introduces small but sizeable changes in the nucleon form-factors, particularly at higher Q-squared values, with the data generally centered in the middle of the theoretical band.

# Nucleon Form Factors in Point-Form Spectator-Model Constructions

## Key claim
The point-form spectator model (PFSM) construction of electromagnetic currents for nucleon form factors contains a residual theoretical indetermination that cannot be fully removed by Poincaré invariance alone; imposing charge normalization and time-reversal invariance reduces but does not eliminate the ambiguity, producing a theoretical band whose width increases with momentum transfer, with experimental data generally falling in the middle of this band.

## Method
The authors constructed electromagnetic current operators in the point-form formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics using a spectator-model approach where only one quark is explicitly coupled to the photon, while many-body contributions arise from kinematic and normalization factors. They constrained the current operator by requiring charge normalization (fixing the exponent x=3) and time-reversal invariance (restricting the parameter y to produce symmetric or arithmetic combinations), then evaluated the resulting Sachs form factors for the nucleon within the Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) constituent-quark model. The theoretical uncertainty was estimated from the spread between the geometric (symmetric) and arithmetic normalization choices, and a one-parameter best fit was performed at Q²=3 GeV².

## Key numbers
- Charge normalization requires the exponent x=3 in the normalization factor to recover the proton charge at Q²=0.
- The geometric normalization factor is given by N_S = (M/Σω_i)^(3/2) (M'/Σω'_i)^(3/2), corresponding to z=0.5 in the general parametrization.
- The arithmetic normalization factor is N_ari = 1/2 [(M/Σω_i)^3 + (M'/Σω'_i)^3], corresponding to z=0 or z=1.
- The one-parameter best fit used z=1/6 in the general normalization expression, determined at Q²=3 GeV² momentum transfer.
- The theoretical band between the geometric and arithmetic curves broadens with increasing Q², with the geometric prediction showing a strong fall-off above Q²=1 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the point-form spectator model applied to the GBE constituent-quark model, which previously reproduced nucleon electromagnetic ground-state properties (Wagenbrunn 2000, Glozman 2001, Boffi 2001, Berger 2004). It addresses the need for careful uncertainty assessment in light of newer polarization transfer measurements (Jones 1999, Gayou 2001, Punjabi 2005, MacLachlan 2006) that disagree with earlier Rosenbluth data (Andivahis 1994, Walker 1989). The approach is complementary to field-theoretic treatments (Oettel 2000, Alkofer 2004) and is valid for low and intermediate momentum transfers; the residual theoretical band provides an estimate of the PFSM construction uncertainty that could be further reduced by finding additional constraints.
