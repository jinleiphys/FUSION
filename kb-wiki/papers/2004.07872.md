# Discrete symmetries in the cluster shell model

> The role of discrete (or point-group) symmetries is discussed in the framework of the Cluster Shell Model which describes the splitting of single-particle levels in the deformed field of cluster potentials. We discuss the classification of the eigenstates for the cases of a triangular and tetrahedral configuration of alpha-particles in terms of the irreducible representations of the double point groups D'(3h) and T'(d), respectively, and show how the discrete symmetry of a given eigenstate can be determined. Finally, we derive the Coriolis coupling for each one of these geometrical configurations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that eigenstates of the Cluster Shell Model for triangular and tetrahedral alpha-particle configurations can be classified by irreducible representations of the double point groups D'(3h) and T'(d), respectively, and that the discrete symmetry of a given eigenstate can be determined by evaluating expectation values of triplex and doublex operators, with the caveat that for tetrahedral symmetry the doublex operator alone is insufficient and additional operators are needed to distinguish between representations.

## Method

The Cluster Shell Model Hamiltonian, consisting of kinetic energy, central potential, spin-orbit interaction, and Coulomb potential for protons, was diagonalized in a harmonic oscillator basis for single-particle states moving in the deformed field generated by clusters of alpha-particles in equilateral triangular (three alpha-particles) and tetrahedral (four alpha-particles) configurations. The eigenstates were classified according to irreducible representations of the double point groups D'(3h) and T'(d) using triplex and doublex operators, and the Coriolis coupling was derived for each geometric configuration.

## Key numbers

- For triangular symmetry, the distance of alpha-particles from the center-of-mass was set to beta = 1.74 fm, determined from the first minimum in the elastic form factor of 12C.
- For triangular symmetry, the triplex operator satisfies (T_z)^12 = 1 for odd systems, giving half-integer mu values up to modulo 6: mu = +/- 1/2, +/- 3/2, +/- 5/2.
- For tetrahedral symmetry, the doublex operator satisfies (D_z)^8 = 1 for odd systems, giving half-integer mu values up to modulo 4: mu = +/- 1/2, +/- 3/2.
- The resolution table for D'(3h) shows that for J^P = 1/2+, the representation E_{1/2} appears once, while E_{5/2} and E_{3/2} appear zero times.
- The resolution table for T'(d) shows that for J^P = 3/2+, the representation G_{3/2} appears once, while E_{1/2} and E_{5/2} appear zero times.

## Context

This work extends the Cluster Shell Model previously applied to 9Be, 9B, and 13C (CSM1, CSM2, BI2019) by providing a systematic classification of eigenstates using double point group symmetries. It builds on earlier studies of discrete symmetries in nuclear physics by Dudek (Dudek1, Dudek2) for tetrahedral and octahedral deformations, and on experimental evidence for triangular D_{3h} symmetry in 12C and tetrahedral T_d symmetry in 16O (Daniel, C12-1, C12-2, O16-1, O16-2, Bijker2016). The results enable the prediction of rotational band structures and Coriolis mixing patterns in odd-mass alpha-cluster nuclei, providing signatures for experimental verification.
