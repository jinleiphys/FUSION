# The impact of the off-diagonal cross-shell interaction on $^{14}$C

> The shell-model investigation is performed to show the impact on the structure of $^{14}$C contributed by the off-diagonal cross-shell interaction, $\langle pp \mid$V$\mid sdsd\rangle$, which represents the mixing between the $0$ and $2\hbarω$ configurations in the $psd$ model space. Observed levels of the positive states in $^{14}$C can be nicely described in $0-4\hbarω$ or a larger model space through the well defined Hamiltonians, YSOX and WBP, with a reduction on the strength of the $\langle pp \mid$V$\mid sdsd\rangle$ interaction in the latter. The observed B(GT) values for $^{14}$C can be generally described by YSOX, while WBP and their modifications on the $\langle pp \mid$V$\mid sdsd\rangle$ interaction fail for some values. Further investigation shows the effect of such interaction on the configuration mixing and occupancy. The present work shows examples on how the off-diagonal cross-shell interaction strongly drives the nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The off-diagonal cross-shell interaction $\langle pp |V| sdsd\rangle$, which mixes $0\hbar\omega$ and $2\hbar\omega$ configurations, is shown to be crucial for reproducing the observed levels and transition rates of $^{14}$C, but only when its strength is reduced relative to the diagonal cross-shell interaction and when the model space is extended to at least $4\hbar\omega$ excitations.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the $psd$ model space using the Hamiltonians YSOX, WBP, and modified versions (WBP$-$ with a factor 0.6 on central TBME of $\langle pp |V| sdsd\rangle$, YSOX$+$ with equal strength for both cross-shell types, and YSOX$-$ with further reduction). The model space was extended from $0-2\hbar\omega$ up to $0-6\hbar\omega$ using the KSHELL code, with center-of-mass correction via the Gloeckner-Lawson method ($\beta=10$). Energies, B(GT) values, B(E2) values, and B(M1) values were computed and compared to experimental data and to results from NCSM and AMD.

## Key numbers

- The $0^{+}_1$ ground state of $^{14}$C has 77.95% $0\hbar\omega$, 20.90% $2\hbar\omega$, and 1.15% $4\hbar\omega$ configuration in YSOX at $4\hbar\omega$ model space; the $2^{+}_1$ state has 49.12% $0\hbar\omega$, 47.47% $2\hbar\omega$, and 3.41% $4\hbar\omega$ in the same calculation.
- The $2^{+}_2$ state in YSOX at $4\hbar\omega$ has 15.11% $0\hbar\omega$, 78.36% $2\hbar\omega$, and 6.53% $4\hbar\omega$; the $2^{+}_3$ state has 10.41% $0\hbar\omega$, 83.11% $2\hbar\omega$, and 6.48% $4\hbar\omega$.
- The B(GT) transition matrix element M$_{0p_{1/2} \rightarrow 0p_{3/2}}$ for $^{14}$N$_{g.s.}$ $\rightarrow$ $^{14}$C$_{2^{+}_2}$ is 0.89 in YSOX at $4\hbar\omega$, compared to 0.26 in YSOX$+$ and 0.28 in WBP$-$ at the same model space.
- The B(GT) transition matrix element M$_{0p_{1/2} \rightarrow 0p_{3/2}}$ for $^{14}$N$_{g.s.}$ $\rightarrow$ $^{14}$C$_{2^{+}_3}$ is 0.72 in YSOX at $4\hbar\omega$, compared to 0.46 in YSOX$+$ and 0.26 in WBP$-$.
- Quenching factors of 0.72 for YSOX and 0.64 for WBP were used to reproduce observed B(GT) values among nuclei around $^{14}$C.
- Effective charges $e_p=1.27$, $e_n=0.23$ and effective g factors $\delta g^{(l)}_{\pi,\nu}=\pm0.1~\mu_N$, $g^{(eff)}_s/g_s=0.95$ were used for electromagnetic transitions.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the off-diagonal cross-shell interaction in the $psd$ region, following the construction of the YSOX Hamiltonian by Yuan (2012) which included this interaction based on binding energies of B, C, N, O isotopes. It addresses the long-standing problem of the $^{14}$C lifetime and level ordering, which could not be well described by earlier shell-model Hamiltonians (WBP, SFO) in $0-2\hbar\omega$ model spaces, nor by NCSM (Aroua 2003) or AMD (Enyo 2014) approaches. The results enable systematic study of multi-$\hbar\omega$ configuration mixing in light nuclei and show that the off-diagonal cross-shell interaction cannot be fully replaced by simply increasing the model space size.
