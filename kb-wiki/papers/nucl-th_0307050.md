# On the effectiveness of effective field theory in peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering

> Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering is analysed in the framework of an effective field theory. Distorted-wave methods are used to remove the effects of one-pion exchange. Two-pion exchange and recoil corrections to one-pion exchange are then subtracted pertubatively. This removes all contributions up to order Q^3 in the chiral expansion. We have applied this to the 1D2, 1F3 and 1G4 waves, using phase shifts from various partial-wave analyses by the Nijmegen group. In regions where these analyses agree we find no evidence for a breakdown of the chiral expansion. One of the terms in the effective short-range potential, the leading one in the 1D2 channel, is larger might be expected, but in general these terms have momentum scales of about 300-400 MeV. We also see hints of isospin breaking in the pi-N couplings.

## Key claim

Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering in the 1D2, 1F3, and 1G4 partial waves shows no evidence for a breakdown of the chiral expansion up to order Q^3, provided one uses the correct pi-N coupling and includes recoil corrections to one-pion exchange; however, the leading short-range term in the 1D2 channel is larger than naturalness would suggest, corresponding to a momentum scale of about 200 MeV rather than the expected 300-400 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied a distorted-wave method to remove the effects of one-pion exchange (OPE) to all orders from empirical phase shifts, then subtracted two-pion exchange (TPE) and recoil corrections to OPE perturbatively, removing all contributions up to order Q^3 in the chiral expansion. They used phase shifts from five Nijmegen partial-wave analyses (PWA93, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, Reid93, and ESC96) for the np 1D2, 1F3, and 1G4 spin-singlet partial waves. The residual short-range potential was extracted as an energy-dependent delta-shell interaction at radius R0 = 0.1 fm, with the TPE potential using the Nijmegen values c1 = -0.76 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.78 GeV^-1, and c4 = 3.96 GeV^-1.

## Key numbers

- The leading short-range term in the 1D2 channel has an intercept at about 0.16 fm^6, corresponding to a momentum scale of about 200 MeV.
- The slope of the residual short-range potential in the 1D2 wave is dV^(4)/dp^2 ≈ 0.012 fm^8, corresponding to a scale of about 370 MeV.
- The typical size of the residual strength after removing only OPE in the 1D2 wave, V^(2), is about 0.1 fm^6, corresponding to a scale of approximately 300 MeV.
- In the 1F3 and 1G4 waves, the residual scattering strengths at energies below 100 MeV correspond to scales in the region 300-400 MeV.
- The pi-N coupling used was f_piNN^2 = 0.075, matching the value used in the Nijmegen PWA's.
- The delta-shell radius used for numerical implementation was R0 = 0.1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the distorted-wave approach of Birse and Barford (2002) to peripheral NN scattering, addressing concerns raised by Entem and Machleidt (2002) about the reliability of chiral TPE parameters extracted from Nijmegen fits. The method improves upon earlier variable-phase approaches by Ballot et al. (1998) and Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2000) by avoiding the need for a radial cut-off that mixes short-range physics with regulator artefacts. The results enable future tests of order-Q^4 three-pion exchange forces and can be applied to the newer chiral partial-wave analyses from the Nijmegen group (1999, 2003) once those become available.
