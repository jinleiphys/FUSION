# Electromagnetic Decay of Molecular States

> Electromagnetic transitions from deformed structures based on $α$ configurations or on heavier clusters are discussed, drawing the link between multiparticle-multihole excited bands and cluster structures. Enhanced E2 and E1 transitions are reviewed in the light nuclei, $^8$Be, $^{10}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{18}$O and heavier ones like $^{212}$Po. Connections between cluster structures and superdeformed configurations in $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ca are discussed. What the cluster states based on heavier substructures like $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are concerned, recent results on the resonant radiative capture reaction $^{12}$C($^{16}$O,$γ$)$^{28}$Si are presented, in particular the strong decay mode involving the feeding of low-lying $^{28}$Si 1$^+$ and 2$^+$ T=1 states by enhanced M1 isovector transitions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that cluster (molecular) states in light and medium-mass nuclei are connected by enhanced electromagnetic transitions, and presents new data from the \(^{12}\text{C}(^{16}\text{O},\gamma)^{28}\text{Si}\) resonant radiative capture reaction showing that low-spin resonances near the Coulomb barrier decay preferentially via isovector M1 transitions to \(T=1\) states in \(^{28}\text{Si}\), a decay pattern that may be specific to self-conjugate nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed the \(^{12}\text{C}(^{16}\text{O},\gamma)^{28}\text{Si}\) radiative capture reaction at five \(^{16}\text{O}\) bombarding energies between 15.4 and 21.1 MeV (center-of-mass energies 6.6 to 9.0 MeV) using the Dragon recoil spectrometer at TRIUMF. The \(^{28}\text{Si}\) recoils were identified with a beam rejection factor better than \(10^{12}\), and coincident gamma rays were detected with a 30-element BGO array. Monte Carlo GEANT simulations of the full experimental setup were used to fit the measured gamma spectra, with the entrance resonance spin as the main free parameter.

## Key numbers

- In \(^8\)Be, the \(4^+ \rightarrow 2^+\) gamma transition has a reduced B(E2) strength of \(25.8 \pm 8.4\) W.u.
- In \(^9\)Be, the \(\frac{5}{2}^- \rightarrow \frac{3}{2}^-\) intraband transition has B(E2) = \(24.4 \pm 1.8\) W.u., and the \(\frac{7}{2}^- \rightarrow \frac{5}{2}^-\) transition has B(E2) = \(8.5 \pm 3.6\) W.u.
- In \(^{10}\)Be, the E1 transition from the \(0^+\) cluster state at 6.18 MeV to the \(1^-\) cluster state at 5.96 MeV has a strength of \(4.3 \pm 1.6 \times 10^{-2}\) W.u. (E\(_\gamma\) = 219 keV).
- In \(^{12}\)C, the E2 strength from the Hoyle state (\(0^+\) at 7.65 MeV) to the first \(2^+\) state at 4.43 MeV is \(8.1 \pm 1.1\) W.u.; the \(2^+ \rightarrow 0^+_{\text{gs}}\) transition has B(E2) = \(4.7 \pm 0.2\) W.u.
- In \(^{16}\)O, the \(2^+\) (6.92 MeV) \(\rightarrow\) \(0^+\) (6.05 MeV) transition has B(E2) = \(27 \pm 3\) W.u., and the \(4^+\) (10.36 MeV) \(\rightarrow\) \(2^+\) (6.92 MeV) transition has B(E2) = \(65 \pm 6\) W.u.
- In \(^{36}\)Ar, the superdeformed band has deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.46 \pm 0.03\) and the \(4^+ \rightarrow 2^+\) transition has B(E2) = \(53 \pm 9\) W.u.
- In \(^{40}\)Ca, the superdeformed band has deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.59 \pm 0.09\) and the \(4^+ \rightarrow 2^+\) transition has B(E2) = \(170 \pm 40\) W.u.
- In \(^{212}\)Po, E1 decay strengths between \(\alpha\)-\(^{208}\)Pb cluster states range from \(10^{-3}\) to \(2 \times 10^{-2}\) W.u.
- For the \(^{12}\text{C}(^{16}\text{O},\gamma)^{28}\text{Si}\) reaction, the best fit at E\(_{\text{c.m.}}\) = 9.0 MeV gives a \(6^+\) entrance resonance; at E\(_{\text{c.m.}}\) = 6.6 MeV, a \(2^+\) entrance resonance is assigned, feeding the \(1^+\) state at 11.45 MeV in \(^{28}\text{Si}\).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the known connection between multiparticle-multihole deformed bands and cluster structures, building on earlier studies of alpha-cluster bands in light nuclei (e.g., the Hoyle state in \(^{12}\)C) and superdeformed bands in \(^{36}\)Ar and \(^{40}\)Ca (Svensson 2001, Ideguchi 2001). The new \(^{12}\text{C}(^{16}\text{O},\gamma)^{28}\text{Si}\) measurements at TRIUMF extend the earlier radiative capture work of Sandorfi (1980s) by accessing lower bombarding energies and resolving low-energy gamma transitions that were previously missed. The results suggest that the low-spin resonances observed may be the low-lying members of the molecular-like \(^{12}\text{C}\)-\(^{16}\text{O}\) breakup band identified by the CHARISSA collaboration (Metelko 2004). Future experiments with higher-resolution LaBr\(_3\) arrays (PARIS collaboration) are needed to confirm the decay scenario and to test whether the M1 \(\Delta T=1\) decay pattern is specific to self-conjugate nuclei, e.g., via the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{14}\text{C}\) reaction.
