# Symmetries and Interactions from Lattice QCD

> Precision experimental tests of the Standard Model of particle physics (SM) are one of our best hopes for discovering what new physics lies beyond the SM (BSM). Key in the search for new physics is the connection between theory and experiment. Forging this connection for searches involving low-energy hadronic or nuclear environments requires the use of a non-perturbative theoretical tool, lattice QCD. We present two recent lattice QCD calculations by the CalLat collaboration relevant for new physics searches: the nucleon axial coupling, $g_A$, whose precise value as predicted by the SM could help point to new physics contributions to the so-called "neutron lifetime puzzle", and hadronic matrix elements of short-ranged operators relevant for neutrinoless double beta decay searches.

## Key claim

The CalLat collaboration presents lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) and hadronic matrix elements for short-ranged neutrinoless double beta decay operators, with the \(g_A\) result reaching 1% precision but still insufficient to directly compete with experimental measurements, though it already provides the strongest constraint on right-handed beyond-Standard-Model currents.

## Method

The \(g_A\) calculation used a Feynman-Hellmann technique that produces data for all Euclidean time separations from a single calculation, enabling controlled excited-state removal through fits over varying time regions and numbers of included states. The calculation employed a mixed-action approach coupling MILC HISQ ensembles with domain-wall fermion propagators, performed on 16 ensembles with lattice spacings 0.09-0.15 fm, pion masses 130-450 MeV, and volumes 2.4-5.8 fm. For the neutrinoless double beta decay calculation, the collaboration computed hadronic matrix elements of short-ranged four-quark operators that arise from matching heavy beyond-Standard-Model physics onto the electroweak scale.

## Key numbers

- \(g_A = 1.2711\) with individual errors: statistics (103), pion mass dependence (39), discretization (15), finite volume (19), isospin breaking (04), and model selection (55) (all errors in units of \(10^{-4}\))
- Lattice spacings used: 0.09-0.15 fm
- Pion mass range: 130-450 MeV
- Volume range: 2.4-5.8 fm
- Experimental PDG average: \(g_A = 1.2724(23)\)
- Perkeo group measurement: \(\lambda = -1.27641(45)(33)\)
- UCNA group measurement: \(\lambda = -1.2783(22)\)

## Context

This work extends the long history of lattice QCD calculations of \(g_A\) from collaborations including PACS-CS (Yamazaki 2008, 2009), LHPC (Bratt 2010), RBC/UKQCD (Lin 2008), ETMC (Abdel-Rehim 2015, Alexandrou 2017), and others, which had not achieved precision competitive with experiment despite the apparent simplicity of the calculation. The result provides the strongest constraint on right-handed beyond-Standard-Model currents \(\xi_{ud}\) compared to collider experiments, pion decays, and super-allowed beta decays as shown in the analysis by Alioli (2017), and establishes a path toward sub-percent calculations that could resolve the neutron lifetime puzzle. The neutrinoless double beta decay calculation enables matching of short-distance beyond-Standard-Model physics to experimental searches in nuclei, with the next generation of ton-scale experiments aiming for lifetime bounds of \(10^{27}\) to \(10^{28}\) years.
