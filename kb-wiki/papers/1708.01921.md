# On the hydrodynamic attractor of Yang-Mills plasma

> There is mounting evidence suggesting that relativistic hydrodynamics becomes relevant for the physics of quark-gluon plasma as the result of nonhydrodynamic modes decaying to an attractor apparent even when the system is far from local equilibrium. Here we determine this attractor for Bjorken flow in N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory using Borel summation of the gradient expansion of the expectation value of the energy momentum tensor. By comparing the result to numerical simulations of the flow based on the AdS/CFT correspondence we show that it provides an accurate and unambiguous approximation of the hydrodynamic attractor in this system. This development has important implications for the formulation of effective theories of hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The Borel sum of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion provides a direct and unambiguous approximation of the hydrodynamic attractor for Bjorken flow in N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, and this approximation is accurate for dimensionless proper time w > 0.3, well before the Navier-Stokes approximation becomes valid at w ≈ 0.7.

## Method

The author calculates the Borel sum of the gradient expansion of the pressure anisotropy for Bjorken flow in N=4 SYM, using 240 coefficients previously obtained via the AdS/CFT correspondence by Heller (2013). The Borel transform is analytically continued using diagonal Pade approximants, and the Laplace integral is performed numerically over real values of ξ from zero to infinity. The result is compared to numerical simulations of Bjorken flow based on the AdS/CFT correspondence from Jankowski (2014) and to the attractor of HJSW hydrodynamics (Aniceto 2015) as a test case.

## Key numbers

- The Borel sum of the gradient expansion for N=4 SYM is accurately reproduced by the rational fit: pa_0 = (2530w - 276)/(3975w^2 - 570w + 120) for essentially all w > 0.
- The Borel sum tracks the numerically determined attractor in HJSW theory closely down to w ≈ 0.4, and is still reasonable at w ≈ 0.3.
- The Navier-Stokes approximation to hydrodynamics becomes accurate at w ≈ 0.7 (Jankowski 2014).
- The hydrodynamization threshold in Jankowski (2014) was found centered around w_H = 0.65, with pressure anisotropy pa(w_H) ≈ 0.7.
- For most histories shown, the pressure anisotropy at hydrodynamization exceeds 100%.

## Context

This work extends the identification of hydrodynamic attractors by Heller (2015) from the level of hydrodynamic equations to the microscopic level in N=4 SYM theory. It contrasts with the BRSSS approach (Baier 2007), where Borel summation is ambiguous due to branch-point singularities on the real axis, requiring transseries sectors. The result enables formulating effective theories of hydrodynamics that reproduce the attractor rather than just low-order gradient expansion terms, which could have consequences for interpreting observables sensitive to early-time dynamics in quark-gluon plasma.
