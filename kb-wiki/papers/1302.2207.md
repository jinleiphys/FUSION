# Strange baryons, nuclear dripline and shrinkage : A Relativistic Mean Field study

> Neutron and proton driplines of single-$Λ$ and double-$Λ$ hypernuclei, $Ξ^{-}$ hypernuclei as well as normal nuclei are studied within a relativistic mean field approach using an extended form of the FSU Gold Lagrangian density. Hyperons are found to produce bound nuclei beyond the normal nuclear driplines. Radii are found to decrease in hypernuclei near the driplines, in line with observations in light $Λ$ hypernuclei near the stability valley, The inclusion of a $Ξ^{-}$ introduces a much larger change in radii than one or more $Λ$'s.

## Key claim

Hyperons can produce bound nuclei beyond the normal nuclear driplines, but the effect is significant only in lighter mass regions and diminishes for heavier nuclei due to the weakness of the hyperon-nucleon coupling compared to nucleon-nucleon contributions; the proton dripline shifts only in a few light nuclei for single-Λ hypernuclei, while Ξ− hyperons produce much more prominent shifts.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach in coordinate space with spherical approximation, using an extended form of the FSU Gold Lagrangian density that includes hyperon terms. Pairing correlations were treated with a delta interaction of constant strength V0 = -350 MeV, and the resonant-BCS approach was used to include contributions from bound states and the resonant continuum. The equations were solved on a grid of size 0.08 fm with a box size of 20 fm, using the tagging approximation for odd-mass nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The delta interaction pairing strength was set to V0 = -350 MeV.
- An increase in box size from 20 fm to 22 fm changes total energy by less than 0.005% and radius by less than 0.05% in 64Ca.
- At the proton dripline, the r.m.s charge radius decreases for N ≤ 4, with changes below 1%.
- At the neutron dripline, the neutron r.m.s radius decreases for Z ≤ 7 and for the hypernucleus 35ΛSi, with changes below 1%.
- For the Ca isotopes, the authors found no shift of the neutron dripline, in contrast to a prior prediction of a shift from N=52 to N=54.

## Context

This work extends prior RMF studies of hypernuclei by Bhowmick et al. (2011) and applies the FSU Gold Lagrangian to dripline systems for the first time. It contradicts the results of an earlier study that predicted a neutron dripline shift for Ca hypernuclei. The findings enable future experimental searches for hypernuclei beyond normal driplines, particularly at facilities like J-PARC, and suggest that Ξ− hypernuclei near driplines may show measurable shrinkage effects that could be tested experimentally.
