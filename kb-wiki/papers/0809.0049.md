# Charm hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions within a quark combination model

> We investigate the charm hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with the quark combination model. The $p_{T}$ dependencies of the charm baryon to meson ratios such as $\frac{Λ_{c}+\barΛ_{c}}{D^{0}+\bar{D}^{0}}$, $\frac{Λ_{c}+\barΛ_{c}}{D^{+}+D^{-}}$ and $\frac{Λ_{c}+\barΛ_{c}}{D_{s}^{+}+D_{s}^{-}}$ in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV are obtained. The charm baryon enhancement in the intermediate $p_{T}$ range is very prominent, which further, together with the strangeness enhancement, affect the charm hadron ratios and lead to a $\sim17%$ larger charm cross-section given by PHENIX. The dependencies of the charm hadron ratios on energy, centrality, and other parameters are also investigated. Predictions of the charm hadron ratios for the upgrade of RHIC and for LHC are presented.

## Key claim

The quark combination model (QCM) predicts that charm baryon-to-meson ratios in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV are substantially enhanced in the intermediate $p_T$ range due to coalescence/recombination hadronization, and that the resulting modification of the ratio $R_{e/c\bar{c}}$ used by PHENIX leads to a ~17% increase of the extracted charm cross section $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}$, though this correction is not sufficient to fully resolve the discrepancy between STAR and PHENIX measurements.

## Method

The authors extended their quark combination model (QCM) from SU(3) to SU(4) flavor symmetry to describe charm hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The model assumes that quarks and antiquarks nearest in rapidity combine into hadrons, with all ground-state hadrons of the SU(4) 120-plet baryons and 64-plet mesons considered systematically, and final-state hadrons obtained after resonance decays treated via PYTHIA6.4.16. The charm quark spectrum was extracted by fitting STAR data for $D^0(\bar{D}^0)$ and electron spectra, and the total number of $c\bar{c}$ pairs was taken proportional to the number of binary collisions $N_{bin}$ with $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}=1.4$ mb.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $R_{e/c\bar{c}}$ in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is $8.48^{+1.05}_{-0.90}\%$ using PDG branching ratios, or $8.10^{+0.99}_{-0.84}\%$ using updated PDGlive branching ratios, compared to the 9.5% used by PHENIX, leading to a ~17% increase of $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}$.
- The ratio $R_{D^{0}/c\bar{c}}$ in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is 0.534 from the hadron ratio method, very close to the 0.54 used by STAR, leading to only ~1% correction of $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}$.
- The ratio $D_s^+/D^+$ in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is approximately 0.976, much higher than the 0.56 used by PHENIX.
- The ratio $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is 0.301, compared to 0.1 used by PHENIX.
- The total $N_{c\bar{c}}$ in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is about 35, using $\langle N_{bin}\rangle=1051$ and $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}=1.4$ mb.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination model previously applied to $e^+e^-$ annihilations and $pp(\bar{p})$ collisions (Xie 1988, Xie 1997) to charm hadron production in heavy ion collisions, providing the first extensive predictions of charm hadron ratios and key conversion ratios $R_{e/c\bar{c}}$, $R_{D^{0}/c\bar{c}}$, and $R_{\mu/c\bar{c}}$ within a coalescence/recombination framework. The results differ from the statistical hadronization model predictions of Andronic (2003, 2006) and suggest that the discrepancy between STAR and PHENIX measurements of $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN}$ cannot be fully explained by the modified charm hadron ratios in heavy ion collisions. The predictions can be tested at RHIC after detector upgrades and at LHC energies.
