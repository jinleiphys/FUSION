# Gamow Shell-Model Description of Weakly Bound and Unbound Nuclear States

> Recently, the shell model in the complex k-plane (the so-called Gamow Shell Model) has been formulated using a complex Berggren ensemble representing bound single-particle states, single-particle resonances, and non-resonant continuum states. In this framework, we shall discuss binding energies and energy spectra of neutron-rich helium and lithium isotopes. The single-particle basis used is that of the Hartree-Fock potential generated self-consistently by the finite-range residual interaction.

# Gamow Shell-Model Description of Weakly Bound and Unbound Nuclear States

## Key claim
The Gamow Shell Model (GSM) with a finite-range Surface Gaussian Interaction (SGI) and a self-consistent Gamow Hartree-Fock (GHF) basis can describe binding energies and spectra of neutron-rich helium and lithium isotopes, but the description of lithium isotopes requires an ad hoc linear reduction of the T=0 neutron-proton interaction strength with increasing neutron number to avoid severe overbinding.

## Method
The authors formulated the GSM using a complex Berggren ensemble of single-particle states (bound states, resonances, and non-resonant continuum) generated by a self-consistent GHF potential derived from a Woods-Saxon potential with the "5He" parameter set (radius 2 fm, diffuseness 0.65 fm, central strength 47 MeV, spin-orbit strength 7.5 MeV) and a finite-range Surface Gaussian Interaction (SGI) residual interaction. For helium isotopes, the valence space consisted of 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 GHF resonant states plus 14 discretized continuum points for each partial wave up to Re[k]=8 fm^-1, with configurations restricted to at most two particles in the non-resonant continuum. For lithium isotopes, the same valence space was used but with the addition of a one-body Coulomb potential from a uniformly charged 4He core, and the T=0 interaction strengths were made linearly dependent on the number of valence neutrons.

## Key numbers
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: radius R0=2 fm, diffuseness d=0.65 fm, central field strength V0=47 MeV, spin-orbit strength Vso=7.5 MeV
- SGI coupling constants for helium: V0(J=0,T=1) = -403 MeV fm^3, V0(J=2,T=1) = -315 MeV fm^3
- SGI coupling constants for lithium T=0 interactions: alpha10=-600 MeV fm^3, beta10=-50 MeV fm^3, alpha30=-625 MeV fm^3, beta30=-100 MeV fm^3 (with linear dependence on valence neutron number n)
- In 1p-1h truncated calculations, the real part of the ground-state energy relative to 4He was: 0.75 MeV for 5He, 0.58 MeV for 6He, 1.27 MeV for 7He, and -1.40 MeV for 8He
- The non-resonant continuum was discretized with 14 points (M=14) extending from Re[k]=0 to Re[k]=8 fm^-1
- For 7Li calculated with T=0 strength adjusted to 6Li, the ground state becomes overbound by 13 MeV
- In the DMRG test for 6He, the total dimension of the GSM Hamiltonian was 462 and the rank of the largest matrix diagonalized in GSM+DMRG was 32, using 20 p1/2 and 20 p3/2 continuum shells with 6 vectors kept in the P space

## Context
This work extends the Gamow Shell Model formalism previously introduced by Michel, Nazarewicz, Ploszajczak, and Rotureau (2002, 2003) for neutron-rich helium and oxygen isotopes, replacing the earlier Surface Delta Interaction (SDI) with a finite-range Surface Gaussian Interaction (SGI) that enables self-consistent GHF calculations. The results for helium isotopes confirm the Borromean nature of 6He found in earlier GSM studies, while the lithium calculations reveal a strong neutron-number dependence of T=0 interactions consistent with findings from the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) approach of Luo, Mazury, and collaborators. The paper also presents the first adaptation of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) technique of White (1992) to the non-hermitian GSM problem, which promises to enable calculations in model spaces currently inaccessible, such as including the 1s1/2 antibound state needed for a proper description of 11Li.
