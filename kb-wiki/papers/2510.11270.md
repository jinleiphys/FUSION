# QCD phase structure & equation of state: A functional perspective

> The phase structure of QCD remains an open fundamental problem of standard model physics. In particular at finite density, our knowledge is limited. Yet, numerous model studies point towards a rich and complex phase diagram at large density. Functional methods like the functional renormalization group and Dyson-Schwinger equations offer a way to study hot and dense QCD matter directly from first principles. I will discuss the phase structure of QCD and its experimental signatures through the lens of these methods.

## Key claim

Functional methods (DSEs and FRG) now provide first direct, first-principles results on the QCD phase diagram at nonzero baryon chemical potential, but all results assume a homogeneous phase; it cannot be excluded that an inhomogeneous state with lower free energy exists at \(\mu_B \gtrsim 600\) MeV, so the homogeneous chiral crossover is very likely to end around \((T,\mu_B) \approx (110,630)\) MeV, but whether it ends in a critical endpoint, a spatially modulated phase, or both is not certain.

## Method

The paper uses the functional renormalization group (FRG) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to solve exact relations for QCD correlation functions, converting the path integral into coupled differential equations. Truncations of the infinite tower of equations are necessary, guided by the observed hierarchy from low- to high-order correlations, with systematic error control as the biggest challenge. The chiral condensate and meson correlation functions are computed at finite temperature and chemical potential, and the equation of state is obtained from the pressure via thermodynamic derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint location: \((T,\mu_B)_{\rm CEP} \approx (110,630)\) MeV with an uncertainty of about 10%, corroborated by lattice QCD extrapolations based on Yang-Lee edge singularities and thermodynamic relations.
- Chiral critical temperature in the massless limit: \(T_c = 142.58\) MeV; the critical exponent \(\delta\) extracted from fits to the chiral condensate ceases to follow its universal value for pion masses \(m_\pi \gtrsim 5\) MeV.
- Two-flavor superconducting (2SC) diquark gap: \(\Delta_{\rm 2SC} \approx 150-300\) MeV at 5-10 times nuclear saturation density.
- The kurtosis of the net-baryon distribution shows a nonmonotonic beam-energy dependence, with data for \(\sqrt{s} \approx 4-8\) GeV identified as crucial for experimental discovery of a chiral phase transition.
- The deviation from the noncritical baseline at \(\sqrt{s} = 19.6\) GeV (corresponding to \(\mu_B \approx 200\) MeV) reported by STAR lies in a well-understood region and is highly unlikely to be related to a critical endpoint.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results at zero baryon chemical potential (Bellwied 2015, Bazavov 2018) to nonzero \(\mu_B\) using functional methods, with the first benchmarked results from FRG (Fu 2019) and DSEs (Gao 2020, Gunkel 2021). It enables first-principles calculations of the QCD equation of state beyond the range of lattice extrapolations, and opens the possibility for experimental discovery of spatially modulated phases through the moat regime (Pisarski 2021) and its signatures in heavy-ion collisions, such as Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations or dilepton yields.
