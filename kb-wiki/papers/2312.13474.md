# Impact of tensor interactions and scalar mixing on covariant energy density functionals

> The recent pioneering campaigns conducted by the Lead Radius Experiment (PREX) and the Calcium Radius Experiment (CREX) collaborations have uncovered major deficiencies in the theoretical description of some fundamental properties of atomic nuclei. Following a recent refinement to the isovector sector of covariant energy density functionals [1], we present here additional improvements to the functional by including both tensor couplings and an isoscalar-isovector mixing term in the scalar sector. Motivated by the distinct surface properties of calcium and lead, we expect that the tensor terms that generate derivative couplings will help break the linear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of these two nuclei. Moreover, the addition of these new terms mitigates most of the problems identified in Ref.[1] in describing the properties of both finite nuclei and neutron stars. While significant progress has been made in reconciling the PREX-CREX results without compromising other observables, the final resolution awaits the completion of a proper calibration for this new class of functionals. We expect that powerful reduced basis methods used recently to create efficient emulators will be essential to accomplish this task.

## Key claim

The authors show that adding tensor couplings (derivative couplings of vector mesons) and an isoscalar-isovector mixing term in the scalar sector to covariant energy density functionals can partially break the linear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of calcium and lead, but a full resolution of the PREX-CREX discrepancy awaits a proper calibration of the new functional parameters.

## Method

The authors extended a covariant energy density functional by including the isovector-scalar delta-meson, tensor couplings for both the omega-meson and rho-meson (involving derivatives of the vector fields), a quartic rho-meson self-interaction, and a mixed isoscalar-isovector coupling in the scalar sector. They derived the mean-field equations for finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, then performed a preliminary chi-square calibration using ground-state energies and charge radii of Ca-40, Ca-48, and Pb-208, along with the weak-skin form factors from the PREX and CREX experiments.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of Pb-208: R_skin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm (from PREX)
- Neutron skin thickness of Ca-48: R_skin = 0.121 ± 0.035 fm (from CREX)
- Slope of symmetry energy L = 106 ± 37 MeV (derived from PREX result)
- Predicted Ca-48 neutron skin thickness from earlier covariant EDFs: R_skin = 0.229 ± 0.035 fm (Piekarewicz 2021)
- Momentum transfer for PREX: q = 0.398 fm^-1
- Momentum transfer for CREX: q = 0.873 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the recent refinement by Reed and collaborators (2023) that added the isovector-scalar delta-meson to covariant EDFs but produced overly stiff neutron star equations of state. The new terms aim to resolve the "PREX-CREX dilemma" where the large Pb-208 neutron skin (implying stiff symmetry energy) conflicts with the small Ca-48 neutron skin and with neutron star observations from NICER (Miller 2019, Riley 2019) and LIGO-Virgo (Abbott 2017, 2018). The authors note that previous attempts by Hu (2021), Reinhard (2022), Zhang (2022), Mondal (2022), Papakonstantinou (2022), Yuksel (2022), Li (2022), Thakur (2022), and Miyatsu (2023) have all failed to reconcile these measurements. A full Bayesian calibration using reduced basis methods (Giuliani 2022) is deferred to future work.
