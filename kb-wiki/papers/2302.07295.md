# Differential charge radii: self-consistency and proton-neutron interaction effects

> The analysis of self-consistency and proton-neutron interaction effects in the buildup of differential charge radii has been carried out in covariant density functional theoretical calculations without pairing interaction. Two configurations of the $^{218}$Pb nucleus, generated by the occupation of the neutron $1i_{11/2}$ and $2g_{9/2}$ subshells, are compared with the ground state configuration in $^{208}$Pb. The interaction of added neutron(s) and the protons forming the $Z=82$ proton core is responsible for a major contribution to the buildup of differential charge radii. It depends on the overlaps of proton and neutron wave functions and leads to a redistribution of single-particle density of occupied proton states which in turn modifies the charge radii. Self-consistency effects affecting the shape of proton potential, total proton densities and the energies of the single-particle proton states provide only secondary contribution to differential charge radii. The buildup of differential charge radii is a combination of single-particle and collective phenomena. The former is due to proton-neutron interaction, the impact of which is state dependent, and the latter reflects the fact that all occupied proton single-particle states contribute to this process. The neglect of either one of these aspects of the process by ignoring proton-neutron interaction and self-consistency effects as it is done in macroscopic+microscopic approach or by introducing the core as in spherical shell model introduces uncontrollable errors and restricts the applicability of such approaches to the description of differential charge radii.

## Key claim

The buildup of differential charge radii in Pb isotopes is dominated by the proton-neutron interaction between added neutrons and the protons of the Z=82 core, with self-consistency effects providing only a secondary contribution; the magnitude of this effect depends critically on the principal quantum number of the occupied neutron orbital and its wave function overlap with proton states.

## Method

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations without pairing were performed using the NL3* energy density functional, comparing the ground state configuration of 208Pb with two configurations of 218Pb where ten neutrons occupy either the 1i11/2 or 2g9/2 spherical subshells. The contributions of individual proton subshells to differential charge radii were decomposed by analyzing changes in single-particle proton rms radii and wave function overlaps between proton and neutron states.

## Key numbers

- Differential charge radius δ⟨r²⟩p for the "Conf-1i11/2" configuration in 218Pb relative to 208Pb: 1.31 fm²
- Differential charge radius δ⟨r²⟩p for the "Conf-2g9/2" configuration in 218Pb relative to 208Pb: 0.61 fm²
- 96.7% of the differential charge radius in the "Conf-1i11/2" configuration comes from n=1 proton subshells
- 53.3% of the differential charge radius in the "Conf-2g9/2" configuration comes from n=2 proton subshells
- Overlap of neutron 1i11/2 wave function with proton 1h11/2 state: 0.98
- Overlap of neutron 2g9/2 wave function with proton 2d5/2 state: 0.75
- The Z=82 core contains 62 protons in n=1 subshells, 18 in n=2 subshells, and 2 in n=3 subshells

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Reinhard and Flocard (1995) and the global study of Perera et al. (2021) on the microscopic origin of charge radii kinks at neutron shell closures, specifically the N=126 kink in Pb isotopes. The results contradict the interpretation of Goddard, Stevenson, and Rios (2013) that the pull of neutron orbitals on proton states operates via symmetry energy, showing instead that proton-neutron interaction and wave function overlaps are the primary mechanism. The findings enable assessment of which theoretical frameworks (macroscopic+microscopic approaches, spherical shell model with inert cores) introduce uncontrollable errors by neglecting these effects, and suggest that Coriolis interaction in deformed nuclei and residual proton-neutron interaction in odd-odd nuclei could affect odd-even staggering in charge radii.
