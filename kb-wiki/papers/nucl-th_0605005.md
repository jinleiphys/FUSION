# Charged and neutral hyperonic effects on the driplines

> Modification of neutron and proton driplines by the capture of strange hyperon(s) by normal nuclei has been investigated. A generalised mass formula (BWMH) based on the strangeness dependent extended liquid drop model is used to calculate the binding energy of normal nuclei as well as strange hypernuclei. The neutron (Sn) and proton (Sp) separation energies of all hypernuclei with neutral hyperons Lambda, double Lambda or charged hyperons Cascade(-), Theta(+) inside are calculated using BWMH mass formula. The normal neutron and proton driplines get modified due to the addition of the hyperon(s)(Lambda, double Lambda, Cascade(-), Theta(+) etc.) to the core of normal nuclei. The hypernuclei containing the charged hyperon(s) like those with neutral hyperon(s) have similar nucleon separation energies like core nuclei if proton number instead of net charge is used in the symmetry term. Due to the effect of opposite charges present in Theta(+) and Cascade(-), hyperons their corresponding driplines get separated from each other. All the hyperons modify mean field potential due to strong hyperon-nucleon coupling. Addition of a single charged hyperon in normal nuclei affects the entire proton drip line more prominently than that by neutral hyperon. The neutral hyperonic effect on proton dripline is significant for lighter nuclei than for heavier ones whereas both the charged as well as neutral hyperons affect almost the entire neutron dripline.

## Key claim

The modification of neutron and proton driplines by the capture of strange hyperons depends on the hyperon charge and the choice of proton number versus net charge in the symmetry term of the mass formula; the effect is more prominent for charged hyperons on the proton dripline and for neutral hyperons on the neutron dripline, with the detailed structure of driplines differing from normal nuclei due to the combined influence of net charge, mass, and strangeness.

## Method

The authors used a generalized strangeness-dependent liquid drop model mass formula (BWMH) to calculate the binding energies of normal nuclei and hypernuclei containing neutral hyperons (Lambda, double Lambda) or charged hyperons (Cascade-minus, Theta-plus). They computed the neutron and proton separation energies for all isotopes of each element from these binding energies, then identified the dripline nuclei as the last bound nuclei before the respective separation energy becomes negative. The calculations were performed both with the proton number and with the net charge in the symmetry term to study the effect of charged hyperons.

## Key numbers

* Normal neutron drip nucleus for Zc=10 shifts from N=22 to N=24 for Lambda-hypernuclei, and for Zc=61 from N=146 to N=148.
* Normal neutron drip nucleus for Zc=8 shifts from N=18 to N=20 for double Lambda-hypernuclei, and for Zc=70 from N=170 to N=172.
* For Holmium (proton number 67), the proton dripline has N=78 for Xi-minus-hypernuclei and N=80 for Theta-plus-hypernuclei, compared to N=79 for normal nuclei.
* For Holmium (proton number 67), the neutron dripline has N=162 for Xi-minus-hypernuclei and N=164 for Theta-plus-hypernuclei, compared to N=162 for normal nuclei.
* For Zc=55, using net charge in the symmetry term for Xi-minus-hypernuclei shifts the neutron dripline from N=132 (normal and with Zc in symmetry term) to N=130, and the proton dripline from N=61 (normal) and N=60 (Zc in symmetry term) to N=59.
* For Theta-plus-hypernuclei with Zc=5, the neutron dripline shifts from N=12 (normal) to N=14; for Zc=78, from N=190 (normal) to N=194.
* For Theta-plus-hypernuclei with Zc=5, the proton dripline shifts from N=3 (normal) to N=4; for Zc=78, from N=91 (normal) to N=93.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula for light nuclei (BWM, Samanta 2002) to a generalized version (BWMH) applicable to both strange and nonstrange nuclei, as developed recently by the authors (Chowdhury, Samanta, Basu 2006). The study investigates how the addition of hyperons modifies the driplines predicted by the liquid drop model, complementing experimental efforts in hypernuclear physics (Ba90, Ta01, Saha05, step03) and theoretical work on hyperon-nucleon interactions (Keil00, Keil02). The results point to the need for more sophisticated experiments using radioactive ion beams and microscopic theoretical studies to explore the binding and structure of weakly bound systems near the driplines.
