# Chiral dynamics and peripheral transverse densities

> In the partonic (or light-front) description of relativistic systems the electromagnetic form factors are expressed in terms of frame-independent charge and magnetization densities in transverse space. This formulation allows one to identify the chiral components of nucleon structure as the peripheral densities at transverse distances b = O(M_pi^{-1}) and compute them in a parametrically controlled manner. A dispersion relation connects the large-distance behavior of the transverse charge and magnetization densities to the spectral functions of the Dirac and Pauli form factors near the two-pion threshold at timelike t = 4 M_pi^2. Using relativistic chiral effective field theory in the leading-order approximation, we (a) derive the asymptotic behavior (Yukawa tail) of the isovector transverse densities in the "chiral" region b = O(M_pi^{-1}) and the "molecular" region b = O(M_N^2/M_pi^3); (b) perform the heavy-baryon expansion; (c) explain the relative magnitude of the peripheral charge and magnetization densities in a simple mechanical picture; (d) include Delta intermediate states and study the densities in the large-N_c limit of QCD; (e) quantify the spatial region where the chiral components are numerically dominant; (f) calculate the chiral divergences of the b^2-weighted moments of the transverse densities (charge and magnetic radii) and determine their spatial support. Our approach provides a concise formulation of the spatial structure of the nucleon's chiral component and offers new insights into basic properties of the chiral expansion. It relates the information extracted from low-t elastic form factors to the generalized parton distributions probed in peripheral high-energy scattering processes.

## Key claim

The paper uses a dispersion relation to connect the large-distance behavior of the nucleon's transverse charge and magnetization densities to the spectral functions of the Dirac and Pauli form factors near the two-pion threshold, and computes these peripheral densities using leading-order relativistic chiral effective field theory, showing that the chiral component becomes numerically dominant only at surprisingly large distances b greater than about 2 fm.

## Method

The authors employ a dispersion representation of the transverse charge and magnetization densities, which expresses them as integrals of the imaginary parts (spectral functions) of the Dirac and Pauli form factors in the timelike region. They compute the spectral functions near the two-pion threshold at t = 4 M_pi^2 using leading-order relativistic chiral effective field theory, then evaluate the transverse densities via Fourier transform. The method also includes Delta isobar intermediate states and studies the densities in the large-N_c limit of QCD.

## Key numbers

- The chiral component of the isovector transverse densities becomes numerically dominant only at distances b greater than or approximately equal to 2 fm.
- The two-pion threshold is at timelike t = 4 M_pi^2.
- The "chiral" region is at transverse distances b = O(M_pi^{-1}) and the "molecular" region at b = O(M_N^2/M_pi^3).
- The empirical anomalous magnetic moments are kappa_p = 1.79 and kappa_n = -1.91.
- The chiral periphery influences form factor behavior at very low spacelike momentum transfers |t| less than approximately 10^{-2} GeV^2.
- In the large-N_c limit, the isovector magnetization density from N and Delta intermediate states gives a value 3/2 times the density from intermediate N alone.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion analysis of nucleon form factors and chiral effective field theory calculations by Gasser (1987), Bernard (1996), Becher (1999), Kubis (2000), and Kaiser (2003) to the spatial representation of transverse densities. It provides a framework for connecting low-t elastic form factor data to generalized parton distributions probed in peripheral high-energy scattering processes, as discussed by Strikman (2003, 2009, 2010). The approach enables quantification of the spatial region where chiral components dominate and offers new insights into the convergence of the heavy-baryon expansion for nucleon form factors.
