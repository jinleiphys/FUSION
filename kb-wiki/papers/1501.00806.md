# Analytical solution for the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian with sextic potential for $γ=30^{\circ}$

> An analytical solution for the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian with a sextic oscillator potential for the variable $β$ and $γ$ fixed to $30^{\circ}$, is proposed. The model is conventionally called Z(4)-Sextic. For the considered potential shapes the solution is exact for the ground and $β$ bands, while for the $γ$ band an approximation is adopted. Due to the scaling property of the problem the energy and $B(E2)$ transition ratios depend on a single parameter apart from an integer number which limits the number of allowed states. For certain constraints imposed on the free parameter, which lead to simpler special potentials, the energy and $B(E2)$ transition ratios are parameter independent. The energy spectra of the ground and first $β$ and $γ$ bands as well as the corresponding $B(E2)$ transitions, determined with Z(4)-Sextic, are studied as function of the free parameter and presented in detail for the special cases. Numerical applications are done for the $^{128,130,132}$Xe and $^{192,194,196}$Pt isotopes, revealing a qualitative agreement with experiment and a phase transition in Xe isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper presents an analytical solution for the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian with a sextic oscillator potential in the β variable and γ fixed to 30°, but the solution is exact only for the ground and β bands; for the γ band an approximation involving the average of y² is adopted to make the problem tractable.

## Method
The authors solve the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian for γ=30° by separating the angular variables from the β shape variable, solving the angular equation using Wigner functions, and transforming the β equation into a Schrödinger form with a sextic potential plus a centrifugal term. The sextic potential problem is made quasi-exactly solvable by imposing constraints on the potential coefficients and the centrifugal term factor, which allows algebraic treatment for a finite subset of eigenstates. For the γ band, the centrifugal term is approximated by replacing y² with its average value on the eigenstates of the remaining Hamiltonian for each angular momentum L.

## Key numbers
- The energy spectrum normalized to the first excited state depends on a single parameter α and the integer K, where K limits the number of analytically determined states (e.g., K=1 allows states up to L=6, K=2 up to L=10).
- The critical point for a first-order shape phase transition occurs at α_c, where the signature ratio R_{4/2}(α) reaches its absolute maximum; for K=1,2,3,4 the limiting value R_{4/2}=2 is reached at α = -0.964, -0.804, -0.666, and -0.545 respectively.
- For the special case α=0, the potential simplifies to v^K_m ∼ -4c^K_m y² + y⁶, producing parameter-free energy and B(E2) ratios.
- The staggering quantity S(4) for the γ band exceeds 0.56 for α > -2, with the highest value at α_c being very close to that of the Davydov triaxial rigid rotor model.
- The rms values for Z(4)-Sextic fits to experimental data are: 0.516, 0.478, 0.403 for ¹²⁸,¹³⁰,¹³²Xe; and 0.614, 0.543, 0.682 for ¹⁹²,¹⁹⁴,¹⁹⁶Pt.

## Context
This work extends the Z(4) model of Bonatsos et al. (2004), which used an infinite square well potential for β with γ=30°, by introducing a sextic potential that can describe spherical, deformed, or coexisting minima through continuous variation of a single parameter. The model enables systematic study of shape phase transitions in triaxial nuclei with γ rigidity, and numerical applications to ¹²⁸,¹³⁰,¹³²Xe and ¹⁹²,¹⁹⁴,¹⁹⁶Pt isotopes show qualitative agreement with experiment, revealing a phase transition in Xe isotopes. The approach also reproduces the Z(4)-β² model of M. A. Caprio (2005) in the α→∞ limit and provides a framework for describing intermediate cases between existing exactly solvable models.
