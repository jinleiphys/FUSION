# Kinetic evolution of the glasma and thermalization in heavy ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions, a highly occupied gluonic matter is created shortly after initial impact, which is in a non-thermal state and often referred to as the Glasma. Successful phenomenology suggests that the glasma evolves rather quickly toward the thermal quark-gluon plasma and a hydrodynamic behavior emerges at very early time $\sim \hat{o}(1)$ fm/c. Exactly how such "apparent thermalization" occurs and connects the initial conditions to the hydrodynamic onset, remains a significant challenge for theory as well as phenomenology. We briefly review various ideas and recent progress in understanding the approach of the glasma to the thermalized quark-gluon plasma, with an emphasis on the kinetic theory description for the evolution of such far-from-equilibrium and highly overpopulated, thus weakly-coupled yet strongly interacting glasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "apparent thermalization" of the glasma toward a hydrodynamic quark-gluon plasma within approximately 1 fm/c can be understood via kinetic theory descriptions of a weakly-coupled yet strongly interacting system, but emphasizes that a precise understanding of how the initial overpopulated glasma connects to the hydrodynamic onset remains an outstanding theoretical challenge.

## Method

The authors review the kinetic theory approach based on the Boltzmann transport equation for gluons, where the phase space distribution function evolves via collision kernels that include elastic and inelastic scattering processes. They discuss how the system transitions from classical field descriptions (valid for high occupation numbers f >> 1) to kinetic quanta descriptions (valid for weak coupling α_s << 1 and f ≤ 1/α_s), and examine both static box cases and boost-invariant longitudinally expanding cases relevant to heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s is estimated as Q_s ∼ 1-2 GeV for RHIC AuAu collisions and Q_s ∼ 2-3 GeV for LHC PbPb collisions
- The initial phase space density of gluons in the saturated regime is f ∼ 1/α_s
- The time scale for instabilities to bring quantum fluctuations to the level of background fields is τ_s ∼ (1/Q_s) ln²(1/g)
- Hydrodynamic simulations require the hydrodynamic stage to start at a very short time, typically 0.5 ∼ 1 fm/c after initial collision
- In the expanding case with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, number density evolves as n = n_0 × (t_0/t) and energy density as ε = ε_0 × (t_0/t)^{1+δ} where δ = P_L/ε

## Context

This review extends and contextualizes prior work on the thermalization problem in heavy ion collisions, including classical-statistical lattice simulations by Berges et al. (2012, 2013) and kinetic theory studies by Blaizot et al. (2011, 2013) and Kurkela et al. (2011). The paper specifically connects the kinetic evolution framework to the phenomenon of dynamical Bose-Einstein condensation predicted for overpopulated initial conditions, and discusses how these results may enable matching to anisotropic hydrodynamics frameworks such as those developed by Martinez et al. (2010, 2012).
