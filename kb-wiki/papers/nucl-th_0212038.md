# Parity Violating Interactions and Currents in the Deuteron

> We investigate parity-violating asymmetries in polarized n p radiative capture, and deuteron electro-disintegration in quasi-elastic kinematics, using the DDH model for the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction. We find dramatic cancellations between the asymmetries induced by the parity-violating interaction and those arising from the associated parity-violating pion-exchange currents. In np capture, the model-dependence of the result is nevertheless quite small because of constraints arising through the Siegert evaluation of the relevant E1 matrix element. In quasi-elastic electron scattering these processes are found to be insignificant comared to the asymmetry produced by the gamma-Z interference on individual nucleons. These two experiments, then, provide clean probes of different aspects of weak-interaction physics associated with parity violation in the np system.

# Parity Violating Interactions and Currents in the Deuteron

## Key claim
The parity-violating asymmetry in polarized np radiative capture at thermal energies is dominated by long-range pion-exchange physics and shows small model dependence due to constraints from the Siegert evaluation of the E1 matrix element, despite dramatic cancellations between the parity-violating interaction and the associated parity-violating pion-exchange currents. In deuteron electro-disintegration at quasi-elastic kinematics, the asymmetry from hadronic weak interactions is two orders of magnitude smaller than that from gamma-Z interference, making the SAMPLE experiment a clean probe of single-nucleon properties.

## Method
The authors solved the full Schrodinger equation for the np scattering state and deuteron bound state using the Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange model for the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction, combined with three different strong-interaction models: AV18, Bonn-CD, and Nijmegen-I. For the radiative capture asymmetry, they calculated the E1 transition using both the Siegert long-wavelength approximation and explicit current operators, including parity-conserving and parity-violating two-body currents from pion and rho exchange, as well as Delta-excitation and omega-pi-gamma transition currents. For the electro-disintegration asymmetry, they computed the parity-violating response function in quasi-elastic kinematics at momentum transfers of 100-300 MeV/c, including the one-body anapole current and the two-body pion-exchange current from the DDH interaction.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for np capture at thermal energies: 334.2(0.5) mb or 332.6(0.7) mb from experiment, with calculated values in good agreement across strong interaction models
- Parity-violating asymmetry from the Siegert form of the E1 operator: -5.02 x 10^-8 for the AV18 interaction with DDH best-guess weak pion-nucleon coupling
- Parity-violating asymmetry from the full current operator (sum of interaction and current contributions): +0.17 x 10^-8 for the same AV18 interaction, showing the large cancellation
- SAMPLE experiment kinematics: Q^2 equal to 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 and 0.043 (GeV/c)^2
- The asymmetry from gamma-Z interference is two orders of magnitude larger than that from hadronic weak interactions at SAMPLE kinematics

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of parity violation in the np system by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and previous theoretical work on np capture asymmetries, while updating and sharpening earlier predictions by Hwang (1980, 1981) that did not include parity-violating currents. The results directly support interpretation of the ongoing LANSCE experiment measuring the photon asymmetry in low-energy np radiative capture and the SAMPLE experiments at MIT-Bates facility (Hasty, 2000) measuring parity-violating asymmetries in deuteron electro-disintegration. The finding that two-nucleon mechanisms are negligible in the SAMPLE kinematics enables reliable extraction of single-nucleon weak form factors from those measurements, while the np capture asymmetry provides a clean probe of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant that has been controversial due to conflicting results from fluorine-18 and cesium-133 experiments.
