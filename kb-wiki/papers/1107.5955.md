# QCD sum rules for the baryon octet in nuclear matter

> The baryon self-energies are expressed in terms of the QCD condensates of the lowest dimension in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter within the QCD sum-rule approach. The self-energies are shown to satisfy the Gell-Mann--Okubo relations in the linear SU(3) breaking approximation. The results are in qualitative agreement with those obtained by the standard nuclear physics methods.

## Key claim
The baryon self-energies in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are expressed in terms of the lowest-dimension QCD condensates within the gas approximation, and the results are shown to satisfy the Gell-Mann–Okubo relations in the linear SU(3) breaking approximation, but the numerical results are limited to the case of nonstrange nuclear matter (protons and neutrons only) and are studied as functions of the scalar quark condensate, with the hedge that the approach can be extended to arbitrary baryon mixtures.

## Method
The finite-density QCD sum rule approach is extended to the full baryon octet by expressing the in-medium correlation function in terms of vacuum expressions plus terms linear in nuclear matter density, using the gas approximation for the quark and gluon condensates. The Borel-transformed sum rules for the three Lorentz structures (q, I, p) are solved to extract the baryon effective masses and vector self-energies, with the OPE coefficients depending on the invariant s = (p+q)^2 and the density-dependent continuum threshold. The coefficients for the linear density terms are derived from the vector and scalar quark condensates and the gluon condensate, with the baryon-specific flavor coefficients tabulated for each octet member.

## Key numbers
- Potential depth of Λ hyperon: U_Λ ≈ -30 MeV at saturation density ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Potential depth of Ξ hyperon: U_Ξ ≈ -18 MeV
- Strange quark mass: m_s ≈ 150 MeV with 20% uncertainty
- Strange quark condensate parameter: γ = -0.2 (default value)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ ≈ 45 MeV (conventional) or σ ≈ 60 MeV (latest), corresponding to κ ≈ 8 or κ ≈ 11 respectively
- Strange quark content parameter: y = 0.08 (default value used in the paper)
- Isospin-symmetric vector condensate combination: v = v_u + v_d = 3
- Isospin-asymmetric vector condensate combination: v^- = v_u - v_d = 1
- Isospin-asymmetric scalar condensate: ζ = 0.54 (from PCQM)
- Vacuum baryon masses from fit: m_N = 0.934 GeV, m_Λ = 1.103 GeV, m_Σ = 1.104 GeV, m_Ξ = 1.207 GeV
- Vacuum continuum thresholds: W_N^2 = 2.119 GeV^2, W_Λ^2 = 3.069 GeV^2, W_Σ^2 = 3.157 GeV^2, W_Ξ^2 = 3.729 GeV^2
- Borel mass fiducial interval: 0.8 GeV^2 < M^2 < 1.4 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends the finite-density QCD sum rule approach of Drukarev (2011) from the nucleon to the full baryon octet, including the Ξ hyperon for the first time in this framework. It complements earlier sum rule calculations for Λ and Σ hyperons by Jin (1994, 1995), Zhong (2007), and Li (2008) by focusing on broken SU(3) symmetry effects and extending to asymmetric nuclear matter, which is relevant for neutron star equation of state calculations. The results are in qualitative agreement with standard nuclear physics methods such as the relativistic mean-field approximation (Glendenning 1991, Schaffner 1996) and the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach (Schulze 1998, 2006; Yamamoto 2000), and provide a framework for calculating hyperon in-medium potentials without relying on phenomenological baryon parameters.
