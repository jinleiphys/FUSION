# Saturation of Elliptic Flow and the Transport Opacity of the Gluon Plasma at RHIC

> Differential elliptic flow and particle spectra are calculated taking into account the finite transport opacity of the gluon plasma produced in Au+Au at Ecm ~ 130 A GeV at RHIC. Covariant numerical solutions of the ultrarelativistic Boltzmann equation are obtained using the MPC parton cascade technique. For typical pQCD (~3 mb) elastic cross sections, extreme initial gluon densities, dN/deta ~ 15000, are required to reproduce the elliptic flow saturation pattern reported by STAR. However, we show that the solutions depend mainly on the transport opacity, $χ=\int dz σ_tρ_g$, and thus the data can also be reproduced with dN/deta ~ 1000, but with extreme elastic parton cross sections, \~45 mb. We demonstrate that the spectra and elliptic flow are dominated by numerical artifacts unless parton subdivisions ~100-1000 are applied to retain Lorentz covariance for RHIC initial conditions.

# Saturation of Elliptic Flow and the Transport Opacity of the Gluon Plasma at RHIC

## Key claim

The observed saturation of elliptic flow at high transverse momentum at RHIC can be reproduced by covariant Boltzmann transport solutions, but only if the transport opacity is sufficiently high; the data do not uniquely determine the initial gluon density or the parton cross section separately, because the solutions depend primarily on the transport opacity parameter χ = ∫ dz σ_t ρ_g rather than on either quantity alone.

## Method

The authors solved the ultrarelativistic Boltzmann equation for a massless gluon gas with elastic 2→2 scattering using the MPC parton cascade technique, with Debye-screened pQCD matrix elements. Initial conditions were taken as a longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken tube in local thermal equilibrium at proper time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c, with transverse density proportional to the binary collision distribution for Woods-Saxon nuclear profiles, normalized to HIJING minijet spectra for Au+Au at √s = 130A GeV. Parton subdivision factors of 100–5000 were applied to eliminate Lorentz-violating numerical artifacts.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon density from HIJING: dN(0)/dη = 210, with T₀ = 700 MeV
- Typical pQCD elastic gg cross section: ~3 mb
- To reproduce STAR elliptic flow with 3 mb cross section requires extreme initial gluon density: dN_g/dη ~ 15000
- Alternatively, with dN_g/dη ~ 1000, requires extreme elastic cross section: ~45 mb
- Transport opacities needed to quantitatively reproduce STAR data: χ_{b=0} ~ 8–14
- For χ_{b=0} = 47.8, with dN_g(0)/dη = 1000, corresponds to σ_t ≈ 14 mb and σ₀ ≈ 45 mb
- Parton subdivisions of ~100–1000 required to retain Lorentz covariance for RHIC initial conditions
- At χ ~ 14–16, quenching reaches a factor of ten suppression at p⊥ > 6 GeV
- For b = 8 fm and χ = 9.74, particle subdivision of ℓ ~ 200–250 required

## Context

This work extends earlier covariant transport studies by Zhang (1999) and Molnar (2001) by computing p⊥-differential elliptic flow, investigating hadronization effects, and using realistic diffuse nuclear geometry. It contrasts with ideal hydrodynamic predictions by Kolb (2000) which fail to saturate at high p⊥, and with string-based hadronic cascade models by Sorge (1999) and Bleicher (2000) which underpredict elliptic flow. The results enable extraction of the transport opacity of the gluon plasma from combined elliptic flow and spectra measurements, and motivate development of inelastic 2↔3 partonic processes in covariant transport.
