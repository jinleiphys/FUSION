# On the role of magnetic field in photon excess in heavy ion collisions

> Synchrotron photon spectrum in heavy-ion collisions is computed taking into account the spatial and temporal structure of magnetic field. It is found that a significant fraction of photon excess in heavy-ion collisions in the region $k_\bot=1-3$GeV can be attributed to the synchrotron radiation. Azimuthal anisotropy of the synchrotron photon spectrum is characterized by the Fourier coefficients $v_2=4/7$ and $v_4=1/10$ that are independent of photon momentum and centrality.

## Key claim

A significant fraction of the observed photon excess in heavy-ion collisions at transverse momenta \(k_\bot = 1-3\) GeV can be attributed to synchrotron radiation from the quark-gluon plasma, but the mechanism cannot account for all of the excess; the azimuthal anisotropy coefficients \(v_2 = 4/7\) and \(v_4 = 1/10\) are derived analytically and are independent of photon momentum and centrality.

## Method

The synchrotron photon emission rate for a relativistic quark in a constant magnetic field was transformed to the laboratory frame and integrated over the quark momentum distribution in a thermal plasma at temperature \(T\). The spatial and temporal structure of the magnetic field was modeled using an analytic expression derived from the induced field in a conducting medium, with the field depending on impact parameter \(b\), electrical conductivity \(\sigma\), and time \(t\). The photon multiplicity spectrum was then computed by integrating the emission rate over the plasma volume and lifetime up to freeze-out time \(t_f = 10\) fm, and the azimuthal Fourier coefficients were extracted analytically in the high-\(k_\bot\) limit.

## Key numbers

- Photon excess attributed to synchrotron radiation is significant in the region \(k_\bot = 1-3\) GeV at central rapidity.
- Azimuthal anisotropy coefficients: \(v_2 = 4/7\) (approximately 0.57) and \(v_4 = 1/10\) (0.10), independent of photon momentum and centrality.
- Magnetic field peak strength at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is estimated as \(eB = m_\pi^2\).
- Freeze-out time used in the calculation: \(t_f = 10\) fm.
- Centralities considered: \(0\%-20\%\) (impact parameter \(b = 4.3\) fm) and \(40\%-60\%\) (\(b = 10.2\) fm).
- Temperatures used in the calculation: \(T = 200\) MeV and \(T = 400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constant-field approximation used in Tuchin (2012) and Tuchin (2010) by incorporating the realistic space-time dependence of the magnetic field generated in heavy-ion collisions, as modeled in Tuchin (2013). The results address the longstanding puzzle of photon excess and large azimuthal asymmetry observed by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare 2014, Adare 2011), which traditional approaches such as those by Linnyk (2013), van Hees (2011), and Shen (2013) have not fully resolved. The findings enable future studies that incorporate time-dependent plasma temperature and electrical conductivity, as well as realistic hydrodynamic flow, to refine the synchrotron contribution.
