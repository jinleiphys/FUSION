# Anharmonicity of multi-octupole-phonon excitations in $^{208}$Pb: analysis with multi-reference covariant density functional theory and subbarrier fusion of $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb

> We discuss anharmonicity of the multi-octupole-phonon states in $^{208}$Pb based on a covariant density functional theory, by fully taking into account the interplay between the quadrupole and the octupole degrees of freedom. Our results indicate the existence of a large anharmonicity in the transition strengths, even though the excitation energies are similar to those in the harmonic limit. We also show that the quadrupole-shape fluctuation significantly enhances the fragmentation of the two-octupole-phonon states in $^{208}$Pb. Using those transition strengths as inputs to coupled channels calculations, we then discuss the fusion reaction of $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier. We show that the anharmonicity of the octupole vibrational excitation considerably improves previous coupled-channels calculations in the harmonic oscillator limit, significantly reducing the height of the main peak in the fusion barrier distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multi-octupole-phonon states in 208Pb exhibit large anharmonicity in transition strengths even though their excitation energies remain close to the harmonic limit, and that this anharmonicity significantly improves coupled-channels calculations for the 16O+208Pb fusion reaction by reducing the height of the main peak in the fusion barrier distribution.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference covariant density functional theory calculations using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the PC-F1 relativistic energy functional, superposing quantum-number projected mean-field reference states with quadrupole (β2) and octupole (β3) deformation parameters. They first examined one-dimensional octupole vibrations by freezing β2=0, then extended to two-dimensional (β2, β3) deformation mixing. The calculated transition strengths were used as inputs to semi-microscopic coupled-channels calculations solved with the code CCFULL to compute fusion cross sections and barrier distributions for 16O+208Pb.

## Key numbers

- The spin-average excitation energy for two-octupole-phonon states is 9.6 MeV, approximately twice the one-octupole phonon energy of 4.3 MeV
- The spin-average excitation energy for three-octupole-phonon states is 13.1 MeV, approximately three times the one-octupole phonon energy
- The B(E3; 3−1 → 0+1) value from the octupole-quadrupole calculation is consistent with 21.93 W.u. from Gogny D1S force calculations
- The B(E1) value from the first 4+ state to the first 5− state is 1.5×10−4 W.u., compared to the experimental upper bound of 1.0×10−4 W.u.
- The empirical coupling strength β=0.144 was used, estimated from the measured B(E3) strength with radius parameter r0=1.1 fm
- The internuclear potential parameters used were V0=178 MeV, R0=0.978×(161/3+2081/3) fm, and a=1.005 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multi-octupole-phonon excitations in 208Pb that have been experimentally searched for by Yeh (1996, 1998), Vetter (1998), and Valnion (2001), and theoretically predicted to show strong fragmentation by Ponomarev (1999) and Brown (2000). The results address a long-standing problem where previous coupled-channels calculations for 16O+208Pb fusion by Morton (1999), Esbensen (2007), and YHR12 overestimated the main peak height in the fusion barrier distribution. The findings enable future systematic reexamination of nuclear vibrations with fully microscopic multi-reference density functional approaches based on both nonrelativistic and relativistic energy functionals.
