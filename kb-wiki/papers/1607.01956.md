# Systematic Comparison of Tsallis Statistics for Charged Pions Produced In $pp$ Collisions

> The energy dependence of Tsallis statistics parameters is presented for charged pions produced at beam energies ranging between 6.3 GeV and 7 TeV. It is found that deviations from Boltzmann statistics are monotonically growing with beam energy. This may be attributed to the dominance of low $x$ at higher energies leading to the power-like NLO QCD spectra for which Tsallis statistics may serve as effective theory. At the same time, for larger $x$ at lower beam energies the Gaussian falloff of transverse-momentum-dependent distributions is crucial and correspondent effective description is provided by the Boltzmann distribution.

## Key claim

The paper finds that deviations from Boltzmann statistics for charged pions in pp collisions grow monotonically with beam energy, but this is presented as an effective description: the Tsallis distribution may serve as an effective theory for the power-like NLO QCD spectra that dominate at low x (high energies), while at lower beam energies the Gaussian falloff of transverse-momentum-dependent distributions makes the Boltzmann distribution the appropriate effective description.

## Method

The authors fit the Tsallis single-particle distribution function (Eq. 1) to experimental transverse momentum distributions of negatively charged pions from the NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, and CMS collaborations, covering center-of-mass energies from 6.3 GeV to 7 TeV. The fits yield the three parameters of the Tsallis distribution: temperature T, radius R (related to volume), and the non-extensivity parameter q, with the chemical potential set to zero. The quality of each fit is quantified by chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

*   The parameter q for negatively charged pions increases with beam energy, following the parametrization q = 1 + ln( (sqrt(s)/sqrt(s0))^a0 ), with a0 = 0.0219 +/- 0.0014 and sqrt(s0) = 0.7805 +/- 0.3673 GeV.
*   At the lowest energy, sqrt(s) = 6.3 GeV (NA61/SHINE), the fit yields T = 96.76 +/- 8.69 MeV, R = 4.431 +/- 0.344 fm, and q = 1.0449 +/- 0.0223.
*   At the highest energy, sqrt(s) = 7000 GeV (CMS), the fit yields T = 68.53 +/- 2.62 MeV, R = 5.090 +/- 0.182 fm, and q = 1.1987 +/- 0.0057.
*   The temperature T slowly decreases with beam energy, from approximately 97 MeV at 6.3 GeV to approximately 69 MeV at 7 TeV.
*   The parameter q is bounded by the theoretical constraint q < 4/3, derived from the convergence of the energy density integral for massless particles.

## Context

This work extends previous Tsallis analyses of charged hadrons at LHC energies (Sorin 2015) down to the lower beam energies of the NA61/SHINE experiment, and specifically uses pion data rather than charged hadron data to obtain a better fit. The results are compared to a blast-wave description of heavy-ion collisions (prl2012) which gives a similar kinetic freeze-out temperature of T_kin = 95 +/- 10 MeV. The energy dependence of the parameters suggests a transition from Boltzmann-dominated behavior at low energies to Tsallis-dominated behavior at high energies, which the authors attribute to the increasing role of low-x perturbative QCD.
