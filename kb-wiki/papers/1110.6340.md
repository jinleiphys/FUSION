# The gluon propagator close to criticality

> The deconfinement phase transition of pure Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature is reflected in the behavior of gauge-fixed gluonic correlation functions. This is one of many examples of how physical information can be extracted from gauge-dependent correlations which is not always obvious. Here, we study the behavior of the Landau-gauge gluon propagator of pure SU(2) across the phase transition in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions in order to assess to what extend the corresponding critical behavior is reflected in these correlations. We discuss why it should emerge from a continuum perspective and test our expectations in lattice simulations. A comparison with SU(3) furthermore reveals quite clear indications for a sensitivity of the gluon propagator to the order of the transition.

## Key claim

The Landau-gauge gluon propagator should reflect critical scaling near the deconfinement phase transition, but the presence of a non-perturbative Gribov screening mass at all temperatures interferes with the critical signal, making it difficult to extract clean critical exponents from lattice data.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of pure SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge in both 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions, using the minimal Landau gauge condition. They computed the electric (longitudinal) gluon propagator and extracted its screening mass as a function of reduced temperature across the phase transition, then fitted the screening mass to an Ansatz combining a constant Gribov mass with a critical scaling term proportional to |t|^(gamma/2). They also compared with SU(3) results to assess sensitivity to the order of the transition.

## Key numbers

- For 3d SU(2) on a 4x128^2 lattice: fitted exponents gamma_+ = 1.39(+10/-9) above T_c and gamma_- = 1.134(+2/+3) below T_c, compared to the 2d Ising universality class value gamma = 1.75
- For 3d SU(2) on a 4x256^2 lattice: fitted exponents gamma_+ = 1.39(+12/-10) and gamma_- = 1.02(+3/-3)
- For 4d SU(2) on a 4x46^3 lattice: fitted exponents gamma_+ = 1.26(+11/-16) and gamma_- = 0.19(+0.43/+378), compared to the 3d Ising universality class value gamma = 1.2372(5)
- The electric screening mass at the phase transition remains non-zero, with values around 0.22-0.37 GeV depending on lattice volume in 3d, and around 0.25 GeV in 4d

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of gauge-fixed gluon propagators at finite temperature by Cucchieri et al. (2007) and Fischer et al. (2010), using a significantly refined temperature mesh and increased statistics for SU(2) in four dimensions, and presenting the first such investigation for SU(2) in three dimensions. The analysis builds on the functional renormalization group framework of Braun et al. (2007) and Marhauser et al. (2008), which showed that the effective potential of the Polyakov loop can be computed from gauge-fixed propagators and must exhibit critical scaling. The results enable future work to disentangle the Gribov mass contribution from critical scaling through finite-size scaling analyses across multiple volumes and discretizations.
