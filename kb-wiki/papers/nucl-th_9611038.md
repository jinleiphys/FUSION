# Transverse Radial Expansion and Directed Flow

> The effects of an interplay of radial expansion of the thermalized system created in a heavy ion collision and directed flow are discussed. It is shown that the study of azimuthal anisotropy of particle distribution as a function of rapidity and transverse momentum could reveal important information on both radial and directed flow.

# Transverse Radial Expansion and Directed Flow

## Key claim

The paper argues that the interplay between azimuthally symmetric radial expansion and directed flow in heavy ion collisions produces characteristic shapes in the first harmonic coefficient \(v_1\) as a function of transverse momentum, and that measuring this dependence can separately reveal information about both the radial expansion velocity and the directed flow velocity. The analysis is limited to cases where the directed flow velocity is small (\(\beta_a \leq 0.1\)), where the derived analytic formula is accurate at the level of a few percent.

## Method

The author derives a general analytic expression for \(v_1(p_t)\) by applying a Lorentz boost to an azimuthally symmetric source distribution in its rest frame, assuming small directed flow velocity. This formula is then applied to three specific models: a non-relativistic radially expanding thermal source, a relativistic generalization with transverse rapidity, and a thermalized spherical shell model. In each case, the invariant distribution in the source rest frame is integrated over the orientation of the radial expansion velocity, and the resulting \(v_1(p_t)\) is expressed analytically in terms of modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- For protons treated non-relativistically, the slope of \(v_1(p_t)\) without radial expansion is \(0.5 \beta_a / T\), where \(\beta_a\) is the directed flow velocity and \(T\) is the temperature.
- The condition for \(v_1(p_t)\) to become negative at low transverse momentum is \(m \beta_0^2 / 2 > T\); for \(T = 100\) MeV this requires \(\beta_0 > 0.46\).
- The non-relativistic treatment of protons is restricted to the region \(p_t \leq 0.5\) GeV.
- The directed flow velocity is expected to be \(\beta_a \leq 0.1\).
- In the spherical shell model, the expansion velocity needed to produce a dip in \(v_1(p_t)\) at low \(p_t\) is about 15% larger than in the transverse-plane-only expansion model.

## Context

This work extends the Fourier decomposition approach to flow analysis introduced by Voloshin and Zhang (1996) and the study of event shape anisotropy as a function of transverse momentum by Zhang and Wessels (1995) and Li, Ko, and Li (1996). The paper provides a theoretical framework for interpreting the preliminary results of the E877 Collaboration (Chang, 1996) in gold-gold collisions at the AGS, which suggested that a regime of strong radial expansion with anti-flow of low-transverse-momentum particles could be realized. The analysis enables experimental extraction of both radial expansion and directed flow parameters from the \(v_1(p_t)\) shape, and is complementary to two-particle interferometry studies by Heinz (1996) and Csorgo and Lorstad (1996).
