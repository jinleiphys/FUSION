# Meson-Meson and Meson-Baryon Interactions in a chiral Non-perturbative Approach

> A qualitative account of the meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions using chiral Lagrangians and the inverse amplitude method in coupled channels is done. The method, imposing exact unitarity, proves to be very useful tool to extend the information contained in the chiral Lagrangians at energies beyond the realm of applicability of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The inverse amplitude method generalized to coupled channels, using chiral Lagrangians up to \({\cal O}(p^4)\), can extend the applicability of chiral perturbation theory to energies up to about 1.2 GeV, generating resonances such as the \(\sigma\), \(f_0(980)\), \(a_0(980)\), \(\rho(770)\), and \(K^*\) dynamically, but the approximation that replaces the full \({\cal O}(p^4)\) amplitude with a polynomial tree-level term plus a loop term works well only for \(J=0\) and requires a separate treatment for higher partial waves.

## Method

The authors generalize the inverse amplitude method to coupled channels by expanding the inverse of the \(T\) matrix in powers of momentum, using the \({\cal O}(p^2)\) and \({\cal O}(p^4)\) chiral Lagrangians, and imposing exact unitarity via the relation \({\rm Im}\, T^{-1} = -{\rm Im}\, G\). For the meson-meson sector, they approximate the full \({\cal O}(p^4)\) amplitude by a polynomial tree-level contribution \(T_4^P\) (with fitted coefficients \(\hat{L}_i\)) plus a term \(T_2 \cdot {\rm Re}\, G \cdot T_2\), leading to the formula \(T = T_2 \cdot [T_2 - T_4^P - T_2\cdot G \cdot T_2]^{-1} \cdot T_2\). For the \(J=0\) meson-baryon sector (\(\bar{K}N\) interactions), they use only the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian as a potential in a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with a cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff for meson-meson calculations: \(q_{\rm max} = 1.02\) GeV.
- Cutoff for \(\bar{K}N\) calculations: \(q_{\rm max} = 630\) MeV, with \(f = 1.15 f_\pi\).
- The method reproduces meson-meson data up to \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 1.2\) GeV for the \((I,J)=(0,0)\), \((1,1)\), and \((2,0)\) channels.
- The \(\sigma\) resonance appears as a pole at \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 500\) MeV with a width of around 400 MeV.
- The single-channel IAM yields good results up to 1 GeV for most channels, but only up to 700 MeV for the \((0,0)\) channel.
- The \(\eta\) channels in the \(\bar{K}N\) calculation change some cross sections by about a factor three compared to calculations omitting them.

## Context

This work unifies two earlier non-perturbative approaches: the single-channel inverse amplitude method (IAM) of Truong (1988) and the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach of Oller, Oset, and collaborators (1997). It extends the IAM to coupled channels, enabling the description of resonances like the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) that were absent in the single-channel IAM. The method enables subsequent studies of reactions such as \(\gamma\gamma \rightarrow MM\), \(\phi \rightarrow \gamma K^0 \bar{K}\), and \(K^- p \rightarrow \gamma \Lambda, \gamma \Sigma^0\) at energies beyond the reach of standard chiral perturbation theory.
