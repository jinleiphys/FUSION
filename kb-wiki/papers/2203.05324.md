# Shape coexistence in Sr isotopes

> Sr isotopes are located in the mass region $A\approx 100$, where a very quick onset of nuclear deformation exists, being other notable examples of this area Yb, Zr, and Nb nuclei. The presence of the proton subshell closure $Z=40$ allows the existence of particle-hole excitations that produces low-lying intruder bands. Purpose: The goal of this work is the study of the nuclear structure of the even-even $^{92-102}$Sr isotopes through the accurate description of excitation energies, $B(E2)$ transition rates, nuclear radii and two-neutron separation energies. Method: The interacting boson model with configuration mixing will be the framework to calculate all the observables of the Sr isotopes. Only two types of configurations will be considered, namely, 0particle-0hole and 2particle-2hole excitations. The parameters of the model are determined using a least-squares procedure for the excitation energies and the $B(E2)$ transition rates. Results: For the whole chain of isotopes, the value of excitation energies, $B(E2)$'s, two-neutron separation energies, nuclear radii, and isotope shifts have been obtained, with a good agreement between theory and experiment. Also, a detailed analysis of the wave functions have been performed and, finally, the mean-field energy surfaces and the value of the nuclear deformation, $β$, have been obtained. Conclusions: The presence of low-lying intruder states in even-even Sr isotopes have been confirmed and its connection with the onset of deformation has been clarified. Lightest Sr isotopes present a spherical structure while the heaviest ones are clearly deformed. The rapid onset of deformation at neutron number $60$ is due to the crossing of the regular and intruder configurations and, moreover, both families of states present an increase of deformation with the neutron number.

## Key claim

The rapid onset of nuclear deformation at neutron number 60 in even-even Sr isotopes is due to the crossing of regular (spherical) and intruder (deformed) configurations, with both families of states showing an increase in deformation with neutron number, but the authors note that the regular sector parameters for isotopes beyond A=98 could not be fixed from experimental data and were assumed from ⁹⁸Sr, making those results an extrapolation.

## Method

The authors employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), considering only 0p-0h (regular) and 2p-2h (intruder) proton excitations across the Z=40 subshell closure. The Hamiltonian parameters were determined through a least-squares fitting procedure targeting experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates for the even-even ⁹²⁻¹⁰²Sr isotopes, with the constraint that parameters vary smoothly across the isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- The mixing parameter ω between regular and intruder configurations is fixed at 15 keV for all isotopes studied (⁹²⁻¹⁰²Sr)
- The energy needed to excite two protons across the Z=40 shell gap, Δ^(N+2), drops from 1900 keV for ⁹²Sr to 1360 keV for ⁹⁸⁻¹⁰²Sr
- For ⁹⁸Sr, the experimental B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺) is 96(3) W.u., reproduced theoretically as 96 W.u.
- For ⁹⁸Sr, the experimental B(E2; 4₁⁺→2₁⁺) is 124(+10/-9) W.u., with theoretical value 147 W.u.
- For ⁹⁸Sr, the experimental B(E2; 6₁⁺→4₁⁺) is 174.8(18) W.u., with theoretical value 156.9 W.u.
- For ¹⁰⁰Sr, the experimental B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺) is 103(5) W.u., reproduced theoretically as 103 W.u.
- For ⁹⁶Sr, the experimental B(E2; 0₂⁺→2₁⁺) is 15.3(16) W.u., with theoretical value 9.90 W.u.
- The mixing between the first two 0⁺ states in ⁹⁸Sr was experimentally determined as 9%, and for the two 2⁺ states as 1.3% (Park et al., 2016)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IBM-CM analysis of even-even Zr isotopes (García-Ramos et al., 2019, 2020) to the Sr isotopic chain, which exhibits an even more dramatic onset of deformation at N=60. The study builds on the Federman-Pittel mechanism (Federman and Pittel, 1977, 1979) emphasizing the role of neutron-proton spin-orbit partner occupation, and complements large-scale shell-model calculations (Togashi et al., 2016) and mean-field approaches using Skyrme and Gogny interactions (Rodríguez-Guzmán et al., 2010; Nomura et al., 2016). The results enable future experimental studies to test the predicted low-lying 0⁺ states in ⁹⁴Sr and the extrapolated behavior of regular states in ¹⁰⁰⁻¹⁰²Sr.
