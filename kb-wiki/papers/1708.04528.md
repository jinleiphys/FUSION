# Addendum: Muon-neutrino-induced charged current pion production

## Key claim
The GiBUU transport theory, without any special tuning, can simultaneously describe T2K pion production data on a CH target per nucleon and previously published MINERvA data, despite the well-known "pion-production puzzle" where MiniBooNE data disagree with both in shape and magnitude.

## Method
GiBUU, a quantum-kinetic transport theory based on a non-equilibrium Green's function method, was used to calculate cross sections for incoherent charged-current pion production on CH targets. The calculations applied the experimental kinematical cutoffs (muon and pion momentum > 0.2 GeV, cosine of muon and pion angles > 0.2) and used the T2K neutrino beam flux prediction from 2016. Cross sections per nucleon were obtained by summing per-nucleon cross sections for carbon and hydrogen and dividing by 13.

## Key numbers
- Pion momentum spectrum peak for a carbon target lies at about 0.15 GeV, just below the main absorption region
- The experimental lower pion momentum cutoff at 0.2 GeV eliminates the peak region, meaning the experiment sees only a small part of the total pion production cross section
- Coherent production is expected to add strength at small Q² < 0.2 GeV²
- Pion production makes up about 1/2 to 2/3 of the total cross section at DUNE

## Context
This addendum extends the authors' prior work (Mosel and Gallmeister, Phys. Rev. C96, 015503, 2017) which showed that GiBUU could simultaneously describe T2K data on H₂O and MINERvA data on CH, despite the incompatibility of MiniBooNE data with both. The present work adds comparisons to new T2K data on a CH target at the near detector, finding the same degree of agreement. The authors note that the MINERvA analysis (Eberly et al., 2014) used cuts on invariant mass (W < 1.4 GeV) and required reconstruction of Q² and energy transfer, introducing generator dependence. The work enables future tests with T2K data still under analysis and highlights the need for experiments with larger phase-space coverage, particularly for ⁴⁰Ar targets at DUNE energies, where recent ArgoNeuT data show good agreement with GiBUU but other generators overpredict cross sections.
