# Prompt photons in heavy ion collisions at the LHC: A ''multi-purpose'' observable

> I emphasize in this contribution how prompt photons can be used to probe nuclear parton densities as well as medium-modified fragmentation functions in heavy ion collisions. Various predictions in p-A and A-A collisions at LHC energies are given.

## Key claim

Prompt photons in proton-nucleus collisions can be related to nuclear parton density ratios with an accuracy better than 5%, and in nucleus-nucleus collisions the suppression of fragmentation photons due to parton energy loss in the medium may be observable, provided the nuclear parton distribution functions are sufficiently well known.

## Method

The author computes the nuclear production ratio of isolated prompt photons in p-Pb collisions at LHC energies using next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD with the de Florian and Sassot (nDSg) nuclear parton distributions, and compares the full calculation to an analytic approximation that expresses the ratio as a combination of the nuclear structure function ratio and the gluon density ratio. For Pb-Pb collisions, the author calculates the inclusive photon nuclear modification factor assuming both only nuclear parton distribution effects and including additional parton energy loss in the medium with a characteristic gluon energy scale of 50 GeV.

## Key numbers

- In p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV, the nuclear production ratio R_pPb for isolated photons at mid-rapidity (y=0) is computed and shown as a function of x_T = 2 p_T / sqrt(s_NN).
- The analytic approximation for the ratio at y=0 is given by R_approx(x_T, y=0) = 0.5 * R_F2(x_T) + 0.5 * R_G(x_T), where R_F2 is the nuclear structure function ratio and R_G is the nuclear gluon density ratio.
- The analytic approximation for the ratio at forward rapidity y=3 is R_approx(x_T, y=3) = R_G(x_T e^{-y}).
- In Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, the inclusive photon nuclear modification factor R_PbPb is computed assuming parton energy loss with omega_c = 50 GeV, showing significant suppression compared to the case with only nuclear parton distribution effects.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of prompt photon production in nuclear collisions, building on the established agreement between NLO perturbative QCD and existing data from fixed-target to collider energies reviewed by Aurenche (2006). The author shows that prompt photons in p-A collisions can constrain nuclear parton distribution functions, which are currently poorly known beyond the high-x (x > 10^-2) and low Q^2 (Q^2 < 100 GeV^2) region probed by fixed-target experiments. The predictions for photon quenching in Pb-Pb collisions enable future experimental tests of medium-modified fragmentation functions at the LHC, and the author also points to earlier work by Arleo (2004, 2006) on gamma-pion momentum-imbalance distributions as a complementary probe.
