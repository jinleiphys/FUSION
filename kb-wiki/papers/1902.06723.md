# Lattice-based equation of state at finite baryon number, electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials

> We construct an equation of state for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature and chemical potentials for baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$. We use the Taylor expansion method, up to the fourth power for the chemical potentials. This requires the knowledge of all diagonal and non-diagonal $BQS$ correlators up to fourth order: these results recently became available from lattice QCD simulations, albeit only at a finite lattice spacing $N_t=12$. We smoothly merge these results to the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, to be able to reach temperatures as low as 30 MeV; in the high temperature regime, we impose a smooth approach to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. We provide a parameterization for each one of these $BQS$ correlators as functions of the temperature. We then calculate pressure, energy density, entropy density, baryonic, strangeness, electric charge densities and compare the two cases of strangeness neutrality and $μ_S=μ_Q=0$. We also calculate the isentropic trajectories and compare them in the two cases. Our equation of state can be readily used as an input of hydrodynamical simulations of matter created at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The authors construct a QCD equation of state at finite temperature and chemical potentials for baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness using a Taylor expansion up to fourth order, but the lattice QCD input data are only available at a finite lattice spacing of \(N_t=12\) and the Taylor expansion is limited to chemical potentials \(\mu_B \lesssim (2-2.5) T\).

## Method

The pressure is expanded as a Taylor series in \(\mu_B/T\), \(\mu_Q/T\), and \(\mu_S/T\) up to total power four, using all diagonal and non-diagonal BQS correlators from lattice QCD simulations on \(48^3 \times 12\) lattices in the temperature range 135–220 MeV. Each correlator is smoothly merged at low temperatures with the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model down to 30 MeV and at high temperatures with the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, parameterized as a ratio of polynomials in inverse temperature (except \(\chi_2^B\) which uses an exponential-tanh form). From the parameterized pressure, the energy density, entropy density, baryon density, strangeness density, electric charge density, and speed of sound are derived.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD data used from \(48^3 \times 12\) lattices in the temperature range 135 MeV \(< T <\) 220 MeV.
- HRG model used to extend the equation of state down to temperatures as low as 30 MeV.
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for \(\chi_2^B = 1/3\), \(\chi_2^Q = 2/3\), \(\chi_2^S = 1\).
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for \(\chi_4^B = 2/(9\pi^2)\), \(\chi_4^Q = 4/(3\pi^2)\), \(\chi_4^S = 6/\pi^2\).
- Taylor expansion valid for \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 2-2.5\), corresponding to collision energies \(\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 10\) GeV.
- Isentropic trajectories shown for \(s/n_B = 420, 144, 70, 30\), corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200, 62.4, 27, 14.5\) GeV respectively.
- Speed of sound calculated along freeze-out lines with \(T_{FO}(\mu_B=0) = 160\) MeV and 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD equations of state that only considered finite baryon chemical potential (e.g., Borsanyi (2012), Bazavov (2014)) by including electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials simultaneously. It provides a parameterized form usable in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, enabling studies of strangeness-dependent observables such as \(\Lambda\) polarization and kaon flow harmonics, and addressing questions about flavor hierarchy of freeze-out temperatures and the chiral magnetic effect.
