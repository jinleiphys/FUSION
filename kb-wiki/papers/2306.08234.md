# Radial and Non-Radial Oscillations of Inverted Hybrid Stars

> We study the radial and non-radial oscillations of Cross stars (CrSs), i.e., stars with a quark matter crust and a hadronic matter core in an inverted order compared to conventional hybrid stars. We draw comparisons of their oscillation modes with those of neutron stars, quark stars, and conventional hybrid stars. We find that the stellar stability analysis from the fundamental mode of radial oscillations, and the $g$, $f$ modes of non-radial oscillations are quite similar to those of conventional hybrid stars. However, due to the inverted stellar structure, the first non-radial $p$ mode of CrSs behaves in an inverted way and sits in a higher frequency domain compared to that of conventional hybrid stars. These results provide a direct way to discriminate CrSs from other types of compact stars via gravitational-wave probes. Specifically, compact stars emitting $g$-mode gravitational waves within the $0.5$-$1$ kHz range should be CrSs or conventional hybrid stars rather than neutron stars or pure quark stars, and a further GW detection of the first $p$ mode above 8 kHz or an identification of a decreasing trend of frequencies versus star masses associated with it will help identify the compact object to be a CrS rather than a conventional hybrid star.

# Radial and Non-Radial Oscillations of Inverted Hybrid Stars

## Key claim
Cross stars (CrSs), which have a quark matter crust and a hadronic matter core in inverted order compared to conventional hybrid stars, can be distinguished from other compact stars through gravitational-wave asteroseismology: the first non-radial p-mode of CrSs sits in a higher frequency domain (above 8 kHz) and shows a decreasing trend of frequencies with increasing mass, unlike conventional hybrid stars where p-mode frequencies always grow with mass.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for static spherically symmetric stars using a combined equation of state with a hadronic core (APR model) and a quark matter crust (parametrized by bag constant B and QCD correction parameter a_4 for both udQM and SQM hypotheses). Radial oscillation eigenfrequencies were obtained by solving the infinitesimal radial perturbation equations with shooting methods under both slow-conversion and rapid-conversion junction conditions at the phase interface. Non-radial quadrupole (ell=2) oscillation modes (g, f, and p_1 modes) were computed using the Cowling approximation, where fluid perturbations are solved on a fixed background metric.

## Key numbers
- g-mode gravitational waves from CrSs lie within the 0.5-1 kHz range, within the sensitive band of current gravitational-wave detectors
- The first p-mode (p_1) frequencies of CrSs sit in the 7-10 kHz range, with values above 8 kHz serving as a discriminant from conventional hybrid stars (which have p_1 modes at 4-7 kHz for stars with maximum masses above 2 solar masses)
- f-mode frequencies of CrSs lie in the 1.4-2.8 kHz range, between those of neutron stars and pure quark stars
- For CrSs with bag constant B=50 MeV/fm^3, the lowest g-mode frequencies are around 0.5 kHz
- The Cowling approximation differs from full general relativistic treatments by less than 20% for f modes, around 10% for p modes, and 5-10% for g modes
- Variations in hadronic equations of state (e.g., adding hyperons and Delta baryons) affect results at the O(10%) level

## Context
This work extends the concept of Cross stars (CrSs) introduced by Zhang (2022) and compares their oscillation properties to those of neutron stars, pure quark stars (Bodmer 1971, Witten 1984, Terazawa 1979), and conventional hybrid stars (Alford 2004, 2013). The study builds on prior asteroseismology work showing that g-mode gravitational waves can serve as discriminating signatures for hybrid stars (Flores 2013, Kumar 2021, Zhao 2022) and that radial oscillation stability criteria differ between slow and rapid phase conversions (Pereira 2017, Lugones 2021). The results enable gravitational-wave probes from LIGO/VIRGO/KAGRA to potentially identify CrSs as a distinct class of compact objects, with the inverted p-mode frequency trend providing a specific observational signature not present in conventional hybrid stars.
