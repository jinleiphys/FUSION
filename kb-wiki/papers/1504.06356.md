# Small-$x$ evolution of jet quenching parameter

> Concept of transverse deflection probability of a parton that travels through strongly interacting medium, recently introduced by D'Eramo, Liu and Rajagopal, have been used to derive high energy evolution equation for the jet quenching parameter in stochastic multiple scatterings regime. Jet quenching parameter, ${\hat q}(x)$, appears to evolve with $x$, with an exponent $0.9{\bar α}_s$, which is slightly less than that of $x {\cal G} (x)$ where ${\cal G} (x)$ is the gluon distribution function.

## Key claim

The jet quenching parameter $\hat q(x)$ evolves with small-$x$ with an exponent $0.9\bar\alpha_s$, which is slightly less than that of $x\mathcal{G}(x)$ where $\mathcal{G}(x)$ is the gluon distribution function, but this result is derived under the assumption that the $S$-matrices retain a Gaussian form in the dipole transverse size during high-energy evolution, which the author notes is a "fairly crude approximation of the actual evolution."

## Method

The author adopts the concept of transverse deflection probability $P(k_\perp)$ of a parton traveling through a strongly interacting medium, introduced by D'Eramo, Liu and Rajagopal (2010), and relates momentum broadening to the $S$-matrix of a dipole. The Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation for the $S$-matrix evolution is then used to derive the high-energy evolution equation for the jet quenching parameter in the stochastic multiple scatterings regime, with power-counting techniques from Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory (SCET) used to identify leading contributions.

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter evolves as $\hat q(x) \propto (1/x)^{0.9\bar\alpha_s}$, where $\bar\alpha_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi$.
- The exponent $0.9\bar\alpha_s$ is slightly less than that of $x\mathcal{G}(x)$, the gluon distribution function.
- In the single-scattering limit, the double logarithmic enhancement yields $\Delta\hat q = (\bar\alpha_s/2)\,\hat q(0)\,\log^2(Q_s^2/(\hat q l_0))$, where $Q_s$ is the saturation momentum, $L$ is the medium length, and $l_0$ is the size of the medium constituents.
- The saturation momentum and jet quenching parameter are approximately related as $\hat q_0 \sim 4 Q_{s0}^2/L$ at $Y\sim0$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of transverse deflection probability introduced by D'Eramo, Liu and Rajagopal (2010) and applies the BK equation to derive the energy evolution of the jet quenching parameter. It recovers the double logarithmic enhancement first derived by Liou, Mueller and Wu (2013) and studied subsequently by Blaizot et al. (2013), Iancu et al. (2014), and Blaizot et al. (2014) as a special case in the single-scattering limit. The result enables future exploration of the evolution deep inside the saturation region and when the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect modifies the spectrum, which may further suppress the evolution.
