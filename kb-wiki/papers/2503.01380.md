# $Z=14$ Magicity Revealed by the Mass of the Proton Dripline Nucleus $^{22}$Si

> Using the $Bρ$-defined isochronous mass spectrometry technique, we conducted the first mass measurement of the proton dripline nucleus $^{22}$Si. We confirm that $^{22}$Si is bound against particle emission with $S_p/S_{2p}=+1412(114)/+229(54)$ keV, fixing the proton dripline location for the Si element. By analyzing the mass differences of the neighboring $sd$-shell nuclei, we find that $^{22}$Si exhibits a doubly-magic character similar to its mirror partner $^{22}$O, and that the mirror energy difference of $^{22}$Si-$^{22}$O deviates from the predictions assuming mirror symmetry. Gamow shell-model calculations reveal that the average occupations of valence protons in $^{22}$Si are nearly identical to those of valence neutrons in $^{22}$O, supporting the $Z=14$ magicity in $^{22}$Si. The observed mirror-symmetry breaking is attributed to the extended proton distribution in $^{22}$Si arising from a small contribution of the unbound $\pi2s_{1/2}$ orbital.

## Key claim

The first mass measurement of the proton dripline nucleus 22Si confirms it is bound against particle emission, and the analysis of mass differences reveals that 22Si exhibits a doubly-magic character at Z=14 similar to its mirror partner 22O, though the mirror energy difference deviates from predictions assuming perfect mirror symmetry.

## Method

The Bρ-defined isochronous mass spectrometry (Bρ-IMS) technique was employed at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL), using 36Ar15+ projectiles at E/A≈445 MeV bombarding a 15-mm beryllium target to produce fragments via projectile fragmentation. The fragments were separated with the RIBLL2 fragment separator and injected into the CSRe storage ring tuned to isochronous mode with γt=1.381, where revolution times and velocities were measured using two time-of-flight detectors installed 18 m apart. The mass-to-charge ratios were determined by combining the measured velocities with magnetic rigidities derived from the isochronous condition, using 15 known-mass nuclei as references.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 22Si: ME = 31827(54) keV, determined from 17 events
- One-proton separation energy of 22Si: Sp = +1412(114) keV
- Two-proton separation energy of 22Si: S2p = +229(54) keV
- Mass excess of 23Si: ME = 23365(16) keV, with precision improved by a factor of seven compared to previous measurements
- The new mass of 22Si deviates by approximately 1.8 MeV from the AME20 extrapolation value of 33640(500) keV
- 22Si is bound against two-proton emission at a confidence level of 4.2σ

## Context

This work extends the study of shell closures at the proton dripline, where previous investigations have focused on neutron-rich regions showing new magic numbers such as N=14,16 in 22,24O (Thirolf, 2000; PhysRevLett.84.5493) and N=32,34 in 52,54Ca (PhysRevLett.114.202501). The measurement fixes the proton dripline location for the Si element, which was previously uncertain, and enables systematic studies of mirror symmetry breaking between 22Si and 22O. The results support the prediction from ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations (Li, 2023) that 22Si is a doubly-magic nucleus, and the observed mirror-symmetry breaking is attributed to the extended proton distribution in 22Si arising from a small contribution of the unbound π2s1/2 orbital.
