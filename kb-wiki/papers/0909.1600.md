# Neutron halo in deformed nuclei

> Halo phenomena in deformed nuclei are investigated within a deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (DRHB) theory. These weakly bound quantum systems present interesting examples for the study of the interdependence between the deformation of the core and the particles in the halo. Contributions of the halo, deformation effects, and large spatial extensions of these systems are described in a fully self-consistent way by the DRHB equations in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis with the proper asymptotic behavior at large distance from the nuclear center. Magnesium and neon isotopes are studied and detailed results are presented for the deformed neutron-rich and weakly bound nucleus $^{44}$Mg. The core of this nucleus is prolate, but the halo has a slightly oblate shape. This indicates a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core. The generic conditions for the occurence of this decoupling effects are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a deformed neutron halo can exist in the deformed neutron-rich nucleus $^{44}$Mg, and that the halo can have a shape (slightly oblate) that is decoupled from the shape of the core (prolate). This contradicts earlier expectations that halos in deformed drip-line nuclei are unlikely or that deformation effects would be washed out at large distances.

## Method

The authors solved the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DRHB) equations in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis, which provides proper asymptotic behavior at large distances from the nuclear center. The calculations used the NL3 density functional and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force, with parameters fitted to reproduce the proton pairing energy of $^{20}$Mg from a spherical RHB calculation with the Gogny force D1S. The neutron density was decomposed into a "core" part (orbitals with canonical single-particle energies below -2.5 MeV) and a "halo" part (orbitals with energies above -1 MeV, including weakly bound and continuum states).

## Key numbers

- $^{44}$Mg has a quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.32$ and a two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n} = 0.44$ MeV.
- The neutron chemical potential is $\lambda_\mathrm{n} = -230$ keV.
- The rms radius of the neutron core is 3.72 fm; the rms radius of the neutron halo is 5.86 fm.
- The quadrupole moment of the neutron core is 160 fm$^2$ (prolate); the quadrupole moment of the halo is -27 fm$^2$ (oblate).
- The average number of neutrons that are weakly bound or in the continuum is 4.34; of these, 2.92 are in weakly bound orbits 3 and 4.
- The occupation probabilities of the 5th ($\Omega^\pi = 7/2^-$) and 6th ($\Omega^\pi = 1/2^-$) orbitals are 27.2% and 14.3%, respectively.
- The $p$ wave components for the 3rd and 4th orbitals are 66% and 80%, respectively.
- $^{36}$Ne has $\beta_2 = 0.52$; $^{38}$Ne has $\beta_2 = -0.24$.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of halos in deformed nuclei (Li 1996, Pei 2006, Nakada 2008) and directly contradicts conclusions from Hamamoto (2004) that all $\Omega^\pi = 1/2^+$ levels lose deformation at near-zero binding, and from Nunes (2005) that correlations inhibit deformed halos. The paper also builds on the spherical Woods-Saxon basis method developed by Zhou (2003a) and on earlier spherical RHB calculations for halos (Meng 1996, Poschl 1997, Meng 1998b). It enables future studies of the coupling between deformation and weak binding in other quantum many-body systems such as Rydberg atoms.
