# Core Polarization and Tensor Coupling Effects on Magnetic Moments of Hypernuclei

> The effects of core polarization and tensor coupling on the magnetic moments in $^{13}_Λ$C, $^{17}_Λ$O, and $^{41}_Λ$Ca $Λ$-hypernuclei are studied in the Dirac equation with scalar, vector and tensor potentials. It is found that the effect of core polarization on the magnetic moments is suppressed by $Λ$ tensor coupling. The $Λ$ tensor potential reduces the spin-orbit splitting of $p_Λ$ states considerably. However, almost the same magnetic moments are obtained using the hyperon wave function obtained via the Dirac equation either with or without the $Λ$ tensor potential in the electromagnetic current vertex. The deviations of magnetic moments for $p_Λ$ states from the Schmidt values are found to increase with nuclear mass number.

## Key claim

Core polarization effects on magnetic moments of Lambda hypernuclei are strongly suppressed when Lambda tensor coupling is included in the electromagnetic current vertex, but the tensor potential itself has a negligible effect (less than 0.1%) on the magnetic moments despite significantly reducing the spin-orbit splitting of p_Lambda states.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials for the Lambda hypernuclei 13_Lambda C, 17_Lambda O, and 41_Lambda Ca, including a tensor potential term for the Lambda hyperon. The polarized electromagnetic current from the nuclear core was calculated using perturbation theory in symmetric nuclear matter, and the magnetic moments were obtained by summing the polarized Dirac magnetic moment and the anomalous magnetic moment, with and without tensor coupling in the electromagnetic vertex.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for s_Lambda states: -11.1 MeV (13_Lambda C), -13.1 MeV (17_Lambda O), and -19.2 MeV (41_Lambda Ca)
- Spin-orbit splitting of p_Lambda states reduced from 1.15, 1.40, and 1.10 MeV to 0.19, 0.25, and 0.23 MeV for 13_Lambda C, 17_Lambda O, and 41_Lambda Ca respectively when tensor potential is included
- Magnetic moment for 1s_1/2 state in 13_Lambda C: -0.611 n.m. (with tensor coupling), compared to Schmidt value of -0.613 n.m.
- Magnetic moment for 1p_3/2 state in 41_Lambda Ca: -0.667 n.m. (with tensor coupling), compared to Schmidt value of -0.613 n.m.
- Polarized Dirac magnetic moment for 1s_1/2 state in 13_Lambda C: 1.12 x 10^-4 n.m. (with tensor coupling), compared to -395 x 10^-4 n.m. without tensor coupling
- Ratio B_f/rho_N decreases from around 1.45 fm^3 to a constant value of 1.26 fm^3 for rho_N approaching zero

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic calculations of hypernuclear magnetic moments by Cohen and Furnstahl (1987) and Mares and Zofka (1990) by including, for the first time, the tensor potential in the Dirac equation alongside tensor coupling effects on the electromagnetic current. The results confirm the earlier finding by Gattone, Chiapparini, and Izquierdo (1991) that tensor coupling suppresses core polarization effects for s-state Lambda hyperons, but show that the tensor potential itself has negligible impact on magnetic moments despite being crucial for explaining the small Lambda spin-orbit interaction noted by Jennings (1990). The calculations call for experimental measurements of hypernuclear magnetic moments to test these predictions.
