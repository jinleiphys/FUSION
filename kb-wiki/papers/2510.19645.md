# Engineering the shapes of quark-gluon plasma droplets by comparing anisotropic flow in small symmetric and asymmetric collision systems

> The observation of collective flow phenomena in small collision systems challenges our understanding of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation and evolution. This complexity lies in the initial geometries, which are influenced by both nucleon configuration and subnucleonic fluctuations, introducing uncertainties in interpreting flow patterns. We disentangle these contributions through comparative measurements of elliptic ($v_2$) and triangular ($v_3$) flow in asymmetric $d$+Au and symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, which produce medium of comparable sizes but with vastly different initial geometries. The larger $v_2$ in $d$+Au reflects its dominant elliptic geometry, while the similar $v_3$ in both systems is better explained by considering subnucleonic fluctuations. These contrasting flow patterns are quantitatively described by a state-of-the-art hydrodynamic model tuned to large-system Au+Au data, indicating efficient transformation of initial geometries to final-state anisotropies. These results provide evidence for droplet formation in small systems with transport properties that are similar to those observed in large collision systems, consistent with QGP-like behavior.

## Key claim

By comparing elliptic ($v_2$) and triangular ($v_3$) flow in asymmetric $d$+Au and symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the authors disentangle the contributions of nucleon configuration and subnucleonic fluctuations to initial collision geometry, finding that the contrasting flow patterns are quantitatively described by a hydrodynamic model tuned to large-system Au+Au data, consistent with QGP-like behavior in small systems.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured elliptic flow $v_2$ and triangular flow $v_3$ using two-particle correlations (with a pseudorapidity gap $|\Delta\eta|>1.0$ to suppress non-flow) and four-particle correlations in $d$+Au and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The observables $v_n\{2\}$ and $v_n\{4\}$ were obtained from the moments $\langle v_n^2\rangle$ and $\langle v_n^4\rangle$ via two- and four-particle correlations, with non-flow contributions subtracted using low-multiplicity events. Initial-state eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$ were calculated using the PHOBOS Glauber model with nucleon configurations from the Hulthen function (deuteron) and three ab initio models (NLEFT, PGCM, AFDMC) for oxygen, with subnucleonic fluctuations modeled by treating each nucleon as three constituent quarks. The measured flow coefficients were compared to a hydrodynamic model (MUSIC + UrQMD) with parameters constrained by Bayesian inference from Au+Au data.

## Key numbers

- $v_2\{2\}$ reaches nearly 0.06 in $d$+Au collisions, about 40% larger than in O+O collisions at comparable multiplicity
- $v_3\{2\}$ values agree between systems and reach up to 0.015 in central O+O collisions
- $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\} \approx 0.9$ in $d$+Au collisions, while in O+O collisions this ratio decreases with $\nch$ from 0.8 to 0.5
- $v_2^{\mathrm{OO}}\{4\}\approx 0.5 v_2^{\mathrm{OO}}\{2\}$ in central O+O collisions, compared to $v_2^{d\mathrm{Au}}\{4\}\approx 0.95 v_2^{d\mathrm{Au}}\{2\}$
- Dataset includes approximately 500 million minimum bias and 380 million high multiplicity O+O events, and 76 million minimum bias and 70 million high multiplicity $d$+Au events
- Charged particles selected within $0.2 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/$c$ and $|\eta| < 1.5$
- Systematic uncertainties: less than 2% for $v_2\{2\}$, 5% for $v_3\{2\}$, and 10% for $v_2\{4\}$ from event/track selection; less than 2% for $v_2\{2\}$, 5% for $v_3\{2\}$, and 5% for $v_2\{4\}$ from trigger bias

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of anisotropic flow in small asymmetric systems such as $p$+Au, $d$+Au, and $^{3}$He+Au at RHIC (PHENIX 2018, STAR 2022, STAR 2023) by introducing a comparative approach with symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions. The comparison enables disentangling nucleon configuration effects from subnucleonic fluctuations, which previous studies (Schenke 2021, Huang 2025) identified as a key uncertainty. The results rule out scenarios where initial-state momentum correlations (Schenke 2019) play a significant role, and the agreement with hydrodynamic models tuned to Au+Au data (Jahan 2025) provides evidence for QGP droplet formation in small systems. The availability of O+O data at both RHIC and LHC (ATLAS 2025, ALICE 2025, CMS 2025) opens opportunities to study the origin of flow across a wide energy range.
