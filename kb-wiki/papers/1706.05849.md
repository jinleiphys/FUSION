# Violation of Positivity Bounds in Models of Generalized Parton Distributions

> As with parton distributions, flexible phenomenological parameterizations of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are essential for their extraction from data. The large number of constraints imposed on GPDs make simple Lorentz covariant models viable; but, such models are often incomplete in that they employ the impulse approximation. Using the GPD of the pion as a test case, we show that the impulse approximation can lead to violation of the positivity bound required of GPDs. We focus on a particular model of the pion bound-state vertex that was recently proposed and demonstrate that satisfying the bound is not guaranteed by Lorentz covariance. Violation of the positivity bound is tied to a problematic mismatch between the behavior of the quark distribution at the endpoint and the crossover value of the GPD.

## Key claim

The impulse approximation, while Lorentz covariant, is insufficient to describe higher Fock components self-consistently, and the positivity bound on generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can be violated in such models; the authors demonstrate this using a particular model of the pion bound-state vertex proposed by Fanelli (2016), showing that satisfying the bound is not guaranteed by Lorentz covariance alone.

## Method

The authors computed the double distributions (DDs) for a covariant model of the pion that employs a non-point-like Bethe-Salpeter vertex with a built-in regulator mass m_R, then obtained the pion GPD H(x,ξ,t) from the DDs via the standard integral relation. They tested the positivity bound by forming the ratio R(x,ξ) = |H(x,ξ,0)| / sqrt[q(x_f) q(x_i)] for x > ξ, where x_i and x_f are the momentum fractions of the struck quark before and after the interaction, and also tested a weaker bound using the arithmetic mean of the quark distributions. The light-front wavefunction representation was derived from the Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction to analyze the origin of the violation.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m = 0.220 GeV and regulator mass m_R = 1.192 GeV, adopted as central values from Fanelli (2016)
- The two-body Fock state accounts for 84% of the quark distribution in the model
- The average momentum fraction in the full model is ⟨x⟩ = 0.47, while in the two-body sector ⟨x⟩^(2) = 0.50
- The positivity bound is violated in a window ξ < x < x_m(ξ), where the turn-over point x_m(ξ) ~ 0.95 depends mildly on ξ
- As ξ → 0, the approach to saturation of the bound is from above rather than below, opposite to the required behavior

## Context

This work extends the line of investigation from Fanelli (2016), which introduced a covariant smearing of the Bethe-Salpeter vertex to cure ultraviolet divergences, contrasting with earlier point-like vertex models such as those in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio framework (Theussl, 2002; Broniowski, 2007). The authors argue that unlike point-like models where a particular Pauli-Villars subtraction maintains the positivity bound (Broniowski, 2007), the non-point-like Ansatz arises from a non-trivial kernel whose contributions must be omitted in the impulse approximation, leading to positivity violation. This work enables future tests of consistency for GPDs obtained from light-front solutions of Bethe-Salpeter wavefunctions via the Nakanishi integral representation (Karmanov, 2005; Carbonell, 2008, 2017) and from Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches (Nguyen, 2011; Mezrag, 2014).
