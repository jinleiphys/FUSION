# Cross Sections for A(e,e')X Reactions

> Recent JLAB measurements of inclusive (e,A) cross sections as a function of outgoing lepton momentum may provide information on the nucleon spectral function in targets that will be relevant for the planned DUNE neutrino long-baseline experiment. They also provide an important testing ground for neutrino generators. We have therefore used the transport theoretical framework and code GiBUU to calculate the cross sections for the targets C, Ar and Ti. We compare the calculations with the experimental data. The overall agreement is good. Relatively small discrepancies appear mainly on the low-electronenergy side of the QE-peak where QE and 2p2h excitation processes overlap.

# Cross Sections for A(e,e')X Reactions

## Key claim
The GiBUU transport-theory framework reproduces inclusive electron-nucleus cross sections for C, Ar, and Ti at 2.222 GeV and 15.541 degrees with good overall agreement, but a persistent discrepancy appears on the low-electron-energy side of the quasielastic peak where quasielastic and 2p2h excitation processes overlap, amounting to roughly a 5% shift in energy transfer.

## Method
The authors used the GiBUU transport-theoretical framework and code to calculate inclusive (e,e') cross sections for carbon, argon, and titanium targets. The ground state was prepared using a density- and momentum-dependent mean-field potential from an energy-density functional, with inelastic excitations described by nucleon resonances (MAID07 analysis) and DIS processes (PYTHIA), while 2p2h contributions were taken from a global fit to electron scattering data on carbon scaled by mass number A. Final state interactions used the same potential as the ground state, and no special parameter tuning was applied.

## Key numbers
- Incident electron energy: 2.222 GeV; scattering angle: 15.541 degrees for all three targets
- For carbon, argon, and titanium, the theory gives too much strength for energy transfers of about 200–300 MeV on the low-electron-energy side of the quasielastic peak
- The necessary correction to improve agreement would amount to shifting the energy transfer by only about 20 MeV, which is approximately 5% of the 350 MeV energy transfer in the quasielastic region
- The cross sections per proton for all three targets scale closely with Z, with the carbon curve somewhat narrower and higher in the quasielastic peak compared to argon and titanium

## Context
This work extends GiBUU calculations to new JLAB inclusive electron scattering data on C, Ar, and Ti (Dai 2018), providing a crucial test for neutrino event generators such as GENIE, NEUT, and NuWro that will be used in the DUNE experiment. Prior calculations for carbon used the spectral function method (Dai 2018) and for argon used the Relativistic Green's Function method (Dai 2018), but neither covered all three targets nor included inelastic components. The results enable future comparisons with other generators and inform the extraction of nucleon spectral functions from inclusive electron scattering data.
