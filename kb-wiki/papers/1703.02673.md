# Improved Quark Coalescence for a Multi-Phase Transport Model

> The string melting version of a multi-phase transport model is often applied to high-energy heavy-ion collisions since the dense matter thus formed is expected to be in parton degrees of freedom. In this work we improve its quark coalescence component, which describes the hadronization of the partonic matter to a hadronic matter. We removed the previous constraint that forced the numbers of mesons, baryons, and antibaryons in an event to be separately conserved through the quark coalescence process. A quark now could form either a meson or a baryon depending on the distance to its coalescence partner(s). We then compare results from the improved model with the experimental data on hadron $dN/dy$, $p_{_{\rm T}}$ spectra, and $v_2$ in heavy-ion collisions from $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=62.4$ GeV to $5.02$ TeV. We show that, besides being able to describe these observables for low-$p_{_{\rm T}}$ pions and kaons, the improved model also better describes the low-$p_{_{\rm T}}$ baryon observables in general, especially the baryon $p_{_{\rm T}}$ spectra and antibaryon-to-baryon ratios for multistrange baryons.

## Key claim

The authors improve the quark coalescence component of the string-melting AMPT model by removing the previous constraint that forced separate conservation of meson, baryon, and antibaryon numbers per event; a quark now can form either a meson or a baryon depending on the distance to its coalescence partner(s), which better describes low-\(p_{\rm T}\) baryon observables and antibaryon-to-baryon ratios for multistrange baryons.

## Method

The authors modify the quark coalescence algorithm in the string-melting AMPT model so that for each available quark the code concurrently searches for the nearest antiquark (potential meson partner) and the two nearest quarks (potential baryon partners), then decides hadron type by comparing the average three-quark distance \(d_B\) to the quark-antiquark distance \(d_M\) scaled by a new parameter \(r_{BM}=0.61\). They compare the improved model's results for hadron \(dN/dy\), \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra, and elliptic flow \(v_2\) against experimental data from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=62.4\)–\(200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(2.76\) and \(5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers

* The new coalescence parameter \(r_{BM}\) is set to \(0.61\) to reasonably describe proton \(dN/dy\) yields at midrapidity for central Au+Au at \(200\) GeV and central Pb+Pb at \(2.76\) TeV.
* The parton cross section is set to \(1.5\) mb (instead of the earlier \(3\) mb) to better describe overall \(v_2\) data.
* Lund string fragmentation parameters: \(a=0.55\) for Au+Au at \(200\) GeV, \(a=0.20\) for Pb+Pb at \(2.76\) TeV; \(b=0.15\) GeV\(^{-2}\) for both.
* Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s=0.33\); upper limit for strange-to-nonstrange quark production ratio is \(0.40\).
* In the old coalescence, (anti)baryons at midrapidity form about \(2.6\) fm/c later than mesons; the new coalescence forms them much earlier.
* The improved model reproduces charged pion and kaon \(dN/dy\) and \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra below \(p_{\rm T}\approx 2\) GeV at both RHIC and LHC energies.
* The improved model gives lower proton yields and harder \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra than the old model, better matching data at both \(200\) GeV and \(2.76\) TeV.
* Antibaryon-to-baryon ratios for \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\) from the new coalescence are below or near one and agree with experimental data, whereas the old model gave ratios well above one.

## Context

This work extends the string-melting AMPT model (Lin 2004) by fixing a long-standing failure to describe proton \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra and rapidity distributions (Ma 2016) and antibaryon-to-baryon ratios for multistrange baryons. The improvement removes the artificial separate conservation of meson, baryon, and antibaryon numbers that existed in earlier versions (Lin 2014, Ma 2016). The new coalescence enables better description of bulk matter observables across RHIC and LHC energies and is suitable for future studies of baryon number fluctuations near the phase transition.
