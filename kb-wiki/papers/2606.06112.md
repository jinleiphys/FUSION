# Reply to the Comment on "Partial conservation of seniority in semi-magic nuclei"

## Key claim

The Comment raises no scientific errors in the review's treatment of Neergård (2022), only issues of emphasis and phrasing; the subspace decomposition approach remains of limited additional insight unless a unique operator is identified that can directly generate the partially seniority-conserved states, which currently does not exist.

## Method

The Reply analyzes the Comment's objections point by point, examining the mathematical structure of the 18-dimensional M-scheme basis for four identical particles in a single j=9/2 shell. It compares the subspace decomposition of Neergård (2022) with the symbolic shell-model framework of Qian and Qi (2018), demonstrating that angular-momentum projection, seniority projection, and Hamiltonian diagonalization can all isolate the partially seniority-conserved states without requiring the specific transformation proposed in the Comment.

## Key numbers

- There are exactly 18 allowed states with different angular momentum and seniority values for a system of four identical particles in a single j=9/2 shell, corresponding to 18 basis vectors with total magnetic quantum number M=0 in the M-scheme representation.
- The full 18-dimensional M=0 space decomposes into Φ₄₀ (dimension 14) and Φ₄₀⊥ (dimension 4).
- The basis vectors of Φ₄₀⊥ were obtained by solving 14 homogeneous linear equations in 18 unknowns arising from orthogonality to Φ₄₀.

## Context

This Reply addresses the Comment by Kai (2026) on the review by Qi (2026), which discussed the work of Neergård (2022) on partial conservation of seniority in semi-magic nuclei. The Reply extends the symbolic shell-model framework of Qian and Qi (2018) and clarifies that while the subspace decomposition of Neergård (2022) is mathematically correct, the central open challenge remains the identification of a unique operator that projects out the partially seniority-conserved α₁ states directly, analogous to the pair creation operator Pⱼ† for the v=0 state or the angular-momentum projection operator Pᴹᴵ for states with good I.
