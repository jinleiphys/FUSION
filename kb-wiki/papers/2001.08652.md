# Mass Yields of Fission Fragment of Pt to Ra Isotopes

> An effective Fourier nuclear shape parametrization which describes well the most relevant degrees of freedom on the way to fission is used to construct a 3D collective model. The potential energy surface is evaluated within the macroscopic-microscopic approach based on the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) macroscopic energy and Yukawa-folded single particle potential. A phenomenological inertia parameter is used to describe the kinetic properties of the fissioning system. The fission fragment mass yields are obtained by using an approximate solution of the underlying Hamiltonian. The predicted mass fragmentations for even-even Pt to Ra isotopes are compared with available experimental data. Their main characteristics are well reproduced when the neck rupture probability dependent on the neck radius is introduced.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a three-dimensional collective model using Fourier shape parametrization, a macroscopic-microscopic potential, and a phenomenological neck-rupture probability can reproduce the main characteristics of fission fragment mass yields for even-even Pt to Ra isotopes, but the agreement is qualitative and the model systematically underestimates asymmetric yields in some cases (e.g., Hg isotopes) and overestimates symmetric yields in others (e.g., Ra isotopes).

## Method

The authors constructed a 3D collective model with elongation (q2), mass asymmetry (q3), and neck size (q4) as collective degrees of freedom. The potential energy surface was computed using the macroscopic-microscopic approach with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) macroscopic energy and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential for shell and pairing corrections. The fission fragment mass yields were obtained by solving the collective Hamiltonian approximately, using a Wigner function for the probability distribution in the (q3,q4) plane and introducing a Gaussian neck-rupture probability dependent on the neck radius, with two adjustable parameters: zero-point energy E0 and half-width d.

## Key numbers

- The zero-point energy parameter E0 was fixed at 1 MeV for all calculations.
- The half-width parameter d in the neck-rupture probability was fitted to d = 1.5 fm for Hg-Ra isotopes, compared to d ≈ 1.1 fm previously used for Pu isotopes in Pomorski et al. (2018).
- The potential energy surface was evaluated on a 4D grid with q2 ranging from -0.60 to 2.35 in steps of 0.05, q3 from 0.00 to 0.21 in steps of 0.03, and q4 from -0.21 to 0.21 in steps of 0.03.
- For 184Hg, the first saddle point was found at q2 = 1.28 and q3 = 0, and the second saddle at q2 = 1.69 and q3 = 0.03.
- The pairing strength was set to G N^(2/3) = 0.28 ħω0, with ħω0 = 41/A^(1/3) MeV.
- The Strutinsky shell-correction method used a 6th-order polynomial with smearing width γS = 1.2 ħω0.
- Experimental data for Rn and Ra isotopes corresponded to fission at excitation energy E* ≈ 18 MeV, corresponding to initial temperature T ≈ 0.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the model previously applied to Pu isotopes in Pomorski et al. (2018) to the lighter Pt-Ra region, where fission barriers are significantly higher (potential energy difference between saddle and scission of order a few MeV, about one order of magnitude smaller than in actinides). The results are compared with experimental data from Els et al. (2013), Prasad et al. (2015), Gorbachev et al. (2013), Ghys et al. (2014), Ramos et al. (2013), and Schmitt et al. (2000). The model predicts asymmetric fission as the most probable mode in light Hg isotopes, contrary to estimates by Andreev et al. (2013), and the authors note that more advanced models including energy dissipation and particle evaporation (e.g., Langevin dynamics or improved quantum molecular dynamics) would be needed for more precise results.
