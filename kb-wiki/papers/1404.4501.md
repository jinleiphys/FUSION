# Femtoscopy correlations of kaons in $Pb + Pb$ collisions at LHC within hydrokinetic model

> We provide, within the hydrokinetic model, a detailed investigation of kaon interferometry in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV). Predictions are presented for 1D interferometry radii of $K^0_SK^0_S$ and $K^{\pm}K^{\pm}$ pairs as well as for 3D femtoscopy scales in out, side and long directions. The results are compared with existing pion interferometry radii. We also make predictions for full LHC energy.

## Key claim
The hydrokinetic model predicts that kaon interferometry radii for charged and neutral kaons are very similar, and that there is no \(m_T\)-scaling between pion and kaon radii at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, contrary to simple analytical models that predict such scaling under certain conditions. The absence of scaling is attributed to strong transverse flow, but a \(k_T\)-scaling does emerge for \(k_T > 0.4-0.5\) GeV.

## Method
The hydrokinetic model (HKM) simulates heavy-ion collisions through three stages: ideal hydrodynamic expansion, gradual decoupling via hydrokinetics, and a hadronic cascade (UrQMD). The initial conditions use a Glauber Monte Carlo energy density profile (GLISSANDO) with event-averaged initial conditions, a lattice-QCD inspired equation of state, and a chemical freeze-out temperature of 165 MeV. Kaon correlation functions are computed for central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 5.125 TeV, and 1D and 3D Gaussian fits are performed to extract interferometry radii.

## Key numbers
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the maximal initial energy density is \(\epsilon_0 = 1300\) GeV/fm\(^3\) and the initial transverse flow coefficient is \(\alpha = 0.45\) fm.
- The transverse momentum range for kaon pairs is \(0.14 < p_T < 1.5\) GeV/\(c\) and pseudorapidity range is \(|\eta| < 0.8\).
- For full LHC energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.125\) TeV, the interferometry radii for kaons are \(4-7\%\) lower and for pions are \(2-4\%\) lower than at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The suppression parameter \(\lambda_{inv}\) for kaons decreases with \(k_T\) in the 1D case, and is significantly larger than for pions due to reduced resonance decay contributions.

## Context
This work extends the hydrokinetic model (HKM) previously applied to pion femtoscopy at RHIC and LHC energies (Shapoval et al., 2013; hHKM) to kaon interferometry, providing predictions for ALICE data. It contradicts simple analytical models (MakSin, Sinyuk) that predict \(m_T\)-scaling for pions and kaons under negligible transverse flow, and instead shows that strong transverse flow (AkkSin) breaks this scaling. The results enable quantitative tests of the freeze-out dynamics and the role of resonance decays (e.g., \(K^*(892)\) and \(\phi(1020)\)) in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.
