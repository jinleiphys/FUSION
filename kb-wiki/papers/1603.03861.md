# Photodisintegration cross section of $^9$Be up to 16 MeV in the $α$ + $α$ + n three-body model

> The photodisintegration of $^9$Be in the energy region lower than $E_γ= 16$ MeV is investigated by using the $α$~+~$α$~+~$n$ three-body model and the complex scaling method. The cross section exhibits two aspects in the different two energy regions. In the low energy region up to $E_γ= 6$ MeV, the cross section is explained by the transition strengths into the excited resonant states of $^9$Be, while the dipole transition into the non-resonant continuum states of $^8$Be(2$^+$)~+~$n$ dominates the cross section in the energy region of $6 \le E_γ\le 16$ MeV. Furthermore, it is shown that the dipole strength at $E_γ\sim 8$ MeV is understood to be caused by the single-neutron excitation from the $^8$Be(2$^+$)~$\otimes$~$νp_{3/2}$ configuration in the ground state.

## Key claim
The photodisintegration cross section of $^9$Be up to 16 MeV is well reproduced by an $\alpha$ + $\alpha$ + $n$ three-body model, but the calculation slightly underestimates the observed peak at $E_\gamma \sim 8$ MeV, suggesting contributions from degrees of freedom beyond the relative motion of the three-body system, such as 1p-1h excitations from the $\alpha$ particle that are not included in the model.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for the $\alpha$ + $\alpha$ + $n$ three-body system using the orthogonality condition model with the KKNN potential for $\alpha$-$n$ interactions and a folding potential for $\alpha$-$\alpha$ interactions, plus a three-body potential $V_3$ tuned to reproduce ground-state properties. They applied the complex scaling method (CSM) with complex-range Gaussian basis functions to treat scattering states and calculated the photodisintegration cross section using the complex-scaled Green's function formalism, decomposing the cross section into contributions from different continuum channels.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state binding energy: 1.57 MeV (experimental: 1.5736 MeV)
- Ground-state charge radius: 2.53 fm (experimental: 2.519 $\pm$ 0.012 fm)
- Ground-state matter radius: 2.42 fm (experimental: 2.38 $\pm$ 0.01 fm)
- Ground-state composition: 47.06% $^8$Be($0^+$) $\otimes$ $\nu p_{3/2}$ and 46.77% $^8$Be($2^+$) $\otimes$ $\nu p_{3/2}$
- Resonance energies and widths: $5/2^-_1$ at (0.854 MeV, $\sim 3 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV); $1/2^-_1$ at (1.11 MeV, 0.495 MeV); $5/2^+_1$ at (1.47 MeV, 0.323 MeV); $3/2^+_1$ at (3.12 MeV, 1.44 MeV); $3/2^-_2$ at (3.08 MeV, 1.18 MeV)
- Energy-integrated $E1$ strength for $4 \le E_\gamma \le 16$ MeV: 12.1 mb MeV (experimental lower limit: 11.3 mb MeV)
- Energy-integrated $E1$ strength for $4 \le E_\gamma \le 16$ MeV exhausts 56.5% of the energy-weighted cluster dipole sum rule

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Odsuren 2015) which investigated only the $1/2^+$ state and low-energy photodisintegration, by now including all spin-parity states connected to the ground state via electromagnetic transitions and covering energies up to 16 MeV. The calculation reproduces the recent measurement by Utsunomiya (2015) which reported enhanced dipole strength below the giant dipole resonance, and shows that this strength arises from single-neutron excitation from the $^8$Be($2^+$) $\otimes$ $\nu p_{3/2}$ configuration in the ground state into non-resonant continuum states, rather than from isolated resonances. The results enable future detailed analysis of the resonance structures of $^9$Be to gain a comprehensive understanding of its photodisintegration.
