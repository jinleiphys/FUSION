# Two particle-two hole excitations in charged current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

> Contribution to NUFACT 11, XIIIth International Workshop on Neutrino Factories, Super beams and Beta beams, 1-6 August 2011, CERN and University of Geneva (Submitted to IOP conference series)

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE neutrino charged-current quasielastic cross section anomaly can be explained by including multinucleon emission (2p-2h and 3p-3h) events that simulate quasielastic events in the detector, without requiring any modification of the standard axial-vector mass parameter \(M_A=1.03\) GeV/\(c^2\).

## Method

The author reviews three theoretical approaches (Martini et al., Amaro et al., and Nieves et al.) that compute 2p-2h contributions to charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections on \(^{12}\)C. These models start from a relativistic Fermi gas or superscaling description of the nucleus, include random phase approximation (RPA) correlations with \(\Delta\)-hole degrees of freedom, \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) meson exchange, and Landau-Migdal \(g'\) parameters, and add two-body currents (pion-in-flight, contact, and \(\Delta\)-MEC terms) along with nucleon-nucleon correlation contributions. The results are compared to MiniBooNE total and double-differential cross section data.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE neutrino beam average energy: 788 MeV.
- Standard axial cut-off mass: \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/\(c^2\); a larger value \(M_A = 1.35\) GeV/\(c^2\) is needed in the relativistic Fermi gas model without 2p-2h to account for MiniBooNE data.
- The inclusive cross section can be reproduced up to \(E_\nu \simeq 1\) GeV once the np-nh channel is added.
- At momentum transfer \(q = 300\) MeV/c, the Martini et al. 2p-2h contribution to the transverse (e,e') response is of the same order of magnitude as the calculation of Gil et al. (1997).

## Context

This work reviews the theoretical status following the MiniBooNE anomaly (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010) and extends earlier studies of two-nucleon emission in electron scattering, photon absorption, and pion absorption from the 1980s (Shimizu, 1980; Oset, 1987; Alberico, 1983). The review compares three independent models (Martini et al., 2009; Amaro et al., 2010; Nieves et al., 2011) and a phenomenological approach by Bodek et al. (2011), showing that all can explain the MiniBooNE data with the standard axial mass. The results imply that neutrino energy reconstruction algorithms for oscillation experiments must account for the complexity of nuclear dynamics including multinucleon emission.
