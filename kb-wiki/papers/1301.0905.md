# Muonic hydrogen and the proton radius puzzle

> The extremely precise extraction of the proton radius by Pohl et al. from the measured energy difference between the 2P and 2S states of muonic hydrogen disagrees significantly with that extracted from electronic hydrogen or elastic electron-proton scattering. This is the proton radius puzzle. The origins of the puzzle and the reasons for believing it to be very significant are explained. Various possible solutions of the puzzle are identified, and future work needed to resolve the puzzle is discussed.

## Key claim

The muonic hydrogen measurement of the proton radius (0.84087(39) fm) disagrees with the CODATA value (0.8775(51) fm) by about 4% or 7 standard deviations, but the authors note that half of the individual hydrogen spectroscopy values agree with the muonic value at the 1-sigma level, and only the 2S-8D5/2 transition disagrees at the 3-sigma level.

## Method

The authors review the extraction of the proton charge radius from three independent methods: laser spectroscopy of electronic hydrogen transitions (particularly the 1S-2S and 2S-8S,D/12D transitions), elastic electron-proton scattering cross sections analyzed with various functional forms (polynomials, splines, dispersion relations, z-expansion, sum-of-Gaussians), and laser spectroscopy of the 2S-2P Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen using a pulsed infrared laser system tunable from 5.5 to 6.1 micrometers. The muonic hydrogen experiment uses low-energy muons stopped in 1 mbar molecular hydrogen gas, with laser excitation detected via 1.9 keV K-alpha x-rays from the subsequent 2P-1S de-excitation.

## Key numbers

- Muonic hydrogen proton radius: 0.84087(39) fm from Pohl et al. (2010, 2013)
- CODATA 2010 proton radius: 0.8775(51) fm, composed of electronic hydrogen value 0.8758(77) fm and electron scattering value
- Mainz A1 collaboration result: r_p = 0.879 ± 0.005_stat ± 0.004_syst ± 0.002_model ± 0.004_group fm from 1422 relative cross sections over Q^2 = 0.0038 to 0.98 GeV^2
- Jefferson Lab LEDEX result: r_p = 0.875 ± 0.008_exp ± 0.006_fit fm
- Dispersion relation fit (Lorenz 2012): r_p = 0.84 ± 0.01 fm with chi^2/d.o.f. ≈ 2.2
- z-expansion fit (Hill 2010): r_p = 0.870 ± 0.023 ± 0.012 fm (proton data only); r_p = 0.871 ± 0.009 ± 0.002 ± 0.002 fm (including neutron data and pi-pi continuum)
- Sum-of-Gaussians fit (Sick 2011, 2012): r_p = 0.886 ± 0.008 fm
- Resonance center discrepancy: 75 GHz away from CODATA prediction, about four times the resonance width
- Statistical uncertainty of resonance center: 700 MHz; systematic uncertainty: 300 MHz
- Muon mass is about 207 times the electron mass, making the muon about 8 million times more likely to be inside the proton than the electron
- Finite size effect on the 1S level in hydrogen: about 1.2 MHz (L_1S ≈ 8172 + 1.56 r_p^2 MHz with r_p in fm)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing measurements of the proton radius from electronic hydrogen spectroscopy (Parthey 2011, Beauvoir 1997, Schwob 1999) and elastic electron-proton scattering (Bernauer 2010, Zhan 2011) and contrasts them with the muonic hydrogen result (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013). The puzzle contradicts the well-tested principle of lepton universality and would require a change of the Rydberg constant by 6.6 standard deviations if the muonic value were adopted. The authors identify possible solutions including experimental errors in any of the three methods, inadequacies in QED calculations, or new physics beyond the Standard Model, and note that the molecular ion formation hypothesis (Jentschura 2011) has been ruled out by three-body calculations (Karr and Hilico 2012).
