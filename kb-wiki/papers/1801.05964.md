# Investigation of the 9B nucleus and its cluster-nucleon correlations

> In order to study the correlation between clusters and nucleons in light nuclei, we formulate a new superposed THSR wave function which describes both spatial large spreading and cluster-correlated dynamics of valence nucleons. By using the new THSR wave function, the binding energy of 9B is essentially improved comparing with our previous studies. We calculate the excited states of 9B and obtain the energy spectrum of 9B which is consistent with the experimental results, including prediction of the 1/2+ excited state of 9B which is not fixed yet experimentally. We study the proton dynamics in 9B and find that the cluster-proton correlation plays an essential role for the proton dynamics in the ground state of 9B. Further more, we discuss the density distribution of the valence proton with special attention to its tail structure. Finally, the resonance nature of excited states of 9B is illustrated by comparing root-mean-square radii between the ground and excited states.

## Key claim

The new superposed THSR wave function improves the description of the valence proton dynamics in 9B by coupling both a large-spreading configuration (proton orbiting the 8Be core) and cluster-correlated configurations (proton correlated with each individual alpha cluster), yielding binding energies that approach the accuracy of the full generator coordinate method (GCM) while using far fewer basis states.

## Method

The authors construct a superposed THSR wave function for 9B as a linear combination of three terms: two cluster-correlated configurations (Phi_1 and Phi_2) describing the valence proton moving around each alpha cluster, and one large-spreading configuration (Phi_3) describing the proton orbiting the 8Be core. The wave function uses Volkov No. 2 central forces and G3RS spin-orbit interactions, with angular-momentum projection applied to restore rotational symmetry. Variational optimization of the superposition coefficients and Gaussian width parameters is performed for the ground state and excited states.

## Key numbers

- Ground state (3/2-) binding energy: -54.9 MeV with the new THSR wave function, compared to -53.9 MeV with the traditional THSR wave function and -56.3 MeV experimentally
- Improvement (Delta) over traditional THSR: 1.0 MeV for the 3/2- ground state, 1.0 MeV for the 5/2- excited state, and 1.3 MeV for the 7/2- excited state
- Optimized superposition coefficients for the ground state: c = 0.34 and d = 0.32, giving ratio d/c = 0.94
- Predicted 1/2+ excited state at 1.7 MeV above the ground state
- 3/2+ state energy: 4.2 MeV, consistent with the experimental value of 4.3 MeV
- RMS radii: 2.81 fm (3/2-), 2.87 fm (5/2-), 3.00 fm (7/2-), 3.04 fm (1/2-), 4.05 fm (1/2+), 4.16 fm (3/2+)

## Context

This work extends the THSR wave function approach previously applied to 9-11Be isotopes (Lyu 2015, Lyu 2016, Lyu 2017) to the proton-rich nucleus 9B, which has an alpha+alpha+p cluster structure. The new superposed wave function achieves results nearly equivalent to the GCM calculation (which superposes 81 Brink basis states) while using a much simpler variational form. The study enables future investigations of cluster-nucleon correlations in neutron-rich or proton-rich nuclei near the nuclear drip line, and provides a prediction for the experimentally unconfirmed 1/2+ excited state of 9B.
