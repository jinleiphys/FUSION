# Imprints of the nuclear symmetry energy on gravitational waves from the axial w-modes of neutron stars

> The eigen-frequencies of the axial w-modes of oscillating neutron stars are studied using the continued fraction method with an Equation of State (EOS) partially constrained by the recent terrestrial nuclear laboratory data. It is shown that the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ affects significantly both the frequencies and the damping times of these modes. Besides confirming the previously found universal behavior of the mass-scaled eigen-frequencies as functions of the compactness of neutron stars, we explored several alternative universal scaling functions. Moreover, the $w_{II}$-mode is found to exist only for neutron stars having a compactness of $M/R\geq 0.1078$ independent of the EOS used.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho)$ significantly affects both the frequencies and damping times of the axial w-modes of oscillating neutron stars, but this conclusion is based on an EOS whose symmetry energy is constrained only at sub-saturation densities by heavy-ion reaction data and extrapolated to supra-saturation densities according to MDI predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the axial perturbation equations for static neutron stars using the continued fraction method, with the EOS obtained from Hartree-Fock calculations using a modified Gogny interaction (MDI). The MDI EOSs were parameterized by a variable $x$ to mimic different density dependences of the symmetry energy while keeping the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter fixed at 211 MeV and the symmetry energy at saturation density fixed at 31.6 MeV. Neutron star models consisted of neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta-equilibrium, and the calculations were performed for the $w_I$, $w_{II}$, $w_{I2}$, and $w_{I3}$ axial w-modes.

## Key numbers

- The $w_{II}$-mode exists only for neutron stars having a compactness of $M/R \geq 0.1078$, independent of the EOS used.
- For a canonical neutron star of mass $M=1.4 M_\odot$ with the MDIx-1 EOS, the $w_{II}$-mode frequency is about $1.72$ kHz.
- For the MDIx0 EOS with a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star: $w_I$ frequency = 8.04 kHz, $w_I$ damping time = 29.2 $\mu$s, $w_{II}$ frequency = 2.81 kHz, $w_{II}$ damping time = 13.3 $\mu$s.
- For the MDIx-1 EOS with a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star: $w_I$ frequency = 7.21 kHz, $w_I$ damping time = 28.5 $\mu$s, $w_{II}$ frequency = 1.72 kHz, $w_{II}$ damping time = 14.4 $\mu$s.
- For the APR EOS with a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star: $w_I$ frequency = 8.17 kHz, $w_I$ damping time = 29.5 $\mu$s, $w_{II}$ frequency = 3.03 kHz, $w_{II}$ damping time = 13.1 $\mu$s.
- The MDI EOS has an incompressibility $K$ of 211 MeV, while the APR EOS has $K = 266$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axial w-mode universal scaling behaviors found by Benhar et al. and Tsui et al., and the polar w-mode scaling by Andersson et al., by introducing EOSs with the symmetry energy partially constrained by terrestrial nuclear laboratory data from heavy-ion reactions. The study is complementary to earlier work that focused on the stiffness of the EOS rather than the specific role of the symmetry energy. The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, and suggest that simultaneous studies of multiple w-modes will be useful for understanding the imprints of symmetry energy on gravitational waves.
