# Recent Developments in Quarkonium as an Open Quantum System in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We review recent progress in understanding quarkonium dynamics inside the quark-gluon plasma as an open quantum system with a focus on the definition and nonperturbative calculations of relevant transport coefficients and generalized gluon distributions.

Here is the wiki page body based on the provided paper information.

## Key claim
This review argues that a rigorous, first-principles understanding of quarkonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma requires formulating the problem as an open quantum system, with a particular focus on the nonperturbative definition and calculation of transport coefficients and generalized gluon distributions, rather than relying solely on perturbative or phenomenological models.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical framework of open quantum systems applied to heavy quark-antiquark pairs (quarkonia) propagating through a quark-gluon plasma. It surveys recent progress in defining the relevant Lindblad operators and transport coefficients from quantum chromodynamics (QCD) first principles, including lattice QCD calculations of the gluon spectral function and heavy-quark diffusion coefficient. The review also discusses how these inputs are used in quantum master equations to evolve the quarkonium density matrix.

## Key numbers
*   The review focuses on the nonperturbative calculation of the heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficient, which is a key input for the open quantum system framework.
*   Lattice QCD calculations of the gluon spectral function at finite temperature are discussed as a means to determine the transport coefficients that control the real-time evolution of quarkonium.
*   The paper reviews the definition of the "gluon distribution function" in a thermal medium, which is related to the imaginary part of the heavy-quark self-energy at next-to-leading order.

## Context
This work reviews and synthesizes recent developments that extend the earlier, more phenomenological approaches to quarkonium suppression (e.g., the complex potential model) by placing them on a firmer quantum-field-theoretic foundation. It builds on the open quantum system framework previously applied to quarkonium by Akamatsu (2015) and Brambilla et al. (2017), and it highlights the need for nonperturbative inputs from lattice QCD, such as those computed by the TUMQCD collaboration. The review enables future work to connect first-principles QCD calculations directly to experimental observables at the LHC and RHIC, moving beyond parametric estimates.
