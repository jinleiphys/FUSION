# Time delayed K+ N reactions and exotic baryon resonances

> Evidences and hints, both from the theoretical and experimental side, of exotic baryon resonances with B=S, have been with us for the last thirty years. The poor status of the general acceptance of these Z* resonances is partly due to the prejudice against penta-quark baryons and partly due to the opinion that a proof of the existence of exotic states must be rigorous. This can refer to the quality and amount of data gathered, and also to the analytical methods applied in the study of these resonances. It seems then mandatory that all possibilities and aspects be exploited. We do that by analyzing the time delay in K+ N scattering, encountering clear signals of the exotic Z* resonances close to the pole values found in partial wave analyses.

## Key claim

The authors argue that analyzing time delay in K+ N scattering provides clear signals of exotic Z* resonances, and that the pole values found in partial wave analyses agree with the peak values in time delay and speed plots, meeting a stricter consistency requirement than is typically applied to standard hadron resonances.

## Method

The authors calculated time delay and speed plots from T-matrix solutions and phase shifts for K+ N scattering in several partial waves (S01, P01, P13, D03, D15), using the latest available data from Arndt et al. (2003). They compared the peak positions in these plots with the pole masses found in the same partial wave analyses, and also compared their results with earlier speed plot analyses from the 1970s.

## Key numbers

- T-matrix pole values from Arndt et al. (2003): P01(1831 MeV), P13(1811 MeV), D03(1788 MeV), D15(2074 MeV)
- Time delay peaks in S01 partial wave at 1850 MeV (from T-matrix solution) and 1765 MeV (from fit to single energy values)
- Time delay peaks at 1.5 GeV in P01, P13, and D03 partial waves
- Speed plot peaks in P13 and D03 partial waves from Nakajima et al. at approximately 1825 MeV
- Predicted Z* resonance at 1710 MeV in S01 partial wave from Roiesnel (1979)
- Predicted penta-quark state around 1570 MeV from Weigel (1998) chiral soliton model
- Predicted Z* at 1530 MeV from Diakonov et al. (1997) chiral soliton model
- Experimental evidence for an exotic S=+1 baryon with mass around 1.54 GeV reported by Nakano et al. (2003) and CLAS collaboration (2003)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for exotic Z* resonances with baryon number equal to strangeness (B=S), which have been debated since the early 1970s. The authors address concerns raised by Martin and Oades (1976) and Nakajima et al. (1982) that peaks in speed plots did not coincide with resonance pole values, by applying the time delay method which provides a more physically motivated criterion (positive time delay indicates a genuine resonance). The analysis enables a more rigorous evaluation of the four Z* candidates (P01, P13, D03, D15) and suggests the possible existence of a lower-mass Z* around 1.5 GeV, consistent with later experimental discoveries.
