# Nonlocal nucleon-nucleus interactions in (d,p) reactions: Role of the deuteron D-state

> Theoretical models of the (d,p) reaction are exploited for both nuclear astrophysics and spectroscopic studies in nuclear physics. Usually, these reaction models use local optical model potentials to describe the nucleon- and deuteron-target interactions. Within such a framework the importance of the deuteron D-state in low-energy reactions is normally associated with spin observables and tensor polarization effects - with very minimal influence on differential cross sections. In contrast, recent work that includes the inherent nonlocality of the nucleon optical model potentials in the Johnson-Tandy adiabatic-model description of the (d,p) transition amplitude, which accounts for deuteron break-up effects, shows sensitivity of the reaction to the large n-p relative momentum content of the deuteron wave function. The dominance of the deuteron D-state component at such high momenta leads to significant sensitivity of calculated (d,p) cross sections and deduced spectroscopic factors to the choice of deuteron wave function [Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 117}, 162502 (2016)]. We present details of the Johnson-Tandy adiabatic model of the (d,p) transfer reaction generalized to include the deuteron D-state in the presence of nonlocal nucleon-target interactions. We present exact calculations in this model and compare these to approximate (leading-order) solutions. The latter, approximate solutions can be interpreted in terms of local optical potentials, but evaluated at a shifted value of the energy in the nucleon-target system. This energy shift is increased when including the D-state contribution. We also study the expected dependence of the D-state effects on the separation energy and orbital angular momentum of the transferred nucleon. Their influence on the spectroscopic information extracted from (d,p) reactions is quantified for a particular case of astrophysical significance.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the deuteron D-state in nonlocal nucleon-nucleus interactions within the Johnson-Tandy adiabatic model leads to significant sensitivity of calculated (d,p) cross sections and deduced spectroscopic factors to the choice of deuteron wave function, contrary to the conventional wisdom that the D-state has minimal influence on differential cross sections when using local optical potentials.

## Method

The authors generalized the Johnson-Tandy adiabatic model of (d,p) transfer reactions to include the deuteron D-state in the presence of nonlocal nucleon-target interactions of the Perey-Buck type. They performed exact calculations solving the nonlocal integro-differential Schrodinger equation for the deuteron channel distorted waves, including both central (a=0) and tensor (a=2) terms of the nonlocal adiabatic potential, and compared these to approximate lowest-order solutions that can be interpreted in terms of local optical potentials evaluated at shifted energies.

## Key numbers

- The neutron energy shift ΔE for the d+40Ca system at Ed=11.8 MeV ranges from 31.7 MeV (Hulthén S-state) to 75.3 MeV (Reid soft core), with AV18 giving 66.8 MeV and CD-Bonn giving 37.6 MeV
- The D-state probability PD for the NN models studied: 0% (Hulthén), 6.46% (Reid soft core), 5.76% (Argonne V18), 4.85% (CD-Bonn)
- The volume integrals D0 of the transfer vertex range from -125.19 to -126.22 MeV fm^(3/2) across the NN models
- The tensor force effects (a=2 terms) in the nonlocal adiabatic potential change calculated 40Ca(d,p)41Ca differential cross sections by less than 50 μb/sr, representing 0.6% or less
- For 28Si(d,p)29Si at Ed=10 MeV, inclusion of the D-state can result in cross section changes of up to 30% depending on neutron separation energy (varied from 1 to 21 MeV) and orbital angular momentum transfer (l=0,1,2,3)

## Context

This work extends the earlier nonlocal adiabatic model of Timofeyuk and Johnson (2013) that used only S-state deuteron wave functions, and contrasts with previous D-state studies by Johnson (1967) and Delic (1974) that found DWBA cross sections insensitive to the deuteron D-state. The results enable more accurate extraction of spectroscopic factors from (d,p) reactions at radioactive beam facilities, particularly for cases of astrophysical significance such as 26Al(d,p)27Al. The paper also shows that the standard local ADWA and Watanabe folding model calculations show essentially no sensitivity to the D-state, confirming that the nonlocality of nucleon optical potentials is the key mechanism exposing D-state effects.
