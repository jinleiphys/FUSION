# Hydrodynamics at RHIC -- how well does it work, where and how does it break down?

> I review the successes and limitations of the ideal fluid dynamic model in describing hadron emission spectra from Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

Ideal fluid dynamics successfully describes hadron emission spectra from Au+Au collisions at RHIC for transverse momenta below about 1.5-2 GeV/c, but the model breaks down at higher transverse momenta, in peripheral collisions, and at forward rapidities; these breakdowns can be exploited to study transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas.

## Method

The author reviews comparisons between ideal relativistic hydrodynamic calculations and experimental data on single-particle transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow \(v_2\) for identified hadrons from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) and 200 \(A\) GeV at RHIC. Initial conditions and freeze-out parameters are fixed using central collision data on charged particle multiplicity and pion/proton spectra, making all other hadron spectra and elliptic flow predictions parameter-free. The review also examines hybrid calculations where hydrodynamic evolution is followed by hadronic kinetic evolution using the RQMD code, and parton cascade simulations with varying scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Ideal hydrodynamics works for \(p_\perp \lesssim 1.5-2\) GeV/\(c\), where \(>99\%\) of particles are emitted.
- The fireball takes about 9-10 fm/\(c\) to convert completely to hadronic matter and another 7-8 fm/\(c\) to decouple completely.
- Kinetic decoupling temperatures studied: \(T_{\rm cr} = 165\) MeV (directly after hadronization) and \(T_{\rm dec} = 100\) MeV (after hadronic rescattering).
- The critical transverse momentum where QGP viscosity manifests in partonic elliptic flow is \(\approx 750\) MeV/\(c\).
- Teaney concluded the normalized sound attenuation length \(\Gamma_s/\tau = (4/3T\tau)(\eta/s)\) cannot be much larger than about 0.1 at RHIC.
- The dimensionless ratio \(\eta/s\) is brought close to the conjectured lower limit \(\eta/s = \hbar/(4\pi)\).
- Initial energy density in central collisions exceeds \(e > 10\) GeV/fm\(^3\) with thermalization time \(\tau_{\rm therm} < 1\) fm/\(c\).
- For U+U collisions in nose-on-nose configuration, \(\frac{1}{S}\frac{dN_{\rm ch}}{dy}\) is about 60% higher than the largest value plotted for Au+Au in Fig. 4a.

## Context

This review extends the successful application of ideal hydrodynamics at RHIC, previously summarized by Kolb and Heinz (2003), and contrasts with lower-energy heavy-ion collisions where hydrodynamics never worked well enough for quantitative understanding. The work builds on predictions by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (2000) for a non-monotonic beam energy dependence of elliptic flow as a signature of the quark-hadron phase transition. It enables a program to extract transport properties of the QGP by studying the gradual breakdown of ideal hydrodynamics at high \(p_\perp\), in peripheral collisions, and at forward rapidities, and calls for development of 3+1-dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic codes to quantitatively constrain the QGP shear viscosity.
