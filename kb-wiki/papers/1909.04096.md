# Searching for exotic cores with binary neutron star inspirals

> We study the feasibility of detecting exotic cores in merging neutron stars with ground-based gravitational-wave detectors. We focus on models with a sharp nuclear/exotic matter interface, and assume a uniform distribution of neutron stars in the mass range $[1,2] M_\odot$. We find that the existence of exotic cores can be confirmed at the 70% confidence level with as few as several tens of detections. Likewise, with such a sample, we find that some models of exotic cores can be excluded {with high confidence}.

# Searching for exotic cores with binary neutron star inspirals

## Key claim
With as few as several tens of binary neutron star detections, the existence of exotic cores (e.g., hyperonic or quark matter) in neutron stars can be confirmed at the 70% confidence level, provided the exotic matter produces a sharp nuclear/exotic matter interface and the neutron star mass distribution is uniform in the [1,2] solar mass range. Some models of exotic cores can be excluded with high confidence using the same sample size.

## Method
The authors constructed simulated ensembles of binary neutron star mergers with component masses uniformly distributed in [1,2] solar masses, using piecewise linear mass-radius curves for both purely nuclear and hybrid (nuclear mantle plus exotic core) equations of state. They injected Gaussian noise into the inferred radius and masses based on signal-to-noise ratios from a power-law distribution valid for nearby events, then fit the simulated data to piecewise linear models in the chirp mass versus inferred radius plane and the primary mass versus inferred radius plane, searching for kinks (abrupt slope changes) that indicate the presence of exotic cores.

## Key numbers
- Neutron star mass range assumed: [1,2] solar masses
- Critical masses for hybrid models tested: {1.2, 1.4, 1.6, 1.8} solar masses
- Hybrid branch slopes tested: -{0.5, 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10} km/solar mass
- With 40 detections, hybrid models with critical mass ≤ 1.6 solar masses and slope ≤ -6 km/solar mass can be excluded at >80% confidence level if the fitted slope is positive
- For the most pessimistic nuclear model (model D), over 70% confidence of identifying exotic cores can be achieved with 40 observations if the data show a negative chirp mass slope and a slope discontinuity >1 km/solar mass in the primary mass plane
- GW170817-like events have 1-sigma radius and mass uncertainties of 0.75 km and 0.1 solar masses, respectively
- Standard deviation of critical mass estimates from chirp mass fits: 0.05-0.7 solar masses; from primary mass fits: 0.2-0.6 solar masses (with 200 mergers)

## Context
This work extends the study of neutron star equation of state constraints from gravitational wave observations, following the detection of GW170817 by Advanced LIGO-Virgo (2017) which indicated a relatively soft equation of state. The authors build on prior work on hybrid neutron stars with exotic cores by Alford et al. (2013, 2015) and Lindblom (1998), and on universal relations between compactness and tidal deformability by Maselli (2013) and Yagi (2016). The method enables future searches for exotic matter in neutron star cores using the growing catalog of binary neutron star mergers expected from Advanced LIGO-Virgo observations over the next few years, and could be extended to study models with mixed phases of nuclear and quark matter.
