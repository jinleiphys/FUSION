# A G2-QCD neutron star

> The determination of the properties of neutron stars from the underlying theory, QCD, is still an unsolved problem. This is mainly due to the difficulty to obtain reliable results for the equation of state for cold, dense QCD. As an alternative route to obtain qualitative insights, we determine the structure of a neutron star for a modified version of QCD: By replacing the gauge group SU(3) with the exceptional Lie group G2, it is possible to perform lattice simulations at finite density, while still retaining neutrons. Here, results of these lattice simulations are used to determine the mass-radius relation of a neutron star for this theory. The results show that phase changes express themselves in this relation. Also, the radius of the most massive neutron stars is found to vary very little, which would make radius determinations much simpler if this would also be true in QCD.

## Key claim

Using lattice simulations of G2-QCD (a QCD-like theory without a sign problem) to compute the equation of state and then the mass-radius relation of a neutron star, the authors find that phase changes in the baryon density impress themselves onto the mass-radius relation, and that the radius of the most massive neutron stars varies very little; however, the results are qualitative rather than semi-quantitatively reliable because the data are for one flavor with comparatively heavy pions.

## Method

The authors used previously published lattice simulation data for G2-QCD (gauge group G2 instead of SU(3)) at finite baryon density to obtain the baryon density as a function of baryo-chemical potential. They assumed the neutron star consists entirely of neutrons (neglecting diquark contributions) and that the energy density is given by the neutron rest mass times the baryon density, with pressure derived from thermodynamic relations. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for a static, spherically symmetric ideal fluid to obtain the mass-radius relation for various central chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Goldstone (diquark) mass: 247 MeV, with the neutron mass set to 938 MeV to fix the scale (from lattice data of Wellegehausen et al., 2013)
- Maximum mass of the G2-QCD neutron star: approximately 1.2 solar masses
- The interacting case shows a steeper rise in the mass-radius relation compared to the free Fermi gas, with a notable change in slope occurring around a radius of about 11 km
- Central chemical potentials up to 7.6 fm^-1 were used in the profiles

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD-like theories without a sign problem, specifically G2-QCD as reviewed by Maas (2012), using lattice data from Wellegehausen et al. (2013) for the equation of state. It provides the first mass-radius relation for a neutron star based on lattice input from a theory with fermionic baryons, contrasting with the standard non-interacting neutron gas baseline (Glendenning, 1997). The results suggest that phase changes in dense matter could be observable in the mass-radius relation of real neutron stars, and that future gravitational wave data (Abbott, 2016) could provide further constraints.
