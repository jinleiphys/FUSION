# Atomic Mass Dependence of Hadron Production in Deep Inelastic Scattering on Nuclei

> Hadron production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering is studied in an absorption model. In the proposed model, the early stage of hadronization in the nuclear medium is dominated by prehadron formation and absorption, controlled by flavor-dependent formation lengths and absorption cross sections. Computations for hadron multiplicity ratios are presented and compared with the HERMES experimental data for pions, kaons, protons and antiprotons. The mass-number dependence of hadron attenuation is shown to be sensitive to the underlying hadronization dynamics. Contrary to common expectations for absorption models, a leading term proportional to A^{2/3} is found. Deviations from the leading behavior arise at large mass-numbers and large hadron fractional momenta.

## Key claim

Contrary to the common expectation that absorption models predict hadron attenuation proportional to the nuclear path length (A^{1/3}), this paper shows analytically and numerically that the leading term in the absorption model is actually proportional to A^{2/3}, with deviations appearing only at large mass numbers and large hadron fractional momenta.

## Method

The authors model hadron production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering using an absorption model where the struck quark hadronizes via the Lund string fragmentation mechanism, producing a prehadron that then decays into the final hadron. The survival probability of the hadron is computed by solving coupled differential equations for the probabilities of the quark, prehadron, and hadron states as they propagate through the nuclear medium, with flavor-dependent formation lengths derived from the Lund model and absorption cross sections for prehadrons set to 2/3 of the full hadron-nucleon cross section. The multiplicity ratio R_M^h is then calculated for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons and compared with HERMES experimental data on D, He, N, Ne, and Kr targets.

## Key numbers

- Prehadron absorption cross section is set to σ_* = (2/3) σ_h, fitted to reproduce the pion multiplicity ratio on ⁸⁴Kr.
- String constant used is κ = 1 GeV/fm for the vacuum, rescaled in the nucleus as κ_A = (λ_0²/λ_A²) κ based on the partial deconfinement model.
- For π⁺ production at z = 0.25, the leading coefficient c₁ = 0.0095 and the next-order coefficient c₂ = -0.000096, with the A^{4/3} term becoming comparable to the A^{2/3} term at mass number Ā ≈ 980.
- For π⁺ production at z = 0.85, c₁ = 0.0314 and c₂ = -0.001059, with Ā ≈ 160.
- Average hadron formation lengths ⟨l^h⟩ for π⁺ range from 10.15 fm at z = 0.25 to 12.34 fm at z = 0.65, justifying the neglect of hadron interactions inside the nucleus.
- The fit parameter w = 0.19 is used to approximate the integral in the attenuation formula, valid for 0 < r < 5 and 0 < a < 3.5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous absorption model (Accardi et al., 2002) by incorporating a realistic string constant (κ = 1 GeV/fm) with smaller prehadronic cross sections and flavor-dependent formation lengths, improving agreement with HERMES data (2001, 2003, 2004). The finding of an A^{2/3} leading term challenges the common belief that absorption models yield A^{1/3} dependence while energy loss models (Wang and Wang, 2002; Arleo, 2002) yield A^{2/3}, suggesting that the mass-number dependence alone may not cleanly discriminate between the two mechanisms. The model enables future studies of hadronization dynamics by comparing the A-dependence of attenuation for different hadron species, particularly for K⁻ and antiprotons which have shorter formation lengths due to their rank-2 production in the Lund model.
