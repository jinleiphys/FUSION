# Non-empirical pairing functional

> The present contribution reports the first systematic finite-nucleus calculations performed using the Energy Density Functional method and a non-empirical pairing functional derived from low-momentum interactions. As a first step, the effects of Coulomb and the three-body force are omitted while only the bare two-nucleon interaction at lowest order is considered. To cope with the finite-range and non-locality of the bare nuclear interaction, the 1S0 channel of Vlowk is mapped onto a convenient operator form. For the first time, neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing correlations generated in finite nuclei by the direct term of the two-nucleon interaction are characterized in a systematic manner. Eventually, such predictions are compared to those obtained from empirical local functionals derived from density-dependent zero range interactions. The characteristics of the latter are analyzed in view of that comparison and a specific modification of their isovector density dependence is suggested to accommodate Coulomb effects and the isovector trend of neutron gaps at the same time.

## Key claim

The first systematic finite-nucleus calculations using a non-empirical pairing functional derived from low-momentum interactions produce neutron pairing gaps that are strikingly close to experimental odd-even mass staggering across several mass regions, even though the functional is derived only at lowest order from the bare two-nucleon interaction and omits Coulomb, three-body forces, and higher-order polarization effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-empirical pairing functional by taking the direct term of the bare two-nucleon interaction at lowest order, using only the 1S0 channel of the low-momentum potential Vlowk (resolution cutoff Λ ≈ 2 fm⁻¹). To make the finite-range, non-local interaction tractable in systematic Energy Density Functional calculations, they mapped it onto a rank-1 separable operator representation. The non-empirical pairing functional was then combined with the empirical SLy5 Skyrme parametrization for the particle-hole channel, and calculations were performed using the BSLHFB code in a spherical box for about 470 even-even (predicted) spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The low-momentum potential Vlowk was calculated at a resolution cutoff of Λ ≈ 2 fm⁻¹.
- The spherical box used for calculations had radius R = 20 fm, with a discretized Bessel basis up to k_max = 4 fm⁻¹ and a partial-wave cutoff j_max = 45/2.
- The SLy5 Skyrme parametrization gives a density-dependent, momentum-independent isoscalar effective mass m*₀ = 0.7 m at saturation density.
- Neutron gaps from the non-empirical functional are larger than experimental gaps by a few hundred keV in tin and lead isotopes.
- In ¹²⁰Sn, the gap predicted using the separable representation of Vlowk is about twice the value reported in a prior calculation using the Argonne V18 bare interaction (Barranco 2004).

## Context

This work extends the program of constructing non-empirical energy density functionals from low-momentum interactions, made possible by the Renormalization Group techniques of Bogner (2003). It provides the first systematic characterization of pairing correlations generated in finite nuclei by the direct term of the bare two-nucleon interaction, complementing the single-nucleus calculation of Barranco (2004). The results enable future inclusion of Coulomb effects, three-body forces, and higher-order polarization diagrams, and they provide a benchmark for improving empirical local pairing functionals such as those derived from Density Dependent Delta Interactions.
