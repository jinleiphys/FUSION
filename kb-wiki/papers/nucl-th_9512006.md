# Nuclear Production of Charmonium at ELFE Energies

> I discuss the diffractive production of charmonium from nuclei and the anomalous nuclear attenuation --- termed ``color transparency'' --- attributed to such processes, focusing on the few tens of GeV energy regime germane to the ELFE project. A critical review of color transparency calculations is presented, and the major sources of uncertainty are emphasized.

# Nuclear Production of Charmonium at ELFE Energies

## Key claim

The anomalous nuclear attenuation ("color transparency") in diffractive charmonium production from nuclei is expected to appear at few-tens-of-GeV energies relevant to ELFE, but a completely consistent calculation in the near-threshold regime has not been performed, and the major sources of uncertainty, including the choice of charmonium potential, the modeling of the production amplitude, and the inclusion of both C-odd and C-even exchanges, make the magnitude of the effect highly uncertain.

## Method

The paper presents a critical review of archetypal color transparency calculations for diffractive charmonium photoproduction from nuclei, using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model in the charmonium rest frame. The nuclear transparency \(T_A^j\) is computed from the evolution operator \(\hat{U}\) acting on the initial charmonium state, with the soft interaction modeled via two-gluon exchange and the charmonium states described by harmonic oscillator or Richardson potentials. The author examines how the creation length \(l_c\) and formation length \(l_f\) scale with energy and quark mass, and discusses the impact of truncating the sum over intermediate charmonium states.

## Key numbers

- The mass splitting between the lowest-lying \(1^{--}\) states is 660 MeV for \(s\bar s\), 588 MeV for \(c\bar c\), and 563 MeV for \(b\bar b\) sectors.
- The \(J/\psi\) (1S) mass is 3097 MeV experimentally; the \(\psi'\) (2S) mass is 3686 MeV experimentally.
- The \(\psi(4040)\) (3S) mass is 4040 MeV experimentally; the \(\psi(4415)\) (4S) mass is 4415 MeV experimentally.
- Harmonic oscillator fits to the charmonium spectrum: HO\(_1\) uses \(2\hbar\omega = m_{\psi(3685)} - m_{\psi(3097)}\); HO\(_2\) uses \(2\hbar\omega = m_{\psi(4040)} - m_{\psi(3097)}\).
- The Richardson potential fit yields 1S = 3095 MeV, 2S = 3684 MeV, 3S = 4096 MeV, 4S = 4440 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of color transparency from the \((e,e'p)\) reaction (Brodsky and Mueller, 1988) to diffractive charmonium photoproduction at the moderate energies of the proposed ELFE facility. It critically reviews prior calculations by Brodsky and Zakharov (1992), Benhar (1995), and others, emphasizing that the energy dependence of the reaction mechanism (Gardner, 1995; Bertsch, Kerman, and Zhu, 1992) and nuclear correlations (Benhar, 1995) can mimic or obscure color transparency signals. The paper identifies the need for a complete calculation with a quantitative charmonium Hamiltonian, a production amplitude derived from the nonrelativistic three-point Green function (as in Fadin and Khoze for top quark studies), and a diffractive interaction including both two- and three-gluon exchange, to enable reliable interpretation of future ELFE data.
