# What is the Correct pi^- p -> omega n Cross Section at Threshold?

> The pi^- p -> omega n threshold cross section measured in the seventies resisted up to now a consistent theoretical description in line with experiment, mainly caused by too large Born contributions. This led to a discussion in the literature about the correctness of the extraction of these data points. We show that the extraction method used in these references is indeed correct and that there is no reason to doubt the correctness of these data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the extraction method used in the 1970s experiments (Binnie, Keyne, Karami) to obtain the π⁻ p → ω n threshold cross section is mathematically correct, contrary to claims by Hanhart (1999) that the method only covered a fraction of the ω spectral function; the paper argues that the original data should not be doubted or modified.

## Method

The authors re-derive the full relation between experimental count rates and the two-body cross section for π N → ω N, starting from the general cross section formula for π N → n π⁺ π⁻ π⁰. They show that the experimental integration over the incoming pion three-momentum (by summing over beam settings) transforms into an integration over the ω four-momentum squared, which, combined with the normalization of the ω spectral function, yields the correct two-body cross section. They verify that the integration covers at least 7 half-widths of the ω spectral function on either side of m_ω² and that the product of Jacobians varies by less than 2.5 percent for fixed t.

## Key numbers

- The ω spectral function has a width of 8 MeV.
- The incoming pion lab momentum range was k_L ∈ [1.04, 1.265] GeV, translating to a c.m. energy range of √s ∈ [1.6938, 1.8133] GeV.
- The neutron c.m. momentum range was p' ∈ [0.03, 0.21] GeV.
- The integration over q² extends over at least 7 half-widths Γ_ω/2 on either side of m_ω², covering more than 92% of the spectral function.
- The product of Jacobians J_k J_τ varies by less than 2.5 percent in the interval √q² ∈ [m_ω − 2Γ_ω, m_ω + 2Γ_ω].
- The neutron momentum binning of Δp' = 10 MeV introduces at most a 1 percent correction (worst case: p' = 30 MeV).
- The S₁₁ inelastic partial wave cross section from π N → π N (SM00) is approximately 2.4 mb, while π N → 2π N contributes about 2.5 mb; an additional ω N contribution of σ^{S₁₁} ≥ 3 mb would grossly overestimate the inelasticity.

## Context

This work extends the original experimental analyses of Binnie (1970s), Keyne (1970s), and Karami (1970s) on π⁻ p → ω n threshold cross sections, which had resisted consistent theoretical description due to overly large Born contributions. The paper directly contradicts the reanalysis by Hanhart (1999), who claimed the extraction method was flawed and advocated modifying the cross sections, modifications that were adopted by Sibi (2000), Titov (2000), and Hanhart (2001). The authors show that the modified cross sections would produce ω N cross sections of σ ≥ 3 mb in the range 1.72 ≤ √s ≤ 1.74 GeV, which is inconsistent with the inelasticity deduced from π N → π N partial wave analyses (SM00) and Manley (1990s). This resolution enables future coupled-channel analyses (Penner and Mosel, forthcoming) and in-medium models of vector mesons (Post, Friman, Klingl).
