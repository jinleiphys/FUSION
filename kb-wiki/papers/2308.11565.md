# Momentum dependent measures of correlations between mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow in heavy ion collisions

> The correlation between the mean transverse momentum and the harmonic flow coefficients is an observable which is of great interest; it is sensitive to shape fluctuations in the initial state of a relativistic nuclear collision. The measurement of that correlation coefficient in central collisions allows one to infer about the intrinsic deformation of the colliding nuclei. We propose to study the momentum dependent covariance and correlation coefficient between the mean transverse momentum and the harmonic flow in a given transverse momentum bin. Two possible constructions of such observables are provided and predictions are obtained from a viscous hydrodynamic model. We find that such momentum dependent correlation coefficients between the mean transverse momentum and the harmonic flow show a strong and nontrivial momentum dependence. We also explore the effects of granularity (nucleon width) in the initial state, the nuclear deformation, and the shear viscosity on this momentum dependent correlation coefficient. The shape of the momentum dependence of the correlation coefficient for the triangular flow is found to be sensitive to the size of small scale fluctuations in the initial state. On the other hand, the shape of the momentum dependence of the covariance between the mean transverse momentum and the harmonic flow coefficients is found to be sensitive to the value of the shear viscosity and to the granularity of the initial state.

# Momentum dependent measures of correlations between mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The momentum dependent correlation coefficient between mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow in a given transverse momentum bin shows a strong and nontrivial momentum dependence, and the shape of this dependence for triangular flow is sensitive to the size of small-scale fluctuations in the initial state, while the shape of the momentum dependent covariance is sensitive to shear viscosity and initial state granularity.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC model with Glauber or TRENTO initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV. They constructed two definitions of momentum dependent correlation coefficients: rho([p_T], V_n(q)V_n(q)*) and rho([p_T], V_n V_n(q)*), and computed them as functions of transverse momentum q for elliptic and triangular flow across multiple centrality bins. They also studied the effects of varying the nucleon width parameter w (0.3, 0.5, 0.8 fm), the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s (0.08, 0.12, 0.16), and nuclear deformation on these observables.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV were simulated
- Three values of nucleon width for energy deposition were studied: w=0.3, 0.5, and 0.8 fm
- Three values of shear viscosity to entropy density ratio were studied: eta/s=0.08, 0.12, and 0.16
- Momentum dependent correlation coefficients were computed for transverse momentum q below 2 GeV
- Centrality bins studied include 0-5%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 60-70%

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the momentum-independent correlation coefficient rho([p_T], v_n^2) introduced by Bozek (2016), which has been used to infer nuclear deformation parameters by Giacalone (2019) and the ATLAS Collaboration (2021, 2022). The momentum dependent generalization proposed here can clarify the observed dependence of the momentum-independent coefficient on transverse momentum cuts reported by ATLAS (2019). The results enable experimental constraints on initial state granularity, complementing the approach of Giacalone (2021), and could be incorporated into Bayesian analyses of hydrodynamic model parameters as done by the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020) and Nijs (2020).
