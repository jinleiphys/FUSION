# Jet Quenching in The Most Vortical Fluid: A Holographic Approach

> The STAR collaboration at the RHIC facility has recently announced the exciting discovery of direct evidence for extremely large vorticity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma generated in peripheral collisions, seen in the form of global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons. This prompts the question: does this vorticity have any effect on other observed phenomena, such as jet quenching? Using a simple gauge-gravity model, we suggest that such an effect may be detectable, in data from near-future experiments, as a reduction in the jet quenching parameter. This is predicted to be most prominent in collisions corresponding to a narrow range of centralities around $10 \%$. The relative reduction (compared to collisions at zero or, alternatively, large centrality) is predicted to behave in an unexpected and characteristic manner: the model suggests that it is independent of the impact energy.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that vorticity in the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions may reduce the jet quenching parameter, with the effect predicted to be most prominent at centralities around 10% and, unexpectedly, independent of the impact energy. The hedge is that this reduction may not be readily observable against current experimental uncertainties, and the maximal effect occurs at maximal angular momentum rather than maximal vorticity.

## Method

The author uses a holographic gauge-gravity model based on a dyonic AdS-Kerr-Newman black hole with spherical event horizon topology, which incorporates electric charge (for baryonic chemical potential), magnetic charge (to distinguish vorticity from magnetic field effects), and angular momentum (to represent vorticity). The jet quenching parameter is related to the entropy density via the known scaling \(\hat{q} \propto \sqrt{s}\), and the ratio of entropy density to energy density is computed holographically from the black hole parameters. By comparing rotating (peripheral) and non-rotating (central) black hole configurations at fixed temperature and baryonic chemical potential, the relative change in \(\hat{q}\) due to vorticity is extracted.

## Key numbers

- The predicted ratio of the jet quenching parameter at maximal vorticity (10% centrality) to that at zero centrality is \(\hat{q}^{10}/\hat{q}^{0} \approx 0.7\), independent of impact energy.
- The maximal angular momentum parameter for RHIC collisions at 200 GeV per pair is taken as \(a_{\text{max}} = 30\) fm, with \(a_{\text{max}}/L = 0.9\) giving \(L \approx 33.3\) fm.
- The vorticity at maximal angular momentum is \(\approx 0.024\) fm\(^{-1}\), while the maximal vorticity (at impact parameter \(\approx 5\) fm) is \(\approx 0.03\) fm\(^{-1}\), just below the STAR-reported value of roughly \(10^{22}\) s\(^{-1} \approx 0.033\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the model yields \(s/\epsilon \approx 1.30\) fm, in agreement with the literature value of 1.25 fm from Olli (2015).
- The STAR collaboration reported vorticity around \(9 \pm 1 \times 10^{21}\) s\(^{-1}\), \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)-averaged.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to jet quenching by incorporating vorticity, following the STAR collaboration's discovery of global hyperon polarization (STAR, 2017). It builds on prior holographic studies of jet quenching (Hong et al., 2006; Hong et al., 2007) that established the \(\hat{q} \propto \sqrt{s}\) scaling, and on theoretical predictions that peripheral collisions produce large vorticity (Liang et al., 2005; Becattini et al., 2008; Huang et al., 2011). The model enables predictions for future Beam Energy Scan experiments at RHIC (BES II), FAIR, and NICA, suggesting that a reduction in \(\hat{q}\) at 10-15% centrality compared to very central or very peripheral collisions could serve as a signature of vorticity effects, provided experimental precision improves.
