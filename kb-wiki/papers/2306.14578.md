# Radiative Corrections: From Medium to High Energy Experiments

> Radiative corrections are crucial for modern high-precision physics experiments, and are an area of active research in the experimental and theoretical community. Here we provide an overview of the state of the field of radiative corrections with a focus on several topics: lepton-proton scattering, QED corrections in deep-inelastic scattering, and in radiative light-hadron decays. Particular emphasis is placed on the two-photon exchange, believed to be responsible for the proton form-factor discrepancy, and associated Monte-Carlo codes. We encourage the community to continue developing theoretical techniques to treat radiative corrections, and perform experimental tests of these corrections.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections, particularly two-photon exchange (TPE), are essential for resolving the proton form-factor discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer measurements, but the paper emphasizes that these corrections are process-dependent and require continued development of theoretical techniques and experimental tests.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for radiative corrections in lepton-proton scattering, deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), and electron-positron annihilation, presenting the decomposition of elastic scattering amplitudes into six complex scalar functions (generalized form factors) that extend beyond the one-photon-exchange Born approximation. It describes how virtual corrections and real bremsstrahlung modify the Born cross section, and how the leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) radiator method, combined with massive operator matrix elements (OMEs) and renormalization group equations, allows calculation of higher-order QED corrections up to five-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The fine structure constant is given as α ≈ 1/137
- For the Z-mass shift from initial-state radiation QED corrections, the O(α) contribution to the peak is 185.638 MeV (fixed width) and 181.098 MeV (s-dependent width), while O(α^2 L^2) contributes -96.894 MeV and -95.342 MeV respectively
- The O(α^2) contribution to the Z-width shift is -2.218 MeV (fixed width) and -2.001 MeV (s-dependent width)
- For the Z-peak shift, O(α^3 L^3) contributes 23.265 MeV (fixed width) and 22.968 MeV (s-dependent width)
- The O(α^5 L^5) contribution to the Z-peak shift is 0.142 MeV (fixed width) and 0.144 MeV (s-dependent width)
- For elastic ep scattering at E_e = 26.8 GeV and E_p = 820 GeV, the O(α) radiative corrections in leptonic variables deviate by several percent from the O(α^2) corrections
- In the time-like region, TPE contributions of ≥ 5% would be detectable at PANDA at FAIR, while BaBar data showed charge-odd contributions vanish at the 2% level

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on radiative corrections by Mo and Tsai (1969) and Maximon and Tjon (2000), and addresses the proton form-factor discrepancy identified by comparisons of Rosenbluth separation (Rosenbluth, 1950) with polarization transfer measurements (Akhiezer and Rekalo, 1968). The paper enables future precision measurements at facilities such as FAIR and the Electron-Ion Collider by providing the theoretical framework for two-photon exchange corrections and higher-order QED effects, while identifying open questions about the complete treatment of massive operator matrix elements and the need for experimental tests of these corrections.
