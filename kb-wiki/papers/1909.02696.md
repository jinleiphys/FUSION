# Coupled $K^+Λ$ and $K^0Λ$ photoproduction off the nucleon: Consequences from the recent CLAS and MAMI data and the $N(1680)P_{11}$ narrow state

> The new $γn\to K^0Λ$ data obtained from the CLAS and MAMI collaborations are analyzed by employing an effective Lagrangian method. The constructed model can describe all available experimental data in both $γp \to K^+Λ$ and $γn\to K^0Λ$ channels, simultaneously. The background part of the model is built from the appropriate intermediate states involving the nucleon, kaon, and hyperon exchanges, whereas the resonance part is constructed from the consistent interaction Lagrangians and propagators. To check the performance of the model a detailed comparison between the calculated observables and experimental data in both isospin channels is presented, from which a nice agreement can be observed. The discrepancy between the CLAS and MAMI data in the $γn\to K^0Λ$ channel is analyzed by utilizing three different models; M1, M2, and M3 that fit the CLAS, MAMI, and both CLAS and MAMI data sets, respectively. The effect of this discrepancy is studied by investigating the significance of individual nucleon resonances and the predicted beam-target helicity asymmetry $E$ that has been measured by the CLAS collaboration recently. It is found that the $N(1720)P_{13}$, $N(1900)P_{13}$, and $N(2060)D_{15}$ resonances are significant for improving the agreement between model calculation and data. This result is relatively stable to the choice of the model. The helicity asymmetry $E$ can be better explained by the models M1 and M3. Finally, the effect of the $N(1680)P_{11}$ narrow resonance on the cross section of both isospin channels is explored. It is found that the effect is more sensitive in the $γn\to K^0Λ$ channel. In this case the model M3, that fits both CLAS and MAMI data, yields a more realistic effect.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a covariant isobar model can simultaneously describe both the $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ and $\gamma n \to K^0\Lambda$ photoproduction channels, but the agreement depends on which of the discrepant CLAS and MAMI datasets for the neutron channel is used. The effect of the $N(1680)P_{11}$ narrow resonance is found to be more sensitive in the $\gamma n\to K^0\Lambda$ channel, with the model that fits both CLAS and MAMI data yielding a more realistic effect.

## Method
The author employs an effective Lagrangian method with a covariant isobar model to analyze photoproduction data. The background part is built from intermediate states involving nucleon, kaon, and hyperon exchanges, while the resonance part uses consistent interaction Lagrangians and propagators for nucleon resonances up to spin 9/2 and hyperon resonances up to spin 3/2. Three different models (M1, M2, M3) are constructed by fitting to the CLAS data only, the MAMI data only, and both CLAS and MAMI data sets, respectively, to study the discrepancy between these datasets in the $\gamma n\to K^0\Lambda$ channel.

## Key numbers
- 18 nucleon resonances are included in the model, with spins up to 9/2, using properties from the PDG estimates
- 15 hyperon resonances are included, limited to those with spins up to 3/2
- The total database contains 9424 data points across both channels (9003 for $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ and 421 for $\gamma n\to K^0\Lambda$)
- The ratio of neutral to charged kaon resonance transition moments for $K^*(892)$ is $r_{K^0} = -1.53 \pm 0.20$
- The $N(1720)P_{13}$, $N(1900)P_{13}$, and $N(2060)D_{15}$ resonances are found to be significant for improving agreement between model calculation and data

## Context
This work extends the author's previous covariant isobar model (Mart 2017, Clymton 2017) that fitted nearly 7400 $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ data points, now updated to include the latest CLAS 2016 double polarization data and new $\gamma n\to K^0\Lambda$ data from CLAS (2017) and MAMI (2018). The analysis contrasts with a recent study by Kim (2018) that used a similar effective Lagrangian method with Regge formalism for backgrounds but only considered the $\gamma n\to K^0\Lambda$ channel without the MAMI data. The results enable future investigation of neutron helicity amplitudes and provide a framework for testing isospin symmetry in kaon photoproduction.
