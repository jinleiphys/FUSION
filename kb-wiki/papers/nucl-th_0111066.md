# Relativistic mean-field study of light nuclei near drip line

> The relativistic mean field theory is applied to study some exotic properties of neutron rich nuclei as recently observed, namely, extension of the drip-line for $F$ nuclei from $^{29}F$ to $^{31}F$ and the appearence of a new shell closure at neutron number N=16. We find $^{31}F$ to be bound against one-neutron dripping but unbound only marginally for two neutron separation. The calculated functional dependence of one-neutron separation energy with neutron number for different values of $T_Z = (N-Z)/2$ signals a new shell closure at N=16 for neutron rich nuclei with $T_Z\ge 3$. This is further corroborated from the study of the deformation and the gap across the Fermi surface in these light nuclei.

# Relativistic mean-field study of light nuclei near drip line

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculation predicts that $^{31}F$ is bound against one-neutron dripping but unbound by a few hundred keV against two-neutron dripping, contrary to the experimental observation that $^{31}F$ is stable. The calculation also finds a new shell closure at neutron number N=16 for neutron-rich nuclei with $T_Z \ge 3$, though this closure is weaker than the traditional N=20 closure and the dissolution of the N=20 magicity for $T_Z \ge 4$ suggested by experiments is not reproduced.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree (RMF) equations with the NL3 parameter set in an axially deformed basis expansion using 20 oscillator shells, including pairing correlations in the BCS approximation with pairing gaps determined from experimental odd-even mass differences via the four-point formula. For odd nuclei, the blocking approximation is employed to restore time-reversal symmetry. Calculations are performed for isotopic chains of F, Ne, N, O, Na, Mg, and Si from stability to the neutron drip line.

## Key numbers

- $^{31}F$ is bound against one-neutron dripping but unbound against two-neutron dripping by a few hundred keV (exact value not specified in text)
- $^{29}F$ and $^{34}Ne$ are predicted as the drip line nuclei in this calculation
- The gap between the $1d_{3/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ orbitals at N=20 is approximately 6 MeV
- Deformation $\beta_2$ for $^{31}F$ is approximately 0.3 (cited from experimental interpretation)
- For F isotopes at N=16, the deformation has a local minimum with $|\beta_2| \sim 0.14$
- Pairing gaps are taken as $\Delta_n = 1$ MeV for both systems (except N=20 where $\Delta_n=0$) and $\Delta_p = 2$ MeV for Ne and 3.5 MeV for F, with uncertainties of approximately 0.5 MeV
- The calculation reproduces the stability of $^{29}Ne$ and instability of $^{31}Ne$, in agreement with experiment

## Context

This work extends RMF calculations to neutron-rich light nuclei near the drip line around N=20, following experimental results from RIKEN (Sakurai et al., 1999) that shifted the fluorine drip line from $^{29}F$ to $^{31}F$ and suggested a new magic number at N=16 based on analysis of single-neutron separation energies (Ozawa et al., 2000). The results contrast with shell-model calculations (Caurier et al., 1998) that found $^{29}Ne$ unstable, and with Monte-Carlo shell model calculations (Utsuno et al., 1999) that also found $^{31}F$ unbound by a few hundred keV. The study enables comparison between RMF and shell-model approaches for understanding the reorganization of single-particle levels in very neutron-rich systems, and points to the possible necessity of including pionic degrees of freedom in the RMF framework to reproduce the disappearance of the N=20 shell closure at high isospin.
