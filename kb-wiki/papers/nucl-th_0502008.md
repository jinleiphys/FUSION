# Evolution of shower parton distributions in a jet from quark recombination model

> The evolution of shower parton distributions in a jet is investigated in the framework of quark recombination model. The distributions are parameterized and the $Q^2$ dependence of the parameters is given by polynomials of $\ln Q^2$ for a wide range of $Q^2$.

# Evolution of shower parton distributions in a jet from quark recombination model

## Key claim
The shower parton distributions in a jet can be parameterized and their $Q^2$ evolution described by polynomials of $\ln Q^2$ for a wide range of $Q^2$ from 2 to 1000 $({\rm GeV}/c)^2$, but only within the quark recombination model framework and using specific choices of fragmentation function parameterizations (Kretzer for quark jets, KKP for gluon jets).

## Method
The authors fit five independent parton distributions (valence quark non-singlet $K_{\rm NS}$, non-strange sea $L$, strange sea $L_s$, gluon non-strange $G$, gluon strange $G_s$) to five fragmentation functions for pions and kaons using the quark recombination model equations. The fragmentation functions at various $Q^2$ values are obtained from the Kretzer and KKP parameterizations, and the parton distributions are assumed to have the form $f(z)=Az^a(1-z)^b(1+cz^d)$. The $Q^2$ dependence of each of the five parameters $A, a, b, c, d$ is then fitted by a cubic polynomial in $u=\ln (Q^2/\Lambda^2)$ with the QCD scale $\Lambda=0.19 {\rm GeV}/c$.

## Key numbers
- The $Q^2$ range studied is from 2 to 1000 $({\rm GeV}/c)^2$.
- The QCD scale used is $\Lambda=0.19 {\rm GeV}/c$.
- The dimensionless scale is defined as $u=\ln (Q^2/\Lambda^2)$.
- The valence quark distribution satisfies the normalization condition $\int {dz\over z} K_{\rm NS}(z)=1$.
- The $Q^2$ dependence of each parameter is given by a cubic polynomial $A(Q^2)=C_0u^3+C_1u^2+C_2u+C_3$, requiring four constant coefficients per parameter.
- Fitted results are shown for $Q^2=10$ and $100$ $({\rm GeV}/c)^2$ in the figures.

## Context
This work extends the quark recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) which obtained shower parton distributions only at a fixed $Q^2=100$ $({\rm GeV}/c)^2$ from fragmentation functions. The paper provides the $Q^2$ evolution of these distributions, enabling their application to hadron production in different collision systems where hard partons may have varying virtualities. The results can be combined with soft parton distributions to model hadron production in heavy ion collisions, as previously done for AuAu and dAu collisions at RHIC.
