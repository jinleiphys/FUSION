# Representations for Three-Body T-Matrix on Unphysical Sheets: Proofs

> A proof is given for the explicit representations which have been formulated in the author's previous work (nucl-th/9505028) for the Faddeev components of three-body T-matrix continued analytically on unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface. Also, the analogous representations for analytical continuation of the three-body scattering matrices and resolvent are proved. An algorithm to search for the three-body resonances on the base of the Faddeev differential equations is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper provides a rigorous proof that the Faddeev components of the three-body T-matrix, scattering matrices, and resolvent can be analytically continued onto unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface using explicit representations expressed solely in terms of quantities defined on the physical sheet, provided the pair potentials decrease at least exponentially in coordinate space.

## Method
The author analytically continues the momentum-space Faddeev integral equations for the three-body T-matrix components onto both two-body and three-body unphysical sheets of the Riemann surface. This continuation is performed in the sense of distributions over the space of Fourier transforms of smooth compactly supported functions, by deforming integration contours in the complex plane of the relative momenta. The resulting representations express the continued T-matrix components in terms of the physical-sheet T-matrix, the pair scattering matrices, and the free resolvent, with the continuation encoded by a multi-index l that specifies which thresholds have been crossed.

## Key numbers
- The pair potentials are assumed to satisfy the estimate |v_α(k)| ≤ C(1+|k|)^{-N} e^{-2b|Im k|} for some b > 0, with the strip width 2b in the complex momentum plane (the second variant of potentials).
- The three-body continuous spectrum starts at λ_min = min_{α,j} λ_{α,j}, where λ_{α,j} are the negative eigenvalues of the pair subsystem Hamiltonians h_α.
- The domain where the continuation onto three-body sheets is valid is given by the parabolic region P_b = {z: Re z > -b² + (Im z)²/(4b²)}.
- The multi-index l has components l_0 = 0 or ±1 for the three-body threshold, and l_{α,j} = 0 or 1 for each two-body bound state threshold.

## Context
This work provides the complete proofs for the representations conjectured in the author's previous paper Motovilov (1995, nucl-th/9505028). It extends the earlier analytical continuation of s-wave Faddeev equations onto neighboring unphysical sheets by Orlov and collaborators (Orlov, Tur) for separable pair potentials to the general case of local potentials with exponential decay. The results enable a practical algorithm for computing three-body resonances by finding energies where the truncated scattering matrices (constructed from physical-sheet quantities) have zero eigenvalues, analogous to the two-body and multichannel scattering methods of Newton and Bohm.
