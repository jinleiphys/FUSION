# BCS-BEC crossover in three-dimensional Fermi gases with spherical spin-orbit coupling

> We present a systematic theoretical study of the BCS-BEC crossover problem in three-dimensional atomic Fermi gases at zero temperature with a spherical spin-orbit coupling which can be generated by a synthetic non-Abelian gauge field coupled to neutral fermions. Our investigations are based on the path integral formalism which is a powerful theoretical scheme for the study of the properties of the bound state, the superfluid ground state, and the collective excitations in the BCS-BEC crossover. At large spin-orbit coupling, the system enters the BEC state of a novel type of bound state (referred to as rashbon) which possesses a non-trivial effective mass. Analytical results and interesting universal behaviors for various physical quantities at large spin-orbit coupling are obtained. Our theoretical predictions can be tested in future experiments of cold Fermi gases with three-dimensional spherical spin-orbit coupling.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a three-dimensional Fermi gas with spherical spin-orbit coupling can undergo a smooth BCS-BEC crossover driven by tuning the spin-orbit coupling strength \(\lambda\) rather than the s-wave scattering length, even for negative values of \(1/(k_{\rm F}a_s)\) where no bound state exists without spin-orbit coupling. The hedge is that this conclusion is based on a mean-field treatment at zero temperature, and the crossover occurs specifically when the spin-orbit coupling is tuned from small to large values.

## Method

The authors use a functional path integral formalism at zero temperature to study a homogeneous three-dimensional Fermi gas with an s-wave contact attraction and a spherical (Weyl-type) spin-orbit coupling term \(\lambda \boldsymbol{\sigma} \cdot \hat{\bf p}\). They first solve the two-body problem to determine the binding energy and effective mass of the rashbon bound state, then apply a mean-field (saddle-point) approximation to the effective action to obtain the gap equation, number equation, and ground-state energy. The collective excitations are studied by expanding the effective action to quadratic order in fluctuations around the mean field.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the rashbon bound state at the unitary point (\(\lambda a_s \rightarrow \infty\)) is \(E_{\rm B} = 2\lambda^2\).
- The rashbon effective mass at the unitary point is \(m_{\rm B}/(2m) = 3(4+\sqrt{2})/14 \approx 1.16\).
- In the limit \(\kappa = 1/(\lambda a_s) \rightarrow +\infty\), the rashbon effective mass approaches \(m_{\rm B} \rightarrow 2m\); in the limit \(\kappa \rightarrow -\infty\), it approaches \(m_{\rm B} \rightarrow 6m\).
- The binding energy satisfies the algebraic equation \((E_{\rm B} - 2\lambda^2)/\sqrt{E_{\rm B} - \lambda^2} = 1/a_s\), with the bound state existing for any scattering length \(a_s\) provided \(E_{\rm B} > \lambda^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover in Fermi gases with spin-orbit coupling, previously investigated for two-dimensional systems by He and Huang (2012) and for three-dimensional systems with extreme oblate (Rashba) spin-orbit coupling by several groups (e.g., 3D1, 3D2). The paper specifically addresses the spherical (Weyl) spin-orbit coupling case, which preserves rotational symmetry and allows analytical results for the rashbon binding energy and effective mass. The results enable future experimental tests in cold Fermi gases with three-dimensional synthetic spin-orbit coupling generated by non-Abelian gauge fields, as proposed by 3DSOC.
