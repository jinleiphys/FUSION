# Adaptive stepsize and instabilities in complex Langevin dynamics

> Stochastic quantization offers the opportunity to simulate field theories with a complex action. In some theories unstable trajectories are prevalent when a constant stepsize is employed. We construct algorithms for generating an adaptive stepsize in complex Langevin simulations and find that unstable trajectories are completely eliminated. To illustrate the generality of the approach, we apply it to the three-dimensional XY model at nonzero chemical potential and the heavy dense limit of QCD.

## Key claim

Adaptive stepsize algorithms completely eliminate unstable trajectories in complex Langevin simulations, which the abstract presents as a definitive solution; however, the paper hedges by stating "we have no proof that stable evolution is guaranteed" and notes that instabilities are "not a question of principle for complex Langevin dynamics but one of numerical accuracy in following the trajectories."

## Method

The authors construct two adaptive stepsize algorithms for discretized complex Langevin equations. In the first formulation, the stepsize epsilon_n is set inversely proportional to the maximum drift term K^max_n, with a desired average stepsize bar-epsilon controlling the overall scale. In the second formulation, the product epsilon * K^max is kept within a factor p of a reference value K, and the stepsize is iteratively adjusted by that factor when the bound is exceeded. Both algorithms are applied to the three-dimensional XY model at nonzero chemical potential and to the heavy dense limit of QCD with Wilson fermions.

## Key numbers

- In the XY model, the adaptive stepsize algorithm was tested for parameter ranges 0.1 < beta < 2 and 0 < mu < 6, on lattice sizes up to 16^3, with input timestep bar-epsilon = 0.01; the time average of epsilon_n remained close to 0.01 in all cases.
- For the XY model at beta=0.55 on a 6^3 lattice, the action density fit gave <S>/Omega = -0.9433(7) - 0.502(4) mu^2 + 0.19(1) mu^4 for the full theory.
- For the XY model at beta=0.55 on a 6^3 lattice, the phase-quenched action density fit gave <S>_pq/Omega = -0.940(2) - 0.35(2) mu^2 - 0.04(3) mu^4.
- In the heavy dense QCD simulation with beta=5, kappa=0.12, mu=0.7 on a 2^4 lattice, the adaptive algorithm used K = 2e-4, p=2, initial epsilon=1e-5, N_sweep=16, and ran for N_iter=3e5 iterations yielding total Langevin time 48.

## Context

This work extends the complex Langevin approach of Parisi (1984) and Klauder (1983) by addressing the instability problem noted in earlier studies by Klauder (1985) and Ambjorn (1985, 1986). The adaptive stepsize method is demonstrated on the XY model at finite chemical potential, previously studied via world-line formulations by Chandrasekharan (2008), and on the heavy dense limit of QCD, building on the complex Langevin simulations of Aarts (2008). The results enable stable simulations of theories with complex actions at nonzero chemical potential, with the authors noting that convergence to the correct answer remains a separate issue to be addressed in future work.
