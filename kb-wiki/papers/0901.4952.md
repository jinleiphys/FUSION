# Non-extensive statistical effects in high-energy collisions

> Following the basic prescriptions of the relativistic Tsallis' non-extensive thermostatistics, we investigate from a phenomenological point of view the relevance of non-extensive statistical effects on relativistic heavy-ion collisions observable, such as rapidity spectra of net proton production, transverse momentum distributions and transverse momentum fluctuations. Moreover, we study the nuclear and the subnuclear equation of state, investigating the critical densities of a phase transition to a hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of the electric and the baryon charges. The relevance of small deviations from the standard extensive statistics is studied in the context of intermediate and high energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, parameterized by the Tsallis non-extensive parameter \(q\), can account for experimental observations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that are not reproduced by the standard extensive statistical approach; however, the extracted \(q\) values differ significantly between longitudinal (rapidity) and transverse observables, indicating that the non-extensive effects reflect different physical mechanisms (non-equilibrium dynamics versus thermal equilibrium) rather than a single universal parameter.

## Method

The authors apply relativistic Tsallis non-extensive thermostatistics to model heavy-ion collision observables, using a non-linear Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space to describe net-proton rapidity spectra and a Tsallis-like equilibrium distribution to fit transverse momentum spectra and compute transverse momentum fluctuations. They also derive the non-extensive equation of state for hadronic and quark-gluon matter and apply Gibbs conditions with global conservation of electric and baryon charges to study the hadron-quark mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- For net-proton rapidity spectra at RHIC (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV), the fit uses \(q=1.485\) with \(\tau_{int}=0.47\).
- For net-proton rapidity spectra at SPS (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), the fit uses \(q=1.235\) with \(\tau_{int}=0.84\).
- For net-proton rapidity spectra at AGS (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5\) GeV), the fit uses \(q=1.09\) with \(\tau_{int}=0.95\).
- For neutral pion invariant yields in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, the fit uses \(q=1.038\) with \(T=140\) MeV.
- For neutral pion invariant yields in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the fit uses \(q=1.07\) with \(T=160\) MeV.
- The statistical transverse momentum fluctuation measure \(\Phi^{st}_{p_\perp}\) for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV is calculated as 5 MeV at \(T=170\) MeV and \(\mu=60\) MeV using \(q=1.038\), compared to the standard (\(q=1\)) value of 24.7 MeV.
- The extrapolated \(q\) value for LHC net-proton rapidity spectra is \(q=1.68\).

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis non-extensive thermostatistics (Tsallis, 1988) to heavy-ion collision phenomenology, building on prior studies of power-law spectra and non-equilibrium effects by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000), Biro and collaborators (2004, 2005), and Wolschin's three-component diffusion model (2004, 2008). The results enable a unified framework for describing both longitudinal and transverse observables, and the study of the nuclear equation of state with non-extensive effects opens the possibility to investigate the isospin dependence of the hadron-quark phase transition critical densities, which could be tested with future neutron-rich beam experiments.
