# Strange quark stars: the role of excluded volume effects

> We study cold strange quark stars employing an enhanced version of the quark-mass density-dependent model which incorporates excluded volume effects to address non-perturbative QCD repulsive interactions. We provide a comparative analysis of our mass formula parametrization with previous models from the literature. We identify the regions within the parameter space where three-flavor quark matter is more stable than the most tightly bound atomic nucleus (stability window). Specifically, we show that excluded volume effects do not change the Gibbs free energy per baryon at zero pressure, rendering the stability window unaffected. The curves of pressure versus energy density exhibit various shapes -- convex upward, concave downward, or nearly linear -- depending on the mass parametrization. This behavior results in different patterns of increase, decrease, or constancy in the speed of sound as a function of baryon number density. We analyze the mass-radius relationship of strange quark stars, revealing a significant increase in maximum gravitational mass and a shift in the curves towards larger radii as the excluded volume effect intensifies. Excluded volume effects render our models compatible with all modern astrophysical constraints, including the properties of the recently observed low-mass compact object HESSJ1731.

## Key claim

Excluded volume effects in the quark-mass density-dependent model do not alter the Gibbs free energy per baryon at zero pressure, leaving the stability window for three-flavor quark matter unchanged, but they significantly increase the maximum gravitational mass of strange quark stars and shift mass-radius curves toward larger radii.

## Method

The authors employed an enhanced quark-mass density-dependent model that incorporates excluded volume effects to simulate non-perturbative QCD repulsive interactions in cold strange quark matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relationships for strange quark stars, using different parametrizations of the quark mass dependence and excluded volume parameters. The stability window was determined by comparing the Gibbs free energy per baryon of three-flavor quark matter at zero pressure with that of the most tightly bound atomic nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the quark-mass density-dependent model by adding excluded volume effects to better capture non-perturbative QCD repulsion, building on prior studies of strange quark stars using similar mass parametrizations. The results enable more realistic modeling of compact objects and show compatibility with observations of the low-mass compact object HESSJ1731, which previous models struggled to explain. The finding that the stability window is unaffected by excluded volume effects contrasts with some earlier expectations that repulsive interactions might shift the conditions for stable strange quark matter.
