# Understanding the newly observed heavy pentaquark candidates

> We find that several thresholds can contribute to the enhancements of the newly observed heavy pentaquark candidates $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ via the anomalous triangle singularity (ATS) transitions in the specific kinematics of $Λ_b\to J/ψK^- p$. Apart from the observed two peaks we find that another peaks around 4.5 GeV can also be produced by the ATS. We also show that the $Σ_c^{(*)}$ can be produced at leading order in $Λ_b$ decay. This process is different from the triangle diagram and its threshold enhancement only appears as CUSP effects if there is no pole structure or the ATS involved. The threshold interaction associated with the presence of the ATS turns out to be a general phenomenon and plays a crucial role in the understanding of candidates for exotic states.

# Understanding the newly observed heavy pentaquark candidates

## Key claim
The observed enhancements at the $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ masses in $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi K^- p$ can be produced by anomalous triangle singularity (ATS) transitions from several thresholds, rather than necessarily requiring genuine pentaquark pole structures; the ATS can mimic the experimental peaks, and a third peak around 4.5 GeV is also predicted from the $\Lambda_c(2625)\bar{D}(1865)$ threshold.

## Method
The authors analyze triangle and two-point loop diagrams for $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi K^- p$ where intermediate states ($\chi_{cJ}p$, $\Lambda_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$, $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$) can go on-shell simultaneously, satisfying the ATS condition. They compute the $J/\psi p$ invariant mass distributions from these loops, using Breit-Wigner propagators for broad intermediate states, and examine the kinematic dependence by applying different $K^-p$ invariant mass cuts as in the LHCb analysis.

## Key numbers
- The $\chi_{c1}p$ threshold is at 4.449 GeV, and the ATS from this loop exactly locates at the mass position of the observed $P_c(4450)$.
- The $\Lambda_c(2595)\bar{D}$ threshold is at 4.457 GeV; the narrow peak around this threshold can match the $P_c(4450)$ structure when the intermediate $\bar{D}_{sJ}^{(*)}$ mass is taken around 2.86 GeV.
- The $\Lambda_c(2625)\bar{D}(1865)$ threshold at 4.493 GeV gives rise to a third predicted peak around 4.5 GeV.
- The $\Sigma_c(2455)\bar{D}(1865)$ threshold is at 4.321 GeV and $\Sigma_c(2520)\bar{D}(1865)$ at 4.385 GeV, near the $P_c(4380)$ mass.
- The $\chi_{c2}p$ contribution is estimated to be of order $10^{-1}$ relative to $\chi_{c1}p$, based on the ratio of branching fractions $BR(B^+\to K^+\chi_{c2})/BR(B^+\to K^+\chi_{c1})\sim 10^{-1}$.
- The ATS condition for $\chi_{cJ}p$ loops requires $\Lambda^*$ masses in the ranges 1.92-2.20 GeV ($\chi_{c0}$), 1.89-2.11 GeV ($\chi_{c1}$), and 1.83-2.06 GeV ($\chi_{c2}$).

## Context
This work extends the ATS mechanism previously applied to threshold states by Guo (2014) and the pole-trajectory analysis of $\chi_{c1}p$ loops by Guo (2015). It contrasts with molecular-state interpretations by Chen (2015), Karliner (2015), and Roca (2015), which treat $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ as $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*$ molecules, by showing that $\Sigma_c^{(*)}$ can be produced at leading order in $\Lambda_b$ decay and that kinematic singularities can produce the observed structures without requiring dynamical poles. The results enable experimental discrimination between ATS and pole mechanisms through the kinematic dependence of the $J/\psi p$ spectrum under different $K^-p$ mass cuts, and suggest $\Lambda_b^0\to K^- h_c p$ as a promising channel to distinguish $\chi_{cJ}p$ from $\Lambda_c^*\bar{D}^{(*)}$ rescatterings.
