# Neutron matter with chiral EFT interactions: Perturbative and first QMC calculations

> Neutron matter presents a unique system in chiral effective field theory (EFT), because all many-body forces among neutrons are predicted to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). We discuss perturbative and first Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of neutron matter with chiral EFT interactions and their astrophysical impact for the equation of state and neutron stars.

# Neutron matter with chiral EFT interactions: Perturbative and first QMC calculations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions with low cutoffs (around 400 MeV) are perturbative in neutron matter, validated by first nonperturbative Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) benchmarks, but the convergence from N2LO to N3LO reduces the uncertainty band by only a factor of 2/3 rather than the expected 1/3, which the authors attribute to Delta-resonance effects not yet included at N3LO.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative many-body calculations of neutron-matter energy at Hartree-Fock, second-order, and third-order levels using multiple chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials at N2LO and N3LO (EGM and EM families with various cutoffs), including N2LO three-nucleon (3N) forces and all N3LO 3N and four-nucleon (4N) forces at the Hartree-Fock level. They also developed local chiral EFT NN interactions suitable for QMC methods and carried out the first Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of neutron matter with these interactions at LO, NLO, and N2LO, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to handle spin-dependent Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density n0, the complete N3LO neutron-matter energy per particle is E/N = 14.1–21.0 MeV, based on different NN potentials, variation of couplings c1 = −(0.75–1.13) GeV⁻¹ and c3 = −(4.77–5.51) GeV⁻¹, 3N/4N cutoff variation Λ = 2–2.5 fm⁻¹, and many-body uncertainty.
- For EGM potentials only at N3LO, the energy range at n0 is 14.1–18.4 MeV per particle; the corresponding N2LO band is 15.5–21.4 MeV per particle, showing a band reduction by a factor of 2/3.
- The symmetry energy Sv at saturation density is predicted to be 28.9–34.9 MeV, with density derivative L = 43.0–66.6 MeV.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the radius range predicted is 9.7–13.9 km, with a maximal neutron star mass of 3.1 solar masses and corresponding radius of about 14 km.
- The local chiral NN interactions were fit to the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis at laboratory energies 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, and 100 MeV, with cutoff variation R0 = 0.8–1.2 fm; the neutron-neutron scattering length was fit to ann = −18.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of neutron matter (Hebeler et al., 2010) by including all N3LO many-body forces and providing the first QMC benchmarks for chiral interactions. The results are compared with NLO lattice calculations and QMC simulations at low densities (Gezerlis and Carlson, 2008), and with variational calculations based on phenomenological potentials (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) and Auxiliary Field Diffusion MC calculations (Gandolfi, Carlson, and Reddy, 2012) at nuclear densities. The neutron-matter constraints are used to predict neutron star structure, following the methods of Hebeler et al. (2010, 2013), and are compared with equations of state used in core-collapse supernova simulations (Lattimer and Swesty, 1991; Shen et al., 2011; Hempel et al., 2012; Steiner et al., 2012). The local chiral interactions developed here enable future nonperturbative studies of neutron matter including three-nucleon forces.
