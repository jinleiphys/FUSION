# ππ, Kπand πN potential scattering and a prediction of a narrow σmeson resonance

> Low energy scattering and bound state properties of the πN, ππand Kπsystems are studied as coupled channel problems using inversion potentials of phase shift data. In a first step we apply the potential model to explain recent measurements of pionic hydrogen shift and width. Secondly, predictions of the model for pionium lifetime and shift confirm a well known and widely used effective range expression. Thirdly, as extension of this confirmation, we predict an unexpected medium effect of the pionium lifetime which shortens by several orders of magnitude. The σmeson shows a narrow resonance structure as a function of the medium modified mass with the implication of being essentially energy independent. Similarly, we see this medium resonance effect realized for the Kπsystem. To support our findings we present also results for the ρmeson and the Δ(1232) resonance.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a narrow $\sigma$ meson resonance (width $\Gamma \approx 1$ MeV) that appears not as a function of energy but as a function of an effective mass parameter in the coupled-channel equations, implying the resonance is essentially energy-independent and manifests only as a medium effect in the $T=0$ $\pi\pi$ channel and $T=1/2$ $K\pi$ channel.

## Method

The authors use quantum inversion (Gelfand–Levitan and Marchenko methods) to generate local, energy-independent potentials from experimental phase shift data for $\pi N$, $\pi\pi$, and $K\pi$ scattering. These potentials are then employed in coupled-channel Schrodinger equations to study pionic hydrogen and pionium as resonances in charge-exchange channels, and to investigate medium effects by varying the effective mass (reduced mass) in the two-body wave equation while keeping the potential matrix fixed.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths from $\pi N$ s-wave inversion potentials: $a_1 = 0.178 \, m_\pi^{-1}$ and $a_3 = -0.088 \, m_\pi^{-1}$, yielding $\pi NN$ coupling constant $g_{\pi NN}^2 / 4\pi = 13.84$
- Pionic hydrogen $1s$ Coulomb reference energy: $E_{1s}^C = 3234.9408$ eV
- Pionium $1s$ Coulomb reference energy: $E_{1s}^C = 1.85807248$ keV
- $\sigma$ meson resonance width: $\Gamma \approx 1$ MeV (from $\pi\pi$ $T=0$ s-wave as function of effective mass)
- Pionium lifetime shortens by more than three orders of magnitude with small variations of the reduced masses (approximately $\pm 1\%$ change in potential strength)
- $K\pi$ $T=1/2$ s-wave resonance width: $\Gamma > 80$ MeV (increases with laboratory kinetic energy)
- Barrier radius for $\Delta$, $\rho$, $\sigma$, and $K_0^\ast$ resonances: $r \approx 0.16$ fm (barrier height $\sim 20$ GeV for $\pi N$ $P_{33}$)
- $K\pi$ physical reduced mass: $m = 215.94$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the one solitary boson exchange model (OSBEP) for NN scattering by Jae et al. (1997) to study $\pi\pi$, $K\pi$, and $\pi N$ systems as interacting multi-pion and nucleon systems. It contradicts the prevailing view that the $\sigma$ meson is a broad resonance in free $\pi\pi$ scattering (with width up to 600 MeV from analyses such as Harada et al. (1996) and Törnqvist (1996)), instead predicting a narrow resonance that appears only as a medium effect. The results enable a dynamical treatment of resonant particles ($\rho$, $\sigma$, $\Delta$, etc.) in NN interaction models above meson production threshold, and suggest that correlated two-pion exchange in nuclear environments should use an effective mass rather than the physical pion mass.
