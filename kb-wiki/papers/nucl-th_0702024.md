# Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator for the few-nucleon system from chiral dynamics

> We present an exploratory study consisting in the formulation of a relativistic quantum mechanics to describe the few-nucleon system at low energy, starting from the quantum field theoretical chiral Lagrangian involving pions and nucleons. To this aim we construct a Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator and perform a truncation of the Fock space which respects at each stage the relativistic covariance. Such truncation is justified, at sufficiently low energy, in the framework of a systematic chiral expansion. As an illustration we discuss the bound state observables and low-energy phaseshifts of the nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering at the leading order of our scheme.

# Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator for the few-nucleon system from chiral dynamics

## Key claim
The authors present an exploratory formulation of a relativistic quantum mechanics for the few-nucleon system at low energy, constructed from the chiral Lagrangian, but emphasize that the work is limited to leading order and the emphasis is on presenting the formalism rather than on the accuracy of the description.

## Method
The authors construct a Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator in the point-form of relativistic dynamics, starting from the chiral Lagrangian involving pions and nucleons. They perform a truncation of the Fock space justified by a systematic chiral expansion, and apply the framework to the 1-nucleon sector (π-N scattering) and the 2-nucleon sector (NN bound and scattering states) at leading order. The mass operator is defined to be diagonal in the four-velocity using velocity states, with a Gaussian structure function f(m,m') containing a cutoff Λ to regulate integrals and compensate for neglected off-diagonal terms.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff Λ is varied between 300 and 500 MeV for the π-N scattering calculations
- The π-N S-wave scattering lengths are reproduced by adjusting the Weinberg-Tomozawa coupling constant
- The axial coupling g_A is fixed from peripheral NN phaseshifts
- The NN phaseshifts show reasonable agreement with experiment up to center-of-mass kinetic energies of 100 MeV
- The π-N phaseshifts are described at small laboratory momentum less than 50 MeV
- The nucleon mass renormalization counts formally as O(p^3) in the chiral expansion
- The chiral expansion hierarchy for the two-nucleon sector gives O(p^{-2}) + O(p^0) + O(p^2) + ... for successive terms

## Context
This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas construction in the point-form of relativistic dynamics, previously implemented for bound state problems in chiral quark models (Klink 2000), to nucleons interacting with pions according to chiral symmetry. It differs from current ChPT approaches to few-nucleon systems (Weinberg, van Kolck, Epelbaum) by treating relativity exactly from the beginning rather than performing a non-relativistic reduction. The formalism enables a quantitative comparison between relativistic and chiral corrections, and can naturally extend beyond the threshold of pion production, unlike non-relativistic quantum-mechanical treatments.
