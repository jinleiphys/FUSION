# Towards a very precise knowledge of theta13

> Recent experimental developments towards obtaining a very precise value of the third neutrino mixing angle, $θ_{13}$, are summarized. Various implications of the measured value of this angle are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes recent experimental progress toward a precise measurement of the third neutrino mixing angle θ₁₃, noting that contrary to earlier expectations of a possible zero value, the angle turned out to be relatively large, but not large enough to invalidate the perturbative expansion in powers of its sine that is widely used in solar neutrino physics.

## Method

The paper reviews results from reactor neutrino disappearance experiments (Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz) and the accelerator-based T2K appearance experiment, comparing their measured values of sin²2θ₁₃. It then examines the implications of the measured θ₁₃ for solar neutrino survival probability calculations, supernova neutrino oscillations, and nucleosynthesis yields, including a Bayesian analysis of meteoritic data for ¹¹B and ⁷Li.

## Key numbers

- Daya Bay measured sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.089 ± 0.010 (stat) ± 0.005 (syst)
- RENO measured sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.113 ± 0.013 (stat) ± 0.019 (syst)
- Double Chooz measured sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.109 ± 0.030 (stat) ± 0.025 (syst)
- T2K (assuming θ₂₃ = π/4) measured sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.104 +0.060 −0.045
- The expansion parameter α for a 10 MeV solar neutrino with δm²₃₂ ∼ 2 × 10⁻³ eV² and electron density ∼100 N_A/cm³ is α ∼ 3 × 10⁻²
- Using the Daya Bay value, the correction to the cos⁴θ₁₃ term in the solar survival probability is 0.27%, and the correction to the sin⁴θ₁₃ term is about 11.7% (changing it from 5.2 × 10⁻⁴ to 5.8 × 10⁻⁴)

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on neutrino mixing angles following the first direct measurement of θ₁₃ by Daya Bay (An 2012), which confirmed earlier hints from T2K (Abe 2011) and resolved the slight tension between solar and reactor neutrino experiments noted by Balantekin (2008) and Fogli (2008). The measured value enables more precise solar neutrino calculations using the formula of Balantekin (2011) and Fogli (2001), and opens the possibility of using supernova nucleosynthesis yields of ¹¹B and ⁷Li, as studied by Yoshida (2005) and Mathews (2011), to probe the neutrino mass hierarchy.
