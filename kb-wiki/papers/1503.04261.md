# Alpha cluster structures and monopole excitations in $^{13}$C

> The structure of $1/2^{\pm}$ states in $^{13}$C up to around the $3öÝ+n$ threshold ($E_x = 12.3$~MeV) is investigated with a full four-body $3öÝ+n$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculation, where the $3öÝ$ OCM, the model space of which is the subspace of the $3öÝ+n$ model, describes well the structure of the low-lying states of $^{12}$C including the $2^{+}_2$, $0^{+}_3$, and $0^{+}_4$ states, which have been recently observed above the Hoyle state ($0^+_2$). A full spectrum up to the $1/2^{-}_5$ ($1/2^{+}_3$) state is reproduced consistently with the lowest five $1/2^{-}$ (three $1/2^{+}$) states of experimental spectrum. It is shown that the $1/2^{-}_2$ and $1/2^{-}_3$ states are characterized by the dominant cluster configurations of $^{9}$Be($3/2^-$,$1/2^-$)+$öÝ$, while the ground state $1/2^-_1$ has a shell-model-like structure. The observed monopole transition strengths to the $1/2^{-}_{2,3}$ states are consistently reproduced for the first time. These results indicate that the excited $1/2^-$ states have cluster structures. They are compared to those by the previous work with the shell model. On the other hand, the $1/2^{+}_{1}$ state is found to have a loosely bound neutron structure in which the extra neutron moves around $^{12}$C(g.s) core with $1S$ orbit, reflecting the fact that this state appears by $1.9$ MeV just below the $^{12}$C(g.s)+$n$ threshold, while the $1/2^{+}_{2}$ and $1/2^{+}_{3}$ states are characterized by $^{9}$Be+$öÝ$ structures. We found that the $1/2^+_5$ state located above the $3öÝ+n$ threshold is the Hoyle analogue state in $^{13}$C, the wave function of which is described by product states of constituent clusters, $(0S)^3_öÝ(S)_n$, with the probability of $52$ %.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the excited $1/2^{-}_{2,3}$ states in $^{13}$C have dominant alpha-cluster structures ($^{9}$Be+$\alpha$ configurations) rather than shell-model configurations, and that the observed monopole transition strengths to these states are consistently reproduced for the first time by the four-body $3\alpha+n$ orthogonality condition model, whereas previous shell-model calculations had difficulty reproducing them.

## Method

The authors performed a full four-body $3\alpha+n$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM), with the model space large enough to cover $3\alpha+n$ gas, $^{12}$C+$n$, $^{9}$Be+$\alpha$, and $^{8}$Be+$^{5}$He cluster configurations as well as shell-model configurations. The Hamiltonian included effective $\alpha$-$\alpha$ potentials from the Schmid-Wildermuth force, $\alpha$-$n$ potentials from the Kanada-Kaneko potential with parity dependence, and phenomenological three-body and four-body forces fitted to reproduce the energies of $^{9}$Be and the $^{13}$C ground state. The Pauli-blocking effect among clusters was taken into account by removing Pauli-forbidden states between $\alpha$ particles and between $\alpha$ and neutron.

## Key numbers

- The $1/2^{+}_{1}$ state appears 1.9 MeV just below the $^{12}$C(g.s)+$n$ threshold ($E_x = 4.95$ MeV)
- The $1/2^{+}_5$ state located above the $3\alpha+n$ threshold ($E_x = 12.3$ MeV) has a wave function described by product states $(0S)^3_{\alpha}(S)_n$ with 52% probability, identified as the Hoyle analogue state in $^{13}$C
- The $3\alpha+n$ threshold is at $E_x = 12.3$ MeV (abstract) or $E_x = 13.22$ MeV (text); the paper uses both values
- The $^{12}$C(g.s)+$n$ threshold is at $E_x = 4.95$ MeV
- The $^{9}$Be(g.s)+$\alpha$ threshold is at $E_x = 10.68$ MeV
- The $^{12}$C(Hoyle)+$n$ threshold is at $E_x = 12.60$ MeV
- The four-body force strength $V_0(Q=9) = 2.0$ MeV reproduces the ground state energy of $^{13}$C relative to the $3\alpha+n$ threshold
- The three-body force parameters: $\beta_{3\alpha}=0.15$ fm$^{-2}$, $V_{3\alpha}^{(0)}=31.7$ MeV, $\eta_{3\alpha}=(63.0/31.7 -1)/6$, giving strengths 31.7, 63.0, and 136.0 MeV for $L_{3\alpha}=0,2,4$ respectively

## Context

This work extends the successful $3\alpha$ OCM studies of $^{12}$C by Kurokawa (2005) and Ohtsubo (2013), which reproduced the Hoyle state and newly observed $0^{+}_3$, $0^{+}_4$, and $2^{+}_2$ states, to the $^{13}$C system by adding an extra neutron. It addresses the difficulty encountered by shell-model calculations of Millener (1989) and Navratil (2007) in reproducing the C0 monopole transition strengths to the $1/2^{-}_{2,3}$ states. The results enable further exploration of Hoyle-analogue states in neutron-rich light nuclei and provide a framework for studying how cluster structures change when an extra neutron is added to $^{12}$C, which has shell-model-like ($0^+_1$), $3\alpha$-gas-like ($0^+_2$), higher-nodal $^8$Be($0^+$)+$\alpha$ cluster ($0^+_3$), and linear-chain-like ($0^+_4$) structures.
