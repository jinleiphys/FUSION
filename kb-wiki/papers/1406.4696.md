# Effects of three-nucleon forces and two-body currents on Gamow-Teller strengths

> We optimize chiral interactions at next-to-next-to leading order to observables in two- and three-nucleon systems, and compute Gamow-Teller transitions in carbon-14, oxygen-22 and oxygen-24 using consistent two-body currents. We compute spectra of the daughter nuclei nitrogen-14, fluorine-22 and fluorine-24 via an isospin-breaking coupled-cluster technique, with several predictions. The two-body currents reduce the Ikeda sum rule, corresponding to a quenching factor q^2 ~ 0.84-0.92 of the axial-vector coupling. The half life of carbon-14 depends on the energy of the first excited 1+ state, the three-nucleon force, and the two-body current.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two-body currents from chiral effective field theory reduce the Ikeda sum rule for Gamow-Teller transitions in carbon-14, oxygen-22, and oxygen-24, yielding a quenching factor q^2 of approximately 0.84-0.92 of the axial-vector coupling, but this quenching depends on the specific nucleus and on the low-energy constant c_D constrained by triton beta decay. The anomalously long half-life of carbon-14 depends sensitively on the energy of the first excited 1+ state in nitrogen-14, the three-nucleon force, and the two-body current, with the latter partially counteracting the suppression from three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors optimized chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) to proton-proton and proton-neutron scattering data below 125 MeV laboratory energy and to deuteron observables, for three chiral cutoffs (450, 500, 550 MeV). They optimized the two low-energy constants c_D and c_E of the three-nucleon force to reproduce the ground-state energies of A=3 nuclei and the triton lifetime, then computed Gamow-Teller transitions in carbon-14, oxygen-22, and oxygen-24 using consistent two-body currents within a coupled-cluster framework with an N=12 model space and Hartree-Fock reference states. The daughter nuclei nitrogen-14, fluorine-22, and fluorine-24 were computed via a novel isospin-breaking generalization of the coupled-cluster equation-of-motion approach.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor q^2 = 0.84-0.92 from two-body currents for carbon-14, oxygen-22, and oxygen-24, compared to the Ikeda sum rule value 3(N-Z)
- Ground-state energies of mother nuclei at N=12, hbar-Omega=22 MeV, Lambda_chi=500 MeV: -74.4 MeV for carbon-14, -104.6 MeV for oxygen-22, -105.7 MeV for oxygen-24
- Ground-state energies of daughter nuclei relative to mother nuclei: 0.54 MeV for nitrogen-14, -2.62 MeV for fluorine-22, -6.55 MeV for fluorine-24
- Transition matrix element for carbon-14 beta decay with three-nucleon forces and two-body currents: between 5x10^{-3} and 2x10^{-2}, compared to empirical value approximately 6x10^{-4} from the 5700-year half-life
- First excited 1+ state in nitrogen-14: 5.69 MeV (Lambda_chi=450 MeV), 4.41 MeV (Lambda_chi=500 MeV), 3.35 MeV (Lambda_chi=550 MeV), compared to experimental 3.95 MeV
- Only 70%-80% of total beta decay strength S_+ is exhausted below 10 MeV excitation energy

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Holt (2008, 2009) and Maris (2011) that attributed the long half-life of carbon-14 to three-nucleon forces but lacked two-body currents, and by Vaintraub (2009), Menendez (2011), and Engel (2014) who showed chiral two-body currents yield effective quenching of g_A but used Hamiltonians inconsistent with the currents or approximated two-body currents via Fermi-sea averaging. The results enable future calculations of weak transitions in medium-mass nuclei with consistent interactions and currents from chiral effective field theory, and provide predictions and spin assignments for the exotic neutron-rich isotopes fluorine-22 and fluorine-24 that can be tested experimentally.
