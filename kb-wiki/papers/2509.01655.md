# The N$^3$LO Twist-2 Matching of Helicity TMDs and SIDIS $q_\ast$ Spectrum

> We compute the twist-2 matching of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) helicity parton distribution and fragmentation functions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in QCD. This calculation entails the complete set of next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) splitting functions govering the evolution of helicity-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs). Within TMD factorization framework, we quantify the impact of radiative corrections by completing the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N$^3$LL) prediction for lepton-hadron transverse momentum imbalance in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS). Our results provide the most precise theoretical input for probing the helicity structure and confined motion of quarks and gluons at future electron-ion collider (EIC).

## Key claim

The paper computes the twist-2 matching coefficients for helicity TMD parton distribution and fragmentation functions at N$^3$LO in QCD, but this result is conditional on the assumption that the TMD soft function obeys naive Casimir scaling up to this order, which holds only for processes with no more than three Wilson lines and may be violated at N$^4$LL by quartic Casimir contributions.

## Method

The author derives a TMD factorization formula for the lepton-hadron transverse momentum imbalance $q_\ast$ in SIDIS using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), separating hard, collinear, and soft sectors. The matching coefficients for helicity TMDs are computed at N$^3$LO by evaluating the hard matching coefficients and extracting the full set of NNLO polarized DGLAP splitting functions in analytic form. The TMD soft function is treated via non-abelian exponentiation with dipole color structure, and the rapidity renormalization group evolution is performed using the exponential regulator scheme.

## Key numbers

* The unpolarized born-level cross section is $\sigma^{\text{U}}_0 = 2\pi \alpha^2 \frac{1+(1-y)^2}{Q^2}$ and the polarized counterpart is $\sigma^{\text{L}}_0 = 2\pi \alpha^2 \frac{1-(1-y)^2}{Q^2}$, where $Q^2$ is the virtuality of the exchanged photon.
* The TMD soft function obeys naive Casimir scaling up to $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^4)$, with $C_i = C_F$ for fundamental representation and $C_i = C_A$ for adjoint representation.
* The rapidity anomalous dimension $\gamma^i_R$ satisfies $\gamma_i^S = C_i \gamma^S$ and $\gamma^i_R = C_i \gamma_R$ up to N$^3$LL, with strict Casimir scaling violated at N$^4$LL due to quartic Casimirs.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative framework for helicity-dependent TMDs, which previously lacked full N$^3$LO matching coefficients, placing helicity observables on equal theoretical footing with unpolarized TMDs that have reached approximately N$^4$LL accuracy in global fits (Scimemi 2019, Moos 2023, Bacchetta 2017). The computed NNLO polarized DGLAP splitting functions complete the evolution equations for helicity PDFs and FFs, building on recent progress by Moch (2014), Blumlein (2021), and others, and enable N$^3$LL-resummed predictions for the $q_\ast$ spectrum at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
