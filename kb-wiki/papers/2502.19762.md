# Sensitivity of neutron drip lines and neutron star properties to the symmetry energy

> We investigate the influence of the nuclear symmetry energy and its density slope parameter on the neutron dripline and neutron star properties using a semi-classical liquid drop model (LDM) and energy density functionals constrained by chiral effective field theory. To analyze finite nuclei and mass tables, the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed, and the surface tension is determined to minimize the root-mean-square deviation of the total binding energy for 2208 nuclei. Correlations between symmetry energy parameters and neutron driplines, crust-core transition densities, and the radii of $1.4\,\msun$ neutron stars are explored using the LDM framework. Additionally, we examine the relationship between macroscopic properties, such as neutron star radii ($R_{1.4}$), and microscopic properties, including the number of isotopes and the last bound nucleus for $Z=28$, within the LDM context.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the nuclear symmetry energy and its density slope parameter correlate with both the location of the neutron dripline and neutron star properties, but the correlations depend strongly on the inclusion of shell corrections and the specific region of the nuclear chart; the authors find that the compressible liquid drop model with shell effects gives a neutron dripline for nickel isotopes in the range N=66-82, with only moderate dependence on the symmetry energy value.

## Method
The authors employed an incompressible liquid drop model (LDM) and a compressible LDM, both constrained by chiral effective field theory, to calculate nuclear binding energies for 2208 nuclei with Z ≥ 20 and A ≥ 36. The surface tension was determined by minimizing the root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) of total binding energies, and the neutron dripline was identified by requiring S_n > 0, S_p > 0, S_{2n} > 0, and S_{2p} > 0 across a sufficiently large set of (A,Z) combinations. The compressible LDM incorporated variations in central density and allowed the symmetry energy S_v and its slope parameter L to vary independently.

## Key numbers
- RMSD of 0.949 MeV for the incompressible LDM with shell corrections fitted to 2208 nuclei
- RMSD values for the compressible LDM range from 1.004 MeV (S_v=34 MeV, L=50 MeV) to 1.784 MeV (S_v=28 MeV, L=70 MeV)
- The incompressible LDM predicts 8425 bound nuclei with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 119
- The number of nickel (Z=28) isotopes ranges from 36 (S_v=34 MeV, L=30 MeV) to 48 (S_v=28 MeV, L=70 MeV)
- The symmetry energy parameters from the compressible LDM give a minimum RMSD near (S_v, L) = (32.5, 45.0) MeV within the constrained band
- The surface diffuseness parameter d = 0.55 fm was used for Coulomb energy calculations
- Saturation density n_0 = 0.155 fm^{-3} was used for the LDM, giving r_0 = 1.155 fm

## Context
This work extends the liquid drop model framework of Myers (1966) and Steiner (2005) by incorporating chiral effective field theory constraints on the symmetry energy. The authors compare their results with nuclear mass models including FRDM (Moller 2012), Skyrme force averages (Dutra 2012), and UNEDF0/1/2 (Kortelainen 2010-2014), finding that most mass models prefer smaller S_v and L values than those favored by neutron star observations. The study enables joint investigations of finite nuclei properties and neutron star crust-core transition densities, building on prior work by Lattimer (2012) and Oyamatsu (2007) on symmetry energy correlations.
