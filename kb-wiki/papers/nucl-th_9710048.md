# Production and decay of the d* dibaryon

> The production of the isoscalar Jp = 3+ didelta dibaryon d* by proton inelastic scattering from deuteron targets is described in a double-scattering Glauber approximation. Each scattering changes a target nucleon into a Delta with the help of the isovector tensor force transmitted by pi and rho mesons. The differential cross section constructed from empirical Love-Franey nucleon-nucleon t-matrices and a simple model of d* shows a maximum of some 10 micro-b/sr at 70 deg (c.m.) for 500 MeV protons. The partial width of the decay d* -> NN caused by the exchanges of the same mesons is found for this simple model of d* to be about 9 MeV if the d* mass is 2100 MeV. The implications of these results are discussed.

## Key claim
The production cross section for the $d^*$ dibaryon in $pd$ inelastic scattering is calculated using a double-scattering Glauber approximation and a simple model of the $d^*$ as a pure $\Delta^2$ Gaussian wavefunction, yielding a maximum differential cross section of about 13 $\mu$b/sr at 70° (c.m.) for 500 MeV protons, but this result is highly sensitive to the choice of $NN$ dynamics and the $d^*$ wavefunction size, and the calculation neglects quark exchange and delocalization effects.

## Method
The production of the $d^*$ dibaryon via $p(d, p)d^*$ is described using a double-scattering Glauber approximation, where each scattering converts a target nucleon into a $\Delta$ via the isovector tensor force from $\pi$ and $\rho$ meson exchanges. The differential cross section is constructed using empirical Love-Franey (LF) nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrices and a simple Gaussian model for the $d^*$ as a didelta ($\Delta^2$) object, with the deuteron described by a three-term Gaussian fit to the Bonn C S-state wavefunction. The partial width for the decay $d^* \rightarrow NN$ is calculated using the same meson-exchange interactions.

## Key numbers
- The differential production cross section for $pd \rightarrow pd^*$ shows a maximum of about **13 $\mu$b/sr** at **70° (c.m.)** for a $d^*$ mass of **2100 MeV** and a proton beam energy of **500 MeV**, when using the average lab energy prescription for the $NN$ $t$-matrix.
- The partial width for the decay $d^* \rightarrow NN$ is found to be about **9 MeV** for a $d^*$ mass of **2100 MeV** in the simple model.
- The production cross section calculated with the Full Bonn potential in the Born approximation is higher by a factor of about **2.8** compared to the result using the Love-Franey $t$-matrix.
- The $d^*$ wavefunction is modeled with an average $\Delta\Delta$ separation of **$2r^* = 1.4$ fm**.

## Context
This work extends theoretical efforts to describe nonstrange dibaryons, specifically the isoscalar $J^\pi = 3^+$ $d^*$, which is a candidate for a low-mass state predicted by the Quark Delocalization and Color Screening (QDCS) Model (Goldberg et al., 1989; Wang et al., 1992; Goldberg et al., 1995) at around 2100 MeV, in contrast to higher masses from R-matrix analyses (Lomon, 1990). The paper provides a first estimate of the production cross section in $pd$ scattering and the $NN$ decay width using standard nuclear reaction techniques, aiming to guide experimental searches. It notes that a recent R-matrix analysis of $NN$ phase shifts (Lomon, 1997) finds no sign of a $d^*$ resonance with a width greater than 1 MeV below 2240 MeV, but does not exclude a narrower resonance.
