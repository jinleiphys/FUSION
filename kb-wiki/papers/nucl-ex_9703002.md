# Eta-Helium Quasi-Bound States

> The cross section and tensor analysing power t_20 of the d\vec{d}->eta 4He reaction have been measured at six c.m. momenta, 10 < p(eta) < 90 MeV/c. The threshold value of t_20 is consistent with 1/\sqrt{2}, which follows from parity conservation and Bose symmetry. The much slower momentum variation observed for the reaction amplitude, as compared to that for the analogous pd->eta 3He case, suggests strongly the existence of a quasi-bound state in the eta-4He system and optical model fits indicate that this probably also the case for eta-3He.

## Key claim
The much slower momentum variation of the reaction amplitude for \(d\vec{d} \to \eta\,^4\mathrm{He}\) compared to the analogous \(p d \to \eta\,^3\mathrm{He}\) case suggests strongly the existence of a quasi-bound state in the \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) system, and optical model fits indicate this is probably also the case for \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\); however, the argument does not itself prove that \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\) is bound, though this is suggested strongly by the optical potential analysis taken in conjunction with the \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) data.

## Method
The cross section and tensor analysing power \(t_{20}\) of the \(\vec{d} d \to \eta\,^4\mathrm{He}\) reaction were measured at six center-of-mass momenta between 10 and 90 MeV/c using a tensor-polarised deuteron beam at the SATURNE synchrotron, with the SPES III large-acceptance magnetic spectrometer detecting \(\alpha\) particles. Background from three-body \(\alpha\pi\pi\) production was subtracted using data taken below the \(\eta\) threshold and exploiting the beam polarisation to isolate the \(\eta\) signal in the helicity \(m = \pm 1\) channel. The spin-averaged squared amplitude was extracted assuming an isotropic angular distribution, and the data were fitted with an optical potential model to extract \(\eta\)-nucleon and \(\eta\)-nucleus scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- The threshold value of the tensor analysing power \(t_{20}\) is consistent with \(1/\sqrt{2}\), which follows from parity conservation and Bose symmetry.
- The \(\eta\) production cross section was measured at six beam energies \(T_d\) between 1121 and 1139 MeV, corresponding to \(\eta\) center-of-mass momenta \(10 < p_\eta < 90\) MeV/c.
- The overall accuracy in the cross section normalisation is \(\pm 12\%\).
- The beam tensor polarisation was \(\rho_{20} = 0.649 \pm 0.011\).
- The best optical model fit yields an \(\eta\)-nucleon scattering length \(a(\eta N) \approx (0.52 + 0.25i)\) fm.
- The derived \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\) scattering length is \(a(\eta\,^3\mathrm{He}) \approx (-2.3 + 3.2i)\) fm.
- The derived \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) scattering length is \(a(\eta\,^4\mathrm{He}) \approx (-2.2 + 1.1i)\) fm.
- The real part of the inverse scattering length for \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) is \(0.36\) fm\(^{-1}\), larger than the value \(0.15\) fm\(^{-1}\) for \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\), consistent with stronger binding in the heavier system.

## Context
This work extends earlier measurements of the \(p d \to \eta\,^3\mathrm{He}\) reaction by Berger et al. (1988) and Mayer et al. (1995), which showed a strong energy variation interpreted as evidence for a final-state interaction in the \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\) system. The present data on the \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) system show a much flatter momentum dependence, consistent with a more bound quasi-bound state, and the optical model analysis suggests that both \(\eta-^3\mathrm{He}\) and \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) are bound, with the \(\eta-^4\mathrm{He}\) system more bound due to the smaller nuclear radius and extra nucleon. This enables further studies of the \(\eta\)-deuteron channel to seek an exotic dibaryon at the \(\eta\) threshold, as proposed by Piekarewicz et al.
