# Exact evaluation of the nuclear form factor for new kinds of majoron emission in neutrinoless double beta decay

> We have developed a formalism, based on the Fourier-Bessel expansion, that facilitates the evaluation of matrix elements involving nucleon recoil operators, such as appear in serveral exotic forms of neutrinoless double beta decay ($ββ_{0ν}$). The method is illustrated by applying it to the ``charged'' majoron model, which is one of the few that can hope to produce an observable effect. From our numerical computations within the QRPA performed for $^{76}Ge$, $^{82}Se$, $^{100} Mo$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{150}Nd$ nuclei, we test the validity of approximations made in earlier work to simplify the new matrix elements, showing that they are accurate to within 15%. Our new method is also suitable for computing other previously unevaluated $ββ_{0ν}$ nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper develops an exact Fourier-Bessel formalism for evaluating nuclear matrix elements involving nucleon recoil operators in exotic neutrinoless double beta decay, and demonstrates that the approximations used in earlier work (Bar96) to simplify these matrix elements are accurate to within 15%, contrary to the suspicion that neglecting nucleon momentum terms might be unreliable.

## Method

The authors derive multipole expansions for the three components (AA, AV, VV) of the charged majoron emission operator, expressing them in terms of spherical one-body operators. They then evaluate the reduced single-particle form factors using harmonic oscillator radial wave functions and perform numerical calculations within the QRPA framework for five nuclei, comparing the full exact matrix elements with the simplified approximation that neglects velocity-dependent terms and uses the relation (σ_n·k̂)(σ_m·k̂) ≈ (1/3)k²σ_n·σ_m.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous publication (Bar96) by providing a rigorous formalism to compute previously neglected recoil operators in charged majoron models (CMMs), which were proposed by Burgess and Cline (1993) and Carone (1993) as alternatives to the classic Gelmini-Roncadelli majoron model excluded by LEP. The method is also applicable to standard neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements (Krm92, Krm94) and to supersymmetric contributions (Hir96b), enabling future calculations of recoil matrix elements that had previously required complicated two-body operator treatments (Doi85, Tom86, Tom91).
