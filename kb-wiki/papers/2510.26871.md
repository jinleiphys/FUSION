# Improved calculation of radiative corrections to $τ\toππν_τ$ decays

> A reliable calculation of radiative corrections to $τ\toππν_τ$ decays is an important prerequisite for using hadronic $τ$ decays for a data-driven evaluation of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ^\text{HVP, LO}[ππ,τ]$. In this Letter, we present an improved model-independent analysis of these radiative corrections, including, for the first time, effects beyond point-like pions in the evaluation of the loop diagrams. These structure-dependent corrections, implemented via a dispersive representation of the pion form factor, lead to significant changes compared to previous calculations due to enhancements near the $ρ(770)$ resonance. We also devise strategies for the matching to chiral perturbation theory and a stable implementation of the real corrections down to the two-pion threshold, which shows that some higher-order isospin-breaking corrections need to be kept due to a strong threshold enhancement. Finally, we perform dispersive fits to the currently available $τ\toππν_τ$ spectra and discuss the consequences for isospin-breaking corrections in the evaluation of $a_μ^\text{HVP, LO}[ππ,τ]$.

# Improved calculation of radiative corrections to \(\tau\to\pi\pi\nu_\tau\) decays

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved model-independent calculation of radiative corrections to \(\tau\to\pi\pi\nu_\tau\) decays that includes, for the first time, structure-dependent virtual corrections beyond point-like pions via a dispersive representation of the pion form factor; these corrections produce significant changes near the \(\rho(770)\) resonance, but the overall uncertainty remains dominated by the scheme dependence in the short-distance electroweak correction factor \(S_\text{EW}^{\pi\pi}\) rather than by the radiative corrections themselves.

## Method

The authors compute the electromagnetic correction factor \(G_\text{EM}(s)\) by combining a dispersive evaluation of the box diagram (using a dispersive representation of the pion form factor \(f_+(s)\)) with chiral perturbation theory matching at \(s=t=0\) to incorporate short-distance contributions. Real photon corrections are calculated including radiation off the tau lepton and charged pion, resonance contributions saturating the low-energy constants \(L_9\) and \(L_{10}\), and the Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly, with a change of integration variables to handle the singular threshold behavior. Dispersive fits to the \(\tau\to\pi\pi\nu_\tau\) spectral functions from Belle, ALEPH, CLEO, and OPAL are performed iteratively to determine the imaginary part of the pion form factor self-consistently with the applied radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- Experimental world average of the muon anomalous magnetic moment: \(a_\mu^\text{exp} = 11\,659\,207.15(1.45) \times 10^{-10}\)
- Standard Model prediction: \(a_\mu^\text{SM} = 11\,659\,203.3(6.2) \times 10^{-10}\)
- Electroweak correction factor: \(S_\text{EW}^{\pi\pi} = 1.0233(3)(24)\)
- Global fit branching fraction: \(\text{Br}[\tau\to \pi\pi\nu_\tau] = 25.49(9)\%\) with correlation \(-0.19\) with \(\text{Br}[\tau\to e\nu_\tau\bar{\nu}_e]\)
- Full correction to \(a_\mu^\text{HVP, LO}[\pi\pi,\tau]\) from all data sets: \(-24.80(12)(39) \times 10^{-10}\) (first error experimental, second theoretical)
- Resulting \(\tilde a_\mu\) (after tau-specific isospin-breaking corrections but before matrix-element and \(e^+e^-\) corrections): \(509.5(2.4)(0.2) \times 10^{-10}\) for the global fit
- Net change in central value of the full correction compared to previous work: approximately \(-3\) units in \(10^{-10}\)
- Uncertainty due to \(G_\text{EM}(s)\) reduced by almost a factor of three compared to the previously assigned error in Aliberti (2025)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive treatment of radiative corrections previously applied to \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\) by Ignatov (2022) and Colangelo (2022) to the \(\tau\to\pi\pi\nu_\tau\) channel, where earlier calculations relied on chiral perturbation theory (Cirigliano 2001, 2002) combined with resonance models (Flores-Baez 2006, Miranda and Roig 2020, Davier 2023, Castro 2024). The improved calculation enables a more reliable data-driven evaluation of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment from tau decays, addressing tensions in the \(e^+e^-\) data base highlighted by the CMD-3 measurement. The results motivate new measurements of the \(\tau\to\pi\pi\nu_\tau\) spectral function at Belle II to resolve existing tensions among current data sets and further reduce uncertainties.
