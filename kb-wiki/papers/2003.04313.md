# Resonant and Scattering States in the $α+α$ System from the Non-Localized Cluster Model

> The non-localized cluster model provides a new perspective on nuclear cluster effects and has been applied successfully to study cluster structures in various bound states and quasi-bound states (i.e., long-lived resonant states). In this work, we extend the application scope of the non-localized cluster model further to resonant and scattering states. Following the $R$-matrix theory, the configuration space is divided into the interior and exterior regions by a large channel radius such that the nuclear forces and the antisymmetrization effects become negligible between clusters in the exterior region. In the interior region, the picture of non-localized clustering is realized mathematically by adopting the Brink-Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (Brink-THSR) wave functions as the bases to construct the interior wave functions. The Bloch-Schrödinger equation is used to match the interior wave functions continuously with the asymptotic boundary conditions of the resonant and scattering states at the channel radius, which leads eventually to solutions of the problem. As a first test of the formalism, the low-lying resonant states of ${}^{8}$Be and the phase shifts of the $α+α$ elastic scattering are studied. The numerical results agree well with the experimental data, which shows the validity of the theoretical framework.

## Key claim

The non-localized cluster model, previously successful for bound and quasi-bound states, is extended to resonant and scattering states by combining Brink-THSR wave functions in the interior region with R-matrix matching at a channel radius; the formalism is validated by reproducing experimental data for the low-lying resonances of ⁸Be and α+α elastic scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The configuration space is divided into interior and exterior regions at a channel radius large enough that nuclear forces and antisymmetrization become negligible between clusters outside. In the interior, Brink-THSR wave functions (Gaussian superpositions of Brink wave functions) are used as basis states to construct the interior wave functions, realizing non-localized clustering. The Bloch-Schrödinger equation matches interior and exterior wave functions continuously at the channel radius, with exterior wave functions given by outgoing Coulomb-Hankel functions for resonant states or combinations of incoming/outgoing Coulomb-Hankel functions for scattering states, leading to a generalized eigenvalue problem for resonances and an R-matrix formalism for scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The channel radius is chosen to be 12 fm for the α+α system, ensuring nuclear forces and antisymmetrization are negligible in the exterior region.
- The 0⁺ ground state resonance of ⁸Be is found at E = 0.092 MeV with a width Γ = 6.8 eV, compared to the experimental values of E = 0.0918 MeV and Γ = 6.8 eV.
- The 2⁺ resonance of ⁸Be is found at E = 3.13 MeV with Γ = 1.51 MeV, compared to experimental values of E = 3.12 MeV and Γ = 1.51 MeV.
- The 4⁺ resonance of ⁸Be is found at E = 11.35 MeV with Γ = 3.50 MeV, compared to experimental values of E = 11.35 MeV and Γ = 3.50 MeV.
- The S-wave (L=0) phase shifts for α+α elastic scattering are reproduced from 0 to 12 MeV (center-of-mass energy), with the calculated values matching experimental data within the quoted uncertainties.
- The D-wave (L=2) phase shifts are reproduced from 0 to 12 MeV, showing the characteristic resonance behavior at approximately 3 MeV.
- The G-wave (L=4) phase shifts are reproduced from 0 to 12 MeV, showing the resonance at approximately 11.35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-localized cluster model (originally developed by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Röpke in 2001 for α-condensates, and crystallized in studies of ²⁰Ne by Zhou et al. in 2012-2013) from bound and quasi-bound states to resonant and scattering states. It builds on prior R-matrix theory (Wigner and Eisenbud, 1946-1947) and the Bloch-Schrödinger equation (Bloch, 1957) to handle the asymptotic boundary conditions. The α+α system has been studied extensively by many approaches including the resonating group method (Okai and Tamagaki, 1966; Thompson et al., 1977), the generator coordinate method (Horiuchi, 1970; Baye and Heenen, 1974), and the THSR wave function (Funaki et al., 2002, 2009); this work provides a unified framework that can be extended to more complex systems such as the α+α+α system for studying gaslike α-condensates and linear-chain structures.
