# Puzzles at High $p_T$ at RHIC and Their Resolution

> Several puzzles about the data at high $p_T$ in heavy-ion collisions are listed. The resolution of them all is given in the framework of parton recombination. More specifically, it is the recombination of the soft and semi-hard shower partons that enhances the region $3<p_T<9$ GeV/c, and gives rise to the large $p/π$ ratio in AuAu collisions. The Cronin effect can be explained in terms of final-state interaction for both $π$ and $p$. The structure of jets produced in AuAu is different from that in $pp$ collisions. The suppression of $R_{CP}$ in forward production can also be understood by extending the same hadronization scheme at $η=0$ to $η>0$ without the introduction of any new physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that all observed "anomalies" in high-$p_T$ heavy-ion data at RHIC can be resolved by replacing the standard fragmentation model with parton recombination, specifically the recombination of soft thermal partons with semi-hard shower partons, without invoking new physics beyond conventional hadronization mechanisms.

## Method

The author develops a recombination model where hadron production at intermediate $p_T$ (3–9 GeV/c) is dominated by mixed terms combining thermal parton distributions (${\cal T}$) and shower parton distributions (${\cal S}$). The thermal parton distribution is determined phenomenologically from low-$p_T$ pion data using the ${\cal TT}$ term, while shower parton distributions $S^j_i(x)$ are extracted from known fragmentation functions at a fixed $Q = 10$ GeV/c. The inclusive distributions for pions and protons are then computed using recombination functions, with the hard-parton distribution $f_i(k)$ and an average escape fraction $\xi$ for hard partons traversing the dense medium.

## Key numbers

- The $p/\pi$ ratio reaches approximately 1 at $p_T \approx 3$ GeV/c in Au+Au collisions.
- The ${\cal TS}$ component dominates pion production in the $p_T$ region between 3 and 9 GeV/c.
- $R_{CP}$ for protons is greater than $R_{CP}$ for pions in d+Au collisions for $1 < p_T < 3$ GeV/c.
- At forward rapidity $\eta = 3.2$ in d+Au collisions, $R_{CP}$ rises no higher than 0.5 at $p_T \sim 3$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the recombination/coalescence approach previously applied by other groups (Greco, Ko, Levai; Fries, Muller, Nonaka, Bass) to heavy-ion collisions, and directly addresses four specific anomalies in RHIC data: the large $p/\pi$ ratio, the Cronin effect in d+Au, altered jet structure in Au+Au, and baryon-meson differences in elliptic flow $v_2$. The paper enables a unified description of hadron production across all rapidities and centralities without requiring color glass condensate or initial-state multiple scattering as the primary explanation for observed suppression patterns.
