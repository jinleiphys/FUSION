# Internal Plateau in Short GRBs and Quark Stars

> I summarize our recent calculations on quark stars (QSs), for the purpose of explaining some short gamma-ray bursts characterized by internal plateau in their early X-ray afterglow. According to the present plateau sample, the QS central engine model is demonstrated to more preferred than the original neutron star (NS) one. New QS equation of states (PMQS1, PMQS2, PMQS3) are then proposed, respecting fully the observed burst data and the mass distribution of the Galactic NS-NS systems.

# Internal Plateau in Short GRBs and Quark Stars

## Key claim
Quark stars (QSs) are more preferred than neutron stars (NSs) as the central engine for short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs) that exhibit an internal plateau in their early X-ray afterglow, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific sample of plateau bursts analyzed and the particular set of equations of state (EoSs) used for comparison.

## Method
The author compared theoretical predictions from QS and NS central engine models against observed properties of a sample of SGRBs with internal plateaus, using the `rns` code to compute uniformly rotating, axisymmetric configurations for multiple EoSs. For four individual bursts with known redshift measurements (GRB 060801, GRB 080905A, GRB 070724A, GRB 101219A), the collapse time was calculated as a function of protomagnetar mass using a spin-down formula combined with a parameterized maximum mass relation, and the results were compared to observed values. New QS EoSs (PMQS1, PMQS2, PMQS3) were constructed using the MIT bag model, with static maximum masses chosen to match the observed posterior mass distribution of binary NS systems.

## Key numbers
- For NS EoSs, the maximum mass $M_{\rm max}$ is roughly 18–20% higher than the nonrotating maximum mass $M_{\rm TOV}$; for QS EoSs, the increase is approximately 40%.
- The corresponding increase in equatorial radius $R_{\rm eq}$ is 31–36% for NS EoSs and 57–60% for QS EoSs.
- The observed posterior mass distribution from binary NS systems has a mean of approximately $2.46 M_{\odot}$, with a 68% confidence interval of $M = 2.46_{-0.15}^{+0.13} M_{\odot}$.
- Three new QS EoSs were constructed with static maximum masses: PMQS1 at $2.31 M_{\odot}$, PMQS2 at $2.46 M_{\odot}$, and PMQS3 at $2.59 M_{\odot}$.
- For the PMQS EoSs, the Keplerian spin periods are: PMQS1 at $0.79 \times 10^{-3}$ s, PMQS2 at $0.84 \times 10^{-3}$ s, and PMQS3 at $0.88 \times 10^{-3}$ s.
- The maximum moment of inertia for PMQS EoSs ranges from $10.83 \times 10^{45}$ g cm² (PMQS1) to $15.28 \times 10^{45}$ g cm² (PMQS3).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Gao (2016) on NS central engine models and by Lasky (2014) on supramassive NS collapse times, showing that QS scenarios provide a better agreement with the observed collapse time distribution of SGRB plateaus. The new PMQS EoSs are designed to be used in compact star astronomy applications including SGRBs, luminous supernovae, gravitational wave events, and kilonovae, and are parameterized in a simple analytic form for ease of use by the community.
