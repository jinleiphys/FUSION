# Hadron Production in the Trans-fragmentation Region in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the production of hadrons in Au+Au collisions in the region $0.6<x_F<1.2$, which we refer to as the trans-fragmentation region (TFR), since it corresponds roughly to $η'>0$, where $η'=η-y_{\rm beam}$, depending on $p_T$. We show how hadrons can be produced in that region when the hadronization process is parton recombination. The inclusive $x$-distributions for proton and pion production are calculated with momentum degradation taken into account. The results show that the proton yield is significantly higher than that of the pions in the TFR. Without particle identification the existing data cannot be used for comparison with our result on the $p/π$ ratio. Without $p_T$ determination it is not feasible to relate the $x$ distribution to the experimental $η'$ distribution. Nevertheless, on theoretical grounds we have shown why the production of hadrons in the TFR is not forbidden by momentum conservation.

# Hadron Production in the Trans-fragmentation Region in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Hadrons can be produced in the trans-fragmentation region (TFR, defined as 0.6 < x_F < 1.2) in Au+Au collisions via parton recombination from different nucleons, and this production is not forbidden by momentum conservation. The proton yield is predicted to be significantly higher than the pion yield in this region, but the authors note that without particle identification the existing data cannot be used for comparison, and without p_T determination it is not feasible to relate the x distribution to the experimental eta' distribution.

## Method
The authors apply the recombination model (RM) to Au+Au collisions, calculating inclusive x-distributions for proton and pion production in the TFR using a factorizable 3-quark distribution where each quark originates from a separate nucleon in the same longitudinal tube. Momentum degradation of valons traversing nuclear matter is incorporated via a Poisson distribution of collisions with a survival parameter kappa, and the nuclear geometry is treated using the Glauber model with thickness functions.

## Key numbers
- The TFR is defined as the range 0.6 < x < 1.2, corresponding roughly to eta' > 0 where eta' = eta - y_beam
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, the average number of wounded nucleons in A is 6.1 for 0-5% centrality (b = 1 fm) and 3.9 for 30-40% centrality (b = 8 fm)
- The valon distribution parameters are alpha = 1.75 and beta = 1.05, determined from fitting CTEQ parton distribution functions at low Q^2
- The sea quark saturation factor Z is 3.42
- The proton x-distributions are fit by exp[h_0^p(b) - h_1^p(b)x] for 0.6 < x < 1.2; for kappa = 0.8 at b = 1 fm, h_0^p = 10.58 and h_1^p = 9.80; at b = 8 fm, h_0^p = 9.90 and h_1^p = 8.18
- For kappa = 0.6 at b = 1 fm, h_0^p = 7.03 and h_1^p = 8.41; at b = 8 fm, h_0^p = 7.93 and h_1^p = 8.04
- The p/pi ratio at x ~ 1 is roughly 10^3 for any combination of b and kappa
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma is 41 mb
- The nuclear radius is taken as R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously applied to pp and pA collisions (Hwa and Yang, various years) to the novel kinematic region beyond x = 1 in AA collisions, which the authors term the trans-fragmentation region. The approach contrasts with the dual parton model (Capella et al.) and fragmentation-based formalisms, which cannot produce hadrons with x > 1. The paper builds on the valon model for nucleon structure and on earlier treatments of momentum degradation in pA collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2003) that successfully described baryon stopping. The results are intended to motivate experimental efforts to improve particle identification and p_T determination in the forward region at RHIC, and to provide a low-p_T baseline for future studies of forward hadron production at intermediate p_T, where competition between the enhancement from recombination and suppression effects observed in d+Au collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2004) may arise.
