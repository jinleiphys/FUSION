# Elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies with the bound states of $^{16}$O in effective field theory

> The elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=0,1,2,3$ channels at low energies is studied, including the energies of excited bound states of $^{16}$O, in effective field theory. We introduce a new renormalization method due to the large suppression factor produced by the Coulomb interaction when fitting the effective range parameters to the phase sift data. After fitting the parameters, we calculate asymptotic normalization constants of the $0_2^+$, $1_1^-$, $2_1^+$, $3_1^-$ states of $^{16}$O. We also discuss the uncertainties of the present study when the amplitudes are interpolated to the stellar energy region of the $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O reaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating the excited bound-state energies of $^{16}$O into an effective field theory (EFT) fit to elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C phase shift data requires a new renormalization method because the Coulomb self-energy terms in the dressed $^{16}$O propagator are unnaturally large (up to two orders of magnitude) compared to the amplitude strength estimated from experimental data, necessitating cancellation by effective range counter terms.

## Method

The author constructed an EFT for elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering treating $\alpha$ and $^{12}$C as point-like spinless particles, with the first excited states of these nuclei defining the large momentum scale $\Lambda_H \sim 150$ MeV. The scattering amplitudes for $l=0,1,2,3$ partial waves were expressed in terms of effective range parameters, and the binding energies of the $0_2^+$, $1_1^-$, $2_1^+$, $3_1^-$ states of $^{16}$O were used to constrain the first effective range parameter $a_l$ via the pole condition. A new renormalization method was introduced where the unnaturally large Coulomb self-energy terms were canceled by including effective range parameters up to third order ($r_l$, $P_l$, $Q_l$) for $l=0,1,2$ channels and up to fourth order ($r_l$, $P_l$, $Q_l$, $R_l$) for the $l=3$ channel, with these parameters fitted to phase shift data from Tischhauser et al. (2009) below resonance energies.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of $^{16}$O excited states from the $\alpha$-$^{12}$C threshold: $B_1(0^+) = 1.113$ MeV, $B_2(3^-) = 1.032$ MeV, $B_3(2^+) = 0.245$ MeV, $B_4(1^-) = 0.045$ MeV
- Binding momenta: $\gamma_l = 79.843$ MeV ($0_2^+$), $15.860$ MeV ($1_1^-$), $37.007$ MeV ($2_1^+$), $75.954$ MeV ($3_1^-$)
- Reduced mass $\mu = 2795.079$ MeV with $m_\alpha = 3727.379$ MeV and $m_C = 11174.862$ MeV
- ANC for $1_1^-$ state: $|C_b| = (1.6$-$1.9) \times 10^{14}$ fm$^{-1/2}$, in good agreement with experimental values $(2.10\pm 0.14) \times 10^{14}$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (Avila et al. 2015)
- ANC for $2_1^+$ state: $|C_b| = (2.1$-$2.4) \times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$, underestimating experimental values such as $(12.2\pm 0.7)\times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (Avila et al. 2015)
- ANC for $0_2^+$ state: $|C_b| = (6.4$-$7.4)\times 10^2$ fm$^{-1/2}$, underestimating the experimental value $(15.6\pm 1.0) \times 10^2$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (Avila et al. 2015)
- ANC for $3_1^-$ state: $|C_b| = (1.2$-$1.5)\times 10^2$ fm$^{-1/2}$, in very good agreement with the experimental value $(1.39\pm 0.09)\times 10^2$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (Avila et al. 2015)
- At $k=104$ MeV (corresponding to $T_\alpha=2.6$ MeV), the factor $C_\eta^2 \simeq 6 \times 10^{-6}$ and $C_\eta^2 k\cot\delta_0 = -0.019$ MeV, while the first term of $2\kappa Re H_0(k)$ is $7.441$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous EFT study (Ando 2016) of the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O radiative capture reaction by incorporating the excited bound states of $^{16}$O into the elastic scattering parameter fitting, which was not done previously. The new renormalization method addresses the mismatch between the Coulomb self-energy terms and the phase shift data, a problem noted in earlier effective range analyses by Konig et al. (2013) and Orlov et al. (2016). The calculated ANCs enable improved extrapolation of the elastic amplitudes to the Gamow peak energy $T_G=0.3$ MeV for the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction, which determines the C/O ratio in stellar helium burning, though significant uncertainties remain particularly in the $l=2$ channel (16-94% error in $ReD_{2G}$).
