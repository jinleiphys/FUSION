# Radiative corrections to Gamow-Teller decays

> Radiative corrections in the electroweak sector constitute an essential component in the ability to disentangle Beyond Standard Model physics from experimental data. This is particularly relevant for strongly bound systems undergoing weak decays such as nuclear $β$ decay, where its contribution to top-row CKM unitarity tests is essential. In this Letter we note the need for an additional radiative correction to the Gamow-Teller form factor in allowed decays. It concerns a combination of electrostatic final state interactions and QCD-induced currents. We review the basic derivation and report analytical results. Due to differences in their theoretical treatment in the literature, effects on the neutron and mirror systems are distinct. Significant consequences appear for a comparison of the former with lattice QCD, while changes occur in the $|V_{ud}|$ extraction in the latter. We discuss new limits on right-handed currents and provide a new value for $|V_{ud}|$ from mirror decays.

# Radiative corrections to Gamow-Teller decays

## Key claim
The paper identifies an additional radiative correction to the Gamow-Teller form factor in allowed beta decays, arising from the interplay between electrostatic final-state interactions and QCD-induced currents, which has been neglected in previous treatments. This correction is of order αZ/MR and produces distinct consequences for neutron and mirror systems due to differences in how the literature has treated these effects.

## Method
The authors derive the correction using the Behrens-Bühring formalism for the transition matrix element in the presence of electromagnetic effects, starting from the generalized matrix element of Stech and Schülke (1964). They evaluate the interference term between vector and axial-vector amplitudes with a virtual photon in the final state, which yields an energy-independent renormalization of the Gamow-Teller form factor. For the neutron, this renormalization is expressed analytically as a modification of the axial-vector coupling ratio λ; for mirror decays, the correction is applied to the f_A/f_V phase-space ratio.

## Key numbers
- Neutron: δλ = (2.0 ± 0.1) × 10⁻³, using charge radius ⟨r²⟩¹/² = 0.87(4) fm for a uniformly charged sphere
- Weak magnetism coupling: g_M = κ_p − κ_n = 3.706
- New |V_ud| from mirror decays: |V_ud|^mirror = 0.9743(14), which is 0.3% (2.2σ) higher than the previous result of 0.9712(14)
- Corrected average ℱt₀ for mirror decays: 6136(17) with χ²/ν = 0.14
- Individual corrected f_A/f_V values: ¹⁹Ne 1.0012(2), ²¹Na 1.0019(4), ²⁹P 0.9992(1), ³⁵Ar 0.9930(14), ³⁷K 0.9957(9)
- Phase space integral f_V = 1.6887(2)
- Inner radiative correction Δ_R^V = 2.467(22)%

## Context
This work extends the classical treatment of radiative corrections by Wilkinson (1982) and subsequent authors, who assumed the phase-space integrals for vector and axial terms differed by only a few parts in 10⁶. The correction was originally noted by Bottino (1974) and Holstein (1974, 1979) but was neglected in later analyses. For the neutron, the correction affects comparisons between experimental λ measurements and lattice QCD calculations from the CalLat (Chang 2018) and PNDME (Gupta 2018) collaborations, where it mimics exotic right-handed currents. For mirror decays, the correction resolves a double-counting issue in the f_A/f_V ratio used by Severijns (2008) and Naviliat-Cuncic (2009), improving internal consistency and bringing the mirror-derived |V_ud| into agreement with the superallowed Fermi value from Hardy (2015).
