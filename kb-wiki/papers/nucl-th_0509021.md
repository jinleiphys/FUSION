# Effective three-body interactions in the alpha-cluster model for the ^{12}C nucleus

> Properties of the lowest $0^{+}$ states of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ are calculated to study the role of three-body interactions in the $α$-cluster model. An additional short-range part of the local three-body potential is introduced to incorporate the effects beyond the $α$-cluster model. There is enough freedom in this potential to reproduce the experimental values of the ground-state and excited-state energies and the ground-state root-mean-square radius. The calculations reveal two principal choices of the two-body and three-body potentials. Firstly, one can adjust the potentials to obtain the width of the excited $0_2^+$ state and the monopole $0_2^+ \to 0_1^+ $ transition matrix element in good agreement with the experimental data. In this case, the three-body potential has strong short-range attraction supporting a narrow resonance above the $0_2^+$ state, the excited-state wave function contains a significant short-range component, and the excited-state root-mean-square radius is comparable to that of the ground state. Next, rejecting the solutions with an additional narrow resonance, one finds that the excited-state width and the monopole transition matrix element are insensitive to the choice of the potentials and both values exceed the experimental ones.

## Key claim

The alpha-cluster model can reproduce the experimental ground-state and excited-state energies and ground-state root-mean-square radius of the lowest 0+ states of 12C, but the fine properties (width of the 0+2 state and monopole transition matrix element) are either overestimated by about a factor of two for most choices of the three-body potential, or can be brought into agreement only by introducing a strong short-range attractive three-body term that supports an additional narrow resonance above the 0+2 state for which there is no experimental evidence.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for three alpha-particles using an expansion of the total wave function in hyperspherical harmonics and a system of hyper-radial equations. The two-body alpha-alpha potential was a modified Ali-Bodmer s-wave potential with a Coulomb term, whose parameters were varied to reproduce the experimental energy (91.89 keV) and width (within 6.8 ± 1.7 eV) of the 8Be ground-state resonance. The three-body potential was taken as a sum of two Gaussian terms depending only on the hyper-radius, with four parameters adjusted to fix the ground-state energy, excited-state energy, and ground-state root-mean-square radius at experimental values, leaving one degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 12C fixed at E_gs = -7.2747 MeV (experimental value from Ajzenberg 1990)
- Excited-state (0+2) resonance energy fixed at E_r = 0.3795 MeV (experimental value from Ajzenberg 1990)
- Ground-state root-mean-square radius fixed at R^(1) = 2.47 fm (experimental value from Reuter 1982)
- Experimental width of the 0+2 state: Gamma = 8.5 ± 1.0 eV (Ajzenberg 1990)
- Experimental monopole transition matrix element: M_12 = 5.48 ± 0.22 fm^2 (Ajzenberg 1990)
- For most solutions (common area): Gamma ≈ 16.5 eV and M_12 ≈ 9.0 fm^2, with excited-state rms radius R^(2) ≈ 3.9 fm
- For the best-fit solution (marked by asterisk in Fig. 1): Gamma = 7.0 eV, M_12 = 6.0 fm^2, R^(2) = 2.76 fm, using three-body potential parameters V_0 = -22.867 MeV, b_0 = 4.5109 fm, V_1 = -1710.00 MeV, b_1 = 0.41009 fm
- Alpha-alpha resonance width in 8Be: gamma = 6.8 ± 1.7 eV (Ajzenberg 1988)
- Ground-state rms radius of alpha-particle: R_alpha = 1.47 fm

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model calculations of Fedotov (2004) and Fedorov (1996) by introducing a two-Gaussian three-body potential to account for effects beyond the simple cluster picture, such as alpha-particle distortions and non-alpha-cluster structure at short distances. The study directly addresses the long-standing problem of reproducing the extremely narrow width of the Hoyle state (0+2 state) and the monopole transition matrix element, which are crucial for understanding the triple-alpha reaction in stellar nucleosynthesis as originally proposed by Hoyle (1954). The results enable future investigations using non-local three-body potentials that couple to the twelve-nucleon channel at short distances.
