# Connection between Effective Chiral Lagrangians and Landau-Migdal Fermi Liquid Theory of Nuclear Matter

> I discuss in this lecture how to make a connection between effective chiral Lagrangians -- low-energy effective theory of QCD -- and Landau Fermi liquid theory extended by Migdal to nuclear matter. The practical purpose of such a connection is to provide a link between certain observables in relativistic heavy-ion processes and those in low-energy spectroscopy, giving a new insight into how chiral dynamics manifests itself in nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective chiral Lagrangians incorporating the scale anomaly of QCD can be mapped onto Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory of nuclear matter, thereby linking the universal scaling of hadron masses (BR scaling) to low-energy nuclear observables such as the effective nucleon mass, the axial-vector coupling constant, and orbital gyromagnetic ratios, but only when the distinction between the Walecka-type scalar mass and the Landau effective mass is properly accounted for via pionic Fock terms.

## Method

The author starts from an effective chiral Lagrangian that includes a chiral-singlet scalar field χ to incorporate the QCD trace anomaly, and treats baryons as skyrmions. This Lagrangian is mapped onto a meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian with four-Fermi contact interactions in the isoscalar scalar (σ) and isoscalar vector (ω) channels, treated in mean field with BR scaling. The connection to Landau-Migdal theory is made by computing the quasiparticle interaction from the four-Fermi terms and the one-pion-exchange Fock term, then using Galilean invariance to relate the Landau parameter F₁ to the effective mass, and finally evaluating the orbital gyromagnetic ratio δg_l from the same Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

- Universal scaling factor at nuclear matter density: Φ(ρ₀) = 0.78, identified with the ρ-meson mass ratio m_ρ^⋆/m_ρ from QCD sum rules.
- QCD sum-rule result for the ρ-meson mass in medium: m_ρ^⋆/m_ρ = 0.78 ± 0.08 at ρ = ρ₀.
- QCD sum-rule result for the nucleon mass in medium: m_N^⋆/m_N = 0.67 ± 0.05 at ρ = ρ₀.
- Landau effective mass predicted from chiral Lagrangian: m_N^⋆/m_N = 0.69(7) at ρ = ρ₀, using Φ = 0.78 and the pionic Fock contribution F̃₁^π ≈ −0.153.
- Pionic Fock contribution to the Landau parameter: (1/3) F̃₁^π ≈ −0.153 at nuclear matter density (p_F ≈ 2m_π).
- Incompressibility modulus from phenomenological mean-field theory: K ∼ 200 MeV, with anomalous dimension d_a ≈ 1.7 for the σ field.

## Context

This work extends the BR scaling proposal of Brown and Rho (1991) by providing a formal connection to Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory, building on earlier work by Friman and Rho and by Brown and Rho (1995). It enables a unified description of both low-energy nuclear spectroscopy (e.g., orbital gyromagnetic ratios, axial-vector coupling quenching) and high-energy heavy-ion observables (e.g., dilepton production from dropping ρ and ω masses, as analyzed by Li, Ko and Brown in 1995). The approach also connects to the relativistic mean-field theory of Furnstahl, Tang and collaborators, who found a large anomalous dimension for the scalar field, and to QCD sum-rule calculations of in-medium hadron masses by Jin and collaborators.
