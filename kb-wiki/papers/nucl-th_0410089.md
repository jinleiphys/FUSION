# Polarized secondary particles in unpolarized high energy hadron-hadron collisions?

> In this short note I speculate on some consequences of the high energy collision picture in which the orbital angular momentum of the colliding hadrons can be converted into secondary particle angular spin momentum via some spin-orbital interaction. In particular I discuss a possibility to observe a non-zero polarization of secondary particles (e.g. hyperons) at midrapidity ($x_F=0$) and at low transverse momentum. I also speculate that such effects could contribute to the produced particle directed and elliptic flow observed in relativistic nuclear collisions.

# Polarized secondary particles in unpolarized high energy hadron-hadron collisions?

## Key claim
The orbital angular momentum of colliding hadrons can be converted into secondary particle spin via spin-orbital interactions, potentially producing non-zero polarization of hyperons at midrapidity (x_F=0) and low transverse momentum, even in unpolarized hadron-hadron collisions. The author speculates that this mechanism could also contribute to directed and elliptic flow observed in relativistic nuclear collisions, but emphasizes that the polarization would only be observable if one can determine the direction of the orbital angular momentum.

## Method
The author proposes using the azimuthal distribution of particles in the forward rapidity region to infer the reaction plane (the plane perpendicular to the orbital momentum) in p+p collisions, analogous to the procedure used for directed flow analysis in nuclear collisions. By selecting events with a particular particle azimuthal distribution, one can determine the preferential direction of the system's angular momentum and then measure hyperon polarization relative to that axis. The author also discusses how vector resonance decays with angular distributions proportional to sin^2(theta) would contribute to elliptic flow measurements.

## Key numbers
- Hyperon polarization exhibits saturation at about p_t ~ 1 GeV/c in previous measurements
- Elliptic flow at RHIC in the transverse momentum region p_t ~ 3 GeV/c cannot be explained by any model, suggesting a possible contribution from the proposed mechanism
- Vector resonance decay products would have angular distribution proportional to sin^2(theta) and cos(2*phi), where phi is the azimuthal angle with respect to the reaction plane

## Context
This work extends the mechanism discussed in Wang (2004) for non-central nuclear collisions to elementary hadron-hadron collisions, and relates to earlier work by Troshin and Liang on hyperon polarization and single-spin asymmetries in transversely polarized proton collisions. The author suggests that nuclear collisions could be viewed as coherent superpositions of elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions with strong angular momentum correlations, and notes that the proposed effect could complicate parity violation searches in nuclear collisions as discussed in a prior parity violation paper.
