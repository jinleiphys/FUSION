# Spectrum of Light Hexaquark States in Triquark-antitriquark Configuration

> To understand the nature of $X(2075)$ and $X(2085)$ observed by the BESIII collaboration in the $p\barΛ$ system, we systematically investigate the possibility that these states are compact hexaquark with triquark-antitriquark configurations for the first time. Within the framework of QCD sum rules, the mass spectrum and decay constants of such hexaquark states with quantum numbers $J^P=0^-, 0^+, 1^-, 1^+$ are studied. Consequently, six independent and nondegenerate hexaquark candidates are obtained, among which two $J^P = 1^-$ states exhibit masses consistent with $X(2075)$, while the two $J^P = 1^+$ states differ markedly from the mass of $X(2075)$ or $X(2085)$. The remaining two states with $J^P = 0^+$ and $0^-$ may serve as predictions for potential compact hexaquark configurations. Furthermore, the possible decay modes of these hexaquark states are analyzed, which could be the experimental signatures for their identification.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the BESIII-observed resonances X(2075) and X(2085) in the p-bar-Lambda system may be compact hexaquark states in a triquark-antitriquark configuration, but only the two J^P = 1^- states have masses consistent with X(2075), while the two J^P = 1^+ states differ markedly from the mass of either X(2075) or X(2085), suggesting X(2085) may require a different interpretation.

## Method

The authors construct interpolating currents for triquark-antitriquark configurations with color structure [3_c]-[bar-3_c] containing one strange quark, considering two possible arrangements: [3_c]_{qqq}-[bar-3_c]_{qqs} and [3_c]_{qqq}-[bar-3_c]_{qsq}. They compute two-point correlation functions within the QCD sum rule framework, retaining operator product expansion contributions up to dimension-13 condensates, and extract masses and decay constants by requiring Borel window stability with pole contribution at least 15% and convergent OPE.

## Key numbers

* Six independent nondegenerate hexaquark states are obtained from 24 possible structures; the other 8 show no stable Borel window.
* J^P = 0^+ state (Type-I, [3_c]_{qqq}-[bar-3_c]_{qqs}): mass = 1.93 +/- 0.06 GeV, decay constant = (2.77 +/- 0.14) x 10^{-5} GeV^8, with sqrt(s_0) = 2.7 +/- 0.1 GeV and M_B^2 = 2.3-3.0 GeV^2.
* J^P = 1^+ states (Type-I): masses = 1.93 +/- 0.04 GeV and 1.94 +/- 0.04 GeV for the two configurations, with decay constants (2.65 +/- 0.08) and (2.62 +/- 0.03) x 10^{-5} GeV^8.
* J^P = 0^- state (Type-II, [3_c]_{qqq}-[bar-3_c]_{qqs}): mass = 1.92 +/- 0.18 GeV, decay constant = (2.86 +/- 0.61) x 10^{-5} GeV^8.
* J^P = 1^- states (Type-II): masses = 1.95 +/- 0.17 GeV for both configurations, with decay constants (2.79 +/- 0.56) and (2.76 +/- 0.55) x 10^{-5} GeV^8.
* The strange quark mass is taken as m_s = 95 +/- 5 MeV; the light quark condensate is <bar-q q> = -(0.24 +/- 0.01)^3 GeV^3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of p-bar-Lambda and p-bar-Sigma molecular states (Zhang 2024) by investigating compact hexaquark configurations as an alternative interpretation for X(2075) and X(2085) observed by BESIII. It fills a gap in compact hexaquark studies, which have been comparatively scarce relative to molecular configurations. The results enable future experimental searches at BESIII and Belle-II to distinguish between molecular and compact hexaquark structures, particularly for the J^P = 1^+ state X(2085) which cannot be explained by molecular configurations from the prior work.
