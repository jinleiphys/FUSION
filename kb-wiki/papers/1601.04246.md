# Impact parameter dependence of pion ratio in probing the nuclear symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions

> The impact parameter dependence of \rpi ratio is examined in heavy-ion collisions at 400MeV/nucleon within a transport model. It is shown that the sensitivity of \rpi ratio on symmetry energy shows a transition from central to peripheral collisions, i.e., the stiffer symmetry energy leads to a larger \rpi ratio in peripheral collisions while the softer symmetry energy always leads this ratio to be larger in central collisions. After checking the kinematic energy distribution of \rpi ratio, we found this transition of sensitivity of \rpi ratio to symmetry energy is mainly from less energetic pions, i.e., the softer symmetry energy gets the less energetic pions to form a smaller \rpi ratio in peripheral collisions while these pions generate a larger \rpi ratio in central collisions. Undoubtedly, the softer symmetry energy can also lead more energetic pions to form a larger \rpi ratio in peripheral collisions. Nevertheless, considering that most of pions are insufficient energetic at this beam energy, we therefore suggest the \rpi ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy effective only in central at most to midcentral collisions, thereby avoiding the possible information of low-density symmetry energy carried in \rpi ratio from peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio to the nuclear symmetry energy reverses between central and peripheral collisions: a stiffer symmetry energy gives a larger π⁻/π⁺ ratio in peripheral collisions, while a softer symmetry energy gives a larger ratio in central collisions. This transition is driven primarily by less energetic pions (kinetic energy below 250 MeV), and the authors therefore recommend using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a probe of high-density symmetry energy only in central to midcentral collisions.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon across a range of impact parameters. The symmetry energy was varied using a parameter x, with x=1 representing a softer density dependence and x=0 representing a stiffer density dependence, while keeping the saturation properties fixed (symmetry energy 30.5 MeV at ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³). The dynamic pion ratio (π⁻/π⁺)ₗᵢₖₑ was defined including Δ-resonance contributions, and the kinematic energy distribution of pions was analyzed with a 250 MeV cut to separate less and more energetic pions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for Pb+Pb collisions.
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; symmetry energy at saturation E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.5 MeV.
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: 212 MeV; binding energy: −16 MeV.
- A kinematic energy cut of 250 MeV was used to separate less energetic and more energetic pions.
- The average density of the participating region over the whole reaction time is above saturation density in central collisions but below saturation density in peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the IBUU model (Xiao 2009) and QMD model (Feng 2010) that showed conflicting stiffness preferences for the symmetry energy from the π⁻/π⁺ ratio, and addresses the impact parameter dependence noted in the authors' earlier work on neutron-skin thickness effects (Wei 2014). The study also relates to findings that pion potential (Hong 2014, Guo 2015) and pion production threshold modifications (Song 2015) can alter the sensitivity of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio to symmetry energy. The results enable more careful selection of collision centrality when using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a high-density symmetry energy probe, avoiding contamination from low-density information in peripheral collisions.
