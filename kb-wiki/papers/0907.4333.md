# $η/s$ of a Relativistic Hadron Gas at RHIC: Approaching the AdS/CFT bound?

> Ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are thought to have produced a state of matter called the quark-gluon plasma, characterized by a very small shear-viscosity to entropy-density ratio $η/s$, near the lower bound predicted for that quantity by AdS/CFT methods. As the produced matter expands and cools, it evolves through a phase described by a hadron gas with rapidly increasing $η/s$. We calculate $η/s$ as a function of temperature in this phase both in and out of chemical equilibrium and find that its value poses a challenge for viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, which requires small values of $η/s$ in order to successfully describe the collective flow observables at RHIC. We therefore conclude that the origin of the low viscosity matter at the RHIC must be in the partonic phase of the reaction.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy-density ratio (η/s) in the hadronic phase of a relativistic heavy-ion collision is significantly larger than the AdS/CFT lower bound, implying that the low viscosity required to describe RHIC elliptic flow data must originate in the partonic (quark-gluon plasma) phase, not the hadronic phase.

## Method
The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which solves the Boltzmann equation for 55 baryonic species and 32 mesonic species, to simulate a hadron gas in a box with periodic boundary conditions. They extracted the shear viscosity via the Green-Kubo formalism, computing the autocorrelation function of the shear component of the energy-momentum tensor, and calculated the entropy density using the Gibbs formula. The calculation was performed both in full chemical and kinetic equilibrium and under conditions of chemical nonequilibrium with non-unit pion/kaon fugacities and finite baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The minimum value of η/s for the equilibrium hadron gas at zero baryochemical potential is approximately 0.9–1.0.
- This minimum value is significantly higher than the KSS (Kovtun-Son-Starinets) bound of η/s ≈ 0.08.
- A value of η/s of at most 0.24 is needed to reproduce RHIC elliptic flow data.
- The speed of sound squared extracted from the pressure versus energy density relation is c_s² = 0.18.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC is near T_chem ∼ 160 MeV, while the kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_kin ∼ 130 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of η/s for chiral pions (Prakash, year not given) and 3-flavor perturbative QCD (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, year not given) by providing a full hadron gas calculation. It directly challenges the common assumption in viscous hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., those by the Viscous Hydrodynamics Collaboration, year not given) of a temperature-independent η/s, showing that the hadronic phase has a rapidly increasing η/s that would spoil the fit to RHIC elliptic flow data if included. The results enable future work incorporating a temperature-dependent η/s into viscous hydrodynamics and motivate separate calculations of bulk viscosity (Denicol, year not given) for a complete description of the hadronic phase.
