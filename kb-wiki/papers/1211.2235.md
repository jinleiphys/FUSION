# Reaction Cross Section in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Previously a compact formula for total reaction cross section for heavy-ion collisions as a function of energy was obtained by treating the angular momentum $l$ as a continuous variable. The accuracy of the continuum approximation is assessed and corrections are evaluated. The accuracy of the compact equation can be improved by a simple modification, if a higher accuracy is required. Simple rules to determine the barrier heights and the penetration probability for the $l$ partial wave from experimental data are presented, for the collision of identical or non-identical light nuclei.

## Key claim

The compact formula for total reaction cross section (Equation 1) obtained by treating angular momentum as a continuous variable incurs errors that can be quantified; the accuracy can be improved by explicitly treating the lowest partial waves discretely, but for identical spin-0 nuclei at high energies the continuum approximation fails due to stepwise increases in cross section from individual partial waves.

## Method

The author evaluates the total reaction cross section using the Hill-Wheeler penetration probability for each partial wave, then compares three formulations: Formula I (full continuum approximation from Wong 1973), Formula II (discrete treatment of the lowest partial wave with continuum approximation for higher waves), and Formula III (Formula II plus third-order correction terms). For light nuclei, the barrier heights and penetration probabilities are extracted from experimental data by plotting the dimensionless cross section σ_r/πλ̅² as a function of energy and identifying the energies where it equals l(l+1)+K, with K depending on nuclear identity and spin.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O+¹⁴N (unequal nuclei): Formula I gives ~4.5% error in the sub-barrier region; Formula II gives <1% error; Formula III gives <0.01% error; at high energies all three give ≤0.3% error. Fit parameters: E₀=8.8 MeV, Γ=0.40 MeV (ℏω=2.51 MeV), r₀=1.45 fm.
- For ¹⁶O+¹⁶O (identical spin-0 nuclei): Formula I gives ~6% error in the sub-barrier region; Formulas II and III give <0.3% error; at high energies Formulas I and II give ~5% error while Formula III gives up to 15% error. Fit parameters: E₀=9.5 MeV, Γ=0.4 MeV, r₀=1.3 fm.
- For ¹²C+¹³C: the barriers E₀=4.7 MeV, E₁=5.4 MeV, E₂=5.85 MeV, Γ=0.15 MeV, r₀=1.3 fm were used in a partitioned fit (l=0,1,2 discrete; l≥3 continuum) that improved agreement near E~5 MeV.
- Barrier analysis rule for dimensionless cross section at barrier E_l: σ_r/πλ̅² = l(l+1)+K, where K=1/2 for non-identical nuclei, K=1 for identical spin-0 nuclei, K=1/4 for even-l identical spin-1/2 nuclei, K=3/4 for odd-l identical spin-1/2 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the compact formula of Wong (1973) for heavy-ion fusion cross sections by quantifying its accuracy and providing corrections. It addresses the need for theoretical extrapolations to sub-barrier energies relevant to astrophysical processes (Gasques 2005) and to high-energy fusion where Esbensen (2012) observed stepwise increases for identical spin-0 nuclei. The barrier analysis method provides a diagnostic tool for cases like ¹²C+¹³C, where the assumption of linear dependence of E_l on l(l+1) fails (Notani 2012, Dayras 1976), and enables extraction of penetration probabilities from experimental data for light nuclei collisions.
