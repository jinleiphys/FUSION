# Azimuthal correlations of forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions suppressed by saturation

> RHIC experiments have recently measured the azimuthal correlation function of forward di-hadrons. The data show a disappearance of the away-side peak in central d+Au collisions, compared to p+p collisions, as was predicted by saturation physics. Indeed, we argue that this effect, absent at mid-rapidity, is a consequence of the small-x evolution into the saturation regime of the Gold nucleus wave function. We show that the data are well described in the Color Glass Condensate framework.

# Azimuthal correlations of forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions suppressed by saturation

## Key claim

The disappearance of the away-side peak in the azimuthal correlation function of forward di-hadrons in central d+Au collisions compared to p+p collisions is a consequence of small-x evolution into the saturation regime of the Gold nucleus wave function, and this effect is quantitatively described in the Color Glass Condensate framework with no free parameters beyond those already constrained by single-inclusive forward hadron production data.

## Method

The authors compute the double-inclusive forward hadron production cross section in d+Au collisions using the Color Glass Condensate framework, where the qA → qgX cross section is expressed in terms of correlators of Wilson lines with a Gaussian approximation for the color source distribution. The small-x dynamics of the dipole correlator is obtained by solving the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation, with initial conditions (x0 = 0.02, Q̄²s0 = 0.4 GeV²) taken from fits to single-inclusive forward hadron production data. The coincidence probability is then computed for forward rapidities (2.4 < y < 4) at √s = 200 GeV, using CTEQ6 NLO quark distributions and KKP NLO fragmentation functions, with the factorization and fragmentation scales set to the transverse momentum of the leading hadron.

## Key numbers

- √s = 200 GeV: center-of-mass energy of the d+Au collisions at RHIC
- xp ∼ 0.4 and xA ∼ 10⁻³: typical longitudinal momentum fractions probed in the deuteron and nucleus respectively for forward particle detection at RHIC
- Q̄²s0 = 0.4 GeV²: centrality-averaged initial saturation scale at the starting point of evolution x0 = 0.02, extracted from minimum-bias single-inclusive hadron production data
- Trigger hadron: |p1⊥| > 2 GeV; associated hadron: 1 GeV < |p2⊥| < |p1⊥|, with rapidity range 2.4 < y < 4
- Centrality averaging uses b = 5.47 fm as the impact parameter corresponding to the minimum-bias saturation scale

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based description of single-inclusive forward hadron suppression in d+Au collisions at RHIC (Albacete 2010) to the more differential observable of di-hadron azimuthal correlations, providing a test that distinguishes saturation physics from alternative explanations such as partonic energy loss through nuclear matter. The calculation predicts that the away-side peak disappears for central d+Au collisions but reappears for peripheral collisions and for higher transverse momenta, and that the near-side peak is independent of centrality. The results are consistent with preliminary STAR data and with a separate description based on the KLN saturation model (Tuchin 2009). Future p+Pb collisions at the LHC are identified as providing definitive tests of saturation physics.
