# Magnetic screening in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We show that classical chromomagnetic fields produced coherently in the initial stage of a heavy-ion collision exhibit screening. From the two-point field strength correlator we determine the magnetic mass for SU(2) to be m ~ 5 times the saturation scale. Magnetic screening leads to an intuitive understanding of the area law scaling of spatial Wilson loops observed previously. The presence of screening effects in the initial state provides a basis for defining kinetic processes in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, with electric and magnetic masses of the same order.

## Key claim

The paper claims that classical chromomagnetic fields produced in the initial stage of a heavy-ion collision exhibit screening, with a magnetic mass of about 5 times the saturation scale for SU(2) gauge theory; this result is obtained from a gauge-invariant two-point correlator of the longitudinal magnetic field strength, not from the gauge-dependent correlator used in earlier work.

## Method

The authors compute the gauge-invariant two-point correlator of the longitudinal magnetic field strength \(C^{(2)}(r)\) on a two-dimensional periodic lattice for SU(2) Yang-Mills fields, using the classical Yang-Mills equations with static color charges on the light cone. The magnetic screening mass is extracted by fitting the correlator to the form of a screened propagator in two dimensions, \(C^{(2)}(r) \sim \exp(-m_M r)/\sqrt{m_M r}\). Lattice calculations are performed on \(2048^2\) and \(1024^2\) lattices with \(\mu_L = g^2 \mu a = 0.1\) and \(0.15\), where \(\mu_L N_s \gtrsim 100\) to control finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic mass for SU(2): \(m_M \simeq (5 \pm 0.5) \; Q_s\), extracted from fits to the two-point correlator \(C^{(2)}(r)\).
- Spatial string tension reproduced from the correlator: \(\sigma_M \simeq 0.12 \; Q_s^2\), matching the result of Dumitru (2013) to about 15%.
- Area law scaling of spatial Wilson loops sets in for radii \(R \sim 0.8 / Q_s\).

## Context

This work extends the classical Yang-Mills description of the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions (Kovner 1995, Krasnitz 1998) by demonstrating that the chromomagnetic fields produced are screened, not long-range. It provides a direct explanation for the area law scaling of spatial Wilson loops observed by Dumitru (2013), and shows that the magnetic screening mass is of the same order as the electric mass, which enables the definition of kinetic processes among hard on-shell particles in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. The result also indicates that unscreened perturbation theory applies only well beyond the saturation scale \(Q_s\).
