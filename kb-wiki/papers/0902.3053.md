# Skyrme-QRPA calculations for low-lying excitation modes in deformed neutron-rich nuclei

> Low-frequency modes of excitation in deformed neutron-rich nuclei are studied by means of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field. We investigate the microscopic structure of the soft $K^π=0^{+}$ modes systematically in neutron-rich Magnesium isotopes with $N=22, 24, 26$ and 28 close to the drip line, and it is found that the strong collectivity in $^{34}$Mg and $^{40}$Mg is acquired due to the coherent coupling between the $β$ vibration and the pairing vibration of neutrons. Microscopic structure of the $K^π=2^{+}$ modes changes gradually associated with the location of the Fermi level of neutrons, and it is found that the proton particle-hole excitation generating the $γ-$vibrational mode in $^{24}$Mg continues to play a key role in the near-drip-line nucleus $^{40}$Mg. The low-frequency octupole excitations are also investigated and the microscopic mechanism for the enhancement of transition strengths is discussed.

# Skyrme-QRPA calculations for low-lying excitation modes in deformed neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The soft Kπ=0+ modes in 34Mg and 40Mg acquire strong collectivity through coherent coupling between β vibration and neutron pairing vibration, but this collectivity is sensitive to the neutron number and only appears when up-sloping and down-sloping orbitals coexist near the Fermi level; the effect is not present in all neutron-rich Magnesium isotopes studied.

## Method

The author solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with axial and reflection symmetries, employing the SkM* interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent contact interaction for the pairing field. The quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) was then solved in the matrix formulation using the self-consistent quasiparticle basis, with the residual interaction derived from the Skyrme density functional and the pairing functional. The model space included quasiparticle states up to Ωπ=15/2± with a cutoff at 60 MeV, and the residual interactions were renormalized to eliminate spurious modes.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength parameter t0′ = −295 MeV·fm3 for the mixed-type interaction (t3′ = −18.75 t0′) with γ=1 reproduces the experimental pairing gap of 34Mg (Δexp = 1.7 MeV) yielding ⟨Δν⟩ = 1.71 MeV in 34Mg.
- The dimension of the QRPA matrix for the quadrupole Kπ=0+ excitation in 40Mg is about 16,400, requiring 24.2 GB of memory.
- Normalization factors for the residual interactions are fph = 1.106 and fpp = 1.219.
- Neutron chemical potentials range from −4.16 MeV (34Mg) to −1.56 MeV (40Mg); proton chemical potentials range from −19.8 MeV (34Mg) to −24.4 MeV (40Mg).
- Neutron deformation parameters β2ν range from 0.35 (34Mg) to 0.28 (40Mg); proton deformation parameters β2π range from 0.41 (34Mg) to 0.36 (40Mg).
- Neutron root-mean-square radii increase from 3.51 fm (34Mg) to 3.76 fm (40Mg); proton radii increase from 3.16 fm (34Mg) to 3.22 fm (40Mg).
- The difference in neutron and proton radii grows from 0.35 fm in 34Mg to 0.54 fm in 40Mg.
- The soft Kπ=0+ mode in 34Mg appears at 2.65 MeV with transition strength enhanced 10.6 times over the unperturbed strength; in 40Mg it appears at 2.30 MeV.
- The quadrupole-pair transition strength to the lowest Kπ=0+ state in 40Mg is enhanced about 28.4 times relative to the 2qp excitation (ν[310]1/2)2.
- One-neutron emission threshold energies: 3.86 MeV (38Mg) and 3.09 MeV (40Mg); two-neutron emission thresholds: 4.81 MeV (38Mg) and 3.12 MeV (40Mg).
- The ratio (Mν/Mπ)/(N/Z) for the lowest Kπ=0+ state increases from 1.57 (34Mg) to 1.91 (40Mg); for Kπ=2+ it increases from 1.41 (34Mg) to 1.79 (40Mg).
- The unperturbed transition strength of the ν[440]1/2 ⊗ ν[310]1/2 excitation is 313 fm6 in 38Mg and 720 fm6 in 40Mg.
- The proton transition strength B(E3; 0+gs → 3−Kπ=0−) is 33 e2fm6 in 34Mg but only 5.0 e2fm6 in 40Mg.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent deformed QRPA framework developed by Yoshida (2008) to systematically study low-lying excitation modes in neutron-rich Magnesium isotopes near the drip line. It addresses limitations of earlier calculations by Nakatsukasa (2005), Inakura (2006), and others that did not include pairing correlations or relied on the BCS approximation, which is inappropriate for drip-line nuclei due to the unphysical nucleon gas problem identified by Dobaczewski (1984). The results enable future experimental exploration of excitation spectra toward the drip line, as called for by Gade (2007) and Baumann (2007), and provide microscopic understanding of the interplay between deformation, pairing, and the continuum in exotic nuclei.
