# Casimir interaction among heavy fermions in the BCS-BEC crossover

> We investigate a two-species Fermi gas with a large mass ratio interacting by an interspecies short-range interaction. Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, we determine the interaction energy of two heavy fermions immersed in the Fermi sea of light fermions as a function of the s-wave scattering length. In the BCS limit, we recover the perturbative calculation of the effective interaction between heavy fermions. The p-wave projection of the effective interaction is attractive in the BCS limit while it turns out to be repulsive near the unitarity limit. We find that the p-wave attraction reaches its maximum between the BCS and unitarity limits, where the maximal p-wave pairing of heavy minority fermions is expected. We also investigate the case where the heavy fermions are confined in two dimensions and the p-wave attraction between them is found to be stronger than that in three dimensions.

## Key claim

Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation valid for large mass ratios, the p-wave projection of the effective Casimir interaction between two heavy fermions immersed in a Fermi sea of light fermions is attractive in the BCS limit but becomes repulsive near the unitarity limit, with the maximum attraction occurring between these two limits rather than at unitarity.

## Method

The author considered a two-species Fermi gas with a large mass ratio M/m ≫ 1 interacting via an interspecies short-range interaction. The Born-Oppenheimer approximation was employed to compute the interaction energy of two heavy fermions fixed in position while light fermions move in their presence, with the s-wave scattering length a as a tunable parameter. The energy levels of light fermions were determined for both bound and continuum states as functions of the heavy-fermion separation r, and the total energy reduction relative to the noninteracting system was computed by filling these levels up to the Fermi momentum k_F. The effective interaction V(r) was obtained by subtracting twice the single-heavy-fermion chemical potential, and its p-wave projection was evaluated for both three-dimensional and two-dimensional heavy fermion configurations.

## Key numbers

- The p-wave effective interaction in 3D reaches its maximum attraction at (a k_F)^{-1} ≈ -2.7, -2.5, -1.7, -1.0 for p/k_F = 0.4, 0.8, 1.2, 1.6 respectively
- The p-wave effective interaction in 2D reaches its maximum attraction at (a k_F)^{-1} ≈ -1.2, -1.2, -0.85, -0.52 for p/k_F = 0.4, 0.8, 1.2, 1.6 respectively
- The critical mass ratio for Efimov instability is 13.6 in pure 3D and 6.35 in the 2D-3D mixture
- In the BCS limit a → -0, the interaction energy takes the form v(k_F r) → (|a| k_F)^2 [2k_F r cos(2k_F r) - sin(2k_F r)] / [2π (k_F r)^4] + O(|a|^3)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of the effective interaction between same-species fermions by Bulgac (2006) from the weak-coupling BCS limit into the strongly interacting unitary regime, using the large mass ratio as a controlled parameter. The results are directly relevant to the Fermi-Fermi mixture of 40K and 6Li realized by Taglieber (2008), Wille (2008), and Voigt (2008), and enable predictions for p-wave pairing of heavy minority fermions in both three-dimensional and mixed-dimensional geometries, with potential applications to topological quantum computation using non-Abelian vortices as discussed by Read (2000) and Tewari (2007).
