# Rigorous constraints on three-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory from fast and accurate calculations of few-body observables

> We explore the constraints on the three-nucleon force (3NF) of chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) that are provided by bound-state observables in the $A=3$ and $A=4$ sectors. Our statistically rigorous analysis incorporates experimental error, computational method uncertainty, and the uncertainty due to truncation of the $χ$EFT expansion at next-to-next-to-leading order. A consistent solution for the ${}^3$H binding energy, the ${}^4$He binding energy and radius, and the ${}^3$H $β$-decay rate can only be obtained if $χ$EFT truncation errors are included in the analysis. All of these except the $β$-decay rate give essentially degenerate constraints on the 3NF low-energy constants, so it is crucial for estimating these parameters. We use eigenvector continuation for fast and accurate emulation of No-Core Shell Model calculations of the considered few-nucleon observables. This facilitates sampling of the posterior probability distribution, allowing us to also determine the distributions of the hyperparameters that quantify the truncation error. We find a $χ$EFT expansion parameter of $Q=0.33 \pm 0.06$ for these observables.

## Key claim
A consistent description of triton and helium-4 observables within chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order requires explicit inclusion of truncation errors; without them, no single set of three-nucleon force parameters can simultaneously reproduce all data. The tritium beta-decay rate provides the only non-degenerate constraint on the two three-nucleon force low-energy constants, making it essential for parameter estimation.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian parameter estimation for the two three-nucleon force low-energy constants cD and cE using bound-state observables in the A=3 and A=4 sectors: triton binding energy, helium-4 binding energy and charge radius, and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element from beta-decay. They employed eigenvector continuation to create fast emulators of No-Core Shell Model calculations, enabling Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling of the posterior probability distribution that included experimental error, computational method uncertainty, and chiral EFT truncation error modeled as a Gaussian process with hyperparameters Q and cbar.

## Key numbers
- Chiral EFT expansion parameter Q = 0.33 ± 0.06 for the few-body observables studied
- The truncation error covariance matrix uses the form (genobsref * cbar * Q^(k+1))^2 / (1 - Q^2) for each observable, where k is the chiral order (k=2 for next-to-next-to-leading order)

## Context
This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods previously applied in the nucleon-nucleon sector by Wesolowski (2018) and Melendez (2017) to the three-nucleon force, building on the use of tritium beta-decay for constraining cD and cE by Gazit (2009) and Baroni (2016). The results can be compared to constraints from nucleon-deuteron scattering by Epelbaum (2018) and neutron-alpha scattering by Kravvaris (2020), and the open-source python package fit3bf accompanying the paper enables reproduction of all figures and further exploration of correlated truncation error models.
