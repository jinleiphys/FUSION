# Ion Heat and Parallel Momentum Transport by Stochastic Magnetic Fields and Turbulence

> The theory of turbulent transport of parallel momentum and ion heat by the interaction of stochastic magnetic fields and turbulence is presented. Attention is focused on determining the kinetic stress and the compressive energy flux. A critical parameter is identified as the ratio of the turbulent scattering rate to the rate of parallel acoustic dispersion. For the parameter large, the kinetic stress takes the form of a viscous stress. For the parameter small, the quasilinear residual stress is recovered. In practice, the viscous stress is the relevant form, and the quasilinear limit is not observable. This is the principal prediction of this paper. A simple physical picture is developed and shown to recover the results of the detailed analysis.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the kinetic stress from the interaction of stochastic magnetic fields and turbulence takes the form of a viscous stress in the relevant strong-turbulence regime, and that the quasilinear residual stress limit is not observable in practice, because the turbulent scattering rate is almost always larger than the rate of parallel acoustic dispersion.

## Method

The authors calculate the responses of pressure and parallel velocity perturbations to stochastic magnetic field perturbations by integrating over perturbed particle trajectories, using a cumulant expansion to model the effect of ambient drift-wave turbulence as a perpendicular turbulent diffusivity. They then close the fluxes for kinetic stress and compressive energy flux, and analyze the resulting expressions in the limits of strong turbulence (where turbulent scattering dominates) and weak turbulence (where parallel acoustic propagation dominates), including the effects of magnetic shear.

## Key numbers

* The critical parameter distinguishing regimes is the ratio of the turbulent scattering rate \(k_\perp^2 D_T\) to the parallel acoustic dispersion rate \(k_z c_s\); the strong turbulence limit corresponds to \(k_\perp^2 D_T > k_z c_s\) and the weak turbulence limit to \(k_z c_s > k_\perp^2 D_T\).
* In the strong turbulence limit, the kinetic stress is \(K = - D_{st} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \langle u_z \rangle\) and the compressive energy flux is \(H = - D_{st} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \langle p \rangle\), where the hybrid diffusivity is \(D_{st} \equiv \sum_k |\widetilde{b}_{x,k}^2| c_s^2/k_\perp^2 D_T\).
* In the weak turbulence limit, the kinetic stress is \(K = -D_M \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \langle p \rangle\) and the compressive energy flux is \(H = -\rho c_s^2 D_M \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \langle u_z \rangle\), where \(D_M\) is the magnetic diffusivity.
* The acoustic width is defined as \(x_s \equiv L_s/(k_y c_s \tau_{c,k})\), where \(\tau_{c,k}\) is the decorrelation time due to perpendicular turbulent scattering.

## Context

This work extends the classic theory of transport by stochastic magnetic fields from Rechester and Rosenbluth (1978) by including the effect of ambient electrostatic turbulence, which provides the necessary scattering to break reversibility. It contrasts with the quasilinear predictions for electromagnetic turbulence from Peng (2016), where the relevant base rate is the wave frequency rather than the acoustic frequency, making those predictions more robust. The results enable testing via computer simulations that scan turbulence intensity to pass from the weak to strong turbulence regime, and open the door to studying the effect of stochastic fields on geodesic acoustic modes (GAMs) as discussed by Winsor (1968), Itoh (2005), Melnikov (2006), Miki (2010), and Zarzoso (2013).
