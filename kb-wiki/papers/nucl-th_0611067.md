# Coulomb sum rule in quasielastic region

> Within a relativistic single particle model, we calculate the Coulomb sum rule of inclusive electron scattering from $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb in quasielastic region. Theoretical longitudinal and transverse structure functions are extracted for three momentum transfers from 300 to 500 MeV/c and compared with the experimental data measured at Bates and Saclay. We find that there is no drastic suppression of the longitudinal structure function and that the Coulomb sum rule depends on nucleus in our theoretical model.

# Coulomb sum rule in quasielastic region

## Key claim

Within a relativistic single particle model, the authors find that there is no drastic suppression of the longitudinal structure function (such as the 50% reduction previously claimed by the Saclay group), but the Coulomb sum rule does exhibit a nucleus-dependent deviation from unity that is not as large as earlier experimental reports suggested.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive (e,e') cross sections for 40Ca and 208Pb using a relativistic single particle model with bound state wave functions from the sigma-omega model of Horowitz and Serot (1981), including electron Coulomb distortion via an approximate treatment that agrees with full distorted wave Born approximation calculations to within a few percent. Longitudinal and transverse structure functions are extracted via a Rosenbluth separation at fixed momentum transfers, and the Coulomb sum rule is obtained by integrating the longitudinal structure function over energy transfer with the electric form factor removed, using integration limits chosen to exclude the elastic peak.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: 300, 350, 400, 450, and 500 MeV/c
- For 40Ca at Bates: suppression of about 30% in the q range from 300 MeV/c to 450 MeV/c was previously reported
- For 208Pb at Saclay: reduction of about 50% in the effective momentum transfer from 350 MeV/c to 550 MeV/c was previously reported
- Integration lower limit: omega_min = 10 MeV for all calculations
- Integration upper limits: omega_max = 200 MeV for q = 300 and 350 MeV/c; omega_max = 225 MeV for q = 400 MeV/c; omega_max = 250 MeV for q = 450 and 500 MeV/c
- Morgenstern and Meziani (2001) claimed suppression of about 40% at effective momentum transfer 500 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the Coulomb sum rule, which began with measurements at Bates (Williamson et al., 1997) showing suppression for 40Ca and at Saclay (Zghiche et al., 1994) showing larger suppression for 208Pb. The authors' relativistic single particle model contradicts the Saclay group's claim of 50% suppression and supports the view of Jourdan (1996) that the longitudinal structure function is not as strongly suppressed, while also showing nucleus dependence contrary to Jourdan's conclusion. The results enable comparison with expected JLAB measurements at higher momentum transfer where correlations become smaller, and the authors note that their approximate treatment of electron Coulomb distortion (previously validated by Kim et al., 1996, 2001) allows the separation of longitudinal and transverse structure functions that full distorted wave calculations cannot provide.
