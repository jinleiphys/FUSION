# Resonances of 7He in the complex scaling method

> We study the resonance spectroscopy of 7He in the 4He+n+n+n cluster model, where the motion of valence neutrons is described in the cluster orbital shell model. Many-body resonances are treated on the correct boundary condition as the Gamow states in the complex scaling method. We obtain five resonances and investigate their properties from the configurations. In particular, the 1/2- state is found in a low excitation energy of 1.1 MeV with a width of 2.2 MeV, while the experimental determination of the position of this state is not so clear. We also evaluate the spectroscopic factors of the 6He-n components in the obtained 7He resonances. The importance of the 6He(2+) state is shown in several states of 7He.

## Key claim
The authors claim to have identified five resonances of 7He using the complex scaling method, but the ground state is slightly overbound (0.184 MeV above the 6He+n threshold) compared to experimental values (0.44 MeV), and the 1/2- state is found at a low excitation energy of 1.05 MeV with a large width of 2.19 MeV, despite experimental uncertainty about its position.

## Method
The authors employed a 4He+n+n+n cluster orbital shell model (COSM) with the complex scaling method (CSM) to treat many-body resonances as Gamow states under correct boundary conditions. The Hamiltonian used the modified KKNN potential for 4He-n interactions and the Minnesota potential (u=0.95) for n-n interactions, with a three-cluster potential V^{alpha n n} added to reproduce the observed energies of 5,6He. The wave functions were expanded using Gaussian basis functions with angular momenta up to l=2 (d-waves), and the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation was solved with a scaling angle of 29 degrees.

## Key numbers
- Ground state 3/2-1: energy -0.790 MeV (0.184 MeV above 6He+n threshold), width 0.014 MeV (fitted to 0.14 MeV when adjusting to experimental E_r=0.44 MeV)
- 1/2- state: energy 0.26 MeV (excitation energy 1.05 MeV), width 2.19 MeV
- 3/2-2 state: energy 2.58 MeV, width 1.95 MeV
- 3/2-3 state: energy 4.53 MeV, width 5.77 MeV
- 5/2- state: energy 2.46 MeV, width 1.50 MeV
- 6He ground state binding energy: -0.974 MeV from 4He+n+n threshold
- 6He(2+1) resonance: (E_r, Gamma) = (0.840, 0.107) MeV
- 6He matter radius: 2.36 fm (experiment 2.33 +/- 0.04 fm)

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of He isotopes using the complex scaling method by Myo et al. (2001) and the cluster orbital shell model by Suzuki et al. (1988) and Masui et al. (2006, 2007). It addresses the need for proper treatment of many-body decay channels (6He+n, 5He+2n, 4He+3n) that were not fully accounted for in earlier ab initio calculations such as the no-core shell model by Navratil et al. (1998) and Green's function Monte Carlo by Pieper et al. (2004), which used bound state approximations. The results enable comparison with ongoing experimental efforts to resolve the 1/2- state position, with experiments by Meister et al. (2002), Skorodumov et al. (2006), and Ryezayeva et al. (2006) supporting a low excitation energy around 1 MeV, while Wuosmaa et al. (2005) and Borge et al. (2005) suggest higher energies.
