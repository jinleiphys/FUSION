# Pseudo-gauge dependence of quantum fluctuations of energy in a hot relativistic gas of fermions

> Explicit expressions for quantum fluctuations of energy in subsystems of a hot relativistic gas of spin-$1/2$ particles are derived. The results depend on the form of the energy-momentum tensor used in the calculations, which is a feature described as pseudo-gauge dependence. However, for sufficiently large subsystems the results obtained in different pseudo-gauges converge and agree with the canonical-ensemble formula known from statistical physics. As different forms of the energy-momentum tensor of a gas are a priori equivalent, our finding suggests that the concept of quantum fluctuations of energy in very small thermodynamic systems is pseudo-gauge dependent. On the practical side, the results of our calculations determine a scale of coarse graining for which the choice of the pseudo-gauge becomes irrelevant.

## Key claim

Quantum fluctuations of energy in subsystems of a hot relativistic gas of spin-1/2 particles depend on the specific form of the energy-momentum tensor (pseudo-gauge) used in the calculation, but for sufficiently large subsystems the results from different pseudo-gauges converge and agree with the canonical-ensemble formula from statistical physics.

## Method

The authors consider a hot relativistic gas of spin-1/2 fermions described by a canonical ensemble at temperature T. They define a Gaussian subsystem of size a and compute the variance of the normal-ordered energy density operator for four different forms of the energy-momentum tensor: the canonical (Noether) form, the Belinfante-Rosenfeld (BR) form, the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) form, and the Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen (HW) form. Thermal averages are evaluated using the Fermi-Dirac distribution and the expectation values of products of creation and annihilation operators, with divergent vacuum contributions discarded.

## Key numbers

- For T = 0.15 GeV and m = 1.0 GeV, the normalized standard deviation of energy fluctuations differs between pseudo-gauges for subsystem sizes a < 0.5 fm, with differences growing as a decreases.
- For T = 0.5 GeV and m = 0.01 GeV, the normalized standard deviation decreases with growing system size, and fluctuations depend significantly on the m/T ratio.
- In the large-a thermodynamic limit, the variance for all pseudo-gauges converges to sigma^2 = [4g/((2pi)^(3/2) a^3)] * integral d^3k/(2pi)^3 * omega_k^2 * f(omega_k)(1-f(omega_k)).
- The large-a limit can be expressed as V_a * sigma_n^2 = T^2 c_V / epsilon^2, where V_a = a^3 (2pi)^(3/2) is the effective volume of the Gaussian subsystem.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of quantum fluctuations in a relativistic boson gas (Das 2020) to the spin-1/2 fermion case, while introducing the novel aspect of pseudo-gauge dependence. The study connects to recent discussions of pseudo-gauge transformations in spin-polarized media (Florkowski 2018, Speranza 2020) and to the relativistic hydrodynamics of hot matter produced in heavy-ion collisions (Jaiswal 2016, Florkowski 2017, Romatschke 2017). The results determine a coarse-graining scale for which pseudo-gauge choice becomes irrelevant, which may be useful for hydrodynamic modeling of high-energy collisions.
