# Precision calculation of the recoil--finite-size correction for the hyperfine splitting in muonic and electronic hydrogen

> We present a high-precision calculation of the recoil--finite-size correction to the hyperfine splitting (HFS) in muonic and electronic hydrogen based on nucleon electromagnetic form factors obtained from dispersion theory. This will help guide the upcoming searches of the HFS transition in muonic hydrogen, and will allow a precise determination of the polarizability and Zemach radius contributions when this transition is found.

# Precision calculation of the recoil–finite-size correction for the hyperfine splitting in muonic and electronic hydrogen

## Key claim

The recoil–finite-size correction to the hyperfine splitting in muonic and electronic hydrogen is calculated with improved precision using nucleon electromagnetic form factors from dispersion theory, yielding uncertainties at the few-ppm level that are smaller than previous determinations. The reduced uncertainty enables more reliable extraction of the polarizability and Zemach radius contributions from future muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting measurements, but the separation between recoil and polarizability corrections depends upon a chosen protocol.

## Method

The recoil correction is evaluated from a dispersive integral over the Dirac and Pauli form factors (F1, F2) and Sachs form factors (GE, GM) of the proton, using the formalism of Hagelstein (2015). The form factors are obtained from a dispersion-theoretical analysis that fits spectral functions to world data on electron-proton scattering and e+e- ↔ p̄p, n̄n reactions, with statistical uncertainties from bootstrap resampling and systematic uncertainties from varying the number of vector meson poles. The calculation is performed for both muonic hydrogen (μp) and electronic hydrogen (H) by substituting the appropriate lepton mass and reduced mass.

## Key numbers

- Δ_recoil^(μp) = (837.6 +2.8 -1.0) × 10^(-6) = (837.6 +2.8 -1.0) ppm, with statistical error +1.7 -1.0 and systematic error +2.2 -0.1 from bootstrap and pole variation respectively
- Δ_recoil^(H) = (526.9 +1.7 -0.4) × 10^(-8), with statistical error +1.1 -0.3 and systematic error +1.3 -0.2
- Previous value from Tomalak (2017): Δ_recoil^(μp) = 844(5) × 10^(-6) and Δ_recoil^(H) = 532.8(4.9) × 10^(-8)
- Proton charge radius from the same form factor analysis: r_p = 0.840 +0.003 -0.002 +0.002 -0.002 fm (statistical and systematic)
- Zemach radius from the same analysis: r_z = 1.054 +0.003 -0.002 +0.000 -0.001 fm
- Fermi energy for μp: E_F = 182.443 meV
- Fermi energy for H: E_F^H = 1418840.082(9) kHz

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework of Carlson (2008), Tomalak (2017), and Hagelstein (2015) for two-photon exchange contributions to hyperfine splitting, using the improved nucleon form factors from Lin (2021). The reduced uncertainty in the recoil correction (from about 5 ppm to about 3 ppm) is timely given ongoing experimental searches for the hyperfine splitting transition in muonic hydrogen by three collaborations (Amaro 2021, Kanda 2020, Pizzolotto 2020). The result enables more precise extraction of the polarizability contribution, which currently shows tension between chiral perturbation theory predictions (37 ± 95 ppm from Antognini 2022) and data-driven approaches (364 ± 89 ppm from Tomalak 2017), and can also refine the prediction of the two-photon exchange contribution in muonic hydrogen via the scaling procedure of Hagelstein (2015).
