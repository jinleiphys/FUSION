# Phase-dependent cross sections of deuteron-triton fusion in dichromatic intense fields with high-frequency limit

> We investigate the influence of strong dichromatic laser fields (i.e. 1ω-2ω and 1ω-3ω) with high-frequency limit on the cross sections of deuteron-triton(DT) fusion in Kramers-Henneberger(KH) frame. We focus on the transitions of phase-dependent effects depending on a dimensionless quantity n_{d} , which equals the ratio of the quiver oscillation amplitude to the geometrical touching radius of the deuteron and triton as defined in our previous research. Theoretical calculations show that the angle-dependent as well as phase-dependent Coulomb barrier penetrabilities can be enhanced in dichromatic intense fields, and the corresponding angle-averaged penetrabilities and the fusion cross sections increase significantly compared with field-free case. Moreover, we find that there are twice shifts of the peak in the cross sections whenever the frequency becomes sufficiently low or the intensity sufficiently high. The reason for the first shift is the angle-dependence effects for sub-barrier fusion, while the second shift is due to the accumulation of over-barrier fusion, these mechanisms are analyzed in detail in this paper.

## Key claim

The phase-dependent enhancement of deuteron-triton fusion cross sections in dichromatic laser fields (1ω-2ω and 1ω-3ω) undergoes two distinct shifts in the peak cross section as the dimensionless parameter n_d (ratio of quiver oscillation amplitude to geometrical touching radius) increases, with the first shift arising from angle-dependent sub-barrier fusion effects and the second from accumulation of over-barrier fusion contributions.

## Method

The authors model DT fusion in the Kramers-Henneberger (KH) frame under the nonrelativistic dipole approximation, treating the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with a two-body potential consisting of a short-range attractive nuclear well and a long-range repulsive Coulomb potential. For high-frequency laser fields where the characteristic collision time is much longer than the field period, the time-dependent potential is replaced by its time-averaged effective potential V_eff. The Coulomb barrier penetrability is computed using the Wenzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation for each inclination angle θ, then angle-averaged to obtain the tunneling probability, which is inserted into the Gamow form for the fusion cross section while assuming the astrophysical S-factor is unchanged by the laser fields.

## Key numbers

- For n_d = 18 at collision energy ε = 64 keV, the fusion cross section is enhanced by about 8 times compared to the field-free case.
- The maximum fusion cross section for n_d = 18 is 63.05 barns, occurring at ε = 47 keV rather than ε = 64 keV.
- The laser parameters for n_d = 18 are ℏω = 2.80 × 10³ eV and intensity I = 8.36 × 10²⁶ W/cm².
- The laser parameters for n_d = 11 are ℏω = 2.80 × 10³ eV and intensity I = 3.16 × 10²⁶ W/cm².
- The laser parameters for n_d = 1 are ℏω = 2.80 × 10³ eV and intensity I = 2.57 × 10²⁴ W/cm².
- The Gamow energy factor for DT fusion is ε_G = 1.18 MeV.
- The quiver oscillation range for φ = 0 is about [-1.10 n_d, 1.10 n_d] and for φ = π/2 is about [-1.25 n_d, 0.75 n_d].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of DT fusion in monochromatic intense laser fields (Lv 2019) to dichromatic fields (1ω-2ω and 1ω-3ω), introducing phase-dependent effects analogous to those studied in atomic ionization by Schafer (1992) and Chen (1998, 2000). The results suggest that the Lawson criterion for fusion ignition might be relaxed under these conditions, though the authors note that the power required to create and maintain the super-strong electromagnetic fields is not included in the energy balance and will be addressed in future work.
