# An $\textit{ab initio}$ strategy for taming nuclear-structure dependence of $ V_{ud} $ extractions: the $ {}^{10}\mathrm{C} \rightarrow {}^{10}\mathrm{B} $ superallowed transition

> We report the first \textit{ab initio} calculation of the nuclear-structure-dependent radiative correction $ δ_{ \mathrm{NS} } $ to the $ {}^{10}\mathrm{C} \rightarrow {}^{10}\mathrm{B} $ superallowed transition, computed with the no-core shell model and chiral effective field theory. We obtain $δ_{ \mathrm{NS} } = - 0.422 (29)_{ \mathrm{nuc} } (12)_{ n,\mathrm{el} } $ with a $1.6$-times reduction in the total uncertainty when compared to the current literature estimate based on the shell model and Fermi gas picture. This work paves the way for a precise determination of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed beta decays within a systematically improvable framework.

## Key claim
This paper reports the first *ab initio* calculation of the nuclear-structure-dependent radiative correction δ_NS for the ¹⁰C → ¹⁰B superallowed transition, achieving a 1.6-times reduction in total uncertainty compared to the current literature estimate based on the shell model and Fermi gas picture. The result is obtained within a systematically improvable framework using the no-core shell model and chiral effective field theory, though the authors note that several truncation errors (basis size, multipole expansion, chiral order, and approximate SRG evolution) must be estimated independently and contribute significantly to the final uncertainty.

## Method
The authors computed the nuclear γW-box diagram contribution to the superallowed beta decay using the no-core shell model (NCSM) with chiral effective field theory interactions. The Compton amplitude T₃ was evaluated via a multipole expansion of electroweak currents, with the loop integral handled through contour deformation (Wick rotation) plus explicit treatment of residue contributions from the electron propagator and low-lying nuclear states. Uncertainties were estimated from truncation of the many-body harmonic oscillator basis (controlled by N_max), truncation of the multipole expansion (J_max), chiral truncation in the Hamiltonian and currents, approximate SRG evolution of operators, and the dipole approximation for single-nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers
- δ_NS = -0.422 (29)_nuc (12)_n,el (total uncertainty 3.1 × 10⁻⁴), compared to the previous literature estimate of 5.0 × 10⁻⁴ uncertainty from Hardy (2020)
- Nuclear uncertainty breakdown: ε_hc = 1.4 × 10⁻⁴ (higher-order currents and SRG evolution), ε_sh = 2.4 × 10⁻⁴ (nuclear shadowing), ε_χ = 0.9 × 10⁻⁴ (chiral truncation), ε_Ω = 0.4 × 10⁻⁴ (HO frequency dependence), ε_Jmax = 0.1 × 10⁻⁴ (multipole truncation), ε_Nmax = 0.1 × 10⁻⁴ (basis truncation), ε_Mscale = 0.04 × 10⁻⁴ (form factor dipole mass)
- Vector dipole mass varied over 800–1000 MeV; axial dipole mass varied over 1.09–1.270 GeV
- Enhancement in vector electromagnetic current for M1 transitions: 2–4%; quenching in axial weak current for GT transitions: 3–5%

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation framework for radiative corrections developed by Seng (2018) and Gorchtein (2018, 2023), who identified flaws in the previously assumed scale separation and increased the δ_NS uncertainty to its current dominant role in V_ud extraction. The calculation directly addresses the largest source of error in |V_ud| from superallowed decays, which currently stands at 0.97361(5)_exp(6)_δR'(4)_δC(28)_δNS(10)_ΔRV as reported by Gorchtein (2023). The *ab initio* approach enables systematic improvement through larger basis sizes, higher chiral orders, and consistent SRG evolution of operators, and the authors note that most developments can be extended to other electroweak radiative corrections.
