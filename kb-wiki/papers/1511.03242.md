# The pi0, eta, eta' -> gamma gamma*(Q^2) Decay Rates and Radii

> The low $Q^2$ slopes of the the transition form factors provide a unique method to measure the sizes of the neutral pseudo-scalar mesons, since they do not have electromagnetic form factors. From the slope one obtains the "axial transition RMS radius" $ R_{PS,A} = \sqrt{<r^2>}$ for each PS meson. The present status of theory and experiment for these quantities are presented. A comparison of the $ R_{PS,A}$ is presented along with the electromagnetic and scalar radii of the $π^{\pm}$ mesons and the proton. We observe the striking similarity of the values of axial transition radii of all of the pseudoscalar mesons to each other and to the charge radius of the $π^{\pm}$. In the $Q^2$ = 0 limit the transition form factor is a measure of the pseudo-scalar meson radiative width (lifetime) and is a possible fourth (unexploited) method to perform such a measurement. The $π^{0} \rightarrow γγ$ decay rate is a test of QCD at the confinement scale. There is a firm QCD prediction with a theoretical uncertainty of $\simeq $ 1 \% which calls for an experimental test at the same level of accuracy. There are three methods that have been utilized to perform this measurement and the present status of the experimental tests are outlined. The current accuracy is significantly less than the theoretical uncertainty. The efforts to improve this are briefly summarized.

## Key claim

The axial transition radii of the neutral pseudoscalar mesons (π⁰, η, η') are strikingly similar to each other and to the charge radius of the charged pion, but the scalar radius of the pion is significantly larger; the π⁰ → γγ decay rate provides a test of QCD at the confinement scale with a theoretical uncertainty of about 1%, but current experimental accuracy is significantly less than this theoretical precision.

## Method

The paper reviews the extraction of axial transition RMS radii from the low-Q² slope of the PS → γ*γ transition form factor for π⁰, η, and η', using the definition R_PS,A = √⟨r²⟩ obtained from F(Q²) = F_PS(0)(1 − Q²⟨r²⟩/6 + ...). For the π⁰ → γγ decay rate, the author compares three experimental methods (direct decay distance measurement, Primakoff effect, and e⁺e⁻ two-photon production) against the chiral anomaly prediction with chiral perturbation theory corrections.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly prediction for Γ(π⁰ → γγ) in the chiral limit is 7.725 ± 0.044 eV, with 0.6% uncertainty from the experimental error in F_π
- Chiral perturbation theory calculations predict an increase of Γ(π⁰ → γγ) of 4.5 ± 1.0% due to isospin breaking from finite quark masses
- The experimental ratio R_A,π⁰ / R_charge,π± = 0.953 ± 0.012
- The pion scalar radius is higher than the axial transition radius by 0.14 ± 0.03 fm (22 ± 4%)
- Vector (rho) resonance dominance predicts R = √6/m_ρ = 0.62 fm
- The charged pion RMS radius is slightly higher than the π⁰ axial transition radius by 0.03 ± 0.01 fm
- A recent calculation predicts a 7% reduction in the η form factor slope, corresponding to 3.5% reduction in its transition radius (0.025 fm)

## Context

This work extends the comparison of pseudoscalar meson radii from dispersion relation calculations (Martin), chiral perturbation theory (R-ChPT), and experimental measurements from the Mainz A2 group (Reta-A2). The paper contrasts these axial transition radii with the charged pion radius from the Particle Data Group (PDB), the scalar radius from dispersive analysis of π-π scattering (Rpi-scalar), and the proton charge radius. The results enable future experimental tests at JLab (PrimEx2), Compass at CERN, and the DAPHNE group at Frascati, as well as motivate further lattice calculations and physical interpretation of the differences between charge, scalar, and axial transition RMS radii.
