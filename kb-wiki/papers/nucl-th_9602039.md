# Dynamical chaos and critical behavior in Vlasov simulations of nuclear multifragmentation

> We discuss the presence of both dynamical chaos and signals of a second--order phase transition in numerical Vlasov simulations of nuclear multifragmentation. We find that chaoticity and criticality are strongly related and play a crucial role in the process of fragments formation. This connection is not limited to our model and seems a rather general feature.

## Key claim

The authors show that deterministic chaos and signals of a second-order phase transition coexist in Vlasov simulations of nuclear multifragmentation, and they argue that this connection is not limited to their model but appears to be a general feature. The hedge is that the critical behavior is observed only when an event-to-event analysis is performed on an ensemble of simulations started inside the spinodal region, and the system is not necessarily close to the standard liquid-gas critical point.

## Method

The authors solved the Vlasov equation numerically on a two-dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a local Skyrme interaction to generate the mean field. They initialized a fermion gas at a fixed temperature of 3 MeV inside the spinodal region of nuclear matter, perturbing the homogeneous density with a small white noise (1% of the average density) to mimic initial uncertainties. They then followed the time evolution until well-defined fragments formed, analyzing the chaoticity via Lyapunov exponents, scatter plots of excited modes, and fractal correlation dimensions, and extracting critical exponents from an event-to-event analysis of cluster size distributions for 954 simulations.

## Key numbers

* Largest Lyapunov exponent λ ranged from 0.028 to 0.1 c/fm depending on the initial density inside the spinodal region; outside this region λ=0.
* The time scale for chaos onset was approximately 40-50 fm/c, with the chaotic time scale τ=1/λ of order 10-30 fm/c.
* The fractal correlation dimension D₂ reached 1.96±0.05 for mode n_k=5 and 1.94±0.06 for mode n_k=14 at t=120 fm/c, indicating complete randomness.
* The ratio of critical exponents β/γ = 0.37 ± 0.2 was extracted from the slopes of the two branches in the ln P vs. ln ⟨M₂⟩ plot, giving τ = 2.27 ± 0.2.
* The freeze-out density used to define fragments was ρ_freeze-out = 0.1 fm⁻², corresponding to approximately 20% of the saturation density ρ₀=0.55 fm⁻².
* The time to form final fragments ranged from approximately 80 fm/c for ρ/ρ₀=0.4 to approximately 300 fm/c for ρ/ρ₀=0.6.

## Context

This work extends earlier Vlasov-Nordheim simulations by the same group (Atalmi, Baldo, Burgio, Rapisarda, 1994-1995) that first found deterministic chaos in multifragmentation, and it confirms similar findings from Classical Molecular Dynamics calculations (Aldo, 1995). The results provide theoretical support for statistical models of multifragmentation (Gross, Bondorf) by showing that chaotic dynamics can fill phase space uniformly on short time scales, and they demonstrate that critical behavior can emerge from mean-field dynamics without explicit two-body collisions. The analysis follows the event-to-event method introduced by Campi (1994) for percolation and nuclear data, and the extracted critical exponents are compared with those from 2D and 3D percolation, liquid-gas phase transitions, and experimental data from the EOS collaboration (1995) and Waddington and Freier (1985).
