# Significance of the compound nucleus surface energy coefficients in the synthesis of the superheavy nuclei with $\textbf{Z=112-120}$

> This paper investigates the impacts of the different surface energy coefficients on the compound nucleus decay modes during heavy ion fusion reactions, with focus given to the superheavy nuclei (SHN) in the range of $Z=112-118$. The evaporation-residue (ER) cross sections were calculated for different surface asymmetric constants, $k_{s}$ and surface energy constants, $γ_{0}$. In these calculations, the di-nuclear system model and proximity potential, along with considering deformed nuclei, were employed. Comparing the experimental data and this theoretical approach, the best values of $k_{s}$ and $γ_{0}$ are $0.7546$ and $0.9180~\mathrm{MeV~fm^{-2}}$, respectively. Furthermore, this new model was used to investigate the probability of synthesis of experimentally unknown heavier systems with $Z=119$ and $120$. There exist five promising combinations to synthesize SHN with $Z=119$: a) ${^{249}}\mathrm{Cf}({^{45}}\mathrm{Sc},3n){^{291}}119$, b) ${^{249}}\mathrm{Cf}({^{45}}\mathrm{Sc},4n){^{290}}119$, c) ${^{247}}\mathrm {Bk}({^{50}}\mathrm{Ti},3n){^{294}}119$, d) ${^{254}}\mathrm{Es}({^{48}}\mathrm{Ca},3n){^{299}}119$, and e) ${^{254}}\mathrm{Es}({^{48}}\mathrm{Ca},4n){^{298}}119$. In addition, it is found that the best combinations to synthesize SHN with $Z=120$ are ${^{249}}\mathrm{Cf}({^{50}}\mathrm{Ti},3n){^{296}}120$, and ${^{251}}\mathrm{Cf}({^{50}}\mathrm{Ti},3n){^{298}}120$.

## Key claim

The surface energy coefficients of the compound nucleus, specifically the surface asymmetric constant \(k_s\) and the surface energy constant \(\gamma_0\), significantly affect the calculated evaporation-residue cross sections for superheavy nuclei synthesis; the best agreement with existing experimental data for \(Z=112-118\) is obtained with \(k_s = 0.7546\) and \(\gamma_0 = 0.9180~\mathrm{MeV~fm^{-2}}\), which differ substantially from the commonly used values of \(k_s = 4.0\) and \(\gamma_0 = 1.460734~\mathrm{MeV~fm^{-2}}\).

## Method

The authors employed the di-nuclear system (DNS) model with a proximity potential, including nuclear deformations, to calculate the total interaction potential for colliding nuclei. The evaporation-residue cross sections were computed by combining the capture cross section (via the Wong formula), the fusion probability (using the Zagrebaev and Greiner model), and the survival probability of the compound nucleus against fission, which depends on the competition between neutron emission and fission widths. The surface energy coefficient \(\gamma = \gamma_0 [1 - k_s (N-Z)^2 / A^2]\) was varied using 13 different parameter sets from the literature, and the calculated cross sections for reactions producing \(Z=112-118\) were compared to experimental data to determine the optimal \(k_s\) and \(\gamma_0\) values.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit surface energy parameters: \(k_s = 0.7546\) and \(\gamma_0 = 0.9180~\mathrm{MeV~fm^{-2}}\).
- For \(Z=119\) synthesis, the most promising combination is \({}^{254}\mathrm{Es}(^{48}\mathrm{Ca},3n)^{299}119\) with \(\sigma_{3n} = 9115.15~\mathrm{fb}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 208~\mathrm{MeV}\) (\(E^* = 32.14~\mathrm{MeV}\)).
- For \(Z=119\), the \({}^{249}\mathrm{Cf}(^{45}\mathrm{Sc},3n)^{291}119\) reaction yields \(\sigma_{3n} = 417.1~\mathrm{fb}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 219~\mathrm{MeV}\) (\(E^* = 39.84~\mathrm{MeV}\)).
- For \(Z=120\) synthesis, the best combination is \({}^{249}\mathrm{Cf}(^{50}\mathrm{Ti},3n)^{296}120\) with \(\sigma_{3n} = 51.19~\mathrm{fb}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 228~\mathrm{MeV}\) (\(E^* = 33.19~\mathrm{MeV}\)).
- The alternative \(Z=120\) combination \({}^{251}\mathrm{Cf}(^{50}\mathrm{Ti},3n)^{298}120\) gives \(\sigma_{3n} = 43.17~\mathrm{fb}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 227~\mathrm{MeV}\) (\(E^* = 33.82~\mathrm{MeV}\)).

## Context

This work extends the DNS model calculations for superheavy nuclei synthesis by systematically testing 13 different surface energy coefficient sets from the literature, including those from Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and Möller et al. (1995). The study provides optimized surface parameters that improve agreement with experimental data for known \(Z=112-118\) nuclei produced via hot fusion reactions with \({}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\) projectiles on actinide targets, as reported by Oganessian et al. (2004-2013). The results enable predictions for the synthesis of the yet-unobserved \(Z=119\) and \(Z=120\) nuclei, identifying specific projectile-target combinations and optimal beam energies for future experimental efforts.
