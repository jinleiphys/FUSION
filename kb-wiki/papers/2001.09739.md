# Equation of state and composition of the inner crust of an accreting neutron star: multicomponent model

> Correct interpretation of X-ray observations of transiently accreting neutron stars requires modeling of nuclear-physical processes in these objects. We consider a chain of nuclear reactions that drives the crust composition in an accreting neutron star and heats up the star. We constructed multicomponent approach with the kinetics of nuclear reactions described in simplified stepwise manner. The redistribution of nucleons between nuclei by emission and capture of neutrons is shown to significantly affect the nuclear reaction chains and the composition of the inner crust. In particular, even if the outer crust has one-component composition, the appearance of free neutrons in the inner crust leads to branching of reaction chains and formation of the multicomponent composition. We apply the compressible liquid drop nuclear model, which includes effects of free neutrons on nuclear energies. It allows us to calculate the composition, the heating profile and the equation of state of matter up to densities ρ~ 2 \times 10^{13} g cm^{-3}.

## Key claim
The paper shows that even if the outer crust of an accreting neutron star starts with a one-component composition (pure 56Fe), the appearance of free neutrons in the inner crust leads to branching of reaction chains and formation of a multicomponent composition, contradicting the simplifying one-component assumption used in prior models such as Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 2008).

## Method
The authors constructed a multicomponent model using a simplified stepwise kinetic approach where at each pressure step only a small fraction (alpha = 0.01) of nuclei undergoes the energetically allowed nuclear reaction, with reactions offered in a fixed priority order: neutron emission, two-neutron emission, neutron capture, two-neutron capture, electron capture, and pycnonuclear fusion. Nuclear binding energies were calculated using the Mackie and Baym compressible liquid drop model, which includes effects of free neutrons on nuclear surface tension. The model assumes zero temperature, quasineutrality, and a uniform distribution of free neutrons, and calculates the total pressure as the sum of electron, free neutron, and lattice contributions.

## Key numbers
- Neutron drip point density: rho = 6.11 x 10^11 g cm^-3
- Model applicable up to densities rho ~ 2 x 10^13 g cm^-3
- At densities 7 x 10^12 g cm^-3, the free neutron abundance is about 75%
- Integrated heat release is about 2 MeV per accreted nucleon at rho = 2 x 10^13 g cm^-3
- The impurity parameter never exceeds approximately 10, with pronounced peaks in regions where pycnonuclear reactions are active
- Temperature is assumed to be T less than or equal to 5 x 10^8 K, suppressing nuclear fusion

## Context
This work extends the one-component models of Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 2008) by incorporating neutron exchange between nuclei and allowing multicomponent composition in the inner crust. It also extends the reaction network model of Lau et al. (2018), which was restricted to densities below 2 x 10^12 g cm^-3, up to 2 x 10^13 g cm^-3. The model enables improved interpretation of X-ray observations of transiently accreting neutron stars by providing smoother heating profiles and equation of state, which are important for modeling thermal evolution and neutron star seismology.
