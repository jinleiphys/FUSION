# Properties of $^{208}$Pb predicted from the relativistic equation of state in the full Dirac space

> Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory in the full Dirac space allows one to determine uniquely the momentum dependence of scalar and vector components of the single-particle potentials. In order to extend this new method from nuclear matter to finite nuclei, as a first step, properties of $^{208}$Pb are explored by using the microscopic equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter and a liquid droplet model. The neutron and proton density distributions, the binding energies, the neutron and proton radii, and the neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb are calculated. In order to further compare the charge densities predicted from the RBHF theory in the full Dirac space with the experimental charge densities, the differential cross sections and the electric charge form factors in the elastic electron-nucleus scattering are obtained by using the phase-shift analysis method. The results from the RBHF theory are in good agreement with the experimental data. In addition, the uncertainty arising from variations of the surface term parameter $f_0$ in the liquid droplet model is also discussed.

## Key claim

The RBHF theory in the full Dirac space, combined with a liquid droplet model, predicts neutron skin thicknesses for $^{208}$Pb of 0.11-0.12 fm, which are significantly smaller than the PREX-II experimental value of $0.283 \pm 0.071$ fm, indicating that the equation of state from this ab initio approach is rather soft compared to the stiff EOS suggested by PREX-II.

## Method

The authors first performed self-consistent RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn A potential, obtaining the equation of state with uniquely determined momentum-dependent scalar and vector single-particle potentials. They then used a liquid droplet model with a surface energy parameter $f_0$ to map the nuclear matter EOS to finite nuclei, parameterizing neutron and proton density distributions as two-parameter Fermi functions and minimizing the total energy. Finally, they computed elastic electron-nucleus scattering differential cross sections and charge form factors using the phase-shift analysis method to compare with experimental charge densities.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness $\Delta r_{np} = 0.11$ fm for $f_0 = 60$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ and $\Delta r_{np} = 0.12$ fm for $f_0 = 70$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ in $^{208}$Pb
- Neutron radius $r_n = 5.59$ fm and proton radius $r_p = 5.48$ fm for $f_0 = 60$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$; $r_n = 5.67$ fm and $r_p = 5.55$ fm for $f_0 = 70$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$
- PREX-II experimental neutron skin thickness: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.283 \pm 0.071$ fm, implying slope of symmetry energy $L = 106 \pm 37$ MeV
- Empirical neutron skin values from pionic probes: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.15 \pm 0.08$ fm and $\Delta r_{np} = 0.14 \pm 0.10$ fm; from pionic atom potentials: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.18 \pm 0.05$ fm; from symmetry energy constraints: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.18 \pm 0.05$ fm

## Context

This work extends the RBHF theory in the full Dirac space, previously applied to nuclear matter and neutron star properties by Wang et al. (2021, 2022), to finite nuclei for the first time using a liquid droplet model. The results contradict the stiff EOS implied by the PREX-II measurement (Adhikari 2021) but are compatible with empirical values from pionic probes (Friedman 2012) and symmetry energy constraints (Tsang 2012). The approach enables future improvements through more microscopic mapping methods such as the local density approximation (Negele 1970) and provides a benchmark for comparing ab initio relativistic calculations with experimental charge densities via electron scattering.
