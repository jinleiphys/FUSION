# Collapse to the Center and Ambiguity in the Asymptotic Behavior of the Off-Shell Scattering Amplitude in Singular Three-Body Problems

> We discuss some examples of equations of the three-body problem with the oscillating asymptotics at large momentum: (i) the fixed-center approximation, (ii) the unitarized equation in the fixed-center approximation, (iii) Skornyakov--Ter-Martirosyan equation, and (iv) equations with operators used in the effective field theory, i.e., which can be expanded in power-series with positive powers of momentum. We show that in the aforementioned three-body problems the situation analogous to the falling down to the center in the two-body problem takes place -- there appears an infinite number of bound states. The energy of these states is not bounded from below. In that sense the situation is close to the falling down to the center in the two-body problem.

# Collapse to the Center and Ambiguity in the Asymptotic Behavior of the Off-Shell Scattering Amplitude in Singular Three-Body Problems

## Key claim

The paper argues that in several three-body problems (fixed-center approximation, unitarized fixed-center approximation, Skornyakov–Ter-Martirosyan equation, and effective field theory expansions), the appearance of an infinite number of bound states with energy unbounded from below is analogous to the "falling down to the center" phenomenon in two-body singular potentials. The key hedge is that this ambiguity arises specifically when the two-body scattering length is positive (attractive case), while for negative scattering length (repulsive case) the solutions are unambiguous.

## Method

The authors analyze four classes of three-body integral equations: (i) the fixed-center approximation (FCA) equation for a light particle interacting with two heavy particles via a constant scattering length, which is exactly solvable; (ii) the unitarized fixed-center approximation (UFCA) using a unitary two-body amplitude; (iii) the Skornyakov–Ter-Martirosyan (STM) equation; and (iv) an effective field theory (EFT) expansion of the two-body amplitude in positive powers of momentum. For each case, they examine the asymptotic behavior of solutions at large momentum, the existence of nontrivial solutions to the homogeneous equation, and the dependence of numerical solutions on the cutoff parameter Λ when integration is restricted to a finite interval [0, Λ]. They compare these three-body results to the two-body problem with a singular potential V(r) = -β/r², analyzed in both coordinate and momentum representations.

## Key numbers

- For the FCA equation with a > 0, the homogeneous solution has the form R_Hom(p,p') = 4πB sin(pa) sin(p'a), where B is an arbitrary constant.
- For the UFCA equation with ξ ≪ 1, the first pure imaginary solutions of the characteristic equation tan(απ/2) = α√(2ξ) appear at α ≈ ± i/√(2ξ).
- Critical cutoff values Λ_cr for the FCA equation (with a = 0.005 MeV⁻¹): 445, 1145, 1845, 2545 MeV.
- Critical cutoff values Λ_cr for the UFCA equation: 490, 1260, 2035, 2810 MeV.
- Critical cutoff values Λ_cr for the EFT equation (FCA plus expansion): 335, 643, 1118, 1435 MeV.
- For the two-body singular potential V = -β/r², the condition for ambiguous phase shifts is γ > 1/4, where γ = 2mβ/ħ².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the fixed-center approximation (Kudryavtsev, Romanov, and Gani, 2016; Kudryavtsev, Romanov, and Gani, earlier reference) and addresses the cyclic dependence of three-body amplitudes on the cutoff parameter Λ, which was previously discussed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) in the context of effective field theory and the STM equation. The paper demonstrates that the requirement of cutoff independence formulated by Bedaque et al. (1999) is not justified when the homogeneous equation has nontrivial solutions, and that the cutoff Λ should instead be treated as a physical parameter related to the three-body scattering length. The analysis connects three-body ambiguities to the well-studied two-body problem of singular potentials discussed in standard quantum mechanics textbooks (Landau and Lifshitz; Morse and Feshbach) and by Case (1950). The results enable a unified understanding of when additional three-body input (such as the three-body scattering length) is required to obtain unambiguous predictions.
