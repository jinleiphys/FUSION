# Phase diagram for interacting Bose gases

> We propose a new form of the inversion method in terms of a selfenergy expansion to access the phase diagram of the Bose-Einstein transition. The dependence of the critical temperature on the interaction parameter is calculated. This is discussed with the help of a new condition for Bose-Einstein condensation in interacting systems which follows from the pole of the T-matrix in the same way as from the divergence of the medium-dependent scattering length. A many-body approximation consisting of screened ladder diagrams is proposed which describes the Monte Carlo data more appropriately. The specific results are that a non-selfconsistent T-matrix leads to a linear coefficient in leading order of 4.7, the screened ladder approximation to 2.3, and the selfconsistent T-matrix due to the effective mass to a coefficient of 1.3 close to the Monte Carlo data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the critical temperature of an interacting Bose gas rises linearly with the interaction parameter in leading order, reaches a maximum, and then decreases until Bose condensation vanishes at a maximal coupling strength; this nonlinear behavior is obtained from a weak-coupling T-matrix summation via an inversion method, with the specific linear coefficient depending on the approximation scheme used (non-selfconsistent, screened ladder, or selfconsistent with effective mass).

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix using a separable Yamaguchi potential, then take the contact-interaction limit to obtain a medium-dependent scattering length. They derive a condition for Bose-Einstein condensation from the pole of the T-matrix (equivalent to the divergence of the medium-dependent scattering length) and apply an inversion method to the series expansion of the critical temperature in the interaction parameter, which yields a nonlinear phase diagram. They further improve the approximation by including a screened ladder summation (replacing the bare potential with a screened one) and by incorporating selfconsistency via an effective mass from the quasiparticle selfenergy.

## Key numbers

* Non-selfconsistent T-matrix: linear coefficient \(c_1 = 4.657\) (from \(c_1 = 16\pi / [3 \zeta(3/2)^{4/3}]\)).
* Screened ladder approximation: linear coefficient \(c_1^{\rm s} = 2.33\) (from \(c_1^{\rm s} = 8\pi / [3 \zeta(3/2)^{4/3}]\)).
* Selfconsistent T-matrix with effective mass: linear coefficient \(c_1 = 1.32\) (from Table I, for T-matrix with \(m^*(\epsilon)\)).
* Maximal critical temperature ratio \((T_c/T_0)_{\rm max} = 1.56\) for the T-matrix and screened T-matrix; 1.22 for the selfconsistent T-matrix with effective mass; 1.07 for the selfconsistent T-matrix with fixed effective mass \(m^*(0)\).
* Critical coupling parameter where Bose condensation vanishes: \(y_0^{\rm crit} = 0.16\) for the T-matrix and selfconsistent T-matrix; 0.32 for the screened T-matrix.
* Second-order coefficient in the inversion expansion: \(c_2 = 8.325\); third-order coefficient: \(c_3 = -14.032\).

## Context

This work extends the study of the critical temperature shift in interacting Bose gases, a topic where Monte Carlo simulations (e.g., Arnold and Moore (2001), Kashurnikov et al. (2001)) and variational perturbation theory (Kleinert (2003)) have reported a linear coefficient \(c_1 \approx 1.3\), while earlier non-selfconsistent bubble summations (Baym et al. (1999)) gave \(c_1 \approx 2.33\). The paper introduces the inversion method as an alternative to variational perturbation theory (Kleinert (2004)) and exact renormalization group calculations (Ledowski et al. (2004)) for accessing the strong-coupling regime, and it enables future work on selfconsistent treatments and the inclusion of condensate effects leading to phonon-like dispersions.
