# Generalized weak-binding relations of compositeness in effective field theory

> We study the compositeness of near-threshold states to investigate the internal structure of exotic hadron candidates. Within the framework of effective field theory, Weinberg's weak-binding relation is extended to more general cases by easing several preconditions. First, by evaluating the contribution from the decay channel, we obtain the generalized relation for unstable quasibound states. Next, we generalize the relation to include the nearby CDD (Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson) pole contribution with the help of the Pade approximant. The validity of the estimation with the generalized weak-binding relations is examined by numerical calculations. The method to systematically evaluate the error in the weak-binding relation is presented. Finally, by applying the extended relation to Lambda(1405), f0(980) and a0(980), we discuss their internal structure, in comparison with other approaches.

## Key claim

The paper extends Weinberg's weak-binding relation for compositeness to more general cases by relaxing preconditions: it generalizes the relation to unstable quasibound states with a decay channel and to states with a nearby CDD pole, while also providing a systematic method to estimate the uncertainty from higher-order correction terms.

## Method

The authors use nonrelativistic effective field theory with contact interactions and a sharp momentum cutoff to describe s-wave two-body scattering. For the bound state case, they derive the scattering amplitude and compositeness from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and completeness relation, then expand the scattering length around the bound state pole to obtain the weak-binding relation. For the quasibound state, they extend the formalism to a coupled-channel system with two scattering channels and a discrete channel, using the Gamow state normalization for the unstable state. For the CDD pole case, they employ the Pade approximant to handle the situation where the effective range expansion does not converge.

## Key numbers

* For the bound state, the weak-binding relation gives the scattering length as \(a_0 = R\left\{ \frac{2X}{1+X} + \mathcal{O}(R_{\mathrm{typ}}/R)\right\}\), where \(R = 1/\sqrt{-2\mu E_h}\) is the wave function radius and \(X\) is the compositeness.
* The leading term of the loop function derivative is \(G^\prime(E_h) = \frac{\mu}{4\pi E_hR }\left\{1+ \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{R_{\mathrm{typ}}}{R}\right)\right\}\).
* The higher-order correction \(\Delta G\) from the loop function expansion is \(\Delta G = \frac{\mu}{4\pi R}\left[1+\mathcal{O}\left(\frac{R_{\mathrm{typ}}}{R}\right)\right]\).
* The uncertainty band for the compositeness is estimated by \(X_{\mathrm{u}} = \frac{a_0/R+ \xi}{2-a_0/R - \xi}\) and \(X_{\mathrm{l}} = \frac{a_0/R- \xi}{2-a_0/R + \xi}\), where \(\xi \equiv R_{\mathrm{typ}}/R\).

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's original weak-binding relation (Weinberg, 1965) for stable bound states to unstable quasibound states and states with nearby CDD poles, building on prior approaches by Baru et al. (2003), Hanhart et al. (2011), and Hyodo et al. (2013). The method enables model-independent determination of compositeness for exotic hadron candidates such as Lambda(1405), f0(980), and a0(980), and provides a systematic error evaluation framework for practical applications to near-threshold states.
