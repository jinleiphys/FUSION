# Peculiar properties of the cluster-cluster interaction induced by the Pauli exclusion principle

> Role of the Pauli principle in the formation of both the discrete spectrum and multi-channel states of the binary nuclear systems composed of clusters is studied in the Algebraic Version of the resonating-group method. Solutions of the Hill-Wheeler equations in the discrete representation of a complete basis of the Pauli-allowed states are discussed for 4He+n, 3H+3H, and 4He+4He binary systems. An exact treatment of the antisymmetrization effects are shown to result in either an effective repulsion of the clusters, or their effective attraction. It also yields a change in the intensity of the centrifugal potential. Both factors significantly affect the scattering phase behavior. Special attention is paid to the multi-channel cluster structure 6He+6He as well as to the difficulties arising in the case when the two clustering configurations, 6He+6He and 4He+8He, are taken into account simultaneously. In the latter case the Pauli principle, even in the absence of a potential energy of the cluster-cluster interaction, leads to the inelastic processes and secures an existence of both the bound state and resonance in the 12Be compound nucleus.

## Key claim

The Pauli exclusion principle, when treated exactly within the Algebraic Version of the resonating-group method, can produce either an effective repulsion or an effective attraction between approaching clusters, depending on whether the eigenvalues of the Pauli-allowed states are less than or greater than unity; this contrasts with earlier approximate treatments that simulated the Pauli principle solely with a repulsive potential.

## Method

The authors constructed a complete discrete basis of harmonic-oscillator states allowed by the Pauli principle, classified by SU(3) symmetry indices and their eigenvalues of the norm kernel. They derived and solved the Hill-Wheeler equations in this discrete representation for binary cluster systems, including the single-channel cases of 4He+n, 3H+3H, and 4He+4He, and the multi-channel case of 6He+6He and 4He+8He simultaneously. The effective potential induced by the antisymmetrization operator was extracted from the finite-difference form of the kinetic-energy equations, and its dependence on the ratio of successive eigenvalues of the norm kernel was analyzed.

## Key numbers

* For the 4He+4He system, the eigenvalues of the norm kernel for the Pauli-allowed states are positive and approach unity from below as the number of oscillator quanta increases, leading to a partial suppression of the centrifugal potential.
* For the 3H+3H system, the eigenvalues approach unity from above as the number of oscillator quanta increases, resulting in a strengthening of the centrifugal potential.
* The effective potential generated by the Pauli principle is given by the expression U_nu,nu^Pauli = (1/2)(1 + Lambda_{nu-2}/Lambda_nu) * (2l+1)^2/(16 nu) + (1/4)(1 - Lambda_{nu-2}/Lambda_nu) * (nu + 1/2), where nu is the number of oscillator quanta and l is the angular momentum of cluster relative motion.
* For the 12Be compound nucleus with two open channels (6He+6He and 4He+8He), the Pauli principle alone, even in the absence of a potential energy of cluster-cluster interaction, leads to inelastic processes and secures the existence of both a bound state and a resonance.

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Version of the resonating-group method developed by Filippov and collaborators (Fewbody, Fewbody2) to provide a complete treatment of antisymmetrization effects beyond the orthogonality condition model of Saito (Saito) and the phenomenological repulsive-potential approaches of Baz (Baz, Golb, Baz1) and Zhukov (Zhuk). The exact treatment of the Pauli principle in the discrete basis of allowed states enables a detailed analysis of how the eigenvalues of the norm kernel modify the centrifugal potential and generate an effective attraction or repulsion, which is essential for understanding multi-channel cluster structures such as those in 12Be.
