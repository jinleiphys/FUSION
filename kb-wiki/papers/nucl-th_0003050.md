# Pion-nucleon interaction in a covariant hadron-exchange model

> We develop a relativistic covariant and unitary description of the pion-nucleon interaction in a hadron-exchange model. The model is based on the solution of a dimensionally reduced (quasipotential) Bethe-Salpeter equation for the partial-wave off-shell $ŽĆN$ scattering amplitudes with the potential consisting of the field-theoretical $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon, Roper, Delta, $D_{13}$, $S_{11}$ exchanges, and the $t$-channel $Žü$ and $Žā$ meson exchanges. The contributions of the spin-3/2 Delta and $D_{13}$ resonances are treated within the Rarita-Schwinger formalism and different forms of the $ŽĆN╬ö$ vertex are investigated. The free parameters of the model are fitted to the $ŽĆN$ phase-shift data of the KH80 and SM95 partial-wave analyses in the region up to 600 MeV pion kinetic energy. The resulting on-shell solution provides a good description of the $ŽĆN$ scattering lengths, as well as the energy behavior of the S, P, and D partial waves. The sensitivity of the phase shifts on various model-dependent effects is examined.

## Key claim

The paper develops a relativistic covariant and unitary model of pion-nucleon scattering using a quasipotential (equal-time) reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, but the description is limited to pion kinetic energies up to 600 MeV and the resonances (Delta, Roper, D13, S11) are all included as explicit elementary fields rather than being generated dynamically.

## Method

The authors solve a three-dimensional equal-time quasipotential equation for the partial-wave off-shell ŽĆN scattering amplitudes, using a tree-level potential constructed from s- and u-channel exchanges of the nucleon, Roper, Delta, D13, and S11 resonances, plus t-channel Žü and Žā meson exchanges. The spin-3/2 Delta and D13 contributions are treated within the Rarita-Schwinger formalism, and both the conventional and a gauge-invariant ŽĆN╬ö coupling are investigated. The free parameters are fitted to the KH80 and SM95 partial-wave phase-shift analyses up to 600 MeV pion kinetic energy.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic one-boson-exchange approach previously applied to NN interactions (e.g., Gross and Surya (1993), Pearce and Jennings (1991)) to the ŽĆN system using a covariant quasipotential framework. The equal-time equation preserves charge conjugation symmetry, unlike some other quasipotential reductions such as the spectator equation. The model enables future studies of pion scattering and production on light nuclei in a self-consistent relativistic framework, and provides a basis for comparing with chiral perturbation theory calculations (e.g., Weinberg (1966, 1967)) and with four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter solutions such as those of Nieland and Tjon (1968) and Lahiff and Afnan (1998).
