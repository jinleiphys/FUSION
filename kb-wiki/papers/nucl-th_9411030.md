# The Effective Non-Relativistic Hamiltonian for Electron-Nucleus Interaction

> We present the effective Hamiltonian for an electron-nucleus interaction in non-relativistic limit up to the second order in the inverse nucleon mass. This Hamiltonian takes into account the distortion of electron waves and allows to calculate its effect as well as matrix elements for off-shell transitions in $(e,e'p)$ reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD investigation of the expansion of a small-sized hadronic wave packet (a model for a point-like configuration) in the pion channel, using SU(2) gauge theory with Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation. The authors find that the matrix elements of the soft interaction operator \(\hat b^2\) between the excited and ground states decrease rapidly with the energy of the excited state, which is an important component for color transparency, but caution that the initial wave packet in their calculation is not truly small and its relation to color transparency physics is unclear.

## Method

The authors model a small-sized wave packet using a point hadronic source (pseudoscalar interpolating field) on a \(12^3 \times 24\) lattice with SU(2) gauge theory at \(\beta = 2.5\) and three values of the hopping parameter (\(\kappa = 0.146, 0.148, 0.149\)). They compute the Euclidean time-dependent Bethe-Salpeter amplitude \(\Psi(r,t)\) and evaluate \(b^2(t) = \int d^3 r \Psi(r,t) \psi_\pi(r) (r \cos\theta)^2\), which represents the strength of the soft interaction of the expanding wave packet with a pion. The time dependence of \(b^2(t)\) and the two-point correlation function \(G_2(t)\) are simultaneously fit as a superposition of three states (ground state plus two excited states) using a correlated \(\chi^2\) fit with a full covariance matrix, enabling analytic continuation to Minkowski time.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.09 \pm 0.012\) fm, determined from the \(\rho\) mass.
* Quark masses for the three hopping parameters: \(m_q = 275\) MeV, \(174\) MeV, and \(124\) MeV (corresponding to \(\kappa = 0.146, 0.148, 0.149\)).
* Critical hopping parameter (extrapolation to \(m_\pi = 0\)): \(\kappa_{cr} = 0.151(1)\).
* The matrix element \(b^2_n\) decreases by a factor of 2 with each excited state; \(b^2_3\) is negative, allowing formation of a reduced size wave packet.
* Expansion time \(\tau_{exp}\) (time for \(b^2(\tau)\) to reach the ground state contribution \(b^2_1\)): approximately \(0.03\) fm for the actual wave packet; an upper limit of \(\tau_{exp} \approx 2\) fm is obtained by varying the coefficients \(c_n\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of hadron-hadron interactions (Green, 1994) and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (Negele, 1994) to the specific problem of wave packet expansion relevant for color transparency. The authors use a model soft interaction operator \(\hat b^2\) introduced by Miller (1994) and apply a many-pole fitting procedure that addresses concerns raised by Derek (1994) and Kilcup (1994) about the reliability of such fits. The results enable future lattice evaluations of wave packets created by the electromagnetic current operator, which would be more directly relevant to color transparency in \((e,e'p)\) reactions.
