# New Results on Quantum Chaos in Atomic Nuclei

> In atomic nuclei, ordered and chaotic states generally coexist. In this paper the transition from ordered to chaotic states will be discussed in the framework of roto-vibrational and shell models. In particular for $^{160}Gd$, in the roto-vibrational model, the Poincarè sections clearly show the transition from order to chaos for different values of rotational frequency. Furthermore, the spectral statistics of low-lying states of several $fp$ shell nuclei are studied with realistic shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in atomic nuclei, ordered and chaotic states coexist, and that a transition from order to chaos (and back to order) occurs as a function of rotational frequency in the roto-vibrational model and as a function of energy in shell-model calculations, with the caveat that the term "chaos" is used in a restricted semiclassical sense where spectral statistics distinguish ordered from chaotic behavior when the semiclassical limit is not known.

## Method

For the roto-vibrational model, classical trajectories of the nuclear shape variables were computed using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method, and Poincaré sections were constructed for the nuclei 160Gd and 166Er at various rotational frequencies and energies. For the shell-model calculations, exact diagonalizations were performed in the (f7/2, p3/2, f5/2, p1/2) space assuming 40Ca as an inert core, using the Lanczos algorithm with the code ANTOINE, for nuclei in the A=46-50 region with 6 active nucleons. The spectral statistics P(s) (nearest-neighbor spacing distribution) and Δ3(L) (spectral rigidity) were computed for low-lying states up to 6 MeV above the yrast line, and the Brody distribution parameter ω was used to quantify the degree of chaoticity.

## Key numbers

- For 160Gd at energy 5.5 MeV, Poincaré sections show a chaos-order transition as rotational frequency increases, studied at ħω = 0 MeV, ħω = 0.5 MeV, and ħω = 1 MeV.
- For 166Er at rotational frequency ω = 0, Poincaré sections show a chaos-order transition at energies E = 1 MeV, E = 6 MeV, E = 9 MeV, and E = 12 MeV, with the transition less sharp than for 160Gd.
- The number of J=0-9 spacings below 4, 5, and 6 MeV ranges from 42, 66, and 105 in 46Ca to 86, 149, and 231 in 46Ti, respectively.
- For 166Er, spectral statistics P(s) and Δ3(L) confirm classical results: for energies above the saddle energy (~4 MeV) there is prevalently chaotic behavior; for higher energies there is mixed behavior.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of order and chaos coexistence in nuclei by Bohigas and Weidenmüller (1988) and by Lopez-Arias, Manfredi, and Salasnich (1994), using both the roto-vibrational model and shell-model calculations. The roto-vibrational results build on the formalism of Eisenberg and Greiner (1970) and Faessler and Greiner (1962), while the shell-model calculations use a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic force with monopole improvements, following the approach of Caurier, Gomez, Manfredi, and Salasnich (1996). The finding that calcium isotopes show significant deviations from random-matrix theory predictions, attributed to weaker proton-neutron interactions compared to neighboring nuclei, enables further investigation of how the balance between proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron interactions determines the degree of quantum chaos in nuclear spectra.
