# Relativistic Hartree-Fock Chiral Lagrangians with confinement, nucleon finite size and short-range effects

> A relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangian including a chiral potential and nucleon polarisation is investigated in hopes of providing a better description of dense nuclear matter. We fully consider the contribution of the exchange Fock term to the energy and the self-energies, and in addition we investigate the nucleon's compositeness and finite size effects (confinement and form factors) and short range correlations modeled by a Jastrow ansatz. These effects are added step by step, such that their impact on the dense matter properties can be analysed in details. The parameters of the model are adjusted to reproduce fundamental properties related to the QCD theory at low energy, such as the chiral symmetry breaking, nucleon's quark substructure and Lattice-QCD predictions, as well as two empirical properties at saturation: the binding energy and the density. All other empirical parameters, e.g., symmetry energy and its slope, incompressibility modulus, effective mass, as well as spin-isospin Landau-Midgal parameter are predictions of the models and can be used to evaluate the gain of the different approximation schemes in describing nuclear properties. Bayesian statistics is employed in order to propagate parameter uncertainties into predictions for the nuclear matter properties. We show that the splitting of the effective Landau mass is largely influenced by the value of the $ρ^T$ coupling, and we show that the fit to the symmetry energy, which induces an increase of the coupling constant $g_ρ$ by about 20-25% compared to the case where it is fixed by the quark model, provides a very good EoS compatible with the present nuclear physics knowledge.

## Key claim

The authors construct a relativistic Hartree-Fock chiral Lagrangian that incorporates nucleon confinement, finite-size effects (form factors), and short-range correlations (Jastrow ansatz) to improve the description of dense nuclear matter, showing that the fit to the symmetry energy requires increasing the coupling constant \(g_\rho\) by about 20-25% compared to the quark model prediction, which then yields an equation of state compatible with current nuclear physics knowledge.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Lagrangian with chiral symmetry breaking (linear sigma model) and nucleon polarisability, then extend the previous Hartree treatment (RMF-CC) by fully including the exchange Fock terms for scalar (\(s\)), vector (\(\omega\)), isovector (\(\rho\), \(\delta\)), and pseudoscalar (\(\pi\)) mesons. They add stepwise improvements: model A is pure relativistic Hartree-Fock; model B applies the Orsay prescription to remove contact spin-isospin contributions; model C introduces finite-size effects via form factors at interaction vertices; model D adds short-range correlations via a Jastrow ansatz. Parameters are fitted to Lattice-QCD inputs (\(a_2\), \(a_4\)) and empirical saturation properties (binding energy \(E_\mathrm{sat}\) and density \(n_\mathrm{sat}\)), with Bayesian statistics used to propagate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: \(n_\mathrm{sat} = 0.155 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^{-3}\) (empirical input, from Margueron 2018)
* Saturation energy: \(E_\mathrm{sat} = -15.8 \pm 0.3\) MeV (empirical input, from Margueron 2018)
* Lattice-QCD input parameters: \(a_2 = 1.553 \pm 0.136\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (L1 set, from LWY 2003) and \(a_4 = -0.509 \pm 0.054\) GeV\(^{-3}\) (L1 set, from LWY 2003); alternative L2 set: \(a_2 = 1.1 \pm 0.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(a_4 = -0.225 \pm 0.05\) GeV\(^{-3}\) (from Armour 2010)
* Fitting the symmetry energy increases \(g_\rho\) by about 20-25% compared to the quark model value \(g_\rho = g_\omega/3\)
* Three values of the \(\rho\) tensor coupling \(\kappa_\rho\) are explored: 0.0 (no \(\rho_T\)), 3.7 (weak, from Vector Dominance Model, Bhaduri 1988), and 6.6 (strong, from scattering data, Hohler 1975)
* Fixed meson masses: \(m_\omega = 783.0\) MeV, \(m_\rho = 779.0\) MeV, \(m_\delta = 984.7\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.6\) MeV; nucleon mass \(M_N = 938.9\) MeV; pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 94.0\) MeV; axial coupling \(g_A = 1.25\)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field chiral confinement (RMF-CC) model of Somasundaram (2022), which was limited to the Hartree level, by incorporating the full Fock exchange terms essential for including pion contributions. The approach builds on the identification of the nuclear physics sigma meson with the radial fluctuation of the chiral condensate (Chanfray 2001) and includes nucleon polarisability (Chanfray 2005). The results enable systematic comparison of approximation schemes (Hartree vs. Hartree-Fock, with and without form factors and short-range correlations) for predicting nuclear matter properties such as symmetry energy, incompressibility modulus, effective mass, and the Landau-Midgal parameter, and can inform neutron star equation of state modeling.
