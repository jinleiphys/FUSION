# Event patterns (particle scatter plots) extracted from charged particle spectra in $pp$ and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

> The transverse momentum ($p_T$) and pseudorapidity ($η$) spectra of charged particles produced in proton-proton ($pp$) and lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions at the large hadron collider (LHC) are described by a hybrid model. In the model, the $p_T$ spectrum is described by a two-component distribution which contains an inverse power-law suggested by the QCD (Quantum Chromodynamic) calculus and an Erlang distribution resulted from a multisource thermal model. The $η$ spectrum is described by a Gaussian rapidity ($y$) distribution resulted from the Landau hydrodynamic model and the two-component $p_T$ distribution, where the conversion between $y$ and $η$ is accurately considered. The modelling results are in agreement with the experimental data measured by the ATLAS Collaboration in $pp$ collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV and in Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. Based on the parameter values extracted from $p_T$ and $η$ or $y$ spectra, the event patterns or particle scatter plots in three-dimensional velocity and momentum spaces are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hybrid model combining a two-component transverse momentum distribution (inverse power-law plus Erlang) and a Gaussian rapidity distribution from the Landau hydrodynamic model can reproduce charged-particle spectra measured by ATLAS in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and that the extracted parameters can be used to reconstruct event patterns in velocity and momentum space at kinetic freeze-out. The hedge is that the Gaussian rapidity distribution is only approximately used as a representation of the methodology, and the authors note that in some cases a two-Gaussian distribution would be more appropriate.

## Method

The authors fit the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles using a two-component distribution: an inverse power-law from QCD calculus for the hard scattering process and an Erlang distribution from a multisource thermal model for the soft excitation process, with a parameter k controlling the relative contribution. The pseudorapidity spectra are described by a Gaussian rapidity distribution from the Landau hydrodynamic model, with the conversion between rapidity and pseudorapidity performed via a Monte Carlo method that also generates azimuthal angles uniformly. Using the fitted parameter values, the Monte Carlo procedure produces three-dimensional velocity and momentum distributions at kinetic freeze-out, from which event patterns (particle scatter plots) are constructed.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, the inverse power-law parameters are p0=0.82±0.04 GeV/c and n=6.90±0.10, with k=0.90±0.05 and Erlang mean <pTi>=0.37±0.02 GeV/c (Table 1).
- For 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the inverse power-law parameters are p0=0.66±0.03 GeV/c and n=6.24±0.10, with k=0.41±0.05 and Erlang mean <pTi>=0.38±0.02 GeV/c (Table 1).
- The rapidity distribution width sigma_y for pp collisions ranges from 3.40±0.17 (for pT interval 1.7-2.0 GeV/c) down to 1.70±0.09 (for pT interval 59.8-94.8 GeV/c) (Table 2).
- The rapidity distribution width sigma_y for 0-5% Pb-Pb collisions ranges from 3.20±0.16 (for pT interval 1.7-2.0 GeV/c) down to 1.40±0.07 (for pT interval 59.8-94.8 GeV/c) (Table 2).
- The relative yield of particles in the lowest pT interval (1.7-2.0 GeV/c) compared to the highest (59.8-94.8 GeV/c) is 2.75×10^6 for pp and 2.25×10^6 for 0-5% Pb-Pb collisions (Table 4).
- The root-mean-square velocity components in pp collisions for the lowest pT interval are sqrt(beta_x^2)=0.328±0.010, sqrt(beta_y^2)=0.343±0.010, sqrt(beta_z^2)=0.879±0.006 (Table 3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method of extracting event patterns from net-baryon spectra in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies (Chen and Liu, 2015) and from Z boson and quarkonium spectra in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies (Chen and Liu, 2016). The present study applies a different combination of functions (inverse power-law plus Erlang for pT, Gaussian for rapidity) to charged particles, demonstrating the flexibility of the approach. The results enable visualization of the three-dimensional velocity and momentum distributions at kinetic freeze-out for different collision systems and centralities, which can be compared with hydrodynamic model predictions and used to study the transition from soft to hard scattering processes.
