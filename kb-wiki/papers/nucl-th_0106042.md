# Deuteron distribution in nuclear matter

> We analyze the properties of deuteron-like structures in infinite, correlated nuclear matter, described by a realistic hamiltonian containing the Urbana $v_{14}$ two-nucleon and the Urbana TNI many-body potentials. The distribution of neutron-proton pairs, carrying the deuteron quantum numbers, is obtained as a function of the total momentum by computing the overlap between the nuclear matter in its ground state and the deuteron wave functions in correlated basis functions theory. We study the differences between the S- and D-wave components of the deuteron and those of the deuteron-like pair in the nuclear medium. The total number of deuteron type pairs is computed and compared with the predictions of Levinger's quasideuteron model. The resulting Levinger's factor in nuclear matter at equilibrium densityis 11.63. We use the local density approximation to estimate the Levinger's factor for heavy nuclei, obtaining results which are consistent with the available experimental data from photoreactions.

# Deuteron distribution in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The Levinger factor for nuclear matter at equilibrium density is calculated to be 11.63, which is about 20% larger than the phenomenological value of 9.26 reported in the literature, but the results for heavy nuclei are consistent with experimental photoreaction data because the phenomenological values from Levinger and Laget bracket the Correlated Basis Function results.

## Method

The authors used Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory to compute the overlap between the nuclear matter ground state wave function and deuteron wave functions, employing a realistic Hamiltonian with the Urbana v14 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana TNI many-body potential. The distribution of neutron-proton pairs carrying deuteron quantum numbers was obtained as a function of total momentum by evaluating the two-body density matrix using a dressed leading order cluster approximation. The total number of quasideuteron pairs was then used to extract the Levinger factor, and the local density approximation was applied to extend the nuclear matter results to finite heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Levinger factor for nuclear matter at equilibrium density: L(infinity) = 11.63
- Total number of quasideuteron pairs per particle in correlated nuclear matter: P_D/A = 2.895, compared to the Fermi gas model result of 3.406
- Fermi gas Levinger factor: L_FG(infinity) = 13.6
- Phenomenological Levinger factor from literature: L_Lev(infinity) = 9.26
- Nuclear matter equilibrium density used: rho = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Computed Levinger factor for heavy nuclei follows L(A) = 11.63 - 9.76 A^{-1/3}
- The radius of the quasideuteron configuration differs from the deuteron radius by approximately 2%

## Context

This work extends the quasideuteron model originally proposed by Levinger (1951) and Gottfried (1958) to explain nuclear photoabsorption, and provides an ab initio calculation of the Levinger factor that had previously only been extracted phenomenologically from photoreaction data by Anghinolfi (1986) and Carlos (1982). The results are consistent with earlier Green's Function Monte Carlo studies of deuteron-like configurations in light nuclei by Forest (1996), which found that np pair density distributions in He-3, He-4, Li-6, Li-7, and O-16 exhibit size and structure similar to the free deuteron. The calculations enable more quantitative predictions for lepton-nucleus reactions where ejected hadrons appear in kinematical regions forbidden for lepton-nucleon processes.
