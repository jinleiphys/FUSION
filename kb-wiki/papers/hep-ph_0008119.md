# Bremsstrahlung from a Microscopic Model of Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We compute bremsstrahlung arising from the acceleration of individual charged baryons and mesons during the time evolution of high-energy Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider using a microscopic transport model. We elucidate the connection between bremsstrahlung and charge stopping by colliding artificial pure proton on pure neutron nuclei. From the intensity of low energy bremsstrahlung, the time scale and the degree of stopping could be accurately extracted without measuring any hadronic observables.

## Key claim
Bremsstrahlung from relativistic heavy ion collisions can be used to extract both the time scale and degree of charge stopping without measuring any hadronic observables, but the extraction requires subtracting a non-negligible mesonic contribution that acts as "noise" relative to the baryon signal.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (sqrt(s)=100 GeV/nucleon, impact parameter b <= 3 fm) and computed classical bremsstrahlung from the instantaneous accelerations of all charged hadrons at each microscopic collision or decay event. They summed the radiation amplitude over 160 events in the center-of-momentum frame, treating each hadron's velocity change as a delta-function acceleration. To isolate baryon stopping from charge stopping, they also simulated artificial collisions of a pure proton nucleus (Z=197) against a pure neutron nucleus (A=197) and developed a procedure to model and subtract the mesonic contribution using random sampling of final meson charges and velocities.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy limit: photons with energy less than about 200 MeV do not resolve individual hadron collision details (typical proper time ~1 fm/c)
- Beam energy: sqrt(s)=100 GeV/nucleon for Au+Au at RHIC
- Impact parameter: b <= 3 fm for central collisions
- Number of events: 160 simulated nucleus-nucleus collisions
- Global charge stopping time extracted from UrQMD: t_f between 1.2 and 1.4 fm/c
- Special angle for stopping measurement at RHIC: q_h ~ 7.93 degrees
- Beam velocity at RHIC: v_0 ~ 0.999956
- Average number of charged mesons at RHIC from UrQMD: <N_+> ~ <N_-> ~ 3000
- Net charge difference from mesons at SPS and in UrQMD: Delta Q typically below 40

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of bremsstrahlung in nucleus-nucleus collisions by Kapusta (1977) at ~1 GeV/nucleon and by Bjorken and McLerran (1985) and Dumitru (1999) at higher energies, and builds on the coarse-grained space-time evolution approach of Kapusta, Wong, and collaborators (1999-2000). The authors use the microscopic UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999) to go beyond simple Landau-like or Bjorken-like scenarios, showing that realistic collisions exhibit mixed behavior. The method enables determination of the global charge stopping time, which no hadronic measurement can provide, and connects bremsstrahlung to the stopping variable S defined by Wong (2000).
