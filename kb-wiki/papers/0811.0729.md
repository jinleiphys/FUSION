# Second order hydrodynamic coefficients from kinetic theory

> In a relativistic setting, hydrodynamic calculations which include shear viscosity (which is first order in an expansion in gradients of the flow velocity) are unstable and acausal unless they also include terms to second order in gradients. To date such terms have only been computed in supersymmetric N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory at infinite coupling. Here we compute these second-order hydrodynamic coefficients in weakly coupled QCD, perturbatively to leading order in the QCD coupling, using kinetic theory. We also compute them in QED and scalar lambda phi^4 theory.

## Key claim

The paper computes second-order hydrodynamic coefficients in weakly coupled QCD, QED, and scalar λφ⁴ theory using kinetic theory, finding that the dimensionless ratios of second-order to first-order coefficients are larger than in strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory, but the authors caution that weakly coupled QCD should be viewed as a "toy model" for QCD at realistic couplings since the coupling expansion is not believed to converge very well.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation order by order in gradients, first obtaining the first-order departure from equilibrium f₁ (proportional to shear viscosity η) by inverting the linearized collision operator, then computing the second-order stress-energy tensor by evaluating three contributions: the streaming term 2Pᵞ∂ᵞf₁, the quadratic collision term C₁₁[f₁], and the matrix-element correction C₁;M₁[f₁], all weighted by the known function χ(p) from the first-order solution. The collision operator includes only 2↔2 and effective 1↔2 scattering processes, and the calculation is performed to leading order in the gauge coupling.

## Key numbers

- (ε+P)ητΠ/η² = 5.9 to 5.0 (varies with coupling) in QCD; 6.10517 in λφ⁴ theory
- (ε+P)λ₁/η² = 5.2 to 4.1 (varies with coupling) in QCD; 6.13264 in λφ⁴ theory
- (ε+P)λ₂/η² = -2 × (ε+P)ητΠ/η² exactly in conformal kinetic theory
- (ε+P)κ/η² = 0 and (ε+P)λ₃/η² = 0 at leading order
- In e⁺e⁻ QED at realistic coupling: (ε+P)τΠ/η = 5.9664 and (ε+P)λ₁/η² = 5.4156
- In N=4 SYM at strong coupling: (ε+P)ητΠ/η² = 4-2ln(2) ≈ 2.6137, (ε+P)λ₁/η² = 2, (ε+P)λ₂/η² = -4ln(2) ≈ -2.7726, (ε+P)κ/η² = 4

## Context

This work extends the program of Baier et al. (2008) who identified five second-order hydrodynamic coefficients in conformal theories and computed them in strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory. The authors provide the first perturbative calculation of these coefficients in weakly coupled QCD using kinetic theory, complementing the strong-coupling results. The findings enable viscous hydrodynamics simulations of the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC to incorporate realistic estimates of second-order transport coefficients, with the expectation that QCD values at realistic couplings will fall between the weak-coupling results and the generally smaller strong-coupling SYM values. The relation λ₂ = -2ητΠ agrees with earlier work by Israel and Stewart (1979) and Rischke (1998), but the authors find λ₁ ≠ ητΠ contrary to Rischke's result, which they attribute to that work fixing an Ansatz for the departure from equilibrium and dropping nonlinear contributions from the collision operator.
