# Magnetic moments of octet baryons at finite density and temperature

> We investigate the change of magnetic moments of octet baryons in nuclear matter at a finite density and temperature. Quark-meson coupling models are employed in describing properties of octet baryons and their interactions. Magnetic moments of octet baryons are found to increase non-negligibly as density and temperature increase, and we find that temperature dependence can be strongly correlated with the quark-hadron phase transition. Model dependence is also examined by comparing the results from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to those by the modified QMC (MQMC) model where the bag constant is assumed to depend on density. Both models predict sizable dependence on density and temperature, but the MQMC model shows a more drastic change of magnetic moments. Feasible changes of the nucleon mass by strong magnetic fields are also reported in the given models.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of octet baryons increase non-negligibly with both density and temperature, and this temperature dependence may be strongly correlated with the quark-hadron phase transition; however, the magnitude and even the sign of the change depend on whether the bag constant is taken as density-independent (QMC model) or density-dependent (MQMC model).

## Method

The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and its modified version (MQMC) to describe baryons as MIT bags in hot, dense nuclear matter. Mean-field values for the σ and ω mesons are determined self-consistently by maximizing the pressure at a given baryon density and temperature. Magnetic moments are then computed from SU(6) quark wave functions using the magnetic moment operator, with the bag radius for each baryon fixed by minimizing the effective mass.

## Key numbers

- Free-space bag radius chosen as \(R_b = 1.0\) fm for all baryons.
- Bare quark masses: \(m_u = m_d = 0\) MeV, \(m_s = 150\) MeV.
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\); binding energy per baryon 16 MeV; compression modulus \(\sim 280\) MeV at saturation.
- At \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0.1\) and \(T = 250\) MeV in the MQMC model, the effective nucleon mass is reduced to about \(0.65 m_N\).
- For a proton in free space, \(\kappa_p \simeq 1.79 \mu_N\) with \(\mu_N = 3.15 \times 10^{-18}\) MeV G\(^{-1}\); at \(B/B_c^e \sim 10^5\) (where \(B_c^e = 4.414 \times 10^{13}\) G), the mass correction \(|\kappa_p B| \simeq 25\) MeV, about 3% of the free nucleon mass.
- At \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0.1\) and \(T = 250\) MeV in the MQMC model, the net correction from temperature and magnetic fields is about 11% of the effective nucleon mass, more than three times the free-space correction.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of baryon properties in hot and dense matter using relativistic mean-field models (Zakout 2005, Klahn 2006) and chiral motivated models (Yazaki 1989, Dexheimer 2008), and specifically builds on the QMC model of Guichon (1988). The results are relevant for interpreting magnetic moment measurements in hypernuclei (Tamura 2007) and for understanding the QCD phase transition and medium effects in high-energy nuclear collisions and proto-neutron stars. The authors note that strong magnetic fields up to \(1.5 \times 10^{19}\) G predicted for LHC energies (Sokokov 2009) and \(10^{18}\) G in neutron star cores could make the corrections to baryon masses and magnetic moments even more significant, potentially providing a signal for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration.
