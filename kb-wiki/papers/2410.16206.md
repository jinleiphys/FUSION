# Locating the QCD critical point through contours of constant entropy density

> We propose a new method to investigate the existence and location of the conjectured high-temperature critical point of strongly interacting matter via contours of constant entropy density. By approximating these lines as a power series in the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, one can extrapolate them from first-principle results at zero net-baryon density, and use them to locate the QCD critical point, including the associated first-order and spinodal lines. As a proof of principle, we employ currently available continuum-extrapolated first-principle results from the Wuppertal--Budapest collaboration to find a critical point at a temperature and a baryon chemical potential of $T_c = 114.3 \pm 6.9$ MeV and $μ_{B,c} = 602.1 \pm 62.1$ MeV, respectively. We advocate for a more precise determination of the required expansion coefficients via lattice QCD simulations as a means of pinpointing the location of the critical endpoint in the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method to locate the QCD critical point by extrapolating contours of constant entropy density from zero baryon chemical potential, finding a critical point at (T_c = 114.3 ± 6.9 MeV, μ_{B,c} = 602.1 ± 62.1 MeV) at leading expansion order O(μ_B^2), but notes this estimate carries additional systematic uncertainty from the mean-field approximation and truncation error.

## Method

The authors expand the temperature along lines of constant entropy density as a power series in the baryon chemical potential μ_B, anchored at μ_B = 0 where lattice QCD results are available. The expansion coefficient α_2(T_0) is expressed in terms of the entropy density s(T) and baryon number susceptibility χ_2^B(T) at μ_B = 0, including their temperature derivatives. The critical point is located by solving the conditions (∂T_s/∂T_0)_{μ_B} = 0 and (∂^2 T_s/∂T_0^2)_{μ_B} = 0, which correspond to the vanishing first and second derivatives of temperature with respect to entropy density at fixed chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical point temperature: T_c = 114.3 ± 6.9 MeV
- Critical baryon chemical potential: μ_{B,c} = 602.1 ± 62.1 MeV
- T_{0,c} (anchor temperature at μ_B = 0): 140.9 ± 2.0 MeV
- Pearson correlation coefficient between T_c and μ_{B,c}: -0.91
- Second solution at imaginary chemical potential: T̃_c = 197.1 ± 7.1 MeV, μ̃_{B,c}/T̃_c = i(3.50 ± 0.30)
- Consistency check using smoothing splines gives (T_c, μ_{B,c}) = (119.5 ± 5.4, 556.5 ± 49.8) MeV
- Consistency check using HotQCD parametrization gives (T_c, μ_{B,c}) = (111.4, 634.8) MeV
- Holographic model test: method recovers CP at (104.5, 637.6) MeV versus true (103, 599) MeV, relative errors 1.4% in T_c and 6.4% in μ_{B,c}

## Context

This work extends recent efforts to locate the QCD critical point using lattice QCD input, including analyses of Lee-Yang edge singularities by Basar (2023) and Clarke (2024), and functional renormalization group and Dyson-Schwinger approaches by Fu (2019), Gunkel (2021), and Gao (2020). The method is distinct from standard Taylor expansion of thermodynamic pressure, which cannot describe contour crossings needed for a critical point. The results are compatible with recent CP estimates in the range 400 ≤ μ_B ≤ 650 MeV from Hippert (2023) and others, and suggest the collision energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 4-6 GeV is closest to the estimated CP location. The method also reproduces the known Roberge-Weiss endpoint at imaginary chemical potential, with T_RW ≈ 208 ± 5 MeV from Bonati (2016) and Dimopoulos (2021).
