# Color Transparency and the Vanishing Deuterium Shadow

> We find that the final state interactions in the $d(e,e'np)$ amplitude depend strongly on the final momentum of the spectator nucleon. This means that color transparency effects can be studied at rather low four-momentum transfer $Q^2\geq$ 4 (GeV/c)$^2$ using un-polarized and polarized deuteron targets.

## Key claim

Color transparency (CT) effects in deuteron electrodisintegration can be observed at four-momentum transfers as low as Q² ≥ 4 (GeV/c)², provided the spectator nucleon is detected in "perpendicular kinematics" where its momentum is approximately transverse to the virtual photon direction, because the final state interactions depend strongly on the spectator momentum.

## Method

The authors compute the d(e,e'pn) scattering amplitude using an eikonal approximation that includes the final state np interaction via a parametrized scattering amplitude. They define the transparency T as the ratio of the cross section with final state interactions to the plane wave impulse approximation cross section, and compute T for unpolarized and polarized deuteron targets using two models of color transparency: the quantum diffusion model and a three-state model where the point-like configuration is a superposition of three baryonic states.

## Key numbers

- Q² ≥ 4 (GeV/c)² is the threshold where color transparency effects become accessible in deuteron experiments
- At p_t = 0, T ≈ 0.97 (shadowing); at p_t = 0.2 GeV/c, T ≈ 0.5; for p_t > 300 MeV/c, T > 1
- The np total cross section σ^{pn}_{tot} ≈ 40 mb, with a_n ≈ -0.2 and b_n ≈ 6-8 GeV⁻² for Q² > 3 (GeV/c)²
- The expansion length scale l_h = 2p_n/ΔM² with ΔM² = 0.7 GeV², giving l_h ∼ 0.4 (p/GeV) fm
- Color transparency effects modify cross section ratios by up to 30% for Q² = 6-10 (GeV/c)²
- The ratio of d(e,e'pn) cross sections at p_t ∼ 200 MeV/c and p_t ∼ 300 MeV/c shows 20-40% effects for Q² ∼ 4-10 (GeV/c)²
- The tensor asymmetry in polarized deuteron experiments shows 50-100% change at Q² ∼ 4-10 (GeV/c)²
- Combined corrections from meson exchange currents, isobar contributions, and charge exchange are ≤ 5-10% for Q² > 2 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for color transparency developed by Farrar, Frankfurt, Strikman, and Liu (1988) and Frankfurt, Greenberg, Miller, and Strikman (1992) to the specific case of deuteron electrodisintegration. It proposes that the deuteron, as the simplest nuclear system with a well-known wave function (Brown and Jackson, 1976), provides a cleaner environment to observe CT than heavier nuclei, because the relevant propagation distances are short (∼1.8 fm). The paper enables experimental searches at CEBAF and HERMES by identifying specific kinematic conditions (perpendicular kinematics, α ≈ 1) where competing effects from meson exchange currents, isobar contributions, and relativistic corrections are suppressed to the few-percent level.
