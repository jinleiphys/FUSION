# Novel shape evolution in Sn isotopes from magic numbers 50 to 82

> A novel shape evolution in the Sn isotopes by the state-of-the-art application of the Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations is presented in a unified way for the 100-138Sn isotopes. A large model space consisting of eight single-particle orbits for protons and neutrons is taken with the fixed Hamiltonian and effective charges, where protons in the 1g9/2 orbital are fully activated. While the significant increase of the B(E2; 0+1 -> 2+1) value, seen around 110Sn as a function of neutron number (N), has remained a major puzzle over decades, it is explained as a consequence of the shape evolution driven by proton excitations from the 1g9/2 orbital. A second-order quantum phase transition is found around N=66, connecting the phase of such deformed shapes to the spherical pairing phase. The shape and shell evolutions are thus described, covering topics from the Gamow-Teller decay of 100Sn to the enhanced double magicity of 132Sn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing puzzle of the anomalous increase in B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) values around ¹¹⁰Sn is explained as a consequence of shape evolution driven by proton excitations from the 1g₉/₂ orbital, with a second-order quantum phase transition occurring around neutron number N=66 that connects a moderately deformed phase to a spherical pairing phase.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) calculations for even-N Sn isotopes from ¹⁰⁰Sn to ¹³⁸Sn using a large model space of eight single-particle orbits for both protons and neutrons, including the full sdg harmonic oscillator shell and the lower part of the next shell. A single fixed Hamiltonian was constructed from existing well-tested two-body matrix element sets (JUN45, SNBG3, V_MU) with minor adjustments, and effective charges (e_p, e_n) = (1.25e, 0.75e) were used throughout. The calculations were visualized using T-plots, which show the intrinsic quadrupole moments of deformed Slater determinant basis vectors weighted by their importance in the eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) values show a large bump around N=60 (¹¹⁰Sn), with the maximum calculated B(E2) occurring at ¹¹⁰Sn
- Effective charges used: (e_p, e_n) = (1.25e, 0.75e)
- The maximum model space dimension reaches 7.5 × 10²³, beyond 10¹² times the current limit of conventional shell model diagonalization
- The calculated B(GT) value for the Gamow-Teller decay of ¹⁰⁰Sn to ¹⁰⁰In is 9.2 (with quenching factor 0.70) or 10.3 (with quenching factor 0.74), compared to the experimental value 9.1⁺²·⁶₋₃·₀
- Calculated g-factors for ²⁺₁ states of ¹¹²⁻¹²⁴Sn range from 0.13 to -0.07
- The second-order quantum phase transition occurs around N=66, with a discontinuity in ΔS₂n (the second derivative of ground-state energy) between N=54-66 and N=70-80

## Context

This work resolves a decades-long puzzle where previous shell model calculations using only valence neutrons (such as the SNBG3 Hamiltonian) failed to reproduce the observed B(E2) bump around N=60, despite successfully reproducing 2⁺₁ excitation energies. The paper extends the approach of Togashi (2016) on Zr isotopes to the Sn isotopic chain, and contrasts the second-order quantum phase transition found here with the first-order quantum phase transition previously identified in Zr isotopes. The results enable future experimental studies of charge radii and neighboring nuclei, and provide constraints on specific two-body matrix elements around the critical point.
