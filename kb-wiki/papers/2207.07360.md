# SS-HORSE Extension of the No-Core Shell Model: Application to Resonances in $^7{\mathrm He}$

> Theoretical ab initio studies of resonances in the unbound ${\rm^{7}He}$ nucleus are presented. We perform no-core shell model calculations with $NN$ interactions Daejeon16 and JISP16 and utilize the SS-HORSE method to calculate the $S$ matrix for two-body channels $n{-}{\rm^{6}He}$ and $n{-}{\rm^{6}He^{*}}$ with ${\rm^{6}He}$ respectively in the ground and excited $2^{+}$ states as well as for the four-body democratic decay channel ${{\rm^{4}He}+n+n+n}$. The resonant energies and widths areobtained by numerical location of the $S$-matrix poles. We describe all experimentally known ${\rm^{7}He}$ resonances and suggest an interpretation of an observed wide resonance of unknown spin-parity.

## Key claim

The SS-HORSE method, when combined with no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using realistic NN interactions, can describe all experimentally known resonances in the unbound ⁷He nucleus and suggest an interpretation of a wide resonance of unknown spin-parity, though the results are sensitive to the choice of NN interaction (Daejeon16 vs. JISP16) and the resonance parameters carry significant uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations of ⁷He eigenenergies using the Daejeon16 and JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interactions, with basis sizes up to Nmax=16 for negative parity and Nmax=17 for positive parity states and oscillator frequencies ħΩ ranging from 10 to 50 MeV. They applied the SS-HORSE method to compute the S-matrix for two-body decay channels (n-⁶He and n-⁶He* with ⁶He in the 2+ excited state) and for the four-body democratic decay channel (⁴He+n+n+n), obtaining resonant energies and widths by numerically locating S-matrix poles in the complex energy plane. The effective range function Kℓ(E) was parameterized using Padé approximants fitted to selected NCSM eigenenergies from the three largest model spaces, with uncertainties estimated from the spread of results across different model space selections.

## Key numbers

* Low-lying 3/2⁻₁ resonance: energy Er = 0.28(4) MeV and width Γ = 0.13(2) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 0.665(12) MeV and Γ = 0.57(4) MeV with JISP16 (energies relative to n-⁶He threshold)
* 1/2⁻ resonance: Er = 2.7(4) MeV and Γ = 4.3(3) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 2.7(8) MeV and Γ = 5.0(6) MeV with JISP16
* 5/2⁻ resonance (combined prediction): Er = 3.4(4) MeV and Γ = 1.8(5) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 4.1(7) MeV and Γ = 2.0(7) MeV with JISP16
* 3/2⁻₂ resonance (combined prediction): Er = 4.7(7) MeV and Γ = 3.4(8) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 5.6(7) MeV and Γ = 4.0(7) MeV with JISP16
* 3/2⁺ resonance (combined prediction): Er = 3.9(6) MeV and Γ = 4.2(7) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 6.5(1.6) MeV and Γ = 5.9(1.0) MeV with JISP16
* 5/2⁺ resonance (combined prediction): Er = 3.7(7) MeV and Γ = 4.4(9) MeV with Daejeon16; Er = 6.7(1.5) MeV and Γ = 5.8(8) MeV with JISP16

## Context

This work extends the SS-HORSE-NCSM approach, previously applied to ⁵He and ⁵Li (Shirokov et al., 2015-2019) and to the tetraneutron (Shirokov et al., 2016), to the more challenging case of ⁷He which has no bound states and fragmentary experimental data. The results are compared with other ab initio and phenomenological approaches including NCSMC (Navratil et al., 2010-2016), NCSMch (Rodkin and Tchuvil'sky, 2020), GSM (Michel et al., 2002), G-DMRG (Rotureau et al., 2006), and CS-COSM (Myo et al., 2007), and the authors suggest that the wide resonance at 6.2±0.3 MeV with width 4±1 MeV of unknown spin-parity may correspond to a combination of the predicted 3/2⁺ and 5/2⁺ resonances. The method enables future studies of resonances in other unbound nuclei and can provide total widths (as opposed to partial widths from channel-projection methods) by locating common S-matrix poles across all decay channels.
