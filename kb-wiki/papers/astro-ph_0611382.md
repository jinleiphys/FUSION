# Nuclear reactions in hot stellar matter and nuclear surface deformation

> Cross-sections for capture reactions of charged particles in hot stellar matter turn out be increased by the quadrupole surface oscillations, if the corresponding phonon energies are of the order of the star temperature. The increase is studied in a model that combines barrier distribution induced by surface oscillations and tunneling. The capture of charged particles by nuclei with well-deformed ground-state is enhanced in stellar matter. It is found that the influence of quadrupole surface deformation on the nuclear reactions in stars grows, when mass and proton numbers in colliding nuclei increase.

## Key claim

Cross-sections for capture reactions of charged particles in hot stellar matter are increased by quadrupole surface oscillations, but only if the corresponding phonon energies are of the order of the star temperature; the enhancement is studied in a model that combines barrier distribution induced by surface oscillations and tunneling.

## Method

The author evaluates capture reaction cross-sections by summing contributions from the ground state and the first 2+ surface oscillation state of the target nucleus, weighted by their temperature-dependent occupation probabilities calculated via a statistical partition function. The transmission coefficients for barrier penetration are computed using the WKB approximation, with the deformed nuclear surface described by a harmonic oscillator model and averaging over both orientation angles and deformation parameter values. The interaction potential includes Coulomb, nuclear (Woods-Saxon), and centrifugal terms, with deformation incorporated into the nuclear radius and Coulomb field.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction α+⁵²Fe at 15 MeV collision energy, the spherical barrier height is 7.98 MeV; with deformation β=β_vib=0.308, the barrier ranges from 7.35 MeV (θ=0) to 8.30 MeV (θ=π/2).
- For the reaction α+⁸⁰Sr at 15 MeV collision energy, the spherical barrier height is 11.13 MeV; with deformation β=β_vib=0.404, the barrier ranges from 9.97 MeV (θ=0) to 11.70 MeV (θ=π/2).
- The 2+ vibrational state in ⁵²Fe has excitation energy ε_vib=0.849 MeV and vibrational amplitude β_vib=0.308; in ⁸⁰Sr, ε_vib=0.386 MeV and β_vib=0.404.
- The occupation probability of the 2+ state exceeds that of the ground state at temperatures kT > 0.53 MeV for ⁵²Fe and at kT > 0.24 MeV for ⁸⁰Sr.
- The 2+ state in ³²Mg has excitation energy ε_vib=0.8855 MeV and vibrational amplitude β_vib=0.473.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of charged-particle capture reactions in stellar matter by incorporating the effect of low-energy 2+ surface oscillations in soft nuclei, which had not been considered previously. Prior calculations for p- and α-capture on deformed nuclei typically used an effective spherical potential of equal volume, as in the works of Rauscher and Thielemann (2000) and others. The results enable more accurate evaluation of reaction rates during silicon and oxygen burning phases in massive stars, potentially affecting nucleosynthesis abundance calculations and the balance between direct and inverse reactions.
