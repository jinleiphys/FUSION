# The neutron polaron as a constraint on nuclear density functionals

> We study the energy of an impurity (polaron) that interacts strongly in a sea of fermions when the effective range of the impurity-fermion interaction becomes important, thereby mapping the Fermi polaron of condensed matter physics and ultracold atoms to strongly interacting neutrons. We present Quantum Monte Carlo results for this neutron polaron, and compare these with effective field theory calculations that also include contributions beyond the effective range. We show that state-of-the-art nuclear density functionals vary substantially and generally underestimate the neutron polaron energy. Our results thus provide constraints for adjusting the time-odd components of nuclear density functionals to better characterize polarized systems.

## Key claim
The neutron polaron energy provides a novel constraint on the time-odd components of nuclear density functionals, because state-of-the-art functionals vary substantially and generally underestimate this energy compared to Quantum Monte Carlo results, especially at low densities where the unitary limit applies.

## Method
The authors generalized the Fermi polaron concept from ultracold atoms to strongly interacting neutrons, where the effective range is significant. They performed Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Auxiliary-Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for a single spin-down neutron impurity in a sea of spin-up neutrons, using S-wave interactions from the Argonne v18 potential and a modified Poschl-Teller potential. They compared these results to effective field theory (dEFT) calculations that include effective-range contributions and to microscopic chiral EFT interactions at N3LO, and then evaluated the polaron energy predicted by various Skyrme and Gogny nuclear density functionals.

## Key numbers
- Neutron scattering length: a = -18.5 fm; effective range: r_e = 2.7 fm.
- At unitarity, the polaron energy scales as E_pol = eta E_F with eta = -0.607 (Chevy variational bound) and eta = -0.6158 (full many-body treatment).
- QMC polaron energy at k_F = 0.3 fm^-1: E_pol/E_F = -0.522 +/- 0.006 (GFMC) and -0.531 +/- 0.008 (AFDMC).
- QMC polaron energy at k_F = 0.4 fm^-1: E_pol/E_F = -0.589 +/- 0.005 (GFMC) and -0.567 +/- 0.006 (AFDMC).
- Effective mass of the polaron: m*/m = 1.04(3) for all densities considered.
- New UNEDF1-pol functional parameters: C_0^{s,0}+C_1^{s,0} = 48 +/- 24 MeV fm^3, C_0^{s,D}+C_1^{s,D} = 61 +/- 14 MeV fm^{3+delta}, delta = -0.288 +/- 0.026.

## Context
This work extends the Fermi polaron concept from condensed matter physics and ultracold atoms (Chevy 2006, Combescot 2007) to neutron matter, where the large effective range is important. It builds on previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of neutron matter (Gezerlis 2010, 2011) and chiral EFT calculations of neutron matter (Hebeler 2010, Tews 2013). The results provide constraints for adjusting the time-odd components of nuclear density functionals, such as the Skyrme family (SIII, SGII, SkM*, SLy4, SLy5, SkO, SkO', BSk9, SAMi) and Gogny D1N, which were previously fit to nuclear masses and radii but not to polarized systems. The new UNEDF1-pol functional enables better characterization of polarized systems and provides an anchor for the equation of state of the neutron-star crust.
