# Decay of the low-energy nuclear $^{229m}$Th isomer via atomic Rydberg states

> In the paper, a unique process of the decay of the $^{229m}$Th($3/2^+,8.28\pm 0.17$ eV) low energy nuclear isomer via the internal conversion (IC) channel on Rydberg states is considered for the first time. The Rydberg atom $^{229m}$Th$^{+}+e^-_{Ry}$ is a unique object where IC is possible exclusively on the Rydberg electron $e^-_{Ry}$. It is shown that in the $^{229m}$Th$^{4+}+e^-_{Ry}$ system a) IC on the electron states with relatively small values of the principal quantum number $n$ and the orbital moment $l$ is practically completely suppressed, b) IC probability, $W_{IC}$, on the $ψ_{ns_{1/2}}({\bf{r}})$ states is proportional to $|ψ_{ns_{1/2}}(0)|^2$ and can be related with the energy of the hyperfine interaction of the Rydberg electron with the nucleus, c) $W_{IC}$ decreases rapidly with the increase of $n$ in the range from 10 to 50, and in the range $n\gtrsim 150$ $W_{IC}$ changes as ${\text{Const}}/n^3$ typical for hydrogen-like ions, d) at $n\thickapprox 10$--30, $W_{IC}$ as a function of $l$ has a characteristic "knee" between $l=3$ and $l=4$, i.e. a three order of magnitude decrease of $W_{IC}$ due to the qualitative change in the ratio between the centrifugal and shielding potentials.

# Decay of the low-energy nuclear $^{229m}$Th isomer via atomic Rydberg states

## Key claim
The paper proposes that internal conversion (IC) on Rydberg electron states in the $^{229m}$Th$^{4+}+e^-_{Ry}$ system can be a viable decay channel for the $^{229m}$Th isomer, but only under specific conditions: IC is completely suppressed on states with small principal quantum number $n$ and orbital momentum $l$, while for $ns_{1/2}$ states the IC probability scales as $|ψ_{ns_{1/2}}(0)|^2$ and can be related to the hyperfine interaction energy of the Rydberg electron with the nucleus.

## Method
The author calculates internal conversion coefficients (ICC) for the $M1$ and $E2$ multipoles of the $^{229m}$Th($3/2^+,8.28$ eV) isomer decay on Rydberg states in the $^{229m}$Th$^{4+}+e^-_{Ry}$ system. Wave functions for Rydberg states are obtained by solving the Dirac equation in a potential that includes the nuclear charge distribution (uniform sphere) and the electron shell potential of Th$^{4+}$ derived from self-consistent DFT calculations. For states with large $l$ where the Rydberg electron does not penetrate the electron core, analytical Dirac bispinors for a point Coulomb potential with $Z_{ion}=4$ are used instead.

## Key numbers
- Isomer energy: $E_{is} = 8.28 \pm 0.17$ eV (from Seiferle (2019))
- For $n \gtrsim 150$, the $M1$ internal conversion coefficient scales as $\alpha_{M1}(ns_{1/2}) \sim \text{Const}/n^3$
- The proportionality constant for $ns_{1/2}$ states: $\alpha_{M1}(ns_{1/2})/g^2_{ns_{1/2}}(0) = c_1 = 6.03 \times 10^5$
- For the hydrogen-like Be$^{4+}+e^-_{Ry}$ model with $Z_{Be}=4$: $\alpha_{M1}(ns_{1/2}) = 1.03 \times 10^7 / n^3$ for $n \geq 10$
- The principal quantum number at which IC probability on $ns_{1/2}$ equals the $\gamma$-radiation probability: $n \simeq 3300$
- At $n \approx 10$–30, the IC probability drops by three orders of magnitude between $l=3$ and $l=4$ (the "knee" feature)
- Reduced nuclear transition probability ranges: $0.006 \leq B_{W.u.}(M1,3/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+) \leq 0.04$ and $6 \leq B_{W.u.}(E2,3/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+) \leq 29$ (from Tkalya (2015) and Bilous (2017))
- For the $np_{3/2}$ shells with average reduced probabilities: $W_{IC}(np_{3/2})/W_{IC}(ns_{1/2}) \approx 2.6$
- The Th$^{4+}$ electron core size is approximately $2a_B$
- The Th$^{4+}$ electron density satisfies $\int_0^{\infty}\rho_e(x)x^2dx = Z_{nucl} - Z_{ion} = 86$

## Context
This work extends the study of $^{229m}$Th decay channels beyond the previously considered internal conversion on valence shells (Strizhov (1991)) and excited atomic states (Bilous (2017)), and the electronic bridge mechanism (Strizhov (1991), Porsev (2010), Karpeshin (2017)). The paper enables experimental control of the $^{229m}$Th isomer lifetime by selecting Rydberg states with specific $n$ and $l$ quantum numbers, achievable with existing experimental techniques at $n=30$–40 and $l=4$–6. This could simplify measurement of the nuclear isomer energy and aid development of a nuclear time standard (Peik (2003), Campbell (2012)) and optical $\gamma$-ray laser concepts (Tkalya (2011)).
