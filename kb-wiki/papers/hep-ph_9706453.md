# Soft Modes associated with Chiral Transition at Finite Temperature

> Using a novel resummation procedure of thermal loops, real-time correlations in the scalar and pseudo-scalar channels are studied in the O(4) linear sigma model at finite temperature. A threshold enhancement of the spectral function in the scalar channel is shown to be a noticeable precritical phenomenon of the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

Using a resummation procedure that avoids tachyonic masses in the linear sigma model at finite temperature, the authors show that a threshold enhancement of the spectral function in the scalar (sigma) channel is a precritical phenomenon of the chiral phase transition, occurring even at relatively low temperatures due to the combined effect of partial chiral symmetry restoration and strong sigma-2pi coupling.

## Method

The authors adopt the O(4) linear sigma model with explicit symmetry breaking and develop a resummation procedure based on adding and subtracting a chiral-invariant mass term, following the approach of Banerjee and Mallik. They compute the retarded meson propagators and spectral functions in the scalar (sigma) and pseudo-scalar (pion) channels at finite temperature using a one-loop calculation in the real-time formalism, with the fictitious mass parameter m^2 chosen by a variant of the fastest apparent convergence condition.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at T=0 set to m_pi = 140 MeV; pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV
- Sigma peak position at T=0 chosen as 550 MeV (with alternative values 750 MeV and 1 GeV checked)
- Resultant renormalized parameters: mu^2 = (283 MeV)^2, lambda = 73.0, h = (123 MeV)^3, kappa = 255 MeV
- At T=0, the sigma peak has a total width of 260 MeV; for alternative peak positions of 750 MeV and 1 GeV, widths are 657 MeV and 995 MeV respectively
- Threshold enhancement in the sigma channel is observed for temperatures up to T = 145 MeV; above this temperature, simple poles without width appear
- For a smaller pion mass of 50 MeV (corresponding to m_q / m_q^phys. ~ 0.13), the chiral condensate as a function of temperature is also shown

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of soft modes at finite temperature by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and others, who used effective theories of QCD, by incorporating the strong coupling between scalar and pseudo-scalar channels which had not been seriously studied before. The resummation method builds on the approach of Banerjee and Mallik (1991) and reduces to hard thermal loop resummation at high temperature. The results enable future experimental searches for precritical chiral phenomena in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC through decays such as sigma to 2pi, 2gamma, or e+e-; the authors note that a calculation based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) suggests collective modes may survive as long as T/T_c is not far from unity.
