# New Half-life Limits on Double Beta Decays of Pd110 and Pd102 into Excited States

> A search for excited state transitions of double beta decay in Pd110 and double electron capture in Pd102 has been performed in the HADES underground laboratory with two low background HPGe detectors in sandwich configuration. After an exposure of 35.92 kg*d, no signal was found. The frequentist spectral analysis resulted in lower half-life limits of 1.98e20 yr and 1.72e20 yr (95% CL) for the first 0+ and 2+ excited state in Pd110 respectively. This is an improvement by more than a factor of 3 with respect to previous measurements. In Pd102, the lower half-life limit could be improved to 5.95e18 yr (95% CL) for the first 0+ excited state. Furthermore, first experimental lower half-live limits are found for all possible excited states in the Pd110 and Pd102 systems.

## Key claim

The paper reports improved lower half-life limits for double beta decay transitions of Pd110 and double electron capture transitions of Pd102 into excited states, but no signal was observed; the limits are derived from a frequentist spectral analysis at 95% confidence level and represent an improvement by more than a factor of 3 over previous measurements for Pd110, while for Pd102 first experimental limits are set for several excited state transitions.

## Method

A 802.35 g palladium sample was measured for 44.77 days (35.92 kg·d exposure) in the HADES underground laboratory using two low-background HPGe detectors in a sandwich configuration. The analysis employed a frequentist Feldman-Cousins method on the summed gamma-ray spectrum to extract upper limits on peak counts, with detection efficiencies determined from EGS4 Monte Carlo simulations that included coincidence summing corrections.

## Key numbers

- Lower half-life limit for Pd110 first 0+ excited state: 1.98e20 yr (95% CL)
- Lower half-life limit for Pd110 first 2+ excited state: 1.72e20 yr (95% CL)
- Lower half-life limit for Pd102 first 0+ excited state: 5.95e18 yr (95% CL)
- Total exposure: 35.92 kg·d from 44.77 days of live time
- Sample mass: 802.35 g of palladium with certified purity >99.95%
- Q-value of Pd110: 2017.85(64) keV (from Fink 2012)
- Q-value of Pd102: 1172 keV

## Context

This work extends the previous measurements by Lehnert (2011), which were the first to investigate excited states in palladium double beta decays, improving the half-life limits by more than a factor of 3 for Pd110. The results provide experimental constraints for theoretical calculations of nuclear matrix elements, including the single-state dominance hypothesis for two-neutrino double beta decay, and enable validation of models such as the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) calculations by Suhonen (2011) and the single-state dominance calculations by Domin (2005). The first experimental limits for the 2+2, 0+2, and 2+3 excited states in Pd110 and the 2+2 state in Pd102 are reported.
