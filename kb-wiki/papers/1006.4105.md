# Negative Differential Resistivity from Holography

> Negative differential resistivity (NDR) in a (3+1)-dimensional quantum system of strongly correlated charge carriers is theoretically reproduced by using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Our system is microscopically defined, and the analysis does not rely on any phenomenological models of NDR mechanism. The interaction between the heat reservoir and the nonequilibrium charge carriers is also taken into account. The system realizes insulation due to the formation of the neutral bound states of the charge carriers at low temperatures. However, the insulation is broken and the field-induced conductor phase appears by applying the external electric field greater than the critical value. We find that the NDR appears right above the critical electric field in this phase, and the pair creation of the charge carriers is crucial to realize the NDR. The present result suggests a possibility to observe a similar NDR in excitonic insulators or in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma, where the insulation originates in the formation of the neutral bound states.

## Key claim

Negative differential resistivity (NDR) is reproduced in a holographic model of a (3+1)-dimensional strongly correlated system without relying on any phenomenological NDR mechanism; the NDR appears only in a narrow window of current density right above the critical electric field for insulator-to-conductor transition, and the pair creation of charge carriers is essential for the effect.

## Method

The author employs the AdS/CFT correspondence (D3-D7 system) to model a strongly coupled SU(N_c) N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory with N_f fundamental hypermultiplets, taking the probe limit N_c >> N_f so that the gluonic sector acts as a fixed-temperature heat bath. The nonlinear current-voltage relation is obtained from the reality condition of the on-shell D7-brane action, which forces the electric field E and current density J to satisfy a specific relationship determined by the D7-brane embedding profile θ(z). The equation of motion for θ is solved numerically between the singular shell and the AdS boundary, yielding J as a function of E and the quark mass parameter m_q.

## Key numbers

* The critical electric field for insulator-to-conductor transition at m_q = 1.315 is E_c = 0.11 (in units where T = √2/π and z_H = 1).
* At m_q = 1.315, NDR appears in the current range J ≤ 0.0031 and is absent for E ≥ 0.19.
* The J-m_q curve at E = 0.2 extends until J = 0.288, and the width of the smaller-J (NDR) branch along the J axis is less than 2% of the full range.
* If the scale unit is meV, the critical electric field corresponds to E_c ~ 5 × 10^{-1} V/m and the current density at E = E_c is J ~ 1 × 10^{-4} mA/mm^2.
* The highest value of m_q approaches approximately 1.310 at the E → +0 limit, indicating E_c = 0 for m_q < 1.310.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to nonlinear conductivity pioneered by Karch and O'Bannon (2007) and the insulator-to-conductor transition studied by Erdmenger et al. (2007) and Albash et al. (2007). The results suggest that NDR may be observable in excitonic insulators or in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), where insulation arises from neutral bound state formation. The author notes that most prior theoretical studies of NDR rely on phenomenological models, except for some (quasi) one-dimensional systems studied by Ajisaka (2009), Beneti (2009), and others.
