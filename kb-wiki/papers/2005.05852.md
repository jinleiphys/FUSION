# Nuclear modification factors for jet fragmentation

> Using a recently-developed perturbative-QCD approach for jet evolution in a dense quark-gluon plasma, we study the nuclear modification factor for the jet fragmentation function. The qualitative behaviour that we find is in agreement with the respective experimental observations in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC: a pronounced nuclear enhancement at both ends of the spectrum. Our Monte Carlo simulations are supplemented with analytic estimates which clarify the physical interpretation of the results. The main source of theoretical uncertainty is the sensitivity of our calculations to a low-momentum cutoff which mimics confinement. To reduce this sensitivity, we propose a new observable, which describes the jet fragmentation into subjets and is infrared-and-collinear safe by construction. We present Monte Carlo predictions for the associated nuclear modification factor together with their physical interpretation.

## Key claim
The nuclear enhancement observed in the jet fragmentation function at both small and large longitudinal momentum fraction x in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can be reproduced qualitatively by a perturbative QCD approach, but the fragmentation function itself is not infrared-and-collinear safe, leading to large theoretical uncertainties from a low-momentum cutoff that mimics confinement; the authors therefore propose a new infrared-and-collinear-safe observable based on primary subjets.

## Method
The authors use a Monte Carlo implementation of a factorized perturbative QCD parton shower that separates vacuum-like emissions (VLEs) from medium-induced emissions (MIEs) via controlled weak-coupling approximations, with the medium treated as a uniform "brick" of length L with a fixed jet quenching parameter q̂. The parton cascade includes angular-ordered VLEs with a transverse momentum cutoff k⊥,min, BDMPS-Z multiple branchings for MIEs, and Gaussian transverse momentum broadening from elastic collisions; jets are reconstructed using the anti-k⊥ algorithm on the final partons.

## Key numbers
- Default medium parameters: q̂ = 1.5 GeV²/fm, L = 4 fm, ᾱ_s = 0.24, giving θ_c = 0.0408, ω_c = 60 GeV, ω_br = 3.456 GeV
- The initial parton spectrum scales as dN^hard/dp_T0 ∝ 1/p_T0^n with n ≥ 5
- The critical energy for medium-induced emissions is ω_c ≡ q̂L²/2
- The BDMPS-Z spectrum for soft emissions: ω dP_med/dω = (ᾱ_s N_c/π) √(2ω_c/ω) Θ(ω_c - ω)
- The infrared cutoff is set to k⊥,min = 0.25 GeV (central value)
- The angular cutoff for the vetoed region is θ_c ≡ 2/√(q̂L³)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previously developed pQCD approach (Caucal 2018, Caucal 2019) that successfully described the jet R_AA and z_g-distribution at the LHC, now applied to the jet fragmentation function. The results are qualitatively consistent with ATLAS measurements (Aaboud 2018) showing nuclear enhancement at both ends of the x spectrum, and the authors confirm the correlation between the large-x enhancement and the jet energy loss bias previously discussed by Spousta (2015) and Casalderrey-Solana (2018). The proposed subjet-based observable aims to overcome the infrared sensitivity that limits the fragmentation function as a theoretical tool, enabling more quantitative comparisons between theory and future LHC data.
