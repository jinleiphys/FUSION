# Spin asymmetry for the 16O(gamma,pi- p) reaction in the Delta(1232) region within an effective Lagrangian approach

> The spin asymmetry of the photon in the exclusive A(gamma,pi N)A-1 reaction is computed employing a recently developed fully relativistic model based on elementary pion production amplitudes that include a consistent treatment of the spin-3/2 nucleon resonances. We compare the results of this model to the only available data on Oxygen [Phys. Rev. C 61 (2000) 054609] and find that, contrary to other models, the predicted spin asymmetry compares well to the available experimental data in the Delta(1232) region. Our results indicate that no major medium modifications in the Delta(1232) properties are needed in order to describe the measured spin asymmetries.

## Key claim

The authors' relativistic model for the exclusive A(γ,πN)A-1 reaction, which includes a consistent treatment of spin-3/2 resonances and uses no medium modifications of the Δ(1232) properties, reproduces the measured spin asymmetry for 16O(γ,π− p) data from LEGS. The paper claims that no major medium modifications of the Δ(1232) are needed to describe the spin asymmetry, but cautions that this does not rule out in-medium effects in other observables.

## Method

The authors compute the spin asymmetry for the 16O(γ,π− p) reaction within a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), using a fully relativistic elementary pion photoproduction operator based on an effective Lagrangian approach. The model includes Born terms, vector-meson exchanges (ρ and ω), and all four-star nucleon resonances up to spin-3/2 from the PDG, with a consistent treatment of spin-3/2 interactions that avoids pathologies of earlier models. The bound nucleon wave functions are solutions of the Dirac equation from the σ-ω model (NLSH parameterization), and the calculation is fully unfactorized, including contributions from s1/2, p1/2, and p3/2 shells in 16O.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 290 to 325 MeV (incident photon energies at LEGS)
- Nucleon kinetic energy integration range: TN ∈ [50, 100] MeV
- Proton angles measured: 55° and 75°
- Pion angles: 36° to 140° in 8° steps
- Momentum transfer limit for quasifree region: below 300 MeV/c
- The model applies up to approximately 1.2 GeV of photon energy

## Context

This work extends the relativistic pion photoproduction model of Fernandez-Ramirez et al. (2006, 2007) from free nucleons to nuclei, and directly addresses the spin asymmetry data from Hicks et al. (2000) at LEGS. Prior calculations by Li, Wright, and Bennhold (1993) using harmonic oscillator wave functions systematically overestimated the measured spin asymmetries, leading to suggestions that Δ medium modifications were needed. The present model achieves better agreement without such modifications, enabling future studies of in-medium Δ effects in other observables where sensitivity may be higher.
