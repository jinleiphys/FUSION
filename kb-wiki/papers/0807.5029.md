# Realistic Shell-Model Calculations for Exotic Nuclei around Closed Shells

> We report on a study of neutron-rich nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion of the bare potential is renormalized by constructing a low-momentum potential, V-low-k, that is used directly as input for the calculation of the effective interaction. We present results for the four nuclei beyond the N=82 shell closure 134Sn, 134Sb, 136Sb, and 136Te. Comparison shows that our results are in very good agreement with the experimental data presently available for these exotic nuclei. We also present our predictions of the hitherto unknown spectrum of 136Sn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the low-momentum potential V-low-k approach reproduce available experimental data for neutron-rich nuclei beyond the N=82 shell closure, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the effective interaction contains no adjustable parameters and the results depend on the choice of effective charges for electromagnetic transitions.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations assuming 132Sn as a closed core, with valence protons occupying the five levels of the 50-82 shell and valence neutrons occupying the six levels of the 82-126 shell. The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized through the V-low-k procedure with a cutoff momentum of 2.2 fm^-1, using a folded-diagram method with the Q-box including diagrams up to second order in the two-body interaction. Single-particle energies were taken from experiment, and the oscillator parameter was set to hbar-omega = 7.88 MeV.

## Key numbers

- B(E2; 0+ -> 2_1+) in 134Sn: calculated value 0.033 e^2 b^2, experimental value 0.029(4) e^2 b^2 (effective neutron charge 0.70 e)
- B(E2; 6- -> 4-) in 136Sb: calculated value 131 e^2 fm^4, experimental value 170(40) e^2 fm^4 (effective proton charge 1.55 e, effective neutron charge 0.70 e)
- B(E2; 0+ -> 2+) in 136Te: calculated value 0.180 e^2 b^2, experimental value 0.103(15) e^2 b^2 from Radford (2002), with newer measurements about 20% and 50% higher
- Largest discrepancy for 134Sb multiplet: 7- state lies about 130 keV above its experimental counterpart
- Largest discrepancy for 136Sb: about 70 keV
- 134Sn and 136Sn: the three lowest calculated states (2+, 4+, 6+) lie at practically the same energy in both nuclei
- N/Z ratios: 134Sn 1.68, 134Sb 1.63, 136Sb 1.67, 136Te 1.61, 136Sn 1.72, compared to 124Sn at 1.48

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of nuclei beyond 132Sn (Coraggio 2005, Coraggio 2006, Covello 2007a, Covello 2007b, Simpson 2007) using the V-low-k approach as an alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method (Bogner 2002). The calculations reproduce experimental data for 134Sn, 134Sb, 136Sb, and 136Te, and provide predictions for the unknown spectrum of 136Sn, which may guide future experiments on these exotic nuclei with N/Z ratios up to 1.72.
