# Dragging Heavy Quarks in Quark Gluon Plasma at the Large Hadron Collider

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of charm and bottom quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma (QGP) have been evaluated for conditions relevant to nuclear collisions at Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The dead cone and Landau-Pomeronchuk-Migdal (LPM) effects on radiative energy loss of heavy quarks have been considered. Both radiative and collisional processes of energy loss are included in the {\it effective} drag and diffusion coefficients. With these effective transport coefficients we solve the Fokker Plank (FP) equation for the heavy quarks executing Brownian motion in the QGP. The solution of the FP equation has been used to evaluate the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{\mathrm AA}$ for the non-photonic single electron spectra resulting from the semi-leptonic decays of hadrons containing charm and bottom quarks. The effects of mass on $R_{\mathrm AA}$ has also been highlighted.

## Key claim
The authors evaluate effective drag and diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks that include both collisional and radiative energy loss (with dead cone and LPM effects), and use these to solve the Fokker-Planck equation; the resulting nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\mathrm AA}\) for non-photonic single electrons at LHC conditions shows substantial suppression, but the quantitative predictions depend on the assumed velocity of sound in the medium and the initial temperature.

## Method
The drag and diffusion coefficients for heavy quarks are computed by combining contributions from elastic scattering processes (\(gQ \rightarrow gQ\), \(qQ \rightarrow qQ\), \(\bar{q}Q \rightarrow \bar{q}Q\)) and from radiative energy loss, where the radiative loss includes both dead cone suppression and a separate implementation of the Landau-Pomeronchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect via a formation-time restriction. The effective transport coefficients are then used in a Fokker-Planck equation to evolve the heavy quark momentum distributions, starting from initial \(p_T\) distributions taken from the NLO MNR code. The resulting heavy quark spectra are fragmented using the Peterson function and decayed into non-photonic single electrons to compute \(R_{\mathrm AA}\).

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for LHC conditions: \(T_i = 700\ \mathrm{MeV}\); initial thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.08\ \mathrm{fm}/c\); these reproduce a predicted hadron multiplicity \(dN/dy = 2100\).
- Transition temperature: \(T_c = 170\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For RHIC comparisons: initial temperature \(T_i = 400\ \mathrm{MeV}\), initial thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.2\ \mathrm{fm}/c\), reproducing \(dN/dy = 1100\).
- The nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\mathrm AA}\) is evaluated for non-photonic single electrons from \(D\) and \(B\) meson decays as a function of \(p_T\); results show substantial depletion at high \(p_T\) for charm, with less suppression for bottom quarks.
- The ratio \(R_{\mathrm AA}^D / R_{\mathrm AA}^B\) is plotted as a function of \(p_T\) to illustrate mass effects.
- For RHIC data at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 200\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the model reproduces experimental data from STAR and PHENIX using \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{5}\).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark energy loss in QGP (e.g., Bjorken (1982), Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001), Wicks et al. (2007)) by simultaneously including both collisional and radiative processes with dead cone and LPM effects in a Fokker-Planck framework. The predictions for LHC conditions enable comparison with future measurements of non-photonic single electron suppression, and the successful description of RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX without enhancing the pQCD cross section (unlike the authors' previous work, Das et al. (2009)) demonstrates the importance of including both energy-loss mechanisms.
