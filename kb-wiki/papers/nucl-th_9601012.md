# Nuclear Forces and Chiral Theories

> Recent successes in {\it ab initio} calculations of light nuclei (A=2-6) will be reviewed and correlated with the dynamical consequences of chiral symmetry. The tractability of nuclear physics evinced by these results is evidence for that symmetry. The relative importance of three-nucleon forces, four-nucleon forces, multi-pion exchanges, and relativistic corrections will be discussed in the context of effective field theories and dimensional power counting. Isospin violation in the nuclear force will also be discussed in this context.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry, arising from QCD at the quark level, persists in nuclear physics described in terms of pions and nucleons and provides a dimensional power-counting scheme that makes many-nucleon forces small and nuclear physics tractable; the tractability of nuclear physics is itself evidence for that symmetry, though the tests are indirect and the quantitative sophistication of chiral perturbation theory does not yet approach that of traditional nuclear physics.

## Method

The paper reviews ab initio solutions of the Schrödinger equation for A=2-6 systems using Green's Function Monte Carlo techniques with a Hamiltonian containing a recent accurately fit NN force and a weak three-nucleon force adjusted to fit the triton binding energy, with no four-nucleon force included. The author then analyzes isospin violation in the nuclear force using the Nijmegen phase shift analysis (PSA) results for pion-nucleon coupling constants, interpreting them within van Kolck's effective-field-theory formalism that separates isospin violation into contributions from the u-d quark-mass difference, hard electromagnetic processes, and soft electromagnetic processes.

## Key numbers

- OPEP dominates the potential energy in light nuclei: ⟨Vπ⟩/⟨V⟩ ∼ 75%
- Nijmegen PSA determined pion masses: mπ± = 139.4(10) MeV, mπ0 = 135.6(13) MeV
- Baseline triton binding energy using local NN potentials: ∼ 7.62 MeV
- Calculated ground-state energies (theory vs. experiment, in MeV): ²H: -2.22 (both); ³H: -8.47(2) vs. -8.48; ⁴He: -28.3(1) vs. -28.3; ⁵He(3/2⁻): -26.5(2) vs. -27.2; ⁵He(1/2⁻): -25.7(2) vs. -25.8; ⁶Li(1⁺): -32.4(9) vs. -32.0
- Average potential energies: ⟨VNN⟩ ∼ 20 MeV/pair, ⟨V3Nf⟩ ∼ 1 MeV/triplet, ⟨V4Nf⟩ ≲ 0.1 MeV/quartet
- Reference value f²(d=1) = 0.0718(5) from eq. (3) using current gA and fπ
- Nijmegen coupling constants (np and pp data): f²π0pp = 0.0751(6), fπ0pp fπ0nn = 0.0752(8), f²πcnp = 0.0741(5)
- GT discrepancy d-1 = 1.6(5)% from combined np and pp data; 2.1(5)% from np only

- Dimensional-power-counting estimates: β1 ∼ 6·10⁻³, β̄10 ∼ 2·10⁻³

## Context

This invited talk at the 15th European Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics (1995) reviews the then-recent successes of ab initio calculations for A=2-6 systems, extending the work of the Nijmegen group's phase shift analysis (de Swart and collaborators) and the development of potentials like the Argonne V18 model. The paper places chiral perturbation theory (χPT) in the context of earlier work by Ordóñez et al. (who developed the first chiral force) and Ballot et al. (who showed how chiral symmetry arranges cancellations), and applies van Kolck's formalism for isospin violation to interpret the Nijmegen PSA results. The work enables systematic treatment of isospin violation in nuclear forces within an effective-field-theory framework and establishes that weak many-nucleon forces are essential for the tractability of nuclear physics calculations.
