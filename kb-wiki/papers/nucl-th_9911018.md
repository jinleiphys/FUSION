# Precision Calculation of n+p->d+gamma Cross Section for Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis

> An effective field theory calculation of the n+p->d+gamma cross section accurate to 1% for center of mass energy E up to ~1 MeV is presented. At these energies, which are relevant for big-bang nucleosynthesis, isovector magnetic transitions M1 and isovector electric transitions E1 give the dominant contributions. The M1 amplitude is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and the contribution from the associated four-nucleon-one-photon operator is determined from the cold neutron capture rate. The E1 amplitude is calculated up to NNNNLO. The four-nucleon-one-photon operator contribution to E1 is determined from the related deuteron photodisintegration reaction gamma+d->n+p.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory calculation of the n+p→d+γ cross section with a theoretical uncertainty estimated to be ≤1% for center-of-mass energies up to ~1 MeV, improving upon the previous 4% uncertainty from Chen and Savage (1999) by including higher-order terms in the perturbative expansion.

## Method

The authors used a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) without dynamical pions, where pions and heavier degrees of freedom are integrated out, and nucleon-nucleon interactions are described by four-nucleon local operators. The isovector magnetic M1_V amplitude was calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO), with the four-nucleon-one-photon operator contribution determined from the cold neutron capture rate σ_exp = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb. The isovector electric E1_V amplitude was calculated up to N^4LO, with the four-nucleon-one-photon operator contribution determined from a χ^2 fit to deuteron photodisintegration γ+d→n+p data.

## Key numbers

- Cold neutron capture cross section: σ_exp = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb at incident neutron speed v = 2200 m/s
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.2246 MeV, corresponding to binding momentum γ = √(M_N B)
- Effective range in ^3S_1 channel: ρ_d = 1.764 fm; shape parameter w_2 = 0.778 fm^3
- Singlet channel neutron-proton scattering length: a_exp = -23.75 fm; effective range r_0 = 2.73 fm
- D-wave to S-wave ratio at deuteron pole: η_d = 0.02534
- P-wave coupling combination: C_p = -1.49 fm^4, fixed from Nijmegen phase shift analysis
- Estimated L_E1 ≈ 2 fm^3; estimated L_np ≈ -9 fm^2; estimated L̃_np ≈ +3 fm^2
- Theoretical error estimate for E1_V: ~1% at E ~ 1 MeV; for M1_V: ~3% at E = 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory calculation of Chen and Savage (1999), which predicted a 4% theoretical uncertainty, by going one order higher in the perturbative expansion. The calculation improves upon earlier model-dependent results from Smith, Kawano and Malaney (SKM), who assigned a 5% error that may actually be as large as 10-15%, and the even earlier calculation by Fowler, Caughlan and Zimmerman (1967), which agrees at low energies but disagrees significantly at higher energies. The improved precision enables more accurate predictions of primordial deuterium abundance for big-bang nucleosynthesis, which is sensitive to the baryon density of the universe.
