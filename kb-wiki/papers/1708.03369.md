# Universal physics of the few-body system of two neutrons and one flavored meson

> We investigate the $s$-wave three-body system of two neutrons and one flavored meson with total spin-isospin $J=0,I=3/2$. The meson-neutron scattering length can become infinitely large in an unphysical region of the quark mass when extrapolated from strangeness to charm in the so-called zero coupling limit. Using a low-energy cluster effective field theory, we demonstrate that the Efimov effect is manifest in the three-body system when the meson-neutron scattering length is extrapolated to the unitary limit of the two-body interaction. We thereby discuss the consequence of universal physics in the physical $K^{-}nn$ and $D^{0}nn$ systems.

## Key claim

The Efimov effect is formally manifest in the three-body system of two neutrons and one flavored meson when the meson-neutron scattering length is extrapolated to the unitary limit, but this unitary limit occurs only in an unphysical region of the quark mass between strangeness and charm, and only when the couplings to sub-threshold decay channels are artificially switched off (the zero coupling limit).

## Method

The authors employed a low-energy cluster effective field theory (pionless EFT) to analyze the s-wave three-body system of two neutrons and one flavored meson (K- or D0) with total spin-isospin J=0, I=3/2. They performed an asymptotic analysis of the three-body integral equations to examine renormalization group limit cycle behavior and the Efimov effect, using a coupled-channel contact interaction model with flavor SU(4) symmetry to extrapolate from the strangeness sector to the charm sector by varying a linear parameter x from 0 (strangeness) to 1 (charm).

## Key numbers

- The K-n scattering length from the ETW model: a0,K-n = -0.135 - i0.410 fm
- The D0n scattering length from the coupled-channel model: a0,D0n = 0.764 - i0.615 fm
- In the zero coupling limit (ZCL), the K-n scattering length: a0,K-n(ZCL) = -0.394 fm
- In the zero coupling limit (ZCL), the D0n scattering length: a0,D0n(ZCL) = 4.141 fm
- The unitary limit (1/a0 = 0) is achieved at extrapolation parameter x = 0.615, corresponding to a flavored meson mass of m1(0.615) = 1336.61 MeV
- The Sigma_c(2800) resonance mass and width used to constrain the D0n interaction: M = 2800 MeV, Gamma = 72 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of universal few-body physics from three-nucleon systems (Braaten 2003, Konig 2016) and hyperon systems such as nnLambda (Ando 2015) to meson-nucleus systems with strange and charm quarks. The authors build on prior investigations of antikaon-nucleon systems (Hyodo 2011, Gal 2016) and heavy flavor meson interactions in nuclei (Hosaka 2016, Mizutani 2006), using the ETW model (Ikeda 2012) constrained by SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data. The work enables future lattice QCD simulations of universal physics at unphysical quark masses, as previously done for few-nucleon systems (Barnea 2013), and provides a framework for assessing the feasibility of bound K-nn or D0nn trimer formation.
