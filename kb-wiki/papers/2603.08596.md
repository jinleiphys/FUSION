# Radiative corrections to the nucleon isovector $g_V$ and $g_A$

> Electroweak, QCD, and QED radiative corrections to the nucleon low-energy coupling constants $g_V$ and $g_A$ are enhanced by large perturbative logarithms between the electroweak and hadronic scale, as well as between the hadronic scale and the low-energy MeV scale. Additionally, higher-order pion-mass splitting corrections to the nucleon axial-vector charge might be large. By consistently incorporating these effects, we provide an updated relation between the lattice-QCD and physical $g_A$, finding a total radiative correction of $3.5(2.1)\%$ ($5.6(0.7)\%$). This leads to an expected lattice-QCD result of $g^{\mathrm{QCD}}_A = 1.265(26)$ ($g^{\mathrm{QCD}}_A = 1.240(9)$) when based on a combination of lattice-QCD and data-driven (or only data-driven) inputs, respectively. Future phenomenological, chiral perturbation theory, and lattice-QCD studies can improve both the central value and the uncertainty of this estimate.

## Key claim
The paper provides an updated relation between lattice-QCD and physical values of the nucleon axial-vector coupling g_A by consistently incorporating electroweak, QCD, and QED radiative corrections enhanced by large logarithms across multiple scales, as well as higher-order pion-mass splitting effects. The total radiative correction is reported as 3.5(2.1)% when based on a combination of lattice-QCD and data-driven inputs, or 5.6(0.7)% when based on data-driven inputs alone, with the caveat that future studies in phenomenology, chiral perturbation theory, and lattice QCD can improve both the central value and uncertainty.

## Method
The authors computed electroweak, QCD, and QED radiative corrections to the nucleon isovector coupling constants g_V and g_A, accounting for large perturbative logarithms between the electroweak scale, the hadronic scale, and the low-energy MeV scale. They also incorporated higher-order corrections from pion-mass splitting effects on the nucleon axial-vector charge. The analysis combined lattice-QCD results with data-driven inputs to derive the total radiative correction and the corresponding lattice-QCD prediction for g_A.

## Key numbers
- Total radiative correction to g_A: 3.5(2.1)% when using a combination of lattice-QCD and data-driven inputs.
- Total radiative correction to g_A: 5.6(0.7)% when using only data-driven inputs.
- Expected lattice-QCD result: g^{QCD}_A = 1.265(26) based on combined lattice-QCD and data-driven inputs.
- Expected lattice-QCD result: g^{QCD}_A = 1.240(9) based on data-driven inputs only.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of radiative corrections to nucleon couplings by consistently including large logarithms across the electroweak, hadronic, and MeV scales, as well as pion-mass splitting effects that were previously neglected. The updated relation between lattice-QCD and physical g_A enables more precise comparisons between lattice calculations and experimental determinations of the nucleon axial charge, and it provides a target for future lattice-QCD studies and chiral perturbation theory analyses to refine the central value and reduce the uncertainty.
