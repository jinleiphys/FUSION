# High-precision covariant one-boson-exchange potentials for np scattering below 350 MeV

> Using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), we have found One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) potentials that fit the 2006 world np data below 350 MeV with a χ^2/N_data very close to 1, for a total of 3788 data. Our potentials have significantly fewer adjustable parameters than previous high-precision potentials, and they also reproduce the experimental triton binding energy without introducing additional irreducible three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The authors claim that, contrary to the common belief that the One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) mechanism is missing some important feature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, it is possible within the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) to derive realistic OBE potentials that fit the 2006 world np data below 350 MeV with a χ²/N_data very close to 1, using significantly fewer adjustable parameters than previous high-precision potentials.

## Method

The authors constructed two OBE potential models (WJC-1 and WJC-2) within the Covariant Spectator Theory, fitting the parameters directly to the 2006 world np scattering data below 350 MeV rather than to phase shifts. WJC-1 includes exchange of one isoscalar and one isovector meson in each of the pseudoscalar (η, π), scalar (σ₀, σ₁), vector (ω, ρ), and axial vector (h₁, a₁) boson pairs, with axial vector mesons treated as contact interactions, using 27 adjustable parameters; WJC-2 eliminates axial vector mesons and other degrees of freedom, leaving 15 adjustable parameters. Charge symmetry is broken by treating charged and neutral pions independently and adding a simplified one-photon-exchange interaction.

## Key numbers

- WJC-1 achieves χ²/N_data = 1.06 for the 2007 database (data prior to 2007), with 27 adjustable parameters, fitting 3788 total np data below 350 MeV.
- WJC-2 achieves χ²/N_data = 1.12 for the 2007 database, with only 15 adjustable parameters.
- WJC-1 reproduces the experimental triton binding energy at E_t = -8.48 MeV; WJC-2 gives E_t = -8.50 MeV, both without introducing additional irreducible three-nucleon forces.
- For comparison, the Nijmegen group's best pure OBE potential (Nijm93) reached χ²/N_data = 1.87 with 15 parameters, while their high-precision Nijm I potential achieved χ²/N_data = 1.03 with 41 parameters.

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory approach previously used by Gross (1992) and Stadler (1997), which had fitted potential parameters to Nijmegen or VPI phase shifts rather than directly to data. The authors show that their OBE potentials contradict the earlier conclusion (e.g., from the Argonne group's AV18 potential) that the OBE mechanism cannot provide a perfect fit to the data. The models enable further applications to electromagnetic interactions and systems with A > 2, and WJC-1 provides a new phase shift analysis updated for all data until 2006.
