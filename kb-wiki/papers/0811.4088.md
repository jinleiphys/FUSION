# Modifications of single-particle properties in nuclear matter induced by three-body forces

> Within the self-consistent Green's functions formalism, we study the effects of three-body forces on the in-medium spectral function, self-energy and effective mass of the nuclear matter constituents, analyzing the density and momentum dependence.

## Key claim
Three-body forces modify single-particle properties in nuclear matter by about 20-30%, with the largest changes at higher densities, but the direction of the effect depends on isospin asymmetry: spectral functions are broadened in symmetric matter and narrowed in pure neutron matter.

## Method
The authors used the self-consistent Green's functions formalism to compute the in-medium spectral function, self-energy, and effective mass for symmetric and pure neutron matter at zero temperature. Three-body forces were included via an effective two-body interaction added to the in-medium T-matrix, derived by closing an outgoing nucleon line with an ingoing one in the three-body diagram in all topologically different ways. The calculations used the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials implemented with the Urbana three-body force.

## Key numbers
- Ratios of single-particle quantities (with vs. without three-body forces) show changes of about 20-30% across the densities studied.
- Calculations were performed at three densities: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, 2ρ₀, and 3ρ₀.
- The ratio X³-body/X²-body approaches approximately 1 in the high-momentum region above the Fermi momentum.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent Green's functions calculations (Soma and Bozek, 2008) of macroscopic and microscopic properties of nuclear matter using CD-Bonn and Nijmegen potentials with Urbana three-body forces. The results confirm the necessity of three-body forces to describe saturation behavior correctly and provide input for estimating in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections relevant to heavy-ion reactions and neutron star physics. The different response in symmetric versus neutron matter highlights the isospin dependence of three-body force effects.
