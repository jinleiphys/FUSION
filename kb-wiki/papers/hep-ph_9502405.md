# Attractive Channel Skyrmions and the Deuteron

> The deuteron is described as a quantum state on a ten-dimensional manifold $M_{10}$ of Skyrme fields of degree two, which are obtained by calculating the holonomy of $SU(2)$ instantons. The manifold $M_{10}$ includes both toroidal configurations of minimal energy and configurations which are approximately the product of two Skyrmions in the most attractive relative orientation. The quantum Hamiltonian is of the form $-Δ+V$, where $Δ$ is the covariant Laplace operator on $M_{10}$ and $V$ is the potential which $M_{10}$ inherits from the Skyrme potential energy functional. Quantum states are complex-valued functions on the double cover of $M_{10}$ satisfying certain constraints. There is a unique bound state with the quantum numbers of the deuteron, and its binding energy is approximately 6 MeV. Some of the deuteron's electrostatic and magnetostatic properties are also calculated and compared with experiment.

## Key claim

The deuteron is described as a unique bound state on a ten-dimensional manifold $M_{10}$ of Skyrme fields of degree two, with a binding energy of approximately 6 MeV, but this result depends on the specific boundary condition that the radial wavefunction is finite and has zero derivative at the minimal separation parameter $\rho_0$, rather than vanishing at $\rho=0$ as assumed in prior work by Verbaarschot et al. (1990).

## Method

The authors constructed a one-parameter family of attractive channel Skyrme fields by computing the holonomy of $SU(2)$ instantons using the Jackiw-Nohl-Rebbi formula, with the Hartshorne circle and ellipse geometry fixed to minimize the Skyrme potential energy at each separation. They then quantized the system on the resulting ten-dimensional manifold $M_{10}$ (three translations, three rotations, three iso-rotations, and one separation parameter $\rho$) by deriving the quantum Hamiltonian $H = -\Delta + V$ from the Skyrme Lagrangian restricted to these fields, and solved the resulting radial Schrodinger equation on the interval $[\rho_0, \infty)$ with the boundary condition that the wavefunction is finite and its derivative vanishes at $\rho_0$.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the deuteron state: approximately 6 MeV (from abstract; section 7 reports "of order 5 MeV")
- Minimal separation parameter $\rho_0 = 1.71$ (in units where $2/eF_\pi = 0.755$ fm)
- Toroidal configuration energy: $1.19 \times 24\pi^2 = 282.0$ (in units where $F_\pi/4e = 5.58$ MeV)
- Twice the hedgehog Skyrmion mass: $2M = 294.4$ (in the same units)
- Hedgehog Skyrmion mass: $M = 1.243 \times 12\pi^2 = 147.2$ (in geometrical units)
- Nucleon mass prediction from instanton-generated hedgehog: $M = 153.1$ in geometrical units, corresponding to 854.1 MeV in physical units
- Conversion factors: $F_\pi/4e = 5.58$ MeV, $2/eF_\pi = 0.755$ fm, $\hbar = 46.8$ in geometrical units

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to nuclear binding pioneered by Skyrme (1961) and developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), and directly builds on the eight-dimensional quantization of Braaten and Carson (1988) which found a much larger binding energy. The authors correct the quantization scheme of Verbaarschot et al. (1990) who used a ten-dimensional family but required the wavefunction to vanish at $\rho=0$ and found no bound state; the different boundary condition here (finite wavefunction with zero derivative at $\rho_0$) yields a bound deuteron state. The results enable future work on the full twelve-dimensional manifold $M_{12}$ of two-Skyrmion fields, which should provide a more complete description of the two-nucleon system.
