# Non-perturbative Calculation of the Positronium Mass Spectrum in Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We report on recent improvements to our non-perturbative calculation of the positronium spectrum. Our Hamiltonian is a two-body effective interaction which incorporates one-photon exchange terms, but neglects fermion self-energy effects. This effective Hamiltonian is diagonalized numerically in a harmonic oscillator basis at strong coupling ($α=0.3$) to obtain the mass eigenvalues. We find that the mass spectrum compares favorably to the Bohr spectrum of non-relativistic quantum mechanics evaluated at this unphysical coupling.

# Positronium Mass Spectrum in Basis Light-Front Quantization

## Key claim
The positronium mass spectrum computed non-perturbatively via diagonalization of a two-body effective Hamiltonian in Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) at the unphysical coupling α=0.3 agrees well with the non-relativistic Bohr spectrum including fine-structure corrections, but only after dropping a divergent term in the effective interaction that would otherwise prevent convergence with respect to the transverse basis truncation.

## Method
The authors truncate the Fock space to the |e+e−⟩ and |e+e−γ⟩ sectors, then derive a two-body effective interaction acting only in the |e+e−⟩ sector using the Bloch form of the effective Hamiltonian, incorporating one-photon exchange while neglecting fermion self-energy effects. They diagonalize this effective Hamiltonian numerically in a basis that combines a 2D harmonic oscillator basis for transverse degrees of freedom with discretized longitudinal momentum via a box of length L, using the Nmax truncation scheme. The divergent term in the effective interaction that produces a Dirac delta potential in coordinate space is dropped to achieve convergent results, and the fictitious photon mass μ used to regulate the Coulomb singularity is extrapolated to zero.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant: α = 0.3 (unphysical strong coupling chosen to make binding energy and hyperfine splitting easier to resolve numerically)
- Optimal basis energy scale for ground state: b = 0.4 mf (determined variationally at K = Nmax = 25)
- Ground state (1¹S₀) and 1³S₁ state masses at μ→0 limit: nearly identical to DLCQ results of 1.97376 mf and 1.97708 mf respectively from Pauli and Wolz (1993)
- Energies change by approximately 0.001 mf over the range Nmax = 19 to Nmax = 50, indicating convergence
- Hyperfine splitting between 1¹S₀ and 1³S₁ states: approximately 1% of the binding energy at K = Nmax = 19, b = μ = 0.1 mf
- Fictitious photon mass used for convergence studies: μ = 0.04 mf

## Context
This work extends previous BLFQ calculations by Wiecki et al. (2014) on the positronium mass spectrum, using the same non-perturbative Hamiltonian approach that combines light-front dynamics with methods from the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) developed by Navratil et al. (2000). The results are nearly identical to those obtained by Pauli and Wolz (1993) using Discretized Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ) with the same physics assumptions. The authors note that previous studies by Pauli and Wolz (1993), Trittmann (1997), and Karmanov (1996) identified a dependence on the ultraviolet cutoff arising from a term that tends to a non-zero constant at large momentum transfer, which the present work handles by dropping that term. This enables future extension to heavy quarkonia systems by adding a confining quadratic potential motivated by the soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Brodsky et al. (2006).
