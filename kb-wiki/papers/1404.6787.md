# Effective Chiral Symmetry Restoration for Heavy-Light Mesons

> We study the spectrum of heavy-light mesons within a model with linear instantaneous confining potential. The single-quark Green function and spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry are obtained from the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation. For the meson spectrum we derive a Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE). We solve thiss equation numerically in the heavy-light limit and obtain effective restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries at large spins.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a model with a linear instantaneous confining potential, heavy-light mesons exhibit effective restoration of chiral and \(U(1)_A\) symmetries at large angular momenta, even though the chiral order parameter (quark condensate) remains non-zero in the vacuum.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation for the single-quark Green function to obtain spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, then derive and numerically solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for meson bound states in the heavy-light limit (\(m_1 = 0\), \(m_2 \rightarrow \infty\)). The BSE is diagonalized to exhibit heavy-quark spin symmetry, and the infrared divergences inherent in the linear confining potential are shown to cancel exactly in the BSE, yielding finite binding energies and wave functions.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate: \(\langle \bar q q \rangle = -0.012\, \sigma^{3/2}\), where \(\sigma\) is the string tension.
* Ground-state binding energy (in units of \(\sqrt{\sigma}\)) for the \(\{0^-,1^-\}\) heavy-spin multiplet: 1.84.
* Ground-state binding energy for the \(\{0^+,1^+\}\) multiplet: 2.04.
* Energy gap between opposite-parity chiral partners for the \(\{0,1\}\) spin multiplet (ground state): 0.20 (in units of \(\sqrt{\sigma}\)).
* Energy gap for the \(\{1,2\}\) spin multiplet (ground state): 0.02.
* Energy gap for the \(\{2,3\}\) spin multiplet (ground state): 0.01.
* Energy gap for the \(\{3,4\}\) and \(\{4,5\}\) spin multiplets (ground states): 0.00.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of effective chiral symmetry restoration in the chirally symmetric confined model, previously shown analytically and numerically for quadratic potentials by Kalashnikova (2005) and for light-light systems with linear potentials by Wagenbrunn et al. (2010). The results confirm that the phenomenon persists for the phenomenologically more realistic linear confining potential in the heavy-light sector, and the paper provides explicit numerical evidence that parity-doublet degeneracy emerges at high angular momenta, consistent with the predictions of Glozman (2007) and lattice simulations by the Mario collaboration. The findings enable further exploration of missing hadronic states and the role of chiral symmetry in excited hadron spectra.
