# Threshold photoproduction of neutral pions off protons in nuclear model with explicit mesons

> We apply the nuclear model with explicit mesons to photoproduction of neutral pions off protons at the threshold. In this model the nucleons do not interact with each other via a potential but rather emit and absorb mesons that are treated explicitly on equal footing with the nucleons. We calculate the total cross section of the reaction for energies close to threshold and compare the calculations with available experimental data. We show that the model is able to reproduce the experimental data and determine the range of the parameters where the model is compatible with the experiment.

## Key claim

The nuclear model with explicit mesons, where nucleons interact only by emitting and absorbing mesons rather than via a potential, can quantitatively reproduce the experimental total cross section for neutral pion photoproduction off protons near threshold, but only for a relatively broad range of the two model parameters (pion-nucleon coupling range and strength), with the best fit around \(b_w = 3.9\) fm and \(S_w = 41.5\) MeV.

## Method

The authors apply the one-pion approximation to dress a bare proton with a virtual pion cloud, solving the coupled Schrödinger equations for the two-component wavefunction (bare proton and bare nucleon plus one pion) using a Gaussian basis expansion and a Gaussian form factor for the pion-nucleon coupling. They then compute the total cross section for the \(\gamma p \to p \pi^0\) reaction via Fermi's golden rule, using the electromagnetic interaction operator in the radiation gauge without multipole expansion, and compare the calculated cross section as a function of photon energy to experimental data from Bergstrom, Fuchs, and Schmidt.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit parameters are \(b_w = 3.9\) fm (range parameter) and \(S_w = 41.5\) MeV (strength parameter).
- For these parameters, the relative weight of the \(\pi^0\) component in the dressed proton wavefunction is about 25%.
- The virtual pions contribute approximately \(-586\) MeV to the mass of the dressed proton.
- The model parameters are compatible with experiment for \(b_w\) in the range from 3.7 fm to 4.0 fm, with corresponding strength \(S_w\) adjusted to fit.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear model with explicit mesons previously introduced by Fedorov and Mikkelsen (2022) for the deuteron bound state, applying it for the first time to pion photoproduction. The model aims to reduce the number of free parameters compared to conventional phenomenological potential models, using only two parameters per meson (range and coupling strength). The results enable future tests of the model by calculating the deuteron with the found parameters, and suggest that two-pion effects may be non-negligible given the large virtual pion contribution to the proton mass.
