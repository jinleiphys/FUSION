# Kinematic and dynamical origins of mean-$p_T$ fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the mean transverse momentum (mean-$p_T$) provide a sensitive probe of collective dynamics beyond single-particle spectra and anisotropic flow. We present a systematic study of mean-$p_T$ fluctuation observables using a Bayesian-calibrated multistage hydrodynamic framework, including quantitative comparisons to RHIC measurements and model-based investigations of beam-energy and kinematic-acceptance effects. The experimental definitions employed by the STAR and ALICE Collaborations are implemented explicitly and found to yield consistent results within controlled limits. We study the centrality and beam-energy dependence of the observable, its sensitivity to key soft-sector ingredients, and the impact of the kinematic $p_T$ acceptance. By introducing scaled-$p_T$ cuts, we demonstrate that a part of the apparent energy dependence arises from kinematic projection effects, while the remaining trends reflect genuine collective dynamics. Our results establish mean-$p_T$ fluctuations as a nontrivial and independent validation of calibrated hydrodynamic descriptions of the quark--gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a substantial part of the apparent beam-energy dependence of mean-\(p_T\) fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions arises from kinematic projection effects due to fixed absolute \(p_T\) cuts, rather than solely from genuine changes in collective dynamics; the remaining trends after accounting for these effects still reflect genuine collective dynamics.

## Method

The study uses a Bayesian-calibrated multistage hydrodynamic framework (JETSCAPE v3.7 with TRENTo initial conditions, MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics, and SMASH hadronic afterburner) to compute the mean-\(p_T\) fluctuation observable \(R_{p_T} = \sqrt{C_m}/\langle p_T \rangle\). The experimental definitions used by the STAR and ALICE Collaborations are implemented explicitly at the particle level, and the model calculations are compared to RHIC measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. The authors introduce a scaled-\(p_T\) cut prescription anchored to the STAR acceptance at 200 GeV to disentangle kinematic projection effects from genuine dynamical trends when comparing results across beam energies.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the model systematically underestimates the measured fluctuation magnitude when using the experimental lower \(p_T\) cut of 0.2 GeV; lowering \(p_T^{\min}\) to 0.15 GeV improves agreement with STAR data.
- The scaled-cut prescription yields energy-dependent momentum windows: \(0.226 < p_T < 2.264\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV and \(0.236 < p_T < 2.360\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, anchored to the STAR acceptance of \(0.2 < p_T < 2\) GeV at 200 GeV.
- Reducing the nucleon size from \(w=1.12\) fm to 0.8 fm yields the largest enhancement of \(R_{p_T}\) among model variations, with a robust increase across both 30-40% and 50-60% centrality classes.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian-calibrated hydrodynamic modeling approach of JETSCAPE (2020) and the recent study of radial flow fluctuations \(v_0(p_T)\) by Du (2025) to the mean-\(p_T\) fluctuation observable. It provides quantitative comparisons to STAR measurements (2019) and addresses the challenge that different experiments (STAR and ALICE) use distinct definitions and kinematic acceptances. The results establish mean-\(p_T\) fluctuations as an independent validation tool for calibrated hydrodynamic descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma, and the scaled-cut methodology enables future studies of RHIC Beam Energy Scan data to separate kinematic from dynamical effects.
