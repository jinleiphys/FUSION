# Spin Physics through QCD Instantons

> We review some aspects of spin physics where QCD instantons play an important role. In particular, their large contributions in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and polarized proton on proton scattering. We also review their possible contribution in the $\mathcal{P}$-odd pion azimuthal charge correlations in peripheral $AA$ scattering at collider energies.

## Key claim

QCD instantons can produce large spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and polarized proton-proton scattering, and may contribute to P-odd pion azimuthal charge correlations in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, but these effects depend on the instanton liquid model parameters and the dilute instanton approximation.

## Method

The authors review the role of QCD instantons in spin physics by calculating single-spin asymmetries (SSA) in semi-inclusive DIS and polarized pp scattering using the instanton liquid model, where the vacuum is populated by semi-classical interacting instantons and anti-instantons. They compute the non-perturbative contributions to the hadronic tensor and scattering amplitudes through one-instanton and two-instanton vertices, deriving the Pauli form factor and chirality-flip effective vertex. The results are compared to experimental data from HERMES, STAR, and PHENIX collaborations, and predictions are made for double-spin asymmetries and P-odd effects.

## Key numbers

- Instanton size in the vacuum: ρ ≈ 1/3 fm
- Instanton density in the vacuum: n ≈ 1 fm⁻⁴
- Induced magnetic moment from instantons: μ_D ≈ n ρ⁴, compared to perturbative QCD magnetic moment μ_PT ≈ g_s
- Constituent quark mass in the instanton vacuum: m_q* ≈ 300 MeV
- Effective gluon mass in the instanton vacuum: m_g ≈ 420 MeV
- Typical near-mode parameter: λ* ≈ 1/(0.2 GeV)³
- Magnetic field in peripheral AA collisions: eB/m_π² ≈ 1 at RHIC and eB/m_π² ≈ 15 at LHC
- Comparison of chromo-magnetic to electro-magnetic field strength: m_π² ρ_c² ≈ 0.004 ≪ 1

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Kochelev (1999) and Dorokhov (2009) on instanton contributions to spin asymmetries, and builds on the instanton liquid model developed by Schafer (1996) and Nowak (1996). The paper shows that instanton-induced effects can reproduce the magnitude of observed single-spin asymmetries in polarized pp scattering measured by STAR and PHENIX, and provides predictions for double-spin asymmetries and P-odd charge correlations that could be tested at RHIC and LHC. The work also connects to lattice investigations by Giordano (2009, 2011) showing instanton contributions to gluon saturation.
