# The Evolution of Dynamical Screening in the Parton Cacade Model

> We determine the time evolution of the colour screening mass in high energy nuclear collisions, as provided by the parton cascade model. Using our result, we discuss the onset of deconfinement and the onset of quarkonium suppression in a general, not necessarily equilibrated environment of strongly interacting constituents.

## Key claim

The time evolution of the colour screening mass in high-energy nuclear collisions, calculated using the parton cascade model, suggests that the onset of deconfinement and quarkonium suppression depends on collision energy and system size; however, the applicability of the equilibrium critical screening mass from lattice QCD to the non-equilibrium parton cascade environment is not obvious and requires further validation.

## Method

The authors used the parton cascade model (VNI Monte Carlo simulation) to obtain the phase-space distribution of partons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions. From this distribution, they calculated the colour screening mass in the one-loop approximation, assuming the non-equilibrium formula holds for the early-stage partonic matter. They then compared the resulting screening mass as a function of proper time and transverse distance to the critical screening mass for deconfinement (0.4–0.6 GeV from lattice QCD) and to the dissociation screening masses for various quarkonium states from potential theory.

## Key numbers

* For central S–S collisions at SPS energy, the screening mass is always less than 0.25 GeV, well below the deconfinement point of about 0.5 GeV.
* For central Pb–Pb collisions at SPS, a hot central region at early times reaches screening masses around the critical value of 0.4–0.6 GeV.
* For Au–Au collisions at RHIC, the screening mass increases by more than a factor of three compared to SPS, so essentially the entire collision volume falls into the deconfined region.
* The critical screening masses for quarkonium dissociation from potential theory are: J/ψ at 0.70 GeV, χc at 0.34 GeV, Υ at 1.57 GeV, Υ′ at 0.67 GeV, and χb at 0.56 GeV.
* The hard-soft division scale is p0(√s) = 0.5 (√s)^0.27, and the soft interaction parameter β is taken as 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model of Geiger and Muller (1992) to study deconfinement dynamically, rather than assuming thermal equilibrium. The results agree with earlier percolation-based approaches by Armesto (1996) and Satz (1998) and with string percolation models. The approach enables future studies of charmonium and bottonium suppression in evolving media, and the authors suggest that solving the bound-state problem with time-dependent screening would provide more quantitative estimates of quarkonium dissociation.
