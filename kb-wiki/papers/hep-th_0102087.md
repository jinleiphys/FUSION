# Centers of Mass and Rotational Kinematics for the Relativistic N-Body Problem in the Rest-Frame Instant Form

> In the Wigner-covariant rest-frame instant form of dynamics it is possible to develop a relativistic kinematics for the N-body problem. The Wigner hyperplanes define the intrinsic rest frame and realize the separation of the center-of-mass. Three notions of {\it external} relativistic center of mass can be defined only in terms of the {\it external} Poincaré group realization. Inside the Wigner hyperplane, an {\it internal} unfaithful realization of the Poincaré group is defined. The three concepts of {\it internal} center of mass weakly {\it coincide} and are eliminated by the rest-frame conditions. An adapted canonical basis of relative variables is found. The invariant mass is the Hamiltonian for the relative motions. In this framework we can introduce the same {\it dynamical body frames}, {\it orientation-shape} variables, {\it spin frame} and {\it canonical spin bases} for the rotational kinematics developed for the non-relativistic N-body problem.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the Wigner-covariant rest-frame instant form of dynamics, a complete relativistic kinematics for the N-body problem can be developed that resolves longstanding issues with relativistic center-of-mass definitions, but only for configurations with timelike total 4-momentum and only after gauge-fixing the internal center of mass to the origin of the Wigner hyperplane, which makes the worldline of the origin coincide with the Fokker-Pryce 4-center of inertia.

## Method

The authors start from parametrized Minkowski theory for N free scalar positive-energy particles, restrict the foliation to spacelike hyperplanes orthogonal to the conserved total 4-momentum (Wigner hyperplanes), and impose the rest-frame conditions (vanishing internal 3-momentum). They then separate the external 4-center of mass (a non-covariant canonical variable) from the internal particle variables, construct an adapted canonical basis of relative variables using the classical counterpart of the Gartenhaus-Schwartz transformation, and apply the Hamiltonian methods for constructing canonical spin bases and dynamical body frames previously developed for the non-relativistic N-body problem.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass of the N-body configuration is given by \( M_{sys} = \sum_{i=1}^N \sqrt{m_i^2 + \vec{\kappa}_i^2} \), where \( \vec{\kappa}_i \) are the internal particle momenta.
- The rest-frame conditions impose \( \vec{\kappa}_{+} = \sum_{i=1}^N \vec{\kappa}_i \approx 0 \), reducing the 6N internal canonical variables to 6N-3.
- For N=2 with equal masses \( m_1 = m_2 = m \), the Lagrangian takes the closed form \( L = -\epsilon m \sqrt{4 - \dot{\vec{\rho}}^2} \), where \( \vec{\rho} \) is the relative coordinate.
- The spin tensor inside the Wigner hyperplane is \( \bar{S}^{rs}_s \equiv \sum_{i=1}^N (\eta_i^r \kappa_i^s - \eta_i^s \kappa_i^r) \), and the total spin vector is \( \vec{\bar{S}} = \sum_{i=1}^N \vec{\eta}_i \times \vec{\kappa}_i \approx \sum_{a=1}^{N-1} \vec{\rho}_a \times \vec{\pi}_a \).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of rotational kinematics using orientation-shape SO(3) principal bundles (Littlejohn and Reinsch, 1997) and canonical spin bases (Alba, Lusanna, and Pauri, 2000) to the relativistic domain. It resolves the long-standing problem of defining relativistic centers of mass, clarifying the relationships among the canonical non-covariant center of mass, the Moller center of energy, and the Fokker-Pryce center of inertia. The framework enables the introduction of mutual action-at-a-distance interactions among relativistic particles and provides a classical basis for the Tomonaga-Schwinger formulation of quantum field theory.
