# $Z_c(3900)$: what has been really seen?

> The $Z^\pm_c(3900)/Z^\pm_c(3885)$ resonant structure has been experimentally observed in the $Y(4260) \to J/ψππ$ and $Y(4260) \to \bar{D}^\ast D π$ decays. This structure is intriguing since it is a prominent candidate of an exotic hadron. Yet, its nature is unclear so far. In this work, we simultaneously describe the $\bar{D}^\ast D$ and $J/ψπ$ invariant mass distributions in which the $Z_c$ peak is seen using amplitudes with exact unitarity. Two different scenarios are statistically acceptable, where the origin of the $Z_c$ state is different. They correspond to using energy dependent or independent $\bar D^* D$ $S$-wave interaction. In the first one, the $Z_c$ peak is due to a resonance with a mass around the $D\bar D^*$ threshold. In the second one, the $Z_c$ peak is produced by a virtual state which must have a hadronic molecular nature. In both cases the two observations, $Z^\pm_c(3900)$ and $Z^\pm_c(3885)$, are shown to have the same common origin, and a $\bar D^* D$ bound state solution is not allowed. Precise measurements of the line shapes around the $D\bar D^*$ threshold are called for in order to understand the nature of this state.

## Key claim
The $Z_c(3900)/Z_c(3885)$ structure seen in $Y(4260)$ decays can be described by two statistically acceptable scenarios: either as a genuine resonance with a mass near the $D\bar D^*$ threshold, or as a virtual state of hadronic molecular nature; the available data cannot fully discriminate between these two possibilities.

## Method
The authors performed a coupled-channel analysis of the $\bar D^* D$ and $J/\psi\pi$ channels with exact unitarity, using a $T$-matrix formalism with Gaussian regulators. They simultaneously fitted the $J/\psi\pi^-$ and $\bar D^{*-}D^0$ invariant mass distributions from BESIII data for $Y(4260)$ decays, considering both energy-dependent and energy-independent $\bar D^* D$ $S$-wave interactions. The $Y(4260)$ was treated as a $D_1(2420)\bar D$ bound state, and the $Z_c$ structure emerged from the $\bar D^* D$ interaction with coupling to $J/\psi\pi$ through intermediate states.

## Key numbers
- For the resonance scenario with $\Lambda_2=1.0$ GeV: $M_{Z_c} = 3894 \pm 6 \pm 1$ MeV, $\Gamma_{Z_c}/2 = 30 \pm 12 \pm 6$ MeV (first error statistical, second systematic)
- For the resonance scenario with $\Lambda_2=0.5$ GeV: $M_{Z_c} = 3886 \pm 4 \pm 1$ MeV, $\Gamma_{Z_c}/2 = 22 \pm 6 \pm 4$ MeV
- For the virtual state scenario with $\Lambda_2=1.0$ GeV: pole at $3831 \pm 26^{+7}_{-28}$ MeV (below $D\bar D^*$ threshold)
- For the virtual state scenario with $\Lambda_2=0.5$ GeV: pole at $3844 \pm 19^{+12}_{-21}$ MeV
- Ratio of partial widths $R_\text{th} = \Gamma(Z_c \to D\bar D^*)/\Gamma(Z_c \to J/\psi\pi)$: $6.0 \pm 3.5 \pm 0.5$ for $\Lambda_2=1.0$ GeV resonance fit
- Reduced $\chi^2/\text{dof}$ values: $1.02$ (resonance, $\Lambda_2=1.0$ GeV), $1.09$ (resonance, $\Lambda_2=0.5$ GeV), $1.31$ (virtual, $\Lambda_2=1.0$ GeV), $1.36$ (virtual, $\Lambda_2=0.5$ GeV)

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of Guo (2014) by using fully resummed and unitarized amplitudes rather than one-loop calculations, and provides the first simultaneous description of both $J/\psi\pi$ and $\bar D^* D$ spectra from BESIII. The results contradict interpretations of the $Z_c$ as a simple kinematical cusp effect (ruled out by Guo 2014) and show that a $\bar D^* D$ bound state solution is not allowed. The analysis calls for precise measurements of line shapes around the $D\bar D^*$ threshold at BESIII to distinguish between the resonance and virtual state scenarios, which would determine whether the $Z_c$ can be a tetraquark or must be a hadronic molecule.
