# The Multifragmentation Freeze--Out Volume in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The reduced velocity correlation function for fragments from the reaction Fe + Au at 100 A~MeV bombarding energy is investigated using the dynamical--statistical approach QMD+SMM and compared to experimental data to extract the Freeze--Out volume assuming simultaneous multifragmentation.

# The Multifragmentation Freeze-Out Volume in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The reduced velocity correlation functions for fragments from the reaction Fe + Au at 100 A MeV are consistent with a simultaneous multifragmentation break-up at a freeze-out density of 0.1 to 0.3 times normal nuclear matter density, but the data show less pronounced recoil correlations from a heavy third fragment than the QMD+SMM calculations predict, suggesting the actual break-up pattern is more symmetric than the model describes.

## Method
The authors used a two-step dynamical-statistical approach: first, the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model simulated the initial reaction stage for central collisions (impact parameter 0-6 fm) of Fe (100 A MeV) on Au; second, the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) described the simultaneous break-up of excited pre-fragments into nucleons and fragments within a variable freeze-out volume V = V0(1+κ), where κ is a model parameter. The reduced velocity correlation function 1+R(v_red) was calculated from the ratio of true correlated fragment pairs to event-mixed background pairs, and compared to experimental data for three charge-product bins (Z1·Z2 = 25-64, 65-129, and 130-250) with the smallest detected charge Z=5.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out density extracted: ρ = 0.1 – 0.3 ρ0, corresponding to volume parameter κ between 2 and 10 (where V = V0(1+κ))
- Volume parameter values tested: κ = 1, 2, 5, 10, and 15; results with κ=1 and κ=15 clearly disagreed with data for heavier fragments
- Bombarding energy: 100 A MeV for the Fe projectile on Au target
- Impact parameter range for central collisions: b = 0 – 6 fm
- Emission time scale from sequential model comparison: less than 500 fm/c, comparable to the time a fragment needs to traverse a freeze-out volume of roughly 5 times the nuclear volume
- Average IMF multiplicity (Z=4-20) decreased by a factor of two when κ was reduced from 15 to 1

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts by Sangster et al. (1992) and Lacey et al. to extract emission time scales using sequential decay models with fixed freeze-out volumes, and contrasts with the Koonin-Pratt formalism used by Kim et al. The results are consistent with previous investigations using the sequential three-body trajectory code MENEKA (Sangster et al., 1993), which found emission times of order 500 fm/c for a fixed freeze-out volume equivalent to κ=1. The findings enable future studies to distinguish between simultaneous and sequential multifragmentation mechanisms, and suggest that smaller fragments may be emitted earlier from a smaller volume rather than from a single freeze-out volume.
