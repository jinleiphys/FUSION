# Crossover trimers connecting continuous and discrete scaling regimes

> For a system of two identical fermions and one distinguishable particle interacting via a short-range potential with a large s-wave scattering length, the Efimov trimers and Kartavtsev-Malykh trimers exist in different regimes of the mass ratio. The Efimov trimers are known to exhibit a discrete scaling invariance, while the Kartavtsev-Malykh trimers feature a continuous scaling invariance. We point out that a third type of trimers, "crossover trimers", exist universally regardless of short-range details of the potential. These crossover trimers have neither the discrete nor continuous scaling invariance. We show that the crossover trimers continuously connect the discrete and continuous scaling regimes as the mass ratio and the scattering length are varied. We identify the regions for the Kartavtsev-Malykh trimers, Efimov trimers, crossover trimers, and non-universal trimers as a function of the mass ratio and the s-wave scattering length by investigating the scaling property and model-independence of the trimers.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a third class of universal three-body bound states, "crossover trimers", that exist for two identical fermions and one distinguishable particle with large s-wave scattering length, which have neither the discrete scaling invariance of Efimov trimers nor the continuous scaling invariance of Kartavtsev-Malykh trimers, and which continuously connect these two scaling regimes as the mass ratio and scattering length are varied.

## Method

The authors solved the Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation for the (ℓ₁,ℓ₂) = (0,1) channel of two identical fermions and one distinguishable particle, using a finite momentum cutoff Λ that serves as the three-body parameter. They varied the mass ratio α = m_F/m_L and the normalized inverse scattering length (Λa_s)⁻¹ to calculate trimer binding energies, and quantified the degree of discrete or continuous scaling invariance by comparing adjacent energy levels and the scaling of dissociation points.

## Context

This work unifies two previously separate lines of research: Efimov trimers (Efimov 1973) which exist above mass ratio 13.606 and exhibit discrete scaling, and Kartavtsev-Malykh trimers (Kartavtsev and Malykh 2007) which exist below that mass ratio and exhibit continuous scaling. The paper extends these studies by systematically varying both mass ratio and scattering length, revealing that crossover trimers bridge the two regimes and that the boundaries between trimer types are gradual rather than sharp transitions. This enables future experimental searches for crossover trimers in ultracold atomic systems with tunable mass ratios and Feshbach resonances.
