# Probing Generalized Parton Distributions through the photoproduction of a $γπ$ pair

> We study in the framework of collinear QCD factorization the photoproduction of a $γ\,π$ pair with a large invariant mass and a small transverse momentum, as a new way to access generalized parton distributions. In the kinematics of JLab 12-GeV, we demonstrate the feasibility of this measurement and show the extreme sensitivity of the unpolarized cross section to the axial quark GPDs.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of a $\gamma\pi$ pair with large invariant mass and small transverse momentum can access generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through collinear QCD factorization, and the unpolarized cross section is dominated by axial quark GPDs rather than vector GPDs, in contrast to the case of $\rho$ meson production studied by Boussarie (2016).

## Method

The authors computed the scattering amplitude for $\gamma N \to \gamma \pi^\pm N'$ in the framework of collinear QCD factorization, expressing the amplitude in terms of Compton-like form factors ${\cal H}_\pi$, ${\cal E}_\pi$, $\tilde {\cal H}_\pi$, and $\tilde {\cal E}_\pi$. They used asymptotic distribution amplitudes for the pion and modeled GPDs using double distributions with known parton distribution functions, employing two scenarios for polarized PDFs: a "standard" flavor-symmetric light sea and a "valence" flavor-asymmetric light sea. The integration over the quark momentum fraction $z$ was done analytically, while the integration over $x$ was done numerically, and a dipole form factor with $C=0.71$ GeV$^2$ was used for the $t$-dependence.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections $d\sigma/dM^2_{\gamma\pi}$ were computed for $S_{\gamma N}$ values of 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, and 20 GeV$^2$, covering the JLab energy range.
- The cutoff for collinear factorization validity was set at $\Lambda = 1$ GeV for $-t', -u' > \Lambda^2$.
- With an expected luminosity ${\cal L}=100$ pb$^{-1}$s$^{-1}$ over 100 days of run: between $1.3 \times 10^4$ (valence scenario) and $8.0 \times 10^4$ $\gamma\pi^+$ pairs (standard scenario), and between $4.4 \times 10^4$ (valence scenario) and $8.9 \times 10^4$ $\gamma\pi^-$ pairs (standard scenario).
- The dipole form factor parameter was $C = 0.71$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $2\to3$ processes initiated by Ivanov (2002) and Enberg (2006) for diffractive photoproduction of two vector mesons, and follows the strategy advocated by Beiyad (2010) and Boussarie (2016) to enlarge the number of processes for extracting chiral-even GPDs. The process is complementary to deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production (Goeke 2001, Diehl 2003, Belitsky 2005, Boffi 2007, Guidal 2008), and extends timelike Compton scattering (Mueller 1998, Berger 2001, Pire 2011). The results enable near-future measurements at JLab with 12 GeV electron beams, and could be extended to higher $S_{\gamma N}$ at Compass at CERN and at LHC in ultraperipheral collisions, as well as at future EIC and LHeC projects.
