# Three-body model for an isoscalar spin-triplet neutron-proton pair in $^{102}{\rm Sb}$

> We discuss the isoscalar $T=0, S=1$ pairing correlation in the low-lying states of $^{102}{\rm Sb}={}^{100}{\rm Sn}+p+n$ nucleus. To this end, we employ ${\rm core}+p+n$ three-body model with the model space constructed by self-consistent mean-field calculations. The model is developed with both non-relativistic and relativistic effective interactions, the latter of which are found to be more realistic for the present case due to the pseudo-spin symmetry. It turns out that the $(L,S,T)=(0,1,0)$ pairing scheme is strongly hindered in $^{102}$Sb with the relativistic model because of the near degeneracy of the $g_{7/2}$ and $d_{5/2}$ orbitals in the valence space. This pair-breaking effect is clearly seen in the charge-exchange Gamow-Teller-type transitions rather than in the binding energies of $T=0$ and $T=1$ states.

## Key claim
The isoscalar spin-triplet (L,S,T)=(0,1,0) pairing scheme is strongly hindered in 102Sb when using relativistic mean-field models because the near degeneracy of the g7/2 and d5/2 orbitals in the valence space prevents efficient pairing energy gain, though the abstract does not specify that this hindrance depends on the choice of energy density functional and is most clearly seen in Gamow-Teller transition strengths rather than in binding energies.

## Method
The authors constructed a core+p+n three-body model for 102Sb = 100Sn + p + n, using self-consistent mean-field calculations with both non-relativistic Skyrme (SLy4, SkM*, SIII) and relativistic point-coupling (PC-F1, PC-PK1, PC-LA) energy density functionals to generate the single-particle basis. The residual interaction between valence nucleons was a density-dependent zero-range force with separate spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels, where the singlet strength V0 was fitted to reproduce the empirical neutron pairing gap of 101Sn (0.8 MeV) and the triplet-to-singlet ratio f was varied between 1.2 and 1.5 based on fits to 18F, 42Sc, and 58Cu data. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized on a two-particle basis of proton and neutron single-particle states up to a cutoff energy of 20 MeV within a radial box of 40 fm.

## Key numbers
- The empirical neutron pairing gap of 101Sn used to fix V0 is Δ = 0.8 MeV, derived from S2n(102Sn) and 2Sn(101Sn) data from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation.
- The spin-singlet interaction strength V0 for Ecut = 20 MeV ranges from −555 MeV fm3 (PC-PK1) to −754 MeV fm3 (SkM*).
- The fitted ratio f of spin-triplet to spin-singlet interaction strength ranges from 1.15 (PC-F1 for 42Sc) to 1.52 (PC-LA for 18F).
- The B(GT) value for the transition from the 102Sn ground state to the first 1+ state of 102Sb varies strongly with the single-particle energy difference Δε = ε(1g7/2) − ε(2d5/2), with values near 0.2 for relativistic functionals (Δε ≈ 0) and near 1.0 for non-relativistic functionals (Δε ≈ 2–3 MeV) at f = 1.5.
- The sum of observed B(GT+) values from 102Sn β+ decay is 4.0(6) (Faester02) and 4.2(8) (Karny06), implying the total B(GT−) sum rule is ≥ 10 for 102Sn.
- The opening angle θpn between valence nucleons in the first 1+ state is slightly less than 90° for all cases, compared to 67.9° for 6He (Kiku10) and 66.33°, 65.29°, 82.37° for 6He, 11Li, and 24O (Hagi05).

## Context
This work extends the core+p+n three-body model previously applied to 18F (Tanimura 2014) to the heavier N=Z system 102Sb near the doubly-magic 100Sn core, where the near degeneracy of g7/2 and d5/2 orbitals predicted by relativistic mean-field models (due to pseudo-spin symmetry) suppresses the isoscalar spin-triplet pairing that is prominent in lighter systems like 18F. The results contradict the expectation from systematic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Bertulani 2010, Gezerlis 2011) that isoscalar pair condensation might occur in heavy N≈Z regions, and instead show that the (L,S,T)=(0,1,0) coupling is hindered in 102Sb. The study enables future experimental tests via Gamow-Teller transition measurements, which are predicted to be far more sensitive to the pairing structure than binding energies, and provides a benchmark for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals in neutron-proton pairing studies.
