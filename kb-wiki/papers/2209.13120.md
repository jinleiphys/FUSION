# Four-quark scatterings in QCD I

> We investigate dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the emergence of mesonic bound states from the infrared dynamics of four-quark scatterings. Both phenomena originate from the resonant scalar-pseudoscalar channel of the four-quark scatterings, and we compute the functional renormalisation group (fRG) flows of the Fierz-complete four-quark interaction of up and down quarks with its $t$ channel momentum dependence. This is done in the isospin symmetric case, also including the flow of the quark two-point function. This system can be understood as the fRG analogues of the complete Bethe-Salpeter equations and quark gap equation. The pole mass of the pion is determined from both direct calculations of the four-quark flows in the Minkowski regime of momenta and the analytic continuation based on results in the Euclidean regime, which are consistent with each other.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the emergence of mesonic bound states can be studied from the infrared dynamics of four-quark scatterings using functional renormalisation group (fRG) flows, but only if the system is solved for generic quark masses; in the chiral limit with vanishing current quark masses the constituent quark mass develops an unphysical divergence, requiring extrapolation.

## Method
The authors computed the fRG flows of the Fierz-complete four-quark interaction for up and down quarks in the isospin symmetric case, including the t-channel momentum dependence of the scalar-pseudoscalar channel and the flow of the quark two-point function. They solved the coupled system of ten vertex dressings and the quark propagator, first with momentum-independent tensor structures and then including the t-channel momentum dependence to access timelike momenta. The pion pole mass was determined both from direct calculations in the Minkowski regime and from analytic continuation of Euclidean results.

## Key numbers
- The chiral symmetry breaking cutoff scale is approximately \(k_\chi \approx 0.5\) GeV, below which resonant interaction channels are expected.
- The cutoff scale for the matter sector analysis is \(k \lesssim 1\) GeV, where gluons decouple successively.
- The Fierz-complete basis for two-flavour QCD contains ten tensor structures; for 2+1 flavours the basis has 26 basis elements.

## Context
This work extends the functional renormalisation group approach for bound state computations in QCD developed by Braun (2014), Mitter (2014), Rennecke (2015), Cyrol (2017), Alkofer (2018), Fu (2019), and Fukushima (2021). It complements previous studies that treated only the scalar-pseudoscalar channel with dynamical hadronisation by instead keeping the full Fierz-complete four-quark interaction with momentum dependence. The results enable future embedding of these four-quark flows within full QCD (planned in companion papers) and provide a foundation for studying hadron resonances and their formation in medium at finite temperature and density.
