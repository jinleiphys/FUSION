# The dynamic polarization potential and dynamical non-locality in nuclear potentials: Deuteron-nucleus potential

> The consequences for direct reactions of the dynamical non-locality generated by the excitation of the target and projectile are much less studied than the effects of non-locality arising from exchange processes. Here we are concerned with the dynamical non-locality due to projectile excitation in deuteron induced reactions. The consequences of this non-locality can be studied by the comparison of deuteron induced direct reactions calculated with alternative representations of the elastic channel wave functions: (i) the elastic channel wave functions from coupled channel (CC) calculations involving specific reaction processes, and, (ii) elastic channel wave functions calculated from local potentials that exactly reproduce the elastic scattering $S$-matrix from the same CC calculations. In this work we produce the local equivalent deuteron potentials required for the study of direct reactions involving deuterons. These will enable the study of the effects of dynamical non-locality following a method previously employed in an investigation of the effects of non-locality due to target excitation. In this work we consider only excitations due to deuteron breakup, and some new properties of the breakup dynamical polarization potential (DPP) emerge that reveal dynamical non-locality directly. In addition, we evaluate the TELP inversion method and find that it fails to reproduce some features of the DPP due to breakup.

# The dynamic polarization potential and dynamical non-locality in nuclear potentials: Deuteron-nucleus potential

## Key claim

The dynamical non-locality generated by deuteron breakup coupling produces local equivalent potentials that exhibit non-additivity, providing direct evidence that the underlying dynamical polarization potentials (DPPs) are genuinely non-local. The authors demonstrate that the sum of the local equivalent DPPs for separate L=0 and L=2 breakup channels does not equal the local equivalent DPP when both channels are present without mutual coupling, which would be required if the underlying potentials were local.

## Method

The authors performed coupled discretized continuum channel (CDCC) calculations for 30 MeV deuterons incident on 16O, modeling deuteron breakup into bins in momentum space up to kmax = 0.7 fm−1 with width Δk = 0.1 fm−1. They then applied iterative-perturbative (IP) S-matrix inversion to extract local equivalent potentials that exactly reproduce the elastic scattering S-matrix from the CDCC calculations, and subtracted the bare potential to obtain the DPP. Five sets of CDCC calculations were performed with different combinations of n+p continuum relative angular momenta (L=0, L=2, L=4) and varying degrees of continuum-continuum coupling.

## Key numbers

- For 30 MeV deuterons on 16O with L02 coupling: ΔJR = −9.04 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 35.42 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 69.60 mb, breakup cross section = 83.32 mb
- For 30 MeV deuterons on 16O with L024 coupling: ΔJR = −11.55 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 38.11 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 55.60 mb, breakup cross section = 93.04 mb
- For L0 coupling alone: ΔJR = +15.61 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 26.14 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 67.2 mb, breakup cross section = 63.36 mb
- For L2 coupling alone: ΔJR = −24.89 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 35.25 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 79.60 mb, breakup cross section = 83.30 mb
- For L0andL2 coupling (no inter-continuum coupling): ΔJR = −14.31 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 68.34 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 134.10 mb, breakup cross section = 135.29 mb
- The sum of L0 and L2 ΔJR values (−9.28 MeV fm³) differs from the L0andL2 value (−14.31 MeV fm³), demonstrating non-additivity
- For 56 MeV deuterons on 58Ni with L02 coupling: ΔJR = −1.55 MeV fm³, ΔJI = 18.29 MeV fm³, ΔCS = 86.7 mb

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Mackintosh and Keeley (2014) for studying dynamical non-locality due to target excitation, now applied to projectile excitation in deuteron-induced reactions. The approach contrasts with standard treatments of non-locality such as the Perey-Buck prescription, which addresses exchange non-locality rather than dynamical non-locality. Previous work by Mackintosh (1988) and by Canto and Mackintosh (1990) found that dynamical non-locality from inelastic processes produces effects very different from the standard Perey factor, including an "anti-Perey" effect where the generalized Perey factor exceeds unity. The present results enable future studies of how dynamical non-locality affects (d,p), (d,t), and (d,6Li) transfer reactions, which will be presented in a subsequent paper.
