# Polaron Variational Methods In The Particle Representation Of Field Theory : I. General Formalism

> We apply nonperturbative variational techniques to a relativistic scalar field theory in which heavy bosons (``nucleons'') interact with light scalar mesons via a Yukawa coupling. Integrating out the meson field and neglecting the nucleon vacuum polarization one obtains an effective action in terms of the heavy particle coordinates which is nonlocal in the proper time. As in Feynman's polaron approach we approximate this action by a retarded quadratic action whose parameters are to be determined variationally on the pole of the two-point function. Several ansätze for the retardation function are studied and for the most general case we derive a system of coupled variational equations. An approximate analytic solution displays the instability of the system for coupling constants beyond a critical value.

## Key claim

The paper applies Feynman's polaron variational method to a relativistic scalar field theory (the Wick-Cutkosky model) in the quenched approximation, showing that the variational equations cease to have real solutions beyond a critical coupling constant, which the formalism itself identifies as the region where the ground state becomes unstable.

## Method

The authors integrate out the light scalar meson field in the quenched approximation (neglecting nucleon pair creation) to obtain a nonlocal effective action for the heavy particle coordinates, expressed as a path integral over proper time. They then approximate this action by a retarded quadratic trial action, analogous to Feynman's polaron approach, and determine the variational parameters at the pole of the two-point function using Jensen's inequality (or stationarity). Several ansätze for the retardation function are studied, and a system of coupled variational equations is derived for the most general case.

## Key numbers

- The Feynman polaron approximation (the nonrelativistic analog) deviates less than 2.2% from exact Monte-Carlo results for the ground-state energy and less than 12% for the effective mass, as cited from prior work by Alexandrou and Rosenfelder.
- The effective action for the Wick-Cutkosky model in the particle representation is given by Eq. (eff action pot') with the meson propagator 1/(q^2 + m^2), and the interaction term S_1 is proportional to g^2/(8π^2) times an integral over proper time and a Bessel function K_1(m y) (Eq. eff action pot''').
- The classical potential along the minimum path is V^(0)(Φ, φ_min) = (1/2) M_0^2 Φ^2 - (g^2/(2 m^2)) Φ^4, showing an inverted double well structure (Eq. inverted double well).

## Context

This work extends Feynman's variational polaron method (Feynman 1955) from nonrelativistic solid-state physics to relativistic quantum field theory, specifically the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) which is a scalar Yukawa theory used as a toy model for nucleon-meson dressing. It contrasts with the approach of Simonov and Tjon (1993), who studied the same model in the quenched approximation but neglected all self-energy and vertex corrections, thus avoiding renormalization and the instability seen here. The formalism enables future variational calculations of the dressed nucleon mass and bound-state problems beyond the ladder approximation, with planned extensions to include spin and multiple nucleons.
