# Early time dynamics and hard probes in heavy-ion collisions

> We present an overview of the current state of understanding of the early time dynamics of high-energy heavy-ion collisions, emphasizing recent developments and connections between the physics of the initial state and that of hard probes in heavy-ion collisions. Based on a weak-coupling description, we first establish a microscopic picture of the early time dynamics and equilibration process and subsequently discuss their macroscopic manifestations along with a novel macroscopic description of the pre-equilibrium phase. Some phenomenological consequences concerning the role of the pre-equilibrium phase in large (AA) and small (pp/pA) collision systems are briefly discussed along with open questions and opportunities for future improvements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a weak-coupling description of the early time dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, based on QCD kinetic theory, can now provide a complete event-by-event macroscopic description of the pre-equilibrium phase, but this description is limited to the Yang-Mills sector and does not include dynamical fermions in the kinetic description.

## Method

The author reviews the established microscopic picture of the pre-equilibrium evolution, starting from classical field dynamics (IP-Glasma model) for times tau ~ 1/Q_s, then transitioning to an effective QCD kinetic theory description based on a Boltzmann equation with elastic 2-to-2 and inelastic 1-to-2 (Bremsstrahlung) processes, including dynamical screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. A novel macroscopic description is then constructed using linear response theory out-of-equilibrium, where the non-equilibrium evolution of the energy-momentum tensor is separated into a local average background and small fluctuations propagated via non-equilibrium Green's functions, which have been calculated in QCD kinetic theory and are publicly available as the KoMPoST package.

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario first described by Baier et al. (2000) and builds on numerical solutions of QCD kinetic theory from Berges et al. (2013-2014) and Kurkela et al. (2014-2015). The macroscopic description using linear response functions, developed in Kurkela et al. (2018), enables for the first time an event-by-event description of the pre-equilibrium phase that can be matched to viscous hydrodynamic simulations, as demonstrated by matching classical Yang-Mills simulations at early times (tau less than 0.2 fm/c) through the kinetic description to hydrodynamic evolution beyond tau ~ 1.0 fm/c. This framework opens the possibility for integrated studies of hard probes and bulk dynamics in both large (AA) and small (pp/pA) collision systems, where a genuine non-equilibrium description will be needed for the latter.
