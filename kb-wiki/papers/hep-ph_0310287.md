# Universal light quark mass dependence and heavy-light meson spectroscopy

> Clean predictions are presented for all the spin-averaged heavy-light meson spectroscopies. A new symmetry is identified wherein the energy eigenstates have a universal dependence on both the light and heavy quark masses. This universality is used in an efficient analysis of these mesons within the QCD string/flux tube picture. Unique predictions for all the D, D_s, B, and B_s type mesons in terms of just four measured quantities.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new symmetry, "universal light quark mass dependence" (UMD), where all energy eigenstates of heavy-light mesons have the same dependence on the light quark mass, so that excitation energy differences for a given light flavor are independent of that mass. The authors note that this universality is not exact, with a small violation of about 8 +/- 3 MeV observed in the comparison of D and D_s P-wave to S-wave differences.

## Method

The authors analyze heavy-light meson spectroscopy within the relativistic flux tube (RFT) model, a QCD string model with a static string tension and a short-range Coulomb interaction. They use the UMD principle to determine the model parameters (string tension a, Coulomb constant k, and heavy quark masses m_c and m_b) from only four measured quantities: the spin-averaged S-wave ground states for charm and bottom, and one spin-averaged P-wave multiplet from the D_s system. They then predict the full spectra for D, D_s, B, and B_s mesons up to five radial and five angular excitations.

## Key numbers

- String tension a = 0.18 GeV^2, derived from the universal Regge slope alpha' = 0.88 GeV^2 via the relation alpha' = 1/(2 pi a)
- Coulomb constant k = 0.52, determined by matching the RFT model prediction to the experimental D_s spin-averaged 1P-1S mass difference of 439 +/- 4 MeV
- Spin-averaged D_s,1S mass = 2076 +/- 1 MeV, computed as (3/4) D*_s + (1/4) D_s
- Spin-averaged D_s,1P mass = 2515 +/- 3 MeV
- Experimental B_s - B mass difference = 91 +/- 1 MeV, used to establish the relation between strange and non-strange light quark masses
- Predicted D 1S state at 1.974 GeV, D 1P state at 2.409 GeV (for m_u,d = 300 MeV, m_s = 495 MeV, m_c = 1330 MeV, m_b = 4670 MeV)
- Predicted B 1S state at 5.314 GeV, B 1P state at 5.749 GeV (same parameters)
- Predicted D_s 1S state at 2.065 GeV, D_s 1P state at 2.513 GeV
- Predicted B_s 1S state at 5.405 GeV, B_s 1P state at 5.853 GeV

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark effective theory approach to heavy-light mesons by introducing a new symmetry that simplifies the analysis of excited states across all four flavor families (D, D_s, B, B_s). The UMD principle is supported by experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2003) and by analytic demonstration using a relativistic potential model with the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. The predictions enable future experimental searches at B factories, CLEOc, HERA, and hadron colliders, and the authors note that the small observed violations of UMD (about 10 MeV) likely arise from 1/m_Q corrections to heavy quark symmetry, which remain a topic for future investigation.
