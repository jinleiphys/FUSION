# Hadronic Freeze-Out in A+A Collisions meets the Lattice QCD Parton-Hadron Transition Line

> We analyze hadrochemical freeze-out in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS and LHC energies. Employing the UrQMD hybrid transport model we study the effects of the final hadron/resonance expansion phase on the hadron multiplicities established at hadronization. The bulk meson yields freeze out directly at hadronization whereas the baryon-antibaryon sector is subject to significant alterations, due to annihilation and regeneration processes. We quantify the latter changes by survival factors for each species which are applied to modify the statistical model predictions for the data. The modified SM analysis recovers the hadronization points, which coincide with the recent lattice QCD predictions of the parton-hadron transition line at finite baryochemical potential.

## Key claim

The hadrochemical freeze-out points recovered by applying UrQMD-derived survival factors to statistical model fits coincide with the lattice QCD predictions of the parton-hadron transition line at finite baryochemical potential, but only after accounting for the 20-50% yield alterations in the baryon-antibaryon sector caused by annihilation and regeneration during the hadronic expansion phase.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hybrid transport model, which couples a 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic expansion with a Cooper-Frye hadronization mechanism and a subsequent hadronic cascade afterburner, to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS and LHC energies. They quantified the effects of the hadronic expansion phase by calculating modification factors (survival factors) for each hadron species as the ratio of yields after the afterburner to yields at hadronization. These modification factors were then applied to statistical model fits of experimental data from NA49 (SPS) and ALICE (LHC) to recover the primordial hadronization points, which they identify as the last hadronic chemical equilibrium (LHCE) points.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, 20% central Pb+Pb): standard SM fit gives T = 156 +/- 5 MeV, mu_B = 1 +/- 12 MeV, chi^2/NDF = 26.5/9; modified fit with UrQMD factors gives T = 166 +/- 3 MeV, mu_B = 2 +/- 6 MeV, chi^2/NDF = 11.5/9
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (5% central Pb+Pb): standard fit T = 151 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 266 +/- 9 MeV; modified fit T = 163 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 250 +/- 9 MeV
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 8.7 GeV (5% central Pb+Pb): standard fit T = 148 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 385 +/- 11 MeV; modified fit T = 161 +/- 6 MeV, mu_B = 376 +/- 15 MeV
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV (5% central Pb+Pb): standard fit T = 140 +/- 1 MeV, mu_B = 437 +/- 5 MeV; modified fit T = 156 +/- 5 MeV, mu_B = 426 +/- 4 MeV
- Antibaryon suppression from the hadronic afterburner at SPS energies ranges from 25% to 50% for antiprotons, anti-Lambdas, and anti-Xis
- The critical energy density for hadronization was assumed to be 0.8 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of statistical model analyses of hadron yields in A+A collisions, which began with observations at the BEVALAC and was formalized by Becattini (2003), Andronic (2008), and Braun-Munzinger (2004). It directly addresses the discrepancy noted by Cleymans (2005) between the freeze-out curve and lattice QCD predictions at higher baryochemical potential, and the poor chi^2 fits observed by Andronic (2012) at LHC energies. The key advance is the use of UrQMD hybrid model calculations by Steinheimer (2012) and Becattini (2012) to quantify the previously neglected effects of the hadronic expansion phase on baryon-antibaryon yields. The resulting LHCE points agree with the lattice QCD phase boundary calculations by Endrodi (2011) and Kaczmarek (2011), enabling the experimental location of the QCD phase transition line at finite baryochemical potential.
