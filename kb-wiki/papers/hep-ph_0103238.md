# Parametrization of nuclear parton distributions

> Optimum nuclear parton distributions are obtained by analyzing available experimental data on electron and muon deep inelastic scattering (DIS). The distributions are given at Q^2=1 GeV^2 with a number of parameters, which are determined by a chi^2 analysis of the data. Valence-quark distributions are relatively well determined at medium x, but they are slightly dependent on the assumed parametrization form particularly at small x. Although antiquark distributions are shadowed at small x, their behavior is not obvious at medium x from the F_2 data. The gluon distributions could not be restricted well by the inclusive DIS data; however, the analysis tends to support the gluon shadowing at small x. We provide analytical expressions and computer subroutines for calculating the nuclear parton distributions, so that other researchers could use them for applications to other high-energy nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first \(\chi^2\) analysis of nuclear parton distributions using only inclusive electron and muon deep inelastic scattering data, but they note that the gluon distributions could not be restricted well by these data and that the antiquark behavior at medium \(x\) is not obvious from the \(F_2\) data alone.

## Method

The authors parametrize nuclear parton distributions at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) as the product of a nucleon parton distribution (MRST-LO central gluon) and a nuclear weight function \(w_i(x,A,Z)\). The weight functions are taken in either a quadratic or cubic polynomial form in \(x\), with a factor \((1-x)^{-\beta_i}\) for the large-\(x\) behavior and a leading \(A^{-1/3}\) dependence for the nuclear size. The parameters are determined by a \(\chi^2\) fit to 309 data points of the ratio \(F_2^A / F_2^D\) from EMC, SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, and E665 collaborations, using the DGLAP evolution to evolve the distributions to the experimental \(Q^2\) values.

## Key numbers

* Minimum \(\chi^2\) for the quadratic fit: 583.7 (for 309 data points).
* Minimum \(\chi^2\) for the cubic fit: 546.6 (for 309 data points).
* Initial scale for the parametrization: \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
* Kinematical range of the provided computer subroutines: \(10^{-9} \le x \le 1\) and \(1\ \text{GeV}^2 \le Q^2 \le 10^5\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* Number of experimental data points used: 309, covering 14 nuclei (He, Li, Be, C, N, Al, Ca, Fe, Cu, Ag, Sn, Xe, Au, Pb).

## Context

This work extends earlier model-dependent attempts to extract nuclear parton distributions (e.g., the SAGA group's own prior work) and the model-independent approach of Eskola, Kolhinen, and Ruuskanen (1999) by introducing a formal \(\chi^2\) minimization. It provides the first systematic, data-driven parametrization of nuclear parton distributions at leading order, enabling other researchers to use the provided analytical expressions and computer subroutines for applications to high-energy nuclear reactions such as heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that the existing nucleon parametrizations (e.g., MRST) should be modified because nuclear data were partially used without nuclear corrections.
