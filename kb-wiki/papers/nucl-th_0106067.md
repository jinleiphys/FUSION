# Heavy Quarkonium Dissociation Cross Sections in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Many of the hadron-hadron cross sections required for the study of the dynamics of matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be calculated using the quark-interchange model. Here we evaluate the low-energy dissociation cross sections of $J/蠄$, $蠄'$, $蠂$, $违$, and $违'$ in collision with $蟺$, $蟻$, and $K$, which are important for the interpretation of heavy-quarkonium suppression as a signature for the quark gluon plasma. ## Key claim

The quark-interchange model can be used to calculate low-energy dissociation cross sections of heavy quarkonia by light mesons, but these cross sections must be understood and incorporated into simulations of heavy-ion collisions before quark-gluon plasma formation can be established through heavy-quarkonium suppression as a signature.

## Method

The authors employed the Barnes-Swanson quark-interchange model, using nonrelativistic quark potential model wavefunctions and interquark interactions (color-Coulomb, linear confining, and spin-spin hyperfine terms) to evaluate Born-order scattering amplitudes for meson-meson rearrangement reactions. They calculated the spatial overlap integrals by expanding meson wavefunctions as linear combinations of Gaussian basis functions, and evaluated spin matrix elements using Wigner 9-j symbols. The cross sections were obtained by integrating the differential cross section over the invariant momentum transfer t, with the post and prior formalisms checked for numerical consistency.
