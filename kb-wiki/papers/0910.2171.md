# Using baryon octet magnetic moments and masses to fix the pion cloud contribution

> Using SU(3) symmetry to constrain the pion BB' couplings, assuming SU(3) breaking comes only from one-loop pion cloud contributions, and using the the covariant spectator theory to describe the photon coupling to the quark core, we show how the experimental masses and magnetic moments of the baryon octet can be used to set a model independent constraint on the strength of the pion cloud contributions to the octet, and hence the nucleon, form factors at Q2=0.

## Key claim

Using SU(3) symmetry to constrain the pion-baryon couplings and assuming SU(3) breaking comes only from one-loop pion cloud contributions, the authors show that the experimental masses and magnetic moments of the baryon octet can be used to set a model-independent constraint on the strength of the pion cloud contributions to the octet, and hence the nucleon, form factors at Q2=0. The hedge is that this result depends on the assumption that the pion cloud contributions are well described by the lowest order one-loop contributions, and that the SU(3) mixing parameter α is fixed near 0.6 as favored by SU(6) and the fit to baryon octet masses.

## Method

The authors used the covariant spectator theory (CST) to describe the photon coupling to the quark core, with baryons composed of three dressed valence quarks. They assumed the coupling of the pion to the baryon octet is fixed by SU(3) symmetry, and that the pion cloud contributions to baryon masses and magnetic moments are described by the lowest order one-loop contributions. They then used the experimental masses of the baryon octet (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) to determine the SU(3) mixing parameter α, and used the experimental magnetic moments of the octet (seven measured values) to fit the quark anomalous moments and four pion cloud coefficients (B1, B2, C2, D2), with the strange quark anomalous moment κs fixed from a previous study of the Ω- magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) mixing parameter α = 0.6943, determined from a best fit to the average baryon masses (MN = 939.0 MeV, MΛ = 1115.7 MeV, MΣ = 1192.4 MeV, MΞ = 1318.1 MeV)
- The fitted quark anomalous moments: κu = 1.929, κd = 1.911 (with κs = 1.462 fixed from the Ω- moment)
- The pion cloud coefficients: B1 = 0.2531, B2 = 0.5648, C2 = -0.06599, D2 = -0.08321
- The maximum deviation of the best fit from experimental magnetic moments is 0.10 μN (nuclear magnetons), compared to 0.22 μN for the naive quark model and 0.19 μN for the Cloudy Bag Model
- The pion cloud corrections to the nucleon and Σ- moments (as measured by the ratio RB = ZB δμB / μBbest) are all about 40%: proton 40%, neutron 42%, Σ- 42%
- The ratio r = μΞ- / μΛ is about 1.06 from experiment; the model gives r > 1, solving a longstanding problem for SU(6) constituent quark models where r < 1

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator theory quark model previously introduced by the same authors (Gross, Ramalho, and Tsushima) for the nucleon and Omega baryon, and provides a model-independent constraint on the size of pion cloud contributions to nucleon form factors. It improves upon the naive quark model (NQM) which had a maximum deviation of 0.22 μN, and the Cloudy Bag Model (CBM) of Theberge (1983), Tsushima, and Leinweber (1999) which had a maximum deviation of 0.19 μN. The results enable assessment of the many models of the pion cloud currently in use, and the authors compare their nucleon pion cloud contributions (1.129 μN for proton, -0.807 μN for neutron) with those from chiral perturbation theory (Puglia 2000, Geng 2009), the Cloudy Bag Model (Leinweber 1999), and other constituent quark models (Barik 1986, Ha 1998, Faessler 2006, Miller 2002, Wang 2007, Cloet 2008).
