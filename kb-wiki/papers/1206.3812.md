# Neutrinoproduction of Photons and Pions From Nucleons in a Chiral Effective Field Theory for Nuclei

> Neutrino-induced productions (neutrinoproduction) of photons and pions from nucleons and nuclei are important for the interpretation of neutrino-oscillation experiments, as they are potential backgrounds in the MiniBooNE experiment [A. A. Aquilar-Arevalo et al. (MiniBooNE Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 100}, 032301 (2008)]. These processes are studied at intermediate energies, where the Δ(1232) resonance becomes important. The Lorentz-covariant effective field theory, which is the framework used in this series of study, contains nucleons, pions, Δs, isoscalar scalar (σ) and vector (ω) fields, and isovector vector (ρ) fields. The lagrangian exhibits a nonlinear realization of (approximate) $SU(2)_L \otimes SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry and incorporates vector meson dominance. In this paper, we focus on setting up the framework. Power counting for vertices and Feynman diagrams is explained. Because of the built-in symmetries, the vector current is automatically conserved (CVC), and the axial-vector current is partially conserved (PCAC). To calibrate the axial-vector transition current (N $\leftrightarrow$ Δ), pion production from the nucleon is used as a benchmark and compared to bubble-chamber data from Argonne and Brookhaven National Laboratories. At low energies, the convergence of our power-counting scheme is investigated, and next-to-leading-order tree-level corrections are found to be small.

## Key claim
The paper sets up a Lorentz-covariant chiral effective field theory framework for neutrino-induced photon and pion production from nucleons, but the framework is only applicable for neutrino laboratory energies up to 0.5 GeV, where the Δ(1232) resonance dominates and where the vector-meson-dominance form factors remain valid (failing above Q² ≈ 0.3 GeV²).

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral effective field theory Lagrangian containing nucleons, pions, Δ(1232) resonances, and isoscalar (σ, ω) and isovector (ρ) meson fields, with electroweak couplings introduced via the external field technique and vector meson dominance. They calculated tree-level Feynman diagrams for charged-current and neutral-current pion production and neutral-current photon production from nucleons, keeping all diagrams through next-to-leading order in their power-counting scheme. The axial-vector N↔Δ transition current was calibrated by fitting to bubble-chamber data from Argonne National Laboratory and Brookhaven National Laboratory, using a parametrization from Graczyk (2009) to determine the vector-meson-dominance constants.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino laboratory energy range: E_ν^Lab ≤ 0.5 GeV (intermediate-energy region where Δ resonance is important)
- Δ(1232) resonance mass: 1.232 GeV
- ρ meson mass: m_ρ = 0.776 GeV; ω meson mass: m_v = 0.783 GeV; a₁ meson mass: m_{a₁} = 1.26 GeV
- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.26; axial mass parameter: M_A = 1.05 GeV
- Vector-meson-dominance form factors become inadequate at Q² ≈ 0.3 GeV²
- Fitted transition form factor parameters: h_A = 1.40, h_{Δa₁} = -3.98; c_{1Δ} = 1.21, c_{3Δ} = -0.61, c_{6Δ} = -0.078; d_{2Δ} = -0.087, d_{4Δ} = 0.20, d_{7Δ} = -0.04

## Context
This work extends the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) effective field theory approach developed by Serot and collaborators (1997, 2000, 2007) to neutrino-induced reactions, providing a framework that respects chiral symmetry and vector meson dominance while including the Δ resonance as an explicit degree of freedom. It differs from earlier approaches such as Rein and Sehgal (1981) and Alvarez-Ruso (1999) that sum resonances incoherently, and from Hernandez (2007) and Hill (2009) that use Born approximation extrapolated to several GeV, by insisting on low-energy validity and consistent power counting. The calibrated framework enables future calculations of neutrino-induced photon and pion production from nuclei, which are needed to interpret backgrounds in oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE and MicroBooNE.
