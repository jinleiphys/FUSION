# Pentaquarks in a Breathing Mode Approach to Chiral Solitons

> In this talk I report on a computation of the spectra of exotic pentaquarks and radial excitations of the low--lying ${1/2}^+$ and ${3/2}^+$ baryons in a chiral soliton model. In addition I present model results for the transition magnetic moments between the N(1710) and the nucleon.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton model with a dynamically quantized breathing (monopole) mode reproduces the masses of the observed exotic pentaquarks \(\Theta^+\) and \(\Xi_{3/2}\) within about 30–50 MeV of experimental values without fine-tuning, and predicts that the \(N(1710)\) resonance is not a pure antidecuplet state but a mixture with octet radial excitations, altering its predicted transition magnetic moments.

## Method

The author starts from a chiral Lagrangian in flavor \(SU(3)\) and constructs a classical soliton solution. Collective coordinates for flavor rotations and a scaling variable for radial (breathing) excitations are quantized canonically, yielding a Hamiltonian whose eigenstates are superpositions of \(SU(3)\) representations (octet, antidecuplet, 27-plet) and their radial excitations. Flavor symmetry breaking is treated exactly by diagonalizing the full Hamiltonian matrix, and the Skyrme parameter \(e\) is varied in the range 5.0–5.5 to compare predictions with experiment.

## Key numbers

* \(\Theta^+\) mass: predicted 1.57 GeV (\(e=5.0\)) and 1.59 GeV (\(e=5.5\)), compared to experimental 1.537 ± 0.010 GeV.
* \(\Xi_{3/2}\) mass: predicted 1.89 GeV (\(e=5.0\)) and 1.91 GeV (\(e=5.5\)), compared to experimental 1.862 ± 0.002 GeV.
* Mass difference between \(\Theta^+\) and \(\Xi_{3/2}\) is reproduced within 10 MeV.
* Roper resonance (\(N(1440)\)) mass difference from nucleon: predicted 413 MeV (\(e=5.0\)) and 445 MeV (\(e=5.5\)), compared to Breit-Wigner 501 MeV and pole position 426 MeV.
* Transition magnetic moment for the \(N(1710)\) candidate (\(m=2\)) in the proton channel: –0.28 nuclear magnetons (\(e=5.0\)), compared to 0.00 for a pure antidecuplet state.
* Predicted masses for \(J=3/2\) pentaquarks from the 27-plet range from 1.66 GeV (\(\Theta_{27}\), \(e=5.0\)) to 2.10 GeV (\(\Omega_{27}\), \(e=5.0\)).

## Context

This work extends the breathing-mode chiral soliton model of Schwesinger et al. (1991) and Weigel (1998), which previously predicted the \(\Theta^+\) mass, to include the \(\Xi_{3/2}\) and the full 27-plet of exotic baryons. It contrasts with the earlier estimate of Diakonov et al. (1997), which identified \(N(1710)\) as a pure antidecuplet state and predicted a \(\Xi_{3/2}\) mass of 2070 MeV, and with the calculation of Walliser and Kopeliovich (2003) that omitted dynamical monopole excitations. The results enable experimental tests of the mixing scheme via electromagnetic transition measurements and provide specific mass predictions for as-yet-unobserved spin-3/2 pentaquarks.
