# Neutrino Spectral Split in the Exact Many Body Formalism

> We consider the many-body system of neutrinos interacting with each other through neutral current weak force. Emerging many-body effects in such a system could play important roles in some astrophysical sites such as the core collapse supernovae. In the literature this many-body system is usually treated within the mean field approximation which is an effective one-body description based on omitting entangled neutrino states. In this paper, we consider the original many-body system in an effective two flavor mixing scenario under the single angle approximation and present a solution without using the mean field approximation. Our solution is formulated around a special class of many-body eigenstates which do not undergo any level crossings as the neutrino self interaction rate decreases while the neutrinos radiate from the supernova. In particular, an initial state which consists of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos of an orthogonal flavor can be entirely decomposed in terms of those eigenstates. Assuming that the conditions are perfectly adiabatic so that the evolution of these eigenstates follow their variation with the interaction rate, we show that this initial state develops a spectral split at exactly the same energy predicted by the mean field formulation.

## Key claim

Under perfectly adiabatic conditions, an initial many-body state consisting of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos of an orthogonal flavor can be decomposed entirely into a special class of eigenstates that do not undergo level crossings, and this state develops a spectral split at exactly the same energy predicted by the mean field formulation.

## Method

The authors apply the Richardson-Gaudin (Bethe ansatz) formalism to diagonalize the exact many-body Hamiltonian of self-interacting neutrinos in a two-flavor mixing scenario under the single-angle approximation, without using the mean field approximation. They identify a class of many-body eigenstates whose eigenvalues do not cross as the neutrino self-interaction rate decreases, and decompose an initial state of electron neutrinos into these eigenstates. Assuming perfectly adiabatic evolution, they follow the transformation of these eigenstates as the neutrino density decreases, rather than performing a full dynamical evolution calculation.

## Key numbers

* The technique allows numerical work with as many as 10^8 neutrinos.
* A proto-neutron star cools in about 10 s by emitting of the order of 10^58 neutrinos.
* The vacuum oscillation frequency is given by omega = (m_2^2 - m_1^2)/(2E), where E is the neutrino energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Friedland (2003), Bell (2003), and Friedland (2003) that examined whether the mean field approximation accurately describes self-interacting neutrinos, but those studies ignored vacuum oscillations. Unlike those works, the present paper includes vacuum oscillations and obtains a spectral split in the exact many-body formalism for the first time. The results enable comparison between exact many-body solutions and the BBGKY hierarchy approach developed by Volpe (2013, 2015) for going beyond mean field theory, and suggest that the equivalence of mean field and exact results for this initial state may indicate symmetries in the higher-order terms of the hierarchy.
