# Neutrino recoil force in electron-capture decay of polarized nuclei: measurement prospects and potential applications

> Due to a directional asymmetry of neutrino emission caused by parity violation, a sample of radioactive atoms experiences a small recoil force from neutrino radiation accompanying electron capture by polarized nuclei. An expression for this force is obtained for the case of allowed nuclear transitions. Prospects to measure this force by the use of modern micromechanical devices are considered. Numerical estimates for the force are presented for a number of most suitable radioactive isotopes. Potential applications for the weak interaction studies are discussed including the possibility to search for hypothetical Lorentz invariance violation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a corrected expression for the neutrino recoil force in electron-capture decay of polarized nuclei, showing that for the pure Gamow-Teller transition \(J_i \to J_i-1\) the force is three times smaller than the qualitative estimate given in DeAngelis (2012), because the earlier work overestimated the directionality of the neutrino beam by assuming all neutrinos are emitted opposite to the polarization axis.

## Method

The authors derive the angular distribution of neutrinos from electron capture by polarized nuclei for allowed nuclear transitions (\(J_i \to J_f = J_i, J_i \pm 1\)) using the Fermi golden rule and the weak interaction Hamiltonian, including the possibility of a finite neutrino mass. They then integrate the momentum transfer over the angular distribution to obtain the recoil force on a sample, expressing it in terms of the sample activity, branching ratio, neutrino energy, asymmetry coefficient, and nuclear polarization. Numerical estimates are computed for a list of suitable isotopes using data from the IAEA website, with polarization calculated from the Boltzmann distribution in a magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* For the pure Gamow-Teller transition \(J_i \to J_i-1\) with complete polarization, the asymmetry coefficient \(B_n = -1\), and the recoil force is a factor of 1/3 smaller than the estimate in DeAngelis (2012).
* For a sample in a magnetic field \(B = 10\) T at temperature \(T = 1\) K, the thermal polarization \(P\) for \({}^{163}\)Er is 0.10% (Table t1).
* The force parameter \(f_n\) for \({}^{163}\)Er (transition \(5/2^- \to 7/2^-\)) is \(8.0 \times 10^{-9}\) N/g (Table t1).
* The minimal sample mass \(m_{\rm min}\) for \({}^{163}\)Er is \(1.3 \times 10^{-12}\) g, corresponding to a minimal activity \(\alpha_{\rm min}\) of 0.71 MBq (Table t1).
* For the mixed Fermi and Gamow-Teller transition in \({}^{37}\)Ar (\(3/2^+ \to 3/2^+\)), the maximal recoil force \(F_n\) is \(1.3 \times 10^{-19}\) N for a sample mass of \(1.0 \times 10^{-10}\) g (Table t3).
* The neutrino energy \(E_{\nu n}\) for \({}^{37}\)Ar is 811 keV (Table t3).
* The parameter \(\beta_0 = \mu_N B_0 / (k_B T_0) = 3.658 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(B_0 = 1\) T and \(T_0 = 1\) K.

## Context

This work corrects the overestimated recoil force from DeAngelis (2012), which considered only the pure Gamow-Teller transition \(J_i \to J_i-1\) and assumed a fully directed neutrino beam. The paper extends the analysis to all allowed transitions (including mixed Fermi and Gamow-Teller) and provides a rigorous derivation including the asymmetry coefficient. The results enable realistic estimates for measuring the neutrino recoil force using magnetic resonance force microscopy, and open the possibility of using such measurements to study the weak interaction and search for Lorentz invariance violation, as also discussed in Vos (2015a).
