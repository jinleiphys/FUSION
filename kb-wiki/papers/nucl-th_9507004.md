# Recent Chalk River Experiments on Superallowed $0^{+} \to 0^{+}$ Beta Decays

> Two experiments, (1) a measurement of the superallowed $0^{+} \rightarrow 0^{+}$ branching ratio in $^{10}$C, and (2) a measurement of (or limit on) non-analogue $0^{+} \rightarrow 0^{+}$ branches in $^{38m}$K, $^{46}$V, $^{50}$Mn and $^{54}$Co, are described. The implications these experiments have on the test of the conservation of the weak vector current (CVC) and the unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix are surveyed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the unitarity test of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix from the sum of squares of the first-row elements yields 0.9972 ± 0.0013, differing from unity at the 98% confidence level, but notes that this apparent non-unitarity may reflect undiagnosed inadequacies in the evaluation of V_us or in the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections (δ_C) rather than necessarily requiring extensions to the three-generation Standard Model.

## Method

Two experiments were performed at the Chalk River TASCC facility. The first measured the superallowed 0+ → 0+ branching ratio in 10C using the 8π spectrometer (20 Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors with a BGO inner ball) to determine the ratio of 1022 keV to 718 keV gamma rays from β-delayed decays, with pileup rejection and interleaved efficiency measurements via (p,p') reactions. The second experiment searched for non-analogue 0+ → 0+ branches in 38mK, 46V, 50Mn, and 54Co using a 68% HPGe detector with thin plastic scintillators for positron tagging to reduce bremsstrahlung background, enabling observation of branches down to the 10 ppm level.

## Key numbers

- 10C superallowed branching ratio: [1.4625 ± 0.0020 (stat) ± 0.0015 (syst)]%
- 10C ft value: 3040.1 ± 5.1 s (using Q_EC = 1907.77(9) keV and t_1/2 = 19.209(12) s)
- Average Ft value for nine precision data: 3072.3 ± 2.0 s
- CVC verification level: fractional uncertainty of 4 × 10^-4 on the average Ft value
- V_ud = 0.9740 ± 0.0005
- CKM first-row unitarity sum: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 0.9972 ± 0.0013
- 46V non-analogue δ_IM^1: 0.053(5)% (experiment) vs 0.046(5)% (THH theory) and 0.054(50)% (OB theory)
- 54Co non-analogue δ_IM^1: 0.035(5)% (experiment) vs 0.037(8)% (THH theory) and 0.003(50)% (OB theory)
- 38mK non-analogue δ_IM^1 limit: < 0.28%
- 50Mn non-analogue δ_IM^1 limit: < 0.016%

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of superallowed 0+ → 0+ beta decays previously surveyed by Hardy et al. (1990) and updated by Towner and Hardy (1995), providing a new high-precision data point for the lightest such emitter (10C) and experimental tests of the isospin-mixing corrections calculated by Towner-Hardy-Harvey (THH) and Ormand-Brown (OB). The results enable a refined test of the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis and a more precise determination of V_ud, while the apparent CKM unitarity deficit at the 98% confidence level motivates further scrutiny of the theoretical corrections (δ_C and δ_R) and of the evaluation of V_us, as previously suggested by Gasser and Leutwyler (1992).
