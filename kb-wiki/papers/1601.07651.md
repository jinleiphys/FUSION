# Odd tensor E3 transitions and the generalized seniority in Sn-isotopes

> In our recent paper [Phys. Lett. B 753, (2016) 122], we have shown that both the odd and even tensor electric transition probabilities exhibit similar behavior within the generalized seniority scheme in a multi-j environment. This microscopic approach was used to show for the first time the occurrence of seniority isomers in the $ {13}^-$ isomers of Sn-isotopes, which decay by odd tensor $E1$ transition to the same seniority ($Δv = 0$) state. In this letter, we extend our studies to odd tensor $E3$ transitions connecting different seniority states ($Δv = 2$), and show for the first time that the generalized seniority scheme explains reasonably well the systematics of the $B(E3)$ values for the $(0^+ \rightarrow 3_1^-)$ transitions in the Sn-isotopes. Additionally, we support these results by seniority guided Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations. The generalized seniority results are able to single out the most crucial valence space required in the LSSM calculations.

## Key claim

The generalized seniority scheme, previously shown to explain odd-tensor E1 transitions with Δv=0, also explains the systematics of odd-tensor E3 transitions with Δv=2 for the (0⁺ → 3₁⁻) transitions in Sn-isotopes, provided the valence space includes both d and h orbits.

## Method

The authors calculated B(E3↑) values for the first 3⁻ states in Sn-isotopes using the generalized seniority formalism for multi-j degenerate orbits, with the seniority reduction formula for Δv=2 transitions. They fitted the B(E3) values for ¹¹⁶Sn and ¹¹⁸Sn using two valence spaces: Ω=9 (d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂) and Ω=11 (d₅/₂ ⊗ d₃/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂), with ¹⁰⁸Sn and ¹⁰⁶Sn as cores respectively. They then performed seniority-guided Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations using the Nushell code with SN100PN interaction, using the same valence spaces and neutron effective charges of 1.5 and 1.6.

## Key numbers

- B(E3↑) values for ¹¹⁶Sn and ¹¹⁸Sn were used as fitting points for the generalized seniority calculations with Ω=9 and Ω=11, respectively.
- The Ω=9 valence space corresponds to d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂; the Ω=11 valence space corresponds to d₅/₂ ⊗ d₃/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂.
- Neutron single-particle energies used in LSSM: -10.6089 MeV (0g₇/₂), -10.2893 MeV (1d₅/₂), -8.7167 MeV (1d₃/₂), -8.6944 MeV (2s₁/₂), -8.8152 MeV (0h₁₁/₂).
- Neutron effective charges: 1.5 for the d₅/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂ valence space; 1.6 for the d₅/₂ ⊗ d₃/₂ ⊗ h₁₁/₂ valence space.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter was ℏω = 45A⁻¹/³ − 25A⁻²/³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generalized seniority study of odd-tensor E1 transitions (Maheshwari and Jain, 2016) to odd-tensor E3 transitions with Δv=2. It builds on the generalized seniority framework of Arima and Ichimura and on earlier measurements of 3⁻ states in ¹¹²⁻¹²⁴Sn by Jonsson et al. (1981) using (p,p'γ) and Coulomb excitation. The results support the octupole vibrational character of the 3⁻ states, as the d and h orbits differ in l by 3 units, and the generalized seniority scheme successfully identifies the minimal valence space needed for LSSM calculations. The approach enables simplification of LSSM calculations by singling out the most critical orbits, though a deviation at ¹¹⁴Sn (N=64, a possible magic number for octupole collectivity) remains unexplained by the LSSM calculations.
