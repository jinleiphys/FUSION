# The finite 't Hooft coupling correction on the jet quenching parameter in a $\mathcal N=4$ Super Yang-Mills Plasma

> We derive the quadratic action of the fluctuations around the classical world sheet underlying the jet quenching from AdS/CFT. After obtaining the correspondence partition function, the expansion of the jet quenching parameter of $\mathcal N=4$ super symmetric Yang-Mills theory is carried out to the sub-leading term in the large 't Hooft coupling $λ$ at a nonzero temperature. The strong coupling corresponds to the semi-classical expansion of the string-sigma model, the gravity dual of the Wilson loop operator, with the sub-leading term expressed in terms of functional determinants of fluctuations. The contribution of these determinants are evaluated numerically. We find the jet quenching parameter is reduced due to world sheet fluctuations by a factor $(1-1.97λ^{-1/2}) $

## Key claim

The paper computes the sub-leading correction to the jet quenching parameter in N=4 super Yang-Mills theory at finite but large 't Hooft coupling, finding that the leading-order result is reduced by a factor of (1 - 1.97 λ^{-1/2}), where this correction arises from world sheet fluctuations and dominates over the previously known O(λ^{-3/2}) correction from string theory corrections to the background geometry.

## Method

The authors derive the quadratic action for fluctuations around the classical string world sheet that underlies the jet quenching Wilson loop in the AdS/CFT correspondence, using the Nambu-Goto action for bosonic fluctuations and the Green-Schwarz action for fermionic fluctuations in the Schwarzschild-AdS5 x S5 background. They evaluate the one-loop functional determinants of these fluctuations using the Gelfand-Yaglom method and perturbation theory in the small parameter ε = (2π)^{3/2} TL / Γ^2(1/4), extracting the O(λ^{-1/2}) correction to the jet quenching parameter from the ε^2 term of the Taylor expansion of the effective action.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order jet quenching parameter for N=4 SYM is given by: q̂_SYM^(0) = (π^{3/2} Γ(3/4) / Γ(5/4)) √λ T^3
- The finite coupling correction factor is: (1 + κ λ^{-1/2}) with κ = -1.97
- The previously known correction from string theory background corrections multiplies q̂_SYM^(0) by (1 - 1.765 λ^{-3/2})
- The asymptotic behavior of the determinant coefficients for large ω: ln C_1(ω) ≈ 0.61802/ω, ln C_2(ω) ≈ 6.1802/ω, ln C_3(ω) ≈ 0.92704/ω
- The asymptotic behavior for small ω: C_1(ω) ≈ 0.927/ω, C_2(ω) ≈ 26.0736/ω^5, C_3(ω) ≈ 1/(2ω^2)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order AdS/CFT calculation of the jet quenching parameter by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006), who found q̂ proportional to √λ T^3, by including the dominant finite-coupling correction from world sheet fluctuations. The correction is of order O(λ^{-1/2}) and therefore dominates over the O(λ^{-3/2}) correction from string theory corrections to the AdS-Schwarzschild background previously calculated by Armesto (2006). The result enables more precise theoretical predictions for jet quenching at the finite values of the 't Hooft coupling relevant for comparison with heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and the LHC.
