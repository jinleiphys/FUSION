# Deuteron normalization and channel-dependent formation dynamics in pion and kaon color transparency

> A combined view of the Jefferson Lab data on nuclear transparency in $A(e,e'π^+)$ and $A(e,e'K^+)$ reveals two simple but nontrivial features of the onset of color transparency. First, normalization to deuterium does not play the same role in the two reactions. In pion electroproduction, the missing-mass selection suppresses the neutron-induced $Δ$ channel so strongly that the deuteron normalization becomes effectively proton dominated. In kaon electroproduction, the nearby hyperon channels cannot be removed in the same way, and the deuteron retains a genuine proton--neutron average. Second, the $Q^2$ dependence indicates different in-medium formation dynamics. The pion transparency is well reproduced by the standard quantum diffusion model with $ΔM_π^2 \simeq 0.7~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, whereas the kaon data favor a faster geometric expansion characterized by the scale $R_K \sim \sqrt{σ_{KN}/π}$ and are strongly underestimated by the same pion-like diffusion scale. These results suggest that the pion and kaon data already contain evidence that the onset of color transparency is reaction dependent both in normalization and in propagation through nuclear matter.

# Deuteron normalization and channel-dependent formation dynamics in pion and kaon color transparency

## Key claim

The onset of color transparency in pion and kaon electroproduction is not governed by a universal effective formation law; instead, the deuteron normalization is channel-dependent (proton-dominated for pions due to missing-mass suppression of the neutron-induced Δ channel, but a genuine proton-neutron average for kaons), and the extracted in-medium formation dynamics differ, with pion data favoring the standard quantum diffusion model while kaon data favor a faster geometric expansion.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed Jefferson Lab nuclear transparency data from $A(e,e'\pi^+)$ and $A(e,e'K^+)$ reactions by comparing hydrogen-normalized transparency $T_A$ with deuteron-normalized transparency $T_{A/D}$ as a function of $Q^2$ for several nuclei. They evaluated the role of the neutron contribution ratio $X_\varphi = \sigma_n^{(\varphi)}/\sigma_p^{(\varphi)}$ for each meson channel and compared the observed $Q^2$ dependence against two phenomenological models: the standard quantum diffusion model (QDM) with formation length $l_f = 2p_\varphi/\Delta M_\varphi^2$, and a geometric expansion model (NPM) with formation length $l_f = (E_\varphi/m_\varphi)R_\varphi$ and transverse size $R_K \sim \sqrt{\sigma_{KN}/\pi}$.

## Key numbers

- Pion data are well described by the standard QDM with $\Delta M_\pi^2 \simeq 0.7~\mathrm{GeV}^2$
- The inverse-square moment of the deuteron wave function is $\left<r^{-2}\right>_D = 0.31~\mathrm{fm}^{-2}$
- For the kaon NPM description, $\sigma_{KN} = 17~\mathrm{mb}$ is used to estimate $R_K = \sqrt{\sigma_{KN}/\pi}$
- The deuteron-normalized kaon curves are evaluated with $X_K = 0.8$
- A quadratic-expansion description of pion data requires a very small effective radius $R_\pi \approx 0.06~\mathrm{fm}$, while the physical pion-size scale is $R_\pi \sim 0.64~\mathrm{fm}$ (Zhu 2021)
- Within the QDM for kaons, a comparable slope to the data is obtained only with an effective value $\Delta M_K^2 = 0.15~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ (dashed curve), far below the pion-like value of $0.7~\mathrm{GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the separate analyses of JLab pion transparency data by Qian (2010) and Choi (2025) and kaon transparency data by Nuruzzaman (2011), Das (2019), and Kong (2026) by providing a unified comparison that reveals channel-dependent normalization and formation dynamics. The finding that pion and kaon color transparency are not described by a common phenomenological formation scenario enables future work to explore how flavor dependence enters through meson structure and the excitation spectrum relevant for space-time evolution of compact color-singlet configurations in nuclear matter.
