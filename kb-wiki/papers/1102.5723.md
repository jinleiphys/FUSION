# Quark Recombination in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Data on high energy nuclear collisions collected at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider over the past decade have provided convincing evidence that hadronization is quite different in hot nuclear environments compared to p+p collisions. In particular, the data suggest that we see traces of quark degrees of freedom in elliptic flow, with the implication that collective flow is generated on the parton level and is transfered to hadrons through a simple recombination step. In this contribution we review the experimental evidence for quark recombination and discuss some recombination models which are used to describe these effects.

## Key claim

Quark recombination models provide strong evidence that collective flow is partonic in origin, but the quark-number scaling law for elliptic flow has only been proved for unrealistic space-momentum correlations in the flow field that are not consistent with hydrodynamic concepts.

## Method

The paper reviews instantaneous quark recombination models, which project the density matrix of effective constituent quarks onto hadron states via overlap integrals of Wigner functions for partons and hadrons. It also discusses the resonance recombination model (RRM) by Ravagli and Rapp, which couples mesons as resonances to a fixed quark background using a Boltzmann equation with gain and loss terms, conserving energy and momentum while enforcing detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- Proton-to-pion ratios in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC were up to a factor 4 larger than expected from e+e- or p+p collisions.
- Elliptic flow for baryons showed peak values roughly 50% larger than for mesons, following the scaling law (1/3) v2^B (3 P_T) = (1/2) v2^M (2 P_T).
- The intermediate P_T range where recombination effects dominate is roughly between 1 and 5 GeV/c.
- In the FMNB model, the crossover between recombination-dominated and fragmentation-dominated regimes occurs around 4 GeV/c for mesons and around 6 GeV/c for baryons.
- The resonance recombination model can produce negative values of v2 (e.g., in c cbar to J/psi coalescence) using quarks with strictly positive v2.

## Context

This review extends the experimental findings from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) that showed anomalous baryon enhancement and quark number scaling of elliptic flow, first reported by Adler (2003) and Adams (2003, 2004). The paper contrasts recombination models with hydrodynamic approaches (which fail to describe the phi meson behavior) and hadronic transport models (which produce similar scaling but with too small elliptic flow). It enables future work on incorporating confinement and chiral symmetry breaking into recombination models, and on testing whether quark-number scaling holds at higher collision energies at the Large Hadron Collider.
