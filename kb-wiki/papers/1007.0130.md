# Highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics for early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We introduce a new framework of highly-anisotropic hydrodynamics that includes dissipation effects. Dissipation is defined by the form of the entropy source that depends on the pressure anisotropy and vanishes for the isotropic fluid. With a simple ansatz for the entropy source obeying general physical requirements, we are led to a non-linear equation describing the time evolution of the anisotropy in purely-longitudinal boost-invariant systems. Matter that is initially highly anisotropic approaches naturally the regime of the perfect fluid. Thus, the resulting evolution agrees with the expectations about the behavior of matter produced at the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The equilibration is identified with the processes of entropy production.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a framework of highly-anisotropic hydrodynamics where dissipation is defined by an entropy source that depends on the pressure anisotropy and vanishes for isotropic fluids; the model is intended to describe the early, non-equilibrium stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the caveat that the specific form of the entropy source (and thus the dynamics) must be supplied as external input rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors propose an energy-momentum tensor that allows different longitudinal and transverse pressures, together with an entropy flux proportional to the entropy density times the flow four-vector. For a purely longitudinal, boost-invariant system, the energy-momentum conservation and entropy growth equations reduce to ordinary differential equations for the entropy density and the anisotropy parameter \(x = (\lambda_\perp/\lambda_\parallel)^2\), where \(\lambda_\perp\) and \(\lambda_\parallel\) are transverse and longitudinal temperature-like parameters. A specific ansatz for the entropy source \(\Sigma = [(1-\sqrt{x})^2/\sqrt{x}] \, \sigma / \tau_{\rm eq}\) is adopted, leading to a non-linear equation for \(x(\tau)\) that is solved numerically for initial conditions \(x_0 = 100\) and \(x_0 = 0.01\) at \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm used for all numerical evolutions.
- For initial anisotropy \(x_0 = 100\) and relaxation times \(\tau_{\rm eq} = 0.25, 0.5, 1.0\) fm, the entropy increases by about 70%, 85%, and 105%, respectively, relative to the Bjorken solution.
- For \(x_0 = 100\), the anisotropy parameter \(x\) approaches \(x \approx 1\) for \(\tau \geq 2 \tau_{\rm eq}\).
- The function \(H(x)\) for large \(x\) is approximated as \(H(x) \approx 6 x^{3/2}\); for \(|x-1| \ll 1\) the leading term is \(H(x) \approx (45/16)(x-1)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier formulations of anisotropic (magneto)hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2008, Florkowski 2009) by relaxing the assumption of isentropic flow and explicitly including entropy production. It contrasts with standard viscous (Israel-Stewart) hydrodynamics, which the authors argue may be inadequate for the very early, highly anisotropic stage, and with kinetic models that are more complicated to solve. The framework is intended to enable modeling of the continuous equilibration of pressures in heavy-ion collisions, with possible generalizations to 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions and to different forms of the entropy source inspired by microscopic mechanisms or ADS/CFT correspondence.
