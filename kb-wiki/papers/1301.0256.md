# Novel shell-model analysis of the $^{136}$Xe double beta decay nuclear matrix elements

> Neutrinoless double beta decay, if observed, could distinguish whether neutrino is a Dirac or a Majorana particle, and it could be used to determine the absolute scale of the neutrino masses. $^{136}$Xe is one of the most promising candidates for observing this rare event. However, until recently there were no positive result for the allowed and less rare two-neutrino double beta decay mode. The small nuclear matrix element associated with the small half-life represents a challenge for nuclear structure models used for its calculation. We report a new shell-model analysis of the two-neutrino double beta decay of $^{136}$Xe, which takes into account all relevant nuclear orbitals necessary to fully describe the associated Gamow-Teller strength. We further use the new model to analyze the main contributions to the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life, and show that they are also diminished.

## Key claim

The paper shows that extending the shell-model valence space to include the spin-orbit partner orbitals $0g_{9/2}$ and $0h_{9/2}$ (the $jj77$ model space) can reproduce the experimentally measured two-neutrino double beta decay half-life of $^{136}$Xe without requiring an artificially small Gamow-Teller quenching factor, and that this extension also reduces the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale configuration-interaction (shell-model) calculations using the $jj77$ model space, which adds the $0g_{9/2}$ and $0h_{9/2}$ orbitals to the standard $jj55$ space ($0g_{7/2},1d_{5/2},1d_{3/2},2s_{1/2},0h_{11/2}$). They constructed a new effective interaction ($jj77a$) using the $V_{lowk}$ method from the N$^3$LO potential, renormalized into the $jj77$ space assuming a $^{100}$Sn closed core, with single-particle energies adjusted to reproduce experimental spectra of $^{131}$Sn and $^{133}$Sb. The two-neutrino double beta decay nuclear matrix element was calculated using a strength-function approach with the NuShellX code, allowing up to one particle excited from the $0g_{9/2}$ orbital or to the $0h_{9/2}$ orbital for the $0^+$ states and up to two such excitations for the $1^+$ intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The EXO-200 measured $2\nu\beta\beta$ half-life of $^{136}$Xe is $2.11\pm0.04(stat)\pm0.21(sys)\times10^{21}$ yr, corresponding to a nuclear matrix element of 0.019 MeV$^{-1}$.
- The $2\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix element in the $jj55$ model space with standard quenching factor 0.74 is 0.062 MeV$^{-1}$; with the reduced quenching factor 0.45 it becomes 0.022 MeV$^{-1}$.
- In the $jj77$ model space with $n=1$ for $0^+$ states and $n=2$ for $1^+$ states, the $2\nu\beta\beta$ matrix element decreases to 0.020 MeV$^{-1}$, close to the experimental value, while the Ikeda sum-rule is satisfied at 84.
- The Ikeda sum-rule in the $jj55$ model space is 52, compared to the expected value of 84.
- The $0\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix element for light neutrino exchange ($M^{0\nu}_\nu$) decreases from 2.21 (SRC1) in $jj55$ to 1.46 (SRC1) in the largest $jj77$ space.
- The upper limit for the effective neutrino mass $m_{\beta\beta}$ extracted from the EXO-200 lower limit of $1.6\times10^{25}$ yr is 0.42-0.46 eV.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of double beta decay nuclear matrix elements for $^{136}$Xe, which had shown significant sensitivity to the effective interaction and required quenching factors varying from 0.74 (Caurier et al., 1996) to 0.45 (Caurier et al., 2008). The results contradict the assumption that including spin-orbit partners would increase the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements, as sometimes used to explain differences between shell-model and other methods such as QRPA (Rodin et al., 2007), IBA-2 (Barea and Iachello, 2009), PHFB, and GCM. The new calculation enables more reliable extraction of neutrino physics parameters from ongoing experiments such as EXO-200 and KamLAND-Zen.
