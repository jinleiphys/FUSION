# Chirally improved quark Pauli blocking in nuclear matter and applications to quark deconfinement in neutron stars

> The relativistic mean field (RMF) model of the nuclear matter equation of state has been modified by including the effect of Pauli-blocking owing to quark exchange between the baryons. Different schemes of a chiral enhancement of the quark Pauli blocking have been suggested according to the adopted density dependence of the dynamical quark mass. The resulting equations of state for the pressure are compared to the RMF model DD2 with excluded volume correction. On the basis of this comparison a density-dependent nucleon volume is extracted which parametrises the quark Pauli blocking effect in the respective scheme of chiral enhancement. The dependence on the isospin asymmetry is investigated and the corresponding density dependent nuclear symmetry energy is obtained in fair accordance with phenomenological constraints. The deconfinement phase transition is obtained by a Maxwell construction with a quark matter phase described within a higher order NJL model. Solutions for rotating and nonrotating (hybrid) compact star sequences are obtained which show the effect of high-mass twin compact star solutions for the rotating case.

## Key claim

The authors modify the relativistic mean field (RMF) model of nuclear matter by including quark Pauli blocking effects from baryon substructure, but the actual effect on the equation of state depends on which scheme for the density-dependent dynamical quark mass is adopted (constant, Brown-Rho scaling, or from a higher-order Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), and the resulting deconfinement transition to quark matter is constructed via a Maxwell construction that assumes a large surface tension at the hadron-quark interface.

## Method

The authors add a quark exchange contribution to the nucleon selfenergy, derived from a nonrelativistic quark potential model with harmonic oscillator confinement, to the linear Walecka (RMF) model for nuclear matter. They implement three schemes for the density dependence of the constituent quark mass: constant mass, linear Brown-Rho scaling, and a density dependence from a higher-order Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (hNJL) model. The deconfinement phase transition to quark matter, described by the hNJL model with higher-order interactions, is obtained via a Maxwell construction, and the resulting hybrid equations of state are used to compute rotating and nonrotating compact star sequences.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy \(E/A = -15.8\) MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter with constant quark mass, the Pauli blocking shift coefficients are \(a_1^{\rm (SNM)} = -197.77\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(a_2^{\rm (SNM)} = 1944.45\) MeV fm\(^5\)
- For pure neutron matter with constant quark mass, the Pauli blocking shift coefficients are \(a_1^{\rm (PNM)} = -131.85\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(a_2^{\rm (PNM)} = 3252.57\) MeV fm\(^5\)
- Adopted constituent quark mass: \(m = 350\) MeV; nucleon wave function width parameter: \(b = 0.59\) fm
- Compressibility \(K\) at saturation for the LW+Qex model (constant quark mass): 331.958 MeV; for LW+MQex (Brown-Rho scaling): 481.713 MeV; for LW+MhNJL: 582.831 MeV
- Symmetry energy \(E_s\) at saturation: 32.04 MeV (LW+Qex), 34.12 MeV (LW+MQex), 31.55 MeV (LW+MhNJL)
- Radius of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star: 13.70 km (LW+Qex), 14.40 km (LW+MQex), 14.29 km (LW+MhNJL)
- Extracted nucleon hard-core radius from comparison with excluded volume models: approximately 0.35–0.45 fm

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ropke (1986) and Blaschke (1988) on quark exchange in nuclear matter by incorporating a density-dependent quark mass for chiral improvement, and connects to the excluded volume approach of Typel (2016) by extracting a density-dependent nucleon volume from the Pauli blocking effect. The resulting hybrid star sequences with a deconfinement transition to hNJL quark matter enable the study of high-mass twin compact star solutions for rotating neutron stars, relevant for interpreting observations of massive pulsars and the hyperon puzzle.
