# Dynamic description of ternary and quaternary splits of heavy nuclear systems in the deep-inelastic regime

> Colliding heavy nuclear systems in the deep-inelastic regime may undergo partitioning into multiple fragments when fusion can not be achieved. While multiple breakups are common at Fermi energy, they are rather exotic in the deep-inelastic regime, where density, excitation and, in general, transport conditions, are expected to be different. Abundant ternary and quaternary splits have been observed in recent experiments, for instance in symmetric semi-central and semi-peripheral collisions with heavy systems, like $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ + $^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ at 15 MeV per nucleon. In these conditions, we undertook a microscopic description of the reaction dynamics. Relying on the full solution of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation implemented in the BLOB approach, we could follow in time the development of instabilities along deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ternary and quaternary splits in deep-inelastic collisions of very heavy systems like Au+Au at 15 MeV per nucleon arise from surface instabilities of Rayleigh type near nuclear saturation density, not from volume instabilities at low density as in the Fermi-energy regime; the breakup process shows an overlap between direct dynamical fragmentation and secondary non-isotropic fission, rather than two clearly separated regimes.

## Method

The authors solved the full Boltzmann-Langevin equation in three dimensions using the BLOB (Boltzmann-Langevin One Body) approach, which incorporates stochastic fluctuations from nucleon-nucleon collisions into a mean-field description. They simulated 500 to 4000 events for the system 197Au+197Au at 15 A MeV across impact parameter intervals from b=0-1 fm to b=9-10 fm, using a SKM* effective interaction with incompressibility modulus k=200 MeV. The dynamical evolution was followed until the stopping time t_stop (the time of last fragment separation), after which secondary decays were propagated using the Simon afterburner.

## Key numbers

- The system studied is 197Au+197Au at 15 A MeV (15 MeV per nucleon)
- 500 BLOB events were run for impact parameter intervals b=[0,1] through [7,8] fm; 700 events for b=[9,10] fm; 4000 events for b=[5,6] fm
- The stopping time window ranged from 200-1000 fm/c depending on impact parameter, with t_stop searched in windows of 400-1000 fm/c for b=[5,6] fm and 200-800 fm/c for b=[9,10] fm
- For semi-central collisions (b=[5,6] fm), ternary events are about three times more frequent than quaternary events at t_stop
- The mean field was sampled using N_test=40 test particles per nucleon
- The incompressibility modulus of the effective interaction is k=200 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field simulations of the same system by Rizzo (2014) using the SMF model, which could not reproduce multiple splits at this energy due to incomplete treatment of fluctuations. It also contrasts with molecular dynamics calculations by Tian (2010), which produced more compact or fractal-like configurations rather than the elongated shapes seen here. The results enable distinguishing the deep-inelastic fragmentation mechanism from Fermi-energy fragmentation studied by Colonna (2017) and others, where breakup is driven by reaching densities far below saturation rather than by surface instabilities of Rayleigh type.
