# Resonance nucleon scattering amplitude in the photonuclear reaction

> The cross section of the photonuclear reaction in the nucleon-resonance region is calculated to search the resonance-nucleon scattering amplitude in the nucleus. It is assumed that the resonance $R$ is produced and decayed in the elementary photon-nucleon $(γN)$ reaction in the nucleus as $γN \to R \to γN$. The resonance interacts with the nucleons while propagating through the nucleus. Therefore, the calculated photonuclear reaction cross section is compared with the data to extract the resonance-nucleon scattering parameters in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the photonuclear reaction cross section in the nucleon-resonance region (0.2-1.2 GeV) to extract the in-medium resonance-nucleon scattering amplitude (not just the cross section) by fitting the calculated cross section to data, under the assumption that the resonance is produced and decays via the elementary photon-nucleon reaction and interacts with nucleons while propagating through the nucleus.

## Method

The forward Compton scattering amplitude for both photonucleon and photonuclear reactions is evaluated using Lagrangians for the resonance-nucleon-photon coupling, with the measured mass and width of all four-starred resonances (except Delta(1232)) taken from the Particle Data Group. The resonance propagator is treated in the eikonal approximation of the Glauber model, and the resonance-nucleus optical potential is generated by folding the forward resonance-nucleon scattering amplitude with the nuclear density. The Fermi-motion of the bound nucleon is accounted for using the Fermi-suppression factor from Kondratyuk et al. The resonance-nucleon-photon coupling constants are first extracted by fitting the calculated photonucleon reaction cross sections to data, then the photonuclear cross sections are fitted to data to extract the in-medium resonance-nucleon scattering parameters (the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the scattering amplitude and the total cross section).

## Key numbers

*   The resonance-nucleon-photon coupling constant for the Delta(1232) resonance is f_{Delta+(1232)p gamma} = 0.114 for the proton and f_{Delta0(1232)n gamma} = 0.102 for the neutron.
*   The extracted in-medium total resonance-nucleon scattering cross section for the Delta(1232) resonance in carbon is sigma_t^{*RN} = 54.11 mb, with alpha_{RN}^* = 1.34.
*   The extracted in-medium total resonance-nucleon scattering cross section for the N(1520) resonance in carbon is sigma_t^{*RN} = 426.25 mb, with alpha_{RN}^* = 0.57.
*   The extracted in-medium total resonance-nucleon scattering cross section for the N(1680) resonance in carbon is sigma_t^{*RN} = 425.33 mb, with alpha_{RN}^* = -0.31.
*   The resonance-nucleon-photon coupling constant for the N(1520) resonance is f_{p(1520)p gamma} = 3.2 x 10^{-2} for the proton and f_{n(1520)n gamma} = 2.57 x 10^{-2} for the neutron.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kondratyuk et al., who also extracted resonance-nucleon cross sections from photonuclear data, by using the measured masses and widths of all four-starred resonances and evaluating the spin-isospin matrix elements via Lagrangians rather than relying solely on the vector-meson dominance model used by Rapp et al. The extracted in-medium scattering parameters for the Delta(1232), N(1520), and N(1680) resonances differ significantly from those reported by Kondratyuk et al., and the calculated average cross section for carbon and lead reproduces the measured average photonuclear cross section, enabling future studies of resonance-nucleon interactions in the nuclear medium.
