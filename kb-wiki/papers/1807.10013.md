# Confronting nuclear equation of state in the presence of dark matter using GW170817 observation in relativistic mean field theory approach

> We confront admixture of dark matter inside neutron star using gravitational wave constraints coming from binary neutron star merger. We consider a relativistic mean field model including $σ-ω-ρ$ meson interaction with NL3 parameterization. We study fermionic dark matter interacting with nucleonic matter via Higgs portal mechanism. We show that admixture of dark matter inside the neutron star soften the equation state and lower the value of tidal deformability. Gravitational wave GW170817 observation puts an upper bound on tidal deformability of a binary neutron star with low spin prior at 90\% confidence level, which disfavors stiff equation of state such as Walecka model with NL3 parameterization. However, we show that Walecka model with NL3 parameterization with a fermionic dark matter component satisfy the tidal deformability bound coming from the GW170817 observation.

## Key claim

The Walecka model with NL3 parameterization, which is disfavored by the GW170817 tidal deformability upper bound at 90% confidence level when considered alone, can satisfy that bound if a fermionic dark matter component with Fermi momentum between 0.02 GeV and 0.06 GeV is included inside the neutron star.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model including sigma-omega-rho meson interactions with NL3 parameterization for the nucleonic sector, and coupled it to a fermionic dark matter field interacting with nucleons via the Higgs portal mechanism with Yukawa coupling y=0.07. They solved the coupled field equations self-consistently in the mean field approximation, computed the equation of state (EoS) for beta-equilibrated neutron star matter including leptons, and then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations together with the tidal deformability differential equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for varying dark matter Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass without dark matter: 2.8 solar masses; with dark matter Fermi momentum k_F^DM = 0.06 GeV: 2.1 solar masses
- Dark matter number density taken as approximately 10^{-3} times nuclear saturation density (n_B ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}), giving dark matter Fermi momentum k_F^DM ~ 0.033 GeV; calculations varied k_F^DM from 0.02 GeV to 0.06 GeV
- Dark matter mass fraction relative to neutron star mass approximately 1/6
- Dark matter particle mass M_chi = 200 GeV; Higgs portal coupling y = 0.07; proton-Higgs form factor f = 0.35
- Tidal Love number k_2 in the range 0.09-0.13 for a 1.5 solar mass neutron star
- GW170817 upper bound on combined dimensionless tidal deformability: tilde-Lambda <= 800 at 90% confidence (low spin prior); reanalysis gives tilde-Lambda <= 1000; lower bound from kilonova models: tilde-Lambda >= 400
- Chirp mass of GW170817 fixed at M_c = 1.188 solar masses; primary mass range 1.365 < m_1/M_sun < 1.60

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dark matter inside neutron stars by Panotopoulos and Lopes (2017), who used a simpler sigma-omega RMF model that fails to reproduce nuclear saturation properties, and by Ellis et al. who considered self-interacting dark matter cores. The authors show that the NL3 parameterization, which is otherwise ruled out by the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint from Abbott et al. (2017), can be revived by including a small fermionic dark matter component. This enables future work to explore whether other stiff EoS models excluded by gravitational wave observations might similarly be reconciled with dark matter admixture, and motivates more detailed two-fluid treatments of non-uniform dark matter distributions inside neutron stars.
