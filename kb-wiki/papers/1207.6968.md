# Parity- and Time-Reversal-Violating Moments of Light Nuclei

> I present the calculation of parity- and time-reversal-violating moments of the nucleon and light nuclei, originating from the QCD theta term and effective dimension-six operators. By applying chiral effective field theory these calculations are performed in a unified framework. I argue that measurements of a few light-nuclear electric dipole moments would shed light on the mechanism of parity and time-reversal violation.

## Key claim

Measurements of a few light-nuclear electric dipole moments (EDMs) could distinguish the QCD theta term from beyond-the-Standard-Model sources of parity and time-reversal violation, but only if the pattern of EDM values across different nuclei matches the characteristic chiral-symmetry-breaking signatures of each source.

## Method

The author uses chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) to calculate parity- and time-reversal-violating moments of the nucleon, deuteron, helion (³He), and triton (³H) originating from the QCD theta term and from effective dimension-six operators (quark EDMs, quark chromo-EDMs, gluon chromo-EDM, and four-quark operators). The nuclear wave functions are obtained from modern high-quality phenomenological potentials, and the parity- and time-reversal-violating properties are computed by inserting the corresponding currents into the wave functions or by perturbing the wave functions with the parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon potential. The low-energy constants in the chiral Lagrangian are estimated using naive dimensional analysis.

## Key numbers

* Neutron EDM limit constrains the QCD vacuum angle to theta-bar < 10^{-10} (from Cre79).
* For dimension-six operators, the neutron EDM limit sets bounds: (tilde-delta)/M^2 and delta/M^2 are less than or approximately (10^5 GeV)^{-2}, and w/M^2 is less than or approximately (10^6 GeV)^{-2}.
* The deuteron EDM is given by d_d = d_n + d_p - 0.19 (gbar_1 / F_pi) e fm, where F_pi = 186 MeV.
* For the theta term, the ratio m_d * M_d / d_d (deuteron mass times magnetic quadrupole moment divided by deuteron EDM) is of order 10; for dimension-six sources the ratio is of order 1 or smaller.
* Helion EDM: d_{³He} = 0.88 d_n - 0.05 d_p - (0.15 gbar_0/F_pi + 0.28 gbar_1/F_pi) e fm.
* Triton EDM: d_{³H} = -0.05 d_n + 0.90 d_p + (0.15 gbar_0/F_pi - 0.28 gbar_1/F_pi) e fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon EDMs (Cre79; nEDMLO; nEDMNLO) and the parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon potential (TVpotential) to light nuclei, motivated by proposed storage-ring experiments (storageringexpts) that aim to measure the EDMs of the proton, deuteron, and helion with accuracy exceeding the current neutron EDM limit by two to three orders of magnitude. The calculations enable a systematic program to identify the dominant source of parity and time-reversal violation at the QCD scale by comparing the pattern of EDM values across the nucleon, deuteron, helion, and triton, and could ultimately help infer the dominant source at the electroweak scale.
