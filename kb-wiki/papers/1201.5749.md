# New variational Monte Carlo method with an energy variance extrapolation for large-scale shell-model calculations

> We propose a new variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method with an energy variance extrapolation for large-scale shell-model calculations. This variational Monte Carlo is a stochastic optimization method with a projected correlated condensed pair state as a trial wave function, and is formulated with the M-scheme representation of projection operators, the Pfaffian and the Markov-chain Monte Carlo (MCMC). Using this method, we can stochastically calculate approximated yrast energies and electro-magnetic transition strengths. Furthermore, by combining this VMC method with energy variance extrapolation, we can estimate exact shell-model energies.

# New variational Monte Carlo method with an energy variance extrapolation for large-scale shell-model calculations

## Key claim
The authors propose a variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method that, when combined with energy variance extrapolation, can estimate exact shell-model energies for large-scale problems, but the VMC alone yields only approximated yrast energies and electromagnetic transition strengths; the exact energies are obtained only in the limit of zero energy variance via extrapolation from a series of systematically approximated wave functions.

## Method
The method uses a trial wave function consisting of a projected correlated condensed pair state, with a Pfaffian formulation for overlaps and a correlation factor diagonal in the M-scheme basis. The Markov-chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling is performed on M-scheme states using either Metropolis-Hastings or Gibbs sampling algorithms, with optimization of variational parameters via stochastic reconfiguration and steepest descent. For the energy variance extrapolation, a truncation scheme based on particle-hole excitations across the shell gap is introduced, and the energy variance is computed efficiently using a reweighting factor that avoids explicit four-body interaction calculations.

## Key numbers
- For $^{56}$Ni in the $pf$ shell with the GXPF1A interaction, the M-scheme dimension is about 1.09 billion.
- The $J$-projected energy for the $J=0$ state of $^{56}$Ni after 500000 MCMC steps with 10 random walkers is -205.333 $\pm$ 0.004 MeV.
- The calculated B(E2) values with VBP wave functions are 690 $\pm$ 8 $e^2fm^4$ for $0\rightarrow 2$ and 134 $\pm$ 2 $e^2fm^4$ for $2\rightarrow 4$, using effective charges $e_{\pi}=1.5$ and $e_{\nu}=0.5$.
- Statistical errors during optimization are a few tens keV, too small to be shown in the convergence figure.

## Context
This work extends the variational Monte Carlo approach previously applied to the Hubbard model (Tahara, 2008) into nuclear shell-model calculations, and builds on earlier energy variance extrapolation techniques (Imada, 2001; Sorella, 2001) that had been applied to the nuclear shell model (ExLanc, 2009; ExMCSM, 2011). The method enables large-scale shell-model calculations beyond the limits of exact diagonalization (Caurier, 2005; Shimizu, 2010), and the authors note that the stochastic variation-after-projection (VAP) concerning J-projection could further improve accuracy. The particle-hole truncation scheme used for extrapolation is analogous to truncation schemes in the No-core Shell Model (Navratil, 2009).
