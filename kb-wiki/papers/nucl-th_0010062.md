# Electromagnetic Structure of Few-Nucleon Systems: a Critical Review

> Our current understanding of the structure of nuclei with up to A=8, including energy spectra, electromagnetic form factors, and capture reactions, is critically reviewed within the context of a realistic approach to nuclear dynamics based on two- and three-nucleon interactions and associated electromagnetic currents.

## Key claim

The "nuclear standard model" (non-relativistic nucleons with two- and three-nucleon potentials and associated electromagnetic currents) successfully predicts many properties of nuclei with A ≤ 8, but systematic discrepancies remain, particularly in the isovector magnetic form factor of trinucleons, the low-energy S-factor for pd capture, and the nd capture cross section, indicating that the model-independent two-body currents derived from the Argonne v18 interaction are too weak at moderate momentum transfers and that the doublet M1 transition strength is not correctly described.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations using the Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon potential, with wave functions obtained from Green's function Monte Carlo or variational Monte Carlo methods. Electromagnetic observables are computed with one-body currents plus model-independent two-body currents (derived from the two-nucleon potential via current conservation) and model-dependent Δ-isobar currents treated via the transition-correlation-operator scheme. For capture reactions, the continuum states are described with phenomenological cluster potentials or microscopic wave functions, and reduced matrix elements for M1 and E1 transitions are extracted.

## Key numbers

- AV18 and Bonn two-nucleon potentials alone give triton binding energies of 24.28 MeV and 26.26 MeV respectively, compared to the experimental value of 28.3 MeV.
- The calculated isoscalar magnetic moment of the trinucleons is roughly 4% too large relative to the experimental value.
- For pd capture at Ep = 40 keV, the theoretical reduced matrix element |p4| = 0.378 (full calculation) versus the fitted experimental value 0.312 ± 0.009; the ratio rE1 ≡ |p4/p2|² is ≈ 1 in theory versus ≈ 0.74 ± 0.04 from the fit.
- The nd capture cross section at thermal neutron energy is calculated as 578 μb with one- and many-body currents (AV18/UIX), which is 15% larger than the experimental value of (508 ± 15) μb.
- The doublet M1 reduced matrix element |m2| for pd capture at 40 keV is 0.322 (full theory) versus the fitted value 0.340 ± 0.010.

## Context

This review extends the program of realistic nuclear dynamics based on the AV18 potential and UIX three-nucleon force, following the work of Wiringa (2000) and the Nijmegen group (1993) on binding energies, and Friar (1977) on unitary equivalence of one-pion-exchange off-shell prescriptions. The results show that while charge form factors of ³H and ³He are reproduced excellently, the magnetic form factors and low-energy capture reactions reveal deficiencies in the two-body current model that point to needed improvements in the treatment of Δ-isobar currents and possibly electromagnetic spin-orbit interactions in continuum states. The αd capture calculation for ⁶Li production in big bang nucleosynthesis is identified as a key open problem requiring a full six-body scattering calculation from the realistic Hamiltonian.
