# Overview and Perspectives in Nuclear Physics

> This presentation reviews recent guiding themes in the broad context of nuclear physics, from developments in chiral effective field theory applied to nuclear systems, via the phases and structures of QCD, to matter under extreme conditions in heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.

# Overview and Perspectives in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
This review presents a personal selection of guiding themes in nuclear physics, from chiral effective field theory to matter under extreme conditions, with the author explicitly acknowledging that the overview is unavoidably influenced by personal selection and apologizing for omissions.

## Method
The paper surveys developments across multiple subfields of nuclear physics: chiral effective field theory applied to nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions, in-medium chiral perturbation theory for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, lattice QCD studies of the nucleon-nucleon force and nucleon structure, experimental measurements of hadron properties in nuclear matter, and results from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and neutron star observations. The review connects these topics through the unifying framework of QCD phases and structures, emphasizing the role of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for the QCD hadron-to-quark-gluon phase transition: $T_c \sim 0.2$ GeV
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi \simeq 0.09$ GeV
- Physical pion mass: $m_\pi = 0.14$ GeV
- Spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale: $4\pi f_\pi \sim 1$ GeV
- Empirical nuclear matter saturation density: $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3} \simeq 0.45\,m_\pi^3$
- Nuclear matter Fermi momentum at saturation: $p_F \simeq 0.26$ GeV
- Critical temperature for nuclear liquid-gas transition from in-medium chiral effective field theory: $T_c \simeq 15$ MeV (compared to empirical range $T_c \sim 16-18$ MeV)
- Ratio of differential cross sections for $pn$ versus $pp$ short-range correlations on $^{12}C$ at missing momenta 0.3-0.6 GeV/c: $d\sigma(e,e'pn)/ d\sigma(e,e'pp) = 18\pm 4$
- Quark spin contribution to nucleon spin: $\Delta\Sigma \simeq 0.3 - 0.4$
- Quark orbital angular momentum contribution from lattice QCD: $|L_q| \lesssim 0.04$
- Proton form factor ratio $\mu_P G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)$ drops to 0.5 at about $Q^2 = 0.4$ GeV$^2$
- Initial energy densities at RHIC: $10 - 20$ GeV$\cdot$fm$^{-3}$
- Empirical chemical freeze-out temperature at zero chemical potential: about 160 MeV
- Canonical neutron star mass from double neutron star binaries: $1.4\, M_\odot$

## Context
This review extends the program of Taketani (1951) on the separation-of-scales hierarchy for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, now realized through chiral effective field theory. It connects to lattice QCD developments by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2007) on the NN potential, and to in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations by Finelli, Kaiser, Weise, and Vretenar (2005-2006) for nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The review enables future work on extrapolating nuclear properties to extreme isospin for astrophysical applications (nucleogenesis and r-process), and frames open questions about the QCD phase diagram, the nucleon spin puzzle, and the equation of state of neutron star matter at densities relevant to stars potentially reaching $2\, M_\odot$.
