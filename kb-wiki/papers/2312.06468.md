# Constraints on initial baryon stopping and equation of state from directed flow

> Our investigation focuses on the rapidity-dependent directed flow, $v_1(y)$, of identified hadrons in Au+Au collisions across a broad range of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ from 7.7 to 200 GeV. Employing a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework, our study successfully reproduces the characteristic features of the measured $v_1(y)$ for both mesons and baryons across the considered beam energies. Notably, our analysis reveals the constraining power of baryonic $v_1(y)$ on the initial baryon stopping mechanism. Together with mesonic $v_1(y)$, the directed flow serves as a crucial tool for probing the equation of state governing dense nuclear matter at finite chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the sign change in the slope of proton directed flow near midrapidity around 10-20 GeV is a consequence of the initial baryon stopping mechanism, not evidence of a first-order phase transition as previously proposed.

## Method

The authors employ a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework that starts hydrodynamic evolution at a constant proper time with parametric initial conditions, then transitions to a kinetic transport description at a given local energy density. They introduce a rapidity-independent plateau component in the initial baryon density profile, alongside a transverse-position-dependent longitudinal shift, to simultaneously reproduce the measured net-proton rapidity distributions and the directed flow of identified hadrons across beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 7.7 to 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality: 10-40% Au+Au collisions
- Without the plateau component, baryon directed flow reaches a maximum value of |v1|max ≈ 0.07 around |y| = 1 at 19.6 GeV, significantly overshooting observed data
- The plateau component dominates the midrapidity region at 62.4 and 200 GeV, producing nearly flat v1(y) for baryons within |y| ≲ 1
- At 7.7 GeV, the baryon v1(y) begins declining beyond |y| ≳ 0.7, contrasting with the measured trend that continues to rise

## Context

This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Du (2022) and Shen (2020) by introducing a novel plateau component in the initial baryon profile, which the authors link to the string junction conjecture proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and Sjostrand (2002). The results contradict the earlier proposal by Stoecker (2004) that the non-monotonic behavior of dv1/dy near midrapidity indicates a first-order phase transition. The framework enables future Bayesian model selection using upcoming Beam Energy Scan phase II measurements to discriminate between different initial baryon stopping mechanisms.
