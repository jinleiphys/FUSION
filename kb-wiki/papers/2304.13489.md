# On Sequential Single-Pion Production in Double-Pionic Fusion

> Recently a two-step process has been proposed for the double-pionic fusion to deuterium $pn (I=0) \to dπ^+π^-$. Its calculation is solely based on total cross section data for the two sequential single-pion production steps $pn (I=0)\to ppπ^-$ followed by $pp \to dπ^+$. Though this sequential process was aimed to explain the dibaryon resonance $d^*(2380)$ peak in double-pionic fusion, we demonstrate that this is not the case. It rather fits to a possible broad bump at 2.31 GeV in the energy dependence of the $pn \to dπ^0π^0$ reaction, which was recently interpreted as a consequence of dibaryonic excitations in isoscalar single-pion production.

# On Sequential Single-Pion Production in Double-Pionic Fusion

## Key claim
The two-step sequential process proposed by Molina, Ikeno and Oset (2023) for double-pionic fusion to deuterium cannot explain the dibaryon resonance d*(2380) peak when fed with proper experimental data and partial-wave constraints, but it may account for a smaller, broader bump at 2.31 GeV observed in the pn → dπ⁰π⁰ reaction.

## Method
The authors evaluated the two-step reaction chain pn(I=0) → ppπ⁻ followed by pp → dπ⁺ by replacing the artificially narrowed Breit-Wigner parametrization used by Molina, Ikeno and Oset (2023) with the actual partial-wave analysis results from ASnew (2023) for the pn(³D₃) → NN(¹D₂)π cross section. They also corrected the pp → dπ⁺ cross section by using only the ¹D₂P partial wave contribution (62% of the total) from the SAID partial-wave analysis, rather than the total cross section parametrization used in the original proposal.

## Key numbers
- The d*(2380) resonance has a pole at 2.38 GeV with a width of 70 MeV, observed in isoscalar NNππ channels.
- The isoscalar single-pion production cross section peaks at 2.31(1) GeV with a width of 150(10) MeV, not at 2.33–2.34 GeV with 70–80 MeV width as claimed by Molina, Ikeno and Oset (2023).
- Only 1/3 of the strength in the isoscalar pp invariant-mass spectrum lies above the 2.015 GeV threshold for the step-2 reaction pp → dπ⁺.
- The ¹D₂ partial wave contributes only a few percent to the total cross section of the step-1 reaction pn(I=0) → ppπ⁻.
- The corrected two-step calculation yields a peak cross section of about 0.02 mb for a structure around 2.32 GeV with width ~150 MeV, compared to the d*(2380) peak cross section which is an order of magnitude larger.
- The ¹D₂P partial wave accounts for 62% of the total pp → dπ⁺ cross section in the energy range 2.015–2.30 GeV.

## Context
This work refutes the alternative explanation of the d*(2380) dibaryon resonance proposed by Molina, Ikeno and Oset (2023), who argued that a two-step sequential process could reproduce the resonance peak without invoking a genuine dibaryon state. The authors instead support the interpretation of Bashkanov and Clement (2023) that the small bump at 2.32 GeV in the pn → dπ⁰π⁰ channel arises from dibaryonic excitations in isoscalar single-pion production, specifically from ³S₁–³D₁ and ¹P₁ partial waves with N*N structure involving Roper resonance decay. The analysis enables future photoproduction measurements at MAMI to distinguish between the sequential process and genuine dibaryon interpretations through polarization observables.
