# Energy dependence of hard bremsstrahlung production in proton-proton collisions in the Delta(1232) region

> Hard bremsstrahlung production in proton-proton collisions has been studied with the ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Juelich in the energy range of 353-800 MeV by detecting the final proton pair {pp}_s from the pp -> {pp}_s reaction with very low excitation energy. Differential cross sections were measured at small diproton c.m. angles from 0 to 20 degrees and the average over this angular interval reveals a broad peak at a beam energy around 650 MeV with a FWHM of about 220 MeV, suggesting the influence of Delta(1232)N intermediate states. Comparison with deuteron photodisintegration shows that the cross section for diproton production is up to two orders of magnitude smaller, due largely to differences in the selection rules.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the near-forward differential cross section for the $pp\to\{pp\}_s\gamma$ reaction reveals a broad peak around 650 MeV beam energy with a FWHM of about 220 MeV, suggesting the influence of $\Delta(1232)N$ intermediate states, though the peak is shifted approximately 80 MeV higher than the analogous peak in $pn\to d\gamma$ because the $S$-wave $M1$ transition that dominates the deuteron case is forbidden for the diproton.

## Method

The experiment used the ANKE magnetic spectrometer at COSY-Jülich with a hydrogen cluster-jet target. Two coincident protons were identified via time-of-flight and momentum reconstruction, and events with missing mass consistent with zero and diproton excitation energy $E_{pp}<3$ MeV were selected as $pp\to\{pp\}_s\gamma$ events. Differential cross sections were measured at six beam energies (353, 500, 550, 625, 700, and 800 MeV) for diproton center-of-mass angles $0^\circ$ to $20^\circ$, with the $\gamma$ signal extracted from missing-mass-squared distributions via fits that included Monte Carlo simulations of detector response.

## Key numbers

- Average differential cross section $\mathrm{d}\sigma/\mathrm{d}\Omega_{(0-20)}$ at 353 MeV: $5.1\pm0.5$ nb/sr
- Average differential cross section at 500 MeV: $17.9\pm1.4$ nb/sr
- Average differential cross section at 550 MeV: $33.5\pm3.0$ nb/sr
- Average differential cross section at 625 MeV: $46.5\pm9.7$ nb/sr
- Average differential cross section at 700 MeV: $37.9\pm3.5$ nb/sr
- Average differential cross section at 800 MeV: $17.0\pm9.9$ nb/sr
- Peak in energy dependence occurs near 650 MeV beam energy with FWHM $\approx 220$ MeV
- Ratio of $pn\to d\gamma$ to $pp\to\{pp\}_s\gamma$ cross sections drops from over 100 at low energies to about 20 at 800 MeV
- Angular dependence at 700 MeV: $34\pm5$ nb/sr ($0^\circ-7^\circ$), $37\pm4$ nb/sr ($7^\circ-12^\circ$), $42\pm6$ nb/sr ($12^\circ-20^\circ$)

## Context

This work extends earlier ANKE measurements at 353, 500, and 550 MeV (Komarov et al., 2008) and CELSIUS data at 310 MeV (Johansson et al., 2009) to higher energies, revealing for the first time a clear maximum in the $\Delta(1232)$ region. The results contrast with $^3\mathrm{He}(\gamma,pp)n$ photodisintegration studies (Emura et al., 1994; Tedeschi et al., 1994) that showed little indication of $\Delta$ excitation, and the observed peak shift relative to $pn\to d\gamma$ (Crawford et al., 1996) supports the interpretation that the $M1$ transition is forbidden for the diproton while $P$-wave $\Delta N$ configurations require extra energy. The data provide a benchmark for theoretical models, challenging existing coupled-channel calculations (Wilkin, 1995) that predicted no $\Delta N$ signal, and motivate further measurements of angular distributions and spin correlation parameters at ANKE.
