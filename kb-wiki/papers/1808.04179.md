# Strange matter and kaon to pion ratio in SU(3) PNJL model

> The behavior of strange matter in the frame of the SU(3)Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including $U_A(1)$ anomaly is considered. We discuss the appearance of a peak in the ratio of the number of strange mesons to non-strange mesons known as the "horn". The PNJL model gives a schematic description of the chiral phase transition and meson properties at finite temperature and density. Using the model, we can show that the splitting of kaon and anti-kaon masses appears as a result of introduction of density. This may explain the difference in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio and $K^-/π^-$ ratio at low $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ and their tendency to the same value at high $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. We also show that the rise in the ratio $K^+/π^+$ appears near CEP when we build the $K^+/π^+$ ratio along the phase transition diagram and it can be considered as a critical region signal.

# Strange matter and kaon to pion ratio in SU(3) PNJL model

## Key claim

The SU(3) Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can qualitatively reproduce the "horn" structure in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio as a function of collision energy, but only when the strange quark chemical potential differs from the light quark chemical potential (Case II: $\mu_s = 0.55 \mu_u$); when all chemical potentials are equal (Case I: $\mu_u = \mu_d = \mu_s$), the enhancement in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio is absent. The peak in the ratio appears near the critical endpoint (CEP) and may serve as a critical region signal.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) PNJL model with scalar-pseudoscalar interaction and the t'Hooft interaction that breaks $U_A(1)$ symmetry, in the mean-field approximation. Meson masses (pion, kaon, and their antiparticles) are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation at zero momentum, and number densities for $K^\pm$ and $\pi^\pm$ are computed from Bose-Einstein distributions using the calculated meson masses and chemical potentials. The $K^+/\pi^+$ and $K^-/\pi^-$ ratios are evaluated along the chiral phase transition line in the $T-\mu_B$ plane, with experimental data rescaled into the variable $T/\mu_B$ using the Cleymans parametrization of freeze-out conditions.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint (CEP) for Case I: $\mu_{B, CEP} = 0.99$ GeV, $T_{CEP} = 0.1$ GeV
- Critical endpoint (CEP) for Case II ($\mu_s = 0.55 \mu_u$): $\mu_{B, CEP} = 0.972$ GeV, $T_{CEP} = 0.11$ GeV
- Crossover transition temperature at $\mu_B = 0$: $T_c = 0.218$ GeV (higher than the Lattice QCD value $T_c = 154(9)$ MeV from Bazavov)
- Mott temperatures at zero chemical potential: $T^\pi_{Mott} = 0.232$ GeV, $T^K_{Mott} = 0.23$ GeV
- Model parameters: current quark masses $m_{0u} = m_{0d} = 5.5$ MeV, $m_{0s} = 0.131$ GeV; cutoff $\Lambda = 0.652$ GeV; couplings $g_S = 4.3$ GeV$^{-5}$, $g_D = 89.9$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Pion chemical potential fixed at $\mu_\pi = 0.135$ GeV
- Cleymans parametrization coefficients: $a = 0.166 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $b = 0.139 \pm 0.016$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c = 0.053 \pm 0.021$ GeV$^{-3}$, $d = 1.308 \pm 0.028$ GeV, $e = 0.273 \pm 0.008$ GeV$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the "horn" structure in $K/\pi$ ratios observed by the NA49 Collaboration and later at RHIC-BES, which had been interpreted as a possible signal of deconfinement onset. The authors build on earlier PNJL model calculations by Costa et al. (2009) and Fu et al. (2008), and on transport model studies by Bratkovskaya et al. that incorporated partial chiral symmetry restoration. The key advance is showing that within the PNJL model, the $K^+/\pi^+$ enhancement is sensitive to the strange quark chemical potential and localizes near the CEP, suggesting the peak could serve as a critical region signal. The results enable future work to test this hypothesis by including vector interactions in the PNJL model (which can shift or remove the CEP) and by exploring different medium compositions (beta-equilibrium matter, strange matter, etc.).
