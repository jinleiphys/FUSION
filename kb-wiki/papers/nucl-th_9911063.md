# Origin of Relativistic Effects in the Reaction D(e,e'p)n at GeV Energies

> In a series of recent publications, a new approach to the non-relativistic reduction of the electromagnetic current operator in calculations of electro-nuclear reactions has been introduced. In one of these papers, the conjecture that at energies of a few GeV, the bulk of the relativistic effects comes from the current and not from the nuclear dynamics was made, based on the large relativistic effects in the transverse-longitudinal response. Here, we explicitly compare a fully relativistic, manifestly covariant calculation performed with the Gross equation, with a calculation that uses a non-relativistic wave function and a fully relativistic current operator. We find very good agreement up to missing momenta of 400 MeV/c, thus confirming the previous conjecture. We discuss slight deviations in cross sections for higher missing momenta and their possible origin, namely p-wave contributions and off-shell effects.

## Key claim

The authors confirm the conjecture that at energies of a few GeV, the bulk of relativistic effects in electro-nuclear reactions comes from the electromagnetic current operator rather than from nuclear dynamics, based on very good agreement between a fully relativistic calculation and a calculation using a non-relativistic wave function with a fully relativistic current operator for missing momenta up to 400 MeV/c, with slight deviations at higher missing momenta attributed to p-wave contributions and off-shell effects.

## Method

The authors performed a direct comparison of two plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations for the D(e,e'p)n reaction: a fully relativistic, manifestly covariant calculation using the Gross equation (Model R), and a calculation using a non-relativistic Argonne V18 wave function with the full relativistic current operator (Model A1). They also compared with a calculation using the strict non-relativistic reduction of the current (Model NR) and with calculations using the Gross S-wave and D-wave components plus the full relativistic current (Model G1). The comparison was carried out for fixed momentum transfer q = 1.4 GeV/c at various energy transfers and for fixed outgoing proton kinetic energy of 1 GeV in parallel and perpendicular kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Very good agreement between fully relativistic calculation (Model R) and AV18 plus full relativistic current (Model A1) was found for missing momenta up to 400 MeV/c
- At fixed q = 1.4 GeV/c and ω = 0.75 GeV (quasi-free conditions), the fully relativistic and AV18 plus full relativistic current calculations agreed nicely, with the fully relativistic curve slightly lower for momenta around 350 MeV/c and slightly higher for momenta above 500 MeV/c
- At fixed q = 1.4 GeV/c and ω = 0.94 GeV (above quasi-elastic peak), the AV18 plus full relativistic current result dropped off relative to the fully relativistic result for missing momenta above 400 MeV/c
- In perpendicular kinematics with fixed outgoing proton kinetic energy of 1 GeV, the fully relativistic and AV18 plus exact current results agreed up to pm = 450 MeV/c, then began to diverge
- The non-relativistic current calculation (Model NR) differed significantly from relativistic results starting from the lowest missing momenta
- The PWIA and PWBA results differed by only 2% to 3% at most for the kinematics considered
- The P-wave contributions in the fully relativistic calculation had an effect only at the highest energy transfer (ω = 0.94 GeV), slightly increasing the result

## Context

This work extends a series of recent publications introducing a new approach to non-relativistic reduction of the electromagnetic current operator in electro-nuclear reactions, specifically testing the conjecture from Jeschonnek and Van Orden (1999) that relativistic effects at GeV energies come primarily from the current rather than nuclear dynamics. The comparison uses the Gross equation approach for the fully relativistic calculation and the Argonne V18 potential for the non-relativistic wave function, both of which give good fits to NN scattering data. The results enable the use of non-relativistic wave functions with fully relativistic current operators for calculations of electro-nuclear reactions at GeV energies, which is particularly valuable for medium and heavy nuclei where fully relativistic treatments are not available.
