# On the Couplings of the Rho Meson in AdS/QCD

> We argue that in generic AdS/QCD models (confining gauge theories dual to string theory on a weakly-curved background), the couplings g_{rho HH} of any rho meson to any hadron H are quasi-universal, lying within a narrow band near m(rho)^2/f(rho). The argument relies upon the fact that the rho is the lowest-lying state created by a conserved current, and the detailed form of the integrals which determine the couplings in AdS/QCD. Quasi-universality holds even when rho-dominance is violated. The argument fails for all other hadrons, except the lowest-lying spin-two hadron created by the energy-momentum tensor. Explicit examples are discussed.

## Key claim

In generic AdS/QCD models (confining gauge theories dual to string theory on a weakly-curved background), the couplings \(g_{\rho HH}\) of any \(\rho\) meson to any hadron \(H\) are quasi-universal, lying within a narrow band near \(m_\rho^2/f_\rho\), even when rho-dominance is violated; the argument fails for all other hadrons except the lowest-lying spin-two hadron created by the energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors compute form factors and couplings in AdS/QCD by integrating the five-dimensional wave function of a gauge boson (the non-normalizable mode for the form factor, or the normalizable mode for the \(\rho\) meson) against the current built from the ten-dimensional wave functions of the hadron \(H\). They estimate the integrals using the properties that the \(\rho\) is the lowest cavity mode of a five-dimensional gauge boson, has a structureless positive-definite wave function, and satisfies Neumann boundary conditions at the infrared cutoff \(z_{max}\). Explicit checks are performed in the hard-wall model and the D3/D7 model.

## Key numbers

* In the D3/D7 model, for the \(|2,9\rangle\) state, the terms \(f_n g_{n,9,9}^{(2)}/ m_n^2\) for \(n=0,1,2,3,4\) are \(1.49,\ -1.21,\ 1.23,\ -1.12,\ 1.08\) respectively, showing that rho-dominance fails but the \(\rho\) coupling remains near 1.
* In the hard-wall model, the combination \(f_0 g_{0aa}^\Delta / m_0^2\) for scalar states \(|\Delta,a\rangle\) with all \(\Delta<40\) lies in a narrow range near 1 (shown in Fig. 1).
* The \(\rho\) meson mass and decay constant satisfy \(f_0/m_0^2 = (1/g_5) \int dz\ \mu_0(z) \varphi_0(z) \sim (1/g_5) \hat\mu_0 \hat\varphi_0 \delta z\), with \(\delta z \sim z_{max}/2\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing "rho-coupling universality" conjecture (Sakurai, 1960s) by providing a new AdS/QCD argument that does not rely on rho-dominance or hidden local symmetry (Bando et al., 1985). It builds on earlier AdS/QCD calculations of hadronic couplings (Hong, Yoon, Strassler, 2004) and the AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena, 1998; Witten, 1998). The results enable future tests of whether similar quasi-universality holds in QCD at finite 't Hooft coupling, potentially through lattice simulations of large-\(N\) (quenched) QCD-like theories.
