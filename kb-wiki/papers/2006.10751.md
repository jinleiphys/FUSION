# Asymmetric jet clustering in deep-inelastic scattering

> We propose a new jet algorithm for deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) that accounts for the forward-backward asymmetry in the Breit frame. The Centauro algorithm is longitudinally invariant and can cluster jets with Born kinematics, which enables novel studies of transverse-momentum-dependent observables. Furthermore, we show that spherically-invariant algorithms in the Breit frame give access to low-energy jets from current fragmentation. We propose novel studies in unpolarized, polarized, and nuclear DIS at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new jet algorithm for deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) that is longitudinally invariant along the Breit frame beam axis but can cluster jets close to the Born configuration, which standard longitudinally-invariant algorithms cannot do because they cannot form jets enclosing the backward beam direction (y = -∞). The algorithm is specifically designed to account for the forward-backward asymmetry in the Breit frame.

## Method

The authors introduce the Centauro algorithm, which modifies the distance measure of spherically-invariant e+e- algorithms by replacing the angular variable with an asymmetric function f(η̄) that behaves like the angle in the backward region but diverges in the forward region, preventing jets from enclosing the proton beam direction. They simulate DIS events with Q > 10 GeV using Pythia 8 with 10 and 100 GeV electron and proton beam energies, and compare jet clustering results from the Centauro algorithm against longitudinally-invariant anti-kT and spherically-invariant anti-kT algorithms. They also perform a perturbative calculation of the Centauro jet energy spectrum at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy matched to leading-order DGLAP evolution, supplemented with a nonperturbative shape function.

## Key numbers

- Q > 10 GeV: minimum virtuality of the photon in simulated DIS events
- Particles with |η| > 4 or pT < 200 MeV in the laboratory frame are excluded from the analysis
- The jet energy scale uncertainty at HERA reached 1% for high-pT jets
- The qT = pjet⊥/zjet spectrum for zjet > 0.5 peaks at qT < Qmin/4, which is ideal for TMD phenomenology
- For spherically-invariant anti-kT jets with zjet < 0.5, the qT spectrum peaks at qT ∼ Q

## Context

This work extends the longitudinally-invariant kT-type algorithms (Ellis 1993, Dokshitzer 1997, Wobisch 1998, Cacciari 2008) that cannot cluster jets enclosing the backward beam direction in the Breit frame, and the spherically-invariant algorithms first adapted to DIS by Catani (1992). The Centauro algorithm enables novel studies of transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) observables in the Breit frame, which were previously proposed using the Born configuration (Gutierrez-Reyes 2018, 2019). The paper also proposes measurements of the zjet spectrum at HERA (not previously done) and at the future Electron-Ion Collider, as well as studies of quark TMDs, the strong coupling constant from n-jet rates, and cold-nuclear matter effects.
