# Properties of odd nuclei and the impact of time-odd mean fields: A systematic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock analysis

> We present a systematic analysis of the description of odd nuclei by the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach augmented with pairing in BCS approximation and blocking of the odd nucleon. Current and spin densities in the Skyrme functional produce time-odd mean fields (TOMF) for odd nuclei. Their effect on basic properties (binding energies, odd-even staggering, separation energies and spectra) is investigated for the three Skyrme parameterizations SkI3, SLy6, and SV-bas. About 1300 spherical and axially-deformed odd nuclei with 16 < Z < 92 are considered. The calculations demonstrate that the TOMF effect is generally small, although not fully negligible. The influence of the Skyrme parameterization and the consistency of the calculations are much more important. With a proper choice of the parameterization, a good description of binding energies and their differences is obtained, comparable to that for even nuclei. The description of low-energy excitation spectra of odd nuclei is of varying quality depending on the nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper systematically demonstrates that time-odd mean fields (TOMF) from current and spin densities in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach have a generally small effect on binding energies, odd-even staggering, separation energies, and spectra of odd nuclei, with the influence of the Skyrme parameterization and calculation consistency being much more important; the TOMF effect on binding energies never exceeds 1 MeV and is usually much smaller.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing and blocking of the odd nucleon for about 1282 spherical and axially-deformed odd nuclei with 16 ≤ Z ≤ 92, using three Skyrme parameterizations (SkI3, SLy6, and SV-bas) with effective masses m*/m = 0.58, 0.69, and 0.9 respectively. They compared three options for the Skyrme functional: one using only time-even densities, one adding the minimal Galilean-invariance-restoring time-odd terms (current j and spin-current coupling), and one further including the spin-squared terms s². The calculations were performed in cylindrical coordinate space with 1 fm mesh, and axial equilibrium deformations were determined by total energy minimization.

## Key numbers

- TOMF effect on binding energies never exceeds 1 MeV across the entire nuclear chart (16 ≤ Z ≤ 92), and is usually much smaller
- For Sn isotopes, inclusion of spin terms (min+s² option) reduces binding by 0.1-0.3 MeV relative to the minimal Galilean-invariant option
- R.m.s. deviation for binding energies of all nuclei (even + odd) with SV-bas is 2.83 MeV for the min+s² option, compared to 3.66 MeV for SkI3 and 4.15 MeV for SLy6
- R.m.s. deviation for neutron pairing gaps Δ⁽³⁾_N with SV-bas is 0.36 MeV for the min+s² option, compared to 1.01 MeV for SkI3 and 0.60 MeV for SLy6
- R.m.s. deviation for proton pairing gaps Δ⁽³⁾_Z with SV-bas is 0.35 MeV for the min+s² option, compared to 0.82 MeV for SkI3 and 0.66 MeV for SLy6

## Context

This work extends previous SHF studies of TOMF in odd nuclei that were limited to light nuclei and a few Skyrme forces (SIII and SLy4) by Satula (1999) and Passler (1976), as well as a recent SHF study of rare-earth odd nuclei by Schunck (2010). It also compares with RMF studies by Afanasjev (2010) that found TOMF effects in light nuclei near the proton drip-line. The systematic coverage of 1282 odd nuclei from sulfur to uranium enables a global assessment of TOMF impact and provides a benchmark for future improvements in nuclear density functional theory, particularly for astrophysical applications requiring accurate binding and separation energies.
