# A possible determination of the quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter

> We calculate the differential Drell-Yan production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions by including both next-to-leading order perturbative effects and effects of the nuclear medium. We demonstrate that dilepton production in fixed target experiments is an excellent tool to study initial-state parton energy loss in large nuclei and to accurately determine the stopping power of cold nuclear matter. We provide theoretical predictions for the attenuation of the Drell-Yan cross section at large values of Feynman $x_F$ and show that for low proton beam energies experimental measurements at Fermilab's E906 can clearly distinguish between nuclear shadowing and energy loss effects. If confirmed by data, our results may help determine the quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter $X_0 \sim 10^{-13}$ m.

# A possible determination of the quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter

## Key claim

If confirmed by data from Fermilab's E906 experiment, the authors' calculations suggest that the quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter is approximately $X_0 \sim 10^{-13}$ m, which would be the shortest radiation length in nature. The determination depends on the ability to distinguish initial-state energy loss from nuclear shadowing effects at low proton beam energies ($E_{\rm beam} = 120$ GeV).

## Method

The authors calculate the differential Drell-Yan production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD, incorporating both leading-twist nuclear shadowing effects via nuclear parton distributions and initial-state parton energy loss via medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung. The energy loss is implemented by convolving parton distribution functions with a probability distribution $P_{q,g}(\epsilon)$ for fractional energy loss, derived from the differential bremsstrahlung spectrum in the Poisson approximation. The radiation length $X_0$ is extracted from the relation $-dE/dx = E/X_0$ in the small energy loss limit.

## Key numbers

- Quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter: $X_0 \sim 10^{-13}$ m (approximately 30 fm to 160 fm for 100 GeV quarks, depending on the momentum transfer parameter $\xi$)
- Momentum transfer per interaction with the medium: $\xi = 0.175$ GeV to $\xi = 0.5$ GeV, inducing approximately five-fold uncertainty in the stopping power
- Gluon mean free path: $\lambda_g = 1$ fm
- Proton beam energy for E906: $E_{\rm beam} = 120$ GeV, corresponding to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 15$ GeV
- Invariant mass squared of lepton pairs: $M^2 = Q^2 \geq 10$ GeV$^2$
- Drell-Yan cross section attenuation at $x_F = 0.9$ shows approximately linear dependence on nuclear size $A^{1/3}$
- Anticipated accuracy for constraining the radiation length: approximately 20%

## Context

This work extends theoretical developments in initial-state parton energy loss by Vitev (2007), which showed that initial-state radiative energy loss in QCD retains characteristics of induced electromagnetic bremsstrahlung and can be described by a radiation length. The authors aim to resolve the ambiguity between nuclear shadowing (parameterized by Eskola (1998)) and energy loss effects in explaining the suppression of Drell-Yan production observed in Fermilab E772/E866 experiments by Johnson (2000). The predictions are designed for the upcoming Fermilab E906 experiment (Reimer, 2007), which at low beam energy can cleanly separate these competing effects and enable the first precise determination of the quark radiation length in cold nuclear matter.
