# Pairing in high-density neutron matter including short- and long-range correlations

> The influence of short-range correlations (SRC) on the spectral distribution of neutrons is incorporated in the solution of the gap equation for the ${}^3P_2-{}^3F_2$ coupled channel in pure neutron matter at high density. This effect is studied for three different realistic interactions. The gap in this channel is strongly suppressed by these correlations but does not vanish. For a consistent treatment we also include for the first time the effect of long-range correlations (LRC) by incorporating polarization terms in addition to the bare interaction. This allows the neutrons to exchange density and spin fluctuations governed by the strength of Landau parameters with values that are consistent with the available literature. While these LRC have an antiscreening tendency, they only slightly increase the gap in the ${}^3P_2-{}^3F_2$ coupled channel for all three realistic interactions as long as SRC are included. All three interactions generate maximum gaps around 0.1 to 0.2 MeV at most with a small dependence on the hardness of the interaction. These results are relevant for the cooling scenarios of neutron stars, in particular the young neutron star in Cassiopeia A.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) strongly suppress the neutron pairing gap in the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) coupled channel at high density, but a small nonvanishing gap remains; long-range correlations (LRC) provide a slight antiscreening enhancement, but only when SRC are already included.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equation for the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) coupled channel in pure neutron matter, using three realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (AV18, CDBonn, and N3LO). Short-range correlations were incorporated by replacing the BCS quasiparticle energy with a convolution of self-consistently calculated normal spectral functions, obtained from a finite-temperature ladder-diagram summation extrapolated to zero temperature. Long-range correlations were added by including polarization terms (density and spin fluctuations) in the pairing interaction, using Landau parameters extrapolated from Cao (2006) to higher densities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) gaps with SRC only: 0.04 MeV (AV18), 0.07 MeV (CDBonn), about 0.1 MeV (N3LO).
- Maximum \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) gaps with SRC and LRC combined: about 0.08 MeV (AV18), 0.1 MeV (CDBonn), about 0.2 MeV (N3LO).
- Density range for maximum gaps with SRC and LRC: Fermi momentum \(k_F \approx 1.7\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to \(k_F \approx 1.9\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- BCS-only maximum gaps (no SRC or LRC) for the \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel: 1.46 MeV (AV18), 2.68 MeV (CDBonn), 1.33 MeV (N3LO).
- For the \(^1S_0\) channel with SRC and LRC, all three interactions yield a maximum gap of about 1.8 MeV at \(k_F \approx 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Muther (2005), who showed that SRC suppress the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) gap in symmetric nuclear matter, to the neutron \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel at high density. It also generalizes the LRC treatment of Cao (2006) for the \(^1S_0\) channel to the coupled \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) channel for the first time. The resulting small gaps (0.1–0.2 MeV) are relevant for neutron-star cooling scenarios, particularly the young neutron star in Cassiopeia A, and provide a consistent input for future cooling calculations that also require a consistent equation of state.
