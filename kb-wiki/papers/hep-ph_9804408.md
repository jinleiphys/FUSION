# Flavor Asymmetry of the Sea Quarks in the Baryon Octet

> We show that the chiral $SU(n)\otimes SU(n)$ flavor symmetry on the null-plane severely restricts the sea quarks in the baryon octet. It predicts large asymmetry for the light sea quarks $(u,d,s)$, and universality and abundance for the heavy sea quarks. Further it is shown that existence of the heavy sea quarks constrained by the same symmetry reduces the theoretical value of the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule substantially.

# Flavor Asymmetry of the Sea Quarks in the Baryon Octet

## Key claim
The chiral SU(n)⊗SU(n) flavor symmetry on the null-plane severely restricts the sea quark content of the baryon octet, predicting large flavor asymmetry for light sea quarks (u, d, s) and universal, abundant heavy sea quarks, provided the pomeron is assumed to be flavor singlet and the regularization scheme using a soft pomeron with intercept 1.0808 is adopted.

## Method
The author derives sum rules from current anticommutation relations on the null-plane, extending the modified Gottfried sum rule formalism to the full baryon octet. The matrix elements of the bilocal current are decomposed into F and D couplings under chiral SU(3)⊗SU(3) symmetry, and the sea quark distributions are extracted by regularizing divergent integrals using the soft pomeron model of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992). The symmetry is then extended to SU(4)⊗SU(4) and higher to constrain heavy sea quarks and spin-dependent distributions.

## Key numbers
- Modified Gottfried sum rule value for the proton: 0.26 (from the integral of A_3^p, Eq. 21)
- Mean hypercharge sum rule value for the proton: 2.12 (from the integral of A_8^p, Eq. 22)
- Extracted F coupling constant: 0.89 (Eq. 23)
- Extracted D coupling constant: -0.50 (Eq. 24)
- Soft pomeron intercept used for regularization: α_P(0) = 1.0808 (from Donnachie and Landshoff, 1992)
- Universal sea quark small-x coefficient a = 1.2 for u, d, s (from Ref. K95)
- Experimental values used for spin analysis: F = 0.46 ± 0.01, D = 0.79 ± 0.01 (from Hsu, year not specified in text)

## Context
This work extends the author's previous development of the modified Gottfried sum rule (Koretune, 1980, 1984, 1993, 1995) from the proton to the full baryon octet, using chiral symmetry constraints on the null-plane. It contradicts the original Gottfried sum rule prediction of 1/3 (Gottfried, 1967) and the perturbative QCD approach of Ross (1978) which could not explain the NMC experimental violation. The results enable predictions for sea quark asymmetries in all octet baryons and provide a mechanism to reduce the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974) without requiring large gluon polarization, instead using intrinsic charm sea quarks.
