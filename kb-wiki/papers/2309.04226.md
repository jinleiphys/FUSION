# Microscopic optical potentials for medium-mass isotopes derived at the first order of the Watson multiple scattering theory

> We perform a first-principle calculation of optical potentials for nucleon elastic scattering off medium-mass isotopes. Fully based on a saturating chiral Hamiltonian, the optical potentials are derived by folding nuclear density distributions computed with ab initio self-consistent Green's function theory with a nucleon-nucleon $t$ matrix computed with a consistent chiral interaction. The dependence on the folding interaction as well as the convergence of the target densities are investigated. Numerical results are presented and discussed for differential cross sections and analyzing powers, with focus on elastic proton scattering off Calcium and Nickel isotopes. Our optical potentials generally show a remarkable agreement with the available experimental data for laboratory energies in the range 65-200 MeV. We study the evolution of the scattering observables with increasing proton-neutron asymmetry by computing theoretical predictions of the cross section and analyzing power over the Calcium and Nickel isotopic chains.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that microscopic optical potentials for nucleon elastic scattering off medium-mass isotopes (Calcium and Nickel chains) can be derived from first principles using a saturating chiral Hamiltonian, with the folding interaction and target densities both computed from consistent chiral interactions, achieving remarkable agreement with experimental data for laboratory energies between 65 and 200 MeV.

## Method

The optical potentials are constructed at the first order of the Watson multiple scattering theory within the impulse approximation, folding a nucleon-nucleon $t$ matrix (computed from a chiral interaction) with nuclear density distributions obtained from ab initio self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory using the algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(n)] truncation scheme. The SCGF densities are computed in a harmonic oscillator basis for closed-shell isotopes via the Dyson equation and for semi-magic open-shell isotopes via the Gorkov formulation, with the density matrix transformed from the harmonic oscillator basis to momentum space. The resulting optical potential is then used to solve a one-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation for elastic scattering observables.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energy range studied: 65-200 MeV for elastic proton scattering
- Target systems: Calcium and Nickel isotopic chains (including both closed-shell and semi-magic open-shell isotopes)
- SCGF calculations performed in harmonic oscillator basis with up to $N_{\rm max}$+1 shells, with kinetic energy computed using up to 300 harmonic oscillator shells for the "full $\hat{T}$ space" correction
- ADC(3) truncation implemented for Dyson SCGF (closed-shell nuclei); ADC(2) available for Gorkov SCGF (open-shell nuclei)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous multiple scattering approach (Vorabbi 2015, 2017, 2018) from light nuclei ($A \lesssim 30$) accessible via no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations to medium-mass isotopes using SCGF theory, which has better scaling with mass number. The SCGF approach is particularly interesting because it also delivers accurate ab initio predictions of ground-state observables and the irreducible self-energy provides an exact extension of Feshbach theory (Feshbach 1958, 1962) to include scattering states and overlap functions. This enables future investigations of medium effects beyond the impulse approximation and opens the possibility of computing microscopic optical potentials for a wide range of nuclei away from stability, relevant for rare-isotope beam facilities.
