# Density fluctuations and multifragmentation of nuclear matter

> The density fluctuations of nuclear matter are studied within a mean-field model in wich fluctuations are generated by an external stochastic field. The constraints imposed on the random force by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem are analyzed. It is shown that in the proximity of the borders of the spinodal region the assumption of a white-noise stochastic field can be reliably used. The domain distribution of the liquid phase in the spinodal decomposition of nuclear matter is derived. The related distribution of fragment sizes compares favourably with the experimental fragment distribution observed in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that a white-noise stochastic field can reliably generate density fluctuations in nuclear matter only when the system is near the boundary of the spinodal region in the phase diagram, both inside and outside that region; this condition is necessary because the fluctuation-dissipation theorem generally forbids a white-noise assumption for the stochastic mean field.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as an infinite homogeneous system and add a stochastic Gaussian white-noise term to the mean-field potential, then impose the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to constrain the random force. Using the linearized Vlasov equation (a semiclassical approximation to the random phase approximation) with a Skyrme-like effective interaction, they derive the response function and show that the white-noise assumption is valid only in the classical limit ω/T ≪ 1, which holds near the spinodal boundary. From the resulting Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process for density fluctuations, they obtain the probability distribution of domain sizes in the liquid phase and convert it into a fragment-size distribution.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ϱ_eq = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility modulus: 201 MeV
- Binding energy at saturation: 15.75 MeV
- Skyrme parameters: a = −356.8 MeV, b = 303.9 MeV, α = 1/6, d = 130 MeV·fm⁵
- For T = 5 MeV, the spinodal region starts at ϱ_c = 0.617 ϱ_eq
- Critical wave number for unstable modes: k_c = √(|f″|/d)
- Wavelength of fastest-growing mode: λ_M = 2π/k_M with k_M = k_c/√3, exceeding 10 fm
- For the metastable case at ϱ_0 = 0.65 ϱ_eq and T = 5 MeV, the asymptotic correlation length L(∞) ≈ 3.0 fm
- For the unstable case at ϱ_0 = 0.58 ϱ_eq and T = 5 MeV, the asymptotic correlation length L(∞) ≈ 12 fm
- At t ∼ 200 fm/c, L(t) reaches its asymptotic value in the metastable case but remains much smaller than L(∞) in the unstable case

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Langevin approach of Ayik (1990), Randrup (1990), and Colonna (1994) by introducing a different source of fluctuations, Landau damping, rather than nucleon-nucleon collisions. The authors compare their fragment-size distribution with experimental data from heavy ion collisions and note that their linear theory applies to the early stage of spinodal decomposition, consistent with Monte Carlo simulations by Lebowitz (1982) and Marro (1983). The approach enables a closed-form solution for density fluctuations near the spinodal boundary and provides a framework for connecting nuclear matter properties to multifragmentation observables.
