# Implication of Existence of Hybrid stars and Theoretical Expectation of Submillisecond Pulsars

> We derive the bulk viscous damping timescale of hybrid stars, neutron stars with quark matter core. The r-mode instability windows of the stars show that the theoretical results are consistent with the rapid rotation pulsar data, which may give an indication for the existence of quark matter in the interior of neutron stars. Hybrid stars instead of neutron or strange stars may lead to submillisecond pulsars.

# Implication of Existence of Hybrid stars and Theoretical Expectation of Submillisecond Pulsars

## Key claim
The r-mode instability windows of hybrid stars (neutron stars with a quark matter core) are consistent with observed rapid rotation pulsar data, suggesting that submillisecond pulsars, if they exist, should be hybrid stars rather than normal neutron stars or strange stars. The authors qualify that this consistency provides an indication, not proof, of quark matter existence in neutron star interiors.

## Method
The authors derived the bulk viscous damping timescale for hybrid stars by computing bulk viscosity coefficients for strange quark matter, hadron matter, and their mixed phase using the relaxation time approximation. They then solved the critical rotation frequency equation (balancing gravitational radiation driving against shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and surface rubbing damping) to construct r-mode instability windows for hybrid stars with a polytropic equation of state (n=1) and compared these windows to those of strange stars and normal neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- The gravitational radiation timescale for an n=1 polytrope is τ_gr = -47 s × (M/1.4 M⊙)^(-1) × (R/10 km)^(-4) × (1000/ν)^6.
- The surface rubbing timescale is τ_sr = 200 s × (M/1.4 M⊙) × (R/10 km)^(-2) × (T/10^8 K) × (1000/ν)^(1/2).
- The bulk viscous damping timescale for the quark phase is τ^QP_bv = 7.9 s × (M/1.4 M⊙)^2 × (R/10 km)^(-4) × (T/10^9 K)^(-2) × (m_s/100 MeV)^(-4) × (1000/ν)^2.
- For a hybrid star with M=1.4 M⊙ and bag constant B^(1/4)=165 MeV, the radius is 9.87 km and the structure constant A=0.6886.
- A hybrid star can reach a rotation frequency of about 700 Hz (1.43 ms period) following the instability region track.

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations by Madsen (2000) on strange star limiting rotation and by Andersson (2000) and Bildsten (2000) on neutron star r-mode damping via viscous boundary layers. The authors address the drawback that strange stars evolve to limiting frequencies at temperatures of 10^6–10^7 K, while millisecond pulsars have inferred core temperatures of a few times 10^8 K. The hybrid star model reconciles this temperature discrepancy and suggests that future searches for submillisecond pulsars could test whether quark matter exists in compact star interiors.
