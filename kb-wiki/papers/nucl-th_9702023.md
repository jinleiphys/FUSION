# Nucleon flow and dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions

> Nucleon flow in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon region and dilepton spectra in S+Au collisions at SPS energies are analysed in the relativistic transport model. They are shown to be sensitive to the nucleon vector and scalar potentials, respectively, and are thus useful probes of these potentials in dense matter. A recently developed effective Lagrangian with self-interactions of vector as well as scalar fields is found to describe both data reasonably well.

## Key claim

Nucleon flow in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon and dilepton spectra in S+Au collisions at SPS energies can both be described by a single effective Lagrangian (the FST-T1 model) that includes self-interactions of the vector field, but the agreement for flow requires additionally applying a momentum-dependent correction extracted from Dirac phenomenology, which is not fully self-consistent.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model (based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation) with mean-field potentials derived from the FST-T1 effective Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot, which includes scalar and vector field self-interactions and scalar-vector coupling. For nucleon flow, the momentum-independent mean-field potentials were scaled with a momentum dependence extracted from Dirac phenomenology of proton-nucleus scattering. For dilepton production, vector meson masses were assumed to scale with the nucleon effective mass as in Eq. (1), and the transport model was initialized using the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) model.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon flow parameter in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon: FST-T1 model with momentum-dependent correction gives about 330 MeV, in good agreement with data; without correction it gives about 380 MeV.
- Walecka model flow parameter: about 520 MeV without momentum-dependent correction, about 450 MeV with correction, both too large compared to data.
- Non-linear sigma-omega model flow parameter: about 280 MeV (from prior work by Li and Ko (1995)).
- Vector potential at 3 times nuclear matter density: about 670 MeV in FST-T1 model, compared to about 810 MeV in Walecka model.
- FST-T1 model parameters: saturation density about 0.15 fm^{-3}, binding energy about 16 MeV, nucleon effective mass M_N*/M_N about 0.6, compression modulus about 200 MeV.
- Vector coupling constant in FST-T1 model: g_omega^2/4pi about 12.0; in Walecka model: about 10.6.

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic transport studies by Li and Ko (1995) that used a non-linear sigma-omega model with weak potentials to describe flow, and by Li, Ko, Brown, and Lee (1996) that used the Walecka model to describe dilepton spectra. It addresses the concern that the Walecka model overpredicts nucleon flow and has an unrealistically large compression modulus (about 560 MeV). The FST-T1 model simultaneously describes nuclear matter saturation properties, finite nuclei properties (Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot, 1995, 1996), nucleon flow data from the EOS Collaboration, and dilepton data from the CERES Collaboration. The separation of sensitivity (flow to vector potential, dileptons to scalar potential) enables future studies of hyperon and kaon properties in dense matter and connects to chiral symmetry restoration in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as discussed by Brown, Buballa, and Rho (1996).
