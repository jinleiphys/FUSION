# Finite-size behavior near the critical point of QCD phase-transition

> It is pointed out that finite-size effect is not negligible in locating critical point of QCD phase transition at current relativistic heavy ion collisions. Finite-size behavior near critical point, in particular, finite-size scaling and fixed-point behavior of the final observables can be served as a quantitative identification of critical point and the nearby boundary of the QCD phase transition. The validity of the method at finite detector acceptances at RHIC is discussed.

# Finite-size behavior near the critical point of QCD phase-transition

## Key claim

The finite-size effect is not negligible in locating the QCD critical point in current relativistic heavy ion collisions, because the ratio of correlation length to system size is estimated to be in the range of 1/6 to 1/2, which exceeds the threshold of 1/6 where finite-size effects must be taken into account. The fixed-point behavior of finite-size scaled observables at critical incident energy can serve as a reliable identification of the critical point and the nearby boundary of QCD phase transition, but only if the detected subsystem is large enough.

## Method

The authors propose a finite-size scaling form for critical related observables in heavy ion collisions, where the observable Q depends on incident energy and system size L, and can be written as Q = L^(lambda/nu) * F_Q(tau * L^(1/nu)) with tau being reduced incident energy. They demonstrate how to locate the fixed point by scanning a scaling exponent a and computing the width D of scaled observables across different system sizes at each incident energy, using the 3D-Ising model as an example. The method is tested on a subsystem containing 50% of lattice sites to simulate finite detector acceptance at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The system size at freeze-out is estimated to be less than 12 fm, based on HBT measurements at RHIC and work by Stephanov (2004).
- The correlation length is estimated to be around 6 fm for typical nuclear collisions, based on work by Antoniou and Stephanov (2004).
- After considering finite evolution time, the maximum correlation length may not exceed 2-3 fm at the critical point, based on work by Rajagopal (1999) and others.
- The ratio of correlation length to system size is estimated to be in the range of 1/6 to 1/2, which is larger than the threshold of 1/6 where finite-size effects become important.
- In the 3D-Ising model demonstration, the critical temperature is found to be T_c = 4.51 J, and the fixed point is identified at the same temperature in both the full system and a subsystem containing 50% of lattice sites.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling approach previously applied to nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions in multifragmentation experiments, and addresses the concern raised by Stephanov (2004) and others that finite-size effects may be significant at RHIC energies. The authors contrast their fixed-point method with the previously suggested non-monotonic behavior as an indicator of the critical point, noting that non-monotonic behavior is not unique to critical points and can also appear in first-order phase transitions or crossovers, as shown by lattice QCD results. The method enables experimental identification of the QCD critical point through beam energy scan data at RHIC and future FAIR experiments, provided the detector acceptance is large enough for finite-size scaling to remain valid.
