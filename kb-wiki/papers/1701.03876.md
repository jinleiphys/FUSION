# $Λ_c$ Semileptonic Decays in a Quark Model

> Hadronic form factors for semileptonic decay of the $Λ_c$ are calculated in a nonrelativistic quark model. The full quark model wave functions are employed to numerically calculate the form factors to all relevant orders in ($1/m_c$, $1/m_s$). The form factors obtained satisfy relationships expected from the heavy quark effective theory (HQET). The differential decay rates and branching fractions are calculated for transitions to the ground state and a number of excited states of $Λ$. The branching fraction of the semileptonic decay width to the total width of $Λ_c$ has been calculated and compared with other theoretical estimates and experimental results. The branching fractions for $Λ_c\toΛ^{*} l^{+}ν_l\toΣπl^{+}ν_l$ and $Λ_c\toΛ^{*} l^{+}ν_l\to N\bar{K} l^{+}ν_l$ are also calculated. Apart from decays to the ground state $Λ$(1115), it is found that decays through the $Λ(1405)$ provide a significant portion of the branching fraction $Λ_c\to X_s lν_l$. A new estimate for $f=B(Λ_c^{+}\toΛl^{+}ν_l)/B(Λ_c^{+}\to X_s l^{+}ν_l)$ is obtained.

## Key claim

The authors calculate hadronic form factors for semileptonic decays of the Λc to ground-state and excited Λ baryons using a nonrelativistic quark model, finding that decays through the Λ(1405) provide a significant contribution to the inclusive branching fraction Λc → Xs l νl, contrary to the common assumption that only the ground-state Λ(1115) contributes.

## Method

The authors employ a constituent quark model with full quark model wave functions obtained from variational diagonalization of a quark model Hamiltonian. They compute form factors numerically to all relevant orders in 1/mc and 1/ms, using two approximations: first with single-component wave functions and analytic form factors, then with full relativistic quark spinors. The form factors are then used to calculate differential decay rates and branching fractions for Λc decays to six Λ states (Λ(1115), Λ(1600), Λ(1405), Λ(1520), Λ(1890), Λ(1820)), including four-body final states Λc → Λ* l νl → Σπ l νl and Λc → Λ* l νl → N K̄ l νl.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction B(Λc+ → Λ l+ νl) = 2.8 ± 0.5% (with assumption that Λc decays only to ground state Λ(1115))
- PDG fit for B(Λc+ → pK−π+) = 6.35 ± 0.33%
- Ratio f = B(Λc+ → Λ l+ νl)/B(Λc+ → Xs l+ νl) = 0.87+0.13−0.17 (PDG estimate with assumption F=1.0)
- Previous estimate of f by Pervin, Roberts and Capstick: 0.85 ± 0.04
- Belle measurement of B(Λc+ → pK−π+): 6.84+0.32−0.40%
- BESIII measurement of B(Λc+ → pK−π+): 5.84 ± 0.27 ± 0.23%

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model calculations of Pervin, Roberts and Capstick (PRC) for ΛQ semileptonic decays and the work of Mott and Roberts on Λb rare decays, which indicated sensitivity to form factor precision. The authors compare with covariant quark model calculations by Gutsche et al., QCD light cone sum rules by Liu et al., and the molecular-state treatment of Λ(1405) by Ikeno and Oset. The results enable improved extraction of CKM matrix elements from semileptonic Λc decays and provide predictions for experimental searches of semileptonic decays to excited Λ states, which have not yet been reported.
