# $Œõ_c$ Semileptonic Decays in a Quark Model

> Hadronic form factors for semileptonic decay of the $Œõ_c$ are calculated in a nonrelativistic quark model. The full quark model wave functions are employed to numerically calculate the form factors to all relevant orders in ($1/m_c$, $1/m_s$). The form factors obtained satisfy relationships expected from the heavy quark effective theory (HQET). The differential decay rates and branching fractions are calculated for transitions to the ground state and a number of excited states of $Œõ$. The branching fraction of the semileptonic decay width to the total width of $Œõ_c$ has been calculated and compared with other theoretical estimates and experimental results. Apart from decays to the ground state $Œõ$(1115), it is found that decays through the $Œõ(1405)$ provide a significant portion of the branching fraction $Œõ_c\to X_s lŒΩ_l$. A new estimate for $f=B(Œõ_c^{+}\toŒõl^{+}ŒΩ_l)/B(Œõ_c^{+}\to X_s l^{+}ŒΩ_l)$ is obtained.

## Key claim

The authors calculate hadronic form factors for semileptonic decays of the Œõc to ground-state and excited Œõ baryons using a nonrelativistic quark model, finding that decays through the Œõ(1405) provide a significant contribution to the inclusive branching fraction Œõc ‚Üí Xs l ŒΩl, contrary to the common assumption that only the ground-state Œõ(1115) contributes.

## Method

The authors employ a constituent quark model with full quark model wave functions obtained from variational diagonalization of a quark model Hamiltonian. They compute form factors numerically to all relevant orders in 1/mc and 1/ms, using two approximations: first with single-component wave functions and analytic form factors, then with full relativistic quark spinors. The form factors are then used to calculate differential decay rates and branching fractions for Œõc decays to six Œõ states (Œõ(1115), Œõ(1600), Œõ(1405), Œõ(1520), Œõ(1890), Œõ(1820)), including four-body final states Œõc ‚Üí Œõ* l ŒΩl ‚Üí Œ£œÄ l ŒΩl and Œõc ‚Üí Œõ* l ŒΩl ‚Üí N KÃÑ l ŒΩl.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model calculations of Pervin, Roberts and Capstick (PRC) for ŒõQ semileptonic decays and the work of Mott and Roberts on Œõb rare decays, which indicated sensitivity to form factor precision. The authors compare with covariant quark model calculations by Gutsche et al., QCD light cone sum rules by Liu et al., and the molecular-state treatment of Œõ(1405) by Ikeno and Oset. The results enable improved extraction of CKM matrix elements from semileptonic Œõc decays and provide predictions for experimental searches of semileptonic decays to excited Œõ states, which have not yet been reported.
