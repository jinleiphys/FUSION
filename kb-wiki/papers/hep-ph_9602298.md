# Isospin Breaking in the Nucleon Isovector Axial Charge from QCD Sum Rules

> The isospin breaking in the nucleon isovector axial charge, $g_A^3$, are calculated within the external field QCD sum-rule approach. The isospin violations arising from the difference in up and down current quark masses and in up and down quark condensates are included; electromagnetic effects are not considered. We find $δg^3_A/g^3_A \approx (0.5-1.0)\times 10^{-2}$, where $δg^3_A = (g^3_A)_p + (g^3_A)_n$ and $ g^3_A = [(g^3_A)_p - (g^3_A)_n]/2$. Using the Goldberger-Treiman relation, we also obtain an estimate of the isospin breaking in the pion-nucleon coupling constant, $(g_{ppπ_0}-g_{nnπ_0})/g_{NNπ} \approx (2-7) \times 10^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The isospin breaking in the nucleon isovector axial charge, arising from the difference in up and down current quark masses and in up and down quark condensates (but excluding electromagnetic effects), is found to be approximately (0.5-1.0) x 10^{-2}, with the precise value depending on the magnitude of the isospin breaking in the quark condensates parameterized by gamma.

## Method

The author uses the external field QCD sum-rule approach, where a constant external isovector axial vector field is introduced and the linear response of the nucleon correlation function is computed via operator product expansion (OPE). The sum rules are derived from the invariant function Pi_1(q^2), which is identified as the most stable sum rule, and are analyzed by sampling over the fiducial Borel region 0.8 <= M^2 <= 1.4 GeV^2 with a logarithmic minimization procedure. The coupling strengths lambda^2_{p(n)} are extracted from nucleon mass sum rules using experimental nucleon masses corrected for electromagnetic contributions, and the parameters (g^3_A)_{p(n)}, A_{p(n)}, and (s_0)_{p(n)} are extracted from the sum rules.

## Key numbers

* delta g^3_A / g^3_A is approximately (0.5-1.0) x 10^{-2}, where delta g^3_A = (g^3_A)_p + (g^3_A)_n and g^3_A = [(g^3_A)_p - (g^3_A)_n]/2
* The isospin breaking in the pion-nucleon coupling constant, (g_{pp pi_0} - g_{nn pi_0})/g_{NN pi}, is approximately (2-7) x 10^{-3}, derived using the Goldberger-Treiman relation
* The fiducial Borel region used for analysis is 0.8 <= M^2 <= 1.4 GeV^2
* The quark mass difference ratio used is delta m/(m_u + m_d) = 0.28 +/- 0.03, from Gasser and Leutwyler (1982)
* The vacuum condensate parameters used are a = 0.55 GeV^3 (with m_u + m_d approximately 11.8 MeV) and b = 0.5 GeV^4
* The external field response parameters used are chi = -2 f_pi^2 (with f_pi = 93 MeV) and kappa approximately -0.2 chi

## Context

This work extends previous external field QCD sum-rule studies of the nucleon isovector axial charge by Belyaev and Kogan (1983) and Chiu, Pasupathy, and Wilson (1985), which ignored isospin breaking effects. The calculation includes isospin violations from the up-down quark mass difference and from isospin breaking in vacuum condensates, with the parameter gamma (the relative difference between down and up quark condensates) taken in the range -0.01 to -0.006 based on estimates from Paver et al. (1987), Pascual and Tarrach (1982), and others. The result enables estimates of isospin splitting in the pion-nucleon coupling constant via the Goldberger-Treiman relation, which is qualitatively compatible with the QCD sum-rule result from Henley and Meissener (1996) and with various model calculations, though the magnitude is somewhat smaller than that of Henley and Meissener.
