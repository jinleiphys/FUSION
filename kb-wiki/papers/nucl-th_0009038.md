# Dibaryon Systems In SU(3) Chira Quark Model

> The possible candidates of $S-$wave dibaryons with various strange numbers are studied under the chiral SU(3) quark model. It is shown that there are three types of baryon-baryon bound states. The states of the first type are called deuteron-like states. If chiral fields can provide enough attraction between interacting baryons, these systems, such as $[ΞΩ- Ξ^{*}Ω]_{(1,1/2)}$, $[ΞΞ]_{(0,1)}$, $[N Ω]_{(2,1/2)}$ would be weakly bound. The states of the second type such as $[Σ^{*} Δ]_{(0,5/2)}$, $[Σ^{*} Δ]_{(3,1/2)}$, $[ΔΔ]_{(0,3)}$ and $[ΔΔ]_{(3,0)}$ are named as $ΔΔ$-like states. Due to the highly symmetric character in orbital space, these systems could be relatively deeply bound, but the strong decay modes of composed baryons cause the widths of the states much broader. The states of the third type are entitled as $ΩΩ$-like states. Due to the same symmetry character shown in the systems of the second type and the only weak decay mode of composed baryons, for instance in $[ΩΩ]_{(0,0)}$, or at most one strong decay mode of composed baryons, for example in $[Ξ^{*}Ω]_{(0,1/2)}$, these states are deeply bound states with narrow widths. The states of latter two types are most interesting new dibaryon states and should be carefully investigated both theoretically and experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the chiral SU(3) quark model, S-wave dibaryon systems can be classified into three types based on symmetry properties, with the most promising candidates for deeply bound, narrow-width states being the ΩΩ-like systems where the constituent baryons decay only weakly or at most via one strong decay mode.

## Method

The authors employed the chiral SU(3) quark model, which includes one-gluon-exchange, confinement, and chiral-field-induced interactions between quarks, to study S-wave baryon-baryon bound states. The Resonating Group Method was used to dynamically solve the six-quark system and calculate binding energies and root-mean-square radii. Model parameters were first fixed by fitting baryon properties and nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon scattering data, then applied without additional parameters to the dibaryon calculations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 0.2 MeV (Model I, Set I) and -0.7 MeV (Model I, Set II), with RMS radii of 1.63 fm and 1.68 fm respectively
- H particle (ΛΛ-NΞ-ΣΣ coupled channel, S=0, T=0): binding energy -2.0 MeV (Set I) and 8.2 MeV (Set II), with RMS radii of 1.15 fm and 0.91 fm respectively
- [ΩΩ](0,0) system: binding energy 116.1 MeV (Set I) and 98.5 MeV (Set II), with RMS radii of 0.66 fm and 0.67 fm respectively
- [Ξ*Ω](0,1/2) system: binding energy 92.4 MeV (Set I) and 76.5 MeV (Set II), with RMS radii of 0.71 fm and 0.72 fm respectively
- [ΔΔ](3,0) (d*): binding energy 22.2 MeV (Set I) and 18.5 MeV (Set II), with RMS radius of 1.01 fm and 1.05 fm respectively
- [ΞΩ - Ξ*Ω](1,1/2) coupled system: binding energy 32.9 MeV (Set I) and 32.6 MeV (Set II), with RMS radii of 0.78 fm and 0.77 fm respectively
- [NΩ](2,1/2): binding energy 3.5 MeV (Set I) and 12.7 MeV (Set II), with RMS radius of 1.18 fm and 0.98 fm respectively
- [ΞΞ](0,1): binding energy 4.1 MeV (Set I) and 0.4 MeV (Set II), with RMS radius of 1.17 fm and 1.30 fm respectively

## Context

This work extends previous dibaryon studies by Jaffe (1977) on the H particle and by Yu et al., Zhang et al., and Li and Shen on specific systems like ΩΩ and ΞΩ, providing a systematic survey across all strangeness sectors. The chiral SU(3) quark model used here had previously been shown to reproduce nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon scattering data, and the authors note that the H particle mass near the ΛΛ threshold (around 2.232 GeV) is consistent with experimental lower limits from Imai et al. The results enable future experimental searches for deeply bound dibaryons with narrow widths, particularly the ΩΩ and Ξ*Ω systems, and suggest that channel coupling effects can significantly enhance binding as seen in the ΞΩ-Ξ*Ω system.
