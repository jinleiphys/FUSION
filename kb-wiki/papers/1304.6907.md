# Does a hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter preclude the existence of massive neutron stars ?

> We study the impact of a hadron-quark phase transition on the maximum neutron-star mass. The hadronic part of the equation of state relies on the most up-to-date Skyrme nuclear energy density functionals, fitted to essentially all experimental nuclear mass data and constrained to reproduce the properties of infinite nuclear matter as obtained from microscopic calculations using realistic forces. We show that the softening of the dense matter equation of state due to the phase transition is not necessarily incompatible with the existence of massive neutron stars like PSR J1614-2230.

# Does a hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter preclude the existence of massive neutron stars?

## Key claim
The softening of the dense matter equation of state due to a hadron-quark phase transition does not necessarily preclude the existence of massive neutron stars like PSR J1614-2230, provided the hadronic equation of state is sufficiently stiff. However, the resulting maximum mass of about 2.02 solar masses could be challenged by observations of significantly more massive neutron stars unless the sound speed in quark matter exceeds the perturbative QCD prediction of c/√3.

## Method
The authors constructed hybrid neutron star models by combining a hadronic equation of state (BSk21, based on generalized Skyrme energy density functionals fitted to nuclear mass data) with a quark phase described by an effective bag-model-like equation of state constrained by perturbative QCD calculations. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for nonrotating stars to determine the gravitational mass versus radius relation, assuming a first-order phase transition from hadronic to deconfined quark matter above a baryon density of 0.2 fm^-3.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of a purely hadronic neutron star with the BSk21 equation of state: 2.28 solar masses
- Maximum mass of a hybrid star with a quark core: 2.02 solar masses
- Transition density threshold for the onset of the hadron-quark phase transition: 0.2 fm^-3
- Effective bag constant associated with the BSk21 hadronic equation of state: 56.7 MeV fm^-3
- Speed of sound in quark matter constrained by perturbative QCD: cannot exceed c/√3 (where c is the speed of light)

## Context
This work extends the family of unified equations of state (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) developed by Pearson (2011, 2012) and Goriely (2010), which were fitted to essentially all measured nuclear masses. The authors previously found that the BSk19 equation of state was too soft to support neutron stars as massive as PSR J1614-2230 (Chamel 2011). The present results show that hybrid stars with quark cores can still reach about 2 solar masses, but this could be challenged by observations of even more massive neutron stars reported by other groups (2002, 2008, 2011) unless the sound speed in quark matter is larger than predicted by perturbative QCD calculations (Kurkela 2010).
