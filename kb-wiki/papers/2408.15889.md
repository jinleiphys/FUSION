# Strongly Interacting Quark Matter in Massive Quark Stars

> This paper investigates the properties of strongly coupled matter at high baryon densities (\(ρ_B\)) in quark stars (QSs). The QS model is based on the density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) framework, modified (MDDQM) by enhancing the single-gluon interaction to generate higher repulsive pressure. The model parameters are constrained using Bayesian inference, incorporating observational data from the pulsars HESS J1731$-$347, PSR J0030$+$0451, PSR J0740$+$6620, and PSR J0952$-$0607. Our results show that the MDDQM model produces QSs with higher mass and compactness compared to the DDQM model. Among the four MDDQM parameterizations studied, two yield maximum star masses of 1.86 and 2.10 \(\rm M_\odot\) and exhibit near-conformal behavior in the underlying quark matter (QM). The other two parameterizations, yielding QS masses of 2.30 and 2.37 \(\rm M_\odot\), correspond to a stronger interaction in the underlying QM. These findings provide important insights into the equation of state of deconfined QM and its implications for the structure and stability of QSs.

## Key claim
The modified density-dependent quark mass (MDDQM) model, which enhances repulsive pressure by introducing an extra density-dependent term to the single-gluon exchange interaction, can produce quark stars with higher mass and compactness than the standard DDQM model, but only two of the four studied parameterizations yield maximum masses (2.30 and 2.37 solar masses) that satisfy the most stringent observational constraints, while the other two (1.86 and 2.10 solar masses) exhibit near-conformal behavior in the underlying quark matter.

## Method
The authors modified the standard density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model by replacing the constant one-gluon exchange strength `C` with a density-dependent function `C(ρ_B) = 1 + κ ρ_B^(1/3)`, creating the MDDQM model. They used Bayesian inference to constrain the free parameters `D` and `κ` by fitting to observational mass and radius data from the pulsars HESS J1731-347, PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0952-0607. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to determine the mass-radius relations and internal properties of static, non-rotating quark stars.

## Key numbers
- Two MDDQM parameterizations yield maximum quark star masses of **1.86** and **2.10** solar masses, with the underlying quark matter exhibiting near-conformal behavior.
- Two other MDDQM parameterizations yield maximum quark star masses of **2.30** and **2.37** solar masses, corresponding to a stronger interaction in the underlying quark matter.
- The standard DDQM model, when constrained with the same astrophysical data, reached a maximum mass of approximately **2.18** solar masses.
- For comparison, the vector MIT bag model, under the same constraints, reached a maximum mass of approximately **2.54** solar masses.

## Context
This work extends the density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model of Peng (1999) and Xia (2014) to produce more compact quark stars, addressing the difficulty many phenomenological quark models have in reaching the 2 solar mass threshold set by observations of pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013). The modification is inspired by the vector MIT bag model (PhysRevD.9.3471, Serot 1992), which produces higher maximum masses, and the Cornell potential for heavy-quark confinement (Eichten, 1978). The results enable further study of the equation of state of deconfined quark matter and the potential for near-conformal behavior at densities found in neutron star interiors, as discussed by Annala (2023).
