# Viscosities of the Baryon-Rich Quark-Gluon Plasma from Beam Energy Scan Data

> This work presents the first Bayesian inference study of the (3+1)D dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) viscosities using an event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamics + hadronic transport theoretical framework and data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) Beam Energy Scan program. Robust constraints on initial state nuclear stopping and the baryon chemical potential-dependent shear viscosity of the produced quantum chromodynamic (QCD) matter are obtained. The specific bulk viscosity of the QCD matter is found to exhibit a preferred maximum around $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV. This result allows for the alternative interpretation of a reduction (and/or increase) of the speed of sound relative to that of the employed lattice-QCD based equation of state (EOS) for net baryon chemical potential $μ_B \sim 0.2\, (0.4)$ GeV.

# Viscosities of the Baryon-Rich Quark-Gluon Plasma from Beam Energy Scan Data

## Key claim

The specific bulk viscosity of QCD matter exhibits a preferred maximum around center-of-mass energy 19.6 GeV, which allows for the alternative interpretation of a reduction (and/or increase) of the speed of sound relative to the employed lattice-QCD based equation of state for net baryon chemical potential around 0.2 (0.4) GeV, though the 90% confidence bands still cover zero difference.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference study using an event-by-event (3+1)D dynamical initialization model (3d-Glauber) coupled with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) and hadronic transport (URQMD) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV. They trained Gaussian Process emulators on 1,000 design points in a 26-dimensional model parameter space, with 1,000 minimum bias events at 200 GeV and 2,000 at each lower energy, then used Monte Carlo Markov Chain sampling to obtain posterior distributions constrained by 604 experimental data points from STAR and PHOBOS.

## Key numbers

- The specific bulk viscosity peak temperature parameter T_zeta,0 was varied with prior range [0.15, 0.25] GeV
- The specific shear viscosity parameters eta_0, eta_2, eta_4 each had prior range [0.001, 0.3] at mu_B = 0, 0.2, and 0.4 GeV respectively
- The bulk viscosity maximum zeta_max had prior range [0, 0.2]
- The switching energy density e_sw had prior range [0.15, 0.5] GeV/fm^3
- The average rapidity loss at 200 GeV was constrained to approximately 2, consistent with BRAHMS measurements
- The bulk viscosity peak was favored around T = 200-220 MeV from 200 GeV data alone
- The non-monotonic bulk viscosity enhancement at 19.6 GeV was found for temperatures between 0.15 GeV and 0.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Bayesian analyses that assumed longitudinal boost invariance (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2019, Nijs 2020, JETSCAPE 2020) by incorporating full (3+1)D dynamics and baryon chemical potential dependence. It significantly expands over Auvinen (2017) which studied only a 5-dimensional parameter space. The results enable future searches for a QCD critical point and first-order phase transition at large net baryon densities, and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming RHIC Beam Energy Scan phase II measurements and experiments at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research.
