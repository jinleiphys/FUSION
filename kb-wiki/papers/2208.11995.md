# Pole determination of $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ and possible $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$ in $B^-\to J/ψΛ\bar{p}$

> First hidden-charm pentaquark candidate with strangeness, $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$, was recently discovered in $B^-\to J/ψΛ\bar{p}$ by the LHCb Collaboration. $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ shows up as a bump at the $Ξ_c\bar{D}$ threshold in the $J/ψΛ$ invariant mass ($M_{J/ψΛ}$) distribution. The $M_{J/ψΛ}$ distribution also shows a large fluctuation at the $Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ threshold, hinting the existence of a possible $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$. In this work, we determine the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ and $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$ pole positions for the first time. For this purpose, we fit a $B^-\to J/ψΛ\bar{p}$ model to the $M_{J/ψΛ}$, $M_{J/ψ\bar{p}}$, $M_{Λ\bar{p}}$, and $\cosθ_{K^*}$ distributions from the LHCb simultaneously; $χ^2/{\rm ndf}\sim 1.21$. Then we extract $P_{ψs}^Λ$ poles from a unitary $Ξ_c\bar{D}$-$Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel scattering amplitude built in the model. In our default fit, the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ pole is found at $( 4338.2\pm 1.4)-( 1.9\pm 0.5 )\,i$ MeV while the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$ pole at $4254.7\pm 0.4$ MeV. The $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ and $P_{ψs}^Λ(4255)$ are mostly $Ξ_c \bar{D}$ bound and $Λ_c\bar{D}_s$ virtual states, respectively. Through our analysis, the data disfavors a hypothesis of $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ as merely a kinematical effect. This pole determination, which is important in its own right, sets a primary basis to study the nature of the $P_{ψs}^Λ$ states.

## Key claim

The paper determines the pole positions of the hidden-charm pentaquark with strangeness, $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$, and a possible $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4255)$, by fitting a unitary coupled-channel model to LHCb data, finding that the data disfavors the hypothesis that the $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ peak is merely a kinematical threshold cusp.

## Method

The authors constructed a model of $B^-\to J/\psi\Lambda\bar{p}$ that includes weak decay vertices followed by hadronic rescattering in a unitary $\Xi_c\bar{D}$-$\Lambda_c\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel amplitude, with transitions to $J/\psi\Lambda$ treated perturbatively. They simultaneously fitted the model to the LHCb data for the $M_{J/\psi\Lambda}$, $M_{J/\psi\bar{p}}$, $M_{\Lambda\bar{p}}$, and $\cos\theta_{K^*}$ distributions, then extracted pole positions by analytically continuing the coupled-channel scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ pole position: $(4338.2 \pm 1.4) - (1.9 \pm 0.5)\,i$ MeV (default fit)
* $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4255)$ pole position: $4254.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV (default fit, virtual state)
* Fit quality: $\chi^2/{\rm ndf} \sim 1.21$ for the default model
* The existence of a nearby pole is favored by the LHCb data with $2.6\sigma$ significance

## Context

This work extends the LHCb discovery of $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ (LHCb Collaboration, 2022) by replacing the Breit-Wigner approximation used in the experimental amplitude analysis with a proper pole-extraction method using a unitary coupled-channel amplitude. The pole determination provides a primary basis for studying the nature of the $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda$ states, and the analysis also addresses the possibility raised by some authors (2022) that the structure could be a triangle singularity. The results enable future discrimination between different solutions using higher statistics data, particularly from $\Xi^-_b \to J/\psi\Lambda K^-$ decays.
