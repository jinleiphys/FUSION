# Holographic neutrino transport in dense strongly-coupled matter

> A (toy) model for cold and luke-warm strongly-coupled nuclear matter at finite baryon density is used to study neutrino transport. The complete charged current two-point correlators are computed in the strongly-coupled medium and their impact on neutrino transport is analyzed. The full result is compared with various approximations for the current correlators and the distributions, including the degenerate approximation, the hydrodynamic approximation as well as the diffusive approximation and we comment on their successes. Further improvements are discussed.

## Key claim

Using a holographic toy model (Einstein-Yang-Mills theory dual to dense unpaired quark matter), the authors compute charged-current neutrino opacities in strongly-coupled matter and find that, while the hydrodynamic approximation works well at high baryon density (within 30-50% error), the full holographic two-point function is needed at lower densities; the results suggest that strong coupling suppresses neutrino opacity by about an order of magnitude compared to perturbative QCD estimates.

## Method

The authors employ a bottom-up holographic model with a 5-dimensional Einstein-Yang-Mills action containing U(N_f)_L × U(N_f)_R gauge fields dual to chiral current operators, on a charged AdS black hole background (Reissner-Nordström) representing dense quark matter at finite temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ. They compute the retarded charged-current two-point correlators numerically for energies ω and momenta k between 0 and a few times the horizon radius r_H, then use these correlators to evaluate the neutrino emissivity j(E_ν) and mean free path λ(E_ν) via the Boltzmann equation. The full numerical results are compared with three approximations: the degenerate approximation (μ ≫ T), the hydrodynamic approximation (leading order in r_H ω, r_H k), and the diffusive approximation (time-time component dominance).

## Key numbers

- Temperature fixed to T = 10 MeV, typical of young neutron stars.
- Baryon density range investigated: 10^{-3} fm^{-3} ≤ n_B ≤ 1 fm^{-3}.
- Neutrino energy range: 0 ≤ E_ν ≤ 100 MeV.
- At n_B = 0.11 fm^{-3} and T = 10 MeV, the holographic neutrino opacity κ_{e^-}(0) ≈ 6.2 × 10^2 km^{-1} (n_B / 0.1 fm^{-3})^{5/3}, about an order of magnitude larger than nuclear matter calculations and about two orders of magnitude smaller than perturbative QCD results.
- For n_B ≳ 10^{-1} fm^{-3} (T / 10 MeV)^3, the hydrodynamic approximation error is 0-50% for neutrinos and less than 30% for anti-neutrinos.
- For n_B ≳ 10^{-2} fm^{-3}, the diffusive approximation formula κ_{e,0} = (G_F^2 σ / π^2) μ_ν^4 agrees well with exact results.
- The hydrodynamic conductivity σ = (|M_ud|^2 / 8 r_H) N_c (Mℓ)^3 w_0^3 and diffusivity D = r_H / 2, where r_H is the horizon radius.

## Context

This work initiates the holographic study of neutrino transport in dense strongly-coupled matter, extending prior holographic analyses of QCD thermodynamics and transport coefficients (e.g., Hoyos et al. (2020, 2021) on viscosities and conductivities in quark matter) to neutrino radiative coefficients. The results are compared with non-relativistic nuclear matter calculations by Oertel et al. (2020) and weakly-coupled quark matter calculations by Iwamoto (1982), finding that strong coupling suppresses opacities relative to perturbative estimates. The authors note that the toy model lacks chiral symmetry breaking and isospin asymmetry, and they identify several improvements for future work: adding isospin chemical potential, including Chern-Simons terms, upgrading to DBI actions, and using more realistic top-down models such as the Sakai-Sugimoto model or V-QCD.
