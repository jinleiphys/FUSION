# Direct capture astrophysical S factors at low energy

> We investigate the energy dependence of the astrophysical S factors for the reactions 7Be(p,gamma)8B, the primary source of high-energy solar neutrinos in the solar pp chain, and 16O(p,gamma)17F, an important reaction in the CNO cycle. Both of these reactions have predicted S factors which rise at low energies; we find the source of this behavior to be a pole in the S factor at a center-of-mass energy E = -E_B, the point where the energy of the emitted photon vanishes. The pole arises from a divergence of the radial integrals.

# Direct capture astrophysical S factors at low energy

## Key claim
The low-energy rise in the astrophysical S factors for 7Be(p,gamma)8B and 16O(p,gamma)17F is caused by a pole in the S factor at a center-of-mass energy E = -E_B, where the photon energy vanishes, arising from a divergence of the radial integrals; this pole is present only for weakly bound final states that are halo in nature.

## Method
The authors performed direct capture calculations using the formalism of Williams and Koonin (1981), expressing the S factor in terms of radial integrals of initial and final state wave functions. They analyzed the asymptotic behavior of the Coulomb wave function and Whittaker function for large radii to determine the analytic structure of the S factor, and fitted the resulting Laurent series expansion to experimental data for both reactions using a form S = n(1 + c1 E + c2 E^2)/(E + E_B).

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of 8B: E_B = 137.5 keV (from Ajzenburg-Selove, 1990)
- Binding energy of the first excited state of 17F: E_B = 105.2 keV (from Tilley et al., 1993)
- For 16O(p,gamma)17F*: c1 = 1.18 MeV^-1, c2 = 0, n = 1.59 x 10^3 keV^2 b
- For 7Be(p,gamma)8B: c1 = 5.36 MeV^-1, c2 = 1.80 MeV^-2, n = 3.74 keV^2 b (Kavanagh data normalization) or n = 2.99 keV^2 b (Filippone data normalization)
- The radial integrals peak at large radii: 40 fm for 7Be(p,gamma)8B and 65 fm for 16O(p,gamma)17F* at threshold
- The straight line fit for E_gamma S is valid for 16O(p,gamma)17F* up to approximately 3 MeV
- The quadratic approximation for 7Be(p,gamma)8B is not valid above 0.4 MeV due to the resonance at 0.633 MeV

## Context
This work extends the direct capture calculations of Christy and Duck (1961), Rolfs (1973), and Williams and Koonin (1981) by identifying the mathematical origin of the low-energy S factor rise as a pole at E_gamma = 0. The analysis explains why the Taylor series expansion of S_17 from Williams and Koonin (1981) converges only within a radius equal to the binding energy, and motivates a new fitting form that explicitly includes the pole contribution. This enables more reliable extrapolation of S factors to astrophysically relevant energies near 20 keV for 7Be(p,gamma)8B and 25 keV for 16O(p,gamma)17F, which are inaccessible to direct experimental measurement.
