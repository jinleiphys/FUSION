# Likely existence of bound states and the Efimov effect in the triple-$J/ψ$ system

> The ground-breaking discovery of the first fully charmed tetraquark state $X(6900)$ in the $J/ψJ/ψ$ invariant mass distribution by the LHCb collaboration has inspired intensive theoretical studies. Various interpretations, such as molecular states, compact tetraquark states, and coupled-channel effects, have been proposed for these states. Of particular interest is the ongoing search for the triple-$J/ψ$ state--a fully-charmed hexaquark state. To deepen our understanding of the triple-$J/ψ$ state and to guide future experimental searches, we study the triple-$J/ψ$ system in this work employing the Gaussian expansion method and the $J/ψJ/ψ$ potential parameterized to yield a shallow bound state, as suggested in several theoretical works. Our results support a triple-$J/ψ$ bound state, even in cases where the attractive interaction between the two $J/ψ$ mesons is very weak. Moreover, our analysis implies the Efimov effect in the triple-$J/ψ$ system. In addition, we extend our investigation to the triple-$Υ(1S)$ system and obtain results similar to those for the triple-$J/ψ$ system.

## Key claim
The triple-$J/\psi$ system is very likely to form a bound molecular state, and exhibits at least two Efimov states for all parameter sets studied, with a third Efimov state appearing when the two-body interaction becomes shorter ranged and weaker. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the two-body $J/\psi J/\psi$ interaction is attractive enough to produce a shallow bound state with binding energy between 0.001 and 5 MeV.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) for identical $J/\psi$ mesons in $S$-wave. They employed two functional forms of a short-range contact potential (Gaussian and tripole) between $J/\psi$ pairs, with the coupling strengths determined by reproducing assumed two-body binding energies $B_2$ ranging from 0.001 to 5 MeV and cutoffs $R$ ranging from 0.15 to 2 fm. The same approach was then applied to the triple-$\Upsilon(1S)$ system.

## Key numbers
- Triple-$J/\psi$ ground state binding energy ranges from 0.89 to 246.66 MeV (Gaussian potential) and 1.28 to 329.97 MeV (tripole potential) across all parameter sets.
- For a cutoff $R = 0.5$ fm and two-body binding energy $B_2 = 0.001$ MeV, the triple-$J/\psi$ binds with a binding energy of about 15 MeV (12.51 MeV for Gaussian, 17.39 MeV for tripole).
- The universal scaling factor for Efimov trimers is $\lambda_0^2 \approx 1.94 \times 10^{-3}$ for the binding energy ratio $\Delta E^{n+1}/\Delta E^n$ in the unitary limit.
- The root-mean-square radius of the triple-$J/\psi$ ground state is about 1 fm for $R = 0.5$ fm, consistent with a hadronic molecule.
- For the triple-$\Upsilon(1S)$ system with a reasonable cutoff $R = 0.2$ fm, the binding energy is about 65 MeV and the size about 0.34 fm.

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of fully-charmed multiquark states following the LHCb discovery of $X(6900)$ in the $J/\psi J/\psi$ invariant mass distribution (LHCb, 2020) and the CMS observation of simultaneous triple-$J/\psi$ production (CMS, 2021). It builds on prior suggestions of a shallow $J/\psi J/\psi$ bound state called $X(6200)$ from coupled-channel analyses by Dong (2020) and Wang (2022), and from the QCD string approach by Nefediev (2021). The results enable future experimental searches for triple-$J/\psi$ and triple-$\Upsilon(1S)$ molecular states below their respective thresholds, and provide a theoretical framework for studying the Efimov effect in fully-heavy hadronic systems.
