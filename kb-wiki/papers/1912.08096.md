# A new state of dense matter in neutron stars with nucleon structure

> The existence of stars with a large mass of 2 solar masses means that the equation of state is stiff enough to provide high enough pressure at large central densities. Previous work shows that such a stiff equation of state is possible if the ground state has nucleons as its constituents. We find this to be so in a chiral soliton ( skyrmion ) model for a composite nucleon which has bound state quarks. The strong binding of the quarks in this composite nucleon is plausibly the origin of the nucleon-nucleon hard core. In this model we find a new state of superdense matter at high density which is a 'topological'cubic crystal of overlapping composite nucleons that are solitons with relativistic quark bound states. The quarks are frozen in a filled band of a unique state, which not an eigenstate of spin or isospin but an eigenstate of spin plus isospin, $ \vec S + \vec I = 0$. In this alternative model we find that all neutron stars have no regular `free'quark matter. Neutron stars whose central density crosses a threshold baryon density of approximately, $n_b \sim 1/fm^3 $, will become unstable and go through a decompression (sudden) density discontinuity to conventional quark matter. Sequentially, this contraction of the core of the star will soften the equation of state release a large amount of gravitational potential energy which can give rise to a shock wave and matter ejection. Since the merger of two neutron stars gives a compact state whose mass is larger than the allowed maximum mass, this will be followed by a jet and a short gamma ray burst while transiting into a black hole.

## Key claim

In a chiral soliton (skyrmion) model with composite nucleons containing bound-state quarks, dense matter in neutron stars forms a new "topological" cubic crystal state of overlapping composite nucleons rather than conventional free quark matter, and neutron stars whose central density exceeds approximately \(n_b \sim 1/\text{fm}^3\) become unstable and undergo a sudden decompression to conventional quark matter.

## Method

The author employs a chirally symmetric mean-field theory with quarks coupled to sigma and pion fields, using a skyrmion soliton model for the nucleon with three valence quark bound states. A relativistic Dirac band-structure calculation is performed for a cubic lattice of such composite solitonic nucleons, tracking the \(0^+\) valence quark band as a function of baryon density. The Maxwell construction is also applied between the APR 98 nuclear equation of state and a pion-condensed quark matter phase to compare phase transition densities.

## Key numbers

- The quark binding energy in the nucleon is estimated at \(1.17\, g f_\pi \sim 821\) MeV, using \(g \sim 7.55\) and \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- The quark bound state reaches the continuum at a soliton radius \(R \sim 0.46\) fm, corresponding to a baryon density \(n_B = 1/(2R)^3 \sim 1.29\,\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- In the band-structure calculation, the filled \(0^+\) valence band meets the positive-energy continuum at a cell length of \(\sim 1\) fm, translating to a baryon density of \(1/\text{fm}^3\).
- For the APR [A18 + dv + UIX] equation of state, the central density of a \(1.9\) solar mass star is \(n_B \sim 0.7/\text{fm}^3\).
- For the APR [A18 + UIX] equation of state, the central density of a \(1.9\) solar mass star is \(n_B \sim 0.57/\text{fm}^3\).
- In the Maxwell construction with sigma mass \(\sim 850\) MeV, the common tangent between nuclear and quark phases begins at \(n_B \sim 0.57/\text{fm}^3\) in the nuclear phase and ends at \(n_B \sim 0.8/\text{fm}^3\) in the quark phase.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dense matter using the APR 98 equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) and chiral soliton models (Soni and Bhattacharya, various years). It contradicts the conventional expectation that neutron stars contain quark matter cores at moderate densities, instead proposing that nucleons with strongly bound quarks persist as a crystalline state up to much higher densities. The model enables a scenario where neutron star mergers produce a short gamma-ray burst and black hole formation via a sudden decompression and gravitational energy release, connecting to observations of the GW170817 merger event (Abbott et al., 2017).
