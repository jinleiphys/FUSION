# Relativity and the low energy nd Ay puzzle

> We solve the Faddeev equation in an exactly Poincare invariant formulation of the three-nucleon problem. The dynamical input is a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction that is exactly on-shell equivalent to the high precision CDBonn NN interaction. S-matrix cluster properties dictate how the two-body dynamics is embedded in the three-nucleon mass operator. We find that for neutron laboratory energies above 20 MeV relativistic effects on Ay are negligible. For energies below 20 MeV dynamical effects lower the nucleon analyzing power maximum slightly by 2% and Wigner rotations lower it further up to 10 % increasing thus disagreement between data and theory. This indicates that three-nucleon forces must provide an even larger increase of the Ay maximum than expected up to now.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering, specifically the nucleon analyzing power \(A_y\), are found to worsen the disagreement with experimental data rather than resolve it: for neutron laboratory energies below approximately 20 MeV, dynamical relativistic effects lower the \(A_y\) maximum by about 2%, and inclusion of Wigner rotations lowers it further by up to 10%, increasing the discrepancy between theory and data and implying that three-nucleon forces must provide an even larger increase of the \(A_y\) maximum than previously expected.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equation in an exactly Poincare invariant (instant-form) formulation of the three-nucleon problem, using a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction constructed to be exactly on-shell equivalent to the high-precision CDBonn NN interaction via the Kato-Birman invariance principle. The two-body dynamics was embedded in the three-nucleon mass operator using S-matrix cluster properties, and the relativistic Faddeev kernel was treated exactly, including Wigner spin rotations, without the approximations used in earlier work.

## Key numbers

- For neutron laboratory energies above approximately 20 MeV, relativistic effects on \(A_y\) are negligible.
- For neutron laboratory energies below approximately 20 MeV, dynamical relativistic effects lower the nucleon analyzing power maximum by approximately 2%.
- Inclusion of Wigner rotations lowers the \(A_y\) maximum further by up to approximately 10% relative to the nonrelativistic prediction.
- At a neutron laboratory energy of 3 MeV, a previous perturbative estimate (Schwenk, 2005) using the plane-wave impulse approximation found a large increase of the \(A_y\) maximum by approximately 10%, which the present exact calculation does not confirm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Poincare invariant three-nucleon studies (Witala et al., 2005; Witala et al., 2006; Skibinski et al., 2006) by including the full treatment of Wigner rotations, which were omitted in those earlier calculations. It directly contradicts a perturbative estimate by Schwenk (2005) that suggested relativistic effects could significantly reduce the low-energy \(A_y\) puzzle discrepancy; instead, the exact calculation shows relativity worsens the disagreement. The results indicate that three-nucleon forces must provide an even larger increase of the \(A_y\) maximum than previously expected to resolve the puzzle, motivating further work on three-nucleon force models.
