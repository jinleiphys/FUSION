# Soft Gluon Radiation off Heavy Quarks beyond Eikonal Approximation

> We calculate the soft gluon radiation spectrum off heavy quarks (HQs) interacting with light quarks (LQs) beyond small angle scattering (eikon- ality) approximation and thus generalize the dead-cone formula of heavy quarks extensively used in the literatures of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) phenomenology to the large scattering angle regime which may be im- portant in the energy loss of energetic heavy quarks in the deconfined Quark-Gluon Plasma medium. In the proper limits, we reproduce all the relevant existing formulae for the gluon radiation distribution off energetic quarks, heavy or light used in the QGP phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the soft gluon radiation spectrum off heavy quarks scattering with light quarks without assuming small-angle scattering (the eikonal I approximation), thereby generalizing the dead-cone formula to the regime where the transverse momentum transfer \(q_{\bot}\) is not negligible compared to the heavy quark energy. The work shows that for moderate non-eikonality (\(\zeta = q_{\bot}/\sqrt{s} = 0.15\)), the non-eikonal contribution to the radiation spectrum can be up to 50% larger than the eikonal result.

## Method

The authors compute the \(2 \to 3\) process Qq \(\to\) Qqg at \(\mathcal{O}(g^3)\) in the COM frame, retaining terms of \(\mathcal{O}(t/s)\) and higher in the Feynman amplitudes while working in the soft gluon limit (\(\omega/\sqrt{s} \to 0\)). They derive the full squared matrix element \(|\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{Qq}\to\mathrm{Qqg}}|^2\) in terms of the mass parameter \(\Delta_M = m/\sqrt{s}\), the scattering variable \(t/s\), and the gluon emission angle \(\theta\), then extract the radiation spectrum \(W(x,k_{\bot}^2)\) which contains the non-eikonal corrections through functions \(\mathcal{F}_{35}\) and \(\mathcal{F}_{45}\) that depend on \(q_{\bot}/\sqrt{s}\). The energy loss per unit length is estimated by integrating the gluon spectrum over a thermal bath of light quarks at \(T = 300\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- For \(\Delta_M = 0.1\) and non-eikonality parameter \(\zeta = 0.15\), the non-eikonal spectrum exceeds the eikonal spectrum by up to 50% (Fig. 1, red dashed curve).
- For \(\zeta = 0.30\), the excess reaches approximately 30%; for \(\zeta = 0.45\), the excess is approximately 15% (Fig. 1, brown dotted and blue dot-dashed curves).
- The thermal bath temperature used for energy loss estimation is \(T = 300\) MeV.
- The quark thermal mass is given by \(m_f^2 = \pi \alpha_s(T) T^2 C_F / 2\) and the gluon thermal mass by \(m_g^2 = 2\pi \alpha_s(T) T^2 (C_A + N_f/2) / 3\).

## Context

This work extends the non-collinear heavy quark radiation spectrum of Abir et al. (2012) by additionally removing the eikonal I approximation (neglect of heavy quark recoil due to scattering). It reproduces the Dokshitzer-Kharzeev dead-cone formula (2001) in the soft-eikonal-collinear limit, the Gunion-Bertsch formula (1982) for massless quarks in the eikonal limit, and the non-eikonal light quark result of Abir et al. (2013) in the massless limit. The results enable more precise calculations of heavy quark radiative energy loss in QGP, particularly for large-angle scattering events that may affect observables such as the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\).
