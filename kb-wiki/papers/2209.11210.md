# Stability of multi-component relativistic viscous hydrodynamics from Israel-Stewart and reproducing DNMR from maximizing the entropy

> The Quark Gluon Plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions has three relevant conserved charges: baryon number (B), strangeness (S), and electric charge (Q). Here we derive the Israel-Stewart framework for BSQ diffusion coupled to shear and bulk viscosity and the thermodynamic derivatives needed to couple this to a BSQ equation of state. We reproduce a subset of the ${\mathcal{J}}$ terms in the DNMR approach and propose a technique to derive other terms from the maximum entropy principle. Finally, we preform a stability analysis that can be used to constraint transport coefficients in BSQ hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete Israel-Stewart framework for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with three conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge) coupled to shear and bulk viscosity, and shows that this framework can reproduce a subset of the DNMR \({\mathcal{J}}\) terms via the maximum entropy principle, with the caveat that the stability analysis provides constraints on transport coefficients rather than uniquely determining them.

## Method

The authors extend the Israel-Stewart phenomenological approach to include all three BSQ charge currents simultaneously, deriving relaxation-type equations for bulk pressure, shear stress tensor, and the diffusive charge currents from an entropy current that includes second-order coupling terms between shear-bulk, shear-diffusion, and bulk-diffusion. They then perform a linear stability analysis to find constraints linking transport coefficients to the equation of state, and connect their results to the DNMR framework by identifying which terms in the relaxation equations correspond to the systematic expansion in Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers.

## Key numbers

- For BSQ conserved charges, the total number of independent transport coefficients is 43: 10 for bulk, 9 for shear (after subtracting one shared coefficient), and 24 for diffusion.
- At zero chemical potential, the ratio of bulk viscosity to its relaxation time is \(\zeta/\tau_\Pi = 15(1/3 - c_s^2)^2 (\varepsilon + p)\).
- At zero chemical potential, the ratio of shear viscosity to its relaxation time is \(\eta/\tau_\pi = (\varepsilon + p)/5\).
- For a one-component massless gas in the Boltzmann limit, the ratio of baryon diffusion coefficient to its relaxation time is \(\kappa_B/\tau_B = n_B \left( \frac{1}{3}\coth(\alpha_B) - \frac{n_B T}{\varepsilon + p} \right)\).

## Context

This work extends previous Israel-Stewart derivations for single-charge diffusion (Denicol 2018, Du 2019) and ideal BSQ dynamics (Schafer 2021) to a full multi-component viscous framework with all three conserved charges. It also builds on the DNMR approach (Denicol 2012) by showing how the maximum entropy principle can reproduce a subset of the DNMR \({\mathcal{J}}\) terms, and provides the first linear stability analysis for BSQ diffusion that can constrain transport coefficients in future heavy-ion collision simulations at finite baryon density.
