# Quark and gluon distribution functions in a viscous quark-gluon plasma medium and dilepton production via $q\bar{q}$-- annihilation

> Viscous modifications to the thermal distributions of quark-antiquarks and gluons have been studied in a quasi-particle description of the quark-gluon-plasma medium created in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. The model is described in terms of quasi-partons that encode the hot QCD medium effects in their respective effective fugacities. Both shear and bulk viscosities have been taken in to account in the analysis and the modifications to thermal distributions have been obtained by modifying the the energy momentum tensor in view of the non-trivial dispersion relations for the gluons and quarks. The interactions encoded in the equation of state induce significant modifications to the thermal distributions. As an implication, dilepton production rate in the $q\bar{q}$ annihilation process has been investigated. The equation of state is found to have significant impact on the dilepton production rate along with the viscosities.

## Key claim

Viscous modifications to quark and gluon thermal distributions in the quark-gluon plasma are obtained by coupling a quasi-particle model with non-trivial dispersion relations to kinetic theory, and these modifications significantly affect the dilepton production rate beyond what is captured by ideal-gas or simple viscous corrections; the effect depends on the specific lattice QCD equation of state used, and the results are reliable only for temperatures above the QCD transition temperature where the quasi-particle model is valid.

## Method

The authors employ an effective fugacity quasi-particle model that encodes hot QCD medium effects through temperature-dependent fugacities \(z_g\) and \(z_q\), which modify the single-particle dispersion relations for gluons and quarks. They obtain viscous corrections to the equilibrium distribution functions by matching the kinetic theory expression for the energy-momentum tensor (modified to incorporate the non-trivial dispersions) with the hydrodynamic decomposition containing shear and bulk viscous stresses, using a quadratic ansatz for the perturbation. The resulting viscous-modified distributions are then used to compute the dilepton production rate from \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation, evaluating the contributions from equilibrium, shear viscosity, and bulk viscosity separately.

## Key numbers

- The quasi-particle model uses effective fugacities \(z_g\) and \(z_q\) whose temperature dependence is fixed from lattice QCD equation of state data from Cheng et al. (2008), with the model reliable for temperatures above \(T_c\) and beyond approximately \(1.5 T_c\) where strange quark mass effects can be neglected.
- The integrals \(I_g\) and \(I_q\) that determine the viscous correction coefficients are expressed analytically in the high-temperature limit as \(I_g = \frac{4 \nu_g T^3}{\pi^2 \mathcal{S}} \text{PolyLog}[5, z_g]\) and \(I_q = -\frac{4 \nu_q T^3}{\pi^2 \mathcal{S}} \text{PolyLog}[5, -z_q]\), where \(\nu_g = 2(N_c^2-1) = 16\) for gluons and \(\nu_q = 2 \times 2 \times N_c \times 3 = 36\) for quarks with \(N_c=3\).
- The equilibrium dilepton production rate is modulated by a factor \(z_q^2\), which is shown to deviate significantly from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit (\(z_q \rightarrow 1\)) even at temperatures up to \(3.5 T_c\).
- The shear viscous correction to the dilepton rate contains a factor \(\frac{1}{4 \nu_q T^3 \text{PolyLog}[5,-z_q]/\pi^2 \mathcal{S}}\), and the bulk viscous correction contains terms proportional to \(\frac{2}{3} \frac{2 \zeta}{10 \mathcal{S} T^3} p^\alpha p^\beta \Delta_{\alpha\beta} \Theta\) and \(-\frac{2}{5} \frac{\zeta}{4 \mathcal{S} T^3} M^2 \Theta\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle description of hot QCD developed by Chandra and Ravishankar (2011) to include viscous effects, building on the approach of Dusling (2010) who used effective mass quasi-particle models. The results enable more realistic modeling of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating both the lattice QCD equation of state and viscous corrections, going beyond the ideal QGP equation of state used in earlier works such as Bhatt et al. (2011). The formalism can be applied to study other observables like photon production and heavy quark transport in a viscous QGP medium with realistic equation of state effects.
