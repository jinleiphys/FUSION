# Diffractive Dijet Production in Deep Inelastic Scattering and Photon-Hadron Collisions in the Color Glass Condensate

> We study exclusive dijet production in coherent diffractive processes in deep inelastic scattering and real (and virtual) photon-hadron ($γ^{(*)}$-h) collisions in the Color Glass Condensate formalism at leading order. We show that the diffractive dijet cross section is sensitive to the color-dipole orientation in the transverse plane, and is a good probe of possible correlations between the $q\bar{q}$-dipole transverse separation vector $\r$ and the dipole impact parameter $\b$. We also investigate the diffractive dijet azimuthal angle correlations and $t$-distributions in $γ^{(*)}$-h collisions and show that they are sensitive to gluon saturation effects in the small-$x$ region. In particular, we show that the $t$-distribution of diffractive dijet photo-production off a proton target exhibits a dip-type structure in the saturation region. This effect is similar to diffractive vector meson production. Besides, at variance with the inclusive case, the effect of saturation leads to stronger azimuthal correlations between the jets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that exclusive diffractive dijet production in deep inelastic scattering and photon-hadron collisions is sensitive to the orientation of the color-dipole in the transverse plane, and that the cross section can be written in terms of a convolution of two forward dipole amplitudes. The authors claim that the t-distribution of diffractive dijet photoproduction off a proton target exhibits a dip-type structure in the saturation region, similar to diffractive vector meson production, but note that this result depends on the assumption of azimuthal symmetry in the dipole amplitude and the use of impact-parameter dependent saturation models whose parameters were fitted to HERA data at low virtualities.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order cross section for exclusive diffractive dijet production in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, where the virtual photon wavefunction interacts eikonally with the target described as a classical shockwave field. The diffractive cross section is obtained by averaging over target color configurations at the amplitude level (rather than at the cross section level as in inclusive processes), yielding expressions in terms of the dipole operator S_01 and the dipole amplitude N_01. The final formulas are simplified by assuming azimuthal symmetry (independence of the dipole amplitude on the angle between the dipole transverse separation vector r and the impact parameter b), and are evaluated numerically using the IP-Sat and b-CGC saturation models with parameters fixed by fits to HERA data.

## Key numbers

- The diffractive dijet cross section for longitudinal photons contains a factor 8 z_0^2 z_1^2 epsilon^2, where epsilon^2 = z_0 z_1 Q^2, and involves Bessel functions K_0(epsilon r) and K_0(epsilon r')
- The diffractive dijet cross section for transverse photons contains a factor 2[z_0^2 + z_1^2] (r dot r')/(r^2 r'^2) and involves Bessel functions [epsilon r K_1(epsilon r)] and [epsilon r' K_1(epsilon r')]
- For numerical results, the authors use proton beam energy E_p = 5 TeV and photon-proton center-of-mass energy W_gamma p = 4 TeV
- For photoproduction, the virtuality is taken as Q^2 approximately 0.04 to 0.1 GeV^2
- Light quark masses are taken as m_q approximately 4 to 10 MeV
- The saturation models are considered reliable for p_T less than 6 GeV and for x less than 0.01 with Q^2 less than 40-45 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based calculations that focused on inclusive dijet production (Mueller 2012, Mueller 2013) to the exclusive diffractive case, showing that diffractive dijet production involves only the dipole gluon distribution (two-point functions) rather than the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution required for inclusive dijets. The dip-type structure in the t-distribution is similar to that reported for diffractive vector meson photoproduction (Rezaeian 2013), but the authors note that diffractive dijet production is free from final-state hadronization effects, making it a cleaner probe. The results enable tests of gluon saturation effects at the LHC and in future electron-proton colliders, and provide predictions that can distinguish between saturation models (IP-Sat, b-CGC) and the non-saturation 1-Pomeron model.
