# Neutrino nucleus cross sections

> We present the results of our calculation which has been performed to study the nuclear effects in the quasielastic, inelastic and deep inelastic scattering of neutrinos(antineutrinos) from nuclear targets. These calculations are done in the local density approximation. We take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb effect, renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium in the case of the quasielastic reaction. The inelastic reaction leading to production of pions is calculated in a $Δ$- dominance model taking into account the renormalization of $Δ$ properties in the nuclear medium and the final state interaction effects of the outgoing pions with the residual nucleus. We discuss the nuclear effects in the $F_{3}^{A}(x)$ structure function in the deep inelastic neutrino(antineutrino) reaction using a relativistic framework to describe the nucleon spectral function in the nucleus.

# Neutrino nucleus cross sections

## Key claim
Nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb corrections, RPA correlations, and pion final-state interactions) significantly reduce neutrino-nucleus cross sections for quasielastic, inelastic, and deep inelastic processes, with the magnitude of reduction depending on neutrino energy and target nucleus; the calculations are performed in the local density approximation and are intended to improve upon the simpler Fermi gas models used in existing Monte Carlo event generators.

## Method
The authors calculate charged-current quasielastic lepton production cross sections using a local Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb distortion of the outgoing muon, and RPA correlations via a nucleon-nucleon potential with pion and rho exchanges. Inelastic one-pion production is treated in a Δ-dominance model with medium-modified Δ mass and width (from Oset et al.) and Monte Carlo simulation of pion final-state interactions. Deep inelastic structure functions F₃^A(x,Q²) for iron are computed using a relativistic nucleon spectral function in the local density approximation, incorporating binding, off-mass-shell, and Fermi motion effects.

## Key numbers
- For quasielastic ν_μ on ¹²C at E_ν = 1 GeV, total reduction in cross section is about 20% when RPA effects are included (compared to free nucleon case); at E_ν = 0.4 GeV the reduction is about 40%, and at E_ν = 2 GeV about 18%.
- For charged-current one π⁺ production on ¹²C at E_ν = 1 GeV, nuclear medium effects reduce the cross section by 30-35% in the 1-2 GeV range, with a further 15% reduction from pion absorption, giving a total reduction of about 45%.
- For neutral-current π⁰ production on ¹²C at E_ν = 1 GeV, the reduction in the pion momentum distribution peak is about 40% from nuclear medium effects, with an additional 15% from pion absorption.
- The ratio R = F₃^A(x)/(A F₃^N(x)) for iron at Q² = 5 GeV² shows suppression for x < 0.7 and enhancement for x > 0.7, with the GLS integral deficit ΔGLS = (1/3)(3 - ∫₀¹ F₃^A dx) in reasonable agreement with CCFR, CHARM, and IHEP-JINR experimental data for Q² > 5 GeV².

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by the same group (Singh et al., Athar et al.) for quasielastic, inelastic, and deep inelastic neutrino-nucleus reactions, using the local density approximation and nuclear many-body theory. It directly addresses the need for improved neutrino-nucleus cross sections in Monte Carlo generators (NUANCE, NEUGEN, NEUT) that currently rely on simpler models such as Llewellyn Smith (1972) for quasielastic, Rein and Sehgal (1981) for inelastic, and GRV98 with Bodek modifications for deep inelastic scattering. The results are compared with those of Nieves et al., Amaro et al., Kulagin and Petti (2006), Hirai et al., and Tzanov et al. for deep inelastic structure functions. The calculations enable more accurate modeling of neutrino oscillation experiments (MiniBooNE, K2K, T2K, NOνA, MINERνA) that use nuclear targets such as ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ar, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb in the few-GeV energy region.
