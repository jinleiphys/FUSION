# The Dimensionally Reduced Effective Theory for Quarks in High Temperature QCD

> We show that QCD undergoes dimensional reduction at high temperatures also in the quark sector. In the kinematic region relevant to screening physics, where the lowest Matsubara modes are close to their ``mass-shells'', all static Green's functions involving both quarks and gluons, are reproducible in the high-$T$ limit by a renormalizable three dimensional Lagrangian up to order $\tilde{g}^2(T)\sim 1/ln T$. This three dimensional theory only contains explicitly the lightest bosonic and fermionic Matsubara modes, while the heavier modes correct the tree-level couplings and generate extra local vertices. We also find that the quark degrees of freedom that have been retained in the reduced theory are nonrelativistic in the high-$T$ limit. We then improve our result to order $\tilde{g}^4(T)$ through an explicit nonrelativistic expansion, in the spirit of the heavy quark effective theory. This effective theory is relevant for studying QCD screening phenomena with observables made from quarks, e.g. mesonic and baryonic currents, already at temperatures not much higher than the chiral transition temperature $T_c$.

## Key claim

The authors show that QCD undergoes dimensional reduction in the quark sector at high temperatures, but only in the kinematic region where the lowest Matsubara modes are close to their mass-shells; the effective three-dimensional theory reproduces static Green's functions involving both quarks and gluons up to order \(\tilde{g}^2(T)\sim 1/\ln T\), with the quark degrees of freedom becoming nonrelativistic in the high-temperature limit.

## Method

The authors Fourier transform the four-dimensional Euclidean QCD Lagrangian in the temporal direction, retaining only the static gluonic modes (zero Matsubara frequency) and the lightest fermionic Matsubara modes (\(\omega_\pm = \pm \pi T\)). They then compute one-loop corrections from the heavy Matsubara modes to the quark self-energy and quark-gluon vertex, expanding around the mass-shell condition in a (2+1)-dimensional Minkowskian interpretation, and match these corrections to local vertices in a three-dimensional renormalizable Lagrangian. They further improve the result to order \(\tilde{g}^4(T)\) by performing a nonrelativistic expansion analogous to the heavy quark effective theory.

## Key numbers

- The effective theory is valid up to order \(\tilde{g}^2(T)\sim 1/\ln T\) for the leading reduced Lagrangian, and up to order \(\tilde{g}^4(T)\) after the nonrelativistic expansion.
- The lightest fermionic Matsubara modes have tree-level masses of \(\pi T\), while the next lightest mode is at \(3\pi T\).
- The coupling in the reduced theory is \(g_3^2 = g^2(\mu) T\), with the subtraction scale chosen as \(\mu = (4\pi e^{-\gamma_E - c}) T\).
- The quark self-energy correction from heavy modes is suppressed relative to the tree-level piece by a factor of \(\mathcal{G}^2(T) \equiv \tilde{g}^2(T)/(16\pi^2)\).
- The numerical constants appearing in the self-energy involve \(\zeta'(-1) \approx -0.16542\) and \(\gamma_E \approx 0.57722\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of dimensional reduction, previously applied to pure gluonic sectors by Ginsparg (1980), Appelquist and Pisarski (1981), Nadkarni (1983), Landsman (1989), and Kajantie et al. (1990s), to observables that explicitly involve quarks, such as mesonic and baryonic screening correlators. The authors had previously demonstrated a similar mechanism in the Gross-Neveu model (Huang and Lissia, 1994). The resulting effective theory enables nonperturbative lattice studies of screening phenomena with quark observables at temperatures not much higher than the chiral transition temperature \(T_c\), and provides a systematic framework for computing screening mass splittings and masses.
