# The $πf_0(500)$ decay of the $a_1(1260)$

> We evaluate the $a_1(1260) \to πσ(f_0(500))$ decay width from the perspective that the $a_1(1260)$ resonance is dynamically generated from the pseudoscalar-vector interaction and the $σ$ arises from the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interaction. A triangle mechanism with $a_1(1260) \to ρπ$ followed by $ρ\to ππ$ and a fusion of two pions within the loop to produce the $σ$ provides the mechanism for this decay under these assumptions for the nature of the two resonances. We obtain widths of the order of $13-22$ MeV. Present experimental results differ substantially from each other, suggesting that extra efforts should be devoted to the precise extraction of this important partial decay width, which should provide valuable information on the nature of the axial vector and scalar meson resonances and help clarify the role of the $πσ$ channel in recent lattice QCD calculations of the $a_1$.

## Key claim

The $a_1(1260) \to \pi\sigma(f_0(500))$ decay width is evaluated under the specific assumption that the $a_1(1260)$ is dynamically generated from pseudoscalar-vector interaction and the $\sigma$ arises from pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interaction, yielding widths of 13-22 MeV that depend critically on how the $\sigma$ mass distribution is treated.

## Method

The decay is modeled through a triangle mechanism where the $a_1(1260)$ decays to $\rho\pi$, the $\rho$ decays to $\pi\pi$, and two pions within the loop fuse to produce the $\sigma$ meson. The amplitude is computed using couplings from the chiral unitary approach, with the $a_1\to\rho\pi$ coupling taken from Ref. Luis and the $\sigma\to\pi\pi$ coupling from the unitarized $\pi\pi$ interaction of Ref. npa. The width is obtained by integrating over the $\sigma$ mass distribution using the imaginary part of the $I=0$ $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude, followed by a convolution with the $a_1(1260)$ mass distribution using COMPASS parameters.

## Key numbers

- Partial decay width $\Gamma(a_1(1260)\to\pi\sigma) = 13.4$ MeV (central value from the chiral unitary approach)
- Branching ratio $\mathcal{B}(a_1(1260)\to\pi\sigma) \simeq (3.3-4.0)\%$ based on the $a_1$ width of $367\pm 9^{+28}_{-25}$ MeV from COMPASS
- Width range of $13-22$ MeV when scaling by different $\sigma\pi\pi$ couplings from the literature (Caprini: $g=3.58\pm0.03$ GeV giving 19.3 MeV; Pelaez-Rios: $g=3.5$ GeV giving 18.3 MeV; Masjuan: $g=3.8\pm0.4$ GeV giving 21.6 MeV)
- The $a_1\to\rho\pi$ coupling used: $g_{a_1,\rho\pi}=(-3795+i\,2330)$ MeV
- The $\sigma\to\pi\pi$ coupling used: $g_{\sigma,\pi\pi}=\sqrt{2}\,(1248-i\,2717)$ MeV
- Cutoff parameters: $q_\text{max}=800$ MeV for the $a_1$ vertex, $q'_\text{max}=750$ MeV for the $\sigma$ vertex

## Context

This work extends the triangle mechanism approach previously applied to $f_1(1285)\to\pi a_0(980)$ decay by Aceti and to $a_1(1260)\to\pi f_0(980)$ by AceDai, which was originally misidentified as the $a_1(1420)$ resonance by COMPASS. The calculation is complementary to the three-body framework of ZhangXie for $a_1(1260)\to\pi^+\pi^+\pi^-$ decay, where the $\pi\sigma$ mode was not isolated. The results are essential for assessing systematic effects in recent lattice QCD calculations of the $a_1(1260)$ by Mai (2021), where the $\pi\sigma$ channel is the largest source of uncertainty in the pole extraction. The predicted branching ratio of $(3.3-6.6)\%$ overlaps with the range $(3.6-9)\%$ extracted from the recent BESIII experiment by newbes.
