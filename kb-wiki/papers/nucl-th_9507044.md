# Density Dependent Hadron Field Theory

> A fully covariant approach to a density dependent hadron field theory is presented. The relation between in--medium NN interactions and field--theoretical meson--nucleon vertices is discussed. The medium dependence of nuclear interactions is described by a functional dependence of the meson--nucleon vertices on the baryon field operators. As a consequence, the Euler--Lagrange equations lead to baryon rearrangement self--energies which are not obtained when only a parametric dependence of the vertices on the density is assumed. It is shown that the approach is energy--momentum conserving and thermodynamically consistent. Solutions of the field equations are studied in the mean--field approximation. Descriptions of the medium dependence in terms of the baryon scalar and vector density are investigated. Applications to infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei are discussed. Density dependent coupling constants obtained from Dirac--Brueckner calculations with the Bonn NN-potentials are used. Results from Hartree calculations for energy spectra, binding energies and charge density distributions of $^{16}O$, $^{40,48}Ca$ and $^{208}Pb$ are presented. Comparisons to data strongly support the importance of rearrangement in a relativistic density dependent field theory. Most striking is the simultanuous improvement of charge radii, charge densities and binding energies. The results indicate the appearance of a new "Coester line" in the nuclear matter equation of state.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully covariant density dependent hadron field theory in which the meson–nucleon vertices depend functionally on the baryon field operators, leading to baryon rearrangement self‑energies that are not obtained when the vertices depend only parametrically on the density; this approach is shown to be energy‑momentum conserving and thermodynamically consistent.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic field theory where the meson‑nucleon coupling strengths are treated as functionals of the baryon field operators, then derive the Euler‑Lagrange equations including rearrangement terms. They solve the field equations in the mean‑field approximation, using density‑dependent coupling constants obtained from Dirac‑Brueckner calculations with the Bonn NN‑potentials, and apply the model to infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Hartree calculations for energy spectra, binding energies and charge density distributions are presented for \(^{16}\text{O}\), \(^{40,48}\text{Ca}\) and \(^{208}\text{Pb}\).
- The results indicate the appearance of a new “Coester line” in the nuclear matter equation of state.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean‑field approach by incorporating a functional density dependence of the meson‑nucleon vertices, going beyond earlier parametric treatments. It builds on Dirac‑Brueckner calculations with the Bonn NN‑potentials and aims to improve simultaneous descriptions of charge radii, charge densities and binding energies in finite nuclei. The results open the way to thermodynamically consistent, covariant descriptions of nuclear matter and finite‑nucleus structure.
