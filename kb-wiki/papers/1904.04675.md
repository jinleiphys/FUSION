# Application of the complex scaling method in quantum scattering theory

> This manuscript, presented to receive habilitated doctor degree, contains an overview of my recent works in developing complex scaling method and its applications in the few-body physics.

## Key claim

This habilitation thesis claims that the complex scaling method can be extended to solve few-body scattering problems involving more than two particles, including cases with Coulomb interactions and breakup channels, despite the long-standing belief that the technique is inappropriate for long-range interactions and collisions with incoming waves.

## Method

The author develops and applies the complex scaling method to few-body scattering problems by transforming the Schrödinger equation using an analytical coordinate rotation, which converts outgoing scattering waves into exponentially bound functions. For collisions involving incoming waves, the method requires special treatment of the diverging incoming-wave components, and for three-body systems with Coulomb interactions, the approach is combined with distorted wave and Faddeev-Merkuriev equation formalisms. The method is implemented using Jacobi coordinates and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, with numerical solutions obtained via expansion in square-integrable basis functions.

## Key numbers

- Three-nucleon scattering calculations (neutron and proton on deuteron) using a simplistic nucleon-nucleon interaction model produced "surprisingly accurate" results for both breakup and elastic scattering amplitudes using "very limited numerical resources" compared to conventional calculations.
- Deuteron scattering on 12C nucleus calculations included elastic d+12C, neutron transfer to p+13C, and deuteron breakup n+p+12C channels, producing "very accurate results" for elastic, transfer, and breakup reaction cross sections when compared with an alternative momentum-space approach.
- Four-nucleon reactions (p+3He and n+3H collisions) with both three-cluster and four-nucleon breakup channels produced "very reliable results."
- Electron scattering on ground states of Hydrogen and Positronium atoms, plus the e+ + H(n=1) ↔ p + Ps(n=1) reaction, produced "accurate results" in a wide energy region extending beyond the atom ionization threshold.

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method from its original applications in calculating resonance eigenvalues in atomic physics (Ho 1983, Mo 1998) and the pioneering but interrupted scattering work of Nuttall et al. (1972) to general few-body collisions. It builds on the spectral function formalism variant developed by Kato, Giraud et al. (2001-2004) and the demonstration by Kruppa et al. (2007) that collisions with residual Coulomb interaction could be addressed for two-particle cases. The method enables description of complex many-particle reactions involving multiple transfer, rearrangement, and breakup channels where conventional scattering theory methods requiring explicit treatment of boundary conditions fail or become technically overcomplicated.
