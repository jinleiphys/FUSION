# Ab-initio calculations of four-nucleon elastic scattering

> We present microscopic calculations of low energy scattering observables in all possible four nucleon systems : n-3H, p-3He and p-3H. Results were obtained by solving Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space, appropriately modified to include Coulomb and three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that ab-initio four-nucleon scattering calculations using Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space can reproduce experimental observables, but only when three-nucleon forces (3NF) are included; without 3NF, realistic NN potentials overestimate zero-energy cross sections and fail to correctly position the virtual state in p-3H scattering.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space for all four-nucleon elastic scattering systems (n-3H, p-3He, p-3H). They modified the equations to include Coulomb interactions by splitting the Coulomb potential into long-range and short-range parts using a cutoff function, following Merkuriev's approach for three-body equations. Three-nucleon forces were incorporated following the method of Gloeckle for four identical particles, and the partial wave components were expanded in a basis of three-dimensional splines to convert the integro-differential equations into a system of linear equations.

## Key numbers
- n-3H scattering lengths: MT I-III potential gives 4.10 fm (Jπ=0+) and 3.63 fm (Jπ=1+); Av.14 gives 4.28 fm and 3.81 fm; Av.18+UIX gives 4.04 fm and 3.60 fm.
- p-3He scattering lengths: MT I-III gives 11.5 fm (Jπ=0+) and 9.20 fm (Jπ=1+); Av.14 gives 12.7 fm (Jπ=0+); Av.18+UIX gives 11.3 fm (Jπ=0+).
- p-3H scattering lengths: MT I-III gives -63.1 fm (Jπ=0+) and 5.50 fm (Jπ=1+); Av.14 gives -13.9 fm and 5.77 fm; Av.18+UIX gives -16.5 fm and 5.39 fm.
- n-3H total cross section shows a resonance peak at center-of-mass energy Ecm ≈ 3 MeV.
- The 0+ excitation of 4He is located at ER ≈ 0.4 MeV above the p-3H threshold with width Γ ≈ 0.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior Faddeev-Yakubovski calculations by Carbonell (2002) and Fonseca (1999) to include Coulomb and three-nucleon forces, enabling realistic treatment of all four-nucleon scattering channels. The results contradict earlier calculations that ignored Coulomb effects, which incorrectly placed the 4He 0+ state as a bound state below the n-3He threshold. The inclusion of UIX-3NF with the Av.18 NN potential brings p-3H scattering lengths and excitation functions into agreement with experimental data from Pt_exp, whereas pure two-nucleon force models fail. This enables future extension of calculations above the n-3He threshold and detailed analysis of resonance properties.
