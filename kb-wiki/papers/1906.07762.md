# Nonlinear Langevin dynamics via holography

> In this work, we consider non-linear corrections to the Langevin effective theory of a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled CFT plasma. In AdS/CFT, this system can be identified with that of a string stretched between the boundary and the horizon of an asymptotically AdS black-brane solution. We compute the Feynman-Vernon influence phase for the heavy quark by evaluating the Nambu-Goto action on a doubled string configuration. This configuration is the linearised solution of the string motion in the doubled black-brane geometry which has been proposed as the holographic dual of a thermal Schwinger-Keldysh contour of the CFT. Our expression for the influence phase passes non-trivial consistency conditions arising from the underlying unitarity and thermality of the bath. The local effective theory obeys the recently proposed non-linear fluctuation dissipation theorem relating the non-Gaussianity of thermal noise to the thermal jitter in the damping constant. This furnishes a non-trivial check for the validity of these relations derived in the weak coupling regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the universal non-linear Langevin dynamics previously derived for weakly coupled baths continues to describe the non-linearities in a strongly coupled system, specifically, a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled CFT plasma, thereby providing a non-trivial check of the recently proposed non-linear fluctuation dissipation theorem that relates non-Gaussianity of thermal noise to thermal jitter in the damping constant.

## Method

The authors compute the Feynman-Vernon influence phase for a heavy quark by evaluating the Nambu-Goto action on a doubled string configuration in an asymptotically AdS black-brane geometry, which serves as the holographic dual of a thermal Schwinger-Keldysh contour of the CFT. The string configuration is constructed as a linearised solution of the string motion in the doubled black-brane spacetime, following the near-horizon prescription of Glorioso-Crossley-Liu (2018) to handle the IR divergences that previously obstructed non-linear corrections. The resulting influence phase is then expanded in a derivative expansion to extract the local effective theory parameters.

## Key numbers

* The inverse Hawking temperature of the black brane is given by β = 4π/(d r_h), where r_h is the horizon radius and d is the spacetime dimension.
* The non-linear fluctuation dissipation relation derived is ζ_N = -(12/(β m_p)) ζ_γ, connecting the non-Gaussianity parameter ζ_N to the thermal jitter in damping ζ_γ.
* The metric of the asymptotically AdS_{d+1} black brane in ingoing Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates is ds² = 2 dv dr - r²(1 - r_h^d/r^d) dv² + r² dx²_{d-1}, with the AdS radius set to unity.

## Context

This work extends the non-linear Langevin theory developed by Chakrabarty et al. (2018, 2019) for weakly coupled baths, where general sum rules relating non-linear Langevin parameters to thermal spectral functions were derived, to the strongly coupled regime using holography. The holographic Brownian motion model, previously studied by de Boer (2008), Son and Teaney (2009), Herzog (2006), and others, serves as a testbed for the non-linear fluctuation dissipation theorem. The computation implements the near-horizon prescription of Glorioso-Crossley-Liu (2018) for holographic Schwinger-Keldysh saddle points, extending the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to interacting fields for the first time, and enables future systematic studies of real-time questions within string theory.
