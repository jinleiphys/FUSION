# Nuclear moments of inertia and wobbling motions in triaxial superdeformed nuclei

> The wobbling motion excited on triaxial superdeformed nuclei is studied in terms of the cranked shell model plus random phase approximation. Firstly, by calculating at a low rotational frequency the γ-dependence of the three moments of inertia associated with the wobbling motion, the mechanism of the appearance of the wobbling motion in positive-γnuclei is clarified theoretically -- the rotational alignment of the πi_{13/2} quasiparticle(s) is the essential condition. This indicates that the wobbling motion is a collective motion that is sensitive to the single-particle alignment. Secondly, we prove that the observed unexpected rotational-frequency dependence of the wobbling frequency is an outcome of the rotational-frequency dependent dynamical moments of inertia.

## Key claim

The wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei with positive γ-deformation becomes possible only when the rotational alignment of πi13/2 quasiparticle(s) makes the total moment of inertia about the rotation axis (Jx) larger than that about the y-axis (Jy), contrary to the irrotational-flow expectation; the observed decreasing wobbling frequency with increasing rotational frequency is explained by the frequency-dependent dynamical moments of inertia, not by constant moments.

## Method

The authors employed the cranked Nilsson model plus random phase approximation (RPA) with residual doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, treating the quadrupole deformation parameters (ε2, γ) and pairing gaps (Δn, Δp) as fixed parameters. They solved the RPA dispersion relation for the wobbling mode (signature α=1) in even-even 168Hf and odd-Z 167Lu, calculating the three moments of inertia (Jx, Jy(eff), Jz(eff)) and interband E2 transition amplitudes as functions of γ, ε2, pairing gaps, and rotational frequency ℏωrot.

## Key numbers

- For 168Hf at ℏωrot = 0.25 MeV with ε2 = 0.43, γ = 20°, Δn = Δp = 0.3 MeV: the calculated transition quadrupole moment Qt = 11.4+1.1-1.2 eb reproduces the measured value.
- The interband B(E2) ratio B(E2:I→I-1)out / B(E2:I→I-2)in is typically ~0.1 for 168Hf at the same parameters.
- For the wobbling mode in 168Hf, the RPA wave function gives a collectivity amplitude |cn=wob| ≃ 0.6–0.8 in the sum rule, accounting for about one half of the measured interband B(E2) strength.
- In 167Lu, the wobbling frequency decreases at high ℏωrot in each configuration region, but the calculation does not reproduce the experimental data quantitatively; the calculated Jx exceeds the experimentally deduced value by about 20–30%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RPA treatment of wobbling motion developed by Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1990s) and Matsuzaki (1990s) for normally-deformed and negative-γ nuclei, applying it to the triaxial superdeformed (TSD) bands first observed in 163Lu by the group of Ragnarsson (2001). The paper resolves the contradiction that irrotational moments of inertia (Jy > Jx for γ>0) would forbid wobbling, showing that aligned quasiparticle contributions reverse the inequality. It enables future microscopic calculations of wobbling in even-even TSD nuclei like 168Hf and 174Hf, where interband γ-rays have not yet been resolved, and suggests that the νj15/2 alignment may cause anomalies in the wobbling frequency.
