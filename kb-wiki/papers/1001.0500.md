# Observation of the Higgs Boson of strong interaction via Compton scattering by the nucleon

> It is shown that the Quark-Level Linear $σ$ Model (QLL$σ$M) leads to a prediction for the diamagnetic term of the polarizabilities of the nucleon which is in excellent agreement with the experimental data. The bare mass of the $σ$ meson is predicted to be $m_σ=666$ MeV and the two-photon width $Γ(σ\toγγ)=(2.6\pm 0.3)$ keV. It is argued that the mass predicted by the QLL$σ$M corresponds to the $γγ\toσ\to NN$ reaction, i.e. to a $t$-channel pole of the $γN\to Nγ$ reaction. Large -angle Compton scattering experiments revealing effects of the $σ$ meson in the differential cross section are discussed. Arguments are presented that these findings may be understood as an observation of the Higgs boson of strong interaction while being part of the constituent quark.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the Quark-Level Linear σ Model (QLLσM) predicts a σ meson bare mass of 666 MeV and a two-photon width of (2.6 ± 0.3) keV, and that this σ meson, observed as a t-channel pole in nucleon Compton scattering, can be understood as the Higgs boson of strong interaction while being part of the constituent quark. The hedge is that this interpretation applies specifically to the σ meson as observed in space-like Compton scattering (γγ → σ → NN), not necessarily to the broader spectrum of scalar mesons below 1 GeV.

## Method
The author applies the Quark-Level Linear σ Model (QLLσM), derived from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model via bosonization, to predict the σ meson mass and its two-photon decay width from quark triangle diagrams. The σ meson pole contribution to nucleon Compton scattering is then calculated using dispersion relations, specifically through the invariant amplitude A₁, and compared with experimental electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon. The t-channel contribution to the polarizability difference (α − β)ᵗ is computed directly from the γγ → σ → NN reaction using the predicted σ meson properties.

## Key numbers
- σ meson bare mass: m_σ = 666.0 MeV, predicted by the QLLσM using the Delbourgo-Scadron relation with g = 2π/√N_c = 3.63, constituent quark mass M = 325.8 MeV, and f_π^cl = 89.8 MeV.
- Two-photon width of the σ meson: Γ(σ → γγ) = (2.6 ± 0.3) keV, derived from quark triangle diagrams with α_e = 1/137.04, f_π = 92.42 MeV, and average constituent quark mass m̂_q = 330 MeV.
- t-channel contribution to the polarizability difference: (α − β)ᵗ = 15.2 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), calculated using M(σ → γγ) = 5α_e/(3πf_π), m_σ = 666 MeV, and g_σNN = 13.169 ± 0.057.
- σ and non-σ components of polarizabilities for proton and neutron: α_p(σ) = −β_p = 7.6, α_p(non-σ) = 4.4, β_p(non-σ) = 9.5, α_n(non-σ) = 5.8, β_n(non-σ) = 9.4 (all in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³).
- Weighted average of eight γγ → ππ analyses gives Γ(σ → γγ) = (2.3 ± 0.4) keV, consistent with the QLLσM prediction.

## Context
This work extends the author's systematic series of studies on the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon (Schumacher 2006, 2007a, 2007b, 2008, 2009) and prior experimental Compton scattering work reviewed in Schumacher (2005). It directly builds on the QLLσM developed by Delbourgo and Scadron (1995) and the NJL model framework. The paper contrasts its direct t-channel calculation using the γγ → σ → NN reaction with earlier approaches by Bernabeu et al. (1974) that relied on the two-step process γγ → σ → ππ and ππ → σ → NN via the BEFT sum rule. The findings enable a precise determination of the scalar t-channel contribution to nucleon polarizabilities and support the interpretation of the σ meson as the Higgs boson of strong interaction, with implications for understanding constituent quark structure and the scalar meson nonet below 1 GeV.
