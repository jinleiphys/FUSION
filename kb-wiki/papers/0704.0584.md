# Effective interactions from q-deformed inspired transformations

> From the mass term for the transformed quark fields, we obtain effective contact interactions of the NJL type. The parameters of the model that maps a system of non-interacting transformed fields into quarks interacting via NJL contact terms are discussed.

# Effective interactions from q-deformed inspired transformations

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a non-interacting system of transformed quark fields can be mapped onto the interacting Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model through a set of quantum algebra inspired transformations, but only under a mean-field approximation and with a temperature- and deformation-dependent mapping function A(T;q) that must be determined numerically.

## Method

The authors start from a mass term for transformed quark fields defined via q-deformed transformations applied to both up and down quark components. They expand the Lagrangian in terms of standard quark fields, reduce the resulting six-point contact interactions to four-point terms using a mean-field approximation, and then compare the resulting Lagrangian to the standard NJL Lagrangian. By requiring equivalence between the two Lagrangians for any temperature T and deformation parameter q, they derive conditions relating the transformed mass M, the NJL coupling G, the bare quark mass m₀, and the mapping function Γ = (q⁻¹−1)/A(T;q).

## Key numbers

- The scalar density ρₛ is computed as ρₛ = −(NcNf/π²)∫₀^Λ dp p² (m/E)[1−n−n̄], with Nc=3, Nf=2, and Λ the momentum cutoff.
- The vector density ρᵥ is computed as ρᵥ = (NcNf/π²)∫₀^Λ dp p² [n−n̄].
- The gap equations are m = m₀ − 2Gρₛ and μ = μ₀ − (G/Nc)ρᵥ.
- The parameter α is defined as α = −2G/(m₀ρₛ) > 0.
- The two solutions for Γ are Γ± = αρᵥ(1 ± √(1 + 1/(αρᵥ²))), with Γ₋ associated with q<1 and Γ₊ with q>1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of quantum su(2) algebra deformations applied to the NJL model (Timoteo and Lima, earlier works). It builds on prior studies of q-deformed quark fields (Ubri, Trip, and VST) but differs by applying the transformation to both quark components simultaneously rather than only one. The result enables a mapping between a non-interacting deformed system and the interacting NJL model, with the mapping function A(T;q) inheriting the phase transition behavior of the NJL model.
