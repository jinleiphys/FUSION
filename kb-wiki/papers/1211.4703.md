# Net baryon number probability distribution near the chiral phase transition

> We discuss properties of the net baryon number probability distribution near the chiral phase transition to explore the effect of critical fluctuations. Our studies are performed within Landau theory, where the coefficients of the polynomial potential are parametrized, so as to reproduce the mean-field (MF), the $Z(2)$ and $O(4)$ scaling behaviors of the cumulants of the net baryon number. We show, that in the critical region, the structure of the probability distribution changes, dependently on values of the critical exponents. In the MF approach, as well as in the $Z(2)$ universality class, the contribution of the singular part of the thermodynamic potential tends to broaden the distribution. By contrast, in the model with $O(4)$ scaling, the contribution of the singular part results in a narrower net baryon number probability distribution with a wide tail.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the shape of the net baryon number probability distribution near the chiral phase transition depends on the universality class: for mean-field and Z(2) scaling the singular part of the thermodynamic potential broadens the distribution relative to the Skellam distribution, while for O(4) scaling it produces a narrower distribution with a wide tail, but only within the chiral limit where the critical behavior is most prominent.

## Method

The authors model the chiral phase transition using Landau theory, constructing a thermodynamic potential whose regular part yields the Skellam distribution and whose singular part is parametrized to reproduce mean-field, O(4), and Z(2) scaling behaviors by tuning the critical exponent alpha. The canonical partition function and probability distribution P(N) are obtained analytically for the mean-field case (alpha=0) and numerically via contour integration for the O(4) (alpha=-0.21) and Z(2) (alpha=0.11) cases, with the system volume kept finite at V=30 fm^3.

## Key numbers

- The O(4) critical exponent used is alpha = -0.21; the Z(2) critical exponent used is alpha = 0.11.
- The transition temperature at zero chemical potential is set to T_c = 0.15 GeV.
- The volume used for numerical calculations is V = 30 fm^3.
- The numerical integration reproduces the analytical mean-field result up to |N| approximately 60, at which P(N) is about 10^{-12}.
- The probability distribution P^alpha(N) is calculated numerically up to |N| = 67 at T/T_c = 0.99 and mu=0.

## Context

This work extends studies of net baryon number fluctuations as probes of the QCD phase transition, building on prior work by Karsch (2008), Braun-Munzinger (2011), and Skokov (2010) on cumulants and their relation to O(4) criticality. The paper contrasts with the hadron resonance gas model prediction of a Skellam distribution for the net baryon number probability, and complements functional renormalization group studies of the O(4) universality class by Morita (2012). The results enable experimental searches for critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where the STAR Collaboration has measured net proton multiplicity distributions.
