# Calculation of the Thomas-Ehrman shift in $^{16}$F and $^{15}$O(p,p) cross section with the Gamow shell model

> The $^{16}$F nucleus is situated at the proton drip-line and is unbound by proton emission by only about 500 keV. Continuum coupling is then prominent in this nucleus. Added to that, its low-lying spectrum consists of narrow proton resonances as well. It is then a very good candidate to study nuclear structure and reactions at proton drip-line. The low-lying spectrum and scattering proton-proton cross section of $^{16}$F have then been calculated with the coupled-channel Gamow shell model framework for that matter using an effective Hamiltonian. Experimental data are very well reproduced, as well as in its mirror nucleus $^{16}$N. Isospin-symmetry breaking generated by the Coulomb interaction and continuum coupling explicitly appears in our calculations. In particular, the different continuum couplings in $^{16}$F and $^{16}$N involving $s_{1/2}$ partial waves allow to explain the different ordering of low-lying states in their spectrum.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Ehrman shift in the mirror nuclei \(^{16}\)F and \(^{16}\)N is explained by the different continuum couplings involving \(s_{1/2}\) partial waves: the stronger continuum coupling in the \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states of \(^{16}\)F (about 0.35) compared to the \(2^-\) and \(3^-\) states (about 0.2) provides more binding energy, causing the different ordering of low-lying states, while the isospin of many-body eigenstates remains almost exactly \(T=1\) with deviations of order \(10^{-3}\) or less.

## Method

The low-lying spectrum of \(^{16}\)F and the \(^{15}\)O(p,p) scattering cross section were calculated using the coupled-channel Gamow shell model (GSM-CC) with a \(^{12}\)C core plus valence nucleons. The valence nucleons interact via the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) nuclear interaction plus Coulomb, with the model space built from \(spd\) partial waves; the proton part uses a Berggren basis (including \(0p_{1/2}\), \(0d_{5/2}\), \(1s_{1/2}\), \(0d_{3/2}\) poles plus scattering contours discretized with 15 points each), while the neutron part uses harmonic oscillator shells. The Hamiltonian parameters were fitted to low-lying states of \(^{14}\)N, \(^{15}\)O, and \(^{16}\)F, and the same Hamiltonian (with protons and neutrons exchanged) was applied to the mirror nucleus \(^{16}\)N.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{16}\)F nucleus is unbound by proton emission by about 500 keV.
- The maximal deviation of GSM eigenenergies from experiment is around 200 keV for both \(^{16}\)F and \(^{16}\)N.
- The \(^{16}\)N ground state binding energy is about 830 keV too bound with respect to experimental data.
- The proton-emission width of the \(1^-\) state in \(^{16}\)F is larger by a little more than 10 keV compared to experiment; other widths differ by at most 3 keV.
- The expectation values of the kinetic operator and nuclear interaction for protons in \(^{16}\)F are about 8 MeV smaller in the \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states than in the \(2^-\) and \(3^-\) states; in \(^{16}\)N, this value is close to 5 MeV for its neutron part.
- Continuum coupling is about 0.35 in the \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states of \(^{16}\)F and about 0.2 in the \(2^-\) and \(3^-\) states.
- The average proton occupation of the \(s_{1/2}\) partial wave is close to one in the \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states of \(^{16}\)F and about 0.1 in the \(2^-\) and \(3^-\) states.
- The GSM energy differences between \(^{16}\)F and \(^{16}\)N are about 6 MeV for the \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states and 6.3 MeV for the \(2^-\) and \(3^-\) states.
- The GSM-CC cross section peak at the \(2^-\) state is about 1.5 times higher than that of the R-matrix fit of PhysRevC.90.014307.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Gamow shell model and its coupled-channel variant (GSM-CC) to the proton-drip-line nucleus \(^{16}\)F and its mirror \(^{16}\)N, following previous GSM studies of isospin-symmetry breaking in oxygen and carbon isotopes (PhysRevC.100.064303, PhysRevC.103.044319) and the successful description of the \(^{14}\)O(p,p) reaction (PhysRevLett.121.262502). The results enable a quantitative understanding of the Thomas-Ehrman shift as arising from the interplay of Coulomb interaction and continuum coupling, and provide a fully predictive calculation of the \(^{15}\)O(p,p) cross section that reproduces experimental data, though it lies at the upper end of the error bars, suggesting the possible influence of neglected emission channels.
