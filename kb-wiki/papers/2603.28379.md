# The force of attraction between nucleons due to vacuum fluctuation

> In this letter, we derive the interaction energy and the force between two parallel metal plates, arising from quantum vacuum fluctuations when they are very close to each other. We consider the vacuum to be composed of a meson field. In Quantum Hadrodynamics, mesons are the carriers of the nuclear force.

# The force of attraction between nucleons due to vacuum fluctuation

## Key claim
The paper derives an attractive Casimir-like force between two parallel metal plates arising from quantum vacuum fluctuations of a massive scalar pion field, rather than the massless photon field of the standard electromagnetic Casimir effect. The interaction energy and force depend on the pion's Compton wavelength, and the derivation assumes the vacuum is composed of virtual meson fields, with the pion field as the primary example.

## Method
The author calculates the difference in zero-point energy of a massive scalar pion field between two configurations: a perfectly conducting plate placed at a small distance a from one wall of a cubic cavity, and the same plate far away. The energy difference is regularized using an exponential (heat-kernel) regulator and evaluated via the Euler-Maclaurin formula to extract the finite part. The force per unit area is then obtained by differentiating the interaction energy with respect to plate separation.

## Key numbers
- Pion Compton wavelength: λ_c = 1.46 fm (used for numerical plots)
- In the small-separation limit (a ≪ λ_c), the interaction energy per unit area is δE/L² ≈ −21 ħ c π² / (960 a³)
- In the small-separation limit, the force per unit area is F/L² ≈ −21 ħ c π² / (320 a⁴)
- At large separation (a → ∞), the interaction energy per unit area saturates to −ħ c / (24π λ_c³)
- At large separation, the force per unit area tends to zero
- For a ≫ λ_c, the asymptotic force per unit area is F/L² ≈ −ħ c π / (12 a³ λ_c)

## Context
This work extends the concept of the Casimir effect (Casimir, 1948) from massless electromagnetic fields to massive scalar meson fields, motivated by Quantum Hadrodynamics where mesons are the carriers of the nuclear force. The author draws an analogy between proton-neutron fluctuations (p ↔ n + π⁺) and the virtual particle exchanges that produce the Casimir force. The results suggest a possible mechanism for nucleon-nucleon attraction mediated by vacuum fluctuations of the pion field, though the paper does not directly compute nucleon-nucleon potentials. Experimental confirmation of this mesonic Casimir effect could provide new insights into the role of vacuum fluctuations in nuclear binding.
