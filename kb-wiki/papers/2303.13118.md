# Ground state and fission properties of even-$A$ uranium isotopes from multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field model

> The multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFTs) have been developed to study the influence of octupole and triaxial deformations on the ground state and fission properties. In this paper, we present a brief review of the applications of MDC-CDFTs and discuss the results of a systematical study of even-$A$ uranium isotopes with the MDC-RMF model which is one of MDC-CDFTs with pairing correlations treated by using the BCS approach. We examine in detail the two-dimensional potential energy surfaces $E(β_{20},β_{30})$ of these U isotopes and discuss the ground state and fission properties as well as third and fourth minima on the potential energy surfaces. The emphasis is put on the effects of octupole and triaxial deformations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model systematically reproduces ground state and fission properties of even-\(A\) uranium isotopes, but the accuracy is qualified by noting that the model systematically overestimates charge radii by about 1% and that differences with other models (e.g., DRHBc and HFB-14) arise from differing treatments of pairing, continuum effects, and deformation degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors used the MDC-RMF model, one of the multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFTs), with the effective interaction PC-PK1 and pairing correlations treated via the BCS approach with a separable pairing force of finite range. They calculated two-dimensional potential energy surfaces \(E(\beta_{20},\beta_{30})\) for even-\(A\) uranium isotopes from \(^{214}\)U to \(^{350}\)U, assuming axial symmetry, and then broke axial symmetry only near each saddle point to include triaxial deformation effects on barrier heights. Ground state properties, primary barrier heights, and hyperdeformed minima were extracted from these surfaces.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of \(^{242}\)U has a maximal quadrupole deformation \(\beta_{20} = 0.30\) among isotopes in the mass region \(^{226-262}\)U.
- The ground state of \(^{230}\)U has a pear shape with \(\beta_{20} = 0.23\) and \(\beta_{30} = 0.17\).
- For \(^{276}\)U (neutron number \(N=184\)), the primary barrier height is 17.48 MeV, indicating high stability against fission.
- The charge radii calculated for \(^{234}\)U, \(^{236}\)U, and \(^{238}\)U are 5.864 fm, 5.882 fm, and 5.900 fm, respectively; experimental values are 5.829 fm, 5.843 fm, and 5.857 fm, showing agreement within 1%.
- The two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\) for \(^{350}\)U is predicted to be the last bound even-\(A\) uranium isotope; the DRHBc mass table also predicts \(^{350}\)U as the last bound isotope but gives \(S_{2n} = 1.4\) MeV for \(^{342-350}\)U.
- For \(^{326}\)U, the ground state has an oblate shape with \(\beta_{20} = -0.19\); for \(^{332}\)U, \(\beta_{20} = -0.02\) (nearly spherical).
- The inner barrier for \(^{234}\)U from the MDC-RMF model is about 0.5 MeV lower than that from the FUNF program.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of MDC-CDFTs to actinide nuclei, such as studies of fission barriers in actinides by Lu et al. (2012, 2014) and investigations of third minima in light actinides by Zhao et al. (2015). It also compares results with the deformed relativistic Hartree–Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) mass table by Zhang et al. (2022), the HFB-14 calculations by Goriely et al. (2009), and empirical barrier heights from RIPL-3 by Capote et al. (2009) and the FUNF program by Zhang et al. (1993, 2012). The findings enable future studies of fission dynamics and \(\alpha\)-decay half-lives based on the calculated potential energy surfaces, and highlight the need for improved treatment of continuum effects near the neutron drip line.
