# Electron capture and \b{eta}-decay rates for nuclei with A=65-80

> Recently, a list of the top 50 most important electron capture (EC) and $β$-decay (BD) nuclei, averaged throughout the stellar trajectory for $0.500 > Y_e > 0.400$, was published. The current study presents the calculation of EC and BD rates, from the published list with $A = 65\text{--}80$, on a detailed temperature-density grid. The EC and BD rates were calculated using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation model (pn-QRPA). Our calculation did not employ the Brink-Axel hypothesis. A systematic comparison of the current calculation with previous pn-QRPA and independent particle model (IPM) results is presented for the first time. The reported EC rates are nearly the same when compared with the previous pn-QRPA calculation. On the other hand, the reported BD rates are generally smaller by up to an order of magnitude. Comparison with IPM results shows that our calculated rates are larger by two orders of magnitude. The current calculation may contribute to a more realistic simulation of the late phases of stellar evolution and the modeling of X-ray bursts.

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model produces electron capture rates that are nearly identical to previous pn-QRPA calculations for nuclei with A=65-80, but beta-decay rates are generally smaller by up to an order of magnitude; compared to the independent particle model (IPM), both rates are larger by up to two orders of magnitude, with the caveat that these differences are most pronounced at high temperatures and densities.

## Method
The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model to compute electron capture and beta-decay rates for 24 neutron-rich nuclei with A=65-80 on a detailed temperature-density grid. The calculation did not employ the Brink-Axel hypothesis for excited-state Gamow-Teller strength distributions, instead performing a state-by-state summation over Boltzmann-weighted, microscopically estimated GT strengths for all parent excited levels. The GT strength parameters were tuned to reproduce measured beta-decay half-lives, and the model used a large model space up to 7ħω with separable schematic interaction.

## Key numbers
- Electron capture rates from pn-QRPA-21 are bigger than IPM-03 rates by up to two orders of magnitude at high temperatures
- Beta-decay rates from pn-QRPA-21 are bigger than IPM-03 rates by up to two orders of magnitude at all temperatures and densities
- pn-QRPA-21 beta-decay rates are generally smaller than pn-QRPA-04 rates by up to an order of magnitude at high temperatures for all densities
- Rates were computed for temperatures from 0.1 to 30 GK and densities from 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3
- The constant D in the rate formula was taken as 6143 s
- The ratio g_A/g_V was taken as -1.2694

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of Nabi (2021) for the top 50 most important electron capture and beta-decay nuclei, focusing on the A=65-80 mass range. It provides the first systematic comparison of these rates with the earlier pn-QRPA calculation of Nabi (2004) and the IPM rates of Pruet (2003), which have been used in many core-collapse simulation codes. The results may contribute to more realistic simulations of late stellar evolution phases and X-ray burst modeling, and the authors note that a detailed calculation for proton-rich nuclei with A=80-110 is in progress.
