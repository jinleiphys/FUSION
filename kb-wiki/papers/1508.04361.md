# Inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in dense neutron-star matter

> An increasing number of model results suggests that chiral symmetry is broken inhomogeneously in a certain window at intermediate densities in the QCD phase diagram. This could have significant effects on the properties of compact stars, possibly leading to new astrophysical signatures. In this contribution we discuss this idea by reviewing recent results on inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking under an astrophysics-oriented perspective. After introducing two commonly studied spatial modulations of the chiral condensate, the chiral density wave and the real kink crystal, we focus on their properties and their effect on the equation of state of quark matter. We also describe how these crystalline phases are affected by different elements which are required for a realistic description of a compact star, such as charge neutrality, the presence of magnetic fields, vector interactions and the interplay with color-superconductivity. Finally, we discuss possible signatures of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in the core of compact stars, considering the cases of mass-radius relations and neutrino emissivity explicitly.

## Key claim

Inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking is a robust model prediction for the QCD phase diagram at intermediate densities relevant to compact stars, but the actual extension of the inhomogeneous window is still uncertain, with Dyson-Schwinger and some NJL studies suggesting a very wide range of densities while other models predict a smaller size.

## Method

The authors review results from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model calculations in the mean-field approximation, considering two one-dimensional spatial modulations of the chiral condensate: the chiral density wave (CDW) and the real kink crystal (RKC). They analyze how these crystalline phases are affected by charge neutrality, magnetic fields, vector interactions, and color superconductivity, and discuss possible astrophysical signatures in mass-radius relations and neutrino emissivity.

## Key numbers

- The spatial period of both CDW and RKC modulations is roughly of order 2-3 fm at zero temperature
- For the RKC ansatz, the inhomogeneous phase starts essentially at zero baryon density, while for the CDW solution the minimum density is around 1.4 n0 (where n0 ≈ 0.5 fm^-3 is the quark number density in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation)
- The upper density of the inhomogeneous island is about 2.2 n0 for both modulations
- For electrically neutral matter at T=0, the isospin chemical potential μI was found to be around 70-80 MeV in the inhomogeneous phase
- Magnetic field values considered: eH = (28 MeV)^2 (H = 4.4 × 10^17 G) and eH = (90 MeV)^2 (H = 4.5 × 10^18 G)

## Context

This review extends earlier work on inhomogeneous chiral condensates, particularly the studies by Nickel (2009) who derived the RKC ansatz from 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model solutions, and by Frolov (2010) who calculated CDW quark energies in magnetic fields. The paper builds on the observation that inhomogeneous phases might significantly affect compact star properties, as previously discussed for crystalline color superconductivity by Anglani (2013). The results enable future work on realistic neutron star modeling, including the effects of isospin asymmetry studied by Nowakowski (2015) and the flavor-mixing interactions estimated by Frank (2003).
