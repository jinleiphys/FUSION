# Exact diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian for rotational nuclei: Dynamical gamma softness and triaxiality

> Detailed quantitative predictions are obtained for phonon and multiphonon excitations in well-deformed rotor nuclei within the geometric framework, by exact numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian in an SO(5) basis. Dynamical gamma deformation is found to significantly influence the predictions through its coupling to the rotational motion. Basic signatures for the onset of rigid triaxial deformation are also obtained.

## Key claim

Exact diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian in an SO(5) basis reveals that dynamical gamma deformation significantly influences predictions for well-deformed rotor nuclei through its coupling to rotational motion, even for nominally axial rotors where the gamma wave function extends far from gamma=0 degrees.

## Method

The angular part of the Bohr Hamiltonian, consisting of the SO(5) Casimir operator Lambda^2 plus a potential V(gamma) of the form chi[(1-cos3gamma) + xi cos^2 3gamma], was diagonalized exactly in a basis of SO(5) superset SO(3) spherical harmonics truncated to maximum seniority v_max=50. The Hamiltonian matrix elements of cos3gamma were evaluated as triple overlaps of spherical harmonics, equivalent to SO(5) superset SO(3) generalized Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and matrix elements of cos^n 3gamma were obtained by matrix multiplication.

## Key numbers

- For chi=50 with xi=0, the energy ratio E(2+_gamma)/E(2+_1) is approximately 10, matching well-deformed rare earth nuclei
- For chi=50 with xi=0, the ground state band energy ratio E(4+_1)/E(2+_1) varies from 2.5 (gamma-independent limit) to 3.33 (rigid axial rotation limit)
- For chi=50 with xi=0, the two-phonon gamma band energies are anharmonic: E_{gammagamma,4}/E_gamma approximately 1.7 and E_{gammagamma,0}/E_gamma approximately 1.9
- For chi=50 with xi=0, the effective gamma deformation gammabar is approximately 15 degrees for ground state band members and approximately 23 degrees for gamma band members
- For chi=200 with xi=0, the quadrupole moments approach the rotational limit values of approximately +/-2.03 (negative for ground state band, positive for gamma band), expressed relative to B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1)^{1/2}
- The ground state band energy ratio E(4+_1)/E(2+_1) reaches 3.33 in the rigid axial rotation limit

## Context

This work extends the algebraic collective model (ACM) framework of Rowe (2004, 2005) and Caprio (2005, 2009) to provide exact numerical solutions for the Bohr Hamiltonian in rotational nuclei, going beyond approximations such as the rotation-vibration model of Faessler (1965) and the rigid triaxial rotor of Davydov (1958). The results enable systematic investigation of how dynamical gamma softness affects spectroscopic predictions for well-deformed nuclei, and provide benchmarks for interpreting experimental data on gamma bands in rare earth nuclei such as Sm-152, Gd-156, and Er-162 studied by McCutchan (2007).
