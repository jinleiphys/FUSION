# Classical vs. quantum corrections to jet broadening in a weakly coupled QGP

> We compute double-logarithmically enhanced corrections to $\widehat{q}$ at relative order $O(g^2)$ in the setting of a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, observing how the thermal scale affects the region of phase space, which gives rise to these corrections. We furthermore clarify how the region of phase from which these corrections are borne is situated with respect to that from which the classical corrections arise at relative order $O(g)$. This represents a significant step towards our eventual goal of understanding which class of corrections dominate, thereby pushing forward our quantitative grasp on the phenomenon of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.

# Classical vs. quantum corrections to jet broadening in a weakly coupled QGP

## Key claim
The thermal scale ω_T (approximately 2πT/e^{γ_E}) cuts off the double-logarithmically enhanced quantum corrections to the jet broadening coefficient q̂ at relative order O(g²), and the region of phase space producing these quantum corrections is adjacent to the region producing the classical O(g) corrections computed by Caron-Huot (2008).

## Method
The author computes double-logarithmically enhanced corrections to the transverse momentum broadening coefficient q̂ at relative order O(g²) in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, replacing the factor 1 in the k⁺ integrand with (1+2n_B(k⁺)) to account for Bose-Einstein stimulated emission and absorption of radiated gluons. The phase space is divided into a "few scattering" regime (regions 1 and 2) and a "strict single scattering" regime (regions 3 and 4), with the double-logarithmic contributions from each regime summed to obtain the total correction. Power-law corrections from the semi-collinear region are shown to cancel against arc contributions from the analytic continuation of the k⁺ integral, confirming the connection between the classical and quantum correction regions.

## Context
This work extends the identification of double-logarithmically enhanced radiative corrections to q̂ first found by Liou (2013) and Blaizot (2013), and clarifies how the thermal scale modifies the phase space from which these corrections arise. The classical O(g) corrections computed by Caron-Huot (2008) using Electrostatic QCD (EQCD) arise from a neighboring region of phase space, and the power-law cancellations demonstrated here confirm the connection between these two classes of corrections. This enables future work toward solving the LPM resummation equation derived in Ghiglieri (2022) for the region where both single and multiple scattering processes contribute, potentially using the improved opacity expansion of Barata (2021).
