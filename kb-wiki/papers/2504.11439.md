# Combined Evidence for the $X_{17}$ Boson After PADME Results on Resonant Production in Positron Annihilation

> The Positron Annihilation into Dark Matter Experiment at the Laboratori Nazionali di Frascati has reported an excess of $e^+e^-$ final-state events from positron annihilation on fixed-target atomic electrons. While the global significance remains at the $(1.77\pm 0.15)\,σ$ level, the excess is centered around $\sqrt{s} \sim 17\,\text{MeV}$, coinciding with the invariant mass at which anomalous $e^+e^-$ pair production has previously been observed in nuclear transitions from excited to ground states in $^8$Be, $^4$He and $^{12}$C, thereby strengthening the case for a common underlying origin, possibly involving a hypothetical new $X_{17}$ boson. We discuss the significance of this independent accelerator-based evidence. Combining it with existing nuclear physics results, we obtain a value for the $X_{17}$ mass of $m_{X_{17}} = 16.88 \pm 0.05\,\text{MeV}$, reducing the uncertainty from nuclear physics determinations by more than a factor of two, and mitigating the impact of poorly known correlations among their systematic errors.

# Combined Evidence for the X17 Boson After PADME Results on Resonant Production in Positron Annihilation

## Key claim

The PADME experiment at LNF Frascati reports an excess of e+e- events from positron annihilation on fixed-target atomic electrons centered at sqrt(s) ~ 17 MeV, with a global significance of only (1.77 ± 0.15) sigma, but the location coincides with the invariant mass at which anomalous e+e- pair production has been observed in nuclear transitions in 8Be, 4He, and 12C. Combining the PADME result with existing nuclear physics data yields an X17 mass of 16.88 ± 0.05 MeV, reducing the uncertainty from nuclear physics determinations by more than a factor of two and mitigating the impact of poorly known correlations among systematic errors in the nuclear measurements.

## Method

The authors perform a global chi-squared fit to all available X17 mass measurements, constructing a covariance matrix that separates statistical uncertainties (diagonal) from systematic uncertainties, with off-diagonal systematic entries set to the minimum of the two corresponding systematic variances to conservatively account for correlations. The fit includes six nuclear physics results from the ATOMKI collaboration (8Be, 4He, 12C, and 8Be GDR transitions) and one from VNU-University of Science, plus the PADME particle-physics result from resonant e+e- → X17 production, and marginalizes over the partial width ratio R_Be. Two extreme assumptions are tested: fully correlated systematic errors among nuclear measurements versus uncorrelated systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- X17 mass from combined fit including PADME: m_X17 = 16.88 ± 0.05 MeV (1 sigma)
- X17 mass from nuclear physics only: m_X17 = 16.78 ± 0.12 MeV (1 sigma)
- PADME excess center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s) = 16.90 MeV, with uncertainties 0.02 MeV (beam energy step) and 0.05 MeV (absolute beam momentum precision)
- PADME global significance: (1.77 ± 0.15) sigma; local significance: 2.5 sigma
- First ATOMKI 8Be measurement: m_X = 16.86 ± 0.06 (stat) ± 0.50 (syst) MeV, with R_Be = (6.8 ± 1.0) × 10^-6
- Second ATOMKI 8Be measurement: m_X = 17.17 ± 0.07 (stat) ± 0.20 (syst) MeV, with R_Be = (4.7 ± 2.1) × 10^-6
- ATOMKI 4He measurement: m_X = 16.94 ± 0.12 (stat) ± 0.21 (syst) MeV
- ATOMKI 12C measurement: m_X = 17.03 ± 0.11 (stat) ± 0.20 (syst) MeV
- ATOMKI 8Be GDR measurement: m_X = 16.95 ± 0.48 (stat) ± 0.35 (syst) MeV
- VNU-UoS 8Be measurement: m_X = 16.66 ± 0.47 (stat) ± 0.35 (syst) MeV
- MEG II 90% CL upper limit on m_X: < 16.81 MeV for R_Be = 6 × 10^-6

## Context

This work extends the decade-long investigation of the X17 anomaly first reported by the ATOMKI collaboration in 8Be transitions (Krasznahorkay, 2015), with subsequent anomalies observed in 4He (Krasznahorkay, 2019, 2021), 12C (Krasznahorkay, 2022), and the 8Be giant dipole resonance (Krasznahorkay, 2023). The MEG II experiment at PSI (2024) found no significant evidence but reported results compatible with ATOMKI within 1.5 sigma. The PADME result provides the first independent accelerator-based evidence from a particle physics experiment, free from nuclear physics systematic uncertainties. The improved mass determination and reduced sensitivity to unknown correlations provide robust prior information for future X17 searches, including those planned by other experimental collaborations.
