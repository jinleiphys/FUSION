# Heavy-flavor transport and hadronization in a small fireball

> We study heavy-flavor hadron production in high-energy pp collisions, assuming the formation of a small, deconfined and expanding fireball where charm quarks can undergo rescattering and hadronization. We adopt the same in-medium hadronization mechanism developed for heavy-ion collisions, which involves Local Color-Neutralization (LCN) through recombination of charm quarks with nearby opposite color charges from the background fireball. Diquark excitations in the hot medium favor the formation of charmed baryons. The recombination process, involving closely aligned partons from the same fluid cell, effectively transfers the collective flow of the system to the final charmed hadrons. This framework can qualitatively reproduce the observed experimental findings in heavy-flavor particle-yield ratios, $p_T$-spectra and elliptic-flow coefficients. Our results provide new, complementary support to the idea that the collective phenomena observed in small systems have the same origin as those observed in heavy-ion collisions.

# Heavy-flavor transport and hadronization in a small fireball

## Key claim

The same in-medium hadronization mechanism (Local Color-Neutralization via recombination) developed for heavy-ion collisions can qualitatively reproduce heavy-flavor observables in pp collisions, assuming a small deconfined fireball is formed; the authors state their framework "can qualitatively reproduce" experimental findings, not that it provides a quantitative match.

## Method

The authors model charm quark propagation through a small deconfined fireball using a relativistic Langevin equation, then hadronize the quarks via Local Color-Neutralization (LCN) at the QCD hadronization temperature. In LCN, each heavy quark recombines with the closest opposite color charge (antiquark or diquark) from the thermal fireball to form a color-singlet cluster, which then decays into final charmed hadrons; heavier clusters above approximately 4 GeV are fragmented as Lund strings using PYTHIA 6.4.

## Key numbers

- pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV are simulated for charmed hadron production
- The LCN model reproduces the enhanced Lambda_c+/D^0 and Xi_c^0/D^0 ratios, with peaks shifting to higher pT in denser systems (from minimum-bias pp to high-multiplicity pp to central Pb-Pb collisions)
- Clusters with mass greater than approximately 4 GeV are fragmented as Lund strings rather than undergoing isotropic 2-body decay
- Predictions for D^0 elliptic-flow coefficient v2 in minimum-bias and high-multiplicity pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV are compared to CMS results at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV

## Context

This work extends the same LCN model previously applied to AA collisions (Beraudo 2022) to the pp case (Beraudo 2023), following similar ideas proposed by Minissale (2020) and Song (2018). The study is motivated by the ALICE (2020) observation of enhanced charmed baryon-to-meson ratios in pp collisions that cannot be reproduced by standard hadronization models tuned to e+e- data. The results provide independent support for the interpretation that collective phenomena in small systems share the same origin as those in heavy-ion collisions, and are relevant for correctly quantifying medium effects in heavy-ion collisions where the pp benchmark enters the nuclear modification factor R_AA.
