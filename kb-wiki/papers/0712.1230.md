# Standard Model Gauging of the WZW Term: Anomalies, Global Currents and pseudo-Chern-Simons Interactions

> The standard model $SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y$ gauging of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term requires a modified counterterm when background fields, needed to generate the full set of currents, are introduced. The modified counterterm plays an essential role in properly defining covariant global currents and their anomalies. For example, it is required in order to correctly derive the gauge invariant baryon number current and its anomalous divergence. The background fields can also be promoted to a description of the physical spin-1 vector and axial-vector mesons in QCD and the counterterm leads to novel interactions. These are (pseudo-) Chern-Simons terms, such as $ε^{μνρσ} ω_μZ_ν\partial_ρA_σ$ and $ε^{μνρσ} ρ^{\pm}_μW^{\mp}_ν\partial_ρA_σ$ that mediate new interactions between neutrinos and photons at finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The standard model $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ gauging of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term requires a new local counterterm, beyond the Bardeen counterterm, to maintain gauge invariance when background fields are introduced; this counterterm leads to observable pseudo-Chern-Simons interactions between gauge bosons and vector mesons that survive in the physical theory, contrary to the intuition from vectorlike gauging where such terms are cancelled.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic toy model with a single quark and lepton, gauging $U(1)_L \times U(1)_R$ and introducing a background $\omega$ field coupled to baryon number, to demonstrate the necessity of a new counterterm. They then generalize to arbitrary chiral flavor symmetry groups $G$ with gauged subgroup $G'$, showing how to construct the counterterm that preserves $G'$ gauge invariance in the presence of background spin-1 fields. The formalism is applied to the $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ gauging of the $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ chiral symmetry of QCD with vector meson backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- $f_\pi \approx 93\ {\rm MeV}$: the pion decay constant appearing in the chiral lagrangian kinetic term
- The consistent anomaly coefficient for Weyl fermion triangle diagrams is $1/(24\pi^2)$ per fermion, as used in the toy model
- The covariant anomaly coefficient for the axial current in QED is $1/(16\pi^2)$, differing from the consistent anomaly by a factor of 3
- The counterterm in the toy model takes the form $-(1/24\pi^2) \int [-2\omega A_R dA_R - \omega A_R d\omega + 2\omega A_L dA_L + \omega A_L d\omega]$

## Context

This work extends the treatment of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term beyond the vectorlike gauging case (where the Bardeen counterterm suffices) to the non-diagonal standard model gauge group, building on foundational work by Wess and Zumino (1971), Witten (1983), and Bardeen (1969). The new counterterm enables proper derivation of covariant global currents and their anomalies, including the gauge-invariant baryon number current, and predicts specific pseudo-Chern-Simons interactions such as $\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \omega_\mu Z_\nu \partial_\rho A_\sigma$ that mediate neutrino-photon interactions at finite baryon density, with potential relevance to the MiniBooNE experiment. The approach predicts coefficients for these interactions in terms of strong coupling constants, unlike previous ad hoc treatments by Kaiser, Klingl, and Truhlik.
