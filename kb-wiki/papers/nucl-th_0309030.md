# An Infrared Renormalization Group Limit Cycle in QCD

## Key claim
The authors conjecture that QCD can be tuned to an infrared limit cycle in the three-nucleon system by adjusting the up and down quark masses to critical values where both the deuteron and its spin-singlet partner have zero binding energy simultaneously. At this critical point, the triton would exhibit infinitely many arbitrarily-shallow excited states, with the ratio of successive binding energies approaching a universal constant close to 515.

## Method
The authors combine chiral extrapolations of the two-nucleon scattering lengths from effective field theories with pions, using central values from Epelbaum (2002) for the inverse scattering lengths as functions of the pion mass. They then apply the pionless effective field theory framework of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999), which exhibits an ultraviolet limit cycle in the three-body sector, to compute the three-nucleon spectrum as a function of the pion mass, approximating the scaling-violation parameter by its physical value.

## Key numbers
- The universal constant for the ratio of successive binding energies of Efimov states is approximately 515, corresponding to a scaling factor of about 22.7.
- The transcendental number s0 that determines the period of the limit cycle is approximately 1.00624.
- The critical pion mass at which the triplet scattering length diverges and the deuteron becomes unbound lies in the range 170 MeV < m_pi < 210 MeV.
- The critical pion mass at which the singlet scattering length likely diverges is not much larger than 150 MeV.
- The physical scaling-violation parameter Lambda_* is taken as 189 MeV for m_pi = 138 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) for nonrelativistic bosons with large scattering length to the three-nucleon sector of QCD, building on the effective field theory framework developed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) for bosons and nucleons. The authors use chiral extrapolations from Beane (2002) and Epelbaum (2002) to predict that tuning the quark masses could produce an exact infrared limit cycle, which would enable lattice gauge theory and effective field theory calculations to demonstrate discrete scale invariance in QCD.
