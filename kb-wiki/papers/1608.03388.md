# Magnetic Moments of Octet Baryons in Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter

> We have calculated the in-medium magnetic moments of octet baryons in the presence of hot and dense symmetric nuclear matter. Effective magnetic moments of baryons have been derived from medium modified quark masses within chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. Further, for better insight of medium modification of baryonic magnetic moments, we have considered the explicit contributions from the valence quarks, sea quarks as well as sea orbital angular momentum of sea quarks. These effects have been successful in giving the description of baryonic magnetic moments in vacuum. The magnetic moments of baryons are found to vary significantly as a function of density of nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of octet baryons vary significantly as a function of nuclear medium density, but the calculation depends on the specific contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks, and the orbital angular momentum of sea quarks, all derived from medium-modified quark masses within the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model.

## Method

The authors used the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model to compute effective quark masses at finite temperature and density through the medium modification of scalar isoscalar fields (sigma and zeta) and the scalar dilaton field chi. Effective baryon masses were then calculated from these quark masses, and the magnetic moments were obtained by summing contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks (via Goldstone boson emission splitting into quark-antiquark pairs), and the orbital angular momentum of sea quarks, using mass-adjusted quark magnetic moments that incorporate confinement and relativistic corrections.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum quark masses used as input: m_u = m_d = 313 MeV, m_s = 490 MeV
- Vacuum values of scalar fields: sigma_0 = -92.8 MeV, zeta_0 = -96.5 MeV, with pion decay constant F_pi = 92.8 MeV and kaon decay constant F_K = 115 MeV
- Pion fluctuation parameter a = 0.1 in the symmetric limit
- Spin-spin correction energies for baryons: E_N_spin = -477 MeV, E_Lambda_spin = -756.9 MeV, E_Sigma_spin = -531 MeV, E_Xi_spin = -705 MeV
- Confining potential coupling constant k_c = 100 MeV.fm^{-2}
- Additional mass term for strange quark m_1 = 29 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of baryon magnetic moments in free space using chiral quark models (Cheng and Li, 1990s) and in-medium calculations using the quark meson coupling model (Ryu et al., 2010s). It also builds on the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model developed by Wang et al. (2000s) for finite nuclei properties. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from heavy-ion collision facilities such as LHC at CERN, CBM at FAIR, and FSI at EMC, and may inform studies of chiral symmetry restoration and the equation of state for hadronic matter at high density.
