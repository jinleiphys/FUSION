# Probing nucleon strangeness in phi electroproduction

> We investigate $ϕ$ meson electroproduction to probe the hidden strangeness content of the nucleon. We found that even a small amount of the $s\bar{s}$ admixture in the nucleon wavefunction can lead to a significant change in several double polarization asymmetries in $ϕ$ electroproduction, which can be tested experimentally at current electron facilities.

# Probing nucleon strangeness in phi electroproduction

## Key claim
Even a small $s\bar{s}$ admixture in the nucleon wavefunction (on the order of 0.5–1.0%) can produce measurable changes in double polarization asymmetries in $\phi$ electroproduction, even though the knockout process is suppressed in the differential cross section and cannot be distinguished from background there.

## Method
The authors calculate $\phi$ electroproduction ($e+p \to e+p+\phi$) using a model that includes background processes (Pomeron exchange via the Donnachie-Landshoff model and one-boson-exchange contributions from $\pi$ and $\eta$ mesons) plus a direct knockout amplitude from an $s\bar{s}$ admixture in the nucleon wavefunction. The proton wavefunction is expanded in Fock space with an $s\bar{s}$ cluster of spin 0 or 1 and orbital angular momentum $\ell=1$, and the strangeness content $|B|^2 = |b_0|^2 + |b_1|^2$ is varied. Double polarization asymmetries (beam-target and beam-recoil) are computed for longitudinally polarized electrons at forward scattering angles.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross section for $\gamma^* p \to \phi p$ is shown at $W = 2.94$ GeV and $Q^2 = 0.23$ GeV$^2$, with experimental data from DGHH77-DGLS79.
- Double polarization asymmetries are computed at $W = 2.15$ GeV and $Q^2 = 0.135$ GeV$^2$ as functions of $\phi$ production angle $\theta$.
- Beam-target asymmetry at $\theta = 0^\circ$ and beam-recoil asymmetry at $\theta = 45^\circ$ are shown as functions of $Q^2$.
- The $s\bar{s}$ admixture is varied at values $|B|^2 = 0$, $0.5\%$, and $1.0\%$, producing distinct asymmetry curves.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous studies on $\phi$ photoproduction (Oh et al., 1997–1998) to electroproduction, finding similar sensitivity of polarization observables to nucleon strangeness. It builds on the suggestion that $\phi$ production probes hidden strangeness via OZI-evading processes, following earlier work on $p\bar{p}$ annihilation (AMSL98; EKKS95). The results indicate that current electron facilities can test these predictions by measuring double polarization asymmetries, which are more sensitive than cross sections alone.
