# Determination of polarized parton distribution functions and their uncertainties

> We investigate the polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their uncertainties by using the world data on the spin asymmetry A_1. The uncertainties of the polarized PDFs are estimated by the Hessian method. The up and down valence-quark distributions are determined well. However, the antiquark distributions have large uncertainties at this stage, and it is particularly difficult to fix the gluon distribution. The χ^2 analysis produces a positively polarized gluon distribution, but even Δg(x)=0 could be allowed according to our uncertainty estimation. In comparison with the previous AAC (Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration) parameterization in 2000, accurate SLAC-E155 proton data are added to the analysis. We find that the E155 data improve the determination of the polarized PDFs, especially the polarized antiquark distributions. In addition, the gluon-distribution uncertainties are reduced due to the correlation with the antiquark distributions. We also show the global analysis results with the condition Δg(x)=0 at the initial scale, Q^2=1 GeV^2, for clarifying the error correlation effects with the gluon distribution.

# Determination of polarized parton distribution functions and their uncertainties

## Key claim

The chi-squared analysis produces a positively polarized gluon distribution, but even Δg(x)=0 could be allowed according to the uncertainty estimation, and the gluon distribution remains particularly difficult to fix from polarized deep inelastic scattering data alone.

## Method

The authors performed a global chi-squared analysis of 399 spin asymmetry A_1 data points from polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments (EMC, SMC, E130, E143, E155, HERMES, E142, E154) with Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, using the Hessian method to estimate uncertainties of the polarized parton distribution functions. The polarized PDFs were parameterized at the initial scale Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 in the next-to-leading order MSbar scheme with N_f=3, using the functional form Δf(x) = [δ x^ν − κ (x^ν − x^μ)] f(x) where f(x) is the unpolarized PDF from GRV98, and the DGLAP evolution equations were used to evolve distributions to experimental Q^2 points.

## Key numbers

- Total chi-squared = 346.5 for 399 data points, giving chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.893
- First moment of polarized gluon distribution at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: Δg = 0.499 ± 1.266 (AAC03 result)
- First moment of polarized antiquark distribution at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: Δqbar = −0.062 ± 0.023 (AAC03 result)
- Quark spin content at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: ΔΣ = 0.213 ± 0.138 (AAC03 result)
- Delta chi-squared for 11 optimized parameters at 68.26% confidence level: Δχ^2 = 12.647
- Valence quark first moments fixed from beta decay: ΔU_v = 0.926, ΔD_v = −0.341
- Lambda_QCD for N_f=3: 299 MeV
- Kinematical coverage of data: 0.004 < x < 0.75 and 1 < Q^2 < 72 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the previous AAC (Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration) parameterization from 2000 (AAC00) by adding accurate SLAC-E155 proton data, which significantly reduce the uncertainties of the polarized PDFs, especially the antiquark and gluon distributions through error correlations. The results are compared with other contemporary parameterizations including GRSV01 (standard scenario), BB (ISET=3), and LSS (MSbar scheme), which used similar experimental data sets but averaged data tables over x and Q^2 rather than full tables. The uncertainty estimation enables future quantification of uncertainties in physical observables such as scattering cross sections and spin asymmetries, and clarifies the importance of future experiments at RHIC and JLab for determining the polarized gluon and flavor-dependent antiquark distributions.
