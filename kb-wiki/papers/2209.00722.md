# Proton distribution radii of $^{16-24}$O: signatures of new shell closures and neutron skin

> The root mean square radii of the proton density distribution in $^{16-24}$O derived from measurements of charge changing cross sections with a carbon target at $\sim$900$A$ MeV together with the matter radii portray thick neutron skin for $^{22 - 24}$O despite $^{22,24}$O being doubly magic. Imprints of the shell closures at $N$ = 14 and 16 are reflected in local minima of their proton radii that provide evidence for the tensor interaction causing them. The radii agree with it ab initio calculations employing the chiral NNLO$_{\mathrm{sat}}$ interaction, though skin thickness predictions are challenged. Shell model predictions agree well with the data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the root mean square point proton radii of oxygen isotopes, derived from charge-changing cross section measurements, show local minima at neutron numbers N=14 and N=16, providing evidence for the tensor interaction causing these shell closures; however, the abstract's claim that "thick neutron skin for 22-24 O" is qualified by the finding that the skin thickness predictions from ab initio coupled-cluster theory with the NNLOsat interaction underestimate the data for these isotopes, while shell model predictions agree well.

## Method

The experiment used the Fragment Separator FRS at GSI to produce 16-24 O isotopes by fragmentation of a 1A GeV 40 Ar beam on a 6.3 g/cm2 Be target. Charge-changing cross sections (sigma_CC) were measured using the transmission technique with a 4.010 g/cm2 carbon target at approximately 900A MeV, where the ratio of particles transmitted without proton loss to incoming particles was determined event-by-event using a multi-sampling ionization chamber (MUSIC) for Z identification. The root mean square point proton radii were extracted by comparing measured sigma_CC to Glauber model calculations using harmonic oscillator density profiles, and point matter radii were derived from interaction cross sections using the nucleon-target Glauber model.

## Key numbers

*   Charge-changing cross section for 16 O: 848(4) mb at 857A MeV, yielding a point proton radius of 2.54(2) fm, consistent with the electron-scattering value of 2.55(1) fm.
*   Charge-changing cross section for 22 O: 837(3) mb at 937A MeV, yielding a point proton radius of 2.50(2) fm, the smallest among the measured isotopes and reflecting the N=14 subshell closure.
*   Charge-changing cross section for 24 O: 839(11) mb at 866A MeV, yielding a point proton radius of 2.51(4) fm, similar to that of 22 O.
*   Point matter radius for 23 O: 2.99(10) fm (using sigma_I from Kanungo (2011)) and 3.20(4) fm (using sigma_I from Ozawa (2000)), showing a large increase compared to 22 O.
*   Point matter radius for 24 O: 3.18(12) fm, indicating a thick neutron skin for 22-24 O.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton radii in light nuclei, such as the dip at N=6 seen in He to B isotopes (Angeli) and the hint of a dip at N=14 in nitrogen isotopes (Bagchi (2019)). The results provide the first experimental determination of point proton radii for 19-24 O and confirm the N=14 and N=16 shell closures in oxygen, supporting the role of the tensor interaction. The data challenge the ab initio coupled-cluster predictions with the NNLOsat interaction (Hagen and collaborators) for neutron skin thickness, while the shell model predictions with the YSOX Hamiltonian (Yuan (2012)) successfully describe the large neutron surface for 22,23 O, opening avenues for refining chiral interactions.
