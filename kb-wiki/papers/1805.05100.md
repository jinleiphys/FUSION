# Three-Body $J^P = 0^+,1^+,2^+$ $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ Bound States

> Three body systems with short-range interactions display universal features that have been extensively explored in atomic physics, but apply to hadron physics as well. Systems composed of two non-interacting identical particles (species H) of mass $M$ and a third particle (species P) of mass $m$ that interacts attractively with the other two have the property that they are more likely to bind for larger values of the mass ratio $M/m$. This is particularly striking if the HHP system is in P-wave (while the interacting pair is in S-wave), in which case one would not normally expect the formation of a three body state. If we assume that the $B^* \bar{K}$ binds to form the $B^*_{s1}$ heavy meson and notice that the mass ratio of the $B^*$ to $\bar{K}$ is $M /m = 10.8$, concrete calculations indicate that there should be a three body $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ bound state between $30-40\,{\rm MeV}$ below the $B^*_{s1} B^*$ threshold. For the $Ξ_{bb} Ξ_{bb} \bar{K}$ system the mass imbalance is about $M /m = 20.5$ and two bound states are expected to appear, a fundamental and an excited one located at $50-90$ and $5-15\,{\rm MeV}$ below the $Ξ_{bb} Ω^*_{bb\frac{1}{2}}$ threshold (where $Ω^*_{bb\frac{1}{2}}$ denotes the $Ξ_{bb} \bar{K}$ bound state). We indicate the possibility of analogous P-wave three body bound states composed of two heavy baryons and a kaon or antikaon and investigate the conditions under which the Efimov effect could appear in these systems.

# Three-Body $J^P = 0^+,1^+,2^+$ $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ Bound States

## Key claim
Concrete calculations indicate that P-wave three-body bound states exist for the $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ system at 30-40 MeV below the $B_{s1}^* B^*$ threshold, and for the $\Xi_{bb} \Xi_{bb} \bar{K}$ system two bound states appear at 50-90 and 5-15 MeV below the $\Xi_{bb} \Omega_{bb\frac{1}{2}}^*$ threshold, but these predictions depend on the assumption that the HP subsystem binds and on the choice of cutoff regulator in the contact interaction.

## Method
The author solves Faddeev equations for the HHP (heavy-heavy-light) system in P-wave using contact-range interactions, where the HH subsystem is non-interacting and antisymmetric in spatial coordinates with odd orbital angular momentum $L_{12}=1$, and the HP interaction is of Weinberg-Tomozawa type. Both non-relativistic and relativistic treatments of the antikaon kinematics are presented, with the relativistic prescription following Garcilazo and Mathelitsch (1984) that reproduces the Kadyshevsky equation in the two-body sector. The coupling constant is determined from the condition that the two-body T-matrix has a pole at the location of the $B_{s1}^*$ meson, with cutoff variation in the $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0$ GeV window.

## Key numbers
- Mass ratio $M/m = 10.8$ for $B^*$ to $\bar{K}$, and $M/m = 20.5$ for $\Xi_{bb}$ to $\bar{K}$
- $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ three-body binding energy: 32-42 MeV (non-relativistic) and 32-33 MeV (relativistic) below the $B_{s1}^* B^*$ threshold
- $\Xi_{bb} \Xi_{bb} \bar{K}$ fundamental state: 50-90 MeV (non-relativistic) and 52-83 MeV (relativistic) below threshold
- $\Xi_{bb} \Xi_{bb} \bar{K}$ excited state: 8-14 MeV (non-relativistic) and 2-14 MeV (relativistic) below threshold
- Two-body $B^* \bar{K}$ binding energy: 57-74 MeV (non-relativistic) and 59-81 MeV (relativistic)
- Two-body $\Xi_{bb} \bar{K}$ binding energy: 60-83 MeV (non-relativistic) and 66-93 MeV (relativistic)
- Critical mass ratio for P-wave Efimov effect: $M/m \geq 13.6$ for standard case, $M/m \geq 20.587$ for the specific isospin factor $c_I = 3/4$
- For the privileged cutoff reproducing $DK$ binding: $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ at 40 MeV (non-relativistic) and 30 MeV (relativistic); $\Xi_{bb} \Xi_{bb} \bar{K}$ fundamental at 81 MeV (non-relativistic) and 67 MeV (relativistic), excited at 9 MeV (non-relativistic) and 4 MeV (relativistic)

## Context
This work extends the universal three-body physics of mass-imbalanced systems, previously explored in atomic physics with lithium and cesium atoms (Pires 2014), to hadron physics. It builds on the discovery by Kartavtsev and Malykh (2006) that P-wave three-body bound states exist for mass ratios $M/m \geq 8.176$, and on the observation that the $D K$ and $D^* K$ systems bind at about 45 MeV (Kolomeitsev 2003, Guo 2006-2011), which by heavy flavor symmetry implies $B^* \bar{K}$ binding. The $B^* B^* \bar{K}$ state can also be predicted in a complementary two-body description using one antikaon exchange potential (SanchezSanchez 2017). The work enables searches for P-wave three-body bound states in other heavy hadron systems with large mass imbalances, particularly those involving doubly heavy tetraquarks predicted from heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry (Savage 1990), whose stability is suggested by the LHCb discovery of the $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ baryon (Aaij 2017).
