# A projected correlation function approach to the pi NN coupling constant in QCD sum rules

> We propose a new approach to construct QCD sum rules for the pi NN coupling constant, g, starting from the vacuum-to-pion correlation function of the interpolating fields of two nucleons and taking its matrix element with respect to nucleon spinors. The new approach with the projected correlation function is advantageous because even in the chiral limit the dispersion integral can be parametrized with well-defined physical parameters. Another advantage of the new approach is that unwanted pole contribution is projected out. Calculating the Wilson coefficients of the operator product expansion of the correlation function up to O(M_B^{-4}) and O(m_pi) where M_B and m_pi are the Borel mass and the pion mass, respectively, we construct new QCD sum rules for the pi NN coupling constant from the projected correlation function with consistently including O(m_pi) corrections. By numerically analyzing the obtained four sum rules we identified the most prominent one. After roughly estimating errors we obtaind, g=10 +/- 3, as a result of this sum rule, which is in reasonable agreement with the empirical value. It is also found that the O(m_pi) correction is about 5%.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new "projected correlation function" approach to QCD sum rules for the πNN coupling constant that avoids ill-defined terms in the dispersion integral that plague previous approaches, particularly in the chiral limit. The authors obtain g = 10 ± 3, which is in reasonable agreement with the empirical value, and find that the O(m_π) correction is about 5%.

## Method

The authors start from the vacuum-to-pion correlation function of interpolating fields of two nucleons and take its matrix element with respect to nucleon spinors, defining a projected correlation function. They calculate the Wilson coefficients of the operator product expansion up to O(M_B^{-4}) and O(m_π), where M_B is the Borel mass and m_π is the pion mass, and construct four new QCD sum rules by combining even and odd parts of the vacuum-to-pion and vacuum-to-vacuum sum rules. The coupling constant is extracted by finding a plateau in the Borel mass squared over the range 1 GeV^2 ≤ M_B^2 ≤ 2 GeV^2 after determining optimal effective continuum thresholds.

## Key numbers

- g = 10 ± 3, the final result from the most prominent (first) sum rule, in reasonable agreement with the empirical value g_emp = 13.4
- The O(m_π) correction to the coupling constant in the first sum rule is about 5%
- The quark condensate uncertainty ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(225 ± 25 MeV)^3 changes g by ±1.7
- Effective continuum thresholds determined as ω_0 = 2.5 ± 0.14 GeV and ω_π = 2.0 ± 0.14 GeV, changing g by ±1.3 and ±1.4 respectively
- The parameter δ^2 = 0.2 ± 0.02 GeV^2 gives an error of less than 5% in g; taking δ^2 = 0.35 GeV^2 gives g ≈ 14
- The parameter m_0^2 in the range 0.6 GeV^2 to 1.4 GeV^2 gives an error less than 1% in g

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to the πNN coupling constant pioneered by Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1985) and improved by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994), who used invariant correlation functions. It addresses issues raised by Birse and Krippa (1996) and Kim, Lee and Oka (1998) regarding the chiral limit behavior and the choice of effective Lagrangian coupling schemes. The new projected correlation function approach enables consistent inclusion of O(m_π) corrections and removes ill-defined terms in the dispersion integral, providing a more reliable sum rule that can be compared with future lattice QCD calculations or improved experimental determinations.
