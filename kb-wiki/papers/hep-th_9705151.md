# Rational Maps, Monopoles and Skyrmions

> We discuss the similarities between BPS monopoles and Skyrmions, and point to an underlying connection in terms of rational maps between Riemann spheres. This involves the introduction of a new ansatz for Skyrme fields. We use this to construct good approximations to several known Skyrmions, including all the minimal energy configurations up to baryon number nine, and some new solutions such as a baryon number seventeen Skyrme field with the truncated icosahedron structure of a buckyball. The new approach is also used to understand the low-lying vibrational modes of Skyrmions, which are required for quantization. Along the way we discover an interesting Morse function on the space of rational maps which may be of use in understanding the Sen forms on the monopole moduli spaces.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new ansatz for Skyrme fields based on rational maps between Riemann spheres, which provides good approximations to known Skyrmion solutions up to baryon number nine and enables construction of new solutions such as a baryon number seventeen buckyball, though the ansatz works best for shell-like structures and fails to capture certain configurations like the pretzel-shaped B=3 saddle point.

## Method

The authors construct Skyrme fields by identifying concentric spheres in R^3 with the domain S^2 of a rational map R(z) of degree N, and the target S^2 with spheres of latitude on S^3, using the ansatz U(r,z) = exp(if(r) n_R · σ) where f(r) is a radial profile function and n_R is the unit vector associated with the rational map value. They minimize the energy functional by first minimizing the angular integral I over rational maps of given degree and symmetry, then solving for the optimal profile function f(r) using conjugate gradient methods on a discretized radial coordinate.

## Key numbers

- B=1 hedgehog Skyrmion energy: E = 1.232 (in units of energy divided by 12π²B)
- B=2 Skyrme field from map R(z)=z²: E = 1.208, approximately 3% above the true Skyrmion energy
- B=3 tetrahedral Skyrmion approximation: E = 1.184
- B=3 toroidal saddle point approximation: E = 1.256
- B=4 octahedral Skyrmion approximation: E = 1.137, compared to true Skyrmion energy E = 1.116
- B=4 instanton-generated Skyrme field energy: E = 1.132
- Angular integral I for B=3 tetrahedral map: I = 13.58
- Angular integral I for B=3 toroidal map: I = 18.67
- Angular integral I for B=5 octahedral map (b=0, a=-5): I = 52.05

## Context

This work extends the connection between BPS monopoles and Skyrmions previously explored by Braaten et al. (1990) and Battye and Sutcliffe (1997), building on Donaldson's (1984) rational map description of monopoles and Jarvis's (1997) recent construction relating rational maps to monopoles via Hitchin's equation. The rational map ansatz enables systematic construction of Skyrmion approximations with specified symmetries, including icosahedral symmetry for B=17 which had been conjectured but not previously realized, and provides a framework for understanding low-lying vibrational modes needed for quantization, complementing the numerical studies of Barnes et al. (1997) on B=2 and B=4 Skyrmion vibrations.
