# Anti-halo effects on reaction cross sections for $^{14,15,16}$C isotopes

> We study anti-halo effects on reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}$ for $^{14,15,16}$C scattering from a $^{12}$C target at 83~MeV/nucleon, using the $g$-matrix double-folding model. $^{15}$C is described by the $^{14}$C~+~$n$ two-body model that reproduces the measured large s-wave spectroscopic factor, i.e., the shell inversion that the 1s$_{1/2}$ orbital is lower than the 0d$_{5/2}$ orbital in energy. $^{16}$C is described by the $^{14}$C~+~$n$~+~$n$ three-body model with the phenomenological three-body force (3BF) that explains the measured small s-wave spectroscopic factor. The 3BF allows the single-particle energies of the $^{14}$C + $n$ subsystem to depend on the position $r$ of the second neutron from the center of mass of the subsystem. The 1s$_{1/2}$ orbital is lower than the 0d$_{5/2}$ orbital for large $r$, but the shell inversion is restored for small $r$. Anti-halo effects due to the "partial shell inversion" make $σ_{\rm R}$ for $^{16}$C smaller than that for $^{15}$C. We also investigate projectile breakup effects on the mass-number dependence of $σ_{\rm R}$ with the continuum discretized coupled-channels method.

## Key claim

The strong odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for $^{14,15,16}$C scattering from $^{12}$C at 83 MeV/nucleon, quantified by $\Gamma_{\rm R}^{\rm exp}=2.0 \pm 0.8$, is explained not only by pairing (di-neutron) correlations but also by a "partial shell inversion" induced by a surface-type three-body force (3BF), where the 1s$_{1/2}$ orbital is lower than the 0d$_{5/2}$ orbital for large separations but the normal shell ordering is restored at small distances.

## Method

The authors used the Melbourne $g$-matrix double-folding model (DFM) to calculate reaction cross sections $\sigma_{\rm R}$ for $^{14,15,16}$C projectiles on a $^{12}$C target at 83 MeV/nucleon. $^{15}$C was described by a $^{14}$C + $n$ two-body orthogonality condition model (OCM) that reproduces the measured large s-wave spectroscopic factor, while $^{16}$C was described by a $^{14}$C + $n$ + $n$ three-body OCM with a phenomenological surface-type three-body force (3BF) that reproduces the measured small s-wave spectroscopic factor. Projectile breakup effects were investigated using the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC).

## Key numbers

* The s-wave spectroscopic factor for $^{15}$C is $\lambda_s(^{15}{\rm C}) = 0.97 \pm 0.08$, and for $^{16}$C it is $\lambda_s(^{16}{\rm C}) = 0.35 \pm 0.2$, from Coulomb breakup and longitudinal momentum distribution measurements, respectively.
* The experimental odd-even deviation parameter for the reaction cross sections is $\Gamma_{\rm R}^{\rm exp}=2.0 \pm 0.8$ for $^{14,15,16}$C + $^{12}$C scattering at 83 MeV/nucleon.
* For the surface-type 3BF model (model IV), the DFM calculation yields $\Gamma_{\rm R}=1.24$, and including projectile breakup effects via CDCC yields $\Gamma_{\rm R}=1.78$, consistent with the experimental value.
* The matter radius of $^{14}$C from HFB calculations is ${\bar r}$($^{14}$C)$=2.51$ fm; the matter radius of $^{15}$C from the two-body model is ${\bar r}$($^{15}$C)$=2.87$ fm; the ratio $\delta={\bar r}(^{16}{\rm C})/{\bar r}(^{15}{\rm C})$ is 0.95 for the surface-type 3BF model.
* The two-neutron separation energy for $^{16}$C is $S_{2n}({\rm exp})=5.47$ MeV, reproduced by both volume-type and surface-type 3BF models.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sasabe et al. (2013), who used a three-body OCM with a volume-type 3BF and obtained $\Gamma_{\rm R}=0.77$, significantly undershooting the experimental value. The present study introduces a surface-type 3BF that produces a "partial shell inversion," which is shown to be more important than the pairing anti-halo effect proposed by Hagino and Sagawa (2011, 2012). The results enable future systematic measurements of $\Gamma_{\rm R}$ at higher incident energies (e.g., 240 MeV/nucleon for Ne isotopes) to detect the partial shell inversion in other neutron-rich nuclei.
