# Quark spin-orbit correlations

> The proton spin puzzle issue focused the attention on the parton spin and orbital angular momentum contributions to the proton spin. However, a complete characterization of the proton spin structure requires also the knowledge of the parton spin-orbit correlation. We showed that this quantity can be expressed in terms of moments of measurable parton distributions. Using the available phenomenological information about the valence quarks, we concluded that this correlation is negative, meaning that the valence quark spin and kinetic orbital angular momentum are, in average, opposite. The quark spin-orbit correlation can also be expressed more intuitively in terms of relativistic phase-space distributions, which can be seen as the mother distributions of the standard generalized and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions. We present here for the first time some examples of the general multipole decomposition of these phase-space distributions.

## Key claim

The paper defines a new independent observable, the quark spin-orbit correlation \(C^q_z\), which quantifies the average alignment between a quark's spin and its kinetic orbital angular momentum inside a proton. Using available phenomenological fits, the author finds that this correlation is negative for both valence \(u\) and \(d\) quarks, meaning their spin and kinetic orbital angular momentum are, on average, antiparallel; however, the numerical estimates rely on the second moment of the helicity distribution, which cannot be extracted from deep-inelastic scattering alone without additional assumptions about polarized sea-quark distributions.

## Method

The author derives an expression for the kinetic quark spin-orbit correlation in terms of twist-2 generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and form factors, specifically the second moment of the helicity GPD \(\tilde H_q\) and the Dirac and tensor form factors. This expression is then evaluated using the LSS fit (Leader, 2010) to inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data at \(\mu^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), and compared with predictions from the naive quark model, the light-front constituent quark model, and the light-front chiral quark-soliton model at \(\mu^2 \sim 0.26\) GeV\(^2\), as well as with lattice QCD results from the LHPC collaboration (Bratt, 2010). The paper also presents a multipole decomposition of the relativistic phase-space (Wigner) distributions that serve as the mother distributions for generalized parton distributions and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions.

## Key numbers

*   Second moment of the \(u\)-quark helicity distribution: \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\, x\tilde H_u(x,0,0) \approx 0.19\) at \(\mu^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) from the LSS fit.
*   Second moment of the \(d\)-quark helicity distribution: \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\, x\tilde H_d(x,0,0) \approx -0.06\) at \(\mu^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) from the LSS fit.
*   Resulting kinetic quark spin-orbit correlation for \(u\) quarks: \(C^u_z \approx -0.9\).
*   Resulting kinetic quark spin-orbit correlation for \(d\) quarks: \(C^d_z \approx -0.53\).
*   Light-front constituent quark model prediction for the second moment of the \(u\)-quark helicity distribution: \(0.345\) at \(\mu^2 \sim 0.26\) GeV\(^2\).
*   Light-front constituent quark model prediction for the second moment of the \(d\)-quark helicity distribution: \(-0.086\) at \(\mu^2 \sim 0.26\) GeV\(^2\).
*   Lattice QCD (LHPC collaboration) value for the second moment of the \(u\)-quark helicity distribution: \(\approx 0.20\) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and pion mass \(m_\pi = 293\) MeV.
*   Lattice QCD (LHPC collaboration) value for the second moment of the \(d\)-quark helicity distribution: \(\approx -0.05\) at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and pion mass \(m_\pi = 293\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the proton spin structure beyond the standard decomposition into quark and gluon spin and orbital angular momentum contributions, which was the focus of the "proton spin puzzle" (Leader, 2013; Wakamatsu, 2014). It builds on Ji's (1996) relation linking quark orbital angular momentum to GPDs and Burkardt's (2005) interpretation of the extra \(x\)-factor as providing orbital information. The derived expressions for the spin-orbit correlation in terms of twist-3 GPDs and generalized transverse-momentum dependent distributions (GTMDs) provide new sum rules that can be tested in future experiments and lattice QCD calculations, and the multipole decomposition of Wigner distributions offers a framework for visualizing all possible angular correlations in the nucleon.
