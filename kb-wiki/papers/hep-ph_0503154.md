# Erratum "The Quark-Gluon-Plasma Liquid"

> An error in the calculation of the Coulomb coupling parameter of the quark-gluon plasma is corrected.

# The Quark-Gluon-Plasma Liquid (Erratum)

## Key claim
The corrected Coulomb coupling parameter for the quark-gluon plasma is \(\Gamma = 1.5\)–5 at \(T \simeq 200\) MeV, which still indicates the QGP is in the liquid phase, but the gas-liquid phase transition now occurs at a few times the transition temperature from the hadronic phase to the QGP, rather than at lower temperatures as previously estimated.

## Method
The author corrected an error in the calculation of the Coulomb coupling parameter \(\Gamma\) by noting that in QCD using Heaviside-Lorentz units, the Coulomb potential must be divided by a factor of \(4\pi\) compared to CGS units. The magnetic interaction, which is of the same magnitude as the electric interaction in an ultrarelativistic plasma, was also taken into account, reducing the coupling parameter by about a factor of 6. The cross section enhancement was recalculated using the corrected Coulomb radius.

## Key numbers
- Corrected Coulomb coupling parameter \(\Gamma = 1.5\)–5 for a QGP at \(T \simeq 200\) MeV.
- Critical coupling for the gas-liquid phase transition is \(\Gamma_c \simeq 1\).
- Corrected Coulomb radius \(\rho = 0.2\)–1 fm.
- Ratio \(\beta = \rho/\lambda_D = 1\)–5.
- Maximum impact parameter of \((1.4\)–\(3.3)\lambda_D\).
- Cross section enhancement factor of 2 to 9.

## Context
This erratum corrects the original calculation by Thoma (2005) in *J. Phys. G: Nucl. Phys.* **31** L7–L12. The corrected parameters shift the predicted gas-liquid phase transition to higher temperatures, making it potentially observable during the expansion of the fireball in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC, as suggested by Peshier. The phase transition from QGP liquid to QGP gas expands the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter to high temperatures, ending at a critical point above which a supercritical fluid exists.
