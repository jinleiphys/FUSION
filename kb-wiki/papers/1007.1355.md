# Anomalous tensor magnetic moments and form factors of the proton in the self-consistent chiral quark-soliton model

> We investigate the form factors of the chiral-odd nucleon matrix element of the tensor current. In particular, we aim at the anomalous tensor magnetic form factors of the nucleon within the framework of the SU(3) and SU(2) chiral quark-soliton model. We consider $1/N_c$ rotational corrections and linear effects of SU(3) symmetry breaking with the symmetry-conserving quantization employed. We first obtain the results of the anomalous tensor magnetic moments for the up and down quarks: $κ_{T}^{u}=3.56$ and $κ_{T}^{d}=1.83$, respectively. The strange anomalous tensor magnetic moment is yielded to be $κ_{T}^{s}=0.2\sim -0.2$, that is compatible with zero. We also calculate the corresponding form factors $κ_{T}^{q}(Q^{2})$ up to a momentum transfer $Q^{2}\leq 1\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}$ at a renormalization scale of $0.36\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anomalous tensor magnetic moments of the proton for up and down quarks are positive and sizable, while the strange quark contribution is compatible with zero, but these results depend on the specific constituent quark mass parameter and the model framework (SU(2) versus SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model), with the strange moment being at the numerical limit of the model.

## Method

The authors computed the anomalous tensor magnetic form factors of the proton within the self-consistent SU(3) and SU(2) chiral quark-soliton model, including 1/N_c rotational corrections and linear SU(3) symmetry breaking effects from the strange quark mass. They extracted the quantity H_T^{*q}(0) from the nucleon matrix element of the tensor current in the Breit frame and combined it with previously calculated tensor charges to obtain the anomalous tensor magnetic moments. The model parameters were fixed to mesonic observables, with the constituent quark mass (420 MeV) chosen to reproduce the proton electric charge radius.

## Key numbers

- Up quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment: kappa_T^u = 3.56 (SU(3) result at renormalization scale 0.36 GeV^2)
- Down quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment: kappa_T^d = 1.83 (SU(3) result at renormalization scale 0.36 GeV^2)
- Strange quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment: kappa_T^s = 0.2 to -0.2 (range from varying constituent quark mass between 400 and 450 MeV)
- Ratio kappa_T^u / kappa_T^d = 1.95 (SU(3) result)
- Form factors calculated up to momentum transfer Q^2 <= 1 GeV^2
- Renormalization scale of the calculation: 0.36 GeV^2
- Constituent quark mass used: M = 420 MeV
- Strange current quark mass: m_s = 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon tensor form factors within the chiral quark-soliton model by Ledwig et al. (2010) and provides the first SU(3) calculation of the anomalous tensor magnetic moments. The results are compared with the SU(2) chiral quark-soliton model calculation by Wakamatsu (2008), lattice QCD results by Hagler et al. (2008), and light-front constituent quark model results by Pasquini et al. (2007). The anomalous tensor magnetic moment is related to the Boer-Mulders function h_1^{perp q} through a relation derived by Burkardt (2005), and these results can inform future extractions of transversity distributions from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering data.
