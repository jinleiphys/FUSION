# Masses of vector and pseudovector hybrid mesons in a chiral symmetric model

> We enlarge the chiral model, the so-called extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM), by including the low-lying hybrid nonet with exotic quantum numbers $J^{PC}=1^{-+}$ and the nonet of their chiral partners with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ to a global $U(3)_r \times U(3)_l$ chiral symmetry. We use the assignment of the $π_1^{hyb}= π_1(1600)$ as input to determine the unknown parameters. Then, we compute the lightest vector and pseudovector hybrid masses that could guide ongoing and upcoming experiments in searching for hybrids.

# Masses of vector and pseudovector hybrid mesons in a chiral symmetric model

## Key claim
The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) can predict masses for the lightest vector and pseudovector hybrid meson nonets, but only after fixing unknown parameters by assigning the known exotic resonance π₁(1600) as the isovector member of the vector hybrid nonet and setting the mass of its chiral partner b₁^(hyb) to an estimated 2 GeV.

## Method
The author enlarged the eLSM Lagrangian, which implements global U(3)ₗ × U(3)ᵣ chiral symmetry, by constructing chiral multiplets for hybrid meson nonets with exotic quantum numbers J^PC = 1⁻⁺ (vector hybrids) and their chiral partners with J^PC = 1⁺⁻ (pseudovector hybrids). The mass term in the Lagrangian was written in terms of left-handed and right-handed hybrid fields, and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking via the scalar condensate generated mass splittings between the chiral partners. The unknown parameters were fixed using the experimental mass of π₁(1600) as input for the vector hybrid nonet and an estimated 2 GeV for the b₁^(hyb) state.

## Key numbers
- π₁^(hyb) mass: 1600 MeV (input using π₁(1600) from experiment)
- η_(1,N)^(hyb) mass: 1660 MeV (predicted)
- η_(1,S)^(hyb) mass: 1751 MeV (predicted)
- K₁^(hyb) mass: 1707 MeV (predicted)
- b₁^(hyb) mass: 2000 MeV (input set as an estimate)
- h_(1N,B)^(hyb) mass: 2000 MeV (predicted)
- K^(hyb)_(1,B) mass: 2063 MeV (predicted)
- h_(1S,B)^(hyb) mass: 2126 MeV (predicted)

## Context
This work extends the eLSM framework, previously used to describe scalar glueballs (Stanic, 2017), pseudoscalar glueballs (Eshraim, 2013-2015), and charmed mesons (Eshraim, 2015-2017), to include hybrid mesons for the first time within a chiral symmetric model. The predictions for the vector hybrid nonet masses and their pseudovector chiral partners can guide ongoing and upcoming experiments at COMPASS and other facilities searching for hybrid states, particularly the unobserved members of the nonet such as K₁^(hyb) and η₁^(hyb). The results are consistent with lattice QCD predictions from Dudek (2011) which report an upper limit of about 2.4 GeV for the pseudovector hybrid states.
