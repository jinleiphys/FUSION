# Nonlinear collective nuclear motion

> For each real number $Λ$ a Lie algebra of nonlinear vector fields on three dimensional Euclidean space is reported. Although each algebra is mathematically isomorphic to $gl(3,{\bf R})$, only the $Λ=0$ vector fields correspond to the usual generators of the general linear group. The $Λ< 0$ vector fields integrate to a nonstandard action of the general linear group; the $Λ>0$ case integrates to a local Lie semigroup. For each $Λ$, a family of surfaces is identified that is invariant with respect to the group or semigroup action. For positive $Λ$ the surfaces describe fissioning nuclei with a neck, while negative $Λ$ surfaces correspond to exotic bubble nuclei. Collective models for neck and bubble nuclei are given by irreducible unitary representations of a fifteen dimensional semidirect sum spectrum generating algebra $gcm(3)$ spanned by its nonlinear $gl(3,{\bf R})$ subalgebra plus an abelian nonlinear inertia tensor subalgebra.

# Nonlinear Collective Nuclear Motion

## Key claim

The paper reports a family of nonlinear collective models for nuclear motion, parameterized by a real number Λ, that are mathematically isomorphic to the standard linear general collective motion algebra gcm(3) but physically distinct. The claim is conditional on Λ: positive Λ describes fissioning nuclei with a neck, while negative Λ describes exotic bubble nuclei, but the models are only well-defined for specific ranges of r relative to the cube root of |Λ|.

## Method

The authors construct a nine-dimensional Lie algebra of nonlinear vector fields on three-dimensional Euclidean space, defined by operators N_{jk} = x_j p_k - i δ_{jk} ħ/2 + (Λ x_j x_k / r^5) r·p, which close under commutation to form an algebra isomorphic to gl(3,R). They identify a family of invariant surfaces given by the equation 1 = Σ Ξ_{jk} (1 + Λ/r^3)^{2/3} x_j x_k, and show that these surfaces transform under the action of the general linear group (for Λ ≤ 0) or a local Lie semigroup (for Λ > 0). Quantum collective models are constructed via irreducible unitary representations of the fifteen-dimensional semidirect sum algebra gcm(3), using the Mackey inducing construction on associated SO(3) bundles.

## Key numbers

- For negative Λ, the spherical bubble has radius ∛|Λ|, and the droplet volume excluding the bubble equals 4π a b c / 3, where a, b, c are positive real numbers defining the canonical form Ξ = diag(a^{-2}, b^{-2}, c^{-2}).
- For negative Λ, the half-lengths of the droplet's principal axes are ∛(a^3 + |Λ|), ∛(b^3 + |Λ|), and ∛(c^3 + |Λ|).
- For positive Λ, the half-lengths of the principal axes are ∛(a^3 - Λ), ∛(b^3 - Λ), and ∛(c^3 - Λ), and the droplet volume equals 4π (a b c - Λ) / 3 when ∛Λ is smaller than all half-lengths.
- For positive Λ with axially symmetric shapes (a=c=1, b=2), the neck pinches to a single point at ∛Λ = 1, and the figure collapses to a point when ∛Λ equals the long axis b=2.
- For positive Λ with triaxial shapes (a=1, b=3, c=2), the pinched neck is squeezed in the x-direction as ∛Λ advances through a=1, then collapses to a point at c=2, and the figure disappears at b=3.
- For negative Λ with ∛|Λ| ≫ a, b, c, the thin shell width along a principal axis is approximately (a^3/|Λ|)^{2/3} a/3.

## Context

This work extends the standard linear geometrical models of nuclear collective motion based on GL(3,R), which describe ellipsoidal surfaces and are used in the Bohr-Mottelson model and the adiabatic rotational model. The nonlinear models address limitations noted by Radomski (1976), Kunz and Mosel (1979), and Fleckner et al. (1980) regarding the inapplicability of linear transformations to fissioning or exotically shaped nuclei. The paper enables the description of necked shapes along the fission path and bubble nuclei using the same mathematical framework as the linear theory, allowing existing computational codes for the Bohr-Mottelson liquid drop model to be applied with adjusted coefficients. The authors prove that their construction is exhaustive: every set of divergence-free vector fields on three-dimensional Euclidean space that is isomorphic to sl(3,R) and contains the angular momentum algebra so(3) equals some Λ representation.
