# Space-time extensions from space-time densities and Bose-Einstein correlations

> Using a (3+1)-dimensional solution of the relativistic Euler-equations for $Pb+Pb$ at 160 $AGeV$, space-time extensions of kaon emission zones are calculated from space-time densities and compared to the inverse widths of two-kaon Bose-Einstein correlation functions. The comparison shows a satisfactory agreement and it is concluded that because of the Gaussian shape of the kaon correlation functions, the space-time parameters of the kaon source can be calculated directly from space-time densities. In the case of intensity interferometry of identical pions this simplification is not recommended when applying Gaussian fits because of the present strong effects of resonance decays. The whole discussion is based on the assumption that hadron emission in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is purely chaotic or that coherence is at least negligible.

# Space-time extensions from space-time densities and Bose-Einstein correlations

## Key claim

For kaon sources in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the space-time parameters (inverse widths of Bose-Einstein correlation functions) can be calculated directly from space-time densities, with maximal differences of 0.54 fm for the longitudinal radius, 0.15 fm for the sideward radius, and 0.17 fm for the outward radius compared to direct fits of the correlation functions, but this simplification is not recommended for pions due to strong resonance decay effects, and the entire discussion assumes purely chaotic hadron emission with negligible coherence.

## Method

The author used the HYLANDER model, a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic one-fluid-dynamics solution of the Euler equations, to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV. From this hydrodynamical analysis, kaon emission functions were computed and two-kaon Bose-Einstein correlation functions were calculated; these were then fitted with a Gaussian parametrization to extract the inverse widths R_long, R_side, R_out, and R_cross. Separately, space-time averages of the kaon source density distribution were computed directly from the hydrodynamical freeze-out hypersurface, and the variances and covariances of the space-time coordinates were related analytically to the inverse widths of the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Maximal difference between direct-fit and space-time-density calculations for R_long: 0.54 fm
- Maximal difference for R_side: 0.15 fm
- Maximal difference for R_out: 0.17 fm
- For pions, maximal differences were 0.78 fm (R_long), 0.20 fm (R_side), and 0.20 fm (R_out)
- Less than 10% of K- come from the K* resonance decay, with negligible effect on the correlation functions
- Calculations performed for Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical analysis of Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies previously performed by Schlei and collaborators using the HYLANDER model (Schlei 1995, 1996). It builds on the formal framework for relating Bose-Einstein correlation radii to source geometry developed by Chapman (1995), Csorgo (1995), and Wiedemann (1996). The key finding, that kaon correlation functions are Gaussian and can be directly related to space-time densities, enables a simplified extraction of source parameters from kaon interferometry data, avoiding the need for full correlation function calculations. For pions, however, the strong distortion of correlation functions by resonance decays (as shown in the paper's Fig. 1) means this simplification is not valid, and the author cautions that experimental pion correlation functions may appear Gaussian despite underlying complications.
