# Langevin dynamics and decoherence of heavy quarks at high temperatures

> A Langevin equation of heavy quarks in high-temperature quark-gluon plasma is derived. The dynamics of heavy quark color is coupled with the phase space dynamics and causes a macroscopic superposition state of heavy quark momentum. Decoherence of the superposition state allows one to use a classical description. The time scale of decoherence gives an appropriate discretization time scale $Δt\sim \sqrt{M/C_{\rm F}γ}$ for the classical Langevin equation, where $M$ is heavy quark mass and $γ$ is heavy quark momentum diffusion constant.

# Langevin dynamics and decoherence of heavy quarks at high temperatures

## Key claim
The Langevin equation for heavy quarks in high-temperature quark-gluon plasma is not closed in phase space but couples to quantum color dynamics, producing a macroscopic superposition (Schrödinger's cat) state of heavy quark momentum. Decoherence of this superposition state justifies a classical description only at a discretization time scale $\Delta t\sim \sqrt{M/C_{\rm F}\gamma}$, which depends on the heavy quark mass and the momentum diffusion constant.

## Method
The author derives a stochastic master equation for the heavy quark reduced density matrix using the influence functional formalism, starting from a quantum master equation obtained in earlier work (Akamatsu 2014). Taking the classical point-particle limit where the wave packet is much smaller than the gluon correlation length, coupled Langevin equations are obtained for the heavy quark position, momentum, and color expectation value $\langle \bm{t}^a \rangle$. The momentum update depends on the color state, and the color dynamics evolves under random SU($N_{\rm c}$) rotations. The decoherence time scale is estimated by equating the time step of the classical Langevin equation to the decoherence time of the momentum superposition state, using the Caldeira-Leggett-type decoherence term from the color-averaged master equation.

## Key numbers
- The discretization time scale for the classical Langevin equation is $\Delta t \sim \sqrt{M/C_{\rm F}\gamma}$, where $M$ is the heavy quark mass and $\gamma$ is the heavy quark momentum diffusion constant.
- For a charm quark in QGP with $T \sim 200\ {\rm MeV}$, the decoherence time scale is $\Delta t \sim 3\ {\rm fm}$.
- For a bottom quark in QGP with $T \sim 200\ {\rm MeV}$, the decoherence time scale is $\Delta t \sim 5\ {\rm fm}$.
- The typical lifetime of QGP in heavy-ion collisions is $\tau_{\rm QGP} \sim 10\ {\rm fm}$.
- The hadronization time scale is estimated as $1/\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 1\ {\rm fm}$.
- The spatial extension of the heavy quark wave function in the superposition state is $\Delta x \sim (C_{\rm F}\gamma M)^{-1/4} \sim 0.6\ {\rm fm}$ for charm and $0.45\ {\rm fm}$ for bottom.
- The drag parameter scales as $\gamma \sim g^4 \ln(1/g) T^3$ and the color rotation parameter as $\alpha \sim g^2 T$.
- The relaxation time of heavy quark momentum is $\tau_{\rm relax} \sim MT/C_{\rm F}\gamma$, much longer than $\Delta t$.

## Context
This work extends the quantum open-systems approach to heavy quarks in QGP developed by Akamatsu (2014), who derived the master equation for the reduced density matrix. It contrasts with classical treatments using Wong's equation for color dynamics, instead treating color as a quantum degree of freedom in an $N_{\rm c}$-dimensional Hilbert space. The decoherence analysis builds on the Caldeira-Leggett master equation framework (Caldeira and Leggett 1985). The phenomenological implications connect to heavy quark energy loss studies reviewed by Rapp (2009) and to statistical hadronization models (Braun-Munzinger et al. 2000). The results suggest that classical Langevin simulations may be inadequate for heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions, and motivate development of a novel kinetic theory resolving sub-decoherence time scales, potentially using the Kadanoff-Baym equation for a 4-dimensional Wigner function.
