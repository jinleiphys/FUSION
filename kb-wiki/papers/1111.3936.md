# On perturbative limits of quadrupole evolution in QCD at high energy

> We consider the perturbative (weak field) limit of the small $x$ QCD evolution equation for quadrupole, the normalized trace of four Wilson lines in the fundamental representation, which appears in di-hadron angular correlation in high energy collisions. We linearize the quadrupole evolution equation and then expand the Wilson lines in powers of $g\, A_╬╝$ where $A_╬╝$ is the gauge field. The quadratic terms in the expansion ($\sim g^2\, A^2$) satisfy the BFKL equation as has been recently shown. We then consider the quartic terms ($\sim g^4\, A^4$) in the expansion and show that the linearized quadrupole evolution equation, written in terms of color charge density $Žü$, reduces to the well-known BJKP equation for the imaginary part of four-reggeized gluon exchange amplitude. We comment on the possibility that the BJKP equation for the evolution of a $n$-reggeized gluon state can be obtained from the JIMWLK evolution equation for the normalized trace of $n$ fundamental Wilson lines when non-linear (recombination) terms are neglected.

# On perturbative limits of quadrupole evolution in QCD at high energy

## Key claim

The JIMWLK evolution equation for the quadrupole operator, when non-linear (recombination) terms are neglected and the Wilson lines are expanded to quartic order in the gauge field, reduces to the BJKP equation for the imaginary part of the four-reggeized gluon exchange amplitude; this equivalence holds only in the dilute (weak field) limit where all transverse sizes are much smaller than the inverse saturation scale.

## Method

The author linearizes the JIMWLK evolution equation for the quadrupole operator (normalized trace of four fundamental Wilson lines) by dropping non-linear terms proportional to products of T-matrices. The Wilson lines are then expanded in powers of the gauge field A_mu, and terms of order g^2 A^2 and g^4 A^4 are isolated. The quadratic terms are shown to satisfy the BFKL equation, while the quartic terms are Fourier transformed to momentum space and rewritten in terms of color charge density rho to recover the BJKP equation for the four-reggeized gluon state.

## Key numbers

- The linearized quadrupole evolution equation reduces to the BFKL equation at order g^2 A^2, with the dipole kernel (r-s)^2 / [(r-z)^2 (s-z)^2] appearing in the standard form.
- At order g^4 A^4, the evolution equation for the four-point function T_4 in momentum space contains a virtual correction term: - (N_c alpha_s)/(2 pi)^2 integral d^2 p_t [l_1^2 / (p_t^2 (l_1 - p_t)^2) + {l_1 -> l_2, l_3, l_4}] T_4(l_1,l_2,l_3,l_4).
- The real part of the evolution for T_4 in terms of color charge density rho is given by: d/dy hat{T}_4(l_1,l_2,l_3,l_4) = (N_c alpha_s)/(pi^2) integral d^2 p_t [p^i/p_t^2 - (p^i - l_1^i)/(p_t + l_1)^2] . [p^i/p_t^2 - (p^i - l_2^i)/(p_t + l_2)^2] hat{T}_4(p_t + l_1, l_2 - p_t, l_3, l_4) + ... minus the virtual term above.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Dominguez, Marquet, Mueller, Xiao (2011) that the quadrupole evolution reduces to a sum of BFKL equations at quadratic order in the gluon field. It connects the JIMWLK formalism for higher-point Wilson line correlators to the established BJKP hierarchy for multi-reggeized gluon states developed by Bartels, Jaroszewicz, Kwiecinski, Praszalowicz (1980s-1990s) and Chen, Mueller (1990s). The result enables future analytic studies of the JIMWLK equation in the dilute regime by leveraging known properties of the BJKP equation, and suggests that the linearized JIMWLK equation for n-point Wilson line correlators may be equivalent to the full BJKP hierarchy for n-reggeized gluon exchange amplitudes.
