# Classical QGP : IV. Thermodynamics

> We construct the equation of a state of the classical QGP valid for all values of Gamma=V/K, the ratio of the mean Coulomb to kinetic energy. By enforcing the Gibbs relations, we derive the pertinent pressure and entropy densities for all Gamma. For the case of an SU(2) classical gluonic plasma our results compare well with lattice simulations. We show that the strongly coupled component of the classical QGP contributes significantly to the bulk thermodynamics across T_c.

## Key claim

The authors construct an equation of state for the classical quark-gluon plasma (cQGP) valid for all values of the plasma parameter Gamma (ratio of mean Coulomb to kinetic energy), by interpolating between one-loop analytical results at small Gamma and molecular dynamics simulations at large Gamma, and show that the strongly coupled component contributes significantly to bulk thermodynamics across the transition temperature T_c.

## Method

The authors combine one-loop analytical results for the excess free energy (valid for small Gamma) with molecular dynamics simulation results for the excess energy (valid for large Gamma) using a phenomenological interpolation formula borrowed from classical electromagnetic plasma physics. For an SU(2) gluonic plasma, they fit the resulting energy density to SU(2) lattice data to extract the running coupling constant alpha_s(T), then use the Gibbs relations to derive the pressure and entropy densities.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop excess energy for SU(2) cQGP is given by U_anal/(NT) = -(3/2)sqrt(3) Gamma^(3/2) + (9/2) delta Gamma^2 - (45/2) sqrt(3) delta^2 Gamma^(5/2), where delta = sigma/a_WS is the core parameter
- The molecular dynamics excess energy for SU(2) plasma follows U_mol/(NT) ≈ -4.9 - 2 Gamma + 3.2 Gamma^(1/4) + 2.2/Gamma^(1/4)
- The interpolation function uses a power function 5.5×10^2 Gamma^(5.4) to combine analytical and molecular dynamics results
- The core parameter delta varies in the range 0.2-0.5 for Gamma in the range 0.1-1, with a fixed value delta=0.4 used in the interpolation
- The SU(2) transition temperature is T_c = 215 MeV with Lambda_L = 5 MeV from lattice data of Engels et al.
- The strongly coupled component contributes significantly in the range (1-2.5) T_c

## Context

This work extends the classical QGP framework developed by Gelman, Shuryak, and Zahed (2006) by constructing a full equation of state valid for all Gamma, building on the authors' own one-loop analytical results (Cho and Zahed, 2008) and molecular dynamics simulations (Gelman, Shuryak, Zahed, 2006). The approach borrows interpolation techniques from classical electromagnetic plasma physics (Ichimaru, 1982) and Debye-Huckel plus Hole theory (Nordholm, 1984). The results enable extraction of the running coupling constant from lattice data and prediction of transport properties such as diffusion, viscosity, and energy loss in the transition region, which the authors state will be discussed in a subsequent work.
