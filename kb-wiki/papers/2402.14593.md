# Bayesian inference of thermal effects in dense matter within the covariant density functional theory

> The high temperatures reached in a proto-neutron star or during the post-merger phase of a binary neutron star coalescence lead to non-negligible thermal effects on the equation of state (EOS) of dense nuclear matter. Here we study these effects within the covariant density functional theory employing the posteriors of a Bayesian inference, which encompasses a large sample of EOS models. Different densities and temperatures are considered. We find that for a number of quantities thermal effects are strongly correlated with the Dirac effective mass ($m^*$) of the nucleons and/or its logarithmic derivative as a function of density. These results can be explained within the low temperature approximation though they survive beyond this limit.

## Key claim
Thermal effects on the equation of state of dense nuclear matter within covariant density functional theory are strongly correlated with the Dirac effective mass of nucleons and its logarithmic derivative, and these correlations can be explained by a low-temperature approximation but survive beyond its validity domain.

## Method
The authors employed a Bayesian inference posterior sample of 10^5 equation of state models (the DDB* set from Beznogov et al. 2023) based on covariant density functional theory with density-dependent couplings, considering only nucleonic degrees of freedom. They computed thermal contributions to state variables (energy density, pressure, chemical potential) and thermal coefficients (entropy per baryon, heat capacities, thermal index, adiabatic index, speed of sound) for symmetric nuclear matter at densities of 0.15 fm^-3 and 0.60 fm^-3 and temperatures of 20 MeV and 50 MeV, then examined correlations with the Dirac effective mass and the quantity q defined in terms of effective mass and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of the DDB* model set: median 0.153 fm^-3 with 68% confidence interval +0.0049/-0.0049 fm^-3
- Dirac effective mass at saturation: median 0.657 m_N with 68% confidence interval +0.041/-0.045 m_N
- Symmetry energy at saturation: median 32.1 MeV with 68% confidence interval +1.8/-1.8 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope: median 42.3 MeV with 68% confidence interval +15/-13 MeV
- Compression modulus at saturation: median 247 MeV with 68% confidence interval +33/-28 MeV
- At T=50 MeV, the increase of m* with temperature ranges from approximately 2% to 8% depending on the model
- Specific heat at constant volume (C_V) exceeds 1.5 for condition II (n=0.15 fm^-3, T=50 MeV)
- For condition III (n=0.60 fm^-3, T=20 MeV), the system lies within the validity domain of the low-temperature approximation for all EOS models

## Context
This work extends the analytical framework of Constantinou et al. (2014, 2015), who showed that in non-relativistic potential models thermal response functions are controlled by Landau effective masses and their density derivatives. It complements the systematic confrontation of mean-field models by Raduta et al. (2021), which found that thermal behavior in CDF models strongly depends on mesonic couplings but could not easily identify governing parameters. The results enable future numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers to test whether the Dirac effective mass sensitivity found in non-relativistic simulations (Schneider et al. 2019, Yasin et al. 2020, Fields et al. 2023) also manifests in CDF-based equation of state models.
