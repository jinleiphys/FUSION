# Dipole polarizabilities of charged pions

> We discuss main experimental works, where dipole polarizabilities of charged pions have been determined. Possible reasons for the differences between the experimental data are discussed. In particular, it is shown that the account of the $σ$-meson gives a significant correction to the value of the polarizability obtained in the latest experiment of the COMPASS collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large discrepancy between the COMPASS collaboration result for the charged pion dipole polarizability and other experimental values can be largely resolved by including the contribution of the σ-meson, which was omitted in the COMPASS analysis; the authors show that accounting for the σ-meson shifts the COMPASS value of \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}}\) from 4.0 to approximately 10–11 (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\)).

## Method

The authors review experimental determinations of charged pion dipole polarizabilities from three types of reactions: radiative pion photoproduction from the proton (\(\gamma p \to \gamma\pi^+ n\)), scattering of high-energy pions in the Coulomb field of heavy nuclei via the Primakoff effect (\(\pi^- A \to \gamma\pi^- A'\)), and two-photon production of pion pairs (\(\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\)). For the COMPASS Primakoff experiment, they calculate the contribution of the σ-meson to the Compton scattering amplitude \(M_{++}\) using dispersion relations with the σ-meson treated as a pole on the second Riemann sheet, with parameters \(M_\sigma = 441\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_\sigma = 544\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{\sigma\gamma\gamma} = 1.98\ \mathrm{keV}\), and \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi} = 3.31\ \mathrm{GeV}\). They then solve for the corrected value of \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}}\) by equating the cross section without the σ-meson to the cross section including it.

## Key numbers

*   MAMI (2005) radiative photoproduction result: \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}} = 11.6 \pm 1.5_{\mathrm{stat}} \pm 3.0_{\mathrm{syst}} \pm 0.5_{\mathrm{model}}\) in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Serpukhov (1983) Primakoff result: \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}} = 13.6 \pm 2.8 \pm 2.4\) in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   COMPASS (2007) Primakoff result: \(\alpha_\pi = 2.0 \pm 0.6_{\mathrm{stat}} \pm 0.7_{\mathrm{syst}}\) in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\), obtained assuming \(\alpha_1 = -\beta_1\), corresponding to \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}} = 4.0 \pm 1.2 \pm 1.4\).
*   After including the σ-meson contribution, the corrected value from the COMPASS kinematics is \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}} \sim 10\) (using σ-meson parameters from Oller (1999)), and can reach \(\sim 11\) with parameters from Pennington (2006).
*   For the Serpukhov kinematics, the σ-meson correction is \(\Delta (\alpha-\beta)_\sigma \gtrsim 2.7\) in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Global fit to \(\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\) data up to 2.5 GeV (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2006): \((\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{\pi^{\pm}} = 13.0^{+2.6}_{-1.9}\) in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work reviews and reconciles conflicting experimental determinations of the charged pion dipole polarizability, which range from 4 to 40 in units of \(10^{-4}\,\mathrm{fm}^3\). It extends the dispersion relation approach of Fil'kov and Kashevarov (2006) by explicitly calculating the σ-meson contribution to the COMPASS Primakoff analysis, showing that its omission is a key reason for the discrepancy with the MAMI (2005) and Serpukhov (1983) results. The paper calls for new, more accurate data on \(\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\) below 800 MeV to resolve the remaining uncertainties and enable a definitive determination of the pion polarizabilities.
