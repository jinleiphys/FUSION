# Pion production in proton-proton collisions in a covariant one boson exchange model

> Motivated by the renewed interest in studying the pion production on nuclei with protons at few GeV incident energies, we investigate the pion production in proton-proton collisions over an energy range of 300 $MeV$ to 2 $GeV$. Starting from a realistic one-boson exchange model with parameters fitted to the amplitudes of the elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering, we perform fully covariant calculations for the total, double and triple differential cross-sections of the $p(p,nŽĆ^+)p$ and $p(p,pŽĆ^0)p$ reactions. The calculations incorporate the exchange of $ŽĆ, Žü,Žē$ and $Žā$ mesons and treat nucleon and delta isobar as intermediate states. We obtain a reasonably good agreement with the experimental data in the entire range of beam energies. The form of the covariant delta propagator, the cut-off parameter for the $ŽĆNN$ and $ŽĆN╬ö$ vertex form factors and the energy dependence of the delta isobar decay width is investigated.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a fully covariant one-boson exchange model, with parameters fixed solely by fitting elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering data above 1 GeV, can reproduce total, double, and triple differential cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions from 300 MeV to 2 GeV without any adjustment to pion production data. The hedge is that the agreement is "reasonably good" and that the model's parameters must be energy-dependent (Eq. 11) to fit elastic data across the full range, which introduces a phenomenological element.

## Method

The authors performed fully covariant Feynman diagram calculations for the reactions \(p(p,n\pi^+)p\) and \(p(p,p\pi^0)p\), using an effective one-boson exchange model that includes exchange of \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma\), and \(\omega\) mesons. They considered both nucleon and delta isobar intermediate states, included pre-emission and post-emission graphs, and incorporated the decay of the \(\rho\) meson into two pions in flight. Coupling constants and cutoff parameters were determined by fitting to elastic proton-proton and proton-neutron scattering data at beam energies of 1.73, 2.24, and 3.18 GeV, with an exponential energy dependence for the coupling constants, and were then held fixed for all pion production calculations.

## Key numbers

*   Total cross sections for \(p(p,n\pi^+)p\) and \(p(p,p\pi^0)p\) are reproduced reasonably well over the beam energy range 300 MeV to 2 GeV.
*   The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_\pi\) for the \(\pi NN\) vertex is 1.005 GeV; using a value of 0.63 GeV leads to a smaller cross section and a poorer fit to the data.
*   The cutoff parameters for the \(\pi N\Delta\) and \(\rho N\Delta\) vertices are \(\Lambda_\pi^* = 1.421\) GeV and \(\Lambda_\rho^* = 2.273\) GeV, respectively; the coupling constant \(g_\rho^*\) is 7.4.
*   The free delta isobar decay width \(\Gamma_0\) is taken as 0.120 GeV; the parameter \(\epsilon\) in the Dmitriev form of the width is 0.16.
*   At beam energies of 1ŌĆō2 GeV, the contribution of \(\rho\) meson exchange to the total cross section is less than 10% of the pion exchange contribution.
*   For the triple differential cross section at 800 MeV, using the delta decay width of Dmitriev with \(\Gamma_0 = 0.100\) GeV correctly reproduces the peak positions of the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends earlier covariant calculations of \(NN \rightarrow NN\pi\) reactions (e.g., Verwest (1990), Dmitriev et al.) by explicitly including \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) meson exchange, which were not considered in prior studies. It also systematically examines different forms of the covariant delta isobar propagator (Benmerrouche et al. (1989) vs. Williams) and various momentum-dependent delta decay widths. The results enable more reliable parameter fixing for covariant two-nucleon models of \(A(p,\pi)B\) reactions (e.g., Shyam et al.) and provide a basis for studying weaker inelastic processes like \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) meson emission.
