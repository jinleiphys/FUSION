# Inclusive Neutrino and Antineutrino Scattering on the $^{12}$C Nucleus Within the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model

> We investigate quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering on the $^{12}$C nucleus utilizing a novel scaling variable, $ψ^*$. This variable is derived from the interacting relativistic Fermi gas model, which incorporates both scalar and vector interactions, leading to a relativistic effective mass for the interacting nucleons. For inclusive lepton scattering from nuclei, we develop a new scaling function, denoted as $f^\text{QE}(ψ^*)$, based on the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). This model serves as a natural extension of the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model applicable to finite nuclei. In this study, we compute theoretical predictions and compare them with experimental data from Miner$ν$a and T2K for inclusive (anti)neutrino cross-sections. The scaling function is derived within the CDFM framework, employing a relativistic effective mass of $m_N^*$ = 0.8 $m_N$. The findings demonstrate a high degree of consistency with experimental data across all (anti)neutrino energy ranges.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), when combined with a novel scaling variable derived from an interacting relativistic Fermi gas with a relativistic effective mass of 0.8 times the free nucleon mass, yields theoretical predictions for quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering on carbon-12 that show a high degree of consistency with experimental data from the Miner$ν$a and T2K collaborations across all energy ranges.

## Method
The authors derived a new scaling variable, ψ*, from the interacting relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, which includes scalar and vector interactions that produce a relativistic effective mass for nucleons. They then constructed a new scaling function, f^QE(ψ*), within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which naturally extends the RFG model to finite nuclei. This scaling function was used to compute inclusive cross-sections for quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering on 12C, and the results were compared to experimental data from the Miner$ν$a and T2K experiments.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model by incorporating nuclear structure effects through the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which is a natural extension of the RFG for finite nuclei. It builds on previous work using scaling functions for inclusive lepton scattering, such as the approach by Donnelly and Sick, by introducing a new scaling variable that accounts for nucleon interactions in the medium. The demonstrated consistency with Miner$ν$a and T2K data enables more reliable predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments that rely on accurate cross-section models for carbon-based detectors.
