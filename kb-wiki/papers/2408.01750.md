# The $pΛ$ and $ppΛ$ correlation functions

> In this work we present the study of $pΛ$ and $ppΛ$ scattering processes using femtoscopic correlation functions. This observable has been recently used to access the low-energy interaction of hadrons emitted in the final state of high-energy collisions, delivering unprecedented precision information of the interaction among strange hadrons. The formalism for particle pairs is well established and it relates the measured correlation functions with the scattering wave function and the emission source. In the present work we analyze the $NNΛ$ scattering in free space and relate the corresponding wave function to the $ppΛ$ correlation measurement performed by the ALICE collaboration. The three-body problem is solved using the hyperspherical adiabatic basis. Regarding the $pΛ$ and $ppΛ$ interactions, different models are used and their impact on the correlation function is studied. The three body force considered in this work is anchored to describe the binding energy of the hypertriton and to give a good description of the two four-body hypernuclei. As a main result we have observed a huge, low-energy peak in the $ppΛ$ correlation function, mainly produced by the $J^π=1/2^+$ three-body state. The study of this peak from an experimental as well as a theoretical point of view will provide important constraints to the two- and three-body interactions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a prominent, low-energy peak in the $pp\Lambda$ correlation function is produced primarily by the $J^\pi=1/2^+$ three-body state, and that studying this peak will provide important constraints on two- and three-body interactions. However, the abstract glosses over the fact that the three-body force used in the calculations is specifically anchored to describe the binding energy of the hypertriton and to give a good description of two four-body hypernuclei, meaning the results are conditional on that specific force.

## Method
The authors compute the $p\Lambda$ and $pp\Lambda$ correlation functions using femtoscopic formalism, solving the three-body $NN\Lambda$ scattering problem in free space with the hyperspherical adiabatic (HA) basis. They model the $p\Lambda$ interaction using Gaussian potentials that reproduce the low-energy scattering parameters from chiral-EFT potentials (NLO13, NLO19, SMS N2LO) and the Bodmer-Usmani potential, and include a repulsive three-body force to reproduce the hypertriton binding energy. The theoretical correlation functions are then compared to experimental data from the ALICE collaboration.

## Key numbers
* The $p\Lambda$ correlation function is compared to ALICE data using a source radius of $r_0 = 1.19$ fm, determined from a fit to the data.
* The Gaussian representation of the NLO19 potential with a cutoff at 600-650 MeV provides the best description of the $p\Lambda$ correlation function, though with a deviation of $n_{tot} = 6.5$ standard deviations in the region $k < 120$ MeV/c.
* A set of scattering parameters from a simultaneous fit of femtoscopy and scattering data yields a singlet scattering length $a_0 = -2.10$ fm and effective range $r_e^0 = 3.21$ fm, and a triplet scattering length $a_1 = -1.54$ fm and effective range $r_e^1 = 3.16$ fm.
* The hypertriton binding energy predicted by the Gaussian potential from the above fit is $B(^3_\Lambda{\rm H}) = 2.40$ MeV, close to the experimental value.

## Context
This work extends the femtoscopy technique, previously successful for two-body systems like $p\Lambda$ (ALICE, 2022), to the three-body $pp\Lambda$ system. It builds on theoretical studies of three-nucleon correlation functions ($ppp$, $pd$) by the same group (Garrido et al., 2023) to establish the formalism for systems involving strange hadrons. The results enable future experimental and theoretical studies of the low-energy $NN\Lambda$ dynamics, providing a unique method to access three-body forces in the strangeness sector, which is difficult with traditional nuclear experiments.
