# Relativistic Guiding-Center Motion: Action Principle, Kinetic Theory, and Hydrodynamics

> We treat the guiding-center dynamics in a varying external Maxwell field using a relativistically covariant action principle which reproduces the known Vandervoort expression for the drift velocity and extends it to curved spacetime. We derive the corresponding kinetic theory and ideal hydrodynamic theory. In contrast to conventional five-equation hydrodynamics, the guiding-center hydrodynamics needs only three equations due to a constraint on the motion across magnetic field. We argue that such a hydrodynamics is applicable to strongly coupled plasmas where kinetic theory fails.

## Key claim
The authors derive a fully relativistic, manifestly Lorentz covariant description of guiding-center motion using an action principle, which reproduces the known Vandervoort expression for drift velocity and extends it to curved spacetime. They argue that the resulting ideal hydrodynamic theory, which requires only three equations instead of the conventional five, is applicable to strongly coupled plasmas where kinetic theory fails.

## Method
The authors start from a relativistically covariant action principle for a single particle with Lagrange multipliers enforcing the on-shell condition and the constraint that the averaged perpendicular momentum vanishes. From the equations of motion, they derive the drift velocity expression and then construct a kinetic theory using the Liouville equation with an invariant phase-space measure. Finally, they integrate the kinetic equation over momentum to obtain ideal hydrodynamic equations, using the equilibrium distribution function to derive constitutive relations and the second law of thermodynamics to constrain the thermodynamic potentials.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic fields exceeding $10^{15}~\text{G}$ have been found on magnetar surfaces; fields in peripheral heavy-ion collisions may exceed $10^{19}~\text{G}$.
- The critical field where electron cyclotron motion becomes relativistic is $B > m_e^2c^3/(e\hbar) \approx 4.41\times10^{13}~\text{G}$.
- The condition for the guiding-center kinetic theory to work for a relativistic electron-positron plasma is $B \gg e^{3}T^2$.
- The cyclotron radius is $a = p_\perp/(eB_*)$, and the expansion parameter is $a/L \ll 1$ where $L$ is the magnetic field variation scale.
- The damping rate for perpendicular fluid motion is $\Gamma_\perp = \sigma B^2/(\epsilon+P)$, and for an electron-positron plasma $\sigma \sim T/e^2$.

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic guiding-center theory of Vandervoort (1960) and Northrop to a fully relativistic and manifestly covariant formulation. It provides a systematic derivation of kinetic and hydrodynamic theories for plasmas in strong magnetic fields, complementing earlier work on anisotropic pressure in magnetized plasmas by Chew (1956), Hernandez (2017), and Hattori (2022). The resulting three-equation hydrodynamics enables numerical simulations of relativistic astrophysical plasmas and provides a framework for computing transport coefficients such as Hall conductivity and odd viscosity in quantizing magnetic fields.
