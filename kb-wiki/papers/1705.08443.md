# The transverse momentum distribution of hadrons within jets

> We study the transverse momentum distribution of hadrons within jets, where the transverse momentum is defined with respect to the standard jet axis. We consider the case where the jet substructure measurement is performed for an inclusive jet sample $pp\to\text{jet}+X$. We demonstrate that this observable provides new opportunities to study transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) which are currently poorly constrained from data, especially for gluons. The factorization of the cross section is obtained within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), and we show that the relevant TMDFFs are the same as for the more traditional processes semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and electron-positron annihilation. Different than in SIDIS, the observable for the in-jet fragmentation does not depend on TMD parton distribution functions which allows for a cleaner and more direct probe of TMDFFs. We present numerical results and compare to available data from the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the transverse momentum distribution of hadrons within jets in inclusive proton-proton collisions provides a cleaner and more direct probe of transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) than traditional processes like semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, because the in-jet observable does not depend on transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions, and the relevant TMDFFs are the same as those probed in SIDIS and electron-positron annihilation.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the cross section of hadron production inside inclusive jets, focusing on the kinematic region where the hadron transverse momentum relative to the jet axis satisfies \(j_\perp \ll p_T R\). They introduce semi-inclusive TMD fragmenting jet functions (siTMDFJFs) which are refactorized into hard matching functions, soft functions, and standard TMDFFs, and calculate all components to next-to-leading order (NLO). Renormalization group equations are solved to resum large logarithms of the form \(\ln(p_T R/j_\perp)\) and \(\ln R\), and the formalism is compared to LHC data.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic jet scale is \(\mu_J \sim \omega_J \tan(R/2) \to p_T R\), which sets the natural scale for the hard matching functions.
- The logarithm \(L\) appearing in the hard matching functions is defined as \(L = \ln\left( \mu^2 / [\omega_J^2 \tan^2(R/2)] \right)\).
- The NLO soft function in momentum space includes a factor \(\left( \nu \tan(R/2) / \mu \right)^\eta\) with color factors \(C_i = C_F\) for quarks and \(C_i = C_A\) for gluons.
- The TMDFFs in \(b\)-space at NLO contain terms proportional to \(\ln(\mu^2/\mu_b^2)\) where \(\mu_b = 2e^{-\gamma_E}/b\), with color factors \(C_F\) and \(C_A\) for quarks and gluons respectively.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for jet substructure observables, building on earlier studies of longitudinal momentum distributions of hadrons inside jets (Procura 2009, Jain 2011, Kang 2016) and on TMD factorization for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and electron-positron annihilation (Collins 2011). The authors note that the same TMDFFs appear in the in-jet observable as in SIDIS, but without the convolution with TMD parton distribution functions, enabling a more direct extraction of TMDFFs, particularly for gluons which are poorly constrained. The formalism enables resummation of large logarithms and provides a framework for future phenomenological extractions of TMDFFs from LHC data, complementing measurements at RHIC (Aschenauer 2013, 2015, 2016).
