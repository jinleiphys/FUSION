# Strong Coupling Improved Equilibration In High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> In high energy nuclear collisions, most calculations on the early equilibration of the parton plasma showed that the system does not come close to full equilibrium, especially for the fermion components. However, since the system is constantly evolving, the cooling effect due to expansion will lead to a decrease of the average parton energies. So the interactions should be enhanced by a corresponding increase of the running coupling. We show that this leads to a faster and improved equilibration. This improvement is more important for the fermions compensating partially for their weaker interactions and slower equilibration.

## Key claim
The paper argues that using a time-dependent running coupling, which increases as the expanding parton plasma cools, leads to faster and more complete equilibration than calculations using a fixed coupling; the improvement is more significant for quarks than for gluons, but comes at the cost of a shorter deconfined phase duration.

## Method
The author solves the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation with collision terms constructed from tree-level perturbative QCD, including binary collisions, 2-to-3 gluon multiplication, and their reverse processes. The time evolution is studied by comparing results for fixed values of the strong coupling (0.3, 0.5, 0.8) with a time-varying coupling obtained from the one-loop running coupling formula evaluated at the scale of the average parton energy at each time. Initial conditions are taken from HIJING after allowing the parton gas to free-stream to an isotropic distribution.

## Key numbers
- At LHC energies, gluon fugacities at around 4.0 fm/c are already reasonably close to 1.0 for all coupling values studied.
- At RHIC energies, gluon fugacities at around 6.0 fm/c are reasonably close to 1.0 for all coupling values studied.
- Fixed coupling values used for comparison are alpha_s = 0.3, 0.5, and 0.8, with the time-varying coupling denoted alpha_s^v.
- The average momentum transfer for the fixed coupling case of alpha_s = 0.3 is around 2.0 GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier parton cascade models by Geiger and Muller (1992) and Geiger (1992) that showed approximate thermalization in the first few fm/c, and by Geiger and Kapusta (1993), Biro et al. (1993), and Wong (1996) that found chemical equilibration impossible in existing models. The paper shows that using a time-dependent running coupling, rather than a fixed value, improves equilibration significantly for quarks and removes a free parameter, but at the expense of reducing the duration of the deconfined phase. This enables more consistent modeling of the early-time evolution of the parton plasma in high-energy nuclear collisions.
