# $ϕ$ meson mass and decay width in strange hadronic matter

> The in-medium mass and decay width of the $ϕ$ meson in the hot asymmetric strange hadronic matter are calculated using an effective Lagrangian approach for $ϕ$ $K\bar K$ interaction. The in-medium contributions of $K \bar K$ loop to the $ϕ$ meson self-energy are computed by the medium modified kaon and antikaon mass which is evaluated using the chiral SU(3) model. To deal with the ultraviolet divergence, we regularize the loop integral of self-energy with a dipole form factor, and present the results for cut-off mass, $Λ_c$=3 GeV. In chiral model calculations, for strange matter, we find that the mass of kaons and antikaons decreases with the increase in baryonic density whereas the finite temperature causes an increase in the mass. We observed that the in-medium $ϕ$ meson mass decreases slowly with baryonic density whereas the decay width increases rapidly. The results in the present investigation support result in the literature which indicate a small downward shift in mass and a large broadening in the decay width. In the asymmetric strange hadronic matter, the study of the $ϕ$ mesons can be relevant for the compressed strange baryonic matter and experimental observables such as dilepton spectra which can result from the experiments in the future FAIR facility at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the in-medium mass and decay width of the phi meson in hot, asymmetric strange hadronic matter using an effective Lagrangian approach, finding a small downward shift in mass and a large broadening in decay width, with the specific results depending on the regularization cut-off parameter (set to Lambda_c = 3 GeV) and the medium-modified kaon and antikaon masses from the chiral SU(3) model.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and vector fields (omega, rho, phi) of the chiral SU(3) model in mean-field approximation to obtain the in-medium scalar and vector densities of baryons (including nucleons and hyperons). Using these fields, they compute the in-medium masses of kaons and antikaons via dispersion relations derived from the interaction Lagrangian. These medium-modified kaon and antikaon masses are then used as input to calculate the phi meson self-energy from the K-anti-K loop diagram, regularized with a dipole form factor at cut-off mass Lambda_c = 3 GeV, from which the in-medium phi mass and decay width are obtained.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum phi meson mass is taken as 1019.461 MeV and the coupling constant g_phi is determined as 4.539 from the empirical vacuum decay width (PDG 2015).
* The cut-off mass for regularization of the loop integral is Lambda_c = 3 GeV.
* At nuclear saturation density rho_0, the sigma field changes by 67% compared to its vacuum value at rho_B = 4 rho_0 in symmetric non-strange matter at zero temperature, while the zeta field changes by only 14%.
* In symmetric strange matter at zero temperature and rho_B = 4 rho_0, the zeta field changes by 16% compared to its value at f_s = 0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson properties in nuclear matter by Ko et al. (1992), Klingl et al. (1998), and Martinez et al. (2016) to include finite temperature, isospin asymmetry, and strangeness fraction simultaneously, using the chiral SU(3) model. The results support the general consensus of a small downward mass shift and large broadening of the phi decay width in medium, and are relevant for interpreting dilepton spectra from compressed strange baryonic matter at future FAIR facility experiments.
