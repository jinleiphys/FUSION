# Shear viscosity and the r-mode instability window in superfluid neutron stars

> We analyze how recent computations of the shear viscosity $η$ in the core of superfluid neutron stars affect the r-mode instability window. We first analyze the contribution of superfluid phonons to the viscosity, both in their hydrodynamical and ballistic regime. We also consider the recent computation of $η$ arising from the collisions of electrons with electrons and protons by Shternin and Yakovlev, and discuss how the interactions among superfluid phonons and electrons might contribute to the shear viscosity. For assessing the r-mode instability window we compare the shear viscosity due to phonons in the hydrodynamical regime with respect to the shear viscosity due to electron collisions. Only at high temperatures the superfluid phonon contribution to $η$ starts to dominate the process of r-mode damping. While our results for the instability window are preliminary, as other dissipative processes should be taken into account as well, they differ from previous evaluations of the r-mode damping due to the shear viscosity in superfluid neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors find that the r-mode instability window in superfluid neutron stars is modified from previous estimates when including the shear viscosity from superfluid phonons in the hydrodynamical regime and the corrected electron shear viscosity from Shternin and Yakovlev (2008), but they state that their results are preliminary because other dissipative processes (such as bulk viscosities and mutual friction) have not yet been included.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity from superfluid phonon-phonon interactions using effective field theory techniques, considering both the hydrodynamical and ballistic regimes of phonons. They also incorporated the electron shear viscosity from Shternin and Yakovlev (2008), which includes both longitudinal and transverse plasmon exchange with proper screening, including suppression factors for proton superconductivity. Using the APR equation of state in a causal parametrization, they compared the phonon and electron contributions to shear viscosity and assessed their impact on the r-mode instability window for a simplified neutron star model with neutrons, protons, and electrons.

## Key numbers

- The electron shear viscosity from Flowers and Itoh (used in previous r-mode studies) was overestimated by "a few orders of magnitude" compared to the Shternin and Yakovlev (2008) computation
- The critical temperature for the neutron superfluid transition is taken in the range \(T_c \sim 10^{10}\) K
- The proton superconductor transition temperature is taken as \(T_{cp} = 10^9\) K
- The normal saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a mass density of \(\rho_0 \sim 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\)
- The shear viscosity from electron collisions is shown for three temperatures: \(T = 10^8\) K, \(T = 10^9\) K, and \(T = 5 \times 10^9\) K (the latter being close to the expected neutron superfluid transition temperature)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-mode damping in superfluid neutron stars by including the previously ignored contribution of superfluid phonons to shear viscosity, following the authors' own computation in Manuel (2011). It also incorporates the corrected electron shear viscosity from Shternin and Yakovlev (2008), which showed that earlier results by Flowers and Itoh had overestimated this quantity. The results differ from previous evaluations of r-mode damping due to shear viscosity in superfluid neutron stars, such as those by Haskell (2009), and the authors note that future work should include other dissipative processes like bulk viscosities from Manuel (2013) and mutual friction effects.
