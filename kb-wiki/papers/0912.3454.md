# A relativistic coupled-channel formalism for the pion form factor

> The electromagnetic form factor of a confined quark-antiquark pair is calculated within the framework of point-form relativistic quantum mechanics. The dynamics of theexchanged photon is explicitly taken into account by treating theelectromagnetic scattering of an electron by a meson as a relativistic two-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator. This approach guarantees Poincare invariance. Using a Feshbach reduction the coupled-channel problem can be converted into a one-channel problem for the elastic electron-meson channel. By comparing the one-photon-exchange optical potential at the constituent and hadronic levels, we are able to unambiguously identify the electromagnetic meson form factor. Violations of cluster-separability properties, which are inherent in the Bakamjian-Thomas approach, become negligible for sufficiently large invariant mass of the electron-meson system. In the limit of an infinitely large invariant mass, an equivalence with form-factor calculations done in front-form relativistic quantum mechanics is established analytically.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Poincare-invariant method for calculating the electromagnetic form factor of a confined quark-antiquark pair, but the extracted form factor acquires an additional dependence on the invariant mass of the electron-meson system that violates cluster separability; this dependence vanishes only in the limit of infinitely large invariant mass, where the result becomes equivalent to front-form calculations.

## Method

The authors treat electron-meson scattering as a relativistic two-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator, where the second channel contains the photon. Using a Feshbach reduction, they convert the coupled-channel problem into a single-channel problem for the elastic electron-meson channel, then extract the meson form factor by comparing the one-photon-exchange optical potential at the hadronic and constituent levels. At the constituent level, the meson is modeled as a confined quark-antiquark pair with an instantaneous confinement potential, and the photon coupling to electrons and quarks is adapted from quantum electrodynamics within the Bakamjian-Thomas framework.

## Key numbers

- The form factor F(Q) is given by an integral over bound-state wave functions: F(Q) = integral d^3 k_q' sqrt(m_qqbar / m_qqbar') S Psi*(k_q') Psi(k_q), where Q^2 = q^2 is the momentum transfer squared.
- The additional dependence of the form factor on |k_M| (the center-of-mass momentum of the meson, equivalent to the invariant mass of the electron-meson system) vanishes quickly with increasing |k_M|, and the limit |k_M| -> infinity yields equivalence with front-form calculations.
- Using a two-parameter harmonic-oscillator wave function with parameters taken from Cardarelli and Coon (1988) and Cardarelli and Pace (2005), the calculated pion form factor provides a reasonable fit to experimental data from Amendolia (1986), Bebek (1973, 1974, 1976, 1978), and Huber (2008).

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas approach (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953; Keister and Polyzou, 1991) to electromagnetic form factors of mesons, using the velocity-state representation developed by Klink (1998). The method enables a Poincare-invariant treatment of the exchanged photon dynamics that was previously missing in point-form calculations. The equivalence established with front-form calculations (Cardarelli and Coon, 1988; Cardarelli and Pace, 2005) shows that relativity is treated equivalently in both approaches, and the formalism can be generalized to electroweak form factors of arbitrary few-body bound systems and to handle exchange-current effects.
