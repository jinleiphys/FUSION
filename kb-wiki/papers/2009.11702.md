# Dilepton production in microscopic transport theory with in-medium $ρ$-meson spectral function

> We use the microscopic GiBUU transport model to calculate dilepton ($e^+e^-$) production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS18 energies focusing on the effect of collisional broadening of the $ρ$-meson. The collisional width of the $ρ$-meson at finite temperature and baryon density in nuclear matter is calculated on the basis of the collision integral of the GiBUU model. A systematic comparison with HADES data on dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions is performed. The collisional broadening of the $ρ$ improves the agreement between theory and experiment for the dilepton invariant-mass distributions near the $ρ$ pole mass and for the excess radiation in Au+Au at $1.23 A$ GeV. We furthermore show that some remaining underprediction of the experimental dilepton spectra in C+C at $1 A$ GeV and Au+Au at $1.23 A$ GeV at intermediate invariant masses $0.2-0.4$ GeV can be accounted for by adjusting the $pn$ bremsstrahlung cross section in a way to agree with the inclusive dilepton spectrum from $dp$ collisions at $1.25 A$ GeV.

# Dilepton production in microscopic transport theory with in-medium ρ-meson spectral function

## Key claim

The collisional broadening of the ρ-meson, calculated from the collision integral of the GiBUU transport model without invoking thermal equilibrium or chiral symmetry restoration, improves agreement with HADES dilepton data near the ρ pole mass and for excess radiation in Au+Au at 1.23 A GeV, but some underprediction remains at intermediate invariant masses 0.2–0.4 GeV that can be accounted for by adjusting the pn bremsstrahlung cross section to agree with dp collision data.

## Method

The GiBUU (Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) microscopic transport model was used to simulate dilepton (e⁺e⁻) production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS18 energies, solving coupled semi-classical quantum-kinetic equations for baryons, antibaryons, and mesons with relativistic mean-field potentials and off-shell transport for broad particles via the off-shell potential ansatz. The collisional width of the ρ-meson in nuclear matter was calculated from the collision integral of the transport equation, specifically from resonance production ρN → R on nucleons of the Fermi sea, and added to the free ρ decay width to construct the in-medium ρ spectral function. Dilepton production channels included direct vector meson decays (ρ, ω, φ), Dalitz decays (π⁰, η, η′, ω, Δ(1232)), and bremsstrahlung (pn, pp, π±N), with a shining method used to simulate rare dilepton decays.

## Context

This work extends earlier purely hadronic resonance model calculations of ρ-meson in-medium broadening by Rapp (1997), Peters (1997), Post (2000, 2003), and Muehlich (2006), which described observed broadening without chiral symmetry restoration, and contrasts with approaches using chirally gauged linear sigma models with quarks by Jung (2016, 2019) that demonstrated ρ broadening via chiral symmetry restoration from Functional Renormalization Group flow equations. The present microscopic transport approach without thermal equilibrium or quark-gluon degrees of freedom enables a systematic test of whether HADES dilepton data can be interpreted as evidence for chiral symmetry restoration as suggested by Seck (2017) and Adamczewski-Musch (2019), or alternatively explained by collisional broadening alone. The calculations provide predictions for Ag+Ag at 1.58 A GeV that can be tested by future HADES measurements.
