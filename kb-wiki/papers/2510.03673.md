# Effects of hadronic molecule $N(2080)3/2^-$ on $K^{*+}Λ$ photoproduction

> In our previous work [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 101}, 014003 (2020)], we have analyzed all available data on differential cross sections and spin density matrix elements for the $γp \to K^{\ast +} Λ$ reaction using an effective Lagrangian approach. There, the $t$-channel $K$, $K^*$, and $κ$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Σ^*$ exchanges, the $s$-channel $N$, $N(2060)5/2^-$, and $N(2000)5/2^+$ exchanges, and the interaction current were taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes. It was found that, overall, the agreement of the theoretical results with the corresponding data is fairly good. However, noticeable discrepancies between the model results and the data on spin density matrix elements $ρ_{00}$ are still observed in the center-of-mass energy region around $W \approx 2.08$ GeV, indicating the need for additional reaction mechanisms. On the other hand, the hidden-strange pentaquark-like state $N(2080)3/2^-$, which is proposed to be a $K^*Σ$ molecular state as the strange partner of $P_c(4457)$ hadronic molecule, has been found to play quite active roles in reproducing the data for $K^{*+}Σ^0$, $K^{*0}Σ^+$, and $ϕp$ photoproduction reactions. Based on these observations, in the present work, we reanalyze the data for the $γp \to K^{\ast +} Λ$ reaction by incorporating the pentaquark-like state $N(2080)3/2^-$ in our previously proposed model. The results show that with the inclusion of the contributions of $N(2080)3/2^-$, the quality of the theoretical description of the $γp \to K^{*+}Λ$ data can be considerably improved.

## Key claim

Including the hadronic molecule \(N(2080)3/2^-\) as an \(s\)-channel exchange in the effective Lagrangian model for \(\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda\) significantly improves the description of spin density matrix elements \(\rho_{00}\) near the center-of-mass energy \(W \approx 2.08\) GeV, which were previously underestimated in the model of Wei et al. (2020) that omitted this state.

## Method

The authors extended their previous tree-level effective Lagrangian model for \(\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda\) (Wei et al., 2020) by adding the \(s\)-channel exchange of the \(N(2080)3/2^-\) resonance, treated as a \(K^*\Sigma\) molecular state. The couplings of this state to \(K^{*}\Lambda\) and \(\gamma N\) were calculated via full triangle loop diagrams, with the \(N(2080)3/2^- \to K^*\Sigma\) coupling fixed using Weinberg's compositeness criterion. All available data on differential cross sections and spin density matrix elements were refitted, with the mass and width of \(N(2080)3/2^-\) fixed at 2080 MeV and 141 MeV, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for \(N(2080)3/2^- \to K^*\Sigma\) was fixed at \(g_{R\Sigma K^*} = 1.72\), derived from a binding energy \(\epsilon \equiv m_{K^*} + m_\Sigma - m_R\) using Weinberg's compositeness criterion.
- The fitted mass of \(N(2000)5/2^+\) is \(2009 \pm 1\) MeV, with width \(400 \pm 1\) MeV; the fitted mass of \(N(2060)5/2^-\) is \(2035 \pm 1\) MeV, with width \(70 \pm 4\) MeV.
- The cutoff parameters for the triangle loop form factors are \(\Lambda_M = 837 \pm 7\) MeV (meson exchange) and \(\Lambda_B = 733 \pm 9\) MeV (baryon exchange).
- The global \(\chi^2/N\) improved from 5.27 (Wei et al., 2020) to 5.04 in the present work; for the near-threshold region \(W \le 2217\) MeV, \(\chi^2/N\) improved from 4.53 to 3.94.
- For \(\rho_{00}\) data in the near-threshold region (\(W \le 2217\) MeV), \(\chi^2_{\rho_{00}}/N_{\rho_{00}}\) improved from 9.03 (Wei et al., 2020) to 7.70.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian analysis of \(\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda\) by Wei et al. (2020), which included \(s\)-channel \(N(2060)5/2^-\) and \(N(2000)5/2^+\) exchanges but left discrepancies in \(\rho_{00}\) near \(W \approx 2.08\) GeV. The \(N(2080)3/2^-\) state, proposed as the strange partner of the \(P_c(4457)\) hidden-charm pentaquark (Lin et al., 2018; He et al., 2017; Zou, 2018), had previously been shown to play active roles in \(K^{*+}\Sigma^0\), \(K^{*0}\Sigma^+\), and \(\phi p\) photoproduction (Ben et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2023; Shim et al., 2024). The present results support the hadronic molecular interpretation of \(N(2080)3/2^-\) and enable further studies of hidden-strange pentaquark-like states in photoproduction reactions.
