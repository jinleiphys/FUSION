# Spin polarized neutron matte and magnetic susceptibility within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation

> The Brueckner--Hartree--Fock formalism is applied to study spin polarized neutron matter properties. Results of the total energy per particle as a function of the spin polarization and density are presented for two modern realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, Nijmegen II and Reid93. We find that the dependence of the energy on the spin polarization is practically parabolic in the full range of polarizations. The magnetic susceptibility of the system is computed. Our results show no indication of a ferromagnetic transition which becomes even more difficult as the density increases.

## Key claim

The total energy per particle of neutron matter depends on spin polarization in a nearly parabolic way up to full polarization, and the magnetic susceptibility computed from this parabolic dependence shows no indication of a ferromagnetic phase transition at densities up to seven times nuclear saturation density, contrary to some earlier model predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation using the continuous prescription for the single-particle potential, constructing G-matrices for each spin combination (up-up, down-down, and coupled up-down channels). They employed two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, Nijmegen II and Reid93, and computed the total energy per particle as a function of total density and spin polarization, from which the magnetic susceptibility was derived via the second derivative of the energy with respect to polarization at zero polarization.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter used as reference is \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Densities explored range from \(\rho_0/2\) up to \(7\rho_0\).
- The ratio \(\chi/\chi_F\) (magnetic susceptibility relative to the Pauli free Fermi gas value) decreases rapidly at small densities and more slowly at higher densities, starting from 1 at zero density.
- For densities above \(2\rho_0\), the discontinuous prescription results for \(\chi/\chi_F\) differ by more than 10% from the continuous prescription results.
- The magnetic susceptibility results show very good agreement with the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculation of Fantoni et al. (2001), which found a reduction of about a factor of 3 relative to the Fermi gas value.

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner theory calculations by Backmann and Kallman (1973) using the Reid soft-core potential, and variational calculations by Pandharipande et al. (1972) who found no ferromagnetic transition for \(k_F \leq 5\) fm\(^{-1}\). It contradicts earlier predictions from hard-sphere gas models by Brownell and Callaway (1969) and Rice (1969) that found ferromagnetic transitions at lower densities, as well as Skyrme-type effective interaction results by Vidaurre et al. (1984) predicting transitions at \(k_F \approx 1.73\)–\(1.97\) fm\(^{-1}\). The results enable more reliable calculations of neutrino mean free paths in dense matter for supernova and proto-neutron star studies, as motivated by the recent AFDMC work of Fantoni et al. (2001).
