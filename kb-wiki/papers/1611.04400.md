# Heavy-Light Mesons in Chiral AdS/QCD

> We discuss a minimal holographic model for the description of heavy-light and light mesons with chiral symmetry, defined in a slab of AdS space. The model consists of a pair of chiral Yang-Mills and tachyon fields with specific boundary conditions that break spontaneously chiral symmetry in the infrared. The heavy-light spectrum and decay constants are evaluated explicitly. In the heavy mass limit the model exhibits both heavy-quark and chiral symmetry and allows for the explicit derivation of the one-pion axial couplings to the heavy-light mesons.

# Heavy-Light Mesons in Chiral AdS/QCD

## Key claim

The paper presents a minimal bottom-up holographic model for heavy-light mesons that exhibits both chiral symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry in the heavy mass limit, but the model's predictions for the heavy-light spectrum, decay constants, and axial couplings deviate significantly from experimental and lattice values, with the authors noting that the heavy-light meson wavefunctions are independent of the heavy quark mass in contrast to their earlier top-down approach.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model in a slab of AdS space (0 < z ≤ z₀) with a pair of chiral Yang-Mills fields (A_L, A_R) and tachyon fields (X_L, X_R), where chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken by infrared boundary conditions at z = z₀. Heavy-light mesons are identified with off-diagonal fluctuations of the gauge fields (Φ fields) that acquire mass through coupling to the tachyon background, with the transverse modes giving vector and axial-vector states and the longitudinal modes mixing with tachyonic modes to give scalar and pseudo-scalar states. The one-pion axial couplings to heavy-light mesons are derived from the Chern-Simons action and the bulk action using mode decompositions in the heavy quark limit.

## Key numbers

- Lowest vector mode: k₀ = 2.40/z₀; lowest axial mode: k₁ = 3.83/z₀ (from Bessel function zeros)
- Splitting between axial-vector and vector states in heavy quark limit: Δ_Q ≈ (k₁² - k₀²)/(2m_Q) = 8.91/(2m_Q z₀²)
- Predicted splitting ratio Δ_C/Δ_B ≈ m_B/m_C ≈ 3.28, compared to empirical ratio 420/396 = 1.06 (CLEO, 2009)
- Ratio of B-meson to D-meson decay constant: f_B/f_D = m_D/m_B = 1869/5279 = 0.35, compared to lattice ratio 0.88 (Aoki, 2014) and general argument 0.55
- Axial coupling for H-multiplet (0⁻, 1⁻): g_H = 0.10, compared to experimental value 0.65 (CLEO, 2009)
- Axial coupling for G-multiplet (0⁺, 1⁺): g_G = 0.14
- Cross-multiplet coupling: g_HG = -0.45, compared to g_HG ≈ 0.18 for charmed and 0.10 for bottom mesons in the top-down approach (Liu and Zahed, 2015)
- Ratio of longitudinal mode mixing: (1/2)(k₀/k₁ + k₁/k₀) = (1/2)(2.4/3.83 + 3.83/2.4) = 1.11

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier top-down holographic construction (Liu and Zahed, 2015) based on D-branes in the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005), providing a simpler bottom-up alternative that captures chiral symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry without the technical difficulties of the D-brane setup. The model builds on prior bottom-up holographic QCD approaches (Erlich et al., 2005; Da Rold and Pomarol, 2005; Brodsky et al., 2015) and hidden chiral symmetry methods (Bando et al., 1985). The results enable future applications to electromagnetic decays of heavy-light mesons and the description of heavy-light baryons as holographic solitonic bound states, though the authors note the hard-wall model's well-known shortcoming of not producing Regge trajectories, which could be remedied by a soft wall (Karch et al., 2006) or improved holographic QCD (Kiritsis et al., 2006).
