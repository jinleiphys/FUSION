# Sum rules for total hadronic widths of light mesons and rectilineal stitch of the masses on the complex plane

> Mass formulae for light meson multiplets derived by means of exotic commutator technique are written for complex masses and considered as complex mass sum rules (CMSR). The real parts of the (CMSR) give the well known mass formulae for real masses (Gell-Mann--Okubo, Schwinger and Ideal Mixing ones) and the imaginary parts of CMSR give appropriate sum rules for the total hadronic widths - width sum rules (WSR). Most of the observed meson nonets satisfy the Schwinger mass formula (S nonets). The CMSR predict for S nonet that the points $(m,Γ)$ form the rectilinear stitch (RS) on the complex mass plane. For low-mass nonets WSR are strongly violated due to ``kinematical'' suppression of the particle decays, but the violation decreases as the mass icreases and disappears above $\sim 1.5 GeV$. The slope $k_s$ of the RS is not predicted, but the data show that it is negative for all S nonets and its numerical values are concentrated in the vicinity of the value -0.5. If $k_s$ is known for a nonet, we can evaluate ``kinematical'' suppressions of its individual particles. The masses and the widths of the S nonet mesons submit to some rules of ordering which matter in understanding the properties of the nonet. We give the table of the S nonets indicating masses, widths, mass and width orderings. We show also mass-width diagrams for them. We suggest to recognize a few multiplets as degenerate octets. In Appendix we analyze the nonets of $1^+$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper extends standard SU(3) flavor mass sum rules (Gell-Mann-Okubo, Schwinger, Ideal Mixing) to complex masses, deriving corresponding width sum rules (WSR) for total hadronic widths of light meson nonets. The central claim is that for Schwinger-type nonets, the points (mass, width) lie on a straight line (rectilinear stitch) on the complex mass plane, but the slope is not predicted by theory and the WSR are strongly violated at low masses due to kinematic suppression, only becoming valid above approximately 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The author uses the exotic commutator technique, assuming that certain commutators of SU(3) flavor generators with time derivatives vanish, and applies an infinite-momentum approximation for the one-particle Hamiltonian to derive operator equations for squared masses. These equations are then generalized to complex masses by introducing a complex mass operator m_c^2 = m^2 - i m Gamma, and the resulting complex mass sum rules (CMSR) are solved for the real parts (giving standard mass formulae) and imaginary parts (giving width sum rules). The analysis is applied to seven identified Schwinger nonets of light mesons, comparing predicted relations with experimental data from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends the exotic commutator technique previously used to derive Gell-Mann-Okubo, Schwinger, and Ideal Mixing mass formulae for real masses (Gell-Mann, 1961; Okubo, 1962; Schwinger, 1964) to the complex mass plane, providing the first systematic derivation of sum rules for total hadronic widths. The paper identifies seven Schwinger nonets (1⁻⁻, 1⁺⁻, 1⁺⁺, 0⁺⁺, 2⁺⁺, 2⁻⁺, 3⁻⁻) and shows that the pseudoscalar nonet (pi, K, eta, eta') is the only recognized Gell-Mann-Okubo nonet. The results enable future evaluation of kinematic suppression factors for individual meson decays and suggest that several higher-mass multiplets (rho(1450), K*(1410), omega(1420); rho(1700), K*(1680), omega(1650), Phi(1680); a4(2040), K4(2045), f4(2050)) can be recognized as degenerate octets where width sum rules are well satisfied.
