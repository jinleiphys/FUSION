# Why the observed jet quenching at RHIC is due to parton energy loss

> Significant jet quenching in central $Au+Au$ collisions has been discovered at RHIC. This paper provides theoretical arguments and lists experimental evidence that the observed jet quenching at RHIC is due to parton energy loss instead of hadron rescattering or absorption in a hadronic medium. These include: (1) hadron formation time based on the uncertainty principle, (2) $p_T$ dependence and (3) centrality dependence of the observed jet quenching, (4) jet-like leading hadron correlations (5) high-$p_T$ azimuthal anisotropy and (6) experimental data from $Pb+Pb$ collisions at SPS and $e+A$ collisions. Direct measurements of the parton energy loss in the direction of a triggered high-$p_T$ hadron and the medium modified fragmentation function on the back-side are proposed to further verify the partonic nature of the observed jet quenching. The importance of jet quenching studies at lower energies at RHIC is also discussed.

# Why the observed jet quenching at RHIC is due to parton energy loss

## Key claim
The observed jet quenching in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is caused mainly by parton energy loss, not hadron rescattering or absorption in a hadronic medium, based on six specific experimental and theoretical arguments. The claim is conditional on the hadron formation time being much longer than the medium lifetime, the flat pT dependence of suppression being incompatible with hadronic energy loss, and the centrality dependence of suppression being inconsistent with a finite formation time shorter than the medium size.

## Method
The paper compares theoretical predictions from parton energy loss models (including the non-Abelian LPM interference effect and detailed balance between gluon emission and absorption in a hot medium) against experimental data from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) for single inclusive hadron spectra, two-particle azimuthal correlations, and high-pT azimuthal anisotropy. The author also analyzes hadron formation times using the uncertainty principle and dipole models, calculates elastic energy loss for pions in a hadronic gas, and computes the centrality dependence of the suppression factor using a path-integral formulation of parton energy loss proportional to gluon density along the propagation trajectory.

## Key numbers
- Hadron formation time for a 10 GeV pion: 35-70 fm/c (using R_h = 0.5-1 fm) or approximately 40 fm/c (using dipole model with Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV)
- Elastic energy loss for a pion in a hadronic gas at T ~ 150 MeV: approximately 0.036 GeV/fm, independent of pion energy
- Suppression factor of about 5 for single hadron spectra in most central Au+Au collisions
- Initial gluon density at tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c is about 30 times that in normal nuclear matter
- Initial energy density deduced: epsilon = 15 GeV/fm^3, about 100 times the energy density in cold nuclear matter
- Average energy loss for jets producing a leading hadron at pT_trig = 5-15 GeV/c in most central collisions: 1.4-2.2 GeV
- Average z = pT_trig / <E_T>^jet in p+p collisions: about 0.6-0.7
- Calculated suppression factor at SPS energy (pT = 4 GeV/c) is about a factor of 3 smaller than the data

## Context
This paper extends the original proposal of jet quenching due to radiative parton energy loss by Gyulassy and Wang (1990) and Wang and Gyulassy (1992), and the subsequent theoretical developments including the non-Abelian LPM effect by Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (1997), Zakharov (1996), and others. It directly contradicts the alternative explanation of hadronic rescattering or absorption, using experimental data from PHENIX, STAR, and PHOBOS collaborations at RHIC, as well as HERMES data from deeply inelastic scattering and WA98 data from SPS. The work enables future direct measurements of parton energy loss via jet energy reconstruction and medium-modified fragmentation functions, and motivates jet quenching studies at lower RHIC energies to explore threshold behavior.
