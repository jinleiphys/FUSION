# Enhancement of low-mass dileptons in ultraperipheral collisions

> It is shown that production of low-mass $e^+e^-$-pairs in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions is enhanced due to the Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov (SGS) factor. This effect is especially strong near the threshold of creation of unbound $e^+e^-$-pairs with low masses in the two-photon fusion. Coulomb attraction of the non-relativistic components of such pairs may lead to the increased intensity of 511 keV photons. It can be recorded at the NICA collider and has some astrophysical implications. The analogous effect can be observed at LHC in dilepton production.

## Key claim

The production of low-mass electron-positron pairs in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions is enhanced by the Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov (SGS) factor, but this enhancement is modest in overall contribution, about 4.6% at the peak of the mass-squared distribution and 2.5% at the peak of the velocity-squared distribution at NICA energy, and the cross section still tends to zero at the exact threshold due to energy-momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors start from the equivalent photon approximation for ultraperipheral collisions, where the cross section for two-photon fusion into lepton pairs is expressed as an integral over photon fluxes and the perturbative QED cross section for γγ → e⁺e⁻. They then incorporate the non-perturbative Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov factor, which accounts for Coulomb attraction between the non-relativistic pair components near threshold, by multiplying the perturbative differential mass distribution by the SGS factor T = 2πα / [v(1 − exp(−2πα/v))]. The resulting distributions for dielectron mass and relative velocity are evaluated numerically for NICA (√sₙₙ = 11 GeV) and LHC (√sₙₙ = 5.02 TeV) energies, and the enhancement is compared to the perturbative-only case.

## Key numbers

- At NICA energy (√sₙₙ = 11 GeV), the SGS factor enhances the cross section by about 4.6% at the peak of the M² distribution and 2.5% at the peak of the v² distribution; the integral contributions differ by 3.4%.
- The cross section for parapositronium production is about 20 mb, while the integrated cross section for unbound e⁺e⁻ pairs with masses from 2m = 1.022 MeV to 1.027 MeV is 40165 mb, and from 1.022 MeV to 1.029 MeV is 63296 mb.
- The widths of the 511 keV gamma-ray peaks observed from the Milky Way vary from 2.5 keV to 3.5 keV, corresponding to mass intervals from 1.022 MeV to 1.027 MeV and 1.022 MeV to 1.029 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of lepton pair production in ultraperipheral collisions, originally formulated by Landau and Lifshitz (1934) and later developed via the equivalent photon approximation by Weizsacker (1934) and Williams (1934), by incorporating the non-perturbative SGS factor previously applied to bound-state production (parapositronium) and dimesoatoms. The enhancement of low-mass unbound pairs is shown to be orders of magnitude larger than parapositronium decay, which may explain the abundant 511 keV gamma quanta observed during thunderstorms in the Earth atmosphere and in signals from the Universe, as discussed in Dremin (2020). The results suggest that experiments at the NICA collider could provide quantitative data on these pairs, and analogous effects should be observable at the LHC in dilepton production.
