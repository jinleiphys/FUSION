# Effect of neutron skin thickness on projectile fragmentation

> The fragment production in collisions of $^{48,50}$Ca+$^{12}$C at 50 MeV/nucleon are simulated via the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model followed by the {GEMINI code}. {By changing the diffuseness parameter of neutron density distribution to obtain different neutron skin size, the effects of neutron skin thickness ($δ_{np}$) on projectile-like fragments (PLF) are investigated. The sensitivity of isoscaling behavior to neutron skin size is studied, from which it is found that the isoscaling parameter $α$ has a linear dependence on $δ_{np}$. A linear dependence between $δ_{np}$ and the mean $N/Z$ [N(Z) is neutron(proton) number] of PLF is obtained as well.} The results show that thicker neutron skin will lead to smaller {isoscaling parameter} $α$ and N/Z. Therefore, it may be probable to extract information of neutron skin thickness from {isoscaling parameter} $α$ and N/Z.

## Key claim

The isoscaling parameter α and the mean N/Z of projectile-like fragments (PLF) both have a linear dependence on the neutron skin thickness δnp of the projectile, but only for heavy residues with charge numbers Z=15–19 in peripheral collisions; lighter fragments that reach chemical equilibrium lose this sensitivity.

## Method

The authors simulated 50 MeV/nucleon collisions of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁵⁰Ca projectiles on a ¹²C target using the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model, then de-excited the primary fragments with the GEMINI statistical decay code. For ⁵⁰Ca, the neutron diffuseness parameter fn in the Fermi-type density distribution was varied from 1.0 to 1.6 to produce different neutron skin thicknesses (δnp from 0.08 fm to 0.54 fm), while the proton distribution was kept fixed. Only peripheral collisions (reduced impact parameter 0.6 < b/bmax < 1.0) were analyzed, and fragments were constructed via a coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff P₀ = 300 MeV/c and distance cutoff R₀ = 3.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thicknesses used for ⁵⁰Ca: δnp = 0.08 fm (fn=1.0), 0.21 fm (fn=1.2), 0.37 fm (fn=1.4), and 0.54 fm (fn=1.6).
- The IQMD potential used a soft equation of state with incompressibility K = 200 MeV and symmetry energy strength Csym = 32 MeV.
- Coalescence parameters: relative momentum P₀ = 300 MeV/c, relative distance R₀ = 3.5 fm.
- The isoscaling parameter α was found to decrease linearly with increasing δnp for fragments with Z = 15–19.
- The average N/Z of PLF (Z = 15–19) also showed a linear decrease with increasing δnp.

## Context

This work extends earlier IQMD studies by Sun et al. (2010) on the correlation between the neutron-to-proton ratio of emitted fragments and neutron skin thickness, and by the same group (Dai et al., 2014) on the triton-to-³He ratio. It also builds on the statistical abrasion-ablation model results of MacW. (2004) showing that neutron skin suppresses neutron-rich fragment yields. The finding that isoscaling of heavy residues depends on δnp, unlike the constant isoscaling parameter for light fragments, suggests that measuring α or N/Z of projectile-like fragments in peripheral collisions could serve as a new experimental probe of neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich nuclei.
