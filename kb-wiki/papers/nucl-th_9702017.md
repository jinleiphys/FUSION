# Comparison of Fermion SU(3) and Boson SU(3) models for scissors mode excitations

> For a $Q \cdot Q$ interaction the energy weighted sum rule for isovector orbital magnetic dipole transitions is proportional to the difference $\sum B(E2, isoscalar) - \sum B(E2, isovector)$, not just to $\sum B(E2, physical)$. This fact is important in ensuring that one gets the correct limit as one goes to nuclei, some of which are far from stability, for which one shell (neutron or proton) is closed. In $0p$ shell calculations for the even-even Be isotopes it is shown that the Fermion SU(3) model and Boson SU(3) model give different results for the energy weighted scissors mode strengths.

# Comparison of Fermion SU(3) and Boson SU(3) models for scissors mode excitations

## Key claim

The energy weighted sum rule for isovector orbital magnetic dipole transitions is proportional to the difference between summed isoscalar and summed isovector B(E2) values, not simply to the summed physical B(E2); this distinction becomes critical for nuclei with one closed shell (neutron or proton), where the isoscalar and isovector B(E2) become equal and the scissors mode strength vanishes, even though the physical B(E2) can be large.

## Method

The authors performed 0p shell calculations for the even-even Be isotopes (6Be, 8Be, 10Be, 12Be) using a Q·Q interaction with a pairing interaction between like particles. They computed the energy weighted sum rule for isovector orbital M1 transitions (scissors mode) in the fermion SU(3) model and compared the results with predictions from the boson SU(3) model (IBA-2) by evaluating the expectation value of Qπ·Qν in both frameworks.

## Key numbers

- For 8Be, the calculated B(M1, 0₁ → 1₁) is 2/π μN² (approximately 0.637 μN²)
- For 10Be, the calculated B(M1) values are 9/(32π) μN² (0.0895 μN²) for T=1 → T=1 and 15/(32π) μN² (0.149 μN²) for T=1 → T=2
- The ratio of isovector orbital B(M1) for 10Be relative to 8Be is 3/8
- The energy weighted sum rule values are 6.411 MeV for 8Be and 2.030 MeV for 10Be, using χ = 0.5762 MeV/fm⁴ for 8Be and 0.3615 MeV/fm⁴ for 10Be
- For 6Be, the B(E2) analytic expression is (50/(4π)) b⁴ ep², giving 15.63 (in units of e² fm⁴) for both isoscalar and isovector
- For 8Be, the B(E2) isoscalar is 73.54 e² fm⁴ and isovector is 6.469 e² fm⁴
- For 10Be, the B(E2) isoscalar is 63.24 e² fm⁴ and isovector is 31.19 e² fm⁴ to the 2₁⁺ state
- The ratio ⟨Qπ·Qν⟩ for 10Be relative to 8Be is 33/72 = 0.458 in the fermion SU(3) model, but 1 in the boson SU(3) model

## Context

This work extends the energy weighted sum rule for scissors mode excitations developed by Zheng and Zamick (1991, 1992) from the fermion SU(3) model to a comparison with the boson SU(3) model of IBA-2, following earlier work by Fayache, Sharma and Zamick (1996) on 8Be and 10Be. The study was motivated by the empirical observation by Ziegler, Ranacharyulu, Richter and collaborators (1990, 1991) that B(M1) for the scissors mode in Sm isotopes is proportional to B(E2, 0₁ → 2₁). The results show that the fermion and boson SU(3) models give different predictions for the ratio of scissors mode strengths in 10Be relative to 8Be, with the fermion model yielding a ratio of 0.458 while the boson model gives 1; this discrepancy arises because a Q·Q interaction between fermions does not imply a Q·Q interaction between proton bosons and neutron bosons. The work enables future studies of scissors mode excitations in nuclei far from stability, particularly those with one closed shell.
