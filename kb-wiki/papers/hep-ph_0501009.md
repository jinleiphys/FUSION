# The chiral limit of QCD and above

> I review aspects of chiral dynamics pertinent to the structure of baryons and few-nucleon systems, such as chiral extrapolations for the nucleon and the delta mass, double pion photoproduction off protons, single neutral pion electroproduction off the deuteron, pion photoproduction in the delta region, the quark mass dependence of the nuclear forces and the possibility of an infrared limit cycle in QCD.

# The chiral limit of QCD and above

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) can provide controlled extrapolations of baryon and nuclear properties from the physical quark masses to the chiral limit, but the theoretical uncertainty grows rapidly for pion masses above approximately 550 MeV, and the approach to the chiral limit is non-analytic in the quark mass matrix, producing the well-known "chiral logs."

## Method

The paper reviews applications of chiral effective field theory (CHPT and its variants, including the small-scale expansion for the delta resonance) to baryon masses, photo- and electroproduction reactions, and nuclear forces. For the nucleon and delta masses, fourth-order chiral expansions are fitted to partially quenched lattice data from the MILC collaboration. For photo-nuclear processes, threshold multipole expansions are combined with deuteron wave functions from next-to-next-to-leading order nuclear chiral EFT. For nuclear forces, the quark mass dependence of the deuteron binding energy and S-wave scattering lengths is computed at next-to-leading order including two-pion exchange and contact terms.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the chiral SU(2) limit: m0 = 880 MeV, determined from fourth-order CHPT fits to pion-nucleon scattering data.
- Nucleon mass in the chiral SU(3) limit: m0^(SU(3)) in the range [710, 1070] MeV.
- Threshold energy for γ p → π⁰ π⁰ p: E_γ^(thr) = 308.8 MeV.
- Total cross section for γ p → π⁰ π⁰ p near threshold: σ_tot(E_γ) = C × [(E_γ - 308.8 MeV) / 10 MeV]² nb, with C = 0.6 (central value) or 0.9 (upper limit).
- Deuteron binding energy in the chiral limit: B_D^(CL) = 9.6 ± 1.9 (+1.8 / -1.0) MeV, where the first error is from uncertainty in the LEC D̅_(³S₁) and the second from uncertainty in d̅₁₆.
- Spin-singlet scattering length in the chiral limit: a_CL(¹S₀) = -4.1 ± 1.6 (+0.0 / -0.4) fm.
- Spin-triplet scattering length in the chiral limit: a_CL(³S₁) = 1.5 ± 0.4 (+0.2 / -0.3) fm.
- Critical pion mass range where the deuteron becomes unbound: 170 MeV < M_π < 210 MeV.
- Efimov scaling factor for the three-nucleon system: λ₀ = 22.7, with λ₀² = e^(2π/s₀) ≈ 515, where s₀ = 1.00624.

## Context

This review extends the program of baryon CHPT established by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (various years, e.g., the review BKMrev) and the small-scale expansion for the delta resonance developed by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (HHK, 1998). The chiral extrapolation work for the nucleon mass builds on earlier two-flavor calculations by Bernard, Hemmert, and Meißner (BHMcut) and extends to three flavors following Frink, Meißner, and Scheller (FMS). The photo-production predictions for double neutral pion production were tested by the TAPS collaboration at MAMI in 2004, confirming the central CHPT prediction from Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (BKM2pi, 1997). The quark mass dependence of nuclear forces follows the work of Epelbaum, Meißner, and Glöckle (EMG, 2003), and the conjecture of an infrared limit cycle in QCD was proposed by Braaten and Hammer (BH, 2003). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations at lower pion masses to be connected to physical observables via controlled chiral extrapolations, and suggest that tuning the up and down quark masses separately could produce a critical point where both S-wave scattering lengths diverge simultaneously, potentially revealing Efimov physics in QCD.
