# Towards robust constraints on nuclear effective field theory from lattice QCD

> We will discuss several new results from the NPLQCD Collaboration that combine lattice QCD results on (hyper)nuclear systems at unphysical pion masses together with nuclear effective field theories. Two-baryon channels with strangeness $0$ to $-4$ are analyzed, with findings that point to interesting symmetries observed in hypernuclear forces as predicted in the limit of QCD with a large number of colors. Also, several matrix elements of light nuclei are studied. The tritium axial charge, related to the Gamow-Teller matrix element, and the longitudinal momentum fraction of $^3$He that is carried by the isovector combination of $u$ and $d$ are extracted and extrapolated to the physical point. For this latter case, it can be seen how including lattice results to experimental determinations can have imminent potential to enable more precise determinations and to reveal the QCD origins of the EMC effect.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD results at unphysical pion masses (m_pi ~ 450 MeV and m_pi ~ 806 MeV) can be combined with nuclear effective field theories to constrain low-energy coefficients and extrapolate nuclear observables to the physical point, but the extrapolated values carry large uncertainties that limit the robustness of the constraints.

## Method

The NPLQCD Collaboration computed two-baryon energy levels for nine channels with strangeness 0 to -4 using lattice QCD at m_pi ~ 450 MeV, then extracted scattering parameters and matched them to the low-energy coefficients of SU(3) flavor-symmetric and SU(6) spin-flavor symmetric effective field theories. For the triton axial charge and the 3He isovector momentum fraction, the collaboration computed three-point correlation functions using the background-field technique on the same ensembles, and performed finite-volume extrapolations using finite-volume effective field theory (FVEFT) and pion-mass extrapolations using linear and quadratic functional forms.

## Key numbers

- Two-baryon channels studied: nine channels with strangeness S = 0 to -4, at m_pi ~ 450 MeV, extending previous work that only covered two-nucleon systems at that pion mass.
- Triton-to-proton axial charge ratio extrapolated to the physical point: g_A^3H / g_A^p = 0.91^{+0.07}_{-0.09}, compared to the experimental value 0.9511(13).
- Ratio of isovector momentum fractions: 3 * <x>_{u-d}^{3He} / <x>_{u-d}^{p} = 1.033(38) at the physical point, extrapolated from lattice data at m_pi ~ 806 MeV.
- The SU(3) flavor symmetry-breaking LECs extracted from the two-baryon channels are compatible with zero, indicating approximate SU(3) flavor symmetry persists at m_pi ~ 450 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's previous studies at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point (Wagman 2017) and at m_pi ~ 450 MeV for two-nucleon systems only (Orginos 2015) to include hypernuclear channels. The triton axial charge calculation (Parreno 2021) addresses a long-standing difficulty in describing the deviation of nuclear axial charges from unity directly from the Standard Model, while the 3He momentum fraction calculation (Detmold 2020) demonstrates how lattice QCD can constrain nuclear parton distribution functions and potentially reveal the QCD origins of the EMC effect, as shown by Bayesian reweighting with the nNNPDF2.0 global analysis (AbdulKhalek 2020).
