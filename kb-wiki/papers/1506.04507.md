# Pairing phase transition: A Finite-Temperature Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov study

> Background: The relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory has recently been developed and it provides a unified and highly predictive description of both nuclear mean field and pairing correlations. Ground state properties of finite nuclei can accurately be reproduced without neglecting exchange (Fock) contributions. Purpose: Finite-temperature RHFB (FT-RHFB) theory has not yet been developed, leaving yet unknown its predictions for phase transitions and thermal excitations in both stable and weakly bound nuclei. Method: FT-RHFB equations are solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis considering two kinds of pairing interactions: finite or zero range. Such a model is appropriate for describing stable as well as loosely bound nuclei since the basis states have correct asymptotic behaviour for large spatial distributions. Results: Systematic FT-RH(F)B calculations are performed for several semi-magic isotopic/isotonic chains comparing the predictions of a large number of Lagrangians, among which are PKA1, PKO1 and DD-ME2. It is found that the critical temperature for a pairing transition generally follows the rule $T_c = 0.60Δ(0)$ for a finite-range pairing force and $T_c = 0.57Δ(0)$ for a contact pairing force, where $Δ(0)$ is the pairing gap at zero temperature. Two types of pairing persistence are analysed: type I pairing persistence occurs in closed subshell nuclei while type II pairing persistence can occur in loosely bound nuclei strongly coupled to the continuum states. Conclusions: This first FT-RHFB calculation shows very interesting features of the pairing correlations at finite temperature and in finite systems such as pairing re-entrance and pairing persistence.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the pairing phase transition in finite nuclei generally follows the rule $T_c = 0.60\Delta(0)$ for a finite-range pairing force and $T_c = 0.57\Delta(0)$ for a contact pairing force, where $\Delta(0)$ is the pairing gap at zero temperature; however, deviations from this linear relation emerge for pairing gaps above about 2.5 MeV due to enhanced continuum-state contributions that are specific to finite systems.

## Method

The finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-RHFB) equations were solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis, which provides correct asymptotic behavior for large spatial distributions. Two types of pairing interactions were employed: the finite-range Gogny D1S force and a density-dependent contact interaction (DDCI). Systematic calculations were performed for several semi-magic isotopic and isotonic chains using 14 different covariant density functional Lagrangians, including PKA1, PKO1, and DD-ME2.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature ratio for finite-range pairing force: $T_c = 0.60\Delta(0)$
- Critical temperature ratio for contact pairing force: $T_c = 0.57\Delta(0)$
- For $^{124}$Sn, the ratio $T_c/\Delta_n(0)$ showed dispersion of less than 4% across 14 different Lagrangians, with no significant correlation to effective mass, incompressibility modulus, or symmetry energy
- Deviations from the linear $T_c/\Delta(0)$ relation emerged for pairing gaps $\Delta_n(0) \gtrsim 2.5$ MeV, where continuum contributions became sizable
- The pairing window for the DDCI interaction was set to 100 MeV in quasiparticle space
- For the DDCI pairing in $^{124}$Sn, the strength parameter $V_0$ values were 335 MeV fm$^{-3}$ (PKO2) and 342 MeV fm$^{-3}$ (DD-ME2) for RHFB calculations

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature BCS and HFB approaches of Sano (1963) and Goodman (1981) to the fully relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, including both Fock exchange terms and the small components of Dirac spinors in the pairing channel. Previous studies by Niu (2013) and Niksic (2014) had performed finite-temperature RHB calculations but neglected either Fock contributions or the small components. The use of a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis improves upon harmonic oscillator basis calculations (Dobaczewski 1984, Stoitsov 1998) for describing weakly bound nuclei near the drip line. This first FT-RHFB calculation enables future studies of pairing re-entrance and pairing persistence phenomena in both stable and loosely bound nuclei.
