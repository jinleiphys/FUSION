# New Tool to Detect Inhomogeneous Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> In this letter, we discuss a novel method to search for inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in theories with fermions. The prime application we have in mind is QCD, but the method is also applicable for other theories, including solid-state applications. It is based on an extension of the chiral susceptibility to inhomogeneous phases and it works as a stability analysis. Our method allows us to determine when homogeneous solutions have the tendency to create spatially modulated condensates. As proof of principle, we apply this technique to a rainbow-ladder QCD model and find that its phase diagram contains an inhomogeneous region.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a novel method for detecting inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking based on an extension of the chiral susceptibility to spatially modulated condensates, which works as a stability analysis that does not depend on any test-function and is not restricted to any region of the phase diagram. The method determines when homogeneous solutions have the tendency to create spatially modulated condensates, but as a stability analysis it does not say much about the fully self-consistent solution of the system.

## Method

The method applies a space-dependent chiral rotation of the quark fields, transforming the Lagrangian to include an additional term \(i\gamma_5\tau_3 \slashed q\), then computes an inhomogeneous chiral susceptibility \(\chi(q)\) in the symmetric phase by taking the derivative of the dynamically generated mass with respect to the bare quark mass. For QCD, the susceptibility depends on quark momentum and must be solved self-consistently, but the quark dressing functions do not change under the chiral rotation in the symmetric phase, so only the homogeneous Dyson-Schwinger equations need to be solved. As proof of principle, the technique is applied to a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD using the Qin-Chang model for the effective running coupling.

## Key numbers

- In the NJL model, the inhomogeneous chiral susceptibility \(\chi_\text{NJL}(q)\) reproduces exactly the unstable region found in Nickel (2009), with the union of regions where \(\chi_\text{NJL}(q)\) is negative for some value of \(q\) matching the previously known inhomogeneous phase boundary.
- In the rainbow-ladder QCD model, a proto-Lifschitz point (pLP) is found near \(T = 85\) MeV, where the stability boundary of the symmetric phase with respect to inhomogeneous fluctuations meets the left spinodal of the homogeneous first-order transition.
- A triple-point (3P) is found at \(T \simeq 60\) MeV where the instability line of the symmetric phase crosses the homogeneous first-order phase boundary, establishing that below this temperature the symmetric phase is unstable against inhomogeneous fluctuations in a region where it is favored over the homogeneous chiral broken solution.

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis framework previously generalized from model applications to QCD by Motta et al. (2023, 2024), which relied on specific test-functions and was limited to the left spinodal line. The new method overcomes these limitations and confirms the earlier finding of a proto-Lifschitz point in a rainbow-ladder model of QCD from Motta et al. (2024). The technique is applicable to any theory with fermions, including solid-state applications, and can be used with functional methods such as Dyson-Schwinger equations or the functional renormalisation group, enabling future studies of inhomogeneous phases in more realistic truncations of QCD and other theories such as inhomogeneous color superconductivity.
