# A Nakanishi-based model illustrating the covariant extension of the pion GPD overlap representation and its ambiguities

> A systematic approach for the model building of Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), based on their overlap representation within the DGLAP kinematic region and a further covariant extension to the ERBL one, is applied to the valence-quark pion's case, using light-front wave functions inspired by the Nakanishi representation of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (BSA). This simple but fruitful pion's GPD model illustrates the general model building technique and, in addition, allows for the ambiguities related to the covariant extension, grounded on the Double Distribution (DD) representation, to be constrained by requiring a soft-pion theorem to be properly observed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a systematic technique for building pion GPD models by extending the DGLAP-region overlap representation to the ERBL region via the Radon transform, but shows that this covariant extension is inherently ambiguous due to terms lying along the line β=0 in double distribution space; these ambiguities can be constrained at zero momentum transfer by requiring the soft-pion theorem to be satisfied.

## Method

The authors start from light-front wave functions (LFWFs) derived from a Nakanishi-representation Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the pion, using a simple algebraic model with a delta-function Nakanishi weight ρ(ω,z)=δ(ω)(1−z²). They compute the DGLAP-region GPD from the overlap of two-body LFWFs, then obtain the double distribution h(β,α,t) by inverting the Radon transform relation. The ERBL-region GPD is reconstructed from this double distribution, with the remaining D⁺ and D⁻ terms (which only contribute to the ERBL region) fixed by applying the soft-pion theorem at ξ=1, t=0.

## Key numbers

- The model PDF is q(x) = 30 x² (1−x)², obtained from the forward limit of the DGLAP GPD.
- The model mass parameter is M = 318 ± 4 MeV, fixed by matching the pion charge radius rπ = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm.
- The pion form factor expansion gives Fπ(t) = 1 − (4/21)(−t/M²) + O(t²).
- In the large-x limit, the DGLAP GPD behaves as H(x,ξ,t) = 30 (1−x)²/(1−ξ²) [1 − 2(1−x)/(1−ξ²) + O((1−x)²)], with the leading term independent of t.
- The model form factor behaves as 1/t² at large t, whereas the expected pQCD behavior is 1/t.

## Context

This work extends the general Radon-transform-based covariant extension technique developed by Chouika et al. (2017) to a specific pion model based on Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from Mezrag et al. (2014, 2016). It contrasts with earlier approaches such as Hwang et al. (2007) which developed the overlap-plus-extension technique only for a specific algebraic model of LFWFs. The paper shows that the soft-pion theorem from Polyakov (1998) can constrain the D⁺ and D⁻ ambiguities at t=0, and notes that the model's large-t form factor behavior (1/t² instead of 1/t) is understood as arising from the omission of pseudovector components in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, as discussed by Maris (1997) and Qin (2014).
