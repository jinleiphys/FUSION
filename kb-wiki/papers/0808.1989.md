# Charged pions multiplicities at the NA49 energy

> The wounded quark-diquark model predictions for charged pions multiplicities in PbPb and pPb collisions in the central rapidity region at sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV c.m. energy are presented.

# Charged pions multiplicities at the NA49 energy

## Key claim
The wounded quark-diquark model provides predictions for charged pion multiplicities in PbPb and pPb collisions at sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV that are precise at the level of 2–3%, but the model cannot provide separate predictions for pi+ and pi- multiplicities because the pi+/pi- ratio at this energy is strongly influenced by isospin effects.

## Method
The author applies the wounded quark-diquark model, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density with R_Pb=6.62 fm and skin depth d=0.546 fm, and the total inelastic pp cross section sigma_in=31.46 mb measured by NA49. The average number of wounded quarks and diquarks per pp collision is taken as w_p^(q+d)=1.18, with probabilities p_q = w_p^(q+d)/3 and p_d = 2p_q for a quark or diquark to interact in a single pp collision. The midrapidity charged pion density in pp collisions N_pp(0)=1.413 (with 3% uncertainty) is used as the baseline to scale predictions for nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers
- sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV c.m. energy for PbPb, pPb, and pp collisions
- Total inelastic pp cross section sigma_in=31.46 mb (from NA49)
- Nuclear radius R_Pb=6.62 fm, skin depth d=0.546 fm
- Average number of wounded quarks and diquarks per pp collision w_p^(q+d)=1.18 (per colliding proton)
- Midrapidity charged pion density in pp collisions N_pp(0)|_{pi++pi-}=1.413 with 3% uncertainty
- Total systematic uncertainty on NA49 pp data: 2.0% (quadratic sum) and 4.8% (upper limit)
- Model predictions for nuclear collisions are precise at the level of 2–3%

## Context
This work extends the wounded quark-diquark model, previously applied at LHC energies by Bzdak (2008) and collaborators, to the NA49 energy of sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV. The model predictions are compared with the wounded nucleon model (WNM) of Bialas and Czyz (1977). The results enable future tests of the model’s strong prediction that the ratio R_AA/(w/2) is independent of rapidity, once NA49 data for PbPb collisions become available. The analysis relies on precise pp data from the NA49 collaboration (2008) as input.
