# Galilean-Invariant XEFT

> XEFT is a low-energy effective field theory for charm mesons and pions that provides a systematically improvable description of the X(3872) resonance. A Galilean-invariant formulation of XEFT is introduced to exploit the fact that mass is very nearly conserved in the transition D*0 --> D0 pi0. The transitions D*0 --> D0 pi0 and X --> D0 D0-bar pi0 are described explicitly in XEFT. The effects of the decay D*0 --> D0 gamma and of short-distance decay modes of the X(3872), such as J/psi --> pi+ pi-, can be taken into account by using complex on-shell renormalization schemes for the D*0 propagator and for the D*0 D0-bar propagator in which the positions of their complex poles are specified. Galilean-invariant XEFT is used to calculate the D*0 D0-bar scattering length to next-to-leading order. Galilean invariance ensures the cancellation of ultraviolet divergences without the need for truncating an expansion in powers of the ratio of the pion and charm meson masses.

## Key claim

Galilean-invariant XEFT removes the frame dependence and simplifies ultraviolet divergences that plagued the original formulation, enabling calculations to arbitrary order in the ratio of pion to charm meson masses without truncation. The Galilean symmetry exploits the near-conservation of mass in the transition D*0 → D0 π0, where the sum of D0 and π0 masses is only about 3.5% lower than the D*0 mass.

## Method

The author reformulates XEFT as a Galilean-invariant nonrelativistic effective field theory for D*0, D0, and π0 by assigning kinetic masses equal to physical masses (M for D0, m for π0, M+m for D*0) and absorbing mass differences into rest energies. The NLO Lagrangian includes pion interaction vertices with Galilean-covariant derivative operators, contact interactions for D*0 D0-bar scattering, and ∇2 interactions, with complex on-shell renormalization schemes for the D*0 propagator (including the D0 γ decay width) and the D*0 D0-bar propagator. The D*0 D0-bar scattering length is calculated to next-to-leading order in the XEFT power counting.

## Key numbers

- δ_X ≡ (M_* + M) - M_X = 0.11 ± 0.23 MeV (difference between D*0 D0-bar threshold and X(3872) mass)
- δ ≡ M_* - (M + m) ≈ 7.14 ± 0.07 MeV (difference between D*0 mass and sum of D0 and π0 masses)
- Reduced kinetic mass μ for D*0 D0-bar = 965.0 MeV
- Reduced mass μ_π for D0 π0 = 125.87 MeV
- Ratio r = μ_π/μ = 0.1304
- Mean separation ⟨r⟩_X = 6.8^{+∞}_{-3.9} fm for the X(3872) constituents (from binding energy δ_X = 0.11^{+0.23}_{-0.11} MeV)
- Mass ratio m/M = 0.072; square root of mass ratio = 0.27
- Ratio δ/m = 0.051

## Context

This work extends the original XEFT formulation by Fleming, Kusunoki, Mehen, and van Kolck (2007), which lacked Galilean invariance and required truncation of expansions in powers of m/M to control ultraviolet divergences. The Galilean-invariant formulation enables calculations to all orders in m/M and provides a systematic framework for including decay modes outside XEFT's domain (such as D*0 → D0 γ with ~38% branching fraction and X(3872) → J/ψ π+ π-) through complex rest energies. This enables more accurate quantitative predictions for the X(3872) as a near-threshold S-wave bound state of D*0 and D0-bar, and can be extended to charged charm mesons and charged pions.
