# Pre-hydrodynamic evolution and its signatures in final-state heavy-ion observables

> We investigate the effects of pre-hydrodynamic evolution on final-state observables in heavy-ion collisions using state-of-the art event simulations coupled to different pre-hydrodynamic scenarios, which include the recently-developed effective kinetic transport theory evolution model KoMPoST. Flow observables are found to be insensitive to the details of pre-hydrodynamic evolution. The main effect we observe is in the $p_T$ spectra, particularly the mean transverse momentum. However, at least part of this effect is a consequence of the underlying conformal invariance assumption currently present in such approaches, which is known to be violated in the temperature regime probed in heavy-ion collisions. This assumption of early time conformal invariance leads to an artificially large out-of-equilibrium bulk pressure when switching from (conformal) pre-hydrodynamic evolution to hydrodynamics (using the non-conformal QCD equation of state), which in turn increases the transverse momentum. Our study indicates that a consistent treatment of pre-hydrodynamic evolution in heavy-ion collisions requires the use of non-conformal models of early time dynamics.

## Key claim

Flow observables are largely insensitive to the details of pre-hydrodynamic evolution, but the mean transverse momentum is significantly affected; however, at least part of this effect arises from the assumption of conformal invariance in pre-hydrodynamic models, which produces an artificially large out-of-equilibrium bulk pressure when matching to non-conformal hydrodynamics, rather than from genuine pre-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method

The authors simulated Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using a hybrid model chain: TRENTo initial conditions, KoMPoST for pre-equilibrium evolution (either free-streaming or effective kinetic theory with eta/s = 0.16), MUSIC for 2D+1 relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities, iSS for particlization, and UrQMD for the hadronic cascade. Three scenarios were compared: no pre-equilibrium evolution (hydro starting at tau_0 = 0.2 fm), free-streaming evolution from tau_0 = 0.2 fm to tau_hydro = 1.2 fm, and EKT evolution over the same time interval.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.2 fm; hydrodynamization time tau_hydro = 1.2 fm
- Normalization rescaling factors: Scenario B (free-streaming) initial energy multiplied by 0.86, Scenario C (EKT) multiplied by 1.09, relative to Scenario A
- Shear viscosity in EKT scenario: eta/s = 0.16
- Number of degrees of freedom for pure gluon plasma: nu_g = 16
- Freezeout sampling: 5 x 10^5 particles per unit rapidity
- Transverse momentum range for integrated flow: 0.2 < p_T < 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of Bernhard (2018) by testing the sensitivity of final-state observables to different pre-equilibrium models, specifically the recently developed KoMPoST effective kinetic theory framework of Kurkela (2018). The results show that flow observables are robust to pre-equilibrium details, but the mean transverse momentum is affected by the conformal invariance assumption in current pre-hydrodynamic models. This indicates that future work must develop non-conformal models of early-time dynamics to achieve a consistent treatment of heavy-ion collisions, and that PCA-based flow observables can serve as clean probes of hydrodynamic evolution isolated from pre-equilibrium effects.
