# Decay Spectroscopy of $^{129}$Cd

> Excited states of $^{129}$In populated following the $β$-decay of $^{129}$Cd were experimentally studied with the GRIFFIN spectrometer at the ISAC facility of TRIUMF, Canada. A 480-MeV proton beam was impinged on a uranium carbide target and $^{129}$Cd was extracted using the Ion Guide Laser Ion Source (IG-LIS). $β$- and $γ$-rays following the decay of $^{129}$Cd were detected with the GRIFFIN spectrometer comprising the plastic scintillator SCEPTAR and 16 high-purity germanium (HPGe) clover-type detectors. %, along with the $β$-particles were detected with SCEPTAR. From the $β$-$γ$-$γ$ coincidence analysis, 32 new transitions and 7 new excited states were established, expanding the previously known level scheme of $^{129}$In. The $\log ft$ values deduced from the $β$-feeding intensities suggest that some of the high-lying states were populated by the $ν0 g_{7/2} \rightarrow π0 g_{9/2}$ allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transition, which indicates that the allowed GT transition is more dominant in the $^{129}$Cd decay than previously reported. Observation of fragmented Gamow-Teller strengths is consistent with theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transition ν0g7/2 → π0g9/2 is more dominant in the β-decay of 129Cd than previously reported, based on log ft values deduced from β-feeding intensities, though the observation of fragmented GT strengths is consistent with theoretical calculations.

## Method

The β-decay of 129Cd was studied using the GRIFFIN spectrometer at TRIUMF-ISAC, with 129Cd produced by a 480-MeV proton beam on a UCx target and extracted using the Ion Guide Laser Ion Source (IG-LIS). β-γ and β-γ-γ coincidence analysis was performed using the SCEPTAR plastic scintillator array and 16 HPGe clover detectors, with a maximum β-γ correlation time of 800 ns. The level scheme of 129In was constructed from γ-ray transitions identified after subtracting granddaughter 129Sn contributions using a beam-on/beam-off cycling method.

## Key numbers

- 32 new γ-ray transitions and 7 new excited states were established in 129In, expanding the previously known level scheme.
- The absolute photopeak detection efficiency of the GRIFFIN array was 9.4(3)% for a 1-MeV γ-ray and 3.3(2)% for a 4-MeV γ-ray.
- The excitation energy of the 1/2− β-decaying isomer in 129In was determined to be 450.5(8) keV, in agreement with the previously reported value of 451(1) keV from EURICA.
- The 2352.3(6) keV transition has a relative intensity of 0.7(2)% and its sum with the 994.9(5) keV transition gives 3347.2(8) keV, matching the 3347.2(10) keV ground-state transition.
- The ratio of reduced transition probabilities for the 2352.3 keV transition was deduced as B(M3)/B(E2) = 7.5(15)×10^7 [μN²/e²], compared to the Weisskopf single-particle estimate of 27.8 [μN²/e²].

## Context

This work extends the study of 129Cd decay previously reported by Arndt et al. (2005) and EURICA (2015), resolving discrepancies in the level scheme of 129In with higher statistics from the GRIFFIN spectrometer. The expanded level scheme and GT strength fragmentation provide improved input for r-process abundance calculations near the N=82 shell closure, which is responsible for the second abundance peak at A≈130. The results enable more detailed comparisons with shell-model calculations for nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn, building on prior work by Coraggio (2009) and Kartamyshev (2007).
