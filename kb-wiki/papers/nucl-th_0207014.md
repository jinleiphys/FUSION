# Flavor ordering of elliptic flows at high transverse momentum

> Based on the quark coalescence model for the parton-to-hadron phase transition in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, we relate the elliptic flow ($v_2$) of high $\pt$ hadrons to that of high $\pt$ quarks. For high $\pt$ hadrons produced from an isospin symmetric and quark-antiquark symmetric partonic matter, magnitudes of their elliptic flows follow a flavor ordering as $(v_{2,π}=v_{2,N}) > (v_{2,Λ}=v_{2,Σ}) > v_{2,K} > v_{2,Ξ} > (v_{2,ϕ}=v_{2,Ω})$ if strange quarks have a smaller elliptic flow than light quarks. The elliptic flows of high $\pt$ hadrons further follow a simple quark counting rule if strange quarks and light quarks have same high $\pt$ spectrum and coalescence probability.

# Flavor ordering of elliptic flows at high transverse momentum

## Key claim

If strange quarks have a smaller elliptic flow than light quarks at high transverse momentum, then the elliptic flows of high-\(p_T\) hadrons produced from an isospin-symmetric and quark-antiquark-symmetric partonic matter follow a specific flavor ordering: \((v_{2,\pi}=v_{2,N}) > (v_{2,\Lambda}=v_{2,\Sigma}) > v_{2,K} > v_{2,\Xi} > (v_{2,\phi}=v_{2,\Omega})\). This ordering is conditional on the assumption that the partonic matter is isospin symmetric and quark-antiquark symmetric, and that the quark coalescence model correctly describes hadron formation from the partonic phase.

## Method

The authors used the quark coalescence model, in which high-\(p_T\) hadrons are formed predominantly by the coalescence of one high-\(p_T\) parton (from initial hard scatterings) with one or two soft partons (from the thermalized partonic medium). They derived analytic expressions relating the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of high-\(p_T\) hadrons to the \(v_2\) of their constituent quarks, incorporating the capture probabilities \(c_i\) for soft partons and the transverse momentum spectra \(f_i(p_T)\) of high-\(p_T\) partons. The analysis was restricted to SU(3) hadrons composed of \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks and antiquarks, and considered the limits of isospin symmetry and quark-antiquark symmetry to simplify the relations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of probabilities for forming a high-\(p_T\) meson from one high-\(p_T\) parton plus one soft parton versus two equal high-\(p_T\) partons is proportional to \((e p_T / 4 p_0)^n\), where \(n\) is the exponent of the inverse power law for the high-\(p_T\) parton spectrum and \(p_0\) is the momentum scale separating soft and hard spectra (expected to be a few GeV/\(c\)).
- The leading-order parton transverse momentum spectrum from two-parton hard scattering is given by \(d\sigma/d\hat t \propto 1/p_T^4\).
- The \(\bar p/p\) ratio at RHIC is about 0.7, and the \(K^+/K^-\) ratio is close to one.
- In the limit \(r_s = 1\) (same spectrum and capture probability for strange and light quarks), the quark counting rule gives: \(v_{2,K} = (v_{2,q} + v_{2,s})/2\), \(v_{2,\Lambda} = (2v_{2,q} + v_{2,s})/3\), \(v_{2,\Xi} = (v_{2,q} + 2v_{2,s})/3\).
- The observed saturation of hadron elliptic flow at \(p_T > 2\) GeV/\(c\) contradicts hydrodynamical model predictions but is consistent with large parton transport opacity or energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow from low-\(p_T\) (where mass ordering is observed and explained by hydrodynamical models such as Huovinen (2001)) to high-\(p_T\) (where hydrodynamical predictions fail). It builds on the quark coalescence approach previously used in the ALCOR model (Biro, 1994), the MICOR model (Csizmadia, 1998), and the AMPT model with string melting (Lin, 2001) to describe hadron abundances and elliptic flow at RHIC. The predictions enable experimental tests at RHIC and LHC to determine whether a partonic matter is formed and whether the quark coalescence model describes hadron formation; the derived relations among four hadron species are independent of the unknown parton spectra and capture probabilities, making them directly testable.
