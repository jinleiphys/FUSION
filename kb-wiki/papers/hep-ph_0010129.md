# Nucleon Polarizabilities

> The subject of nucleon polarizabilities in real, virtual, and doubly virtual Compton scattering is discussed with respect to what is known and how such quantities can be extracted from data.

## Key claim

Nucleon polarizabilities can be extracted from real, virtual, and doubly virtual Compton scattering data, but the extraction requires careful subtraction of large background contributions (Bethe-Heitler and Born diagrams) and the interpretation depends on dispersion relations and chiral perturbation theory, with the caveat that for virtual Compton scattering the subtraction "constants" become functions of Q² whose form must be assumed.

## Method

The paper reviews the extraction of electric (α_E) and magnetic (β_M) polarizabilities from real Compton scattering (RCS) at low energies, using an effective Hamiltonian expansion in powers of photon energy ω. For spin-dependent polarizabilities, a global analysis of unpolarized Compton data combined with dispersion relations and sum rules (including the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule) is used to extract four spin-polarizabilities (γ_E1, γ_M1, γ_E2, γ_M2) and higher-order polarizabilities. For virtual Compton scattering (VCS), generalized polarizabilities are extracted from electron scattering experiments after subtracting Bethe-Heitler and Born contributions, using multipole expansions that include terms with no RCS analogs.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: α_E^p = (12.1 ± 0.8 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (older SAL/MAMI value)
- Proton magnetic polarizability: β_M^p = (2.1 ∓ 0.8 ∓ 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (older SAL/MAMI value)
- Preliminary new values from TAPS/LARA: α_E^p = (12.24 ± 0.24 ± 0.54) × 10⁻⁴ fm³; β_M^p = (1.57 ∓ 0.24 ∓ 0.54) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Forward spin polarizability combination γ_π = (15.7 ± 2.3 ± 2.8 ± 2.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ from LEGS global analysis (pion pole subtracted)
- Preliminary TAPS value: γ_π = (7.4 ± 2.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Forward spin polarizability γ_0 = -0.75 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ from MAID analysis
- Dispersive evaluation of spin polarizabilities (in 10⁻⁴ fm⁴): γ_E1^p = -4.4, γ_M1^p = 2.9, γ_E2^p = 2.2, γ_M2^p = 0.0
- HBχpt predictions for spin polarizabilities (in 10⁻⁴ fm⁴): γ_E1^p = -1.8, γ_M1^p = 2.9, γ_E2^p = 1.8, γ_M2^p = 0.7
- Generalized polarizabilities from MAMI at q = 0.6 GeV: P_LL - P_TT/ε = 23.7 ± 2.2 ± 0.6 ± 4.3 GeV⁻²; P_LT = -5.0 ± 0.8 ± 1.1 ± 1.4 GeV⁻²

## Context

This review extends the established extraction of electric and magnetic polarizabilities from real Compton scattering (pioneered by experiments at SAL and MAMI) to the newer domains of virtual and doubly virtual Compton scattering. The work connects the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule at Q² = 0 to the Bjorken sum rule at high Q² via QCD operator product expansion and chiral perturbation theory, as argued by Ji and Osborne. Ongoing experiments at Bates (using the OOPS spectrometer at q = 230 MeV) and JLab will test chiral predictions for generalized polarizabilities, while the dispersive analysis by Drechsel et al. provides a bridge between empirical photoproduction data and theoretical predictions from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Hemmert).
