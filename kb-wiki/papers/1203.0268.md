# From nucleons to nuclei to fusion reactions

> Nuclei are prototypes of many-body open quantum systems. Complex aggregates of protons and neutrons that interact through forces arising from quantum chromo-dynamics, nuclei exhibit both bound and unbound states, which can be strongly coupled. In this respect, one of the major challenges for computational nuclear physics, is to provide a unified description of structural and reaction properties of nuclei that is based on the fundamental underlying physics: the constituent nucleons and the realistic interactions among them. This requires a combination of innovative theoretical approaches and high-performance computing. In this contribution, we present one of such promising techniques, the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method, and discuss applications to light nuclei scattering and fusion reactions that power stars and Earth-base fusion facilities.

# From nucleons to nuclei to fusion reactions

## Key claim

The ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) provides a unified description of nuclear structure and reactions for light nuclei, enabling calculations of scattering and fusion processes from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, but the approach is computationally intensive and currently limited to light systems, with three-nucleon force inclusion still in progress.

## Method

The authors extend the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) by incorporating cluster basis states in the resonating-group method (RGM) formalism, where projectile and target nuclei are described as NCSM eigenstates and their relative motion is treated via coupled integral-differential equations. The norm and Hamiltonian kernels, which encode antisymmetrization and inter-cluster interactions, are computed using harmonic-oscillator expansions and second-quantization techniques with Slater-determinant target wave functions, and the resulting equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. The calculations employ similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon potentials, with three-nucleon force terms derived but not yet included in the applications presented.

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian matrix for target nuclei in the NCSM can reach dimensions up to $10^9$, requiring Lanczos algorithm diagonalization.
- The matching radius for the R-matrix method is set at $r_0 = 18$ fm for separating internal and external configuration regions.
- For reactions involving deuterium-nucleus entrance and nucleon-nucleus exit channels, the norm kernel contains two terms (overlap and one-nucleon exchange) and the Hermitized potential kernel contains four separate terms corresponding to six diagrams.
- The three-nucleon force potential kernel for identical $(A-1,1)$ mass partitions contains two additional terms depending on two-body and three-body density matrix elements of the target nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio NCSM approach of Navratil (2000) to treat scattering and reactions, building on the resonating-group method originally proposed by Wheeler (1937). It provides a microscopic alternative to phenomenological approaches such as the continuum shell model of Bennaceur (1999) and the Gamow shell model of Michel (2002), and enables first-principles calculations of fusion reactions relevant to astrophysics (e.g., $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B, $^3$He$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Be) and fusion energy research (e.g., $^3$H$+d \rightarrow ^4$He$+n+\gamma$). The method allows future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and extension to heavier systems, addressing longstanding uncertainties in nuclear reaction data for stellar modeling and fusion experiments.
