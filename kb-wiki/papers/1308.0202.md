# In-medium decay widths of hidden and open charm vector mesons in a field theoretic model for composite hadrons

> We calculate the decay widths of the charmonium states, $J/ψ$, $ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$, to $D\bar D$ pairs, as well as the decay width of $D^* \rightarrow Dπ$, in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter, using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with quark constituents. For this purpose we use the quark antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian written in terms of the constituent quark field operators, and use explicit charmonium, $D$, $\bar {D}$, $D^*$ and $π$ states to evaluate the matrix elements for the charmonium as well as $D^*$ decay amplitudes. The medium modifications of the partial decay widths of charmonium to $D\bar D$ pair, arising from the mass modifications of the $D(\bar D)$ and the charmonium states calculated in a chiral effective model, are also included. The effects of the isospin asymmetry, the strangeness fraction of the hadronic matter on the masses of the charmonium states and $D(\bar D)$ mesons and hence on the decay widths, have also been studied. The decay width of $D^* \rightarrow D π$ in the hadronic matter has also been calculated within the composite quark model in the present work, accounting for the medium modifications of the $D$ and $D^*$ masses. The density modifications of the charmonium states and $D(D^*)$ mesons, which are observed to be appreciable at high densities, will be of relevance in the compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at the future facility of FAIR, GSI, where charmed hadrons will be produced by annihilation of antiprotons on nuclei. The interactions of the charmonium states and $D(D^*)$ with the nuclear medium could lead to the possiblility of the formation of exotic bound states of the nuclei with the (excited) charmonium states as well as with $D(D^*)$ mesons.

## Key claim

The decay widths of charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ(3686), ψ(3770)) to D D̅ pairs and of D* → Dπ in isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic matter are calculated using a field-theoretic model for composite hadrons, where the matrix element is multiplied by a strength parameter fitted from the observed vacuum decay width (e.g., from ψ(3770) → D D̅ or D* → Dπ), and the medium modifications arise solely from the mass changes of the mesons computed in a chiral effective model.

## Method

The authors use the quark-antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian written in terms of constituent quark field operators, constructing explicit wavefunctions for charmonium (1S, 2S, 1D harmonic oscillator states), D, D̅, D*, and π mesons. The S-matrix element for each decay is evaluated at lowest order, with the pair creation strength parameter γ_ψ fitted to reproduce the vacuum decay width of ψ(3770) → D D̅ or D* → Dπ. Medium modifications of the partial decay widths are included by using the in-medium masses of the charmonium states and D(D̅) mesons calculated in a chiral effective model that incorporates scale symmetry breaking via a scalar dilaton field.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium charmonium and D-meson masses using a chiral effective model (Mishra et al., 2013) and the quark pair creation model (³P₀ model) used by Barnes et al. and Friman et al. The present composite hadron model provides an alternative field-theoretic framework for calculating the decay widths, using the Dirac Hamiltonian's pair creation term rather than the ³P₀ assumption. The results are relevant for compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at FAIR, GSI, where charmed hadrons will be produced by antiproton annihilation on nuclei, and may enable studies of exotic bound states of nuclei with charmonium or D(D*) mesons.
