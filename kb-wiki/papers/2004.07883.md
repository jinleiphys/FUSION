# Weak magnetic field corrections to light vector or axial mesons mixings and vector meson dominance

> Weak magnetic field induced corrections to effective coupling constants describing light vector mesons mixings and vector meson dominance (VMD) are derived. The magnetic field must be weak with respect to an effective quark mass $M^*$ such that: $eB_0/{M^*}^2< 1$ or $eB_0/{M^*}^2<<1$.For that, a flavor SU(2) quark-quark interaction due to non perturbative one gluon exchange is considered. By means of methods usually applied to the Nambu Jona Lasinio (NJL) and Global Color Models (GCM), leading light vector/axial mesons couplings to a background electromagnetic field are derived. The corresponding effective coupling constants are resolved in the structureless mesons and longwavelength limits. Some of the resulting coupling constants are redefined such as to become magnetic field induced corrections to vector or axial mesons couplings. Due to the approximated chiral symmetry of the model, light axial mesons mixings induced by the magnetic field are also obtained. Some numerical estimates are presented for the coupling constants and for some of the corresponding momentum dependent vertices. The contributions of the induced VMD and vector mesons mixing couplings for the low momentum pion electromagnetic form factor and for the (off shell) charge symmetry violation potential at the constituent quark level are estimated. The relative overall weak magnetic field-induced anisotropic corrections are of the order of $(eB_0/{M^*}^2)^n$, where $n=2$ or $n=1$ respectively.

## Key claim

Weak magnetic field corrections to light vector meson mixing (rho-omega) and vector meson dominance (VMD) coupling constants are derived under the condition that the magnetic field is weak relative to an effective quark mass M*, specifically eB0/M*^2 < 1 or eB0/M*^2 << 1, with the relative overall anisotropic corrections being of order (eB0/M*^2)^n where n=2 or n=1 respectively.

## Method

The author considers a flavor SU(2) quark-quark interaction from non-perturbative one gluon exchange within a Global Color Model (GCM) framework, using methods similar to those applied in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models. The one-loop background field method is employed, light vector and axial mesons are introduced as auxiliary fields in the structureless mesons limit, and the sea quark determinant is expanded in the limit of large quark effective mass and small electromagnetic background field. The effective coupling constants are resolved in the long-wavelength limit, and momentum-independent VMD couplings that break gauge invariance are eliminated by field redefinitions following the approach of Kroll et al.

## Key numbers

- The experimental rho-omega mixing strength is given by <rho^0|H|omega> = -4520 +/- 600 MeV^2
- The weak magnetic field condition is eB0 approximately 0.5 m_pi^2 approximately 0.1 M*^2 approximately 10^17 G for M* approximately 0.33 GeV
- The ratio of coupling constants is f_r = g_omegaA/g_rhoA = 1/3, which is close to usual fittings of 1/f_r approximately 3.5
- The vector meson mass is taken as M_v^(0) approximately 0.77 GeV, with the effective quark mass M* = 330 MeV in vacuum
- The axial meson-vector meson mass difference from the model is Delta M approximately sqrt(2) x 0.33 = 0.467 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson mixing and VMD (Thomas et al., Kroll et al.) by incorporating weak magnetic field effects, which are relevant for non-central heavy ion collisions and magnetars. The approach builds on the author's prior development of effective field theories for pions, constituent quarks, and vector/axial mesons (Braghin, 2016, 2018). The results enable estimates of magnetic field corrections to the pion electromagnetic form factor and the charge symmetry violation potential at the constituent quark level, and also predict previously uninvestigated axial meson mixings induced by the magnetic field.
