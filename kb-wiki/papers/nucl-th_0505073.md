# Enhancement of nuclear Schiff moments and time reversal violation in atoms due to combination of soft nuclear octupole and quadrupole vibrations

> Nuclear forces violating parity and time reversal invariance P,T-odd produce P,T-odd nuclear moments, for example, the nuclear Schiff moment. In turn, this moment can induce the electric dipole moment (EDM) in the atom. The contribution to the Schiff moment from the soft collective quadrupole and octupole vibrations in spherical nuclei is calculated in the framework of the quasiparticle random phase approximation with separable quadrupole and octupole forces. The values of nuclear Schiff moments predicted for odd 217-221Ra and 217-221Ra isotopes indicate a possibility of enhancement by a factor of 50 or more as compared to the experimentally studied spherical nuclei 199Hg and 129Xe. Since the EDM in very heavy atoms, such as Ra, Rn, and Fr, has an additional enhancement rapidly increasing with nuclear charge $Z$, the EDM enhancement can exceed two orders of magnitude. We discuss the nuclear structure effects causing an enhancement of the Schiff moment.

# Enhancement of nuclear Schiff moments and time reversal violation in atoms due to combination of soft nuclear octupole and quadrupole vibrations

## Key claim
The authors calculate that spherical nuclei with both soft quadrupole and octupole vibrational modes can produce nuclear Schiff moments enhanced by a factor of 50 or more compared to the experimentally studied spherical nuclei 199Hg and 129Xe, but this enhancement depends critically on the existence of close opposite-parity levels of the same spin and on the specific quasiparticle-phonon structure of the nuclear wave functions.

## Method
The authors used the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with separable quadrupole and octupole forces to calculate phonon states in even-even Ra and Rn cores, then solved the secular equation for odd-neutron isotopes 217-221Ra and 217-221Rn to obtain wave functions containing single-quasiparticle and quasiparticle-phonon components. The Schiff moment was computed from the off-diagonal matrix element of the Schiff operator between the ground state and the first opposite-parity state of the same spin, multiplied by the matrix element of the P,T-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction and divided by the energy difference between these states.

## Context
This work extends the idea of Hayes (2000) that soft octupole vibrations could produce Schiff moment enhancement similar to static octupole deformation, which was previously studied by Spevak and Flambaum (1996) and Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak (1996). The authors had earlier shown in Flambaum and Zelevinsky (2003) that soft octupole modes alone give nearly the same enhancement as static deformation. The present paper adds the crucial role of simultaneous quadrupole softness, which allows the ground state to contain a large admixture of the quadrupole phonon coupled to the odd nucleon, enabling the Schiff moment to be generated through the collective quadrupole-octupole phonon matrix element. This mechanism makes isotopes of Ra and Rn promising candidates for future atomic EDM experiments, which are currently in progress at several laboratories.
