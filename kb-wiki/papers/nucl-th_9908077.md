# eta' meson production in proton-proton collisions

> The pp -> pp eta' reaction is investigated within a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. We explore the role of nucleonic and mesonic, as well as the N* resonance currents, in producing eta' mesons. In order to learn more about the production mechanisms, new measurements in the energy region far from the threshold are required.

# η' meson production in proton-proton collisions

## Key claim

The pp → ppη' reaction is investigated within a relativistic meson-exchange model, but the authors conclude that a quantitative determination of the NNη' coupling constant using this reaction is unlikely to be possible until the roles of nucleonic, mesonic, and N* resonance currents are better constrained by new measurements in the energy region far from threshold.

## Method

The transition amplitude for pp → ppη' is calculated in the Distorted Wave Born Approximation using a relativistic meson-exchange model, with the final state pp interaction treated explicitly via a T-matrix generated from a modified Bonn B NN potential. The initial state interaction is accounted for by a reduction factor of 0.33 derived from the ^3P_0 partial wave phase shift and inelasticity at the threshold incident energy of T_lab = 2.404 GeV. The η'-emission current is constructed as a sum of nucleonic, resonance (S_11(1897) and P_11(1986)), and mesonic (ρρη', ωωη', and σηη' exchange) currents, with coupling constants determined from photoproduction analyses, SU(3) symmetry, and radiative decay data.

## Key numbers

- Threshold incident energy: T_lab = 2.404 GeV
- Initial state interaction reduction factor: λ_α = 0.33 for the ^3P_0 partial wave, using δ_(^3P_0) = -50° and η_(^3P_0) = 0.75
- NNη' coupling constant used: g_NNη' = 6.1, derived from SU(3) symmetry with g_NNη = 6.14 and mixing angle θ_P ≈ -9.7°
- Resonance masses and widths: m_S11 = 1.897 GeV, Γ_S11 = 0.396 GeV; m_P11 = 1.986 GeV, Γ_P11 = 0.296 GeV
- Resonance coupling constants: g_NS11η' = 2.9, g_NS11π = 2.4; g_NP11η' = 11.7, g_NP11π = 5.2
- Vector meson coupling constants: g_ρρη' = 4.95, g_ωωη' = 4.90
- σηη' coupling constant (upper estimate): |g_σηη'| ≈ 1.14, from Γ(η' → η + 2π^o) = 42 keV
- Cutoff parameters: Λ_N = 1.2 GeV (nucleonic form factor), Λ_π = 0.8 GeV (pion form factor), Λ_v = 1.45 GeV (vector meson form factor)
- The nucleonic current contribution is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the mesonic current contribution

## Context

This work extends the relativistic meson-exchange approach previously applied to vector meson production in pp collisions (Nakayama et al., prior work) to the η' meson, which is of particular interest due to its strong coupling to gluons via the QCD anomaly and its connection to the nucleon-spin crisis through the Goldberger-Treiman relation. The analysis uses resonance parameters from η' photoproduction analysis (Plotzke et al.) and builds on earlier theoretical studies of η' production (Sibirtsev, Bernard, Gedalin, Baru). The authors find that the resonance current (particularly S_11(1897)) and vector meson exchange currents can both produce cross sections comparable to existing COSY-11 and SPESIII data, but the nucleonic current is too small relative to other contributions to allow a clean determination of g_NNη'. New measurements at higher excess energies are needed to distinguish between competing production mechanisms and to resolve the sign of the σηη' coupling.
