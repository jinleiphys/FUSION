# Collective modes of the order parameter in a triplet superfluid neutron liquid

> The complete spectrum of collective modes of the triplet order parameter in the superfluid neutron matter is examined in the BCS approximation below the pair-breaking threshold. The dispersion equations both for the unitary and nonunitary excitations are derived and solved in the limit of $q\rightarrow0$ by taking into account the anisotropy of the energy gap for the case of $P$-wave pairing. By our analysis, there is only one Goldstone mode which is associated with the broken gauge symmetry. We found no additional Goldstone modes associated with the broken rotational symmetry but found that the oscillations of the total angular momentum are qualitatively similar to the "normal-flapping" mode in the A-phase of superfluid Helium. There are also two collective modes associated with internal vibrations of the structure of the order parameter oscillating with $ω(T=0) =1.20Δ_{0}$ and $ω(T=0) =0.61Δ_{0}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a triplet superfluid neutron liquid with \(^{3}P_{2}\) pairing, there is only one Goldstone mode (associated with broken gauge symmetry) and no additional Goldstone modes from broken rotational symmetry; however, this conclusion is conditional on the BCS approximation and on discarding residual particle-hole interactions, whose Fermi-liquid effects are unknown for dense asymmetric baryon matter.

## Method

The author derives dispersion equations for collective modes of the triplet order parameter in the BCS approximation below the pair-breaking threshold, using the Matsubara finite-temperature formalism and linearizing the nonequilibrium gap equations for small periodic perturbations. The analysis focuses on the \(^{3}P_{2}\) state with magnetic quantum number \(M=0\), taking into account the anisotropy of the energy gap, and solves the dispersion equations in the limit \(q \rightarrow 0\) separately for unitary (vector) and nonunitary (axial-vector) excitations.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, two collective modes associated with internal vibrations of the order parameter structure oscillate at \(\omega(T=0) = 1.20 \Delta_{0}\) and \(\omega(T=0) = 0.61 \Delta_{0}\).
- The undamped collective modes exist only in the frequency range \(0 \leq \omega < 2\Delta\sqrt{1/2}\), where \(\Delta\) is the gap amplitude.
- The Goldstone mode (associated with broken gauge symmetry) has \(\omega_{0}(y) \equiv 0\) at \(q=0\) for the \(M=0\) perturbation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective modes in superfluid neutron matter, which had discussed the anisotropic Goldstone mode (Leinson 2011) and predicted two spin-wave modes in the average-angle approximation (Leinson 2010, 2011). It contrasts with the complete analysis of order-parameter collective modes in superfluid \(^{3}\)He-A by Wolfe (1976), noting that the spin-orbit pairing forces in neutron matter prevent direct application of those results. The findings enable future calculations of thermodynamic properties of neutron star matter at low temperatures, where undamped collective oscillations may dominate over pair-breaking excitations.
