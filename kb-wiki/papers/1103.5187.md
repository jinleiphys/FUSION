# Triangular flow in heavy ion collisions in a multiphase transport model

> We have obtained a new set of parameters in a multiphase transport (AMPT) model that are able to describe both the charged particle multiplicity density and elliptic flow measured in Au+Au collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), although they still give somewhat softer transverse momentum spectra. We then use the model to predict the triangular flow due to fluctuations in the initial collision geometry and study its effect relative to those from other harmonic components of anisotropic flows on the di-hadron azimuthal correlations in both central and mid-central collisions.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a new set of parameters for the AMPT model that simultaneously describe both the charged particle multiplicity density and elliptic flow measured in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, although the transverse momentum spectra remain somewhat softer than experimental data; they then predict the triangular flow from initial-geometry fluctuations and find it has an appreciable effect on the away-side double-peak structure in di-hadron azimuthal correlations.

## Method

The authors use the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model, which converts hadrons from HIJING string fragmentation into valence quarks and antiquarks, evolves them via Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC), and hadronizes them via spatial coalescence followed by hadronic rescattering in the ART model. They adjust the Lund string fragmentation parameters (a, b) and the parton scattering cross section (controlled by αs and screening mass μ) to a new set (set B: a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV⁻², αs=0.33, μ=3.2 fm⁻¹) that yields a parton cross section of about 1.5 mb with a more isotropic angular distribution, compared to the previous set A (10 mb). Anisotropic flows v₂ and v₃ are computed using the two-particle cumulant method, and di-hadron azimuthal correlations are constructed from trigger particles with pT>2.5 GeV/c and associated particles with 1<pT<2 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity density from parameter set B is consistent with BRAHMS data for Au+Au at √sNN=200 GeV across all centrality bins; set A gives values larger than data at all centralities.
- Parton scattering cross section for set B is about 1.5 mb; for set A it is about 10 mb.
- Triangular flow v₃ from set B is smaller than from set A and shows weaker centrality dependence than elliptic flow v₂.
- For b=0 fm (central collisions), the numbers of trigger and associated particles per event are 1.336 and 128.6, respectively; for b=8 fm they are 0.565 and 42.3.
- The specific viscosity ηs/s from set B is about 0.377 at RHIC energy (initial temperature ~378 MeV) and about 0.273 at LHC energy (initial temperature ~468 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier AMPT studies (Lin 2005, Chen 2004) that used larger parton cross sections (set A) and could not simultaneously describe multiplicity and elliptic flow at RHIC. It builds on the recognition that triangular flow from initial-geometry fluctuations (Alver 2010) is important for understanding the away-side double-peak structure in di-hadron correlations (Alver 2010, Xu 2011a). The results are compared with viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Schenke 2011) and transport+hydrodynamics hybrid approaches (Petersen 2010), and the specific viscosity values are much larger than the AdS/CFT lower bound of about 0.08 (Kovtun 2005), raising questions about the constant-specific-viscosity assumption in hydrodynamic models. The work enables future studies that disentangle nonflow contributions from flow fluctuations in di-hadron correlations.
