# Extraction of P11 resonances from pi N data

> We show that two P11 nucleon resonance poles near the pi Delta threshold, obtained in several analyses, are stable against large variations of parameters within a dynamical coupled-channels analysis based on meson-exchange mechanisms. By also performing an analysis based on a model with a bare nucleon state, we find that this two-pole structure is insensitive to the analytic structure of the amplitude in the region below pi N threshold. Our results are M_pole = (1363^{+9}_{-6} -i79^{+3}_{-5}) MeV and (1373^{+12}_{-10} -i114^{+14}_{-9}) MeV. We also demonstrate that the number of poles in the 1.5 GeV < W < 2 GeV region could be more than one, depending on how the structure of the single-energy solution of SAID is fitted. For three-pole solutions, our best estimated result of a pole near N(1710) listed by Particle Data Group is (1829^{+131}_{-65} -i192^{+88}_{-110}) MeV which is close to the results of several previous analyses. Our results indicate the need of more accurate pi N reaction data in the W > 1.6 GeV region for high precision resonance extractions.

## Key claim

The two-pole structure of P11 nucleon resonances near the πΔ threshold (around 1.3 GeV) is stable across large variations of model parameters within a dynamical coupled-channels analysis based on meson-exchange mechanisms, and this structure is insensitive to the analytic structure of the amplitude in the region below πN threshold. However, the number of poles in the 1.5 GeV < W < 2 GeV region could be more than one, depending on how the structure of the single-energy solution of SAID is fitted.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical coupled-channels (EBAC-DCC) analyses of πN scattering data in the P11 partial wave, using meson-exchange mechanisms for non-resonant interactions and including bare N* states. They varied parameters of the meson-exchange interactions and bare N* states much more freely than in previous fits, and also constructed a model with a bare nucleon state (N0) following the approach of Afnan and Pearce, to examine model dependence of extracted resonance poles. Resonance poles were found by analytic continuation of the amplitudes to the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- Two poles near the πΔ threshold: Mpole = (1363+9-6 − i 79+3-5) MeV and (1373+12-10 − i 114+14-9) MeV (averaged values from multiple fits)
- For three-pole solutions, the pole near N(1710) listed by PDG: (1829+131-65 − i 192+88-110) MeV
- The 2N*-4p fit produced an additional pole at MR = 1630 − i45 MeV (from oscillating structure near W ~ 1.6 GeV)
- χ² per data point values ranged from 2.41 (1N*-3p-H fit) to 5.33 (1N*-3p-L fit), compared to 2.94 for SAID-EDS

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels analysis of Kamano et al. (2010) (JLMS fit) and investigates the stability of P11 resonance poles previously found by Cutkosky and Wang (1990), the SAID group, and Döring et al. (Jülich group). The results confirm the two-pole structure near the πΔ threshold reported in those analyses, while also demonstrating that the existence of additional poles in the higher mass region (including N(1710)) depends on how oscillating structures in the SAID single-energy solution are fitted. The findings indicate the need for more accurate πN reaction data above W > 1.6 GeV for high precision resonance extractions, consistent with the conclusions of Ceci, Svarc, and Zauner (2008) regarding the importance of fitting πN → ηN data.
