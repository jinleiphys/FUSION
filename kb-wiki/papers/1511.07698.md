# Leptons from heavy-quark semileptonic decay in pA collisions within the CGC framework

> We study single lepton production from semileptonic decays of heavy flavor hadrons ($D,B\rightarrow~l$) in pp and p$A$ collisions at RHIC and the LHC within the saturation/Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) framework. Using the gluon distribution function obtained with the dipole amplitude, whose energy dependence is described by the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling effect, we compute the transverse-momentum ($p_\perp$) spectra of the lepton yields at mid and forward rapidities. We find that a large fraction of leptons at low $p_\perp$ stems from the saturation regime of the incoming gluons in the target, especially in p$A$ collisions at the LHC. The resultant $p_\perp$ spectra is slightly harder than the data, but the nuclear modification factor seems consistent with the data within some uncertainty. We also update the nuclear modification factors for J/$ψ$ and $D$ meson at the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The authors claim that single lepton production from heavy-flavor semileptonic decays in pp and pA collisions can be described within the Color-Glass-Condensate framework, but the resulting transverse-momentum spectra are slightly harder than the experimental data, and the nuclear modification factor is consistent with data only within some uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark pair production at leading order in the strong coupling constant within the CGC framework, using the dipole amplitude whose rapidity evolution is described by the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling (rcBK). The heavy quarks are fragmented into D and B mesons using Kartvelishvili fragmentation functions, and the lepton production from semileptonic decays is obtained by convoluting the meson production cross-section with a decay distribution function. Two parameter sets for the initial dipole amplitude (MV^gamma and MV^e) are used, and both the k_perp-factorized formula and the hybrid formula (collinear gluon on the proton side) are employed for forward rapidities.

## Key numbers

- The initial saturation scale for the proton is Q_{s0,p}^2 = 0.1597 GeV^2 for the MV^gamma set and Q_{s0,p}^2 = 0.06 GeV^2 for the MV^e set.
- For heavy nuclei, the initial saturation scale is set to Q_{s0,A}^2 = 3 Q_{s0,p}^2, corresponding to c = 0.5 in Q_{s0,A}^2 = c A^{1/3} Q_{s0,p}^2.
- The nuclear radius for Pb is chosen as R_A = 6.24 fm (MV^gamma) or 6.66 fm (MV^e) to enforce R_{pA} = 1 at high p_perp.
- The branching ratios used are Br(c -> l) = 0.103 for D decay and Br(b -> l) = 0.1086 for B decay.
- The fragmentation function parameter is alpha = 3.5 for D mesons and alpha = 13.5 for B mesons.
- The mass of the produced particle X in D decay is M_X = M_K = 0.497 GeV with M_D = 1.86 GeV; in B decay M_X = M_D = 1.86 GeV with M_B = 5.28 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous CGC calculations of open heavy flavor meson production in pp and pA collisions by Fujii et al. (2013) to include the semileptonic decay channel to leptons, which is the only observable for heavy flavor at forward rapidity at RHIC and the LHC. The study uses the rcBK equation constrained by HERA DIS data, similar to the approach in Albacete et al. (2012, 2014), and applies the same framework previously used for J/psi and D meson production. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental lepton spectra and nuclear modification factors from RHIC and LHC, and the authors also update their previous nuclear modification factors for J/psi and D mesons using the revised saturation scale for nuclei.
