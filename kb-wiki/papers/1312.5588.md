# Gluon field fluctuations in nuclear collisions: Multiplicity and eccentricity distributions

> We discuss different sources of fluctuations in nuclear collisions and their realization in the IP-Glasma model. We present results for multiplicity distributions in p+p and p+A collisions and compare eccentricity distributions in A+A collisions to the flow harmonic distributions in 10 centrality classes measured by the ATLAS collaboration.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma model, which includes additional Gaussian fluctuations in gluon numbers per flux tube, improves the description of multiplicity distributions in p+p and p+A collisions but does not fully reproduce the experimental shape; the eccentricity distributions from the model provide a good description of flow harmonic distributions in A+A collisions, except for the large-\(v_n\) tail in centralities above 25% where non-linear hydrodynamic evolution corrections become important.

## Method

The IP-Glasma model combines the IP-Sat model (which determines the saturation scale \(Q_s\) as a function of gluon longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) and transverse position, with parameters fit to HERA deeply inelastic scattering data) with classical Yang-Mills simulations. Nucleon positions are sampled for nuclei, color charge densities are computed from the IP-Sat \(Q_s\) distribution, individual color charges are sampled, and the resulting currents are used to solve the Yang-Mills equations to determine gluon fields before and at the moment of collision. The field energy-momentum tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) is evolved forward in time to obtain gluon multiplicity distributions and spatial energy density distributions, from which eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n\) are computed.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity distribution in p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV: IP-Glasma results with and without Gaussian gluon number fluctuations are compared to CMS charged hadron multiplicity data (Khachatryan 2010).
- Multiplicity distribution in p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV: IP-Glasma results with additional gluon number fluctuations are compared to uncorrected CMS \(N_{\rm track}\) data (CMS 2012, Chatrchyan 2013).
- Eccentricity distributions \(\varepsilon_2\), \(\varepsilon_3\), \(\varepsilon_4\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV are compared to ATLAS flow harmonic \(v_n\) distributions (Aad 2013) across 10 centrality classes (0-5%, 5-10%, 10-15%, 15-20%, 20-25%, 25-30%, 30-35%, 35-40%, 40-45%, 45-50%).

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012a, Schenke 2012b) by incorporating additional fluctuations from varying gluon numbers per flux tube, which were neglected in the first implementation. The eccentricity distributions are compared to ATLAS flow harmonic measurements (Aad 2013) and build on earlier work showing that eccentricities are good predictors of harmonic flow coefficients up to non-linear hydrodynamic evolution corrections (Gale 2012, Niemi 2012). The results enable improved initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations and highlight the need for including non-linear coupling effects (especially for \(v_4\) from \(\varepsilon_2\)) to describe the large-\(v_n\) tail in centralities above 25%.
