# Search for the Efimov state near the 3$α$ threshold

> A high-precision experiment in search of the predicted Efimov state in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ at 7.458 MeV excitation energy was performed. Using a state-of-the-art detector system and novel analysis techniques, it was possible to observe the Efimov state at the predicted energy level above the 3$α$ threshold with much better sensitivity in the $^{12} \mathrm{C}$ excitation energy spectrum compared to the existing data. The mutual $^{8} \mathrm{Be}$ resonance (91.84 keV) condition filters out a total of 21 probable Efimov state events around 7.458 MeV. With 2$σ$ confidence, it gives an upper limit of 0.014$\%$ for the Efimov state $α$-decay width relative to that of the Hoyle state, which is about an order of magnitude smaller than the latest upper limit found in the literature. This observation was supported by a new penetrability calculation assuming a relatively extended structure of the Efimov state compared to the Hoyle state. The effect of the Efimov state was also explored in a nuclear astrophysical scenario, where the triple-$α$ reaction rate, including both the Hoyle state and the Efimov state, was found to be larger than the allowed limit, while the temperature dependence of the combined rate was found to be compatible with the helium shell flash criterion of the AGB stars.

## Key claim

The experiment reports an upper limit for the Efimov state α-decay width relative to the Hoyle state of 0.014% at 2σ confidence, which is about an order of magnitude smaller than the previous best limit; however, the authors caution that this limit is derived from only 21 probable events filtered by the mutual ⁸Be resonance condition, and the result is consistent with a spatially extended Efimov state structure.

## Method

A pulsed ⁵⁷ MeV ¹²C beam was incident on a natural carbon target, and the three α breakup particles from excited ¹²C were detected using two arrays of double-sided silicon strip detectors (DSSD) placed symmetrically at 50° relative to the beam axis. Events were filtered through kinematic gates from Monte-Carlo three-body breakup simulations, timing gates, momentum conservation gates, and energy conservation gates to select kinematically complete 3α events. The ¹²C excitation energy was reconstructed from the measured energies and positions of the three α particles, and events were further filtered by requiring all three α-pair relative energies to lie between 60 keV and 120 keV (the ⁸Be resonance condition).

## Key numbers

- The Efimov state is predicted at an excitation energy of 7.458 MeV, calculated as (2/3) × (0.092 MeV × 3) + 7.274 MeV = 7.458 MeV.
- A total of 21 potential Efimov state events were identified after applying the mutual ⁸Be resonance condition (relative energy range 60 keV to 120 keV).
- The 2σ upper limit for the Efimov state α-decay width relative to the Hoyle state (Γα^ES/Γα^HS) is 0.014%.
- The experiment recorded approximately 2.21 × 10⁵ Hoyle state events.
- Using an alternative analysis method (counts above the simulated Gaussian curve below 7.5 MeV), the 2σ upper limit becomes 0.025%.
- The penetrability ratio of the Efimov state to the Hoyle state matches the experimental upper limit when the inner turning radius ratio (R_N^ES/R_N^HS) equals 3.85.
- The combined triple-α reaction rate through the Hoyle state and Efimov state exceeds the rate calculated by Tsumura et al. (2021) by over six orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for the predicted Efimov state in ¹²C at 7.458 MeV, following theoretical predictions by Zheng et al. (2018, 2020). Previous experimental limits were set by Zhang et al. (2019) at 0.3%, Bishop et al. (2021) at 0.69%, and Cardella et al. (2022) at 0.2%, all with poorer energy resolution than the present study. The improved upper limit enables refined constraints on the triple-α reaction rate, with implications for stellar nucleosynthesis in AGB stars, particularly the helium shell flash criterion and carbon production.
