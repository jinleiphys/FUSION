# Holographic thermalization with radial flow

> Recently, a lot of effort has been put into describing the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma using the gauge/gravity duality. In this context we here present a full numerical solution of the early far-from-equilibrium formation of the plasma, which is expanding radially in the transverse plane and is boost invariant along the collision axis. This can model the early stage of a head-on relativistic heavy ion collision. The resulting momentum distribution quickly reaches local equilibrium, after which they can be evolved using ordinary hydrodynamics. We comment on general implications for these hydrodynamic simulations, both for central and non-central collisions, and including fluctuations in the initial state.

# Holographic thermalization with radial flow

## Key claim

The paper presents a full numerical solution of the early far-from-equilibrium formation of quark-gluon plasma that expands radially in the transverse plane and is boost invariant along the collision axis, finding that the resulting momentum distribution quickly reaches local equilibrium and can then be evolved using ordinary hydrodynamics. The hedge is that the initial bulk metric follows from a simple model (close to vacuum AdS with modified near-boundary coefficients) rather than from a first-principles mapping of the experimental state creation, though preliminary findings suggest the results do not depend strongly on this choice.

## Method

The authors solve Einstein's equations with cosmological constant Lambda = -6 in a 2+1 dimensional gravitational problem using pseudo-spectral evolution, assuming boost-invariance in the longitudinal direction and rotational symmetry in the transverse plane. They specify initial conditions at tau_in approximately 0.12 fm using two models: a Glauber-model energy density profile with approximate radius 6.5 fm for a central collision, and a Gaussian of width 0.5 fm for a fluctuation, both with vanishing initial radial momentum and vanishing longitudinal pressure. The bulk metric functions B and C are taken as in vacuum AdS but with modified b_4 and c_4 coefficients to enforce zero longitudinal pressure initially.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at origin at tau = 0.6 fm is set to epsilon_0 = 187 GeV/fm^3, chosen to reproduce final multiplicities of central heavy-ion collisions at LHC
- Initial time for evolution is tau_in approximately 0.12 fm
- The Glauber-model energy profile has an approximate radius of 6.5 fm
- The fluctuation profile is a Gaussian of width 0.5 fm
- The stress tensor is excellently described by hydrodynamics as soon as tau = 0.35 fm for both the nucleus model and the fluctuation model
- The slope of radial velocity times energy density at the origin at the end of simulation equals 0.66 GeV/fm^4
- A small (3%) regulator energy density was added and checked to not affect results

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of far-from-equilibrium plasma (Chesler 2010, Heller 2011, Heller 2012) that assumed homogeneity in the transverse plane, making it impossible to study radial flow. It differs from the simulation by Bantilan, Gubser and Pretorius (2012) which had both longitudinal and radial expansion but could not maintain boost-invariance and was always in the hydrodynamic regime. The results confirm the fast thermalization found in earlier studies and provide radial velocity profiles that can serve as improved initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, where radial velocity was previously usually taken to be zero.
