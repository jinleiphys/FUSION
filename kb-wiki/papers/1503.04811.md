# Chiral power counting of one- and two-body currents in direct detection of dark matter

> We present a common chiral power-counting scheme for vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar WIMP-nucleon interactions, and derive all one- and two-body currents up to third order in the chiral expansion. Matching our amplitudes to non-relativistic effective field theory, we find that chiral symmetry predicts a hierarchy amongst the non-relativistic operators. Moreover, we identify interaction channels where two-body currents that so far have not been accounted for become relevant.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry of QCD predicts a hierarchy among the non-relativistic operators used in direct-detection dark matter analyses, and two-body currents that have not been accounted for previously become relevant in certain interaction channels, specifically in the AV and VA channels.

## Method

The authors develop a common chiral power-counting scheme for vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar WIMP-nucleon interactions, deriving all one- and two-body currents up to third order in the chiral expansion. They match the resulting amplitudes onto non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) to identify which operators appear at each chiral order and to determine the hierarchy among them.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: Fπ = 92.2 MeV
- Quark mass ratio: ξ = (md − mu)/(md + mu) = 0.36 ± 0.04
- Scalar meson couplings: fuπ = 0.32 ± 0.03, fdπ = 0.68 ± 0.03
- η-meson couplings: fuη = (6.9 ± 0.4) × 10−3, fdη = (14.7 ± 0.4) × 10−3, fsη = 1.05
- Axial charge values at Q² = 5 GeV²: Δup = 0.842 ± 0.012, Δdp = −0.427 ± 0.013, Δsp = −0.085 ± 0.018
- Leading-order axial form factors: G_A³(t) = gA, G_A⁸(t) = (3F − D)/√3 ≡ gA⁸
- Leading-order pseudoscalar form factors: G_P³(t) = −4mN² gA/(t − mπ²), G_P⁸(t) = −4mN² gA⁸/(t − mη²)

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT treatments of scalar two-body currents by Prezeau (2003) and Cirigliano (2012), axial-vector two-body currents by Menendez (2012) and Klos (2013), and vector currents by Pastore (2008) and Kolling (2009, 2011). It combines vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar interactions into a common power-counting framework for the first time, enabling systematic inclusion of two-body currents in direct-detection nuclear structure factor calculations. The results provide the foundation for evaluating nuclear response functions that will allow experimental analyses to account for chiral-symmetry constraints on operator hierarchies and two-body contributions.
