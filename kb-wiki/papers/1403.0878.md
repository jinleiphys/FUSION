# Neutron in a Strong Magnetic Field: Finite Volume Effects

> We investigate the neutron's response to magnetic fields on a torus with the aid of chiral perturbation theory, and expose effects from non-vanishing holonomies. The determination of such effects necessitates non-perturbative treatment of the magnetic field; and, to this end, a strong-field power counting is employed. Using a novel coordinate-space method, we find the neutron propagates in a coordinate-dependent effective potential that we obtain by integrating out charged pions winding around the torus. Knowledge of these finite volume effects will aid in the extraction of neutron properties from lattice QCD computations in external magnetic fields. In particular, we obtain finite volume corrections to the neutron magnetic moment and magnetic polarizability. These quantities have not been computed correctly in the literature. In addition to effects from non-vanishing holonomies, finite volume corrections depend on the magnetic flux quantum through an Aharonov-Bohm effect. We make a number of observations that demonstrate the importance of non-perturbative effects from strong magnetic fields currently employed in lattice QCD calculations. These observations concern neutron physics in both finite and infinite volume.

## Key claim

The paper derives finite volume corrections to the neutron magnetic moment and magnetic polarizability in strong magnetic fields using chiral perturbation theory, but these corrections are complicated by coordinate-dependent holonomy effects and are only applicable in extreme limits; the author cautions lattice practitioners against using previously published formulae that inconsistently treat the lattice size as finite in one part of the calculation and infinite in another.

## Method

The computation uses heavy-nucleon chiral perturbation theory with a strong-field power counting that treats the magnetic field non-perturbatively by summing charged pion couplings via Schwinger's proper-time method, including charged pion Landau levels. The author employs a novel coordinate-space method on a spatial torus of side length L, constructing the finite-volume charged pion propagator from magnetic periodic images of the infinite-volume propagator and integrating out charged pions winding around the torus to obtain a coordinate-dependent effective potential for the neutron. The neutron two-point function is computed at one-loop order, with the static approximation used for nucleon propagators.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field quantization condition on the torus is \( Q B L^2 = 2 \pi N_\Phi \), where \( N_\Phi \) is the magnetic flux quantum (an integer).
- The strong-field power counting assumes \( |e B| / m_\pi^2 \sim 1 \), treating the magnetic field as strong relative to the pion mass.
- The pion decay constant is taken as \( f \approx 130 \) MeV.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \( \Lambda_\chi \sim 2 \sqrt{2} \pi f \).
- The nucleon axial charge is \( g_A \sim 1.25 \).
- The \( p \)-regime constraint requires \( m_\pi L \sim 2 \pi |\vec{n}| \gg 1 \) for non-zero momentum modes.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of finite volume effects in external magnetic fields for lattice QCD, correcting prior inconsistent calculations of the neutron magnetic moment and magnetic polarizability by Hall (2013) that did not account for non-vanishing holonomies or non-perturbative magnetic field effects. The results enable proper extraction of neutron properties from lattice QCD computations in external magnetic fields by providing correct finite volume corrections that account for the Aharonov-Bohm effect and magnetic flux quantization, building on the treatment of uniform magnetic fields on a torus by AlHashimi (2008).
