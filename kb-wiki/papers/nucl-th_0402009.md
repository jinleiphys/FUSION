# Subthreshold antiproton production in pC, dC and $α$C reactions

> Data from KEK on subthreshold antiproton as well as on charged pion and kaon production in pC, dC and $α$C reactions at energies between 3.5 and 12.0 A GeV are described for the first time within a unified approach. We use a model which considers a nuclear reaction as an incoherent sum over collisions of varying numbers of projectile and target nucleons. It samples complete events and thus allows for the simultaneous consideration of all final particles including the decay products of the nuclear residues. The enormous enhancement of the antiproton cross section as well as the moderate increase of meson production in dC and $α$C compared to pC reactions is well reproduced. In our approach, the observed enhancement near the production threshold is mainly due to the contributions from the interactions of few-nucleon groups.

# Subthreshold antiproton production in pC, dC and αC reactions

## Key claim

The observed enormous enhancement of subthreshold antiproton production in deuteron- and alpha-induced reactions compared to proton-induced reactions is primarily due to interactions involving few-nucleon groups (multiple projectile and target nucleons), not simply from the internal Fermi motion of nucleons within the projectile. The ROC model reproduces all measured particle species simultaneously with a single parameter set, contradicting the "first-chance NN collision" interpretation.

## Method

The authors used the Rossendorf collision (ROC) model, a Monte Carlo event generator that treats nuclear reactions as an incoherent sum over collisions of varying numbers of projectile and target nucleons (clusters). The model calculates complete final states including participant interactions (via fireball formation and quark recombination) and spectator decay, using Glauber theory for partial cross sections and Lorentz-invariant phase-space sampling with empirical matrix elements. Fermi motion is implemented via Gaussian momentum distributions from the independent particle model, with no special high-momentum component added.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton cross sections in dC and αC reactions at 3.5 A GeV are nearly two and three orders of magnitude larger, respectively, than in pC reactions at the same energy per nucleon.
- The strange quark suppression factor used in the model is λ_s = 0.15 for the ratio u:d:s = 1:1:λ_s.
- The maximal residue temperature parameter is fixed at Θ_A^max = Θ_B^max = 12 MeV, determined from comparison with 2.1 GeV proton-carbon fragment spectra.
- The width of the Fermi momentum distribution for a residue is given by σ_a = √(aA/5/(A-1)) p_F, where A is the number of spectator nucleons and p_F is the Fermi limit.
- The nucleon density parameter for light nuclei (2 < A < 20) uses f = 1.55 fm with a shell-model-derived form factor.

## Context

This work extends the ROC model previously applied to pC reactions (Komarov, 2004) to dC and αC reactions, providing the first unified description of all projectile types and all measured ejectiles (π±, K±, and antiprotons) from the KEK experiment (Sugaya, 1998). It contradicts the "first-chance NN collision" model of Shor (1990), which could reproduce pC data but severely underestimated dC antiproton cross sections even with high-momentum deuteron wavefunctions. The approach also differs from transport calculations (Cassing, 1994; Li, 1994) and thermodynamical models by treating spectator decay explicitly and requiring no special assumptions about re-absorption or self-energies. The results suggest that cluster-cluster interactions are the key mechanism for subthreshold particle production, applicable beyond light-ion reactions to heavy-ion collisions.
