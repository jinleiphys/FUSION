# Pairing at High Spin

## Key claim

Pair correlations in rotating nuclei are described by the HFB approximation applied to a uniformly rotating system (Cranking model), but the paper argues that nuclei are not truly superfluid because the coherence length of Cooper pairs (approximately 18 fm) is substantially larger than the size of the nucleus, meaning nuclei are "made of the surface layer of a superfluid, so to speak."

## Method

The paper reviews the Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) approximation applied to the Routhian (Hamiltonian in the rotating frame), where the quasiparticle creation operators are generated by the Bogoliubov transformation and the pair field is obtained from a selfconsistency condition. The Cranked Shell Model (CSM) approach of Bengtsson and Frauendorf interprets rotational bands as quasiparticle configurations in a rotating deformed potential with a fixed pair field, using experimental rotational frequency and plotting Routhians and angular momenta as functions of angular frequency relative to a reference vacuum. The transition to the unpaired state is studied by comparing CHFB calculations with experimental rotational spectra across the critical frequency region.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length of Cooper pairs in nuclei: ξ = 18 fm, calculated from gap parameter Δ = 1 MeV, Fermi momentum ħ/p_F = 0.75 fm, and Fermi energy e_F = 37 MeV
- Typical critical frequency estimate for rare earth nuclei: ħω_c = 0.28 MeV, with J_cP = 8.5 ħ and J_cN = 22.6 ħ, using E_N - E_P = 2 MeV, J_P = 30 ħ² MeV⁻¹, J_N = 80 ħ² MeV⁻¹
- AB crossing frequency in quasineutron Routhians: ħω_AB = 0.23 MeV (calculated CSM value)
- Alignment from AB crossing: i_AB = 11 ħ, composed of i_A = 6 ħ and i_B = 5 ħ from i₁₃/₂ quasiparticles
- Reference parameters for ¹⁶⁰Yb: J_0 = 17 ħ² MeV⁻¹ and J_1 = 70 ħ⁴ MeV⁻³
- Reference parameters for ¹⁶¹Yb: J_0 = 19.5 ħ² MeV⁻¹ and J_1 = 64 ħ⁴ MeV⁻³
- CHFB calculations found Δ_n = 0 for ω > ħω_c = 0.22 MeV (¹⁶⁸Yb) and 0.3 MeV (¹⁶²Er) (Banerjee, Mang, and Ring)
- Experimental critical frequency from ¹⁶⁶Yb (+,0) band crossing with (-,1) band: ħω_c = 0.32 MeV
- Mottelson and Valatin first estimate of critical frequency: ħω_c = 0.14 MeV
- Binary interaction matrix elements between quasiparticles: ranging from -300 to 50 keV, increasing linearly with ω (Frauendorf et al.)
- Migdal term contribution to moment of inertia: approximately 15% of the total

## Context

This review extends the work of Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) who first suggested that pair correlations like those causing superconductivity in metals are responsible for the reduction of nuclear moments of inertia, and builds on the Belyaev (1959) expression for moments of inertia and the Migdal (1959) expression incorporating local Galilean invariance. The paper enables systematic classification of rotational bands as multi-quasiparticle configurations using the Cranked Shell Model of Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979), and provides the framework for understanding the rotation-induced transition from paired to unpaired nuclear matter, which has been observed experimentally by Deleplanque et al. and studied in CHFB calculations by Banerjee, Mang, and Ring (1973), Goodman (1976), and others.
