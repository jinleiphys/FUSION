# Spectral Properties of Faddeev Equations in Differential Form

> Three-body Faddeev equations are considered as a spectral problem for nonsymmetrical matrix operator. Invariant subspaces related to physical and spurious solutions to Faddeev equations and its adjoint are described. Respective eigenvectors corresponding to invariant subspaces are constructed for Faddeev operator and its adjoint. Extensions of the formalism on CCA equations are discussed.

## Key claim

The Faddeev operator and its adjoint have coinciding spectra consisting of the physical spectrum of the three-body Hamiltonian and the spurious spectrum of the free Hamiltonian; the physical and spurious eigenfunctions together form a complete and biorthogonal set in the Hilbert space, resolving the question of whether solutions of types other than physical and spurious can exist.

## Method

The paper analyzes the spectral properties of the matrix operator corresponding to the Faddeev equations in differential form, which is non-selfadjoint. Invariant subspaces for the Faddeev operator (the spurious subspace where the sum of components is zero) and its adjoint (the physical subspace where all components are equal) are identified. Spectral representations for the resolvents of the Faddeev operator and its adjoint are derived using the spectral representation of the three-body Hamiltonian, and explicit forms of the physical and spurious eigenfunctions are constructed. The formalism is then extended to the general class of coupled channel array (CCA) equations.

## Key numbers

* The spectrum of the Faddeev operator **H** equals the union of the spectrum of the three-body Hamiltonian \(H\) and the spectrum of the free Hamiltonian \(H_0\): \(\sigma(\mathbf{H}) = \sigma(H) \cup \sigma(H_0)\).
* The spurious subspace of **H** is defined by \(\sum_{\alpha} f_{\alpha} = 0\) and the physical subspace of \(\mathbf{H}^{*}\) by \(g_1 = g_2 = g_3 = g\).
* There are two independent spurious eigenfunctions \(U^k(P)\) for **H** and two independent spurious eigenfunctions \(W^k(P)\) for \(\mathbf{H}^{*}\), labeled by \(k=1,2\), each associated with the free Hamiltonian eigenvalue \(P^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier identification of spurious solutions in Faddeev equations by Friar (year not given) and the construction of spurious solutions for fixed total angular momentum by Pupyshev and collaborators (years not given) and by Roudnev and Yakovlev (year not given). The paper provides a complete spectral classification of the Faddeev operator and its adjoint, proving that no other types of solutions exist beyond the physical and spurious ones. This result enables a rigorous understanding of the completeness of the Faddeev component approach and provides a framework for analyzing the spectrum of any matrix operator arising from the coupled channel array (CCA) method introduced by Levin (year not given).
