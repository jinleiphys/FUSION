# QCD Sum Rules and the Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling

> We present an extension of the QCD sum rule method in the external fields so as to determine the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P, which tests the validity of the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis. This is essentially that we pick out the "higher-order" effects of both the hadron and quark (QCD) sides. A specific QCD sum rules for g_P is obtained and its prediction is briefly analyzed. It turns out that the final prediction on g_P is extremely stable. In view of the versatile nature of the present QCD sum rule methods, we appendix some discussions on the possible future of the method.

## Key claim

The paper extends the QCD sum rule method in external fields to determine the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \(g_P\), which tests the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis; the final prediction is extremely stable with respect to the Borel mass, but relies on an order-of-magnitude estimate for the new condensate susceptibility \(\chi'\) because no reliable method to determine it independently exists.

## Method

The authors embed the nucleon in an external axial field \(Z_\mu(x)\) that includes both a constant term and a term linear in \(x^\nu\) (via \(Z_{\mu\nu}\)), then evaluate the correlation function \(\Pi(p)\) at the quark level using the quark propagator in the presence of gluonic and \(Z\) fields, including terms up to dimension \(D=6\). By comparing the coefficients of the Lorentz structure \(g_{\mu\nu} Z^{\mu\nu}_S \gamma_5\) on the hadronic and quark sides, performing a Borel transform, and combining the resulting sum rule with the known QCD sum rule for \(g_A\) from Henley, Hwang, and Kisslinger (1992), they obtain a sum rule for \(g_P\).

## Key numbers

*   \(g_P \approx (-132.4 \pm 5.7)\), obtained from the sum rule evaluated at Borel mass \(M_B = 1.1\ \text{GeV}\), with the error from varying \(M_B\) by \(\pm 0.1\ \text{GeV}\).
*   The PCAC prediction for \(g_P\) is approximately \(-124\), derived from \(g_P \approx -2 M g_A / m_\pi^2\) using \(g_A = 1.2695 \pm 0.0029\) and \(m_\pi\) as the pion mass.
*   The dominant contribution to the sum rule comes from the \(\chi' a\) term, where \(a = -(2\pi)^2 \langle \bar{q} q \rangle \approx 0.55\ \text{GeV}^3\).
*   The Borel mass is fixed at \(M_B = 1.1\ \text{GeV}\), taken from QCD sum rules for the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method in external fields, previously applied to the axial coupling \(g_A\) by Henley, Hwang, and Kisslinger (1992) and to anomalous magnetic moments by Ioffe and Smilga (1984), to the induced pseudoscalar coupling \(g_P\). The result enables a direct QCD-based test of the PCAC hypothesis and the Goldberger-Treiman relation (Goldberger and Treiman, 1958), and it opens the way to determining other induced couplings and form factors, provided the new condensate parameters (such as \(\chi'\) and \(\kappa'\)) can be constrained by further theory or experiment.
