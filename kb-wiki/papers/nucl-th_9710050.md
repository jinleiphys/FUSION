# Two-nucleon emission in the longitudinal response

> The contribution of the two-nucleon emission in the longitudinal response for inclusive electron scattering reactions is studied. The model adopted to perform the calculations is based upon Correlated Basis Function theory but it considers only first order terms in the correlation function. The proper normalization of the wave function is ensured by considering, in addition to the usually evaluated two-point diagrams, also the three-point diagrams. Results for the 12C nucleus in the quasi-elastic region are presented.

# Two-nucleon emission in the longitudinal response

## Key claim

The contribution of two-nucleon emission to the inclusive (e,e') longitudinal response for 12C in the quasi-elastic region is calculated using a model based on Correlated Basis Function theory truncated to first order in the correlation function, but the results show high sensitivity to the choice of correlation function and the model provides no internal criterion to select among correlations.

## Method

The authors adopt a Correlated Basis Function (CBF) approach truncated to terms containing a single correlation line h(r_ij) = f^2(r_ij) - 1, using a purely scalar correlation function. They evaluate the transition amplitude ξ_if(q) for the charge operator by including both two-point and three-point diagrams (totaling 8 topologically distinct diagrams after symmetry considerations) to ensure proper normalization of the wave function. Calculations are performed for 12C using single-particle wave functions from a Woods-Saxon potential, with four different scalar correlation functions tested: Gaussian, ACA Euler, Nuclear Matter, and OMY.

## Key numbers

- The limit of the transition amplitude ξ_if(q) as q→0 and i→f equals Z/A = 6/12 = 0.5 for 12C.
- Results obtained with the OMY correlation are approximately one order of magnitude larger than those from the other correlations (OMY responses divided by a factor of 10 in the figure).
- The Gaussian and Nuclear Matter correlations produce responses of similar magnitude, consistent with their similar functional forms.
- The model produces 4 two-point diagrams and 12 three-point diagrams, which reduce to 8 topologically distinct diagrams.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of two-nucleon emission in the transverse response induced by meson exchange currents (Co' and Lallena, 1994) to the longitudinal response, where short-range correlations are expected to dominate over MEC contributions. The approach contrasts with prior (e,e'2N) calculations (Giusti et al., 1991; Ryckebusch et al., 1995) that considered only two-point diagrams and thus did not satisfy the normalization condition of Eq. (xilim). The model is validated by comparison with full Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations for ground-state densities (Co' et al., 1995; Arias de Saavedra et al., 1997), and the authors note that a comparison with FHNC responses is a necessary next step, with work in progress.
