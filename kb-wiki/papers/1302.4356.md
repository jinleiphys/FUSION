# Partial dynamical symmetry as a selection criterion for many-body interactions

> We propose the use of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) as a selection criterion for higher-order terms in situations when a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by some states and is strongly broken in others. The procedure is demonstrated in a first systematic classification of many-body interactions with SU(3) PDS that can improve the description of deformed nuclei. As an example, the triaxial features of the nucleus 156Gd are analyzed.

# Partial dynamical symmetry as a selection criterion for many-body interactions

## Key claim
Partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) can serve as a selection criterion for higher-order many-body interactions in situations where a prescribed symmetry is obeyed by some states and is strongly broken in others, but the approach is demonstrated only for SU(3) PDS in the interacting boson model (IBM) and applied to a single nucleus, 156Gd.

## Method
The authors construct SU(3) tensor operators for two- and three-boson creation and annihilation operators, then build Hamiltonians from combinations of these tensors that annihilate specific states (such as ground-band or beta-band members) while allowing mixing in other states. They classify all possible interactions up to third order in the IBM under general, SU(3) dynamical symmetry (DS), and SU(3) PDS scenarios, then apply a specific PDS Hamiltonian to 156Gd by fitting parameters to reproduce the beta-band head energy and moments of inertia, with additional three-body terms tuned to describe gamma-band odd-even staggering.

## Key numbers
- For a single nucleus, the number of independent interactions up to third order reduces from 16 (general) to 3 (SU(3) DS) to 9 (SU(3) PDS)
- In 156Gd, the SU(3) DS Hamiltonian parameters are alpha1 = -7.6 keV, alpha2 = 12.0 keV, alpha3 = 0
- The PDS calculation uses three-body interaction strengths eta2 = -18.1 keV and eta3 = 46.2 keV
- The gamma-band wave functions in the PDS calculation involve 15% SU(3) admixtures into the dominant (2N-4,2) component
- The effective boson charge in the E2 operator is e_b = 0.166 eb with chi = -0.168
- Observed B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) = 0.933 e^2b^2 (with uncertainty 25 in the last digits)

## Context
This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (Leviatan 2011) from two-body interactions to include three-body terms in the IBM, providing a systematic classification of SU(3) PDS interactions. It contrasts with earlier approaches that used only a single cubic term (Heyde 1984, Casten 1985) to describe triaxiality, showing that both eta2 and eta3 terms are necessary for accurate description of odd-even staggering in 156Gd. The method enables non-perturbative treatment of strong symmetry-breaking in deformed nuclei and sets the stage for extending ab-initio and beyond-mean-field methods to heavy nuclei.
