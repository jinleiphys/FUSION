# Coulomb Breakup Mechanism of Neutron-Halo Nuclei in a Time-Dependent Method

> The mechanism of the Coulomb breakup reactions of the nuclei with neutron-halo structure is investigated in detail. A time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the halo neutron is numerically solved by treating the Coulomb field of a target as an external field. The momentum distribution and the post-acceleration effect of the final fragments are discussed in a fully quantum mechanical way to clarify the limitation of the intuitive picture based on the classical mechanics. The theory is applied to the Coulomb breakup reaction of $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb. The breakup mechanism is found to be different between the channels of $j^π=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ and $\frac{3}{2}^{-}$, reflecting the underlying structure of $^{11}$Be. The calculated result reproduces the energy spectrum of the breakup fragments reasonably well, but explains only about a half of the observed longitudinal momentum difference.

## Key claim

The Coulomb breakup mechanism of neutron-halo nuclei depends sensitively on the level structure of excited states, with different behavior in the \(j^\pi = \frac{1}{2}^-\) and \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) channels of \(^{11}\)Be; the time-dependent calculation reproduces the energy spectrum reasonably well but explains only about half of the observed longitudinal momentum difference.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the halo neutron in the projectile rest frame, treating the Coulomb field of the target nucleus as a time-dependent external potential. The wave function was expanded in partial waves and evolved using a split-operator method with the Crank-Nicolson formula, including multipole expansion of the external field up to quadrupole. The internal Hamiltonian included a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction to describe the neutron-\(^{10}\)Be relative motion, and the breakup component was isolated by projecting out all bound states.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Be: 0.503 MeV (used to set the \(1s\) ground state energy)
- Bound excited \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) state energy: \(-0.183\) MeV from neutron threshold
- Incident energy of \(^{11}\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb: 72 MeV/nucleon (\(v/c = 0.37\))
- Impact parameter used for detailed analysis: \(b = 12\) fm
- Calculated \(B(E1)\) strength to the bound \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) level: 0.251 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\) (compared to measured 0.115\(\pm\)0.011 \(e^2\)fm\(^2\))
- Average longitudinal momentum difference from calculation: \(\langle k_{\parallel}\rangle = -0.019\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Average transverse momentum difference from calculation: \(\langle k_{\perp}\rangle = 0.053\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Measured longitudinal momentum difference: about 0.04 fm\(^{-1}\) (close to classical value \(k_c = 0.046\) fm\(^{-1}\))
- For the \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) channel alone: \(\langle k_{\parallel}\rangle = -0.022\) fm\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent calculation (Kido 1995) by including the spin-orbit interaction between the halo neutron and the \(^{10}\)Be core, which was absent in earlier treatments. The study clarifies the limitations of classical intuitive pictures (direct breakup, resonant breakup, free-particle breakup) that had been used to interpret experiments by Ieki (1993) and Nakamura (1994) on \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{11}\)Be Coulomb breakup. The finding that the breakup mechanism differs between the \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) and \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) channels, and that the longitudinal momentum difference remains underestimated by a factor of two, points to the need for more refined treatments of the nuclear structure or reaction dynamics beyond the present model.
