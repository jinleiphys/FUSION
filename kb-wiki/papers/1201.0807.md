# Study of $qqqc\bar{c}$ five quark system with three kinds of quark-quark hyperfine interaction

> The low-lying energy spectra of five quark systems $uudc\bar{c}$ (I=1/2, S=0) and $udsc\bar{c}$ (I=0, S=-1) are investigated with three kinds of schematic interactions: the chromomagnetic interaction, the flavor-spin dependent interaction and the instanton-induced interaction. In all the three models, the lowest five quark state ($uudc\bar{c}$ or $udsc\bar{c}$) has an orbital angular momentum L=0 and the spin-parity $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$; the mass of the lowest $udsc\bar{c}$ state is heavier than the lowest $uudc\bar{c}$ state.

## Key claim

All three hyperfine interaction models (chromomagnetic, flavor-spin, and instanton-induced) predict that the lowest-energy hidden-charm five-quark states \(uudc\bar{c}\) and \(udsc\bar{c}\) have orbital angular momentum \(L=0\) and spin-parity \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\), with the \(udsc\bar{c}\) state always heavier than the \(uudc\bar{c}\) state; however, the specific dominant internal four-quark configuration differs between models, leading to different predicted decay patterns.

## Method

The authors constructed non-relativistic harmonic-oscillator wave functions for the five-quark systems \(uudc\bar{c}\) (isospin \(I=1/2\), strangeness \(S=0\)) and \(udsc\bar{c}\) (\(I=0\), \(S=-1\)) in the constituent quark model, using Jacobi coordinates and \(S_4\) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for color-flavor-spin coupling. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian (kinetic, confinement, and hyperfine terms) separately for three hyperfine interactions: the chromomagnetic (CM) interaction based on one-gluon exchange, the flavor-spin (FS) interaction based on meson exchange, and the instanton-induced (Inst.) interaction based on non-perturbative QCD vacuum structure, including configuration mixing within each spin-parity subspace.

## Key numbers

*   Lowest \(uudc\bar{c}\) state (\(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\)) mass after mixing: 4267 MeV (CM model), 3933 MeV (FS model), 4114 MeV (Inst. model).
*   Lowest \(udsc\bar{c}\) state (\(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\)) mass after mixing: 4273 MeV (CM model), 4084 MeV (FS model), 4209 MeV (Inst. model).
*   Lowest \(uudc\bar{c}\) positive-parity state (\(J^{P}=1/2^{+}\)) mass: 4456 MeV (CM), 4138 MeV (FS), 4396 MeV (Inst.).
*   Lowest \(udsc\bar{c}\) positive-parity state (\(J^{P}=1/2^{+}\)) mass: 4622 MeV (CM), 4291 MeV (FS), 4487 MeV (Inst.).
*   Constituent quark masses used: \(m_u=m_d=340\) MeV, \(m_s=460\) MeV, \(m_c=1652\) MeV; oscillator frequency \(\omega_5=228\) MeV.
*   For the CM model, the \(3/2^{-}\) state of \(uudc\bar{c}\) is about 150 MeV heavier than the corresponding \(1/2^{-}\) state.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of five-quark (\(qqqq\bar{q}\)) systems with light and strange quarks (e.g., Lipkin (1987), Stancu (2000)) to the hidden-charm sector, motivated by predictions of dynamically generated \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}\) and \(\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}\) resonances from coupled-channel unitary approaches (Wu et al., 2010; Wang et al., 2011). The results contrast with the chiral quark model study of \(uudd\bar{c}\) by Helminen (2002), where the lowest state had positive parity; here the heavy charm quark weakens hyperfine splitting, keeping the negative-parity state lowest. The different mass ordering of \(uudc\bar{c}\) vs. \(udsc\bar{c}\) predicted here (penta-quark picture) is opposite to that from meson-baryon bound-state models, offering a test for future experiments at PANDA and JLab 12 GeV.
