# Supernova Inelastic Neutrino-Nucleus Cross Sections from High-Resolution Electron Scattering Experiments and Shell-Model Calculations

> Highly precise data on the magnetic dipole strength distributions from the Darmstadt electron linear accelerator for the nuclei 50Ti, 52Cr and 54Fe are dominated by isovector Gamow-Teller-like contributions and can therefore be translated into inelastic total and differential neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections at supernova neutrino energies. The results agree well with large-scale shell-model calculations, validating this model.

## Key claim

High-precision magnetic dipole (M1) strength distributions from electron scattering on 50Ti, 52Cr, and 54Fe can be translated into inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for supernova neutrino energies, provided that the isoscalar and orbital contributions to the M1 operator are negligible and that corrections are applied for strength outside the experimental energy window.

## Method

The authors used existing high-resolution inelastic electron scattering data from the Darmstadt electron linear accelerator to extract M1 strength distributions for 50Ti, 52Cr, and 54Fe. They converted these M1 strengths into Gamow-Teller (GT0) strengths using the relation B(M1) = 3(g_s^p - g_s^n)^2 μ_N^2 / (16 g_A^2 π) B(GT0), and then computed total and differential neutrino-nucleus cross sections from the GT0 distributions. Large-scale shell-model calculations using the KB3G residual interaction in the complete pf model space (for 50Ti) and truncated spaces (for 52Cr and 54Fe) were performed for comparison, with 400 Lanczos iterations and an effective spin operator s_eff = 0.75 s.

## Key numbers

- Summed experimental B(M1) strength in the energy interval 8.5–11.6 MeV for 50Ti: 4.5(5) μ_N^2, compared to shell-model value of 4.3 μ_N^2
- Summed experimental B(M1) strength in the energy interval 7.0–11.8 MeV for 52Cr: 8.1(5) μ_N^2, compared to shell-model value of 7.6 μ_N^2
- Summed experimental B(M1) strength in the energy interval 7.0–11.8 MeV for 54Fe: 6.6(4) μ_N^2, compared to shell-model value of 8.6 μ_N^2 (or 8.4 with GXPF1 interaction)
- Total shell-model B(M1) strengths: 7.2 μ_N^2 for 50Ti, 8.7 μ_N^2 for 52Cr, and 10.2 μ_N^2 for 54Fe
- Experimental sensitivity limit: B(M1) ≈ 0.04 μ_N^2
- The orbital M1 strength is smaller than the spin M1 strength by about an order of magnitude
- Contributions from multipoles other than GT0 become important for neutrino energies E_ν > 20 MeV and dominate for E_ν > 35 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to relate M1 and GT strength for individual transitions (e.g., in 12C by Haxton (1978) and Donnelly and Peccei (1979)) and total cross sections (e.g., in 208Pb by Fuller, Haxton, and McLaughlin (1999) and Jachowicz, Heyde, and Ryckebusch (2002)). The results validate large-scale shell-model calculations for determining inelastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections on iron-region nuclei, which are needed for supernova simulations that currently lack such data. The validated shell-model approach enables calculation of cross sections for the many nuclei in the medium-mass range present in a supernova environment, including finite-temperature effects and contributions from other multipoles via RPA.
