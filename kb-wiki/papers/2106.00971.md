# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Photon-proton Elastic Scattering and the Electromagnetic Polarizability

> The effect of the self-energy on the photon-proton elastic scattering is investigated for the backward and the forward directions. The shape of the Thomson scattering at the photon energy $ω\to 0$ is broken by taking into account the self-energy of proton. The electromagnetic polarizabilities $\barα\pm\barβ$ are calculated by the lowest-order perturbative treatment.

# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Photon-proton Elastic Scattering and the Electromagnetic Polarizability

## Key claim

The self-energy of the proton, arising from its interaction with virtual pions, breaks the Thomson scattering shape at the photon energy ω → 0 and modifies the electromagnetic polarizabilities; however, the low-energy theorem requires that the joint effect of the real vertex and the photon-photon-proton-proton 4-point vertex must be included to restore the correct low-energy limit, and among the various models classified by the order of the expansion, the n=3 model best preserves the Thomson limit while the n=5 model best reproduces the forward-scattering polarizability sum.

## Method

The photon-proton elastic scattering amplitude was constructed using a non-perturbative self-energy Σ(q) for the nucleon propagator, expressed as a power series in a ≡ γ·q − M and truncated at various orders n = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, and ∞. The differential cross section was evaluated for backward (θ = π) and forward (θ = 0) scattering in the laboratory system, with the vertex modified by the Ward-Takahashi identity to include the anomalous magnetic moment (κ = 1.79). The electric and magnetic polarizabilities ᾱ ± β̄ were extracted from the linear term in the low-energy expansion of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- For the n=3 model, the low-energy limit of the cross section returns to the Thomson form because c₁(M²) = 0 when the pion mass is neglected; including the pion mass shifts ᾱ − β̄ from 6.68 to 6.63 (about 1% change).
- ᾱ − β̄ (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) for models n=2 through ∞: 6.46 (n=2), 6.68 (n=3), 7.15 (n=4), 9.63 (n=5), 7.64 (n=6), 5.98 (n=∞); the experimental value is 7.03 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Zieger).
- ᾱ + β̄ (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) for models n=2 through ∞: −2.67 (n=2), −3.18 (n=3), −3.20 (n=4), −13.2 (n=5), −3.69 (n=6), −2.47 (n=∞); the experimental value is 13.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Olmos).
- For the n=5 model, including the seagull diagram via the low-energy theorem yields ᾱ + β̄ = 13.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, in agreement with the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous non-perturbative approach to the pion-nucleon system (Kinpara) by applying the same self-energy formalism to photon-proton elastic scattering. It contrasts with the Klein-Nishina formula for point-like particles and with the low-energy theorem of Gell-Mann and Goldberger (1954) and the textbook treatment by Bjorken and Drell, which require the Thomson limit to be independent of dynamical structure. The calculation enables a systematic comparison of self-energy models (classified by expansion order n) against experimental polarizability data from Zieger and from Olmos de Leon et al., and suggests that the n=5 model, which includes the photon-pion interaction via the seagull diagram, is the most promising for describing the forward-scattering polarizability sum.
