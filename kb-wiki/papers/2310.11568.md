# The mechanical radius of the proton

> We present the first determination of the proton mechanical radius. The result was obtained by employing a novel theoretical approach that connects experimental data of deeply virtual Compton scattering with the spin = 2 interaction that is characteristic of gravity coupling with matter. We find that the proton mechanical radius is significantly smaller than its charge radius, consistent with the latest Lattice QCD computation.

## Key claim

The proton's mechanical radius, determined from deeply virtual Compton scattering data via the gravitational form factor \(D(t)\), is \(0.634 \pm 0.057\) fm, which is about 25% smaller than the proton's charge radius of \(0.8409 \pm 0.0004\) fm; this result is model-dependent due to the parameterization used for the Compton Form Factors, with systematic uncertainties accounted for in the fit.

## Method

The authors extracted the gravitational form factor \(D(t)\) from experimental data on deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off the proton, using beam-spin asymmetry and unpolarized cross section measurements from CLAS at Jefferson Lab. They employed a parameterization of the Compton Form Factor \(\mathcal{H}(\xi,t)\) based on Kumericki (2016) and used a subtracted dispersion relation at fixed \(t\) to relate the real and imaginary parts, with \(D(t)\) as the subtraction term. The \(D(t)\) form factor was fitted to a multipole form \(D(t) = D [1 + (-t)/M^2]^{-\alpha}\), and the mechanical mean square radius was computed as \(\langle r_p^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{mech}} = 6(\alpha - 1)/M^2\).

## Key numbers

* Proton mechanical radius: \(\sqrt{\langle r_p^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{mech}}} = 0.634 \pm 0.057\) fm, from DVCS data at Jefferson Lab.
* Proton mechanical mean square radius: \(\langle r_p^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{mech}} = 0.402 \pm 0.072\) fm\(^2\).
* Fit parameters: \(D = 1.46 \pm 0.24\), \(M^2 = 1.02 \pm 0.13\) GeV\(^2\), \(\alpha = 2.76 \pm 0.23\).
* Proton charge radius (for comparison): \(\sqrt{\langle r_p^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{charge}}} = 0.8409 \pm 0.0004\) fm, from the 2022 Review of Particle Physics.
* Neutron charge mean square radius: \(\langle r_n^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{charge}} = -0.1161 \pm 0.0022\) fm\(^2\), from the 2022 Review of Particle Physics.

## Context

This work provides the first experimental determination of the proton's mechanical radius, extending theoretical foundations from Kobzarev (1962) and Pagels (1966) and the GPD framework of Ji (1996) and Polyakov (2002). The result is consistent with the latest Lattice QCD computation by Hackett (2023), and it contrasts with the proton's charge radius measured by Hofstadter (1955) and updated in the 2022 Review of Particle Physics. This enables future studies of the gravitational structure of nucleons and other hadrons, with improved precision expected from new DVCS data at 10.6 GeV beam energy from CLAS (2022).
