# From finite nuclei to neutron stars : the essential role of high-order density dependence in effective forces

> A unified description of finite nuclei and equation of state of neutron stars present a major challenge as well as opportunities for understandings of nuclear interactions.Inspired by the Lee-Huang-Yang formula of hard-sphere gases, we developed effective nuclear interactions with an additional high-order density dependent term.The original Skyrme force SLy4 is widely used in studies of neutron stars but is not satisfied for global descriptions of finite nuclei. The refitted SLy4${'}$ force can improve descriptions of finite nuclei but slightly reduces the radius of neutron star of 1.4 solar mass.We found that the extended SLy4 force with a higher-order density dependence can properly describe properties of both finite nuclei and GW170817 binary neutron stars, including the mass-radius relation and the tidal deformability. This demonstrated the essential role of high-order density dependence at ultrahigh densities. Our work provides a unified and predictive model for neutron stars, as well as new insights for the future development of effective interactions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that adding a higher-order density dependent term (inspired by the Lee-Huang-Yang formula for hard-sphere gases) to the standard Skyrme SLy4 force allows a single effective interaction to simultaneously describe both finite nuclei properties and neutron star observables from GW170817, whereas the original SLy4 force describes neutron stars well but fails for finite nuclei, and the refitted SLy4' force improves finite nuclei but softens the equation of state too much.

## Method

The authors extended the standard Skyrme energy density functional by adding a second density dependent term with exponent γ₂=1/2 (keeping γ₁=1/6 from SLy4), then refitted only the momentum-independent parameters t₀, t₃ᵢ, x₀, x₃ᵢ to binding energies and charge radii of groups of light, heavy, and global nuclei. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation for npeμ-equilibrated matter to obtain mass-radius relations and computed tidal deformabilities from the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Global root-mean-square deviation in binding energies for 603 even-even nuclei: SLy4 gives 4.372 MeV, refitted SLy4' gives 2.860 MeV, extended SLy4E_global gives 2.303 MeV (Table I).
- Global root-mean-square deviation in charge radii for 339 nuclei: SLy4 gives 0.028 fm, SLy4E_global gives 0.0244 fm (Table I).
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: SLy4' gives 11.42 km, SLy4 gives 11.67 km, SLy4E_global gives 11.70 km.
- Tidal deformability of 1.4 solar mass neutron star: SLy4 and SLy4E_global results agree with the GW170817 Bayesian estimate Λ₁.₄=190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ at 90% confidence level.
- Maximum masses of all forces are larger than 2.0 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the widely used SLy4 Skyrme parameterization (Chabanat et al., 1998) which is known to describe neutron stars well but has a large global RMS deviation of 4.37 MeV for finite nuclei, in contrast to the UNEDF0 force (Kortelainen et al., 2010) which achieves 1.5 MeV RMS for binding energies but fails for neutron stars. The extended SLy4E_global force provides a unified model that can simultaneously describe finite nuclei and the GW170817 binary neutron star constraints, enabling predictive studies of neutron star structure including superfluidity and non-uniform crusts in future work.
