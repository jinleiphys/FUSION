# A direct probe of the in-medium pn scattering cross section

> Hard photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions is examined as a probe of the in-medium pn scattering cross section within a transport model. Uncertainty of photon production probability pn-pngamma is cancelled out by using the ratio of hard photon spectra R_{12C+12C/p+n}(gamma) from two reactions. The in medium pn scattering cross section is constrained by using the ratio of hard photon production cross sections of proton-induced reactions p+12C and p+2H. A reduction factor sigma_pn^medium/sigma_pn^free of about 0.5 ~ 0.7 around saturation density is obtained by comparing with the existing experimental data.

# A direct probe of the in-medium pn scattering cross section

## Key claim
The ratio of hard photon spectra from heavy-ion reactions (specifically $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C relative to p+n) can constrain the in-medium pn scattering cross section with a reduction factor $\sigma_{pn}^{medium}/\sigma_{pn}^{free}$ of about 0.5 to 0.7 around saturation density, but this result is obtained only by comparing with existing experimental data from proton-induced reactions (p+$^{12}$C and p+$^{2}$H) rather than from the ideal symmetric heavy-ion system.

## Method
The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with an isospin-dependent in-medium reduced nucleon-nucleon cross section and a momentum- and isospin-dependent single nucleon potential. Hard photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung was treated perturbatively, summing over all pn collisions in the reaction history. The uncertainty in the elementary $pn\rightarrow pn\gamma$ probability was cancelled by taking the ratio of hard photon spectra $R_{^{12}C+^{12}C/p+n}(\gamma)$ from two reactions, and the in-medium pn cross section was constrained by comparing the ratio of hard photon production cross sections from p+$^{12}$C and p+$^{2}$H reactions to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Reduction factor $\sigma_{pn}^{medium}/\sigma_{pn}^{free}$ of about 0.5 to 0.7 around saturation density, obtained by comparing BUU calculations with experimental data from p+$^{12}$C and p+$^{2}$H reactions at a beam energy of 140 MeV/nucleon.
- The ratio $R_{^{12}C+^{12}C/p+n}(\gamma)$ shows a sensitivity of about 60% to changes in the pn scattering cross section at a beam energy of 140 MeV/nucleon.
- The single differential probability for photon production from the semiclassical hard sphere model is $p^a_{\gamma}=1.55\times10^{-3}\times\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{\gamma}}(\beta_{i}^{2}+\beta_{f}^{2})$, where $\varepsilon_{\gamma}$ is the photon energy and $\beta_i$, $\beta_f$ are initial and final proton velocities in the pn center-of-mass frame.
- The quantum one boson exchange model gives $p^b_{\gamma}=2.1\times10^{-6}\frac{(1-y^{2})^{\alpha}}{y}$, where $y=\varepsilon_{\gamma}/E_{max}$ and $\alpha=0.7319-0.5898\beta_i$.

## Context
This work extends the use of hard photon production as a probe of the symmetry energy (Yong 2008) to directly constrain the in-medium pn scattering cross section, which is a fundamental input for nuclear equation of state studies and astrophysics (Li 2005, Yong 2011-2014). The approach improves upon hadronic probes (such as nuclear flow and nuclear stopping) that suffer from final-state strong interactions, and the ratio method cancels uncertainties in the elementary $pn\rightarrow pn\gamma$ probability that affected earlier transport model studies (Bertsch 1988, Ni 1990, Cassing 1990). The obtained reduction factor is somewhat larger than predictions from Brueckner approach calculations (Li 1993, Fuchs 2001, Zhang 2007). Future experiments with symmetric $N\sim Z$ heavy-ion systems and p+n collisions are needed to constrain the in-medium pn cross section at different densities and nucleonic momenta.
