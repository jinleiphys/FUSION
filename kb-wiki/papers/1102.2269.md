# $α$ cluster states in $^{44, 46, 52}$Ti

> $α$ decaying states of $^{44, 46, 52}$Ti were investigated with angular correlation functions between $t$ and $α$ with the $^{40, 42, 48}$Ca($^7$Li, $tα$)$^{40, 42, 48}$Ca reactions at E = 26.0 MeV. Many $α$ cluster states were newly observed in the 10 - 15 MeV excitation energy of $^{44}$Ti and their spin-parities were assigned, in which $J^π=7^-$ state was found at 11.95 MeV as a candidate for the member of the $K=0_1^-$ negative parity band. In $^{46}$Ti many $α$ cluster states were also found in the 11 - 17 MeV excitation energy with the $^{42}$Ca($^7$Li, $tα$)$^{42}$Ca reaction, though its strength is weak compared to $^{44}$Ti. No $α$ cluster states were detected for the $^{48}$Ca($^7$Li, $tα$)$^{48}$Ca reaction, in which the number of coincidence events decaying from $^{48}$Ca was very small.

# α cluster states in ⁴⁴,⁴⁶,⁵²Ti

## Key claim

The (⁷Li, tα) angular correlation reaction reveals many new α cluster states in ⁴⁴Ti and ⁴⁶Ti up to 16–17 MeV excitation, but the α cluster strength is progressively suppressed as neutrons fill the 0f₇/₂ shell, becoming undetectable in ⁵²Ti. The 11.95 MeV 7⁻ state in ⁴⁴Ti is a candidate member of the predicted K=0₁⁻ negative parity band, though the spin assignment is not unique because L=4 also improves the fit at large angles.

## Method

The authors bombarded ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴²Ca, and ⁴⁸Ca targets with 26.0 MeV ⁷Li ions from the Kyoto University Pelletron Accelerator and detected tritons at a fixed angle of 7.5° in coincidence with α particles detected at eight angles between +103.2° and +173.2°. Angular correlation functions of the t–α coincidences were fitted with |P_L(cosθ)|² to assign transferred angular momenta L and hence spin-parities of α cluster states in the residual ⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁶Ti, and ⁵²Ti nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Incident ⁷Li beam energy: 26.0 MeV
- Triton detector angle: 7.5°; solid angle 2.0 msr; energy resolution within 70 keV
- In ⁴⁴Ti, a J^π = 7⁻ state was found at 11.95 MeV excitation energy
- In ⁴⁴Ti, uniquely assigned new α cluster states at: 10.70 MeV (4⁺), 11.04 MeV (4⁺), 11.66 MeV (3⁻), 11.95 MeV (7⁻), 12.11 MeV (4⁺), 12.58 MeV (4⁺), 13.44 MeV (5⁻), and 13.97 MeV (3⁻)
- In ⁴⁶Ti, uniquely assigned states at: 12.40 MeV (2⁺), 13.32 MeV (4⁺), 13.49 MeV (3⁻), 14.03 MeV (7⁻), 15.83 MeV (1⁻), 16.34 MeV (5⁻), and 16.55 MeV (3⁻)
- Relative (⁷Li,tα) cross section for ⁴²Ca target decaying to ⁴²Ca(g.s.): 0.056 (5.6%) of the ⁴⁰Ca target value
- Relative cross section for ⁴²Ca target decaying to ⁴²Ca(2₁⁺): 0.084 (8.4%) of the ⁴⁰Ca target value
- Upper limit for ⁴⁸Ca target: ≪ 5.2 × 10⁻⁶ (≪ 0.14% of the ⁴⁰Ca target value)

## Context

This work extends previous α transfer studies on ⁴⁴Ti using (⁶Li,d) reactions by Yamaya et al. (1990, 1996) and Guazzoni et al. (1993), which could not resolve states above 11 MeV, and the (⁶Li,dα) correlation experiment by Artemov et al. (1995) which reported states from 11–16 MeV but did not find negative parity states with J > 5. The present (⁷Li,tα) method, previously applied to ³⁸Ar by Fukada et al. (2005), overcomes the continuous deuteron background problem. The systematic suppression of α strength from ⁴⁴Ti to ⁴⁶Ti to ⁵²Ti quantifies how filling the 0f₇/₂ neutron shell weakens α clustering, analogous to the persistence of α clustering in neutron-rich light nuclei such as ¹⁰Be and ²²Ne studied by Oertzen et al. (1997, 2006) and Kimura (2007). The absence of detectable α cluster states in ⁵²Ti sets an experimental upper bound for future searches with higher-sensitivity techniques.
