# Testing Parameterizations of the Deuteron Quasi-Elastic Peak

> This study introduces common parameterizations of the quasi-elastic peak in the electron scattering spectrum off deuterium and provides the comparison of the parameterized cross sections with published experimental data. The comparison is performed in the wide $Q^{2}$ range from $\sim$0.3 GeV$^{2}$ to $\sim$4 GeV$^{2}$. In this way the performance of the parameterizations and their ability to describe experimental measurements are impartially tested and the conclusion on the description reliability is made.

# Testing Parameterizations of the Deuteron Quasi-Elastic Peak

## Key claim

The Bosted parameterization of the deuteron quasi-elastic peak, in its default implementation using the Paris wave function, systematically overestimates measured integral cross sections under the quasi-elastic peak, with the data description quality decreasing from several percent to almost 20% as Q² grows from 0.3 GeV² to 4 GeV²; the alternative implementation using a scaling function from Bodek (2014) systematically underestimates them, with quality improving from ~15% to a few percent over the same Q² range.

## Method

The authors compared parameterized cross sections from the Bosted parameterization (Bosted, 2007) and peak cross section approximations from Durand (1961) and Kocevar (1967) against two published experimental datasets: six measurements from Hanson (1973) at beam energies 0.5–1.6 GeV and Q² from 0.3–1.8 GeV², and two measurements from Rock (1991) at beam energies 9.8 and 12.6 GeV and Q² of 2.5 and 4 GeV². The comparison was performed by integrating all distributions under the quasi-elastic peak within asymmetrical limits (μ−σ to μ+3σ from Gaussian fits) and computing ratios of experimental to parameterized integrals.

## Key numbers

- The Bosted parameterization (default, Paris wave function) yields σ_exp/σ_par ratios of 0.94 at Q² = 0.273 GeV² (E_beam = 0.449 GeV) and 0.84 at Q² = 1.752 GeV² (E_beam = 1.598 GeV) for the Hanson (1973) dataset.
- For the Rock (1991) dataset, the default Bosted parameterization gives σ_exp/σ_par = 0.83 at Q² = 2.495 GeV² (E_beam = 9.761 GeV) and 0.84 at Q² = 3.989 GeV² (E_beam = 12.589 GeV).
- The alternative Bosted parameterization (scaling function from Bodek, 2014) yields σ_exp/σ_par = 1.14 at Q² = 0.273 GeV² and 1.06 at Q² = 1.752 GeV² for the Hanson (1973) dataset.
- For the Rock (1991) dataset, the alternative parameterization gives σ_exp/σ_par = 1.05 at Q² = 2.495 GeV² and 1.04 at Q² = 3.989 GeV².
- After normalization to the Durand (1961) peak approximation, the default parameterization achieves σ_exp/σ_par,norm = 1.02 at Q² = 0.273 GeV² and 1.03 at Q² = 0.389 GeV² for the Hanson (1973) dataset.
- The Bosted parameterization of the proton elastic peak agrees with experimental data from Goitein (1970), Sill (1992), and Christy (2004) within a few percent over Q² from ~0.3 GeV² to ~5 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the testing of quasi-elastic parameterizations beyond the proton elastic case, where the Bosted parameterization (Bosted, 1994) is known to describe data within a few percent. The study directly compares two implementations of the Bosted parameterization for deuterium (Bosted, 2007) against experimental data from Hanson (1973) and Rock (1991), and evaluates the peak cross section approximations of Durand (1961) and Kocevar (1967). The results indicate that the internal structure of the deuteron is not yet fully understood, and the findings can serve as a reference for deuteron target analyses that use auxiliary extracted quasi-elastic cross sections to verify normalization and electron selection quality.
