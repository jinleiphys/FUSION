# Probing nuclear quadrupole deformation from correlation of elliptic flow and transverse momentum in heavy ion collisions

> In heavy ion collisions, elliptic flow $v_2$ and radial flow, characterized by event-wise average transverse momentum $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$, are related to the shape and size of the overlap region, which are sensitive to the shape of colliding atomic nuclei. The Pearson correlation coefficient between $v_2$ and $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$, $ρ_2$, was found to be particularly sensitive to the quadrupole deformation parameter $β$ that is traditionally measured in low energy experiments. Built on earlier insight that the prolate deformation $β>0$ reduces the $ρ_2$ in ultra-central collisions (UCC), we show that the prolate deformation $β<0$ enhances the value of $ρ_2$. As $β>0$ and $β<0$ are the two extremes of triaxiality, the strength and sign of $v_2^2-[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ correlation can be used to provide valuable information on the triaxiality of the nucleus. Our study provide further arguments for using the hydrodynamic flow as a precision tool to directly image the deformation of the atomic nuclei at extremely short time scale ($<10^{-24}$s).

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Pearson correlation coefficient between elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum, ρ₂, can distinguish between prolate (β>0) and oblate (β<0) nuclear quadrupole deformation in ultra-central collisions, with prolate deformation reducing ρ₂ and oblate deformation enhancing it, thereby providing a probe of nuclear triaxiality.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT transport model (v2.26t5 with string-melting mode and 3.0 mb partonic cross section) to simulate Au+Au and U+U collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with various quadrupole deformation parameters β ranging from -0.28 to 0.4. They calculated the Pearson correlation coefficient ρ₂ between v₂² and event-wise average transverse momentum [pT] using multi-particle cumulant methods, and compared these final-state results with initial-state estimators based on participant nucleon distributions from Monte Carlo Glauber simulations. The analysis focused on ultra-central collisions (0-1% centrality) where volume fluctuation effects are minimized.

## Key numbers

- For U+U collisions, the default deformation parameter is β = 0.28 from the most recent table of nuclear deformations (Moller 2015)
- For Au+Au collisions, the default deformation parameter is β = -0.13
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used were R₀ = 6.81 fm and a = 0.535 fm for U+U, and R₀ = 6.37 fm and a = 0.54 fm for Au+Au
- The average multiplicity in the top 0-5% centrality decreases by approximately 1% for the realistic deformation value of β = 0.28
- For the largest deformation considered (β = 0.4), the nuclear volume ratio is 1 + (3/4π)β² + (√5/28π³/²)cos(3γ)β³ = 1.029 + 0.0006cos(3γ)
- The [pT] variance increases by about 10% in U+U collisions from β = 0 to β = 0.4
- The ρ₂ in ultra-central collisions approximately follows a linear dependence: ρ₂ ≈ b₁ + b₂β

## Context

This work extends earlier insight by Giacalone (2019) that prolate deformation (β>0) reduces ρ₂ in ultra-central collisions, and demonstrates that oblate deformation (β<0) produces the opposite effect. The study builds on prior work showing that quadrupole deformation enhances event-by-event fluctuations of ε₂ and v₂ (Giacalone 2021, Jia 2021), and that the mean square fluctuation of v₂ depends quadratically on β. The results enable future model-data comparisons to constrain nuclear shape parameters at time scales below 10⁻²⁴ seconds, complementing traditional low-energy nuclear structure measurements of β from rotational spectra and electric quadrupole moments.
