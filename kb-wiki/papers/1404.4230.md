# Probing charge correlations of quark gluon plasma by identified two-hadron rapidity correlations in ultra-relativistic AA collisions

> We propose a new kind of two-particle correlation of identified hadrons in longitudinal rapidity space, called $G_{αβ}(y_α,y_β)$, which can reflect clearly the charge correlations of hot quark system produced in AA collisions at LHC energies. It is derived from the basic scenario of quark combination mechanism of hadron production. Like the elliptic flow of identified hadrons at intermediate transverse momentum, this correlation is independent of the absolute hadronic yields but dependent only on the flavor compositions of hadrons, and thus exhibits interesting properties for different kinds of hadron species. We suggest the measurement of this observable in AA collisions at LHC to gain more insights into the charge correlation properties of produced hot quark matter.

# Probing charge correlations of quark gluon plasma by identified two-hadron rapidity correlations in ultra-relativistic AA collisions

## Key claim

The proposed two-hadron correlation function $G_{\alpha\beta}(y_1,y_2)$ can reflect charge correlations of the hot quark system produced in AA collisions at LHC energies, and is independent of the absolute hadronic yields and of the precise form of the hadronization kernel $\mathcal{P}$, depending only on flavor compositions of hadrons and on quark-level correlations, provided the quark combination mechanism (QCM) is the correct description of hadron production.

## Method

The authors derive $G_{\alpha\beta}(y_1,y_2)$ from the quark combination mechanism (QCM) in one-dimensional longitudinal rapidity space, assuming ideal equal-y combination of neighboring quarks (average rapidity interval ~$10^{-3}$ at LHC). They express the hadronic correlation as a linear combination of quark charge correlations $G_{ab}(\Delta y)$ for $u,d,s$ flavors, using a Gaussian parametrization for short-range correlation (SRC) and global charge conservation (GCC) contributions. Three scenarios for the charge correlation matrix $\chi_{ab}$ of the quark system are compared: diagonal (quasi-free quarks), negative off-diagonal (tight quark-antiquark correlations), and positive off-diagonal (tight quark-quark correlations). Decay contamination from short-lived resonances is evaluated for SU(3) ground-state hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Quark rapidity densities used in calculations: $\langle n_u \rangle = \langle n_d \rangle = 710$ and $\langle n_s \rangle = 290$, from fitting pion and kaon data in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (Alice 2013).
- GCC distribution width $\sigma_g = 3.8$, fixed by charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution data (Alice 2013).
- SRC width $\sigma_s = 0.45$, fixed by charge balance function data (Alice 2013).
- For scenario (3) thermal ansatz: hadronization temperature $T = 165$ MeV, quark masses $m_u = 330$ MeV, $m_s = 500$ MeV, giving off-diagonal elements $\chi_{ud} \approx 0.17 \chi_{uu}$ and $\chi_{us} \approx 0.26 \chi_{ss}$.
- $G_{K^+ K^-}$ correlation magnitude decreases by about 25% after decays; $G_{p K^-}$ decreases less than 10%; several correlations involving hyperons and anti-hyperons are almost unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions beyond charged-particle balance functions (Bass 2005, Jeon 2002, Bialas 2004, Bozek 2005, Pratt 2011) to identified hadrons, using the quark combination mechanism developed by the Shandong group (Song 2012, Shao 2011). The proposed $G_{\alpha\beta}$ differs from previous balance functions by having the product of two hadron yields in the denominator rather than either one. The results enable extraction of quark charge correlations $G_{ab}$ from measurable hadronic correlations at LHC, with specific predictions for $p\bar{\Lambda}$, $p\bar{\Xi}^0$, and $p\bar{\Omega}^+$ that can discriminate between different correlation scenarios in the quark system before hadronization.
