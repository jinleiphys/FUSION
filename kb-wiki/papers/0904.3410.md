# Kaonic production of Lambda(1405) off deuteron target in chiral dynamics

> The K^- induced production of Lambda(1405) is investigated in K^- d to pi Sigma n reactions based on coupled-channels chiral dynamics, in order to discuss the resonance position of the Lambda(1405) in the KbarN channel. We find that the K^-d to Lambda(1405)n process favors the production of Lambda(1405) initiated by the KbarN channel. The present approach indicates that the Lambda(1405) resonance position is 1420 MeV rather than 1405 MeV in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectra of K- d to pi Sigma n reactions. This is consistent with an observed spectrum of the K^- d to pi^+ Sigma^- n with 686-844 MeV/c incident K^- by bubble chamber experiments done in the 70's. Our model also reproduces the measured Lambda(1405) production cross section.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the resonance position of the Lambda(1405) appears at 1420 MeV rather than 1405 MeV in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectra of K- d to pi Sigma n reactions, because the production mechanism favors the KbarN channel which couples to the higher-mass pole of the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405). This claim is conditional on the use of the chiral unitary approach with a specific set of subtraction constants and the assumption that the reaction proceeds dominantly through double scattering diagrams with kaon exchange.

## Method

The authors calculate the K- d to pi Sigma n reaction using a chiral unitary approach for the two-body meson-baryon scattering amplitudes, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with s-wave interaction kernels from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian. They consider three diagrams: one direct production (impulse approximation) and two double scattering diagrams with kaon exchange, where the Lambda(1405) is produced via the KbarN channel in the T2 amplitude (KbarN to pi Sigma). The deuteron wavefunction is parametrized by analytic s-wave functions, and the cross section is integrated over the neutron scattering angle and pion solid angle to obtain the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda(1405) resonance position in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectra of K- d to pi Sigma n is found to be 1420 MeV, not 1405 MeV.
- The incident K- momenta used in the bubble chamber experiment were between 686 and 844 MeV/c.
- The subtraction constants used in the chiral unitary approach are: a_KbarN = -1.84, a_piSigma = -2.00, a_piLambda = -1.83, a_etaLambda = -2.25, a_etaSigma = -2.38, a_KXi = -2.67, with renormalization scale mu = 630 MeV.
- The KbarN scattering amplitudes are evaluated at center-of-mass energies from 1600 to 1800 MeV for incident K- momenta 600 to 1000 MeV/c.
- The deuteron binding energy is about 2 MeV.
- The Sigma(1385) resonance has mass 1385 MeV and width 37 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary description of the Lambda(1405) as a dynamically generated resonance with two poles, as established by Jido (2003), where one pole around 1420 MeV couples dominantly to KbarN and another around 1390 MeV couples strongly to pi Sigma. The calculation is compared to bubble chamber data from Braun (1977) for K- d to pi+ Sigma- n at 686-844 MeV/c. The results support the double-pole nature of the Lambda(1405) and enable future studies of Kbar-nucleus interactions by clarifying which resonance pole is excited in KbarN-initiated reactions.
