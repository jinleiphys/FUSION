# Reduced uncertainty of the axial $γZ$-box correction to the proton's weak charge

> We present the fully up-to-date calculation of the $γZ$-box correction which needs to be taken into account to determine the weak mixing angle at low energies from parity-violating electron proton scattering. We make use of neutrino and antineutrino inclusive scattering data to predict the parity-violating structure function $F_3^{γZ}$ by isospin symmetry. Our new analysis confirms previous results for the axial contribution to the $γZ$-box graph, and reduces the uncertainty by a factor of~2. In addition, we note that the presence of parity-violating photon-hadron interactions induces an additional contribution via $F_3^{γγ}$. Using experimental and theoretical constraints on the nucleon anapole moment we are able to estimate the uncertainty associated with this contribution. We point out that future measurements are expected to significantly reduce this latter uncertainty.

## Key claim

The authors present an updated calculation of the axial γZ-box correction to the proton's weak charge that confirms previous results from Blunden (2011) but reduces the uncertainty by a factor of two, achieved by using neutrino and antineutrino inclusive scattering data to constrain the parity-violating structure function F₃^{γZ} via isospin symmetry rather than relying on ad hoc models for multi-hadron intermediate states.

## Method

The authors use a dispersive representation of the axial γZ-box diagram, decomposing the structure function F₃^{γZ} into elastic, Nπ, resonance, Regge, and deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) contributions. They relate the dominant iso-triplet part of F₃^{γZ} to the charged-current structure function F₃^{νp+ν̄p} from inclusive neutrino scattering data via isospin symmetry, fitting a Regge parameterization with vector meson dominance to data from CCFR, BEBC/Gargamelle, and WA25 experiments. The DIS contribution is evaluated using the full O(αₛ⁴) pQCD expression for the first Mellin moment, and an additional contribution from parity-violating photon-hadron interactions (γγ-box) is estimated using constraints on the nucleon anapole moment.

## Key numbers

- The total axial γZ-box correction at E=155 MeV (P2 experiment kinematics) is □_{γZ}^A = (44.6 ± 2.1) × 10⁻⁴, compared to the previous evaluation of 44(4) × 10⁻⁴ from Blunden (2011)
- At E=1.165 GeV (QWeak experiment kinematics), □_{γZ}^A = (39.7 ± 2.2) × 10⁻⁴, compared to the previous 37(4) × 10⁻⁴
- The DIS contribution is 30.4 × 10⁻⁴, with the O(αₛ⁴) result differing from the O(αₛ) result by only about 2%
- The Regge contribution is 6.6(2.1) × 10⁻⁴ at E=155 MeV and 7.0(2.2) × 10⁻⁴ at E=1.165 GeV
- The elastic contribution is 4.7(3) × 10⁻⁴ at E=155 MeV and 0.56(6) × 10⁻⁴ at E=1.165 GeV
- Including the parity-violating γγ-box gives a total correction of (42.3 ± 4.3) × 10⁻⁴ at P2 energies
- The nucleon anapole moment shift is δg_A^{ep} = 0.26(43) from chiral perturbation theory, leading to F_{3,el}^{γγ} = -(0.20±0.34) F_{3,el}^{γZ}

## Context

This work extends the treatment of the axial γZ-box correction originally calculated by Blunden (2011) by replacing ad hoc modeling of multi-hadron intermediate states with a data-driven Regge parameterization fitted to inclusive neutrino scattering data, a technique previously introduced for the axial γW-box in neutron and nuclear beta decay by Seng (2018). The reduced uncertainty enables more precise extraction of the weak mixing angle from upcoming experiments like P2 at MESA, which plans to measure the proton's weak charge to 1.4% at 155 MeV beam energy. The authors note that future measurements of the nucleon anapole moment, planned via dedicated backward angle measurements at P2, could further reduce the uncertainty from parity-violating photon-hadron interactions.
