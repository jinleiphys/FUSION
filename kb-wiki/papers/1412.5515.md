# Charge Symmetry Breaking in Electromagnetic Nucleon Form Factors in Elastic Parity-Violating Electron-Nucleus Scattering

> The effects of charge symmetry breaking in nucleon electromagnetic form factors on parity- violating elastic electron-12C scattering is studied, and found to be much smaller than other known effects. The analysis of a planned experiment is discussed.

# Charge Symmetry Breaking in Electromagnetic Nucleon Form Factors in Elastic Parity-Violating Electron-Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in nucleon electromagnetic form factors produces corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-¹²C scattering that are at least an order of magnitude smaller than corrections from nuclear isospin violation, and generally more than an order of magnitude smaller than corrections from nucleon strangeness, for the momentum transfer range of the planned P2 experiment at Mainz.

## Method

The parity-violating asymmetry for a J^π = 0⁺ target is expressed in terms of the ratio of weak neutral current to electromagnetic nuclear form factors, including corrections from CSB form factors F^{s} and F^{v} (isoscalar and isovector charge-symmetry-breaking form factors), strangeness form factors parameterized by ρ_s and μ_s, and nuclear isospin violation from differences in proton and neutron densities. The CSB form factors are computed using leading-order pion cloud contributions and vector meson (ω-ρ mixing) resonance saturation, while nuclear densities are taken from a density functional calculation by Bulgac et al. The nucleon electromagnetic form factors of Kelly (2004) are used throughout.

## Key numbers

- The reference PV asymmetry for a pure isospin-0 nucleus is A⁰ ≈ 3.22 × 10⁻⁶ × (Q² / fm⁻²), where the coefficient contains 2 sin²θ_W.
- The momentum transfer range of the planned P2 experiment is 0 ≤ Q² ≤ 0.0625 GeV².
- Nuclear isospin violation corrections are about 10⁻³ of the weak nuclear charge at Q² = 0.01 GeV².
- The weak mixing angle used in numerical work is s_W = 0.234.
- The strangeness parameter ρ_s is taken as 0.92 ± 0.58 from Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014).
- The planned experimental relative precision for the weak charge of ¹²C is 0.3%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear isospin mixing by Moreno (2008) and nucleon strangeness effects by Musolf (1994) and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) to include charge symmetry breaking in nucleon electromagnetic form factors, using the formalism developed by Wagman (2014) for CSB in proton form factors. The results are directly relevant to the P2 experiment at Mainz (Gerz, 2013), which aims to measure the weak charge of ¹²C. The paper shows that CSB effects are negligible compared to nuclear isospin violation and strangeness contributions, and that all corrections remain linear in Q² within the experimental kinematic range, enabling a measurement of the weak mixing angle if data are taken at multiple Q² values.
