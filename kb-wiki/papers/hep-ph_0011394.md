# Electroweak Physics for Color Superconductivity

> We construct the effective theories describing the electroweak interactions for the low energy excitations associated with the color superconductive phases of QCD at high matter density. The main result, for the 3 flavor case, is that the quasiparticle Goldstone boson $π^0$ decay into two physical massless photons is identical to the zero density case once we use the new Goldstone decay constant and the modified electric charge $\widetilde{e}=e \cosθ$, with $\tanθ=2e/\sqrt{3}g_s$ and $g_s$ the strong coupling constant. For 2 flavors we find that the coupling of the quarks to the neutral vector boson $Z^0$ is modified with respect to the zero density case. We finally point out possible applications of our result to the physics of compact objects.

## Key claim

For the 3-flavor color-superconducting phase, the decay of the quasiparticle Goldstone boson π⁰ into two physical massless photons proceeds identically to the zero-density case, provided one uses the modified electric charge \(\widetilde{e}=e\cos\theta\) (with \(\tan\theta=2e/\sqrt{3}g_s\)) and the new Goldstone decay constant; this result contradicts the earlier finding of Hatsuda, Ruggieri, and Zhang (2000), who obtained a coefficient differing by a factor of 3.

## Method

The authors constructed effective field theories for the electroweak interactions of low-energy excitations in color-superconducting QCD at high baryon density, using the nonlinear realization framework for Goldstone bosons. For the 3-flavor case, they gauged the Wess-Zumino-Witten term to incorporate QED and the axial anomaly, matching the anomalous variation at the fundamental quark level to fix the topological coefficient. For the 2-flavor case, they generalized the existing low-energy effective Lagrangian to include electroweak gauge fields and identified the modified coupling of quarks to the Z⁰ boson.

## Key numbers

- The modified electric charge in the 3-flavor superconducting phase is \(\widetilde{e}=e\cos\theta\), with \(\tan\theta=2e/\sqrt{3}g_s\) and \(g_s\) the strong coupling constant.
- The Wess-Zumino coefficient is \(C=-i N_c/(240\pi^2)\) with \(N_c=3\), identical to the zero-density value.
- The \(\pi^0\rightarrow\gamma\gamma\) rate in the superconducting phase is augmented by a factor of \(N_c^2\) (i.e., a factor of 9) relative to the result of Hatsuda, Ruggieri, and Zhang (2000).
- In the 2-flavor case, the coupling of quarks to the Z⁰ boson is modified with respect to the zero-density case, and this effect is not suppressed relative to standard weak processes.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy effective theories for color-superconducting QCD developed by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) for 3 flavors and by Casalbuoni, Duan, and Sannino (2000) for 2 flavors, by incorporating electroweak interactions. The result for the π⁰ decay contradicts the earlier calculation of Hatsuda, Ruggieri, and Zhang (2000), who found a different anomalous coefficient. The findings enable phenomenological studies of electroweak processes in compact objects such as quark stars with a color-flavor-locked core, including neutrino emission and cooling via modified neutral-current scattering.
