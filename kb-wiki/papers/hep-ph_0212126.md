# KamLAND and solar neutrino data eliminate the LOW solution

> The KamLAND reactor antineutrino experiment has detected a 3.4σflux suppression relative to the expectation if no neutrino oscillations occur. We combine KamLAND data with solar neutrino data and show that the LMA solution is the only viable oscillation solution to the solar neutrino problem at the 4.4σC. L.

# KamLAND and solar neutrino data eliminate the LOW solution

## Key claim
The combination of KamLAND reactor antineutrino data with solar neutrino data rules out the LOW oscillation solution at the 4.4σ confidence level, leaving the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution as the only viable oscillation explanation for the solar neutrino problem. The abstract's claim of "4.4σ C. L." applies specifically to the exclusion of the LOW solution, not to the overall evidence for oscillations.

## Method
The authors performed a combined χ² analysis of solar neutrino data (from their prior analysis in Barger (2002)) and KamLAND reactor antineutrino data. For KamLAND, they computed the expected event rate from 20 nuclear reactors in Japan and South Korea using two-flavor vacuum oscillations, adopting the fission product spectra from ²³⁵U, ²³⁹Pu, ²³⁸U and ²⁴¹Pu, the inverse neutron β-decay cross-section with nucleon recoil corrections, and the KamLAND detector response function with 7.5%/√E(MeV) energy resolution and a 2.6 MeV threshold. The total χ² was the sum of the solar and KamLAND contributions, with a floating normalization factor α for the KamLAND analysis.

## Key numbers
- KamLAND detected a 3.4σ flux suppression relative to the no-oscillation expectation.
- Best-fit oscillation parameters from KamLAND data alone: Δm² = 7.1 × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan²θ = 0.64, with α = 1.008 and χ² = 5.57.
- The LOW solution from KamLAND data alone gave χ² = 19.89, acceptable only at 3.4σ C. L.
- Combined analysis best-fit: Δm² = 7.1 × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan²θ = 0.42, with α = 0.994 and χ² = 57.08.
- The LOW solution in the combined analysis had χ² = 79.78, excluded at 4.4σ C. L.
- With solar neutrino data alone, the LOW solution was allowed at 99% C. L. (approximately 2.6σ) per Barger (2002).

## Context
This work extends the global analysis of solar neutrino data by Barger (2002) by incorporating the first KamLAND results, which provided the first terrestrial confirmation of neutrino oscillations from reactor antineutrinos. The KamLAND collaboration had already shown that the LOW solution was disfavored at 99.95% C. L. (approximately 3.5σ), but the combined analysis with solar data strengthened the exclusion to 4.4σ. This result definitively resolved the solar neutrino problem in favor of the LMA solution, enabling future precision measurements of oscillation parameters as the next step.
