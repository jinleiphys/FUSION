# Strangeness and Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> The implications of chiral symmetry breaking and SU(3) symmetry breaking have been studied in the chiral constituent quark model ($χ$CQM). The role of hidden strangeness component has been investigated for the scalar matrix elements of the nucleon with an emphasis on the meson-nucleon sigma terms. The $χ$CQM is able to give a qualitative and quantitative description of the "quark sea" generation through chiral symmetry breaking. The significant contribution of the strangeness is consistent with the recent available experimental observations.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) can provide a qualitative and quantitative description of the quark sea generation through chiral symmetry breaking, but the numerical results for strangeness-dependent quantities such as the strangeness content \(y_N\) and strangeness fraction \(f_s\) are highly sensitive to whether SU(3) symmetry breaking is included; without SU(3) symmetry breaking, these values become unrealistically large (5-6 times higher).

## Method

The authors used the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), in which constituent quarks fluctuate into a Goldstone boson plus a recoil quark, generating a quark-antiquark sea. They calculated the scalar matrix elements \(\langle N | \bar{q} q | N \rangle\) for the nucleon by incorporating the transition probabilities for Goldstone boson emission, including SU(3) symmetry breaking parameters \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(\zeta\) that account for the heavier strange quark mass and the mass hierarchy of the pseudoscalar meson nonet. From these matrix elements, they derived the strangeness content \(y_N\), strangeness fraction \(f_s\), and the meson-nucleon sigma terms \(\sigma_{\pi N}\), \(\sigma_{KN}\), and \(\sigma_{\eta N}\) using the quark mass ratio \(m_s/\hat{m} = 22\).

## Key numbers

- Strangeness content in the nucleon: \(y_N = 0.044\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the phenomenological value \(0.11 \pm 0.07\) from Gasser (1991) and the NQM value of 0.0.
- Strangeness fraction of the nucleon: \(f_s = 0.042\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the phenomenological value \(0.10 \pm 0.06\) from the NuTeV experiment and the NQM value of 0.0.
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 31.32\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the NQM value of 28.57 MeV.
- Kaon-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_{KN} = 195.90\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the NQM value of 164.29 MeV.
- Eta-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_{\eta N} = 30.60\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the NQM value of 9.52 MeV.
- Strangeness sigma term: \(\sigma_s = 15.12\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (χCQM with SU(3) symmetry breaking), compared to the NQM value of 0.
- Under SU(3) symmetric assumptions (\(\alpha=\beta=-\zeta=1\)), the values become \(y_N = 0.26\) and \(f_s = 0.21\), which are approximately 5-6 times larger than the symmetry-breaking case.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model previously applied by Cheng (1995) and the authors' own earlier studies of spin polarization, magnetic moments, and Gottfried sum rule violation, to the scalar matrix elements and meson-nucleon sigma terms. The results address the long-standing discrepancy between sigma term values extracted from pion-nucleon scattering experiments (Koch, 1982; Gasser, 1991; Pavan, 2002) and those from hadron spectroscopy (Riazuddin, 2003; Cheng, 1997). The non-zero strangeness content found here supports the violation of the OZI rule in the nucleon and provides predictions for future DA\(\Phi\)NE experiments that will measure kaon-nucleon sigma terms, which could further constrain the model parameters and improve understanding of the strangeness content in the nucleon.
