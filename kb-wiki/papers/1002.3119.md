# The quark-gluon-plasma phase transition diagram, Hagedorn matter and quark-gluon liquid

> In order to study the nuclear matter in the relativistic heavy ion collisions and the compact stars, we need the hadronic density of states for the entire ($μ_B-T$) phase transition diagram. We present a model for the continuous high-lying mass (and volume) spectrum density of states that fits the Hagedorn mass spectrum. This model explains the origin of the tri-critical point besides various phenomena such as the quarkyonic matter and the quark-gluon liquid. The Hagedorn mass spectrum is derived for the color-singlet quark-gluon bag with various internal structures such as the unimodular unitary, orthogonal and color-flavor locked symplectic symmetry groups. The continuous high-lying hadronic mass spectrum is populated at first by the unitary Hagedorn states. Then the spectrum turns to be dominated by the colorless orthogonal states as the dilute system is heated up. Subsequently, the liquid/gas of orthogonal Hagedorn states undergoes higher order deconfinement phase transition to quark-gluon plasma. Under the deconfinement phase transition process, the color-singlet states is broken badly to form the colored $SU(N_c)$ symmetry group. On the other hand, when the hadronic matter is compressed to larger $μ_{B}$ and heated up, the colorless unitary states undergoes first order phase transition to explosive quark-gluon plasma. The tri-critical point emerges as a change in the characteristic behaviour of the matter and as an intersection among various phases with different internal symmetries. When the saturated hadronic matter is cooled down and compressed to higher density, it turns to be dominated by the colorless symplectic states. This matter exhibits the first order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma when it is heated up to higher temperature. The role of chiral phase transition is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the QCD phase diagram contains multiple intermediate hadronic phases dominated by Hagedorn states with different internal color symmetries (unitary, orthogonal, symplectic), and that the tri-critical point emerges as an intersection among these phases with different internal symmetries, rather than being a single deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors derive the continuous high-lying hadronic mass spectrum (Hagedorn spectrum) for color-singlet quark-gluon bags with unimodular unitary, orthogonal, and color-flavor locked symplectic symmetry groups using the canonical ensemble construction. They analyze how the Vandermonde effective potential from the symmetry group Haar measure regulates differently under extreme hot and dense conditions, leading to Gross-Witten phase transitions between different symmetry-dominated hadronic phases. The grand canonical partition function is evaluated for each symmetry group to determine the order of deconfinement phase transitions to colored SU(N_c) quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- The exponent alpha in the mass spectral density rho(m) ~ c m^{-alpha} e^{b m} determines the order of deconfinement: for alpha <= 5/2 the transition is smooth cross-over; for alpha > 7/2 the system undergoes first order deconfinement phase transition; for 5/2 < alpha <= 7/2 the system undergoes higher order deconfinement phase transition
- The non-strange discrete mass spectrum consists of 76 mesons and 64 baryons
- The unitary ensemble U(N) has N_fun = N fundamental eigenvalues and N_adj = N^2 parameters, with (N^2 - N) redundant parameters integrated out
- The orthogonal ensemble has N_fun = N fundamental eigenvalues and N_adj = (1/2)N(N+1) adjoint parameters, with (1/2)N(N-1) redundant parameters
- The symplectic ensemble has N_fun = N eigenvalues and N_adj = N(2N-1), with 2N(N-1) redundant parameters
- The symmetry decomposition from U(N) to orthogonal O_(S)(N) leads to N(N-1)/2 Goldstone bosons emerging as glueballs

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn bootstrap model (Hagedorn 1965, Frautschi 1971) and the color-singlet state canonical ensemble approach (Kapusta 1981, 1982) to include multiple internal symmetry groups. It connects to the Polyakov loop approach (Dumitru 2003, 2004, 2005) where the Vandermonde potential determines the effective potential, and to random matrix model studies of QCD phase transitions (Klein 2003, Kogut 2000, Halasz 1998). The paper enables a framework for understanding the tri-critical point in relation to quarkyonic matter (McLerran 2007) and provides a theoretical basis for interpreting experimental observations from RHIC and future heavy-ion collision experiments, as well as applications to compact star physics.
