# Relativistic third-order dissipative fluid dynamics from kinetic theory

> We present the derivation of a novel third-order hydrodynamic evolution equation for shear stress tensor from kinetic theory. Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation for the collision term is solved iteratively using Chapman-Enskog like expansion to obtain the nonequilibrium phase-space distribution function. Subsequently, the evolution equation for shear stress tensor is derived from its kinetic definition up-to third-order in gradients. We quantify the significance of the new derivation within one-dimensional scaling expansion and demonstrate that the results obtained using third-order viscous equations derived here provides a very good approximation to the exact solution of Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation. We also show that the time evolution of pressure anisotropy obtained using our equations is in better agreement with transport results when compared with an existing third-order calculation based on the second-law of thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a novel third-order hydrodynamic evolution equation for the shear stress tensor from kinetic theory using an iterative Chapman-Enskog-like solution of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation, without resorting to Grad's 14-moment approximation. The derivation claims that within one-dimensional scaling expansion, the third-order equations provide a very good approximation to the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation and show better agreement with transport results (BAMPS) than an existing third-order calculation based on the second-law of thermodynamics.

## Method

The Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation for the collision term is solved iteratively using a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion to obtain the nonequilibrium phase-space distribution function order-by-order in gradients. The evolution equation for the shear stress tensor is then derived directly from its kinetic definition, keeping terms up to third-order in gradients. For the one-dimensional Bjorken scaling expansion, the evolution equations for energy density and shear pressure are reduced to a simplified form, and the transport coefficients are computed explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used in numerical comparisons: η/s = 3/4π (approximately 0.24)
- Initial temperature for Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider conditions: T₀ = 300 MeV at initial time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c
- Initial temperature for Large Hadron Collider conditions: T₀ = 500 MeV at initial time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- Transport coefficients from the present work for Bjorken expansion: τ_π = η/β_π, β_π = 4P/5, λ = 38/21, χ = 72/245
- Transport coefficients from the entropy method (El 2009): τ_π' = η/β_π', β_π' = 2P/3, λ' = 4/3, χ' = 3/4

## Context

This work extends the second-order Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1979) and the second-order derivation by Jaiswal (2013) that avoided the 14-moment approximation, by incorporating third-order gradient corrections. It improves upon an existing third-order calculation by El (2009) that was derived from entropy considerations (second-law of thermodynamics), showing that the entropy method fails to capture all terms even at third-order. The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for large shear viscosities where second-order theory shows disagreement with transport results.
