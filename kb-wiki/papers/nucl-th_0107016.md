# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of Ge76, Se82, Mo100 and Xe136 to excited 0^+ states

> The neutrinoless double beta decay transition to the first excited 0^+ collective final state is examined for A=76, 82, 100 and 136 nuclei by assuming light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms as well as the trilinear R-parity violating contributions. Realistic calculations of nuclear matrix elements have been performed within the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation. Transitions to the first excited two-quadrupole phonon 0^+ state are described within a boson expansion formalism and alternatively by using the operator recoupling method. We present the sensitivity parameters to different lepton number violating signals, which can be used in planning the neutrinoless double beta decay experiments. The half-life of neutrinoless double beta decay to the first excited state 0^+_1 is by a factor of 10 to 100 larger than that of the transition to the ground state.

# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of Ge76, Se82, Mo100 and Xe136 to excited 0+ states

## Key claim

The half-life of neutrinoless double beta decay to the first excited 0+ state is larger than that of the ground-state transition by a factor of 10 to 100, but the reduced Q-value may be compensated by lower background from coincidence detection of beta particles with gamma rays from the excited final state, making the sensitivity potentially comparable depending on the ratio of nuclear matrix elements.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay to the first excited two-quadrupole phonon 0+ state using the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA), which includes Pauli exclusion principle corrections. The transition to the excited state was described using a boson expansion formalism and alternatively by an operator recoupling method, considering light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms as well as trilinear R-parity violating contributions from gluino exchange.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of 0νββ-decay to the first excited 0+ state is larger than the ground-state transition by a factor of 10 to 100
- Experimental energies of the first excited 0+ state relative to ground state: 1.122 MeV for A=76, 1.488 MeV for A=82, 1.130 MeV for A=100, 1.579 MeV for A=136
- Experimental energies of the first excited 2+ state relative to ground state: 0.559 MeV for A=76, 0.777 MeV for A=82, 0.540 MeV for A=100, 0.818 MeV for A=136
- The effective Majorana neutrino mass <m_nu> is estimated from neutrino oscillation data to lie within the range 10^-3 eV to 1 eV
- Current generation experiments achieve half-life sensitivities of 10^24 to 10^25 years, corresponding to <m_nu> approximately 0.5 to 1 eV
- The GENIUS experiment is expected to probe <m_nu> down to 10^-2 eV using several tons of enriched Ge76
- The CUORE and NEMO 3 experiments aim for sensitivity to <m_nu> on the order of 0.1 eV
- The mean nuclear radius is taken as R = r0 A^(1/3) with r0 = 1.1 fm
- The axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.25
- The mass scale of the nucleon formfactor m_A = 850 MeV
- Structure coefficients for the R-parity violating pion-exchange mechanisms: alpha^(1pi) = -4.4 x 10^-2, alpha^(2pi) = 2 x 10^-1

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Suhonen (1999) for A=76 and 82 nuclei within a higher QRPA framework, which found that transitions to excited collective 0+ final states are reduced compared to ground-state decay. The present study includes momentum-dependent induced nucleon currents that were ignored in that prior work, and applies the RQRPA approach to four isotope systems (Ge76, Se82, Mo100, Xe136). The results provide sensitivity parameters for different lepton number violating signals that can be used in planning future experiments such as GENIUS, CUORE, and NEMO 3, and may help determine whether transitions to excited states offer experimental advantages through coincidence detection of gamma rays.
