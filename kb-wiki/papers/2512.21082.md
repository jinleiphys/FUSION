# Electron spectral shape of the third-forbidden $β$-decay of $^{87}$Rb measured using a Rb$_2$ZrCl$_6$ crystal scintillator

> In recent years, interest in experimental studies of $β$-decay electron spectra -- often referred to as $β$ spectra -- has been growing. This is particularly true for $β$ transitions where the electron spectra are sensitive to the effective value of the weak axial coupling, $g_{\rm A}$. Such measurements serve as important benchmarks for nuclear physics calculations and can also be used to characterize background in astroparticle physics experiments. In this work, a dedicated experiment has been carried out to investigate the spectral shape of the third-forbidden $^{87}$Rb $β$-decays, with the goal of estimating the effective $g_{\rm A}$ value for this transition and of deriving the T$_{1/2}$ value. This was done by comparing the experimental spectral shape with the estimates from various phenomenological models. The $^{87}$Rb source was embedded directly within the detector material of a new Rb$_2$ZrCl$_6$ crystal scintillator; the data taking was performed deep underground at Gran Sasso National Laboratory. The obtained experimental half-life value for the studied process is T$_{1/2} = 5.08(13) \times$ 10$^{10}$ yr; while a $g_{\rm A}$ value in the range 0.4 to 0.6 is obtained when accounting for uncertainties and depending on the model adopted as discussed in detail in the text.

## Key claim

The measured electron spectral shape of the third-forbidden non-unique beta decay of ⁸⁷Rb yields an effective weak axial coupling gA in the range 0.4 to 0.6, depending on the nuclear model adopted and accounting for uncertainties, rather than a single precise value.

## Method

A 0.4647 g Rb₂ZrCl₆ crystal scintillator was grown using the vertical Bridgman-Stockbarger technique, embedding the ⁸⁷Rb source directly within the detector material for near-100% detection efficiency. Data were taken deep underground at Gran Sasso National Laboratory over 80418.8 s live time, and the measured beta spectrum was unfolded using Monte Carlo response functions from Geant4 simulations to correct for detector energy resolution and efficiency. The experimental spectral shape was compared with theoretical predictions from multiple phenomenological nuclear models to extract the effective gA value and the half-life.

## Key numbers

* Half-life of ⁸⁷Rb: T₁/₂ = 5.08(13) × 10¹⁰ yr, measured with the Rb₂ZrCl₆ crystal scintillator.
* Effective weak axial coupling gA extracted in the range 0.4 to 0.6, depending on the nuclear model and accounting for uncertainties.
* Q-value of the decay: Qβ = 282.275(6) keV, from the AME2020 database.
* Number of ⁸⁷Rb nuclei in the sample: N₈₇ = 3.279(2) × 10²⁰.
* Energy threshold of the measurement: 10 keV, with energy resolution FWHM[keV] = 1.41 × √(E[keV]).
* Counting rate from ⁸⁷Rb alone: 123 Hz; with ¹³³Ba calibration source: 157 Hz.
* Detection efficiency above 99.3% for beta kinetic energies above 16 keV at the 10 keV threshold.

## Context

This work extends recent experimental studies of forbidden non-unique beta-decay spectral shapes used to probe the effective value of gA, following similar analyses for ¹¹³Cd (Bodnar 2020, Kostensalo 2021, 2023, Banerjee 2024) and ¹¹⁵In (Leder 2022, Pagliaroli 2024, Kostensalo 2024), as well as the second-forbidden decay of ⁹⁹Tc (Paul 2023). The results enable improved benchmarks for nuclear structure calculations of forbidden transitions and help characterize background contributions from ⁸⁷Rb in rare-event searches such as neutrinoless double beta decay and dark matter experiments. The new Rb₂ZrCl₆ crystal scintillator demonstrates the source-equals-detector approach for studying long-lived beta emitters with high efficiency.
