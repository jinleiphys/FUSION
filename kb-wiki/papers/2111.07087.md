# Light-front wavefunctions of mesons by design

> We develop a mechanism to build the light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) of meson bound states on a small-sized basis function representation. Unlike in a standard Hamiltonian formalism, the Hamiltonian in this method is implicit, and the information of the system is carried directly by the functional form and adjustable parameters of the LFWFs. In this work, we model the LFWFs for four charmonium states, $η_c$, $J/ψ$, $ψ'$, and $ψ(3770)$ as superpositions of orthonormal basis functions. We choose the basis functions as eigenfunctions of an effective Hamiltonian, which has a longitudinal confining potential in addition to the transverse confining potential from light-front holographic QCD. We determine the basis function parameters and superposition coefficients by employing both guidance from the nonrelativistic description of the meson states and the experimental measurements of the meson decay widths. With the obtained wavefunctions, we study the features of those meson states, including charge radii and parton distribution functions. We use the $J/ψ$ LFWF to calculate the meson production in diffractive deep inelastic scattering and ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions, and the $η_c$ LFWF to calculate its diphoton transition form factor. Both results show good agreement with experiments. The obtained LFWFs have simple-functional forms and can be readily used to predict additional experimental observables.

## Key claim

The authors construct light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) for charmonium states by directly choosing basis coefficients and parameters, rather than solving an eigenvalue equation; the resulting wavefunctions are designed to exactly satisfy a chosen set of constraints (normalization, symmetries, nonrelativistic limit, and experimental decay widths) rather than being derived from a specific Hamiltonian.

## Method

The LFWFs for four charmonium states (ηc, J/ψ, ψ′, ψ(3770)) are expanded as superpositions of orthonormal basis functions that are eigenfunctions of a generalized holographic confining Hamiltonian H0, which includes both transverse confinement from light-front holographic QCD and a longitudinal confining potential. The basis coefficients and the two adjustable parameters (κ and mf) are determined by imposing normalization, mirror parity and charge conjugation symmetries, guidance from the nonrelativistic 3D harmonic oscillator limit, and experimental dilepton (for vector mesons) or diphoton (for pseudoscalar) decay widths. The resulting LFWFs are then used to compute charge radii, parton distribution functions, J/ψ production in diffractive deep inelastic scattering and ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions, and the ηc diphoton transition form factor.

## Key numbers

- Four charmonium states are modeled: ηc, J/ψ, ψ′, and ψ(3770).
- The basis functions depend on two adjustable parameters: the confining strength κ and the fermion mass mf (with mq = mq̄ = mf for charmonium).
- The expectation value of H0 for a basis state is M²n,m,l(κ, mq, mq̄) = (mq + mq̄)² + 2κ²(2n + |m| + l + 3/2) + [κ⁴/(mq + mq̄)²] l(l+1).
- The longitudinal basis parameter α = β = 4mf²/κ for charmonium.
- The 2S state is approximated as ψLF-2S = √(2/3) ψ1,0,0 − √(1/3) ψ0,0,2.
- The 1D0 state is approximated as ψLF-1D0 = √(1/3) ψ1,0,0 + √(2/3) ψ0,0,2.

## Context

This work extends the line of modeled LFWFs such as the boosted Gaussian (Kowalski, 2006) and the DGKP model (Dosch, 1996), but differs by using basis functions inspired by the BLFQ Hamiltonian (Li, 2015; Li, 2017) and by imposing exact constraints from experimental decay widths. The approach enables the calculation of a wide variety of observables (production cross sections, form factors, parton distribution functions) with analytically tractable wavefunctions, and can be extended to larger basis sizes or additional constraints in future work.
