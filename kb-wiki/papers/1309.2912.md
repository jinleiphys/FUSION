# Hadronic absorption cross sections of $B_{c}$

> The cross sections of $B_{c}$\ absorption by $π$ mesons are calculated using hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(5) flavor symmetry. Calculated cross sections are found to be in range 2 to 7 mb and 0.2 to 2 mb for the processes $B_{c}^{+}π\rightarrow DB$ and $B_{c}^{+}π\rightarrow D^{\ast}B^{\ast}$ respectively, when the monopole form factor is included. These results could be useful in calculating production rate of $B_{c}$ meson in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The absorption cross sections of \(B_c\) mesons by pions are calculated to be in the range 2–7 mb for \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow DB\) and 0.2–2 mb for \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow D^*B^*\), but these values are highly dependent on the choice of monopole form factor cutoff parameter (varied between 1 and 2 GeV) and on the value of the four-point contact coupling, which cannot be fixed empirically.

## Method
The authors construct a hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(5) flavor symmetry, including mass terms that break the symmetry, and derive interaction terms for the processes \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow DB\) and \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow D^*B^*\). Scattering amplitudes are computed from Feynman diagrams involving \(D^*\), \(B^*\), \(D\), and \(B\) meson exchanges, plus a four-point contact term for the second process. Cross sections are calculated with and without a monopole form factor at the vertices, using a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) varied from 1 to 2 GeV to account for the finite size of hadrons.

## Key numbers
- For \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow DB\): cross section ranges from 2 to 7 mb when the monopole form factor is included and the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) is varied between 1 and 2 GeV (threshold energy 7.15 GeV).
- For \(B_c^+\pi \rightarrow D^*B^*\): cross section ranges from 0.2 to 2 mb when the form factor is included, with \(\Lambda\) between 1 and 2 GeV and the four-point coupling set to 200 (threshold energy 7.34 GeV).
- Suppression due to the form factor at \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV is roughly a factor of 11 for the first process and a factor of 45 for the second process; at \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, suppression is roughly a factor of 3 and 7, respectively.
- The coupling constants used: \(g_{\pi DD^*} = 4.4\) (from \(D^*\) decay width), \(g_{\pi BB^*} = 12.4\) (from heavy quark symmetries), \(g_{B_c BD^*} = g_{B_c B^* D} = 11.9\) (from vector meson dominance and SU(5) symmetry), and the four-point coupling \(g_{\pi B_c B^* D^*}\) ranges from 105 to 295 (from SU(5) symmetry, with a mean value of 200).

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of heavy quarkonium absorption (e.g., \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) absorption by hadrons) using hadronic Lagrangians based on flavor symmetry, as done by Lin (2001) and Haglin (2000). It also follows a prior calculation of \(B_c\) absorption by nucleons using a meson-baryon exchange model by Lodhi (2007). The results are intended to help interpret the production rate of \(B_c\) mesons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the presence of quark-gluon plasma may enhance \(B_c\) formation from unpaired \(b\) and \(c\) quarks.
