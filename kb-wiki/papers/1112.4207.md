# Nuclear structure study around Z=28

> Yrast levels of Ni, Cu and Zn isotopes for $40 \leq N \leq50$ have been described by state-of-the-art shell model calculations with three recently available interactions using $^{56}$Ni as a core in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space. The results are unsatisfactory viz. large $E(2^+)$ for very neutron rich nuclei, small B(E2) values in comparison to experimental values. These results indicate an importance of inclusion of $πf_{7/2}$ and $νd_{5/2}$ orbitals in the model space to reproduce collectivity in this region.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space with three modern interactions systematically fail to reproduce experimental yrast energies and $B(E2)$ values for neutron-rich Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes, producing large $E(2^+)$ and small $B(E2)$ values; this failure indicates that the $\pi f_{7/2}$ and $\nu d_{5/2}$ orbitals must be included in the model space to capture the observed collectivity.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for yrast levels of Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes with $40 \leq N \leq 50$, using $^{56}$Ni as an inert core and the $p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, and $g_{9/2}$ valence space. Three effective interactions were employed: LSSMI (based on G-matrix theory with monopole modifications by Nowacki), LSSMII (the JJ44B interaction by Lisetskiy), and LSSMIII (the JUN45 interaction by Honma et al.). All calculations were carried out with the ANTOINE code.

## Key numbers

- For $^{70}$Ni, LSSMI predicts the $2^+$ state 406 keV above the experimental value; LSSMII predicts it 172 keV above.
- For $^{72}$Ni and beyond, both LSSMI and LSSMII predict $2^+$ and $4^+$ levels nearly 500 keV higher than experimental values.
- For $^{70}$Zn, the first $2^+$ state at 885 keV is predicted at 997 keV by LSSMI and at 957 keV by LSSMII.
- For $^{74}$Zn, the experimental $2^+$ at 606 keV is predicted at 879 keV by LSSMI and at 826 keV by LSSMII.
- For $^{76}$Zn, the experimental $2^+$ at 599 keV is predicted at 840 keV by LSSMI and at 911 keV by LSSMII.
- For $^{78}$Zn, the experimental $2^+$ at 730 keV is predicted at 1060 keV by LSSMI and at 1032 keV by LSSMII.
- For $^{80}$Zn, the experimental $2^+$ at 1492 keV is predicted at 1731 keV by LSSMI and at 1353 keV by LSSMII.
- For $^{68}$Ni, the $B(E2)$ value with effective charges $e_\pi = 1.76$ and $e_\nu = 0.97$ is closest to experiment.
- With LSSMIII, the first $1/2^-$ state in Cu isotopes is too high by approximately 800 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich F, Fe, Mn, Co, and Ga isotopes (Srivastava et al., 2009-2011) to the Ni-Cu-Zn region. The systematic discrepancies found here contradict the ability of the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space to describe collectivity beyond $N=40$, consistent with findings by Caurier et al. (2002) and Lenzi et al. (2010) that inclusion of $\pi f_{7/2}$ and $\nu d_{5/2}$ orbitals is necessary. The results motivate future calculations with the LNPS interaction in the $fpgd$ space, which has already been shown to account for collectivity in Cr, Fe, and Ni isotopes (Lenzi et al., 2010).
