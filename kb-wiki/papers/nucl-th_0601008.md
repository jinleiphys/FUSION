# Signature inversion -- manifestation of drift of the rotational axis in triaxial nuclei

> A possible scheme of realizing shell model calculations for heavy nuclei is based on a deformed basis and the projection technique. Here we present a new development for odd-odd nuclei, in which one starts with triaxially-deformed multi-quasi-particle configurations, builds the shell-model space through exact three-dimensional angular-momentum-projection, and diagonalizes a two-body Hamiltonian in this space. The model enables us to study the old problem of signature inversion from a different view. With an excellent reproduction of the experimental data in the mass-130 region, the results tend to interpret the phenomenon as a manifestation of dynamical drift of the rotational axis with presence of axial asymmetry in these nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that signature inversion in odd-odd nuclei can be naturally described by a triaxial projected shell model without invoking unusual assumptions about shape changes or interactions; the phenomenon is interpreted as a manifestation of dynamical drift of the rotational axis from the shortest principal axis (x-axis) to the intermediate principal axis (y-axis) as spin increases, with the caveat that the degree of triaxiality in the deformed basis determines the magnitude of signature splitting and the occurrence of inversion.

## Method
The authors construct a shell-model space for odd-odd nuclei by starting with triaxially-deformed multi-quasi-particle configurations (up to 6-qp states), performing exact three-dimensional angular-momentum projection, and diagonalizing a two-body Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing forces. The basis is generated from a Nilsson Hamiltonian with quadrupole deformation parameters ε₂ and triaxial deformation γ, and calculations are performed for the πh₁₁/₂νh₁₁/₂ bands in ¹¹⁸–¹³⁰Cs isotopes.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation ε₂ ranges from 0.30 (¹¹⁸Cs) to 0.19 (¹³⁰Cs), and triaxial deformation γ ranges from 30° (¹¹⁸Cs) to 39° (¹³⁰Cs) (Table 1).
- Reversion spin I_rev increases with neutron number: 14.5 for ¹¹⁸Cs, 16.5 for ¹²⁰Cs, 17.5 for ¹²²Cs, 18.5 for ¹²⁴Cs, 20.5 for ¹²⁶Cs, with predictions of 21.5 for ¹²⁸Cs and 22.5 for ¹³⁰Cs.
- Monopole pairing strength G_M uses G=19.6 MeV for neutrons and 17.2 MeV for protons, with the standard form G/A.
- Quadrupole pairing strength G_Q is fixed as 0.16 times G_M for all nuclei.
- In ¹²⁴Cs, with F_np=0 (no neutron-proton QQ interaction), I_rev=15.5; with F_np=1, I_rev=16.5; with F_np=2, I_rev=17.5.
- The rotational axis drift occurs roughly in the spin interval I=12–24 for ¹²⁴Cs.

## Context
This work extends the projected shell model (PSM) approach of Hara and Sun (1995) from axially deformed bases to triaxially deformed bases for odd-odd nuclei, addressing the long-standing problem of signature inversion first observed experimentally in 1982. Prior explanations invoked γ-reversed moment-of-inertia (Hamamoto 1983, Ikeda 1989) or neutron-proton interactions (Semmes 1990), but the authors show that triaxiality alone in a fully quantum-mechanical treatment can reproduce the data without adjustable interaction strengths. The model enables future studies of other triaxial phenomena such as chiral doublet bands and γ-vibrational states in heavy nuclei.
