# A New Look At Small-$x$ Helicity Phenomenology: Investigating Uncertainties And The Impact Of Polarized Proton-proton Data

> This dissertation highlights the contributions I have made to the field of theoretical nuclear physics, specifically in high-energy Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). High-energy QCD is a robust subject and my research is refined to the sub-field of small-$x$ spin physics; small-$x$ physics is characterized by high-energy and density collisions and is well-suited for the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory. Small-$x$ spin physics takes the ultra-relativistic description of high-energy QCD and gives special attention to spin-dependent interactions suppressed by powers of the center-of-mass energy. My expertise lies in exploring the theory and phenomenology relating to the KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations, a rubric that allows one to make predictions of the quarks' and gluons' distributions of spin at small-$x$. These predictions are heavily influenced by the initial conditions of the evolution, and the initial conditions are determined through analyses of world polarized data. My contributions focus on Bayesian parameter analysis, numerical and analytical calculations to discretize and cross-check the evolution equations, and the incorporation of a new observable into the pool of analyzed data. The results of such work show that the net amount of spin from quarks and gluons in the small-$x$ regime is predicted to be negative and/or potentially small; an analysis of polarized deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) data resulted in a net small-$x$ spin prediction that can be large and negative, but new results with the inclusion of data for single-inclusive jet production in polarized proton-proton ($pp$) collisions now estimate that the net amount of parton spin at small $x$ is small, with 1-$σ$ uncertainty that spans zero.

# A New Look At Small-$x$ Helicity Phenomenology: Investigating Uncertainties And The Impact Of Polarized Proton-proton Data

## Key claim
The net amount of parton spin from quarks and gluons in the small-$x$ regime is predicted to be small, with 1-$\sigma$ uncertainty that spans zero, when data for single-inclusive jet production in polarized proton-proton collisions are included; this contrasts with earlier analyses of polarized deep-inelastic scattering and semi-inclusive DIS data alone, which yielded a net small-$x$ spin prediction that could be large and negative.

## Method
The author applies Bayesian parameter analysis to the KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations, which describe the spin-dependent distributions of quarks and gluons at small Bjorken-$x$. Numerical and analytical calculations are performed to discretize and cross-check the evolution equations, and the initial conditions are determined through global analyses of world polarized data, including polarized deep-inelastic scattering, semi-inclusive DIS, and newly incorporated single-inclusive jet production data from polarized proton-proton collisions. The author also generalizes the decomposition of the Sivers function of a nucleus in the quasi-classical approximation with a rigid rotator model for the nucleus, extending prior work for the case of nucleons with arbitrary polarization.

## Key numbers
- The polarized cross-sections for tree-level Reggeon and Pomeron exchanges scale as $1/(zs)$ relative to their spin-summed counterparts, where $z$ is the longitudinal momentum fraction of the interacting quark/antiquark and $s$ is the center-of-mass energy squared.
- The Born-level polarized cross-section for Reggeon exchange is $\Delta\sigma_{reggeon}^{Born}/d^2k = -2(\alpha_s^2 C_F^2/N_c)(1/(zs))(1/k_T^2)\delta_{-\sigma,\sigma'}$.
- The Born-level polarized cross-section for Pomeron exchange is $\Delta\sigma_{pomeron}^{Born}/d^2k = 2(\alpha_s^2 C_F/N_c)(1/(zs))(1/k_T^2)$.
- The net small-$x$ spin prediction from the analysis including polarized proton-proton data is small, with 1-$\sigma$ uncertainty that spans zero (no specific numerical value for the spin prediction is given in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the small-$x$ helicity phenomenology developed by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert, and Tawabutr (2015-2022) and Hatta, Xiao, and Yuan (2016), among others, which established the KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations. The author's contributions include Bayesian parameter analysis and the incorporation of polarized proton-proton jet production data, which had not been included in prior small-$x$ helicity analyses. The generalization of the nuclear Sivers function decomposition for arbitrary nucleon polarization states builds on the rigid rotator model for nuclei from Kovchegov (2013) and the Boer-Mulders TMD generalization from Kovchegov (2015). This work enables future investigation of sub-eikonal medium-induced drift effects in cold nuclear matter and their impact on spin-dependent observables.
