# Next Generation Relativistic Models

> The current generation of covariant mean-field models has had many successes in calculations of bulk observables for medium to heavy nuclei, but there remain many open questions. New challenges are confronted when trying to systematically extend these models to reliably address nuclear structure physics away from the line of stability. In this lecture, we discuss a framework for the next generation of relativistic models that can address these questions and challenges. We interpret nuclear mean-field approaches as approximate implementations of Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT), which is widely used in condensed matter and quantum chemistry applications. We look to effective field theory (EFT) for a systematic approach to low-energy nuclear physics that can provide the framework for nuclear DFT.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current covariant mean-field models can be systematically improved by reinterpreting them as approximate implementations of Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT) guided by effective field theory (EFT), but the success of this program depends on properly absorbing "hard" Dirac-sea physics into local counterterms rather than treating it explicitly.

## Method

The author interprets nuclear mean-field approaches as approximate Kohn-Sham DFT and uses effective field theory to provide a systematic power counting for constructing improved covariant energy functionals. The effective action formalism is employed to show that the "no-sea approximation" for ground states corresponds to subtracting the Tr ln evaluated at zero chemical potential, which removes explicit negative-energy contributions while preserving fixed consequences for linear response (RPA) that still involve negative-energy states. The analysis uses naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to estimate the size of individual contributions to energy functionals and applies this to neutron skin calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi momentum k_F is small compared to the nucleon mass, making nuclei nonrelativistic
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is roughly 1 GeV (600 MeV is probably closer in practice)
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV is used in NDA scaling
- For the NN potential, intermediate states with relative momenta as high as q = 20 fm^{-1} are needed for convergence, but elastic scattering data only constrain the potential for q less than or equal to 3 fm^{-1}
- The collapse of different NN potentials to a common low-momentum potential occurs for Lambda between about 500 and 600 MeV (or 2.5 to 3 fm^{-1})
- In chiral EFT phase shifts, LO, NLO, and NNLO show systematic improvement; N^3LO calculations by Entem and Machleidt achieve chi^2 approximately 1 for phase-shift data up to 300 MeV lab energy
- The dimensionless constants from NDA scaling of LO and NLO constants range from 1/3 to 3 for cutoffs from 500 to 600 MeV, with one exception (f_pi^2 C_T is unnaturally small)
- The RHA energy functional yields phi^5 and higher terms with an overall factor of M^4, which is much larger than the NDA factor of f_pi^2 Lambda_chi^2
- With M*/M approximately 0.6 (required for realistic spin-orbit splittings), the energy per particle from the phi^5 term is two orders of magnitude too large

## Context

This lecture extends the line of work on quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) and relativistic mean-field models initiated by Serot (1986) and others, while addressing the open questions identified by Ring (1996) and Serot (1997) about extending these models away from stability. The paper builds on the EFT framework for nuclear physics developed by Furnstahl (2000) and the chiral EFT for few-nucleon systems by Epelbaum (2003) and Entem and Machleidt (2003), proposing that the effective action formalism provides a natural framework for systematically improving mean-field functionals. The work enables the construction of improved covariant functionals with EFT power counting, analogous to recent work on systematic Kohn-Sham functionals for cold atomic gases.
