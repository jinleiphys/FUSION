# Fayans functional for deformed nuclei. Uranium region

> Fayans energy density functional (EDF) FaNDF^0 has been applied to the nuclei around uranium region. Ground state characteristics of the Th, U and Pu isotopic chains, up to the two-neutron drip line, are found and compared with predictions from several Skyrme EDFs. The two-neutron drip line is found for FaNDF^0, SLy4 and SkM^* EDFs for a set of elements with even proton number, from Pb up to Fm.

# Fayans functional for deformed nuclei. Uranium region

## Key claim

The Fayans energy density functional FaNDF^0, originally fitted only to spherical nuclei not heavier than lead, yields ground-state properties and two-neutron drip line predictions for Th, U, and Pu isotopic chains that are broadly consistent with those from several popular Skyrme EDFs (SLy4, HFB-17, HFB-27), except that the SkM* functional systematically predicts higher two-neutron separation energies and consequently farther drip lines. The authors caution that all such extrapolations to the drip line carry a typical uncertainty of ±10 mass units or more, and that an additional shift from the possible chemical-potential dependence of EDF parameters remains unquantified.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the axial computer code HFBTHO with 25 oscillator shells, limiting deformation to quadrupole (β₂) only and assuming reflection symmetry. They employed the localized Fayans functional FaNDF^0 with a simplified volume pairing model (h^ξ = 0, f^ξ_ex = −0.440) and a quasiparticle cut-off energy of 60 MeV. For comparison, they repeated all calculations with the Skyrme functionals SkM* and SLy4 using the same code, and additionally compared with published HFB-17 and HFB-27 results from the reference site.

## Key numbers

- For the uranium chain, the FaNDF^0 functional shows a deviation of 0.5 MeV from experimental S₂n values for heavy U isotopes.
- The two-neutron drip line point for uranium with FaNDF^0 is A = 300; with SLy4 it is A = 298; with SkM* it is A = 318; with HFB-17 it is A = 308; with HFB-27 it is A = 304.
- For thorium, the FaNDF^0 drip line point is A = 294; for HFB-17 and HFB-27 it is approximately 300; for SkM* it is A = 306.
- For plutonium, the SkM* drip line point is A = 324, significantly higher than for other functionals.
- The deformation parameter β₂ at the drip line for uranium with FaNDF^0 is 0.26; for SLy4 it is 0.22; for SkM* it is 0.21; for HFB-17 it is 0.25; for HFB-27 it is 0.24.
- For the SKM* functional, the drip line for californium (Cf) is anomalously high at A = 354 with β₂ = 0.00, due to competition between spherical and prolate minima.
- The authors state that all drip line predictions carry a typical uncertainty of ±10 mass units or more.

## Context

This work extends the first application of the Fayans EDF to deformed nuclei (Tolokonnikov et al., 2014) by systematically computing ground-state properties and drip lines for Th, U, and Pu chains, and for even-Z elements from Pb to Fm. It compares the FaNDF^0 results with predictions from the Skyrme functionals SkM* (Bartel et al., 1982), SLy4 (Chabanat et al., 1998), HFB-17, and HFB-27 (Goriely et al., 2009), and with experimental masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation. The two-neutron drip line results are relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis studies in stars (Kajino et al., 2012; Panov et al., 2013). The authors note that the effect of chemical-potential dependence of EDF parameters near the drip line (Mueller et al., 2012) could shift the predicted drip lines further, but this effect has not yet been incorporated into a fully readjusted EDF.
