# A Novel Approach For Event-By-Event Early Gluon Fields

> We report on efforts to construct an event generator that calculates the classical gluon field generated at early times in high energy nuclear collisions. Existing approaches utilize numerical solutions of the Yang-Mills equations after the collision. In contrast we employ the analytically known recursion relation in the forward light cone. The few lowest orders are expected to lead to reliable results for times of up to the inverse saturation scale, tau_0 ~ 1/Q_s. In these proceedings we sketch some calculational details, and show some preliminary results.

## Key claim

The authors propose an event generator for early-time classical gluon fields in high-energy nuclear collisions that uses an analytically known recursion relation in the forward light cone, rather than full numerical solutions of the Yang-Mills equations. The approach is expected to yield reliable results only for times up to the inverse saturation scale, tau_0 ~ 1/Q_s, and the preliminary results shown are restricted to second order in the recursion.

## Method

The method samples Gaussian color charge densities for each nucleus according to the McLerran-Venugopalan model, then solves the Poisson equation for the covariant potential using a coarse-grained Green's function. A gauge transformation to Fock-Schwinger gauge is applied, and the gluon fields after the collision are computed using a power series expansion A = sum_n A_(n) tau^n in proper time tau, with recursion relations that solve the Yang-Mills equations analytically order by order.

## Key numbers

- The recursion is expected to give reliable results for times up to tau_0 ~ 1/Q_s, where Q_s is the saturation scale.
- The coarse graining scale lambda satisfies 1/Q_s >> lambda >> a, where a is the lattice constant.
- The correlation function <alpha^a(r_1) alpha^a(r_2)> was computed numerically over 7800 events and compared to the analytic result.
- The time evolution of the energy density is shown between tau = 0 fm/c and tau = 0.1 fm/c for a head-on collision of nuclei with radius R_A = 3 fm.
- The initial energy density at tau=0 is epsilon_0 = (E_0^2 + B_0^2)/2.

## Context

This work extends the recursive solution approach to the Yang-Mills boundary value problem in the forward light cone, previously discussed by Fries (2006) and Chen (2013, 2015), and contrasts with existing numerical approaches such as the IP-Glasma model by Schenke (2012) which solves the Yang-Mills equations numerically and matches to viscous fluid dynamics. The approach enables event-by-event calculations without solving differential equations for time evolution, but is limited to early times; future work includes implementing more realistic charge density models (e.g., IP-Sat) and pushing to higher orders in the recursion.
