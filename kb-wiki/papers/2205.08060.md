# Semi-inclusive Diffractive Deep Inelastic Scattering at Small-$x$

> Inspired by a recent study of Iancu, Mueller and Triantafyllopoulos [1] and earlier papers by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff [2,3], we propose semi-inclusive diffractive deep inelastic scattering (SIDDIS) to investigate the gluon tomography in the nucleon and nuclei at small-$x$. The relevant diffractive quark and gluon parton distribution functions (DPDF) can be computed in terms of the color dipole S-matrices in the fundamental and adjoint representations, respectively. Novel correlations from the gluon tomography in the dipole S-matrix can be experimentally studied through the DPDFs in these processes at the future electron-ion collider (EIC).

## Key claim

The authors propose semi-inclusive diffractive deep inelastic scattering (SIDDIS) as a new probe of gluon tomography at small-x, claiming that the relevant diffractive parton distribution functions (DPDFs) can be computed from color dipole S-matrices and that the QCD factorization results are consistent with Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) calculations in the kinematics where both apply, though the consistency at higher orders beyond the q-qbar-g final state requires future investigation.

## Method

The authors compute the quark and gluon transverse-momentum-dependent diffractive parton distribution functions (TMD DPDFs) from their operator definitions, evaluating them in the CGC framework by resumming multiple interactions with the background field via Wilson lines. The DPDFs are expressed in terms of Fourier transforms of the fundamental and adjoint color dipole S-matrices, and the resulting expressions are evaluated numerically using a Gaussian model for the dipole amplitudes. The integrated (collinear) DPDFs are then derived and parameterized with analytic forms based on endpoint behavior at β→0 and β→1.

## Key numbers

- The quark DPDF at large transverse momentum behaves as 1/k_perp^4, specifically: x d f_q^D/(dY_IP dt) |_{k_perp >> Q_s} = (alpha_s^2/(2 pi)) (C_q/k_perp^4) (H_g(x_IP, Delta_perp))^2, where C_q = beta^3 (1-beta)^2 / N_c
- The gluon DPDF at large transverse momentum behaves as: x d f_g^D/(dY_IP dt) |_{k_perp >> Q_s} = (alpha_s^2/(2 pi)) (C_g/k_perp^4) (H_g(x_IP, Delta_perp))^2, where C_g = (1+2beta)^2 (1-beta)^3 N_c^2/(N_c^2-1)
- The integrated quark DPDF parameterization: D_q(beta) = (b_1 + b_2 (1-beta)) beta (1-beta), with b_1 = 3 pi^2/16 - 1 and b_2 = (20 - 3 pi^2)/16
- The integrated gluon DPDF parameterization: D_g(beta) = (a_0 + a_1 beta) (1-beta)^2, with a_0 = ln(2)/2 and a_1 = (45 pi^2 - 272)/256 - ln(2)/2
- The ratio of total momentum fractions carried by quarks and gluons: (2 N_f int dbeta x f^D_q)/(int dbeta x f^D_g) ≈ 0.33 for N_f = 3
- The total momentum fraction normalization: int dbeta x [2 N_f d f^D_q/(dY_IP dt) + d f^D_g/(dY_IP dt)] ≈ S_perp^2/(2 pi)^3 Q_s^2
- The adjoint saturation scale relates to the fundamental as Q_as^2 = (C_A/C_F) Q_s^2 ≈ 2 Q_s^2

## Context

This work extends the study of diffractive dijet production by Hatta, Xiao, and Yuan (2016) and the trijet proposal by Iancu, Mueller, and Triantafyllopoulos (2021), establishing a unified connection between QCD factorization for hard diffractive processes and the CGC/dipole formalism at small-x. The computed DPDFs can serve as initial-scale inputs for collinear QCD factorizations applicable to all hard diffractive processes, and the results enable experimental studies of gluon tomography and spin-orbital correlations at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). The parameterizations agree with earlier work by Buchmuller (1998) for the quark case, while a factor-of-2 difference in the gluon case is attributed to an inconsistent parameterization of the adjoint dipole S-matrix in Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999).
