# Systematic analysis of hadron spectra in p+p collisions using Tsallis distribution

> Using the experimental data from the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE and CMS programs on the rapidity and energy dependence of the $p_T$ spectra in p+p collisions, we show that a universal distribution exists. The energy dependence of temperature $T$ and parameter $n$ of the Tsallis distribution are also discussed in detail. A cascade particle production mechanism in p+p collisions is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a universal Tsallis distribution can describe hadron transverse momentum spectra in p+p collisions across a wide range of collision energies, but the temperature parameter \(T\) is not universal across particle species; it depends on particle mass and the specific form of the Tsallis distribution used, with pions showing a nearly constant temperature around 0.13 GeV while kaons and protons show increasing temperature with collision energy.

## Method

The authors collected published \(p_T\) spectra data for \(\pi^0\), \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(p\), \(\bar p\), and charged particles from the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS collaborations at \(\sqrt{s} = 62.4\), 200, 900, 2760, and 7000 GeV. They fitted the data using a simplified Tsallis distribution of the form \((E d^3N/dp^3)_{|\eta|<a} = A (1 + E_T/(nT))^{-n}\), where \(E_T = m_T - m\), and extracted the parameters \(A\), \(n\), and \(T\) for each particle species at each energy. The fits were performed separately for each particle species without requiring a common temperature.

## Key numbers

*   Pion temperature \(T\) is approximately constant at 0.13 GeV across all collision energies from 62.4 to 7000 GeV, e.g., \(T = 0.135\) GeV for \(\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV and \(T = 0.141\) GeV for \(\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV.
*   Proton temperature \(T\) increases with collision energy, from 0.156 GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV to 0.243 GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV (from PHENIX and CMS data respectively).
*   Kaon temperature \(T\) also increases with energy, from 0.160 GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV to 0.228 GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV (from PHENIX and CMS data respectively).
*   The parameter \(n\) for pions decreases with collision energy, from \(n = 13.26\) at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV to \(n = 6.4\) at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV.
*   The Tsallis distribution fits charged particle spectra over 15 orders of magnitude up to \(p_T = 200\) GeV/c, with parameters for charged particles at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV: \(n = 6.61\) and \(T = 0.148\) GeV.
*   The relation between \(n\) and the entropic factor \(q\) is \(n = 1/(q-1)\), giving limits \(q \in [1.063, 1.25]\) for mesons and \(q \in [1.05, 1.25]\) for protons.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using Tsallis distributions in p+p collisions, particularly the work of Khandai et al. (2013) which assumed a common temperature for all particles, and Wong et al. (2012) which introduced a rapidity-cut-aware form. The authors propose a cascade particle production mechanism where heavier particles (kaons, protons) are produced earlier with higher temperatures, while pions are produced throughout the collision and maintain a constant temperature. The results enable further investigation of particle production mechanisms at higher energies, such as 8 TeV p+p collisions, and provide a baseline for comparison with p+Pb and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
