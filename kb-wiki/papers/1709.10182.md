# Difference in proton radii of mirror nuclei as a possible surrogate for the neutron skin

> It has been recently suggested that differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei are proportional to the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei and to the slope of the symmetry energy $L$ [B.A. Brown Phys. Rev. Lett. 119, 122502 (2017)]. The determination of the neutron skin has important implications for nuclear physics and astrophysics. Although the use of electroweak probes provides a largely model-independent determination of the neutron skin, the experimental challenges are enormous. Thus, the possibility that differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei may be used as a surrogate for the neutron skin is a welcome alternative. To test the validity of this assumption we perform calculations based on a set of relativistic energy density functionals that span a wide region of values of $L$. Our results confirm that the difference in charge radii of various neutron-deficient nickel isotopes and their corresponding mirror nuclei is indeed strongly correlated to both the neutron-skin thickness and $L$. Moreover, given that various neutron-star properties are also sensitive to $L$, a data-to-data relation emerges between the difference in charge radii of mirror nuclei and the radius of low-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei are strongly correlated to both the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei and the slope of the symmetry energy L, but only within the context of the relativistic mean field models employed; the authors caution that the correlation between the neutron skin of 208Pb and that of 48Ca may not be as strong as suggested, indicating possible model dependence.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using a set of 14 relativistic energy density functionals that span a wide range of values for the slope parameter L (approximately 50 to 140 MeV). They computed the difference in proton radii between neutron-deficient nickel isotopes (50Ni, 52Ni, 54Ni) and their corresponding mirror nuclei (50Ti, 52Fe, 54Fe) along the A=50, 52, 54 isobars, and compared these to the neutron-skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb. They also computed neutron-star radii for masses of 0.8, 1.0, 1.2, and 1.4 solar masses as a function of the mirror-nuclei radius difference.

## Key numbers

- The set of 14 relativistic energy density functionals spans a range of the slope parameter L from approximately 50 to 140 MeV.
- The corresponding neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb ranges from about 0.15 to 0.33 fm.
- To determine the neutron skin of 48Ca to a precision of 0.02 fm, the charge radius of 54Ni must be measured to better than 0.004 fm; the charge radius of its stable mirror nucleus 54Fe is already known to 0.002 fm.
- For the A=50 case, the charge radius of 50Ni needs to be determined to 0.012 fm.
- The PREX-II experiment aims to determine the neutron radius of 208Pb to 0.06 fm.
- The correlation coefficient between R_mirr^50 and the radius of a 0.8 solar mass neutron star is r=0.96.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the correlation weakens to r=0.8.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Brown (2017), who first proposed that differences in charge radii of mirror nuclei are proportional to the neutron-skin thickness and the slope of the symmetry energy L using Skyrme functionals. The present study confirms these correlations within the relativistic mean field framework and extends them to neutron-star properties, establishing a data-to-data relation between the difference in charge radii of mirror nuclei and the radius of low-mass neutron stars. This enables the possibility of using charge radius measurements of neutron-deficient nickel isotopes at facilities such as ISOLDE-CERN and RIKEN-SCRIT to constrain both the neutron skin of neutron-rich nuclei and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter.
