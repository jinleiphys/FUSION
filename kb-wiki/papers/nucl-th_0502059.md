# Isospin dependence of 6He+p optical potential and the symmetry energy

> A consistent folding analysis of the elastic p(6He,6He)p scattering and charge exchange p(6He,6Li*)n reaction data measured at Elab=41.6A MeV has been performed within the coupled channels formalism. We have used the isovector coupling to link the isospin dependence of 6He+p optical potential to the cross section of p(6He,6Li*)n reaction exciting the 0+ isobaric analog state (IAS) at 3.563 MeV in 6Li. Based on these results and the Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter using the same isospin-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, we were able to confirm that the most realistic value of the symmetry energy Esym is around 31 MeV. Our analysis has also shown that the measured charge exchange p(6He,6Li*)n data are quite sensitive to the halo tail of the 6He density used in the folding calculation and the IAS of 6Li is likely to have a halo structure similar to that established for the ground state of 6He.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy \(E_{\rm sym}\) is confirmed to be around 31 MeV, based on a coupled channels folding analysis of elastic p(6He,6He)p scattering and charge exchange p(6He,6Li*)n reaction data at 41.6A MeV, with the qualification that the extracted value is \(31 \pm 1\) MeV and depends on the isovector strength of the CDM3Y6 interaction being about 10% stronger than the isoscalar strength.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled channels (CC) analysis of elastic p(6He,6He)p scattering and charge exchange p(6He,6Li*)n reaction data at \(E_{\rm lab}=41.6A\) MeV, using the folding model to calculate the real parts of the optical potential and the (p,n) form factor from the CDM3Y6 density- and isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction. The isovector coupling (Lane potential) was used to link the isospin dependence of the 6He+p optical potential to the charge exchange cross section exciting the 0+ isobaric analog state (IAS) at 3.563 MeV in 6Li. The scaling factors \(N_{\rm R}\approx 0.85\) and \(N_{\rm I}\approx 0.55\) for the real and imaginary parts of the optical potential were determined from the elastic scattering data and the total reaction cross section constraint of about 400 mb, and the isovector strength \(C_1\) was fine-tuned to fit the charge exchange data. A Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter using the same interaction then yielded the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy \(E_{\rm sym} = 31 \pm 1\) MeV, deduced from the Hartree-Fock calculation using the CDM3Y6 interaction with the isovector strength determined from the charge exchange data.
* Total reaction cross section \(\sigma_{\rm R} = 409 \pm 22\) mb measured for the 6He+p system at 36A MeV, used as a constraint; the COSMA density with RMS = 2.57 fm gave \(\sigma_{\rm R} = 408\) mb and the COSMA density with RMS = 2.48 fm gave \(\sigma_{\rm R} = 399\) mb.
* Renormalization factors for the folded optical potential: \(N_{\rm R} \approx 0.85\) and \(N_{\rm I} \approx 0.55\), giving a ratio \(N_{\rm I}/N_{\rm R} \approx 0.65\).
* The best fit to the charge exchange data required the isovector strength \(C_1\) to be about 10% stronger than the isoscalar strength \(C_0\).

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach of Khoa (2002, 2003) and the CDM3Y6 interaction of Khoa (1997) to the charge exchange reaction induced by a neutron-rich beam, providing a direct link between the isospin dependence of the optical potential and the nuclear symmetry energy. The results are complementary to neutron-skin studies by Brown (2000) and Furuta (2002), which gave \(E_{\rm sym} \approx 27-31\) MeV for a neutron skin \(\Delta R \approx 0.1-0.2\) fm in 208Pb, and to heavy-ion fragmentation analyses by Shetty (2004) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, which gave \(S(\rho \approx 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}) \approx 18-20\) MeV. The analysis also shows that the charge exchange data favor the COSMA density with RMS = 2.57 fm for 6He, supporting the interpretation that the IAS of 6Li has a halo structure similar to the ground state of 6He, and provides an improved description over earlier JLM G-matrix analyses by Cortina-Gil (1998) and Visscher (2001).
