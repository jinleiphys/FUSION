# Ab Initio Calculations of Even Oxygen Isotopes with Chiral Two- Plus Three-Nucleon Interactions

> We formulate the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IM-SRG) for open-shell nuclei using a multi-reference formalism based on a generalized Wick theorem introduced in quantum chemistry. The resulting multi-reference IM-SRG (MR-IM-SRG) is used to perform the first ab initio study of even oxygen isotopes with chiral NN and 3N Hamiltonians, from the proton to the neutron drip lines. We obtain an excellent reproduction of experimental ground-state energies with quantified uncertainties, which is validated by results from the Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model and the Coupled Cluster method. The agreement between conceptually different many-body approaches and experiment highlights the predictive power of current chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, and establishes the MR-IM-SRG as a promising new tool for ab initio calculations of medium-mass nuclei far from shell closures.

## Key claim
The multi-reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (MR-IM-SRG) achieves an excellent reproduction of experimental ground-state energies for all even oxygen isotopes from the proton to the neutron drip line when using chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians, with the caveat that the agreement depends on including the full chiral three-nucleon interaction and that the method slightly overestimates the energy of the unbound ⁰²⁶O resonance due to limitations of the harmonic-oscillator basis.

## Method
The authors formulate the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group for open-shell nuclei using a multi-reference formalism based on a generalized Wick theorem from quantum chemistry, which allows normal-ordering with respect to an arbitrary reference state rather than a single Slater determinant. Reference states for each oxygen isotope are obtained by solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in 15 major harmonic-oscillator shells with projection on good proton and neutron numbers, and the MR-IM-SRG flow equations are integrated to decouple the ground state from excitations while truncating three-body operators and terms containing irreducible three-body density matrices. The method is validated by comparing results with Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model (IT-NCSM) calculations and Coupled Cluster (CC) calculations using the same chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energies for oxygen isotopes from ⁰¹²O to ⁰²⁶O are reproduced within 1–2% of experimental values for the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian with Λ₃N = 400 MeV/c.
- The MR-IM-SRG(2) ground-state energies lie 1.5–2% below the IT-NCSM results, with about 1% of this deviation caused by the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation.
- The overall uncertainty of the MR-IM-SRG oxygen energies is estimated at the level of 3–3.5%, consistent with previous closed-shell IM-SRG calculations.
- For ⁰²⁶O, the IT-NCSM computation of the complete NMax sequence requires about 200,000 CPU hours, while a corresponding MR-IM-SRG sequence requires only about 3,000 CPU hours.
- Reducing the 3N basis truncation from E3max = 14 to 12 changes MR-IM-SRG(2) ground-state energies by less than 1%.
- At the optimal harmonic-oscillator frequency, increasing the single-particle basis from eMax = 12 to 14 changes the ground-state energy by 0.1%.

## Context
This work extends the IM-SRG framework (Tsukiyama 2011, Hergert 2012) from closed-shell to open-shell nuclei, enabling the first ab initio study of all even oxygen isotopes with chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians. The results are validated against IT-NCSM calculations (Roth 2009, 2011) and Coupled Cluster calculations (Jansen 2011, Hagen 2012), and the agreement between these conceptually different many-body approaches demonstrates the predictive power of current chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2009, Machleidt 2011). The method's polynomial scaling (O(N⁶)) makes it suitable for medium- and heavy-mass open-shell nuclei far from shell closures, where the No-Core Shell Model is not feasible and standard Coupled Cluster methods are not applicable.
