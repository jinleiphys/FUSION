# Supplementary Methods for "Comment on 'Nuclear Emissions During Self-Nucleated Acoustic Cavitation'"

> Neutron simulation methods and statistical fitting methods for "Comment on 'Nuclear Emissions During Self-Nucleated Acoustic Cavitation'" are presented. The fit that underlies the Comment's background-subtracted "Cf-252" curve is shown. Also included are numerically sampled distributions of the goodness-of-fit variable for the four candidate neutron sources and a sketch of the assumed geometry.

# Supplementary Methods for "Comment on 'Nuclear Emissions During Self-Nucleated Acoustic Cavitation'"

## Key claim

The observed neutron spectrum from cavitation experiments is statistically consistent with a californium-252 (Cf-252) fission source (within one equivalent standard deviation), while three other candidate neutron sources (2.45 MeV, 2.45 MeV with shielding, and PuBe) are statistically inconsistent (outside five equivalent standard deviations), based on a Poisson-likelihood chi-square goodness-of-fit test.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations using Geant4 calculated detector response functions for four candidate neutron sources: 2.45 MeV (with and without paraffin shielding), Cf-252, and PuBe, assuming isotropic emission from the center of the flask and including elastic scattering, thermal capture, (n, n' gamma) scattering, and neutron-induced fission. The raw "cavitation off" and "cavitation on" count data from the original experiment were modeled by a sum of two exponentials for background plus a scaled response function, and the five fit parameters were determined by minimizing the Poisson likelihood chi-square statistic. Numerically sampled distributions of the goodness-of-fit variable were generated from synthetic data sets drawn from Poisson distributions to compute Z-values for each candidate source.

## Key numbers

- The Cf-252 fit yields an observed chi-square value within **one equivalent standard deviation** of the mean of the sampled distribution, indicating statistical consistency.
- The other three candidate fits (2.45 MeV, 2.45 MeV with shielding, and PuBe) are each **outside five equivalent standard deviations**, indicating statistical inconsistency.
- The analysis uses data from channels **k = 11 to 249** of the pulse-height spectrum.
- Each experimental run (cavitation on and cavitation off) has a duration of **300 seconds**.

## Context

This work provides the detailed simulation and statistical methodology underlying the Comment by Naranjo on the original cavitation neutron emission claims of Taleyarkhan (2006). The Monte Carlo approach using Geant4 extends prior neutron detection response calculations (Verbinski 1968) and radioisotope source characterizations (Lajtai 1990, Anderson 1972). The statistical fitting method follows the Poisson likelihood chi-square formalism of Baker (1984) and the minimization routine of James (1975). The results enable quantitative rejection of three of the four proposed neutron source hypotheses for the observed cavitation emissions.
