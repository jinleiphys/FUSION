# Determining the neutron skin thickness by relativistic semi-isobaric collisions

> The neutron skin thickness of the benchmark nucleus $^{208}$Pb is crucial for our understanding of the equation of state of nuclear matter. In this paper, we discuss the effect of the neutron skin on the flow ratio observables in the semi-isobaric collisions $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au using iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic simulations. Our results suggest that $^{208}$Pb and $^{197}$Au should have the same magnitude of neutron skin thickness to describe the anisotropic flow ratios between the semi-isobaric systems. Our method provides an unconventional way to determine the neutron skin with the existing relativistic heavy ion collision data.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the neutron skin thicknesses of ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹⁹⁷Au must be approximately equal in magnitude to simultaneously describe the anisotropic flow ratios between Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions, which disfavors the large difference in neutron skin thickness between these nuclei reported by the PREX-II and STAR experiments.

## Method

The authors performed iEBE-VISHNU hybrid hydrodynamic simulations (with TRENTo initial conditions and UrQMD hadronic afterburner) for ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They introduced halo-type neutron skin thicknesses by varying the diffuseness parameter a in the Woods-Saxon distribution for the mass density while keeping the radius parameter R₀ fixed, using three values of Δrnp (0, 0.17, and 0.28 fm) for both nuclei. The anisotropic flow coefficients v₂{2}, v₂{4}, and v₃{2} were calculated using the Q-cumulant method, and ratios of these observables between the two collision systems were compared to experimental data from STAR and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb measured by PREX-II: Δrnp = 0.28 ± 0.07 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of ¹⁹⁷Au measured by STAR via ρ⁰ photoproduction: Δrnp = 0.17 ± 0.03 ± 0.08 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca measured by CREX: Δrnp = 0.121 ± 0.026 ± 0.024 fm
- Woods-Saxon radius parameter for ²⁰⁸Pb: R₀ = 6.62 fm (for all Δrnp values)
- Woods-Saxon radius parameter for ¹⁹⁷Au: R₀ = 6.38 fm (for all Δrnp values)
- Diffuseness parameter for ²⁰⁸Pb with Δrnp = 0 fm: a = 0.546 fm; with Δrnp = 0.17 fm: a = 0.6177 fm; with Δrnp = 0.28 fm: a = 0.661 fm
- Diffuseness parameter for ¹⁹⁷Au with Δrnp = 0 fm: a = 0.535 fm; with Δrnp = 0.17 fm: a = 0.605 fm; with Δrnp = 0.28 fm: a = 0.647 fm
- Collision energies: Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV; Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology of isobar collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at √sNN = 200 GeV, studied by STAR (2021) and Xu et al. (2017)) to semi-isobaric systems (Pb+Pb and Au+Au) at different collision energies, where no exact isobar partner for ²⁰⁸Pb exists in existing data. Previous studies by Li et al. (2019) and Xu et al. (2021) proposed using multiplicity and mean transverse momentum differences in isobar collisions to probe neutron skin and symmetry energy. The present work enables a new constraint on the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb using existing flow data from RHIC and LHC, complementing the parity-violating electron scattering measurements from PREX-II (Adhikari, 2021) and CREX (2022), and the ρ⁰ photoproduction method from STAR (2022).
