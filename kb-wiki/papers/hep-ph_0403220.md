# Mass of the rho^0 meson in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision

> We study the behavior of the rho vector mass in the context of the almost baryon-free environment of an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision. We show that rho scattering within the hadronic phase of the collision leads to a temperature dependent, decrease of its intrinsic mass at rest, compared to the value in vacuum. The main contributions arise from s-channel scattering with pions through the formation of a1 resonances as well as with nucleons through the formation of even parity, spin 3/2 [N(1720)] and 5/2 [Delta(1905)] nucleon resonances. We show that it is possible to achieve a shift in the intrinsic rho^0 mass of order - 40 MeV, when including the contributions of all the relevant mesons and baryons that take part in the scattering, for temperatures between chemical and kinetic freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the intrinsic mass of the rho^0 meson can decrease by up to approximately -40 MeV due to scattering within the hadronic phase of an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision, for temperatures between chemical and kinetic freeze-out. This shift arises from s-channel scattering with pions through a1 resonance formation and with nucleons through N(1720) and Delta(1905) resonance formation, without requiring a drop in the nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop rho meson self-energy using the imaginary-time formulation of thermal field theory, evaluating contributions from scattering with mesons (pions, kaons, and resonances including a1, omega, h1, K1, and pi') and with baryons (nucleons and the resonances N(1520), N(1720), Delta(1700), and Delta(1905)). They used a manifestly covariant formalism with interaction Lagrangians that respect current conservation and parity invariance, and determined coupling constants by fitting experimentally measured branching ratios into the rho-N channel. The thermal mass was extracted from the component Pi^11 of the rho self-energy in the limit of vanishing three-momentum.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic rho^0 mass shifts from 770 MeV (vacuum value) to 764-730 MeV for temperatures between T=120-180 MeV, corresponding to a shift of up to -40 MeV.
- The main baryonic contributions come from N(1720) (spin 3/2+, vacuum decay width into rho-N of 100 MeV, total width 150 MeV, coupling constant f=7) and Delta(1905) (spin 5/2+, vacuum decay width into rho-N of 210 MeV, total width 350 MeV, coupling constant f=12.9).
- The main mesonic contribution for q0 near m_rho comes from s-channel a1(1260) resonance formation (dominant decay into rho-pi, total width ~400 MeV, coupling constant g=13.27 GeV^-1, isospin factor 2).
- The rho-pi-pi coupling constant used was g_rho-pi-pi = 6.06, and the rho-NN coupling constants were f_rho-NN = 2.63 and kappa = 6.1.
- The rho vacuum width was taken as Gamma^vac = 150 MeV, and the rho lifetime is tau ~ 1.3 fm compared to the system lifetime ~ 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of temperature-driven modifications to rho meson properties from interactions with mesons (Rapp, 1999) and from dense nuclear matter effects using non-relativistic approximations (Peters, 1998; Teodorescu, 2000; Friman, 1999). The calculation uses a manifestly covariant formalism rather than non-relativistic approximations, and shows that baryon scattering contributions at finite temperature are significant without invoking a drop in the nucleon mass. The results are relevant for interpreting the STAR collaboration's observation of a -40 MeV shift in p+p and -70 MeV shift in peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, and for distinguishing intrinsic mass changes from phase-space distortions of decay products in dilepton and hadronic decay measurements.
