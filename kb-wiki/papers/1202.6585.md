# Physics Opportunities of a Fixed-Target Experiment using the LHC Beams

> We outline the many physics opportunities offered by a multi-purpose fixed-target experiment using the LHC proton and Pb beams extracted by a bent crystal. In a proton run with the LHC 7-TeV beam, one can analyze pp, pd and pA collisions at sqrt(s_NN)~115 GeV and even higher using the Fermi motion in a nuclear target. In a Pb run with a 2.76 TeV-per-nucleon beam, sqrt(s_NN) is as high as 72 GeV. Bent crystals can be used to extract about 5x10^8 protons/s; the integrated luminosity over a year reaches 0.5fb-1 on a typical 1 cm-long target without species limitation. Such an extraction mode does not alter the performance of the collider experiments at the LHC. By instrumenting the target-rapidity region, gluon and heavy-quark proton and neutron PDFs can be accessed at large x and even at x larger than 1 in the nuclear case. Single diffractive physics and, for the first time, the large negative-xF domain can be accessed. The nuclear target-species versatility provides a unique opportunity to study nuclear matter vs. the features of the hot and dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, which can be studied in PbA collisions over the full range of target-rapidity domain with a large variety of nuclei. The polarization of hydrogen and nuclear targets allows an ambitious spin program, including measurements of the QCD lensing effects which underlie the Sivers single-spin asymmetry, the study of transversity distributions and possibly of polarized PDFs. We also emphasize the potential offered by pA ultra-peripheral collisions where the nucleus target A is used as a coherent photon source, mimicking photoproduction processes in ep collisions. Finally, we note that W and Z bosons can be produced and detected in a fixed-target experiment and in their threshold domain for the first time, providing new ways to probe the partonic content of the proton and the nucleus.

## Key claim

A fixed-target experiment using LHC beams extracted by bent crystals can achieve center-of-mass energies of sqrt(s_NN) ~ 115 GeV in proton runs and ~ 72 GeV in lead runs, with integrated luminosities up to 0.5 fb^-1 per year, without degrading the performance of the LHC collider experiments. The authors argue that such a facility would enable a broad physics program including parton distribution functions at large x, spin physics, nuclear matter studies, and first measurements of W and Z bosons in the threshold domain, though the feasibility depends on successful crystal extraction technology and detector instrumentation.

## Method

The paper surveys the physics potential of a proposed fixed-target facility (AFTER) using slow extraction of LHC proton and lead-ion beams via bent crystals. It calculates achievable luminosities for various target materials (solid H, liquid H, liquid D, Be, Cu, W, Pb) based on extraction rates of 5x10^8 protons/s and 2x10^5 Pb/s, then evaluates the expected yields for quarkonia, Drell-Yan pairs, jets, direct photons, and vector bosons. The analysis covers the kinematic reach in rapidity and Feynman x_F, particularly the backward (target-rapidity) region, and discusses specific measurements for gluon PDFs, intrinsic charm, Sivers asymmetries, quark-gluon plasma diagnostics, and ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for 7 TeV proton beam on fixed target: sqrt(s_NN) ~ 115 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy for 2.76 TeV per nucleon Pb beam on fixed target: sqrt(s_NN) ~ 72 GeV
- Extracted proton rate: 5x10^8 protons/s using bent crystal
- Extracted Pb ion rate: 2x10^5 Pb/s
- Integrated luminosity per year for proton beam on 1 cm solid hydrogen target: 260 pb^-1 yr^-1
- Integrated luminosity per year for proton beam on 1 cm tungsten target: 310 pb^-1 yr^-1
- Integrated luminosity per year for Pb beam on 1 cm solid hydrogen target: 11 nb^-1 yr^-1
- Expected J/psi yield per unit rapidity at y=0 for 10 cm solid hydrogen target: 5.2x10^7 yr^-1
- Expected Upsilon yield per unit rapidity at y=0 for 10 cm solid hydrogen target: 1.0x10^5 yr^-1
- Expected J/psi yield per unit rapidity at y=0 for 1 cm tungsten target: 1.1x10^9 yr^-1
- Expected Upsilon yield per unit rapidity at y=0 for 1 cm tungsten target: 2.3x10^6 yr^-1
- Center-of-mass rapidity boost for 7 TeV proton beam: y_cms = 4.8
- Accessible rapidity range in laboratory frame: -4.8 <= y_cms <= 1
- Fermi motion in nuclear target induces approximately 10% spread in sqrt(s)

## Context

This paper extends the concept of fixed-target experiments using LHC beams, building on earlier proposals for crystal extraction tested at SPS by Scandale (2011) and Uggerhoj (2005). It complements the physics programs of RHIC (pp and dAu collisions at 200 GeV) and the LHC collider experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) by providing access to the backward rapidity region and large negative x_F domain not covered by collider configurations. The proposed facility would enable measurements of gluon PDFs at large x where current constraints from CT10 global analysis are poor, test intrinsic charm models motivated by EMC measurements by Harris (1995), and study Sivers asymmetries in Drell-Yan to test the sign-change prediction relative to SIDIS measurements from HERMES by Airapetian (2004) and COMPASS by Alexakhin (2005).
