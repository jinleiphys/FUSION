# On the $M_T$ scaling of dilepton spectra in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The so-called $M_T$ scaling for dilepton spectra at RHIC energies is examined. It is seen that a proper accounting of the complete set of dilepton producing processes forces us to abandon the proposed scaling around $M_T$ = 2.6 GeV, which has been put forward as a possible signature of the presence of quark-matter. Any substantial transverse expansion in the QGP phase itself will also offset this scaling behaviour. The rates of lepton pair production are time--integrated in Bjorken hydrodynamics and the different sources are compared against each other.

## Key claim

The proposed \(M_T\) scaling of dilepton spectra around \(M_T = 2.6\) GeV, previously suggested as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, does not hold once a complete set of dilepton-producing processes in hadronic matter is included; the scaling is also offset by any substantial transverse expansion in the QGP phase itself.

## Method

The authors time-integrated dilepton production rates using Bjorken longitudinal hydrodynamics for head-on collisions of gold nuclei, assuming a first-order phase transition from QGP to hadronic matter. They compared the standard assumption of only \(\pi^+\pi^- \rightarrow l^+l^-\) in the hadronic phase against a full set of hadronic processes including decays and two-body reactions of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons (\(\pi, \eta, \rho, \omega, \eta^\prime, \phi, K, K^*\)) and the \(a_1\) meson, using effective chiral Lagrangian models. The Drell-Yan contribution was also calculated for lead-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200A\) GeV using Duke-Owens structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The full hadronic rate scales as \(\sim T K_1(M/T)\) to high accuracy between \(M = 0.3\) GeV and \(3\) GeV, with a small breaking above \(M = 2.5\) GeV due to the \(a_1 a_1 \rightarrow e^+ e^-\) threshold.
- For initial conditions \(T_0 = 250\) MeV and \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm/c, with critical temperature \(T_c = 160\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV, the total dilepton yield at fixed \(M_T = 2.6\) GeV shows no scaling when all hadronic processes are included; the QGP contribution is only a small fraction of the total.
- For a high initial temperature \(T_0 = 500\) MeV and \(\tau_0 = 1/3T_0\), the invariant mass window for QGP emissions is pushed beyond \(2\) GeV, but the QGP signal is still not significantly larger than the Drell-Yan or hadronic contributions.
- Including only pion annihilation, the quark annihilation dominates beyond \(M \approx 1.5\) GeV by two orders of magnitude; with the full hadronic set, the enhancement factor is 10–50 compared to pion-only rates up to \(M \approx 3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Gale (1994) which first included a complete set of light mesons for dilepton rates in hot hadronic matter, showing that the safe window for QGP dileptons is pushed to higher invariant masses. It directly contradicts the proposal by Ko (1993) that \(M_T\) scaling around 2.6 GeV could serve as a clean QGP signature. The results also align with Parton Cascade Model studies by Geiger and collaborators (1994) which found strong violation of \(M_T\) scaling even at the parton level due to lack of equilibration. The paper enables future work to focus on more realistic hydrodynamic treatments including transverse expansion and to carefully disentangle QGP signals from hadronic and Drell-Yan backgrounds at RHIC energies.
