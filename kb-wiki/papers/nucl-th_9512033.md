# An Effective Lagrangian with Broken Scale and Chiral Symmetry II: Pion Phenomenology

> We extend an effective Lagrangian embodying broken scale and chiral symmetry to include explicit chiral symmetry breaking and an additional chiral invariant term which allows for an axial coupling constant greater than unity. We also include a chiral Lagrangian for the isotriplet vector mesons which leads to a renormalization of the pion field. The properties of nuclear matter and nuclei, low energy $πN$ scattering and the behavior of quantities such as the pion mass and axial coupling at finite density are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed effective Lagrangian that incorporates broken scale and chiral symmetry to include explicit chiral symmetry breaking and an additional chiral invariant term, enabling the axial coupling constant to exceed unity; the authors find that this extended model can describe nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, but the predicted density dependence of vector meson masses (only 3% reduction at saturation) is significantly smaller than QCD sum rule estimates of roughly 20±10%.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian with broken scale and chiral symmetry (from their previous paper I) that includes nucleons, sigma and pion fields, a glueball field, and omega vector mesons, with vector meson masses generated by coupling to the glueball field. They add explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms to give the pion a mass, introduce a chiral invariant term proportional to a constant D to raise the axial coupling above unity, and include isotriplet vector and axial vector mesons (rho and a1) in a linear chiral model, which leads to pi-a1 mixing and renormalization of the pion field. The model is then applied to calculate nuclear matter properties, finite nuclei (oxygen, calcium, lead), low-energy pi-N scattering amplitudes, and the density dependence of quantities such as the pion mass and axial coupling.

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.148 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per particle at saturation: 16 MeV
- Compression modulus K: in the range 200-300 MeV (current estimates), with the G4>0 case favored
- Effective mass ratio at saturation M*/M: 0.73±0.09 (QCD sum rule estimate), with the G4>0 case giving better agreement
- Vector self-energy of nucleon in nuclear matter g_omega*omega_0: 0.22 GeV (G4=0) and 0.28 GeV (G4>0), compared to QCD sum rule estimate of 0.31±0.06 GeV
- Vacuum energy: in good agreement with QCD sum rule estimates of (240 MeV)^4
- Larger scalar mass m_> = 1.5 GeV (fixed)
- Lower scalar mass m_< approximately 0.5 GeV

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: approximately 45 MeV with ~15% error (Gasser et al. estimate), with best fits giving reasonable values

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Carter, Ellis, Rudaz, referred to as I) which described nuclear matter and finite nuclei with an effective Lagrangian incorporating broken scale and chiral symmetry, by now including explicit chiral symmetry breaking, an axial coupling constant greater than unity, and pi-a1 mixing. The approach contrasts with the standard linear sigma model supplemented by omega meson repulsion, which has long been known (Kerman) not to yield a normal saturating equation of state, and with models that generate vector meson masses through coupling to the sigma field or include one-loop vacuum corrections, which fail to reproduce observed nuclear properties (Carter, Ellis, Rudaz; Furnstahl, Serot, Tang). The results enable further testing of the Lagrangian against pion phenomenology and predict a density dependence of vector meson masses that is much weaker than suggested by QCD sum rule estimates (QCD sum rules) and the scaling arguments of Brown and Rho.
