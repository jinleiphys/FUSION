# Charge-Exchange Reaction pD->n(pp) in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> The deuteron charge - exchange reaction pD->n(pp) for the low values of momentum transfer and small excitation energies of final pp - pair is considered in the framework of Bethe - Salpeter approach. The method of calculation of the observables is developed for the case, when the pp - pair is in $^1S_0$ - state. The methodical numerical calculations of the differetial cross sections and tensor analysing powers are presented. The reaction under consideration is predicted to be a solid base for construction of the deuteron tensor polarimeter at high energies, and also to obtain some additional information about elementary nucleon - nucleon charge - exchange amplitude.

# Charge-Exchange Reaction pD→n(pp) in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

## Key claim
The deuteron charge-exchange reaction pD→n(pp) can serve as a basis for a deuteron tensor polarimeter at high energies (up to proton momenta of 2.5 GeV/c at COSY), but only if the final pp pair is assumed to be in a ¹S₀ state and the plane-wave approximation is used; the model shows qualitative agreement with low-energy Saclay data but systematically underestimates cross sections at the lowest excitation energies (0–1 MeV), indicating that final-state interactions are not negligible.

## Method
The authors compute differential cross sections and tensor analyzing powers for the reaction pD→n(pp) using the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, with the deuteron BS amplitude obtained from a realistic kernel with six one-meson exchanges. The final pp pair is restricted to a ¹S₀ state (spin-singlet, J=0), and the elementary charge-exchange subprocess is treated via on-shell helicity amplitudes from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and the SAID parameterization, with the necessary Lorentz boosts and Wick helicity rotations applied. The plane-wave approximation is used for the pp pair, neglecting final-state interactions.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfer range: 0 ≤ |t| ≤ 0.16 (GeV/c)²
- Excitation energy bins for the pp pair: I: 0 ≤ E_x ≤ 1 MeV; II: 1 MeV ≤ E_x ≤ 4 MeV; III: 4 MeV ≤ E_x ≤ 8 MeV
- Invariant mass range of the pp pair: 4m² ≤ s_p ≤ (2m + 8 MeV)², corresponding to excitation energies up to 8 MeV
- Initial proton momenta used for comparison with Saclay data: |p| = 444 and 599 MeV/c
- Predicted initial proton momentum for COSY energies: |p| = 2.5 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier experimental and theoretical studies of the pD→n(pp) reaction performed at Saclay (SATURN-II) for deuteron kinetic energies of 200 and 350 MeV, where a deuteron tensor polarimeter was successfully constructed (Polder, 1990s). The authors re-examine the reaction for higher proton beam energies available at the COSY synchrotron in Jülich, following the basic ideas of earlier Bethe-Salpeter approaches (Wilbois, 1990s; Dorkin, 1990s). The plane-wave results show that final-state interactions must be included for quantitative agreement at low excitation energies, and that higher partial waves (triplet states of the pp pair) may be needed at higher excitation energies. The predictions suggest the reaction remains viable for polarimetry at COSY energies.
