# Short-range correlations in finite nuclear systems

> Recent results concerning the use of the Correlated Basis Function to investigate the ground state properties of medium-heavy doubly magic nuclei with microscopic interactions are presented. The calculations have been done by considering a Short-Range Correlation between nucleons. The possibility of identifying effects produced by Short-Range Correlations in electromagnetically induced phenomena is discussed.

# Short-range correlations in finite nuclear systems

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) are necessary to tame the repulsive core of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions in microscopic calculations of medium-heavy doubly magic nuclei, but their effects on electromagnetic observables are small and often masked by meson exchange currents (MEC) and final state interactions (FSI), making clear experimental identification difficult.

## Method

The author uses the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory with a variational ansatz where the nuclear ground state is a Slater determinant multiplied by a many-body correlation function with operatorial dependence up to eight channels. The Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) approach extended to finite systems, with the Single Operator Chain approximation and inclusion of elementary diagrams (FHNC-1), is used to evaluate ground state properties of 16O and 40Ca with the Argonne V8' two-body interaction plus the Urbana IV three-body force. For excited states and electromagnetic responses, the author retains only diagrams containing a single correlation line (two-body and three-body cluster diagrams), neglecting collective effects.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 16O: full minimization gives -5.48 MeV, correlation-only minimization gives -5.41 MeV, experimental value is -7.97 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca: full minimization gives -6.97 MeV, correlation-only minimization gives -6.64 MeV, experimental value is -8.55 MeV
- Sum rules exhausted at the 0.1% level when a specific elementary diagram is included (FHNC-1 approximation)
- With the Single Operator Chain approximation, sum rules exhausted at about the 5% level
- Differences between full and approximate calculations of nuclear matter charge responses are of order 10^{-5}
- FSI reduces maximum values of inclusive responses by a 20-30% factor

## Context

This work extends the FHNC theory from infinite nuclear matter and light nuclei (up to A=8 via Monte Carlo) to medium-heavy doubly magic nuclei 16O and 40Ca using realistic interactions. It builds on prior CBF developments by Clark (1979), Fantoni and Pandharipande (1987), and earlier finite-nucleus FHNC applications by Co' (1992) and Arias de Saavedra (1996). The results show that SRC effects on electromagnetic observables are small compared to FSI and MEC, suggesting that two-nucleon emission processes may be more promising for isolating SRC signatures, though the Delta excitation contribution remains a complicating factor.
