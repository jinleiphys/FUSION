# Nuclear structure studies of double Gamow-Teller strength

> The double Gamow-Teller strength distributions in even-$A$ Calcium isotopes were calculated using the nuclear shell model by applying the single Gamow-Teller operator two times sequentially on the ground state of the parent nucleus. The number of intermediate states actually contributing to the results was determined. The sum rules for the double Gamow-Teller operator in the full calculation were approximately fulfilled. In the case that the symmetry is restored approximately by introducing degeneracies of the $f$-levels, and the $p$-levels in the $fp$-model space, the agreement with the sum rules was very close.

## Key claim

The double Gamow-Teller (DGT) strength distributions in even-A Calcium isotopes were calculated using the nuclear shell model by applying the single Gamow-Teller operator two times sequentially, and the sum rules for the DGT operator were approximately fulfilled in the full calculation; only when the symmetry is approximately restored by introducing degeneracies of the f-levels and p-levels in the fp-model space does the agreement with the sum rules become very close.

## Method

The nuclear shell model code NuShellX@MSU was used with the FPD6 and KB3G interactions in the complete fp-model space to obtain wave functions for the initial ground state, intermediate 1+ states, and final 0+ and 2+ states in even-A Calcium isotopes. The single Gamow-Teller operator was applied two times sequentially: first from the parent nucleus 0+ ground state to all 1+ intermediate states, then from each 1+ intermediate state to each 0+ or 2+ final state in the daughter nucleus. The maximum number of intermediate states was 1000, and calculations were done up to 5000 final states when the total number exceeded 5000.

## Key numbers

- For 42Ca, the DGT total strength S(DGT) for 0+ final states is 28.1 (FPD6), with a sum rule upper limit of 36; for 2+ final states, S(DGT) is 19.5 with a sum rule lower limit of 0.
- For 44Ca, S(DGT) for 0+ is 102.0 (sum rule upper limit 120); for 2+ is 284.0 (sum rule lower limit 240).
- For 46Ca, S(DGT) for 0+ is 223.7 (sum rule upper limit 252); for 2+ is 752.6 (sum rule lower limit 720).
- For 48Ca, S(DGT) for 0+ is 385.0 (sum rule upper limit 432).
- The B1 strength (transition to the first final state) for 42Ca 0+ is 16.172 (FPD6) and 17.010 (KB3G); for 48Ca 0+, B1 is 0.109 (FPD6) and 0.072 (KB3G).
- The average energy for 46Ca 0+ is 21.2 MeV (FPD6) and 19.0 MeV (KB3G); for 48Ca 0+, the average energy is 24.6 MeV (FPD6) and 21.9 MeV (KB3G).
- About 500 intermediate 1+ states in 48Sc actually contribute to the final DGT results, although the total number of 1+ states is many thousands.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of DGT strength in even-A Neon isotopes by Muto (1992) and the recent calculation for 48Ca by another group (2018), using a different method where the single GT operator is applied sequentially rather than the approach used in those earlier works. The results are relevant for interpreting double charge-exchange reactions with light ions, particularly the NUMEN program in Catania and the RIKEN program, and for calibrating nuclear matrix elements for double beta-decay (both 2-nu and 0-nu modes). The study shows that the DGT distributions have multiple peaks (not single-peaked), and the DGT giant resonance in 48Ca appears at energies around 20-30 MeV, consistent with a recent experimental peak at 25 MeV excitation in the 56Fe(11B,11Li) reaction reported by Ejiri (2017).
