# Low lying eigenmodes and meson propagator symmetries

> In situations where the low lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator are suppressed one observed degeneracies of some meson masses. Based on these results a hidden symmetry was conjectured, which is not a symmetry of the Lagrangian but emerges in the quantization process. We show here how the difference between classes of meson propagators is governed by the low modes and shrinks when they disappear.

## Key claim

The paper shows analytically that the difference between classes of meson propagators is governed by the low-lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator, and that these differences shrink when those modes are suppressed; however, the emergence of the full CS (chiral-spin) symmetry requires additional conditions beyond just the suppression of low modes, specifically for the "h-equivalent" propagator pairs.

## Method

The author uses the spectral representation of the Euclidean Dirac operator to express meson propagators as sums over eigenmodes, then derives analytic expressions for the differences between propagator pairs for various Dirac matrix structures (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial vector). The analysis separates the eigenvalue sums into two weight functions, g(m,eta) and h(m,eta), which determine how propagator differences depend on the eigenvalue density and the overlap matrix elements of the eigenvectors.

## Key numbers

* The weight function g(m,eta) = m/(m^2 + eta^2) is peaked at eta=0 and approaches (pi/2)delta(eta) for small masses m -> 0; propagator differences weighted by g vanish if there is a gap in the eigenvalue density at low eta.
* The weight function h(m,eta) = eta/(m^2 + eta^2) is peaked at eta=m and falls like 1/eta for large eta; propagator differences weighted by h require further constraints beyond a low-eigenvalue gap to vanish.
* For the U_A(1) susceptibility difference, the expression is chi(1) - chi(i gamma_5) = - (4/V) sum_{n>0} g(m,eta_n)^2, which vanishes if the eigenvalue density rho(m,eta) behaves as O(eta^3) or faster for eta -> 0.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD observations by Denissenya et al. (2014, 2015) and Glozman (2015) that meson masses become degenerate when low-lying Dirac eigenmodes are artificially removed, and by Rohrhofer et al. (2017) showing similar degeneracies at high temperature. The paper provides an analytic foundation for these numerical results, showing that the axial symmetries SU(2)_A and U(1)_A are restored when low modes are suppressed, while the full CS symmetry requires additional dynamical conditions on the eigenvector overlaps. The approach builds on the Banks-Casher relation (1979) and earlier work on scalar-pseudoscalar susceptibilities by Cohen (1996) and Aoki et al. (2012).
