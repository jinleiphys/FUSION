# Role of relativity and nucleon compositeness in few-body systems

> Recent progress is reviewed in four areas where new experimental data have been obtained: proton-proton bremsstrahlung, threshold pion production in proton-proton collisions, elastic electron-deuteron scattering and deuteron photodisintegration at several GeV photon energy. Relativistic effects are expected to be significant in these processes. High energy photodisintegration suggests that partonic behavior could be relevant in subamplitudes at large momentum transfer.

# Role of relativity and nucleon compositeness in few-body systems

## Key claim

Relativistic effects are expected to be significant in few-body dynamics when momenta are comparable to the nucleon mass, but identifying a relativistic effect is subtle because different formalisms (e.g., spectator, equal-time, light-front) can shuffle the same physics between wave functions, currents, and contact terms; equivalent results should be found if each formalism is fully evaluated with consistent currents.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical progress in four few-body reactions: proton-proton bremsstrahlung at 190–389 MeV, threshold neutral-pion production in proton-proton collisions, elastic electron-deuteron scattering up to Q² = 6 (GeV/c)², and deuteron photodisintegration at photon energies up to 4 GeV. For each process, the author compares experimental data to calculations from relativistic meson-exchange models, chiral perturbation theory, soft-photon approximations, and various three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (spectator, equal-time, light-front), assessing how well each formalism describes the data and where ambiguities remain.

## Key numbers

- Bremsstrahlung: 190 MeV polarized proton beam at KVI; typical photon energy ~60 MeV; data at 389 MeV from RCNP Osaka and at 300 MeV from COSY.
- Threshold π⁰ production: cross sections for pp → ppπ⁰ were five times larger than existing theoretical predictions (Koltun 1966, Miller 1991); spin observables measured at Indiana University Cyclotron Facility allow separation of s-wave and p-wave contributions.
- Elastic e-D scattering: new A(Q²) data extend to Q² = 6 (GeV/c)²; t₂₀ data up to Q² ≈ 1.5 (GeV/c)² are well described by relativistic impulse approximation; B(Q²) minimum near Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)² shows a sign change in ρπγ exchange-current contribution near Q² ≈ 1 (GeV/c)².
- Deuteron photodisintegration: photon energies up to 4 GeV at Jefferson Laboratory; cross sections at θ_cm = 90° exhibit s¹¹ dσ/dt ≈ constant scaling; at E_γ = 4 GeV and θ_cm = 90°, momentum transfer r² ≈ -3.7 (GeV/c)²; induced polarization tends to zero for E_γ > 1 GeV at θ_cm = 90°; polarization transfer observables tend to zero for E_γ > 2 GeV.

## Context

This review extends an earlier review by Wallace (1997) and covers work by Arenhövel, Ritz, and Wilbois (2000) on consistent currents with 1/M corrections; Gross and Riska (1987), Adam, Van Orden, and Gross (1997), and Blankleider and Kvinkhidze (1998) on spectator-formalism currents; Phillips and Wallace (1998) on equal-time formalism currents; and Carbonell, Desplanques, Karmanov, and Mathiot (1998) on light-front currents. The bremsstrahlung discussion addresses Fearing's critique (1998, 1999) that off-shell effects are ambiguous due to field transformations. The threshold pion production section builds on work by Lee and Riska (1993) and the Jülich group (Hanhart 1995, 1998, 2000). The photodisintegration results connect to the scaling prediction from perturbative QCD (Isgur 1980, 1989; Radyushkin 1984) and to the hard pn scattering model of Frankfurt, Miller, Sargsian, and Strikman. The review identifies open questions: the magnetic form factor puzzle in e-D scattering, the precise nature of short-range contributions in threshold pion production, and whether a transition from hadronic to partonic interactions occurs in deuteron photodisintegration.
