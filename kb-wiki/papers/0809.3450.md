# Non-Gaussian fluctuations near the QCD critical point

> We study the effect of the QCD critical point on non-Gaussian moments (cumulants) of fluctuations of experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions. We find that these moments are very sensitive to the proximity of the critical point, as measured by the magnitude of the correlation length xi. For example, the cubic central moment of multiplicity ~ xi^4.5 and the quartic cumulant ~ xi^7. We estimate the magnitude of critical point contributions to non-Gaussian fluctuations of pion and proton multiplicities.

# Non-Gaussian fluctuations near the QCD critical point

## Key claim
Higher-order non-Gaussian moments (cumulants) of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations are much more sensitive to the proximity of the QCD critical point than quadratic variance measures, scaling as higher powers of the correlation length ξ, but the magnitude of the effect is subject to large uncertainty from the poorly known coupling constant G of the critical mode to observed particles.

## Method
The author calculates the critical point contribution to the cubic and quartic cumulants of multiplicity fluctuations by expanding the effective free energy functional for the critical mode σ in powers of σ and gradients, then computing the leading singular contributions to n-particle correlators via diagrammatic methods. The results are applied to pion and proton multiplicities by estimating the coupling constants λ₃, λ₄, and G using universal Ising-class scaling relations and previous estimates from Stephanov (1999).

## Key numbers
- Cubic central moment of multiplicity scales as ⟨(δN)³⟩ ∼ ξ⁴·⁵
- Quartic cumulant scales as ⟨(δN)⁴⟩_c ∼ ξ⁷
- For pions at T ≈ 120 MeV: ω₃(N_π)_σ ≈ 1. × (λ̃₃/4) × (G/300 MeV)³ × (ξ/3 fm)^(9/2)
- For pions at T ≈ 120 MeV: ω₄(N_π)_σ ≈ 12. × ((2λ̃₃²−λ̃₄)/50) × (G/300 MeV)⁴ × (ξ/3 fm)⁷
- For protons at SPS freeze-out (T, μ_B) ≈ (168, 266) MeV: ω₃(N_p)_σ ≈ 6. × (λ̃₃/4) × (g/10)³ × (ξ/1 fm)^(9/2)
- For protons at SPS freeze-out: ω₄(N_p)_σ ≈ 46. × ((2λ̃₃²−λ̃₄)/50) × (g/10)⁴ × (ξ/1 fm)⁷
- The correlation length ξ is limited to at most 2–3 fm due to finite-time effects from critical slowing down (Berdnikov 2000)
- For a gas of classical free particles (Poisson distribution), ω₃(N) = 1; Bose statistics gives approximately 1.3 for pions at T = 120 MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of critical point signatures in heavy-ion collisions beyond quadratic fluctuation measures (Stephanov 1999) by showing that non-Gaussian cumulants scale with higher powers of the correlation length ξ (ξ^4.5 and ξ^7 versus ξ^2 for variances), making them more sensitive probes. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC, SPS, and FAIR through energy scans that look for non-monotonous behavior of ω₃ and ω₄ as a function of collision energy √s, though the author notes that other sources (initial fluctuations, flow, jets) also contribute and must be separated via the energy scan. The scaling exponents agree with critical exponents of baryon number cumulants from universality (Hatta 2003).
