# Baryonic Resonances from Baryon Decuplet-Meson Octet Interactions and the Exotic Resonance S=1, I=1, J^P=3/2-

> Using the lowest order chiral Lagrangian we study s-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons. We find two bound states in the flavour SU(3) limit corresponding to the octet and decuplet representations. These are found to split into eight different trajectories in the complex plane when the SU(3) symmetry is broken gradually. Finally, we are able to provide a reasonable description for a good number of 4-star 3/2- resonances listed by the Particle Data Group. In particular, the Xi(1820), the Lambda(1520) and the Sigma(1670) states are well reproduced. We predict a few other resonances and also evaluate the couplings of the observed resonances to the various channels from the residues at the poles of the scattering matrix from where partial decay widths into different channels can be evaluated.

# Baryonic Resonances from Baryon Decuplet-Meson Octet Interactions and the Exotic Resonance S=1, I=1, J^P=3/2-

## Key claim

Using only the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) with no free parameters beyond a natural-sized subtraction constant, the authors dynamically generate multiple 4-star 3/2- resonances as s-wave bound states of baryon decuplet and pseudoscalar meson octet, including the exotic S=1, I=1 state; however, the identification with known resonances relies on matching pole positions and couplings to channels, and some generated states have no observed counterpart.

## Method

The tree-level scattering amplitude from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian was used as the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain a unitarized transition matrix in coupled channels, with dimensional regularization and a single subtraction constant of natural size. Poles of the scattering matrix were searched in the complex plane across different Riemann sheets, starting from an SU(3) symmetric limit where all baryon and meson masses were equalized, then gradually breaking SU(3) symmetry to reach physical masses. Residues at the poles provided couplings to each channel, from which partial decay widths into decuplet baryon plus meson were evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda(1520), Sigma(1670), and Xi(1820) resonances are well reproduced by the model.
- A resonance found in S=-3, I=0 is associated with the Omega(2250) based on quantum numbers, pole position, and compatibility of the calculated partial decay width with the total width of the Omega(2250).
- A predicted Delta(1500) resonance has a width around 300 MeV.
- A predicted Xi(2160) resonance has a width of about 40 MeV.
- The exotic S=1, I=1 state appears as a pole in the unphysical Riemann sheet and produces Delta K cross sections much larger than the corresponding Delta K cross sections in I=2.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to baryon octet-meson octet s-wave scattering, which successfully described J^P=1/2- resonances such as the N*(1535), Lambda(1405), Lambda(1670), Sigma(1620), and Xi(1620) (Kaiser, Ramos, Oller; Inoue; Jido; Carmen; Bennhold). The method was suggested by Lutz as suitable for studying d-wave resonances via s-wave interactions of the meson octet with the baryon decuplet. The results enable predictions for partial decay widths into decuplet baryon plus meson channels that can be tested experimentally, and suggest that the exotic S=1, I=1 state could be observed in Delta K production in pp -> Lambda Delta K and pp -> Sigma Delta K reactions.
