# Can Tsallis distribution fit all the particle spectra produced at RHIC and LHC?

> The Tsallis distribution has been tested to fit the all particle spectra at mid-rapidity from central events produced in d+Au, Cu+Cu, Au+Au collisions at RHIC and p+Pb, Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. Even though there are strong medium effects in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions, the results show that the Tsallis distribution can be used to fit most of particle spectra in the collisions studied except in Au+Au collisions where some deviations are seen for proton and $Λ$ at low $p_T$. In addition, as the Tsallis distribution can only fit part of the particle spectra produced in Pb+Pb collisions where $p_T$ is up to 20 GeV/c, a new formula with one more fitting degree of freedom is proposed in order to reproduce the entire $p_T$ region.

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution can fit most particle spectra from d+Au, Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and p+Pb collisions, but it fails for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies where it can only fit part of the spectrum (up to about 10 GeV/c in the low-\(p_T\) region or starting from 4 GeV/c in the high-\(p_T\) region), and shows deviations for proton and \(\Lambda\) at low \(p_T\) in Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors collected published experimental data for identified particle transverse momentum spectra at mid-rapidity from the most central collisions in d+Au (200 GeV), Cu+Cu (200 GeV), Au+Au (62.4 and 200 GeV) at RHIC and p+Pb (5.02 TeV) and Pb+Pb (2.76 TeV) at LHC, selecting data with the highest \(p_T > 5\) GeV/c for most particles. They fitted the data using the Tsallis distribution in the form \((E d^3N/dp^3)_{|\eta|<a} = A (1 + E_T/(nT))^{-n}\) where \(E_T = m_T - m\), and evaluated fit quality with a metric \(M^2 = \sum_i [1 - y_i(\text{fit})/y_i(\text{data})]^2\). For Pb+Pb collisions where the Tsallis distribution failed, they proposed a new four-parameter formula \(A \exp[-(b/T)\arctan(E_T/b)] / [1 + (E_T/b)^4]^c\).

## Key numbers

- In d+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the Tsallis distribution fits all particles with \(\chi^2/\text{ndf}\) values ranging from 1.54/18 (for \(K^+\)) to 30.66/21 (for \(\gamma\)); the charged particle spectrum extends up to \(p_T = 45\) GeV/c.
- In Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the Tsallis distribution yields \(\chi^2/\text{ndf} = 54866.3/26\) for protons and 2739.02/18 for \(\Lambda\), indicating significant deviations at low \(p_T\).
- In Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the Tsallis distribution can only fit the low \(p_T\) region up to about 10 GeV/c or the high \(p_T\) region starting from 4 GeV/c, but not the full spectrum up to 20 GeV/c.
- The proposed new formula fits Pb+Pb data with \(\chi^2/\text{ndf}\) values from 1.84/4 (for \(\phi\)) to 174.54/59 (for charged pions) across all particles.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of Tsallis distribution fits in p+p collisions (Zheng and Zhu, 2015) to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It confirms that the Tsallis distribution works well for pA systems where nuclear modification factors are near unity, as seen in d+Au and p+Pb data from PHENIX and ALICE. The failure in Pb+Pb collisions motivates a new fitting formula inspired by the Fokker-Planck equation solution, following the same logic as the historical transition from exponential to Tsallis distributions when intermediate \(p_T\) data became available. This enables future systematic analysis of particle spectra in p+p and pA collisions using the Tsallis distribution, while the new formula provides a tool for describing the full \(p_T\) range in Pb+Pb collisions.
