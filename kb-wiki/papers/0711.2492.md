# Heavy Baryons in a Quark Model

> A quark model is applied to the spectrum of baryons containing heavy quarks. The model gives masses for the known heavy baryons that are in agreement with experiment, but for the doubly-charmed baryon Cascade_{cc}, the model prediction is too heavy. Mixing between the Cascade_Q and Cascade_Q^\prime states is examined and is found to be small for the lowest lying states. In contrast with this, mixing between the Cascade_{bc} and Cascade_{bc}^\prime states is found to be large, and the implication of this mixing for properties of these states is briefly discussed. We also examine heavy-quark spin-symmetry multiplets, and find that many states in the model can be placed in such multiplets. We compare our predictions with those of a number of other authors.

## Key claim

The quark model reproduces the masses of known heavy baryons, but for the doubly-charmed baryon Xi_cc the model prediction is too heavy compared to the experimental candidate mass of 3.519 GeV reported by the SELEX Collaboration, a state that itself lacks confirmation from BaBar, Belle, and Focus.

## Method

A non-relativistic quark model Hamiltonian similar to that of Isgur and Karl (1978-1981) and Copley, Isgur, and Karl (1979) was applied to baryons containing one or more heavy quarks. The model allowed mixing between SU(3) antitriplet and sextet states for Xi_c and Xi_b baryons, and between Xi_bc and Xi'_bc states, without restricting the multiplet structure a priori, and examined heavy-quark spin-symmetry multiplets for states up to J^P = 7/2^+.

## Key numbers

* The model gives masses for known singly-charmed baryons in agreement with experiment: Lambda_c at 2.285 GeV, Sigma_c at 2.455 GeV, Xi_c at 2.469 GeV, and Omega_c at 2.698 GeV (all J^P = 1/2^+).
* For the doubly-charmed baryon Xi_cc, the SELEX Collaboration reported a candidate mass of 3.519 GeV, but most theoretical models predict the lowest-lying doubly-charmed states to be about 100 MeV heavier.
* The Xi_c states at 2.468 and 2.471 GeV are assigned as predominantly antitriplet, while the states at 2.576 and 2.578 GeV are predominantly sextet.
* The Sigma_b states are given as 5.812 GeV (1/2^+) and 5.833 GeV (3/2^+), and Lambda_b at 5.624 GeV (1/2^+).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic quark model approach of Isgur and Karl (1978-1981) and Copley, Isgur, and Karl (1979) to heavy baryons, and compares predictions with a wide range of other theoretical treatments including quark models by Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin (2005-2007), Silvestre-Brac (1996), and Karliner, Keren-Zur, Lipkin, and Rosner (2007), as well as lattice QCD results from Flynn, Mescia, and Tariq (2003) and Na and Gottlieb (2007). The results enable future studies of mixing effects in double-heavy baryon decays and tests of heavy-quark spin-symmetry multiplet assignments.
