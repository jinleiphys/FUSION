# Collective Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions from AGS to SPS

> Collective transverse flow in heavy-ion collisions at incident energies E_{lab} = (1 -160)A GeV is analyzed within the model of 3-fluid dynamics (3FD). Simulations are performed with purely hadronic equation of state (EoS). At the AGS energies the flow turns out to be sensitive to the stopping power of nuclear matter rather than only to the stiffness of the EoS. When the stopping power is fixed to reproduce other observables, the flow data favor more and more soft EoS with the incident energy rise, which can be associated with ``a transition from hadronic to string matter'' reported in the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) model. Problems, which are met in simultaneous reproduction of directed and elliptic flows within the 3FD, suggest that the transverse flow is very sensitive to the character of the transverse-momentum nonequilibrium at the initial stage of collision. Arguments in favor of "early-stage" nature of the flow observable are put forward. This suggests that the flow (especially the directed one) is determined by early-stage evolution of the collision rather than freeze-out stage.

## Key claim

The paper argues that collective transverse flow in heavy-ion collisions from AGS to SPS energies is sensitive to both the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and the stopping power of nuclear matter, but that the simultaneous reproduction of directed and elliptic flows within the 3-fluid dynamics model is problematic, suggesting that the flow is determined by early-stage nonequilibrium in transverse-momentum distribution rather than by freeze-out stage.

## Method

The authors used the 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions at incident energies E_lab = (1-160)A GeV, employing a purely hadronic equation of state with various incompressibilities (hard K=380 MeV, intermediate K=210 MeV, soft K=130 MeV, extrasoft K=100 MeV, and a gas EoS). They computed directed flow (⟨p_x⟩ and v_1) and elliptic flow (v_2) for nucleons/protons and pions, comparing results to experimental data from Bevalac, AGS (E895, E877), and SPS (NA49) collaborations, and also performed conventional 1-fluid dynamics (1FD) simulations for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range analyzed: E_lab = (1-160)A GeV
- Incompressibilities of hadronic EoS versions: K = 380 MeV (hard), 210 MeV (intermediate), 130 MeV (soft), 100 MeV (extrasoft)
- Normal nuclear density: n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}; binding energy: -16 MeV
- Impact parameters used: b = 2 fm for Au+Au at AGS energies, b = 2.5 fm for Pb+Pb at SPS energies, b = 6 fm for mid-central flow calculations, b = 5.6 fm for SPS v_1 and v_2 calculations
- At E_lab = 8A GeV, the extrasoft EoS (K=100 MeV) is preferable for directed flow; at E_lab = 10.5A GeV, it is difficult to choose between soft and extrasoft EoS
- At SPS energies (40A and 158A GeV), proton v_1 data favor the extrasoft EoS, while the gas EoS produces a wiggle in proton v_1 not observed experimentally
- For elliptic flow at AGS energies (2A, 4A, 6A GeV), the hard EoS reasonably reproduces v_2, while soft and intermediate EoS are off the data
- At SPS energies, stiffness of the EoS only weakly affects v_2; the 3FD model overestimates proton v_2 at both 40A and 158A GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 3FD simulations (Russkikh and Ivanov, 2005) which used an intermediate hadronic EoS to reproduce rapidity distributions, transverse-mass spectra, and multiplicities, but failed to describe transverse flow at E_lab ≥ 40A GeV. The analysis contrasts with microscopic models such as JAM (Ivanov et al., 2005), UrQMD (Bleicher et al., 1999), and HSD (Zabrodin et al., 2004), which achieve softer dynamics at early stages due to reduced mean-field effects, and with the constraint on EoS from Danielewicz et al. (2002) based on AGS flow analysis. The results enable future studies incorporating momentum-dependent forces and phase transitions (e.g., deconfinement) into the 3FD framework, and highlight the need for genuine nonequilibrium treatment of transverse-momentum anisotropy at the initial stage.
