# Shear and interface modes in neutron stars with pasta structures

> We carefully examine the shear and interface modes, which are excited due to the presence of crust elasticity, in neutron stars with pasta structures, adopting the relativistic Cowling approximation. We find that the shear modes are independent of the presence of the cylindrical-hole and spherical-hole nuclei at least up to a few kilohertz, while the interface modes strongly depend on the presence of the cylindrical-hole and spherical-hole nuclei. In addition, we find empirical relations for the interface mode frequencies multiplied by the stellar mass and for the shear mode frequencies multiplied by the stellar radius. These relations are expressed as a function of the stellar compactness almost independently of the stiffness in a higher-density region inside the neutron star, once one selects the crust equation of state. Thus, if one would simultaneously observe the shear and interface modes from a neutron star, one might extract the neutron star mass and radius with the help of the constraint on the crust stiffness obtained from terrestrial experiments.

## Key claim

Shear modes are independent of the presence of cylindrical-hole and spherical-hole nuclei at least up to a few kilohertz, while interface modes strongly depend on those pasta phases; empirical relations for interface mode frequencies multiplied by stellar mass and shear mode frequencies multiplied by stellar radius exist as functions of stellar compactness, almost independent of the stiffness in the higher-density region once the crust equation of state is selected.

## Method

The author adopts the relativistic Cowling approximation (fixed metric during oscillations) and constructs non-rotating, spherically symmetric neutron star equilibrium models using the Oyamatsu-Iida phenomenological equations of state (OI-EOSs) with fixed incompressibility K0 = 230 MeV and varying symmetry energy slope L. The perturbation equations for polar-type oscillations (ℓ=2) are integrated outward from the center and inward from the stellar surface, with eigenfrequencies determined by matching solutions at the crust-envelope boundary; the shear modulus for spherical nuclei uses the standard bcc lattice formula, while moduli for cylindrical, cylindrical-hole, and spherical-hole phases are derived from Coulomb energy considerations, with slab-like nuclei having zero shear modulus for linear perturbations.

## Key numbers

- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with radius 12.4 km constructed with the OI-EOS (K0=230 MeV, L=73.4 MeV), the fundamental (f) mode frequency is 2.237 kHz and the first pressure (p1) mode frequency is 6.074 kHz, essentially unchanged by crust elasticity.
- In the same stellar model, shear mode frequencies (s1 through s5) range from 0.668 kHz (s1) to 2.139 kHz (s5) in the realistic model including all pasta phases, compared to 0.730 kHz (s1) to 2.316 kHz (s5) when only spherical nuclei are elastic.
- Interface mode frequencies in the same model are i1 = 0.036 kHz, i2 = 0.030 kHz, and i3 = 0.027 kHz; the i1 mode is associated with the cylindrical/slab-like nuclei interface, i2 with the envelope/crust interface, and i3 with the cylindrical-hole/spherical-hole region.
- The ratio of the elastic region thickness composed of spherical and cylindrical nuclei (ΔR_SpCy) to that of cylindrical-hole and spherical-hole nuclei (ΔR_CHSH) is 179 for the L=73.4 MeV model, explaining why shear modes in the CHSH region would have frequencies roughly 100 times higher than those in the SpCy region.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-radial oscillations in neutron stars with elastic crusts (Finn 1990, Sotani and Lamb 2002, Passamonti and Bruni 2005, Kruger et al. 2015) by systematically examining shear and interface modes across multiple stellar models that include the full pasta phase sequence (spherical, cylindrical, slab-like, cylindrical-hole, spherical-hole nuclei). The empirical relations found for mode frequencies as functions of stellar compactness, nearly independent of the high-density equation of state stiffness, suggest that simultaneous observation of shear and interface modes could enable extraction of neutron star mass and radius when combined with crust stiffness constraints from terrestrial experiments.
