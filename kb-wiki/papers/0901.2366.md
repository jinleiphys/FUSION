# Coherent Pion Production in the Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering in Few-GeV Region

> We study coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering in the energy region relevant to the recent neutrino oscillation experiments. Our approach is based on the combined use of the Sato-Lee model and the Delta-hole model. Our initial numerical results are compared with the recent data from K2K and SciBooNE.

## Key claim

The combined Sato-Lee and Delta-hole model, with all free parameters fixed by fitting to pion-nucleus scattering data, produces initial results for neutrino-induced coherent pion production that are significantly larger than the experimental upper limit from K2K, though the authors note that the experimental upper limit was derived using other theoretical models still under active debate.

## Method

The authors combined the Sato-Lee model for electroweak pion production off a single nucleon with the Delta-hole model to incorporate medium effects, including changes to the Delta mass and width and final-state interactions between the outgoing pion and the nucleus. They constructed an optical potential for pion-nucleus scattering involving four free complex parameters (two for the Delta spreading potential and two for phenomenological pion-nucleus interactions proportional to the square of nuclear density), all fixed by fitting to elastic and total cross section data for pion-nucleus scattering. After testing the model against coherent pion photo-production data, they applied it without free parameters to calculate neutrino-induced coherent pion production.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for neutrino-induced charged-current coherent pion production on 12C at E_nu = 1 GeV: 9.5 x 10^-40 cm^2
- Experimental upper limit for the total cross section averaged over the neutrino spectrum from K2K: 7.7 x 10^-40 cm^2
- Photon energy for the coherent pion photo-production test on 12C: E_gamma = 290 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee model (Sato and Lee, 1996) and the Delta-hole model (Karaoglu and others) to coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering, motivated by the need for more accurate theoretical descriptions for experiments such as T2K and MiniBooNE. The initial results show inconsistency with data from K2K (Hasegawa, 2005) and SciBooNE (Hiraide, 2008), and the authors plan to study the origin of this discrepancy before applying the model to neutral-current reactions and anti-neutrino processes.
