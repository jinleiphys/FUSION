# Nucleon-nucleon scattering up to next-to-next-to-leading order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory: peripheral phases

> We study the nucleon-nucleon interaction up to next-to-next-to-leading order using time-ordered perturbation theory in the framework of manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory. We present the two-pion exchange contribution at one-loop level, which is consistent with the corresponding non-relativistic expressions in the large-nucleon-mass limit. Using the Born series truncated at one-loop order, we calculate the phase shifts and mixing angles of the partial waves with the angular momentum $l\geq 2$. Comparing with the results of non-relativistic formulation, we find an improved description of the phase shifts for some $D$ waves such as the $^3D_3$ one. For the other partial waves, both approaches show the globally similar results.

## Key claim
The authors find that using manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory with time-ordered perturbation theory yields an improved description of phase shifts for some D-waves (such as the \(^3D_3\) partial wave) compared to the non-relativistic formulation, while for other peripheral partial waves both approaches give globally similar results.

## Method
The authors derived the two-pion exchange (TPE) contribution to the nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using time-ordered perturbation theory with a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian, applying subtractive renormalization to remove ultraviolet divergences and power-counting violating pieces. They calculated phase shifts and mixing angles for partial waves with orbital angular momentum \(l \geq 2\) using the Born series truncated at one-loop order, employing the Kadyshevsky equation and perturbative unitarization via Eq. (18). The once-iterated one-pion-exchange potential was obtained by eliminating the energy dependence of the OPE potential and expanding in powers of \(E - \omega_p - \omega_{p'}\).

## Key numbers
- Pion mass \(M_\pi = 138\) MeV and nucleon mass \(m_N = 938.918\) MeV used in calculations.
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV fixed to its physical value.
- Axial coupling \(g_A = 1.267\) for LO calculation, changed to \(1.29\) at NLO and NNLO to account for the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy.
- Low-energy constants from Roy-Steiner analysis: \(c_1 = -0.74\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_2 = 1.81\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.61\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 2.17\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The once-iterated OPE potential is UV convergent for all partial waves, with \(V_\mathrm{OPE}^{(0)} \sim 1/p\) for fixed \(p'\) and large \(p\).

## Context
This work extends the manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral EFT framework for nucleon-nucleon scattering proposed by Epelbaum (2012) and Baru (2019) from leading order to next-to-next-to-leading order, deriving the two-pion exchange contributions at one-loop level. It provides a systematic comparison with the non-relativistic chiral EFT approach of Kaiser (1997) and Weinberg (1990, 1991), showing consistency in the large-nucleon-mass limit while offering improved ultraviolet behavior. The results enable future calculations of lower partial waves and full NN scattering up to NNLO in this Lorentz-invariant framework, potentially allowing larger cutoff variation than non-relativistic approaches.
