# The $β$-decay properties of $N=Z$ nuclei: Role of neutron-proton pairing and the shell model interpretation

> We study the recently measured beta-decay of $^{70}$Kr into $^{70}$Br within the framework of the large-scale shell model. The enhancement in the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength in $^{70}$Br compared to the $β$-decay of the lighter $^{62}$Ge was suggested as an indication for increased neutron-proton ($np$) pairing correlation. To explore the $np$ correlations in nuclei, we systematically examined the $β$-decay properties of the even-even nuclei $A=58,62,66,$ and $70$ into $N=Z$ odd-odd nuclei. By employing an interaction involving solely $J=1, T=0$ and $J=0, T=1$ pairing matrix elements, we observe that the pairing does not necessarily lead to an enhancement in the GT strength for the same coupling strength. But with the inclusion of the $g_{9/2}$ orbital, the GT strength can be increased with increasing $np$ pairing in connection with the enhanced contribution from the $g_{9/2}$ orbital. We further compare those results with realistic calculations in the $fp$ and $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space to gauge the contribution from $f_{7/2}$ and $g_{9/2}$ orbitals in the GT strengths. With the JUN45 interaction, there is an increment for the yrast $1^+$ state for the decay of $^{70}$Kr as compared to the decay of $^{62}$Ge due to increased $g_{9/2}$ contribution. Additionally, we probe the effect of $np$ pairing on $B_{\rm GT}$ by modifying the single-particle energies and the $T = 0$ matrix elements of the interaction responsible for the decay transition strength. In calculations with realistic interaction, we find that the accumulated transition strength can increase with enhanced $np$ pairing.

## Key claim

The enhancement in Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strength observed in the beta-decay of $^{70}$Kr compared to $^{62}$Ge, previously suggested as evidence for increased neutron-proton ($np$) pairing correlation, does not necessarily arise from pairing alone; the increase is instead linked to the inclusion of the $g_{9/2}$ orbital and its enhanced contribution with stronger $np$ pairing, as demonstrated through schematic and realistic shell-model calculations.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model configuration interaction calculations for the beta-decay transitions of four even-even $N=Z$ nuclei ($^{58}$Zn, $^{62}$Ge, $^{66}$Se, $^{70}$Kr) to their odd-odd $N=Z$ daughters. They employed schematic Hamiltonians containing only $J=1, T=0$ and $J=0, T=1$ pairing matrix elements in the $p_{3/2}p_{1/2}f_{5/2}$ and $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model spaces, and compared these with realistic calculations using the JUN45, GXPF1J, GXPF1A, and KB3G interactions, varying single-particle energies and $T=0$ matrix elements to probe the effect on GT strengths.

## Key numbers

- For $^{62}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{62}$Ga, the JUN45 interaction predicts $B_{\rm GT} = 0.0621$ for the $1^+_1$ state, in good agreement with the experimental value of 0.068(6).
- For $^{70}$Kr $\rightarrow$ $^{70}$Br, the experimental $B_{\rm GT}$ value for the first $1^+$ state is 0.26(3).
- For $^{58}$Zn $\rightarrow$ $^{58}$Cu, the full $fp$-shell calculation with GXPF1J gives $B_{\rm GT} = 0.2773$ for the $1^+_1$ state, close to experimental data, while the JUN45 calculation yields a much larger value of 4.1577.
- For $^{66}$Se $\rightarrow$ $^{66}$As, the JUN45 interaction predicts $B_{\rm GT} = 0.1637$ for the $1^+_1$ state and $B_{\rm GT} = 1.5120$ for the $1^+_3$ state; the GXPF1J interaction gives $B_{\rm GT} = 0.0045$ for $1^+_1$ and $B_{\rm GT} = 0.5882$ for $1^+_7$.
- The $B(E2; 0^+_{\rm g.s.} \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ transition strength for $^{70}$Kr using GXPF1J is 1878 ${\rm e}^2 \, {\rm fm}^4$, and for $^{70}$Se is 2040 ${\rm e}^2 \, {\rm fm}^4$.

## Context

This work extends the systematic shell-model study of GT decays along the $N=Z$ line, following experimental measurements by Vitez-Sveiczer et al. (2022) on $^{70}$Kr decay and earlier work on $^{62}$Ge decay by PhysRevLett.113.092501 and PhysRevC.103.014324. The study contradicts the simple interpretation that enhanced $np$ pairing directly increases GT strength, as previously assumed in discussions of isoscalar pairing fingerprints. The results enable future experimental and theoretical investigations of $^{66}$Se decay (for which no data yet exist) and motivate extending model spaces to include both $f_{7/2}$ and $g_{9/2}$ orbitals for a more consistent description of $N\sim Z$ nuclei, though such calculations remain computationally expensive.
