# Parton Distribution Function from the Hadronic Tensor on the Lattice

> The path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor W_{μν} of deep inelastic scattering is reviewed. It is shown that there are 3 gauge invariant and topologically distinct contributions. The separation of the connected sea partons from those of the disconnected sea can be achieved with a combination of the global fit of the parton distribution function (PDF), the semi-inclusive DIS data on the strange PDF and the lattice calculation of the ratio of the strange to $u/d$ momentum fraction in the disconnected insertion. We shall discuss numerical issues associated with lattice calculation of the hadronic tensor involving a four-point function, such as large hadron momenta and improved maximum entropy method to obtain the spectral density from the hadronic tensor in Euclidean time. We also draw a comparison between the large momentum approach to the parton distribution function (PDF) and the hadronic tensor approach.

# Parton Distribution Function from the Hadronic Tensor on the Lattice

## Key claim

The Euclidean path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor reveals three gauge-invariant and topologically distinct contributions (valence plus connected sea, connected sea anti-partons, and disconnected sea partons), and the connected sea partons can be separated from disconnected sea partons using a combination of global PDF fits, semi-inclusive DIS data on strange PDFs, and lattice calculations of the ratio of strange to u/d momentum fraction in the disconnected insertion, but this separation relies on the assumption that the distribution of s(x)+s̄(x) is proportional to that of u^{ds}(x)+ū^{ds}(x).

## Method

The hadronic tensor W_{μν} for deep inelastic scattering is obtained from a Euclidean four-point function on the lattice, involving two sequential inversions, by computing the ratio of a four-point correlator (with nucleon interpolating fields and two current insertions) to a two-point nucleon correlator. The Euclidean-time hadronic tensor \widetilde{W}_{μν}(τ) is then converted to the Minkowski-space hadronic tensor via an inverse Laplace transform, which requires solving an inverse problem for the spectral density using an improved Maximum Entropy Method. The approach is compared to the large-momentum (quasiparton) approach of Ji (2013), which requires renormalization of non-local operators and large hadron momenta.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R = ⟨x⟩_{s+ s̄} / ⟨x⟩_{u+ū}(DI) = 0.857(40) from lattice calculations (Doi et al., 2008) is used to separate connected and disconnected sea contributions.
- The extraction of connected sea anti-partons ū^{cs}(x)+d̄^{cs}(x) is performed at Q² = 2.5 GeV² in the region 0.03 < x < 0.4.
- With |q⃗| = |p⃗| = 3 GeV and Q² = 2 GeV², the Bjorken x is 0.058, which is low enough to distinguish connected sea from disconnected sea distributions.
- The small-x behavior for valence and connected sea partons is ∝ x^{-1/2}, while for disconnected sea partons it is ∝ x^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor developed by Liu (1993, 1998, 1999) and independently by Aglietti (1998) and Detmold (2005), which classifies parton contributions by topology. It addresses the Gottfried sum rule violation by separating connected sea (CS) from disconnected sea (DS) partons, a distinction not made in global PDF fits such as CT10. The approach enables direct lattice calculation of valence, CS, and DS contributions separately, which could resolve the persistent tension between lattice calculations of the second moment ⟨x⟩_{u-d} and global fitting results (Bali, 2014; Yang, 2015). It also offers a theoretically simpler alternative to the large-momentum approach of Ji (2013), at the cost of more numerically intensive four-point function calculations and the challenge of solving the inverse Laplace transform via improved MEM (Burnier, 2013).
