# Memory Effect and Fast Spinodal Decomposition

> We consider the modification of the Cahn-Hilliard equation when a time delay process through a memory function is taken into account. We then study the process of spinodal decomposition in fast phase transitions associated with a conserved order parameter. The introduced memory effect plays an important role to obtain a finite group velocity. Then, we discuss the constraint for the parameters to satisfy causality. The memory effect is seen to affect the dynamics of phase transition at short times and has the effect of delaying, in a significant way, the process of rapid growth of the order parameter that follows a quench into the spinodal region.

## Key claim

The memory effect introduced into the Cahn-Hilliard equation through an exponential memory function yields a finite group velocity and a causality constraint (\(\gamma > D\)), which delays the spinodal decomposition process significantly at short times; for parameters relevant to RHIC, the time scale for the onset of spinodal growth increases by nearly 200% compared to the ordinary (memoryless) Cahn-Hilliard equation.

## Method

The authors generalized the Cahn-Hilliard equation for a conserved order parameter by replacing the instantaneous irreversible current with a convolution over a memory kernel of exponential form, \({\cal M}(x,t) = (\Gamma/\gamma) e^{-t/\gamma} \delta^{(3)}(x)\). This yields a modified Cahn-Hilliard equation containing a second-order time derivative term. They solved the linearized version analytically to obtain the dispersion relation, group velocity, and fastest-growing mode time scale, and performed numerical simulations on a discrete spatial lattice using a semi-implicit scheme with fast Fourier transform in spatial coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The group velocity at \(k=0\) is \(v(0) = \sqrt{D/\gamma}\), where \(D = 2b\Gamma\).
- The causality constraint derived is \(\gamma > D\).
- For RHIC parameters (\(\xi \sim 1\) fm, \(\gamma = 3D\), \(D \sim 3.7\) fm), the time scale of the fastest-growing mode with memory is \(\tau_c \sim 6.1\) fm, compared to \(\tau_{nc} \sim 2.2\) fm without memory.
- The critical momentum for the modified equation is \(k'^2_c = [-b + \sqrt{b^2 + a/(4\gamma\Gamma)}]/a\).
- Numerical simulations used dimensionless time \(\bar{t} = (8/\tau_{nc}) t\) and \(\bar{\gamma} = (8/\tau_{nc})\gamma\); results are shown for \(\bar{\gamma}=0\) and \(\bar{\gamma}=40.5\).

## Context

This work extends the Cahn-Hilliard equation by incorporating memory effects to address causality violations and sum-rule failures known from the ordinary diffusion equation, following earlier microscopic derivations showing that coarse-grained dynamics yields a telegraph-like equation (Koide, 2005) rather than a pure diffusion equation. The results are relevant for fast phase transitions in the early universe and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where the typical reaction time (10-20 fm, Hama et al.) is comparable to the memory time scale (\(\sim 1\) fm, Koide-M). The delay in spinodal growth found here is even larger than the time delay estimated for nonconserved order parameters by Fraga and Krein (2005).
