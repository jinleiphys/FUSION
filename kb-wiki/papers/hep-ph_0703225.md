# New Hadron States

> The past four years has witnessed the renaissance of the hadron spectroscopy. Many interesting new hadron states were discovered experimentally, some of which do not fit into the quark model easily. I will give a concise overview of these states and their possible interpretations. Topics covered in this review are: (1) candidates of new light hadrons including $p {\bar p}$ threshold enhancement, X(1835), X(1576), $f_0(1810)$, recent candidates of the $1^{-+}$ exotic mesons, Y(2175), $p\barΛ$ threshold enhancement etc; (2) charmed mesons including p-wave non-strange charmed mesons, $D_{sj}(2317)$ and $D_{sj}(2460)$, recent candidates of higher excited charmed mesons, $D_{sj}(2632)$ etc; (3) charmonium and charmonium-like states such as X(3872), Y(4260), X(3940), Y(3940), Z(3930) etc. The effect from the nearby S-wave open channels on the quark model spectrum above or near strong decay threshold is emphasized. Dynamical lattice simulations of $D K$ and $D^0\bar D^{\ast 0}$ scattering and the extraction of their phase shifts may help resolve the underlying structure of $D_{sj}(2317)$, $D_{sj}(2460)$ and X(3872).

## Key claim

This review paper argues that many newly discovered hadron states do not easily fit into the constituent quark model (CQM), but the author hedges that CQM is only a phenomenological model not derived from QCD, so the physical spectrum of QCD remains ambiguous and no one can rigorously prove or exclude the existence of non-conventional states since the QCD confinement issue is not solved.

## Method

The author provides a concise overview of new hadron states discovered experimentally in the past four years (2003-2007), categorizing them into three classes: candidates of new light hadrons (including threshold enhancements, exotic mesons, and scalar states), charmed mesons (including P-wave states and the D_sJ resonances), and charmonium or charmonium-like states (including X(3872), Y(4260), and related states). For each state, the author summarizes experimental observations and reviews proposed theoretical interpretations from the literature, emphasizing the effect of nearby S-wave open channels on the quark model spectrum above or near strong decay thresholds.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing discussion of hadron spectroscopy beyond the constituent quark model, following decades of theoretical and experimental exploration of non-conventional hadrons including glueballs, hybrids, tetraquarks, and molecular states. The author notes that quenched lattice QCD simulations predict the scalar glueball mass around 1.5-1.7 GeV and the pseudoscalar glueball around 2.6 GeV, citing lattice work from the literature. The paper enables future experimental investigations, particularly noting that BESIII will start taking data in 2007 and that dynamical lattice simulations of DK and D⁰D̄*⁰ scattering may help resolve the structure of D_sJ(2317), D_sJ(2460), and X(3872).
