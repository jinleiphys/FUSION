# SU(3) symmetry breaking in lower fp-shell nuclei

> Results of shell-model calculations for lower fp-shell nuclei show that SU(3) symmetry breaking in this region is driven by the single-particle spin-orbit splitting. However, even though states of the yrast band exhibit SU(3) symmetry breaking, the results also show that the yrast band B(E2) values are insensitive to this fragmentation of the SU(3) symmetry; specifically, the quadrupole collectivity as measured by B(E2) transition strengths between low lying members of the yrast band remain high even though SU(3) appears to be broken. Results for $^{44,46,48}Ti$ and $^{48}Cr$ using the Kuo-Brown-3 two-body interaction are given to illustrate these observations.

# SU(3) symmetry breaking in lower fp-shell nuclei

## Key claim
The single-particle spin-orbit splitting is the primary driver of SU(3) symmetry breaking in lower fp-shell nuclei, but the yrast band B(E2) transition strengths remain high and close to the SU(3) symmetry limit even when SU(3) appears broken, indicating an "adiabatic" mixing of SU(3) irreps. This coherence holds for all yrast states in 44Ti but breaks down after the first few yrast states in heavier nuclei like 46Ti, 48Ti, and 48Cr.

## Method
Full fp-shell model calculations were performed using the Lanczos algorithm in an m-scheme basis for 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Ti, and 48Cr with the Kuo-Brown-3 (KB3) two-body interaction. The SU(3) content of yrast states was analyzed via the second-order Casimir operator C2, and B(E2) transition strengths were computed using Siegert's theorem with effective charges e_p = 1.5e and e_n = 0.5e. The spin-orbit interaction was systematically turned off by setting single-particle energies to their centroid values (KB3p_f and KB3pf interactions) to isolate its effect on SU(3) symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers
- For 44Ti with KB3 interaction: the leading SU(3) irrep (12,0) with C2 = 180 contributes only about 10% to the J=12 yrast state; the largest contribution (over 50%) comes from the (8,2) irrep with C2 = 114.
- For 44Ti with KB3p_f interaction (spin-orbit off): the leading irrep contributes more than 52% to the ground state, over 80% to J=8, 91% to J=10, and more than 97% to J=12.
- For 44Ti ground state with KB3: average C2 = 110 with skewness delta C2 = 33; with KB3p_f: average C2 = 139 with skewness delta C2 = -37.
- For 48Cr with KB3: the leading irrep (C2 = 396) accounts for only about 10% of the total strength distribution; with KB3p_f the leading irrep dominates with more than 55% of the total strength.
- B(E2:2+ -> 0+) for 44Ti: 104.82 e^2 fm^4 (KB3), 138.58 e^2 fm^4 (KB3p_f), 122.69 e^2 fm^4 (experiment).
- B(E2:2+ -> 0+) for 46Ti: 208 e^2 fm^4 (KB3), 299.83 e^2 fm^4 (KB3p_f), 199.82 e^2 fm^4 (experiment).
- B(E2:2+ -> 0+) for 48Ti: 202.4 e^2 fm^4 (KB3), 445.32 e^2 fm^4 (KB3p_f), 144.23 e^2 fm^4 (experiment).

## Context
This work extends the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) from the sd-shell into the lower fp-shell, where leading SU(3) irreps alone do not provide satisfactory results for low-lying eigenstates. The concept of adiabatic mixing, proposed by Rochford and Rowe (1988) and further developed by Zuker, Retamosa, Poves, and Caurier (1995) and Rowe, Bahri, and Wijesundera (1998), is tested quantitatively. The results show that while the spin-orbit interaction breaks SU(3) symmetry, the quadrupole collectivity survives through coherent mixing of irreps, supporting the quasi-SU(3) dynamical symmetry picture. This enables future studies of deformed nuclei in the fp-shell using truncated SU(3)-based models, and motivates experimental measurements of higher-spin B(E2) values to test the predicted breakdown of adiabatic mixing beyond the first few yrast states.
