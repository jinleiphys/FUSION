# Tidal Waves in $^{102}$Pd: A Rotating Condensate of Multiple $d$ bosons

> Low-lying collective excitations in even-even vibrational and transitional nuclei may be described semi-classically as quadrupole running waves on the surface of the nucleus ("tidal waves"), and the observed vibrational-rotational behavior can be thought of as resulting from a rotating condensate of interacting $d$ bosons. These concepts have been investigated by measuring lifetimes of the levels in the yrast band of the $^{102}$Pd nucleus with the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method. The extracted $B(E2)$ reduced transition probabilities for the yrast band display a monotonic increase with spin, in agreement with the interpretation based on rotation-induced condensation of aligned $d$ bosons.

## Key claim

The yrast band of \(^{102}\)Pd up to spin \(I=14\) provides the first clear evidence for rotation-induced condensation of aligned \(d\) bosons, with the measured \(B(E2)\) values increasing linearly with spin as expected for such a condensate; however, the observed anharmonicities appear as a constant upshift of the reduced transition probability and moment of inertia as a function of spin, interpreted as arising from a small fraction of non-aligned \(d\) bosons.

## Method

Lifetimes of yrast states in \(^{102}\)Pd were measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM) following the \(^{76}\)Ge(\(^{30}\)Si, 4n) reaction at 110 MeV beam energy at the ATLAS facility. Gamma rays were detected with the Gammasphere array (98 Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors), and lineshape analyses were performed using a modified version of the LINESHAPE package with Monte Carlo simulations of recoil velocity histories. Side-feeding was modeled by a five-state rotational cascade, and \(\chi^2\) minimization was used to extract lifetimes and transition probabilities.

## Context

This work extends the tidal wave concept previously applied to near-equidistant band structures in \(^{182}\)Os by Pattison et al. (2003) and Cullen et al. (2005), and to alternating-parity sequences in \(^{220}\)Th by Reviol et al. (2006). The results confirm the prediction by Frauendorf (2010) that the yrast line of \(^{102}\)Pd corresponds to a rotating condensate of aligned \(d\) bosons, and the measurements establish the characteristic increase of tidal wave amplitude up to \(I=14\). The semiclassical cranking plus micro-macro calculations using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential reproduce the observed behavior without any adjustable parameters, enabling future studies of multi-phonon excitations in transitional nuclei.
