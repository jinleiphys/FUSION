# Spin-orbit splitting in low-j neutron orbits and proton densities in the nuclear interior

> On the basis of relativistic mean field calculations, we predict that the spin-orbit splitting of $p_{3/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ neutron orbits depends sensitively on the magnitude of the proton density near the center of the nucleus, and in particular on the occupation of $s_{1/2}$ proton orbits. We focus on two exotic nuclei, $^{46}$Ar and $^{206}$Hg, in which the presence of a pair of $s_{1/2}$ proton holes is predicted to cause the splitting between the $p_{3/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ neutron orbits near the Fermi surface to be much smaller than in the nearby doubly-magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. We note that these two exotic nuclei depart from the long-standing paradigm of a central potential proportional to the ground state baryon density and a spin-orbit potential proportional to the derivative of the central potential.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit splitting of low-\(j\) neutron \(p_{3/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) orbits depends sensitively on the proton density near the center of the nucleus, specifically on the occupation of \(s_{1/2}\) proton orbits; the authors predict that in \(^{46}\)Ar and \(^{206}\)Hg, the presence of a pair of \(s_{1/2}\) proton holes causes the splitting to be much smaller than in the nearby doubly-magic nuclei \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb, though they note that correlations beyond the mean-field approximation may modify the quantitative predictions.

## Method

Relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations were performed using a Lagrangian with novel nonlinear couplings between isoscalar and isovector mesons, as developed in Ho et al. (2001a, 2001b) and applied in Todd et al. (2003). The model was used to compute proton point densities, Schrodinger-equivalent spin-orbit potentials, and neutron \(p\)-orbital wavefunctions for \(^{46}\)Ar, \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{206}\)Hg, and \(^{208}\)Pb. The spin-orbit splitting was estimated by folding the spin-orbit potential with the normalized \(p_{3/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) neutron wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The \(p_{3/2}\)-\(p_{1/2}\) spin-orbit splitting in \(^{208}\)Pb is \(-0.60\) MeV, collapsing to \(-0.02\) MeV in \(^{206}\)Hg.
- The spin-orbit splitting in \(^{46}\)Ar is reduced by almost an order of magnitude relative to \(^{48}\)Ca (exact numerical values for \(^{46}\)Ar and \(^{48}\)Ca are not given in the text, but the reduction is described as "almost an order of magnitude").
- The predicted root-mean-square charge radii for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb are in excellent agreement with experiment (Vries et al., 1987).

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-orbit splitting in exotic nuclei, which has previously focused on the weakening of the spin-orbit force on high-\(j\) neutron orbits near the neutron drip line due to large surface diffuseness and continuum effects (Nazarewicz et al., 2001; Carlson et al., 2000b). The authors propose a new mechanism for the collapse of spin-orbit splitting among low-\(j\) neutron orbits, driven by depletion of proton density in the nuclear interior rather than by neutron surface properties. This prediction challenges the long-standing paradigm that the central potential is proportional to the ground state baryon density and the spin-orbit potential is proportional to its derivative, and suggests that \(^{46}\)Ar and \(^{206}\)Hg may be accessible for experimental verification using stripping or pickup reactions in inverse kinematics with polarized targets.
