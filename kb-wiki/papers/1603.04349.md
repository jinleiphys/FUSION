# Evidence of strong proton shape fluctuations from incoherent diffraction

> We show within the saturation framework that measurements of exclusive vector meson production at high energy provide evidence for strong geometric fluctuations of the proton. In comparison, the effect of saturation scale and color charge fluctuations is weak. This knowledge will allow detailed future measurements of the incoherent cross section to tightly constrain the fluctuating geometry of the proton as a function of the parton momentum fraction $x$.

## Key claim
The paper shows that measurements of exclusive vector meson production at high energy provide evidence for strong geometric fluctuations of the proton, while the effect of saturation scale and color charge fluctuations is weak. The claim is conditional on the saturation framework (IPsat and IP-Glasma models) and the specific constituent quark model used to implement geometric fluctuations.

## Method
The authors calculate diffractive J/Psi photoproduction cross sections within the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering, using both the impact parameter dependent saturation model (IPsat) and the IP-Glasma model. Geometric fluctuations are implemented by replacing the smooth Gaussian proton profile with a sum of three Gaussian profiles centered on constituent quarks, whose positions are sampled from a Gaussian distribution. The dipole-target scattering amplitude is computed event-by-event, and the coherent cross section is obtained from the averaged amplitude while the incoherent cross section is obtained from the variance of the amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Incoherent cross section for a smooth proton (B_qc=1.0 GeV^-2, B_q=3.0 GeV^-2) underestimates HERA data by more than an order of magnitude for |t| greater than about 1 GeV^2
- A strongly fluctuating proton with B_qc=3.5 GeV^-2 and B_q=0.5-1.0 GeV^-2 produces incoherent cross section compatible with HERA data at <W>=100 GeV
- Average distance of a constituent quark from the proton center is sqrt(2 B_qc) = 0.28 fm for the smooth proton and 0.52 fm for the lumpy proton
- In the IP-Glasma model, a round proton with B_p=4 GeV^-2 and m=0.4 GeV produces incoherent cross section that underestimates data by up to several orders of magnitude
- Saturation scale fluctuations with sigma=0.5 increase the incoherent cross section by approximately 40% for |t| greater than about 0.3 GeV^2
- The real part correction to the cross section is of order 10% in the kinematical range considered
- The skewedness correction is numerically important, of order 40% in the relevant kinematical region

## Context
This work extends the IPsat model of Kowalski and Teaney (2003) and the IP-Glasma model of Schenke, Tribedy, and Venugopalan (2012) to include event-by-event geometric fluctuations of the proton. It contradicts the assumption that color charge fluctuations alone (as studied by Marquet, Soyez, and Xiao in 2010) can explain the measured incoherent diffractive cross section. The results enable future measurements at a proposed Electron Ion Collider to tightly constrain the fluctuating shape of the proton as a function of Bjorken x, and can be improved by implementing QCD evolution via the JIMWLK equation.
