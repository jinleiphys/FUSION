# QCD and Rescattering in Nuclear Targets

> We review the extension of the factorization formalism of perturbative QCD to soft rescattering associated with hard processes in nuclei.

# QCD and Rescattering in Nuclear Targets

## Key claim

The authors review a perturbative QCD factorization formalism extended to include soft rescattering in nuclear targets, arguing that for suitably defined inclusive cross sections (such as jet production), multiple soft scatterings are power-suppressed by factors of $1/Q^2$ rather than being leading-order effects, with the leading correction proportional to $A^{1/3} \times \lambda^2 / Q^2$ where $\lambda$ represents a nonperturbative scale.

## Method

The authors develop a generalized factorization theorem at next-to-leading power (twist-4) for hard scattering in nuclear targets, classifying multiple scattering into three categories: interactions internal to the nucleus (which modify twist-2 nuclear parton distributions, producing the EMC effect), initial-state parton-nucleus interactions, and final-state parton-nucleus interactions. They show that the $A$-enhancement from double scattering arises from twist-4 parton-parton correlation functions (quark-gluon and gluon-gluon) where integrals over light-cone coordinates can grow with nuclear size when momentum fraction integrations are dominated by regions where two of the three momentum fractions vanish, corresponding to poles in the hard scattering functions.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter for power suppression of multiple scattering is $A^{1/3} \times \lambda^2 / Q^2$, where $\lambda$ is a nonperturbative scale identified with a higher-twist parton distribution
- From Fermilab E683 di-jet momentum imbalance data, the extracted value is $\lambda^2 \sim 0.05-0.1$ GeV$^2$
- From Fermilab E772 and CERN NA10 Drell-Yan data, the extracted value is $\lambda_{\rm DY}^2 \sim 0.01$ GeV$^2$, considerably smaller than the di-jet value
- The E683 data parameterizes the averaged momentum imbalance as $(1.44 + 0.174 A^{1/3})$ GeV$^2$, with the $A^{1/3}$ term attributed to multiple scattering
- The di-jet momentum imbalance double scattering contribution scales as $A^{4/3}$ with color factors $C_F = 4/3$ and $C_G = 3$
- The Lorentz transformation of gluon field strength gives a $1/\gamma^2$ suppression, translating to $1/Q^4$ suppression for potential factorization-breaking interactions

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD factorization formalism of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1980s) to power-suppressed (higher-twist) contributions in nuclear targets, providing a framework that complements the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) analysis of energy loss from many soft scatterings (Baier et al., Zakharov) by focusing on momentum transfer from hard scattering with a single additional soft interaction. The formalism enables systematic treatment of $O(1/Q^2)$ effects in processes such as di-jet momentum imbalance in photoproduction (measured by Fermilab E683), direct photon production (Fermilab E706), and Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening (Fermilab E772, CERN NA10), with the goal of unifying these phenomena through universal twist-4 parton correlation functions.
