# Solution to Faddeev equations with two-body experimental amplitudes as input and application to J^P=1/2^+, S=0 baryon resonances

> We solve the Faddeev equations for the two meson-one baryon system $ππN$ and coupled channels using the experimental two-body $t$-matrices for the $πN$ interaction as input and unitary chiral dynamics to describe the interaction between the rest of coupled channels. In addition to the $N^*(1710)$ obtained before with the $ππN$ channel, we obtain, for $J^π=1/2^+$ and total isospin of the three-body system $I=1/2$, a resonance peak whose mass is around 2080 MeV and width of 54 MeV, while for $I=3/2$ we find a peak around 2126 MeV and 42 MeV of width. These two resonances can be identified with the $N^* (2100)$ and the $Δ(1910)$, respectively. We obtain another peak in the isospin 1/2 configuration, around 1920 MeV which can be interpreted as a resonance in the $N a_0(980)$ and $N f_0(980)$ systems.

## Key claim
The authors solve Faddeev equations for the two-meson–one-baryon system using experimental two-body πN t-matrices as input, rather than relying solely on chiral unitary amplitudes, under the ansatz that the cancellation between off-shell parts of the amplitudes and three-body forces, rigorously proven only within lowest-order chiral dynamics, remains valid at higher energies up to about 2200 MeV.

## Method
The Faddeev equations are solved in a coupled-channel approach for the ππN system and its coupled channels (πKΣ, πKΛ, πηN, and NK̄K) with total Jᴾ = 1/2⁺ and strangeness S = 0. The πN interaction is taken from experimental phase shifts and inelasticities for s-waves above threshold, while the ππ interaction and other meson-baryon amplitudes are obtained from unitary chiral dynamics. The three-body T-matrix is evaluated as a function of the total energy √s and the invariant mass of the meson-baryon subsystem √s₂₃, and peaks are identified as resonances.

## Key numbers
- N*(1710) reproduced at 1704 MeV in the ππN amplitude with I=1/2 and I_{ππ}=0, consistent with prior work.
- For I=1/2 with 14 coupled channels, a resonance peak at 2080 MeV with width 54 MeV, identified with N*(2100).
- For I=3/2 with 14 coupled channels, a resonance peak at 2126 MeV with width 42 MeV, identified with Δ(1910).
- In the NK̄K system with I=1/2 and I_{K̄K}=1, a peak at 1924 MeV with width 20 MeV, interpreted as a bound Na₀(980) state.
- In the NK̄K system with I=1/2 and I_{K̄K}=0, a peak at 1923 MeV with width 30 MeV (Nf₀(980)) and another at 2052 MeV with width 60 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier Faddeev study of the ππN system by Khemchandani et al. (2008), which found the N*(1710) using chiral unitary πN amplitudes limited to √s < 1600 MeV. By replacing those amplitudes with experimental πN data, the authors generate the N*(2100) and Δ(1910) as three-body resonances, which were absent in the previous approach. The finding of a bound Na₀(980) state near 1920 MeV agrees with suggestions by Jido et al. (2008) from effective two-body potentials. The results enable future studies of baryon resonances using purely experimental two-body input, though the authors note that the neglect of πN decay channels leads to narrower widths than experimental values.
