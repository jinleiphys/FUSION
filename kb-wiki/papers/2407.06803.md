# Charged current weak production of $Δ(1232)$ induced by electrons and positrons

> The charged current weak production of $Δ(1232)$ from the free proton target induced by the electron/positron in the intermediate energy range corresponding to the beam energy available at JLab and Mainz, has been studied. The results for the differential scattering cross section $\frac{dσ}{dQ^2}$, the angular distribution $\frac{dσ}{dΩ_Δ}$, and the total scattering cross section $σ(E_e)$ for both the electron and positron induced processes are presented, for the various energies in the range of 0.5--4~GeV. The cross section $σ(E_e)$ is found to be of the order of $10^{-39}$~cm$^{2}$ for the electron/positron energies in the few GeV range. The availability of electron/positron beams having well defined energy and direction with very high luminosity of the order of $10^{38}-10^{39}$~cm$^{-2}$~sec$^{-1}$, makes it possible to observe the weak charged current production of $Δ(1232)$ and determine the axial vector form factors $C_{i}^{A} (Q^2);~(i=3-5)$. The sensitivity of the differential cross section $\frac{dσ}{dQ^2}$ to the subdominant form factors $C_{3}^{A}(Q^2)$ and $C_{4}^{A} (Q^2)$ is found to be strong enough, especially in the low $Q^2$ region, which can be used to determine them phenomenologically and to test the various theoretical models proposed to calculate them.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that charged-current weak production of the Δ(1232) resonance induced by electron and positron beams on free protons can be used to determine the subdominant axial vector form factors \(C_3^A(Q^2)\) and \(C_4^A(Q^2)\), which are poorly constrained by existing neutrino scattering data due to flux uncertainties. The authors claim the differential cross section \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) shows sufficient sensitivity to these form factors, especially at low \(Q^2\), to enable their phenomenological determination, provided the well-defined energy and luminosity of electron/positron beams at JLab and Mainz eliminate the systematic errors that plague neutrino experiments.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section \(d\sigma/dQ^2\), angular distribution \(d\sigma/d\Omega_\Delta\), and total cross section \(\sigma(E_e)\) for the reactions \(e^- + p \rightarrow \Delta^0 + \nu_e\) and \(e^+ + p \rightarrow \Delta^{++} + \bar{\nu}_e\) using the standard \(N-\Delta\) transition matrix element expressed in terms of vector form factors \(C_i^V(Q^2)\) (determined from electromagnetic data via CVC and isospin symmetry) and axial vector form factors \(C_i^A(Q^2)\). The axial form factors were parameterized using the model of Lalakulich et al. (2006) with \(C_5^A(0)=1.2\) and \(M_A=1\) GeV, while the subdominant \(C_3^A\) and \(C_4^A\) were varied to test sensitivity, using the Adler model relation \(C_3^A=0\), \(C_4^A = -C_5^A/4\) as a baseline.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section \(\sigma(E_e)\) is of the order of \(10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\) for electron/positron energies in the few GeV range (0.5–4 GeV).
- The vector form factor parameters used are \(C_3^V(0)=2.13\), \(C_4^V(0)=-1.51\), \(C_5^V(0)=0.48\) with vector dipole mass \(M_V=0.84\) GeV.
- The axial form factor \(C_5^A(0)=1.2\) is used with axial dipole mass \(M_A=1\) GeV in the parameterization of Lalakulich et al. (2006).
- Phenomenological values from Graczyk et al. (2009) give \(C_4^A(0) = -0.67 \pm 0.42\) with \(M_{A4} = 0.4^{+1.1}_{-0.4}\) GeV, and \(C_5^A(0) = 1.17 \pm 0.13\) with \(M_{A5} = 0.95 \pm 0.07\) GeV.
- Luminosity of available electron/positron beams is of the order of \(10^{38}\)–\(10^{39}\) cm\(^{-2}\) sec\(^{-1}\) at JLab and Mainz.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical calculations of charged-current \(\Delta(1232)\) production by electrons and positrons, previously studied by Hwang et al. (1987) using a constituent quark model and by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (1997) using model-independent form factors from neutrino data. The paper aims to address the unsatisfactory determination of the axial vector form factors \(C_3^A\) and \(C_4^A\) from ANL and BNL neutrino experiments, which suffer from flux uncertainties; the authors propose electron/positron beams as a cleaner alternative. This could enable future experiments at JLab and Mainz to test theoretical predictions from quark models (e.g., Isgur-Karl, SU(6)), lattice QCD (Alexandrou et al., 2006, 2007), and QCD sum rules (Kucukarslan, 2015), and to independently determine all three axial form factors \(C_{3-5}^A(Q^2)\).
