# Hidden Symmetry from Supersymmetry in One-Dimensional Quantum Mechanics

> When several inequivalent supercharges form a closed superalgebra in Quantum Mechanics it entails the appearance of hidden symmetries of a Super-Hamiltonian. We examine this problem in one-dimensional QM for the case of periodic potentials and potentials with finite number of bound states. After the survey of the results existing in the subject the algebraic and analytic properties of hidden-symmetry differential operators are rigorously elaborated in the Theorems and illuminated by several examples.

## Key claim

When several inequivalent supercharges form a closed superalgebra in one-dimensional quantum mechanics, the resulting extended supersymmetry necessarily produces hidden symmetry operators that are differential operators of odd order and cannot be expressed as polynomials of the Hamiltonian; these operators encode all characteristic spectral points including bound-state energies and band edges.

## Method

The authors rigorously analyze the algebraic structure of extended (N=4) nonlinear supersymmetric quantum mechanics with two independent supercharges, deriving the conditions under which the anticommutator of a K-type and P-type supercharge yields a t-antisymmetric differential symmetry operator. They prove that for periodic potentials with smooth real-valued coefficients, any non-minimizable t-antisymmetric symmetry operator has periodic coefficients, its kernel consists precisely of the band-edge wavefunctions, and it admits factorization into products of first-order and second-order intertwining operators whose intermediate Hamiltonians share the same spectrum. For potentials with a finite number of bound states, the analysis is extended to complex potentials and non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians, showing that the symmetry operator's kernel contains all bound-state wavefunctions and associated functions.

## Key numbers

- The order N of the t-antisymmetric symmetry operator e is odd, and the polynomial P_e(h) = e^t e = -e^2 has degree equal to the order of e.
- For periodic potentials, there are ((N+1)/2)! distinct nonsingular factorizations of e into one first-order and (N-1)/2 second-order intertwining operators.
- The algebraic multiplicity of each band-edge energy E_j in the spectrum of the matrix S of e is exactly 1.
- The order of the t-antisymmetric operator E(H) from the extended SUSY algebra is N_e = N + M, where N and M are the orders of the two supercharges.

## Context

This work extends the earlier results on nonlinear supersymmetric quantum mechanics and hidden symmetries from Andrianov and Sokolov (2007) and Andrianov, Cannata, and Sokolov (2007), providing rigorous theorems on the structure of t-antisymmetric symmetry operators. It generalizes previous studies of self-isospectral periodic systems by Dunne and Feinberg (1998) and Fernandez, Negro, and Nieto (2000) to include complex potentials and non-Hermitian PT-symmetric Hamiltonians following Bender (2007). The results enable the systematic construction of Lax pairs for finite-gap periodic potentials and the classification of hidden symmetries in one-dimensional quantum systems with reflectionless potentials.
