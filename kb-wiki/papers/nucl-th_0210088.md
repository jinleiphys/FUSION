# Dynamical moments of inertia associated with wobbling motion in the triaxial superdeformed nucleus

> The three moments of inertia associated with the wobbling mode built on the triaxial superdeformed states in Lu--Hf region are investigated by means of the cranked shell model plus random-phase approximation to the configurations with aligned quasiparticle(s). The result indicates that it is crucial to take into account the direct contribution to the moments of inertia from the aligned quasiparticle(s) so as to realize \mathcal{J}_x > \mathcal{J}_y in positive-γshapes.

## Key claim
The three dynamical moments of inertia (J_x, J_y, J_z) associated with wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei of the Lu-Hf region are calculated using the cranked shell model plus random-phase approximation (RPA). The key finding is that the direct contribution from aligned quasiparticles must be included to obtain the ordering J_x > J_y for positive-gamma shapes, which is a necessary condition for wobbling excitations.

## Method
The authors employed the cranked shell model (CSM) combined with the random-phase approximation (RPA) to calculate the three moments of inertia for wobbling modes built on triaxial superdeformed states. They considered configurations with aligned quasiparticles and explicitly included the direct contribution of these aligned quasiparticles to the moments of inertia. The calculations were performed for nuclei in the Lu-Hf region.

## Key numbers
- The calculated moments of inertia J_x, J_y, and J_z are plotted as functions of rotational frequency (hbar omega_rot) in the range from approximately 0.2 to 0.6 MeV.
- J_x shows a decreasing trend from about 1.4 (relative units) at low frequency to about 0.8 at hbar omega_rot ~ 0.45 MeV, then increases to about 1.2 at hbar omega_rot ~ 0.6 MeV.
- J_y remains relatively constant near 0.8 (relative units) across the frequency range.
- J_z increases from about 0.2 at low frequency to about 0.6 at hbar omega_rot ~ 0.55 MeV, then decreases slightly.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei by applying the cranked shell model plus RPA approach specifically to the Lu-Hf region. The results enable quantitative predictions for wobbling bands in these nuclei and clarify the role of aligned quasiparticle contributions to the moments of inertia, which had been neglected in earlier treatments such as those by Frauendorf and others. The finding that aligned quasiparticles are crucial for realizing J_x > J_y provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting experimental wobbling bands in this mass region.
