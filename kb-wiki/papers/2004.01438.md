# The scalar $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances and vector mesons in the single Cabibbo-suppressed decays $Λ_c \to p K^+K^-$ and $pπ^+π^-$

> In the chiral unitary approach, we have studied the single Cabibbo-suppressed decays $Λ_c\to pK^+K^-$ and $Λ_c \to p π^+π^-$ by taking into account the $s$-wave meson-meson interaction as well as the contributions from the intermediate vectors $ϕ$ and $ρ^0$. Our theoretical results for the ratios of the branching fractions of $Λ_c\to p \bar{K}^{*0}$ and $Λ_c\to p ω$ with respect to the one of $Λ_c\to p ϕ$ are in agreement with the experimental data. Within the picture that the scalar resonances $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, and $a_0(980)$ are dynamically generated from the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interactions in $s$-wave, we have calculated the $K^+K^-$ and $π^+π^-$ invariant mass distributions respectively for the decays $Λ_c\to pK^+K^-$ and $Λ_c\to pπ^+π^-$. One can find a broad bump structure for the $f_0(500)$ and a narrow peak for the $f_0(980)$ in the $π^+π^-$ invariant mass distribution of the decay $Λ_c\to pπ^+π^-$. For the $K^+K^-$ invariant mass distribution, in addition to the narrow peak for the $ϕ$ meson, there is an enhancement structure near the $K^+K^-$ threshold mainly due to the contribution from the $f_0(980)$. Both the $π^+π^-$ and $K^+K^-$ invariant mass distributions are compatible with the BESIII measurement. We encourage our experimental colleagues to measure these two decays, which would be helpful to understand the nature of the $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, and $a_0(980)$.

## Key claim

Within the chiral unitary approach, the scalar resonances \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\) are dynamically generated from pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interactions in \(s\)-wave, and the calculated \(K^+K^-\) and \(\pi^+\pi^-\) invariant mass distributions for the decays \(\Lambda_c \to p K^+K^-\) and \(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^+\pi^-\) are compatible with BESIII data, but the results depend strongly on the sign and value of the color factor \(C\) (the relative weight of external versus internal \(W\) emission), with the data favoring \(C=2\) and a much smaller contribution from the \(a_0(980)\).

## Method

The authors used the chiral unitary approach, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels with a kernel from chiral Lagrangians, to describe the \(s\)-wave final-state interactions of pseudoscalar meson pairs. They considered both internal and external \(W\) emission mechanisms for the weak decays \(\Lambda_c \to p K^+K^-\) and \(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^+\pi^-\), and added incoherently the contributions from intermediate vector mesons \(\phi\) and \(\rho^0\) decaying in \(p\)-wave. The loop function for two-meson propagators was regularized with a cutoff \(|\vec{q}_{\rm max}| = 600\) MeV, and the transition amplitudes were obtained from the matrix \(T = [1 - VG]^{-1}V\).

## Key numbers

* Ratio of branching fractions \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \bar{K}^{*0}) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \phi)\): theoretical value 21.6, compared with experimental value \(18.3 \pm 3.5\) from PDG (2018).
* Ratio \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \omega) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \phi)\): theoretical value 0.636, compared with experimental value \(0.85 \pm 0.39\) from PDG (2018).
* Ratio \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \rho^0) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \phi)\): theoretical value 0.640.
* Fitted value of \((V'_P)^2 / \Gamma_{\Lambda_c} = (4.5 \pm 0.4) \times 10^3\) MeV from the two-body decays \(\Lambda_c \to p \bar{K}^{*0}\), \(\Lambda_c \to p \phi\), and \(\Lambda_c \to p \omega\).
* Estimated branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \rho^0) = (6.3 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-4}\).
* BESIII measurements: \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \phi) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^-\pi^+) = (1.81 \pm 0.33 \pm 0.13)\%\); \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^+K^-)_{\text{non-}\phi} / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^-\pi^+) = (9.36 \pm 2.22 \pm 0.71)\%\); \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^+\pi^-) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^-\pi^+) = (6.70 \pm 0.48 \pm 0.25)\%\) from Ablikim et al. (2016).
* LHCb measurements: \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^+K^-) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^-\pi^+) = (1.70 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.03)\%\); \(\mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^+\pi^-) / \mathcal{B}(\Lambda_c \to p K^-\pi^+) = (7.44 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.18)\%\) from Aaij et al. (2017).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to decays of \(D^0\) (Xie et al., 2014), \(D^+_s\) (Molina et al., 2019), \(\bar{B}\) and \(\bar{B}_s\) (Liang et al., 2014, 2015), \(\chi_{c1}\) (Liang et al., 2016), \(\tau^-\) (Dai et al., 2018), and \(J/\psi\) (Liang et al., 2019) to the single Cabibbo-suppressed decays of \(\Lambda_c\). The results are compared with BESIII data from Ablikim et al. (2016) and LHCb data from Aaij et al. (2017). The calculations enable future experimental measurements of \(\Lambda_c \to p K^+K^-\) and \(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^+\pi^-\) to test the molecular nature of the scalar resonances \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\), and the authors suggest that a sizeable \(a_0(980)\) contribution would imply the observable process \(\Lambda_c \to p \pi^0 \eta\), which has not been reported.
