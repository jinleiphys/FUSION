# Variations of nuclear binding with quark masses

> We investigate the variation with light quark mass of the mass of the nucleon as well as the masses of the mesons commonly used in a one-boson-exchange model of the nucleon-nucleon force. Care is taken to evaluate the meson mass shifts at the kinematic point relevant to that problem. Using these results, the corresponding changes in the energy of the 1 S0 anti-bound state, the binding energies of the deuteron, triton and selected finite nuclei are evaluated using a one-boson exchange model. The results are discussed in the context of possible corrections to the standard scenario for big bang nucleosynthesis in the case where, as suggested by recent observations of quasar absorption spectra, the quark masses may have changed over the age of the Universe.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies how changes in light quark mass affect nuclear binding energies, finding that a small variation of 0.05 MeV in the quark mass produces changes in single-particle energies of order 0.1 MeV, with the binding energy per nucleon decreasing linearly as quark mass increases. The results are presented as possible corrections to the standard big bang nucleosynthesis scenario, conditional on the hypothesis that quark masses may have changed over cosmic time as suggested by quasar absorption spectra observations.

## Method

The authors used a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model of the nucleon-nucleon force, calculating the variation of meson masses (sigma, rho, omega, pi, eta) with quark mass using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the sigma, lattice QCD fits for the rho and omega, and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for the pion and eta. The nucleon mass variation was obtained from the pion-nucleon sigma commutator using lattice QCD fits. These mass variations were then input into the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for finite nuclei (Li-7, C-12, O-16) and into the Bryan-Scott OBE potential solved via the method of moments for the deuteron, triton, and the S-1-0 anti-bound state.

## Key numbers

*   The pion-nucleon sigma commutator was taken as 45 +/- 6 MeV, giving delta m_N / m_N = 0.048 delta m_q / m_q.
*   The sigma meson mass variation coefficient nu_sigma ranged from 0.089 to 0.032 depending on the bare sigma mass, with the QMC model favouring m_sigma(OBE) near 550 MeV.
*   The rho meson mass variation coefficient was nu_rho = 0.034, and the omega meson coefficient was nu_omega = 0.024.
*   For Li-7, the fractional change in binding energy per nucleon was delta|E|/nucleon / |E|/nucleon = -2.571 delta m_q / m_q.
*   For C-12, the fractional change in binding energy per nucleon was delta|E|/nucleon / |E|/nucleon = -1.438 delta m_q / m_q.
*   For O-16, the fractional change in binding energy per nucleon was delta|E|/nucleon / |E|/nucleon = -1.082 delta m_q / m_q.
*   The S-1-0 anti-bound state pole for the Bryan-Scott potential was found at E_P = -0.0711548 MeV when truncating the effective range expansion to include the k^4 term.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Flambaum and Wiringa (2011), who studied nuclear binding variation with quark mass using the Argonne potential and Schwinger-Dyson estimates of meson mass variations, by instead using a one-boson-exchange model with careful treatment of meson mass shifts at the kinematic point relevant to nuclear forces (near zero momentum transfer). The results enable more precise estimates of possible corrections to big bang nucleosynthesis predictions under the hypothesis of varying fundamental constants, and the comparison between the two approaches provides a way to gauge model dependence in these calculations.
