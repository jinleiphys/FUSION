# Kaon Condensation in Dense Matter

> The kaon energy in neutron matter is calculated analytically with the Klein-Gordon equation, by making a Wigner-Seitz cell approximation and employing a $K^-N$ square well potential. The transition from the low density Lenz potential, proportional to scattering length, to the high density Hartree potential is found to begin at fairly low densities. Exact non-relativistic calculations of the kaon energy in a simple cubic crystal of neutrons are used to test the Wigner-Seitz and the Ericson-Ericson approximation methods. All the calculations indicate that by $\sim 4$ times nuclear matter density the Hartree limit is reached, and as the Hartree potential is less attractive, the density for kaon condensation appears to higher than previously estimated. Effects of a hypothetical repulsive core in the $K^-N$ potential are also studied.

## Key claim

The transition from the low-density Lenz potential (proportional to scattering length) to the high-density Hartree potential begins at fairly low densities, and by approximately 4 times nuclear matter density the Hartree limit is reached; because the Hartree potential is less attractive than previously assumed, the density required for kaon condensation appears higher than earlier estimates.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for kaons in neutron matter using a Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with a square well \(K^-N\) potential, whose depth and range are fixed by the \(K^-n\) scattering length of \(-0.41\) fm and a range of 0.7 fm. They compare these analytic results to exact non-relativistic calculations of the kaon energy in a simple cubic crystal of fixed neutrons, and also test the Ericson-Ericson approximation method. The effects of a hypothetical repulsive core in the \(K^-N\) potential are studied by adjusting the attractive well depth to maintain the same scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The \(K^-n\) scattering length used is \(a_{K^-n} = -0.41\) fm, taken from Brown, Lee, Rho, and Thorsson (1990s).
- The square well potential range is \(R = 0.7\) fm, yielding a potential depth \(V_0 = 122\) MeV with the recoil-corrected Klein-Gordon equation and 126 MeV non-relativistically.
- The kaon mass is \(m_K = 494\) MeV and the neutron mass is \(m_N = 939.5\) MeV.
- The Lenz potential is valid only at very small densities; the next-order correction becomes a quarter of the leading Lenz potential at \(\rho \simeq \rho_0/16\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density.
- The Hartree limit is reached by \(\sim 4 \rho_0\).
- For a repulsive core with \(R=1\) fm and core radius \(R_c = R/2\), the attractive depth required is \(V_0 = 153\) MeV to maintain \(a_{K^-n} = -0.41\) fm.
- The phonon correction to the Hartree potential at \(\rho = 4 \rho_0\) is estimated at \(\sim -16\) MeV for the square well with 0.7 fm radius.
- The Ericson-Ericson correlation parameter for realistic neutron matter is \(\xi \rho r_0 \sim 1.23\) at densities \(\geq 2 \rho_0\), while for a simple cubic lattice it is \(\xi \rho r_0 = 1.54\) and for a neutron Fermi gas it is 0.92.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of kaon condensation originally suggested by Kaplan and Nelson (1980s) and later treated by Brown, Lee, Rho, and Thorsson (1990s) using effective Lagrangians, and by Kaiser, Rho, Waas, and Weise (1990s) using coupled-channel chiral calculations. The authors find that the Hartree limit, which is less attractive than the Lenz potential, pushes the condensation density higher than the \(\sim 4 \rho_0\) estimated by Weise and collaborators. The results enable more realistic estimates of kaon condensation in neutron stars, suggesting that with modern equations of state from Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1990s) and without quark matter admixtures, condensation is unlikely up to \(7 \rho_0\), just above the estimated central densities of massive neutron stars.
