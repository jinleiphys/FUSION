# Continuum-continuum coupling and polarization potentials for weakly bound system

> We investigate the influence of couplings among continuum states in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. For this purpose, we compare cross sections for complete fusion, breakup and elastic scattering evaluated by continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations, including and not including these couplings. In our study, we discuss this influence in terms of the polarization potentials that reproduce the elastic wave function of the coupled coupled channel method in single channel calculations. We find that the inclusion of couplings among the continuum states renders the real part of the polarization potential more repulsive, whereas it leads to weaker apsorption to the breakup channel. We show that the non-inclusion of continuum-continuum couplings in CDCC calculations may not lead to qualitative and quantitative wrong conclusions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of continuum-continuum couplings (CCC) in CDCC calculations renders the real part of the polarization potential repulsive and reduces its imaginary part, which suppresses complete fusion and non-capture breakup cross sections while enhancing elastic scattering at large angles; without CCC, the polarization potential is attractive with stronger absorption, leading to qualitatively and quantitatively wrong conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations for the \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{8}\)B + \(^{58}\)Ni systems, comparing results with and without continuum-continuum couplings. They extracted approximate angular-momentum-independent polarization potentials following the prescription of Thompson et al. (1989), averaging over angular momentum using radial wave functions and S-matrices from the CDCC calculations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb, inclusion of CCC leads to a reduction of the complete fusion cross section by about two orders of magnitude at near- and sub-barrier energies.
- For \(^{8}\)B + \(^{58}\)Ni, elastic scattering calculations were performed at a laboratory energy of 23.77 MeV, corresponding to \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 20.8\) MeV.
- The imaginary part of the polarization potential is always negative, both with and without CCC, for the \(^{8}\)B + \(^{58}\)Ni system.

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC studies by Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) on \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb, which first showed the importance of CCC for fusion suppression, and by Lubian and Nunes (2007) on \(^{8}\)B + \(^{58}\)Ni breakup. The results agree qualitatively with the dynamic polarization potential analysis of Sakuragi et al. (1983) for \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{28}\)Si and \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{40}\)Ca at higher energies. The findings enable a clearer physical interpretation of CCC effects through the polarization potential formalism, distinguishing genuine breakup from a collection of inelastic channels.
