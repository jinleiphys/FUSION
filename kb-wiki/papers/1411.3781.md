# On the Structure of the Lambda 1405

> For almost 50 years the structure of the Lambda 1405 resonance has been a mystery. Recently, a new lattice QCD simulation showing that its strange magnetic form factor vanishes, together with a comprehensive Hamiltonian analysis of the lattice QCD energy levels, has unambiguously established that the structure is dominated by a bound anti-kaon--nucleon component [1]. Here we present supplementary information for Ref. [1] including a presentation of the relevant Hamiltonian effective field theory and an illustration of the volume dependence of the results and their connection to the infinite volume limit of Nature.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD calculation, combined with a Hamiltonian effective field theory analysis, has unambiguously established that the Λ(1405) resonance structure is dominated by a bound anti-kaon–nucleon (K̅N) component, as evidenced by the vanishing strange quark contribution to its magnetic form factor in the regime where the up and down quark masses approach their physical values.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of the Λ(1405) using PACS-CS 2+1 flavor configurations. They constructed a finite-volume Hamiltonian effective field theory including four octet meson-baryon interaction channels (πΣ, K̅N, KΞ, ηΛ) with S-wave interactions, where the coupling constants are related to SU(3)-flavor singlet couplings. The Hamiltonian eigenvalues were fitted to the lattice QCD energy levels by minimizing chi-square for the parameters m₀ and α₀ at different values of m_π², and the infinite-volume resonance energy was reconstructed via principal-value continuum integrals.

## Key numbers

- The πΣ decay width of the Λ(1405) takes the physical value of 50 ± 2 MeV at the physical pion mass, used to set the SU(3) coupling parameter ξ₀ = 0.75
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV is used in the interaction coupling formula
- The regularization scale of the dipole regulator is Λ = 0.8 GeV
- The scale is set via the Sommer parameter with r₀ = 0.492 fm
- At the lightest quark mass, m_π = 156 MeV, the volume dependence of the spectrum is examined
- The bootstrap error analysis provides a resonance energy of 1.48 +0.17 / -0.07 GeV at the physical pion mass in infinite volume
- The most probable value from the bootstrap distribution is 1.41 GeV, in good agreement with experiment
- For m_π < 216 MeV, the πΣ channel opens and the infinite-volume wave-function renormalisation can no longer be defined in the resonant region

## Context

This work resolves a nearly 50-year mystery regarding the structure of the Λ(1405) resonance, first discovered in the 1960s by Engler (1965). The result extends earlier lattice QCD work by Menadue (2011) that first discovered a low-lying Λ(1405) mass, which was independently confirmed by Engel (2012). The Hamiltonian effective field theory approach follows a procedure similar to that used for the Δ → Nπ analysis by Hall (2013). The finding that the Λ(1405) is a molecular K̅N bound state rather than a πΣ state or an elementary three-quark state enables future studies of exotic hadron structure and the role of sea-quark loops in generating five-quark states from three-quark operators.
