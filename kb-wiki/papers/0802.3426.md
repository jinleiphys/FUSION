# alpha-alpha Scattering in Halo Effective Field Theory

> We study the two-alpha-particle (alpha-alpha) system in an Effective Field Theory (EFT) for halo-like systems. We propose a power counting that incorporates the subtle interplay of strong and electromagnetic forces leading to a narrow resonance at an energy of about 0.1 MeV. We investigate the EFT expansion in detail, and compare its results with existing low-energy alpha-alpha phase shifts and previously determined effective-range parameters. Good description of the data is obtained with a surprising amount of fine-tuning. This scenario can be viewed as an expansion around the limit where, when electromagnetic interactions are turned off, the Be-8 ground state is at threshold and exhibits conformal invariance. We also discuss possible extensions to systems with more than two alpha particles.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a power-counting scheme for alpha-alpha scattering in Halo Effective Field Theory where the narrow $^8$Be ground-state resonance arises from a fine-tuning that, in the absence of Coulomb interactions, would place the system at the unitary limit exhibiting conformal invariance; the Coulomb interaction breaks this scale invariance and moves the ground state away from threshold.

## Method
The authors construct an Effective Field Theory for two alpha particles using a scalar-isoscalar field for the alpha particle and an auxiliary scalar dimeron field for the $S$-wave $^8$Be resonance, with Coulomb interactions included non-perturbatively via the Kong-Ravndal formalism. They propose a power counting where the dimeron mass parameter scales as $\Delta \sim M_{lo}^2/\mu$ (with $M_{lo} \sim 20$ MeV) rather than the simpler $\Delta \sim M_{lo}M_{hi}/\mu$ used in nucleon-nucleon scattering, and expand the amplitude around the resonance pole position to avoid multiple kinematical fine-tunings. The EFT expansion is compared to existing low-energy alpha-alpha phase shift data and previously determined effective-range parameters.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy: $E_R \simeq 0.1$ MeV above threshold in center-of-mass frame (corresponding to $E_{LAB}^R = 184.15 \pm 0.07$ keV)
- Resonance width: $\Gamma_R \simeq 6$ eV (corresponding to $\Gamma_{LAB}^R = 11.14 \pm 0.50$ eV)
- Resonance momentum: $k_R = \sqrt{2\mu E_R} \approx 20$ MeV (later refined to $k_R \approx 18.5$ MeV)
- Coulomb momentum scale: $k_C = Z_1 Z_2 \alpha_{em} \mu \approx 60$ MeV
- High-energy scale estimate: $M_{hi} \sim \sqrt{m_N E^{*}_{\alpha}} \sim m_\pi \simeq 140$ MeV
- Expansion parameter estimate: $M_{lo}/M_{hi} \sim 1/7$
- Effective-range parameters from Ref. [R67]: $a_0 = (-1.65 \pm 0.17) \times 10^3$ fm, $r_0 = (1.084 \pm 0.011)$ fm, ${\cal P}_0 = (-1.76 \pm 0.22)$ fm$^3$
- EFT applicability limit: laboratory energies up to $E_{LAB} \sim 3$ MeV (CM momentum $\sim 70$ MeV)

## Context
This work extends the Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory framework previously developed for neutron-alpha scattering (BHvK1, BHvK2) and proton-alpha scattering (BRvK) to the alpha-alpha system, which is a necessary input for describing multi-alpha systems such as $^9$Be and the Hoyle state of $^{12}$C. The proposed power counting differs from the standard approach used for nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kong and Ravndal, 2000) and for $P$-wave $N\alpha$ scattering, and the resulting expansion around the resonance pole provides a systematic alternative to the effective-range expansion. The findings enable future calculations of three-alpha systems where, in the unitary limit without Coulomb, $^{12}$C would exhibit an exact Efimov spectrum.
