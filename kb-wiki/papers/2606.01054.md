# Examination of the $α$-cluster breaking in the four $0^+$ bands of $^{12}$C with the variation of multiple bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> I investigate $^{12}$C, particularly the four kinds of the $0^+$ bands with various types of the $3α$ configurations. These states are obtained in the variation of the multiple bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, where the bases are optimized simultaneously in the variation of the total energy. In the $0^+_2$ Hoyle state and the $0^+_3$ linear-chain state, I confirm a mixture of the $α$-cluster breaking of the $s$-wave configuration with contributions from the spin-orbit force, while the $0^+_4$ state exhibits a relatively pure $3α$ cluster state characterized by a large radius. The $2^+_{2-4}$ and $4^+_{2-4}$ states also tend to be the pure $3α$ cluster states. The monopole transitions between the $0^+_{2}$ and $0^+_{4}$ bands exhibit large values, suggesting breathing mode of the $3α$ states in the $0^+_4$ band. This conclusion aligns with the predictions of the $3α$ models with an $α$ condensate and also with a neural network, although the order of the $0^+_3$ and $0^+_4$ bands is reversed in the present results due to the attraction of the spin-orbit force.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that the $0^+_2$ Hoyle state and the $0^+_3$ linear-chain state in $^{12}$C contain a mixture of $\alpha$-cluster breaking from the $s$-wave configuration due to spin-orbit force contributions, while the $0^+_4$ state remains a relatively pure $3\alpha$ cluster state with large radius, though the order of the $0^+_3$ and $0^+_4$ bands is reversed compared to $3\alpha$ cluster model predictions because of the spin-orbit force attraction.

## Method
The author applied the variation of multiple bases of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), using a "multicool method" that simultaneously optimizes multiple AMD basis states through imaginary-time evolution. Excited-state configurations were generated by introducing a pseudopotential with varying strength $\lambda$ to enforce orthogonality to the ground-state configurations, and the resulting basis states were superposed with angular-momentum projection to solve the eigenvalue problem for each $J^\pm$ state of $^{12}$C.

## Key numbers
- Total energy of the $^{12}$C ground state ($0^+_1$): $-88.59$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of $-92.16$ MeV
- Matter radius of the $0^+_1$ state: 2.35 fm, matching the experimental value of 2.35(2) fm
- Charge radius of the $0^+_1$ state: 2.48 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.4702(22) fm
- Expectation value of the principal quantum number operator $\langle N \rangle$ for the $0^+_1$ state: 9.55; for $0^+_2$: 28.60; for $0^+_3$: 39.17; for $0^+_4$: 47.63
- Matter radii of the $0^+$ states: $0^+_1$ = 2.35 fm, $0^+_2$ = 3.63 fm, $0^+_3$ = 4.10 fm, $0^+_4$ = 4.48 fm
- Energy gain from single AMD basis to multicool basis: 6.2 MeV for positive parity, 6.6 MeV for negative parity
- The $0^+_2$ Hoyle state is located 0.4 MeV above the $3\alpha$ breakup threshold energy

## Context
This work extends previous AMD studies of light nuclei by Myo and collaborators (2023-2026) using the multicool method, and it directly addresses the ongoing debate between $3\alpha$ cluster models and non-cluster models regarding the number and ordering of $0^+$ bands in $^{12}$C. The results align with predictions from $3\alpha$ models with an $\alpha$ condensate (Tohsaki, 2001; Bo, 2020) and neural network approaches (Cheng, 2025), but reverse the order of the $0^+_3$ and $0^+_4$ bands due to spin-orbit force effects that cannot be treated in pure $3\alpha$ models. The work enables future studies of cluster states using the multicool method combined with complex scaling for proper treatment of unbound resonances.
