# Recombinant Charmonium in strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We update our previous work of a Langevin-with-interaction model for charmonium in heavy-ion collisions, by considering the effect due to recombination. We determine the contribution to J/psi yields from charm-anticharm pairs whose constituent quarks originate from two different hard processes. Like the surviving J/psi states, the recombinant J/psi also undergo both a stochastic interaction, determined by a hydrodynamical simulation of the heavy-ion collision, and an interaction determined by the heavy quark-antiquark potentials measured on the lattice for appropriate temperatures. From the results of these simulations, we determine both the direct and the recombinant contribution to the J/psi yields for RHIC conditions, and find that for central collisions, between 30% and 50% of the J/psi yield is due to recombinant production. We compare our results with other models and look for how the recombinant contribution differs from the surviving contribution in the differential p_t yields. Including the recombinant contribution improves the agreement with the latest analysis of charmonium at RHIC, which shows an absence of anomalous suppression except in the most central collisions.

# Recombinant Charmonium in strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The recombinant contribution to J/psi yields in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is between 30% and 50% of the total yield, but this fraction depends on the value of the critical temperature T_c; a higher T_c (190 MeV) gives a smaller recombinant fraction (less than 1/3) while improving overall agreement with data.

## Method

The authors simulate an ensemble of charm-anticharm pairs generated by PYTHIA event generation, then evolve them using a Langevin-with-interaction model in a hydrodynamical simulation of the heavy-ion collision. The drag and random force terms are constrained by the diffusion coefficient for charm from independent measurements, and the heavy quark-antiquark potentials are taken from lattice QCD correlators for two Polyakov lines. For each ensemble element containing multiple charm pairs, the probability of recombinant charmonium formation is calculated by averaging over possible pairings of unbound quarks, simplified by the observation that at RHIC densities only 5.5% of charm quarks have one neighbor and 0.2% have more than one neighbor within binding range.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, between 30% and 50% of the J/psi yield is due to recombinant production.
- The average number of charm-anticharm pairs reaches a maximum of 19 pairs per central collision.
- 5.5% of charm quarks in the most central Au+Au collisions have exactly one neighbor (anti-quark from a different hard process) within binding range defined by V_cornell(r) < 0.88 GeV; only 0.2% have more than one neighbor.
- Results are shown for two values of the critical temperature: T_c = 165 MeV and T_c = 190 MeV.
- The sQGP phase exists for tau ~ 5 fm/c in realistic RHIC simulations.
- The measured cross sections for charm production reach about 800 microbarns for semi-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Langevin-with-interaction model (Young and Shuryak, 2008) by adding recombinant charmonium production from charm quarks originating from different hard processes. It contrasts with the statistical hadronization approach of Braun-Munzinger (2000), which assumes complete thermal equilibration, and with the two-component model of Grandchamp and Rapp (2002), which introduces a correlation volume parameter. The results resemble those of Zhao and Rapp (2008) but differ in that the present model treats direct and recombinant components dynamically without free parameters, and the direct charmonium states form a quasi-equilibrium distribution. The improved agreement with PHENIX data using the new cold nuclear matter analysis (Leitch, 2009) enables future work on differential p_T yields at LHC energies where charm densities are higher.
