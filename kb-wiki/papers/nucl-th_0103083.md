# J/Psi suppression in central Pb-Pb collisions

> We discuss the recent NA50 J/Psi production data in Pb--Pb collisions, in particular the second drop at high transverse energies which correspond to the most central collisions. Using a model which relates the J/Psi suppression to the local energy density, we show that the data can be explained by taking into account transverse energy fluctuations at a given impact parameter. Predictions of this model for RHIC are briefly discussed.

# J/Psi suppression in central Pb-Pb collisions

## Key claim

The second drop in J/Psi production at high transverse energies observed by NA50 in central Pb-Pb collisions can be explained by taking into account transverse energy fluctuations at a given impact parameter, without requiring a sharp phase transition. However, the structure in the suppression pattern cannot be interpreted as a signal of successive meltings of charmonium resonances, because an identically good fit is obtained for gradual suppression above a single threshold.

## Method

The authors use a geometrical model where J/Psi suppression occurs when the local energy density exceeds a critical threshold, with the local energy density assumed proportional to the density of participant nucleons per unit transverse area calculated in a Glauber model. They incorporate transverse energy fluctuations by taking the distribution of E_T at fixed impact parameter as a gaussian, with mean proportional to the number of participants and dispersion determined by a dimensionless parameter, and then replace the energy density estimate with one proportional to (E_T / <E_T>(b)) times the participant density.

## Key numbers

- The fit parameters for the E_T distribution are q = 0.274 GeV (mean E_T per participant) and a = 1.27 (dimensionless dispersion parameter), as determined by the NA50 collaboration.
- The intrinsic calorimeter resolution is given by sigma_intrinsic(E_T)/E_T = alpha/sqrt(E_T) + beta, with alpha = 0.2 GeV^(1/2) and beta = 0.005.
- Physical E_T fluctuations differ by only 10% from observed fluctuations for central collisions at E_T ~ 100 GeV.
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section used is sigma_NN = 32 mb at SPS energies and sigma_NN = 41 mb at RHIC energies.
- Total multiplicity is larger by 70% at RHIC than at SPS in central collisions, so the energy density is assumed increased by 70% at RHIC.
- Around the knee, the suppression for Au-Au collisions at RHIC is about 2.5 times greater than for Pb-Pb at SPS.

## Context

This work extends the earlier geometrical model of Blaizot (1996) that related anomalous J/Psi suppression to local energy density, and addresses the second drop in the NA50 data of Abreu (2000). The authors incorporate E_T fluctuations following the approach of Kharzeev (1997) and compare with the centrality dependence of average transverse momentum measured by NA50 (Abreu 2000). The predictions for RHIC build on the PHOBOS measurement of Back (2000) and consider the possible role of multiple c-cbar pair production as discussed by Braun-Munzinger (2000), which may mask the suppression mechanism at higher energies.
