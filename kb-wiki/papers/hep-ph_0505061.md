# On the Ground State of Two Flavor Color Superconductor

> The diquark condensate susceptibility in neutral color superconductor at moderate baryon density is calculated in the frame of two flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. When color chemical potential is introduced to keep charge neutrality, the diquark condensate susceptibility is negative in the directions without diquark condensate in color space, which may be regarded as a signal of the instability of the conventional ground state with only diquark condensate in the color 3 direction.

## Key claim

The conventional ground state of a two-flavor color superconductor, which has a diquark condensate only in the color-3 direction, is unstable when color charge neutrality is enforced via a nonzero color chemical potential μ₈, because the diquark condensate susceptibility κ in the other two color directions becomes negative.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, introducing a color chemical potential μ₈ to enforce color neutrality and an electric chemical potential μₑ for electric neutrality. They compute the diquark condensate susceptibility κ, defined as the second derivative of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the condensates Δ₁ and Δ₂, by perturbing around the conventional ground state where Δ₁ = Δ₂ = 0 and Δ₃ ≠ 0. The susceptibility is evaluated analytically and numerically using the gap equation and neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- The minimum baryon chemical potential for the color superconductivity phase to start is μ_b/3 = 330 MeV, using parameters G_S = 5.01 GeV⁻², Λ = 0.653 GeV, and G_D = 3G_S/4.
- The color chemical potential μ₈ is only a few MeV in the whole color-breaking phase, much smaller than the order parameter Δ, the baryon chemical potential μ_b, and the momentum cutoff Λ (all of order hundreds of MeV).
- The diquark condensate susceptibility κ is negative in the entire color-breaking phase, both with and without electrical charge neutrality, as shown analytically and numerically at temperature T = 0.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutral color superconductivity (Neutral-2, 2003; Gapless, 2003) by examining the stability of the conventional diquark condensate direction when color symmetry is explicitly broken by μ₈. It contradicts the assumption that the ground state with only Δ₃ ≠ 0 remains valid under neutrality constraints, as previously used in the two-flavor NJL model (Schafer, 2000). The result motivates future searches for alternative ground states, such as those with condensates in multiple color directions, and connects to studies of negative Meissner screening masses (Meissner, 2004) and finite-temperature effects (Calabash, 2004).
