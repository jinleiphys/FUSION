# Photoproduction of eta and eta-prime mesons off nucleons close to threshold

> The importance of Born terms and resonance exchange for eta and eta-prime photoproduction off both the proton and neutron within U(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory is investigated. Low-lying resonances such as the vector mesons and J^P= 1/2^+, 1/2^- baryon resonances are included explicitly and their contributions together with the Born terms are calculated. The coupling constants of the resonances are determined from strong and radiative decays. We obtain reasonable agreement with experimental data near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within a U(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory framework, the contributions from Born terms and low-lying resonances (vector mesons and J^P = 1/2+, 1/2- baryon resonances) can yield reasonable qualitative agreement with experimental data for eta and eta-prime photoproduction near threshold, but the author explicitly states that this simplified treatment does not allow reproduction of all experimental data in detail and that chiral loops and further resonances are needed for a better description.

## Method

The author constructed a U(3) chiral effective Lagrangian including pseudoscalar meson nonet (pi, K, eta_8, eta_0) coupled to the ground state baryon octet (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) at lowest order, then explicitly included vector meson exchange in the t-channel (rho, omega, phi) and S-wave (S_11(1535)) and P-wave (P_11(1440)) baryon resonances in the s- and u-channels. Coupling constants for the resonances were determined from experimental strong and radiative decay widths rather than fitted to photoproduction data, and the eta-eta-prime mixing angle was taken from two-photon decay measurements.

## Key numbers

* Eta photoproduction on the proton: total cross section near threshold is dominated by the S_11(1535) resonance, with the Born terms alone giving negligible contribution (dashed lines in Fig. 4 show Born contributions are much smaller than full calculation)
* Eta-prime photoproduction on the proton: total cross section approximately 1.5 microbarns near threshold, consistent with experimental data from ELSA (Pl, year not given) which had energy bins of 100 MeV and 200 MeV width
* Eta-prime photoproduction on the neutron: the author provides predictions for cross sections (no specific numerical value given in the truncated text)
* The S_11(1535) resonance mass used: M_S = 1.535 GeV; the P_11(1440) resonance mass used: M_P = 1.44 GeV
* Coupling constants determined from decays: D_S = 0.37, F_S = -0.21, lambda_S = -0.07, d_S = -0.07 GeV^{-1}, f_S = -0.06 GeV^{-1} for the S-wave octet; D_P = 0.32, F_P = 0.16, d_P = -0.05 GeV^{-1}, f_P = 0.08 GeV^{-1} for the P-wave octet
* Vector meson couplings to eta: g_{rho gamma eta} = 1.8 GeV^{-1}, g_{omega gamma eta} = 0.23 GeV^{-1}; to eta-prime: g_{rho gamma eta-prime} = 1.31 GeV^{-1}, g_{omega gamma eta-prime} = 0.45 GeV^{-1}
* Eta-eta-prime mixing angle: approximately -20 degrees, determined from two-photon decays of pi^0, eta, eta-prime

## Context

This work extends the U(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Borasoy (year not given) for including the eta-prime meson, applying it to photoproduction processes for the first time. It contrasts with earlier effective Lagrangian approaches (ZMB, year not given) that used a pseudoscalar coupling of eta-prime to nucleons and found dominance of the D_13(2080) resonance, and with quark model calculations (Li, year not given) that emphasized off-shell effects of S_11(1535). The experimental data from ELSA (Pl, year not given) had suggested coherent excitation of two resonances S_11(1897) and P_11(1986) for eta-prime photoproduction, but this work finds that the S_11(1535) resonance alone can explain the cross section near threshold. The approach enables predictions for eta-prime photoproduction on the neutron, which had been measured at ELSA (Ho, year not given) for eta but not for eta-prime.
