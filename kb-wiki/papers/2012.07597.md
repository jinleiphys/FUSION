# Global $Λ$ polarization in moderately relativistic nuclear collisions

> Predictions for the global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at moderately relativistic collision energies, 2.4 $\leq\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq$ 11 GeV, are made. These are based on the thermodynamic approach to the global polarization incorporated into the model of the three-fluid dynamics. Centrality dependence of the polarization is studied. It is predicted that the polarization reaches a maximum or a plateau (depending on the equation of state and centrality) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx$ 3 GeV. It is found that the global polarization increases with increasing width of the rapidity window around the midrapidity.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Λ hyperons in Au+Au collisions is predicted to reach a maximum or a plateau at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx\) 3 GeV, depending on the equation of state and centrality, rather than a simple monotonic increase with decreasing collision energy.

## Method

The thermodynamic approach to global polarization was implemented into the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the early nonequilibrium stage of the collision as two counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids and a fireball fluid for newly produced particles. Calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions at impact parameters b = 2, 4, 6, and 8 fm using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a first-order phase transition EoS, and a crossover EoS. The polarization was averaged over the midrapidity region \(|y_h| \lesssim 0.5\), roughly simulating the STAR acceptance in pseudorapidity.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization reaches 6–8% at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =\) 3.3 GeV for impact parameter b = 8 fm, depending on the equation of state.
- The maximum or plateau in polarization occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx\) 3 GeV, in agreement with the vorticity maximum predicted by Deng (2020).
- The energy range studied is 2.4 \(\leq \sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq\) 11 GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The impact parameter b = 8 fm roughly corresponds to the STAR centrality selection of 20–50%.
- The rapidity window half-width is approximately 0.5, but is 0.45 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =\) 2.42 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic and transport model calculations of global Λ polarization (Karpenko 2016, Xie 2016, Ivanov 2019) to the lower energy range of HADES, BM@N, FAIR, and NICA facilities, where the STAR fixed-target program and HADES are already taking data. The prediction of a maximum in polarization at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx\) 3 GeV agrees with the vorticity maximum found by Deng (2020) and contrasts with the monotonic increase expected from total angular momentum arguments, because vorticity migrates to backward-forward rapidities at higher energies (Ivanov 2019). The results enable experimental tests at NICA (4–11 GeV), FAIR (2.7–4.9 GeV), and HADES (2.3–2.6 GeV), and the preliminary HADES result at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =\) 2.4 GeV does not contradict the 3FD prediction at b = 6 fm.
