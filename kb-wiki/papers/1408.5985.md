# Shell-gap reduced level densities in $^{89,90}$Y

> Particle-$γ$ coincidences from the $^{89}$Y($p,p' γ$)$^{89}$Y and $^{89}$Y($d,p γ$)$^{90}$Y reactions were utilized to obtain $γ$-ray spectra as function of excitation energy. The Oslo method was used to extract the level density from the particle-$γ$ coincidence matrices. The impact of the $N=50$ shell closure on the level densities is discussed within the framework of a combinatorial quasi-particle model.

## Key claim

The level densities of $^{89,90}$Y are strongly reduced by the $N=50$ shell closure, and the constant-temperature behavior observed above $E \approx 3$ MeV is interpreted as a signature of a first-order phase transition, but this interpretation depends on the microcanonical combinatorial model's ability to reproduce the data.

## Method

Particle-gamma coincidence data were collected from the $^{89}$Y($p,p' \gamma$)$^{89}$Y reaction with a 17-MeV proton beam and the $^{89}$Y($d,p \gamma$)$^{90}$Y reaction with an 11-MeV deuteron beam at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory. The outgoing charged particles were measured with the SiRi silicon telescope system, and coincident gamma rays were detected with the CACTUS NaI(Tl) array. The Oslo method was applied to extract level densities from the primary gamma-ray spectra by iteratively fitting the product of level density and transmission coefficient to the two-dimensional coincidence matrices.

## Key numbers

- Level density of $^{89}$Y at $E = 7$ MeV: $\rho(7\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 690 \pm 170\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$, estimated from systematics of neighboring Sr and Y isotopes.
- Level density of $^{90}$Y at the neutron separation energy $S_n = 6.857$ MeV: $\rho(S_n) = 3.77(81) \times 10^3\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$, calculated from the neutron resonance spacing $D_0 = 3700(400)$ eV.
- Level density of $^{89}$Y at $S_n = 11.478$ MeV: $\rho(S_n) = 60(20) \times 10^3\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$, extrapolated from the $E=7$ MeV anchor point using a constant-temperature formula with $T = 1.00$ MeV.
- The level density of odd-odd $^{90}$Y is on average 6 times that of even-odd $^{89}$Y across the measured excitation energy range.
- Below $E = 2.6$ MeV, there are 36 known levels in $^{90}$Y and only 7 levels in $^{89}$Y.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Oslo method to nuclei near the $N=50$ shell closure, where level densities are particularly low and difficult to measure. The results are compared with the temperature-dependent combinatorial level density calculations using the D1M Gogny force by Hilaire et al. (2012), which underestimate the experimental level densities by factors of 10-100 at low excitation energies, indicating that those calculations employ too large an effective $N=50$ shell gap. The microcanonical combinatorial model (muCM) developed here reproduces the data well and enables predictions of parity asymmetry and spin distributions in the quasi-continuum, which are relevant for reaction cross-section calculations in astrophysical nucleosynthesis and nuclear energy applications.
