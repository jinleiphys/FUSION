# Prediction of narrow $N^{*}$ and $Λ^*$ resonances with hidden charm above 4 GeV

> The interaction between various charmed mesons and charmed baryons are studied within the framework of the coupled channel unitary approach with the local hidden gauge formalism. Several meson-baryon dynamically generated narrow $N^*$ and $Λ^*$ resonances with hidden charm are predicted with mass above 4 GeV and width smaller than 100 MeV. The predicted new resonances definitely cannot be accommodated by quark models with three constituent quarks and can be looked for at the forthcoming PANDA/FAIR experiments.

# Prediction of narrow $N^{*}$ and $\Lambda^*$ resonances with hidden charm above 4 GeV

## Key claim
The paper predicts six narrow $N^*$ and $\Lambda^*$ resonances with hidden charm, with masses above 4 GeV and widths smaller than 100 MeV, dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions. The authors hedge that these states are predicted within a specific coupled-channel unitary approach using the local hidden gauge formalism, and that the results depend on regularization parameters (subtraction constant $a_\mu \approx -2.3$ or cutoff $\Lambda \approx 0.8$ GeV) chosen to be within the natural range for effective theories.

## Method
The authors study pseudoscalar-baryon ($PB$) and vector-baryon ($VB$) interactions by exchanging a vector meson, using effective Lagrangians from the local hidden gauge formalism extended from three flavors to four (SU(4)). The transition potentials are derived under a low-energy approximation, and the coupled-channel scattering matrix $T = [1-VG]^{-1}V$ is solved via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, with the loop function $G$ regularized using dimensional regularization (subtraction constant $a_\mu = -2.3$) or a three-momentum cutoff ($\Lambda \approx 0.8$ GeV). Poles in the $T$ matrix are identified as bound states (first Riemann sheet below threshold) or resonances (second Riemann sheet above threshold), and decay widths are estimated by coupling to light meson-baryon and charmonium-baryon channels through box diagrams with heavy charmed meson exchanges.

## Key numbers
- Six bound states are found: four from $PB \to PB$ and two from $VB \to VB$, with pole positions at 4269 MeV ($\bar{D}\Sigma_c$, $I=1/2$, $S=0$), 4213 MeV and 4403 MeV ($\bar{D}_s\Lambda_c^+$ and $\bar{D}\Xi_c$ coupled, $I=0$, $S=-1$), 4418 MeV ($\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$, $I=1/2$, $S=0$), and 4370 MeV and 4550 MeV ($\bar{D}_s^*\Lambda_c^+$ and $\bar{D}^*\Xi_c$ coupled, $I=0$, $S=-1$).
- After including decay channels, the masses and total widths are: $N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)$ with $\Gamma = 56.9$ MeV; $\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}(4209)$ with $\Gamma = 32.4$ MeV; $\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}(4394)$ with $\Gamma = 43.3$ MeV (from $PB$); $N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4412)$ with $\Gamma = 47.3$ MeV; $\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}(4368)$ with $\Gamma = 28.0$ MeV; $\Lambda^*_{c\bar{c}}(4544)$ with $\Gamma = 36.6$ MeV (from $VB$).
- The partial decay width of $N^{*+}_{c\bar{c}}(4265)$ to $J/\psi\;p$ is estimated at about 0.01 MeV, three orders of magnitude smaller than its decay to $\eta_c p$ (23.4 MeV).
- For the reaction $p\bar{p} \to p\bar{p} \eta_c$ at FAIR ($\bar{p}$ beam of 15 GeV/c, $\sqrt{s}=5470$ MeV), the new mechanism via $N^{*+}_{c\bar{c}}(4265)$ gives a cross section of about $0.1\ \mu$b, compared to $0.1$ nb from conventional mechanisms, corresponding to over 80000 events per day at the designed luminosity of $10^{31}\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}$.
- For $p\bar{p} \to p\bar{p} J/\psi$, the contribution from $N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4415)$ is estimated at about 2 nb, corresponding to about 1700 events per day.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel unitary approach of Ramos and Oset (2000) from three flavors to four, predicting hidden-charm baryon resonances as meson-baryon molecular states analogous to the well-known $N^*(1535)$ and $\Lambda^*(1405)$ in the light-quark sector. The predicted states have masses above 4.2 GeV, which the authors argue cannot be accommodated by conventional three-quark quark models, unlike lower-mass dynamically generated states that are difficult to distinguish from quark-model predictions. The results enable experimental searches at the forthcoming PANDA/FAIR facility, with estimated production rates high enough for observation, and may have implications for puzzles in charmonium production near threshold, such as the spin-spin correlation in $pp$ elastic scattering observed by Brodsky (2003) and difficulties in $J/\psi$ production cross sections and polarizations discussed by Brambilla (2004) and Gongbin (2008).
