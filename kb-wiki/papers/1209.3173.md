# Charged current neutrino interactions in core-collapse supernovae in a virial expansion

> Core-collapse supernovae may depend sensitively on charged current neutrino interactions in warm, low density neutron rich matter. A proton in neutron rich matter is more tightly bound than is a neutron. This energy shift ΔU increases the electron energy in ν_e + n --> p + e, increasing the available phase space and absorption cross section. Likewise ΔU decreases the positron energy in \bar ν_e + p --> n + e^+, decreasing the phase space and cross section. We have calculated ΔU using a model independent virial expansion and we find ΔU is much larger, at low densities, than the predictions of many mean field models. Therefore ΔU could have a significant impact on charged current neutrino interactions in supernovae. Preliminary simulations of the accretion phase of core-collapse supernovae find that ΔU increases \bar ν_e energies and decreases the ν_e luminosity.

## Key claim

The energy shift ΔU (difference between neutron and proton potential energies in neutron-rich matter) calculated via a model-independent virial expansion is much larger at low densities than predictions from many mean-field models, leading to significantly larger changes in charged-current neutrino absorption cross sections than previously estimated.

## Method

The authors calculated the energy shift ΔU using a virial expansion of the pressure and free energy density to second order in neutron and proton fugacities, with virial coefficients derived from nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering phase shifts. They then computed in-medium neutrino and antineutrino absorption cross sections using a mean-field response function that incorporates ΔU, and performed spherically-symmetric general-relativistic hydrodynamics simulations of the accretion phase of a 15 solar mass core-collapse supernova using the nuGR1D code with modified absorption cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At density n=0.001 fm^-3 and temperature T=5 MeV, the virial expansion gives ΔU = 2.27 MeV (using chemical potential difference method), compared to 0.23 MeV for the GM3 mean-field model and 1.11 MeV for the IUFSU mean-field model
- At n=0.001 fm^-3 and T=5 MeV with neutrino energy 15 MeV, the neutrino absorption cross section is enhanced by 73% and the antineutrino absorption cross section is reduced by 28% due to energy shifts
- In supernova simulations at 100 ms after bounce, the energy shift increases the average antineutrino energy by approximately 0.25 MeV (1.8%) and decreases the electron neutrino luminosity by 1.5 B s^-1 (3%)
- At 450 ms after bounce, the average antineutrino energy increase grows to approximately 0.94 MeV (5%) and the electron neutrino luminosity decrease grows to 2.5 B s^-1 (10%)

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Roberts (2012) and Roberts and Reddy (2012) that strong interaction energy shifts in warm low-density neutron-rich matter affect charged-current neutrino cross sections, but uses a model-independent virial expansion rather than the mean-field approximations employed in those earlier studies and in simulations by Martinez-Pinedo (2012). The virial expansion approach builds on earlier calculations of the symmetry energy at low densities by Horowitz (2005) and is consistent with heavy-ion collision measurements by Kowalski (2007) that found symmetry energies much larger than mean-field predictions. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino spectra and luminosities in core-collapse supernovae, with implications for supernova neutrino detection and r-process nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven wind.
