# Coalescence of two exceptional points in the anti-hermitian 3-level pairing model

> The formation of a higher-order singularity by the coalescence of two exceptional points is studied in the anti-hermitian limit of the complex-extended 3-level Richardson-Gaudin model.

# Coalescence of two exceptional points in the anti-hermitian 3-level pairing model

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the anti-hermitian limit of the non-integrable 3-level Richardson-Gaudin pairing model, two exceptional points (EPs) can coalesce to form a higher-order singularity, a pseudo-diabolic point (pseudo-DP), at the specific ratio \( g'/g = -1/2 \), where the real parts of all eigenenergies become identical while two eigenvectors merge into a Jordan block, unlike an ordinary diabolic point in a hermitian system.

## Method

The authors study the non-integrable 3-level pairing Hamiltonian with complex coupling constants \( g \) and \( g' = \gamma g \), using the pair representation of SU(2) algebra with three levels of degeneracies \( \Omega_1=2, \Omega_2=6, \Omega_3=2 \) and single-particle energies \( \varepsilon_1=0, \varepsilon_2=1, \varepsilon_3=2 \). They locate eigenvalue degeneracies in the complex \( g \)-plane by numerically finding sharp minima of the discriminant polynomial \( D(g) \) of degree \( M=12 \) (for \( n=4 \) many-body states), and analyze the behavior of eigenvalues and eigenvectors along trajectories encircling the singularity.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-DP forms at \( g = -i/(4\sqrt{2}) \approx -0.177i \) in the complex \( g \)-plane, at the coupling ratio \( g'/g = -1/2 \).
- The model space contains \( n=4 \) many-body states, with level degeneracies \( \Omega_1=2, \Omega_2=6, \Omega_3=2 \) and single-particle energies \( \varepsilon_1=0, \varepsilon_2=1, \varepsilon_3=2 \).
- The discriminant polynomial \( D(g) \) has maximal degree \( M = n(n-1) = 12 \) for the non-integrable Hamiltonian.
- An additional EP is observed at \( g = -0.142276i \), formed by eigenvalues '1' and '3' near the pseudo-DP.

## Context

This work extends the study of exceptional points and diabolic points from closed quantum systems (hermitian) to open quantum systems (non-hermitian), building on the continuum shell model (CSM) frameworks of Okolowicz (2003) and the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) of Betan (2002) and Michel (2002). The paper demonstrates that in open quantum systems, exact degeneracies of eigenvalues for states with the same quantum numbers are allowed, unlike in closed quantum systems, and that these topological anomalies (EP, DP, pseudo-DP) may have observable consequences in resonance spectroscopy and low-energy nuclear reactions when studied with realistic two-body interactions in future CSM calculations.
