# QCD phase diagram: overview of recent lattice results

> Two parameters that have a strong influence on the finite temperature QCD transition, and play an important role in various physical scenarios are the quark density and the external magnetic field. The effect of these parameters on the thermal properties of QCD is discussed, and an overview of the latest lattice results is given.

## Key claim

The QCD transition at zero chemical potential and zero magnetic field is an analytic crossover, not a first-order phase transition; this means the transition temperature is not unique but depends on the observable used for its definition.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD simulations that use the grand canonical partition function, discretized on a four-dimensional space-time grid. For nonzero chemical potential, a Taylor expansion in μ around μ=0 is employed to circumvent the complex action problem; for nonzero magnetic fields, standard Monte-Carlo methods are applicable because the fermion determinant remains real. Continuum extrapolations are performed using multiple lattice spacings, and quark masses are tuned to their physical values.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature at zero chemical potential and zero magnetic field is scattered around T_c ≈ 160 MeV, depending on the observable used.
- The curvature of the transition line at small chemical potential is parameterized as T_c(μ) = T_c(0) · [1 − κ · μ²/T_c²(0)], with κ depending on the observable.
- The magnetic field reduces the transition temperature: T_c(B) decreases as B increases, as shown in the phase diagram in the T–B plane.
- The Polyakov loop is increased by the magnetic field around T_c, shifting its inflection point to lower temperatures.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Aoki 2006, Borsanyi 2010, 2011) and others (Bazavov 2011) on the zero-density transition to nonzero chemical potential and nonzero magnetic field. The finding of inverse magnetic catalysis around T_c contradicts most low-energy effective models and theories, which predicted magnetic catalysis to persist at all temperatures and T_c(B) to increase. The results enable first-principles mapping of the three-dimensional QCD phase diagram and provide input for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and neutron star physics.
