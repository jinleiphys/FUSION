# A Regge Model for Nucleon-Nucleon Spin Dependent Amplitudes

> There are currently no models readily available that provide nucleon-nucleon spin dependent scattering amplitudes at high energies ($s \geq 6$ $ GeV^2$). This work aims to provide a model for calculating these high energy scattering amplitudes. The foundation of the model is Regge theory since it allows for a relativistic description and full spin dependence. We present our parameterization of the amplitudes, and show comparisons of our solutions to the data set we have collected. Overall the model works as intended, and provides an adequate description of the scattering amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors present a Regge-theory-based model for calculating nucleon-nucleon spin-dependent scattering amplitudes at high energies (Mandelstam \(s \geq 6\) GeV\(^2\)), claiming it provides an "adequate description" of the data; however, the model is explicitly intended for extrapolation to higher energies where data are scarce, and the authors note that the SAID partial-wave analysis is more precise in the overlapping lower-energy region.

## Method

The authors parameterize the five independent nucleon-nucleon helicity amplitudes by relating Regge exchanges to Fermi invariants (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor), which trivializes the crossing relations and reduces the Regge analysis to the spinless case. Linear Regge trajectories are used, with residues parameterized in one of three forms (exponential, exponential with a cutoff, or scaled by \(t/4m^2\)), and an additional cutoff factor \(\zeta(s,t)\) is introduced to decouple \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel contributions at low \(s\). Electromagnetic effects for proton-proton scattering are included via a one-photon-exchange amplitude modified by helicity-dependent constants and phases fitted to data. The model is fitted simultaneously to a large dataset of unpolarized and polarized observables for both proton-proton and proton-neutron scattering, with weights applied to balance the influence of different observable types.

## Key numbers

* The model is designed for Mandelstam \(s \geq 6\) GeV\(^2\), with the SAID analysis covering proton-neutron amplitudes up to \(s \approx 6\) GeV\(^2\) and proton-proton amplitudes up to \(s \approx 9.8\) GeV\(^2\).
* The unpolarized solution is based on 6111 data points with 132 parameters, yielding a total \(\chi^2 = 10885.1\) (\(\chi^2/N = 1.8\)).
* The polarized solution is based on 13869 data points with 138 parameters, yielding a total \(\chi^2 = 43558.0\) (\(\chi^2/N = 3.1\)).
* For the polarized solution, the \(\chi^2/N\) for proton-proton differential cross section data (\(s > 20\) GeV\(^2\)) is 1.7 (2853.5 \(\chi^2\) over 1635 points).
* The nucleon mass used is \(m = 0.93895\) GeV.
* The model extrapolates results to \(s \approx 20\) GeV\(^2\) with "reasonable confidence."

## Context

This work extends the SAID partial-wave analysis (SAID, various years), which provides nucleon-nucleon amplitudes only up to \(s \approx 6\)–\(9.8\) GeV\(^2\), by using Regge theory to reach higher energies. The primary motivation is to describe final-state interactions in deuteron electrodisintegration at Jefferson Lab kinematics, where previous calculations (Van Orden, 2008; Van Orden, 2009) relied on SAID amplitudes that are insufficient for the higher-energy final-state nucleons. The model builds on prior Regge phenomenology for nucleon-nucleon scattering (Irving, various years) and provides a framework that can be used to compute spin-dependent amplitudes for both proton-proton and proton-neutron systems, which the authors plan to release to the nuclear physics community.
