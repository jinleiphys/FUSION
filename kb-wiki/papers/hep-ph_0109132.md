# Equation of state of strongly coupled QCD at finite density

> Using an effective strongly coupled lattice QCD Hamiltonian and Wilson fermions we calculate the equation of state for cold and dense quark matter by constructing an ansatz which exactly diagonalizes the Hamiltonian to second order in field operators for all densities. This ansatz obeys the free lattice Dirac equation with a chemical potential term and a mass term which is interpreted as the dynamical quark mass. We find that the order of chiral phase transition depends on the values of input parameters. In the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry the quark Fermi sea has negative pressure indicating its mechanical instability. This result is in qualitative agreement with those obtained using continuum field theory models with four--point interactions.

# Equation of state of strongly coupled QCD at finite density

## Key claim

Using an effective strongly coupled lattice QCD Hamiltonian with Wilson fermions, the order of the chiral phase transition at finite density depends on the values of input parameters (the effective coupling constant K and the Wilson parameter r), and in the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry the quark Fermi sea has negative pressure indicating mechanical instability, a qualitative result that agrees with continuum four-fermion interaction models but contradicts some earlier Hamiltonian lattice results.

## Method

The author constructs an ansatz for the quark field operator that obeys the free lattice Dirac equation with a chemical potential term and a dynamical quark mass, then uses this ansatz in Smit’s effective strongly coupled lattice QCD Hamiltonian. By equating the equation of motion from the free Hamiltonian to that from the effective Hamiltonian, the author derives coupled equations for the dynamical quark mass (gap equation) and the total chemical potential, which are solved self-consistently to lowest order in N_c. The thermodynamic potential density is then evaluated numerically to obtain the pressure as a function of quark number density.

## Key numbers

- For naive fermions (Wilson parameter r = 0) with coupling K = 0.8, the critical chemical potential for a first-order chiral phase transition is μ_C ≈ 1.042 (in lattice units).
- For naive fermions (r = 0) with K = 1.0, the critical chemical potential is μ_C ≈ 1.329.
- For Wilson fermions (r = 0.25) with K = 0.8, a second-order chiral phase transition occurs at μ_C ≈ 0.836.
- For Wilson fermions (r = 0.25) with K = 0.9, a first-order chiral phase transition occurs at μ_C ≈ 1.086.
- Quark number densities at the chiral phase transition point for r = 0.25 are n_q = 0.013 (K = 0.8) and n_q = 0.025 (K = 0.9).

## Context

This work extends earlier analytical studies of strongly coupled QCD at finite chemical potential using both Euclidean lattice formulations (Damgaard et al., 1985; Ilgenfritz et al., 1985; Bilic et al., 1992) and Hamiltonian formulations (Le Yaouanc et al., 1988; Greenwood et al., 2000). The author finds first-order chiral phase transitions with naive fermions, agreeing with the Euclidean results and with Greenwood et al. (2000), but disagreeing with Le Yaouanc et al. (1988) who found a second-order transition using the same effective Hamiltonian. The negative pressure in the broken symmetry phase is consistent with mean-field calculations using the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio model (Buballa, 1996) and the instanton-induced ’t Hooft interaction model (Alford et al., 1998), which led those authors to speculate about quark droplet formation reminiscent of the MIT bag model.
