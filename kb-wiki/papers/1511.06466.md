# Topological Susceptibility in Three-Flavor Quark Meson Model at Finite Temperature

> We study $U_A(1)$ symmetry and its relation to chiral symmetry at finite temperature through the application of functional renormalization group to the $SU(3)$ quark meson model. Very different from the mass gap and mixing angel between $η$ and $η'$ mesons which are defined at mean field level and behavior like the chiral condensates, the topological susceptibility includes a fluctuations induced part which becomes dominant at high temperature. As a result, the $U_A(1)$ symmetry is still considerably broken in the chiral symmetry restoration phase.

## Key claim

The topological susceptibility includes a fluctuations-induced part that becomes dominant at high temperature, leading to considerable $U_A(1)$ symmetry breaking even in the chiral symmetry restoration phase, contrary to the behavior of the mass gap and mixing angle which are defined at mean field level and behave like the chiral condensates.

## Method

The authors applied the functional renormalization group (FRG) method to the $SU(3)$ quark-meson model at finite temperature, solving the flow equation for the effective potential under the local potential approximation. They defined the topological charge density in the model through the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) term and calculated the topological susceptibility by summing all 6-field correlations from the connected correlation function, separating contributions from condensates and fluctuations. The flow equation was solved numerically using a two-dimensional grid method in the plane of chiral invariants.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral phase transition in chiral limit: $T_c = 140$ MeV
- Pseudoscalar mixing angle in chiral restoration phase (chiral limit): $\theta_P \simeq 35^0$
- Ultraviolet momentum cutoff: $\Lambda = 1$ GeV
- Yukawa coupling strength: $g = 6.5$
- $U_A(1)$ breaking parameter: $c = 4807.84$ MeV
- Initial meson mass parameter: $m_\Lambda^2 = (867.76\ \text{MeV})^2$
- Chiral breaking parameters: $h_u = 2(120.73\ \text{MeV})^2$, $h_s = \sqrt{2}(336.41\ \text{MeV})^2$

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of meson masses in the linear sigma model (Jiang, 2013) and NJL model (Xia, 2014) to the topological susceptibility and $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle in the $SU(3)$ quark-meson model. It addresses the open question of whether $U_A(1)$ symmetry is restored in the chiral symmetric phase, a topic of debate between lattice QCD collaborations HotQCD (2012) and JLQCD (2012), who reported conflicting results at $T = 1.2 T_c$. The FRG approach enables inclusion of fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation, showing that fluctuations maintain $U_A(1)$ breaking at high temperature even when chiral symmetry is restored.
