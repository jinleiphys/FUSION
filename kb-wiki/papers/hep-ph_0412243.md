# Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations and the anomalous dimension of QCD

> Bose-Einstein (or HBT) correlation functions are evaluated for the fractal structure of QCD jets. These correlation functions have a stretched exponential (or Levy-stable) form. The anomalous dimension of QCD determines the Levy index of stability, thus the running coupling constant of QCD becomes measurable with the help of two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions. These considerations are tested on NA22 and UA1 two-pion correlation data.

# Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations and the anomalous dimension of QCD

## Key claim
The Lév y index of stability extracted from two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions can be directly related to the anomalous dimension of QCD, enabling a measurement of the running strong coupling constant α_s from HBT data. This relationship holds under the assumption that the fractal structure of QCD jets produces a source distribution that is stable under convolution, and that the τ-model provides the correct mapping between invariant momentum difference and proper-time distribution.

## Method
The authors evaluate Bose-Einstein correlation functions for Lév y-stable source distributions, which arise naturally from the fractal (multiplicative) cascade structure of QCD jets described by the Lund color-dipole picture. They derive an analytic relation between the Lév y index of stability α_Levy and the QCD anomalous dimension, then convert this to a relation between α_s and the exponent α_BEC measured from invariant relative momentum dependent correlation functions via the τ-model. Previously published fits to NA22 and UA1 two-pion correlation data using the Lév y-stable form of eq. (e:BEC-Levy) are reinterpreted to extract α_s.

## Key numbers
- For NA22 data: α_BEC = 0.67 ± 0.07, yielding α_s = 0.24 ± 0.05 from eq. (e:QCDa).
- For UA1 data: α_BEC = 0.49 ± 0.01, yielding α_s = 0.13 ± 0.01 from eq. (e:QCDa).
- The fractal dimension of the partonic baseline curve is d_f = 1 + sqrt(3 α_s / (2π)), where the second term is the anomalous dimension of QCD.
- The Lév y index of stability α satisfies 0 < α ≤ 2 for positive source distributions.
- The Lév y source tails decay as f_α(s) ∝ s^{-1-α} for large s when α < 2.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical connection between intermittency and Bose-Einstein correlations first proposed by Bialas (1992), building on the fractal description of QCD jets developed by the Lund group (Dahlqvist 1989, Gustafson 1990-1992) and by Dokshitzer and Dremin (1992). The mathematical framework for Lév y-stable source distributions in HBT correlations was established by three of the present authors (Csorgo 2003, 2004). The results are similar in spirit to the numerical investigations of Wilk and collaborators (Utyuzh 1999). The extracted α_s values from NA22 and UA1 data demonstrate a proof-of-principle that two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations can serve as a probe of the running coupling constant, though the authors note the τ-model conversion yields different α_s values at the two different energy scales.
