# Low mass enhanced probability of pion in hadronic matter due to its Landau cut contributions

> In the real-time thermal field theory, the pion self-energy at finite temperature and density is evaluated where the different mesonic and baryonic loops are considered. The interactions of pion with the other mesons and baryons in the medium are governed by the effective hadronic Lagrangian densities whose effective strength of coupling constants have been determined from the experimental decay widths of the mesons and baryons. The detail branch cut structures of these different mesonic and baryonic loops are analyzed. The Landau cut contributions of different baryon and meson loops become only relevant around the pion pole and it is completely appeared in presence of medium. The in-medium spectral function of pion has been plotted for different values of temperature, baryon chemical potential as well as three momentum of the pion. A noticeable low mass probability in pion spectral function promise to contribute in the low mass dilepton enhancement via indirect modification of $ρ$ self-energy for $ππ$ loop.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Landau cut contributions of mesonic and baryonic loops, which only appear in the presence of a medium, produce a noticeable low-mass enhancement in the pion spectral function that may contribute to low-mass dilepton enhancement via indirect modification of the rho meson self-energy for the ππ loop, but this is presented as a future interest rather than a demonstrated result.

## Method

The author evaluated the pion self-energy at finite temperature and density using real-time thermal field theory, considering mesonic loops (πσ and πρ) and an extensive set of baryonic loops (including N(940), Δ(1232), and ten additional N* and Δ* resonances). The effective coupling constants for the hadronic interactions were determined from experimental decay widths of the mesons and baryons. The branch cut structures of the loops were analyzed, and the in-medium pion spectral function was computed from the imaginary and real parts of the self-energy for different values of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and three-momentum.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants used are gσ = 5.82 and gρ = 6, fixed from experimental decay widths of σ and ρ mesons in their ππ channels.
- The Landau region for the πσ loop extends from M = 0 to mσ - mπ = 0.25 GeV; for the πρ loop it extends from M = 0 to mρ - mπ = 0.63 GeV.
- The unitary region for the πσ loop starts at M = mσ + mπ = 0.53 GeV; for the πρ loop it starts at M = mρ + mπ = 0.91 GeV.
- The Landau region of the NΔ loop is from M = 0 to mΔ - mN = 0.292 GeV.
- At (T, μN, vk) = (0.15, 0.453, 0) GeV, the imaginary part of pion self-energy from the NΔ loop alone is 1.4 MeV and from all NB loops is 1.4 MeV; the real part from NΔ alone is 8.4 MeV and from all NB loops is 11.4 MeV.
- At vk = 300 MeV, ImΠ/mπ from NΔ is 38.6 MeV and from all NB loops is 43.5 MeV; at vk = 500 MeV, ImΠ/mπ from NΔ is 3.3 MeV and from all NB loops is 10.1 MeV.
- The soft monopole form factor used at the πNB vertex has Λ = 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of in-medium pion modification by Shuryak (1990s), Rapp et al. (1990s-2000s), and Tolos et al. (2000s), with the major new contribution being the inclusion of an extensive set of baryonic loops beyond the standard NΔ loop. The results are qualitatively similar to those of Rapp et al. for the momentum dependence of the pion optical potential but show quantitative differences, and differ from the monotonically increasing behavior found by Tolos et al. The work is motivated by the low-mass dimuon enhancement measured by the NA60 collaboration, where a strong broadening of the rho meson is considered the main explanation. The author states that applying the in-medium pion spectral function to generate dilepton spectra via rho meson modification is the next interest for future work.
