# Pion transverse charge density from timelike form factor data

> The transverse charge density in the pion can be represented as a dispersion integral of the imaginary part of the pion form factor in the timelike region. This formulation incorporates information from e+e- annihilation experiments and allows one to reconstruct the transverse density much more accurately than from the spacelike pion form factor data alone. We calculate the transverse density using an empirical parametrization of the timelike pion form factor and estimate that it is determined to an accuracy of ~10% at a distance b ~ 0.1 fm, and significantly better at larger distances. The density is found to be close to that obtained from a zero-width rho meson pole over a wide range and shows a pronounced rise at small distances. The resulting two-dimensional image of the fast-moving pion can be interpreted in terms of its partonic structure in QCD. We argue that the singular behavior of the charge density at the center requires a substantial presence of pointlike configurations in the pion's partonic wave function, which can be probed in other high-momentum transfer processes.

## Key claim

The transverse charge density in the pion can be reconstructed much more accurately from timelike form factor data via a dispersion integral than from spacelike form factor data alone, with an estimated accuracy of approximately 10% at a transverse distance b ~ 0.1 fm, provided one uses an empirical parametrization of the timelike pion form factor that respects analyticity.

## Method

The authors used a dispersion relation to express the transverse charge density ρ_π(b) as an integral over the imaginary part (spectral function) of the timelike pion form factor, weighted by the modified Bessel function K_0(√t b). They evaluated this integral using the Gounaris-Sakurai parametrization of the timelike pion form factor from Bruch (2004), which includes the first four ρ meson resonances and an infinite series of higher excitations via a dual resonance model, with parameters determined from e+e- annihilation data up to √t ≈ 3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The transverse charge density is determined to an accuracy of ±(1.5, 7, 13)% at b = (0.5, 0.1, 0.02) fm, based on the 1σ error band from the parametrization uncertainties.
- The probability accumulation P(b) reaches 1/2 at b = 0.33 fm, compared to the RMS transverse radius ⟨b²⟩^{1/2}_π = 0.53 fm.
- The RMS transverse radius from the dispersion integral agrees with the experimental value ⟨r²⟩_π = (3/2)⟨b²⟩_π = 0.439 ± 0.008 fm² from πe scattering experiments (Dally 1981, Amendolia 1984).
- At b = 0.1 fm, the region √t > 3 GeV contributes only about 1% of the total dispersion integral, so even a factor 2-3 change in Im F_π(t) at high energies would change the density by only 2-3%.
- The CLEO measurement (Pedlar 2005) at √t = 3.67 GeV gives |F_π| = 0.075 ± 0.008 (stat) ± 0.005 (syst), much larger than the GS parametrization value of 0.034; adding a hypothetical resonance at this mass would contribute +(0.04, 4, 16)% at b = (0.5, 0.1, 0.02) fm.

## Context

This work extends the transverse density formalism of Soper (1976) and Miller (2007, 2009) to the pion using timelike data, overcoming the poor knowledge of the spacelike pion form factor at high Q² from electroproduction experiments (Bebek 1974, Ackermann 1977, Volmer 2000, Horn 2006). The dispersion method exploits the filtering property of the Bessel kernel to extract the density at small b, enabling constraints on pointlike configurations in the pion's partonic wave function, which are essential for understanding color transparency (Bertsch 1981, Frankfurt 1993) and hard meson production processes.
