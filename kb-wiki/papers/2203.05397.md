# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter and Neutron Star Properties in Relativistic ab initio Theory in the Full Dirac Space

> The long-standing controversy about the isospin dependence of the effective Dirac mass in ab initio calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter is clarified by solving the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations in the full Dirac space. The symmetry energy and its slope parameter at the saturation density are $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ_0)=33.1$ MeV and $L=65.2$ MeV, in agreement with empirical and experimental values. Further applications predict the neutron star radius $R_{1.4M_\odot}\approx 12$ km and the maximum mass of a neutron star $M_{\text{max}}\leq 2.4M_\odot$.

## Key claim

The long-standing controversy about whether the neutron or proton has a larger effective Dirac mass in asymmetric nuclear matter is resolved by solving the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations in the full Dirac space, yielding the result \(M^*_{D,n} < M^*_{D,p}\) (neutron Dirac mass smaller than proton Dirac mass), which contradicts the momentum-independence approximation but is qualitatively consistent with the projection method.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations self-consistently in the full Dirac space, including matrix elements between positive-energy states (PESs) and negative-energy states (NESs) as well as between NESs, for asymmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium scattering matrix (G matrix) was obtained from the covariant Thompson equation using the Bonn-A, Bonn-B, and Bonn-C potentials. The resulting equation of state was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation for neutron star mass-radius relations, with the high-density part of the equation of state replaced by a maximally stiff causal form above a critical density of \(0.57\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0) = 33.1\) MeV, consistent with empirical value \(31.7 \pm 3.2\) MeV.
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy at saturation density: \(L = 65.2\) MeV, consistent with empirical value \(58.7 \pm 28.1\) MeV.
- Neutron-proton Dirac mass splitting in pure neutron matter: \((M^*_{D,p} - M^*_{D,n})/M = 0.095\) at \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Symmetry energy at twice saturation density (\(0.32\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)): \(51.6\) MeV, within the ASY-EOS constraint of \(50.8\)–\(60.4\) MeV.
- Radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: \(R_{1.4} = 11.97\) km (Bonn A), \(12.13\) km (Bonn B), \(12.27\) km (Bonn C).
- Maximum neutron star mass: \(M_{\text{max}} \leq 2.4 M_\odot\) (upper bounds: \(2.43 M_\odot\) for Bonn A and B, \(2.44 M_\odot\) for Bonn C).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent RBHF calculation in the full Dirac space for symmetric nuclear matter (Wang 2021) to asymmetric nuclear matter, resolving a controversy between two approximate methods: the momentum-independence approximation (Brockmann 1990) which gave \(M^*_{D,n} > M^*_{D,p}\), and the projection method (Gross-Boelting 1999) which gave the opposite sign. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron star properties, showing that the secondary compact object in GW190814 with mass \(2.50\)–\(2.67 M_\odot\) might not be a neutron star, and providing radii consistent with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019).
