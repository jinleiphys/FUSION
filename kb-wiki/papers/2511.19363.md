# Numerical solution of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation for gluons

> The nonlinear boson diffusion equation is taken as a basis to account for the fast thermalization of gluons in the initial stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. For constant drift and diffusion coefficients with schematic initial conditions, this equation has previously been solved exactly. In order to achieve a more realistic time evolution towards thermalization, energy-dependent transport coefficients are introduced, requiring numerical solutions of the nonlinear equation. Their accuracy is tested against the exact analytical results in the limit of constant coefficients. The consequences for transient gluon-condensate formation through elastic scatterings in overoccupied systems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing energy-dependent drift and diffusion coefficients into the nonlinear boson diffusion equation removes an unphysical overshoot of the equilibrium condensate fraction that occurs when constant coefficients are used, but the numerical solutions are only tested against exact analytical results in the limiting case of constant coefficients, not against experimental data or microscopic QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the nonlinear boson diffusion equation for massless gluons using the method of lines with symmetric finite-difference discretization in the energy domain, implemented in the Julia programming language with the DifferentialEquations.jl package. For constant transport coefficients, they compare the numerical results to previously derived exact analytical solutions to validate the numerical approach. They then extend the numerical method to energy-dependent drift and diffusion coefficients, which cannot be solved analytically, and compute the time-dependent gluon distribution functions and condensate fractions for parameters characteristic of Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* Equilibrium temperature T = 443 MeV for Q_s = 1 GeV and initial occupation n_i^0 = 1, derived from the relation T = [15 n_i^0 / (4 pi^4)]^(1/4) Q_s
* Transport coefficients for constant-coefficient case: D = 0.67 GeV^2/fm and v = -1.51 GeV/fm, obtained from equilibration time tau_eq = 0.13 fm using D = 4 T^2 / (9 tau_eq) and v = -4 T / (9 tau_eq)
* Numerical grid uses N = 5000 cells with spatial resolution Delta epsilon = 1e-3 GeV
* Numerical solution is accurate to 4-5 decimal places when compared to the exact analytical solution at the saturation scale Q_s
* Critical initial occupation separating under- from overoccupied systems: n_i^0 = 0.154, where N_eq^(mu=0) / N_i = 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical solutions of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation for constant transport coefficients (Wolschin 2018, 2022; Rössler and Wolschin 2024) to the physically more realistic case of energy-dependent coefficients. It complements extensive numerical solutions of quantum Boltzmann equations for gluon thermalization and condensate formation (Kurkela and Moore 2011; Blaizot et al. 2017) by providing a simpler nonlinear diffusion model that can be solved numerically and validated against exact results in the constant-coefficient limit. The paper enables future work on energy-dependent transport coefficients that could be derived from microscopic theory, and addresses the open question of whether transient gluon condensate formation through elastic scatterings is physically possible despite the faster timescale of inelastic collisions.
