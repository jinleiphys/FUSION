# Non-eikonal corrections to multi-particle production in the Color Glass Condensate

> We consider the non-eikonal corrections to particle production in the Color Glass Condensate stemming from the relaxation of the shockwave approximation for the target that acquires a finite longitudinal dimension. We derive a modified expression of the Lipatov vertex which takes into account this finite target width. This expression is employed to compute single, double and triple gluon production in the Glasma graph limit valid for the scattering of two dilute objects, at all orders in the expansion in the number of colors. We justify and generalize previous results, and discuss the possible implications on two particle correlations of these non-eikonal corrections that induce differences between the away- and near-side peaks.

## Key claim

Non-eikonal corrections to multi-gluon production in the Color Glass Condensate, arising from relaxing the shockwave approximation for the target (giving it a finite longitudinal dimension), modify the Lipatov vertex by a phase factor \(e^{i\frac{k_\perp^2}{2k^+}x_1^+}\) and induce differences between away- and near-side peaks in two-particle correlations, though the paper presents analytical expressions and preliminary numerical results rather than a definitive quantitative prediction for experimental observables.

## Method

The authors derived a non-eikonal Lipatov vertex by computing gluon emission from a projectile quark scattering off a target with finite longitudinal extent, summing diagrams where the gluon is emitted before, during, and after interaction with the target field. They then applied this vertex to single, double, and triple gluon production in the dilute-dilute (Glasma graph) limit, expanding Wilson lines to first order in the target field and using a generalized McLerran-Venugopalan model for the projectile color charge correlator and a finite-width target field correlator with a Yukawa-type potential.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of non-eikonal to eikonal single inclusive gluon production cross sections shows up to 20% relative weight of non-eikonal corrections for a color correlation length \(\lambda^+ = 1\) fm at pseudorapidity \(\eta = 2\).
* For \(\lambda^+ = 0.5\) fm, the relative weight of non-eikonal corrections varies between roughly 15% and 2% for pseudorapidities up to \(\eta = 2.5\), depending on the transverse momentum of the produced gluon.
* The non-eikonal correction function for single inclusive production is \({\cal G}_1^{\rm NE}(k^-;\lambda^+) = \frac{1}{k^-\lambda^+}\sin(k^-\lambda^+)\), where \(k^- = k_\perp^2/(2k^+)\).
* The target field correlator uses a Yukawa-type potential \(|a(q)|^2 = m^2/(q^2 + m^2)^2\) with Debye screening mass \(m\) and color correlation length \(\lambda^+\) much smaller than the total target longitudinal width \(L^+\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic non-eikonal expansion of the gluon propagator developed by Altinoluk et al. (2014, 2015) and its application to particle production in the CGC by Altinoluk et al. (2015), providing a resummation to all orders in the non-eikonal phase that was previously only expanded to next-to-next-to-leading order. The non-eikonal Lipatov vertex derived here generalizes similar expressions found in jet quenching calculations by Wiedemann (2000), Gyulassy et al. (2000), and Mehtar-Tani et al. (2011), but is identified for the first time as a building block for CGC calculations. The results enable future studies of the impact of finite target width on azimuthal correlations in small systems, potentially addressing the odd harmonic puzzle in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC and RHIC.
