# Non-Abelian chiral kinetic theory with self-consistent semiclassical expansion

> We derive the chiral kinetic theory in a non-Abelian gauge field using a self-consistent semiclassical expansion. Within this new expansion scheme, we disentangle the Wigner equations up to second order and demonstrate that they do not introduce additional constraint equations. By integrating the covariant chiral kinetic equations in eight-dimensional phase space, we obtain the corresponding equations in seven-dimensional phase space. This reduction facilitates numerical simulations and practical applications.

## Key claim

The authors derive a non-Abelian chiral kinetic theory using a self-consistent semiclassical expansion that rescales the gauge potential by ℏ, which eliminates the extra constraint equations that appear in prior covariant gradient expansions for non-Abelian fields (which are automatically satisfied in the Abelian case).

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner function approach for massless fermions in a non-Abelian background gauge field, using the chiral Wigner function decomposition. They perform a semiclassical expansion that rescales the gauge potential Aμ(x) by 1/ℏ, which shifts field-strength-dependent terms to higher order compared to the standard covariant gradient expansion. They then solve the Wigner equations order by order up to second order, obtaining constitutive relations and kinetic equations for the chiral Wigner functions in eight-dimensional phase space, and finally integrate over the energy component pn to obtain equations in seven-dimensional phase space.

## Key numbers

- The zeroth-order Wigner function satisfies the on-shell condition p² = 0, with solution 𝒥ₙ⁽⁰⁾ = pₙ 𝒥ₙ⁽⁰⁾ δ(p²).
- The first-order correction to the velocity is given by ẋμ⁽¹⁾ = (λ s)/(2|p̄|²) ε̄μαβ p̄β 𝒟α, where λ = ±1 denotes positive/negative energy and s = ±1 denotes right/left chirality.
- The second-order on-shell condition acquires a modification proportional to δ'(p²), with terms involving the field strength tensor Fμν and covariant derivatives of the zeroth-order Wigner function.

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of chiral kinetic theory from Abelian to non-Abelian gauge fields, addressing the issue of extra constraint equations found in the covariant gradient expansion of Ochs (1998) and Luo (2021). The new self-consistent expansion scheme eliminates these constraints and provides consistent order-by-order solutions up to second order. This enables numerical simulations of chiral kinetic phenomena in non-Abelian plasmas, such as those relevant to heavy-ion collisions studied by Gao (2012), Stephanov (2012), and Son (2012).
