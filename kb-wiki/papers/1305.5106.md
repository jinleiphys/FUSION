# Pseudo-rapidity Distributions of Charged Hadrons in pp and pA Collisions at the LHC

> In the framework of Color Glass Condensate, the pseudo-rapidity distributions of charged hadrons in pp and pA collisions at the LHC are studied with the UGD function from the GBW model. With a $χ^{2}$ analysis of the CMS data in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=0.9,~2.36,~7$ TeV, the normalization factor is obtained and the theoretical results are in good agreement with the perimental data. Then, considering the influence of nucleon hard partons distribution on the number of participants in pA collisions by a Glauber Monte Carlo method, we give the predictive results for the multiplicity distributions in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=4.4$ TeV.

## Key claim
The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism with the GBW unintegrated gluon distribution (UGD) function can describe charged hadron pseudo-rapidity distributions in pp collisions at LHC energies, but the normalization factor must be obtained by a chi-squared fit to data; the predictive results for p+Pb collisions depend on the choice of nucleon parton transverse distribution in the Glauber Monte Carlo approach.

## Method
The authors used the CGC formalism with the GBW model UGD function to compute inclusive hadron production cross sections, integrating over transverse momentum and applying a Jacobian transformation to obtain pseudo-rapidity distributions. For pp collisions, they performed a chi-squared analysis of CMS data at three energies to extract the normalization factor K. For p+Pb collisions, they employed a Glauber Monte Carlo method with a dipole-form nucleon parton transverse distribution (derived from J/psi photo-production data) to compute the number of participating nucleons and the nuclear saturation scale.

## Key numbers
- Normalization factor k = K/S_pp = 0.098, obtained from chi-squared fit with chi-squared_min/(degree of freedom) = 0.2597
- pp collisions studied at sqrt(s) = 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV
- Predictive results for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 4.4 TeV
- Saturation scale parameters: Q_0 = 0.6 GeV, x_0 = 0.01, lambda_bar = 0.205
- Running coupling freezes at alpha_max = 0.52 with Lambda = 0.226 GeV
- Inelastic cross sections used: sigma_in = 52, 60, 65.75, 70.45 mb at sqrt(s) = 0.9, 2.36, 4.4, 7 TeV respectively
- Dipole mass parameter m_g ~ 1.1 GeV^2 for nucleon parton transverse distribution

## Context
This work extends the CGC-based description of hadron multiplicities from RHIC energies to LHC energies, using the GBW model that previously described HERA and RHIC data (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999). The authors compare their results with the KLN model and provide predictions for p+Pb collisions at the LHC before those data were available. The approach enables testing of parton saturation effects at very small Bjorken x (~10^-6) accessible at LHC energies, and the Glauber Monte Carlo method with a dipole nucleon shape improves upon the simple hard-sphere model used in earlier work.
