# Investigating $β$-decay properties of spherical nuclei along the possible r-process path

> The spherical QRPA method is used for the calculations of the $β$-decay properties of the neutron-rich nuclei in the region near the neutron magic numbers N=82 and N=126 which are important for determination of the r-process path. Our calculations differ from previous works by the use of realistic forces for the proton-neutron interaction. Both the allowed and first-forbidden $β$-decays are included. Detailed comparisons with the experimental measurements and the previous shell-model calculations are performed. The results for half-lives and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities will serve as input for the r-process nucleosynthesis simulations.

## Key claim

The spherical QRPA method with realistic forces (G-matrix from CD-Bonn potential) can reproduce beta-decay half-lives and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities for neutron-rich spherical nuclei near N=82 and N=126, but the agreement depends on empirical quenching factors (g_A/g_A0 = 0.4 for GT, g_A/g_A0 = 0.5 and g_V/g_V0 = 0.5 for FF) that are tuned to limited experimental data and may not be universal.

## Method

The authors used a proton-neutron QRPA (pn-QRPA) method with realistic forces derived from the CD-Bonn potential via the Brueckner G-matrix, applied to even-even and odd-even nuclei in isotonic chains near N=82 and N=126. Both allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden (FF) beta-decay transitions were calculated, with single-particle energies from Hartree-Fock with the SkX Skyrme interaction and BCS pairing with constant gaps from the five-term formula. The model space included all single-particle levels up to 5 MeV for both protons and neutrons, and empirical quenching factors were introduced to account for missing multi-phonon and configuration-space truncation effects.

## Key numbers

* For Cd isotopes (N=70-84), the quenching factor g_A/g_A0 = 0.4 reproduces half-lives; calculated half-lives range from 2484 s for 118Cd to 0.12 s for 130Cd and 0.14 s for 132Cd.
* For 130Cd (N=82), the calculated half-life is 0.12 s compared to the experimental value of 0.162(7) s.
* For 132Cd (N=84), the calculated half-life is 0.14 s compared to the experimental value of 0.097(10) s.
* For FF transitions in 136Te, 138Xe, and 140Xe near 132Sn, quenching factors g_A/g_A0 = 0.5 and g_V/g_V0 = 0.5 reduce logft deviations to less than 0.3 for most decay branches.
* For 207Tl decay, the calculated logft = 5.11 (with quenching) compared to the experimental value of 5.02.
* For N=82 and N=126 isotonic chains, the general discrepancy among different microscopic methods for half-lives is within a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QRPA study of deformed nuclei (Fang, Brown, Suzuki 2012) to spherical nuclei near the neutron magic numbers N=82 and N=126, which are critical for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations. The method differs from earlier QRPA approaches using separable forces (Moller, Pfeiffer, Kratz 2003; Hirsch, Bender, Heenen, Mierzejewski 1996) and continuum QRPA (Borunov 2003, 2011) by using realistic G-matrix interactions that provide a full spectrum of odd-odd daughter nuclei. The results are compared with shell-model calculations (Suzuki, Yoshida, Kajino, Otsuka 2012; Zhi, Cuenca-Garcia, Chibisov, Langanke, Martinez-Pinedo, Sieja 2013) and can serve as input for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, though the empirical quenching factors limit predictive power for nuclei far from stability.
