# Alpha-Particle Condensation in Nuclear Systems

> The onset of quartetting, i.e. alpha-particle condensation, in symmetric nuclear matter is studied with the help of an in-medium modified four nucleon equation. It is found that at very low density quartetting wins over pairing, because of the strong binding of the alpha-particles. The critical temperature can reach values up to around 6 MeV. Also the disappearance of alpha-particles with increasing density, i.e. the Mott transition, is investigated. In finite nuclei the Hoyle state, that is the 0_2^+ of 12C, is identified as an "alpha-particle condensate" state. It is conjectured that such states also exist in heavier n alpha-nuclei, like 16O, 20Ne, etc. For instance the 6-th 0^+ state of 16O at 15.1 MeV is identified from a theoretical analysis as being a strong candidate for an alpha condensate state. Exploratory calculations are performed for the density dependence of the alpha condensate fraction at zero temperature to address the suppression of the four-particle condensate below nuclear-matter density. Possible quartet condensation in other systems is discussed briefly

## Key claim

The paper argues that at very low densities in symmetric nuclear matter, four-nucleon clustering (quartetting) into alpha particles dominates over deuteron pairing, and that the Hoyle state in 12C and the 6th 0+ state in 16O at 15.1 MeV are finite-nucleus examples of alpha-particle condensation, though the authors hedge that the condensate in infinite matter disappears at densities around rho_0/3 and that the variational approach used represents only a first attempt at describing the transition from quartetting to pairing.

## Method

The authors solved an in-medium modified four-nucleon equation using the Faddeev-Yakubovski technique with Pauli blocking to study alpha-particle binding in symmetric nuclear matter as a function of density and temperature. For finite nuclei, they employed a microscopic THSR wave function (a product of Gaussian alpha-particle wave functions with a common center-of-mass motion parameter B and intrinsic size parameter b) to describe alpha-condensate states in 8Be, 12C, and 16O, evaluating the energy expectation value with a Gogny-type effective nucleon-nucleon force fitted to alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for alpha-particle condensation in symmetric nuclear matter can reach values up to around 6 MeV.
* The Mott density at which the alpha-particle bound state with zero center-of-mass momentum vanishes is approximately rho_0/10, where rho_0 = 0.16 nucleons/fm^3 is the saturation density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.
* The critical density at which the alpha condensate disappears is estimated to be rho_0/3.
* The Hoyle state (0_2+ state of 12C) lies at 7.654 MeV, about 300 keV above the three-alpha breakup threshold, with a width of 8.7 eV and a corresponding lifetime of 7.6 x 10^-17 s.
* The 6th 0+ state of 16O at 15.1 MeV is identified as a strong candidate for an alpha condensate state.
* The rms radius of 8Be is about 3.7 fm, compared to the nuclear systematics value R = r_0 A^(1/3) = 2.44 fm, giving an average density of about rho_0/3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of alpha-clustering in light nuclei by Kamimura (1970s) and Uegaki (1970s), who first reproduced the Hoyle state from microscopic three-alpha calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method and Resonating Group Method, and by Horiuchi who noted the gas-like nature of the three alphas in the Hoyle state based on the orthogonality condition model. The paper also builds on the BCS theory of pairing in nuclear matter by proposing an analogous quartet condensation mechanism, and it enables future searches for alpha-condensate states in heavier self-conjugate 4n nuclei such as 20Ne, as well as investigations of quartetting in other fermion systems with four different fermion species, such as cold atoms in four magnetic substates.
