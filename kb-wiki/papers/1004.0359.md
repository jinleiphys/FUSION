# Many Body Approach for Quartet Condensation in Strong Coupling

> The theory for condensation of higher fermionic clusters is developed. Fully selfconsistent nonlinear equations for the quartet order parameter in strongly coupled fermionic systems are established and solved. The breakdown of the quasiparticle picture is pointed out. Derivation of numerically tractable approximation is described. The momentum projected factorisation ansatz of Ref. \cite{slr09} for the order parameter is employed again. As a definite example the condensation of $α$ particles in nuclear matter is worked out.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a fully selfconsistent nonlinear equation for the quartet order parameter in strongly coupled fermionic systems, analogous to the BCS gap equation for pairing, but finds that the quasiparticle picture breaks down completely for quartet condensation and that selfconsistent solutions exist only for negative or near-zero chemical potentials, not in the weak-coupling regime.

## Method

The authors derive the single-particle mass operator for quartet condensation using a Dyson equation approach with effective four-body vertices and an in-medium four-body Schrödinger equation. They employ a momentum-projected mean-field ansatz (Slater determinant projected onto zero total momentum) for the quartet order parameter and a separable two-body interaction with Gaussian form factor, reducing the problem to solving for a single 0S wave function by iteration. The calculation cycle fixes a chemical potential, computes the mass operator and occupation numbers from the spectral function, then updates the single-particle wave function until convergence.

## Key numbers

* Free alpha-particle binding energy: -28.3 MeV, with radius 1.7 fm, obtained using interaction strength λ = -992 MeV fm³ and range parameter b = 1.43 fm⁻¹
* Maximum chemical potential for which convergent wave functions could be obtained: μ = 0.55 MeV; for μ > 0.55 MeV no convergent solution exists
* At μ = 0.55 MeV, the maximum occupation number reaches only 0.35, far below the saturation value of 1
* Nuclear matter reference scales: Fermi energy ε_F = 35 MeV, inverse Fermi momentum k_F⁻¹ = 1.35⁻¹ fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of alpha particle condensation in infinite nuclear matter, where they evaluated the critical temperature using a four-body generalization of the Thouless criterion (Röpke et al. 1998, Sogo et al. 2009). The paper contrasts quartet condensation with standard BCS pairing theory, showing that the three-hole level density vanishes at ω = 0 for positive chemical potentials, unlike the single-hole level density in pairing which remains finite at the Fermi level. This explains why Cooper pairs can strongly overlap while quartets cannot, and why the quartet solution breaks down in the weak-coupling (positive μ) regime. The formalism enables future investigations of the transition from quartet condensation to superfluid pairing phases.
