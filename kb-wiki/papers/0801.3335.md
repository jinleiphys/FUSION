# Multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei and kaon condensation

> We extend previous relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of multi-$\bar K$ nuclei, using vector boson fields with SU(3) PPV coupling constants and scalar boson fields constrained phenomenologically. For a given core nucleus, the resulting $\bar K$ separation energy $B_{\bar K}$, as well as the associated nuclear and $\bar K$-meson densities, saturate with the number $κ$ of $\bar K$ mesons for $κ> κ_{\rm sat} \sim 10$. Saturation appears robust against a wide range of variations, including the RMF nuclear model used and the type of boson fields mediating the strong interactions. Because $B_{\bar K}$ generally does not exceed 200 MeV, it is argued that multi-$\bar K$ nuclei do not compete with multihyperonic nuclei in providing the ground state of strange hadronic configurations and that kaon condensation is unlikely to occur in strong-interaction self-bound strange hadronic matter. Last, we explore possibly self-bound strange systems made of neutrons and ${\bar K}^0$ mesons, or protons and $K^-$ mesons, and study their properties.

## Key claim

Multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei exhibit saturation of $\bar{K}$ separation energy $B_{\bar{K}}$ and nuclear densities for $\kappa > \kappa_{\rm sat} \sim 10$, with $B_{\bar{K}}$ generally not exceeding 200 MeV, making kaon condensation unlikely in strong-interaction self-bound strange hadronic matter and implying that multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei do not compete with multihyperonic nuclei as the ground state of strange hadronic configurations.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for nuclear systems containing multiple $\bar{K}$ mesons, using vector boson fields with purely F-type SU(3) coupling constants and scalar boson fields constrained phenomenologically to reproduce single-$\bar{K}$ binding energies of 100-150 MeV. The coupled system of Dirac equations for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson mean fields and antikaons was solved iteratively, with the $\bar{K}$ self-energy including scalar-meson contributions, vector-meson contributions, and an imaginary absorptive part fitted to $K^-$ atomic data. The calculations were performed for several RMF parameter sets (NL-SH, NL-TM1, NL-TM2) and for various combinations of meson fields ($\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\phi$, $\sigma^*$).

## Key numbers

- $B_{\bar{K}}$ generally does not exceed 200 MeV for multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei across the periodic table
- Saturation occurs for $\kappa > \kappa_{\rm sat} \sim 10$ antikaons, with even $^{208}{\rm Pb}$ requiring no more than approximately 10 antikaons to saturate
- The SU(3) vector potential at threshold in nuclear matter is $V^{K^-}_{\rm RMF-vector} = -76.7$ MeV, using $g_{\omega K}^{\rm SU(3)} = 3.02$, $m_{\omega} = 783$ MeV, and $\rho_0^{\rm NL-SH} = 0.146$ fm$^{-3}$
- The QM scalar potential is $V^{K^-}_{\rm RMF-scalar} = -66.3$ MeV, using $g_{\sigma K}^{\rm QM} = 3.48$, $m_{\sigma} = 526.1$ MeV, and $\rho^s_0 \approx 0.9 \rho_0$
- In $^{40}{\rm Ca}+\kappa K^-$, central nuclear density saturates for $\kappa=8$ at a value about twice larger than that for $\rho_N$ in $^{40}{\rm Ca}$
- In $8n+8{\bar K}^0$, the central $\bar{K}$ density reaches $\rho_{\bar K}(0) \sim 5 \rho_0$
- Neutron $1s_{1/2}$ level shifts downward by about 70 MeV in $^{16}{\rm O}+8K^-$ and by about 130 MeV in $8n+8{\bar K}^0$
- Spin-orbit splitting reaches 43 MeV in $^{16}{\rm O}+8K^-$ (compared to 7 MeV for $\kappa=0$) and 56 MeV in $8n+8{\bar K}^0$

## Context

This work extends preliminary RMF calculations by Gazda et al. (2007) on multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclear configurations, demonstrating the robustness of saturation against variations in RMF models and meson field compositions. The results contradict the scenario proposed by Yamazaki et al. (2004) that $\bar{K}$ mesons might provide the relevant degrees of freedom for self-bound strange hadronic matter, and instead support the conventional picture of strange hadronic matter composed of $\Lambda$ and $\Xi$ hyperons as reviewed by Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2000). The finding that $B_{\bar{K}}$ does not exceed 200 MeV, well below the threshold of approximately 320 MeV needed for $\Lambda \to \bar{K}+N$ conversion, rules out kaon condensation in finite nuclei and leaves open the question of whether kaon condensation can occur in neutron stars at densities above $3\rho_0$, as previously studied by Ellis, Knorren and Prakash (1995).
