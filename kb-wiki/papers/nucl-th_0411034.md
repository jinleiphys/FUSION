# Neutron Fraction and Neutrino Mean Free Path Predictions in Relativistic Mean Field Models

> The equation of state (EOS) of dense matter and neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in a neutron star have been studied by using relativistic mean field models motivated by effective field theory (ERMF). It is found that the models predict too large proton fractions, although one of the models (G2) predicts an acceptable EOS. This is caused by the isovector terms. Except G2, the other two models predict anomalous NMFP. In order to minimize the anomaly, besides an acceptable EOS, a large M* is favorable. A model with large M* retains the regularity in the NMFP even for a small neutron fraction.

## Key claim

The paper finds that relativistic mean field models motivated by effective field theory (ERMF) predict too large proton fractions in neutron star matter, and that only one model (G2) produces both an acceptable equation of state and a regular neutrino mean free path, while the other two models (NLZ and VA4) show anomalous neutrino mean free path behavior.

## Method

The authors calculated the equation of state and neutrino mean free path for neutron star matter using three relativistic mean field models: G2 (ERMF finite-range), NLZ (standard RMF finite-range), and VA4 (ERMF point-coupling). They determined the relative fractions of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons from chemical potential equilibrium and charge neutrality at zero temperature, then computed the neutrino mean free path for the direct URCA process using the polarization tensor formalism, neglecting RPA correlations.

## Key numbers

- The G2 model predicts an equation of state that agrees with experimental data from heavy ion collisions at densities from 2 to 5 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0)
- For 1 ≤ rho_B/rho_0 ≤ 5, the effective mass M* of G2 is larger than that of VA4; for rho_B/rho_0 ≥ 5, M* of G2 becomes smaller than that of VA4
- The NLZ model shows a maximum neutrino mean free path at M* ≈ 200 MeV and neutron fraction ≈ 0.75
- At a neutrino energy of E_nu = 5 MeV, the neutrino mean free paths of G2 and VA4 are approximately equal at high density, while NLZ shows a larger mean free path from medium to high density
- For rho_B/rho_0 smaller than 1, all three models give similar neutrino mean free path predictions

## Context

This work extends the comparison of relativistic mean field models to high-density neutron star matter, building on earlier studies of the G2 parameter set by Serot and Furnstahl (1997) and the variational calculation of Akmal et al. (1998). The authors note that all RMF finite-range models yield lower threshold densities for the direct URCA process than variational calculations, a point previously raised by Blaschke and Kolo (1998) and Migdal et al. (1990). The paper identifies the need for improvements in the isovector sector of ERMF models, and suggests that a relatively large effective mass M* is favorable for producing regular neutrino mean free path behavior, in contrast to the Hartree-Fock calculations of Niembro et al. (2001) where all models showed regular behavior.
