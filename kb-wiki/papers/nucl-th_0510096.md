# Energy Systematics of Jet Tomography

> The collision energy dependence of jet tomography is investigated within the GLV formalism. We estimate systematic uncertainties resulting from the interplay of energy loss fluctuations and the rapid increase of the parton transverse momentum slopes as $\sqrt{s}$ decreases from 200 to 62.4 AGeV.

# Energy Systematics of Jet Tomography

## Key claim
The collision energy dependence of jet quenching at RHIC is investigated within the GLV formalism, with the key hedge that the predicted nuclear modification factor at lower energy (62.4 AGeV) carries systematic uncertainties from the interplay between energy loss fluctuations and the steepening of parton transverse momentum slopes, and that these uncertainties are larger than the abstract suggests because the shape of the fluctuation distribution itself (uniform vs. squeezed) produces significantly different predictions for the energy dependence of R_AA.

## Method
The authors calculate the neutral pion nuclear modification factor R_AA(p_T, y=0, sqrt(s)) for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=62.4 and 200 AGeV using collinear factorized pQCD with the GLV radiative energy loss formalism. They incorporate two simplified forms of the energy loss fluctuation distribution P(epsilon, bar-epsilon), a "uniform" model and a "squeezed" model, to assess systematic uncertainties, fitting the average fractional energy loss bar-epsilon_g(200) to PHENIX pi0 data at 200 AGeV and then extrapolating to 62.4 AGeV using PHOBOS multiplicity systematics. The calculation neglects k_T smearing, Cronin enhancement, shadowing, and nonperturbative baryon contributions, restricting validity to pi0 spectra above p_T > 4-5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For the "squeezed" fluctuation distribution at 200 AGeV, the fitted range of the average gluon fractional energy loss is 0.65 < bar-epsilon_g(200) < 0.76.
- For the "uniform" fluctuation distribution at 200 AGeV, the fitted range is 0.70 < bar-epsilon_g(200) < 0.80.
- The PHOBOS multiplicity systematics give dN_g/dy(sqrt(s)=62.4) approximately 650-770, compared to about 1000 at 200 AGeV.
- For the "squeezed" distribution at 62.4 AGeV with dN_g/dy = 650 (770), the predicted range is 0.42 (0.50) < bar-epsilon_g(62.4) < 0.49 (0.59).
- For the "uniform" distribution at 62.4 AGeV with dN_g/dy = 650 (770), the predicted range is 0.46 (0.54) < bar-epsilon_g(62.4) < 0.52 (0.62).

## Context
This work extends prior GLV formalism calculations (Gyulassy 2003, Gyulassy 2001) and provides predictions for the lower RHIC energy of 62.4 AGeV, where data from PHOBOS (Back 2004) were becoming available. The results are consistent with earlier predictions by Wang (2003) and Vitev (2004), though Vitev included Cronin interactions and k_T smearing which are omitted here. The ratio observable R_s(s) = R_AA(s)/R_AA(200) is proposed as a cleaner probe of the energy dependence of jet quenching, as it cancels uncertainties in multiplicity extrapolations and can differentiate between fluctuation distribution models.
