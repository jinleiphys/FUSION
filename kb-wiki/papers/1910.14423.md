# Neutron-antineutron oscillations in the deuteron studied with $NN$ and $\bar NN$ interactions based on chiral effective field theory

> Neutron-antineutron ($n-\bar n$) oscillations in the deuteron are considered. Specifically, the deuteron lifetime is calculated in terms of the free-space $n-\bar n$ oscillation time $τ_{n-\bar n}$ based on $NN$ and $\bar NN$ interactions derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT). This results in $(2.6\pm 0.1) \times 10^{22}\,τ^2_{n-\bar n}$ s, which is close to the value obtained by Dover and collaborators more than three decades ago, but disagrees with recent EFT calculations that were performed within the perturbative scheme proposed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise. Possible reasons for the difference are discussed.

## Key claim

The deuteron reduced lifetime for neutron-antineutron oscillations is calculated to be \(R = (2.6 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\), which agrees with the older result of Dover et al. (1983) but disagrees by about a factor of 2 with the recent EFT calculation of Oosterhof et al. (2019) that used the perturbative Kaplan-Savage-Wise scheme; the authors attribute the discrepancy to the lack of a proper deuteron wave function in the KSW approach.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in momentum space for the \(np\) and \(\bar n p\) systems, using chiral EFT potentials for both the \(NN\) interaction (from Epelbaum et al., 2005, 2015) and the \(\bar NN\) interaction (from Kang et al., 2013, and Dai et al., 2017). The deuteron decay width is obtained from the imaginary part of the overlap integral between the deuteron wave function and the \(\bar n p\) wave function, and the reduced lifetime \(R\) is extracted via \(\tau_d = R\,\tau_{n-\bar n}^2\).

## Key numbers

* Reduced lifetime for the deuteron: \(R = (2.6 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\), based on chiral EFT interactions.
* For the N\(^3\)LO \(\bar NN\) interaction (Dai et al., 2017): \(R = 2.56 \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\).
* For the N\(^2\)LO \(\bar NN\) interaction (Kang et al., 2013): \(R = 2.49 \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\).
* For the Dover-Richard potentials DR\(_1\) and DR\(_2\) (Dover et al., 1983): \(R = 2.56 \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\) and \(R = 2.40 \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\), respectively.
* For the KSW-based calculation (Oosterhof et al., 2019) using the N\(^3\)LO scattering length: \(R = (1.2 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{22}~\text{s}^{-1}\).
* The \(\bar n p\) \(^3S_1\) scattering length from the N\(^3\)LO chiral interaction: \(a = 0.44 - i\,0.96\) fm.
* The deuteron binding energy: \(B_d = 2.225\) MeV; binding momentum: \(\kappa \approx 45.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark calculations of Sandars (1980) and Dover et al. (1983) by using modern chiral EFT interactions for both the \(NN\) and \(\bar NN\) systems, which are fitted to up-to-date \(\bar pp\) scattering data from the LEAR facility. The result confirms the older phenomenological value of Dover et al. (1983) but contradicts the recent EFT calculation of Oosterhof et al. (2019) that employed the perturbative Kaplan-Savage-Wise scheme; the authors suggest the KSW scheme fails to properly represent the deuteron wave function, as previously seen in studies of the deuteron quadrupole moment (Kaplan, 1999). The calculation enables more reliable extraction of the free neutron-antineutron oscillation time from deuteron experiments.
