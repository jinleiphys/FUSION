# On the extraction of the quark mass ratio (m_d - m_u)/m_s from Gamma(eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^-)/Gamma(eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^-)

> The claim that the light quark mass ratio (m_d - m_u)/m_s can be extracted from the decay width ratio Gamma(eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^-)/Gamma(eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^-) is critically investigated within a U(3) chiral unitary framework. The influence of the recent VES data on the eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^- decay is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper critically investigates the claim that the quark mass ratio (m_d - m_u)/m_s can be extracted from the decay width ratio Gamma(eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^-)/Gamma(eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^-) and finds that the two underlying assumptions of the original proposal are not justified within a U(3) chiral unitary framework.

## Method

The authors employ a U(3) chiral effective field theory combined with a relativistic coupled-channels approach, deriving s- and p-wave interaction kernels for meson-meson scattering from the chiral effective Lagrangian and iterating them in a Bethe-Salpeter equation. They calculate the decay widths and branching ratios for eta' -> 3 pi decays, testing the assumptions that the eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^- amplitude is proportional to the eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^- amplitude via pi^0-eta mixing and that both amplitudes are constant over phase space. The analysis uses fits to meson-meson scattering phase shifts and eta, eta' decay data, including recent VES data on the eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^- spectral shape.

## Key numbers

- The double quark mass ratio Q^2 = (m_s - m_hat)/(m_d - m_u) * (m_s + m_hat)/(m_d + m_u) is set to (24.1)^2 following Dashen's theorem
- The upper limit for the branching fraction of eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^- is 1.75% from VES, corresponding to an upper limit of 3.8 keV for the partial decay width
- Without VES data, the decay width ratio r = Gamma(eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^-)/Gamma(eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^-) ranges from (0.35 to 1.5)% across four fit clusters
- Including VES data, the best fit gives r = 3.9% with Gamma(eta' -> pi^0 pi^+ pi^-) = 3120 eV
- The Dalitz parameter a for eta' -> eta pi^+ pi^- from theory is -0.109, compared to the VES experimental value of -0.127 +/- 0.024
- For Q = 22, the range of r increases to (0.4 to 2.8)%

## Context

This work extends the U(3) chiral unitary approach developed by Borasoy and Nißler (2005) to critically examine the claim by Gross, Treiman, and Wilczek (1979) that the quark mass ratio can be extracted from the simple relation r = (16.8)*(3/16)*((m_d - m_u)/m_s)^2. The results contradict this earlier claim and show that final-state interactions, particularly isospin I=1 p-wave contributions from the rho^+- (770) resonances, invalidate both assumptions. The paper enables future experimental programs at WASA at COSY, MAMI-C, and KLOE at DAPhiNE to provide more precise data needed to clarify the extraction of the quark mass ratio.
