# Chiral Lagrangian for strange hadronic matter

> A generalized Lagrangian for the description of hadronic matter based on the linear $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ $σ$-model is proposed. Besides the baryon octet, the spin-0 and spin-1 nonets, a gluon condensate associated with broken scale invariance is incorporated. The observed values for the vacuum masses of the baryons and mesons are reproduced. In mean-field approximation, vector and scalar interactions yield a saturating nuclear equation of state. We discuss the difficulties and possibilities to construct a chiral invariant baryon-meson interaction that leads to a realistic equation of state. It is found that a coupling of the strange condensate to nucleons is needed to describe the hyperon potentials correctly. The effective baryon masses and the appearance of an abnormal phase of nearly massless nucleons at high densities are examined. A nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry is considered, to retain a Yukawa-type baryon-meson interaction and to establish a connection to the Walecka-model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized chiral SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R Lagrangian for strange hadronic matter that can reproduce vacuum masses of baryons and mesons and yield a saturating nuclear equation of state in mean-field approximation, but the authors explicitly note that constructing a chiral invariant baryon-meson interaction that leads to a realistic equation of state involves significant difficulties, and a coupling of the strange condensate to nucleons is required to describe hyperon potentials correctly.

## Method

The authors construct a linear sigma model extended to SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R symmetry, including the baryon octet, spin-0 and spin-1 meson nonets, and a dilaton field representing the gluon condensate associated with broken scale invariance. They examine three different forms of baryon-meson interaction (linear, quadratic, and cubic in the meson fields) combined with explicit symmetry breaking terms, and apply the mean-field approximation to study nuclear matter properties, hyperon potentials, and effective baryon masses at finite density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- The vector meson coupling constants follow the additive quark model relations: g_{Λω} = g_{Σω} = 2 g_{Ξω} = (2/3) g_{Nω} = 2 g₈^V; g_{Λφ} = g_{Σφ} = g_{Ξφ}/2 = (√2/3) g_{Nω}
- The vacuum expectation values of scalar fields are fixed by PCAC: σ₀ = -f_π; ζ₀ = (1/√2)(f_π - 2 f_K)
- The parameter δ from the QCD beta function for three flavors and three colors is δ = 6/33
- The ω and ρ meson masses are degenerate: m_ω² = m_ρ² = Z_ω m_V²; the φ meson mass satisfies m_φ² = Z_φ m_V²

## Context

This work extends previous SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R linear sigma model studies of nuclear matter (Heide et al., 1994; Carter et al., 1995; Furnstahl et al., 1995; Mishustin et al., 1993) to include strangeness via SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R symmetry. The paper addresses difficulties in simultaneously describing baryon masses and hyperon potentials that arise when extending from the nucleon doublet to the full baryon octet, and it enables future studies of multistrange hypernuclear systems, kaon condensation in neutron stars, and the possible chiral phase transition to nearly massless nucleons at high densities.
