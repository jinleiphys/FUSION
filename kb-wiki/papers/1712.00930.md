# Relation between the Vertex Function and the Propagators in the Pseudovector Coupling Pion-Nucleon System

> The procedure for deriving the Ward-Takahashi identity is applied to the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon system in which the isovector and axial-vector current does not conserved. The relation is given in closed form although it is more complicated than that of the quantum electrodynamics. The application to the propagator has revealed the interesting results.

# Relation between the Vertex Function and the Propagators in the Pseudovector Coupling Pion-Nucleon System

## Key claim

The Ward-Takahashi-like identity derived for the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon system yields a non-perturbative term in the vertex function that, when applied to the nucleon self-energy, produces a renormalized result independent of the coupling constant \(f_\pi\), but only under the approximation that the exact propagators and vertex are replaced by their free or lowest-order forms.

## Method

The author generalizes the QED Ward-Takahashi identity procedure to the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon system, where the axial-vector current is not conserved (\(\partial \cdot J^{5}_i(x) \neq 0\)). Starting from the interacting Lagrangian density \(L(x) = -\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi}\,\bar{\psi}(x)\,\gamma_5\,\gamma_\mu\,\tau_i\,\psi(x)\,\partial^\mu\phi_i(x)\), the equal-time commutation relations and the equation of motion for the pion field are used to derive a generalized relation for T-products. This relation is then applied to the two-point (pion propagator) and three-point (pion-nucleon vertex) functions, yielding a decomposition of the vertex into a perturbative part and a non-perturbative part \(\Gamma_i(p,q)_n \equiv -i\,G(p)^{-1}\,\tau_i\,\gamma_5 -i\,\tau_i\,\gamma_5\,G(q)^{-1}\). The non-perturbative effects on the pion polarization function and the nucleon self-energy are computed using dimensional regularization with \(n = 4-\epsilon\) and renormalization conditions at the on-shell points.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized pion polarization function: \(\Pi^{R}(q) = -\frac{1}{\pi^2}\,(\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi})^2\,M^2\,(I_0+m_\pi^2\,I_1)\,(q^2-m_\pi^2) + \frac{1}{\pi^2}\,(\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi})^2\,M^2\,(q^2\,I(q)-m_\pi^2\,I_0)\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass and \(m_\pi\) the pion mass.
- The renormalized nucleon self-energy under the free-propagator approximation: \(\Sigma^R(p) = \frac{M\,a^2}{M^2+m_\pi^2-\frac{M a}{2}-\frac{a^2}{2}}\), where \(a \equiv \gamma\cdot p - M\).
- The lowest-order polarization function contains a divergent term: \(\Pi^{0}(q)_{per} = \frac{1}{\pi^2}(\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi})^2\,M^2\,q^2\,(-\frac{2}{\epsilon}+I(q))\).
- The approximate self-energy integrals yield: \(A_0(p) =\frac{12 M}{(4 \pi)^2\,\epsilon}\,(\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi})^2\,(M^2+m_\pi^2-\frac{M}{2}\,a-\frac{a^2}{2}) + O(\epsilon^0)\) and \(B_0(p) = 1-\frac{6}{(4 \pi)^2\,\epsilon}\,(\frac{f_\pi}{m_\pi})^2\,(M^2+m_\pi^2-\frac{M}{2}\,a-\frac{a^2}{2}) + O(\epsilon^0)\).

## Context

This work extends the Ward-Takahashi identity formalism, originally developed for QED by Nishijima, to the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon system, a non-conserved current case. It builds on the author's earlier Bethe-Salpeter potential model for nucleon-nucleon interaction (Kinpara), where the axial-vector equation described proton-proton elastic scattering spin observables better than the pseudoscalar equation. The derived non-perturbative vertex relation enables future exact treatment of higher-order quantum corrections in two-nucleon systems, particularly to resolve the apparent charge-independence breaking between proton-proton and proton-neutron elastic scattering where the pseudovector coupling constant \(f_\pi\) shows energy dependence.
