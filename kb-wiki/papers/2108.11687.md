# Numerical Study on Beam-based Alignment of SXFEL Undulator Lattice

> The undulator line of the Shanghai soft X-ray Free-electron Laser facility (SXFEL) has very tight tolerances on the straightness of the electron beam trajectory. However, the beam trajectory cannot meet the lasing requirements due to the influence of beam position, launch angle and quadrupole offsets. Traditional mechanical alignment can only control the rms of offsets to about 100 $μ$m, which is far from reaching the requirement. Further orbit correction can be achieved by beam-based alignment (BBA) method based on electron energy variations. K modulation is used to determine whether the beam passes through the quadrupole magnetic center, and the Dispersion-Free Steering (DFS) method is used to calculate the offsets of quadrupole and BPM. In this paper, a detailed result of simulation is presented which demonstrates that the beam trajectory with rms and standard deviation ($σ$) less than 10 $μ$m can be obtained.

## Key claim

Simulations demonstrate that a three-step beam-based alignment (BBA) procedure, incorporating a two-to-two correction structure and a new "beam info constraint," can reduce the rms and standard deviation of the electron beam trajectory in the SXFEL SASE undulator line to less than 10 μm, starting from initial offsets of about 100 μm from mechanical alignment.

## Method

The authors performed a numerical simulation of the SXFEL SASE undulator line using a custom Python program based on transfer matrices. The BBA procedure consists of three steps: (1) "Ideal orbit-based alignment" (IOBA) using a full response matrix with a novel "beam info constraint" to solve for quadrupole and BPM offsets via singular value decomposition (SVD); (2) "Beam info alignment" (BIA) using K modulation on two quadrupoles in a two-to-two corrector structure to steer the beam through the quadrupole magnetic centers; (3) "Beam orbit-based alignment" (BOBA) using a reduced response matrix to correct residual offsets relative to the beam's straight-line orbit.

## Key numbers

- Initial rms quadrupole offset: 66.54 μm; initial rms BPM offset: 66.20 μm.
- After IOBA (three iterations): rms quadrupole position 13.28 μm, rms BPM position 9.23 μm; rms beam orbit at 1.0 GeV reduced from 415.43 μm to 22.86 μm.
- After full three-step BBA: rms quadrupole position 6.54 μm, rms BPM position 6.63 μm; final beam orbit rms 6.63 μm and standard deviation 4.20 μm (averaged over energies).
- Dispersion η reduced from an initial rms of 1360.06 μm to a final rms of 0.65 μm.
- Beam energies used in simulation: 1.0 GeV, 1.2 GeV, and 1.5 GeV.
- BPM resolution assumed: 1 μm.

## Context

This work extends the beam-based alignment techniques previously applied to FEL undulator lines such as LCLS (Emma, 1999), SACLA (Yamamoto, 2010), FLASH-I (Gubeam, 2013), European XFEL SASE1 (Jin, 2013), and PAL-XFEL (Parc, 2015). The authors propose a new "beam info constraint" to replace the conventional "soft constraint" in the SVD solution of the DFS method. The simulation results enable a practical alignment procedure for the SXFEL SASE undulator line, which will be further optimized by combining with photon beam-based alignment (p-BBA) during commissioning.
