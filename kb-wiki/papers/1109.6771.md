# In-medium effects on $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ spectra in lighter systems

> We aim to explore the in-medium effects on the transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ in lighter mass system $^{12}C+^{12}C$.

# In-medium effects on $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ spectra in lighter systems

## Key claim

In lighter systems like $^{12}C+^{12}C$, the in-medium kaon-nucleon potential causes a difference in the transverse momentum spectra of $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ that is not obscured by poorly known secondary reaction channels, but the effect is only clearly separable when the potential strength factor x is varied and the direct production channel is isolated from secondary contributions.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate $^{12}C+^{12}C$ collisions at 2 AGeV with impact parameters 0-4 fm. They applied a density-dependent kaon mass parametrization $\omega({\bf{k}}=0,\rho) = m_{\rm K^{\pm}}(\rho=0) (1+ \alpha_{\rm K^{\pm}} \frac{\rho}{\rho_{0}})$ with $\alpha_{\rm K^+} = 0.07$ and $\alpha_{\rm K^-} = -0.22$, and multiplied these coefficients by an artificial factor x (x = 0, 1, 2) to study the influence of the potential strength. $K^{-}$ mesons were separated into two classes by tracing back to their antistrange partner: direct production from $BB \to BB K^{+} K^{-}$ and secondary production from $\pi Y$ or $BY \to K^{-}$ reactions.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 2 AGeV for $^{12}C+^{12}C$ collisions
- Impact parameter range: 0-4 fm
- $\alpha_{\rm K^+} = 0.07$ and $\alpha_{\rm K^-} = -0.22$ for the density-dependent kaon mass parametrization
- With the potential switched on, $\omega({\bf{k}}=0,\rho) < 500$ MeV for $K^{-}$ and $\omega({\bf{k}}=0,\rho) > 500$ MeV for $K^{+}$
- Potential strength factor x was varied as 0, 1, and 2

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium kaon properties from heavy-ion collisions to lighter systems, following earlier theoretical calculations by Korpa and Lutz (2004) who derived the density-dependent kaon masses. The authors use the IQMD model as described in Hartnack et al. (1998) and the review by Hartnack et al. on strangeness production. By choosing a lighter system ($^{12}C+^{12}C$), the authors aim to minimize uncertainties from poorly known cross sections for secondary $K^{-}$ production channels ($BY \to BB K^{-}$ and $\pi Y \to K^{-} N$) that dominate in heavier systems, enabling a cleaner test of the in-medium potential effects on kaon spectra.
