# Relativistic mean field description for the shears band mechanism in $^{84}$Rb

> For the first time, the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model is applied to the shears band recently observed in $_{37}^{84}$Rb. Signals of the appearance of the shears mechanism, such as smooth decreases of the shears angle and of the $B$(M1)/$B$(E2) ratio with keeping the nearly constant tilt angle, are well reproduced. Thus it is shown that the microscopic RMF model can nicely describe the shears band in this nucleus.

## Key claim

For the first time, the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model is applied to the shears band in \(^{84}\)Rb, and it reproduces the characteristic signals of the shears mechanism. However, the calculated \(B\)(M1)/\(B\)(E2) ratio must be multiplied by a factor of 0.3 to match the experimental data, and the authors note that only the frequency dependence of this ratio is reliable because pairing correlations are not included.

## Method

The RMF Lagrangian, including \(\sigma\)-, \(\omega\)-, \(\rho\)-mesons and the photon field, is generalized to a uniformly rotating frame with a constant rotational frequency \(\Omega\). The resulting equations of motion are solved by expanding nucleon and meson fields in 3-dimensional harmonic-oscillator eigenfunctions with cutoff parameters \(N_{\rm F}=10\) and \(N_{\rm B}=10\), using the NL3 parameter set. The proton configuration is fixed to \(\pi(pf)^{7}(1g_{9/2})^{2}\) (with respect to \(Z=28\)) and the neutron configuration to \(\nu(1g_{9/2})^{-3}\) (with respect to \(N=50\)), and minima in the tilt-angle plane are searched for each \(\Omega\) from 0.1 to 0.65 MeV. Pairing correlations are not taken into account.

## Key numbers

- The deformation decreases from \(\beta_{2}\sim 0.18\) at \(\Omega=0.3\) MeV to \(\beta_{2}\sim 0.16\) at \(\Omega=0.65\) MeV.
- The tilt angle \(\theta\) stays nearly constant in the range \(\theta\simeq 55^{\circ}-65^{\circ}\) as \(\Omega\) increases.
- The calculated \(B\)(M1)/\(B\)(E2) ratio is multiplied by a factor of 0.3 to compare with experiment; the smooth decrease up to \(\Omega\sim 0.65\) MeV is reproduced.
- Increasing the cutoff parameters to \(N_{\rm F}=N_{\rm B}=12\) changes total energies by only \(0.1\%\), total quadrupole moments by \(2-3\%\), and total angular momenta by \(0.5\%\).

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of shears bands, which had previously been limited to the pairing+QQ Hamiltonian (Frauendorf 1993, Baldsiefen 1994), by applying the more sophisticated RMF model for the first time. It complements shell-model approaches (Frauendorf, Reif, and Winter 1996) that are less suitable for describing polarization of low-\(j\) orbitals. The results enable future RMF studies of other shears bands in the \(A\sim 80\) region, such as \(^{82}\)Rb, and motivate inclusion of pairing correlations to improve quantitative agreement.
