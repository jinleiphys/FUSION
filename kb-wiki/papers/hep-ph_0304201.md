# The perspectives of decoding the nature of the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons and of defining the relative phase between the three-gluon and one-photon amplitudes in the J/psi decays

> It is argued that the search of the J/psi --> f0(980)omega and J/psi --> a0(980)rho decays and the more precise definition of B(J/psi --> f0(980)phi) are the urgent purposes in the J/ψspectroscopy. It is shown that the study of the omega-rho0 interference pattern in the J/psi --> (ρ^0+ω)eta --> pi+pi-eta decay provides evidence for the large (nearly pi/2) relative phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon decay amplitudes .

# Wiki Page: Perspectives on the Nature of the a0(980) and f0(980) Mesons and the Relative Phase in J/ψ Decays

## Key claim
The paper argues that the relative phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon decay amplitudes in J/ψ decays is large (nearly 90°), based on reanalysis of ω-ρ⁰ interference in J/ψ → (ρ⁰+ω)η → π⁺π⁻η data from the MARK III and DM2 Collaborations, but notes that the DM2 result gives a phase of (106±10)° while the MARK III result gives (60±15)°, with the difference attributed to different fitting procedures.

## Method
The author analyzes the ω-ρ⁰ interference pattern in the J/ψ → (ρ⁰+ω)η → π⁺π⁻η decay channel using known ρ⁰-ω mixing parameters. The relative phase between the ρ⁰ and ω production amplitudes is extracted from fits to the π⁺π⁻ invariant mass spectra published by the MARK III Collaboration (1988) and the DM2 Collaboration (1990), then corrected for the small isoscalar one-photon contribution to obtain the phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon amplitudes. For the scalar meson analysis, the author compares branching ratios of J/ψ decays to a0(980)ρ, a2(1320)ρ, f0(980)ω, f0(980)φ, f2(1270)ω, and f2'(1525)φ from the Particle Data Group (2002) and earlier references to test two-quark versus four-quark models of the a0(980) and f0(980).

## Key numbers
- B(J/ψ → a0(980)ρ) < 4.4 × 10⁻⁴ (upper limit, from Kopke 1989)
- B(J/ψ → a2(1320)ρ) = (109 ± 22) × 10⁻⁴ (PDG 2002)
- Ratio B(J/ψ → a0(980)ρ) / B(J/ψ → a2(1320)ρ) < 0.04 ± 0.008
- B(J/ψ → f0(980)ω) = (1.4 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ (PDG 2002)
- B(J/ψ → f0(980)φ) = (3.2 ± 0.9) × 10⁻⁴ (PDG 2002)
- B(J/ψ → f2(1270)ω) = (4.3 ± 0.6) × 10⁻³ (PDG 2002)
- B(J/ψ → f2'(1525)φ) = (8 ± 4) × 10⁻⁴ (PDG 2002)
- Ratio B(J/ψ → f0(980)ω) / B(J/ψ → f2(1270)ω) = 0.033 ± 0.013
- Ratio B(J/ψ → f0(980)ω) / B(J/ψ → f0(980)φ) = 0.44 ± 0.2
- Ratio B(J/ψ → f0(980)φ) / B(J/ψ → f2'(1525)φ) = 0.4 ± 0.23
- ε(m_ω) + g_{ωππ}/g_{ρππ} = (3.41 ± 0.24) × 10⁻² exp{i(102 ± 1)°} (from the author's analysis)

- Relative phase δ between isovector one-photon and three-gluon amplitudes: (60 ± 15)° from MARK III data, (106 ± 10)° from DM2 data (after corrections)

## Context
This work extends the debate on whether the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons are conventional two-quark states, four-quark states (as proposed by Jaffe 1977 in the MIT bag model), or states with a gluon component. The author argues that the two-quark interpretation for both mesons is excluded by the J/ψ decay data, and that the f0(980) is likely a near s\bar{s} state with a small gluon component, while the a0(980) is best described as a four-quark state. The phase analysis extends earlier work by Castro (1995) and Suzuki (1998, 1999) who found large relative phases between one-photon and three-gluon amplitudes in J/ψ decays, but the author notes those analyses relied on SU_f(3) symmetry assumptions and incorrect treatment of ρ-ω mixing. The results enable model-independent tests of the nature of these scalar mesons and of the mechanism of J/ψ decays, with implications for observability of CP-violating decays. The author recommends further study at KEDR in Novosibirsk, BES II in Beijing, and a τ-CHARM factory.
