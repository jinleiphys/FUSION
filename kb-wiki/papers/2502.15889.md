# Perturbative Corrections to Quark TMDPDFs in the Background-Field Method: Gauge Invariance, Equations of Motion, and Multiple Interactions

> We calculate the perturbative corrections in the strong coupling to the unpolarized quark transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution function (TMDPDF) operator within a background-field framework, extending the approach of Ref. [1]. We focus on ensuring gauge invariance, identifying two key components needed: a gauge-invariant TMDPDF operator with a transverse gauge link at spatial infinity, and accounting of the equations of motion (EoM) of the background fields. We go beyond next-to-leading order in strong coupling expansion, considering multiple interactions with the background field at all orders of strong coupling. By examining the interplay between quark and gluon contributions, we show that spurious singularities, proportional to EoM, can be misinterpreted as genuine divergences in QCD factorization unless properly identified and removed.

## Key claim

The authors show that a "naive" calculation of the NLO correction to the quark TMDPDF operator yields different results in Feynman gauge versus axial gauge, and that achieving gauge invariance requires both the inclusion of a transverse gauge link at spatial infinity and the enforcement of the equations of motion for the background fields; spurious singularities proportional to the equations of motion can be misinterpreted as genuine divergences in QCD factorization unless properly identified and removed.

## Method

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative correction to the unpolarized quark TMDPDF operator using a background-field method, first in the dilute limit with two background quark fields. They perform the calculation in both Feynman gauge and axial gauge (with gauge fixing condition \(A_+ = 0\)), then compare the results and analyze the difference using the vertex identity (Ward identity) applied to all interaction vertices. The analysis is extended beyond the dilute limit to an arbitrary number of interactions with the background field at all orders of strong coupling.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling expansion, with the background field consisting of two quarks in the dilute limit.
* The analysis considers background quarks with finite transverse momenta \(p_\perp \gtrsim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), corresponding to large \(b_\perp \lesssim \Lambda^{-1}_{\rm QCD}\).
* The collinear expansion is valid at small \(b_\perp \ll \Lambda^{-1}_{\rm QCD}\), where the DGLAP logarithms dominate the IR structure of TMDPDFs.
* At small \(x\), the IR structure of TMDPDFs in the MSTT scheme is dominated by large logarithms of the BFKL type.

## Context

This work extends the MSTT factorization approach introduced by Mukherjee, Skokov, Tarasov, and Tiwari (2023), which previously analyzed the unpolarized gluon TMD distribution. The paper addresses the gauge invariance of perturbative calculations in the MSTT scheme, which aims to bridge the gap between the large-\(x_B\) (collinear factorization) and small-\(x_B\) (high-energy rapidity factorization) regimes. The results are general and applicable to perturbative calculations of PDFs in any QCD factorization scheme, and the authors argue that the equations-of-motion ambiguity plays a crucial role in calculations with dense background fields, particularly in small-\(x\) physics.
