# Shape coexistence and evolution in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes

> Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations have been performed to investigate shape coexistence and evolution in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes ${}^{72,74,76}$Kr. The ground-state shape is found to change from oblate in ${}^{72}$Kr to prolate in ${}^{74,76}$Kr, in agreement with experimental data. Quadrupole deformations of the ground states and coexisting $0^{+}_{2}$ states as well as excitation energies of the latter are also well reproduced. While the general agreement between calculated moments of inertia and those deduced from observed spectra confirms the prolate nature of the low-lying yrast states of all three isotopes (except the ground state of ${}^{72}$Kr), the deviation at low spins suggests significant shape mixing. The role of triaxiality in describing shape coexistence and evolution in these nuclei is finally discussed.

## Key claim

Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations reproduce the ground-state shape transition from oblate in \(^{72}\)Kr to prolate in \(^{74,76}\)Kr, but the deviation between calculated and experimental moments of inertia at low spins suggests significant shape mixing that is not explicitly included in the model.

## Method

The authors performed pairing-deformation-frequency self-consistent TRS calculations based on the cranked shell model, using a non-axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential for single-particle levels and including both monopole and quadrupole pairings with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection. The total energy was computed in a three-dimensional deformation space \((\beta_2, \gamma, \beta_4)\) and minimized to find equilibrium deformations for each rotational frequency up to \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.5\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of \(^{72}\)Kr: calculated \(\beta_2 = 0.333\) (experimental \(\beta_2 = 0.330(21)\) from GBB05), with \(\gamma = 60^\circ\) indicating oblate shape.
- Ground state of \(^{74}\)Kr: calculated \(\beta_2 = 0.381\) (experimental \(\beta_2 = 0.419(25)\) from RNT01), with \(\gamma = 2^\circ\) indicating near-prolate shape.
- Ground state of \(^{76}\)Kr: calculated \(\beta_2 = 0.372\) (experimental \(\beta_2 = 0.409(6)\) from RNT01), with \(\gamma = 3^\circ\) indicating near-prolate shape.
- Excited \(0^+_2\) state energy in \(^{72}\)Kr: calculated 0.673 MeV, experimental 0.6710(10) MeV.
- Excited \(0^+_2\) state energy in \(^{74}\)Kr: calculated 0.547 MeV, experimental 0.509(1) MeV.
- Excited \(0^+_2\) state energy in \(^{76}\)Kr: calculated 0.307 MeV, experimental 0.76987(10) MeV.
- \(\gamma\)-ray energies in \(^{72}\)Kr: \(E_\gamma(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 709.72(14)\) keV, \(E_\gamma(4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1) = 611.68(14)\) keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes, including lifetime measurements by GCC05 and Coulomb excitation experiments by GBB05 and CGK07, as well as theoretical beyond-mean-field studies by BBH06 (Skyrme force) and GDG09 (Gogny D1S force). The TRS calculations correctly locate the ground-state shape transition where beyond-mean-field Skyrme studies predicted oblate dominance throughout the chain. The results enable future investigations of shape mixing dynamics at low spins and the role of triaxiality, which the authors find is not crucial in their description, contrasting with the conclusions of GDG09 and FMX13.
