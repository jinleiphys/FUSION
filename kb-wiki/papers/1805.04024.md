# $N Ω$ interaction: meson exchanges, inelastic channels, and quasibound state

> Based on a baryon-baryon interaction model with meson exchanges, we investigate the origin of the strong attraction in the $N Ω( {}^{5}S_{2} )$ interaction, which was indicated by recent lattice QCD simulations. The long range part of the potential is constructed by the conventional mechanisms, the exchanges of the $η$ meson and of the correlated two mesons in the scalar-isoscalar channel, denoted by "$σ$" in the literature. The short range part is represented by the contact interaction. We find that the meson exchanges do not provide sufficient attraction. This means that most of the attraction is attributed to the short range contact interaction. We then evaluate the effect of the coupled channels to the $N Ω( {}^{5}S_{2} )$ interaction. We find that, while the $D$-wave mixing of the $N Ω$ channel is negligible, the inelastic $ΛΞ$, $ΣΞ$, and $ΛΞ(1530)$ channels via the $K$ meson exchange give the attraction of the $N Ω( {}^{5}S_{2} )$ interaction to the same level with the elastic meson exchanges. Although the elimination of these channels induces the energy dependence of the single-channel $N Ω$ interaction, this effect is not significant. With the present model parameters fitted to reproduce the scattering length of the HAL QCD result of the nearly physical quark masses, we obtain the $N Ω( {}^{5}S_{2} )$ quasibound state with its eigenenergy $2611.3 - 0.7 i$ MeV, which corresponds to the binding energy $0.1$ MeV and width $1.5$ MeV for the decay to the $ΛΞ$ and $ΣΞ$ channels. From the analysis of the spatial structure and the compositeness, the quasibound state is shown to be the molecular state of $NΩ$. We also construct an equivalent local potential for the $N Ω( {}^{5}S_{2} )$ system which is useful for various applications.

## Key claim

The strong attraction in the \(N\Omega({}^{5}S_{2})\) interaction, previously indicated by lattice QCD simulations, originates primarily from short-range contact interactions rather than from conventional meson-exchange mechanisms; the \(\eta\) exchange and correlated two-meson ("\(\sigma\)") exchange do not provide sufficient attraction on their own.

## Method

The authors constructed a baryon-baryon interaction model using meson-exchange diagrams: the \(\eta\) exchange, correlated two-meson exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel (treated via a dispersion relation and the chiral unitary approach), and a contact term for short-range contributions. They also evaluated inelastic contributions from the \(\Lambda\Xi\), \(\Sigma\Xi\), and \(\Lambda\Xi(1530)\) channels via \(K\) meson exchange, represented as box diagrams, and solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the single-channel \(N\Omega({}^{5}S_{2})\) scattering amplitude with model parameters fitted to reproduce the HAL QCD scattering length at nearly physical quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The \(N\Omega({}^{5}S_{2})\) quasibound state eigenenergy is \(2611.3 - 0.7 i\) MeV.
- The binding energy of the quasibound state is \(0.1\) MeV.
- The width for decay to the \(\Lambda\Xi\) and \(\Sigma\Xi\) channels is \(1.5\) MeV.
- The \(\Lambda\Xi\) threshold is at 2434 MeV, the \(\Sigma\Xi\) threshold at 2511 MeV, and the \(N\Omega\) threshold at 2611 MeV.
- The \(\Lambda\Xi(1530)\) threshold is at 2649 MeV.
- The cutoff \(t_c\) for the correlated two-meson dispersion integral is set at \((1.2\ \text{GeV})^2\).
- The coupling constant \(f_{PBD} = 1.8\) reproduces \(\Gamma_{\Delta(1232)\to\pi N} = 63\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\Sigma(1385)\to\pi\Lambda} = 33\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\Sigma(1385)\to\pi\Sigma} = 5\) MeV, and \(\Gamma_{\Xi(1530)\to\pi\Xi} = 14\) MeV.
- The coupling constant \(f_{PDD} = 2.09\) is derived from the nucleon axial charge \(g_A = 1.26\).
- The meson decay constants used are \(f_\pi = 92.1\) MeV, \(f_K = 1.2 f_\pi\), and \(f_\eta = 1.3 f_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD lattice QCD results for the \(N\Omega({}^{5}S_{2})\) interaction (Etminan 2014, Doi 2017, Iritani 2018) by identifying the physical origin of the attraction through a meson-exchange model, and by including inelastic channels that were assumed negligible in the HAL QCD analyses. It also complements a chiral perturbation theory study of the \(N\Omega\) interaction by Haidenbauer (2017) and provides an equivalent local potential for future few-body calculations of \(\Omega\) nuclei, as proposed in the context of heavy-ion collision correlations by Morita (2016).
