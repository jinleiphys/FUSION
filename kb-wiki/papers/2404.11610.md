# Photoproduction of QED bound states in future electron-ion colliders

> In this work we perform an exploratory study of the photoproduction of singlet QED bound states $(l^-l^+)_S$ in electron - ion collisions at the EicC, EIC and LHeC energies. The total cross - sections, event rates per year and rapidity distributions associated with the parapositronium, paramuonium and paratauonium production are estimated. Moreover, we consider the decay of these states in a two - photon system and implement kinematical cuts on the rapidities and energies of the photons in the final state. We demonstrate the paramuonium can, in principle, be observed for the first time in all these colliders and that the EIC is a potential collider to discover the paratauonium state.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that paramuonium can, in principle, be observed for the first time at the EicC, EIC, and LHeC, and that the EIC is a potential collider to discover the paratauonium state, but only when realistic experimental cuts on photon rapidities and energies are applied; without cuts, the raw event rates are much larger but the signal would be inseparable from backgrounds.

## Method

The total cross-section for photoproduction of singlet QED bound states in electron-ion collisions is calculated using the equivalent photon approximation, where the photon flux from the electron and the nucleus are convolved with the Born-level two-photon fusion cross-section given by the Low formula. The nuclear photon spectrum uses a realistic Wood-Saxon charge form factor. The decay of the produced bound states into two photons is simulated with Pythia6, and kinematical cuts on photon pseudorapidity (|eta| <= 3.5) and minimum photon energy (1.0 GeV or 0.5 GeV) are applied to model detector acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Parapositronium total cross-section at EIC: 48.45 x 10^3 nb, corresponding to 4.84 x 10^12 events per year (assuming 100 fb^-1 integrated luminosity).
- Paramuonium total cross-section at EIC: 150.88 x 10^-3 nb, corresponding to 15.09 x 10^6 events per year.
- Paratauonium total cross-section at EIC: 41.44 x 10^-6 nb, corresponding to 4.14 x 10^3 events per year.
- Paramuonium cross-section at EIC after cuts (E_gamma >= 1.0 GeV): 3.18 x 10^-3 nb, yielding 0.32 x 10^6 events per year.
- Paratauonium cross-section at EIC after cuts (E_gamma >= 1.0 GeV): 28.80 x 10^-6 nb, yielding 2.88 x 10^3 events per year.
- Paramuonium cross-section at LHeC after cuts (E_gamma >= 1.0 GeV): 9.33 x 10^-3 nb, yielding 9.33 x 10^3 events per year (with 1.0 fb^-1 luminosity).
- Paratauonium cross-section at EicC after cuts (E_gamma >= 1.0 GeV): 3.05 x 10^-9 nb, yielding 0.15 events per year (effectively unobservable).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of QED bound state production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and FCC by Francener (2021) and in e+e- and hadronic colliders by Serbo and others, now applying the formalism to electron-ion colliders for the first time. The results enable experimental searches for the never-observed paramuonium and paratauonium states at the upcoming EIC, EicC, and LHeC facilities, and motivate further Monte Carlo implementation and extension to electroproduction of these states.
