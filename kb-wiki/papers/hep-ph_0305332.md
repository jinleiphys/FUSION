# Baryon Stopping and the Valence Quark Distribution at Small x

> We argue that the amount of baryon stopping observed in the central rapidity region of heavy ion collisions at RHIC is proportional to the nuclear valence quark distributions at small x. By generalizing Mueller's dipole model to describe Reggeons we construct a non-linear evolution equation for the valence quark distributions at small x in the leading double-logarithmic approximation. The equation includes the effects of gluon saturation in it. The solution of the evolution equation gives a valence quark distribution function $dn_{val}/dy \sim e^{-(0.4\div0.5) y}$. We show that this y-dependence as well as the predictions of Regge theory are consistent with the net-proton rapidity distribution reported by BRAHMS.

## Key claim

The amount of baryon stopping observed in the central rapidity region of heavy ion collisions at RHIC is proportional to the nuclear valence quark distributions at small x, with the caveat that non-perturbative topological excitations of the gluon field might also contribute to baryon number but are expected to be small at weak coupling and are not considered in this work.

## Method

The authors generalize Mueller's dipole model to describe Reggeons, constructing a non-linear evolution equation for valence quark distributions at small x in the leading double-logarithmic approximation that includes gluon saturation effects. They first construct the quasi-classical valence quark wave function of a nucleus in the McLerran-Venugopalan model, then derive a small-x evolution equation within the dipole picture, and finally solve the full non-linear evolution equation using a simple theta function model of the dipole scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The solution of the evolution equation gives a valence quark distribution function $dn_{val}/dy \sim e^{-(0.4\div0.5) \, y}$
- The quark saturation scale is defined as $Q_s^{quark} = (2/3) \, Q_s$
- For the valence quark distribution from ladder diagrams, $dn_{val} / dx \sim 1/x^{0.5 - 0.7}$ when taking $\alpha_s \sim 0.25 - 0.5$
- The ratio of valence quarks to gluons at lowest order scales as $dn_{val}/dy \div dn_G/dy = z N_c/2 \sim e^{-y}$

## Context

This work extends the understanding of valence quark distributions at small x, which was previously not understood, by applying techniques developed for gluon distributions in the Color Glass Condensate framework. The authors build upon the BFKL evolution equation (BFKL, 1978) and DGLAP evolution (Dokshitzer, 1977; Gribov and Lipatov, 1972; Altarelli and Parisi, 1977), as well as the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) and Mueller's dipole model (Mueller, 1994). The results enable comparison with net-proton rapidity distributions from the BRAHMS experiment at RHIC and suggest the existence of an intermediate phase between the Color Glass Condensate and the parton gas, called the Color Quantum Fluid.
