# Distinct Hagedorn temperatures from particle spectra: a higher one for mesons, a lower one for baryons

> We analyze experimental particle spectra and show that the Hagedorn temperature is significantly larger for mesons than for baryons. The effect can be explained within dual string models: excitations of three strings in the baryon produce ``faster'' combinatorics than a single string in the meson, hence lead to a more rapid growth of baryons than mesons. Predictions of other approaches for the gross features of particle spectra are also discussed.

## Key claim

The Hagedorn temperature extracted from experimental particle spectra is significantly larger for mesons (195 MeV) than for baryons (141 MeV), contradicting the long-held assumption of a single universal Hagedorn temperature; however, the extracted values depend strongly on the assumed functional form of the pre-exponential factor, so the Hagedorn temperature cannot be treated as an absolute parameter without specifying the model.

## Method

The author analyzed cumulants of meson and baryon spectra from the Particle Data Group tables, defined as the number of states with mass lower than a given value, weighted by spin-isospin degeneracy. A Hagedorn-like fit of the form ρ(m) = f(m) exp(m/T) was applied, with f(m) = A/(m² + (500 MeV)²)^(5/4), using least-square fits to log N(m) over the range up to m = 1.8 GeV, skipping the lightest particle in each set. Various alternative forms of f(m) were also tested to assess the sensitivity of the extracted temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Meson Hagedorn temperature: T_meson = 195 MeV; baryon Hagedorn temperature: T_baryon = 141 MeV (for the fit with f(m) = A/(m² + (500 MeV)²)^(5/4))
- The ratio T_meson / T_baryon = √3 in the asymptotic dual string model prediction
- Effective Hagedorn temperatures (including pre-exponential effects) at m̄ = 1 GeV: T_eff(meson) = 311 MeV, T_eff(baryon) = 186 MeV
- In the compound-hadron-model fit, the average level spacing ΔE = 100 MeV for mesons and ΔE = 106 MeV for baryons
- To make the meson line parallel to the baryon line would require aggregating approximately 500 additional meson states up to m = 1.8 GeV, compared to the present number of about 400

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn hypothesis (Hagedorn, 1960s) by showing that mesons and baryons have distinct Hagedorn temperatures, a result previously pointed out by Florkowski and Broniowski (1999-2000). The effect is explained within Dual String Models (Jacob, 1970s), where baryons have three independently vibrating strings leading to faster combinatorial growth than the single string in mesons. The paper contrasts this with Statistical Bootstrap Models and Bag Models (MIT Bag Model, Kapusta, Gagnon), which predict equal Hagedorn temperatures for mesons and baryons, and also discusses alternative descriptions such as the Compound Hadron Model and quark models (Godfrey, Capstick; Freund and Rosner). The results enable future tests of whether the exponential growth continues above 2 GeV, whether Regge trajectories terminate, and whether a "light-flavor desert" exists.
