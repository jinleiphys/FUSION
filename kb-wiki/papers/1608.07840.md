# Longitudinal Wobbling in $^{133}$La

> Excited states of $^{133}$La have been investigated to search for the wobbling excitation mode in the low-spin regime. Wobbling bands with $n_ω$ = 0 and 1 are identified along with the interconnecting $ΔI$ = 1, $E2$ transitions, which are regarded as one of the characteristic features of the wobbling motion. An increase in wobbling frequency with spin implies longitudinal wobbling for $^{133}$La, in contrast with the case of transverse wobbling observed in $^{135}$Pr. This is the first observation of a longitudinal wobbling band in nuclei. The experimental observations are accounted for by calculations using the quasiparticle-triaxial-rotor (QTR) model, which attribute the appearance of longitudinal wobbling to the early alignment of a $π=+$ proton pair.

# Longitudinal Wobbling in $^{133}$La

## Key claim

This paper reports the first observation of a longitudinal wobbling band in any nucleus, identified in $^{133}$La. The wobbling frequency increases with spin, which is the hallmark of longitudinal wobbling, in contrast to the transverse wobbling previously observed in the neighboring isotone $^{135}$Pr; the authors attribute this change to the early alignment of a pair of positive-parity quasiprotons with the short axis.

## Method

Excited states of $^{133}$La were populated via the $^{126}$Te($^{11}$B, 4n) fusion-evaporation reaction at 52 MeV beam energy, using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 21 Compton-suppressed clover HPGe detectors. Gamma-gamma-gamma coincidence data were analyzed to construct the level scheme, and directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios, polarization asymmetries, and angular distribution measurements were used to determine spin-parity assignments and multipolarities of transitions. The experimental results were compared with quasiparticle-triaxial-rotor (QTR) model calculations, both with and without the harmonic frozen approximation (HFA), and with tilted-axis cranking (TAC) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Wobbling bands with $n_\omega = 0$ and $n_\omega = 1$ identified; the $n_\omega = 1$ band has a band head at $13/2^-$.
- Yrast band extended to $I^\pi = 43/2^-$; yrare (wobbling) band extended to $I^\pi = 29/2^-$.
- Mixing ratios for interconnecting transitions: $\delta = -1.48^{+0.45}_{-0.32}$ (618 keV), $-2.05^{+0.39}_{-0.30}$ (758 keV), $-2.60^{+0.46}_{-0.47}$ (874 keV), $-3.07^{+0.47}_{-0.65}$ (982 keV).
- $E2$ fractions for interconnecting transitions increase with spin: 68.6% (618 keV), 80.8% (758 keV), 87.1% (874 keV), 90.4% (982 keV).
- Experimental $B(E2_{out})/B(E2_{in})$ ratios: $1.127^{+0.140}_{-0.130}$ (758 keV), $0.716^{+0.079}_{-0.079}$ (874 keV), $0.545^{+0.057}_{-0.059}$ (982 keV).
- QTR model parameters: deformation $\varepsilon = 0.16$, $\gamma = 26^\circ$; core moments of inertia for $^{133}$La: $\mathcal{J}_m = 21 \times 0.73\ \hbar^2$/MeV, $\mathcal{J}_l = 4 \times 0.73\ \hbar^2$/MeV, $\mathcal{J}_s = \Theta_0 + \Theta_1 R$ with $\Theta_0 = 12.5 \times 0.73\ \hbar^2$/MeV, $\Theta_1 = 0.9 \times 0.73\ \hbar$/MeV.
- Ratios $\mathcal{J}_m/\mathcal{J}_s/\mathcal{J}_l$ at $I = 33/2$: 21/19/4 for $^{133}$La (longitudinal), 21/15.4/4 for $^{135}$Pr (transverse).

## Context

This work extends the study of wobbling motion in triaxial nuclei from the previously observed transverse wobbling in $^{135}$Pr (Mattia et al., 2015) and in Lu and Ta isotopes (Odegard et al., 2001; Schonwaßer et al., 2003; Amro et al., 2003; Bringel et al., 2005; Hartley et al., 2009) to the first case of longitudinal wobbling. The theoretical framework builds on the semiclassical analysis of Frauendorf and Dönau (2014), which distinguishes longitudinal and transverse wobbling modes in odd-A nuclei based on the orientation of the odd particle's angular momentum relative to the axis with the largest moment of inertia. The results enable further investigation of how quasiparticle alignments can switch the wobbling character between neighboring isotones, and suggest that the dipole band observed at high spin in $^{133}$La may have magnetic rotation character with a possible chiral partner.
