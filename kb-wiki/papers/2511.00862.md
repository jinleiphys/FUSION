# Investigation of $2־½$ECEC in $^{132}$Ba and revisited for $^{78}$Kr

> We present a theoretical investigation of two-neutrino double electron capture ($2־½$ECEC) in $^{132}$Ba and $^{78}$Kr based on large-scale shell-model calculations. The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the $2־½$ECEC process in $^{132}$Ba and $^{78}$Kr have been calculated using the SN100PN and GWBXG effective interactions, respectively. The reliability of these interactions is first assessed by reproducing the spectroscopic properties of the parent, intermediate, and granddaughter nuclei of the decay. Two different formulations are employed to estimate the half-lives. We also examine the cumulative contribution of $1^+$ and $0^+$ states in the intermediate nuclei to the NMEs associated with the Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi transitions, respectively. Our results provide an improved prediction of the NME and half-life for $2־½$ECEC in $^{78}$Kr compared to previous studies and offer a precise theoretical benchmark for future experimental investigations in $^{132}$Ba.

# Investigation of $2\nu$ECEC in $^{132}$Ba and revisited for $^{78}$Kr

## Key claim
The authors present the first shell-model calculation of the nuclear matrix element for two-neutrino double electron capture in $^{132}$Ba, and provide an improved prediction for $^{78}$Kr by extending the neutron model space to include excitations above the $N=50$ shell closure; however, the half-life predictions depend strongly on the effective axial-vector coupling constant $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$, and for $^{132}$Ba the calculated half-lives exceed the current experimental lower limit by approximately three orders of magnitude.

## Method
Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the SN100PN effective interaction for $^{132}$Ba (with the $0g_{7/2}1d2s0h_{11/2}$ model space and truncations for computational feasibility) and the GWBXG effective interaction for $^{78}$Kr (with extended neutron space allowing one particle-one hole excitations across $N=50$ into $0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, and $2s_{1/2}$ orbitals). The reliability of each interaction was first assessed by comparing calculated spectroscopic properties (energy levels and $B(E2)$ transition strengths) of parent, intermediate, and granddaughter nuclei against experimental data. Two formulations were employed to estimate half-lives: one using only Gamow-Teller transitions and another incorporating both Gamow-Teller and Fermi transitions, with cumulative contributions from $1^+$ and $0^+$ states in the intermediate nuclei examined.

## Key numbers
- For $^{132}$Ba: Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix element $M_{2\nu} = 0.0654$; calculated half-life range $(2.20-7.33) \times 10^{24}$ yr for $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ from 1.27 to 0.94; experimental lower limit $> 2.2 \times 10^{21}$ yr (Meshik, 2011)
- For $^{132}$Ba with GT+Fermi transitions: total NME $0.0615$ ($g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=1.27$) and $0.0583$ ($g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=0.94$); corresponding half-lives $2.49 \times 10^{24}$ yr and $9.22 \times 10^{24}$ yr
- For $^{78}$Kr: Gamow-Teller NME $0.2311$ (improved from previous value $0.1997$); calculated half-life range $(1.38-8.78) \times 10^{22}$ yr for $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}$ from 1.27 to 0.8; experimental half-life $[1.9^{+1.3}_{-0.7}({\rm stat}) \pm 0.3({\rm syst})] \times 10^{22}$ yr (Ratkevich, 2024)
- For $^{78}$Kr with GT+Fermi transitions: total NME $0.2083$ ($g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=1.27$) and $0.1738$ ($g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff}=0.8$); corresponding half-lives $1.70 \times 10^{22}$ yr and $15.53 \times 10^{22}$ yr
- Fermi NME for $^{132}$Ba: $M_{2\nu}^{\rm F} = 0.0063$ (approximately 10 times smaller than GT NME)
- Fermi NME for $^{78}$Kr: $0.0367$ (approximately 6.3 times smaller than GT NME)
- Phase-space factor $G_{2\nu}^{\rm ECEC} = 40.87 \times 10^{-24}$ yr$^{-1}$ for $^{132}$Ba and $520.518 \times 10^{-24}$ yr$^{-1}$ for $^{78}$Kr (Nitescu, 2024)

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model studies of two-neutrino double electron capture, notably the authors' own earlier calculation for $^{78}$Kr (Patel, 2023) which used a more restricted neutron model space without orbitals above $N=50$. The present results for $^{78}$Kr show improved agreement with direct experimental observations from Gavrilyuk (2017) and Ratkevich (2024) using low-background gas proportional counters. For $^{132}$Ba, this is the first shell-model calculation of the NME, providing a theoretical benchmark for future experimental investigations; the only existing experimental constraint is a geochemical lower limit from Meshik (2011). The study also complements previous theoretical work using other nuclear models such as the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (Suhonen, 2010), interacting boson model (Yoshida, 2013), and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model (Raina, 2013) for double-beta decay and double electron capture processes. The results enable more precise guidance for future experimental searches for $2\nu$ECEC in $^{132}$Ba and provide improved nuclear matrix element inputs for neutrinoless double-beta decay searches.
