# Momentum Broadening in Weakly Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the probability distribution P(k_\perp) for the momentum perpendicular to its original direction of motion that an energetic quark or gluon picks up as it propagates through weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the probability distribution \(P(k_\perp)\) for the transverse momentum that an energetic quark or gluon acquires as it propagates through a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium, going beyond the usual hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. The leading contribution to \(P(k_\perp)\) is found to be of order \(g^2\) for \(k_\perp < gT\) and of order \(g^4\) for \(k_\perp > T\), indicating that the distribution has qualitatively different behavior in different transverse momentum regimes.

## Method

The authors compute the probability distribution \(P(k_\perp)\) by evaluating the expectation value of two Wilson lines in the appropriate representation along the light cone, separated in the transverse spatial direction, in a thermal equilibrium QCD medium. They expand the Wilson line correlator in the coupling \(g\) and use the resummed gluon propagator in the light-cone gauge, expressing the result in terms of the retarded gluon self-energy components \(F\) and \(G\). The calculation is performed in the limit of infinite medium thickness \(L \to \infty\) and for parton energy \(E\) much greater than any medium momentum scales, with the final distribution expressed as a delta function plus a "plus distribution" that renders it finite and integrable.

## Key numbers

- The leading contribution to \(P(k_\perp)\) is of order \(g^2\) for transverse momenta \(k_\perp < gT\)
- The leading contribution to \(P(k_\perp)\) is of order \(g^4\) for transverse momenta \(k_\perp > T\)
- The probability distribution is normalized such that \(\int \frac{d^2 k_\perp}{(2\pi)^2} P(k_\perp) = 1\)
- The expression for \(P(k_\perp)\) involves the medium thickness \(L = L^-/\sqrt{2}\) and the temperature \(T\) of the equilibrium plasma

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of jet momentum broadening that used the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation by evaluating the full resummed propagator contributions. The general expression for \(P(k_\perp)\) in terms of Wilson lines was derived in D'Eramo (2010) using soft collinear effective theory (SCET), and the same formalism was previously applied to the strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma in D'Eramo (2010). The results enable future calculations of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) (the second moment of \(P(k_\perp)\)) and provide a benchmark for comparing weakly coupled plasma predictions with data from heavy ion collisions.
