# Description of the odd $^{249-253}$No nuclei using Skyrme functionals with modified spin-spin interaction

> The Skyrme energy-density functionals with modified spin-spin interaction and pairing strength are used for description of the $^{249}$No, $^{251}$No, and $^{253}$No excited states. The results of the HFB and cranked HFB calculations taking into account the blocking effect are in reasonable agreement with the available data. For many states, including rotational and three-quasiparticle states, the results of self-consistent calculations were obtained for the first time.

## Key claim

The Skyrme energy-density functionals UNEDF1 and SV-bas$_{-0.44}$, with modified spin-spin interaction and newly adjusted pairing strengths, can describe the excited states of odd $^{249-253}$No nuclei in reasonable agreement with available experimental data, with the UDF1m functional showing somewhat better agreement than bas44; however, the calculations rely on the specific choice of pairing parameters fitted to actinide odd-even mass differences and the results for many states, including rotational and three-quasiparticle states, are obtained for the first time in a self-consistent framework.

## Method

The author performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) and cranked SHFB calculations using a modified version of the HFODD code (version 3.18j) with a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis of 969 states (N=16 quanta). Neutron and proton pairing strengths for the modified UNEDF1 (UDF1m) and SV-bas$_{-0.44}$ (bas44) functionals were adjusted by fitting the calculated three-point odd-even mass differences to experimental values for actinide nuclei ($^{237}$Np, $^{243}$Am, $^{249}$Bk, $^{233}$Th, $^{251}$Cf, and $^{253}$No). Excited states were obtained by blocking relevant one-quasiparticle and three-quasiparticle levels, and rotational bands were treated via the cranking approximation with a constraint on the angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths for UDF1m: $(V_n, V_p) = (-228.4, -270)$ MeV.
- Pairing strengths for bas44: $(V_n, V_p) = (-252.9, -306)$ MeV.
- For $^{253}$No, the ground state is $9/2^-$ (0 keV); the first excited rotational state $(11/2^-)$ is at 63 keV (UDF1m) vs. 64.0(10) keV experimental.
- For $^{253}$No, the $7/2^+$ state is predicted at 220 keV (UDF1m) and 513 keV (bas44), compared to an experimental value of 355 keV (Lopez-Martens 2007).
- For $^{251}$No, the ground state $7/2^+$ is at 0 keV; the $(9/2^+)$ rotational state is at 63 keV (UDF1m) and 58 keV (bas44), vs. 60.3(3) keV experimental.
- For $^{249}$No, the ground state $5/2^+$ is at 0 keV; the $1/2^+$ state is predicted at 254 keV (UDF1m), vs. approximately 125 keV experimental.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent Skyrme-EDF calculations to superheavy odd nobelium isotopes ($^{249-253}$No), where previous theoretical studies were mostly based on phenomenological mean fields such as the Woods-Saxon potential (Cwiok 1994; Parkhomenko 2005) or the quasiparticle-phonon model (Adamian 2011; Shirikova 2015; Adamian 2018). Prior self-consistent calculations for $^{253}$No (Bender 2003; Dobaczewski 2015) showed significant discrepancies with experiment, and the author notes that some earlier SHFB results (Adamian 2011; Adamian 2021) used incorrect pairing strengths. The modified functionals UDF1m and bas44, previously tested on lighter nuclei (Sassarini 2022; Bonnard 2023; Gray 2023), are here applied for the first time to superheavy nuclei, enabling future comparisons with experimental data on rotational bands and three-quasiparticle states in the transfermium region.
