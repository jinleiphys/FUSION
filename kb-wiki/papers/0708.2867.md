# Nuclear matter in the crust of neutron stars derived from realistic NN interactions

> Properties of inhomogeneous nuclear matter are evaluated within a relativistic mean field approximation using density dependent coupling constants. A parameterization for these coupling constants is presented, which reproduces the properties of the nucleon self-energy obtained in Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter but also provides a good description for bulk properties of finite nuclei. The inhomogeneous infinite matter is described in terms of cubic Wigner-Seitz cells, which allows for a microscopic description of the structures in the so-called ``pasta-phase'' of nuclear configurations and provides a smooth transition to the limit of homogeneous matter. The effects of pairing properties and finite temperature are considered. A comparison is made to corresponding results employing the phenomenological Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach and the consequences for the Thomas-Fermi approximation are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) parameterization that reproduces Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock (DBHF) results for asymmetric nuclear matter at high densities, but requires a small correction to the omega-meson coupling constant around saturation density to also achieve good agreement with empirical binding energies and radii of finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field calculations using density-dependent coupling constants fitted to reproduce the nucleon self-energies obtained from DBHF calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter by van Dalen et al. (2007). Inhomogeneous infinite matter was described using cubic Wigner-Seitz cells to allow for microscopic description of pasta-phase structures, with pairing correlations included via finite-temperature BCS formalism and the meson field equations solved in spatial representation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.166 fm^-3 in DDRMF (compared to 0.181 fm^-3 in DBHF)
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.23 MeV in DDRMF (compared to -16.15 MeV in DBHF)
- Compression modulus K: 335 MeV in DDRMF (compared to 230 MeV in DBHF)
- Symmetry energy coefficient a_s: 29.92 MeV in DDRMF (compared to 34.36 MeV in DBHF)
- Binding energy per nucleon for 208Pb: -7.87 MeV (experimental value: -7.87 MeV)
- Charge radius for 208Pb: 5.31 fm (experimental value: 5.50 fm)
- Binding energy per nucleon for 16O: -8.35 MeV (experimental value: -7.98 MeV)
- Charge radius for 16O: 2.78 fm (experimental value: 2.74 fm)

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to derive effective Lagrangians from DBHF calculations by Schiller (2001) and Hofmann (2001), and is based on the DBHF calculations of van Dalen et al. (2007) for asymmetric nuclear matter. The cubic Wigner-Seitz cell approach follows earlier Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme forces by Bonche and Vautherin (1981) and Goegelein (2007), but replaces the phenomenological Skyrme interaction with a microscopically grounded relativistic mean field model, enabling more reliable predictions for neutron star crust properties at high densities, large asymmetries, and finite temperatures.
