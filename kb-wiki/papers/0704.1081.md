# Asymmetry of in-medium rho-mesons as a signature of Cherenkov effects

> Cherenkov gluons may be responsible for the asymmetry of dilepton mass spectra near rho-meson observed in experiment. They can be produced only in the low-mass wing of the resonance. Therefore the dilepton mass spectra are flattened there and their peak is slightly shifted to lower masses compared with the in-vacuum rho-meson mass. This feature must be common for all resonances.

## Key claim

Cherenkov gluons, produced only in the low-mass wing of a resonance where the nuclear index of refraction exceeds 1, can explain the observed asymmetry and low-mass excess of dilepton mass spectra near the rho-meson, with the effect being universal for all resonances.

## Method

The authors propose a formula for the dilepton mass spectrum that adds a Cherenkov term proportional to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude (and thus to the index of refraction) to the standard Breit-Wigner term, but only for masses below the resonance peak. They fit the parameters A and Gamma to the measured mass spectrum in the range 0.75–0.9 GeV from NA60 data, then determine the single parameter w by fitting the excess spectrum from 0.4 GeV to 0.75 GeV.

## Key numbers

- In-vacuum rho-meson mass m_rho = 775 MeV.
- Fitted in-medium width Gamma = 0.354 GeV, larger than the in-vacuum rho-meson width of 150 MeV.
- Fitted parameter A = 104 GeV^3.
- Fitted Cherenkov weight parameter w = 0.19.
- Estimated downward shift of the peak about 40 MeV compared to m_rho.
- Cherenkov dominance region of masses from 400 MeV to 600 MeV below the rho-resonance.
- Ratio of vector meson resonances estimated as rho:omega:phi = 10:1:2.

## Context

This work extends the idea of Cherenkov gluons, previously considered for very high energies (Dremin, 2000) and resonance production (Dremin, 2005), to explain the low-mass dilepton excess observed in nucleus-nucleus collisions by experiments such as NA60 and CERES. It offers an alternative to explanations based on chiral symmetry restoration (Pisarski, 1982) or anti-baryonic effects from hadronic many-body theory (Rapp and Wambach, 2000; Rapp, 2001). The prediction of asymmetric in-medium widening on the low-mass side of any resonance is universal and could be tested by measuring angular distributions of lepton pairs relative to a companion jet axis.
