# Diffusion of Neon in White Dwarf Stars

> Sedimentation of the neutron rich isotope $^{22}$Ne may be an important source of gravitational energy during the cooling of white dwarf stars. This depends on the diffusion constant for $^{22}$Ne in strongly coupled plasma mixtures. We calculate self-diffusion constants $D_i$ from molecular dynamics simulations of carbon, oxygen, and neon mixtures. We find that $D_i$ in a mixture does not differ greatly from earlier one component plasma results. For strong coupling (coulomb parameter $Γ>$ few), $D_i$ has a modest dependence on the charge $Z_i$ of the ion species, $D_i \propto Z_i^{-2/3}$. However $D_i$ depends more strongly on $Z_i$ for weak coupling (smaller $Γ$). We conclude that the self-diffusion constant $D_{\rm Ne}$ for $^{22}$Ne in carbon, oxygen, and neon plasma mixtures is accurately known so that uncertainties in $D_{\rm Ne}$ should be unimportant for simulations of white dwarf cooling.

## Key claim
The self-diffusion constant for ²²Ne in carbon, oxygen, and neon plasma mixtures is accurately known from classical molecular dynamics simulations, so uncertainties in D_Ne should be unimportant for simulations of white dwarf cooling, provided the plasma is in the strongly coupled liquid phase (coulomb parameter Γ > few).

## Method
Classical molecular dynamics simulations were performed for a three-component mixture of 49% ¹²C, 49% ¹⁶O, and 2% ²²Ne (by number), with ions interacting via screened Yukawa potentials using Thomas-Fermi screening. Self-diffusion constants D_i were calculated from the time integral of the velocity autocorrelation function for each ion species, with simulations run for N = 3456, 8192, and 27648 ions to test finite-size effects, and results were scaled to the one-component plasma fit D_0 = 3 ω̄_p a² / Γ⁴/³.

## Key numbers
- For the three-component mixture at Γ = 9.742 with N = 27648 ions, D_i/D_0 = 1.087 (¹²C), 0.904 (¹⁶O), and 0.788 (²²Ne).
- For the three-component mixture at Γ = 194.84 with N = 27648 ions, D_i/D_0 = 0.783 (¹²C), 0.638 (¹⁶O), and 0.533 (²²Ne).
- For pure ¹⁶O at Γ = 247.00 with N = 27648 ions, D/D_0 = 0.441.
- The screening length used for the three-component mixture was λ = 2.816 a, where a is the ion sphere radius.
- A fifty percent decrease in λ changed diffusion constants by only two percent or less at Γ = 9.75.

## Context
This work extends earlier molecular dynamics simulations of diffusion in one-component plasmas by Hansen et al. (1975) and binary mixtures by Hansen et al. (1985), and complements studies of ²²Ne sedimentation in white dwarfs by Garcia-Berro et al. (2008, 2010). The results provide the diffusion coefficients needed to model gravitational energy release from ²²Ne sedimentation during white dwarf cooling, and the authors note that future work should simulate diffusion in the solid phase, where D_i is often arbitrarily assumed to be zero.
