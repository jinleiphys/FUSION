# Phenomenological spin-orbit three-body force

> In order to improve the description of the N-d vector analyzing powers Ay and iT11 (the so-called Ay puzzle) a spin-orbit three-body force has been introduced. The L.S term in the NN potential has been modified including a two-parameter three-body function depending on the hyperradius ρ. The modification has been performed in channels where the pair nucleons (i,j) are coupled to spin Sij=1 and isospin Tij=1. Calculations have been done in the energy region from Elab=648 keV up to Elab=10 MeV. A noticeable improvement in the description of the polarization observables has been obtained

# Phenomenological spin-orbit three-body force

## Key claim

A phenomenological spin-orbit three-nucleon force (SO-3NF), introduced as a two-parameter modification of the L·S term in the NN potential for channels where the pair nucleons are coupled to spin S_ij=1 and isospin T_ij=1, can substantially improve the description of the N-d vector analyzing powers A_y and iT_11 (the "A_y puzzle") across an energy range from 648 keV to 10 MeV, while leaving the bound state structure and other observables essentially unaffected. The improvement is achieved without requiring additional charge-symmetry breaking effects, though the specific choice of range and strength parameters (α, W_0) shows slightly different sensitivity at different energies.

## Method

The author modified the L·S term in the AV18 NN potential by replacing the two-body function v^ls_11(r_ij) with a three-body function w^ls_11(r_ijk) = v^ls_11(r_ij) + W_0 exp(-αρ), where ρ is the hyperradius of the three-nucleon system. Three parameter sets (α, W_0) = (0.7 fm^-1, -1 MeV), (1.2 fm^-1, -10 MeV), and (1.5 fm^-1, -20 MeV) were selected to simulate long, medium, and short range forces, with the strength adjusted at E_lab = 3.0 MeV to improve vector observables. Calculations were performed using the Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis with the generalized Kohn variational principle for scattering states, covering energies from 648 keV to 10 MeV, including both below and above the deuteron breakup threshold.

## Key numbers

- Three parameter sets for the SO-3NF: (α = 0.7 fm^-1, W_0 = -1 MeV), (α = 1.2 fm^-1, W_0 = -10 MeV), (α = 1.5 fm^-1, W_0 = -20 MeV)
- Energy range studied: from E_lab = 648 keV up to E_lab = 10 MeV
- The SO-3NF produces a repulsive effect on the ^3He binding energy of no more than 50 keV
- At 10 MeV, the χ^2 per datum for A_y improved by a factor of approximately 25 going from AV18 alone to AV18+LS2 (α = 1.2 fm^-1, W_0 = -10 MeV)
- At 3 MeV, the χ^2 per datum for the differential cross section with AV18 alone was 30.3, reducing to 4.0 with AV18+UR (Urbana 2π-3NF included)
- The AV18 potential alone underpredicts A_y and iT_11 by about 30%

## Context

This work addresses the "A_y puzzle" in N-d scattering, where all realistic NN potentials (including AV18) underpredict the vector analyzing powers due to insufficient splitting in the ^4P_J phase shifts and underprediction of the ε_3/2- mixing parameter. Previous attempts using two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces such as the Tucson-Melbourne (TM), Brazil (BR), and Urbana (UR) models were unsuccessful in resolving this discrepancy, as they tend to reduce the ^4P_J splitting rather than increase it. The SO-3NF introduced here is complementary to these 2π-3NFs: it selectively affects the vector observables with minimal impact on binding energies, whereas the 2π-3NFs primarily affect the J=1/2+ state and binding energies. This work enables further studies including the combined effect of both types of three-nucleon forces, extension to higher energies, and application to the four-body p-^3He reaction.
