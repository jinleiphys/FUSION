# Revisiting the T2K data using different models for the neutrino-nucleus cross sections

> We present a three-flavour fit to the recent νμ--> νe and νμ--> νμT2K oscillation data with different models for the neutrino-nucleus cross section. We show that, even for a limited statistics, the allowed regions and best fit points in the (θ_{13},δ_{CP}) and (θ_{23},Δm^2_{atm}) planes are affected if, instead of using the Fermi Gas model to describe the quasielastic cross section, we employ a model including the multinucleon emission channel.

# Revisiting the T2K data using different models for the neutrino-nucleus cross sections

## Key claim

Even with limited statistics, the allowed regions and best fit points for the oscillation parameters \((\theta_{13},\delta_{CP})\) and \((\theta_{23},\Delta m^2_{atm})\) shift when the quasielastic cross section is modeled with a multinucleon emission channel (the MECM model) instead of the Fermi Gas model used by the T2K collaboration; the effect is that the MECM model predicts a larger CCQE cross section, requiring a smaller \(\theta_{13}\) in the appearance channel and a larger \(\Delta m^2_{atm}\) in the disappearance channel to fit the same data.

## Method

The authors performed a three-flavor fit to T2K \(\nu_\mu \to \nu_e\) appearance and \(\nu_\mu \to \nu_\mu\) disappearance data using two different neutrino-nucleus cross section models: the Fermi Gas model (as implemented in the NEUT Monte Carlo generator, with the Rein and Sehgal model for pion production) and the MECM model of Martini, Ericson, Chanfray and Marteau (2010), which includes multinucleon emission (2p-2h and 3p-3h excitations) via nuclear response functions calculated in random phase approximation. They normalized both models to the T2K near detector inclusive event rate (1529 \(\nu_\mu\) CC events with \(2.9 \times 10^{19}\) POT) and then computed expected event rates at the far detector, applying energy-dependent efficiency corrections extracted from the T2K Monte Carlo expectations for \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}=0.1\). The \(\chi^2\) analysis used GloBES and MonteCUBES software, marginalizing over all oscillation parameters not shown, with external errors of 3% on \(\theta_{12}\) and \(\Delta m^2_{sol}\), 8% on \(\theta_{23}\), and 6% on \(\Delta m^2_{atm}\).

## Key numbers

- Best fit \(\sin^2(2\theta_{13})\) in the appearance channel: 0.108 (Fermi Gas) vs. 0.073 (MECM), with \(\delta_{CP}=0\) in both cases; \(\chi^2_{min}=1.69\) (FG) and 1.53 (MECM).
- At 1\(\sigma\) in the appearance channel: \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}^{MECM} = 0.08^{+0.07}_{-0.05}\) and \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}^{FG} = 0.12^{+0.08}_{-0.09}\).
- Best fit in the disappearance channel (Fermi Gas): \(\sin^2 2\theta_{23}=0.99\) (47.9\(^\circ\)), \(\Delta m^2_{atm}=2.56 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\); (MECM): \(\sin^2 2\theta_{23}=1.00\) (45.0\(^\circ\)), \(\Delta m^2_{atm}=2.62 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\).
- 90% CL intervals for disappearance (Fermi Gas): \(\sin^2 2\theta_{23} > 0.86\), \(2.22 \times 10^{-3} < \Delta m^2_{atm} (\text{eV}^2) < 2.90 \times 10^{-3}\); (MECM): \(\sin^2 2\theta_{23} > 0.91\), \(2.31 \times 10^{-3} < \Delta m^2_{atm} (\text{eV}^2) < 2.93 \times 10^{-3}\).
- With 10 times the current statistics (projected), 90% intervals for \(\sin^2 2\theta_{13}\): FG [0.041-0.211] (best fit 0.105), MECM [0.023-0.154] (best fit 0.092); for \(\theta_{23}\): FG [40.1\(^\circ\)-51.3\(^\circ\)] (best fit 47.6\(^\circ\)), MECM [41.1\(^\circ\)-49.9\(^\circ\)] (best fit 45.4\(^\circ\)); for \(\Delta m^2_{atm}\): FG [2.45-2.67] \(\times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\) (best fit 2.56), MECM [2.49-2.67] \(\times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\) (best fit 2.60).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fernandez-Martinez (2010) on the impact of different quasielastic cross section models in the low-gamma beta-beam regime to the T2K experiment. The MECM model was previously shown by Martini, Ericson, Chanfray and Marteau (2010) to explain the MiniBooNE quasielastic total cross section, which appeared too large relative to predictions using the standard axial mass; subsequent models by Nieves (2011), Amaro (2011), Bodek (2011), and Lalakulich (2012) also included multinucleon contributions. The results demonstrate that nuclear model uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross sections can shift the extracted oscillation parameters at a level that may become distinguishable with increased T2K statistics (a factor of 10 more data), motivating combined appearance-disappearance analyses and more refined nuclear treatments for future precision measurements.
