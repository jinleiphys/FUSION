# Probing in-medium vector meson decays by double-differential di-electron spectra in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

> Within a transport code simulation for heavy-ion collisions at bombarding energies around 1 AGeV, we demonstrate that double-differential di-electron spectra with suitable kinematical cuts are useful to isolate (i) the $ρ$ meson peak even in case of strong broadening, and (ii) the in-medium $ω$ decay contribution. The expected in-medium modifications of the vector meson spectral densities can thus be probed in this energy range via the di-electron channel.

# Probing in-medium vector meson decays by double-differential di-electron spectra in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

## Key claim

The double-differential di-electron spectrum dN/dM² dM⊥² with suitable kinematical cuts can isolate the ρ meson peak even under conditions of strong in-medium broadening, and can separate the in-medium ω decay contribution from vacuum decays, but the results are not quantitative predictions and depend on specific parameterizations of the in-medium spectral functions.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport code simulations for central collisions of gold nuclei at 1 AGeV beam energy, including elementary processes of pion annihilation, direct vector meson decays, bremsstrahlung, and Dalitz decays. They employed parameterized density-dependent spectral functions for ρ and ω mesons with variable mass shifts and width broadenings, and computed double-differential di-electron spectra dσ/dM dM⊥ with specific cuts on transverse mass M⊥ and transverse momentum Q⊥.

## Key numbers

- For the ρ meson, a strong broadening parameter a = 3 (i.e., Γρ(0)(n) = 3 × 118 MeV) and mass shift Δmρ = 0 MeV was used; selecting the M⊥ window 850 MeV < M⊥ < 950 MeV made the ρ peak clearly visible despite the broadening.
- For the ρ meson with a = 3 and Δmρ = 150 MeV, the double-differential spectrum with the same M⊥ window still showed the mass shift clearly, while the invariant mass spectrum showed no ρ peak.
- For the ω meson, in-medium parameters δmω = 70 MeV and δΓω = 50 MeV were used; selecting di-electrons with Q⊥ < 100 MeV isolated the in-medium ω decay contribution as a shifted bump.
- The analysis focused on the invariant mass region M > 400 MeV where pion annihilation dominates over Dalitz decay and bremsstrahlung contributions.

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic fireball model calculations by Wolf, Pavlenko, and Kampfer (2003) to a full BUU transport simulation without assuming thermal or chemical equilibrium, and without rescattering of ρ and ω mesons. It addresses the difficulty identified by Weise (2000) that ρ meson spectroscopy becomes impossible due to broadening, and follows the proposal by Cassing (2002) to select low-Q⊥ di-electrons for ω in-medium studies. The results are intended to inform measurement strategies for the HADES detector at SIS18 at GSI/Darmstadt, enabling future experimental searches for in-medium vector meson modifications via the di-electron channel.
