# Hamiltonian effective field theory study of the $\mathbf{N^*(1535)}$ resonance in lattice QCD

> Drawing on experimental data for baryon resonances, Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) is used to predict the positions of the finite-volume energy levels to be observed in lattice QCD simulations of the lowest-lying $J^P=1/2^-$ nucleon excitation. In the initial analysis, the phenomenological parameters of the Hamiltonian model are constrained by experiment and the finite-volume eigenstate energies are a prediction of the model. The agreement between HEFT predictions and lattice QCD results obtained on volumes with spatial lengths of 2 and 3 fm is excellent. These lattice results also admit a more conventional analysis where the low-energy coefficients are constrained by lattice QCD results, enabling a determination of resonance properties from lattice QCD itself. Finally, the role and importance of various components of the Hamiltonian model are examined.

# Hamiltonian effective field theory study of the N*(1535) resonance in lattice QCD

## Key claim

The Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) model, with parameters constrained by experimental data, predicts finite-volume energy levels for the N*(1535) resonance that agree with lattice QCD results on volumes of 2 and 3 fm; however, when lattice QCD results are used to constrain the model parameters, the extracted pole position of 1602 ± 48 − i 88.6 MeV lies just outside one-sigma agreement with the Particle Data Group estimate of 1510 ± 20 − i 85 ± 40.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian model containing a bare three-quark N*0 state coupled to πN and ηN scattering channels via vertex interactions, plus a separable potential describing direct meson-baryon to meson-baryon transitions. The model parameters are first fit to experimental πN scattering phase shifts and inelasticities in the J^P = 1/2^- channel, then the finite-volume energy levels are computed by discretizing momenta in a periodic box. The lattice QCD results from two volumes (L ≈ 1.98 fm and L ≈ 2.90 fm) are then used in a second analysis where the bare mass and its quark-mass dependence are fit to extract the infinite-volume resonance pole position.

## Key numbers

- Pole position from experimental fit: 1531 ± 29 − i 88 ± 2 MeV, compared to PDG estimate of 1510 ± 20 − i 85 ± 40
- Bare mass from experimental fit: m0 = 1601 ± 14 MeV
- Coupling constants from experimental fit: g^S_πN = −0.0608 ± 0.0004, g_N*0 πN = 0.186 ± 0.006, g_N*0 ηN = 0.185 ± 0.017
- χ²/dof = 6.8 for fitting 56 experimental data points with four parameters
- Pole position from lattice-constrained fit (with separable potential): 1602 ± 48 − i 88.6^{+0.7}_{−2.8} MeV
- Bare mass from lattice-constrained fit: m0 = 1644^{+34}_{−30} MeV, with slope α0 = 0.77^{+0.15}_{−0.16} GeV⁻¹
- χ²/dof = 1.7 for fitting 23 lattice energy eigenstates over three levels on two volumes
- Pole position from lattice-constrained fit (without separable potential): Re(pole) = 1563^{+52}_{−80} MeV, −Im(pole) = 89.2^{+0.2}_{−4.2} MeV, with χ²/dof = 4.6
- Regulator mass parameter Λ = 0.8 GeV; pion decay constant f = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the HEFT approach previously applied to the Δ resonance by Hall et al. (2013) and the Λ(1405) by Hall et al. (2014) to the N*(1535) resonance, which couples to both πN and ηN channels in S-wave. The study uses lattice QCD results from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards et al., 2011, 2013), Lang and Verduci (2012), the CSSM lattice group in Adelaide (Stokes et al., Kiratidis et al., 2015, Mahbub et al., 2012, 2013), and the Cyprus collaboration using AMIAS (Alexandrou et al., 2014). The method enables determination of resonance properties from lattice QCD itself, and the authors identify the separable potential terms as essential for accurate description of the lowest-lying scattering state. The formalism is proposed for future application to the Roper resonance in the 1/2⁺ channel.
