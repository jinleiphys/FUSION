# QCD Phase Diagram at NICA energies: $K^+/π^+$ horn effect and light clusters in THESEUS

> We discuss recent progress in the development of the three-fluid hydrodynamics-based program THESEUS towards an event generator suitable for applications to heavy-ion collisions at the intermediate energies of the planned NICA and FAIR experiments. We follow the strategy that modifications of particle distributions at the freeze-out surface in the QCD phase diagram may be mapped directly to the observable ones within a sudden freeze-out scheme. We report first results of these investigations for the production of light clusters (deuterons and tritons) which can be compared to experimental data from the HADES and the NA49 experiment and for the interpretation of the "horn" effect observed in the collision energy dependence of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio. Medium effects on light cluster production in the QCD phase diagram are negligible at the highest NICA energies but shall play a dominant role at the lowest energies. A sharp "horn"-type signal in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio can be obtained when the onset of Bose condensation modelled by a pion chemical potential results in an enhancement of pions at low momenta (which is seen at LHC energies) and would occur already in the NICA energy range.

## Key claim

The THESEUS three-fluid hydrodynamics framework can reproduce the "horn" effect in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio when the onset of Bose condensation, modeled via a nonequilibrium pion chemical potential, enhances low-momentum pions already in the NICA energy range; medium effects on light cluster production are negligible at the highest NICA energies but become dominant at the lowest energies.

## Method

The authors extended the three-fluid hydrodynamics-based event generator THESEUS with a sudden freeze-out scheme that maps modifications of particle distributions at the freeze-out surface in the QCD phase diagram directly to observables. For light clusters (deuterons and tritons), they compared thermal statistical model calculations with and without in-medium selfenergy effects against preliminary HADES data at \(E_{\rm lab}=1.23\) A GeV and against the 3FD model with coalescence. For the \(K^+/\pi^+\) horn effect, they used a Beth-Uhlenbeck expression for the ratio of kaon and pion yields, with in-medium phase shifts calculated within the PNJL model, and scanned the phase diagram along a line combining the critical line with a straight constant-temperature segment.

## Key numbers

- At the lowest available collision energies, inclusion of light clusters increases the freeze-out density by a factor of two compared to the thermal statistical model without clusters (Blaschke 2020).
- Preliminary HADES data for Au+Au collisions at \(E_{\rm lab}=1.23\) A GeV are compared with THESEUS simulations at impact parameter \(b=4\) fm.
- The scan line for the \(K/\pi\) ratios includes a straight part for a pion chemical potential \(\mu_\pi = 134.5\) MeV.
- Medium effects on light cluster production are negligible at energies above \(E_{\rm lab}\approx 20\) A GeV but required at lower energies.

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid hydrodynamics approach of Batyuk (2016) and the THESEUS event generator toward the NICA and FAIR energy regimes. It builds on the thermal statistical model of Cleymans (2005) for chemical freeze-out and the Mott-line formalism of Schuck (1999) and Roepke (2017) for in-medium cluster dissociation. The horn-effect analysis uses the PNJL model of Dubinin (2016) and Blaschke (2019) and connects to the experimental observation by Gazdzicki (1998). The results enable future inclusion of in-medium effects on cluster and hadron distribution functions into the THESEUS particlization routine to improve descriptions of light cluster formation and the horn effect.
