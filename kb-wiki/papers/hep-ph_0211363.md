# From DIS to proton-nucleus collisions in the Color Glass Condensate model

> We show that particle production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions in the Color Glass Condensate model can be related to Deep Inelastic Scattering of leptons on protons/nuclei (DIS). The common building block is the quark antiquark (or gluon-gluon) dipole cross section which is present in both DIS and pA processes. This correspondence in a sense generalizes the standard leading twist approach to pA collisions based on collinear factorization and perturbative QCD, and allows one to express the pA cross sections in terms of a universal quantity (dipole cross section) which, in principle, can be measured in DIS or other processes. Therefore, using the parameterization of dipole cross section at HERA, one can calculate particle production cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions at high energies. Alternatively, one could use proton-nucleus experiments to further constrain models of the dipole cross-section. We show that the McLerran-Venugopalan model predicts enhancement of cross sections at large p_t (Cronin effect) and suppression of cross sections at low p_t. The cross over depends on rapidity and moves to higher p_t as one goes to more forward rapidities.

## Key claim

The authors show that particle production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions within the Color Glass Condensate model can be expressed in terms of the same universal dipole cross-section that appears in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS), thereby generalizing the standard leading-twist collinear factorization approach to include all higher-twist effects, provided the dipole cross-section is known from DIS measurements or other processes.

## Method

The authors derive the amplitude for virtual photon production in pA collisions (qA → qγ*X) using the Color Glass Condensate model, where the nucleus is treated as a classical color field and the proton is described by standard parton distributions. They then compare this amplitude to the DIS amplitude (γ*A → qq̅X) and show that both can be expressed in terms of the same dipole cross-section σ_dipole(r_⊥), defined as the Fourier transform of the correlator of Wilson lines U(x_⊥). The quark scattering amplitude in pA collisions is similarly related to the dipole cross-section, allowing forward pion production to be computed via convolution with fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The Golec-Biernat-Wüsthoff model fit to HERA DIS data (x < 0.01) gives σ₀ = 23 mb, λ = 0.29, and x₀ = 3 × 10⁻⁴ for the dipole cross-section parameterization σ_dipole(x, r_⊥) = σ₀(1 − exp(−r_⊥²/4R₀²(x))) with R₀²(x) [GeV⁻²] = (x/x₀)^λ.
- The large q_⊥ behavior of the Fourier transform of the dipole cross-section in the McLerran-Venugopalan model is C(q_⊥) = 2 Q_s²/q_⊥⁴ + (8/π) Q_s⁴/q_⊥⁶ (ln(q_⊥/Λ_QCD) − 1) + O(Q_s⁶/q_⊥⁸), where Q_s² ∼ α_s² ∫ dz⁻ μ²(z⁻) is the saturation scale.
- The leading term of the q_⊥ spectrum scales like A (from the transverse area πR² times A¹/³ from Q_s²), while the next-to-leading term scales like A⁴/³ and is positive, producing a Cronin effect.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian (2002) and Gelis and Jalilian-Marian (2002) for forward particle production in pA collisions by explicitly connecting it to DIS via the universal dipole cross-section. It generalizes the leading-twist collinear factorization approach used in standard pA calculations, which break down due to higher-twist multiple scattering effects. The relation enables predictions for pA collisions at RHIC using dipole cross-section parameterizations from HERA (e.g., Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff, 1998; Bartels, Golec-Biernat, and Kowalski, 2002), and conversely allows pA experiments to constrain dipole cross-section models.
