# Transverse momentum fluctuations due to temperature variation in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The event-by-event p_T-fluctuations due to the temperature variation are considered. The so-called Phi-measure is computed and shown to be a linear function of the temperature variance. The fluctuations of temperature naturally explain the data on Phi(p_T) in proton-proton and central Pb-Pb collisions but independent measurements of the temperature fluctuations are needed to confirm the explanation. Feasibility of such an event-by-event measurement is discussed.

# Transverse momentum fluctuations due to temperature variation in high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The event-by-event temperature fluctuations can explain the measured values of the Phi-measure for transverse momentum in both proton-proton and central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, but independent measurements of the temperature variance are needed to confirm this explanation.

## Method

The authors compute the Phi-measure for transverse momentum fluctuations assuming that the single-particle transverse momentum distribution follows a thermal form with a temperature that varies from event to event. They derive an analytical expression for Phi as a linear function of the temperature variance in the massless limit, and then perform Monte Carlo simulations of proton-proton collisions using a thermal distribution for pions with realistic multiplicity distributions and finite detector acceptance effects.

## Key numbers

- Measured Phi(p_T) in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon: 4.6 ± 1.5 MeV; after subtracting short-range Bose-Einstein correlations: 0.6 ± 1.0 MeV
- Measured Phi(p_T) in proton-proton collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon: 5 ± 1 MeV
- To reproduce the p-p Phi value, the required temperature variance is sqrt(<T^2> - <T>^2) = 20 ± 3 MeV at <T> = 167 MeV, corresponding to q-1 = 0.015 ± 0.003
- For central Pb-Pb collisions with <T> = 180 MeV and <N> = 270, the temperature dispersion from Phi = 0.6 ± 1.0 MeV is sqrt(<T^2> - <T>^2) = 0.5 ± 0.4 MeV; with <T> = 120 MeV, it is 0.4 ± 0.4 MeV
- The heat capacity per pion extracted from p-p data is c_v = 6 ± 2, compared to 10.8 for massless non-interacting bosons

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Korus and Mrowczynski, 2001) of the correlation between average transverse momentum and event multiplicity, which was found to be too weak to explain the experimental Phi values. The paper builds on the concept of temperature fluctuations in nuclear collisions discussed by Stodolsky (1995), Shuryak (1998), and Stephanov (1999), and relates Phi to the non-extensivity parameter q of Tsallis statistics as shown by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000). The results enable future experimental measurements of event-by-event temperature fluctuations to determine the specific heat of hadronic matter at freeze-out, with a proposed method using the event's average transverse mass.
