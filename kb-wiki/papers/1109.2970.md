# Parity reversion in ^{12}_ΛBe

> The spectrum of $^{12}_Λ$Be is studied by an extended version of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei. The result predicts the positive-parity ground state of $^{12}_Λ$Be that is reverted to the normal one by the impurity effect of $Λ$ particle. The reversion of the parity is due to the difference of $Λ$ binding energy in the positive- and negative-parity states that originates in the difference of $α$ clustering and deformation.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the ground state of \(^{12}_\Lambda\)Be has negative parity, reverting the parity inversion seen in the core nucleus \(^{11}\)Be; this reversion depends on the difference in \(\Lambda\) binding energy between positive- and negative-parity states, which originates in the different \(\alpha\) clustering and deformation of those states.

## Method

The authors used an extended version of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) to calculate the spectrum of \(^{12}_\Lambda\)Be. The variational wave function was optimized under a constraint on the matter quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta\) from 0 to 1.2 in steps of 0.025, followed by angular-momentum projection and generator coordinate method (GCM) superposition. Three different \(\Lambda N\) effective interactions (YNG-ND, YNG-NF, and Improved-NF) were employed, with the Gogny D1S force used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \(^{12}_\Lambda\)Be (\(0^-_1\)) has a binding energy of 74.69 MeV; the first excited doublet (\(0^+_1\)) lies at 0.25 MeV (250 keV) excitation energy, with binding energy 74.44 MeV (Improved-NF interaction).
* The \(\Lambda\) binding energy \(B_\Lambda\) is 10.24 MeV for the \(0^-_1\) state and 9.67 MeV for the \(0^+_1\) state, a difference of about 500 keV.
* The matter quadrupole deformation \(\beta\) is 0.47 for the \(0^-_1\) state and 0.70 for the \(0^+_1\) state; the root-mean-square radii are 2.51 fm and 2.67 fm, respectively.
* In the core nucleus \(^{11}\)Be, the calculated ground state (\(1/2^+_1\)) binding energy is 64.77 MeV and the first excited state (\(1/2^-_1\)) is at 0.32 MeV (320 keV) excitation, with deformations \(\beta = 0.72\) and 0.52, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of hypernuclear impurity effects, where adding a \(\Lambda\) particle can modify nuclear structure, as previously seen in stabilization (Mod90, Ohk95) and size changes (Hiy99, Tan01). It specifically addresses the "parity inversion" and breakdown of the \(N=8\) magic number in \(^{11}\)Be (Wil59, Tal60, Ajz75), predicting that the \(\Lambda\) impurity reverts the parity order in \(^{12}_\Lambda\)Be. The result suggests that the different deformation between the ground and first excited states of \(^{11}\)Be can be probed by measuring the \(\Lambda\) binding energy difference, and that the excitation energies of certain bands are sensitive to the odd-parity part of the \(\Lambda N\) interaction, as previously noted by Hiyama et al. (1997) for \(^{9}_\Lambda\)Be.
