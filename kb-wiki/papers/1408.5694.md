# A systematic study of magnetic field in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions in the RHIC and LHC energy regions

> The features of magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are systematically studied by using a modified magnetic field model in this paper. The features of magnetic field distributions in the central point are studied in the RHIC and LHC energy regions. We also predict the feature of magnetic fields at LHC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 900, 2760 and 7000 GeV based on the detailed study at RHIC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 62.4, 130 and 200 GeV. The dependencies of the features of magnetic fields on the collision energies, centralities and collision time are systematically investigated, respectively.

## Key claim

The magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions depends strongly on collision energy, impact parameter, and proper time, with the magnitude decreasing more rapidly at LHC energies than at RHIC energies; the authors predict that the chiral magnetic effect should decrease with increasing energy above approximately 200 GeV, contrary to some earlier expectations of energy independence.

## Method

The authors used a modified magnetic field model incorporating a Wood-Saxon nucleon distribution (instead of the uniform distribution used in the Kharzeev-McLerran-Warringa (KMW) model) to calculate the magnetic field at the central point of Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions. They computed contributions from both spectator and participant nucleons, using a baryon stopping distribution with parameter a ≈ 1/2, and evaluated the magnetic field as a function of proper time τ, impact parameter b, and center-of-mass energy √s_NN across RHIC and LHC energy ranges.

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field at RHIC: eB ≈ 10⁵ MeV² at τ = 0.02 fm, b ≈ 12 fm, and √s_NN ≈ 200 GeV
- At LHC √s_NN = 900 GeV: maximum eB ≈ 3.0 × 10³ MeV²
- At LHC √s_NN = 2760 GeV: maximum eB ≈ 4.0 × 10² MeV²
- At LHC √s_NN = 7000 GeV: maximum eB ≈ 80.0 MeV²
- At τ = 2.0 fm, the maximum magnetic field is eB ≈ 50 MeV²
- The magnetic field at the earliest times is estimated to be of order m_π² ≈ 10¹⁸ Gauss
- At RHIC energies (√s_NN = 62.4, 130, 200 GeV), magnetic field magnitude increases with impact parameter for τ ≤ 1.0 fm
- At LHC √s_NN = 7000 GeV, magnetic field decreases with increasing impact parameter for τ ≥ 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the Kharzeev-McLerran-Warringa (KMW) model (2008) by replacing the uniform nuclear density with a more realistic Wood-Saxon distribution, and systematically maps the magnetic field dependence across both RHIC and LHC energies. The results support the suggestion by other authors (2010, 2011) that the chiral magnetic effect should either not change or should decrease with increasing energy, and contradict the argument that the CME should strongly decrease at higher energies due to faster magnetic field decay. The systematic predictions for LHC energies (900, 2760, and 7000 GeV) provide quantitative input for interpreting charge-dependent azimuthal correlation measurements from the ALICE Collaboration and for designing future experimental searches for local parity violation in QCD.
