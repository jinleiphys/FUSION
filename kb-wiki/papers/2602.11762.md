# QCD phase diagram in a magnetic field with baryon and isospin chemical potentials

> Based on the chiral perturbation theory at the leading order, we present the phase diagram of low-energy QCD in a magnetic field at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials. The phase diagram consists of the QCD vacuum, the chiral soliton lattice, the uniform charged pion condensation, an Abrikosov vortex lattice of the charged pions, a baryonic vortex lattice composed of neutral and charged pion vortices with their topological linking number being the baryon number, and a hybrid phase of chiral soliton and vortex lattices, with their intersections carrying the baryon number. While the chiral soliton lattice demands ultra-strong magnetic field $\sim 10^{19}$ G,the intersection phase appears at $\sim10^{17}$ G, which is more realistic in neutron stars.

# QCD phase diagram in a magnetic field with baryon and isospin chemical potentials

## Key claim
The paper presents the first three-dimensional phase diagram of low-energy QCD in a magnetic field at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials, obtained within leading-order chiral perturbation theory. While the chiral soliton lattice requires ultra-strong magnetic fields of order 10^19 G, the newly identified CSL-AVL intersection phase appears at approximately 10^17 G, which is more realistic for neutron star interiors.

## Method
The authors use leading-order chiral perturbation theory with the Wess-Zumino-Witten term to describe low-energy QCD in a uniform magnetic field with both baryon and isospin chemical potentials. They classify phases based on the presence or absence of charged pion condensation, vortex lattices, and chiral soliton lattices, establishing all phase boundaries analytically up to one numerical constant fitted to numerical solutions of the baryonic vortex equations of motion.

## Key numbers
- Critical magnetic field for the chiral soliton lattice: B_CSL = 1.02 × 10^19 G at μ_B = 1 GeV
- Critical magnetic field for the CSL-AVL intersection phase: B^c = 4.28 × 10^17 G at μ_B = 1 GeV
- Critical isospin chemical potential at the triple point: μ_I^c = 307 MeV at μ_B = 1 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV; pion mass: m_π = 139 MeV
- Ginzburg-Landau parameter κ = μ_I / e f_π > m_π / e f_π ~ 4.93, confirming type-II superconductivity
- Upper critical field for the Abrikosov vortex lattice: B_c2 = (μ_I^2 / e) (1 - m_π^4 / μ_I^4)
- Lower critical field: B_c1 ~ (e f_π^2 / 2) (1 - m_π^4 / μ_I^4) log(μ_I / e f_π)
- Fitting constant for baryonic vortex magnetic field: c_v = 1.06

## Context
This work extends the study of the QCD phase diagram under magnetic fields from Son and collaborators (2007) on the chiral soliton lattice, and from Adhikari and collaborators (2015, 2018) on Abrikosov vortex lattices in charged pion condensates. It builds on the recent discovery of baryonic vortices by Hamada and collaborators (2025) and Qiu and collaborators (2024), where linked neutral and charged pion vortices carry baryon number. The results enable phenomenological applications to neutron star cores, where magnetic fields of 10^16–10^18 G and isospin chemical potentials of 50–300 MeV may coexist, and open the question of how these phases compete with hyperonic and Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov phases.
