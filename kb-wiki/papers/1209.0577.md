# Size measurement of dynamically generated resonances with finite boxes

> The structure of dynamically generated states is studied from a viewpoint of the finite volume effect. We establish the relation between the spatial size of a stable bound state and the finite volume mass shift. In a single-channel scattering model, this relation is shown to be valid for a bound state dominated by the two-body molecule component. We generalize this method to the case of a quasi-bound state with finite width in coupled-channel scattering. We define the real-valued size of the resonance in a given closed channel using the response to the finite volume effect on the channel. Applying this method to physical resonances we find that Lambda(1405) and f_{0}(980) are dominated by the Kbar N and K Kbar scattering states, respectively, and that the distance between Kbar N (K Kbar) inside Lambda(1405) [f_{0}(980)] is 1.7-1.9 fm (2.6-3.0 fm). The root mean squared radii of Lambda(1405) and f_0(980) are also estimated from the mean distance between constituents.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the spatial size (mean distance between constituents) of dynamically generated resonances can be extracted from the finite-volume mass shift, and that this method yields a real-valued size for resonances with finite decay width, unlike the complex-valued mean squared radius obtained from form factor methods.

## Method

The authors use a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with a factorized interaction kernel to describe scattering amplitudes, where bound and resonance states appear as poles. They replace the infinite-volume loop integral with a discretized finite-volume loop function for a specific closed channel while keeping other channels in infinite volume, then extract the coupling constant from the leading-order exponential mass shift formula ΔM_B(L) ∝ -g² exp(-μ̄L)/L. The mean distance between constituents is then computed from the extracted coupling using the compositeness relation ⟨r²⟩ = (1-Z)/(4μ B_E).

## Key numbers

- For Λ(1405): the mean distance between K̄ and N constituents is 1.7–1.9 fm
- For f₀(980): the mean distance between K and K̄ constituents is 2.6–3.0 fm
- For a single-channel bound state with binding energy 10 MeV and constant interaction (case I): mean distance √⟨r²⟩ = 1.73 fm from pole residue, 1.7–1.8 fm from finite-volume method
- For a single-channel bound state with binding energy 10 MeV and bare-pole interaction (case II): mean distance √⟨r²⟩ = 0.44 fm from pole residue, 0.5–0.8 fm from finite-volume method
- The bound state mass in the single-channel model is M_B = 1424.6 MeV (binding energy 10 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume techniques developed by Luscher (1985), Beane (2003), Koma (2004), Sasaki (2006), and Davoudi (2011) for bound states to the case of unstable resonances with finite width. It builds on the chiral unitary approach to meson-baryon and meson-meson scattering (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 1997, Oller 1998, Lutz 2001, Hyodo 2011) and the compositeness analysis of Hyodo (2011). The method enables model-independent size determination for hadronic molecules such as Λ(1405) and f₀(980), and can be applied to lattice QCD calculations of resonance properties.
