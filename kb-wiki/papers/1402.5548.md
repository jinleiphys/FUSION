# Nonlinear waves in second order conformal hydrodynamics

> In this work we study wave propagation in dissipative relativistic fluids described by a simplified set of the 2nd order viscous conformal hydrodynamic equations. Small amplitude waves are studied within the linearization approximation while waves with large amplitude are investigated using the reductive perturbation method. Our results indicate the presence of a "soliton-like" wave solution in 2nd order conformal hydrodynamics despite the presence of dissipation and relaxation effects.

# Nonlinear waves in second order conformal hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors claim that "soliton-like" wave solutions exist in Israel-Stewart (second-order conformal) hydrodynamics despite the presence of dissipation and relaxation effects, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of transport coefficients and initial conditions, for large relaxation times or steep initial gradients, the nonlinear perturbations can become unstable or unphysically amplified, suggesting an upper bound on the relaxation time that is not visible in linear perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors linearize the simplified conformal Israel-Stewart equations around equilibrium to obtain a lossy wave equation and its dispersion relation, then analyze stability and causality for the three modes. For nonlinear waves, they apply the reductive perturbation method with stretched coordinates, expanding the energy density, velocity, and shear stress tensor to third order in a small parameter σ, and derive a coupled system: a Burgers' equation for the first-order energy perturbation (identical to the Navier-Stokes case) and a second equation for the second-order perturbation that contains relaxation time effects. They solve these equations numerically using sech² and Gaussian initial profiles for various values of shear viscosity η₀/s₀ and relaxation time τ̂π.

## Key numbers

- For strongly coupled 𝒩=4 SYM fluid: η₀/s₀ = 1/(4π) and τ̂π = [2−ln(2)]/(2π) ≈ 0.080 (dimensionless, from the text's explicit expression)
- The dispersion relation yields three modes; the sound modes at small k̂ have attenuation Im[ω̂I] = Im[ω̂II] = −(χ/2)k̂² + O(k̂⁴) where χ = (4/3)(η₀/s₀)
- The non-hydrodynamic mode ω̂III = −i/τ̂π + iχk̂² + O(k̂⁴) defines a microscopic relaxation scale
- For large viscosity test case: η₀/s₀ = 1 and τ̂π = 5 η₀/s₀ (i.e., τ̂π = 5)
- For the relaxation time variation study: τ̂π = 120 η₀/s₀ (large) compared to τ̂π = 0 (Navier-Stokes limit)
- Gaussian initial profile widths tested: D₁ = D₂ = 2 and D₁ = D₂ = 20; amplitudes C₁ = 0.5, C₂ = 0.3
- Sech² initial profile parameters: A₁ = 0.8, A₂ = 0.2, B₁ = B₂ = 0.5 (narrow) and B₁ = B₂ = 3 (wide)

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear wave analysis of Fogaça, Navarra, and Noronha (2013) from relativistic Navier-Stokes theory to Israel-Stewart (IS) second-order hydrodynamics, incorporating the shear viscosity relaxation time τπ. The linear stability and causality analysis follows the framework of Pu, Koide, and Rischke (2010) and shows that IS theory with strongly-coupled 𝒩=4 SYM transport coefficients is causal and stable for all wavenumbers. The reductive perturbation method was previously applied to relativistic fluids by several authors (Davidson 1972, Leblond 1978, and others). The results enable future studies of how relaxation time affects nonlinear fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the possibility of constraining τπ from nonlinear wave stability, a constraint not available from linear analysis alone.
