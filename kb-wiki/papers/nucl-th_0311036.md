# Nuclear structure with accurate chiral perturbation theory nucleon-nucleon potential: Application to 6Li and 10B

> We calculate properties of A=6 system using the accurate charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential at fourth order of chiral perturbation theory. By application of the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) and a variational calculation in the harmonic oscillator basis with basis size up to 16 hbarOmega we obtain the 6Li binding energy of 28.5(5) MeV and a converged excitation spectrum. Also, we calculate properties of 10B using the same NN potential in a basis space of up to 8 hbarOmega. Our results are consistent with results obtained by standard accurate NN potentials and demonstrate a deficiency of Hamiltonians consisting of only two-body terms. At this order of chiral perturbation theory three-body terms appear. It is expected that inclusion of such terms in the Hamiltonian will improve agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The N$^3$LO chiral perturbation theory nucleon-nucleon potential, when used alone without three-body terms, underbinds $^6$Li by about 3.5 MeV relative to experiment and fails to predict the correct ground-state spin of $^{10}$B, demonstrating a systematic deficiency of Hamiltonians containing only two-body interactions.

## Method

The authors applied the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with a two-body effective interaction derived from the N$^3$LO NN potential, performing calculations in a harmonic oscillator basis up to $16\hbar\Omega$ for $^6$Li and $^8\hbar\Omega$ for $^{10}$B using the improved multi-shell version of the ANTOINE code. For $^6$Li, they also performed variational calculations using the bare N$^3$LO potential in the harmonic oscillator basis and extrapolated to infinite basis size using an exponential fit. Results were compared to those obtained with the CD-Bonn 2000 and AV8$^\prime$ potentials.

## Key numbers

- $^6$Li binding energy: 28.5(5) MeV (experimental value is 31.995 MeV, underbinding by about 3.5 MeV)
- $^4$He binding energy: 25.36(4) MeV (obtained with basis spaces up to $N_{\rm max}=18$)
- $^3$H binding energy: 7.85(1) MeV (obtained with basis spaces up to $N_{\rm max}=40$)
- $^4$He point-nucleon root mean square radius: 1.515(10) fm
- $^6$Li quadrupole moment: -0.08(2) $e$ fm$^2$
- $^6$Li $3^+ 0$ excitation energy over-predicted by about 0.7 MeV compared to experiment
- $^{10}$B ground state incorrectly predicted as $1^+ 0$ instead of experimental $3^+ 0$

## Context

This work extends the application of the N$^3$LO chiral perturbation theory NN potential developed by Entem and Machleidt (2003) beyond the two-nucleon system to $A=6$ and $A=10$ nuclei, using the NCSM method previously applied to light nuclei with phenomenological potentials like Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn 2000. The results are consistent with those obtained by standard accurate NN potentials and confirm the need for three-body terms, which appear at N$^2$LO in chiral perturbation theory, to resolve both the underbinding and the incorrect level ordering in $^{10}$B. The work enables future NCSM calculations that include the consistent chiral three-nucleon forces, which are expected to improve agreement with experiment.
