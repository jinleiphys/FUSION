# Updated three-body model of $^6$He $β$ decay into the $α$ + $d$ continuum

> The $β$-decay process of the $^6$He halo nucleus into the $α+d$ continuum is studied in an updated three-body model. The $^6$He nucleus is described as an $α+n+n$ system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange-mesh. The shape and absolute values of the transition probability per time and energy units of new experiments are reproduced with a modified $α+d$ potential. The obtained total transition probabilities are $2.48 \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ for the full energy region and $2.40 \times 10^{-6}$ s$^{-1}$ for the cut-off $E>150$ keV. The strong cancellation between the internal and halo parts of the $β$ decay matrix element is a challenge for future {\it ab initio} calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces the shape and absolute values of new experimental data for the β-decay of ⁶He into the α + d continuum by slightly modifying the α + d Gaussian potential, but the strong cancellation between internal and halo parts of the matrix element means that model-independent quantitative predictions remain very difficult and the potential must be fitted to β-decay data because phase shifts alone do not constrain it enough.

## Method

The ⁶He nucleus is described as an α + n + n three-body system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh, with the ground-state wave function expressed in Jacobi coordinates. The transition probability per time and energy units is computed from the Gamow-Teller reduced transition probability, which involves an integral over the overlap of the ⁶He wave function with the deuteron s-wave radial function and the l = 0 scattering wave function of the α + d system. The α + d potential is refitted from V_M(r) = -79.4 exp(-0.21 r²) to V_N(r) = -80.55 exp(-0.2135 r²) to describe the new experimental data while preserving the ⁶Li ground-state binding energy and the s-wave α + d phase shift up to 4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total transition probability with the new V_N potential: W = 2.48 × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹ for the full energy region.
- Total transition probability with the new V_N potential for cut-off E > 150 keV: W = 2.40 × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹.
- Experimental branching ratio from the 2015 measurement: [2.78 ± 0.07(stat) ± 0.17(sys)] × 10⁻⁶.
- Experimental total transition probability from the 2015 measurement: [2.39 ± 0.06(stat) ± 0.15(sys)] × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹.
- Mass difference Q for the decay: 2.03 MeV.
- Binding energy of the ⁶Li ground state reproduced by both potentials: 1.474 MeV.
- Previous theoretical result from the authors' earlier model: 2.04 × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹ for the full energy range, or 1.59 × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹ for a cutoff E > 370 keV.
- Experimental branching ratio from the 2009 ISOLDE measurement: B = (1.65 ± 0.10) × 10⁻⁶ for deuteron energies above 350 keV, corresponding to W = (1.42 ± 0.09) × 10⁻⁶ s⁻¹.

## Context

This work updates the three-body model of Tursunov, Baye, and Descouvemont (2006) to describe new experimental data from the ISOLDE collaboration (Raabe 2009) and the REX-ISOLDE facility (Pfutzner 2015), which showed a 20% larger total transition probability than the earlier theoretical prediction. The paper confirms the cancellation mechanism between internal and halo parts of the Gamow-Teller matrix element first explained by Baye, Suzuki, and Descouvemont (1994), and suggests that the effective integral I_E(R) can provide a link between phenomenological three-body models and future ab initio calculations, which have studied ⁶He (Quaglioni 2016) and α + d scattering (Hupin 2015) but may struggle with the delicate cancellation and the extended halo up to about 20 fm.
