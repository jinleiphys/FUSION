# Next-to-leading order effective field theory $ΛN\to NN$ potential in coordinate space

> The potential in coordinate space for the $ΛN\to NN$ weak transition, which drives the weak decay of most hypernuclei, is derived within the effective field theory formalism up to next-to-leading order. This coordinate space potential allows us to discuss how the different contributions to the potential add up at the different scales. Explicit expressions are given for each two-pion-exchange diagram contributing to the interaction. The potential is also reorganized into spin and isospin operators, and the coefficient for each operator is given in analytical form and represented in coordinate space. The relevance of explicitly including the mass differences among the baryons appearing in the two-pion-exchange diagrams is also discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives the coordinate-space potential for the weak ΛN → NN transition up to next-to-leading order in effective field theory, but the authors note that neglecting baryonic mass differences (MΛ − MN and MΣ − MΛ) in the two-pion-exchange diagrams yields a simplified potential that is "accurate enough" for practical calculations of non-mesonic weak decay rates, despite the more exact treatment producing complex-valued potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the momentum-space EFT potentials for the ΛN → NN transition previously derived up to NLO (Pérez-Obiol et al., 2015) and Fourier-transform them into coordinate space, including all two-pion-exchange loop diagrams (ball, triangle, crossed-box, and box topologies). They reorganize the resulting potentials into 20 spin and isospin operator structures, and separately derive both the exact potentials (including baryonic mass differences) and approximate potentials (setting MΛ = MΣ = MN) to compare their effects.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi constant times the pion mass squared is GF mπ² = 2.21 × 10⁻⁷
- The pion mass is mπ = 138 MeV and the kaon mass is mK = 495 MeV
- The nucleon mass is MN = 939 MeV and the average mass of the nucleon and Λ is M̄ = 1027 MeV
- The strong couplings used are gNNπ = 13.16 and gΛNK = −13.61 from the Nijmegen 97f model
- The weak pionic couplings are Aπ = 1.05 and Bπ = −7.15, fixed by the mesonic decay of the Λ
- The weak kaonic couplings are CKPC = −18.9, DKPC = 6.63, CKPC = 0.76 and DKPC = 2.09, derived using SU(3) symmetry
- The initial momentum |p| is fixed to 150 MeV in all plotted potentials

## Context

This work extends the EFT treatment of the ΛN → NN weak transition from leading order (Jun, 2000; Parreño et al., 2005; Pérez-Obiol et al., 2013) to next-to-leading order in coordinate space, complementing the momentum-space NLO calculation of Pérez-Obiol et al. (2015). The derived potentials are intended for use in ab-initio few-body computations of hypernuclear weak decay (Hiyama et al., 2009; Nogga et al., 2013; Roth et al., 2014; Lonardoni et al., 2014), and the authors note that the approximate potentials (neglecting baryonic mass differences) should be fast enough for such calculations while remaining accurate.
