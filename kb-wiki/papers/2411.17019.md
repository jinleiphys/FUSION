# Universal Wong formula for capture cross sections from light to super-heavy systems

> A universal Wong formula is proposed with refined model parameters for a systematic description of the capture cross sections for heavy-ion fusion reactions from C+C to Ni+U, in which the barrier parameters and the barrier distribution are determined by the entrance-channel nucleus-nucleus potential based on the Skyrme energy density functional. With introducing a constraint to the width of the barrier distribution and a pocket-depth dependent barrier radius, the capture excitation functions for a number of fusion reactions involving different nuclear structure effects are remarkably well reproduced, particularly for the reactions between light nuclei and those forming super-heavy nuclei. The systematic decreasing behavior of the geometric radii with the depth of capture pocket due to the influence of deep inelastic scattering is clearly observed in the TDHF calculations for super-heavy systems. The predicted capture cross sections for $^{54}$Cr + $^{238}$U at above barrier energies are evidently smaller than the corresponding results of more asymmetric projectile-target combination $^{50}$Ti + $^{242}$Pu due to the shallower capture pocket in Cr+U.

# Universal Wong formula for capture cross sections from light to super-heavy systems

## Key claim
The authors propose a universal Wong formula (FUSION-v2) that can systematically describe capture cross sections for heavy-ion fusion reactions from C+C to Ni+U, but only after introducing a constraint on the barrier distribution width and a pocket-depth dependent barrier radius to account for deep inelastic scattering in super-heavy systems. The formula works for reactions involving different nuclear structure effects, though it is based on a parabolic barrier assumption and may not be applicable for deep sub-barrier fusion.

## Method
The capture cross sections are calculated using a universal Wong formula with a barrier distribution composed of two Gaussian functions, where the barrier parameters and distribution are determined from the entrance-channel nucleus-nucleus potential based on the Skyrme energy density functional under the frozen approximation. A constraint is added so that the width coefficient w is at least the full width at half maximum (FWHM) of a single parabolic barrier, and a pocket-depth dependent factor F_DIS is introduced to modify the barrier radius for super-heavy systems where deep inelastic scattering reduces capture cross sections. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations are used to calibrate the relationship between capture pocket depth and the reduction in geometric radius.

## Key numbers
- For $^{14}$N + $^{16}$O, the width coefficient w = 0.29 MeV without constraint, while the finite width FWHM ≈ 0.56 ħω = 0.61 MeV; with the constraint the experimental data are well reproduced.
- For $^{52}$Cr + $^{232}$Th, the depth of the capture pocket is only about 4.2 MeV, much smaller than for light systems.
- For $^{54}$Cr + $^{238}$U, the capture pocket depth B_cap = 3.80 MeV; for $^{50}$Ti + $^{242}$Pu, B_cap = 4.58 MeV.
- The factor F_DIS uses c_1 = 2.0 MeV in the error function expression.
- The structure factor g is constrained to 0 < g ≤ 2, with c_0 = 0.5 MeV$^{-1}$ for ΔQ < 0 and c_0 = 0.1 MeV$^{-1}$ for ΔQ > 0.
- For $^{16}$O + $^{144}$Sm, the structure factor g = 0.29; for $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm, g = 2.00.
- The coefficient f = 0.926 is taken based on the SkM* parameter set.

## Context
This work extends the universal Wong formula approach of the FUSION-v1 code (Liu Min et al.) by adding a constraint on the barrier distribution width based on systematic studies of 443 fusion reactions by Chen et al. (2023), who found the extracted width coefficient W ≈ (0.014 + 0.135 λ̄_B) V_B. The pocket-depth dependent barrier radius is motivated by TDHF calculations (Yao et al., 2024) showing that contact times below 2 zs (∼600 fm/c) indicate deep inelastic scattering rather than quasi-fission. The results are compared with the empirical coupled channel (ECC) approach (Wang et al.) and KEWPIE2-EBD (Siwek-Wilczynska and Wilczynski, 2004). The predicted smaller capture cross sections for $^{54}$Cr + $^{238}$U compared to $^{50}$Ti + $^{242}$Pu are consistent with recent evaporation residue measurements by Oganessian et al. (2024). This enables more reliable predictions of evaporation residue cross sections for synthesizing new super-heavy nuclei.
