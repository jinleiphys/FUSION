# Anisotropic hydrodynamics -- basic concepts

> Due to the rapid longitudinal expansion of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, potentially large local rest frame momentum-space anisotropies are generated. The magnitude of these momentum-space anisotropies can be so large as to violate the central assumption of canonical viscous hydrodynamical treatments which linearize around an isotropic background. In order to better describe the early-time dynamics of the quark gluon plasma, one can consider instead expanding around a locally anisotropic background which results in a dynamical framework called anisotropic hydrodynamics. In this proceedings contribution we review the basic concepts of the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework presenting viewpoints from both the phenomenological and microscopic points of view.

# Anisotropic hydrodynamics -- basic concepts

## Key claim
Anisotropic hydrodynamics reorganizes viscous hydrodynamics by incorporating large momentum-space anisotropies at leading order, rather than linearizing around an isotropic background as in canonical viscous treatments. This framework is motivated by the observation that in the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the magnitude of momentum-space anisotropies can be so large that linear corrections generate unphysical results such as negative particle pressures and negative one-particle distribution functions.

## Method
The authors present two complementary approaches to anisotropic hydrodynamics. The phenomenological approach directly constructs tensor structures for an anisotropic fluid, using energy-momentum conservation and entropy production laws with a generalized equation of state relating energy density, entropy density, and an anisotropy parameter x. The microscopic approach uses the Boltzmann equation with the Romatschke-Strickland form for the parton distribution function, characterized by two momentum scales (transverse and longitudinal), and takes zeroth and first moments to obtain dynamical equations.

## Key numbers
- The energy-momentum tensor at very early proper times (tau much less than 1/Q_s) from Color Glass Condensate theory has the form T^{mu nu} = diag(epsilon, epsilon, epsilon, -epsilon), implying negative longitudinal pressure with transverse pressure equal to energy density.
- At later proper times (tau much greater than 1/Q_s), the energy-momentum tensor takes the form T^{mu nu} = diag(epsilon, epsilon/2, epsilon/2, 0), implying zero longitudinal pressure.
- The anisotropy parameter x is related to the pressure ratio by the approximation P_parallel/P_perp approximately equal to x^{-3/4}.
- In the microscopic approach, matching to Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics in the small-anisotropy limit fixes the relaxation rate Gamma = 2/tau_pi, where tau_pi = (5/4)(eta/P_eq), yielding Gamma = 2T/(5 eta_bar) with eta_bar = eta/S_eq.

## Context
This work extends the framework of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (Israel 1979, Muronga 2003, Baier 2006, Romatschke 2007) by addressing the failure of linearization around isotropic backgrounds when momentum-space anisotropies become large, as shown by Martinez (2009). The approach enables direct matching with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions (Lappi 2006, Fukushima 2007) and resolves the unphysical behavior of standard viscous hydrodynamics in the large shear viscosity limit. Future work can connect anisotropic hydrodynamics to AdS/CFT studies of anisotropic systems (Mateos 2011, Chernicoff 2012, Heller 2011).
