# Structure of Strange Dwarfs with Color Superconducting Core

> We study effects of two-flavor color superconductivity on the structure of strange dwarfs, which are stellar objects with similar masses and radii with ordinary white dwarfs but stabilized by the strange quark matter core. We find that unpaired quark matter is a good approximation to the core of strange dwarfs.

## Key claim
The authors find that two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) has a negligible effect on the mass-radius relation of strange dwarfs, except for a slight increase in the minimum mass, meaning unpaired quark matter is a good approximation for modeling the core of these objects.

## Method
The authors solve the general relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for strange dwarfs composed of a strange quark matter core and a nuclear crust, using the MIT bag model for the quark equation of state (EOS) and the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) EOS for the crust. Color superconductivity is incorporated as a chemical-potential-dependent effective bag constant, with the pairing gap calculated from the gap equation using a one-gluon-exchange interaction. The TOV equation is integrated with a fixed crust pressure corresponding to the nucleon drip density and a wide range of central pressures.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon drip density is set to \(\epsilon_\mathrm{drip} = 4.3 \times 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\).
- Quark masses are \(m_u = m_d = 10\) MeV and \(m_s = 150\) MeV; the bag constant is \(B^{1/4} = 160\) MeV.
- Strange dwarfs in the third region have radii around \(R \sim 10^4\) km up to the maximum mass, with masses around \(M/M_\mathrm{sun} \sim 10^{-2}\) in the second region.
- A strange dwarf with \(M/M_\mathrm{sun} = 0.465\) is compared to a white dwarf with \(M/M_\mathrm{sun} = 0.466\) to show the former is more compact.

## Context
This work extends the study of strange dwarfs proposed by Glendenning et al. (1990s) and the identification of eight candidates by Mathews et al. (2006). It examines the effect of two-flavor color superconductivity, which was previously studied for strange stars with the color-flavor locking phase by Lugones and Horvath (2002). The results support the conjecture of Mathews et al. (2006) that unpaired quark matter is sufficient for modeling strange dwarf cores, and suggest that future work could explore cooling effects or color-flavor locked cores at higher densities.
