# The three-pion $K$-matrix at NLO in ChPT

> The three-particle $K$-matrix, $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$, is a scheme-dependent quantity that parametrizes short-range three-particle interactions in the relativistic-field-theory three-particle finite-volume formalism. In this work, we compute its value for systems of three pions in all isospin channels through next-to-leading order in Chiral Perturbation Theory, generalizing previous work done at maximum isospin. We obtain analytic expressions through quadratic order (or cubic order, in the case of zero isospin) in the expansion about the three-pion threshold.

## Key claim

The three-pion K-matrix, $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$, is computed through next-to-leading order (NLO) in Chiral Perturbation Theory for all isospin channels, generalizing previous work that was restricted to maximum isospin; the results are obtained as analytic expressions expanded about the three-pion threshold up to quadratic order (or cubic order for zero isospin), with the caveat that the K-matrix is a scheme-dependent quantity.

## Method

The authors compute the three-particle K-matrix $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ for three-pion systems using Chiral Perturbation Theory at next-to-leading order, extending the earlier leading-order calculation at maximum isospin. They evaluate the NLO contributions numerically at a pion mass of $M_\pi = 340$ MeV and also provide analytic threshold expansions. The results are decomposed into partial waves of the interacting pair and separated into one-pion-exchange (OPE), bull's head (BH), and non-OPE contributions, with uncertainties estimated from the low-energy constants (LECs).

## Key numbers

- The physical point for the ratio $(M_\pi/F_\pi)^4$ is approximately 5.25, shown as a vertical line in the results figures.
- Numerical evaluations are performed at $M_\pi = 340$ MeV for the convergence plots and partial-wave decompositions.
- The threshold expansion is computed through quadratic order for most isospin channels, and through cubic order for the isospin-0 channel.
- The K-matrix components are plotted over a wide range of $(M_\pi/F_\pi)^4$ from 0 to approximately 170, with the zoomed region covering 0 to 16 shown in gray boxes.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ for three pions at maximum isospin by Baeza-Ballesteros et al. (2023) to all isospin channels at next-to-leading order in Chiral Perturbation Theory. The results provide the necessary input for the relativistic-field-theory three-particle finite-volume formalism, enabling lattice QCD calculations of three-pion scattering and resonance properties. The analytic threshold expansions and partial-wave decompositions facilitate comparisons with future lattice data and phenomenological studies of three-pion systems.
