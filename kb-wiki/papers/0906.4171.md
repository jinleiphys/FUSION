# $Ď€^0Îł$ invariant mass distribution in the low energy $Îłp \to Ď‰p$ reaction

> We study the reaction mechanism for the correlated $Ď€^0Îł$ emission in the photon induced reaction on the proton target. Since this reaction is studied in the GeV region, we assume that it proceeds through the formation of the vector meson in the intermediate state. The vector meson, being an unstable particle, decays into $Ď€^0Îł$ bosons after propagating certain distance. Our analysis shows that the $Ď€^0Îł$ event seen in coincidence in the final state dominantly arises due to the decay of $Ď‰$ meson. ## Key claim

The paper argues that the measured $\pi^0\gamma$ invariant mass distribution in the $\gamma p \to \omega p$ reaction at low energy (GeV region) is dominated by the decay of the $\omega$ meson produced in the intermediate state, but only after accounting for the detector resolution (55 MeV FWHM) and the wide energy spread of the bremsstrahlung photon beam (0.64â€“2.53 GeV), which together broaden the intrinsic narrow $\omega$ peak.

## Method

The reaction is modeled as a three-step process: $\gamma p \to V p$ (formation of vector mesons $\rho^0$, $\omega$, $\phi$), propagation of the vector meson, and its decay into $\pi^0\gamma$. The differential cross section $d\sigma/dm$ for the $\pi^0\gamma$ invariant mass is computed using the $\omega$ meson propagator, the forward amplitude $f_{\gamma p \to \omega p}(0)$ extracted from SAPHIR data on $d\sigma(\gamma p \to \omega p)/dq^2$, and the $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ decay width. The calculation is then folded with the beam profile function $W(E_\gamma) \propto 1/E_\gamma$ over $E_\gamma = 0.64$â€“$2.53$ GeV and convolved with a Gaussian detector resolution function of 55 MeV FWHM to match the experimental conditions at ELSA.

## Key numbers

- Measured total cross sections at $E_\gamma = 1.5$ GeV: $\sigma_t(\gamma p \to \rho^0 p) \simeq 23\ \mu$b, $\sigma_t(\gamma p \to \omega p) \simeq 6.51\ \mu$b, $\sigma_t(\gamma p \to \phi p) \simeq 0.17\ \mu$b.
- Resonance masses and total widths: $m_{\rho^0} \simeq 768$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\rho^0} \simeq 151$ MeV; $m_\omega \simeq 782$ MeV, $\Gamma_\omega \simeq 8.43$ MeV; $m_\phi \simeq 1020$ MeV, $\Gamma_\phi \simeq 4.43$ MeV.
- Decay widths for $\pi^0\gamma$ branch: $\Gamma_{\rho^0 \to \pi^0\gamma} \approx 0.12$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\omega \to \pi^0\gamma} \approx 0.72$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\phi \to \pi^0\gamma} \approx 0.006$ MeV.
- The $\omega$ meson contribution to the $\pi^0\gamma$ emission is estimated to be a factor of about 521 times larger than that from the $\rho^0$ meson at $E_\gamma = 1.5$ GeV, and about $10^3$ times larger at $E_\gamma = 1.2$ GeV.
- The detector resolution FWHM is 55 MeV, about 6.5 times larger than the free $\omega$ meson width of 8.43 MeV.
- The $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ coupling constant $f_{\omega\pi\gamma} = 0.095$, extracted from the $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$ width.
- The $\omega$ meson mass distribution spectra are calculated for four momentum bins: $0.2 < k_\omega < 0.4$ GeV/c, $0.4 < k_\omega < 0.6$ GeV/c, $0.6 < k_\omega < 1$ GeV/c, and $1 < k_\omega < 1.4$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of vector meson production in photon-induced reactions, specifically addressing the $\pi^0\gamma$ invariant mass measurement by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration at ELSA (2009). The study builds on prior measurements of $\gamma p \to \omega p$ cross sections by SAPHIR (Bar1) and four-momentum transfer distributions by Shmt and Stk. It enables a quantitative understanding of the $\omega$ meson's contribution to the $\pi^0\gamma$ channel in the presence of experimental smearing, and provides a framework for future studies of vector meson properties in nuclear media, such as the medium modification of the $\omega$ meson in Nb nuclei.
