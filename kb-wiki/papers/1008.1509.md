# Deformed Pseudospin Doublets as a Fingerprint of a Relativistic Supersymmetry in Nuclei

> The single-particle spectrum of deformed shell-model states in nuclei, is shown to exhibit a supersymmetric pattern. The latter involves deformed pseudospin doublets and intruder levels. The underlying supersymmetry is associated with the relativistic pseudospin symmetry of the nuclear mean-field Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector potentials.

## Key claim

The single-particle spectrum of deformed shell-model states in nuclei exhibits a supersymmetric pattern, consisting of twin towers of pairwise degenerate deformed pseudospin doublets and an additional non-degenerate nodeless intruder state, which arises from the relativistic pseudospin symmetry of the nuclear mean-field Dirac Hamiltonian when the sum of the scalar and vector potentials is a constant.

## Method

The author analyzes the Dirac Hamiltonian for a nucleon moving in axially-symmetric scalar and vector potentials, imposing the pseudospin symmetry condition \(V_S + V_V = \Delta_0\). The wave functions of the resulting degenerate pseudospin doublets are derived, and the conditions for nodeless bound states (intruder levels) are identified. The supersymmetric structure is constructed by defining conserved, anticommuting operators \(\hat{R}\) and \(\hat{B}\) from the pseudospin generators and the Dirac Hamiltonian, which generate the SUSY algebra and produce the observed spectral pattern.

## Key numbers

- Typical values for relativistic mean-field potentials in nuclei: \(V_S(0) \sim -400\) MeV and \(V_V(0) \sim 350\) MeV.
- The pseudospin symmetry condition is \(V_S(\rho,z) + V_V(\rho,z) = \Delta_0\), where \(\Delta_0\) is a constant.
- The supersymmetric Hamiltonian is \({\cal H} = (M + \Delta_0 - H)^2\), where \(H\) is the pseudospin-invariant Dirac Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous finding of a supersymmetric pattern in spherical shell-model states (Leviatan, 2004) to the case of axially-deformed potentials. It builds on the identification of pseudospin symmetry as a relativistic SU(2) symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian (Ginocchio, 1997; Ginocchio and Leviatan, 1998) and its connection to pseudospin doublets in deformed nuclei (Bohr, 1982). The results enable a deeper understanding of the origin of pseudospin doublets and intruder levels in deformed nuclei, and the framework can be extended to identify additional supersymmetries of Dirac Hamiltonians with mixed Lorentz structure.
