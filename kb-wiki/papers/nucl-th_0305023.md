# Magnetic moments of the N(1535) resonance in the chiral unitary model

> We calculate the magnetic moments of the N(1535) resonance using the chiral unitary model, where the resonance is dynamically generated in the scatterings of the lowest-lying mesons and baryons. We obtain the magnetic moments of the resonance as +1.1 and -0.25 for p(1535) and n(1535), respectively, in units of the nuclear magneton. We discuss the origin of these numbers within the chiral unitary model, then we compare the present results with those of the quark model and the chiral doublet model. The possibility to observe the magnetic moments in experiments is also investigated.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of the N(1535) resonance are calculated as +1.1 and -0.25 nuclear magnetons for the proton-like and neutron-like charge states, respectively, under the assumption that the resonance is a dynamically generated object from meson-baryon scattering in the chiral unitary model, rather than a conventional three-quark state.

## Method

The authors used the chiral unitary model, where the N(1535) resonance emerges as a pole in the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering of the lowest-lying octet mesons and baryons, with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the driving term. They computed the photon-resonance coupling by gauging the effective chiral Lagrangian and evaluating the resulting loop diagrams, extracting the magnetic moment both on the real axis (using the K-Sigma channel, which has the largest coupling) and in the complex plane at the resonance pole to eliminate background contributions.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic moment of the neutral N(1535) resonance (n*): -0.25 nuclear magnetons, combining complex-plane result |mu| = 0.248 mu_N and real-axis result -0.266 +/- 0.01 mu_N.
* Magnetic moment of the charged N(1535) resonance (p*): +1.1 nuclear magnetons, combining complex-plane result |mu| = 1.13 mu_N and real-axis result 1.26 +/- 0.02 mu_N.
* Resonance pole positions: z_n* = 1536.01 - 37.06i MeV (Q=0) and z_p* = 1531.01 - 36.38i MeV (Q=1), giving masses and half-widths.
* Coupling strengths to the K-Sigma channel: |g_KSigma|^2 = 7.29 (n*) and 7.37 (p*), the largest among all channels.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1670) by Jido et al. (2002) to the N(1535) resonance, providing an alternative to the quark model calculation by Chiang et al. (2002) and the chiral doublet model. The results enable experimental searches for the N(1535) magnetic moment via bremsstrahlung processes such as gamma p -> gamma eta p or pi- p -> gamma eta n, and test the SU(3) octet relation mu_n* = 2 mu_Lambda* against the Lambda(1670) value of -0.29 mu_N from the same framework.
