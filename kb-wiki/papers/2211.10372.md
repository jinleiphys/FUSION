# Out-of-equilibrium photon production and electric conductivity in a holographic Bjorken expanding plasma

> We analytically compute the out-of-equilibrium direct photon production rate and electric conductivity, in a strongly coupled and expanding Bjorken plasma, from holography. Our results are valid at late times where the expanding plasma asymptotes Bjorken hydrodynamics. The out-of-equilibrium rates are substantially harder and larger, early on in the Bjorken expansion phase.

## Key claim

The out-of-equilibrium direct photon production rate and electric conductivity in a strongly coupled Bjorken expanding plasma are computed analytically from holography, with the results valid only at late times where the expanding plasma asymptotes Bjorken hydrodynamics; the out-of-equilibrium rates are substantially harder and larger early in the Bjorken expansion phase compared to the equilibrium rates.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a strongly coupled SU(Nc) N=4 super Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature and zero density, coupling a U(1) gauge field via a Maxwell term to gravity. They work in the holographic dual of a falling black hole geometry that maps to a static black hole frame, solving the equation of motion for transverse U(1) gauge field fluctuations analytically in terms of hypergeometric functions. The retarded Green's function is extracted from the on-shell boundary action, and the result is transformed from the static black hole frame to the physical Bjorken frame using the proper time dependent temperature T(tau) = T0/(tau/tau0)^(1/3).

## Key numbers

* The electric conductivity at small frequencies (omega << T0_tilde) is given by sigma = (2 pi R / 3) T0_tilde + (1/3) i R log(2) omega + (R pi omega^2)/(36 T0_tilde) + O(omega^5), where T0_tilde = T0 tau0/t0 and R is the AdS radius.
* The electric conductivity at large frequencies (omega >> T0_tilde) is sigma = -(16 i pi^4 R T0_tilde^4)/(45 omega^3) + (2/3) i R omega (-log(pi T0_tilde) + log(omega) + gamma - i pi/2 - log(2)) + O(1/omega^6).

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium photon and dilepton production calculations for N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma by Caron-Huot (2006) to the out-of-equilibrium regime of a Bjorken expanding plasma. It contrasts with the out-of-equilibrium photon production studied by Baier (2012) using a holographic falling shell model, where the background metric is not explicitly time dependent. The results enable quantitative assessment of non-equilibrium effects on electromagnetic emissivities in strongly coupled plasmas, with potential relevance to photon rates measured at collider energies, particularly addressing the deficit in the low mass region noted by Dusling (2009) when using weakly coupled plasma rates.
