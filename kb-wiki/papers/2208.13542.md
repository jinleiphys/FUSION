# Status of the hyperon-nucleon interaction in chiral effective field theory

> The Jülich-Bonn group aims at an extensive study of the baryon-baryon ($BB$) interaction involving strange baryons ($Λ$, $Σ$, $Ξ$) within SU(3) chiral effective field theory. An overview of achievements and new developments over the past few years is provided. The topics covered are: 1) Derivation of the leading charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction for the $ΛN$ system and its application in a study of CSB effects in $A$=$4$ $Λ$-hypernuclei. 2) Updated results for the $ΞN$ interaction at NLO and predictions for $Ξ^- p$ correlation functions. 3) Extension of the $ΛN$-$ΣN$ interaction to next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The Jülich-Bonn group has extended chiral effective field theory for hyperon-nucleon interactions to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) for the ΛN-ΣN system, but the new data from J-PARC at momenta around 500 MeV/c may already be in a region where even N2LO cannot be expected to be quantitatively reliable, and the ΛNπ channel opens around that energy.

## Method

The authors construct baryon-baryon potentials using SU(3) chiral effective field theory with a semilocal momentum-space regularization scheme, where a local regulator is applied to pion-exchange contributions and a non-local regulator to contact terms. For the ΛN-ΣN interaction at N2LO, they fit S-wave low-energy constants to 36 low-energy scattering data points for Λp, Σ-p, and Σ+p, then fit P-wave LECs to differential cross sections from the E40 Collaboration at J-PARC. For charge-symmetry breaking in A=4 Λ-hypernuclei, they constrain the Λ-neutron interaction using the experimentally known difference of Λ separation energies in mirror nuclei. For the ΞN interaction, they use constraints from the ΛΛ scattering length and experimental upper bounds on ΞN cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The fit to 36 low-energy YN data points yields a χ2 of approximately 16 for the N2LO interaction
- For charge-symmetry breaking in A=4 hypernuclei, the experimental values are ΔE(0+) = 233 ± 92 keV and ΔE(1+) = -83 ± 94 keV
- The predicted CSB scattering length difference ΔaCSB = aΛp - aΛn is 0.62 ± 0.08 fm for the 1S0 partial wave and -0.10 ± 0.02 fm for 3S1
- For the NLO19 potential with cutoff 550 MeV, the Λp singlet scattering length is -2.640 fm and the Λn singlet scattering length is -3.205 fm
- The resulting level splittings for NLO19(550) are ΔE(0+) = 252 keV and ΔE(1+) = -72 keV
- The Ξ-p correlation function predictions use source radii of 1.43 fm for 5.02 TeV and 1.18 fm for 13 TeV from ALICE pp fits

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn group's previous NLO hyperon-nucleon potentials (YN2013, YN2019) to N2LO for the ΛN-ΣN system, motivated by new differential cross section data from the E40 Collaboration at J-PARC (Miwa 2021, Nanamura 2022). The charge-symmetry breaking study builds on the NLO13 and NLO19 potentials to constrain the Λ-neutron interaction using recent measurements of 4ΛH at Mainz (Schulz 2016) and 4ΛHe at J-PARC (Yamamoto 2015). The ΞN interaction work extends the 2015 and 2019 potentials (YY2015, YY2019) and provides predictions for Ξ-p correlation functions that can be tested against ALICE data from p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and pp collisions at 13 TeV. The results enable future studies of CSB in A=7,8 Λ-hypernuclei and further tests of Ξ-nuclear interactions.
