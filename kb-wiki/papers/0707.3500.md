# Coulomb tunneling for fusion reactions in dense matter: Path integral Monte Carlo versus mean field

> We compare Path Integral Monte Carlo calculations by Militzer and Pollock (Phys. Rev. B 71, 134303, 2005) of Coulomb tunneling in nuclear reactions in dense matter to semiclassical calculations assuming WKB Coulomb barrier penetration through the radial mean-field potential. We find a very good agreement of two approaches at temperatures higher than ~1/5 of the ion plasma temperature. We obtain a simple parameterization of the mean field potential and of the respective reaction rates. We analyze Gamow-peak energies of reacting ions in various reaction regimes and discuss theoretical uncertainties of nuclear reaction rates taking carbon burning in dense stellar matter as an example.

# Coulomb tunneling for fusion reactions in dense matter: Path integral Monte Carlo versus mean field

## Key claim

The Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) calculations of Coulomb tunneling by Militzer and Pollock (2005) agree very well with a much simpler semiclassical mean-field WKB approximation at temperatures higher than approximately one-fifth of the ion plasma temperature, but the agreement degrades at lower temperatures where pycnonuclear effects become dominant.

## Method

The authors compared PIMC contact probabilities g(0) from Militzer and Pollock (2005) for 36 plasma parameter points (Γ from 0.5 to 600, η from 0.1 to 2) against contact probabilities calculated using a WKB Coulomb barrier penetration through a radial mean-field potential H(r) derived from classical Monte Carlo simulations of a one-component plasma. They parameterized the mean-field potential u(x) = H(r)/(Z²e²/a) for Γ values from 1 to 800 and computed the resulting reaction rates, then analyzed the Gamow-peak energies and theoretical uncertainties using carbon burning as an example.

## Key numbers

- Agreement between PIMC and mean-field WKB is good for T > ~(1/5)Tp, where Tp is the ion plasma temperature defined as Tp = ħωp/kB
- The function h₀(Γ) for strong screening is given by h₀(Γ) ≈ 1.0563 Γ + 1.0208 Γ^0.3231 − 0.2748 ln Γ − 1.0843 for 1 ≤ Γ ≤ 170 (from DeWitt and Slattery 1999)
- The leading correction term h₁(Γ,ζ) = −(5/32) Γ ζ² from Jancovici (1977) applies for ζ ≲ 1 (T/Tp ≳ 0.37)
- Alastuey and Jancovici (1978) extended h₁ to h₁ = −(5/32) Γ ζ² + 0.014 Γ ζ³ + 0.0128 Γ ζ⁴ for ζ ≤ 1.6 (T/Tp > 0.18)
- The zero-temperature pycnonuclear regime occurs at T ≲ Tq, where Tq = 0.5 Tp/ln(Tl/Tp)
- Carbon burning times of 1 s and 10¹⁰ years define the astrophysically relevant region in the T-ρ diagram

## Context

This work extends the comparison between PIMC calculations by Militzer and Pollock (2005) and earlier mean-field approaches by Jancovici (1977), Alastuey and Jancovici (1978), Ogata et al. (1991, 1993), and Ogata (1997). It validates the simpler mean-field WKB method for temperatures above ~0.2 Tp, which covers the thermonuclear regime with strong screening and part of the thermo-pycnonuclear regime. The results enable improved analytic fits for nuclear reaction rates in dense stellar matter, particularly for carbon burning in white dwarfs and neutron star envelopes, and clarify the theoretical uncertainties in the function h₁(Γ,ζ) that were previously debated.
