# Second-order dissipative hydrodynamics for plasma with chiral asymmetry and vorticity

> By making use of the chiral kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation, we derive an Israel-Stewart type formulation of the hydrodynamic equations for a chiral relativistic plasma made of neutral particles (e.g., neutrinos). The effects of chiral asymmetry are captured by including an additional continuity equation for the axial charge, as well as the leading-order quantum corrections due to the spin of particles. In a formulation of the chiral kinetic theory used, we introduce a symmetric form of the energy-momentum tensor that is suitable for the description of a weakly nonuniform chiral plasma. By construction, the energy and momentum are conserved to the same leading order in the Planck constant as the kinetic equation itself. By making use of such a chiral kinetic theory and the Chapman-Enskog approach, we obtain a set of second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations. The effects of the fluid vorticity and velocity fluctuations on the dispersion relations of chiral vortical waves are analyzed.

## Key claim

The authors derive a second-order Israel-Stewart type formulation of dissipative hydrodynamic equations for a chiral relativistic plasma of neutral particles (e.g., neutrinos) using chiral kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation, but they explicitly note that this approximation may conflict with Lorentz covariance and that a consistent collision term would necessarily be nonlocal.

## Method

Starting from the chiral kinetic theory with a relaxation-time collision term, the authors introduce a symmetric energy-momentum tensor suitable for weakly nonuniform chiral plasma and use the Chapman-Enskog method with an iterative solution of the Boltzmann equation to derive evolution equations for dissipative functions. The analysis includes the effects of chiral asymmetry through an additional continuity equation for axial charge and leading-order quantum corrections from particle spin, with the fluid vorticity incorporated into the equilibrium distribution function via a spin-dependent dispersion relation. The resulting hydrodynamic equations are truncated following an approach similar to that in Denicol (2012), Jaiswal (2013), and Jaiswal (2015), but with special attention to chiral asymmetry and vorticity effects.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium fermion-number density: \( n_{\text{eq}} = \frac{\mu(\mu^2 + 3\mu_5^2 + \pi^2 T^2)}{3\pi^2} \)
- Equilibrium axial-charge density: \( n_{5,\text{eq}} = \frac{\mu_5(\mu_5^2 + 3\mu^2 + \pi^2 T^2)}{3\pi^2} \)
- Equilibrium energy density: \( \epsilon_{\text{eq}} = \frac{\mu^4 + 6\mu^2\mu_5^2 + \mu_5^4}{4\pi^2} + \frac{T^2}{2}(\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + \frac{7\pi^2 T^4}{60} \)
- Equilibrium chiral vortical current: \( \nu_{\text{eq}}^\mu = \frac{\mu\mu_5}{\pi^2} \hbar \omega^\mu \)
- Equilibrium axial vortical current: \( \nu_{5,\text{eq}}^\mu = \frac{3(\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + \pi^2 T^2}{6\pi^2} \hbar \omega^\mu \)
- Equilibrium momentum density: \( h_{\text{eq}}^\mu = \frac{\mu_5(\mu_5^2 + 3\mu^2 + \pi^2 T^2)}{3\pi^2} \hbar \omega^\mu \)
- Equation of state for the relativistic plasma: \( P = \epsilon/3 \)

## Context

This work extends the second-order dissipative hydrodynamics framework of Israel (1976, 1979) and the moment expansion methods of Denicol (2012) and Jaiswal (2013, 2015) to include chiral asymmetry and fluid vorticity effects in a relativistic plasma. The derivation uses the chiral kinetic theory developed by Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), and Chen (2014), and the resulting equations enable analysis of chiral vortical wave dispersion relations including the effects of fluid vorticity and velocity fluctuations, with potential applications to heavy-ion collisions and primordial magnetic field generation in cosmology.
