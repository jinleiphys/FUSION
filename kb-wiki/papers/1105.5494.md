# Determination of the pi Sigma scattering lengths from the weak decays of Lambda_c

> The scattering lengths of the pi Sigma systems are key quantities in understanding the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance and the subthreshold extrapolation of the barK N interaction. We demonstrate that the pi Sigma scattering lengths can be extracted from the threshold cusp phenomena in the weak Lambda_c -> pi pi Sigma decays, analogously with Cabibbo's method for determination of the pi pi scattering length. We show that the substantial cusp effect should be observed in the spectrum, when the pi Sigma interaction in I=0 is strongly attractive to generate a near-threshold singularity, such as a bound state or a virtual state.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the pi Sigma scattering lengths can be extracted from threshold cusp effects in the weak decays of Lambda_c, but this extraction is conditional on the pi Sigma interaction in isospin I=0 being strongly attractive enough to generate a near-threshold singularity such as a bound state or virtual state; without such a singularity, the cusp effect may not be substantial enough to observe.

## Method

The authors analyze the three-body weak decay Lambda_c -> pi pi Sigma, focusing on the invariant mass spectrum of a pi Sigma pair near the threshold of a higher-energy charge combination of pi Sigma. They expand the decay amplitude in powers of the dimensionless momentum variable delta around the higher-energy threshold, relating the linear coefficient B to the off-diagonal pi Sigma scattering length a_{h->l}. The method is analogous to Cabibbo's approach for extracting the pi pi scattering length from K+ -> pi+ pi0 pi0 decays, and requires fitting the experimental spectrum below and above the threshold to extract the coefficients A, B, and C.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratios for the relevant Lambda_c decay modes are: for mode a^{-+}, Lambda_c -> pi+(pi+Sigma-) at 1.7 +/- 0.5% and Lambda_c -> pi+(pi-Sigma+) at 3.6 +/- 1.0%; for mode a^{00}, Lambda_c -> pi+(pi+Sigma-) at 1.7 +/- 0.5% and Lambda_c -> pi+(pi0Sigma0) at 1.8 +/- 0.8%; for mode a^{0+}, Lambda_c -> pi0(pi+Sigma0) at 1.8 +/- 0.8% and the lower-energy channel branching ratio is not known.
- The leading-order chiral perturbation theory predictions for the scattering lengths are: a^{-+} = 0.00 fm, a^{00} = 0.23 fm, and a^{0+} = -0.23 fm.
- The relevant energy region for the cusp analysis is |W - W_th| less than about 10 MeV, where the dimensionless variable delta satisfies |delta| less than about 0.37, giving O(delta^3) errors at the 5% level.
- The pion decay constant used is f = 92.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends Cabibbo's method (Cabibbo, 2004, 2005) for determining the pi pi scattering length from threshold cusps in kaon decays to the pi Sigma system using Lambda_c weak decays. The pi Sigma scattering lengths are key to understanding the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance and the subthreshold extrapolation of the barK N interaction, which is relevant for studies of kaonic nuclei at facilities such as J-PARC E15, FOPI at GSI, and AMADEUS at DAPhiNE. The paper enables experimental extraction of these scattering lengths at Belle and Babar, which would constrain models of the coupled-channels barK N-pi Sigma interaction and help resolve the two-pole structure of Lambda(1405) identified in chiral unitary models (Jido, 2003).
