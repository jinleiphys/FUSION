# Viscosity of an ideal relativistic quantum fluid: A perturbative study

> We show that a quantized ideal fluid will generally exhibit a small but non-zero viscosity due to the backreaction of quantum soundwaves on the background. We use an effective field theory expansion to estimate this viscosity to first order in perturbation theory. We discuss our results, and whether this estimate can be used to obtain a more model-independent estimate of the "quantum bound" on the viscosity of physical systems

# Viscosity of an ideal relativistic quantum fluid: A perturbative study

## Key claim
A quantized ideal fluid will exhibit a small but non-zero viscosity due to the backreaction of quantum soundwaves on the background, estimated to first order in perturbation theory. The result suggests that a finite "quantum bound" on the dimensionless ratio eta/s may exist, but only if the divergences in the vortex speed c_T and the degeneracy factor g are correlated such that c_T^7 g^4 remains of order unity.

## Method
The author starts from the Lagrangian formulation of an ideal relativistic quantum fluid, where the degrees of freedom are the spatial coordinates of comoving fluid volume elements, and expands around a hydrostatic background to obtain a linearized Lagrangian for longitudinal soundwaves and transverse vortices. Using the equivalence between the Kubo formula and the Boltzmann equation at leading order, the shear viscosity is computed from the mean free path and interaction cross-sections of a thermalized gas of soundwaves and vortices, with scattering matrix elements taken from prior work by the same author. The entropy density of the gas is calculated from the free Bose-Einstein distribution for massless bosons with two different propagation speeds (c_s for soundwaves and c_T for vortices), and the ratio eta/s is evaluated analytically in the limit Lambda/T going to infinity.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity formula for the gas of soundwaves and vortices is eta = (1/3) * (ave{p_L} + ave{p_T}) * (ave{n_L} + ave{n_T}) / ave{sigma}, where ave{sigma} includes thermal averages of scattering processes LL, LT, and TT.
- The cross-section kernel is Phi(p,w_0) = (1/p^2) * (p^4 / w_0)^2, where w_0 is the microscopic enthalpy density.
- The prefactor for TT scattering is alpha_{TT} = (1/(256 pi)) * (13/15) * (1/c_T^2), showing a divergence as c_T goes to 0.
- The thermal average of the kernel yields dave{Phi} = volfact^2 * 80640 * H(c_1,c_2) * T^12 / w_0^2, with H(c_1,c_2) involving zeta(3), zeta(5), zeta(7), and zeta(9).
- The number density for a given propagation speed c is ave{n}_c = volfact * (2/c^2) * zeta(3) * T^3.
- The entropy density for a given speed c is s_c = volfact * (4 pi^4 / (45 c^3)) * T^3.
- The final expression for eta/s is eta/s = K_0 * (c_T^14 * g^8) / (B^2 * (dF/dB)^6), where K_0 is approximately 1.96 * 10^(-9).
- The convergence condition for eta/s is c_T^7 * g^4 ~ order(1).

## Context
This work extends the quantum hydrodynamics program of the author's prior papers (Torrieri, 2011) and addresses open questions raised in those works about the stability and dissipative corrections of quantized ideal fluids. It also builds on the observation by Moore and Sohrabi (2011) that the low-viscosity limit is non-trivial due to hydrodynamic fluctuations, where the bare viscosity must be renormalized by soundwave backreaction. The calculation provides a perturbative estimate of the "quantum bound" on eta/s, which has been previously estimated using non-rigorous Boltzmann equation arguments (Danielewicz and Gyulassy, 1985) or holographic supergravity duals (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). The result suggests that a finite lower limit for eta/s may exist even in the ideal fluid limit, but the numerical value (approximately 10^(-9) times a function of the equation of state) is orders of magnitude smaller than previous estimates.
