# Coulomb Excitation of Multi-Phonon Levels of the Giant Dipole Resonance

> A closed expression is obtained for the cross-section for Coulomb excitation of levels of the giant dipole resonance of given angular momentum and phonon number. Applications are made to the Goldhaber-Teller and Steinwedel-Jensen descriptions of the resonance, at non-relativistic and relativistic bombarding energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed analytic expression for the Coulomb excitation cross-section of giant dipole resonance (GDR) states with specified angular momentum and phonon number, decomposing the previously known total excitation probability into contributions from individual angular-momentum channels, but the authors caution that this highly simplified pure-oscillator model with linear coupling is intended only to indicate general trends rather than to reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the GDR as an isotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator and approximate the electromagnetic coupling as linear in the oscillator coordinates. They solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation exactly in a Cartesian basis, obtaining Poisson-distributed occupation amplitudes, then transform to a spherical basis using a polynomial identity involving solid harmonics to extract excitation probabilities for states of given phonon number \(N\) and angular momentum \(\ell\). The formalism is applied to both the Goldhaber-Teller (bulk relative oscillation) and Steinwedel-Jensen (local isovector fluctuation) macroscopic models, at relativistic bombarding energies (straight-line trajectory, Lienard-Wiechert potentials) and non-relativistic bombarding energies (hyperbolic trajectory, Alder-Winther formalism).

## Key numbers

* For the Goldhaber-Teller model, the characteristic GDR oscillation energy is approximately \(\hbar\omega \simeq 79 A^{-1/3}\) MeV, leading to an oscillator parameter \(\nu \simeq 1.91 \frac{NZ}{A^{4/3}}\) fm\(^{-2}\).
* For a \(^{40}\)Ca target, the amplitude of the proton mass-center oscillation is \(f/\sqrt{\nu} \simeq 0.212\) fm, small compared to the nuclear surface thickness of approximately 1 fm.
* For relativistic \(^{208}\)Pb projectiles on a \(^{40}\)Ca target, the straight-line trajectory approximation is valid for projectile kinetic energies above about 54 MeV per nucleon, where the deflection angle is less than \(1^\circ\).
* The relativistic and non-relativistic calculations for one-phonon GDR excitation in \(^{40}\)Ca agree at approximately 50 MeV per nucleon bombarding energy.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical Coulomb excitation formalism of Alder and Winther (1975) and Winther and Alder (1979) by providing angular-momentum-resolved excitation probabilities for multiphonon GDR states, whereas previous published formulae gave only the total excitation probability summed over all angular momenta for a given phonon number. The authors note that more realistic calculations exist (e.g., those by L'Allier et al., Carr et al., Bortignon et al., and Abe et al.) that have proven useful in analyzing a wide variety of Coulomb excitation experiments, but argue that their simplified model may indicate general trends in the variation of cross-section with bombarding energy and final-state angular momentum. The work enables future interpretation of angular distributions of GDR decay products (e.g., gamma rays from two-phonon states) to extract information about the angular momentum of excited states.
