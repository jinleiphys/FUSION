# Systematic investigation of the Hoyle-analog states in light nuclei

> We investigate resonance states in three-cluster continuum of some light nuclei $^{9}$Be, $^{9}$B, $^{10}$B, $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C. These nuclei are considered to have a three-cluster configuration consisting of two alpha-particles and neutron, proton, deuteron, triton and nucleus $^{3}$He. In this study, we make use two different microscopic three-cluster models. The first model employs the Hyperspherical Harmonics basis to numerate channels and describe three-cluster continuum. The second model is the well-known complex scaling method. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is modeled by the semi-realistic Minnesota and Hasegawa-Nagata potentials. Our main aim is to find the Hoyle-analog states in these nuclei or, in other words, whether it is possible to synthesize these nuclei in a triple collision of clusters. We formulate the criteria for selecting such states and apply them to resonance states, emerged from our calculations. We found that there are resonance states obeying the formulated criteria which make possible syntheses of these nuclei in a stellar environment.

## Key claim

The authors systematically search for Hoyle-analog states, very narrow resonance states lying close to the three-cluster threshold with total orbital momentum \(L=0\), in the light nuclei \(^{9}\text{Be}\), \(^{9}\text{B}\), \(^{10}\text{B}\), \(^{11}\text{B}\), and \(^{11}\text{C}\), and claim that such states exist in each of these nuclei, enabling their synthesis via triple-cluster collisions in stellar environments, though the identification depends on the specific criteria (narrow width, proximity to threshold, and \(L=0\)) and on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential parameters.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary microscopic three-cluster models: the Algebraic Model of Scattering using Hyperspherical Harmonics Basis (AMHHB) and the Complex Scaling Method (CSM). Both models treat each nucleus as a system of two alpha particles plus a third cluster (neutron, proton, deuteron, triton, or \(^{3}\text{He}\)), using the semi-realistic Minnesota potential (MP) or modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential (MHNP) for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Resonance states were identified by solving the Schrödinger equation in a hyperspherical harmonics basis, extracting energies and widths from the \(S\)-matrix poles, and applying the criteria of narrow width, proximity to the three-cluster threshold, and total orbital momentum \(L=0\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\text{C}\) (Hoyle state): energy \(E = 0.3796 \pm 0.0002\) MeV above the three-alpha threshold (modern experimental value cited); width \(\Gamma = 2.33 \times 10^{-5}\) keV (calculated with MP, \(u=0.940\), \(b=1.285\) fm).
- For \(^{9}\text{Be}\) and \(^{9}\text{B}\): input parameters for MHNP are \(m=0.0332\), \(b=1.317\) fm; the \(0^{+}\) resonance in \(^{8}\text{Be}\) obtained with these parameters has \(E_R = 0.859\) MeV and \(\Gamma_R = 958.4\) keV.
- For \(^{10}\text{B}\): input parameters for MP are \(u=0.915\), \(b=1.395\) fm; the \(0^{+}\) resonance in \(^{8}\text{Be}\) has \(E_R = 0.426\) MeV and \(\Gamma_R = 69.0\) keV.
- For \(^{11}\text{B}\) and \(^{11}\text{C}\): input parameters for MP are \(u=0.920\), \(b=1.322\) fm; the \(0^{+}\) resonance in \(^{8}\text{Be}\) has \(E_R = 0.317\) MeV and \(\Gamma_R = 19.8\) keV.
- The hyperspherical harmonics basis used \(K_{\max}=14\) for positive parity states and \(K_{\max}=13\) for negative parity states, with hyperradial excitations up to 70.

## Context

This work extends prior studies on Hoyle-analog states in light nuclei, particularly the investigations of \(^{11}\text{B}\) and \(^{11}\text{C}\) using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) combined with the Gauss expansion method by Yamada et al. (2010, 2012) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) by Kanada-En'yo et al. (2007, 2011). The authors also build on their own earlier analyses of \(^{9}\text{B}\) and \(^{9}\text{Be}\) (Vasilevsky et al., 2017) and of \(^{11}\text{B}\) and \(^{11}\text{C}\) (Vasilevsky et al., 2013). The systematic application of both AMHHB and CSM across five nuclei enables a comparative assessment of Hoyle-analog candidates, and the results suggest that triple-cluster synthesis of these nuclei may be possible in stellar environments, motivating further experimental searches for the predicted narrow resonances.
