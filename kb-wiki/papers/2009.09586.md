# Collectivity in large and small systems formed in ultrarelativistic collisions

> Collective flow of the final-state hadrons observed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions or even in smaller systems formed in high-multiplicity pp and p/d/$^3$He-nucleus collisions is one of the most important diagnostic tools to probe the initial state of the system and to shed light on the properties of the short-lived, strongly-interacting many-body state formed in these collisions. Limited, in the initial years, to the study of mainly the directed and elliptic flows -- the first two Fourier harmonics of the single-particle azimuthal distribution -- this field has evolved in recent years into a much richer area of activity. This includes not only higher Fourier harmonics and multiparticle cumulants, but also a variety of other related observables, such as the ridge seen in two-particle correlations, flow decorrelation, symmetric cumulants and event-plane correlators which measure correlations between the magnitudes or phases of the complex flows in different harmonics, coefficients that measure the nonlinear hydrodynamic response, statistical properties, such as the non-Gaussianity of the flow fluctuations, etc. We present a Tutorial Review of the modern flow picture and the various aspects of the collectivity -- an emergent phenomenon in quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

This Tutorial Review argues that collective flow, originally established as a diagnostic tool in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, has evolved into a much richer framework that now also applies to smaller systems formed in high-multiplicity pp and p/d/³He-nucleus collisions, though the origin of collectivity in these smaller systems remains debated.

## Method

The paper presents a pedagogical review of the mathematical framework underlying collective flow, including the characterization of the initial-state geometry via complex eccentricity vectors \(\mathcal{E}_n\) and the final-state momentum anisotropy via Fourier flow coefficients \(V_n\). It details how multiparticle azimuthal correlations and cumulants (two-, four-, six-, and eight-particle) are used to extract flow harmonics \(v_n\{m\}\) while suppressing nonflow contributions, and discusses probability density functions of flow fluctuations, the ridge in two-particle correlations, flow decorrelation, and mixed-harmonic observables such as symmetric cumulants and event-plane correlators.

## Key numbers

- ALICE collaboration has presented results on flow harmonics up to \(n=9\) (Acharya 2020).
- ATLAS data on probability density functions \(P(v_2), P(v_3), P(v_4)\) were measured at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV for various centrality bins (Aad 2013).
- ALICE reported similar \(P(v_n)\) results at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV (Acharya 2018).
- CMS data show \(v_2\{2\} > v_2\{4\} \approx v_2\{6\} \approx v_2\{8\}\) in PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV (Sirunyan 2017).
- The quantity \(nc_3\{4\} \equiv -v_3\{4\}^4/v_3\{2\}^4\) is nonzero for triangular flow except in the most central collisions, indicating non-Gaussian fluctuations (Aaboud 2019).
- The relative flow fluctuation measure is defined as \(F(v_n) \equiv \sqrt{(v_n\{2\}^2 - v_n\{4\}^2)/(v_n\{2\}^2 + v_n\{4\}^2)} = \sigma_{v_n}/\langle v_n \rangle\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on anisotropic flow from Ollitrault (1992) and Voloshin (1994) and the multiparticle cumulant method of Borghini (2001). It builds on the modern flow picture established by Luzum (2011) and the characterization of initial-state eccentricities by Alver (2010) and Heinz (2013). The paper enables future high-statistics measurements of kurtosis of elliptic flow fluctuations to provide deeper insights into hydrodynamic behavior and the equation of state of quark-gluon plasma, and it leaves open the question of whether quark-gluon plasma is formed in high-multiplicity pp and p-nucleus collisions.
