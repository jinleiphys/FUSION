# Structure and Dynamics of Charmonium

> We apply a confining q\bar{q} potential to charmonium and open charm in order to model wave functions and to begin studying structure. Results (in momentum space) provide form-factor input to a four-flavor effective chiral Lagrangian which models dynamics of charmonium in hot hadronic matter. Estimates are made for J/ψdissociation cross sections and rates within a fireball. Our study attempts to improve on previous comover suppression models since it includes gauge-invariant form-factor formalism constrained by quark-model phenomenology.

# Structure and Dynamics of Charmonium

## Key claim
The paper provides improved estimates for J/ψ dissociation cross sections and rates in hot hadronic matter by using gauge-invariant form factors constrained by quark-model phenomenology with a confining potential, but the results are limited by the assumption that chiral symmetry remains fully symmetric even though the charmed quark mass breaks the symmetry to a deeper extent.

## Method
A confining q̄q potential with Coulomb-plus-linear central part and hyperfine interaction was solved numerically via the Schrödinger equation to obtain meson wave functions, masses, and rms radii for charmonium and open charm states. The resulting momentum-space form factors were used as input to a four-flavor effective chiral Lagrangian with monopole vertex form factors, and J/ψ dissociation cross sections and rates were calculated for pion- and rho-induced reactions in a fireball with and without radial flow.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.836 GeV
- Calculated J/ψ mass: 3101 MeV (observed: 3097 MeV); calculated η_c mass: 3005 MeV (observed: 2980 MeV)
- J/ψ rms radius: 0.40 fm; form-factor cutoff parameter Λ = 3.32 GeV
- Chiral coupling constant g = 4.3 gives Γ_ρ = 151 MeV at pole mass 770 MeV
- Charged D* decay width from chiral Lagrangian: 21.1 keV (experiment: 96 ± 4 ± 22 keV)
- J/ψ breakup cross sections range from a few tenths to a few millibarns
- At high J/ψ momentum (5 GeV/c) and high system temperature (200 MeV), dissociation rate of 10 MeV was found
- With radial flow velocity |v| = 0.6, dissociation rate increased by a factor of roughly 3

## Context
This work extends earlier comover suppression models by Haglin (2000) and Haglin and Gale (2001) by incorporating a confining potential from Roberts and Silvestre-Brac (1998) to constrain form factors with quark-model phenomenology, rather than using purely phenomenological form factors. The study builds on the Matsui and Satz (1986) proposal that charmonium breakup in quark-gluon plasma could signal deconfinement, and addresses the need to distinguish genuine QGP effects from ordinary hadronic comover absorption as studied by Matinyan and Müller (1998), Oh, Song and Lee (2001), and Wong, Swanson and Barnes (2002). The results enable more refined estimates of J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, with future work needed to fold in realistic p_T distributions for J/ψ.
