# Multipole Expansion of Bremsstrahlung in Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> Using a multipole expansion of the radiated field generated by a classical electric current, we present a way to interprete the bremsstrahlung spectra of low energy heavy ion collisions. We perform the calculation explicitely for the system ^{12}C+ ^{12}C at 84AMeV and compare the result with the experimental data of E. Grosse et al. Using simple model assumptions for the electromagnetic source current we are able to describe the measured data in terms of coherent photon emission. In this context, the information contained in the measured data is discussed.

# Multipole Expansion of Bremsstrahlung in Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The measured bremsstrahlung spectra for the system ¹²C+¹²C at 84AMeV can be described by completely coherent photon emission from correlated, collective motion of nucleons, rather than by incoherent emission from uncorrelated particles in a thermalized gas. However, the authors explicitly state that the fair agreement with data "is no proof for the correctness of the model."

## Method
The authors perform a multipole expansion of the electromagnetic field generated by a classical electric current, using vector spherical harmonics and outgoing spherical Bessel functions. They construct a model current for four colliding particles undergoing two subsequent two-body collisions, with a temporal delay between collision events, and compute the bremsstrahlung spectrum from the Fourier transform of this current. The spectrum is then averaged over impact parameters weighted by geometric cross section, with the charge participating in the collision obtained from a geometric overlap integral, and compared to experimental data from Grosse et al. (1985).

## Context
This work extends the multipole expansion approach to bremsstrahlung previously applied to nuclear collisions below the Coulomb barrier by Reinhardt (unreferenced in text but cited as JR). It contrasts with earlier calculations that assumed either incoherent summation due to uncorrelated nucleon motion (Ko, 1980s) or contributions from nucleus-nucleus collisions at large impact parameters with dipole-like structure (Vasak, 1986). The authors also contrast their coherent treatment with interpretations of the "exponential" shape of the energy spectrum as caused by incoherent photon emission from a thermalized nucleon gas (Niefenecker, 1980s). The work enables future calculations incorporating detailed stopping mechanisms (as in Stahl, 1990s) and spatial extension of the colliding particles, and suggests that the measured angular distribution does not provide proof for thermalization or incoherent photon production.
