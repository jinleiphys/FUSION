# Light-neutrino mass hierarchies, nuclear matrix elements, and the observability of neutrinoless double-beta decay

> Results for neutrino flavor oscillations and neutrino mixing mechanisms, obtained from the analysis of the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), the SuperKamiokande (SK), CHOOZ, KamLAND and WMAP data, are used to calculate the effective neutrino mass relevant for the neutrinoless double-beta decay. The observability of the decay of 76Ge is discussed within different light-neutrino mass hierarchies and by presenting a systematics on the available nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim

The effective electron-neutrino mass extracted from neutrino oscillation and WMAP data (approximately 0.24 eV) does not match the central value of 0.39 eV reported for the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment on 76Ge, nor does it agree with standard pnQRPA nuclear matrix element calculations, indicating that the observability of neutrinoless double-beta decay depends critically on both the neutrino mass hierarchy and the nuclear model used.

## Method

The authors combined the best-fit neutrino mixing matrix from Bandyopadhyay, Choubey, Goswami and Kar (BCGK) with WMAP constraints on the sum of neutrino masses to calculate the effective electron-neutrino mass for normal, inverse, and degenerate mass hierarchies. They then compiled a systematics of nuclear matrix elements for 76Ge from the literature, including spherical pnQRPA calculations, shell-model results, and other pnQRPA-related approaches, and used these to extract effective neutrino masses from experimental half-life limits.

## Key numbers

- WMAP upper limit on the sum of neutrino masses: 0.71 eV
- Largest effective neutrino mass from neutrino data: approximately 0.24 eV (degenerate hierarchy)
- Smallest effective neutrino mass from neutrino data: approximately 0.7 × 10^{-4} eV (degenerate hierarchy)
- pnQRPA central nuclear matrix element for 76Ge: M_GT^{(0nu)}(1-chi_F) = 3.65
- Shell-model nuclear matrix element for 76Ge: M_GT^{(0nu)}(1-chi_F) = 1.74
- Effective neutrino mass from pnQRPA matrix element with half-life 2.5 × 10^{25} yr: 0.35 eV
- Effective neutrino mass from shell-model matrix element with half-life 2.5 × 10^{25} yr: 0.74 eV
- Present pnQRPA calculation yields effective neutrino mass: 0.39 eV (using half-life 2.5 × 10^{25} yr) and 0.50 eV (using half-life 1.5 × 10^{25} yr)
- Nuclear matrix element needed to reach WMAP limit of 0.24 eV: M_GT^{(0nu)}(1-chi_F) = 5.36
- VAMPIR approach yields effective neutrino mass: 0.19 eV (considered unrealistically large due to omission of proton-neutron residual interaction)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrino oscillation data from SNO, SuperKamiokande, CHOOZ, KamLAND, and WMAP to calculate the effective neutrino mass for neutrinoless double-beta decay, building on the formalism of Suhonen and Civitarese (1998). It directly addresses the discrepancy between the neutrino mass scale from oscillation experiments (approximately 0.10-0.20 eV from Pierce and Maruyama, or lower from Ellis) and the range 0.4-1.7 eV from earlier double-beta decay studies by Vogel (2002). The paper enables future experimental planning by showing that next-generation experiments could potentially reach the sensitivity needed to test the neutrino oscillation predictions, but that current nuclear matrix element uncertainties (spanning factors of 2-3) remain a critical limitation.
