# Proton and kaon production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=3$ GeV

> Within an extended isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, we study the protons, $K^+$ mesons and $Λ$ hyperons production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=3$ GeV. For the collision in 0-10% centrality, we study the transverse momentum spectra and rapidity dependent mean transverse momentum for protons. For the collision in 10-40% centrality, we study the directed and elliptic flows for protons and $K^+$ mesons. The results show that the momentum-dependent nuclear mean field with an incompressibility $K_0=230$ MeV can fit fairly the STAR experimental data, while the momentum-independent nuclear mean field with both $K_0=230$ MeV and $K_0=380$ MeV can only partially describe the experimental results. In addition, we also study the directed and elliptic flows for the associated $Λ$, observations reveal the same conclusions as for kaons. These findings indicate that the momentum dependence of nuclear mean field plays a significant role in understanding nuclear matter properties in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=3$ GeV.

# Proton and kaon production in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV

## Key claim

The momentum-dependent nuclear mean field with incompressibility K0=230 MeV can fairly fit all STAR experimental data for protons, K+ mesons, and Lambda hyperons at sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV, while momentum-independent mean fields with either K0=230 MeV or K0=380 MeV can only partially describe the experimental results. The authors qualify that the momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field plays a significant role in understanding nuclear matter properties in heavy-ion collisions at this energy, but note that systematic uncertainties such as cross sections, initialization, and cluster formation may affect quantitative observations.

## Method

The authors used an extended isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV. They compared three nuclear mean field scenarios: a momentum-dependent (MDI) mean field with K0=230 MeV, and momentum-independent (MID) mean fields with both K0=230 MeV (soft EoS) and K0=380 MeV (stiff EoS). For 0-10% centrality collisions they studied proton transverse momentum spectra and rapidity-dependent mean transverse momentum; for 10-40% centrality they analyzed directed and elliptic flows for protons, K+ mesons, and Lambda hyperons, comparing all results to STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility values used: K0=230 MeV (soft EoS) and K0=380 MeV (stiff EoS) for momentum-independent mean field; K0=230 MeV for momentum-dependent mean field
- Saturation density rho0=0.16 fm^-3 used as reference density for nuclear matter properties
- Symmetry energy slope fixed at L=62.47 MeV for all scenarios
- Binding energy of nuclear matter at saturation: -16 MeV
- Isoscalar effective mass m*_s=0.7m for the MDI scenario
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(rho0)=32.5 MeV
- Kaon-nucleon scattering length alpha_KN approximately -0.255 fm, leading to a repulsive kaon potential of 30 MeV at rho0
- 1x10^5 events simulated for 0-10% centrality; 6x10^5 events for 10-40% centrality

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear equation of state at densities 3-4 times saturation density using high-precision STAR data at sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV (M.S22, M.I24). It addresses the ambiguity noted in previous studies where both momentum-independent soft and stiff EoS could fit experimental data (M.S22, M.K24), while Zhou (2025) found only momentum-dependent mean field with soft EoS could provide good fits. The results confirm the findings of Zhou (2025) and demonstrate that kaon and Lambda flow observables provide additional discrimination between mean field scenarios. The work enables more precise constraints on the nuclear EoS by showing that momentum dependence must be included when extracting incompressibility from heavy-ion collision data at this energy.
