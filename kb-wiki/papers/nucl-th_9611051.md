# The effect of electro-magnetic formfactors on dilepton production off pp-collisions

> We investigate the effect of the electric formfactor on dilepton production off pp-collisions. In the model we use, the formfactor is separated in a pion-cloud contribution and a Vector Meson Dominance part. We find that the dilepton spectrum is hardly affected by the pion-cloud part, but significantly by the VMD contribution. We point out that data with sufficient resolution would provide a crucial test of the validity of VMD for the nucleon formfactor in the timelike region.

## Key claim

The off-shell dependent pion-cloud contribution to the nucleon electric formfactor hardly affects the dilepton production spectrum from pp collisions, while the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) part significantly increases the cross-section and produces a sharp omega-meson peak that could serve as a test of VMD validity in the timelike region, provided experimental resolution is sufficient.

## Method

The authors used the model of Schaefer et al. (1994) to calculate the differential cross-section for dilepton production off pp collisions, incorporating nucleon and Delta-isobar intermediate states with an effective One-Boson-Exchange parametrization for the NN interaction. They employed the half-off-shell nucleon formfactors calculated by Doenges et al. (1995), which separate the formfactor into a pion-cloud contribution and a VMD part with rho and omega meson poles, and applied a VMD-type factor to the N-Delta-gamma vertices.

## Key numbers

- At a laboratory energy of 2 GeV, the full calculation including both nucleonic and Delta contributions with all formfactors shows a broad enhancement due to the rho meson and a sharp peak from the omega meson in the dilepton spectrum
- At distances of approximately 20 MeV from the omega peak, interference effects between VMD terms reach up to 25%
- At q^2 = 500 MeV, interference effects diminish to only 5%
- The omega peak arises exclusively from nucleonic contributions, not from Delta intermediate states
- The effect of using pseudo-vector versus pseudo-scalar coupling for the pion in the OBE interaction is almost of the same order as the VMD formfactor effect

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of off-shell effects in dilepton production (de Jong and Mosel, 1996) and uses the formfactor model of Doenges et al. (1995), which provides an excellent fit to on-shell formfactors in the spacelike region. The paper addresses the need for experimental tests of VMD for the nucleon formfactor in the timelike region, which has been inaccessible for on-shell nucleons below the N-antinucleon threshold, and suggests that data from facilities like COSY at 2 GeV could provide such a test.
