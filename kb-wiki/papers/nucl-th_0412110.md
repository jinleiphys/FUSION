# Strange matter in rotating compact stars

> We have constructed equations of state involving various exotic forms of matter with large strangeness fraction such as hyperon matter, Bose-Einstein condensates of antikaons and strange quark matter. First order phase transitions from hadronic to antikaon condensed and quark matter are considered here. The hadronic phase is described by the relativistic field theoretical model. Later those equations of state are exploited to investigate models of uniformly rotating compact stars. The effect of rotation on the third family branch for the equation of state involving only antikaon condensates is investigated. We also discuss the back bending phenomenon due to a first order phase transition from $K^-$ condensed to quark matter.

# Strange matter in rotating compact stars

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the existence and stability of a "third family" of compact stars (beyond the neutron star branch) depends on rotation rate, disappearing above a certain angular velocity; the back bending phenomenon in moment of inertia can signal a first order phase transition from antikaon condensed matter to quark matter, but only in supramassive sequences.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state (EoS) for dense matter including hyperons, Bose-Einstein condensates of antikaons, and strange quark matter, with first order phase transitions between phases. The hadronic phase was described using a relativistic field theoretical model with baryon-baryon interactions mediated by scalar and vector mesons including strange mesons, while quark matter was treated in the MIT bag model. These EoS were then used to solve Einstein's field equations for both static (Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff) and uniformly rotating compact star configurations, computing mass-radius relations, moment of inertia, and fixed baryon number evolutionary sequences.

## Key numbers
- Mixed phase of hadronic and K- condensed matter begins at energy density epsilon = 6.11 x 10^14 g/cm^3 (or 2.23 n0, where n0 = 0.153 fm^-3)
- For case I, second order K0 condensation occurs after mixed phase ends at epsilon = 1.17 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- For case II, first order K- condensed to quark matter phase transition begins at epsilon = 1.59 x 10^15 g/cm^3 and ends at epsilon = 2.77 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Maximum neutron star mass for case I (non-rotating): 1.57 solar masses with radius 12.8 km
- Maximum mass in third family branch (case I, non-rotating): 1.55 solar masses with radius 10.7 km
- Third family branch disappears beyond fixed angular velocity Omega = 2158 sec^-1
- Back bending period range: 1.10 ms to 1.35 ms, corresponding to angular velocity from 5700 s^-1 to 4660 s^-1
- Antikaon optical potential used: -160 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- Bag constant B^1/4 = 200 MeV and strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV for quark EoS

## Context
This work extends previous studies of exotic matter in compact stars by Banik, Hanauske, and Bandyopadhyay (2002-2004) and others such as Kettner (1995), Schaffner-Bielich (1996), and Glendenning (1997) on third family branches and antikaon condensation. It also builds on the back bending phenomenon discussed by Glendenning (1997), Weber (1999), and Zdunik (2001) as a potential signature of first order phase transitions. The results enable future comparisons with observations of rotating neutron stars such as EXO 0748-676 (Cottam 2002, Villarreal and Strohmayer 2004) and the double pulsar system PSR J0737-3039, and can be constrained by upcoming experiments on antikaon-nucleus interactions at J-PARC and antibaryon-nucleus studies at GSI.
