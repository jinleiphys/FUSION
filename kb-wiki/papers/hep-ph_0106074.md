# Detailed Balance and Sea-Quark Flavor Asymmetry of Proton

> In this study, the proton is taken as an ensemble of quark-gluon Fock states. Using the principle of detailed balance, the probabilities of finding every Fock states of the proton are obtained without any parameter. A new origin of the light flavor sea quark asymmetry, i.e., $\bar{u} \not= \bar{d}$, is given as a pure statistical effect. It is found that $\bar{d}-\bar{u} \approx 0.124$, which is in surprisingly agreement with the experimental observation.

# Detailed Balance and Sea-Quark Flavor Asymmetry of Proton

## Key claim

The proton’s light-flavor sea-quark asymmetry ( d̄ > ū ) arises as a pure statistical effect from the principle of detailed balance applied to an ensemble of quark-gluon Fock states, without any free parameters. The result depends on the simplifying assumption that only parton number (not spin, color, or wave-function details) controls the transition rates between Fock states.

## Method

The proton is treated as an ensemble of light-cone Fock states labeled by the numbers of u ū pairs (i), d d̄ pairs (j), and gluons (k). Using detailed balance, the ratio of probabilities for two Fock states is set equal to the ratio of their transition rates, which are taken to be proportional only to the number of partons that can split or recombine. This yields a closed-form expression for ρᵢⱼₖ relative to the valence-only state, and all probabilities are then fixed by normalization. The calculation is performed both without and with the inclusion of the g ⇌ gg process.

## Key numbers

- **d̄ – ū = 0.124** (without g ⇌ gg process), compared to the experimental value 0.118 ± 0.012 from the FNAL E866/NuSea Collaboration (2001).
- **d̄ – ū = 0.123** (with g ⇌ gg process included), also in agreement with the same experimental result.
- **ū = 0.308**, **d̄ = 0.432**, **g = 0.997** (without g ⇌ gg).
- **ū = 0.304**, **d̄ = 0.426**, **g = 1.109** (with g ⇌ gg).
- **Total number of intrinsic partons** (valence + sea quarks + gluons) ≈ **5.5**.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone Fock-state approach of Brodsky, Pauli, and Pinsky (1998) and the baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1996) by providing a complete, parameter-free statistical description of all Fock states. It offers a new mechanism for the flavor asymmetry observed in deep inelastic scattering by the New Muon Collaboration (1991) and in Drell-Yan experiments by the NA51 Collaboration (1994) and the FNAL E866/NuSea Collaboration (2001). The success suggests that statistical methods, though limited in applicability due to the small number of partons (≈ 5.5), can be extended to other baryons and may motivate more sophisticated treatments.
