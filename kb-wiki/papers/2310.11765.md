# Causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in a one-dimensional expanding system

> We derive equations of motion of hydrodynamic fluctuations performing perturbative expansion of the energy-momentum conservation equations around the boost invariant solution in one-dimensional expanding system. In the course of derivation, we do not assume any specific forms of constitutive equations for shear stress tensor $π^{μν}$ and bulk pressure $Π$. Therefore, the framework enables us to employ any constitutive equations beyond the Navier-Stokes theory which satisfy the causality. Employing Israel-Stewart equations as examples of the constitutive equations, we demonstrate the dynamics of causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in (1+1)-dimensional Milne coordinates. We observe that structure of energy density fluctuations is almost frozen in the early stage of the expansion. Two-point correlations of energy density fluctuations turn out to be closely related with the properties of the medium such as sound velocity, viscosity, and relaxation time. Furthermore, we show that two-particle correlation functions of final hadrons after freezeout inherit correlations of thermodynamic variables and flow rapidity. This opens a new door for an analysis of transport properties of the medium produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives equations of motion for causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in a (1+1)-dimensional expanding system by linearizing energy-momentum conservation around a boost-invariant (Bjorken) background, without assuming specific constitutive equations. The framework is demonstrated using Israel-Stewart equations, but the authors note that the perturbative approach breaks down at late times because fluctuations of shear pressure eventually exceed the background value.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion of the energy-momentum conservation equations around the Bjorken boost-invariant solution in Milne coordinates, keeping terms up to first order in fluctuations of energy density, flow rapidity, shear pressure, and bulk pressure. They employ Israel-Stewart equations as constitutive relations with Gaussian white noise terms satisfying fluctuation-dissipation relations, and solve the resulting stochastic partial differential equations on an event-by-event basis using a second-order stochastic Runge-Kutta method with a momentum cutoff to suppress short-wavelength singularities. The system is evolved from proper time τ = 1 fm with smooth initial conditions (zero fluctuations) and periodic boundary conditions in space-time rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time: τ_ini = 1 fm; initial background energy density: e_0 = 10 GeV/fm³
- Numerical grid: Δτ = 0.01 fm, Δx = Δy = 2 fm, Δη_s = 0.1, σ_η_s = 0.1 (Gaussian smearing width for noise in rapidity)
- Space-time rapidity range: η_s from 0 to 10
- Default specific shear viscosity: η/s = 1/4π (from AdS/CFT); default relaxation time: τ_π = (2 - ln 2)/(2πT)
- The ratio |δπ/π_0| exceeds unity at τ ≈ 4 fm for conformal EoS and at τ ≈ 2.5 fm for lattice EoS with default transport coefficients
- All ratios |δe/e_0| and |δy| remain below 0.1 throughout the evolution

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hydrodynamic fluctuations in one-dimensional expanding systems by Kapusta (2012) and Chattopadhyay (2017), which used first-order (Navier-Stokes) constitutive equations or ensemble-averaged quantities, respectively. Unlike those approaches, the present paper solves stochastic differential equations event-by-event with causal (Israel-Stewart) constitutive equations and introduces a momentum cutoff to avoid short-wavelength singularities. The framework enables future analysis of transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma from two-particle correlation functions of final hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
