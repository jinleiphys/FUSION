# Non-coherent character of isoscalar pairing probed with Gamow-Teller strength: New insight into $^{14}$C dating $ﾎｲ$ decay

> We investigate the phase coherence of isoscalar pairs from the $B({¥rm GT};0^+_1¥,T¥!=¥!1 ¥to 1^+_1¥,T¥!=¥!0)$ values in two-particle configurations of $A=6$, 18, and 42 nuclei and two-hole configurations of $A=14$ and 38 ones. We find that these Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix elements are always constructive and thus enlarged under isovector- and isoscalar-pairing Hamiltonians, whereas the observed GT strengths are strongly hindered for the two-hole configurations, including the famous $^{14}$C dating $ﾎｲ$ decay. This indicates that the actual isoscalar pair, unlike the isovector pair, has no definite phase coherence, which can work against forming isoscalar-pair condensates.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed strong hindrance of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in two-hole configurations (including the famous $^{14}$C dating beta decay) indicates that actual isoscalar pairs, unlike isovector pairs, lack definite phase coherence, which works against forming isoscalar-pair condensates, this conclusion is conditional on the finding that ideal isoscalar-pairing Hamiltonians always produce constructive interference and enhanced GT strength, but realistic interactions do not.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations of $B({¥rm GT};0^+_1¥,T¥!=¥!1 ¥to 1^+_1¥,T¥!=¥!0)$ values for two-particle configurations in $A=6$, 18, and 42 nuclei and two-hole configurations in $A=14$ and 38 nuclei, using both realistic interactions (CKII for the $p$ shell) and idealized isovector/isoscalar pairing Hamiltonians. They analyzed the sign structure of off-diagonal Hamiltonian matrix elements in a "pairing phase convention" and applied the Perron-Frobenius theorem to prove that ideal pairing interactions always produce same-sign eigenvector components, while realistic interactions produce mixed signs analogous to geometrical frustration.

## Context

This work extends the study of the low-energy super Gamow-Teller state identified by Fujita et al. (2014) and addresses the long-standing puzzle of the $^{14}$C dating beta decay lifetime, previously attributed to tensor forces by Jancovici and Talmi (1954). The paper provides a mathematically rigorous explanation for why isoscalar pairing condensates remain elusive despite strong attractive forces in the isoscalar channel, in contrast to well-established isovector pairing. The results enable future investigations of how non-coherent isoscalar pairing affects observables in complex nuclei, including double-beta decay matrix elements as discussed by Vogel (1986), Engel (1988), Hinohara (2014), and Menendez (2016).
