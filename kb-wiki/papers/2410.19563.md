# $P$-wave charmonium contribution to hidden-charm states from reanalysis of lattice QCD data

> We reanalyze, considering the contribution of $P$-wave charmonia, lattice data for the $D \bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel of S. Prelovsek et al. [JHEP 06, 035 (2021)] and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems of S. Prelovsek et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 111, 192001 (2013)] with $m_π\simeq 280$ and $266$ MeV, and $L=24a/32a$ ($a\simeq 0.09$ fm) and $L=16a$ ($a\simeq0.1239(13)$ fm), respectively. The hidden-charm states with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$, $1^{++}$, and $2^{++}$ quantum numbers are then searched for. For $0^{++}$, the analysis reveals three poles in the $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel amplitude, corresponding to three states. Two of these poles, located near the $D\bar{D}$ and $D_s\bar{D}_s$ thresholds, can be interpreted as mostly molecular states. A third pole above the $D_s\bar{D}_s$ threshold is originated from the $P$-wave $χ_{c0}(2P)$ charmonium state. The number of poles found in the $D\bar D$-$D_s \bar D_s$ system is the same as that found in the original lattice analysis though the position of the third pole changes sizeably. In the $1^{++}$ sector, we find two poles in the complex energy plane. The first one is related to the molecular $X(3872)$ state, with a compositeness exceeding $90\%$, while the second one, stemming from the $χ_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium, appears above the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold and it likely corresponds to the recently discovered $χ_{c1}(4010)$ state. In the $2^{++}$ sector, we also report two poles and find that the dressed $χ_{c2}(2P)$ is lighter than the $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular state, with the dynamics of the latter closely related to that of the heavy-quark spin-symmetry partner of the $X(3872)$. Our exploratory study of the $1^{++}$ and $2^{++}$ sectors offers valuable insights into their dynamics, but given that the fits that we carry out are underconstrained, more lattice data are required to draw robust conclusions.

## Key claim

The reanalysis of lattice QCD data for the $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems, including explicit $P$-wave charmonium contributions, reveals that the number of poles in each $J^{PC}$ sector matches the number of bare charmonium states plus molecular states, but the fits for the $1^{++}$ and $2^{++}$ sectors are underconstrained and require more lattice data to draw robust conclusions.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel scattering formalism that includes both $S$-wave charmed meson pairs and bare $P$-wave charmonium states ($\chi_{c0}(2P)$, $\chi_{c1}(2P)$, $\chi_{c2}(2P)$), with interactions constrained by heavy-quark spin symmetry. They fit the low-energy constants to lattice QCD energy levels from Prelovsek et al. (2021) for the $0^{++}$ $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ system and from Prelovsek et al. (2013) for the $1^{++}$ $D\bar{D}^*$ system, using Lüscher's method reformulated with Gaussian regulators to extract infinite-volume scattering amplitudes and pole positions.

## Key numbers

- For the $0^{++}$ sector: three poles are found in the $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ coupled-channel amplitude; two poles near the $D\bar{D}$ and $D_s\bar{D}_s$ thresholds are mostly molecular, and a third pole above the $D_s\bar{D}_s$ threshold originates from the $\chi_{c0}(2P)$ charmonium state
- For the $1^{++}$ sector: the molecular $X(3872)$ state has a compositeness exceeding $90\%$; the second pole from $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ appears above the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold and likely corresponds to the $\chi_{c1}(4010)$ state discovered by Belle with mass $(4014.3 \pm 4.0 \pm 1.5)$ MeV and width $(4\pm 11 \pm 6)$ MeV
- For the $2^{++}$ sector: the dressed $\chi_{c2}(2P)$ is lighter than the $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular state, with the latter's dynamics closely related to the heavy-quark spin-symmetry partner of the $X(3872)$
- Lattice parameters: $m_\pi \simeq 280$ MeV and $L=24a/32a$ ($a\simeq 0.09$ fm) for the $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ system; $m_\pi \simeq 266$ MeV and $L=16a$ ($a\simeq0.1239(13)$ fm) for the $D\bar{D}^*$ system

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD analyses of Prelovsek et al. (2021) for the $0^{++}$ $D\bar{D}$-$D_s\bar{D}_s$ system and Prelovsek et al. (2013) for the $1^{++}$ $D\bar{D}^*$ system by explicitly including $P$-wave charmonium contributions, following the formalism of Cincioglu et al. (2016). The study tests three hypotheses for charmonium-like states near open-charm thresholds: pure molecular, pure charmonium, and coexistence of both. The results support the coexistence picture (view III) and enable future lattice calculations with more data to distinguish between competing interpretations of the $XYZ$ states.
