# The QCD Phase Structure at High Baryon Density

> We consider the possibility that color deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration do not coincide in dense baryonic matter at low temperature. As a consequence, a state of massive "constituent" quarks would exist as an intermediate phase between confined nuclear matter and the plasma of deconfined massless quarks and gluons. We discuss the properties of this state and its relation to the recently proposed quarkyonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that at low temperature and high baryon density, color deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration may not coincide, creating an intermediate phase of massive "constituent" quarks between confined nuclear matter and the conventional quark-gluon plasma. This scenario is presented as a possibility rather than a certainty, with the authors noting that at vanishing baryon density lattice studies show the two transitions do coincide.

## Method

The authors use percolation theory with two intrinsic spatial scales, the hadronic confinement radius (R_h ~ 1 fm) and the constituent quark radius (R_q ~ 0.3 fm), to define density thresholds for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration. They determine constituent quark properties (mass and size) from lattice QCD heavy quark correlation studies, the additive quark model, and perturbative QCD estimates of the effective quark mass from the chiral condensate. The phase boundaries are then calculated by applying percolation conditions to permeable mesons at high temperature and hard-core nucleons at high baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass M_q = 0.3–0.4 GeV, determined from lattice and experimental data
- Constituent quark radius R_q = 0.33 fm from additive quark model cross sections (sigma_qq ~ 3.3 mb)
- Constituent quark radius R_q = 0.26 fm from perturbative QCD estimate using Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV and chiral condensate^(1/3) = 0.2 GeV, yielding M_q = 375 MeV
- Hadronic confinement radius R_h ~ 1 fm, with hadron radius R_h ~ 0.8–0.9 fm from percolation analysis
- Deconfinement temperature at mu=0: T_dec ~ 180 MeV
- Deconfinement baryochemical potential at T=0: mu_dec ~ 1.1 GeV
- Deconfinement baryon density at T=0: n_B ~ 0.9 fm^(-3) (about 5 times nuclear matter density n_0 ~ 0.17 fm^(-3))
- Chiral symmetry restoration baryon density: n_B^ch ~ 3.5 fm^(-3) ~ 3.9 times the deconfinement density
- Chiral symmetry restoration baryochemical potential: mu_B^ch ~ 2.2 GeV
- Gluonic dressing energy F_g ~ 0.6 GeV from lattice studies, confirmed by D meson mass minus charm quark mass (0.60 ± 0.10 GeV) and B meson mass minus bottom quark mass (0.53 ± 0.15 GeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals for a constituent quark phase by Shuryak, Pisarski, Baym and Bielefeld, and others, and relates to the recently proposed "quarkyonic" matter from large N_c QCD arguments by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The paper uses percolation theory (Pomeranchuk 1951; Stauffer 1979) and lattice QCD results at vanishing baryon density (Kaczmarek et al. 2002; Doering et al. 2007; Huebner et al. 2008) to quantify the two-scale scenario. It enables experimental searches for this intermediate phase at future facilities exploring high baryon density regions of the QCD phase diagram, and provides a geometric framework for understanding the relationship between deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration.
