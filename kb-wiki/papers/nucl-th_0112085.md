# Neutron Radii in Mean-Field Models

> Bulk nuclear observables such as charge radii and binding energies are well described by both nonrelativistic and covariant mean-field models. However, predictions of neutron radii, which are not tightly constrained by reliable data, vary significantly. The nature of this variation is investigated using correlations between basic properties of the models and the neutron skin thickness in lead. The results suggest that conventional covariant models are too limited. The study is guided by principles and insights of effective field theory (EFT), such as power counting, and the relation of mean-field models to a more general EFT approach to nuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

The variation in neutron radius predictions across mean-field models is primarily driven by differences in the bulk symmetry energy and its density dependence in infinite nuclear matter, rather than by surface or finite-range effects; conventional covariant models are too limited in their isovector structure to capture the correct density dependence.

## Method

The study compares predictions from a large set of nonrelativistic Skyrme, covariant meson, and covariant point-coupling mean-field models, all fit to roughly the same bulk nuclear data (binding energies, charge densities, spin-orbit splittings of doubly magic nuclei). Correlations between the predicted neutron skin thickness in 208Pb and basic properties of the energy functionals are examined graphically, with additional parameter sets generated by forcing particular neutron radii while maintaining acceptable fits to standard observables.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb varies significantly across models, with conventional covariant meson models generally predicting larger values than conventional Skyrme models
- The leading isovector contribution to the energy per particle in 208Pb is suppressed relative to the leading isoscalar contribution by a factor of [(Z-N)/2A]^2, putting it on the order of 1-2 MeV

## Context

This work extends correlation analyses previously applied to covariant models for deformations (Furnstahl, 1987) and spin-orbit splittings (Furnstahl, 1998), and parallels similar studies by Brown (2000) using Skyrme models and by Typel and Brown (2001) for covariant models. The results support earlier macroscopic-model conclusions by Oyamatsu (1998) and Bodmer (1960) that the neutron skin is dominated by bulk symmetry energy properties, and the comparison with microscopic approaches tied to free-space nucleon-nucleon scattering implies that conventional covariant models based on heavy meson interactions are too simple. The study enables systematic improvement of mean-field functionals within an effective field theory framework, with the goal of embedding them into a more general density functional theory/EFT approach to nuclei.
