# Why {\em Explicit} Strangeness Is Not Relevant In Compact Stars

> Drawing largely from my work with co--workers, I present arguments that strangeness does not play an {\it explicit} role in compact-star matter. They are based on the skyrmion description of dense matter combined with Wilsoninan renormalization group approach to kaon nuclear interactions. The key idea is that quark degrees of freedom, carrying strangeness, can be traded in by topology for hadron degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
The paper argues that strangeness degrees of freedom (kaon condensation and hyperons) do not play an explicit role in compact-star matter, but this conclusion is conditional on a consistent treatment of both baryon and meson sectors on the same footing, which has not been fully achieved to date. The author claims that the verdict ruling out kaon condensation based on 2-solar-mass star observations is premature given the large theoretical uncertainties in conventional treatments.

## Method
The author uses a skyrmion description of dense matter combined with a Wilsonian renormalization group approach to kaon nuclear interactions, based on a scale-chiral Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry (sHLS) and baryons included either explicitly or generated as skyrmions. The analysis employs a mean-field treatment of the three-flavor baryonic sHLS Lagrangian endowed with intrinsic density dependence (IDD), and uses the Callan-Klebanov bound-state model to treat kaons and hyperons simultaneously in skyrmion crystal configurations.

## Key numbers
- The half-skyrmion transition density is estimated at n_{1/2} ~ (2-3)n_0, where n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3} is normal nuclear matter density.
- The Bedaque-Steiner constraint requires the density range Δ where Λ interactions become repulsive to satisfy 1 < Δ/n_0 ≲ 3.
- In the mean-field calculation, μ_Λ - m_Λ crosses zero at a density 1.5 < n/n_0 < 2.0, meeting the Bedaque-Steiner constraint.
- In the skyrmion crystal model with dilaton mass m_σ ≈ 720 MeV, the kaon mass vanishes at density ≈ 4.0 n_0 (the dilaton limit fixed point), though the author notes this density is too low and the realistic treatment gives n > ~10 n_0.
- The binding energy of the kaon at nuclear matter density comes out to be ~ (80-100) MeV for m_σ = 720 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Rho and collaborators (bsHLS, 2017) that described massive compact stars without strangeness degrees of freedom, and directly addresses the "strangeness problem" raised by the discovery of ~2-solar-mass stars (Antoniadis et al., 2013; 1.97S). It contradicts the standard SU(3) chiral perturbation approach of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and reviews by BLR, which predicted kaon condensation at ~3n_0. The analysis enables a path toward a full three-flavor V_{lowk}RG calculation that treats hyperons and kaons consistently, and suggests that the hyperon problem can be avoided within the formalism developed in LPR (2015) and Bedaque and Steiner (2015).
