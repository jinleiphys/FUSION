# Continuous differential operators and a new interpretation of the charmonium spectrum

> The definition of the standard differential operator is extended from integer steps to arbitrary stepsize. The classical, nonrelativistic Hamiltonian is quantized, using these new continuous operators. The resulting Schroedinger type equation generates free particle solutions, which are confined in space. The angular momentum eigenvalues are calculated algebraically. It is shown, that the charmonium spectrum may be classified by the derived angular momentum eigenvalues for stepsize=2/3.

# Continuous differential operators and a new interpretation of the charmonium spectrum

## Key claim

The charmonium spectrum can be classified by angular momentum eigenvalues derived from a Schroedinger-type equation using continuous differential operators with stepsize p=2/3, but only for n=0,1,2,4 does the agreement fall within experimental errors; for n=3 there is a 5% deviation, and an additional correction term Δτ is needed for the j=3 states.

## Method

The author extends the standard differential operator ∂ to arbitrary positive real stepsize p, defining D = ∂^p via the Gamma function acting on basis functions x^ν. This operator is used to quantize the classical nonrelativistic Hamiltonian, yielding a Schroedinger-type equation HΨ = (-ℏ²/2m) DⁱD_i Ψ + V(Xⁱ)Ψ = iℏ ∂_t Ψ. The angular momentum eigenvalues are calculated algebraically using generalized Euler operators and Casimir operators of an SO(3) algebra, then applied to classify the charmonium spectrum with p=2/3 using mass formulas H|jm⟩ = m₀c² + αJ² + BL_z|jm⟩.

## Key numbers

- For p=2/3, the L_z eigenvalues for n=0 through n=6 are: 0, 1, 1.46099849, 1.86073502, 2.22222222, 2.55674735, 2.87084832 (in units of ℏ)
- For p=2/3, the J² eigenvalues for n=0 through n=6 are: 0, 2, 3.59551506, 5.32306984, 7.16049383, 9.09370437, 11.11261839 (in units of ℏ²)
- The experimental L_z ratio for χ-particles (j=2,m=2) yields 1.468, matching the theoretical value 1.46099849 within experimental errors of ±2 MeV
- Fitted parameters: m₀c² = 2434.72 MeV, α = 272.64 MeV, B = 107 MeV
- Predicted mass for the <00> state: 2434.72 MeV, matching the C₁(Σ_c)(2430) particle
- For the D-meson region (above 3.73 GeV): B_D = 270 MeV, obtained from Ψ(4040)<31> - Ψ(3770)<30>
- Predicted <33> state at 4272 MeV ± 15 MeV
- Theoretical Ψ(3770)<30> mass: 3886 MeV (116 MeV above experimental value)
- Theoretical Ψ(4410)<40> mass: 4387 MeV (matches experimental value within errors)

## Context

This work extends the mathematical concept of fractional calculus to quantum mechanical operators, proposing an alternative to standard potential models for charmonium such as those by Eichten et al. (1975) and Krammer and Krasemann (1979). The approach requires no input from QCD and claims to reproduce the charmonium spectrum with only 4 parameters over a 2 GeV range, predicting two new particles. The linearization procedure generalizes Dirac's approach and connects to the Levy-Leblond (1967) treatment of the Schroedinger equation, suggesting an inherent SU(3) symmetry for p=2/3. The next step identified is the inclusion of relativistic effects.
