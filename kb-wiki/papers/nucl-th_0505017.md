# Analytic Description of Critical Point Actinides in a Transition from Octupole Deformation to Octupole Vibrations

> An analytic collective model in which the relative presence of the quadrupole and octupole deformations is determined by a parameter (phi_0), while axial symmetry is obeyed, is developed. The model [to be called the analytic quadrupole octupole axially symmetric model (AQOA)] involves an infinite well potential, provides predictions for energy and B(EL) ratios which depend only on phi_0, draws the border between the regions of octupole deformation and octupole vibrations in an essentially parameter-independent way, and describes well 226-Th and 226-Ra, for which experimental energy data are shown to suggest that they lie close to this border. The similarity of the AQOA results with phi_0=45 degrees for ground state band spectra and B(E2) transition rates to the predictions of the X(5) model is pointed out. Analytic solutions are also obtained for Davidson potentials, leading to the AQOA spectrum through a variational procedure.

# Analytic Description of Critical Point Actinides in a Transition from Octupole Deformation to Octupole Vibrations

## Key claim

The AQOA model provides an essentially parameter-independent description of the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations, with the nuclei 226-Th and 226-Ra identified as lying close to this border based on experimental energy systematics; however, the model does not predict the odd-even staggering characteristic of octupole deformation because it assumes an infinitely high potential barrier in the octupole degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors develop an analytic collective model (AQOA) that treats quadrupole and octupole deformations on equal footing, with their relative presence determined by a single parameter phi_0, while assuming axial symmetry. An infinite well potential is used in the beta degree of freedom, and separation of variables is achieved by assuming the nucleus is rigid with respect to the angle phi that determines the relative amounts of quadrupole and octupole deformation. The resulting Schrodinger equation reduces to a Bessel equation in the beta variable, yielding energy eigenvalues determined by zeros of Bessel functions, while the phi-part gives harmonic oscillator solutions.

## Key numbers

- Energy ratios R(L)=E(L)/E(2) are stable in the region 30 degrees <= phi_0 <= 60 degrees
- Minima of energy ratios for the ground state band and maxima of B(EL) ratios are located between phi_0=40 degrees and 43 degrees
- Minima of energy ratios for excited bands (s=2, 3) are located near phi_0=35 degrees
- For 226-Ra, the experimental 0_2+ bandhead normalized to 2_1+ is 12.186, compared to AQOA prediction of 11.226 for phi_0=56 degrees
- For 226-Th, the experimental 0_2+ bandhead normalized to 2_1+ is 11.152, compared to AQOA prediction of 12.410 for phi_0=60 degrees
- The model of Bizzeti provides a value of 8.528 for the normalized 0_2+ bandhead in 226-Th
- X(5) model predicts a 0_2+ bandhead value of 5.65

## Context

This work extends the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) for quadrupole shape phase transitions to include octupole degrees of freedom, providing an analytic description of the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations in light actinides. The model differs from the approach of Bizzeti (2004), which is non-analytic and freezes the quadrupole degree of freedom while keeping octupole active. The results enable future studies of potential energy surfaces in the spdf-interacting boson model of Engel and Iachello (1987) and may be extended to include odd-even staggering and applications to the rare earth region near A=150.
