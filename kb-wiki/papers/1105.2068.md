# Resonances Driven by a Neutrino Gyroscope and Collective Neutrino Oscillations in Supernovae

> We show that flavor evolution of a system of neutrinos with continuous energy spectra as in supernovae can be understood in terms of the response of individual neutrino flavor-isospins (NFIS's) to the mean field. In the case of a system initially consisting of nu_e and anti-nu_e with the same energy spectrum but different number densities, the mean field is very well approximated by the total angular momentum of a neutrino gyroscope. Assuming that NFIS evolution is independent of the initial neutrino emission angle, the so-called single-angle approximation, we find that the evolution is governed by two types of resonances driven by precession and nutation of the gyroscope, respectively. The net flavor transformation crucially depends on the adiabaticity of evolution through these resonances. We show that the results for the system of two initial neutrino species can be extended to a system of four species with the initial number densities of nu_e and anti-nu_e significantly larger than those of nu_x and anti-nu_x. Further, we find that when the dependence on the initial neutrino emission angle is taken into account in the multi-angle approximation, nutation of the mean field is quickly damped out and can be neglected. In contrast, precession-driven resonances still govern the evolution of NFIS's with different energy and emission angles just as in the single-angle approximation. Our pedagogical and analytic study of collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae provides some insights into these seemingly complicated yet fascinating phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper shows that collective neutrino flavor evolution in supernovae can be understood through two types of resonances driven by precession and nutation of a "neutrino gyroscope" representing the mean field, but only under the single-angle approximation where NFIS evolution is independent of initial neutrino emission angle; in the multi-angle approximation, nutation is quickly damped out and only precession-driven resonances remain relevant.

## Method

The authors model neutrino flavor evolution using neutrino flavor-isospins (NFIS) in a three-dimensional flavor space, governed by equations of motion that include vacuum mixing and neutrino-neutrino forward scattering. They approximate the mean field of all NFIS by a "neutrino gyroscope" consisting of two effective NFIS representing initial ν_e and anti-ν_e, and analyze its precession and nutation using Euler angles and conserved quantities (spin magnitude σ, total angular momentum component S_z, and gyroscope energy E_gyro). They apply this analysis to a supernova example with initial number densities of ν_e and anti-ν_e significantly larger than those of ν_x and anti-ν_x, using the inverted mass hierarchy with δm² = 3×10⁻³ eV² and θ̃_v = 10⁻⁵, evolving from the neutrino sphere at R_ν = 10 km to r = 250 km.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae, which was first shown to occur for the atmospheric mass-squared difference by Duan et al. (2006) and subsequent authors. It builds on the discovery of stepwise spectral swaps at characteristic "split" energies by Fogli et al. (2007) and the finding of multiple spectral swaps by Dasgupta et al. (2007) and others. The paper provides a pedagogical analytic framework using the neutrino gyroscope analogy from Hannestad et al. (2006) and Raffelt et al. (2007), enabling a physical understanding of how precession-driven and nutation-driven resonances produce the observed spectral swaps, and showing that multi-angle effects suppress nutation while preserving precession-driven resonances.
