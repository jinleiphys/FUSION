# Electroproductions of Light $Λ$- and $Σ$-Hypernuclei

> Theoretical estimations of production cross sections of light $Λ$ and $Σ$ hypernuclei in $(e,e'K^{+})$ reactions at around CEBAF energies are given. Because of dominant spin-flip amplitudes and large momentum transfers, unnatural parity states and stretched states of hypernuclei are favorably excited. They are compared with quasifree hyperon productions.

## Key claim

The paper provides theoretical estimates of production cross sections for light Lambda and Sigma hypernuclei via (e,e'K+) reactions at CEBAF energies, but the author notes that the model and parameter values for the elementary N(gamma,K+)Y processes have large uncertainties because of the limitation of experimental data.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic impulse approximation with the Walecka model for nuclei and hypernuclei, treating the elementary N(e,e'K+)Y processes as one-photon-exchange with N(gamma,K+)Y vertices described by a sum of one-particle-exchange mechanisms. Coupling constants are taken from earlier work by Hsiung (1987) and Bennhold (1989) to reproduce known experimental data. The calculations use plane-wave approximation for the outgoing kaon, with the justification that kaons near 1 GeV interact weakly with nuclei and only light targets are considered.

## Key numbers

- For Lambda hypernuclei: the [(p3/2)-1_p,(p3/2)_Lambda]_{Jf=3} stretched state in 16N has a cross section of 2.485 nb/sr^2/GeV; the same state in 12B has 2.667 nb/sr^2/GeV
- For the unnatural parity state 4H* (Jf=1), the cross section is 2.260 nb/sr^2/GeV, which is larger than the natural parity 4H ground state (Jf=0) at 0.020 nb/sr^2/GeV by two orders of magnitude
- For Sigma hypernuclei: the [(p3/2)-1_p,(s1/2)_Sigma]_{Jf=2} stretched state in 16N gives 0.314 nb/sr^2/GeV (PS+delta model) and 0.176 nb/sr^2/GeV (PS model); in 12B the same state gives 0.420 and 0.276 nb/sr^2/GeV respectively
- For 4H* (Jf=1) Sigma hypernucleus: 4.021 nb/sr^2/GeV (PS+delta) and 2.600 nb/sr^2/GeV (PS)
- The approximate on-shell expression for the elementary process differs from exact calculations by 20-40%
- The minimum momentum transfer in the chosen kinematics is about 270 MeV/c at theta_K ~ 4 degrees, rising to about 320 MeV/c at 10 degrees and 420 MeV/c at 15 degrees
- The sticking probability for the most favorable Lambda hypernuclear states is about 1.3% of (2j+1) times the elementary cross section

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical estimations of hypernuclear photoproduction by Hsiung (1987) and Sotona (1989) to electroproduction, and improves upon the rough approximations used for electroproduction in Hsiung (1987) and the treatment limited to Lambda hypernuclei in Cohen (1987). The calculations are performed at energies corresponding to CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab), and the results are compared with quasifree hyperon production as the dominant background process. The paper enables experimental planning for electroproduction of hypernuclei, particularly for observing unnatural parity states and stretched states that are weakly excited in (K-,pi) and (pi,K+) reactions.
