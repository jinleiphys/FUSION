# Effective proton-neutron interaction in mirror nuclei

> Effective proton-neutron interactions, $V_{pn}$, in mirror nuclei are systematically analyzed using the ground-state atomic masses. A mirror symmetry of $V_{pn}$ is found for bound nuclei with a standard deviation of $σ=32$ keV. However, this mirror symmetry is apparently broken for some mirror-nuclei pairs when a proton-unbound nucleus is involved in extracting the $V_{pn}$ values. Such a mirror-symmetry breaking is attributed to the Thomas-Ehrman shift of the proton-unbound nucleus and investigated by using the Gamow shell model. It is concluded that the Thomas-Ehrman shift originates mainly from reduced Coulomb energies in the proton-unbound nuclei due to the extended radial density distribution of valence protons.

## Key claim

The effective proton-neutron interaction \(V_{pn}\) in mirror nuclei exhibits a mirror symmetry with a standard deviation of 32 keV when only bound nuclei are used, but this symmetry is broken for mirror pairs that involve a proton-unbound nucleus, with the breaking attributed to the Thomas-Ehrman shift arising from reduced Coulomb energies due to extended radial density distributions of valence protons.

## Method

The authors extracted empirical residual proton-neutron interaction values \(V_{pn}\) from ground-state atomic masses using four-point binding-energy difference formulas (Eqs. 1-4) applied to the latest Atomic-Mass Evaluation AME20 and recent mass measurements from \(B\rho\)-defined isochronous mass spectrometry and LEBIT. They compared \(V_{pn}\) values between mirror nuclei by computing \(\Delta V_{pn} = V_{pn}(T_z^{<},A) - V_{pn}(T_z^{>},A)\), first using only bound nuclei and then including cases where a proton-unbound nucleus participated in the extraction. Gamow shell model calculations with a chiral effective field theory two-body interaction and Coulomb force were performed for selected mirror pairs (\(^{21}\)Mg-\(^{21}\)F, \(^{17}\)F-\(^{17}\)O, \(^{18}\)Ne-\(^{18}\)O) to analyze the radial density distributions and quantify the Thomas-Ehrman shift.

## Key numbers

* Mirror symmetry of \(V_{pn}\) for bound nuclei: standard deviation \(\sigma = 32\) keV, with distribution centered at mean value \(-9\) keV.
* After excluding four data points related to proton-halo nuclei (\(^{27,28}\)P and \(^{28}\)S), the distribution centers at \(-4\) keV with \(\sigma = 28\) keV.
* Calculated \(\Delta V_{pn}\) from Gamow shell model: \(-71\) keV for \(^{21}\)Mg, \(-473\) keV for \(^{17}\)F, and \(-222\) keV for \(^{18}\)Ne, compared to experimental values of \(-66\) keV, \(-527\) keV, and \(-200\) keV respectively.
* Proton separation energy of \(^{15}\)F: \(S_p = -1270(14)\) keV from AME20.

## Context

This work systematically extends earlier studies of proton-neutron interactions in mirror nuclei (Basu 1971, PhysRevC.6.467) and the charge-symmetric Kelson-Garvey mass relations (Kelson 1966), using the improved mass data from AME20 and recent measurements (Zhang 2023, PhysRevLett.130.192501). The finding that mirror symmetry of \(V_{pn}\) holds at 32 keV for bound nuclei but breaks when proton-unbound nuclei are involved provides a quantitative basis for understanding limitations of the Kelson-Garvey mass predictions for proton-rich nuclei, as previously noted by Comay (1988) and Tian (2013). The Gamow shell model analysis demonstrates that the Thomas-Ehrman shift originates from reduced Coulomb energies due to extended radial density distributions, enabling future studies of isospin symmetry breaking near the proton dripline.
