# Quantum Interference in Jet Substructure from Spinning Gluons

> Collimated sprays of hadrons, called jets, are an emergent phenomenon of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at collider experiments, whose detailed internal structure encodes valuable information about the interactions of high energy quarks and gluons, and their confinement into color-neutral hadrons. The flow of energy within jets is characterized by correlation functions of energy flow operators, with the three-point correlator, being the first correlator with non-trivial shape dependence, playing a special role in unravelling the dynamics of QCD. In this Letter we initiate a study of the three-point energy correlator to all orders in the strong coupling constant, in the limit where two of the detectors are squeezed together. We show that by rotating the two squeezed detectors with respect to the third by an angle $ϕ$, a $\cos (2ϕ)$ dependence arising from the quantum interference between intermediate virtual gluons with $+/-$ helicity is imprinted on the detector. This can be regarded as a double slit experiment performed with jet substructure, and it provides a direct probe of the ultimately quantum nature of the substructure of jets, and of transverse spin physics in QCD. To facilitate our all-orders analysis, we adopt the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) for light-ray operators in conformal field theory and develop it in QCD. Our application of the light-ray OPE in real world QCD establishes it as a powerful theoretical tool with broad applications for the study of jet substructure.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the squeezed limit of the three-point energy correlator in QCD jets imprints a $\cos(2\phi)$ interference pattern on the detector, arising from quantum interference between intermediate virtual gluons with positive and negative helicity. This effect is presented as a direct probe of transverse spin physics in QCD, but the authors note that the interference terms are at the few percent level for inclusive jets due to cancellation between $g\to q\bar q$ and $g\to gg$ splittings, and can be enhanced to an $\mathcal{O}(1)$ effect with idealized b-tagging.

## Method
The authors perform an all-orders analysis of the three-point energy correlator $\langle \mathcal{E}(\hat n_1) \mathcal{E}(\hat n_2) \mathcal{E}(\hat n_3) \rangle$ in the squeezed limit where two detectors are brought together ($\hat n_2 \to \hat n_3$). They develop the light-ray Operator Product Expansion (OPE) for QCD, computing the $\mathcal{E}(\hat n_1) \mathcal{E}(\hat n_2)$ OPE and the $\mathcal{E}(\hat n_1) \mathbb{O}^{[J]}(\hat n_2)$ OPE as analytic functions of collinear spin $J$, then iterating these OPEs to describe the squeezed limit. The resummation of large logarithmic corrections is performed by solving renormalization group equations for the light-ray operators.

## Key numbers
- For quark jets at leading order: $\text{Sq}^{(0)}_q (\phi) = 10.54 + 0.1156 n_f + (0.1778 - 0.0593 n_f) \cos(2\phi)$, where $n_f$ is the number of light flavors.
- For gluon jets at leading order: $\text{Sq}^{(0)}_g (\phi) = (35.28 + 1.24 n_f) + (0.4 - 0.133 n_f) \cos(2\phi)$.
- The $\cos(2\phi)$ interference terms at leading order are identical for quark and gluon jets, with opposite signs for $g\to q\bar q$ (coefficient $-20/225$) and $g\to gg$ (coefficient $+10/225$).
- The leading twist OPE coefficients involve operators with collinear spin $J=3$ for the $\mathcal{E}(\hat n_1) \mathcal{E}(\hat n_2)$ OPE, and the iterated OPE closes onto operators $\vec{\mathbb{O}}^{[4]}(\hat n_1)$.
- Numerical predictions use $Q = 1$ TeV, $\theta_L^2 = 0.1$, and $\alpha_s(1\text{ TeV}) = 0.087$ with one-loop $\beta$-function.

## Context
This work extends the study of energy correlators in QCD, building on the two-point correlator (EEC) studied by Basham (1978), Konishi (1979), and Dixon (2019), and the three-point correlator introduced by Chen (2019). It applies the light-ray OPE formalism originally developed for conformal field theories by Hofman (2008) and Kravchuk (2018) to real-world QCD, establishing it as a tool for jet substructure. The results enable precision studies of transverse spin physics at the LHC without requiring polarized beams or hadrons, and provide an analytic framework for resumming spin interference effects that are not included in standard parton shower simulations like Herwig.
