# Parity violation in reactions with B- and Li- nuclei using polarized thermal neutrons

> Parity violation in the $(n,α)$ reactions on $^6$Li and $^{10}$B nuclei and in the $γ$-transition between two lowest levels of $^7$Li nucleus is discussed. It appears that the effects (neutral currents contribution in particular) are measurable due to the large values of the neutron cross sections. This holds promise for the theoretical extraction of the constants of the weak NN potential because of the relative simplicity of the investigated systems.

## Key claim

Parity-violating effects in the (n,α) reactions on ⁶Li and ¹⁰B and in the γ-transition in ⁷Li are measurable with modern experimental precision (order of several 1×10⁻⁸), despite the absence of parity-mixing doublets in these lightest nuclei, because the continuum mixing mechanism produces effects comparable to non-enhanced processes.

## Method

The authors measured P-odd asymmetries a_PV in the reactions ⁶Li(n,α)³H and ¹⁰B(n,α₀,₁)⁷Li using a polarized thermal neutron beam (polarization P=0.8, intensity 3×10¹⁰ n/s) from the WWR-M reactor at PNPI, with 24 double ionization chambers in a proportional chamber setup. For the γ-transition in ⁷Li (0.478 MeV, M1+E1), they used NaI(Tl) detectors and an integral current method with a natural boron target in a ⁶LiF box. Theoretically, they developed a shell-model expansion of the α+t continuum wave function within the orthogonality condition model to compute the P-odd asymmetry a_γ_PV in terms of weak NN coupling constants (h_π, h_ρ, h_ω).

## Key numbers

- a_α_PV for ⁶Li(n,α)³H: -(6.44 ± 5.50)×10⁻⁸
- a_α₁_PV for ¹⁰B(n,α₁)⁷Li: -(2.5 ± 1.6)×10⁻⁷
- a_α₀_PV for ¹⁰B(n,α₀)⁷Li: (3.4 ± 6.7)×10⁻⁷
- a_γ_PV for ⁷Li γ-transition (0.478 MeV): (6.8 ± 3.7)×10⁻⁷ (tentative measurement)
- Predicted a_γ_PV = -4.17×10⁻⁸ using Dubovik-Zenkin PV constants; -7.24×10⁻⁸ using DDH "best values"
- Neutral current contribution: -1.28×10⁻⁸ (30.6%) for Dubovik-Zenkin; -3.76×10⁻⁸ (51.9%) for DDH "best values"
- Effective energy distance ΔE_eff = 17.8 MeV for the 1/2⁻ excited state and 21.2 MeV for the 3/2⁻ ground state of ⁷Li
- Continuum integration required up to ~300 MeV for convergence

## Context

This work extends parity violation studies into the lightest nuclei region (A≤10), where previous efforts were limited by the absence of parity-mixing doublets that enhance effects in heavier nuclei. Prior work by Adelberger and Haxton (AH) established the importance of parity-mixing doublets, while calculations by Ryabov et al. (rb) and by Belyaev et al. (bdktt) showed the parity-forbidden α-decay of the 3.56 MeV level in ⁶Li was extremely small. The authors demonstrate that the ⁷Li γ-transition is the most promising case for extracting weak NN constants, and their theoretical approach using shell-model expansion of continuum wave functions can be extended to other cluster channels and direct reactions.
