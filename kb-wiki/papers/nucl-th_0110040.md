# Analysis of particle production in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions within a two-source statistical model

> The experimental data on hadron yields and ratios in central lead-lead and gold-gold collisions at 158 AGeV/$c$ (SPS) and $\sqrt{s} = 130$ AGeV (RHIC), respectively, are analysed within a two-source statistical model of an ideal hadron gas. A comparison with the standard thermal model is given. The two sources, which can reach the chemical and thermal equilibrium separately and may have different temperatures, particle and strangeness densities, and other thermodynamic characteristics, represent the expanding system of colliding heavy ions, where the hot central fireball is embedded in a larger but cooler fireball. The volume of the central source increases with rising bombarding energy. Results of the two-source model fit to RHIC experimental data at midrapidity coincide with the results of the one-source thermal model fit, indicating the formation of an extended fireball, which is three times larger than the corresponding core at SPS.

## Key claim

The two-source statistical model (TSM) improves agreement with experimental hadron yields and ratios at SPS energies compared to the standard single-source thermal model, but at RHIC energies the TSM fit coincides with the single-source fit, indicating the formation of an extended fireball whose volume is about three times larger than the corresponding core at SPS.

## Method

The authors fit a two-source statistical model of an ideal hadron gas to experimental data on hadron yields and ratios from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV/c (SPS) and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV (RHIC). The model divides the reaction volume into an inner hot source (source 2) and an outer cooler source (source 1), each in local thermal and chemical equilibrium with independent temperatures, volumes, baryon densities, and strangeness densities, constrained only by total strangeness conservation. Fits are performed including resonance decay feed-down but without strangeness suppression or excluded volume corrections, and results are compared to standard single-source model fits to the same data sets.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (Pb+Pb 158 AGeV/c): temperature of inner source (S2) = 155 MeV, outer source (S1) = 117 MeV; volume of inner source = 1705 fm^3 (0.7 V_0, where V_0 is the volume of a lead nucleus), outer source = 7250 fm^3 (3 V_0); net baryon density in outer source = 0.048 fm^{-3}, in inner source = 0.014 fm^{-3} (14 times larger in the halo)
- At SPS: chi^2/DOF = 16/5 for TSM versus 46/9 for single-source model (Table 1 data set)
- At RHIC (Au+Au sqrt(s)=130 AGeV): temperature of both sources approximately 186 MeV; total volume = 5157 fm^3, about three times larger than the SPS core volume of 1705 fm^3; chi^2/DOF = 9.6/7 for SM and 9.6/3 for TSM
- At RHIC: energy per particle E/N = 1.1 GeV, entropy per baryon S/A = 121
- Temperature of central fireball at RHIC varies from 176 MeV to 185 MeV depending on inclusion of h- multiplicity

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of hadron production by introducing a two-source scenario, motivated by experimental observations of decreasing antiproton-to-proton ratios with rapidity at SPS (NA49, NA52) and by microscopic cascade calculations (Bratkovskaya 1999, Soff 1999) showing a baryon-dilute central zone. It contrasts with single-source models that require strangeness suppression (Rafelski 1991, Becattini 1996), excluded volume effects (Rischke 1991, Yen 1999, Braun-Munzinger 1999), or chemical non-equilibrium of light quarks (Letessier and Rafelski 1999). The results enable future tests of whether the central fireball volume grows at the expense of the halo at higher RHIC energies (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV) and whether strangeness equilibration occurs separately in each source.
