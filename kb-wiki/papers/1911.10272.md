# Initial state fluctuations of QCD conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions

> We initialize the Quantum Chromodynamic conserved charges of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge arising from gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs for the initial conditions of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A new Monte Carlo procedure that can sample from a generic energy density profile is presented, called Initial Conserved Charges in Nuclear Geometry (ICCING), based on quark and gluon multiplicities derived within the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory. We find that while baryon number and electric charge have nearly identical geometries to the energy density profile, the initial strangeness distribution is considerable more eccentric and is produced primarily at the hot spots corresponding to temperatures of $T\gtrsim 400$ MeV for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that while the initial distributions of baryon number and electric charge closely track the bulk energy density geometry, the initial strangeness distribution is considerably more eccentric and is produced primarily at hot spots corresponding to temperatures above approximately 400 MeV for PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, with the caveat that this is a first 2D model of event-by-event sea quark fluctuations that has not yet been coupled to full hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo procedure called Initial Conserved Charges in Nuclear Geometry (ICCING) that samples gluons from a generic 2D energy density profile (using Trento for PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV), then applies probabilities for gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs of different flavors based on multiplicity ratios derived from the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory. The algorithm uses the saturation scale at each transverse point to determine the flavor chemistry ratios and a probability distribution for the spatial separation of the produced quark-antiquark pair, resulting in distributions of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge densities.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness production occurs primarily at energy densities above approximately 50 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to temperatures above 400 MeV for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- The RMS deviation in ellipticity between positive and negative charge for baryon number and electric charge is about 10% of the size of the bulk ellipticity
- The down quark mass used in the splitting probability calculation is m = 4.8 MeV, with representative values of quark momentum fraction alpha = 0.3 and saturation scale Q_s = 1.5 GeV
- The cutoff in the McLerran-Venugopalan model was taken to be Lambda = 1.2 MeV, with ratio Lambda/m = 0.0241

## Context

This work extends the understanding of event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions, which began with the recognition that such fluctuations are necessary to describe two-particle correlations (Takahashi, 2009) and triangular flow (Alver, 2010). Previous initial condition models at zero baryon chemical potential included energy density fluctuations (Schenke, 2011), initial flow (Gardim, 2011; Gale, 2012), and the full shear stress tensor (Schenke, 2019), but none incorporated the three QCD conserved charges of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge from sea quarks. The ICCING algorithm enables future studies of BSQ hydrodynamics and may help resolve tensions between light and strange particle yields (Floris, 2014; Adamczyk, 2017), fluctuations (Bellwied, 2018), and flow harmonics (Takeuchi, 2015; Almaalol, 2018), as well as provide a QCD contribution to charge splitting measurements relevant to the chiral magnetic effect.
