# A quark model analysis of the Sivers function

> We develop a formalism to evaluate the Sivers function. The approach is well suited for calculations which use constituent quark models to describe the structure of the nucleon. A non-relativistic reduction of the scheme is performed and applied to the Isgur-Karl model of hadron structure. The results obtained are consistent with a sizable Sivers effect and the signs for the u and d flavor contributions turn out to be opposite. This pattern is in agreement with the one found analyzing, in the same model, the impact parameter dependent generalized parton distributions. The Burkardt Sum Rule turns out to be fulfilled to a large extent. We estimate the QCD evolution of our results from the momentum scale of the model to the experimental one and obtain reasonable agreement with the available data.

## Key claim

The authors develop a formalism to calculate the Sivers function within constituent quark models, and applying it to the Isgur-Karl model yields a sizable Sivers effect with opposite signs for u and d quark flavors, consistent with the pattern found from impact parameter dependent generalized parton distributions in the same model, though the calculation relies on a non-relativistic reduction and one-gluon-exchange final state interactions.

## Method

The authors expand the gauge link in the definition of the Sivers function to first order in the strong coupling constant, then perform a non-relativistic reduction of the resulting one-gluon-exchange interaction operator. They evaluate the resulting expression using the Isgur-Karl constituent quark model wave function for the proton, which includes admixtures up to the 2ℏω shell, and apply DGLAP QCD evolution to evolve the results from the model scale to the experimental scale.

## Key numbers

- The Isgur-Karl model wave function coefficients are: a = 0.931, b = −0.274, c = −0.233, d = −0.067
- The harmonic oscillator parameter α² = mω is fixed to 1.23 fm⁻² to reproduce the slope of the proton charge form factor at zero momentum transfer
- In the Isgur-Karl model, ⟨k²⟩/m² ∼ 0.3, indicating small corrections from terms of O(k²/m²)

## Context

This work extends the successful application of constituent quark models from parton distributions (see e.g. Traini et al. (1997), H1 Collaboration) and generalized parton distributions (see e.g. Epelbaum et al. (2004), Boffi et al. (2005)) to the calculation of the Sivers function, which had previously been studied in quark-diquark models (Brodsky et al. (2002), Ji et al. (2003), Bacchetta et al. (2004)), the MIT bag model (Yuan (2003), D'Alesio et al. (2005)), and light-cone models (Lunghi et al. (2004)). The results enable comparison with SIDIS data from HERMES and COMPASS and provide a framework for testing the Burkardt Sum Rule and the relation between the Sivers function and impact parameter dependent GPDs.
