# Determination of Low-Energy Parameters of Neutron--Proton Scattering on the Basis of Modern Experimental Data from Partial-Wave Analyses

> The triplet and singlet low-energy parameters in the effective-range expansion for neutron--proton scattering are determined by using the latest experimental data on respective phase shifts from the SAID nucleon--nucleon database. The results differ markedly from the analogous parameters obtained on the basis of the phase shifts of the Nijmegen group and contradict the parameter values that are presently used as experimental ones. The values found with the aid of the phase shifts from the SAID nucleon--nucleon database for the total cross section for the scattering of zero-energy neutrons by protons, $σ_{0}=20.426 $b, and the neutron--proton coherent scattering length, $f=-3.755 $fm, agree perfectly with the experimental cross-section values obtained by Houk, $σ_{0}=20.436\pm 0.023 $b, and experimental scattering-length values obtained by Houk and Wilson, $f=-3.756\pm 0.009 $fm, but they contradict cross-section values of $σ_{0}=20.491\pm 0.014 $b according to Dilg and coherent-scattering-length values of $f=-3.7409\pm 0.0011 $fm according to Koester and Nistler.

# Determination of Low-Energy Parameters of Neutron–Proton Scattering on the Basis of Modern Experimental Data from Partial-Wave Analyses

## Key claim
The triplet and singlet low-energy parameters extracted from the SAID (GWU) partial-wave analysis differ markedly from those obtained from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis and contradict the parameter values currently used as experimental ones. The authors argue that the SAID-based values for the zero-energy total cross section and coherent scattering length agree with the experimental results of Houk (1971) and Houk and Wilson (1967, 1968) but disagree with the values of Dilg (1975) and Koester and Nistler (1975), implying that a reliable experimental determination of these quantities is needed to resolve which set of low-energy parameters is correct.

## Method
The authors used the effective-range expansion for neutron–proton scattering and fitted the function k cot delta at low energies with polynomials and Pade approximants via the least squares method to the experimental phase shifts from the SAID nucleon–nucleon database (GWU group) and from the Nijmegen group. From the fitted parameters they computed the triplet and singlet scattering lengths, effective ranges, and shape parameters, and then derived the total cross section at zero energy and the coherent scattering length using the standard spin-averaged formulas.

## Key numbers
- Triplet scattering length from GWU phase shifts: a_t = 5.4030 fm; from Nijmegen phase shifts: a_t = 5.420 fm.
- Singlet scattering length from GWU phase shifts: a_s = -23.719 fm; from Nijmegen phase shifts: a_s = -23.739 fm.
- Triplet effective range from GWU phase shifts: r_t = 1.7494 fm; from Nijmegen phase shifts: r_t = 1.753 fm.
- Singlet effective range from GWU phase shifts: r_s = 2.626 fm; from Nijmegen phase shifts: r_s = 2.678 fm.
- Triplet shape parameter from GWU phase shifts: v_{2t} = 0.163 fm^3; from Nijmegen phase shifts: v_{2t} = 0.040 fm^3.
- Singlet shape parameter from GWU phase shifts: v_{2s} = -0.005 fm^3; from Nijmegen phase shifts: v_{2s} = -0.48 fm^3.
- Zero-energy total cross section derived from GWU parameters: sigma_0 = 20.426 b; from Nijmegen parameters: sigma_0 = 20.473 b.
- Coherent scattering length derived from GWU parameters: f = -3.755 fm; from Nijmegen parameters: f = -3.7395 fm.
- The GWU-derived sigma_0 = 20.426 b agrees with the experimental value of Houk (1971): sigma_0 = 20.436 +/- 0.023 b.
- The GWU-derived f = -3.755 fm agrees with the experimental value of Houk and Wilson (1967, 1968): f = -3.756 +/- 0.009 fm.
- The Nijmegen-derived sigma_0 = 20.473 b agrees with the weighted mean of Houk and Dilg: sigma_0 = 20.476 +/- 0.012 b.
- The Nijmegen-derived f = -3.7395 fm agrees with the value of Koester and Nistler (1975): f = -3.7409 +/- 0.0011 fm.
- Correction delta r_t for GWU phase shifts: +0.0163 fm; for Nijmegen phase shifts: -0.001 fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Babenko and Petrov, 2005) of the discrepancy between low-energy parameters from the GWU and Nijmegen partial-wave analyses. It contradicts the widely used experimental parameter sets of Dumbrajs et al. (1983) and Machleidt (2001), which are based on the older cross-section and coherent-scattering-length measurements of Dilg (1975) and Koester and Nistler (1975). The results highlight that the Nijmegen analysis used those older experimental values as input, while the GWU analysis did not, making the GWU-derived parameters an independent determination. Resolving the ambiguity in the zero-energy cross section and coherent scattering length would enable an unambiguous choice between the two sets of low-energy parameters, which is important for constructing realistic nucleon–nucleon potentials and for calculating binding energies of few-nucleon systems such as H-3 and He-4.
