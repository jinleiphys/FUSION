# Baryon and Antibaryon production at RHIC energies in the Dual Parton Model

> We compute the mid-rapidity densities of pions, kaons, baryons and antibaryons in $Au$--$Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 130 GeV in the Dual Parton Model supplemented with final state interactions, and we present a comparison with available data.

## Key claim

The Dual Parton Model (DPM) supplemented with final state interactions can reproduce the mid-rapidity densities of baryons and antibaryons in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, but the agreement depends on introducing a diquark-breaking mechanism (baryon junction transfer) to account for stopping observed at SPS energies, and on using quark counting rules with a specific choice (I2 rather than I3) for net baryon production.

## Method

The authors compute mid-rapidity hadron densities using the DPM string model with shadowing corrections, then apply a diquark-breaking mechanism for net baryon production via baryon junction transfer. These densities serve as initial conditions for gain-and-loss differential equations describing final state interactions, including strangeness exchange reactions such as pi N <-> K Lambda(Sigma), pi Lambda(Sigma) <-> K Xi, and pi Xi <-> K Omega, along with their antiparticle counterparts.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, the average number of inelastic collisions in pp is k = 2
- Strangeness suppression factor S = 0.1 (S/L = 0.22) used in quark counting rules
- For centrality Nch/Nmaxch = 1/2, calculated ratios: anti-p/p = 0.69, anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.72, anti-Xi/Xi = 0.79, Omega/anti-Omega = 0.83
- Measured values for comparison: anti-p/p = 0.63 +/- 0.02 +/- 0.06, anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.73 +/- 0.03, anti-Xi/Xi = 0.83 +/- 0.03 +/- 0.05
- Anti-baryon/baryon ratios show a mild decrease with centrality of about 15% for all baryon species

## Context

This work extends the Dual Parton Model approach previously applied to SPS energies (Capella et al., 2001) to RHIC energies, using the same shadowing corrections and diquark-breaking mechanism. The results enable comparison with PHENIX and STAR data at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, and the enhancement pattern of strange baryons per participant is comparable to that found at SPS between pA and central PbPb collisions, with somewhat larger enhancement for antibaryons.
