# Support for the Jaffe-Wilczek Diquark Model of Pentaquarks

> I examine the diquark model of pentaquarks that was suggested by Jaffe and Wilczek. Based upon this model, I predict the states Theta(1530), N(1710), Sigma(1880) and Xi(1770) to be members of the same anti-decuplet. Moreover I predict the states N(1440), Lambda(1600), Sigma(1660) and Xi(1950) to be members of the corresponding octet.

# Support for the Jaffe-Wilczek Diquark Model of Pentaquarks

## Key claim
The paper argues that the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model successfully predicts the masses of several known pentaquark candidates, including the Theta(1530), N(1710), Sigma(1880), and Xi(1770) as members of an anti-decuplet, and the N(1440), Lambda(1600), Sigma(1660), and Xi(1950) as members of a corresponding octet; however, the parity of the Theta+ has not yet been experimentally determined, and the model assumes positive parity based on the known positive parity of the N(1710).

## Method
The author applies the mass formula proposed by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003), which treats pentaquarks as composed of two diquarks bound by an antiquark. The base mass M0 = 1440 MeV is taken from the Roper N(1440), with additional mass contributions of m_s = 95 MeV for each strange quark and m_alpha = 60 MeV for each diquark containing at least one strange quark. Predicted masses are compared to experimentally observed masses from multiple collaborations.

## Key numbers
- Theta(1530): predicted mass 1535 MeV (M0 + m_s = 1440 + 95), observed mass 1530 MeV
- N(1710): predicted mass 1690 MeV (M0 + 2m_s + m_alpha = 1440 + 190 + 60), observed mass 1710 MeV
- Sigma(1880): predicted mass 1845 MeV (M0 + 3m_s + 2m_alpha = 1440 + 285 + 120), observed mass 1880 MeV
- Xi(1770): predicted mass 1750 MeV (M0 + 2m_s + 2m_alpha = 1440 + 190 + 120), observed mass 1770 MeV
- N(1440): predicted mass 1440 MeV (M0), observed mass 1440 MeV
- Lambda(1600): predicted mass 1595 MeV (M0 + m_s + m_alpha = 1440 + 95 + 60), observed mass 1600 MeV
- Sigma(1660): predicted mass 1595 MeV (M0 + m_s + m_alpha = 1440 + 95 + 60), observed mass 1660 MeV
- Xi(1950): predicted mass 1940 MeV (M0 + 4m_s + 2m_alpha = 1440 + 380 + 120), observed mass 1950 MeV
- Theta+ mass range from experiments: 1526 to 1555 MeV, with width Gamma <= 20 MeV
- Theta+ originally predicted by Diakonov et al. (1997) with mass 1530 MeV and width Gamma <= 15 MeV

## Context
This work extends the diquark model of pentaquarks proposed by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003), which itself built on the chiral soliton model predictions of Diakonov et al. (1997) for the Theta+ pentaquark. The paper provides mass predictions that match several experimentally observed states, including the Theta(1530) confirmed by LEPS, DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR, HERMES, SVD, and COSY-TOF collaborations, and the Xi(1770) recently observed by the CLAS Collaboration. The model predicts the existence of the Xi--(1770) and Xi+(1770) and assigns J^P = 1/2^+ to the Theta(1530), Xi(1770), and Xi(1950), which can be tested by future parity measurements.
