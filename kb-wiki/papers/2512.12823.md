# Perturbative EFT calculation of the deuteron longitudinal response function

> In this work, we study the longitudinal response function of the deuteron up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory (Chiral EFT). We use an approach that maintains exact renormalization group (RG) invariance at each order of the EFT expansion by treating all subleading corrections in perturbation theory. To that end, we extent the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method to allow for such a perturbative treatment. In doing so, we further develop the existing work on strictly RG invariant Chiral EFT, which has so far focused primarily on binding energies and static properties, to inelastic processes. We carefully analyze the convergence properties of the theory and find good agreement with available experimental data. Our findings provide the foundation for similar studies of inelastic processes in a range of nuclei, based on perturbatively renormalized EFT schemes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the longitudinal response function of the deuteron can be calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory while maintaining exact renormalization group invariance at each order, but only by treating all subleading corrections in perturbation theory and extending the Lorentz Integral Transform method to accommodate this perturbative treatment.

## Method

The authors derive a perturbative expansion of the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) for the two-nucleon system, treating only the leading-order (LO) chiral potential nonperturbatively and including all higher-order corrections via distorted-wave perturbation theory. They solve inhomogeneous linear matrix-vector equations in momentum-space partial waves for each multipole of the charge-density operator, using the chiral potentials from Long (2011, 2012) and Wu (2018) and the electric charge operators from Shi (2022). The LIT equations at each perturbative order share the same kernel operator (G0 V^(0) - 1) and differ only in their inhomogeneous terms, allowing sequential evaluation.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal response function is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in chiral effective field theory, with the expansion parameter Q/M_hi where M_hi is the breakdown scale of the EFT.
* The pion mass is taken as m_pi = 138 MeV, the pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, the nucleon axial coupling constant g_A = 1.29, and the average nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV.
* Contact terms are determined by fitting to empirical phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis up to center-of-mass momentum k = 300 MeV.
* The OPE potential is treated nonperturbatively only in the 1S0, 3S1-3D1, and 3P0 channels, where it is attractive and singular.

## Context

This work extends the strictly renormalization-group-invariant chiral EFT approach previously developed for binding energies and static properties (Long 2011, 2012; Wu 2018) to inelastic processes, specifically deuteron electrodisintegration. It builds directly on Shi (2022), who studied static deuteron form factors with perturbative RG-invariant chiral potentials, and extends that work to include continuum final states. The LIT method was first introduced by Efros (1994) and reviewed by Efros (2007); the authors follow the formalism of Martinelli (1995) for three nucleons using the Faddeev approach. This work provides the foundation for similar studies of inelastic processes in a range of nuclei using perturbatively renormalized EFT schemes.
