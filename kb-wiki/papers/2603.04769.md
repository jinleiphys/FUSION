# Single-Particle Resonant States in Relativistic Hartree-Fock Theory: A Green's Function Approach

> Relativistic Hartree-Fock theory is combined with the Green's function method in coordinate space to study both single-particle bound and resonant states within a unified framework. Within this approach, single-particle resonance energies and widths are unambiguously extracted from the density of states, and the influence of the Coulomb exchange effects on proton resonances in $N=82$ isotones are systematically examined. It is found that the exact treatment of Coulomb exchange terms reduces proton resonance energies of approximate $0.09\sim0.21$ MeV, a significantly smaller effect than that obtained from the phenomenological treatment. Moreover, except for rather narrow resonances, the proton resonance widths are visibly reduced by the Coulomb exchange terms, also being much less pronounced than the phenomenological approach. Notably, clear shell effects are observed in the isotonic evolutions of the resonance energy reductions for specific resonances. All these highlight the necessity of a microscopical and exact treatment of the Coulomb exchange terms.

# Single-Particle Resonant States in Relativistic Hartree-Fock Theory: A Green's Function Approach

## Key claim

The exact treatment of Coulomb exchange terms within the relativistic Hartree-Fock Green's function (RHF-GF) framework reduces proton resonance energies by approximately 0.09 to 0.21 MeV in N=82 isotones, which is a significantly smaller effect than that obtained from phenomenological treatments; except for very narrow resonances, the proton resonance widths are also visibly reduced by the Coulomb exchange terms, again much less pronounced than in the phenomenological approach.

## Method

The authors combine relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory with the Green's function method in coordinate space to solve the spherical integro-differential Dirac equation, treating both bound and resonant states within a unified framework. Single-particle resonance energies and widths are extracted from the density of states (DoS) by locating poles of the Green's function in the complex energy plane, using the maximum of the DoS for energies and the positive-to-negative sign transition of the radial integration for half-widths. The non-local Fock terms are handled via equivalent localization based on the non-local density obtained from contour integration of the Green's function, with an inner iteration to ensure convergence of resonance energies and widths.

## Key numbers

* For $^{120}$Sn proton resonances, the RHF-GF method yields $E = 5.648$ MeV and $\Gamma = 0.017$ MeV for the $2f_{7/2}$ state, compared to $E = 6.205$ MeV and $\Gamma = 0.037$ MeV from the RMF-based GF method.
* For $^{120}$Sn, the $3p_{3/2}$ proton resonance is found at $E = 6.801$ MeV with $\Gamma = 0.617$ MeV, while the $3p_{1/2}$ resonance is at $E = 7.168$ MeV with $\Gamma = 0.933$ MeV.
* The $1h_{9/2}$ proton resonance in $^{120}$Sn has $E = 6.842$ MeV and $\Gamma = 0.002$ MeV, the narrowest resonance listed.
* The $2g_{9/2}$ proton resonance in $^{120}$Sn, obtained by the RHF-GF method, has $E = 13.117$ MeV and $\Gamma = 2.483$ MeV.
* The exact Coulomb exchange treatment reduces proton resonance energies in N=82 isotones by approximately $0.09\sim0.21$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function method previously applied within relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory (Sun 2016) to the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework, incorporating explicit Fock terms that are absent in RMF models. The study systematically examines Coulomb exchange effects on proton resonances in N=82 isotones, building on prior phenomenological analyses using the complex scaling method (Niu 2013, Guo 2014). The results demonstrate that exact treatment of Coulomb exchange terms, enabled by the RHF formalism, yields smaller corrections than phenomenological approaches, highlighting the necessity of microscopic treatment for precision studies of proton resonances relevant to proton halos, proton capture processes, and isospin symmetry-breaking corrections to super-allowed beta decays.
