# Excitation of nuclear anharmonic vibrations in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> We discuss the effects of multi-phonon excitations on heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier, focusing especially on the role of anharmonicities. We carry out a systematic study of those effects on the excitation function of the fusion cross section and on the fusion barrier distribution, by using the vibrational limit of the interacting boson model. We also analyze the recently measured high-precision data of the $^{16}$O + $^{148}$Sm fusion reaction with this model and discuss the anharmonic properties of the quadrupole as well as the octupole vibrations in $^{148}$Sm. Negative and positive static quadrupole moments are deduced for the first 2$^+$ and 3$^-$ states in $^{148}$Sm, respectively. It is shown that the fusion barrier distribution strongly depends on the sign of the quadrupole moments, suggesting that subbarrier fusion reactions offer an alternative method to extract the static quadrupole moments of phonon states in spherical nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that subbarrier heavy-ion fusion reactions, particularly the fusion barrier distribution, are highly sensitive to the sign of the static quadrupole moments of excited vibrational states in spherical nuclei, and that this sensitivity can be used as an alternative method to extract those quadrupole moments. The authors qualify that this method works when the anharmonic effects are not so large that the vibrational mode can still be described in terms of the U(5) limit of the interacting boson model.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for heavy-ion fusion reactions using the vibrational (U(5)) limit of the interacting boson model (IBM) to describe anharmonic quadrupole and octupole vibrations in the target nucleus. They extended the formalism to include all-order couplings (beyond the linear coupling approximation) and incorporated both quadrupole and octupole modes using the sdf-IBM framework. The model was applied to analyze high-precision fusion excitation function data for the 16O + 148Sm reaction, with the bare ion-ion potential fitted to experimental data in the 200-400 mb cross section range.

## Key numbers

* Quadrupole deformation parameter for 148Sm: β2 = 0.182, estimated from experimental B(E2) transition probability using target radius RT = 1.06 A1/3 fm.
* Octupole deformation parameter for 148Sm: β3 = 0.236, estimated from experimental E3 transition probability.
* Excitation energies in 148Sm: 0.55 MeV for the first 2+ state and 1.16 MeV for the first 3- state.
* For the model calculations with a fictitious nucleus: excitation energy of first 2+ state = 1 MeV, double-phonon states split by 0.5 MeV, total boson number N = 4, quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = 0.25, target radius RT = 6.35 fm, and χ2 = -3.
* Barrier distributions were obtained using a point-difference formula with ΔE = 2 MeV in the laboratory frame.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the 16O + 144Sm reaction (Hagino, Kuyucak, Takigawa, 1997), where negative quadrupole moments were extracted for both the first 2+ and 3- states in 144Sm. The paper builds on the barrier distribution method developed by Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991) and on earlier work showing that barrier distributions are sensitive to channel couplings (Leigh et al., 1995). The study enables future extraction of static quadrupole moments of phonon states in spherical nuclei through subbarrier fusion measurements, complementing traditional methods such as Coulomb excitation and proton scattering. The authors note that while anharmonic effects on proton scattering were studied by Clement, Pinder, and Masson (1982) and reorientation effects in heavy-ion scattering were examined by Takagaki et al. (1990), no systematic studies of anharmonicities on subbarrier fusion had been performed prior to this work.
