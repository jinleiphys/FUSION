# Hadron Mass Spectrum from Lattice QCD

> Finite temperature lattice simulations of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) are sensitive to the hadronic mass spectrum for temperatures below the "critical" temperature T_c ~ 160 MeV. We show that a recent precision determination of the QCD trace anomaly shows evidence for the existence of a large number of hadron states beyond those known from experiment. The lattice results are well represented by an exponentially growing hadron mass spectrum up to a temperature T = 155 MeV. Using simple parametrizations we show how one may estimate the total spectral weight in these yet undermined states.

# Hadron Mass Spectrum from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The lattice QCD trace anomaly data from Borsanyi (2010) are consistent with an exponentially growing hadron mass spectrum up to a temperature of 155 MeV, but the interaction measure loses sensitivity to hadron masses above 3 GeV, meaning the evidence for unknown states is limited to the mass range below that cutoff.

## Method

The authors computed the QCD interaction measure (trace anomaly) I(T) = (ε-3P)/T^4 from a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model using an exponential mass spectrum ρ(m) = c b e^{bm} with b = (252 MeV)^{-1} and c = 0.715, and compared it to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2010) for temperatures from 130 to 160 MeV. They also tested a Hagedorn-type spectrum ρ_H(m) = A e^{m/T_H} / (m^2 + m_0^2)^{5/4} with T_H = 180 MeV, A = 0.63 GeV^{3/2}, and m_0 = 0.5 GeV, and varied the upper mass cutoff m_c to determine the mass range to which the lattice data are sensitive.

## Key numbers

- The exponential mass spectrum parameter b = (252 MeV)^{-1} and c = 0.715.
- The Hagedorn spectrum parameters: T_H = 180 MeV, A = 0.63 GeV^{3/2}, m_0 = 0.5 GeV.
- The HRG model with only experimentally established states (795 total states: 23 non-strange mesons, 7 strange mesons, 19 non-strange baryons, 17 baryons with open strangeness) describes lattice data up to T ≈ 130 MeV.
- A mass cutoff of at least m_c = 2.5 GeV is needed to fit lattice results up to T = 158 MeV; there is practically no sensitivity to masses above 3 GeV.
- The exponential mass spectrum with an upper cutoff at m_c = 1.7 GeV reproduces the HRG prediction using only known hadrons.
- The Hagedorn spectrum requires a cutoff m_c = 1.9 GeV to match the known hadron spectrum.
- The lattice data deviate from the HRG prediction for T > 155 MeV, approaching the quasi-critical temperature T_c ≈ 160-165 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn hypothesis (Hagedorn, 1965) of an exponentially growing hadron mass spectrum by using modern lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2010) to provide quantitative evidence for unknown high-mass hadron states. It improves upon earlier studies by Karsch (2003) which used unphysically high quark masses or lattice actions with higher T_c, and complements work by Noronha-Hostler (2007-2009) on high-mass resonance contributions to transport properties. The results enable future lattice simulations with higher precision to determine the hadron mass spectrum beyond the experimentally accessible range, and the authors propose extending the analysis to finite baryon chemical potential to separate mesonic and baryonic contributions, following the approach of Chatterjee (2009).
