# Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space with cross-ladder kernel

> A new method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation is developed. It allows to find the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes both in Minkowski and in Euclidean spaces and, as a by product, the light-front wave function. The method is valid for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs. Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front equations for scalar particles with ladder + cross-ladder kernel are solved.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space without using Wick rotation, which is valid for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs; the method is demonstrated for scalar particles with ladder plus cross-ladder kernel, and the authors claim that for a given kernel, Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front approaches give very close results, though the first is always slightly more attractive.

## Method

The method projects the Bethe-Salpeter equation onto the light-front plane using an integral transform that removes singularities, then expresses the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude via the Nakanishi integral representation to obtain a closed-form equation for the weight function g(γ,z). The resulting equation is solved numerically for scalar particles with zero total angular momentum, using ladder and cross-ladder kernels, and the weight function is used to reconstruct the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in both Minkowski and Euclidean spaces as well as the light-front wave function.

## Key numbers

- For ladder kernel with exchange mass μ=0.15 and constituent mass m=1, coupling constant α = 0.5716 at binding energy B=0.01, and α = 5.315 at B=1.00
- For ladder kernel with μ=0.50 and m=1, coupling constant α = 1.440 at B=0.01, and α = 6.712 at B=1.00
- For ladder+cross-ladder kernel with μ=0.50 and m=1, coupling constant α = 1.21 at B=0.01, and α = 4.56 at B=1.00 (BS results, accuracy 1%)
- For ladder+cross-ladder+stretched-box kernel in LFD with μ=0.50 and m=1, coupling constant α = 1.21 at B=0.01, and α = 4.91 at B=1.00
- The stretched-box contribution to binding energy is smaller than 2% and attractive

## Context

This work extends the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) and the Nakanishi integral representation (Nakanishi 1963) to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with cross-ladder kernel in Minkowski space. It builds on prior work by Karmanov and collaborators (2004, 2005) and provides a method that can be generalized to non-zero angular momentum and fermion systems. The results enable direct calculation of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Minkowski space for more complicated interactions, which was previously limited to Euclidean space solutions that could not be used for calculating physical observables like form factors.
