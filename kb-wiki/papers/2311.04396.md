# Viscosity of non equilibrium hot $\&$ dense QCD drop formed at LHC

> We compute the bulk, $ζ$, and shear, $η$, viscosity over entropy density, $s$, for the QCD matter formed in small collision systems at LHC. We consider a scenario of the String Percolation Model by proposing a global form of the color reduction factor that describes both the thermodynamic limit and its maximum deviation due to small-bounded effects. Our method involves estimations at vanishing baryon-chemical potential, assuming local equilibrium for string clusters in the initial state. To compute $η/s$, we employed a kinetic approach that accounts QCD states as an ideal gas of partons, while $ζ/s$ is computed by using two different approaches: a simple kinetic formula and the causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics formulation. Our results align with Lattice QCD computations and Bayesian methods and are consistent with holographic conjecture bounds. Furthermore, our findings support the notion of a strongly interacting medium, similar to that observed in nuclear collisions, albeit with a phase transition occurring outside the thermodynamic limit.

## Key claim

The authors compute bulk and shear viscosity over entropy density for QCD matter formed in small collision systems at LHC, using a modified String Percolation Model that accounts for finite-size effects through a new color reduction factor, with results that align with Lattice QCD and Bayesian methods but show that the phase transition occurs outside the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors propose a global form of the color reduction factor \(F_s(\xi)\) that interpolates between the thermodynamic limit and its maximum deviation due to small-bounded effects, using two weight parameters fitted to simulation results. They compute \(\eta/s\) via a kinetic approach treating QCD states as an ideal gas of partons, and \(\zeta/s\) using two approaches: a simple kinetic formula and the causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics (CDRF) formulation, all at vanishing baryon-chemical potential assuming local equilibrium for string clusters in the initial state.

## Key numbers

* The critical string density is taken as \(\xi_c = 1.128\), with critical temperature \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV from Lattice QCD.
* The minimum \(\eta/s\) for the thermodynamic limit is \(0.190018\) at \(T/T_c = 1.13187\); for the non-thermodynamic limit it is \(0.270179\) at \(T/T_c = 1.22508\), an increase by a factor of \(1.4218\).
* The energy density scaling constant is \(\varsigma = \varepsilon_c / \xi_c = 0.5601\) GeV/fm\(^3\), with a shift in critical temperature \(T_c(F_s)/T_c(F) = 1.096\) relative to the thermodynamic limit.
* The weight parameters for the color reduction factor are \(m = 0.7714731 \pm 0.01468\) (thermodynamic limit contribution) and \(c = 0.0609589 \pm 0.007527\) (deviation from thermodynamic limit).
* The trace anomaly maximum is located at \(T/T_c = 1.13621\) in the thermodynamic limit and at \(T/T_c = 1.21918\) for the non-thermodynamic limit.

## Context

This work extends the String Percolation Model (SPM) framework previously applied to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (Bautista 2011, 2012, 2013; Braun 1999, 2015) by incorporating finite-size effects relevant for small collision systems like \(pp\) collisions, building on studies of clustering with finite size and initial geometry effects (Ramirez 2017, 2019). The results are compared with Lattice QCD computations using p4 and asqtad actions (Bazavov 2009), HISQ action (HotQCD 2014), and Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration (Borsanyi 2013), as well as Bayesian method results (Bernhard 2019) and holographic bounds (Policastro 2001; Buchel 2007). The approach enables computation of transport coefficients for non-thermal equilibrium systems and provides a framework for studying bulk properties near the critical temperature in small collision systems.
