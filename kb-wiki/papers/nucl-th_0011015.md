# Isospin effects on squeeze-out flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The squeeze-out flow in reactions of $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn and $^{124}$Ba + $^{124}$Ba at different incident energies for different impact parameters is investigated by means of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. For the first time, it is found that the more neutron-rich system ($% ^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn) exhibits weaker squeeze-out flow. This isospin dependence of the squeeze-out flow is shown to mainly result from the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross section and the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

Using an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model, the authors find for the first time that the more neutron-rich system (124Sn + 124Sn) exhibits weaker squeeze-out flow than the less neutron-rich system (124Ba + 124Ba), with this isospin dependence shown to mainly result from the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section and the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors employed an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model that includes symmetry energy, Coulomb interaction, isospin-dependent experimental nucleon-nucleon cross sections, and isospin-dependent Pauli blocking. They simulated reactions of 124Sn + 124Sn and 124Ba + 124Ba at various incident energies and impact parameters, using a soft equation of state with incompressibility K=200 MeV and symmetry strength C=32 MeV. The squeeze-out flow strength was quantified by the ratio R_N extracted from Legendre polynomial fits to the azimuthal distribution of mid-rapidity nucleons with normalized transverse momentum Pt/Pproj >= 0.5.

## Key numbers

- For 124Sn + 124Sn at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.086 +/- 0.036 (with C=32 MeV and experimental N-N cross section)
- For 124Ba + 124Ba at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.155 +/- 0.041 (with C=32 MeV and experimental N-N cross section)
- For 124Sn + 124Sn at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.066 +/- 0.029 (with C=0 and experimental N-N cross section)
- For 124Ba + 124Ba at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.137 +/- 0.034 (with C=0 and experimental N-N cross section)
- For 124Sn + 124Sn at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.047 +/- 0.021 (with C=32 MeV and Cugnon N-N cross section)
- For 124Ba + 124Ba at 350 MeV/nucleon and b=6 fm: R_N = 1.066 +/- 0.023 (with C=32 MeV and Cugnon N-N cross section)
- The largest R_N value was obtained at impact parameter b=6 fm (semi-central collisions) for both systems at 350 MeV/nucleon
- The normal nuclear matter density used was rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions, including directed flow (Li et al., 1996; Pak et al., 1997; Chen et al., 1998), radial flow (Chen et al., 1999), and rotational flow (Chen et al., 2000), by providing the first theoretical investigation of isospin effects on squeeze-out flow. The results suggest that the isospin dependence of squeeze-out flow could serve as a probe of the isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, and the findings are consistent with experimental observations of squeeze-out flow in Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon (Bastid et al., 1997; Crochet et al., 1997).
