# High-resolution $(p,t)$ study of low-spin states in $^{240}$Pu: Octupole excitations, $α$ clustering, and other structure features

> A high-resolution $(p,t)$ experiment at $E_{p}$= 24 MeV was performed to populate low-spin states in the actinide nucleus ${}^{240}$Pu. The Q3D magnetic spectrograph of the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratory (MLL) in Munich (Germany) was used to identify the ejected tritons via $dE/E$ particle identification with its focal-plane detection system. Angular distributions were measured at nine different Q3D angles to assign spin and parity to the excited states based on a comparison with coupled-channels DWBA calculations. In total, 209 states have been excited in $^{240}$Pu up to an excitation energy of 3 MeV. Many previously known states have also been observed and their spin-parity assignments were confirmed. However, many of the populated states have been seen for the first time. The low-spin one-octupole phonon excitations, i.e. $K^π = 0^-,1^-,2^-,3^-$, could be observed and a new candidate for the $K = 3$ projection is proposed. Furthermore, the double-octupole or $α$-cluster structure of the $0^+_2$ state in $^{240}$Pu has been studied in more detail. It is shown that the $0^+_2$ state in $^{230}$Th has a distinctly different structure. In addition, strongly excited $1^-$ states have been observed at 1.5\,MeV and 1.8\,MeV in $^{240}$Pu. The present study suggests that similar states might be observed in $^{230}$Th. At least two different and distinct structures for $J^π = 0^+$ states are present in the actinides. These are pairing states and states with enhanced octupole correlations. We have shown that it is crucial to consider negative-parity single-particle states being admixed to some $K^π = 0^+_2$ rotational bands to understand the $α$-decay hindrance factors and enhanced $E1$-decay rates. Based on our analysis, we have identified the double-octupole or $α$-cluster $K^π = 0^+$ candidates from $^{224}$Ra to $^{240}$Pu.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at least two distinct structures exist for \(J^\pi = 0^+\) states in the actinides, pairing states and states with enhanced octupole correlations, and that the \(0^+_2\) state in \(^{240}\)Pu is a candidate for a double-octupole or \(\alpha\)-cluster structure, while the \(0^+_2\) state in \(^{230}\)Th has a distinctly different structure, with the qualification that negative-parity single-particle states must be admixed to some \(K^\pi = 0^+_2\) rotational bands to explain \(\alpha\)-decay hindrance factors and enhanced \(E1\)-decay rates.

## Method

A high-resolution \((p,t)\) experiment was performed at \(E_p = 24\) MeV using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratory (MLL) in Munich, with tritons identified via \(dE/E\) particle identification. Angular distributions were measured at nine laboratory angles from \(5^\circ\) to \(40^\circ\) and compared to coupled-channels DWBA calculations using the CHUCK3 code to assign spin and parity to excited states in \(^{240}\)Pu.

## Key numbers

* 209 states were excited in \(^{240}\)Pu up to an excitation energy of 3 MeV.
* 15 new and firmly assigned \(J^\pi = 0^+\) states were identified.
* The \(0^+_2\) state was observed at 861.2(1) keV with an integrated \((p,t)\) cross section of 33.69(3) \(\mu\)b and a ratio \(\sigma_{\mathrm{exp}}/\sigma_{\mathrm{DWBA}} = 1.4\).
* Strongly excited \(1^-\) states were observed at 1540.1(1) keV and 1807.4(2) keV.
* The ground-state rotational band members were observed at 0.0 keV (\(0^+\)), 42.0(1) keV (\(2^+\)), 141.5(1) keV (\(4^+\)), and 293.5(6) keV (\(6^+\)).
* The one-octupole phonon excitations include \(K^\pi = 0^-\) at 597.2(4) keV (\(1^-\)), \(K^\pi = 1^-\) at 648.8(4) keV (\(3^-\)), and a new candidate for \(K = 3\) projection.
* The energy difference between the double-octupole candidate and a quadrupole-type excitation in \(^{240}\)Pu is \(\Delta E_x = 230\) keV.

## Context

This work completes a series of \((p,t)\) experiments on actinide nuclei, extending prior studies on \(^{230}\)Th (Levon 2009), \(^{228}\)Th (Levon 2013), and \(^{232}\)U (Levon 2015). It builds on the observation of enhanced \(E1\) transitions in \(^{240}\)Pu from Coulomb excitation experiments (Wang 2009) and supports the hypothesis of double-octupole phonon bands or \(\alpha\)-cluster structures, while also addressing the long-standing debate on pairing isomers introduced by Ragnarsson and Broglia (1976). The results enable further investigation of octupole correlations and \(\alpha\)-clustering across the actinide region from \(^{224}\)Ra to \(^{240}\)Pu.
