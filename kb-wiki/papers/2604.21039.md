# Conformal prediction for uncertainties in the neutron star equation of state

> We study uncertainties in the equation of state of neutron stars using conformal prediction as a distribution-free and model-agnostic method that provides coverage guarantees. In particular, we apply the Conformalized Quantile Regression (CQR) method to posterior samples calculated from Bayesian inference, creating reliable uncertainty bands without assuming a specific form of the underlying distribution. We first construct CQR bands as a postprocessing step to the posterior samples of neutron star mas-radius relations provided by the NMMA collaboration and to Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of pure neutron matter. In all cases, empirical coverage studies confirm the robustness of the method.

## Key claim

Conformalized Quantile Regression (CQR), applied as a postprocessing step to Bayesian posterior samples, provides distribution-free and model-agnostic uncertainty bands for the neutron star equation of state that achieve guaranteed marginal coverage without assuming the underlying distribution shape, but this guarantee relies on the exchangeability of the data.

## Method

The authors applied split Conformalized Quantile Regression (CQR) to posterior samples from Bayesian inference, first using a toy model with a polytropic equation of state and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, then as a postprocessing step to posterior samples of neutron star mass-radius relations from the NMMA collaboration and to Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of pure neutron matter. The data was split into training and calibration sets; quantile regression estimated conditional lower and upper quantiles, and conformity scores from the calibration set were used to expand the prediction intervals to achieve the target coverage level. Empirical coverage was evaluated by repeatedly splitting the data into training, calibration, and validation sets and checking whether the CQR bands contained the validation points.

## Key numbers

- For the toy model polytropic EOS at a fixed mass-density of 2.0 × 10^14 g/cm^3, empirical coverage closely followed the ideal line across the full range of 1-α, remaining within the 68% (1σ) and 95% (1.96σ) confidence bands over n_split=1000 independent random splits.
- For the NMMA EOS samples based on chiral EFT without additional filtering, the 90% CQR interval for the radius at 1.4 M⊙ was 11.35_{-1.80}^{+1.74} km.
- After imposing the maximum-mass constraint (M_max ≤ 2.16^{+0.17}_{-0.15} at 2σ uncertainty), the 90% CQR interval for the radius at 1.4 M⊙ narrowed to 11.27_{-1.44}^{+1.40} km.
- With the full set of astrophysical constraints (maximum mass, NICER observation of PSR J0030+0451, GW170817, AT2017gfo, GRB170817A), the 90% CQR interval for the radius at 1.4 M⊙ narrowed further to 11.73_{-0.72}^{+0.80} km.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior application of conformal prediction to nucleon-nucleon scattering observables (Dezdarani 2026) into the domain of neutron star equation of state inference. It complements Bayesian approaches used by the NMMA collaboration (Dietrich 2020) and Quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Armstrong 2025) by providing finite-sample, model-agnostic prediction intervals with guaranteed coverage, without replacing Bayesian methods. The approach enables more reliable uncertainty quantification for neutron star observables that does not depend on distributional assumptions, which is motivated by the non-Gaussian features revealed in Q-Q plots of the radius distributions at fixed mass.
