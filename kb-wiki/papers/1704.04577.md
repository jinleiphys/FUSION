# A Unified Description for Comprehensive Sets of Jet Energy Loss Observables with CUJET3

> Jet energy loss in heavy ion collisions, as quantified by the traditional observable of high $p_T$ hadron's nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$, provides highly informative "imaging" of the hot medium created in heavy ion collisions. There are now comprehensive sets of available data, from average suppression to azimuthal anisotropy, from light to heavy flavors, from RHIC 200GeV to LHC 2.76TeV as well as 5.02TeV collisions. A unified description of such comprehensive data presents a stringent vetting of any viable model for jet quenching phenomenology. In this contribution we report such a systematic and successful test of CUJET3, a jet energy loss simulation framework built upon a nonperturbative microscopic model for the hot medium as a semi-quark-gluon-monopole plasma (sQGMP) which integrates two essential elements of confinement, i.e. the Polyakov-loop suppression of quarks/gluons and emergent magnetic monopoles.

## Key claim

The CUJET3 jet energy loss simulation framework, built on a nonperturbative semi-quark-gluon-monopole plasma (sQGMP) model, successfully provides a unified description of comprehensive jet quenching data across multiple observables, collision energies, and hadron flavors, but only after its two key parameters are constrained by a quantitative chi-squared analysis using light flavor data.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic chi-squared per degree of freedom analysis to constrain two key CUJET3 model parameters: the QCD running coupling at the nonperturbative scale Tc (alpha_c) and the coefficient for magnetic screening mass (c_m). They compared CUJET3 simulations against experimental data for high pT pion R_AA and v_2 from central and semi-central collisions at RHIC 200 GeV, LHC 2.76 TeV, and LHC 5.02 TeV. They then used the parameter-constrained model to make independent predictions for heavy flavor D meson R_AA and v_2 at LHC 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Optimal parameter region spanned by (alpha_c = 0.8, c_m = 0.22) and (alpha_c = 1.0, c_m = 0.28) with chi-squared per degree of freedom close to unity, from combined R_AA and v_2 data for pions at RHIC 200 GeV, LHC 2.76 TeV, and LHC 5.02 TeV
- CUJET3 compared with data at three collision energies: RHIC 200 GeV, LHC 2.76 TeV, and LHC 5.02 TeV
- Heavy flavor predictions tested at LHC 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV for D meson R_AA and v_2

## Context

This work extends previous CUJET3 studies (Xu 2014, Xu 2015) that had already shown success with 2.76 TeV and 200 GeV data, now adding successful tests against 5.02 TeV data and heavy flavor observables. The study addresses challenges identified in prior work: azimuthal anisotropy was found to pose severe challenges for models and hint at nontrivial temperature dependence of jet-medium coupling (Liao 2008, Zhang 2012, Das 2015); beam-energy dependence suggested reduction of average medium opaqueness from RHIC to LHC (Horowitz 2011, Betz 2012, Zhang 2012, Burke 2013); and the proximity between light and heavy flavor energy loss indicated the need for elastic energy loss and magnetic screening (Buzzatti 2011, Xu 2014). The results enable future event-by-event jet energy loss studies to quantify higher harmonic anisotropy coefficients of high pT hadrons.
