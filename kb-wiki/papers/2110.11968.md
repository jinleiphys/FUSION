# Jet Launching from Merging Magnetized Binary Neutron Stars with Realistic Equations of State

> We perform general relativistic, magnetohydrodynamic (GRMHD) simulations of binary neutron stars in quasi-circular orbit that merge and undergo delayed or prompt collapse to a black hole (BH). The stars are irrotational and modeled using an SLy or an H4 nuclear equation of state. To assess the impact of the initial magnetic field configuration on jet launching, we endow the stars with a purely poloidal magnetic field that is initially unimportant dynamically and is either confined to the stellar interior or extends from the interior into the exterior as in typical pulsars. Consistent with our previous results, we find that only the BH + disk remnants originating from binaries that form hypermassive neutron stars (HMNSs) and undergo delayed collapse can drive magnetically-powered jets. We find that the closer the total mass of the binary is to the threshold value for prompt collapse, the shorter is the time delay between the gravitational wave peak amplitude and jet launching. This time delay also strongly depends on the initial magnetic field configuration. We also find that seed magnetic fields confined to the stellar interior can launch a jet over $\sim 25\,\rm ms$ later than those with pulsar-like magnetic fields. The lifetime of the jet [$Δt\lesssim 150\,\rm ms$] and its outgoing Poynting luminosity [$L_{\rm EM}\sim 10^{52\pm 1}\rm erg/s$] are consistent with typical short gamma-ray burst central engine lifetimes, as well as with the Blandford--Znajek mechanism for launching jets and their associated Poynting luminosities. Our numerical results also suggest that the dynamical ejection of matter can be enhanced by the magnetic field. Therefore, GRMHD studies are required to fully understand kilonova signals from GW170818-like events.

## Key claim

Incipient, magnetically-powered jets are launched only from BH + disk remnants that originate from binaries forming hypermassive neutron stars (HMNSs) and undergoing delayed collapse, regardless of the nuclear equation of state (SLy or H4) used; the time delay between gravitational wave peak and jet launching depends strongly on both the binary total mass relative to the prompt collapse threshold and the initial magnetic field configuration.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic (GRMHD) simulations of irrotational binary neutron stars in quasi-circular orbit, using piecewise polytropic representations of the SLy and H4 nuclear equations of state. They seeded the stars with a dynamically unimportant, purely poloidal magnetic field that was either confined to the stellar interior or extended into the exterior as in pulsars, and evolved the systems through merger and either delayed or prompt collapse to a black hole, monitoring jet formation, Poynting luminosity, and dynamical ejecta.

## Key numbers

- Jet lifetime: Δt ≲ 150 ms, consistent with short gamma-ray burst central engine lifetimes.
- Outgoing Poynting luminosity: L_EM ∼ 10^(52±1) erg/s.
- For an H4 configuration with HMNS lifetime τ_HMNS ∼ 2.5 ms (H4M3.0P), jet launched ∼19 ms after BH formation; for H4M2.8P (τ_HMNS ∼ 9.6 ms), jet launched ∼27 ms after BH formation.
- For an SLy binary with pulsar-like magnetic field (SLyM2.7P), jet launched ∼20 ms after BH formation; with interior-confined field (SLyM2.7I), jet launched ∼60 ms after BH formation.
- Bolometric kilonova luminosity: L_knova = 10^(40.6±0.5) erg/s, with rise times τ_peak ∼ 0.4–5.1 days and effective temperature T_peak ∼ 10^3.5 K, implying peak wavelength λ_peak ∼ 730–1830 nm.
- Magnetic field imprints on gravitational waves detectable by aLIGO/Virgo/KAGRA only for events within ≲ 6.0 Mpc; detectable with next-generation observatories (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) within ≲ 50 Mpc with SNR ≳ 30.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GRMHD simulations using a simple Γ=2 polytropic equation of state (Ruiz 2016, Ruiz 2017) to realistic nuclear equations of state (SLy and H4), confirming that jet launching from delayed collapse remnants is robust across EOS choices. It contrasts with earlier studies (Kawamura 2016, Ciolfi 2017) that found no magnetically-driven outflows in similar setups, likely due to insufficient evolution time or resolution, and with Kiuchi (2014) which found no jets after ∼39 ms post-merger. The results enable quantitative predictions for kilonova signatures from GW170817-like events and establish that GRMHD modeling is necessary to interpret such multimessenger observations.
