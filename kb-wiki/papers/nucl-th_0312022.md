# Exploring the nucleus in the context of low-energy QCD

> These lecture notes address a central problem of theoretical nuclear physics: how to establish a relationship between low-energy, non-perturbative QCD and nuclear phenomenology which includes both nuclear matter and finite nuclei. We develop a microscopic covariant description of nuclear many-body dynamics constrained by chiral symmetry and in-medium QCD sum rules. A relativistic point-coupling model is derived, based on an effective Lagrangian with density-dependent contact interactions between nucleons. These interactions are constructed from chiral one- and two-pion exchange, combined with the large isoscalar nucleon self-energies that arise through changes in the quark condensate and the quark density at finite baryon density. Nuclear binding and saturation are almost completely generated by chiral (two-pion exchange) fluctuations in combination with Pauli effects, whereas strong scalar and vector fields of about equal magnitude and opposite sign, induced by changes of the QCD vacuum in the presence of baryonic matter, generate the large effective spin-orbit potential in finite nuclei. Promising results are found for the nuclear matter equation of state and for the bulk and single-nucleon properties of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear binding and saturation arise almost completely from chiral (two-pion exchange) fluctuations combined with Pauli blocking effects, while the large scalar and vector fields of approximately equal magnitude and opposite sign, induced by changes in the QCD vacuum at finite baryon density, generate the strong spin-orbit potential in finite nuclei. The authors hedge that this is a "conjecture" establishing links between low-energy QCD and the nuclear many-body problem, with "promising" rather than definitive results.

## Method

The authors develop a microscopic covariant description of nuclear many-body dynamics constrained by chiral symmetry and in-medium QCD sum rules. They derive a relativistic point-coupling model based on an effective Lagrangian with density-dependent contact interactions between nucleons, constructed from chiral one- and two-pion exchange combined with large isoscalar nucleon self-energies arising from changes in the quark condensate and quark density at finite baryon density. The model is then applied to calculate nuclear matter equation of state and bulk and single-nucleon properties of finite nuclei from ¹⁶O to ⁹⁰Zr.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = (92.4 ± 0.3) MeV
- Quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ −(240 MeV)³ ≈ −1.8 fm⁻³
- Nucleon sigma term: σ_N = (45 ± 8) MeV from dispersion relation analysis; lattice QCD extrapolation gives σ_N = (47 ± 3) MeV
- Axial vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.267 ± 0.003
- Baryon density in bulk of heavy nucleus: ρ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Pion mass: m_π related to quark masses via GOR relation m²_π = −(1/f²)(m_u + m_d)⟨q̄q⟩ + O(m²_u,d)
- Quark masses: m_u + m_d ≈ 12 MeV at renormalisation scale of order 1 GeV
- ρ meson mass: m_ρ ≈ 0.77 GeV; a₁ meson mass: m_a₁ ≈ 1.23 GeV

## Context

These lecture notes extend the framework of Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (Serot & Walecka, 1997; Ring, 1996) by incorporating explicit chiral symmetry constraints from low-energy QCD. The work builds on in-medium chiral perturbation theory and finite-density QCD sum rules (Cohen, Furnstahl & Griegel, 1991; Drukarev & Levin, 1990; Jin, 1994) to derive the large scalar and vector nucleon self-energies from changes in the quark condensate, rather than treating them phenomenologically through ad-hoc sigma and omega meson exchange. This enables a first-principles connection between QCD vacuum structure and nuclear phenomenology, with the next step being application to a wider range of nuclei and further refinement of the density-dependent coupling strengths.
