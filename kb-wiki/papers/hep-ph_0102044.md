# Connection between Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Deconfinement

> We propose a simple explanation for the connection between chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in QCD at high temperature. In the Higgs description of the QCD vacuum both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and effective gluon masses are generated by the condensate of a color octet quark-antiquark pair. The transition to the high temperature state proceeds by the melting of this condensate. Quarks and gluons become (approximately) massless at the same critical temperature. For instanton-dominated effective multiquark interactions and three light quarks with equal mass we find a first order phase transition at a critical temperature around 170 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and effective gluon masses (confinement) in the QCD vacuum are generated by the same condensate of a color octet quark-antiquark pair, so that the melting of this condensate at high temperature causes chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement to occur at the same critical temperature. The author finds a first-order phase transition for three light quarks with equal mass, but notes that the result depends on the specific form of the effective multiquark interactions and the treatment of thermal fluctuations.

## Method

The author computes the temperature-dependent effective potential for the octet condensate using a mean-field-type approximation that includes thermal fluctuations from gluons, quarks, and pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons. The vacuum potential is modeled using instanton-induced effective multiquark interactions, with the octet condensate generating masses for gluons (via the Higgs mechanism) and quarks (via Yukawa coupling). The critical temperature is determined by finding where the minima of the effective potential at nonzero and zero condensate become degenerate.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for three massless quarks with instanton-induced potential (A): \(T_c = 154\) MeV
- Critical temperature for three light quarks with equal mass (average pseudoscalar mass \(M_{PS} = 390\) MeV): \(T_c = 170\) MeV
- Range of critical temperatures for three massless quarks across different vacuum potentials: \(T_c \approx 130-160\) MeV
- Vacuum octet condensate: \(\chi_0 \approx 130\) MeV (from \(\rho\)-meson mass 770 MeV and gauge coupling \(g(770\ \text{MeV}) = 6\))
- \(\eta^\prime\)-meson mass: \(M_{\eta^\prime} = 960\) MeV; its decay constant: \(f_\theta \approx 150\) MeV
- Relation for critical temperature in chiral limit: \(T_c^4 = 0.013 M_{\eta^\prime}^2 f_\theta^2 R^{-1/4}\) with \(R < 1\) a constant of order one
- Higgs contribution to vector meson mass slightly below \(T_c\) for potential (A): \(\bar\mu_\rho^{(SSB)}(T_c) = 290\) MeV
- Higgs contribution to baryon mass slightly below \(T_c\) for potential (A): \(M_q^{(SSB)}(T_c) = 580\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the Higgs picture of the QCD vacuum proposed by Wetterich (1999) and BerW, where a color octet condensate spontaneously breaks color symmetry and generates gluon masses via the Higgs mechanism, with gluons identified as vector mesons (\(\rho, K^*, \omega\)) and quarks as baryons. The paper addresses the longstanding puzzle, noted in lattice simulations (LATB, lattice, KLP), that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur at the same temperature, which previous analytical approaches such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (NJL) could not simultaneously describe. The results enable a quantitative analytical description of the QCD phase transition that can be tested against lattice data and heavy-ion collision experiments (EXP), and suggest a possible lattice test via the existence of stable \(Z_3\)-vortices in the low-temperature phase.
