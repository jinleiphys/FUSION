# Thermal modifications of charmonia and bottomonia from spatial correlation functions

> We present our study on the thermal modifications of charmonia and bottomonia from spatial correlation functions at zero and nonzero momenta in quenched QCD. To accommodate the heavy quarks on the lattice we performed simulations on very fine lattices at a fixed beta value corresponding to a lattice spacing $a^{-1}=22.8$ GeV on $192^3\times32$, $192^3\times48$, $192^3\times56$, $192^3\times64$ and $192^3\times96$ lattices using clover-improved Wilson fermions. These lattices correspond to temperatures of $2.25 T_c$, $1.50 T_c$, $1.25 T_c$, $1.10 T_c$ and $0.75 T_c$. To increase the signal to noise ratio in the axial-vector and scalar channels we used multi-sources for the measurement of spatial correlation functions. By investigating on the differences between spatial and temporal correlators as well as the temperature dependence of screening masses we will discuss the thermal effects in different channels of quarkonium states. Besides this the dispersion relation of the screening mass at different momenta is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal modifications of charmonia and bottomonia can be studied via spatial correlation functions in quenched QCD, but the results are limited to the quenched approximation (no dynamical quarks) and the dispersion relation appears unmodified only because the maximum momentum studied (3.17 GeV) is of the order of the quarkonium masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on isotropic quenched lattices with a fixed lattice spacing of a^{-1}=22.8 GeV, using clover-improved Wilson fermions on 192^3 x N_tau lattices with N_tau = 32, 48, 56, 64, and 96, corresponding to temperatures from 0.75 T_c to 2.25 T_c. They calculated spatial correlation functions for pseudo-scalar, vector, scalar, and axial-vector channels at zero and nonzero momenta up to 3.17 GeV, using multi-sources to improve the signal-to-noise ratio. Screening masses were extracted via correlated two-state fits to the exponential decay of the spatial correlators at large distances.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 22.8 GeV, corresponding to a spatial extent of 192 lattice sites.
* Temperatures studied: 0.75 T_c, 1.10 T_c, 1.25 T_c, 1.50 T_c, and 2.25 T_c.
* For bottomonia S-wave states, the screening mass increases by approximately 5.6% at 2.25 T_c relative to 0.75 T_c.
* For charmonia S-wave states, the screening mass increases by approximately 54% at 2.25 T_c relative to 0.75 T_c.
* Momentum range studied: |p_perp| from 0 to 3.17 GeV.
* At nonzero momenta, the relative error in the vector channel correlator at the middle point is approximately 1.5% using a single source.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of quarkonium screening masses in quenched QCD and compares with results from 2+1 flavor HISQ simulations by Bazavov et al. (2014). The quenched calculations show similar temperature dependence to the dynamical simulations, with S-wave screening masses increasing monotonically and P-wave screening masses showing a dip, though the dip occurs at different temperatures (1.10 T_c in quenched versus 1.43 T_c in dynamical QCD). The results enable future comparisons with full QCD calculations and provide input for understanding sequential suppression of bottomonia observed in heavy-ion collisions at CMS.
