# Approach to Parton Equilibration

> Perturbative QCD-based models of parton production and equilibration in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are reviewed with an emphasis on the treatment of quantum interference effects. Uncertainties in the initial parton production and their effects on later parton equilibration are considered. Probes of early parton dynamics are also discussed.

# Approach to Parton Equilibration

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD-based models of parton production and equilibration in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can produce a quark-gluon plasma (more specifically a gluon plasma) at RHIC and LHC energies, but the uncertainties in initial parton production, particularly the cut-off parameter separating soft from hard processes, prevent a definite prediction, especially at RHIC energy.

## Method

The paper reviews pQCD-based models (HIJING and PCM) that simulate initial parton production via hard and semihard parton scatterings using parton distribution functions and Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations, then applies rate equations for the dominant processes $gg \leftrightarrow ggg$ and $gg \leftrightarrow q\bar{q}$ to model chemical equilibration. The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect is incorporated by imposing a formation-time suppression factor $\theta(\lambda_f - \tau_{\rm QCD})$ on gluon radiation, and Debye screening mass $\mu_D^2 = 4\pi\alpha_s T^2\lambda_g$ regularizes infrared singularities in the radiative cross section.

## Key numbers

- Initial parton formation time: $1/p_T \sim 0.2$ fm/$c$ for hard/semihard partons
- Most partons produced within 0.4 fm/$c$ after complete overlap of colliding nuclei
- At RHIC ($Au+Au$): $\tau_{\rm iso} = 0.7$ fm/$c$, $\varepsilon_0 = 3.2$ GeV/fm$^3$, $n_0 = 2.15$ fm$^{-3}$, $\langle k_\perp\rangle = 1.17$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.55$ GeV, $\lambda_g^0 = 0.05$, $\lambda_q^0 = 0.008$
- At LHC ($Au+Au$): $\tau_{\rm iso} = 0.5$ fm/$c$, $\varepsilon_0 = 40$ GeV/fm$^3$, $n_0 = 18$ fm$^{-3}$, $\langle k_\perp\rangle = 1.76$ GeV, $T_0 = 0.82$ GeV, $\lambda_g^0 = 0.124$, $\lambda_q^0 = 0.02$
- Debye screening mass: $\mu_D^2 = 4\pi\alpha_s T^2\lambda_g$
- Quark equilibration rate: $R_2 \approx 0.24 N_f \alpha_s^2 \lambda_g T \ln(5.5/\lambda_g)$ for $N_f = 2.5$
- Parton gas at RHIC cools below $T_c \approx 200$ MeV in 1-2 fm/$c$; at LHC deconfined phase may last 4-5 fm/$c$
- With initial densities increased by factor 4 at RHIC, deconfined phase lasts about 4 fm/$c$

## Context

This review extends the pQCD-based parton model approach developed in HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) (Geiger and Muller, 1992), which were initially applied to $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions. The work builds on the Bjorken scaling solution for hydrodynamic expansion and the rate-equation approach of Biró, Müller, and Wang (1993) for chemical equilibration. The analysis of LPM interference effects follows Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and Baier et al. (1995). The paper identifies open questions about the nonperturbative cut-off $p_0$ separating soft and hard processes, the gluon distribution at small $x$, nuclear shadowing of gluons, and the treatment of soft parton production from color flux-tube models (Koch, Müller, and Greiner, 1993).
