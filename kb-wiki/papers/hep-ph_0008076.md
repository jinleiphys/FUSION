# In-medium pi-pi Correlation Induced by Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry

> We show that both the linear and the non-linear chiral models give an enhancement of the pi-pi cross section near the 2pi threshold in the scalar-iso-scalar (I=J=0) channel in nuclear matter. The reduction of the chiral condensate, i.e., the partial chiral restoration in nuclear matter, is responsible for the enhancement in both cases. We extract an effective 4pi-nucleon vertex which is responsible for the enhancement but has not been considered in the non-liear models for in-medium pi-pi interaction. Relation of this vertex and a next-to-leading order terms in the heavy-baryon chiral lagrangian, L_piN^(2), is also discussed.

# In-medium pi-pi Correlation Induced by Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim

Both the linear sigma model and the nonlinear chiral model produce an enhancement of the pi-pi cross section near the 2pi threshold in the scalar-isoscalar (I=J=0) channel in nuclear matter, provided that the reduction of the chiral condensate (partial chiral restoration) is properly incorporated. The enhancement occurs even in the limit where the sigma meson mass is taken to infinity, meaning the effect does not rely on a light sigma meson but arises from the density-dependent contact interaction.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium pi-pi scattering amplitude in the I=J=0 channel using both the linear sigma model (LsigmaM) and the nonlinear chiral model with a polar parameterization of the chiral field. They incorporate medium effects through the mean-field reduction of the chiral condensate, parameterized as Phi(rho) = 1 - C rho/rho_0 with C = 0.1-0.3, and unitarize the tree-level amplitude using the inverse amplitude method to restore unitarity. They then extract an effective 4pi-nucleon vertex from the heavy-scalar and heavy-baryon limit of the linear sigma model and identify it with a next-to-leading order term in the heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian L_piN^(2).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV
- Vacuum chiral condensate (pion decay constant): <sigma>_0 = f_pi = 93 MeV
- Vacuum sigma mass: m_sigma*(rho=0) = 550 MeV (corresponding to lambda/4pi = 7.8); also checked with m_sigma*(rho=0) = 750 MeV (lambda/4pi = 15.0) with no qualitative change
- Chiral condensate reduction parameterization: Phi(rho) = 1 - C rho/rho_0 with C = 0.1-0.3
- Low-energy constants from L_piN^(2): c_1 = -0.87 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 3.34 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -5.25 GeV^{-1} (quoted with the caveat of large potential uncertainties)

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Hatsuda, Kunihiro, and Shimizu (1999) that the linear sigma model produces an enhanced spectral function near the 2pi threshold, and addresses whether the same effect appears in nonlinear chiral models. The CHAOS collaboration (Bonutti et al., 1996, 1999) had reported an enhanced pi-pi cross section near threshold in pi A -> pi+ pi- A' reactions, which state-of-the-art calculations using nonlinear chiral Lagrangians with pi N many-body dynamics (Rapp et al., 1999; Vicente-Vacas and Oset, 1999) could not reproduce simultaneously in the I=0 and I=2 channels. The authors identify a specific 4pi-nucleon vertex from L_piN^(2) that has not been included in previous nonlinear calculations (such as Oset and Vicente Vacas, 2000) and argue that its inclusion may resolve the discrepancy with the CHAOS data. This vertex is related to the c_3 term in the heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995).
