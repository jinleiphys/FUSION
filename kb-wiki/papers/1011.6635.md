# Spin Observables in Transition-Distribution-Amplitude Studies

> Exclusive hadronic reactions with a massive lepton pair (l^+l^-) in the final state will be measured with PANDA at GSI-FAIR and with Compass at CERN, both in p+p-bar -> l^+l^-+pi and pi+N -> N'+l^+l^-. Similarly, electroproduction of a meson in the backward region will be studied at JLAB. We discuss here how the spin structure of the amplitude for such processes will enable us to disentangle various mechanisms. For instance, target-transverse-spin asymmetries are specific of a partonic description, where the amplitude is factorised in terms of baryon to meson or meson to baryon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) as opposed to what is expected from baryon-exchange contributions.

## Key claim

Target-transverse-spin asymmetries are specific to a partonic description where the amplitude factorises in terms of baryon-to-meson or meson-to-baryon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), as opposed to what is expected from baryon-exchange contributions; this provides a model-independent way to disentangle the underlying mechanism, provided that the transverse momentum transfer \(\Delta_T\) is non-zero and the TDA integrals develop imaginary parts from DGLAP-region contributions.

## Method

The authors analyse the spin structure of the amplitudes for exclusive reactions such as \(\bar p p \to \ell^+ \ell^- \pi\) and backward electroproduction \(\gamma^\star N \to N' \pi\) at leading twist and leading order in \(\alpha_s\). They compute the scaling of the amplitude squared with \(Q^2\), the dominance of transverse virtual-photon polarisation, and the single-transverse-spin asymmetry (SSA) for a polarised target, using the factorised TDA formalism with Sudakov decompositions and explicit hard-scattering integrals.

## Key numbers

* For \(\bar p p \to \ell^+ \ell^- \pi\) at GSI-FAIR, the maximal reachable \(W^2 \simeq 30\ \text{GeV}^2\) (for \(E_{\bar p}=15\ \text{GeV}\)) and the highest photon invariant mass \(Q^2_{max} \simeq 30\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The amplitude squared for \(\bar p p \to \gamma^\star \pi\) scales as \(Q^{-6}\) at fixed \(\xi\).
* At leading twist, only the transverse polarisation contribution \({\cal S}_T^2 = (2(1+\xi) Q^2)/\xi\) survives, implying the lepton-pair angular distribution follows \(1+\cos^2 \theta_\ell\).
* The SSA for backward electroproduction is proportional to \((|\Delta_T|/M) \sin(\phi-\phi_S) \, \Im m(\Ip \I^\ast)\) and is leading-twist when \(\Delta_T \neq 0\) and the TDA integrals are not purely real or imaginary.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) introduced by Pire et al. (2005) and builds on earlier studies of backward electroproduction (Lansberg et al., 2007) and \(\bar p p\) annihilation (Pire et al., 2004; Lansberg et al., 2007; Lutz et al., 2009). It also references recent TDA model calculations by Pasquini et al. (2009) using the meson-cloud model and by Pire et al. (2010) using spectral representations. The paper enables future experimental tests at PANDA (GSI-FAIR), COMPASS (CERN), and JLAB, where spin observables can distinguish partonic TDA mechanisms from baryon-exchange contributions, and it suggests analogous studies for \(J/\psi \pi\) production and meson-beam reactions such as \(\pi^- p \to n \gamma^\star\) and \(K^- p \to \Lambda \gamma^\star\).
