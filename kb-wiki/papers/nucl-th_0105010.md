# Prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability from Langevin dynamics of nuclear fission

> A theoretical model of one-body nuclear friction which was developed earlier, namely the chaos-weighted wall formula, is applied to a dynamical description of compound nuclear decay in the framework of the Langevin equation coupled with statistical evaporation of light particles and photons. We have used both the usual wall formula friction and its chaos-weighted version in the Langevin equation to calculate the fission probability and prescission neutron multiplicity for the compound nuclei $^{178}$W, $^{188}$Pt, $^{200}$Pb, $^{213}$Fr, $^{224}$Th, and $^{251}$Es. We have also obtained the contributions of the presaddle and postsaddle neutrons to the total prescission multiplicity. A detailed analysis of our results leads us to conclude that the chaos-weighted wall formula friction can adequately describe the fission dynamics in the presaddle region. This friction, however, turns out to be too weak to describe the postsaddle dynamics properly. This points to the need for a suitable explanation for the enhanced neutron emission in the postsaddle stage of nuclear fission.

## Key claim

The chaos-weighted wall formula (CWWF) friction can adequately describe fission dynamics in the presaddle region, but it turns out to be too weak to describe the postsaddle dynamics properly, pointing to the need for a suitable explanation for enhanced neutron emission in the postsaddle stage of nuclear fission.

## Method

The authors applied a one-dimensional Langevin equation coupled with statistical evaporation of neutrons and gamma rays to model compound nuclear decay, using both the standard wall formula (WF) friction and its chaos-weighted version (CWWF). The shape-dependent chaoticity factor mu was calculated from single-particle dynamics to scale the wall friction for compact nuclear shapes, and a combined dynamical and statistical model was used where a switch to statistical decay was made after the fission process reached stationary regime. Calculations were performed for six compound nuclei (W-178, Pt-188, Pb-200, Fr-213, Th-224, and Es-251) at multiple excitation energies, with no adjustable parameters in the entire calculation.

## Key numbers

- The stationary fission width with CWWF friction is about twice that with WF friction.
- For the compound nucleus Fr-213, the reduced friction coefficient shows strong suppression of the original wall formula for compact shapes (c approximately 1), where chaoticity is very small.
- The prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability change by less than 5% when input fission rates are changed by 15%.
- Calculations with values of the transition point c_win beyond 1.5 gave agreement within 5% for prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability.
- The critical excitation energy for postsaddle neutron dominance decreases with increasing compound nuclear mass; for Es-251, most prescission neutrons predicted by CWWF are accounted for by postsaddle neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the chaos-weighted wall formula approach developed earlier by Pal and colleagues, which modifies the standard wall formula of Blocki et al. by relaxing the full randomization assumption for single-particle motion. The results confirm the phenomenological shape-dependent friction found by Frobrich et al. (1990s) which required friction about 10% of the wall formula value for near-spherical shapes but strong increase at large deformations, while also providing physical justification for that empirical friction. The finding that CWWF is too weak for postsaddle dynamics indicates the need for additional dissipative mechanisms beyond one-body chaos-weighted friction in the later stages of fission.
