# All-Orders Evolution of Parton Distributions: Principle, Practice, and Predictions

> Parton distribution functions (DFs) are defining expressions of hadron structure. Exploiting the role of effective charges in quantum chromodynamics, an algebraic scheme is described which, given any hadron's valence parton DFs at the hadron scale, delivers predictions for all its DFs -- unpolarised and polarised -- at any higher scale. The scheme delivers results that are largely independent of both the value of the hadron scale and the pointwise form of the charge; and, inter alia, enables derivation of a model-independent identity that relates the strength of the proton's gluon helicity DF, $ΔG_p^ζ$, to that of the analogous singlet polarised quark DF and valence quark momentum fraction. Using available data fits and theory predictions, the identity yields $ΔG_p(ζ_{\rm C}=\surd 3{\rm GeV})=1.48(10)$. It furthermore entails that the measurable quark helicity contribution to the proton spin is $\tilde a_{0p}^{ζ_{\rm C}}=0.32(3)$, thereby reconciling contemporary experiment and theory.

## Key claim

The paper describes an algebraic scheme for all-orders evolution of parton distribution functions (DFs) that, given any hadron's valence parton DFs at the hadron scale, delivers predictions for all DFs at any higher scale; the scheme is largely independent of both the value of the hadron scale and the pointwise form of the effective charge, and enables derivation of a model-independent identity relating the proton's gluon helicity DF to the singlet polarised quark DF and valence quark momentum fraction.

## Method

The authors exploit the concept of an effective charge in quantum chromodynamics, defining an effective coupling α₁ℓ(k²) that, when used to integrate leading-order perturbative DGLAP equations, defines an evolution scheme for all parton DFs that is all-orders exact. They specify the hadron scale ζ_H as the scale at which all properties of a given hadron are carried by its valence quasiparticle degrees of freedom, meaning glue and sea DFs are identically zero at that scale. The evolution equations are formulated algebraically in terms of Mellin moments, with the evolution kernel determined by the valence quark momentum fraction, and the scheme incorporates Pauli blocking effects via a modification of the gluon splitting function.

## Key numbers

- ΔG_p(ζ_C = √3 GeV) = 1.48(10), obtained from the model-independent identity using available data fits and theory predictions
- The measurable quark helicity contribution to the proton spin is ã_{0p}^{ζ_C} = 0.32(3), reconciling contemporary experiment and theory
- For Pauli blocking parameters: b_d = 0.34 = -b_u, b_s = 0 = b_c, used to explain the asymmetry of antimatter in the proton
- Quark flavour threshold parameters: δ_u = δ_d = 0, δ_s = 0.1 GeV, δ_c = 0.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach to parton DFs introduced by Gluck, Reya, and Vogt (1995, 1998) [GRV], which supposed all DFs are nonzero at some low scale and used perturbative QCD coupling to evolve them; however, in the all-orders approach ζ_H is not a parameter, glue and sea DFs are identically zero at this scale, and valence DFs are obtained from nonperturbative calculations rather than parametrised. The scheme exploits progress with continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) in treating QCD, as developed by Eichmann (2016), Burkert (2017), Fischer (2018), and Qin (2020), and builds on the process-independent strong running coupling defined by Binosi (2016) and Cui (2019). The results enable reconciliation of contemporary experiment and theory for the proton spin decomposition, and the algebraic formulation allows all DFs to be determined from valence DFs at the hadron scale.
