# A Skyrme model approach to the spin-orbit force

> The spin-orbit force is a vital tool in describing finite nuclei and nucleon interactions; however its microscopic origin is not fully understood. In this paper we study a model inspired by Skyrmions which provides a classical explanation of the force. To simplify the calculations the Skyrmions are approximated as two-dimensional rigid discs which behave like quantum cogwheels.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the spin-orbit force in nuclei can be explained classically through the pion field structure of Skyrmions, but only in a simplified two-dimensional toy model where Skyrmions are approximated as rigid discs with a contact potential; the authors hedge that the model is a "toy" and that the true strength of the coupling is expected to lie between the analytically tractable limits.

## Method

The authors model two-dimensional slices of three-dimensional Skyrmion configurations as rigid discs with a colour field, interacting through a cosine potential that enforces the "attractive channel" condition. They quantise the system using collective coordinates, reduce the problem to a Mathieu equation via a coordinate transformation that exploits conserved total angular momentum and isospin, and solve analytically in the small-coupling (perturbative) and large-coupling (tight-binding) limits. The analysis is performed first for two equal-sized discs (modelling nucleon-nucleon interactions) and then for unequal discs (modelling a nucleon orbiting a magic-number core).

## Key numbers

- For two equal discs, the condition for energetically favourable states is \(|q_\gamma| = |l - 2d^2(s_1+s_2)|/(2+4d^2) \leq 1/2\), where \(d\) is a dimensionless separation parameter, \(l\) is orbital angular momentum, and \(s_1,s_2\) are disc spins (half-integers).
- For the unequal disc system with a small disc (fermion, spin \(s_1 = 1/2\)) orbiting a large disc (boson, spin \(s_2 = 0\)), the allowed orbital angular momentum for energetically favourable states is \(l \in \left[ -\frac{n^3+3}{2(n^2-n+1)},\frac{5n^3+3}{2(n^2-n+1)} \right]\), where \(n\) is the number of colour repeats on the large disc.
- The large-disc moment of inertia scales as \(I_2 = n^5 I_1\) and the reduced mass term as \(\mu r^2 = \frac{2n^3}{n^3 + 1}(n+1)^2 I_1\), based on Skyrmion-inspired scaling where radius scales as \(B^{1/3}\) and mass scales linearly with baryon number \(B\).

## Context

This work extends the idea from Halcrow and Manton's earlier work (2014) that Skyrmion configurations in the attractive channel naturally align spin and orbital angular momentum, providing a classical origin for the spin-orbit force. It contrasts with the meson-exchange approach (Ring, 1996; Ring, 2001) which requires fitted parameters and ignores nucleon structure, and with the asymptotic Skyrme model treatment (Riska, 1985) valid only at large separations. The model enables a unified treatment of the spin-orbit and tensor forces from a classical microscopic perspective, and suggests a natural explanation for why orbital angular momentum increases with core size in magic nuclei.
