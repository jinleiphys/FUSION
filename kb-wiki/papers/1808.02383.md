# Radial Flow and Differential Freeze-out in Proton-Proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}= 7$ TeV at the LHC

> We analyse the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$)-spectra as a function of charged-particle multiplicity at midrapidity ($|y| < 0.5$) for various identified particles such as $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $K_S^0$, $p+\overline{p}$, $ϕ$, $K^{*0} + \overline {K^{*0}}$, and $Λ$ + $\barΛ$ in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV using Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) model and thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution function. We obtain the multiplicity dependent kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T_{\rm kin}$) and radial flow ($β$) of various particles after fitting the $p_{\rm T}$-distribution with BGBW model. Here, $T_{\rm kin}$ exhibits mild dependence on multiplicity class while $β$ shows almost independent behaviour. The information regarding Tsallis temperature and the non-extensivity parameter ($q$) are drawn by fitting the $p_{\rm T}$-spectra with Tsallis distribution function. The extracted parameters of these particles are studied as a function of charged particle multiplicity density ($dN_{ch}/dη$). In addition to this, we also study these parameters as a function of particle mass to observe any possible mass ordering. All the identified hadrons show a mass ordering in temperature, non-extensive parameter and also a strong dependence on multiplicity classes, except the lighter particles. It is observed that as the particle multiplicity increases, the $q$-parameter approaches to Boltzmann-Gibbs value, hence a conclusion can be drawn that system tends to thermal equilibrium. The observations are consistent with a differential freeze-out scenario of the produced particles.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV, the kinetic freeze-out temperature shows only a mild dependence on charged-particle multiplicity while radial flow is almost independent of multiplicity, and that the non-extensive Tsallis parameter q approaches the Boltzmann-Gibbs value (q=1) as multiplicity increases, indicating the system tends toward thermal equilibrium. However, these conclusions are qualified by the observation that the BGBW model fits worsen for lower multiplicity classes and that lighter particles (pions, kaons, K_S^0) show nearly multiplicity-independent q values.

## Method

The authors fitted the transverse momentum spectra of seven identified particle species (π±, K±, K_S^0, p+anti-p, φ, K*0+anti-K*0, and Λ+anti-Λ) measured by ALICE at midrapidity in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV across ten multiplicity classes. They used the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) model to extract kinetic freeze-out temperature (T_kin) and radial flow (β) from the low-pT region (up to ~3 GeV/c), and a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution function to extract the Tsallis temperature (T) and non-extensivity parameter (q) over the full pT range.

## Key numbers

- For pions (π±) in the highest multiplicity class (Mul1, ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ = 21.3±0.6): T_kin = 0.093±0.001 GeV (from Tsallis fit), q = 1.163±0.001
- For protons (p+anti-p) in Mul1: T_kin = 0.183±0.006 GeV, q = 1.112±0.003
- For Λ+anti-Λ in Mul1: T_kin = 0.245±0.0 GeV, q = 1.086±0.006
- For Ξ−+anti-Ξ+ in Mul1: T_kin = 0.308±0.0 GeV, q = 1.069±0.015
- For Ω−+anti-Ω+ in combined Mul[1+2]: T_kin = 0.422±0.0 GeV, q = 1.035±0.028
- For pions in the lowest multiplicity class (Mul10, ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ = 2.26±0.12): T_kin = 0.068±0.001 GeV, q = 1.143±0.001
- For protons in Mul10: T_kin = 0.017±0.001 GeV, q = 1.136±0.001
- The Tsallis temperature T for pions increases from 0.068 GeV (Mul10) to 0.093 GeV (Mul1); for protons from 0.017 GeV to 0.183 GeV
- The χ²/NDF for pions in Mul1 is 7.574; for kaons in Mul1 is 0.290; for protons in Mul1 is 0.483

## Context

This work extends the application of the BGBW model and Tsallis non-extensive statistics to high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies, following similar analyses in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (e.g., STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, CMS collaborations). The study builds on earlier observations of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity pp collisions by ALICE (Adam 2016) and previous Tsallis fits to particle spectra in e+e-, hadronic, and heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Cleymans 2011, Bhattacharyya 2015). The results support a differential freeze-out scenario where heavier particles freeze out earlier than lighter ones, and suggest that high-multiplicity pp collisions exhibit thermalization tendencies similar to those seen in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (Biro 2014, Urmossy 2015). The findings enable further investigation of the microscopic mechanisms driving equilibration in small systems.
