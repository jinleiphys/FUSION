# Equilibrium under flow

> Using information theory we derive a thermodynamics for systems evolving under a collective motion, i.e. under a time-odd constraint. An illustration within the Lattice gas Model is given for two model cases: a collision between two complex particles leading to a incomplete relaxation of the incoming momentum, and a self-similar expansion. A semi-quantitative connection with the determination of thermodynamical quantities in multifragmentation reactions is done showing that they are affected in a sizeable way only when the flow dominates the global energetic.

## Key claim

Using information theory, a thermodynamically consistent description of open finite nuclear systems under collective flow is possible, but the disordered thermal motion is always more efficient than collective motion at breaking up the system; consequently, the negative heat capacity signal observed experimentally cannot be due to neglected collective components in the data analysis, and transparency can be neglected only if the relaxation of the incoming momentum exceeds about 90%.

## Method

The authors derive statistical ensembles by maximizing entropy under constraints of time-odd observables (incomplete momentum relaxation and radial flow) using Lagrange multipliers, then apply these ensembles to the Lattice Gas Model (LGM) with a constant nearest-neighbor coupling. For the incomplete momentum relaxation ensemble (IMRE), they impose constraints on total energy and the memory of initial momenta along the beam axis; for radial flow, they impose constraints on total energy and the average local radial momentum, assuming a self-similar velocity profile. Cluster observables are computed using the Coniglio-Klein prescription for bond probabilities, and comparisons are made between canonical, microcanonical, and flow-constrained ensembles at fixed total energy.

## Key numbers

- For the IMRE at a temperature of β⁻¹ = 0.65ε (the transition temperature in the canonical ensemble) and subcritical pressure λ = 3.3×10⁻⁴, up to 10% transparency (Δp/p) produces cluster size distributions almost identical to the isotropic case.
- In the radial flow case at pressure λ = 1.23×10⁻² and temperature β⁻¹ = 0.65ε, the effect on cluster size distributions is very small up to mα ≈ 0.6, which corresponds to approximately 40% contribution of flow to the kinetic energy.
- When the collective flow exceeds a threshold of ΔEₖ/Eₖ ≈ 50%, the average volume shows an exponential increase and the system undergoes complete fragmentation.
- The longitudinal momentum dispersion was kept fixed at (β/2m + δ)⁻¹ = 0.04ε for the IMRE calculations.

## Context

This work extends the information theory approach of Balian (1991) to treat collective motions in nuclear multifragmentation, building on the Lattice Gas Model of the same group and the Coniglio-Klein prescription of Das Gupta (1990s). It directly addresses the conceptual problem that multifragmentation experiments lack boundary conditions and may contain collective flows from the entrance channel (transparency) or thermal pressure (radial flow), which had cast doubt on the applicability of equilibrium thermodynamics. The results enable a rigorous justification for subtracting radial flow from detected energy in data analysis (as done by Das Gupta) and establish a quantitative criterion (90% relaxation) for when transparency can be neglected in thermodynamic extractions from multifragmentation data.
