# Relating pseudospin and spin symmetries through charge conjugation and chiral transformations: the case of the relativistic harmonic oscillator

> We solve the generalized relativistic harmonic oscillator in 1+1 dimensions, i.e., including a linear pseudoscalar potential and quadratic scalar and vector potentials which have equal or opposite signs. We consider positive and negative quadratic potentials and discuss in detail their bound-state solutions for fermions and antifermions. The main features of these bound states are the same as the ones of the generalized three-dimensional relativistic harmonic oscillator bound states. The solutions found for zero pseudoscalar potential are related to the spin and pseudospin symmetry of the Dirac equation in 3+1 dimensions. We show how the charge conjugation and $γ^5$ chiral transformations relate the several spectra obtained and find that for massless particles the spin and pseudospin symmetry related problems have the same spectrum, but different spinor solutions. Finally, we establish a relation of the solutions found with single-particle states of nuclei described by relativistic mean-field theories with scalar, vector and isoscalar tensor interactions and discuss the conditions in which one may have both nucleon and antinucleon bound states.

# Relating pseudospin and spin symmetries through charge conjugation and chiral transformations: the case of the relativistic harmonic oscillator

## Key claim

The authors show that charge-conjugation and chiral ($\gamma^5$) transformations connect the spectra of the relativistic harmonic oscillator for the cases $\Delta=0$ (spin symmetry) and $\Sigma=0$ (pseudospin symmetry), but only for massless particles do these two symmetry-related problems yield exactly the same energy spectrum, while the spinor solutions remain different. The paper qualifies that these results apply to the 1+1 dimensional generalized relativistic harmonic oscillator, and the main features are stated to be the same as those of the three-dimensional case.

## Method

The authors solve the 1+1 dimensional Dirac equation with a generalized harmonic oscillator potential consisting of a quadratic scalar-vector combination ($\Sigma$ or $\Delta$) and a linear pseudoscalar potential $V_p = k_2 x$, for the cases $\Delta=0$ and $\Sigma=0$ separately. They derive second-order Schrödinger-like equations for the upper ($\psi_+$) and lower ($\psi_-$) spinor components, obtain the bound-state solutions using Hermite polynomials, and apply charge-conjugation and chiral transformations to relate the spectra of the $\Delta=0$ and $\Sigma=0$ cases. The isolated solutions at $E = \pm mc^2$ are treated separately from the Sturm-Liouville problem.

## Key numbers

- For the pure pseudoscalar case ($k_1=0$, the 1+1 Dirac oscillator), the energy eigenvalues are $E = \pm \sqrt{m^2 c^4 + 2(n+1)\hbar c |k_2|}$ with $n=0,1,2,\ldots$, independent of the sign of $k_2$, and $E > mc^2$ for all bound states.
- The quantization condition for the $\Delta=0$ case with $\Sigma = \frac{1}{2} k_1 x^2$ and $V_p = k_2 x$ is $E^2 - m^2 c^4 = (2n+1)\hbar c \sqrt{\frac{E+mc^2}{2} k_1 + k_2^2} + \hbar c k_2$, where $n=0,1,2,\ldots$.
- For $k_1 > 0$, bound-state energies satisfy $E > -mc^2 - 2k_2^2/k_1$; for $k_1 < 0$, they satisfy $E < -mc^2 + 2k_2^2/|k_1|$.
- The nonrelativistic limit gives $E - mc^2 = (n+\frac{1}{2})\hbar \sqrt{k_1/m + (k_2/mc)^2} + \hbar k_2/(2mc)$, valid when $\hbar^2 |k_1|/(m^3 c^4) \ll 1$ and $\hbar |k_2|/(m^2 c^3) \ll 1$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic harmonic oscillators with scalar and vector potentials previously studied by Chen et al. (2003), Kukulin et al. (1975), and Ginocchio (2005), particularly regarding the $\Sigma=0$ condition associated with pseudospin symmetry in nuclei. The paper also builds on the work of Zhou et al. (2003) connecting spin symmetry of antinucleons to pseudospin symmetry of nucleons via charge conjugation, and on Ginocchio (2005) who analyzed the harmonic oscillator for antinucleons with spin symmetry. The results enable further study of antinucleon bound states in nuclei within relativistic mean-field models and provide a framework for understanding the connection between pseudospin and chiral symmetries, though the authors note that imposing chiral symmetry on these states would require all potentials to vanish, yielding only massless free fermions.
