# Emergence of thermal recoil jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> In the established paradigm of jet quenching in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, jets from initial hard parton scatterings are suppressed due to their interaction with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as they traverse the hot medium, serving as crucial tomographic probes of QGP properties. The QGP is also capable of absorbing and reprocessing energy deposited by the hard jets into emergent jet-like objects, providing a novel production mechanism of thermal recoil jets. These emergent thermal recoil jets exhibit distinct transverse momentum ($p_\mathrm{T}$) and jet-size ($R$) dependencies different from the hard jets, and naturally explain the puzzling observation of the enhanced yields of hadron or photon triggered jets at large azimuthal angle and solely at small $p_\mathrm{T}$ and large $R$. These thermal recoil jets are predicted to have unique substructures, such as their jet shape that increases with the radius and the thermal-like distribution of their constituents, which can be verified in future experimental analyses.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of low-transverse-momentum, large-cone-size hadron-triggered recoil jet yields at large azimuthal angles in heavy-ion collisions does not arise from medium modification of hard jets from initial parton scatterings, but instead from jet-like objects formed by the QGP medium's response to deposited energy, "thermal recoil jets", which dominate when the thermal fraction of jet energy exceeds 50%.

## Method

The authors used the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model coupled with (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (CLVisc) to simulate the evolution of jet partons, thermal recoil partons, and radiated gluons through the quark-gluon plasma in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Hard jet partons were generated with Pythia 8, and the LBT model tracked color flows, allowing partons to be passed back to Pythia for vacuum showering and hadronization after exiting the QGP boundary. The medium modification was quantified by the I_AA factor (ratio of hadron-jet spectra in A+A to p+p), and the fractional transverse momentum from medium response (ξ_th) was computed to identify thermal recoil jets.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement of recoil jet yield up to a factor of 6 at large azimuthal deviations from Δφ = π observed by ALICE at 10 < p_T,jet < 20 GeV for cone sizes R = 0.4 and 0.5, but not for R = 0.2, in central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- A 4.7σ signal of enhanced jet yields was reported by ALICE for these kinematic conditions
- The thermal fraction ξ_th exceeds 0.5 for low-p_T jets (below approximately 20 GeV) with large cone size (R = 0.4), indicating dominance of medium response over hard jet shower contributions
- The hadronization temperature of the QGP was set to T_h = 165 MeV, and the starting time of hydrodynamic evolution was τ_0 = 0.6 fm
- The virtuality scale for matching between LBT and Pythia was Q_m = 2 GeV, with hadronization scale Q_h = 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the LBT model framework (Wang 2013, He 2015, Cao 2016) to explain puzzling observations from ALICE (2023) and STAR (2025) of enhanced recoil jet yields at large azimuthal angles that could not be described by existing models including JETSCAPE (2021), JEWEL (Zapp 2008, 2013), and the Hybrid model (Casalderrey-Solana 2014). The identification of thermal recoil jets enables a new approach to probe jet-QGP interactions and transport properties of the QGP, and the predicted unique jet shapes (increasing with radius) and thermal-like constituent transverse mass distributions can be verified in future experimental analyses at both LHC and RHIC.
