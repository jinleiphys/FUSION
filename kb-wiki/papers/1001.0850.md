# Suppression of Charge Equilibration leading to the Synthesis of Exotic Nuclei

> Charge equilibration between two colliding nuclei can take place in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. A basic mechanism of charge equilibration is presented in terms of the extension of single-particle motion from one nucleus to the other, from which the upper energy-limit of the bombarding energy is introduced for significant charge equilibration at the early stage of the collision. The formula for this limit is presented, and is compared to various experimental data. It is examined also by comparison to three-dimensional time-dependent density functional calculations. The suppression of charge equilibration, which appears in collisions at the energies beyond the upper energy-limit, gives rise to remarkable effects on the synthesis of exotic nuclei with extreme proton-neutron asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that there is an upper limit to the bombarding energy for fast charge equilibration in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, determined by the minimum Fermi velocity among protons and neutrons in the two colliding nuclei; above this limit, charge equilibration is suppressed, which can actually enhance the synthesis of exotic nuclei with extreme proton-neutron asymmetry.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical formula for the upper energy-limit of charge equilibration based on the condition that the relative velocity of the two colliding nuclei must be below the minimum Fermi velocity of the four nucleon species (protons and neutrons in each nucleus). They then performed systematic three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using Skyrme interactions (SLy4d and SkM* parameter sets) for nine different collision systems, varying impact parameters in 0.25 fm increments and summing events with geometric cross-section weighting to extract yield distributions as functions of N/Z ratio.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb + 132Sn collisions, the upper energy-limit from TDHF is 6.5 ± 0.5 MeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame, while the analytical formula gives 6.36 MeV/A
- For 208Pb + 40Ca, the TDHF upper limit is 3.5 ± 0.5 MeV/A, with the formula giving 3.66 MeV/A
- For 208Pb + 16O, the TDHF upper limit is 1.5 ± 0.5 MeV/A, with the formula giving 1.75 MeV/A
- For 24Mg + 24O, the TDHF upper limit is 5.5 ± 1.0 MeV/A, with the formula giving 5.99 MeV/A
- For 112Sn + 124Sn collisions, the formula predicts an upper limit of 27.6 MeV/A in the laboratory frame, explaining the observed disappearance of charge equilibration at 35 and 50 MeV/A
- The charge equilibration time scale is approximately 10^-22 seconds, corresponding to nucleons traveling at roughly a quarter of light velocity

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charge equilibration that had focused primarily on the relation with the isovector giant dipole resonance (iv-GDR), as discussed by Berlanger (1979), Hernandez (1981), and others. The paper provides a unified mechanism based on single-particle motion and the Pauli principle, and its formula agrees with experimental data from Gatty (1975) showing charge equilibration at 7.0 MeV/A for 40Ar + 58Ni, and from Breuer (1979) at 8.3 MeV/A for 56Fe + 165Ho, while explaining the absence of equilibration observed by Milazzo (2002) and Tsang (private communication) at higher energies. The results enable predictions for the 3rd generation RI-beam facilities, suggesting that bombarding energies above the upper limit will enhance production of exotic neutron-rich nuclei even from collisions of beta-stable nuclei.
