# Field-to-particle transition based on the zero-brane approach to quantization of multiscalar field theories and its application for Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity

> The field-to-particle transition formalizm based on the effective zero-brane action approach is generalized for arbitrary multiscalar fields. As a fruitful example, by virtue of this method we derive the non-minimal particle action for the Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity at fixed gauge in the vicinity of the black hole solution as an instanton-dilaton doublet. When quantizing it as the theory with higher derivatives, it is shown that the appearing quantum equation has SU(2) dynamical symmetry group realizing the exact spin-coordinate correspondence. Finally, we calculate the quantum corrections to the mass of the JT black hole.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the field-to-particle transition formalism (zero-brane approach) to arbitrary multiscalar field theories in two-dimensional flat spacetime, and applies it to Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity specifically in a fixed gauge near the black hole solution, with the caveat that the quantization is performed on the reduced theory rather than the complete dilaton gravity.

## Method

The author develops a formalism for transitioning from multiscalar field degrees of freedom to point-particle degrees of freedom by introducing collective coordinates and eliminating zero modes through constraints derived from extremizing the action with respect to field fluctuations about a solitary-wave solution. This yields an effective zero-brane action describing a point particle with curvature terms. The method is then applied to Jackiw-Teitelboim gravity by mapping the dilaton-gravity system to a biscalar field theory via a linear rearrangement of fields, solving the eigenvalue problem for the separation constant using an approximating Pöschl-Teller potential, and quantizing the resulting higher-derivative particle action using constrained Hamiltonian methods with SU(2) dynamical symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The classical energy of the Jackiw-Teitelboim black hole solution is given by E_BH = gamma M, with event horizon R_BH = sqrt(2 G gamma M / m^3), where G is the gravitational coupling constant and m is the mass parameter.
- The effective zero-brane action parameter mu equals M = 2 C_2 m / G, where C_2 is an integration constant from the dilaton solution.
- The eigenvalue c from the Sturm-Liouville problem is determined to be c = m^2.
- The ground-state mass of the quantum particle coincides with the classical mass: Mass_0 = mu.
- The first-order quantum correction to the mass yields Mass = mu +/- sqrt(c)/2 + O(hbar^2 c/mu^2), which for the JT black hole becomes Mass = M +/- m/2 + O(m^2/M^2).

## Context

This work extends the zero-brane approach previously developed for single-scalar theories such as phi^4 (KPP) and applied to phi^3 and Liouville models (Zloshchastiev 1999) to arbitrary multiscalar fields. The Jackiw-Teitelboim dilaton gravity, discovered by Jackiw and Teitelboim (1984), can be obtained as a dimensional reduction of the 3D BTZ black hole (Banados, Teitelboim, Zanelli 1992) and spherically symmetric solutions of 4D dilaton Einstein-Maxwell gravity used for near-extremal black hole evaporation (Callan, Myers 1994). Unlike previous quantum studies of JT gravity that focused on group features in general, this work quantizes the theory in the vicinity of a specific static solution to obtain quantum corrections to the black hole mass spectrum, enabling further investigation of black hole quantum properties within the brane framework.
