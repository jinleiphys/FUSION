# High Statistics Analysis using Anisotropic Clover Lattices: (II) Three-Baryon Systems

> We present the results of an exploratory Lattice QCD calculation of three-baryon systems through a high-statistics study of one ensemble of anisotropic clover gauge-field configurations with a pion mass of m_π~ 390 MeV. Because of the computational cost of the necessary contractions, we focus on correlation functions generated by interpolating-operators with the quantum numbers of the $Ξ^0Ξ^0 n$ system, one of the least demanding three baryon systems in terms of the number of contractions. We find that the ground state of this system has an energy of E_{Ξ^0Ξ^0n}= 3877.9\pm 6.9\pm 9.2\pm3.3 MeV corresponding to an energy-shift due to interactions of δE_{Ξ^0Ξ^0n}=E_{Ξ^0Ξ^0n}-2M_{Ξ^0} -M_n=4.6\pm 5.0\pm 7.9\pm 4.2 MeV. There are a significant number of time-slices in the three-baryon correlation function for which the signal-to-noise ratio is only slowly degrading with time. This is in contrast to the exponential degradation of the signal-to-noise ratio that is observed at larger times, and is due to the suppressed overlap of the source and sink interpolating-operators that are associated with the variance of the three-baryon correlation function onto the lightest eigenstates in the lattice volume (mesonic systems). As one of the motivations for this area of exploration is the calculation of the structure and reactions of light nuclei, we also present initial results for a system with the quantum numbers of the triton (pnn). This present work establishes a path to multi-baryon systems, and shows that Lattice QCD calculations of the properties and interactions of systems containing four and five baryons are now within sight.

## Key claim
The paper presents an exploratory Lattice QCD calculation of a three-baryon system (Ξ⁰Ξ⁰n) and finds that the signal-to-noise ratio degrades only slowly over many time-slices rather than exponentially, due to a volume-suppressed overlap of the variance correlation function onto light mesonic states. This behavior suggests that Lattice QCD calculations of systems containing four and five baryons are now within reach, though the authors caution that the energy shift due to interactions is consistent with zero within the current uncertainties.

## Method
The NPLQCD Collaboration performed a high-statistics Lattice QCD calculation using a single ensemble of anisotropic clover gauge-field configurations with n_f=2+1 flavors, a pion mass of m_π ~ 390 MeV, spatial lattice spacing b_s ~ 0.1227 fm, anisotropy ξ=3.5, and lattice volume 20³×128. They computed two-point correlation functions for single baryons and three-baryon systems using smeared-smeared (SS) and smeared-point (SP) interpolating operators, then applied a matrix-Prony method to construct diagonalized correlation functions that more cleanly project onto the ground state. The analysis used an average of 218 randomly distributed measurements on each of 1191 configurations, totaling approximately 260,000 measurements.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of the Ξ⁰Ξ⁰n system: E_Ξ⁰Ξ⁰n = 3877.9 ± 6.9 ± 9.2 ± 3.3 MeV (statistical, fitting, and alternate analysis uncertainties)
- Energy shift due to interactions: δE_Ξ⁰Ξ⁰n = E_Ξ⁰Ξ⁰n - 2M_Ξ⁰ - M_n = 4.6 ± 5.0 ± 7.9 ± 4.2 MeV, consistent with zero
- Pion mass of the ensemble: m_π ~ 390 MeV
- Spatial lattice spacing: b_s = 0.1227(8) fm
- Lattice volume: 20³ × 128
- Number of configurations: 1194 (1191 used in analysis)
- Total measurements: ~260,000 (218 per configuration on 1191 configurations)
- χ²/d.o.f. for the three-baryon energy fit: 1.9
- χ²/d.o.f. for the energy-splitting fit: 2.0

## Context
This work extends the high-statistics single-hadron study of Beane et al. (2009) to three-baryon systems, building on the earlier two-baryon calculations by the same collaboration (Beane et al., 2006) that used the Luscher method to extract nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths. The observation of a plateau in the signal-to-noise ratio for multi-baryon correlation functions, previously noted for single baryons, enables a path toward Lattice QCD calculations of light nuclei such as the triton (pnn system, for which initial results are presented). The authors note that analytical tools to extract two- and three-body interactions from the volume dependence of three-baryon energy shifts are not yet in place, unlike the situation for multi-meson systems studied by Beane et al. (2007) and Detmold et al. (2008).
