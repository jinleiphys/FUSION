# Ferromagnetic instabilities in quarkyonic matter

> We investigate the magnetic properties of quarkyonic matter, which naturally bridges nuclear and quark matter at intermediate densities relevant to neutron star cores. We extend the quarkyonic model to include spin polarization, where nucleons near the Fermi surface can be polarized while quarks in the deep Fermi sea remain unpolarized due to strong Pauli blocking. After including neutron interactions with spin-dependent terms, we find that quarkyonic matter can develop ferromagnetic instabilities at low densities, characterized by negative magnetic susceptibility. This ferromagnetic behavior occurs in pure neutron matter, independent of proton contributions, and results from the competition between attractive spin-dependent interactions and kinetic energy costs. The system returns to paramagnetic behavior at higher densities when Pauli pressure dominates. Our results demonstrate that the splitting of Fermi momenta of quarkyonic matter produces fundamentally different magnetic responses compared to conventional nuclear matter, with important implications for neutron star magnetism and magnetar physics.

## Key claim

Quarkyonic matter can develop ferromagnetic instabilities at low densities, characterized by negative magnetic susceptibility, but only if the spin-dependent interaction parameter \(\tilde{p}\) is negative (attractive); the system returns to paramagnetic behavior at higher densities above approximately \(5.5 n_0\) when kinetic energy costs dominate.

## Method

The authors extended the quarkyonic matter model to include spin polarization, treating nucleons near the Fermi surface as polarizable while quarks in the deep Fermi sea remain unpolarized due to strong Pauli blocking. They incorporated neutron interactions with spin-dependent terms using a parametrization from Gandolfi (2013) that includes an attractive coefficient \(\tilde{a} = -28.6\) MeV, a repulsive coefficient \(\tilde{b} = 9.9\) MeV, and a spin-dependent term proportional to \(\tilde{p} (n_n^{\uparrow} - n_n^{\downarrow})^2\). The magnetic susceptibility \(\chi\) was computed as the second derivative of the total energy density with respect to the spin polarization parameter \(\xi\) at \(\xi = 0\), for pure neutron matter with fixed momentum shell parameter \(\alpha = 1.5\).

## Key numbers

* For \(\tilde{p} = -0.002\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\), the magnetic susceptibility \(\chi\) becomes negative at densities below approximately \(5.5 n_0\) (where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density), indicating a ferromagnetic instability.
* The interaction potential coefficients used are \(\tilde{a} = -28.6 \pm 1.2\) MeV and \(\tilde{b} = 9.9 \pm 3.7\) MeV, with the spin-dependent parameter varied as \(\tilde{p} = 0.002\), \(0\), and \(-0.002\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\).
* The nucleon mass is fixed at \(M_N = 939\) MeV and the constituent quark mass at \(M_Q = M_N / 3 = 313\) MeV; the QCD scale parameter is set to \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 300\) MeV.
* The spin polarization parameter \(\xi\) is varied as \(\xi = 0, 0.03, 0.06\) in the symmetric matter calculations.

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework of McLerran (2007) and Zhao (2020) to include spin polarization and magnetic properties, which had previously been studied separately for purely nuclear matter or fully deconfined quark matter. The finding that ferromagnetic instabilities can arise in pure neutron matter within the quarkyonic framework contrasts with earlier work by Dong (2013), who attributed negative magnetic susceptibility primarily to proton interactions in beta-equilibrium matter. The results enable future studies of neutron star magnetism and magnetar physics, and highlight the need for better experimental constraints on the spin-dependent interaction parameter \(\tilde{p}\) at high densities.
