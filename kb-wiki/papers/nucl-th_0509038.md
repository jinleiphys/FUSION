# Unusual photon isospin mixing and instantaneous Coulomb effects on the thermodynamics of compact matter

> A hidden local symmetry formalism with a two-photon counterterm approach is performed based on the relativistic continuum quantum many-body theory. The underlying electromagnetic under-screening as well as screening effects between the electric charged point-like electrons and composite protons are discussed by analyzing the in-medium isospin mixing of Lorentz vector with scalar due to electromagnetic photon. Besides the usual screening results, the main conclusion is that an effective oscillatory instantaneous Coulomb potential between the like-charged collective electrons contributes a very large negative term to the equation of state. This counterintuitive like-charged attraction results from the modulation factor of the opposite charged baryon superfluid background. The anomalous long distance quantum dragging effects between the collective electrons can be induced in a compact Coulomb confinement environment. The physics is of the strongly coupling characteristic in a specific dilute regime.

## Key claim
The paper claims that an effective oscillatory instantaneous Coulomb potential between like-charged collective electrons contributes a very large negative term to the equation of state, but this result is conditional on the presence of an opposite-charged baryon superfluid background and applies only in a specific dilute regime with strongly coupling characteristics.

## Method
The author uses a hidden local symmetry (HLS) formalism with a two-photon counterterm approach based on relativistic continuum quantum many-body theory. The in-medium isospin mixing of the photon with the sigma meson is analyzed via polarization tensor calculations at finite temperature, and the thermodynamics of strongly correlated electrons is derived through an effective potential with a mass gap multiplier determined by the Thomson stability condition.

## Key numbers
- The proton Thomas-Fermi screening mass at empirical saturation density (Fermi momentum \(k_f = 256.1\) MeV, effective nucleon mass \(M^* \sim 0.8 M_N\)) is \(M_D^P \sim 43.46\) MeV.
- The electron Thomas-Fermi screening mass at the same density is \(M_D^e = 24.69\) MeV.
- The static limit of the sigma meson self-energy is negative, \(\sim -10^6\) MeV\(^2\), in a wide density and temperature regime.
- The pressure at zero temperature is given by \(P_e = \mu_e^4 / (12 \pi^2) \times (1 - 286.9 \, \alpha / \pi)\), where \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi)\).
- The universal factor for the binding energy density in the strongly coupling unitarity limit is \(\xi = (d-2)(d+1)/d^2\) for \(d\) spatial dimensions, yielding \(\xi = 4/9\) for \(d=3\).

## Context
This work extends the author's prior study (Chen 2005) on Coulomb frustration effects in compact matter and addresses the strongly coupled electron thermodynamics in a neutron star crust or supernova explosion context. It contrasts with the weak-coupling perturbative resummation result of Kapusta (1989), which gave a much smaller negative pressure correction of order \(3\alpha/(2\pi)\). The paper enables further study of quantum transport and the pasta phase in dilute nuclear matter, and its analytical result for the universal factor \(\xi\) is consistent with Monte Carlo studies (physics/0303094).
