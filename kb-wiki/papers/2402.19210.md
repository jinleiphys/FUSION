# Symmetry Energy from Two-Nucleon Separation Energies of Pb and Ca Isotopes

> We investigate the symmetry energy in relation with the two-proton and two-neutron separation energies using different nuclear mass data. For this aim, we exploit the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in the continuum (DRHBc), FRDM2012 and AME2020 data. First, we study the two-proton and two-neutron separation energies in Pb and Ca isotopes by subtracting the contribution of Coulomb energy. They show a strong correlation with neutron number as well as with the neutron skin thickness. By taking the relative difference of both separation energies, we derive the symmetry energy from Ca and Pb isotopes. Since the nuclear surface contributes to the symmetry energy, we deduce the volume symmetry energy by subtracting the surface contribution using several mass models. The obtained symmetry energy coefficient, $a_{sym}$, is 20.0 $\sim $ 22.7 MeV for Pb isotopes and 18.7 $\sim$ 19.3 MeV for Ca isotopes from the DRHBc mass table data, while the results from other mass tables are 19.6 $\sim$ 22.1 (20.7 $\sim$ 22.3) MeV for Pb isotopes and 18.9 $\sim$ 19.0 (19.6 $\sim$ 19.7) MeV for Ca isotopes from AME2020 (FRDM2012) data. The volume contribution to the asymmetry coefficient, $a_{sym}^v$, which depends on the ratio of the surface to the volume energy coefficients, $a_s / a_v$, is also provided for each mass model. Since the ratio $a_s / a_v$ is neither determined by nuclear theory, nor by experimental data, we have investigated $a_{sym}^v$ by using the ratio $a_s / a_v$ as a free parameter, and have obtained $a_{sym}^v = $ 27.0 MeV, almost irrespective of nuclear model and isotopic chain, with the ratio $a_s / a_v$ constrained as $a_s / a_v = 1.10 \sim 1.13$.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient $a_{sym}$ extracted from two-nucleon separation energies of Pb and Ca isotopes depends on the Coulomb energy correction scheme and the mass model used, with values ranging from 18.7 to 22.7 MeV; however, the volume symmetry energy coefficient $a_{sym}^v$ converges to approximately 27.0 MeV when the ratio of surface to volume energy coefficients $a_s/a_v$ is treated as a free parameter constrained to 1.10–1.13.

## Method

The authors used the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in the continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional, along with the FRDM2012 mass model and AME2020 experimental data, to calculate two-proton and two-neutron separation energies for even-even Pb and Ca isotopes. They subtracted the Coulomb energy contribution using four different parameter sets from Tian (2014) and the DRHBc model's own Coulomb energy, then extracted the symmetry energy coefficient $a_{sym}$ from the linear relation between the difference $S_{2p}^* - S_{2n}^*$ and the isospin asymmetry $I^* = (N-Z)/(A-2)$. The volume symmetry energy $a_{sym}^v$ was obtained by correcting for the surface contribution using the relation $a_{sym} I^2 A = (a_{sym}^v A + a_{sym}^s A^{2/3}) I^2$, with the ratio $a_s/a_v$ taken from the HFB, FRDM, and Lunn (2003) mass formulas, and also treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- $a_{sym}$ for Pb isotopes from DRHBc: 20.0–22.7 MeV (depending on Coulomb correction scheme)
- $a_{sym}$ for Ca isotopes from DRHBc: 18.7–19.3 MeV
- $a_{sym}$ for Pb isotopes from AME2020: 19.6–22.1 MeV
- $a_{sym}$ for Pb isotopes from FRDM2012: 20.7–22.3 MeV
- $a_{sym}$ for Ca isotopes from AME2020: 18.9–19.0 MeV
- $a_{sym}$ for Ca isotopes from FRDM2012: 19.6–19.7 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from DRHBc: 0.257 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of $^{40}$Ca from DRHBc: –0.043 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca from DRHBc: 0.223 fm
- Volume symmetry energy $a_{sym}^v$ = 27.0 MeV, obtained with $a_s/a_v$ = 1.10–1.13, nearly independent of nuclear model and isotopic chain

## Context

This work extends the approach of Tian (2014), who extracted the symmetry energy from binding energy differences of mirror nuclei using AME2012 data, and Kim (2023), who used full-range mass data from DRHBc and AME2020. The present study focuses specifically on Pb and Ca isotopic chains and explicitly separates volume and surface contributions to the symmetry energy. The results enable improved constraints on the nuclear matter equation of state and neutron star properties, particularly through the connection between the symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness, which is relevant to interpreting PREX II and CREX experimental data.
