# Final state interaction effects in $D(e,e'p)$ scattering

> We present a systematic study of the final-state interaction (FSI) effects in $D(e,e'p)$ scattering in the CEBAF energy range with particular emphasis on the phenomenon of the angular anisotropy of the missing momentum distribution. We find that FSI effects dominate at missing momentum $p_m \gsim 1.5 $fm$^{-1}$. FSI effects in the excitation of the $S$-wave state are much stronger than in the excitation of the $D$-wave.

## Key claim

Final-state interaction (FSI) effects completely dominate the observed missing momentum distribution in $D(e,e'p)$ scattering at missing momenta $p_m \gsim 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$, with the angular anisotropy arising mostly from FSI distortions of the $S$-wave contribution rather than the $D$-wave contribution, and the forward-backward asymmetry originates from the nonvanishing real part of the proton-neutron scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic Glauber-theory calculation of the unpolarized $D(e,e'p)n$ scattering cross section, modeling the final-state interaction between the struck proton and spectator neutron via an eikonal $S$-matrix with a profile function parameterized by the total cross section, slope parameter, and ratio of real to imaginary parts of the forward elastic $p$-$n$ amplitude. They used realistic Bonn wave functions for the deuteron ground state, considered the photon as a scalar operator for simplicity, and computed the missing momentum distribution $W(\vec{p}_m)$ for both $S$-wave and $D$-wave contributions separately, including the effect of smearing the step-function in the eikonal Green function to account for finite nucleon size.

## Key numbers

- FSI effects dominate at missing momenta $p_m \gsim 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The dip in the $S$-wave contribution occurs at $p_{\perp} \sim 1.3$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The forward and backward peaks in parallel kinematics become sensitive to nucleon finite-size effects at $p_m \gsim 3$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The $p_{\perp}$-integrated longitudinal missing momentum distribution $F(p_z)$ shows a depletion of $\sim 7\%$ at small $p_z$, and an enhancement of $\sim (30-40)\%$ at $p_z \gsim 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ relative to the plane-wave impulse approximation.
- Calculations were performed for $T_{kin} \sim 1$ GeV ($Q^2 \sim 2$ GeV$^2$), with $b_0 \approx 0.5$ fm, $\sigma_{tot} \approx 40$ mb, and $\rho \approx -0.4$.
- The smearing parameter used was $z_0 = 0.25$ fm (half the short-range correlation radius $r_c \sim 0.5$ fm).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous classification of FSI effects in $^4He(e,e'p)$ scattering (Bianconi et al., 1994) to the deuteron target, which is of direct relevance to the experimental program at CEBAF. The study shows that despite the deuteron being a dilute target, FSI effects are large and must be accounted for when extracting the nucleon momentum distribution from measured missing momentum distributions, particularly for $y$-scaling analyses. The results are consistent with the numerical estimates of a contemporaneous work (authors of reference [18] in the paper) that used a different technique and focused on corrections to $y$-scaling, but the present work provides the first systematic study of the angular anisotropy of FSI effects in deuteron electroproduction.
