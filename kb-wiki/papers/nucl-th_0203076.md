# On the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy

> We perform large-scale shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations for many nuclei in the mass range A=56-65 in the complete pfg_{9/2}d_{5/2} model space using an effective quadrupole-quadrupole+pairing residual interaction. Our calculations are performed at finite temperatures between T=0.33-2 MeV. Our main focus is the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy which we determine from the energy differences between various isobaric pairs with the same pairing structure and at different temperatures. Our SMMC studies are consistent with an increase of the symmetry energy with temperature. We also investigate possible consequences for core-collapse supernovae events.

# On the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy

## Key claim

The symmetry energy increases with temperature, with an average relative increase of about 6% between T=0.33 MeV and T=1.23 MeV, supporting the earlier QRPA prediction of Donati et al. However, when applied to electron capture rates along a typical core-collapse supernova trajectory, the effect on the calculated rates is marginal and only significant during a brief period of the collapse.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations for nuclei in the mass range A=56-65 within the complete pfg_{9/2}d_{5/2} model space, using a schematic pairing+quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction that avoids the sign problem. They computed energy expectation values at finite temperatures between T=0.33-2 MeV for multiple isobaric pairs with the same pairing structure (even-even, odd-odd, odd-A), and extracted the symmetry energy coefficient a_symm from the energy differences between pairs at T=0.33 MeV and T=1.23 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Average relative increase of the symmetry energy: delta_a_symm = (6.2 ± 1.8)% between T=0.33 MeV and T=1.23 MeV, averaged over 9 isobaric pairs
- Temperature range studied: T = 0.33-2 MeV
- Model space: complete pfg_{9/2}d_{5/2} orbitals
- Interaction parameters: pairing strength G = 0.212 MeV, quadrupole strength chi = 0.0104 MeV^{-1} fm^2
- Statistical sampling: 4096 samples with beta-slices of Delta_beta = 1/32 MeV^{-1}
- At T=0.33 MeV, a_symm values ranged from 7-12 MeV across the isobaric pairs (compared to the experimental value of 17 MeV from masses)
- For nucleus 72Zn at density ~10^10 g/cm^3, the electron capture rate decreased by a factor of approximately 3 when including the temperature-dependent symmetry energy increase

## Context

This work extends the earlier argument by Donati et al. (2000) that the symmetry energy should increase with temperature due to the temperature dependence of the omega-mass in the Fermi gas model, which was supported by QRPA calculations finding an ~8% increase between T=0 and T=1 MeV. The present SMMC study improves upon an earlier SMMC attempt by Dean et al. (1995) that found no evidence for an increase, which was limited by the g-extrapolation procedure and a smaller pf-shell model space. The results enable more accurate modeling of electron capture rates in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for nuclei heavier than A=65 where hybrid RPA approaches with partial occupation numbers are used, though the authors conclude the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy has only marginal impact on collapse trajectories.
