# Nuclear Schiff moment of fluorine isotope $^{19}$F

> Nuclear Schiff moments (NSMs) are sensitive probes for physics beyond the Standard Model of particle physics, signaling violations of time-reversal and parity-inversion symmetries in atomic nuclei. In this Letter, we report the first-ever calculation of a NSM in a nuclear ab initio framework, employing the no-core shell model to study the fluorine isotope $^{19}$F. We further perform quantum-chemistry calculations to evaluate the sensitivity of the hafnium monofluoride cation, HfF$^+$, to the NSM of $^{19}$F. Combined with recent high-precision measurements of the molecular electric dipole moment of HfF$^+$, our results enable the first experimental bound on the NSM of $^{19}$F. Although the resulting bounds on the pion-nucleon-nucleon ($π$NN) coupling constants are not yet the most stringent, this work establishes the foundation for constraining $π$NN interactions using nuclear ab initio methods.

# Nuclear Schiff moment of fluorine isotope \(^{19}\)F

## Key claim

The paper reports the first-ever *ab initio* calculation of a nuclear Schiff moment (NSM), performed for \(^{19}\)F using the no-core shell model, and combines it with quantum-chemistry calculations of molecular sensitivity factors to derive the first experimental bound on the NSM of \(^{19}\)F. However, the resulting bounds on the pion-nucleon-nucleon (\(\pi\)NN) coupling constants are not yet the most stringent, and the authors note that contact interactions (neglected here) may also contribute at the same order in chiral effective field theory.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the \(^{19}\)F NSM using chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, specifically NN-N\(^4\)LO + 3N\(^*_\mathrm{lnl}\) and NN-N\(^3\)LO + 3N\(_\mathrm{lnl}\). They employed the Lanczos strength method to solve the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation with the leading-order \(\mathcal{P,T}\)-violating NN interaction from one-pion exchange, computing the NSM via the charge-distribution operator. Separately, they performed relativistic exact two-component coupled-cluster singles and doubles (X2C-CCSD) calculations to determine molecular sensitivity factors \(W_S\) for several fluorine-containing molecules, including HfF\(^+\), using an extended basis set for \(^{19}\)F.

## Key numbers

* The \(^{19}\)F NSM is expressed as \(S(^{19}\mathrm{F}) = (-2.9\, g\bar{g}_0 - 2.4\, g\bar{g}_1 - 2.0\, g\bar{g}_2) \times 10^{-2}~e~\mathrm{fm}^3\), with an estimated uncertainty of about 50%.
* The molecular sensitivity factor for HfF\(^+\) in the \(a\,^3\Delta_1\) state is \(W_S = 115\) in units of \(\frac{e}{4\pi \epsilon_0 a_0^4} \approx 44.3~h~\mathrm{Hz}/(e~\mathrm{fm}^3)\).
* The experimental energy splitting from HfF\(^+\) measurements is \(h f = (-14.6 \pm 22.8_\mathrm{stat} \pm 6.9_\mathrm{syst})~h~\mu\mathrm{Hz}\).
* The resulting bound on the NSM of \(^{19}\)F is \(|S(^{19}\mathrm{F})| < 9.0 \times 10^{-9}~e~\mathrm{fm}^3\) at 90% confidence level.
* Derived bounds on \(\mathcal{P,T}\)-violating couplings are \(|\bar{g}_0| < 2.3 \times 10^{-8}\), \(|\bar{g}_1| < 2.8 \times 10^{-8}\), and \(|\bar{g}_2| < 3.3 \times 10^{-8}\) at 90% confidence level.
* The bound on the QCD \(\theta\) term is \(|\bar{\theta}| < 1.6 \times 10^{-6}\) at 90% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the application of nuclear *ab initio* methods to NSM calculations, which had previously relied on phenomenological nuclear models that can vary by an order of magnitude and even disagree in sign (Engel 2025). The calculation for \(^{19}\)F is enabled by its relatively low mass, making it tractable within the NCSM, and by the presence of a low-lying opposite-parity excited state at approximately 110 keV above the ground state (Tilley 1995). The results enable the first experimental bound on the NSM of \(^{19}\)F by reinterpreting the HfF\(^+\) molecular EDM measurement of Roussy (2023), which primarily constrained the electron EDM. This work establishes a foundation for future *ab initio* constraints on \(\pi\)NN interactions and provides a benchmark for extending such calculations to heavier, octupole-deformed nuclei like \(^{225}\)Ra and \(^{227}\)Ac, which are currently only accessible via phenomenological models (Dobaczewski 2005, Athanasakis-Kaklamanakis 2025).
