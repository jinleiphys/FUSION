# Medium effects on the electrical conductivity of a hot pion gas

> The electromagnetic response of an interacting system of pions has been studied at finite temperature. The corresponding transport parameter i.e. electrical conductivity has been estimated by solving the relativistic transport equation in presence of a finite electric field employing the Chapman-Enskog technique where the collision term has been treated in the relaxation time approximation. The scattering amplitudes of charged pions modeled by $ρ$ and $σ$ meson exchange using an effective Lagrangian have been obtained at finite temperature by introducing self-energy corrections in the thermal propagators in the real time formalism. The temperature behavior of electrical conductivity for a hot pion gas shows significant quantitative enhancement due to the inclusion of medium effects in scattering.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of a hot pion gas shows significant quantitative enhancement when medium effects are included in the scattering amplitudes via self-energy corrected thermal propagators, compared to calculations using vacuum cross sections.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic transport equation using the Chapman-Enskog technique with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation. The scattering amplitudes for charged pions were modeled by ρ and σ meson exchange using an effective Lagrangian, with medium effects introduced through self-energy corrections in the thermal propagators evaluated in the real time formalism of thermal field theory. The in-medium ππ cross sections were then used to compute thermal relaxation times for each pion charge state, from which the electrical conductivity was derived.

## Key numbers

- The ρππ coupling constant used is g_{ρππ} = 6.05 and the σππ coupling constant is g_{σππ} = 0.525 GeV.
- The fine structure constant is taken as e²/(4π) = 1/137.
- In-medium cross sections were computed at three temperatures: T = 100, 130, and 160 MeV.
- The temperature-dependent pion chemical potential is parametrized as μ_π(T) = a + bT + cT² + dT³ with a = 0.824, b = 3.04, c = -0.028, d = 6.05 × 10⁻⁵ (T and μ_π in MeV).
- The ω chemical potential is given by μ_ω = 3 × 0.88 μ_π.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in hot hadronic matter, specifically following the same technique used to obtain the temperature dependence of viscosities (Mitra, 2015), thermal conductivity (Mitra, 2015), and relaxation times of dissipative flows (Mitra, 2015). While prior estimations of electrical conductivity in the hadronic sector mostly employed the correlator technique (Fraile, 2013; Fraile, 2014; Zahed, 2015) or used constant and experimental cross sections with Breit-Wigner resonances (Denicol, 2013), this work provides a theoretical framework that reproduces vacuum cross sections and is amenable to incorporating medium effects. The results enable more realistic inputs for soft photon and dilepton emission rates and for hydrodynamic evolution calculations in heavy ion collisions.
