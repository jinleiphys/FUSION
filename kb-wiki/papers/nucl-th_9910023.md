# The Role of Gluons in Dilepton Production from the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study high mass dilepton production from gluon-induced processes, $g q \to q γ^*$, $g \bar q \to \bar q γ^*$, and $g g \to q \bar q γ^*$, in a thermally equilibrated but chemically under-saturated partonic matter that is expected to be created in the initial stage of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Regulating the divergence in these processes by the thermal quark mass, we find that gluon-induced processes are more important than the leading-order $q\bar q$ annihilation process as a result of the larger number of gluons than quarks in the partonic matter. The dependence of the thermal dilepton yield from the partonic stage of heavy ion collisions on the initial conditions for the partonic matter is also studied. We further discuss the feasibility of observing thermal dileptons from the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion experiments.

## Key claim

Gluon-induced processes (Compton and two-gluon fusion) can dominate over leading-order quark-antiquark annihilation for thermal dilepton production in a chemically under-saturated quark-gluon plasma, but even with this enhancement the total thermal dilepton yield remains at least a factor of 5–30 below the Drell-Yan background for any reasonable variation of initial conditions consistent with the total transverse energy constraint.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal dilepton production rates from the gluon-induced processes \(gq \rightarrow q\gamma^*\), \(g\bar{q} \rightarrow \bar{q}\gamma^*\), and \(gg \rightarrow q\bar{q}\gamma^*\) in a thermally equilibrated but chemically under-saturated partonic matter, using Boltzmann distributions with fugacity parameters \(\lambda_g\) and \(\lambda_q\). Divergences in the partonic cross sections were regulated by introducing a thermal quark mass \(\mu\) that depends on the fugacities and temperature. The thermal dilepton yield for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC was obtained by integrating the rates over a Bjorken-expanding fireball with a schematic time evolution where fugacities increase linearly and temperature decreases as \(T \propto \tau^{-1/2}\), constrained by a fixed initial transverse energy density.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for the default scenario: \(\tau_0 = 0.7\) fm/\(c\), \(T_0 = 570\) MeV, \(\lambda_{g,0} = 0.060\), \(\lambda_{q,0} = 0.0072\); quarks and antiquarks make about 15% of partons; thermal quark mass is about 75 MeV at a normalization scale of 2 GeV.
- For the default scenario, the total thermal dilepton yield is a factor of 30 or more (depending on dilepton mass) below the Drell-Yan yield.
- For the upper-limit scenario with \(\lambda_{g,0}^{UL} = \lambda_{q,0}^{UL} \simeq 0.028\) (relative chemical equilibrium), the total thermal dilepton yield is about a factor of 6 higher than the default, but still a factor of 5 or more below the Drell-Yan yield.
- The initial rapidity density of transverse energy in the default scenario is \(dE_{\rm T}/dy \sim 350\) GeV at central rapidity; including string fragmentation gives a final \(dE^F_{\rm T}/dy \sim 900\) GeV.
- For a fully-equilibrated QGP at \(T_0 = 570\) MeV with \(\tau_0 = 0.7\) fm/\(c\), the rapidity density of transverse energy would be \(\sim 12,000\) GeV, more than an order of magnitude larger than most model estimates.
- Increasing \(T_0\) while keeping the same total \(dE_{\rm T}/dy\) gives at most a 15% increase at dilepton mass of 2 GeV and no more than a factor of 2 at 4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of thermal dilepton production from a chemically equilibrated QGP (Keijo; Baier; Cleymans) and from chemically non-equilibrated QGP (Shuryak; Kampfer; Strickland; Traxler) by including gluon-induced processes at leading order in \(\alpha_s\). The authors find that the conventional use of a K-factor of 2 to account for higher-order contributions (Strickland) underestimates the enhancement from gluonic processes. The results enable future calculations using parton cascade codes (such as ZPC) to self-consistently determine the time evolution of parton fugacities and include pre-thermal dilepton production, as previously attempted for the LO \(q\bar{q}\) process alone (Lin and Ko, 1999).
