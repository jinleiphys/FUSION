# Microscopic description of nuclear quantum phase transitions

> The relativistic mean-field framework, extended to include correlations related to restoration of broken symmetries and to fluctuations of the quadrupole deformation, is applied to a study of shape transitions in Nd isotopes. It is demonstrated that the microscopic self-consistent approach, based on global effective interactions, can describe not only general features of transitions between spherical and deformed nuclei, but also the singular properties of excitation spectra and transition rates at the critical point of quantum shape phase transition.

# Microscopic description of nuclear quantum phase transitions

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field framework, extended with symmetry restoration and quadrupole fluctuation correlations, can reproduce the singular behavior of excitation spectra and transition rates at the critical point of a quantum shape phase transition in Nd isotopes, but only when generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing is included, angular-momentum projection alone from the mean-field potential energy curve yields a rotor (SU(3)) spectrum, not the X(5) critical symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with the PC-F1 point-coupling effective interaction, including Lipkin-Nogami BCS pairing correlations with a density-independent delta-force (proton pairing strength V_p = -260 MeV, neutron pairing strength V_n = -285 MeV). They then applied angular-momentum and particle-number projection, followed by generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing of projected states constrained on the mass quadrupole moment, to compute excitation spectra and B(E2) transition rates for the chain of Nd isotopes from 142Nd to 152Nd.

## Key numbers

- The potential energy curve of 150Nd shows a wide flat minimum on the prolate side with an additional minimum at approximately 1.8 MeV excitation energy on the oblate side, separated by a potential barrier of approximately 4.5 MeV.
- The GCM spectrum of 150Nd, normalized to the experimental energy of the 2+1 state, shows excellent agreement with both experimental data and X(5)-symmetry predictions for excitation energies and intraband and interband B(E2) values.
- When mixing with oblate (gamma = 60 degrees) configurations is allowed in the GCM spectrum of 150Nd, the ground state 0+1 is lowered in energy by approximately 100 keV, while other states and E2 transition rates are not affected.
- The GCM results for 148Nd and 152Nd show ground-state band excitation energies and B(E2) values that agree well with available data and correspond to U(5)-like and SU(3)-like spectra respectively, not the X(5) pattern seen in 150Nd.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent mean-field approach beyond earlier studies (Meng 2005, FBL 2006) that calculated potential energy curves for isotopic chains but could not quantitatively analyze critical point nuclei because mean-field alone cannot compute excitation spectra and transition rates. The authors build on their own development of the relativistic GCM model with angular-momentum and particle-number projection (NVR 2006). The results demonstrate that the X(5) critical symmetry, previously introduced by Iachello (2000) and identified experimentally in 150Nd by Rei (2002), emerges naturally from a universal energy density functional (PC-F1) when quadrupole fluctuations are included, rather than requiring a model-specific Hamiltonian. This enables future microscopic studies of quantum phase transitions across the nuclear chart using global effective interactions.
