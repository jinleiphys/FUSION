# Charmonium Spectroscopy in Strong Magnetic Fields by QCD Sum Rules: S-Wave Ground States

> We investigate quarkonium mass spectra in external constant magnetic fields by using QCD sum rules. We first discuss a general framework of QCD sum rules necessary for properly extracting meson spectra from current correlators computed in the presence of strong magnetic fields, that is, a consistent treatment of mixing effects caused in the mesonic degrees of freedom. We then implement operator product expansions for pseudoscalar and vector heavy-quark current correlators by taking into account external constant magnetic fields as operators, and obtain mass shifts of the lowest-lying bound states $η_c$ and $J/ψ$ in the static limit with their vanishing spatial momenta. Comparing results from QCD sum rules with those from hadronic effective theories, we find that the dominant origin of mass shifts comes from a mixing between $η_c$ and $J/ψ$ with a longitudinal spin polarization, accompanied by other subdominant effects such as mixing with higher excited states and continua.

## Key claim
The dominant origin of mass shifts for static $\eta_c$ and $J/\psi$ in strong magnetic fields comes from a mixing between $\eta_c$ and $J/\psi$ with longitudinal spin polarization, with other subdominant effects such as mixing with higher excited states and continua playing a secondary role; the QCD sum rule results are consistent with those from hadronic effective theories, though with slight discrepancies.

## Method
The authors performed QCD sum rule analyses for pseudoscalar and vector heavy-quark current correlators in a constant external magnetic field, implementing operator product expansions up to dimension-4 operators including external magnetic field operators $\langle F_{\alpha\beta} F_{\mu\nu} \rangle$ and the scalar gluon condensate $\langle G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a \, \mu\nu} \rangle$. They constructed a phenomenological spectral density that consistently incorporates mixing effects between $\eta_c$ and longitudinal $J/\psi$ states, and extracted mass shifts in the static limit with vanishing spatial momenta.

## Key numbers
- The mass splitting between $\eta_c$ and $J/\psi$ in vacuum is less than 100 MeV, as predicted by QCD sum rules and confirmed by experiments.
- Formation time estimates indicate charmonium and bottomonium produced in heavy-ion collisions form faster than 0.2 fm/c, where magnetic field strengths range from $0.1 m_\pi^2$ to $m_\pi^2$ at RHIC energies.
- The analysis focuses on the lowest-lying S-wave ground states $\eta_c$ and $J/\psi$ in the static limit.

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule framework, originally developed for heavy-quark systems by Novikov et al. (1977) and Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), to the case of strong external magnetic fields. It builds on recent lattice QCD simulations and analytic calculations showing that strong magnetic fields modify QCD vacuum properties such as quark condensates (Buividovich et al., 2010; Bali et al., 2012) and gluon condensates (Bali et al., 2013; Ozaki, 2014). The mixing effect between $\eta_c$ and longitudinal $J/\psi$ is consistent with a preceding potential model study by Alford and Strickland (2013). The framework provides a general guideline for including mixing effects in correlation-function-based approaches, applicable to other systems such as $B$ mesons studied by Machado et al. (2014).
