# Anomalous thermoelectric phenomena in lattice models of multi-Weyl semimetals

> The thermoelectric transport coefficients are calculated in a generic lattice model of multi-Weyl semimetals with a broken time-reversal symmetry by using the Kubo's linear response theory. The contributions connected with the Berry curvature-induced electromagnetic orbital and heat magnetizations are systematically taken into account. It is shown that the thermoelectric transport is profoundly affected by the nontrivial topology of multi-Weyl semimetals. In particular, the calculation reveals a number of thermal coefficients of the topological origin which describe the anomalous Nernst and thermal Hall effects in the absence of background magnetic fields. Similarly to the anomalous Hall effect, all anomalous thermoelectric coefficients are proportional to the integer topological charge of the Weyl nodes. The dependence of the thermoelectric coefficients on the chemical potential and temperature is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that anomalous thermoelectric coefficients in multi-Weyl semimetals (with topological charges n=1,2,3) have a topological origin and are proportional to the integer topological charge of the Weyl nodes, but unlike the Bardeen-Zumino term in the electric current, these thermal coefficients are not exact topological analogues because they appear only at finite temperatures.

## Method

The authors calculate thermoelectric transport coefficients in a generic lattice model of multi-Weyl semimetals with broken time-reversal symmetry using Kubo's linear response theory. They systematically include contributions from Berry curvature-induced electromagnetic orbital magnetization and heat magnetization, which are necessary to correctly capture the thermoelectric response and satisfy Onsager reciprocal relations. The lattice model uses periodic functions of quasimomentum for the Hamiltonian, with specific forms for d1 and d2 functions that distinguish single-Weyl (n=1), double-Weyl (n=2), and triple-Weyl (n=3) semimetals.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous Hall conductivity in the zero-temperature, zero-chemical-potential limit is sigma_AHE = -n e^2 b_z / (2 pi^2), where n is the topological charge (1, 2, or 3) and b_z is the chiral shift parameter.
- The electromagnetic orbital magnetization in the limit of small chemical potential and zero temperature is M_l = n e mu b_l / (2 pi^2), where mu is the chemical potential and b_l is the chiral shift component.
- The chiral shift parameter b_z is given by b_z = (1/a) arccos((-t_0 - 2 t_2)/t_1), where a is the lattice spacing and t_0, t_1, t_2 are material-dependent energy parameters.
- The quasiparticle width is modeled as Gamma(omega) = Gamma_0 (1 + omega^2 / epsilon_0^2), where epsilon_0 is the characteristic energy scale set by the size of the "dome" between Weyl nodes.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on Bardeen-Zumino currents in lattice models of Weyl semimetals (Gorbar et al., 2017) to thermoelectric phenomena. It contrasts with semiclassical Boltzmann equation approaches used by Kim (2014), Lundgren (2014), and Sharma (2016), which could not naturally reproduce topological response coefficients proportional to the chiral shift. The results enable systematic calculation of anomalous Nernst and thermal Hall effects in multi-Weyl semimetals, and the authors note that the anomalous Nernst effect was previously predicted only when lattice models were employed (Sharma, 2016), being absent in linearized models (Lundgren, 2014).
