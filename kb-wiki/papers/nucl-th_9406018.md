# Temperature Dependence of the Chiral Condensate from an Interacting Pion Gas

> By exploiting the fact that the chiral condensate is related to the derivative of the free-energy density with respect to the bare quark mass we calculate the temperature dependence of the condensate ratio $\ave{\bar qq}_T/\ave{\bar qq}_0$ from an iteracting pion gas. When using the Weinberg Lagrangian at the Hartree level we find a depression of the condensate with temperature. For $T < 100$ MeV the results are in good agreement with chiral perturbation theory in the three loop approximation. Near $T_c$, however, there are marked differences due to non-perturbative nature of our approach.

## Key claim

The chiral condensate ratio decreases with temperature, but in the Hartree approximation using the Weinberg Lagrangian, it reaches a constant asymptotic value of about 0.7 at high temperature rather than monotonically dropping to zero as predicted by chiral perturbation theory (Gerber and Leutwyler, 1993). This difference arises from non-perturbative effects that become important near the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermodynamic potential of an interacting pion gas in thermal and chemical equilibrium using the Weinberg Lagrangian at the Hartree level, which sums an infinite set of self-energy diagrams. The chiral condensate ratio was obtained via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, relating the derivative of the free-energy density with respect to the bare quark mass to the condensate, and then expressed in terms of the derivative of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the pion mass squared. The self-consistent Hartree approximation yielded a temperature-dependent effective pion mass and propagator residue, which were solved numerically from an integral equation for the quantity d(T).

## Key numbers

- For physical pion mass and coupling (fπ = 93 MeV), the chiral condensate ratio reaches a constant asymptotic value of approximately 0.7 at high temperature.
- In the chiral limit (mπ → 0), the critical temperature Tc for the chiral phase transition is identified as the point where the residue of the single-pion propagator becomes imaginary; this occurs when j(Tc) = 1/24λ, and Tc equals the well-known result Tc = √2 fπ.
- At Tc in the chiral limit, the condensate ratio remains non-zero at about 0.7 due to non-perturbative effects.
- For temperatures below 100 MeV, the results agree well with chiral perturbation theory in the three-loop approximation.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gerber and Leutwyler (1993), who used chiral perturbation theory up to three loops (equivalent to a virial expansion in powers of T up to T^8), by employing a non-perturbative Hartree method adapted from phonon-phonon interaction theory in condensed matter physics (Luttinger and Ward, 1960; Carneiro and Pethick, 1975). The results enable improved modeling of the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, particularly near the critical temperature where perturbative methods break down, and suggest that including resonances such as the rho meson and order-parameter fluctuations would provide a more complete description.
