# Neutrino mass limits from SDSS, 2dFGRS and WMAP

> We investigate whether cosmological data suggest the need for massive neutrinos. We employ galaxy power spectrum measurements from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) and the Two Degree Field Galaxy Redshift Survey (2dFGRS), along with cosmic microwave background (CMB) data from the Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe (WMAP) and 27 other CMB experiments. We also use the measurement of the Hubble parameter from the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) Key Project. We find the sum of the neutrino masses to be smaller than 0.75 eV at 2σ(1.1 eV at 3σ).

## Key claim

The sum of neutrino masses is constrained to be less than 0.75 eV at 2σ (1.1 eV at 3σ), but this bound depends on including a prior on the Hubble constant from the Hubble Space Telescope Key Project and on restricting the analysis to linear scales (k less than about 0.15 h/Mpc).

## Method

The authors combined galaxy power spectrum data from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS, 17 band powers) and the Two Degree Field Galaxy Redshift Survey (2dFGRS, 32 band powers) with cosmic microwave background data from WMAP (899 TT and 449 TE power spectrum measurements) and 27 other CMB experiments (151 band powers). They computed theoretical power spectra using CAMB in the linear approximation, assumed a flat universe with a cosmological constant and three neutrino species, and performed a grid-based likelihood analysis over parameters including the total matter density, neutrino fraction, baryon density, Hubble constant, reionization optical depth, spectral index, and two scale-independent galaxy bias parameters.

## Key numbers

- Sum of neutrino masses Σ ≤ 0.75 eV at 2σ (95% confidence level)
- Sum of neutrino masses Σ ≤ 1.1 eV at 3σ (99.7% confidence level)
- Best-fit neutrino fraction f_ν = 0.02, corresponding to Σ ≈ 0.28 eV from the best-fit parameters
- Best-fit Hubble constant h = 0.66 (H_0 = 66 km/s/Mpc)
- Best-fit spectral index n_s = 0.96
- Best-fit baryon density ω_B = 0.023
- Best-fit total matter density ω_M = 0.15
- Best-fit reionization optical depth τ = 0.075
- SDSS bias parameter b_SDSS = 1.13 (+0.08, -0.13) at 1σ
- 2dFGRS bias parameter b_2dF = 1.20 (+0.10, -0.13) at 1σ
- χ²_min = 1499.83 for 1416 degrees of freedom

## Context

This work extends earlier cosmological neutrino mass constraints from the SDSS collaboration (2003), which found Σ ≤ 1.7 eV at 95% C.L. using SDSS and WMAP data alone, and from Hannestad (2003), who found a 95% C.L. upper limit of 1 eV using 2dFGRS, CMB data, and stronger priors. The tighter bound of 0.75 eV results from combining a larger dataset (SDSS, 2dFGRS, WMAP, and 27 other CMB experiments) with an HST prior on the Hubble constant, which breaks the degeneracy between neutrino mass and matter density. The results reject a quasi-degenerate neutrino mass spectrum and enable future lensing measurements to probe a hierarchical spectrum with Σ ≈ 0.04 eV, as discussed by Kaplinghat (2003).
