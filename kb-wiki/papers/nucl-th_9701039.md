# Symmetries of anisotropic harmonic oscillators with rational ratios of frequencies and their relations to U(N) and O(N+1)

> The concept of bisection of a harmonic oscillator or hydrogen atom, used in the past in establishing the connection between U(3) and O(4), is generalized into multisection (trisection, tetrasection, etc). It is then shown that all symmetries of the N-dimensional anisotropic harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies (RHO), some of which are underlying the structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, can be obtained from the U(N) symmetry of the corresponding isotropic oscillator with an appropriate combination of multisections. Furthermore, it is seen that bisections of the N-dimensional hydrogen atom, which possesses an O(N+1) symmetry, lead to the U(N) symmetry, so that further multisections of the hydrogen atom lead to the symmetries of the N-dim RHO. The opposite is in general not true, i.e. multisections of U(N) do not lead to O(N+1) symmetries, the only exception being the occurence of O(4) after the bisection of U(3).

## Key claim

All symmetries of the N-dimensional anisotropic harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies (RHO) can be obtained from the U(N) symmetry of the corresponding isotropic oscillator by applying appropriate combinations of multisections (generalizations of bisection), and also from the O(N+1) symmetry of the N-dimensional hydrogen atom via a bisection followed by further multisections; however, the converse is not generally true, as multisections of U(N) do not lead to O(N+1) symmetries, with the sole exception of the bisection of U(3) yielding O(4).

## Method

The authors generalize the concept of bisection (selecting states with a given parity of a quantum number) to multisection (trisection, tetrasection, etc.), defined by selecting states with a specific value of mod(n_z, n) for the harmonic oscillator or mod(L-M, n) for the hydrogen atom. They apply these operations to the completely symmetric irreducible representations of U(N) (for the isotropic harmonic oscillator) and O(N+1) (for the hydrogen atom) in N=3,4,5,6 dimensions, and tabulate the resulting degeneracy patterns, identifying them with the frequency ratios of the corresponding RHO.

## Key numbers

*   For the 3-dimensional isotropic HO (U(3)), degeneracies are 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21, ... (dimensions of symmetric irreps [N00]).
*   A bisection of U(3) yields degeneracies 1, 2, 4, 6, 9, 12, 16, 20, ... for the 2:2:1 RHO.
*   A trisection of U(3) yields degeneracies 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 9, 12, 15, 18, ... for the 3:3:1 RHO.
*   A tetrasection of U(3) yields degeneracies 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, 18, ... for the 4:4:1 RHO.
*   Two bisections of U(3) yield degeneracies 1, 1, 3, 3, 6, 6, 10, 10, ... for the 2:1:1 RHO.
*   Two trisections of U(3) yield degeneracies 1, 1, 1, 3, 3, 3, 6, 6, 6, ... for the 3:1:1 RHO.
*   For the 4-dimensional HO (U(4)), degeneracies are 1, 4, 10, 20, 35, 56, ... (dimensions of symmetric irreps [N000]).
*   Two bisections of U(4) yield degeneracies 1, 2, 5, 8, 14, 20, 30, 40, 55, 70, ... for the 2:2:1:1 RHO; the numbers 1, 5, 14, 30, 55, ... correspond to integer irreps of O(5), but the half-integer irreps are off by a factor of 2.
*   For the 5-dimensional HO (U(5)), degeneracies are 1, 5, 15, 35, 70, ... (dimensions of symmetric irreps [N0000]).
*   Three bisections of U(5) yield degeneracies 1, 2, 6, 10, 20, 30, 50, 70, 105, 140, 196, ... for the 2:2:1:1:1 RHO; the numbers 1, 6, 20, 50, 105, 196, ... correspond to integer irreps of O(6), but the half-integer irreps are off by a factor of 2.
*   For the 6-dimensional HO (U(6)), degeneracies are 1, 6, 21, 56, ... (dimensions of symmetric irreps [N00000]).
*   Four bisections of U(6) yield degeneracies 1, 2, 7, 12, 27, 42, 77, 112, 182, 252, ... for the 2:2:1:1:1:1 RHO; the numbers 1, 7, 27, 77, 182, ... correspond to integer irreps of O(7), but the half-integer irreps are off by a factor of 4.
*   A bisection of the 3-dimensional hydrogen atom (O(4)) yields degeneracies 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, ... (U(3) degeneracies).

## Context

This work extends the concept of bisection introduced by Ravenhall et al. (Rav) for the 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator and hydrogen atom, which established the connection between U(3) and O(4). The authors generalize this to multisection, showing that all RHO symmetries relevant to superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei (Mot, Rae, Ros, Bha, Naz) and the Bloch-Brink alpha-cluster model (RZ, ZR, Bri) can be derived from U(N) or O(N+1). This unification provides a common algebraic framework for the diverse symmetries of RHOs, enabling further work on constructing an algebra where RHO degeneracies appear as irreducible representations (BDKL).
