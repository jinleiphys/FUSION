# Self-consistent microscopic description of neutron scattering by $^{16}$O based on the continuum particle-vibration coupling method

> The microscopic description of neutron scattering by $^{16}$O below 30 MeV is carried out by means of the continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method with the Skyrme nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) effective interaction. In the cPVC method, a proper boundary condition on a nucleon in continuum states is imposed, which enables one to evaluate the transition matrix in a straightforward manner. Experimental data of the total and total-elastic cross sections are reproduced quite well by the cPVC method. An important feature of the result is the fragmentation of the single-particle resonance into many peaks as well as the shift of its centroid energy. Thus, some part of the fine structure of the experimental cross sections at lower energies is well described by the cPVC framework. The cPVC method based on a real $NN$ effective interaction is found to successfully explain about 85% of the reaction cross section, through explicit channel-coupling effects.

## Key claim

The continuum particle-vibration coupling (cPVC) method, using only a real Skyrme effective interaction with no imaginary part, reproduces about 85% of the experimental reaction cross section for neutron scattering on $^{16}$O below 30 MeV through explicit channel-coupling effects, though the agreement is not perfect and some discrepancy remains at backward angles.

## Method

The authors solve a Dyson equation for the PVC Green function to obtain the transition matrix for neutron-${}^{16}$O elastic scattering, using the Skyrme SkM* interaction for both the Hartree-Fock mean field and the continuum random-phase-approximation (RPA) response. The self-energy, which generates the imaginary part of the optical potential, is constructed from couplings between the neutron and collective phonon states of ${}^{16}$O (multipolarities $2^+, 3^-, 4^+, 5^-$ up to 60 MeV excitation energy) with an orbital angular momentum cutoff of $l_{\rm cut}=7\hbar$. The continuum is treated explicitly by imposing outgoing boundary conditions on the nucleon wave function, and the Fermi momentum for the residual force is set to $k_{\rm F}=1.33$ fm${}^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section $\sigma_{\rm tot}(E)$ converges for incident energies $E \ge 4.0$ MeV when the resolution parameter $\eta = 0.1$ MeV is used.
- The Hartree-Fock calculation overshoots the total-elastic cross section by about 80% above 15 MeV compared to experimental data.
- The cPVC method explains about 85% of the experimental reaction cross section $\sigma_{\rm R}(E)$ on average for $8 \le E \le 30$ MeV.
- The discrepancy between theoretical and experimental $\sigma_{\rm R}(E)$ is about 15% on average over the range $8 \le E \le 30$ MeV.
- The single-particle resonance from the ${\rm f}_{7/2}$ orbit appears around 4.6 MeV in the Hartree-Fock calculation and is fragmented by the PVC.

## Context

This work extends the continuum particle-vibration coupling method previously developed for single-particle structures in ${}^{40}$Ca, ${}^{208}$Pb, and ${}^{24}$O (Mizuyama et al., 2012) to the calculation of neutron scattering observables. It improves upon earlier PVC approaches (Colo et al., 2010; Litvinova et al., 2008; Giai et al., 2009) by treating the continuum explicitly with proper boundary conditions and including a large model space of phonon states up to 60 MeV. The results enable future applications to proton scattering, inelastic scattering, and photo-induced reactions, and suggest that scattering observables can provide new constraints on Skyrme interaction parameters that were previously determined only from bound-state properties.
