# Cold quarks in medium: an equation of state

> We derive a compact, semi-algebraic expression for the cold quark matter equation of state (EoS) in a covariant model that exhibits coincident deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoring transitions in-medium. Along the way we obtain algebraic expressions for: the number- and scalar-density distributions in both the confining Nambu and deconfined Wigner phases; and the vacuum-pressure difference between these phases, which defines a bag constant. The confining interaction materially alters the distribution functions from those of a Fermi gas and consequently has a significant impact on the model's thermodynamic properties, which is apparent in the EoS.

## Key claim
The paper derives a compact, semi-algebraic equation of state for cold quark matter using a covariant model where the confining interaction materially alters the distribution functions from those of a Fermi gas, but the results are limited to a rainbow-truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations and neglect quark Cooper pairing and hadron contributions.

## Method
The authors solve the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger gap equation at zero temperature using a contact interaction (Munczek 1983) that reduces integral equations to algebraic ones. They obtain analytic expressions for the quark number-density and scalar-density distributions in both the confining Nambu (DCSB) and deconfined Wigner (chirally symmetric) phases, then compute the thermodynamic pressure via the steepest-descent approximation to the effective action.

## Key numbers
- Bag constant for two light flavours: \( \mathcal{B}_{N_f=2} = (0.102\,\eta)^4 = (0.111\,\text{GeV})^4 \), with \(\eta \approx 1.09\,\text{GeV}\) set by the rho meson mass
- Critical chemical potential for the first-order transition from Nambu to Wigner phase: \(\mu_{\text{cr}} = 0.276\,\eta = 300\,\text{MeV}\)
- In the Wigner phase at low chemical potential, the zero-momentum number density distribution scales as \( f_1^W(|\vec{p}|=0;\mu) = (2\sqrt{2}/\pi)(\mu/\eta) \) for \(\mu^2 \ll 2\eta^2\)
- The Wigner-phase thermodynamic pressure scales as \( P_{\text{T}}^W \propto \mu^5 \) for \(\mu \lesssim \eta\), in contrast to the noninteracting Fermi gas \( \propto \mu^4 \)

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to cold quark matter, building on prior studies of in-medium propagators (Bender 1997, Chen 2008) that established coincident first-order chiral and deconfinement transitions. It provides analytic results that improve upon the numerical analysis of Blaschke (1997) and corrects the approach of Zong (2008), which used a meromorphic propagator and omitted the vacuum pressure difference. The derived equation of state enables future astrophysical applications to neutron star cores, though it must be augmented by a nuclear matter equation of state for small chemical potentials and by a treatment of hadron contributions omitted in the rainbow truncation.
