# Di-neutron correlation in monopole two-neutron transfer modes in Sn isotope chain

> We study microscopic structures of monopole pair vibrational modes and associated two-neutron transfer amplitudes in neutron-rich Sn isotopes by means of the linear response formalism of the quasiparticle random phase approximation(QRPA). For this purpose we introduce a method to decompose the transfer amplitudes with respect to two-quasiparticle components of the QRPA eigen mode. It is found that pair-addition ibrational modes in neutron-rich $^{132-140}$Sn and the pair rotational modes in $^{142-150}$Sn are commonly characterized by coherent contributions of quasaiparticle states having high orbital angular momenta $l \gesim 5$, which suggests transfer of a spatially correlated neutron pair. The calculation also predicts a high-lying pair vibration, the giant pair vibration, emerging near the one-neutron separation energy in $^{110-130}$Sn, and we find that they have the same di-neutron characters as that of the low-lying pair vibration in $^{132-140}$Sn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-lying pair-addition vibrational modes in neutron-rich \(^{132-140}\)Sn and pair-rotational modes in \(^{142-150}\)Sn are commonly characterized by coherent contributions from many quasiparticle states with high orbital angular momenta (\(l \gtrsim 5\)), which suggests the transfer of a spatially correlated neutron pair (di-neutron). This claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis is performed within a specific theoretical framework (Skyrme-HFB + QRPA with DDDI pairing) and that the di-neutron character is inferred from the angular momentum composition of the QRPA phonon, not directly measured.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model with SLy4 interaction and density-dependent delta pairing (DDDI) to describe pair-correlated ground states of even-even Sn isotopes (\(A=100-150\)). They applied the linear response formalism of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to compute monopole (\(L=0\)) pair-addition and pair-removal strength functions, and introduced a method to decompose the transition densities and QRPA phonon amplitudes into individual two-quasiparticle components. The forward (\(X^{\nu}_{ii'}\)) and backward (\(Y^{\nu}_{ii'}\)) amplitudes were extracted from the linear response solutions, enabling analysis of the microscopic structure of each collective mode.

## Key numbers

* The low-lying pair vibration in \(^{134}\)Sn has excitation energy \(\hbar\omega_{\nu}=3.81\) MeV and pair-addition strength \(B({\rm Pad}0)=3.23\), compared to single-particle strengths \(B_{s.p.}=0.18\) for \([2f_{7/2}]^2\) and \(0.15\) for \([3p_{3/2}]^2\), indicating a collective enhancement factor >10.
* The low-lying pair vibration in stable \(^{120}\)Sn has \(\hbar\omega_{\nu}=2.44\) MeV and \(B({\rm Pad}0)=0.40\), with only three dominant two-quasiparticle components (\([1h_{11/2}]^2\), \([2d_{3/2}]^2\), \([3s_{1/2}]^2\)) having \(|X^{\nu}_{ii'}|>0.1\).
* Two giant pair vibration (GPV) peaks appear in \(^{120}\)Sn at \(\hbar\omega\approx 12.4\) MeV (GPV1) and \(\hbar\omega\approx 15.9\) MeV (GPV2), both below the two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}=16.2\) MeV.
* The transition density tail of the low-lying pair vibration in \(^{134}\)Sn extends to \(r\sim 16\) fm, well beyond the neutron rms radius \(R_{N,rms}=4.93\) fm.
* The forward amplitude for the dominant \([3p_{3/2}]^2\) component in \(^{134}\)Sn is \(X^{\nu}_{ii'}=0.752\), with six other components having \(|X^{\nu}_{ii'}|>0.1\) (including \([1h_{9/2}]^2\), \([2f_{7/2}]^2\), \([2f_{5/2}]^2\), \([1i_{13/2}]^2\), \([3p_{1/2}]^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Shimoyama et al., 2011) on monopole pair vibrations in Sn isotopes by analyzing the microscopic two-quasiparticle structure and identifying di-neutron correlation signatures. It also predicts giant pair vibrations (GPV) in \(^{110-130}\)Sn, a concept previously discussed by Broglia (1977) and Lotti (1989), and shows that these high-lying modes share the same di-neutron character as the low-lying pair vibrations in \(^{132-140}\)Sn. The results enable future experimental searches for di-neutron correlated pair transfer in neutron-rich nuclei using two-neutron transfer reactions, and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting such measurements.
