# Using the $K^+p\to π^+K^+n$ reaction to determine the $Θ^+$ quantum numbers

> We study the $K^+p\to π^+K^+n$ reaction with some kinematics suited to the production of the $Θ^+$ resonance recently observed and show that the measurement of cross sections and polarization observables can shed light on the spin,isospin and parity of the $Θ^+$ state.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the cross section and polarization observables of the \(K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n\) reaction, under the specific kinematic condition of small final pion momentum, can determine the spin, isospin, and parity of the \(\Theta^+\) resonance, but the effectiveness of the method depends on the resonance's quantum numbers, with the clearest signal expected for \(I,J^P=0,1/2^+\).

## Method

The authors model the \(K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n\) reaction using chiral Lagrangians, including pion-pole, contact, and \(\rho\)-exchange diagrams from Oset (1996). They restrict the final \(\pi^+\) momentum to be small relative to the initial kaon momentum to suppress the \(\rho\)-exchange term and other background mechanisms, leaving spin-flip amplitudes proportional to \(\bm{\sigma}\). The \(\Theta^+\) resonance is introduced via final-state interaction in the \(K^+n\) system, with couplings for \(s\)-wave (\(J^P=1/2^-\)) and \(p\)-wave (\(J^P=1/2^+, 3/2^+\)) cases related to the resonance width. The double differential cross section and a spin-flip polarization observable (measuring cross section with initial proton spin \(+1/2\) and final neutron spin \(-1/2\)) are calculated for different quantum number assignments.

## Key numbers

- Initial kaon momentum in the laboratory frame: \(k_{in}(Lab)=850\) MeV/\(c\), corresponding to \(\sqrt{s}=1722\) MeV, allowing \(K^+n\) invariant masses up to \(M_I=1580\) MeV.
- The \(\Theta^+\) resonance mass is around 1540 MeV, as observed by LEPS, DIANA, CLAS, and SAPHIR.
- The calculations assume a \(\Theta^+\) width of 20 MeV, described as an experimental upper bound.
- The spin-flip polarization test at \(\theta=90^\circ\) shows that for \(s\)-wave cases (\(J^P=1/2^-\)) the resonant shape disappears, while for \(I,J^P=0,1/2^+\) a sizeable resonant peak remains.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark, following its experimental observation by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano, 2003) and confirmation by DIANA (Barmin, 2003), CLAS (Stepanyan, 2003), and SAPHIR (Barth, 2003). The reaction \(K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n\) was previously modeled by Oset (1996), and the present paper adapts that model to include the \(\Theta^+\) resonance. The proposed polarization measurement provides a specific test to distinguish between the \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave coupling of the \(\Theta^+\) to \(K^+n\), which would determine its parity, and could confirm the quantum numbers \(I,J^P=0,1/2^+\) predicted by Diakonov (1997).
