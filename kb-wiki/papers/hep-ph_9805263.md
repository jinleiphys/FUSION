# Covariant Model of Relativistic Extended Particles based on the Oscillator Representation of the Poincaré Group

> While internal space-time symmetries of relativistic particles are dictated by the little groups of the Poincaré group, it is possible to construct representations of the little group for massive particles starting from harmonic oscillator wave functions for the quark model. The resulting oscillator wave functions are covariant and can be Lorentz-boosted. It is thus possible to obtain the parton model by boosting the quark model. A review of Wigner's theory of the little groups is given. It is shown that the covariant oscillator wave functions become squeezed as as the system becomes boosted. It is shown also that the Lorentz-squeezed quark distribution exhibits the peculiarities of Feynman's parton model including the lack of coherence in the calculation of cross sections. A historical review of the concept of covariance is given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quark model (valid for slow hadrons) and Feynman’s parton model (valid for hadrons moving near the speed of light) are two different manifestations of the same covariant entity, unified through Lorentz-boosted harmonic oscillator wave functions that undergo squeeze transformations.

## Method

The author constructs representations of Wigner’s O(3)-like little group for massive particles using harmonic oscillator wave functions, then applies Lorentz boosts along the longitudinal direction. The boost is expressed in light-cone coordinates, where the transformation expands one coordinate and contracts the other, preserving the product. The resulting space-time and momentum-energy wave functions are Fourier-transformed to obtain the parton distribution in the infinite-momentum limit.

## Key numbers

- For a proton with energy 900 GeV from the Fermilab accelerator, the ratio of the interaction time to the oscillator period becomes \(10^{-6}\).
- The uncertainty product in light-cone variables is \( \langle u^2 \rangle \langle q_u^2 \rangle = 1/4 \) and \( \langle v^2 \rangle \langle q_v^2 \rangle = 1/4 \), demonstrating Lorentz invariance of Planck’s constant.
- The product of the widths of the longitudinal position and momentum distributions is \( \langle z^2 \rangle \langle q_z^2 \rangle = (1/4)[\cosh(2\eta)]^2 \), which increases with the boost parameter \(\eta\).

## Context

This work extends Wigner’s (1939) theory of little groups for the Poincaré group and the harmonic oscillator quark model of Feynman et al. (1971) and Fujimura et al. (1970). It also builds on the group contraction formalism of Inonu and Wigner (1953) and the observation by Ferrara and Savoy (1982) that the E(2)-like little group for massless particles arises as a Lorentz-boost limit of the O(3)-like little group. The formalism enables a unified description of static quark-model hadrons and fast-moving parton distributions, with Hussar (1981) showing that the Lorentz-boosted oscillator wave function produces a reasonably accurate parton distribution.
