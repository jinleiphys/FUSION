# Searching for flavor dependence in nuclear quark behavior

> The observed correlation between the EMC effect and the contribution of short-range correlations (SRCs) in nuclei suggests that the modification of the quark distributions of bound protons and neutrons might occur within SRCs. This raises the possibility that the EMC effect may have an isospin dependence arising from the np dominance of SRCs. We discuss previous attempts to test this possibility and perform a new analysis of existing data. We find no experimental support for the observation of an isospin dependence of the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The paper finds no experimental support for an isospin dependence of the EMC effect, but the analysis cannot strongly favor either the isospin-independent local density (LD) model or the isospin-dependent high virtuality (HV) model; the LD model yields a universal function with no significant variation with nuclear mass A, while the HV model yields a slope that is nearly 2 standard deviations from zero.

## Method

The authors performed a new analysis of existing EMC effect and short-range correlation (SRC) data, extracting universal modification functions under two competing hypotheses: the HV model (assuming only np SRC pairs contribute, yielding isospin-dependent modification) and the LD model (assuming all short-distance NN pairs contribute equally, yielding isospin-independent modification). They applied consistent isoscalar corrections to all data sets and compared the slopes of the resulting universal functions between x=0.3 and x=0.7 as a function of nuclear mass A.

## Key numbers

- The LD model universal function slope shows no significant variation with A, while the HV model universal function slope is nearly 2 standard deviations from zero when fit to the form slope(A) = m × ln(A) + b.
- The analysis covers the EMC effect region 0.3 < x < 0.7, using the slope of R_EMC(x) = (sigma_A/A)/(sigma_D/2) in this region as the measure of the EMC effect size.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Arrington (2012) which first examined the EMC-SRC correlation under HV and LD assumptions, and responds to the recent analysis of Schmookler (2019) which claimed evidence for an isospin-dependent EMC effect based on the HV model. The paper shows that when both models are tested with consistent isoscalar corrections, the LD model yields a more universal function. The results enable future Jefferson Lab experiments (E12-10-008, E12-06-105, E12-10-103) to better map the EMC-SRC correlation and test isospin dependence through measurements on mirror nuclei, parity-violating DIS, and flavor-tagged measurements.
