# Novel features of attractors and transseries in non-conformal Bjorken flows

> In this work we investigate the impact of conformal symmetry breaking on hydrodynamization of a far-from-equilibrium fluid. We find a new kind of transseries solutions for the non-conformal hydrodynamic equations of a longitudinal boost invariant expanding plasma. The new transseries solutions unveil a rich physical structure which arises due to the interplay of different physical scales. In the perfect fluid case the non-conformal speed of sound slows down the cooling of the temperature due to the emergence of logarithmic corrections that depends on the mass of the particle. These terms propagate into the perturbative and non-perturbative sectors of the transseries once viscous corrections are included. The logarithmic mass contributions increase the asymptotic value of the Knudsen number while decreasing the damping rate of the transient non-hydrodynamic modes and thus, yielding to an extremely slow hydrodynamization process where flow lines merge to their forward attractor at extremely late times. The early time free streaming expansion is modified and receives logarithmic mass corrections induced by the shear-bulk couplings. The global flow structure and numerical analyses carried out in our work demonstrate the existence of the early and late-time attractors for the shear viscous tensor and bulk viscous pressure.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that conformal symmetry breaking in a Bjorken-expanding plasma introduces logarithmic corrections (depending on particle mass) that fundamentally alter the transseries structure of hydrodynamic solutions, leading to an extremely slow hydrodynamization process where flow lines merge to their forward attractor at much later times than in conformal cases.

## Method

The authors study second-order Chapman-Enskog non-conformal fluid dynamics for a longitudinally boost-invariant plasma with massive particle constituents, using the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann equation. They derive new transseries solutions for the temperature, shear viscous tensor, and bulk viscous pressure by applying superasymptotic and hyperasymptotic techniques to the coupled ordinary differential equations. The global flow structure is analyzed by identifying convergent points at early and late times and determining the invariant subspace of flows connecting these points.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound \(c_s^2\) depends on \(z = m/T\) and is given by \(c_s^2(z) = \alpha_1(z)/\alpha_2(z)\), where \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\) are expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions
- The relaxation time is parametrized as \(\tau_R(T) = \theta_0 / T^{\bar{\Delta}}\) with \(\theta_0 > 0\) and \(0 \le \bar{\Delta} \le 1\)
- Five convergent points are found at \(\tau = 0\): three present in the conformal case and two appearing purely due to non-zero mass
- The leading order perfect fluid temperature behaves as \(T_{\rm pf} \sim 1/\log(m\tau)\) at late times
- The energy density in the free streaming case behaves as \(\varepsilon \sim 1/\tau\) for non-interacting systems, modified to \(\varepsilon \sim 1/\tau^\beta\) with conformally invariant interactions

## Context

This work extends the seminal resurgence analysis of Heller and Spalinski (2015) on conformal Bjorken flows to non-conformal fluids with massive constituents. It generalizes transasymptotic techniques developed by Basar (2015), Behtash (2018, 2019, 2020), and others to systems where conformal symmetry is explicitly broken. The results enable future studies of hydrodynamization in more realistic QGP models with physical quark masses and provide a mathematical framework for analyzing attractors in non-conformal relativistic fluids.
