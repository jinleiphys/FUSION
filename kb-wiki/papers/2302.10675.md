# Phases of cold holographic QCD: baryons, pions and rho mesons

> We improve the holographic description of isospin-asymmetric baryonic matter within the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by accounting for a realistic pion mass, computing the pion condensate dynamically, and including rho meson condensation by allowing the gauge field in the bulk to be anisotropic. This description takes into account the coexistence of baryonic matter with pion and rho meson condensates. Our main result is the zero-temperature phase diagram in the plane of baryon and isospin chemical potentials. We find that the effective pion mass in the baryonic medium increases with baryon density and that, as a consequence, there is no pion condensation in neutron-star matter. Our improved description also predicts that baryons are disfavored at low baryon chemical potentials even for arbitrarily large isospin chemical potential. Instead, rho meson condensation sets in on top of the pion condensate at an isospin chemical potential of about $9.4\, m_π$. We further observe a highly non-monotonic phase boundary regarding the disappearance of pion condensation.

## Key claim

The paper improves the holographic description of isospin-asymmetric baryonic matter within the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by including a realistic pion mass, dynamically computing the pion condensate, and allowing rho meson condensation through an anisotropic gauge field in the bulk; the main result is the zero-temperature phase diagram in the plane of baryon and isospin chemical potentials, which shows that the effective pion mass in baryonic matter increases with baryon density and consequently there is no pion condensation in neutron-star matter.

## Method

The authors employ the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model, a top-down holographic approach from type-IIA string theory, working in the confined phase geometry with the Yang-Mills approximation to the Dirac-Born-Infeld action for two flavors. They use the "homogeneous ansatz" where bulk gauge fields depend only on the holographic coordinate, apply a chiral rotation to simplify boundary conditions, and include an effective mass term to account for a non-zero pion mass. The equations of motion for the bulk gauge fields are solved with a generalized anisotropic ansatz that allows for rho meson condensation, and the free energy is minimized to determine the phase boundaries.

## Key numbers

- The onset of rho meson condensation on top of the pion condensate occurs at an isospin chemical potential of about \(9.4\, m_\pi\).
- The parameter fit to reproduce physical meson properties uses \(M_{\rm KK}=949\, {\rm MeV}\), \(\lambda=16.6\), yielding \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV, \(m_\rho \simeq 776\) MeV, and \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\) MeV.
- The onset chemical potential for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is \(\mu_0\simeq 1430\, {\rm MeV}\) with saturation density \(n_0 \simeq 0.43 \, {\rm fm}^{-3}\) for the \(\lambda=16.6\) fit.
- An alternative parameter choice with \(\lambda=7.8\) reproduces \(\mu_0\simeq 923\, {\rm MeV}\) and \(n_0 \simeq 0.15 \, {\rm fm}^{-3}\) at the cost of a smaller \(f_\pi\).
- At \(\mu_B=0\), the second-order transition to rho meson condensation occurs at \(\mu_I=776\, {\rm MeV}\simeq 5.5\, m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic description of isospin-asymmetric baryonic matter from Kovensky, Poole, and Schmitt (2021) by including a realistic pion mass and anisotropic gauge fields, improving upon earlier studies that used an isotropic approximation and chiral limit. The results connect mesonic phases at large isospin chemical potential (previously studied in the WSS model by Aharony, Sonnenschein, and Yankielowicz (2007) in the absence of baryons) with nuclear matter at large baryon chemical potential, and enable predictions for the phase diagram of dense QCD matter relevant to neutron star interiors. The finding that pion condensation is absent in neutron-star matter validates the assumption used in the authors' previous holographic neutron star construction (Kovensky, Poole, and Schmitt, 2021) and is consistent with traditional expectations for s-wave pion condensation.
