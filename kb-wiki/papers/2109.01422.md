# The QCD Equation of State in Small Systems

> We present first results on finite system size corrections to the equation of state, trace anomaly, and speed of sound for two model systems: 1) free, massless scalar theory and 2) quenched QCD with periodic boundary conditions (PBC). We further present work-in-progress results for quenched QCD with Dirichlet boundary conditions.

## Key claim

Finite system size corrections to the QCD equation of state, trace anomaly, and speed of sound are shown to be significant for the small volumes relevant to hadronic collisions, with quenched QCD showing corrections of approximately 20% for the trace anomaly near the phase transition, which will have a quantitative impact on extracted bulk and shear viscosities.

## Method

The authors computed finite-size thermodynamic quantities analytically for a free massless scalar field with Dirichlet boundary conditions in 1, 2, or 3 compactified directions. They then performed lattice simulations of quenched QCD (pure SU(3) gauge theory) on anisotropic spatial volumes with periodic boundary conditions, using the gradient flow definition of the energy-momentum tensor to extract pressure, energy density, and trace anomaly as functions of temperature and system size.

## Key numbers

- For a free massless scalar field with Dirichlet boundary conditions, finite-size effects on pressure are approximately 10% even at T x L approximately 20, and grow as T x L decreases
- For central nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC, initial T x L approximately 8 (using initial temperature approximately 400 MeV and transverse size approximately 4 fm from impact parameter b approximately 4 fm)
- For semi-central collisions (b approximately 9 fm), initial T x L approximately 4
- For high multiplicity p+p collisions (proton radius approximately 1 fm, T approximately 400 MeV), initial T x L approximately 2
- Quenched QCD with periodic boundary conditions shows corrections of approximately 20% for the trace anomaly near the phase transition (T_c approximately 270 MeV)
- Free massless scalar field finite-size corrections reach approximately 50% for p+p collision conditions (T x L approximately 2)

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-volume effects on QCD thermodynamics motivated by the observation that multiparticle correlations in small collision systems are consistent with viscous relativistic hydrodynamics calculations (Weller 2017), which use infinite-volume equations of state. The analytic calculations build on prior work for scalar fields in a box (Mogliacci 2018) and perturbative QCD trace anomaly calculations (Andersen 2004), while the lattice results extend quenched QCD simulations with periodic boundary conditions (Kitazawa 2019). The results enable future work on implementing Dirichlet boundary conditions in lattice simulations (Rothkopf 2021) and extending to full QCD with fermions, which the authors anticipate will increase the magnitude of finite-size corrections.
