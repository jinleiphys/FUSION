# Pairwise final state interactions in the p d --> p d eta reaction near threshold

> A model for the p d --> p d eta reaction published earlier, including the final state interaction (FSI) of all particles, is revisited to investigate the low energy data on this reaction. The three body problem of p-d-eta scattering in the final state is approximated in terms of pairwise interactions between the three particles in the final state. Apart from a comparison with some preliminary data, two new findings relevant to the near threshold data analysis are reported. The first one points toward the limitations of an FSI factor used conventionally to extract the eta-deuteron scattering length and infer subsequently on the existence of eta-mesic states. The second result emphasizes the role of the $p-d$ FSI and the strong Coulomb repulsion near threshold. Finally, a comparison of the above model calculation with low energy data, excludes very large eta-nucleon scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The conventional factorization of the reaction amplitude into a production amplitude and a final-state-interaction (FSI) factor, used to extract the eta-deuteron scattering length from near-threshold data, can be misleading because the full calculation including all pairwise interactions gives a different energy dependence; additionally, the proton-deuteron FSI (including Coulomb repulsion) is stronger at low energies than previously appreciated.

## Method

The authors model the p d → p d η reaction using a two-step mechanism: first, a proton interacts with a nucleon in the deuteron to produce a deuteron and a pion (p p(n) → d π⁺(π⁰)), then the pion interacts with another nucleon to produce an η (π N → η N). The final-state three-body problem is approximated by including pairwise interactions: the η-d interaction is treated by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for η-d elastic scattering using an on-shell t-matrix constructed from the η-d scattering length, while the p-d interaction is included via an inverse Jost function that accounts for both strong and Coulomb interactions.

## Key numbers

- The η-d scattering length A_ηd = 1.64 + i 2.99 fm (from Ref. 20, using a_ηN = 0.55 + i 0.30 fm) is mentioned as a value preferred by WASA data analysis.
- The p-d bound state in ³He has a separation energy |E_B| = 5.48 MeV, used in the doublet Jost function factor (1 + |E_B|/E).
- The p-d FSI enhances the cross section by a factor of 3 to 6 at low relative momenta, after accounting for Coulomb repulsion.
- The best agreement with experimental data comes from η-nucleon scattering lengths a_ηN = 0.44 + i 0.30 fm and a_ηN = 0.579 + i 0.399 fm, which correspond to η-d scattering lengths of (1.15 + i 1.89) fm and (0.34 + i 3.31) fm respectively (from Ref. 20).
- Using different deuteron wave functions (Paris, Hulthen, Gaussian) changes total cross sections by at most a factor of 1.6 at higher excess energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Upadhyay et al., 2008) for the p d → p d η reaction to low excess energies (Q < 14 MeV), where data exist from SATURNE (Q = 1.1 and 3.3 MeV) and COSY-11 (Q = 3.2, 6.1, and 9.2 MeV). It contradicts the conventional approach of using a simple FSI factor (Eq. 1) to extract the η-d scattering length, as done in earlier analyses of WASA data (Bilger et al., 2004). The results enable more reliable extraction of the η-d scattering length and constrain the η-N scattering length, excluding very large values; they also inform experimental searches for η-mesic states in few-nucleon systems.
