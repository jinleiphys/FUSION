# Lambda-Nucleon and Sigma-Nucleon potentials from space-time correlation function on the lattice

> The hyperon-nucleon interaction with the strangeness $S=-1$ region is complicated and difficult to investigate because its flavor sector involves all the irreducible representation except the flavor singlet and has the worst signal-to-noise ratio among the strangeness regions. In order to overcome such difficulties the content of this report is twofold: (i) We present an implementation of extended effective baryon block algorithm. This is a straightforward extension of the original which was reported in LATTICE 2013. (ii) We perform single channel analysis for the $ΛN$ system at nearly physical quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx(146,525)$~MeV and large volume $(La)^4=(96a)^4\approx$ (8.1 fm)$^4$. Scattering phase shifts for $ΛN$ system are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a single-channel analysis of the $\Lambda N$ system that reduces statistical uncertainty compared to the earlier coupled-channel analysis, but the results are valid only below the $\Sigma N$ threshold and the effects of coupling to the $\Sigma N$ channel are implicitly included rather than explicitly treated.

## Method

The authors employ the HAL QCD method to extract $\Lambda N$ potentials from four-point correlation functions computed on a lattice using an extended effective baryon block algorithm with Fast Fourier Transform. The potentials are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation for the $R$-correlator projected onto $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channels, and scattering phase shifts are derived by parametrizing the lattice potentials with an analytic functional form.

## Key numbers

- Pion and kaon masses: $(m_\pi, m_K) \approx (146, 525)$ MeV at nearly physical quark masses
- Lattice volume: $(La)^4 = (96a)^4 \approx (8.1\ \mathrm{fm})^4$
- Number of iterations to evaluate the four-point correlator using the effective block algorithm: 370, compared to naive counting of 3,981,312
- Number of baryon block types needed for the $\Lambda_{\mathrm{dsu}}$ interpolating field in the full calculation of 52 correlation functions: 2,584 total declarations across the eight classification forms

## Context

This work extends the earlier coupled-channel $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ analysis reported by Nemura at LATTICE 2017, which suffered from large statistical noise in the $\Sigma N-\Sigma N$ potential. The single-channel approach enables extraction of scattering phase shifts below the $\Sigma N$ threshold with reduced uncertainty, and the extended effective baryon block algorithm allows simultaneous computation of 52 baryon-baryon correlation channels from $NN$ to $\Xi\Xi$, enabling future comprehensive studies of hyperonic nuclear forces relevant to neutron star structure.
