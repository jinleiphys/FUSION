# Data-driven reevaluation of $ft$-values in superallowed $β$ decays

> We present a comprehensive re-evaluation of the $ft$ values in superallowed nuclear $β$ decays crucial for the precise determination of $V_{ud}$ and low-energy tests of the electroweak Standard Model. It consists of the first, fully data-driven analysis of the nuclear $β$ decay form factor, that utilizes isospin relations to connect the nuclear charged weak distribution to the measurable charge distributions. This prescription supersedes previous shell-model estimations, and allows for a rigorous quantification of theory uncertainties in $f$ which is absent in the existing literature. Our new evaluation shows an overall downward shift of the central values of $f$ at the level of 0.01\%.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first fully data-driven analysis of the nuclear β decay form factor in superallowed transitions, using isospin relations to connect the nuclear charged weak distribution to measurable charge distributions, which supersedes previous shell-model estimations and allows rigorous quantification of theory uncertainties in the statistical rate function f that was absent in existing literature; the new evaluation shows an overall downward shift of central values of f at the level of 0.01%.

## Method
The authors evaluated the statistical rate function f for superallowed β⁺ decays by solving the full Dirac equation numerically with a given nuclear charge distribution to obtain the Fermi function, and used the isospin formalism to relate the charged weak distribution ρ_cw(r) to the charge distributions of members of the superallowed isotriplet via the relation ρ_cw(r) = ρ_ch,1(r) + Z₀(ρ_ch,0(r) − ρ_ch,1(r)). They selected nuclear charge distribution data from compilations including Angeli and Marinova (2013) and Li et al. (2021), supplemented by newer measurements, and applied a selection criteria based on accuracy and precision to determine secondary distribution parameters from the de Vries et al. (1987) compilation.

## Key numbers
- The new evaluation shows an overall downward shift of central values of f at the level of 0.01%.
- The k=2 correction to f is smaller than 0.0003% for all measured transitions up to A=74.
- For ¹⁸Ne, the RMS charge radius is 2.9714(76) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ¹⁸O, the RMS charge radius is 2.7726(56) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ²²Mg, the RMS charge radius is 3.0691(89) fm (Li et al., 2021).
- For ²²Ne, the RMS charge radius is 2.9525(40) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ²⁶ᵐAl, the RMS charge radius is 3.130(15) fm (Plattner, 2023).
- For ²⁶Mg, the RMS charge radius is 3.0337(18) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ³⁴Ar, the RMS charge radius is 3.3654(40) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ³⁴S, the RMS charge radius is 3.2847(21) fm (Angeli, 2013).
- For ³⁸Ca, the RMS charge radius is 3.467(1) fm (Miller, 2019).

## Context
This work extends the approach pioneered by Seng (2022) that connects the charged weak distribution to charge distributions using model-independent isospin relations, superseding the shell-model estimations used in the series of reviews by Hardy and Towner (2004, 2008, 2014, 2020). The new data-driven method enables a more rigorous extraction of V_ud from superallowed β decays with quantifiable theory uncertainties, addressing the current 2.8σ deficit in first-row CKM unitarity reported by Cirigliano (2022) and the mild tension between V_ud values from superallowed decays (0.97361(31) from Hardy, 2020) and neutron decay (0.97404(42) from Gorchtein, 2023).
