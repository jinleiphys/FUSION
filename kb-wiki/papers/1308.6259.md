# Emissivity and conductivity of parton-hadron matter

> We investigate the properties of the QCD matter across the deconfinement phase transition. In the scope of the parton-hadron string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, we study the strongly interacting matter in equilibrium as well as the out-of equilibrium dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We present here in particular the results on the electromagnetic radiation, i.e. photon and dilepton production, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the relevant correlator in equilibrium, i.e. the electric conductivity. By comparing our calculations for the heavy-ion collisions to the available data, we determine the relative importance of the various production sources and address the possible origin of the observed strong elliptic flow $v_2$ of direct photons.

## Key claim

The strong elliptic flow \(v_2\) of direct photons observed by PHENIX in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV can be explained within the Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach without invoking unconventional physics, provided that the experimental subtraction procedure for hadron decay backgrounds is carefully accounted for; the direct photon \(v_2\) in PHSD is attributed primarily to hadronic binary meson reactions that are not subtracted in the data, with the QGP contributing only indirectly through the flow of the parent hadrons.

## Method

The authors employed the covariant off-shell transport approach Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD), which describes the full evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions from initial hard scatterings through the deconfinement phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and subsequent hadronization. The electric conductivity \(\sigma_0\) was computed for interacting partonic/hadronic systems in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions by measuring the response to an external electric field. Photon production in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV was calculated including partonic channels (\(q+\bar q\to g+\gamma\), \(q(\bar q)+g\to q(\bar q)+\gamma\)), hadronic interactions (\(\pi+\pi\to\rho+\gamma\), \(\rho+\pi\to\pi+\gamma\), meson-meson bremsstrahlung), and hadron decays, with the elliptic flow of direct photons extracted using two different subtraction procedures.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(\sigma_0/T\) in the partonic phase for \(T_c \le T \le 2.2 T_c\) is described by \(\sigma_0(T)/T \approx 0.01 + 0.16 (T-T_c)/T_c\), where \(T_c = 158\) MeV.
- The ratio \(R_\gamma = N^{incl}/N^{BG}\) (inclusive to background photon yield) extracted from dileptons in the invariant mass window \(M=0.15-0.3\) GeV is approximately \(R_\gamma \approx 1.2\).
- The ratio \(R_\gamma\) extracted from the calculated real photon spectrum in PHSD is approximately \(R_\gamma \approx 1.08\).
- The pQCD prediction for the asymptotic value of \(\sigma_0/T\) is approximately 65 in leading order of the coupling.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD transport approach studies of electromagnetic probes (Linnyk et al., 2010-2013) and the electric conductivity calculations (Cassing et al., 2013) to address the puzzle of the large direct photon elliptic flow reported by the PHENIX Collaboration (PHENIX, 2012). The results contradict earlier theoretical expectations and predictions (Chatterjee, 2005; Liu, 2009; Dion, 2011) that QGP photons should show little flow, and suggest that the observed strong \(v_2\) arises from hadronic binary reactions rather than unconventional sources such as pre-equilibrium gluon interactions with magnetic fields (Bzdak, 2012; Basar, 2012) or enhanced emission at the QGP surface (Goloviznin, 2012). The findings enable a more nuanced interpretation of direct photon data, indicating that the QGP contributes indirectly through the flow of parent hadrons rather than through direct photon emission.
