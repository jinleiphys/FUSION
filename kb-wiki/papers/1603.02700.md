# Insight from elliptic flow of open charm mesons using quark coalescence model at RHIC and LHC energies

> A study of elliptic flow of open charm mesons, $D^0$ and $D_S ^\pm$ using quark coalescence as a mechanism of hadronization of heavy quarks implemented in conjunction with A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model has been presented. We have studied the transverse momentum dependence of the elliptic flow parameter at mid-rapidity ($|y|$ $<$ 1.0) for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (LHC) for different values of partonic interaction cross-section and QCD coupling constant. We have compared our calculations with the experimentally measured data at the LHC energy. We have also studied the effect of shear viscosity on elliptic flow of open charm mesons within the transport model approach. Our study indicates that the elliptic flow of open charmed mesons is more sensitive to viscous properties of QGP medium as compared to light charged hadrons.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of open charm mesons is more sensitive to the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma than that of light charged hadrons, with the ratio of v₂ for different viscosities showing a 30–40% change for D⁰ mesons compared to ~15% for charged hadrons at low pT, though this conclusion depends on the specific tuning of the AMPT model parameters.

## Method

The authors used the string melting mode of the AMPT model (version 2.26) to generate parton phase-space information at freezeout, then applied a quark coalescence mechanism to form D⁰ and Dₛ⁺ mesons from charm, up/down, and strange quarks. The parton-parton interaction cross-section was varied (3 mb and 10 mb at RHIC; 1.5 mb and 10 mb at LHC), and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was tuned by adjusting the QCD coupling constant and screening mass while keeping the cross-section fixed at 10 mb. Elliptic flow was calculated at mid-rapidity (|y| < 1.0) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV with σPP = 10 mb, the v₂ of charm quarks is smaller than that of u/d and s quarks for pT < 2.0 GeV/c, consistent with early freezeout of charm quarks due to their large mass (1.35 GeV for c quark, 6 MeV for u, 199 MeV for s).
- The ratio of v₂ for ηs/s = 0.08 to v₂ for ηs/s = 0.18 shows a change of ~15% for charged hadrons and 30–40% for D⁰ mesons for pT < 2.0 GeV/c in 10–40% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- At LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV), model calculations with LHC-tuned parameters (a = 0.5, b = 0.9 GeV⁻², αs = 0.33) for parton cross-sections of 1.5 mb and 10 mb both under-predict the measured D-meson v₂ from ALICE for pT > 2 GeV/c in 30–50% central Pb+Pb collisions.
- The size parameter σ for the harmonic oscillator wave function used in coalescence is 0.47 fm².

## Context

This work extends the use of the AMPT model with quark coalescence, previously applied to light hadrons by Lin et al. (2005) and to charged hadron v₂ at RHIC and LHC, to open charm mesons. It builds on studies of D⁰ and Dₛ⁺ production at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2014) and LHC (ALICE Collaboration, 2015) and on the observation of number-of-constituent-quark scaling for light hadrons. The finding that charm meson v₂ is more sensitive to shear viscosity than light hadron v₂ suggests that high-precision measurements of open charm v₂ at low pT, particularly from the planned ALICE upgrade, could provide constraints on the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma at early times.
