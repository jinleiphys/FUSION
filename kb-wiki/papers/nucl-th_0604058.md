# Ferromagnetic instabilities in neutron matter at finite temperature with the Gogny interaction

> The properties of spin polarized neutron matter are studied both at zero and finite temperature using the D1 and the D1P parameterizations of the Gogny interaction. The results show two different behaviors: whereas the D1P force exhibits a ferromagnetic transition at a density of $ρ_c \sim 1.31$ fm$^{-3}$ whose onset increases with temperature, no sign of such a transition is found for D1 at any density and temperature, in agreement with recent microscopic calculations.

## Key claim

The D1P parameterization of the Gogny interaction predicts a ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter at a critical density of approximately 1.31 fm\(^{-3}\) at zero temperature, with the onset density increasing with temperature; however, the D1 parameterization shows no sign of such a transition at any density or temperature, in agreement with recent microscopic calculations.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree–Fock calculations of spin-polarized neutron matter at zero and finite temperature using the D1 and D1P parameterizations of the Gogny interaction. They computed the energy per particle, entropy per particle, and free energy per particle from self-consistent momentum distributions, and extracted the inverse magnetic susceptibility from the curvature of the free energy with respect to the spin polarization parameter \(\Delta\). The single-particle potentials and effective masses were analyzed, and a quadratic approximation to the single-particle spectrum was used to study the entropy behavior.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for ferromagnetic transition with D1P at T=0 MeV: \(\rho_c = 1.31\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Isospin instability density for D1P: \(\rho_I \sim 7 \rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- Isospin instability density for D1: \(\rho_I \sim 3 \rho_0\).
- Spinodal instability critical density for D1 and D1S: \(\rho_S \sim 4 \rho_0\).
- Discrepancy between exact and approximated entropy per particle: not larger than 3%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ferromagnetic instabilities in neutron matter, which had been addressed with Skyrme interactions (Rios 2005) and with Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculations using realistic interactions (Vidana 2002a, 2002b; Bombaci 2006). The results show that the Gogny D1P force, unlike Skyrme forces, yields a critical density above 1 fm\(^{-3}\) and a thermal behavior consistent with intuition (critical density increases with temperature), while the D1 force agrees with microscopic calculations that exclude a ferromagnetic transition up to high densities. The analysis of the entropy and effective masses provides a criterion to understand the temperature dependence of the transition.
