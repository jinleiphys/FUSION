# Variation of Fundamental Couplings and Nuclear Forces

> The dependence of the nuclear force on standard model parameters plays an important role in bounding time and space variations of fundamental couplings over cosmological time scales. We discuss the quark-mass dependence of deuteron and di-neutron binding in a systematic chiral expansion. The leading quark-mass dependence of the nuclear force arises from one-pion exchange and from local quark-mass dependent four-nucleon operators with coefficients that are presently unknown. By varying these coefficients while leaving nuclear observables at the physical values of the quark masses invariant, we find scenarios where two-nucleon physics depends both weakly and strongly on the quark masses. While the determination of these coefficients is an exciting future opportunity for lattice QCD, we conclude that, at present, bounds on time and space variations of fundamental parameters from the two-nucleon sector are much weaker than previously claimed. This brings into question the reliability of coupling-constant bounds derived from more complex nuclei and nuclear processes.

# Variation of Fundamental Couplings and Nuclear Forces

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that, due to presently unknown coefficients of quark-mass-dependent four-nucleon operators, the two-nucleon sector (deuteron and di-neutron) can exhibit either weak or strong dependence on quark masses depending on the choice of these coefficients; consequently, bounds on time and space variations of fundamental parameters derived from the two-nucleon sector are much weaker than previously claimed, bringing into question the reliability of coupling-constant bounds derived from more complex nuclei.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) with chiral symmetry to compute the quark-mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes in the singlet S-wave (1S0) channel and the triplet S-wave / D-wave (3S1-3D1) coupled channels at next-to-leading order (NLO). For the 1S0 channel they use dimensional regularization with power-divergence subtraction, while for the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels they employ a position-space square-well regularization, fixing the renormalized coefficients C0, D2, and C2 to reproduce the physical scattering length and effective range (1S0) or deuteron binding energy and effective range (3S1-3D1), then varying the unknown D2 coefficient within naturalness bounds to explore the range of possible quark-mass dependences.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy at physical pion mass: B_d = 2.224644 ± 0.000034 MeV
- 1S0 scattering length at physical pion mass: a^(1S0) = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm
- 1S0 effective range at physical pion mass: r^(1S0) = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm
- Physical pion mass used: m_pi = 139 MeV; physical pion decay constant: f_pi = 135 MeV; axial coupling: g_A = 1.25
- For a "weak-dependence" parameter set in the 1S0 channel: C0 = -4.09 fm^2, D2 = 0.50 fm^4, C2 = 4.94 fm^4 (at physical m_pi); the scattering length remains negative (no di-neutron) over the range m_pi = 60-180 MeV
- For a "strong-dependence" parameter set in the 1S0 channel: C0 = -4.00 fm^2, D2 = 0.31 fm^4, C2 = 4.57 fm^4; the scattering length becomes positive (di-neutron bound) for some range of pion masses
- For a "weak-dependence" parameter set in the 3S1-3D1 channel: C0 = -6.17 fm^2, D2 = 0.67 fm^4, C2 = 0.75 fm^4 (at square-well radius R* = 0.45 fm); the deuteron remains loosely bound over a wide range of pion masses
- For a "strong-dependence" parameter set in the 3S1-3D1 channel: C0 = -5.05 fm^2, D2 = -1.60 fm^4, C2 = 0.75 fm^4; the deuteron becomes unbound for m_pi less than approximately 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Bedaque, Beane, Savage, and van Kolck (BBSvK, 2001) to systematically study the quark-mass dependence of two-nucleon systems, and critically reexamines earlier claims by Dent (2000), Fairbairn (2000), and Shuryak (2000) who used naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to derive bounds on fundamental coupling variations from nuclear processes. The authors show that the unknown coefficients of quark-mass-dependent four-nucleon operators (D2) prevent any firm bounds from the two-nucleon sector at present, and that lattice QCD determinations of these coefficients will be required to make progress. This work also provides the theoretical framework needed to connect future lattice QCD calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering at unphysical quark masses to the physical world.
