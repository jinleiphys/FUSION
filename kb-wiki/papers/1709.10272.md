# Charge-exchange dipole excitations in neutron-rich nuclei: $-1 \hbar ω_0$, anti-analog pygmy, and anti-analog giant resonances

> The occurrence of the low-lying charge-exchange non spin-flip dipole modes below the giant resonance in neutron-rich nuclei is predicted on the basis of nuclear density functional theory. The ground and excited states are described in the framework of the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and the proton-neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation employing a Skyrme-type energy density functional. The model calculations are performed for the spherical neutron-rich Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes. It is found that the low-lying states appear sensitively to the shell structure associated with the $-1 \hbar ω_0$ excitation below the Gamow-Teller states. Furthermore, the pygmy resonance emerges below the giant resonance when the neutrons occupy the low-$\ell (\ell \leq 2 -3)$ orbitals analogous to the pygmy resonance seen in the electric dipole response.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the occurrence of low-lying charge-exchange non spin-flip dipole modes below the giant resonance in neutron-rich nuclei, specifically the $-1 \hbar \omega_0$ excitation and an anti-analog pygmy dipole resonance (APDR), but the emergence of these modes is sensitive to the shell structure and the occupation of low-$\ell$ neutron orbitals, and the results depend on the energy density functional employed.

## Method

The ground states of spherical neutron-rich Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes were described using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the SkM* Skyrme energy density functional, solved in coordinate space with a mesh size of 0.6 fm and a box boundary condition at 14.7 fm. Excited states were then calculated using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a two-quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV, where the residual interaction was derived self-consistently from the same energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- For $^{86}$Ni, the pygmy resonance appears at a QRPA energy $\hbar \omega = 25.8$ MeV (excitation energy $E_{\rm T} = 7.8$ MeV), while the giant resonance appears at $\hbar \omega = 35.4$ MeV ($E_{\rm T} = 17.4$ MeV).
- For $^{54}$Ca, the state below the giant resonance appears at $\hbar \omega = 23.0$ MeV ($E_{\rm T} = 10.6$ MeV), and the giant resonance at $\hbar \omega = 31.2$ MeV ($E_{\rm T} = 18.8$ MeV).
- The low-energy $-1 \hbar \omega_0$ strength was evaluated by summing strengths below a QRPA energy of $\hbar \omega = 15$ MeV.
- The pygmy resonance strength was evaluated in the QRPA energy range $15\ {\rm MeV} < \hbar \omega < \hbar \bar{\omega} - \Gamma$, with a giant resonance width parameter $\Gamma = 7$ MeV.
- A sudden jump in the pygmy resonance fraction occurs at neutron numbers $N=28 \to 30$, $50 \to 52$, and a moderate increase at $N=82 \to 84$.
- The mean excitation energy of the $1 \hbar \omega_0$ states with respect to the isobaric analog state (IAS), divided by $A^{-1/3}$, shows a negative correlation with the calculated neutron skin thickness for Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the study of charge-exchange dipole resonances, which were first observed in the 1980s via ($p,n$) reactions (Bai80, Ste80), and builds on recent efforts to separate the anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR) from the spin-flip dipole resonance using polarized proton beams (Yas13). The paper also connects to studies of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in electric dipole response (Ina11, Ina13, Eba14), where low-$\ell$ neutron orbitals play a decisive role. The findings enable future extraction of neutron skin thickness from measurements of the AGDR and APDR excitation energies, following the approach proposed by Kra13 and Cao15, and may impact calculations of $\beta$-decay rates and $\beta$-delayed neutron emission probabilities for $r$-process waiting-point nuclei.
