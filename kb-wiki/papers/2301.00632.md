# Investigating the Role of Electric Fields on Flow Harmonics in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Using the blast-wave model, we explore the effect of electric fields on spectra and flow harmonics (especially the elliptic flow) for charged pions and protons. We incorporate the first-order correction to the single-particle distribution function due to the electric fields and the dissipative effect while calculating the invariant yields of hadron in the Cooper-Frey prescription at the freezeout hypersurface. We find a noticeable correction to the directed and elliptic flow of pions and protons for unidirectional and azimuthal asymmetric electric fields in the transverse plane of magnitude $\sim m_π^{2}$. Further, we observe mass dependency of the directed flow generated due to the electric fields. The splitting of particle and antiparticle's elliptic flow is also discussed.

## Key claim

Using a blast-wave model with first-order viscous corrections, the authors find that electric fields of magnitude ~m_π^2 can produce noticeable corrections to directed and elliptic flow of charged pions and protons, but only for anisotropic field configurations in the transverse plane; isotropic fields leave flow harmonics unchanged.

## Method

The authors employ a blast-wave model with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and parameterized transverse fluid velocity that generates elliptic flow. They incorporate first-order non-equilibrium corrections to the single-particle distribution function from both viscosity (via a relaxation time τ_c) and electric fields, then compute invariant yields using the Cooper-Frye formalism on a constant-temperature freeze-out hypersurface. Four different parameterized electric field configurations in the transverse plane are tested: isotropic (config-1), prolate-like (config-2), oblate-like (config-3), and unidirectional (config-4).

## Key numbers

- Electric field magnitude is set to ~m_π^2 (approximately 0.0195 GeV^2) for all configurations
- Blast-wave parameters for π^+: u_0 = 1.2, T = 130 MeV, R = 10 fm, τ = 6 fm, c_2 = 0.1, m = 139.5 MeV
- Blast-wave parameters for protons: u_0 = 1.22, T = 140 MeV, R = 10 fm, τ = 6 fm, c_2 = 0.15, m = 938 MeV
- Relaxation time τ_c set to 1 fm for viscous cases
- For config-4 (unidirectional field), fitted v_1 = 0.218 ± 1.0e-07 for π^+ and v_1 = 0.081 ± 7.9e-08 for protons
- For config-4, fitted v_2 = 0.064 ± 9.8e-08 for π^+ and v_2 = 0.096 ± 7.9e-08 for protons

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic field effects on heavy-ion collision observables, particularly following studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect by Kharzeev (2007) and others, and earlier work on electric field effects in heavy-ion collisions by PhysRevC.89.054905 and PhysRevC.98.055201. The authors note that a more accurate estimate would require full relativistic resistive magnetohydrodynamic evolution as in Nakamura (2022) or Inghirami (2016), rather than the blast-wave parameterization used here. The study enables future investigations of how second-order transport coefficients arising from magnetic fields (whose temperature and mass dependence is shown for coefficients such as δ_πB, δ_VB, and others) might further modify these results.
