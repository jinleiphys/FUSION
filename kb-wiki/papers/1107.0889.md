# Photons at RHIC: The role of viscosity and of initial state fluctuations

> We study real photons produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, and we calculate their spectrum and its azimuthal momentum anisotropy. The photons from a variety of sources are included, and the interplay and the time-evolution of those sources are modelled in a full 3D hydrodynamic simulation. We quantify the v_2 of thermal photons produced in ideal and viscous fluids, and the consequences of using different initial conditions are explored.

# Photons at RHIC: The role of viscosity and of initial state fluctuations

## Key claim

The authors show that shear viscosity reduces the azimuthal momentum anisotropy (v₂) of thermal photons produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, making a small signal even smaller, while fluctuating initial conditions raise the photon v₂ compared to smooth averaged initial conditions. These results are conditional on using a full 3D viscous hydrodynamic simulation (MUSIC) with specific viscous corrections to the photon production rates.

## Method

The authors model Au-Au collisions at RHIC using the 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic code MUSIC, which solves conservation laws for the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current. They include photon production from multiple sources: pQCD prompt photons, thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron gas (HG), and photons from jet-plasma interactions. The thermal photon rates are computed using out-of-equilibrium distribution functions modified by shear viscosity, and the yields are obtained by integrating these rates over the hydrodynamic evolution. The authors compare results using smooth averaged initial conditions (AIC) versus fluctuating initial conditions (FIC).

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon spectrum shows non-negligible changes from viscous corrections at intermediate transverse momentum, using η/s = 1/(4π) where s is entropy density.
- The net v₂ coefficient for thermal photons is reduced by shear viscous effects compared to the ideal hydrodynamic case, for conditions prevalent at RHIC.
- Fluctuating initial conditions raise the photon v₂ relative to averaged initial conditions in the 0-20% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends earlier photon studies by Chatterjee (2005) and Heinz (2006) who showed that azimuthal anisotropy of photons is sensitive to the underlying degrees of freedom, and by Alver (2010) who linked fluctuating initial conditions to odd components of momentum asymmetry. The authors provide the first photon yield and v₂ results obtained in a realistic 3D viscous hydrodynamics approach, using the MUSIC code previously shown to reproduce hadronic spectra (Schenke 2010). This enables future comparisons with LHC photon data and further theoretical investigations of viscous effects on electromagnetic probes.
