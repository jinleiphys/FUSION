# Heavy mesons with dynamical gluon on the light front

> We investigate the structure of charmonium, bottomonium, and $\rm B_c$ meson systems within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach, including both the quark-antiquark ($|q\bar{q}\rangle$) and quark-antiquark-gluon ($|q\bar{q}g\rangle$) Fock sectors. Our input light-front Hamiltonian incorporates a confining potential inspired by light-front holography, as well as the quark-gluon interaction from Quantum Chromodynamics. By adjusting model parameters to reproduce the mass spectra for low-lying states, we obtain the light-front wave functions for the heavy meson states. Based on these wave functions, we calculate electromagnetic form factors, decay constants, parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), and parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the quarks and gluons in the heavy mesons. Our results for the charge radii and decay constants reasonably agree with experimental data and other theoretical approaches. The PDAs are consistent with the predictions from the earlier BLFQ calculations with an effective one-gluon exchange interaction. Furthermore, we present the first predictions within the BLFQ framework for the gluon PDFs in heavy mesons based on the light-front wave function in the $|q\bar{q}g\rangle$ sector.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach for heavy mesons by including both the quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors, rather than relying on an effective one-gluon exchange interaction as in prior work. The agreement with experimental mass spectra is achieved only after fitting model parameters to PDG data, and the results show improved rms mass deviations compared to the earlier effective OGE approach.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the light-front mass eigenvalue equation within the BLFQ framework using a basis of plane waves in the longitudinal direction and two-dimensional harmonic oscillator wave functions in the transverse direction, with truncation parameters Nmax=12 and K=17. The input Hamiltonian includes a confining potential inspired by light-front holography, QCD quark-gluon interactions, and a regularization scheme involving a multiplicative function applied to the instantaneous gluon exchange interaction to balance ultraviolet cutoffs between dynamical and instantaneous gluons. Model parameters (quark masses, vertex masses, effective gluon mass, basis scale, and instantaneous regulator scale) are determined by fitting the mass spectra of low-lying charmonium and bottomonium states to experimental data from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium, the rms mass deviation between the lowest 8 states and PDG data is 17.24 MeV in the MJ=0 sector, compared to 31 MeV from the earlier BLFQ OGE calculation (Li and Tang, prior work)
- For bottomonium, the rms mass deviation is 23.40 MeV in the MJ=0 sector, compared to 38 MeV from the earlier BLFQ OGE calculation
- The strong coupling constant is set to alpha_s=0.30 for charmonium and alpha_s=0.23 for bottomonium
- The leading Fock sector probability N_qq^2 for the J/psi(1S) is 0.639, for the Upsilon(1S) is 0.815, and for the B_c(1S) is 0.724
- The effective gluon mass m_g is set to 0.50 GeV for all three systems
- The basis scale parameter b is 1.23 GeV for charmonium, 1.84 GeV for bottomonium, and 1.56 GeV for B_c mesons

## Context

This work extends prior BLFQ studies of heavy mesons by Li and Tang (prior work) that employed only the leading |qq̅⟩ Fock sector with an effective one-gluon exchange interaction, by explicitly including a dynamical gluon in the |qq̅g⟩ sector. The approach is similar to that applied to light mesons by Lan and collaborators (prior work) and to the nucleon by the same group. The results enable first BLFQ predictions for gluon parton distribution functions in heavy mesons and provide a more realistic initial condition for QCD evolution to higher scales.
