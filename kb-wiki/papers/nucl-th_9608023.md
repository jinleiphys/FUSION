# Change of MIT Bag Constant in Nuclear Medium and Implication for the EMC Effect

> The modified quark-meson coupling model, which features a density dependent bag constant and bag radius in nuclear matter, is checked against the EMC effect within the framework of dynamical rescaling. Our emphasis is on the change in the average bag radius in nuclei, as evaluated in a local density approximation, and its implication for the rescaling parameter. We find that when the bag constant in nuclear matter is significantly reduced from its free-space value, the resulting rescaling parameter is in good agreement with that required to explain the observed depletion of the structure functions in the medium Bjorken $x$ region. Such a large reduction of the bag constant also implies large and canceling Lorentz scalar and vector potentials for the nucleon in nuclear matter which are comparable to those suggested by the relativistic nuclear phenomenology and finite-density QCD sum rules.

## Key claim

When the MIT bag constant is significantly reduced in nuclear matter (to about 35-40% of its free-space value at saturation density), the resulting increase in average bag radius in nuclei yields a rescaling parameter in good agreement with that required to explain the EMC effect in the medium Bjorken x region (0.2 < x < 0.7), with the result being largely independent of the specific model used for the in-medium bag constant.

## Method

The authors used the modified quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which allows the bag constant and bag radius to depend on nuclear density, to compute the average bag radius in finite nuclei via a local density approximation with Woods-Saxon density distributions. They then applied the dynamical rescaling framework, which relates the nuclear structure function to the free nucleon structure function through a rescaling parameter xi_A(Q^2) determined by the ratio of confinement sizes, to calculate the rescaling parameter from the change in average bag radius relative to the free nucleon bag radius.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant in nuclear matter is reduced to B/B_0 ~ 35-40% at nuclear matter saturation density (rho_N^0 = 0.17 fm^-3)
- The bag radius in saturated nuclear matter is 25-30% larger than its free-space value when B/B_0 ~ 35-40%
- For iron-56, the predicted rescaling parameter xi_A ~ 2.00-2.02 at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2, with average bag radius ratio R_A/R_0 ~ 1.15
- For carbon-12, xi_A ~ 1.69-1.70 at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2
- For gold-197, xi_A ~ 2.24-2.27 at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2
- The effective nucleon mass ratio M_N*/M_N ~ 0.72 and vector potential U_v/M_N ~ 0.21 when B/B_0 ~ 35-40%
- Free nucleon bag parameters for R_0 = 0.6, 0.8, 1.0 fm give B_0^(1/4) = 188.1, 157.5, 136.3 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the modified QMC model developed by Jin and Jennings (1996) by testing its predictions against the EMC effect, which reveals distortion of nucleon internal structure in nuclei. The dynamical rescaling approach was previously developed by Close, Jaffe, and collaborators (1983-1988), who estimated confinement size changes by modeling nucleon overlap and found that iron required a ~15% increase in confinement size. The present work shows that the reduction of the bag constant in the modified QMC model produces similar predictions for the rescaling parameter, suggesting that the dropping bag constant effectively parametrizes the physics of nucleon overlapping. The large reduction of the bag constant also yields scalar and vector potentials comparable to those from relativistic nuclear phenomenology (Wallace, 1987) and finite-density QCD sum rules (Cohen, 1995), enabling a unified description of both low-energy nuclear phenomena and the EMC effect.
