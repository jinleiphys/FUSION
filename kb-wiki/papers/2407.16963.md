# Covariant formulation of spinodal decomposition in rapidly expanding quark gluon plasma

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is expected to have a first order phase transition between the confined hadron gas and the deconfined quark gluon plasma at high baryon densities. This will result in phase boundary effects in the metastable and unstable regions. It is important to include these effects in phenomenological models of heavy ion collisions to identify experimental signatures of a phase transition. This requires building intuition on phase separation in rapidly expanding fluids. In this work we present the covariant equations of relativistic hydrodynamics with a phase boundary, provide prescriptions to extend the equation of state to metastable and unstable regions, and show the effects of spinodal separation in a Bjorken flow.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first covariant formulation of spinodal decomposition in a rapidly expanding relativistic fluid, showing that the surface energy terms from a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional can be incorporated into relativistic hydrodynamics, but the numerical demonstration is limited to a simplified Bjorken flow scenario where boost invariance of energy density is approximately preserved and the baryon density perturbation remains smoothly varying.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant stress-energy-momentum tensor that includes gradient terms proportional to a surface energy coefficient K, derived from a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional with a baryon density gradient term. They provide a polynomial interpolation scheme to extend the equation of state into metastable and unstable regions between the hadron gas and quark gluon plasma phases. The equations are solved numerically for a 1+1D Bjorken flow with a sinusoidal baryon density perturbation seeded at proper time 10 fm, using a QCD equation of state with a critical point at T=130 MeV and mu_B=450 MeV, with baryon diffusion included via a simplified conductivity expression.

## Key numbers

- The surface energy coefficient K is taken as 5 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-4} (approximately 1.5 x 10^{7} MeV fm^{5}) for the nonzero case
- The system is initialized at proper time tau_x = 10 fm
- The critical point is placed at T = 130 MeV and mu_B = 450 MeV
- The baryon conductivity dimensionless parameter C_B is set to 0.5
- The violation of boost invariance in energy density is less than 3% for the system considered
- The baryon density evolution is essentially identical for K values less than about 10^{-4} MeV^{-4}

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Cahn-Hilliard theory of spinodal decomposition (Cahn and Hilliard, 1958) to a covariant relativistic framework suitable for heavy ion collisions, building on prior efforts to model first-order phase transitions in nuclear collisions by Randrup (2009, 2010), Steinheimer (2012, 2013), and Pratt (2017). The paper provides the mathematical foundation for incorporating phase boundary effects into realistic heavy ion simulations, enabling future work that can include realistic initial states, hydrodynamic fluctuations, and comparisons with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
