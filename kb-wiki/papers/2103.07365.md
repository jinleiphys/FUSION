# Speed of sound and baryon cumulants in heavy-ion collisions

> We present a method that may allow an estimate of the value of the speed of sound as well as its logarithmic derivative with respect to the baryon number density in matter created in heavy-ion collisions. To this end, we utilize well-known observables: cumulants of the baryon number distribution. In analyses aimed at uncovering the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter, cumulants gather considerable attention as their qualitative behaviour along the explored range of collision energies is expected to aid in detecting the QCD critical point. We show that the cumulants may also reveal the behavior of the speed of sound in the temperature and baryon chemical potential plane. We demonstrate the applicability of such estimates within two models of nuclear matter, and explore what might be understood from known experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that cumulants of the baryon number distribution can be used to estimate the isothermal speed of sound squared and its logarithmic derivative with respect to baryon number density in matter created in heavy-ion collisions, but the method relies on a low-temperature approximation (mu_B/T >> 1) that is only valid for collisions at low beam energies.

## Method
The authors derive analytic relations connecting the isothermal speed of sound squared c_T^2 and the combination c_T^2 + (d ln c_T^2 / d ln n_B)_T to ratios of baryon number cumulants (kappa_1, kappa_2, kappa_3). They validate these approximations by comparing exact model calculations against the cumulant-based formulas in two hadronic models: the vector density functional (VDF) model and the Walecka model. They then apply the formulas to experimental cumulant data from the STAR and HADES collaborations at collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The low-temperature approximation for c_T^2 (Eq. 1) is valid for T less than or approximately 100 MeV and mu_B greater than or approximately 600 MeV, based on model comparisons.
- The approximation for (d ln c_T^2 / d ln n_B)_T (Eq. 2) is qualitatively valid for mu_B greater than or approximately 200 MeV, except in the Walecka model at T = 200 MeV.
- At the lowest collision energy sqrt(s) = 2.4 GeV from HADES, c_T^2 obtained from the approximation is less than half of the ideal gas value.
- The nuclear saturation density used in the VDF model is n_0 = 0.160 fm^{-3}, with the QGP-like critical point placed at T_c = 100 MeV and n_c = 3 n_0.

## Context
This work extends prior attempts to extract the speed of sound from heavy-ion collisions, such as Gardim (2019) who estimated c_s at zero baryon chemical potential using charged particle multiplicity and mean transverse momentum, and Steinheimer (2012) who used the Landau model and UrQMD simulations to locate a minimum in c_s between sqrt(s_NN) = 4-9 GeV. The method enables connecting baryon cumulant measurements, which are central observables in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, to the equation of state of dense nuclear matter relevant for neutron star studies. The authors note that their model comparisons suggest collisions at the lowest STAR and HADES energies may be probing regions affected by the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition rather than the QCD critical point.
