# A polarization relation and the measurement of the longitudinal response in pseudoscalar meson electroproduction off the nucleon

> For pseudoscalar meson electroproduction off the nucleon in parallel kinematics a relation between three polarization observables is derived. It is shown that, without Rosenbluth separation, a measurement of the longitudinal strength can be achieved through three different ways. They are discussed with preliminary MAMI data for the p(\vec e, e'\vec p)π^0 reaction in the energy range of the Delta(1232) resonance.

# Polarization relation and longitudinal response in pseudoscalar meson electroproduction

## Key claim

For pseudoscalar meson electroproduction off the nucleon in parallel kinematics, a model-independent relation between three polarization observables allows extraction of the longitudinal-to-transverse response ratio without Rosenbluth separation, but the method using only the longitudinal polarization component suffers from severe error amplification (a 6.2% statistical error in the reduced polarization propagates to 57.6% error in the ratio), making the two-transverse-component method preferable.

## Method

The authors derived a relation between reduced recoil polarization components (χ_x, χ_y, χ_z) for the p(e,e'p)π⁰ reaction in parallel kinematics, where the recoil proton is detected along the momentum transfer direction. They expressed the structure functions in terms of helicity amplitudes and CGLN amplitudes, then used the equality R_T = R_{TT'}^l (a peculiarity of parallel kinematics) to obtain χ_x² + χ_y² = (1/ε_L) χ_z(1-χ_z). Three extraction methods for R_L/R_T were compared using preliminary MAMI data at the Δ(1232) resonance energy.

## Key numbers

- For the consistency check of the polarization relation: χ_x² + χ_y² = 0.0348 ± 0.0045 (stat) ± 0.0031 (syst); (1/ε_L) χ_z(1-χ_z) = 0.0519 ± 0.022 (stat) ± 0.007 (syst)
- R_L/R_T extracted from χ_z alone (Eq. 3): 0.066 +0.038/-0.034 (stat) ± 0.011 (syst), with a 6.2% relative statistical error in χ_z amplified to 57.6% in R_L/R_T
- R_L/R_T extracted from all three polarization components (Eq. 2): 0.044 ± 0.006 (stat) ± 0.004 (syst)
- R_L/R_T extracted from the two transverse reduced polarizations (Eq. LT_short): 0.040 ± 0.006 (stat) ± 0.004 (syst)

## Context

This work extends the use of double-polarization observables, previously applied successfully to elastic and quasielastic scattering for nucleon electromagnetic structure (Jones 1999, Eden 1994, Ostrick 1999, Herberg 1999, Passchier 1999, Rohe 1999), to the resonant regime of pion electroproduction. It provides an alternative to the standard Rosenbluth separation technique (Blomqvist 1996, Distler 1998) which avoids its experimental difficulties, and the derived relation constrains phenomenological models such as the Mainz Unitary Isobar Model (Drechsel 1999). The method enables measurement of the longitudinal electromagnetic coupling of nucleon resonances, particularly the quadrupole excitation of the Δ(1232) resonance, using the p(e,e'p)π⁰ reaction at MAMI.
