# Collectivity in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions

> We present full (3+1)D dynamical simulations to study collective behavior in ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions (UPC) at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) with the 3DGlauber+MUSIC+UrQMD framework. By extrapolating from asymmetric p+Pb collisions, we simulate a quasi-real photon $γ^*$ interacting with the Pb nucleus in an ultra-peripheral collision at the LHC, assuming strong final-state effects. We study the elliptic flow hierarchy between p+Pb and $γ^*$+Pb collisions, which is dominated by the difference in longitudinal flow decorrelations. Our theoretical framework provides a quantitative tool to study collectivity in small asymmetric collision systems at current and future collider experiments.

## Key claim
The elliptic flow hierarchy observed by ATLAS between ultra-peripheral γ*+Pb and p+Pb collisions is dominated by differences in longitudinal flow decorrelations, not by differences in initial-state transverse geometry. The claim depends on the assumption of strong final-state effects and applies specifically to events with charged hadron multiplicity 40–50 at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors performed full (3+1)D dynamical simulations using the 3DGlauber+MUSIC+UrQMD framework, extrapolating from asymmetric p+Pb collisions to simulate quasi-real photon interactions with Pb nuclei in ultra-peripheral collisions. They computed the probability distribution of center-of-mass energies for γ*+A collisions from the photon energy spectrum, then simulated events at the highest γ*+Pb energy (894 GeV) and compared with p+Pb collisions at 5020 GeV. The elliptic flow event-plane correlations were evaluated between pseudorapidity intervals η ∈ [-2.5, -1] and η ∈ [1, 2.5] with a rapidity gap |Δη| > 2.

## Key numbers
- The center-of-mass collision energy for γ*+Pb collisions at LHC fluctuates over a wide range, with the highest energy being 894 GeV for Pb+Pb at 5020 GeV.
- For Pb+Pb UPC events at 5020 GeV, the rapidity shift between the center-of-mass frame and lab frame fluctuates from -2 to -8.5.
- In p+Pb collisions at 5020 GeV, the elliptic flow event-plane correlation between the two η intervals is very close to unity.
- Reducing the collision energy to 894 GeV weakens the event-plane correlation to approximately 0.9.
- The extra global rapidity shift in γ*+Pb collisions further reduces the correlation strength to approximately 0.7.

## Context
This work extends the (3+1)D dynamical framework previously developed by Zhao (2022) and Shen (2022) for small asymmetric systems, and directly addresses the ATLAS Collaboration (2021) measurement of two-particle azimuthal correlations in ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions. The results demonstrate that full (3+1)D simulations beyond Bjorken boost invariance are necessary for these asymmetric systems, and provide a quantitative tool for studying collectivity in photon-nucleus collisions at current LHC and future collider experiments.
