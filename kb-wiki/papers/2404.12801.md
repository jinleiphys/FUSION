# Hypernuclear constraints on the existence and lifetime of a deeply bound $H$ dibaryon

> We study to what extent the unique observation of $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei by their weak decay into known $Λ$ hypernuclei, with lifetimes of order 10$^{-10}$ s, rules out the existence of a deeply bound doubly-strange (${\cal S}$=$-$2) $H$ dibaryon. Treating ${_{ΛΛ}^{~~6}}{\rm He}$ (the Nagara emulsion event) in a realistic $Λ-Λ-{^4}$He three-body model, we find that the ${_{ΛΛ}^{~~6}}{\rm He}\to H + {^4{\rm He}}$ strong-interaction lifetime increases beyond 10$^{-10}$ s for $m_H < m_Λ+m_n$, about 176 MeV below the $ΛΛ$ threshold, so that such a deeply bound $H$ is not in conflict with hypernuclear data. Constrained by $Λ$ hypernuclear $Δ{\cal S}$=1 nonmesonic weak-interaction decay rates, we follow EFT methods to evaluate the $Δ{\cal S}$=2 $H\to nn$ weak-decay lifetime of $H$ in the mass range $2m_n \lesssim m_H < m_Λ+m_n$. The resulting $H$ lifetime is of order 10$^5$ s, many orders of magnitude shorter than required to qualify for a dark-matter candidate.

## Key claim

A deeply bound $H$ dibaryon with mass below the $\Lambda + n$ threshold (about 176 MeV below the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold) is not ruled out by the observed weak-decay lifetimes of $\Lambda\Lambda$ hypernuclei, because the strong-interaction decay ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He} \to H + {^4}{\rm He}$ becomes slower than the hypernuclear weak-decay timescale of order $10^{-10}$ s for such masses; however, the resulting $H$ weak-decay lifetime is of order $10^5$ s, far too short to qualify as a dark-matter candidate.

## Method

The author treated ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He}$ (the Nagara emulsion event) in a $\Lambda-\Lambda-{^4}{\rm He}$ three-body model with a factorized Gaussian wavefunction constrained by pionless-EFT calculations, including a short-range correlation factor. The strong-interaction decay rate to $H + {^4}{\rm He}$ was computed using a Gaussian $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential fitted to the HAL-QCD scattering length, with the $H$ dibaryon represented by a fully symmetric six-quark spatial wavefunction supplemented by a flavor-color-spin factor of $\sqrt{1/1000}$. The $\Delta{\cal S}=2$ weak decay $H \to nn$ was evaluated using leading-order EFT contact interactions, with the $\Delta{\cal S}=2$ low-energy constant estimated from the $\Delta{\cal S}=1$ $\Lambda n \to nn$ coupling constant constrained by $\Lambda$ hypernuclear nonmesonic weak-decay data.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda\Lambda$ r.m.s. distance in ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He}$ is $3.65 \pm 0.10$ fm, adopted from pionless-EFT six-body calculations.
- For $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 176$ MeV (corresponding to $m_H = m_\Lambda + m_n$), the ${_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}}{\rm He} \to H + {^4}{\rm He}$ strong-interaction lifetime $\tau = \hbar/\Gamma$ lies in the interval $[1.1 \times 10^{-9}~\text{s}, 1.7 \times 10^{-8}~\text{s}]$, exceeding the free-$\Lambda$ lifetime $\tau_\Lambda = 2.6 \times 10^{-10}$ s.
- The $H \to nn$ weak-decay lifetime $\tau_H$ is $(0.78 \pm 0.09) \times 10^5$ s for $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 176$ MeV, $(0.83 \pm 0.10) \times 10^5$ s for $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 200$ MeV, and $(1.35 \pm 0.16) \times 10^5$ s for $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 300$ MeV.
- The $\Delta{\cal S}=1$ contact interaction LEC extracted from $\Lambda$ hypernuclear data is $C_1^{(\lambda=4)}(\Lambda n) = -(379 \pm 23)~\text{eV}\cdot\text{fm}^3$.

## Context

This work extends the debate over the $H$ dibaryon, originally predicted by Jaffe (1977) to lie about 80 MeV below the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold, and later challenged by Dalitz et al. (1989) and Gal (2013) using hypernuclear stability arguments. It directly addresses the conjecture by Farrar (2003) and Farrar and Zaharijas (2004) of a deeply bound compact $H$ dibaryon (sexaquark) as a dark-matter candidate with mass 1850–2050 MeV and lifetime of order $10^8$ yr. The calculated $H$ lifetime of order $10^5$ s agrees roughly with Donoghue, Golowich, and Holstein (1986) but is 10 orders of magnitude shorter than the Farrar-Zaharijas estimate, ruling out the deeply bound $H$ as a dark-matter candidate while showing that hypernuclear data alone do not forbid its existence.
