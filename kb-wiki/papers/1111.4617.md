# Rapidly rotating axisymmetric neutron stars with quark cores

> We present a systematic study of the properties of pure hadronic and hybrid compact stars. The nuclear equation of state (EoS) for beta-equilibrated neutron star matter was obtained using density dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction which satisfies the constraints from the observed flow data from heavy-ion collisions. The energy density of quark matter is lower than that of this nuclear EoS at higher densities implying the possibility of transition to quark matter inside the core. We solve the Einstein's equations for rotating stars using pure nuclear matter and quark core. The beta- equilibrated neutron star matter with a thin crust is able to describe highly massive compact stars but find that the nuclear to quark matter deconfinement transition inside neutron stars causes reduction in their masses. Recent observations of the binary millisecond pulsar J1614-2230 by P. B. Demorest et al. [1] suggest that the masses lie within 1.97\pm0.04 M\odot where M\odot is the solar mass. In conformity with recent observations, pure nucleonic EoS determines that the maximum mass of NS rotating with frequency below r-mode instability is ~1.95 M\odot with radius ~10 kilometers. Although compact stars with quark cores rotating with Kepler's frequency have masses up to ~2 M\odot, but if the maximum frequency is limited by the r-mode instability, the maximum mass ~1.7 M\odot turns out to be lower than the observed mass of 1.97\pm0.04 M\odot, by far the highest yet measured with such certainty, implying exclusion of quark cores for such massive pulsars.

## Key claim

Pure hadronic neutron stars can reach masses up to ~1.95 M☉ when rotation is limited by r-mode instability, consistent with the observed 1.97±0.04 M☉ of PSR J1614-2230; however, hybrid stars with quark cores under the same r-mode instability limit reach only ~1.7 M☉, which is too low to explain this massive pulsar, implying that quark cores are excluded for such massive objects but not for pulsars with masses ~1.7 M☉ or less.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to construct the equation of state (EoS) for beta-equilibrated neutron star matter, satisfying constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data. For quark matter, they employed a perturbative QCD EoS with two massless and one massive quark flavor and a running coupling constant, treating the bag constant B as a free parameter (choosing B^1/4 = 110 MeV). They solved Einstein's field equations for rotating axisymmetric configurations using the rns code, combining crustal EoS (FMT, BPS, BBP) with the nuclear and quark matter EoS, and considered both Keplerian rotation and rotation limited by r-mode instability.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of static pure hadronic neutron star: 1.92 M☉ with radius ~9.7 km
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic neutron star rotating at Kepler frequency: 2.27 M☉ with equatorial radius ~13.1 km
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic neutron star with r-mode instability limit (1.5 ms period): 1.95 M☉ with radius ~9.9 km
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic neutron star with r-mode instability limit (2.0 ms period): 1.94 M☉ with radius ~9.8 km
- Maximum mass of static hybrid star (with quark core): 1.68 M☉ with radius ~10.4 km
- Maximum mass of hybrid star rotating at Kepler frequency: 2.02 M☉ with equatorial radius ~14.3 km
- Maximum mass of hybrid star with r-mode instability limit (1.5 ms period): 1.72 M☉ with radius ~10.7 km
- Maximum mass of hybrid star with r-mode instability limit (2.0 ms period): 1.71 M☉ with radius ~10.6 km
- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97±0.04 M☉ (Demorest et al. 2010)
- Transition density where quark matter energy density becomes lower than nuclear EoS: 0.405 fm^-3 for bag constant B^1/4 = 110 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the DDM3Y interaction for nuclear matter (Chowdhury et al. 2010, PRC 2011) and applies it to rotating stars with quark cores. It directly addresses the observational constraint from Demorest et al. (2010) on PSR J1614-2230, which ruled out many EoS involving exotic matter such as kaon condensates or hyperons (Lattimer & Prakash 2007, Glendenning 2008). The result that hybrid stars with quark cores cannot reach 1.97 M☉ under r-mode instability limits contradicts the possibility of quark deconfinement in such massive pulsars, but leaves open the possibility for less massive stars. The work enables future studies to explore mechanisms that could stiffen the quark EoS, such as more realistic inclusion of quark pairing effects, or to investigate scenarios where deconfinement is prevented.
