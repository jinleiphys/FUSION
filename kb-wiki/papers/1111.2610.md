# Resonance-Parton Duality in $(e,e'π)$ off Nucleons

> Regge-pole based descriptions of pion-electroproduction on nucleons have given a very good description of the longitudinal components of the cross sections. However, these very same models grossly underestimate the transverse components. A related problem appears in QCD-based scaling arguments that predict the predominance of longitudinal over transverse electroproduction of pions by terms $\propto Q^2$. However, data from JLAB, Cornell and DESY, covering a wide kinematical range $1 < Q^2 < 11\, {\rm GeV}^2$ and $2\, {\rm GeV} < W < 4\, {\rm GeV}$, do not show this expected behavior. We address here this issue of the transverse response in pion-electroproduction by considering the contributions of high-lying ($W > 2$ GeV) nucleon resonances to pion production. The coupling strengths and form factors are obtained through resonance-parton duality. We show that in a wide range of electron energies and four-momentum transfers such a model describes all the available data very well.

## Key claim
The large transverse cross section in exclusive pion electroproduction, which is underestimated by Regge-pole models and contradicts QCD scaling predictions of longitudinal dominance, can be explained by including contributions from high-lying nucleon resonances (W > 2 GeV) whose coupling strengths and form factors are determined through resonance-parton (Bloom-Gilman) duality.

## Method
The authors replaced the standard s-channel nucleon Born term in the Regge-based model of Vanderhaeghen et al. (1997) with an integral over high-lying nucleon resonances, using Bloom-Gilman duality to relate the resonance electromagnetic couplings and form factors to the deep inelastic structure function F₂. The combined nucleon-resonance contribution was then absorbed into an effective Born term with a modified, harder form factor Fₛ(Q²,s), which was parameterized in dipole form. The model was compared to separated and unseparated cross section data from JLAB, Cornell, and HERMES experiments.

## Key numbers
- At Q² = 3.91 GeV², the transverse cross section σ_T is about a factor of two larger than the longitudinal cross section σ_L, contrary to QCD-based expectations.
- At Q² = 2.15 GeV², σ_T has the same size as σ_L.
- The model reproduces data across a kinematic range of 1 < Q² < 11 GeV² and 2 GeV < W < 4 GeV.
- At JLAB, the model describes unseparated cross sections for Q² from about 1 GeV² up to 5 GeV², with specific comparisons at average Q² = 1.1 GeV² and Q² = 4.7 GeV².
- At HERMES, the model works in kinematic windows up to 4 < Q² < 11 GeV².
- Resonances contribute about 30% to the longitudinal cross section at forward angles (small -t).

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing puzzle where Regge-pole models (Vanderhaeghen et al., 1997) successfully described longitudinal cross sections but grossly underestimated transverse components in pion electroproduction, while QCD scaling arguments (Brodsky, 1974; Collins, 1996) predicted longitudinal dominance that was not observed in data from JLAB (Horn, 2006; Horn, 2007; Tadevosyan, 2007), Cornell (Bebek, 1977), and DESY/HERMES (2007). The approach extends Bloom-Gilman duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970; 1971) from inclusive structure functions to exclusive pion production, and has been extended to π⁰ production (Kaskulov, 2011) without new parameters. The results enable predictions for color transparency measurements in exclusive pion electroproduction off nuclei and for the upcoming JLAB 12 GeV program.
