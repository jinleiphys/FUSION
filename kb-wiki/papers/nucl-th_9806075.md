# Saturnalia

## Key claim

This concluding talk at the "les 20 ans de Saturne-2" colloquium presents a personal selection of highlights from the Saturne-2 facility's physics programme over twenty years, arguing that the accelerator could have continued productively despite its closure in December 1997.

## Method

The author reviews experimental programmes carried out at the Saturne-2 facility, including elastic proton-nucleus scattering using SPESI, intermediate energy pick-up reactions, threshold meson production (particularly η mesons) studied at SPESIV and SPESII, nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering with polarised beams, charge-exchange experiments using the (³He,t) and (d,2p) reactions, and few-body studies with polarised deuteron beams. The talk also covers calibrations of polarimeters such as AHEAD and POLDER for use at other facilities.

## Key numbers

- The ¹²C(p,d)¹¹C* reaction was measured at 700 MeV to half a dozen excited states of ¹¹C using SPESI
- The dp → ³He η reaction was measured near threshold at SPESIV, showing a surprisingly high cross section
- Triple spin parameters in dp → pd were measured at 1.6 GeV, representing a unique measurement
- The deuteron tensor analysing power T₂₀ was measured as a function of beam energy for dp → pd in the backward direction and for dp → ³He π⁰
- MIMAS provided beams of ions up to ¹²⁹Xe (30⁺), but heavy ion requests represented only about 10% of total requests over many years
- The wheel of Beurtey and Saudinos allowed simultaneous measurements of A_y in proton-proton elastic scattering at 16 different energies

## Context

This talk extends the history of the Saturne-2 facility, which was a clone of the Cosmotron, and references the earlier Cosmotron work of Palevsky and friends on elastic scattering. The threshold η production work at Saturne opened the possibility of studying rare η decays (η → γγ and η → μ⁺μ⁻) and led to searches for quasibound η-nuclear states at Jülich and GSI, as well as production studies of the η³He system at Uppsala. The calibrations of deuteron tensor polarimeters at Saturne enabled measurements of T₂₀ in elastic ed → e'd' at CEBAF reaching q² values twice as large as earlier experiments.
