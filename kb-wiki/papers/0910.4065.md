# Spin Symmetry for Anti-Lambda Spectrum in atomic nucleus

> The spin symmetry of anti-Lambda spectrum in nucleus $^{16}$O has been studied in the relativistic mean field theory. The spin-orbit splittings of spin doublets are found to be around 0.03-0.07 MeV and the dominant components of the Dirac spinor for the anti-Lambda spin doublets are found to be near identical. It indicates that there is an even better spin symmetry in the anti-Lambda spectrum than that in the anti-nucleon spectrum.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit splittings in the anti-Lambda spectrum in \(^{16}\)O are found to be around 0.03–0.07 MeV, which is even smaller than those in the antineutron spectrum (0.06–0.20 MeV), indicating a better spin symmetry for anti-Lambda than for antinucleons. However, the authors note that the imaginary part of the optical potential and polarization effects have been neglected, which may make experimental observation difficult.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the PK1 effective interaction for nucleons and coupling constants for the anti-Lambda derived from SU(3) symmetry in the naive quark model (\(g_{\sigma \bar{\Lambda}} = \frac{2}{3} g_{\sigma N}\), \(g_{\omega \bar{\Lambda}} = -\frac{2}{3} g_{\omega N}\)). The coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and anti-Lambda, together with Klein-Gordon equations for mesons, were solved self-consistently in the mean-field and no-sea approximations for the nucleus \(^{16}\)O. The spin-orbit splitting was computed from the energy differences of spin doublets and compared with the antineutron case from Zhou (2003).

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splittings for anti-Lambda p states in \(^{16}\)O: 0.03–0.06 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splittings for anti-Lambda other orbits in \(^{16}\)O: 0.03–0.07 MeV.
- Energy differences between anti-Lambda spin doublets for p states: 0.09–0.17 MeV.
- For comparison, the spin-orbit splitting in the Lambda spectrum in \(^{16}\)O: 0.75 MeV.
- For comparison, the spin-orbit splitting in the antineutron spectrum in \(^{16}\)O: 0.09–0.18 MeV.
- The anti-Lambda mass used: \(M_{\bar{\Lambda}} = 1115.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin symmetry in antinucleon spectra, first investigated by Zhou (2003) using RMF theory, to the anti-Lambda hyperon. It shows that the spin symmetry is even better for anti-Lambda than for antineutrons due to the shallower potential and larger mass of the anti-Lambda. The results motivate further studies including the imaginary part of the optical potential and self-consistent polarization effects, which the authors state are in progress.
