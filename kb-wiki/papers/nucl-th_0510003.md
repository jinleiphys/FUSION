# Form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics approaches and space-time translation invariance

> Invariance of form factors under Lorentz boosts is a criterion often advocated to determine whether their estimate in a RQM framework is reliable. It is shown that verifying relations stemming from covariance properties under space-time translations could be a more important criterion. Form factors calculated in different approaches for a simple system are discussed with these various respects. An approximate method is shown to remove the main discrepancies related to a violation of the above relations.

# Form factors in relativistic quantum mechanics approaches and space-time translation invariance

## Key claim
The paper argues that satisfying constraints from space-time translation invariance (energy-momentum conservation at the interaction vertex) is a more important criterion for evaluating relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) approaches than invariance under Lorentz boosts, which has often been the primary criterion used to judge reliability.

## Method
The author calculates charge form factors for the ground state of the Wick-Cutkoski model (scalar particles exchanging a massless scalar meson) using the single-particle current approximation in different RQM approaches: instant form (Breit frame and parallel configuration), front form (perpendicular and parallel configurations), and point form (two versions). These RQM results are compared to the exact Bethe-Salpeter solution, which serves as the "experiment." The violation of relations stemming from space-time translation invariance is quantified, and an approximate correction is applied to account for missing many-body currents.

## Key numbers
- In the point-form approach ("P.F."), the violation of space-time translation invariance relations ranges from a factor of 30 up to 35000.
- The charge radius in several approaches scales like the inverse of the mass of the system, leading to the charge radius going to infinity when the system mass goes to zero.
- The asymptotic behavior of the exact form factor is expected to be \( Q^{-4} \).

## Context
This work extends earlier comparisons of RQM approaches for form factor calculations, particularly referencing Amghar (2002) for evidence of large discrepancies in the one-body current approximation. The paper builds on Dirac's (1949) classification of RQM forms (instant, front, point) and addresses the Bakamjian (1961) and Sokolov (1985) version of point-form as well as a more recent Dirac point-form variant by Desplanques (2004). The results confirm the standard front-form (\( q^+ = 0 \)) and instant-form (Breit frame) as more reliable frameworks in the single-particle current approximation, while showing that Lorentz invariance alone (as in point-form) does not guarantee good results. The work enables future inclusion of many-body currents in RQM calculations by providing an approximate correction method based on space-time translation constraints.
