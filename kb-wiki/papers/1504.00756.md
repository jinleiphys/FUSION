# Systematics of capture and fusion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform a systematic study of capture excitation functions by using an empirical coupled-channel model. In this model, a barrier distribution is used to take effectively into account the effects of couplings between the relative motion and intrinsic degrees of freedom. The shape of the barrier distribution is of an asymmetric Gaussian form. The effect of neutron transfer channels is also included in the barrier distribution. Based on the interaction potential between the projectile and the target, empirical formulas are proposed to determine the parameters of the barrier distribution. Theoretical estimates for barrier distributions and calculated capture cross sections together with experimental cross sections of 220 reaction systems with $182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640$ are tabulated. The results show that our empirical formulas work quite well in the energy region around the Coulomb barrier. This model can provide prediction of capture cross sections for the synthesis of superheavy nuclei as well as valuable information on capture and fusion dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors propose an empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model that uses an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution to effectively account for couplings between relative motion and intrinsic degrees of freedom, including neutron transfer channels, and they claim this model works "quite well" in the energy region around the Coulomb barrier for 220 reaction systems, but only for those with \(182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640\) and with the caveat that there is no mathematical derivation proving the barrier distribution form from the coupled Schrodinger equations.

## Method

The authors collected 220 sets of experimental capture excitation functions and fitted them using an ECC model where the penetration probability is calculated via the Hill-Wheeler formula integrated over an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution. The barrier distribution parameters (left width, right width, and central value) were first obtained by fitting to data using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, then empirical formulas were derived based on the static and dynamical quadrupole deformations of the projectile and target, using the potential energy surface at the saddle point. The effect of positive Q-value neutron transfer channels was included by broadening the barrier distribution widths by \(g Q(2n)\) with \(g = 0.32\).

## Key numbers

* 220 reaction systems were studied, covering the range \(182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640\).
* For spherical systems with \(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} < 1150\), the central value parameter \(a = 0.26\); for \(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \ge 1150\), \(a = 0.5\).
* For deformed systems with \(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} < 1150\), \(a = 0.23\); for \(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \ge 1150\), \(a = 0.37\).
* The left width parameter \(b = 0.32\) for spherical systems and \(b = 0.12\) for deformed systems; the right width parameter \(c = 0.93\) for spherical and \(c = 1.12\) for deformed systems.
* For the reaction \(^{48}{\rm Ca}+{}^{208}{\rm Pb}\), the Coulomb barrier for two spherical nuclei \(V_{\rm B}^{\rm Sp} = 180.2\) MeV, the barrier at the saddle point \(V_{\rm B}^{\rm S} = 163.6\) MeV, and the deformation energy at the saddle point is 12.44 MeV.
* 89 of the 220 reaction systems have a positive Q value for one neutron pair transfer channel.
* The neutron transfer broadening constant \(g = 0.32\).
* The nuclear potential depth \(V_0 = 80\) MeV, radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.16\) fm, and diffuseness \(a\) given by \(1.17[1+0.53(A_{\rm P}^{-1/3}+A_{\rm T}^{-1/3})]^{-1}\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the empirical coupled-channel approaches of Zagrebaev (2001, 2004) and Liu (2006) by providing systematic empirical formulas for the barrier distribution parameters based on static and dynamical deformations, applicable to a large set of 220 reactions. It enables predictions of capture cross sections for the synthesis of superheavy nuclei where full coupled-channel calculations become intractable due to the large number of channels required. The model has already been applied to study fusion reactions such as \(^{32}{\rm S} + ^{94,96}{\rm Zr}\) and \(^{40}{\rm Ca} + ^{94,96}{\rm Zr}\) (Wang, 2016) and extended to weakly bound nuclei at above-barrier energies (Wang, 2016).
