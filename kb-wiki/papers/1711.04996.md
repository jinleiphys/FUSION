# Photoproduction of $Λ$ and $Σ^{0}$ hyperons off protons with linearly polarized photons at $E_γ = 1.5-3.0$ GeV

> We report the measurement of the $γp \rightarrow K^{+}Λ$ and $γp \rightarrow K^{+}Σ^{0}$ reactions at SPring-8. The differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries are measured at forward $K^{+}$ production angles using linearly polarized tagged-photon beams in the range of $E_γ=1.5$--3.0 GeV. With increasing photon energy, the cross sections for both $γp \rightarrow K^{+}Λ$ and $γp \rightarrow K^{+}Σ^{0}$ reactions decrease slowly. Distinct narrow structures in the production cross section have not been found at $E_γ=1.5$--3.0 GeV. The forward peaking in the angular distributions of cross sections, a characteristic feature of $t$-channel exchange, is observed for the production of $Λ$ in the whole observed energy range. A lack of similar feature for $Σ^{0}$ production reflects a less dominant role of $t$-channel contribution in this channel. The photon-beam asymmetries remain positive for both reactions, suggesting the dominance of $K^{*}$ exchange in the $t$ channel. These asymmetries increase gradually with the photon energy, and have a maximum value of +0.6 for both reactions. Comparison with theoretical predictions based on the Regge trajectory in the $t$ channel and the contributions of nucleon resonances indicates the major role of $t$-channel contributions as well as non-negligible effects of nucleon resonances in accounting for the reaction mechanism of hyperon photoproduction in this photon energy regime.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries for $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Lambda$ and $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ are measured at forward $K^{+}$ production angles in the energy range $E_{\gamma}=1.5$–3.0 GeV, filling a gap in existing data between 2.5 and 6.0 GeV; no distinct narrow resonance structures are observed in the cross sections, but the data indicate the major role of $t$-channel contributions with non-negligible effects from nucleon resonances, especially in the $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ channel.

## Method

The experiment used linearly polarized tagged photon beams from laser backscattering at the LEPS facility (SPring-8) incident on a 16 cm liquid hydrogen target. Charged particles were detected by the LEPS spectrometer covering forward angles ($\pm 0.4$ rad horizontal, $\pm 0.2$ rad vertical), with particle identification via time-of-flight and momentum measurements. Differential cross sections were extracted from missing mass spectra after side-band subtraction for pion contamination, and photon-beam asymmetries were determined from the $\cos(2\phi_{K^{+}})$ modulation of yields with vertically and horizontally polarized beams.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: $E_{\gamma}=1.5$–3.0 GeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=1.92$–2.53 GeV
- Degree of linear polarization: 88% at $E_{\gamma}=3.0$ GeV, dropping to 28% at $E_{\gamma}=1.5$ GeV
- Number of events: approximately $2.6 \times 10^{4}$ for $K^{+}\Lambda$ and $1.8 \times 10^{4}$ for $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$
- Maximum photon-beam asymmetry: +0.6 for both reactions
- Normalization factor for photon flux ratio between polarization directions: $n=1.014$
- Systematic uncertainties: 3% from photon-beam flux, 1% from target length

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by LEPS (2006), SAPHIR (2004), CLAS (2006, 2010), and GRAAL, providing new data in the $E_{\gamma}=1.5$–3.0 GeV region that bridge the gap between lower-energy resonance studies and high-energy Regge regime data at $E_{\gamma}=5$–16 GeV from SLAC (Boyarski 1969, Quinn 1979). The results constrain Regge trajectory modeling of $t$-channel $K$ and $K^{*}$ exchange and help disentangle contributions from nucleon resonances such as $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, and $D_{13}(1900)$ for $N^{*}$, and $S_{31}(1900)$, $P_{31}(1910)$, $D_{33}(1700)$, and $P_{33}(1920)$ for $\Delta^{*}$, as identified in the Bonn-Gatchina and Regge-plus-resonance models.
