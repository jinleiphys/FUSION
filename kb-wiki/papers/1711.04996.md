# Photoproduction of $Î›$ and $ÎŁ^{0}$ hyperons off protons with linearly polarized photons at $E_Îł = 1.5-3.0$ GeV

> We report the measurement of the $Îłp \rightarrow K^{+}Î›$ and $Îłp \rightarrow K^{+}ÎŁ^{0}$ reactions at SPring-8. The differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries are measured at forward $K^{+}$ production angles using linearly polarized tagged-photon beams in the range of $E_Îł=1.5$--3.0 GeV. With increasing photon energy, the cross sections for both $Îłp \rightarrow K^{+}Î›$ and $Îłp \rightarrow K^{+}ÎŁ^{0}$ reactions decrease slowly. Distinct narrow structures in the production cross section have not been found at $E_Îł=1.5$--3.0 GeV. The forward peaking in the angular distributions of cross sections, a characteristic feature of $t$-channel exchange, is observed for the production of $Î›$ in the whole observed energy range. A lack of similar feature for $ÎŁ^{0}$ production reflects a less dominant role of $t$-channel contribution in this channel. The photon-beam asymmetries remain positive for both reactions, suggesting the dominance of $K^{*}$ exchange in the $t$ channel. These asymmetries increase gradually with the photon energy, and have a maximum value of +0.6 for both reactions. Comparison with theoretical predictions based on the Regge trajectory in the $t$ channel and the contributions of nucleon resonances indicates the major role of $t$-channel contributions as well as non-negligible effects of nucleon resonances in accounting for the reaction mechanism of hyperon photoproduction in this photon energy regime.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries for $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Lambda$ and $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ are measured at forward $K^{+}$ production angles in the energy range $E_{\gamma}=1.5$â€“3.0 GeV, filling a gap in existing data between 2.5 and 6.0 GeV; no distinct narrow resonance structures are observed in the cross sections, but the data indicate the major role of $t$-channel contributions with non-negligible effects from nucleon resonances, especially in the $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ channel.

## Method

The experiment used linearly polarized tagged photon beams from laser backscattering at the LEPS facility (SPring-8) incident on a 16 cm liquid hydrogen target. Charged particles were detected by the LEPS spectrometer covering forward angles ($\pm 0.4$ rad horizontal, $\pm 0.2$ rad vertical), with particle identification via time-of-flight and momentum measurements. Differential cross sections were extracted from missing mass spectra after side-band subtraction for pion contamination, and photon-beam asymmetries were determined from the $\cos(2\phi_{K^{+}})$ modulation of yields with vertically and horizontally polarized beams.
