# Nuclear effects on the longitudinal structure function at small x

> We discuss the longitudinal structure function in nuclear DIS at small $x$. We work within the framework of universal parton densities obtained in DGLAP analyses at NLO. We show that the nuclear effects on the longitudinal structure function closely follow those on the gluon distribution. The error analyses available from newest sets of nuclear PDFs also allow to propagate the uncertainties from present data. In this way, we evaluate the minimal sensitivity required in future experiments for this observable to improve the knowledge of the nuclear glue. We further discuss the uncertainties on the extraction of $F_2$ off nuclear targets, introduced by the usual assumption that the ratio $F_L/F_2$ is independent of the nuclear size. We focus on the kinematical regions relevant for future lepton-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The nuclear effects on the longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) closely follow those on the gluon distribution at small \(x\), but the predictive power of current nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) is reliable only in the region of \(x\) covered by experimental data, with extrapolations outside this region remaining linked to the functional form of the initial condition used in the analysis.

## Method

The authors worked within the framework of universal parton densities obtained in DGLAP analyses at next-to-leading order (NLO), using CTEQ6.1M PDFs for the proton and the corresponding nuclear ratios from the EPS09, nDS, HKN07, and FGS10 sets of nPDFs. They computed the structure functions \(F_2\) and \(F_L\) for both nucleon and nuclear cases using standard NLO expressions in the zero-mass \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, and then evaluated the nuclear ratios \(R_f^A\) and the relative uncertainty \(\Delta F_2^A\) introduced by assuming that \(F_L/F_2\) is independent of nuclear size.

## Key numbers

* For Pb (\(A=208\)), the nuclear ratio \(R_{F_L}^A\) closely follows the gluon ratio \(R_g^A\) at both \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\) (see Fig. 1).
* The uncertainty in extracting \(F_2^A\) due to neglecting nuclear effects on \(F_L/F_2\) can reach up to \(\sim 10\%\) at small \(x\) and moderate \(Q^2\).
* For the EIC kinematics (100 GeV/nucleon on 20 GeV electron) and LHeC kinematics (2750 GeV/nucleon on 50 GeV electron), the relative uncertainty \(\Delta F_2^A\) is sizable, rising up to \(\sim 10\%\) for small to moderate \(Q^2\) and small \(x\).
* The FGS10 parametrization produces a correction with opposite sign to all other central values (EPS09, nDS, HKN07), reflecting a different relative amount of gluon and quark shadowing.

## Context

This work extends the DGLAP-based global analyses of nuclear PDFs pioneered by Frankfurt (1990), Eskola (1992, 1998), and later developed by de Florian (2003), Hirai (2007), Eskola (2009), and Schienbein (2009), which have only marginal constraints on the gluon distribution. The study shows that measuring \(F_L\) on nuclear targets could quantify nuclear effects on the gluon distribution at small \(x\), which are essentially unconstrained in present-day analyses. The results stress the need for future lepton-ion colliders (EIC, LHeC) to provide experimental data for nuclear DIS in terms of reduced cross sections or to perform a collision energy scan to experimentally extract the nuclear longitudinal structure function.
