# Study of $N(1440)$ structure via $γ^*p\to N(1440)$ transition

> We study the photoproduction of the $N(1440)$ resonance in $γ^*p\to N^*$ process in quark models, where the $N(1440)$ takes different wave functions: first radial excitation of the nucleon imported from low-lying baryon mass spectrum calculations, a general radial excitation of the nucleon, and a $q^3$ state with positive parity. The comparison between the theoretical results and experimental data on the helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$ and the analysis of the spatial wave function of the $N(1440)$ resonance reveal that the $N(1440)$ resonance is mainly the $q^3$ first radial excitation.

# Study of $N(1440)$ structure via $\gamma^*p\to N(1440)$ transition

## Key claim

The $N(1440)$ resonance is mainly the $q^3$ first radial excitation of the nucleon, but may contain small additional components beyond radial excitations of three quarks, such as $D$-wave three quark states, hadronic molecule states, or pentaquarks. The claim is hedged by the finding that a perfect fit to the helicity amplitude data requires allowing the $N(1440)$ spatial wave function to be non-orthogonal to the proton ground state, indicating a 3% non-orthogonality component.

## Method

The authors calculate the proton electric form factor $G_E$ and the transverse ($A_{1/2}$) and longitudinal ($S_{1/2}$) helicity amplitudes for the $\gamma^*p \to N(1440)$ transition in a constituent quark model with three-quark wave functions. They test three different spatial wave function assignments for the $N(1440)$: (1) the first radial excitation from low-lying baryon mass spectrum calculations, (2) a general radial excitation orthogonal to the proton ground state (Fitting I), and (3) a positive-parity $q^3$ state not constrained to be orthogonal to the proton (Fitting II). The proton spatial wave function is first extracted by fitting the calculated $G_E$ to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The $N(1440)$ spatial wave function from Fitting II yields a non-orthogonality with the proton wave function of $|\braket{\psi^O_{p}|\psi^O_{N(1440)}}|^2 = 0.03$, indicating a 3% component beyond radial excitations.
- The theoretical results for $A_{1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$ from Fitting II provide a "perfect fit" to $A_{1/2}$ data and a "very reasonable fit" to $S_{1/2}$ data, particularly at the real photon point ($Q^2 = 0$).
- The proton electric form factor calculated with the wave function from baryon mass spectrum calculations is consistent with experimental data at low $Q^2$, with the three-quark core dominantly contributing even at very low $Q^2$.
- The quark mass used for $u$ and $d$ quarks is $m = 5$ MeV.
- The helicity amplitude data for $N(1440)$ extends up to $Q^2 = 4.5$ GeV$^2$, with the theoretical results describing data well in the region $1.5 \leq Q^2 \leq 4.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Roper resonance structure, particularly the authors' own prior work (Kaewsnod et al., 2019; 2020) which addressed the mass ordering problem of $N(1440)$, $N(1520)$, and $N(1535)$ by including pentaquark components. It also builds on light-front relativistic quark model calculations (PhysRevC.76.025212; PhysRevC.78.045209; Capstick, 2007) and covariant spectator quark model calculations (PhysRevD.81.074020), which reproduced the right sign of transverse amplitudes at high $Q^2$ but failed at low $Q^2$. The results enable future studies incorporating additional components such as $D$-wave three quark states, hadronic molecules, and pentaquarks into the $N(1440)$ wave function, which the authors state are under way.
