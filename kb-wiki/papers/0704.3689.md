# Thermodynamics of system with density- and/or temperature-dependent mass particles

> The thermodynamics with medium effects expressed by the dispersion relation of the temperature and density dependent particle mass is studied. Many previous treatments have been reviewed. A new thermodynamical treatment based on the equilibrium state is suggested. Employing the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent model, the discrepancies between our treatment and others are addressed.

# Thermodynamics of system with density- and/or temperature-dependent mass particles

## Key claim
The paper argues that previous thermodynamic treatments of systems with density- and/or temperature-dependent particle masses are inconsistent because they apply partial derivative relations (e.g., dΩ = -S dT - p dV - N dμ) that do not account for the medium effect encoded in the mass function m*(T,n). The authors propose that thermodynamic quantities should be computed directly at an equilibrium state using the thermodynamic potential Ω, internal energy U, and Gibbs function G, rather than through reversible-process derivatives.

## Method
The authors review and categorize prior approaches into two types: (I) those that compute extra derivative terms from the composite function m*(T,n) along a reversible process, and (II) those that add extra terms to the Hamiltonian or thermodynamic potential to preserve standard derivative relations. They then propose a new treatment that calculates all thermodynamic quantities (U, n, G, F, S, p) from the equilibrium-state definitions using the thermodynamic potential Ω, internal energy U, and Gibbs function G, without performing partial derivatives along a reversible process. They apply this treatment to the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model for strange quark matter, using the dispersion relation ε(k,T,n) = [k² + m*(T,n)²]^(1/2) with m_q = B(T)/(3n_B) for u,d quarks and m_s = m_s0 + B(T)/(3n_B), where B(T) = B₀[1 - (T/T_c)²].

## Key numbers
- At T = 50 MeV, the saturation baryon density from the new treatment is n_B0 = 0.45 fm⁻³, with energy per baryon ε/n_B = 1007 MeV.
- For comparison, the treatment in Refs. [21,22] gives n_B0 = 0.46 fm⁻³ and ε/n_B = 1023 MeV; treatment IA gives n_B0 = 0.55 fm⁻³ and ε/n_B = 1083 MeV; treatment IB gives n_B0 = 0.36 fm⁻³ and ε/n_B = 1120 MeV; treatment IC gives n_B0 = 0.34 fm⁻³ and ε/n_B = 1084 MeV.
- The parameters used are B₀ = 170 MeV fm⁻³, m_s0 = 150 MeV, and T_c = 170 MeV.
- The TAGX collaboration experiment found that at nucleon medium density 0.7 n₀ (where n₀ is saturation density), the effective mass of the neutral ρ-meson reduces to 610 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model of Fowler (1981) and the temperature-dependent modification by Zhang (2001, 2002) to address inconsistencies in thermodynamic treatments. The authors show that prior approaches, such as those of Chakrabarty (1989, 1991, 1993), Benrenuto (1995), Peng (1999, 2000), and Wen (2005), produce negative pressure at low density or require unphysical constraints like lim_{T→0} ∂m*/∂T = 0. The new treatment removes negative pressure and satisfies the third law of thermodynamics without such constraints, enabling self-consistent studies of strange quark matter thermodynamics.
