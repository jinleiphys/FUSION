# Sound velocity in dense stellar matter with strangeness and compact stars

> The phase state of dense matter in the intermediate density range ($\sim$1-10 times the nuclear saturation density) is both intriguing and unclear and could have important observable effects in the present gravitational wave era of neutron stars. As the matter density increases in compact stars, the sound velocity is expected to approach the conformal limit ($c_s/c=1/\sqrt{3}$) at high densities and should also fulfill the causality limit ($c_s/c<1$). However, its detailed behavior remains a hot topic of debate. It was suggested that the sound velocity of dense matter could be an important indicator for a deconfinement phase transition, where a particular shape might be expected for its density dependence. In this work, we explore the general properties of the sound velocity and the adiabatic index of dense matter in hybrid stars, as well as in neutron stars and quark stars. Various conditions are employed for hadron-quark phase transition with varying interface tension. We find that the expected behavior of the sound velocity can also be achieved by the nonperturbative properties of the quark phase, in addition to a deconfinement phase transition. And it leads to a more compact star with a similar mass. We then propose a new class of quark star equation of states, which could be tested by future high-precision radius measurements of pulsar-like objects.

# Sound velocity in dense stellar matter with strangeness and compact stars

## Key claim

The characteristic shape of sound velocity as a function of density, a peak followed by a decrease toward the conformal limit, can be produced not only by a first-order deconfinement phase transition from hadronic to quark matter, but also by the nonperturbative properties of pure quark matter alone, specifically through a dynamically-scaled bag parameter in the perturbation model. This means that the sound velocity behavior alone may not uniquely distinguish between hybrid stars with a phase transition and pure quark stars.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for nuclear matter using five models (TW99, DDME2, QMF18, BCPM, APR) and for strange quark matter using four effective models (MIT bag model with perturbative corrections, perturbation model with dynamically-scaled bag parameter, equivparticle model, and quasiparticle model). They computed the adiabatic index and sound velocity for pure nuclear matter, pure quark matter, and hybrid matter under Maxwell construction, Gibbs construction, and intermediate interface tensions (1–50 MeV/fm²) using a Wigner-Seitz approximation for the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 2.8 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ (n₀ ≈ 0.15–0.16 fm⁻³)
- Sound velocity in pure APR nuclear matter violates causality (cₛ/c > 1) at approximately 6.45 ρ₀
- Central density of maximum-mass APR neutron star (~2.2 M☉) reaches ~9.75 ρ₀, exceeding the causality violation density
- In the perturbation model with in-medium bag (Δμ = 800 MeV), sound velocity peaks at ~5 ρ₀ with a value up to ~0.9c
- Maximum masses of pure neutron stars for the five nuclear models range from 1.98 M☉ (BCPM) to 2.50 M☉ (DDME2)
- Radii of 1.4 M☉ neutron stars for the five nuclear models range from 11.4 km (APR) to 13.1 km (DDME2)
- Surface density of the equivparticle model (C=0.7, √D=129 MeV) is the lowest among quark models, around 0.1 fm⁻³
- Adiabatic index Γ for nuclear matter lies mostly between ~2 and 3 in the intermediate density range
- In the Gibbs construction hybrid case, Γ sharply decreases by almost a factor of two at the quark threshold density, then reaches a maximum of ~3.2 before decreasing

## Context

This work extends studies of sound velocity as a potential indicator for deconfinement phase transitions, following suggestions by Bedaque and Steiner (2015) and Tews et al. (2018) that a peak in cₛ might signal a phase transition. The authors show that pure quark matter with nonperturbative effects can mimic the same cₛ shape, complicating the interpretation. The results connect to gravitational-wave constraints from GW170817 (tidal deformability 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀) and NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 (mass 1.44⁺⁰.¹⁵₋₀.₁₄ M☉, radius 13.02⁺¹.²⁴₋₁.₀₆ km). The proposed new class of quark star equations of state could be tested by future high-precision radius measurements of pulsar-like objects.
