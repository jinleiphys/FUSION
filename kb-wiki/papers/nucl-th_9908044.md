# Dyson-Schwinger studies of meson masses and decay constants

> The masses and decay constants of the light mesons are studied within a ladder-rainbow truncation of the set of Dyson-Schwinger equations using a model 2-point gluon function. The one phenomenological parameter and two current quark masses are fitted to reproduce $f_π$, $m_π$ and $m_K$. Our results for $f_K$, and for the vector mesons $ρ$, $ϕ$, and $K^\star$ are in good agreement with the experimental values.

# Dyson-Schwinger studies of meson masses and decay constants

## Key claim
Within a ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations using a model 2-point gluon function, the masses and decay constants of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons can be reproduced in good agreement with experiment, provided the one phenomenological parameter and two current quark masses are fitted to \(f_\pi\), \(m_\pi\), and \(m_K\). The approach is expected to be reliable for pseudoscalar and vector mesons but not for scalar mesons under this truncation.

## Method
The dressed-quark propagator is solved from its Dyson-Schwinger equation using a rainbow truncation for the quark-gluon vertex, together with a ladder truncation for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel that preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The effective coupling includes an infrared-enhanced term and a term that reproduces the one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD. The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved numerically as an eigenvalue problem for the meson masses, and the decay constants are computed from the resulting Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes via exact loop integrals.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: calculated 0.138 GeV, experimental 0.1385 GeV; decay constant: calculated 0.131 GeV, experimental 0.1307 GeV.
- Kaon mass: calculated 0.497 GeV, experimental 0.496 GeV; decay constant: calculated 0.155 GeV, experimental 0.160 GeV.
- Rho meson mass: calculated 0.742 GeV, experimental 0.770 GeV; decay constant: calculated 0.207 GeV, experimental 0.216 GeV.
- K-star meson mass: calculated 0.936 GeV, experimental 0.892 GeV; decay constant: calculated 0.241 GeV, experimental 0.225 GeV.
- Phi meson mass: calculated 1.072 GeV, experimental 1.020 GeV; decay constant: calculated 0.259 GeV, experimental 0.237 GeV.
- Fitted parameters: \(D = 0.93\;\mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(m_{u/d}(\mu) = 5.5\;\mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s(\mu) = 130\;\mathrm{MeV}\) scaled to \(\mu = 1\;\mathrm{GeV}\); \(\omega = 0.4\;\mathrm{GeV}\), \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^{N_f=4} = 0.234\;\mathrm{GeV}\), renormalization point \(\mu = 19\;\mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to meson phenomenology by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997-1999) and is consistent with earlier studies of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. It demonstrates that a simple ladder-rainbow truncation with a phenomenologically motivated gluon model can simultaneously describe pseudoscalar and vector meson properties, while noting that scalar mesons require a more sophisticated treatment as argued by Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1996). The results enable further studies of heavy mesons and provide a framework for systematic improvements beyond the ladder truncation.
