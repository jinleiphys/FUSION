# Measurement of the EMC effect in light and heavy nuclei

> Inclusive electron scattering from nuclear targets has been measured to extract the nuclear dependence of the inelastic cross section in Hall C at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator facility. Results are presented for 2H, 3He, 4He, 9B, 12C, 63Cu and 197Au at an incident electron beam energy of 5.77 GeV for a range of momentum transfer from Q^2 = 2 to 7 (GeV/c)^2. These data improve the precision of the existing measurements of the EMC effect in the nuclear targets at large x, and allow for more detailed examinations of the A dependence of the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The paper presents new, high-precision inclusive electron scattering cross-section ratios for light and heavy nuclei (²H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, ¹⁹⁷Au) relative to deuterium, covering a momentum transfer range Q² = 2 to 7 (GeV/c)² at a beam energy of 5.77 GeV, with the hedge that the extracted EMC ratios assume the longitudinal-to-transverse virtual-photon cross-section ratio R is target-independent, so that the cross-section ratios directly correspond to the F₂ structure function ratios.

## Method

Inclusive electron scattering was performed in Hall C at Jefferson Lab using a 5.776 GeV (and some data at 5.01 GeV) unpolarized electron beam incident on cryogenic (²H, ³He, ⁴He) and solid (⁹Be, ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, ¹⁹⁷Au) targets. The High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) detected scattered electrons at angles of 40° and 50°, and per-nucleon cross-section ratios with respect to deuterium were extracted for a range of Bjorken x from approximately 0.09 to 0.9. The analysis applied updated isoscalar corrections and a slightly modified radiative correction scheme compared to the earlier report by Seely (2009), with the impact of these modifications being at most about 1% for the isoscalar correction and 0.6% for the radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- Cross-section ratios were extracted for ²H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, and ¹⁹⁷Au at an incident electron beam energy of 5.776 GeV.
- The momentum transfer range covered was Q² = 2 to 7 (GeV/c)².
- Data were taken at scattering angles of 40° and 50°.
- Additional data for ¹²C and ²H were taken at 8 kinematic settings, half at E = 5.776 GeV and half at 5.01 GeV.
- The updated isoscalar correction impacted the light-target results by at most about 1%.
- The modified radiative correction scheme impacted the light-target results by at most about 0.6%.

## Context

This work extends the measurements of the EMC effect to light nuclei (³He, ⁴He, ⁹Be) with improved precision, where previous data from SLAC experiment E139 had large uncertainties, and provides the first measurements on ³He in the valence region. The data enable more detailed tests of the A-dependence of the EMC effect and allow direct comparison to few-body calculations with minimal nuclear structure uncertainties, which can help constrain nuclear effects in the deuteron critical for extracting the neutron structure function (as in Whitlow 1992 and Arrington 2008). The results also support studies of short-range correlations (Frankfurt 1993, Egiyan 2003, 2006, Fomin 2012) by testing whether two-body effects contribute differently in few-body nuclei compared to heavy nuclei where effects may be saturated.
