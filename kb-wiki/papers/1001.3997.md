# Effects of bulk viscosity on hadron spectra and Hanbury-Brown Twiss radius by the causal viscous hydrodynamics

> The particle spectra and Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) radius of Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy are investigated by a hydrodynamical expanding source with both shear and bulk viscosities ($ζ$). With a large width of the ratio of $ζ$ to entropy density $s$, both the particle transverse momentum spectra and the ratio $R_{out}/R_{side}$ of HBT radii in the direction of the total transverse momentum of detected two particles ($R_{out}$) and perpendicular to both this direction and the beam direction ($R_{side}$) become a little steeper.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether including bulk viscosity in causal viscous hydrodynamics can bring the calculated Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) radii closer to experimental data for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but finds that while bulk viscosity steepens both the particle spectra and the ratio \(R_{out}/R_{side}\), it does not change the ratio substantially and the HBT results remain far from the experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved the Israel-Stewart second-order causal viscous hydrodynamic equations for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using a one-dimensional algorithm with radial symmetry and longitudinal boost invariance. They used a Glauber-type initial condition, an equation of state from Laine and Schroder with a phase transition temperature \(T_c \approx 190 \pm 20\) MeV, and the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription. Two different relaxation times for bulk pressure were tested (\(\tau_{\Pi} = \frac{\zeta}{s}\frac{8}{T}\) and \(\tau_{\Pi} = \frac{\zeta}{s}\frac{30}{T}\)), and three cases were compared: shear viscosity only (\(\eta/s = 0.08\)), a narrow peak of \(\zeta/s\) with height 0.3, and a broad peak of \(\zeta/s\) with height 0.1.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature \(T_0 = 0.34\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 0.17\) MeV were chosen to fit the pion spectrum.
- Shear viscosity was fixed at the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi \approx 0.08\) throughout the evolution.
- For the narrow bulk viscosity case: \((\zeta/s)_{max} = 0.3\) changing linearly to \((\zeta/s)_{min} = 0.005\) at \(0.98T_c\) and \(1.02T_c\).
- For the broad bulk viscosity case: \((\zeta/s)_{max} = 0.1\) changing linearly to \((\zeta/s)_{min} = 0.005\) at \(0.9T_c\) and \(1.2T_c\).
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV was assumed to be \(40\) mb for the Glauber model initialization.

## Context

This work extends earlier ideal hydrodynamic studies that successfully described elliptic flow and transverse momentum spectra but failed to reproduce HBT radii, as noted in prior work by Heinz and Kolb (2002) and others. The authors specifically test a suggestion by Pratt (2009) that bulk viscosity might bring HBT results closer to experimental data, but find that in their approach the effect is insufficient to resolve the HBT puzzle. The paper points to the need for different equations of state and alternative freeze-out mechanisms such as granular source models as possible next steps.
