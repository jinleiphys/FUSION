# Nucleon charge exchange on the deuteron: A critical review

> The existing experimental data on the d(n,p)nn and d(p,n)pp cross sections in the forward direction are reviewed in terms of the Dean sum rule. It is shown that the measurement of the ratio of the charge exchange on the deuteron to that on the proton might, if taken together with other experimental data, allow a direct construction of the np -> np scattering amplitude in the backward direction with few ambiguities.

## Key claim

The ratio \(R_{np}(0)\) of forward charge-exchange cross sections on the deuteron to those on the nucleon is a robust observable that, when combined with measurements of the spin-dependent total cross sections and deuteron tensor analysing power, may allow a direct construction of the \(np\to np\) scattering amplitude in the backward direction with few ambiguities, though two discrete sign ambiguities remain.

## Method

The authors review existing experimental data on the \(d(n,p)nn\) and \(d(p,n)pp\) forward cross sections and apply the Dean sum rule, which relates the ratio \(R_{np}(0)\) to the spin-transfer parameters \(K_{0nn0}\) and \(K_{0ll0}\) of backward \(np\) elastic scattering. They compare the measured \(R_{np}(0)\) values from three reaction types (\(nd\to p\{nn\}\), \(pd\to n\{pp\}\), and \(dp\to \{pp\}n\)) with predictions from the SAID phase-shift analysis up to 1.3 GeV, and discuss how additional observables (unpolarised cross section, tensor analysing power \(t_{20}\), and spin correlation \(C_{n,n}\)) can fix the three complex charge-exchange amplitudes \(\alpha^{ce}(0)\), \(\beta^{ce}(0)\), and \(\epsilon^{ce}(0)\).

## Key numbers

* \(R_{np}(0) = 0.553 \pm 0.030\) at 200 MeV from the \(nd\to p\{nn\}\) reaction (Dzhgarkava et al., 1962).
* \(R_{np}(0) = 0.564 \pm 0.045\) at 2000 MeV from the \(nd\to p\{nn\}\) reaction (Sharov et al., 2007).
* \(R_{np}(0) = 0.660 \pm 0.080\) at 800 MeV from the \(pd\to n\{pp\}\) reaction (Bjonbakken et al., 1976).
* The Dean sum rule converges to within 1% of its limiting value for excitation energies \(E_{pp} \lesssim 15\) MeV.
* The shadow effect typically reduces \(R_{np}(0)\) by about 5% at high energies.
* The spin-independent term \(|\alpha^{ce}(0)|^2\) contributes less than 10% to the forward charge-exchange cross section between 200 MeV and 1.3 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the work of Dean (1972) and Bugg and Wilkin (1978) on sum rules for charge exchange on the deuteron, and compiles data from experiments such as those by Sharov et al. (2007) up to 2 GeV. The analysis shows that existing SAID phase-shift predictions (available up to 1.3 GeV) agree reasonably with measured \(R_{np}(0)\) values above 300 MeV. The authors propose that combining \(R_{np}(0)\) with measurements of the tensor analysing power \(t_{20}\) (from experiments by Ellerkmann et al., 1993; Kox et al., 1993; Chiba et al., 2006) and the spin correlation \(C_{n,n}\) could enable a direct amplitude reconstruction for backward \(np\) scattering, with new measurements possible at the ANKE facility at COSY-Jülich up to 2.9 GeV.
