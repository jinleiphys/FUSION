# Binomial level densities

> It is shown that nuclear level densities in a finite space are described by a continuous binomial function, determined by the first three moments of the Hamiltonian, and the dimensionality of the underlying vector space. Experimental values for $^{55}$Mn, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{60}$Ni are very well reproduced by the binomial form, which turns out to be almost perfectly approximated by Bethe's formula with backshift. A proof is given that binomial densities reproduce the low moments of Hamiltonians of any rank: A strong form of the famous central limit result of Mon and French. Conditions under which the proof may be extended to the full spectrum are examined.

# Binomial level densities

## Key claim

Nuclear level densities in a finite shell-model space can be described by a continuous binomial function determined solely by the first three moments of the Hamiltonian and the dimensionality of the vector space, but the binomial form may fail if the skewness and kurtosis satisfy γ₂/γ₁² > 1, and its validity for the full spectrum depends on the Hamiltonian lacking sufficiently strong collective components.

## Method

The author derives a continuous binomial density ρ_b(x,N,p,S) parameterized by an effective particle number N, asymmetry p, and energy scale S, with parameters extracted from the Hamiltonian's low moments (centroid, variance, skewness) and the space dimensionality d. For the three test nuclei, the neutral choice p=0.5 and N = ln d / ln 2 is made, the span S is estimated from single-particle energies plus correlation corrections from exact pf-shell calculations, and the ground-state shift Δ is allowed to vary within ±ε/2. The binomial moments are shown analytically to match the low moments of any Hamiltonian of arbitrary rank through a generalization of the Mon and French (1975) central limit theorem, with the proof holding for K < √N.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁵Mn: N = 42, S = 150 MeV, Δ = 1.15 MeV; Bethe fit yields (4a, Δ) = (21.5, -1.5)
- For ⁵⁶Fe: N = 44, S = 165 MeV, Δ = -0.40 MeV; Bethe fit yields (4a, Δ) = (21.3, -3.4)
- For ⁶⁰Ni: N = 49, S = 185 MeV, Δ = 0.20 MeV; in an enlarged space (adding d₃/₂ and s₁/₂ shells) parameters become N = 65, S = 285 MeV, Δ = 0.8 MeV
- Single-particle energy estimates for the span S: (138, 148, 170) MeV for (⁵⁵Mn, ⁵⁶Fe, ⁶⁰Ni); correlation corrections add 10–20 MeV
- Binomial densities can be trusted in an interval of approximately 0.15S above the ground state (about 25 MeV for ⁶⁰Ni)
- The binomial and Bethe forms are nearly identical up to approximately 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the central limit theorem result of Mon and French (1975) for Hamiltonian moments to a continuous binomial form that reproduces level densities in finite spaces, and shows that the binomial is almost perfectly approximated by Bethe's formula with backshift (Bethe 1936). The approach provides a microscopic parametrization alternative to Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations (Nakada and Alhassid 1997; Alhassid et al. 1999) and to earlier phenomenological treatments (Thiessen 1997; Mughabghab 1998). The formalism enables reanalysis of the extensive empirical Bethe-formula fits in terms of the binomial parameters, and the author proposes that Hamiltonian matrices at fixed quantum numbers may always have binomial level densities (Zuker 2000).
