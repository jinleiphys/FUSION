# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Neutron Matter

> Uniform neutron matter is approximated by a cubic box containing a finite number of neutrons, with periodic boundary conditions. We report variational and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of the ground state of fourteen neutrons in a periodic box using the Argonne $\vep $ two-nucleon interaction at densities up to one and half times the nuclear matter density. The effects of the finite box size are estimated using variational wave functions together with cluster expansion and chain summation techniques. They are small at subnuclear densities. We discuss the expansion of the energy of low-density neutron gas in powers of its Fermi momentum. This expansion is strongly modified by the large nn scattering length, and does not begin with the Fermi-gas kinetic energy as assumed in both Skyrme and relativistic mean field theories. The leading term of neutron gas energy is ~ half the Fermi-gas kinetic energy. The quantum Monte Carlo results are also used to calibrate the accuracy of variational calculations employing Fermi hypernetted and single operator chain summation methods to study nucleon matter over a larger density range, with more realistic Hamiltonians including three-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reports variational and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of the ground state of fourteen neutrons in a periodic box, finding that the energy of low-density neutron gas does not begin with the Fermi-gas kinetic energy as assumed in Skyrme and relativistic mean field theories; instead, the leading term is approximately half the Fermi-gas kinetic energy due to the large neutron-neutron scattering length.

## Method

The authors approximated uniform neutron matter by placing 14 neutrons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using the Argonne v8' two-nucleon interaction. They performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC), Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) with constrained-path and unconstrained propagation, and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations at densities of 0.04, 0.08, 0.16, and 0.24 fm^-3. Finite box-size effects were estimated using variational wave functions combined with cluster expansion and chain summation techniques, and the results were extrapolated to the uniform gas limit.

## Key numbers

- GFMC unconstrained total energy per neutron at ρ = 0.04 fm^-3: approximately -2.5 MeV (from Figure 1, text states GFMC-UC energies are 5-10% lower than VMC)
- GFMC unconstrained total energy per neutron at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3 (nuclear matter density): approximately -8 MeV (from Figure 1)
- GFMC unconstrained total energy per neutron at ρ = 0.24 fm^-3 (1.5 ρ_0): approximately -4 MeV (from Figure 1)
- The kinetic energy of 14 noninteracting neutrons in the periodic box is 1.014 times the Fermi-gas kinetic energy (T_PB = 1.014 T_FG)
- The two-neutron scattering length in the ^1S_0 state is a ≈ -18 fm
- At ρ = 0.04 fm^-3, the total contribution of clusters with n ≥ 4 is approximately 30% of the total energy
- At ρ = 0.16 fm^-3, the total contribution of clusters with n ≥ 4 is approximately 10% of the total energy
- The VCS total energy at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3 is higher than VMC by 8% at ρ_0/4 and lower by 2% at 1.5 ρ_0
- GFMC-UC energies are below VCS by 12% to 4% over the density range studied

## Context

This work extends previous Brueckner theory calculations (Engvik 1997, Baldo et al. 1997) and variational chain summation methods (Wiringa et al. 1988, Akmal et al. 1998) for neutron matter by providing quantum Monte Carlo results that can calibrate the accuracy of these approximate methods. The calculations enable improved constraints on energy-density functionals used to study neutron-rich nuclei and neutron stars, and the authors note that the main remaining error at low densities is likely due to neglect of superfluidity, which can lower energies by approximately 10% as suggested by pairing studies (Engelbrecht et al. 1997).
