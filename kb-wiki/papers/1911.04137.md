# Radiative corrections in proton--antiproton annihilation to electron-positron and their application to the PANDA experiment

> Radiative corrections to the annihilation of proton--antiproton into electron--positron are revisited, including virtual and real (soft and hard) photon emission. This issue is relevant for the time-like form factors measurements planned at the PANDA experiment at the FAIR facility, in next future. The relevant formulas are given. A stand-alone Monte-Carlo integrator is developed on the basis of the calculated radiative cross section and its application to the PANDA experiment is illustrated.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to the annihilation process \(\bar{p}p \to e^+e^-\) are recalculated including virtual and real (soft and hard) photon emission, and the authors show that the interference between initial-state and final-state radiation (which is neglected in the standard PHOTOS package used by PANDA) can destroy the symmetry of the angular distribution and must be accounted for when extracting proton electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the one-loop virtual corrections (vacuum polarization, proton and electron vertex corrections, and box-type two-photon exchange) and for soft and hard real photon emission from initial and final states, using a point-like proton approximation for the virtual corrections. They implement these formulas in a stand-alone Monte-Carlo integrator that computes the radiatively corrected differential cross section, and compare the results with previous calculations for the PANDA experiment kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton beam momentum range considered for PANDA is \(P = 1.5 \div 15\) GeV.
- The soft photon energy cutoff is set to \(\Delta E/E = 0.01\) for all numerical applications.
- The proton form factor ratio can be measured at PANDA with total relative uncertainty between \(3.3\%\) at \(s = 5.08\) GeV\(^2\) and \(57\%\) at \(s = 13.9\) GeV\(^2\), assuming an integrated luminosity of \(2\) fb\(^{-1}\) per beam momentum setting.
- The relative uncertainty on \(|G_E|\) is between \(2.2\%\) and \(48\%\), and on \(|G_M|\) between \(3.5\%\) and \(9.7\%\), over the same energy range.
- The total cross section measurement can be extended to energies up to \(\sim 40\) GeV\(^2\) depending on experimental efficiency.

## Context

This work extends earlier radiative correction calculations for \(\bar{p}p \to e^+e^-\) by Ahmadov (2010) and VandeWiele (2012), providing a more complete treatment that includes the interference between initial-state and final-state radiation. The results are directly applicable to the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where previous feasibility studies by Sudol (2009) and Singh (2016) used the PHOTOS package that neglects these interference effects. The paper enables more precise extraction of time-like proton electromagnetic form factors from the angular distribution of the final lepton, which is essential for resolving the proton form factor puzzle.
