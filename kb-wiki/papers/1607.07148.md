# Effects of entrance channel on fusion probability in hot fusion reactions

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system (DNS) model, the fusion reactions leading to the compound nuclei 274Hs and 286Cn are investigated. The fusion probability as a function of DNS excitation energy is studied. The calculated results are in good agreement with the available experimental data. The obtained results show that the fusion probabilities are obviously enhanced for the reactions located at high place in potential energy surface, although these reactions may have small values of mass asymmetry. It is found that the enhancement is due to the large potential energy of the initial DNS.

# Effects of entrance channel on fusion probability in hot fusion reactions

## Key claim
The fusion probability is enhanced for reactions whose initial dinuclear system (DNS) sits at a high place on the potential energy surface, even when those reactions have smaller mass asymmetry than competing channels; this enhancement arises from the extra local excitation energy released as the DNS relaxes toward the minimum trajectory.

## Method
The authors solve master equations for nucleon transfer along proton and neutron degrees of freedom within the dinuclear system (DNS) model, treating quasifission via a temperature-dependent rate. Fusion probabilities are extracted by integrating the probability distribution up to the Businaro-Gallone point, and evaporation residue cross sections are computed using a statistical model with neutron-emission and fission widths.

## Key numbers
- For reactions leading to **²⁷⁴Hs***: quasifission barrier \(B_{\text{qf}}\) = 10.72 MeV (²⁶Mg+²⁴⁸Cm), 8.96 MeV (³⁶S+²³⁸U), 6.65 MeV (⁴⁸Ca+²²⁶Ra); inner fusion barrier \(B_{\text{fus}}\) = 2.71 MeV (³⁶S+²³⁸U), 6.82 MeV (⁴⁸Ca+²²⁶Ra); the ²⁶Mg+²⁴⁸Cm reaction has no reported \(B_{\text{fus}}\) value.
- For the reaction **³⁶S+²³⁸U → ²⁷⁴Hs***, the predicted maximal 4n-channel evaporation residue cross section is 11.6 pb at compound-nucleus excitation energy \(E^{*} = 41\) MeV.
- For reactions leading to **²⁸⁶Cn***: \(B_{\text{qf}}\) = 6.25 MeV (⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U), 6.60 MeV (⁵²Ca+²³⁴U), 6.70 MeV (⁵³Ca+²³³U), 5.86 MeV (⁵⁵Sc+²³¹Pa); \(B_{\text{fus}}\) = 10.29 MeV (⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U), 9.08 MeV (⁵²Ca+²³⁴U), 7.86 MeV (⁵³Ca+²³³U), 10.47 MeV (⁵⁵Sc+²³¹Pa).
- The calculated evaporation residue cross sections for all studied reactions agree with experimental data within a factor of 1–3.

## Context
This work extends the DNS-model studies of superheavy-nucleus synthesis by Feng et al. (2007, 2009) and Adamian et al. (2004), who established that fusion probability typically increases with mass asymmetry due to lower inner fusion barriers and higher quasifission barriers. The present results contradict that simple trend for radioactive-beam-induced reactions (⁵³Ca+²³³U, ⁵⁵Sc+²³¹Pa), showing that the potential energy of the initial DNS, quantified by \(\Delta U\), can dominate over the mass-asymmetry effect. This enables future searches for favorable projectile-target combinations in synthesizing elements with \(Z > 118\), where capture cross sections and survival probability must also be considered.
