# Novel mechanism of high-$p_T$ production from an opaque quark-gluon plasma

> We find that in an opaque quark-gluon plasma, significant particle production at high transverse momenta can occur via acceleration of (initially) lower-momentum partons. This new mechanism, which is the opposite of parton energy loss (jet quenching), has important implications for observables, such as elliptic flow, in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies and above.

## Key claim

In an opaque quark-gluon plasma with opacities about six times higher than perturbative estimates, a significant fraction of high-transverse-momentum particles can originate from low-momentum partons that are accelerated via multiple scatterings, rather than from jet quenching or corona emission. This mechanism is presented as a complementary source to parton energy loss, not a replacement, and its importance depends on the specific opacity.

## Method

The author used covariant parton transport theory (Molnar's Parton Cascade algorithm, MPC) to solve inelastic Boltzmann equations for massless gluons, light quarks/antiquarks, and charm quarks/antiquarks in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm. All leading-order \(2 \to 2\) QCD processes (elastic and inelastic) were included, with parton densities increased two-fold and cross sections increased about three-fold relative to perturbative estimates, yielding \(\sigma_{gg\to gg} = 10\) mb and \(dN(b=0)/d\eta = 2000\) at midrapidity. The transport solutions were obtained using parton subdivision to maintain Lorentz covariance.

## Key numbers

- Parton densities were increased two-fold and cross sections increased about three-fold from perturbative estimates, giving \(\sigma_{gg\to gg} = 10\) mb and \(dN(b=0)/d\eta = 2000\) at midrapidity.
- At final \(p_T \sim 7-8\) GeV, energy loss gives only one half of the yield, while almost one-third of the partons are accelerated from initially lower momenta.
- Partons have \(\langle N_{coll}\rangle \approx 14\) scatterings on average at the opacities considered.
- The accelerated soft partons predominantly come from initial \(p_{T,i} \sim 1\) GeV, where the initial parton \(p_T\) distribution has a strong peak.
- The study considered six times higher opacities than that of a weakly-coupled perturbative parton plasma, which is 2-3 times smaller than opacities estimated from RHIC data.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of high-\(p_T\) production beyond the standard jet quenching picture (parton energy loss) and challenges geometric elliptic flow bounds argued by Shuryak (2004), which assumed extreme quenching. The results provide a natural explanation for why elliptic flow at RHIC exceeds those geometric bounds and decreases relatively slowly at high transverse momenta, as observed by Aihong (2004). The study enables future investigations at larger opacities (e.g., at LHC) and motivates inclusion of radiative \(2 \leftrightarrow 3\) processes.
