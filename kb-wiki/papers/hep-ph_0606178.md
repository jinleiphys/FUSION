# Higgs instability in gapless superfluidity/superconductivity

> In this letter we explore the Higgs instability in the gapless superfluid/superconducting phase. This is in addition to the (chromo)magnetic instability that is related to the fluctuations of the Nambu-Goldstone bosonic fields. While the latter may induce a single-plane-wave LOFF state, the Higgs instability favors spatial inhomogeneity and cannot be removed without a long range force. In the case of the g2SC state the Higgs instability can only be partially removed by the electric Coulomb energy. But this does not exclude the possibility that it can be completely removed in other exotic states such as the gCFL state.

# Higgs instability in gapless superfluidity/superconductivity

## Key claim

The Higgs instability, arising from magnitude fluctuations of the superconducting order parameter, is a universal feature of all gapless superfluids/superconductors that exhibit the Sarma instability; while the electric Coulomb energy can partially remove this instability in the g2SC state at low and high momenta, it cannot eliminate it completely for intermediate momenta, though complete removal may be possible in other exotic states such as the gCFL state.

## Method

The authors analyze the Higgs instability by starting from the bosonized 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the g2SC phase, computing the thermodynamic potential including Higgs fluctuations at zero temperature. They derive the Higgs self-energy function Π(k) and its expansion coefficients A_H and B_H for small momenta, then incorporate the electric Coulomb energy via the Legendre transformed free energy to obtain the Coulomb-improved self-energy function Π̃(k) across all momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The Sarma instability condition for the g2SC state in weak coupling (δμ << μ̄): (∂²Ω/∂Δ²)_{\mu_e} = (4μ̄²/π²)[1 - δμ/√(δμ² - Δ²)] < 0
- The coefficient B_H for small momenta: B_H = (2μ̄²)/(9π²Δ²)[1 - (δμ)³/((δμ² - Δ²)^{3/2})]
- The Coulomb-improved zero-momentum coefficient: Ã_H = [4(b² - 3a²)μ̄²(δμ - √(δμ² - Δ²))] / [π²(3a²√(δμ² - Δ²) + b²(2δμ + √(δμ² - Δ²)))] > 0 for the entire g2SC region
- The Higgs self-energy Π(k) reaches its minimum at k ≃ 4Δ, indicating phase separation
- For k >> Δ, Π(k) ≃ (μ̄²/π²)(ln(k/Δ) + const.) > 0

## Context

This work extends the understanding of instabilities in gapless superconducting phases beyond the previously identified (chromo)magnetic instability related to Nambu-Goldstone boson fluctuations (chromo-ins-g2SC, chromo-ins-gCFL, 2004-2005). While the chromomagnetic instability may induce a single-plane-wave LOFF state (FF-Ren, 2005), the Higgs instability favors spatial inhomogeneity and cannot be removed without a long range force. The analysis explains the failure to observe the breached pairing (BP) state in imbalanced cold atom experiments (cold-atom-Exp, 2003-2005) and suggests that phase separation or crystalline structures such as the heterotic mixture of normal and superconducting phases (mixed-phase, 2003) or multi-plane-wave LOFF states (lo-phase, 2005) may be the true ground states. The general formulation can be generalized to the gCFL phase to examine whether electric and color Coulomb energies can completely eliminate the Higgs instability there.
