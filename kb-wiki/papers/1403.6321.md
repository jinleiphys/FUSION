# Evolution of transverse flow and effective temperatures in the parton phase from a multi-phase transport model

> I study the space-time evolution of transverse flow and effective temperatures in the dense parton phase with the string melting version of a multi-phase transport model. Parameters of the model are first constrained to reproduce the bulk data on the rapidity density, $p_{\rm T}$ spectrum and elliptic flow at low $p_{\rm T}$ for central and mid-central Au+Au collisions at $200A$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $2760A$ GeV. I then calculate the transverse flow and effective temperatures in volume cells within mid-spacetime-rapidity $|η|<1/2$. I find that the effective temperatures extracted from different variables, which are all evaluated in the rest frame of a volume cell, can be very different; this indicates that the parton system in the model is not in full chemical or thermal equilibrium locally, even after averaging over many events. In particular, the effective temperatures extracted from the parton energy density or number density are often quite different than those extracted from the parton mean $p_{\rm T}$ or mean energy. For these collisions in general, effective temperatures extracted from the parton energy density or number density are higher than those extracted from the parton mean $p_{\rm T}$ in the inner part of the overlap volume, while the opposite occurs in the outer part of the overlap volume. I argue that this indicates that the dense parton matter in the inner part of the overlap volume is over-populated; I also find that all cells with energy density above 1 GeV/fm$^3$ are over-populated after a couple of fm/$c$.

## Key claim

The parton system in the AMPT string melting model is not in full chemical or thermal equilibrium locally, even after averaging over many events; effective temperatures extracted from different variables (energy density, number density, mean pT) can be very different, and the dense parton matter in the inner part of the overlap volume is over-populated relative to an ideal QGP at the same mean pT.

## Method

The string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model was used, with Lund string fragmentation parameters tuned to reproduce bulk data (rapidity density, pT spectrum, and elliptic flow at low pT) for central and mid-central Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2760A GeV. The reaction volume within mid-spacetime-rapidity |η|<1/2 was divided into cells with transverse width 1 fm, and event-averaged quantities were calculated as functions of time and transverse location for hundreds to thousands of events at each energy and centrality. Effective temperatures were extracted from parton mean pT, number density, and energy density using Boltzmann distribution relations for a massless QGP.

## Key numbers

- Lund string fragmentation parameters tuned to a=0.55, b=0.15 GeV⁻² for RHIC Au+Au at 200A GeV; a=0.30, b=0.15 GeV⁻² for LHC Pb+Pb at 2760A GeV
- Strong coupling constant α_s=0.33 and parton cross section of 3 mb used for all simulations
- For the center cell in central Pb+Pb at 2760A GeV at t=0.2 fm/c: T_ε=548 MeV, T_⟨pT⟩=322 MeV, giving n ~ 8.4 n(T_⟨pT⟩)
- For the center cell in central Pb+Pb at 2760A GeV at t=5.0 fm/c: T_ε=199 MeV, T_⟨pT⟩=138 MeV, giving n ~ 4.3 n(T_⟨pT⟩)
- Critical temperature T_c=150 MeV used, corresponding to critical energy density ε_c=1.05 GeV/fm³
- All cells with energy density above 1 GeV/fm³ are over-populated after a couple of fm/c
- The relation T_ε⁴ ≈ T_n³ T_⟨pT⟩ holds within 6% for all cells with T_ε > 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the AMPT model (Lin 2004) and its string melting version to investigate the dense parton phase created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The finding that the parton system is not in full equilibrium locally, with over-population in the inner overlap region, is analogous to the glasma produced from color-glass-condensate initial conditions (Blaizot 2011, Lappi 2006). The space-time evolution data from this study have been posted online for use as bulk matter background in studies of jet propagation and interactions (Betz 2013, Burke 2013).
