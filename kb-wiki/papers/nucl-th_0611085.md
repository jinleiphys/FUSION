# Heavy ion collisions in the used nucleon model

## Key claim
The used nucleon model, combined with the assumption that the nucleon consists of two constituents (a quark and a diquark), describes the PHOBOS data on particle production at midrapidity, provided the parameter μ is set to approximately 0.41. The model is almost independent of the details of the diquark (whether p_d = p_q or p_d = 2p_q), and the only thing that really matters is the number of constituents.

## Method
The author applies the used nucleon model, a generalization of the wounded nucleon model, where the number of particles produced from one wounded nucleon depends on the number of inelastic collisions that nucleon underwent. For a nucleon undergoing k collisions, the multiplicity is given by n_k = n (1 - μ^k)/(1 - μ), where n is the average multiplicity in a single proton-proton collision and μ is a free parameter. The average multiplicity at midrapidity for symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions is computed using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density (R_Au = 6.37 fm, d = 0.54 fm) and a Gaussian form for the differential proton-proton cross-section with γ = 0.92. The model is then compared to PHOBOS data at four RHIC energies (√s = 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) using inelastic pp cross-sections of 32, 36, 41, and 42 mb respectively.

## Key numbers
- The parameter μ = 0.41 gives a good description of the RHIC Au-Au data.
- The wounded quark model would require μ ≈ 0.6 (from w^(q) ≈ 1.2), which leads to significantly larger multiplicities than observed.
- The wounded quark-diquark model with w^(q+d) = 1.18 to 1.19 gives the proper value μ ≈ 0.41.
- The alternative case with p_d = 2p_q and w^(q+d) = 1.185 gives results that differ by less than 5% from the μ = 0.41 case.
- Inelastic pp cross-sections used: 32 mb at √s = 19.6 GeV, 36 mb at 62.4 GeV, 41 mb at 130 GeV, and 42 mb at 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the used nucleon model proposed by Glauber (2006) as a generalization of the wounded nucleon model, which fails to describe correctly the observed particle multiplicities at RHIC. The author shows that the wounded quark model (with three constituent quarks) cannot reproduce the PHOBOS data, but the wounded quark-diquark model (with two constituents) naturally describes the data. This enables a consistent interpretation of particle production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies and suggests that the number of nucleon constituents, rather than their detailed properties, is the key factor.
