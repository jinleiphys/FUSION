# Quantum Statistics and Thermodynamics in the Harmonic Approximation

> We describe a method to compute thermodynamic quantities in the harmonic approximation for identical bosons and fermions in an external confining field. We use the canonical partition function where only energies and their degeneracies enter. The number of states of given energy and symmetry is found by separating the center of mass motion, and counting the remaining states of given symmetry and excitation energy of the relative motion. The oscillator frequencies that enter the harmonic Hamiltonian can be derived from realistic model parameters and the method corresponds to an effective interaction approach based on harmonic interactions. To demonstrate the method, we apply it to systems in two dimensions. Numerical calculations are compared to a brute force method that is considerably more computationally intensive.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for computing thermodynamic quantities of identical bosons and fermions in the harmonic approximation that works for any number of particles in principle, but the degeneracy counting relies on the crucial insight that the number of properly symmetrized states does not depend on the two-body interaction strength, only on the particle number and statistics.

## Method

The authors construct an N-body harmonic Hamiltonian where all two-body interactions are replaced by harmonic oscillators with frequency ω_int, and the external trap has frequency ω_0. They separate center-of-mass motion from relative motion, then compute the canonical partition function by recursively determining the degeneracies of properly symmetrized states using Eq. (e103), which subtracts center-of-mass contributions from the total number of non-interacting many-body states. The method is demonstrated for two-dimensional systems of identical bosons and fermions, with parameters fixed by matching the two-body binding energy and spatial extent from the zero-range Busch model.

## Key numbers

- The relative motion frequency is given by ω_r² = ω_int² (N/2) + ω_0² (Eq. e40)
- Center-of-mass degeneracy in two dimensions: d_{N_cm} = N_cm + 1
- For bosons, the lowest completely symmetric excited state of relative motion appears at N_rel = 2, creating a gap of 2ℏω_r in the excitation spectrum
- For fermions, the gap is ℏω_r
- The single-particle density of states at the Fermi level in a two-dimensional harmonic trap is roughly g_F ∼ (2N)^(1/2)/ℏω_0
- The many-body level density for fermions is proportional to exp[√(π² (2N)^(1/2) N_rel / 6)]

## Context

This work extends the harmonic approximation method previously described by Armstrong et al. (2011) into the thermodynamic regime, providing a computationally efficient alternative to brute-force symmetrization that grows exponentially with particle number. The method connects to the zero-range interaction model of Busch et al. (1998) for fixing parameters, and the results can be compared to experimental studies of ultracold atomic gases in two-dimensional traps, including measurements of heat capacity and compressibility near phase transitions as explored by Hu (2007) and Ku (2011).
