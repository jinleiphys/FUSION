# A New Look into the Partial Wave Decomposition of Three-Nucleon Forces

> We demonstrate that the partial wave decomposition of three-nucleon forces used up to now in momentum space has to be necessarily unstable at high partial waves. This does not affect the applications performed up to now, which were restricted to low partial waves. We present a new way to perform the partial wave decomposition which is free of that defect. This is exemplified for the most often used $2π$-exchange Tuscon-Melbourne three-nucleon force. For the lower partial waves the results of the old method are reproduced.

## Key claim

The partial wave decomposition of three-nucleon forces used previously in momentum space is necessarily numerically unstable at high partial waves (l ≥ 4, λ ≥ 4) due to an alternating sum that cancels an artificial singularity, but this instability did not affect earlier applications because they were restricted to low partial waves.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate the origin of the numerical instability using a simplified spin- and isospin-independent model of the 2π-exchange three-nucleon force, showing that the old decomposition introduces an apparent singularity in |p − p′| that must be canceled by an alternating sum over angular momentum quantum numbers, causing catastrophic loss of precision for high partial waves. They then develop a new partial wave decomposition that splits each term of the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force into two quasi two-body parts (each corresponding to a single pion exchange) linked by a change of Jacobi coordinates, avoiding the introduction of artificial singularities entirely. The new method requires permutation operators and two-fold interpolations using cubic hermitian splines for numerical efficiency.

## Key numbers

- The numerical instability in the old method appears for diagonal matrix elements with p ≥ 6 fm⁻¹, l = 4, q ≥ 12 fm⁻¹, and λ ≥ 4, where consecutive terms in the alternating sum are equal to within 8 digits, causing complete loss of numerical accuracy.
- The expansion coefficients H_{l₁l₂}(p,a) in the old method scale as p^{l₁−1} for large p, requiring cancellation by the alternating sum (1−1)^{l₁}.
- The new method reproduces the results of the Los Alamos-Iowa group for bound state calculations when extended to higher partial waves.

## Context

This work extends the partial wave decomposition techniques for three-nucleon forces originally developed by Coon and Glöckle (1981) for the Tucson-Melbourne 2π-exchange force. The new method enables inclusion of higher partial waves (beyond l = 4, λ = 4) in three-nucleon scattering calculations, which is necessary for reliable predictions at nucleon lab energies above 100 MeV where the authors previously predicted large three-nucleon force effects based on truncated partial wave bases. The improved decomposition allows future studies of three-nucleon force effects in the continuum and in bound states without the numerical limitations of the previous approach.
