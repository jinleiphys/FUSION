# Phase Boundary of Nuclear Matter in Magnetic Field

> Nuclear matter with a strong magnetic field is prevalent inside neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions. In a sufficiently large magnetic field the ground state is either a chiral soliton lattice (CSL), an array of solitons of the neutral pion field, or a domain-wall Skyrmion phase in which Skyrmions emerge inside the chiral solitons. In the region of large chemical potential and a magnetic field lower than its critical value for CSL, a Skyrmion crystal is expected to take up the ground state based on the chiral perturbation theory at the next leading order. We determine the phase boundary between such a Skyrmion crystal and the QCD vacuum. There was a conjecture that a magnetic field deforms the Skyrmion into a pancake shape whose boundary is a superconducting ring of charged pions. In contrast, through the exact Skyrmion solution, we find that the pancake conjecture holds approximately in a strong magnetic field, but fails for a weak one. We also validate that a Skyrmion would shrink to null without the Skyrme term, although Derrick's scaling law is modified by a background magnetic field, and the stability at the leading order is not ruled out in theory.

## Key claim

The authors determine the phase boundary between a Skyrmion crystal phase and the QCD vacuum in the presence of a magnetic field, finding that the pancake Skyrmion conjecture of Son and Stephanov holds approximately for strong magnetic fields but fails for weak ones, and that the critical chemical potential for Skyrmion nucleation coincides with the CSL critical line only at a specific crossing point.

## Method

The authors solve the gauged Skyrme model at next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, incorporating the Wess-Zumino-Witten term to account for the baryon chemical potential and magnetic field effects. They numerically compute the mass of a single Skyrmion as a function of magnetic field using an axial hedgehog ansatz with cylindrical symmetry, then determine the critical chemical potential where the free energy becomes negative, signaling the nucleation of a Skyrmion from vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass predicted by the model: \(m_{\text{N}} = 864.3\ \text{MeV}\) at zero magnetic field (8% discrepancy from experimental 938.9 MeV due to lack of quantization)
- Crossing point of Skyrmion crystal and CSL phase boundaries: \(B^{\ast} = 4.55\ m_{\pi}^{2}\) and \(\mu^{\ast} = 0.932\ m_{\text{N}}\)
- Critical chemical potential for excited Skyrmion (spin anti-parallel to magnetic field): \(\mu_{+} = M_{+}(B)\), with \(M_{+} > M_{-}\) for all B
- Pion decay constant used: \(f_{\pi} = 54\ \text{MeV}\); pion mass: \(m_{\pi} = 138\ \text{MeV}\); Skyrme parameter: \(s = 4.84\)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Skyrmion crystals in magnetic fields by Kawaguchi (2018) and Chen (2021), and directly tests the pancake Skyrmion conjecture of Son and Stephanov (2007). The phase boundary determined here enables future investigation of the transition or crossover between the Skyrmion crystal phase and the domain-wall Skyrmion phase discovered by Eto (2023) and Amari (2024), and suggests that the baryon crystal analysis of Evans (2022, 2023) may need reexamination in light of the DWSk phase.
