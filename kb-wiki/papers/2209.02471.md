# Cross-channel constraints on resonant antikaon-nucleon scattering

> Chiral perturbation theory and its unitarized versions have played an important role in our understanding of the low-energy strong interaction. Yet, so far, such studies typically deal exclusively with perturbative or nonperturbative channels. In this letter, we report on the first global study of meson-baryon scattering up to one-loop order. It is shown that covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, including its unitarization for the negative strangeness sector, can describe meson-baryon scattering data remarkably well. This provides a highly non-trivial check on the validity of this important low-energy effective field theory of QCD. We show that the $\bar{K}N$ related quantities can be better described in comparison with those of lower-order studies, and with reduced uncertainties due to the stringent constraints from the $πN$ and $K N$ phase shifts. In particular, we find that the two-pole structure of $Λ(1405)$ persists up to one-loop order reinforcing the existence of two-pole structures in dynamically generated states.

## Key claim

The authors report the first global study of meson-baryon scattering across all three strangeness sectors (S=0, +1, -1) using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), with a unitarization procedure for the nonperturbative S=-1 sector. The key hedge is that the description of antikaon-nucleon quantities is improved and uncertainties are reduced compared to lower-order studies, but only because of the stringent constraints imposed by the pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon phase shifts.

## Method

The authors employ covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory in the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) formulation to compute scattering amplitudes up to NNLO for the perturbative πN and KN channels. For the nonperturbative K̅N channel, they restore two-body unitarity using a matrix-inverse method where the unitarized amplitude is constructed from the chiral amplitude and a loop function, ensuring that the unitarized and perturbative amplitudes coincide when expanded to third chiral order. A global fit of 33 free parameters (27 low-energy constants plus six subtraction constants) is performed simultaneously to πN, KN, and K̅N scattering data, with the πN-related constants fixed from a prior study (Lu, 2018).

## Key numbers

- Best fit χ²/d.o.f. for K̅N channel: 1.56 (173 data points), compared to about 2 from the NLO study of Guo (2012)
- Best fit χ²/d.o.f. for πN: 1.28 (78 data points); for KN_{I=0}: 0.46 (60 data points); for KN_{I=1}: 1.46 (60 data points)
- K̅N scattering length: a_{K⁻p} = (-0.71 ± 0.07) + i(0.84 ± 0.07) fm
- Threshold ratios: γ = 2.35 ± 0.19, R_c = 0.684 ± 0.033, R_n = 0.198 ± 0.019
- Lower Λ(1405) pole (Λ(1380)): 1392 ± 8 − i(102 ± 15) MeV
- Higher Λ(1405) pole: 1425 ± 1 − i(13 ± 4) MeV
- Λ(1670) pole: (1676, −25) MeV

## Context

This work extends the NNLO BCHPT calculation of πN and KN scattering performed by Lu (2018) by unifying it with a nonperturbative formulation of the K̅N amplitude, enabled by SU(3) flavor symmetry and its breaking. It improves upon previous NLO unitarized studies such as Guo (2012), Ikeda (2012), and Mai (2014) by simultaneously constraining all three strangeness sectors, which reduces the uncertainties in the extracted Λ(1405) pole positions. The confirmation of the two-pole structure of Λ(1405) at NNLO reinforces the interpretation of dynamically generated states as molecular, and the formalism enables tighter constraints on extracted amplitudes and resonances for future experiments including SIDDHARTA-2, AMADEUS, and J-PARC E15/E31/E57/E62.
