# $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ global polarization from the core-corona model

> We report on work aimed to describe the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ global polarizations in a heavy-ion collision environment using the core-corona model, where the source of these hyperons is a high-density core and a less dense corona. We show that the overall properties of the polarization excitation functions can be linked to the relative abundance of $Λ$s and $\barΛ$s coming from the core versus those coming from the corona. Both global polarizations peak at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 10$ GeV. The exact positions and heights of these peaks depend not only on a reversal of relative abundances with collision energy, but also on the centrality class, both related to the QGP volume and lifetime.

# Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) Global Polarization from the Core-Corona Model

## Key claim

The overall properties of the Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) global polarization excitation functions can be linked to the relative abundance of hyperons coming from a high-density core versus a less dense corona, with both polarizations peaking at collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 10\) GeV; however, the exact positions and heights of these peaks depend not only on a reversal of relative abundances with collision energy but also on the centrality class, both related to the QGP volume and lifetime.

## Method

The authors employed a core-corona two-component model where Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) hyperons are produced either in a high-density core (QGP) via coalescence-type processes or in a less dense corona via nucleon-nucleon reactions. The average global polarization was computed as a function of the number of Λs produced in each region, the ratios of \(\bar{Λ}\)s to Λs in core and corona, and intrinsic polarizations derived from a field-theoretical model for quark spin alignment with vorticity. The number of Λs from each region was estimated using a critical density of participants, above which QGP is formed, with the participant density obtained from thickness functions and the energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section.

## Key numbers

- Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) global polarizations peak at collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 10\) GeV.
- For Ag+Ag collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.55\) GeV in the 10-40% centrality class, the model initially predicted zero polarization due to the critical density for QGP formation not being achieved.
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV, the Λ global polarization as a function of impact parameter shows an increasing behavior up to \(b \simeq 6\) fm, where \(\NREC \sim \NQGP\), then rapidly decreases to zero for \(b \simeq 7.5\) fm.
- The mean transverse polarization from p+p collisions in the energy range \(10 \leq \sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 63\) GeV is \(\mathcal{P} = -0.38 \pm 0.06\) (from Panagiotou, 1986).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous core-corona model (Ayala, 2020; Ayala, 2021) for Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) global polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which was previously applied to Au+Au systems. The model is compared to STAR Collaboration data (STAR, 2017) and preliminary HADES Collaboration results for Ag+Ag collisions (Kornas, 2022). The approach provides a differentiated behavior for Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) polarizations at low collision energies, relevant for experiments such as HADES (2-2.4 GeV), STAR-FXT (3-7.7 GeV), STAR-BES II (7.7-19 GeV), and future MPD at NICA (4-11 GeV). The authors identify limitations in describing peripheral collisions and propose future improvements including a smooth transition between core and corona regions and inclusion of Λ polarization from corona processes.
