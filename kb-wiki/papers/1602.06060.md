# Electromagnetic and Weak Nuclear Structure Functions $F_{1,2}(x,Q^2)$ in the Intermediate Region of $Q^2$

> We have studied nuclear structure functions $F_{1A}(x,Q^2)$ and $F_{2A}(x,Q^2)$ for electromagnetic and weak processes in the region of $1 GeV^2 < Q^2 <8 GeV^2$. The nuclear medium effects arising due to Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions and shadowing effects are taken into account using a many body field theoretical approach. The calculations are performed in a local density approximation using a relativistic nucleon spectral function. The results are compared with the available experimental data. Implications of nuclear medium effects on the validity of Callan-Gross relation are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects cause a deviation from the Callan-Gross relation (2xF1A/F2A = 1) at the nuclear level, and that electromagnetic and weak nuclear structure functions differ by 1-2% in carbon and up to about 4% in iron at low x, with the difference becoming negligible at high x.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear structure functions F1A and F2A for both electromagnetic and weak processes using a many-body field theoretical approach with a local density approximation and a relativistic nucleon spectral function. They incorporate Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic (pion and rho) cloud contributions, and shadowing effects via Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering theory, using CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions at leading and next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The difference between electromagnetic and weak structure functions is 1-2% for carbon at low x, and increases to about 4% for iron at x=0.1.
- Mesonic cloud contributions increase 2xF1A and F2A by 20% at x=0.2, 12% at x=0.3, and about 2% at x=0.5, for iron at Q2=5 GeV2.
- The calculations are performed in the intermediate Q2 region of 1 GeV2 < Q2 < 8 GeV2.
- Results for F2A in iron at Q2=6 GeV2 are compared with experimental data from CCFR, EMC139, EMC140, and NuTeV experiments.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Haider (2015) to compare electromagnetic and weak nuclear structure functions in the intermediate Q2 region, complementing studies at Jefferson Lab (Mamyan, 2012) and neutrino experiments at Fermilab. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for experiments like MINERvA, and provide a microscopic description of the violation of the Callan-Gross relation due to nuclear medium effects.
