# Unified equation of state for neutron stars on a microscopic basis

> We derive a new equation of state (EoS) for neutron stars (NS) from the outer crust to the core based on modern microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations using the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential plus three-body forces computed with the Urbana model. To deal with the inhomogeneous structures of matter in the NS crust, we use the recent Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) nuclear energy density functional that is directly based on the same microscopic BHF calculations, and which is able to reproduce the ground-state properties of nuclei along the periodic table. The EoS of the outer crust requires the masses of neutron-rich nuclei, which are obtained through Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the BCPM functional when they are unknown experimentally. To compute the inner crust, Thomas-Fermi calculations in Wigner-Seitz cells are performed with the same functional. Existence of nuclear pasta is predicted in a range of average baryon densities between $\simeq$0.067 fm$^{-3}$ and $\simeq$0.0825 fm$^{-3}$, where the transition to the core takes place. The NS core is computed from the nuclear EoS of the BHF calculation assuming non-exotic constituents (core of $npeμ$ matter). In each region of the star, we discuss the comparison of the new EoS with previous EoSes for the complete NS structure, in particular, with the Lattimer-Swesty EoS and with the Shen et al. EoS widely used in astrophysical calculations. The new microscopically derived EoS fulfills at the same time a NS maximum mass of 2~$M_\odot$ with a radius of 10 km, and a 1.5~$M_\odot$ NS with a radius of 11.7 km.

# Unified equation of state for neutron stars on a microscopic basis

## Key claim

The authors derive a unified neutron star equation of state from outer crust to core using microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the Argonne v18 potential plus three-body forces, but the crust description relies on the BCPM energy density functional (with four adjustable parameters) rather than a fully ab initio calculation, and the core is restricted to non-exotic npeμ matter without hyperons or quarks.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential with three-body forces from the Urbana model, reduced to a density-dependent two-body force. For the outer crust, they used the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland formalism with experimental nuclear masses from AME2012 supplemented by Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the BCPM functional for unknown masses. The inner crust was computed via Thomas-Fermi calculations in Wigner-Seitz cells using the BCPM functional, which was constructed from the same BHF nuclear matter results via local density approximation plus a phenomenological surface term with four adjustable parameters. The core was treated as homogeneous npeμ matter using the direct BHF equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.160 fm^{-3} with energy per particle -16.00 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: 213.75 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 31.92 MeV, with slope parameter L = 52.96 MeV
- Nuclear pasta shapes predicted between average baryon densities of approximately 0.067 fm^{-3} (1.13×10^{14} g/cm^3) and 0.0825 fm^{-3}
- Maximum neutron star mass: 2 solar masses with a radius of 10 km
- A 1.5 solar mass neutron star has a radius of 11.7 km (11.6 km in the abstract)
- Root-mean-square deviation of BCPM functional for binding energies of 467 spherical nuclei: 1.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional approach (Baldo et al., 2008, 2010, 2013) to construct a unified neutron star equation of state, building on earlier microscopic BHF calculations. It provides a microscopically-based alternative to the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state (1991) based on Skyrme forces and the Shen et al. equation of state (1998) based on relativistic mean field models, which are widely used in astrophysical simulations. The new equation of state enables consistent neutron star structure calculations from crust to core within a single microscopic framework, and can be compared with the unified equations of state from the Brussels-Montreal group (Chamel et al., 2011; Pearson et al., 2012) based on BSk Skyrme forces.
