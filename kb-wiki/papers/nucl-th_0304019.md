# Leakage Effect on J/psi Pt Distributions in Different Centrality Bins for Pb-Pb Collisions at E/A=160 GeV

> A transport approach including a leakage effect for J/psi's in the transverse phase space is used to calculate the ratios between the J/psi transverse momentum distributions in several centrality bins for Pb-Pb collisions at E/A = 160 GeV. From the comparison with the CERN-SPS data, where the centrality is characterized by the transverse energy Et, the leakage effect is extremely important in the region of high transverse momentum and high transverse energy, and both the threshold and the comover models can describe the ratio well for all centrality bins except the most central one (Et < 100 GeV), for which the comover model calculation is considerably better than the threshold one.

# Leakage Effect on J/ψ p_t Distributions in Different Centrality Bins for Pb-Pb Collisions at E/A=160 GeV

## Key claim
The leakage effect, where high-transverse-momentum J/ψ mesons escape the region of anomalous suppression before being absorbed, is extremely important in the region of high transverse momentum (1–3 GeV/c) and high transverse energy (E_t > 80 GeV), and both the threshold and comover models can describe the ratio of J/ψ p_t distributions between centrality bins well for all bins except the most central one (E_t < 100 GeV), for which the comover model calculation is considerably better than the threshold one.

## Method
A transport equation describing the time evolution of J/ψ density in transverse phase space, including a leakage term from free streaming with transverse velocity v_t = p_t/√(p_t² + m_J/ψ²), was solved analytically. The ratio R_{i/j}(p_t) of J/ψ p_t distributions between different transverse energy (E_t) bins was calculated for Pb-Pb collisions at E/A = 160 GeV, using both a threshold model (instantaneous absorption above a critical participant density n_c) and a comover model (continuous absorption by comoving particles over a finite time), with the results compared to NA50 data.

## Key numbers
- Average anomalous suppression time t_A = 3–4 fm/c for central Pb-Pb collisions, extracted from comparison of theoretical calculations with data.
- Five E_t bins used: ΔE_t = [5,34], [34,60], [60,80], [80,100], [100,140] GeV, corresponding to bins i = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 respectively.
- Threshold model parameters: σ_abs = 6.4 mb, n_c = 3.75 fm⁻², ⟨p_t²⟩_NN = 1.11 (GeV/c)², a_gN = 0.081 (GeV/c)² fm⁻¹.
- Comover model parameters: n_co = 1.5 n_p, t_0 = 1 fm, n_f = 1 fm⁻², σ_abs = 4.5 mb, σ_co = 1 mb, with average comover action time t_A = 3.5 fm/c.
- Mean transverse momentum values: ⟨p_t⟩ < 1.2 GeV/c and ⟨p_t²⟩ < 2 (GeV/c)² for Pb-Pb collisions.
- Transverse energy distribution parameters: a = 1.27, q = 0.274 GeV, σ_NN = 32 mb.

## Context
This work extends the transport approach of Hufner (2002) by focusing on the ratio of J/ψ p_t distributions across centrality bins to isolate the leakage effect, which was previously studied only through mean ⟨p_t²⟩ values. It compares two competing models for anomalous J/ψ suppression, the threshold model of Blaizot (1996) and the comover model of Capella (1997) and Kharzeev (1997), and finds that the comover model with continuous absorption over a long time (3–4 fm/c) better describes the data for the most central collisions. The results support the interpretation that anomalous suppression is not instantaneous but takes a finite time, enabling future work to distinguish between QGP-based and hadronic suppression mechanisms.
