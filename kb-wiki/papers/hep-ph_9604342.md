# Strange quark distribution and corrections due to shadowing and isospin symmetry breaking

> The strange sea quarks distributions of nucleons obtained by two global analyses based on available structure function data of muon and neutrino deep inelastic scatterings are different from the strange sea quark distribution measured by the CCFR Collaboration from dimuon events in neutrino scattering. We discuss possible contributions to this discrepancy from the nuclear shadowing in the deuteron and from the isospin symmetry breaking in the sea between the neutron and the proton.

# Strange quark distribution and corrections due to shadowing and isospin symmetry breaking

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the strange sea quark distributions from global analyses (CTEQ and MRS) and the CCFR dimuon measurement can be partially explained by nuclear shadowing in the deuteron and isospin symmetry breaking in the sea between neutron and proton, but these corrections are too small to fully resolve the conflict, suggesting a new mechanism such as strangeness quark-antiquark asymmetry is needed.

## Method

The author derived the strange quark distribution from the equality (5/6)F2^{nu d}(x) - 3F2^{mu d}(x) = x s(x) using structure function data from NMC muon scattering on deuterium and CCFR neutrino scattering on iron, converted to deuterium via heavy-target correction factors. Corrections were calculated by adding a shadowing term Delta F2^{mu d} to the deuteron structure function following the estimate of Zoller (1992), and by adding an isospin symmetry breaking term (I/2)(F2^{nu n} - F2^{nu p}) using a non-Pomeron form O_q^n(x) - O_q^p(x) = a(1-x)^b with b=25 and a adjusted to reproduce the NMC Gottfried sum S_G = 0.240.

## Key numbers

- The shadowing correction in the deuteron (3/2)Delta F2^{mu d} following Zoller (1992) is about 30% of the strange quark distribution measured by CCFR, sufficient to explain the discrepancy between CCFR and MRS results.
- The isospin symmetry breaking correction (I/2)(F2^{nu n} - F2^{nu p}) is about 4% of the strange quark distribution measured by CCFR, too small to explain the discrepancy between CCFR and either CTEQ or MRS.
- To reproduce the observed Gottfried sum S_G = 0.240 reported by NMC, the integral of the sea quark difference between neutron and proton is int_0^1 dx [O_q^n(x) - O_q^p(x)] = 0.084 without shadowing, or 0.165 with the adopted shadowing correction.
- The MRS analysis applied a renormalization factor 0.94 to the CCFR structure function data, which is the reason for the large difference between CTEQ and MRS strange quark distributions.

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of quark distributions by the CTEQ Collaboration (1993) and the Durham-RAL group (MRS, 1993), which used NMC muon scattering data on protons and deuterium and CCFR neutrino scattering data on iron. The paper addresses the discrepancy between those global fits and the CCFR dimuon measurement of the strange quark distribution (1993). The isospin symmetry breaking correction follows the suggestion of Ma (earlier work) as an alternative explanation for the Gottfried sum rule violation reported by NMC (1991). The author concludes that neither shadowing nor isospin breaking fully resolves the conflict, and points to a proposed strangeness quark-antiquark asymmetry in the nucleon sea (Brodsky and Ma, 1996) as a needed new mechanism.
