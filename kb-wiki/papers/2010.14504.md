# Combined Explanation of the $Z\to b\bar b$ Forward-Backward Asymmetry, the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, $τ\toμνν$ and $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ Data

> In this article we propose a simple model which can provide a combined explanation of the $Z\to b\bar b$ forward-backward asymmetry, the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly (CAA), $τ\toμνν$ and $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ data. This model is obtained by extending the Standard Model (SM) by two heavy vector-like quarks (an $SU(2)_L$ doublet (singlet) with hypercharge $-5/6$ (-1/3)), two new scalars (a neutral and a singly charged one) and a gauged $L_μ-L_τ$ symmetry. The mixing of the new quarks with the SM ones, after electroweak symmetry breaking, does not only explain $Z\to b\bar b$ data but also generates a lepton flavour universal contribution to $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ transitions. Together with the lepton flavour universality violating effect, generated by loop-induced $Z^\prime$ penguins involving the charged scalar and the heavy quarks, it gives an excellent fit to data ($6.1\,σ$ better than the SM). Furthermore, the charged scalar (neutral vector) gives a necessarily constructive tree-level (loop) effect in $μ\to eνν$ ($τ\to μνν$), which can naturally account for the CAA (${\rm Br}[τ\toμνν]/{\rm Br}[τ\to eνν]$ and ${\rm Br}[τ\toμνν]/{\rm Br}[μ\to eνν]$).

## Key claim

The paper proposes a specific beyond-Standard-Model particle extension that can simultaneously explain four distinct anomalies (Z→bb forward-backward asymmetry, Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, τ→μνν ratios, and b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ data) with a combined significance of 6.1σ better than the Standard Model for the b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ fit alone, but the model requires new particles at the TeV scale and the explanation of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly depends on whether "new nuclear corrections" are included, with the preferred coupling strength changing by roughly a factor of two between the two cases.

## Method

The authors extend the Standard Model by adding two heavy vector-like quarks (an SU(2)ₗ doublet with hypercharge -5/6 and an SU(2)ₗ singlet with hypercharge -1/3), two new scalars (a neutral singlet S and a singly charged scalar φ⁺), and a gauged L_μ-L_τ symmetry. After electroweak symmetry breaking, the vector-like quarks mix with Standard Model down-type quarks, modifying Zbb and Zbs couplings; the charged scalar contributes to muon decay at tree level, affecting the determination of the Fermi constant; and the Z′ boson from the L_μ-L_τ symmetry generates loop-level effects in τ→μνν and, together with the charged scalar and heavy quarks, produces Z′ penguin contributions to b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ transitions. The authors perform global fits using the HEPfit code to constrain the model parameters against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The fit to Z→bb data requires |λ_b^Q|² = (1.12 ± 0.46) × (M_Q/TeV) and |λ_b^D|² = (0.18 ± 0.09) × (M_Q/TeV)
- For the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly without new nuclear corrections: |ξ|² = (0.043 ± 0.010) × (m_φ²/TeV²); with new nuclear corrections: |ξ|² = (0.021 ± 0.013) × (m_φ²/TeV²)
- The Z′ contribution to τ→μνν ratios gives Δ = (4.7 ± 2.3) × 10⁻³, and for m_Z′ = 1 TeV: (g′)² = (1.9 ± 0.9) × (m_Z′/TeV)
- The global fit to b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ data yields a pull of 6.1σ relative to the Standard Model with a p-value of 47.5%, with best-fit Wilson coefficients C^V_9μ = -1.06 ± 0.16, C^U_10′ = -0.24 ± 0.17, and C^U_10 = 0.18 ± 0.19
- The B_s-anti-B_s mixing constraint gives Δm_Bs/Δm_Bs^SM = 1.11 ± 0.09 for real new physics contributions

## Context

This work extends the literature on combined explanations of flavor anomalies, which previously included correlations between b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ data and the muon anomalous magnetic moment (Bauer 2015, Crivellin 2019), b→cτν data (Buttazzo 2017, Crivellin 2018), and the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly with b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ using a heavy vector boson (Capdevila 2020). The model is the first to simultaneously address all four anomalies (Z→bb, Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, τ→μνν, and b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻) with a single particle content. The new particles at the TeV scale provide discovery potential for the HL-LHC and future colliders such as FCC-ee, ILC, CEPC, and CLIC, while precision measurements of τ decays at Belle II and b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ patterns at HL-LHC could test the model.
