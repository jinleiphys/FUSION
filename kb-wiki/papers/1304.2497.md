# Effect of pairing on nuclear dynamic

> The effect of pairing on small and large amplitude dynamics is discussed. Pairing correlation is treated in a fully microscopic transport theory using a simplified BCS version of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogolyubov (TDHFB) theory. The approach is applied to study the Giant Dipole and Giant Quadrupole Resonance in spherical and/or deformed magnesium isotopes showing very good agreement with QRPA theory. The present framework also provides a consistent approach to describe nuclear reactions around the Coulomb barrier. As an illustration, the influence of pairing correlations on multi-nucleon transfer is studied for reactions at sub-barrier energy.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations affect both small and large amplitude nuclear dynamics, but the effect on high-lying giant resonances (like the IV-GDR) is primarily due to the initial fragmentation of occupation numbers near the Fermi energy, while low-lying collective modes (like the IS-GQR below 5 MeV) require the propagation of correlations in time and cannot be captured by frozen-occupation approximations.

## Method

The authors use a simplified BCS version of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogolyubov (TDHFB) theory, called TDHF+BCS or Canonical basis TDHFB (CbTDHFB), where the many-body trial wave-function is written as a quasi-particle state in BCS form and the equations of motion are solved for occupation numbers and anomalous density components. The transport equations are solved on a 3D mesh using a Skyrme functional (Skm*) for the mean-field and a contact interaction for the pairing channel. For transfer reactions, a double projection technique is applied to restore particle number conservation, and the Frozen Occupation Approximation (FOA) is used to avoid continuity equation issues when cutting the system into two pieces.

## Key numbers

- The IV-GDR in 28Mg shows a small shift of the main peak energy towards low energy when pairing is included, while the width (lifetime) is globally unchanged.
- The IS-GQR response in 34Mg (deformation parameter β2=0.33) shows good agreement between TDHF+BCS and QRPA for both K=0 and K=2 axes, including peak position and height.
- Low-energy collective modes below 5 MeV in the IS-GQR of 34Mg are present in QRPA and TDHF+BCS but absent in the FOA approximation.
- For the reaction 48Ca+40Ca at sub-barrier energies (Coulomb barrier VB=51.9 MeV, beam energy 48.6 MeV), pairing enhances one-neutron transfer probability slightly and significantly increases two-neutron transfer probability.
- The ratio P2n(BCS)/P2n(MF) for reactions XCa+40Ca (X=40 to 50) at a center-of-mass energy 6 MeV below the Coulomb barrier is strongly correlated with the initial pairing gap of the XCa nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the TDHF+BCS approach previously developed by Ebata et al. (2010) and Scamps et al. (2012, 2013) to study both giant resonances and nuclear collisions, showing that the simplified BCS version of TDHFB is a good compromise between numerical effort and description of pairing. The results for giant resonances are compared with QRPA calculations by Losa et al. (2010), showing good agreement. The study of pair transfer at sub-barrier energies addresses recent interest in understanding transfer as a competing or contributing effect to fusion, as discussed by Corradi et al. (2011) and Sarazin et al. (2012). The approach enables systematic studies of pairing effects on multi-nucleon transfer reactions and low-lying collective modes that cannot be treated by small-amplitude approximations.
