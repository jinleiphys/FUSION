# Neutral Pion Lifetime Measurements and the QCD Chiral Anomaly

> A fundamental property of QCD is the presence of the chiral anomaly, which is the primary component of the $π^0\rightarrowγγ$ decay amplitude. Based on this anomaly and its small ($\simeq$ 4.5%) chiral correction, a firm prediction of the $π^0$ lifetime can be used as a test of QCD at confinement scale energies. The interesting experimental and theoretical histories of the $π^0$ meson are reviewed, from discovery to the present era. Experimental results are in agreement with the theoretical prediction, within the current ($\simeq$ 3%) experimental error; however, they are not yet sufficiently precise to test the chiral corrected result, which is a firm QCD prediction and is known to $\simeq$ 1% uncertainty. At this level there exist experimental inconsistencies, which require attention. Possible future work to improve the present precision is suggested.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly provides the primary component of the π⁰→γγ decay amplitude, and with a small (≈4.5%) chiral correction, a firm QCD prediction of the π⁰ lifetime exists. However, current experimental results (≈3% precision) are not yet sufficiently precise to test this chiral-corrected result, which is known to ≈1% uncertainty, and at this level there exist experimental inconsistencies requiring attention.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical history of the π⁰→γγ decay, from the early current-algebra calculations through the discovery of the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly, and the subsequent development of chiral perturbation theory corrections. It surveys the experimental history of π⁰ lifetime measurements, including Primakoff effect measurements, direct measurements, and e⁺e⁻ colliding beam measurements, with particular focus on the PrimEx experiment at Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly provides the primary component of the π⁰→γγ decay amplitude, with a small chiral correction of ≈4.5%
- The theoretical prediction for the π⁰ lifetime is known to ≈1% uncertainty
- Current experimental results have ≈3% precision and agree with the theoretical prediction within that error
- The Particle Data Group (2010) lists the π⁰ lifetime as a key quantity

## Context

This review extends the theoretical and experimental history of the π⁰ lifetime from its discovery through the present era, building on the foundational work of Bell and Jackiw (1969) and Adler (1969) on the chiral anomaly, and the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985). The paper enables future precision tests of QCD at confinement-scale energies, particularly through the PrimEx experiment at Jefferson Lab and potential measurements at KLOE-2, which could test the chiral-corrected QCD prediction at the 1% level.
