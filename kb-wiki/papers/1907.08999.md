# Algebraic cluster models calculations for shape phase transitions of boson-fermion systems

> The Algebraic Cluster Model(ACM) is an interacting boson model that gives the relative motion of the cluster configurations in which all vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom are present from the outset. We schemed a solvable extended transitional Hamiltonian based on affine $ {SU(1,1)} $ Lie algebra within the framework for two-, three- and four- body algebraic cluster models that explains both regions $ O(4)\leftrightarrow U(3) $, $ O(7)\leftrightarrow U(6) $ and $ O(10)\leftrightarrow U(9) $, respectively . We offer that this method can be used to study of $kőĪ+ x$ nucleon structures with k = 2, 3,4 and x = 1, 2, . . . , in specific x = 1,2 such as structures $^{9}Be$,$^{9}B$,$^{10}B$ ; $^{13}C$, $^{13}N$, $^{14}N$; $^{17}O$, $^{17}F$. Numerical extraction to the energy levels, the expectation value of boson number operator and behavior of the overlap of the ground-state wave function within the control parameters of this evaluated Hamiltonian are presented. The effect of the coupling of the odd particle to an even-even boson core is discussed along the shape transition and, in particular, at the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper presents a solvable extended transitional Hamiltonian based on affine SU(1,1) Lie algebra that can describe shape phase transitions between O(4)‚ÜĒU(3), O(7)‚ÜĒU(6), and O(10)‚ÜĒU(9) dynamical symmetry limits for two-, three-, and four-body algebraic cluster models, respectively, with the claim that this method can be applied to kőĪ + x nucleon structures; however, the numerical results are limited to the specific case of k=2,3,4 with x=1,2, and the Hamiltonian parameters are extracted via least-square fitting to experimental data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct a transitional Hamiltonian using SU(1,1) pairing algebras for s and b bosons, with control parameters c_s and c_b that interpolate between the rigid (U) and non-rigid (O) dynamical symmetry limits. For each cluster system (two-body with U(4) algebra, three-body with U(7) algebra, four-body with U(10) algebra), they couple the boson core to fermions with specific angular momenta (j=3/2 for two-cluster odd-A nuclei, j=1/2 for three- and four-cluster odd-A nuclei) and solve the resulting Bethe ansatz equations to obtain energy eigenvalues. The Hamiltonian parameters are determined by minimizing the root mean square deviation between calculated and experimental energy spectra using Matlab software.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model (ACM) approach previously applied to őĪ-cluster nuclei such as ‚ĀłBe, ¬Ļ¬≤C, and ¬Ļ‚Ā∂O by Iachello (1981) and Bijker (2000-2002) to include boson-fermion systems with additional nucleons. The method builds on the SU(1,1) algebraic Bethe ansatz technique developed by Pan et al. (2000-2005) for transitional descriptions in the vibron model and őĪ-cluster model, and applies it to kőĪ + x nucleon structures previously studied within the cluster shell model (CSM) framework by various authors (2000-2015). The results enable systematic study of shape phase transitions in light nuclei with cluster structures, particularly for isotopes of Be, B, C, N, O, and F, and allow investigation of how an odd fermion affects the bosonic core's shape transition at the critical point.
