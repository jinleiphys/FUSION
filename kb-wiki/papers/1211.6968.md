# Unique first-forbidden $β$-decay rates for neutron-rich nickel isotopes in stellar environment

> In astrophysical environments, allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions are important, particularly for $β$-decay rates in presupernova evolution of massive stars, since they contribute to the fine-tuning of the lepton-to-baryon content of the stellar matter prior to and during the collapse of a heavy star. In environments where GT transitions are unfavored, first-forbidden transitions become important especially in medium heavy and heavy nuclei. Particularly in case of neutron-rich nuclei, first-forbidden transitions are favored primarily due to the phase-space amplification for these transitions. In this work the total $β$-decay half-lives and the unique first-forbidden(U1F) $β$-decay rates for a number of neutron-rich nickel isotopes, $^{72-78}$Ni, are calculated using the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory in stellar environment for the first time. For the calculation of the $β$-decay half-lives both allowed and unique first-forbidden transitions were considered. Comparison of the total half-lives is made with measurements and other theoretical calculations where it was found that the pn-QRPA results are in better agreement with experiments and at the same time are suggestive of inclusion of rank 0 and rank 1 operators in first-forbidden rates for still better results.

# Unique first-forbidden β-decay rates for neutron-rich nickel isotopes in stellar environment

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model produces total β-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nickel isotopes (⁷²⁻⁷⁸Ni) that are in better agreement with experimental measurements when unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions are included alongside allowed Gamow-Teller transitions, though the authors note that inclusion of rank 0 and rank 1 operators in first-forbidden rates would likely yield still better results.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions, constructing a quasiparticle basis via Bogoliubov transformation with pairing interactions in a deformed Nilsson oscillator potential. They calculated both allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden (U1F) β-decay rates for ⁷²⁻⁷⁸Ni, extending the calculations from terrestrial to stellar environments (temperature range T₉ = 0.01–30, density range 10–10¹¹ g/cm³) for the first time using this model. Experimental (XUNDL) data were incorporated where available, replacing calculated excitation energies with measured levels when they agreed within 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷⁴Ni and ⁷⁵Ni, pn-QRPA half-lives including U1F transitions achieved "excellent agreement" with measured half-lives; the DF3 + CQRPA calculation of Borzov (2006) overestimates total β-decay rates for these isotopes by roughly a factor of two.
- For ⁷²Ni and ⁷³Ni, pn-QRPA half-lives are roughly a factor of two larger than experimental half-lives from Fraile et al. (1998).
- For ⁷⁶⁻⁷⁸Ni, pn-QRPA half-lives are again roughly a factor of two larger than measured half-lives; the DF3 + CQRPA calculation of Borzov (2006) is 20%–30% larger than measured half-lives for ⁷⁷⁻⁷⁸Ni.
- Inclusion of U1F transitions decreased calculated half-lives from about 36% for ⁷²Ni to about 47% for ⁷⁸Ni.
- For ⁷⁸Ni at stellar density range 10²–10⁶ g/cm³, total β-decay rates increase from 3.5 s⁻¹ at T₉ = 0.01 to approximately 225 s⁻¹ at T₉ = 30; at density 10¹⁰ g/cm³, rates decrease to around 67 s⁻¹ at T₉ = 30.
- For low and intermediate stellar densities (10² and 10⁶ g/cm³), phase space increases by 2–5 orders of magnitude as stellar temperature goes from T₉ = 0.01 to 1.
- For ⁷²⁻⁷⁵Ni, phase space increases by 1–2 orders of magnitude for U1F transitions as stellar temperature and density increase.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of allowed weak interaction rates (Nabi 1999, 2004) and the terrestrial U1F β-decay studies of Homma et al. (1996) into the stellar domain for neutron-rich nickel isotopes. The results are compared with the self-consistent density functional plus continuum QRPA calculations of Borzov (2006) and with large-scale shell-model calculations including first-forbidden transitions by Zhi et al. (2013), which found smaller forbidden contributions (5%–25% reduction) for N=50 waiting-point nuclei. The calculated stellar β-decay rates for the waiting-point nucleus ⁷⁸Ni are intended for use in r-process nucleosynthesis simulations and presupernova evolution modeling, with the authors noting that enhanced U1F rates may yield higher electron fractions prior to collapse and speed up r-matter flow relative to calculations using only allowed GT transitions.
