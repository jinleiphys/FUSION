# Photo- and Electron-Production of Mesons on Nucleons and Nuclei

> In these lectures I will show some results obtained with the chiral unitary approach applied to the photo and electroproduction of mesons. The results for photoproduction of $ηπ^0 p$ and $K^0 π^0 Σ^+$, together with related reactions will be shown, having with common denominator the excitation of the $Δ(1700)$ resonance which is one of those dynamically generated in the chiral unitary approach. Then I will show results obtained for the $e^+ e^- \to ϕf_0(980)$ reaction which reproduce the bulk of the data except for a pronounced peak, giving support to a new mesonic resonance, X(2175). Results will also be shown for the electromagnetic form factors of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance, also dynamically generated in this approach. Finally, I will show some results on the photoproduction of the $ω$ in nuclei, showing that present experimental results claiming a shift of the $ω$ mass in the medium are tied to a particular choice of background and are not conclusive. One the other hand, the same experimental results show unambiguously a huge increase of the $ω$ width in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach dynamically generates several resonances (including the Δ(1700) and N*(1535)) from meson-baryon multiple scattering, and the paper shows that this framework reproduces experimental data for photo- and electroproduction reactions, though the claimed shift of the ω meson mass in nuclear medium is not conclusive and depends on background choices.

## Method

The authors apply the chiral unitary approach, using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorized kernels derived from lowest-order chiral Lagrangians, to compute scattering amplitudes for meson-baryon and meson-meson interactions in coupled channels. For the ω photoproduction in nuclei, they perform Monte Carlo simulations of the nuclear reaction including Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, final-state interactions, and the ω spectral function with in-medium self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1700) resonance is dynamically generated from coupled channels πΔ, ηΔ, and KΣ(1535); the squared couplings to Σ*K and Δη are factors 27.5 and 24 larger, respectively, than those predicted by the SU(3) decuplet assignment in the PDG.
- For the e+e- → φf0(980) reaction, the model reproduces the bulk of the data in strength and shape, but a peak around 2175 MeV is not reproduced, supporting the existence of a new resonance X(2175).
- The A1/2 helicity amplitude for the N*(1535) at real photon point (Q2=0) is 66 × 10-3 GeV-1/2 in the MAID2007 analysis, in agreement with the authors' results.
- The ω free width is Γfree = 8.49 MeV; the reanalysis of CBELSA/TAPS data gives an in-medium ω width of about 90 MeV at nuclear matter density ρ0 = 0.16 fm-3, with no conclusive evidence for a mass shift of about 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oller and Meissner (2000), and Jido (2003) for meson-baryon scattering to photo- and electroproduction reactions, dynamically generating resonances such as Δ(1700) and N*(1535) that appear as molecular states rather than ordinary three-quark states. The results for ω photoproduction in nuclei reanalyze the CBELSA/TAPS data from Trnka (2005), showing that the claimed ω mass shift is not conclusive while the large increase of the ω width in medium is unambiguous. The study enables further experimental searches for the X(2175) resonance and provides predictions for pion-induced reactions and nuclear transparency ratios that can be tested at facilities like ELSA.
