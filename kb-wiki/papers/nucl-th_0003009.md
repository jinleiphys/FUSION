# Compton scattering in a unitary approach with causality constraints

> Pion-loop corrections for Compton scattering are calculated in a novel approach based on the use of dispersion relations in a formalism obeying unitarity. The basic framework is presented, including an application to Compton scattering. In the approach the effects of the non-pole contribution arising from pion dressing are expressed in terms of (half-off-shell) form factors and the nucleon self-energy. These quantities are constructed through the application of dispersion integrals to the pole contribution of loop diagrams, the same as those included in the calculation of the amplitudes through a K-matrix formalism. The prescription of minimal substitution is used to restore gauge invariance. The resulting relativistic-covariant model combines constraints from unitarity, causality, and crossing symmetry.

## Key claim

Pion-loop corrections for Compton scattering are calculated using a novel approach that combines dispersion relations with a K-matrix formalism, enforcing unitarity, causality, and crossing symmetry; the abstract glosses over the fact that the model is restricted to a simple space with only nucleon, pion, and photon degrees of freedom, and that the results are not directly compared to experimental data because the Delta-resonance and other important channels are omitted.

## Method

The authors construct a K-matrix from tree-level diagrams using dressed nucleon-pion and nucleon-photon vertices and dressed nucleon propagators, where the dressing is expressed in terms of real form factors and self-energy functions obtained by applying dispersion relations to the pole contributions of loop diagrams. The imaginary parts of loop integrals are found using Cutkosky rules, considering only intermediate states with one nucleon and one pion, and the real parts are constructed via dispersion integrals; gauge invariance is restored through minimal substitution, including contact terms. The resulting T-matrix is obtained from the K-matrix via the equation T = K (1 - K i delta)^{-1}, ensuring unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The bare pseudovector form factor for the pion-nucleon vertex is chosen as a dipole with half-width Lambda^2 = 1.28 GeV^2.
- The convergence criterion for the iteration procedure is a normalized root-mean-square difference d_n < 10^{-8} between subsequent iteration steps.
- The physical anomalous magnetic moments used for normalization are F_2^{+,s}(m^2) = -0.06 and F_2^{+,v}(m^2) = 1.85.
- The bare renormalization constants obtained are kappa_B^s = 0.03 and kappa_B^v = 1.51 for one choice of the contact term, and kappa_B^s = 0 and kappa_B^v = 1.6 for the alternative contact term.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is taken as g = 13.02.

## Context

This work extends a previous relativistic covariant model for pion-nucleon scattering (Kondratyuk and Scholten, 1999) to include photon-induced processes, incorporating analyticity constraints that are known to be important near the pion-production threshold (Bergmann et al., 1993; L'vov et al., 1997; Hung et al., 1997; Hanstein et al., 1998). The approach uses dispersion relations for the gamma N N-vertex similar to earlier work by Bincer (1960) and dynamical models with perturbative meson-loop dressing (Naus et al., 1987), but goes beyond these by implementing analyticity in a unitary K-matrix framework. The model enables future calculations of pion photoproduction and Compton scattering that include the Delta-isobar degree of freedom and t-channel meson exchanges for direct comparison with experiment.
