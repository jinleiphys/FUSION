# Ab initio uncertainty quantification of neutrinoless double-beta decay in $^{76}$Ge

> The observation of neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay would offer proof of lepton number violation, demonstrating that neutrinos are Majorana particles, while also helping us understand why there is more matter than antimatter in the Universe. If the decay is driven by the exchange of the three known light neutrinos, a discovery would, in addition, link the observed decay rate to the neutrino mass scale through a theoretical quantity known as the nuclear matrix element (NME). Accurate values of the NMEs for all nuclei considered for use in $0νββ$ experiments are therefore crucial for designing and interpreting those experiments. Here, we report the first comprehensive ab initio uncertainty quantification of the $0νββ$-decay NME, in the key nucleus $^{76}$Ge. Our method employs nuclear strong and weak interactions derived within chiral effective field theory and recently developed many-body emulators. Our result, with a conservative treatment of uncertainty, is an NME of $2.60^{+1.28}_{-1.36}$, which, together with the best-existing half-life sensitivity and phase-space factor, sets an upper limit for effective neutrino mass of $187^{+205}_{-62}$ meV. The result is important for designing next-generation germanium detectors aiming to cover the entire inverted hierarchy region of neutrino masses.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first comprehensive ab initio uncertainty quantification of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) in ⁷⁶Ge, yielding a recommended value of 2.60⁺¹·²⁸₋₁·₃₆ (68% confidence level), but this result is conditional on the assumption that errors from different sources are normally distributed and mutually independent, and that the decay is driven by light Majorana neutrino exchange.

## Method

The authors employed two ab initio many-body methods, the in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM) and the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG), using nuclear strong and weak interactions derived from chiral effective field theory (χEFT) with explicit Δ-isobars at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). To propagate uncertainties from the low-energy constants (LECs) of the chiral interaction through the many-body calculations, they used a Sampling/Importance Resampling Bayesian approach combined with a Multi-output Multi-fidelity Deep Gaussian Process (MM-DGP) emulator for the VS-IMSRG, which allowed rapid evaluation of billions of LEC samples. The total uncertainty was decomposed into contributions from LEC calibration (ε_LEC), χEFT truncation (ε_χEFT), many-body truncation (ε_MBT), operator truncation (ε_OP), and emulator error (ε_EM), which were added independently.

## Key numbers

- The recommended total NME for ⁷⁶Ge is M⁰ν = 2.60⁺¹·²⁸₋₁·₃₆ (68% confidence level), with component uncertainties: ε_LEC = 0.75, ε_χEFT = 0.3, ε_MBT = 0.88, ε_OP = 0.47, ε_EM < 0.06.
- The long-range (LR) NME from VS-IMSRG using the EM1.8/2.0 chiral interaction was 2.14(9); including the short-range (SR) contact operator increased the total to 2.94(8).
- The IM-GCM calculation yielded a long-range NME of 1.67 and a total NME of 2.25(11) with the same interaction.
- The short-range operator contribution was M⁰ν_SR = [0.399, 0.526], with about 27% uncertainty propagated from the synthetic datum.
- The N2LO operator correction to the NME was 0.079 at renormalization scale μ = 500 MeV.
- Combined with the GERDA half-life limit T₁/₂ > 1.8 × 10²⁶ years, phase-space factor G₀ν = 0.237 × 10⁻¹⁴ years⁻¹, and g_A = 1.27, the effective neutrino mass upper limit is ⟨m_ββ⟩ = 187⁺²⁰⁵₋₆₂ meV.
- A next-generation tonne-scale germanium experiment with sensitivity up to 1.3 × 10²⁸ years would set ⟨m_ββ⟩ = 22⁺²⁴₋₇ meV.

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of the 0νββ-decay NME in ⁴⁸Ca (Yao 2020, Belley 2021, Novario 2021) to the experimentally crucial nucleus ⁷⁶Ge, and is the first to include the short-range contact transition operator (Cirigliano 2018) and comprehensive uncertainty quantification in a heavy candidate nucleus. The result addresses the longstanding factor-of-three spread in NMEs from phenomenological models such as the interacting shell model (Menendez 2009, Horoi 2016), quasiparticle random-phase approximation (Fang 2018), and energy-density functional methods (Rodriguez 2010, Song 2017), and enables more reliable interpretation of current and next-generation germanium experiments (GERDA, Majorana Demonstrator, LEGEND) aiming to cover the inverted neutrino mass hierarchy region.
