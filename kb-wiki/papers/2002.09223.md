# Strangeness in Nuclei and Neutron Stars

> We review the present status of the experimental and theoretical developments in the field of strangeness in nuclei and neutron stars. We start by discussing the $\bar K N$ interaction, that is governed by the presence of the $Λ(1405)$. We continue by showing the two-pole nature of the $Λ(1405)$, and the production mechanisms in photon-, pion-, kaon-induced reactions as well as proton-proton collisions, while discussing the formation of $\bar K NN$ bound states. We then move to the theoretical and experimental analysis of the properties of kaons and antikaons in dense nuclear matter, paying a special attention to kaonic atoms and the analysis of strangeness creation and propagation in nuclear collisions. Next, we examine the $ϕ$ meson and the advances in photoproduction, proton-induced and pion-induced reactions, so as to understand its properties in dense matter. Finally, we address the dynamics of hyperons with nucleons and nuclear matter, and the connection to the phases of dense matter with strangeness in the interior of neutron stars.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the state-of-the-art in experimental and theoretical efforts on strangeness in nuclei and neutron stars, but emphasizes that many two- and three-body interactions in the strangeness sector remain unknown, and interpretations of existing data are often too model-dependent.

## Method

The authors review experimental and theoretical developments for strange hadrons (kaons, antikaons, phi mesons, and hyperons) interacting with nucleons and nuclear matter, covering production mechanisms, in-medium properties, and bound-state searches. They connect these microscopic interactions to the equation of state of dense matter in neutron stars, discussing constraints from heavy-ion collisions, hypernuclei measurements, and astrophysical observations including gravitational wave events.

## Key numbers

- The repulsive KN interaction potential is V = +20 to +40 MeV at saturation density and zero kaon momentum.
- Theoretical predictions for the K⁻pp bound state show binding energies ranging up to approximately 100 MeV, with decay widths possibly comparable to the binding.
- The PREX experiment reported a parity-violating asymmetry of A^{Pb}_{PV} = 656 ± 60 (stat) ± 14 (syst) ppb and a derived neutron skin thickness of R^{Pb208}_{skin} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm.
- Heavy-ion collision analyses rule out strongly repulsive nuclear equations of state and phase transitions at densities less than three times that of stable nuclei, favoring soft equations of state at higher densities.
- Isospin diffusion measurements in Sn+Sn reactions at 50 AMeV constrain the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities (0.4 ≤ ρ/ρ₀ ≤ 1.2).
- The ASY-EOS experiment on Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon shows a linear dependence of symmetry energy with density, favoring a soft equation of state.
- Neutron stars have typical masses of 1-2 M⊙ and radii within the range 10-12 km.
- The hyperon puzzle arises because the onset of hyperons softens the equation of state, potentially leading to maximum masses incompatible with the recent 2 M⊙ observations.

## Context

This review extends the work of previous reviews on the nuclear equation of state (Oertel 2016, Burgio 2018) and neutron skin measurements (Thiel 2019) by incorporating the strange degree of freedom. It connects experimental efforts at facilities such as JPARC, DaΦNE, JLab, and MAMI with theoretical approaches ranging from variational calculations and renormalization group methods to lattice QCD. The paper enables future work on constraining the hyperon puzzle through upcoming measurements of hyperon-nucleon correlations, kaonic-deuterium experiments, and high-precision X-ray astronomy missions such as NICER and eXTP.
