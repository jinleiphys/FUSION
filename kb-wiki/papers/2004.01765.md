# Correlation between mean transverse momentum and anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The correlation between the mean transverse momentum of outgoing particles, $\langle p_t \rangle$, and the magnitude of anisotropic flow, $v_n$, has recently been measured in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider, as a function of the collision centrality. We confirm the previous observation that event-by-event hydrodynamics predicts a correlation between $v_n$ and $\langle p_t \rangle$ that is similar to that measured in data. We show that the magnitude of this correlation can be directly predicted from the initial condition of the hydrodynamic calculation, for $n=2,3$, if one replaces $v_n$ by the corresponding initial-state anisotropy, $\varepsilon_n$, and $\langle p_t\rangle$ by the total energy per unit rapidity of the fluid at the beginning of the hydrodynamic expansion.

## Key claim

The correlation between mean transverse momentum and anisotropic flow in Pb+Pb collisions can be predicted directly from the initial condition of the hydrodynamic calculation, without running the full hydrodynamic evolution, by replacing the final-state flow harmonics with the corresponding initial-state anisotropies and the mean transverse momentum with the initial energy per unit rapidity of the fluid.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 viscous relativistic hydrodynamics (V-USPHYDRO code) with TRENTO initial conditions to simulate 50,000 minimum-bias Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, then compared the resulting Pearson correlation coefficient ρ_n between ⟨p_t⟩ and v_n^2 to ATLAS data. They then derived an analytic expression for ρ_n in terms of initial-state quantities only (initial energy per entropy E_i/S and initial anisotropy ε_n), and evaluated this expression using 20 million TRENTO-generated events with narrow centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic simulations used 50,000 minimum-bias Pb+Pb events at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, with initialization time τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c, shear viscosity η/s = 0.047, and freeze-out temperature 150 MeV
- TRENTO model parameters: p=0 (geometric mean of thickness functions), nucleon width w=0.51 fm, fluctuation parameter k=1.6 for hydro calculations and k=2 for initial-state calculations
- Initial-state predictor calculation used 20 million minimum-bias Pb+Pb events sorted into 0.25% centrality bins
- For fixed-entropy test at impact parameter b=2.5 fm, 850 events were simulated with freeze-out temperature T_f = 156.5 MeV
- The initial energy per unit rapidity E_i is defined as τ_0 times the integral of energy density over the transverse plane
- The initial size R is defined as sqrt(2 ∫ (x²+y²) s dxdy / ∫ s dxdy) where s is entropy density

## Context

This work extends the ATLAS Collaboration's (2019) measurement of ρ_n in Pb+Pb collisions, which followed a suggestion by Bożek (2016). The authors confirm that event-by-event hydrodynamics reproduces the data, as also shown by Bożek (2020), but go further by tracing the correlation to initial-state quantities. They contradict earlier claims by Broniowski (2009) and Bożek (2012, 2017) that ⟨p_t⟩ fluctuations are driven by fluctuations in fireball size R, showing instead that initial energy per entropy E_i/S is the key predictor. The work enables rapid evaluation of ρ_n from initial-condition models without full hydrodynamic simulations, and suggests that ρ_n has limited sensitivity to transport coefficients, consistent with findings by Schenke, Shen, and Teaney (2020).
