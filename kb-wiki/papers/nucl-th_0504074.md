# Self-Consistent Nuclear Shell-Model Calculation Starting from a Realistic NN Potential

> First self-consistent realistic shell-model calculation for the light p-shell nuclei is performed, starting from the high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) CD-Bonn potential. This realistic potential is renormalized deriving a low-momentum NN potential V-low-k that preserves exactly the two-nucleon low-energy physics. This V-low-k is suitable to derive a self-consistent Hartree-Fock basis that is employed to derive both effective single-particle energies and residual two-body matrix elements for the shell-model hamiltonian. Results obtained show the reliability of such a fundamental microscopic approach.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully self-consistent realistic shell-model calculation for light p-shell nuclei, starting from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, with the caveat that the overall agreement with experiment is "quite satisfactory" but that inclusion of a realistic three-body force could lead to further improvement.

## Method

The CD-Bonn potential is first renormalized into a low-momentum potential V-low-k with a cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1, which preserves two-nucleon low-energy physics. This V-low-k is used to solve Hartree-Fock equations for the 4He doubly closed-shell core, expanding single-particle states in N=3 harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions with hbar-omega = 18 MeV. The HF single-particle eigenvectors define the model space (0p3/2 and 0p1/2 orbits), and an effective Hamiltonian is derived via time-dependent perturbation theory using the Q-box folded-diagram method generalized for non-degenerate model spaces, including diagrams through third order in V-low-k.

## Key numbers

*   Cutoff momentum for V-low-k: Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1
*   Number of harmonic-oscillator basis states for HF expansion: N = 3
*   Oscillator constant: hbar-omega = 18 MeV, derived from hbar-omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3}
*   Calculated 4He binding energy (third-order): 25.967 MeV
*   Calculated 6Li binding energy (third-order): 31.501 MeV (experiment: 31.995 MeV)
*   Calculated 6He binding energy (third-order): 29.797 MeV (experiment: 29.269 MeV)
*   Calculated 7Li binding energy: 39.556 MeV (experiment: 39.243 MeV)
*   Calculated 7Be binding energy: 37.751 MeV (experiment: 37.600 MeV)
*   Calculated 8Be binding energy: 54.010 MeV (experiment: 56.50 MeV)
*   Calculated 8Li binding energy: 44.365 MeV (experiment: 41.276 MeV)
*   Calculated 8B binding energy: 40.429 MeV (experiment: 37.74 MeV)

*   Calculated 6Li ground state quadrupole moment: -0.442 e mb (experiment: -0.818(17) e mb)
*   Calculated 6Li ground state magnetic moment: +0.866 nm (experiment: +0.822 nm)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent shell-model approach of Zamick (2002) by using the V-low-k renormalization technique of Bogner (2002) to overcome the short-range repulsion of realistic NN potentials, which previously required the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock procedure with its ambiguities for states above the Fermi surface. The results are compared with the authors' earlier semi-phenomenological two-frequency shell-model calculation (Coraggio, 2001) and show comparable or better agreement. This approach enables fully microscopic shell-model calculations without empirical single-particle energies, and the authors note that including a realistic three-body force, as in the work of Pieper (2001) and Navratil (2003), could further improve agreement with experiment.
