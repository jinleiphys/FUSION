# The Effect of Quantum Fluctuations in the High-Energy Cold Nuclear Equation of State and in Compact Star Observables

> We present a novel technique to obtain exact equation of state (EoS) by the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) method, using the expansion of the effective potential in a base of harmonic functions at finite chemical potential. Within this theoretical framework we determined the equation of state and the phase diagram of a simple model of massless fermions coupled to scalars through Yukawa-coupling at the zero-temperature limit. We compared our results to the 1-loop and the mean field approximation of the same model and other high-density nuclear matter equation of states. We found a $10-20\%$ difference between these approximations. As an application, we used our exact, FRG-based equation of states to test the effect of the quantum fluctuations in superdense nuclear matter of a compact astrophysical object for the first time. We calculated the mass-radius relation for a compact star using the Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation and observed a $\sim 5\%$ effect in compact star observables due to quantum fluctuations.

## Key claim
The authors present a novel technique using the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) method to obtain an exact equation of state for a simple Yukawa-type model, finding that quantum fluctuations produce a 10-20% difference compared to mean-field and 1-loop approximations, and a ~5% effect on compact star observables. The key qualifier is that this result applies to a simplified model of massless fermions coupled to scalars, not to full quantum chromodynamics or realistic nuclear matter.

## Method
The authors applied the Wetterich equation with a three-dimensional Litim regulator at zero temperature and finite chemical potential to a Yukawa-type model with one bosonic and one fermionic degree of freedom. They used the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) and solved the flow equation by introducing a coordinate transformation that maps the circular Fermi surface to a rectangular one, combined with a harmonic expansion of the effective potential. The resulting equation of state was then used as input for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation to compute the mass-radius relation of a static, spherically symmetric compact star.

## Key numbers
- The FRG-based equation of state is 10% softer than the 1-loop approximation and 20% softer than the mean-field approximation at the same energy density.
- The maximum mass of a compact star using the FRG-based equation of state is approximately 1.38 solar masses, with a corresponding radius of about 7.7 km.
- The mean-field and 1-loop approximated mass-radius curves produce stars that are ~5% less massive (about 0.1 solar masses less) and smaller by about half a kilometer compared to the FRG result.
- The chemical potential at the first-order phase transition in the mean-field approximation is approximately 0.6177 times the nucleon mass (0.938 GeV).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Barnafoldi 2016) on applying FRG methods to Yukawa-type models at finite chemical potential. The results are compared to the SQM3, WFF1, and AP4 equations of state from Ozel (2016), which are commonly used in compact star modeling. The FRG-based equation of state shows closest agreement with the SQM3 model, especially at high energy densities, though the simple Yukawa model never reaches the observed ~2 solar mass compact star maximum. This work enables future studies incorporating wave function renormalization and running Yukawa coupling to improve the model's realism.
