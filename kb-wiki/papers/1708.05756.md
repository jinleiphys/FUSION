# Photoproduction prospects at the EIC

> We discuss how photoproduction and electroproduction processes will provide a precise tool to study nuclear structure in the small-$x$ region in the future Electron-Ion Collider. In particular, we emphasize how exclusive vector meson production at the EIC can be used to accurately map the gluonic density profile of nuclei, including its event-by-event fluctuations. In addition, we show how measurements of the total diffractive cross section will be sensitive to saturation effects.

## Key claim

Exclusive vector meson production at the future Electron-Ion Collider can map the gluonic density profile of nuclei, including event-by-event fluctuations, and measurements of the total diffractive cross section will be sensitive to saturation effects, but the actual sensitivity depends on the meson species: for heavy mesons like J/Ψ, saturation effects are not especially large because small dipole sizes dominate, whereas lighter mesons like ρ or φ are more sensitive but suffer from unreliable perturbative calculations at low Q².

## Method

The paper uses the dipole picture of diffractive scattering, where a virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters elastically off the target. The scattering amplitude for exclusive vector meson production is expressed as an integral over dipole size, impact parameter, and longitudinal momentum fraction, involving the overlap of the photon and vector meson wave functions and the dipole-target cross section. The coherent cross section (target remains in the same quantum state) is the squared amplitude averaged over target configurations, while the incoherent cross section (target breaks up) is the variance of the amplitude, measuring fluctuations in the target wave function.

## Key numbers

- The proposed maximum center-of-mass energy for electron-proton scattering at the EIC is √s = 141 GeV, which is the region already covered by HERA.
- For electron-nucleus mode, the proposed maximum energy is √s = 40…90 GeV.
- Saturation scales accessed at the EIC in electron-gold collisions can be up to 2 GeV².
- The saturation scale Q_s² scales like A^{1/3} as there are approximately A^{1/3} overlapping nucleons.
- For the double ratio of J/Ψ to light meson production in central versus minimum bias events, the ratio approaches Q_{s,central}^4 / Q_{s,min.bias}^4 at low Q².

## Context

This work extends the HERA measurements of proton structure (Abramowicz 2015) and the EIC White Paper (Accardi 2012) and science case (Boer 2011) by focusing on how diffractive processes at the EIC can probe saturated gluonic matter described by the Color Glass Condensate effective theory (Gelis 2010). The paper builds on simulations of EIC capabilities for exclusive vector meson production (Toll 2012) and on studies of centrality-dependent diffractive scattering (Lappi 2014), and it enables future experimental programs to map nuclear gluon densities and test saturation models with unprecedented precision.
