# Update of the Electron and Muon g-Factors

> I give an overview of the different contributions to the electron and muon anomalous magnetic moments in the Standard Model. Special emphasis is given to recent QED results as well as to the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomaly.

## Key claim

The Standard Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment shows a persistent 3.6 sigma discrepancy with the experimental value, but the largest theoretical errors come from the hadronic vacuum polarization and hadronic light-by-light scattering contributions, which must be reduced before the planned FNAL and JPARC experiments can improve the experimental precision from 0.54 ppm to 0.14 ppm.

## Method

The paper reviews the QED contributions to the electron and muon g-factors up to tenth order in the fine structure constant, using the known analytic coefficients for lower orders and the numerical calculations by Kinoshita and collaborators for the eighth and tenth order terms. For the hadronic contributions, the author discusses the integral representation of the lowest-order hadronic vacuum polarization in terms of e+e- annihilation cross sections, and examines the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution using the Constituent Chiral Quark Model (CchiQM) of Manohar and Georgi, which incorporates Nambu-Goldstone modes and constituent quarks with an axial coupling g_A = 1.

## Key numbers

- Electron anomaly experimental value: a_e(exp) = 1,159,652,180.73 (0.28) x 10^{-12} (0.24 ppb precision, Harvard group)
- Electron anomaly Standard Model prediction: a_e(SM) = 1,159,652,181.82 (78) x 10^{-12} (0.67 ppb precision)
- Inverse fine structure constant extracted from electron anomaly: alpha^{-1}(a_e) = 137.035,999,1736 (331) (0.25 ppb precision)
- Muon anomaly experimental value: a_mu(E821-BNL) = 116,592,089 (54)_stat (33)_syst x 10^{-11} (0.54 ppm precision)
- Muon anomaly QED contribution: a_mu(QED) = 116,584,718.845 (37) x 10^{-11}
- Muon anomaly hadronic light-by-light contribution: a_mu(HLbyL) = 105 (26) x 10^{-11}
- Muon anomaly total Standard Model: a_mu(SM) = 116,591,801 (49) x 10^{-11}
- Constituent quark mass range reproducing HVP: M_Q = (240 plus or minus 10) MeV
- Leading log approximation for pion-exchange HLbyL: 95 x 10^{-11}

## Context

This review updates the status of lepton g-factor calculations following the work of Kinoshita and collaborators (2007, 2012) on the tenth-order QED contributions, and the BNL E821 collaboration measurement of the muon anomaly. The paper extends the earlier analyses by emphasizing the Constituent Chiral Quark Model as a reference model for hadronic contributions, following the approach of Manohar and Georgi (1984) and recent work by de Rafael and Greynat (2012). The results enable future experimental programs at FNAL and JPARC that aim to reduce the muon anomaly experimental uncertainty by a factor of approximately four, requiring corresponding improvements in the theoretical hadronic contributions.
