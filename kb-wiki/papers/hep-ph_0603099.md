# Quark pair production in high energy pA collisions: General features

> A consistent treatment of both multiple scattering and small x quantum evolution effects on pair production in high energy pA collisions is feasible in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate (hep-ph/0402257). We first discuss the properties of quark pair production in the classical effective theory where only multiple scattering effects are included. Explicit results are given for pair production as a function of the invariant mass of pairs, the pair momenta, the atomic mass number A and the quark mass. We relate the logarithms that appear in our formulation of pair production to logarithms that appear in the limit of collinear factorization in QCD. Violations of kT factorization and medium modifications, as represented by the Cronin effect, are also investigated. We next consider how small x quantum evolution (shadowing) effects modify the results for pair production. In particular, we provide results for the rapidity distribution of pairs and the dependence of the Cronin effect on rapidity. We discuss the dependence of our results on the initial conditions for small x evolution and comment on its implications for pair production at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

Quark pair production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions can be treated consistently within the Color Glass Condensate framework including both multiple scattering and small-x quantum evolution, but the cross-section is not generally kT-factorizable; it requires 2-point, 3-point, and 4-point multi-parton correlation functions of Wilson lines, which simplify only in the large-N and large-A limits.

## Method

The authors compute the quark pair production cross-section from the amputated time-ordered quark propagator in the classical color field of the collision, working to lowest order in the proton source and all orders in the nuclear source. They evaluate the multi-parton correlators (2-point, 3-point, and 4-point Wilson line correlators) in two regimes: the McLerran-Venugopalan model, which includes multiple scattering but no small-x evolution, and the Balitsky-Kovchegov mean-field limit at large N and large A, which incorporates small-x quantum evolution via solutions of the BK equation. The cross-section is expressed in terms of pair invariant mass, pair transverse momentum, and pair rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale of the nucleus is denoted Qs, with Qs^2 >> Lambda_QCD^2; the proton saturation momentum is denoted Qs,p when distinguished from the nuclear scale.
- The nuclear color charge density correlator is mu_A^2 = A/(2 pi R_A^2), proportional to A^{1/3}.
- The Gaussian weight functional for the nucleus is valid in the kinematic domain ln(A^{1/3}) << y_A << A^{1/6}, where y_A = ln(1/x_A).
- The cubic term in the nuclear weight functional is parametrically suppressed by A^{1/6} relative to the Gaussian term.
- The large-N limit of the 4-point correlator is given by (N^2/2) exp[-(N/4) mu_A^2 (Gamma(x-v) + Gamma(y-u))].

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) for pair production in pA collisions, where kT-factorization was shown to be broken at leading order. It relates the logarithms appearing in the CGC formulation to DGLAP logarithms in collinear factorization (Dokshitzer 1977, Altarelli and Parisi 1977, Gribov and Lipatov 1972). The results enable quantitative studies of the Cronin effect for quark pairs and its rapidity dependence, and provide a framework for comparing with data from RHIC (Deuteron-Gold collisions) and the LHC (proton-lead collisions).
