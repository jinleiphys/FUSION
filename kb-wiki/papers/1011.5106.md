# Self-Consistent RPA based on a Many-Body Vacuum

> Self-Consistent RPA is extended in a way so that it is compatable with a variational ansatz for the ground state wave function as a fermionic many-body vacuum. Employing the usual equation of motion technique, we arrive at extended RPA equations of the Self Consistent RPA structure. In principle the Pauli principle is, therefore, fully respected. However, the correlation functions entering the RPA matrix can only be obtained from a systematic expansion in powers of some combinations of RPA amplitudes. We demonstrate for a model case that this expansion may converge rapidly.

## Key claim

The authors extend Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) to be compatible with a variational fermionic many-body vacuum ground state, but the correlation functions entering the RPA matrix can only be obtained from a systematic expansion in powers of combinations of RPA amplitudes, and the practical success depends on the convergence rate of this expansion in realistic situations.

## Method

The authors construct a correlated ground state as an exponential of a two-body operator acting on a Hartree-Fock state, then build an extended RPA destruction operator that includes both the standard particle-hole terms and additional two-body terms that correct for the Pauli principle. They use the equation-of-motion technique to derive SCRPA equations with the usual mathematical structure, and evaluate the required correlation functions through a systematic expansion scheme. The method is tested on the two-level Lipkin model with separable monopole-monopole interaction.

## Key numbers

- For N=2 particles in the Lipkin model, the solution yields excitation energies Omega_+ = +sqrt(1+V^2) and Omega_- = -sqrt(1+V^2), which coincide with the exact solution.
- The correction term eta in the extended RPA operator is eta = 2/N, making it of order 1/N.
- The ground state expectation value of J_0 for general N is given by <J_0>/<Z|Z> = -(N/2) * [1 - 4z^2(N-1)^2] / [1 - 4z^2(N-1)(N-3)].
- Results are shown for N=4, N=6, N=10, and N=20 particles, with SCRPA(eta) systematically improving over SCRPA(0) in the range chi < 1.

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA approach previously developed by the same group (Schuck, 2000s) by placing it on a more consistent theoretical foundation with an actual many-body vacuum state. It addresses the long-standing problem that standard RPA violates the Pauli principle due to the quasi-boson approximation, and that previous SCRPA formulations lacked a true vacuum state. The approach is related to Coupled Cluster Theory (CCT) and the BBGKY hierarchy of time-dependent density matrices (TDDM), but differs by maintaining a variational Rayleigh-Ritz character. The method enables future applications to symmetry-broken phases and more realistic fermionic systems, though the convergence rate of the expansion scheme in realistic situations remains an open question.
