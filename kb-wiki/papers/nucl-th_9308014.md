# A Gauge Invariant Unitary Theory for Pion Photoproduction

> A covariant, unitary and gauge invariant theory for pion photoproduction on a single nucleon is presented. To achieve gauge invariance at the operator level one needs to include both the $πN$ and $γπN$ thresholds. The final amplitude can be written in terms of a distorted wave in the final $πN$ channel provided one includes additional diagrams to the standard Born term in which the photon is coupled to the final state pion and nucleon. These additional diagrams are required in order to satisfy gauge invariance.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant, unitary, and gauge invariant theory for pion photoproduction on a single nucleon, but the gauge invariance is achieved only to first order in the electromagnetic coupling and requires including both the πN and γπN thresholds simultaneously, contrary to earlier approaches that included only the πN threshold for unitarity.

## Method

The authors start from the one-particle irreducible πNN three-point Green's function and apply minimal substitution (pμ → pμ − eAμ) to gauge the theory, following Ohta's procedure. They then apply LSZ reduction to obtain the photoproduction amplitude, classify diagrams by irreducibility following Araki and Afnan (1987), and expose both the πN and γπN unitarity cuts to derive integral equations for the πN ← γN amplitude. The final amplitude is expressed in terms of a distorted wave in the final πN channel, with additional non-Born diagrams required to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The πN and γπN thresholds are at the same energy, requiring simultaneous inclusion for gauge invariance.
- The photoproduction amplitude is derived to first order in the electromagnetic coupling.
- The amplitude is expressed as M(0) = (t(0)g + 1)ṽ, where ṽ contains both the Born amplitude ṽB and additional diagrams ṽR needed for gauge invariance.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Araki and Afnan (1987) for two-body unitarity in πN scattering to include electromagnetic interactions, and follows the gauging procedure developed by Ohta (1989). It contradicts the common practice of unitarizing the Born amplitude using only the πN final state interaction, showing that such an approach (Eq. 7) does not satisfy gauge invariance. The theory enables consistent calculations of pion photoproduction that simultaneously respect unitarity and gauge invariance, which is necessary for reliable predictions in low-energy nuclear physics.
