# Spectra and femtoscopic scales in a hydrokinetic model for baryon-rich fireballs

> The hydrokinetic model and the scheme of dynamical freeze out proposed earlier for the RHIC energy are extended to lower colliding energy for non-vanishing baryon chemical potential. In this case a new two-phase equation of state is applied where baryon-rich hadronic matter is described within modified relativistic mean-field theory. As an example, the approach is employed for analyzing pion and kaon spectra and interferometry radii of Pb+Pb collisions at laboratory energies 40 and 158 AGeV. A good agreement is observed for the transverse mass dependence of the longitudinal radius for both energies studied. The transverse radii $R_{out}, R_{side}$ reproduce experiment at $E_{lab}=$158\ AGeV but at $E_{lab}=$40\ AGeV there is a marked (20 \%) difference from experimental points. This discrepancy is more noticeable in analysis of the ratio $R_{out}/R_{side}$. Nevertheless, considered examples of application of the hydrokinetic model with equation of state including the first order phase transition are quite successful and further development of the model is required.

## Key claim

The hydrokinetic model, previously successful at RHIC energies, is extended to lower collision energies with non-zero baryon chemical potential and a two-phase equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition; while the model reproduces longitudinal pion interferometry radii well at both 40 and 158 AGeV, the transverse radii at 40 AGeV show a marked (20%) deviation from experimental data, indicating that further model development is required.

## Method

The authors apply the HydroKinetic Model (HKM) to central Pb+Pb collisions at laboratory energies of 40 and 158 AGeV, using boost-invariant Bjorken-like initial conditions with a thermalization time of 1 fm/c and Glauber-like initial energy density profiles. The equation of state combines a modified relativistic mean-field model (SHMC) for the chemically equilibrated region above T_ch with an ideal hadron-resonance gas including an excluded volume correction below T_ch, matched at chemical freeze-out temperatures and baryon chemical potentials of {160 MeV, 220 MeV} for 158 AGeV and {148 MeV, 370 MeV} for 40 AGeV. The dynamical decoupling of hadrons is treated via escape probabilities and emission functions derived from Boltzmann equations, with collision rates calculated using the UrQMD method.

## Key numbers

- Maximal initial energy density: ε₀ = 5 GeV/fm³ (average 3.5 GeV/fm³) for E_lab = 40 AGeV; ε₀ = 8 GeV/fm³ (average 5.6 GeV/fm³) for E_lab = 158 AGeV
- Average initial transverse velocity: ⟨v_T⟩ = 0.196 for both energies
- Chemical freeze-out parameters: {T, μ_B} = {160 MeV, 220 MeV} for 158 AGeV; {T, μ_B} = {148 MeV, 370 MeV} for 40 AGeV
- Energy and net baryon densities at matching: {ε, n_B} = {0.4 GeV/fm³, 0.085 1/fm³} for 158 AGeV; {ε, n_B} = {0.34 GeV/fm³, 0.12 1/fm³} for 40 AGeV
- Excluded volume parameter: v₀ = 0.2 fm³
- Transverse radii R_out, R_side at 40 AGeV show a 20% difference from experimental points

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic model previously applied at RHIC energies (Sinyukov et al., PRL, PRC) to lower SPS energies where baryon chemical potential is significant, using a two-phase equation of state based on the SHMC model (Khvorostukhin et al., 2007, 2008) that incorporates a first-order phase transition between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma. The approach enables predictions for future FAIR and NICA experiments at similar energy ranges, and demonstrates that even at 40 AGeV the description of experimental data requires inclusion of quark-gluon degrees of freedom in the initial thermodynamic conditions.
