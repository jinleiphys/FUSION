# Vorticity in heavy-ion collisions at the JINR Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility

> Vorticity of matter generated in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at energies of the Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna is studied. Simulations are performed within the model of the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) which reproduces the major part of bulk observables at these energies. Comparison with earlier calculations is done. Qualitative pattern of the vorticity evolution is analyzed. It is demonstrated that the vorticity is mainly located at the border between participants and spectators. In particular, this implies that the relative $Λ$-hyperon polarization should be stronger at rapidities of the fragmentation regions than that in the midrapidity region.

# Vorticity in heavy-ion collisions at the JINR Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility

## Key claim

The vorticity generated in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies is primarily located at the border between participant and spectator matter, not in the bulk of the participant region; this implies that relative Λ-hyperon polarization should be stronger at rapidities of the fragmentation regions than in the midrapidity region, contrary to what might be expected from volume-averaged results.

## Method

The authors performed simulations within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball fluids separately with friction terms coupling them, using a crossover equation of state (EoS) that was previously found most successful in reproducing bulk observables. They computed both the relativistic kinematic vorticity (ωμν) and the thermal vorticity (ϖμν) for the baryon-rich subsystem in semi-central (b = 6 fm) Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3.3, 4.9, and 7.7 GeV, with proper-energy-density weighting and temperature constraints to isolate regions relevant for particle production.

## Key numbers

- Total angular momentum is conserved with accuracy of 1.5% in the numerical scheme for semi-central Au+Au at √sNN = 7.7 GeV.
- The angular momentum of the fireball fluid is almost two orders of magnitude lower than that of the overlapped baryon-rich fluids at √sNN = 7.7 GeV.
- Baryon-rich fluids become mutually stopped and unified at t ≳ 4 fm/c for √sNN = 4.9 GeV and at t ≳ 2 fm/c for √sNN = 7.7 GeV.
- At the initial nonequilibrium stage (t ≲ 4 fm/c for 4.9 GeV and t ≲ 2 fm/c for 7.7 GeV) the temperature concept is inapplicable, making thermal vorticity poorly defined.
- Mean weighted kinematic vorticity at the final stage is compatible with values at √sNN = 5 GeV from Teryaev (2015) within the hadron-string dynamics model.
- Mean weighted thermal vorticity at √sNN = 7.7 GeV is an order of magnitude higher than values reported by Csernai (2014) for √sNN = 8 GeV; applying a T > 150 MeV constraint reduces these values by an order of magnitude, making them comparable.

## Context

This work extends prior vorticity studies at NICA energies by Csernai (2014) using the relativistic PICR hydro approach and by Teryaev (2015) using the hadron-string dynamics model, which both presented volume-averaged results that showed vorticity occupying the bulk of participants. The present 3FD simulations reveal that this bulk occupation was an artifact of averaging over all y-slices, and that the true vorticity is concentrated at the participant-spectator border, consistent with the "femto-vortex sheet" structure observed by Baznat (2015, 2013) in the kinetic Quark-Gluon String Model. The results enable predictions for Λ-hyperon polarization measurements at NICA and the STAR Fixed-Target Program at RHIC, and suggest that temperature gradients at the participant-spectator border are much stronger in the 3FD model than in the PICR hydro approach.
