# Systematic study of $α$-decay half-lives of super-heavy nuclei with 106$\leq$Z$\leq$118

> The $α$- decay half-lives of the superheavy nuclei are systematically studied using different versions of proximity potential and a exact method to calculate Coulomb potential between spherical and deformed nuclei in the framework of the double folding model. To reproduce the $α$-decay half-life, the experimental $α$-decay energy and Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin approximation have been used. It is found that the computed values by the Ng$\hat{o}$ 80 are in good compromise with the experimental half-lives in comparison with other versions. Also, by using this version and within $Q_{WS4}$ for determination $α$-decay energies for superheavy elements, we had predicted the $α$-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei which have not been reported yet. The long half-lives with magnitude about $100$ seconds are predicted for the superheavy nuclei which are not in stability islands which indicating remarkable stability in comparison with their neighbors. These results are also in good agreement with the predictions of other semi-empirical formulas.

# Systematic study of α-decay half-lives of super-heavy nuclei with 106≤Z≤118

## Key claim

The Ngô 80 version of the proximity potential, combined with experimental α-decay energies and the WKB approximation, reproduces experimental half-lives for superheavy nuclei (106≤Z≤118) better than 27 other proximity potential versions tested. The authors predict that some unknown superheavy nuclei outside the stability islands may have half-lives of about 100 seconds, indicating "remarkable stability" relative to their neighbors, though these predictions depend on using Q-values from the WS4 mass model rather than experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated α-decay half-lives for 70 superheavy nuclei using 28 different versions of the proximity potential for the nuclear interaction, combined with a double folding model for the Coulomb potential that accounts for spherical-deformed nuclear pairs. The WKB approximation was used to compute penetration probabilities, with experimental Qα values for known nuclei and QWS4 predictions for unknown nuclei. The assault frequency was derived from the global quantum number formalism, and penetrability was averaged over all orientation angles of the deformed daughter nucleus. Standard deviations between calculated and experimental half-lives were used to select the best proximity potential version.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of proximity potentials for α-decay, including the work of Yao (2015) who tested 14 proximity potentials for even-even nuclei, and Ghodsi (2016) who tested 28 proximity potentials for 344 nuclei with 52≤Z≤107. The present study focuses specifically on the superheavy region (Z=106-118) and incorporates deformed Coulomb potentials, which earlier studies did not always include. The predictions for unknown nuclei using QWS4 values enable comparison with semi-empirical formulas such as the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski relationship, the Royer analytical formula, and the Universal Decay Law of Qi (2009). The results suggest that deformed proximity formalisms may further improve accuracy in future work.
