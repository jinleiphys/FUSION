# Self-consistent calculations of quadrupole moments of spherical nuclei

> The self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. with the set DF3-a of parameters fixed previously is used to calculate three kinds of quadrupole moments. At first, we examined systematically quadrupole moments of odd neighbors of semi-magic lead and tin isotopes and $N=50,N=82$ isotones. Second, we found quadrupole moments of the first $2^+$ states in the same two chains of isotopes. Finally, we evaluated quadrupole moments of odd-odd nuclei neighboring to double magic ones. Reasonable agreement with available experimental data has been obtained. Predictions are made for quadrupole moments of nuclei in the vicinity of unstable magic nuclei

## Key claim

The self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Fayans Energy Density Functional with the DF3-a parameter set achieves reasonable agreement with experimental quadrupole moments for odd nuclei near magic cores, but the agreement is significantly worse for high-j intruder states and for proton-rich nuclei, where the theoretical quadrupole field is too strong.

## Method

The authors solved the TFFS equations in a self-consistent basis obtained from the Energy Density Functional method with the DF3-a functional, using surface pairing. For odd nuclei, the quadrupole moment was calculated from the diagonal matrix element of the effective field in the static external field, including the Bogolyubov factor (u^2 - v^2) which depends on the single-particle energy relative to the chemical potential. For the first 2+ states in even-even nuclei, the calculation included phonon coupling (PC) effects beyond the QRPA, specifically all g^2 terms including tadpole contributions and ground state correlations (GSC). For odd-odd near-magic nuclei, the interaction between the two odd particles was neglected, reducing the problem to calculations of quadrupole moments of the corresponding odd nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 42 quadrupole moments of odd-neutron nuclei, the root-mean-square deviation between theory and experiment is 0.189 e b; for 15 intruder states it is 0.269 e b, while for 27 normal states it is 0.125 e b
- For odd-proton nuclei, the rms deviation is 0.254 e b
- For odd-neutron neighbors of even N=50 isotones, the rms deviation is 0.041 e b; for N=82 isotones it is 0.093 e b
- For 209Bi with a double-magic core, the effective proton charge is e^p_eff = 1.4; for 209Pb the effective neutron charge is e^n_eff = 0.9
- In nuclei with unfilled neutron shells, effective charges become much larger, e_eff approximately 3 to 6
- For the first 2+ state in 112Sn, the theoretical quadrupole moment is -0.45 e b compared to experimental -0.03(11) e b; for 208Pb the theoretical value is 0.05 e b compared to experimental -0.7(3) e b
- The contribution of the ground state correlations (GSC) term to the 2+ quadrupole moments is often more than 50-60% of all triangle contributions
- For the odd-odd nucleus 210Bi, the theoretical quadrupole moment is +0.16 e b compared to experimental +0.136(1) e b; for 208Bi the theoretical value is -0.35 e b compared to experimental -0.64(6) e b

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously applied to quadrupole moments of odd semi-magic nuclei (Kamerdzhiev et al., 2012) and to quadrupole moments of the first 2+ states in even lead and tin isotopes (Voitenkov et al., 2012). The results differ significantly from standard QRPA calculations, which yield considerably smaller Q values for the 2+ states. The approach enables predictions for quadrupole moments of unstable nuclei near magic cores, including neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei where experimental data are scarce, and provides a framework for including phonon coupling effects beyond the mean-field approximation.
