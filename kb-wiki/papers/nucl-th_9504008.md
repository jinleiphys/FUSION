# SPA+RPA approach to canonical and grandcanonical treatments of nuclear level densities

> Using an exactly solvable pairing model Hamiltonian in the static path approximation together with small-amplitude quantal fluctuation corrections in random phase approximation (SPA+RPA), we have analyzed the behaviour of canonical (number projected) and grandcanonical treatments of nuclear level densities as a function of temperature and number of particles. For small particle numbers at a low temperature, we find that though the grandcanonical partition function in SPA+RPA approach is quite close to its exact value, the small errors in its estimation causes significant suppression of level density obtained using number projected partition function. The results are also compared with the smoothed out exact values of level density. Within this model study, it appears that due to saddle point approximation to multiple Laplace-back transform, the grandcanonical treatment of level density at low temperature may be reliable only for relatively large number of particles.

## Key claim

Within an exactly solvable pairing model, the grandcanonical treatment of nuclear level densities at low temperature may be reliable only for relatively large numbers of particles, because small errors in the SPA+RPA grandcanonical partition function cause significant suppression of the level density obtained via number projection for small particle numbers.

## Method

The authors used an exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonian in the quasi-spin formalism and constructed the grandcanonical partition function via the static path approximation (SPA) plus random phase approximation (RPA) corrections. They then obtained the canonical (number-projected) partition function by Fourier inversion of the grandcanonical partition function. Level densities in saddle-point approximation were computed from both canonical and grandcanonical partition functions, and compared to the exact level density and its smoothed version for systems with N=5 and N=10 particles.

## Key numbers

- For N=5 at T=0.35 MeV, the ratio of exact canonical to exact grandcanonical level density (ρ̂^c_ex/ρ̂^g_ex) equals 20.83.
- For N=10 at T=0.35 MeV, the ratio of exact canonical to exact grandcanonical level density (ρ̂^c_ex/ρ̂^g_ex) equals 0.94.
- For T > 0.45 MeV, the ratio ρ̂^c_ex/ρ̂^g_ex becomes nearly equal to one for both N=5 and N=10.
- The saddle-point approximation is not applicable at temperatures below T=0.25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SPA+RPA approach previously applied to grandcanonical partition functions for pairing and Lipkin model Hamiltonians (Puddu, Broglia, Quick, various years) by analyzing the canonical (number-projected) treatment of level densities. It directly addresses the violation of the "microcanonical analyticity constraint" noted by Gross (1993) in Bethe's formula, and shows that the saddle-point approximation to multiple Laplace-back transforms makes grandcanonical treatments unreliable at low temperature for small particle numbers. The results enable more careful application of SPA+RPA methods to realistic nuclear level density calculations, particularly for nuclei with small particle numbers.
