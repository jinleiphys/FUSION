# Anomalous Pseudoscalar-Photon Vertex In and Out of Equilibrium

> The anomalous pseudoscalar-photon vertex is studied in real time in and out of equilibrium in a constituent quark model. The goal is to understand the in-medium modifications of this vertex, exploring the possibility of enhanced isospin breaking by electromagnetic effects as well as the formation of neutral pion condensates in a rapid chiral phase transition in peripheral, ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. In equilibrium the effective vertex is afflicted by infrared and collinear singularities that require hard thermal loop (HTL) and width corrections of the quark propagator. The resummed effective equilibrium vertex vanishes near the chiral transition in the chiral limit. In a strongly out of equilibrium chiral phase transition we find that the chiral condensate drastically modifies the quark propagators and the effective vertex. The ensuing dynamics for the neutral pion results in a potential enhancement of isospin breaking and the formation of $π^0$ condensates. While the anomaly equation and the axial Ward identity are not modified by the medium in or out of equilibrium, the effective real-time pseudoscalar-photon vertex is sensitive to low energy physics.

## Key claim

The effective real-time pseudoscalar-photon vertex is sensitive to low-energy physics and is not fully determined by the anomaly equation or axial Ward identity, which remain unmodified by the medium; in equilibrium near the chiral transition the resummed vertex vanishes in the chiral limit, while in a strongly out-of-equilibrium chiral phase transition the chiral condensate drastically modifies the quark propagators and the effective vertex, potentially enhancing isospin breaking and enabling neutral pion condensate formation.

## Method

The authors used the real-time closed time path (CTP) formalism of Keldysh and Schwinger within a Gell-Mann–Lévy type linear sigma model coupled to constituent quarks and photons, integrating out the fermions to one-loop order to obtain the effective action for the pseudoscalar-photon vertex. In equilibrium, they computed the triangle diagram with bare and resummed fermion propagators, incorporating hard thermal loop (HTL) and width corrections to handle infrared and pinch singularities. For the out-of-equilibrium case, they considered a quenched chiral phase transition where the chiral order parameter rolls toward its minimum, obtaining time-dependent quark propagators in a mean-field approximation and deriving the effective vertex in the limit of quasistatic classical electromagnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark mass ≈ 300 MeV ≈ 2g f_π with f_π = 93 MeV, giving g ≈ 1.5 > e ≈ 0.3.
- The η meson lifetime ≈ 1.2 keV, with η → γγ occurring at a 40% branching ratio.
- The fireball lifetime in heavy-ion collisions ≈ 50 fm/c.
- For peripheral collisions, the semiclassical contribution to F_μν\tilde{F}^μν ∝ \vec{E}·\vec{B} ∝ Z^2 e^2 b/R^6 with b the impact parameter.
- The condition T >> g σ_0(T) is satisfied near T_c, where σ_0(T) vanishes as σ_0(0) √(1 − T/T_c) for T → T_c^− in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature analysis of the anomalous pseudoscalar-photon vertex by Pisarski (1996), Pisarski, Trueman, and Tytgat (1997), and Gelis (1997), who studied the vertex in equilibrium using Euclidean and imaginary-time formulations, finding that the π^0 → γγ amplitude vanishes near the chiral transition. The authors apply the real-time CTP formalism to obtain both equilibrium and nonequilibrium results, enabling the construction of real-time equations of motion for the neutral pion condensate. This connects to proposals by Asakawa and Müller (1998) regarding DCC formation via anomalous electromagnetic interactions in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, and to earlier work on disoriented chiral condensates by many authors. The study enables quantitative assessment of in-medium modifications to the anomalous vertex for phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical contexts.
