# Nucleon Sigma Terms with $N_f = 2 + 1$ O($a$)-improved Wilson fermions

> We present a lattice-QCD based analysis of the nucleon sigma terms using gauge ensembles with $N_f = 2 + 1$ flavors of ${\cal O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions, with a complete error budget concerning excited-state contaminations, the chiral interpolation as well as finite-size and lattice spacing effects. We compute the sigma terms determined directly from the matrix elements of the scalar currents. The chiral interpolation is based on SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory using the extended on-mass shell renormalization scheme. For the pion nucleon sigma term, we obtain $σ_{πN} = (43.7\pm3.6)$ MeV, where the error includes our estimate of the aforementioned systematics. The tension with extractions based on dispersion theory persists at the 2.4-$σ$ level. For the strange sigma term, we obtain a non-zero value, $σ_s=(28.6\pm9.3)$ MeV.

## Key claim
The authors obtain a pion-nucleon sigma term of σπN = (43.7 ± 3.6) MeV from a direct lattice-QCD calculation of the scalar current matrix element, which remains in tension with dispersion-theory extractions at the 2.4-σ level even after including a complete systematic error budget for excited-state contamination, chiral interpolation, finite-size effects, and lattice-spacing artifacts.

## Method
The authors compute nucleon scalar matrix elements directly from connected and disconnected three-point correlation functions using Nf = 2+1 flavors of O(a)-improved Wilson fermions on CLS ensembles with lattice spacings from 0.050 fm to 0.086 fm and pion masses from 128 MeV to 352 MeV. Ground-state matrix elements are extracted using three methods: a window-averaged summation method, two-state fits to the summed correlator, and two-state fits to the effective form factor, with the latter two employing priors for the energy gap Δ. The chiral extrapolation to the physical point uses SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory in the extended on-mass shell renormalization scheme, with simultaneous continuum and finite-volume corrections, and final results are obtained by AIC-weighted model averaging across analysis variations.

## Key numbers
- σπN = 43.7(1.2)(3.4) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic
- σs = 28.6(6.2)(7.0) MeV, a non-zero value for the strange sigma term
- σ0 = 41.3(1.2)(3.4) MeV, the combination σπN − (2ml/ms)σs
- The tension with the dispersive result of Hoferichter (2015), after isospin-limit correction to 55.9(3.5) MeV, persists at the 2.4-σ level
- Lattice spacings used range from 0.050 fm to 0.086 fm, with pion masses from 128 MeV to 352 MeV and MπL values from 3.00 to 5.83

## Context
This work extends the direct lattice determination of nucleon sigma terms using CLS ensembles, complementing the approach of RQCD (2022) which extracted σπN from the quark-mass dependence of the nucleon mass on partially overlapping ensembles. The results confirm the lower σπN value (~45 MeV) from earlier lattice calculations by Durr (2011), Bali (2012), and others, in contrast to the larger value (59.1 ± 3.5 MeV) from dispersive analyses by Hoferichter (2015). The inclusion of explicit two-state fits with priors on the energy gap, similar to the approach of Gupta (2021), shows an upward trend for σπN but not as pronounced, and the authors identify excited-state treatment as the dominant systematic uncertainty.
