# Equation of state of magnetar crusts from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov atomic mass models

> The equation of state (EoS) of the outer crust of a cold non-accreting magnetar has been determined using the model of Lai and Shapiro (1991). For this purpose, we have made use of the latest experimental atomic mass data complemented with a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass model. Magnetar crusts are found to be significantly different from the crusts of ordinary neutron stars.

## Key claim

The outer crust equation of state of a cold non-accreting magnetar is significantly different from that of ordinary neutron stars, but only when the magnetic field strength exceeds the critical value \(B_c \simeq 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G; the effects become less important at higher densities as more Landau levels are populated.

## Method

The authors applied the Lai and Shapiro (1991) model for the structure of the outer crust of a cold non-accreting magnetar, using the most recent experimental atomic mass data from a preliminary unpublished update of the Atomic Mass Evaluation. For masses not yet measured experimentally, they employed the microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass model HFB-21 of Goriely (2010). The equilibrium composition was determined by accounting for the quantization of electron motion into Landau levels in the strong magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field strength is \(B_c \equiv m_e^2 c^3/(e\hbar) \simeq 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G.
- The nucleus \(^{66}\)Ni, present in the outer crust of ordinary neutron stars at \(B=0\), disappears for \(B > 67 B_c\).
- The nucleus \(^{88}\)Sr appears only in magnetar crusts for \(B > 859 B_c\).
- The pressure at the neutron drip transition increases from \(7.82 \times 10^{29}\) dyn cm\(^{-2}\) for \(B=0\) to \(1.05 \times 10^{30}\) dyn cm\(^{-2}\) for \(B = 1000 B_c\).
- A surface magnetic field of about \(2.4 \times 10^{15}\) G has been inferred in SGR 1806-20, with internal fields possibly as high as \(10^{18}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Lai and Shapiro (1991) for magnetar crusts by incorporating the latest experimental atomic masses and the HFB-21 mass model of Goriely (2010). It contrasts with the zero-field equation of state for ordinary neutron stars computed by Pearson (2011), showing that strong magnetic fields suppress neutron drip and alter the crustal composition. The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar crust properties, which may have implications for the interpretation of pulsar glitches.
