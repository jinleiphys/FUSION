# Self-consistent solution of Galitskii-Feynman equations at finite temperature

> We solve the in-medium T-matrix equation at finite temperature including the off-shell propagation of nucleons. In this way a self-consistent spectral function for the nucleons is obtained. The results are compared to a calculation using the quasiparticle approximation in the T-matrix equation. Also the effective in-medium cross sections for the two cases are compared.

# Self-consistent solution of Galitskii-Feynman equations at finite temperature

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully self-consistent solution of the in-medium T-matrix equation with off-shell nucleon propagators yields spectral functions and self-energies that are qualitatively and quantitatively different from the quasiparticle approximation, but this conclusion is limited to finite temperatures above the pairing instability (T ≥ 8 MeV) and uses a simple separable Yamaguchi potential rather than a realistic nuclear interaction.

## Method

The author solves the coupled set of Galitskii-Feynman equations (T-matrix equation, self-energy equation, and Dyson equation for the spectral function) by iteration at finite temperature using the real-time Green's functions formalism. The in-medium T-matrix is calculated with full off-shell two-particle propagators that include the spectral functions of the intermediate nucleons, using a separable rank-one Yamaguchi potential in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ partial waves. The quasiparticle approximation is implemented for comparison by replacing the off-shell propagators with on-shell propagators using a delta-function spectral function with Z=1.

## Key numbers

- The calculation uses a Yamaguchi potential with form factor parameter γ = 285.9 MeV and a momentum cutoff at |p| < 1200 MeV with grid spacing of approximately 10 MeV.
- The lowest temperature at which stable self-consistent iterations could be obtained is 8 MeV, which is above the critical temperature for the pairing transition (approximately 4 MeV in the quasiparticle approximation with the same interaction).
- The energy integrals were performed in the range |ω| < 2400 MeV with a smallest grid spacing of 1.75 MeV at low temperature.
- The Fermi energy at T = 10 MeV is approximately μ ≈ -21 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy from the cutoff-modified free scattering is 2.1 MeV.
- Typically around 10 iterations are needed for the self-energy to become stable.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle T-matrix calculations of Alm et al. (1996) and Vonderfecht et al. (1993) by including off-shell nucleon propagation in the ladder diagrams, addressing a need recognized but not previously implemented except for restricted kinematical conditions. The self-consistent spectral functions obtained are relevant for electron scattering on nuclei, subthreshold particle production, and in-medium effective cross sections for heavy-ion collision modeling. The method could also be applied to Brueckner-type calculations at low temperatures, though an explicit method for energy integration near the Fermi energy would need to be implemented.
