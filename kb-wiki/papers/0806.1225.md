# Influence of quenched jets on di-hadron correlations

> A simple jet absorption model is used to study the influence of hadron pairs produced by quenched jets, on di-hadron angular correlations at intermediate transverse momentum ($p_T$). We demonstrate that such pairs can dominate both the yield and the shape of angular correlations, and may account for the similar properties between the near-side "ridge" and the away-side "double-humped" structure seen in recent data. These hadron pairs also show azimuthal anisotropy which is sensitive to the emission angle of hadrons relative to that of the jet. Measurement of this anisotropy may provide a constraint for elucidating the production mechanism for near- and away-side hadron pairs at intermediate $p_T$.

## Key claim

A simple jet absorption model shows that hadron pairs produced by quenched jets (medium-medium pairs) can dominate both the yield and shape of di-hadron angular correlations at intermediate transverse momentum, and may account for the similar properties between the near-side ridge and the away-side double-humped structure seen in recent data, provided that the average multiplicity of medium response hadrons exceeds that of fragmentation hadrons.

## Method

The authors use a Monte Carlo Glauber simulation of Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV to generate back-to-back dijet pairs, then propagate them through a medium whose density is given by the participant density profile. Jet survival probability follows an exponential absorption model with a single absorption strength parameter (kappa = 0.7) tuned to reproduce the observed suppression factor of 4.4 for hadrons in 0-5% central collisions. Fragmentation hadrons are assigned Gaussian widths around the jet direction, while medium response hadrons from quenched jets are distributed at +/-1.1 radians relative to the original jet direction with a Gaussian width of 0.3 radians.

## Key numbers

- Absorption strength kappa = 0.7, tuned to reproduce the observed constant suppression factor of 4.4 for hadrons in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Mean multiplicity for fragmentation hadrons per survived jet: <N_jet> = 1; mean multiplicity for medium response hadrons per quenched jet: <N_med> = 2
- Medium response hadrons emitted at D = 1.1 radians relative to the original jet direction, with Gaussian width of 0.3 radians
- Near-side jet width sigma_near = 0.2; k_T smearing sigma_kT = 0.4, matching measured widths for 1-3 GeV/c range in p+p and d+Au collisions
- Only 30% of inter-jet pairs are assumed detected, accounting for finite detector acceptance in pseudorapidity

## Context

This work extends the jet absorption model of Drees, Feng, and Jia (2005) to include medium-medium pairs, which were neglected in previous correlation analyses that considered only jet-jet and jet-medium pairs. The finding that medium-medium pairs can serve as a common source for both the near-side ridge and away-side cone is consistent with experimental data from PHENIX (Adare et al., 2008) and STAR (Adams et al., 2007) showing similar particle composition and spectral slopes between the ridge and cone. The results suggest that measurement of the reaction plane dependence of hadron pair yield (rather than per-trigger yield) could help constrain the underlying mechanisms of medium response to jets.
