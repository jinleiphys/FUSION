# Hadronization Approach for a Quark-Gluon Plasma Formed in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> A transport model is developed to describe hadron emission from a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The quark-gluon plasma is controlled by ideal hydrodynamics, and the hadron motion is characterized by a transport equation with loss and gain terms. The two sets of equations are coupled to each other, and the hadronization hypersurface is determined by both the hydrodynamic evolution and the hadron emission. The model is applied to calculate the transverse momentum distributions of mesons and baryons, and most of the results agree well with the experimental data at RHIC.

# Hadronization Approach for a Quark-Gluon Plasma Formed in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The authors develop a coupled transport-hydrodynamic model for hadron emission from a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) where the hadronization hypersurface is determined self-consistently by both the hydrodynamic evolution and the hadron evaporation, rather than prescribed a priori. The model reproduces most RHIC data for soft hadron transverse momentum spectra, but the agreement is limited to low-to-moderate transverse momentum (below about 4 GeV) and the authors note that high-pT particles require jet and fragmentation mechanisms not included in this approach.

## Method
The QGP evolution is described by ideal hydrodynamics with an equation of state that includes quarks, gluons, and their resonant states (diquarks and qg states). Hadron motion is governed by a classical transport equation with loss and gain terms representing absorption and production in the QGP, solved analytically under a quasi-static, one-dimensional approximation with a relaxation-time treatment of elastic scattering. The hadronization hypersurface is determined by coupling the hydrodynamic equations to the energy and momentum loss from hadron emission, with the fluid splitting into droplets at the critical temperature and hadronization beginning when the energy density reaches a free parameter epsilon_b.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_c = 166 MeV, corresponding to critical energy density epsilon_c = 1.91 GeV/fm^3 (1.24 times the Stefan-Boltzmann limit)
- Hadronization energy density epsilon_b = 0.21 GeV/fm^3, giving ratio eta = epsilon_b/epsilon_c = 0.11
- Chemical potential at hadronization mu_B = 27 MeV
- Average meson-quark inelastic cross section <sigma_m> = 7 mb
- Baryon cross section taken as 1.5 times meson cross section: <sigma_b> = 1.5 <sigma_m>
- Volume effect parameter y_o = 0.83
- Meson-quark coupling constant g_M^2 = 5.11, baryon-quark coupling constant g_B^2 = 34.24
- Evolution duration of QGP fluid from hydrodynamics: about 8.4 fm/c
- Formation time of thermalized fluid tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Strange quark mass m_s = 95 MeV; diquark masses: m_qq = 485 MeV (0+ channel), m_qq = 725 MeV (1+ channel), m_qg = 610 MeV (1/2- channel)
- Statistical error for dN/dy from Monte Carlo simulation: less than 0.5%-1%; for pT distributions in region 0.3-4 GeV: less than 5% for most hadrons

## Context
This work extends earlier evaporation models (referenced as "Evaporation models" developed many years ago) by coupling the hadron emission self-consistently to the hydrodynamic evolution of the QGP, rather than assuming a fixed critical temperature hypersurface or a simple thermal distribution for emitted hadrons. It incorporates the strongly coupled nature of the QGP formed at RHIC by including diquark and qg resonant states in the equation of state, building on the diquark baryon mechanism of Gao and Zhuang (2005) and the volume effect treatment of Pratt and others. The model enables systematic calculation of soft hadron spectra across many hadron species (mesons and baryons listed in Tables 1 and 2) from a single coupled framework, though the authors note that high-pT particles require jet quenching and fragmentation mechanisms (such as those in the work of Wang and others) not included here, and that the overestimation of kaons may be resolved by including hadron rescattering channels like N + anti-K -> Sigma(Lambda) + pi.
