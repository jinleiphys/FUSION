# Finite temperature QCD phase transition with 3 flavors of Möbius domain wall fermions

> We investigate the finite temperature QCD phase transition with three degenerate quark flavors using Möbius domain wall fermions. To explore the order of phase transition on the lower left corner of Columbia plot and if possible, to locate the critical endpoint we performed simulations at temperatures around 181 and 121 MeV with lattice spacing $a=0.1361(20)$ fm corresponding to temporal lattice extent $N_τ=8,12$ with varying quark mass for two different volumes with aspect ratios $N_σ/N_τ$ ranging from 2 to 3. By analyzing the volume and mass dependence of the chiral condensate, disconnected chiral susceptibility and Binder cumulant we find that there is a crossover at $m_q^{\mathrm{\overline {MS}}}(2\, \mathrm{GeV}) \sim 44\, \mathrm{MeV}$ for $\mathrm{T_{pc}}\sim$ 181 MeV, At temperature 121 MeV, the binder cumulant suggests a crossover at $m_q^{\mathrm{\overline {MS}}}(2\, \mathrm{GeV}) \sim 3.7\, \mathrm{MeV}$, although a study of volume dependence would be important to confirm this.

## Key claim

The authors find that for three-flavor QCD with Möbius domain wall fermions at a lattice spacing of 0.1361(20) fm, the phase transition is a crossover at a quark mass of approximately 44 MeV for a temperature of 181 MeV; at the lower temperature of 121 MeV, the Binder cumulant suggests a crossover at a quark mass of approximately 3.7 MeV, but the authors caution that a study of volume dependence is needed to confirm this result.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three degenerate quark flavors using the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action and Möbius domain wall fermion action at fixed gauge coupling β=4.0, corresponding to lattice spacing a=0.1361(20) fm. They generated ensembles on 16³×8 and 24³×8 lattices (T≈181 MeV) and 24³×12 lattices (T≈121 MeV) with a range of quark masses, and analyzed the volume and mass dependence of the chiral condensate, disconnected chiral susceptibility, and Binder cumulant to determine the order of the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1361(20) fm, determined from the Wilson flow t₀
- Residual mass: a m_res = 0.00613(9), determined by extrapolating to zero input quark mass
- At T ≈ 181 MeV (N_τ=8), the disconnected chiral susceptibility shows a peak at m_q^{MS-bar}(2 GeV) ∼ 44 MeV, indicating a crossover
- At T ≈ 121 MeV (N_τ=12), the disconnected chiral susceptibility shows a peak at m_q^{MS-bar}(2 GeV) ∼ 3.7 MeV
- The mass renormalization constant Z_m^{MS-bar}(2 GeV) = 0.834348 for β=4.0

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the N_f=3 QCD phase transition using staggered and Wilson fermion formulations, which have shown conflicting results regarding the order of the transition in the chiral limit. Prior work with improved staggered (HISQ) action by the HotQCD collaboration (2022) found a second-order transition in the N_f=3 chiral limit, while O(a)-improved Wilson fermion studies (2020) found an upper bound on the critical pion mass. The present study is the first to use a chiral fermion formulation (Möbius domain wall fermions) for this problem, which preserves chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacing. The results are consistent with the trend that the critical mass becomes smaller as lattice spacing decreases, and the authors plan to investigate larger volumes (36³×12) and larger fifth dimension (L_s=32) to confirm the nature of the transition at the lower temperature.
