# Detailed study of decay properties of 7He nucleus states in ab initio approach

> The energies and decay widths of the states of the exotic 7He nucleus are studied in an ab initio approach. The spectrum of these states is calculated using No-Core Shell Model and corresponding extrapolation procedure. Well-proven on a large amount of data the Daejeon16 potential is used in the calculations. The previously developed NCSM-based approach, which includes a method for constructing the basis of orthogonalized functions of cluster channels and a procedure for matching the cluster form factors obtained within this method with the corresponding asymptotic wave functions, is applied to compute the decay widths of the levels. The possibilities of the approach for calculating the partial decay widths of nuclear states into various channels strongly differing in type of fragmentation, spin, angular momentum of relative motion, and amplitude are demonstrated. The results obtained are compared with the results of other microscopic calculations.

## Key claim
The No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) combined with the Cluster Channel Orthogonalized Functions Method (CCOFM) and an extrapolation procedure can reproduce the energies and decay widths of 7He resonance states, but the accuracy depends on using extrapolated rather than limited-basis energies, and the method assumes a matching procedure that works best for narrow resonances where the penetrability of the potential barrier is small.

## Method
The authors performed NCSM calculations using the Daejeon16 NN-potential with a basis limited by cut-off parameter N*max_tot=13 (Nmax_tot=16) and oscillator parameter ħω=12.5 MeV, then applied a five-parameter "Extrapolation A5" procedure to obtain total binding energies in an infinite basis. Cluster form factors (CFFs) were constructed via the CCOFM by projecting NCSM eigenfunctions onto orthogonalized cluster-channel basis functions, and these CFFs were matched to asymptotic Coulomb wave functions (irregular solutions for narrow resonances, or R-matrix theory for wide resonances) to compute partial decay widths.

## Key numbers
- Ground state of 6He: extrapolated total binding energy 29.397 MeV, deviating from experimental 29.269 MeV by 128 keV (0.4%)
- 3/2−1 state of 7He: extrapolated resonance energy 547 keV (experimental 430 keV); calculated total width 334 keV (experimental 182 keV)
- 5/2−1 state of 7He: extrapolated resonance energy 3437 keV (experimental 3360 keV); calculated total width 1941 keV (experimental 1990 keV)
- 1/2−1 state of 7He: extrapolated resonance energy 2318 keV; total width 1850 keV (experimental 750 keV, with other results 2.0 MeV and 10.0 MeV)
- 3/2+1 state of 7He: extrapolated resonance energy 3492 keV; total width 2591 keV (sum of partial widths to 0+1 and 2+1 channels)
- 5/2+1 state of 7He: extrapolated resonance energy 3564 keV; total width 2251 keV (sum of partial widths to 0+1, 2+1, and 2+2 channels)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NCSM-based approach applied to 7Li and 8Be (Rodkin and Tchuvil'sky, 2020) to the unbound 7He nucleus, and compares results with other ab initio methods: NCSMC (Navratil et al., 2016), GSM (Papadimitriou et al., 2013), and SS-HORSE (Shirokov et al., 2016). The use of the Daejeon16 potential (based on chiral EFT softened by SRG) and the extrapolation procedure enables calculation of partial decay widths into multiple channels (including 6He in 2+1 and 2+2 excited states), which is difficult for methods that only compute total widths. The approach enables future studies of other exotic neutron-rich nuclei where experimental data are incomplete or unreliable.
