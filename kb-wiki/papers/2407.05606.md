# Surface and curvature tensions of cold dense quark matter: a term-by-term analysis within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> In this paper, we conduct a thorough investigation of the surface and curvature tensions, $σ$ and $γ$, of three-flavor cold quark matter using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector interactions. Our approach ensures both local and global electric charge neutrality, as well as chemical equilibrium under weak interactions. By employing the multiple reflection expansion formalism to account for finite size effects, we explore the impact of specific input parameters, particularly the vector coupling constant ratio $η_V$, the radius $R$ of quark matter droplets, as well as charge-per-baryon ratio $ξ$ of the finite size configurations. We focus on the role of the contributions of each term of the NJL Lagrangian to the surface and curvature tensions in the mean field approximation. We find that the total surface tension exhibits two different density regimes: it remains roughly constant at around $100 \, \mathrm{MeV \, fm^{-2}}$ up to approximately $2-4$ times the nuclear saturation density, and beyond this point, it becomes a steeply increasing function of $n_B$. The total surface and curvature tensions are relatively insensitive to variations in $R$ but are affected by changes in $ξ$ and $η_V$. We observe that the largest contribution to $σ$ and $γ$ comes from the regularized divergent term, making these quantities significantly higher than those obtained within the MIT bag model.

## Key claim

The total surface tension of cold dense quark matter within the NJL model exhibits two distinct density regimes: it remains roughly constant at around 100 MeV fm⁻² up to approximately 2-4 times nuclear saturation density, then becomes a steeply increasing function of baryon density beyond that point, with the largest contribution coming from the regularized divergent term rather than from the free Fermi gas term.

## Method

The authors employed the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with SU(3) flavor symmetry and two types of vector interactions (flavor-independent Model 1 and flavor-dependent Model 2) within the mean-field approximation. They applied the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism to account for finite-size effects in spherical quark matter droplets, separating the thermodynamic potential into volume, surface, and curvature contributions for each term of the NJL Lagrangian (divergent, free, condensate, determinant, and vector terms). The calculations enforced both local and global electric charge neutrality, chemical equilibrium under weak interactions, and used the HK parameter set with an ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 631.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total surface tension remains roughly constant at approximately 100 MeV fm⁻² up to about 2-4 times nuclear saturation density (n₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³)
- The first-order phase transition in the bulk case occurs at μq = μc ∼ 350 MeV, where Mu and Md drop sharply from 335 MeV to a significantly lower value
- Infrared cutoff ΛIR values for u,d quarks (mass 5.5 MeV): 51.77 MeV for R = 3 fm, 32.86 MeV for R = 5 fm, 18.34 MeV for R = 10 fm
- Infrared cutoff ΛIR values for s quarks (mass 135.7 MeV): 95.07 MeV for R = 3 fm, 62.15 MeV for R = 5 fm, 33.42 MeV for R = 10 fm
- The charge-per-baryon ratio ξ is explored in the range -0.75 < ξ ≤ 0, with specific calculations at ξ = 0, -0.2, and -0.4
- Vector coupling constant ratio ηV is examined at values of 0, 0.5, and 1 for both Model 1 and Model 2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of surface and curvature tensions in quark matter using the MRE formalism, which had previously been applied to the NJL model (Lugones 2013) and the vector MIT bag model (Lugones 2021). The authors note that earlier thin-wall formalism calculations found low surface tension values (σ < 30 MeV/fm²) across various EoS models including NJL (Garcia 2013, Ke 2013, Pinto 2012), linear sigma model (Palhares 2010, Pinto 2012, Kroff 2014), and Polyakov-quark-meson model (Mintz 2012). The present work provides the first comprehensive term-by-term analysis of how each interaction channel in the NJL Lagrangian contributes to σ and γ, enabling more accurate modeling of the quark-hadron mixed phase (pasta phase) in hybrid neutron stars.
