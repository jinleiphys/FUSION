# Approach to thermalization and hydrodynamics

> I review recent progress in thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, with particular emphasis on hydrodynamic attractor, and also report recent progress in hydrodynamic fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hydrodynamics can be extended far from equilibrium via the concept of a hydrodynamic attractor, which characterizes solutions beyond the regime where gradient expansions work, and that the existence of a free-streaming infrared fixed point is essential to this attractor behavior in weakly coupled plasmas.

## Method

The author reviews the hydrodynamic attractor in the Bjorken expansion using conformal causal hydrodynamics and the relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic theory, analyzing fixed points of logarithmic growth rates of moments of the momentum distribution. The paper also reviews the hydro-kinetic theory for hydrodynamic fluctuations in the kinetic regime, where the wave number balances relaxation and background expansion, and reports on numerical simulations of fluctuating causal hydrodynamics with a modified fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Key numbers

* Pressure anisotropy $P_L/P_T$ is accurately described by hydrodynamic gradient expansion for $P_L/P_T \gtrsim 0.4$ (Heller 2011, Kurkela 2018).
* The hydrodynamic attractors of causal hydrodynamics are within 10% error from that of the RTA kinetic theory.
* In the free streaming limit, the energy density scales as $e(\tau) \propto \tau^{-1}$; in the hydrodynamic scaling, $e(\tau) \propto \tau^{-4/3}$.
* The free-streaming UV fixed point in the 2-moment truncation is at $(g_0, g_1) \approx (-2.21, -2.21)$; the free-streaming IR fixed point at $(g_0, g_1) \approx (-0.93, -0.93)$; the hydrodynamic IR fixed point at $(g_0, g_1) = (-4/3, -2)$.
* At the non-thermal fixed point of QCD effective kinetic theory, scaling exponents approach $\alpha \approx -0.7$, $\beta = 0$, and $\gamma \approx 0.3$ (Baier 2000, Bodeker 2005).
* The noise-averaged longitudinal pressure in the Bjorken expansion includes a term $+ 1.08318 T / (4\pi \gamma_\eta \tau)^{3/2}$ from hydrodynamic fluctuations (Akamatsu 2016).

## Context

This review extends work on the hydrodynamic attractor pioneered by Heller (2011) and Romatschke (2017), and the fixed point analysis builds on the RTA kinetic theory studies of Blaizot (2017, 2019). The paper enables phenomenological applications such as estimating initial energy density from charged particle multiplicity using the attractor (Giacalone 2019), and it points to open questions including the role of plasma instability, three-dimensional expansion, and freezeout fixed points.
