# Equation of state of low--density neutron matter and the $^1S_0$ pairing gap

> We report results of the equation of state of neutron matter in the low--density regime, where the Fermi wave vector ranges from $0.4 fm^{-1} \leq k_F \leq 1.0 fm^{-1}$. Neutron matter in this regime is superfluid because of the strong and attractive interaction in the $^1S_0$ channel. The properties of this superfluid matter are calculated starting from a realistic Hamiltonian that contains modern two-- and three--body interactions. The ground state energy and the $^1S_0$ superfluid energy gap are calculated using the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method. We study the structure of the ground state by looking at pair distribution functions as well as the Cooper-pair wave function used in the calculations.

## Key claim

The superfluid phase of low-density neutron matter is only marginally favored energetically over the normal phase, but the BCS-type trial wave function is necessary to compute the superfluid energy gap. The authors find that the BCS state gives lower energy at the two lowest densities studied (k_F = 0.4 and 0.6 fm^{-1}), while the normal Fermi gas trial function gives lower energy at the two highest densities (k_F = 0.8 and 1.0 fm^{-1}), with differences of at most about 4 percent.

## Method

The authors used the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to solve for the ground state of neutron matter in a periodic box, using a realistic Hamiltonian with the AV8' two-nucleon interaction and the Urbana-IX three-nucleon interaction. They simulated systems of up to 68 neutrons at four densities corresponding to Fermi wave vectors from 0.4 to 1.0 fm^{-1}, using both a normal Fermi gas trial wave function (Slater determinant) and a BCS-type trial wave function written as a pfaffian to incorporate pairing correlations. The superfluid gap was computed from the energy difference between odd and even neutron systems using Eq. (gap).

## Key numbers

- At k_F = 0.4 fm^{-1} (density 0.00216 fm^{-3}), the energy per particle with the BCS trial function is 1.239(2) MeV, compared to 1.289(2) MeV with the normal trial function.
- At k_F = 0.6 fm^{-1} (density 0.00730 fm^{-3}), the energy per particle with the BCS trial function is 2.579(2) MeV, compared to 2.606(4) MeV with the normal trial function.
- At k_F = 0.8 fm^{-1} (density 0.01729 fm^{-3}), the energy per particle with the BCS trial function is 4.305(3) MeV, compared to 4.277(7) MeV with the normal trial function.
- At k_F = 1.0 fm^{-1} (density 0.03377 fm^{-3}), the energy per particle with the BCS trial function is 6.231(3) MeV, compared to 6.197(2) MeV with the normal trial function.
- The superfluid gap at k_F = 0.4 fm^{-1} is 1.5(2) MeV for 66 neutrons and 1.79(6) MeV for 14 neutrons.
- The superfluid gap at k_F = 0.6 fm^{-1} is 2.1(2) MeV for 66 neutrons and 2.59(6) MeV for 14 neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier AFDMC study of neutron matter (Gandolfi 2008) and provides a systematic comparison with several prior calculations. The equation of state results are compared with the diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), who used a simpler Hamiltonian with only the 1S0 channel of AV18, the variational cluster summation of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) using the older Urbana v14 interaction, and the chiral effective field theory lattice results of Epelbaum, Krebs, Lee, and Meissner (2009). The superfluid gap results are compared with a range of many-body calculations including those of Wambach et al. (1993), Chen et al. (1993), Schulze et al. (1996), Schwenk et al. (2003), Cao et al. (2006), Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), and Margueron et al. (2008), showing that microscopic Monte Carlo methods predict a larger gap (maximum about 2.1 MeV) than effective-interaction approaches (maximum about 1 MeV). The work enables more reliable predictions for neutron star crust properties and benchmarks for chiral effective field theory calculations.
