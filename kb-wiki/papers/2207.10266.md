# Triaxiality explored by an odd quasi-particle

> The triaxiality of odd-mass nuclei is investigated by coupling a quasiparticle to an even-even core through the core-quasiparticle coupling model. Both soft and rigid triaxial cores are considered. The "soft core" is described by the collective model with rotation-vibrational motion, while the "rigid core" is described by the triaxial rotor model, which is a limiting case of the collective model with only rotational motion. We show that the presence of the odd quasiparticle modifies the collective quadrupole dynamics of the core to appear more "rigid".

## Key claim

The presence of an odd quasiparticle modifies the collective quadrupole dynamics of an even-even core to make it appear more "rigid" in triaxial shape, even when the core itself is soft, thereby resolving the apparent conflict between evidence for soft triaxial modes in even-even nuclei and rigid triaxial cores in their odd-A neighbors.

## Method

The authors used the core-quasiparticle coupling (CQPC) model to couple a single quasiparticle (either a neutron in the h11/2 shell or a proton in the g9/2 shell) to an even-even core described by two alternative models: a "soft core" using the collective Bohr Hamiltonian with a triaxial Davidson potential, and a "rigid core" using the triaxial rotor model with a moment of inertia that increases with core angular momentum. The collective model parameters for the soft core were determined by fitting to experimental ratios E(2_2+)/E(2_1+), E(4_1+)/E(2_1+), and E(0_2+)/E(2_1+) together with the staggering of the quasi-gamma band, while the rigid core parameters were adjusted separately. The resulting Hamiltonians were diagonalized numerically to obtain excitation energies and transition strengths for odd-mass Pd, Ru, and Rh isotopes in the A≈110 mass region.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle triaxial rotor (QTR) model pioneered by Meyer-ter-Veen (1970) and Toki and Faessler (1976) and the wobbling classification into transverse and longitudinal types introduced by Frauendorf and Dönau (2014). It addresses the apparent contradiction that quasi-gamma bands in even-even nuclei indicate soft triaxiality (as reviewed by the staggering analysis of Zamfir and Casten, 1991) while wobbling and chiral bands in odd-A nuclei are well described by rigid triaxial rotors. The results enable systematic predictions of wobbling modes in odd-mass Pd, Ru, and Rh isotopes around A≈110, with experimental confirmation already reported for 105Pd.
