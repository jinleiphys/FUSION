# Spin polarizabilities of the nucleon

> Spin polarizabilities of the nucleon are discussed in the framework of fixed-t and backward-angle dispersion relations, chiral perturbation theory, and the non-relativistic quark model. Calculations with the dispersion relations generally confirm findings from HBChPT and disagree with a recent experimental result from LEGS for the backward spin polarizability.

## Key claim

The paper argues that theoretical calculations of the nucleon's spin polarizabilities from heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), dispersion relations, and the non-relativistic quark model all agree with each other and disagree with a recent experimental result from LEGS for the backward spin polarizability, suggesting the experimental value may need verification.

## Method

The author compares three theoretical frameworks for computing the four spin polarizabilities (γ_E1, γ_M1, γ_E2, γ_M2) of the nucleon: fixed-t and backward-angle dispersion relations using photoproduction amplitudes from the SAID code, HBChPT at leading non-vanishing order O(p^3) with explicit inclusion of the Δ-isobar, and a non-relativistic quark model (NQM) that evaluates contributions from higher resonances beyond the Δ(1232) using a closure over the 1ℏω shell. The dispersion relations determine three combinations of spin polarizabilities from unsubtracted fixed-t dispersion relations at θ=0°, while the backward spin polarizability γ_π requires a separate backward-angle dispersion relation that includes t-channel exchanges of π^0, η, and η'.

## Key numbers

* The backward spin polarizability of the proton from the LEGS experiment is γ_π = -27.1 ± 3.4 (units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴), while theory anticipates γ_π ≈ -37 to -40 (same units).
* In the chiral limit, the leading-order HBChPT loop contribution gives X = 1.11 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ and the π⁰-exchange contribution gives X_a = +11.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ for the proton.
* The Δ-isobar contribution to γ_M1 is between +2.4 and +4.0 (10⁻⁴ fm⁴), depending on the transition magnetic moment used.
* The π⁰-exchange contribution to γ_π for the proton is γ_π^(π⁰) = -4X_a ≈ -45 (10⁻⁴ fm⁴), while other terms (πN loop, Δ, πΔ loop) sum to approximately +7 to +9 (10⁻⁴ fm⁴).
* In the dispersion theory, the s-channel contribution to γ_π is 7.0 and the t-channel contribution is -46.6 (10⁻⁴ fm⁴), giving a total consistent with HBChPT.

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersion relation calculations by Babu (1998) and Drechsel (1998) and HBChPT calculations by Hemmert (1998), and directly contradicts the experimental result from Tonnison (1998) at LEGS for the backward spin polarizability. The paper adds a non-relativistic quark model argument showing that higher resonances beyond the Δ(1232) have small effects on the spin polarizabilities, supporting the conclusion that these quantities are dominated by low-energy πN production and π⁰-exchange. The results enable future dedicated experiments with polarized beams and targets to test the predicted value of γ_π.
