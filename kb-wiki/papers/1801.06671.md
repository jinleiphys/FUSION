# Total and differential cross sections of $η$-production in proton-deuteron fusion for excess energies between $Q_η=13\;\text{MeV}$ and $Q_η=81\;\text{MeV}$

> New data on both total and differential cross sections of the production of $η$ mesons in proton-deuteron fusion to ${}^3\text{He}\,η$ in the excess energy region $13.6\;\text{MeV}\leq Q_η\leq 80.9\;\text{MeV}$ are presented. These data have been obtained with the WASA-at-COSY detector setup located at the Forschungszentrum Jülich, using a proton beam at 15 different beam momenta between $p_p = 1.60\;\text{GeV}/c$ and $p_p = 1.74\;\text{GeV}/c$. While significant structure of the total cross section is observed in the energy region $20\;\text{MeV}\lesssim Q_η\lesssim 60\;\text{MeV}$, a previously reported sharp variation around $Q_η\approx 50\;\text{MeV}$ cannot be confirmed. Angular distributions show the typical forward-peaking that was reported elsewhere. For the first time, it is possible to study the development of these angular distributions with rising excess energy over a large interval.

## Key claim

The paper reports new total and differential cross sections for the reaction \(pd\rightarrow {}^3\text{He}\,\eta\) at 15 excess energies between \(Q_\eta=13.6\;\text{MeV}\) and \(Q_\eta=80.9\;\text{MeV}\). While significant structure in the total cross section is observed in the region \(20\;\text{MeV}\lesssim Q_\eta\lesssim 60\;\text{MeV}\), a previously reported sharp variation around \(Q_\eta\approx 50\;\text{MeV}\) (Adlarson 2014) cannot be confirmed; the authors show that the earlier variation was caused by an incorrect assumption regarding the differential cross section of single pion production.

## Method

The measurement was performed with the WASA detector at the COSY storage ring in Juelich, using a proton beam at 15 momenta between \(p_p = 1.60\;\text{GeV}/c\) and \(p_p = 1.74\;\text{GeV}/c\) incident on a deuterium pellet target. The reaction was identified via a missing-mass analysis of the final-state momentum of \({}^{3}\text{He}\) nuclei in the center-of-mass frame, with no assumption on the \(\eta\) decay. Differential cross sections were extracted by fitting background-subtracted signal yields in bins of \(\cos\vartheta_{\eta}^*\), corrected for detector acceptance and reconstruction efficiency using an iterative Monte Carlo procedure that accounted for nuclear breakup of \({}^{3}\text{He}\) and interactions with evaporated target gas. Luminosity was determined by normalizing the total cross section at \(Q_\eta=61.7\;\text{MeV}\) to the ANKE value of \(\sigma=(388.1\pm 7.2_{\text{stat.}})\;\text{nb}\) (Rausmann 2009), and relative normalization for the other 14 beam momenta was performed using proton-deuteron elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section at \(Q_\eta=13.6\;\text{MeV}\): \(\sigma = 300.3\;\text{nb}\) with statistical point-to-point uncertainty \(\pm 6.5\;\text{nb}\) and systematic uncertainties \(-14.9\;\text{nb}\) / \(+12.5\;\text{nb}\).
* Total cross section at \(Q_\eta=61.7\;\text{MeV}\): \(\sigma = 388.1\;\text{nb}\) (fixed to ANKE value), with an overall normalization uncertainty of \(16.3\%\).
* Total cross section at \(Q_\eta=80.9\;\text{MeV}\): \(\sigma = 408.7\;\text{nb}\) with statistical point-to-point uncertainty \(\pm 7.9\;\text{nb}\) and systematic uncertainties \(+2.3\;\text{nb}\) / \(-7.4\;\text{nb}\).
* Asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) at \(Q_\eta=13.6\;\text{MeV}\): \(\alpha = 0.64\) with statistical uncertainty \(\pm 0.04\) and systematic uncertainties \(-0.03\) / \(+0.02\).
* Asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) at \(Q_\eta=80.9\;\text{MeV}\): \(\alpha = 0.64\) with statistical uncertainty \(\pm 0.04\) and systematic uncertainties \(-0.03\) / \(+0.02\).
* Beam momenta used: 15 values between \(p_p = 1.60\;\text{GeV}/c\) and \(p_p = 1.74\;\text{GeV}/c\) with a step size of \(10\;\text{MeV}/c\) and momentum spread \(\Delta p/p \approx 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the sparse database for the reaction \(pd\rightarrow {}^3\text{He}\,\eta\) at excess energies above the near-threshold region, where previous measurements by CELSIUS/WASA (Bilger 2002), COSY-11 (Adam 2007), ANKE (Rausmann 2009), and GEM (Betigeri 1999) showed inconsistencies. The new data contradict the sharp cross-section variation reported by Adlarson (2014) and instead confirm the gradual rise and plateau observed by Bilger (2002) and Rausmann (2009), but with much finer energy binning. The differential cross sections, measured over a large energy interval for the first time by a single experiment, enable detailed study of the energy dependence of the angular distributions and the asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\), which is used to search for indications of \(\eta\)-mesic states below threshold (Mersmann 2007, Wilkin 2007). The results provide high-quality input for theoretical models, which currently fail to reproduce both total and differential cross sections away from threshold (Kelkar 2013).
