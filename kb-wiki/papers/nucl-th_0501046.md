# Relativistic Green's function approach to parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering

> A relativistic Green's function approach to parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering is presented. The components of the hadron tensor are expressed in terms of the single particle Green's function, which is expanded in terms of the eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential, in order to account for final state interactions without any loss of flux. Results for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca are presented and discussed. The effect of the strange quark contribution to the nuclear current is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic Green's function approach to parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering that accounts for final state interactions (FSI) without any loss of flux, using a complex optical potential whose imaginary part redistributes strength among reaction channels rather than absorbing it. The authors claim this method treats FSI consistently in inclusive and exclusive reactions, but note that correlations (not included in their model) may affect the longitudinal axial-vector response at low and moderate momentum transfer.

## Method

The hadron tensor components are expressed in terms of the single-particle Green's function, which is expanded in a biorthogonal basis of eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. The relativistic bound state wave functions are obtained as Dirac-Hartree solutions of a relativistic mean field Lagrangian, while the scattering state is calculated using the energy-dependent and A-dependent EDAD1 complex phenomenological optical potential fitted to proton elastic scattering data up to 1040 MeV. The transition matrix elements are computed in the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) using direct Pauli reduction for the scattering state.

- The axial mass M_A = (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV, taken as the weighted average from (quasi)elastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering experiments
- The strange vector form factors use M_V = 0.843 GeV and τ = Q^2/(4M_p^2)
- For 12C at q = 500 MeV/c, ω = 120 MeV, and θ = 30°, the asymmetry reduces up to 40% as ρ^s varies in the range -3 ≤ ρ^s ≤ +3, and changes up to 15% for -1 ≤ μ^s ≤ +1
- The asymmetry ranges from a few × 10^(-6) at forward angle and low momentum transfer to a few × 10^(-5) at backward angle and greater q
- The integral term in the hadron tensor expression gives a 10-15% reduction of the maximum at momentum transfers of 400 and 500 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Green's function approach previously applied to parity-conserving inclusive electron scattering (Meucci, Giusti, Pacati, 2003) and charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering (Meucci, Giusti, Pacati, 2004) to parity-violating electron scattering. It builds on earlier nonrelativistic Green's function treatments (Capuzzi, Giusti, Pacati, 1991) and relativistic mean field models of parity-violating observables (Horowitz, 1995), as well as relativistic Fermi gas calculations (Donnelly, Dubach, Sick, 1989; Alberico et al., 1993). The approach enables consistent treatment of final state interactions in inclusive reactions while maintaining flux conservation, and allows investigation of strange quark contributions to nucleon form factors through parity-violating asymmetry measurements on 12C, 16O, and 40Ca targets.
