# Low-energy particle physics and chiral extrapolations

> In this review I discuss the role of chiral extrapolations for the determination of several phenomenologically relevant quantities, including light quark masses, meson decay constants and the axial charge of the nucleon. In particular, I investigate whether chiral extrapolations are sufficiently controlled in order to rightfully claim the accuracy which is quoted in recent compilations of these quantities. While this is the case for the masses of the light quarks and the ratio fK/fpi of decay constants, small inconsistencies in the chiral and continuum behaviour of individual decay constants fK and fpi, as well as the hadronic radii r0, r1 remain and must be clarified. In the case of the nucleon axial charge, gA, the chiral behaviour is still poorly understood due to the presence of other systematic effects.

# Low-energy particle physics and chiral extrapolations

## Key claim
Chiral extrapolations are sufficiently controlled to justify the few-percent accuracy claimed for light quark masses and the ratio fK/fpi of decay constants, but small inconsistencies remain in the chiral and continuum behaviour of the individual decay constants fK and fpi and the hadronic radii r0 and r1; for the nucleon axial charge gA, the chiral behaviour is still poorly understood due to other systematic effects such as excited-state contamination and finite-volume effects.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD results for light quark masses, meson decay constants, and the nucleon axial charge, examining whether chiral extrapolations guided by Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) are reliable given the minimum pion masses achieved. For quark masses, the stability of results under variations in the fit ansatz (SU(2) ChPT at NLO versus Taylor expansion) and under cuts on the minimum pion mass is assessed using data from the BMW and PACS-CS collaborations. For decay constants, chiral fits from RBC/UKQCD, ALPHA, and CLS are compared, and for the axial charge, the technique of summed insertions and studies of source-sink separation dependence are evaluated.

## Key numbers
- Light quark masses from FLAG global estimates (Nf=2+1): m_ud^MSbar(2 GeV) = 3.43(11) MeV, m_s^MSbar(2 GeV) = 94(3) MeV, m_s/m_ud = 27.4 ± 0.4
- BMW results for strange quark mass: m_s^MSbar(2 GeV) = 95.5 ± 1.9 MeV, m_s/m_ud = 27.53 ± 0.22 (from simulations with pion masses as low as 120 MeV)
- PACS-CS reweighted result for strange quark mass: m_s^MSbar(2 GeV) = 86.7 ± 2.3 MeV (at m_pi = 156 MeV, with m_pi_min L ≈ 2.3)
- Ratio fK/fpi from FLAG: 1.193 ± 0.005 (Nf=2+1), 1.210 ± 0.006 ± 0.017 (Nf=2)
- Form factor f+(0) from FLAG: 0.9597 ± 0.0038 (Nf=2+1), 0.9604 ± 0.0075 (Nf=2)
- CKM unitarity test using fK/fpi and nuclear beta decays: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 = 0.9999 ± 0.0006
- RBC/UKQCD new preliminary value for fpi: 125(2)(3) MeV (still below experimental value)
- ALPHA lattice spacings from fK: a ≈ 0.075, 0.066, and 0.049 fm

## Context
This review, presented at Lattice 2011, assesses the reliability of chiral extrapolations in lattice QCD following the FLAG report (FLAG 2010). It extends discussions of systematic errors in light quark masses (BMW 2010, PACS-CS 2009), meson decay constants (RBC/UKQCD 2010, MILC 2009, ETMC 2009), and the nucleon axial charge (ETMC 2010, Mainz group). The work identifies unresolved inconsistencies in individual decay constants and hadronic radii that must be clarified before these quantities can be used reliably for scale setting, and it highlights that excited-state contamination and finite-volume effects remain poorly controlled for baryonic quantities like gA.
