# Brueckner Rearrangement Effects in $^5_Λ$He and $^6_{ΛΛ}$He

> Rearrangement effects in light hypernuclei are investigated in the framework of the Brueckner theory. We can estimate without detailed numerical calculations that the energy of the $α$-core is reduced by more than 2.5 MeV when the $Λ$ adheres to $^4$He to form $^5_Λ$He. Similar assessment of rearrangement contributions is essential to deduce the strength of $ΛΛ$ interaction from experimentally observed $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$. The recently observed experimental value of $\sim$ 1 MeV for the $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ of \hll suggests that the matrix element of $<ΛΛ|v|ΛΛ>$ in \hll is around -2 MeV.

# Brueckner Rearrangement Effects in $^5_\Lambda$He and $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He

## Key claim

The energy of the $\alpha$ core in $^4$He is reduced by more than 2.5 MeV when a $\Lambda$ is added to form $^5_\Lambda$He, and this rearrangement contribution must be accounted for to extract the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction strength from the experimentally observed $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ of $\sim$1 MeV in $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He. The authors show that the $s$-wave matrix element $\langle\Lambda\Lambda|v|\Lambda\Lambda\rangle$ in $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He is around -2 MeV once these rearrangement effects are included, rather than being directly read from the experimental $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$.

## Method

The authors employ the lowest-order Brueckner theory with $G$-matrix interactions, using the $SU(6)$ quark model potential fss2 for baryon-baryon matrix elements. They derive analytic expressions for the rearrangement energy of the $\alpha$ core in $^5_\Lambda$He by relating the change in nucleon potential energy to the $\Lambda$ separation energy and the wound integral $\kappa_N$ of nucleon pairs, avoiding detailed numerical calculations. For $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, they extend the formalism to include the Pauli rearrangement contribution for the $\Lambda$ particle and the wound integral $\kappa_\Lambda$ for $\Lambda N$ pairs, computing the $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ from the difference of $G$-matrices between the three systems.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda$ separation energy $\epsilon_\Lambda$ of $^5_\Lambda$He is experimentally -3.12 MeV.
- The wound integral $\kappa_N$ for nucleon pairs is estimated to be about 0.2, using a Fermi momentum $k_F = 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ corresponding to an average density of 0.106 fm$^{-3}$ for $^4$He.
- The reduction of the $\alpha$ core energy $\Delta PE$ in $^5_\Lambda$He is estimated as 2.5 to 2.9 MeV, for $\Lambda$ oscillator constants $\nu_\Lambda = 0.4$ to 0.5 fm$^{-2}$.
- The wound integral $\kappa_\Lambda$ for $\Lambda N$ pairs is about 0.05.
- The Pauli blocking effect $P$ for $\Lambda N$ pairs is estimated as 0.23 MeV.
- The matrix element $\langle\Lambda\Lambda|G_{\Lambda\Lambda}|\Lambda\Lambda\rangle$ from the quark model potential fss2 is -2.17 MeV for $\nu = 0.40$ fm$^{-2}$ and -2.65 MeV for $\nu = 0.50$ fm$^{-2}$.
- The resulting $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ is 1.24 MeV for $\nu = 0.40$ fm$^{-2}$ and 1.53 MeV for $\nu = 0.50$ fm$^{-2}$, with the rearrangement contribution $2D$ being 0.93 MeV and 1.12 MeV respectively.
- The kinetic energy contribution $\Delta T_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ is 0.37 MeV for $\nu = 0.56$ fm$^{-2}$ and $\nu_\Lambda = 0.5$ fm$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier discussion by Bando and Shimodaya (1980) on the overbinding problem of $^5_\Lambda$He, providing a more solid estimation by connecting the rearrangement energy to the $\Lambda$ separation energy and wound integrals rather than relying on direct numerical evaluation of matrix elements. The authors contrast their results with variational calculations by Nemura, Akaishi and Suzuki, who found a 4.7 MeV core energy decrease, and with earlier variational work by Bodmer and Usmani that suggested a small rearrangement energy. The paper enables a proper extraction of the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction strength from the newly measured $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ of about 1 MeV in $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, showing that the Nijmegen model-D gives stronger attraction than the quark model potential, while the Nijmegen soft core model has insufficient attraction when rearrangement is included. The authors note that similar considerations should be applied to $^9_\Lambda$Be and $^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be.
