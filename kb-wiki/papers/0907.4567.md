# Complementary studies on $N^*$ from $e^+e^-$, $pp$ and $p\bar p$ collisions

## Key claim

While conventional $N^*$ studies rely on $\pi N$ and $\gamma^{(*)}N$ reactions, $e^+e^-$, $pp$, and $p\bar p$ collisions can provide novel insights into baryon resonances, particularly for states that have weak couplings to $\pi N$ and $\gamma N$ but stronger couplings to other channels such as $g^3N$, $\sigma N$, or $\rho^+p$. The paper argues that these alternative production mechanisms may reveal "missing" resonances and help resolve the mass-reverse problem of the lowest excited baryons, but the actual evidence remains suggestive rather than conclusive for many of the predicted states.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from BES on $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ decays to baryon-antibaryon final states (e.g., $J/\psi \to \bar p n \pi^- + c.c.$), from COSY and CELSIUS-WASA on $pp$ collisions (e.g., $pp \to p K^+ \Lambda$, $pp \to pn\pi^+$, $pp \to nK^+\Sigma^+$), and presents theoretical predictions for $\bar pp$ collisions at PANDA/FAIR (e.g., $\bar pp \to \bar p n \pi^+$). The analysis uses effective Lagrangian approaches, partial wave analyses, and comparisons of invariant mass spectra divided by Monte Carlo phase space to identify resonance structures.

## Key numbers

- $N^*(1440)$ pole mass from $J/\psi \to \bar p n \pi^-$: $1358 \pm 6 \pm 16$ MeV, width $179 \pm 26 \pm 50$ MeV, consistent with PDG pole value $1365 \pm 15$ MeV and $190 \pm 30$ MeV
- New $N^*$ peak above 2 GeV from $J/\psi \to \bar p n \pi^-$: mass $2068 \pm 3^{+15}_{-40}$ MeV, width $165 \pm 14 \pm 40$ MeV
- Branching ratio for $J/\psi \to \bar p N^*(1535)^+$: $(10 \pm 3) \times 10^{-4}$
- Branching ratios for $J/\psi \to \bar\Sigma^- \Sigma^*(1385)^+$: $(3.1 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}$; for $J/\psi \to \bar\Xi^+ \Xi^*(1530)^-$: $(5.9 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-4}$
- With $10^{10}$ $J/\psi$ and $10^9$ $\psi'(3686)$ events at BESIII, accessible mass ranges: $N^*$ up to 2740 MeV, $\Delta^*$ up to 2450 MeV, $\Lambda^*$ up to 2570 MeV, $\Sigma^*$ up to 2490 MeV, $\Xi^*$ up to 2360 MeV
- Ratio of effective coupling constants $g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda} / g_{N^*(1535)N\eta}$ deduced to be around 1 (from branching ratios) or around 0.6 (from unitary chiral theory)
- $\bar d - \bar u \approx 0.12$ in the proton, demanding at least 12% 5-quark components

## Context

This review extends the discussion of baryon spectroscopy beyond traditional $\pi N$ and $\gamma N$ experiments (Capstick, 2000; Zhao, 2002) by proposing that charmonium decays at BES, $pp$ collisions at COSY and CSR, and $\bar pp$ collisions at PANDA/FAIR can access resonances that are "missing" from the PDG listings (PDG, 2008). The paper specifically addresses the mass-reverse problem of $N^*(1535)$ and $\Lambda^*(1405)$ relative to simple 3-quark constituent quark models, and argues that penta-quark models (Helminen, 1998; Zhu, 2004; Zou, 2007) or meson cloud models (Kaiser, 1995; Jido, 2003) can naturally explain this ordering. The work enables a coordinated multi-facility program for baryon spectroscopy, with BESIII, HPLUS at Lanzhou CSR, and PANDA/FAIR joining existing efforts at CEBAF/JLab, ELSA, Spring-8, and JPARC.
