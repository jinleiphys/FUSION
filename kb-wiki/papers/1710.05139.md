# Light nuclei production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> Using the coalescence model based on the phase-space distribution of nucleons from an extended blast-wave model that includes the space-momentum correlation of high momentum nucleons, we study the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows of deuteron and helium-3 in Pb+Pb collisions at the energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. We find that the measured elliptic flows of deuteron can be satisfactorily described if nucleons of large transverse momenta are more spread in space when their momenta are more aligned along the reaction plane.

# Light nuclei production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

## Key claim

The coalescence model using nucleon phase-space distributions from an extended blast-wave model can describe the measured elliptic flow of deuterons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, provided that nucleons with large transverse momenta are more spatially spread when their momenta are more aligned along the reaction plane. This claim is conditional on the specific parameter choices $p_0=0.9$ GeV/$c$ and $a=0.05$ (GeV/$c)^{-1}$ for the 10-20% centrality bin, which introduce the space-momentum correlation for high-momentum nucleons.

## Method

The authors used an extended blast-wave model with ten parameters fitted to measured proton transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows from ALICE for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at centralities of 10-20% and 30-40%. They then applied a coalescence model using Wigner functions for deuteron and helium-3, obtained from harmonic oscillator wave functions with widths determined by empirical charge radii, to compute the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows of these light nuclei from the nucleon phase-space distributions.

## Key numbers

- For 10-20% centrality: kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_K = 120$ MeV, radial flow velocity $\beta_0 = 0.84$, average transverse radius $R_0 = 17.0$ fm, proper time $\tau_0 = 13.5$ fm/$c$, spatial eccentricity $s_2 = -0.07$, and space-momentum correlation parameters $p_0 = 0.9$ GeV/$c$ and $a = 0.05$ (GeV/$c)^{-1}$
- For 30-40% centrality: $T_K = 120$ MeV, $\beta_0 = 0.825$, $R_0 = 13.0$ fm, $\tau_0 = 10.5$ fm/$c$, $s_2 = -0.12$, $p_0 = 0.9$ GeV/$c$, and $a = 0.02$ (GeV/$c)^{-1}$
- Deuteron fugacity $\xi_d = 14.0$ for 10-20% centrality and $\xi_d = 13.0$ for 30-40% centrality
- Helium-3 fugacity $\xi_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 15.5$ for 10-20% centrality and $\xi_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 14.0$ for 30-40% centrality
- The blast-wave model with deuteron mass gives deuteron elliptic flow smaller than coalescence model results for transverse momentum above about 3 GeV/$c$ at 10-20% centrality
- At 30-40% centrality, the blast-wave model gives deuteron elliptic flow visibly smaller than coalescence model for transverse momentum above about 2 GeV/$c$, and helium-3 elliptic flow deviation becomes obvious above about 2.5 GeV/$c$

## Context

This work extends the study of Yin et al. (2017) for Au+Au collisions at RHIC to Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, using the same extended blast-wave model with space-momentum correlation. It addresses the apparent contradiction with the ALICE finding that deuteron data were more consistent with the blast-wave model than with a naive coalescence model that assumes deuteron elliptic flow equals twice the proton elliptic flow at half the deuteron transverse momentum (Kolb and Heinz, 2004). The results show that light nuclei production provides a probe of nucleon emission source properties, complementing Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry studies (Bertsch, 1988; Pratt, 1990; Mrowczynski, 1992).
