# Measurement of the energy of the 8.3-eV isomer of 229mTh with photoelectric effect

> It is proposed to use the photoelectric effect in the inner shells of the 229Th atoms to refine the energy of the 8.3-eV isomer. The calculation was performed using the Feinberg-Migdal shaking theory, which leads to the probability of isomer formation up to several units of 10^-4 in the case of the K shell. As a result, two lines are predicted in the photoelectron spectrum, their separation energy providing the energy of the isomer. Other ways of using the method to study the properties of the isomer are also discussed: through shaking during the formation of radioactive beams in storage rings, etc. Moreover, recording the effect in an experiment will provide the isomer's partial lifetime.

# Measurement of the energy of the 8.3-eV isomer of 229mTh with photoelectric effect

## Key claim
The photoelectric effect in inner shells of 229Th atoms can be used to determine the isomer energy by measuring the energy separation between two predicted photoelectron lines, with the isomer excitation probability reaching up to 2.62×10⁻⁴ for the K-shell. The method relies on the Feinberg-Migdal shaking mechanism and the mixing of ground and isomeric nuclear states via resonant conversion, but the experimental feasibility depends on resolving linewidths that range from 88.2 eV (K-shell) down to 15.5 eV (M₁-shell).

## Method
The author calculates the probability of isomer formation during photoionization of thorium atoms using the Feinberg-Migdal shaking theory, applied to the sudden creation of a hole in the electron shell. Discrete internal conversion coefficients (ICC) for M1 transitions between s-states are computed and combined with the radiative width of the nuclear transition to obtain the mixing probability β² via perturbation theory. The photoelectron spectrum is predicted to contain two lines separated by the isomer energy ωₙ, with the linewidth determined by the hole width of the ionized shell.

## Key numbers
- Isomer energy: ωₙ = 8.338(24) eV (from Sandro et al.)
- Discrete ICC for M1(7s→7s): α_d = 3.90×10¹⁰ eV (neutral atoms)
- Discrete ICC for M1(1s→1s): α_d = 5.14×10¹⁸ eV (neutral atoms)
- Discrete ICC for M1(2s→2s): α_d = 1.77×10¹⁷ eV (neutral atoms)
- Discrete ICC for M1(3s→3s): α_d = 7.84×10¹⁵ eV (neutral atoms)
- Isomer creation probability for K-shell (1s): β² = 2.62×10⁻⁴
- Isomer creation probability for L₁-shell (2s): β² = 0.90×10⁻⁵
- Isomer creation probability for M₁-shell (3s): β² = 4.00×10⁻⁷
- Isomer creation probability for valence 7s: β² = 1.99×10⁻¹²
- Hole width for K-shell: Γ = 88.2 eV
- Hole width for L₁-shell: Γ = 14.3 eV
- Hole width for M₁-shell: Γ = 15.5 eV
- Natural linewidth of the isomeric transition: 10⁻⁵ Hz

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of the Warsaw effect (Zylicz et al.) on mixing of ground and isomeric nuclear levels in hydrogen-like 229Th ions, applying it to neutral atoms and inner-shell ionization. The method complements ongoing experimental efforts to determine the isomer energy, such as crystal-lattice experiments (Sandro et al.) and frequency-comb laser scanning (Zhang et al.), and could reduce the current uncertainty in the 8.338(24) eV value. If realized, the technique would also provide the partial lifetime of the isomer and could be combined with storage-ring experiments at GSI Darmstadt or ISF Lanzhou for radioactive beam studies.
