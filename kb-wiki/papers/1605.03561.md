# Predictions for bottomonia suppression in 5.023 TeV Pb-Pb collisions

> We compute the suppression of the bottomonia states Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), Upsilon(3S), chi_b(1P), chi_b(2P), and chi_b(3P) states in Large Hadron Collider (LHC) sqrt(s_NN)) = 5.023 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. For the background evolution we use 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics with conditions extrapolated from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and we self-consistently compute bottomonia decay rates including non-equilibrium corrections to the interaction potential. For our final results, we make predictions for R_AA as function of centrality, rapidity, and p_T for the Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) states, including feed down effects. In order to assess the dependence on some of the model assumptions, we vary the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio, 4 pi eta/s = (1, 2, 3), and the initial momentum-space anisotropy parameter, xi_0 = (0, 10, 50), while holding the total light hadron multiplicity fixed.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that bottomonia suppression in 5.023 TeV Pb-Pb collisions will be significantly enhanced compared to 2.76 TeV collisions, with the caveat that these predictions depend on extrapolated initial conditions and model assumptions including the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio and initial momentum-space anisotropy.

## Method
The authors used 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics for the QGP background evolution, with initial conditions extrapolated from 2.76 TeV collisions assuming temperature scales as the fourth root of collision energy. They computed bottomonia decay rates self-consistently using a complex-valued heavy-quark potential that includes non-equilibrium corrections from momentum-space anisotropy, solving the 3d Schrodinger equation on a 256^3 lattice via the finite difference time domain method. The suppression was calculated by integrating rate equations over proper time, including feed-down contributions from excited states using experimentally determined fractions from p-p data.

## Key numbers
- Initial central transverse temperature for 5.023 TeV collisions with xi_0=0 and 4pi eta/s=1: Lambda_0 = 0.641 GeV (compared to 0.552 GeV at 2.76 TeV)
- Predicted 34% decrease in inclusive Upsilon(1S) R_AA for central collisions with 4pi eta/s=1 when going from 2.76 to 5.023 TeV
- Predicted 25% decrease in inclusive Upsilon(1S) R_AA in the lowest p_T bin for 4pi eta/s=1
- Predicted 26% decrease in inclusive Upsilon(1S) R_AA at |y|=0 for 4pi eta/s=1
- Primordial R_AA decrease for central collisions from 2.76 to 5.023 TeV: approximately 31% for Upsilon(1S), 48% for Upsilon(2S), 44% for Upsilon(3S), 51% for chi_b(1P), 45% for chi_b(2P), and 44% for chi_b(3P)
- Rest-frame formation times: 0.2 fm/c for Upsilon(1S), 0.4 fm/c for Upsilon(2S) and chi_b(1P), 0.6 fm/c for Upsilon(3S), chi_b(2P), and chi_b(3P)
- Feed-down fraction for Upsilon(1S) from direct production: 0.668; from chi_b(1P): 0.170

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations at 2.76 TeV (Krouppa and Strickland, 2015) to the higher collision energy of 5.023 TeV recently achieved at the LHC. It uses the same anisotropic hydrodynamics framework and potential model as earlier studies by Strickland and collaborators (2011, 2014), but with extrapolated initial conditions. The predictions enable experimental comparison with upcoming LHC data from 5.023 TeV Pb-Pb runs, and the systematic variation of eta/s and initial anisotropy parameters allows assessment of model uncertainties.
