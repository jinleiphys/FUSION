# In-medium modified energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon within the framework of a $π$-$ρ$-$ω$ soliton model

> We investigate the energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon in nuclear medium, based on an in-medium modified $π$-$ρ$-$ω$ soliton model, with medium modifications of the mesons considered. The results allow us to establish general features of medium modifications of the structure of nucleons bound in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the in-medium modifications of the nucleon's energy-momentum tensor form factors can be studied within a $\pi$-$\rho$-$\omega$ soliton model, but the results are conditional on two different implementations of medium modifications (Model I and Model II) that treat the $\rho$- and $\omega$-meson responses to nuclear matter differently, yielding qualitatively similar but quantitatively distinct predictions.

## Method

The authors start from an in-medium modified effective chiral Lagrangian with $\pi$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ meson degrees of freedom, where the nucleon arises as a topological soliton. They employ spherically symmetric hedgehog Ansätze for the meson fields, minimize the classical soliton mass to obtain the radial profile functions, and then collectively quantize the soliton to compute the energy-momentum tensor densities and form factors. The medium modifications are implemented through density-dependent functionals $\alpha_p$, $\alpha_s$, and $\zeta_V$, with two variants: Model I assumes the KSRF relation holds in medium for both $\rho$ and $\omega$ mesons, while Model II applies it only to the $\rho$ meson with the $\omega$ meson kept as in free space.

## Key numbers

- Free space energy density at nucleon center: $T_{00}(0) = 3.56$ GeV/fm$^3$ (Model I) and $3.51$ GeV/fm$^3$ (Model II)
- Free space energy mean-squared radius: $\langle r_{00}^2 \rangle = 0.78$ fm$^2$ (Model I) and $0.79$ fm$^2$ (Model II)
- Free space $d_1(0)$ form factor: $-5.03$ (Model I) and $-5.13$ (Model II)
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho = \rho_0$, the energy density ratio $T_{00}^*(0)/T_{00}(0) = 0.61$ (Model I) and $0.52$ (Model II)
- At $\rho = \rho_0$, the energy mean-squared radius ratio $\langle r_{00}^2 \rangle^* / \langle r_{00}^2 \rangle = 1.36$ (Model I) and $1.46$ (Model II)
- At $\rho = \rho_0$, the $d_1^*(0)/d_1(0)$ ratio = $1.11$ (Model I) and $1.33$ (Model II)
- Free space pressure at origin: $p(0) = 0.58$ GeV/fm$^3$ (Model I) and $0.59$ GeV/fm$^3$ (Model II)
- Free space node of pressure distribution: $r_0 = 0.55$ fm (both models)

## Context

This work extends the previous study of energy-momentum tensor form factors in the $\pi$-$\rho$-$\omega$ soliton model for free nucleons by Jung et al. (2013) to nuclear matter, building on the medium-modified soliton approach developed by Jung et al. (2012). It complements the earlier investigation of medium-modified EMT form factors in the Skyrme model by Kim et al. (2012), showing that despite large differences in absolute values between the Skyrme and $\pi$-$\rho$-$\omega$ soliton models, the relative impact of nuclear environment on nucleon properties is qualitatively similar. The results provide predictions that could be tested against future deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab, and may help resolve conflicting theoretical predictions for EMT form factors of nuclei.
