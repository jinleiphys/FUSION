# Models of $n\bar{n}$ transition in medium

> The present state of the $n\bar{n}$ transition problem is briefly outlined. The models based on the diagram technique for direct reactions, potential description of $\bar{n}$-medium interaction and field-theoretical approach are considered. It is shown that for the $n\bar{n}$ transition in medium field-theoretical approach should be used. The lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time $τ_{\rm min}$ is found to be: $10^{16}\; {\rm yr}>τ_{\rm min}>1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$.

# Models of $n\bar{n}$ transition in medium

## Key claim

The paper argues that the potential model and the diagram technique for direct reactions (model 1) are both unsuitable for describing neutron-antineutron transitions in medium, and that a field-theoretical approach (model 2) must be used instead. However, the result is extremely sensitive to the value of the antineutron self-energy, leading to a wide range of permissible lower limits on the free-space oscillation time: $10^{16}\; {\rm yr} > \tau_{\rm min} > 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$.

## Method

The author compares three approaches: the potential model using optical potentials for antineutron-medium interaction, a diagram technique for direct reactions (model 1) that introduces a virtual decay vertex $A \rightarrow n + (A-1)$, and a field-theoretical approach (model 2) where the initial neutron state is described by a wave function (plane wave or bound state) and the antineutron propagator can be either bare or dressed with a self-energy. The process probability and resulting lower limits on the free-space oscillation time are derived for each approach, with particular attention to infrared divergences arising from zero momentum transfer in the $n\bar{n}$ transition vertex.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from model 1 using oxygen stability: $\tau^1_{\rm min}({\rm O}) = 1.1\cdot 10^{8}\; {\rm s}$
- Lower limit on free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from model 1 using iron stability: $\tau^1_{\rm min}({\rm Fe}) = (8\div 11)\cdot 10^{8}\; {\rm s}$
- Lower limit on free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from model 2 with bare propagator: $\tau^b_{\rm min} = 10^{16}\; {\rm yr}$
- Lower limit on free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from model 2 with dressed propagator, using $\Gamma = 100$ MeV and $\Sigma \approx 10$ MeV: $\tau^d_{\rm min} = 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$
- Experimental bound on neutron lifetime in oxygen from Super-Kamiokande collaboration: $T_{n\bar{n}} > 1.77\cdot 10^{32}\; {\rm yr}$
- The lower limit $\tau^d_{\rm min} = 1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$ exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14 and the potential model limit by a factor of 5

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the correct theoretical treatment of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclear matter, directly criticizing the potential model used by earlier authors (1980-1981) and the diagram technique for direct reactions proposed by Nazaruk (earlier work) and applied to deuteron by others. The paper identifies a fundamental problem: the potential model uses a non-unitary S-matrix with an optical potential, while the diagram technique incorrectly describes the bound neutron state via a propagator rather than a wave function. The field-theoretical approach (model 2) is presented as the correct framework, but the extreme sensitivity to the antineutron self-energy leaves a wide range of possible oscillation times, motivating further theoretical work to determine the self-energy and experimental searches to narrow the allowed range.
