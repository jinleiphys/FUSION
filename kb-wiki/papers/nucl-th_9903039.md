# Neutron Star Vortex Dynamics and Magnetic Field Decay: Implications for High Density Nuclear Matter

> We investigate the effect of the density-dependent proton and neutron gaps on vortex dynamics in neutron stars. We argue that the persistence of neutron star magnetic fields on timescales of $10^9$ y suggests a superconducting gap curve with local maximum at intermediate density. We discuss the implications for exotic core phenomena such as pion/kaon condensation or a transition to quark matter.

## Key claim

The persistence of neutron star magnetic fields on timescales of 10^9 years requires that the proton superconducting gap curve have a local maximum at intermediate density, creating a region at higher density that traps flux vortices; without this shape, all flux vortices would be ejected on timescales of 10^6 years, inconsistent with observed field lifetimes.

## Method

The author calculates the force per unit length on magnetic flux vortices (FVs) arising from the radial gradient of the density-dependent proton superconducting gap, compares it to the lepton drag force to obtain expulsion timescales, and evaluates whether interactions with superfluid vortices (SVs) or crustal pinning can prevent FV ejection. The proton gap curve is computed using Fermi surface effective field theory with experimentally determined pp phase shifts and the beta-stability condition, and the resulting vortex dynamics are analyzed for two possible gap shapes: monotonically increasing (curve 1) versus peaked (curve 2).

## Key numbers

- Expulsion time for magnetic vortices due to the proton gap force: tau_Delta ~ 10^6 y
- Ohmic decay timescale for magnetic fields in the outer crust (rho < 10^12 g/cm^3): tau_omega ~ 10^7 y
- Observed persistence of magnetic fields in millisecond pulsars: 10^9 y
- Superfluid vortex area density: n_SV ~ 10^4 / P(sec) cm^{-2}
- Flux vortex number density: n_FV ~ 10^19 B_{12} cm^{-2}, where B_{12} = B / 10^12 Gauss
- Flux quantum: Phi_0 = 2 x 10^{-7} Gauss/cm^2
- Maximum of the proton gap curve occurs at density ~ 2 rho_0, where rho_0 = 2 x 10^14 g/cm^3
- Quark matter superconducting gap expected to be at least 10 MeV, possibly 50-100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of vortex buoyancy by Muslimov and Tsygan (1990) and the drag force calculations by Jones (1991), arguing that the proton gap gradient force dominates over buoyancy and that pinning to superfluid vortices or the crust is insufficient to prevent flux vortex ejection. The paper uses the Fermi surface effective field theory technique of Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (1998) to compute gap curves, and concludes that exotic core phases such as pion condensation, kaon condensation, or quark matter can only occur at very high density (greater than a few times rho_0) and occupy at most a small fraction of the star's volume, disfavoring their presence in most neutron stars.
