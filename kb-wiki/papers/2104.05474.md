# Cutoff effects in Hartree-Fock calculations at leading order of chiral effective field theory

> We explore the effects on nuclear bulk properties of using regularization cutoffs larger than the nucleon mass within the chiral effective field theory with a power counting that ensures order-by-order renormalization in the two-nucleon system. To do so we calculate ground-state properties of the $^{16}$O nucleus in the Hartree--Fock approach in a basis made up of plane waves confined in a cube. We find that regularization cutoff effects manifest themselves in two distinct ways: a strong sensitivity to the the counter-terms in attractive singular partial waves (related to the sign of the corresponding low-energy constant) and to the correction for spurious deeply bound states (for high enough cutoffs). In fact the latter happens to deprive the Hartree--Fock approximation of yielding bound solutions in nuclei. We conclude that, when using a leading-order chiral potential in the Nogga--Timmermans--van Kolck's power counting (with a regularization cutoff higher than the nucleon mass), one cannot produce a selfconsistent mean field free of spurious bound-state effects that can serve as a reference state for beyond-mean-field methods. For high regularization cutoffs which yield an attractive $^3S_1$ contact potential, one can at best incorporate in the mean-field solution a partial correction for spurious bound states. Then the remaining correction has to be added to the residual interaction in a treatment beyond the Hartree--Fock approximation. In fact a ``full'' correction in the $^3D_2$ channel, with energy shifts of the order of or somewhat larger than those recommended in Phys. Rev. C 103, 054304 (2021), is possible.

## Key claim

When using a leading-order chiral potential in the Nogga-Timmermans-van Kolck power counting with regularization cutoffs above the nucleon mass, the Hartree-Fock approximation cannot produce a selfconsistent mean field free of spurious bound-state effects that could serve as a reference state for beyond-mean-field methods; at best one can incorporate a partial correction for spurious bound states, with the remaining correction to be added to the residual interaction.

## Method

The authors calculated ground-state properties of 16O using the Hartree-Fock approximation in a basis of plane waves confined in a cubic box, implementing a leading-order chiral potential in the Nogga-Timmermans-van Kolck power counting (the "minimal scheme" NTvK_min). They considered regularization cutoffs of 500, 1000, and 1500 MeV, and treated spurious deeply bound two-nucleon states by adding scaled projectors onto those states with energy shifts of order 10 to 15 GeV. The low-energy constants in the 1S0, 3S1, 3P0, 3P2, and 3D2 channels were fitted to scattering data, with the 1S0 channel using a di-baryon auxiliary field formalism.

## Key numbers

- For Lambda = 500 MeV, no spurious bound states appear; UV convergence is achieved for single-particle momentum cutoff k_max greater than approximately 3.5 fm^-1, somewhat higher than the NN potential regularization cutoff of about 2.53 fm^-1.
- For Lambda = 1000 MeV, one spurious bound state appears in the 3P0 channel at -418.15 MeV.
- For Lambda = 1500 MeV, two spurious bound states appear: one in the coupled (3S1,3D1) channel at -2066.48 MeV and one in the 3P0 channel at -234.83 MeV, plus an additional state in the 3D2 channel at -1352.39 MeV.
- For Lambda = 1000 MeV, disregarding the spurious bound-state problem yields a positive ground-state energy and a charge radius oscillating between values larger than 5 fm, corresponding to an unbound solution.
- The recommended energy shifts for correcting spurious bound states are of the order of 10 to 15 GeV, following Yang (2021).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) on renormalization-group invariant potentials in chiral effective field theory, and addresses difficulties encountered in coupled-cluster calculations by Yang and collaborators (2021) who found that the Hartree-Fock ground-state solution at leading order in 16O had to be deformed to obtain a correlated ground-state energy below the 4-alpha threshold. The results enable future beyond-mean-field treatments (such as coupled-cluster or in-medium similarity renormalization group methods) by clarifying which corrections must be applied to the Hartree-Fock reference state when using high regularization cutoffs.
