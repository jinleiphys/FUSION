# Effective interactions for light nuclei: an effective (field theory) approach

> One of the central open problems in nuclear physics is the construction of effective interactions suitable for many-body calculations. We discuss a recently developed approach to this problem, where one starts with an effective field theory containing only fermion fields and formulated directly in a no-core shell-model space. We present applications to light nuclei and to systems of a few atoms in a harmonic-oscillator trap. Future applications and extensions, as well as challenges, are also considered.

# Effective interactions for light nuclei: an effective (field theory) approach

## Key claim

The paper proposes that effective field theory (EFT) principles can be implemented directly within the no-core shell model (NCSM) to construct effective interactions in truncated model spaces, bypassing the need for phenomenological potentials; however, the approach is demonstrated only at leading order (LO) in the pionless EFT, and the authors acknowledge that extending to higher orders or including pions significantly increases the number of low-energy constants that must be determined.

## Method

The authors formulate a pionless EFT Hamiltonian containing only contact two-body interactions in the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) nucleon-nucleon channels and a contact three-body interaction in the \(^2S_{1/2}\) channel, with coupling constants that depend on the model-space parameters \(N_{max}\) and \(\omega\). For light nuclei, the three coupling constants are fixed by reproducing the deuteron, triton, and \(^4\)He binding energies simultaneously in each model space, and energies are then extrapolated to large UV cutoff \(\Lambda\) and to \(\omega \to 0\) using analytic forms. For trapped fermion systems, the two-body coupling constant is fixed by matching the lowest two-body energy in the trap to the exact Busch formula relating trap energies to the scattering length, and few-body energies are computed by diagonalization in a finite harmonic-oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

* The predicted energy of the first \((0^+,0)\) excited state of \(^4\)He is 18.5 MeV, within 10% of the experimental value.
* The \(^6\)Li ground-state energy from the leading-order fit underbinds by about 9 MeV (within 30% of experiment); including a logarithmic term in the extrapolation reduces the overbinding to 15%.
* For three fermions in a harmonic trap at unitarity (\(b/a_2 \to 0\)), the LO results converge to the exact semi-analytical result of Werner (2006) within a few percent even for modest model-space sizes.
* The three-body ground-state energy in the limit of large \(b/a_2\) becomes \(E_3 \approx -1/(2\mu a_2^2)\), indicating the system is near the threshold for S-wave scattering of one fermion on a bound pair.

## Context

This work extends the application of EFT principles, previously used mainly with continuum free-particle bases for \(A \le 4\) systems, to the NCSM framework for larger finite nuclei. The approach contrasts with the traditional NCSM method of constructing effective interactions via unitary transformations (Navratil and Barrett, 1996; Navratil et al., 2000), which produces potential-dependent operators. The trapped-fermion implementation provides an HO-basis analog of Luscher's formula (Luscher, 1986) for connecting energy levels in a finite volume to scattering parameters, following the earlier work of Busch et al. (1998). This enables future determination of low-energy constants from two-body data alone, and ultimately from lattice QCD simulations, while also allowing extension to heavier nuclei through improved convergence in smaller model spaces.
