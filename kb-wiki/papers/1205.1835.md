# A Cusp in QED at g=2

> We explore nonperturbative properties of QED allowing a gyromagnetic ratio $g\ne g_{\rm D}\equiv 2$. We study the effective action $V_{\mathrm{eff}}$ for an arbitrarily strong constant and homogeneous field. Using the external field method, we find a cusp as a function of the gyromagnetic factor $g$ in: a) The QED $b_0$-renormalization group coefficient; b) A subclass of light-light scattering coefficients obtained in the long wavelength limit expansion. We recognize possibility of asymptotic freedom in an Abelian theory for certain domains of $g$.

## Key claim

The Dirac value \(g=2\) is a cusp point (non-analytic singularity) in the effective action of \(g\)-QED, arising from the periodic crossing of Landau eigenstates; this cusp appears in the renormalization group coefficient \(b_0\) and in light-light scattering coefficients, and implies that perturbative D-QED expansion around \(g=2\) is incomplete at sufficiently high order.

## Method

The authors extend QED to arbitrary gyromagnetic ratio \(g\) using the renormalizable Klein-Gordon-Pauli (KGP) action, a dimension-4 operator. They compute the Heisenberg-Euler-Schwinger effective action for constant electromagnetic fields by summing Landau eigenvalues, employing the Euler summation formula to obtain periodic Bernoulli functions. For \(|g|>2\), they define the physical Hilbert space by selecting states with \(K\ge 0\) (where \(K\) is the scaled eigenvalue), which yields a self-adjoint extension and an analytic continuation of the proper-time integrand via the series expansion in Eq. (meroexpB).

## Key numbers

* The renormalization group coefficient \(b_0(g) = -\frac{4}{3}f_{1,0}(g)\) with \(f_{1,0}(g) = \frac{3}{8}(g-4k)^2 - \frac{1}{2}\) in each domain \(g\in[g_{k-1},g_k]\); at \(g=2\) (the cusp), \(f_{1,0}(2)=1\).
* The switch between infrared-stable and asymptotically free behavior occurs at \(g = \pm 2/\sqrt{3} \approx \pm 1.155\) in the principal domain \(|g|\le 2\), and periodically at \(g = 4 - 2/\sqrt{3} \approx 2.845\).
* Light-light scattering coefficients: \(f_{2,0}(g) = -\frac{15(g-4k)^4}{128} + \frac{15(g-4k)^2}{16} - \frac{7}{8}\) and \(f_{0,2}(g) = \frac{15(g-4k)^4}{224} - \frac{1}{14}\), both normalized to their values at \(g=2\).
* The effective action for \(|g|\le 2\) uses the integrand \(F(x,y,g/2) = \frac{x\cosh(g x/2)}{\sinh x} \frac{y\cos(g y/2)}{\sin y} - 1\), valid for \(|g/2|\le 1\).

## Context

This work extends the Heisenberg-Euler-Schwinger effective action (Heisenberg 1935, Weisskopf, Schwinger 1951) to arbitrary gyromagnetic ratio \(g\) in the renormalizable \(g\)-QED framework (Vaquera Araujo 2012, Angeles Martinez 2011). It resolves the non-unitarity problem identified by Veltman (1997) for \(g\ne 2\) theories by a new eigenstate sorting based on the sign of \(K\). The results enable exploration of Abelian asymptotic freedom for certain \(g\) domains and show that perturbative D-QED expansion around \(g=2\) is incomplete, motivating a nonperturbative treatment of the cusp in higher-order calculations.
