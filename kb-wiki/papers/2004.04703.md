# Hierarchy of kinetic freeze-out parameters in low energy heavy-ion collisions

> We study the mass dependent hierarchy of kinetic freeze-out parameters of hadrons in low energy heavy-ion collisions. For this purpose, the transverse momentum and rapidity spectra of the identified hadrons produced in central Pb+Pb collisions, available at SPS energies ranging from $\rm E_{Lab}=20A-158A $ GeV, are analyzed within a generalized non boost-invariant blast wave model. We consider separate simultaneous fits for light hadrons ($π^{-}$, $K^{\pm}$) and heavy strange hadrons ($Λ$, $\barΛ$, $ϕ$, $Ξ^{\pm}$, $Ω^{\pm}$), for which the transverse momentum spectra as well as rapidity spectra are available. We also perform a separate fit to transverse momentum spectra of charmonia ($J/Ψ$, $Ψ'$) at $158A $ GeV collisions. We find a clear mass dependent hierarchy in the fitted kinetic freeze-out parameters. Further, we study the rapidity spectra using analytical Landau flow solution for non-conformal systems. We find that the fitted value of sound velocity in the medium also shows a similar hierarchy.

# Hierarchy of kinetic freeze-out parameters in low energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors claim a clear mass-dependent hierarchy in kinetic freeze-out parameters of hadrons produced in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (20A to 158A GeV), with heavier hadrons decoupling from the fireball earlier (at higher temperature and lower transverse flow velocity) than lighter hadrons. This hierarchy is conditional on the use of a non boost-invariant blast-wave model with separate simultaneous fits for light hadrons (pions, kaons), heavy strange hadrons (Lambda, anti-Lambda, phi, Xi, Omega), and charmonia (J/Psi, Psi'), rather than a single combined fit for all species.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum and rapidity spectra of identified hadrons from central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (20A to 158A GeV) using a generalized non boost-invariant blast-wave model. They performed separate simultaneous fits for light hadrons (pi-, K+) and heavy strange hadrons (Lambda, anti-Lambda, phi, Xi+, Omega+), where both pT and rapidity spectra were available, and a separate fit to pT spectra of charmonia (J/Psi, Psi') at 158A GeV. An iterative procedure was employed to extract three free parameters: kinetic freeze-out temperature (Tkin), mean transverse flow velocity (beta_T), and space-time rapidity width (eta_max), with rapidity spectra also analyzed using the analytical Landau flow solution for non-conformal systems to extract the squared speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- Heavy strange hadrons: kinetic freeze-out temperature in the range 93.09 +/- 0.19 MeV (at 20A GeV) to 109.24 +/- 0.11 MeV (at 158A GeV)
- Heavy strange hadrons: mean transverse flow velocity in the range 0.4418 +/- 0.0032 (at 20A GeV) to 0.4688 +/- 0.0016 (at 158A GeV)
- Charmonia (J/Psi, Psi') at 158A GeV: Tkin = 164 MeV, eta_max = 1.70, mean beta_T approximately 0.2
- Squared speed of sound for heavy strange hadrons: 0.1602 +/- 0.0006 (20A GeV) to 0.2511 +/- 0.0003 (158A GeV)
- Squared speed of sound for light hadrons: 0.0755 +/- 0.0000 (20A GeV) to 0.2276 +/- 0.0001 (158A GeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of light hadrons (Rode, 2018) using the same non boost-invariant blast-wave model (Dobler, 1999) to now include heavy strange hadrons and charmonia, systematically demonstrating a mass-dependent hierarchy in kinetic freeze-out parameters for the first time at SPS energies. The results support the expectation that heavier hadrons decouple earlier due to smaller medium-induced momentum changes, and the extracted freeze-out parameters enable partial reconstruction of the expansion history of the fireball. The analysis complements earlier work by Gorenstein and collaborators, who analyzed J/Psi, Psi', and Omega pT spectra within a boost-invariant blast-wave model with fixed Tkin = 170 MeV, and extends the investigation to multiple strange hadron species using both pT and rapidity distributions.
