# Viscous properties of hot and dense QCD matter in the presence of a magnetic field

> We have studied the effect of strong magnetic field on the viscous properties of hot QCD matter at finite chemical potential by calculating the shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities. The viscosities are calculated using kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. The interactions are incorporated through the quasiparticle masses of partons at strong magnetic field and finite chemical potential. From this study, one can understand the effects of strong magnetic field and chemical potential on the sound attenuation through the Prandtl number (Pl), on the nature of the flow by the Reynolds number (Rl), and on the relative behavior between shear viscosity and bulk viscosity through the ratio $ζ/η$. We have found that, $η$ and $ζ$ get increased in a strong magnetic field and the additional presence of chemical potential further enhances their magnitudes. With the increase of temperature, $η$ increases in a strong magnetic field as well as in the absence of magnetic field, whereas $ζ$ decreases with the temperature, contrary to its increase in the absence of magnetic field. We have observed that, the Prandtl number gets increased in the presence of strong magnetic field and chemical potential as compared to the isotropic one, but it always remains larger than 1, thus the momentum diffusion largely affects the sound attenuation in the medium. The Reynolds number gets lowered than 1 in a strong magnetic field and it becomes further decreased in an additional presence of chemical potential, so the kinematic viscosity dominates over the characteristic length scale of the system. Finally, $ζ/η$ becomes larger than 1, contrary to its value in the absence of magnetic field and chemical potential where it is less than 1, so the bulk viscosity prevails over the shear viscosity for the hot and dense QCD matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in the strong magnetic field limit (|q_fB| ≫ T² and |q_fB| ≫ m_f²), both shear viscosity (η) and bulk viscosity (ζ) of hot QCD matter increase, and the additional presence of finite chemical potential further enhances their magnitudes; however, this result depends on the quasiparticle model used to incorporate interactions and the relaxation time approximation in kinetic theory.

## Method

The shear and bulk viscosities were calculated using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation time approximation, first for an isotropic dense QCD medium in the absence of a magnetic field and then for a hot and dense QCD medium in the presence of a strong magnetic field. In the strong magnetic field limit, quarks are restricted to the lowest Landau level (n=0), making their motion effectively one-dimensional along the magnetic field direction, while gluons are unaffected directly but influenced indirectly through their thermal masses. The interactions among partons were incorporated through quasiparticle masses at finite temperature, strong magnetic field, and finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The strong magnetic field strength ranges from eB = m_π² (≈ 10¹⁸ Gauss) at RHIC to 15 m_π² at LHC, as cited from Skokov (2009).
- The quark chemical potential may reach approximately 100 MeV near the critical temperature (around 160 MeV) of phase transition, and in the presence of a strong magnetic field, it rises up to 200 MeV, as cited from Fukushima (2016).
- The Prandtl number (Pl) remains larger than 1 in the presence of strong magnetic field and chemical potential, indicating momentum diffusion dominates over thermal diffusion in sound attenuation.
- The Reynolds number (Rl) becomes lower than 1 in a strong magnetic field and decreases further with additional chemical potential, implying kinematic viscosity dominates over the characteristic length scale.
- The ratio ζ/η becomes larger than 1 in the presence of strong magnetic field and chemical potential, contrary to its value less than 1 in their absence.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in hot QCD matter by incorporating both strong magnetic fields and finite chemical potential, building on prior calculations of shear and bulk viscosities using the Kubo formula (Hattori 2017), perturbative QCD in weak magnetic fields (Li 2018), the Chapman-Enskog method with effective fugacity (Kurian 2019), and holographic approaches (Rebhan 2012, Jain 2015, Finazzo 2016). The authors had previously studied charge and thermal transport (electrical and thermal conductivities) in the same environment (Rath 2020), and this work enables understanding of how viscous properties affect sound attenuation, flow type, and the relative importance of shear versus bulk viscosity in heavy-ion collision conditions at RHIC, LHC, and the CBM experiment at FAIR.
