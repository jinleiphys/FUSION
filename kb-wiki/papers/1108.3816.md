# Subleading contributions to the chiral three-nucleon force II: Short-range terms and relativistic corrections

> We derive the short-range contributions and the leading relativistic corrections to the three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim
The paper derives the short-range contributions and leading relativistic (1/m) corrections to the three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in chiral effective field theory, and finds that the one-pion-exchange-contact topology yields no net contribution after antisymmetrization, while the two-pion-exchange-contact topology and relativistic corrections produce nonvanishing results that depend on unitary transformation parameters (β8, β9) which must be fixed consistently with the two-nucleon force.

## Method
The authors employ the method of unitary transformation to derive the irreducible three-nucleon potential from the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons, integrating out pion fields perturbatively. They evaluate Feynman-like diagrams for the one-pion-exchange-contact topology, two-pion-exchange-contact topology, and relativistic corrections using dimensional regularization, and perform antisymmetrization with respect to nucleon labels to obtain the final three-nucleon force expressions.

## Key numbers
- The one-pion-exchange-contact topology contribution vanishes exactly after antisymmetrization of nucleons 2 and 3, using identities involving Pauli spin and isospin matrices.
- The two-pion-exchange-contact topology yields a contribution proportional to gA^4 CT / (48 π Fπ^4) with terms involving the loop function A(q) = (1/(2q)) arctan(q/(2 Mπ)).
- The gA^2 CT contribution to the two-pion-exchange-contact topology is -gA^2 CT / (24 π Fπ^4) multiplied by τ1·τ2 (σ2·σ3) [Mπ + (2 Mπ^2 + q1^2) A(q1)].
- The relativistic corrections involve parameters β8 and β9; consistency with the N3LO two-nucleon potential of Epelbaum (2004) requires β8 = 1/4.

## Context
This work completes the derivation of the three-nucleon force at N3LO in chiral effective field theory, extending the long-range parts computed by Bernard (2007) and Ishikawa (2007). The results enable consistent N3LO calculations in three-nucleon systems and show that the full three-nucleon force at this order depends only on the two low-energy constants D and E from N2LO, with no new unknown parameters. An exploratory study of the long-range parts in the triton was presented by Skibinski (2011).
