# Symmetry-conserving vortex clusters in small rotating clouds of ultracold bosons

> The properties of a special class of correlated many-body wave functions, named rotating vortex clusters (RVCs), that preserve the total angular momentum of a small cloud of trapped rotating bosons are investigated. They have lower energy and provide a superior description for the formation of vortices compared to the mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) states that break the rotational symmetry. The GP vortex states are shown to be wave packets composed of such RVC states. Our results suggest that, for a small number of bosons, the physics is different from that of ideal Bose-Einstein condensates which characterize larger assemblies

## Key claim

For small numbers of trapped bosons, the rotating vortex cluster (RVC) states that conserve total angular momentum provide a superior description of vortex formation compared to mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) states that break rotational symmetry, and the GP vortex states are shown to be wave packets composed of RVC states.

## Method

The authors use a two-step many-body method of symmetry breaking followed by symmetry restoration via projection techniques. They start with the many-body Gross-Pitaevskii vortex solution and apply a projection operator that restores good total angular momentum, yielding RVC wave functions. The spectral decomposition of GP states in terms of RVC states is then calculated numerically using the overlap kernel between rotated and unrotated GP wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For N=9 bosons with interparticle repulsion strength R_delta=50, at rotational frequency Omega/omega_0=0.5, the GP solution exhibits a (0,4) single polygonal ring of four vortices with average angular momentum 26.93, while the corresponding RVC has total angular momentum L=28.
- At Omega/omega_0=0.565, the GP solution exhibits a (2,5) double polygonal ring of seven vortices with average angular momentum 38.11, while the corresponding RVC has L=36.
- For Omega/omega_0=0.2, the GP vortex solution exhibits a (0,2) single polygonal-ring configuration with RVC angular momenta obeying the selection rule L=2k.
- For Omega/omega_0=0.45, the GP vortex solution exhibits a (0,3) single polygonal-ring configuration with RVC angular momenta obeying the selection rule L=3k.
- For Omega/omega_0=0.55, the GP vortex solution exhibits a double (2,5) polygonal-ring structure with an approximate selection rule L=2k_1+5k_2.
- The GP total-angular-momentum expectation values for the cases in the spectral decomposition are: 0.0 at Omega/omega_0=0, 11.01 at Omega/omega_0=0.2, 21.42 at Omega/omega_0=0.45, and 36.53 at Omega/omega_0=0.55.

## Context

This work extends the method of rotational symmetry restoration via projection techniques, previously introduced for quantum dots (Yannouleas and Landman, 2002) and harmonic traps (Romanovsky et al., 2004, 2006), to define vortex states with good total angular momentum. The RVC approach contrasts with exact diagonalization calculations in the lowest Landau level for N=9 bosons (Baksmaty et al., 2007; Barberan et al., 2006), which exhibit quantized ground-state "magic" angular momenta following a selection rule with the additional condition that the total number of vortices equals N, unlike the RVC behavior. The results suggest that for small bosonic clouds the physics differs markedly from larger assemblies described by ideal Bose-Einstein condensates, and may motivate further experimental research in correlated states of small bosonic systems.
