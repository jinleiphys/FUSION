# Transverse momentum dependence of Hanbury Brown-Twiss radii of pions from a perfectly opaque source with hydrodynamic flow

> We investigate the transverse momentum dependence of pion HBT radii on the basis of a hydrodynamical model. Recent experimental data show that $R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}} < 1$, which suggests a strong opaqueness of the source. In addition to the opaqueness naturally caused by transverse flow, we introduce an extrinsic opacity by imposing restrictions on the pion emission angle. Comparing the HBT radii obtained from the normal Cooper-Frye prescription and the opaque emission prescription, we find that $R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}$ is less than unity only for small values of the transverse momentum with an opaque source. However, HBT radii for large values of the transverse momentum are dominated by the transverse flow effect and are affected less by the source opaqueness.

## Key claim

The authors find that introducing an extrinsic opacity by restricting pion emission angles in a hydrodynamical model yields \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}} < 1\) only for small transverse momentum values; at larger transverse momentum, the transverse flow effect dominates and the opacity has little influence on the HBT radii.

## Method

The authors model \(\sqrt{s}=200A\) GeV Au+Au central collisions at RHIC using a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical evolution with a single freeze-out temperature of 130 MeV. They compute two-pion correlation functions via the Cooper-Frye prescription and then impose two constraints to create a perfectly opaque source: a step function \(\theta(p\cdot d\sigma)\) to forbid inward emission into the fluid, and a factor \(\theta(\cos(\phi-\psi))\) to prohibit emission opposite to the radial flow direction. HBT radii are extracted by fitting the correlation functions to a Gaussian form in the side, out, and long directions.

## Key numbers

* The freeze-out temperature is set to \(T_{\text{f}} = 130\) MeV.
* The initial maximum energy density is \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 6.9\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
* The opaque emission conditions cause an energy loss of 11%; 89% of emitted energy quanta satisfy the conditions.
* The resultant multiplicity \(dN/d\eta_p\) at \(\eta_p = 0\) decreases from 618 to 593 when opacity is imposed.
* The time duration \(\Delta t\) is 6 fm/\(c\) for \(k_{\text{T}} = 200\) MeV/\(c\).
* \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) becomes less than unity only in the smallest \(K_{\text{T}}\) bin (around 50 MeV/\(c\)); no improvement is seen beyond \(K_{\text{T}} \sim 0.5\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work addresses the "HBT puzzle" at RHIC, where experimental data from STAR and PHENIX show \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}} < 1\), contradicting conventional hydrodynamical models that predict a larger ratio due to a prolonged emission duration from a phase transition (Rischke, 1998). The authors extend their earlier hydrodynamical analysis (Morita, 2002) by introducing an explicit opaque emission prescription, building on the concept of an opaque source proposed by Heiselberg (1999) and Tomasik (1998). The results show that opacity alone cannot resolve the puzzle in the presence of transverse flow, pointing toward the need for other mechanisms such as viscosity corrections, \(\rho\) meson broadening, or improved freeze-out hypersurfaces.
