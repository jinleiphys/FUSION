# Coupled-cluster theory of a gas of strongly-interacting fermions in the dilute limit

> We study the ground-state properties of a dilute gas of strongly-interacting fermions in the framework of the coupled-cluster expansion (CCE). We demonstrate that properties such as universality, opening of a gap in the excitation spectrum and applicability of s-wave approximations appear naturally in the CCE approach. In the zero-density limit, we show that the ground-state energy density depends on only one parameter which in turn may depend at most on the spatial dimensionality of the system.

# Coupled-cluster theory of a gas of strongly-interacting fermions in the dilute limit

## Key claim

In the zero-density limit of the unitarity problem, the ground-state energy density ratio ξ depends on only one parameter (the coefficient B₀ of the irregular s-wave spherical Bessel component n₀(kr) in the 2-hole function), which in turn may depend at most on the spatial dimensionality of the system; this universality emerges naturally from the coupled-cluster expansion without invoking variational gap parameters as in BCS mean-field theory.

## Method

The authors apply the coupled-cluster expansion (CCE) formalism to the infinite matter system of spin-1/2 fermions interacting via a zero-range contact interaction with infinite s-wave scattering length (the Bertsch problem). They truncate the cluster operator at the 2-particle–2-hole level (S₂), which is justified in the dilute limit, and solve the resulting nonlinear equations for the ground-state energy density and the S₂ amplitudes. The analysis uses a specific cosh⁻² potential (Carlson et al., 2003) that yields infinite scattering length, and introduces a 2-hole function formalism to examine the partial-wave structure of the correlation amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The noninteracting Fermi gas energy density is ε₀ = (3/5) ε_F, with ε_F = k_F²/(2m), and the total density ρ₀ = k_F³/(3π²).
- The quantum Monte Carlo result for the universal ratio ξ = ε/ε₀ is ξ_QMC = 0.44 ± 0.01 (Carlson et al., 2003).
- The mean-field BCS-Leggett value is ξ_MF = 0.59, indicating beyond-mean-field effects change the result by about 25%.
- The potential range parameter r₀ = 2/α for the cosh⁻² potential V(r) = -(1/m) α²/cosh²(αr).
- The dilute limit corresponds to α r_s → ∞, where r_s = 1/(γ k_F) with γ = [4/(9π)]^(1/3); Carlson et al. performed QMC calculations at α r_s = 12 and 24.
- In the zero-density limit, the only nonvanishing contribution to (ξ₀ − 1) comes from the coefficient B₀ of the irregular spherical Bessel function n₀(kr); all regular j_ℓ(kr) components vanish.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-cluster approach to the Bertsch problem, originally posed as a parameter-free model of neutron matter at subnuclear density, and now central to the BCS-BEC crossover in ultracold fermionic atom gases. The authors demonstrate that CCE reproduces expected universal behavior and the opening of a gap in the single-particle excitation spectrum without invoking variational gap parameters as in the standard BCS mean-field picture (Leggett, 1980). The results are consistent with quantum Monte Carlo studies (Carlson et al., 2003) and provide a formal framework for future numerical calculations of the crossover, as well as for extending first-principles calculations to systems with large particle numbers.
