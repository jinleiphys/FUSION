# 6Li Inelastic Form Factors in a Cluster Model

> Longitudinal and transverse form factors are calculated for the transition into the low lying excited T=0 and T=1 states of 6Li in the framework of the resonating group model. All form factors are reproduced simultaneously using three cluster-wavefunctions. Meson exchange currents yield only minor corrections and do not lead to any specific structures at large momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The resonating group model (RGM) with three cluster wavefunctions (α-np) simultaneously reproduces all measured longitudinal and transverse form factors for low-lying T=0 and T=1 excited states of ⁶Li, but the agreement is only fair for excitation energies due to fixed width parameters, and meson exchange currents yield only minor corrections without introducing structure at large momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors used completely antisymmetrised RGM cluster wavefunctions in the form α-np with an effective nucleon-nucleon potential, applying the Ritz variational principle for the ground state and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the corresponding model spaces for the T=0 spin-orbit triplet (3⁺, 2⁺, 1⁺) states. For the T=1 states, they used Ritz variational principle for the 0⁺ state (allowing P-waves on intercluster coordinates) and applied the same parameters to the 2⁺ state (allowing S-, P-, and D-wave combinations), with additional ⁵Li-n and ⁵He-p configurations tested for the 0⁺T=1 state. Electromagnetic transition form factors were calculated using standard one-body operators for charge density, convection current, and magnetisation density, with meson exchange currents treated via the prescription of Ohta (1989) and single nucleon form factors applied via the dipole formula.

## Key numbers

- Dipole form factor parameter: A₁ = 0.71 (GeV/ℏc)², used for single nucleon form factor correction
- For the 0⁺T=1 state variational calculation, the wavefunction parameters were β=0.2838, γ=0.1620, δ₁=0.2609, and δ₂=0.03624 (see reference [9] for parameter definitions)
- Additional ⁵Li-n and ⁵He-p configurations in the 0⁺T=1 wavefunction gained 0.7 MeV additional binding energy
- For the 2⁺T=1 state, the longitudinal form factor result is "some orders of magnitude smaller" than for T=0 states (no exact numerical factor given)
- Meson exchange currents reduced the calculated results for the 0⁺T=1 state by a small amount, and for the 2⁺T=1 state by "a few percent"

## Context

This work extends the cluster model calculations of Hofmann (1990) from the ⁶Li ground state to all low-lying excited states, using the RGM framework. The results are similar to those of Faddeev equation calculations by Lehman (1990) for the T=0 triplet states, but disagree in magnitude and form for the 2⁺T=1 state. The paper enables future comparisons with experimental data for states where no data currently exist (due to width of T=0 states or smallness of longitudinal form factors for 2⁺T=1), and suggests that additional structures in the ground state wavefunction would be needed to improve the quadrupole moment while maintaining form factor agreement.
