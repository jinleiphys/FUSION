# Heavy quarkonia in strong magnetic fields

> We investigate the influence of a homogeneus and constant strong external magnetic field on the heavy-meson spectrum. Quarkonium states $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$ are described within a non-relativistic framework and by means of a suitable potential model based on the Cornell parametrization. In particular, in this work we propose a model which takes into account the possible anisotropies emerging at the level of the static quark-antiquark potential, as observed in recent lattice studies. The investigation is perfomed both with and without taking into account the anisotropy of the static potential, in order to better clarify its effects

## Key claim

The paper proposes a model for heavy quarkonia in strong magnetic fields that incorporates the anisotropy of the static quark-antiquark potential observed in lattice QCD studies, and finds that this anisotropy significantly modifies the mass spectrum, especially for bottomonium, while leaving the magnetic-field-induced mixing between spin states largely unchanged.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic two-body Schrodinger equation for charmonium and bottomonium states in a constant external magnetic field using a Hamiltonian that includes kinetic terms, the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement), a spin-spin interaction term, and quark magnetic moment couplings. The static potential is made anisotropic by introducing angle-dependent Coulomb and string-tension parameters fitted to lattice QCD results from an earlier study (Bonati et al., 2014), with the magnetic field directed along the z-axis. Eigenvalues are computed numerically using the Finite Difference Time Domain method in imaginary time, and the center-of-mass kinetic energy is subtracted using the pseudomomentum formalism.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium at maximum field |e|B = 0.3 GeV^2, the anisotropy increases masses by 30-40 MeV compared to the isotropic case.
- The ratio eB hbar / (m^2 c^3) for charm quarks at |e|B = 0.3 GeV^2 is about 0.18, comparable to the non-relativistic validity estimate (M_J/Psi - 2m_c)/M_J/Psi.
- For bottomonium, the mass shift due to the anisotropy is about one order of magnitude larger than the shift from quark magnetic moments alone.
- The mixing between eta_b and the s_z=0 component of Upsilon is reduced by approximately a factor of 3 (roughly m_b/m_c) compared to the charmonium case.
- The string tension anisotropy parameters from lattice data: A^{sigma_xy} = 0.29 +/- 0.02, C^{sigma_xy} = 0.9 +/- 0.1, A^{sigma_z} = -0.34 +/- 0.01, C^{sigma_z} = 1.5 +/- 0.1.
- The Coulomb coupling anisotropy parameters: A^{alpha_xy} = -0.24 +/- 0.04, C^{alpha_xy} = 0.7 +/- 0.2, A^{alpha_z} = 0.24 +/- 0.03, C^{alpha_z} = 1.7 +/- 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic potential model approach of Alford and Strickland (2013) by incorporating the anisotropic static potential measured in lattice QCD by Bonati et al. (2014), which showed that the string tension increases in directions transverse to the magnetic field and decreases longitudinally. The results enable predictions for how heavy quarkonium masses and decay patterns might be modified in the early stages of non-central heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, where magnetic fields of order |e|B ~ 0.2-0.3 GeV^2 are produced, and suggest that bottomonium states are more sensitive to these non-perturbative magnetic field effects than charmonium states.
