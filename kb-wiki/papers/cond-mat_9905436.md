# The SO(5) Theory as a Critical Dynamical Symmetry

> We use generalized coherent states to analyze the SO(5) theory of high-temperature superconductivity and antiferromagnetism. We demonstrate that the SO(5) symmetry can be embedded in a larger algebra that allows it to be interpreted as a critical dynamical symmetry interpolating between antiferromagnetic and superconducting phases. This dynamical interpretation suggests that SO(5) defines a phase with the character of a spin-glass for a significant range of doping.

# The SO(5) Theory as a Critical Dynamical Symmetry

## Key claim

The SO(5) symmetry proposed by Zhang (1997) for unifying antiferromagnetism and high-temperature superconductivity can be embedded in a larger U(4) algebra, where it acts as a critical dynamical symmetry that interpolates between antiferromagnetic and superconducting phases. For a significant range of doping, the SO(5) symmetry defines a phase with the character of a spin glass, rather than directly describing either the antiferromagnetic or superconducting state.

## Method

The authors constructed a U(4) algebra from 16 bilinear fermion operators and used generalized coherent states (symmetry-constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov variational states) to analyze the SO(5) subgroup embedded within it. They computed expectation values of the SO(5) Casimir operator and a generalized Hamiltonian parameterized by a mixing parameter p, where p = 1/2 corresponds to exact SO(5) symmetry, p = 0 to SU(2) (superconducting) symmetry, and p = 1 to SO(4) (antiferromagnetic) symmetry. The ground-state energy surfaces were evaluated as functions of a single variational parameter β related to the staggered magnetization, for different lattice occupation fractions.

## Key numbers

- The SO(5) Casimir operator expectation value was evaluated using the relation ⟨C₅⟩ = ⟨C_{su(4)}⟩ − ⟨D†D⟩ − ⟨𝒬·𝒬⟩ + (n−Ω)/2, where ⟨C_{su(4)}⟩ = Ω/2 (Ω/2 + 4) and Ω is the lattice degeneracy.
- The particle number expectation value in the coherent state is n ≡ ⟨N̂⟩ = 2Ω(α² + β²) = Ω(v₊² + v₋²), where α and β are variational parameters constrained to a circle of radius √(n/2Ω).
- The z-component of staggered magnetization is ⟨𝒬_z⟩ = 2Ω β (n/(2Ω) − β²)^{1/2} = ½ Ω (v₊² − v₋²).
- For the SO(5) limit (p = 1/2) at half filling (n/Ω = 1), the energy surface is almost flat for broad ranges of β, indicating large-amplitude fluctuations in antiferromagnetic order.
- For p = 0.52 (slightly broken SO(5)), the stable point ⟨β⟩₀ = 0 for small n (superconducting order), while for n near Ω (half filling), ⟨β⟩₀ ≃ ±0.5 (antiferromagnetic order).

## Context

This work extends the SO(5) theory of high-temperature superconductivity proposed by Zhang (1997) by embedding it in a larger U(4) algebra introduced by the same authors in a companion paper (Guidry et al., 1999). The analysis uses generalized coherent state methods developed by Gilmore (1972, 1974) and Perelomov (1972), and the concept of critical dynamical symmetries previously applied in nuclear structure physics, particularly the SO(7) critical dynamical symmetry of the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (Wu et al., 1994). The results enable a reinterpretation of the cuprate phase diagram: underdoped compounds correspond to weakly broken SO(5) symmetry, while optimally doped superconductors and antiferromagnetic insulators are associated with SU(2) and SO(4) subgroups of SU(4), respectively.
