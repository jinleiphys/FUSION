# Insight into nuclear midshell structures by study of $K$-isomers in rare-earth neutron-rich nuclei

> Inspired by the newly discovered isomeric states in the rare-earth neutron-rich nuclei, high-$K$ isomeric states in neutron-rich samarium and gadolinium isotopes are investigated within the framework of the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlation treated by a particle-number-conserving (PNC) method. The experimental multi-particle state energies and moments of inertia are reproduced quite well by the PNC-CSM calculations. A remarkable effect from the high-order deformation $\varepsilon_{6}$ is demonstrated. Based on the occupation probabilities, the configurations are assigned to the observed high-$K$ isomeric states. The lower $5^-$ isomeric state in $^{158}$Sm is preferred as the two-proton state with configuration $π\frac{5}{2}^{+}[413]\otimesπ\frac{5}{2}^{-}[532]$. More low-lying two-particle states are predicted. The systematics of the electronic quadrupole transition probabilities, $B(E2)$ values along the neodymium, samarium, gadolinium and dysprosium isotopes and $N=96,98,100,102$ isotones chains is investigated to reveal the midshell collectivities.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the cranked shell model with particle-number-conserving pairing (PNC-CSM) reproduces experimental multi-particle state energies and moments of inertia for high-$K$ isomers in neutron-rich samarium and gadolinium isotopes quite well, but this agreement depends critically on including the high-order deformation parameter $\varepsilon_{6}$, which causes 50-250 keV variations in state energies and is strongly model-dependent, especially for neutrons in the neutron-rich region where single-particle level knowledge is limited.

## Method
The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing correlations was diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space using the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method, which treats Pauli blocking spontaneously without the BCS or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle formalism. Nilsson single-particle levels were calculated within valence shells (proton N=0-5, neutron N=0-6) using deformation parameters $\varepsilon_{2}$, $\varepsilon_{4}$, and $\varepsilon_{6}$ from the tables of Moller et al. (1995, 2016). The effective pairing strengths were fitted to moments of inertia due to lack of experimental binding energy data, with CMPC spaces of about 700 dimensions for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers
- The $5^-$ isomeric state in $^{158}$Sm is calculated at 1.227 MeV (two-proton $\pi\frac{5}{2}^{+}[413]\otimes\pi\frac{5}{2}^{-}[532]$ configuration), compared to the experimental value of 1.279 MeV.
- The $6^-$ isomer in $^{164}$Sm is calculated at 1.773 MeV with $\varepsilon_{6}=0.04$ (from Moller et al. 2016), compared to the experimental value of 1.486 MeV; with $\varepsilon_{6}=0.053$ (from Moller et al. 1995) the energy shifts up by 579 keV to a worse value.
- The $11^{+}$ four-particle state in $^{160}$Sm is calculated at 2.784 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.757 MeV.
- The $4^+$ state in $^{166}$Gd is calculated at 0.839 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.350 MeV.
- The $6^-$ state in $^{166}$Gd is calculated at 1.690 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.601 MeV.
- Effective pairing strengths used: $G_{0p}=0.20$ MeV, $G_{2p}=0.02$ MeV, $G_{0n}=0.23$ MeV, $G_{2n}=0.02$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied successfully to normal deformed nuclei in the A=170 mass region (Zeng et al. 1994, Wu et al. 1991), superdeformed nuclei in A=150,190 (Wu et al. 1992, Liu et al. 2002), and high-$K$ isomers in rare-earth and actinide nuclei (Li et al. 2013, Zhang et al. 2009), to the neutron-rich rare-earth region for the first time. It directly addresses recent experimental discoveries of $K$-isomers in $^{160}$Sm, $^{164}$Sm, and $^{166}$Gd (Patel et al. 2014, 2016) and in odd-A samarium isotopes (Patel et al. 2017, Yokoyama et al. 2017), providing configuration assignments and predictions of additional low-lying two-particle states. The results enable further investigation of midshell collectivity through $B(E2)$ systematics along neodymium, samarium, gadolinium, and dysprosium isotopes and N=96,98,100,102 isotone chains, and highlight the need for more comprehensive studies of $\varepsilon_{6}$ deformation effects in this region.
