# Dipole polarizability of 120Sn and nuclear energy density functionals

> The electric dipole strength distribution in 120Sn between 5 and 22 MeV has been determined at RCNP Osaka from a polarization transfer analysis of proton inelastic scattering at E_0 = 295 MeV and forward angles including 0°. Combined with photoabsorption data an electric dipole polarizability α_D(120Sn) = 8.93(36) fm^3 is extracted. The dipole polarizability as isovector observable par excellence carries direct information on the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence. The correlation of the new value with the well established α_D(208Pb) serves as a test of its prediction by nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs). Models based on modern Skyrme interactions describe the data fairly well while most calculations based on relativistic Hamiltonians cannot.

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability of 120Sn is measured with 4% precision, and when correlated with the well-established value for 208Pb, it tests nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs). The abstract's claim that "models based on modern Skyrme interactions describe the data fairly well while most calculations based on relativistic Hamiltonians cannot" glosses over the fact that this conclusion depends on the specific correlation between the two nuclei; the relativistic models are off-center in the correlation plot, with only one DD-PC parametrization (with J=32 MeV) lying within the experimental correlation box.

## Method

The electric dipole strength distribution in 120Sn between 5 and 22 MeV was determined at RCNP Osaka using polarized proton inelastic scattering at E0 = 295 MeV and forward angles including 0 degrees, with polarization transfer observables (DLL and DSS) measured to separate non-spinflip (E1) and spinflip (M1) contributions via the total spin transfer Σ. The E1 strength below 5 MeV was taken from (γ,γ') experiments, above neutron threshold from (γ,xn) experiments, and the dipole polarizability αD was extracted by integrating the photoabsorption cross section weighted by 1/Ex^2 over the full energy range up to 135 MeV.

## Key numbers

- αD(120Sn) = 8.93(36) fm^3, extracted with 4% precision
- Contribution to αD from 5 to 10 MeV: 1.12(7) fm^3 (about 12.5% of total)
- Contribution from the IVGDR region (10 to 22 MeV): 7.00(29) fm^3
- Contribution from 22 to 28.9 MeV: 0.51(6) fm^3
- Contribution from higher energies up to 135 MeV: 0.31(10) fm^3
- Neutron skin thickness r_skin derived from the correlation: 0.148(34) fm
- Summed B(E1) strength up to 9 MeV from MDA: 48.7(29) e^2fm^2; from PTA: 54.1(41) e^2fm^2

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to 208Pb by Tamii et al. (2011) to a new nucleus, 120Sn, providing a second precise data point for testing EDFs. The correlation between αD(120Sn) and αD(208Pb) serves as a critical test: modern Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) interactions (e.g., SV-min from Kortelainen et al. 2009, RD-min from Erler et al. 2010) describe the data well, while most relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (DD-ME, FSU, FSU2) fail to reproduce the correlation, indicating needed improvements in the isovector channel of RMF functionals. The result enables future systematic studies of dipole polarizability across the tin isotopic chain, including measurements of stable tin isotopes and relativistic Coulomb excitation of neutron-rich tin isotopes 124-134Sn at GSI.
