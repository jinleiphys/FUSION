# The triton with long-range chiral N3LO three nucleon forces

> Long-range contributions to the three-nucleon force that have been recently worked out in chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order are for the first time included in the triton and the doublet nucleon-deuteron scattering length calculations. The strengths of the two short-range terms available at this order in the chiral expansion are determined from the triton binding energy and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length. The structure of the resulting three-nucleon force is explored and effects for the two-nucleon correlation function in the triton are investigated. Expectation values of the individual contributions to the three-nucleon force in the triton are found to be in the range from a few 100 keV to about 1 MeV. Our study demonstrates that the very complicated operator structure of the novel chiral three-nucleon forces can be successively implemented in three-nucleon Faddeev calculations.

## Key claim

The paper implements for the first time the long-range three-nucleon force (3NF) contributions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in chiral effective field theory in triton and doublet nucleon-deuteron scattering length calculations, but the study is explicitly incomplete because the short-range one-pion-exchange-contact, two-pion-exchange-contact, and relativistic correction terms at N3LO are not yet available and therefore omitted.

## Method

The authors performed momentum-space Faddeev calculations for the triton bound state and neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length, incorporating the N3LO two-pion-exchange, two-pion-one-pion-exchange, and ring (three-pion-exchange) 3NF topologies combined with the next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) one-pion-exchange-contact and short-range contact terms. The partial wave decomposition of the complicated N3LO operator structures was carried out using the automatized partial wave decomposition (aPWD) method of Golak et al. (2010). The two low-energy constants c_D and c_E were fitted to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy and neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length for five different cutoff combinations (Λ, Λ̃) used to regularize both the NN potential and the 3NF.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy experimental value: E^3H = 8.481821(5) MeV, used as one of two fitting observables
- Neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length experimental value: ^2a_nd = 0.645(7) fm, used as the second fitting observable
- Expectation values of individual N3LO 3NF contributions in the triton range from a few 100 keV to about 1 MeV
- Three cutoff values Λ = 450, 550, and 600 MeV were used for regularization of the 3NF
- The LECs c_i used: c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.40 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.40 GeV^{-1}, with shifted values c̃_1 = -0.94 GeV^{-1}, c̃_3 = -2.51 GeV^{-1}, c̃_4 = 2.51 GeV^{-1}
- The NN potential cutoff Λ̃ from spectral function regularization takes five combinations with Λ as listed in Table 1 of the paper

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of three-nucleon forces from N2LO (Epelbaum et al., 2002) to the subleading N3LO order, where the long-range contributions derived by Bernard et al. (2008) are implemented for the first time in three-body calculations. The study demonstrates that the complicated operator structure of the N3LO 3NF can be handled numerically, enabling future complete N3LO calculations once the remaining short-range and relativistic correction terms (currently being worked out by Epelbaum, private communication) are included. This is a step toward reducing the theoretical uncertainty seen in spin observables for nucleon-deuteron scattering, where N2LO calculations show a relatively large spread compared to data.
