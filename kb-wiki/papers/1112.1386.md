# Electron shielding of vortons in high-density quark matter

> We consider the the effect of the electron cloud about a vorton in the CFL-$K^0$ high-density phase by numerically solving the ultrarelativistic Thomas-Fermi equation about a toroidal charge. Including electrons removes the electric monopole contribution to the energy, and noticeably decreases the equilibrium radius of these stable vortex loops.

## Key claim
Including the electron cloud around a vorton in the CFL-K⁰ phase removes the electric monopole contribution to the energy and significantly reduces the equilibrium radius of these stable vortex loops, compared to the unshielded case previously estimated in Bedaque (2011).

## Method
The authors numerically solved the ultrarelativistic Thomas-Fermi equation in toroidal coordinates for a toroidal charge representing the vorton, using a relaxation method on a 32×32 grid. The electron density was computed semiclassically from the local Fermi momentum, and the electric potential was found self-consistently from Gauss's law and the equilibrium condition that the Fermi energy minus the electric potential is constant. The resulting electric field energy was then used to replace the multipole-approximated electric energy in the vorton energy functional, which was minimized to find the equilibrium radius.

## Key numbers
- The numerical solutions achieved charge neutrality (total electron charge = -Z) to within 1.8% for every choice of parameters; only 2% of solutions disagreed by more than 1%.
- The Thomas-Fermi calculation was performed for radii R in the range [80, 800] fm in steps of 20 fm, string thickness δ in [1, 35] fm in steps of 1 fm, and charges Z in {250, 500, 750} ∪ ([1250, 50000] in steps of 1250).
- For small radii (R ≲ 100 fm), the electron-free multipole approximation overestimates the electric energy by at least a factor of 2; at larger radii the approximation is off by roughly a factor of 5.
- Example parameters used for comparison with unshielded vortons: gap Δ = 66 MeV, chemical potential μ = 450 MeV, quark masses (m_u, m_d, m_s) ≈ (2, 5, 95) MeV.
- For Z ≳ 6000, electron shielding shrinks the equilibrium radius; the effect becomes stronger for larger Z.

## Context
This work extends the quantitative theory of quark matter vortons developed in Bedaque (2011), which estimated equilibrium sizes using multipole approximations and neglected electron shielding. It addresses the concern raised by Buckley (2002) that currents necessary to stabilize vortons could quench superconductivity, and that electric charge could counteract this effect. The Thomas-Fermi approach used here is analogous to its application in atomic physics, but adapted to toroidal geometry. The results enable more realistic predictions for vorton stability in neutron star cores, where the CFL-K⁰ phase may be relevant.
