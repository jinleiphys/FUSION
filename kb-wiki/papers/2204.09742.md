# Time-like Gravitational Formfactors and Shear Viscosity

## Key claim
The gravitational formfactor analogous to shear viscosity can be identified in hadronic structure, and in the time-like region it corresponds to the production of exotic hybrid mesons with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\). The smallness of the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio may be due to the smallness of the hybrid meson coupling (approximately 50 MeV) and the hadronic "temperature" represented by the average intrinsic transverse momentum.

## Method
The author identifies the shear viscosity term in the energy-momentum tensor with a gravitational formfactor \(E(t)\) by equating the hadronic analog of the viscous term \(\eta \, \partial v^\nu / \partial x_\mu\) to \(E(t) P_\nu \Delta_\mu\). The ratio of shear viscosity \(\eta\) to entropy density \(s\) is then estimated as \(\eta/s \sim (E(t)/A(t)) \cdot (T/M)\), where \(A(t)\) is the formfactor from the standard liquid energy-momentum term. The analysis is extended to the time-like region by crossing, where the formfactor becomes a Generalized Distribution Amplitude (GDA) for the production of \(\pi\eta\) pairs with exotic quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- The hybrid meson coupling is approximately 50 MeV, as cited from Anikin (2006) and Anikin (2004).
- The ratio \(\eta/s\) is estimated as \(\eta/s \sim (E(t)/A(t)) \cdot (T/M)\), where \(T\) is the hadronic "temperature" and \(M\) is the hadron mass.
- When restoring fundamental constants, the estimate takes the form \(\eta/s \sim (\hbar/k_B) \cdot (E(t)/A(t)) \cdot (k_B T / M c^2)\).

## Context
This work extends the study of gravitational formfactors from the space-like region, previously applied to pressure distributions in hadrons (Polyakov 2018) and to exclusive pion pair production (Kumano 2017), into the time-like region where they connect to shear viscosity. The smallness of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio is linked to the holographic bound from Policastro (2001) and Kovtun (2004), and the exotic hybrid meson coupling is taken from Anikin (2006) and Anikin (2004). The results enable a new link between hadronic structure physics and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, with potential experimental tests using BELLE data and studies at the NICA complex at JINR.
