# Gauge dependence identities for color superconducting QCD

> Using generalized Nielsen identities a formal proof is given that the fermionic quasiparticle dispersion relations in a color superconductor are gauge independent. This turns out to involve gluonic tadpoles which are calculated to one-loop order in a two-flavor color superconductor. Regarding the appearance of gluon tadpoles, we argue that in QCD the color superconducting phase is automatically color neutral.

## Key claim

Using generalized Nielsen identities, the authors provide a formal proof that fermionic quasiparticle dispersion relations in color superconducting QCD are gauge independent, but this proof requires the inclusion of gluonic tadpoles which may be nonvanishing in the color superconducting phase; the gauge independence holds provided the singularities of the ghost propagator and the 1PI parts of the gauge variation do not coincide with those of the quark propagator.

## Method

The authors derive a gauge dependence identity (generalized Nielsen identity) for the effective action of QCD in the color superconducting phase, then take second functional derivatives to obtain an identity for the inverse quark propagator in the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism. They evaluate the determinant of the inverse propagator and show that its total variation under gauge fixing changes is proportional to the determinant times a trace of ghost-related quantities, implying that the zeros of the determinant (and thus the quasiparticle dispersion relations) are gauge independent. They also perform a one-loop calculation of the gluon tadpole in a two-flavor color superconductor using a specific ansatz for the gap function.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order gap in asymptotic QCD is \(\phi = (b_0/g^5) \mu \exp(-c/g)\) with \(c = 3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}\) (from Son (1998)).
* The one-loop gluon tadpole in a two-flavor color superconductor is approximately \(\mathcal{T}^a \simeq -(2g/\pi^2) (T^a)_{33} \, \mu \, (\phi_0^+)^2 \ln(\phi_0^+ / (2\mu)) + \mathcal{O}((g\mu\phi_0^+)^2)\), where \((T^a)_{33} = -\delta^{a8}/\sqrt{3}\).
* The effective chemical potential for color charge number eight arising from the gluon tadpole is of order \(\phi^2/(g\mu)\), where \(m_D \propto g\mu\) is the Debye mass.

## Context

This work extends the gauge independence proof for finite-temperature QCD given by Kobes, Kunstatter, and Rebhan (KKR) to the color superconducting phase, addressing the gauge dependence issues found in gap equation calculations by Rajagopal and Shuster (2000) for \(g > g_c \sim 0.8\). The authors argue that in QCD (unlike in NJL models where color neutrality must be imposed as an additional condition, as done by Steiner, Reddy, and Prakash (2002)), the color superconducting phase is automatically color neutral due to the integration over zero-momentum modes of \(A_0^a\) in the path integral. This enables a rigorous foundation for perturbative calculations of quasiparticle properties in color superconducting quark matter.
