# Electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei using chiral effective theory

> I describe the use of chiral effective theory (ChiET) to compute electromagnetic reactions in two- and three-nucleon systems. I first explain how chiral perturbation theory can be extended to the few-nucleon sector. I then explain the predictions of the resulting ChiET for electron-deuteron scattering, and how they will be tested by forthcoming data from BLAST. I conclude by displaying predictions for elastic Compton scattering from deuterium and Helium-3 nuclei. These computations, in concert with future data from MAX-Lab and HIGS, should give significant new information on neutron polarizabilities, and hence yield insight into the structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim
Chiral effective theory (ChiET) can provide a systematic, controlled description of electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei, but the predictions for deuteron form factors and Compton scattering carry residual uncertainties from short-distance physics and cutoff dependence that must be carefully managed, particularly for observables sensitive to two-pion-exchange contributions and Delta(1232) resonance effects.

## Method
The author extends chiral perturbation theory to the few-nucleon sector by applying the Weinberg prescription: the chiral expansion is applied to the NN potential rather than the NN amplitude, and this potential is then iterated via the Schrodinger equation to generate bound and scattering states. For electron-deuteron scattering, the deuteron charge operator is computed up to order P^3 relative to leading, using wave functions obtained from potentials that include one-pion exchange at leading order and two-pion exchange at next-to-leading order, with cutoff regularization. For Compton scattering on deuterium and Helium-3, the gamma-nucleus amplitude is computed by evaluating the operator O-hat up to order e^2 P (NLO) using ChiET wave functions, with the operator including both one-body nucleon Compton amplitudes and two-body pion-exchange currents.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy reproduced by adjusting constant C: B = 2.22457 MeV
- Deuteron asymptotic S-state normalization: A_S = 0.885 fm^{-1/2}
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio: eta = 0.0256
- Deuteron quadrupole moment experimental value: Q_d = 0.2859(3) fm^2
- For gamma-d Compton scattering at omega = 65 MeV, two-nucleon exchange currents provide about 50% of the differential cross section
- For gamma-d Compton scattering at omega = 94.5 MeV, the NLO prediction fails to describe backward-angle data, with the discrepancy attributed to Delta(1232) M1 excitation
- For gamma-d Compton scattering in the range omega = 50-100 MeV, theoretical accuracy of about 3% is achievable
- For deuteron form factor G_Q at |q| = 2 fm^{-1}, remaining theoretical uncertainty from short-distance NN physics is 3%
- For gamma-He3 scattering at 80 MeV, the differential cross section shows sensitivity to shifts in alpha_n and beta_n

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective theory approach pioneered by Weinberg (1990) for NN scattering to electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei. The calculations for deuteron electromagnetic form factors build on prior work by Beane (2002), Pavon (2005), and Birse (2006) on renormalization of the NN problem, and the predictions for G_C/G_Q will be tested by forthcoming BLAST data. The Compton scattering calculations extend earlier work by Beane (1999) and Beane (2005) on gamma-deuteron scattering, and the gamma-He3 calculations by Choudhury (2007) represent the first consistent ChiET calculation of an electromagnetic reaction on the three-body system. The results enable extraction of neutron polarizabilities from future experiments at MAX-Lab and HIgammaS, and provide a framework for understanding the interplay between pion-cloud mechanisms and other contributions to nucleon polarizabilities.
