# Estimation of the chiral magnetic effect considering the magnetic field response of the QGP medium

> The magnetic field plays a major role in the searching of the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. If the lifetime of the magnetic field is too short, as expected by simulations of the field in the vacuum, the chiral magnetic effect will be largely suppressed. However, the lifetime of the magnetic field will become longer when the QGP medium response is considered. We give an estimate of the effect, especially considering the magnetic field response of the QGP medium, and compare it with the experimental results of background-subtracted correlator $H$ at RHIC and LHC energies. The results show that our method explains better for the experimental results at the top RHIC energy than that of the LHC energy.

# Estimation of the chiral magnetic effect considering the magnetic field response of the QGP medium

## Key claim
The KMW model, when combined with a magnetic field that evolves under the assumption of an ideally conducting QGP plasma, explains the background-subtracted correlator H better at top RHIC energy (200 GeV Au-Au) than at LHC energy (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb), where the model fails to reproduce the centrality dependence. The authors note that the discrepancy at LHC may arise because the magnetic field is assumed homogeneous in space, an assumption that is not satisfied at higher energies.

## Method
The authors adopt the KMW model (Kharzeev, McLerran, Warringa 2007) to estimate the CME-induced charge separation, using the time-dependent magnetic field from an ideally conducting QGP medium. The magnetic field evolution is computed from Maxwell's equations with Bjorken longitudinal expansion and linearized transverse hydrodynamic flow, using initial magnetic fields from the Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution (Mo 2013). The undetermined parameters (screening length λ and constant κ) are fixed by fitting the experimental observable H_SS - H_OS, and the resulting correlators a_{++} and a_{+-} are compared to the background-subtracted correlator H for Au-Au at 200 GeV and Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers
- For Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the initial magnetic field eB_y^0 ranges from 2161.4 MeV² (0-5% centrality, b = 2.21 fm) to 2001.3 MeV² (50-60% centrality, b = 11.00 fm).
- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2760 GeV, the initial magnetic field eB_y^0 ranges from 700.8 MeV² (0-5% centrality, b = 2.43 fm) to 64.5 MeV² (50-60% centrality, b = 11.58 fm).
- The QGP formation time t_0 for Au-Au at 200 GeV ranges from 0.132 fm (0-5% centrality) to 0.205 fm (50-60% centrality); for Pb-Pb at 2760 GeV it ranges from 0.093 fm to 0.145 fm.
- For Cu-Cu collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the initial magnetic field eB_y^0 is 3 to 5 times lower than for Au-Au at the same energy and centrality, e.g., 619.7 MeV² (0-5%) vs. 2161.4 MeV² for Au-Au.
- The correlator |a_{+-}|/a_{++} increases with impact parameter b and with screening length λ, approaching 1 for no screening.

## Context
This work extends the KMW model (Kharzeev, McLerran, Warringa 2007) by incorporating the magnetic field response of the QGP medium, following earlier studies by Tuchin (2010, 2013, 2015) and Deng (2012) who showed that finite electric conductivity prolongs the magnetic field lifetime. The authors compare their results with the background-subtracted correlator H measured by STAR (Adamczyk 2014) for Au-Au at RHIC and by ALICE (2013) for Pb-Pb at LHC. The failure at LHC energy points to the need for spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field treatments in future work, and the method enables estimates for smaller systems such as Cu-Cu collisions where experimental data are absent.
