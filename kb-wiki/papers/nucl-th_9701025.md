# Coherence length of neutron superfluids

> The coherence length of superfluid neutron matter is calculated from the microscopic BCS wavefunction of a Cooper pair in momentum space making use of the Bonn meson-exchange potential. We find that the coherence length is proportional to the Fermi momentum-to pairing gap ratio, in good agreement with simple estimates used in the literature, and we establish the appropriate fitting constants using our numerical data. Our calculations can be applied to the problem of inhomogeneous superfluidity of hadronic matter in the crust of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The coherence length of superfluid neutron matter is proportional to the ratio of Fermi momentum to pairing gap, confirming simple estimates from the literature, but the authors establish specific fitting constants from their numerical data rather than assuming a universal constant value.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS equations for uniform neutron matter in momentum space using the Bonn A meson-exchange potential. They computed the Cooper pair wavefunction in momentum space as chi(k) = Delta(k)/E(k), then calculated the coherence length from the momentum-space integral of |d chi(k)/dk|^2 relative to |chi(k)|^2, which is equivalent to the mean squared pair separation in coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length values range from approximately 10 fm to 80 fm over the Fermi momentum range studied (k_F = 0.1 to 1.4 fm^{-1}), as shown in the final figure.
- The proportionality constant K in the relation xi = K * (k_F / Delta_F) was determined numerically, with values of theta = 0.814 and theta = 0.836 shown for two different fitting approaches (where theta relates to K).
- Calculations were performed for Fermi momenta k_F = 0.1, 0.4, 0.8, and 1.2 fm^{-1} as shown in the figures of chi(k) and r^2|phi(r)|^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier BCS calculations of neutron superfluidity (Elgaroy et al., 1996) by providing the first microscopic determination of the coherence length from the full momentum-dependent pairing gap, rather than relying on the simple constant-gap approximation found in textbooks (de Gennes, 1966). The results enable quantitative studies of inhomogeneous superfluidity in neutron star crusts, where the typical nuclear size (R_N approximately 4-6 fm) is comparable to the coherence length, a situation previously investigated by Noi et al. and by Petra et al. The coherence length is critical for determining vortex pinning forces in pulsar glitch models and for assessing whether Anderson's theorem applies to neutron star matter.
