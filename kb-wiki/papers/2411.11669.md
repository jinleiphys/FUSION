# Prospective report of the French QCD community to the ESPPU 2025 with respect to the program of the LHC Run 5 and beyond and future colliders at CERN

> This document summarizes the prospective physics plans of the French QCD and Heavy-Ion community, including the experimental programs at the LHC Run 5 and beyond and future colliders at CERN, within the context of the French contribution to the update of the European Strategy in Particle Physics (ESPPU 2025), as discussed in the workshop on European Strategy for Particle Physics Update 2025 organised by the QCD GdR in Oct. 2024.

## Key claim

This prospective report outlines the physics priorities of the French QCD and heavy-ion community for the LHC Run 5 and beyond, advocating for continued heavy-ion operations through the full LHC lifetime and for French participation in the ALICE3 and LHCb Upgrade 2 projects, while also supporting the future FCC-ee and FCC-hh colliders. The document is a community consensus statement rather than a paper presenting new results, and its recommendations are conditional on the outcomes of the European Strategy for Particle Physics Update 2025 process.

## Method

The report summarizes discussions from a workshop on the European Strategy for Particle Physics Update 2025 organized by the QCD GdR in October 2024. It reviews the physics case for heavy-ion collisions at the LHC through Run 5, including the need for varied collision systems (p-A, O-O, Ar-Ar, Xe-Xe, Pb-Pb) and the complementarity with the EIC program. The document describes the planned detector upgrades for ALICE3 (a next-generation dedicated heavy-ion detector) and LHCb Upgrade 2 (with MAPS-based trackers for high-multiplicity events), and outlines the French community's intended technical contributions to these projects.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at FCC-hh: nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy of 39 TeV, producing energy densities of about 40 GeV/fm³ at nominal times of τ₀ ≈ 1 fm/c
- Central Pb-Pb collisions at FCC-hh: about 500 charm quark pairs produced per collision
- FCC-hh QGP state duration: up to τ ≈ 15 fm/c
- FCC-ee: expected samples of 8×10¹² Z bosons, 5×10⁸ W bosons, and 2×10⁶ Higgs bosons
- LHCb-U2: instantaneous luminosity up to 1.5×10³⁴ cm⁻² s⁻¹ in pp collisions, targeting 300 fb⁻¹ total integrated luminosity
- ALICE3 Middle Layers and Outer Tracker: radial coverage from 7 cm to 80 cm, covering |η| < 1.5 with cylindrical layers and 1.5 < |η| < 3-4 with disks, about 55 m² of active silicon
- ALICE3 MAPS sensor requirements: spatial resolution < 10 μm, time resolution < 100 ns, material budget < 50 μm thickness per layer, power consumption O(20-40 mW/cm²), radiation tolerance < 10¹⁴ 1-MeV n_eq cm⁻²
- LHCb effective temperature range for QGP studies: T_eff ∈ [190, 225] MeV
- p-Pb collisions at FCC-hh: nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy of 63 TeV, probing gluon fractional momenta down to x ≈ 10⁻⁷

## Context

This report extends the community planning discussions from the previous European Strategy update (European Strategy for Particle Physics Preparatory Group, 2019) and builds on the ALICE3 Letter of Intent (ALICE, 2022). It complements the ongoing heavy-ion program at RHIC and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider in the USA, while advocating for continued European leadership in QGP physics through the full LHC lifetime and into the FCC era. The document enables future decisions on detector construction (ALICE3, LHCb Upgrade 2) and accelerator development (FCC-ee, FCC-hh, LHeC) by articulating the French community's priorities and technical capabilities.
