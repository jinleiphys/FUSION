# Pion-assisted $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ dibaryons, and beyond

> Experimental evidence for $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$ $ΔΔ$ dibaryon ${\cal D}_{03}(2370)$ has been presented recently by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration. Here I review new hadronic-basis Faddeev calculations of $L=0$ nonstrange pion-assisted $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ dibaryon candidates. These calculations are so far the only ones to reproduce the relatively small ${\cal D}_{03}(2370)$ width of 70--80 MeV. Predictions are also given for the location and width of ${\cal D}_{30}$, the $I(J^P)=3(0^+)$ exotic partner of ${\cal D}_{03}(2370)$. Extensions to strangeness ${\cal S}=-1$ dibaryons are briefly discussed.

# Pion-assisted \(N\Delta\) and \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryons, and beyond

## Key claim

The paper argues that hadronic-basis Faddeev calculations of pion-assisted \(N\Delta\) and \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryons are the only ones so far to reproduce the relatively small width of the \({\cal D}_{03}(2370)\) \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryon (70–80 MeV), a value considerably below the phase-space estimate of \(\Gamma_\Delta \leq \Gamma({\cal D}_{03}) \leq 2\Gamma_\Delta\) with \(\Gamma_\Delta \approx 118\) MeV. The calculations also predict the location and width of the exotic \(I(J^P)=3(0^+)\) partner \({\cal D}_{30}\), with the caveat that the input \((I,S)=(2,1)\) \(N\Delta'\) interaction was taken the same as for \((I,S)=(1,2)\) due to the absence of \(NN\) observables to constrain it.

## Method

The author reviews Faddeev calculations for \(L=0\) nonstrange dibaryons using relativistic kinematics in a \(\pi NN\) three-body system, where the \(\pi N\) subsystem is dominated by the \(P_{33}\) \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and the \(NN\) subsystem by the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels. For the \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryon \({\cal D}_{03}\), a \(\pi N\Delta'\) three-body model was solved where \(\Delta'\) is a stable \(\Delta(1232)\) and the \(N\Delta'\) interaction is dominated by the \({\cal D}_{12}\) dibaryon, fitted within a \(NN\)–\(\pi NN\)–\(N\Delta'\) coupled-channel caricature model to the \(NN\) \(^1D_2\) \(T\)-matrix. The coupled Faddeev equations yield effective Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the \(S\)-matrix poles of the dibaryon candidates.

## Key numbers

* \({\cal D}_{03}(2370)\) \(\Delta\Delta\) dibaryon: mass approximately 2370 MeV, width \(\Gamma \approx 70\)–80 MeV, as reported by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration from \(pn \to d\pi\pi\) reactions.
* \({\cal D}_{12}\) \(N\Delta\) dibaryon pole: \(W = 2147 - i60\) MeV (for large form factor) and \(W = 2159 - i70\) MeV (for small form factor), compared to phenomenological values \(W = 2148 - i63\) MeV (Arndt 1987) and \(W = 2144 - i55\) MeV (Hoshizaki 1992).
* \({\cal D}_{21}\) \(N\Delta\) dibaryon pole: \(W = 2165 - i64\) MeV (large form factor) and \(W = 2169 - i69\) MeV (small form factor).
* \({\cal D}_{03}\) calculated pole with width-suppression factor \(x=2/3\): average mass 2363 MeV, average width 66 MeV (from \(W_{\rm av} = 2363 - i33\) MeV).
* \({\cal D}_{30}\) calculated pole with width-suppression factor \(x=2/3\): average mass 2390 MeV, average width 64 MeV (from \(W_{\rm av} = 2390 - i32\) MeV), approximately 30 MeV above \({\cal D}_{03}\).
* The \(\Delta(1232)\) free-space width: \(\Gamma_\Delta \approx 118\) MeV.
* The spectator-\(\Delta'\) complex mass used in the propagator: \(W(\Delta') = 1211 - i x 49.5\) MeV, with \(x=1\) or \(x=2/3\).
* The momentum-space soft cutoff for the \(N\Delta'\) separable-interaction form factor: \(\Lambda \leq 3\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the 1964 Dyson-Xuong SU(6)-based classification of nonstrange \(s\)-wave dibaryons, which predicted \({\cal D}_{12}\) and \({\cal D}_{03}\) using a mass formula with constant \(B \approx 47\) MeV. The hadronic-basis calculations reviewed here reproduce the experimentally established \({\cal D}_{12}\) (from \(pp({^1D_2}) \leftrightarrow \pi d({^3P_2})\) coupled-channel analyses by Arndt (1987) and Hoshizaki (1992)) and the \({\cal D}_{03}\) observed by WASA-at-COSY (2011, 2013, 2014), while quark-based calculations by H. Huang et al. (2013) found widths twice as large (150–200 MeV) and only marginal contributions from six-quark hidden-color configurations. The methodology enables predictions for exotic dibaryons such as \({\cal D}_{30}\) and can be extended to strangeness \({\cal S} = -1\) dibaryons, where a \(\pi YN\) coupled-channel Faddeev calculation predicted a dibaryon resonance 10–20 MeV below the \(\pi\Sigma N\) threshold, though a search by the HADES Collaboration at GSI (2014) found no signal.
