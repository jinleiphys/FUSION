# Inclusive and pion production neutrino-nucleus cross sections

> We analyze the experimental data on the inclusive double differential cross section by neutrinos charged current, measured by T2K, with the same model which was successful for the MiniBooNE quasielastic cross sections. As in our previous analysis the multinucleon component is needed in order to reproduce the data. For the total cross section our evaluation is smaller than the SciBooNE data above 1 GeV. This indicates the opening of a new channel not included in our evaluation, presumably the two pion emission channel. We also check that our description holds for the exclusive single pion production channel by confronting our evaluation with the MiniBooNE double differential cross section for a single charged pion and the Q^2 distribution. Both are compatible with data.

# Inclusive and pion production neutrino-nucleus cross sections

## Key claim
The same nuclear model that successfully described MiniBooNE quasielastic cross sections also reproduces T2K inclusive double differential data and MiniBooNE single-pion production data, but only when multinucleon (np-nh) components are included. The model systematically underestimates the total cross section above 1 GeV, which the authors attribute to the missing two-pion emission channel rather than to a failure of the existing framework.

## Method
The authors compute neutrino-nucleus cross sections using nuclear response functions in a Fermi gas model, with the quasielastic response treated in the random phase approximation (RPA) following Alberico et al. (1981). The multinucleon (2p-2h) contribution is taken from the microscopic calculation of Alberico et al. (1983) on inclusive electron scattering, and single-pion production is assumed to arise exclusively from Delta resonance decay, with in-medium Delta width parametrized following Oset and Salcedo (1987). The model is compared to T2K flux-averaged inclusive double differential cross sections on carbon and to MiniBooNE flux-averaged charged-current single-pion production data on CH₂.

## Key numbers
- T2K neutrino beam peaks at E_ν ≈ 600 MeV, similar to MiniBooNE but narrower and closer to monochromatic.
- The model fits T2K inclusive double differential data up to E_ν ≈ 1 GeV but underestimates the SciBooNE total cross section above 1 GeV.
- For the forward angular bin (0.94 < cosθ < 1 in T2K, 0.95 < cosθ < 1 in MiniBooNE), some underevaluation by the theory appears, potentially due to missing low-energy collective giant resonance excitations (10–30 MeV).
- In single-pion production, the model reproduces MiniBooNE double differential cross sections as a function of muon kinetic energy and angle, and the Q² distribution, but the total cross section fit is of inferior quality, with data below predictions at low energy and above predictions above 1 GeV.
- The coherent pion production contribution is significant but not dominant in the most forward bin (0.95 < cosθ < 1).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analyses of MiniBooNE quasielastic-like cross sections (Martini et al., 2009, 2010, 2011, 2013) to T2K inclusive data and MiniBooNE single-pion production data, providing the first test of the multinucleon emission necessity with a different neutrino flux. The missing two-pion channel above 1 GeV was also noted by Nieves et al. (2011) and Buss et al. (2011). The forward-angle sensitivity to coherent pion production and the spin-longitudinal response connects to theoretical work by Delorme and Ericson (1985) on the pion branch collective state. The results enable future studies of two-pion production in neutrino interactions, which has been evaluated near threshold by Hernandez et al. (2007) but requires extension to higher energies, and motivate improved angular resolution to isolate coherent pion production.
