# The Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti Sum Rule Revisited

> The Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule for pion photoproduction on the nucleon is evaluated by dispersion relations at constant t, and the corrections to the sum rule due to the finite pion mass are calculated. Near threshold these corrections turn out to be large due to pion-loop effects, whereas the sum rule value is closely approached if the dispersion integrals are evaluated for sub-threshold kinematics. This extension to the unphysical region provides a unique framework to determine the low-energy constants of chiral perturbation theory by global properties of the excitation spectrum.

## Key claim

The Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule for pion photoproduction on the nucleon, which relates the anomalous magnetic moment to a dispersion integral over the imaginary part of the invariant amplitude \(A_1\), receives large finite-pion-mass corrections near threshold due to pion-loop effects; however, the sum rule value is closely approached when the dispersion integrals are evaluated for sub-threshold kinematics, specifically at \(\nu=0\) along the path \(t=t_{\rm{thr}}\).

## Method

The authors evaluated the FFR sum rule using fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations at \(t=t_{\rm{thr}}\), the only constant-\(t\) path where the imaginary part of the amplitude is fully contained in the physical region of the Mandelstam plane. They computed the finite-mass corrections \(\Delta_N(\nu,t_{\rm{thr}})\) from the loop and counterterm contributions of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) at fourth order in \(1/M_N\), and compared these to the dispersion integral evaluated with the MAID03 unitary isobar model for the imaginary parts of the amplitudes. The invariant amplitude \(A_1\) was reconstructed from S-, P-, and D-wave multipoles at threshold.

## Key numbers

* The isovector anomalous magnetic moment from the FFR sum rule in the soft-pion limit is \(\kappa^V = \kappa_p - \kappa_n = 3.706\); the isoscalar value is \(\kappa^S = \kappa_p + \kappa_n = -0.120\).
* For the proton, the dispersion integral at \(\nu = \nu_{\rm{thr}}\) yields \(\kappa_p + \Delta_p = 2.24\), compared to the soft-pion limit value of 1.793; at \(\nu = 0\) the dispersion integral gives 1.66.
* For the neutron, the dispersion integral at \(\nu = \nu_{\rm{thr}}\) yields \(-\kappa_n + \Delta_n = 2.44\), compared to the soft-pion limit value of 1.913; at \(\nu = 0\) the dispersion integral gives 1.82.
* The finite-mass correction \(\Delta_p(\nu_{\rm{thr}}, t_{\rm{thr}})\) is approximately 0.5 for the proton and approximately 0.65 for the neutron; the correction vanishes at \(\nu \approx 70\) MeV.
* The threshold value of the S-wave multipole \(E_{0+}^p\) from HBChPT is \(-1.23 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\)), in agreement with the experimental value \(-1.23 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.03 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).
* The experimental threshold value of \(\kappa_p + \Delta_p\) reconstructed from multipole data is 2.16, decomposed as 2.06 from the S-wave, 0.26 from \(\bar{P}_2\), \(-0.19\) from \(\bar{P}_3\), and 0.03 from the D-wave.

## Context

This work extends the original FFR sum rule derived by Fubini, Furlan, and Rossetti (1965) on the basis of current algebra and PCAC in the soft-pion limit, and the subsequent evaluation by Adler and Gilman (1966) using an early multipole analysis. It also updates the more recent evaluation by Arndt and Workman (1995) using the VPI data basis, which gave \(\kappa^V = 3.92\) and \(\kappa^S = -0.138\). The study provides a framework to determine the low-energy constants of chiral perturbation theory from global properties of the nucleon excitation spectrum, and it highlights the need for a fully relativistic treatment of ChPT in the unphysical region far below pion threshold, as the nonrelativistic approximations of HBChPT become problematic there.
