# Rotating NSs/QSs and recent astrophysical observations

> Both fast and slow configurations of rotating neutron stars (NSs) are studied with the recently-constructed unified NS EoSs. The calculations for pure quark stars (QSs) and hybrid stars (HSs) are also done, using several updated quark matter EoSs and Gibbs construction for obtaining hadron-quark mixed phase. All three types of EoSs fulfill the recent 2-solar-mass constrain. By confronting the glitch observations with the theoretical calculations for the crustal moment of inertia (MoI), we find that the glitch crisis is still present in Vela-like pulsars. An upcoming accurate MoI measurement (eg., a possible 10\% accuracy for pulsar PSR J0737-3039A) could distinguish QSs from NSs, since the MoIs of QSs are generally $> \sim 1.5 $ times larger than NSs and HSs, no matter the compactness and the mass of the stars. Using tabulated EoSs, we compute stationary and equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating, relativistic stars in general relativity from the well-tested $rns$ code, assuming the matter comprising the star to be a perfect fluid. All three observed properties of the short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs) internal plateaus sample are simulated using the rotating configurations of NSs/QSs as basic input. We finally argue that for some characteristic SGRBs, the post-merger products of NS-NS mergers are probably supramassive QSs rather than NSs, and NS-NS mergers are a plausible location for quark deconfinement and the formation of QSs.

## Key claim

The authors argue that for some characteristic short gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs), the post-merger products of neutron star mergers are probably supramassive quark stars rather than neutron stars, and that neutron star mergers are a plausible location for quark deconfinement and the formation of quark stars. This claim is hedged by noting that quantitative differences would exist for other quark star model equations of state, but the same conclusions should hold because of similar quark star rotational properties.

## Method

The authors computed stationary and equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating relativistic stars in general relativity using the well-tested rns code, assuming the matter to be a perfect fluid. They used unified neutron star equations of state (BCPM, BSk20, BSk21, Shen) and quark star equations of state (CIDDM, CDDM1, CDDM2) with Gibbs construction for hadron-quark mixed phase in hybrid stars. They confronted glitch observations with theoretical calculations for crustal moment of inertia, and performed Monte Carlo simulations to reproduce observed properties of the Swift SGRB internal plateaus sample.

## Key numbers

- The Vela pulsar spin period is 89.33 milliseconds.
- For the BSk20 neutron star equation of state, the fractional crustal moment of inertia ranges from 5.03% at 1.0 solar masses to 1.16% at 2.0 solar masses.
- The predicted fractional moments of inertia based on three unified neutron star equations of state (BCPM, BSk21, BSk20) are no more than 6.48%.
- The glitch constraint with crustal entrainment shifts to I_c/I greater than or equal to 7%, which could hardly be fulfilled by a pulsar heavier than 1.0 solar masses except a narrow range of 1.0 to 1.06 solar masses in the matched BHF+NV+BPS equation of state case.
- The moments of inertia of quark stars are generally greater than approximately 1.5 times larger than those of neutron stars and hybrid stars.
- The Keplerian spin limits for neutron star equations of state range from 0.5391 ms (BSk20) to 0.7143 ms (Shen), and for quark star equations of state from 0.8326 ms (CIDDM) to 1.1249 ms (CDDM2).
- The static gravitational maximum masses range from 1.98 solar masses (BCPM) to 2.45 solar masses (CDDM2).
- The allowed static gravitational maximum mass range from the supramassive star fraction constraint is 2.17 to 2.45 solar masses.
- The increase of maximum mass with frequency is approximately 40% for quark stars versus 18-20% for neutron stars.
- The observed supramassive star fraction is approximately 22%.
- For the internal plateau sample, the initial spin periods for quark star scenarios range from 0.95-1.05 ms (CIDDM) to 1.10-1.70 ms (CDDM2), with surface dipole magnetic fields around 10^15 G.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotating neutron stars and quark stars by using recently-constructed unified equations of state that satisfy the 2-solar-mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al. 2010; Fonseca et al. 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al. 2013). The glitch crisis analysis builds on earlier work by Link et al. (1999) and Chamel (2012, 2013). The moment of inertia calculations follow studies by Lattimer and Schutz (2005) and Bejger and Haensel (2004), and the SGRB internal plateau modeling extends previous work by using both neutron star and quark star equations of state. The results suggest that upcoming accurate moment of inertia measurements, such as a possible 10% accuracy for pulsar PSR J0737-3039A, could distinguish quark stars from neutron stars and hybrid stars.
