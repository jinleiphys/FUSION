# Color transparency and short-range correlations in exclusive pion photo- and electroproduction from nuclei

> A relativistic and quantum mechanical framework to compute nuclear transparencies for pion photo- and electroproduction reactions is presented. Final-state interactions for the ejected pions and nucleons are implemented in a relativistic eikonal approach. At sufficiently large ejectile energies, a relativistic Glauber model can be adopted. At lower energies, the framework possesses the flexibility to use relativistic optical potentials. The proposed model can account for the color-transparency (CT) phenomenon and short-range correlations (SRC) in the nucleus. Results are presented for kinematics corresponding to completed and planned experiments at Jefferson Lab. The influence of CT and SRC on the nuclear transparency is studied. Both the SRC and CT mechanisms increase the nuclear transparency. The two mechanisms can be clearly separated, though, as they exhibit a completely different dependence on the hard scale parameter. The nucleon and pion transparencies as computed in the relativistic Glauber approach are compared with optical-potential and semi-classical calculations. The similarities in the trends and magnitudes of the nuclear transparencies indicate that they are not subject to strong model dependencies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic and quantum mechanical framework for computing nuclear transparencies in exclusive pion photo- and electroproduction that can simultaneously account for color transparency (CT) and short-range correlations (SRC), with the hedge that both mechanisms increase transparency but can be clearly separated through their different dependence on the hard scale parameter.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic eikonal approach using a Relativistic Multiple-Scattering Glauber Approximation (RMSGA) for high-energy ejectiles and a Relativistic Optical Model Eikonal Approximation (ROMEA) for lower energies, with bound-state wave functions from a relativistic mean-field model (Walecka model with W1 parametrization). Final-state interactions for ejected pions and nucleons were implemented through Glauber eikonal phase operators, with the pion-nucleon scattering parameters (total cross section, slope parameter, and ratio of real to imaginary parts) fitted to Particle Data Group data. Short-range correlations were incorporated through a central correlation function inducing density correlations into the final system, while color transparency was modeled through the reduction of final-state interactions at high momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Proton kinetic energies down to about 300 MeV are the threshold below which the Glauber model approximations become inapplicable, corresponding to de Broglie wavelengths of about 1.5 fm
- For pions, comparable wavelengths are reached for kinetic energies of about 700 MeV
- Results are presented for kinematics corresponding to completed and planned experiments at Jefferson Lab, including the $^4\text{He}(\gamma,p\pi^-)$ reaction and $A(e,e'\pi^+ n)$ reactions on $^2\text{H}$, $^{12}\text{C}$, $^{27}\text{Al}$, $^{63}\text{Cu}$ and $^{197}\text{Au}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic model for pion photoproduction (Cosyn, 2006) to electroproduction reactions and provides a more detailed formalism than the semi-classical model of Gao, Holt and Pandharipande (1996). The framework enables comparison with completed Jefferson Lab experiments on $^4\text{He}(\gamma,\pi^- p)$ (Dutta, 2003) and planned electroproduction measurements (Clasie, 2007), while the treatment of short-range correlations builds on approaches developed for $A(e,e'p)$ reactions by Nikolaev (1993), Frankel (1994), and Alvioli (2003).
