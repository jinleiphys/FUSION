# Quartet of spin-3/2 baryons in chiral multiplet $(1, 1/2) \oplus (1/2, 1) $ with mirror assignment

> We study the possible existence of chiral partners in the spin-$\thalf$ sector of the baryon spectrum. We consider a quartet scheme where four spin-3/2 baryons, $P_{33}$, $D_{33}$, $D_{13}$ and $P_{13}$, group into higher-dimensional chiral multiplets $(1, \half)\oplus (\half,1)$ with a mirror assignment. With an effective $SU(2)_R\times SU(2)_L$ Lagrangian, we derive constraints imposed by chiral symmetry together with the mirror assignment on the masses and coupling constants of the quartet. Using the effective Lagrangian, we try to find a set of baryons suitable for the chiral quartet. It turns out that two cases reasonably agree with the mass pattern of the quartet: ($Δ(1600)$, $Δ(1940)$, $N(1520)$, $N(1720)$) and ($Δ(1920)$, $Δ(1940)$, $N(2080)$, $N(1900)$).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that two specific quartets of spin-3/2 baryons, (Δ(1600), Δ(1940), N(1520), N(1720)) and (Δ(1920), Δ(1940), N(2080), N(1900)), can be understood as chiral partners forming a (1, 1/2) ⊕ (1/2, 1) multiplet with mirror assignment, but only "reasonably agree with the mass pattern" rather than providing exact fits, and the analysis is limited to on-shell states using an effective Lagrangian approach.

## Method

The author constructs an effective SU(2)_R × SU(2)_L Lagrangian for spin-3/2 baryons using a projection method that decomposes quark-diquark products into irreducible chiral representations. Two sets of baryon fields are introduced, a "naive" set (N_1^μ, Δ_1^{μi}) and a "mirror" set (N_2^μ, Δ_2^{μi}) with opposite axial transformation properties, and their mixing via a chiral-scalar mirror mass m_0 generates physical positive- and negative-parity states. Mass matrices are diagonalized to obtain mass eigenvalues and mixing angles, and the Goldberger-Treiman relations connect the resulting axial charges to π-baryon coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- For the naive sector alone, the mass relation |m_{Δ1}| : |m_{N1}| = 2 : 1 is derived; assigning N(1520) as N_1 gives a predicted Δ_1 mass of approximately 3000 MeV, for which no observed baryon is found.
- The mass constraints from the mirror assignment are: (m_{Δ+} + m_{Δ-}) ≥ (m_{N+} + m_{N-}) and m_{Δ-} − m_{Δ+} = 2(m_{N+} − m_{N-}).
- In the m_0 → 0 limit, the axial charges reach maximum magnitudes: |g_A^{Δ±Δ±}| → 1, |g_A^{N*±N*±}| → 5/3, and |g_A^{N*±Δ±}| → 4/√3.
- Two candidate quartets are identified: (Δ(1600), Δ(1940), N(1520), N(1720)) and (Δ(1920), Δ(1940), N(2080), N(1900)).

## Context

This work extends the quartet scheme (QS) first proposed by Jido et al. (1999) for spin-1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 baryons, and builds on the author's own projection method (Nagata 2007, 2008) that incorporates Dirac structure into the Lagrangian, enabling transition terms between J=1/2 and J=3/2 states. The paper tests whether the approximate degeneracy of four high-mass baryons noted by Glozman (2007) as a possible signature of effective chiral restoration can be explained by the chiral quartet mechanism, and provides a framework to test other assignments beyond the previously studied set (Δ(1232), Δ(1700), N(1520), N(1720)).
