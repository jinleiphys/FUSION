# Quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We study an azimuthally-symmetric boost-invariant quark-gluon plasma using quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics including the effects of both shear and bulk viscosities. We compare results obtained using the quasiparticle method with the standard anisotropic hydrodynamics and viscous hydrodynamics. We consider the predictions of the three methods for the differential particle spectra and mean transverse momentum. We find that the three methods agree for small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, but show differences at large $η/s$. Additionally, we find that the standard anisotropic hydrodynamics method shows suppressed production at low transverse-momentum compared to the other two methods, and the bulk-viscous correction can drive the primordial particle spectra negative at large $p_T$ in viscous hydrodynamics.

# Quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

For small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s), quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP), standard anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), and second-order viscous hydrodynamics (vHydro) agree well; however, at large η/s, the standard aHydro method shows suppressed particle production at low transverse momentum, and the bulk-viscous correction in vHydro can drive primordial particle spectra negative at high pT.

## Method

The authors model an azimuthally-symmetric, boost-invariant quark-gluon plasma using three approaches: quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP), which imposes a realistic lattice-based equation of state via a temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass; standard anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), which assumes approximate conformality; and second-order viscous hydrodynamics (vHydro). All three methods use the relaxation time approximation for the collision kernel and perform anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out, with aHydro and aHydroQP using a distribution function that remains positive definite in all phase-space regions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T₀ = 600 MeV at proper time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c; freeze-out temperature T_FO = 150 MeV for all cases.
- For 4πη/s = 3, the vHydro differential pion spectrum becomes negative at pT ∼ 1.6 GeV.
- For 4πη/s ∼ 5, the vHydro mean transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ for pions becomes negative.
- Comparisons are shown for 4πη/s = 1 and 4πη/s = 3 for pion, kaon, and proton spectra.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics program (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010, 2012, Ryblewski 2012, Bazow 2013, Nopoush 2014) by incorporating a realistic lattice QCD equation of state (Borsanyi 2010) via quasiparticle masses, addressing the non-conformality of the QGP. It compares aHydroQP with standard aHydro and second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Denicol 2012, 2014), showing that aHydro methods avoid the unphysical negative spectra that appear in vHydro at large η/s. The results enable future extension of aHydroQP to 3+1d simulations for more realistic heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
