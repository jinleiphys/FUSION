# Transition Distribution Amplitudes : from JLab to EIC

> Baryon-to-meson Transition Distribution Amplitudes extend both the concepts of generalized parton distributions and baryon distribution amplitudes encoding valuable complementary information on the 3-dimensional hadronic structure. The recent analysis of backward meson electroproduction at JLab supports the hope to perform femto-photography of hadrons from a brand new perspective.and in particular opens a new domain for EIC experiments.

# Transition Distribution Amplitudes: from JLab to EIC

## Key claim
Baryon-to-meson Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) provide a complementary framework to generalized parton distributions for probing the three-dimensional hadronic structure, with recent JLab data supporting the applicability of the TDA formalism at moderate Q² values, though a more detailed experimental analysis is required for confirmation of the concept's relevance for hard processes.

## Method
The authors analyze backward meson electroproduction reactions (γ* N → π N' and γ* N → ω p) using collinear QCD factorization, where the hard scattering amplitude factorizes into a coefficient function convolved with a baryon distribution amplitude and a baryon-to-meson TDA. The TDA formalism involves a three-quark operator on the light cone, with matrix elements taken between a baryon and a meson state, characterized by the skewness variable ξ and transverse momentum transfer Δ_T. Experimental separation of transverse (σ_T) and longitudinal (σ_L) cross sections is performed to test the predicted dominance of the transverse amplitude.

## Key numbers
- CLAS collaboration measured the Q²-dependence of σ_T + ε σ_L for e p → e' n π⁺ at Q² > 1.7 GeV², with ⟨-u⟩ = 0.5 GeV² and W² > 4 GeV²
- Hall C experiment at JLab measured σ_T and σ_L for e p → e' p' ω, demonstrating σ_T dominates over σ_L at sufficiently large Q²
- The near-backward kinematics is defined by small -u = -(p₁ - p_π)², with the quoted value ⟨-u⟩ = 0.5 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the GPD-based description of hard exclusive near-forward meson electroproduction (Pire 2016) to the complementary near-backward kinematical regime, replacing the quark-antiquark operator with a three-quark operator. The TDA concept shares features with both baryon distribution amplitudes (baryon-to-vacuum matrix elements) and generalized parton distributions. The experimental results from CLAS (Park 2017) and Hall C (Li-Huber) provide first indications of TDA validity, and the authors anticipate that the Electron-Ion Collider will enable further exploration at higher Q² in the backward region, while noting that detailed predictions for higher energies have not yet been worked out.
