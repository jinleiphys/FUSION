# The possibility to study in-medium modification of $J/ψ$ mesons from their photoproduction on nuclei near threshold in the case of presence of the LHCb pentaquark states $P^+_c$ in this photoproduction

> We study the $J/ψ$ photoproduction from nuclei in the near-threshold beam energy region of $E_γ$=5--11 GeV within the nuclear spectral function approach by considering incoherent direct ($γN \to {J/ψ}N$) and two-step ($γp \to P^+_c(4312)$, $P^+_c(4312) \to {J/ψ}p$; $γp \to P^+_c(4440)$, $P^+_c(4440) \to {J/ψ}p$; $γp \to P^+_c(4457)$, $P^+_c(4457) \to {J/ψ}p$) $J/ψ$ production processes. We calculate the absolute excitation functions for the non-resonant and resonant production of $J/ψ$ mesons off $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei within the different scenarios for in-medium modification of the directly photoproduced $J/ψ$ mesons and for branching ratios of the decays $P^+_c(4312) \to {J/ψ}p$, $P^+_c(4440) \to {J/ψ}p$ and $P^+_c(4457) \to {J/ψ}p$. We show that the non-resonant subthreshold $J/ψ$ production in $γA$ reactions reveals some sensitivity to employed in-medium modification scenarios for $J/ψ$ mesons, which is not masked by the resonantly produced $J/ψ$ mesons, only if above branching ratios are less than 5\%. We also demonstrate that if these branching ratios more than 5\% then the presence of the $P^+_c(4312)$, $P^+_c(4440)$ and $P^+_c(4457)$ resonances in $J/ψ$ photoproduction on nuclei produces above threshold additional enhancements in the behavior of the total $J/ψ$ creation cross section on nuclei, which could be also studied in the future JLab experiments at the CEBAF facility to provide both further evidence for their existence and valuable information on their nature.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of subthreshold non-resonant J/ψ photoproduction on nuclei to in-medium J/ψ mass shifts can be experimentally distinguishable only if the branching ratios of the decays Pc+(4312) → J/ψ p, Pc+(4440) → J/ψ p, and Pc+(4457) → J/ψ p are all less than 5%; if these branching ratios exceed 5%, the resonant contributions mask the in-medium modification signal.

## Method

The author extended the nuclear spectral function approach of Paryev (2019) to include two-step resonant production channels γ p → Pc+(4312) → J/ψ p, γ p → Pc+(4440) → J/ψ p, and γ p → Pc+(4457) → J/ψ p alongside the direct non-resonant γ N → J/ψ N process. The free elementary cross section for γ N → J/ψ N was parameterized as an incoherent sum of two-gluon and three-gluon exchange contributions, fitted to GlueX data at 8.38 and 11.62 GeV. The in-medium J/ψ mass was approximated by an average value with a scalar potential V0 at normal nuclear density, and the resonant production was modeled using Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the pentaquark states with vacuum widths Γc1=9.8 MeV, Γc2=20.6 MeV, and Γc3=6.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The free J/ψ meson mass mJ/ψ is modified by an effective scalar potential V0 at normal nuclear density ρ0; options considered: V0 = 0, -25, -50, -100, and -150 MeV.
- The J/ψ–nucleon absorption cross section used is σJ/ψ N = 3.5 mb.
- The exponential t-slope parameter for the differential cross section of γ p → J/ψ p is b ≈ 1.67 GeV−2.
- The normalization coefficients for the elementary cross section are a2g = 44.780 nb/GeV2 and a3g = 2.816 nb/GeV2.
- The pole masses of the pentaquark states are M_c1 = 4311.9 MeV, M_c2 = 4440.3 MeV, and M_c3 = 4457.3 MeV; their vacuum total decay widths are Γc1 = 9.8 MeV, Γc2 = 20.6 MeV, and Γc3 = 6.4 MeV.
- The resonant threshold energies for photoproduction on a free proton at rest are EγR1 = 9.44 GeV, EγR2 = 10.04 GeV, and EγR3 = 10.12 GeV.
- The Pc+–nucleon inelastic cross section used is σPci N = 33.5 mb; the effective absorption cross section averaged over the three states is approximately 37 mb.
- At incident photon energy of 5 GeV, the J/ψ non-resonant yield is enhanced by a factor of about 4.0 for V0 = -50 MeV compared to V0 = 0; enhancement factors from V0 = -50 to -100 MeV and from -100 to -150 MeV are about 3.5 and 2.5, respectively.
- At beam energy of 8 GeV, the enhancement factors for the same mass shift steps are about 1.3, 1.3, and 1.25.
- The non-resonant J/ψ production cross sections at beam energies of 7–8 GeV have measurable strength of approximately 0.1–40 nb.

## Context

This work extends the model of Paryev (2019), which studied the role of the previously claimed Pc+(4450) resonance, to incorporate the three new narrow pentaquark states Pc+(4312), Pc+(4440), and Pc+(4457) reported by the LHCb Collaboration (2019). The calculations use the nuclear spectral function approach and incorporate new GlueX experimental data for the γ p → J/ψ p reaction. The results enable future JLab CEBAF experiments at up to 12 GeV to search for pentaquark signals in nuclear targets and to extract information on J/ψ in-medium mass shifts, provided the branching ratios of the pentaquark decays to J/ψ p are sufficiently small.
