# Vorticity-induced effects from Wess-Zumino-Witten terms

> We study vorticity-induced effects arising from the Wess-Zumino-Witten terms for Nambu-Goldstone modes in chiral perturbation theory. We first provide an alternative derivation of the Wess-Zumino-Witten terms in the presence of external vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar fields using a derivative expansion of the fermion determinant. We then employ the previously found correspondence in which vorticity is treated as an axial-vector field coupled to Dirac fermions in flat spacetime. Using this, we derive vorticity-induced contributions for Nambu-Goldstone modes in the presence of electromagnetic fields at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials, including a vorticity-induced current, a magnetic-field-induced angular momentum, and a vorticity-modified photon-pion coupling. We also briefly discuss the phenomenological implications of these vorticity-induced effects.

## Key claim

The authors derive vorticity-induced effects for Nambu-Goldstone modes in chiral perturbation theory by treating vorticity as an emergent axial-vector field, but the results are limited to fourth order in the external fields and do not yet capture the helical magnetic effect or couplings of neutral pions to vorticity at finite chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors start from a linear sigma model Lagrangian with Dirac fermions coupled to external vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar fields, then integrate out the fermions using a derivative expansion of the fermion determinant following the method of Aitchison (1985). They compute the Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action up to fourth order in the external fields, specializing to Abelian vector and axial-vector fields for N_f = 2 flavor symmetry, and then apply the correspondence from Yamamoto (2021) where vorticity is identified as an axial-vector field A_mu = omega_mu/2.

## Key numbers

* The effective action for the theta-VV term yields a coefficient of 1/(8 pi^2) in the flavor trace, up to second order in derivatives.
* The effective action for the theta-AA term yields a coefficient of 1/(24 pi^2) in the flavor trace, up to second order in derivatives.
* The anomalous charge density from vorticity is n = (i/(4 pi^2 f_pi^2)) omega dot (nabla pi^+ x nabla pi^-), where f_pi is the pion decay constant.
* The anomalous angular momentum in a magnetic field is J = -(e n_I/(8 pi^2 f_pi^2)) B, where n_I is the non-anomalous isospin charge.
* The divergence of the axial current from the Abelian Bardeen anomaly is partial_mu j^mu_A = (N_c/(4 pi^2)) epsilon^{munualphabeta} [(e^2/4) Tr_f Q^2 F^Q_{munu} F^Q_{alphabeta} + (N_f/12) F^A_{munu} F^A_{alphabeta}].

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Wess-Zumino-Witten terms in chiral perturbation theory to include vorticity as an axial-vector field, building on the correspondence established by Yamamoto (2021). The results reproduce the consistent anomaly for Abelian vector and axial-vector fields and recover the anomalous terms responsible for the chiral soliton lattice phase in QCD at finite baryon chemical potential in a magnetic field, previously derived by Son (2004, 2007) and Brauner (2016). The new vorticity-induced couplings to charged pions may be relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology, particularly for dilepton production and charged pion condensation under rotation and magnetic fields as studied by Liu (2017) and Chen (2019).
