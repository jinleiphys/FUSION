# Heavy and heavy-light tensor and axial-tensor mesons in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> We present the first calculation of tensor and axial-tensor mesons with total spin $J\geq2$ within the Covariant Spectator Theory. We employ a refined quark-antiquark interaction kernel that incorporates the momentum dependence of the strong coupling, replacing the previously used constant term of the kernel. Global least-squares fits to the masses of experimentally established heavy and heavy-light meson states yield an excellent description of the mass spectrum for $J^P=0^\pm, 1^\pm, 2^\pm$, and $3^\pm$ using only eight adjustable parameters.

## Key claim

The Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) is extended for the first time to compute tensor and axial-tensor mesons with total spin J≥2, using a refined quark-antiquark interaction kernel that incorporates the momentum dependence of the strong coupling rather than the previously used constant term. The claim of an "excellent description" of the mass spectrum is hedged by the fact that the best fit uses only eight adjustable parameters and that the fit to all 49 established states yields results very similar to fitting only the 33 non-axial states, for which the Gross equation is best suited.

## Method

The one-channel CST Gross equation for quark-antiquark bound states was solved for meson sectors from bbbar to cqbar (q=u,d) with J^P = 0±, 1±, 2±, and 3±. The interaction kernel includes covariant generalizations of linear confining and constant potentials plus a one-gluon-exchange interaction in Feynman gauge, with the strong running coupling α_s(q^2) now depending on momentum transfer rather than being constant. Nine model parameters (including constituent quark masses m_u, m_s, m_c, m_b, string tension σ, and cut-off parameters) were adjusted through global least-squares fits to experimentally measured states, with three parameter models evaluated using different sets of fitted states (10 pseudoscalar, 33 non-axial, and 49 states of all channels).

## Key numbers

- Best overall fit uses N_f=2 active flavours with 8 adjustable parameters (the constant interaction strength C is consistently ≈0 and thus omitted)
- For the fit to all 49 established states: σ = 0.176 GeV², α_s(0) = 0.522, m_b = 4.859 GeV, m_c = 1.517 GeV, m_s = 0.353 GeV, m_q = 0.197 GeV, λ_L = 2.903, λ_G = 2.243
- For the fit to 33 non-axial states: σ = 0.179 GeV², α_s(0) = 0.507, m_b = 4.852 GeV, m_c = 1.508 GeV, m_s = 0.343 GeV, m_q = 0.185 GeV, λ_L = 2.812, λ_G = 2.266
- For the fit to 10 pseudoscalar states: σ = 0.216 GeV², α_s(0) = 0.419, m_b = 4.794 GeV, m_c = 1.441 GeV, m_s = 0.274 GeV, m_q = 0.133 GeV, λ_L = 1.219, λ_G = 1.786

## Context

This work extends previous CST calculations by Leitão et al. (2017) and Stadler et al. (2018) that computed masses and vertex functions of heavy and heavy-light mesons only up to total spin J≤1, and earlier studies by Gross, Milana, and Savkli on pseudoscalar and vector mesons. By incorporating the momentum dependence of the strong coupling and generalizing the formalism to arbitrary spin-parity, the authors achieve a unified fit of all currently established quark-antiquark meson states containing at least one heavy quark, enabling predictions of higher-spin states and guidance for J^P assignment of experimentally observed but unconfirmed states listed by the Particle Data Group. The work establishes groundwork for modeling light mesons of arbitrary J^P via the four-channel CST equation.
