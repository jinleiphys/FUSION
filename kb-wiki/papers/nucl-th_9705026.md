# Medium effects in K^+ nuclear interactions

> Total and reaction cross sections are derived self consistently from the attenuation cross sections measured in transmission experiments at the AGS for K^+ on Li^6, C, Si and Ca in the momentum range of 500-700 MeV/c by using a V_{opt}=t_{eff}(rho)rho optical potential. Self consistency requires, for the KN in-medium t matrix, that Im t_{eff}(rho) increases linearly with the average nuclear density in excess of a threshold value of 0.088+-0.004 fm^-3. The density dependence of Re t_{eff}(rho) is studied phenomenologically, and also applying a relativistic mean field approach, by fitting the integral cross sections. The real part of the optical potential is found to be systematically less repulsive with increasing energy than expected from the free-space repulsive KN interaction. When the elastic scattering data for Li^6 and C at 715 MeV/c are included in the analysis, a tendency of Re V_{opt} to generate an attractive pocket at the nuclear surface is observed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that self-consistent fits to K⁺ nucleus total and reaction cross sections require a density-dependent optical potential where the imaginary part of the effective KN t-matrix increases linearly with average nuclear density above a threshold value of 0.088 ± 0.004 fm⁻³, while the real part becomes systematically less repulsive with increasing energy than expected from free-space KN interactions.

## Method
Total (σ_T) and reaction (σ_R) cross sections were derived self-consistently from attenuation cross sections measured in transmission experiments at the AGS for K⁺ on ⁶Li, C, Si, and Ca at beam momenta of 488, 531, 656, and 714 MeV/c. The analysis used a Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential of the form V_opt = t_eff(ρ)ρ, where the imaginary part of t_eff was parameterized to increase linearly with average nuclear density above a threshold density ρ_th. The real part of the optical potential was studied both phenomenologically using density-dependent parameterizations and via a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with scalar and vector meson fields, fitting to the integral cross sections and, at 714 MeV/c, also to elastic scattering angular distributions for ⁶Li and C.

## Key numbers
- Threshold density for imaginary part enhancement: ρ_th = 0.088 ± 0.004 fm⁻³
- Enhancement parameter β = 13.0 ± 3.4 fm³ for the linear density dependence of Im t_eff
- Im b_eff enhancement over free-space values: 5-15% depending on momentum
- Self-consistent σ_T and σ_R values differ from previous analyses by about 5%, always exceeding previous values
- At 714 MeV/c, Re V_opt is essentially consistent with zero (within uncertainties)
- RMF scaling factors C_S and C_V become vanishingly small at 714 MeV/c, yielding net repulsion of about 5 MeV for carbon
- Systematic uncertainties in cross sections estimated at 5-10%

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent analysis framework introduced by Friedman, Gal, and Mareš (1997) to derive integral cross sections from transmission experiments, updating the procedure of Friedman et al. (1997). The results contradict predictions of the simple first-order tρ optical potential, which underestimates cross sections for dense nuclei (C, Si, Ca) by up to about 25%. The paper discusses several theoretical proposals, nucleon swelling (Siegel et al., 1985), dropping vector meson masses (Brown et al., 1988), meson exchange currents (Jiang and Koltun, 1992; Garcia-Recio et al., 1995), and medium effects on exchanged mesons (Caillon and Labarsouque, 1992), but finds none capable of reproducing the required density dependence of Im t_eff. The results enable future studies of inelastic excitations (reported by Chrien et al., 1997) and motivate theoretical work on nonconventional in-medium reaction channels.
