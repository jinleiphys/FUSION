# Structure of Pairs in Heavy Weakly-Bound Nuclei

> We study the structure of nucleon pairs within a simple model consisting of a square well in three dimensions and a delta-function residual interaction between two weakly-bound particles at the Fermi surface. We include the continuum by enclosing the entire system in a large spherical box. To a good approximation, the continuum can be replaced by a small set of optimally-determined resonance states, suggesting that in many nuclei far from stability it may be possible to incorporate continuum effects within traditional shell-model based approximations.

# Structure of Pairs in Heavy Weakly-Bound Nuclei

## Key claim
Continuum effects in weakly-bound nuclei far from stability, but not too near the drip lines, can be approximated by replacing the full continuum with a small set of optimally-determined resonance states, enabling traditional shell-model based treatments. This conclusion is drawn from a simple two-particle model and may not hold for extremely weakly-bound nuclei very near the drip lines where shell structure may be smoothed out.

## Method
The authors model two particles near the Fermi surface in a finite spherical square well plus a surface spin-orbit force, with an attractive delta-function residual interaction. They include the continuum by enclosing the system in a large spherical box (30-50 fm) and truncate the continuum to avoid a singularity, using an interaction strength of 831 MeV-fm³ outside the well (with a 40 MeV cutoff) and 300 MeV-fm³ inside the well. They compare full diagonalization in a space of 0⁺ states (dimension ≈1000-2500) with approximations that first truncate to resonant states and then further reduce each resonance to a single optimally-defined single-particle state.

## Key numbers
- Interaction strength outside the well: 831 MeV-fm³, with a kinetic energy cutoff of 40 MeV
- Interaction strength inside the well: 300 MeV-fm³
- Square well radius: approximately 5.2 fm, depth 40-50 MeV
- Spin-orbit strength: approximately 0.4-0.5 fm²
- Continuum single-particle states never make up more than 25% of the ground state eigenvector
- For a single bound 0g₇/₂ level at E_b = -1.24 MeV: full ground state energy = -6.33 MeV; bound-state-only energy = -2.48 MeV; bound-plus-resonances energy = -5.61 MeV
- For the same system with the single-state-per-resonance approximation: ground state energy = -5.79 MeV
- For a case with active bound 2s₁/₂ orbit: E_b = -1.26 MeV

## Context
This work extends studies of pairing in weakly-bound nuclei by Dobaczewski et al. (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov in coordinate space), Bertsch and Esbensen (Green function framework with discretized continuum for light halo nuclei), and Otsuka (Variational Shell Model). The authors focus on heavy nuclei far from stability, particularly those along the r-process path with separation energies of 1-2 MeV, rather than nuclei at the drip lines. The results enable the use of shell-model techniques such as the broken-pair (generalized-seniority) approximation for calculating beta decay in r-process nuclei, where existing treatments are schematic and not totally reliable.
