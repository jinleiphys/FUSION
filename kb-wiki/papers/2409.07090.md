# Unraveling the Effects of Cluster Transfer-Induced Breakups on $^{12}$C Fragmentation in Hadron Therapy

> The capability of standard Geant4 PhysicsLists to address the fragmentation of $^{12}$C$-^{12}$C was assessed through a comparative analysis with experimental cross sections reported by Divay et al. and Dudouet et al. The standard PhysicsLists were found to be inadequate in explaining the fragmentation systematics. To address this limitation, the breakup component of fragmentation was systematically integrated into the standard PhysicsList, which successfully replicated the differential and double differential cross sections for $α$ production. This breakup component was modeled using fresco CDCC-CRC calculations. This novel physics process was then incorporated into the Geant4 framework, facilitating the calculation of dose distributions in water and tissue. The application of this method demonstrated a precise reproduction of the dose deposited at the Bragg peak region, corroborating the experimental data from Liedner et al., thereby enhancing the accurate visibility of dose tailing.

# Unraveling the Effects of Cluster Transfer-Induced Breakups on $^{12}$C Fragmentation in Hadron Therapy

## Key claim

The standard Geant4 QBBC_ABLA PhysicsList fails to reproduce experimental differential and double differential cross sections for $\alpha$ production in $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C collisions at therapeutic energies (50 and 95 MeV/u), but incorporating a coherent CDCC-CRC model of transfer-induced and inelastic breakup components into the simulation successfully replicates the measured angular distributions, energy spectra, and dose deposition in water, particularly in the Bragg peak tail region.

## Method

The authors performed QBBC_ABLA simulations in Geant4 for $^{12}$C beams at 50 and 95 MeV/u on thin graphite targets, scoring $10^9$ events per energy, and compared the resulting proton and $\alpha$ angular distributions and energy spectra against experimental data from Divay et al. (50 MeV/u) and Dudouet et al. (95 MeV/u). To address discrepancies, they modeled the direct reaction component using the fresco CDCC-CRC framework, treating transfer-induced breakup ($^{12}$C + $^{12}$C $\rightarrow$ $^8$Be + $^{16}$O, with $^8$Be decaying to 2$\alpha$) and inelastic breakup ($^{12}$C $\rightarrow$ $^8$Be + $\alpha$ above 7.37 MeV threshold) as coherent processes coupled to the entrance channel. The breakup cross sections were then incorporated as a new physics process into Geant4 for dose calculations in water and tissue at 280 MeV/u, using spectroscopy factors from HFB calculations and optical model potentials from Akyuz and Winther.

## Key numbers

- At 50 MeV/u, QBBC_ABLA underpredicts $\alpha$ cross sections at forward angles below 10$^\circ$ and overpredicts them above 10$^\circ$ by about an order of magnitude compared to Divay et al. data.
- At 95 MeV/u, QBBC_ABLA fails to reproduce $\alpha$ angular distributions across all angles when compared to Dudouet et al. measurements.
- The breakup continuum was discretized in wave number intervals of 0.1 fm$^{-1}$ up to 20 MeV excitation for both transfer-induced and inelastic breakup channels.
- The $^{12}$C inelastic breakup threshold is 7.37 MeV; 32 excited states of $^{12}$C including the ground state were included in the mass partition.
- For transfer-induced breakup at 50 MeV/u, $^8$Be states up to 3.03 MeV were coupled; at 95 MeV/u, $^8$Be states up to 16.62 MeV were coupled with $^{16}$O states up to 11.096 MeV.
- The excitation function for inclusive $\alpha$ production was calculated up to 500 MeV/u and compared with data from Divay et al. (50 MeV/u), Napoli et al. (62 MeV/u), Dudouet et al. (95 MeV/u), and Kummali et al. (400 MeV/u).
- Dose simulations in water at 280 MeV/u used a 30 cm $\times$ 30 cm $\times$ 30 cm target geometry with 0.1 mm cut values.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of $^{12}$C fragmentation beyond the QBBC_ABLA model (Allison 2016, Ivantchenko 2012), which includes Fermi breakups but lacks sequential breakup mechanisms, by incorporating CDCC-CRC calculations from fresco (Thompson). The approach successfully reproduces experimental data from Divay et al. and Dudouet et al. that previous models (QMD, BIC, INCL++) could not match, particularly for $\alpha$ angular distributions and energy spectra. The method enables improved dose calculations in hadron therapy, corroborating experimental Bragg peak data from Liedner et al. (2018) and providing a physics-driven alternative to purely phenomenological simulation approaches. Future work requires exclusive experimental measurements for each breakup mode from resonant and continuum states to further constrain the many-body treatment of multifragmentation.
