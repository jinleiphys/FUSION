# Phase Transitions in Rotating Neutron Stars

> As rotating neutron stars slow down, the pressure and the density in the core region increase due to the decreasing centrifugal forces and phase transitions may occur in the center. We extract the analytic behavior near the critical angular velocity $Ω_0$, where the phase transitions occur in the center of a neutron star, and calculate the moment of inertia, angular velocity, rate of slow down, braking index, etc. For a first order phase transition these quantities have a characteristic behavior, e.g., the braking index diverges as $\sim(Ω_0-Ω)^{-1/2}$. Observational consequences for first, second and other phase transitions are discussed.

# Phase Transitions in Rotating Neutron Stars

## Key claim

As rotating neutron stars slow down, the increasing core pressure can trigger phase transitions at a critical angular velocity Ω₀; for a first-order phase transition, the braking index diverges as ~(Ω₀−Ω)^{-1/2}, but this behavior is conditional on the existence of a finite density difference between the two phases and applies only for angular velocities just below the critical value.

## Method

The authors first solve the Newtonian hydrostatic equations analytically for a simple two-incompressible-fluid equation of state with a first-order phase transition, extracting the scaling behavior of the dense core radius R₀ and moment of inertia near Ω₀. They then generalize to general relativity by solving the rotating version of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation (the l=0 part of Hartle's equations) numerically for a Bethe-Johnson polytropic equation of state with a first-order phase transition at density ρ₁=3.2ρ₀ to ρ₂=4ρ₀, for a neutron star of mass 1.4M⊙.

## Key numbers

- For the Bethe-Johnson polytrope (BJP) equation of state, the critical angular velocity is Ω₀ ≈ 6 kHz, comparable to a millisecond binary pulsar.
- The coefficient c₂ in the moment of inertia expansion is c₂ ≈ 0.07 ≈ 2.2 ω₀³ for the BJP equation of state.
- The central density of a non-rotating 1.4M⊙ neutron star with the BJP equation of state is approximately 3.4ρ₀, where ρ₀ = mₙ × 0.15 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter mass density.
- The first-order phase transition is modeled at density ρ₁ = 3.2ρ₀ to a high-density phase of ρ₂ = 4ρ₀.
- General relativistic corrections are of order GM/R ≈ 10% for a neutron star of mass M ≈ 1.4M⊙.
- The dense core radius scales as R₀ ∝ √(Ω₀²−Ω²) for angular velocities just below Ω₀.
- The moment of inertia contains a term proportional to (Ω₀²−Ω²)^{3/2} near the critical angular velocity.

## Context

This work extends the study of rapidly rotating neutron stars by Glendenning, Pei and Weber (1997), who found that a drastic softening of the equation of state by a phase transition to quark matter can cause a sudden contraction and backbending moment of inertia. The present paper analytically extracts the universal scaling behavior near the critical angular velocity for first-order phase transitions, showing that the braking index diverges and the slowdown rate acquires a characteristic √(1−t/t₀) dependence. This enables observational searches for first-order phase transitions by monitoring rapidly rotating pulsars such as binary millisecond pulsars or young pulsars recently formed in supernovae, where the signal scales with Ω₀³ and may be detectable through careful timing of the angular velocity or braking index.
