# Transverse momentum fluctuations and their correlation with elliptic flow in nuclear collision

> We propose observables $v_0$ and $v_0(p_T)$ which quantify the relative fluctuations in the total transverse momentum at fixed multiplicity. We first study the factorization of the fixed multiplicity momentum dependent two particle correlation function into a product of $v_0(p_T^a)$ and $v_0(p_T^b)$ within realistic hydrodynamic simulations. Then we present computations of $v_0(p_T)$ for different particle types. We determine the relation between the integrated $v_0$ and previously measured observables, and compare results from a hybrid hydrodynamics based model to experimental data. The effects of bulk viscosity and an initial pre-equilibrium stage on the results are quantified. We find that $v_0$ is strongly correlated with the initial state entropy per elliptic area, $S/A$. Using this result, we explain how the observed correlations between the elliptic flow and the transverse momentum (both in simulations and experiment) reflect the initial state correlations between $1/A$ and ellipticity $\varepsilon_2$ at fixed multiplicity. We argue that the systematic experimental study of $v_0$, with the same sophistication as used for the other $v_n$, can contribute significantly to our understanding of quark gluon plasma properties.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observable $v_0$, which quantifies relative fluctuations in total transverse momentum at fixed multiplicity, is strongly correlated with the initial state entropy per elliptic area $S/A$, and that systematic experimental study of $v_0$ can contribute significantly to understanding quark-gluon plasma properties, though the factorization assumption underlying the definition holds only approximately, with significant deviations appearing at the largest momenta ($p_T^a \sim p_T^b \sim 3$ GeV).

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations using the IP-Glasma initial conditions coupled with the Music relativistic hydrodynamic code and UrQMD hadronic afterburner for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV and $\sqrt{s}=2760$ GeV. They computed the fixed-multiplicity two-particle correlation function $C(p_T^a, p_T^b)$ and extracted $v_0(p_T)$ under the assumption of factorization, then compared these results to experimental data from ALICE and ATLAS on transverse momentum fluctuations and their correlation with elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- The correlation function $C(p_T^a, p_T^b)$ factorizes to a good approximation, with significant deviations appearing only for $p_T^a \sim p_T^b \sim 3$ GeV in 30-40% central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV
- The charged hadron $v_0(p_T)$ scales approximately as $(\sqrt{dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta})^{-1}$, with small deviations at $p_T < 0.5$ GeV caused by differences in averaged radial flow
- The entropy per elliptic area $S/A$ provides the best single-term predictor for transverse momentum fluctuations, with the Pearson correlation coefficient $Q_\xi$ showing dramatic improvement over entropy per circular area when going from central to peripheral collisions
- The slope of $\sqrt{C_m}/\langle\bar{p}_T\rangle$ as a function of multiplicity $dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta$ in the model is steeper than the experimental data from ALICE at $\sqrt{s}=2760$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the principal component analysis of momentum fluctuations by Gardim (2019) and the partial covariance method of Olszewski (2017), building on earlier studies of transverse momentum fluctuations by Bozek (2012, 2016) and Mazeliauskas (2015). The paper provides a simpler alternative to principal component analysis for studying momentum fluctuations, which can be sensitive to non-flow effects, and enables direct comparison between hydrodynamic simulations and experimental measurements of $v_0$ and its correlation with elliptic flow, as recently measured by ATLAS (2019).
