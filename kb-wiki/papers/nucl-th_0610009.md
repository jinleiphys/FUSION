# The $qqqq\bar q$ components and hidden flavor contributions to the baryon magnetic moments

> The contributions from the $qqqq\bar q$ components to the magnetic moments of the octet as well as the $Δ^{++}$ and $Ω^-$ decuplet baryons are calculated for the configurations that are expected to have the lowest energy if the hyperfine interaction depends both on spin and flavor. The contributions from the $u\bar u$, $d\bar d$ and $s\bar s$ components are given separately. It is shown that addition of $qqqq\bar q$ admixtures to the ground state baryons can improve the overall description of the magnetic moments of the baryon octet and decuplet in the quark model without SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking, beyond that of the different constituent masses of the strange and light-flavor quarks. The explicit flavor (and spin) wave functions for all the possible configurations of the $qqqq\bar q$ components with light and strange $q\bar q$ pairs are given for the baryon and octet and decuplet. Admixtures of ~ 10% of the $qqqq\bar q$ configuration where the flavor-spin symmetry is $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$, which is likely to have the lowest energy, in particular reduces the deviation from the empirical values of the magnetic moments of the $Σ^ -$ and the $Ξ^0$ compared with the quark model.

## Key claim

Adding approximately 10% admixture of the specific $qqqq\bar q$ configuration with flavor-spin symmetry $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$ to the conventional three-quark wave functions can reduce the deviation of calculated magnetic moments from experimental values for the baryon octet, particularly for the $\Sigma^-$ and $\Xi^0$ hyperons, but only if the $qqqq\bar q$ component is much more compact than the $qqq$ component (with radius ~0.3 fm).

## Method

The authors constructed explicit flavor and spin wave functions for all possible $qqqq\bar q$ configurations in the baryon octet and decuplet using SU(3) flavor symmetry and the weight diagram method, considering configurations where the $qqqq$ subsystem has mixed spatial symmetry $[31]_X$ and the antiquark is in its ground state. They calculated diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements of the magnetic moment operator between the $qqq$ and $qqqq\bar q$ components using harmonic oscillator constituent quark model wave functions, with the $qqqq\bar q$ probability set to 18% based on the measured $\bar d - \bar u$ asymmetry in the proton.

## Key numbers

- The $qqqq\bar q$ probability is set to $P_{5q} = 0.18$ (18%) to reproduce the experimentally measured $\bar d - \bar u = 0.12$ in the proton
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = m = 274$ MeV, $m_s = 419$ MeV
- The oscillator parameter $\omega_5 = 0.57$ GeV for the compact $qqqq\bar q$ component, corresponding to a radius of ~0.3 fm
- The overlap integral $C_{35} = 0.24$ when $\omega_5 \sim 2.3\,\omega_3$
- For the $\Sigma^-$ hyperon, the deviation from experiment drops from 13.7% in the $qqq$ model to 4.3% with $qqqq\bar q$ admixture
- For the $\Xi^0$ hyperon, the deviation drops from 20.8% in the $qqq$ model to 3.0% with $qqqq\bar q$ admixture
- The average deviation for the octet baryons drops from ~9% to ~4%
- The calculated strangeness magnetic moment of the proton is $\mu_s = 0.17$, within the experimental range $0.37 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.26 \pm 0.07$ from the SAMPLE experiment
- For the $\Omega^-$ decuplet baryon, the calculated magnetic moment is -2.02 (compared to experimental -2.02) with $P_{3q}=0.60$ and $C_{35}=0.24$

## Context

This work extends the conventional $qqq$ constituent quark model, which provides a qualitative description of baryon magnetic moments but with deviations up to ~15% (largest for $\Sigma^-$ and $\Xi^0$), by explicitly including $qqqq\bar q$ components. Previous attempts to improve the $qqq$ model included covariant reformulations (Bruno, 2002) and adding meson exchange and orbital excitations (Franklin, 1968; Dann, 1996). The approach is motivated by experimental evidence for $q\bar q$ pairs in the nucleon from the $\bar d/\bar u$ asymmetry measurements (Towell, 2001; NA51, 1994; NMC, 1991; HERMES, 1998) and parity violation experiments indicating $s\bar s$ contributions to the proton magnetic moment (HAPPEX, 2000; SAMPLE, 2000). The explicit $qqqq\bar q$ wave functions and magnetic moment expressions enable future calculations of strangeness magnetic moments and separation of individual $u\bar u$, $d\bar d$, and $s\bar s$ contributions for all octet and decuplet baryons.
