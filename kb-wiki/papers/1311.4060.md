# Structure of odd $^{79,81,83}$Se isotopes with proton and neutron excitations across $Z=28$ AND $N= 40$

> The recently measured experimental data of $^{79,81,83}$Se isotopes have been interpreted in terms of shell model calculations. The calculations have been performed in ${f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}}$ space with the recently derived interactions, namely with JUN45 and jj44b. To study the importance of the proton excitations across the $Z=28$ shell in this region. We have also performed calculation in ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space using an ${fpg}$ effective interaction with $^{48}$Ca core and imposing a truncation. Excitation energies, $B(2)$ values, quadrupole moments and magnetic moments are compared with experimental data when available. Present study reveals the importance of proton excitations across the $Z=28$ shell for predicting quadrupole and magnetic moments.

# Structure of odd $^{79,81,83}$Se isotopes with proton and neutron excitations across $Z=28$ AND $N=40$

## Key claim

Shell model calculations in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space using JUN45 and jj44b interactions, and in the $fpg_{9/2}$ space with a $^{48}$Ca core, can reproduce experimental excitation energies, $B(E2)$ values, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments for $^{79,81,83}$Se, but only the inclusion of the $\pi f_{7/2}$ orbital (proton excitations across $Z=28$) yields good agreement for quadrupole moments, and the model space must be enlarged with the $\nu d_{5/2}$ orbital to properly describe nuclei approaching $N=50$.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in two valence spaces: a full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space (including $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$ orbits) with a $^{56}$Ni core using the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions, and an $fpg_{9/2}$ space (including $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$ orbits) with a $^{48}$Ca core using an $fpg$ effective interaction with truncation allowing up to four particle excitations from $f_{7/2}$ to upper $fp$ orbitals for protons and from upper $fp$ to $g_{9/2}$ for neutrons. Excitation energies, $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments were computed using the shell model code ANTOINE and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{79}$Se, the JUN45 interaction predicts the first $1/2^-$ level 52 keV lower than experiment; jj44b predicts it 29 keV lower.
- For $^{81}$Se, the experimental energy difference between the $7/2^+$ and $9/2^+$ levels is 191 keV; JUN45 predicts 170 keV and jj44b predicts 220 keV.
- For $^{83}$Se, the $1/2^+$ level is experimentally at 360 keV; JUN45 predicts 1636 keV and jj44b predicts 2485 keV.
- The $B(E2; 9/2_1^+ \rightarrow 7/2_1^+)$ for $^{79}$Se is 20.37 W.u. (JUN45), 27.77 W.u. (jj44b), and 32.03 W.u. ($fpg$); for $^{81}$Se it is 17.65 W.u. (JUN45), 17.60 W.u. (jj44b), and 10.27 W.u. ($fpg$); for $^{83}$Se it is 2.40 W.u. (JUN45), 0.13 W.u. (jj44b), and 9.94 W.u. ($fpg$).
- The experimental quadrupole moment for $^{79}$Se $7/2_1^+$ is +0.8(2) eb; JUN45 gives +0.38 eb, jj44b gives +0.59 eb, and $fpg$ gives +0.65 eb.
- The experimental magnetic moment for $^{79}$Se $7/2_1^+$ is -1.018(15) $\mu_N$; JUN45 gives -1.17 $\mu_N$, jj44b gives -1.29 $\mu_N$, and $fpg$ gives -1.20 $\mu_N$.
- Neutron $g_{9/2}$ occupancy for the $^{79}$Se ground state is 6.57 (JUN45) and 6.97 (jj44b); for $^{81}$Se ground state it is 8.15 (JUN45) and 8.12 (jj44b); for $^{83}$Se $9/2_1^+$ it is 9.00 (JUN45 and jj44b).

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of odd Se isotopes by Yoshinaga et al. (pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space) and follows the authors' own work on even Se isotopes. The inclusion of the $\pi f_{7/2}$ orbital follows the approach of Cheal (2010), who demonstrated its importance for Ga isotopes. The results show that while JUN45 and jj44b interactions in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space reproduce many features, the $fpg$ interaction with proton excitations across $Z=28$ is essential for quadrupole moments. The overprediction of excitation energies in $^{83}$Se indicates that the $d_{5/2}$ orbital must be included to account for neutron excitations across $N=50$, pointing toward needed future theoretical development.
