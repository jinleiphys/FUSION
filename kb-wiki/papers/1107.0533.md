# Sensitivity of Cross Sections for Elastic Nucleus-Nucleus Scattering to Halo Nucleus Density Distributions

> In order to clear up the sensitivity of the nucleus-nucleus scattering to the nuclear matter distributions of exotic halo nuclei, we have calculated differential cross sections for elastic scattering of the $^6$He and $^{11}$Li nuclei on several nuclear targets at the energy of 0.8 GeV/nucleon with different assumed nuclear density distributions in $^6$He and $^{11}$Li.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of differential cross sections for elastic nucleus-nucleus scattering to the shape of the nuclear matter distribution of exotic halo nuclei is of the same order of magnitude as that of nucleus-proton scattering, contrary to the expectation that nucleus-nucleus scattering would be significantly more sensitive to the nuclear periphery. The authors conclude that performing experiments to measure elastic nucleus-nucleus scattering for studying nuclear matter distributions in exotic nuclei is "hardly justified."

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross sections for elastic scattering of \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li on protons and nuclear targets \(^4\)He, \(^9\)Be, \(^{12}\)C, \(^{58}\)Ni, \(^{90}\)Zr, and \(^{208}\)Pb at 0.8 GeV/nucleon incident energy using Glauber theory within the "rigid-target" approximation. Different assumed nuclear density distributions were used for the halo nuclei, including distributions with and without a halo, and with and without an exponential density tail at large radii. The core density was described by a Gaussian, the halo by a Gaussian for \(^6\)He and a 1p-shell harmonic oscillator-type function for \(^{11}\)Li, and target nuclei by Gaussian or Fermi distributions.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 0.8 GeV/nucleon for all calculations.
- Free NN scattering parameters used: total cross section \(\sigma_{\rm NN} = 42.5\) mb, ratio of real to imaginary part \(\epsilon_{\rm NN} = -0.18\), amplitude slope \(\beta_{\rm NN} = 0.2\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^6\)He with a halo: core rms radius R\(_{\rm c}\) = 1.95 fm, halo rms radius R\(_{\rm h}\) = 2.88 fm, total matter rms radius R\(_{\rm m}\) = 2.30 fm.
- For \(^6\)He with a density tail: halo rms radius R\(_{\rm h}'\) = 3.34 fm.
- For \(^{11}\)Li with a halo: R\(_{\rm c}\) = 2.50 fm, R\(_{\rm h}\) = 5.86 fm, R\(_{\rm m}\) = 3.37 fm.
- For \(^4\)He target: rms matter radius R\(_{\rm m}\) = 1.49 fm.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb target: Fermi distribution parameters R\(_0\) = 6.70 fm, a = 0.54 fm, w = -0.06, giving R\(_{\rm m}\) = 5.54 fm.
- The density tail in \(^6\)He contains approximately 1% of the total nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Alkhazov and collaborators (2000s) on elastic proton-nucleus scattering in inverse kinematics, which showed insufficient sensitivity to the far periphery of halo nuclei. The authors test the suggestion from a prior paper (referred to as [3]) that reaction cross sections for nucleus-nucleus scattering are significantly more sensitive to the nuclear periphery than proton-nucleus scattering. The results contradict that expectation for elastic scattering, and the authors recommend against pursuing such experiments, though they note that future facilities like FAIR at Darmstadt could potentially perform them.
