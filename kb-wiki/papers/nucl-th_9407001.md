# Relativistic treatment of spin-transfer observables in quasielastic $(\vec{\bf p},\vec{\bf n})$ scattering

> We calculate all spin-transfer observables for the quasielastic $(\vec{p},\vec{n})$ reaction in a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation. The nuclear-structure information is contained in a large set of nuclear-response functions that are computed in nuclear matter using a relativistic random-phase approximation to the Walecka model. A reduced value of the nucleon mass in the medium induces important dynamical changes in the residual isovector interaction relative to its nonrelativistic counterpart. As a result, good agreement is found for all spin observables --- including the spin-longitudinal to spin-transverse ratio --- when compared to the original ($q=1.72$~fm$^{-1}$) NTOF experiment. In contrast, the spin-longitudinal to spin-transverse ratio is underpredicted at $q=1.2$~fm$^{-1}$ and overpredicted at $q=2.5$~fm$^{-1}$. We comment on the role of distortions as a possible solution to this discrepancy.

## Key claim

The authors calculate all spin-transfer observables for quasielastic $(\vec{p},\vec{n})$ scattering using a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation and find good agreement with experiment at momentum transfer $q=1.72$ fm$^{-1}$, but the spin-longitudinal to spin-transverse ratio is underpredicted at $q=1.2$ fm$^{-1}$ and overpredicted at $q=2.5$ fm$^{-1}$, with distortions suggested as a possible resolution to this discrepancy.

## Method

The nuclear response functions are computed in infinite nuclear matter using a relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) to the Walecka model, incorporating a reduced effective nucleon mass in the medium. The residual isovector particle-hole interaction is modeled with $\pi + \rho + g'$ contributions, where the Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$ is set to 0.7, and the spin-transverse component $g'_\rho$ is adjusted to 0.3 for the relativistic case (compared to 0.65 for the free-mass case) based on electron-scattering data. Spin-transfer observables are then obtained as linear combinations of spin-dependent cross sections calculated from the RPWIA formalism.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: $q = 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$, $q = 1.72$ fm$^{-1}$, and $q = 2.5$ fm$^{-1}$
- Landau-Migdal parameter: $g' = 0.7$ (standard value)
- Spin-transverse Landau-Migdal parameter: $g'_\rho = 0.3$ for $M^\star \ne M$; $g'_\rho = 0.65$ for $M^\star = M$
- Effective $NN\pi$ coupling in medium scales as $(M^\star/M)$ at low density and $(M^\star/M)^2$ at high density
- Quasielastic peak position shifts from $\omega_{QE} = Q^2/(2M)$ to $Q^2/(2M^\star)$; width increases from $\Delta\omega \simeq qk_F/M$ to $qk_F/M^\star$
- Transverse response compared to ${}^{40}$Ca$(e,e')$ data at $q = 410$ MeV and $q = 550$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic calculations of $R_L/R_T$ (Horowitz and Piekarewicz, 1993) to include all spin-transfer observables, building on the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986). The calculations address the long-standing puzzle of the missing enhancement in $R_L/R_T$ predicted by Alberico, Ericson, and Molinari (1982), which was not observed in NTOF experiments (Carey et al., 1984; Rees et al., 1986; McClelland et al., 1992; Chen et al., 1993). The relativistic treatment with reduced nucleon mass provides a dynamical mechanism for suppressing the longitudinal response without requiring large phenomenological adjustments, and the results enable future comparisons with distorted-wave calculations to resolve the momentum-transfer-dependent discrepancies.
