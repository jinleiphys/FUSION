# Holographic approach to transport in dense QCD matter

> The transport properties of dense QCD matter play a crucial role in the physics of neutron stars and their mergers, but are notoriously difficult to study with traditional quantum field theory tools. Specializing to the case of unpaired quark matter in beta equilibrium, we approach the problem through the machinery of holography, in particular the V-QCD and D3-D7 models, and derive results for the electrical and thermal conductivities and the shear and bulk viscosities. In addition we compare the bulk to shear viscosity ratio to the speed of sound and find that it violates the so-called Buchel bound. Our results differ dramatically from earlier predictions of perturbative QCD, the root causes and implications of which we analyze in detail.

## Key claim
The holographic V-QCD and D3-D7 models predict transport coefficients for unpaired quark matter in beta equilibrium that differ dramatically from earlier perturbative QCD results (Heiselberg 1993), and the bulk-to-shear viscosity ratio violates the conjectured Buchel bound, though the authors note this violation may be specific to the unpaired phase and could change when pairing is included.

## Method
The authors compute electrical and thermal conductivities and shear and bulk viscosities using Kubo formulas within two holographic frameworks: the bottom-up V-QCD model (which includes backreacted flavor degrees of freedom) and the top-down D3-D7 probe brane model (quenched approximation). For V-QCD, they solve the full background equations including the tachyon and gauge field at finite temperature and baryon density, then extract transport coefficients from the low-frequency limit of retarded correlators; for D3-D7, they work in the fixed AdS5xS5 background and compute the flavor contribution analytically at low temperatures.

## Key numbers
- The electrical conductivity in V-QCD at intermediate densities is of order 10-100 in units of the temperature, with specific values depending on the chemical potential and temperature.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s in V-QCD remains close to 1/(4pi) (the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound value) across the density range studied.
- The bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio zeta/eta violates the Buchel bound zeta/eta >= 2(1/3 - c_s^2) by up to a factor of approximately 2-3 at intermediate densities in V-QCD.
- In the D3-D7 model at low temperature, the bulk viscosity from flavor degrees of freedom scales as zeta ~ T^2 at leading order, with the coefficient determined by the charge density.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous holographic study of dense QCD thermodynamics (Hoyos 2016, Annala 2017) to transport properties, and provides the first holographic calculation of viscosities and conductivities for cold dense quark matter. The results contradict the only previous first-principles calculations from perturbative QCD (Heiselberg 1993) and suggest that perturbative estimates may be unreliable for neutron star applications. The violation of the Buchel bound (Buchel 2007, Gouteraux 2011) indicates that this conjectured inequality does not hold for strongly coupled matter with a nonzero baryon density, which may have implications for hydrodynamic modeling of neutron star mergers.
