# Renormalization group analysis of nuclear current operators

> A Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation for nuclear current operators in two-nucleon systems is derived. Nuclear current operators relevant to low-energy Gamow-Teller transitions are analyzed using the WRG equation. We employ the axial two-body current operators from phenomenological models and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, which are quite different from one another in describing small scale physics. After reducing the model space of the operators using the WRG equation, we find that there still remains a significant model dependence at Λ= 200 MeV, where Λis the sharp cutoff specifying the size of the model space. A model independent effective current operator is found at a rather small cutoff value, Λ= 70 MeV. By simulating the effective current operator at Λ=70 MeV, we obtain a current operator based on a pionless theory, thereby arguing an equivalence relation between nuclear current operators of phenomenological models and those of effective field theories.

## Key claim

After reducing the model space of nuclear current operators using a Wilsonian renormalization group equation, significant model dependence among phenomenological models and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory operators persists at a cutoff of \(\Lambda = 200\) MeV, but a model-independent effective current operator emerges at the smaller cutoff \(\Lambda = 70\) MeV, which can be simulated by a pionless effective field theory.

## Method

The authors derived a Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation for nuclear current operators in two-nucleon systems by imposing invariance of the matrix element under changes of the sharp momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\). They applied this equation to five different axial two-body current operators, two phenomenological models (Model I and Model II) and three heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory operators with cutoff values \(\Lambda_{eft}=500\), 600, and 800 MeV, and evolved them down to \(\Lambda = 200\) MeV and \(\Lambda = 70\) MeV, evaluating Gamow-Teller matrix elements for the \(\nu d \to \nu pn\) reaction.

## Key numbers

- At \(\Lambda = \infty\), the one-body operator contributes 98.87% of the Gamow-Teller matrix element for all five operators; two-body contributions range from 0.72% (Model II) to 1.42% (\(\Lambda_{eft}=500\) MeV).
- At \(\Lambda = 200\) MeV, the one-body contribution drops to 97.05% for all models, but two-body contributions still vary: 2.96% (Model I), 2.56% (Model II), and 3.23–3.25% (pionful EFT operators).
- At \(\Lambda = 70\) MeV, the one-body contribution falls to 69.22% for all models, and two-body contributions converge to 30.39–31.08%, with the deuteron \(S\)-wave contribution dominating (30.36–31.05%) and \(D\)-wave contributions nearly vanishing (0.03%).
- The model dependence in the tensor-type operator remains visible at \(\Lambda = 200\) MeV but disappears at \(\Lambda = 70\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the WRG analysis previously applied to nuclear forces by Nakamura (2004) to nuclear current operators, specifically axial-vector currents relevant to low-energy Gamow-Teller transitions. The authors show that, analogous to the convergence of phenomenological nuclear potentials to the model-independent \(V_{low-k}\) interaction found by Bogner et al. (2003), different current operators converge to a single effective operator at sufficiently small cutoff, enabling a connection between phenomenological models and effective field theories. This result enables the construction of model-independent low-energy current operators for neutrino-deuteron reactions and other electroweak processes in few-nucleon systems.
