# Glueball-glueball potential in a constituent gluon model

> In this work we use a mapping technique to derive in the context of a constituent gluon model an effective Hamiltonian that involves explicit gluon degrees of freedom. We study glueballs with two gluons using the Fock-Tani formalism. In the present work we calculate the glueball-glueball potential, in the context of the constituent gluon model, with gluon interchange.

## Key claim
The paper derives a glueball-glueball potential using the Fock-Tani formalism within a constituent gluon model, but the result is a short-range, deep potential whose ability to form bound states remains an open question.

## Method
The authors start from a microscopic Hamiltonian with gluon kinetic energy and a two-gluon potential taken from the Cornwall-Soni constituent gluon model. They apply the Fock-Tani transformation to map composite two-gluon glueball states onto ideal (canonical) glueball operators, then compute the effective glueball-glueball interaction at Born order. The scattering amplitude is expressed in terms of six integrals (R1 through R6) involving the glueball wavefunction parameters and the gluon-gluon potential, and the coordinate-space potential is obtained via Fourier transform.

## Key numbers
- Glueball-glueball potential computed for the \(0^{++}\) state with parameters: glueball rms radius \(r_0 = 0.58\) fm, \(\beta = 0.1\), \(\lambda = 1.8\), \(k = 0.21\), gluon mass \(m = 0.6\) GeV.
- Quark model parameters used for comparison: \(m_q = 0.55\) GeV, \(\alpha_s = 0.6\).
- The scattering amplitude \(h_{fi}\) is expressed in terms of Mandelstam variables \(s\), \(t\), and \(u\), with the glueball mass \(M_G\) appearing in the exponential factor \(R_0\) as \(\exp\left[-\frac{1}{2b^2}\left(\frac{s}{4} - M_G^2\right)\right]\), where \(b = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}\,r_0}\).

## Context
This work extends the Fock-Tani formalism previously applied to two-gluon glueballs (da Silva et al., J. Phys. G, cited as jphysg) by calculating the glueball-glueball interaction potential via gluon interchange. It uses the constituent gluon model of Cornwall and Soni (1983, 1985) and contrasts with quark-model approaches to hadron-hadron interactions. The derived potential enables future studies of whether glueball-glueball bound states exist, which is left as an open question.
