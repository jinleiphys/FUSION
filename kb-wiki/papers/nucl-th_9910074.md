# Chiral quark-soliton model in the Wigner-Seitz approximation

> In this paper we study the modification of the properties of the nucleon in the nucleus within the quark-soliton model. This is a covariant, dynamical model, which provides a non-linear representation of the spontaneously broken SU(2)_L X SU(2)_R symmetry of QCD. The effects of the nuclear medium are accounted for by using the Wigner-Seitz approximation and therefore reducing the complex many-body problem to a simpler single-particle problem. We find a minimum in the binding energy at finite density, a change in the isoscalar nucleon radius and a reduction of the in-medium pion decay constant. The latter is consistent with a partial restoration of chiral symmetry at finite density, which is predicted by other models.

## Key claim

The paper finds a minimum in the binding energy at finite density, a change in the isoscalar nucleon radius, and a reduction of the in-medium pion decay constant consistent with partial restoration of chiral symmetry; however, these results depend on the choice of boundary conditions for the Wigner-Seitz cell and on whether the spurious center-of-mass energy correction is included, which can be larger than the band width and strongly dependent on the boundary condition for the level.

## Method

The authors apply the Wigner-Seitz approximation to the chiral quark-soliton model, reducing the many-body problem of nuclear matter to a single-particle problem by enclosing the nucleon in a spherically symmetric cell of radius R with average boundary conditions. They solve the self-consistent integro-differential equations for the quark Dirac fields (u and v) and the chiral angle θ, including one-loop quark fluctuations (the Dirac sea) via an effective action expanded to second order in the soliton field. Three distinct sets of boundary conditions are tested: set I imposes unit topological charge (θ(X)=0) with v(X)=0 for the bottom and u(X)=0 for the top of the band; set II imposes flatness of the baryon density at the cell boundary (ρ'(X)=0) with θ'(X)=0; set III combines flat baryon density with unit topological charge.

- The pion decay constant in free space, f_π, is used to fix the regularization scale; the Pauli-Villars mass scale M_PV is given by M_PV = M exp(2π² f_π² / (N_c M²)).

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-Seitz approximation previously applied to the Skyrme model (Wüst 1987, Amore 1998), non-topological soliton models (Reinhardt 1985, Birse 1988, Weber 1998), the hybrid soliton model (Banerjee 1985, Gleissl 1986, Hahn 1987, Weber 1998), and the global color model (Johnson 1996-1998). Unlike those earlier calculations, this paper includes one-loop quark fluctuations (the Dirac sea) which generate the pion kinetic term and a non-local attractive contribution, and it systematically explores the sensitivity to different boundary conditions. The results enable future work that includes the Casimir energy contribution and projects out good spin-isospin quantum numbers via collective quantization.
