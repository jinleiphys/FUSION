# Model calculations of the Sivers function satisfying the Burkardt Sum Rule

> It is shown that, at variance with previous analyses, the MIT bag model can explain the available data of the Sivers function and satisfies the Burkardt Sum Rule to a few percent accuracy. The agreement is similar to the one recently found in the constituent quark model. Therefore, these two model calculations of the Sivers function are in agreement with the present experimental and theoretical wisdom.

## Key claim

The MIT bag model, when including both helicity-conserving and helicity-flip contributions from the one-gluon exchange (which were omitted in prior bag model calculations), can describe the available Sivers function data and satisfy the Burkardt Sum Rule to approximately 5% accuracy, contrary to previous claims that the bag model failed in this regard.

## Method

The authors calculated the Sivers function \(f_{1T}^{\perp \cal{Q}} (x, {k_T} )\) in the MIT bag model by expanding the gauge link to next-to-leading order and evaluating the full matrix element including both the helicity-conserving term (proportional to \(\delta_{m_3m_4}\)) and the helicity-flip term (proportional to \(\delta_{m_3,-m_4}\)) in the lower part of the Feynman diagram, which had been omitted in the earlier bag model calculation of Yuan (2003). The calculation used an \(SU(6)\) proton wave function and the Trento convention for the Sivers function definition. The results were evolved at NLO to the experimental scale of \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) for comparison with HERMES data.

## Key numbers

- The Burkardt Sum Rule violation in the previous bag model calculation (Yuan 2003) gave a value of 8.73 MeV, corresponding to a ratio \(r \simeq 0.60\) (60% violation).
- The present calculation yields a Burkardt Sum Rule value of -0.78 MeV, with \(r \simeq 0.05\) (5% violation).
- The constituent quark model calculation of Courtoy et al. (2008) satisfied the Burkardt Sum Rule at the 2% level.
- The evolution was performed starting from a scale of \(\mu_0^2 \simeq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) with only valence quarks carrying a momentum fraction \(\simeq 0.55\) at 0.34 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between the MIT bag model and the constituent quark model (CQM) of Courtoy et al. (2008) regarding the Sivers function, showing that both models can describe the HERMES data and satisfy the Burkardt Sum Rule (Burkardt 2004) when the full calculation is performed. The previous bag model calculation by Yuan (2003) had omitted the helicity-flip term in the one-gluon exchange, leading to a 60% violation of the sum rule, while the instanton-modified bag model of Drago (2005) also failed to satisfy it. This enables consistent model comparisons with upcoming JLab data on transversely polarized \(^3\)He targets and future SIDIS measurements.
