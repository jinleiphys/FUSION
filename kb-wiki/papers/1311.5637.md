# Shape coexistence and triaxiality in nuclei near 80Zr

> Total-Routhian-Surface calculations have been performed to investigate the shape evolutions of $A\sim80$ nuclei, $^{80-84}$Zr, $^{76-80}$Sr and $^{84,86}$Mo. Shape coexistences of spherical, prolate and oblate deformations have been found in these nuclei. Particularly for the nuclei, $^{80}$Sr and $^{82}$Zr, the energy differences between two shape-coexisting states are less than 220 keV. At high spins, the $g_{9/2}$ shell plays an important role for shape evolutions. It has been found that the alignment of the $g_{9/2}$ quasi-particles drives nuclei to be triaxial.

# Shape coexistence and triaxiality in nuclei near 80Zr

## Key claim

Total-Routhian-Surface calculations predict that shape coexistence of spherical, prolate, and oblate deformations occurs in A~80 nuclei, with the energy differences between coexisting states in 80Sr and 82Zr being less than 220 keV; at high spins, the alignment of g9/2 quasiparticles drives these nuclei toward triaxial shapes, but the calculations are limited to even-even isotopes and do not include neutron-proton pairing effects on deformation.

## Method

Total-Routhian-Surface (TRS) calculations were performed using a non-axial deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the Lipkin-Nogami pairing approach, including both monopole and quadrupole pairings, in a deformation space of (β2, γ, β4). The calculations were run self-consistently with respect to pairing, deformation, and rotational frequency for even-even 76-80Sr, 80-84Zr, and 84,86Mo isotopes. Kinematic moments of inertia were computed from the calculated TRS minima and compared with existing experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 80Sr: prolate (β2=0.39) and oblate (β2=-0.23) coexisting states lie only 70 keV and 290 keV above the spherical ground state, respectively.
- For 82Zr: the energy difference between prolate and oblate deformations is -0.112 MeV (i.e., oblate lies 112 keV lower than prolate).
- For 76Sr: oblate deformation (β2=-0.31) coexists 2.04 MeV above the prolate ground state; for 78Sr: oblate (β2=-0.24) lies 1.53 MeV above prolate.
- For 78Zr: prolate-oblate energy difference is 2.02 MeV; for 80Zr: 1.28 MeV.
- For 84Mo: an oblate shape coexists 420 keV above the spherical ground state.
- For 80Zr at ground state: prolate deformation β2=0.42; triaxiality develops at ħω=0.55 MeV and becomes yrast at ħω≈0.7 MeV with I~19ħ, remaining stable up to 38ħ.
- For 84Zr: the triaxial rotational band's kinematic moments of inertia agree well with experiment, showing a steep upbending at rotational frequency around 0.5 MeV/ħ.

## Context

This work extends prior mean-field calculations of shape coexistence in the A~80 region, including the predictions of triaxial deformations in 80Sr and 84Zr by Nazarewicz et al. and Dudek et al., and the global axial-asymmetry calculations for ground states by Moller et al. (2006). The results provide theoretical support for interpreting experimental γ-vibrational bands observed in 80,82Sr, 82,84Zr, and 86Mo by Sienko, Tabor, Rudolph, Doring, and Andgren, and for the superdeformed bands in 80-83Sr and 83,84,86Zr. The predicted triaxiality at high spins enables future experimental searches for wobbling bands in this mass region, analogous to those observed in 163,165,167Lu by Jensen, Schmitz, and Amro.
