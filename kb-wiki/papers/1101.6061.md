# Exploring hybrid star matter at NICA and FAIR

> We discuss constraints for the equation of state of hybrid star matter which can be obtained from heavy-ion collisions at FAIR and NICA. Particular emphasis is on the planned NICA facility at JINR Dubna which shall provide fixed-target and collider experiments just in the relevant energy ranges.

# Exploring hybrid star matter at NICA and FAIR

## Key claim

The paper argues that upcoming heavy-ion collision experiments at NICA and FAIR can provide crucial constraints on the equation of state of hybrid star matter, but only if the equation of state is sufficiently stiff at high densities to accommodate the recently measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses). The authors caution that two-phase approaches using Maxwell or Glendenning constructions cannot make trustworthy statements about the hadron-to-quark matter phase transition region.

## Method

The authors apply a testing scheme from Klahn et al. (2006) that evaluates nuclear equations of state against multiple constraints: maximum neutron star mass, direct URCA cooling avoidance, mass-radius domains from X-ray sources, baryon number of PSR J0737-3039(B), and heavy-ion collision flow and kaon production data. They update this scheme with the new mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 and add a hybrid equation of state (DBHF-NJL) that includes a three-flavor color superconducting NJL model with self-consistently determined masses and diquark gaps, including a vector meson meanfield. The flow constraint from Danielewicz et al. (2002) is applied to the isospin-symmetric part of neutron star equations of state to derive upper bounds on maximum mass.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 mass: (1.97 ± 0.04) solar masses, the heaviest neutron star observed with sufficient accuracy and confidence
- Nuclear saturation density: n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Subthreshold K+ production at E_lab < 1.58 A GeV requires a sufficiently soft equation of state
- Flow data analyzed for E_lab = 0.4 to 10 A GeV from Danielewicz et al. (2002)
- The DBHF equation of state appears too stiff at densities above 3.5 times saturation density
- Assuming deconfinement phase transition at the maximally tolerable stiffness of the Danielewicz et al. constraint gives an upper limit for maximum compact star mass of 2.1 solar masses
- Only four of eight formerly tested nuclear equations of state are compatible with the upper mass value (2.01 solar masses) of PSR J1614-2230; five are compatible with the lower value (1.93 solar masses)
- The DBHF-NJL hybrid equation of state passes 4 out of 6 tests for the strict interpretation and 6 out of 6 for the weakened interpretation
- Neutron star masses in the observed range between 1.23 and 2.01 solar masses may all be hybrid stars with quark matter cores

## Context

This work extends the testing scheme of Klahn et al. (2006) by incorporating the precise mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 from Demorest et al. (2010), which provides a stricter constraint on the stiffness of the equation of state at ultra-high densities. The paper contrasts the astrophysical requirement for a stiff equation of state (to support high neutron star masses) with the heavy-ion collision requirement for a soft equation of state (from subthreshold kaon production data). The authors identify the NICA facility at JINR Dubna and FAIR as ideal experimental venues to probe the deconfinement phase transition in the energy range E_lab = 1 to 5 A GeV (Nuclotron fixed-target range) and E_lab = 10 to 60 A GeV (CBM range and NICA collider range), which would enable testing whether quark matter exists in compact stars.
