# Describing Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter with Gauged Linear sigma-Model

> To describe nuclear matter at high temperature and high baryon density appropriate for RHIC and LHC, an effective theory is proposed. Three developments underlie the effective theory: (1) relativistic mean field theory description of nuclear matter with mesons mediating interactions; (2) topological soliton description of the nucleon with hidden local symmetry; (3) phenomenological knowledge of nucleon-nucleon interaction and nucleon structure obtained from elastic NN scattering at c.m. energies of hundreds of GeV. When these developments are combined together, a gauged linear sigma-model with anomalous action and condensed quark-antiquark ground state emerges as the effective theory.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a gauged linear sigma-model with anomalous action and a condensed quark-antiquark ground state can serve as an effective theory for nuclear matter at high temperature and high baryon density, but the claim is conditional on combining three distinct developments (relativistic mean field theory, topological soliton description of the nucleon, and phenomenological analysis of high-energy elastic scattering) and on taking quarks rather than nucleons as the basic fermions.

## Method

The author constructs a Lagrangian framework starting from the linear sigma-model of Gell-Mann and Levy, then introduces hidden local symmetry [SU(2) x U(1)] to gauge the pion sector, adding vector mesons (rho and omega) as gauge bosons. The fermion-scalar-meson sector is rewritten in terms of new fermionic variables that are invariant under global chiral transformations but transform under the hidden local symmetry, and the Wess-Zumino-Witten anomalous action is included. The resulting Lagrangian combines the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD-II) model for nuclear matter with the gauged nonlinear sigma-model for the nucleon as a topological soliton, and the scalar-isoscalar field zeta is identified as an order parameter whose critical behavior can produce a condensed ground state.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant f_pi is approximately 93 MeV, used as the vacuum value of the scalar field zeta.
* High energy elastic pp and pbar-p scattering was analyzed in the center-of-mass energy range sqrt(s) = 23-630 GeV, providing phenomenological knowledge of nucleon structure.
* The topological soliton model of the nucleon predicts a soliton mass of about 1500 MeV, compared to the actual nucleon mass of 939 MeV, a discrepancy the condensed ground state mechanism aims to resolve.
* At RHIC, temperatures of the order of 200 MeV and nuclear densities about ten times normal nuclear density are expected; at LHC in lead-lead collisions, the c.m. energy per nucleon pair will be 5.4 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field theory framework of Serot and Walecka (1997) and the topological soliton description of the nucleon developed by Meissner et al. (1986, 1987) and others, by incorporating phenomenological constraints from high-energy elastic scattering analysis by Islam et al. (1987). The proposed effective theory aims to enable investigation of the density and temperature dependence of nuclear matter and the chiral phase transition in conditions relevant to RHIC and LHC, building on prior effective theories based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the Lee-Wick-Friedberg model, and the meson-quark-dilaton model.
