# Bose-Einstein condensation in finite drops of alpha particles

> Ground-state properties of finite drops of alpha particles (Q-balls) are studied within a field-theoretical approach in the mean-field approximation. The strong interaction of alphas is described by the scalar field with a sextic Skyrme-like potential. The radial profiles of scalar- and Coulomb fields are found by solving the coupled system of Klein-Gordon and Poisson equations. The formation of shell-like nuclei, with vanishing density around the center, is predicted at high enough attractive strength of Skyrme potential. The equilibrium values of energy and baryon number of Q-balls and Q-shells are calculated for different sets of interaction parameters. Empirical binding energies of alpha-conjugate nuclei are reproduced only if the gradient term in the Lagrangian is strongly enhanced. It is demonstrated that this enhancement can be explained by a finite size of alpha particles.

## Key claim

Empirical binding energies of alpha-conjugate nuclei can be reproduced only when the gradient term in the Lagrangian is strongly enhanced by a factor of about 8-9, and this enhancement can be explained by the finite size of alpha particles rather than by point-like interactions assumed in the Skyrme potential.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Klein-Gordon and Poisson equations in the mean-field approximation for spherical systems of charged alpha particles described by a scalar field with a sextic Skyrme-like potential containing attractive and repulsive terms. They computed radial profiles of the scalar field, Coulomb field, baryon density, and effective mass for different values of the chemical potential and interaction parameters, then calculated total particle numbers and binding energies for both Q-ball and Q-shell configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum particle number for stable Q-balls with parameter Set I (a=7853, b=78.94 MeV^-2): Q_max approximately 14.1, corresponding to Delta=0 (mu=m)
- Binding energy per particle for the largest stable Q-ball with Set I: W approximately 5.6 MeV at Q approximately 14.1
- For Q-shell solutions with a=10^4, b=80 MeV^-2 and Delta=0: Q approximately 33.5, W approximately 8.5 MeV
- For Q-ball solution with same parameters (a=10^4, b=80 MeV^-2, Delta=0): Q approximately 27.3, W approximately 10.1 MeV
- Best-fit parameters (Set II): a=10^4, b=75 MeV^-2, gradient enhancement factor xi_s=8.5
- Surface tension of cold alpha matter for Set I: approximately 0.2 MeV/fm^2, about a factor of 5 smaller than empirical value for ordinary nuclei
- Predicted lifetimes for metastable Q-balls with best-fit parameters: tau greater than or approximately 10^-10 s for mu-m less than or approximately 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field studies of homogeneous uncharged alpha matter (Satarov 2017, 2021) and alpha+N matter (Mishustin 2016, Satarov 2019, 2020) to finite charged systems, building on the Q-ball formalism of Coleman (1985) and Lee (1989). The model predicts shell-like nuclei (Q-shells) at high attractive strength, similar to solutions found by Arodz (2009), Ishihara (2021), and Heeck (2021). The results enable quantitative comparison with empirical binding energies of alpha-conjugate nuclei compiled by von Oertzen (2006) and suggest that alpha-clustered nuclei may be metastable states with lifetimes long enough to be observable in heavy-ion reactions.
