# Confinement from Correlated Instanton-Dyon Ensemble in SU(2) Yang-Mills Theory

> We study the confinement phase transition in $SU(2)$ Yang-Mills theory, based on a statistical ensemble model of correlated instanton-dyons. We show for the first time that such a model provides a quantitative description, in light of the lattice data, for the temperature dependence of the order parameter. We characterize the short-range interaction which plays a crucial role for the properties of such ensemble. The chromo-magnetic charge density as well as the spatial correlations is found to be consistent with known lattice and phenomenological information.

## Key claim

The paper shows for the first time that a statistical ensemble model of correlated instanton-dyons can provide a quantitative description of the confinement phase transition in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, with the holonomy potential minimum moving to the confining value when the ensemble becomes dense enough that short-range repulsive interactions become important.

## Method

The authors construct a statistical ensemble of four types of instanton-dyons (L, M, anti-L, anti-M) with fugacity factors derived from one-loop quantum fluctuations, and include both long-range Coulomb interactions (with Debye screening) and a short-range core-like repulsive potential characterized by strength parameter V_c and range parameter ζ_c. They perform extensive numerical simulations of this ensemble across a wide range of action values S (corresponding to varying temperature) and parameter choices, extracting the holonomy potential and Polyakov loop expectation value. The temperature scale is set via a two-loop running coupling relation S(T) ≈ (22/3) log(T/Λ) + (34/11) log[2 log(T/Λ)].

## Key numbers

- The optimal short-range interaction parameters are V_c = 5 (strength) and ζ_c = 2.4 (range), with χ²/d.o.f ≈ 1.21 for the Polyakov loop temperature dependence compared to lattice data.
- The range parameter ζ_c that gives good agreement with lattice Polyakov loop data lies in the interval [2.2, 2.6].
- The critical scaling near T_c follows the 3D Ising universality class exponent 0.3265, with the form ⟨L_∞⟩ = b(T/T_c - 1)^0.3265 [1 + d(T/T_c - 1)^0.530].
- The chromo-magnetic charge density from the instanton-dyon ensemble is consistent with lattice results from D'Alessandro et al. (2007), with ζ_c = 1.8 giving the best agreement.
- The density of magnetic charges extracted from phenomenological studies near T_c is about ρ T^(-3) ≈ (N_c - 1) · (0.4 to 0.6).
- The spatial density-density correlations show a correlation length on the order of (0.5 to 1) · 1/T.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-dyon ensemble approach pioneered by Shuryak (2013) and Larsen et al. (2015), and builds on earlier analytic studies by Diakonov (2004, 2007, 2009) and numerical investigations by Faccioli (2013), Larsen (2015), Liu (2015), Ramamurti (2018), and Lopez-Ruiz (2016). The results are compared directly with lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory by Digal et al. (2003) and Hubner et al. (2008) for the Polyakov loop, and by D'Alessandro et al. (2007) for chromo-magnetic charge density. The model enables future tests including Polyakov loop behavior in higher representations and topological susceptibility in the transition region.
