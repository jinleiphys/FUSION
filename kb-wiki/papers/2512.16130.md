# Unraveling the anomaly in the production of $^{60}$Fe nucleus in massive stars

> The production of $^{60}$Fe is crucial for nucleosynthesis in massive stars and supernovae. In this work, by using the microscopic EP+IPM (exact pairing plus the independent-particle model) for the nuclear level density (NLD) and extended EP+PDM (exact pairing plus phonon damping model) for the $γ$-ray strength function (gSF), we re-evaluate the substantial enhancement of $^{60}$Fe production recently reported in {\it A. Spyrou et al., Nat. Comm. {\bf 15}, 9608 (2024)}, which was attributed to an unexpectedly large Maxwellian-averaged cross section (MACS). Our analysis demonstrates that this enhancement indeed originates from the choice of NLD, which, despite being constrained to reproduce the total NLD and gSF data, lacks a reliable spin dependence, a critical input for Hauser-Feshbach calculations of nuclear reaction rate. In contrast, our predictions yield a significantly lower MACS, calling the claimed enhancement into question. In particular, our approach highlights the microscopic nature of the low-energy enhancement of the gSF, the so-called upbend resonance, which arises from strong particle-particle ($pp$) and hole-hole ($hh$) excitations that emerge only at finite temperature, thereby further reinsisting on the invalidity of the Brink-Axel hypothesis in this low-energy region. Overall, our study reopens the question on the long-standing problem of $^{60}$Fe production in massive stars.

## Key claim

The enhancement of the Maxwellian-averaged cross section (MACS) for the $^{59}$Fe$(n,\gamma)^{60}$Fe reaction reported by Spyrou et al. (2024) originates from their choice of nuclear level density (NLD) model, which, although constrained to reproduce total NLD data, lacks reliable spin dependence; the authors' own microscopic calculations yield a significantly lower MACS, calling the claimed enhancement into question.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic exact pairing plus independent-particle model (EP+IPM) to calculate the NLD and the extended exact pairing plus phonon damping model (EP+PDM) to calculate the $\gamma$-ray strength function (gSF) for $^{60}$Fe. These inputs were then used in the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model within the TALYS code (v1.95) to compute the $^{59}$Fe$(n,\gamma)^{60}$Fe cross section and its MACS. The EP+IPM NLD was calculated using the canonical-ensemble partition function built on exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian, while the EP+PDM gSF included contributions from $E1$, $M1$, and the upbend resonance (UBR), with the UBR microscopically treated as arising from particle-particle ($pp$) and hole-hole ($hh$) excitations at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The EP+IPM NLD without any normalization agrees with experimental data for $^{60}$Fe across the entire excitation energy range, while HFBC and HFBCS models deviate at $E^*$ below 2-3 MeV.
- The MACS predicted by the EP+IPM and EP+PDM lies at the upper boundary of the Yan et al. (2021) evaluation, which is notably lower than that of Spyrou et al. (2024).
- The Spyrou et al. (2024) MACS is about 1.6-2.0 times higher than the authors' prediction and Yan et al.'s value.
- The UBR gSF at $E_\gamma < 4$ MeV is mainly contributed by $pp$ and $hh$ excitations, with the $pp$ contribution significantly stronger than the $hh$ one.
- The UBR resonance parameters for $^{60}$Fe are: energy 1.4 MeV, cross section 0.35 mb, and width 4.08 MeV (calculated directly from excitations, not a fitted parameter).
- The nuclear temperature for the UBR calculation was chosen at $T=1.1$ MeV, in agreement with the $T\sim 1.0$ MeV value from discrete nuclear levels and the constant temperature model.

## Context

This work directly challenges the enhanced $^{60}$Fe production reported by Spyrou et al. (2024) in Nature Communications, which used the $\beta$-Oslo method to extract NLD and gSF from experimental $\beta^-$-decay spectra of $^{60}$Mn. The authors argue that the enhancement stems from the choice of the renormalized HFBC NLD model, which lacks reliable spin dependence for Hauser-Feshbach calculations, unlike their fully microscopic EP+IPM and EP+PDM approach. The study also extends prior work by Yan et al. (2021), who used the surrogate-ratio method with phenomenological BSFG and SLO models, and by Uberseder et al. (2014), who used Coulomb-dissociation techniques. The results reopen the long-standing problem of $^{60}$Fe production in massive stars and highlight the critical role of microscopic nuclear structure inputs in astrophysical modeling.
