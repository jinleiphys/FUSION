# Momentum anisotropy generation in a hybrid approach

> Anisotropic flow emerges in all three of hybrid approaches: initial conditions, viscous relativistic hydrodynamics as well as hadronic transport. Previous works focus mainly on a constant or temperature dependent shear viscosity $η/s$. Here instead, we study qualitatively the effect of a generalized $η/s(T,μ_B)$ in the hybrid approach SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid. The parameterization takes into account the constraints of matching to the transport coefficients in the hadronic phase, as well as of recent Bayesian analysis results. We compare the effect of the different parameterizations in the intermediate energy region of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 - 39.0 GeV. We observe that using the energy density dependent parameterization decreases the effect of the point of particlization. In addition, we quantify the uncertainty due to different initial state profiles, including the SMASH initial conditions as well as TrENTo and IP-Glasma profiles. It can be shown that the initial state transverse momentum impacts final state momentum anisotropy.

## Key claim
Using a generalized shear viscosity parameterization that depends on both temperature and baryon chemical potential, eta/s(T, mu_B), in the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid approach qualitatively changes momentum anisotropy generation in heavy-ion collisions, but the effect is reduced when an energy-density-dependent parameterization is used; the initial-state transverse momentum profile is shown to impact the final-state momentum anisotropy.

## Method
The authors implemented a generalized eta/s(T, mu_B) parameterization in the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid framework, which couples the SMASH hadronic transport model with the vHLLE viscous hydrodynamics code. They compared this generalized viscosity model against constant and temperature-only-dependent eta/s parameterizations across the intermediate beam-energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 39.0 GeV. The study also quantified uncertainties from different initial-state profiles by comparing SMASH initial conditions with TRENTo and IP-Glasma profiles.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy range studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 39.0 GeV
- Three initial-state profile types compared: SMASH initial conditions, TRENTo profiles, and IP-Glasma profiles

## Context
This work extends previous hybrid-model studies that focused on constant or temperature-dependent shear viscosity eta/s (e.g., the constant eta/s approach commonly used in earlier vHLLE applications) by incorporating a baryon-chemical-potential-dependent eta/s parameterization constrained by Bayesian analysis results and hadronic-phase transport coefficients. The study enables future systematic investigations of how initial-state fluctuations and viscosity parameterizations jointly affect anisotropic flow observables across the Beam Energy Scan energy range, and highlights the need for consistent treatment of particlization when using generalized viscosity models.
