# The longitudinal and transverse responses in the inclusive electron scattering

> The splitting between the charge-longitudinal and spin-transverse responses is explained in a model whose inputs are the effective interactions in the particle-hole channels in the first order boson loop expansion. The interplay between $ω$-meson exchange and box diagrams mainly governs the longitudinal response, while in the transverse one the direct $Δ$ excitations almost cancel the one-loop correction and the response is ruled by the $ρ$-meson rescattering.

## Key claim

The splitting between the charge-longitudinal and spin-transverse nuclear responses in inclusive electron scattering can be explained within a boson loop expansion (BLE) framework, where the longitudinal response is governed by the interplay between omega-meson exchange and box diagrams (two-meson exchange with Delta excitation), while the transverse response is dominated by rho-meson rescattering because direct Delta excitations nearly cancel the one-loop correction. The model requires a density-dependent short-range correlation cut-off and a renormalized omega-meson coupling in the scalar-isoscalar channel to reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors employed a boson loop expansion (BLE) scheme, starting from a path-integral bosonization of a system of nucleons and pions, to classify Feynman diagrams by the number of boson loops. At the one-loop level, they included self-energy, exchange, and correlation diagrams (fig. 2) with effective interactions in the particle-hole channels for correlated pi-, rho-, and omega-meson exchange, plus a Landau-Migdal parameter g' to incorporate short-range correlations. The response functions were computed for 12C and 40Ca at various momentum transfers, with the mean field (RPA) level and one-loop corrections treated separately, including nucleon and Delta excitations in the fermion loops.

## Key numbers

- For 12C at a transferred momentum of 300 MeV/c, the longitudinal response is examined in detail; the box diagrams (two-meson exchange with Delta excitation) dominate and soften the peak.
- The pion coupling constants used: f^2_piNN/(4pi) = 0.08, f^2_piNDelta/(4pi) = 0.32, f^2_piDeltaDelta/(4pi) = 0.016.
- The rho-meson mass was set to 600 MeV in the spin-transverse channel; the rho coupling constants C_rhoNN = C_rhoNDelta = C_rhoDeltaDelta = 2.3 in the spin-transverse channel and 0.05 in the scalar-isovector channel.
- The omega coupling constants: C_omegaNN = C_omegaDeltaDelta = 0.15 in the scalar-isoscalar channel and 1.5 in the isoscalar spin-transverse channel.
- The Landau parameter g'_0 = 0.35; short-range correlation cut-offs: q_cL = 800 MeV/c (longitudinal), q_cT = 1300 MeV/c (transverse).
- Vertex cut-offs: Lambda_piNN = 1300 MeV/c, Lambda_piNDelta = Lambda_piDeltaDelta = 1000 MeV/c, Lambda_rhoNN = 1750 MeV/c (spin-transverse channel), all other vertex cut-offs = 1000 MeV/c.
- For 40Ca, the Fermi momentum was taken as k_F = 1.2 fm^-1; the model produces a more pronounced depletion of the longitudinal response than for 12C, consistent with Saclay data.
- The Coulomb sum rule for 12C was quenched to about 90% in Saclay data (Me-al-84, Me-al-85) but to about 60% for 40Ca; Jourdan (1995) found the corrected sum rule compatible with Z within 1% at q = 570 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the boson loop expansion approach of Cenni et al. (1987) to simultaneously explain both the longitudinal and transverse nuclear responses, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between Saclay data (Me-al-84, Me-al-85) and Bates data (Ya-al-93) regarding the Coulomb sum rule quenching. The model shows that the apparent discrepancy between Carbon and Calcium data can be explained as a density-dependent effect within the BLE framework, without violating the Coulomb sum rule as reanalyzed by Jourdan (1995). The results enable future microscopic calculations of the rho-meson effective mass in the nuclear medium to resolve remaining discrepancies between Saclay and Jourdan data for the transverse response.
