# Indication of a Co-Existing Phase of Quarks and Hadrons in Nucleus - Nucleus Collisions

> The variation of average transverse mass of identified hadrons with charge multiplicity have been studied for AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. The observation of a plateau in the average transverse mass for multiplicities corresponding to SPS energies is attributed to the formation of a co-existence phase of quark gluon plasma and hadrons. A subsequent rise for RHIC energies may indicate a deconfined phase in the initial state. Several possibilities which can affect the average transverse mass are discussed. Constraints on the initial temperature and thermalization time have been put from the various experimental data available at SPS energies.

# Indication of a Co-Existing Phase of Quarks and Hadrons in Nucleus - Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The observed plateau in average transverse mass as a function of charge multiplicity at SPS energies is attributed to the formation of a mixed phase of quark-gluon plasma and hadrons, but the authors note that even if a QGP phase is created at SPS, its space-time volume could be too small to affect the dynamics of the system and hence their conclusions. The subsequent rise at RHIC energies may indicate a deconfined phase in the initial state, but this is presented as a possibility rather than a definitive observation.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data on transverse mass spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from AGS, SPS, and RHIC collisions spanning center-of-mass energies from 2A GeV to 200A GeV, extracting the average transverse mass from inverse slope parameters. They performed (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamic calculations with and without a QCD phase transition, using a hadronic equation of state up to strange sector and a QGP bag model equation of state, to compare the predicted variation of average transverse mass with initial energy density against the experimental trend.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is taken as T_c = 170 MeV, based on lattice QCD predictions.
- The mixed phase exists within the energy density range 0.56 < epsilon (GeV/fm^3) < 1.7, with epsilon_H = 0.56 GeV/fm^3 for the hadronic phase and epsilon_Q = 1.7 GeV/fm^3 for the QGP phase at T_c.
- For pions, the average transverse mass is about 400 MeV for values of initial energy density corresponding to the mixed phase, similar to experimental values at SPS.
- The slope of the average transverse mass versus initial energy density curve for pions in the hadronic scenario is larger than the QGP scenario by a factor of 2.5 at epsilon_i = 1 GeV/fm^3.
- A lower bound on the initial temperature at SPS energies is T_i > 158 MeV, derived from the experimental average transverse mass of pions (approximately 315 MeV after flow subtraction) using the relation T_i > <m_T>_expt / 2.
- The average temperature from direct photon spectra (WA98) is T_av ~ 178 MeV, obtained from an inverse slope of approximately 275 MeV with average radial velocity <v_r> ~ 0.4.
- The thermalization time at SPS energies is constrained to tau_i <= 1.2 fm/c, using dN/dY ~ 700 and the lower bound on initial temperature.

## Context

This work extends the original proposal by Van Hove (1982) that a plateau in transverse momentum versus multiplicity would indicate the onset of a mixed phase in a first-order phase transition scenario. The authors note that previous attempts using cosmic ray data were inconclusive, and data from A-A collisions at fixed center-of-mass energies with varying centrality (WA98, RHIC experiments) had not shown this behavior. The study enables constraints on the initial temperature and thermalization time at SPS energies, and suggests that future experiments at higher RHIC energies may probe a purely deconfined QGP phase.
