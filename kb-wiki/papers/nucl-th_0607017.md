# Partial-Wave Analysis of Single-Pion Production Reactions

> We present an overview of our efforts to analyze pion-nucleon elastic scattering data, along with data from related photo- and electroproduction reactions, in order to study the baryon spectrum. We then focus on the Delta(1232) resonance. Fits to pion photo- and electroproduction data have been used to extract values for the R_EM = E2/M1 and R_SM = S2/M1 ratios as functions of Q^2. These results are compared to other recent determinations.

## Key claim

The authors present a partial-wave analysis framework for single-pion production reactions that extracts the ratios R_EM = E2/M1 and R_SM = S2/M1 for the Delta(1232) resonance as functions of Q^2, but note that their R_EM results appear at odds with other determinations while not being ruled out by recent lattice data.

## Method

The analysis uses a K-matrix formalism for pion photoproduction multipoles, anchored to pion-nucleon elastic scattering partial-wave amplitudes determined from fits to elastic scattering, charge-exchange, and eta N production data constrained by forward and fixed-t dispersion relations. For electroproduction, the Q^2 = 0 photoproduction results are extended to non-zero Q^2 using a phenomenological factor f(Q^2) containing a universal cutoff, a W-dependent term, and searched constants a, b, and c for each multipole. The fits cover the Delta(1232) resonance region, avoiding complications from channels beyond pi N.

## Key numbers

- R_EM at Q^2 = 0: -1.79 ± 0.18 %, determined from pion photoproduction partial-wave analysis
- Electroproduction multipoles extracted for Q^2 values up to 1.50 GeV^2 (shown in figures for Q^2 < 1.50 GeV^2 and Q^2 > 1.00 GeV^2)
- The phenomenological factor f(Q^2) includes a term 1/(1 + Q^2/0.7) with Q^2 in GeV^2
- Branch points identified: pi Delta(1232) at 1350 - i50 MeV, eta N at 1487 - i0 MeV, rho N at 1715 - i73 MeV
- Partial-wave amplitudes analyzed for T_pi from 0 to 2.6 GeV
- R_SM shows qualitative agreement among determinations up to about 4 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the SAID group's ongoing partial-wave analysis program (Arndt et al., various years) for pion-nucleon elastic scattering and photoproduction to electroproduction reactions, providing a unified framework for studying the baryon spectrum. The results are compared to the MAID05 analysis (Drechsel et al., 2005), the DMT model, and experimental results from JLab, MAMI-B, MIT-Bates, and ELSA, as well as lattice QCD calculations. The analysis enables future comparisons of R_EM and R_SM determinations as the electroproduction database expands, with the authors noting that their R_EM curve shows a cross-over from negative to positive values whose exact point has shifted with database growth.
