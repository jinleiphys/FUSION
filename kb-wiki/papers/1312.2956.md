# Longitudinal Coherence in a Holographic Model of p-Pb Collisions

> As a model of the longitudinal structure in heavy ion collisions, we simulate gravitational shock wave collisions in anti-de Sitter space in which each shock is composed of multiple constituents. We find that all constituents act coherently, and their separation leaves no imprint on the resulting plasma, when this separation is $\lesssim 0.26 / T_{hyd}$, with $T_{hyd}$ the temperature of the plasma at the time when hydrodynamics first becomes applicable. In particular, the center-of-mass of the plasma coincides with the center-of-mass of all the constituents participating in the collision, as opposed to the center-of-mass of the individual collisions. We discuss the implications for nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a holographic model of p-Pb collisions, the plasma formed at mid-rapidity is insensitive to the longitudinal substructure of the colliding projectiles (i.e., the separation between constituent nucleons) provided that the characteristic size of each projectile is less than approximately 0.26 divided by the plasma temperature at the time hydrodynamics first becomes applicable. This "longitudinal coherence" means the center-of-mass of the plasma coincides with the center-of-mass of all participating constituents, not with the center-of-mass of individual nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Method

The authors simulate collisions of planar gravitational shock waves in anti-de Sitter space (AdS5), where each shock is composed of multiple Gaussian constituents representing nucleons. They vary the separation between constituents (characterized by the parameter ℓ±) and the width of each constituent (w) to model symmetric collisions (single-single and double-double) and asymmetric collisions (single-double). The resulting energy density of the plasma is computed via the gauge/gravity duality, and the apparent horizon on the dual black hole is used to study the thermal scale physics.

## Key numbers

- The coherence threshold is ℓc ≲ 0.26 / Thyd, where Thyd is the plasma temperature at hydrodynamization.
- For the coherent regime, the hydrodynamization temperature is Thyd = 0.30 μ (from Table 1, single-single and single-double collisions with ℓc Thyd = 0.05 and 0.12).
- For the incoherent regime, ℓc Thyd = 0.36 and 0.48 (from Table 1), leading to about a 30% difference in energy density at mid-rapidity (scaling as Thyd^4).
- The hydrodynamization time for single-single collisions of thin shocks is μ thyd = 0.88 (from Table 1).
- For p(d)+A collisions at strong coupling, the predicted rapidity shift of the maximum particle production is ymax = 0.9–1.2 (LHC) and 1.3–1.7 (RHIC), assuming NA = 15–30 participating nucleons.
- At weak coupling, the predicted shift is ymax = (1/(2 λ̄)) log(NA/NB) + yNN, with λ̄ ≃ 0.25, yielding a value about a factor of 4 larger than the strong-coupling prediction.

## Context

This work extends the holographic shock wave collision model of Chesler (2010) and Casalderrey-Solana (2013) to asymmetric collisions with multiple constituents, motivated by the observation of flow signals in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at the LHC (Aad 2013, Chatrchyan 2013, Abelev 2012) and d-Au collisions at RHIC (Adare 2013, Sickles 2013). The results enable a comparison between strong-coupling (holographic) and weak-coupling (perturbative QCD, using saturation scales as in Kharzeev 2001, 2004) predictions for the rapidity shift of the maximum particle production in p(d)+A collisions, which could be tested against LHC p-Pb data (ALICE 2012).
