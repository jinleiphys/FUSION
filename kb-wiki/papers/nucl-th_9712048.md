# Electromagnetic Probes of Dense Matter in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Dilepton and photon production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies is studied in the relativistic transport model that incorporates self-consistently the change of hadron masses in dense matter. It is found that the dilepton spectra in proton-nucleus reactions can be well described by the conventional mechanisms of Dalitz decay, primary vector meson decay, decay of charmed mesons, and the initial Drell-Yan processes. However, to provide a quantitative explanation of the observed dilepton spectra in central heavy-ion collisions requires contributions other than these direct decays and also various medium effects. Introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium, we find that the low-mass dilepton enhancement can be satisfactorily explained. Furthermore, to explain the intermediate-mass dilepton enhancement in heavy-ion collisions, secondary processes such as $πa_1\to l{\bar l}$ are found to be very important. Finally, the single photon spectra in our calculations with either free or in-medium meson masses do not exceed the upper limit measured by the WA80 Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons in central heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies can be explained by introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium, while the intermediate-mass dilepton enhancement requires secondary hadronic processes such as πa₁ → l\bar{l}, and the single photon spectra do not exceed the WA80 upper limit even without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the Walecka-type model, with initial conditions from the RQMD string fragmentation model, to simulate dilepton and photon production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies. They incorporated self-consistent medium effects through scalar and vector fields, including dropping vector meson masses via a meson-quark coupling model, and calculated contributions from Dalitz decays, direct vector meson decays, pion-pion annihilation, kaon-antikaon annihilation, and secondary processes such as πρ, πω, πa₁, K\bar{K}, and K\bar{K}* annihilation with cross sections constrained by e⁺e⁻ annihilation data.

## Key numbers

- In S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, using in-medium meson masses produces a factor of 2-3 enhancement of dilepton yield in the mass region from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV compared to free meson masses, bringing agreement with CERES data.
- For Pb+Au collisions at 160 AGeV, the average charged particle pseudo-rapidity density dNch/dη is about 440 in the pseudo-rapidity range of 2 to 3.
- The direct photon excess over background sources is about 5% for central S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, as measured by the WA80 Collaboration.
- The coefficient C_{l⁺l⁻} in the dielectron channel is 8.814×10⁻⁶, 0.767×10⁻⁶, and 1.344×10⁻⁶ for ρ, ω, and φ mesons, respectively.
- The πa₁ process is the most important source for dimuon yields in the intermediate-mass region from about 1 GeV to about 2.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Cassing et al. (1996) using Hadron-String Dynamics and Srivastava et al. (1996) using hydrodynamical models, which also found that standard scenarios with vacuum meson properties underestimate low-mass dilepton data from the CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations. The dropping vector meson mass scenario builds on predictions by Brown and Rho (1991) and Hatsuda and Lee (1992) regarding chiral symmetry restoration, and the results are consistent with the alternative approach of Rapp et al. (1996) using rho meson spectral function broadening. The study enables quantitative use of intermediate-mass dileptons as a probe of phase transitions and QGP formation, while showing that the WA80 photon data can be explained without invoking QGP if sufficient hadronic degrees of freedom are included, consistent with the findings of Sollfrank et al. (1996) and Cleymans et al. (1997).
