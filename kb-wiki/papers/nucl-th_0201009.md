# Interpretation of the Wigner Energy as due to RPA Correlations

## Key claim
In a schematic model with equidistant fourfold degenerate single-nucleon levels, an isovector pairing force, and a symmetry force, the RPA correlation energy rises almost linearly with isospin T, producing a Wigner term that yields the empirical proportionality of the symmetry energy to T(T+1). The linear term arises specifically from the zero-point energy of the collective iso-rotational normal mode, i.e., from quantal fluctuation of the isospin.

## Method
The author constructs a schematic Hamiltonian with equidistant single-nucleon levels (Ω levels, spacing η), an isovector pairing force (coupling G), and a symmetry force (coupling κ). The RPA correlation energy is calculated from a Hartree-Bogolyubov (not Fock) self-consistent state derived from a Routhian that constrains particle number and isospin projection Tz. The RPA ground state energy is obtained via boson expansion, with parameters chosen to simulate the A=48 isobaric chain (Ω=24, η=2.1 MeV, Δ=1.7 MeV, κ=1.2 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Ω = 24 equidistant single-nucleon levels, spacing η = 2.1 MeV, pairing gap Δ = 1.7 MeV, symmetry force coupling κ = 1.2 MeV (parameters simulating the A=48 isobaric chain).
- The Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov energy E₀ varies as E₀ − E₀,T=0 ≈ ½(η+κ)T², i.e., quadratic in T.
- The RPA correlation energy E₂ − E₂,T=0 equals μ/2 for T≈0, giving a linear term ½(η+κ)T.
- The total ground state energy becomes E − E_T=0 = ½(η+κ)T(T+1).
- For Δ=0, the first sum in the expression for E₂ produces a Wigner term of only κ/(η+κ) ≈ 35% of the full value.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the Wigner energy, the extra binding in nearly symmetric nuclei not captured by the quadratic symmetry term of the semi-empirical mass formula (Myers and Swiatecki, 1966). Prior explanations include Wigner's estimate of T(T+4) dependence from bond counting, Talmi's proof of T(T+1) dependence for seniority-conserving forces in a single j-shell, and Bohr and Mottelson's attribution to an interaction proportional to the scalar product of nucleon isospins. The present result shows that the RPA correlation energy, arising from the collective iso-rotational mode due to the isobaric non-invariance of the isovector pair field, generates the full T(T+1) dependence. This explains why Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with Skyrme forces produce no Wigner term (Satuła and Wyss, 2001) while the present RPA treatment does, and it enables more accurate comparisons of mean-field calculations with empirical masses.
