# Quasi-Elastic $d(\vec{p},\vec{n})2p$ Reaction and Spin Response Functions of the Deuteron

> We calculated response functions of the deuteron for charge exchange processes, including the final state interaction between two protons. Using them we evaluated the double differential cross section and polarization observables of $d(\vec{p},\vec{n})2p$ by means of plane wave impulse approximation with an optimal factorization. Calculation well reproduced the shape of the energy spectra of the cross section, though somewhat overestimated the magnitude. It also reproduced the spin observables well.

## Key claim

The authors calculated deuteron spin response functions for charge-exchange processes including the final-state interaction between the two outgoing protons, and used them to evaluate the $d(\vec{p},\vec{n})2p$ reaction observables within the plane wave impulse approximation with optimal factorization; the calculation reproduced the shape of the energy spectra of the cross section well but somewhat overestimated the magnitude, while spin observables were reproduced well.

## Method

The authors employed the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with an optimal factorization formalism, treating the transition operator as the sum of NN transition operators. They calculated the deuteron wave function using the Reid soft core potential and solved the Schrodinger equation for the final $2p$ scattering states with the same potential for $J \leq 2$, while for $J \geq 3$ the nuclear interaction was neglected and only Coulomb wave functions were used. The NN scattering amplitudes were taken from two sources: Bugg and Wilkin, and the SAID system (phase shift SM89 of Arndt et al.).

## Key numbers

- The incident proton energy was 495 MeV at laboratory angle $\theta_{lab}=18^{\circ}$, corresponding to the experiment of Chen et al.
- The momentum transfer range considered was $|\mathbf{q}| \gtrsim 1$ fm$^{-1}$ for the quasi-elastic region.
- The ratio $R^{d}_{L}/R^{d}_{T}$ was found to be close to 1 at the peak region but deviated rather much from 1 at high and low $\omega$, consistent with the result of Pandharipande et al.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the $d(p,n)2p$ reaction by Phillips, Dass and Queen, and Ramavataram and Ho-Kim, who calculated at lower incident energies (30-160 MeV and 25-100 MeV respectively) with simpler treatments of the final-state correlation. The calculation directly addresses the experiment of Chen et al. (LAMPF, 495 MeV) and the earlier work of Carey et al., who had found $R_{L}/R_{T} \lesssim 1$ for the deuteron, contradicting the prediction of Alberico et al. that the ratio should be much larger than 1 due to pion condensation precursor phenomena. The present results, together with those of Pandharipande et al., show that the deuteron ratio is close to 1 at the peak, supporting the use of the deuteron as a reference for extracting nuclear response functions from $(p,n)$ experiments.
