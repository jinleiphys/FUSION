# A study of $J/ψ$ mass shift and bound states: Impact of $D D$ and $DD^*$ meson loops

> We investigate the modification of the $J/ψ$ meson mass in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures employing an effective Lagrangian approach that considers the contributions of $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loops. The medium dependence of $D$ meson masses is determined using the hadronic chiral SU(3) model, where scalar condensates are calculated and subsequently utilized in the QCD sum rules approach. Our findings indicate that an increase in baryonic density results in a negative mass shift of the $J/ψ$ meson. This suggests that the $J/ψ$ meson is attracted to nuclear mean fields indicating the possibility of the formation of meson-nucleus bound states. Moreover, we have also determined the binding energy and absorption decay width of the $J/ψ$ meson for both the ground and excited states of $\text{O}^{16}$, $\text{Ca}^{40}$, $\text{Zr}^{90}$, and $\text{Pb}^{208}$ nuclei. These results are expected to contribute to the understanding of experimental data from upcoming studies at Jefferson lab and the facility for antiproton and ion research, where low-momentum charmed mesons can be produced and examined within nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $J/\psi$ meson experiences a negative mass shift in asymmetric nuclear matter due to $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loop contributions, with the magnitude of this shift depending on baryonic density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry; the abstract does not specify that the mass shift is computed using an effective Lagrangian approach where the medium dependence of $D$ meson masses is determined by combining the hadronic chiral SU(3) model with QCD sum rules.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium masses of $D$ and $D^*$ mesons using QCD sum rules, where the scalar quark condensates $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle_{\rho_B}$ and $\langle \bar{d}d \rangle_{\rho_B}$ and the gluon condensate $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a\mu\nu} \rangle_{\rho_B}$ are obtained from the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean-field model. These medium-dependent $D$ and $D^*$ masses are then used as input in an effective Lagrangian approach to evaluate the $J/\psi$ self-energy via $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loops, yielding the $J/\psi$ mass shift. The binding energies and absorption decay widths of $J/\psi$ for ground and excited states of $^{16}\text{O}$, $^{40}\text{Ca}$, $^{90}\text{Zr}$, and $^{208}\text{Pb}$ are determined by solving the Klein-Gordon equation.

## Key numbers

- The magnitude of $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle_{\rho_B}$ is $-1.10 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{GeV}^3$ at $T=0$ MeV and changes to $-1.17 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{GeV}^3$ at $T=100$ MeV, at nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.15\,\text{fm}^{-3}$ in symmetric nuclear matter.
- The magnitude of $\langle \bar{d}d \rangle_{\rho_B}$ is $-0.74 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{GeV}^3$ at $T=0$ MeV and changes to $-0.80 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{GeV}^3$ at $T=100$ MeV, at nuclear saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter.
- At nuclear saturation density, the quark condensate $\langle q\bar{q} \rangle_{\rho_B}$ drops to nearly 36% of its vacuum value, while the gluon condensate $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a\mu\nu} \rangle_{\rho_B}$ experiences a reduction of only 6% of its vacuum value.
- The up and down quark masses used are $m_u = 5$ MeV and $m_d = 7$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $J/\psi$ mass shifts in nuclear matter, which reported values ranging from $-4$ to $-7$ MeV using QCD sum rules (Klingl 1998, Hayashigaki 1998, Kim 2000) to approximately $-21$ MeV using color polarizability (Sibirtsev 2005), and $-16$ to $-24$ MeV using the quark-meson coupling model (Krein 2010). The present study specifically builds on the work of Chhabra (2020), which analyzed the $J/\psi$ mass shift in symmetric nuclear matter using only the $DD$ meson loop, by extending the analysis to asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures and including both $DD$ and $DD^*$ meson loops. The results are intended to inform upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab and the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR), where low-momentum charmed mesons can be produced and examined within nuclei.
