# Formation of hadrons at chemical freeze-out

> We use a kinetic condition to predict the chemical freeze-out parameters for hadronic species produced in heavy ion collisions. The resulting freeze-out lines for different hadrons lie close to one another in the temperature and baryochemical potential plane, defining a universal, narrow region. The chemical freeze-out is driven by the localization of hadrons due to the chiral symmetry breaking and confining aspects of the QCD transition.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions is driven by a rapid formation of hadrons triggered by chiral symmetry breaking and colour confinement, rather than being a purely statistical process; the freeze-out lines for different hadron species lie close together in the temperature–baryochemical potential plane, defining a narrow universal region.

## Method

The authors impose a kinetic freeze-out condition equating the fireball expansion rate (estimated from entropy-conserving flow) with the collision time scale for flavour-changing hadron–hadron reactions. Hadron–hadron cross sections are modelled using the geometric Povh–Hüfner scaling law, with hadron radii derived from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, and the string tension is given a phenomenological medium dependence. The freeze-out lines for pions, nucleons, kaons, and lambda hyperons are then computed in the temperature–baryochemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

* The freeze-out temperature difference between kaons and pions at zero baryochemical potential is ΔT_f ≈ 4.7 MeV, and this level persists for μ ≲ 0.6 GeV.
* The universal parametrization of the freeze-out line is T(μ) = a − bμ² − cμ⁴ with a = 0.166 ± 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 ± 0.016 GeV⁻¹, and c = 0.053 ± 0.021 GeV⁻³.
* The energy dependence of the baryochemical potential is given by μ(√s) = d / (1 + e√s) with d = 1.308 ± 0.028 GeV and e = 0.273 ± 0.008 GeV⁻¹.
* The vacuum string tension is λ₀ = 0.197 GeV⁻²; the phenomenological string tension uses n₀ = 0.08 fm⁻³ and α ∼ 2.
* The dimensionless constant relating expansion time to entropy density is a = 2.86, with entropy density factors ξ_F = 4.2 and ξ_B = 3.6.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to chemical freeze-out (Cleymans 2005, Andronic 2005) by providing a dynamical mechanism based on hadron formation, rather than a purely empirical fit. It directly relates freeze-out parameters to the QCD transition order parameters (chiral condensate and string tension), and its predictions are closer to the HADES point at √s = 2.4 GeV than the earlier universal parametrization. The model enables future unified kinetic descriptions of both chemical and kinetic freeze-out, and suggests that the QCD transition at high baryon densities (as explored by HADES, FAIR, and NICA) should lie near the chemical freeze-out line.
