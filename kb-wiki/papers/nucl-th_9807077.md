# Consistent off-shell $πN N$ vertex and nucleon self-energy

> We present a consistent calculation of half-off-shell form factors in the pion-nucleon vertex and the nucleon self-energy. Numerical results are presented. Near the on-shell point the pion-nucleon vertex is dominated by the pseudovector coupling, while at large nucleon invariant masses we find a sizable pseudoscalar admixture.

## Key claim

The paper presents a consistent, non-perturbative calculation of half-off-shell form factors in the pion-nucleon vertex and the nucleon self-energy using a Schwinger-Dyson equation approach with dispersion relations, finding that near the on-shell point the vertex is dominated by pseudovector coupling while at large nucleon invariant masses a sizable pseudoscalar admixture appears, but only when the cut-off function half-width is below a critical value of about 1.3 GeV².

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon self-energy and the half-off-shell πNN vertex using an iterative procedure based on dispersion relations and Cutkosky rules, including only the one-pion-nucleon channel. The imaginary parts of the form factors and self-energy functions were calculated from the pole contributions of loop integrals, and the real parts were reconstructed via unsubtracted dispersion relations. A phenomenological cut-off function was introduced to regularize the divergent integrals, and the iteration was continued until convergence to six significant digits was achieved (about 100 iterations).

## Key numbers

- Critical cut-off half-width for convergence: Λ²_I(λ_c) = 1.28 GeV² for the first cut-off function and Λ²_II(d_c) = 1.33 GeV² for the second cut-off function
- Critical cut-off parameter λ_c ≈ 1.7 GeV for cut-off function G⁰_I(p²) and d_c ≈ 1.65 for cut-off function G⁰_II(p²)
- On-shell nucleon mass shift from self-energy: Σ_L(m) = -48.8 MeV for case (I) and -47.2 MeV for case (II)
- Ratio of pseudoscalar to pseudovector form factor at the pion threshold: G_PS(w²_th)/g ≈ 2.1% for both cases (I) and (II)
- Physical pion-nucleon coupling constant used: g = 13.02

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of off-shell nucleon-meson vertices using dispersion relation techniques (Bincer, 1960) and perturbative one-meson loop dressing (photoffloop), but introduces a non-perturbative solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equation that treats the vertex and self-energy consistently. The results are designed for use in a K-matrix model for pion-nucleon scattering and pion photoproduction (Goudsmit, 1994; Korsch, 1997), and the finding of a strong momentum-dependent pseudoscalar admixture at large off-shell momenta extends earlier phenomenological observations in pion photoproduction (Kamalov, 1998).
