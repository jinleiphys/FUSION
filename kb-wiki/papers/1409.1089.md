# Relativistic viscous hydrodynamics order by order

> In this paper, we propose a method of solving the viscous hydrodynamics order by order in a derivative expansion. In such a method, the zero-order solution is just one of the ideal hydrodynamics. All the other higher order corrections satisfy the same first-order partial differential equations but with different inhomogeneous terms. We take the Bjorken flow as an example to test the validity of our method and present how to deal with the problems about the initial condition and perturbation evolution in our formalism.

# Relativistic viscous hydrodynamics order by order

## Key claim

The authors propose a perturbative method for solving relativistic viscous hydrodynamics by expanding all quantities in powers of the Knudsen number and requiring the conservation equations to be satisfied order by order independently, with the zero-order solution being exactly the ideal hydrodynamic solution. The method is demonstrated for conformal fluids and tested on Bjorken flow, but the authors note that "more general cases can be extended straightforwardly and will be presented elsewhere."

## Method

The authors expand the fluid 4-velocity and temperature in power series of the Knudsen number, then require the energy-momentum conservation equation ∂νT_i^{νμ}=0 to hold for each order i independently. This yields a recursive structure where the nth-order corrections satisfy the same first-order partial differential equations as the lower orders, but with different inhomogeneous source terms that depend only on previously solved lower-order solutions. The method is applied to (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken flow for a conformal non-charged fluid in the Landau frame, solving explicitly up to second order.

## Key numbers

- For Bjorken flow, the zero-order energy density is ε₀(τ) = C/τ^{4/3}, where C is a constant and τ is proper time
- The first-order temperature correction is T₁ = (η̂₀)/(2τ₀^{2/3})[1-(τ₀/τ)^{2/3}]T₀, where η̂₀ is a constant and τ₀ is the initial proper time
- The first-order energy density correction is ε₁ = (2η̂₀)/(τ₀^{2/3})[1-(τ₀/τ)^{2/3}]ε₀
- The second-order temperature correction is T₂ = (η̂₀τ̂_{Π0} - λ̂_{1,0})/(6τ₀^{4/3})[1-(τ₀/τ)^{4/3}]T₀, where τ̂_{Π0} and λ̂_{1,0} are dimensionless second-order transport coefficients
- The validity condition for the expansion is η̂₀/τ₀^{2/3} ≪ 1 and |η̂₀τ̂_{Π0} - λ̂_{1,0}|/τ₀^{4/3} ≪ 1
- The perturbation δ̃W (a transverse mode) grows as (τ/τ₀)^{1/3} with proper time, showing no exponential instability

## Context

This work extends the second-order viscous hydrodynamics framework of Israel and Stewart (1979) and the conformal hydrodynamic expansion of Baier et al. (2008) by providing a systematic order-by-order recursive solution method. The approach is similar to methods used by Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) and Banerjee et al. (2008) in the context of fluid-gravity duality. The method enables controlled calculations of higher-order viscous corrections to ideal hydrodynamics, with potential applications to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and addresses the initial condition problem by allowing all initial data to be assigned to the zero-order ideal solution.
