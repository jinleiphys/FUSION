# Vud from Nuclear Mirror Transitions

> We review the determination of Vud from nuclear mirror transitions and describe current experimental and theoretical efforts aimed at improving its precision.

# Vud from Nuclear Mirror Transitions

## Key claim

The value of Vud extracted from nuclear mirror transitions is 0.9719(17), but the precision is dominated by experimental error on the mixing ratios rather than by theoretical uncertainties, and the result currently carries less weight than determinations from superallowed pure Fermi transitions.

## Method

The analysis combines experimental measurements of QEC-values, branching ratios, and half-lives for mirror transitions between isobaric analogue states in isospin doublets, together with theoretical isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) corrections and transition-dependent radiative corrections. The corrected decay rate Ft^m is constructed from the vector statistical rate function f_V, the partial half-life t, radiative corrections delta'_R and delta_NS, the ISB correction delta_C, and the mixing ratio rho that quantifies the relative Gamow-Teller and Fermi contributions.

## Key numbers

- Vud = 0.9719(17) from nuclear mirror transitions, with the uncertainty dominated by experimental errors on mixing ratios
- The 19Ne half-life has been measured at KVI, TRIUMF, and GANIL with relative uncertainties smaller than 5x10^-4
- A measurement of the beta-nu angular correlation a in 35Ar decay achieved a statistical precision of about 0.4-0.5%
- The largest difference between two calculations of ISB corrections (shell-model Wood-Saxon versus density functional theory with Skyrme interaction) occurs for 37K and amounts to 0.7%

## Context

This work extends the determination of Vud beyond the superallowed pure Fermi transitions reviewed by Towner and Hardy (2010), which achieved a precision of 2x10^-4. The mirror transition approach provides an independent cross-check of the CVC hypothesis and the Cabibbo angle. The paper describes ongoing experimental programs at GANIL (Paul trap measurements of beta-nu correlation), at the new BECOLA end-station at NSCL (relative beta-asymmetry measurements), and at KVI and TRIUMF (lifetime measurements), which together aim to reduce the dominant uncertainty from mixing ratios and enable a more precise Vud determination.
