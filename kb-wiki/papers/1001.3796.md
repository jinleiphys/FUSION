# Lattice QCD predictions for shapes of event distributions along the freezeout curve in heavy-ion collisions

> We present lattice QCD results along the freezeout curve of heavy-ion collisions for the ongoing RHIC energy scan as well as the LHC heavy-ion runs planned for this year. The skew and kurtosis of the event distribution of baryon number are positive at all energies studied, We predict three ratios of non-linear susceptibilities along the freezeout curve as functions of the colliding energy, Sqrt[S_{NN}]. They have smooth behaviour except in the vicinity of a critical point; this can have multiple benefits.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD predictions for three ratios of non-linear susceptibilities (skew, kurtosis, and their combination) along the freezeout curve of heavy-ion collisions, claiming these ratios have smooth behavior except in the vicinity of a critical point. The hedge is that the predictions depend sensitively on the assumed crossover temperature T_c = 170 MeV, and changing T_c by 5 MeV can shift results by up to 10% at high energies, with much larger effects near the critical region.

## Method

The authors use Padé resummations of Taylor series expansions of the baryon number susceptibility, computed from lattice QCD at zero chemical potential with two lattice spacings (N_t = 4 and 6) and a pion mass of about 230 MeV. They map the freezeout curve from experimental data to the dimensionless variables z = μ_B/T and T/T_c, then compute the ratios m_1 = T χ^(3)/χ^(2), m_2 = T^2 χ^(4)/χ^(2), and m_3 = T χ^(4)/χ^(3) along this curve for collision energies from √s_NN = 5 GeV to 5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At √s_NN = 200 GeV (top RHIC energy), with T_c = 170 MeV: m_1 = 0.140 ± 0.008, m_2 = 0.95 ± 0.06, m_3 = 6.99 ± 0.06 (statistical errors only)
- With T_c = 175 MeV at √s_NN = 200 GeV: m_1 = 0.14 ± 0.01, m_2 = 1.04 ± 0.07, m_3 = 7.3 ± 0.1
- The freezeout parameters range from z = 4.7(2) and T/T_c = 0.70(3) at √s_NN = 5.0 GeV to z = 0.0058(2) and T/T_c = 0.98(1) at √s_NN = 5.0 TeV
- The kurtosis m_2 becomes negative in the critical region for the smallest lattice spacing, a behavior impossible in the hadron resonance gas model

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD computations of non-linear susceptibilities by Gavai and Gupta (2008, 2009) to make direct predictions along the experimentally determined freezeout curve from Cleymans et al. (2006). It enables experimental tests of lattice QCD in a thermal environment at RHIC and LHC, and provides a baseline for detecting a critical point through non-monotonic behavior in the ratios. The results contradict the hadron resonance gas model prediction of m_2 = 1 at all energies, and the authors note that an ideal quark gas would give m_2 = 2/(3π^2), which is very different from their results.
