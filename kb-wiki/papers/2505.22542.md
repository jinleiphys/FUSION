# Renormalizing the Quark-Meson-Diquark Model

> We present a comprehensive study of the two-flavor Quark--Meson--Diquark (QMD) model by comparing a renormalization approach with a renormalization-group (RG) consistent mean-field formulation based on the functional renormalization group (FRG). The renormalized QMD model allows analytical investigations of key quantities such as the zero-temperature diquark gap and the critical temperature for color superconductivity, ultimately reproducing the exact BCS relation in the high-density limit. We carry out the same analysis for different schemes of RG-consistent QMD models. We show that the RG-consistent approach yields a phase diagram and thermodynamic properties qualitatively similar to those of the renormalized model, provided both are embedded within a unified scheme that ensures consistent vacuum properties. In particular, both treatments recover the Stefan--Boltzmann limit at high densities. On the other hand, whether the BCS relation for the critical temperature is satisfied depends on the details of the RG-consistent setup. Our results highlight the relevance of renormalization and RG-consistent methods for accurately capturing the thermodynamics of QMD and related effective models with diquark degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a renormalized Quark-Meson-Diquark (QMD) model and an RG-consistent mean-field formulation yield qualitatively similar phase diagrams and thermodynamic properties when both are embedded in a unified scheme ensuring consistent vacuum properties, but whether the RG-consistent approach reproduces the exact BCS relation for the critical temperature depends on the details of the RG-consistent setup.

## Method
The authors implemented a renormalization procedure for the two-flavor QMD model using three-momentum cutoff regularization, absorbing all ultraviolet divergences into the bare parameters of the Lagrangian at the mean-field level. Separately, they formulated an RG-consistent mean-field approximation within the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework, integrating out quantum fluctuations while maintaining RG invariance of the effective potential, and compared the two approaches by fixing model parameters to identical physical vacuum observables (pion mass, decay constant, vanishing diquark gap in vacuum).

## Key numbers
- The zero-temperature diquark gap and critical temperature for color superconductivity were derived analytically, reproducing the exact BCS relation in the high-density limit.
- Both the renormalized and RG-consistent approaches recover the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high densities.
- The asymptotic expansion of the quark loop contribution at large cutoff shows divergences scaling as: \(- \frac{3}{2\pi^2} \Lambda^4 - \frac{(3 m_q^2 + 2 \Delta_{\text{gap}}^2)}{2\pi^2} \Lambda^2 + \left( \frac{3 m_q^4}{4\pi^2} + \frac{m_q^2 \Delta_{\text{gap}}^2}{\pi^2} + \frac{\Delta_{\text{gap}}^4}{2\pi^2} - \mu^2 \frac{2 \Delta_{\text{gap}}^2}{\pi^2} \right) \ln \Lambda\).
- The bare couplings must scale with cutoff as: \(m_\phi^2(\Lambda) = \frac{3 g_\phi^2}{\pi^2} \Lambda^2 + \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(m_\Delta^2(\Lambda) = \frac{g_\Delta^2}{\pi^2} \Lambda^2 + \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(Z_\Delta(\Lambda) = -\frac{g_\Delta^2}{2\pi^2} \ln \Lambda + \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(\lambda_\phi(\Lambda) = -\frac{3 g_\phi^4}{\pi^2} \ln \Lambda + \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(\lambda_{\text{mix}}(\Lambda) = -\frac{2 g_\phi^2 g_\Delta^2}{\pi^2} \ln \Lambda + \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(\lambda_\Delta(\Lambda) = -\frac{g_\Delta^4}{2\pi^2} \ln \Lambda + \mathcal{O}(1)\).

## Context
This work extends the renormalization approach recently discussed in Gholami (2024) and explicitly demonstrated using dimensional regularization in Andersen (2024) for the QMD model, and builds on the RG-consistent mean-field methodology applied to the three-flavor NJL model in Gholami (2024). The QMD model serves as a controlled testing ground for directly contrasting renormalization and RG-consistent schemes, providing insight into their quality for other effective models of QCD with diquark degrees of freedom, particularly for studying color-superconducting phases at high baryon density.
