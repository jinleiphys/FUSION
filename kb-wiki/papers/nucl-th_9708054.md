# Pion Cloud Contributions to the Proton's Weak Magnetic Form Factor

> We report on the contributions to the nucleon weak magnetic and electric form factors arising from isospin-breaking form factors. The relativized quark model used in the calculations includes isospin impurities in the large basis nucleon wavefunctions and uses constituent quark currents with both Pauli and Dirac components. Significant contributions are found to arise from the Pauli component when it is interpreted as arising from pion-baryon loops. Including these pion cloud contributions reduces the strange magnetic form factor extracted from the recent SAMPLE measurement of the proton's weak neutral magnetic form factor at low Q^2 by 35 to 52%.

# Pion Cloud Contributions to the Proton's Weak Magnetic Form Factor

## Key claim

Including pion cloud contributions to the Pauli component of constituent quark currents reduces the strange magnetic form factor extracted from the SAMPLE measurement of the proton's weak neutral magnetic form factor at low Q^2 by 35 to 52%, but this reduction depends on the assumption that the Pauli term arises from pion-baryon loops and that the ratio of u and d quark anomalous moments is approximately -2 to -3.

## Method

The authors used a relativized quark model based on the light-cone formalism with a large basis of harmonic oscillator states (up to 8 hbar omega) to calculate isospin-breaking form factors for the nucleon. The model includes different masses for u and d quarks, electromagnetic interactions between quarks, and both Dirac and Pauli electromagnetic form factors for constituent quarks, where the Pauli component is interpreted as arising from pion-nucleon and pion-delta loops. The parameters of the quark model Hamiltonian were determined by fitting the baryon spectrum, with the quark mass difference m_d - m_u = 3.6 MeV fixed by fitting the nucleon mass difference M_n - M_p = 1.29 MeV, and the anomalous moments and form factor parameters were determined by fitting observed proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass difference m_d - m_u = 3.6 MeV was fixed by fitting the nucleon mass difference M_n - M_p = 1.29 MeV
- The rms average isospin violation from T=1/2 dynamic distortion is A = 1.57 x 10^{-3} and from T=3/2 admixture is B = 0.88 x 10^{-3}, giving a total average violation C = 1.8 x 10^{-3}
- The anomalous moments used are kappa^{(p)}_u = 0.12 = -kappa^{(n)}_d and kappa^{(p)}_d = -0.04 = -kappa^{(n)}_u
- The form factor parameters are Lambda_1^2 = 1.22 GeV^2 and Lambda_2^2 = 0.30 GeV^2
- At Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2, the contribution to the weak neutral form factor from the pion cloud is between -0.02 and -0.03 n.m.
- The SAMPLE extracted value of G^S_M = +0.23 +/- 0.09 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.05 at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 is reduced by 35 to 52% when including pion cloud contributions
- The u+d isospin-breaking magnetic form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 is 124 x 10^{-3} n.m. with Pauli contributions included, compared to 2.3 x 10^{-3} n.m. without Pauli contributions
- The u+d isospin-breaking electric form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 is -27 x 10^{-3} with Pauli contributions, compared to 0.0 x 10^{-3} without

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the SAMPLE experiment (Mueller et al., 1997) which measured the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron scattering from the proton at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2, by calculating isospin-breaking form factors that modify the standard relation between the neutral weak magnetic form factor and the strange magnetic form factor. The calculations use the relativized quark model of Capstick and Keister (1995) and Capstick and Isgur (1986), and the isospin-breaking formalism follows Dmitrasinovic and Pollock (1995). The results indicate that pion cloud effects are comparable in size to kaon cloud effects expected for strange form factors, and the authors note that further work is needed to understand the effects of rho-omega mixing and omega-phi mixing on these form factors, as well as planned experiments at Mainz and TJNAF that could provide information on both pion and kaon cloud components.
