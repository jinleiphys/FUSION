# Analysis of the differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data for $γp \to η^\prime p$

> The photoproduction reaction of $γp \to η^\prime p$ is investigated based on an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level approximation, with the purpose being to understand the reaction mechanisms and to extract the resonance contents and the associated resonance parameters in this reaction. Apart from the $t$-channel $ρ$ and $ω$ exchanges, $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon exchanges, and generalized contact term, the exchanges of a minimum number of nucleon resonances in the $s$ channel are taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the experimental data. It is found that a satisfactory description of the available data on both differential cross sections and photon beam asymmetries can be obtained by including in the $s$ channel the exchanges of the $N(1875)3/2^-$ and $N(2040)3/2^+$ resonances. The reaction mechanisms of $γp \to η^\prime p$ are discussed and a prediction for the target nucleon asymmetries is presented.

# Analysis of the differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data for γp → η′p

## Key claim

A satisfactory description of both differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data for γp → η′p can be obtained by including only the N(1875)3/2⁻ and N(2040)3/2⁺ resonances in the s channel, but the authors note that fits with three or more nucleon resonances produce many solutions with similar quality, preventing conclusive resonance content extraction from the currently available limited data.

## Method

The reaction was analyzed using an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level, including t-channel ρ and ω exchanges, s- and u-channel nucleon exchanges, a generalized contact current, and a minimum number of s-channel nucleon resonances. The t-channel amplitudes were Reggeized using an interpolating form factor to smoothly transition from Feynman to Regge amplitudes. A total of 877 data points (681 differential cross sections from CLAS, 120 from A2, 14 beam asymmetry data from GRAAL, and 62 from CLAS) were fitted simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Fitted mass of N(1875)3/2⁻: 1853 ± 1 MeV (PDG range: [1850–1920] MeV)
- Fitted width of N(1875)3/2⁻: 392 ± 2 MeV (PDG range: [120–250] MeV)
- Fitted mass of N(2040)3/2⁺: 2049 ± 1 MeV
- Fitted width of N(2040)3/2⁺: 301 ± 4 MeV
- χ²/N for beam asymmetry data: 1.8
- χ²/N for differential cross section data: 3.2
- χ²/N for all data: 3.1
- g_NNη′ coupling constant: 0.83 ± 0.01
- Cutoff mass Λ_ρ: 864 ± 1 MeV
- Cutoff mass Λ_ω: 517 ± 1 MeV
- Product g^(1)_RNγ g_RNη′ for N(1875)3/2⁻: 24.75 ± 0.10
- Product g^(2)_RNγ g_RNη′ for N(1875)3/2⁻: −11.93 ± 0.16
- Product g^(1)_RNγ g_RNη′ for N(2040)3/2⁺: −1.02 ± 0.04
- Product g^(2)_RNγ g_RNη′ for N(2040)3/2⁺: 4.75 ± 0.04
- Decay branching ratio β_Nη′ for N(2040)3/2⁺: 1.29 ± 0.01%

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts on γp → η′p that could only describe differential cross sections before beam asymmetry data became available, notably the BnGa group partial-wave analysis (Anisovich 2017, 2018) which found significant contributions from N(1895)1/2⁻, N(2100)1/2⁺, N(2120)3/2⁻, and N(1900)3/2⁺, and the updated ηMAID model (2018) which claimed N(1895)1/2⁻, N(1880)1/2⁺, N(1860)5/2⁺, and N(1990)7/2⁺ were significant. The present analysis finds a different minimal resonance content (N(1875)3/2⁻ and N(2040)3/2⁺) and provides predictions for target nucleon asymmetries to guide future experimental measurements.
