# Liquid-gas phase transition in strange hadronic matter

> The liquid-gas phase transition in strange hadronic matter is studied utilizing an extended Furnstahl-Serot-Tang model with nucleons and hyperons. The system is treated as of two components. The phase transition is analyzed by investigating the stability of the system and Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium. A two-dimensional binodal surface resulted from the two-phase equilibrium, namely the phase boundary, is obtained. For each temperature ranging from T = 8 MeV to T = 12 MeV, a limit pressure on the binodal surface section is found, while a critical point is spotted for the temperature around T = 13 MeV. The Maxwell constructions are also illustrated to give a vivid description of the course of the phase transition. Moreover, the entropy per baryon and heat capacity per baryon as functions of temperature are examined. The entropy is continuous during the phase transition but the heat capacity is discontinuous, indicating that the phase transition is of second order. By these efforts, the L-G phase transition can be concluded to exist in the strange hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition exists in strange hadronic matter (SHM) and is of second order by Ehrenfest's definition, with continuous entropy per baryon and discontinuous heat capacity per baryon during the transition; however, this conclusion is conditional on the extended Furnstahl-Serot-Tang (FST) model with nucleons and hyperons, and the transition occurs only for strangeness fractions below approximately 0.8 and temperatures up to about 13 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the FST model to include nucleons, Λ and Ξ hyperons, and two additional strange mesons (σ* and φ) mediating hyperon-hyperon interactions, treating the system as a two-component mixture with chemical equilibrium from reactions Λ+Λ ⇌ n+Ξ⁰ and Λ+Λ ⇌ p+Ξ⁻. They analyzed mechanical stability via pressure-density isotherms and chemical stability via chemical potential-strangeness fraction isobars, then applied Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium to construct the two-dimensional binodal surface and performed Maxwell constructions for the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Mechanical inflection point at T = 13 MeV occurs at pressure p = 0.24 MeV fm⁻³, baryon density ρ_B = 0.063 fm⁻³, and strangeness fraction f_s^M = 0.5
- Critical point at T = 13 MeV: critical pressure p^C = 0.28 MeV fm⁻³, critical strangeness fraction f_s^C = 0.65
- Limit pressure at T = 10 MeV: p_lim = 0.095 MeV fm⁻³, above which no liquid-gas phase transition occurs
- Mechanical inflection point at T = 14 MeV occurs at p = 0.18 MeV fm⁻³, ρ_B = 0.04 fm⁻³, and f_s^M = 0.1
- Temperature range for phase transition analysis: T = 8 MeV to T = 12 MeV for limit pressure behavior, with critical point at T ≈ 13 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter by Muller and Serot (1995) and Qian et al. (2000-2003) to include strangeness degrees of freedom via hyperons. It builds on the authors' previous development of the extended FST model for strange hadronic matter (Zhang et al., 2000; Qian et al., 2003) and addresses the open question of whether such a transition exists in SHM beyond the deconfinement transition to quark-gluon plasma. The results enable future studies of strange hadronic matter thermodynamics in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical contexts such as neutron star interiors.
