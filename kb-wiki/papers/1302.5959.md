# Recent results in chiral effective field theory for the NN system

> I describe recent progress towards a theory of the NN force which captures the consequences of QCD's chiral symmetry and the pattern of its breaking, and is formulated as an expansion in a ratio of low and high mass scales, M_{lo}/M_{hi}. This "chiral effective field theory" of the NN system is a firm foundation for explorations of nuclear structure and reactions that are grounded in QCD's low-energy symmetries. While calculations that use a ChiPT expansion for the NN potential have proven very successful, they can only be used with a narrow range of momentum-space cutoffs, which leaves the expansion parameter for observable quantities somewhat murky. Here we seek a truly systematic effective field theory for the NN amplitude, that is manifestly renormalization-group invariant at each order in a demonstrably perturbative expansion.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard Weinberg-style chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) system, while phenomenologically successful, lacks a clear expansion parameter and proper renormalization-group invariance. The author proposes a "new leading order" that treats one-pion exchange non-perturbatively only in partial waves where its tensor part is attractive and strong, supplemented by additional contact interactions beyond those required by naive dimensional analysis, to achieve a systematically improvable and renormalization-group-invariant expansion.

## Method
The author analyzes the short-distance behavior of NN wave functions in the presence of the singular tensor part of one-pion exchange, which behaves as 1/r³. By solving the Schrödinger equation with the leading-order potential and examining the cutoff dependence of phase shifts, the author identifies which partial waves require additional contact interactions to achieve renormalization-group invariance. Higher-order corrections from two-pion exchange are then treated perturbatively, with their renormalization requirements determined by analyzing matrix elements between leading-order wave functions at short distances.

## Key numbers
- Λ_NN ≈ 300 MeV: the momentum scale at which iterates of one-pion exchange become comparable to tree-level, defined as 16π f_π²/(g_A² M)
- Cutoff range 500–800 MeV: the range of momentum-space cutoffs for which standard χEFT potentials yield predictions with little residual cutoff dependence
- Cutoff range 0.6–0.9 fm: the range of coordinate-space cutoffs r_c used in the "new leading order" calculations for P-wave phase shifts
- Λ_χSB nominally m_ρ ∼ 4π f_π: the high-energy scale in the chiral expansion
- T_lab = 10 MeV and 50 MeV: laboratory energies at which phase shifts are shown as a function of cutoff for the ³P₂ channel

## Context
This work extends the program of chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez (1996), Epelbaum (1999, 2002, 2005), and Entem and Machleidt (2002, 2003). It directly addresses deficiencies identified by Beane (2002), Entem and Sick (2003), Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005), and Birse (2006) regarding the lack of renormalization-group invariance when cutoffs exceed ∼800 MeV. The proposed "new leading order" builds on the work of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and has been implemented numerically by Pavon Valderrama (2009, 2011) and Long and Yang (2011, 2012). The approach enables systematic error estimates and a rigorous connection between nuclear physics and QCD's chiral symmetry, with implications for three-nucleon forces and electroweak operators.
