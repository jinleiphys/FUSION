# Hadron Masses in Medium and Neutron Star Properties

> We investigate the properties of the neutron star with relativistic mean field models. We incorporate in the quantum hadrodynamics and in the quark-meson coupling models a possible reduction of meson masses in nuclear matter. The equation of state for neutron star matter is obtained and is employed in Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to extract the maximum mass of the stable neutron star. We find that the equation of state, the composition and the properties of the neutron stars are sensitive to the values of the meson masses in medium.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how a possible reduction of meson masses in nuclear matter affects neutron star properties, finding that the equation of state, composition, and maximum mass are sensitive to in-medium meson masses. However, the authors note that the scaling models (QHD-S and MQMC-S) produce a maximum neutron star mass of about 2.9 solar masses, which is larger than observed values, and that the work is only a first step requiring further refinement.

## Method

The authors incorporated in-medium meson mass reductions into two classes of relativistic mean field models: quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) and quark-meson coupling (QMC) models. They implemented the Brown-Rho scaling law as a density-dependent function for meson masses in QHD-S and MQMC-S models, and treated heavy mesons as MIT bags in the MQMC-MB model. The equation of state for neutron star matter was obtained under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, then integrated using the Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to extract neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- QHD and MQMC models give maximum neutron star mass M_max ~ 2.0 solar masses with radius R ~ 10.8 km and central density epsilon_c ~ 2.5 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- QHD-S model gives maximum mass ~ 2.9 solar masses with radius R ~ 13.7 km and central density epsilon_c ~ 1.5 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Threshold density for direct URCA process is rho_D ~ 2.1 rho_0 for QHD and MQMC, and rho_D ~ 1.6 rho_0 for QHD-S and MQMC-S
- In QHD-S, the in-medium meson mass ratio m*_M/m_M = 0.781 at saturation density, and nucleon effective mass ratio m*_N/m_N = 0.667
- Including hyperons reduces maximum mass to 1.7 solar masses for QHD and 1.5 solar masses for MQMC

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of in-medium hadron mass changes, including the Brown-Rho scaling proposal (Brown and Rho, 1991), QCD sum rule calculations (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992), and quark-meson coupling models (Saito et al., 1997). It also responds to experimental reports of meson mass reductions from the CERES/NA45 and KEK-PS E325 experiments, and to astronomical observations of neutron star masses up to 2.2 solar masses (Stairs, 2004). The results enable future studies of how exotic degrees of freedom (hyperons, meson condensation, quark matter) interact with in-medium meson mass changes to produce realistic neutron star masses and cooling scenarios.
