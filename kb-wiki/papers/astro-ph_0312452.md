# Strangeness in Neutron Stars

> We discuss the role of strangeness on the internal constitution and structural properties of neutron stars. In particular, we report on recent calculations of hyperon star properties derived from microscopic equations of state for hyperonic matter. Next, we discuss the possibility of having a strange quark matter core in a neutron star, or the possible existence of strange quark matter stars, the so-called strange stars.

# Strangeness in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons softens the equation of state of dense matter and reduces the maximum mass of neutron stars by 0.5–0.8 solar masses, but current microscopic equations of state for hyperonic matter based on G-matrix Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations predict maximum masses too low to explain observed neutron star masses such as the 1.4411 solar mass pulsar PSR1913+16, suggesting that repulsive three-body baryonic forces involving hyperons may be missing from the models.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of hyperonic matter equations of state using an extension of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory to include hyperonic degrees of freedom, with the Nijmegen soft-core potential (NSC97e) describing hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. The pure nucleonic contribution to the equation of state is included using a parametrization of the Akmal-Pandharipande equation of state with a semi-phenomenological three-nucleon interaction of the Urbana type. The author also discusses kaon condensation, hybrid stars with quark matter cores, strange stars, and the possible connection between quark-deconfinement phase transitions and gamma-ray bursts.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear at a density of about 2 times the normal saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm^-3) of nuclear matter
- The mass difference between the Sigma- and Lambda hyperons is 81.76 MeV
- The maximum stellar mass is reduced by Delta M_max ~ 0.5–0.8 solar masses when hyperons are included
- For pure nucleonic stars using the Argonne v18 interaction: M_max = 2.00 solar masses, radius R = 10.54 km, central density rho_c = 1.11 fm^-3
- For hyperon stars using the same interaction: M_max = 1.22 solar masses, radius R = 10.46 km, central density rho_c = 1.25 fm^-3
- The measured mass of the neutron star in PSR1913+16 is (1.4411 +/- 0.0007) solar masses
- The critical density for kaon condensation is in the range 2.5–5.0 n0
- The compact object SAX J1808.4-3658 has a pulsation period of 2.49 ms and a peak X-ray luminosity of 6 x 10^36 erg/s in its bright state
- Energy released in the conversion of a hadronic star to a strange star is in the range (1–4) x 10^53 erg
- Gamma-ray burst energies are of the order of 10^51 erg for jet-like emission or 10^53 erg for isotropic emission

## Context

This review extends the line of work on hyperons in neutron stars first proposed by Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and later investigated using relativistic mean field models by Glendenning (1985) and others. The paper contrasts microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Vidana et al., 2000) with relativistic mean field approaches (Glendenning and Moszkowski, 1991) and discusses the problem that current microscopic equations of state for hyperonic matter are too soft to explain observed neutron star masses. The work enables future investigation of repulsive three-body baryonic forces involving hyperons and connects quark-deconfinement phase transitions to gamma-ray burst models, particularly the metastable hadronic star model of Berezhiani et al. (2002) which can explain different time delays between supernova explosions and gamma-ray bursts.
