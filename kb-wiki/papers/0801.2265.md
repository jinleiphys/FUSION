# Parton Distributions at Hadronization from Bulk Dense Matter Produced at RHIC

> We present an analysis of $Ω$, $Ξ$, $Λ$ and $ϕ$ spectra from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV in terms of distributions of effective constituent quarks at hadronization. Consistency in quark ratios derived from various hadron spectra provides clear evidence for hadron formation dynamics as suggested by quark coalescence or recombination models. We argue that the constituent quark distribution reflects properties of the effective partonic degrees of freedom at hadronization. Experimental data indicate that strange quarks have a transverse momentum distribution flatter than that of up/down quarks consistent with hydrodynamic expansion in partonic phase prior to hadronization. After the AMPT model is tuned to reproduce the strange and up/down quark distributions, the model can describe the measured spectra of hyperons and $ϕ$ mesons very well where hadrons are formed through dynamical coalescence.

## Key claim

The paper argues that transverse momentum distributions of effective constituent quarks at hadronization can be extracted directly from multi-strange hadron spectra, with the hedge that this approach is valid in the intermediate transverse momentum region up to 5 GeV/c for baryons and 4 GeV/c for mesons, and relies on the assumption that strange and anti-strange quark distributions are identical.

## Method

The authors analyzed STAR data for Omega, Xi, Lambda and phi meson transverse momentum spectra from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. They extracted strange and light quark transverse momentum distributions by taking ratios of hadron spectra at quark-number-scaled transverse momenta (p_T/n_q), using the coalescence model assumption that baryons at p_T form from quarks at p_T/3 and mesons at p_T from quarks at p_T/2. They then tuned the AMPT model with string melting to reproduce these extracted quark distributions and compared the resulting hadron spectra to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Radial flow velocity for strange quarks: v_T = (0.54 +/- 0.13)c with freeze-out temperature T_th = (131 +/- 48) MeV, chi^2/ndf = 10.7/12
- Radial flow velocity for light quarks: v_T = (0.36 +/- 0.19)c with freeze-out temperature T_th = (170 +/- 40) MeV, chi^2/ndf = 28.1/41
- With T_th fixed to 170 MeV: v_T = (0.43 +/- 0.03)c for strange quarks (chi^2/ndf = 11/12) and v_T = (0.36 +/- 0.02)c for light quarks (chi^2/ndf = 26.6/41)
- Feed-down contribution to Xi- spectrum from Xi0(1530) decays: 46% +/- 14%
- Feed-down contribution to Lambda spectrum from Sigma(1380) decays: 26% +/- 5.9%
- Thermal model prediction for primordial Sigma0/Lambda ratio: 0.67; final Sigma0 to inclusive Lambda ratio: 0.36

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence and recombination model calculations by Fries (2003), Hwa and Yang (2003), and Ko (2003) that used pion and kaon spectra, by instead using multi-strange hadrons (Omega, Xi, phi) which have smaller hadronic interaction cross sections and minimal decay feed-down contributions. The extracted quark distributions provide a constraint on partonic evolution history that can be compared with theoretical calculations of partonic cascading without involving complicated hadronization and hadronic rescattering processes. The approach complements the constituent quark number scaling observed in elliptic flow measurements at RHIC.
