# Initiative in Nuclear Theory at the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre

> We recall the path breaking contributions of the nuclear theory group of the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre, Kolkata. From a beginning of just one person in 1970s, the group has steadily developed into a leading group in the country today, with seminal contributions to almost the entire range of nuclear physics, viz., low energy nuclear reactions, nuclear structure, deep inelastic collisions, fission, liquid to gas phase transitions, nuclear matter, equation of state, mass formulae, neutron stars, relativistic heavy ion collisions, medium modification of hadron properties, quark gluon plasma, and cosmology of early universe.

## Key claim

The paper recounts the historical development and scientific contributions of the nuclear theory group at the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre (VECC), Kolkata, from its inception in the 1970s with a single member to a leading national group covering nearly all areas of nuclear physics; the narrative is explicitly subjective and retrospective, presented as a lecture honoring Prof. Bikash Sinha, rather than as a formal research paper with new results.

## Method

The paper is a historical review and narrative summary of the group's research output over approximately three decades, organized by subfield (optical model, deep inelastic collisions, fission, heavy-ion reactions, break-up of light ions, and quark-gluon plasma). It describes specific theoretical developments and calculations performed by group members, including analytical derivations, numerical techniques, and comparisons with experimental data, but does not present new experimental measurements or original theoretical results beyond those already published in the cited literature.

## Key numbers

- The equivalent sharp radius of the nuclear optical model potential for protons (R_V) and the nuclear density distribution (R_rho) were related as R_V = R_rho + 0.55 (no units given, but radii in fm implied by context).
- The stripping channel was found to contribute about 30% of the empirically observed energy dependence of the real part of the deuteron-40Ca optical model potential.
- A global relation for the viscosity coefficient mu was obtained: mu(E/A, A_CN) = a*(E/A) + b*A^3_CN, with a = 0.160 +/- 0.023 and b = 0.357 x 10^{-6} +/- 0.26 x 10^{-7} (units not specified in text).
- The one-body friction coefficient was found to be much stronger than the two-body one, with nucleon exchange accounting for about 1/2 to 1/3 of total one-body friction, the rest due to inelastic excitations.
- Nucleon transfer was shown to contribute about half of the phenomenological strengths of the absorptive nucleus-nucleus optical model potential.

## Context

This paper reviews the work of the VECC nuclear theory group, extending from the early optical model studies of Sinha (1970s) and the folding model approach, through later developments in deep inelastic collisions, fission dynamics, and heavy-ion reactions in the Fermi energy domain. It enables future work by documenting the group's contributions to topics such as the break-up of light ions (relevant for radioactive nuclear beams), the PEP model for promptly emitted particles, and the asymptotic expansion method for fission dynamics, while also noting the group's later shift toward quark-gluon plasma physics.
