# Temperature-resolved sensitivities of $^{56}{\rm Ni}$ production to helium-burning reactions in pair-instability supernovae

> We propose a temperature-resolved Monte Carlo (MC) approach to identify the temperature regimes in which low-energy helium-burning reaction rates most strongly affect nucleosynthesis in very massive stars that undergo pair-instability supernovae (PISNe). By performing MC simulations of PISNe, we quantify how temperature-dependent variations in key helium-burning reaction rates, i.e., the triple-$α$ and $^{12}{\rm C}(α,γ)^{16}{\rm O}$ rates, influence $^{56}{\rm Ni}$ synthesis. Thousands of stellar evolution calculations using $\texttt{MESA}$ reveal that both the $^{12}{\rm C}(α,γ)^{16}{\rm O}$ and triple-$α$ reactions exhibit their strongest sensitivity at $T \simeq 2.5 \times 10^{8}\,{\rm K}$, but with opposite correlation signs. We show that this temperature corresponds to the regime in which the ratio of the sampled rate multipliers is most clearly imprinted on the pre-carbon-burning C/O composition. This demonstrates that PISN nucleosynthesis can probe helium-burning reaction rates in specific low-temperature regimes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the triple-alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction rates exhibit their strongest sensitivity on 56Ni production in pair-instability supernovae at a specific temperature of T ~ 2.5 x 10^8 K, but with opposite correlation signs, rather than being uniformly sensitive across all temperatures as previous uniform-scaling studies assumed.

## Method

The authors performed 10,000 stellar evolution calculations using the MESA code (version r15140) for hydrogen-envelope-stripped very massive stars with an initial mass of 100 solar masses and metallicity Z = 10^-5. They implemented a temperature-resolved Monte Carlo approach where independent random multipliers (sampled uniformly from [0.5, 2.0]) were applied to the triple-alpha and 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction rates at each temperature point, then computed Pearson correlation coefficients between the sampled multipliers and the final 56Ni yield as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Peak sensitivity temperature for both reactions: T ~ 2.5 x 10^8 K
- Pearson correlation coefficient for 12C(alpha,gamma)16O at peak: r ~ +0.644
- Pearson correlation coefficient for triple-alpha at peak: r ~ -0.548
- Random multiplier range: uniform distribution over [0.5, 2.0]
- Initial stellar model mass: 100 solar masses
- Metallicity: Z = 10^-5
- Number of stellar evolution calculations: 10,000
- Failed or incomplete models: less than about 10% of total sample
- Observationally inferred 56Ni range for SN 2018ibb: 25-44 solar masses
- Helium-core mass for SN 2018ibb models: 130 solar masses
- For SN 2018ibb application, reaction rates varied from 0.1 to 10 times standard values

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Takahashi (2018) and Kawashimo (2024) that showed 56Ni yield in PISNe depends strongly on helium-burning reaction rates, but which used uniform scaling over the full temperature range. The temperature-resolved approach identifies that the rate-multiplier ratio at T ~ 2.5 x 10^8 K is most clearly imprinted on the pre-carbon-burning C/O composition, explaining why localized low-temperature variations can significantly alter the final 56Ni yield. This framework enables future observational constraints from PISN candidates like SN 2018ibb to probe specific low-energy helium-burning reaction rates, and can be extended to more complex nucleosynthesis systems involving multiple coupled reactions.
