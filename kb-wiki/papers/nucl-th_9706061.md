# The complex Kohn variational method applied to N-d scattering

> The three-nucleon ground state and the N--d scattering states are obtained using variational principles. The wave function of the system is decomposed into angular-spin-isospin channels and the corresponding two dimensional spatial amplitudes are expanded in a correlated polynomial basis. For the scattering states, the complex form of the Kohn variational principle is used to determine the $S$--matrix. Special attention is given to the convergence pattern of the phase-shift and mixing parameters. The calculations have been performed using realistic local NN potentials and three-nucleon forces. Important features of the method are anomaly-free solutions and the low dimensionality of the matrices involved allowing for the inclusion of a large number of states. Very precise and stable numerical results have been obtained.

# The Complex Kohn Variational Method Applied to N-d Scattering

## Key claim

The complex Kohn variational principle, when applied directly to the S-matrix using a correlated polynomial (PHH) basis expansion, yields anomaly-free, stable, and precise numerical results for N-d scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold, provided the nonlinear variational parameter γ and the number of Laguerre polynomials m are chosen appropriately (γ ≈ 1.5 fm⁻¹ for scattering, m ≥ 12 for 0.1% accuracy).

## Method

The three-nucleon wave function is decomposed into angular-spin-isospin channels, and the two-dimensional spatial amplitudes are expanded in a Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis with hyperradial functions expressed as products of Laguerre polynomials and an exponential tail. For scattering states, the complex Kohn variational principle is applied directly to the S-matrix, with the internal part of the wave function expanded in the same polynomial basis and the asymptotic part constructed from regular and regularized irregular solutions of the two-body N-d Schrödinger equation. The linear parameters are determined by solving a system of inhomogeneous equations, and the S-matrix is obtained from a stationary functional; unitarity of S is checked to verify basis completeness and detect spurious solutions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ³H calculated with AV14+TM potential: convergence to within ~1 keV when increasing channels from N_c=18 to N_c=26.
- Number of basis elements in final bound-state calculation: 1900 (N_c=26, m=16, γ=2.0 fm⁻¹).
- For zero-energy N-d scattering, relative difference between first- and second-order S-matrix results: 0.1 for m=4, improving to 10⁻⁴ for m=20.
- Scattering length variation when increasing channels from N_c=18 to N_c=26: less than 10⁻³ fm.
- For positive incident energy (E_lab=3.0 MeV), increasing m from 16 to 20 gives no appreciable contribution to phase shifts or mixing parameters.
- For the J=3/2⁻ state, unitarity condition |SS†-I| fulfilled up to 10⁻⁹ (without three-nucleon force) and 10⁻⁶ (with three-nucleon force).
- Optimal nonlinear parameter for scattering: γ=1.5 fm⁻¹; for bound states: γ=2.0 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the Kohn variational method for the K-matrix applied to N-d scattering by Kievsky et al. (1994) by implementing the complex form directly for the S-matrix, following developments in atomic and molecular physics (e.g., electron-atom collisions) and in nucleon-nucleon scattering by Tomio (1980s). The method enables accurate theoretical predictions for N-d observables such as differential cross sections and analyzing powers, and is being extended to treat the breakup channel (Kievsky et al., 1997). The results are compared with Faddeev equation solutions in configuration space (sctl, 1990s) and with earlier Kohn variational calculations (Kievsky et al., 1994).
