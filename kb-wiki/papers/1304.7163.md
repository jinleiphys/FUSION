# Incompressibility of finite fermionic systems: stable and exotic atomic nuclei

> The incompressibility of finite fermionic systems is investigated using analytical approaches and microscopic models. The incompressibility of a system is directly linked to the zero-point kinetic energy of constituent fermions, and this is a universal feature of fermionic systems. In the case of atomic nuclei, this implies a constant value of the incompressibility in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei. The evolution of nuclear incompressibility along Sn and Pb isotopic chains is analyzed using global microscopic models, based on both non-relativistic and relativistic energy functionals. The result is an almost constant incompressibility in stable nuclei and systems not far from stability, and a steep decrease in nuclei with pronounced neutron excess, caused by the emergence of a soft monopole mode in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the incompressibility of finite fermionic systems is directly linked to the zero-point kinetic energy of constituent fermions, which implies a constant incompressibility for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei; however, this constancy holds only for stable nuclei and systems not far from stability, while a steep decrease occurs in neutron-rich nuclei due to the emergence of a soft monopole mode.

## Method

The authors derived analytical relations for nuclear incompressibility using free Fermi gas and harmonic oscillator models, linking incompressibility to the zero-point kinetic energy. They then performed microscopic calculations using the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) method with the Skyrme functional SLy5, and quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations based on both non-relativistic (Skyrme SLy5) and relativistic (DD-ME2) energy density functionals, applied to Sn and Pb isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- For non-interacting nuclear matter at saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the density-dependent incompressibility K(ρ₀) = 220 MeV; at the crossing density (ρ_c = 0.1 fm⁻³), K(ρ_c) = 160 MeV.
- The zero-point kinetic energy for nucleons is T₀ ≈ 30 MeV, yielding K_A ≈ 210 MeV from the Fermi gas model and K_A ≈ 140 MeV from the harmonic oscillator model.
- The three microscopic models (CHFB, non-relativistic QRPA, relativistic QRPA) predict values of K_A for individual nuclei that do not differ by more than 10%.
- The constant K_A values near stability are close to 140 MeV, in quantitative agreement with the harmonic oscillator prediction.
- The boundary between low-energy and GMR regions is set at 13.5 MeV for Sn isotopes and at 11 MeV for Pb isotopes.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Blaizot (1980) and Bohigas (1979) that analyzed incompressibility for only four doubly magic nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, ²⁰⁸Pb), by systematically studying entire isotopic chains of Sn and Pb. The results confirm the absence of the mutual magicity enhancement (MEM) effect predicted by Khan (2009b) and not found experimentally by Garg (2012), while showing a magicity effect from the soft monopole mode beyond shell closures. The findings enable experimental searches for the soft monopole mode in neutron-rich nuclei beyond ¹³²Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb, and provide constraints for the density dependence of nuclear incompressibility relevant to astrophysical processes such as core-collapse supernovae.
