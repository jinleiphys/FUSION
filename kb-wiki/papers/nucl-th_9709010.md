# HBT Effect Based on a Hydrodynamical Model

> We investigate HBT radii based on the numerical solutions of the hydrodynamical model which are so tuned as to reproduce the recent experimental data at the CERN SPS. Comparing the sizes of freeze-out hypersurface with HBT radii, we discuss dynamical effect of the systematic flow on the apparent HBT radii. Finally we compare HBT radii of the QGP phase transition model with those of the hadron gas model without phase transition.

# HBT Effect Based on a Hydrodynamical Model

## Key claim
Based on numerical solutions of a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model tuned to reproduce CERN SPS data, the systematic flow of the fluid causes the apparent out-ward and longitudinal HBT radii to become smaller than the geometric source size, while the side-ward radius remains proportional to the transverse width. The authors find that it is very difficult to distinguish between a QGP phase transition model and a hadron gas model without phase transition using HBT radii alone.

## Method
The authors numerically solved a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model and used the resulting freeze-out hypersurface to construct a c-number source function for pion emission. Two-particle correlation functions were computed and fitted with a Gaussian form to extract the HBT radii \(R_\Side\), \(R_\Out\), and \(R_\Long\). The HBT radii were compared to the geometric widths \(\Delta r\), \(\Delta z\), and \(\Delta t\) of the freeze-out hypersurface, both with and without the effect of hydrodynamical flow, for S+S, S+Cu, S+Ag, and S+Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- \(R_\Side\) is proportional to \(\Delta r\) in both static and flow cases; the value of \(R_\Side\) does not equal \(\Delta r\) due to non-Gaussian source shape.
- \(R_\Out\) is proportional to \(\sqrt{\Delta r^2 + \beta_T^2 \Delta t^2}\) without systematic flow, but becomes smaller than this value when flow is present.
- \(R_\Long\) is not proportional to the source size even without flow, and becomes smaller when flow exists.
- The hadron gas model gives larger \(R_\Side\) than the phase transition model because \(\Delta r\) of the hadron gas model is larger.
- For \(R_\Out\) and \(R_\Long\), the results of the phase transition model and the hadron gas model are hardly distinguishable.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous numerical analysis of a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model applied to CERN WA80 data (Morita, Muroya, Nakamura, prior papers). It directly compares HBT radii extracted from the correlation function to the geometric size of the freeze-out hypersurface, clarifying the role of systematic flow. The comparison between the QGP phase transition model and the hadron gas model without phase transition addresses the question of whether HBT measurements can serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, concluding that HBT radii alone are insufficient to distinguish the two scenarios.
