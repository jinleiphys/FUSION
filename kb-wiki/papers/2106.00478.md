# Space-average electromagnetic fields and EM anomaly weighted by energy density in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the space-average electromagnetic (EM) fields weighted by the energy density in the central regions of heavy ion collisions. These average quantities can serve as a barometer for the magnetic-field induced effects such as the magnetic effect, the chiral separation effect and the chiral magnetic wave. Comparing with the magnetic fields at the geometric center of the collision, the space-average fields weighted by the energy density are smaller in the early stage but damp slower in the later stage. The space average of squared fields as well as the EM anomaly $\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}$ weighted by the energy density are also calculated. We give parameterized analytical formula for these average quantities as functions of time by fitting numerical results for collisions in the collision energy range $7.7-200$ GeV with different impact parameters.

## Key claim

The space-average electromagnetic fields weighted by energy density provide a more appropriate barometer for magnetic-field-induced effects (such as the chiral magnetic effect, chiral separation effect, and chiral magnetic wave) than the magnetic field at the geometric center, because the average fields are smaller in the early stage but damp slower in the later stage, and the fireball expansion means regions close to spectators have larger fields than at the geometric center.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions, calculating electromagnetic fields from charged particles via the Lienard-Wiechert potential in vacuum. They computed space-average fields weighted by energy density by dividing space into grids (optimized to dz=0.05 fm and dx=dy=0.5 fm) and summing over grids in the mid-rapidity range (-0.5 < Y < 0.5), considering only particles in that rapidity range for density calculations but all charged particles including spectators for field calculations. They fitted numerical results to obtain parameterized analytical formulas for the average quantities as functions of time for collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV with different impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- The peak value of the energy-density-weighted average magnetic field ⟨eB_y⟩_E is proportional to the impact parameter b for small b in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV
- The maximum values of ⟨eB_y⟩_E are almost proportional to the collision energy for b=9 fm in Au+Au collisions
- At the same collision energy and b=9 fm, the peak value of ⟨(eB_y)^2⟩_E is about one order of magnitude larger than ⟨(eB_x)^2⟩_E and about two to four orders of magnitude larger than ⟨(eB_z)^2⟩_E
- For electric fields at the same collision energy and b=9 fm, the peak values of ⟨(eE_x)^2⟩_E and ⟨(eE_y)^2⟩_E are comparable and about one to three orders of magnitude larger than ⟨(eE_z)^2⟩_E
- The peak values of ⟨e^2 E·B⟩_E are about 50% smaller than values in Zhao (2019) when comparing zone-averages
- All average quantities peak at about t=0.08 fm/c after collision and fall by 2-3 orders of magnitude within about 1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends event-by-event simulations without medium feedback (Skokov 2009, Bzdak 2011, Voronyuk 2011, Deng 2012, Bloczynski 2012) by proposing energy-density-weighted spatial averages rather than using fields at the geometric center. The results enable more accurate estimates of chiral effects (CME, CSE, CMW) in hydrodynamic simulations where magnetic fields are typically put in by hand, and provide parameterized formulas useful for studying vector meson spin alignment (Sheng 2019, Sheng 2020) and charge-dependent elliptic flow differences observed by STAR (Adamczyk 2015).
