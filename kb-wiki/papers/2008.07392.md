# Efimov effect in non-integer dimensions induced by an external field

> The Efimov effect can be induced by means of an external deformed one-body field that effectively reduces the allowed spatial dimensions to less than three. To understand this new mechanism, conceptually and practically, we employ a formulation using non-integer dimension, which is equivalent to the strength of an external oscillator field. The effect most clearly appears when the crucial two-body systems are unbound in three, but bound in two, dimensions. We discuss energy variation, conditions for occurrence, and number of Efimov states, as functions of the dimension. We use practical examples from cold atom physics of $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs, $^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb, $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^{6}$Li, and $^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb-$^{39}$K. Laboratory tests of the effect can be performed with two independent parameters, i.e. the external one-body field and the Feshbach two-body tuning. The scaling and (dis)appearance of these Efimov states occur precisely as already found in three dimensions.

# Efimov effect in non-integer dimensions induced by an external field

## Key claim
The Efimov effect can be induced by applying an external deformed one-body oscillator field that squeezes the system in one spatial direction, effectively reducing the allowed spatial dimensions to a non-integer value between 2 and 3. The effect appears most clearly when the two-body subsystems are unbound in three dimensions but become bound in two dimensions, and the scaling and (dis)appearance of these Efimov states occur precisely as already found in three dimensions.

## Method
The authors employ a formulation using non-integer dimension parameter d, which is equivalent to the strength of an external oscillator field, and solve the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method for three-body systems. They compute the eigenvalues λ(d) from the angular Schrodinger equation and analyze the effective radial potential to determine when the condition ξ² < 0 is satisfied, indicating the Efimov effect. The translation between the dimension parameter d and the external oscillator frequency ω_ho is given by ω_pp/ω_ho = 2(d-2)/[(d-1)(3-d)], where ω_pp is the equivalent two-body oscillator frequency.

## Key numbers
- For three identical bosons, the Efimov effect requires d > 2.298, which translates into ω_ho < 1.02 ω_pp
- The Efimov point occurs at d_E = 2.75 for the chosen interactions, with asymptotic constants ξ² ≈ -0.858 (m_H/m_L = 1/1), -2.679 (m_H/m_L = 133/6), and -0.270 (m_H/m_L = 87/39)
- The energy scale factors at d_E = 2.75 are 882.8 (three identical particles), 46.5 (m_H/m_L = 133/6), and 180214 (m_H/m_L = 87/39)
- For m_H/m_L = 133/6, the five computed Efimov state energies at d = d_E are (in units of ħ²/(μb²)): -3.050×10⁻², -6.985×10⁻⁴, -1.498×10⁻⁵, -3.225×10⁻⁷, and -6.931×10⁻⁹
- The energy scale factors between successive Efimov states for m_H/m_L = 133/6 are 43.7, 46.6, 46.5, and 46.5, agreeing with the value ≈46.47 from ξ² = -2.679
- The lower limit for the Efimov effect for three identical bosons is d_L = 2.298; for m_H/m_L = 133/6, d_L ≈ 2.3; for m_H/m_L = 87/39, d_L ≈ 2.54
- The tiny dimension interval 2.7499 < d < 2.7501 is sufficient to see essentially all Efimov states appearing and disappearing

## Context
This work extends the non-integer dimension method developed by Nielsen et al. (2001), who predicted that the Efimov effect is only possible in dimensions between 2.3 and 3.8. It builds on recent implementations by Garrido et al. (2019a, 2019b, 2020) and Christensen et al. (2018) for confined two- and three-body systems, and connects to the confinement-induced Efimov effect introduced by Nishida and Tan (2008, 2009). The key advance is demonstrating that an external deformed oscillator field provides a second independent controllable parameter (alongside Feshbach tuning) to reach the Efimov condition, which enables laboratory tests with more flexibility than using only Feshbach tuning alone.
