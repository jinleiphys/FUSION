# Mass spectra of $NΩ$ dibaryons in the $^{3}S_1$ and $^{5}S_2$ channels

> We study the mass spectra of the $NΩ$ dibaryons in the $^{3}S_1$ and $^{5}S_2$ channels with $J^{P}=1^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ respectively, by using the method of QCD sum rules. We construct two dibaryon interpolating currents in the molecular picture and calculate their correlation functions and spectral densities up to dimension-16 condensates. Our results indicate that there may exist an $NΩ$ dibaryon bound state in the $^{5}S_2$ channel with a binding energy of about $21\ \mathrm{MeV}$. The masses of the $^{3}S_1$ $NΩ$ dibaryons with $J^{P}=1^{+}$ are predicted to be higher than the $NΩ$ and $ΛΞ$ thresholds, and thus can decay into these final states directly in S-wave. The $NΩ(^{5}S_2)$ dibaryon bound state can decay into the octet-octet final states $ΛΞ$ and $ΣΞ$ in D-wave via the quark rearrangement mechanism. The existence of these $NΩ$ dibaryons may be identified in the relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments in the future.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules, the authors find evidence for a possible bound state of an \(N\Omega\) dibaryon in the \(^{5}S_2\) channel with \(J^{P}=2^{+}\), with a binding energy of about 21 MeV relative to the \(N\Omega\) threshold; the \(^{3}S_1\) channel with \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) is predicted to lie above threshold and thus not form a bound state.

## Method

The authors construct two local interpolating currents for the \(N\Omega\) system in the molecular picture, using Ioffe currents for the nucleon and a standard current for the \(\Omega\) baryon. They compute the two-point correlation functions and spectral densities up to dimension-16 condensates at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) via the operator product expansion. Laplace sum rules are then established, and the hadron masses are extracted by requiring OPE convergence and Borel stability within suitable working regions for the continuum threshold \(s_0\) and Borel mass \(M_B^2\).

## Key numbers

* Mass of the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon in the \(^{5}S_2\) channel (\(J^{P}=2^{+}\)) from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,1}\): \(2.59 \pm 0.17\) GeV, which is about 21 MeV below the \(N\Omega\) threshold.
* Mass of the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon in the \(^{3}S_1\) channel (\(J^{P}=1^{+}\)) from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,1}\): \(2.79 \pm 0.13\) GeV, above the \(N\Omega\) and \(\Lambda\Xi\) thresholds.
* Mass of the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon in the \(^{5}S_2\) channel from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,2}\): \(2.91 \pm 0.23\) GeV.
* Mass of the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon in the \(^{3}S_1\) channel from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,2}\): \(2.95 \pm 0.13\) GeV.
* Coupling constant for the \(J^{P}=2^{+}\) state from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,1}\): \((6.68 \pm 0.46) \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^8\).
* Coupling constant for the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) state from current \(J^{N\Omega}_{\mu\nu,1}\): \((5.19 \pm 1.20) \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^8\).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of dibaryons, such as the \(\Omega\Omega\) system by Chen et al. (2019). The predicted binding energy of about 21 MeV for the \(^{5}S_2\) \(N\Omega\) state is roughly consistent with the recent HAL QCD lattice simulation near physical points by Iritani et al. (2018), which found a binding energy of 1.54–2.46 MeV, and with the STAR experimental measurement of the \(p\Omega\) correlation function, which favored a binding energy of about 27 MeV. The results suggest that the \(^{5}S_2\) \(N\Omega\) dibaryon could be identified in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments such as those at STAR, RHIC, and J-PARC.
