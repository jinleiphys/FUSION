# Lepton angular distribution of $W$ boson productions

> The lepton angular distribution coefficients $A_i$ for $Z$ boson production in $pp$ and $\bar p p$ collisions have been measured at the LHC and the Tevatron. A recent study showed that many features of the measured angular distribution coefficients, including the transverse momentum ($q_T$) and rapidity dependencies and the violation of the Lam-Tung relation, can be well described using an intuitive geometric approach. In this paper, we extend this geometric approach to describe the angular distribution coefficients for $W$ boson produced in $\bar{p} p$ collisions at the Tevatron. We first compare the data with a perturbative QCD calculation at $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$. We then show that the data and QCD calculations can be well described with the geometric approach. Implications for future studies at the LHC energy are also discussed.

# Lepton angular distribution of $W$ boson productions

## Key claim
The geometric approach previously applied to $Z$ boson production can also describe the lepton angular distribution coefficients for $W$ boson production in $\bar{p}p$ collisions at the Tevatron, but the statistical precision of the CDF data is only marginal, so the authors use $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$ perturbative QCD calculations as a reference to validate the geometric model rather than relying on the data alone.

## Method
The authors compare CDF measurements of the angular coefficients $A_0$, $A_2$, $A_3$, and $A_4$ for $W$ boson production at $\sqrt{s}=1.8$ TeV with perturbative QCD calculations at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$ using the DYNNLO code and CT14NNLO parton distribution functions. They then express all angular coefficients in terms of three geometric quantities: the polar angle $\theta_1$ of the quark-antiquark collision axis, the non-coplanarity angle $\phi_1$ between the hadron and quark planes, and the parity-violating parameter $a$ (with $|a|=2$ for $W$ bosons). The geometric model parameters are fit to the QCD calculations rather than directly to the data.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: $\sqrt{s} = 1.8$ TeV for $\bar{p}p$ collisions at the Tevatron
- Event selection: $E^e_T (P^\mu_T) \geq 20$ GeV, $\cancel{E}_{T} > 20$ GeV, $|\eta^{e,\mu}| \leq 1$, and $15 < q^W_T < 105$ GeV
- Best-fit fraction of $q\bar{q}$ annihilation process: $f = 0.610 \pm 0.002$
- Best-fit average non-coplanarity angle: $\phi_1 = 12.6^\circ$, corresponding to $\cos 2\phi_1 = 0.905 \pm 0.004$
- Best-fit reduction factor for $A_4$: $r_4 = 0.738 \pm 0.002$
- Best-fit reduction factor for $A_3$: $r_3 = 0.0540 \pm 0.001$
- For $W$ boson production, the parity-violating parameter $|a| = 2$, giving ranges: $0 \leq A_0 \leq 1$, $-1 \leq A_2 \leq 1$, $-2 \leq A_3 \leq 2$, $-2 \leq A_4 \leq 2$

## Context
This work extends the geometric approach developed by Peng et al. (2016) and Chang et al. (2017) for $Z$ boson production to the $W$ boson case, where parity-violating couplings introduce additional angular coefficients $A_3$ and $A_4$. The study builds on earlier theoretical work by Lam and Tung (1978) on the Lam-Tung relation and by Mirkes (1992, 1994) on angular distributions in $W$ and $Z$ production. The results enable future high-precision measurements at the LHC, where the $A_2$ coefficient for $W$ plus multiple-jet events is predicted to be smaller than for $W$ plus single-jet events due to nonzero $\phi_1$ angles.
