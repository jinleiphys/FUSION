# Coexistence of normal, super-, and hyper-deformation in nuclei: A study with angular momentum projection

> Angular-momentum-projected energy surface calculations for A~110 nuclei indicate three distinct energy minima occurring at different angular-momenta. These correspond to normal, super-, and hyper-deformed shapes coexisting in one nucleus. 110Pd is studied in detail, with a quantitative prediction on super- and hyper-deformed spectra by the Projected Shell Model calculation. It is suggested that several other neighboring nuclei in the A-110 mass region, with the neutron number around 64, also exhibit clear super- and hyper-deformation minima.

## Key claim

Angular-momentum-projected energy surface calculations for A~110 nuclei indicate three distinct energy minima occurring at different angular momenta, corresponding to normal, super-, and hyper-deformed shapes coexisting in one nucleus; the abstract's claim that these minima exist is hedged by the finding that the second (super-deformed) minimum is fully developed only at spin I=24 and above, and the third (hyper-deformed) minimum starts showing up only at a rather high spin I~44.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum-projected energy surface calculations using the Projected Shell Model (PSM), which builds a shell-model space from deformed Nilsson single-particle states with pairing correlations incorporated via BCS calculations. They calculated the energy E^I as a function of static quadrupole moment <Q> for 110Pd, using a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian with quadrupole-pairing term, and diagonalized the Hamiltonian in the projected space. Nucleons in three major shells (N=3,4,5 for both neutrons and protons) were activated for the smaller deformation region (Region I), and four major shells (N=4,5,6,7 for neutrons and N=3,4,5,6 for protons) for the larger deformation region (Region II).

## Key numbers

- The first minimum in 110Pd starts at <Q> = 0.7 eb at spin I=0 and moves to <Q> = 1.0 eb (deformation ε2 ≈ 0.25) at higher spin.
- The second minimum in 110Pd is located at <Q> ≈ 2.8 eb, corresponding to ε2 ≈ 0.57, and is fully developed only at spin I=24 and above.
- The third minimum in 110Pd appears at <Q> ≈ 3.9 eb, corresponding to ε2 ≈ 0.79, starting at spin I~44.
- For the hyper-deformed minimum at ε2=0.84, the highest-j intruder occupation ratio r reaches about 14.5% for i13/2 protons and 16.5% for j15/2 neutrons.
- Monopole pairing strengths were taken as 17.5/A for neutrons and 19.5/A for protons, with quadrupole pairing strength G_Q = 0.16 G_M.
- Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strengths in Region I: χ_nn = 0.0377 MeV, χ_pp = 0.0302 MeV, χ_np = 0.0337 MeV; in Region II: χ_nn = 0.0355 MeV, χ_pp = 0.0284 MeV, χ_np = 0.0318 MeV.
- For Zr, Mo, Ru, Pd, and Cd isotopes with neutron number around 64, a super-deformed minimum is found at ε2 ≈ 0.56 starting from I≈24, and a hyper-deformed minimum at ε2 ≈ 0.80 from I≈44.

## Context

This work extends the search for exotic nuclear shapes following the discovery of super-deformed rotational bands by Twin (1988) and the observation of a highly deformed rotational band in 108Cd by Clark et al. (2004). The authors' previous PSM analysis of 108Cd (Lee et al., 2002) found that the observed band contained a component of the proton i13/2 orbital from the N=6 harmonic oscillator shell. The present study provides quantitative predictions for super- and hyper-deformed spectra in 110Pd and neighboring nuclei, suggesting that nuclei with neutron number around 64 are promising candidates for observing hyper-deformed rotational bands, which would enable experimental confirmation of three coexisting deformation minima in a single nucleus.
