# Exclusive Hadronic Processes and Color Transparency

## Key claim
The paper argues that the Brodsky-Lepage factorization scheme for exclusive hadronic processes fails at laboratory energies because the momentum scales of exchanged gluons become too small for perturbative QCD to apply reliably, yet the quark-counting scaling laws still agree with data for reasons that remain mysterious. The authors propose that nuclear filtering, using the nuclear medium to preferentially attenuate long-distance (soft) amplitudes, provides a theoretically cleaner environment to study short-distance QCD than free-space experiments.

## Method
The authors review the impact-parameter factorization formalism of Botts and Sterman (1990) and Li and Sterman (1992), which retains the transverse momentum dependence of the hard scattering kernel and includes Sudakov form factors via renormalization-group summation. For free-space processes, they fit phenomenological models of interfering short-distance (perturbative) and long-distance (Landshoff pinch) amplitudes to experimental data for pion and proton electromagnetic form factors, as well as for πp → π'p' and γp → π⁺n scattering at fixed angle. For nuclear targets, they compute transparency ratios using leading-order pQCD with Sudakov effects for electroproduction processes, and model the more complex hadronic reactions by extending the interfering-amplitude fits to include nuclear attenuation via a transverse-separation-dependent cross section σ_attenuation = k b².

## Key numbers
- The pion form factor calculation using impact-parameter factorization shows correct asymptotic Q² behavior beyond Q ≈ 1 GeV, irrespective of the choice of distribution amplitude.
- For the three-component model fit to πp → π'p' at 90°, the χ² per degree of freedom is 1.97, while the Brodsky-Lepage short-distance model gives χ² per degree of freedom = 99 and is objectively ruled out.
- The parameter k in σ_attenuation = k b² is adjusted to approximately 10 to match SLAC proton transparency data.
- The predicted effective attenuation cross section σ_eff for protons extracted from Monte Carlo calculations is about 31 mb at Q² = 4 GeV², substantially below the standard Glauber theory value of 40 mb.
- The calculations use Λ_QCD = 200 MeV.
- The pion transparency ratio calculations cover Q² up to 5 GeV², where the relativistic boost factor γ = Q²/(2 m_π²) ≈ 25 (Q²/GeV²).

## Context
This review extends the authors' earlier work on nuclear filtering (Jain and Ralston, 1992; Ralston and Pire, 1990; Pire and Ralston, 1992) and contrasts with the original color transparency proposal of Brodsky and Mueller (1982), which assumed that large momentum transfer alone selects short-distance configurations. The paper directly addresses the failures of the Brodsky-Lepage factorization scheme identified by Isgur and Llewellyn-Smith (1989) and Radyushkin (1991), and the soft overlap models of Diehl et al. (2000). The calculations enable future experimental tests at CEBAF for processes such as γA → π⁺n(A-1) and πA → π'p(A-1), where oscillatory transparency ratios are predicted.
