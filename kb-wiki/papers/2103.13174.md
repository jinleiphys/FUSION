# $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment of $^{135}$Pr, $^{105}$Pd, and $^{187}$Au in wobbling motion

> The $g$-factor and static quadrupole moment of the nuclides $^{135}$Pr, $^{105}$Pd, and $^{187}$Au in the wobbling motion are investigated in the particle-rotor model as functions of the total spin $I$. The $g$-factor of $^{105}\mathrm{Pd}$ increases with increasing $I$, due to the negative gyromagnetic ratio of a neutron valence-neutron. This behavior is in contrast to the decreasing $g$-factor of the other two nuclides, $^{135}$Pr and $^{187}$Au, which feature a valence-proton. The static quadrupole moment $Q$ depends on all three expectation values of the total angular momentum. It is smaller in the yrast band than in the wobbling band for the transverse wobblers $^{135}$Pr and $^{105}$Pd, while larger for the longitudinal wobbler $^{187}$Au.

## Key claim

The g-factor and static quadrupole moment of three wobbling nuclei are calculated within the particle-rotor model, showing that the g-factor of the neutron-valence nucleus 105Pd increases with spin due to a negative gyromagnetic ratio, while the g-factors of the proton-valence nuclei 135Pr and 187Au decrease; the static quadrupole moment is smaller in the yrast band than in the wobbling band for the transverse wobblers (135Pr, 105Pd) but larger for the longitudinal wobbler (187Au), though no experimental data exist for these quantities in these three nuclei.

## Method

The particle-rotor model (PRM) was used to calculate the g-factor via the generalized Lande formula, expressed in terms of the gyromagnetic ratios of the valence nucleon (gp) and rotor (gR), the total spin I, the valence-particle angular momentum j, and the expectation value of the squared rotor angular momentum ⟨R²⟩. The static quadrupole moment Q(I) was computed from the expectation values of the squared total angular momentum components along the three principal axes (⟨Î₁²⟩, ⟨Î₂²⟩, ⟨Î₃²⟩) using the intrinsic quadrupole moment Q₀' and the triaxial deformation parameter γ. The deformation parameters, moments of inertia, and gyromagnetic ratios were taken from prior PRM studies that reproduced experimental energy spectra and transition probabilities for these nuclei.

## Key numbers

- 135Pr: g-factor decreases from g(I=15.5) = 0.7; constant offset (gp+gR)/2 = 0.8; j = 5.5
- 105Pd: g-factor increases from g(I=5.5) = -0.2 at bandhead to g(I=15.5) = 0.2 at highest calculated I; constant offset (gp+gR)/2 = 0.1; j = 5.5
- 187Au: g-factor decreases from g(I=4.5) = 0.75 at bandhead to g(I=18.5) = 0.5; constant offset (gp+gR)/2 = 0.6; j = 4.5
- 135Pr and 105Pd: rotor angular momentum expectation value R̅ increases from R̅(I=5.5)=2 (yrast) and R̅(I=6.5)=4 (wobbling) to R̅(I=18.5)=15 for both bands
- 135Pr: static quadrupole moment curves for yrast and wobbling bands cross at Q(I=16.5) = -1.1 e·b
- 105Pd: static quadrupole moment in yrast band becomes larger than in wobbling band at Q(I=14.5) = -1.15 e·b
- 187Au: yrast band SQM starts at Q(I=4.5) = -2.1 e·b, decreases to Q(I=6.5) = -2.4 e·b, then increases to Q(I=18.5) = -1.0 e·b; wobbling band SQM decreases from Q(I=5.5) = -1.75 e·b to Q(I=9.5) = -2.0 e·b, then increases to Q(I=17.5) = -1.17 e·b

## Context

This work extends the particle-rotor model analysis of g-factors and static quadrupole moments previously applied to 133La by Chen et al. (2020) to three additional wobbling nuclei across different mass regions: 135Pr (A≈130, transverse wobbler), 105Pd (A≈100, first odd-neutron wobbling nucleus), and 187Au (A≈190, longitudinal wobbler). The results provide predictions for future experimental measurements of these electromagnetic moments, which could test the angular momentum geometry underlying triaxial nuclear shapes, and motivate more sophisticated theoretical approaches such as random-phase approximation based on cranking (Shimizu 1995, Matsuzaki 2002, Frauendorf 2015), collective Hamiltonian based on tilted axis cranking (Chen 2014, 2016), or triaxial projected shell model (Sheikh 2016, Shimada 2018, Wang 2020).
