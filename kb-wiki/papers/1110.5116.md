# Superfluid Neutrons in the Core of the Neutron Star in Cassiopeia A

> The supernova remnant Cassiopeia A contains the youngest known neutron star which is also the first one for which real time cooling has ever been observed. In order to explain the rapid cooling of this neutron star, we first present the fundamental properties of neutron stars that control their thermal evolution with emphasis on the neutrino emission processes and neutron/proton superfluidity/superconductivity. Equipped with these results, we present a scenario in which the observed cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A is interpreted as being due to the recent onset of neutron superfluidity in the core of the star. The manner in which the earlier occurrence of proton superconductivity determines the observed rapidity of this neutron star's cooling is highlighted. This is the first direct evidence that superfluidity and superconductivity occur at supranuclear densities within neutron stars.

## Key claim

The observed rapid cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A is interpreted as being due to the recent onset of neutron superfluidity in the core of the star, with the earlier occurrence of proton superconductivity determining the observed rapidity of this cooling. This is presented as the first direct evidence that superfluidity and superconductivity occur at supranuclear densities within neutron stars.

## Method

The authors present fundamental properties of neutron stars that control thermal evolution, emphasizing neutrino emission processes and neutron/proton superfluidity and superconductivity. They develop simple analytical models of neutron star cooling to gain physical insight, complemented by numerical simulations employing full general relativity and state-of-the-art microphysics, using the Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (APR) equation of state for illustrative purposes.

## Key numbers

- The fastest known radio pulsar Ter5ad (PSR J1748-2446ad) has period P = 1.39 ms, giving a causality-based upper limit on neutron star radius of R < 65 km
- The gravitational binding argument gives a lower limit on average density of rho_bar > 8 x 10^13 g cm^-3
- Nuclear density is rho_nuc ~ 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3, with theoretical models showing densities up to 10 rho_nuc possibly reachable
- For the APR equation of state, the critical neutron star mass for allowing the direct Urca process is 1.97 solar masses
- The direct Urca process has neutrino emissivity ~ 10^27 R T_9^6 erg cm^-3 s^-1 (fast)
- The modified Urca process has neutrino emissivity ~ 2 x 10^21 R T_9^8 erg cm^-3 s^-1 (slow)
- The Cooper pair process has neutrino emissivity ~ 5 x 10^21 R T_9^7 erg cm^-3 s^-1 (medium)
- The critical proton fraction for the direct Urca process is x_p > 1/9 ~ 11% without muons, or larger than about 15% in the presence of muons

## Context

This work extends the understanding of neutron star thermal evolution by applying it to the first observed case of real-time cooling in a neutron star, that in the supernova remnant Cassiopeia A. The paper builds on prior work on neutrino emission processes by Lattimer (1991), Friman (1979), and Prakash (1992), and on neutron star cooling models by Page (2004). The interpretation enables future observational tests of superfluidity at supranuclear densities and constrains the nuclear equation of state at high density.
