# Effective field theories for heavy quarkonium

> We review recent theoretical developments in heavy quarkonium physics from the point of view of Effective Field Theories of QCD. We discuss Non-Relativistic QCD and concentrate on potential Non-Relativistic QCD. Our main goal will be to derive QCD Schrödinger-like equations that govern the heavy quarkonium physics in the weak and strong coupling regime. We also discuss a selected set of applications, which include spectroscopy, inclusive decays and electromagnetic threshold production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theories (EFTs), specifically potential Non-Relativistic QCD (pNRQCD), provide a rigorous and systematic derivation of QCD Schrödinger-like equations for heavy quarkonium in both the weak-coupling and strong-coupling regimes, resolving long-standing issues with infrared divergences and missing logarithmic corrections that plagued earlier potential model approaches.

## Method

The authors construct a hierarchy of EFTs by sequentially integrating out high-energy degrees of freedom: starting from QCD, they first integrate out the hard scale (the heavy quark mass m) to obtain Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD), then further integrate out the soft scale (relative momentum p ~ mv) to obtain potential Non-Relativistic QCD (pNRQCD). The matching between theories is performed by equating Green's functions computed in QCD and in the EFT at the desired order in the strong coupling αs and in the 1/m expansion, using dimensional regularization as both an ultraviolet and infrared regulator. The resulting pNRQCD Lagrangian contains potentials as matching coefficients, which can be computed either from Feynman diagrams or from Wilson-loop amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The heavy quarkonium system is characterized by three widely separated scales: the hard scale m (the heavy quark mass), the soft scale p ~ mv (relative momentum), and the ultrasoft scale E ~ mv² (kinetic energy), with v ≪ 1.
* The static potential at leading order in the weak-coupling regime is V⁰(r) = -C_F αs/r, where C_F = (N_c² - 1)/(2 N_c) = 4/3 for N_c = 3.
* The QCD beta function coefficients are β₀ = (11/3)C_A - (4/3)T_F n_f and β₁ = (34/3)C_A² - (20/3)C_A T_F n_f - 4 C_F T_F n_f, with C_A = 3 and T_F = 1/2.
* The NRQCD Lagrangian is organized as a power series in 1/m up to O(1/m²), with the kinetic term coefficient c_k = 1 and the constraint 2 c_F - c_S - 1 = 0 from Poincaré invariance.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on NRQCD by Caswell and Lepage (1986) and Lepage et al. (1987), and the subsequent development of pNRQCD by Pineda and Soto (1998). It addresses the shortcomings of traditional potential models derived from Wilson loops, which missed hard logarithms and certain potentials at relative order αs² in the spectrum, as pointed out by Lucha et al. (1991) and Brambilla et al. (2000). The EFT framework enables precision determinations of Standard Model parameters (αs and heavy quark masses) in the weak-coupling regime, and provides a rigorous connection to QCD in the strong-coupling regime where heavy quarkonia serve as a probe of the QCD vacuum structure.
