# Mass splittings of SU(3) baryons within a chiral soliton model

> Considering simultaneously isospin and SU(3) flavor symmetry breakings, we investigate the complete mass splittings of SU(3) baryons within a chiral soliton model, a {}"model-independent approach" being employed. In linear order several new mass relations are derived, which are mostly generalizations of existing mass formulae. The dynamical quantities appearing in the expressions for the masses are fixed by fitting them to the masses of the baryon octet and the masses of $Ω^{-}$ and $Θ^{+}$ as input rather than by extracting them from a calculated self-consistent soliton profile. In particular, the consideration of isospin symmetry breaking allows us to use the experimental data of the whole octet baryon masses as input. We predict the masses of the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet without any further adjustable free parameter. In addition, we also obtain the pion-nucleon sigma term which turns out to be $Σ_{πN} = 36.4\pm3.9\,\mathrm{MeV}$. We get the ratio of the current light quark masses $R = 58.1\pm1.3$. The present results indicate that the recent experimental data for the $Θ^{+}(1524)$ are compatible with the experimental data of the octet and decuplet masses.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by simultaneously including isospin and SU(3) flavor symmetry breakings in a chiral soliton model, the masses of the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet can be predicted without any further adjustable free parameters after fitting to the octet, Omega-, and Theta+ masses, but the existence of the Theta+(1524) itself remains disputable and is used as input rather than a confirmed prediction.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian for SU(3) baryons within a chiral soliton model, including rotational excitations and symmetry-breaking terms from both SU(3) flavor breaking (strange quark mass) and isospin breaking (up-down quark mass difference and electromagnetic corrections). The dynamical parameters (moments of inertia I1, I2, and the parameters alpha, beta, gamma) are fixed by fitting to the experimental masses of the baryon octet, the Omega-(1672), and the Theta+(1524), rather than by extracting them from a self-consistent soliton profile. The electromagnetic mass corrections are treated using a collective operator formalism with parameters c(8) and c(27) determined from empirical data.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: Sigma_{pi N} = 36.4 +/- 3.9 MeV
- Ratio of current light quark masses: R = 58.1 +/- 1.3
- Theta+ mass used as input: M_Theta = 1.524 +/- 0.002 +/- 0.003 GeV/c^2 (from LEPS collaboration)
- Electromagnetic parameters: c(8) = -0.15 +/- 0.23 MeV, c(27) = 8.62 +/- 2.39 MeV
- The generalized Guadagnini mass formula deviates from experimental data by about 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model approach of Diakonov (1997) by incorporating isospin symmetry breaking alongside SU(3) flavor breaking, allowing the use of the full octet baryon masses from the Particle Data Group as input. It builds on the electromagnetic mass splitting analysis of Yang (2010) and the isospin breaking studies of Praszalowicz (1992) and Blotz (1994) within the chiral quark-soliton model. The results enable predictions for the baryon decuplet and antidecuplet masses without adjustable parameters, and the mass relations derived generalize the Gell-Mann-Okubo, Coleman-Glashow, and Guadagnini mass formulae.
