# Time-like detonation in presence of magnetic field

> We study the effect of magnetic field in an implosion process achieved by radiation. A time-varying sinusoidal magnetic field is seen to affect the continuous transition of space-like detonation to time-like detonation at the core of implosion region. The oscillating varying magnetic field has a significant effect in increasing the volume of the time-like detonation of the core of implosion and also modify the time of the implosion process. This transition can have significant outcome both theoretically and experimentally in the areas of high energy hadronization of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) matter and Inertial Confinement Fusion efforts of fuels.

# Time-like detonation in presence of magnetic field

## Key claim

A time-varying sinusoidal magnetic field can increase the volume of the time-like detonation region at the core of an implosion and modify the implosion timing, but only if the magnetic field is time-varying (a constant magnetic field does not affect the heating dynamics). The effect depends on the magnetic field strength relative to the radiation contribution, and the analysis neglects compression of the fuel and any interaction between radiation and magnetic field.

## Method

The authors model a spherical core with vanishing opacity surrounded by a rapidly igniting shell that radiates isotropically, solving for the temperature profile T(r,t) from radiative heating. They then add a time-varying sinusoidal magnetic field (B = B₀ sin(ωt)) to the first law of thermodynamics via the magnetic work term M dB, where magnetization M = Nμ²B/(k_B T), and solve the resulting nonlinear differential equation numerically for the temperature as a function of radius and time. The critical point (r_c, t_c) where the slope of the contour T(r,t) = T_c equals ±1 marks the transition from space-like to time-like detonation.

## Key numbers

- For the non-magnetic case, the critical point occurs at r_c = 0.51, t_c = 2.26 (analytic) or r_c = 0.52, t_c = 2.25 (numerical), for a critical temperature T_c = 3 (in units where K₁ = 2πκQ/C_v = 1).
- For sinusoidal magnetic field with K₂ = K₁ = 1 (equal magnetic and radiation contribution), the critical point shifts to r_c = 0.664, t_c = 1.6364.
- For sinusoidal magnetic field with K₂ = 0.1 (magnetic field one-tenth the strength), the critical point shifts to r_c = 0.587, t_c = 2.091.
- For the slowly varying magnetic field approximation (sin(ωt) ≈ ωt), the critical point is r_c = 0.56, t_c = 2.15.
- The time required to heat the center of the core to T_c = 3 (non-magnetic) is t = 2.5 from the analytic formula T(0,t) = (4πκQ/C_v)(t-1).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Csernai (1992) on continuous transition from space-like to time-like detonation in radiation-driven implosions, which was previously applied to hadronization of quark-gluon plasma and inertial confinement fusion. The authors note that prior experimental ICF efforts at the Omega facility (Chang, Hohenburger) and MagLIF (Slutz, Cuneo, Gomez) used axial magnetic fields for confinement and compression via PdV work, but did not consider the M dB magnetic work term. The results suggest that time-varying magnetic fields could expand the time-like detonation region, potentially reducing Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities that have hindered ICF experiments (Hurricane, Park, Casey).
