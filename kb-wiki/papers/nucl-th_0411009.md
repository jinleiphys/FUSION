# Transport analysis of K+ production in proton-nucleus reactions

> The production of $K^+$ mesons in proton-nucleus collisions from 1.0 to 2.3 GeV is analyzed with respect to one-step nucleon-nucleon $(NN\to N Y K^+$) and two-step $Δ$-nucleon $(ΔN \to K^+ Y N$) or pion-nucleon $(πN \to K^+ Y $) production channels on the basis of a coupled-channel transport approach (CBUU) including the kaon final-state-interactions (FSI). Momentum-dependent potentials for the nucleon, hyperon and kaon in the final state are included as well as $K^+$ elastic rescattering in the target nucleus. The transport calculations are compared to the experimental $K^+$ spectra taken at COSY-Jülich. Our systematic analysis of $K^+$ spectra from $^{12}C$, $^{63}Cu$, $^{107}Ag$ and $^{197}Au$ targets as well as their momentum differential ratios gives a repulsive $K^+$ potential of $20\pm 5 $ MeV at normal nuclear matter density.

## Key claim

The paper extracts a repulsive in-medium kaon potential of 20 ± 5 MeV at normal nuclear matter density by comparing coupled-channel transport calculations to experimental K⁺ momentum spectra from proton-nucleus collisions, but this value depends on the assumption that the production matrix element squared does not change in the medium and that the shape of spectral ratios is practically independent of bombarding energy.

## Method

The authors used the coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (CBUU) transport model to simulate K⁺ production in proton collisions with ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, ¹⁰⁷Ag, and ¹⁹⁷Au targets at bombarding energies from 1.0 to 2.3 GeV. They included one-step nucleon-nucleon (NN → N Y K⁺) and two-step Δ-nucleon (ΔN → K⁺ Y N) or pion-nucleon (πN → K⁺ Y) production channels, momentum-dependent potentials for nucleons, hyperons, and kaons, and K⁺ elastic rescattering and final-state interactions. The calculated K⁺ momentum spectra and their ratios for different target combinations were compared to experimental data from the ANKE collaboration at COSY-Jülich, and a χ²-fit was performed to determine the kaon potential strength.

## Key numbers

- Repulsive K⁺ potential at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³: 20 ± 5 MeV, extracted from a χ²-fit to the ratio of K⁺ spectra from p+Au to p+C reactions.
- Bombarding energy range studied: 1.0 to 2.3 GeV.
- At 1.0 GeV bombarding energy, the nuclear potential enhances the K⁺ yield by about a factor of 2 relative to calculations without nuclear potential, while the additional repulsive K⁺ potential decreases the yield by a factor of 2-3.
- At 2.3 GeV, secondary channels contribute about 30% of the K⁺ yield for Au targets and less than 20% for C targets.
- The maximum in the Au/C spectral ratio appears at about 230 MeV/c with the repulsive kaon potential, compared to slightly below 200 MeV/c when only Coulomb repulsion is included.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies of K⁺ production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies (Cassing 1997) and proton-nucleus reactions (Sibirtsev 1998, Rudy 2002) by systematically comparing to new differential K⁺ spectra from the ANKE collaboration (Barsov 2001, Buescher 2004). The extracted repulsive K⁺ potential of 20 ± 5 MeV agrees with the scattering length approximation of Lutz (1998) and provides a benchmark for chiral perturbation theory predictions of kaon self-energies, which have been debated due to higher-order terms (Gal 1998, Lutz 1998, Ramos 2000). The analysis enables future studies of the momentum dependence of kaon self-energies and can guide searches for cooperative production mechanisms by two- or three-nucleon clusters at very low bombarding energies.
