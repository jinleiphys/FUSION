# Hadronic Spectral Functions in Lattice QCD

> QCD spectral functions of hadrons in the pseudo-scalar and vector channels are extracted from lattice Monte Carlo data of the imaginary time Green's functions. The maximum entropy method works well for this purpose, and the resonance and continuum structures in the spectra are obtained in addition to the ground state peaks.

# Hadronic Spectral Functions in Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The maximum entropy method (MEM) can reconstruct hadronic spectral functions from lattice QCD data without a priori assumptions on the spectral shape, producing resonance and continuum structures beyond the ground state peaks, but the spectral "shape" of the second and third peaks has marginal or insignificant statistical significance given the data quality.

## Method
The authors used quenched lattice QCD simulations with the plaquette gluon action and Wilson quark action on a 20³ × 24 lattice at β = 6.0, computing Euclidean correlation functions D(τ) for the pseudo-scalar (PS) and vector (V) channels. They applied the maximum entropy method to perform the inverse Laplace transform from imaginary-time data to real-frequency spectral functions, using the Shannon-Jaynes entropy with a default model motivated by perturbative QCD and integrating over the hyperparameter α.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.0847 fm (a⁻¹ = 2.33 GeV) at β = 6.0
- Lattice size: 20³ × 24, spatial size Ls a = 1.69 fm
- Number of gauge configurations Nconf = 161 for each of three hopping parameters κ = 0.153, 0.1545, and 0.1557
- Pion peak analysis gave κc = 0.1570(3), consistent with 0.1571 from asymptotic behavior
- ρ-meson mass in chiral limit: mρa = 0.348(15), consistent with mρa = 0.331(22) from asymptotic behavior
- Second peak positions relative to ρ mass: m²ⁿᵈ/mρ = 1.88(8) for PS channel, 2.44(11) for V channel
- High-energy continuum estimate for V channel at ω ≈ 6 GeV: ρV ≈ 0.86; for PS channel: ρPS ≈ 2.0
- ω-space discretization: Δω = 10 MeV; ωmax ≈ π/a ≈ 7.3 GeV; image dimension Nω ≈ 750

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of static observables (Lattice98) by addressing the ill-posed problem of analytic continuation from imaginary-time to real-time spectral functions, which previous approaches (others) required strong assumptions on spectral shape. The MEM had been successfully applied in condensed matter quantum Monte Carlo simulations (PhysRep, Linden) and image reconstruction; this paper represents the first serious attempt to apply it to lattice QCD data. The results enable systematic study of higher resonances and finite-temperature spectral changes, though the authors note that direct chiral extrapolation of the MEM image does not work straightforwardly and remains an open problem.
