# The Octet Meson and Octet Baryon Interaction with strangeness and the $Λ(1405)$

> The octet meson and baryon interaction with strangeness $S=-1$ is studied fully relativistically with chiral Lagrangian. In this work, a Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with spectator quasipotential approximation is applied to study the reactions $K^-p\to MB$ with $MB=K^-p,\bar{K}^0n, π^-Σ^+,π^0Σ^0, π^+Σ^-$ and $ηΛ$ with all possible partial waves and theoretical results are comparable with experimental data. It is found that the Weinberg-Tomozawa potential derived from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian only provides the contributions from partial waves with spin-parities $J^P=1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$. Two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ is confirmed with poles at $1383+99 i$ and $1423+14i$ MeV. The lower and higher poles originate from $Σπ$ interaction as a resonance and $\bar{K}N$ interaction as a bound state, respectively.

## Key claim

The two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) is confirmed using a fully relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with spectator quasipotential approximation, but only the Weinberg-Tomozawa term from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian is included, and the model parameters are not fitted directly to experimental data but rather determined by reproducing threshold ratios.

## Method

The authors applied a Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with covariant spectator quasipotential approximation to study the coupled-channel reactions K⁻p → MB for MB = K⁻p, K̄⁰n, π⁻Σ⁺, π⁰Σ⁰, π⁺Σ⁻, and ηΛ. The potential was derived from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term only), and the integral equation was solved after partial wave expansion into helicity amplitudes with all possible partial waves. The single free parameter α (controlling the cutoff Λ via Λ = m + αΛ_QCD with Λ_QCD = 0.22 GeV) was fixed to α = 1.63 by reproducing three experimental threshold ratios, and the chiral constant was set to f = 1.15f_π with f_π = 93 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Two poles of the Λ(1405) found at 1383 + 99i MeV and 1423 + 14i MeV in the complex energy plane for Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ partial wave
- The lower pole (1383 + 99i MeV) originates from πΣ interaction as a resonance; the higher pole (1423 + 14i MeV) originates from K̄N interaction as a bound state
- Threshold ratio γ = Γ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻)/Γ(K⁻p → π⁻Σ⁺) = 2.36 (experimental: 2.36 ± 0.04)
- Threshold ratio R_c = Γ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻ + π⁻Σ⁺)/Γ(K⁻p → inelastic) = 0.619 (experimental: 0.664 ± 0.011)
- Threshold ratio R_n = Γ(K⁻p → π⁰Λ)/Γ(K⁻p → neutral) = 0.221 (experimental: 0.189 ± 0.011)
- K⁻p scattering length a_{K⁻p} = -1.10 + 0.78i fm (experimental: -0.66 + 0.81i fm)
- Cutoff parameter α = 1.63; chiral constant f = 1.15 × 93 MeV = 106.95 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oset and Ramos (1997) and Jido et al. (2003) by using a fully relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation with spectator quasipotential approximation rather than the on-shell factorization method. The two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) confirms the findings of the chiral unitary approach, with the lower pole from πΣ resonance and higher pole from K̄N bound state. The results enable future studies that include s- and u-channel diagrams and next-to-leading order terms to improve agreement with experimental data, particularly for the K⁻p scattering length.
