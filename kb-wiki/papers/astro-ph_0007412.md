# Presupernova collapse models with improved weak-interaction rates

> Improved values for stellar weak interaction rates have been recently calculated based upon a large shell model diagonalization. Using these new rates (for both beta decay and electron capture), we have examined the presupernova evolution of massive stars in the range 15-40 Msun. Comparing our new models with a standard set of presupernova models by Woosley and Weaver, we find significantly larger values for the electron-to-baryon ratio Ye at the onset of collapse and iron core masses reduced by approximately 0.1 Msun. The inclusion of beta-decay accounts for roughly half of the revisions, while the other half is a consequence of the improved nuclear physics. These changes will have important consequences for nucleosynthesis and the supernova explosion mechanism.

## Key claim

Using shell-model weak-interaction rates (for both beta decay and electron capture) in presupernova evolution models of 15–40 solar mass stars yields significantly larger electron-to-baryon ratios and iron core masses reduced by about 0.1 solar masses compared to the standard Woosley and Weaver (1995) models; roughly half of the revision comes from including beta decay and half from the improved nuclear physics.

## Method

The authors incorporated new shell-model weak-interaction rates (electron capture, positron emission, and beta decay) for nuclei with mass numbers A=45–65, supplemented by rates from Oda et al. for lighter nuclei, into stellar evolution calculations of massive stars (15–40 solar masses). They kept all other stellar physics as close as possible to the earlier Woosley and Weaver (1995) models, which used Fuller-Fowler-Newman (FFN) rates for electron capture and older beta-decay rates from Mazurek and Hansen. The evolution was followed through core oxygen burning, silicon burning, and up to iron core collapse.

## Key numbers

- Central electron-to-baryon ratio \(Y_e\) at onset of core collapse increased by 0.01–0.015 for stars in the mass range 11–40 solar masses
- Iron core masses reduced by approximately 0.1 solar masses overall; for stars less than about 20 solar masses the reduction is about 0.05 solar masses
- For a 15 solar mass star, central silicon burning ignites at \(7 \times 10^5\) s before collapse and terminates at \(6.6 \times 10^4\) s before collapse; silicon shell burning starts at \(3.2 \times 10^4\) s before collapse
- For a 25 solar mass star, central silicon burning ignites at \(5.6 \times 10^4\) s before collapse and terminates at \(10^4\) s before collapse; silicon shell burning starts at \(4.7 \times 10^3\) s before collapse
- Core contraction sets in at about 280 s before collapse for the 15 solar mass star and about 230 s before collapse for the 25 solar mass star
- Shell-model electron-capture rates are smaller than FFN rates by on average an order of magnitude for pf-shell nuclei

## Context

This work extends the presupernova models of Woosley and Weaver (1995) by replacing the older FFN electron-capture rates and the beta-decay rates from Mazurek and Hansen with new shell-model weak-interaction rates calculated by Langanke et al. (2000). The inclusion of realistic beta-decay rates, which were foreseen as important by Fuller (1994) based on one-zone models, is demonstrated here in complete stellar models for the first time. The results enable improved core-collapse simulations with detailed neutrino transport, which are needed to draw conclusions about the supernova explosion mechanism, and provide guidance for future charge-exchange experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities.
