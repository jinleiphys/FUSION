# Finite Temperature Phase Diagram of a Two-Component Fermi Gas with Density Imbalance

> We investigated possible superfluid phases at finite temperature in a two-component Fermi gas with density imbalance. In the frame of a general four-fermion interaction theory, we solved in the BCS region the gap equations for the pairing gap and pairing momentum under the restriction of fixed number densities, and analyzed the stability of different phases by calculating the superfluid density and number susceptibilities. The homogeneous superfluid is stable only at high temperature and low number asymmetry, the inhomogeneous LOFF survives at low temperature and high number asymmetry, and in between them there exists another possible inhomogeneous phase, that of phase separation. The critical temperatures and the orders of the phase transitions among the superfluid phases and normal phase are calculated analytically and numerically. The phase diagram we obtained in the temperature and number asymmetry plane is quite different from the one in temperature and chemical potential difference plane for a system with fixed chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The homogeneous superfluid (HS) phase in a two-component Fermi gas with density imbalance is stable only at high temperature and low number asymmetry, while the inhomogeneous LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) phase survives at low temperature and high number asymmetry; between them exists a phase separation (PS) region. The authors qualify that they consider only the simplest single-plane-wave LOFF (FF) state, arguing that a general LOFF state would not change the qualitative phase structure.

## Method

The authors solved the mean-field gap equations for the pairing gap Δ and pairing momentum q under fixed number densities for a two-component Fermi gas with a four-fermion interaction in the BCS region (coupling p_F a_s = -0.6). They calculated the superfluid density ρ_s and number susceptibility χ as functions of temperature and population imbalance α to determine the stability of the homogeneous superfluid, then included the LOFF state with finite pair momentum and compared free energies to map the phase boundaries.

## Key numbers

- The maximum population imbalance for the homogeneous superfluid phase is α_HS = 0.059 at coupling p_F a_s = -0.6.
- The critical imbalance at zero temperature is given by α_0 = 3Δ_0/(4E_F), where Δ_0 is the symmetric BCS gap.
- The LOFF momentum q vanishes at the turning temperature T_1, with critical behavior q ∼ (1 − T/T_1)^{1/2} as T → T_1^−.
- The superfluid density near the critical temperature T_c behaves as ρ_s = ρ_c (1 − T/T_c) > 0, and near the onset temperature T_o as ρ_s = ρ_o (1 − T/T_o) < 0.

## Context

This work extends studies of population-imbalanced Fermi gases in the BCS regime, where earlier work by Sarma (1963) identified the instability of the homogeneous gapless phase, and by Larkin, Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde, Ferrell (1964) proposed the inhomogeneous LOFF state. The paper builds on zero-temperature analyses by Pao, Wu, and Yip (2006) and by Sheehy and Radzihovsky (2006) by including finite temperature effects, and it resolves the competition between the homogeneous Sarma phase, the LOFF phase, and phase separation. The results enable future experimental searches for LOFF phases in ultracold atomic gases, particularly in systems like ⁶Li with scattering length a_s = −2160 a_B and density n = 3.8 × 10¹² cm⁻³.
