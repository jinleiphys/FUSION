# Towards a Model-Independent Low Momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> We provide evidence for a high precision model-independent low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction. Performing a momentum-space renormalization group decimation, we find that the effective interactions constructed from various high precision nucleon-nucleon interaction models, such as the Paris, Bonn, Nijmegen, Argonne, CD Bonn and Idaho potentials, are identical. This model-independent low momentum interaction, called V_{low k}, reproduces the same phase shifts and deuteron pole as the input potential models, without ambiguous assumptions on the high momentum components, which are not constrained by low energy data and lead to model-dependent results in many-body applications. V_{low k} is energy-independent and does not necessitate the calculation of the Brueckner G matrix.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a renormalization-group decimation of various high-precision nucleon-nucleon potential models (Paris, Bonn, Nijmegen, Argonne, CD Bonn, and Idaho) yields a single, model-independent low-momentum interaction, called \(V_{\text{low k}}\), provided the momentum cutoff is \(\Lambda \lesssim 2.1\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) (corresponding to laboratory energies \(E_{\text{lab}} \lesssim 350\;\text{MeV}\)). The hedge is that this model independence holds only below that cutoff; above it, the input potentials still differ.

## Method

The authors perform a momentum-space renormalization group (RG) decimation: they integrate out high-momentum components (above a cutoff \(\Lambda\)) of each bare nucleon-nucleon potential using a Bloch-Horowitz \(\widehat{Q}\)-box formalism, then convert the resulting energy-dependent effective potential into an energy-independent \(V_{\text{low k}}\) via Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded diagrams and a Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation. The RG flow equation \(d V_{\text{low k}}/d\Lambda\) preserves the half-on-shell \(T\) matrix (and thus the phase shifts and deuteron pole) of the original potential for all momenta below \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- The model-independent regime is reached for cutoff \(\Lambda \lesssim 2.1\;\text{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to laboratory energies \(E_{\text{lab}} \lesssim 350\;\text{MeV}\).
- The scattering lengths reproduced by \(V_{\text{low k}}\) are \(a_{^1\text{S}_0} = -23.73\;\text{fm}\) and \(a_{^3\text{S}_1} = 5.42\;\text{fm}\).
- The discontinuity in the \(^3\)S\(_1\) channel at the deuteron pole (\(k_{\text{D}} = 0.23\;\text{fm}^{-1}\)) is \(\Delta V_{\text{low k}}(0,0;k_{\text{D}})_{^3\text{S}_1} = 22.8\;\text{fm}\), computed from the effective range \(r_0 = 1.75\;\text{fm}\).
- For the Idaho potential, off-diagonal matrix elements deviate from the others at \(\Lambda \approx 2\,m_\pi = 1.4\;\text{fm}^{-1}\); lowering the cutoff to \(\Lambda \lesssim 1.4\;\text{fm}^{-1}\) collapses them as well.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Birse et al. (1998) and applies it to realistic high-precision potentials (Paris, Bonn, Nijmegen, Argonne, CD Bonn, and Idaho). It contrasts with chiral effective field theory (EFT) treatments (e.g., Ordonez 1994, Kaplan-Savage-Wise 1998, Epelbaum 1999) by not expanding the interaction in powers of local operators and by preserving the exact \(T\) matrix without truncation. The resulting \(V_{\text{low k}}\) enables model-independent many-body calculations: it has been used as a shell-model effective interaction for nuclei such as \(^{18}\)O and \(^{134}\)Te (Bogner 2001), in Fermi liquid theory to connect free-space and quasiparticle interactions (Schwenk 2002), and in microscopic calculations of pairing gaps in neutron matter (Schwenk 2003). Preliminary few-body results indicate that the three-body force needed with \(V_{\text{low k}}\) is weaker than that for the Argonne potential.
