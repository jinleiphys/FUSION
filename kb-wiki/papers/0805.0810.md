# Looking for intrinsic charm in the forward region at BNL RHIC and CERN LHC

> The complete understanding of the basic constituents of hadrons and the hadronic dynamics at high energies are two of the main challenges for the theory of strong interactions. In particular, the existence of intrinsic heavy quark components in the hadron wave function must be confirmed (or disproved). In this paper we propose a new mechanism for the production of $D$-mesons at forward rapidities based on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism and demonstrate that the resulting transverse momentum spectra are strongly dependent on the behavior of the charm distribution at large Bjorken $x$. Our results show clearly that the hypothesis of intrinsic charm can be tested in $pp$ and $p(d) A$ collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hypothesis of intrinsic charm can be tested in pp and p(d)A collisions at RHIC and LHC by measuring D-meson transverse momentum spectra at forward rapidities, but only if the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism is used instead of standard collinear factorization, which is expected to break down at large Feynman x_F.

## Method

The authors adapt the DGLAP ⊗ CGC factorization scheme previously used for light hadron production to D-meson production at forward rapidities. They treat the projectile as a dilute system evolving according to DGLAP dynamics and the target as a dense system described by the CGC formalism, using the CTEQ charm distribution functions and the dipole scattering amplitude parameterized to fit RHIC data. The charm quarks are assumed to fragment into D mesons according to fragmentation functions from BKK, and the calculation contains no new free parameters.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) and fixed rapidity y = 4, the typical x_p values for D-meson production are in the range 0.4 lesssim x_p lesssim 0.95.
* At LHC energy (sqrt(s) = 14000 GeV) and fixed rapidity y = 6, the typical x_p values are in the range 0.04 lesssim x_p lesssim 0.6.
* The BHPS and meson cloud intrinsic charm models predict cross sections larger than the no-IC model at RHIC in the whole p_T range considered.
* At LHC, the enhancement from intrinsic charm grows with p_T and can reach a factor of 10 at large transverse momenta.
* For d-Au collisions at RHIC at forward rapidity y = 3.2, a similar enhancement in the p_T spectra is predicted, occurring at lower rapidities than in pp collisions.
* At y = 4 in pp collisions at RHIC, the intrinsic charm distribution is almost a factor 10 larger than the PYTHIA estimate in all the p_T range considered.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based formalism for light hadron production developed by Dumitru, Hayashigaki, and Jalilian-Marian (2006) and applied to RHIC data by Boer, Utermann, and Wessels (2007) to the case of D-meson production with intrinsic charm. It contrasts with earlier studies of intrinsic charm by Halzen et al. (1978), Brodsky, Hoyer, Peterson, and Sakai (1980), and Ingelman and Schlein (1985), which treated the emergence of charmed mesons through non-perturbative coalescence, whereas this work provides a perturbative treatment of the intrinsic charm interaction with the CGC target. The results are complementary to a contemporaneous study by Kharzeev, Kovchegov, Schmidt, and Szymanowski (2008) which searched for intrinsic charm effects in D-meson p_T distributions at Tevatron. The paper enables experimental searches for intrinsic charm at forward rapidities at RHIC and LHC, where standard PYTHIA predictions would be insufficient to describe the data if intrinsic charm exists.
