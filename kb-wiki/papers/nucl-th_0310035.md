# Nonequilibrium chiral dynamics by the time dependent variational approach with squeezed states

> We investigate the inhomogeneous chiral dynamics of the O(4) linear sigma model in 1+1 dimensions using the time dependent variational approach in the space spanned by the squeezed states. We compare two cases, with and without the Gaussian approximation for the Green's functions. We show that mode-mode correlation plays a decisive role in the out-of-equilibrium quantum dynamics of domain formation and squeezing of states.

# Nonequilibrium chiral dynamics by the time dependent variational approach with squeezed states

## Key claim

The authors show that including off-diagonal components of the two-point Green's functions in momentum space (mode-mode correlations) is decisive for the formation of large disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains and the amplification of quantum fluctuations in the O(4) linear sigma model, while the Gaussian approximation that ignores these correlations produces only small, noisy domain structures and negligible fluctuation growth.

## Method

The authors apply the time dependent variational approach (TDVA) with squeezed states to the O(4) linear sigma model in 1+1 dimensions, solving the equations of motion for the mean fields and the full two-point Green's functions (including off-diagonal momentum components) on a lattice with periodic boundary conditions. They compare two cases: case I retains all components of the Green's functions, while case II (the Gaussian approximation) sets off-diagonal components to zero, corresponding to the assumption of translational invariance for the Green's functions. The quench initial condition is implemented by randomly distributing the mean field variables according to a Gaussian distribution with width δ = 0.19v, and the initial Green's functions are taken as those of a coherent state with degenerate mass m₀ = 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: lattice spacing d = 1.0 fm, total length L = 64 fm, momentum cutoff Λ = 1071 MeV
- Model parameters: λ = 3.44, v = 110 MeV, h₀ = (103 MeV)³, giving pion mass M_π = 138 MeV, sigma meson mass M_σ = 500 MeV, and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV in vacuum
- Initial Gaussian width for mean fields: δ = 0.19 v
- Domain structure in case I continues to grow until approximately t = 40 fm
- In case II, growth of spatial correlation ends by approximately t = 5 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DCC formation that used classical approximations (refs. classical01, classical02) and quantum treatments with the Hartree or large N approximations (Boyanovski 2001, Cooper 2001), which typically assumed spatial homogeneity and suffered from insufficient thermalization and inability to describe domain structures. It also goes beyond recent quantum mechanical studies of spatially inhomogeneous systems (Berges, Cooper 2002, Smit, Bettencourt) that adopted the Gaussian approximation with diagonal Green's functions. The results demonstrate that mode-mode correlations are essential for DCC domain formation, and the authors suggest this has implications for identical particle correlation measurements in heavy ion collisions, where the assumption of independent emission from different momentum modes may need revision.
