# Many-Quark Model with su(4) Algebraic Structure

> With the use of a certain type of the Schwinger boson representation of the su(4) algebra, the Bonn model for many-quark systems and an extension of the model, in which the energies of the various exact states are controled, namely, the energies are enhanced or de-enhanced, are investigated. Especially, the triplet formation and the pairing correlation in many-quark states are discussed. In the boson space, all the results are analytically expressed in the exact forms.

## Key claim

Using a Schwinger boson representation of the su(4) algebra, the Bonn model for many-quark systems and a modified version that adds the su(3) Casimir operator with an adjustable strength parameter \(\chi\) are solved exactly in the boson space, revealing that colored states can be energetically lower than color-neutral ones in the original model but can be raised by the new force to restore color neutrality.

## Method

The authors construct a Schwinger boson representation of the su(4) algebra using eight boson operators (\(\hat a_i, \hat b_i, \hat a, \hat b\) with \(i=1,2,3\)) to bosonize the Bonn model Hamiltonian \(\tilde H = -(\tilde S^1 \tilde S_1 + \tilde S^2 \tilde S_2 + \tilde S^3 \tilde S_3)\) and its modification \(\tilde H_m = \tilde H + \chi \tilde{\mathbf Q}^2\), where \(\tilde{\mathbf Q}^2\) is the su(3) Casimir operator. They identify the boson images of the fermion number operators and the level degeneracy \(\Omega\), then reformulate the system in a hole picture using the su(1,1) algebra generated by \(\hat T_+, \hat T_-, \hat T_0\) to obtain analytically exact energy eigenvalues and eigenstates for both the triplet formation and the pairing correlation cases.

## Key numbers

- The minimum weight state parameters satisfy \(\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq \sigma_3 \geq 0\) from positivity conditions, with \(\sigma_i\) related to quark numbers via \(n_1 = \Omega + \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 - \sigma_3\), \(n_2 = \Omega + \sigma_2 - \sigma_3 - \sigma_1\), \(n_3 = \Omega + \sigma_3 - \sigma_1 - \sigma_2\).
- For the full symmetric representation, \(\sigma_1 = \sigma_2 = \sigma_3 = \sigma_0\) and \(n_1 = n_2 = n_3 = n_0\); for the partial symmetric representation, \(\sigma_1 > \sigma_2 = \sigma_3\) and \(n_1 > n_2 = n_3\).
- The boson operators carry fermion numbers: \(\hat a^*\) carries 3, \(\hat a^*_i\) carries 2, \(\hat b^*_i\) carries 1, and \(\hat b^*\) carries 0.
- The total quark number operator in the boson space is \(\hat N = 3n_0 + 3\hat a^*\hat a + 2\sum_j \hat a^*_j \hat a_j + \sum_j \hat b^*_j \hat b_j\).

## Context

This work extends the Bonn model originally proposed by Petry et al. (the Bonn group) for many-quark systems, which describes the nucleus as a system of interacting quarks within a MIT bag and is related to the su(4) algebra. The paper provides analytically exact solutions for both the original model and a modified version with an adjustable su(3) Casimir term, enabling controlled enhancement or suppression of colored state energies. This enables future studies of phase transitions between color-neutral and colored configurations and clarifies the relationship between triplet formation and pairing correlations, complementing earlier work on su(4) algebraic models for nuclear physics (isospin vector and scalar pairing model), high-temperature superconductivity, and the four-single-particle-level Lipkin model.
