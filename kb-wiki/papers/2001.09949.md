# Bulk properties and multi-particle correlations in large and small systems

> Charged particle production is calculated in a hybrid framework consisting of the IP-Glasma initial state, MUSIC viscous relativistic fluid dynamics, and the UrQMD microscopic hadronic cascade. Using one set of parameters, we compute observables for a large variety of collision systems. We compare to experimental data in p+p, p+Pb, Xe+Xe, Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at various energies, and make predictions for potential O+O runs at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

Using a single fixed set of parameters in a hybrid IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD framework, the authors demonstrate that bulk observables and multiparticle correlations can be described across a wide range of collision systems and energies, but the model shows a decreasing trend of \(v_2\{2\}\) with multiplicity in pp collisions that disagrees with the slight increase seen in experimental data.

## Method

The authors employed an event-by-event hybrid framework: the IP-Glasma model (including nucleon position fluctuations, three sub-nucleon hotspots per nucleon, and color charge fluctuations) provides the initial state at \(\tau_{\rm init}=0.4\,{\rm fm}/c\); MUSIC viscous relativistic fluid dynamics evolves the system with constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s=0.12\) and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio; below a switching temperature of \(T_{\rm sw}=145\,{\rm MeV}\), the UrQMD microscopic hadronic cascade handles the final stage. The same parameters were used for all collision systems, varying only target/projectile species and collision energy.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio fixed at \(\eta/s=0.12\) for all systems.
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: \(T_{\rm sw}=145\,{\rm MeV}\).
- Initial time for hydrodynamic simulation: \(\tau_{\rm init}=0.4\,{\rm fm}/c\).
- Predictions made for \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_3\{2\}\) in O+O collisions at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (5.02 TeV) energies.
- The model predicts \(v_n\{2\}\)(p/dAu 200 GeV) > \(v_n\{2\}\)(AuAu 200 GeV) at highest multiplicities, while \(v_n\{2\}\)(pPb 5020 GeV) < \(v_n\{2\}\)(PbPb 5020 GeV) at the respective highest multiplicity reached in the smaller system.

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD framework previously described by Schenke et al. (2019) to a comprehensive comparison across p+p, p+Pb, Xe+Xe, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions at multiple energies, using a single parameter set. It enables predictions for future O+O runs at RHIC and LHC, and provides a baseline for extracting information on the initial state and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma. The discrepancy in pp \(v_2\{2\}\) trends suggests that final state effects may be underestimated relative to initial state anisotropies in the calculation.
