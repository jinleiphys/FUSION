# The doubly virtual $(ֿ€^0,־·,־·')\to־³^*־³^*$ transition form factors in the light-front quark model

> We report our investigation on the doubly virtual TFFs $F_{{\rm P}־³^*}(Q^2_1,Q^2_2)$ for the ${\rm P}\to־³^*(q_1)־³^*(q_2) \;({\rm P}=ֿ€^0,־·,־·')$ transitions using the light-front quark model (LFQM). Performing a LF calculation in the exactly solvable manifestly covariant Bethe-Salpeter (BS) model as the first illustration, we used $q^+_1=0$ frame and found that both LF and manifestly covariant calculations produce exactly the same results for $F_{{\rm P}־³^*}(Q^2_1,Q^2_2)$. This confirms the absence of the LF zero mode in the doubly virtual TFFs. We then mapped this covariant BS model to the standard LFQM using the more phenomenologically accessible Gaussian wave function provided by the LFQM analysis of meson mass spectra. For the numerical analyses of $F_{{\rm P}־³^*}(Q^2_1,Q^2_2)$, we compared our LFQM results with the available experimental data and the perturbative QCD (pQCD) and the vector meson dominance (VMD) model predictions. As $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2)\to\infty$, our LFQM result for doubly virtual TFF is consistent with the pQCD prediction, i.e. $F_{{\rm P}־³^*}(Q^2_1, Q^2_2)\sim 1/(Q^2_1 + Q^2_2)$, while it differs far from the result of VMD model which behaves $F^{\rm VMD}_{{\rm P}־³^*}(Q^2_1, Q^2_2)\sim 1/(Q^2_1 Q^2_2)$. Our LFQM prediction for $F_{־·'־³^*}(Q^2_1,Q^2_2)$ shows an agreement with the very recent experimental data obtained from the BaBar collaboration for the ranges of $2< Q^2_1, Q^2_1 <60$ GeV$^2$.

# Doubly virtual $(\pi^0,\eta,\eta')\to\gamma^*\gamma^*$ transition form factors in the light-front quark model

## Key claim

The light-front quark model (LFQM) reproduces the doubly virtual transition form factors for $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$ mesons and confirms the perturbative QCD (pQCD) asymptotic behavior $F_{{\rm P}\gamma^*}(Q^2_1, Q^2_2)\sim 1/(Q^2_1 + Q^2_2)$ rather than the vector meson dominance (VMD) scaling $1/(Q^2_1 Q^2_2)$, with the LFQM prediction for $F_{\eta'\gamma^*}$ showing reasonable agreement with BaBar data within the data's large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors first performed a light-front (LF) calculation in an exactly solvable manifestly covariant Bethe-Salpeter (BS) model using the $q^+_1=0$ frame, verifying that both LF and covariant calculations yield identical results and confirming the absence of LF zero-mode contributions. They then mapped the covariant BS model to the standard LFQM using a Gaussian wave function obtained from previous LFQM analyses of meson mass spectra, applying the $\eta-\eta'$ mixing scheme in the quark-flavor basis with a mixing angle $\phi=37^\circ\pm5^\circ$ to compute the transition form factors for all three pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: $m_{u(d)} = 0.22$ GeV, $m_s = 0.45$ GeV; Gaussian parameters: $\beta_{Q\bar{Q}} = 0.3659$ GeV, $\beta_{s\bar{s}} = 0.4128$ GeV (from Table I).
- At $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2) = (6.48, 6.48)$ GeV$^2$, LFQM gives $F_{\eta'\gamma^*} = 13.91^{+0.69}_{-0.79}\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ compared to experimental $14.32^{+1.95}_{-1.89}\pm 0.83\pm 0.14\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- At $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2) = (16.85, 16.85)$ GeV$^2$, LFQM gives $F_{\eta'\gamma^*} = 5.55^{+0.27}_{-0.31}\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ compared to experimental $5.35^{+1.71}_{-2.15}\pm 0.31\pm 0.42\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- At $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2) = (14.83, 4.27)$ GeV$^2$, LFQM gives $F_{\eta'\gamma^*} = 10.32^{+0.51}_{-0.59}\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ compared to experimental $8.24^{+1.16}_{-1.13}\pm 0.48\pm 0.65\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- At $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2) = (38.11, 14.95)$ GeV$^2$, LFQM gives $F_{\eta'\gamma^*} = 3.71^{+0.18}_{-0.21}\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ compared to experimental $6.07^{+1.09}_{-1.07}\pm 0.35\pm 1.21\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- At $(Q^2_1, Q^2_2) = (45.63, 45.63)$ GeV$^2$, LFQM gives $F_{\eta'\gamma^*} = 2.08^{+0.10}_{-0.11}\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ compared to experimental $8.71^{+3.96}_{-4.02}\pm 0.50\pm 1.04\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The NLO pQCD contribution to $F_{\pi\gamma^*}(Q^2,Q^2)$ is greater than 10% for $Q^2 \leq 2$ GeV$^2$ and becomes less than 5% for $Q^2 \geq 6$ GeV$^2$.
- The experimental data from BaBar cover the range $2 < Q^2_1, Q^2_2 < 60$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of single-virtual transition form factors $F_{{\rm P}\gamma}(Q^2,0)$ for $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$ (Choi, Ji, and collaborators, 2017) to the doubly virtual case, which is required for hadronic light-by-light scattering contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment $(g-2)_\mu$. The results contradict the VMD model predictions (which scale as $1/(Q^2_1 Q^2_2)$) and support pQCD scaling (Braaten, 1983), while also providing the first LFQM comparison to the recent BaBar double-tag measurements of $F_{\eta'\gamma^*}$ (BaBar Collaboration, 2018). The low-energy behavior was previously investigated within a Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter framework (Eichmann, 2017).
