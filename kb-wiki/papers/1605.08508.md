# Spectroscopic factor strengths using $ab ~ initio$ approaches

> We have calculated the spectroscopic factor strengths for the one-proton and one-neutron pick-up reactions $^{27}$Al($d$,$^{3}$He)$^{26}$Mg and $^{27}$Al($d$,$t$)$^{26}$Al within the framework of the shell model. We employed two different $ab ~ initio$ approaches : an in-medium similarity renormalization targeted for a particular nucleus, and the coupled- cluster effective interaction. We also compared our results with recently determined experimental spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim

The paper calculates spectroscopic factor strengths for one-proton and one-neutron pick-up reactions on $^{27}$Al using two ab initio shell-model approaches (IM-SRG and CCEI) and compares them to experimental values, finding that the phenomenological USDB interaction reproduces experimental energy levels better than the ab initio methods, while the summed spectroscopic factors from IM-SRG and CCEI show similar trends to the experimental values.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed using the NuShellX code with three different effective interactions: an in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) approach based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions with nucleus-dependent valence spaces, an ab initio coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) using N3LO chiral NN and N2LO local chiral NNN interactions, and the phenomenological USDB interaction. Spectroscopic amplitudes were computed as reduced matrix elements of the creation operator between initial and final nuclear states, and the resulting CÂ²S values were compared to experimental spectroscopic factors extracted from zero-range DWBA analysis of the $^{27}$Al(d,$^{3}$He)$^{26}$Mg and $^{27}$Al(d,t)$^{26}$Al reactions at 25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of ab initio shell-model methods (IM-SRG from Stroberg (2015) and CCEI from previous coupled-cluster work) to spectroscopic factor calculations for $^{27}$Al pick-up reactions, complementing experimental studies by Vishal (2015) for $^{26}$Mg and Vishal (2015) for $^{26}$Al. The results enable comparison of ab initio predictions with the well-established phenomenological USDB interaction (Brown and Richter, 2006) and with the broader survey of neutron spectroscopic factors for Z=8-28 nuclei by Tsang (2012). The paper also addresses the ongoing debate about the observability of spectroscopic factors, referencing the work of Furnstahl and Hameer (2014) on effective field theory and the importance of long-range correlations discussed by Barbieri (2015).
