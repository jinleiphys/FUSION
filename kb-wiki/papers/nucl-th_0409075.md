# Collective dynamics of the fireball

> I analyse the identified single-particle p_t spectra and two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations from RHIC. They indicate a massive transverse expansion and rather short lifetime of the system. The quantitative analysis in framework of the blast-wave model yields, however, unphysical results and suggests that the model may not be applicable in description of two-particle correlations. I then discuss generalisations of the blast-wave model to non-central collisions and the question how spatial asymmetry can be disentangled from flow asymmetry in measurements of v_2 and azimuthally sensitive HBT radii.

## Key claim

The blast-wave model, when fitted to identified single-particle spectra and two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations from RHIC, yields unphysical freeze-out temperatures (as low as 28-33 MeV) for the HBT radii, suggesting the model may not be applicable for describing two-particle correlations, even though the spectra alone can be fitted with conventional temperatures around 115-140 MeV.

## Method

The author fits the blast-wave model to identified single-particle p_t spectra of pions, kaons, and protons, and to two-pion HBT radii (R_o, R_s, R_l) from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 and 200 AGeV at RHIC. The model assumes a boost-invariant longitudinally expanding fireball with a linear transverse rapidity profile, uniform transverse density, and a Gaussian smearing in freeze-out time. For non-central collisions, the model is generalized with an ellipsoidal transverse profile and azimuthally dependent transverse flow, and the author calculates v_2 and azimuthally sensitive HBT radii to disentangle spatial anisotropy from flow anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV: spectra fits overlap at 95% confidence level at T ≈ 120-140 MeV and ⟨v_t⟩ ≈ 0.45-0.5; best fit to HBT radii gives T=33 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩=0.73, R=24.1 fm, τ0=21.3 fm/c, Δτ=1.1 fm/c.
- Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV: spectra fits overlap at 1σ level at T ≈ 115 MeV and ⟨v_t⟩ ≈ 0.57; best fit to HBT radii gives T=28 MeV, ⟨v_t⟩=0.71, R=27.7 fm, τ0=24.9 fm/c, Δτ=1.7 fm/c.
- For non-central collisions at 200 AGeV (centrality 20-30%), Model 1 fit to v_2 and azimuthal HBT radii uses T=120 MeV, ρ0=0.99, ρ2=0.035, a=0.94646, R=9.4 fm, τ0=5.0 fm/c, Δτ=2.9 fm/c.
- The transverse size expands from an original rms radius of about 3.5 fm (Au nucleus) to about 12 fm in conventional models; total lifetime estimated as τ0+Δτ ≈ 9 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model analysis of RHIC data, following earlier hydrodynamic simulations by Kolb (2003) and the single-freeze-out model (SFO). The failure of the blast-wave model to simultaneously fit spectra and HBT radii echoes similar problems observed at SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV). The analysis of non-central collisions builds on prior work by Retiere and Lisa (2004) and enables future disentanglement of spatial anisotropy from flow asymmetry using combined v_2 and azimuthally sensitive HBT measurements from STAR and PHENIX.
