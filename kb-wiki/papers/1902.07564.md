# Benchmarking a Non-Equilibrium Approach to Photon Emission in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In this work, the production of photons through binary scattering processes is investigated for equilibrated hadronic systems. More precisely, a non-equilibrium hadronic transport approach to describe relativistic heavy-ion collisions is benchmarked with respect to photon emission. Cross sections for photon production in $π+ ρ\to π+ γ$ and $π+ π\to ρ+ γ$ scattering processes are derived from an effective chiral field theory and implemented into the hadronic transport approach, SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons). The implementation is verified by systematically comparing the thermal photon rate to theoretical expectations. Further, the impact of form factors is discussed, scattering processes mediated by $ω$ mesons are found to contribute significantly to the total photon production. Several comparisons of the yielded photon rates are performed: to parametrizations of the very same rates, as used in hydrodynamic simulations, to previous works relying on different cross sections for the production of direct photons from the hadronic stage, and to partonic rates. Finally, the impact of considering the finite width of the $ρ$ meson is investigated, where a significant enhancement of photon production in the low-energy region is observed. This benchmark is the first step towards a consistent treatment of photon emission in hybrid hydrodynamics+transport approaches and a genuine dynamical description.

## Key claim

The paper benchmarks a non-equilibrium hadronic transport approach (SMASH) for photon emission against theoretical thermal photon rates, finding excellent agreement within uncertainties, but only under the assumption of zero-width rho mesons and using Boltzmann equilibrium statistics; the finite width of the rho meson is later shown to significantly enhance low-energy photon production.

## Method

Cross sections for photon production in pi + rho -> pi + gamma and pi + pi -> rho + gamma scattering processes were derived from an effective chiral field theory (massive Yang-Mills approach) and implemented into the SMASH hadronic transport code. The implementation was validated by comparing thermal photon rates from infinite-matter SMASH simulations (cubic box of 10 fm length, 100 fractional photons, 200 fm runtime, 100,000 events) to numerically integrated theoretical expectations using Boltzmann statistics, at temperatures of 100, 150, and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon rates from SMASH at T = 150 MeV show "excellent agreement" with theoretical expectations within uncertainties for both (pi, rho, a1)-mediated and omega-mediated processes (Figs. 4 and 5).
- Photon rate increases by approximately three orders of magnitude between T = 100 MeV and T = 200 MeV, independent of scattering process (Fig. 8).
- For omega-mediated processes, the coupling constant at the pi-rho-omega vertex is adjusted from 11.93 GeV^-1 to 22.6 GeV^-1 when form factors are applied, based on the experimental decay width of omega -> pi + gamma.
- The hadronic dipole form factor uses a cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV (Eq. 10).
- The constant elastic cross section used for collision finding in test cases is sigma = 30 mb.

## Context

This work extends the photon production framework of Turbide (2003) by implementing its cross sections into the SMASH transport code, enabling a consistent dynamical description of photon emission in hybrid hydrodynamics+transport approaches. It contrasts with earlier transport implementations in UrQMD and PHSD that relied on cross sections from Kapusta (1992), which cannot describe omega-mediated processes. The benchmark enables future coupling of SMASH as an afterburner to hydrodynamic simulations like MUSIC, and addresses the long-standing discrepancy between measured photon yields and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC.
