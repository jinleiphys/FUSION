# Complete inclusion of parity-dependent level densities in the statistical description of astrophysical reaction rates

> Microscopic calculations show a strong parity dependence of the nuclear level density at low excitation energy of a nucleus. Previously, this dependence has either been neglected or only implemented in the initial and final channels of Hauser-Feshbach calculations. We present an indirect way to account for a full parity dependence in all steps of a reaction, including the one of the compound nucleus formed in a reaction. To illustrate the impact on astrophysical reaction rates, we present rates for neutron captures in isotopic chains of Ni and Sn. Comparing with the standard assumption of equipartition of both parities, we find noticeable differences in the energy regime of astrophysical interest caused by the parity dependence of the nuclear level density found in the compound nucleus even at sizeable excitation energies.

## Key claim

The authors present an indirect method to incorporate parity-dependent nuclear level densities into all stages of Hauser-Feshbach calculations, including the compound nucleus formation step, which had not been done before. They find that this full parity treatment can cause noticeable differences in astrophysical reaction rates compared to the standard assumption of parity equipartition, but the effect is sensitive to the neutron separation energy and the competition between E1 and M1 transitions.

## Method

The authors modify the standard Hauser-Feshbach cross section formula by introducing a weighting factor β(E,J,π) = 2·ρ(E,J,π)/[ρ(E,J,+) + ρ(E,J,-)] that multiplies the parity-independent transmission coefficients. They apply this modification to all three stages of the reaction: the target nucleus, the compound nucleus formed by neutron capture, and the residual nucleus after γ decay. Neutron capture rates are calculated for isotopic chains of Ni and Sn using parity-dependent level densities from Hilaire and Goriely (2006) and separately using the parity distribution from Mocelj et al. (2007) combined with backshifted Fermi gas level densities from Rauscher and Thielemann (1997).

## Key numbers

- For the ⁵⁸Ni(n,γ)⁵⁹Ni reaction, including parity dependence in the compound nucleus reduces the stellar reaction rate by about 30% compared to calculations without parity dependence
- The neutron separation energy in ⁵⁹Ni is 9 MeV
- For the most neutron-rich Ni isotopes, neutron thresholds are as low as 0.52 MeV (⁷⁸Ni) and 0.17 MeV (⁸⁰Ni)
- The neutron threshold in ⁸⁰Ni is 3.7 MeV due to pairing
- For tin isotopes ¹²⁰-¹³⁰Sn, the parity-dependent rates change only mildly because the h₁₁/₂ intruder state leads to fast parity equilibration at energies around the neutron thresholds
- All rate comparisons are shown for a stellar plasma temperature of T = 1 GK

## Context

This work extends previous statistical model calculations by Hilaire (2006) and Mocelj et al. (2007) that only included parity-dependent level densities in the initial and final channels, but not in the compound nucleus formation step. The method enables improved predictions of neutron capture rates for nuclei far from stability, particularly those relevant to the r-process path, though the authors note that the low level density for very neutron-rich isotopes may make the statistical model questionable and that direct neutron capture contributions should also be considered.
