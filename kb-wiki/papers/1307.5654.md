# Dispersive analysis for $η\to γγ^*$

> A dispersion integral is derived that connects data on $η\to π^+π^-γ$ to the $η\to γγ^*$ transition form factor. A detailed analysis of the uncertainties is provided. We find for the slope of the $η$ transition form factor at the origin $b_η= (2.05 {}^{+0.22}_{-0.10})\, {\rm GeV}^{-2}$. Using an additional, plausible assumption, one finds for the corresponding slope of the $η'$ transition form factor, $b_{η'} = (1.58 {}^{+0.15}_{-0.08})\, {\rm GeV}^{-2}$. Both values are consistent with all recent data, but differ from some previous theoretical analyses.

## Key claim

The authors derive a dispersion integral that connects data on the radiative decays $\eta\to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ to the $\eta\to \gamma\gamma^*$ transition form factor in a model-independent way for the isovector part, while the isoscalar contribution requires a vector-meson-dominance assumption; the resulting slope parameters are consistent with recent data but differ from some previous theoretical analyses.

## Method

The authors use dispersion theory to relate the isovector part of the $\eta\to \gamma\gamma^*$ transition amplitude to the $\eta\to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ decay amplitude, which is parametrized as a product of the pion vector form factor $F_V(Q^2)$ and a linear polynomial $P(Q^2)=1+\alpha Q^2$. A once-subtracted dispersion integral is constructed for the isovector contribution, with input from the slope parameter $\alpha$ extracted from $\eta\to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ data and the pion vector form factor from $e^+e^-$ and $\tau$ decays. The isoscalar contribution is modeled by narrow $\omega$ and $\phi$ meson resonances, with weight factors determined from branching ratios of $V\to\eta\gamma$ and $V\to e^+e^-$ decays.

## Key numbers

- Slope of the $\eta$ transition form factor at the origin: $b_\eta = (2.05^{+0.22}_{-0.10})\,\text{GeV}^{-2}$
- Slope of the $\eta'$ transition form factor at the origin: $b_{\eta'} = (1.53^{+0.15}_{-0.08})\,\text{GeV}^{-2}$ (using an additional plausible assumption)
- Slope parameter for $\eta\to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma$: $\alpha = (1.32\pm 0.13)\,\text{GeV}^{-2}$ from KLOE (2013) data
- Isoscalar contribution to the $\eta$ slope: $b_\eta^{(I=0)} \approx -0.036\,\text{GeV}^{-2}$ for standard mixing angle $\theta_P = \arcsin(-1/3) \approx -19.5^\circ$
- Isoscalar contribution to the $\eta'$ slope: $b_{\eta'}^{(I=0)} = (0.34\pm 0.05)\,\text{GeV}^{-2}$ (conservative estimate)
- Weight factor for $\eta\omega\gamma$ from data: $w_{\eta\omega\gamma} \approx (0.78\pm 0.04)\times 1/8$
- Weight factor for $\eta\phi\gamma$ from data: $w_{\eta\phi\gamma} \approx (0.75\pm 0.03)\times (-2/8)$

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of Stollenwerk et al. (2012) that connected $\eta\to \pi\pi\gamma$ decays to the pion vector form factor, now using those results to predict the $\eta\to \gamma\gamma^*$ transition form factor. The analysis provides a nearly model-independent determination of the isovector contribution, while the isoscalar part relies on vector-meson dominance assumptions similar to those in Landsberg (1985). The results are relevant for reducing theoretical uncertainties in hadronic light-by-light contributions to the muon $(g-2)$, as discussed in recent reviews (Krakauer et al., Trento conference). The extracted slope values differ from some previous theoretical analyses but are consistent with all recent data, and the method can be applied to $\eta'$ decays once better data become available from BES-III and CLAS.
