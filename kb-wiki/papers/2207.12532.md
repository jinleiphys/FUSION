# Unveiling the dynamics of nucleosynthesis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Like nucleosynthesis during the early universe, light nuclei are also produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Although the deuteron ($d$) yields in these collisions can be well described by the statistical hadronization model (SHM), which assumes that particle yields are fixed at a common chemical freezeout near the phase boundary between the quark-gluon plasma and the hadron gas, the recently measured triton ($^3\text{H}$) yields in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7-200$ GeV are overestimated systematically by this model. Here, we develop a comprehensive kinetic approach to study the effects of hadronic re-scatterings, such as $πNN\leftrightarrowπd$ and $πNNN\leftrightarrowπ^3\text{H}~(^3\text{He})$, on $d$, $^3\text{H}$, and $^3\text{He}$ production in these collisions. We find that these reactions have little effects on the deuteron yield but reduce the $^3\text{H}$ and $^3\text{He}$ yields by about a factor of 1.8 from their initial values given by the SHM. This finding helps resolve the overestimation of triton production in the SHM and provides the evidence for hadronic re-scattering effects on nucleosynthesis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a kinetic approach that includes hadronic re-scatterings (πNN↔πd and πNNN↔π³H/³He) and find that these reactions reduce triton and helium-3 yields by about a factor of 1.8 from their initial statistical hadronization model (SHM) values, resolving the systematic overestimation of triton production by the SHM, but only for most collision energies, with the single data point at √sNN=2.76 TeV remaining an exception.

## Method

The authors extend relativistic kinetic equations derived from real-time Green's functions to include nonlocal collision integrals for light-nuclei production and dissociation, using the impulse approximation to relate π-nucleus reactions to π-nucleon scattering. They incorporate finite nuclear sizes via Gaussian Wigner functions (σd=3.2 fm for deuteron, reproducing the empirical rms radius of 1.96 fm) and solve the kinetic equations using a stochastic test-particle method. The hadronic matter evolution is modeled with viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) with NEOS-BQS equation of state, initial conditions from a collision-geometric-based 3D model, and hadronization via the Cooper-Frye formula on a constant energy density hypersurface, with most hadronic scattering channels from the ART model included alongside the light-nuclei reactions.

## Key numbers

- Triton and ³He yields are reduced by a factor of 1.8 from their initial SHM values during hadronic matter expansion in central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN=20–5020 GeV.
- The final deuteron yield is about 95% of its initial SHM value in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV.
- The deuteron dissociation cross section renormalization factor is Fd≈0.72; for ³He it is F³He≈0.51.
- The deuteron Wigner function uses σd=3.2 fm to reproduce the empirical deuteron rms radius of rd=1.96 fm.
- The equilibrated triton number relates to proton and deuteron numbers by N³H≈α Nd²/Np with α≈0.29 for a uniform source and 4/9 for a large Gaussian source.

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic studies of deuteron production (DanNPA533, OhPRC76, OhPRC80, OliPRC99) by including for the first time the four-body reactions πNNN↔π³H and πNNN↔π³He, which were previously neglected due to the difficulty of treating many-body scatterings. It contradicts the findings of Neidig (2021) using rate equations in an isentropic expansion model, which found triton and helium-3 yields remain almost unchanged during hadronic evolution. The results justify the nucleon coalescence model assumption that light nuclei yields are determined at the late stage of hadronic evolution, and enable future extensions to (anti-)helium-4, (anti-)hypertriton, and exotic QCD molecular states like X(3872).
