# QCD condensates and hadron parameters in nuclear matter: self-consistent treatment, sum rules and all that

> We review various approaches to the calculation of QCD condensates and of the nucleon characteristics in nuclear matter. We show the importance of their self-consistent treatment. The first steps in such treatment appeared to be very instructive. It is shown that the alleged pion condensation anyway can not take place earlier than the restoration of the chiral symmetry. We demonstrate how the finite density QCD sum rules for nucleons work and advocate their possible role in providing an additional bridge between the condensate and hadron physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a self-consistent treatment of QCD condensates and hadron parameters in nuclear matter is essential, and that the first steps in such a treatment show that pion condensation cannot occur before chiral symmetry restoration, meaning that Goldstone pions never condense.

## Method

The authors review multiple approaches to calculating QCD condensates and nucleon parameters in nuclear matter, including the gas approximation, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, quark-meson models, Skyrmion models, Brown-Rho scaling, and QCD sum rules. They then present a self-consistent analysis of the scalar quark condensate beyond the gas approximation, using a meson-exchange model of nucleon-nucleon interactions and incorporating the contribution of the pion cloud, which is enhanced by a factor of about 12 due to the pion's scalar condensate expectation value.

## Key numbers

*   The vacuum scalar quark condensate is \(\langle0|\bar uu|0\rangle = (-240\ \text{MeV})^3\), obtained from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.
*   The vacuum gluon condensate is \(\langle0|\frac{\alpha_s}\pi G^a_{\mu\nu}G^{\mu\nu}_a|0\rangle \approx (0.33\ \text{GeV})^4\).
*   The pion-nucleon sigma-term is \(\sigma = (45\pm7)\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to \(\langle N|\bar qq|N\rangle \approx 8\).
*   The parameter \(m_0^2\) for the mixed quark-gluon condensate is \(m_0^2 \approx 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\) for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks.
*   The expectation value of the mixed quark-gluon condensate in the nucleon is \(\langle N|\bar\psi\frac{\alpha_s}\pi\frac{\lambda^a}2 G^{\mu\nu}_a\sigma_{\mu\nu}\psi|N\rangle \approx 0.6\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   At densities near saturation \(\rho_0\), the gluon condensate \(g(\rho)\) differs from its vacuum value by about 6\%.

## Context

This review extends the work of Drukarev and Levin (1990) on the scalar condensate in nuclear matter and the analysis of the gluon condensate by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979). It also discusses the sigma-term calculations by Gasser, Leutwyler, and others, and the QCD sum rules analysis of Belyaev and Ioffe (1982). The paper advocates for using finite-density QCD sum rules as a tool for future self-consistent investigations, aiming to bridge the gap between condensate physics and hadron parameters in dense matter.
