# Coalescence, Percolation and Nuclear Multifragmentation

> We show that the coalescence model for fragment formation leads to an approximate site percolation model. Features characteristic of a percolation model also appear in microscopic models of disassembly.

# Coalescence, Percolation and Nuclear Multifragmentation

## Key claim

The coalescence model for fragment formation, when phase-space cells are chosen with a momentum scale set by the Fermi momentum, leads to an approximate site percolation model in three dimensions, although the mapping relies on several approximations that are substantiated only by the mean-field calculation presented at the end of the paper.

## Method

The authors establish a formal mapping between a coalescence model (where nucleons in the same or adjoining phase-space cells form clusters) and a site percolation model, using the Pauli principle to restrict occupation of phase-space cells. They then perform Monte-Carlo simulations of site percolation on a lattice both with and without explicit spin-isospin degrees of freedom, and compare the resulting cluster distributions. Finally, they run a Vlasov (mean-field) propagation of a hot nuclear blob with 40 and 64 nucleons, using a Skyrme interaction and the test-particle method, and count clusters after 81 fm/c of evolution.

## Key numbers

- The momentum cell size is set by \((\Delta p)^3 = \frac{4\pi}{3} p_F^3\), where \(p_F\) is the Fermi momentum, independent of mass number \(A\).
- The mapping between a calculation with spin-isospin (N sites, occupation probability p) and one without (N' sites, probability p') is given by \(N'p' = 4Np\) and \(1-p' = (1-p)^4\).
- Monte-Carlo simulations use \(A = 64\) nucleons; each point in Figs. 1 and 2 is an average over 1000 runs.
- The Vlasov calculation uses a Gaussian width \(\alpha = 0.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), an average density \(\bar{\rho} = 0.18\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), a configuration space of \((40\ \text{fm})^3\) with cube size \(1\ \text{fm}^3\), and 50 test particles per nucleon.
- The Skyrme interaction parameters are \(A = -356\ \text{MeV}\), \(B = 303\ \text{MeV}\), with \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The system evolves for \(81\ \text{fm}/c\) before cluster counting.
- The energy per nucleon in a cold nucleus of 40 nucleons is approximately \(-11\ \text{MeV}\).
- Each point in Fig. 3 is an average of 40 runs at a given energy per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the percolation model introduced by Bauer (1988) and Campi (1988) for nuclear fragmentation by providing a physical derivation from the coalescence model, connecting percolation parameters to entropy as computed by Bertsch and Cugnon (1981). The authors also show that features characteristic of percolation (such as the maximum in the second moment \(M_2\) as a function of reduced multiplicity) appear in a microscopic Vlasov propagation model similar to that of Knoll and Strack (1984) and Gale and Das Gupta (1985). This enables a more direct link between experimental observables and percolation-based interpretations of the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclear systems.
