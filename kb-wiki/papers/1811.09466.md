# Universal scaling of the sigma field and net-protons from Langevin dynamics of model A

> In this paper, we investigate the Kibble-Zurek scaling of the sigma field and net-protons within the framework of Langevin dynamics of model A. After determining the characteristic scales $τ_{kz},l_{kz}$ and $θ_{kz}$ and properly rescaling the traditional cumulants, we construct universal functions for the sigma field and approximate universal functions for net-protons in the critical regime, which are insensitive to the relaxation time and the chosen evolving trajectory. Besides, the oscillating behavior for the higher order cumulants of net-protons near the critical point is also drastically suppressed, which converge into approximate universal curves with these constructed Kibble-Zurek functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, within Langevin dynamics of model A, universal functions for the sigma field and approximate universal functions for net-protons can be constructed via Kibble-Zurek scaling, which are insensitive to the relaxation time and the chosen evolving trajectory, but only for specifically chosen trajectories (type A with fixed chemical potential and type B with constant theta_KZ) and under the assumption of spatially uniform temperature and chemical potential.

## Method

The authors evolve the non-conserved order parameter sigma field in 3+1 dimensions using Langevin dynamics (model A) with a noise term satisfying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, coarse-grained over spatial extension to avoid grid-size dependence. The effective potential is constructed through a mapping between the hot QCD system and the three-dimensional Ising model, with the heat bath evolving along chosen trajectories (type A with fixed chemical potential, type B with approximately equal equilibrium correlation length) following a Hubble-like temperature drop. Cumulants of the sigma field and net-protons (via sigma-NN coupling with Boltzmann distribution) are computed event-by-event, then rescaled using characteristic Kibble-Zurek scales (tau_KZ, l_KZ, theta_KZ) determined from the crossing of relaxation time and quench time.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical critical exponent used: z = 3 (from model H)
- Hubble-like temperature evolution: T(tau)/T_I = (tau/tau_I)^{-0.45}
- Boundary of critical regime defined by equilibrium correlation length xi_min = 1 fm
- For type B trajectories, theta_KZ is tuned to a constant value of 0.1
- sigma-NN coupling constant: g = 3.3
- Physical mass of proton: m_0 = 938.27 MeV
- Critical temperature: T_c = 160 MeV; critical chemical potential: mu_c = 395 MeV (from Fodor (2004))
- Relaxation time parameter tau_rel/tau_c values used: 0.02, 0.06, 0.10, 0.14
- Type B trajectory r_c values used: 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9

## Context

This work extends the Kibble-Zurek mechanism analysis of Mukherjee (2016) from the Fokker-Planck equation (zero-mode only) to full Langevin dynamics of the sigma field in position space, enabling coupling to final hadrons for net-proton fluctuations. The results contradict the strong sensitivity of cumulants to relaxation time and trajectory seen in equilibrium treatments, and enable future extension to model B (conserved charges) and hydro+ frameworks for more realistic comparison with Beam Energy Scan data from RHIC.
