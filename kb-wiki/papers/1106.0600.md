# Deriving the existence of $B\bar{B}^*$ bound states from the X(3872) and Heavy Quark Symmetry

> We discuss the possibility and the description of bound states between $B$ and $\bar{B}^*$ mesons. We argue that the existence of such a bound state can be deduced from (i) the weakly bound X(3872) state, (ii) certain assumptions about the short range dynamics of the $D\bar{D}^*$ system and (iii) heavy quark symmetry. From these assumptions the binding energy of the possible $B\bar{B}^*$ bound states is determined, first in a theory containing only contact interactions which serves as a straightforward illustration of the method, and then the effects of including the one pion exchange potential are discussed. In this latter case three isoscalar states are predicted: a positive and negative C-parity $^3S_1-{}^3D_1$ state with a binding energy of $20\,{\rm MeV}$ and $6\,{\rm MeV}$ below threshold respectively, and a positive C-parity $^3P_0$ shallow state located almost at the $B\bar{B}^*$ threshold. However, large uncertainties are generated as a consequence of the $1/m_Q$ corrections from heavy quark symmetry. Finally, the newly discovered isovector $Z_b(10610)$ state can be easily accommodated within the present framework by a minor modification of the short range dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the existence of bound states between \(B\) and \(\bar{B}^*\) mesons can be deduced from the weakly bound X(3872) state, assumptions about the short-range dynamics of the \(D\bar{D}^*\) system, and heavy quark symmetry, but note that large uncertainties arise from \(1/m_Q\) corrections.

## Method

The authors employ a leading-order effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions (pionless) and then include one-pion exchange (pionfull theory). They determine the counterterm \(C_0\) by saturation from the weak decay constant \(f_D\) and fix the cutoff by reproducing the X(3872) binding energy in the charm sector, then extrapolate to the bottom sector using heavy quark symmetry to predict \(B\bar{B}^*\) bound states.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) mass is \(3871.56 \pm 0.22\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with the \(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\) threshold at \(3871.79 \pm 0.21\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The charged \(D^+ D^{*-}\) channel lies \(8.06\ \mathrm{MeV}\) above the neutral channel.
- In the pionfull theory, three isoscalar \(B\bar{B}^*\) states are predicted: a positive C-parity \(^3S_1-{}^3D_1\) state with binding energy \(20\ \mathrm{MeV}\), a negative C-parity \(^3S_1-{}^3D_1\) state with binding energy \(6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and a positive C-parity \(^3P_0\) state located almost at the \(B\bar{B}^*\) threshold.
- The maximum binding energy for which the contact EFT is reliable in the bottom sector is \(B_{\mathrm{max}} \sim m_\pi^2 / (2\mu_{B\bar{B}^*}) \sim 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the molecular description of the X(3872) state, previously studied in short-range interaction pictures (e.g., Braaten 2004, Gamermann 2009) and potential pictures (e.g., Tornqvist 1993, Liu 2008), to predict bound states in the bottom sector. It builds on the argument of Ericson and Karl (1993) that heavier reduced mass favors binding, and uses heavy quark symmetry to correlate charm and bottom sectors. The predictions enable experimental searches for \(B\bar{B}^*\) bound states and accommodate the newly discovered \(Z_b(10610)\) state with minor modifications.
