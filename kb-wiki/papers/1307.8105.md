# Neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic and 2p2h interactions up to 10 GeV

> We extend to 10 GeV results from a microscopic calculation of charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions that do not produce a pion in the final state. For the class of events coming from neutrino interactions with two nucleons producing two holes (2p2h), limiting the calculation to three-momentum transfers less than 1.2 GeV produces a two dimensional distribution in momentum and energy transfer that is roughly constant as a function of energy. The cross section for 2p2h interactions scales with the number of nucleons for isoscalar nuclei, similar to the quasi-elastic (QE) cross section. When limited to momentum transfers below 1.2 GeV, the cross section is 26% of the QE cross section at 3 GeV, but 14% if we neglect a Delta1232 resonance absorption component. The same quantities are 33% and 17% for anti-neutrinos. For the quasi-elastic interactions, the full nuclear model with long range correlations produces an even larger, but approximately constant distortion of the shape of the four-momentum transfer at all energies above 2 GeV. The 2p2h enhancement and long-range correlation distortions to the cross section for these interactions is significant enough they should be observable in precision experiments to measure neutrino oscillations and neutrino interactions at these energies, but also balance out and produce less total distortion than each effect does individually.

## Key claim

The paper extends a microscopic calculation of charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions without pion production up to 10 GeV, but the 2p2h (two-particle two-hole) computation is restricted to three-momentum transfers below 1.2 GeV because the model does not include 2p2h production via resonances beyond the Delta1232 or related interference terms, and is not adequately accurate for high three-momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors used a local Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, removal energy, and Coulomb distortion for the quasi-elastic process, incorporating long and short range nucleon-nucleon correlations via the random phase approximation (RPA) with both nucleon-hole and Delta1232-hole components. The 2p2h component was constructed from a many-body expansion of modes where the exchanged W boson is absorbed by two nucleons, including processes with Delta kinematics where DeltaN to NN absorption occurs, and the calculation was limited to three-momentum transfers below 1.2 GeV. The free nucleon form factors used a dipole axial form factor with axial mass M_A = 1.0 GeV and vector form factors from Galster (1971).

## Key numbers

- The 2p2h cross section is 26% of the quasi-elastic cross section at 3 GeV for neutrinos when limited to momentum transfers below 1.2 GeV, and 14% if the Delta1232 resonance absorption component is neglected
- For antineutrinos at 3 GeV, the 2p2h cross section is 33% of the quasi-elastic cross section with the Delta component, and 17% without it
- At 10 GeV, the 2p2h cross section rises to 32% of the QE cross section for neutrinos and 17% without the Delta component; for antineutrinos the values are 32% and 17% respectively
- The 2p2h cross section without the Delta absorption component varies linearly with the size of an isoscalar nucleus; for oxygen-16 relative to carbon-12 the ratio is 1.5, and for calcium-40 relative to carbon-12 it is 4.0
- The 2p2h calculation yields 67% of the cross section coming from pn pairs in the nucleus for 3 GeV neutrinos, integrated to three-momentum transfer of 1.2 GeV
- The RPA suppression factor reaches 0.6 at Q^2 = 0, and the maximum enhancement is 35% for neutrinos and 25% for antineutrinos near Q^2 = 1.1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculations of Nieves et al. (2011, 2012, 2013) from energies below 1.5 GeV up to 10 GeV, where previous results compared well with MiniBooNE data and independent calculations by Martini et al. (2009, 2010). The paper provides the first detailed calculation of 2p2h interactions at these higher energies, which are relevant for experiments like MINOS, MINERvA, NOvA, and LBNE. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for precision oscillation measurements, showing that the 2p2h enhancement and long-range correlation distortions are significant enough to be observable but partially cancel each other, producing less total distortion than each effect individually.
