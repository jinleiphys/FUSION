# The pion-nucleon interaction as an effective field theory

> After a brief survey of effective field theories, the linear sigma model is discussed as a prototype of an effective field theory of the standard model below the chiral-symmetry-breaking scale. Although it can serve as a toy model for the pion-nucleon interaction, the linear sigma model is not a realistic alternative to chiral perturbation theory. The heavy-baryon approach to chiral perturbation theory allows for a systematic low-energy expansion of Green functions and amplitudes with baryons. The chiral-invariant renormalization of the effective field theory for the pion-nucleon system to O(p^3) in the chiral expansion is reviewed.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model, while useful as a toy model for the pion-nucleon interaction, is not a realistic effective field theory of QCD at low energies because it imposes specific relations between couplings (such as \(g_A=1\)) that disagree with experiment; chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) with the heavy-baryon approach provides the correct systematic low-energy expansion.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of effective field theories for the pion-nucleon system, starting from the linear sigma model and then moving to chiral perturbation theory. It describes the heavy-baryon approach, where velocity-dependent fields are introduced to move the nucleon mass from propagators to vertices, enabling a simultaneous expansion in powers of momentum over \(4\pi F_\pi\) and momentum over the nucleon mass. The chiral-invariant renormalization of the one-loop functional at \(O(p^3)\) is performed using the heat kernel method, with divergences cancelled by counterterms in the effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant at lowest order: \(F_\pi = 93.2\ \text{MeV}\) (with quark-mass corrections of order \(O(m_{\text{quark}})\)).
- The axial-vector coupling constant in the linear sigma model is \(g_A = 1\), whereas the physical value is \(g_A = 1.26\).
- The scalar resonance \(f_0(1000)\) has a width \(\Gamma \simeq 700\ \text{MeV}\).
- Two parameter sets from a fit to \(\pi N\) phase shifts: for pseudoscalar mixing parameter \(x = 0.2\), the scalar coupling \(G_\sigma = 43\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and vector coupling \(G^V_\rho = 30\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\); for \(x = 0\), \(G_\sigma = 23\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(G^V_\rho = 60\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The energy scale of the standard model is larger than \(O(100\ \text{GeV})\).

## Context

This paper extends the effective field theory approach to the pion-nucleon system, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) on chiral perturbation theory. It contrasts the linear sigma model (Gell-Mann and Levy, 1960) with the heavy-baryon CHPT method developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991), and it reviews the renormalization to \(O(p^3)\) performed by Ecker (1994). The work enables systematic calculations of single-nucleon Green functions and amplitudes (form factors, \(\pi N \to \pi \ldots \pi N\), etc.) and sets the stage for future extensions to \(O(p^4)\), inclusion of higher baryon states, and chiral \(SU(3)\).
