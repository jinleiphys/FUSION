## Method

The authors performed a non-perturbative numerical computation using a lattice discretization of a classical effective field theory for SU(2) gauge theory, solving the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1 dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field with boost invariance assumed. ## Context

This work extends the classical effective field theory approach developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the application to nuclear collisions by Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995), providing the first non-perturbative numerical computation of the initial gluon energy density. The results are consistent with an estimate by Mueller (1999) for the number of produced gluons per unit area per unit rapidity, and the authors compare their energy estimates to mini-jet calculations by Kajantie (1999), finding remarkable closeness for RHIC values. This work enables more precise determination of initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors plan to improve estimates by performing the analysis for SU(3) and computing energy and number distributions to match mini-jet calculations at large transverse momenta.
