# The initial energy density of gluons produced in very high energy nuclear collisions

> In very high energy nuclear collisions, the initial energy of produced gluons per unit area per unit rapidity, $dE/L^2/dη$, is equal to $f(g^2μL) (g^2μ)^3/g^2$, where $μ^2$ is proportional to the gluon density per unit area of the colliding nuclei. For an SU(2) gauge theory, we perform a non--perturbative numerical computation of the function $f(g^2μL)$. It decreases rapidly for small $g^2μL$ but varies only by $\sim 25$%, from $0.208\pm 0.004$ to $0.257\pm 0.005$, for a wide range 35.36--296.98 in $g^2μL$, including the range relevant for collisions at RHIC and LHC. Extrapolating to SU(3), we estimate the initial energy per unit rapidity for Au-Au collisions in the central region at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The initial energy per unit area per unit rapidity of gluons produced in very high energy nuclear collisions can be expressed as a function f(g^2μL) times (g^2μ)^3/g^2, where this function varies only by about 25% (from 0.208 ± 0.004 to 0.257 ± 0.005) over a wide range of g^2μL values from 35.36 to 296.98 that includes the range relevant for RHIC and LHC collisions, but the authors caution that for the largest g^2μL values there may be a potentially large systematic error in the extrapolated continuum value.

## Method

The authors performed a non-perturbative numerical computation using a lattice discretization of a classical effective field theory for SU(2) gauge theory, solving the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1 dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field with boost invariance assumed. They computed the Hamiltonian density on the lattice for each color charge configuration, took Gaussian averages over 40 to 160 independent ρ trajectories depending on lattice size, and extrapolated the asymptotic values of ετ/(g^2μ)^3 to the continuum limit using linear fits. The function f(g^2μL) was then extracted from these continuum-extrapolated values.

## Key numbers

- The function f(g^2μL) takes the value 0.208 ± 0.004 at g^2μL = 35.36 and 0.257 ± 0.005 at g^2μL = 296.98
- For g^2μL = 8.84, f = 0.427 ± 0.004; for g^2μL = 5.66, f = 0.436 ± 0.007
- The relaxation rate γ (inverse formation time in units of g^2μ) ranges from 0.101 ± 0.024 at g^2μL = 8.84 to 0.378 ± 0.053 at g^2μL = 296.98
- Extrapolating to SU(3), the initial energy density at RHIC is estimated as ε ≈ 66.49 GeV/fm^3 and at LHC as ε ≈ 1315.56 GeV/fm^3
- The initial energy per unit rapidity E_T is estimated as 2703 GeV for RHIC and 24572 GeV for LHC (without K-factor), or 5406 GeV and 49144 GeV respectively with a K-factor of about 2

## Context

This work extends the classical effective field theory approach developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the application to nuclear collisions by Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995), providing the first non-perturbative numerical computation of the initial gluon energy density. The results are consistent with an estimate by Mueller (1999) for the number of produced gluons per unit area per unit rapidity, and the authors compare their energy estimates to mini-jet calculations by Kajantie (1999), finding remarkable closeness for RHIC values. This work enables more precise determination of initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors plan to improve estimates by performing the analysis for SU(3) and computing energy and number distributions to match mini-jet calculations at large transverse momenta.
