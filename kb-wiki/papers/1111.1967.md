# The formation and decay of superheavy nuclei produced in $^{48}Ca$-induced reactions

> The formation of superheavy nuclei in $^{48}Ca+^{232}Th$, $^{238}U$, $^{242,244}Pu$ and $^{248}Cm$ reactions and their subsequent decay are studied within the quantum mechanical fragmentation theory (QMFT) and the QMFT based preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) of Gupta and collaborators. According to QMFT, all these $^{48}Ca$-induced reactions are cold fusion reactions with relative excitation energies larger than for the $Pb$-induced cold fusion reactions and smaller than for the lighter beam i.e. $Mg$, $Si$ or $S$-induced hot fusion reactions. The same reactions were first suggested by Gupta et al. in 1977 on the basis of QMFT, and this study re-establishes the same result. In fact, for such heavy isotopes of Z=110 to 116, $^{50}Ca$ is shown to be a better beam for cold fusion, but $^{50}Ca$ is a radioactive nucleus. The $α$-decay half-lives of these nuclei after 3n and/ or 4n evaporations, i.e. of the evaporation residues of these compound systems, calculated on PCM compare reasonably well with experiments published by Dubna group and another recent calculation. As expected for such rare decays, PCM calculations show that the $α$-preformation factors are small, $\sim 10^{-8}$ to $10^{-10}$. The possible competition of $α$-decays with heavy cluster emissions from these superheavy nuclei is also probed from the point of view of searching for new nuclear structure information and possible future experiments with such exotic nuclei. The decay half-lives for some clusters are in fact shown to be lower than the limits of experiments for nuclei with enough available atoms.

## Key claim

The paper re-establishes that all $^{48}Ca$-induced reactions on actinide targets are cold fusion reactions, but with "intermediate" excitation energies (30–35 MeV) that place them between the colder $Pb$-induced reactions (10–20 MeV) and the hotter reactions using lighter beams like $Mg$, $Si$, or $S$ (40–50 MeV); the authors also show that $^{50}Ca$ would be a better beam for the heavier isotopes of Z=110–116, but it is radioactive.

## Method

The authors applied the quantum mechanical fragmentation theory (QMFT) to calculate fragmentation potentials and mass distribution yields for the compound systems $^{280}110^*$, $^{286}112^*$, $^{290,292}114^*$, and $^{296}116^*$ formed in $^{48}Ca$ + $^{232}Th$, $^{238}U$, $^{242,244}Pu$, and $^{248}Cm$ reactions. They then used the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM), also based on QMFT, to compute $\alpha$-decay half-lives and preformation factors for the evaporation residues after 3n and/or 4n emission, and extended the analysis to heavy cluster decays for the $^{277}110$ decay chain.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energies for $^{48}Ca$-induced reactions are $E^*\sim$30–35 MeV, compared to $E^*\sim$10–20 MeV for $Pb$-induced cold fusion and $E^*\sim$40–50 MeV for hot fusion with lighter beams.
- The $\alpha$-preformation factors $P_0$ calculated in PCM are $\sim 10^{-8}$ to $10^{-10}$.
- The assault frequency $\nu_0$ used in PCM is $2.7\times 10^{21} s^{-1}$ for odd parents and $2.7\times 10^{23} s^{-1}$ for even parents.
- A decrease in Q-value by about 0.5 MeV increases the $T_{1/2}$-value by about one order of magnitude.
- The calculated half-lives for $^{49-51}$Ca cluster decays lie below the present experimental limits of $\sim 10^{28}$ s for nuclei with enough available atoms.

## Context

This work extends the original QMFT predictions of Gupta, Sandulescu, and Greiner (1977) by using updated binding energy data from Audi and Wapstra (1995) and Moller et al. (1995), and compares the PCM results with Dubna group experiments and the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) calculations of Royer and Gherghescu (2002, 2000). The study enables future experimental searches for heavy cluster radioactivity in superheavy nuclei, particularly for $^{49-51}$Ca clusters, and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting the $\alpha$-decay chains of elements 110–116.
