# All-order momentum correlations of three ultracold bosonic atoms confined in triple-well traps: Signatures of emergent many-body quantum phase transitions and analogies with three-photon quantum-optics interference

> All-order momentum correlation functions associated with the time-of-flight spectroscopy of three spinless ultracold bosonic interacting neutral atoms confined in a linear three-well optical trap are presented. The underlying Hamiltonian employed for the interacting atoms is an augmented three-site Hubbard model. Our investigations target matter-wave interference of massive particles, aiming at the establishment of experimental protocols for characterizing the quantum states of trapped attractively or repulsively interacting ultracold particles, with variable interaction strength. The manifested advantages and deep physical insights that can be gained through the employment of the results of our study for a comprehensive understanding of the nature of the quantum states of interacting many-particle systems, via analysis of the all-order (that is 1st, 2nd and 3rd) momentum correlation functions for three bosonic atoms in a three well confinement, are illustrated and discussed in the context of time-of-flight inteferometric interrogations of the interaction-strength-induced emergent quantum phase transition from the Mott insulating phase to the superfluid one. Furthermore, we discuss that our inteferometric interrogations establish strong analogies with the quantum-optics interference of three photons, including the aspects of genuine three-photon interference, which are focal to explorations targeting the development and implementation of quantum information applications and quantum computing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that all-order (first, second, and third) momentum correlation functions for three ultracold bosonic atoms in a triple-well trap can reveal signatures of an emergent superfluid-to-Mott-insulator quantum phase transition, but the correspondence between featureless single-particle momentum densities and interaction strength is not one-to-one; a unique characterization of a phase regime requires both second-order and third-order momentum correlations beyond the single-particle momentum density.

## Method

The authors solved the three-site Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian analytically for three spinless bosonic atoms, obtaining all ten eigenvalues and eigenvectors as functions of the interaction parameter U/J. They then constructed momentum-space wave functions by augmenting the Hubbard model with Gaussian single-particle orbitals representing each well, and derived closed-form analytic expressions for the third-order, second-order, and first-order momentum correlation functions via Fourier transform and integration over unresolved momentum variables.

## Key numbers

- The Hubbard Hamiltonian yields ten eigenvalues; at U=0 the eigenvalues are -3√2, -2√2, -√2 (doubly degenerate), 0 (doubly degenerate), √2 (doubly degenerate), 2√2, and 3√2 (in units of J)
- For large |U|, eigenvalues cluster into three groups: one eigenvalue near 0 (singly occupied sites), six eigenvalues near ±|U| (doubly occupied sites), and three eigenvalues near ±3|U| (triply occupied sites)
- The interwell distance used in the Gaussian orbital model is d=3.8 μm, with a single-particle momentum distribution spectral width given by the inverse of s=0.5 μm
- The ordering between E4 and E5 interchanges for |U/J| ≥ 3√2 = 4.24264

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of two-particle two-well systems (Yannouleas and Landman, 2019) to the more complex three-particle three-well case, where the Hilbert space increases from 4 to 10 states. The analytic treatment provides benchmark results for comparison with numerical Hubbard model treatments (Gallego et al., 2015; Raveh et al., 2017) and enables direct analogies with three-photon quantum-optics interference, including genuine three-photon interference effects that cannot be determined from lower-order correlations alone. The results are directly applicable to ongoing time-of-flight experiments with few ultracold atoms in optical tweezers (Preiss et al., 2019; Bergschneider et al., 2019; Becher et al., 2020).
