# Spin-Orbit Force from Lattice QCD

> We present a first attempt to determine nucleon-nucleon potentials in the parity-odd sector, which appear in 1P1, 3P0, 3P1, 3P2-3F2 channels, in Nf=2 lattice QCD simulations. These potentials are constructed from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for J^P=0^-, 1^- and 2^-, which correspond to A1^-, T1^- and T2^- + E^- representation of the cubic group, respectively. We have found a large and attractive spin-orbit potential VLS(r) in the isospin-triplet channel, which is qualitatively consistent with the phenomenological determination from the experimental scattering phase shifts. The potentials obtained from lattice QCD are used to calculate the scattering phase shifts in 1P1, 3P0, 3P1 and 3P2-3F2 channels. The strong attractive spin-orbit force and a weak repulsive central force in spin-triplet P-wave channels lead to an attraction in the 3P2 channel, which is related to the P-wave neutron paring in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors present a first lattice QCD determination of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials in the parity-odd sector, finding a large and attractive spin-orbit force in the isospin-triplet channel that is qualitatively consistent with phenomenological determinations from experimental scattering phase shifts, though the results are obtained at a heavy pion mass of approximately 1133 MeV and thus represent a qualitative rather than quantitative match to physical nuclear forces.

## Method

The authors extract NN potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions computed in Nf=2 lattice QCD on a 16^3 × 32 lattice with a pion mass of about 1133 MeV and nucleon mass of about 2158 MeV. They construct sources with non-zero momentum to access parity-odd states (P and F waves), project onto irreducible representations of the cubic group corresponding to J^P = 0^-, 1^- and 2^-, and use a modified time-dependent method to extract the spin-orbit potential V_LS(r) and central potentials from the four-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1555(17) fm, spatial extension L = 16a = 2.489(27) fm
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 1133 MeV; nucleon mass: m_N ≈ 2158 MeV
- Dispersion relation parameter α = 0.88(1) for the nucleon at m_N = 2152(3) MeV, with χ^2/d.o.f = 2.6
- Free (non-relativistic) center-of-mass energy used: E = (2π/L)^2/m_N = 115 MeV
- Time separation used for potential extraction: t - t_0 = 8

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous determinations of NN potentials in the parity-even sector (S and D waves) from Ishii (2007) and Aoki (2010) into the parity-odd sector, specifically the 1P1, 3P0, 3P1, and 3P2-3F2 channels. The strong attractive spin-orbit force found in the isospin-triplet channel is related to P-wave neutron pairing in neutron stars, as discussed by Tamagaki and Hoffberg, and affects neutron star cooling properties as studied by Page (2013). The method enables future studies at lighter quark masses and with different interpolating operators to test the scheme dependence of the derived potentials.
