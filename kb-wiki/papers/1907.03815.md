# Scaling properties of direct photon yields in heavy ion collisions

> A recent analysis from the PHENIX collaboration of available direct photon measurement results in collisions of various systems such as Au+Au, Cu+Cu, and Pb+Pb, at different beam energies ranging from 39 to 2760 GeV, has shown a universal, within experimental uncertainties, $multiplicity$ scaling, in which direct photon $p_{T}$-spectra for transverse momenta up to 2 GeV/$c$ are scaled with charged hadron pseudorapidity density at midrapidity raised to power $α=1.25$. On the other hand, those direct photon $p_{T}$-spectra also exhibit $geometrical$ scaling in the similar $p_{T}$ range. Assuming power-law dependence of the scaled photon spectra for both scaling laws, we formulate two independent conditions for the power $α$, which overshoot experimental data by $\sim 10\%$ on average. We discuss possible sources that might improve this estimate.

# Scaling properties of direct photon yields in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The multiplicity scaling exponent α = 1.25 observed by PHENIX for direct photon pT-spectra can be derived from geometrical scaling assumptions, yielding theoretical estimates of α = 1.34 and α = 1.40 that overshoot the experimental value by approximately 10% on average, suggesting that additional components of direct photon production may contribute.

## Method

The authors assume power-law functional forms for both the geometrically scaled photon spectra Fγ(τ) ∼ 1/τⁿ and the multiplicity-scaled spectra G(pT) ∼ (Q₀/pT)ᵐ, using fits to PHENIX and ALICE direct photon data for Au+Au, Cu+Cu, and Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energies from 39 to 2760 GeV. They then equate the pT, energy, and Npart dependence of both scaling laws, deriving two independent conditions for α: one from the geometry (Npart dependence) and one from the energy dependence of the saturation scale.

## Key numbers

- Experimental multiplicity scaling exponent α = 1.25, extracted from PHENIX analysis of direct photon pT-spectra up to 2 GeV/c
- First theoretical estimate: α = 1.40, derived from geometry condition using n = 4 and λch = 0.35
- Second theoretical estimate: α = 1.34, derived from energy dependence condition using n = 4, λ = 0.2, and λch = 0.35
- Both theoretical estimates overshoot the experimental value by approximately 10%
- Saturation scale exponent for photons: λ ≈ 0.2, extracted from fits to direct photon data
- Saturation scale exponent for charged particles: λch ≈ 0.35, extracted from charged hadron multiplicity data
- Geometrical scaling parameter δ ≈ 2/3 for the Npart dependence of the saturation scale
- Power-law exponents from fits: n ≈ 4 for GS function Fγ, m ≈ 4.5 for MS function G
- Energy dependence of overlap area ST parametrized with λ′ ≈ 0.06 from Glauber model calculations

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological observation of multiplicity scaling by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare 2018) and geometrical scaling previously observed in deep inelastic scattering (Stasto 2000) and applied to heavy ion collisions (Klein-Bosing 2014, Praszalowicz 2018). The authors connect two empirical scaling laws for direct photons, building on earlier initial-state model calculations (Chiu 2012, McLerran 2014, Khachatryan 2018) that suggested geometrical scaling of photon spectra. The results enable future combined analyses of multiplicity and geometrical scaling to identify different components of direct photon production, potentially addressing the "direct photon flow puzzle" described by Shen (2016).
