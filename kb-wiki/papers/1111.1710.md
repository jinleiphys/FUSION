# The chiral and deconfinement aspects of the QCD transition

> We present results on the chiral and deconfinement properties of the QCD transition at finite temperature. Calculations are performed with 2+1 flavors of quarks using the p4, asqtad and HISQ/tree actions. Lattices with temporal extent N_tau=6, 8 and 12 are used to understand and control discretization errors and to reliably extrapolate estimates obtained at finite lattice spacings to the continuum limit. The chiral transition temperature is defined in terms of the phase transition in a theory with two massless flavors and analyzed using O(N) scaling fits to the chiral condensate and susceptibility. We find consistent estimates from the HISQ/tree and asqtad actions and our main result is T_c=154 +/- 9 MeV.

## Key claim

The chiral transition temperature of QCD at physical light quark masses is determined to be \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV, based on O(N) scaling fits to lattice data with 2+1 flavors of quarks using the HISQ/tree and asqtad actions, after extrapolation to the continuum limit. The quoted uncertainty of 9 MeV combines statistical and systematic errors including a 1 MeV uncertainty from the temperature scale setting, and the result is about 2 MeV lower when using the O(4) scaling Ansatz compared to O(2).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations with 2+1 flavors of quarks using three different discretizations: p4, asqtad, and HISQ/tree actions. They used lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\) to control discretization errors and extrapolate to the continuum limit. The chiral transition temperature was defined via O(N) scaling fits to the chiral condensate and the total chiral susceptibility, using simultaneous fits to data at multiple light quark masses (primarily \(m_l/m_s = 1/20\) and \(1/40\)) to extract the pseudocritical temperature at the physical point \(m_l/m_s = 1/27\).

## Key numbers

- The final continuum-extrapolated transition temperature at physical light quark masses is \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV, combining statistical and systematic errors with an additional 1 MeV uncertainty from the scale setting.
- For the HISQ/tree action on \(N_\tau = 12\) lattices, the O(4) scaling fit gives \(T_c = 155.5 \pm 3.0\) MeV at \(m_l/m_s = 1/27\).
- For the asqtad action on \(N_\tau = 12\) lattices, the O(4) scaling fit gives \(T_c = 167.4 \pm 4.2\) MeV at \(m_l/m_s = 1/27\).
- The O(2) scaling fits yield transition temperatures approximately 2 MeV higher than the O(4) fits across all lattice spacings.
- The range of variation of the central value among different continuum extrapolation fits is about 5 MeV, leading to the 8 MeV combined error estimate.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the MILC collaboration (Bernard 2004) which found \(T_c = 169(12)(4)\) MeV in the chiral limit using the asqtad action, and by the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (Cheng 2006) which reported \(T_c = 192(7)(4)\) MeV using the p4 action on coarser lattices. The present result is approximately 1 sigma lower than the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration's (Aoki 2009, Borsanyi 2010) continuum extrapolation using the stout action, which found \(T_c = 147(2)(3)\) MeV from the peak in the renormalized chiral susceptibility. The analysis enables a more reliable determination of the QCD transition temperature by using simultaneous O(N) scaling fits to both the chiral condensate and susceptibility, resolving ambiguities from earlier inflection-point methods.
