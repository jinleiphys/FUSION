# Effective Field Theory for Dilute Fermions with Pairing

> Effective field theory (EFT) methods for a uniform system of fermions with short-range, natural interactions are extended to include pairing correlations, as part of a program to develop a systematic Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT) for medium and heavy nuclei. An effective action formalism for local composite operators leads to a free-energy functional that includes pairing by applying an inversion method order by order in the EFT expansion. A consistent renormalization scheme is demonstrated for the uniform system through next-to-leading order, which includes induced-interaction corrections to pairing.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) methods for uniform fermion systems with short-range, natural interactions can be extended to include pairing correlations through an effective action formalism, but the use of local composite operators and zero-range interactions introduces new ultraviolet divergences even at the mean-field level that require additional renormalization beyond what is needed for the normal ground state.

## Method

The authors apply an effective action formalism for local composite operators to calculate the free energy order by order in an EFT expansion, using an inversion method that parallels the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward construction. They introduce an external source coupled to the pair density and carry out a functional Legendre transformation via the inversion method of Fukuda et al., working in Euclidean space with the Matsubara formalism at zero temperature. Renormalization is performed using the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme, which generalizes dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction to verify cancellation of divergences order by order through next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The PDS expression for the four-fermion coupling constant is \( C_0(\Lambda) = \frac{4\pi}{M} \left( \frac{1}{-\Lambda + 1/a_s} \right) = \frac{4\pi a_s}{M} + \frac{4\pi a_s^2}{M}\Lambda + \mathcal{O}(\Lambda^2) \), where \(a_s\) is the scattering length and \(\Lambda\) is the PDS subtraction scale.
- The linear-in-\(\Lambda\) contribution from the beachball diagram (Fig. Wexpansion(b)) is \(\delta\mathcal{E}_2 = -\frac{1}{8\pi} C_0^2 M (1 - 1/\nu) \Lambda \rho^2\), where \(\nu=2\) is the spin degeneracy and \(\rho\) is the fermion density.
- The zeroth-order chemical potential for a uniform system at zero temperature is \(\mu_0(N) = (6\pi^2 N/\nu V)^{2/3} \equiv k_F^2/2M \equiv \varepsilon_F^0\), with \(k_F\) the Fermi momentum.

## Context

This work extends the EFT-based Kohn-Sham density functional theory program of Furnstahl, Hammer, and Puglia (2003, 2004) for normal ground states of dilute Fermi systems to include pairing correlations, as part of a broader effort to systematically describe medium and heavy nuclei. The approach generalizes the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method (Stoitsov et al., 2003) and addresses renormalization problems identified by Banks and Raby (1976) for effective potentials of local composite operators. The NLO results contain the universal correction to the pre-factor of the gap at low density first calculated by Gorkov (1961), and the formalism enables future extension to finite systems with spatially dependent sources.
