# Finite-temperature electron-capture rates for neutron-rich nuclei around N=50 and effects on core-collapse supernovae simulations

> The temperature dependence of stellar electron-capture (EC) rates is investigated, with a focus on nuclei around $N=50$, just above $Z=28$, which play an important role during the collapse phase of core-collapse supernovae (CCSN). Two new microscopic calculations of stellar EC rates are obtained from a relativistic and a non-relativistic finite-temperature quasiparticle random-phase approximation approaches, for a conventional grid of temperatures and densities. In both approaches, EC rates due to Gamow-Teller transitions are included. In the relativistic calculation contributions from first-forbidden transitions are also included, and add strongly to the EC rates. The new EC rates are compared with large-scale shell model calculations for the specific case of $^{86}$Kr, providing insight into the finite-temperature effects on the EC rates. At relevant thermodynamic conditions for core-collapse, the discrepancies between the different calculations of this work are within about one order of magnitude. Numerical simulations of CCSN are performed with the spherically-symmetric GR1D simulation code to quantify the impact of such differences on the dynamics of the collapse. These simulations also include EC rates based on two parametrized approximations. A comparison of the neutrino luminosities and enclosed mass at core bounce shows that differences between simulations with different sets of EC rates are relatively small ($\approx 5\%$), suggesting that the EC rates used as inputs for these simulations have become well constrained.

## Key claim

The paper finds that despite order-of-magnitude differences between the new finite-temperature electron-capture rate calculations for nuclei around N=50, the impact on core-collapse supernova simulations is relatively small (approximately 5% differences in neutrino luminosities and enclosed mass at core bounce), suggesting that electron-capture rates used as inputs for these simulations have become well constrained.

## Method

The authors performed two new microscopic calculations of stellar electron-capture rates using a relativistic and a non-relativistic finite-temperature quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-QRPA) approach for 71 neutron-rich nuclei around N=50. The non-relativistic calculation used a Skyrme functional with the finite amplitude method to compute Gamow-Teller transition strengths, while the relativistic calculation employed a meson-exchange energy density functional and included both Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions. The resulting electron-capture rate tables were then used as inputs for spherically-symmetric core-collapse supernova simulations using the GR1D simulation code, and compared with simulations using two parametrized rate approximations and large-scale shell model calculations for 86Kr.

## Key numbers

- The discrepancies between the different microscopic calculations at relevant thermodynamic conditions for core-collapse are within about one order of magnitude
- Differences between supernova simulations with different sets of electron-capture rates are relatively small, approximately 5% for neutrino luminosities and enclosed mass at core bounce
- The study covers 71 nuclei in the "diamond region" around N=50 near 78Ni
- The non-relativistic calculation considered QRPA energies from -30 MeV to +30 MeV
- The phase space integrals used an 80 point grid for both Gauss-Legendre and Gauss-Laguerre quadratures
- The contour integration method used an 88 point Gauss-Legendre grid for all contours

## Context

This work extends previous electron-capture rate calculations by providing the first finite-temperature microscopic rate tables covering the full "diamond region" of nuclei around N=50 near 78Ni, which were identified in earlier studies by Sullivan (2015) and Pascal (2020) as having the largest impact on the evolution of electron fraction during core-collapse. The study builds on zero-temperature QRPA calculations by Titus (2019) and recent finite-temperature calculations by Dzhioev (2020) and Litvinova (2021) on selected nuclei, while also comparing with the hybrid SMMC-RPA approach of Langanke (2003) and the parametrized approximation of Raduta (2017). The results enable more reliable extrapolation of electron-capture rates to the extreme thermodynamic conditions of core-collapse supernovae, though the small impact on simulation outcomes suggests that further improvements to rate calculations may not significantly change collapse dynamics.
