# Varied Signature Splitting Phenomena in Odd Proton Nuclei

> Varied signature splitting phenomena in odd proton rare earth nuclei are investigated. Signature splitting as functions of $K$ and $j$ in the angular momentum projection theory is explicitly shown and compared with those of the particle rotor model. The observed deviations from these rules are due to the band mixings. The recently measured $^{169}$Ta high spin data are taken as a typical example where fruitful information about signature effects can be extracted. Six bands, two of which have not yet been observed, were calculated and discussed in detail in this paper. The experimentally unknown band head energies are given.

## Key claim

Signature splitting in odd proton rare earth nuclei can persist beyond the \(K = {1\over 2}\) band up to at least \(K = {5\over 2}\) in the angular momentum projection theory, which is a feature not present in the particle rotor model; observed deviations from the expected splitting rules are caused by band mixing.

## Method

The authors used the angular momentum projection theory with a deformed Nilsson hamiltonian and BCS calculation, employing three major shells (N = 4, 5, 6 for neutrons; N = 3, 4, 5 for protons) as configuration space. Basis states included one- and three-quasiparticle configurations selected via energy windows of 1.5 MeV and 3 MeV respectively, with about 50 low-lying configurations constructed. The hamiltonian included quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions, with strengths adjusted to reproduce known quadrupole deformation and energy gaps. Band diagrams were computed from diagonal elements of the projected hamiltonian, and full diagonalization was performed to account for band mixing.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation \(\epsilon_2 = 0.225\) for \(^{169}\)Ta.
- Monopole pairing strength given by \(G_M = \left[ 20.12 \mp 13.13 \frac{N-Z}{A}\right] \cdot A^{-1}\) (minus for neutrons, plus for protons).
- Quadrupole pairing strength \(G_Q\) set to 0.20 times \(G_M\) for all bands.
- The \({9\over 2}^-[514]\) band crosses the 3-qp band at spin \({31\over 2} \hbar\).
- The \({1\over 2}^-[541]\) band crossing with its 3-qp band occurs at spin \({37\over 2} \hbar\).
- The three positive parity bands (\({1\over 2}^+[411]\), \({5\over 2}^+[402]\), \({7\over 2}^+[404]\)) cross their respective 3-qp bands roughly at spin \({29\over 2} \hbar\).
- Band head excitation energies relative to the \(I = {5\over 2}\) level of \({5\over 2}^+[402]\): 142 keV (\(I = {1\over 2}\) in \({1\over 2}^+[411]\)), 215 keV (\(I = {7\over 2}\) in \({7\over 2}^+[404]\)), 52 keV (\(I = {9\over 2}\) in \({9\over 2}^-[514]\)), 755 keV (\(I = {7\over 2}\) in \({7\over 2}^-[523]\)), 807 keV (\(I = {5\over 2}\) in \({1\over 2}^-[541]\)).
- The \({1\over 2}^-[541]\) band is split into two branches separated by about 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of signature splitting, which had mainly focused on odd neutron rare earth nuclei (Ko. 1983; SBG. 1987; RAJ. 1992; MJR. 1994), to odd proton systems where the Fermi level is surrounded by more \(j\)-subshells. The angular momentum projection theory (HI. 1979; HI. 1980) is applied to side bands in an odd-A system for the first time, following successful explanation of anomalous crossing frequencies in odd proton rare earth nuclei (Sun, Feng, Wen 1994). The calculations predict two previously unobserved bands in \(^{169}\)Ta (\({7\over 2}^+[404]\) and \({7\over 2}^-[523]\)) and provide band head energies that could guide future experimental identification.
