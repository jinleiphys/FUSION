# The Imaginary Potential of Heavy Quarkonia Moving in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> The melting of a heavy quark-antiquark bound state depends on the screening phenomena associated with the binding energy, as well as scattering phenomena associated with the imaginary part of the potential. We study the imaginary part of the static potential of heavy quarkonia moving in the strongly coupled plasma. The imaginary potential dependence on the velocity of the traveling bound states is calculated. Non-zero velocity leads to increase of the absolute value of the imaginary potential. The enhancement is stronger when the quarkonia move orthogonal to the quark-gluon plasma maximizing the flux between the pair. Moreover, we estimate the thermal width of the moving bound state and find it enhanced compared to the static one. Our results imply that the moving quarkonia dissociate easier than the static ones in agreement with the expectations.

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the static potential for heavy quarkonia moving in strongly coupled plasma increases in absolute value with velocity, and this enhancement is stronger when the quarkonia move orthogonal to the quark-gluon plasma wind, but the analysis is constrained by the condition that for parallel motion the velocity must satisfy v < sqrt(2/3) to obtain a negative imaginary potential.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT duality (gauge/gravity duality) to compute the imaginary part of the static potential for a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma at finite temperature. They boost the AdS-Schwarzschild metric along one spatial direction to represent the moving plasma wind, then compute the Nambu-Goto action for a rectangular Wilson loop aligned either parallel or transverse to the wind direction, extracting the imaginary potential from thermal fluctuations of the string worldsheet around its classical turning point.

## Key numbers

- For parallel motion, the imaginary potential is negative only when the turning point satisfies gamma^2 u_h^4 < u_0^4 < 3 u_h^4, which imposes an upper velocity limit v < sqrt(2/3) for negative ImV.
- For transverse motion, the imaginary potential is negative when gamma^2 u_h^4 < u_0^4 < 3 gamma^2 u_h^4, with no direct upper bound on velocity from this condition.
- The imaginary potential formula for parallel motion: Im V = -(1/(8 sqrt(2) alpha')) * (sqrt(u_0^4 - u_h^4 gamma^2) / (u_0^3 sqrt(u_0^4 - u_h^4))) * (u_h^4 - u_0^4/3).
- The imaginary potential formula for transverse motion: Im V = -(1/(8 sqrt(2) alpha')) * (sqrt(u_0^4 - u_h^4 gamma^2) / (u_0^3 sqrt(u_0^4 - u_h^4))) * (gamma^2 u_h^4 - u_0^4/3).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the imaginary potential for static quarkonia in strongly coupled plasma using gauge/gravity duality, particularly the method developed by Noronha (2009) and Giataganas (2013) for generating the imaginary potential from fluctuations at the deepest point of the string worldsheet. It also builds on earlier work by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006-2008) on moving bound states in AdS/CFT, where the screening length was found to decrease with velocity. The results enable comparison of screening and Landau damping contributions to quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where quarkonia are produced with high momenta.
