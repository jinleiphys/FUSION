# Low-momentum NN interactions and all-order summation of ring diagrams of symmetric nuclear matter

> We study the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter using a ring-diagram approach in which the particle-particle hole-hole ($pphh$) ring diagrams within a momentum model space of decimation scale $Λ$ are summed to all orders. The calculation is carried out using the renormalized low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction $V_{low-k}$, which is obtained from a bare NN potential by integrating out the high-momentum components beyond $Λ$. The bare NN potentials of CD-Bonn, Nijmegen and Idaho have been employed. The choice of $Λ$ and its influence on the single particle spectrum are discussed. Ring-diagram correlations at intermediate momenta ($k\simeq$ 2 fm$^{-1}$) are found to be particularly important for nuclear saturation, suggesting the necessity of using a sufficiently large decimation scale so that the above momentum region is not integrated out. Using $V_{low-k}$ with $Λ\sim 3$ fm$^{-1}$, we perform a ring-diagram computation with the above potentials, which all yield saturation energies $E/A$ and Fermi momenta $k_F^{(0)}$ considerably larger than the empirical values. On the other hand, similar computations with the medium-dependent Brown-Rho scaled NN potentials give satisfactory results of $E/A \simeq -15$ MeV and $k_F^{(0)}\simeq 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$. The effect of this medium dependence is well reproduced by an empirical 3-body force of the Skyrme type.

## Key claim

Using a ring-diagram approach that sums particle-particle hole-hole (pphh) diagrams to all orders, the authors find that free-space low-momentum NN interactions alone, even with a sufficiently large decimation scale of Λ ∼ 3 fm⁻¹, yield saturation energies and Fermi momenta considerably larger than empirical values; only by incorporating medium-dependent Brown-Rho scaled NN potentials or an equivalent Skyrme-type three-body force do the results approach the empirical saturation point.

## Method

The authors construct the low-momentum NN interaction Vlow-k from bare CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, and Idaho potentials via a T-matrix equivalence method with a momentum-space cutoff Λ, then solve the model-space random-phase approximation (RPA) equation within the space k ≤ Λ to sum all pphh ring diagrams to all orders. The single-particle spectrum is taken as the Hartree-Fock spectrum for momenta within the model space and a free-particle spectrum outside. For medium-dependent calculations, the bare Nijmegen potential is first modified by Brown-Rho scaling of meson masses (vector meson masses reduced by 15% at saturation density, σ meson mass reduced by 7.5–9%) before constructing Vlow-k and performing the ring-diagram summation.

## Key numbers

- Empirical nuclear matter saturation: E/A ≃ −16 MeV and kF ≃ 1.36 fm⁻¹ (stated in Introduction)
- With Vlow-k from CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, and Idaho potentials at Λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹, saturation energies range from −19 to −23 MeV and saturation Fermi momenta from 1.75 to 1.85 fm⁻¹
- With Brown-Rho scaled Nijmegen II potential (Cσ ∼ 0.08–0.09), results give E/A ≃ 14–17 MeV and kF⁰ ≃ 1.30–1.45 fm⁻¹
- With Brown-Rho scaling at Cσ = 0.087, the result is E/A ≃ −15 MeV and kF⁰ ≃ 1.4 fm⁻¹
- The Skyrme-type three-body force with t₃ = 1250 reproduces the Brown-Rho scaled results
- At saturation density, the Brown-Rho scaling function Φ(n₀) ≃ 0.8
- Vector meson masses decrease by approximately 10–15% at normal nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends the ring-diagram approach of Song et al. (1987) and Jiang et al. (1988), who used G-matrix effective interactions and found overbound nuclear matter (E/A ≃ −18 MeV, kF ≃ 1.6 fm⁻¹). The authors show that using Vlow-k alone, even with all-order ring summation, still fails to reproduce empirical saturation, consistent with the Coester band problem identified by Coester (1970) and the need for three-body forces emphasized by Wiringa (1995) and Bogner et al. (2005). The results enable future BCS calculations of pairing gaps in nuclear matter using Vlow-k and suggest that medium-dependent interactions or three-body forces are essential for realistic nuclear matter equations of state.
