# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Calculation of Specific Heat of the Inner Crust of Neutron Stars

> We calculate the specific heat of the inner crust of neutron stars within a local-density approximation to an improved relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. Non-uniformness of the system enhances the specific heat in particular at low temperatures. The degree of enhancement is similar to that in the spherical phase of Elgarøy et al. We examine a schematic interpolation between the results of Broglia et al. adopting the Gogny force and ours based on the Lagrangian of the relativistic mean field model.

## Key claim

The specific heat of the inner crust of neutron stars is enhanced by a factor of 3-4 at low temperatures due to non-uniformness of the system, but this enhancement depends critically on the pairing gap in the interior region of the nucleus at the center of the Wigner-Seitz cell, which vanishes in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description but survives in earlier Gogny-force calculations.

## Method

The authors adopted a local-density approximation within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, parametrizing the neutron density in each spherical cell using a Woods-Saxon shape fitted to density-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. They calculated the local Fermi momentum and pairing gap as functions of position using a relativistic mean field interaction with a momentum cutoff determined to reproduce the Bonn-B potential pairing properties, then computed the specific heat from the entropy integral over the Wigner-Seitz cell. The total specific heat included contributions from both neutrons and degenerate electron gas, with proton contributions neglected.

## Key numbers

- Five representative densities were studied: 0.46, 0.28, 0.12, 0.034, and 0.005 times the saturation density rho_0
- Wigner-Seitz cell radii ranged from 15.0 fm at the highest density to 45.7 fm at the lowest density
- The momentum cutoff for the gap equation was Lambda = 3.60 fm^{-1}
- The enhancement factor C_Vtot(non-uniform)/C_Vtot(uniform) was factor 3-4 at low temperatures
- The interior neutron density parameters rho_{0,n} ranged from 0.098 fm^{-3} to 0.114 fm^{-3} across the five densities
- The external neutron density rho_{ext,n} ranged from 0.0005 fm^{-3} to 0.0737 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic calculations of Elgaroy et al. (1996) and Broglia et al. (1995) to a relativistic mean field framework, serving as a relativistic counterpart to those studies. The key difference from Broglia et al. (1995) using the Gogny force is that the pairing gap in the interior region of the nucleus vanishes in the relativistic description because the cutoff parameter was determined to reproduce the Bonn-B potential, whereas the Gogny force predicts superfluidity surviving well above saturation density. The results resemble those of the spherical phase in Elgaroy et al. (1996) using the Bonn-A potential. The work enables future refinements including non-spherical nuclear shapes and the proximity effect beyond the local-density approximation.
