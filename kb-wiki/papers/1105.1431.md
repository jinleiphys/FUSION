# Big-bang nucleosynthesis with a long-lived charged massive particle including $^4$He spallation processes

> We propose helium-4 spallation processes induced by long-lived stau in supersymmetric standard models, and investigate an impact of the processes on light elements abundances. We show that, as long as the phase space of helium-4 spallation processes is open, they are more important than stau-catalyzed fusion and hence constrain the stau property.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to naive expectations, spallation of helium-4 nuclei bound to long-lived staus (charged massive particles) dominates over stau-catalyzed fusion in big-bang nucleosynthesis, as long as the phase space for spallation is kinematically open (mass difference between stau and neutralino greater than about 0.026 GeV). This imposes new constraints on supersymmetric model parameters from the overproduction of deuterium and tritium rather than lithium-6.

## Method

The authors analytically calculated the reaction rates for three helium-4 spallation channels from a stau-helium bound state: producing a triton plus neutron, a deuteron plus two neutrons, or a proton plus three neutrons. They compared these rates with the stau-catalyzed fusion rate and then performed a full numerical calculation of primordial light element abundances including all exotic reactions (spallation, catalyzed fusion, and internal conversion) to find allowed regions of the minimal supersymmetric standard model parameter space.

## Key numbers

- The spallation processes dominate over stau-catalyzed fusion when the mass difference between stau and neutralino satisfies delta m greater than about 0.026 GeV (at a universe temperature of 30 keV, with stau mass 350 GeV, sin theta_tau = 0.8)
- The stau-catalyzed fusion cross section is larger than the standard He-4 + d -> Li-6 + gamma reaction by 6 to 7 orders of magnitude (citing Hamaguchi 2007)
- The allowed relic abundance of staus that fits both dark matter density and light element abundances is Y_tau = 2 x 10^{-13} at 3 sigma for the high observational value of Li-7/H (Melendez 2004)
- The standard big-bang nucleosynthesis predicts Log10(Li-7/H) = -9.35 plus or minus 0.06 using the WMAP baryon-to-photon ratio of (6.225 plus or minus 0.170) x 10^{-10}
- Observational Li-7/H values range from Log10(Li-7/H) = -9.63 plus or minus 0.06 (high value, Melendez 2004) to -9.90 plus or minus 0.09 (low value, Bonifacio 2006)
- The deuteron-to-hydrogen ratio constraint used is D/H = (2.80 plus or minus 0.20) x 10^{-5} (Pettini 2008)
- The upper bound on Li-6/Li-7 used is less than 0.046 plus 0.022 (Asplund 2005)
- The Fermi coupling constant is G_F = 1.166 x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Jittoh 2007, 2008, 2010) of stau effects on big-bang nucleosynthesis by including helium-4 spallation processes that had been previously ignored. It contradicts the expectation from earlier work (Pospelov 2006, Hamaguchi 2007) that stau-catalyzed fusion to lithium-6 would be the dominant bound-state reaction. The results enable simultaneous resolution of the lithium-7 discrepancy and dark matter abundance in supersymmetric models with bino-like neutralino LSP and stau NLSP, complementing other proposed mechanisms (Jedamzik 2004, Bird 2007, Pospelov 2010, Kawasaki 2010, Erken 2011).
