# Holographic Description of the Phase Diagram of a Chiral Symmetry Breaking Gauge Theory

> The large N_c N=4 gauge theory with quenched N=2 quark matter in the presence of a magnetic field displays chiral symmetry breaking. We study the temperature and chemical potential dependence of this theory using its gravity dual (based on the D3/D7 brane system). With massless quarks, at zero chemical potential, the theory displays a first order thermal transition where chiral symmetry is restored and simultaneously the mesons of the theory melt. At zero temperature, these transitions with chemical potential are second order and occur at different chemical potential values. Between the three there are two tri-critical points, the positions of which we identify. At finite quark mass the second order transition for chiral symmetry becomes a cross over and there is a critical point at the end of the first order transition, while the meson melting transition remains similar to the massless quark case. We track the movement of the critical points as the mass is raised relative to the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a large-Nc N=4 gauge theory with quenched N=2 quark matter and a magnetic field, the phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane contains two tri-critical points (not just one as in QCD), and that the second order chiral symmetry restoration transition becomes a cross over at finite quark mass while the meson melting transition remains similar to the massless case.

## Method

The authors use the holographic D3/D7 brane system to model the gauge theory, solving the D7 brane DBI action numerically with Mathematica to find embeddings in an AdS5 black hole geometry. They classify embeddings as Minkowski (off the black hole) or black hole (touching the horizon), extract the quark mass and condensate from asymptotic behavior, and compute thermodynamic potentials (grand potential and Helmholtz free energy) to determine phase transitions via Maxwell construction and free energy minimization.

## Key numbers

- The first order chiral symmetry restoration transition at zero chemical potential occurs at a critical temperature of tw_H = 0.2516 (scaled units with B = 1/2R^2)
- At zero temperature, the second order chiral symmetry restoration transition occurs at a critical density of td = 0.3197 (scaled units)
- The low temperature regime (tw_H = 0.15) shows chiral symmetry breaking with the blue embedding thermodynamically preferred over the red at tm = 0
- At high temperature (tw_H = 300), the magnetic field becomes negligible and the system shows the usual finite temperature meson melting transition

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field induced chiral symmetry breaking in the N=4 gauge theory with N=2 quark matter, previously studied at zero density by Johnson (2008) and Erdmenger (2008), to include chemical potential. It also builds on the zero magnetic field finite density studies by Karch (2009) and Myers (2007). The results enable mapping of the full phase diagram and show that the structure with two critical points persists even in the infinite mass limit (pure N=2 theory without magnetic field), a feature not previously reported in the works of Myers (2007) or Sin (2008).
