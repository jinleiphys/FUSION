# r-mode instability of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries: effects of Fermi surface depletion and superfluidity of dense matter

> The nucleon-nucleon correlation between nucleons leads to the Fermi surface depletion measured by a $Z$-factor in momentum distribution of dense nuclear matter. The roles of the Fermi surface depletion effect ($Z$-factor effect) and its quenched neutron triplet superfluidity of nuclear matter in viscosity and hence in the gravitational-wave-driven $r$-mode instability of neutron stars (NSs) are investigated. The bulk viscosity is reduced by both the two effects, especially the superfluid effect at low temperatures which is also able to reduce the inferred core temperature of NSs. Intriguingly, due to the neutron superfluidity, the core temperature of the NSs in known low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) are found to be clearly divided into two groups: high and low temperatures which correspond to NSs with short and long recurrence times for nuclear-powered bursts respectively. Yet, a large number of NSs in these LMXBs are still located in the $r$-mode instability region. If the density-dependent symmetry energy is stiff enough, the occurence of direct Urca process reduces the inferred core temperature by about one order of magnitude. Accordingly, the contradiction between the predictions and observations is alleviated to some extent, but some NSs are still located inside the unstable region.

## Key claim
The Fermi surface depletion effect (Z-factor) and its quenched neutron triplet superfluidity reduce bulk viscosity in neutron star cores, but these effects do not resolve the long-standing contradiction that many neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries remain inside the predicted r-mode instability region; the disagreement is only partially alleviated if the direct Urca process operates in stars with stiff symmetry energy.

## Method
The author calculated bulk viscosity from modified Urca and direct Urca processes in β-stable npeμ matter, incorporating the Z-factor as a multiplicative factor in the phase-space integrals and including neutron ³PF₂ superfluid gaps from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with the Argonne V18 interaction plus three-body force. The r-mode instability window was determined by balancing gravitational radiation driving against viscous damping from bulk viscosity, shear viscosity (electron and neutron contributions), and viscous boundary layer damping at the crust-core interface, using the APR equation of state and the IMP1 Skyrme energy density functional for stellar structure. Core temperatures for neutron stars in known low-mass X-ray binaries were inferred from the condition L_heat = L_ν, where neutrino luminosity included modified Urca, direct Urca, and Cooper pair breaking and formation processes.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity at ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and Ω = 10⁴ s⁻¹ is reduced by about 50% by the Z-factor alone, and by about six orders of magnitude at T = 10⁷ K when neutron ³PF₂ superfluidity is included.
- The peak neutron ³PF₂ superfluid gap is about 0.04 MeV when the Z-factor is included, lower than the 0.08 MeV constrained by Page et al. (2011) from Cas A observations.
- The inferred core temperatures for neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries lie in the range (1–3) × 10⁸ K.
- When the direct Urca process operates (e.g., with symmetry energy slope L = 60 MeV), the inferred core temperature is reduced by about one order of magnitude.
- The r-mode saturation amplitude for PSR J1023+0038 is constrained to α_m ≈ 5 × 10⁻⁸ (Haskell and Patruno 2017), and for most known low-mass X-ray binaries in the unstable region α_m = 10⁻⁹ to 10⁻⁸ (Haskell et al. 2012).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Ho et al. (2011) by incorporating the Z-factor effect from nucleon-nucleon correlations and its quenched neutron ³PF₂ superfluidity, previously studied by Dong et al. (2013, 2016) for neutron star thermal evolution. The results show that while these effects reduce inferred core temperatures via enhanced Cooper pair breaking and formation neutrino emission, they do not resolve the discrepancy that many neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries lie inside the predicted r-mode instability window. The paper suggests that additional damping mechanisms, such as mutual friction from superfluid vortices (Haskell et al. 2009), hyperonic bulk viscosity (Jha et al. 2010), or very small r-mode saturation amplitudes (Haskell and Patruno 2017; Schwenzer et al. 2017), may be required to reconcile theory with observations.
