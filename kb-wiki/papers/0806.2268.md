# Anisotropic fluid dynamics in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A formalism for anisotropic fluid dynamics is proposed. It is designed to describe boost-invariant systems with anisotropic pressure. Such systems are expected to be produced at the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, when the timescales are too short to achieve equal thermalization of transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom. The approach is based on the energy-momentum and entropy conservation laws, and may be regarded as a minimal extension of the boost-invariant standard relativistic hydrodynamics of the perfect fluid. We show how the formalism may be used to describe the isotropization of the system (the transition from the initial state with no longitudinal pressure to the final state with equal longitudinal and transverse pressure).

## Key claim

The paper proposes a minimal extension of boost-invariant perfect-fluid hydrodynamics that can describe systems with anisotropic pressure, using energy-momentum and entropy conservation, where the transition from purely transverse to isotropic expansion is encoded in a single "pressure relaxation function" whose form must be assumed at the hydrodynamic level rather than derived from microscopic theory.

## Method

The author constructs an energy-momentum tensor with distinct transverse and longitudinal pressures, imposes boost-invariant forms for the fluid four-velocity and the spacelike vector defining the longitudinal direction, and derives evolution equations from energy-momentum conservation together with entropy conservation. The resulting hydrodynamic equations have the same form as standard boost-invariant hydrodynamics but require a generalized equation of state where energy density depends on both entropy density and proper time through a pressure relaxation function R(x). The formalism is illustrated with analytic solutions for 1+1 boost-invariant expansion without transverse flow, comparing cases of isotropic hydrodynamics, transverse hydrodynamics (vanishing longitudinal pressure), smooth isotropization, and sudden isotropization.

## Key numbers

- For standard boost-invariant hydrodynamics: temperature scales as T ~ τ^{-1/3} and energy density as ε ~ τ^{-4/3}
- For transverse hydrodynamics (P_L = 0): temperature becomes independent of time (T = const) and energy density scales as ε ~ τ^{-1}
- The isotropization timescale is parameterized by τ_I where τ_I^2 = x_I τ_0^2, with example calculations shown for τ_I = 2 τ_0
- The pressure relaxation function R(x) is constrained by P_T ≥ P_L and P_L ≥ 0, requiring R'(x) ≥ 0 and R(x) ≥ 6x R'(x), so R cannot grow faster than x^{1/6}

## Context

This work extends the "transverse hydrodynamics" approach of Bialas (2007) and Chojnacki (2007), where only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized and longitudinal pressure vanishes, toward a formalism that can describe the full isotropization process. It contrasts with kinetic theory approaches (Zhang, 2008) and dissipative hydrodynamics (Kovchegov, 2005; Bozek, 2007) that produce entropy and particle production, instead conserving entropy as suggested by PHOBOS data (Back, 2004) showing similar hadron yields in d+Au and Au+Au collisions. The approach enables phenomenological studies of early-stage dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions where the isotropization timescale can be extracted from data fitting, and may relate to entropy-conserving collisionless kinetic equations based on mean-field quark-gluon plasma dynamics (Dumitru, 2005).
