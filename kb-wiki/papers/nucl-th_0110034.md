# An isobar model for eta photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon

> Eta photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon is studied using an isobar model. The model contains Born terms, and contributions from vector meson exchanges and nucleon resonances. Our results are compared with recent eta photoproduction data for differential and total cross sections, beam asymmetry, and target asymmetry, as well as electroproduction data. Besides the dominant S11(1535) resonance, we show that the second S11 resonance, S11(1650), is also necessary to be included in order to extract S11(1535) resonance parameters properly. In addition, the beam asymmetry data allow us to extract very small (<0.1%) N* --> eta N decay branching ratios of the D13(1520) and F15(1680) resonances because of the overwhelming s-wave dominance. This model (ETA-MAID) is implemented as a part of the MAID program.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the second S11 resonance, S11(1650), must be included alongside the dominant S11(1535) to properly extract S11(1535) resonance parameters from eta photoproduction data, and that beam asymmetry data allow extraction of very small (below 0.1%) N* → ηN decay branching ratios for the D13(1520) and F15(1680) resonances due to overwhelming s-wave dominance.

## Method

The authors constructed an isobar model (ETA-MAID) containing Born terms, vector meson exchange contributions (ρ and ω mesons), and nucleon resonance excitations parameterized with Breit-Wigner shapes. They performed least-squares fitting to world data on total and differential photoproduction cross sections from MAMI and GRAAL, photon asymmetry data from GRAAL, and electroproduction cross sections from JLab, treating resonance masses, widths, branching ratios, and photon couplings as free parameters within PDG ranges.

## Key numbers

- S11(1535) resonance: fitted mass 1541 MeV, total width 191 MeV, ηN branching ratio 50%, photoexcitation helicity amplitude A^p_{1/2} = +118 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- S11(1650) resonance: fitted mass 1638 MeV, total width 114 MeV, ηN branching ratio 7.9%, photoexcitation helicity amplitude A^p_{1/2} = +68 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- D13(1520) resonance: ηN branching ratio 0.06% (fitted), photoexcitation helicity amplitude A^p_{1/2} = -52 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, A^p_{3/2} = +166 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- F15(1680) resonance: ηN branching ratio 0.06% (fitted), photoexcitation helicity amplitude A^p_{1/2} = -21 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, A^p_{3/2} = +125 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- g_{ηNN}^2 / 4π = 0.4 ± 0.2 from angular distribution analysis, compared to g_{πNN}^2 / 4π = 14.3
- Vector meson parameters: ρ meson mass 768.5 MeV, coupling λ_ρ = 0.810; ω meson mass 782.6 MeV, coupling λ_ω = 0.291

## Context

This work extends the earlier isobar model by Knoechlein et al. (1995) and continues the unitary isobar model MAID developed by Drechsel et al. (1998) for pions, now applied to eta production. The model addresses the long-standing discrepancy where S11(1535) photon resonance coupling A_{1/2} from eta photoproduction analyses is only 0.5-0.6 times the value obtained in pion photoproduction analyses (Benmerrouche 1995, Krusche 1997). The results enable precise study of the Q^2 dependence of both transverse and longitudinal couplings for eta electroproduction, and provide a framework for interpreting eta production off neutrons in deuteron experiments (Ritz 2001).
