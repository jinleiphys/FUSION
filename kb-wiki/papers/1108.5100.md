# Semi-microscopic description of the backbending phenomena in some deformed even-even nuclei

> The mechanism of backbending is semi-phenomenologically investigated based on the hybridization of two rotational bands. These bands are defined by treating a model Hamiltonian describing two interacting subsystems: a set of particles moving in a deformed mean field and interacting among themselves through an effective pairing force and a phenomenological deformed core whose intrinsic ground state is an axially symmetric coherent boson state. The two components interact with each other by a quadrupole-quadrupole and a spin-spin interaction. The total Hamiltonian is considered in the space of states with good angular momentum, projected from a quadrupole deformed product function. The single-particle factor function defines the nature of the rotational bands, one corresponding to the ground band in which all particles are paired and another one built upon a $i_{13/2}$ neutron broken pair. The formalism is applied to six deformed even-even nuclei, known as being good backbenders. Agreement between theory and experiment is fairly good.

## Key claim

Backbending in deformed even-even nuclei is described as the result of band crossing between the ground band (all particles paired) and a two-quasiparticle band built upon a broken $i_{13/2}$ neutron pair, treated via hybridization of two rotational bands in a semi-phenomenological model. The agreement between theory and experiment is described as "fairly good," with the model intended to reproduce only the first backbending at low and intermediate spins.

## Method

The authors construct a model Hamiltonian describing two interacting subsystems: particles in a deformed mean field with an effective pairing force, and a phenomenological deformed core described by an axially symmetric coherent boson state. The particle-core interaction includes quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin terms. Angular momentum projection from quadrupole deformed product states generates basis states with good angular momentum for both the ground band (0 quasiparticle) and the S-band (2 quasiparticle with a broken $i_{13/2}$ neutron pair). The non-orthogonal basis is orthogonalized, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized to obtain yrast energies for six deformed even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Six nuclei studied: $^{156}$Dy, $^{160}$Yb, $^{158}$Er, $^{160}$Er, $^{164}$Hf, $^{166}$Hf
- Deformation parameters $d$ range from 2.5000 ($^{160}$Yb) to 3.1414 ($^{156}$Dy), corresponding to $\beta_2$ values from 0.195 to 0.237
- Boson frequency $\hbar\omega_b$ ranges from 0.9778 MeV ($^{158}$Er) to 1.1039 MeV ($^{164}$Hf)
- Quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength $X_C$ ranges from 50.81 keV ($^{160}$Er) to 65.59 keV ($^{160}$Yb)
- Pairing strength $G$ ranges from 0.1737 MeV ($^{166}$Hf) to 0.1854 MeV ($^{160}$Yb)
- Spin-spin interaction strength $C$ ranges from 3.690 keV ($^{160}$Yb) to 9.363 keV ($^{160}$Er)
- Maximum angular momentum for the $i_{13/2}$ intruder orbital is 24, achieved with 6 or 8 particles
- Total angular momentum $J$ runs from 0 to 36
- Core angular momentum runs up to $J_c \leq 60$
- Number of neutrons in the $i_{13/2}$ shell: 2 for $^{156}$Dy, $^{158}$Er, $^{160}$Yb, $^{164}$Hf, $^{160}$Er; 4 for $^{166}$Hf
- Pairing strength interpolation formula: $G = \frac{1}{A}(g_0 - g_1\frac{N-Z}{A})$ with $g_0 = 37.44$ MeV, $g_1 = 62.25$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the coherent state model of Raduta (2000) by adding a microscopic treatment of intruder particles from the $i_{13/2}$ orbital to describe backbending. It builds on the band crossing mechanism proposed by Stephens and Simon (1972) and the Coriolis anti-pairing effect of Mottelson and Valatin (1960), while addressing limitations of cranked mean-field approaches such as angular momentum dispersion discussed by Satula (1990). The model enables semi-microscopic calculation of yrast energies and alignment properties for rare-earth backbenders without the symmetry-breaking issues of cranking methods.
