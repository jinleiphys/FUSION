# Critical Coupling for Two-dimensional $ϕ^4$ Theory in Discretized Light-Cone Quantization

> We solve for the critical coupling in the symmetric phase of two-dimensional $ϕ^4$ field theory using Discretized Light-Cone Quantization. We adopt periodic boundary conditions, neglect the zero mode, and obtain a critical coupling consistent with the critical coupling reported using conformal truncation in light-front quantization. We find a 17% dfference from the critical coupling reported with light-front quantization in a symmetric polynomial basis.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a critical coupling of \(22.64 \pm 0.17\) for the symmetric-to-broken phase transition in two-dimensional \(\phi^4\) theory using Discretized Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ) with periodic boundary conditions and zero mode omitted, which is consistent within uncertainties with the conformal truncation result of \(23.12 \pm 0.38\) reported by Anand et al. (2020) but differs by 17% from the symmetric polynomial basis result of \(26.39 \pm 0.63\) reported by Burkardt et al.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the light-front Hamiltonian for two-dimensional \(\phi^4\) theory in DLCQ with periodic boundary conditions, omitting the zero mode, and compute the critical coupling at which the mass gap vanishes for the lowest odd-sector state, the lowest even-sector state, and the first excited odd-sector state at fixed dimensionless longitudinal momentum \(K\). They perform polynomial fits in \(1/\sqrt{K}\) and \(1/K\) to extrapolate these finite-\(K\) critical couplings to the continuum limit \(K \rightarrow \infty\), using three independent codes (MFD, lfphi4MH-00, and LFHC_phi4_SJ) with Lanczos diagonalization and LAPACK/petsc/slepc packages.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated continuum-limit critical coupling \(\lambda_c/\mu^2 = 22.64 \pm 0.17\) from combined fits to three states (lowest odd, lowest even, first excited odd).
- Critical coupling from conformal truncation (Anand et al., 2020): \(23.12 \pm 0.38\).
- Critical coupling from symmetric polynomial basis (Burkardt et al.): \(26.39 \pm 0.63\).
- Ratio of even-sector to odd-sector critical couplings in the continuum limit: \(B_0 \approx 1.000267\), consistent with unity within uncertainties.
- DLCQ calculations performed for \(K\) values up to 96, with matrix dimensions reaching \(5.9 \times 10^7\) for the odd sector at \(K=96\) without boson number truncation.

## Context

This work extends the early DLCQ study of two-dimensional \(\phi^4\) theory by Harindranath and Vary (1987, 1988), which extrapolated from \(K \leq 20\) to a critical coupling of approximately 33, by reaching much higher \(K\) values and providing a precise continuum extrapolation. The result is consistent with the conformal truncation approach of Anand et al. (2020) but not with the symmetric polynomial basis method of Burkardt et al., highlighting sensitivity to the treatment of small longitudinal momentum in light-front quantization. The findings validate DLCQ as a non-perturbative method for strong-coupling field theories and are directly relevant to the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) program for 3+1 dimensional problems.
