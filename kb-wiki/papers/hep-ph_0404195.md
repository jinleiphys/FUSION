# Higher twist analysis of the proton g_1 structure function

> We perform a global analysis of all available spin-dependent proton structure function data, covering a large range of Q^2, 1 < Q^2 < 30 GeV^2, and calculate the lowest moment of the g_1 structure function as a function of Q^2. From the Q^2 dependence of the lowest moment we extract matrix elements of twist-4 operators, and determine the color electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton to be χ_E = 0.026 +- 0.015 (stat) + 0.021/-0.024 (sys) and χ_B = -0.013 -+ 0.007 (stat) - 0.010/+0.012 (sys), respectively.

## Key claim

The twist-4 coefficient extracted from the lowest moment of the proton g1 structure function is small and consistent with zero for Q^2 above 2-3 GeV^2, contrary to earlier analyses that found larger values; the extracted color electric and magnetic polarizabilities are positive and negative respectively, with uncertainties that allow values close to zero.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of all available spin-dependent proton structure function data from SLAC, CERN, DESY, and Jefferson Lab experiments, covering Q^2 from 1 to 30 GeV^2, using a single consistent set of inputs for the extraction of g1 from longitudinal and transverse asymmetries. They computed the lowest Nachtmann moment M1(Q^2) of the g1 structure function and fitted its Q^2 dependence using the operator product expansion to extract the leading twist-2 singlet axial charge a0_inv from high-Q^2 data (Q^2 > 5 GeV^2), then extracted the dynamical twist-4 coefficient f2 and the twist-6 coefficient mu6 from the low-Q^2 data (1 to 5 GeV^2). The color electric and magnetic polarizabilities chi_E and chi_B were then derived from the combination of f2 and the twist-3 matrix element d2.

## Key numbers

- Singlet axial charge: a0_inv = 0.145 +/- 0.018 (stat.) +/- 0.103 (sys.) +/- 0.041 (low x) +/- 0.006/0.010 (alpha_s), extracted from data with Q^2 > 5 GeV^2
- Twist-4 coefficient: f2 = 0.039 +/- 0.022 (stat.) +/- 0.000/0.018 (sys.) +/- 0.030 (low x) +/- 0.007/0.011 (alpha_s), normalized at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2
- Twist-6 coefficient: mu6/M^4 = 0.011 +/- 0.013 (stat.) +/- 0.010/0.000 (sys.) +/- 0.011 (low x) +/- 0.000 (alpha_s)
- Color electric polarizability: chi_E = 0.026 +/- 0.015 (stat.) +/- 0.021/0.024 (sys.)
- Color magnetic polarizability: chi_B = -0.013 +/- 0.007 (stat.) +/- 0.010/0.012 (sys.)
- Low-x extrapolation contributes approximately 7% and 15% of the asymptotic value of M1 at Q^2 = 1 and 3 GeV^2, respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological analyses by Ji and Melnitchouk (1997) and Kao et al. (2004), which found larger values of f2 (0.10 +/- 0.05 and 0.15-0.18 respectively) but did not include the 1/Q^4 twist-6 corrections that the present analysis finds important even at Q^2 near 1 GeV^2. The extracted color polarizabilities disagree with QCD sum rule predictions (which give negative chi_E and positive chi_B) and instanton vacuum model calculations, but are consistent with MIT bag model results. The analysis enables future extractions of higher twist contributions with improved precision from upcoming measurements at Q^2 between 1 and 10 GeV^2, and highlights the need for better determination of the g2 structure function and the d2 matrix element.
