# Structure of double pionic atoms

> We study theoretically the structure of double pionic atoms, in which two negatively charged pions ($π^-$) are bound in the atomic orbits. The double pionic atom is considered to be an interesting system from the point of view of the multi bosonic systems. In addition, it could be possible to deduce valuable information on the isospin $I = 2$ $ππ$ interaction and the pion-nucleus strong interaction. In this paper, we take into account the $ππ$ strong and electromagnetic interactions, and evaluate the effects on the binding energies by perturbation theory for the double pionic atoms in heavy nuclei. We investigate several combinations of two pionic states and find that the order of magnitude of the energy shifts due to the $ππ$ interaction is around 10 keV for the strong interaction and around 100 keV for the electromagnetic interaction for the ground states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy shifts of double pionic atoms due to the ππ interaction are around 10 keV for the strong interaction and around 100 keV for the electromagnetic interaction for the ground states, but these values are obtained using first-order perturbation theory and depend strongly on the choice of single-pion wave function; the shifts are much smaller when the realistic optical potential wave functions are used compared to point-Coulomb wave functions.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation with an Ericson-Ericson type optical potential and finite-size Coulomb potential to obtain realistic single-pion wave functions for heavy nuclei. They then constructed symmetrized product wave functions for two pions and evaluated the energy shifts from the ππ electromagnetic Coulomb potential and a contact (delta-function or Gaussian) strong interaction potential using first-order perturbation theory. The strength of the strong interaction was fixed from the isospin I=2 ππ scattering length obtained from leading-order chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Energy shift from ππ electromagnetic interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ¹²¹Sn: ΔE_em = 91.0 keV (with realistic optical potential wave function)
- Energy shift from ππ strong interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ¹²¹Sn: ΔE_s = 7.5 keV (with realistic optical potential wave function, delta-function potential)
- Energy shift from ππ electromagnetic interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ²⁰⁷Pb: ΔE_em = 97.8 keV
- Energy shift from ππ strong interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ²⁰⁷Pb: ΔE_s = 9.4 keV
- Energy shift from ππ electromagnetic interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ²³⁷U: ΔE_em = 98.4 keV
- Energy shift from ππ strong interaction for the (1s,1s) ground state in ²³⁷U: ΔE_s = 9.6 keV
- Single pionic 1s binding energy in ¹²¹Sn: B_π = 3850.0 keV
- Single pionic 1s binding energy in ²⁰⁷Pb: B_π = 6933.7 keV
- Single pionic 1s binding energy in ²³⁷U: B_π = 7848.0 keV
- Isospin I=2 ππ scattering length from chiral perturbation theory: a_2 = -0.045 m_π⁻¹ (with f_π = 93 MeV)
- For the (2p,2p) state in ¹²¹Sn with total angular momentum L=0: ΔE_s = 8.0 keV (largest strong interaction shift among all cases considered)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of double pionic atoms first proposed around 30 years ago in formation reaction studies by Nieves (1992) and others, revisiting the topic using modern knowledge from deeply bound single pionic atoms. The single-pion wave functions rely on the optical potential parameters from Seki and Masutani (1983) and the charge density parameters from Fricke (1995) and for ²³⁸U from a separate reference. The paper enables future experimental searches for double pionic atoms and provides baseline predictions for the energy shifts that could be compared with measurements, though the authors note that extracting information on the density dependence of the ππ interaction from such measurements would be very difficult.
