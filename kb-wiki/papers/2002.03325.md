# Impact of longitudinal bulk viscous effects to heavy quark transport in a strongly magnetized hot QCD medium

> The effects of longitudinal bulk viscous pressure on the heavy quark dynamics have been estimated in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma within the Fokker-Planck approach. The bulk viscous modification to the momentum distribution of bulk degrees of freedom has been obtained in the presence of a magnetic field while incorporating the realistic equation of state of the hot magnetized QCD medium. As the magnetic field breaks the isotropy of the medium, the analysis is done along the directions longitudinal and transverse to the field. The longitudinal bulk viscous contribution is seen to have sizable effects in the heavy quark momentum diffusion in the magnetized medium. The dependence of higher Landau levels and the equation of state on the viscous correction to the heavy quark transport has been explored in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that longitudinal bulk viscous effects in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) have a sizable impact on heavy quark momentum diffusion, but the effect is conditional: it suppresses the quark contribution to diffusion while enhancing the gluonic contribution, and these corrections are most pronounced in the temperature regime near the transition temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the Fokker-Planck approach to describe heavy quark dynamics, solving the effective relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to obtain the non-equilibrium momentum distribution function with longitudinal bulk viscous corrections. They incorporated the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) to account for the QCD equation of state in a strongly magnetized medium, and derived the longitudinal bulk viscous pressure evolution equation. The analysis was performed separately for directions longitudinal and transverse to the magnetic field, considering higher Landau level (HLL) contributions in the regime where gT is much less than the square root of |q_f eB|.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal bulk viscous coefficient β_{Π∥} increases with temperature at |eB| = 15 m_π^2, with HLL effects more visible at higher temperatures.
- The Debye screening mass is reduced by longitudinal bulk viscous corrections at τ = 0.3 fm^{-1} (proper time parameter).
- The longitudinal bulk viscous correction enhances the gluonic contribution to the HQ diffusion coefficient K^{gluon}_{∥} at |eB| = 15 m_π^2 with l=4 (Landau levels).
- The bulk viscous correction reduces the perpendicular quark contribution K^{quark}_{⊥} to HQ momentum diffusion at |eB| = 15 m_π^2 with l=4.
- The anisotropy ratio R = (K̄_∥/K̄_⊥)/(K_∥/K_⊥) approaches unity asymptotically at high temperatures, with bulk viscous effects most pronounced near the transition temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark dynamics in magnetized QGP that assumed a thermalized static medium (Fukushima 2015, Kurian 2019) by incorporating non-equilibrium viscous effects. It builds on theoretical indications that longitudinal bulk viscosity is dominant in strong magnetic fields (Hattori 2017, Kurian 2018) and that bulk viscosity is enhanced in QCD matter (Meyer 2007, Karsch 2007). The results provide anisotropic HQ transport coefficients that can be used as inputs for Langevin dynamics simulations to study heavy meson directed flow v_1 and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies, extending earlier calculations that ignored magnetic field and viscosity effects on HQ drag and diffusion (Das 2016, Chatterjee 2018, Coci 2019).
