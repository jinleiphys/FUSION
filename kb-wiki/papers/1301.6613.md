# Euclidean Relativistic Quantum Mechanics

> We discuss a formulation of exactly Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics where the input is model Euclidean Green functions or their generating functional. We discuss the structure of the models, the construction of the Hilbert space, the construction and transformation properties of single-particle states, and the construction of GeV scale transition matrix elements. A simple model is utilized to demonstrate the feasibility of this approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formulation of exactly Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics that uses model Euclidean Green functions or their generating functional as input, with the hedge that while the framework is formally exact, reflection positivity is a nontrivial constraint that is not stable under small perturbations and will require future investigation.

## Method

The authors construct a Hilbert space from reflection-positive Euclidean generating functionals, define single-particle states as mass eigenstates of the Poincaré generators, and build two-Hilbert-space injection operators for Haag-Ruelle scattering theory. They compute approximate sharp-momentum transition matrix elements using narrow Gaussian wave packets and approximate S-matrix elements using the Kato-Birman invariance principle with Chebyshev polynomial expansions of the compact operator exp(-beta H). The method is tested on an exactly solvable relativistic model with a mass operator M^2 = 4(k^2 + m^2) - |g> lambda <g|, where m is the nucleon mass and lambda is chosen to produce a deuteron-mass bound state.

## Key numbers

- To obtain sharp-momentum transition matrix elements to 0.1% accuracy, the wave packet width k_w must be about 3% of the initial momentum k_0, tested up to 2 GeV (e.g., at k_0 = 0.1 GeV, alpha = 105000 GeV^{-2}, k_w = 0.00308607 GeV, k_w/k_0 = 0.030)
- For n = 300 iterations, the S-matrix approximation yields ten-figure accuracy in both real and imaginary parts for a 2 GeV incident center-of-mass momentum (Re = -2.83165905257121e-6, Im = 2.37492527262432e-3, exact Re = -2.83165905227843e-6, exact Im = 2.37492527259701e-3)
- Chebyshev polynomial approximations of degree slightly larger than n achieve 10-13 figure accuracy uniformly across the spectrum of exp(-beta H) (e.g., at x = 0.1, n = 200, degree 250 gives poly error 1.925e-11%)
- Approximate transition matrix elements for center-of-mass momenta from 0.1 GeV to 1.9 GeV are all within less than 0.1% of exact results (e.g., at k_0 = 0.1 GeV, Real T = -2.30337e-1, Im T = -4.09325e-1, error 0.0956%)

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean approach to quantum field theory reconstruction developed by Osterwalder and Schrader (1973, 1974) and Frohlich (1974), providing a practical computational framework for few-body systems at the GeV energy scale. It enables direct computation of bound state and scattering observables without analytic continuation, using model Euclidean Green functions as input. The approach builds on the two-Hilbert-space formulation of Haag-Ruelle scattering theory by Coester (1965) and uses the Kato-Birman invariance principle as applied by Chandler (1976), with the key open question being how to construct models that maintain reflection positivity, which is known to be unstable under small perturbations as shown by Wessels (2003).
