# The Renormalisation Group and Applications in Few-Body Physics

> We study the distorted wave renormalisation group (DWRG), a tool for constructing power-counting schemes in systems where some diagrams must be summed to all orders. We solve the DWRG eqaution for a variety of long-range potentials including the Coulomb, Yukawa and inverse square. We derive established results in the case of the Yukawa and Coulomb potentials and new results in the case of the inverse square potential. We generalise the DWRG to systems of three-bodies and use it to find the power-counting for the three-body force in the KSW EFT. This power-counting corresponds to perturbations in the renormalisation group about a limit-cycle. We also derive equations for the LO and NLO KSW EFT distorted waves for three bosons and for three nucleons in the neutron-deuteron spin 1/2 channel. The physical results in the nuclear system agree well, once the LO three-body force is determined, with predictions of more sophistocated potential models.

# The Renormalisation Group and Applications in Few-Body Physics

## Key claim
The distorted wave renormalisation group (DWRG) provides a systematic tool for constructing power-counting schemes in systems where certain diagrams must be summed to all orders, with the power-counting for the three-body force in KSW EFT corresponding to perturbations about a limit-cycle in the renormalisation group flow.

## Method
The author solves the DWRG equation for long-range potentials including Coulomb, Yukawa, and inverse square potentials, deriving both established and new fixed-point solutions. For the three-body system, the Faddeev equations are used to handle disconnected diagrams, and the DWRG is generalised to three identical bosons and three nucleons in the neutron-deuteron spin 1/2 channel. The analysis involves finding fixed-point solutions of the RG equation, perturbing around them to obtain RG eigenvalues, and deriving the distorted wave effective range expansion (DWERE).

## Key numbers
- For the non-trivial fixed point in the two-body system, the RG eigenvalues are ν = 2n - 1 for n = 0, 1, 2, ..., giving values ν = -1, 1, 3, ...
- The scattering length coefficient is given by Ĉ_{-1} = π/(2Λ₀a) and the effective range coefficient by Ĉ₁ = -πΛ₀r_e/4
- The condition for RG flow controlled by the non-trivial fixed point is Λ > π/(2a) = Λ₀Ĉ_{-1}
- For the attractive Coulomb potential, bound states appear at binding energies E_n = |κ|²/n² with wavefunctions ψ_n(r) = √(κ³/n⁵) 2r e^{-κr/n} L_{n-1}¹(2κr/n)
- The three-body momentum perturbations occur at orders 2, 4, 6, ... corresponding to the same orders as the energy perturbations

## Context
This work extends the renormalisation group approach of Birse et al. (2004) for constructing effective field theories in few-body systems. The DWRG method provides a unified framework for understanding both Weinberg power-counting (associated with the trivial fixed point for weakly interacting systems) and KSW power-counting (associated with the non-trivial fixed point for systems with large scattering lengths). The three-body analysis enables systematic treatment of the three-body force in pionless EFT, building on the work of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck on limit-cycle behaviour in three-body systems.
