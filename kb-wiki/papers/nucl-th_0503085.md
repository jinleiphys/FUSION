# Investigation of Zero-Sound Dispersion Equation in the Complex Plane of Frequency

## Key claim

The known solutions to the zero-sound dispersion equation from Landau kinetic theory and random phase approximation (RPA) are systematically classified according to their placement on physical and unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane, with the specific condition that the classification depends on the value of the dimensionless coupling constant \(F_0\) and the wave vector \(k\). The paper shows that all solutions at \(F_0 < 0\) belong to a single continuous family \(\omega_{sd}(k)\) that can move between physical and unphysical sheets as \(F_0\) changes, rather than being separate solution types.

## Method

The author solves the zero-sound dispersion equation \(1 = \mathcal{F} \Pi^{0R}(\omega,k)\) for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature and equilibrium density \(\rho = \rho_0\), using both the Landau kinetic theory (where solutions are linear functions \(\omega = k s \, p_F/m\)) and the microscopic RPA (which yields branches \(\omega(k)\)). The retarded polarization operator \(\Pi^{0R}(\omega,k)\) is analytically continued onto unphysical sheets of the complex frequency plane by adding shifts of \(\pm 2\pi i\) to the logarithms that define the cuts, and the dispersion equation is solved on each sheet for different values of the scalar coupling constant \(F_0\).

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum: \(p_F = 0.268\) GeV; quasiparticle mass: \(m = 0.8 m_0\) with \(m_0 = 0.94\) GeV.
* For \(F_0 = 2\) in RPA, the wave vector at which zero-sound frequency overlaps the particle-hole continuum is \(k_d / p_F = 0.51\).
* For \(F_0 = -1.1\) and \(F_0 = -1.2\), the imaginary branches \(\omega_{sd}(k)\) on the physical sheet reach a maximum growth rate at a finite \(k\) and then go under the cut to the unphysical sheet at a wave vector denoted \(k_{fin}\).
* The stability condition breaks at \(F_0 < -1\), where amplified solutions appear on the imaginary axis of the physical sheet.

## Context

This work extends the classic Landau kinetic theory of zero sound (Landau, 1957) and the RPA treatment (Pines and Nozieres, 1966; Fetter and Walecka, 1971) by providing a unified picture of all solutions in the complex frequency plane, including damped and growing modes. The classification of unphysical sheets \(I\), \(I'\), \(II\), \(II'\), \(\tilde I\), and \(\tilde I'\) enables future study of how meson exchange modifies zero-sound modes and how amplified solutions signal phase transitions such as pion condensation (Migdal, 1978). The results are directly applicable to the spin-isospin channel where the pion dispersion equation is closely related, and the author notes that solutions responsible for pion condensation appear on sheets \(I\) and \(I'\) before moving to the physical sheet.
