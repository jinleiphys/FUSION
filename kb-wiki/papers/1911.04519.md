# Extracting many-body correlators of saturated gluons with precision from inclusive photon+dijet final states in deeply inelastic scattering

> We highlight the principal results of a computation in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (CGC EFT) of the next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor for inclusive photon+dijet production at Bjorken $x_{\rm Bj} \ll 1$ in deeply inelastic electron-nucleus (e+A DIS) collisions. When combined with extant results for next-to-leading log $x_{\rm Bj}$ JIMWLK renormalization group (RG) evolution of gauge invariant two-point ("dipole") and four-point ("quadrupole") correlators of light-like Wilson lines, the inclusive photon+dijet e+A DIS cross-section can be determined to $\sim 10$\% accuracy. Our computation simultaneously provides the ingredients to compute fully inclusive DIS, inclusive photon, inclusive dijet and inclusive photon+jet channels to the same accuracy. This makes feasible quantitative extraction of many-body correlators of saturated gluons and precise determination of the saturation scale $Q_{S,A}(x_{\rm Bj})$ at a future Electron-Ion Collider. An interesting feature of our NLO result is the structure of the violation of the soft gluon theorem in the Regge limit. Another is the appearance in gluon emission of time-like non-global logs which also satisfy JIMWLK RG evolution.

# Extracting many-body correlators of saturated gluons with precision from inclusive photon+dijet final states in deeply inelastic scattering

## Key claim

The inclusive photon+dijet cross-section in deeply inelastic electron-nucleus scattering at small Bjorken x can be computed to approximately 10% accuracy at next-to-leading order plus next-to-leading log x in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, but this statement is modulo the scale and scheme dependence of the results, which remain to be quantified.

## Method

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor for inclusive photon+dijet production in deeply inelastic electron-nucleus scattering within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, using dressed quark and gluon propagators in the A^-=0 light-cone gauge that resum multiple scattering off the nuclear shock wave background. Real and virtual gluon emission diagrams (approximately 400 real emission diagrams alone) are categorized by their color structures, with soft divergences regulated by a cutoff at the initial evolution scale, ultraviolet divergences extracted via dimensional regularization in d=2-epsilon dimensions, and collinear divergences absorbed into a jet algorithm using the small cone approximation. The NLO impact factor is combined with extant results for next-to-leading log x JIMWLK renormalization group evolution of dipole and quadrupole Wilson line correlators to achieve NLO+NLLx accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The inclusive photon+dijet e+A DIS cross-section can be determined to approximately 10% accuracy when the NLO impact factor is combined with NLLx JIMWLK evolution.
- The saturation scale Q_{S,A}(x_Bj) is identified as the emergent scale where gluon phase-space occupancy n becomes of order 1/alpha_S, with alpha_S(Q_S) much less than 1.
- The LO cross-section formula contains the factor alpha_{em}^2 q_f^4 y^2 N_c / (512 pi^5 Q^2) multiplied by 1/(2 pi)^4 and 1/2 times the lepton tensor contracted with the hadron tensor.
- The MV model variance mu_A^2 scales approximately as A^{1/3}, where A is the atomic number.
- The dominant collinear contribution takes the form alpha_S (A ln(R) + B) where A and B are of O(1), with all non-collinearly divergent contributions phase space suppressed by powers of R^2 (jet cone radius R much less than 1).
- The final hadron tensor at O(alpha_S^3 ln(1/x)) accuracy contains a factor (1 + (2 alpha_S C_F/pi) * {-3/4 ln(R^2 |p_{J_perp}| |p_{K_perp}| / (4 z_J z_K Q^2 e^{gamma_E})) + 7/4 - pi^2/6}) multiplying the LO jet hadron tensor.

## Context

This work extends prior NLO studies of deeply inelastic scattering at small x, which focused on fully inclusive DIS (Bartels 2000, Balitsky 2010, Beuf 2011) and on diffractive dijet and exclusive vector meson production (Boussarie 2014, 2016), by computing a more differential final state. The computation provides the ingredients to simultaneously determine cross-sections for fully inclusive DIS, inclusive photon, inclusive dijet, and inclusive photon+jet channels to the same accuracy, enabling quantitative extraction of many-body correlators of saturated gluons and precise determination of the saturation scale at a future Electron-Ion Collider. The results also demonstrate an explicit realization of the conformal spacelike-timelike correspondence noted by Mueller (2018) and identify a violation of the soft gluon theorem in the Regge limit with a color structure (Q - DD) that may be restored by modifying boundary conditions of the quadrupole and dipole operators.
