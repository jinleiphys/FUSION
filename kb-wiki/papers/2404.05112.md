# Quasiparton distributions in massive QED2: Toward quantum computation

> We analyze the quasi-parton distributions of the lightest $η'$ meson in massive two-dimensional Quantum electrodynamics (QED2) by performing a digital quantum simulation on a classical computer (exact diagonalization). The Hamiltonian and boost operators are mapped onto spin qubits in a spatial lattice with open boundary conditions. The lowest excited state in the exact diagonalization is shown to interpolate continuously between an anomalous $η'$ state at strong coupling,and a non-anomalous heavy meson at weak coupling, with a cusp at the critical point. The boosted $η'$ state follows relativistic kinematics but with large deviations in the luminal limit. The spatial quasi-parton distribution function and amplitude for the $η'$ state are computed numerically for increasing rapidity both at strong and weak coupling, and compared to the exact light front results. The numerical results from the boosted form of the spatial parton distributions, compare fairly with the inverse Fourier transformation of the luminal parton distributions, derived in the lowest Fock space approximation. Our analysis points out some of the limitations facing the current lattice program for the parton distributions.

## Key claim

The authors show that the quasiparton distribution functions for the lightest η' meson in massive QED2 can be extracted from a digital quantum simulation using exact diagonalization on a spatial lattice, but the method suffers from large deviations in the luminal limit (v → 1) due to finite lattice size and limited qubit count, with symmetric relative errors exceeding 10% for velocities above approximately 0.83c.

## Method

The Hamiltonian and boost operators of massive QED2 were mapped onto spin qubits using Kogut-Susskind staggered fermions and a Jordan-Wigner transformation on a spatial lattice with open boundary conditions. The lowest excited state was obtained by exact diagonalization with Gauss law enforcement (zero electric charge condition), and the boosted η' state was constructed by applying the exponentiated boost operator. The spatial quasiparton distribution functions and amplitudes were computed numerically for increasing rapidity at both strong and weak coupling, and compared to the exact light front results from the 't Hooft equation with the U(1) anomaly term.

## Key numbers

- The critical point separating strong and weak coupling regimes occurs at approximately m/g ≈ 1/π, consistent with the observed crossing at m/g ∼ 1/3 in the mass gap data for N=20 qubits.
- The symmetric relative error of the boosted η' state dispersion exceeds 10% at v ≳ 0.83 and exceeds 20% at v ≳ 0.91, for N=24 qubits with lattice spacing a=1 and g=1.
- The mass gap at rest for zero current mass in the strong coupling regime is approximately m_s/E_0 ∼ 1, where m_s^2 = g^2/π.
- In the weak coupling regime, the η' mass asymptotes to m_η → 2m, with the fit E/E_0 = (0.33 + 1.99 m)/E_0 for the improved mass.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quasiparton distributions previously carried out in two-dimensional QCD in the large N_c limit by Ji (2018) and in a schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Li (2021, 2022). The authors use massive QED2 (the Schwinger model) as a prototype gauge theory to test the feasibility and numerical limitations of extracting partonic distributions from a digitized lattice formulation, pointing out shortcomings that affect the current lattice QCD program for parton distributions. The results provide benchmarks for future quantum computation of partonic observables, building on prior work by Lamm (2019), Perez-Salinas (2020), and Briceno (2020) on partonic observables in quantum computations.
