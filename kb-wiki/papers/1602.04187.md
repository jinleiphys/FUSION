# Evolution of the jet opening angle distribution in holographic plasma

> We use holography to analyze the evolution of an ensemble of jets, with an initial probability distribution for their energy and opening angle as in proton-proton (pp) collisions, as they propagate through an expanding cooling droplet of strongly coupled plasma as in heavy ion collisions. We identify two competing effects: (i) each individual jet widens as it propagates; (ii) the opening angle distribution for jets emerging from the plasma within any specified range of energies has been pushed toward smaller angles, comparing to pp jets with the same energies. The second effect arises because small-angle jets suffer less energy loss and because jets with a higher initial energy are less probable in the ensemble. We illustrate both effects in a simple two-parameter model, and find that their consequence in sum is that the opening angle distribution for jets in any range of energies contains fewer narrow and wide jets. Either effect can dominate in the mean opening angle, for not unreasonable values of the parameters. So, the mean opening angle for jets with a given energy can easily shift toward smaller angles, as experimental data may indicate, even while every jet in the ensemble broadens.

## Key claim

The authors use holography to show that while every individual jet widens as it propagates through strongly coupled plasma, the opening angle distribution for jets emerging within any specified energy range is pushed toward smaller angles compared to proton-proton jets with the same energies, because small-angle jets suffer less energy loss and higher-energy jets are less probable in the ensemble.

## Method

The authors model jets as open fundamental strings in an anti-de-Sitter (AdS) black hole spacetime, evolving them through an expanding, cooling droplet of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma whose temperature profile mimics a central PbPb collision at the LHC. They initialize an ensemble of strings with a distribution of initial energies proportional to (E_jet^init)^{-6} and opening angles taken from perturbative QCD calculations of the C_1^{(1)} angular variable for quark jets, then propagate each string through the plasma and extract the final energy and opening angle after freeze-out at 175 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy distribution of jets is proportional to (E_jet^init)^{-6}
- The freeze-out temperature is set to 175 MeV (defined with b=0.78)
- The number of participants in a central PbPb collision is N_part approximately 383, with dN_ch/dy approximately 1870 at mid-rapidity in 2.76 ATeV 0-5% centrality PbPb collisions at the LHC
- The blast-wave expansion uses v_T=0.6 and R=6.7 fm, with initialization at tau=1 fm/c
- The model parameter b=0.78 corresponds to the QCD value for multiplicity per entropy S/N_ch approximately 7.25 and S/(T^3 V) approximately 15 at T approximately 300 MeV
- The jet radius parameter R=0.3 is used in the definition of C_1^{(1)}, matching the reconstruction used in CMS measurements

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of single jet propagation in static plasma by Chesler et al. (2014, 2015) to an ensemble of jets with realistic initial distributions from perturbative QCD, and introduces a two-parameter model connecting the holographic opening angle sigma_0 to the QCD observable C_1^{(1)}. The results provide qualitative insights into jet shape modifications seen by CMS (2013, 2015), suggesting that the depletion of wide jets due to their greater energy loss may explain why jets in heavy ion collisions appear narrower than those in pp collisions, even while every individual jet broadens.
