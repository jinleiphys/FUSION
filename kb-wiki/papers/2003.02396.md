# An extended $R^{(2)}_{Ψ_m}(ΔS_2)$ correlator for detecting and characterizing the Chiral Magnetic Wave

> The extended $R^{(2)}_{Ψ_{m}}(ΔS_{2})$ correlator is presented and examined for its efficacy to detect and characterize the quadrupole charge separation ($ΔS_{2}$) associated with the purported Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) produced in heavy-ion collisions. Sensitivity tests involving varying degrees of proxy CMW signals injected into events simulated with the Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT), show that the $R^{(2)}_{Ψ_{m}}(ΔS_{2})$ correlator provides discernible responses for the background- and CMW-driven charge separation. This distinction could aid identification of the CMW via measurements of the $R^{(2)}_{Ψ_{2}}(ΔS_{2})$ and $R^{(2)}_{Ψ_{3}}(ΔS_{2})$ correlators, relative to the second- ($Ψ_2$) and third-order ($Ψ_3$) event planes. The tests also indicate a level of sensitivity that would allow for robust experimental characterization of the CMW signal.

## Key claim

The extended \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S_2)\) correlator can distinguish background-driven quadrupole charge separation from that induced by the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) in heavy-ion collisions, but only when the correlator is evaluated relative to the second-order event plane (\(\Psi_2\)); the correlator relative to the third-order plane (\(\Psi_3\)) is insensitive to the CMW signal because the magnetic field is not strongly correlated with the \(\Psi_3\) orientation.

## Method

The authors injected proxy CMW signals into events simulated with the Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT) for 10–50% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV. The proxy signal was implemented by swapping the positions of in-plane and out-of-plane light quarks for a fraction \(f_q\) of the quarks at the start of the partonic stage, creating a quadrupole charge separation. The \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S_2)\) correlator was then constructed as the ratio of correlation functions for charge separation parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field, with corrections applied for particle number fluctuations and event-plane resolution.

## Key numbers

- The proxy CMW signal strength is characterized by the fraction \(f_q\), with values tested including \(f_q = 0\%\), \(f_q > 0\%\) (specific values not given in the truncated text, but Fig. 3 shows a linear dependence of the slope \(S\) on \(f_q\)).
- The slope \(S\) extracted from \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S_2'')\) vs. \(\Delta S_2''\) distributions shows a linear dependence on \(f_q\) for 10–50% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV.
- For \(f_q = 5\%\), the slope \(S\) extracted from \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_2}\) shows little change across \(q_2\)-selected event classes, while \(v_2\) increases with \(q_2\).
- The slope \(S\) decreases with charge asymmetry \(A_{\mathrm{ch}}\) for \(A_{\mathrm{ch}} < 0\) and can change sign for sufficiently large negative \(A_{\mathrm{ch}}\); slopes for \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_3}\) are insensitive to \(A_{\mathrm{ch}}\).

## Context

This work extends the \(R^{(1)}_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S_1)\) correlator previously proposed by Magdy et al. (2017) for detecting the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) dipole charge separation, to a new correlator \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S_2)\) for the CMW quadrupole charge separation. The approach follows the lead of Shen et al. (2019) and aims to address the significant background influences reported by the ALICE, CMS, and STAR collaborations in prior CMW measurements using the \(\Delta v_2\) method. The results enable future experimental characterization of the CMW via measurements of \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S_2)\) and \(R^{(2)}_{\Psi_3}(\Delta S_2)\) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
