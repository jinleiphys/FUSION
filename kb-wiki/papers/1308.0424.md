# Spin-isospin response of deformed neutron-rich nuclei in a self-consistent Skyrme energy-density-functional approach

> We develop a new framework of the self-consistent deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (pnQRPA), formulated in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) single-quasiparticle basis. The same Skyrme force is used in both the HFB and pnQRPA calculations except in the proton-neutron particle-particle channel, where an S=1 contact force is employed. Numerical application is performed for Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and $β$-decay rates in the deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes located around the path of the rapid-neutron-capture process nucleosynthesis. It is found that the GT strength distributions are fragmented due to deformation. Furthermore we find that the momentum-dependent terms in the particle-hole residual interaction leads to a stronger collectivity of the GT giant resonance. The T=0 pairing enhances the low-lying strengths cooperatively with the T=1 pairing correlation, which shortens the $β$-decay half lives by at most an order of magnitude. The new calculation scheme reproduces well the observed isotopic dependence of the $β$-decay half lives of deformed $^{100-110}$Zr isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully self-consistent deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) framework, built on a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) single-quasiparticle basis solved in coordinate space, can reproduce the observed isotopic dependence of beta-decay half-lives for deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes, but only when the T=0 pairing interaction is included; without it, the calculated half-lives would be up to an order of magnitude longer.

## Method

The authors solved the HFB equations in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with axial and reflection symmetry, employing the SkM* and SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functionals for the mean field and a density-dependent contact interaction for the pairing field. They then solved the pnQRPA equation in matrix formulation using the self-consistent HFB quasiparticle basis, with the same Skyrme force used for the particle-hole residual interaction and a constant S=1 contact force for the proton-neutron particle-particle channel. The quasiparticle energy cutoff was set at 60 MeV, and the calculation was performed for Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta-decay rates in deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes from A=98 to A=112.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength parameter t0' = -240 MeV fm^3 (SkM*) and -290 MeV fm^3 (SLy4) gave neutron pairing gaps of 1.20 MeV and 1.24 MeV in 120Sn, respectively.
- The T=0 pairing strength v0 = -395 MeV fm^3 (SkM*) and -320 MeV fm^3 (SLy4) gave calculated beta-decay half-lives of 7.08 s and 7.63 s for 100Zr, compared to the experimental value of 7.1 s.
- The centroid energy of the GT giant resonance in spherical 98Zr was about 20 MeV, increasing to about 25 MeV in 112Zr.
- The deformation splitting between K=0 and K=1 states of the GT giant resonance was at most 1 MeV.
- The change in centroid energy of the GT giant resonance due to T=0 pairing was 0.14 MeV in 104Zr.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g0' was 0.94 for SkM* and 0.90 for SLy4, compared to the empirical value of about 1.8.
- The slope parameters (kappa_sigma_tau - kappa_tau) fitted for SkM* and SLy4 in deformed Zr isotopes were -16.7 MeV and -15.6 MeV, respectively, compared to the systematic value of -14.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Skyrme-pnQRPA approach to deformed nuclei, building on the deformed like-particle QRPA method developed by Yoshida (2008) and complementing the recent fully self-consistent Skyrme-pnQRPA model in an HFB single-canonical basis by Mustonen and collaborators (2013). It contrasts with earlier work by the Madrid group (Sarazin and collaborators, 1998, 2001, 2012), which used BCS pairing instead of HFB and treated residual interactions in a separable approximation, and with the work of Fracasso and collaborators (2007) who studied momentum-dependent terms in spherical systems. The results enable more reliable predictions of beta-decay half-lives for deformed neutron-rich nuclei along the r-process path, relevant to the recent measurements at RIKEN RIBF by Nishimura and collaborators (2011) and the systematic Skyrme-EDF calculations of deformation by Stoitsov and collaborators (2003).
