# The One-Body and Two-Body Density Matrices of Finite Nuclei and Center-of-Mass Correlations

> A method is presented for the calculation of the one-body and two-body density matrices and their Fourier transforms in momentum space, that is consistent with the requirement for translational invariance, in the case of a nucleus (a finite self-bound system). We restore translational invariance by using the so-called fixed center-of-mass approximation for constructing an intrinsic nuclear ground state wavefunction by starting from a non-translationally invariant wavefunction and applying a projection prescription. We discuss results for the one-body and two-body momentum distributions of the 4He nucleus calculated with the Slater determinant of the harmonic oscillator orbitals, as the initial non-translationally invariant wavefunction. Effects of such an inclusion of CM correlations are found to be quite important in the momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for calculating one-body and two-body density matrices and their momentum-space Fourier transforms that restores translational invariance in finite nuclei, but the results shown are limited to the specific case of 4He using a simple harmonic-oscillator Slater determinant as the starting non-translationally-invariant wavefunction, with the authors noting that center-of-mass correlation effects are "quite important" in the momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors restore translational invariance by applying the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler fixed-center-of-mass projection prescription, which constructs an intrinsic nuclear ground state wavefunction from a non-translationally-invariant wavefunction. They evaluate the intrinsic one-body and two-body momentum distributions using an algebraic technique based on the Cartesian representation, where coordinate and momentum operators are expressed as linear combinations of creation and destruction operators for oscillating quanta. The method is applied to the 4He nucleus using a Slater determinant of harmonic oscillator orbitals as the initial non-translationally-invariant wavefunction, with the oscillator parameter determined by fitting the experimental charge rms radius.

## Key numbers

- Experimental charge rms radius of 4He: 1.67 fm
- Proton rms radius used: 0.8 fm
- Harmonic oscillator parameter without CM corrections: b0 = 1.197 fm
- Harmonic oscillator parameter with CM corrections: bcm = 1.382 fm (where bcm = sqrt(4/3) * b0)
- For the simple harmonic oscillator model without CM correlations, the correlation measure xi = 1 - 1/A = 0.75 for all momenta p and k
- The CM-corrected one-body momentum distribution: eta_EST(p) = 4 * (4^(3/2) * bcm^3) / (3^(3/2) * pi^(3/2)) * exp(-(4/3) * p^2 * bcm^2)
- The CM-corrected two-body momentum distribution: eta^[2]_EST(p,k) = 12 * (2^(3/2) * bcm^6) / pi^3 * exp(-(3/2) * p^2 * bcm^2) * exp(-(3/2) * k^2 * bcm^2) * exp(-p·k * bcm^2)

## Context

This work extends the treatment of center-of-mass correlations in finite nuclei following the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler fixed-CM approximation (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1973) and earlier work by Dieperink and collaborators (1975). The method enables more reliable extraction of intrinsic nuclear properties from experimental data on electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission reactions such as (gamma, NN) and (e,e'NN) being studied at facilities like ELSA, MAMI, and JLab. The authors plan to extend the formalism to include Jastrow-type short-range correlations and to apply it to other light Z=N nuclei.
