# Hadronization using the Wigner function approach for a multiphase transport model

> In the string melting version of a multiphase transport model, the hadronization algorithm has been improved by favoring parton combinations close in not only coordinate space but also momentum space. Formation probabilities of mesons, baryons, and antibaryons during hadronization are determined by their corresponding Wigner functions for the valence parton combinations with no free parameters, and the net baryon, electric, and strangeness charges are conserved during quark coalescence. Effects of the hadronization on anisotropic flows, collision energy dependence of the proton directed flow, relative particle yield ratios, as well as di-hadron correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the energies ranging from RHIC beam energy scan to LHC have been extensively discussed.

# Hadronization using the Wigner function approach for a multiphase transport model

## Key claim

The improved hadronization algorithm in the string melting version of the AMPT model, which uses Wigner functions to determine coalescence probabilities based on both coordinate and momentum space distances among partons, preserves better information of partonic dynamics than the original spatial coalescence approach. The authors find that this improvement is essential for reproducing the sign change of the proton directed flow slope at RHIC-BES energies and the near-side anti-correlation structure in baryon-baryon correlations in p+p collisions, though the model still cannot simultaneously reproduce meson-to-baryon and baryon-to-antibaryon yield ratios at all RHIC-BES energies.

## Method

The authors replaced the original spatial coalescence algorithm in the AMPT model with a dynamical coalescence approach where formation probabilities for mesons, baryons, and antibaryons are determined by Wigner functions of the valence parton combinations, using Gaussian width parameters fixed by the RMS radii of pions (0.61 fm) and protons (0.877 fm) with no free parameters. Instead of combining nearest partons in coordinate space, the algorithm selects parton combinations with the largest Wigner function value in their center-of-mass frame, and the preference to form mesons versus baryons is determined entirely by the phase-space distance among constituent partons. The hadronization procedure iterates until all freeze-out partons are used up, conserving net baryon, electric, and strangeness charges.

## Key numbers

- RMS radius of 0.61 fm for pi+ and 0.877 fm for protons used to determine Wigner function widths
- Parton scattering cross section of 3 mb needed in improved AMPT at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (versus 1.5 mb in original) to reproduce elliptic flow data
- Parton scattering cross section of 2 mb needed in improved AMPT at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV (versus 1.5 mb in original)
- Slope of freeze-out parton directed flow changes sign from positive to negative between sqrt(s_NN)=11.5 GeV and 19.6 GeV for both original and improved AMPT
- Improved AMPT yields meson-to-baryon ratio of 2.19 at 7.7 GeV, 3.82 at 11.5 GeV, 5.16 at 19.6 GeV, 5.72 at 27 GeV, and 6.18 at 39 GeV
- Improved AMPT yields baryon-to-antibaryon ratio of 95.24 at 7.7 GeV, 28.36 at 11.5 GeV, 9.64 at 19.6 GeV, 5.54 at 27 GeV, and 3.56 at 39 GeV
- Lund string fragmentation parameters: a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV^{-2} for Au+Au at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV; a=2.2, b=0.5 GeV^{-2} for Au+Au from 7.7 to 39 GeV

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model hadronization framework previously developed by Lin (2005) and the spatial coalescence approach, incorporating the dynamical coalescence formalism of Greco, Greco, Fries, and Hwa (2003-2005). It builds on earlier studies by Zhang (2016) showing hadronization impacts on anisotropic flow scaling relations, and by Guo (2017, 2018) demonstrating that hadronization significantly affects proton directed flow. The improved algorithm removes the ambiguity of coalescence ordering present in the original AMPT and the parameter controlling meson-versus-baryon preference introduced by He and Lin (2017). The results enable more reliable extraction of QGP properties from transport model comparisons to experimental data at RHIC and LHC energies, though the authors note that reproducing relative particle yield ratios at RHIC-BES energies may require modifying initial quark components or introducing inelastic partonic scatterings.
