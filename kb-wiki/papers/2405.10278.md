# Locating the critical point for the hadron to quark-gluon plasma phase transition from finite-size scaling of proton cumulants in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform a finite-size scaling analysis of net-proton number cumulants in Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies between $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 2.4$ GeV and 54.4 GeV to search for evidence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram. In our analysis, we use both susceptibility and Binder cumulants which we extract from the second and fourth moments of the net-proton number distributions. We take measurements in different rapidity bin widths, corresponding to different subvolumes of the system, as probes of different length scales. We use model simulations to verify the applicability of this approach, then apply it to data and find evidence for a critical point near the baryon chemical potential of $μ_{B} \approx 625$ MeV and temperature of $T \approx 140$ MeV. The Binder cumulants, also analyzed in varying rapidity bin widths, provide complementary evidence for a critical point in a similar region. This is the first analysis of experimental data to locate the critical point in a range consistent with theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have located the QCD critical point near baryon chemical potential μB ≈ 625 MeV and temperature T ≈ 140 MeV, but the result carries a systematic uncertainty of ±60 MeV in μB and depends on the assumption that finite-size scaling (FSS) is applicable to rapidly expanding heavy-ion collision systems, which the authors acknowledge may be questioned due to the short lifetime of the collisions and the presence of short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors performed a finite-size scaling analysis of net-proton number cumulants from Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies between √sNN = 2.4 GeV and 54.4 GeV, using data from the STAR and HADES experiments. They extracted the second-order susceptibility χ2 and Binder cumulants from the second and fourth moments of net-proton distributions measured in different rapidity bin widths, which serve as proxies for different subvolume sizes, and searched for data collapse when scaling χ2W−γ/ν as a function of W1/ν(μB−μB,c)/μB,c using 3-D Ising universality class critical exponents (γ = 1.237, ν = 0.630). The analysis was validated using model simulations of uniform nuclear matter in a periodic box with a known critical point.

## Key numbers

* Critical point located at baryon chemical potential μB,c = 625 ± 60 MeV and temperature Tc ≈ 140 ± 13 MeV, extracted from finite-size scaling of net-proton cumulants in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 2.4 to 54.4 GeV.
* Best fit to data at μB < μB,c using all data from 7.7 to 54.4 GeV yields μB,c = 635+16−12 MeV; excluding 39 and 54.4 GeV data yields μB,c = 662+22−20 MeV.
* Using mean-field critical exponents (γ = 1.0, ν = 0.5) yields μB,c = 565 MeV.
* Using μfo/Tfo as the independent variable gives (μfo/Tfo)c = 4.45+0.12−0.08, with best fit parameters a = 3.65, b = −1.26 for the scaling function f(x) = a(−x)b.
* The 2.4 and 3.0 GeV data (μfo = 776 and 720 MeV, respectively) could not be made to collapse on a common scaling curve with higher energy data or with each other.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-size scaling analyses of heavy-ion collisions by Palhares (2009), Fraga (2011), and Lacey (2016), which varied system size through collision centrality classes, but introduces the novel approach of using rapidity bin widths as subvolume probes within the same collision system. The result is consistent with recent theoretical predictions from Lee-Yang edge singularity analyses by Basar (2023), functional renormalization group methods by Fu (2019), and a holographic QCD model by Hippert (2023), which predict critical points near (T, μB) = (100, 580) MeV, (107, 635) MeV, and (104±3, 589±36) MeV respectively. The analysis enables future joint interpretation of data from experiments with different detector acceptances and motivates further experimental study at μB > 700 MeV, where the nuclear liquid-gas critical point near μB ≈ 900 MeV and T ≈ 15 MeV (Pochodzalla, 1995; Moretto, 2002) may interfere with the QCD critical point signal.
