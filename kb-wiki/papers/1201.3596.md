# S-wave scattering of strangeness -3 baryons

> We explore the interactions of two strangeness -3 baryons in multiple spin channels with lattice QCD. This system provides an ideal laboratory for exploring the interactions of multi-baryon systems with minimal dependence on light quark masses. Model calculations of the two-$Ω^-$ system in two previous works have obtained conflicting results, which can be resolved by lattice QCD. The lattice calculations are performed using two different volumes with $L\sim2.5$ and 3.9 fm on anisotropic clover lattices at $m_π\sim 390$ MeV with a lattice spacing of $a_s \sim 0.123$ fm in the spatial direction and $a_t\sim{a}_s/3.5$ in the temporal direction. Using multiple interpolating operators from a non-displaced source, we present scattering information for two ground state $Ω^-$ baryons in both the S=0 and S=2 channels. For S=0, $k\cotδ$ is extracted at two volumes, which lead to an extrapolated scattering length of $a^{ΩΩ}_{S=0}=0.16 \pm 0.22 \ \text{fm}$, indicating a weakly repulsive interaction. Additionally, for S=2, two separate highly repulsive states are observed. We also present results on the interactions of the excited strangeness -3, spin-1/2 states with the ground spin-3/2 states for the spin-1 and spin-2 channels. Results for these interactions are consistent with attractive behavior.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of the two-$\Omega^-$ system at a pion mass of approximately 390 MeV resolve conflicting model predictions, finding a weakly repulsive interaction in the S=0 channel rather than the deeply bound state predicted by the chiral quark model or the repulsive interaction predicted by the quark dislocation model.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on anisotropic clover lattices with spatial lattice spacing $a_s \sim 0.123$ fm and temporal spacing $a_t \sim a_s/3.5$, using two volumes with $L \sim 2.5$ fm and $L \sim 3.9$ fm at $m_\pi \sim 390$ MeV. They constructed two-$\Omega^-$ correlation functions using multiple interpolating operators from non-displaced sources, projected onto the appropriate lattice irreducible representations ($A_1^+$ for S=0, $E^+$ and $T_2^+$ for S=2), and extracted energy shifts via a matrix-Prony analysis of shell-shell and shell-point correlators to obtain scattering parameters through the Luescher finite-volume method.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length for the S=0 channel: $a^{\Omega\Omega}_{S=0} = 0.16 \pm 0.22$ fm, indicating a weakly repulsive interaction.
- Lattice spatial extents: $L \sim 2.5$ fm ($20^3 \times 256$ ensemble) and $L \sim 3.9$ fm ($32^3 \times 256$ ensemble).
- Pion mass: $m_\pi \sim 390$ MeV.
- Lattice spacing: $a_s \sim 0.123$ fm in the spatial direction, with anisotropy $a_s/a_t \sim 3.5$.
- For the S=2 channel, two separate highly repulsive states were observed (no numerical values for the repulsion strength are provided in the text).

## Context
This work directly addresses conflicting model predictions for the $\Omega\Omega$ system: the chiral quark model of Zhang (2000) predicted a bound state with binding energy of approximately 116 MeV, while the quark dislocation model of Wang (1995) predicted a weakly repulsive interaction with $\Delta E = 43 \pm 18$ MeV. The lattice QCD result supports the weakly repulsive scenario. The study extends lattice techniques for multi-baryon scattering, previously applied to systems like the deuteron and $\Xi\Xi$ system by Beane (2011), to the strangeness -3 sector. Future work can use this system to probe tensor interactions and three-baryon forces with minimal dependence on light quark masses, and to develop effective field theories for multi-$\Omega^-$ systems.
