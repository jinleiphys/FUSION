# Effective field theory approach for the M1 properties of A=2 and 3 nuclei

> The magnetic moments of ${}^2{H}$, ${}^3{He}$ and ${}^3{H}$ as well as the thermal neutron capture rate on the proton are calculated using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory {\it à la} Weinberg. The M1 operators have been derived up to {N$^3$LO}. The nuclear matrix elements are evaluated with the use of wave functions obtained by carrying out variational Monte Carlo calculations for a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian involving high-precision phenomenological potentials like Argonne Av18 and Urbana IX tri-nucleon interactions. We discuss the potential- and cutoff-dependence of the results.

## Key claim

The authors calculate magnetic moments of A=2 and 3 nuclei and the thermal neutron capture rate on the proton using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N3LO, but the results for the A=3 magnetic moments show a 2-3% discrepancy with experimental values, which they attribute to possible inaccuracies in the variational Monte Carlo wave functions and neglected N4LO three-body currents.

## Method

The M1 operators were derived up to N3LO in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one-body, one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and contact-term contributions. Nuclear matrix elements were evaluated using variational Monte Carlo wave functions obtained from realistic nuclear Hamiltonians that include high-precision phenomenological potentials such as Argonne Av18 and Urbana IX tri-nucleon interactions. The two unknown contact-term coefficients were fixed by reproducing the experimental deuteron magnetic moment and the thermal neutron capture cross section on the proton.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic moment: 0.8574 μ_N (used as input to fix contact terms)
- Thermal neutron capture cross section: 332.6 ± 0.7 mb (used as input to fix contact terms)
- Calculated ³H magnetic moment: 3.035 ± 0.013 μ_N (Av18+U9, Λ=600 MeV) versus experimental 2.979 μ_N
- Calculated ³He magnetic moment: -2.198 ± 0.013 μ_N (Av18+U9, Λ=600 MeV) versus experimental -2.128 μ_N
- Calculated sum μ(³H)+μ(³He): 0.838 (Av18+U9, Λ=600 MeV) versus experimental 0.851
- Calculated difference μ(³H)-μ(³He): 5.233 ± 0.025 (Av18+U9, Λ=600 MeV) versus experimental 5.107
- Binding energy of ³H with Av18+U9: 8.24 ± 0.01 MeV (VMC) versus experimental 8.48 MeV
- Binding energy of ³He with Av18+U9: 7.48 ± 0.01 MeV (VMC) versus experimental 7.72 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MEEFT (More Effective Effective Field Theory) approach previously applied to the hep process by Park et al. (2003) and to the isoscalar amplitude of n+p→d+γ by Park et al. (1996). It provides the first EFT calculation of the magnetic moments of A=3 systems, demonstrating that the method yields results largely independent of the choice of phenomenological potential (Av18 vs Av14, with or without three-nucleon forces) and cutoff (500-800 MeV). The results enable future calculations of the ³He+n→⁴He+γ reaction and motivate inclusion of N4LO three-body currents to resolve the remaining 2-3% discrepancy with experiment.
