# An Exactly Solvable Supersymmetric Model of Semimagic Nuclei

> A simple model of nucleons coupled to angular momentum zero (s-pairs) occupying the valance shell of a semi-magic nuclei is considered. The model has a separable, orbit dependent pairing interaction which dominates over the kinetic term. It is shown that such an interaction leads to an exactly solvable model whose (0+) eigenstates and energies can be computed very easily with the help of the algebraic Bethe ansatz method. It is also shown that the model has a supersymmetry which connects the spectra of some semimagic nuclei. The results obtained from this model for the semimagic Ni isotopes from 58Ni to 68Ni are given. In addition, a new and easier technique for calculating the energy eigenvalues from the Bethe ansatz equations is also presented.

# An Exactly Solvable Supersymmetric Model of Semimagic Nuclei

## Key claim
The paper shows that a separable, orbit-dependent pairing interaction in the degenerate limit (where all single-particle energy levels are equal) yields an exactly solvable model for semimagic nuclei, with a supersymmetry connecting spectra of nuclei with fewer than half-filled valence shells to those with more than half-filled shells. This supersymmetry is broken away from the degenerate limit, meaning the exact solvability and the spectral connections hold only when the kinetic term is negligible compared to the pairing interaction.

## Method
The authors consider a Hamiltonian of nucleons coupled to angular momentum zero (s-pairs) in the valence shell of semimagic nuclei, with a separable pairing interaction whose orbit dependence is encoded in dimensionless constants \(c_j\) and overall strength \(|G|\). In the degenerate limit where all single-particle energies are equal, the Hamiltonian reduces to a product form \(\hat{H}_D = -|G| (\sum_j c^*_j S_j^+)(\sum_{j'} c_{j'} S_{j'}^-)\). Exact eigenstates and energies are obtained using the algebraic Bethe ansatz method, where states are constructed as products of generalized pair creation operators \(S^+(x)\) acting on the vacuum, with parameters \(x\) satisfying Bethe ansatz equations. A supersymmetry is identified via operators \(\hat{B}^+\) and \(\hat{B}^-\) that commute with each other and allow the Hamiltonian to be written as \(\hat{H}_D = -|G| \hat{B}^- \hat{B}^+\), connecting states with \(N\) particle-pairs to states with \(N-1\) hole-pairs. For the two-orbit case, the energy eigenvalues are obtained more easily by solving Heun's differential equation for polynomial solutions.

## Key numbers
- The model is applied to Ni isotopes from \(^{58}\)Ni to \(^{68}\)Ni, with the core being the doubly magic \(^{56}\)Ni nucleus.
- The pairing strength \(|G|\) is chosen to reproduce the ground state energy of \(^{58}\)Ni relative to the \(^{56}\)Ni core in agreement with experimental data.
- All calculated energies are given in MeV relative to the \(^{56}\)Ni core (see Figure 2 in the paper for the exact spectrum).
- The zero energy states of \(^{58}\)Ni and \(^{60}\)Ni are doubly degenerate, indicated by double lines in the figure.
- The spectrum of \(^{64}\)Ni is the same as the spectrum of \(^{60}\)Ni except that the zero energy states are absent for \(^{64}\)Ni.

## Context
This work extends the exactly solvable pairing models developed by Pan (1997), Balantekin (2007), and others, who showed that the degenerate-limit pairing Hamiltonian is integrable via the Bethe ansatz. The new contribution is the identification of a supersymmetry connecting particle-pair and hole-pair spectra, which allows the calculation of energies for nuclei with more than half-filled shells (e.g., \(^{64}\)Ni–\(^{68}\)Ni) from those with fewer than half-filled shells (e.g., \(^{58}\)Ni–\(^{62}\)Ni). The results are compared with experimental data from Auerbach (references in the paper). The method enables future calculations of pairing energies in semimagic nuclei without solving the full shell model, and the Heun equation approach for two orbits provides a simpler computational route that may be extendable to more orbits.
