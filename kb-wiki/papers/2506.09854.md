# Baryon-antibaryon generalized distribution amplitudes and $e^+ e^- \to B \bar{B} γ$

> Baryon-antibaryon generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) give an access to timelike gravitational form factors (GFFs) which are complementary to the spacelike ones which can be deduced from the hadronic generalized parton distributions (GPDs) measured in deep exclusive electroproduction processes. They allow to probe the GFFs of unstable baryons in the baryon octet, since the second moments of hadronic generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) lead to the timelike GFFs. These GDAs can be measured in the process $e^+ e^- \to B \bar{B} γ$, in the generalized Bjorken regime where the invariant mass of the $B \bar{B}$ pair is near threshold at high energy facilities, such as BESIII, Belle II, and the proposed Super Tau-Charm Facility. In this work, we investigate this process using the QCD collinear factorization framework, where the scattering amplitudes are expressed in terms of the baryon timelike electromagnetic (EM) FFs and Compton FFs. We also provide a numerical estimate of the cross sections with a model for baryon-antibaryon GDAs. Our work provides us a possibility to extract the timelike baryon GFFs from near future experimental measurements, and these GFFs may be further used to study longstanding questions in hadronic physics such as the baryon spin decomposition and D-term.

## Key claim

Baryon-antibaryon generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) can be accessed through the process \(e^+ e^- \to B \bar{B} \gamma\) in the generalized Bjorken regime, provided the photon virtuality is much larger than the invariant mass of the baryon pair, enabling extraction of timelike gravitational form factors (GFFs) for unstable baryons in the octet.

## Method

The authors apply QCD collinear factorization at leading twist and leading order in \(\alpha_s\) to the process \(e^+ e^- \to B \bar{B} \gamma\), separating the amplitude into a \(C\)-even QCD subprocess described by baryon-antibaryon GDAs and a \(C\)-odd initial state radiation (ISR) subprocess described by timelike electromagnetic form factors. They model the unknown proton-antiproton GDAs by analogy with the known pion-pion GDAs, using the pion vector GDA parametrization from Kumano (2017) with the proton mass substituted for the pion mass. The timelike electromagnetic form factors for the proton are taken from a global fit by Tomasi-Gustafsson (2020) that includes a three-pole component and an oscillating term.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy of the \(e^+ e^-\) system: \(\sqrt{s} = 4\) GeV, chosen as typical for BESIII and the proposed Super Tau-Charm Facility (STCF).
- Invariant mass range for the proton-antiproton pair: \(2.0\) GeV \(< W = \sqrt{\hat{s}} < 2.7\) GeV, to satisfy the QCD factorization condition \(s \gg \hat{s}\).
- Designed peak luminosity of STCF: \(0.5 \times 10^{35}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) or higher.
- Parameters for the effective proton electromagnetic form factor model: \(F_0 = 9.7\), \(m_0^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\), \(m_a^2 = 7.1\) GeV\(^2\), \(A = 0.073\), \(B = 1.05\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(C = 5.51\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(D = 0.04\).
- Parameters for the ratio \(R(\hat{s}) = |G_E|/|G_M|\): \(r_0 = 3\), \(r_1 = 0.5\), \(r_2 = 1.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(r_3 = 9.3\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Axial charges from lattice QCD (Alexandrou, 2024): \(g_A^u = 0.832\), \(g_A^d = -0.417\).
- Parameter \(\Lambda\) in the GDA model: \(\Lambda = 1.928\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of generalized distribution amplitudes from meson pairs, where Belle (2015, 2017) and BaBar (2015) measured \(\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar{M}\) and \(\gamma^* \to M \bar{M} \gamma\), to baryon-antibaryon pairs. It builds on the formalism for baryon GDAs first proposed by Diehl (2002) for \(\gamma \gamma \to B \bar{B}\) and on the meson GDA analysis of Lu (2006) for \(\gamma^* \to M \bar{M} \gamma\). The results enable future extraction of timelike gravitational form factors for unstable baryons from measurements at BESIII, Belle II, and the proposed Super Tau-Charm Facility, which can be related via dispersion relations to spacelike GFFs and used to study baryon spin decomposition and the D-term.
