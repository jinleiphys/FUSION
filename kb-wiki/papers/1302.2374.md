# Extended evolution equations for neutrino propagation in astrophysical and cosmological environments

> We derive the evolution equations for a system of neutrinos interacting among themselves and with a matter background, based upon the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy. This theoretical framework gives an (unclosed) set of first-order coupled integro-differential equations governing the evolution of the reduced density matrices. By employing the hierarchy, we first rederive the mean-field evolution equations for the neutrino one-body density matrix associated with a system of neutrinos and antineutrinos interacting with matter and with an anisotropic neutrino background. Then, we derive extended evolution equations to determine neutrino flavor conversion beyond the commonly used mean-field approximation. To this aim we include neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations to the two-body density matrix. The inclusion of these new contributions leads to an extended evolution equation for the normal neutrino density and to an equation for the abnormal one involving the pairing mean-field. We discuss the possible impact of neutrino-antineutrino correlations on neutrino flavor conversion in the astrophysical and cosmological environments, and possibly upon the supernova dynamics. Our results can be easily generalized to an arbitrary number of neutrino families.

## Key claim

The paper derives extended evolution equations for neutrino flavor conversion beyond the commonly used mean-field approximation by including neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations in the two-body density matrix, which have been neglected in prior work based on the argument that their expectation values over free states oscillate fast around zero.

## Method

The authors employ the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy, which replaces the Liouville von-Neumann equation for the many-body density matrix with an unclosed set of coupled integro-differential equations for reduced density matrices. They first truncate the hierarchy at lowest order to rederive the mean-field evolution equations for the neutrino one-body density matrix including interactions with matter and an anisotropic neutrino background. Then they include neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations in the two-body correlation function, leading to extended time-dependent mean-field equations for both a normal and an abnormal neutrino density matrix involving the pairing mean-field.

## Key numbers

* The mean-field contribution from neutrino interaction with matter (MSW effect) is proportional to the electron number density: \(\Gamma_{\nu_e}(\rho_e) = (2 \pi)^3 2 E_{k} \delta^3(\vec{k}-\vec{k}') \sqrt{2} G_F n_e\), where \(G_F\) is the Fermi coupling constant and \(n_e\) is the electron number density.
* The total electron number is given by \(N_e = 2 V \int {{d^3 \vec{p}} \over{(2 \pi)^3}} \rho^e_{\vec{p}}\), where the factor of 2 comes from summation over electron helicity states and \(V\) is the quantization volume.
* The anticommutation relations for particle operators are \(\{ a(\vec{p},h), a^{\dagger}(\vec{p}\,',h') \} = (2 \pi)^3 2 E_{p}\delta^3(\vec{p} - \vec{p}\,')\delta_{hh'}\).

## Context

This work extends prior formulations of neutrino evolution equations, including the mean-field approach of Pantaleone (1992) and the Boltzmann-type equations with collision terms derived by Raffelt and Sigl (1992) and Sigl and Raffelt (1992). The BBGKY hierarchy framework allows the authors to go beyond the mean-field approximation by including neutrino-antineutrino pairing correlations, which have been neglected in earlier works such as Sigl and Raffelt (1992) and Cardall (2007). The results can be applied to study neutrino flavor conversion in core-collapse supernovae and the early universe, and may impact understanding of supernova dynamics.
