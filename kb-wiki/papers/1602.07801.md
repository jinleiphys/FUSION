# Investigating multi-channel quantum tunneling in heavy-ion fusion reactions with Bayesian spectral deconvolution

> Excitations of colliding nuclei during a nuclear reaction considerably affect fusion cross sections at energies around the Coulomb barrier. It has been demonstrated that such channel coupling effects can be represented in terms of a distribution of multiple fusion barriers. We here apply a Bayesian approach to analyze the so called fusion barrier distributions. This method determines simultaneously the barrier parameters and the number of barriers. We particularly investigate the $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm and $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm systems in order to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method. The present analysis indicates that the fusion barrier distribution for the former system is most consistent with three fusion barriers, even though the experimental data show only two distinct peaks.

## Key claim
The Bayesian spectral deconvolution method determines that the fusion barrier distribution for the $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm system is most consistent with three effective barriers, even though the experimental data show only two distinct peaks; this conclusion holds even when the data point at E = 60.64 MeV is excluded from the fitting.

## Method
The author applied Bayesian spectral deconvolution to fusion barrier distributions defined as $d^2(E\sigma_{\rm fus})/dE^2$, using a test function based on the Wong formula for each effective barrier. The method uses the exchange Monte Carlo algorithm to evaluate the high-dimensional integral for the stochastic complexity (free energy), which determines the most probable number of barriers $K$ by balancing chi-square against model complexity. The analysis was performed on experimental fusion cross section data for the $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm and $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm systems from Leigh (1995).

## Key numbers
- For the $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm system, the most probable number of barriers is $K=3$, with barrier heights of $59.5\pm0.0789$ MeV, $61.5\pm0.153$ MeV, and $65.3\pm0.251$ MeV.
- For the $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm system, the most probable number of barriers is $K=5$, with the lowest barrier at $53.3\pm1.10$ MeV and the highest at $62.4\pm0.751$ MeV.
- In a proof-of-principle test with hypothetical data generated for $K=2$, the correct value $K=2$ was selected 78 times out of 100 independent data sets.
- The posteriori probability for the $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm system was $P(K=2|D_{\rm exp})=0.550$, $P(K=3|D_{\rm exp})=0.266$, $P(K=4|D_{\rm exp})=0.158$, and $P(K=5|D_{\rm exp})=0.0264$.

## Context
This work extends the standard coupled-channels approach to subbarrier fusion reactions (Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson, 1991; Dasgupta et al., 1998) by providing a Bayesian method to determine the number of effective barriers without overfitting. The finding of three barriers for $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm is consistent with double-octupole phonon excitations in the $^{144}$Sm nucleus, for which anharmonicity plays an important role (Hagino, Takigawa, and Kuyucak, 1997). The five-barrier result for $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm confirms the rotational nature of target excitations, indicating that excitations up to the $8^+$ state are important. The method enables future application to quasi-elastic barrier distributions (Timmers et al., 1995) for consistency checks between different reaction processes.
