# Onset Transition to Cold Nuclear Matter from Lattice QCD with Heavy Quarks

> Lattice QCD at finite density suffers from a severe sign problem, which has so far prohibited simulations of the cold and dense regime. Here we study the onset of nuclear matter employing a three-dimensional effective theory derived by combined strong coupling and hopping expansions, which is valid for heavy but dynamical quarks and has a mild sign problem only. Its numerical evaluations agree between a standard Metropolis and complex Langevin algorithm, where the latter is free of the sign problem. Our continuum extrapolated data clearly show a first order phase transition building up at $μ_B \approx m_B$ as the temperature approaches zero. An excellent description of the data is achieved by an analytic solution in the strong coupling limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the silver blaze property (zero baryon density for chemical potentials below the baryon mass) and a first-order phase transition to nuclear matter at zero temperature can be realized in lattice QCD with heavy quarks, using a three-dimensional effective theory derived from strong coupling and hopping expansions. The claim is hedged by the restriction to heavy quarks (hopping parameter κ ≲ 10⁻³) and the observation that the effective theory breaks down for lattice spacings below about 0.09 fm.

## Method

The authors derive a three-dimensional effective action for Polyakov loops from full four-dimensional lattice QCD with Wilson fermions, using combined strong coupling and hopping expansions valid for heavy quarks. They evaluate the partition function numerically using both a standard Metropolis algorithm with reweighting (to handle the mild sign problem) and a complex Langevin algorithm (which is sign-problem-free), finding agreement between the two methods. The zero-temperature limit is approached by simulating at small temperatures (down to 2.5 MeV) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ up to 116, and continuum extrapolations are performed using data from nine gauge couplings corresponding to lattice spacings between 0.07 fm and 0.17 fm.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density jumps from zero to saturation at μB ≈ mB in the static strong coupling limit, with saturation density a³n = 2Nc = 6 for Nc = 3 colors.
- Simulations at T = 10 MeV and mπ = 20 GeV (β = 5.7, κ = 0.0000887, Nτ = 116, a = 0.17 fm) show baryon density consistent with zero until μB approaches mB/3, then rising quickly to saturation.
- The effective gauge coupling λ(β = 5.7, Nτ = 115) ≈ 10⁻²⁷, showing the pure gauge sector is negligible for cold QCD.
- Continuum extrapolations are controlled for lattice spacings a ≳ 0.09 fm; data for a < 0.09–0.11 fm are excluded from extrapolations.
- The physical nuclear density (≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ ≈ 0.15 × 10⁻² m_proton³) is of the same order of magnitude as the saturation density expressed in units of mB.
- At T = 2.5 MeV, the onset is still a smooth crossover (not first order), and for physical quark masses the onset transition persists up to T ≈ 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory approach developed by the same group (Fromm et al., 2012) for the deconfinement transition to the cold and dense regime, where full four-dimensional lattice QCD simulations are prohibited by the sign problem. The silver blaze property was previously demonstrated analytically for finite isospin chemical potential (Cohen, 2004) and numerically for complex scalar fields using Langevin dynamics (Aarts, 2009) and worm algorithms (Gattringer, 2011). The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram at low temperatures and high densities, with planned extensions to include κ⁴ corrections and lighter quark masses.
