# Plane correlations in small colliding systems

> I propose event-plane correlations as a test of collectivity in small colliding systems: In d-Au and $^3$He-Au collisions, I predict a strong anti-correlation between elliptic and triangular flow, generated by the geometry of the light projectile. A significant anti-correlation is also predicted in central p-Pb collisions at the LHC, which is solely generated by fluctuations. Similar, but stronger correlation patterns are predicted in correlations involving dipolar flow.

# Plane correlations in small colliding systems

## Key claim
The paper predicts that event-plane correlations, specifically a strong anti-correlation between elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$), can serve as a test of medium collectivity in small colliding systems, but the strength of this correlation depends critically on whether the initial-state geometry is dominated by fluctuations (as in p-Pb) or by an intrinsic background shape (as in d-Au and $^3$He-Au).

## Method
The author performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations using the PHOBOS model with Gaussian wounding profiles and negative binomial distribution (NBD) weighting for entropy production, generating ten million events each for p-Pb, d-Au, and $^3$He-Au systems. Initial-state anisotropies (ellipticity $\varepsilon_2$, triangularity $\varepsilon_3$, and dipolar asymmetry $\varepsilon_1$) were computed from the initial entropy density profiles, and correlation coefficients analogous to Pearson correlations were defined to quantify the mixing between different harmonic orders. An analytical independent-source model was also developed to understand the scaling of correlations with the number of sources and the role of background geometry.

## Key numbers
- In central p-Pb collisions at the LHC, the correlation coefficient $|\mathcal{P}_{23}^{\varepsilon\varepsilon}|$ between ellipticity and triangularity reaches approximately 10% from simulations with NBD weighting.
- For d-Au collisions, the anti-correlation between $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ is stronger than for $^3$He-Au, following the hierarchy $|\mathcal{P}_{23}^{\varepsilon\varepsilon}(\text{p-Pb})| < |\mathcal{P}_{23}^{\varepsilon\varepsilon}(^3\text{He-Au})| < |\mathcal{P}_{23}^{\varepsilon\varepsilon}(\text{d-Au})|$.
- In the independent-source model for p-Pb, the leading contribution to $\mathcal{P}_{23}^{\varepsilon\varepsilon}$ scales as $1/N^{3/2}$, where $N$ is the number of independent sources, with a geometric prefactor of approximately 1.22 for Gaussian smearing.
- For central p-Pb, after accounting for noise in the linear flow response, the predicted correlation between $V_2$ and $V_3$ is expected to be greater than 8%, assuming $\lambda_2 < 3\%$ and $\lambda_3 < 5\%$ from two-particle correlation breaking.
- To detect $\mathcal{P}_{23}^{vv}$ with a few percent accuracy in central p-Pb at the LHC, approximately one million events per centrality bin are needed.

## Context
This work extends the study of event-plane correlations, previously measured by the ATLAS collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions (Aad, 2014), to small colliding systems where collectivity is debated. It builds on hydrodynamic simulations by Bozek (2011), Kozlov (2014), and Qin (2013) that predicted harmonic flow in p-Pb, d-Au, and $^3$He-Au, and on the independent-source model of Bhalerao (2011). The proposed measurement of $\mathcal{P}_{23}^{vv}$ provides a way to distinguish between collective expansion and alternative explanations such as pre-equilibrium physics (Gyulassy, 2014), and enables future experimental tests of whether the observed long-range correlations in small systems arise from genuine hydrodynamic behavior.
