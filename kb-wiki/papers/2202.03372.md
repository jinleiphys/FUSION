# Polarized Antimatter in the Proton from Global QCD Analysis

> We present the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of spin-dependent parton distribution functions alongside their spin-averaged counterparts and pion, kaon, and unidentified hadron fragmentation functions. This analysis includes all data relevant for constraining the polarized light quark sea asymmetry $Δ\bar{u} - Δ\bar{d}$, in particular the latest polarized $W$-lepton production data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering data from COMPASS, allowing the most robust extraction available with minimal theoretical assumptions. We also extract a self-consistent set of antiquark polarization ratios $Δ\bar{u}/\bar{u}$ and $Δ\bar{d}/\bar{d}$ and determine the signs of the truncated contributions to the proton spin from the light antiquarks.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of spin-dependent and spin-averaged parton distribution functions (PDFs) alongside fragmentation functions (FFs), claiming the most robust extraction of the polarized light quark sea asymmetry Δū − Δd̄ with minimal theoretical assumptions. The key qualifier is that the extraction relies on including the latest polarized W-lepton production data from STAR at RHIC, without which the asymmetry would be consistent with zero for x ≳ 0.07.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo-based Bayesian inference analysis using fixed-order collinear factorization for deep inelastic scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan lepton-pair production, and weak boson and jet production in hadronic collisions. They simultaneously fitted 146 parameters describing 35 leading-twist PDFs and FFs (plus 81 additional parameters for higher-twist corrections, off-shell corrections, and data normalizations) to 7215 data points from polarized inclusive DIS, semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) with pions, kaons, and unidentified hadrons, polarized jet production, W-lepton production, unpolarized DIS, Drell-Yan, W/Z production, and e+e- annihilation. The PDFs and FFs were parameterized at the input scale μ₀ = m_c using the form f(x,μ₀) = N x^α (1−x)^β (1 + η x), with DGLAP evolution at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Key numbers

- Global χ² per data point of 1.08 for N_dat = 7215 total data points (697 polarized, 5954 unpolarized, 564 SIA)
- For STAR W data: χ²/N_dat = 0.65 with 12 data points in the baseline fit, increasing to 3.00 when enforcing Δū = Δd̄
- Truncated moments at Q² = 4 GeV²: Δu⁺ = 0.771(25), Δd⁺ = −0.363(23), Δū = +0.044(17), Δd̄ = −0.056(24)
- Comparison with lattice QCD full moments: Δu⁺ = 0.864(16), Δd⁺ = −0.426(16) from Alexandrou (2020)
- Heavy quark mass thresholds: m_c = 1.28 GeV, m_b = 4.18 GeV in the MSbar scheme
- Center-of-mass energy for RHIC W production: √s = 510 GeV

## Context

This work extends the DSSV08 analysis by De Florian et al. (2009) and the NNPDFpol1.1 analysis by Nocera et al. (2014), which had extracted the polarized sea asymmetry under assumptions of PDF positivity and SU(3) symmetry or used reweighting procedures from prior fits. The simultaneous extraction of unpolarized and polarized PDFs with FFs enables the first self-consistent determination of the antiquark polarization ratios Δū/ū and Δd̄/d̄, and provides unambiguous signs for the truncated contributions of light antiquarks to the proton spin. Future experiments at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade and the Electron-Ion Collider will extend kinematic coverage to lower x and higher Q², particularly for strange and gluon PDFs.
