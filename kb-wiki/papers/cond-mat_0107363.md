# Non-Ergodic Behaviour of the k-Body Embedded Gaussian Random Ensembles for Bosons

> We investigate the shape of the spectrum and the spectral fluctuations of the $k$-body Embedded Gaussian Ensemble for Bosons in the dense limit, where the number of Bosons $m \to \infty$ while both $k$, the rank of the interaction, and $l$, the number of single-particle states, are kept fixed. We show that the relative fluctuations of the low spectral moments do not vanish in this limit, proving that the ensemble is non-ergodic. Numerical simulations yield spectra which display a strong tendency towards picket-fence type. The wave functions also deviate from canonical random-matrix behaviour

## Key claim

The Bosonic k-body Embedded Gaussian Ensembles (BEGOE(k) and BEGUE(k)) are proven to be non-ergodic in the dense limit (m → ∞ with fixed k and l), because the relative fluctuations of the low spectral moments do not vanish asymptotically; this contradicts the ergodic behavior of both canonical random-matrix theory and the Fermionic embedded ensembles.

## Method

The authors analytically compute the second moment of the random k-body interaction for bosons using an eigenvector decomposition, from which they derive the low spectral moments and three ratios (S, R, Q) that characterize the shape and fluctuations of the spectrum. They then take the dense limit m → ∞ with fixed k and l to evaluate these ratios analytically. Numerical simulations for BEGOE(2) with l=2 and m=3000 (1512 realizations) are performed, using both ensemble unfolding and spectral unfolding to compute the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution P(s) and the Δ₃-statistics.

## Key numbers

- For the dense limit (m → ∞), the ratio Q(k,m,l) for the unitary case (β=2) equals 1, indicating the average spectrum has Gaussian shape (Eq. 5.8).
- The limits of S(k,m,l) and R(k,m,l) in the dense limit (Eqs. 5.6 and 5.7) are non-zero, proving non-ergodicity; explicit expressions depend on k, l, and β.
- Numerical simulations use m = 3000 bosons, l = 2 single-particle states, and k = 2, yielding Hilbert space dimension N = m + 1 = 3001.
- The number of independent random variables for BEGOE(2) with l=2 is K₁ = 6.
- For spectral unfolding, the polynomial fit for the staircase function typically reached a minimum χ² around degree 11 (maximum degree considered was 20).
- After unfolding, only 60% of all levels (1800 levels per realization) from the center of each spectrum were analyzed.
- The Δ₃-statistics from spectral unfolding is almost constant up to L ~ 20, suggesting picket-fence behavior.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical approach to Fermionic embedded ensembles (Benet et al., 2001) to the Bosonic case, focusing on the dense limit which has no analogue for fermions. The results contradict earlier numerical simulations for bosonic systems (Man'ko, 1984; Patel et al., 2000) which found agreement with Wigner-Dyson statistics; the authors attribute this disagreement to those studies using too small m/l ratios. The non-ergodicity and picket-fence spectral behavior open questions about how the spectra change as l increases, and about the analytical understanding of the observed Fock-space localization of eigenfunctions.
