# Superfluidity in Disordered Neutron Stars Crusts

> Nonequilibrium conditions imposed by neutrino cooling through the liquid-solid transition lead to disorder in the solid crust of neutron stars. Disorder reduces the superfluid fraction, $ρ_s/ρ$, at densities above that of neutron drip, $ρ_d \approx 4\times 10^{11}\,g/cm^3$. For an amorphous solid crust the suppression of $ρ_s$ is small, except in the highest density regions of the crust. In contrast to the strong reduction in neutron conduction predicted for coherent Bragg scattering in a crystalline crust, the disordered solid crust supports sufficient neutron superfluid density to account for pulsar glitches.

## Key claim

Disorder in the neutron star crust, resulting from rapid neutrino cooling through the liquid-solid transition, reduces the superfluid fraction but only weakly suppresses it throughout most of the inner crust; the disordered solid crust supports sufficient neutron superfluid density to account for pulsar glitches, in contrast to the strong reduction predicted for a crystalline crust from coherent Bragg scattering.

## Method

The authors model the neutron star crust as an amorphous nuclear solid (analogous to metallic glasses) with randomly positioned nuclei, using the theory of disordered superconductors based on Eilenberger's formulation of Gorkov's equations. They calculate the neutron transport mean free path from the density of nuclei and nuclear radii, then compute the pair-breaking parameter α = (π/2)(ξ₀/ℓtr) and the resulting superfluid fraction nₛ/n as a function of neutron density throughout the inner crust, using input data for the ¹S₀ neutron gap and coherence length from Cao et al. (2006) and crust composition from Chamel et al. (2012).

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density: ρ_d ≈ 4.3 × 10¹¹ g/cm³
- Transport mean free path in amorphous crust ranges from ℓ_max = 4360 fm at low density to ℓ_min = 80 fm at high density
- Nuclear radii throughout the crust: R_N ≈ 6–7 fm
- Superfluid coherence length in clean limit ranges from ξ₀_min ≈ 5 nm (50 fm) at low density to ξ₀_max ≈ 300 nm (3000 fm) at high density
- At high densities (n ≈ 0.065 fm⁻³), the superfluid density is suppressed from nₛ_pure ≈ 0.081 fm⁻³ to nₛ_dirty ≈ 0.015 fm⁻³
- Superfluid fraction of crust moment of inertia at the crust-core boundary (P_crust = 0.389 MeV-fm⁻³): Iₛ/I_c = 0.880 for pure neutron matter, 0.782 for amorphous crust, and 0.285 for Bragg entrainment in a crystalline crust
- The amorphous crust superfluid moment of inertia is reduced to approximately 89% of the pure neutron matter value

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kobyakov and Pethick (2014) on the metallurgy of neutron star crusts and builds on Jones (1999, 2004) who showed the inner crust is likely amorphous with implications for electrical conductivity and magnetic fields. It directly contradicts the interpretation of band-structure calculations by Chamel et al. (2005, 2006, 2012) that coherent Bragg scattering in a crystalline lattice suppresses the crust superfluid moment of inertia by an order of magnitude, which led Chamel et al. (2013) to propose that core superfluid must contribute to pulsar glitches. The present results enable a revised understanding of pulsar glitch mechanisms without invoking decoupled core superfluid components.
