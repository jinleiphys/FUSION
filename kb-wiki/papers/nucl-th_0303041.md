# Event Generator Benchmarking for Proton Radiography Applications

> We have benchmarked the QGSM code and event generators of the MARS and LAHET3 codes as potential candidates for high-energy programs to be used in simulations for the Proton Radiography (PRad) Project. We have compiled from the literature experimental data on spectra of particles emitted from proton-induced reactions at incident energies from 30 GeV to 70 GeV on different nuclei and have performed calculations for all reactions for which we found data with these three codes without any modifications and using only default parameters and standard inputs. Our results (514 plots) show that all three codes describe reasonably most of the studied reactions, though all of them should be further improved before becoming reliable tools for PRad. We present here our conclusions concerning the relative roles of different reaction mechanisms in the production of specific secondary particles. We comment on the strengths and weaknesses of QGSM, MARS, and LAHET3 and suggest further improvements to these codes and to other models.

## Key claim

All three tested event generators (QGSM, MARS, and LAHET3) describe most of the studied secondary-particle spectra reasonably well when run with default parameters and no modifications, but none is yet reliable enough for Proton Radiography (PRad) simulations without further improvement, particularly for complex-particle production at high momenta.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data from the literature on double-differential cross sections and mean multiplicities for secondary particles (n, p, d, t, ³He, ⁴He, π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, K⁻, and antiprotons) from proton-induced reactions on targets from ⁹Be to ²⁰⁸Pb at incident energies between 30 and 70 GeV. They then ran the QGSM, MARS (2001 version), and LAHET3 (with FLUKA96) codes for each reaction without any modifications, using only default parameters and standard inputs, and compared the calculated results (514 plots) against the experimental data at angles of 0°, 4.75°, 9°, 13°, 20°, 45°, 60°, 90°, and 159°.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energies benchmarked: 30, 47, 50, 51, 53, 54, and 70 GeV (Table 1).
- Targets studied: ⁹Be, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁴⁸Ti, ⁶⁴Cu, ⁹⁶Mo, ¹¹⁸Sn, ¹⁸⁴W, and ²⁰⁸Pb (Table 1).
- For p(70 GeV) + ²⁰⁸Pb at 9.17°, experimental deuteron spectra extend to momenta up to about 15 GeV/c and tritium spectra up to 19 GeV/c; QGSM describes deuterons well via coalescence but tritium only up to 2.5 GeV/c, while LAHET3 describes only evaporative and preequilibrium deuterons and tritons with momenta not higher than 1 GeV/c.
- For p(51 GeV) + ⁹Be and ⁴⁸Ti at 159°, LAHET3 overestimates the high-energy tails of π⁺ spectra and MARS underestimates them slightly.
- Mean multiplicities of secondary n, p, d, and π⁺ predicted by the three codes for ~50 GeV protons on various targets differ significantly, with differences increasing with target mass number (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work benchmarks three high-energy event generators, QGSM (Amelin, Gudima, and Toneev), MARS (Mokhov et al.), and LAHET3 (LANL transport code with FLUKA96), for the Proton Radiography (PRad) project, which requires reliable simulations of secondary particles from ~50 GeV protons on target and structural materials. The study extends earlier code comparisons by systematically testing all three codes against the same set of experimental data without tuning, and it identifies specific deficiencies (e.g., lack of coalescence in MARS and LAHET3, over/underestimation of pion tails) that motivate further development. The authors note that improved versions of each code already exist (LAQGSM, MARS14(2002), and later FLUKA versions) and suggest that coalescence and multifragmentation mechanisms should be implemented in MARS and LAHET3 to better describe high-energy complex-particle production.
