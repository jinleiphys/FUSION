# Low-energy pion-pion scattering in the $pp \to pp蟺^{+}蟺^{-}$ and $p\bar p \to p\bar p蟺^{+}蟺^{-}$ reactions

> We evaluate the contribution of pion-pion rescattering to the $p p \to p p 蟺^+ 蟺^-$ and $p \bar p \to p \bar p 蟺^+ 蟺^-$ reactions. We compare our results with the close-to-threshold experimental data. The pion-pion rescattering contribution is found there to be negligible. The predictions for future experiments with PANDA detector at High Energy Storage Ring (HESR) in GSI Darmstadt are presented. It is discussed how to cut off the dominant Roper resonance and double-$螖$ excitation mechanisms leading to the $p p 蟺^+ 蟺^-$ channel in final state. Differential distributions in momentum transfers, transverse momentum, effective two-pions mass, relative azimuthal angle between pions, and pion rapidities are presented.

## Key claim

The pion-pion rescattering contribution to the $pp \to pp\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ reaction is found to be negligible compared to the dominant Roper resonance and double-$\Delta$ excitation mechanisms at energies close to threshold, but at higher energies accessible with the PANDA detector at HESR (GSI Darmstadt) the mechanism may become observable after applying kinematic cuts to suppress the dominant backgrounds.

## Method

The authors evaluate the pion-pion rescattering amplitude using the $\pi^{0*}\pi^{0*} \to \pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ subprocess, where the off-shell initial pions are emitted from the nucleon lines via $\pi NN$ vertices with monopole form factors. The on-shell $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude is expanded into partial waves ($S0$, $S2$, $D0$, $D2$) using phase shifts and inelasticities from the dispersive analysis of Kaminski et al. (2006). The Roper resonance contribution is modeled via $\sigma$-meson and $\pi$-meson exchange mechanisms, and the total cross sections are computed by integrating the four-body phase space for both $pp$ and $p\bar p$ initial states.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi NN$ coupling constant is taken as $g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 13.6$, with a range of 13.5--14.6 cited from the literature.
- The monopole form factor cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ for the $\pi NN$ vertex is varied in the range 0.8--1.4 GeV, with a central value of 0.8 GeV used for the main results.
- The off-shell pion form factor cutoff $\Lambda_{off}$ is varied in the range 0.5--2.0 GeV, with a central value of 1.0 GeV.
- The Roper resonance branching ratio to $p\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ is taken as $Br(N^{*}(1440) \to p\pi^{+}\pi^{-}) = 0.1$.
- The $\sigma NN$ coupling constant is $g^2_{\sigma NN}/4\pi = 5.69$, and the $\pi NN^{*}$ and $\sigma NN^{*}$ couplings are both $g^2/4\pi = 2.0$.
- The maximum of the $p\bar p \to p\bar p\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ cross section from pion-pion rescattering occurs at $\sqrt{s} \approx 5$ GeV, the highest energy planned for the FAIR HESR.
- The $\pi\pi$ invariant mass range considered is $M_{\pi\pi} < 1.5$ GeV, with the total cross section integrated with a restriction $M_{\pi\pi} < 1.42$ GeV.
- Cutoff parameters for removing Roper resonance and double-$\Delta$ excitation regions are $\Delta M_{N^*} = 0.4$ GeV and $\Delta M_{\Delta} = 0.2$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the $pp \to pp\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ reaction beyond the dominant Roper resonance and double-$\Delta$ excitation mechanisms previously analyzed by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (1998). The authors apply the precise $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes from the dispersive analysis of Kaminski et al. (2006), which resolved the long-standing "up-down" ambiguity in the $S0$ wave below 1 GeV. The results provide predictions for the PANDA detector at the HESR (FAIR project) and demonstrate how kinematic cuts can isolate the pion-pion rescattering contribution, enabling future studies of low-energy $\pi\pi$ scattering in a hadronic environment.
