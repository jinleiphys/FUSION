# Are there infinitely many decompositions of the nucleon spin ?

> We discuss the uniqueness or non-uniqueness problem of the decomposition of the gluon field into the physical and pure-gauge components, which is the basis of the recently proposed two physically inequivalent gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin. It is crucialy important to recognize the fact that the standard gauge fixing procedure is essentially a process of projecting out the physical components of the massless gauge field. A complexity of the nonabelian gauge theory as compared with the abelian case is that a closed expression for the physical component can be given only with use of the non-local Wilson line, which is generally path-dependent. It is known that, by choosing an infinitely long straight-line path in space and time, the direction of which is characterized by a constant 4-vector $n^μ$, one can cover a class of gauge called the general axial gauge, containing three popular gauges, i.e. the temporal, the light-cone, and the spatial axial gauge. Within this general axial gauge, we have calculated the 1-loop evolution matrix for the quark and gluon longitudinal spins in the nucleon. We found that the final answer is exactly the same independently of the choices of $n^μ$, which amounts to proving the gauge-independence and path-independence simultaneously, although within a restricted class of gauges and paths. By drawing on all of these findings together with well-established knowledge from gauge theories, we argue against the rapidly spreading view in the community that there are infinitely many decompositions of the nucleon spin.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to a rapidly spreading view in the community, there are not infinitely many decompositions of the nucleon spin; within the general axial gauge (characterized by a constant 4-vector \(n^\mu\)), the 1-loop evolution matrix for quark and gluon longitudinal spins is exactly the same regardless of the choice of \(n^\mu\), proving gauge-independence and path-independence simultaneously, though only within this restricted class of gauges and paths.

## Method

The author calculates the 1-loop evolution matrix for the quark and gluon longitudinal spins in the nucleon within the general axial gauge, a class of gauges defined by an infinitely long straight-line path in space and time whose direction is characterized by a constant 4-vector \(n^\mu\). This class includes the temporal, light-cone, and spatial axial gauges. The calculation tests whether the evolution matrix depends on the choice of \(n^\mu\), which simultaneously characterizes the gauge choice and the direction of the straight-line path in the geometric formulation using path-dependent Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- The 1-loop evolution matrix for quark and gluon longitudinal spins in the nucleon is found to be exactly the same independently of the choices of \(n^\mu\) (the constant 4-vector characterizing the general axial gauge), proving gauge-independence and path-independence within this restricted class.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous series of papers (Wakamatsu 2010, 2011, 2012) establishing two physically inequivalent gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, called decompositions (I) and (II), which differ in their orbital angular momentum terms. The paper directly contradicts claims by Lorcé (2012) about a Stueckelberg symmetry leading to infinitely many decompositions, and arguments by Ji et al. (2012) that gauge-invariant extensions using path-dependent Wilson lines are not unique. The result enables a resolution of the debate over whether the gluon spin \(\Delta G\) in the nucleon is a truly gauge-invariant observable.
