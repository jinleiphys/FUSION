# The Non-thermal Energy Window for Laser-Driven Nuclear Reactions

> Astrophysical nuclear reaction rates in stellar environments are governed by the Gamow window, where Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions and quantum tunneling probabilities combine to produce effective reactivity. However, this conventional formulation is inadequate for the non-thermal ion distributions generated in ultra-intense laser-plasma interactions. Here, we introduce an analytical framework, based on a Target Normal Sheath Acceleration (TNSA) mechanism, to evaluate nuclear reaction rates under these non-equilibrium conditions. We identify a new effective energy window and analytical expression of the fusion reactivity distinct from the conventional Gamow window, providing a predictive tool for laboratory astrophysics experiments designed to replicate astrophysical nuclear processes using laser-driven nuclear reactions.

# The Non-thermal Energy Window for Laser-Driven Nuclear Reactions

## Key claim

The paper introduces an analytical framework for nuclear reaction rates under non-thermal ion distributions produced by Target Normal Sheath Acceleration (TNSA), deriving a new effective energy window and closed-form reactivity expression that differs from the conventional Gamow window. The authors claim this framework is valid only when the condition ωₚᵢtₐcc ≫ 1 is satisfied, i.e., when the initial Debye length is much smaller than the plasma expansion scale.

## Method

The authors adopt a self-similar plasma-expansion-into-vacuum model to obtain an analytic form of the ion energy distribution from TNSA, assuming quasi-neutrality (nₑ ≈ Zᵢnᵢ) over the acceleration time. They substitute this non-Maxwellian distribution into the fusion reactivity integral for a pitcher-catcher configuration, treating the target ions as stationary relative to the beam. For non-resonant reactions, they factor the cross section into the astrophysical S-factor and Coulomb penetration factor, then determine the effective peak energy E₀ by maximizing the integrand. The reactivity is evaluated analytically using the modified Bessel function K₀, and the formalism is extended to narrow resonances via the Breit-Wigner formula.

## Key numbers

- For the D+D reaction under LULI conditions (I = 3 × 10¹⁹ W/cm², λ = 1.057 μm, k_BTₑ = 2.068 MeV), the predicted non-thermal peak energy is E₀ = 0.305 MeV, compared to the conventional Gamow peak energy E₀,G = 0.494 MeV (using k_BTₑff,D = 0.7 MeV).
- The maximum reactivity for the D+D reaction occurs at k_BTₑ = 10.85 MeV, corresponding to Iλ_μ² = 6.752 × 10²⁰, where ⟨σv⟩/S(E₀) = 3.356 × 10⁸ cm s⁻¹ MeV⁻¹.
- The optimal electron temperature is given by k_BTₑ,max ≃ 0.1720 B, where B = 32 mᵢE_G/(μZᵢ).
- For the LULI conditions, ωₚᵢtₐcc = 11.08 for protons and ωₚᵢtₐcc = 7.839 for deuterons.

## Context

This work extends the standard Gamow window formalism, developed for Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions in stellar environments (Angulo 1999, Arnould 2003, Iliadis 2015, Rauscher 2010), to the non-equilibrium conditions of laser-driven nuclear reactions. The authors build on the TNSA model of Mora (2003) and the self-similar solution for ion spectra validated against experimental data from Fuchs (2006). The framework enables extraction of low-energy astrophysical S-factors from laser-driven fusion experiments and provides a predictive tool for designing pitcher-catcher experiments to study nuclear reactions relevant to nucleosynthesis. Future extensions could incorporate non-Maxwellian electron distributions, time-dependent electron temperature, multidimensional effects, and magnetic fields.
