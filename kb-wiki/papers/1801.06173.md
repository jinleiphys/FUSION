# On the vacuum-polarization Uehling potential for a Fermi charge distribution

> We present analytical formulas for the vacuum-polarization Uehling potential in the case where the finite size of the nucleus is modeled by a Fermi charge distribution. Using a Sommerfeld-type development, the potential is expressed in terms of multiple derivatives of a particular integral. The latter and its derivatives can be evaluated exactly in terms of Bickley-Naylor functions, which connection to the Uehling potential was already pointed out in the pure Coulomb case, and of usual Bessel functions of the second kind. The cusp and asymptotic expressions for the Uehling potential with a Fermi charge distribution are also provided. Analytical results for the higher-order-contribution Källèn-Sabry potential are given.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytical expressions for the Uehling vacuum-polarization potential when the nuclear charge is modeled by a Fermi (Woods-Saxon) distribution, using a Sommerfeld-type expansion combined with Bickley-Naylor functions and Bessel functions of the second kind; however, the author explicitly states that the formulas are not intended to improve numerical accuracy or speed over the existing rational approximation of Fullerton and Rinker (1976), which provides nine-digit accuracy with minimal computation.

## Method

The author starts from the general expression for the Uehling potential for an arbitrary charge distribution and specializes to the Fermi charge distribution. A Sommerfeld-type expansion (infinite series involving Riemann zeta functions and derivatives of the integrand) is applied to the radial integral over the Fermi density. The resulting multiple derivatives of the integral g(z) are expressed exactly in terms of Bickley-Naylor functions Ki_n(z) and modified Bessel functions K_0(z), K_1(z), using known identities and recurrence relations. Asymptotic forms for small and large r are derived analytically, and the method is extended to the higher-order Källén-Sabry potential.

## Key numbers

* The surface thickness parameter of the Fermi distribution is given as a = t/(4 ln 3) with t = 2.3 fm (Fricke 1971).
* The half-density radius parameter is ξ = 2.2677 × 10⁻⁵ a₀, where a₀ is the Bohr radius.
* The series in the Sommerfeld-type expansion requires 10 terms for convergence to 1 %.
* The asymptotic form of g(z) for small r is g(z) ≈ 9π/32 − 2cr ln(1/(2cr)).
* The value of g(0) is 9π/32.
* The asymptotic form of δV(r) for large r is δV(r) ≈ −(Z/r)[1 + (α/(4√π)) e⁻²ᶜʳ/(cr)³/²].
* The integral I₂ for r=0 evaluates to π/32.

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical expression for the Uehling potential, previously known only for a point-like nucleus (Frolov and Wardlaw 2012, 2014) and for a homogeneous step-function nuclear density (Ginges and Berengut 2016), to the more realistic Fermi charge distribution. The author notes that the most numerically efficient method remains the rational approximation of Fullerton and Rinker (1976), but the derived mathematical expressions may provide new insights through recursion relations, asymptotic expansions, and connections to other physical problems. The work also provides analytical results for the higher-order Källén-Sabry potential, building on the fitting forms of Indelicato (2013).
