# Definition of gravitational local spatial densities for spin-0 and spin-1/2 systems

> We work out details of defining the spatial densities corresponding to the gravitational form factors of spin-0 and spin-1/2 systems using spherically symmetric sharply localized wave packets. The expressions for the spatial densities are provided in the frames with both zero and non-zero expectation values of the momentum operator.

## Key claim

The paper defines gravitational spatial densities for spin-0 and spin-1/2 systems using spherically symmetric sharply localized wave packets, but the resulting density distributions retain dependence on the wave packet profile and size (through normalization factors that diverge or vanish as the packet size goes to zero), unlike the electromagnetic case where such dependence cancels.

## Method

The authors construct normalizable Heisenberg-picture states as spherically symmetric wave packets of one-particle momentum eigenstates, then take the limit of sharp spatial localization (packet size R approaching zero) using dimensional counting. They compute matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor in the zero average momentum frame and in Lorentz-boosted frames, and compare these results with the static (nonrelativistic) approximation obtained by expanding in powers of 1/m before localization.

## Key numbers

- For spin-1/2 systems in the static approximation, the mean squared energy radius is ⟨r²⟩_naive = 6 A'(0), while the wave-packet definition gives ⟨r²⟩ = 4 A'(0); the same factor reduction holds for spin-0 systems.
- The leading term in the spin-0 ZAMF density scales as 1/R (diverging as R→0), while the subleading term scales as R (vanishing as R→0).
- For spin-1/2 in the ZAMF, the normalization factor N_φ,R (multiplying the A and J form factor contributions) scales as 1/R, while N_φ,R,2 (multiplying the D form factor contribution) scales as R.
- In the infinite momentum frame (v→1, γ→∞), the Θ₂ contribution acquires a factor 4πγ, while the Θ₁ contribution acquires a factor 2π/γ times a divergent integral α = lim_{v→1} ∫_{-1}^{+1} dη/(1+vη).

## Context

This work extends the wave-packet localization approach previously applied to electromagnetic charge densities for spin-0 systems (Epelbaum 2022) and spin-1/2 systems (Panteleeva 2022) to the gravitational form factors of the energy-momentum tensor. It contrasts with the traditional Breit-frame Fourier transform interpretation (Polyakov 2002, 2018) and with the light-front impact-parameter approach (Burkardt 2000, Miller 2007). The paper explicitly addresses criticism from Freese (2022) regarding the validity of wave-packet localization for defining internal densities, arguing that the claimed "true internal densities" framework oversimplifies the physics of hadronic systems in wave-packet states. The results enable further study of gravitational densities in moving frames and the infinite momentum frame, where the densities become two-dimensional transverse distributions.
