# Dynamically generated resonances from the vector meson-octet baryon interaction in the strangeness zero sector

> The interaction potentials between vector mesons and octet baryons are calculated explicitly with a summation of t-, s-, u-channel diagrams and a contact term originating from the tensor interaction. Many resonances are generated dynamically in different channels of strangeness zero by solving the coupled-channel Lippman-Schwinger equations with the method of partial wave analysis, and their total angular momenta are determined. The spin partners N(1650)1/2^{-} and N(1700)3/2^-, N(1895)1/2^{-} and N(1875)3/2^-, and the state N(2120)3/2^- are all produced respectively in the isospin I=1/2 sector. In the isospin I=3/2 sector, the spin partners Delta(1620)1/2^- and Delta(1700)3/2^- are also associated with the pole in the complex energy plane. According to the calculation results, a J^P=1/2^- state around 2000 MeV is predicted as the spin partner of N(2120)3/2^-. Some resonances are well fitted with their counterparts listed in the newest review of Particle Data Group(PDG), while others might stimulate the experimental observation in these energy regions in the future.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many nucleon and delta resonances in the strangeness-zero sector can be dynamically generated from the vector meson-octet baryon interaction by including not only the t-channel but also s-channel, u-channel, and contact diagrams, which lifts the spin degeneracy found in earlier t-channel-only calculations. The authors hedge that some generated poles match PDG-listed resonances while others are predictions that "might stimulate the experimental observation in these energy regions in the future."

## Method

The authors constructed the interaction kernel for vector mesons and octet baryons using a hidden-gauge SU(3) Lagrangian that includes a tensor term, summing t-channel (vector meson exchange), s-channel and u-channel (baryon exchange), and a contact diagram. They solved the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in the on-shell factorization approach, using dimensional regularization with a subtraction constant a(μ) = -2 at scale μ = 630 MeV, and performed a partial-wave expansion to extract amplitudes for different total angular momenta J. Poles were searched for in the complex energy plane of the second Riemann sheet, and the mass distributions of the ρ and K* mesons were convoluted into the loop function to account for their finite widths.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constant a(μ) is set to -2, with regularization scale μ = 630 MeV.
- The pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV is used to define the coupling g = M_V/(√2 f_π).
- The ρ meson decay width is taken as 149.1 MeV; the K* meson decay width is taken as 49.75 MeV.
- In the I=1/2, J=1/2 sector, poles are found at 1715+i4 MeV, 1728+i0 MeV, 1855+i1 MeV, 1868+i6 MeV, 1982+i4 MeV, and 1999+i5 MeV.
- In the I=1/2, J=3/2 sector, poles are found at 1715+i4 MeV, 1868+i6 MeV, 1982+i4 MeV, 1999+i5 MeV, and 2045+i25 MeV.
- The pole at 1715+i4 MeV is associated with N(1650)1/2⁻ (J=1/2) and N(1700)3/2⁻ (J=3/2); the pole at 1868+i6 MeV is associated with N(1895)1/2⁻ and N(1875)3/2⁻; the pole at 1982+i4 MeV is associated with N(2120)3/2⁻.
- In the I=3/2 sector, poles are found at 1620+i0 MeV (J=1/2) and 1700+i0 MeV (J=3/2), associated with Δ(1620)1/2⁻ and Δ(1700)3/2⁻ respectively.
- A J^P=1/2⁻ state around 2000 MeV is predicted as the spin partner of N(2120)3/2⁻.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson-baryon interactions that only considered t-channel exchange (Ramos et al., 2010; Garzon et al., 2012; Garzon et al., 2013), which produced spin-degenerate resonances. By including s-channel, u-channel, and contact terms following the approach of Hosaka et al. (2011), the authors obtain resonances with definite total angular momenta. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted J^P=1/2⁻ state near 2000 MeV and clarify the spin-partner relationships among known N* and Δ* resonances.
