# Dirac Phenomenological Analyses of Unpolarized Proton Inelastic Scattering from $^{22}$Ne

> Unpolarized 800 MeV proton inelastic scatterings from an s-d shell nucleus $^{22}$Ne are analyzed using phenomenological optical potentials in the Dirac coupled channel formalism. The first-order rotational collective model is used to obtain the transition optical potentials for the low lying excited collective states that belong to the ground state rotational band of the nucleus. The optical potential parameters of Woods-Saxon shape and the deformation parameters of the excited states are varied phenomenologically using the sequential iteration method to reproduce the experimental differential cross section data. The effective central and spin-orbit optical potentials are obtained by reducing the Dirac equations to the Schrödinger-like second-order differential equations and the surface-peaked phenomena are observed at the real effective central potentials when the scattering from $^{22}$Ne is considered. The obtained deformation parameters of the excited states are compared with those of the nonrelativistic calculations and another s-d shell nucleus $^{20}$Ne. The deformation parameters for the $2^+$ and the $4^+$ states of the ground state rotational band at the nucleus $^{22}$Ne are found to be smaller than those of $^{20}$Ne, indicating that the couplings of those states to the ground state are weaker at the nucleus $^{22}$Ne compared to those at the nucleus $^{20}$Ne. The multistep channel coupling effect is confirmed to be important for the $4^+$ state excitation of the ground state rotational band at the proton inelastic scattering from the s-d shell nucleus $^{22}$Ne.

# Dirac Phenomenological Analyses of Unpolarized Proton Inelastic Scattering from $^{22}$Ne

## Key claim
The deformation parameters for the $2^+$ and $4^+$ states of the ground state rotational band in $^{22}$Ne are smaller than those of $^{20}$Ne, indicating weaker couplings of those states to the ground state, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific Dirac phenomenological optical potential model and the first-order rotational collective model used, with the deformation parameters also found to be slightly smaller than those from nonrelativistic calculations.

## Method
Unpolarized 800 MeV proton inelastic scattering from $^{22}$Ne was analyzed using Dirac coupled channel calculations with phenomenological optical potentials of Woods-Saxon shape in the scalar-vector (S-V) model. The first-order rotational collective model was employed to obtain transition optical potentials for the low-lying excited states of the ground state rotational band, and the optical potential parameters and deformation parameters were varied using a sequential iteration method with minimum chi-squared fitting to reproduce experimental differential cross section data. The Dirac equations were reduced to Schrödinger-like second-order differential equations to extract effective central and spin-orbit optical potentials.

## Key numbers
- Scalar real potential strength: -177.6 MeV, radius 2.793 fm, diffusiveness 0.8065 fm for 800 MeV p + $^{22}$Ne elastic scattering
- Scalar imaginary potential strength: 160.0 MeV, radius 2.213 fm, diffusiveness 0.7792 fm
- Vector real potential strength: 75.95 MeV, radius 3.098 fm, diffusiveness 0.7176 fm
- Vector imaginary potential strength: -101.6 MeV, radius 2.649 fm, diffusiveness 0.6332 fm
- $\chi^2/N$ for elastic scattering fit: approximately 4.3
- Real central potential strength at nucleus center: approximately -50 MeV for $^{22}$Ne, compared to about -3.9 MeV in nonrelativistic calculations
- Imaginary central potential strength at nucleus center: approximately -20 MeV for $^{22}$Ne, compared to about 49.1 MeV in nonrelativistic calculations
- Deformation parameter $\beta_S$ for $2^+$ state of $^{22}$Ne: 0.202; $\beta_V$: 0.313
- Deformation parameter $\beta_S$ for $4^+$ state of $^{22}$Ne: 0.019; $\beta_V$: 0.048
- Deformation parameters for $2^+$ state of $^{20}$Ne: $\beta_S$ = 0.600, $\beta_V$ = 0.587
- Deformation parameters for $4^+$ state of $^{20}$Ne: $\beta_S$ = 0.237, $\beta_V$ = 0.245
- Excitation energy of $2^+$ state in $^{22}$Ne: 1.27 MeV; in $^{20}$Ne: 1.63 MeV
- Excitation energy of $4^+$ state in $^{22}$Ne: 3.36 MeV; in $^{20}$Ne: 4.25 MeV

## Context
This work extends Dirac phenomenological analyses of intermediate energy proton scattering from spherically symmetric nuclei and deformed nuclei such as $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg to the s-d shell nucleus $^{22}$Ne, following prior relativistic approaches by Clark et al. and others. The results confirm the surface-peaked phenomena in real effective central potentials previously observed for $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg, and demonstrate that multistep channel coupling via the $2^+$ state is essential for describing the $4^+$ state excitation, consistent with findings for other axially-symmetric deformed nuclei. The comparison with nonrelativistic calculations from Ref. 16 shows slightly better agreement with experimental data using the Dirac approach, and the deformation parameters are compared with those from nonrelativistic calculations by Ref. 16, 17, and 19.
