# The $nnn$ and $ppp$ correlation functions

> Scattering experiments with three free nucleons in the ingoing channel are extremely challenging in terrestrial laboratories. Recently, the ALICE Collaboration has successfully measured the scattering of three protons indirectly, by using the femtoscopy method in high-energy proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. In order to establish a connection with current and future measurements of femtoscopic three-particle correlation functions, we analyse the scenarios involving $nnn$ and $ppp$ systems using the hyperspherical adiabatic basis. The correlation function is a convolution of the source function and the corresponding scattering wave function. The finite size of the source allows for the use of the free scattering wave function in most of the adiabatic channels except the lowest ones. The scattering wave function has been computed using two different potential models: $(i)$ a spin-dependent Gaussian potential with parameters fixed to reproduce the scattering length and effective range and $(ii)$ the Argonne $v_{18}$ nucleon-nucleon interaction. Moreover, in the case of three protons, the Coulomb interaction has been considered in its hypercentral form. The results presented here have to be considered as a first step in the description of three-particle correlation functions using the hyperspherical adiabatic basis, opening the door to the investigation of other systems, such as the $ppΛ$ system. For completeness, the comparison with the measurement by the ALICE Collaboration is shown assuming different values of the source radius.

## Key claim

The authors compute the three-neutron ($nnn$) and three-proton ($ppp$) femtoscopic correlation functions for the first time using the hyperspherical adiabatic basis, but they present this as a preliminary first step that simplifies the asymptotic treatment of the Coulomb interaction for three protons, rather than a complete solution.

## Method

The correlation function is computed as a convolution of a Gaussian source function with the square of the three-nucleon scattering wave function, which is obtained by solving the hyperangular Hamiltonian in the hyperspherical adiabatic basis. The wave function is constructed using two potential models: a spin-dependent Gaussian potential with parameters fitted to reproduce the scattering length and effective range, and the Argonne $v_{18}$ (AV18) nucleon-nucleon interaction; for the $ppp$ system, the Coulomb interaction is included in a hypercentral approximation. The lowest adiabatic channel is shown to provide sufficient accuracy, and the authors decompose the wave function into partial waves with well-defined total angular momentum and parity, paying attention to the construction of spatial and spin symmetries.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian potential for $pp$ uses $V_0 = -30.45$ MeV and $r_0 = 1.815$ fm, with a projector on spin $S=0$, to reproduce the $pp$ scattering length and effective range.
- The $m_T$-integrated source radius for $pp$ pairs measured by ALICE is $R = 1.249 \pm 0.008\ \mathrm{(stat.)}^{+0.024}_{-0.021}\ \mathrm{(syst.)}$ fm.
- The fraction of correctly identified primary protons in the ALICE measurement is $\lambda_{pp} = 0.67$, with secondary protons from $\Lambda$ decay at $\lambda_{pp_\Lambda} = 0.203$ and other contributions at $\lambda_\mathrm{X} = 0.127$.
- Convergence of the free scattering wave function norm is achieved after inclusion of $\ell$-values up to $\ell = 40$.

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopy technique, previously applied to two-body systems such as $pp$, $p\Lambda$, and $p\Sigma^0$ by the ALICE Collaboration (see the review by Fabbietti et al., 2021), to three-nucleon systems. It builds on the hyperspherical harmonic and adiabatic basis methods used by Kievsky et al. (2001, 2004) and Garrido et al. (2014) for three-nucleon continuum calculations, and on the Gaussian potential representation of the NN interaction used in studies of two-, three-, and four-nucleon systems by Higgins et al. (2020, 2021). The results open the door to investigating other three-particle systems such as $pp\Lambda$, and provide a framework for interpreting the $ppp$ correlation function recently measured by ALICE.
