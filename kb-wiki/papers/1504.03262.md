# Sensitivity of multiplicity fluctuations to freeze-out conditions in heavy ion collisions

> We study the sensitivity of the higher-order moments of produced particle multiplicity distributions to the chemical freeze-out parameters in relativistic heavy ion collisions using the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. We compare the obtained sensitivity level to the one extracted from the ratios of particle yields. We find that, for certain final state hadrons, the fluctuation measurements add significant information to the determination of the hadro-chemical freeze-out properties of the deconfined phase of matter obtained at RHIC and the LHC.

# Sensitivity of multiplicity fluctuations to freeze-out conditions in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The higher-order moments of net-particle multiplicity distributions, particularly the net-kaon $\chi_2/\chi_1$ ratio, can add significant sensitivity to the determination of chemical freeze-out temperature compared to traditional particle yield ratios, but this advantage is particle-species dependent and requires efficiency-corrected experimental data.

## Method
The authors used a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model in partial chemical equilibrium, including strong decays of hadronic resonances from the PDG tables up to 2 GeV/c², to calculate cumulant ratios ($\chi_2/\chi_1$, $\chi_3/\chi_2$, $\chi_4/\chi_2$) for net-particle distributions of various hadron species. They compared the temperature sensitivity of these moment ratios to that of standard particle yield ratios (e.g., $K/\pi$, $p/\pi$) at fixed baryo-chemical potential $\mu_B = 24.3$ MeV, incorporating kinematic cuts, isospin randomization corrections (Kitazawa-Asakawa corrections), and probabilistic resonance decay contributions.

## Key numbers
- Baryo-chemical potential fixed at $\mu_B = 24.3$ MeV for the study at the highest RHIC energy.
- The strange chemical potential $\mu_S$ increases from 3 to 8 MeV between $T = 140$ and 180 MeV in the HRG model.
- For net-$\Omega$ at high $T$, the strangeness chemical potential contribution ($\mu_S = 3 \times 8$ MeV = 24 MeV) offsets the baryo-chemical potential ($\mu_B = 24.3$ MeV).
- Including additional resonance states from PDG-2014 up to 2.5 GeV/c² increases the temperature dependence of multi-strange baryon $\chi_2/\chi_1$ by 10-25% in the relevant temperature range.
- The $K/\pi$ ratio is "almost completely insensitive" to temperature, while net-kaon $\chi_2/\chi_1$ shows strong sensitivity within achievable experimental error bars.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous HRG model analysis (Alba 2014) which found lower freeze-out temperatures from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations (approximately 146 MeV) compared to statistical hadronization fits to all particle multiplicities (approximately 166 MeV). The study addresses the tension between freeze-out temperatures extracted from light quark versus strange quark observables, as suggested by lattice QCD simulations of kurtosis (Bellwied 2013). The results enable experimental determination of separate freeze-out temperatures for light and strange hadron sectors using moment analyses at RHIC and LHC, and provide a baseline for distinguishing critical point signals from equilibrium fluctuations.
