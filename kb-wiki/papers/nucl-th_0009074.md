# Hyperon-Nucleon Final State Interaction in Kaon Photoproduction of the Deuteron

> Final state hyperon-nucleon interaction in strangeness photoproduction of the deuteron is investigated making use of the covariant reaction formalism and the P-matrix approach to the YN system. Remarkably simple analytical expression for the amplitude is obtained. Pronounced effects due to final state interaction are predicted including the manifestation of the 2.13 GeV resonance.

# Hyperon-Nucleon Final State Interaction in Kaon Photoproduction of the Deuteron

## Key claim

The final state hyperon-nucleon interaction in the reaction γd → K⁺Yn (Y = Λ, Σ⁰) produces pronounced effects that can be described by a remarkably simple factorization of the amplitude into a plane-wave part multiplied by a Jost-function enhancement factor, provided the YN interaction is treated within the P-matrix approach. The author predicts that the 2.13 GeV resonance, which is a P-matrix partner of the deuteron rather than a genuine six-quark state, can be observed in strangeness photoproduction, contradicting earlier claims that FSI is insignificant at higher energies.

## Method

The author calculates the amplitude for γd → K⁺Yn using covariant Feynman diagrams, summing a tree-level (plane-wave) diagram and a triangle (loop) diagram with YN final state interaction. The elementary photoproduction amplitude on the proton is taken from a tree-level effective Lagrangian with resonances in s, t, and u channels, while the deuteron vertex uses the Gross relativistic wave function. The YN interaction is described by the P-matrix approach with a single six-quark "primitive" at 2.34 GeV, and the loop integral is evaluated analytically by factoring out the deuteron wave function and the elementary photoproduction amplitude, justified by the short-range nature of the P-matrix form factor.

## Key numbers

- The P-matrix pole (primitive) energy is E_n = 2.34 GeV, with residue λ² = 0.014 GeV², fitted to existing YN data.
- The deuteron binding momentum is α = √(m_n|E_B|) ≈ 45.7 MeV.
- The double differential cross section is calculated at |p_K| = 1.091 GeV and Θ_K = 0°, with the Λn threshold at E_γ = 1.4 GeV and the ΣN threshold at E_γ = 1.5 GeV.
- The factorization approximation (T^(t) + T^(l) ≈ T^(t)/f(-p')) has an accuracy of a few percent, with the correction term c ≤ 0.2 in the region where FSI is important.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of Renard and Renard on FSI in γd → K⁺Yn by introducing a covariant formalism valid beyond low energy and low momentum transfer, and by using the P-matrix approach (Jaffe and Low) which separates dynamical singularities from kinematical threshold effects. The author's prediction of a clear signal at the ΣN threshold from the 2.13 GeV resonance contradicts the conclusions of earlier work (Renard and Renard) that FSI is important only near the Λn threshold and insignificant at higher energies. The results enable experimental investigation of the 2.13 GeV resonance in photoproduction experiments at CEBAF, potentially resolving whether this pole corresponds to a genuine six-quark state or a P-matrix partner of the deuteron.
