# Two-photon final states in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> We discuss processes leading to two photon final states in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Due to the large photon luminosity we show that the continuum subprocess $γγ\to γγ$ can be observed with a large number of events. We study this reaction when it is intermediated by a resonance made of quarks or gluons and discuss its interplay with the continuum process, verifying that in several cases the resonant process ovewhelms the continuum one. It is also investigated the possibility of observing a scalar resonance (the $σ$ meson) in this process. Assuming for the $σ$ the mass and total decay width values recently reported by the E791 Collaboration we show that RHIC may detect this particle in its two photon decay mode if its partial photonic decay width is of the order of the ones discussed in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the continuum process γγ → γγ can be observed with a large number of events in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC due to the high photon luminosity, and that resonant processes (including a possible σ meson) can overwhelm the continuum in several cases. However, the observability of the σ meson in its two-photon decay mode depends critically on its partial photonic decay width being of the order of values discussed in the literature (3.8–10 keV), and the interference between the resonant and continuum amplitudes is destructive for the broad σ resonance.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for the subprocess γγ → γγ (fermion box diagram) using the helicity amplitudes of Karplus and Neuman and De Tollis, with fermion masses for e, μ, u, d, s. They fold this with the two-photon luminosity from the equivalent-photon approximation using the Cahn-Jackson fit for the photon distribution in heavy ions, imposing a peripheral condition (impact parameter b > 2R_A) and an angular cut |cos θ| < 0.5. For resonant production, they use a Breit-Wigner profile for J=0 resonances (η, η′, η(1440), f₀(1710), and σ) and also compute the full helicity amplitudes for scalar and pseudoscalar resonances via effective Lagrangians to study interference with the continuum.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon, with a bin of 200 MeV centered at 700 MeV (free of strong resonances), the continuum process yields 1532 events/year assuming luminosity 2.0 × 10²⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ and 100% efficiency.
- Number of events/year above background: η (mass 547.3 MeV, total width 1.18 keV, Γ_γγ = 0.46 keV) gives 7.44 × 10⁵ events/year; η′ (mass 957.78 MeV, total width 0.203 MeV, Γ_γγ = 4.06 keV) gives 2.67 × 10⁴ events/year; f₀(1710) (mass 1715 MeV, total width 125 MeV, Γ_γγ = 21.25 keV) gives 42 events/year.
- For the σ meson, using E791 Collaboration values (mass 478 MeV, total width 324 MeV), the cross section is computed for three assumed Γ_γγ values: 3.8 keV, 4.7 keV, and 10 keV, all with the angular cut |cos θ| < 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon physics in peripheral heavy ion collisions previously discussed by Baur (1990), Cahn and Jackson (1990), and Bertulani and collaborators (1990s), and follows the proposal by Natale and collaborators (1990s) for observing resonances made of quarks or gluons via γγ fusion. The paper specifically addresses the possibility of detecting the σ meson in its γγ decay mode, motivated by the E791 Collaboration's observation of the σ in ππ decay (1999). The results enable future experimental searches at RHIC for glueball candidates (η(1440), f₀(1710)) and the σ meson, complementing information from hadronic decays and helping to unravel mixing in glueball candidates as discussed by Close (1990s).
