# Pairing properties of semilocal coordinate&momentum-space regularized chiral interactions

> We investigate the pairing properties of state-of-the-art semilocal coordinate-space and semilocal momentum-space regularized chiral interactions. Specifically, we calculate the pairing gaps in $^3SD_1$ channel of symmetric nuclear matter and in $^1S_0$ and $^3PF_2$ channels of pure neutron matter within the BCS approximation using these chiral interactions. We address the regulator and chiral order dependence of the pairing gaps and compare the pairing properties of the chiral interactions with those of the Argonne v18 (Av18) potential. The effects of the tensor force on the pairing gaps in the $^3SD_1$ and $^3PF_2$ channels are illustrated for both the chiral interactions and the Av18 potential. We evaluate the truncation errors of chiral expansions of the pairing gaps with a Bayesian approach. We find that the pairing gaps converge very well at the higher-order chiral expansions in the $^3SD_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels.

## Key claim

The pairing gaps in the $^3SD_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels converge well at higher chiral orders, but the $^3PF_2$ channel requires higher chiral orders to reach convergence, and the tensor force components of the chiral interactions differ significantly from those of the Argonne v18 potential, especially at high densities.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for symmetric nuclear matter ($^3SD_1$ channel) and pure neutron matter ($^1S_0$ and $^3PF_2$ channels) using semilocal coordinate-space (SCS) regularized chiral interactions (LO through N$^4$LO, regulators $R=0.8-1.2$ fm) and semilocal momentum-space (SMS) regularized chiral interactions (LO through N$^4$LO+, regulators $\Lambda=400-550$ MeV). They used a free single-particle spectrum and compared results with the Argonne v18 potential, and estimated truncation errors of the chiral expansion using a Bayesian approach.

## Key numbers

- Maximum $^3SD_1$ pairing gaps in symmetric nuclear matter are of the order of 10 MeV for all SCS regularized chiral interactions.
- Maximum $^1S_0$ pairing gaps in neutron matter are about 3 MeV for all chiral interactions except LO.
- The $^3PF_2$ pairing gaps are nonexistent for the SCS regularized LO interactions.
- Regulators for SCS interactions were chosen in the range $R=0.8,0.9,1.0,1.1,$ and $1.2$ fm.
- Regulators for SMS interactions were chosen in the range $\Lambda=400-550$ MeV.
- The chiral EFT breakdown scale $\Lambda_b$ was taken as 700 MeV.
- The maximum Fermi momentum considered was 515 MeV (corresponding to $\rho=0.6$ fm$^{-3}$ for pure neutron matter).

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing gaps using SCS regularized chiral interactions by Drischler (2016) to include the $^3SD_1$ channel and to compare with the newer SMS regularized interactions of Reinert (2017). The results are compared with the Argonne v18 potential of Wiringa (1994). The Bayesian truncation error analysis follows the methodology of Furnstahl (2015) and Melendez (2017). The work enables future studies incorporating three-nucleon forces and more realistic single-particle spectra via Brueckner Hartree Fock theory to improve predictions for neutron star cooling phenomena.
