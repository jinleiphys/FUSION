# Equivalence of Light-Front and Covariant Field Theory

> In this paper we discuss the relation between the standard covariant quantum field theory and light-front field theory. We define covariant theory by its Feynman diagrams, whereas light-front field theory is defined in terms of light-cone time-ordered diagrams. A general algorithm is proposed that produces the latter from any Feynman diagram. The procedure is illustrated in several cases. Technical problems that occur in the light-front formulation and have no counterpart in the covariant formulation are identified and solved.

# Equivalence of Light-Front and Covariant Field Theory

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Feynman diagrams in covariant field theory can be systematically decomposed into sums of light-cone time-ordered diagrams, establishing equivalence between the two formulations for scalar and spin-1/2 fields. However, this equivalence is shown to hold only when the p^- integrals are regularized in a way that preserves covariance, and the authors explicitly note that renormalization is not discussed in the paper.

## Method
The authors define covariant theory by its Feynman diagrams and light-front field theory by light-cone time-ordered diagrams, then propose a general algorithm that produces the latter from any Feynman diagram by integrating over the light-cone energy p^- variable. The algorithm is illustrated by applying it to the box diagram and crossed-box diagram in phi^3 theory, where the authors evaluate the k^- contour integral and decompose the result into energy denominators corresponding to different intermediate states. For spin-1/2 particles, the non-propagating part i gamma^+ / (2 p^+) of the fermion propagator is identified as the source of additional complications requiring separate treatment.

## Key numbers
- The free fermion propagator is decomposed as i(p/+m)/(p^2 - m^2 + i epsilon) = i(p/_on + m)/(p^2 - m^2 + i epsilon) - i gamma^+/(2 p^+), where the second term is the non-propagating part.
- The on-shell minus-component of momentum is given by p^-_on = (p_perp^2 + m^2)/(2 p^+).
- For the box diagram, the k^- integral yields five distinct regions on the k^+ axis: k^+ < 0, 0 < k^+ < l^+, l^+ < k^+ < p^+, p^+ < k^+ < q^+ + p^+, and p^+ + q^+ < k^+, with the first and last regions giving vanishing contributions.
- The phase-space factor for the box diagram is phi = 16 k^+ (k^+ - l^+) (k^+ - p^+ - q^+) (k^+ - p^+).

## Context
This work extends Dirac's (1949) analysis of forms of relativistic dynamics and the later development of light-front quantization by Chang and Ma (1969) and Kogut and Soper (1970), who showed that Feynman diagrams could be reformulated in terms of light-cone variables. The paper addresses the technical difficulties that arise in light-front formulations, particularly the ill-posed Cauchy problem on null planes identified by Hormander and the zero-mode problem, by working directly at the diagrammatic level rather than through canonical quantization. The algorithm enables the systematic construction of light-cone time-ordered perturbation theory from covariant Feynman rules, which is relevant for applications in quantum chromodynamics and other gauge theories where light-front methods simplify the vacuum structure.
