# The y-scaling in the Delta-region

## Key claim

A scaling variable for electron scattering off nuclei in the Delta-region exists and a scaling law for a large variety of scattering data with different kinematics is found, but this property is very sensitive to the reaction mechanism and follows from drastic medium-induced modifications on the momentum and energy dependence of the Delta width. The scaling property is fulfilled on a nucleus but not on the single nucleon, indicating that the nuclear medium smooths the energy and momentum dependence of the Delta width.

## Method

The authors extended the West-scaling formalism from the quasielastic peak to the Delta-resonance region, defining a new scaling variable y_delta from a Free Fermi Gas model with a Delta propagator. They constructed a scaling function F_delta by factoring out the Mott cross section, the gamma-N-Delta transition form factor, and a kinematic factor, then fitted parameters for direct Delta electroexcitation and Kroll-Rudermann (pion-mediated) terms to experimental inclusive (e,e') data from multiple laboratories (SLAC, Bates, Saclay, others) on various nuclei including 12C. The scaling function was plotted against y_delta to test whether data from different kinematics collapse onto a single curve.

## Key numbers

- The scaling variable is defined as y_delta = (m_delta * (omega - m_delta + m)) / k - k/2, where m_delta is the Delta mass, m the nucleon mass, k the momentum transfer, and omega the energy transfer.
- The gamma-N-Delta form factor used two poles: Lambda_1 = 710 MeV/c and Lambda_2 = 1200 MeV/c.
- For the fit including direct and Kroll-Rudermann terms, m_N^4 * alpha / beta = 17.95 (with m_N the nucleon mass).
- For the fit including two pion rescatterings, m_N^4 * alpha / beta = 5.9 and beta approximately equal to -gamma.
- The scaling region where data collapse is -0.2 < y_delta < 0.2, corresponding to the full Delta-peak region.
- The ratio of the effective coupling constant for Delta-dominance to the coupling from pion photoproduction experiments is approximately 1.5.

## Context

This work extends the y-scaling concept pioneered by West (1975) and developed by the Rome group (Cenni, Ciofi degli Atti, Salme, 1989) from the quasielastic peak into the Delta-resonance region, where previous analyses had not demonstrated scaling. The authors show that while the free Delta width (with its k^3 dependence) destroys scaling, the nuclear medium flattens this dependence, consistent with theoretical predictions by Brown and Weise (1975) that in the medium Gamma scales as k^2 instead of k^3. The results enable extraction of the gamma-N-Delta transition form factor from experimental data and provide a tool to study pion propagation in the nuclear medium through the Kroll-Rudermann term, which is forbidden in vacuum but allowed in nuclei.
