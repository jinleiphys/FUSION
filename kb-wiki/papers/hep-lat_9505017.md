# QCD Phase Transition with Strange Quark in Wilson Formalism for Fermions

> The nature of QCD phase transition is studied with massless up and down quarks and a light strange quark, using the Wilson formalism for quarks on a lattice with the temporal direction extension $N_t=4$. We find that the phase transition is first order in the cases of both about 150 MeV and 400 MeV for the strange quark mass. These results together with those for three degenerate quarks suggest that QCD phase transition in nature is first order.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the QCD phase transition for physical quark masses (massless up and down, strange quark mass around 150 MeV) is first order, based on simulations using the Wilson fermion formalism on a lattice with temporal extension N_t=4. The authors hedge that this result conflicts with staggered fermion results from the Columbia group, and that both approaches may be far from the continuum limit.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the standard one-plaquette gauge action and Wilson quark actions for up, down, and strange quarks on lattices with temporal extension N_t=4 and spatial sizes 8^2 x 10 and 12^3. They used the hybrid R algorithm with molecular dynamics step Δτ=0.01 and employed an "on-K_c" simulation method where up and down quarks were set to massless (K_u=K_d=K_c) while varying the strange quark mass, using the number of iterations needed for quark matrix inversion as a phase transition indicator.

## Key numbers
- For the degenerate N_F=3 case, the transition at K=0.1795(5) and β=4.7 is first order, with quark mass m_q a = 0.175(2) and m_π/m_ρ = 0.873(6)
- The critical quark mass bound for three degenerate quarks is m_q^crit >~ 140 MeV, with (m_π/m_ρ)^crit ≥ 0.873(6)
- The inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} ~ 0.8 GeV estimated from the ρ meson mass
- For N_F=2+1, clear two-state signals were observed for strange quark masses of both about 150 MeV (at β=3.5) and about 400 MeV (at β=3.9)
- The tricritical strange quark mass m_s^* >~ 400 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Iwasaki et al., preliminary reports) on the chiral phase transition with Wilson quarks for two and three degenerate flavors, and contrasts with staggered fermion results from the Columbia group (1994) which found no first-order transition for physical quark masses. The results suggest that the physical QCD phase transition is first order, but the authors note that consistency between Wilson and staggered fermion approaches must be investigated in future work closer to the continuum limit.
