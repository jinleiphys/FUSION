# Shell-Model Calculations for_Λ^{16}O and_Λ^{17}O Using Microscopic Effective Interactions with ΣDegrees of Freedom

> Shell-model calculations in a large model space are performed for _Λ^{16}O and_Λ^{17}O. Effective interactions with degrees of freedom of Σin addition to Λand nucleons are derived from hyperon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions within the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach. Effects of the Σdegrees of freedom and the parity-mixing intershell coupling on the Λhypernuclear structure are investigated, employing the Nijmegen NSC97a-f and NSC89 hyperon-nucleon potentials.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations for _Λ^{16}O and _Λ^{17}O using effective interactions derived via the unitary-model-operator approach show that the Σ degrees of freedom can be well renormalized into a ΛN effective interaction, but the calculated Λ spin-orbit splittings for the NSC97 potentials are considerably larger than the experimental value of 152 ± 54 ± 36 keV from _Λ^{13}C, suggesting these potentials may overestimate the spin-orbit strength.

## Method

The authors derived effective YN interactions including both ΛN and ΣN channels from bare hyperon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen NSC97a-f and NSC89) and the Paris NN potential using the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA), which produces an energy-independent Hermitian effective interaction. They then performed shell-model diagonalizations in particle-hole model spaces for _Λ^{17}O (including d†_Λ|φ₀⟩ and d†_Λ a†b†|φ₀⟩ configurations) and _Λ^{16}O (including d†_Λ b†|φ₀⟩, d†_Σ b†|φ₀⟩, and higher configurations), using a common harmonic-oscillator frequency of ℏω = 14 MeV and removing spurious center-of-mass states.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle energies in _Λ^{17}O for NSC97a-f (ρ_Σ = 5): the 1/2⁻ state ranges from -3.29 MeV (NSC97a) to -1.65 MeV (NSC97f); the 3/2⁻ state ranges from -3.72 MeV (NSC97a) to -2.60 MeV (NSC97f)
- Λ spin-orbit splitting ΔE_ls in _Λ^{17}O ranges from 0.44 MeV (NSC97a) to 0.95 MeV (NSC97f), compared to the experimental value of 152 ± 54 ± 36 keV from _Λ^{13}C
- Ground-state doublet splitting ΔE_{1,0} = E(1₁⁻) - E(0₁⁻) in _Λ^{16}O ranges from 0.69 MeV (NSC97a) to 0.11 MeV (NSC97f)
- First-excited doublet splitting ΔE_{1,2} = E(1₂⁻) - E(2₁⁻) in _Λ^{16}O ranges from 0.96 MeV (NSC97a) to -0.12 MeV (NSC97f), showing a level inversion for NSC97f
- For the NSC89 potential, the ground-state doublet splitting increases from 0.17 MeV (ΛN only) to 0.61 MeV (ρ_Σ = 5), and the first-excited doublet splitting from 0.26 MeV to 0.81 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UMOA calculations of Λ single-particle energies in _Λ^{17}O and _Λ^{41}Ca (Fujii et al., 1999, 2000) to include full shell-model diagonalization for _Λ^{16}O, explicitly treating Σ degrees of freedom rather than only renormalizing them into a ΛN effective interaction as done in earlier approaches. The results contrast with those of Tzeng et al. (1999, 2000), who used G-matrix plus core polarization and folded diagrams with a two-frequency shell model; the present work finds agreement with their Λ-Σ three-body force effects for the NSC89 potential but not uniformly for the NSC97 potentials. The calculations enable systematic testing of modern YN interactions against high-resolution γ-ray spectroscopy data from BNL (Tamura et al., 2001) and provide a framework for microscopic shell-model studies of heavier Λ hypernuclei.
