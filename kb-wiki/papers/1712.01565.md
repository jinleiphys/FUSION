# Value of the axial-vector coupling strength in $β$ and $ββ$ decays: A review

> In this review the quenching of the weak axial-vector coupling strength, $g_{\rm A}$, is discussed in nuclear $β$ and double-$β$ decays. On one hand, the nuclear-medium and nuclear many-body effects are separated, and on the other hand the quenching is discussed from the points of view of different many-body methods and different $β$-decay and double-$β$-decay processes. Both the historical background and the present status are reviewed and contrasted against each other. The theoretical considerations are tied to performed and planned measurements, and possible new measurements are urged, whenever relevant and doable. Relation of the quenching problem to the measurements of charge-exchange reactions and muon-capture rates is pointed out.

## Key claim

This review argues that the effective value of the axial-vector coupling strength \(g_{\rm A}\) in nuclear \(\beta\) and double-\(\beta\) decays is not a single universal number but depends on the nuclear many-body framework, the specific decay process, and the momentum transfer involved, with the caveat that nuclear-medium effects (meson-exchange currents and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom) cannot be cleanly separated from nuclear-model deficiencies in most analyses.

## Method

The author reviews historical and contemporary studies of \(g_{\rm A}\) quenching across multiple nuclear models: the interacting shell model (ISM), the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA), the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2), and the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2). The review separates nuclear-medium effects (meson-exchange currents, \(\Delta\)-isobar admixtures, chiral effective field theory two-body currents) from nuclear-model effects (limited single-particle spaces, seniority truncation, closure approximations) and examines their impact on allowed Gamow-Teller decays, first-forbidden unique and non-unique decays, higher-forbidden decays, spin-multipole strength functions, and nuclear muon capture.

## Key numbers

* Free-nucleon value of the axial-vector coupling: \(g^{\rm free}_{\rm A} = 1.2723(23)\) from neutron beta decay (Patrignani 2016).
* Fundamental quenching factor for infinite nuclear matter: \(q^{\infty}_{\rm F} = 0.76\), giving \(g^{\infty}_{\rm AeF} = 0.96\) (Rho 1974).
* Fundamental quenching factor for finite nuclei (\(A=50-150\)): \(q_{\rm F} = 0.88\), giving \(g_{\rm AeF} \approx 1.1\) (Wilkinson 1974).
* Fundamental quenching for very light nuclei (\(A\le 17\)): \(q_{\rm F} = 0.9\), giving \(g_{\rm AeF} = 1.1\) (Ericson 1971).
* Effective \(g_{\rm A}\) from nuclear-medium-corrected transition operators (Siiskonen 2001): \(g^{\rm eff}_{\rm A} = 1.0\) (1s0d shell), 0.98 (1p0f shell), 0.71 (\({}^{56}\)Ni), 0.52 (\({}^{100}\)Sn).
* Quenching from chiral effective field theory two-body currents in light nuclei: \(q = 0.92-0.96\) for \({}^{14}\)C and \({}^{22,24}\)O (Ekstrom 2014).
* Lattice QCD calculation of free-nucleon \(g_{\rm A}\): \(1.278(21)(26)\) (Berkowitz 2017).
* Effect of two-body currents on \(0\nu\beta\beta\) nuclear matrix elements: \(-35\%\) to \(+10\%\) in ISM for \(A=48-136\) (Menendez 2011); \(10-22\%\) quenching in pnQRPA for \(A=48-136\) (Engel 2014).

## Context

This review extends the long-standing discussion of \(g_{\rm A}\) quenching that began with the early shell-model studies of Gamow-Teller decays and the theoretical work of Ericson (1971, 1973), Rho (1974), and Wilkinson (1974) on meson-exchange currents and the partially conserved axial-current hypothesis. It contrasts the historical ISM results (Towner 1997) with modern pnQRPA calculations (Suhonen 2010, 2011b) and chiral effective field theory approaches (Menendez 2011, Ekstrom 2014). The review enables future work by identifying the need for more measurements of \(\beta\)-spectrum shapes, charge-exchange reactions, and muon-capture rates in medium-heavy nuclei to disentangle nuclear-medium effects from many-body deficiencies, and by pointing toward ab initio methods (coupled-cluster theory, in-medium similarity renormalization group) as the path to a unified low-energy renormalization of \(g_{\rm A}\).
