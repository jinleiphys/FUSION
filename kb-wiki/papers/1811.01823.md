# Measurement of Nuclear Transparency Ratios for Protons and Neutrons

> This paper presents, for the first time, measurements of neutron transparency ratios for nuclei relative to C measured using the (e,e'n) reaction, spanning measured neutron momenta of 1.4 to 2.4 GeV/c. The transparency ratios were extracted in two kinematical regions, corresponding to knockout of mean-field nucleons and to the breakup of Short-Range Correlated nucleon pairs. The extracted neutron transparency ratios are consistent with each other for the two measured kinematical regions and agree with the proton transparencies extracted from new and previous (e,e'p) measurements, including those from neutron-rich nuclei such as lead. The data also agree with and confirm the Glauber approximation that is commonly used to interpret experimental data. The nuclear-mass-dependence of the extracted transparencies scales as A^α with α = -0.289 {\pm} 0.007, which is consistent with nuclear-surface dominance of the reactions.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first measurements of neutron transparency ratios for nuclei relative to carbon using the (e,e'n) reaction, finding that neutron transparencies are consistent with proton transparencies even in neutron-rich nuclei like lead, and that the data confirm the Glauber approximation commonly used to interpret experimental data.

## Method

The authors measured quasi-elastic (e,e'n) and (e,e'p) reactions on carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead targets using a 5.01 GeV electron beam incident on the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab. Transparency ratios were extracted in two kinematic regimes: mean-field (MF) knockout of nucleons with initial momenta below the Fermi momentum, and short-range correlated (SRC) pair breakup kinematics with high missing momentum. For neutron detection, an alternative set of event selection cuts was developed to overcome poor neutron momentum reconstruction resolution, using a data-driven Monte Carlo approach where measured (e,e'p) events were smeared according to neutron momentum resolution to estimate selection efficiency and purity.

## Key numbers

- Neutron transparency ratios were measured for nucleon momenta spanning 1.4 to 2.4 GeV/c
- The nuclear-mass-dependence of transparencies scales as A^alpha with alpha = -0.289 +/- 0.007, consistent with nuclear-surface dominance
- Radiative corrections to transparency ratios were approximately 1% for Al/C, 5% for Fe/C, and 6% for Pb/C
- The alternative selection cuts for neutron events achieved about 90% efficiency with about 10% contamination
- The systematic uncertainty from the mean-field momentum distribution integral calculation was 4.9% (3.8%), 4.2% (5.7%), and 4.3% (4.5%) for protons (neutrons) for Al/C, Fe/C, and Pb/C ratios respectively

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of proton transparency ratios from (e,e'p) reactions by Garino (1992), O'Neill (1995), Abbott (1998), and Garrow (2002) to include neutron knockout for the first time. The data validate the Glauber approximation calculations by Frankfurt (1996), Panda (2003), and Ryckebusch (2003) that are widely used in analyses of nuclear, hadronic, neutrino, and heavy-ion physics experiments. The results enable improved baseline determinations for studies of in-medium nucleon modifications, color transparency effects, and reaction rate estimations in high-energy nuclear physics.
