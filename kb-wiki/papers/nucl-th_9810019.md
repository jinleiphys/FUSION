# Multipole Analyses for p(gamma,pi) and p(gamma,gamma) in the region of the P33 delta Resonance

> Multipole analyses of the p(g,pi0), p(g,pi+) and p(g,g) reactions are carried out using different data sets. With sufficient constraints from polarization observables, the ratio of E2/M1 transition amplitudes for N->delta (EMR) appears to be largely insensitive to differences between recent p(g,pi0) cross section measurements. We deduce a current best estimate of EMR = -(2.85 +/-0.34 +/-0.21)%. Back angle Compton cross sections require a value for the backward spin polarizability that is significantly lower than previous expectations, with a magnitude that is coupled to the (g,pi) cross sections.

## Key claim

With sufficient constraints from polarization observables, the ratio of E2/M1 transition amplitudes for N→Δ (EMR) appears to be largely insensitive to differences between recent p(γ,π0) cross section measurements, yielding a current best estimate of EMR = -(2.85 ± 0.34 ± 0.21)%, though this result depends on the weighting assigned to older Bonn data that cover extreme angles.

## Method

The authors performed energy-dependent multipole analyses of the p(γ,π0), p(γ,π+) and p(γ,γ) reactions in the energy region from 200 MeV to 350 MeV, expanding the π-production amplitude into electric and magnetic partial waves with a K-matrix-like unitarization. They used Compton scattering data to constrain the photo-pion multipoles through dispersion integrals, fitting both the pion-multipole parameters and the proton polarizabilities simultaneously. Separate analyses were performed using π-production cross sections from either LEGS or Mainz and Bonn, but not both simultaneously, to avoid averaging over discrepant data sets.

## Key numbers

- EMR (E2/M1 ratio for N→Δ) = -(2.85 ± 0.34 ± 0.21)%, where the first uncertainty combines statistical and systematic scale errors and the second is model-dependent
- Backward spin polarizability δπ = 27.1 ± 2.2 (stat+sys) when fitting Compton data up to 2π threshold (309 MeV) using LEGS multipoles, with an additional model-dependent uncertainty of +2.8/-2.4
- When data up to 350 MeV are included with LEGS multipoles, δπ = 25.1 ± 2.1
- Using HDT multipoles, δπ = 21.4 ± 0.9; using SP97K multipoles, δπ = 20.9 ± 0.8
- The difference of electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities, ᾱ - β̄ = 10.11 ± 1.74 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), from fits including Compton data up to 2π threshold
- The global average from all data below π-production threshold for ᾱ - β̄ = 10.0 ± 1.5 (stat+sys) ± 0.9 (model)

## Context

This work extends earlier multipole analyses of pion photo-production and Compton scattering in the Δ(1232) resonance region, addressing discrepancies between recent cross section measurements from LEGS and Mainz (Beck 1997). The authors demonstrate that polarization observables stabilize the extracted EMR value, resolving ambiguities first pointed out by Donnachie (1973) regarding truncation of multipole expansions. The finding of a backward spin polarizability significantly lower than the π°-dominated expectation of 36.6 challenges existing chiral perturbation theory calculations (Holstein 1997) and motivates future measurements of beam-target double-polarization observables to independently verify this result.
