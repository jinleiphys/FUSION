# Extrapolation of the astrophysical S factor for ^7Be(p,gamma)^8B to solar energies

> We investigate the energy dependence of the astrophysical $S$ factor for the reaction $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B, the primary source of high-energy solar neutrinos in the solar $pp$ chain. Using simple models we explore the model dependence in the extrapolation of the experimental data to the region of astrophysical interest near 20 keV. We find that below approximately 400 keV the energy dependence is very well understood and constrained by the data for the elastic scattering of low energy neutrons from $^7$Li. Above 400 keV nuclear distortion of the wave function of the incident proton introduces a significant model dependence. This is particularly important for the s-wave contribution to the $S$ factor. The extracted value of S(0) is $19.0 \pm 1.0 \pm 0.2$~eVb. The first error is experimental while the second is an estimate of the theoretical error in the extrapolation.

# Extrapolation of the astrophysical S factor for ^7Be(p,gamma)^8B to solar energies

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the astrophysical S factor for ^7Be(p,gamma)^8B is well understood and constrained below approximately 400 keV by elastic scattering data of low-energy neutrons from ^7Li, but above 400 keV nuclear distortion of the incident proton wave function introduces significant model dependence, particularly for the s-wave contribution. The extracted value of S(0) is 19.0 ± 1.0 ± 0.2 eVb, where the first error is experimental and the second is an estimate of the theoretical error in the extrapolation.

## Method

The authors developed a hard-sphere model with a cutoff radius to approximate nuclear distortion effects, where the initial-state wave function is set to zero inside the cutoff radius and treated as a pure Coulomb wave outside. They compared this simple model to more sophisticated Woods-Saxon potential models, R-matrix calculations, and microscopic generator-coordinate method (GCM) cluster models, using the elastic scattering data of neutrons from ^7Li (the mirror system) to constrain the nuclear potentials. The S factor was parametrized using a rational approximation based on a Laurent series expansion that accounts for the pole at the photon energy vanishing, with the pole position fixed by the separation energy of the valence proton from ^8B (137.5 keV).

## Key numbers

- S(0) = 19.0 ± 1.0 ± 0.2 eVb (first error experimental, second theoretical)
- Separation energy of the valence proton from ^8B: 137.5 keV (determines the pole location)
- For a hard sphere radius of 2.4 fm, the Taylor series expansion about E=0 MeV gives: S(E)/S(0) = 1 - 1.917 E + 15.69 E^2 - 110.28 E^3 + 774.1 E^4 + ... (E in MeV)
- The rational approximation S(E)/S(0) ≈ a/(E_B + E) + b + cE with a = 0.0408 MeV, b = 0.7033, c = 0.2392 MeV^{-1} is valid to better than 1% up to 400 keV
- Scattering lengths for thermal neutrons from ^7Li: a_1 = 0.87 ± 0.07 fm and a_2 = -3.63 ± 0.05 fm (used to constrain potential depths)
- The integrands for the s- and d-wave capture peak at very large radii: 40 fm and 55 fm respectively, extending well beyond 100 fm
- The d-wave nuclear distortion effect is at most 2% even at 1.5 MeV
- A straight line fit to experimental data gives S(20 keV) = 15.3 eVb while a hard sphere model with radius 4.1 fm gives S(20 keV) = 21.0 eVb, a 37% variation

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Christy and Duck (1961) and addresses the long-standing problem of extrapolating the ^7Be(p,gamma)^8B S factor to solar energies near 20 keV, which is critical for predicting the high-energy solar neutrino flux from the pp chain. The authors introduce a rational approximation based on the pole structure of the S factor, improving upon the Taylor series expansions used by Williams and Koonin (1996) and Baye et al. (1997), and demonstrate that the hard-sphere model with an effective radius can reproduce the energy dependence of more sophisticated Woods-Saxon potential models (Barker 1980) and GCM calculations (Descouvemont and Baye 1994, Csoto et al. 1995). The analysis enables a more reliable extrapolation by constraining the model dependence through neutron scattering data from the mirror system ^7Li-n, and identifies that the DB and C2B GCM models give the best consistency among the experimental data sets of Filippone et al. (1983), Vaughn et al. (1970), and Hammache et al. (1998).
