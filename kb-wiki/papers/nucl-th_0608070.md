# The Letter "s" (and the sQGP)

> Data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider over the last five years has led many to conclude that the medium created is not the expected quark gluon plasma (QGP), but rather a strongly coupled or strongly interacting quark gluon plasma (sQGP). We explore the meaning of this possible paradigm shift and some of the experimental and theoretical arguments that are associated with it. In this proceedings we detail only a small subset of the relevant issues as discussed at the Hot Quarks 2006 workshop.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the term "sQGP" (strongly coupled or strongly interacting quark gluon plasma) represents a possible paradigm shift from the expected weakly coupled QGP, but the author emphasizes that the evidence for this shift is based on analogies and interpretations that may not be fully justified, and that the term is used inconsistently across the field.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from RHIC (elliptic flow \(v_2\), jet quenching, heavy quark suppression) and theoretical arguments (hydrodynamic calculations, perturbative QCD estimates, lattice QCD results, AdS/CFT duality) to examine whether the medium created in heavy ion collisions is better described as a strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) rather than a weakly coupled QGP (wQGP). The paper critically evaluates three different interpretations of the letter "s": plasma coupling parameter \(\Gamma\), shear viscosity over entropy density \(\eta/s\), and the QCD coupling \(\alpha_s\), and discusses the limitations of each.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at early times (\(t=1\) fm/c) is above the QGP phase transition value of about 2 GeV/fm\(^3\), though estimates disagree by more than a factor of two.
- Hydrodynamic calculations indicate rapid equilibration times of order \(t \approx 1\) fm/c.
- The plasma coupling parameter \(\Gamma\) for QGP is estimated as \(\Gamma \approx 1.5-5\).
- Perturbative calculations of gluon scattering give equilibration times \(> 2.6\) fm/c, which are too long to explain observed elliptic flow.
- Radiative energy loss calculations (GLV) yield \(dN/dy(\text{gluons}) = 1000\) or \(dN/dy(\text{quarks,gluons}) = 2000\), while entropy constraints give \(dN/dy(\text{quarks,gluons}) < 1300\).
- Effective gluon mass at \(T \approx 250\) MeV is \(mg^{*} \approx 300\) MeV.
- Lattice QCD results show \(\epsilon/T^4\) approaches 80% of the non-interacting gas limit.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion from earlier work by Gyulassy and McLerran (2005) who proposed criteria for QGP discovery and introduced the term sQGP, and by Shuryak and collaborators who proposed the cQGP (classical QGP) model. It also references lattice QCD results from Karsch and others showing that the QGP is not an ideal gas, and AdS/CFT calculations by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004) suggesting a lower bound on \(\eta/s\). The paper enables future work by clarifying the need for quantitatively reliable limits on \(\eta/s\) from three-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations and by highlighting unresolved issues such as the role of \(2 \rightarrow 3\) and \(3 \rightarrow 2\) processes in equilibration.
