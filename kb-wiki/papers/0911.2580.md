# Baryon Resonances

> In this talk I show recent results on how many excited baryon resonances appear as systems of one meson and one baryon, or two mesons and one baryon, with the mesons being either pseudoscalar or vectors. Connection with experiment is made including a discussion on old predictions and recent results for the photoproduction of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance, as well as the prediction of one $1/2^+$ baryon state around 1920 MeV which might have been seen in the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ reaction.

## Key claim

Many excited baryon resonances can be understood as dynamically generated states arising from the interaction of one meson and one baryon, or two mesons and one baryon, rather than as simple quark excitations; the authors hedge that while the approach has great predictive power, some predictions might not be fulfilled in Nature.

## Method

The authors use chiral Lagrangians and the hidden gauge formalism for vector mesons to construct transition potentials for meson-baryon and vector-baryon interactions. They solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in an on-shell factorization approach to find poles in the scattering matrices, which correspond to resonances. For two-meson-one-baryon systems, they solve Faddeev equations using pair interactions obtained from the chiral unitary approach.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1405)$ photoproduction cross sections predicted in 1998 were found to be within 50% of the experimental data from Spring8 and Jefferson Lab.
- A $1/2^+$ baryon state is predicted around 1920 MeV from a $K \bar{K} N$ system, possibly seen in the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ reaction.
- For vector-baryon octet interaction, a pole is found at $1977 + i 53$ MeV (mass 1972 MeV, width 64 MeV) associated with $N(2080)$ and $N(2090)$.
- A $\Lambda$ state is found at $1784 + i 4$ MeV (mass 1783 MeV, width 9 MeV) associated with $\Lambda(1690)$ and $\Lambda(1800)$.
- For vector-decuplet interaction, a $\Delta$ state is found at $1972 + i 49$ MeV (mass 1971 MeV, width 52 MeV) associated with $\Delta(1900)$, $\Delta(1940)$, and $\Delta(1930)$.
- A $\Xi$ state is found at $2039 + i 67$ MeV (mass 2039 MeV, width 64 MeV) associated with $\Xi(1950)$.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to baryon resonances pioneered by Kaiser (1995) and collaborators, which successfully described the $N^*(1535)$ and the two $\Lambda(1405)$ states. It introduces the novel use of the hidden gauge formalism for vector mesons to generate higher-mass resonances, complementing quark model approaches that struggle with states like the $\Delta(5/2^-)$ which requires a $3\hbar\omega$ excitation. The results enable experimental searches for predicted spin partners of known states and motivate new measurements of $\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p$ cross sections near threshold.
