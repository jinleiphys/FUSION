# Elliptic flow of identified particles in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> In this paper, by using a Tsallis-Pareto-type function and the multisource thermal model, the elliptic flow coefficients of particles $π^{\pm }$, $K^{\pm }$, $p+\overline{p}$, $Λ+\overline{% Λ}$, and $K_{S}^{0}$ produced in Pb-Pb collisions at the center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV are investigated. In the process of collisional evolution, because of geometric structure, pressure gradient, and thermal diffusion effects, deformation and translation occurred in the isotropic emission source, leading to anisotropy in the azimuth distribution of the final-state particles. Based on these dynamic factors, the dependence of elliptic flow on transverse momentum is described as well.

## Key claim

The Tsallis-Pareto-type function combined with the multisource thermal model can describe the elliptic flow of identified particles in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the effective temperature extracted from the fit includes both thermal motion and flow effects, making it higher than the true kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Method

The authors used the multisource thermal model, where many local isotropic emission sources are formed along the incident direction, and introduced a Tsallis-Pareto-type function to generate transverse momentum distributions via Monte Carlo methods. To account for anisotropy, they introduced deformation and translation parameters for the emission source along the Ox axis, with the deformation factor \(a_x\) parameterized as an empirical function of transverse momentum. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) was then calculated as the average of \((p_x^{\prime 2} - p_y^2)/(p_x^{\prime 2} + p_y^2)\) and fitted to experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV for centrality intervals 0–70% and rapidity \(|y| < 0.5\).
- For \(\pi^{\pm}\), \(K^{\pm}\), and \(p+\overline{p}\), measurements in ultra-central collisions (0–1%) are also included.
- The non-extensivity parameter \(n\) was fixed to 9 for all fits.
- A data point for \(\Lambda + \overline{\Lambda}\) at \(p_T = 9\) GeV/c in the 60–70% centrality interval deviates seriously from the fitted value.
- The parameter \(k_1\) reaches a maximum when the centrality percentage reaches about 30%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the multisource thermal model (Author, 2015; Author, 2016; Author, 2017) by covering a larger transverse momentum range and more accurately identified particles. The model builds on the one-dimensional string model (Author, 1983), the fireball model (Author, 1976), and the thermalized cylinder model (Author, 1998; Author, 2000). The results enable further extraction of the real kinetic freeze-out temperature by disentangling thermal motion from flow effects in the effective temperature parameter.
