# Beyond-mean-field-model analysis of low-spin normal-deformed and superdeformed collective states of S32, Ar36, Ar38 and Ca40

> We investigate the coexistence of spherical, deformed and superdeformed states at low spin in S32, Ar36, Ar38 and Ca40. The microscopic states are constructed by configuration mixing of BCS states projected on good particle number and angular momentum. The BCS states are themselves obtained from Hartree-Fock BCS calculations using the Skyrme interaction SLy6 for the particle-hole channel, and a density-dependent contact force in the pairing channel. The same interaction is used within the Generator Coordinate Method to determine the configuration mixing and calculate the properties of even-spin states with positive parity. Our calculations underestimate moments of inertia. Nevertheless, for the four nuclei, the global structural properties of the states of normal deformation as well as the recently discovered superdeformed bands up to spin 6 are correctly reproduced with regard to both the energies and the transition rates.

## Key claim

The authors' beyond-mean-field calculation, using configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected BCS states with the Skyrme interaction SLy6, correctly reproduces the global structural properties of normal-deformed and superdeformed bands up to spin 6 in S32, Ar36, Ar38, and Ca40, although the calculated moments of inertia are systematically underestimated.

## Method

The authors constructed microscopic states by configuration mixing of BCS states that were projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with the Skyrme interaction SLy6 for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent contact force for the pairing channel. Constrained Hartree-Fock-BCS (CHFBCS) equations were solved on a three-dimensional mesh with a constraint on the axial mass quadrupole moment operator, and the Lipkin-Nogami prescription was applied to prevent pairing collapse. The resulting particle-number and angular-momentum projected states were then diagonalized within the GCM to obtain orthogonal collective states for even-spin positive-parity states up to spin 10.

## Key numbers

- Ca40: GCM ground state correlation energy (difference between spherical configuration and GCM ground state) is 0.526 MeV.
- Ca40: Calculated monopole transition matrix element |M(E0)| = 1.3 e fm², compared to experimental value (2.6 ± 0.1) e fm².
- Ca40: Experimental transition quadrupole moment for the superdeformed band is Q_c2^(t) = 180^(+39)_(-29) e fm²; calculated values are in good agreement.
- Ca40: Experimental transition quadrupole moment for the normal-deformed band is Q_c2^(t) = 74 ± 14 e fm²; calculated values for the 2_1⁺ and 6_2⁺ transitions agree.
- Ca40: Superdeformed bandhead excitation energy from GCM is 6.52 MeV, compared to experimental value 5.21 MeV.
- Ar38: Calculated transition quadrupole moments for the superdeformed band: Q_c2^(t) = 160 e fm² for 2_2⁺ → 0_3⁺, 147 e fm² for 4_2⁺ → 2_2⁺, and 140 e fm² for 6_1⁺ → 4_2⁺.
- Ar38: Experimental excitation energies for superdeformed band: 4.57 MeV (2⁺), 6.05 MeV (4⁺), 7.49 MeV (6⁺); calculated values are 6.61 MeV, 8.11 MeV, and 10.34 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier beyond-mean-field studies on 16O (Bender 2002) to the sd-shell nuclei S32, Ar36, Ar38, and Ca40, where recent experiments with Euroball and Gammasphere had uncovered normal-deformed and superdeformed rotational bands (Ide 2001, Svensson 2000, 2001, Rudolph 2002). The method bridges the gap between cranked mean-field approaches (Ide 2001) and full shell-model diagonalizations (Caurier 2002) by restoring symmetries broken at the mean-field level and mixing configurations via the Generator Coordinate Method, enabling a description of shape coexistence without assuming an inert core or making a priori choices about the basis.
