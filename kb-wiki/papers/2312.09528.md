# QCD at finite temperature and density: Criticality

> We overview recent theoretical developments in the search for QCD critical point at finite temperature and density, including from lattice QCD, effective QCD theories, and proton number cumulants in heavy-ion collisions. We summarize the available constraints and predictions for the critical point location and discuss future challenges and opportunities.

# QCD at finite temperature and density: Criticality

## Key claim
The QCD critical point, if it exists, is constrained to lie at finite baryon density with recent theoretical estimates converging around T_CP ~ 100-120 MeV and mu_B^CP ~ 500-650 MeV, but these predictions carry large systematic uncertainties and rely on assumptions about the validity of extrapolation methods and effective model approximations that can be questioned.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice QCD extrapolations from mu_B = 0 using Taylor expansion coefficients up to chi_8^B and multi-point Pade approximants to locate Yang-Lee edge singularities in the complex plane, which are then fitted with Z(2) scaling to estimate the critical point location. Effective QCD approaches including Dyson-Schwinger equations, functional renormalization group, and holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton models are used to map the full phase diagram, while heavy-ion collision data on proton number cumulants from STAR and HADES are compared to non-critical hydrodynamic baselines to search for critical fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD extrapolations show no indications for criticality at moderate values of mu_B/T less than or equal to 3 (Parotto, Clarke).
- Pade-based estimates from lattice data report T_CP ~ 90-100 MeV and mu_B^CP ~ 500-600 MeV (Goswami, Basar).
- Functional approaches predict T ~ 100-120 MeV and mu_B ~ 600-650 MeV (Gunkel 2021, Fu 2019, Fu 2023).
- Holographic EMD model places CP at T ~ 105 MeV and mu_B ~ 580 MeV (Hippert 2023).
- All approaches converge to T_CP ~ 100-120 MeV and mu_B^CP/T_CP ~ 5-6.
- Collision energy range of sqrt(s_NN) ~ 3-5 GeV is in closest proximity to predicted CP location.
- Proton number scaled variance excess over non-critical baseline appears at sqrt(s_NN) less than or equal to 10 GeV (STAR BES-I).
- Measurements at 3 GeV (STAR) and 2.4 GeV (HADES) show excess persists toward lower energies.
- Non-critical hydrodynamics baseline matches data at sqrt(s_NN) greater than or equal to 20 GeV.
- Proton factorial cumulants relate to baryon factorial cumulants as C_n^p/C_1^p ~ (1/2^(n-1)) * C_n^B/C_1^B, giving 50% reduction in scaled second moment.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing search for the QCD critical point motivated by the beam energy scan program at RHIC (Bzdak 2019), following the establishment of analytic crossover at vanishing net baryon density from lattice QCD (Aoki 2006). The work summarizes constraints from lattice QCD extrapolations, effective QCD theories including Dyson-Schwinger and functional renormalization group (Gunkel 2021, Fu 2019, Fu 2023), and holographic approaches (Hippert 2023), alongside experimental comparisons with non-critical hydrodynamic baselines (Vovchenko 2021). The results enable future searches at STAR Fixed Target program and CBM at FAIR, while highlighting the need for improved dynamical modeling of critical fluctuations and better control over systematic uncertainties in both theoretical and experimental approaches.
