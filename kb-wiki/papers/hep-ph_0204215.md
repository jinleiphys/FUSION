# The Lightest Scalar Nonet as Higgs Bosons of Strong Interactions

> I discuss how an extra light scalar meson multiplet could be understood as an effective Higgs nonet of a hidden local U(3) symmetry. There is growing evidence that low energy data requires in addition to a conventional (q bar q) nonet near 1.4 GeV, another light scalar nonet-like structure below 1 GeV, (sigma(600), a_0(980), f_0(980), kappa), which could be interpreted as such a Higgs nonet.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an extra light scalar meson nonet below 1 GeV (sigma(600), a_0(980), f_0(980), kappa) can be interpreted as an effective Higgs nonet of a hidden local U(3) symmetry, but only if one assumes that both the conventional q-bar-q scalar nonet and the light scalar nonet have non-zero vacuum expectation values and that a small coupling between them breaks the relative symmetry.

## Method

The author constructs a model with two coupled linear sigma models, each containing a 3x3 matrix field (Phi for conventional q-bar-q states above 1 GeV, and hat-Phi for light states below 1 GeV). A small epsilon-squared coupling term is introduced between the two multiplets, and both fields are given vacuum expectation values. The overall axial U(3) symmetry is then gauged, and the massless pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons are gauged away through a hidden local symmetry mechanism, giving mass to axial vector mesons instead.

## Key numbers

* The conventional scalar nonet from the simple U(3)xU(3) linear sigma model (with five parameters fixed by experimental m_pi^2, m_K^2, (m_eta^2+m_eta'^2), f_pi and f_K) gives a broad sigma near 650 MeV, an a_0 at 1040 MeV, an f_0 near 1200 MeV, and a broad kappa near 1120 MeV.
* The mixing angle between the two pseudoscalar nonets is given by theta = arctan(v/hat-v), where v and hat-v are the vacuum expectation values of the two fields.
* The mass of the massive pseudoscalar nonet is epsilon-squared times (v^2+hat-v^2)/(v*hat-v).
* The axial vector meson mass from the Higgs mechanism is m_A^2 = 2g^2(v^2+hat-v^2).
* The ratio of scalar-to-two-pseudoscalar couplings between the two multiplets is g_{hat-sigma pi pi}/g_{sigma pi pi} = v/hat-v = tan(theta).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate over the light scalar mesons below 1.5 GeV, where there are too many states to be explained by a single q-bar-q nonet. It builds on the proposal by Close and Tornqvist that there is an additional nonet below 1 GeV with large 4-quark and meson-meson components, and on Jaffe's (1977) work on 4-quark states. The model also relates to the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando et al. and to the Syracuse group's (Black et al.) work on instanton effects as a source of symmetry breaking. The scheme predicts a doubled light scalar meson spectrum, which the author claims matches the experimental situation, but notes that detailed comparison requires including loops and unitarizing the model.
