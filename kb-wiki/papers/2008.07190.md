# Triangle singularity as the origin of $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ observed in $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$

> The LHCb collaboration reported the observation of a narrow peak in the $D^- K^+$ invariant mass distributions from the $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$ decay. The peak is parameterized in terms of two resonances $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ with the quark contents $\bar{c}\bar{s}ud$, and their spin-parity quantum numbers are $0^+$ and $1^-$, respectively. We investigate the rescattering processes which may contribute to the $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$ decays. It is shown that the $D^{*-}K^{*+}$ rescattering via the $χ_{c1}K^{*+}D^{*-}$ loop or the $\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ rescattering via the $D_{sJ}^{+}\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ loop simulate the $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ structures. Such phenomena are due to the analytical property of the scattering amplitudes with the triangle singularities located to the vicinity of the physical boundary.

# Triangle singularity as the origin of $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ observed in $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$

## Key claim
The resonance-like structures $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ reported by LHCb in $D^-K^+$ invariant mass distributions can be reproduced by triangle singularity (TS) mechanisms from rescattering processes, without introducing genuine exotic tetraquark states. The authors show that the $D^{*-}K^{*+}$ rescattering via a $\chi_{c1}K^{*+}D^{*-}$ loop and the $\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ rescattering via a $D_{sJ}^{+}\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ loop produce peaks with the correct spin-parity quantum numbers ($0^+$ and $1^-$, respectively) when the triangle singularity lies near the physical boundary.

## Method
The authors computed the $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$ decay amplitude via two triangle loop diagrams: (a) $B^+\to \chi_{c1}K^{*+}$ followed by $\chi_{c1}\to D^+ D^{*-}$ and $D^{*-}K^{*+}\to D^-K^+$; (b) $B^+\to D_{sJ}^{+}\bar{D}_{1}^{0}$ followed by $D_{sJ}^{+}\to D^+ K^0$ and $\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}\to D^-K^+$. They used Breit-Wigner propagators with complex masses to account for the widths of intermediate unstable particles, and evaluated the loop integrals to obtain the $D^-K^+$ invariant mass distributions. The spin-parity quantum numbers of the final $D^-K^+$ system were determined by angular momentum and parity conservation at the strong interaction vertices.

## Key numbers
- $M_{X_0(2900)} = 2.866 \pm 0.007$ GeV, $\Gamma_{X_0(2900)} = 57.2 \pm 12.9$ MeV (LHCb measured values for the $0^+$ structure)
- $M_{X_1(2900)} = 2.904 \pm 0.005$ GeV, $\Gamma_{X_1(2900)} = 110.3 \pm 11.5$ MeV (LHCb measured values for the $1^-$ structure)
- $D^{*-}K^{*+}$ threshold = 2902 MeV; $\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ threshold = 2918 MeV (both close to the observed $X_0$ and $X_1$ masses)
- For the $\chi_{c1}K^{*+}D^{*-}$ loop, when $4311 \leq m_{\chi_{c1}} \leq 4388$ MeV, the triangle singularity lies on the physical boundary (ignoring widths); $\chi_{c1}(4274)$ falls near this region
- For the $D_{sJ}^{+}\bar{D}_{1}^{0}K^{0}$ loop, when $2539 \leq m_{D_{sJ}} \leq 2858$ MeV, the triangle singularity lies on the physical boundary; $D_{s1}^{*}(2700)$ and $D_{s1}^{*}(2860)$ fall in this range
- $K^{*+}$ width set to 50 MeV; $\bar{D}_1(2420)^0$ width set to 32 MeV in the broadened calculations

## Context
This work provides a non-resonance interpretation of the $X_0(2900)$ and $X_1(2900)$ states reported by LHCb in $B^+\to D^+ D^- K^+$ decays, as an alternative to tetraquark interpretations such as those proposed by Cheng (2020) using a color-magnetic interaction model (which predicted a scalar tetraquark at 2850 MeV) and by Karliner (2020) interpreting $X_0(2900)$ as a compact $\bar{c}\bar{s}ud$ isosinglet tetraquark. The triangle singularity mechanism has been previously applied to other $XYZ$ exotic candidates, as reviewed by Guo (2019). The results suggest that future experiments should test whether these peaks persist with different production mechanisms, which would distinguish genuine resonances from kinematic enhancements.
