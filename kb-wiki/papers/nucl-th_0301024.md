# Concerning the quark condensate

> A continuum expression for the trace of the massive dressed-quark propagator is used to explicate a connection between the infrared limit of the QCD Dirac operator's spectrum and the quark condensate appearing in the operator product expansion, and the connection is verified via comparison with a lattice-QCD simulation. The pseudoscalar vacuum polarisation provides a good approximation to the condensate over a larger range of current-quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the operator product expansion (OPE) quark condensate, which sets the ultraviolet scale of the dressed-quark mass function, is directly related to the density of near-zero eigenvalues of the massless Dirac operator via the Banks-Casher relation, but only when the current-quark bare mass satisfies \(m^{\rm bm}(\Lambda) \lesssim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^3 / \Lambda^2\); for larger masses the pseudoscalar vacuum polarisation provides a more accurate measure.

## Method

The authors use the trace of the renormalised massive dressed-quark propagator, defined via the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in the rainbow truncation, to derive a spectral representation and extract the spectral density \(\rho(\lambda)\). They compare this continuum result with the spectral density of the staggered Dirac operator obtained from a quenched lattice-QCD simulation on a \(4^4\) lattice at various \(\beta\) values near the deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

* The OPE condensate in the DSE model is \((-\langle \bar q q \rangle^0_{\zeta=1\,{\rm GeV}}) = (0.24\,{\rm GeV})^3\).
* For a regularisation scale \(\Lambda = 2.0\,{\rm TeV}\), the domain where the Banks-Casher relation applies requires \(m_\delta^{\rm bm}(\Lambda) \sim 10^{-9}\) (in GeV units).
* At \(\beta = 5.4\) on a \(4^4\) lattice, \(\rho(0)a^3 \approx 70\), giving \(\pi \rho(0) a^3/V \approx (0.95)^3\).
* The model parameters used are \(D = (0.884\,{\rm GeV})^2\) and \(\omega = 0.3\,{\rm GeV}\), with \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.234\,{\rm GeV}\) (four-flavour \(\overline{\rm MS}\)).

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1980) by providing a rigorous continuum derivation that connects the infrared Dirac spectral density to the OPE condensate, which is the quantity appearing in the operator product expansion and DSE studies. The analysis clarifies the domain of validity of the Banks-Casher relation and shows that the pseudoscalar vacuum polarisation, previously used in lattice studies (Lattice OPE, 2002), provides a more robust measure for larger current-quark masses. The results enable more accurate extraction of the quark condensate from lattice simulations by specifying the required hierarchy of lattice spacing, volume, and quark mass.
