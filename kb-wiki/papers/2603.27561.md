# Quark-Meson Coupling Model in Heavy-Ion Collision Simulations

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model incorporates quark degrees of freedom into the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework, distinguishing it from traditional quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), which treats nucleons as point-like particles. In this work, we implement the QMC model within the DaeJeon Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (DJBUU) transport code to investigate its applicability to intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. We simulate \textsuperscript{197}Au+\textsuperscript{197}Au collisions at a beam energy of 400 A MeV using both QHD and QMC and find that both approaches yield comparable results for bulk observables such as transverse and directed flow, with good agreement with experimental data. To further assess the model performance, we study pion production in neutron-rich (\textsuperscript{132}Sn+\textsuperscript{124}Sn) and less neutron-rich (\textsuperscript{108}Sn+\textsuperscript{112}Sn) systems at 270 A MeV. In contrast to the QHD case, reproducing the observed pion yields and charge ratios within the QMC framework requires a slightly reduced density-dependent suppression in the in-medium $Δ$ production cross-section. These results demonstrate that the QMC model can be effectively integrated into transport simulations.

## Key claim
The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can be effectively integrated into the DaeJeon Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (DJBUU) transport code, but reproducing observed pion yields and charge ratios requires a slightly reduced density-dependent suppression in the in-medium Δ production cross-section compared to the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) case.

## Method
The authors implemented the QMC model within the DJBUU transport code by replacing the original QHD mean-field treatment of the scalar meson with the QMC approach, which derives baryon-meson couplings from quark-level interactions inside MIT bags. They simulated ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 400 A MeV for flow observables and ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn and ¹⁰⁸Sn+¹¹²Sn collisions at 270 A MeV for pion production, using both self-consistent iterative QMC (QMC_iter.) and density-dependent parameterized QMC (QMC_param.) options.

## Key numbers
- ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at beam energy 400 A MeV with impact parameter b = 4.7 fm (b₀ = 0.35): maximum central baryon density reached at approximately 11 fm/c, with QMC_iter. and QMC_param. showing densities about 3% and 6% higher than QHD, respectively.
- QHD parameters: saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 240 MeV, effective mass m*/m = 0.75, symmetry energy S = 30.5 MeV.
- QMC parameters: saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -15.7 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 280 MeV, effective mass m*/m = 0.8, symmetry energy S = 35 MeV.
- For in-medium Δ production cross-section modification: parameter set (C, x⁺, x⁰, x⁻) = (2.5, 0.0, 0.5, 2.0) from Kim (2022).

## Context
This work extends the application of the QMC model, originally developed by Guichon (1988) for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, into heavy-ion collision simulations for the first time. It contrasts with traditional QHD-based transport models such as RVUU (Ko, 1988), RBUU (Gaitanos, 1998), GiBUU-RMF (Buss, 2012), and the original DJBUU (Kim, 2020), which treat nucleons as point-like particles. The results enable future systematic studies of the nuclear equation of state using QMC in transport simulations, particularly for rare-isotope beam experiments at facilities like RIBF, FRIB, and RAON.
