# Selfconsistent Evaluation of Charm and Charmonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> A selfconsistent calculation of heavy-quark (HQ) and quarkonium properties in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is conducted to quantify flavor transport and color screening in the medium. The main tool is a thermodynamic $T$-matrix approach to compute HQ and quarkonium spectral functions in both scattering and bound-state regimes. The $T$-matrix, in turn, is employed to calculate HQ selfenergies which are implemented into spectral functions beyond the quasiparticle approximation. Charmonium spectral functions are used to evaluate eulcidean-time correlation functions which are compared to results from thermal lattice QCD. The comparisons are performed in various hadronic channels including zero-mode contributions consistently accounting for finite charm-quark width effects. The zero modes are closely related to the charm-quark number susceptibility which is also compared to existing lattice "data". Both the susceptibility and the heavy-light quark $T$-matrix are applied to calculate the thermal charm-quark relaxation rate, or, equivalently, the charm diffusion constant in the QGP. Implications of our findings in the HQ sector for the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio of the QGP are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a selfconsistent off-shell treatment of charm-quark spectral functions within a thermodynamic T-matrix approach yields a fair description of lattice QCD Euclidean correlator ratios for charmonia, but the agreement is qualified by the neglect of several effects (e.g., imaginary parts from scattering off thermal gluons, coupled channels like D-meson and c-cbar-gluon states) which are expected to contribute additional low-energy strength, especially near the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors employ a thermodynamic T-matrix approach to compute heavy-quark (HQ) and quarkonium spectral functions in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), using either the heavy-quark free energy (F) or internal energy (U) from lattice QCD as the two-body potential. They go beyond the quasiparticle approximation by selfconsistently calculating the charm-quark selfenergy from the heavy-light T-matrix and implementing the resulting off-shell spectral functions into the charmonium T-matrix and Euclidean correlator calculations, including zero-mode contributions with finite charm-quark width effects.

## Key numbers

- Bare charm-quark mass set to m_c^0 = 1.264 GeV, with vacuum selfenergy X_infinity^vac/2 = 0.6 GeV, giving fair description of spin-average masses of J/psi-eta_c, psi' and chi_c states
- Imaginary part of selfconsistently calculated c-quark selfenergy is around 0.050-0.100 GeV for low-momentum on-shell charm quarks
- At 1.2 T_c, the eta_c correlator ratio drops to about 0.85 (compared to 0.9 in previous quasiparticle calculations)
- Critical temperature for lattice data is T_c ~ 210 MeV, while for the potential underlying the T-matrix calculations T_c ~ 196 MeV
- Inclusion of finite quark width in zero-mode contribution increases correlator ratios at large tau by approximately 0.1-0.15 compared to zero-width treatment
- Largest discrepancies of approximately 30% at both intermediate and large tau occur in the axialvector (chi_c1) channel

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous T-matrix study (Riek and Rapp, 2010) by going beyond the quasiparticle approximation for heavy quarks and performing a selfconsistent calculation of the HQ selfenergy and in-medium heavy-light T-matrix, as well as expanding comparisons with Euclidean correlator ratios to scalar, vector, and axialvector channels. The approach builds on prior thermodynamic T-matrix work by Mannarelli (2005), Cabrera (2006), and Riek (2010), and the zero-mode treatment follows the formalism of Aarts (2005) and Umeda (2007). The results enable more reliable extraction of in-medium quasiparticle masses for heavy quarks and provide constraints for calculations of charm-quark number susceptibility and heavy-quark transport coefficients in the QGP.
