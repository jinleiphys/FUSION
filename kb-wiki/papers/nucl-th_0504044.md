# S, P, D, F wave KN phase shifts in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The $S$, $P$, $D$, $F$ wave $KN$ phase shifts have been studied in the chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method equation. The numerical results of different partial waves are in agreement with the experimental data except for the cases of $P_{13}$ and $D_{15}$, which are less well described when the laboratory momentum of the kaon meson is greater than 400 MeV.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, extended to include an antiquark and sigma-meson mixing, reproduces S, P, D, F wave KN phase shifts in agreement with experimental data for most partial waves, except that the calculated P13 phase shifts are too repulsive and D15 phase shifts are slightly too attractive when the kaon laboratory momentum exceeds 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved a resonating group method (RGM) equation for the KN system using the chiral SU(3) quark model Hamiltonian, which includes confinement, one-gluon-exchange, and chiral-field-induced potentials between quarks. The model was extended to include an antiquark (anti-s) with direct and annihilation interaction terms, and the mixing of sigma_0 and sigma_8 scalar mesons was considered with two mixing angles (35 degrees and -18 degrees). Model parameters were fixed by constraints from baryon ground states, deuteron binding energy, NN scattering, and YN cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The sigma meson mass was taken as 675 MeV, with a cutoff mass Lambda = 1100 MeV.
- The constituent quark masses used were m_u = 313 MeV and m_s = 470 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter b_u was set to 0.5 fm.
- Two sigma mixing angles were tested: theta^S = 35 degrees (ideal mixing) and theta^S = -18 degrees (from Dai et al.).
- The calculated phase shifts for P13 and D15 waves deviate from experiment when the kaon laboratory momentum exceeds 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for NN and YN scattering (Zhang et al., 1997; L. R. Dai et al., 2003), to the KN system including an antiquark. It improves upon the RGM calculation of Lemaire et al. (2003) by obtaining correct signs for P11, P03, D13, D05, F15, and F07 waves, and improves magnitudes for P01, D03, and D15 channels. The results are compared with the earlier work of Black (2002), where P13 also showed excessive repulsion. The authors suggest that future work should consider coupling to inelastic channels and hidden color channels to resolve the remaining discrepancies.
