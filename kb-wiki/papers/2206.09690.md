# Prospects of searching for unstable nucleus states in relativistic nuclear fragmentation

> The article is dedicated to the experimental study in the relativistic approach to the problems of nuclear cluster physics for the prospects of the \href{http://becquerel.jinr.ru/}{BECQUEREL} experiment. The nuclear emulsion method applied in this experiment makes it possible to study thoroughly the relativistic final states in the fragmentation of nuclei. The focus of the presented research is the dynamics of emergence of the $^{8}$Be nucleus and the Hoyle state, as well as the search for the 4$α$-particle condensate decaying via the above nuclear states. In this context, the analysis of exposure to $^{84}$Kr nuclei at 950 MeV/nucleon is shown. As a continuation of the study of light nuclei, we have demonstrated the search for the isobar-analogue state of the $ ^{13} $N nucleus in the fragmentation of $ ^{14} $N nuclei at 2 GeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear emulsion method in the BECQUEREL experiment can identify unstable nuclear states (such as $^{8}$Be, the Hoyle state, and potential 4$\alpha$ Bose-Einstein condensate candidates) in relativistic nuclear fragmentation, with the hedge that the status of $\alpha$BEC observations remains uncertain and the search for the 15.1 MeV state in $^{16}$O has remained unsuccessful in all cases.

## Method

The authors used nuclear emulsion stacks exposed to relativistic beams of $^{84}$Kr at 950 MeV/nucleon and $^{14}$N at 2 GeV/nucleon, measuring tracks of He and H fragments with angular resolution better than 10$^{-3}$ rad. Invariant masses of fragment ensembles were reconstructed from measured angles under the approximation of conservation of the projectile momentum per nucleon, with selection conditions $Q_{2\alpha} \leq 0.4$ MeV for $^{8}$Be, $Q_{3\alpha} \leq 0.7$ MeV for the Hoyle state, and $Q_{2\alpha p} \leq 0.5$ MeV for $^{9}$B.

## Key numbers

- $^{8}$Be decay energy is 91.8 keV, with width 5.57 $\pm$ 0.25 eV
- Hoyle state (HS) is at 378 keV above the 3$\alpha$ threshold, with width $\Gamma$(HS) = 9.3 $\pm$ 0.9 eV
- $^{9}$B ground state is 185.1 keV above the $^{8}$Be$p$ threshold, with width 0.54 $\pm$ 0.21 keV
- In $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$\alpha$ "white" stars, fraction containing $^{8}$Be decays was 45 $\pm$ 4% and HS decays 11 $\pm$ 3%
- In $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$ "white" stars, fraction containing $^{8}$Be decays was 62 $\pm$ 3% and HS decays 22 $\pm$ 2%
- 9 events in $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$ satisfied $Q_{4\alpha} < 1$ MeV with average $\left\langle Q_{4\alpha} \right\rangle$ = 624 $\pm$ 84(252) keV
- Estimate of $^{16}$O(0$^+_6$) $\to$ $\alpha$ + HS decays is 1.4 $\pm$ 0.5% of "white stars" and 7 $\pm$ 2% of HS events
- 33 events $^{16}$O $\to$ 2$^{8}$Be identified, which is 5 $\pm$ 1% of "white stars" $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$
- In $^{197}$Au at 10.7 GeV/nucleon, relative contributions to $^{8}$Be events: $^{9}$B 25 $\pm$ 4%, HS 6 $\pm$ 2%, 2$^{8}$Be 10 $\pm$ 2%
- In $^{84}$Kr at 950 MeV/nucleon, $N_{n\alpha}(\geq 1^{8}$Be)/$N_{n\alpha}$ ranged from 19 $\pm$ 5% at $n\alpha=4$ to 70 $\pm$ 35% at $n\alpha=9-13$
- One 4$\alpha$ quartet at $n\alpha=6$ with $Q_{4\alpha}=0.6$ MeV identified in $^{84}$Kr fragmentation
- $^{13}$N(15.065) IAS has width $\Gamma = 0.86$ keV, with $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$)$p$ and $^{9}$B$\alpha$ decay probabilities of 5% each

## Context

This work extends the BECQUEREL experiment's program of studying nuclear clustering via relativistic fragmentation in nuclear emulsions, building on prior identification of $^{8}$Be, $^{9}$B, and the Hoyle state in light nuclei fragmentation (Zarubin, 2015; Artemenkov, 2017). The paper addresses the search for $\alpha$-particle Bose-Einstein condensate states, particularly the 4$\alpha$ candidate $^{16}$O(0$^+_6$) at 15.1 MeV, which has been sought in low-energy experiments (Borderie, 2016; Barbui, 2018; Bishop, 2019; Manna, 2020) without success. The results enable extending the search to heavier nuclei at higher energies, with the finding that $^{8}$Be yield increases with $\alpha$-particle multiplicity suggesting formation via $\alpha$-particle interactions rather than parent nucleus excitation.
