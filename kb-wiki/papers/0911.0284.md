# Closed-orbit theory for spatial density oscillations

> We briefly review a recently developed semiclassical theory for quantum oscillations in the spatial (particle and kinetic energy) densities of finite fermion systems and present some examples of its results. We then discuss the inclusion of correlations (finite temperatures, pairing correlations) in the semiclassical theory.

# Closed-orbit theory for spatial density oscillations

## Key claim

The paper presents a semiclassical theory for quantum oscillations in spatial particle and kinetic-energy densities of finite fermion systems, claiming that the oscillatory parts of these densities can be expressed as sums over closed orbits (not periodic orbits) of the corresponding classical system, valid to leading order in вДП and for arbitrary local potentials in any dimension.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed semiclassical theory (Brack and Roccia, 2009) for spatial density oscillations in finite fermion systems, building on Gutzwiller's trace formula (Gutzwiller, 1971) for the oscillating level density and on extended Thomas-Fermi theory for smooth densities. The key result is a local virial theorem ќіѕД(r) вЙИ [ќїћГ вИТ V(r)] ќіѕБ(r) that holds for arbitrary potentials and dimensions, explaining why the Thomas-Fermi kinetic-energy functional (without gradient corrections) works surprisingly well even for oscillating exact densities, a fact noted earlier (Brack, 1997) but not previously understood. The inclusion of finite temperatures and pairing correlations via convolution methods (Brack and Quentin, 1995) enables applications to hot nuclei and superfluid fermion systems, with the anomalous density matrix for pair condensation identified as an open problem for future research.
