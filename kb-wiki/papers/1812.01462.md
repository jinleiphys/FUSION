# Dipole-dipole interactions between neutrons

> In this work we present results of the dipole-dipole interactions between two neutrons, a neutron and a conducting wall, and a neutron between two walls. As input, we use dynamical electromagnetic dipole polarizabilities fitted to chiral EFT results up to the pion production threshold and at the onset of the Delta resonance. Our work can be relevant to the physics of confined ultracold neutrons inside bottles.

## Key claim
The paper calculates Casimir-Polder (retarded van der Waals) interactions involving neutrons using dynamical electromagnetic dipole polarizabilities fitted to chiral effective field theory results, but the results depend on which of three parameter sets (Set 1, 2, or 3) is used, reflecting uncertainty in the underlying polarizability values.

## Method
The authors parametrized the dynamical electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the neutron, α_n(ω) and β_n(ω), by fitting analytic forms to chiral EFT calculations from Lensky et al. (2015) up to the pion production threshold and the onset of the Delta resonance. They then used these polarizabilities in the standard two-photon-exchange formalism of Feinberg and Sucher (1970) to compute the Casimir-Polder potential for three geometries: neutron-neutron, neutron-conducting wall, and neutron between two parallel conducting walls.

## Key numbers
- Static electric dipole polarizability α_n(0) values: 13.9968, 11.6, and 12.5 (all in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) for parameter Sets 1, 2, and 3 respectively.
- Static magnetic dipole polarizability β_n(0) values: 4.2612, 3.7, and 2.7 (all in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) for Sets 1, 2, and 3 respectively.
- The repulsive Fermi pseudopotential for ultracold neutrons in a nickel bottle is V_F ≈ 252 neV, providing a scale against which the attractive Casimir-Polder forces compete.

## Context
This work extends the original calculation of the Casimir-Polder interaction between two neutrons by Arnold (1973), which used only static polarizabilities with outdated values. The authors previously introduced this approach in Babb, Higa, and Hussein (2017) and now include dynamical polarizabilities from modern chiral EFT. The results are relevant for understanding losses of ultracold neutrons confined in material bottles, where the attractive Casimir-Polder forces may compete with the repulsive Fermi pseudopotential used for confinement.
