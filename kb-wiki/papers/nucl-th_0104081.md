# The production of eta-mesons in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold

> Data on the total cross sections for the pp -> pp eta, pn -> pn eta, and pn -> d eta reactions and the pp -> pp eta differential cross section near threshold are analysed in a one-meson-exchange model. After including initial and final-state nucleon-nucleon distortion, the magnitude and most of the energy dependence are well reproduced. It is found that the contribution of rho-exchange is larger than that of pi-exchange. With destructive rho/pi interference in the pp case, the model explains quantitatively the pp -> pp eta/pn -> pn eta cross section ratio and the slope of the pp -> pp eta differential cross section. Such an agreement would be destroyed by any significant eta-exchange term. The residual energy dependence may be associated with eta-nucleon rescattering that has not been taken into account. The pn -> pn eta/pn -> d eta ratio depends weakly upon the nature of the particle exchanges, being determined primarily by the nucleon-nucleon final state interactions. The proton analysing power is predicted to remain small in the low energy region.

## Key claim

A one-meson-exchange model with initial and final-state nucleon-nucleon distortion reproduces the magnitude and most of the energy dependence of near-threshold eta production data, but the residual energy dependence may be associated with eta-nucleon rescattering that has not been taken into account, and any significant eta-exchange term would destroy the agreement.

## Method

The authors evaluated relativistic Born amplitudes for single-meson exchange (pi, eta, rho, omega) at threshold neglecting all distortions, then constructed a potential from the amplitudes in configuration space to include final-state interactions. The energy and angular dependence from elementary amplitudes (pi N to eta N and gamma p to eta p) were introduced perturbatively, with vector-meson-induced amplitudes derived from photoproduction data via the vector-meson dominance model. Initial-state nucleon-nucleon distortion was included using the imaginary part of the relevant phase shift, while eta-nucleon final-state interaction was not taken into account.

## Key numbers

- The pp to pp eta/pn to pn eta cross section ratio near threshold is over 6, and the model reproduces the measured 6.5:1 pn:pp ratio with destructive rho/pi interference in the pp case.
- The threshold value for the pi- p to eta n differential cross section is 4 pi b0 = (9.1 +/- 0.8) mb, with a more recent Crystal Ball measurement giving 4 pi b0 = (7.4 +/- 0.3) mb at 720 MeV/c.
- The eta-nucleon scattering length is taken as a_etaN = (0.83 + 0.27 i) fm.
- The gamma n to eta n / gamma p to eta p cross section ratio near threshold is 0.66 +/- 0.03, with the amplitude ratio r = 0.81 +/- 0.02.
- At threshold, the photoproduction multipole |E0+| = 0.0214 fm, giving |R_gamma| = 0.146 fm^2, with |R_rho| = 0.916 fm^2 and |R_omega| = 0.310 fm^2 at threshold.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is G_pi^2/4pi = 13.6, with a monopole form factor cutoff Lambda_pi = 1.72 GeV/c.
- The rho and omega coupling constants are g_rho = 3.25, kappa_rho = 6.1, g_omega = 15.9, kappa_omega = 0.0, with form factor cutoffs Lambda_rho = 1.4 GeV/c and Lambda_omega = 1.5 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the one-meson-exchange approach of earlier analyses (G. Faldt and C. Wilkin, 1997; J. M. Laget, 1990; V. Vetter, 1993; A. Moalem, 1996) by including rho, omega, pi, and eta exchanges with improved treatment of final-state interactions and using new data from the CELSIUS group on pn to d eta and pp to pp eta reactions. The model enables quantitative understanding of the pp to pp eta/pn to pn eta cross section ratio and the slope of the pp to pp eta differential cross section, and provides an upper bound on the poorly determined eta-nucleon coupling constant. The residual energy dependence suggests that future work should include a consistent three-body treatment of eta-nucleon rescattering simultaneously with the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
