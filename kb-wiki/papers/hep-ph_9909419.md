# The Color-Flavor Locking Phase at T \not =0$

> We study the color-flavor locked phase of QCD with three massless quarks at high chemical potential and small non zero temperatures. We make use of the recently introduced effective action to describe such a phase. We obtain the exact order T^2 behaviour of the condensates and of the pressure by formally comparing the derivative from the QCD functional with symmetry breaking to that from the effective lagrangian with external sources, respecting the residual Z_2 invariance of the color-flavor locked phase. From these exact results, but now at a very tentative level of conjecture, we are lead to think that the phase structure of QCD at very high density consists of two superconducting phases and a symmetric one.

# The Color-Flavor Locking Phase at T ≠ 0

## Key claim
The authors derive exact order T² behavior of condensates and pressure in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD with three massless quarks at high chemical potential and small non-zero temperatures. Based on a tentative extrapolation of these exact low-temperature results, they conjecture that the phase structure at very high density may consist of two distinct superconducting phases (SC1 and SC2) and a symmetric phase, but they emphasize this extrapolation is highly speculative and goes beyond their exact results.

## Method
The authors use an effective action for the CFL phase, previously introduced by themselves, which describes the massless Goldstone bosons arising from the symmetry breaking pattern SU(3)ₓ ⊗ SU(3)ₗ ⊗ SU(3)ᵣ ⊗ U(1) → SU(3)ₓ₊ₗ₊ᵣ × Z₂. They introduce external scalar sources in both the QCD functional and the effective lagrangian, respecting the residual Z₂ invariance, and equate derivatives of the generating functionals to obtain exact relations between condensates and effective lagrangian parameters. The low-temperature behavior is then computed by treating the Goldstone bosons as free particles, following the method of Gasser and Leutwyler for chiral symmetry breaking at zero density.

## Key numbers
- The gluon mass in the CFL phase is given by m_g² = α_T g_s² F_T²/4, where F_T is a decay constant and α_T a parameter from the effective lagrangian.
- For N=3 with all couplings of the same order, the temperature dependence of the condensate components is v₁(T)/v₁(0) ≈ 1 − (19/54)(T²/F²) and v₂(T)/v₂(0) ≈ 1 − (13/54)(T²/F²), where F is the common value of the couplings.
- In the massless limit with velocities v_Y ≈ v_ϕ ≈ 1, the pressure at low temperature is P ≈ (π²/90) N² T⁴.
- The Goldstone boson contribution to the free energy in the massless limit is g₀ = (1/v³)(π² T⁴/45), and the derivative −∂g₀/∂M² = (1/v³)(T²/12).
- The numerical estimates for the energy gap in the CFL phase range between 10 and 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective action approach for the CFL phase previously developed by the same authors (Casalbuoni and Gatto, 1999) to include finite temperature effects. The CFL condensation pattern was originally discussed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998), Schäfer and Wilczek (1999), and Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1999). The method of introducing external sources to relate QCD and effective lagrangian functionals follows the technique of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for chiral perturbation theory at zero density. The tentative conjecture of two superconducting phases at high density, if confirmed by higher-order calculations, would provide a richer phase structure than previously anticipated, with possible implications for neutron star physics and heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
