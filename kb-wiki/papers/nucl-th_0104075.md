# Temperature induced shell effects in deformed nuclei

> The thermal evolution of the shell correction energy is investigated for deformed nuclei using Strutinsky prescription in a self-consistent relativistic mean-field framework. For temperature independent single-particle states corresponding to either spherical or deformed nuclear shapes, the shell correction energy $Δ_{sc}$ steadily washes out with temperature. However, for states pertaining to the self-consistent thermally evolving shapes of deformed nuclei, the dual role played by the single-particle occupancies in diluting the fluctuation effects from the single-particle spectra and in driving the system towards a smaller deformation is crucial in determining $Δ_{sc}$ at moderate temperatures. In rare earth nuclei, it is found that $Δ_{sc}$ builds up strongly around the shape transition temperature; for lighter deformed nuclei like $^{64}Zn$ and $^{66}Zn$, this is relatively less prominent.

# Temperature induced shell effects in deformed nuclei

## Key claim

For deformed nuclei, the shell correction energy does not simply wash out monotonically with temperature; instead, when the single-particle states are allowed to evolve self-consistently with the thermally driven shape change, the shell correction energy can build up strongly around the shape transition temperature, particularly in rare-earth nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set and included pairing via the BCS approximation. They calculated the thermal evolution of deformation and single-particle levels self-consistently, then evaluated the shell correction energy using the Strutinsky prescription with a smearing width of approximately 1.2 times the oscillator spacing. The calculations were performed for isotopes of Sm and Dy in the rare-earth region and for the lighter deformed nuclei 64Zn and 66Zn, using a spherical oscillator basis with twelve shells and a model space up to 2 oscillator quanta above the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- For 150Sm, the ground state deformation is β2 = 0.19; at the shape transition temperature Tp, the shell correction energy Δsc shows an abrupt enhancement of approximately 3 MeV.
- The typical temperature for shell melting for a fixed set of single-particle levels is Tshell ~ ħω0/π, with ħω0 ~ 41 A−1/3, which is approximately 2.5 MeV for rare-earth nuclei.
- Shape transition temperatures Tp for rare-earth nuclei are typically between 1 and 2 MeV.
- The temperature range explored in the calculations is T ≤ 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard understanding that shell correction energy melts away with temperature for fixed single-particle levels (Bohr and Mottelson; Ramamurthy et al.) by showing that for deformed nuclei, self-consistent reorganization of the single-particle field with temperature produces a strong build-up of shell correction at the shape transition temperature. The results are consistent with earlier RMF calculations of shape transitions by Gambhir et al. and relate to the observation by Egido et al. that at high temperature, shell effects driving deformation disappear but not those providing magic numbers in spherical nuclei. The findings have implications for phenomenological analyses of temperature-dependent level densities in deformed nuclei, which typically use a single-wave fluctuation of the level density (Ramamurthy; Shlomo) that does not account for the reorganization of single-particle spectra with temperature.
