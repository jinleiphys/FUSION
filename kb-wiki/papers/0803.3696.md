# Realistic calculations of nuclear disappearance lifetimes induced by neutron-antineutron oscillations

> Realistic calculations of nuclear disappearance lifetimes induced by neutron-antineutron oscillations are reported for oxygen and iron, using antineutron nuclear potentials derived from a recent comprehensive analysis of antiproton atomic X-ray and radiochemical data. A lower limit of 3.3 x 10E8 s on the neutron-antineutron oscillation time is derived from the Super-Kamiokande I new lower limit of 1.77 x 10E32 yr on the neutron lifetime in oxygen. Antineutron scattering lengths in carbon and nickel, needed in trap experiments using ultracold neutrons, are calculated from updated antinucleon optical potentials at threshold, with results shown to be largely model independent.

# Realistic calculations of nuclear disappearance lifetimes induced by neutron-antineutron oscillations

## Key claim

The paper derives a lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time of τ_nn̄ > 3.3 × 10^8 s from the Super-Kamiokande I lower limit on the neutron lifetime in oxygen, but this result depends on using updated antineutron nuclear potentials derived from antiprotonic atom data and carries a theoretical uncertainty of about 10-15%.

## Method

The authors solved coupled radial wave equations for neutron and antineutron single-particle states in oxygen-16 and iron-56, using a Woods-Saxon potential for bound neutrons and an antineutron optical potential derived from global fits to antiprotonic atom X-ray and radiochemical data (Friedman, Gal, and Mareš 2005). The disappearance width per neutron was computed from the overlap of the neutron wavefunction with the imaginary part of the antineutron wavefunction, then averaged over occupied neutron subshells with translationally invariant shell-model occupancies for oxygen. Antineutron scattering lengths for carbon and nickel were extracted from the same antinucleon potentials using a parameterization of the form Re a = (1.54 ± 0.03)A^(0.311 ± 0.005) fm and Im a = -1.00 ± 0.04 fm.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on neutron-antineutron oscillation time: τ_nn̄ > 3.3 × 10^8 s, derived from Super-Kamiokande I lower limit T_d(O) > 1.77 × 10^32 yr on the neutron lifetime in oxygen
- Reduced lifetime T_R for oxygen-16 (translationally invariant): 0.517 × 10^23 s^(-1); for oxygen-16 (shell model): 0.543 × 10^23 s^(-1); for iron-56 (shell model): 0.666 × 10^23 s^(-1)
- Antineutron scattering length for carbon-12 (finite-range potential): a = 3.26 - i 0.97 fm; for nickel-58: a = 5.41 - i 1.12 fm
- Antineutron annihilation width at central nuclear densities: Γ_n̄ ≈ 320 MeV
- Lower limit from Soudan 2 on iron: T_d(Fe) > 7.2 × 10^31 yr, giving τ_nn̄ > 1.4 × 10^8 s by the approximate relation
- Grenoble reactor experiment lower limit: τ_nn̄ > 0.86 × 10^8 s

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Dover, Gal, and Richard (1983) by using updated antineutron nuclear potentials from a comprehensive analysis of antiprotonic atom data (Friedman, Gal, and Mareš 2005), reducing the theoretical uncertainty from the 50-100% range of earlier calculations to about 10-15%. The results provide the necessary link between the Super-Kamiokande I measurement of the neutron disappearance lifetime in oxygen and a lower bound on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time, and also supply antineutron scattering lengths needed for ultracold neutron trap experiments searching for n-n̄ oscillations as proposed by Chetyrkin et al. (1981). The paper notes that two-nucleon processes, estimated by Dover et al. (1983) at 15-30%, could increase the stated lower bound on τ_nn̄.
