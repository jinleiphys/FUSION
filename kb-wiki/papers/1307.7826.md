# Condensated fermion system in the model of four-quark interaction with large correlation length

> Studying a model of four-quark interaction with large correlation length we find out both the features peculiar an unitary fermi gas and the specific anomalous properties of the fermi systems with a fermion condensate. It is argued that a possibility of phase transition originated by interface between the Fermi sphere and fermion condensate appears in such quark systems. The results obtained could be instrumental for phenomenological applications in view of our conclusion about approximately the same behavior of the dynamical characteristics of quark ensembles with different four-quark interaction forms in a practical interval of coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a four-quark interaction model with large correlation length (the Keldysh model), a fermion condensate state can coexist with a standard Fermi sphere, leading to a possible phase transition between these configurations; the authors claim that the dynamical characteristics of quark ensembles with different four-quark interaction forms behave approximately the same in a practical interval of the coupling constant, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the analysis is primarily carried out in the chiral limit and for the Keldysh model specifically.

## Method

The authors study a relativistic quark ensemble using a Hamiltonian density with a four-fermion interaction derived from a gluon field correlator in a color-singlet form with a finite correlation length (the Keldysh model). They employ the Bogolyubov–Hartree–Fock approximation to construct a statistical operator and minimize the thermodynamic potential at fixed mean charge and entropy, solving the resulting system of equations for the quark dynamical mass and density distributions at zero and finite temperature. The analysis is performed in the chiral limit for simplicity, and three types of solutions (first, second, and third) are constructed by combining Fermi sphere, fermion condensate, and vacuum regions on the momentum axis.

## Key numbers

* NJL model parameters used for comparison: cutoff \(\Lambda = 631\) MeV, coupling \(G\Lambda^2/(2\pi^2) \approx 1.3\), current quark mass \(m = 5\) MeV.
* Quark dynamical mass in the NJL model at \(T=0\), \(\mu=0\): \(M_q \sim 340\) MeV.
* In the Keldysh model in the chiral limit, the quark dynamical mass is \(M_q = (4G^2 - \mathbf{p}^2)^{1/2}\) for \(|\mathbf{p}| < 2G\), and \(M_q = 0\) for \(|\mathbf{p}| > 2G\); quark states with \(|\mathbf{p}| < 2G\) are degenerate in energy \(P_0 = 2G\).
* For the second solution at zero temperature, the maximal condensate density occurs at \(x_N = 3/4\) (where \(x = \mu/(2G)\)), with \(N_c \approx 3.56 \times 10^{-3} (2G)^3\); maximal entropy occurs at \(x_S \approx 0.84\), with \(S_c \approx 2.64 \times 10^{-2} (2G)^3\) (for \(N_f=1\)).
* Energy release (absorption) during rearrangement of the quark ensemble: about 20 MeV/fm\(^3\) for \(2G = 300\) MeV.
* Characteristic dimensionless parameters for the third solution: \(x \sim 1\), \(y \sim 0.15\), \(z \sim 0.4\); for the first solution: \(x \sim 0.2\), \(y \sim 0.4\), \(z \sim 0.2\).
* Transition between states occurs at ensemble densities \({\cal N} \sim 8 \times 10^{-3}\) (in dimensionless units).

## Context

This work extends the concept of a fermion condensate, originally proposed for condensed matter systems by Khodel and Shaginyan (1990), to a relativistic quark model with a finite-range four-fermion interaction (the Keldysh model). It contrasts with the point-like Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) and the instanton-liquid approach (Molodtsov and Zinovjev, 2006), showing that the fermion condensate phenomenon is qualitatively similar in both NJL and Keldysh models despite different momentum-space structures. The results enable further study of phase transitions in dense quark matter relevant to astrophysical applications such as supernova outbursts, and suggest a possible connection to the unitary Fermi gas and the origin of turbulence in quark-gluon plasma.
