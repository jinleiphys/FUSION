# Phase separation in asymmetrical fermion superfluids

> Motivated by recent developments on cold atom traps and high density QCD we consider fermionic systems composed of two particle species with different densities. We argue that a mixed phase composed of normal and superfluid components is the energetically favored ground state. We suggest how this phase separation can be used as a probe of fermion superfluidity in atomic traps.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for fermionic systems with two species at different densities, the energetically favored ground state is a mixed phase composed of normal and superfluid components, rather than a homogeneous Sarma (gapless superfluid) state. This conclusion holds under the condition that the mean-field analysis applies and that interface energies between phases are small.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field BCS approach with a pairing Hamiltonian to model a non-relativistic dilute gas of two fermion species A and B with different chemical potentials and masses. They compute the thermodynamic potential for fixed chemical potentials and the energy for fixed particle numbers, then compare the energies of the normal phase, the Sarma phase, and an inhomogeneous mixed phase consisting of normal-phase bubbles in a BCS-phase sea. The mixed phase energy is minimized with respect to the fraction of normal phase and the density in the BCS component, neglecting interface energies.

## Key numbers

- The BCS gap in mean-field approximation is given by Δ₀ ≅ 2(p₀²/M) exp(-π/(2p₀|a|) - 2), where p₀²/M = p_A²/M_A + p_B²/M_B and a is the scattering length.
- The Sarma state gap is Δ_s ≅ √[Δ₀((p_B² - p_A²)/(2√(M_A M_B)) - Δ₀)] for small gaps.
- The Sarma state exists only when Δ₀ ≤ (p_B² - p_A²)/(2√(M_A M_B)) ≤ 2Δ₀.
- For the case M_B = 7M_A and |a|p_A = 0.59, 0.63 ≤ |a|p_B ≤ 0.65, the mixed phase has lower energy than the Sarma phase for all nonzero density asymmetries δn/n.
- The optimal normal-phase fraction in the mixed phase is x_min ≅ √((M_A + M_B)/(2M_B)) for M_B > M_A.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of asymmetric fermion systems in high-density QCD (Alford 2001, Schafer 2003) and cold atom traps (Pethick 2002). It directly addresses the "internal gap" or Sarma state proposed by previous authors (Sarma 1963, Liu and Wilczek 2003) and shows that a mixed phase with spatial separation of normal and superfluid regions is energetically favored over the homogeneous Sarma state. The results enable a proposed experimental method to detect fermion superfluidity in atomic traps by imaging the bubble structure of the mixed phase, though the authors note that a quantitative understanding of surface tension is needed.
