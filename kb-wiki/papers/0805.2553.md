# Impact of the symmetry energy on the outer crust of non-accreting neutron stars

> The composition and equation of state of the outer crust of non-accreting neutron stars is computed using accurate nuclear mass tables. The main goal of the present study is to understand the impact of the symmetry energy on the structure of the outer crust. First, a simple "toy model" is developed to illustrate the competition between the electronic density and the symmetry energy. Then, realistic mass tables are used to show that models with a stiff symmetry energy - those that generate large neutron skins for heavy nuclei - predict a sequence of nuclei that are more neutron-rich than their softer counterparts. This result may be phrased in the form of a correlation: the larger the neutron skin of 208Pb, the more exotic the composition of the outer crust.

## Key claim

The composition of the outer crust of non-accreting neutron stars is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy: models with a stiff symmetry energy that produce large neutron skins in heavy nuclei predict a sequence of more neutron-rich nuclei in the outer crust than models with a softer symmetry energy, with the correlation being that the larger the neutron skin of 208Pb, the more exotic the crustal composition.

## Method

The authors computed the ground-state composition and equation of state of the outer crust by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per particle at constant pressure, using nuclear masses from four different models: the Duflo-Zuker microscopic/macroscopic model, the finite range droplet model of Moller, Nix and collaborators, and two relativistic mean-field models (NL3 and FSUGold). They also developed a simple liquid-drop toy model with an extremely relativistic electron gas to illustrate analytically the competition between the electronic density (which drives the system toward neutron-rich nuclei) and the nuclear symmetry energy (which opposes such a change).

## Key numbers

- The neutron-drip density is approximately 4 x 10^11 g/cm^3
- NL3 predicts a pressure at saturation density of P0 ≈ 6 MeV/fm^3 and a neutron skin in 208Pb of Rn - Rp ≈ 0.28 fm
- FSUGold predicts a pressure at saturation density of P0 ≈ 3 MeV/fm^3 and a neutron skin in 208Pb of Rn - Rp ≈ 0.21 fm
- The outer crust spans densities from about 10^4 g/cm^3 up to the neutron-drip density of about 4 x 10^11 g/cm^3
- Nuclear matter saturation density is 2.5 x 10^14 g/cm^3
- The liquid-drop model fit to 2049 nuclei yields asymmetry coefficient aa = 23.37837 MeV
- At neutron-drip density, the Fermi momentum is approximately pF_max ≈ 40 MeV, giving an electronic contribution of about εe_max ≈ 30 y^(4/3) MeV
- The toy model predicts a proton fraction at drip density of y_drip = 0.298, compared to y(118Kr) = 0.305 for the conventionally accepted drip nucleus 118Kr

## Context

This work extends the seminal formalism of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) for the outer crust of neutron stars, and builds on correlations established by Horowitz and collaborators (2000-2004) between the neutron skin of 208Pb and neutron-star observables. The study uses the accurately-calibrated relativistic models NL3 (Lalazissis, 1996, 1999) and FSUGold (Todd-Rutel, 2005) to demonstrate how the density dependence of the symmetry energy affects crustal composition, and it connects to the upcoming Parity Radius Experiment (PREx) at Jefferson Laboratory that will measure the neutron skin of 208Pb model-independently. The results enable future constraints on the symmetry energy below saturation density through observations of crustal oscillations in magnetars.
