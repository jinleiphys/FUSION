# J/Ψ\to ϕππ(K \bar{K}) decays, chiral dynamcis and OZI violation

> We have studied the invariant mass distributions of the ππand K \bar{K} systems for invariant masses up to 1.2 GeV from the J/Ψ\to ϕππ(K\bar{K}) decays. The approach exploits the connection between these processes and the ππand K\bar{K} strange and non-strange scalar form factors by considering the ϕmeson as a spectator. The calculated scalar form factors are then matched with the ones from next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory, including the calculation of the the K\bar{K} scalar form factors. Final state interactions in the J/Ψ\to ϕππ(K\bar{K}) processes are taken into account as rescattering effects in the system of the two pseudoscalar mesons. A very good agreement with the experimental data from DM2 and MARK-III is achieved. Furthermore, making use of SU(3) symmetry, the S-wave contribution to the π^+π^- event distribution in the J/Ψ\to ωπ^+π^- reaction is also given and the data up to energies of about 0.7 GeV are reproduced. These decays of the J/Ψto a vector and a pair of pseudoscalars turn out to be very sensitive to OZI violating physics which we parametrize in terms of a direct OZI violation parameter and the chiral perturbation theory low energy constants L_4^r and L_6^r. These constants all come out very different from zero, lending further credit to the statement that the OZI rule is not operative in the scalar 0^{++} channel.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the invariant mass distributions of ππ and K K̅ systems in J/Ψ → φ ππ (K K̅) decays are highly sensitive to OZI rule violation in the scalar 0⁺⁺ channel, and that the low-energy constants L₄ʳ and L₆ʳ of next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) come out very different from zero, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the φ meson acts as a spectator and that final-state interactions are dominated by the coupled ππ/K K̅ system.

## Method

The authors model the J/Ψ → φ ππ (K K̅) decays by treating the φ meson as a spectator and expressing the transition amplitude in terms of the strange and non-strange scalar form factors of the ππ and K K̅ systems. These scalar form factors are calculated using a unitarized approach based on Bethe-Salpeter equations with lowest-order CHPT amplitudes as the driving kernel, then matched to next-to-leading order (one-loop) CHPT expressions for consistency at low energies. The OZI-violating physics is parametrized through a direct OZI violation parameter λ_φ and the CHPT low-energy constants L₄ʳ and L₆ʳ, which are determined by fitting to experimental data from DM2 and MARK-III.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass distributions are studied for ππ and K K̅ systems up to 1.2 GeV, with data from DM2 and MARK-III experiments.
- The S-wave contribution to the π⁺π⁻ event distribution in J/Ψ → ω π⁺π⁻ is reproduced up to energies of about 0.7 GeV.
- The cut-off in three-momentum space for the loop function g(s)ᵢ is q_max = 0.9 GeV.
- The pion mass used is m₁ = 138 MeV, the kaon mass m₂ = 495.7 MeV, and the weak pion decay constant f_π ≃ 93 MeV.
- The scale μ for dimensional regularization is set to μ = 2 q_max / √e ≃ 1.2 q_max.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Oller and Oset (1997) [Ref. npa], which established that scattering data in the 0⁺⁺ I=0,1 sectors up to 1.2 GeV reflect strong rescattering effects between light pseudoscalars, and generalizes the approach to J/Ψ decays. The study complements earlier work on photon fusion reactions (γ γ → π⁰π⁰, π⁺π⁻, K⁺K⁻, K⁰K̅⁰, π⁰η) by Oller and Oset (1998) [Ref. gamma] and predictions for φ → γ π⁰π⁰, γ π⁺π⁻, γ π⁰η decays by Oller (1999) [Ref. marco], which were confirmed by experiments in Novosibirsk. The results challenge the interpretation of the f₀(980) as a simple K K̅ molecule (Isgur and collaborators) and support the view that the OZI rule is not operative in the scalar 0⁺⁺ channel, enabling further studies of the nature of scalar resonances and the determination of CHPT low-energy constants.
