# Hydrodynamic Initial Conditions in Small Systems from Proton Phase-Space Entropy

> The experimental observation of collective behaviour in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions poses a fundamental theoretical question regarding the proper characterization of the initial state underlying hydrodynamic evolution. While relativistic hydrodynamics requires an initial condition (IC) characterized by an entropy current, corresponding to a maximally mixed state, the microscopic description of the proton is based on inherently quantum objects, that are projections of pure states. We show that the appropriate matching between proton wave function and classical hydrodynamics emerges from the coarse-graining of its phase-space distribution quantified by the Wehrl-like entropy. This entropy provides a semi-classical, positive-definite measure of the density of accessible microstates at a given resolution scale, and therefore constitutes the appropriate quantity to characterize entropy deposition in small collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the appropriate matching between the proton wave function (a pure quantum state) and classical hydrodynamics (which requires a maximally mixed initial entropy current) emerges from coarse-graining the proton's phase-space distribution, quantified by a Wehrl-like entropy; this entropy provides a semiclassical, positive-definite measure of accessible microstates at a given resolution scale, but the authors explicitly state that quantitative analysis and comparison with data are left for future work.

## Method

The authors use light-cone wave function formalism to describe the proton as a superposition of partonic Fock states, then construct a Husimi distribution by Gaussian smearing of the Wigner function with a resolution scale parameter ℓ. They relate this to the gluon distribution function G(x,Q²) and its transverse-coordinate-dependent version, arguing that the Wehrl entropy of the coarse-grained phase-space distribution can be expressed as an integral over the gluon distribution up to the resolution scale Λ, where Λ ∼ ℓ⁻¹. This entropy density is then proposed as the initial entropy profile for hydrodynamic evolution in small collision systems.

## Key numbers

* The Wehrl entropy diverges in the ultraviolet limit as ⟨ln 𝒲⟩ ∼ Λ³, reflecting the proliferation of modes in quantum field theory.
* The resolution scale Λ is related to the spatial size L of a local thermalized cell via the uncertainty principle as Λ ∼ L⁻¹.
* The Q² dependence of the gluon distribution and the entropy comes from renormalization group running and is ∼ ln Q².

## Context

This work extends the successful application of relativistic hydrodynamics from heavy-ion collisions (Heinz, 2013) to small collision systems like proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, where collective behavior has been observed (Nagle; ATLAS Collaboration, 2018). It builds on the authors' previous work (Rabelo-Soares, 2025) addressing initial conditions in small systems, and connects to the formalism of Belitsky (2003) for light-cone distributions. The approach enables future calculation of eccentricities and flow harmonics v_n in pp and pA collisions from an initial Wigner function, which can be compared with experimental data.
