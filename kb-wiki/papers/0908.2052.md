# Triton Electric Form Factor at Low-Energies

> Making use of the Effective Field Theory(EFT) expansion recently developed by the authors, we compute the charge form factor of triton up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). The three-nucleon forces(3NF) is required for renormalization of the three-nucleon system and it effects are predicted for process and is qualitatively supported by available experimental data. We also show that, by including higher order corrections, the calculated charge form factor and charge radius of $^3$H are in satisfactory agreement with the experimental data and the realistic Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana IX potential models calculations. This method makes possible a high precision few-body calculations in nuclear physics. Our result converges order by order in low energy expansion and also cut-off independent.

## Key claim

Using pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO), the calculated triton charge form factor and charge radius agree with experimental data and realistic potential model calculations (Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX), but only when the three-body force parameter H2 is fixed by the triton binding energy and the cutoff is varied between 200 and 550 MeV.

## Method

The author solved the Faddeev integral equation for the three-body system in the spin-triplet S-wave channel using pionless EFT, including the two-nucleon propagators with effective range corrections and a three-body force term. The charge form factor was computed by gauging the Lagrangian via minimal substitution and evaluating the diagrams where the photon couples to nucleons, dibaryon fields, and the three-body force. The integral equations were solved perturbatively order by order up to N$^2$LO, with the three-body parameter H0 fixed from the nd scattering length and H2 fixed from the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

* Triton charge radius at N$^2$LO: 1.775 fm (with uncertainty ±0.02 fm from cutoff variation)
* Triton charge radius at LO: 1.959 fm; at NLO: 1.846 fm; at N$^2$LO: 1.775 fm
* Experimental triton charge radius: 1.755 ± 0.086 fm (Amroun)
* Three-body parameter H0 fixed from nd scattering length a3 = 0.65 ± 0.04 fm
* H2 determined by triton binding energy B3 = 8.48 MeV
* Deuteron binding momentum γt = 45.7025 MeV; effective range ρt = 1.764 fm
* Singlet scattering length as = -23.714 fm; effective range rs = 2.73 fm
* Momentum transfer range for form factor calculation: 0 < q^2 < 0.3 fm^{-2}
* Cutoff variation range: Λ = 200 MeV to Λ = 550 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach previously applied to neutron-deuteron radiative capture and triton photodisintegration (Sadeghi, 2007-2009) to the triton charge form factor. It improves upon the leading-order calculation by Platter (2005) which gave a charge radius of 2.1 fm (20% error), and achieves agreement comparable to the realistic Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX potential model calculations by Golak (2005) and Marcucci (2005) which gave radii of 1.722-1.725 fm (2% error). The results demonstrate that pionless EFT can provide a simple, systematic framework for including external currents in three-body calculations, enabling high-precision predictions for electroweak structure functions of few-body systems.
