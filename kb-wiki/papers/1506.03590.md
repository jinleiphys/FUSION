# Simulating Chiral Magnetic and Separation Effects with Spin-Orbit Coupled Atomic Gases

> The chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects---quantum-anomaly-induced electric current and chiral current along an external magnetic field in parity-odd quark-gluon plasma---have received intense studies in the community of heavy-ion collision physics. We show that analogous effects occur in rotating trapped Fermi gases with Weyl-Zeeman spin-orbit coupling where the rotation plays the role of an external magnetic field. These effects can induce a mass quadrupole in the atomic cloud along the rotation axis which may be tested in future experiments. Our results suggest that the spin-orbit coupled atomic gases are potential simulators of the chiral magnetic and separation effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that rotating trapped Fermi gases with Weyl-Zeeman spin-orbit coupling can exhibit chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects analogous to those in quark-gluon plasma, with the rotation playing the role of an external magnetic field; however, the quantum anomaly and resulting currents only occur when the Zeeman field satisfies \(h < \lambda \sqrt{2m\mu}\), and disappear when the Zeeman field exceeds this threshold due to a Fermi-surface topology transition.

## Method

The authors derive semiclassical equations of motion for spin-orbit coupled atoms in a rotating trap using an adiabatic approximation for the spin dynamics, obtaining effective electric and magnetic fields where the rotation generates an effective magnetic field \(\mathbf{B} = 2m\boldsymbol{\omega}\). They then write a kinetic equation for the distribution function of each chirality and compute the chiral currents, showing that the Berry curvature leads to a quantum anomaly in the continuity equation. The resulting \(\mathbf{B}\)-induced currents are evaluated for a Fermi gas with Weyl-Zeeman spin-orbit coupling at both zero and finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- For pure Weyl spin-orbit coupling at zero temperature, the \(\mathbf{B}\)-induced conductivity for right-handed fermions is \(\mathcal{C}_R = \frac{1}{4\pi^2}\left(\mu - \frac{h^2}{2m\lambda^2}\right)\Theta\left(\mu - \frac{h^2}{2m\lambda^2}\right)\), with \(\mathcal{C}_L = -\mathcal{C}_R\).
- The chiral magnetic effect gives vector current \(\mathbf{j}_V^{\mathbf{B}\text{-ind}} = \frac{\mu_A}{2\pi^2}\mathbf{B}\) and the chiral separation effect gives axial current \(\mathbf{j}_A^{\mathbf{B}\text{-ind}} = \frac{\mu}{2\pi^2}\mathbf{B}\) at zero Zeeman field and zero temperature.
- The quantum anomaly term in the continuity equation for pure Weyl SOC is \(\frac{c}{4\pi^2}\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\).
- The Berry monopole for Weyl-Zeeman SOC is located at \(\mathbf{k}_0 = -\frac{h}{\lambda}\hat{\mathbf{z}}\) with winding number \(F = 1\).
- The condition for the quantum anomaly to exist is \(h < \lambda\sqrt{2m\mu}\); at \(h = \lambda\sqrt{2m\mu}\) the two Fermi surfaces touch.
- The chiral magnetic waves propagate with velocities \(v_R = B(\partial\mu_R/\partial n_R)/(4\pi^2)\) and \(v_L = B(\partial\mu_L/\partial n_L)/(4\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral magnetic and separation effects from heavy-ion collision physics (Kharzeev 2008, Fukushima 2008) and Weyl/Dirac semimetals (Burkov 2012) to cold atomic gases with synthetic spin-orbit coupling. The paper shows that rotating trapped Fermi gases with Weyl-Zeeman spin-orbit coupling can serve as quantum simulators for these quantum-anomaly-induced phenomena, enabling greater flexibility in controlling parameters compared to quark-gluon plasma or solid-state systems. The predicted mass quadrupole in the atomic cloud could be detected in cold atom experiments using light absorption imaging, and the chiral magnetic waves found here are analogous to those previously studied in quark-gluon plasma (Kharzeev and Yee 2011).
