# Direct photon production and flow at low transverse momenta in pp, p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions

> Low transverse momentum direct photon measurements have been carried out by the ALICE experiment at the CERN LHC in small collision systems (pp, $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ and 8 TeV and p--Pb, $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=5.02$ TeV) as well as in heavy-ion collisions (Pb--Pb, $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=2.76$ TeV). For the first time, also the multiplicity dependence of direct photon production was investigated in p--Pb collisions. Whereas in the small systems no significant thermal photon signal was observed, a $\sim15\%$ excess has been measured in central Pb--Pb collisions in the region of $p_{\rm T}<3$ GeV/$c$. A signal of prompt photon production at high transverse momentum consistent with binary scaling has been observed in all collision systems following NLO pQCD predictions. Direct photon flow has been measured in central and semi-central Pb--Pb collisions and found to be of similar size as the charged hadron and decay photon flow.

## Key claim

In small collision systems (pp and p-Pb) no significant thermal photon signal was observed at low transverse momentum, whereas a ~15% excess was measured in central Pb-Pb collisions for pT < 3 GeV/c; however, the direct photon flow in Pb-Pb collisions was found to be of similar size as charged hadron flow, with a significance of only 1.4 sigma in the 0-20% centrality class for 0.9 < pT < 2.1 GeV/c.

## Method

The ALICE experiment measured inclusive photons using three independent reconstruction techniques: the Photon Conversion Method (PCM) using the ITS and TPC, and two calorimetric methods using the EMCal and PHOS detectors. Direct photon signals were extracted by subtracting decay photons obtained from particle decay simulations using parameterizations of measured hadron spectra, quantifying the excess via the ratio R_gamma = inclusive photons / decay photons. The direct photon flow v2 was calculated from the inclusive photon v2, decay photon v2, and the R_gamma measurement using a Bayesian approach for bins where R_gamma had less than 2 sigma systematic significance from unity.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon excess of ~15% measured in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for pT < 3 GeV/c
- Thermal photon excess of ~9% measured in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Direct photon v2 significance of 1.4 sigma in 0-20% centrality and 1.0 sigma in 20-40% centrality for 0.9 < pT < 2.1 GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- pp collisions measured at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV and 8 TeV; p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV in four V0A multiplicity classes plus full NSD sample
- EMCal energy resolution: sigma_E/E = 4.8%/E + 11.3%/sqrt(E) + 1.7% with E in GeV
- PHOS energy resolution: sigma_E/E = 1.8%/E + 3.3%/sqrt(E) + 1.1% with E in GeV
- PCM conversion probability in inner detector material: 8.9%

## Context

This work extends previous ALICE direct photon measurements in pp and Pb-Pb collisions (Acharya 2018, Adam 2015) by including the first multiplicity-dependent direct photon measurement in p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The results contradict expectations of significant thermal photon production in small systems while confirming thermal photon signals in heavy-ion collisions, and the direct photon flow measurements provide new constraints on the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. The measurements enable improved tests of NLO pQCD calculations (Paquet 2015, Jager 2002) and models of thermal photon production and flow, with the observed large direct photon v2 potentially pointing to late photon production times or additional photon sources.
