# Relativity, chiral symmetry, and the nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> A relativistic interpretation for why the proton's $G_E/G_M$ falls and $QF_2/F_1$ is approximately constant is presented. Reproducing the observed $G_E^n$ mandates the inclusion of the effects of the pion cloud. The full relativistic model with a pion cloud provides a good reproduction of all of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed rapid fall of the proton's \(G_E/G_M\) with \(Q^2\) and the approximate constancy of \(QF_2/F_1\) arise from relativistic spin effects (Melosh rotations) that break the hadron helicity conservation rule, but that reproducing the neutron's \(G_E^n\) at low \(Q^2\) requires the additional inclusion of pion-cloud effects, which the abstract notes as a mandate rather than a suggestion.

## Method

The author constructs a light-front relativistic quark model of the nucleon using a spatial wave function from Schlumpf (1994) with parameters \(\beta = 0.607\) GeV, \(\gamma = 3.5\), and quark mass \(m = 0.267\) GeV. Form factors are computed from matrix elements of the "good" current component \(J^+\) between nucleon light-cone spinors, which naturally incorporate Melosh rotations. To address the neutron charge form factor, the model is extended to the light-front cloudy bag model (LFCBM) by adding pion-loop diagrams evaluated via Feynman-graph integration over the virtual-pion momentum, with a pion-nucleon cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_{\pi N}\) refitted alongside the quark parameters.

## Key numbers

- The model's bare-nucleon prediction for the neutron charge radius squared is \(R^2_{\text{model}} = -0.025\) fm\(^2\), about five times smaller than the experimental value of \(R^2 = -0.113 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^2\).
- The Foldy contribution to the neutron charge radius is \(R_F^2 = -0.111\) fm\(^2\), derived from \(\kappa_n = -1.91\) and the neutron mass.
- The bare-nucleon model gives \(R_1^2 = +0.086\) fm\(^2\) for the \(F_1\) contribution, which nearly cancels the Foldy term.
- Three parameter sets for the LFCBM are listed in Table 1; for Set 1 (solid): \(m = 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\beta = 3.65\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\Lambda = 3.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 4.1\), yielding \(-R^2_n = 0.111\) fm\(^2\), \(-\mu_n = 1.73\) nuclear magnetons, and \(\mu_p = 2.88\) nuclear magnetons.
- The model reproduces data up to \(Q^2 \approx 20\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quark model of Schlumpf (1994) and the earlier cloudy bag model (Thomas, 1984) by combining Poincare invariance with chiral-symmetry-required pion-cloud effects in a light-front framework. It provides a unified explanation for the surprising experimental results from Jefferson Lab (Jones et al., 2000; Gayou et al., 2001) that \(G_E/G_M\) falls linearly with \(Q^2\) while \(QF_2/F_1\) remains roughly constant. The approach enables future inclusion of configuration mixing (Cardarelli, 2000), running quark masses, exchange currents, or intermediate \(\Delta\) contributions to resolve remaining discrepancies at very high \(Q^2\) and in the neutron magnetic form factor.
