# An alternative to perturbative renormalization in 3+1 dimensional field theories

> Perturbative renormalization provides the bedrock of understanding quantum field theories. In this work, I point out an alternative way of renormalizing quantum field theories, which is naturally encountered and well known for the case of large N scalar field theories. In terms of bare parameters, this non-perturbative alternative renormalization differs qualitatively from its perturbative cousin: in the continuum limit, the bare coupling constant goes to zero instead of infinity, and there is no wave-function counterterm. Despite these differences, the resulting n-point functions of the theory are finite. I provide explicit results for alternative renormalization for the O(N) model and QCD with $N_f=12$ flavors in 3+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that quantum field theories in 3+1 dimensions can be renormalized non-perturbatively through an alternative procedure where, in the continuum limit, the bare coupling constant goes to zero instead of infinity and no wave-function counterterm is needed, provided one works within a large N expansion framework rather than a weak-coupling perturbative expansion.

## Method

The author performs non-perturbative renormalization of the O(N) scalar field theory and QCD with Nf=12 flavors using a large N expansion, implemented through successive resummation schemes labeled R0, R1, and R2. The renormalization conditions are imposed by requiring finiteness of the 0-point and 2-point functions at leading order in 1/N, which determines the counterterms for the coupling constant, mass, and vacuum energy but leaves no room for wave-function renormalization. For the O(N) model, the calculation proceeds through dimensional regularization in d=4-2ε dimensions, with the saddle point method used to evaluate the partition function and extract the running coupling and mass parameters.

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative running coupling for the O(N) model at leading order in large N is λ_R(μ̄) = 4π² / ln(Λ²_MS̄ / μ̄²), where Λ_MS̄ is the emergent energy scale corresponding to the Landau pole.
- The critical value of the mass parameter α_R0 at which the saddle point solution ceases to be real is α_c = 1.
- For α_R0 = 0, the vector pole mass is m̄_R0 = √e Λ_MS̄, and for α_R0 = α_c, it is m̄_R0 = Λ_MS̄.
- The vector pole mass satisfies the lower bound m̄_R0 ≥ Λ_MS̄ regardless of the mass renormalization.
- The R1 scheme running coupling includes a 1/N correction: λ_R(μ̄) = 4π² / [(1 + 2/N) ln(Λ²_MS̄ / μ̄²)].
- The R2 level resummation requires a divergent rescaling factor c_Σ = 6 ln ε to cancel a term proportional to m_R0⁴ ln m_R0² ln ε in the free energy.

## Context

This work extends the known non-perturbative renormalization program for large N scalar field theories that has been developed since the 1970s by Abbott (1975), Linde (1976), and Parisi (1975), and more recently in Φ-derivable and 2PI effective theories by van Hees (2001), Blaizot (2003), and Berges (2005). The paper explicitly contrasts the alternative renormalization with standard perturbative renormalization as described in textbooks by Ramond (1981), Ryder (1985), and Peskin (1995), where the bare coupling diverges in the continuum limit and wave-function renormalization is required. The results enable systematic calculation of renormalized observables in large N expansions without relying on perturbative counterterm structures, and the author provides explicit results for both the O(N) model and QCD with Nf=12 flavors.
