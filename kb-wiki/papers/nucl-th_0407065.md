# Comparison of predictions for nuclear effects in the Marteau model with the NUX+FLUKA scheme

## Key claim

The NUX+FLUKA Monte Carlo generator and the Marteau model predict nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus reactions that differ substantially: pion absorption in NUX+FLUKA and non-pionic Delta decays in the Marteau model differ by about 30% at 2 GeV, and the fraction of pions produced from re-interactions after a primary quasi-elastic vertex is four times larger in NUX+FLUKA than in the Marteau model, but the authors note that the NUX+FLUKA scheme is known to be unsatisfactory in the few-GeV region and does not include separate resonance contributions.

## Method

The authors compared predictions for charged-current muon neutrino reactions on oxygen using two approaches: the NUX+FLUKA Monte Carlo generator (run with the PRET driver, producing 10^5 events per energy point for total cross sections and 10^6 inelastic events at 1 GeV for differential distributions) and the Marteau model (with numerical predictions based on Sobczyk (2004)). Events were classified by primary vertex characteristics and final-state pion count; for quasi-elastic scattering, comparisons were made both without final-state interactions (using primary vertex classification) and with final-state interactions (using particles seen in the final state).

## Key numbers

- Pion absorption in NUX+FLUKA and non-pionic Delta decays in the Marteau model differ by about 30% at 2 GeV neutrino energy, causing the NUX+FLUKA cross section to be slightly higher.
- At 2 GeV neutrino energy, the single pion production cross section on oxygen predicted by NUX+FLUKA is a factor of 2 higher than that from the Marteau model.
- The fraction of pions produced from quasi-elastic primary vertices due to nuclear effects (final-state interactions) is four times larger in NUX+FLUKA than in the Marteau model.
- In the Marteau model, a quadratic Fermi momentum distribution with a sharp cutoff at k_F = 225 MeV is assumed, while NUX+FLUKA uses a smooth distribution.
- At 2 GeV, NUX+FLUKA predicts a single pion production cross section higher than the saturation plateau of approaches based on the Rein-Sehgal model, which should be approximately 10 x 10^{-38} cm^2.

## Context

This work extends comparisons between numerical (FLUKA-based) and analytical (RPA-based) treatments of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions at few-GeV energies, following earlier presentations by Zeller (2002) at NuInt02 that showed large differences among Monte Carlo codes. The Marteau model, which describes quasi-elastic and Delta production together including 2-particle-2-hole excitations, was previously investigated by Sobczyk (2004) and is considered a candidate for implementation in Monte Carlo codes; the present comparison reveals that its predictions for pion production are substantially lower than NUX+FLUKA, motivating further improvements in existing Monte Carlo generators.
