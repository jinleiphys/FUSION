# Heavy quark spin multiplet structure of $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$

> Very recently, the LHCb collaboration has reported the new result about the hidden-charm pentaquarks: $P_c(4312)$ near the $\bar{D}Σ_c$ threshold, and $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ near $\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ threshold. We study the heavy quark spin (HQS) multiplet structures of these newly $P_c$ pentaquarks under the heavy quark spin symmetry based on the hadronic molecular picture. We point out that $P_c(4312)$ is the $J^P = 1/2^-$ member of an HQS triplet, and $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ are the $J^P = 3/2^-$ member of the HQS triplet and an HQS singlet with $J^P = 3/2^-$. Namely, the $P_c(4312)$ and one of $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ belong to an HQS triplet. The HQS multiplet structure predicts the existence of $J^P = 5/2^-$ state near $\bar{D}^\astΣ_c^\ast$ threshold.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the newly observed LHCb pentaquarks can be classified into heavy quark spin (HQS) multiplets within the hadronic molecular picture, specifically that Pc(4312) is the J^P = 1/2^- member of an HQS triplet, while Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) are the J^P = 3/2^- member of the HQS triplet and an HQS singlet with J^P = 3/2^-, respectively, with the caveat that the assignment of which of the two heavier states belongs to which multiplet is not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors apply heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) to S-wave hadronic molecular states composed of an anti-charmed meson (D or D*) and a charmed baryon (Sigma_c or Sigma_c*). They use a light-cloud spin (LCS) basis to classify the spin structures of these molecules into HQS multiplets (singlet, doublet, triplet) and analyze the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) to determine which multiplets are attractive. The observed pentaquark masses are then compared to the thresholds of the corresponding meson-baryon pairs to assign them to specific HQS multiplet states.

## Key numbers

*   Pc(4312) mass: M = 4311.9 +/- 0.7 (+6.8/-0.6) MeV, width: Gamma = 9.8 +/- 2.7 (+3.7/-4.5) MeV; near the D Sigma_c threshold (approximately 4320 MeV).
*   Pc(4440) mass: M = 4440.3 +/- 1.3 (+4.1/-4.7) MeV, width: Gamma = 20.6 +/- 4.9 (+8.7/-10.1) MeV; near the D* Sigma_c threshold.
*   Pc(4457) mass: M = 4457.3 +/- 0.6 (+4.1/-1.7) MeV, width: Gamma = 6.4 +/- 2.0 (+5.7/-1.9) MeV; near the D* Sigma_c threshold.
*   The mass splitting between K and K* is about 400 MeV, while the splitting between D and D* is about 140 MeV, illustrating the approach of heavy quark spin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of HQS multiplet structures of P-bar(*) Sigma_Q(*) molecular states (Shimizu, Yamaguchi, Harada, 2018) to the newly reported LHCb pentaquarks (Skwarnicki, 2019). It builds on the hadronic molecular interpretation of pentaquarks, which was previously applied to the earlier Pc(4380) and Pc(4450) states (Chen, 2015; He, 2016; Roca, Nieves, Oset, 2015). The analysis predicts the existence of a J^P = 5/2^- state near the D* Sigma_c* threshold as a missing partner of the HQS triplet, which could serve as a strong test of the molecular picture.
