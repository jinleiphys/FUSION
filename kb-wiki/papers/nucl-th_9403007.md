# New calculations of the PNC Matrix Element for the $J^πT$ 0$^{+}1,0^{-}1$ doublet in $^{14}$N

> A new calculation of the predominantly isoscalar PNC matrix element between the $J^πT$ $0^{+}1,0^{-}1$ (E$_{x} \approx$ 8.7 MeV) states in $^{14}$N has been carried out in a (0+1+2+3+4)$\hbar ω$ model space with the Warburton-Brown interaction. The magnitude of the PNC matrix element of 0.22 to 0.34 eV obtained with the DDH PNC interaction is substantially suppressed compared with previous calculations in smaller model spaces but shows agreement with the preliminary Seattle experimental data. The calculated sign is opposite to that obtained experimentally, and the implications of this are discussed.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the predominantly isoscalar parity-nonconserving (PNC) matrix element between the \(0^{+}1,0^{-}1\) doublet in \(^{14}\)N is substantially suppressed (0.22 to 0.34 eV after including a Woods-Saxon correction) compared to earlier smaller-model-space calculations, and agrees with preliminary Seattle experimental data; however, the calculated sign is opposite to the experimental sign, and the authors discuss that this sign discrepancy may require "in medium" modification of the isoscalar PNC weak coupling constants.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in a (0+1+2+3+4)\(\hbar\omega\) model space using the Warburton-Brown (WBT and WBP) interactions, with single-particle energies adjusted to reproduce the \(^{14}\)N spectrum. The PNC matrix element was computed in a one-body approximation, checked against full two-body calculations to 2% precision, using the DDH weak-coupling constants and harmonic-oscillator wave functions (\(\hbar\omega = 14.0\) MeV), with an additional 32% suppression factor estimated from Woods-Saxon wave functions for the nearly unbound \(1s_{1/2}\) orbit. Seven different calculations were performed varying the interaction, the \(0p-1s0d\) gap, and the inclusion of 4\(\hbar\omega\) configurations.

## Key numbers

- PNC matrix element magnitude range: 0.22 to 0.34 eV (after 32% Woods-Saxon suppression), compared to earlier ZBM model space result of 1.39 eV and (0+1+2)\(\hbar\omega\) Kuo-Brown result of 0.29 eV.
- DDH weak-coupling constants give a range of 0.232 to 0.764 eV across seven calculations, with average around 0.48 eV.
- Experimental magnitude: \(0.38 \pm 0.28\) eV from preliminary Seattle data.
- Dominant \(0p_{1/2} \rightarrow 1s_{1/2}\) contribution suppressed by 37% for protons and 28% for neutrons (average 32%) when using Woods-Saxon wave functions.
- Calculated widths: 5.2 keV for the \(0^{+}\) state and 1020 keV for the \(0^{-}\) state, compared to experimental \(3.8 \pm 0.3\) keV and \(410 \pm 20\) keV.
- Isotensor contribution is about 7% destructive to the isoscalar matrix element.
- 0\(\hbar\omega\) content of the \((0^{+}1)_2\) wave function estimated at 3-5% from B(M1) comparison, giving a 0\(\hbar\omega\)-1\(\hbar\omega\) contribution of approximately -(0.350 - 0.450) eV.

## Context

This work extends previous PNC calculations for the \(^{14}\)N doublet, which had been performed in smaller model spaces such as the ZBM (\(0p_{1/2},0d_{5/2},1s_{1/2}\)) space (Adelberger and Haxton, 1985) and the (0+1+2)\(\hbar\omega\) space with Kuo-Brown and Millener-Kurath interactions (Jin et al., 1988). The new larger-space calculations show a factor of 3-4 suppression relative to the ZBM result, bringing the magnitude into agreement with preliminary experimental data from Zeps (1989) and Zeps et al. (1994). The sign discrepancy with experiment, also noted in earlier factorization-approximation calculations, suggests that further theoretical work on "in medium" modifications of isoscalar PNC coupling constants and more accurate experiments are needed to resolve the puzzle, which also involves the \(\vec{p}+p\) scattering and \(^{19}\)F experiments.
