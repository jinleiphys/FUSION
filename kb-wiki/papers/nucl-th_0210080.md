# Migdal's short range correlations in a covariant model

> We construct a covariant model for short range correlations of a pion emerged in nuclear matter. Once the delta-hole contribution is considered an additional and so far neglected channel opens that leads to significant modifications in the vicinity of the kinematical region defined by ω\sim |\vec q |. We speculate that this novel effect should be important for the quantitative interpretation of charge exchange reactions like C(He,t).

## Key claim
A fully covariant generalization of Dmitriev's model for short-range correlations in nuclear matter reveals that previous treatments missed a coupled-channel structure involving delta-hole contributions, leading to significant modifications of the pion self energy in the kinematical region where ω ∼ |q⃗|, which is relevant for charge-exchange reactions like ¹²C(³He,t).

## Method
The author constructs a covariant model for the pion self energy in nuclear matter using relativistic nucleon and delta propagators with Migdal-type short-range correlation vertices. The Dyson equation is solved by decomposing the loop functions and interaction vertices into a complete set of longitudinal and transverse projectors that account for the additional tensor structures u_μ q_ν and q_μ u_ν, which were neglected in prior non-relativistic treatments. The resulting 4×4 matrix equation for the self energy incorporates both nucleon-hole and delta-hole contributions with their off-diagonal components.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon-pion coupling constant used is f_N = 0.988, and the delta-pion coupling is f_Δ = 2 f_N.
- The Migdal parameters used are g₁₁ = 0.585, g₂₂ = 0.6, and g₁₂ = 0.191 + 0.051 g₂₂, as suggested in prior work by Oset and Rho (1980s) and Nakano et al. (1990s).
- Nuclear saturation density is taken with Fermi momentum k_F = 268 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the pion self energy is enhanced by about a factor of 5 at ω ≈ 350 MeV and |q⃗| = 300 MeV when the full coupled-channel structure is included.
- The spectral function for the delta isobar uses a parameter C = 1.85 with a correction to the formula from Lutz, Wolf, and Friman (2001).

## Context
This work extends the covariant treatment of short-range correlations initiated by Dmitriev (1990) and continued by Nakano et al. (1990s), who introduced relativistic Migdal vertices but missed certain tensor structures in the delta-hole channel. The author shows that the standard non-relativistic formula for the pion self energy with Migdal parameters, as used by Migdal (1978) and Oset and Rho (1979), is inadequate for ω ∼ |q⃗| because it neglects off-diagonal loop components. The corrected model enables more accurate interpretation of charge-exchange reactions such as ¹²C(³He,t) studied by Gaarde (1983) and Roy-Stephan (1984), and suggests that pion condensation studies at ω = 0 are only minimally affected.
