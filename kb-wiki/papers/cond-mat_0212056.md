# Nonextensive information entropy for stochastic networks

> Nature is full of random networks of complex topology describing such apparently disparate systems as biological, economical or informatical ones. Their most characteristic feature is the apparent scale-free character of interconnections between nodes. Using an information theory approach, we show that maximalization of information entropy leads to a wide spectrum of possible types of distributions including, in the case of nonextensive information entropy, the power-like scale-free distributions characteristic of complex systems.

# Nonextensive information entropy for stochastic networks

## Key claim

Maximizing nonextensive Tsallis entropy (rather than Shannon entropy) for the probability distribution of node connections in stochastic networks yields a power-like scale-free distribution that can describe the entire range of connectivity, not just the asymptotic tail. The approach works only when the system possesses intrinsic fluctuations or correlations that prevent the use of standard Shannon entropy.

## Method

The authors maximize the Tsallis entropy \(S_q\) under constraints of normalization and fixed mean value of the number of connections \(\langle k \rangle_q = \lambda_0\), using the standard information-theoretic variational procedure. They then fit the resulting distribution \(P_q(k)\) to empirical data from the World Wide Web network, extracting the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) and the mean connectivity parameter \(\lambda_0\) from the data moments. They also connect the derived distribution to existing network growth models by showing that the evolution equation \(\partial P(k)/\partial k = -P(k)/\lambda(k)\) with a linear \(\lambda(k)\) reproduces the same functional form.

## Key numbers

* For the WWW network with 325,729 nodes and mean connections \(\langle k \rangle = 5.46\), the fitted parameters are \(q = 1.65\) and \(\lambda_0 = 1.91\), yielding an asymptotic power-law exponent \(\gamma = q/(q-1) = 2.54\).
* The condition for finite mean connectivity \(\langle k \rangle < \infty\) requires \(q < 2\); the variance \(Var(k)\) diverges at \(q = 3/2\), which corresponds to the universal exponent \(\gamma = 3\).
* The moments of the distribution are given by \(\langle k \rangle = \lambda_0/(2-q)\) and \(Var(k) = \lambda_0^2 / [(3-2q)(2-q)^2]\).
* For the exponential (Shannon) limit \(q \to 1\), the distribution reduces to \(P(k) = (1/\lambda_0) \exp(-k/\lambda_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the information-theoretic approach to random networks beyond the standard Erdos-Renyi (ER) model (1959), which yields Poisson or exponential distributions, and beyond the preferential attachment model of Barabasi and Albert (1999), which gives a pure power law with exponent \(\gamma = 3\). The authors show that Tsallis entropy (Tsallis, 1988) unifies both cases: \(q=1\) recovers the exponential ER model, while \(q=3/2\) reproduces the scale-free \(\gamma=3\) case. The approach enables systematic classification of complex networks by their \((q, \lambda_0)\) parameters and provides a criterion for comparing competing network growth models: any model that reproduces the same \((q, \lambda_0)\) pair is equally good from an information-theoretic standpoint.
