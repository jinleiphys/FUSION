# Nonrelativistic nucleon effective masses in nuclear matter: BHF versus RHF

> The density and isospin dependences of the nonrelativistic nucleon effective mass ($m^*$) are studied, which is a measure of the nonlocality of the single particle (s.p.) potential. We decouple it further into the so called k-mass ($m^*_k$, i.e., the nonlocality in space) and E-mass ($m^*_E$, i.e., the nonlocality in time). Both masses are determined and compared from the latest versions of the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree Fock (BHF) model and the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) model. The latter are achieved based on the corresponding Schrödinger equivalent s.p. potential in a relativistic framework. We demonstrate the origins of different effective masses and discuss also their neutron-proton splitting in the asymmetric matter in different models. We find that the neutron-proton splittings of both the k-mass and the E-mass have the same asymmetry dependences at considered densities, namely $m^*_{k,n} > m^*_{k,p}$ and $m^*_{E,p} > m^*_{E,n}$. However, the resulting splittings of nucleon effective masses could have different asymmetry dependences in the two models, because they could be dominated either by that of the k-mass (then we have $m^*_n > m^*_p$ in the BHF model) or by that of the E-mass (then we have $m^*_p > m^*_n$ in the RHF model).

## Key claim

The neutron-proton splitting of the total nonrelativistic effective mass can have opposite signs depending on whether the k-mass or the E-mass dominates: the BHF model yields \(m^*_n > m^*_p\) (k-mass dominated), while the RHF model yields \(m^*_p > m^*_n\) (E-mass dominated), and the BHF result is more consistent with empirical nucleon-nucleus scattering data.

## Method

The authors computed the nonrelativistic nucleon effective mass and its decomposition into k-mass (spatial nonlocality) and E-mass (temporal nonlocality) using two many-body approaches: the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) model with a microscopic three-body force (TBF) and the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (parameter set PKA1). For the RHF model, the nonrelativistic effective mass was extracted by rewriting the Dirac equation into a Schrodinger-equivalent form. Results were compared for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at densities \(\rho_0\) and \(2\rho_0\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.20\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (BHF+TBF) and \(0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (RHF).
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: \(-14.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (BHF+TBF), \(-15.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (RHF).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(30.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (BHF+TBF), \(36.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (RHF).
- Compression modulus at saturation: \(226\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (BHF+TBF), \(230\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (RHF).
- Effective mass at saturation for RHF parameter sets: \(m^*_N/m_N = 0.59\) (PKO1), \(0.60\) (PKO2), \(0.59\) (PKO3), \(0.55\) (PKA1).
- Isospin splitting from nucleon-nucleus scattering data: \((m^*_n - m^*_p)/m = (0.41 \pm 0.15)\beta\) at normal density.
- BHF model isospin splitting: \((m^*_n - m^*_p)/m \simeq 0.17\beta\) at normal density.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of effective masses in nuclear matter by explicitly separating the k-mass and E-mass contributions, which had not been clearly discussed for isospin asymmetry. It contrasts the BHF approach (with ladder summation and three-body forces) against the RHF approach (covariant density functional theory with parameter sets PKO1-3 and PKA1 from Long et al. (2006, 2007)). The results enable a more microscopic understanding of why different many-body models yield opposite neutron-proton effective mass splittings, and suggest that the missing short-range correlations (Brueckner ladder diagrams) in the RHF model are responsible for its disagreement with empirical data from Li et al. (2015).
