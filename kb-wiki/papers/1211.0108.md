# An analytic review of DSPIN-11

> A short analytical review of the main results of the DSPIN-11 Workshop (JINR, Dubna, September 20--24, 2011) is given.

## Key claim

This paper provides a summary of the results presented at the DSPIN-11 Workshop, highlighting that while single spin asymmetries are generally described by existing theory, the mechanisms behind large asymmetries observed in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC remain unexplained, and the Sivers function may lose universality depending on the process.

## Method

The authors reviewed the talks and discussions from the DSPIN-11 Workshop held at JINR, Dubna from September 20-24, 2011, covering experimental results from HERMES, COMPASS, CLAS, RHIC (PHENIX and STAR), and JLab, as well as theoretical developments in QCD spin physics including generalized parton distributions (GPDs), T-odd distribution functions, and models of nucleon structure.

## Key numbers

* 113 participants from 24 countries attended the workshop, including 12 women
* HERMES data on the Sivers distribution function for positive kaons in the region of small x showed values approximately 2.5 times larger than theoretical predictions
* New JLab data from experiment GEp(III) indicated flattening of the proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio in the Q²=6-8 [GeV]² region
* The projected eRHIC collider would have a luminosity of 10³⁴ and use polarized protons of 250 GeV with polarized electrons of 20 GeV
* The proposed complex at IKP (Jülich) aims to lower the limit of deuteron electric dipole moment measurement to 10⁻²⁴

## Context

This review extends the series of DSPIN workshops that began in 1981 on the initiative of L.I. Lapidus, providing a snapshot of spin physics progress between the larger International Symposia in spin physics held in even years. The results presented enable future measurements at facilities including eRHIC, the upgraded JLab, the SPASCHARM installation at IHEP Protvino, and the NICA complex at JINR, while the unresolved "spin crisis" and the need to measure orbital angular momenta through GPDs remain open questions for the field.
