# Pion and Kaon parton distributions in the QCD instanton vacuum

> We discuss a general diagrammatic description of n-point functions in the QCD instanton vacuum that resums planar diagrams, enforces gauge invariance and spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. We use these diagrammatic rules to derive the pion and kaon quasi-parton amplitude and distribution functions at leading order in the instanton packing fraction for large but finite momentum. The instanton and anti-instanton zero modes and non-zero modes are found to contribute to the quasi-distributions, but the latter are shown to drop out in the large momentum limit. The pertinent pion and kaon parton distribution amplitudes and functions are made explicit at the low renormalization scale fixed by the inverse instanton size. Assuming that factorization holds, the pion parton distributions are evolved to higher renormalization scales with one-loop DGLAP and compared to existing data.

## Key claim

The paper derives pion and kaon parton distribution functions from first principles in the QCD instanton vacuum, showing that the instanton and anti-instanton zero modes and non-zero modes both contribute to the quasi-distributions, but the non-zero mode contributions drop out in the large momentum limit, leaving results valid at the low renormalization scale set by the inverse instanton size.

## Method

The authors develop a diagrammatic expansion of n-point functions in the random instanton vacuum that resums planar diagrams while enforcing gauge invariance and spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, organized by the small packing fraction parameter kappa approximately 10^{-3}. They derive the quark propagator and effective mass at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the expansion parameter alpha approximately sqrt(kappa), then construct the pion and kaon quasi-parton distribution amplitudes and functions using the Bethe-Salpeter-like equations for the meson vertices. The resulting parton distributions are evolved to higher renormalization scales using one-loop DGLAP evolution assuming factorization holds.

## Key numbers

* Instanton packing fraction: kappa = 3.186 x 10^{-3} (dimensionless, from N rho^4 / (2 V_4 N_c))
* Instanton size: rho approximately 1/3 fm (0.313 +/- 0.016 fm from fit to lattice data)
* Instanton density: n = N/V_4 approximately 2 fm^{-4}
* Effective quark mass at zero momentum: M(0) = 383 +/- 39 MeV (from fit to lattice data in Coulomb gauge)
* Pion decay constant in chiral limit: f_pi = 86 MeV (with constant C = 0.849)
* Pion decay constant for massive pions: f_pi = 93 MeV (with constant C = 0.897)
* Kaon decay constant: f_K = 110 MeV (with constant C = 1.04453)

## Context

This work extends the random instanton vacuum approach previously developed by Diakonov, Petrov, and others (Diakonov and Petrov, various years) for describing the QCD ground state as an ensemble of instantons and anti-instantons, and applies it to parton distribution functions following Ji's proposal (Ji, 2013) to extract light-cone distributions from equal-time quasi-distributions. The results are compared to lattice simulations of the effective quark mass (Bowman, 2005) and to existing experimental data after DGLAP evolution, enabling first-principles semi-classical calculations of parton distributions that can be tested against future lattice QCD results and experimental measurements.
