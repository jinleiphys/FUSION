# Charge separation with fluctuating domains in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Charge separation induced by the chiral magnetic effect suggested that some ${\cal P}$- or ${\cal CP}$-odd metastable domains could be produced in a QCD vacuum in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Based on a multi-phase transport model, our results suggest that a domain-based scenario with final state interactions can describe the solenoidal tracker at RHIC detector (STAR) measurements of both same- and opposite-charge azimuthal angle correlations, $<\cos(ϕ_α+ϕ_β)>$, in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV. The occupancy factor of the total volume of domains over the fireball volume is small, which indicates that the size and number of metastable domains should be relatively small in the early stage of a quark-gluon plasma.

# Charge separation with fluctuating domains in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a domain-based scenario with final state interactions can describe the STAR measurements of same- and opposite-charge azimuthal angle correlations in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, but only when the domain radius is limited to less than 0.5 fm and the transverse momentum cutoff is below 0.5 GeV/c; the occupancy factor of domains over the fireball volume is small (2-4%), indicating that metastable domains are relatively small and few in the early stage of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The authors used a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with a string melting scenario, introducing initial separated charges inside many P- or CP-odd metastable domain bubbles parameterized by a radius upper limit r and a transverse momentum cutoff pT^eff. Within each domain bubble, they switched the p_y values of one pair of u/anti-u and one pair of d/anti-d quarks (four quarks per domain for N_f=2) whose transverse momenta were below pT^eff, then evolved the system through parton cascade, hadronization via quark coalescence, and resonance decays (with hadronic scatterings turned off to ensure charge conservation). The simulations were performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with a 10 mb parton interaction cross section.

## Key numbers
- Domain radius r < 0.5 fm and pT^eff < 0.5 GeV/c produced the best description of STAR data for both same- and opposite-charge correlations
- Domain production rate decreased from 10% (most central) to 7% (most peripheral) for r < 0.5 fm and pT^eff < 0.5 GeV/c
- Occupancy factor of total domain volume over fireball volume for r < 0.5 fm ranged from 2.19% (60-70% centrality) to 4.29% (10-20% centrality)
- For r < 1 fm, occupancy factors reached 41.66% to 70.62% across centrality bins
- For r < 0.3 fm, occupancy factors ranged from 0.19% to 0.40% across centrality bins
- The magnetic field strength was estimated as eB ~ m_pi^2 ~ 10^18 G

## Context
This work extends the earlier AMPT study by Ma and Zhang (2011) which introduced a global initial electric charge dipole distribution but found that final state interactions significantly weakened charge separation; that study did not include a realistic domain scenario. The present paper improves upon that by introducing localized metastable domain bubbles, showing that a domain-based scenario with centrality-dependent domain production rates can better describe the STAR experimental data (Abelev 2009) for the reaction-plane-dependent correlation <cos(phi_alpha+phi_beta)>. However, the reaction-plane-independent correlation <cos(phi_alpha-phi_beta)> still shows large discrepancies with experiment, indicating that non-negligible backgrounds require further investigation. The results enable future work incorporating a real initial domain distribution from the chiral magnetic effect and developing the QGP within chiral kinetic theory.
