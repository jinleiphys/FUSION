# The Influence of Initial State Fluctuations on Heavy Quark Energy Loss in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> We study the effects of initial state fluctuations on the dynamical evolution of heavy quarks inside a quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The evolution of heavy quarks in QGP matter is described utilizing a modified Langevin equation that incorporates the contributions from both collisional and radiative energy loss. The spacetime evolution of the fireball medium is simulated with a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model. We find that when the medium traversed by the heavy quark contains a fixed amount of energy, heavy quarks tend to lose more energy for greater fluctuations of the medium density. This may result in a larger suppression of heavy flavor observables in a fluctuating QGP matter than in a smooth one. The possibility of using hard probes to infer the information of initial states of heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

When the medium traversed by a heavy quark contains a fixed amount of energy, heavy quarks tend to lose more energy for greater fluctuations of the medium density, which may result in a larger suppression of heavy flavor observables in a fluctuating QGP matter than in a smooth one; the effect depends on the assumption that total energy is fixed while the volume fluctuates, and on the correlation between heavy quark production vertices and hot spot positions.

## Method

The authors simulated heavy quark evolution using a modified Langevin equation that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss, with the gluon emission spectrum taken from the higher-twist formalism. The QGP fireball spacetime evolution was modeled with a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model using MC-Glauber initial conditions, comparing smooth (averaged over 100,000 events) and fluctuating (single event) entropy density profiles for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark energy loss was insensitive to the size of a single hot tube when total medium energy was fixed, tested with tube radii varied while keeping total energy constant at a baseline of 200 MeV temperature for R=5 fm.
- Energy loss increased significantly with the number of hot tubes (N) when total medium energy was fixed; tested with N hot tubes of radius 0.5 fm, baseline temperature 500 MeV for N=1.
- For central 0-7.5% Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, fluctuating initial conditions produced about 12% more quenching at high pT compared to smooth initial conditions.
- For 50-60% peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, the quenching increase from fluctuating initial conditions was about 7%.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient was set to D = 6/(2πT), equivalent to a gluon transport coefficient q̂ around 3 GeV²/fm at T = 400 MeV.
- The hydrodynamic starting time was τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, and the freeze-out temperature was 165 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of initial state fluctuations on light flavor jets by Rodriguez (2010), Renk (2011), and Zhang (2012), which had not reached a unified conclusion. It applies the modified Langevin framework previously developed by Cao (2013) for heavy quarks with smooth initial conditions to the case of fluctuating initial conditions. The results suggest that heavy quark quenching could be used as an additional probe of initial state granularity, complementing traditional observables such as higher-order harmonic flow coefficients of soft hadrons, but the authors note that constraints on other uncertainties (in-medium fragmentation, correlation between production vertices and hot spots, different energy loss formalisms) must be improved before firm conclusions can be drawn.
