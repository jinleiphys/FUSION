# Heavy flavor suppression: role of hadronic matter

> The role of hadronic matter in the suppression of open heavy flavored mesons has been studied. The heavy-quarks (HQs) suppression factors have been calculated and contrasted with the experimental data obtained from nuclear collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadronic Collider (LHC) experiments. It is found that the suppression in the hadronic phase at RHIC energy is around $20%-25%$ whereas at the LHC it is around $10%-12%$ for the D meson. In case of B meson the hadronic suppression is around $10%-12%$ and $5%-6%$ at RHIC and LHC energies respectively. Present study suggests that the suppression of heavy flavor in the hadronic phase is significant at RHIC. However, the effect of hadronic suppression at LHC is marginal, this makes the characterization of QGP at LHC less complicated.

## Key claim

The suppression of heavy flavor mesons in the hadronic phase is significant at RHIC energies (20-25% for D mesons) but marginal at LHC energies (10-12% for D mesons), meaning that the characterization of QGP at LHC is less complicated by hadronic contributions.

## Method

The authors modeled heavy quark evolution through QGP using the Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients from collisional and radiative energy loss processes, then fragmented heavy quarks to D and B mesons at the transition temperature (T_c ~ 170 MeV). The subsequent evolution of heavy flavor mesons through hadronic matter was again described by the Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients from interactions with pions, kaons, nucleons, and eta mesons, with the background medium expansion described by relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- D meson suppression in the hadronic phase at RHIC: 20%-25% for p_T = 3-10 GeV
- D meson suppression in the hadronic phase at LHC: 10%-12% for p_T = 3-10 GeV
- B meson suppression in the hadronic phase at RHIC: 10%-12%
- B meson suppression in the hadronic phase at LHC: 5%-6%
- Initial temperature for RHIC calculations: T_i = 0.4 GeV with thermalization time tau_i = 0.2 fm/c, constrained by dN/dy = 1100
- Initial temperature for LHC calculations: T_i = 550 MeV with tau_i = 0.1 fm/c for sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Transition temperature T_c = 170 MeV and freeze-out temperature T_f = 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark energy loss in QGP (Moore and Teaney; Rapp; Ko; Hatsuda) by quantifying the additional suppression from hadronic interactions. The authors use drag and diffusion coefficients for D and B mesons in hadronic matter previously calculated by Ghosh and Das (2013) and compare their results to experimental data from STAR (2011) and PHENIX (2011) at RHIC and ALICE (2012) at LHC. The finding that hadronic suppression is minimal at LHC, especially for B mesons, suggests that B mesons may serve as a cleaner probe of QGP properties than electromagnetic probes, where hadronic contributions are more significant (Rapp and Wambach; Alam et al.).
