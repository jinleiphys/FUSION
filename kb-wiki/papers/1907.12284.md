# Ruling out the supersoft high-density symmetry energy from the discovery of PSR J0740+6620 with mass $2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09}M_\odot$

> Using the very recently reported mass $2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09}M_\odot$ of PSR J0740+6620 together with the data of finite nuclei and the constraints on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter at suprasaturation densities from flow data in heavy-ion collisions, we show that the symmetry energy $E_{\rm sym}(n)$ cannot be supersoft so that it becomes negative at suprasaturation densities in neutron stars (NSs) and thus may make the NS have a pure neutron matter core. This is in contrast to the fact that using the mass $2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$ of PSR J0348+0432 as the NS maximum mass cannot rule out the supersoft high-density $E_{\rm sym}(n)$. Furthermore, we find the stiffer high-density $E_{\rm sym}(n)$ based on the existence of $2.14M_\odot$ NSs leads to a strong constraint of $Λ_{1.4} \ge 348^{+88}_{-51}$ for the dimensionless tidal deformability of the canonical $1.4M_\odot$ NS.

## Key claim

Using the newly measured mass of PSR J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses) together with finite nuclei data and heavy-ion collision flow constraints, the authors show that the supersoft high-density symmetry energy (which becomes negative at suprasaturation densities) can be ruled out, whereas the previously known 2.01 solar mass pulsar PSR J0348+0432 could not exclude such a supersoft behavior.

## Method

The authors employed the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (eSHF) model to simultaneously describe nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron stars. They constructed parameter sets with fixed values of the skewness coefficient J0 and the symmetry energy curvature parameter Ksym, fitting the remaining 10 parameters to 30 experimental data points from finite nuclei (binding energies, charge radii, giant monopole resonance energies, and spin-orbit splittings). They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star maximum masses and tidal deformabilities for each parameter set, imposing constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data on symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses (68.3% credibility interval)
- Using 2.14 solar masses as the maximum NS mass gives lower limits on Ksym of (-183, -157, -123) MeV for L(nc) = (39.5, 47.3, 55.1) MeV respectively
- Using 2.01 solar masses (PSR J0348+0432) gives lower limits on Ksym of (-198, -171, -134) MeV for the same L(nc) values
- The lower bound on the dimensionless tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass NS is Lambda_{1.4} >= 348^{+88}_{-51} when using the 2.14 solar mass constraint
- Using 2.01 solar masses gives Lambda_{1.4} >= 286^{+105}_{-41}
- The upper limit from GW170817 is Lambda_{1.4} <= 580, yielding the combined constraint 348^{+88}_{-51} <= Lambda_{1.4} <= 580
- The largest maximum mass in the eSHF model is (2.30, 2.28, 2.26) solar masses for L(nc) = (39.5, 47.3, 55.1) MeV with J0 = -342 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Zhou and Chen (2019) which showed that the 2.01 solar mass pulsar PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013) could not rule out supersoft symmetry energy. The new measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al., 2019) provides a stronger constraint. The results are consistent with the upper limit on tidal deformability from the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott et al., 2017, 2018) and with microscopic many-body calculations including the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, and variational many-body approaches. The combined constraints on Lambda_{1.4} will inform future neutron star structure calculations and gravitational wave detection.
