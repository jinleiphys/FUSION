# Expansion Aspect of Color Transparency on the Lattice

> The opportunity to observe color transparency (CT) is determined by how rapidly a small-sized hadronic wave packet expands. Here we use SU(2) lattice gauge theory with Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation to investigate the expansion. The wave packet is modeled by a point hadronic source, often used as an interpolating field in lattice calculations. The procedure is to determine the Euclidean time (t), pion channel, Bethe-Salpeter amplitude $Ψ(r,t)$, and then evaluate $b^2(t)=\int d^3 r Ψ(r,t) r^2 sin^2 θΨ_π(r)$. This quantity represents the soft interaction of a small-sized wave packet with a pion. The time dependence of $b^2(t)$ is fit as a superposition of three states, which is found sufficient to reproduce a reduced size wave packet. Using this superposition allows us to make the analytic continuation required to study the wave packet expansion in real time. We find that the matrix elements of the soft interaction $\hat b^2$ between the excited and ground state decrease rapidly with the energy of the excited state.

## Key claim

Using SU(2) lattice gauge theory with Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation, the authors model a small-sized hadronic wave packet via a point hadronic source and find that the matrix elements of the soft interaction operator \(\hat b^2\) between excited and ground states decrease rapidly with the energy of the excited state, which is an important component for color transparency; however, the initial wave packet is not truly small, and its relation to color transparency physics remains unclear.

## Method

The authors use SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a \(12^3 \times 24\) lattice with \(\beta=2.5\) and 60 quenched gauge configurations, employing Wilson fermions at three hopping parameters (\(\kappa=0.146, 0.148, 0.149\)). They model the wave packet by a point hadronic source (pseudoscalar interpolating field) and compute the Euclidean-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude \(\Psi(r,t)\) in the pion channel, from which they evaluate \(b^2(t) = \int d^3 r \Psi(r,t) r^2 \sin^2\theta \Psi_\pi(r)\), representing the soft interaction strength. The time dependence of \(b^2(t)\) and the two-point correlation function \(G_2(t)\) are simultaneously fit as a superposition of three states (ground plus two excited states) using a correlated \(\chi^2\) fit with a full covariance matrix, enabling analytic continuation to Minkowski time to study the wave packet expansion.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.09 \pm 0.012\) fm, determined from the \(\rho\) mass.
- Quark masses for the three hopping parameters: \(m_q = 275, 174, 124\) MeV (using \(\kappa_{cr}=0.151(1)\) extrapolated to \(m_\pi=0\)).
- The expansion time \(\tau_{exp}\) for the wave packet to reach the ground state contribution \(b^2_1\) is \(\approx 0.03\) fm; an upper limit of \(\tau_{exp} \approx 2\) fm is obtained by varying the coefficients \(c_n\).
- The soft interaction matrix element \(b^2_n\) decreases by a factor of 2 with each excited state, and \(b^2_3\) is negative, allowing formation of a reduced size wave packet.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of hadron-hadron interactions (Green, 1994) and color transparency (Brodsky, 1982; Mueller, 1982) by focusing on the nonperturbative expansion of a small-sized wave packet, using a method akin to smearing techniques (Negele, 1990) to isolate meson states. It is the first lattice calculation to extract three states (ground and two excited) and their soft interaction matrix elements, which are crucial for understanding whether color transparency can be observed in high-momentum-transfer reactions like (e,e'p) and (p,pp). The results enable future work using electromagnetic current operators to create more realistic point-like configurations and extending the analysis to baryons.
