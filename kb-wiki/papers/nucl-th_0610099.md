# Saturation properties of nuclear matter in a relativistic mean field model constrained by the quark dynamics

> We have built an effective Walecka-type hadronic Lagrangian in which the hadron masses and the density dependence of the coupling constants are deduced from the quark dynamics using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In order to stabilize nuclear matter an eight-quark term has been included. The parameters of this Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model have been determined using the meson properties in the vacuum but also in the medium through the omega meson mass in nuclei measured by the TAPS collaboration. Realistic properties of nuclear matter have been obtained.

## Key claim

The authors built a Walecka-type hadronic Lagrangian where the density dependence of hadron masses and coupling constants is derived directly from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) quark model, rather than assuming the Brown and Rho scaling, and found that an eight-quark interaction term is essential for stabilizing nuclear matter and obtaining realistic saturation properties.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective hadronic Lagrangian with density-dependent masses and couplings, where the density dependences of the omega and sigma meson masses, the nucleon mass, and the meson-nucleon couplings were calculated from an in-medium NJL model with four- and eight-quark interactions. The NJL model parameters were fitted to the vacuum pion mass (135 MeV), pion decay constant (92.4 MeV), omega meson mass (782 MeV), and the in-medium omega meson mass measured by the TAPS collaboration (722 MeV at 0.6 times saturation density). At the hadronic level, the two free coupling constants g_sigma_NN and g_omega_NN were adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter (binding energy -16 MeV at density 0.17 fm^-3).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 with binding energy E/rho_B - M_N = -16 MeV
- In-medium omega meson mass at 0.6 rho_0: m*_omega = 722 (+4/-4 stat, +35/-5 syst) MeV from TAPS collaboration
- For the central constituent quark mass m = 467 MeV (Lambda = 572 MeV): nucleon effective mass m*_N/M_N = 0.6, incompressibility K = 221 MeV, slope of optical potential U_0/M_N = 0.32
- NJL parameters for m = 467 MeV: m_0 = 5.6 MeV, g_1 = 8.22 GeV^-2, g_2 = 21.4 GeV^-2, g_3 = 1.08 x 10^4 GeV^-8
- Hadronic coupling constants: g_sigma_NN = 6.3, g_omega_NN = 13
- Quark condensate in vacuum: <u_bar u>^1/3 = -240 MeV
- The eight-quark term contributes only 2% of the constituent quark mass at saturation but modifies the rearrangement term by about 40%

## Context

This work extends the approach of Brown, Song, Min and Rho (1990s) by replacing the assumed Brown and Rho scaling with a direct calculation from quark dynamics using an NJL model, similar in spirit to the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon and Saito (1980s-1990s) and the quark-diquark model of Bentz and Thomas (2000s). The model is constrained by recent experimental data from the TAPS collaboration on the in-medium omega meson mass, and it enables a description of nuclear matter saturation properties with only a few free parameters, though the authors note that the nucleon mass dependence on the quark condensate (taken from QCD sum rules of Cohen, 1990s) may need improvement at higher densities.
