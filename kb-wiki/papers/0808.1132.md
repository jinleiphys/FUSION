# Kinetic energy sum spectra in nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei

> We evaluate the coincidence spectra in the nonmesonic weak decay (NMWD) $ΛN\go nN$ of $Λ$ hypernuclei $^{4}_Λ$He, $^{5}_Λ$He, $^{12}_Λ$C, $^{16}_Λ$O, and $^{28}_Λ$Si, as a function of the sum of kinetic energies $E_{nN}=E_n+E_N$ for $N=n,p$. The strangeness-changing transition potential is described by the one-meson-exchange model, with commonly used parameterization. Two versions of the Independent-Particle Shell Model (IPSM) are employed to account for the nuclear structure of the final residual nuclei. They are: (a) IPSM-a, where no correlation, except for the Pauli principle, is taken into account, and (b) IPSM-b, where the highly excited hole states are considered to be quasi-stationary and are described by Breit-Wigner distributions, whose widths are estimated from the experimental data. All $np$ and $nn$ spectra exhibit a series of peaks in the energy interval 110 MeV $<E_{nN}<170$ MeV, one for each occupied shell-model state. The IPSM-a could be a pretty fair approximation for the light $^{4}_Λ$He and $^{5}_Λ$He hypernuclei. For the remaining, heavier, hypernuclei it is very important, however, to take into account the spreading in strength of the deep-hole states, and bring into play the IPSM-b approach. Notwithstanding the nuclear model that is employed the results depend only very weakly on the details of the dynamics involved in the decay process proper. We propose that the IPSM is the appropriate lowest-order approximation for the theoretical calculations of the of kinetic energy sum spectra in the NMWD. It is in comparison to this picture that one should appraise the effects of the final state interactions and of the two-nucleon-induced decay mode.

## Key claim

The kinetic energy sum spectra in nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei depend only very weakly on the details of the decay dynamics itself, and are instead dominated by the recoil effect and the shell structure of the residual nucleus; for heavier hypernuclei beyond the s-shell, the spreading of deep-hole state strength must be included via a phenomenological Breit-Wigner distribution to obtain realistic spectral shapes.

## Method

The authors computed the coincidence spectra S_nN(E) for the ΛN → nN nonmesonic weak decay of ⁴_ΛHe, ⁵_ΛHe, ¹²_ΛC, ¹⁶_ΛO, and ²⁸_ΛSi using the one-meson-exchange model (full OMEP including π, η, K, ρ, ω, K* mesons) for the transition potential. Two versions of the Independent-Particle Shell Model were employed: IPSM-a, which includes only the Pauli principle and the recoil effect, and IPSM-b, which additionally treats highly excited hole states as quasi-stationary with Breit-Wigner strength functions whose widths were estimated from experimental (p,2p) and (e,e'p) knockout data.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy sum spectra cover the energy interval 110 MeV < E_nN < 170 MeV for all hypernuclei studied.
- The recoil effect alone produces peak widths ranging from approximately 4 MeV for ²⁸_ΛSi to approximately 40 MeV for ⁴_ΛHe.
- For ¹²_ΛC, the 1s_1/2 hole state width used in IPSM-b is γ = 9 MeV; for ¹⁶_ΛO, γ(1s_1/2) = 14 MeV and γ(1p_3/2) = 3 MeV; for ²⁸_ΛSi, γ(1s_1/2) = 16 MeV and γ(1p_3/2) = γ(1p_1/2) = 5 MeV.
- The decay rates for ¹²_ΛC with full OMEP in IPSM-a are Γ_n = 0.249 and Γ_p = 0.956 (in units of Γ_Λ⁰ = 2.50 × 10⁻⁶ eV), giving Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.260; IPSM-b gives nearly identical values (Γ_n = 0.246, Γ_p = 0.947, ratio 0.260).
- The Q-value for the decay is Δ = M_Λ − M = 176 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Garbarino, Parreño, and Ramos (2003-2005), who calculated pair distributions for ⁵_ΛHe and ¹²_ΛC using a shell model with intranuclear cascade for final state interactions, and of Bauer (2007), who used a nuclear matter formalism. The paper provides a systematic lowest-order reference calculation (IPSM) against which the effects of final state interactions and two-nucleon-induced decay can be appraised, and it explains why the longstanding Γ_n/Γ_p puzzle has been resolved by recent coincidence measurements from KEK-E462 and other experiments, which yield ratios around 0.4-0.5 for both ⁵_ΛHe and ¹²_ΛC.
