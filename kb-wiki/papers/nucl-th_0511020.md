# Wilsonian RG and Redundant Operators in Nonrelativistic Effective Field Theory

> In a Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) analysis, redundant operators, which may be eliminated by using field redefinitions, emerge naturally. It is therefore important to include them. We consider a nonrelativisitic effective theory (the so-called ``pionless'' Nuclear Effective Field Theory) as a concrete example and show that the off-shell amplitudes cannot be renormalized if the redundant operators are not included. The relation between the theories with and without such redundant operators is established in the low-energy expansion. We perform a Wilsonian RG analysis for the \textit{off-shell} scattering amplitude in the theory with the redundant operator.

## Key claim

The off-shell two-nucleon scattering amplitude in pionless Nuclear Effective Field Theory cannot be renormalized unless a "redundant" operator proportional to the equation of motion is included in the Lagrangian; the Jacobian from the field redefinition that eliminates this operator is nontrivial and must be accounted for in a Wilsonian renormalization group analysis.

## Method

The authors consider a nonrelativistic effective field theory of spinless nucleons with contact interactions (pionless EFT). They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the off-shell two-particle scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame, expanding the amplitude up to quadratic terms in the relative momenta. They include a redundant operator proportional to the equation of motion (the "B-interaction") and derive the equivalence relation between theories with and without this operator by demanding off-shell proportionality of the denominators. They then perform a Wilsonian RG analysis by demanding cutoff independence of the inverse amplitude expanded in momenta and energy, obtaining RG equations for the dimensionless coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The nontrivial fixed point of the RG equations occurs at dimensionless couplings \((\gamma_0^*, \gamma_2^*, \beta^*) = (-1, -1/2, 1/2)\), corresponding to infinite scattering length.
- The linearized RG equations around the fixed point yield eigenvalues: 2, 1, and -1, with the negative eigenvalue indicating one relevant combination of operators.
- The renormalized off-shell amplitude near the nontrivial fixed point is given by \(\left.{{\cal A}'}^{-1}(p^0, \bfp_1,\bfp_2)\right|_* = -\frac{M\Lambda_0}{2\pi^2}c - \frac{Mb}{\pi^2\Lambda_0}\mu^2 - \frac{M\mu}{4\pi} + \cdots\), where \(\mu = \sqrt{-Mp^0 - i\epsilon}\) and \(\Lambda_0\) is a mass parameter.

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian RG approach to nuclear effective field theory pioneered by Birse and collaborators (Birse 1998, Barford 2002), who considered RG equations for energy- and momentum-dependent potentials. The authors address the off-shell ambiguities inherent in potential approaches, which are problematic for three-nucleon systems. The results enable a fully field-theoretic Wilsonian RG treatment of nuclear EFT, with the RG flow determining the phase structure of the nuclear force and identifying the inverse scattering length as an order parameter characterizing the relevant direction near the nontrivial fixed point.
