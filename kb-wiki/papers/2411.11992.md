# Non-linear dynamics of jet quenching

> We develop a comprehensive analytic framework for jet quenching in QCD media, based on a medium-induced parton cascade sourced by collinear virtual splittings. We show that the energy flow out of the jet cone, driven by turbulent gluon cascades, is governed by a non-linear rate equation that resums gluon splittings at arbitrary angles and is enhanced by the medium length, $L$. The solution of this equation sets the initial condition for a non-linear DGLAP-like evolution equation, which describes the collinear early vacuum cascade resolved by the medium at angles exceeding the medium resolution angle, $θ_c$. For asymptotic jet energies, the medium-induced cascade displays an exponential behavior that generalizes the Poisson-like distribution of parton energy loss. This formulation enables the resummation of leading contributions in $α_s \ln (1/R)$, and $α_s \ln (R / θ_c)$, and powers of $α_s L$. We briefly explore the limit of strong quenching, where analytic treatments are feasible, offering insights into the impact of parton cascades on jet quenching. These results provide guidance for future numerical simulations and analytical investigations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a comprehensive analytic framework for jet quenching that unifies medium-induced parton cascades with collinear vacuum evolution, showing that the energy flow out of the jet cone is governed by a non-linear rate equation that resums gluon splittings at arbitrary angles and is enhanced by the medium length L, with the caveat that this formulation applies in the regime where the medium length and scattering center density are sufficiently large for multiple emissions to become significant.

## Method

The authors derive a non-linear master equation for the energy loss distribution of a gluon propagating through a QCD medium, expressed in terms of a Laplace-transformed quenching weight. This equation resums medium-induced gluon splittings using the inelastic splitting rate derived from the harmonic oscillator approximation, combined with elastic broadening described by a diffusion equation with the jet quenching parameter. The solution of this medium-induced cascade then serves as the initial condition for a non-linear DGLAP-like evolution equation that describes the collinear vacuum cascade resolved by the medium at angles exceeding the medium resolution angle θ_c.

## Context

This work extends the Baier-Dokshifter-Mueller-Schiff (BDMS) approach (Baier 2001) by incorporating non-linear effects from multiple gluon branchings that can radiate back into the jet cone, analogous to the non-global logarithms studied in vacuum by Dasgupta (2001) and Banfi (2002). It builds upon the kinetic theory description of medium-induced cascades developed by Blaizot (2013, 2014) and the factorization formula for inclusive jet cross-sections derived by Mehtar-Tani (2024). The framework enables systematic resummation of leading contributions in α_s ln(1/R), α_s ln(R/θ_c), and powers of α_s L, providing guidance for future numerical simulations and analytical investigations of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
