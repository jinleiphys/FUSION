# Unequal rapidity correlators in the dilute limit of JIMWLK

> We study unequal rapidity correlators in the stochastic Langevin picture of Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leonidov-Kovner (JIMWLK) evolution in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. We discuss a diagrammatic interpretation of the long-range correlators. By separately evolving the Wilson lines in the direct and complex conjugate amplitudes, we use the formalism to study two-particle production at large rapidity separations. We show that the evolution between the rapidities of the two produced particles can be expressed as a linear equation, even in the full nonlinear limit. We also show how the Langevin formalism for two-particle correlations reduces to a BFKL picture in the dilute limit and in momentum space, providing an interpretation of BFKL evolution as a stochastic process for color charges.

## Key claim

The authors show that the evolution between the rapidities of two produced particles in the JIMWLK formalism can be expressed as a linear equation even in the full nonlinear limit, and that in the dilute limit the Langevin formalism for two-particle correlations reduces to a BFKL picture, providing an interpretation of BFKL evolution as a stochastic process for color charges.

## Method

The authors start from the bilocal Langevin description of JIMWLK evolution developed by Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos (2013), which encodes correlations between Wilson lines at two different rapidities. They take the dilute limit of this formalism by expanding the Wilson lines to linear order in the color field (small-gluon-field limit), and show explicitly how the evolution equation reduces to a linear iterative equation for the color charge density. They then demonstrate that this linearized evolution recovers the BFKL equation for the unintegrated gluon distribution, with the BFKL Green's function separating the two produced particles.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos (2013) for bilocal Langevin evolution of Wilson lines at different rapidities, which itself built on earlier work by Kovner, Liao, and McLerran (2006-2007). The paper provides a diagrammatic interpretation connecting the JIMWLK Langevin picture to BFKL dynamics, which is relevant for understanding two-particle correlations at large rapidity separations in proton-nucleus collisions. This enables future phenomenological calculations of multi-particle correlations in the Color Glass Condensate framework, particularly for the "single ladder" contribution in dilute-dense scattering where the theoretical understanding was previously less developed.
