# Thermalization at RHIC

> Ideal hydroynamics provides an excellent description of all aspects of the single-particle spectra of all hadrons with transverse momenta below about 1.5-2 GeV/c at RHIC. This is shown to require rapid local thermalization at a time scale below 1 fm/c and at energy densities which exceed the critical value for color deconfinement by an order of magnitude. The only known thermalized state at such energy densities is the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The rapid thermalization indicates that the QGP is a strongly interacting liquid rather than the weakly interacting gas of quarks and gluons that was previously expected.

## Key claim

Ideal hydrodynamics provides an excellent description of all hadron spectra below about 1.5-2 GeV/c at RHIC, but this agreement requires rapid local thermalization at a time scale below 1 fm/c and at energy densities exceeding the critical value for color deconfinement by an order of magnitude, indicating that the quark-gluon plasma is a strongly interacting liquid rather than a weakly interacting gas.

## Method

The author solved the relativistic equations for ideal hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using an equation of state constructed by matching a free ideal quark-gluon gas above T_cr = 165 MeV to a realistic hadron resonance gas below T_cr via Maxwell construction with a latent heat of 1.15 GeV/fm^3. Initial conditions were fixed by fitting total charged multiplicity dN_ch/dy and pion and proton spectra at midrapidity in central (b=0) collisions, using a Glauber model with 25% hard and 75% soft contributions to initial entropy production. The freeze-out prescription used the Cooper-Frye algorithm with decoupling energy density e_dec = 0.075 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time required: tau_eq ≤ 0.6 fm/c (upper limit used in calculations)
- Initial central energy density: e_0 = 30 GeV/fm^3 ≈ 30 e_cr, where e_cr ≈ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Initial central temperature: T_0 = 360 MeV ≈ 2 T_cr (T_cr = 165 MeV)
- Initial central entropy density: s_0 = 110 fm^{-3} at tau_eq = 0.6 fm/c
- Average initial energy density: ⟨e⟩ = 13 GeV/fm^3, an order of magnitude above e_cr = 0.6-1 GeV/fm^3
- Decoupling temperature: T_dec = 100 MeV in chemical non-equilibrium case (versus 130 MeV in chemical equilibrium case)
- For b ≈ 7 fm Au+Au collisions, v_2/epsilon_x is about 75% of the hydrodynamic value, yielding upper limit Delta_tau ≈ 2 fm/c
- For more central collisions, upper limit Delta_tau ≈ 1 fm/c
- Baryon elliptic flow breaks from hydrodynamics at p_perp ≈ 2.3-2.5 GeV/c, meson elliptic flow at p_perp ≈ 1.5 GeV/c
- Hydrodynamics describes more than 99% of all produced hadrons

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously applied at SPS energies (NA49 Collaboration, 2002) to RHIC energies, showing that the ideal hydrodynamic limit is saturated for central and semi-central Au+Au collisions. The analysis builds on earlier hydrodynamic calculations by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (2000) and Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak (2001), and contradicts expectations from parton cascade simulations (Molnar and Gyulassy, 2001) which required an artificial 80-fold increase in partonic opacity to match the data. The results enable experimental extraction of the quark-gluon plasma shear viscosity through systematic comparisons between elliptic flow data and viscous hydrodynamics, with upper limits on eta/s indicating the sQGP is less viscous than liquid helium below the superfluid transition.
