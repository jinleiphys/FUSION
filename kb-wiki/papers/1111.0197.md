# Unified description of hadron-photon and hadron-meson scattering in the Dyson-Schwinger approach

> We derive the expression for the nonperturbative coupling of a hadron to two external currents from its underlying structure in QCD. Microscopically, the action of each current is resolved to a coupling with dressed quarks. The Lorentz structure of the currents is arbitrary and thereby allows to describe the hadron's interaction with photons as well as mesons in the same framework. We analyze the ingredients of the resulting four-point functions and explore their potential to describe a variety of processes relevant in experiments. Possible applications include Compton scattering, the study of two-photon effects in hadron form factors, pion photo- and electroproduction on a nucleon, nucleon-pion or pion-pion scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general, nonperturbative expression for the coupling of a hadron to two external currents from its underlying quark-gluon substructure in QCD, using the gauging-of-equations method; the formalism is valid for arbitrary interaction kernels and does not rely on specific truncations, though practical implementations may employ a rainbow-ladder approximation.

## Method

The authors apply the gauging-of-equations method to the n-quark scattering matrix T, gauging it twice to obtain the four-point function T^{μν} whose pole residues define hadron scattering amplitudes. They express the resulting scattering amplitude J^{μν} in terms of dressed quark propagators, the n-quark kernel K and its gauged counterpart K^μ, and hadron bound-state amplitudes obtained from Bethe-Salpeter or Faddeev equations. The derivation is carried out for general kernels and arbitrary Lorentz structure of the external currents, allowing simultaneous treatment of photon and meson interactions.

## Key numbers

* The pion amplitude depends on 4 covariant basis elements, while a nucleon amplitude includes 64 basis elements (Dirac structure and relative momenta).
* The bound-state condition is λ_i(P² = -M_i²) = 1, where λ_i are eigenvalues of the kernel-propagator product K G_0.
* The canonical normalization condition is \overline{Ψ} (T^{-1})' Ψ = 1, evaluated at the hadron mass shell.

## Context

This work extends the gauging-of-equations method, previously used for hadron form factors (Oettel 1999, Eichmann 2011), to two-current processes such as Compton scattering and hadron-meson scattering. It generalizes the approach of Bicudo (2001) and Cotanch (2002) for ππ scattering from rainbow-ladder truncation to arbitrary interaction kernels and to baryon scattering. The framework enables unified microscopic descriptions of processes including Nγ* → Nγ*, Nπ → Nπ, Nγ* → Nπ, and ππ scattering within QCD's Dyson-Schwinger and bound-state equations.
