# A Combined $ν_μ\to ν_e$ and $\barν_μ\to \barν_e$ Oscillation Analysis of the MiniBooNE Excesses

> The MiniBooNE experiment at Fermilab reports results from an analysis of the combined $ν_e$ and $\bar ν_e$ appearance data from $6.46 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target in neutrino mode and $11.27 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target in antineutrino mode. A total excess of $240.3 \pm 34.5 \pm 52.6$ events ($3.8 σ$) is observed from combining the two data sets in the energy range $200<E_ν^{QE}<1250$ MeV. In a combined fit for CP-conserving $ν_μ\rightarrow ν_e$ and $\barν_μ\rightarrow\barν_e$ oscillations via a two-neutrino model, the background-only fit has a $χ^2$-probability of 0.03% relative to the best oscillation fit. The data are consistent with neutrino oscillations in the $0.01 < Δm^2 < 1.0$ eV$^2$ range and with the evidence for antineutrino oscillations from the Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector (LSND).

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE Collaboration reports a combined excess of neutrino and antineutrino events consistent with two-neutrino oscillations in the 0.01 to 1.0 eV² Δm² range, but the analysis assumes no significant ν_μ, ν̄_μ, ν_e, or ν̄_e disappearance and does not fully account for nuclear effects on carbon, which may affect the fitted oscillation parameters by up to ~20%.

## Method

The experiment used 8 GeV protons from the Fermilab Booster on a beryllium target with a magnetic focusing horn to produce neutrino and antineutrino beams, detected 541 m downstream in a 806-ton mineral oil Cherenkov detector. The analysis performed a combined fit for CP-conserving ν_μ → ν_e and ν̄_μ → ν̄_e oscillations via a two-neutrino model over the full energy range 200 < E_ν^QE < 3000 MeV, using a likelihood-ratio technique with frequentist corrections and a covariance matrix incorporating correlated and uncorrelated systematic errors between neutrino and antineutrino data.

## Key numbers

- Total excess of 240.3 ± 62.9 events (3.8 σ) from combined neutrino and antineutrino data in the energy range 200 < E_ν^QE < 1250 MeV
- Neutrino mode: 6.46 × 10²⁰ protons on target; antineutrino mode: 11.27 × 10²⁰ protons on target
- Neutrino mode excess: 162.0 ± 47.8 events (952 data vs. 790.0 ± 28.1 ± 38.7 background)
- Antineutrino mode excess: 78.4 ± 28.5 events (478 data vs. 399.6 ± 20.0 ± 20.3 background)
- Best two-neutrino oscillation fit at (Δm², sin²2θ) = (0.037 eV², 1.00) for combined data
- Separate best fits: neutrino mode at (3.14 eV², 0.002); antineutrino mode at (0.05 eV², 0.842)
- Background-only fit χ²-probability relative to best oscillation fit: 0.03% for combined data
- Best 3+2 oscillation fit parameters: Δm²₄₁ = 0.082 eV², Δm²₅₁ = 0.476 eV², |U_e4|²|U_μ4|² = 0.1844, |U_e5|²|U_μ5|² = 0.00547, φ = 1.0005π

## Context

This analysis extends the MiniBooNE results previously reported in Aguilar-Arevalo et al. (2007, 2009) by combining neutrino and antineutrino data with approximately twice the antineutrino statistics, using a consistent oscillation model for both modes. The data are consistent with the antineutrino oscillation evidence from the LSND experiment (Athanassopoulos et al., 1996) and with the limits from the KARMEN experiment, supporting the interpretation of short-baseline anomalies in terms of sterile neutrino oscillations; the 3+2 oscillation fit with three additional parameters will be discussed in a future publication.
