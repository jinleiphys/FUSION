# Instanton-dyon Ensemble with two Dynamical Quarks: the Chiral Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim

The chiral symmetry breaking pattern in an ensemble of instanton-dyons with two light dynamical quark flavors is demonstrated through Dirac eigenvalue spectra that show clear chiral symmetry breaking at high dyon density, but the precise interrelation between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking transitions remains to be determined within current accuracy.

## Method

The authors perform numerical simulations of 64 and 128 instanton-dyons in SU(2) gauge theory, including the fermionic determinant factor (det T)^(N_f) with N_f=2 in the partition function. The Dirac eigenvalue spectra at small values are derived from the ensemble, and the chiral condensate is determined using two methods: fitting eigenvalue distributions with random matrix theory formulas (accounting for finite volume effects) and measuring the gap width in the eigenvalue distribution near zero. The free energy is minimized over a three-dimensional parameter space (holonomy, system radius, and number of M-type dyons) using 19 runs with 4000 update cycles each, with the last 1000 cycles used for data collection.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed with 64 and 128 dyons at the same density, with the volume of the 128-dyon sphere being 2 times larger than the 64-dyon sphere
- For the finite volume scaling analysis at S=8 with dyon densities n_M=0.33 and n_L=0.20: Sigma_2 values of 1.30 +/- 0.06 (64 dyons) and 1.28 +/- 0.06 (128 dyons); Sigma_1 values of 0.79 +/- 0.05 (64 dyons) and 0.51 +/- 0.04 (128 dyons)
- The combined chiral condensate Sigma = 0.38 +/- 0.13 from the finite volume scaling formula
- Input parameters scanned: holonomy nu from 0.175 to 0.525 (step 0.025), radius r from 1.05 to 2.00 (step 0.05), N_M from 16 to 26 (step 2), Debye mass M_d from 3 to 6 (step 1), action S from 5 to 9.5 (step 0.5)
- 67200 different parameter combinations evaluated, with 1344000 total runs including lambda integration
- Quark mass of 0.01 used in some simulations to study mass effects on eigenvalue distributions

## Context

This paper is the second in a series following Larsen and Shuryak (2015) on pure gauge SU(2) theory, extending the instanton-dyon ensemble to include dynamical quarks. It builds on the work of Faccioli and Shuryak (2013) who studied 1, 2, and 4 flavor theories with SU(2) color and found chiral symmetry breaking in the first two cases with N_f=4 appearing marginal. The results connect to the analytic mean field theory of Liu, Shuryak and Zahed (2015), who found that the quark condensate satisfies a universal gap equation with non-zero solutions for N_f < 2 N_c. The work enables future determination of the chiral symmetry breaking temperature and its relation to the deconfinement transition temperature in the instanton-dyon framework, with ongoing work to solve the Dirac equation for streamline configurations to improve the hopping matrix element calculation.
