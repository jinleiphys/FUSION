# Multiboson Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlations for partially coherent sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a multiphase transport model

> We use a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to study multi-pion and multi-kaon Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations for the partially coherent particle-emitting sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A density-dependent longitudinal coherent emission length and density-dependent transverse coherent emission length are introduced in calculating the multi-boson HBT correlation functions of the partially coherent sources. We compare the model results of three- and four-pion HBT correlation functions with experimental data in Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$2.76 TeV, and investigate the influences of boson coherent emissions on the multi-pion and multi-kaon correlation functions, respectively. We find that all of the three- and four-pion correlation functions of the partially coherent sources are consistent with experimental data. Coherent emission leads to the intercept decreases of the multi-boson correlation functions. The intercepts of the multi-kaon correlation functions of the partially coherent source are higher than those of the multi-pion correlation functions, because low kaon densities lead to smaller kaon coherent emission lengths than pion emission lengths. The intercepts of multi-boson correlation functions of partially coherent sources in high transverse momentum intervals are higher than those in low transverse momentum intervals because particle de Broglie wavelengths are small at high momenta.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three- and four-pion HBT correlation functions from partially coherent sources constructed in the AMPT model are consistent with experimental data from ALICE (2016) for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but only when the coherent emission lengths are made proportional to both the boson de Broglie wavelengths and the boson densities at generation; the abstract does not emphasize that the proportionality coefficients were tuned to match the pion data and then assumed to be universal for kaons.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model to generate events for Pb-Pb central collisions (impact parameter 0-3.5 fm, centrality 0-5%) at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They introduced density-dependent longitudinal and transverse coherent emission lengths, proportional to the boson de Broglie wavelengths multiplied by the boson densities (longitudinal density M_b/<r_Z> and transverse density M_b/(pi<r_T>^2)), and set the correlator amplitude f(p_i-p_j,X) to zero when both the longitudinal and transverse coordinate differences of a boson pair were smaller than these coherent lengths. They then computed the three- and four-boson correlation functions C_m, c_3, c_4, a_4, and b_4 for pions and kaons using the AMPT freeze-out coordinates and momenta, with the parameters a'_Z=0.0031 fm and a'_T=0.662 fm^2 determined by fitting the pion correlation functions to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

* Pion longitudinal density <D_Z> = 248.840 fm^{-1} and transverse density <D_T> = 3.703 fm^{-2} in the interval 0<k_T<1.0 GeV/c for Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV.
* Kaon longitudinal density <D_Z> = 70.287 fm^{-1} and transverse density <D_T> = 1.017 fm^{-2} in the same momentum interval.
* Longitudinal parameter a'_Z = 0.0031 fm and transverse parameter a'_T = 0.662 fm^2, determined by comparing model results with ALICE three- and four-pion data.
* Three-pion intercept lambda_3 = 0.851 in low K_T3 interval (0.16-0.3 GeV/c) and 0.934 in high K_T3 interval (0.3-1.0 GeV/c) for partially coherent sources.
* Four-pion intercept lambda_4 = 2.049 in low K_T4 interval and 2.335 in high K_T4 interval.
* Three-kaon intercept lambda_3 = 1.409 in low K_T3 interval (0.16-0.4 GeV/c) and 1.740 in high K_T3 interval (0.4-1.0 GeV/c).
* Four-kaon intercept lambda_4 = 5.720 in low K_T4 interval and 5.751 in high K_T4 interval.
* Equivalent coherent fraction for three-pion CF_3 = 0.550 (low K_T3) and 0.522 (high K_T3); for four-pion CF_4 = 0.481 (low K_T4) and 0.451 (high K_T4).
* Equivalent coherent fraction for three-kaon CF_3 = 0.360 (low K_T3) and 0.226 (high K_T3); for four-kaon CF_4 = 0.094 (low K_T4) and 0.088 (high K_T4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of two-pion HBT correlations for partially coherent sources in the AMPT model (Wang, Ye, Zhang, 2024) to three- and four-boson correlations, and introduces density-dependent coherent emission lengths that go beyond the earlier momentum-dependent formulation. The results are compared with ALICE experimental data (2016) for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and the approach enables extraction of equivalent coherent fractions for both pions and kaons, showing that kaon sources have lower coherence than pion sources due to lower kaon densities. The method provides a framework for interpreting multi-boson HBT correlations as a probe of source coherence in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that the equivalent coherent fractions from three- and four-boson correlations do not agree with each other, indicating that the AMPT partially coherent sources differ from the static Gaussian model used in earlier analytic work (Liu, 1986).
