# Charm production in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energy

> We study charm production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$2.76 TeV in the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics transport approach and the charm dynamics in the partonic and hadronic medium. The charm quarks are produced through initial binary nucleon-nucleon collisions by using the PYTHIA event generator taking into account the (anti-)shadowing incorporated in the EPS09 package. The produced charm quarks interact with off-shell massive partons in the quark-gluon plasma and are hadronized into $D$ mesons through coalescence or fragmentation close to the critical energy density, and then interact with hadrons in the final hadronic stage with scattering cross sections calculated in an effective Lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry. The PHSD results show a reasonable $R_{\rm AA}$ and elliptic flow of $D$ mesons in comparison to the experimental data for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV from the ALICE Collaboration. We also study the effect of temperature-dependent off-shell charm quarks in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that the scattering cross sections are only moderately affected by off-shell charm degrees of freedom. However, the position of the peak of $R_{\rm AA}$ for $D$ mesons depends on the strength of the scalar partonic forces which also have an impact on the $D$ meson elliptic flow. The comparison with experimental data on the $R_{\rm AA}$ suggests that the repulsive force is weaker for off-shell charm quarks as compared to that for light quarks. Furthermore, the effects from radiative charm energy loss appear to be low compared to the collisional energy loss up to transverse momenta of $\sim$ 15 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach reproduces the measured $R_{\rm AA}$ and elliptic flow of $D$ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, but the position of the $R_{\rm AA}$ peak depends on the strength of scalar partonic forces, and the repulsive force is weaker for off-shell charm quarks than for light quarks.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, generating initial charm quarks via PYTHIA with EPS09 (anti-)shadowing corrections. Charm quarks interacted with off-shell massive partons in the quark-gluon plasma, hadronized into $D$ mesons via coalescence or fragmentation near the critical energy density, and then underwent hadronic interactions with cross sections from an effective Lagrangian with heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Charm production is suppressed by approximately 30% in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions and by approximately 20% in 30-50% central collisions due to (anti-)shadowing at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.
- The differential cross section of off-shell charm is at most 20-25% larger than that of charm with constant mass (1.5 GeV) in backward scattering at $T = 0.2$ GeV.
- The spatial diffusion constant $D_s$ for off-shell charm is about 10% larger than that of charm with constant mass.
- Radiative charm energy loss effects appear low compared to collisional energy loss up to transverse momenta of approximately 15 GeV/c.
- The coalescence probability of mid-rapidity charm decreases with increasing transverse momentum and is slightly smaller at $p_T = 0$ compared to Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHSD study of charm production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV (Song 2015) to LHC energies, and compares with experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration. The results are consistent with shadowing calculations by Cao (2015) using the same EPS09 package, and the spatial diffusion constants agree with lattice QCD calculations by Banerjee (2011) above $T_c$. The approach enables systematic studies of heavy-flavor dynamics across a wide range of collision energies and tests the validity of the PHSD framework for describing charm production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
