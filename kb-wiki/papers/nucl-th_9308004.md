# Nucleon Flow and Fragment Flow in Heavy Ion Reactions

> The collective flow of nucleons and that of fragments in the 12C + 12C reaction below 150 MeV/nucleon are calculated with the antisymmetrized version of molecular dynamics combined with the statistical decay calculation. Density dependent Gogny force is used as the effective interaction. The calculated balance energy is about 100 MeV/nucleon, which is close to the observed value. Below the balance energy, the absolute value of the fragment flow is larger than that of nucleon flow, which is also in accordance with data. The dependence of the flow on the stochastic collision cross section and its origin are discussed. All the results are naturally understood by introducing the concept of two components of flow: the flow of dynamically emitted nucleons and the flow of the nuclear matter which contributes to both the flow of fragments and the flow of nucleons due to the statistical decay.

## Key claim

The collective flow of nucleons and fragments in the 12C+12C reaction below 150 MeV/nucleon can be understood by separating it into two components: the flow of dynamically emitted nucleons (produced by stochastic collisions, with small absolute flow) and the flow of nuclear matter (which contributes to fragment flow and to nucleon flow from statistical decay, with large absolute flow). The calculated balance energy of about 100 MeV/nucleon is close to the observed value, but the balance energy of alpha particles appears larger than that of nucleons.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized version of molecular dynamics (AMD) with a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets, incorporating stochastic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-alpha collision processes. The density-dependent Gogny force (incompressibility K=228 MeV) was used as the effective interaction. Simulations were run until 150 fm/c, after which statistical decay of excited fragments was calculated using a code similar to Pühlhofer's. The flow was defined as the mean in-plane transverse momentum per nucleon, with the sign determined by the longitudinal momentum direction.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy E_bal = (100 ± 20) MeV/nucleon for the 12C+12C reaction, compared to the experimentally deduced value of (122 ± 12) MeV/nucleon
- Gogny force incompressibility K = 228 MeV
- Width parameter ν = 0.16 fm^{-2} for the Gaussian wave packets
- Impact parameter fixed at b = 2 fm for all flow calculations
- Incident energy range studied: 45 MeV/nucleon ≤ E ≤ 150 MeV/nucleon
- Statistical error in evaluation of 12C ground state energy with 100A test particles: about 2 MeV
- Multiplicity of dynamically emitted nucleons at 45 MeV/nucleon: 2.00 (0.5σ), 3.52 (1.0σ), and 4.95 (1.5σ) where σ is the stochastic collision cross section

## Context

This work extends the AMD framework (previously applied near Fermi energies by Ono, Horiuchi, and Maruyama in earlier papers) to higher incident energies around and above 100 MeV/nucleon. It contrasts with Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) approaches by using a finite-range effective interaction (Gogny force) that automatically includes momentum dependence, whereas the Volkov force (used in earlier AMD work) failed to reproduce the balance energy. The results enable future determination of the nuclear equation of state by comparing fragment momentum distributions with data, and by testing effective interactions with different incompressibilities.
