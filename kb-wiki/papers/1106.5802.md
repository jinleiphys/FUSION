# The flow of heavy flavor in hydrodynamics

> The flow of charm is calculated in 2+1 ideal hydrodynamics by introducing the charge of $c\bar{c}$ pair assuming that the number of $c\bar{c}$ pairs is conserved in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is found that the mean radial flow velocity of charm quarks is smaller than that of bulk matter by 10$\sim$15 \% and the measured $v_2$ of heavy-flavor electrons is reproduced up to $p_T^e=$ 1.5 GeV/c in Au+Au collision at RHIC. The same flow is applied to regenerated $J/ψ$ and its $v_2$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The mean radial flow velocity of charm quarks is smaller than that of bulk matter by 10–15%, and the measured elliptic flow ($v_2$) of heavy-flavor electrons is reproduced only up to $p_T^e = 1.5$ GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, implying that heavy flavor is considerably thermalized only at low transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 ideal hydrodynamic simulations with a first-order phase transition between a quasiparticle-model QGP and a resonance-gas hadron gas, using initial conditions from a Glauber model at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c. They introduced a conserved charge density for $c\bar{c}$ pairs (assuming pair number conservation) and solved its transport equation alongside the energy-momentum conservation equations using the HLLE algorithm. The elliptic flow of heavy-flavor electrons was obtained by generating D mesons according to the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula at $T_c = 170$ MeV, weighting by the local charm fugacity, and then simulating their semileptonic decays.

## Key numbers

- Mean radial flow velocity of charm quarks is smaller than that of bulk matter by 10–15% at $b = 9$ fm in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- The $v_2$ of heavy-flavor electrons from hydrodynamics reproduces experimental data up to $p_T^e = 1.5$ GeV/c at $b = 5.1$ fm in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- The initial thermalization time is $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c; the critical temperature for the phase transition is $T_c = 170$ MeV.
- The lifetimes of QGP, mixed, and hadron gas phases at $b = 9$ fm are 4.6, 5.4, and 10.1 fm/c respectively.
- Entropy in the simulation is conserved within $\pm 4\%$ error.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions to heavy flavor by introducing a conserved $c\bar{c}$ pair charge, building on prior hydrodynamic studies of bulk matter by Heinz (2004) and Song (2009). It contrasts with earlier approaches that assumed the flow velocity of heavy flavor was the same as bulk matter or parameterized it arbitrarily, as in Zhao (2007), Akkelin (2009), and Song (2010). The results enable more realistic calculations of regenerated $J/\psi$ elliptic flow and provide a baseline for comparing with Langevin-based treatments of heavy-quark transport, such as those by Moore (2004) and van Hees (2005).
