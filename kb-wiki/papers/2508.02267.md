# Tribute to Toshimitsu Yamazaki (1934-2025): Quest for Exotic Hadronic Matter

> In this talk I pay tribute to Toshimitsu Yamazaki who died earlier this year. Yamazaki's leading contributions to Hadronic Physics, in particular to Strangeness Nuclear Physics in Japan and elsewhere, are well known. Two of the five Recurring Themes of his research, as listed in the Japan Academy site, are highlighted here: (i) Discovery of deeply bound pionic-atom states, and (ii) Search for kaonic nuclei -- Kaonic Proton Matter (KPM). I conclude by reviewing briefly my own recent work, confirming Farrar's conjecture that a deeply bound $H$ dibaryon is not ruled out by the weak-decay observation of $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei. However, the relatively long lifetime of such a deeply bound $H$ is much too short to qualify it for a Dark-Matter candidate.

# Tribute to Toshimitsu Yamazaki (1934-2025): Quest for Exotic Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The author confirms Farrar's conjecture that a deeply bound H dibaryon is not ruled out by the weak-decay observation of ΛΛ hypernuclei, but finds that the resulting H lifetime (order 10^5 s) is much too short to qualify it as a Dark-Matter candidate, contrary to earlier claims of order 10^8 yr.

## Method

The author reviews Yamazaki's two highlighted research themes: (i) the discovery of deeply bound pionic-atom states via the (d,^3He) reaction at GSI, and (ii) the search for kaonic nuclei including Kaonic Proton Matter (KPM). For the H dibaryon analysis, the author treats ^6_{ΛΛ}He in a Λ-Λ-^4He 3-body model and estimates the ΔS=2 H→nn weak-interaction decay rate using a Leading-Order Effective-Field-Theory approach constrained by Λ hypernuclear ΔS=1 nonmesonic weak-decay rates.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of K^-pp from J-PARC E15: B_{K^-pp}=42±3^{+3}_{-4} MeV, width Γ_{K^-pp}=100±7^{+19}_{-9} MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term extracted from global pionic atom fits: σ_{πN}=57±7 MeV (Friedman and Gal, 2019)
- Binding energy of ΛΛ pair in ^6_{ΛΛ}He: 6.91±0.17 MeV
- LQCD calculation finds H dibaryon bound by 4.6±1.3 MeV relative to ΛΛ threshold (at m_π~400 MeV)
- Chiral extrapolation suggests H becomes unbound by 13±14 MeV relative to ΛΛ threshold at physical quark masses
- H dibaryon lifetime from ΔS=2 weak decay: order 10^5 s (for m_H satisfying 2m_n ≲ m_H < m_n+m_Λ)
- Farrar's earlier claim for H lifetime: order 10^8 yr
- Fermi weak-interaction constant used: G_F m_π^2 = 2.21×10^{-7}

## Context

This tribute paper reviews Yamazaki's pioneering work on deeply bound pionic atoms (Toki and Yamazaki, 1988; Yamazaki, Hayano, et al., 1996) and kaonic nuclei (Yamazaki and Akaishi, 2002), which built on earlier theoretical work by Friedman and Soff (1985) and Nogami (1963). The H dibaryon analysis extends the author's recent work (Gal, 2024) confirming Farrar's conjecture (2003) that hypernuclear physics does not rule out a deeply bound H dibaryon, while contradicting Farrar's later claim (Farrar and Zaharijas, 2004) that such an object could be a Dark-Matter candidate. The result agrees in order of magnitude with Donoghue, Golowich, and Holstein (1986). The work enables future experimental searches for the H dibaryon in the mass range below m_Λ+m_n, and clarifies the stability limits of multi-strange hadronic matter.
