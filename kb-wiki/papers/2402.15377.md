# Worldline Monte Carlo method for few body nuclear physics

> In this work we introduce a worldline based fermion Monte Carlo algorithm for studying few body quantum mechanics of self-interacting fermions in the Hamiltonian lattice formulation. Our motivation to construct the method comes from our interest in studying renormalization of chiral nuclear effective field theory with lattice regularization. In particular we wish to apply our method to compute the lattice spacing dependence of local lattice interactions as we take the continuum limit of the lattice theory. Our algorithm can compute matrix elements of the operator $\exp(-βH)$ where $H$ is the lattice Hamiltonian and $β$ is a free real parameter. These elements help us compute deep bound states that are well separated from scattering states even at values of $β$ which are not very large. Computing these bound state energies accurately can help us study renormalization of the lattice theory. In addition to developing the algorithm, in this work we also introduce a finite volume renormalization scheme for the lattice Hamiltonian of the leading pionless effective field theory and show how it would work in the one and two body sectors.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a worldline-based fermion Monte Carlo algorithm for few-body nuclear physics that can compute deep bound state energies from matrix elements of \(\exp(-\beta H)\) without solving the fermion sign problem, relying instead on the observation that the sign problem is mild in the few-body sector on large lattices.

## Method

The authors formulate a lattice Hamiltonian for leading-order pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\nopi}\)) on a periodic cubic spatial lattice, with nucleon hopping and contact interactions (spin-singlet, spin-triplet, and three-body terms). They compute matrix elements of the transfer matrix \(\exp(-\beta H)\) using a worldline Monte Carlo algorithm that treats fermions as hard-core bosons and includes the fermion permutation sign in observables. The lattice parameters are fixed by matching four low-energy levels (single-nucleon, dineutron, deuteron, and triton ground states) from lattice QCD calculations in a finite volume, and the renormalization of the two-body couplings is performed analytically using the pole of the Green's function.

## Context

This work extends the worldline fermion Monte Carlo approach (Wiese, 1992; PhysRevD.99.074511) to few-body nuclear physics, aiming to study the renormalization of chiral nuclear effective field theory with lattice regularization. It contrasts with constrained path Monte Carlo methods (Zhang, 1996) and exact fermion methods based on fermion determinants, which suffer from sign problems or poor scaling near criticality. The method is designed to enable continuum extrapolations of lattice nuclear calculations, such as those by Lee (2004–2021) and Epelbaum (2009–2020), which are typically performed at a single lattice spacing; understanding the lattice-spacing dependence is necessary to connect lattice results to continuum effective field theory.
