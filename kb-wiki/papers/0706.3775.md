# How particles emerge from decaying classical fields in heavy ion collisions: towards a kinetic description of the Glasma

> We develop the formalism discussed previously in hep-ph/0601209 and hep-ph/0605246 to construct a kinetic theory that provides insight into the earliest ``Glasma'' stage of a high energy heavy ion collision. Particles produced from the decay of classical fields in the Glasma obey a Boltzmann equation whose novel features include an inhomogeneous source term and new contributions to the collision term. We discuss the power counting associated with the different terms in the Boltzmann equation and outline the transition from the field dominated regime to the particle dominated regime in high energy heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Boltzmann-type kinetic equation for particles emerging from decaying classical fields in the Glasma stage of heavy ion collisions, but only under the condition that the system is first averaged over the stochastic ensemble of color sources (the external currents) and that a gradient expansion is valid for those ensemble-averaged quantities. The resulting equation contains an inhomogeneous source term and novel contributions to the collision term that are absent in the conventional Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors start from a scalar \(\phi^3\) field theory coupled to a strong time-dependent external source \(j\), mimicking the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework for heavy ion collisions. They write Dyson-Schwinger equations for the two-point functions in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, separating the connected and disconnected (product of field expectation values) parts. They then introduce an ensemble of particles via modified free propagators containing a distribution function \(f(X,\mathbf{p})\), perform a Wigner transform, and apply a gradient expansion to leading order in \(\partial_X\). The ensemble average over the stochastic sources \(j\) is performed using a Gaussian distribution (the McLerran-Venugopalan model), which justifies the gradient expansion for averaged quantities. Finally, a quasi-particle ansatz is used to convert the Dyson-Schwinger equations into a Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum scale at RHIC energies is of order \(Q_s \sim 1\)–\(2\) GeV; this sets the typical momentum of "hard" particles.
* The inverse nuclear radius for a large nucleus is \(\sim R_A^{-1} \sim 40\) MeV, which is the scale over which ensemble-averaged quantities change appreciably, justifying the gradient expansion.
* In the McLerran-Venugopalan model, the two-point correlator of color sources is \(\langle \rho(\mathbf{x}_\perp) \rho(\mathbf{y}_\perp) \rangle = \mu_A^2(\mathbf{x}_\perp) \delta(\mathbf{x}_\perp - \mathbf{y}_\perp)\), where \(\mu_A^2\) is the density of color charges.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by the same authors in earlier papers (Gelis, 2006a and Gelis, 2006b) for multi-particle production in field theories with strong time-dependent sources. It builds on prior work by Mueller and Son (2004) and Jeon (2005), who derived kinetic equations from a classical approximation in the path integral and a gradient expansion of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The paper aims to describe the transition from the field-dominated regime (early times after a heavy ion collision) to the particle-dominated regime (where kinetic theory applies), specifically for the Glasma stage. This enables a connection between the CGC framework and later hydrodynamic descriptions of the Quark Gluon Plasma.
