# Description of Chiral Doublets in $A\sim130$ Nuclei and the Possible Chiral Doublets in $A\sim100$ Nuclei

> The chiral doublets for nuclei in $A\sim100$ and $A\sim130$ regions have been studied with the particle-rotor model. The experimental spectra of chiral partners bands for four N=75 isotones in $A\sim130$ region have been well reproduced by the calculation with the configuration $πh_{11/2}\otimesνh_{11/2}^{-1}$. The possible chiral doublets in $A\sim100$ region have been predicted by the PRM model with the configuration $πg_{9/2}\otimesνg_{9/2}^{-1}$ based on the analysis of the spectra, the $ω$-I relation, the $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, and the structure of the wave functions. The possible chiral doublets for nuclei with asymmetric particle and hole configuration are also discussed.

# Description of Chiral Doublets in A~130 Nuclei and the Possible Chiral Doublets in A~100 Nuclei

## Key claim
The particle-rotor model (PRM) can reproduce the experimental chiral partner bands for four N=75 isotones in the A~130 region, and predicts that chiral doublets should exist in the A~100 region for a specific γ-deformation interval between -35° and -25°, with the best condition at γ = -30°, provided the configuration is πg₉/₂ ⊗ νg₉/₂⁻¹ and the deformation is sufficiently large.

## Method
The authors used a particle-rotor model (PRM) coupling a single proton particle and a single neutron hole to a triaxially deformed collective rotor. For the A~130 nuclei, the configuration πh₁₁/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂⁻¹ was adopted with a constant moment of inertia of 25 MeV⁻¹; for the A~100 nuclei, the configuration πg₉/₂ ⊗ νg₉/₂⁻¹ was used with a moment of inertia of 30 MeV⁻¹ and γ = -30°. The calculated energy spectra, ω-I relations, B(M1) and B(E2) transition probabilities, and wave function structures were analyzed to identify chiral doublet signatures.

## Key numbers
- Four N=75 isotones in A~130 region were studied: ¹³⁰Cs, ¹³²La, ¹³⁴Pr, ¹³⁶Pm, with configuration πh₁₁/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂⁻¹ and rotor moment of inertia J = 25 MeV⁻¹.
- For ¹³⁰Cs, the energy displacement between chiral bands is ≈0.22 MeV in data and ≈0.25 MeV in calculation for spin interval 11ħ < I < 15ħ.
- For A~100 nuclei with configuration πg₉/₂ ⊗ νg₉/₂⁻¹, calculations used J = 30 MeV⁻¹, γ = -30°, and coupling constants C = 0.1 MeV, 0.2 MeV, and 0.25 MeV, corresponding to deformations β ≈ 0.06, 0.12, and 0.15 respectively.
- Near degeneracy of the two lowest bands in A~100 appears around I = 12ħ for C = 0.1 MeV, in the interval 11ħ < I < 13ħ for C = 0.2 MeV, and in the interval 11ħ < I < 15ħ for C = 0.25 MeV.
- Chiral doublets appear in the γ-deformation interval -35° < γ < -25°, with best condition at γ = -30°.
- For ¹⁰⁴Rh with asymmetric configuration πg₉/₂⁻¹ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂, near degeneracy occurs in the spin interval 11ħ < I < 16ħ for both γ = -25° and γ = -30°.

## Context
This work extends the chiral symmetry breaking predictions of Meng (1997) and the experimental observations of chiral twin bands in N=75 isotones reported by Starosta (2001) and Koike (2001). The study reproduces the experimental spectra of ¹³⁰Cs, ¹³²La, ¹³⁴Pr, and ¹³⁶Pm using the PRM, consistent with earlier calculations by Starosta (2002). It then predicts new chiral doublet candidates in the A~100 region with the πg₉/₂ ⊗ νg₉/₂⁻¹ configuration, and discusses asymmetric particle-hole configurations motivated by Koike's report on ¹⁰⁴Rh. These predictions provide guidance for experimental searches for chiral doublets in the A~100 mass region.
