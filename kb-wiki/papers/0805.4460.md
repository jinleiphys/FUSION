# Hidden-charm scalar tetra-quark mesons

> Decays of hidden-charm scalar tetra-quark mesons as the partners of D_{s0}(2317) which has successfully been assigned to the iso-triplet scalar tetra-quark [c,n][sbar,nbar]_{I=1} meson are studied. Because OZI-rule allowed strong decays are kinematically limited, their radiative decays are expected to be important.

## Key claim

The hidden-charm scalar tetra-quark mesons studied here are expected to have only one OZI-rule allowed strong two-body decay (\(\hat\delta^c \rightarrow \eta_c\pi\)), with all other such decays kinematically forbidden or suppressed, making radiative decays the primary discovery channels; this conclusion depends on the crude mass estimates being "not very far from the true ones."

## Method

The author extends a previous assignment of \(D_{s0}(2317)\) as an iso-triplet tetra-quark \([cn][\bar s\bar n]_{I=1}\) meson to hidden-charm scalar \([cq][\bar c\bar q]\) states. Masses are estimated via naive quark counting using \(\Delta_{cs} \simeq 1\) GeV and \(\Delta_{sn} \simeq 100\) MeV, with \(m_{D_{s0}(2317)} = 2317\) MeV as input. Decay rates for the OZI-allowed \(\hat\delta^c \rightarrow \eta_c\pi\) are computed using a hard-pion approximation and asymptotic \(SU_f(4)\) symmetry, while radiative decay rates are estimated under the vector meson dominance (VMD) hypothesis using \(SU_f(4)\) relations among \(SVV'\) couplings.

## Key numbers

- Estimated mass of \(\hat\delta^c\) (I=1, S=0): \(\sim 3.3\) GeV
- Estimated mass of \(\hat\kappa^c\) (I=1/2, S=1): \(\sim 3.4\) GeV
- Estimated mass of \(\hat\sigma^c\) (I=0, S=0): \(\sim 3.3\) GeV
- Estimated mass of \(\hat\sigma^{cs}\) (I=0, S=0): \(\sim 3.5\) GeV
- Threshold for \(\eta_c\pi\): 3.12 GeV; for \(\eta_c K\): 3.48 GeV; for \(\eta_c\eta\): 3.53 GeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\delta^c \rightarrow \eta_c\pi\): \(5 - 10\) MeV (from \(SU_f(4)\) symmetry)
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\kappa^c \rightarrow K^*\gamma\): \(\sim 0.23\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\delta^c \rightarrow \rho\gamma\): \(\sim 0.22\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\delta^c \rightarrow \psi\gamma\): \(\sim 0.069\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\sigma^c \rightarrow \omega\gamma\): \(\sim 0.22\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\sigma^c \rightarrow \psi\gamma\): \(\sim 0.007\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\sigma^{sc} \rightarrow \phi\gamma\): \(\sim 0.24\) MeV
- Predicted rate for \(\hat\sigma^{sc} \rightarrow \psi\gamma\): \(\sim 0.040\) MeV
- Input asymptotic matrix element \(|\langle D_s^+ | A_{\pi^0} | \hat F_I^+ \rangle|_{SU_f(4)} \sim 0.09 - 0.13\)
- Input \(\Gamma(\hat F_I^+ \rightarrow D_s^+ \pi^0)_{SU_f(4)} \simeq 5 - 10\) MeV
- Input \(\Gamma(\phi \rightarrow a_0(980)\gamma)_{\rm exp} = 0.32 \pm 0.03\) keV
- Input \(\gamma V\) couplings: \(X_\rho = 0.033 \pm 0.003\), \(X_\omega = 0.011 \pm 0.001\), \(X_\phi = -0.018 \pm 0.004\), \(X_\psi = 0.051 \pm 0.012\)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous assignment of \(D_{s0}(2317)\) as an iso-triplet tetra-quark meson (Terasaki, 2007) and the tetra-quark interpretation of \(X(3872)\) (Terasaki, 2007) to the hidden-charm scalar sector. It contrasts with predictions from a unitarized chiral model (Oset et al., 2006) for radiative decays, because that model omits non-loop contributions like \(\hat\sigma^c \rightarrow \psi\omega \rightarrow \gamma\omega\) which are dominant here. The results enable experimental searches for these states via the \(\gamma\gamma \rightarrow \eta_c\pi\) reaction, where two peaks are predicted: a narrower one around 3.3 GeV from the \(\bf{\bar 3_c}\times\bf{3_c}\) state and a broader one at higher energy from the \(\bf{6_c}\times\bf{\bar 6_c}\) state.
