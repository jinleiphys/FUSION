# Theoretical study of projectile fragmentation in the reactions $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn at 1 GeV/nucleon

> We analyze the production cross sections and isotopic distributions of projectile-like residues in the reactions $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn at an incident beam energy of 1 GeV/nucleon measured with the FRS fragment separator at the GSI laboratory. Calculations within the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for an ensemble of excited sources were performed with ensemble parameters determined previously for similar reactions at 600 MeV/nucleon. The obtained good agreement with the experiment establishes the universal properties of the excited spectator systems produced during the dynamical stage of the reaction. It is furthermore confirmed that a significant reduction of the symmetry-energy term at the freeze-out stage of reduced density and high temperature is necessary to reproduce the experimental isotope distributions. A trend of decreasing symmetry energy for large neutron-rich fragments of low excitation energy is interpreted as a nuclear-structure effect.

## Key claim

The statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with ensemble parameters previously determined for reactions at 600 MeV/nucleon successfully reproduces the projectile fragmentation data for 1 GeV/nucleon reactions, establishing universal properties of excited spectator systems; however, the agreement is only qualitative for absolute cross sections, and the analysis focuses on relative isotope distributions rather than absolute yields because the FRS data may underestimate intermediate-mass fragment yields due to acceptance and anisotropy effects.

## Method

The authors performed SMM calculations for an ensemble of excited sources, using the same ensemble parameters (source mass, excitation energy, and N/Z distributions) that were previously determined from ALADIN data at 600 MeV/nucleon. The calculations included secondary de-excitation via evaporation, fission, and Fermi breakup, with a linear interpolation between liquid-drop masses (with variable symmetry-energy coefficient gamma) and standard ground-state masses for excitation energies below 1 MeV/nucleon. The model results were globally normalized to experimental cross sections in the atomic number interval 20 ≤ Z ≤ 25, and the symmetry-energy coefficient gamma was varied to reproduce the measured isotopic distributions for different element groups.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factors of 0.00334 mb and 0.00344 mb per theoretical event for 124Sn and 112Sn projectiles, respectively, obtained by matching calculated to measured elemental cross sections in the interval 20 ≤ Z ≤ 25
- Symmetry-energy coefficient gamma = 16 MeV for Z = 10-17 fragments from both 112Sn and 124Sn projectiles
- Symmetry-energy coefficient gamma = 25 MeV for Z = 38-45 fragments from 112Sn, but only gamma = 18 MeV for the same Z range from 124Sn
- Symmetry-energy coefficient gamma = 14 MeV best reproduces light fragments with Z = 12, while gamma = 25 MeV is more appropriate for Z = 44
- Precision of gamma determination is approximately Δγ = ±1 MeV for both projectile cases
- Effective symmetry-energy coefficient gamma_eff = 17.4 MeV for A = 30 and gamma_eff = 18.0 MeV for A = 36, calculated from the Myers and Swiatecki mass formula, compared to the fitted gamma = 16 MeV for Z = 10-17
- Cross section of 19 ± 4 mb reported for 22Ne production from 124Sn at 1 GeV/nucleon, compared to approximately 40 mb for the same reaction at 600 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the SMM analysis previously applied to ALADIN data at 600 MeV/nucleon (Ogul 2011) to new FRS data at 1 GeV/nucleon (Fohr 2011), confirming the universal properties of excited spectator systems. The results confirm the need for a reduced symmetry-energy coefficient gamma for fragments from the multifragmentation regime, consistent with earlier findings from LeFevre (2005), Ogul (2011), Souliotis (2007), and Hudan (2009), and extend this observation to heavier fragments up to Z ≈ 45. The study enables further investigation of nuclear properties at subnuclear densities relevant to astrophysical environments such as supernova explosions, where the symmetry energy affects electron capture and neutrino-induced reaction rates (Botvina 2010).
