# Charting the future frontier(s) of particle production

> This short note describes the long collaborative effort between Arizona and Kraków, showing some of the key strangeness signatures of quark-gluon plasma. It further presents an annotated catalog of foundational questions defining the research frontiers which I believe can be addressed in the foreseeable future in the context of relativistic heavy ion collision experiments. The list includes topics that are specific to the field, and ventures towards the known-to-be-unknown that may have a better chance with ions as compared to elementary interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several foundational questions about quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and particle production remain open, and that the "K$^+/\pi^+$ horn" may signal the onset of deconfinement, the critical point, or a rapid change in nuclear matter properties, the abstract does not specify that the interpretation of this key observable is still ambiguous.

## Method

The paper reviews the long-standing Arizona–Kraków collaboration on strangeness signatures of QGP, focusing on strange antibaryon enhancement and the K$^+/\pi^+$ ratio as functions of collision energy and system size. It then catalogs open questions about QGP formation, the absence of rapidity scaling (transparency) in heavy-ion collisions, matter–antimatter asymmetry, and the origin of entropy, mass, and flavor, drawing on experimental results from SPS, RHIC, and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Strange antibaryon enhancement in heavy-ion collisions relative to $pp$ and $p$Be is as large as a factor of 20, the largest medium effect in heavy-ion collisions.
- Charged particle multiplicity at central rapidity in A–A collisions rises as $(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}})^{0.310(8)}$, while in $pp$, $p$A, and $d$A it rises as $(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}})^{0.206(4)}$ (ALICE collaboration).
- The QGP lifetime in laboratory collisions is $\tau = 3 \times 10^{-23}$ s, based on a distance scale $R = 10$ fm and relativistic particle speed.
- The early Universe QGP era began at about 10 ns after the big bang and lasted until about 18 $\mu$s.
- The baryo-chemical potential $\mu_\mathrm{B}$ in the early Universe is a fraction of an eV; at LHC it is thought to be a fraction of an MeV or larger.
- LHC experiments operate at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, 25 times the RHIC energy maximum.

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing collaboration between Arizona and Kraków on strangeness as a QGP signature, building on earlier work by Rafelski (1996) and Bialas (1998) on strange antibaryon enhancement. It directly addresses the unresolved interpretation of the K$^+/\pi^+$ horn discovered by Gazdzicki (2010) and the failure of the McLerran–Bjorken transparency model (Anishetty 1980, Bjorken 1982) to describe RHIC and LHC rapidity distributions, as shown by PHOBOS (Back 2002, Alver 2007) and ALICE (Adam 2015). The paper enables future searches for baryon number violation, the origin of entropy, and the nature of flavor in QGP experiments.
