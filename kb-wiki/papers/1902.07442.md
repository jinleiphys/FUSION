# Green's function method for the spin and pseudospin symmetries in the single-particle resonant states

> We investigate the spin and pseudospin symmetry in the single-particle resonant states by solving the Dirac equation containing a Woods-Saxon potential with Green's function method. Taking double-magic nucleus $^{208}$Pb as an example, three spin doublets $3d$, $2h$, and $1j$ and three pseudospin doublets $3\tilde{p}$, $1\tilde{i}$, and $1\tilde{j}$ are obtained for the single-neutron resonant states. By analyzing the energy splittings, we find that the threshold effect plays an important role in resonant pseudospin doubles. Besides, there is a reversed level structure of pseudospin doublets in the continuum. Differently, all the width splittings of either the spin doublets or the pseudospin doublets are systematically positive and the splittings are very small except $1\tilde{j}$ doublet. Further studies show that the splittings of the energies and widths for the resonant (pseudo)spin doublets are independent. Besides, the similarity properties of the wave functions of the spin and pseudospin doublets still maintain well in resonant states.

## Key claim

The spin and pseudospin symmetries in single-particle resonant states are studied using Green's function method, with the finding that the threshold effect plays an important role in resonant pseudospin doublets and that there is a reversed level structure of pseudospin doublets in the continuum, while the width splittings for both spin and pseudospin doublets remain systematically positive.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation containing a Woods-Saxon potential using Green's function method in coordinate space, taking the double-magic nucleus 208Pb as an example. The density of states is calculated from the imaginary part of the Green's function, from which the energies and widths of single-neutron resonant states are extracted. The mean-field potentials are given in Woods-Saxon form with parameters Sigma0 = -66 MeV, Delta0 = 650 MeV, R = 7 fm, and a = 0.6 fm, solved with a mesh step of 0.1 fm and a box cutoff of 20 fm.

## Key numbers

- Three spin doublets (3d, 2h, 1j) and three pseudospin doublets (3p-tilde, 1i-tilde, 1j-tilde) are obtained for single-neutron resonant states in 208Pb
- The 3d3/2 resonant state has energy 0.704 MeV and width 0.358 MeV; the 3d5/2 state has energy 0.221 MeV and width 0.108 MeV
- The 2h9/2 state has energy 7.533 MeV and width 2.520 MeV; the 2h11/2 state has energy 6.261 MeV and width 1.630 MeV
- The 1j13/2 state has energy 6.926 MeV and width 0.100 MeV; the 1j15/2 state has energy 0.648 MeV and width 0.010 MeV
- The 2i13/2 state has energy 14.343 MeV and width 5.200 MeV; the 1k15/2 state has energy 16.629 MeV and width 1.978 MeV
- The reduced SO width splittings for all three spin doublets are less than 0.1 MeV
- The pseudospin doublet 1j-tilde shows a relatively larger width splitting, with the 2i13/2 width of 5.20 MeV and the 1k15/2 width of 1.98 MeV
- For the spin doublet 1j, Delta E_SO = 0.419 MeV while Delta Gamma_SO = 0.006 MeV; for the pseudospin doublet 1i-tilde, Delta E_PSO = -0.051 MeV while Delta Gamma_PSO = 0.118 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of pseudospin symmetry in single-particle resonant states by Zhang et al. (2004), who confirmed similarity properties of lower components of Dirac wave functions for resonant pseudospin doublets, and by Guo et al. (2005, 2006), who studied the dependence of pseudospin breaking on the shape of the mean-field potential. The Green's function method used here was previously developed by the same group for continuum covariant density functional theory (Sun et al., 2014) and allows treating bound states and continuum on the same footing. This work enables future studies of spin and pseudospin symmetries in exotic nuclei near the drip line where resonant states play essential roles.
