# Three-nucleon portrait with pion

> We report on recent results obtained by the above collaboration on the collision processes involving three nucleons, where we pay particular attention on the dynamical role of the pion. After discussing the case at intermediate energies, where real pions can be produced and detected, we have considered the case at lower energies, where the pions being exchanged are virtual. The study has revealed the presence of some new pion-exchange mechanisms, which leads to a new three-nucleon force of tensor structure. Recently, the effect of this tensor three-nucleon force to the spin observables for neutron-deuteron scattering at low energy has been analyzed, and will be briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a new three-nucleon force of tensor structure, generated by explicit treatment of one-pion dynamics in the four-body pion-three-nucleon system, survives at the 30% level after accounting for meson-retardation effects that would otherwise cancel the contribution in conventional approaches.

## Method

The authors generalized the rigorous four-body Grassberger-Sandhas-Yakubovsky theory to the one-pion three-nucleon system and derived a perturbative approximation scheme. To lowest order, this scheme reproduces standard Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon-force diagrams but also yields additional irreducible diagrams, including one where a two-nucleon rescattering occurs while the pion is in flight, leading to a tensor three-nucleon force. The effect of this tensor force was then calculated for neutron-deuteron scattering at low energy using the Paris two-nucleon potential (PEST parameterization).

## Key numbers

- The new tensor three-nucleon force survives as a 30% effect after cancellation between irreducible two-pion exchange diagrams and meson-retardation effects of reducible Born diagrams.
- The subtracted t-matrix (the relevant quantity for the new force) was calculated at E = -150 MeV and for a fixed momentum p' of 0.089 fm^{-1}, shown for the CD-Bonn potential.
- The analyzing power A_y for nd scattering was calculated at 3 MeV (lab energy), comparing results with and without the tensor three-nucleon force.

## Context

This work extends the standard three-nucleon force approach (e.g., Tucson-Melbourne, 1970s-1980s) which successfully addressed the underbinding of the three-nucleon bound state but failed to explain the A_y puzzle in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering. The new tensor three-nucleon force derived from explicit pion dynamics offers a potential resolution to this long-standing puzzle.
