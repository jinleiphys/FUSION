# Bayesian estimation of thermonuclear reaction rates for deuterium+deuterium reactions

> The study of d+d reactions is of major interest since their reaction rates affect the predicted abundances of D, 3He, and 7Li. In particular, recent measurements of primordial D/H ratios call for reduced uncertainties in the theoretical abundances predicted by big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). Different authors have studied reactions involved in BBN by incorporating new experimental data and a careful treatment of systematic and probabilistic uncertainties. To analyze the experimental data, Coc et al. (2015) used results of ab initio models for the theoretical calculation of the energy dependence of S-factors in conjunction with traditional statistical methods based on Chi-2 minimization. Bayesian methods have now spread to many scientific fields and provide numerous advantages in data analysis. Astrophysical S-factors and reaction rates using Bayesian statistics were calculated by Iliadis et al. (2016). Here we present a similar analysis for two d+d reactions, d(d,n)3He and d(d,p)3H, that has been translated into a total decrease of the predicted D/H value by 0.16%.

## Key claim

The Bayesian reanalysis of the d(d,n)³He and d(d,p)³H reactions yields a 0.16% decrease in the predicted primordial D/H abundance ratio, but this update is considered premature because the total uncertainty on D/H remains unchanged at 2.0% and new measurements of the d(p,γ)³He reaction are awaited before a final prediction can be made.

## Method

The authors applied Bayesian statistics with Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithms using the JAGS software to analyze experimental S-factor data from five data sets for each of the two d+d reactions. They adopted the ab initio theoretical S-factor energy dependence from Arai (2011) as a template, fitting an overall scale factor and five individual normalization factors (one per data set) to account for systematic uncertainties, using only data below 0.6 MeV center-of-mass energy where the theoretical model is valid. The reaction rates were then computed by numerical integration of the Bayesian posterior S-factor distributions over 60 temperatures from 1 MK to 10 GK.

## Key numbers

- For d(d,n)³He: scale factor = 0.961 ± 0.010 (68% credible interval); S(0) = 51.70 (+0.54, -0.51) keV·b
- For d(d,p)³H: scale factor = 0.956 (+0.010, -0.011) (68% credible interval); S(0) = 53.26 (+0.55, -0.59) keV·b
- Rate factor uncertainty (f.u.) = 1.011 (i.e., 1.1%) for both reactions at most temperatures
- The new rates produce a total decrease of the predicted D/H value by 0.16%, with the total uncertainty unchanged at 2.0%
- The Bayesian scale factors are larger than the previous Coc (2015) values by 0.21% for d(d,n)³He and 0.12% for d(d,p)³H

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian methodology previously applied by Iliadis (2016) to the d(p,γ)³He reaction, now treating the two d+d reactions that are critical for big bang nucleosynthesis predictions of D, ³He, and ⁷Li. The results are compared directly to the traditional χ² minimization analysis of Coc (2015), showing agreement within 1% at most temperatures but revealing a 15% discrepancy at very low temperatures (near 1 MK) due to an overly high low-energy cutoff in the previous numerical integration. The improved rates enable more precise BBN predictions, but the authors caution that a full D/H update must await new experimental data from LUNA on the d(p,γ)³He reaction and completion of other investigations into weak rate corrections and numerical methods.
