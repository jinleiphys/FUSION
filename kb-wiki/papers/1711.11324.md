# Examining nonextensive statistics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show in detailed numerical solutions of the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation (FPE) which has been associated with nonextensive q-statistics that the available data on rapidity distributions for stopping in relativistic heavy-ion collisions cannot be reproduced with any permitted value of the nonextensivity parameter (1 < q < 1.5). This casts doubt on the nonextensivity concept that is widely used in relativistic heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim

The authors show through detailed numerical solutions of the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation associated with nonextensive q-statistics that rapidity distributions for stopping in relativistic heavy-ion collisions cannot be reproduced with any permitted value of the nonextensivity parameter (1 < q < 1.5), contradicting earlier claims by Lavagno (2002) and Alberico (2008, 2009) that used approximate solutions.

## Method

The authors solved the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation with a sinh-drift term (required by the Boltzmann equilibrium distribution) and a nonlinear diffusion term with exponent (2-q), using three independent numerical schemes: MATLAB's parabolic-elliptic PDE solver and two finite-element-method frameworks (DUNE and FEniCS). They used Gaussian initial conditions centered at the beam rapidities with width sigma = 0.1 corresponding to Fermi motion, and compared the numerical solutions to net-proton (stopping) data from NA49 for PbPb at 17.2 GeV and BRAHMS for AuAu at 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV, the dimensionless diffusion strength from the fluctuation-dissipation relation is gamma = 0.137, while the value required to fit the data is gamma = 1.5, a factor of 11 larger.
- For AuAu at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the diffusion strength from the fluctuation-dissipation relation is gamma = 0.12, while the value required to fit the data is gamma = 8, a factor of about 67 larger.
- The temperature used in calculations was 160 MeV (critical value for hadron-quark-gluon plasma transition), with transverse mass m_perp = 1.17 GeV for PbPb at 17.2 GeV.
- The relative difference between the three numerical solution schemes was around 1 percent, concentrated at the peaks.
- Fitted drift and diffusion coefficients for the linear (q=1) model: for PbPb at 17.2 GeV, A = 4.06 x 10^24 per second and D = 6.67 x 10^24 per second; for AuAu at 200 GeV, A = 5.17 x 10^24 per second and D = 53.4 x 10^24 per second.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the findings of Lavagno (2002) and Alberico (2008, 2009), who claimed that nonextensive q-statistics could fit stopping data at SPS and RHIC energies using approximate solutions of the nonlinear Fokker-Planck equation that started from the stationary solution form. The authors show that when solving the equation with realistic physical initial conditions (delta-function-like Gaussians at beam rapidities), the nonlinearity from q-statistics cannot account for the observed broadening, which instead requires an adjusted diffusion coefficient in the linear model to account for both nonequilibrium thermal broadening and collective expansion, as previously done by Wolschin (1999) and Friman, G. (2017). The result casts doubt on the nonextensivity concept widely used in relativistic heavy-ion physics, particularly for rapidity distributions, though the authors note that q-statistics may still describe transverse momentum distributions.
