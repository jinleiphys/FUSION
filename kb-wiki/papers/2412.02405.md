# Constraint on initial conditions of one-dimensional expanding fluids from nonlinear causality

> The initial conditions of one-dimensional expanding viscous fluids in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are scrutinized in terms of nonlinear causality of the relativistic hydrodynamic equations. Conventionally, it is believed that the matter generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions starts to behave as a fluid all at once at some initial time. However, it is by no means trivial how soon after the first contact of two high-energy nuclei the fluid picture can be applied. It is demonstrated that one-dimensional expanding viscous fluids violate the necessary and the sufficient conditions of nonlinear causality at large departures from local equilibrium. We therefore quantify the inverse Reynolds number to justify the hydrodynamic description to be valid. The initial conditions are strictly constrained not to violate the causality conditions during the time evolution. With the help of the transverse energies per rapidity measured at RHIC and LHC, we obtain the minimum initial proper time and the maximum energy density allowed by nonlinear causality. This analysis strongly suggests that the initial stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions needs to be described by a non-equilibrium description other than the framework of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that one-dimensional expanding viscous fluids violate the necessary and sufficient conditions of nonlinear causality at large departures from local equilibrium, and therefore the initial conditions for relativistic hydrodynamic simulations must be strictly constrained to avoid acausal evolution. The abstract's claim that "the initial stage needs to be described by a non-equilibrium framework" is hedged by the fact that the analysis is restricted to one-dimensional expanding systems with the BRSSS constitutive equation and specific transport coefficients from AdS/CFT.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled equations of energy-momentum conservation and the Baier-Romatschke-Son-Starinets-Stephanov (BRSSS) second-order constitutive equation for a one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) expanding fluid, using both a conformal equation of state (p = e/3) and a lattice QCD parametrization from HotQCD (2014). They translate the general nonlinear causality conditions from Bemfica et al. (2021) into inequalities involving the energy density, pressure, shear pressure, sound velocity, and transport coefficients, then numerically evolve the system from various initial conditions to determine which trajectories satisfy or violate these causality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Necessary condition for causality: -0.47 ≤ φ/(e+p) ≤ 0.23 (conformal EoS with AdS/CFT transport coefficients)
- Sufficient condition for causality: -0.07 ≤ φ/(e+p) ≤ 0.07 (conformal EoS with AdS/CFT transport coefficients)
- Minimum initial proper time normalized by relaxation time: τ₀,min/τπ ≈ 5.3
- Minimum initial proper time formula: τ₀,min ≈ 1.6 × E₀^(-1/3) fm, where E₀ = e₀τ₀ in GeV/fm²
- Bjorken energy density from data: e₀τ₀ ≈ 5 GeV/fm² for top 5% Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV (STAR 2004)
- Bjorken energy density from data: e₀τ₀ ≈ 14 GeV/fm² for top 5% Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (CMS 2012)

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear causality analysis of Bemfica et al. (2021) and the earlier linear causality study of Hiscock (1983) to one-dimensional expanding fluids relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It provides quantitative constraints on hydrodynamic initial conditions that complement Bayesian parameter estimation approaches (Bernhard 2016, 2019; Nijs 2020; JETSCAPE 2020) by adding a theoretical causality requirement. The results enable future studies to exclude acausal initial conditions in hydrodynamic simulations and motivate development of non-equilibrium descriptions for the pre-hydrodynamic stage, such as kinetic theory or holographic approaches.
