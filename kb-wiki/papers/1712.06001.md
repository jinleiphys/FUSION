# Status of the Theoretical Calculation of Nuclear Electric Dipole Moment

> The electric dipole moment is a very sensitive probe of CP violation beyond the standard model. Light nuclei are particularly interesting since the CP violation may be enhanced by nuclear many-body effects. In this proceeding, we present the current status of the theoretical evaluations of the electric dipole moment of light nuclei in the ab initio approach and in the cluster model. We add the preliminary result of the evaluation of the electric dipole moment of $^7$Li which is treated in a cluster model with a triton.

## Key claim

The paper presents the current status of theoretical evaluations of electric dipole moments (EDMs) of light nuclei using ab initio and cluster-model approaches, with the preliminary result for \(^7\)Li showing that its EDM coefficient depends on the isovector CP-odd coupling \(\bar G_\pi^{(1)}\) as \(1.6 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, but this result is preliminary and the overall pattern suggests each light nucleus has its own EDM generation mechanism requiring independent evaluation.

## Method

The nuclear EDM due to polarization from the one-pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force was calculated. For the deuteron, \(^3\)He, and \(^3\)H, the ab initio approach was used with the Av18 potential. For \(^6\)Li, \(^9\)Be, \(^{13}\)C, and \(^7\)Li, cluster models were employed: the \(\alpha\)-cluster model for the first three and the \(\alpha\)-\(^3\)H model for \(^7\)Li, with the CP-odd potential between clusters obtained by folding the bare CP-odd \(N\)-\(N\) interaction.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron EDM coefficient for isovector coupling: \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.45 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^3\)He EDM coefficients: \(a_\pi^{(0)} = 0.59 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.08 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(2)} = 1.68 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^3\)H EDM coefficients: \(a_\pi^{(0)} = -0.59 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.08 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(2)} = -1.70 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^6\)Li EDM coefficient for isovector coupling: \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 2.2 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^7\)Li EDM coefficients (preliminary): \(a_\pi^{(0)} = -0.6 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.6 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(2)} = -1.7 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^9\)Be EDM coefficient for isovector coupling: \(a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.4 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- \(^{13}\)C EDM coefficient for isovector coupling: \(a_\pi^{(1)} = -0.20 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- Neutron EDM coefficients for comparison: \(a_\pi^{(0)} = 1 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm, \(a_\pi^{(2)} = -1 \times 10^{-2} e\) fm
- Oscillator parameters for cluster folding: \(b = 1.358\) fm for CP-odd \(\alpha\)-\(N\) potential, \(b = 1.482\) fm for CP-odd \(\alpha\)-\(^3\)H potential

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of nuclear EDMs by Liu and Bsaisou (2014) and the author's own prior work (Yamanaka, 2015) to include cluster-model results for \(^6\)Li, \(^9\)Be, \(^{13}\)C, and the new preliminary result for \(^7\)Li. The results for \(^6\)Li, \(^7\)Li, and \(^9\)Be suggest an additive counting rule involving cluster EDMs and the CP-odd \(\alpha\)-\(N\) interaction with a contribution of \((0.005 - 0.007) \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\) fm, but the suppressed EDM of \(^{13}\)C (due to poor overlap between opposite-parity states, as noted by Yamada, 2010) shows this rule is not universal. The work enables future experimental searches for nuclear EDMs using storage rings, as discussed by Anastassopoulos and the JEDI collaboration (2015), by providing theoretical predictions for specific light nuclei.
