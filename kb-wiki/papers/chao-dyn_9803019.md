# Chaos and Statistical Mechanics in the Hamiltonian Mean Field Model

> We study the dynamical and statistical behavior of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model in order to investigate the relation between microscopic chaos and phase transitions. HMF is a simple toy model of $N$ fully-coupled rotators which shows a second order phase transition. The canonical thermodynamical solution is briefly recalled and its predictions are tested numerically at finite $N$. The Vlasov stationary solution is shown to give the same consistency equation of the canonical solution and its predictions for rotator angle and momenta distribution functions agree very well with numerical simulations. A link is established between the behavior of the maximal Lyapunov exponent and that of thermodynamical fluctuations, expressed by kinetic energy fluctuations or specific heat. The extensivity of chaos in the $N \to \infty$ limit is tested through the scaling properties of Lyapunov spectra and of the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy. Chaotic dynamics provides the mixing property in phase space necessary for obtaining equilibration; however, the relaxation time to equilibrium grows with $N$, at least near the critical point. Our results constitute an interesting bridge between Hamiltonian chaos in many degrees of freedom systems and equilibrium thermodynamics.

# Chaos and Statistical Mechanics in the Hamiltonian Mean Field Model

## Key claim

The Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model exhibits a peak in the maximal Lyapunov exponent and Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy at the second-order phase transition point, establishing a link between microscopic chaos and thermodynamic fluctuations; however, the relaxation time to equilibrium grows approximately linearly with system size N near the critical point, and the maximal Lyapunov exponent vanishes as N^{-1/3} in the high-temperature phase, meaning chaos is not extensive in that regime.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of the HMF model, a system of N fully-coupled classical rotators with Hamiltonian H = Σ p_i²/2 + (1/2N) Σ_{i,j} [1 - cos(θ_i - θ_j)], using fourth-order symplectic integrators with time step Δt ≈ 0.2 and energy conservation error below 10⁻⁵. They computed Lyapunov spectra via the standard method of Benettin et al. over approximately 10⁶ time steps for system sizes ranging from N=10 to N=20000, and compared numerical results for distribution functions, kinetic energy fluctuations, and specific heat with predictions from canonical ensemble statistical mechanics and stationary Vlasov solutions.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density: U_c = E_c/N = 3/4
- Inverse critical temperature: β_c = 2 (k_B = 1)
- Specific heat at critical point: C_V(β_c) = 5/2
- Specific heat in high-temperature phase: C_V = 1/2
- For U ≥ U_c, maximal Lyapunov exponent scales as λ₁ ∼ N^{-1/3}
- For U < 0.2, maximal Lyapunov exponent scales as λ₁ ∼ √U
- Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy density at small U scales as S_KS/N ∼ U^{3/4}
- For U > U_c, Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy density scales as S_KS/N ∼ N^{-1/5}
- Relaxation time τ_r scales approximately linearly with N (τ_r ∼ N) near the critical point
- Equilibrium temperature for U=0.69: T_μ = 0.4757 (for N=1000)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the HMF model by Antoni and Ruffo (1995) and Latora, Rapisarda, and Ruffo (1998) by providing new numerical data on Lyapunov spectra, Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy, and relaxation dynamics, and by comparing with analytical formulas from Riemannian geometry approaches by Lapo and Firpo. The results are motivated by connections to nuclear multifragmentation phase transitions studied by Ata, Chomaz, and collaborators, where similar energy-temperature relations and critical exponents have been observed experimentally. The paper enables future work on understanding how microscopic chaos governs equilibration in long-range interacting systems and how statistical mechanics descriptions succeed despite complex dynamics, particularly near phase transitions.
