# Comment on ``Comparison of strangeness production between A+A and p+p reactions from 2 to 160 A GeV", by J. C. Dunlop and C. A. Ogilvie

> A recent paper on energy dependence of strangeness production in A+A and p+p interactions written by Dunlop and Ogilvie (Phys. ReV. C61 031901(R) (2000) indicates that there is a significant misunderstanding about the concept of strangeness enhancement and its role as a signal of Quark Gluon Plasma creation. In this comment we will try to clarify some essential points.

# Comment on "Comparison of strangeness production between A+A and p+p reactions from 2 to 160 A GeV"

## Key claim

The proposal by Dunlop and Ogilvie (2000) to search for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) transition by studying the energy dependence of the strangeness enhancement factor (the ratio of strangeness-to-pion yields in A+A collisions relative to N+N collisions) is unjustified and misleading, because the observed enhancement is already large (factor several) at low AGS energies where QGP is not expected, and the simple concept of strangeness enhancement as a QGP signal is therefore incorrect.

## Method

The authors re-analyze the same experimental data on strangeness-to-pion ratios in central A+A collisions (Pb+Pb at 158 A·GeV from NA49, Au+Au at AGS energies from E866) and in N+N interactions, using the measure E_S = (⟨Λ⟩ + ⟨K + K̅⟩)/⟨π⟩ and the Fermi energy measure F = (√s − 2m)^(3/4)/√s^(1/4). They compare these results to predictions of the statistical model of the early stage of A+A collisions, which assumes maximum entropy creation in both confined and deconfined matter, and which predicts a transition to QGP between AGS and SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- The strangeness-to-pion ratio in central A+A collisions increases rapidly up to the top AGS energy (15 A·GeV) and then saturates at the top SPS energy (200 A·GeV), while in N+N interactions the ratio increases monotonically by a factor of about 2 between top AGS and top SPS energies.
- In central S+S collisions at 200 A·GeV, the strangeness-to-pion ratio is two times higher than in N+N interactions at the same energy per nucleon.
- In Si+A collisions at AGS, the enhancement factor is about 3.
- In the statistical model, the mass of strange degrees of freedom in confined matter is m_S ≈ 0.5 GeV, non-strange m_NS ≈ 0.14 GeV, and the critical temperature T_C ≈ 0.170 GeV; in QGP, m_S ≈ 0.170 GeV and m_NS ≈ 0.01 GeV.
- The model predicts the transition onset at ≈ 30 A·GeV and pure QGP phase at ≈ 60 A·GeV for T_C = 200 MeV, or a kink at top AGS energy for T_C = 170 MeV.

## Context

This comment directly responds to Dunlop and Ogilvie (2000, Phys. Rev. C61 031901), who proposed using the strangeness enhancement factor as a QGP signal. The authors argue that their own earlier work (Gazdzicki et al., 1996, on strangeness production; Gazdzicki et al., 1994, on pion production) already established the anomalous energy dependence of the strangeness-to-pion ratio and its connection to a possible QGP transition between AGS and SPS energies. The comment extends the statistical model of the early stage (Gazdzicki et al., 1995) to predict a non-monotonic or kinky energy dependence of the strangeness-to-pion ratio near the transition region, and it enables future searches for QGP by focusing on the absolute strangeness-to-pion ratio in A+A collisions rather than the enhancement factor relative to N+N interactions.
