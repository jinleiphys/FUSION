# Towards Collisions of Inhomogeneous Shockwaves in AdS

> We perform a numerical simulation of the evolution of inhomogeneities with transverse profile in a collision of gravitational shockwaves in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime. This constitutes a step closer towards an accurate holographic description of the thermalization of a strongly coupled plasma, which can model the dynamics of heavy ion collisions. The results indicate that the considered inhomogeneities typically become hydrodynamical earlier or at the same moment when hydrodynamics applies to the background, even though they decay slowly.

## Key claim

The paper claims that inhomogeneities (transverse perturbations) on top of colliding gravitational shockwaves in AdS typically become hydrodynamical earlier or at the same moment when hydrodynamics applies to the background, even though the inhomogeneities decay slowly. This claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis is performed in a linearized approximation around a specific background solution, and the initial conditions for the perturbations are chosen in a particular simple form.

## Method

The author performs a numerical simulation of gravitational shockwave collisions in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime (AdS5), using the characteristic formulation of general relativity with generalized ingoing Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates. The metric ansatz includes transverse directions x1 and x2, and the problem is simplified by decomposing all metric functions into a background plus a planar wave perturbation of the form e^{ik x1}, reducing the dynamics from 3+1 to 2+1 dimensions. Einstein's equations are linearized around the background solution of Chesler and Yaffe (2011), and the system is solved using pseudospectral methods with an Adams-Bashforth time evolution scheme, with the apparent horizon fixed at constant radial coordinate r=1.

## Key numbers

- The shockwave width is set to w = 0.75/μ, where μ is the energy scale.
- The background energy density is δ = 0.075 μ^4.
- The collision point is at y = y0 = 5π/μ.
- The perturbation wavenumber k takes values k = 0.2 and k = 0.5 in the simulations shown.
- The background thermalization time (hydrodynamization) is approximately t ∼ 10/μ.
- Pressure inhomogeneities have not isotropized even at t = 12/μ, much longer than the background isotropization time.

## Context

This work extends the holographic shockwave collision framework of Chesler and Yaffe (2011) and others (Yarom, Mateos, Janik and Heller, Wilke and Pratt) by including transverse dynamics, which were previously neglected in favor of boost-invariant or rotationally symmetric approximations. The study complements the work of van der Schee (2013) on radial flow, which assumed boost-invariance and rotational symmetry. The results support the use of hydrodynamic approximations for studying perturbations during the out-of-equilibrium stage of QGP formation, and suggest that future work could explore mode interactions, turbulence, and quasinormal mode spectra for finite transverse momentum.
