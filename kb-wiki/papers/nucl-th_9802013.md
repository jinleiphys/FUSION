# Production of $ω$-mesons in proton-proton collisions

> The production of $ω$-mesons in proton-proton collisions for proton incident energies up to 2.2 GeV is investigated within a meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. We find a large cancellation between the dominant $πρω$ meson-exchange current and nucleonic current contributions. A comparison with preliminary data from SATURNE calls for the inclusion of off-shell form factors at the $NNω$ and $πρω$ production vertices. Due to the present lack of knowledge of these form factors, together with the destructive interference mentioned above, the relative magnitude of the nucleonic and meson-exchange current contributions cannot be determined from existing total cross section data. However, it is shown that the angular distribution of the produced $ω$-mesons provides an unique and clear signature of the magnitude of these currents, thus allowing one to disentangle these two basic reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the nucleonic and πρω meson-exchange currents interfere destructively in pp → ppω, and that the relative magnitude of these two contributions cannot be determined from total cross section data alone due to the present lack of knowledge of off-shell form factors at the NNω and πρω production vertices. However, the angular distribution of the produced ω-mesons provides a unique and clear signature that allows one to disentangle these two basic reaction mechanisms.

## Method

The authors calculate the pp → ppω reaction within a relativistic meson-exchange model using a Distorted Wave Born Approximation that includes the final-state NN interaction via a T-matrix generated from the Bonn B NN potential, but neglects the initial-state interaction. The ω-emission current is the sum of a nucleonic current (including both positive- and negative-energy nucleon propagations) and a meson-exchange current dominated by the πρω vertex, with phenomenological form factors introduced at the ω-production vertices to account for off-shell effects and fitted to preliminary SATURNE data.

## Key numbers

- The vector coupling constant adopted for the NNω vertex is g²_NNω/4π = 11, taken from Janssen et al.
- The tensor-to-vector coupling ratio κ_ω is considered in the range -0.3 to +0.3; κ_ω = -0.3 yields the largest cross section in that range.
- The ππρω coupling constant is g_πρω = 10 at vanishing four-momentum square of the ρ-meson.
- For the nucleonic current form factor, two solutions are found: Λ_N = 1160 MeV (yielding NC > MEC) and Λ_N = 850 MeV (yielding NC < MEC).
- At threshold, the intermediate nucleon in the post-emission diagram is off-shell by p² - m_N² ~ 2.3 GeV², and in the pre-emission diagram by p² - m_N² ~ -1.5 GeV².
- The π- and ρ-mesons in the exchange current diagram are off-shell by q_π² - m_π² ~ -0.8 GeV² and q_ρ² - m_ρ² ~ -1.4 GeV².
- Without form factors, the calculated total cross section overestimates the preliminary SATURNE data by nearly two orders of magnitude.
- The final-state interaction enhances the total cross section close to threshold by almost an order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of vector-meson production in NN collisions, such as the one-pion exchange model of Sibirtsev (1996) and the meson-exchange calculation of Chung et al. (1997), which did not include final-state interactions. The paper shows that the good agreement of the peripheral on-shell rescattering model of Sibirtsev (1996) with data must be considered accidental because it neglects the large FSI enhancement. The results enable future experiments at accelerators like COSY to determine the dominant ω-production mechanism by measuring angular distributions, which is essential for extracting NNω coupling constants and for understanding vector-meson properties in nuclear medium studies.
