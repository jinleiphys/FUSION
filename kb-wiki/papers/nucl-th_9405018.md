# Large-space shell-model calculations for light nuclei

> An effective two-body interaction is constructed from a new Reid-like $NN$ potential for a large no-core space consisting of six major shells and is used to generate the shell-model properties for light nuclei from $A$=2 to 6. (For practical reasons, the model space is partially truncated for $A$=6.) Binding energies and other physical observables are calculated and compare favorably with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective two-body interaction from a new Reid-like NN potential (Reid93) for a large no-core shell-model space of six major harmonic-oscillator shells, and uses it to calculate binding energies and other observables for light nuclei from A=2 to 6. The results compare favorably with experiment, but the authors note that for A=6 the model space is partially truncated due to computer memory limitations, and the effective interaction involves a free parameter Δ fixed at -35 MeV for all nuclei, which is chosen to yield experimental binding energies under the assumption that neglected effective many-body forces can largely cancel unknown true many-body forces.

## Method

The authors construct a G-matrix effective interaction from the Reid93 NN potential for a no-core harmonic-oscillator model space of six major shells (0s through 2p-1f-0h) with ℏΩ=14 MeV. They approximate the effective two-body interaction by the average of the G-matrix and its conjugate, calculated with a state-dependent starting energy ω' = ε_a + ε_b + Δ, where Δ = -35 MeV is fixed for all nuclei. Shell-model diagonalizations are performed using the Many-Fermion-Dynamics code, with configurations up to 7ℏΩ for A≤4 and up to 5ℏΩ for A>4, and the center-of-mass motion is treated exactly via a Lawson term.

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach of Vary, Zheng, and Barrett (1993) by using a larger six-major-shell model space and an improved Reid93 NN potential from the Nijmegen group, compared to earlier four-major-shell calculations. The results improve upon those of Ceuleneer et al. (1993) for ⁴He, who used a modified Sussex interaction with a model-space-dependent parameter, by employing a theoretically derived effective interaction with a fixed parameter Δ. The approach enables systematic testing of dual convergence criteria (increasing N_max and model-space size d) and provides a foundation for extending no-core shell-model calculations to heavier 0p-shell nuclei, though the authors note that the Monte Carlo shell-model approach of Koonin (1993) may be needed for much heavier systems like ⁴⁰Ca.
