# Flow Fluctuations from Early-Time Correlations in Nuclear Collisions

> We propose that flow fluctuations have the same origin as transverse momentum fluctuations. The common source of these fluctuations is the spatially inhomogeneous initial state that drives hydrodynamic flow. Longitudinal correlations from an early Glasma stage followed by hydrodynamic flow quantitatively account for many features of multiplicity and $p_t$ fluctuation data. We develop a framework for studying flow and its fluctuations in this picture. We then compute elliptic and triangular flow fluctuations, and study their connections to the ridge.

## Key claim

Flow fluctuations and transverse momentum fluctuations share a common origin in the spatially inhomogeneous initial state, specifically local spatial correlations from Glasma flux tubes, rather than solely from global geometric eccentricity variations. The authors argue that if early-time correlations are the only source of these fluctuations, then the two measurements are related with no free parameters.

## Method

The authors construct a two-particle correlation function based on a Glasma flux-tube model, where particles emerging from the same flux tube of transverse size ~Q_s^{-1} are correlated at production. They then model the anisotropic transverse expansion using a blast wave parametrization with an average event eccentricity, and compute the momentum-space two-particle correlation function by integrating the spatial correlation function with the Boltzmann phase-space density over the freeze-out surface. The flow coefficients v_n{2} and v_n{4} are calculated from the two- and four-particle cumulants, and flow fluctuations are quantified by the difference between these coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The correlation strength R is proportional to ⟨K⟩^{-1}, where ⟨K⟩ is the average number of flux tubes per event.
- The Glasma correlation scale R dN/dy = κ α_s^{-1}(Q_s^2) depends only on the saturation scale Q_s.
- The correction Σ_n to v_n{4} is smaller than 1.2% for n=2 in peripheral collisions.
- Blast wave parameters at 2.76 TeV are scaled up from 200 GeV values: velocity by 6% and temperature by 7%.
- The statistical variance from finite particle sampling is σ̂_stat^2 = (1+⟨v_{2n}⟩)/(2⟨N⟩).

## Context

This work extends the framework developed in Gavin (2011) for multiplicity and transverse momentum fluctuations to flow fluctuations, building on the Glasma flux-tube picture of Gavin (2008) and Moschelli (2009). The approach contrasts with purely geometric descriptions of flow fluctuations such as those of Voloshin (2007) and Ollitrault (2009), which attribute fluctuations to event-wise variation of global eccentricity. The results enable connections between flow fluctuations, the ridge phenomenon, and the violation of factorization of Fourier coefficients at low transverse momentum.
