# On the Proof by Reductio ad Absurdum of the Hohenberg-Kohn Theorem for Ensembles of Fractionally Occupied States of Coulomb Systems

> It is demonstrated that the original reductio ad absurdum proof of the generalization of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for ensembles of fractionally occupied states for isolated many-electron Coulomb systems with Coulomb-type external potentials by Gross et al. [Phys. Rev. A 37, 2809 (1988)] is self-contradictory since the to-be-refuted assumption (negation) regarding the ensemble one-electron densities and the assumption about the external potentials are logically incompatible to each other due to the Kato electron-nuclear cusp theorem. It is however proved that the Kato theorem itself provides a satisfactory proof of this theorem.

# On the Proof by Reductio ad Absurdum of the Hohenberg-Kohn Theorem for Ensembles of Fractionally Occupied States of Coulomb Systems

## Key claim
The original reductio ad absurdum proof by Gross et al. (1988) of the ensemble generalization of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem is self-contradictory for Coulomb-type external potentials, because the assumption that two ensemble densities are equal is logically incompatible with the premise that the external potentials differ, due to the Kato electron-nuclear cusp theorem. However, the Kato theorem itself provides a satisfactory proof of the ensemble Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for Coulomb systems.

## Method
The author re-examines the logical structure of the reductio ad absurdum proof given by Gross et al. (1988) for the ensemble generalization of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (EGHK theorem). He shows that for Coulomb-type external potentials, the premises of the proof (that two external potentials differ by more than a constant, and that both Hamiltonians have ground and first excited states) together with the Kato electron-nuclear cusp theorem directly imply that the ensemble one-electron densities must differ, making the to-be-refuted assumption of equal densities already contradictory before the variational argument is applied. The author then demonstrates that the Kato theorem alone suffices to prove the EGHK theorem for Coulomb systems.

## Key numbers
- The ensemble weight w is restricted to the interval [0, 1/2] throughout the analysis.
- The Kato cusp condition for each individual state gives: d/dr ρ_k^{av}(r)|_{r=R_α} = -2Z_α ρ_k(R_α), and similarly for primed quantities, where Z_α is the nuclear charge at position R_α.
- For the ensemble density, the cusp condition becomes: d/dr ρ^{av}(r)|_{r=R_α} = -2Z_α ρ(R_α), and d/dr ρ'^{av}(r)|_{r=R'_α} = -2Z'_α ρ'(R'_α).

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier re-examination (Kryachko, 2005) of the original Hohenberg-Kohn proof, now applied to the ensemble generalization by Gross et al. (1988). It also relates to the degenerate ground-state generalization by van Leeuwen (1994), which the author notes suffers from the same logical flaw. The paper enables a cleaner foundation for ensemble density functional theory by showing that the Kato theorem provides a direct proof for Coulomb systems, avoiding the logical inconsistency of the original reductio ad absurdum approach. The author also references the super-Hamiltonian approach by Theophilou (1979) and later work by Katriel and within the local-scaling-transformation method.
