# Emission of single photons, hadrons, and dileptons in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at CERN SPS and quark hadron phase transition

> The production of single photons in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the CERN SPS as measured by the WA98 experiment is analysed. A quark gluon plasma is assumed to be formed initially, which expands, cools, hadronizes, and undergoes freeze-out. A rich hadronic equation of state is used and the transverse expansion of the interacting system is taken into account. The recent estimates of photon production in quark-matter (at two loop level) along with the dominant reactions in the hadronic matter leading to photons are used. About half of the radiated photons are seen to have a thermal origin. The same treatment and the initial conditions provide a very good description to hadronic spectra measured by several groups and the intermediate mass dileptons measured by the NA50 experiment, lending a strong support to the conclusion that quark gluon plasma has been formed in these collisions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a single set of initial conditions assuming a quark-gluon plasma is formed, combined with a rich hadronic equation of state and two-loop photon production rates, provides a consistent description of single photons, hadrons, and intermediate mass dileptons in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS. The hedge is that the thermal sources contribute only about half of the radiated single photons, and the analysis depends on using corrected two-loop photon rates (which were revised downward by a factor of 4 from the original Aurenche et al. calculation).

## Method

The authors assume a chemically and thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formed at time τ0, with initial temperature determined from the isentropy condition using an average particle rapidity density of 750 for the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions. They solve relativistic hydrodynamic equations including transverse expansion, with a phase transition at T=180 MeV and freeze-out at 120 MeV, using a hadronic equation of state containing all hadrons from the particle data book. Photon production from quark matter is calculated at two-loop order using the corrected rates from Aurenche et al., while hadronic photon rates are taken from Ref. [joe] with the A1 resonance contribution included according to Xiong et al.

## Key numbers

- Average particle rapidity density: 750 for the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS
- Phase transition temperature: 180 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: 120 MeV
- Formation time: τ0 = 1/(3T0) from the uncertainty relation
- Mass number used for non-zero impact parameter: 190, based on number of participants
- Number of quark flavors: approximately 2.5, to account for strange quark mass
- Thermal sources contribute about 50% of the single photons

## Context

This work extends the search for quark-gluon plasma signatures by simultaneously describing three different observables (single photons from WA98, hadronic spectra from NA44, NA49, and WA98, and intermediate mass dileptons from NA50) with a single set of initial conditions. It contrasts with the findings of Rapp (Ref. [rapp]) where most dilepton radiation came from hadronic matter, whereas here the quark matter itself dominates the dilepton production, attributed to the richer equation of state and more sophisticated evolution mechanism. The analysis enables a consistent framework for interpreting multiple electromagnetic and hadronic probes as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation at SPS energies.
