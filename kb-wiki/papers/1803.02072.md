# Nearly isentropic flow at sizeable $η/s$

> Non-linearities in the harmonic spectra of hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions provide evidence for the dynamical response to azimuthal spatial eccentricities. Here, we demonstrate within the framework of transport theory that even the mildest interaction correction to a picture of free-streaming particle distributions, namely the inclusion of one perturbatively weak interaction ("one-hit dynamics"), will generically give rise to all observed linear and non-linear structures. We further argue that transport theory naturally accounts within the range of its validity for realistic signal sizes of the linear and non-linear response coefficients observed in azimuthal momentum anisotropies with a large mean free path of the order of the system size in peripheral ($\sim 50 \%$ centrality) PbPb or central pPb collisions. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$ of such a transport theory is approximately an order of magnitude larger than that of an almost perfect fluid. The phenomenological success of transport simulations thus challenges the perfect fluid paradigm of ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus and hadron-nucleus collisions.

# Nearly isentropic flow at sizeable η/s

## Key claim

Even the mildest perturbative interaction correction to free-streaming particle distributions, a single weak scattering ("one-hit dynamics"), generically produces all observed linear and non-linear harmonic structures in azimuthal momentum anisotropies, and transport theory with a large mean free path (of order the system size) can account for realistic signal sizes in peripheral PbPb and central pPb collisions, challenging the perfect fluid paradigm. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s in such a transport theory is approximately an order of magnitude larger than that of an almost perfect fluid.

## Method

The authors solve massless kinetic transport in the isotropization-time approximation, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and focusing on central spatial rapidity. They expand the distribution function to first order in the inverse mean free path parameter γ (the "one-hit" limit), where the initial free-streaming distribution is perturbed by a single collision kernel, and compute the resulting azimuthal anisotropies of the transverse energy flow analytically in terms of spatial eccentricities δ₂ and δ₃. The expansion parameter is the dimensionless combination γ̂ = R γ ε^{1/4}, proportional to the ratio of system size R to mean free path.

## Key numbers

- Linear response coefficient for elliptic flow: v₂/δ₂ = 0.212 γ̂
- Linear response coefficient for triangular flow: v₃/δ₃ = 0.140 γ̂
- Non-linear mode coupling v₄/δ₂² = 0.063 γ̂
- Non-linear mode coupling v₅/(δ₂ δ₃) = 0.088 γ̂
- For peripheral PbPb collisions at ∼50% centrality with γ̂ ∼ 1, the extracted shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is η/s ≈ 0.5
- The correction to the azimuthally symmetric free-streaming distribution is −0.210 γ̂, rendering the one-hit expansion unphysical for γ̂ > 1/0.210
- The relation between the model parameter γ and η/s is η/s ≈ 0.11/γ

## Context

This work extends the program of establishing benchmark results for transport theory near the free-streaming limit, complementing earlier tests of 2→2 parton cascades between free-streaming and hydrodynamic limits (Gyulassy 1997, Zhang 1998) and more recent comparisons of kinetic theory to fluid dynamics (Kurkela 2015). The results challenge the perfect fluid paradigm (η/s ∼ 1/4π) favored by viscous fluid dynamic simulations (Heinz 2013, Romatschke 2017) by showing that transport codes like AMPT (Lin 2004) and BAMPS can generate collective flow with a mean free path of order the system size and η/s ∼ 0.5, consistent with perturbative QCD estimates (Arnold 2000, 2003). The analysis enables quantitative comparison of one-hit dynamics to data compilations of linear and non-linear response coefficients (Liu 2018) and suggests that transport theory may be applicable to systems with η/s ≳ 0.2.
