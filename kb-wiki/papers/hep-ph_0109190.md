# Heavy-Meson Observables at One-Loop in Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> I present one-loop level calculations of the Isgur-Wise functions for B -> D^{(*)} + e + nu, of the matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators in B and D mesons, and the matrix elements for the radiative decays D^* -> D + gamma in partially quenched heavy quark chiral perturbation theory. Such expressions are required in order to extrapolate from the light quark masses used in lattice simulations of the foreseeable future to those of nature.

# Heavy-Meson Observables at One-Loop in Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The one-loop chiral corrections to heavy-meson observables in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQchiPT) are computed, but the expressions are only valid for extrapolating lattice QCD results from the unphysical light-quark masses used in simulations down to the physical values, provided that the counterterms determined from partially quenched simulations are the same as those in ordinary QCD.

## Method

The author constructs a partially quenched QCD theory with three valence quarks (u,d,s), three sea quarks (j,l,r), and three bosonic quarks, all combined into an SU(6|3) supermultiplet. One-loop calculations are performed in partially quenched heavy quark chiral perturbation theory for the Isgur-Wise functions at zero recoil and away from zero recoil, the matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators in B and D mesons, and the radiative decay widths for D* to D gamma. The loop diagrams include vertex corrections and wavefunction renormalization contributions involving light meson propagators, with the D*-D mass splitting retained explicitly in the propagators for the zero-recoil calculations.

## Key numbers

- The axial coupling constant g_pi is constrained to approximately 0.56 from radiative D* to D gamma decays, with alternative determinations giving 0.24, 0.76 +/- 0.2, or 0.27 +/- 0.07; quenched lattice simulations yield g_pi approximately 0.42.
- The pion decay constant convention used is f approximately 132 MeV.
- The one-loop correction to the Isgur-Wise function xi_{B_q}^{PQ}(w) contains a w-dependent function r(w) = (1/sqrt(w^2-1)) log[w + sqrt(w^2-1)].
- The zero-recoil form factors h_+(1) and h_{A_1}(1) receive corrections proportional to g_pi^2/(16 pi^2 f^2) and g_pi^2/(48 pi^2 f^2) respectively, with loop functions F(m,delta,Lambda) that contain terms of the form delta^2 log(m^2/Lambda^2).
- The radiative decay width for D* to D gamma is Gamma = (alpha/3) |mu_a|^2 |k_gamma|^3, where the transition moments mu_a receive one-loop corrections proportional to g_pi^2 m_pi/(4 pi f^2) and g_pi^2 m_K/(4 pi f^2).
- The one-loop correction to the twist-2 operator matrix elements in B_u mesons contains a factor (1+3 g_pi^2)/(16 pi^2 f^2) times m_{ju}^2 log(m_{ju}^2/Lambda^2).

## Context

This work extends the partially quenched chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Sharpe and Zhang (1996) for heavy meson decay constants to three additional classes of observables: the Isgur-Wise functions for B to D(*) semileptonic decays, radiative D* to D gamma transitions, and twist-2 operator matrix elements. The zero-recoil calculations build on the work of Randall and Wise (1993), who computed the long-distance contributions from D*-D mass splitting to the 1/m_c^2 corrections. The Isgur-Wise function away from zero recoil extends the leading non-analytic contributions computed by Jenkins and Savage (1992) and Goity (1992) in ordinary QCD, and the quenched QCD results of Booth (1995). The twist-2 operator calculations parallel recent work by Chen and Ji (2001) and Arndt and Savage (2001) on chiral corrections to parton distribution moments in nucleons. The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to perform extrapolations from partially quenched simulations to physical quark masses by determining the common counterterms from partially quenched data and inserting them into the QCD expressions.
