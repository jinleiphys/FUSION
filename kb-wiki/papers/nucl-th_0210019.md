# Basis Generator for M-Scheme SU(3) Shell Model Calculations

> A FORTRAN code for generating the leading SU(3) irreducible representation (irrep) of N identical spin 1/2 fermions in a harmonic oscillator mean field is introduced. The basis states are labeled by N--the total number of particles, the SU(3) irrep labels ($λ,μ$), and S -- the total spin of the system. The orthonormalized basis states have two additional good quantum numbers: $ε$ -- the eigenvalue of the quadruple operator, $Q_{0}$; and $M_{J}$ -- the eigenvalues of the projection of the total angular momentum operator, $J_{0}=L_{0}+S_{0}$. The approach that is developed can be used for a description of nuclei in a proton-neutron representation and is part of a larger program aimed at integrating SU(3) symmetry into the best of the currently available exact shell-model technologies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a FORTRAN code that generates the leading SU(3) irreducible representation for N identical spin-1/2 fermions in a harmonic oscillator mean field, but only for the *leading* (maximally deformed) irrep; generation of other non-leading irreps is noted as future work.

## Method

The algorithm defines single-particle levels within a harmonic oscillator shell as symmetric (η,0) irreps of SU(3), orders them by quadrupole moment ε and then by orbital angular momentum projection m_l, and constructs a many-particle highest weight state (HWS) of SU(3) ⊗ SU_S(2) by filling single-particle states from bottom to top to maximize ε. Step operators (p-move, q-move, m-move) are then applied to the HWS to generate all other states of the SU(3) irrep, and finally spin lowering operators produce states with good total angular momentum projection M_J.

## Key numbers

- On a 50 MHz Macintosh IIci with math co-processor and 3 Mb of memory, the longest run time for generating all states of any leading SU(3) irrep was less than 5 minutes.
- On a 200 MHz Power Macintosh 8500 with 4 Mb of memory, the longest run time for generating all states of any leading SU(3) irrep was less than 30 seconds.
- On a 200 MHz SUN workstation, the longest run time for generating all states of any leading SU(3) irrep was less than 15 seconds.
- All states with good M_J for any leading irrep of SU(3) ⊗ SU_S(2) for the p, sd, and pf shells can be generated in less than 20 minutes.
- Beyond the pf-shell (η ≥ 4), the number of configurations in a single SU(3) state may exceed 100,000.
- In the sd-shell, the HWS of the leading irrep for N=6 particles and total spin S=1 is (λ,μ) = (3,3); for S=0 the leading irrep is (6,0).

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) shell-model framework of Elliott (1958) and Hecht (1970s) by providing a practical computational tool for generating SU(3)-adapted basis states in the M-scheme format used by modern shell-model codes such as ANTOINE and OXBASH. The code is the first stage of the "Red Stick" project at Louisiana State University, which aims to integrate SU(3) symmetry-adapted basis states into exact shell-model calculations to simultaneously describe collective and single-particle nuclear phenomena. Future work includes generating non-leading SU(3) irreps and extending the approach to proton-neutron systems via SU(3) coupling or an SU(4) supermultiplet scheme.
