# Proposition of numerical modelling of BEC

> We propose extension of the numerical method to model effect of Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) observed in hadronization processes which allows for calculations not only correlation functions $C_2(Q_{inv})$ (one-dimensional) but also corresponding to them $C_2(Q_{x,y,z})$ (i.e., three-dimensional). The method is based on the bunching of identical bosonic particles in elementary emitting cells (EEC) in phase space in manner leading to proper Bose-Einstein form of distribution of energy (this was enough to calculate $C_2(Q_{inv})$). To obtain also $C_2(Q_{x,y,z})$ one has to add to it also symmetrization of the multiparticle wave function to properly correlate space-time locations of produced particles with their energy-momentum characteristics.

## Key claim

The authors propose an extension to their previously published numerical method for modelling Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) in hadronization, which previously only produced one-dimensional correlation functions \(C_2(Q_{inv})\), to now also produce three-dimensional correlation functions \(C_2(Q_{x,y,z})\) by adding explicit symmetrization of the multiparticle wave function that correlates space-time production points with particle momenta. The method remains a proposition rather than a fully validated technique, as it still omits time-dependent emission, Coulomb and other final-state interactions, and full symmetrization among all particles within an elementary emitting cell.

## Method

The method first generates elementary emitting cells (EECs) in momentum space by adding particles of equal energy \(E_1\) to a seed particle with probability \(P = P_0 \cdot \exp(-E_1/T)\) until first failure, where the single-particle energy distribution is Boltzmann-like \(f(E) \sim \exp(-E/T)\) with temperature \(T\) as a parameter. To obtain three-dimensional correlation functions, the momenta of particles in each EEC are decomposed into components \((p_x, p_y, p_z)\) and the spatial production point density \(\rho(x,y,z)\) is introduced; for each additional particle beyond the first, the momentum components are selected such that \(\cos(\delta_i \cdot Q_i) \le 2 \cdot \text{Rand} - 1\), where \(\delta_i\) are position differences and \(Q_i\) are momentum differences, implementing the plane-wave approximation of the symmetrized wave function.

## Key numbers

- The one-dimensional correlation function \(C_2(Q_{inv}) = 2\) is obtained only in the first bin, i.e., for \(Q_{inv} = 0\).
- Calculations were performed assuming a spherical source \(\rho(r)\) of radius \(R = 1\) fm.
- The energy spread of particles within each EEC is controlled by a Gaussian width parameter \(\sigma\), where \(\sigma = 0\) corresponds to an infinite source and nonzero values arise for finite space-time extensions of hadronizing sources.
- The parameter \(P_0\) is introduced as an additional free parameter controlling the probability of adding particles to an EEC.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own earlier numerical method for BEC modelling (Utyuzh, Wilk, and Wlodarczyk, 2005, references HIP and NUKL) from one-dimensional to three-dimensional correlation functions. The approach converges in some sense to an earlier proposition by Zajc (1987) that was practically impossible to implement because it required symmetrization of the entire source at once, whereas the present method restricts symmetrization to within individual EECs. The method enables future inclusion of time-dependent emission via a \(\delta E \cdot \delta t\) term, Coulomb and other final-state interactions through distorted wave functions, and full symmetrization among all particles within an EEC, though the latter would dramatically increase computational time.
