# Shear Viscosity of a Unitary Fermi Gas

> We present the first ab initio determination of the shear viscosity eta of the Unitary Fermi Gas, based on finite temperature quantum Monte Carlo calculations and the Kubo linear-response formalism. We determine the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s. The minimum of eta/s appears to be located above the critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition with the most probable value being eta/s approx 0.2 hbar/kB, which almost saturates the Kovtun-Son-Starinets universal value hbar/(4 pi kB).

## Key claim

The minimum of the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio for the unitary Fermi gas is most probably about 0.2 hbar/kB, which is close to the Kovtun-Son-Starinets universal bound of hbar/(4 pi kB), but the authors conservatively estimate the minimum is lower than 0.45 hbar/kB due to systematic errors.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) calculations on spatial lattices of 8^3 and 10^3 points with dynamical fermions, evaluating the stress-tensor correlator at zero momentum for 51 imaginary-time points. They extracted the static shear viscosity by inverting the Kubo relation using two complementary methods, singular value decomposition and maximum entropy method, with bootstrap resampling to assess stability, incorporating sum rules and asymptotic tail behavior as prior information.

## Key numbers

- The most probable minimum value of eta/s is approximately 0.2 hbar/kB, located at temperatures around 0.20 to 0.25 epsilon_F.
- The conservative upper bound for the minimum of eta/s is lower than 0.45 hbar/kB.
- The critical temperature for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition is T_c approximately 0.15 epsilon_F.
- The shear viscosity eta/n monotonically decreases with decreasing temperature, with no drastic suppression below T_c.
- Calculations were performed at an average particle number density n = N/V approximately 0.09, with statistical ensembles of 5000 uncorrelated samples per temperature.

## Context

This work provides the first ab initio determination of shear viscosity for the unitary Fermi gas using fully dynamical fermions, extending prior PIMC calculations that used the quenched approximation (Nakamura and Sakai, 2005; Meyer, 2008). The results contradict earlier theoretical predictions from kinetic theory (Bruun and Smith, 2005) and T-matrix approaches (Enss et al., 2011), which gave minimum eta/s values around 0.5, and also lie below experimental measurements from trapped gases (Turlapov et al., 2007; Cao et al., 2011). The findings suggest the unitary Fermi gas may be the best candidate for a perfect fluid, but the authors call for further investigations to control systematic errors from finite volume and effective-range corrections.
