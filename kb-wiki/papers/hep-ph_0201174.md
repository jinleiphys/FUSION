# Flavour-symmetry breaking of the quark condensate and chiral corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation

> The relation between the chiral quark condensate in QCD sum rules and chiral perturbation theory is clarified with the help of a low-energy theorem for the scalar and pseudoscalar correlation functions. It is found that the quark condensate should be identified with the non-normal-ordered vacuum expectation value of quark-antiquark fields. Utilising results on flavour SU(3) breaking of the quark condensate from QCD sum rules, the unphysical low-energy constant H_2^r in the chiral Lagrangian, as well as next-to-leading order corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation are estimated.

# Flavour-symmetry breaking of the quark condensate and chiral corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation

## Key claim

The quark condensate in chiral perturbation theory should be identified with the non-normal-ordered vacuum expectation value of quark-antiquark fields, not the normal-ordered condensate; this identification resolves the apparent mismatch between QCD sum rule and chiral perturbation theory definitions, but the resulting low-energy constant H_2^r depends on the short-distance renormalisation scale and scheme, so its value cannot be deduced from relations to physical quantities alone.

## Method

The author derives a low-energy theorem for the scalar and pseudoscalar two-point correlation functions, showing that the subtraction constant at zero momentum transfer equals the product of quark masses and non-normal-ordered quark condensates. Using QCD sum rule results for the flavour SU(3) breaking ratio of the quark condensates, extracted from heavy B- and B_s-meson decay constant sum rules combined with lattice QCD values for f_{B_s}/f_B, together with the chiral perturbation theory relation for the low-energy constant L_8^r from meson mass and decay constant data, the unphysical low-energy constant H_2^r is estimated. This estimate is then used to compute next-to-leading order chiral corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of strange to light quark condensates: ⟨¯ss⟩/⟨¯qq⟩ = 0.8 ± 0.3, from QCD sum rules for heavy B- and B_s-mesons combined with lattice QCD average f_{B_s}/f_B = 1.16 ± 0.04
- Low-energy constant L_8^r at chiral scale ν_χ = M_ρ: L_8^r = (0.88 ± 0.24) × 10^{-3}, determined from the relation involving M_K^2/M_π^2, f_K/f_π = 1.22 ± 0.01, and quark mass ratio R = m_s/ˆm = 24.4 ± 1.5
- Unphysical low-energy constant H_2^r at ν_χ = M_ρ: H_2^r = (−3.4 ± 1.5) × 10^{-3}
- Chiral correction to the pion GMOR relation: δ_π = 0.047 ± 0.017 (approximately 5%)
- Chiral correction to the kaon GMOR relation: δ_K = 0.61 ± 0.22
- Light quark condensate at 2 GeV: ⟨¯qq⟩(2 GeV) = −(267 ± 16 MeV)^3, using light quark masses (m_u+m_d)(2 GeV) = 8.1 ± 1.4 MeV
- Light quark condensate at 1 GeV: ⟨¯qq⟩(1 GeV) = −(242 ± 15 MeV)^3

## Context

This work clarifies the long-standing issue of how the quark condensate in QCD sum rules (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov 1979) relates to that in chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler 1984). It extends previous analyses by Brodsky (1975, 1981) and others on the low-energy theorem for scalar correlators, and provides the first estimate of H_2^r from flavour SU(3) breaking data. The result contradicts the resonance saturation assumption of Ecker et al. (1989) that H_2^r = 2L_8^r, which would imply vanishing chiral corrections to the GMOR relations. The updated quark condensate values agree with direct determinations from QCD sum rules (Dominguez and Nasrallah 1998) and lattice QCD. Future work should focus on improved extractions of ⟨¯ss⟩/⟨¯qq⟩ from QCD sum rules with proper treatment of condensate definitions and strange mass corrections, as well as next-to-next-to-leading order O(p^6) chiral perturbation theory analyses.
