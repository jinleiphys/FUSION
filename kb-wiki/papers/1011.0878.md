# Eta-Mesic Nucleus and COSY-GEM Data

> The experimental data of the COSY-GEM Collaboration for the recoil-free transfer reaction p (27Al, 3He) π- p' X, leading to the formation of bound state of eta (η) meson in 25Mg nucleus, is reanalyzed in this paper. In particular, predicted values of binding energy and half-width of the η-mesic nucleus 25Mgη, given by different theoretical approaches, are compared with the ones obtained from the experimental missing mass spectrum. It is found that the spectrum can be explained reasonably well if interference effect of another process, where ηis not bound in 25Mg but is scattered by the nucleus and emerge as a pion, is taken into account. The data also indicate that the interaction between N*(1535) and a nucleus is attractive in nature.

# Eta-Mesic Nucleus and COSY-GEM Data

## Key claim

The COSY-GEM missing-mass spectrum for the η-mesic nucleus ²⁵Mg_η can be explained reasonably well only if an interference effect from a second process, where the η is not bound but scatters from the nucleus and emerges as a pion, is taken into account; the data also indicate that the N*(1535)-nucleus interaction is attractive, but the binding energy extracted from the spectrum centroid alone is not the true binding energy of the η-mesic nucleus.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the COSY-GEM data for the recoil-free transfer reaction p(²⁷Al,³He)π⁻p'X by solving a relativistic three-dimensional integral equation for the η-nucleus bound state, using both a scattering-length approach (first-order optical potential with a single ηN scattering length) and a microscopic off-shell optical potential based on the coupled-channel isobar (CCI) model of Bhalerao and Liu. They then fitted the experimental missing-mass spectrum with a coherent sum of two amplitudes: one for the mesic-nucleus doorway process (Process M) and one for a scattering process where the η is not bound (Process S), treating the N*(1535)-nucleus interaction V_N* as a parameter.

## Key numbers

- The COSY-GEM collaboration's original fit gave binding energy ε = (−13.13 ± 1.64) MeV and FWHM Γ = (10.22 ± 2.98) MeV (half-width Γ/2 ≃ 5.1 ± 1.5 MeV) for ²⁵Mg_η.
- The scattering-length approach requires aηN = (0.292 + 0.077i) fm to reproduce the COSY-GEM values, but all published theoretical models give an imaginary part at least a factor of 2.4 greater than 0.077 fm.
- With the CCI model and V_N* = 0 and <B_N> = −30 MeV, the calculated binding energy is ε = −6.5 MeV and half-width Γ/2 = 7.1 MeV.
- To reproduce ε = −13 MeV and Γ/2 = 5.0 MeV with the microscopic optical potential requires <B_N> = −360 MeV, which is unrealistic.
- The experimental centroid at missing mass ΔM = 23.803 GeV leads to E = −(13 + E_x) MeV, implying E_x = 0 for the observed centroid at −13 MeV, so η is captured in the ground state of ²⁵Mg.
- Fits including interference yield three parameter sets: (a) ε = −6.5 MeV, Γ/2 = 7.1 MeV; (b) ε = −8.0 MeV, Γ/2 = 9.6 MeV; (c) ε = −10.0 MeV, Γ/2 = 14.1 MeV, with V_N* = 0, −15 MeV, and −42 MeV respectively.
- The final analysis gives binding energy between −8 and −10 MeV and half-width between 10 and 14 MeV for ²⁵Mg in its ground state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own prediction of η-mesic nuclei from 1986 (Haider and Liu, 1986) and the experimental confirmation by the COSY-GEM collaboration (Budzanowski et al., 2009). It contradicts the simpler interpretation that the centroid of the missing-mass spectrum directly gives the η binding energy, showing instead that interference from a non-binding scattering process shifts the observed peak. The analysis enables future microscopic calculations of both amplitudes using the same η-nucleus optical potential, and provides the first empirical indication that the N*(1535)-nucleus interaction is attractive below the ηN threshold, which is of value for studies of baryon resonances in nuclear matter.
