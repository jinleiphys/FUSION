# Nucleon spin polarization in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Based on a spin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model with spin-dependent potentials incorporated using the lattice Hamiltonian method, we have studied the global spin polarization perpendicular to the reaction plane as well as the local spin polarization in the longitudinal direction in non-central intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, as an extension of similar studies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Both the global and the local spin polarizations are found to be mostly dominated by the time-odd component of the nuclear spin-orbit potential. Impacts of various theoretical uncertainties on the nucleon spin polarization as well as its dependence on the beam energy and impact parameter are discussed. Our study serves as a baseline for understanding the spin polarization mechanism of strongly interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions dominated by nucleon degree of freedom.

## Key claim

Using a spin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SBUU) transport model with a lattice Hamiltonian method, the authors find that both global and local nucleon spin polarization in non-central intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions are mostly dominated by the time-odd component of the nuclear spin-orbit potential, but the quantitative behavior is affected by the strength of the spin-orbit coupling and by uncertainties in how nucleon spin is treated after collisions.

## Method

The authors extended their SBUU transport model by incorporating spin-dependent potentials via the lattice Hamiltonian method, using test particles on a cubic lattice to calculate number, spin, momentum, and spin-current densities. They simulated non-central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 100 AMeV and impact parameters of 4, 8, and 12 fm, with spin-dependent differential cross sections and Pauli blocking for nucleon-nucleon collisions. Two scenarios for nucleon spin after collisions were compared: spin unchanged (pure mean-field effect) and spin randomized (rotated by a random angle around a precession vector), to bracket uncertainties in in-medium spin flip.

## Key numbers

- Default spin-orbit coupling strength: \(W_0 = 150\) MeV fm\(^5\); uncertainty range considered: \(W_0 = 80 \sim 150\) MeV fm\(^5\).
- For Au+Au at 100 AMeV and \(b=8\) fm, the global spin polarization in the \(+y\) direction at midrapidity is stronger for the spin-unchanged scenario than for the spin-randomized scenario; the latter yields negative polarization at large rapidities.
- The global spin polarization saturates around beam energy \(100 \sim 150\) AMeV.
- Local spin polarization in the \(z\) direction at midrapidity for high-\(p_T\) nucleons shows a sign change in its azimuthal angular dependence between midcentral (\(b=4,8\) fm) and peripheral (\(b=12\) fm) collisions.
- Lattice parameters: \(N=200\) parallel events, lattice spacing \(l=1\) fm, shape function range \(n=2\).

## Context

This work extends similar studies of spin polarization from relativistic heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Liang (2005), STAR (2007, 2008, 2017, 2018, 2019), Becattini (2008, 2013, 2017, 2018), and Xia (2018)) down to intermediate energies where nucleon degrees of freedom dominate. It builds on the authors' prior SBUU model work on spin-dependent collective flows (Xu (2013), Xia (2014)) and improves density calculation via the lattice Hamiltonian method (Lenk (1989)). The results provide a baseline for understanding spin polarization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions at a few GeV, and the approach can be extended to study hadronic afterburner effects on hyperon or vector meson spin polarizations in relativistic collisions.
