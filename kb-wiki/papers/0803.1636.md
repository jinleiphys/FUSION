# A gap in the quarkyonic matter

> It has recently been suggested that at a reasonably large chemical potential a confining quarkyonic matter is formed that consists of the quark Fermi sea and confined hadrons on top of this Fermi sea. We study some properties of this matter. It is demonstrated that below the chiral restoration point there are gapless excitations of this matter through excitations of the Goldstone bosons. Above the chiral restoration point the single quarks are still removed from the spectrum of excitations and the only possible excitations are confined color-singlet hadrons with finite mass. Hence there appears a gap in the excitation spectrum of the quarkyonic matter that should be crucially important for its properties above the chiral restoration point. This gap is of a new type and is not related with the condensation of the fermionic system into a quasibosonic system. It is only due to such properties as confinement and manifest chiral symmetry at the same time.

## Key claim

Below the chiral restoration point, quarkyonic matter has gapless excitations via Goldstone bosons; above that point, single quarks are still confined and absent from the excitation spectrum, so a gap appears that is due solely to the combination of confinement and manifest chiral symmetry, not to Cooper-pair condensation.

## Method

The author studies quarkyonic matter in the large-\(N_c\) limit using a 3+1 dimensional, exactly solvable model with a linear instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential (Coulomb-like). The Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy is solved in the rainbow approximation to obtain the chiral (Bogoliubov) angle, and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for meson bound states, both at finite chemical potential with a step-function Fermi distribution. The infrared regularization required for confinement is applied, and the cancellation of infrared divergences in color-singlet observables is verified.

## Key numbers

- The chiral restoration point occurs at a critical Fermi momentum \(p_f^{cr} = 0.109\) (in units of \(\sqrt{\sigma}\), where \(\sigma\) is the string tension).
- Below \(p_f^{cr}\), the pseudoscalar \(0^{-+}\) meson (Goldstone boson) is massless; above \(p_f^{cr}\), its mass becomes finite and increases with chemical potential.
- The scalar \(0^{++}\) meson mass also becomes finite above \(p_f^{cr}\) and increases with chemical potential.
- The gap in the excitation spectrum above the chiral restoration point is given by the finite mass of the lowest-lying pion-like excitation, which can become arbitrarily large as the chemical potential increases.

## Context

This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) for the large-\(N_c\) QCD phase diagram, and builds on the demonstration by Glozman and Wagenbrunn (2008) that confined, chirally symmetric hadrons exist above the chiral restoration point. The paper identifies a new type of gap in a Fermi system that is not due to Cooper-pair condensation (as in BCS theory or superfluidity) but arises from confinement and chiral symmetry together. It enables future study of the transport and dissipative properties of quarkyonic matter, particularly processes without energy dissipation, and raises the question of whether such a gap persists at \(N_c = 3\).
