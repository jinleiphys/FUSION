# Quark spin polarization and spontaneous magnetization in high density quark matter

> By using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks, it is shown that there exists a possibility of a spin polarized phase in quark matter at finite temperature and density. When there exists the spin polarization, the spontaneous magnetization may occur if the effect of the anomalous magnetic moment of quark is taken into account. An implication to the compact star objects with strong magnetic field is discussed when the spin polarization occurs.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks, a spin polarized phase may exist in quark matter at finite temperature and high baryon density; spontaneous magnetization may then occur only if spin polarization is present and the effect of the anomalous magnetic moment of the quark is taken into account.

## Method

The authors start from a two-flavor NJL model Lagrangian that includes a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks. Under a mean-field approximation, they derive the thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, introducing a three-momentum cutoff Λ and treating the tensor coupling constant G_T as a free parameter. To study spontaneous magnetization, they incorporate the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks (with flavor-dependent values μ_u and μ_d) into the mean-field Lagrangian, compute the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature in the presence of an external magnetic field B, and then take the limit B→0 to extract the magnetization via the thermodynamic relation ℳ = −∂Φ/∂B.

## Key numbers

- The three-momentum cutoff is Λ = 0.631 GeV, the scalar coupling constant is G_S = 5.5 GeV⁻², and the tensor coupling constant is G_T = 11.0 GeV⁻².
- For the chiral condensed to normal phase transition, a critical endpoint exists near μ = 0.31 GeV and T = 0.046 GeV.
- The spin polarization F appears above a critical chemical potential μ_c ≈ 0.407 GeV; below this value, F = 0 and no magnetization occurs.
- The anomalous magnetic moments used are μ_u = 1.85 μ_N and μ_d = −0.97 μ_N, where μ_N = e/(2m_p) = 3.15 × 10⁻¹⁷ GeV/T.
- For a hybrid compact star of radius R = 10 km, if quark matter occupies 0.1% of the volume (r_q = 1 km), the surface magnetic flux density reaches about 10¹³ Gauss; if quark matter occupies 1% (r_q = 2.15 km), it reaches about 10¹⁴ Gauss.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin polarized phases at zero temperature using the NJL model with tensor interactions (Bohr 2012, Tsue 2012, Tsue 2013) to finite temperature, and it builds on investigations of QCD ground states under external magnetic fields (Ferrer 2014). The results suggest that strong magnetic fields of order 10¹³–10¹⁴ Gauss, comparable to those observed in magnetars (Harding 2006), could arise from spontaneous magnetization in quark matter cores of hybrid compact stars, and they motivate further study of the phase structure under the strong magnetic fields expected in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2008).
