# Trace anomaly form factors from lattice QCD

> The hadron mass can be obtained through the calculation of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor in the hadron which includes the trace anomaly and sigma terms. The anomaly due to conformal symmetry breaking is believed to be an important ingredient for hadron mass generation and confinement. In this work, we will present the calculation of the glue part of the trace anomaly form factors of the pion up to $Q^2\sim 4.3~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ and the nucleon up to $Q^2\sim 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2$. The calculations are performed on a domain wall fermion ensemble with overlap valence quarks at seven valence pion masses varying from $\sim 250$ to $\sim 540$ MeV, including the unitary point $\sim 340$ MeV. We calculate the radius of the glue trace anomaly for the pion and the nucleon from the $z$ expansion. By performing a two-dimensional Fourier transform on the glue trace anomaly form factors in the infinite momentum frame with no energy transfer, we also obtain their spatial distributions for several valence quark masses. The results are qualitatively extrapolated to the physical valence pion mass with systematic errors from the unphysical sea quark mass, discretization effects in the renormalization sum rule, and finite-volume effects to be addressed in the future. We find the pion's form factor changes sign, as does its spatial distribution, for light quark masses. This explains how the trace anomaly contribution to the pion mass approaches zero toward the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The glue part of the trace anomaly form factor of the pion changes sign as a function of momentum transfer for light quark masses, which explains how the trace anomaly contribution to the pion mass approaches zero toward the chiral limit; however, the results are only qualitatively extrapolated to the physical valence pion mass, with systematic errors from unphysical sea quark mass, discretization effects, and finite-volume effects still to be addressed.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on a (2+1)-flavor domain-wall fermion ensemble (24I) with Iwasaki gauge action, using overlap valence quarks at seven valence pion masses from approximately 250 to 540 MeV. They computed the glue part of the trace anomaly form factor for the pion up to momentum transfer Q^2 of about 4.3 GeV^2 and for the nucleon up to about 1 GeV^2, using three-point functions with grid-source propagators and low-mode substitution, extracting form factors via joint fits of two- and three-point functions and ratio methods. The bare beta/2g factor was determined from the nucleon mass sum rule at one valence mass and applied across all masses, and z-expansion fits were performed to extract radii and spatial distributions.

## Key numbers

- Pion glue trace anomaly form factor computed up to Q^2 ~ 4.3 GeV^2
- Nucleon glue trace anomaly form factor computed up to Q^2 ~ 1 GeV^2
- Seven valence pion masses used: approximately 250, 300, 340 (unitary point), 400, 450, 500, and 540 MeV
- Lattice spacing a = 0.1105(3) fm on a 24^3 x 64 lattice with spatial extent L = 2.65 fm
- Bare value of beta/(2g) determined as -0.129(6) at valence quark mass m_v a = 0.016
- Pion forward matrix element beta/(2g) <F^2>(Q^2=0) found proportional to sqrt(m) for quark masses at and above the unitary mass
- Chiral perturbation theory prediction for the trace anomaly form factor: F_ta,pi^ChPT(Q^2) ~ 1/2 - (1/(2 m_pi^2)) Q^2

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice study by Fangcheng et al. (2021) which found that the spatial distribution of the glue trace anomaly in the pion changes sign for light quark masses, unlike the monotonic distributions seen for the nucleon and rho meson. The results are consistent with predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Novikov 1980, Chen 1998, Hatta) at small Q^2 and with the theoretical expectation by Liu (2023) that the pion trace anomaly form factor should change sign. The nucleon results disagree with the asymptotic sign predictions from perturbative QCD by Tong (2022). This work enables future studies of the full trace anomaly form factor including quark contributions, and provides input for understanding hadron mass structure relevant to the Electron-Ion Collider program.
