# Phase structure of three and four flavor QCD

> We investigate the phase structure of QCD at finite temperature and light-quark chemical potential. We improve upon earlier results for N_f=2+1 dynamical quark flavors and investigate the effects of charm quarks in an extension to N_f=2+1+1. We determine the quark condensate and the Polyakov loop potential using solutions of a coupled set of (truncated) Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators of Landau gauge QCD. At zero chemical potential we find excellent agreement with results from lattice-QCD. With input fixed from physical observables we find only a very small influence of the charm quark on the resulting phase diagram at finite chemical potential. We discuss the location of the emerging critical end-point and compare with expectations from lattice gauge theory.

# Phase structure of three and four flavor QCD

## Key claim

The authors find that charm quarks have only a very small influence on the QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential, despite producing up to 15 percent changes in the gluon dressing functions at large momenta. This result depends on fixing the interaction strength and quark masses using vacuum hadron physics input rather than simply adding a charm quark to the existing setup.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled set of truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators in Landau gauge QCD at finite temperature and light-quark chemical potential. They use temperature-dependent lattice data for the quenched gluon propagator as input and implement the back-reaction of quarks onto gluons by adding the quark-loop in the gluon DSE, with the quark-gluon vertex constructed from the Ball-Chiu vertex multiplied by an infrared-enhanced function. The vertex strength and quark masses are fixed either by reproducing lattice results for the condensate at zero chemical potential or by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations to reproduce vacuum meson masses and decay constants.

## Key numbers

- Chiral transition temperature for $N_f=2+1$ (set $A_{2+1}$): $T_c = 160.2$ MeV from the maximum of the chiral susceptibility, and $T_c = 155.6$ MeV from the inflection point of the light-quark condensate
- Critical end point location for $N_f=2+1$ (set $A_{2+1}$): $(T^c, \mu_q^c) = (115, 168)$ MeV
- Pion mass in vacuum for set $B_{2+1}$: $m_\pi = 135$ MeV; for set $B_{2+1+1}$: $m_\pi = 135$ MeV
- Kaon mass in vacuum for set $B_{2+1}$: $m_K = 497$ MeV; for set $B_{2+1+1}$: $m_K = 497$ MeV
- $\eta_c$ mass in vacuum for set $B_{2+1+1}$: $m_{\eta_c} = 2,982$ MeV
- Ratio of strange to light quark mass for set $B_{2+1+1}$: $m_s/m_l \approx 26$; ratio of charm to strange quark mass: $m_c/m_s \approx 14$
- Reduction of gluon dressing functions due to charm quarks: up to about 15 percent close to the bump at $T = 135$ MeV
- Shift in transition temperature when adding charm quark without readjusting parameters: $\Delta T \approx 23$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations for $N_f=2+1$ QCD reported by Fischer et al. (2012) by correcting the up/down quark masses to physical values and by extending the framework to include charm quarks ($N_f=2+1+1$). The results at zero chemical potential show excellent agreement with lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2010) and Bazavov et al. (2011). The chiral transition line at finite chemical potential agrees well with lattice extrapolations from Endrodi et al. (2011), Kaczmarek et al. (2011), and Cea et al. (2014). The finding that charm quarks have negligible effect on the phase diagram suggests that future studies may only need to include light and strange quark dynamics, and provides guidance for lattice calculations at finite chemical potential where the fermion sign problem limits direct simulations.
