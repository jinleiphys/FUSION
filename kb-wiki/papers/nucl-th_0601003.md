# Baryon-meson interactions in chiral quark model

> Using the resonating group method (RGM), we dynamically study the baryon-meson interactions in chiral quark model. Some interesting results are obtained: (1) The Sigma K state has an attractive interaction, which consequently results in a Sigma K quasibound state. When the channel coupling of Sigma K and Lambda K is considered, a sharp resonance appears between the thresholds of these two channels. (2) The interaction of Delta K state with isospin I=1 is attractive, which can make for a Delta K quasibound state. (3) When the coupling to the Lambda K* channel is considered, the N phi is found to be a quasibound state in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with several MeV binding energy. (4) The calculated S-, P-, D-, and F-wave KN phase shifts achieve a considerable improvement in not only the signs but also the magnitudes in comparison with other's previous quark model study.

# Baryon-meson interactions in chiral quark model

## Key claim
Using the resonating group method (RGM) within the chiral SU(3) quark model and its extended version (which includes vector meson exchanges), the authors find that several baryon-meson systems exhibit attractive interactions sufficient to form quasibound states or resonances, but the results depend on the specific parameter set and whether channel coupling is included; for example, the $N\phi$ bound state appears only in the extended model with channel coupling to $\Lambda K^*$, not in the one-channel calculation.

## Method
The authors apply the resonating group method (RGM) to dynamically study baryon-meson interactions in the chiral SU(3) quark model and an extended version that includes vector meson exchanges. The Hamiltonian includes quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions composed of one-gluon exchange, confinement, and chiral field induced potentials (scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchanges in the standard model, with additional vector meson exchanges in the extended model). The model parameters are fitted to baryon ground state energies, the deuteron binding energy, and $NN$ phase shifts, with three parameter sets used: set I for the chiral SU(3) quark model, and sets II and III for the extended chiral SU(3) quark model.

## Key numbers
- The $\Sigma K$ quasibound state appears with a sharp resonance between the $\Lambda K$ and $\Sigma K$ thresholds; the resonance has spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^-$ and width $\Gamma \approx 5$ MeV.
- The $\Delta K$ state with isospin $I=1$ has an attractive interaction that can produce a bound state with binding energy of about 3, 20, and 15 MeV using the three different parameter sets (set I, II, and III respectively).
- In the one-channel study of $N\phi$, a bound state is obtained only with parameter set II, with binding energies of 1 MeV for $S=1/2$ and 3 MeV for $S=3/2$; with channel coupling to $\Lambda K^*$, binding energies become 3 MeV ($S=1/2$) and 9 MeV ($S=3/2$) for set II, and 1 MeV ($S=1/2$) and 6 MeV ($S=3/2$) for set III.
- The $KN$ phase shifts are calculated for $S$-, $P$-, $D$-, and $F$-waves, achieving correct signs and improved magnitudes compared to previous quark model studies (Lemaire 2003).

## Context
This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for baryon ground states, deuteron binding, $NN$ scattering, and $NY$ cross sections, to baryon-meson systems. The $\Lambda K$-$\Sigma K$ resonance is relevant to understanding the $S_{11}(1535)$ nucleon resonance, which has been explained on the hadron level as a $\Lambda K$-$\Sigma K$ quasibound state (Kaiser 1997, Inoue 2002) and on the quark level as an excited three-quark state (Glozman 1996, Isgur 1979). The $N\phi$ bound state possibility was previously suggested by QCD van der Waals attraction (Gao 2001). The $\Delta K$ state had been studied on the hadron level (Sarkar 2005). Future work is needed to include additional channels and tensor forces to determine exact masses and widths of the predicted resonances and bound states.
