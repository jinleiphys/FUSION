# A Nonequilibrium Quantum Field Theory Description of the Bose-Einstein Condensate

> We study the detailed out of equilibrium time evolution of a homogeneous Bose-Einstein condensate.We consider a nonrelativistic quantum theory for a self-interacting complex scalar field, immersed in a thermal bath, as an effective microscopic model for the description of the Bose-Einstein condensate. This approach yields the following main results:(i) the interaction between fluctuations proves to be crucial in the mechanism of instability generation; (ii) there are essentially two regimes in the $k$-space, with a crossover for $k^2/2m \sim 2λ|ϕ_0|^2$, where, in our notation, $λ$ is the coupling constant and $|ϕ_0|^2$ is the condensate density; (iii) a set of coupled equations that determines completely the nonequilibrium dynamics of the condensate density as a function of the temperature and of the total density of the gas.

## Key claim

The interaction between fluctuations (beyond the Bogoliubov approximation) is crucial for generating the instability that drives macroscopic condensate formation; without it, there is no condensate growth. The authors show this for a homogeneous gas quenched from above to well below the critical temperature, but note that their mean-field treatment neglects incoherent collisional processes, which may explain why their equilibrium condensate fractions are lower than experimental results.

## Method

The authors model a homogeneous nonrelativistic Bose gas using a complex scalar field with a hard-core interaction, coupled to a thermal bath. They decompose the field into a uniform condensate part and a fluctuation part, then apply a mean-field approximation to the fluctuation interaction term beyond the standard Bogoliubov treatment. Using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time-path formalism, they derive coupled integro-differential equations for the condensate density and the fluctuation mode functions, which are solved numerically for a system quenched from an initial high temperature to a final low temperature.

## Key numbers

- The crossover between unstable and stable momentum regimes occurs at \(k^2/2m \sim 2\lambda |\varphi_0|^2\), where \(\lambda\) is the coupling constant and \(|\varphi_0|^2\) is the condensate density.
- Numerical results are shown for a gas parameter \(na^3 = 0.01\) (where \(n\) is total density and \(a\) is the s-wave scattering length) at three final temperatures: \(T_1/T_c = 0.06\), \(T_2/T_c = 0.08\), and \(T_3/T_c = 0.1\).
- The dimensionless time is defined as \(\tau \equiv (\hbar/ma^2)t\) and the dimensionless condensate density as \(\rho_0 \equiv a^3 |\varphi_0|^2\).
- The chemical potential in the mean-field approximation is \(\mu = 4\lambda n\), consistent with the Hartree-Popov approximation.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic nonequilibrium field theory approach of Stoof (1999) by explicitly including fluctuation interactions beyond the Bogoliubov approximation, which Stoof had identified as necessary for condensate formation. It also complements the quantum kinetic theory approach of Gardiner et al. (1997, 1998), which used a master equation equivalent to a Boltzmann equation for quasi-equilibrium growth. The formalism enables future studies of transients in realistic Bose-Einstein condensation experiments and could be generalized to describe the dynamics of a photon gas in a nonlinear Fabry-Perot cavity, as proposed by Chiao and Boyce (1999).
