# Improved quark mass density- dependent model with quark-sigma meson and quark-omega meson couplings

> An improved quark mass density- dependent model with the non-linear scalar sigma field and the $ω$-meson field is presented. We show that the present model can describe saturation properties, the equation of state, the compressibility and the effective nuclear mass of nuclear matter under mean field approximation successfully. The comparison of the present model and the quark-meson coupling model is addressed.

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model, which adds quark-sigma and quark-omega meson couplings to the original QMDD framework, can successfully describe saturation properties, the equation of state, compressibility, and effective nucleon mass of nuclear matter under mean field approximation, but only after discarding an unphysical low-density branch of the scalar field solution that arises from the nonlinear sigma term.

## Method

The authors extend the quark mass density-dependent model by introducing a nonlinear scalar sigma field and a vector omega meson field, with quark-sigma and quark-omega couplings, under mean field approximation at zero temperature. The effective nucleon mass is computed from the bag energy of three quarks confined in a spherical cavity, using a density of states formula fitted to MIT bag model numerics. The model parameters (bag constant B = 174 MeV fm^-3, sigma and omega meson masses, and coupling constants g_sigma^q and g_omega^q) are fixed by fitting the free nucleon mass (939 MeV) and nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy -15 MeV at density 0.15 fm^-3).

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon bag radius: R = 0.85 fm (at zero temperature with sigma and omega fields set to zero)
- Saturation binding energy: E/A = -15 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Compressibility at saturation: K(rho_0) = 210 MeV (for parameters b = -1460 MeV, g_sigma^q = 4.67, g_omega^q = 2.44)
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M*_N(rho_0) = 775 MeV (for the same parameter set)
- Compressibility ranges from 218.8 MeV (b = -800 MeV) to 205.7 MeV (b = -1800 MeV) as coupling constants increase and b decreases
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation ranges from 782.9 MeV (b = -800 MeV) to 772.6 MeV (b = -1800 MeV)
- Comparison with QMC model: IQMDD (b = -1460) gives K = 210 MeV and M*_N = 775 MeV versus QMC giving K = 200 MeV and M*_N = 851 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IQMDD model (Wu, Qian, and Su, 2005), which only included quark-sigma coupling for studying nucleon properties, by adding omega meson coupling to reproduce the short-range repulsion essential for nuclear matter. The model is presented as an alternative to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and Saito and Thomas (1994-1995), with the advantage that the interactions extend throughout free space rather than being confined to MIT bag boundaries, making it more suitable for many-body calculations beyond mean field approximation. The results are compared to the QMC model and the Walecka model (QHD-1), with the IQMDD model producing compressibility and effective mass values closer to empirical expectations than QHD-1 (which gives K = 540 MeV and M*_N = 522 MeV).
