# Anharmonic double-$γ$ vibrations in nuclei and their description in the interacting boson model

> Double-$γ$ vibrations in deformed nuclei are studied in the context of the interacting boson model with special reference to their anharmonic character. It is shown that large anharmonicities can be obtained with interactions that are (at least) of three-body nature between the bosons. As an example the $γ$ vibrations of the nucleus $^{166}_{\phantom{0}68}$Er$^{\phantom{00}}_{98}$ are studied in detail.

# Anharmonic double-γ vibrations in nuclei and their description in the interacting boson model

## Key claim

Large anharmonicities in double-γ vibrations can be obtained in the interacting boson model (IBM-1) only if interactions of at least three-body nature between the bosons are included; with only up to two-body interactions, anharmonicities are always small and the model is essentially harmonic in the limit of large boson number.

## Method

The authors adopt an IBM-1 Hamiltonian containing a quadrupole-quadrupole term, a rotational L² term, and five independent three-body d-boson interactions (with angular momentum l = 0, 2, 3, 4, and 6). They compute the ratios R₀ᵞ and R₄ᵞ (excitation energies of the Kπ=0⁺ and Kπ=4⁺ double-γ band heads, normalized to the single-γ excitation energy) as functions of the quadrupole parameter χ and the three-body strength θₗ/κ, for N=15 bosons. The calculated spectrum and E2 transition probabilities for ¹⁶⁶Er are compared with experimental data using the Consistent-Q Formalism.

## Key numbers

- Experimental double-γ band heads in ¹⁶⁶Er: Kπ=0⁺ at 1.949 MeV and Kπ=4⁺ at 2.029 MeV, giving ratios R₀ᵞ=2.76 and R₄ᵞ=2.50.
- Calculated double-γ band heads with l=4 three-body interaction: 1926 keV (Kπ=0⁺) and 1972 keV (Kπ=4⁺), yielding R₀ᵞ=2.82 and R₄ᵞ=2.45.
- Best-fit parameters: κ=23.8 keV, χ=-0.55, κ'=-1.9 keV, θ₄=93.9 keV, with boson number N=15.
- B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺)=214 W.u. (observed and used to fix effective charge e_eff²=(1.83)² W.u.).
- B(E2;2ᵧ⁺→0₁⁺)=5.5±0.4 W.u. (observed) versus 5.3 W.u. (calculated).
- Ratio B(E2;0ᵧᵧ⁺→2ᵧ⁺)/B(E2;2ᵧ⁺→0₁⁺)=3.8±1.3 (observed) versus 3.2 (calculated).
- Ratio B(E2;4ᵧᵧ⁺→2ᵧ⁺)/B(E2;2ᵧ⁺→0₁⁺)=1.3±0.4 (observed) versus 2.5 (calculated).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that the standard IBM-1 with only two-body interactions cannot accommodate the large anharmonicities observed in double-γ vibrations, as pointed out by Bohr and Mottelson (1982). It builds on the authors' previous intrinsic-frame analysis (Garcia-Ramos et al., 1998) which demonstrated that IBM-1 is harmonic for large boson number with two-body forces. The paper directly addresses the experimental observation of both Kπ=0⁺ and Kπ=4⁺ double-γ states in a single nucleus (¹⁶⁶Er) reported by Fahlander et al. (1996) and Garrett et al. (1997), which provides a stringent test of nuclear models. The results enable systematic searches for the appropriate three-body interactions needed to reproduce observed anharmonicities, and call for further experiments on double-γ vibrations to constrain the interactions at a microscopic level.
