# How chiral forces shape neutron-rich Ne and Mg nuclei

> We compute the structure of the exotic even nuclei $^{20-34}$Ne and $^{34-40}$Mg using interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT). Our results for the ground-state rotational bands in $^{20-32}$Ne and $^{36-40}$Mg agree with data. We predict a well-deformed $^{34}$Ne and find that $^{40}$Mg exhibits an oblate deformed band close to the prolate ground-state, indicating the emergence of shape co-existence at the neutron dripline. A global sensitivity analysis shows that the subleading singlet $S$-wave contact and a pion-nucleon coupling strongly impact deformation in chiral EFT.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral effective field theory interactions can accurately reproduce ground-state rotational bands in neutron-rich neon and magnesium isotopes, and that a global sensitivity analysis reveals that deformation in these nuclei is primarily driven by the subleading pion-nucleon coupling \(c_3\) and the singlet \(S\)-wave contact, though the authors note that for \(^{30}\)Ne the computed \(2^+\) state is significantly higher than experiment, indicating an artificially large \(N=20\) shell gap that hints at deficiencies in the employed interactions.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum projected coupled-cluster computations (singles-and-doubles approximation) for neon isotopes and projected Hartree-Fock computations for magnesium isotopes, using the chiral interaction 1.8/2.0 (EM) and an ensemble of about 100 chiral NNLO interactions with explicit Delta degrees of freedom. They employed a global sensitivity analysis based on variance decomposition, using Monte Carlo integration with approximately \(10^6\) emulated projected Hartree-Fock computations to compute main effects of 17 low-energy constants on the deformation measure \(R_{42} \equiv E(4^+)/E(2^+)\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{34}\)Ne, the computed \(R_{42}\) value is \(3.37 \pm 0.13\) (68% confidence interval) from the ensemble and \(3.38\) from the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction, both close to the rigid-rotor value of \(10/3\).
- For \(^{40}\)Mg, the oblate band head is about 3 MeV above the prolate ground state, indicating shape co-existence.
- The correlation coefficient between the low-energy constant \(C_{^1S_0}\) and \(R_{42}\) in \(^{32}\)Ne is \(r = 0.73\).
- More than 97% of the variance in \(R_{42}\) is explained via main effects, with about 40% driven by the subleading pion-nucleon coupling \(c_3\) and the singlet \(S\)-wave contact.
- The Hartree-Fock computations used a spherical harmonic oscillator basis of up to thirteen major shells, with the \(NNN\) interaction restricted by an energy cut \(E_{\mathrm{3max}} = 16 \hbar\Omega\) and oscillator frequencies varied from 10 to 16 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-cluster computations of neutron-rich neon and magnesium isotopes (Hagen 2022) and projected generator-coordinate-method computations (Frosini 2021) by providing quantified uncertainties and a global sensitivity analysis. The results for \(^{40}\)Mg are consistent with Monte-Carlo shell-model computations (Tsunoda 2020) and offer a possible interpretation of the third observed excited state (Crawford 2019). The sensitivity analysis provides the first step toward understanding nuclear deformation at the resolution scale of chiral effective field theory, connecting low-energy nuclear structure to quantum chromodynamics without solving QCD directly.
