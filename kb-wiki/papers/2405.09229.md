# Theoretical analysis of the reactions induced by interaction of $^{6}$Li with nuclei $^{3}$H and $^{3}$He

> We determine cross sections and astrophysical S-factors of the reactions generated in collisions between $^{6}$Li and $^{3}$H, and $^{6}$Li and $^{3}$He. A microscopic three-cluster model is employed to study the dynamics of reactions occurring in the mirror nuclei $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B. In a previous study [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 109}, 045803 (2024)], this model was successfully applied to investigate the resonance structure of $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B, as well as reactions induced by the interaction of deuterons with $^7$Li and $^7$Be. A fairly good agreement between theoretical results and available experimental data was achieved. To the best of our knowledge, appropriate experimental data for the astrophysical S-factors of the reactions generated by the interaction of $^6$Li with $^3$H and with $^3$He are currently unavailable. Thus, our results can serve as a guideline for future experimental efforts.

## Key claim

The authors calculate cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for reactions induced by collisions of $^{6}$Li with $^{3}$H and $^{6}$Li with $^{3}$He using a microscopic three-cluster model, but note that no experimental data for these S-factors currently exist, so their results serve only as theoretical predictions to guide future experiments.

## Method

The authors employ a microscopic multi-channel cluster model that treats $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B as systems of coupled three-cluster configurations ($^{4}$He+$^{4}$He+n and $^{4}$He+$^{3}$H+d for $^{9}$Be; $^{4}$He+$^{4}$He+p and $^{4}$He+$^{3}$He+d for $^{9}$B). The wave function for each binary channel is constructed as an antisymmetrized product of an s-shell cluster wave function, a two-cluster subsystem wave function, and a relative motion wave function, with the coupled equations solved using a basis of Gaussian functions for two-cluster subsystems and oscillator functions for the compound system. The Minnesota potential with exchange parameter $u=0.95$ and oscillator length $b=1.2846$ fm are used as input parameters.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^{6}$Li calculated as $E = -1.29$ MeV, slightly above the experimental value of $E = -1.47$ MeV
- For $^{9}$B, the lowest $1/2^-$ resonance is found at 0.408 MeV above the $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$He threshold with width $\Gamma = 1.006$ MeV, and the $3/2^-$ resonance at 0.628 MeV with $\Gamma = 0.812$ MeV
- For $^{9}$Be, the $1/2^+$ resonance state at $E = 0.278$ MeV above the $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$H threshold significantly contributes to the S-factor peak for $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$H $\rightarrow$ $^{7}$Li(1/2$^-$)+$d$ at low energies $0 \leq E < 0.3$ MeV
- The reaction $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$H $\rightarrow$ $^{7}$Li(3/2$^-$)+$d$ occurs nearly eight times more frequently than the reaction producing $^{7}$Li in its excited 1/2$^-$ state
- For the $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$H interaction at $T_9 = 0.8$ GK, the Gamow window parameters are $E_0 = 276$ keV and $\Delta E_0 = 318$ keV; for $^{6}$Li+$^{3}$He, $E_0 = 437$ keV and $\Delta E_0 = 401$ keV
- The S-factor for $^{6}$Li($^{3}$He,$d$)$^{7}$Be(3/2$^-$) is approximately seven times greater than that for $^{6}$Li($^{3}$H,$d$)$^{7}$Li(3/2$^-$)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic three-cluster model previously applied to study the resonance structure of $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B and reactions induced by deuterons with $^{7}$Li and $^{7}$Be (Lashko et al., Phys. Rev. C 109, 045803, 2024). The calculations address the cosmological lithium problem by providing theoretical predictions for reactions relevant to $^{7}$Li synthesis and $^{6}$Li burning in the early Universe at low energies below 100 keV. The results enable comparison with existing experimental data from Schiffer et al. (1956), Gould and Boyce (1976), and Elwyn et al. (1980), and can guide future experimental efforts to measure astrophysical S-factors for these reactions, which are currently unavailable.
