# A precise determination of T_c in QCD from scaling

> Existing lattice data on the QCD phase transition are analyzed in renormalized perturbation theory. In quenched QCD it is found that T_c scales for lattices with only 3 time slices, and that T_c/Lambda_msbar=1.15 \pm 0.05. A preliminary estimate in QCD with two flavours of dynamical quarks shows that this ratio depends on the quark mass. For realistic quark masses we estimate T_c/Lambda_msbar=0.49 \pm 0.02. We also investigate the equation of state in quenched QCD at 1-loop order in renormalised perturbation theory.

# A precise determination of \(T_c\) in QCD from scaling

## Key claim
Existing lattice data on the QCD phase transition, when analyzed in renormalized perturbation theory rather than using the bare lattice coupling, show that \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\) is constant down to lattices with only 3 time slices in quenched QCD, and that the same ratio scales well for QCD with two flavours of dynamical quarks at fixed \(m/T_c\), provided the renormalised coupling is used to set the scale.

## Method
The author extracted the renormalised coupling \(\alpha_V\) from published plaquette values at critical bare couplings \(\beta_c\) using the V-scheme and E-scheme definitions, then used the two-loop beta function to compute \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\) via the relation \(a\Lambda = k R(1/4\pi\beta_0\alpha_V)\). For quenched QCD, critical couplings for \(3 \le N_t \le 14\) from later studies that took the thermodynamic limit were used. For two-flavour QCD, plaquette values at fixed \(m/T_c = 0.1\) from staggered quark simulations were interpolated to extract \(\alpha_V\) and set the scale.

## Key numbers
- Quenched QCD: \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} = 1.15 \pm 0.05\) (includes statistical and systematic uncertainties from scheme dependence and fits with \(N_t \ge 3\) or 4).
- Quenched QCD: converting using \(\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} \approx 250\) MeV gives \(T_c \approx 285 \pm 10\) MeV.
- Two-flavour QCD: extrapolation to physical \(M_\rho/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}^{(3)}\) gives \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} = 0.487 \pm 0.023\).
- Two-flavour QCD: \(T_c = 167 \pm 9 {}^{+15}_{-14}\) MeV (first error from extrapolation, second from uncertainty in \(\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}^{(3)} = 343^{+31}_{-28}\) MeV).
- At \(4T_c\) in quenched QCD, the energy density is about 15% lower and the pressure about 19% lower than the ideal gas values.
- Speed of sound squared in quenched QCD: \(c_s^2 \approx 0.1 \pm 0.025\) at \(1.5T_c\), rising to consistent with \(1/3\) at \(4T_c\).
- For two-flavour QCD, dimensional reduction is estimated to be valid only for \(T > 6.5T_c\) (range \(6T_c\) to \(8T_c\) depending on scale choice).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Lepage (1997) and Brodsky (1998) in using renormalised couplings measured on coarse lattices to test continuum scaling, and directly analyzes the lattice data of Boyd (1996), Iwasaki (1997), and Jochen (1999) for quenched QCD, and of the MILC collaboration for two-flavour QCD. It contradicts the common practice of using the bare coupling \(6/\beta\) to set the scale, which shows strong scaling violations, and instead demonstrates that using \(\alpha_V\) yields constant \(T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}\) even on very coarse lattices. The result enables continuum-extracted thermodynamics (energy density, pressure, speed of sound) from coarse lattices using 1-loop renormalised perturbation theory, and provides a precise \(T_c\) in physical units for two-flavour QCD that is consistent with the later estimate using \(O(4)\) critical indices by the CP-PACS collaboration (2000). The analysis also quantifies the temperature range where dimensional reduction is reliable for two-flavour QCD.
