# Rotating solutions of nonideal transverse Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics and the anomalous Hall current

> In order to gain deeper insight into the physics of the novel rotating solution of nonideal transverse magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), presented in one of our recent works, we replace the previously considered Maxwell theory with the ${\cal{CP}}$ violating Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) theory. In this way, dissipationless chiral magnetic (CM) and anomalous Hall (AH) currents appear in the MCS equation of motion, that, together with equations of relativistic hydrodynamics, builds the set of constitutive equations of the nonideal transverse Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics (CSMHD). We are, in particular, interested in the effect of these currents on the evolution of electromagnetic fields in a uniformly and longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma with chirality imbalance. Combining the constitutive equations of CSMHD under these assumptions, we arrive, as expected, at two distinct rotating and nonrotating solutions for electromagnetic fields. The rotation occurs with increasing rapidity and a constant angular velocity $ω_{0}$. Remarkably, the relative angle between the electric and magnetic fields, $δ$, turns out to be given by the coefficient of AH current $κ_{E}$ and the electric conductivity of the medium $σ$, as $δ=\tan^{-1}(κ_{E}/σ)$. Whereas the nonrotating solution implies the AH coefficient to be vanishing, and thus nonrotating electric and magnetic fields to be either parallel or antiparallel, the relative orientation of rotating electric and magnetic fields and the evolution of the CM conductivity $κ_{B}$ are strongly affected by nonvanishing $κ_{E}$. We explore the effect of positive and negative $ω_{0}$ on the evolution of the CM current, and show, in particular, that a rotation of electromagnetic fields with negative $ω_{0}$ implies a sign flip of the CM current in a chiral fluid with nonvanishing AH current.

## Key claim

The relative angle between electric and magnetic fields in a rotating solution of nonideal transverse Chern-Simons magnetohydrodynamics is determined by the ratio of the anomalous Hall current coefficient to the electric conductivity, \(\delta = \tan^{-1}(\kappa_E / \sigma)\), and this angle evolves with proper time when the Hall coefficient is nonvanishing, in contrast to the authors' prior work where \(\delta\) was constant.

## Method

The authors replace the Maxwell theory used in their previous work (Shokri-1) with the CP-violating Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) theory, introducing dissipationless chiral magnetic and anomalous Hall currents into the MCS equation of motion. They combine these MCS equations with relativistic hydrodynamics for a uniformly and longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma with chirality imbalance, assuming translational symmetry in the transverse plane and using Milne coordinates \((\tau, \eta)\). The constitutive equations are solved analytically and numerically to obtain rotating and nonrotating solutions for the electromagnetic fields, with the rotation occurring at constant angular velocity \(\omega_0\) in the rapidity direction.

## Key numbers

* The relative angle between electric and magnetic fields is given by \(\delta = \tan^{-1}(\kappa_E / \sigma)\), where \(\kappa_E\) is the anomalous Hall current coefficient and \(\sigma\) is the electric conductivity.
* For nonvanishing \(\alpha_E \equiv \kappa_E / \sigma\), the Hall coefficient evolves as \(\kappa_E \propto \tau^{-1}\).
* The magnetic field magnitude evolves as \(B \propto \tau^{-1} \exp(\mathcal{M})\), where \(\mathcal{M}\) can be analytically determined when \(\alpha_E \neq 0\).
* A rotation of electromagnetic fields with negative \(\omega_0\) implies a sign flip of the chiral magnetic current in a chiral fluid with nonvanishing anomalous Hall current.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Shokri-1) of nonideal transverse magnetohydrodynamics by incorporating the Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory, which introduces anomalous chiral magnetic and Hall currents via the axionlike field \(\vartheta\). The results contradict the earlier finding that the relative angle \(\delta\) between electric and magnetic fields is constant in proper time and rapidity; here, \(\delta\) evolves with \(\tau\) when the anomalous Hall coefficient is nonzero. The framework enables determination of the angular velocity \(\omega_0\) as a function of initial parameters (unlike the prior work where it was free) and allows analytic computation of the axial chemical potential \(\mu_5\) evolution, which is relevant to axial charge production in the quark-gluon plasma as studied by Tanji (2018).
