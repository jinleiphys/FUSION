> The eigenphase shifts and mixing parameters for elastic p-d scattering below the deuteron-breakup threshold are calculated for the AV14 two-body potential using two different numerical methods. # Phase Shifts and Mixing Parameters for Elastic Proton-Deuteron Scattering

## Key claim

The eigenphase shifts and mixing parameters for elastic p-d scattering below the deuteron-breakup threshold, calculated using the AV14 two-body potential, agree to better than 1% between two independent numerical methods, establishing that accurate p-d scattering calculations are possible at low energies. The authors hedge that these results serve as benchmarks for future calculations, but note that the effects of three-nucleon forces on scattering observables are generally small at these energies.

## Method

Two configuration-space methods were compared: the Pisa group used the Pair-correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis with the complex Kohn variational principle to obtain the S-matrix, while the Los Alamos-Iowa group solved the Faddeev-Noyes equations using a spline expansion with boundary conditions appropriate to two-body Coulomb scattering in the asymptotic region. Both calculations used the Argonne AV14 two-body potential with \(\hbar^2/M = 41.47\) MeV·fm\(^2\), and the phase shifts and mixing parameters were reported in the same conventions as a prior n-d benchmark study.

## Context

This work extends prior benchmark calculations for n-d scattering below the breakup threshold using realistic NN potentials, specifically the n-d benchmark at \(E_{lab}=3\) MeV published by the same community (nd-benchmark, 2000). It addresses the lack of a thorough accuracy study for p-d scattering, where the Coulomb interaction cannot be neglected, and provides a reference for future calculations. The methods can be extended above the deuteron-breakup threshold, with preliminary calculations already published (pd-breakup).
