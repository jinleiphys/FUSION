# Collective flow and long-range correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Making use of recently released data on dihadron correlations by the STAR collaboration, I analyze the long-range ("ridge-like") part of these data and show that the dependence on both transverse momentum as well as orientation with respect to the event plane are consistent with correlations expected from only collective flow. In combination with previously analyzed centrality-dependent data, they provide strong evidence that only collective flow effects are present at large relative pseudorapidy. In contrast, by analyzing a "background subtracted" signal, the authors presenting the new data concluded that the ridge-like part of the measured correlation could not in fact be entirely generated from collective flow of the medium. I explain the discrepancy and illustrate some pitfalls of using the ZYAM prescription to remove flow background.

# Collective flow and long-range correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The long-range ("ridge-like") dihadron correlations measured by STAR are entirely consistent with being generated solely by collective flow effects, contrary to the STAR collaboration's own conclusion that a non-flow component must exist. The discrepancy arises because the ZYAM background subtraction procedure used by STAR produces misleading results when the assumptions of that method do not hold, particularly when odd harmonics and flow fluctuations are not properly accounted for.

## Method
The author performs a Fourier decomposition of the unsubtracted dihadron correlation data at large relative pseudorapidity (|Δη|>0.7) from 200 A*GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC, extracting the first four harmonics V₁Δ, V₂Δ, V₃Δ, and V₄Δ. Each harmonic is compared to the expected contribution from collective flow: V₂Δ from elliptic flow, V₃Δ from triangular flow, V₄Δ from quadrangular flow, and V₁Δ from a combination of directed flow and global momentum conservation. The author then demonstrates how applying the ZYAM subtraction procedure to the same Fourier components produces spurious angular-dependent structures even when only flow contributions are present.

## Key numbers
- Trigger particle transverse momentum ranges: 3–4 GeV and 4–6 GeV, with associated particles in various pₜ bins
- Centrality range analyzed: 20–60%
- Long-range correlations defined by projection on |Δη|>0.7
- The extracted V₃Δ shows no systematic dependence on event plane orientation, consistent with triangular flow being uncorrelated with the event plane
- Comparing normalizations between trigger pₜ 3–4 GeV and 4–6 GeV implies v₃ turns over such that the value for 4–6 GeV is approximately 10% lower than at 3–4 GeV
- The pₜ-differential v₃ from viscous hydrodynamic calculations with η/s = 1/(4π) and Glauber initial conditions, multiplied by 0.09 (representing estimated v₃ of the 3–4 GeV trigger), matches the data

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Alver and collaborators (2010) that long-range correlations could be entirely explained by collective flow including triangular flow from event-by-event fluctuations, and the hydrodynamic calculations by Alver and collaborators (2010) showing that V₃Δ centrality dependence matches triangular flow predictions. It directly contradicts the conclusion of the STAR collaboration (Agakishiev, 2010) that the ridge-like correlation could not be entirely generated from collective flow. The analysis enables a cleaner approach to studying non-flow effects: using the long-range correlation itself as the best measurement of flow, then subtracting it from short-range correlations to study jet-like signals without relying on the ZYAM assumption, similar to what STAR did for their jet-like correlation analysis.
