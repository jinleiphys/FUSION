# Disentangling the pair and quartet condensates

> We study the nontrivial interplay of the well known \emph{pairing} and the more complex \emph{quarteting} correlations in the particular case of $N>Z$ atomic nuclei. Within the new Analytical Disentangled Condensate model, by implementing the notion of \emph{fractional degeneracy} we obtain the clear physical picture of a rather weakly interacting mixture of quartet and neutron pair condensates which mainly feel each other's influence through Pauli blocking. The basic idea of our approach may be generalized in order to scrutinize the extent to which similar manifestations are present in various many-body systems.

# Disentangling the pair and quartet condensates

## Key claim
The ground state of neutron-rich (N>Z) nuclei can be described as a weakly interacting mixture of alpha-like quartet condensates and neutron pair condensates, where the two subsystems interact primarily through Pauli blocking rather than direct dynamical coupling. The authors introduce the Analytical Disentangled Condensate (ADC) model which treats the two condensates as separable except for self-consistently determined effective fractional degeneracies that encode the Pauli blocking between them.

## Method
The authors construct a variational wavefunction as a product of a quartet condensate (built from collective isovector Cooper pairs coupled to total isospin T=0) and a neutron pair condensate (described by number-projected BCS), with mixing amplitudes determined by energy minimization. They implement the Pauli blocking between the two subsystems by introducing the concept of fractional degeneracies: the effective degeneracy available to quartets in each single-particle level is reduced by the average occupation of that level by neutron pairs, and vice versa, solved self-consistently. The analytical expressions for norms and Hamiltonian averages are derived using symbolic computation with the Cadabra2 computer algebra system, enabling efficient evaluation for systems with multiple quartets and neutron pairs.

## Context
This work extends the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) framework developed by Sandulescu et al. (2012) and Negrea et al. (2017, 2018) for describing four-body correlations in N=Z and N>Z nuclei, and the analytical method for pairing and quarteting correlations (2018). The ADC model provides a computationally simpler alternative to the full QCM while clarifying the physical picture of how quartet and pair condensates coexist. The approach enables future generalization to excited states, combined isovector-isoscalar pairing, and a boson formalism description of quartet correlations, as well as potential connections to Richardson equation solutions with fractional degeneracies.
