# High precision measurement of the $^{99}$Tc $β$ spectrum

> Highly precise measurements of the $^{99}$Tc beta spectrum were performed in two laboratories using metallic magnetic calorimeters. Independent sample preparations, evaluation methods and analyses yield consistent results and the spectrum could be measured down to less than 1 keV. Consistent beta spectra were also obtained via cross-evaluations of the experimental data sets. An additional independent measurement with silicon detectors in a $4π$ configuration confirms the spectrum shape above 25 keV. Detailed theoretical calculations were performed including nuclear structure and atomic effects. The spectrum shape was found to be sensitive to the effective value of the axial-vector coupling constant. Combining measurements and predictions, we extracted $Q_β=$295.82(16) keV and $g_A^{\text{eff}} = 1.530 (83)$. Furthermore, we derived the mean energy of the beta spectrum $\overline{E}_β$=98.45(20) keV, $\log f = -0.47660 (22)$ and $\log ft = 12.3478 (23)$.

## Key claim

The beta spectrum of 99Tc is sensitive to the effective value of the axial-vector coupling constant gA, and by combining high-precision measurements with detailed theoretical calculations the authors extract gA_eff = 1.530 (83) and Qβ = 295.82 (16) keV, though the extracted values depend on the specific theoretical model used for nuclear structure and atomic effects.

## Method

Two independent metallic magnetic calorimeter (MMC) measurements were performed at LNHB and PTB, each with different source preparation methods (electrodeposition vs. drop deposition), calibration sources (133Ba vs. 57Co), and analysis codes (MATLAB vs. Python). The raw data were exchanged between laboratories for cross-analysis to identify systematic differences. An additional independent measurement with passivated implanted planar silicon (PIPS) detectors in a 4π configuration confirmed the spectrum shape above 25 keV. Detailed theoretical calculations including nuclear structure and atomic effects were performed and fitted to the combined experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Qβ = 295.82 (16) keV, extracted from the combined fit of measurements and theoretical predictions
- gA_eff = 1.530 (83), the effective axial-vector coupling constant extracted from the spectrum shape
- Mean beta energy Eβ_bar = 98.45 (20) keV
- log f = -0.47660 (22) and log ft = 12.3478 (23)
- Energy threshold down to less than 1 keV achieved with MMC detectors
- Energy resolution of approximately 100 eV (FWHM) up to 384 keV for the LNHB MMC measurement
- Energy resolution of 72 eV at 136.47 keV for the PTB MMC measurement (set 2)
- 7,264,451 events in the final LNHB MMC spectrum and 5,326,682 events in the PTB MMC spectrum

## Context

This work extends the MetroBeta project measurements (Loidl, 2018) and provides significantly improved precision over earlier 99Tc beta spectrum measurements from the 1950s-70s using magnetic (Feldman, 1952; Taimuty, 1951), scintillation (Sny, 1966), and semiconductor (Reich, 1974) spectrometers, which had energy thresholds above 50 keV. The spectrum shape sensitivity to gA was predicted by Kostensalo (2017). The improved Q-value and mean beta energy are relevant for decay heat calculations from spent nuclear fuel, where 99Tc was identified as the most significant contributing radionuclide over 1 to 100,000 years (Doran, 2022).
