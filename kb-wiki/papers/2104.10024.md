# Spin scissors or spin-flip? (comment on the paper in arXiv:2102.13580v1 [nucl-th], 2021)

## Key claim

The terms "spin scissors" and "spin-flip" are different names for the same physical phenomenon, arising from the spin-orbital potential; the apparent contradiction between the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) descriptions is a matter of language and method, not physics. The author argues that the claim by Nest (2021) that spin scissors resonance (SSR) states are actually ordinary non-collective spin-flip excitations is a misinterpretation stemming from different theoretical frameworks.

## Method

The author compares the WFM method (solving TDHFB equations using phase-space moments such as quadrupole moments and angular momenta) with the QRPA method (using particle-hole transitions) for the same nuclear systems, specifically the deformed isotopes 160,162,164Dy and 232Th. Current distributions for each excited state are computed in the WFM approach and decomposed into contributions from conventional orbital scissors, simple spin scissors (spin-up vs. spin-down nucleons), and complicate spin scissors (spin-up protons + spin-down neutrons vs. spin-down protons + spin-up neutrons). The summed M1 strengths and energy distributions from both methods are compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For 164Dy, WFM gives three low-lying magnetic states: E1 = 2.20 MeV with B1(M1) = 1.76 mu_N^2, E2 = 2.87 MeV with B2(M1) = 2.24 mu_N^2, E3 = 3.59 MeV with B3(M1) = 1.56 mu_N^2.
* For 164Dy, the summed M1 strength from WFM is 5.56 mu_N^2 (0-4 MeV), compared to the experimental value of 5.52 mu_N^2.
* For 232Th, WFM gives three low-lying states: E1 = 1.53 MeV with B1(M1) = 1.7 mu_N^2, E2 = 2.21 MeV with B2(M1) = 2.55 mu_N^2, E3 = 2.81 MeV with B3(M1) = 1.51 mu_N^2.
* For 232Th, the summed M1 strength of the two higher WFM levels is 4.07 mu_N^2 with centroid 2.43 MeV, compared to experimental values of 4.26 mu_N^2 and 2.49 MeV respectively.
* The 2.20 MeV state in 164Dy is 51% complicate spin scissors and 47% simple spin scissors; the 2.87 MeV state is 54% simple spin scissors with 32% conventional scissors; the 3.59 MeV state is 62% conventional scissors with 31% complicate spin scissors.
* For 232Th, the 1.53 MeV state is approximately 49% simple and 48% complicate spin scissors; the 2.21 MeV state is 48% simple spin scissors with 36% conventional scissors; the 2.81 MeV state is 60% conventional scissors with 31% complicate spin scissors.

## Context

This comment directly responds to the QRPA calculations of Nest (2021), who claimed that the spin scissors resonance predicted by the WFM method of Balbutsev and collaborators (BaMo, BM3S) is actually a set of ordinary non-collective spin-flip excitations. The author clarifies that the WFM method (developed by Balbutsev and collaborators) and the RPA method are complementary approaches describing the same physics, with spin scissors being the macroscopic interpretation of the microscopic spin-flip transitions driven by the spin-orbital potential. The work extends the earlier WFM predictions for spin scissors in deformed nuclei (BaMo, BM3S, PRC15) and demonstrates that current distributions and angular momentum decompositions can reveal the scissors-like nature of excitations even when RPA labels them as spin-flip. The analysis enables future reconciliation between macroscopic collective models and microscopic QRPA calculations for low-lying 1+ states in heavy deformed nuclei.
