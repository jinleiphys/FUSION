# Weak decay of hypernuclei - Theoretical status

> The physics of the weak decay of hypernuclei is briefly reviewed from a theoretical point of view. Special regard is devoted to the recent progress concerning the determination of the non--mesonic decay widths and the asymmetry parameters. While convincing evidence has been achieved for a solution of the long-standing puzzle on the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p, the discrepancies between theory and experiment on the decay asymmetries clearly highlight the exigence of dedicating further efforts in exploring new aspects of the dynamics underlying the non-mesonic weak decay.

## Key claim

The long-standing puzzle regarding the ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced non-mesonic decay widths (Gamma_n/Gamma_p) has been solved, with values around 0.3-0.4 now common to both theory and experiment for s- and p-shell hypernuclei; however, a new puzzle remains concerning the asymmetry of proton emission from polarized hypernuclei, where theoretical predictions disagree with recent experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations of hypernuclear weak decay using one-meson-exchange (OME) models, including pion, kaon, rho, omega, eta, and K* exchange, as well as direct quark (DQ) mechanisms and scalar-isoscalar sigma meson exchange. For the asymmetry problem, the author describes simulations of nucleon final state interactions (FSI) using an intranuclear cascade code to evaluate the observable asymmetry parameter a^M_Lambda from the intrinsic asymmetry a_Lambda. The analysis of nucleon-nucleon coincidence data from KEK experiments E462 and E508, combined with theoretical modeling of two-nucleon induced decays, provided the basis for resolving the Gamma_n/Gamma_p puzzle.

## Key numbers

- Free Lambda mesonic decay ratio: Gamma_pi-/Gamma_pi0 approximately 1.78, close to 2, suggesting the Delta I=1/2 rule
- Q-value for mesonic decay: Q_M approximately 40 MeV, corresponding to final nucleon momentum of about 100 MeV
- Final nucleon momenta in non-mesonic decay: approximately 420 MeV for one-nucleon induced, 340 MeV for two-nucleon induced channels
- OPE model predictions for Gamma_n/Gamma_p: 0.09 for ^5_Lambda He, 0.08 for ^12_Lambda C
- OME model predictions for Gamma_n/Gamma_p: 0.34-0.46 for ^5_Lambda He, 0.29-0.34 for ^12_Lambda C
- KEK-E462 measured ratio N_nn/N_np for ^5_Lambda He: 0.45 +/- 0.11
- KEK-E508 measured ratio N_nn/N_np for ^12_Lambda C: 0.40 +/- 0.10
- Model-independent analysis yields Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.40 +/- 0.11 (1N) and 0.27 +/- 0.11 (1N+2N) for ^5_Lambda He
- Model-independent analysis yields Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.38 +/- 0.14 (1N) and 0.29 +/- 0.14 (1N+2N) for ^12_Lambda C
- Intrinsic asymmetry a_Lambda predicted by OME models: -0.68 for ^5_Lambda He, -0.73 for ^12_Lambda C
- Observable asymmetry a^M_Lambda with FSI at T_th_p=30 MeV: -0.46 for ^5_Lambda He, -0.37 for ^12_Lambda C
- KEK-E462 measured a^M_Lambda for ^5_Lambda He: 0.11 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.04 (Ma05) and 0.07 +/- 0.08^{+0.08}_{-0.00} (Ma06)
- KEK-E508 measured a^M_Lambda for ^12_Lambda C: -0.20 +/- 0.26 +/- 0.04 (Ma05) and -0.16 +/- 0.28^{+0.18}_{-0.00} (Ma06)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical work on hypernuclear weak decay summarized in earlier reviews (prep, Os98) and addresses the long-standing Gamma_n/Gamma_p puzzle that had persisted since the 1990s with conflicting data from BNL (Sz91) and KEK (No95, No95a). The solution was enabled by recent theoretical calculations (Sasaki 2000, Jido 2001, Parreno and Ramos 2001, Itonaga 2002, Garbarino 2003-2004, Bauer 2006) combined with new coincidence measurements from KEK-E462 and KEK-E508 (Outa, Kang 2006, Kim 2006). The remaining asymmetry puzzle, where OME and DQ models predict large negative a_Lambda values while experiments favor small values near zero, points to the need for further theoretical work incorporating scalar-isoscalar sigma meson exchange as suggested by effective field theory approaches (Parreno 2004). Future experiments at KEK, BNL, J-PARC, and FINUDA are expected to provide improved constraints on both Gamma_n/Gamma_p and the asymmetry parameters.
