# Soft Electromagnetic Radiations from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The production of low mass dileptons and soft photons from thermalized Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) and hadronic matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions is evaluated. A boost invariant longitudinal and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of the systems created in central collision of lead nuclei at CERN SPS, BNL RHIC, and CERN LHC, and undergoing a first order phase transition to hadronic matter is considered. A large production of low mass (M< 0.3 GeV) dileptons, and soft photons (p_T< 0.4 GeV) is seen to emanate from the bremsstrahlung of quarks and pions. We find an increase by a factor of 2--4 in the low mass dilepton and soft photon yield as we move from SPS to RHIC energies, and an increase by an order of magnitude as we move from SPS to LHC energies. Most of the soft radiations are found to originate from pion driven processes at SPS and RHIC energies, while at the LHC energies the quark and the pion driven processes contribute by a similar amount. The study of the transverse mass distribution is seen to provide interesting details of the evolution. We also find a unique universal behaviour for the ratio of M^2 weighted transverse mass distribution for M= 0.1 GeV to that for M= 0.2 and 0.3 GeV, as a function of M_T, for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, in the absence of transverse expansion of the system. A deviation from this universal behaviour is seen as a clear indication of the flow.

# Soft Electromagnetic Radiations from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The production of low mass dileptons (M < 0.3 GeV) and soft photons (p_T < 0.4 GeV) from bremsstrahlung of quarks and pions in thermalized quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter is evaluated, with the hedge that the soft-photon approximation employed is reliable to within a factor of 2, and that the results assume a first-order phase transition with initial thermalization at tau_i = 1 fm/c, which may overestimate yields if Landau-Pomeranchuk suppression is significant at early times.

## Method

The authors used a soft-photon approximation to compute bremsstrahlung production of low-mass dileptons and soft photons from quark and pion scattering processes in a boost-invariant longitudinally expanding and cylindrically symmetric transversely expanding fireball. The space-time evolution assumed a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter at T = 160 MeV, with freeze-out at T = 140 MeV, for central collisions of lead nuclei at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The rates were convoluted over the space-time history using initial conditions derived from particle rapidity densities of 624, 1735, and 5624 respectively, with initial time tau_i = 1 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Particle rapidity densities used: 624 (SPS), 1735 (RHIC), and 5624 (LHC)
- Phase transition temperature: 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature: 140 MeV
- Increase by a factor of 2-4 in low mass dilepton and soft photon yield from SPS to RHIC energies
- Increase by an order of magnitude (factor of 15-20 for dileptons) from SPS to LHC energies
- Soft photon yield at p_T = 200 MeV increases by factor of 2-4 from SPS to RHIC, and by factor of almost 10 from SPS to LHC
- Soft-photon approximation reliable to within a factor of 2 for M <= 0.3 GeV
- For M <= 0.1 GeV, soft-photon approximation gives results very close to QCD perturbation theory; for 0.1 <= M <= 0.3 GeV, results smaller by factor of 1-4
- Pion form factor decreases by about 30% and rho meson decay width increases by factor of 3 at T = 160 MeV due to chiral symmetry restoration effects
- Collision broadening of rho meson decay width: increase of about 80% at T = 160 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Pal et al., 1996) on soft electromagnetic radiations from heavy ion collisions, and builds upon the soft-photon approximation framework developed by Ruckl (1976) and critically examined by Lichard (1995). The results complement calculations of large mass dileptons and high p_T photons from QGP by Kapusta (1989) and others, and address the low-mass region populated by bremsstrahlung processes rather than annihilation. The universal behavior of the M^2-weighted transverse mass distribution ratio in the absence of transverse flow provides a new diagnostic for flow effects, and the results enable experimental comparisons with CERES data (1995) for S+Au collisions and predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC.
