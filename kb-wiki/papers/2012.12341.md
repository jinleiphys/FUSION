# Confinement and Graded Partition Functions for $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM

> Gauge theories with confining phases at low temperatures tend to deconfine at high temperatures. In some cases, for example in supersymmetric theories, confinement can persist for all temperatures provided the partition function includes a grading by $(-1)^F$. When it is possible to define partition functions which smoothly interpolate between no grading and $(-1)^F$ grading, it is natural to ask if there are other choices of grading that have the same effect as $(-1)^F$ on confinement. We explore how this works for $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM on $S^1\times S^3$ in the large $N$ limit at both small and large coupling. We find evidence for a continuous range of grading parameters that preserve confinement for all temperatures at large coupling, while at small coupling only a discrete set of gradings preserves confinement.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in large-\(N\) \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM on \(S^1 \times S^3\), a continuous range of grading parameters \(\theta\) preserves confinement for all temperatures at large 't Hooft coupling, while at small coupling only a discrete set of gradings (\(\theta = \pi/3, \pi, 5\pi/3\)) does so, with the intermediate region exhibiting partial deconfinement.

## Method

The authors study the large-\(N\) partition function of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM on \(S^3_R \times S^1_\beta\) with a grading factor \(e^{3i\theta r}\) that interpolates between the thermal partition function (\(\theta=0\)) and the \((-1)^F\)-graded partition function (\(\theta=\pi\)). At zero coupling, they compute the effective action for the Polyakov loop eigenvalues from single-particle partition functions and determine the phase boundaries from the sign of the Fourier coefficients \(V_n\). At infinite coupling, they use the AdS/CFT correspondence to map the problem to a Reissner-Nordstrom-AdS\(_5\) black hole in Einstein-Maxwell theory, analytically continuing the chemical potential to imaginary values, and compute the Hawking-Page transition temperature from the free energy difference between thermal AdS and the black hole.

## Key numbers

* At zero coupling, the theory confines for all \(\beta/R\) at \(\theta = \pi/3, \pi, 5\pi/3\); for \(\theta \in (\pi/3,\pi)\cup(\pi,5\pi/3)\) the system enters a partially center-broken phase at sufficiently small \(\beta/R\).
* At infinite coupling, the critical inverse temperature for deconfinement is \(\beta_c(\theta) = (2\pi R/3)\sqrt{1 - (\theta/(\pi/3))^2}\), giving \(\beta_c = 2\pi R/3\) at \(\theta=0\) and diverging as \(\theta \to \pi/3\) mod \(2\pi\).
* At infinite coupling, the theory remains confined for all \(\beta/R\) for \(\theta \in [\pi/3, 5\pi/3]\), a continuous window of grading parameters.

## Context

This work extends the known result from Witten (1998) that \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM with a \((-1)^F\) grading (\(\theta=\pi\)) confines at all temperatures at large coupling. It also connects to recent studies of deconfinement in the superconformal index, where Copetti et al. (2020) found that the grading \((-1)^F e^{\pm 2\pi i r}\) similarly marks the boundary between confinement and deconfinement. The results enable further investigation into whether partially-confined phases at small coupling have gravitational duals at large coupling, and whether new black objects corresponding to such phases exist within supergravity.
