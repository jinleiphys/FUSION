# Inverse mass ordering of light scalar mesons in the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model

> The masses of the low-lying scalar mesons are investigated in the three-flavor Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by treating the scalar mesons as composite objects of a quark and an antiquark. It is known that a simple $\bar qq$ picture fails to reproduce so-called inverse mass ordering for the scalar mesons. Recently a new mechanism to reproduce the observed mass spectrum of the scalar mesons was proposed in a linear sigma model by introducing flavor symmetry breaking induced by the U(1) axial anomaly. Motivated by this proposal, we examine whether this new mechanism works also in the NJL model. By calculating the scalar meson masses, we find that the NJL model reproduces the observed mass ordering with sufficient strength of the new term. With this mechanism, it turns out that the constituent strange quark mass gets degenerate to that of the up and down quark if the inverse mass ordering is reproduced. We also discuss the scalar diquark masses to check the consistency of the degeneracy of the constituent quark masses with the light baryon masses.

## Key claim
The Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can reproduce the observed inverse mass ordering of light scalar mesons (where the non-strange a0(980) is heavier than the strange K0*(700)) only if a new U(1) axial anomaly induced term with explicit flavor symmetry breaking via the current quark mass is introduced with sufficient strength, and this mechanism forces the constituent strange quark mass to become nearly degenerate with the light quark mass.

## Method
The authors employ a three-flavor NJL model with a cutoff regularization at Lambda = 650 MeV, adding a g_k term (a four-point interaction derived from the U(1) axial anomaly that includes the current quark mass matrix) to the standard four-point scalar-pseudoscalar interaction and the six-point Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction. They solve the gap equations for constituent quark masses in the mean-field approximation, then calculate scalar and pseudoscalar meson masses as poles of the quark-antiquark scattering T-matrix in the ladder approximation using the Bethe-Salpeter equation. Model parameters (m_q, m_s, G_S, G_D) are fitted for each value of the dimensionless coupling G_k to reproduce the observed masses of pi, K, and eta' and the pion decay constant.

## Key numbers
* For G_k = 2.0, the real part of the a0 meson mass is 912 MeV and the real part of the kappa meson mass is 832 MeV, reproducing the inverse mass ordering (a0 heavier than kappa).
* For G_k = 0.0 (original NJL model without the new term), the a0 mass is 844 MeV and the kappa mass is 970 MeV, showing normal mass ordering (kappa heavier than a0).
* The constituent strange quark mass M_s decreases from 475 MeV at G_k = 0.0 to 338 MeV at G_k = 2.0, while the light constituent quark mass M_q remains fixed at 309 MeV.
* The sigma meson mass remains nearly constant at approximately 581-585 MeV across all G_k values.
* The current quark mass ratio m_s/m_q varies from 25.35 at G_k = 0.0 to 27.17 at G_k = 2.0, consistent with the PDG value of 27.33.

## Context
This work extends the mechanism proposed by KHMJ (2022) in a linear sigma model, where a U(1) axial anomaly induced term with the current quark mass was introduced to reproduce the inverse mass ordering of scalar mesons. Previous attempts in the NJL model with only the KMT interaction (Naito 2002) could not generate sufficient flavor symmetry breaking in the quark condensates to produce the inverse ordering, while Ishida (1999) achieved it in a linear sigma model only by introducing unsatisfactorily strong flavor symmetry breaking on meson decay constants. The present result enables further study of whether the predicted degeneracy of constituent quark masses is consistent with light baryon masses through scalar diquark calculations.
