# Particle-Antiparticle Asymmetries of $Λ$ Production in Hadron-Nucleon Collisions

> The particle-antiparticle asymmetries of $Λ$ production in 250 GeV/c $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, and $p$ --nucleon collisions are studied with two model parametrizations of quark to $Λ$ fragmentation functions. It is shown that the available data can be qualitatively explained by the calculated results in both the quark-diquark model and a pQCD based analysis of fragmentation functions. The differences in the two model predictions are significant for $K^{\pm}$ beams, and high precision measurements of the asymmetries with detailed $x_F$ and $P_T$ information can discriminate between different predictions.

## Key claim

The available E769 data on Lambda-antilambda asymmetries in hadron-nucleon collisions at 250 GeV/c can be qualitatively explained by both the quark-diquark model and a pQCD-based analysis of fragmentation functions, but the two models give significantly different predictions for kaon beams at large x_F, meaning that high-precision measurements with detailed x_F and P_T cuts could discriminate between them.

## Method

The authors calculated Lambda-antilambda production asymmetries in 250 GeV/c pion, kaon, and proton beams on nucleon targets using the collinear factorization framework with parton distribution functions from the GRV parametrization and subprocess cross sections from perturbative QCD. They constructed two sets of quark-to-Lambda fragmentation functions by relating them to Lambda quark distributions via the Gribov-Lipatov relation, using either the quark-diquark model or a pQCD-based analysis for the valence quark flavor structure, with sea quark contributions taken from SU(3) symmetry. The asymmetries were computed as functions of x_F and P_T^2 over the experimental ranges, with three options for the relative strength of unfavored fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: 250 GeV/c for all beams (pion, kaon, proton)
- x_F range for positively charged beams: -0.12 to 0.12
- x_F range for negatively charged beams: -0.12 to 0.4
- P_T^2 range for positively charged beams: 0 to 3 (GeV/c)^2
- P_T^2 range for negatively charged beams: 0 to 10 (GeV/c)^2
- The quark-diquark model predicts u^Lambda(x)/s^Lambda(x) → 0 at x → 1, while the pQCD-based analysis predicts u^Lambda(x)/s^Lambda(x) → 1/2
- The pQCD-based analysis predicts d(x)/u(x) → 1/5 at x → 1 for the proton

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ma, Schmidt, and Yang (2000) on the flavor structure of Lambda quark distributions, and by the same authors (2002) on relating fragmentation functions to distribution functions via SU(3) symmetry. The paper uses data from the Fermilab E769 Collaboration and compares two competing theoretical approaches: the quark-diquark model (Feynman 1972) and a pQCD-based analysis (Farrar 1975, Brodsky 1995). The results enable future experiments to discriminate between these models through high-precision measurements of Lambda-antilambda asymmetries with detailed kinematic cuts, particularly for kaon beams at large x_F.
