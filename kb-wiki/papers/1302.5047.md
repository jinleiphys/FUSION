# Parity Violating Deep Inelastic Electron-Deuteron Scattering: Higher Twist and Parton Angular Momentum

> We study the effect of parton angular momentum on the twist-four correction to the left-right asymmetry in the electron-deuteron parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS). We show that this higher-twist correction is transparent to the dynamics of parton angular momentum needed to account for the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions and spin-independent parton distribution functions. A sufficiently precise measurement of the PVDIS asymmetry may, thus, provide additional information about the parton dynamics responsible for nucleon spin.

## Key claim

The twist-four correction to the left-right asymmetry in electron-deuteron parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) is largely transparent to the parton angular momentum dynamics that generate the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions, because contributions from quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) components with \(l_z = +1\) and \(l_z = -1\) largely cancel each other for \(x_B \gsim 0.4\), a cancellation that appears generic and independent of the detailed model for the light-cone amplitudes.

## Method

The authors computed the twist-four correction \(R_1^{HT}\) to the PVDIS asymmetry using an operator product expansion (OPE) framework, expressing the correction in terms of a deuteron twist-four distribution function \(\tilde{Q}_D(x_B)\). They modeled the nucleon state using light-cone wavefunctions with three valence quarks including all possible quark OAM components (\(l_z = 0, \pm1, 2\)), adopting the specific functional forms derived from a constituent quark model via a Melosh rotation as in Pasquini (2008). The matrix elements for the twist-four distribution and parton distribution functions (PDFs) were evaluated using Monte Carlo integration (Divonne from the CUBA Library), with the deuteron treated via an incoherent impulse approximation relating its hadronic tensor to those of the proton and neutron.

## Key numbers

* The maximum absolute value of the twist-four correction is \(|R_1^{HT}| \approx 2.6 \times 10^{-3}\) between \(0.2 < x_B' < 0.7\) at \(Q^2 = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* The SoLID experiment at Jefferson Laboratory is expected to measure the left-right asymmetry with 0.5% precision over the kinematic range \(0.3 < x_B < 0.7\).
* The strong coupling constant was taken as \(\alpha_s = 0.5\) at 1 GeV, following a 4-loop renormalization group running with quark thresholds at \(M_c = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(M_b = 4.7\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Previous model predictions for the maximum \(R_1^{HT}\) at \(Q^2 = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) ranged from \(0.003\) to \(0.005\) over \(0.2 < x_B < 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of higher-twist corrections to \(e\)D PVDIS by Castorina (1985), Sonny (2001), Belitsky (2002), and Hobbs (2010), which used the MIT bag model or isotropic light-cone wavefunctions with zero parton OAM. The key finding, that the twist-four correction is insensitive to the quark OAM responsible for the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), as studied by Pasquini (2008) and Prokudin (2009), implies that a precise measurement of \(R_1^{HT}\) could isolate other aspects of parton angular momentum or higher Fock-space components (e.g., 3-quark+1-gluon states) not captured by the present model.
