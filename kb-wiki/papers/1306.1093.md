# Novel Monte Carlo representation for shell model in the complex energy plane

> A Monte Carlo method is presented to evaluate quantum states with many particles moving in the continuum. The scattering state is generated at each time by a Monte Carlo random sampling algorithm. The same calculation are repeated until the average energies of all calculations converge. For systems with one and a few particles, our calculations show that the exact solution can be approached with around only one hundred iterations. As examples, we apply the approach to study the structure of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes.

# Novel Monte Carlo representation for shell model in the complex energy plane

## Key claim
A Monte Carlo method can evaluate quantum many-body states in the continuum by randomly sampling only a few scattering states per iteration, converging to the exact complex-energy shell-model solution with around one hundred iterations for one- and two-particle systems, but the accuracy for the imaginary part of the ground state in two-particle calculations can differ by 60 keV when the resonance lies close to the origin.

## Method
The authors replace the full set of discretized Berggren scattering states on a complex-energy contour with a Monte Carlo sampling: at each step, one scattering state is randomly selected on each segment of the contour, and a small Hamiltonian matrix (one bound state, one resonance, and three random scattering states) is diagonalized. The procedure is repeated thousands of times, and the final eigenvalue for each physical state is taken as the average over all individual runs. The method is applied to one-particle Woods-Saxon potentials for 208Pb, and to two- and three-particle systems in the neutron-rich oxygen isotopes 26O and 27O using a separable pairing interaction.

## Key numbers
- One-particle bound state in 208Pb: exact energy −12.526 MeV; Monte Carlo result after 50,000 runs −12.526 MeV with systematic error less than 1 keV.
- One-particle resonance in 208Pb: exact energy (4.322, −0.321) MeV; Monte Carlo result (4.324, −0.328) MeV with error about 3 keV.
- 26O ground state (0+1) from complex shell model (CXSM): 0.150 − i 0.000 MeV; Monte Carlo MC-I (strength G fitted to ground state): 0.150 − i 0.000 MeV; Monte Carlo MC-II (fixed G): 0.149 − i 0.060 MeV (imaginary part differs by 60 keV from CXSM).
- 26O first excited 0+ state from CXSM: 6.780 − i 0.515 MeV; MC-I: 6.779 − i 0.517 MeV; MC-II: 6.780 − i 0.519 MeV; difference below 2 keV.
- 27O ground state (3/2+1) from MC-I: 1.659 − i 0.113 MeV; from MC-II: 1.663 − i 0.138 MeV.
- 27O first excited state (7/2−) from MC-I: 4.605 − i 0.556 MeV; from MC-II: 4.604 − i 0.614 MeV.
- M-scheme dimension for 26O: 66 without continuum, 186,966 with full CXSM continuum, and 1,326 in the Monte Carlo representation.

## Context
This work extends the Berggren-representation shell model in the complex energy plane (Berggren 1968, Liotta 1996, Betan 2002, Michel 2002, Michel 2009) by replacing the large set of discretized scattering states with a Monte Carlo sampling, dramatically reducing the basis dimension. It is consistent with importance-truncation algorithms used in traditional configuration interaction approaches (Shimizu 2012, Shevchenko 2012, Bona 2013). The method enables calculations for neutron-rich oxygen isotopes (26O and 27O) that are unbound and previously difficult to treat with full complex shell model on modern computers, and it can be incorporated into other Monte Carlo truncation schemes generalized to the complex energy plane.
