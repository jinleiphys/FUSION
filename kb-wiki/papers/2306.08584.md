# Transverse momentum spectra of $f_0(980)$ from coalescence model

> We use a coalescence model to generate $f_{0}$(980) particles for four configurations: ${s\bar{s}}$ meson, ${u\bar{u}s\bar{s}}$ tetraquark, ${K^{+}K^{-}}$ molecule and $u\bar{u}$ p-wave state. The phase-space information of the coalescing constituents is taken from a multi-phase transport (AMPT) simulation of proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at the LHC. It is shown that the transverse momentum spectra and production yields of $f_0(980)$ differ significantly among the configurations. It is suggested that the $p_T$ spectra of the $f_0(980)$ compared to those of other hadrons (such as pion) and the ratio of the $f_0(980)$ $p_T$ spectra in pPb over pp can be exploited to tell the configuration of the $f_0(980)$.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra and production yields of the $f_0(980)$$ differ significantly among four possible configurations (${s\bar{s}}$ meson, ${u\bar{u}s\bar{s}}$ tetraquark, ${K^{+}K^{-}}$ molecule, and $u\bar{u}$ p-wave state), and these differences can be exploited to determine the $f_0(980)$ quark content, though the authors caution that their coalescence model is exploratory and not intended to provide quantitative predictions, especially given the questionable validity of coalescence in small collision systems like pp and pPb.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model with Gaussian Wigner functions to generate $f_0(980)$ particles from four different configurations, taking the phase-space information of (anti)quarks and kaons from the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model simulations of minimum-bias proton-proton (pp) and proton-lead (pPb) collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.0$ TeV. For each configuration, if the flavors of the coalescing constituents matched the configuration and a random number was smaller than the Wigner function value, the $f_0(980)$ was formed; the authors also checked the effect of including other hadron formation (such as $K^+$ and $\pi^+$) and found it small compared to the differences among configurations.

## Key numbers

* The yield of $f_0(980)$ with $s\bar{s}$ configuration was reduced by about 30% when including $K^+$ ($u\bar{s}$) formation, and by about 20% when including both $\pi^+$ ($u\bar{d}$) and $K^+$ formation, relative to the case with only $f_0(980)$ formation.
* The total $\pi^+$ yield from the coalescence calculation was 4.05 in pp collisions and 6.6947 in pPb collisions, compared to 2.68 from AMPT and 2.07 from ALICE data.
* The ratio of $f_0(980)$ yield over $\pi^+$ yield in pp collisions was 0.6558(2) for $u\bar{u}$ p-wave, 0.10632(5) for $s\bar{s}$, 0.05412(3) for $u\bar{u}s\bar{s}$ tetraquark, and 0.01698(2) for $K\bar{K}$ molecule.
* The ratio of $f_0(980)$ yield over $\pi^+$ yield in pPb collisions was 0.4342(1) for $u\bar{u}$ p-wave, 0.0481(3) for $s\bar{s}$, 0.0192(2) for $u\bar{u}s\bar{s}$ tetraquark, and 0.01016(1) for $K\bar{K}$ molecule.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coalescence study of $f_0(980)$ elliptic flow ($v_2$) and number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling (Gu, 2019) to transverse momentum spectra, using the same AMPT-based coalescence approach. It builds on earlier suggestions by Cho (2010) that $f_0(980)$ production yields and $p_T$ spectra can probe the $f_0(980)$ structure. The results enable experimental discrimination of the $f_0(980)$ configuration through measurements of $p_T$ spectra ratios (e.g., $f_0(980)/\pi$ in a single system, or pPb/pp ratios), complementing the NCQ scaling method; the authors note that the ALICE measurement of $f_0(980)$ $p_T$ spectra in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV (ALICE, 2022) could be used for comparison, though firm conclusions require more detailed studies.
