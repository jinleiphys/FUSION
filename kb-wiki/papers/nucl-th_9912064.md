# Nonextensive statistics in stellar plasma and solar neutrinos

> Nonextensive and quantum uncertainty effects (related to the quasiparticles composing the stellar core) have strong influence on the nuclear rates and, of course, affect solar neutrino fluxes. Both effects do coexist and are due to the frequent collisions among the ions. The weakly nonextensive nature of the solar core is confirmed. The range of predictions for the neutrino fluxes is enlarged and the solar neutrino problem becomes less dramatic.

# Nonextensive statistics in stellar plasma and solar neutrinos

## Key claim

The solar core is weakly nonextensive (entropic parameter q ≈ 0.989) and quantum uncertainty effects from frequent collisions coexist to modify nuclear reaction rates; these combined effects enlarge the range of predicted solar neutrino fluxes and make the solar neutrino problem "less dramatic," but the quantitative impact depends sensitively on the assumed value of the ³He abundance in the inner core.

## Method

The authors model the solar core as a weakly nonideal plasma where the Debye screening length is comparable to the interparticle distance and collision times are of order the plasma oscillation period. They compute nuclear reaction rates by averaging cross sections over a momentum distribution that includes two non-Maxwellian corrections: a quantum uncertainty tail (Q effect) arising from the finite width of the single-particle spectral function, and a weak nonextensive tail (q effect) from Tsallis statistics with parameter q ≈ 1. The rates are evaluated for the pp chain reactions and CNO cycle using the Gamow energy and the plasma parameter Γ, with the entropic deviation |1−q| derived from the strong Coulomb cross section and the ion–ion correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Plasma parameter in the solar core: Γ = 0.072
- Entropic parameter: q = 0.989 (δ = 0.005) for all components, derived from α₁ = 0.55 and Γ = 0.072
- Range of the correlation parameter α₁: 0.4 < α₁ < 0.89
- Q-effect correction ratios: r₁₁(pp) = 3.5 × 10⁻³ at 0.0759 R⊙; r₃₃(³He,³He) = 4.5 × 10⁸; r₃₄(³He,⁴He) = 3 × 10⁹
- With q = 0.99: Φ(pp) = 62.2 × 10⁹ cm⁻² s⁻¹; Φ(⁷Be) = 2.87 × 10⁹ cm⁻² s⁻¹; Φ(B) = 1.65 × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (SSM: 5.15 × 10⁶, experimental: 2.45 ± 0.08 × 10⁶)
- With q = 0.99: Gallium = 100 SNU (SSM: 129 ± 7, Gallex: 77.5 ± 7.7); Chlorine = 2.84 SNU (SSM: 7.7 ± 1.1, experimental: 2.65 ± 0.23)
- With α₁ = 0.55 and n₃/n₃^M ≈ 3 × 10⁻³: Φ(⁷Be)/Φ^M(⁷Be) = 1/50; Φ(B)/Φ^M(B) ≈ 1/2; Gallium = 81 SNU; Chlorine = 2.8 SNU

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kania et al. (1997) and Corradini et al. (1998) on nonextensive effects in stellar plasmas, and by Starostin et al. (1998) on quantum uncertainty corrections to nuclear rates. The authors apply Tsallis statistics (Tsallis 1988) to the solar neutrino problem, which had been treated with standard solar models by Brunner et al. (1998) and Castellani et al. (1997). The combined Q and q effects allow a wider range of neutrino flux predictions than standard Maxwellian models, potentially reconciling the discrepancy between predicted and observed fluxes without requiring new particle physics. The results depend on the ³He abundance in the inner core, which the authors note is constrained by helioseismology only within a large range for r/R⊙ < 0.2.
