# Pole properties of a resonance: When to subtract partial-decay widths to obtain the pole widths

> When a resonance lies near the threshold of a heavier channel, an interesting feature can occur. The paradigmatic example employed here is the scalar isoscalar $f_0(980)$ resonance that couples to the lighter $ππ$ and heavier $K\bar{K}$ channels. It is shown that the decay width is given by the sum or subtraction of the partial decay widths depending on whether the pole lies in the Riemann sheet that is contiguous with the physical one above or below the $K\bar{K}$ threshold, respectively. Next, we show that the usually disregarded renormalization of bare parameters in Flatté or energy-dependent Breit-Wigner parameterizations is essential to extract physical information. The compositeness of the $f_0(980)$ by using a Flatté parameterization matched to reproduce the pole properties obtained from Roy equations and other analytic constraints is evaluated.

## Key claim

When a resonance lies in the second Riemann sheet (contiguous with the physical sheet below the heavier threshold), the pole width equals the subtraction of the partial-decay widths, not their sum; the renormalization of bare parameters in Flatté parameterizations is essential to extract physical couplings and widths.

## Method

The author analyzes the scalar isoscalar \(f_0(980)\) resonance coupling to \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar{K}\) channels using a Flatté parameterization. The pole position in the complex energy plane is related to the Riemann sheet structure, and the renormalization factor \(\beta\) is derived to convert bare couplings \(\tilde{g}_i\) into physical couplings \(g_i = \beta^{1/2} \tilde{g}_i\). The compositeness of the \(f_0(980)\) is then evaluated by matching a Flatté parameterization to the pole properties from Roy equations and analytic constraints.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of the \(f_0(980)\) from Roy equations: \(2m_K + E_R = 996 \pm 7 - i\,25^{+10}_{-6}\) MeV (Garcia-Martin 2011)
- Physical coupling to \(\pi\pi\): \(g_1 = 0.46 \pm 0.04\)
- Partial-decay width to \(\pi\pi\): \(\Gamma_{\pi\pi} = 100^{+20}_{-17}\) MeV (Burkert 2022)
- Pole width from Roy equations: \(\Gamma = 50^{+20}_{-12}\) MeV (derived from the imaginary part)
- \(\Gamma_{K\bar{K}} = \Gamma_{\pi\pi} - \Gamma = 50^{+26}_{-21}\) MeV (Burkert 2022)
- Total width \(\Gamma_{\rm tot} = \Gamma_{\pi\pi} + \Gamma_{K\bar{K}} = 151^{+44}_{-37}\) MeV
- Branching ratios: \(\text{BR}_{\pi\pi} = 0.67 \pm 0.07\), \(\text{BR}_{K\bar{K}} = 0.33 \pm 0.07\)
- Ratio \(r_{K\bar{K}/\pi\pi} = 0.49 \pm 0.11\)
- For the Flatté parameterizations in Table 1, the renormalization factor \(\beta\) ranges from 0.27 to 0.43, and the physical \(\Gamma_{\pi\pi}\) ranges from 55.7 to 67.2 MeV
- Compositeness analysis: for total compositeness \(X > 0.6\), the resulting \(g_1\) is compatible with \(0.46 \pm 0.04\); at \(X=1\), \(g_1=0.47\); at \(X=0.8\), \(g_1=0.45\); at \(X=0.6\), \(g_1=0.42\)

## Context

This work extends the observation by Wang (2022) that for a pole in the second Riemann sheet the pole width is the subtraction of partial-decay widths, and applies it to the \(f_0(980)\) resonance. It corrects the common misuse of bare Flatté parameters as physical ones, showing that renormalization is essential for extracting meaningful couplings. The compositeness analysis, using the formalism of Guo (2015) and Oller (2017), demonstrates that the \(f_0(980)\) is predominantly a \(K\bar{K}\) composite state, enabling future phenomenological analyses to report renormalized rather than bare parameters.
