# Systematic study of high-K isomers in the midshell Gd and Dy nuclei

> High-K isomers are well known in the rare-earth region and provide unique access to the high spin structures of the nuclei. With the current interest in the study of neutron-rich rare-earth nuclei at Radioactive Ion Beam (RIB) facilities, we present here theoretical results of the band structures of neutron-rich Gd and Dy nuclei, including the high K-isomers. Apart from the already known K-isomers, we predict some more K-isomers and these are suggested for future studies at RIB facilities. Self-consistent Deformed Hartree-Fock and Angular Momentum Projection theories are used to get the intrinsic structures, band-spectra and electromagnetic transitions probabilities of the ground band as well as bands based on isomers.

## Key claim

The authors use self-consistent Deformed Hartree-Fock and Angular Momentum Projection calculations to systematically predict high-K isomers in neutron-rich Gd and Dy nuclei, including some not yet observed experimentally, but they note that their calculated excitation energy for the known K=6+ isomer in $^{170}$Dy (about 1.0 MeV) is significantly lower than the measured value of 1643.91(23) keV, suggesting that extension of the configuration space may be needed.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric Deformed Hartree-Fock (DHF) calculations for valence nucleons outside a $^{132}$Sn core, using a Surface Delta Interaction (SDI) with strength 0.3 MeV for proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron interactions. They constructed intrinsic states for ground bands and high-K isomeric bands by particle-hole excitations across the proton and neutron Fermi surfaces, then restored rotational symmetry via Angular Momentum Projection (AMP) to obtain band spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities. Band mixing among different K-configurations was included through orthonormalization of the projected states.

## Key numbers

* $^{170}$Dy: calculated K=6+ isomer excitation energy about 1.0 MeV, compared to experimental value 1643.91(23) keV from Soderstrom (2016)
* $^{166}$Gd: calculated K=6- isomer at 1.554 MeV (configuration $\nu5/2[512]\otimes\nu7/2[633]$), compared to experimental value 1.288 MeV from Patel (2014); calculated spectroscopic quadrupole moment 4.251 eb compared to experimental extracted value 4.073 eb
* $^{168}$Gd: predicted K=6+ isomer at very low excitation energy about 0.866 MeV (configuration $\nu5/2[512]\otimes\nu7/2[514]$)
* $^{170}$Dy: predicted K=12- 4-qp isomer at 1.965 MeV (configuration $\{\pi5/2[413]\otimes\pi7/2[523]\}\oplus\{\nu5/2[512]\otimes\nu7/2[514]\}$)
* Quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ values for Gd and Dy isotopes (N=90-106) are shown to stabilize near N=100, with maximum at N approximately 100, then decrease beyond N=102

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions for high-K isomers in the rare-earth region, particularly the midshell nucleus $^{170}$Dy, which was previously studied using total Routhian surface methods by Regan (2002) and Skyrme Hartree-Fock by Rath (2003). The calculations are motivated by recent experimental measurements at RIB facilities, including the observation of K=6+ isomer in $^{170}$Dy by Soderstrom (2016) and 2-qp isomers in N=100-104 isotones by Patel (2014) and Yokoyama (2017). The results provide predictions for future experimental studies of neutron-rich rare-earth nuclei at facilities such as RIBF at RIKEN, and may help understand the role of deformation in the rare-earth element peak of the astrophysical abundance pattern.
