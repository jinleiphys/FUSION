# Semiclassical treatment of matter-enhanced neutrino oscillations for an arbitrary density profile

> The matter-enhanced oscillations of two neutrino flavors are studied using a uniform semiclassical approximation. Unlike some analytic studies which have focused on certain exactly-solvable densities, this method can be used for an arbitrary monotonic density profile. The method is applicable to a wider range of mixing parameters than previous approximate methods for arbitrary densities. The approximation is excellent in the adiabatic regime and up to the extreme nonadiabatic limit. In particular, the range of validity for this approximation extends farther into the nonadiabatic regime than for the linear Landau-Zener result. This method also allows calculation of the source- and detector-dependent terms in the unaveraged survival probability, and analytic results for these terms are given. These interference terms may be important in studying neutrino mixing in the sun or in supernovae.

## Key claim

The paper presents a uniform semiclassical approximation for matter-enhanced two-flavor neutrino oscillations that works for an arbitrary monotonic density profile, with validity extending further into the nonadiabatic regime than the linear Landau-Zener result, though the approximation breaks down in the extreme nonadiabatic limit where expansions in 1/λ would be required.

## Method

The authors decouple the coupled first-order MSW equations into second-order Schrödinger-like equations for the flavor-basis wave functions, then apply a uniform semiclassical (Miller-Good) approximation that maps the complex potential onto a linear-density model using parabolic cylinder functions. The asymptotic forms of the wave functions are matched on both sides of the resonance region to obtain connection coefficients, from which the electron neutrino survival probability is derived including both the averaged and interference terms.

## Key numbers

- The adiabaticity parameter at resonance is γ_c = (2/λ) (sin²2θ_v / cos2θ_v) (1/|ζ'/ζ|_{x_c}) ≫ 1 for adiabatic propagation.
- The hopping probability coefficients satisfy |c₁|² = 1 − exp(−Ωπ/λ) and |c₂|² = exp(−Ωπ/λ), with Ω = (2i/π) ∫_{x₀}^{x₀*} dx √(Λ + φ²(x)).
- In the adiabatic limit (Ω/λ ≫ 1), |c₁| → 1, |c₂| → 0, and the survival probability reduces to the standard adiabatic result.
- For solar neutrinos with favored MSW parameters, the vacuum oscillation length is ≈ 1000 km.

## Context

This work extends prior analytic studies of MSW oscillations beyond exactly-solvable densities (e.g., exponential density studied by Bychuk (1994)) and beyond the linear Landau-Zener approximation (Haxton (1986), Parke (1986)) by providing a global mapping that yields correct boundary conditions automatically and more accurate hopping probabilities. The method enables calculation of source- and detector-dependent interference terms that may be important for neutrino mixing in the sun or supernovae, and provides a computationally efficient alternative to numerical integration when exploring large ranges of mixing parameters.
