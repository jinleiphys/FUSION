# Three-nucleon force effects in inclusive spectra of the neutron-deuteron breakup reaction

> We investigate the sensitivity of the non-exclusive nucleon induced deuteron breakup reaction to the three-nucleon interaction and distributions of three-nucleon force effects in inclusive spectra. To this end we solve the three-nucleon Faddeev equation at a number of incoming nucleon laboratory energies using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction alone or combined with the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. Based on these solutions energy spectra of an outgoing nucleon, at a specified detection angle as well as spectra integrated over that angle, are calculated. By integrating the spectra at a given angle over the energy of the outgoing nucleon the angular distributions of three-nucleon force effects in the breakup process are additionally obtained. Contrary to elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, where at higher energies significant three-nucleon force effects were encountered for scattering angles around the minimum of the cross section, for the breakup process only moderate effects are found and they are restricted to forward angles. Results of the present investigation show that the large three-nucleon force effects found for some specific complete breakup configurations are reduced substantially in the incomplete spectra when averaging over contributing complete geometries is performed.

## Key claim

Contrary to elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, where significant three-nucleon force effects appear at higher energies around the cross section minimum, for the inclusive breakup process only moderate three-nucleon force effects are found and they are restricted to forward angles; the large three-nucleon force effects previously found for specific complete breakup configurations are substantially reduced in incomplete spectra when averaging over contributing complete geometries.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation in a partial-wave momentum-space basis at incoming nucleon laboratory energies of 14, 70, 100, 135, 200, 250, and 294 MeV, using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential alone or combined with the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne (TM99) three-nucleon force. From these solutions they calculated the threefold differential cross section d³σ/dΩ₁dE₁ for an outgoing nucleon at a specified laboratory angle, as well as energy spectra integrated over that angle. By integrating the spectra at a given angle over the energy of the outgoing nucleon, they obtained angular distributions of three-nucleon force effects in the breakup process.

## Key numbers

- At 70 MeV incoming neutron energy, three-nucleon force effects in the threefold differential cross section at forward angles (10°) change the cross section by up to 10%, strengthening to approximately 20% at 200 MeV.
- In angle-integrated energy spectra, three-nucleon force effects are distributed more uniformly with magnitudes of approximately 2-3% at 70 MeV and approximately 4-6% at 200 MeV.
- For complete breakup in specific configurations, three-nucleon force effects of magnitude up to approximately 90% were found at 200 MeV, diminishing to approximately 20% in energy spectra at a specific angle and further to approximately 6% after angular integration.
- In angular distributions of the breakup cross section, three-nucleon force effects at 70 MeV are approximately 2-3% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 100°, at 135 MeV approximately 3-4% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 120°, and at 200 MeV approximately 4-5% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 110°.
- At 200 MeV, three-nucleon force effects in elastic scattering amount to approximately 50% in the region of the cross section minimum.
- At 200 MeV, the TM99 three-nucleon force explains approximately 60% of the difference between total nd cross section data and CD Bonn predictions; at 250 MeV only approximately 45% is explained; at 294 MeV the explained part reduces to approximately 30%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon force effects in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering (Witała 1998, Witała 2001) and in kinematically complete breakup configurations (Kuros-Zolnierczuk 2002), where large three-nucleon force effects were found. The results show that the long-range 2π-exchange TM99 three-nucleon force is insufficient to explain total nd cross section data at energies above approximately 200 MeV, indicating the need for short-range components of the three-nucleon force, such as those being developed in chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2002, Piarulli 2018). The findings enable future tests of whether short-range three-nucleon force components can describe the total nd cross section data at higher energies.
