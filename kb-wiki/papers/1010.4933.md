# One dimensional scattering of a two body interacting system by an infinite wall

> The one-dimensional scattering of a two body interacting system by an infinite wall is studied in a quantum-mechanical framework. This problem contains some of the dynamical features present in the collision of atomic, molecular and nuclear systems. The scattering problem is solved exactly, for the case of a harmonic interaction between the fragments. The exact result is used to assess the validity of two different approximations to the scattering process. The adiabatic approximation, which considers that the relative co-ordinate is frozen during the scattering process, is found to be inadequate for this problem. The uncorrelated scattering approximation, which neglects the correlation between the fragments, gives results in accordance with the exact calculations when the scattering energy is high compared to the oscillator parameter.

# One dimensional scattering of a two body interacting system by an infinite wall

## Key claim

The uncorrelated scattering approximation (USA), which neglects correlations between fragments, gives results in accordance with exact calculations when the scattering energy is high compared to the oscillator parameter, while the adiabatic approximation (which freezes the relative coordinate) is found to be inadequate for this problem.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional quantum scattering problem of two particles interacting via a harmonic oscillator potential colliding with an infinite wall potential. Two exact solution methods were developed: direct application of boundary conditions at the wall, and a configuration localised states (CLS) approach that decomposes the wave function into states localised at the zeros of the Hermite polynomials. The exact results were then compared with the adiabatic approximation and the uncorrelated scattering approximation (USA) for equal-mass fragments.

## Key numbers

- The elastic coefficient is identically one for scattering energies below 5/2 ℏω, which corresponds to the energy of the first excited state that can be populated.
- The modulus of the elastic S-matrix element S₀₀ presents a minimum at E = 5 ℏω, indicating maximal loss of flux from the elastic channel at this energy.
- A basis space with 15 even parity harmonic oscillator states was used for the exact calculations.
- The energy scale is expressed in units of ℏω and the length scale in units of the oscillator length a₀ = √(ℏ/μω).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (USA) (Moro 2000, Moro 2001), which was applied to elastic scattering and break-up of deuterons on heavy targets. The one-dimensional model with harmonic interaction provides a benchmark to test approximations used in nuclear physics for halo nuclei reactions, including the adiabatic approximation (Ron 1970, Ron 1997, Tos 1998) and the sudden approximation (Edu 1996, Edu 1997, Edu 1998a, Edu 1998b). The results enable assessment of these approximations in a controlled setting where exact solutions are available, with implications for coupled channels calculations in atomic, molecular, and nuclear scattering.
