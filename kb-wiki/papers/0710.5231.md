# Anomalous shell effect in the transition from a circular to a triangular billiard

> We apply periodic orbit theory to a two-dimensional non-integrable billiard system whose boundary is varied smoothly from a circular to an equilateral triangular shape. Although the classical dynamics becomes chaotic with increasing triangular deformation, it exhibits an astonishingly pronounced shell effect on its way through the shape transition. A semiclassical analysis reveals that this shell effect emerges from a codimension-two bifurcation of the triangular periodic orbit. Gutzwiller's semiclassical trace formula, using a global uniform approximation for the bifurcation of the triangular orbit and including the contributions of the other isolated orbits, describes very well the coarse-grained quantum-mechanical level density of this system. We also discuss the role of discrete symmetry for the large shell effect obtained here.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a surprisingly large shell effect appears during the shape transition from a circular to an equilateral triangular billiard, even though the classical dynamics becomes chaotic; this effect is traced to a codimension-two bifurcation of the triangular periodic orbit, and the authors show that the shell gaps in the non-integrable region are much larger than in either integrable limit.

## Method

The authors study a two-dimensional billiard whose boundary is varied continuously from a circle (alpha=0) to an equilateral triangle (alpha=1) using a parameterized shape function. They compute the quantum energy spectrum by expanding wave functions in circular waves and imposing Dirichlet boundary conditions, classifying states by the C3v point-group symmetry. They then perform a semiclassical analysis using Gutzwiller's trace formula with a global uniform approximation for the codimension-two bifurcation of the triangular orbit, and compare the Fourier transform of the quantum level density to the lengths of classical periodic orbits.

## Key numbers

- The tangent (saddle-node) bifurcation of the triangular orbit occurs at alpha = 0.400, and the touch-and-go bifurcation at alpha = 0.407.
- The normal-form parameters for the triangular orbit bifurcation are a = 0.519252 / sqrt(hbar k R0), b = 2.34950 / (hbar k R0), and epsilon_1 = 0.0322755.
- The diametric orbit (2A) undergoes a period-doubling pitchfork bifurcation at alpha = 0.166, and a further pitchfork bifurcation at alpha = 0.227.
- The Fourier transform peak of the triangular orbit (length L approximately 5.2 R0) culminates around alpha approximately 0.55, above the bifurcation point.

## Context

This work extends periodic orbit theory to a non-integrable billiard with C3v symmetry, a shape transition not previously studied in quantum dot systems. The bifurcation scenario is analogous to those found in elliptic billiards (Sie96, Nishioka1, Magner99) and in 3D spheroidal cavities (Nishioka2) that explain super-deformed and hyper-deformed nuclear shell structure (ASM, Magner02). The results suggest that discrete symmetries like C3v can produce large shell effects that might stabilize exotic nuclear shapes such as tetrahedral and octahedral deformations proposed by Dudek.
