# Quark fragmentation as a probe of dynamical mass generation

> We address the propagation and hadronization of a struck quark by studying the gauge invariance of the color-averaged cut quark propagator, and by relating this to the single inclusive quark fragmentation correlator by means of new sum rules. Using suitable Wilson lines, we provide a gauge-invariant definition for the mass of the color-averaged dressed quark and decompose this into the sum of a current and an interaction-dependent component. The latter, which we argue is an order parameter for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, also appears in the sum rule for the twist-3 $\tilde{E}$ fragmentation function, providing a specific experimental way to probe the dynamical generation of mass in Quantum Chromo Dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper provides a gauge-invariant definition of a "jet mass" \(M_j\) for a color-averaged dressed quark, which can be decomposed into the current quark mass and an interaction-dependent "correlation mass" \(m_q^{corr}\); the latter is argued to be an order parameter for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and is experimentally accessible via sum rules for the twist-3 fragmentation function \(\tilde{E}\), provided that quark-gluon-quark correlations are not neglected.

## Method

The authors study the gauge-invariant color-averaged cut quark propagator (the "inclusive jet correlator") using a spectral decomposition in Dirac space, and relate it to the single-inclusive quark fragmentation correlator via new four-momentum sum rules derived from the commutation relation of the hadron momentum operator with the quark fields. They then use equations-of-motion relations to connect the twist-3 fragmentation functions \(E\) and \(\tilde{E}\) to the jet mass and its dynamical component.

## Key numbers

* The momentum sum rule for the unpolarized twist-2 fragmentation function is \(\sum_{h,S_h} \int dz \, z \, D_1^{h}(z) = 1\).
* The mass sum rule for the twist-3 fragmentation function \(E\) is \(\sum_{h,S_h} \int dz \, M_h E^{h}(z) = M_j\), where \(M_j\) is the gauge-invariant jet mass.
* The sum rule for the dynamical twist-3 fragmentation function \(\tilde{E}\) is \(\sum_{h,S_h} \int dz \, M_h \tilde{E}^{h}(z) = m_q^{corr}\), where \(m_q^{corr}\) is the correlation mass.
* The sum rule for the transverse-momentum-weighted fragmentation function \(\tilde{D}^{\perp}\) is \(\sum_{h,S_h} \int dz \, M_h^2 \tilde{D}^{\perp (1) h}(z) = -\frac{1}{2} \langle \mathbf{P}_{h\perp}^2 / z \rangle\).
* In the light-cone gauge, the jet mass is given by \(M_j = \int_0^{+\infty} d\mu^2 \, \sqrt{\mu^2} \, \rho_1^{lcg}(\mu^2)\), where \(\rho_1^{lcg}\) is the chiral-odd Kallen-Lehman spectral function.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of a jet mass in Accardi (2017) by providing a rigorous field-theoretic proof of the mass sum rule and a gauge-invariant definition of \(M_j\). It generalizes the sum rule for \(\tilde{E}\) previously discussed in Bacchetta (2006), which neglected the non-perturbative component, and corrects the earlier sum rule of Jaffe (1993) that omitted the dynamical mass. The results enable experimental access to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking through measurements of twist-3 fragmentation functions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, electron-positron annihilation, and hadronic collisions, and connect the jet mass to the constituent mass used in quark models.
