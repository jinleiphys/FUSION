# Phase diagram and thermodynamics of the PNJL model

> QCD-based thermodynamics at zero and finite quark chemical potential is studied using an extended Nambu and Jona-Lasinio approach in which quarks couple simultaneously to the chiral condensate and to a background temporal gauge field representing Polyakov loop dynamics. This so-called PNJL model thus includes features of both deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration. We discuss the phase diagram as it emerges from this approach in close comparison with results from lattice QCD thermodynamics. The critical point, separating crossover from first order phase transition, is investigated with special focus on its quark mass dependence, starting from the relatively large masses presently accessible by lattice simulations, down to the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The PNJL model, which couples quarks to both the chiral condensate and a Polyakov loop background field, reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero and finite chemical potential up to about twice the critical temperature, but its range of applicability is limited to temperatures below approximately 2.5 T_c because transverse gluon degrees of freedom are ignored.

## Method

The authors construct a two-flavour Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model by combining a standard NJL four-fermion interaction with a static temporal background gauge field representing Polyakov loop dynamics, using an effective potential for the Polyakov loop fitted to pure-gauge lattice data. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop fields as functions of temperature and quark chemical potential, and the resulting pressure, quark number density, and phase boundaries are compared to lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: T_c approximately 220 MeV (with T_0 = 270 MeV for the pure gauge Polyakov potential); after rescaling T_0 to 190 MeV, the average transition temperature becomes T_c = 180 MeV.
* NJL model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 0.651 GeV, coupling G = 10.08 GeV^{-2}, bare quark mass m_0 = 5.5 MeV; these yield constituent quark mass m = 325 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.3 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 139.3 MeV, and chiral condensate |<bar psi_u psi_u>|^{1/3} = 251 MeV.
* Pure gauge Polyakov potential parameters: a_0 = 6.75, a_1 = -1.95, a_2 = 2.625, a_3 = -7.44, b_3 = 0.75, b_4 = 7.5, with T_0 = 270 MeV.
* The Taylor expansion of the thermodynamic potential to order mu^2 deviates from the full result by less than 10% even at chemical potential as large as mu ~ T_c; expansion to order mu^4 shows no visible difference.

## Context

This work extends the standard NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961; Vogl and Weise, 1991; Klevansky, 1992; Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) by incorporating Polyakov loop dynamics to address the missing confinement in NJL approaches, following earlier ideas by Fukushima (2003). The PNJL model reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics from the MILC collaboration (Boyd et al., 1996; Karsch, 2002; Ali Khan et al., 2001) and the Bielefeld-Swansea group (Allton et al., 2003; Allton et al., 2002), and enables controlled extrapolations of the phase diagram from large quark masses accessible in lattice simulations down to the chiral limit. The model also provides a framework for calculating susceptibilities and higher-order derivatives of the pressure, as subsequently done by Ghosh, Mukherjee, Mustafa, and Ray (2006).
