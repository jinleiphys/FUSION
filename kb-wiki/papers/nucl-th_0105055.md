# Instantons As Unitary Spin Maker

> We investigate the relevance of the instanton-induced determinantal 't Hooft interaction to the eta-nucleon coupling within the framework of a three-flavor linear sigma model in the OZI-rule-respecting basis. Instantons, in combination with the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, are shown to provide the major mechanism for the ideal mixing between pseudoscalar strange and non-strange quarkonia. As long as 't Hooft's interaction captures most of the basic features of the axial QCD gluon anomaly, we identify the anomaly as the main culprit for the appearance of octet flavor symmetry in the anomalous sectors of the pseudoscalar (and axial vector) mesons. Within this context, unitary spin is shown to be an accidental symmetry due to anomalous gluon dynamics rather than a fundamental symmetry in its own right. Though we find the eta-nucleon coupling constant to obey a Goldberger-Treiman like relation, the latter does not take its origin from a pole dominance of the induced pseudoscalar form factor of the octet axial current, but from a subtle flavor-mixing mechanism that is traced back to instanton dynamics. The model presented allows for possible generalizations to non-ideal mixing angles and different values of the meson decay constants in the strange and non-strange sectors, respectively. Finally, we discuss the issue as to what extent the eta meson may be considered as a Goldstone boson under the constraints of the anomaly-produced unitary spin.

## Key claim

Instantons, via the 't Hooft determinantal interaction combined with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, provide the major mechanism for ideal mixing between strange and non-strange pseudoscalar quarkonia, making unitary spin an accidental symmetry of anomalous gluon dynamics rather than a fundamental symmetry; the eta-nucleon coupling obeys a Goldberger-Treiman-like relation that originates from a flavor-mixing mechanism traced to instanton dynamics rather than from pole dominance of the induced pseudoscalar form factor.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-flavor linear sigma model in the OZI-rule-respecting basis (non-strange and strange quarkonia) with a chirally symmetric Lagrangian, adding the instanton-induced 't Hooft determinantal interaction to break U(1)_A symmetry and an explicit symmetry-breaking term. They diagonalized the resulting mass matrix for the isoscalar pseudoscalar mesons to extract the mixing angle and mass relations, then computed the divergences of the strange and non-strange axial currents to derive the eta-nucleon coupling and the weak decay constants of the flavor fields.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar mixing angle in the flavor (ns-s) basis is found in the range phi_P between 49.7 degrees (for x=0.22) and 56.0 degrees (for x=0.37), corresponding to the octet-singlet basis angle theta_P in the range -5 degrees to +2 degrees.
- The beta parameter (strength of the 't Hooft interaction) is determined as beta approximately -1.55 GeV using x=0.37.
- The non-strange weak decay constant is f_eta_ns = sqrt(2) a = f_pi, and the strange weak decay constant is f_eta_s = sqrt(2) b = 2 f_K - f_pi.
- The mixing relations yield sin(2 phi_P) = 0.9202 and cos(2 phi_P) = -0.3911 for the case x=0.37.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of flavor symmetry breaking in the pseudoscalar and axial vector meson sectors, addressing the conflicting flavor symmetries between anomalous and anomaly-free meson sectors previously noted by Kirchbach (1997) and others. The model builds on the 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction (1976) and the linear sigma model framework of Lenaghan et al. (2000) and Napsuciale et al. (2000), showing that the octet flavor symmetry of pseudoscalar mesons arises from anomalous gluon dynamics rather than being fundamental. The results enable a quantitative understanding of the eta-nucleon coupling mechanism and clarify the extent to which the eta meson can be considered a Goldstone boson under the constraints of the anomaly-produced unitary spin.
