# Nonlinear response of flow harmonics in Gubser flow with participant-reaction planes mismatch

> We investigate the nonlinear response of flow harmonics $v_2,v_4$ to initial-state eccentricities $ε_2,ε_4$ within the Gubser-flow framework. By extending the perturbative solutions of Gubser flow, we derive analytic nonlinear response relations connecting the eccentricities $ε_2,ε_4$ to the flow harmonics $v_2,v_4$. Our results reproduce the well-known result $v_4/v_2^2 \to 1/2$ in large transverse momentum $p_T$ limit. Furthermore, we study the effects of a mismatch between the participant and reaction planes. We find that the conventional nonlinear response coefficients acquire an additional factor determined by the participant-plane angles, which is often approximated as statistical noise driven by event-by-event fluctuations. This factor can modify both the strength but even the sign of the effective nonlinear response coefficient, making it sensitive to the initial configuration of the colliding nuclei. Our study provides new analytical insight into the origin of collective phenomena in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic nonlinear response relations connecting the initial eccentricities ε₂, ε₄ to the flow harmonics v₂, v₄ within the Gubser-flow framework, and shows that the conventional nonlinear response coefficients acquire an additional factor determined by the participant-plane angles, which can modify both the strength and even the sign of the effective nonlinear response coefficient, making it sensitive to the initial configuration of the colliding nuclei.

## Method

The authors extend the perturbative solutions of ideal Gubser flow by introducing a specific transverse perturbation S(θ,φ) containing terms proportional to cos2φ and cos4φ, which are related to the eccentricities ε₂ and ε₄. They then compute the flow harmonics v₂ and v₄ using the Cooper-Frye formula at a freeze-out hypersurface defined by constant temperature T_f, and derive analytic expressions for the linear response coefficients χ₂₂, χ₄₄ and the nonlinear response coefficient χ₄₂. The analysis is further extended to include a mismatch between the participant-plane angles ψ₂, ψ₄ and the reaction-plane angles Ψ₂, Ψ₄.

## Key numbers

* The ratio v₄/v₂² approaches 1/2 in the large transverse momentum p_T limit, independent of particle mass and freeze-out temperature.
* In the small p_T limit, χ₂₂ ∝ p_T², while χ₄₂ and χ₄₄ ∝ p_T⁴.
* The nonlinear coefficient χ defined by v₄ = χ₄₄ε₄ + χ v₂² is independent of the parameter B in the small p_T limit.
* The eccentricity ε₄ acquires an additional factor cos[4(ψ₂ − ψ₄)] in its relation to the perturbation parameters when participant-plane angles are included: ε₄ = −(36/35)a₄ − (18/35)cos[4(ψ₂−ψ₄)]a₂².

## Context

This work extends the perturbative Gubser-flow framework developed by Hatta et al. (2014, 2015) to include nonlinear response of v₄ to ε₂ and ε₄, which had not been derived analytically in this setup. It provides an analytic confirmation of the universal result v₄/v₂² → 1/2 for ideal fluids previously pointed out by Borghini (2005), Teaney (2012), Lang (2013), and Gombeaud (2009). The results enable a systematic understanding of why the nonlinear response coefficient χ is sensitive to nuclear deformation parameters such as β₄, as recently observed in phenomenological studies by Xu (2024) and Wang (2024).
