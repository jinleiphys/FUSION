# Ab-initio calculation of the photonuclear cross section of $^{10}$B

> We present for the first-time the photonuclear cross section of $^{10}$B calculated within the ab-initio No Core Shell Model framework. Realistic two-nucleon (NN) chiral forces up to next-to-next-to-next-order (N3LO), which have been softened by the similarity renormalization group method (SRG) to $λ=2.02$ fm$^{-1}$, were utilized. The electric-dipole response function is calculated using the Lanczos method. The effects of the continuum were accounted for by including neutron escape widths derived from R-matrix theory. The calculated cross section agrees well with experimental data in terms of structure as well as in absolute peak height, $σ_{\rm max}=4.85~{\rm mb}$ at photon energy $ω=23.61~{\rm MeV}$, and integrated cross section $85.36\, {\rm MeV \cdotp mb}$. We test the Brink hypothesis by calculating the electric-dipole response for the first five positive-parity states in $^{10}$B and verify that dipole excitations built upon the ground- and excited states have similar characteristics.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab-initio calculation of the photonuclear cross section of \(^{10}\)B using the No Core Shell Model (NCSM) with realistic two-nucleon chiral forces, finding good agreement with experimental data in peak height and integrated cross section, but with the calculated peak shifted about 1.5 MeV higher in photon energy than the experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations using two-nucleon chiral interactions at N3LO softened by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method to \(\lambda=2.02\) fm\(^{-1}\). The electric-dipole response function was computed using the Lanczos method with up to 300 iterations, and continuum effects were included by adding neutron escape widths derived from R-matrix theory, with spectroscopic factors modeled from \(\nmax=3\) and 5 calculations. The photo-absorption cross section was obtained by replacing discrete lines above the neutron separation energy with Breit-Wigner functions using the calculated neutron escape widths.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section: \(\sigma_{\rm max}=4.85\) mb at photon energy \(\omega=23.61\) MeV for the \(\nmax=\infty\) extrapolation
- Integrated cross section: \(\int_0^{35} \sigma(\omega)d\omega=85.36\) MeV\(\cdot\)mb, compared to experimental value \(83.1\pm1.2\) MeV\(\cdot\)mb from Kneissl et al. (1976)
- Neutron separation energy: \(S_n = 8.436\) MeV for \(^{10}\)B
- SRG softening parameter: \(\lambda=2.02\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Harmonic oscillator frequency: \(\hbar\Omega=20\) MeV
- Calculated quadrupole moment at \(\nmax=9\): \(Q=6.22\) fm\(^2\) for the \(J^\pi=3^+\) state
- Calculated radius: \(R=2.11\) fm (point-nucleon radius)
- Deformation parameter from NCSM: \(\beta_{2,{\rm NCSM}} \approx 0.37\); from experimental data: \(\beta_{2,{\rm exp}}=0.34\)
- Extrapolated excitation energy for the fitted dipole state: 18.209 MeV, which is 1.881 MeV lower than the \(\nmax=9\) result

## Context

This work extends ab-initio calculations of photonuclear cross sections from lighter nuclei such as \(^4\)He (Efros 1997, Stetcu 2007), \(^6\)He and \(^6\)Li (Bacca 2002, 2004), and \(^7\)Li (Bacca 2004) to \(^{10}\)B using the NCSM framework. The calculation provides the first fully microscopic verification of the Brink hypothesis for \(^{10}\)B, showing that dipole excitations built upon the ground and first five positive-parity excited states have similar characteristics. The methodology establishes a framework for calculating electroweak observables for light nuclei in general, enabling future studies of photonuclear reactions across the p-shell.
