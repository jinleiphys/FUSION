# Reply to the Comment on "Influence of protons on the capture of electrons by the nuclei of 7Be in the Sun"

> We show that the arguments against our paper raised by B. Davids et al. are either irrelevant or incorrect.

## Key claim

The authors defend their claim that the ternary reaction \(^{7}\text{Be} + e^- + p \rightarrow ^{7}\text{Li} + \nu_e + p\) is a genuine three-body process distinct from the binary electron capture reaction, and that the arguments raised by Davids et al. against this mechanism are either irrelevant or incorrect. The hedge is that the authors acknowledge their model calculations cannot substitute for a full calculation that would provide reliable information on the rate.

## Method

The authors analyze the three-body system quantum-mechanically by solving the Schrödinger equation for the proton-electron-\(^{7}\text{Be}\) system, using an expansion in the small parameter \(\epsilon \approx (m_e/m_p)^{1/2} \approx 0.0233\). The first term of this expansion yields a wave function \(\Psi_0(\vec r, \vec R) = \Psi^C_1(\vec R) \Psi^C(\vec r, Z=Z_1+Z_2)\), where \(\Psi^C_1(\vec R)\) describes the relative motion of the proton and \(^{7}\text{Be}\) and \(\Psi^C(\vec r, Z=5)\) describes the electron moving in the continuum in an effective Coulomb potential of charge \(Z=5\). The electron capture probability is then evaluated at the point \(|\vec r| = R/7\), where the \(^{7}\text{Be}\) nucleus is located relative to the center of mass of the proton-\(^{7}\text{Be}\) system.

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter \(\epsilon \approx (m_e/m_p)^{1/2} \approx 0.0233\) characterizes the small-mass-ratio expansion used for the three-body wave function.
* The mean distance \(R_0\) between protons in the solar plasma is about \(3 \times 10^4\) fm, and the Debye radius \(R_D\) is about \(4 \times 10^4\) fm.
* In the ternary reaction, the electron moves in an effective Coulomb field with charge \(Z=5\), compared to \(Z=4\) in the binary reaction.
* The ratio of the ternary to binary electron capture effects in the Sun is of the order of 10%, but can be of order 1 in dense stars.

## Context

This reply defends the authors' earlier work (Belyaev, Tater, Truhlik, 2007) against a Comment by Davids et al., who argued that the three-body mechanism is actually a binary reaction in a plasma environment and that the wave functions used are incorrect. The authors contrast their quantum-mechanical three-body treatment with the equilibrium plasma and Monte Carlo approach of Ref. [b14], which treated protons as static and only considered screening corrections to the binary reaction. The work extends the formalism of Ref. [b3] for three charged particles in the continuum and is analogous to the treatment of the \(p-e-p\) reaction by Bahcall and May (1969). The authors note that a full calculation of the ternary process rate remains needed.
