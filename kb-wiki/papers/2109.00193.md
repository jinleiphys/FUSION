# Ab initio structure factors for spin-dependent dark matter direct detection

> We present converged ab initio calculations of structure factors for elastic spin-dependent WIMP scattering off all nuclei used in dark matter direct-detection searches: $^{19}$F, $^{23}$Na, $^{27}$Al, $^{29}$Si, $^{73}$Ge, $^{127}$I, and $^{129,131}$Xe. From a set of established two- and three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory, we construct consistent WIMP-nucleon currents at the one-body level, including effects from axial-vector two-body currents. We then apply the in-medium similarity renormalization group to construct effective valence-space Hamiltonians and consistently transformed operators of nuclear responses. Combining the recent advances of natural orbitals with three-nucleon forces expressed in large spaces, we obtain basis-space converged structure factors even in heavy nuclei. Generally results are consistent with previous calculations, but large uncertainties in $^{127}$I highlight the need for further study.

## Key claim
The paper presents converged ab initio calculations of spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus structure factors for all detector-relevant nuclei, finding general consistency with previous large-scale shell model results but noting that large uncertainties in \(^{127}\)I highlight the need for further study.

## Method
The authors used the in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) to construct effective valence-space Hamiltonians and consistently transformed operators from chiral effective field theory interactions (1.8/2.0(EM), N\(^4\)LO+3N\(_{\rm lnl}\), and \(\Delta\)NNLO\(_{\rm GO}\)). They included axial-vector two-body currents via normal-ordering with a Fermi gas reference state and achieved basis-space convergence using natural orbitals with three-nucleon forces expressed in large spaces. Structure factors were computed at 15 momentum transfer points for each nucleus, with final smooth curves obtained by fitting to analytic forms.

## Key numbers
- Structure factor convergence for \(^{129}\)Xe achieved at \(e_{\rm max}=8\) in the natural orbital basis, with \(\hbar\omega = 12-20\) MeV showing nearly independent results.
- Two-body currents suppress the dominant structure factor by 10%–35% at low momentum transfer (\(q \lesssim 100\) MeV).
- The axial radius \(\langle r_A^2 \rangle = 0.46(16)\) fm\(^2\) was used from a global analysis of muon capture and neutrino scattering.
- The density parameter for normal-ordering of two-body currents was taken as \(\rho = 0.08 \ldots 0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For heavy nuclei (\(^{127}\)I, \(^{129,131}\)Xe), calculations used \(e_{\rm max}^{\rm NAT}=14\) and \(e_{\rm max}=8\) with \(\hbar\omega=16\) MeV and \(E_{\rm 3max}=22\).
- The largest differences with LSSM arise in \(^{23}\)Na (dominant factor band reduced by 66%) and \(^{127}\)I (reduced by 92%), while \(^{29}\)Si shows a 120% enhancement.

## Context
This work extends ab initio nuclear theory to spin-dependent dark matter structure factors for medium- and heavy-mass detector nuclei, where previous calculations relied on phenomenological large-scale shell model (LSSM) approaches (Hoferichter et al., 2016; Menendez et al., 2012). The results enable direct comparison with LSSM predictions and provide independent theoretical input for interpreting dark matter direct-detection experiments. The framework can be straightforwardly applied to neutrino scattering and spin-independent dark matter scattering, with ongoing work to extend to inelastic WIMP scattering and coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering.
