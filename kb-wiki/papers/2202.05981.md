# Scaling properties of exclusive vector meson production cross section from gluon saturation

> It is already known from phenomenological studies that in exclusive deep-inelastic scattering off nuclei there appears to be a scaling behavior of vector meson production cross section in both nuclear mass number, $A$, and photon virtuality, $Q^{2}$, which is strongly modified due to gluon saturation effects. In this work we continue those studies in a realistic setup based upon using the Monte Carlo event generator Sar{\em t}re. We make quantitative predictions for the kinematics of the Electron-Ion Collider, focusing on this $A$ and $Q^{2}$ scaling picture, along with establishing a small region of squared momentum transfer, $t$, where there are signs of this scaling that may potentially be observed at the EIC. Our results are represented as pseudo-data of vector meson production diffractive cross section and/or their ratios, which are obtained by parsing data collected by the event generator through smearing functions, emulating the proposed detector resolutions for the future EIC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the scaling behavior of exclusive vector meson production cross section with nuclear mass number $A$ and photon virtuality $Q^{2}$, which is strongly modified by gluon saturation effects, can be observed at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) kinematics, but only within a small region of squared momentum transfer $t$ where signs of this scaling may potentially be observed.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo event generator Sartre, which implements the IPSat (bSat) dipole model with impact parameter dependence and its linearized version IPNonSat, to simulate exclusive vector meson production in diffractive electron-gold (e+Au) scattering at EIC kinematics. They generated pseudo-data for coherent and incoherent diffractive cross sections and their ratios by parsing the event generator output through smearing functions that emulate the proposed detector resolutions for the future EIC. The nuclear scattering amplitude was constructed from the proton amplitude using a Woods-Saxon distribution of nucleon positions, with averaging over nucleon configurations to separate coherent and incoherent contributions.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity used for simulations: $10\,{\rm fb}^{-1}/A$ for e+Au scattering
- Squared momentum transfer bins studied: $|t| = [0.000 - 0.001]\,{\rm GeV^{2}}$, $[0.003 - 0.004]\,{\rm GeV^{2}}$, $[0.006 - 0.007]\,{\rm GeV^{2}}$, $[0.009 - 0.010]\,{\rm GeV^{2}}$, and $[0.012 - 0.013]\,{\rm GeV^{2}}$
- Central values of $t$ bins used for reference: $|t| = 5\times10^{-4}\ {\rm GeV^{2}}$, $3.5\times10^{-3}\ {\rm GeV^{2}}$, $6.5\times10^{-3}\ {\rm GeV^{2}}$, $9.5\times10^{-3}\ {\rm GeV^{2}}$, and $1.25\times10^{-2}\ {\rm GeV^{2}}$
- Proton width parameter: $B_{p} = 4\,{\rm GeV}^{-2}$
- Cut-off scale in gluon DGLAP evolution: $\mu_0^{2} = 1.1$\,GeV$^2$
- Dipole size cut-off for nuclei: $r < 3R_{N}$, where $R_{N}$ is the nuclear radius from Woods-Saxon parametrization
- Dipole size cut-off for proton: $r < 3$\,fm

## Context

This work continues the phenomenological studies of Mantysaari (2017) which demonstrated that nuclear saturation effects significantly modify the $A$ and $Q^{2}$ scaling properties of exclusive vector meson production cross sections when passing from the perturbative QCD regime ($Q^{2} > Q_{s,A}^{2}$) to the saturation regime ($Q^{2} < Q_{s,A}^{2}$). The paper extends those studies by using the Sartre Monte Carlo generator with realistic detector smearing for the upcoming EIC, building on the dipole model frameworks developed by Kowalski (2003) and Kowalski (2006) (the bSat or IPSat model). The results enable experimental planning for observing gluon saturation signatures at the EIC through exclusive vector meson production measurements.
