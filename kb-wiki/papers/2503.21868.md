# Visualizing How Jet Structure Shapes Jet Wakes

> The ATLAS Collaboration has developed a method to analyze large-radius jets composed of skinny $R=0.2$ subjets in heavy-ion collisions. We first demonstrate that the measurements pioneered by ATLAS constrain the value of $L_{\rm res}$, the resolution length of QGP -- and rule out any picture in which an entire parton shower loses energy coherently as a single entity. We then analyze the response of the medium to the passage of large-radius $R=2$ jets containing two skinny subjets in gamma-jet events. We introduce novel jet-shape observables that allow us to visualize how the internal structure of large-radius jets shapes the wakes they excite in the QGP. We find that even when the subjets are $\sim 0.8$ radians apart, the angular shape of the soft hadrons originating from their wake forms a single broad structure. Only when the two subjets are even farther apart are two sub-wakes revealed. We show that the way in which jet structure shapes the structure of jet-induced wakes can be visualized with similar clarity in experiments by using only low-$p_T$ hadrons. The observables we introduce offer a new and distinctive way of seeing jet-induced wakes -- and wake substructure -- in heavy-ion collision data.

## Key claim

The ATLAS measurements of large-radius jet suppression as a function of subjet separation constrain the QGP resolution length \(L_{\rm res}\) and rule out fully coherent energy loss (\(L_{\rm res} = \infty\)), but the data cannot yet distinguish between \(L_{\rm res} = 0\) and \(L_{\rm res} = 2/(\pi T)\) without extending measurements to lower \(\Delta R_{12}\) values using skinnier subjets.

## Method

The authors use the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model, which treats hard jet evolution perturbatively via PYTHIA 8 DGLAP evolution and parton energy loss to the strongly coupled QGP via a holographically derived formula. They implement a QGP resolution length \(L_{\rm res}\) that controls whether two partons lose energy coherently or independently, studying three values: 0 (fully incoherent), \(2/(\pi T)\), and \(\infty\) (fully coherent). For gamma-jet events, they reconstruct large-radius \(R=2\) jets from \(R=0.2\) skinny subjets and introduce novel differential jet shape observables \(\rho(r, r_\perp)\) in a coordinate system aligned with the two subjets, using only low-\(p_T\) hadrons (0.7–1.0 GeV) to isolate wake contributions.

## Key numbers

* ATLAS data for \(R=1.0\) jets with \(200 < p_T < 251\) GeV show that \(R_{\rm AA}\) for jets with multiple subjets (\(\Delta R_{12} > 0\)) is lower than for single-subjet jets by a factor of \(\sim 2\).
* For \(L_{\rm res} = 2/(\pi T)\), \(R_{\rm AA}\) is not flat when \(0.2 < \Delta R_{12} < 0.4\), indicating the resolution length affects suppression at small angular separations.
* Two subjets separated by \(\Delta y_{12} \lesssim 1.2\) radians produce a single broad wake structure; two sub-wakes emerge only when \(\Delta y_{12} \gtrsim 1.4\) radians.
* The wake shape analysis uses hadrons with \(0.7 < p_T < 1.0\) GeV within \(\Delta R = 2.0\) of the jet axis to maximize wake contribution relative to fragmentation hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the ATLAS Collaboration (2023) measurement of large-radius jet suppression using skinny subjets, applying it to constrain the QGP resolution length \(L_{\rm res}\) for the first time. It introduces novel jet shape observables that visualize wake substructure, building on the Hybrid Model framework developed by Chesler and Rajagopal (2014, 2015) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2016). The results enable experimental searches for wake substructure in heavy-ion collision data using only low-\(p_T\) hadrons, offering a new way to probe how jet internal structure shapes medium response.
