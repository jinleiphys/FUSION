# Coupled-channel approach to isotensor $πππ$ scattering from lattice QCD

> The quest to understand three-body dynamics from first-principle QCD includes the study of non-resonant and resonant systems. The isospin $I=2$ system is of particular interest having no three-body resonance but featuring a resonance in a sub-channel, while also being a coupled-channel problem. In this study, we calculate the finite-volume spectrum from lattice QC at two different pion masses, map the amplitude to the infinite volume through a generalized FVU three-body quantization condition, investigate the limit of a narrow $ρ$, and compare with an effective Lagrangian prediction at leading order. Chiral extrapolations between different pion masses are performed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a coupled-channel three-body quantization condition for the isospin \(I=2\) three-pion system, which has no three-body resonance but contains a \(\rho(770)\) resonance in a sub-channel, and applies it to lattice QCD finite-volume spectra at two pion masses. The analysis requires extending the formalism to include both \(\pi\rho\) and \(\pi G\) (isotensor \(\pi\pi\) S-wave) channels, and the authors find that the three-body force can be constrained from lattice data despite the absence of a three-body resonance.

## Method
The authors calculated finite-volume energy spectra from lattice QCD at two different pion masses and applied a generalized FVU (finite-volume unitarity) three-body quantization condition to map the spectrum to infinite-volume amplitudes. The formalism was extended to a coupled-channel system including \(\pi\rho\) (with helicity \(\lambda=-1,0,+1\)) and \(\pi G\) (isotensor \(\pi\pi\) S-wave) channels, with the two-body sub-amplitudes parametrized via the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM). The three-body force \(\tilde C\) was fitted to lattice data for several channel combinations and cutoffs, and chiral extrapolations between the two pion masses were performed using an effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD calculations were performed at two different pion masses (values not specified in the truncated text).
- The isospin factors for the coupled-channel system are: \(\tilde I_F(\pi\rho\to\pi\rho)=1/2\), \(\tilde I_F(\pi\rho\to\pi G)=\sqrt{3}/2\), \(\tilde I_F(\pi G\to\pi G)=-1/2\).
- The matching point for the two-body K-matrix in the unphysical region was chosen as \(\sigma_0=4m_\pi^2\).
- The three-body force \(\tilde C_{L'L}\) is expanded as \(\tilde C_{L'L}(s,p',p)=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (p'/m_\pi)^{L'}\, \tilde c_{L'L}^{\,k}s^k (p/m_\pi)^{L}\) with real coefficients \(\tilde c^{\,k}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of maximal-isospin three-pion systems (Mai 2018, Blanton 2019, Culver 2019, Hansen 2020, Fischer 2020) and resonant three-pion systems such as the \(a_1(1260)\) (Mai 2021) and \(\omega(782)\) (Yan 2024) to the intermediate-isospin \(I=2\) case. It is similar to a recent analysis by Briceno (2025) and enables future studies of three-body dynamics where a sub-channel resonance couples to multiple isobar-spectator configurations. The effective Lagrangian approach of Birse (1996) is used for chiral extrapolations and comparison with the narrow-\(\rho\) limit.
