# Four nucleon systems : a zoom to the open problems in nuclear interaction

> Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space are used to solve four nucleon problem for bound and scattering states. Different realistic interaction models are tested, elucidating open problems in nuclear interaction description. On one hand, by example of nonlocal Doleschall potential, we reveal possibility of reducing three-nucleon force. On the other hand we disclose discrepancies in describing n+$^3$H resonance, which seems to be hardly related with off-shell structure of nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonlocal nucleon-nucleon potentials (specifically the INOY family) can reduce the need for explicit three-nucleon forces in describing four-nucleon binding energies, but that discrepancies in describing the n+³H resonance region persist and appear to be related to the off-shell structure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, particularly in P-waves.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space for four-nucleon bound and scattering states, including the Coulomb interaction. They performed partial-wave decompositions of the FY amplitudes and expanded the components in a basis of three-dimensional splines, converting the integro-differential equations into a system of linear equations. They tested several realistic interaction models: local potentials (AV18, AV14, MT I-III), local potentials with three-nucleon forces (AV18+UIX), and nonlocal potentials (INOY96, INOY03, INOY04, INOY04').

## Key numbers

- INOY04 predicts an α-particle binding energy of 29.11 MeV, overestimating the experimental value (28.30 MeV) by ~800 keV.
- AV18 alone predicts an α-particle binding energy of 24.22 MeV, while AV18+UIX predicts 28.50 MeV (from Nogga et al.).
- INOY04 predicts an α-particle rms radius of 1.377 fm, which is ~10% smaller than the experimental value of 1.47 fm.
- n-³H scattering lengths for INOY04: 4.00 fm (Jπ=0⁺) and 3.52 fm (Jπ=1⁺).
- p-³H scattering lengths for AV18+UIX: -16.5 fm (Jπ=0⁺) and 5.39 fm (Jπ=1⁺); for MT I-III: -63.1 fm (Jπ=0⁺) and 5.50 fm (Jπ=1⁺).
- The 0⁺ resonance in p+³H is located at ER ≈ 0.4 MeV above threshold with width Γ ≈ 0.5 MeV.
- To reproduce n-³H resonance cross sections with the AV18 model, n-n P-wave potentials must be changed by ~20%.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of four-nucleon bound states (the "alpha benchmark" studies) and continuum calculations by the Pisa group using Hyperspherical Harmonics (Viviani et al.) and by Hofmann et al. The results confirm the Tjon-line correlation between α-particle and three-nucleon binding energies, which is a consequence of effective range theory (Platter). The paper shows that nonlocal potentials like INOY can reduce the need for explicit three-nucleon forces, but that the n-³H resonance region remains problematic. The authors suggest that charge dependence in n-n P-waves, which are not directly constrained by experiment, may explain the observed discrepancies, similar to the A_y puzzle in n-d scattering studied by Glöckle and the Pisa group. This work enables future studies to use four-nucleon continuum observables as more stringent tests of the off-shell structure of nuclear interactions.
