# Light Baryon Spectroscopy

> This review treats the advances in Light Baryon Spectroscopy of the last two decades, which were mainly obtained by measuring meson-production reactions at photon facilities all over the world. We provide a consistent compendium of experimental results, as well as a review of the theoretical methods of amplitude analysis used to analyze the data. The most significant datasets are presented in detail and are listed in combination with a full set of the relevant references. In addition, a brief summary of spin-formalisms, which are ubiquitous in Light Baryon Spectroscopy, as well as a review on complete experiments, are provided. The synthesis of the reviewed knowledge is presented in a full interpretation of the new results on the Light Baryon Spectrum.

## Key claim

This review paper provides a comprehensive compendium of experimental results and theoretical methods in light baryon spectroscopy from the last two decades, but the authors note that the field has reached "high precision physics" primarily through photoproduction measurements at photon facilities, while acknowledging that future progress depends on new experiments like CLAS12, GlueX, PANDA, and the KLF facility that are still under construction or in planning stages.

## Method

The authors systematically catalog experimental facilities and detector setups used for light baryon spectroscopy, including GRAAL at ESRF, TAPS and Crystal Ball/TAPS at MAMI, SAPHIR and CBELSA/TAPS at ELSA, CLAS at Jefferson Lab, LEPS and LEPS2/BGOegg at SPring-8, and FOREST and NKS2 at ELPH. They review the spin formalisms used in amplitude analysis, including decompositions of the transition matrix into spin-amplitudes (CGLN amplitudes for photoproduction), polarization observables, and partial-wave expansions in the helicity formalism.

## Key numbers

- GRAAL experiment operated from 1995 to 2008 at ESRF with electrons at 6.03 GeV, providing tagged photons from 550 MeV to 1500 MeV with polarization degree 96-98% at the Compton edge
- MAMI-C upgrade (2006) provides electrons up to 1604 MeV energy
- ELSA accelerator provides electron beams with energies up to 3.5 GeV
- LEPS experiment uses tagged photon energy range from 1.5 GeV to 2.9 GeV with forward spectrometer acceptance of ±20° horizontal and ±10° vertical
- LEPS2/BGOegg provides tagged photon beam from 1.3 to 2.4 GeV with BGOegg calorimeter covering polar angles from 24° to 144°
- NKS2 spectrometer uses dipole magnet with magnetic field strength of 0.42 T at center
- Crystal Ball calorimeter has over 1300 CsI(Tl) detector modules
- FOREST detector covers polar angular range from 4° to 175°

## Context

This review extends the comprehensive treatment of light baryon spectroscopy beyond earlier compilations by covering the last two decades of experimental advances, particularly from photoproduction facilities worldwide. The authors connect to specific prior experimental programs including the Crystal Ball detector at BNL, experiments at PSI and ITEP-PNPI, and the GDH experiment collaboration between Mainz and ELSA. The review enables future work by providing a complete reference for datasets and formalisms, while identifying open questions that will be addressed by upcoming facilities such as CLAS12 for electroproduction studies of hybrid states, the GlueX experiment for exotic mesonic states, PANDA at FAIR for hyperon spectroscopy, and the planned KLF facility at JLab for strange hadron spectroscopy.
