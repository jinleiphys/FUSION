# Novel description of $P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, and $P_c(4457)^+$ with double triangle cusps

> We propose a novel scenario for the peak structures, usually interpreted as hidden charm pentaquark ($P_c$) contributions, in the LHCb's $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$ data. The key idea is to utilize leading or lower-order singularities from double triangle mechanisms. The singularities cause anomalous threshold cusps, which are significantly more singular than the ordinary ones, at the $Σ_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ threshold. We demonstrate that the double triangle amplitudes interfere with other common mechanisms to create peak structure that fit well the $P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, and $P_c(4457)^+$ peaks in the data. This picture is completely different from commonly used ones such as hadron molecules and compact pentaquarks. Meanwhile, $P_c(4440)^+$ is included in the proposed model as a resonance with width and strength significantly smaller than previously estimated. The proposed model can (partly) explain the current data for other processes where $P_c^+$ signals are expected such as: no $P_c^+$ signals in the GlueX $J/ψ$ photoproduction data; a possible signal only from $P_c(4440)^+$ in the LHCb's $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp π^-$ data.

## Key claim

The peak structures in LHCb's $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-$ data usually interpreted as hidden charm pentaquark states ($P_c(4312)^+$, $P_c(4380)^+$, $P_c(4457)^+$) can instead be described by anomalous threshold cusps from double triangle mechanisms, with only $P_c(4440)^+$ required as a genuine resonance whose width and strength are significantly smaller than previously estimated.

## Method

The author constructs a model for $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-$ that includes double triangle diagrams with $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ intermediate states, one-loop diagrams with $\Lambda_c^{(*,**)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ pairs, a Breit-Wigner resonance for $P_c(4440)^+$, and direct decay mechanisms. The $Y_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ interactions are described with a single-channel contact interaction model combined with a perturbative transition to $J/\psi p$, and the full amplitude is fitted to LHCb data using 26 parameters including complex couplings for each mechanism, the $P_c(4440)^+$ mass and width, and a repulsive interaction strength for $\Lambda_c^+\bar{D}^{*0}$.

## Key numbers

- $P_c(4440)^+$ Breit-Wigner mass: $4443.1\pm 1.4$ MeV; width: $2.7\pm 2.4$ MeV (from this analysis)
- LHCb analysis values for $P_c(4440)^+$: mass $4440.3\pm 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}$ MeV; width $20.6\pm 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}$ MeV
- $P_c(4440)^+$ contribution from this analysis is smaller by a factor of approximately 22 than the LHCb's estimate of ${\cal R}\equiv {\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0\to P_c^+K^-){\cal B}(P_c^+\to J/\psi p)/{\cal B}(\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi pK^-)=1.11\pm 0.33^{+0.22}_{-0.10}\%$
- Scattering length for attractive $Y_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ channels: $a \sim 0.5$ fm
- Repulsive $\Lambda_c^+\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering length: $a \sim -0.4$, $-0.2$, and $-0.05$ fm for cutoffs $\Lambda\sim 0.8$, $1$, and $1.5$--$2$ GeV respectively
- Common cutoff used: $\Lambda=1$ GeV at each interaction vertex

## Context

This work proposes a kinematical explanation for the $P_c$ peaks observed by LHCb (2019), in contrast to the commonly used interpretations as hadron molecules or compact pentaquarks. The double triangle cusp mechanism can also explain the absence of $P_c^+$ signals in GlueX $J/\psi$ photoproduction data and the possible signal only from $P_c(4440)^+$ in LHCb's $\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p \pi^-$ data. The approach extends the use of anomalous threshold cusps from $S$-matrix theory to describe resonancelike structures near thresholds, and the author suggests these cusps should now be considered as an option for interpreting such structures in other processes.
