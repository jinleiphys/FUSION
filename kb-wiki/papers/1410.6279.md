# An investigation of the role of spectroscopic factors in the breakup reaction of 11Be

> The experimental elastic cross section data of the projectile 11Be on target 12C at 49.3 MeV/nucleon energy is analysed. The calculations for the elastic scattering is performed by the phenomenological optical model. The different optical potentials to include breakup effects into the calculations, which are neutron+12C, neutron+10Be and 10Be+12C are described with the aid of the global potentials for neutron interactions and fitted to experimental data for the core and target interaction. Also, the first analysis of the optical model for 10Be on target 12C at 39.1 MeV is done for building the interaction potential of the core and the target for 11Be. For investigating the effects of the spectroscopic factor obtained from the direct capture process using the nuclear level density are compared with the previous cross section and spectroscopic factor results. Obtained results for the elastic cross section are reproduced the experimental data very well, and shows the requirement of including spectroscopic properties such as the spectroscopic factors and the density of the excited states to explain this elastic cross section data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the spectroscopic factor obtained from a direct neutron capture calculation using a new nuclear level density model (Canbula 2014) significantly improves the reproduction of elastic scattering data for 11Be on 12C at 49.3 MeV/nucleon, but the spectroscopic factor value of 1.48 is an average value and the direct capture data could not be reproduced below 0.5 MeV neutron energy.

## Method

The authors treat the halo projectile 11Be as a two-body system (10Be core plus valence neutron) and describe the interactions using phenomenological optical potentials for n+12C, n+10Be, and 10Be+12C. The 10Be+12C potential parameters are fitted to elastic scattering data at 39.1 MeV/nucleon, the first optical model analysis of that system. The spectroscopic factor for 11Be is determined by calculating the direct neutron capture cross section for 10Be(n,gamma)11Be using a Laplace-like formula for the nuclear level density parameter, and comparing to deduced data from Coulomb dissociation.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy of 11Be projectile on 12C target: 49.3 MeV/nucleon
- Incident energy for 10Be+12C elastic scattering: 39.1 MeV/nucleon
- Neutron separation energy of 11Be: 0.503 MeV
- Neutron separation energy of 10Be: 6.81 MeV
- Spectroscopic factor obtained from direct capture calculation: 1.48
- Spectroscopic factors from Schmitt et al. (2012) used for comparison: 0.71 (ground state) and 0.62 (first excited state)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter beta2 for 12C first excited state: -0.6
- Coulomb potential radius parameter rc: 1.20 fm
- Level density parameters used: a-tilde = 1.345, beta = 0.285

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of 11Be elastic scattering on 12C at 49.3 MeV/nucleon by Johnson (1997) using an adiabatic approximation, Al-Khalili et al. (1997) using the few-body Glauber model, and Takashina et al. (2003) using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method. Unlike those studies, the authors incorporate nuclear structure information through the spectroscopic factor obtained from a direct capture calculation using a new nuclear level density model (Canbula 2014). This is the first optical model analysis of 10Be+12C elastic scattering at 39.1 MeV/nucleon, and the first use of nuclear level density as a spectroscopic tool in a light exotic nuclei induced reaction. The approach enables future reaction calculations that include nuclear structure effects for weakly-bound halo systems.
