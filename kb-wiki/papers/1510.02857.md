# Realistic estimate of valence transversity from dihadron production

> We have updated our extraction of the transversity parton distribution based on the analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering off transversely polarized targets in collinear factorization. The most recent COMPASS data for proton and deuteron targets, complemented by previous HERMES data on the proton, make it possible to perform a flavor separation of the valence components of the transversity distribution, using di-hadron fragmentation functions taken from the semi-inclusive production of two pion pairs in back-to-back jets in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation. The $e^+ e^-$ data from BELLE have been reanalyzed to reach a more realistic estimate of the uncertainties on the chiral-odd interference fragmentation function. Our results represent the most accurate estimate of the uncertainties on the valence components of the transversity distribution currently available.

## Key claim
The updated extraction of the valence transversity distribution from dihadron production data provides the most accurate estimate of uncertainties currently available, but the error analysis using the replica method gives a more realistic description of the uncertainty specifically for large x outside the data range, where the replicas tend to fill all the phase space available within the Soffer bound.

## Method
The transversity distribution was extracted by simultaneously fitting semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data for pion-pair production off transversely polarized proton and deuteron targets from COMPASS and HERMES, using collinear factorization. The chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) were obtained from the BELLE data for the process e+ e- to (pi+ pi-) (pi+ pi-) X, reanalyzed using a replica method with M=100 Gaussian-noise-perturbed replicas to estimate uncertainties. The valence flavor separation of transversity was achieved by combining the proton and deuteron SIDIS asymmetries, with the functional form constrained by the Soffer inequality at the starting scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- The fit of the chiral-odd interference fragmentation function H_1^{spherical angle} from BELLE data achieved a final chi^2/dof of 0.57 using the traditional Hessian method.
- The replica method used M=100 replicas to determine the 68% uncertainty band for the DiFF extraction.
- At Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, the tensor charge for the flexible scenario with alpha_S(M_Z^2) = 0.125 gives delta u_v = 0.25 +/- 0.05 and delta d_v = -0.25 +/- 0.12.
- The analysis covers the x range 0.0065 <= x <= 0.29 where data exist.
- The STAR asymmetry A_pp was measured at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 200 GeV at average pseudorapidity <eta> = -0.5.

## Context
This work extends the earlier extraction by Bacchetta et al. (2011) and the first flavor-separated parametrization of transversity valence components (h1JHEP), using updated COMPASS proton data from 2014 and a refined uncertainty analysis of the BELLE e+ e- data. The results are consistent with the Collins-effect extraction by Anselmino et al. (2013) and the TMD-evolution calculation by Kang et al. (2014), but provide more realistic uncertainties at large x. The extracted transversity enables predictions for the STAR p p^uparrow to (pi+ pi-) X asymmetry, providing a test of universality, and the tensor charge estimates can be compared with future Jefferson Lab 12 GeV data.
