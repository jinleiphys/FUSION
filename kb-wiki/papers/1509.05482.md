# Spectra and elliptic flow for $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Ω$ in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions

> Using VISHNU hybrid model, we calculate the $p_{\rm T}$-spectra and elliptic flow of $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Ω$ in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions. Comparisons with the STAR measurements show that the model generally describes these soft hadron data. We also briefly study and discuss the mass ordering of elliptic flow among $π$, $K$, $p$, $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Ω$ in minimum bias Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The VISHNU hybrid model generally describes the pT-spectra and elliptic flow of Λ, Ξ, and Ω in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, but it fails to reproduce the mass ordering of elliptic flow among p, Λ, and Ξ, despite correctly describing the ordering among π, K, p, and Ω.

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU hybrid model, which couples (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP phase with the UrQMD hadronic transport model for the hadron resonance gas evolution. Smooth initial conditions were generated from the MC-Glauber model by averaging many fluctuating entropy density profiles in the participant plane, with initial time τ0=0.6 fm/c, specific shear viscosity (η/s)QGP=0.08, and switching temperature 165 MeV. The model calculations were compared to STAR measurements of pT-spectra and elliptic flow for Λ, Ξ, and Ω at various centralities.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ0=0.6 fm/c and specific shear viscosity (η/s)QGP=0.08 used for all calculations.
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade set at 165 MeV.
- VISHNU slightly over-predicts the production of Ω for all centrality bins in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions.
- For 0-10% centrality, VISHNU results for elliptic flow are slightly above the STAR data for Λ, Ξ, and Ω.
- VISHNU correctly describes the v2 mass ordering among π, K, p, and Ω but fails to reproduce the mass ordering among p, Λ, and Ξ in minimum bias Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier VISHNU calculations at LHC energies (Song and collaborators, 2013) to top RHIC energy (200 A GeV Au+Au collisions) and compares with recent STAR data. The study builds on previous successful descriptions of pT-spectra and elliptic flow for pions, kaons, and protons at RHIC (Song and collaborators, 2011). The failure to reproduce the mass ordering among p, Λ, and Ξ suggests that initial flow effects or improved UrQMD hadronic cross-sections may need to be investigated within the VISHNU framework to resolve this issue.
