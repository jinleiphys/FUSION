# Constraints on vector mesons with finite momentum in nuclear matter

> Using the QCD operator product expansion, we derive the real part of the transverse and longitudinal vector-vector correlation function with the quantum numbers of the rho and omega mesons to leading order in density and three momentum (q^2) for energy $ω^2 --> -\infty $. The operator product expansion provides, through the Borel transformed energy dispersion relation, a model independent constraint for the momentum dependence of the vector meson spectral density in nuclear matter. Existing model calculations for the dispersion effect of the rho meson, where the vector-meson nucleon scattering amplitude is obtained by resonance saturation in the s-channel, in general violate this constraint. We trace this to an inconsistent choice for the form factor of the $ΔNρ$ vertex. With a consistent choice, where both the form factor and the coupling constant are obtained from the Bonn potential, the contribution of the $Δ$ is substantially reduced and we find good agreement with the constraint equation. We briefly comment on the implications of our result for attempts to interpret the enhancement of low-mass dileptons in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The QCD operator product expansion provides a model-independent constraint on the three-momentum dependence of vector meson spectral densities in nuclear matter, but existing hadronic model calculations for the rho meson dispersion effect generally violate this constraint unless the form factor for the ΔNρ vertex is chosen consistently with the Bonn potential.

## Method

The authors derived the operator product expansion (OPE) for the transverse and longitudinal vector-vector correlation functions with rho and omega meson quantum numbers, expanded to leading order in density and three-momentum squared (q²) for ω² → -∞. They then applied a Borel-transformed energy dispersion relation to obtain constraints on the spectral density, and compared these constraints with hadronic model calculations where the vector-meson nucleon scattering amplitude is obtained by s-channel resonance saturation, using parameters from Peters et al. (1998) and Rapp et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- The minimum Borel mass M²_min for reliable OPE is approximately 0.8 GeV² for transverse polarization and 2.5 GeV² for longitudinal polarization, where dimension-6 operator contributions are about 40% of dimension-4 contributions.
- The twist-4 matrix element Z₁ ≈ -0.06 GeV², Z₂ ≈ 0.56 GeV², and Z₃ ≈ -0.04 GeV², determined from deep inelastic scattering data at CERN and SLAC.
- The form factor cutoff parameter Λ_ρ = 1.4 GeV, with n_ρ = 1 for the NNρ vertex and n_ρ = 2 for the ΔNρ vertex, reducing the effective coupling at q=0 by a factor of 0.7 for the nucleon and 0.49 for the Δ(1232).
- The continuum threshold s₀ = 1.43 GeV² is fixed by the QCD sum rule at q = 0 from Hatsuda and Lee (1992).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) for vector mesons at zero momentum to finite three-momentum, providing constraints that test hadronic model calculations by Rapp et al. (1997) and Peters et al. (1998). The results show that the strong effective ΔNρ coupling used in those models is incompatible with QCD constraints, but consistent treatment using form factors from the Bonn potential (Machleidt et al., 1987) resolves the discrepancy. This enables more reliable interpretation of low-mass dilepton enhancement in heavy-ion collisions observed by the CERES collaboration.
