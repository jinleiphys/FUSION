# Dissipative tidal effects to next-to-leading order and constraints on the dissipative tidal deformability using gravitational wave data

> Dissipative tidal interactions can be used to probe the out-of-equilibrium physics of neutron stars using gravitational wave observations. In this paper, we present the first post-Newtonian (PN) corrections to the orbital dynamics of a binary system containing objects whose tidal interactions have a dissipative contribution. We derive the 1PN-accurate equations of motion in the center-of-mass frame and a generalized energy-balance law that is valid for dissipative tidal interactions. We show how mass and energy loss due to the absorption of orbital energy change the orbital dynamics and derive the next-to-leading order correction to the gravitational wave phase of a binary system in a quasi-circular orbit containing initially non-spinning components. We then use this waveform model to constrain, for the first time, the individual dissipative tidal deformabilities of each of the binary components that generated the GW170817 event using real data. We find that the GW170817 data requires $Ξ_{1} \lesssim 1121$ and $Ξ_{2} \lesssim 1692$ at 90\% confidence, where $Ξ_{1,2}$ are the individual tidal deformabilities of the primary and secondary binary components that produced the GW170817 event.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first post-Newtonian (PN) corrections to the orbital dynamics of a binary system with dissipative tidal interactions, deriving a 5PN correction (relative to the leading point-particle term) to the gravitational wave phase. Using this model, the authors place the first constraints on the *individual* dissipative tidal deformabilities of the binary components that produced GW170817, finding upper limits of $\Xi_{1} \lesssim 1121$ and $\Xi_{2} \lesssim 1692$ at 90% confidence, though the data is not sufficiently informative to measure these quantities independently.

## Method
The authors specialized the Vines and Flanagan (2010) formalism for 1PN-accurate equations of motion to include dissipative quadrupolar tidal interactions, deriving a generalized energy-balance law that accounts for mass and energy loss due to absorption of orbital energy. They then derived the next-to-leading order (1PN) correction to the gravitational wave phase for a quasi-circular binary with initially non-spinning components. Finally, they performed a Bayesian analysis using this waveform model on the GW170817 event to constrain the individual dissipative tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- The GW170817 data requires $\Xi_{1} \lesssim 1121$ and $\Xi_{2} \lesssim 1692$ at 90% confidence, where $\Xi_{1,2}$ are the individual dissipative tidal deformabilities of the primary and secondary binary components.
- The leading-order dissipative tidal effect enters the gravitational wave phase at 4PN order relative to the leading point-particle term, and the next-to-leading order correction derived here enters at 5PN order.

## Context
This work extends the leading-order dissipative tidal waveform model used in Ripley (2023) to constrain a combination of dissipative tidal deformabilities from GW170817. By adding the next-to-leading order PN correction, the authors break the degeneracy between the individual deformabilities, enabling the first direct constraints on $\Xi_1$ and $\Xi_2$ for neutron stars. This enables future work to map these constraints to bounds on the bulk or shear viscosities inside neutron stars, building on the theoretical framework for calculating dissipative tidal deformabilities for viscous fluids developed in Hegade K. R. (2024).
