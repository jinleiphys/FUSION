# Basic quantities of the Equation of State in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> Based on the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, six basic quantities of the EoS in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are expressed in terms of the nucleon kinetic energy $t(k)$, the isospin symmetric and asymmetric parts of the single-nucleon potentials $U_0(ρ,k)$ and $U_{\text{\text{sym,i}}}(ρ,k)$. The six basic quantities include the quadratic symmetry energy $E_{\text{sym,2}}(ρ)$, the quartic symmetry energy $E_{\text{sym,4}}(ρ)$, their corresponding density slopes $L_2(ρ)$ and $L_4(ρ)$, and the incompressibility coefficients $K_2(ρ)$ and $K_4(ρ)$. By using four types of well-known effective nucleon-nucleon interaction models, namely the BGBD, MDI, Skyrme, and Gogny forces, the density- and isospin-dependent properties of these basic quantities are systematically calculated and their values at the saturation density $ρ_0$ are explicitly given. The contributions to these quantities from $t(k)$, $U_0(ρ,k)$, and $U_{\text{sym,i}}(ρ,k)$ are also analyzed at the normal nuclear density $ρ_0$. It is clearly shown that the first-order asymmetric term $U_{\text{sym,1}}(ρ,k)$ (also known as the symmetry potential in Lane potential) plays a vital role in determining the density dependence of the quadratic symmetry energy $E_{\text{sym,2}}(ρ)$. It is also shown that the contributions from high-order asymmetric parts of the single-nucleon potentials ($U_{\text{sym,i}}(ρ,k)$ with $i>1$) cannot be neglected in the calculations of the other five basic quantities. Moreover, by analyzing the properties of asymmetric nuclear matter at the exact saturation density $ρ_{\text{sat}}(δ)$, the corresponding quadratic incompressibility coefficient is found to have a simple empirical relation $K_{\text{sat,2}}=K_{2}(ρ_0)-4.14 L_2(ρ_0)$.

## Key claim

Based on the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, the six basic quantities of the equation of state in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter can be expressed analytically in terms of the nucleon kinetic energy and the symmetric and asymmetric parts of the single-nucleon potential; the first-order asymmetric term (symmetry potential) plays a vital role in determining the density dependence of the quadratic symmetry energy, while contributions from higher-order asymmetric parts cannot be neglected for the other five quantities.

## Method

The authors derived general expressions for the quadratic and quartic symmetry energies, their density slopes, and their incompressibility coefficients using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, expanding both sides of the neutron and proton Fermi energy equations in powers of isospin asymmetry delta. They then calculated these six quantities at saturation density using 25 parameter sets from four effective interaction models: BGBD (2 sets), MDI (3 sets with x = -1, 0, 1), Skyrme (16 sets), and Gogny (4 sets). The contributions from the kinetic energy and each order of the single-nucleon potential were decomposed and analyzed at normal nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Average quadratic symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym,2(rho_0) = 32.0 MeV (constraint from literature: 31.7 +/- 3.2 MeV)
- Average density slope of quadratic symmetry energy: L_2(rho_0) = 57.0 MeV (constraint from literature: 58.7 +/- 28.1 MeV)
- Average quartic symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym,4(rho_0) = 0.64 MeV (constraint from literature: 0.62 MeV)
- Average quartic density slope: L_4(rho_0) = 1.42 +/- 2.14 MeV
- Average quadratic incompressibility coefficient: K_2(rho_0) = -123.6 +/- 83.8 MeV
- Average quartic incompressibility coefficient: K_4(rho_0) = -1.25 +/- 5.89 MeV
- Average skewness of symmetric nuclear matter: J_0(rho_0) = -411.3 +/- 37.0 MeV
- Empirical relation found: K_sat,2 = K_2(rho_0) - 4.14 L_2(rho_0)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Xu (2011), Wan (2017), and others who re-analyzed the symmetry energies and their slopes, by providing for the first time analytical expressions for the incompressibility coefficients K_2 and K_4 in terms of single-nucleon potentials. The systematic comparison across BGBD, MDI, Skyrme, and Gogny interactions shows that the average values of E_sym,2(rho_0) and L_2(rho_0) are consistent with constraints from Li (2013) and Oertel (2017), while the high-order terms L_4, K_2, and K_4 have large uncertainties. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star properties, particularly at high densities where the skewness J_0 and the incompressibility coefficients become important.
