# An equations-of-motion approach to quantum mechanics: application to a model phase transition

> We present a generalized equations-of-motion method that efficiently calculates energy spectra and matrix elements for algebraic models. The method is applied to a 5-dimensional quartic oscillator that exhibits a quantum phase transition between vibrational and rotational phases. For certain parameters, 10 by 10 matrices give better results than obtained by diagonalising 1000 by 1000 matrices.

# An equations-of-motion approach to quantum mechanics: application to a model phase transition

## Key claim
The generalized equations-of-motion (RRKK) method can yield energy spectra and matrix elements accurate to eight significant figures for low-lying states using matrices as small as 10×10, even when the Hamiltonian's eigenstates in a conventional basis would require matrices of dimension 1000 or more for comparable accuracy. The method is demonstrated for a five-dimensional quartic oscillator that exhibits a quantum phase transition between vibrational and rotational phases, with the caveat that the approach works best when the system is far from the critical point and when the mass parameter M is large.

## Method
The method starts from a Hamiltonian expressed in terms of a spectrum generating algebra (SGA), here su(1,1), and directly determines matrix representations of the algebra's observables in the energy eigenbasis by minimizing an objective function. The objective function enforces the commutation relations of the Lie algebra, the equations of motion for the observables, and the Casimir invariant conditions, with a weighting factor that emphasizes low-energy states and a truncation to N+1 basis states for the infinite sums. Starting guesses are provided by the random phase approximation for small α (spherical vibrator limit) and by an asymptotic harmonic oscillator expansion about the deformed minimum for large α (rotor limit), with stepwise continuation through the transition region.

## Key numbers
- For α=2, v=2, M=50, the first excitation energy (2.420379968671) is reproduced to better than one part in 10^6 using only N=4 basis states.
- With N=10, the method yields the lowest 8 excitation energies for α=2, v=2, M=50 to at least 6 significant figures, with the first 4 energies exact to 12 digits.
- For v=2, α=50, M=5000, the method obtains all spectroscopic properties of the m=8 lowest states in 93 seconds using N=10, while diagonalization required matrices larger than 1500×1500 and took 250 seconds.
- The objective function F' reaches minimum values of order 10^{-20} in typical calculations.
- For α=0.5 (the critical point), the energy spectrum scales as M^{-1/3}, making results for any M inferable from a single calculation.

## Context
This work extends the equations-of-motion formalism of Kerman and Klein (1970s) and the variational technique of Rosensteel and Rowe (1980s) for computing Lie algebra representations, combining them into the RRKK approach. The method is applied to the five-dimensional quartic oscillator Hamiltonian of the nuclear collective model, which exhibits a quantum phase transition between spherical vibrational and deformed rotational phases as the parameter α crosses 0.5. The approach is complementary to conventional matrix diagonalization in a harmonic oscillator basis, which requires hundreds to thousands of basis states for strongly deformed systems (α ≫ 0.5, large M), and enables efficient computation of spectroscopic properties across the phase transition. The method is expected to be particularly advantageous for systems with many degrees of freedom but relatively simple spectrum generating algebras.
