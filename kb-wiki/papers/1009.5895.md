# Few-body decay and recombination in nuclear astrophysics

> Three-body continuum problems are investigated for light nuclei of astrophysical relevance. We focus on three-body decays of resonances or recombination via resonances or the continuum background. The concepts of widths, decay mechanisms and dynamic evolution are discussed. We also discuss results for the triple $α$ decay in connection with $2^+$ resonances and density and temperature dependence rates of recombination into light nuclei from $α$-particles and neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three-body continuum computations using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method can reliably describe resonance structures, decay mechanisms, and recombination rates for light nuclei in stellar environments, but the results depend critically on the position of resonances (such as the lowest 2+ resonance in 12C) which remain uncertain or controversial.

## Method

The authors employ the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method, where the total wave function is expanded in terms of hyperradial functions and angular functions depending on the hyperradius ρ and five angular coordinates. They solve coupled differential equations for the radial functions, where the effective potentials include an angular eigenvalue λ(ρ) and a non-adiabatic diagonal coupling Q(ρ) that accounts for angular restructuring. The method is applied to compute resonance widths from tunneling through hyperradial barriers, momentum distributions via Dalitz plots, and recombination rates for three-body processes.

## Key numbers

- The 0.856 MeV 9Be(5/2-)-resonance exhibits a lowest adiabatic potential with an attractive pocket at short distance; the two lowest potentials cross around ρ of 16 fm
- For 9Be(1/2+), the barrier in the adiabatic potential is entirely due to the Q-term (angular restructuring), with the partial wave decomposition changing drastically around ρ of 7 fm from a 5He+α to a 8Be+n structure
- The triple α reaction rate varies by almost two orders of magnitude when the lowest 2+ resonance energy in 12C is increased from 2 MeV to 5 MeV
- For the 2+ resonance placed at 1.38 MeV versus complete removal, the uncertainty in the triple α rate around 8 GK is approximately a factor of three
- The contribution from the 2+ resonance to triple α recombination is only significant for temperatures above 2-3 GK

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method developed by the authors and collaborators (Jensen et al., 2010; Garrido et al., 2010) to three-body continuum problems of astrophysical relevance. It directly addresses the triple α process leading to 12C, comparing results to the standard reference rate from Angulo et al. (1999). The paper also investigates recombination into 6He and 9Be from mixtures of α-particles and neutrons, and suggests nuclear four-body recombination as an alternative route to bypass the A=5,8 mass gaps, comparing nuclear and electromagnetic recombination rates with results from Sumiyoshi et al. (2002) and Caughlan et al. (1988).
