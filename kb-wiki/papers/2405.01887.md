# Ab initio calculation of hyper-neutron matter

> The equation of state (EoS) of neutron matter plays a decisive role in our understanding of the properties of neutron stars as well as the generation of gravitational waves in neutron star mergers. At sufficient densities, it is known that the appearance of hyperons generally softens the EoS, thus leading to a reduction in the maximum mass of neutron stars well below the observed values of about 2 solar masses. Even though repulsive three-body forces are known to solve this so-called "hyperon puzzle", so far performing \textit{ab initio} calculations with a substantial number of hyperons has remained elusive. In this work, we address this challenge by employing simulations based on Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory with up to 232 neutrons (pure neutron matter) and up to 116 $Λ$ hyperons (hyper-neutron matter) in a finite volume. We introduce a novel auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo algorithm, allowing us to simulate for both pure neutron matter and hyper-neutron matter systems up to 5 times the density of nuclear matter using a single auxiliary field without any sign oscillations. Also, for the first time in {\em ab initio} calculations, we not only include $NΛ$ two-body and $NNΛ$ three-body forces, but also $ΛΛ$ and $N ΛΛ$ interactions. Consequently, we determine essential astrophysical quantities such as the mass-radius relation, the speed of sound and the tidal deformability of neutron stars. Our findings also confirm the existence of the $I$-Love-$Q$ relation, which gives access to the moment of inertia of the neutron star.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that including repulsive hyperonic three-body forces (NNΛ and NΛΛ) in ab initio lattice calculations can produce a neutron-star equation of state stiff enough to support maximum masses above 2 solar masses, thereby resolving the "hyperon puzzle", but only for one of the three interaction variants (HNM(III)), and the resulting mass-radius curve is only marginally compatible with combined observational data.

## Method
The authors used Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with a novel auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo algorithm that employs a single auxiliary field without sign oscillations, enabling simulations of up to 232 neutrons and up to 116 Λ hyperons at densities up to 5 times nuclear saturation density. They fitted two-body NΛ and ΛΛ interactions to experimental cross-section and scattering data, and constrained three-body NNΛ and NΛΛ forces using hypernuclear separation energies and Λ threshold densities, producing three variants of hyper-neutron matter (HNM(I), HNM(II), HNM(III)).

## Key numbers
- Pure neutron matter (PNM) maximum mass: 2.19(1)(1) solar masses
- HNM(I) maximum mass: 1.59(1)(1) solar masses
- HNM(II) maximum mass: 1.94(1)(1) solar masses
- HNM(III) maximum mass: 2.17(1)(1) solar masses
- Λ threshold density for HNM(I): 0.325(2)(4) fm^{-3}
- Λ threshold density for HNM(II): 0.398(2)(5) fm^{-3}
- Λ threshold density for HNM(III): 0.520(2)(6) fm^{-3}
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses for PNM: 13.10(1)(7) km
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses for HNM(I): 12.71(4)(13) km
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses for HNM(II): 13.09(1)(8) km
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses for HNM(III): 13.10(1)(7) km
- Maximum density simulated: 0.8 fm^{-3} (5 times nuclear saturation density)

## Context
This work extends the NLEFT framework of Lahde and Epelbaum (2019) and the chiral N3LO calculations of Elhatisari (2022) to include hyperons, addressing the hyperon puzzle previously studied by Schulze (2011) and Lonardoni (2014) using AFDMC and BHF methods. The results enable future calculations with higher-order chiral forces, inclusion of proton fractions and other hyperons, and extension to rotating neutron stars, though the authors note that the minimal interaction model used may introduce uncertainties at the highest densities.
