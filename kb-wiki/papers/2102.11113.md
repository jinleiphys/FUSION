# Implementation of local chiral interactions in the hyperspherical harmonics formalism

> With the goal of using chiral interactions at various orders to explore properties of the few-body nuclear systems, we write the recently developed local chiral interactions as spherical irreducible tensors and implement them in the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method. We devote particular attention to three-body forces at next-to-next-to leading order, which play an important role in reproducing experimental data. We check our implementation by benchmarking the ground-state properties of $^3$H, $^3$He and $^4$He against the available Monte Carlo calculations. We then confirm their order-by-order truncation error estimates and further investigate uncertainties in the charge radii obtained by using the precise muonic atom data for single-nucleon radii. Having local chiral Hamiltonians at various orders implemented in our hyperspherical harmonics suites of codes opens up the possibility to test such interactions on other light-nuclei properties, such as electromagnetic reactions.

## Key claim

The authors implement recently developed local chiral interactions, including three-body forces at next-to-next-to leading order (N2LO), as spherical irreducible tensors for use in the hyperspherical harmonics formalism, and benchmark ground-state properties of ³H, ³He, and ⁴He against Monte Carlo calculations to validate the implementation.

## Method

The authors write the local chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions as products of irreducible tensors under space rotations, spin, and isospin, enabling their use in the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method. They solve the Schrödinger equation by expanding the nuclear wavefunction in symmetry-adapted hyperspherical harmonics up to a maximal grand-angular quantum number K_max and using generalized Laguerre polynomials for the hyper-radial part, then diagonalize the resulting eigenvalue equation. The 3N forces at N2LO (two-pion exchange, one-pion exchange, and contact terms) are rewritten explicitly as coupled spherical tensors in coordinate-spin and isospin space.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state properties of ³H, ³He, and ⁴He are benchmarked against Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations.
- The 3N force cut-off values used are r₀ = 1.0 fm and r₀ = 1.2 fm, with corresponding fitted LECs: for r₀ = 1.0 fm, c_E = -0.63, c_D = 0.0; for r₀ = 1.2 fm, c_E = 0.085, c_D = 3.5.
- The pion-nucleon LECs used are c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -3.20 GeV⁻¹, and c₄ = 3.40 GeV⁻¹, tuned in the pion-nucleon sector as reported in Machleidt and Entem (2011).
- The spectral-function regularization cut-off for pion loops is set to Λ̃ = 1 GeV, following Lynn et al. (2017).
- Good convergence is observed with n_max ≤ 50 for the Laguerre polynomial expansion.

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics method, previously applied with phenomenological potentials like the Argonne AV18 and AV8' potentials, to local chiral interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. The implementation enables order-by-order truncation error estimates and uncertainty quantification for few-body nuclear observables, following the development of maximally local chiral interactions by Gezerlis et al. (2014) and Lynn et al. (2016, 2017). Having these Hamiltonians available in the hyperspherical harmonics framework opens the possibility to test chiral interactions on other light-nuclei properties such as electromagnetic reactions and to investigate charge radii using precise muonic atom data for single-nucleon radii.
