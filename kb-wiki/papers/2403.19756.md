# Vortical waves in a quantum fluid with vector, axial and helical charges. II. Dissipative effects

> In this paper, we consider the effect of interactions on the local, average polarization of a quantum plasma of massless fermion particles characterized by vector, axial, and helical quantum numbers. Due to the helical and axial vortical effects, perturbations in the vector charge in a rotating plasma can lead to chiral and helical charge transfer along the direction of the vorticity vector. At the same time, interactions between the plasma constituents lead to the dissipation of the helical charge through helicity-violating pair annihilation (HVPA) processes and of the axial charge through the axial anomaly. We will discuss separately a QED-like plasma, in which we ignore background electromagnetic fields and thus the axial charge is approximately conserved, as well as a QCD-like plasma, where instanton effects lead to the violation of the axial charge conservation, even in the absence of background chromomagnetic fields. The non-conservation of helicity and chirality leads to a gapping of the Helical, Axial, and mixed Axial-Helical vortical waves that prevents their infrared modes from propagating. On the other hand, usual dissipative effects, such as charge diffusion, lead to significant damping of ultraviolet modes. We end this paper with a discussion of the regimes where these vortical waves may propagate.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a rotating quantum plasma of massless fermions, the non-conservation of helical and axial charges (due to helicity-violating pair annihilation and the axial anomaly, respectively) introduces a mass gap in the Helical, Axial, and mixed Axial-Helical vortical waves, preventing their infrared modes from propagating, while standard dissipative effects like charge diffusion damp the ultraviolet modes.

## Method

The authors employ a hydrodynamic description in the Landau frame for a Dirac fluid with vector, axial, and helical charges undergoing rigid rotation, extending their previous dissipationless analysis (Morales-Tejera 2024) by incorporating charge non-conservation via a relaxation time approximation. They consider linear perturbations around an unpolarized background (with zero axial and helical chemical potentials) and derive the dispersion relations for longitudinal modes along the vorticity direction, treating separately a QED-like plasma (where axial charge is approximately conserved) and a QCD-like plasma (where instanton effects violate axial charge conservation).

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound in the ultrarelativistic ideal fluid is \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\), valid to \(O(\Omega^2)\).
- The pressure for the unpolarized background is \(P = \frac{7\pi^2 T^4}{180} + \frac{\mu_V^2 T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu_V^4}{12\pi^2}\).
- The vector charge density is \(Q_V = \frac{\mu_V T^2}{3} + \frac{\mu_V^3}{3\pi^2}\).
- The axial vortical conductivity is \(\sigma_A^\omega = \frac{T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu_V^2}{2\pi^2}\).
- The helical vortical conductivity is \(\sigma_H^\omega = \frac{T^2}{\pi^2} \left[{\rm Li}_2(-e^{-\mu_V/T}) - {\rm Li}_2(-e^{\mu_V/T})\right]\).
- The acoustic mode dispersion relation is \(\omega^\pm_{\rm ac.} = \pm c_s k\) with \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the dissipationless analysis of vortical waves in a fluid with vector, axial, and helical charges (Morales-Tejera 2024) by including realistic dissipative effects, and builds on prior studies of the Chiral Vortical Wave (Jiang 2015, Gorbar 2017) and the Helical Vortical Wave (Ambrus 2019). The results enable future investigation of whether these vortical waves can propagate in realistic environments such as the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, and provide a framework for comparing with experimental signatures analogous to those sought for the Chiral Magnetic Wave (ALICE 2023).
