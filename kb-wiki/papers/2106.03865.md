# Partonic collinear structure by quantum computing

> We present a systematic quantum algorithm, which integrates both the hadronic state preparation and the evaluation of real-time light-front correlators, to study parton distribution functions (PDFs). As a proof of concept, we demonstrate the first direct simulation of the PDFs in the 1+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We show the results obtained by exact diagonalization and by quantum computation using classical hardware. The agreement between these two distinct methods and the qualitative consistency with QCD PDFs validate the proposed quantum algorithm. Our work suggests the encouraging prospects of calculating QCD PDFs on current and near-term quantum devices. The presented quantum algorithm is expected to have many applications in high energy particle and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic quantum algorithm for calculating parton distribution functions (PDFs) that integrates hadronic state preparation via a quantum-number-resolving variational quantum eigensolver with evaluation of real-time light-front correlators; the authors demonstrate the first direct simulation of PDFs in the 1+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as a proof of concept, but note that renormalization and calibration of input parameters for the NJL model are not yet considered in this initial exploration.

## Method

The authors discretize the 1+1D NJL model on a lattice with N/2 sites per flavor using staggered fermions, then apply the Jordan-Wigner transformation to map fermionic fields onto Pauli matrices for quantum computation. Hadronic states are prepared using a quantum-number-resolving variational quantum eigensolver with the quantum alternating operator ansatz (QAOA), where the Hamiltonian is decomposed into noncommuting symmetry-preserving pieces. The dynamical light-front correlation function D(z) is evaluated by encoding the real-time evolution into an ancillary qubit that controls and probes the system, with the real and imaginary parts extracted from ancilla measurements.

## Key numbers

- Hadron mass M_h a for the 2-flavor NJL model at m a = 0.2 and N=12: ranges from 1.002 (g=0.2) to 4.352 (g=1.0) from quantum computing, compared to 1.001 to 4.342 from exact diagonalization (percent-level differences)
- Simulation uses N = 12 or N = 18 qubits with lattice spacing a = 1
- Quark mass set to m a = 0.8 for the 1-flavor PDF calculation with N=18
- Total time complexity for calculating PDFs is estimated as O(N^3/epsilon), where N is the number of qubits and epsilon is the desired precision

## Context

This work extends the growing effort to apply quantum computing to nuclear physics, following proposals such as computing hadronic tensors (Lamm 2019), quantum simulations of space-time Wilson loops (Echevarria 2020), and hybrid approaches incorporating quantum computing as a subroutine (Mueller 2019). The results show qualitative agreement with PDFs calculated in two-dimensional QCD (Jia 2018) and with pion PDFs extracted by the JAM Collaboration (Barry 2021). The framework is expected to enable future calculations of scattering processes, parton showers, proton spin configurations, three-dimensional nucleon structure, and jet transport properties in quark-gluon plasma.
