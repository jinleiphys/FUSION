# Repulsive Vector Interaction in Three Flavor Magnetized Quark and Stellar Matter

> The effect of the vector interaction on three flavor magnetized matter is studied within the SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasiono quark model. We have considered cold matter under a static external magnetic field within two different models for the vector interaction in order to investigate how the form of the vector interaction and the intensity of the magnetic field affect the equation of state as well as the strangeness content. It was shown that the flavor independent vector interaction predicts a smaller strangeness content and, therefore, harder equations of state. On the other hand, the flavor dependent vector interaction favors larger strangeness content the larger the vector coupling. We have confirmed that at low densities the magnetic field and the vector interaction have opposite competing effects: the first one softens the equation of state while the second hardens it. Quark stars and hybrid stars subject to an external magnetic field were also studied. Larger star masses are obtained for the flavor independent vector interaction. Hybrid stars may bare a core containing deconfined quarks if neither the vector interaction nor the magnetic field are too strong. Also, the presence of strong magnetic fields seems to disfavor the existence of a quark core in hybrid stars.

# Repulsive Vector Interaction in Three Flavor Magnetized Quark and Stellar Matter

## Key claim
The flavor independent vector interaction (model P2) produces harder equations of state and smaller strangeness content than the flavor dependent vector interaction (model P1), but the authors note that their conclusions on maximum star masses should be regarded as upper limits because they did not consider the color superconducting phase in the interior of hybrid stars, which would make the equation of state softer.

## Method
The authors employed the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio quark model in a mean field approach, incorporating a static external magnetic field and two different vector interaction terms: a flavor dependent form (P1) where each flavor couples separately, and a flavor independent form (P2) where the total baryon density couples. They solved the gap equations for constituent quark masses self-consistently, computed the equation of state for cold matter at zero temperature, and then used the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar mass-radius relations for quark stars and hybrid stars under density-dependent magnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- Maximum quark star masses for model P2 with HK parametrization at zero magnetic field: 1.56, 1.72, and 1.91 solar masses for vector coupling ratios x = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.6 respectively (where x = G_V/G_S)
- Maximum quark star masses for model P2 with HK parametrization at B = 3.1 x 10^18 G: 1.96, 2.03, and 2.12 solar masses for x = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.6 respectively
- Maximum quark star masses for model P2 with RKH parametrization at zero magnetic field: 1.35, 1.54, and 1.74 solar masses for x = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.6 respectively
- Nuclear matter saturation density used: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Magnetic field conversion: eB = 1 GeV^2 corresponds to B = 1.685 x 10^20 G
- Magnetic field values studied: eB = 0, 0.1, 0.3, and 0.6 GeV^2 (corresponding to 5, 15, and 30 times m_pi^2)
- For the RKH parametrization with model P2, the first order phase transition disappears for x = 0.71

## Context
This work extends the two-flavor magnetized NJL study of Robson (2013) to include strangeness, and builds upon the three-flavor NJL investigations of the QCD phase diagram under magnetic fields by Menezes (2009) and Costa (2013). The authors compare two parametrizations of the NJL model, HK (Hatsuda, 1994) and RKH (Rehberg, 1996), and two vector interaction forms previously used by Klimt (1990), Hanauske (2000), and Fukushima (2008). The results enable predictions for the structure of quark stars and hybrid stars that can be compared with the observed 2-solar-mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest, 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (2013), and show that strong magnetic fields disfavor the existence of quark cores in hybrid stars.
