# Loosely bound three-body nuclear systems in the J-matrix approach

> We discuss the extension of the oscillator-basis $J$-matrix formalism on the case of true $A$-body scattering. The formalism is applied to loosely-bound $^{11}$Li and $^6$He nuclei within three-body cluster models ${\rm {^9Li}}+n+n$ and $α+n+n$. The $J$-matrix formalism is used not only for the calculation of the three-body continuum spectrum wave functions but also for the calculation of the $S$-matrix poles associated with the $^{11}$Li and $^6$He ground states to improve the description of the binding energies and ground state properties. The effect of the phase equivalent transformation of the $n{-}α$ interaction on the properties of the $^6$He nucleus is examined.

## Key claim

The paper extends the oscillator-basis J-matrix formalism to true A-body scattering, specifically applying it to the three-body cluster models of loosely-bound Borromean nuclei ¹¹Li and ⁶He, with the claim that calculating S-matrix poles improves the description of binding energies and ground state properties beyond what is achievable through variational diagonalization of the truncated Hamiltonian matrix alone.

## Method

The authors employ the J-matrix formalism with a hyperspherical oscillator basis to describe three-body systems, treating ¹¹Li as ⁹Li+n+n and ⁶He as α+n+n within the democratic decay approximation. They first diagonalize the truncated Hamiltonian matrix in the internal region to obtain variational results, then calculate the 3→3 scattering S-matrix using Eqs. (e413)–(e44) and extend this calculation to the complex momentum plane to locate S-matrix poles associated with bound states via Eq. (Poles). The bound state wave functions are then obtained using Eq. (boundOWF) in the external region and Eq. (e42) in the internal region, with normalization performed numerically.

## Key numbers

- The ¹¹Li two-neutron separation energy is 0.295 ± 0.035 MeV, cited from Benenson (experimental value)
- The Gaussian n-n potential has depth V₀ = 31 MeV and width R = 1.8 fm, fitted to low-energy singlet s-wave phase shifts
- The n-⁹Li two-Gaussian potential has parameters V₁ = 7 MeV, R₁ = 2.4 fm, V₂ = 1 MeV, and R₂ = 3.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix formalism previously developed for two-body scattering (Yamani and Reinhardt, Broad) and for N→N scattering (Smirnov and Shirokov) to the case of three-body Borromean nuclei, building on earlier studies of ¹²C in the α+α+α cluster model (Mikhailyuk) and ΛΛ hypernuclei (Zeit). The approach enables improved calculation of binding energies and ground state wave functions with correct asymptotic behavior for halo nuclei, which is important because variational methods using a finite number of oscillator functions cannot reproduce the slowly decreasing wave functions at large distances. The phase-equivalent transformation of the n-α interaction is also examined for its effect on ⁶He properties.
