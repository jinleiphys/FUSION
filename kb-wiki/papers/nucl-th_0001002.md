# Lambda-Neutron Scattering Lengths from Radiative K-minus Capture

> Radiative capture of the K-minus by the deuteron as a reaction for measurement of the Lambda-neutron scattering lengths. The use of spin information to separate the singlet and triplet scattering lengths is treated.

## Key claim

Radiative K⁻ capture on deuterium (K⁻d → nΛγ) can be used to extract both the singlet and triplet Λ-neutron scattering lengths, provided that spin information (deuteron polarization or photon circular polarization) is measured; without such spin information, the singlet and triplet contributions are incoherent and cannot be separated. The authors show that measuring the deviation of the vector deuteron polarization asymmetry from −1 provides a direct determination of the difference between the singlet and triplet scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors develop a partial-wave formalism for the reaction K⁻d → nΛγ, treating the final-state Λ-n interaction via Jost solutions for exponential potentials fitted to the Λ-proton phase shifts of Rijken et al. (1999). They compute the photon spectrum by analytically integrating over unobserved baryon momenta, separating contributions from different deuteron spin projections and photon polarizations, and compare results using asymptotic versus full Λ-n wave functions. The potentials are parameterized as sums of four exponentials with inverse ranges corresponding to two-pion exchange and shorter-range contributions, fitted to six cases (a–f) of the Rijken et al. potentials.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron scattering length from π⁻d → nnγ experiments is −18.58 fm with an uncertainty of ±0.5 fm (about 2–3%).
- The singlet scattering lengths from the six Rijken et al. cases range from −0.71 fm (case a) to −2.51 fm (case f) for the original potentials; the authors' 1 fm scale fits give −0.73 fm to −2.59 fm.
- The triplet scattering lengths from the six Rijken et al. cases range from −2.18 fm (case a) to −1.75 fm (case f); the authors' 1 fm scale fits give −2.21 fm to −1.74 fm.
- The effective ranges for the singlet (1 fm scale) range from 6.71 fm (case a) to 3.29 fm (case f); for the triplet they range from 2.99 fm (case a) to 3.42 fm (case f).
- The ratio of triplet to singlet rates, in the s-wave limit with an s-wave deuteron and equal scattering lengths, is approximately 2.

## Context

This work extends the technique used for measuring the neutron-neutron scattering length via π⁻d → nnγ (Phillips and Crowe, 1960; McVoy, 1961; Bander, 1961; subsequent PSI and LAMPF experiments) to the Λ-n system, as originally suggested by Gibson et al. (1985). It addresses two unresolved issues from the earlier analysis by Workman and Fearing (1993): the need for full short-range wave functions (since the theoretical uncertainty from short-range effects was ±0.3 fm for the nn case, and the Λ-n scattering length is an order of magnitude smaller) and the problem of separating singlet and triplet contributions (unlike the nn case where the Pauli principle restricts the final state to singlet). The formalism enables future experiments to extract both scattering lengths using polarized deuteron targets or photon polarization measurements.
