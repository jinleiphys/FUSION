# Mass number scaling in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions from a hydrodynamical approach

> We study the different nucleus-nucleus collisions, O+Au, S+S, S+Ag, S+Au and Pb+Pb, at the CERN-SPS energy in a one-fluid hydrodynamical approach using a parametrization based on baryon stopping in terms of the thickness of colliding nuclei. Good agreement with measured particle spectra is achieved. We deduce the mass number scaling behaviour of the initial energy density. We find that the equilibration time is nearly independent of the size of the colliding nuclei.

# Mass number scaling in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions from a hydrodynamical approach

## Key claim

The initial energy density in nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN-SPS energies scales with the product of effective mass numbers as (A_eff B_eff)^0.22, which lies between the Glauber model scaling of (AB)^1/3 and the wounded nucleon model scaling of (AB)^1/6; however, this scaling is extracted from fits to experimental pion spectra and depends on the equation of state used in the hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional ideal fluid hydrodynamic simulations of five different nucleus-nucleus collision systems (O+Au, S+S, S+Ag, S+Au, and Pb+Pb) at CERN-SPS energies, using a parametrization of initial baryon stopping and energy density based on the local nuclear thickness functions of the colliding nuclei in the transverse plane. The initial baryon rapidity distribution was parametrized as a function of the nuclear thickness T_A, with stopping strength controlled by a parameter beta_s = 2.25, and the initial energy distribution was taken as a Gaussian in rapidity with width sigma_epsilon depending on the product of thickness functions. The hydrodynamic evolution used an equation of state with a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at T_c = 165 MeV, and freeze-out was defined on a surface of constant energy density epsilon_f = 0.15 GeV/fm^3 (corresponding to T_f approximately 140 MeV), with an additional calculation for Pb+Pb at epsilon_f = 0.069 GeV/fm^3 (T_f approximately 120 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density scaling exponent: gamma = 0.22 from a fit to transversely averaged initial energy densities across five collision systems, using the form epsilon = epsilon_0 (A_eff B_eff)^gamma
- Fitted relation for model initial energy density: epsilon_R = 0.63 GeV/fm^3 (A_eff B_eff)^0.22
- Fitted relation for Bjorken estimates from experimental transverse energy: epsilon_Bj = 0.55 GeV/fm^3 (A_eff B_eff)^0.16
- Effective equilibration time tau_0_eff approximately 1.3 fm/c for all collisions from S+S to Pb+Pb, with variation of plus or minus 0.2 fm/c giving acceptable fits
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f = 0.15 GeV/fm^3 for standard freeze-out (T_f approximately 140 MeV); for Pb+Pb alternative: epsilon_f = 0.069 GeV/fm^3 (T_f approximately 120 MeV)
- Nuclear density parameters: n_0 = 0.17 fm^-3, a_R = 0.54 fm, nuclear radius R_A = 1.12 fm times A^1/3 minus 0.86 fm times A^-1/3
- Total inelastic pp cross section: sigma_pp = 32 mb at SPS energy
- Parameters for energy width: c_epsilon = 0.8, alpha_epsilon = 0.125
- Asymmetry correction parameter: alpha = 0.8 for momentum balance in asymmetric collisions
- Stopping parameter: beta_s = 2.25
- Ratio of initial energy densities: epsilon_S+Au / epsilon_Pb+Pb approximately 2/3

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic approaches by Sollfrank et al. (1997) and Ornik et al. (1991) by introducing a parametrization of initial conditions based on local nuclear thickness, which relates different collision systems and improves treatment of transverse geometry compared to previous approaches where different collisions were not connected. The mass number scaling result (gamma = 0.22) lies between the Glauber model prediction (AB)^1/3 and the wounded nucleon model prediction (AB)^1/6 from Bialas (1976), and the extracted scaling is compared with Bjorken estimates from experimental transverse energy data by Bachler (1991) and Margetis (1995). The study enables systematic predictions for initial energy densities in other collision systems at SPS energies and provides a basis for interpreting J/Psi suppression in terms of energy density ratios, as discussed by Gonin (1996).
