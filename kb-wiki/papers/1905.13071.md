# Effects of an induced three-body force in the incident channel of (d,p) reactions

> A widely accepted practice for treating deuteron breakup in $A(d,p)B$ reactions relies on solving a three-body $A+n+p$ Schrödinger equation with pairwise $A$-$n$, $A$-$p$ and $n$-$p$ interactions. However, it was shown in [Phys. Rev. C \textbf{89}, 024605 (2014)] that projection of the many-body $A+2$ wave function into the three-body $A+n+p$ channel results in a complicated three-body operator that cannot be reduced to a sum of pairwise potentials. It contains explicit contributions from terms that include interactions between the neutron and proton via excitation of the target $A$. Such terms are normally neglected. We estimate the first order contribution of these induced three-body terms and show that applying the adiabatic approximation to solving the $A+n+p$ model results in a simple modification of the two-body nucleon optical potentials. We illustrate the role of these terms for the case of $^{40}$Ca($d,p$)$^{41}$Ca transfer reactions at incident deuteron energies of 11.8, 20 and 56 MeV, using several parameterisations of nonlocal optical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that induced three-body (I3B) terms, which arise from target excitations coupling to different nucleons in the deuteron and are normally neglected in standard three-body models of (d,p) reactions, can be accounted for in the Adiabatic Distorted-Waves Approximation (ADWA) by a simple modification: doubling the dynamical (energy-dependent) part of the nucleon optical potentials used in the deuteron channel.

## Method

The authors start from the Feshbach projection formalism for the three-body n+p+A system and expand the three-body wave function in a Weinberg basis, retaining only the leading (first) Weinberg component as in the standard ADWA. They then derive an approximate expression for the first-order contribution of the I3B operator U^(1) in terms of the nucleon-target optical potentials, showing that it effectively doubles the dynamical (dispersive) part of these potentials while leaving the Hartree-Fock (energy-independent) part unchanged. Numerical calculations for the 40Ca(d,p)41Ca reaction are performed using the nonlocal dispersive optical model (NLDOM) and two phenomenological nonlocal potentials (GR and GRZ), solving the nonlocal Schrödinger equations exactly for both the deuteron and proton channels.

## Key numbers

- The shift in effective energy for the nucleon optical potentials due to the Weinberg averaging is ⟨T_r⟩/2 = 57 MeV, consistent with the Hulthén model for the deuteron.
- At Ed = 11.8 MeV, including I3B terms reduces the peak cross section by a factor of 0.725 (NLDOM), 0.729 (GR), and 0.630 (GRZ).
- At Ed = 20 MeV, the reduction factors are 0.691 (NLDOM), 0.706 (GR), and 0.621 (GRZ).
- At Ed = 56 MeV, the reduction factors are 0.821 (NLDOM), 0.842 (GR), and 0.786 (GRZ).
- The difference between exact nonlocal solutions and the local transcendental approximation for the deuteron channel is at most 5% at the main peak when I3B terms are included, compared to up to 10% without them.
- The NLDOM spectroscopic factor used for the 41Ca-40Ca overlap is S = 0.73.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Johnson and Tostevin (2014), who showed that projecting the many-body A+2 wave function into the three-body n+p+A channel introduces a three-body operator beyond pairwise potentials, but who only treated the pairwise interactions. The present paper provides a practical first-order estimate of these previously neglected I3B terms within the ADWA. The results suggest that I3B effects could significantly impact spectroscopic factors extracted from (d,p) experiments, and the finding that only the imaginary part of the dynamical potential needs modification when using non-dispersive potentials could enable applications to nuclei beyond 40Ca and 48Ca, where NLDOM potentials are currently unavailable.
