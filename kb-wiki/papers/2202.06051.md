# Artificial Dynamical Effects in Quantum Field Theory

> In Newtonian mechanics, inertial pseudoforces - or fictitious forces - appear in systems studied in non-Galilean reference frames; e.g., a centrifugal force seems to arise if the dynamics is analyzed in a rotating reference frame. The equivalent of Galilean invariance for relativistic kinematics is Poincaré invariance; analogous artificial effects may arise in relativistic quantum field theory (QFT) if a system is studied in a framework violating Poincaré invariance. We highlight how such issues complicate the traditional canonical quantization of QFTs and can lead to a subjective description of natural phenomena. In fact, if the system involves the strong interaction, obtaining objective results can become an intractable problem using canonical quantization because the pseudoforces are essentially nonperturbative. In contrast, the treatment of the same problem using light-front (LF) quantization is free of spurious pseudoeffects because Poincaré invariance is manifest; thus the treatment of strong interaction problems becomes simpler. These statements are illustrated using several examples: the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) relation, a fundamental feature of QFT; the absence of any measurable impact of Lorentz contraction in high-energy collisions; and the fictitious character of vacuum fluctuation contributions to the cosmological constant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that canonical (instant-form) quantization of quantum field theory violates Poincaré invariance, introducing artificial pseudoforces analogous to inertial forces in Newtonian mechanics; these pseudoforces make the description of strong-interaction systems subjective and often intractable, whereas light-front quantization avoids these issues because it preserves Poincaré invariance in practice.

## Method

The authors compare instant-form (IF) and light-front (LF) canonical quantization of relativistic quantum field theory, analyzing how each approach respects or violates Poincaré invariance. They examine three specific examples, the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) relation, Lorentz contraction in high-energy collisions, and vacuum energy contributions to the cosmological constant, to illustrate where IF quantization introduces frame-dependent pseudodynamics that are absent in LF quantization.

## Key numbers

- The GDH relation connects the anomalous magnetic moment κ of a particle to helicity-dependent photoproduction cross sections via the integral ∫₀^∞ [σ_P(ν) − σ_A(ν)]/ν dν = 4π²Sακ²/M², where S is spin, α is the fine-structure constant, and M is the rest mass.
- In the IF derivation of the GDH relation, a Lorentz-violating term appears: 4π²Sα(κ²/M² − (Z+κ)²/(p⁰)²), where p⁰ is the initial-state particle energy and Z its charge.
- Conventional estimates of vacuum energy from IF vacuum fluctuations yield values up to 10¹²⁰ times greater than cosmological observations.
- LF dynamics predicts Λ = 0 for the cosmological constant because the LF vacuum does not fluctuate.

## Context

This work extends the foundational analysis of Dirac (1949) on forms of relativistic dynamics and Brodsky et al. (1997) on light-front quantization, directly addressing the complications that arise in instant-form quantization of strong-interaction systems. The paper contradicts the assumption that Lorentz contraction is an observable dynamical effect in high-energy collisions, clarifying the distinction between measurements at fixed Galilean time versus fixed light-front time as discussed by Terrell (1959) and Penrose (1959). It enables a simpler, frame-independent approach to nonperturbative problems in quantum chromodynamics and resolves the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical vacuum energy estimates and cosmological observations.
