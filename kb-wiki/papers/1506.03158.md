# Elliptic and Triangular Flow of Identified Particles from the AMPT Model at RHIC Energies

> The elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = $ 11.5, 39, and 200 GeV and triangular flow ($v_{3}$) at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = $ 200 GeV of identified particles ($π^{\pm}, K^{\pm}, K^{0}_{S}, p, \bar{p}, ϕ, Λ$ and $\barΛ$) from 0\%--80\% central Au+Au collisions are analyzed using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. It is shown that the experimental results from the $η$-sub event plane method can be reproduced with a parton scattering cross section between 1.5 and 3 mb. We also studied the differential and integrated $v_{2}/v_{3}$ ratios and conclude that they are anti-corrected with the parton scattering cross section.

## Key claim

The AMPT model can reproduce experimental elliptic and triangular flow data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations using a parton scattering cross section between 1.5 and 3 mb, but no single cross section value describes all particle species simultaneously.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 20 million events for 0%–80% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5, 39, and 200 GeV using both default AMPT (v1.25) and string melting AMPT (v2.25) with parton scattering cross sections of 1.5, 3, and 6 mb. They applied the eta-sub event plane method to calculate elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) for identified particles (pi±, K±, K0_S, p, pbar, phi, Lambda, and Lambdabar), using the same analysis steps as in the experimental data analyses.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow (v2) was calculated at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5, 39, and 200 GeV; triangular flow (v3) was calculated at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, both for 0%–80% central Au+Au collisions
- Experimental results were reproduced with a parton scattering cross section between 1.5 and 3 mb
- v3 is about 40% lower than v2 with 1.5 mb parton scattering cross section but comparable with v2 at 6 mb parton scattering cross section
- The integrated v2/v3 ratio shows no mass (particle type) dependence within 10% for string melting AMPT
- The differential v2(pT)/v3(pT) ratio decreases significantly at low transverse momenta (pT < 1.5 GeV/c) and becomes flat at intermediate transverse momenta (1.5 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c)

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies by Lin (2001) and Chen (2004) that required 6–10 mb parton scattering cross sections to reproduce elliptic flow, but those studies used the reaction plane method rather than the event plane method. The results connect to viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Retinskaya (2013, 2014) that suggest combined analysis of elliptic and triangular flow can constrain initial states, and to predictions by Lang (2013) that the v2/v3 ratio should be mass-independent for fast particles. The observed anti-correlation between the v2/v3 ratio and parton scattering cross section may enable quantification of the system's viscosity.
