# Gamma-rigid solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for gamma = 30 degrees compared to the E(5) critical point symmetry

> A gamma-rigid solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for gamma = 30 degrees is derived, its ground state band being related to the second order Casimir operator of the Euclidean algebra E(4). Parameter-free (up to overall scale factors) predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates are in close agreement to the E(5) critical point symmetry, as well as to experimental data in the Xe region around A=130.

## Key claim

The Z(4) model provides an exact solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian with gamma frozen to 30 degrees, yielding parameter-free predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates that closely resemble those of the E(5) critical point symmetry, while the ground state band is related to the Euclidean algebra E(4). The similarity holds for most observables when the root-mean-square gamma of a gamma-unstable model equals the rigid gamma value of a gamma-rigid model.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian by freezing the gamma degree of freedom to 30 degrees, reducing the problem to four variables (beta and the three Euler angles). Separation of variables yields an angular equation solved by Meyer-ter-Vehn (1975) using Wigner functions, and a radial Bessel equation solved for an infinite square well potential in beta. Spectra and B(E2) transition rates are calculated using the resulting Bessel function eigenfunctions with the four-dimensional volume element.

## Context

This work extends the family of critical point symmetry solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian, following the E(5) model of Iachello (2000) for gamma-independent potentials and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) for gamma approximately 0 degrees, as well as the Z(5) model for gamma approximately 30 degrees. The Z(4) model provides the first connection between critical point symmetries and Lie algebraic symmetries through the Euclidean algebra E(4), and its predictions are applicable to nuclei in the Xe region around A=130, which have previously been characterized as O(6) nuclei by Casten (1985).
