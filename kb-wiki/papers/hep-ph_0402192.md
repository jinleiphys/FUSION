# Shear Viscosity in the O(N) Model

> We compute the shear viscosity in the O(N) model at first nontrivial order in the large N expansion. The calculation is organized using the 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action (2PI-1/N expansion) to next-to-leading order, which leads to an integral equation summing ladder and bubble diagrams. We also consider the weakly coupled theory for arbitrary N, using the three-loop expansion of the 2PI effective action. In the limit of weak coupling and vanishing mass, we find an approximate analytical solution of the integral equation. For general coupling and mass, the integral equation is solved numerically using a variational approach. The shear viscosity turns out to be close to the result obtained in the weak-coupling analysis.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity in the O(N) model is computed at first nontrivial order in the large N expansion using the 2PI-1/N expansion to next-to-leading order, and the result is close to the weak-coupling analysis even for general coupling and mass, though the calculation is limited to the symmetric phase and neglects subleading powers of 1/N.

## Method

The calculation uses the 2PI effective action at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion, which generates an integral equation summing ladder and bubble diagrams for the shear viscosity. The integral equation is solved numerically using a variational approach for general coupling and mass, while an approximate analytical solution is found in the limit of weak coupling and vanishing mass. The shear viscosity is extracted from the Kubo formula via the slope of the spectral function of the traceless part of the spatial energy-momentum tensor at zero frequency.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity scaling: eta = N^2 T^3 [F(lambda(mu), m_R/T, mu/T) + O(1/N)], where N is the number of field components, T is temperature, lambda(mu) is the renormalized coupling, and m_R is the renormalized mass in vacuum.
* The Landau pole constraint requires Lambda_L/T >= 40, giving lambda(mu=T) <= 128 (approximately 48 pi^2 / ln 40).
* Thermal width at ultrahard momentum (p >> T) in the weakly coupled massless limit: Gamma_infinity = lambda^2(M) T^2 / (2403 pi N p), used as a scaling factor for width results.
* The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is proportional to N, and therefore "always far above the lower bound conjectured recently, eta/s >= 1/4 pi" (Kovtun 2003).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Aarts 2003) connecting the 2PI effective action to transport coefficients, showing that the lowest nontrivial truncation correctly determines transport coefficients in weak coupling or 1/N expansion at leading (logarithmic) order. The calculation uses the O(N) model as a low-energy effective description of QCD (for N=4) and as a testbed for methods in thermal and nonequilibrium field theory, following earlier diagrammatic approaches by Jeon (for single-component scalar theory) and the kinetic theory calculations of Arnold, Moore, and others for hot gauge theories. The results enable quantitative determination of transport coefficients for nonideal hydrodynamics in heavy ion collisions, where viscous corrections are under investigation (Teaney 2003).
