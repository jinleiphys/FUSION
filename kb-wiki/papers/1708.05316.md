# Beam normal spin asymmetry for the $e p \to e Δ(1232)$ process

> We calculate the single spin asymmetry for the $e p \to e Δ(1232)$ process, for an electron beam polarized normal to the scattering plane. Such single spin asymmetries vanish in the one-photon exchange approximation, and are directly proportional to the absorptive part of a two-photon exchange amplitude. As the intermediate state in such two-photon exchange process is on its mass shell, the asymmetry allows one to access for the first time the on-shell $Δ\to Δ$ as well as $N^\ast \to Δ$ electromagnetic transitions. We present the general formalism to describe the $e p \to e Δ$ beam normal spin asymmetry, and provide a numerical estimate of its value using the nucleon, $Δ(1232)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, and $D_{13}(1520)$ intermediate states. We compare our results with the first data from the Qweak@JLab experiment and give predictions for the A4@MAMI experiment.

## Key claim
The beam normal spin asymmetry for the $e p \to e \Delta(1232)$ process is enhanced relative to the elastic $e p \to e p$ asymmetry at small momentum transfer $Q^2$, because the one-photon exchange cross section for the inelastic process is suppressed by one power of $Q^2$ compared to the elastic case, while the two-photon exchange amplitude does not share this suppression.

## Method
The authors calculated the beam normal spin asymmetry using the leading-order expression proportional to the product of the one-photon exchange amplitude and the absorptive part of the two-photon exchange amplitude. They modeled the hadronic tensor in the two-photon exchange amplitude as a sum over narrow intermediate baryon states: the nucleon, the $\Delta(1232)$, the $S_{11}(1535)$, and the $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances. The electromagnetic form factors for the $N \to \Delta$ transition were taken from the MAID2007 analysis, while the $\Delta$ electromagnetic form factors were estimated from lattice QCD, and the $\Delta \to S_{11}$ and $\Delta \to D_{13}$ transitions were related to the corresponding $N \to S_{11}$ and $N \to D_{13}$ transitions using a constituent quark model.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic dipole moment of the $\Delta^+(1232)$ resonance extracted from radiative $\pi^0$ photoproduction is $\mu_{\Delta^+} = 2.7^{+1.0}_{-1.3} (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 1.5 (\mathrm{syst.}) \pm 3 (\mathrm{theor.})\, \mu_N$, where $\mu_N = e/2M_N$ is the nuclear magneton.
- The magnetic dipole $N \to \Delta$ transition form factor at $Q^2 = 0$ is $G_M^\ast(0) = 3.0$, while the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors are at the few percent level relative to the magnetic dipole form factor in the low $Q^2$ range.
- The beam normal spin asymmetry for the $e p \to e \Delta$ process is predicted to be around an order of magnitude larger than the elastic asymmetry, based on preliminary results from the Qweak Collaboration.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for beam normal spin asymmetries, previously developed for elastic $e p \to e p$ scattering by Afanasev (2002), Gorchtein (2004), and Pasquini (2004), to the inelastic $e p \to e \Delta(1232)$ process. The asymmetry provides a new experimental handle to access the on-shell $\Delta \to \Delta$ electromagnetic transitions, which have not been directly measurable before, and complements earlier attempts to extract the $\Delta$ magnetic dipole moment from radiative $\pi^0$ photoproduction by Drechsel (2001) and Pascalutsa (2004). The results enable comparison with first data from the Qweak@JLab experiment and provide predictions for the A4@MAMI experiment.
