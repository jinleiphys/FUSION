# Parton picture of inclusive quasi-elastic electron scattering off nuclei

> A parton picture of inclusive quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering, where individual nucleons are treated as (non point-like) partons of the nucleus, is presented. All the necessary target-mass corrections to asymptotic scaling are evaluated and a new scaling variable is obtained. Recent inclusive data from Jefferson Lab are analyzed and shown to scale in terms of the new variable. The new approach allows to extract in a model-independent way the nucleon light-cone momentum distribution in the nucleus.

# Wiki Page: Stepwise Destruction of Pair Correlations in Superconducting Microclusters by a Magnetic Field

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in nm-scale superconducting clusters with discrete electronic energy levels, pair correlations are destroyed by a magnetic field in a stepwise manner at low temperatures (T < 0.1 T_c), rather than through the continuous transition seen in bulk superconductors; this stepwise destruction produces observable peaks in magnetic susceptibility and heat capacity, but only for clusters with fewer than approximately 2×10^4 electrons (M < 50) and at temperatures below 0.1 T_c.

## Method

The authors employ a single-shell model Hamiltonian with pairing interaction and Zeeman coupling, treating electrons in a degenerate spherical shell with degeneracy 2M (where M = 2l+1). Exact solutions are obtained using SU(2) group representations from nuclear physics, computing the canonical partition function for fixed electron number. The canonical gap Δ_c, magnetic susceptibility χ, and heat capacity C are calculated as functions of magnetic field (Larmor frequency ω) and temperature T, and compared with mean-field BCS results.

## Key numbers

- For Al clusters with radius R = 5–10 nm, the BCS gap at zero field and temperature is Δ(0) = 0.3–0.4 meV (from Ref. [3]).
- The critical field for complete destruction of pairing at T=0 for a half-filled shell is ω_c = 3Δ(0)/M for even electron number and ω_c = 4Δ(0)/M for odd electron number.
- The mean-field critical temperature at zero field is T_c = Δ(0)/2 for a half-filled shell.
- The diamagnetic susceptibility is estimated as χ_D ∼ −4×10^−6 N^(2/3), which for nm-scale clusters gives χ_D ∼ 10^−3.
- The level spacing between shells is Δe ∼ 10 meV, much larger than the BCS gap parameter Δ < 1 meV.
- The cluster contains N ≈ 2×10^3 to 3×10^5 free electrons for radius R = 1–5 nm.
- The first seniority jump (gapless superconductivity onset) occurs at ω_g = G for even systems and ω_g = G(1 + 1/(M−2)) for odd systems.

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of Ralph, Black, and Tinkham (1997) on nm-scale Al clusters, who found that pair correlations survive magnetic fields of several Tesla (versus 99 Gauss for bulk Al) and suggested stepwise destruction. The authors use exact canonical ensemble methods from nuclear physics (Esebag and Edigo, 1993) to treat the discrete level structure, contrasting with mean-field BCS approaches that overestimate the sharpness of the transition. The results enable quantitative predictions for susceptibility and heat capacity measurements in small superconducting clusters, and connect to the nuclear physics phenomenon of "High-K isomers" (Szymanski, 1983) where states of maximum angular momentum are observed. The temperature-induced pairing reentrance at fields above B_crit is a new prediction requiring experimental verification.
