# Interaction Strengths for the Fock-Space Formulation of the Nuclear Pairing Problem

> A realistic nuclear mean-field hamiltonian with pairing has been diagonalized using Fock space representation that allows for nearly exact treatment of the problem. Calculations were performed for all the even-even nuclei with Z in (20, 100), whose pairing gaps were possible to extract out of the experimental masses. The optimal values of the pairing strength constants for the protons and neutrons have been found.

# Interaction Strengths for the Fock-Space Formulation of the Nuclear Pairing Problem

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nearly exact diagonalization of a realistic nuclear mean-field Hamiltonian with monopole pairing can be performed in Fock space for even-even nuclei with Z between 20 and 100, and that the resulting pairing strength constants G for protons and neutrons can be parametrized as functions of mass number A and isospin I, though the fits show more regional structure than simple parametrizations can capture.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a Hamiltonian consisting of a deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field term plus a monopole pairing term (G constant independent of state indices) in a Fock space basis of 24 particles on 24 doubly-degenerate single-particle levels. They used Lanczos diagonalization after analytically block-diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix using seniority symmetries, reducing the full matrix size of approximately 3.2 × 10^13 to blocks of about 27,000 (seniority-zero) and 26,000 (seniority-two) basis states. The ground-state energy (seniority-zero) and lowest seniority-two energy were computed, and their difference was interpreted as twice the pairing gap, which was compared to experimental three-point mass-difference gaps Δ^(3) for even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Full Hamiltonian matrix size before block diagonalization: 32,247,603,683,100 basis states
- Seniority-zero block size after symmetries: 2,704,156; after basis cut-off: 27,703
- Seniority-two block size after symmetries: 705,432; after basis cut-off: 26,263
- Basis cut-off reducing matrix sizes by roughly two orders of magnitude induces errors of approximately 2%
- Best average fit for neutron pairing gaps: Δ_n = 10.85/√A (MeV)
- Best average fit for proton pairing gaps: Δ_p = 10.02/√A (MeV)
- Alternative fit forms: Δ_n = 23/(√A)^3 and Δ_p = 21/(√A)^3; also Δ_n = 4.66/A^(1/3) and Δ_p = 4.18/A^(1/3)
- Global neutron pairing constant parametrization: G_n = 24.872/A + 0.147 I (MeV)
- Global proton pairing constant parametrization: G_p = 26.861/A + 0.331 I (MeV)
- Five nuclear regions defined for regional fits: Region I (Z 32-38, N 32-44), Region II (Z 40-46, N 56-72), Region III (Z 54-66, N 58-76), Region IV (Z 60-82, N 88-104), Region V (Z 90-100, N 142-156)

## Context

This work extends the Fock-space diagonalization method for pairing introduced in Molique (2000) by applying it to a large-scale test across the nuclear chart, fitting the monopole pairing strength constants to experimental mass data. The approach contrasts with the approximate Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer method and Bogolyubov transformation traditionally used in Strutinsky-type calculations of nuclear total-energy surfaces. The resulting parametrizations are intended for use with the Fock-space diagonalization method, enabling more exact treatment of pairing in calculations of equilibrium deformations, shape coexistence, and fission barriers. The experimental mass gaps used for fitting are from Audi and Wapstra (1995), and comparisons are made to liquid-drop model fits from Madland (1998) and Hill (1998).
