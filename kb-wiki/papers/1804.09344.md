# The contribution of the $ρ^0 - ω$ interference to the violation of the isospin invariance in the $πN$ system

> It is generally believed that the $ρ^0 - ω$ interference is the dominant mechanism underlying the violation of the isospin invariance in the $N N$ system. Assessed in this paper is the role which the mechanism might play in a potential departure from the isospin symmetry in the two $π^\pm p$ elastic-scattering reactions.

## Key claim

The paper assesses whether the ρ⁰-ω interference mechanism, which is believed to dominate isospin violation in the nucleon-nucleon system, could also produce sizeable isospin-breaking effects in the two π±p elastic-scattering reactions, potentially explaining a discrepancy between threshold and above-threshold determinations of the π⁻p scattering length.

## Method

The author computes the contribution of the ρ⁰-ω interference graph to the invariant amplitude B of πN elastic scattering, normalised to the vector-coupling contribution of the ρ-meson-exchange graph. Two analytical parameterisations of the t-dependent ρ⁰-ω mixing amplitude f(t) are employed: one from Piekarewicz and Williams (1993) based on N̄N loops, and one from Krein, Thomas, and Williams (1993) based on qq̄ loops with confinement. The resulting isospin-violation measure IVρω(t) is evaluated for the t ≤ 0 region relevant to π±p elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson coupling constant gππρ = 5.950 ± 0.018, obtained from the ρ → π⁺π⁻ decay width using PDG (2016) values.
- The ρNN vector coupling constant from Hohler's dispersion-relation result: gρNN²/(4π) ≈ 2.2, giving gρNN = 5.26 ± 0.39.
- The ETH model fit to low-energy π±p ES data yields Gρ = 55.37 ± 0.61 GeV⁻² and gρNN = 5.593 ± 0.064.
- The tensor-to-vector coupling ratio κρ from the ETH model fit to ES data is 0.73 ± 0.37, while from combined π⁺p and π⁻p CX data it is 1.47 ± 0.17.
- The ωNN coupling constant from averaging three NN analyses (Nijmegen, Paris, CD-Bonn) gives gωNN = 24.14 ± 0.99 (at t=0).
- The ρ⁰-ω mixing amplitude from Coon and Barrett (1987) using present-day PDG values: ⟨ρ|H|ω⟩ = (-3.39 ± 0.13) × 10⁻³ GeV².

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Matsinos 1997, 2006, 2013, 2017) demonstrating isospin breaking in the πN system, and addresses a specific discrepancy between the π⁻p scattering length obtained from extrapolation of above-threshold data and the direct threshold measurement from pionic-hydrogen experiments at PSI (1995-2005). The ρ⁰-ω interference mechanism was hypothesised by Glashow (1961) and first investigated by Coleman and Glashow (1964); it was established by the late 1970s as the dominant source of isospin violation in the NN system. The present analysis contrasts with the π⁰-η interference mechanism proposed by Cutkosky (1979) for the πN system, and with Chiral Perturbation Theory studies (Hoferichter 2010) that predict isospin-breaking effects in πN interaction below the percent level.
