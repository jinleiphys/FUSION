# eta-photoproduction in the resonance energy region

> The $η$ production in the nucleon resonance energy region is studied within the unitary coupled-channels effective Lagrangian approach of the Giessen model. We demonstrate that the second peak recently observed in the cross section of $η$ photoproduction on the neutron at $\sqrt{s}$=1.66 GeV can be explained in terms of coupled-channel effects due to $S_{11}(1650)$ and $P_{11}(1710)$ resonance excitations.

## Key claim

The second peak observed in the cross section of eta photoproduction on the neutron at sqrt(s)=1.66 GeV can be explained by coupled-channel effects from S11(1650) and P11(1710) resonance excitations, without invoking an exotic narrow state, although the authors note that persisting experimental uncertainties translate into corresponding uncertainties in the theoretically derived parameters.

## Method

The authors performed a unitary coupled-channel analysis within the Giessen model, an effective Lagrangian approach that simultaneously describes pion- and photon-induced reactions up to about 2 GeV. They included experimental data on the ratio of neutron to proton eta photoproduction cross sections from Hoffmann-Rothe (1997) and preliminary total cross section data for gamma n to eta n from Jaegle, varying only the helicity decay amplitudes and eta N N* couplings of isospin-1/2 resonances while keeping all other hadronic parameters fixed from previous analyses.

## Key numbers

- The ratio A^n_1/2(S11(1535))/A^p_1/2(S11(1535)) is approximately -0.8, consistent with previous analyses by Sauermann (1997), Drechsel (1998), Mukhopadhyay (1995), and Chiang (2002)
- The neutron helicity amplitude for S11(1535) is A^n_1/2 = -74 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, significantly larger in magnitude than the previous determination from Shklyar (2004)
- The neutron helicity amplitude for S11(1650) is A^n_1/2 = -0.009 GeV^{-1/2}, close to the value found by Arndt (1996) and Penner and Mosel (2002)
- The second peak in the eta n total cross section appears at sqrt(s) = 1.66 GeV, with kink structures at 1.61 GeV and 1.72 GeV from K Lambda and omega N threshold effects respectively

## Context

This work extends the Giessen model analyses of Shklyar (2004) and Penner (2002) by including eta n photoproduction data to constrain neutron helicity amplitudes. The results contradict the proposal by Arndt (2003) and Azimov (2005) that a narrow resonance with mass 1.675 GeV is needed to explain the observed structure, and also differ from the suggestion by Tiator (Baru, 2006) involving the D15(1675) resonance. The calculations enable predictions for photon beam asymmetry measurements currently being analyzed at GRAAL, and the authors note that precise measurements of eta n differential cross sections in the 1.6-1.7 GeV range could distinguish between their coupled-channel explanation and alternative theoretical approaches.
