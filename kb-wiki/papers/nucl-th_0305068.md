# Hard Photodisintegration of a Proton Pair in 3He

> Hard photodisintegration of the deuteron has been extensively studied in order to understand the dynamics of the transition from hadronic to quark-gluon descriptions of the strong interaction. In this work, we discuss the extension of this program to hard photodisintegration of a $pp$ pair in the $^3$He nucleus. Experimental confirmation of new features predicted here for the suggested reaction would advance our understanding of hard nuclear reactions. A main prediction, in contrast with low-energy observations, is that the $pp$ breakup cross section is not much smaller than the one for $pn$ break up.In some models, the energy-dependent oscillations observed for $pp$ scattering are predicted to appear in the $γ^3{\rm He} \to p p + n$ reaction. Such an observation would open up a completely new field in studies of color coherence phenomena in hard nuclear reactions. We also demonstrate that, in addition to the energy dependence, the measurement of the light-cone momentum distribution of the recoil neutron provides an independent test of the underlying dynamics of hard disintegration.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that, in contrast to low-energy observations, the hard photodisintegration cross section for a proton-proton pair in ³He is not much smaller than that for a proton-neutron pair; in two of the three models considered, the pp breakup cross section is actually larger than the pn one, which would indicate the dominance of quark-gluon dynamics over meson-exchange mechanisms.

## Method

The authors extend theoretical models previously applied to deuteron (pn) photodisintegration, the reduced nuclear amplitude (RNA) model, the two-quark coupling (TQC) model, the quark-gluon string (QGS) model, and the hard rescattering model (HRM), to the reaction γ + ³He → pp + n. They estimate the cross section by multiplying the predicted γ(pp)→pp cross section by a factor that accounts for the probability of a pp pair in the ³He wave function (using the two-nucleon correlation parameter a₂ ≈ 2) and by integrating over the recoil neutron momentum up to 100 MeV/c, yielding an overall scaling factor of approximately 1/3 relative to the γ(pp)→pp subprocess.

## Key numbers

- The RNA model predicts dσ(γ ³He → pp + n) / dσ(γ d → pn) = 16/3, based on a form factor ratio F₁ₚ/F₁ₙ ≈ -2, a reduced cross section ratio of 4, and a ³He correction factor of 1/3.
- The HRM calculation uses a factor (14/15)² from SU(6) nucleon wave functions, with an uncertainty of 10–20%.
- Soft rescattering corrections are estimated at 5–10% for c.m. angles of 40–90°, while charge-exchange rescattering gives only a 1–2% correction.
- The threshold for open charm production in γ ³He → n X is at √s > 6 GeV, corresponding to E_γ(lab) > 4.5 GeV.
- The proposed experiment at Jefferson Lab Hall A can measure the 90° c.m. cross section up to E_γ ≈ 5 GeV with existing equipment, and up to about 7 GeV with the proposed 12 GeV upgrade.

## Context

This work extends the program of hard photodisintegration studies from the deuteron (pn system), which has been measured up to 5 GeV (e.g., by the NE8, NE17, and E89012 collaborations), to the pp system in ³He. The paper builds on four existing theoretical approaches, RNA (Brodsky and Hiller, 1990s), TQC (Radyushkin, 1990s), QGS (Regge-based), and HRM (Sargsian et al., 2000s), that were developed for deuteron photodisintegration but disagree in their predictions for the pp case. Experimental confirmation of the predicted large pp cross section and possible energy-dependent oscillations (analogous to those seen in pp elastic scattering by Ralston and Pire, 1982, and Brodsky and de Teramond, 1988) would open a new field in studies of color coherence phenomena in hard nuclear reactions and enable polarization measurements of the outgoing proton pair.
