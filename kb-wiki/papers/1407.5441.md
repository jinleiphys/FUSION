# Dirac Coupled Channel Analyses of the 2$^-$ Gamma Vibrational band excitation in $^{20}$Ne

> Dirac coupled channel analyses are performed using optical potential model for the high-lying excited states that belong to the 2$^-$ gamma vibrational band at the 800 MeV unpolarized proton inelastic scatterings from $^{20}$Ne. The first order vibrational collective models are used to obtain the transition optical potentials to describe the high-lying excited vibrational collective states and Lorentz-covariant scalar and time-like vector potentials are used as direct optical potentials. The complicated Dirac coupled channel equations are solved phenomenologically to reproduce the differential cross sections data by varying the optical potential and deformation parameters using minimum chi-square method. It is found that relativistic Dirac coupled channel calculation could describe the excited states of the 2$^-$ gamma vibrational band in $^{20}$Ne much better than the nonrelativistic coupled channel calculation, especially for the 2$^-$ and 3$^-$ states of the band. It is shown that the multistep excitation process via channel coupling with the $3^-$ state is essential to describe the $2^-$ state excitation and pure direct transition from the ground state is dominant for the 3$^-$ state excitation of the 2$^-$ gamma vibrational band in $^{20}$Ne.

# Dirac Coupled Channel Analyses of the 2⁻ Gamma Vibrational band excitation in ²⁰Ne

## Key claim

Relativistic Dirac coupled channel calculations describe the 2⁻ and 3⁻ states of the 2⁻ gamma vibrational band in ²⁰Ne at 800 MeV proton inelastic scattering significantly better than nonrelativistic coupled channel calculations, but the improvement is conditional on including multistep excitation via channel coupling with the 3⁻ state for the 2⁻ state, while direct transition from the ground state dominates for the 3⁻ state.

## Method

Dirac coupled channel equations were solved phenomenologically using the ECIS code with Woods-Saxon scalar and time-like vector optical potentials, fitting differential cross section data for 800 MeV unpolarized proton inelastic scattering from ²⁰Ne. Transition potentials for the 2⁻ (4.97 MeV), 3⁻ (5.62 MeV), and 5⁻ (8.45 MeV) states of the Kπ = 2⁻ octupole band were obtained from the first-order vibrational collective model by deforming the direct optical potentials. Parameters were determined by sequential iteration using minimum chi-square fitting, with sequential inclusion of one, two, or all three excited states coupled to the ground state.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering: scalar real potential strength = -200.7 MeV, radius = 2.677 fm, diffusiveness = 0.7578 fm; vector real potential strength = 113.0 MeV, radius = 2.693 fm, diffusiveness = 0.7419 fm
- Elastic scattering: scalar imaginary potential strength = 127.2 MeV, radius = 1.859 fm, diffusiveness = 0.7802 fm; vector imaginary potential strength = -107.4 MeV, radius = 2.409 fm, diffusiveness = 0.6781 fm
- For the 2⁻ state (4.97 MeV) coupled alone: reduced chi-square for inelastic data χ²/N = 56.0; deformation parameters β_S = -0.050, β_V = -0.044
- For the 3⁻ state (5.62 MeV) coupled alone: reduced chi-square for inelastic data χ²/N = 80.5; deformation parameters β_S = 0.445, β_V = 0.452
- When 2⁻ and 3⁻ states are coupled together: χ²/N for 2⁻ state = 18.1 (reduced to about 1/3 of the single-state value); χ²/N for 3⁻ state = 75.4; deformation parameters for 2⁻: β_S = 0.074, β_V = 0.076; for 3⁻: β_S = 0.446, β_V = 0.450
- When all three states (2⁻, 3⁻, 5⁻) are coupled: χ²/N for 2⁻ = 35.3, for 3⁻ = 140, for 5⁻ = 1433; deformation parameters for 5⁻: β_S = 0.071, β_V = 0.111
- Real effective central potential strength from Dirac approach: about -80 MeV at the center of the nucleus, compared to about -3.5 MeV from nonrelativistic Schrödinger approach

## Context

This work extends Dirac phenomenological coupled channel calculations, previously applied to spherical nuclei and a few deformed nuclei (Shim 1999, Shim and Kim 2012), to the s-d shell nucleus ²⁰Ne for high-lying states of the 2⁻ gamma vibrational band. The results contradict the adequacy of nonrelativistic coupled channel calculations (Blanpied 1988) for these states, showing that the relativistic approach yields surface-peaked effective central and spin-orbit potentials naturally, similar to findings for ²⁴Mg (Shim and Kim 2012). The analysis enables future studies of multistep excitation mechanisms in deformed light nuclei and suggests that the 5⁻ state assignment to the Kπ = 2⁻ band may be uncertain, possibly belonging instead to a Kπ = 0⁻ band as proposed by Blanpied (1988).
