# Shape evolution and coexistence in neutron-deficient Nd and Sm nuclei

> The evolution of shapes and low-energy shape coexistence is analyzed in neutron-deficient Nd and Sm nuclei, using a five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (5DCH). Deformation energy surfaces, calculated with the relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1 and a separable finite-range pairing interaction, exhibit a transition from spherical shapes near $N=80$, to $γ-$soft shapes, and to prolate deformed minima in lighter isotopes. The corresponding 5DCH model calculation, based on the self-consistent mean-field potentials, reproduces the empirical isotopic trend of characteristic collective observables, and predicts significantly different deformations for the first two $0^+$ states in the $N=74$ isotones $^{134}$Nd and $^{136}$Sm. In addition to bands based on the triaxial $γ-$soft ground state, in excellent agreement with data, the occurrence of a low-energy rotational band is predicted, built on the prolate deformed ($β\sim0.4$) excited state $0^+_2$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the neutron-deficient \(N=74\) isotones \(^{134}\)Nd and \(^{136}\)Sm exhibit coexisting low-energy triaxial \(\gamma\)-soft and prolate axially-deformed shapes, with the prolate deformed (\(\beta\sim0.4\)) excited state \(0^+_2\) band not yet observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations using the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a separable finite-range pairing interaction to generate deformation energy surfaces in the \((\beta, \gamma)\) plane for neutron-deficient Nd and Sm isotopes. These constrained mean-field solutions provided the microscopic input for the parameters of a five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (5DCH), which was then diagonalized to compute excitation spectra, E2 transition strengths, and quadrupole shape invariants for the low-energy states.

## Key numbers

- The energy ratio \(R_{42} = \frac{E(4^+)-E(0^+)}{E(2^+)-E(0^+)}\) for the ground-state band decreases from \(\sim3.3\) at \(N \sim 66\) to \(\sim2.5\) in the region \(N=74-78\), in excellent agreement with the empirical trend.
- In \(^{134}\)Nd, the average quadrupole deformation \(\langle\beta\rangle\) is \(\sim0.25\) for the \(0^+_1\) state and \(\sim0.4\) for the \(0^+_2\) state, with corresponding \(\langle\gamma\rangle\) values of \(\sim15^\circ\) and \(2^\circ\), respectively.
- The calculated E0 transition strength \(\rho^2(E0; 0^+_2\to 0^+_1)\times 10^3\) is \(44.6\) for \(^{134}\)Nd and \(98.8\) for \(^{136}\)Sm, comparable to values in the well-known shape-coexisting nuclei \(^{98}\)Sr [51(5)] and \(^{100}\)Zr [108(19)].
- The ratio \(E(2^+_\gamma)/E(4^+_1)\) is predicted to be \(\sim1.0\) at \(N=74-78\), characterizing low-energy \(\gamma\)-deformed structures.

## Context

This work extends the global analysis of quadrupole shape invariants by Quan et al. (2017) to a detailed study of shape evolution and coexistence in neutron-deficient Nd and Sm isotopes, using the same relativistic energy density functional framework. The predictions of coexisting triaxial and prolate deformed shapes in \(^{134}\)Nd and \(^{136}\)Sm, with the prolate band built on the \(0^+_2\) state, provide specific targets for experimental confirmation in this mass region, where previous studies by Moller and Bengtsson (2008) predicted triaxial ground states and where shape coexistence has been suggested in \(^{142}\)Sm by Rajbanshi et al. (2014).
