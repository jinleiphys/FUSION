# The role of quark mass in cold and dense pQCD and quark stars

> For almost twenty years the effects of a nonzero strange quark mass on the equation of state of cold and dense QCD were considered to be negligible, thereby yielding only minor corrections to the mass-radius diagram of compact stars. By computing the thermodynamic potential to first order in α_s, and including the effects of the renormalization group running of the coupling and strange quark mass, we show that corrections can be of the order of 25%, and dramatically affect the structure of compact stars.

## Key claim
Corrections from a nonzero strange quark mass to the equation of state of cold and dense QCD, previously considered negligible, can be of order 25% and dramatically affect the mass-radius diagram of compact stars when computed to first order in α_s with renormalization group running of both the coupling and the strange quark mass.

## Method
The thermodynamic potential for cold quark matter was computed to first order in α_s in the MS-bar scheme, including two massless flavors (up and down) and one massive flavor (strange quark). Electrons were included to enforce charge neutrality and beta equilibrium, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations were solved using the resulting equation of state. The renormalization group running of both α_s and the strange quark mass was implemented up to 4-loop order, with the renormalization scale chosen as Λ = (2/3)(μ_u + μ_d + μ_s).

## Key numbers
- The strange quark mass reaches m_s ~ 137 MeV at μ = 500 MeV.
- For massless strange quarks, the effective bag constant is B_eff^(1/4) ≃ 117 MeV and a ≃ 2.81; with running mass these values rise to B_eff^(1/4) ≃ 137 MeV and a ≃ 3.17.
- The most massive star for m_s = 0 has M/M_⊙ ≃ 3.2 and radius ~ 17 km with central density n_B ≃ 0.5 fm^(-3).
- The heaviest star with running strange quark mass has M/M_⊙ ≃ 2.16 at ~ 12 km with central density n_B ≃ 0.83 fm^(-3) (at μ = 470 MeV).
- Corrections to mass and radius of quark stars due to running strange quark mass can be ~ 25%.

## Context
This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state for cold dense matter (Fraga 2001, Fraga 2001) by including a running strange quark mass, which had been neglected in most previous approaches including bag model treatments (Witten 1984, bag model) and even recent QCD-based studies. The results show that finite strange quark mass effects are important in the lower-density sector of the equation of state, as argued by Alford (2003), and enable more realistic modeling of quark star structure; future work will include full α_s^2 corrections and color superconducting gaps.
