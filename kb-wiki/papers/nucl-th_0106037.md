# Event anisotropy in 4.2A GeV/c C+C collisions

> The directed and elliptic flow of protons and negative pions in 4.2A GeV/c C+C collisions is studied using the Fourier analysis of azimuthal distributions. It is found that the protons exhibit pronounced directed flow, while the flow of pions is either non existent or too weak to be detected experimentally. Also, it is found that in the entire rapidity interval the elliptic flow is very small if not zero. These results are confirmed by the Quark-Gluon-String Model (QGSM) and the relativistic transport model (ART 1.0), except that these models predict very weak antiflow of pions. The more detailed comparison with the QGSM suggests that the decay of resonances and rescattering of secondaries dominantly determine the proton and negative pion flow at this energy.

# Event anisotropy in 4.2A GeV/c C+C collisions

## Key claim

Protons in 4.2A GeV/c C+C collisions exhibit pronounced directed flow, while the elliptic flow is very small if not zero across the entire rapidity interval; however, the directed flow of negative pions is either non-existent or too weak to be detected experimentally due to limited reaction plane resolution, even though the Quark-Gluon-String Model (QGSM) predicts a very weak antiflow of pions with respect to the true reaction plane.

## Method

The directed and elliptic flow of protons and negative pions was studied using Fourier analysis of azimuthal distributions, extracting the first harmonic v1 and second harmonic v2 coefficients from 9500 semicentral and central C+C collisions obtained with the 2-m propane bubble chamber at JINR Dubna. The reaction plane was determined using the projectile momentum vector and a transverse momentum vector Q, with resolution evaluated by the subevent method; corrections to the Fourier coefficients were applied using the measured resolution factors. The experimental results were compared with 400000 events generated by the Quark-Gluon-String Model (QGSM) and 200000 events generated by the relativistic transport model ART 1.0, using the same centrality criterion leading to an average impact parameter of approximately 2.6 fm.

## Key numbers

- Proton directed flow magnitude v1 approximately 0.17 at rapidities close to beam rapidity (0.7 < y < 1.5) in 4.2A GeV/c C+C collisions
- Slope of mean transverse momentum projected onto reaction plane at midrapidity F = 144 MeV/c, with scaled flow F_S = 31 MeV/c after normalisation to mass number
- Reaction plane resolution width sigma = 50 degrees, with correction factors <cos(DeltaPhi)> = 0.56 and <cos(2DeltaPhi)> = 0.24
- QGSM predicts very weak directed antiflow of pions with maximum v1 approximately 0.02 around target rapidity with respect to true reaction plane
- Uncorrected elliptic flow v'_2 magnitude less than or equal to 0.02 across entire rapidity interval for both protons and pions
- ART 1.0 cascade mode underestimates proton flow magnitude by approximately 20 percent and predicts small directed antiflow of negative pions with v1 approximately 0.04
- ART 1.0 predicts very small proton and pion elliptic flow with |v2| less than or equal to 0.01

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow to light symmetric systems at intermediate energies, following prior measurements of directed and elliptic flow for heavy symmetric systems at AGS energies by Barrette (1997) and at SPS energies by Appelshaeuser (1998) and Aggarwal (1998). The results are consistent with the trend observed by Chance (1997) that directed flow slowly decreases with increasing beam energy after reaching a maximum around 0.7-2A GeV. The comparison with QGSM and ART 1.0 enables identification of the dominant mechanisms determining flow at this energy, namely decay of resonances and rescattering of secondaries, and suggests that shadowing by cold spectator matter affects only particles produced at the early stage of the collision.
