# Dynamical Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking in Quantum Chromodynamics

> This study proposes that the longstanding problems of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) as an SU(3)_C gauge theory, the confinement mechanism and Θvacuum, can be resolved by dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (DSSB) through the condensation of singlet gluons and quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N \times U(1)_Z gauge theory is produced. The confinement mechanism is the result of massive gluons and the Yukawa potential provides hadron formation. The evidences for the breaking of discrete symmetries (C, P, T, CP) during DSSB appear explicitly: baryons and mesons without their parity partners, the conservation of vector current and the partial conservation of the axial vector current, the baryon asymmetry δ_B \simeq 10^{-10}, and the neutron electric dipole moment Θ< 10^{-9}.

# Dynamical Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking in Quantum Chromodynamics

## Key claim
The paper proposes that QCD's confinement mechanism and theta vacuum problem can be resolved by dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (DSSB) through condensation of singlet gluons, producing quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z gauge theory. This mechanism is presented as an alternative to the Higgs mechanism, with the strong coupling constant as the only free parameter, and claims to explain discrete symmetry violations including baryon asymmetry and the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Method
The author starts from the QCD Lagrangian with a theta vacuum term and no explicit quark mass term, then decomposes gauge fields into charge singlet and nonsinglet components with even and odd parity. A scalar potential V_e(phi) = V_0 + mu^2 phi^2 + lambda phi^4 with mu^2 < 0 is used to model condensation of odd-parity singlet gauge bosons, which triggers spontaneous symmetry breaking from SU(3)_C to SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z and then to U(1)_f. The gauge boson mass is expressed as M_G^2 = M_H^2 - c_f g_s^2 <phi>^2, and the Yukawa potential from massive gluons provides the confinement mechanism.

## Key numbers
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s(M_Z) ~ 0.12 at the Z boson mass and alpha_s(q) ~ 0.48 at nuclear energy scale q ~ 300 MeV
- Gauge boson masses: M_EW ~ 10^2 GeV at weak scale, M_QCD ~ 10^{-1} GeV at strong scale
- Effective coupling constants: G_F ~ 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} at weak scale, G_R ~ 10^5 GeV^{-2} at strong scale, with ratio G_R/G_F = (Lambda_EW / Lambda_QCD)^2 ~ 10^6
- Baryon asymmetry delta_B ~ 10^{-10}
- Neutron electric dipole moment Theta_QCD <= 10^{-9}, with neutron dipole moment d_n = 2.7 to 5.2 x 10^{-16} Theta e cm
- Theta parameter expressed as Theta = 10^{-61} rho_G / rho_m
- Effective electromagnetic color factor alpha_f^{eff} = alpha_s/64 ~ 1/137 when alpha_s ~ 0.48
- Massless photon number density n_{t gamma}^{QCD} ~ 10^{36} cm^{-3} at strong scale
- Gauge boson number densities: n_EW ~ 10^{47} cm^{-3} at weak scale, n_QCD ~ 10^{39} cm^{-3} at strong scale

## Context
This work extends the concept of dynamical symmetry breaking from technicolor models (Susskind 1979, Weinberg 1979) to QCD, proposing that the same mechanism that generates gauge boson masses in electroweak theory can resolve the confinement and theta vacuum problems in strong interactions. The author draws analogy to the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model (Glashow 1961, Weinberg 1967, Salam 1968) and claims that QND as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z gauge theory is the strong-interaction analog of electroweak theory. The paper enables a unified framework where hadron masses, baryon asymmetry, and CP violation in the neutron electric dipole moment all follow from a single DSSB mechanism, though the proposal remains speculative and lacks experimental validation beyond citing existing measurements of the strong coupling constant and neutron dipole moment.
