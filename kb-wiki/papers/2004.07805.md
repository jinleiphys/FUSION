# Quantifying uncertainties and correlations in the nuclear-matter equation of state

> We perform statistically rigorous uncertainty quantification (UQ) for chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) applied to infinite nuclear matter up to twice nuclear saturation density. The equation of state (EOS) is based on high-order many-body perturbation theory calculations with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions up to fourth order in the $χ$EFT expansion. From these calculations our newly developed Bayesian machine-learning approach extracts the size and smoothness properties of the correlated EFT truncation error. We then propose a novel extension that uses multitask machine learning to reveal correlations between the EOS at different proton fractions. The inferred in-medium $χ$EFT breakdown scale in pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter is consistent with that from free-space nucleon-nucleon scattering. These significant advances allow us to provide posterior distributions for the nuclear saturation point and propagate theoretical uncertainties to derived quantities: the pressure and incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, the nuclear symmetry energy, and its derivative. Our results, which are validated by statistical diagnostics, demonstrate that an understanding of truncation-error correlations between different densities and different observables is crucial for reliable UQ. The methods developed here are publicly available as annotated Jupyter notebooks.

## Key claim

The paper provides statistically rigorous uncertainty quantification for chiral effective field theory (χEFT) predictions of the nuclear-matter equation of state up to twice nuclear saturation density, but the results are conditional on the specific choice of expansion parameter Q = k_F/Λ_b and the assumption that the EFT truncation error can be modeled as a Gaussian process with a radial basis function kernel.

## Method

The authors performed high-order many-body perturbation theory calculations with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions up to fourth order in the χEFT expansion, using interactions developed by Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) with momentum cutoffs of 450 and 500 MeV. They extracted observable coefficients from the order-by-order energy per particle in pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, then trained a Gaussian process model to learn the variance and correlation length of these coefficients. The model was extended using multitask machine learning to capture correlations between the equation of state at different proton fractions.

## Key numbers

- The reference scale for the nuclear-matter equation of state is set to y_ref(k_F) = 16 MeV × (k_F/k_F0)^2, where k_F0 corresponds to nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^(-3)
- For the Λ = 500 MeV interactions, the estimated hyperparameters for pure neutron matter are cbar = 1.0 and ℓ = 0.97 fm^(-1)
- For the Λ = 500 MeV interactions, the estimated hyperparameters for symmetric nuclear matter are cbar = 2.9 and ℓ = 0.48 fm^(-1)
- The energy per particle was sampled at densities n = 0.05, 0.06, ..., 0.34 fm^(-3), corresponding to up to twice nuclear saturation density
- A white noise term with variance σ^2 = 5 × 10^(-4) was included in the kernel to regularize the GP framework

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian truncation-error model developed by Melendez et al. (2019) from nucleon-nucleon observables to infinite nuclear matter, and goes significantly beyond the earlier work of Hu et al. (2019) by including three-nucleon forces, analyzing correlations within and between observables, applying model-checking diagnostics, and deriving posteriors for the breakdown scale. The methods enable future work that consistently combines uncertainties on low-energy constants from fitting nuclear interactions to data with truncation errors, and the companion paper by Drischler et al. (2020) applies the analysis to neutron-rich matter for astrophysical applications.
