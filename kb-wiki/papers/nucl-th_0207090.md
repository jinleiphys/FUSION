# Chiral Dynamics of Deeply Bound Pionic Atoms

> We present and discuss a systematic calculation, based on two-loop chiral perturbation theory, of the pion-nuclear s-wave optical potential. A proper treatment of the explicit energy dependence of the off-shell pion self-energy together with (electromagnetic) gauge invariance of the Klein-Gordon equation turns out to be crucial. Accurate data for the binding energies and widths of the 1s and 2p levels in pionic ^{205}Pb and ^{207}Pb are well reproduced, and the notorious "missing repulsion" in the pion-nuclear s-wave optical potential is accounted for. The connection with the in-medium change of the pion decay constant is clarified.

## Key claim
The "missing repulsion" in the s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential is resolved by including the explicit energy dependence of the pion self-energy and enforcing gauge invariance in the Klein-Gordon equation, rather than by introducing a phenomenological repulsive ρ² term. The effect can be interpreted to leading order as an in-medium reduction of the pion decay constant.

## Method
The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation for pionic atoms of ²⁰⁵Pb and ²⁰⁷Pb using a pion polarization operator computed from two-loop in-medium chiral perturbation theory, with the explicit off-shell energy dependence of the πN amplitudes T⁺(ω) and T⁻(ω) retained. Gauge invariance was enforced by replacing the pion energy ω with ω − V_c(r) in the polarization operator wherever it appears. The calculation included the linear density term, Ericson-Ericson double-scattering corrections, a small relativistic correction, and a two-loop correlation term, plus a phenomenological s-wave absorption term ΔΠ_S with Im B₀ = 0.063 m_π⁻⁴ and Re B₀ = 0.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV; charged pion mass: m_π = 139.57 MeV.
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_N = 45 ± 8 MeV, tuned to σ_N = 45 MeV in the calculation.
- Isospin-even threshold πN amplitude: T⁺(m_π) = (−0.04 ± 0.09) fm ≈ 0.
- Isospin-odd threshold πN amplitude: T⁻(m_π) = 1.85 ± 0.09 fm.
- Proton radii: R_p[²⁰⁵Pb] = 6.66 fm, R_p[²⁰⁷Pb] = 6.67 fm; neutron radii: R_n[²⁰⁵Pb] = 6.94 fm, R_n[²⁰⁷Pb] = 6.97 fm; diffuseness a_p = a_n = 0.48 fm.
- Absorption parameter: Im B₀ = 0.063 m_π⁻⁴; Re B₀ = 0 in the standard set.
- Cut-off scale for T⁻(ω): Λ = 737 MeV ≈ 8 f_π.
- Nucleon axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.27.

## Context
This work extends the two-loop in-medium chiral perturbation theory of Kaiser (2001) by including explicit off-shell energy dependence, and addresses the long-standing "missing repulsion" problem in s-wave pion-nucleus optical potentials previously treated phenomenologically by Nieves et al. (1993) and others. The results confirm the interpretation of Weise (2001) that the effect can be understood as an in-medium reduction of the pion decay constant, which had been debated by Wirzba (2002) and Oset (2002). The calculation enables a parameter-free description of deeply bound pionic atom data and clarifies the connection between the in-medium chiral condensate and the observed repulsion.
