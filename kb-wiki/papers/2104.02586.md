# $V_{ud}$ radiative corrections with lattice input

> We will describe several pioneering efforts in the study of electromagnetic radiative corrections to semileptonic decay processes, with particular emphasis on the role of lattice QCD. These studies are essential for the precise extraction of the matrix element $V_{ud}$ from beta decays of pion, free neutron and $J^P=0^+$ nuclei, and are crucial to address several recently-emerged anomalies involving $V_{ud}$ and $V_{us}$, which may provide hints for physics beyond the Standard Model.

# V_{ud} radiative corrections with lattice input

## Key claim
The Cabibbo angle anomaly (CAA), a 3σ deviation from Standard Model CKM unitarity, may be resolved or confirmed by first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the γW-box diagrams that dominate the theory uncertainty in electroweak radiative corrections to beta decays, but the final verdict depends on whether direct lattice calculations of the single-nucleon box diagrams, expected within one to two years, confirm the shifted central value of Δ_R^V that produced the anomaly.

## Method
The author reviews a dispersion relation (DR) analysis of the single-nucleon γW-box diagram amplitude, which expresses it as an integral over Q² of the first Nachtmann moment M_N(Q²) of a parity-odd structure function. For the pion case, the function M_π(Q²) is computed from lattice QCD at low Q² (< 2 GeV²) using four-point correlation functions on five gauge ensembles at physical pion mass, combined with 4-loop perturbative QCD at high Q² (> 2 GeV²). The same framework is extended to the nucleon via Regge-exchange relations between pion and nucleon channels, and to Kπ box diagrams in the flavor SU(3) limit.

## Key numbers
- The 2018 DR analysis gives Δ_R^V = 0.02467(22), yielding |V_{ud}| = 0.97366(15), a significant shift from the 2006 value Δ_R^V = 0.02361(38) from Marciano (2006).
- The first lattice calculation of the pion γW-box gives □_{γW}^{VA}|_π = 2.830(11)_stat(26)_syst × 10^{-3}, achieving 1% overall precision.
- The updated π_{e3} radiative correction is δ = 0.0332(1)_{γW}(3)_{HO}, reducing theory uncertainty by a factor of 3 compared to the ChPT value δ = 0.0334(10)_{LEC}(3)_{HO} from Cirigliano (2002).
- The updated |V_{ud}| from π_{e3} is |V_{ud}|_{π_{e3}} = 0.9740(28)_{exp}(1)_{th}.
- The Regge-based independent assessment gives Δ_R^V = 0.02477(24), consistent with the DR result.

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of Seng (2018) and Seng (2018) that first produced the shifted Δ_R^V value, and the lattice calculation of Feng (2020) for the pion box diagram. It directly addresses the Cabibbo angle anomaly (CAA), the 3σ deviation from unitarity |V_{ud}|²+|V_{us}|²+|V_{ub}|² = 0.9985(3)_{V_{ud}}(4)_{V_{us}} reported by the Particle Data Group (2020), and the 2.5σ discrepancy between |V_{us}| from K_{μ2} (0.2252(5)) and K_{ℓ3} (0.2233(6)). The lattice results enable a theoretically clean extraction of V_{us}/V_{ud} via the ratio R_V = Γ_{K_{e3}}/Γ_{π_{e3}}, and motivate experimental improvements in π_{e3} branching ratio measurements. Direct nucleon γW-box calculations are underway by the PNDME and NME Collaborations at Los Alamos, with first results expected within one to two years.
