# Neutrino Capture Reactions on $^{40}$Ar

> Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in $^{40}$Ar is studied by shell-model calculations with monopole-based universal intearction, which has tensor components of $π$ρ$-meson exchanges. Calculated GT strength is found to be consistent with the experimental data obtained by recent ($p, n$) reactions. Neutrino capture cross sections on $^{40}$Ar for solar neutrinos from $^{8}$B are found to be enhanced compared with previous calculations. The reaction cross sections for multipoles other than $0^{+}$ and $1^{+}$ are obtained by random-phase approximation (RPA). Their contributions become important for neutrino energies larger than 50 MeV.

## Key claim

Neutrino capture cross sections on $^{40}$Ar for solar neutrinos from $^{8}$B are enhanced compared with previous calculations, but this enhancement is conditional on using the SDPF-VMU-LS interaction which includes a two-body spin-orbit term; without that term the enhancement is smaller.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for Gamow-Teller (GT) and isobaric analog (IA) transitions in $^{40}$Ar using the SDPF-VMU-LS interaction, which combines the sdpf-m and GXPF1J interactions with a monopole-based universal cross-shell interaction (VMU) that includes tensor components from $\pi+\rho$-meson exchanges and a two-body spin-orbit term. For multipoles other than $0^{+}$ and $1^{+}$, they used random-phase approximation (RPA) with the SGII interaction. The cross sections were folded over the $^{8}$B neutrino spectrum with the ICARUS condition that electron energies exceed 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cumulative GT strength sum reaches 7.21, which equals $f_q^{2} \times 12$ with quenching factor $f_q = 0.775$ (axial-vector coupling $g_A = -1.263$)
- Cross section for GT transition with SDPF-VMU-LS: $11.95 \times 10^{-43}$ cm$^{2}$ for solar $^{8}$B neutrinos
- Cross section for GT+IA with SDPF-VMU-LS: $14.05 \times 10^{-43}$ cm$^{2}$ for solar $^{8}$B neutrinos
- GT cross section enhanced by about 55% compared to Ormand (1995) calculations; GT+IA enhanced by about 22% compared to Ormand (1995)
- Without two-body spin-orbit interaction (SDPF-VMU), GT cross section is $10.99 \times 10^{-43}$ cm$^{2}$ and GT+IA is $13.10 \times 10^{-43}$ cm$^{2}$ (enhancements of 43% and 14% respectively)
- Contributions from multipoles other than $0^{+}$ and $1^{+}$ become important for neutrino energies larger than 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends shell-model calculations of GT strength in $^{40}$Ar beyond earlier work by Ormand (1995), which produced smaller GT strength than experimental ($p,n$) reaction data from Bhat (1998). The improved treatment uses the VMU interaction with tensor components, successfully reproducing the experimental GT strength distribution. The results enable more accurate predictions for supernova neutrino detection with liquid argon detectors like ICARUS, and the authors recommend direct measurements of the cross section using a liquid Ar TPC. The calculated total cross section is close to that of Kolb (1994) from RPA but enhanced by 20-40% at lower energies (20-40 MeV), while being about three times larger than QRPA results from Chow (1996).
