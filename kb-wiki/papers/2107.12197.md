# Coexistence of isospin I = 0 and I = 1 pairings in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The coexistence of neutron-neutron (n-n), proton-proton (p-p), and neutron-proton (n-p) pairings is investigated by adopting an effective density-dependent contact pairing potential. These three types of pairings can coexist only if the n-p pairing is stronger than the n-n and p-p pairings for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. In addition, the existence of n-n and p-p pairs might enhance n-p pairings in asymmetric nuclear matter when the n-p pairing strength is significantly stronger than the n-n and p-p ones. Conversely, the n-p pairing is reduced by the n-n and p-p pairs when the n-p pairing interaction approaches n-n and p-p pairings.

## Key claim

The three types of pairing (neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton) can coexist in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter only if the neutron-proton pairing is stronger than the neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairings; the existence of neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairs can either enhance or reduce neutron-proton pairing depending on the relative pairing strengths.

## Method

The authors employed an extended Nambu-Gorkov propagator formalism that includes isospin-triplet neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairings alongside isospin-singlet neutron-proton pairing. They solved the coupled gap equations self-consistently with density constraints using an effective density-dependent contact pairing interaction, with parameters calibrated to reproduce pairing gaps from Hartree-Fock calculations at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- At total density ρ = 0.068 fm⁻³, where both I=1 and I=0 pairing interactions are most attractive, the neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing gaps increase with isospin asymmetry β, while the neutron-proton pairing gap decreases with β and vanishes at a certain asymmetry.
- The effective neutron-proton pairing strength ratio used was g_np/g_nn = 1.3837, calibrated from Ref. sdbaldo.
- The energy cutoff for the contact interaction was fixed at approximately 80 MeV for both isospin channels.
- The saturation density used for the density-dependent interaction was ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of single-pairing structures in nuclear matter (Shen, 2003; Baldo, 1998) and the BCS-BEC crossover with coexisting I=0 and I=1 pairings (Huang, 2019). The results enable future investigations of pairing correlations in finite nuclei near N≈Z, where spin-orbit splitting is small, and suggest that the interplay between neutron-neutron and neutron-proton pairings in asymmetric nuclei may mirror the behavior found in asymmetric nuclear matter.
