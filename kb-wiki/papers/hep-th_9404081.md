# Supersymmetry without Fermions

> The simplest $N=2$ supersymmetric quantum mechanical system is realized in terms of the bosonic creation and annihilation operators obeying either ordinary or deformed Heisenberg algebra involving Klein operator. The construction comprises both the exact and spontaneously broken supersymmetry cases with the scale of supersymmetry breaking being governed by the deformation parameter. Proceeding from the broken supersymmetry case, we realize the Bose-Fermi transformation and obtain spin-$1/2$ representation of $SU(2)$ group in terms of one bosonic oscillator. We demonstrate that the constructions can be generalized to the more complicated $N=2$ supersymmetric systems, in particular, corresponding to the Witten supersymmetric quantum mechanics.

# Supersymmetry without Fermions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that $N=2$ supersymmetry can be realized using only bosonic creation and annihilation operators, without any fermionic degrees of freedom, by incorporating the Klein operator. The construction yields both exact and spontaneously broken supersymmetry, with the caveat that the supersymmetry breaking scale is governed by a deformation parameter in the deformed Heisenberg algebra case, and the Hamiltonian in the simplest case acquires a nonlocal character in the Schrödinger representation.

## Method

The author constructs nilpotent supercharge operators $Q^{\pm}_{\epsilon} = a^{\mp}\Pi_{\pm\epsilon}$ using bosonic oscillator operators $a^{\pm}$ and projectors $\Pi_{\pm} = \frac{1}{2}(1\pm K)$ built from the Klein operator $K = \exp(i\pi N)$. The supersymmetric Hamiltonian $H_{\epsilon} = \frac{1}{2}\{a^{+},a^{-}\} - \frac{1}{2}\epsilon K$ is derived from the anticommutator $\{Q^{+}_{\epsilon},Q^{-}_{\epsilon}\}$, and the construction is generalized to deformed Heisenberg algebras $[a^{-},a^{+}] = 1 + \nu K$ and to more general operators $A^{\pm}$ corresponding to Witten supersymmetric quantum mechanics.

## Key numbers

- For the ordinary bosonic oscillator with $\epsilon = -$ (spontaneously broken supersymmetry): energy eigenvalues $E_{n}^{-} = 2[n/2] + 1$, where $[n/2]$ is the integer part of $n/2$, with all states having $E_{n}^{-} > 0$.
- For the ordinary bosonic oscillator with $\epsilon = +$ (exact supersymmetry): energy eigenvalues $E_{n}^{+} = 2[(n+1)/2]$, with the vacuum state having $E_{0}^{+} = 0$.
- For the deformed Heisenberg algebra with deformation parameter $\nu$ and $\epsilon = -$: energy eigenvalues $E_{n}^{-} = 2[n/2] + 1 + \nu$, with the restriction $\nu > -1$ for unitary representation.
- For the deformed Heisenberg algebra with $\epsilon = +$: the spectrum remains $E_{n}^{+} = 2[(n+1)/2]$, unchanged from the ordinary oscillator case.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Brzezinski, Egusquiza, and Macfarlane (1994) on uncovering supersymmetry structure within the bosonic oscillator Fock space, and relates to earlier Bose-Fermi transmutation constructions by Jordan (1935) used in planar physics phenomena. The paper enables generalization of bosonized supersymmetry to $N>2$ supersymmetric systems, parasupersymmetric quantum mechanics, and $(1+1)$-dimensional quantum field theories, as well as providing a concrete connection to Witten supersymmetric quantum mechanics through odd superpotentials.
