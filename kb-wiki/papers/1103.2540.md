# Model independent analysis of polarization effects in elastic proton-electron scattering

> The experimental observables for the elastic reaction induced by protons on electrons are calculated in the Born approximation. Model independent expressions are derived for the differential cross section and polarization observables. Numerical estimations are given for spin correlation coefficients, polarization transfer coefficients and depolarization coefficients, in a wide kinematical range. Specific attention is given to the kinematical conditions, i.e., to the specific range of incident energy and transferred momentum.

# Model independent analysis of polarization effects in elastic proton-electron scattering

## Key claim
The authors derive model-independent expressions for polarization observables in elastic proton-electron scattering within the Born approximation (one-photon exchange), but the numerical estimates rely on the dipole parametrization of proton form factors and are valid only for proton beam energies above approximately 3 MeV, where the Born approximation condition α/v < 0.1c is satisfied and screening effects are manageable.

## Method
The authors calculate the matrix element for elastic proton-electron scattering in the laboratory frame (electron at rest) using the one-photon-exchange approximation, expressing the differential cross section and polarization observables in terms of the Dirac and Pauli (or Sachs) proton electromagnetic form factors. They derive explicit analytic expressions for three classes of polarization observables: polarization transfer coefficients (from polarized electron target to scattered proton), spin correlation coefficients (both initial particles polarized), and depolarization coefficients (polarization transfer from initial to final proton). Numerical evaluations are performed using the standard dipole parametrization of the proton form factors over a wide range of incident proton energies and scattering angles.

## Key numbers
- Maximum four-momentum transfer squared: (−k²)ₘₐₓ = 4m²(E² − M²)/(M² + 2mE + m²), where m is electron mass and M is proton mass; this quantity is proportional to the electron mass squared, restricting the reaction to very small momentum transfers.
- The relation between proton energy E and electron energy ε for equal total energy s: E = (M/m)ε ≈ 2000 ε.
- Maximum scattering angle for the hadron: sin θₕ,ₘₐₓ = m/M, which for the proton gives a maximum angle of order 0.5 mrad.
- Unpolarized total cross section at T = 23 MeV: σᵤₙₚ = 4.4 × 10⁸ mb; at T = 50 GeV: σᵤₙₚ = 1.8 × 10⁷ mb.
- Integrated polarization transfer cross section σₙₙ at T = 23 MeV: −139.3 mb; at T = 50 GeV: −0.5 × 10⁻² mb.
- Integrated polarization coefficient Tₙₙ at T = 23 MeV: −3.8 × 10⁻¹²; at T = 50 GeV: −0.2 × 10⁻⁴.
- The figure of merit F² for polarimetry reaches its maximum at T ≈ 10 GeV; assuming a luminosity of 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹, the beam polarization could be measured with 1% error in approximately 3 minutes.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of elastic electron-proton scattering (Rosenbluth, 1950; Re, 1968) to the inverse kinematics case of proton projectiles on electron targets at rest, which is relevant for beam polarimetry at RHIC (Gl, 1997) and for polarized antiproton beam production at FAIR (Ra, 2004). The authors provide explicit analytic expressions for depolarization coefficients that had not been previously derived in the literature, and they give numerical estimates over a wider kinematic range than earlier calculations. The results enable the design of proton-beam polarimeters based on scattering from polarized electron targets and allow comparison with ongoing experimental efforts at facilities such as FAIR and RHIC.
