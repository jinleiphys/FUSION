# Protons in High Density Neutron Matter

> We discuss the possible implication of the recent predictions of two new properties of high momentum distribution of nucleons in asymmetric nuclei for neutron star dynamics. The first property is about the approximate scaling relation between proton and neutron high momentum distributions weighted by their relative fractions ($x_p$ and $x_n$) in the nucleus. The second is the existence of inverse proportionality of the high momentum distribution strength of protons and neutrons to $x_{p/n}$. Based on these predictions we model the high momentum distribution functions for asymmetric nuclei and demonstrate that it describes reasonably well the high momentum characteristics of light nuclei. We also extrapolate our results to heavy nuclei as well as infinite nuclear matter and calculate the relative fractions of protons and neutrons with momenta above $k_{F}$. Our results indicate that for neutron stars starting at {\em three} nuclear saturation densities the protons with $x_p = {1\over 9}$ will populate mostly the high momentum tail of the momentum distribution while only $2\%$ of the neutrons will do so. Such a situation may have many implications for different observations of neutron stars which we discuss.

# Protons in High Density Neutron Matter

## Key claim
In neutron star matter at densities starting at three times nuclear saturation density, protons with a relative fraction of 1/9 will populate mostly the high momentum tail of the momentum distribution, while only 2% of neutrons will do so, based on extrapolations from short-range correlation properties in asymmetric nuclei.

## Method
The author models high momentum distribution functions for asymmetric nuclei using two predicted properties: an approximate scaling relation between proton and neutron momentum distributions weighted by their relative fractions, and an inverse proportionality of high momentum distribution strength to the relative fraction of each nucleon type. These predictions are checked against realistic calculations for light nuclei using Faddeev equations, Correlated Gaussian Basis approach, and Variational Monte Carlo methods, then extrapolated to heavy nuclei and infinite nuclear matter using the a2 parameter fitted to inclusive A(e,e')X scattering data at short-range correlation kinematics.

## Key numbers
- For 3He, the approximation of Eq. (p=n) in the range of 300-600 MeV/c is good on the level of 15%
- In Gold (197Au), the relative fraction of high momentum (>= kF) protons is 31%, compared to 20% for neutrons, a 50% excess
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, a2(rho0,0) ≈ 7.03 ± 0.41
- At 3rho0 densities, half of the protons will be off-Fermi-shell; at rho >= 4.5rho0, all protons will populate the high momentum tail
- Only about 2% of neutrons populate the high momentum part of the momentum distribution at these densities
- Kinetic energy differences between protons and neutrons range from 1.65 MeV in 11B to 11.53 MeV in 8He

## Context
This work extends predictions from Sargsian (recent work) on two new properties of high momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclei, building on the established dominance of proton-neutron short-range correlations over pp and nn correlations observed in experiments. The results enable predictions for neutron star cooling via Direct URCA processes, proton superfluidity conditions, and equation of state stiffness, and are in qualitative agreement with nuclear matter calculations using Green function methods by Rios. The approach also connects to ultra-cold atomic gas experiments by Shin (2006) and Bulgac, where asymmetric two-component Fermi systems show similar modification of the smaller component.
