# Chiral three-nucleon forces and neutron matter

> We calculate the properties of neutron matter and highlight the physics of chiral three-nucleon forces. For neutrons, only the long-range 2 pi-exchange interactions of the leading chiral three-nucleon forces contribute, and we derive density-dependent two-body interactions by summing the third particle over occupied states in the Fermi sea. Our results for the energy suggest that neutron matter is perturbative at nuclear densities. We study in detail the theoretical uncertainties of the neutron matter energy, provide constraints for the symmetry energy and its density dependence, and explore the impact of chiral three-nucleon forces on the S-wave superfluid pairing gap.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron matter is perturbative at nuclear densities when chiral three-nucleon forces are included, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of low-momentum cutoff and the assumption that only the long-range 2π-exchange part of the leading N²LO three-nucleon forces contributes in pure neutron matter, with the shorter-range c_D and c_E couplings vanishing entirely.

## Method

The authors start from chiral N³LO nucleon-nucleon potentials evolved via renormalization group to low-momentum interactions V_lowk with smooth cutoffs Λ = 1.8–2.8 fm⁻¹, and combine these with the leading N²LO three-nucleon forces. For pure neutron matter, they show that only the c₁ and c₃ terms of the 2π-exchange three-nucleon force contribute, and they construct a density-dependent two-body interaction V̅ by summing the third particle over occupied Fermi-sea states. They then compute the neutron matter energy per particle in a loop expansion around Hartree-Fock, including second-order contributions with fully self-consistent single-particle energies from the Dyson equation.

## Key numbers

- The c₁ coefficient is taken as c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹ and c₃ = -3.2 GeV⁻¹, consistent with the N³LO NN potential of Ref. [N3LO].
- At saturation density (k_F = 1.7 fm⁻¹), the P=0 approximation for the density-dependent two-body interaction V̅ differs from the exact Hartree-Fock result by 340 keV; for lower Fermi momenta, differences are always below 100 keV.
- The density-dependent two-body interaction in the ¹S₀ channel scales approximately as V̅(k',k,k_F) ≈ F(k',k) × (k_F/k̅_F)⁴.
- The effective mass at the Fermi surface shows a larger impact of three-nucleon forces for k_F > 1.3 fm⁻¹ when second-order contributions are included.

## Context

This work extends the systematic chiral effective field theory approach to neutron matter, building on prior developments for nuclear matter saturation by the same authors (Hebeler and Schwenk, 2009) and on low-momentum interactions (V_lowk) from Bogner, Schwenk, and collaborators. The paper provides the first neutron matter results using chiral N²LO three-nucleon forces with evolved NN interactions, and it enables future studies of neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star structure by constraining the symmetry energy and its density dependence. The derived density-dependent two-body interaction framework also allows higher-order many-body calculations that would be prohibitively complex with full three-body forces.
