# Generalized transparency in semi-inclusive processes

> It is argued that the transparency of a medium for passage of a nucleon, knocked-out in a semi-inclusive $(e,e'p)$ reaction and subsequently scattered elastically, is not the same as the one measured in purely elastic scattering. Expressions are given for the properly generalized transparency and those are compared with recently proposed, alternative suggestions. Numerical results are presented for selected nuclear targets and kinematic conditions, applying to the Garino et al and the SLAC NE18 experiment.

## Key claim

The transparency measured in semi-inclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions is not identical to the transparency measured in purely elastic scattering, because the knocked-out nucleon is initially part of the correlated target and the relevant densities in the cross section are non-diagonal, unlike the diagonal densities that appear in elastic scattering.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the generalized transparency in semi-inclusive reactions using two approaches: a closure method based on the first cumulant approximation for binary collisions, and an extreme single-particle model. Both approaches treat final-state interactions (FSI) of the knocked-out proton with the residual core using eikonal approximations and parameterizations of the NN scattering amplitude in terms of total and reaction cross sections. Numerical calculations are performed for selected nuclear targets and kinematic conditions relevant to the Garino et al. and SLAC NE18 experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the elastic transparency, the standard result is given as T^{el,av}(q) = (1/(A sigma_q^{tot})) integral d^2b (1 - exp(-A t(b) sigma_q^{tot})), where sigma_q^{tot} is the total NN cross section and t(b) is the thickness function.
- The generalized semi-inclusive transparency in the Kohama-Yazaki-Seki (KYS) approximation is T^{SI,KYS}(q) = ((A-1) sigma_q^{reac})^{-1} integral d^2b (1 - exp(-(A-1) sigma_q^{reac} t(b))), using the reaction cross section sigma_q^{reac} rather than the total cross section.
- Below the inelastic production threshold, sigma_q^{reac} = 0, causing the KYS transparency to become unity (no FSI), which the authors note is incorrect because elastic FSI contributions are missing.
- The Pandharipande-Pieper transparency is given as T^{SI,PP}(q) approximately = ((A-1) <g> <sigma_q^{tot}>)^{-1} integral d^2b (1 - exp(-(A-1) <sigma_q^{tot}> <g> t(b))), where <g> is an averaged correlation factor.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of transparency in nuclear reactions beyond the elastic scattering formalism, addressing discrepancies observed in the Garino et al. (1990) experiment where the extracted mean-free path exceeded estimates from free NN cross sections by a factor of about 2. The authors compare their approach with the work of Kohama, Yazaki and Seki (KYS) and Pandharipande and Pieper (PP), showing that those earlier formulations produce transparencies independent of the outgoing proton momentum and miss essential elastic FSI contributions below the inelastic threshold. The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear transparencies from knock-out experiments and provide a framework for delineating possible color transparency effects in reactions such as those planned at SLAC NE18.
