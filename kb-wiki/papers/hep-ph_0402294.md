# The number of colors in the decays pi^0, eta, eta-prime -> gamma gamma

> The decays pi^0, eta, eta-prime -> gamma gamma are investigated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the framework of the combined 1/N_c and chiral expansions. Without mixing of the pseudoscalar mesons the N_c independence of the pi^0 and eta decay amplitudes is shown to persist at the one-loop level, although the contribution of the Wess-Zumino-Witten term to the pertinent vertices is not canceled by the N_c dependent part of a Goldstone-Wilczek term. The decay amplitude of the singlet field, on the other hand, depends strongly on N_c and yields under the inclusion of mixing also a strong N_c dependence for the eta decay. Both the eta and eta-prime decay are suited to confirm the number of colors to be N_c=3.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at the one-loop level, the $\pi^0$ and $\eta$ decay amplitudes into two photons remain independent of the number of colors $N_c$ when pseudoscalar meson mixing is neglected, despite the fact that the $N_c$-dependent part of the Goldstone-Wilczek term does not cancel the Wess-Zumino-Witten term contribution at the vertices involving kaons; however, the $\eta$ decay acquires a strong $N_c$ dependence once $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing is included, making both the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ decays suitable for confirming $N_c = 3$.

## Method

The decays $\pi^0, \eta, \eta' \rightarrow \gamma \gamma$ were investigated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the combined $1/N_c$ and chiral expansions (large $N_c$ chiral perturbation theory), including the $\eta'$ explicitly as a dynamical field. The Wess-Zumino-Witten term was decomposed into the conventional $SU(3)$ WZW Lagrangian, a Goldstone-Wilczek term, and counter terms of unnatural parity, and the quark charge matrix was expanded in powers of $1/N_c$ to account for anomaly cancellation. One-loop diagrams and $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing were evaluated to determine the $N_c$ dependence of the decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is $F_\pi \approx 93$ MeV, used as a reference scale in the mixing analysis.
- The tree-level $\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma \gamma$ width is proportional to $N_c^2$, but the $N_c$-dependent part of the Goldstone-Wilczek term cancels the $\pi$-$2\gamma$ and $\eta$-$2\gamma$ vertices at tree level when $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing is neglected.
- The one-loop corrections to the decays at order ${\cal O}(\delta^{3})$ compensate each other, with the first non-vanishing non-analytic piece appearing at order ${\cal O}(\delta^{4})$.
- The mixing angle $\vartheta^{(0)}$ is given by $\sin 2 \vartheta^{(0)} = - \frac{4 \sqrt{2}}{3} \frac{m_K^2 - m_\pi^2}{m_{\eta'}^2 - m_\eta^2}$ at leading order.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Borasoy and Weise (2003) by including loop diagrams and $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing, which were not discussed in that earlier study. It builds on the framework of large $N_c$ chiral perturbation theory developed by Kaiser and Leutwyler (1998, 2000) and by Herrera-Siklody (1998). The results enable a systematic test of $N_c = 3$ through precision measurements of $\eta$ and $\eta'$ two-photon decays, complementing the textbook use of $\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma \gamma$ for this purpose.
