# Are the chiral based $\bar{K}N$ potentials really energy dependent?

## Key claim
The energy dependence of chiral-based $\bar{K}N$ potentials, which produces two poles in the $I=0$ sector associated with the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, is an artifact of the on-shell factorization approximation introduced by Oset and Ramos (1998). When the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved without this approximation, the resulting $T$-matrix has only one pole in the $\Lambda(1405)$ energy region.

## Method
The author constructed a multichannel separable potential model for the $\bar{K}N$ system using the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term of the chiral Lagrangian, with non-relativistic kinematics and dipole form-factor cut-off functions. The full potential was solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation without applying on-shell factorization, yielding a $2n \times 2n$ matrix problem instead of the usual $n \times n$ one. The parameters were fitted to low-energy $K^-p$ cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift, with Coulomb interaction included in the $K^-p$ channel.

## Key numbers
- For the full potential $\bm{V}$: single pole at $z_1(\bm{V}) = (1422 - 26i)\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- For the on-shell factorized potential $\bm{U}$: two poles at $z_1(\bm{U}) = (1427 - 33i)\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and $z_2(\bm{U}) = (1361 - 64i)\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift: $\Delta E(\bm{V}) = 384 - 231i\ \mathrm{eV}$; $\Delta E(\bm{U}) = 313 - 285i\ \mathrm{eV}$; experimental value $(283 \pm 36) - (271 \pm 46)i\ \mathrm{eV}$
- Threshold branching ratio $\gamma$: $\bm{V}$ gives 2.347, $\bm{U}$ gives 2.343, experimental $2.36 \pm 0.04$
- Best-fit meson decay constants: $F_\pi(\bm{V}) = 73.16\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $F_K(\bm{V}) = 98.29\ \mathrm{MeV}$; $F_\pi(\bm{U}) = 102.0\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $F_K(\bm{U}) = 110.2\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Cut-off parameters $\beta_i$ range from 352.4 to 934.6 MeV for $\bm{V}$ and from 444.0 to 1695 MeV for $\bm{U}$

## Context
This work challenges the widely accepted two-pole picture of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance that emerged from chiral perturbation theory calculations following Oset and Ramos (1998). The author argues that previous analyses of CLAS photoproduction data claiming confirmation of two poles are not convincing, noting that one-pole fits to $I=0\ \Sigma^0\pi^0$ data are at least as satisfactory as two-pole fits. The energy-independent potential $\bm{V}$ derived here is suitable for standard quantum mechanical calculations in systems with more than two nucleons, including coordinate-space variational approaches, where energy-dependent potentials cause serious difficulties. The work suggests that calculations of $\bar{K}$-nuclear clusters and strangeness nuclear physics results based on the two-pole chiral interactions may need reconsideration.
