# Unveiling pairing anti-halo effect in odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections of weakly bound nuclei

> We investigate the spatial extension of weakly bound Ne and C isotopes by taking into account the pairing correlation with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method and a 3-body model, respectively. We show that the odd-even staggering in the reaction cross sections of $^{30,31,32}$Ne and $^{14,15,16}$C are successfully reproduced, and thus the staggering can be attributed to the pairing anti-halo effect. A correlation between a one-neutron separation energy and the anti-halo effect is demonstrated for $s$- and p-waves using the HFB wave functions.

# Unveiling pairing anti-halo effect in odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering observed in reaction cross sections of neutron-rich Ne isotopes (A=30-32) and C isotopes (A=14-16) can be attributed to the pairing anti-halo effect, but only when the one-neutron separation energy is sufficiently small (approximately below 1 MeV); for larger separation energies the staggering essentially disappears.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction of surface type, adjusted to reproduce the empirical neutron pairing gap for 30Ne. For the C isotopes, a three-body model was employed. Reaction cross sections were then calculated using Glauber theory with the microscopic densities, incorporating effects beyond the optical limit approximation, and compared to experimental data for 30,31,32Ne on a 12C target at 240 MeV/nucleon and for 14,15,16C on a 12C target at 83 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections for 30,31,32Ne on 12C at 240 MeV/nucleon: with Sn(31Ne)=0.32 MeV, clear odd-even staggering matching experimental data is obtained; with Sn(31Ne)=1.49 MeV, almost no staggering is seen.
- For 31Ne, the empirical separation energy is Sn=0.29±1.64 MeV.
- For 14C, 15C, and 16C, the calculated rms radii are 2.53 fm, 2.90 fm, and 2.81 fm respectively.
- For 11Li, the experimental matter radius is 3.27±0.24 fm; the two-neutron rms radius outside the 9Li core is estimated as (5.68)² fm².
- For single-particle states in 11Li with Sn=0.15 MeV, the rms radii are 6.48 fm for 1p1/2 and 10.89 fm for 2s1/2, leading to predicted 11Li radii of 3.53 fm (pure (1p1/2)²) and 5.14 fm (pure (2s1/2)²), both larger than the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical concept of the pairing anti-halo effect proposed by Bennaceur (2000) using HFB methods, and provides the first clear experimental signature through comparison with reaction cross section data from Takechi (2010) for Ne isotopes and Fang (2004) for C isotopes. The results contradict the expectation from pure mean-field (Hartree-Fock) calculations, which would predict divergent radii for weakly bound s- and p-wave states, and also differ from deformed Woods-Saxon calculations by Minomo (2011) that found no significant odd-even staggering. The work enables systematic interpretation of odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections observed across multiple isotopic chains near the drip line, including C, Ne, and Mg isotopes.
