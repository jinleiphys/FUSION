# Continuum-discretized coupled-channels method for four-body nuclear breakup in $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering

> We propose a fully quantum-mechanical method of treating four-body nuclear breakup processes in scattering of a projectile consisting of three constituents, by extending the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. The three-body continuum states of the projectile are discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile with the Gaussian basis functions. For $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV, the validity of the method is tested by convergence of the elastic and breakup cross sections with respect to increasing the number of the basis functions. Effects of the four-body breakup and the Borromean structure of $^6$He on the elastic and total reaction cross sections are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantum-mechanical method (four-body CDCC) for treating four-body nuclear breakup in scattering of a three-body projectile, and demonstrates its validity for $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV; the abstract glosses over the fact that the method neglects Coulomb breakup processes and that the imaginary part of the coupling potential ($N_I$) must be optimized to fit experimental data, with values of 0.5 at 18 MeV and 0.3 at 229.8 MeV.

## Method

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) is extended to treat four-body breakup by expanding the total scattering wave function in terms of bound and discretized-continuum states of the three-body projectile ($^6$He = $n$+$n$+$^4$He). The three-body continuum states of $^6$He are discretized using the pseudo-state (PS) method, where the internal Hamiltonian of $^6$He is diagonalized with Gaussian basis functions in a Gaussian expansion method (GEM), producing discrete eigenstates that approximate the continuum. The resulting coupled-channels equations are solved for $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV, using double-folding model coupling potentials with the DDM3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction and a complex normalization factor $N_R+iN_I$ (with $N_R=1$ fixed and $N_I$ optimized).

## Key numbers

- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 MeV, the optimum value of $N_I$ determined from measured elastic cross section is 0.5.
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 229.8 MeV, the optimum value of $N_I$ is 0.3.
- The total reaction cross section for $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 18 MeV shows a 10% enhancement relative to that for $^6$Li+$^{12}$C at similar energy; half of this enhancement (about 5%) is attributed to the Borromean structure of $^6$He.
- The real part of the dynamical polarization potential at 18 MeV (grazing angular momentum $J_{\rm gr}=10$) is repulsive and about 30% of the real part of the double-folded potential; the imaginary part is about 20% of the imaginary part of the double-folded potential.
- High-lying states of $^6$He with $\epsilon_{nI} > 12$ MeV (for 18 MeV scattering) and $\epsilon_{nI} > 25$ MeV (for 229.8 MeV scattering) give no effect on the elastic and breakup $S$-matrix elements.

## Context

This work extends the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), previously used for three-body breakup in scattering of two-body projectiles, to treat four-body breakup in scattering of three-body projectiles such as $^6$He and $^{11}$Li. It improves upon earlier semi-classical eikonal and adiabatic calculations (e.g., those applied to $^6$He and $^{11}$Li scattering around 50 MeV/nucleon) which work well at higher energies but are not applicable to low-energy scattering such as the 3 MeV/nucleon $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^6$He)$^{12}$C experiment by Milin (2004). The method also supersedes the di-neutron model used in earlier CDCC analyses of $^6$He scattering, whose accuracy had not been confirmed. The four-body CDCC enables fully quantum-mechanical analysis of four-body breakup and allows quantification of the Borromean effect on reaction cross sections, with future work needed to include Coulomb breakup using complex-range Gaussian basis functions as suggested by Egami.
