# Electrostatic analogy for integrable pairing force Hamiltonians

> For the exactly solved reduced BCS model an electrostatic analogy exists; in particular it served to obtain the exact thermodynamic limit of the model from the Richardson Bethe ansatz equations. We present an electrostatic analogy for a wider class of integrable Hamiltonians with pairing force interactions. We apply it to obtain the exact thermodynamic limit of this class of models. To verify the analytical results, we compare them with numerical solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations for finite systems at half-filling for the ground state.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the electrostatic analogy, previously known for the reduced BCS model, to a wider class of integrable pairing-force Hamiltonians with non-uniform couplings, and use it to obtain the exact thermodynamic limit of these models, provided the pairing strength scales as \(\tilde{g}=G/\Omega\) with fixed \(G\) and the number of pairs grows as \(N=\nu\Omega\).

## Method

The Bethe ansatz equations for the generalized pairing Hamiltonians are recast into algebraic form via the variable transformation \(z_\alpha = \tanh(p e_\alpha)/p\) and \(x_i = \tanh(p u_i)/p\), revealing that the constant external electric field of the uniform BCS case is replaced by two isolated fixed charges at \(\pm 1/p\). The thermodynamic limit is then taken by dividing the equations by \(\Omega\), defining charge densities, and constructing the total electric field as an ansatz function with branch cuts along arcs determined by equipotential conditions; the unknown arc endpoints and screened charge densities are fixed by requiring the field to vanish at infinity and by matching screened to bare charges at the source points.

## Key numbers

* The pairing strength scales as \(\tilde{g}=G/\Omega\) with fixed \(G\) and the number of pairs as \(N=\nu\Omega\), where \(\nu\) is the filling fraction.
* The two isolated fixed charges in the transformed Bethe equations are \(-Q_{\pm}^{(0)} = -\tfrac{1}{2}(\nu-1/2 \pm 1/(pG))\).
* The transformed Debye shell endpoints are \(a_0 = \tanh(p u_1)/p\) and \(b_0 = \tanh(p u_\Omega)/p\).
* For the hyperbolic case (\(p=1\)) with uniform level spacing and Debye shell \([0,2\omega_D]\), the ground-state arc endpoints coincide (\(\Delta=0\)) when \(1/(\rho_0 G) = 2\omega_D\), where \(\rho_0\) is the single-particle energy density.
* Numerical solutions are shown for \(\Omega=200\), \(N=100\) (half-filling) with \(u_i=\eps_i\) ranging from 0 to 2 for the hyperbolic model.

## Context

This work extends the electrostatic analogy originally developed by Gaudin (1976) and Richardson (1977) for the reduced BCS model to the broader class of integrable pairing Hamiltonians with non-uniform couplings introduced by Amico et al. (2001). It enables the exact thermodynamic limit of these generalized models to be obtained analytically, and the results are verified by comparison with numerical solutions of the Bethe ansatz equations for finite systems at half-filling. The approach connects to the quasi-classical limit of inhomogeneous XXZ spin chains and to the work of Sierra and coworkers (2001) on the thermodynamic limit of the BCS model.
