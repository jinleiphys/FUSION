# Two-pion emission decays of negative parity singly heavy baryons

> We investigate two-pion emission decays of singly charmed and bottom baryons, focusing on $Λ_Q^*(1P)$ and $Ξ_Q^*(1P)$ with $Q=c$ (charm) or $b$ (bottom) quarks and $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-$, belonging to antisymmetric flavor triplet $\bar{\boldsymbol{3}}_F$. Our analysis encompasses both sequential processes, involving intermediate states belonging to symmetric flavor sextet $\boldsymbol{6}_F$ such as $Σ_Q(1S)$ and $Ξ_Q^\prime(1S)$ respectively with $J^P=1/2^+,3/2^+$, derived from the chiral quark model, and direct process crucial for comparison with experimental data, whose coupling constants estimated using the chiral-partner scheme. We also incorporate the convolution of the parent particle's mass for the Dalitz plot, enabling a more realistic comparison with experimental data. We scrutinize the Dalitz plots of these negative parity states in light of recent Belle measurements for $Λ_c(2625)^+$. Our findings support the assignment of $Λ_c(2625)^+$ as the $λ$-mode excitation with $J^P=3/2^-$ in the quark model, deduced from the the ${Λ_cπ}$ invariant mass distribution, and we then give predictions for other cases, including the $Ξ_Q^*$ decays. The observed asymmetry in the ${ππ}$ invariant mass distribution underscores the important role of the direct process, reflecting the chiral-partner structure in the heavy baryon sector. It is evident that the presence of the direct process is not significant in the three-body decays unless the $S$-wave resonance contribution is suppressed. We suggest further experimental verification to test our predictions and get more insights into the structure of heavy baryons.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $\lambda$-mode excitation assignment for $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ with $J^P=3/2^-$ is supported by the $\Lambda_c\pi$ invariant mass distribution from Belle data, but they note that the direct two-pion process only becomes significant when the $S$-wave resonance contribution is suppressed, as occurs in this specific decay.

## Method

The authors compute two-pion emission decay amplitudes for $\Lambda_Q^*(1P)$ and $\Xi_Q^*(1P)$ baryons (with $Q=c$ or $b$) using a coherent sum of five Feynman diagrams: four sequential processes through intermediate $\tilde{B}_Q(1S)$ and $\tilde{B}_Q^*(1S)$ states belonging to the symmetric flavor sextet $\boldsymbol{6}_F$, and one direct two-pion coupling. Coupling constants for sequential processes are extracted from the chiral quark model by equating helicity amplitudes, while direct process couplings are estimated using the chiral-partner scheme. The Dalitz plots incorporate a convolution of the parent particle's mass via a Breit-Wigner distribution to account for finite width effects.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ total decay width: calculated 0.570 MeV, experimental upper limit $<0.52$ MeV (PDG)
- $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$ total decay width: calculated 1.936 MeV, experimental $2.6 \pm 0.6$ MeV (PDG)
- $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ decay to $\Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-$ via $\Sigma_c^{0}\pi^+$: calculated 0.029 MeV (experimental branching fraction $5.19\%$)
- $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ decay to $\Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-$ via $\Sigma_c^{++}\pi^-$: calculated 0.028 MeV (experimental branching fraction $5.13\%$)
- $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ direct three-body contribution to $\Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-$: 0.053 MeV
- $\Lambda_b(5912)^0$ total decay width: calculated 0.0108 MeV, experimental upper limit $<0.25$ MeV (PDG)
- $\Lambda_b(5920)^0$ total decay width: calculated 0.022 MeV, experimental upper limit $<0.19$ MeV (PDG)
- Pion decay constant used: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV
- Constituent quark masses: $m_{u(d)} = 0.35$ GeV, $m_s = 0.55$ GeV, $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, $m_b = 5.0$ GeV
- Confinement strength: $k = 0.029$ GeV$^3$

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Arifi et al. (2017, 2018) on three-body decays of $\Lambda_c(2595)^+$ and $\Lambda_c(2625)^+$ to systematically cover $\Lambda_Q^*$ and $\Xi_Q^*$ baryons for both charm and bottom sectors, incorporating finite-width effects on Dalitz plots as introduced by Arifi et al. (2020). The chiral-partner scheme for direct process couplings follows Kawakami et al. (2018, 2019). The results support the $\lambda$-mode assignment over $\rho$-mode excitations for low-lying negative parity states, and provide predictions for $\Lambda_b$ and $\Xi_{c(b)}$ decays that can be tested by LHCb, Belle II, and BESIII experiments.
