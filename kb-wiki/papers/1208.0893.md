# Proton Form Factors: phenomenology

> A general description of proton form factors is presented, in the whole kinematical region. The existing data and selected phenomenological models are briefly discussed.

# Proton Form Factors: phenomenology

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological overview of proton form factors across both space-like and time-like kinematical regions, noting that while the Sachs electromagnetic form factors are well-described by a dipole form in the space-like region at low momentum transfer, recent polarization measurements reveal a monotonic decrease of the ratio μG_E/G_M with q² that deviates from unity, and that in the time-like region the individual determination of electric and magnetic form factors remains poorly constrained due to limited statistics and the need for assumptions about their relative magnitude.

## Method
The paper reviews the standard formalism for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, including the Dirac (F₁) and Pauli (F₂) form factors and the Sachs electric (G_E) and magnetic (G_M) form factors. It discusses the Rosenbluth separation method for unpolarized elastic electron-proton scattering in the space-like region and the polarization transfer method developed by Akhiezer and Rekalo (1968). For the time-like region, it describes the analysis of annihilation reactions p̅ + p ↔ e⁺ + e⁻ and initial state radiation processes e⁺ + e⁻ → p̅ + p + γ, including the extraction of a generalized form factor |F_P| under different assumptions about the G_E/G_M ratio.

## Key numbers
- The Rosenbluth method has determined G_E and G_M up to Q² ≃ 8.8 GeV², and G_M has been extracted up to Q² ≃ 31 GeV² under the assumption that G_E = 0.
- The dipole form is approximated as G_D(Q²) = (1 + Q²/0.71 GeV²)⁻².
- The probability to irradiate one or more photons from a few GeV electron may reach 40%.
- The PANDA experiment at FAIR plans to use an antiproton beam of momentum up to 15 GeV and luminosity ℒ = 2·10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹, with individual form factor determination possible up to q² ∼ 14 (GeV/c)² and absolute cross section measurement up to q² ∼ 28 (GeV/c)².
- The time-like region threshold is at q² > 4M², where M is the nucleon mass.

## Context
This review extends the phenomenological understanding of nucleon form factors by comparing the Rosenbluth method (Rosenbluth, 1950) with the polarization method (Akhiezer and Rekalo, 1968), highlighting the discrepancy between results from the two approaches that is likely attributable to radiative corrections. The work discusses how Vector Dominance Models (VDM) (Wan, 2004) and dispersion relation approaches can describe the complex nature of form factors in the time-like region, and it enables future measurements by experiments at JLab, Mainz, Novosibirsk, BES, and PANDA at FAIR, with the highest priority being the individual determination of electric and magnetic form factors in the time-like region and measurement of polarization observables to determine the relative phase of form factors.
