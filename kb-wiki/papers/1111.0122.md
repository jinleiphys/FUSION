# The Determination of $\sin^2 θ_W$ in Neutrino Scattering: no more anomaly

> We review the corrections to the NuTeV determination of sin2 qW, concluding that it is no longer appropriate to present it as an "anomaly". Indeed, when well understood corrections associated with charge symmetry violation and the iso-vector nuclear force are properly included, the measurement is completely consistent with the Standard Model.

## Key claim

When well-understood corrections for charge symmetry violation (CSV) and the isovector nuclear force (isovector EMC effect) are properly included, the NuTeV measurement of sin²θ_W becomes completely consistent with the Standard Model, removing the previously claimed 3σ anomaly.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical corrections to the NuTeV determination of sin²θ_W from the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, focusing on two main effects: charge symmetry violation arising from the u-d quark mass difference, and the isovector EMC effect in which the repulsive isovector nuclear force shifts momentum from all u quarks to all d quarks in a nucleus with N ≠ Z. The analysis uses analytic expressions for the first moments of CSV parton distributions, lattice QCD simulations of baryon octet PDFs, and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for nuclear medium effects.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV original result: sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), differing from the Standard Model by 3σ
- CSV correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio: ΔR_CSV ≈ -0.0020 (at the quark model scale Q²₀ ≈ 0.5 GeV²)
- Lattice QCD first moments of CSV distributions: δu = -0.0023 ± 0.0006 and δd = 0.0020 ± 0.0003
- Bag model values (at 4 GeV²): δu⁻ = -0.0014 and δd⁻ = 0.0015
- Neutron-proton mass difference: δM = 1.3 MeV; down-up quark mass difference: δm = m_d - m_u ≈ 4 MeV
- Momentum fraction carried by down valence quarks at bag model scale: D_V between 0.2 and 0.33; total valence momentum fraction U_V + D_V ≈ 0.80

## Context

This paper reviews and resolves the "NuTeV anomaly" reported by Zeller (2001), where the NuTeV collaboration's measurement of sin²θ_W on an iron target disagreed with the Standard Model. The work extends earlier CSV calculations by Sather (1991) and Rodionov et al., which had been ignored in the NuTeV analysis, and incorporates the isovector EMC effect predicted by Cloet (2009) using the QMC model of Guichon (1995). The corrected result, shown in a reanalysis by Bentz (2009), brings the NuTeV measurement into agreement with other precision electroweak tests including LEP/SLC Z-pole results, CDF/D0 measurements, and atomic parity violation experiments. The remaining open question concerns the strange-antistrange quark asymmetry s(x) - s̄(x), which the author argues is unlikely to be large enough to significantly affect the result.
