# Modified Knudsen ansatz and elliptic flow in $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV pp collisions

> Assuming that hot spots are formed in initial pp collisions, in a modified Knudsen ansatz, which accounts for the entropy generation in viscous fluid evolution, we have given predictions for elliptic flow in $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV pp collisions. Predicted flow depends on the number of hot spots and hot spot sizes. If two to four hot spots of size $\approx$0.1 fm are formed in initial pp collisions, in events with multiplicity $n_ {mult}\approx$10-15, modified Knudsen ansatz predicted flow is accessible experimentally in 4th order cumulant method.

# Modified Knudsen ansatz and elliptic flow in √s=14 TeV pp collisions

## Key claim

If two to four hot spots of size ≈0.1 fm are formed in initial pp collisions, in events with multiplicity n_mult ≈ 10–15, the modified Knudsen ansatz predicts elliptic flow accessible experimentally via the 4th order cumulant method; the prediction depends on the number of hot spots and their sizes, and the modified ansatz accounts for entropy generation in viscous fluid evolution.

## Method

The author extends the standard Knudsen ansatz (Bhalerao 2005) to include entropy generation during viscous evolution, replacing the ideal-fluid assumption of conserved particle number. The modified ansatz is fitted to PHOBOS measurements of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at √s = 62 and 200 GeV, treating the ideal hydrodynamic limit (v₂/ε)^ih and the scaled viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/(τ_i T_i s) as free parameters. The fitted parameters are then applied to pp collisions at √s = 14 TeV, assuming Gaussian hot spots randomly distributed within a sphere of radius 0.56 fm, to predict elliptic flow as a function of multiplicity, hot spot number, and hot spot size.

## Key numbers

- In √s = 7 TeV pp collisions, ALICE measured charged particle density dN_ch/dη ≈ 6 in |η| < 1, giving n_mult ≈ 9.
- Extrapolation to √s = 14 TeV gives n_mult ≈ 11; only v₂ ≥ 0.16 can be measured via 4th order cumulant.
- In √s = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, peripheral 30–40% collisions have multiplicity ~640 and v₂ ~ 0.08.
- Hydrodynamic simulations (Prasad 2009) for √s = 14 TeV pp collisions give v₂ < 0.02 even in peripheral collisions.
- Fits to PHOBOS data yield (v₂/ε)^ih = 0.36 for both K₀σc_s = 0.05 fm² and 0.23 fm²; η/(τ_i T_i s) = 0.29 for K₀σc_s = 0.05 fm² and 1.37 for K₀σc_s = 0.23 fm².
- For 2–4 hot spots of size 0.1 fm, modified Knudsen ansatz predicts v₂ ≈ 0.18–0.20 at large multiplicity.
- For 2 hot spots of size 0.1 fm, modified ansatz predicts v₂ ≈ 0.2 at large multiplicity; unmodified predicts v₂ ≈ 0.14.
- For 2 hot spots of size 0.2 fm, modified ansatz predicts v₂ ≈ 0.1; for size 0.3 fm, v₂ ≈ 0.06.
- Minimum multiplicity for 4th order cumulant detection with 2 hot spots of size 0.1 fm: n_mult^min = 12 (modified), 15 (unmodified).
- Minimum multiplicity for 2nd order cumulant detection with 2 hot spots of size 0.1 fm: n_mult^min = 30 (modified), 51 (unmodified).

## Context

This work extends the Knudsen ansatz approach of Bhalerao (2005) by incorporating viscous entropy generation, following the modification introduced by Chaudhuri (2010). It builds on the idea of hot spot formation in pp collisions at LHC, previously considered by Casalderrey-Solana (2009), Bozek (2009), and Chaudhuri (2009). The predictions enable experimental searches for elliptic flow in high-multiplicity pp events at √s = 14 TeV, where conventional hydrodynamic simulations (Prasad 2009) predict negligible flow, and test whether collective behavior can arise from hot spot initial conditions.
