# Dynamically generated magnetic moment in the Wigner-function formalism

> We study how the mass and magnetic moment of the quarks are dynamically generated in nonequilibrium quark matter. We derive the equal-time transport and constraint equations for the quark Wigner function in a magnetized quark model and solve them in the semi-classical expansion. The quark mass and magnetic moment are self-consistently coupled to the Wigner function and controlled by the kinetic equations. While the quark mass is dynamically generated at the classical level, the quark magnetic moment is a pure quantum effect, induced by the quark spin interaction with the external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The quark magnetic moment is a pure quantum effect that vanishes at the classical level, while the quark mass can be dynamically generated already at the classical level; the magnetic moment arises only when quantum fluctuations (first-order corrections in the semiclassical expansion) are included, and it requires both a nonzero tensor coupling constant and an external magnetic field.

## Method

The authors derived equal-time transport and constraint equations for the quark Wigner function in an SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a tensor interaction, coupled to an external magnetic field. They solved these kinetic equations using a semiclassical expansion in powers of ℏ, obtaining the classical (zeroth-order) and first-order quantum corrections, and self-consistently determined the dynamical quark mass and magnetic moment from the Wigner function components.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters are fixed by fitting the pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV and the chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = (−250 MeV)³ in vacuum at T = B = 0, giving Gs = 3.52 GeV⁻² and Pauli-Villars mass parameter Λ = 1127 MeV.
- In the lowest-Landau-level approximation, the dynamical magnetic moment F₃ and dynamical quark mass mq are proportional: F₃/mq = Gt/(2Gs), independent of temperature, magnetic field, and regularization scheme.
- Numerical results are shown for a constant magnetic field eB = 10 m²π (where mπ is the pion mass) at various temperatures and tensor coupling strengths Gt.
- At zero temperature, the dynamical magnetic moment is linearly proportional to the external magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the study of dynamically generated anomalous magnetic moments from massless QED (Schwinger 1948) and one-flavor NJL models (Refs. chiralmag2, amm2) to a two-flavor NJL model with tensor interaction, using the Wigner-function formalism for nonequilibrium systems. The approach builds on equal-time transport theory previously developed for QED (Refs. qt3, qt4, qt5) and enables the study of space-time dependent magnetic moments in the early, nonequilibrium stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic fields can reach eB ∼ m²π (Refs. b2, b3, b4, b5).
