# Long-Range Correlations in Closed Shell Nuclei

> The effects of correlations on the bulk properties of nuclei are investigated in large model spaces including up to 21 single-particle orbits. The evaluation of the single-particle Green function is made feasible by means of the BAGEL approximation. The spectral function for single-nucleon pick-up and removal is investigated for the nuclei $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ . Special attention is paid to the effects produced by correlations on the calculated ground state properties of closed shell nuclei. It is observed that correlations beyond the Brueckner Hartree Fock approximation tend to improve the results obtained using realistic nucleon nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

Correlations beyond the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation, specifically low- and medium-energy excitations (up to about 100 MeV) treated via the BAGEL approximation for the single-particle Green function, tend to improve the calculated ground-state properties of closed shell nuclei like 16O and 40Ca when using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, though the Hamiltonian is adjusted to reproduce experimental radii.

## Method

The authors employed a Green-function approach within a finite model space of up to 21 single-particle orbits, using the BAGEL approximation to represent the Green function in terms of a few characteristic poles. The irreducible self-energy included contributions from 2 particle-1 hole (2p1h) and 2 hole-1 particle (2h1p) configurations, with the residual interaction given by a G-matrix that accounts for short-range correlations via the Bethe-Goldstone equation. The Dyson equation was solved by diagonalizing a matrix that couples single-particle states to 2p1h and 2h1p subspaces, with the BAGEL truncation reducing the dimension to a manageable size.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the spin-orbit splitting for l=1 hole states decreased from 3.6 MeV (HF) to 2.4 MeV when correlations beyond HF were included.
- For 16O, the calculated spin-orbit splitting for d5/2 and d3/2 particle states was 4.92 MeV, slightly below the HF result of 5.41 MeV and in good agreement with the experimental value of 5.09 MeV.
- For 16O, the total occupation probability was 0.926 for the p3/2 state and 0.901 for the p1/2 state, with the main peak contributing only 0.781 and 0.790, respectively.
- For 16O, the occupation probability for d-states was 0.052 and for f-states was 0.010.
- For 40Ca, the spectral function for l=0 hole states near the Fermi energy was reproduced, with a broad distribution of l=0 strength predicted down to energies of -100 MeV.
- The BAGEL(20,20) approximation was used for final results, with a folding width of Gamma = 2 MeV for presentation.

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach by including long-range correlations from low- and medium-energy excitations, which are typically neglected in standard BHF calculations that focus on short-range correlations. The study builds on the BAGEL method introduced by Skouras (1991) and Skouras (1992) for representing the Green function, and it enables comparison with experimental data from (e,e')p experiments by Witt (1990), Grab (1992), and Kramer (1993). The results also connect to nuclear matter studies by Kuo (1990) and Kuo (1991) on the importance of particle-particle and hole-hole ladder diagrams for saturation properties.
