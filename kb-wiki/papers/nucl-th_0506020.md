# $η$-meson in nuclear matter

> The $η$-nucleon ($η$N) interactions are deduced from the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading-order terms. Combining the relativistic mean-field theory for nucleon system, we have studied the in-medium properties of $η$-meson. We find that all the elastic scattering $η$N interactions come from the next-to-leading-order terms. The $η$N sigma term is found to be about 280$\pm$130 MeV. The off-shell terms are also important to the in-medium properties of $η$-meson. On application of the latest determination of the $η$N scattering length, the ratio of $η$-meson effective mass to its vacuum value is near $0.84\pm0.015$, while the optical potential is about $-(83\pm5)$ MeV, at the normal nuclear density.

## Key claim

The authors derive the η-nucleon (ηN) interaction from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading-order terms and find that all elastic scattering ηN interactions come from these next-to-leading-order terms, with the leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg term vanishing entirely. The claimed in-medium properties depend critically on the ηN scattering length, which remains uncertain; using the latest determination of 0.91–1.14 fm yields an effective mass ratio of 0.84±0.015 and optical potential of −(83±5) MeV at normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors derived an effective Lagrangian for s-wave ηN interactions from the SU(3)L×SU(3)R chiral Lagrangian in the heavy-baryon formalism, keeping terms up to next-to-leading order (order Q²). They combined this with the relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) using the NL3 parameter set for the nucleon system, solving self-consistently for the scalar density and sigma mean-field value. The η-meson effective mass and optical potential were then computed in the mean-field approximation for uniform nuclear matter as functions of nuclear density, with the off-shell parameter κ determined from the ηN scattering length.

## Key numbers

* ηN sigma term: ΣηN = 280 ± 130 MeV, derived from the KN sigma term ΣKN = 380 ± 100 MeV
* ηN scattering length range used: aηN = 0.91–1.14 fm, based on recent experimental analyses by Green et al. (2005) and Arndt et al.
* Off-shell parameter: κ = 0.40 ± 0.08 fm, determined from the central scattering length aηN = 1.02 fm
* Effective mass ratio at normal nuclear density: m*η/mη = 0.84 ± 0.015, using the latest scattering length range
* Optical potential depth at normal nuclear density: Uη = −(83 ± 5) MeV
* Without off-shell terms (κ=0), the effective mass ratio becomes m*η/mη ≈ 0.94 ± 0.03 and optical potential −(32 ± 16) MeV at normal nuclear density

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of η-meson in-medium properties that used only leading-order chiral perturbation theory, such as Waas and Weise (who obtained Uη ≈ −20 MeV) and the chiral unitary approach of Inoue and Oset (Uη ≈ −54 MeV). It also contrasts with the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima et al. (Uη ≈ −60 MeV) and the work of Chiang et al. (Uη ≈ −34 MeV). The authors find that next-to-leading-order terms are essential and produce a much deeper optical potential than previous models when using the larger scattering lengths favored by recent data. The results enable predictions for η-nucleus bound states and guide future experimental searches for such states.
