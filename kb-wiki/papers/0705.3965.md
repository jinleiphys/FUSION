# In-medium nuclear interactions of low-energy hadrons

> Experimental and theoretical developments of the last decade in the study of exotic atoms and some related low-energy reactions are reviewed, in order to provide information on the in-medium hadron-nucleon t matrix over a wide range of densities up to central nuclear densities. In particular, we review pionic deeply bound atomic states and related evidence for partial restoration of chiral symmetry in dense nuclear matter. The case for relatively narrow deeply bound atomic states for antikaons and antiprotons is made, based on the physics of strong nuclear absorption. Recent experimental suggestions for signals of antikaon-nuclear deeply bound states are reviewed, and dynamical models for calculating binding energies, widths and densities of antikaon nuclear states are discussed. Specific features of low-energy in-medium interactions of kaons, antiprotons and of Sigma hyperons are discussed, and suggestions to study experimentally Cascade atoms are reviewed.

# In-medium nuclear interactions of low-energy hadrons

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that in-medium hadron-nucleon interactions at low energy deviate significantly from free-space values, with the density dependence of the effective scattering amplitudes providing information on partial restoration of chiral symmetry for pions and on the depth of the antikaon-nucleus potential; however, the authors note that the experimental situation for deeply bound antikaon-nuclear states "is not settled at the present time" and that the case for such states relies on physics of strong nuclear absorption rather than on the potential-depth issue alone.

## Method

The authors analyze strong-interaction level shifts, widths, and X-ray yields from exotic atoms (π⁻, K⁻, \bar p, Σ⁻, and the unobserved Ξ⁻) across the periodic table by fitting density-dependent optical potentials of the form V_opt = t(ρ)ρ to comprehensive data sets. For pions, the analysis uses a Kisslinger-type potential with s-wave and p-wave terms including two-nucleon absorption terms (ρ²), while for antikaons the analysis incorporates self-consistent coupled-channel calculations that include Pauli blocking and meson-baryon self energies. Neutron density distributions are parameterized using a linear dependence of r_n−r_p on (N−Z)/A, with three shape models (skin, halo, and average) tested in global fits.

## Key numbers

- For pions: the s-wave isoscalar scattering length b₀ is exceptionally small, requiring second-order effects in the construction of the isoscalar s-wave potential term
- For antikaons: the Λ(1405) subthreshold quasibound state lies approximately 27 MeV below the K⁻p threshold with a width of approximately 50 MeV
- For antiprotons: total cross section measurements were made at momenta as low as 40–50 MeV/c on light nuclei and on hydrogen
- For kaons: K⁺ total and reaction cross sections were measured at the AGS on Li, C, Si and Ca at p_lab = 500–700 MeV/c
- For antiproton atoms: data from the PS209 collaboration provide strong-interaction level shifts and widths for two atomic levels for most target nuclei, with two examples where information is available for three levels
- For neutron densities: the linear parameter γ in r_n−r_p = γ(N−Z)/A + δ is close to 1.0 fm from \bar p studies and close to 1.5 fm from RMF calculations, with differences of 0.05–0.10 fm between RMF and RHB values for medium-weight and heavy nuclei

## Context

This review extends the authors' previous work with Chris Batty (Friedman, Gal, Batty, 1997) on strong interaction physics from hadronic atoms, incorporating the discovery of deeply bound pionic atom states at GSI using the (d,³He) reaction on Sn and Pb isotopes, and recent experimental suggestions from KEK and DAΦNE for antikaon-nuclear deeply bound states. The analysis connects to chiral perturbation theory treatments of pionic atoms (Kienle and Yamazaki, 2004; Lutz, 2004) and to unitarized coupled-channel approaches for antikaons that treat the Λ(1405) resonance. The work enables systematic extraction of neutron density distributions from antiprotonic atoms and provides constraints for models of multistrange hypernuclei through the study of Ξ⁻ atoms.
