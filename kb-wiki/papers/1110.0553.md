# Electron Anomalous Magnetic Moment in Basis Light-Front Quantization Approach

> We apply the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach to the Hamiltonian field theory of Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) in free space. We solve for the mass eigenstates corresponding to an electron interacting with a single photon in light-front gauge. Based on the resulting non-perturbative ground state light-front amplitude we evaluate the electron anomalous magnetic moment. The numerical results from extrapolating to the infinite basis limit reproduce the perturbative Schwinger result with relative deviation less than 0.6%. We report significant improvements over previous works including the development of analytic methods for evaluating the vertex matrix elements of QED.

## Key claim

The Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach, when applied to free-space QED with a truncated basis of electron and electron-photon states, reproduces the perturbative Schwinger result for the electron anomalous magnetic moment to within 0.6% relative deviation after extrapolation to the infinite basis limit, but only when the harmonic oscillator basis frequency is chosen within an optimal plateau between 0.02 MeV and 0.5 MeV.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the light-front QED Hamiltonian in a truncated basis containing only single-electron \(|e\rangle\) and electron-photon \(|e\gamma\rangle\) Fock sectors, using a plane-wave basis in the longitudinal direction and a 2D harmonic oscillator basis in the transverse directions. They remove the external trap potential used in prior work, decouple the harmonic oscillator basis frequency from any trap frequency, remove transverse center-of-mass kinetic energy, and implement sector-dependent basis frequencies to match center-of-mass motion across Fock sectors. The interaction vertex matrix elements are evaluated analytically using Talmi-Moshinsky transforms rather than numerical integration, and the anomalous magnetic moment is extracted from the light-front amplitude via the Pauli form factor \(F_2(0)\) rather than from the squared modulus of helicity-flip components.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger result for the electron anomalous magnetic moment is \(\frac{\alpha}{2\pi e^2} = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \approx 0.012665\) (in units where \(a_e/e^2\)).
- Extrapolated BLFQ results at \(\omega = 0.5\) MeV (even \(N_{\text{max}}/2\)) give an intercept of 0.11285, a 0.5% relative deviation from the Schwinger result.
- Extrapolated BLFQ results at \(\omega = 0.5\) MeV (odd \(N_{\text{max}}/2\)) give an intercept of 0.11248, a 0.1% relative deviation.
- Extrapolated BLFQ results at \(\omega = 0.02\) MeV (even \(N_{\text{max}}/2\)) give an intercept of 0.11255, a 0.02% relative deviation.
- Extrapolated BLFQ results at \(\omega = 0.02\) MeV (odd \(N_{\text{max}}/2\)) give an intercept of 0.11280, a 0.5% relative deviation.
- The QED coupling constant \(\alpha\) was reduced by a factor of \(10^7\) to suppress higher-order contributions.
- The largest basis used contains 44,358,561 basis states at \(N_{\text{max}} = 118\).
- The analytic method reduced computation time for matrix elements at \(N_{\text{max}} = 40\) from approximately 7000 cpu-hours to less than 2 cpu-seconds, with all matrix elements achieving relative errors better than 1 part in \(10^7\).

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach of Vary (2009) and Honkanen (2010) from electrons in an external trap to free-space QED, correcting a factor of 2 in the interaction matrix elements and replacing the approximate spin-flip modulus method with the proper Pauli form factor extraction. The results enable future application of BLFQ to QED with physical coupling, inclusion of higher Fock sectors and all QED vertices, and extension to nonperturbative QCD regimes, building on the AdS/QCD approach of de Teramond (2008).
