# Magnetic moments of active and sterile neutrinos

> Since most of the neutrino parameters are well-measured, we illustrate precisely the prediction of the Standard Model, minimally extended to allow massive neutrinos, for the electron neutrino magnetic moment. We elaborate on the effects of light sterile neutrinos on the effective electron neutrino magnetic moment measured at the reactors. We explicitly show that the kinematical effects of the neutrino masses are negligible even for light sterile neutrinos.

## Key claim

The Standard Model, minimally extended to allow massive neutrinos, predicts an electron neutrino magnetic moment that is well below current experimental limits; however, if a light sterile neutrino exists, it could contribute to the effective magnetic moment measured at reactor experiments without affecting the kinematics of neutrino-electron scattering.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for neutrino-electron scattering via the magnetic moment, including finite neutrino mass effects, and show that mass effects are negligible even for sterile neutrino masses. They then compute the effective neutrino magnetic moment measured at a distance L from a reactor source, incorporating oscillations between active and sterile states, and apply the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality to derive bounds on sterile neutrino contributions. The Standard Model prediction is evaluated using measured neutrino mass differences and mixing angles from the Particle Data Group compilation, with results presented for both Dirac and Majorana neutrinos as functions of the smallest neutrino mass and the sum of neutrino masses.

## Key numbers

- GEMMA spectrometer limit: μν < 2.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μB at 90% C.L. with detector at 13.9 m from reactor core
- TEXONO collaboration limit: μν < 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ μB with detector at 28 m from reactor core
- Red-giant branch limit from globular cluster M5: μν < 4.5 × 10⁻¹² μB (95% CL)
- For one sterile neutrino, fit to reactor flux deficits yields |Ue4| ∼ 0.151 and δm²41 ∼ 1.78 eV²
- Standard Model prediction range for Dirac electron neutrino magnetic moment: [3 × 10⁻²¹, 3 × 10⁻¹⁹] μB for minimum neutrino mass range 10⁻⁴ to 1 eV
- Standard Model prediction range for Majorana electron neutrino magnetic moment: [5 × 10⁻²⁵, 8 × 10⁻²³] μB for minimum neutrino mass range 10⁻⁴ to 1 eV
- Sterile neutrino lifetime from radiative decay for GEMMA upper limit: τ > 1.18 × 10¹² years, longer than the age of the Universe (13.8 × 10⁹ years)
- Effective field theory limit for Dirac neutrinos: μν ≲ 8 × 10⁻¹⁵ μB for Λ ∼ 1 TeV and neutrino mass correction up to ∼ 1 eV

## Context

This work extends the Standard Model prediction for neutrino magnetic moments by incorporating the latest measured neutrino parameters from the Particle Data Group (Beringer 2012) and cosmological mass limits from Planck data (Ade 2013). It builds on earlier theoretical work by Fujikawa (1980) on the Standard Model magnetic moment prediction and by Bell et al. (2005, 2006) on effective field theory bounds connecting magnetic moment to neutrino mass. The paper enables future reactor experiments like GEMMA and TEXONO to interpret their measurements in the context of possible sterile neutrino contributions, and shows that observation of μν ≳ 10⁻¹⁵ μB would indicate Majorana neutrinos.
