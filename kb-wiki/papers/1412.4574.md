# Hidden dibaryons in one- and two-pion production in NN collisions

> Processes of one- and two-pion production in $NN$ collisions are considered in connection with excitation of intermediate dibaryon resonances. In particular, relative contributions of the conventional meson-exchange and dibaryon excitation mechanisms in the reaction $pp \to d π^+$ are investigated in detail. Inclusion of the intermediate isovector dibaryon resonances is shown to essentially improve the description of experimental data for this reaction, provided the soft meson-baryon form factors consistent with $πN$ elastic scattering are used. Manifestation of the intermediate isoscalar and isovector dibaryons in the two-pion production processes is also studied. The role of the isovector dibaryon resonances in the reaction $pp \to pp ππ$ is discussed for the first time. An explanation of the observed strong differences between two-pion production cross sections in $pn$ and $pp$ collisions based in part on the analysis of dibaryon structure is suggested.

## Key claim
The description of the one-pion production reaction $pp \to d \pi^+$ is essentially improved by including intermediate isovector dibaryon resonances, but only when using soft meson-baryon form factors (cut-off parameters $\Lambda < 1$ GeV) that are consistent with $\pi N$ elastic scattering data.

## Method
The authors calculated relativistic helicity amplitudes for the conventional one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) and intermediate $N\Delta$ mechanisms for $pp \to d \pi^+$, using a monopole parametrization for the $\pi NN$ and $\pi N\Delta$ vertex form factors. The cut-off parameter for the $\pi N\Delta$ vertex ($\tilde{\Lambda}_* = 0.3$ GeV) was fixed by fitting the $P_{33}$ partial wave of $\pi N$ elastic scattering in an isobar model. They then added contributions from intermediate isovector dibaryon resonances (primarily $\mathcal{D}_{12}(2150)$) and compared the resulting partial and total cross sections to experimental data and partial-wave analyses. The role of isoscalar and isovector dibaryons in two-pion production reactions ($pn \to d \pi\pi$, $pp \to pp \pi\pi$) was also studied.

## Key numbers
- The cut-off parameter for the $\pi N\Delta$ vertex was determined to be $\tilde{\Lambda}_* = 0.3$ GeV, corresponding to a monopole parameter $\Lambda_* = 0.44$ GeV.
- The cut-off parameter for the $\pi NN$ vertex was taken as $\Lambda \simeq \tilde{\Lambda} = 0.7$ GeV, giving a ratio $\Lambda_* / \Lambda \simeq 0.6$.
- The isoscalar dibaryon resonance $\mathcal{D}_{03}(2380)$ has a mass 80 MeV lower than the threshold for simultaneous excitation of two $\Delta$ isobars and a width $\Gamma_{\mathcal{D}_{03}} \simeq 70$ MeV.
- The isovector dibaryon $\mathcal{D}_{12}(2150)$ has a width $\Gamma_{\mathcal{D}_{12}} \simeq 100$–$120$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate on the existence and role of dibaryon resonances in hadronic processes, following the recent experimental confirmation of the isoscalar dibaryon $\mathcal{D}_{03}(2380)$ in two-pion production reactions (Bashkanov et al., 2009; Adlarson et al., 2011-2015). It addresses the long-standing difficulty in identifying isovector dibaryons like $\mathcal{D}_{12}(2150)$, which previous analyses (Shypit et al., 1988; Anisovich et al., 1992) argued against, by showing that a consistent treatment of soft form factors allows a unified description of one- and two-pion production. The results enable future high-precision experiments on one-pion production to clarify the status of isovector dibaryons, similar to how two-pion experiments revealed the isoscalar resonance.
