# Two-body scattering states in Minkowski space and the Nakanishi integral representation onto the null plane

> The Nakanishi perturbative integral representation of the four-dimensional T-matrix is investigated in order to get a workable treatment for scattering states, solutions of the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation, in Minkowski space. The projection onto the null-plane of the four-dimensional inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation plays a key role for devising an equation for the Nakanishi weight function (a real function), as in the homogeneous case that corresponds to bound states and it has been already studied within different frameworks. In this paper, the whole formal development is illustrated in detail and applied to a system, composed by two massive scalars interacting through the exchange of a massive scalar. The explicit expression of the scattering integral equations are also obtained in ladder approximation, and, as simple applications of our formalism, some limiting cases, like the zero-energy limit and the Wick-Cutkosky model in the continuum, are presented.

## Key claim

The Nakanishi perturbative integral representation (PTIR) can be extended from bound states to scattering states of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, provided one projects the four-dimensional inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation onto the null-plane (light-front hyperplane) to obtain an integral equation for a real Nakanishi weight function; the formal development is illustrated for two massive scalars exchanging a massive scalar, but the method's validity is conditional on the convergence of the underlying perturbation series.

## Method

The authors start from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for two scalar particles of equal mass \(m\) and apply the light-front projection technique, which integrates over the \(k^-\) variable to project the four-dimensional amplitude onto the null-plane \(x^+=0\). They express the scattering Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using the Nakanishi integral representation, then derive an inhomogeneous integral equation for the real Nakanishi weight function, analogous to the homogeneous case previously studied for bound states. Explicit expressions for the scattering integral equations are obtained in ladder approximation for the Yukawa model (massive scalar exchange), and the formalism is applied to limiting cases including the zero-energy limit and the Wick-Cutkosky model in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- The two scalar constituents have equal mass \(m\), and the exchanged scalar boson is massive (Yukawa model) or massless (Wick-Cutkosky model).
- The total momentum is \(p = p_1 + p_2\) with total mass \(M = \sqrt{p^2}\); the relative incoming momentum satisfies \(k_i^2 = m^2 - M^2/4 \le 0\) for half-off-shell scattering.
- The free two-particle propagator is \(G_0^{(12)}(k,p) = \frac{i}{(\frac{p}{2}+k)^2 - m^2 + i\epsilon} \cdot \frac{i}{(\frac{p}{2}-k)^2 - m^2 + i\epsilon}\).
- The zero-energy scattering limit and the Wick-Cutkosky model (massless exchanged boson in ladder approximation) are presented as simple applications, but no explicit numerical scattering results (e.g., phase shifts or cross sections) are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi PTIR approach previously applied to bound states by Carbonell and Karmanov (2010) and by Kusaka and Williams (1995) to the continuum (scattering) region of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The method builds on the light-front projection technique developed by Sales and collaborators (2000-2008) and by Frederico (2010), which relates the valence component of the light-front wave function to the full Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The formalism enables future numerical calculations of relativistic scattering observables for two-body systems in Minkowski space without Wick rotation, with potential applications to hadronic processes such as light-meson decays of heavy hadron resonances.
