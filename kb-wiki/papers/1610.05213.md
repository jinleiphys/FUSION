# Pairing in neutron matter: New uncertainty estimates and three-body forces

> We present solutions of the BCS gap equation in the channels ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3P_2-{}^3F_2$ in neutron matter based on nuclear interactions derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT). Our studies are based on a representative set of nonlocal nucleon-nucleon (NN) plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) as well as local and semilocal chiral NN interactions up to N$^2$LO and N$^4$LO, respectively. In particular, we investigate for the first time the impact of subleading 3N forces at N$^3$LO on pairing gaps and also derive uncertainty estimates by taking into account results for pairing gaps at different orders in the chiral expansion. Finally, we discuss different methods for obtaining self-consistent solutions of the gap equation. Besides the widely-used quasi-linear method by Khodel et al. we demonstrate that the modified Broyden method is well applicable and exhibits a robust convergence behavior. In contrast to Khodel's method it is based on a direct iteration of the gap equation without imposing an auxiliary potential and is straightforward to implement.

## Key claim

The paper presents solutions of the BCS gap equation in neutron matter for the 1S0 and 3P2-3F2 pairing channels, showing for the first time the impact of subleading three-nucleon forces at N3LO on pairing gaps, while also deriving uncertainty estimates based on results at different orders in the chiral expansion rather than on parameter variation.

## Method

The authors solved the zero-temperature BCS gap equation in a partial-wave representation using both the quasi-linear method of Khodel et al. and a modified Broyden direct-iteration method, benchmarking the two approaches against each other. They employed nonlocal nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions up to N3LO, as well as local and semilocal chiral NN interactions up to N2LO and N4LO respectively, with three-nucleon forces included at the normal-ordered two-body level. The theoretical uncertainties were estimated using an order-by-order analysis in the chiral expansion based on results at different chiral orders for fixed cutoff values.

## Key numbers

* The saturation density of nuclear matter is n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
* For the N3LO NN potential EM 500 MeV, the two solution methods (Khodel and modified Broyden) agree to within about 10 eV in both the 1S0 and 3P2-3F2 channels
* The breakdown scale Lambda_b is 600 MeV for cutoffs R0 = 0.8, 0.9, 1.0 fm; 500 MeV for R0 = 1.1 fm; and 400 MeV for R0 = 1.2 fm
* The 3N cutoff scale Lambda_3N is taken in the range (2.0-2.5) fm^-1
* For N2LO 3N forces, the low-energy constants are taken in the ranges c1 = -(0.37-0.73) GeV^-1 and c3 = -(2.71-3.38) GeV^-1; for N3LO 3N forces, c1 = -(0.75-1.13) GeV^-1 and c3 = -(4.77-5.51) GeV^-1
* The 1S0 gap results from different orders agree up to Fermi momenta of approximately (0.6-0.8) fm^-1 depending on the regulator for local potentials

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron pairing gaps in uniform matter based on chiral interactions, such as those by Hebeler et al. (2007, 2010), Maurizio et al. (2014), and Srinivas et al. (2016), by incorporating for the first time subleading three-nucleon forces at N3LO and by applying improved uncertainty estimates based on order-by-order convergence in the chiral expansion rather than cutoff variation. The results enable more systematic assessments of theoretical uncertainties in neutron-star pairing gaps, though the authors note that contributions beyond BCS approximation due to screening and vertex corrections are not included in the uncertainties studied here.
