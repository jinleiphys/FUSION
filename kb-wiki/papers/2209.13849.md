# Initial state and approach to equilibrium

> A possible resolution of the early thermalisation puzzle is provided by the notion of far-from-equilibrium attractors which arise due to the specific kinematics of heavy-ion collisions. Attractors appear in a wide variety of dynamical models, and it is plausible that they also occur in QCD. The physical implications of these observations depend on how robust this effect is when typically made symmetry restrictions are relaxed. I briefly review this line of research and its perspectives.

## Key claim

Far-from-equilibrium attractors in heavy-ion collisions arise due to the specific kinematics of ultrarelativistic collisions, not from a need to revise fluid dynamics fundamentals; however, the physical implications depend on how robust this effect is when typically made symmetry restrictions (conformal invariance and suppression of transverse dynamics) are relaxed.

## Method

The paper reviews the concept of far-from-equilibrium attractors in conformal Bjorken flow, analyzing how the pressure anisotropy \(\mathcal{A}(\tau)\) evolves as a function of the dimensionless variable \(w \equiv \tau T\) in various dynamical models. It examines the attractor solution in the Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) hydrodynamic model, where the pressure anisotropy satisfies a first-order ODE with transport coefficients \(C_\eta, C_{\tau\Pi}, C_{\lambda_1}\), and compares this with kinetic theory models using the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and the AMY collision kernel, as well as strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory via AdS/CFT.

## Key numbers

- The attractor solution in the MIS model is regular at \(w=0\) with value \(\mathcal{A}_\star(0) = 6\sqrt{C_\eta/C_{\tau\Pi}}\), and generic solutions approach it as \(\mathcal{A} - \mathcal{A}_\star \sim w^{-4}\) at early times.
- The free-streaming attractor corresponds to \(\mathcal{A}_\star(0) = 3/2\), which via the relation \(\mathcal{E} \sim \tau^{-\beta}\) gives \(\mathcal{A}_\star(0) = 6(1-3\beta/4)\).
- At late times, the asymptotic behavior of any solution is \(\mathcal{A}(w) = 8C_\eta/w + 16C_\eta(C_{\tau\Pi} - C_{\lambda_1})/(3w^2) + O(1/w^3)\), independent of initial conditions up to exponentially-damped corrections.
- Hydrodynamic simulations are initialized at proper times shorter than \(1\) fm following the collision.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on far-from-equilibrium attractors in heavy-ion collisions, building on the observation by Blaizot (2017) that attractors arise from competition between expansion and collisions among plasma constituents. It connects to studies by Kurkela (2019) and Heller (2020) distinguishing expansion-dominated and collision-dominated regimes, and discusses how attractors appear in MIS theory, kinetic theory with RTA and AMY kernels (Almaalol 2020), and strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM via AdS/CFT (Spaliński 2017). The paper identifies open questions about attractor behavior when conformal symmetry is broken (Chattopadhyay 2021, Jaiswal 2021) and when transverse dynamics are included (Kurkela 2019, Ambrus 2021), and suggests machine learning techniques (Heller 2020) as a path forward for identifying attractors in higher-dimensional phase spaces.
