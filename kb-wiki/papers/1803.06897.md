# Generic features of the neutron-proton interaction

> We show that fully aligned neutron-proton pairs play a crucial role in the low-energy spectroscopy of nuclei. with valence nucleons in a high-j orbital. Their dominance is valid in nuclei with valence neutrons and protons in different high-j orbitals as well as in N = Z nuclei, where all nucleons occupy the same orbital. We demonstrate analytically this generic feature of the neutron-proton interaction for a variety of systems with four valence nucleons interacting through realistic, effective forces. The dominance of fully aligned neutron-proton pairs results from the combined effect of (i) angular momentum coupling and (ii) basic properties of the neutron-proton interaction.

# Generic features of the neutron-proton interaction

## Key claim
Fully aligned neutron-proton pairs (FAPs) dominate the low-energy spectroscopy of nuclei when valence neutrons and protons mainly occupy high-j orbitals, and this dominance is not confined to N=Z nuclei but is a widely occurring phenomenon throughout the nuclear chart. The claim is hedged by the condition that the dominance holds only when valence nucleons dominantly occupy high-j orbitals, and that the effect fades in multi-j scenarios.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for nuclei with two neutrons and two valence nucleons (2+2 systems) using a Hamiltonian expressed in a neutron-neutron/proton-proton (νν-ππ) basis, with two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) from realistic interactions (jj45pna), surface delta interactions (SDI), and quadrupole interactions (Qν·Qπ). They diagonalized the Hamiltonian with only a single non-zero component Jνπ of the neutron-proton interaction to isolate the effect of the fully aligned pair, and compared the resulting yrast spectra to full calculations and experimental data for 128Cd, 212Po, 96Cd, and 124Pd.

## Key numbers
- For 128Cd, the occupation probabilities of ν0h11/2 and π0g9/2 in positive-parity yrast states from 0+ to 18+ are ≥10 and ≥8, respectively, supporting the high-j orbital assumption.
- The 0+1 ground state of 128Cd from the complete jj45pna calculation has components: 0.82|0ν0π;0⟩ + 0.52|2ν2π;0⟩ + ...
- The 2+1 state of 128Cd from the complete jj45pna calculation has components: 0.62|2ν0π;2⟩ + 0.56|0ν2π;2⟩ - 0.36|2ν2π;2⟩ + ...
- With only the FAP component (Vνπ10) active, the 0+1 state is 0.89|0ν0π;0⟩ + 0.43|2ν2π;0⟩ + ... and the 2+1 state is 0.66|2ν0π;2⟩ + 0.60|0ν2π;2⟩ - 0.34|2ν2π;2⟩ + ...
- For 212Po, the FAP state has JνπFAP = 9 (from ν1g9/2 and π0h9/2 orbitals).
- For 124Pd (four neutrons and four protons), the calculation with only the FAP interaction produces results close to the full calculation and to experimental data.

## Context
This work extends the FAP dominance conjecture, originally proposed by Cederwall et al. (2010) for N=Z nuclei like 92Pd, to non-self-conjugate nuclei throughout the nuclear chart. Prior studies by Qi et al. (2011), Xu et al. (2012), Coraggio et al. (2012), and others had debated whether FAP dominance survives in multi-j scenarios. The present paper demonstrates analytically that the dominance arises from angular momentum coupling combined with basic properties of the neutron-proton interaction, and shows it holds for 128Cd, 212Po, and 124Pd. This enables future work on symmetries of a "pairing-plus-FAP" model as an alternative to the traditional pairing-plus-quadrupole model of Elliott (1958).
