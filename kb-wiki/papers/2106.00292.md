# Systematic calculations of reactions with exotic and stable nuclei to establish a unified theoretical approach

> We report on systematical optical model (OM) and continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations applied to describe the elastic scattering angular distributions of exotic and stable nuclei projectiles on heavy targets. Our optical potential (OP) is composed of the nuclear microscopic double folding São Paulo potential (SPP), derived from the nonlocal nature of the interaction, and the Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential, derived from the semiclassical theory of Coulomb excitation. The OP is compared to the trivial equivalent local potential (TELP), extracted from CDCC calculations. The OM and CDCC predictions corroborate each other and account for important differences in the nuclei reaction mechanisms, which are directly related to their structural properties. Thus, OM and CDCC establish a common basis for analyzing or even predicting exotic and stable nuclei reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified theoretical approach combining the São Paulo potential (SPP) and Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential within the optical model (OM) can describe elastic scattering angular distributions for both exotic and stable nuclei, with the CDP contribution becoming increasingly important as projectile binding energy decreases. The OM and continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) predictions corroborate each other, but the CDCC calculations show that nuclear dipolar effects play a major role for some systems (⁶He and ⁹Be) while Coulomb dipolar effects dominate for others (¹¹Li and ¹¹Be).

## Method

The authors performed systematic optical model calculations using a potential composed of the nuclear microscopic double folding São Paulo potential (SPP) and the Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential derived from semiclassical Coulomb excitation theory. They compared these OM results to continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations, extracting trivial equivalent local potentials (TELP) from the CDCC calculations to compare with the OM potentials. The calculations were applied to elastic scattering angular distributions of ⁶He, ⁹,¹¹Li, ⁹,¹¹Be, and ¹²C projectiles on ¹²⁰Sn, ¹⁹⁷Au, and ²⁰⁸Pb targets at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

- Break-up thresholds (εb) for projectiles studied: ¹¹Li (-0.396 MeV), ¹¹Be (-0.502 MeV), ⁶He (-0.975 MeV), ⁹Be (-1.573 MeV), ⁹Li (-4.062 MeV), ¹²C (-7.367 MeV)
- Standard SPP normalization factors: N_R = 1.0 and N_i = 0.78, obtained from systematic analysis of stable tightly bound nuclei
- For ¹¹Li+²⁰⁸Pb at E_LAB = 29.8 MeV, the CDP imaginary potential and TELP converge to similar strengths at distances greater than 16 fm, and fully converge for distances greater than 20 fm
- For ¹¹Li+²⁰⁸Pb, absorption mostly takes place at large interacting distances (R > 15 fm)
- ⁶He two-neutron separation energy: S_2n = 975.45(5) keV
- ¹¹Li two-neutron separation energy: S_2n = 396.15(65) keV
- ¹¹Be one-neutron separation energy: S_1n = 501.64(25) keV
- ⁹Be one-neutron separation energy: S_1n = 1664.54(8) keV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of Alvarez et al. (2020) (PhysRevC.100.064602) which analyzed ⁴,⁶He, ⁶,⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B, ¹⁶,¹⁸O projectiles on ¹²⁰Sn, finding that lower binding energy corresponds to higher absorptive imaginary strength. The present paper extends this approach to additional nuclei (⁹,¹¹Li, ¹¹Be, ¹²C) and explicitly includes projectile binding energy in the optical model, while also performing CDCC calculations with no free parameters. The results enable a common theoretical basis for analyzing and predicting reactions of both exotic and stable nuclei, with implications for astrophysical applications where optical potentials are used as input for theoretical calculations.
