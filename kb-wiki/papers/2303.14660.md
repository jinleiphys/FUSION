# Heavy-Flavour Jets in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Reconstructed jets initiated from heavy quarks provide a powerful tool to probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and to explore the mass hierarchy of jet quenching. In this article, we review the recent theoretical progresses on heavy-flavour jets in high-energy nuclear collisions at the RHIC and LHC. We focus on the yields and substructures of charm and bottom quark jets with jet quenching effect, such as the nuclear modification factors, transverse momentum imbalance, angular correlation, radial profiles, fragmentation functions, the "dead-cone" effect, etc.

## Key claim
Heavy-flavour jets serve as a calibrated probe of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) because the mass of the initiating charm or bottom quark modifies the pattern of parton energy loss, revealing a mass hierarchy in jet quenching. The review argues that while the "dead-cone" effect is qualitatively established, quantitative discrimination between different energy-loss models requires simultaneous measurement of multiple jet substructure observables.

## Method
The authors review theoretical calculations of heavy-flavour jet observables in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au collisions at RHIC, comparing them to experimental data. They focus on the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of charm and bottom jets, the transverse momentum imbalance of di-jet systems, angular correlations between the jet and its heavy-flavour decay products, radial profiles of energy density inside the jet, and fragmentation functions. The review also covers the implementation of the dead-cone effect in Monte Carlo event generators and analytic resummation frameworks.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for D-meson-tagged jets in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV is suppressed to about 0.2-0.3 at p_T ~ 10 GeV/c, indicating strong energy loss.
- For b-jets in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the R_AA is measured to be approximately 0.4-0.5 at p_T ~ 100 GeV/c, showing less suppression than light-flavour jets.
- The momentum imbalance (x_J) of b-bbar di-jets in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV is shifted to lower values compared to pp collisions, with a mean x_J of about 0.6 for the most central events.
- The angular separation Delta R between the heavy-flavour hadron and its associated jet axis is predicted to be smaller for b-jets than for c-jets due to the larger b-quark mass, with typical values of Delta R ~ 0.1-0.2 for b-jets at high p_T.

## Context
This review extends earlier work on inclusive jet quenching (e.g., by the JET Collaboration and the JETSCAPE Collaboration) by focusing specifically on the mass-dependent effects of heavy quarks. It synthesises theoretical progress made since the first LHC heavy-ion runs, enabling future comparisons between models such as the CUJET, LBT, and MARTINI frameworks. The review identifies open questions, including the role of medium response (recoil) on heavy-flavour jet shapes and the need for precise measurements of the dead-cone angle in heavy-ion collisions.
