# Canonical statistical hadronization with local baryon conservation for higher-order cumulants

> We study higher-order cumulants of the conserved baryon number at the LHC within the canonical ensemble with local baryon conservation. We generalize the density correlations approach of [Phys. Rev. C 110, L061902 (2024)] to incorporate the effect of Gaussian local conservation in spatial rapidity space in cumulants up to 6th order. Gaussian local conservation improves upon the commonly employed $V_c$ approach, yielding comparable predictions at midrapidity, but marked differences for larger rapidity acceptances. Our coordinate-space results are in exact agreement with the diffusion master equation approach for all cumulant ratios up to $κ_6/κ_2$. Using the blast-wave model to apply kinematic cuts, we obtain predictions for net-proton cumulants in O--O and Pb--Pb collisions at the LHC that establish an ideal hadron gas baseline. We find that local baryon conservation alone can drive $κ_6/κ_2$ to small or even negative values in restricted acceptance, a behavior often associated with chiral criticality. The conservation baseline must therefore be carefully accounted for when interpreting upcoming LHC measurements.

# Canonical statistical hadronization with local baryon conservation for higher-order cumulants

## Key claim

Local baryon conservation alone, modeled via Gaussian kernels in spatial rapidity, can drive the sixth-order net-baryon cumulant ratio κ₆/κ₂ to small or even negative values in restricted acceptance at LHC energies, a behavior often attributed to chiral criticality; this conservation baseline must therefore be carefully accounted for when interpreting upcoming LHC measurements of net-proton cumulants.

## Method

The authors generalize the density correlator approach of Vovchenko (2024) to incorporate Gaussian local baryon conservation in spatial rapidity space for cumulants up to sixth order, using reflecting boundary conditions implemented via image sums over Jacobi theta functions. They derive closed-form acceptance formulas for net-baryon and species-decomposed cumulants up to sixth order in an ideal hadron gas at zero baryon chemical potential, then apply the blast-wave model with kinematic cuts to obtain predictions for net-proton cumulants in O-O and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian local conservation kernel width for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV is constrained to ση ≈ 0.78 rapidity units (from prior work on second-order cumulants).
- The full spatial rapidity range is V = 2ηmax with ηmax ≈ 5.1, giving a total system size of approximately 10.2 rapidity units.
- The sixth-order cumulant ratio κ₆/κ₂ can become negative in restricted acceptance due solely to local baryon conservation, with the exact value depending on acceptance size and kernel width.
- The coordinate-space results for all cumulant ratios up to κ₆/κ₂ show exact agreement with the diffusion master equation approach of Sakaida (2014).

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian local conservation formalism of Vovchenko (2024), which was restricted to second-order cumulants, to higher orders up to sixth order. It improves upon the commonly employed Vc approach (box-like correlation in rapidity) by using Gaussian kernels with reflecting boundary conditions, which yield comparable predictions at midrapidity but marked differences for larger rapidity acceptances. The results establish a non-critical ideal hadron gas baseline for upcoming LHC measurements of net-proton cumulants in O-O and Pb-Pb collisions, against which signatures of chiral criticality from the QCD crossover transition can be identified.
