# Magic numbers for shape coexistence

> The increasing deformation in atomic nuclei leads to the change of the classical magic numbers (2,8,20,28,50,82..) which dictate the arrangement of nucleons in complete shells. The magic numbers of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator (2,8,20,40,70...) emerge at deformations around epsilon=0.6. At lower deformations the two sets of magic numbers antagonize, leading to shape coexistence. A quantitative investigation is performed using the usual Nilsson model wave functions and the recently introduced proxy-SU(3) scheme.

# Magic numbers for shape coexistence

## Key claim
At intermediate deformations (between zero and approximately ε=0.6), the classical shell-model magic numbers (2,8,20,28,50,82...) and the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator magic numbers (2,8,20,40,70...) produce energy gaps of comparable size, and this competition between the two sets of magic numbers provides a quantitative explanation for the empirically observed phenomenon of shape coexistence in atomic nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed Nilsson model calculations using asymptotic wave functions for the 28-50 and 50-82 major shells, comparing the standard shell-model treatment with the proxy-SU(3) approximation scheme. Energy eigenvalues of the Nilsson Hamiltonian were computed as functions of the deformation parameter ε up to ε=1, and the resulting shell gaps were analyzed for both the full spin-orbit case and the pure three-dimensional harmonic oscillator case.

## Key numbers
- The three-dimensional harmonic oscillator magic numbers at ε=0.6 (prolate axis ratio ω⊥/ωz = 2:1) are: 2, 4, 10, 16, 28, 40, 60, 80, 110...
- For the 28-50 shell, shell gaps appear at nucleon numbers 30, 34, and 40 with increasing deformation
- For the 50-82 shell, shell gaps appear at nucleon numbers 56, 62, and 70 with increasing deformation
- 40 and 70 are the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator magic numbers at zero deformation
- The classical shell-model magic numbers at zero deformation are 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82

## Context
This work extends the earlier finding by Sugawara-Tanabe et al. that three-dimensional harmonic oscillator magic numbers emerge at ε=0.6, and provides a quantitative framework for understanding shape coexistence regions previously mapped in review articles. The proxy-SU(3) scheme, introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2017), is validated as a good approximation to the full Nilsson model at large deformations. The results enable predictions of shape coexistence regions, with extensions to the 82-126 shell and comparisons of other magic number sets promised in future work.
