# Proof of Factorization of Heavy Quarkonium Production in Non-Equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC

> Recently we have proved the NRQCD factorization in heavy quarkonium production at high energy colliders at all orders in coupling constant. In this paper we extend this to non-equilibrium QCD and prove the NRQCD factorization in heavy quarkonium production at all orders in coupling constant in non-equilibrium QCD. This proof is necessary to study heavy quarkonium production from quark gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proves that NRQCD factorization for heavy quarkonium production holds at all orders in coupling constant in non-equilibrium QCD, extending a previous proof for equilibrium QCD. This is necessary for studying quarkonium production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, where the system is known to be formed in non-equilibrium, though it may or may not subsequently thermalize.

## Method

The proof uses the closed-time path integral formalism of non-equilibrium QCD combined with the background field method in the background field gauge fixing. Infrared divergences are analyzed by showing that the gauge field generated by a light-like eikonal current is a pure gauge field, and that uncanceled infrared divergences from color octet heavy quark-antiquark pairs cancel with gauge links in the S-wave color octet non-perturbative matrix elements. The calculation employs finite gauge transformations rather than infinitesimal ones, and the path integral formulation is used to study non-perturbative matrix elements at all orders in coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- RHIC Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 200\) GeV and LHC Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 2.76\) TeV (run-1) and \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 5.02\) TeV (run-2) are the facilities considered for quark-gluon plasma production.
- The temperature of quark-gluon plasma is \(\gsim 200\) MeV.
- The hadronization time scale in QCD is very small, \(\sim 10^{-24}\) seconds.
- PHENIX collaboration data at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV at RHIC and ALICE collaboration data at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV at LHC for pp collisions are explained by NRQCD color octet mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proof of NRQCD factorization in equilibrium QCD (Nayak, 2015) to non-equilibrium QCD, which is required because the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC is formed in a non-equilibrium state due to momentum anisotropy from nuclei traveling near the speed of light. The proof builds on the background field method approach of Tucci (1984) for QED factorization, but uses the background field gauge fixing (Abbott, 1981) rather than the background Feynman gauge fixing, and extends the author's prior work on factorization for quark fragmentation functions (Nayak, 2013), gluon fragmentation functions (Nayak, 2014), and \(\chi_{cJ}\) production in color singlet mechanism (Nayak, 2014) in non-equilibrium QCD. This enables future calculations of heavy quarkonium production cross sections from non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.
