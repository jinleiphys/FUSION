# Spin-Isospin Response Functions and the Effects of the $Δ$-Hole Configurations in Finite Nuclei

> Effects of the delta-isobar $(Δ)$ mixing on the spin-isospin response functions in finite nuclei are studied in the quasi-elastic region. A method to calculate the response function for a finite system composed of nucleon $(N)$ and $Δ$ is formulated in a ring approximation. It is designed to treat the $Δ$-related Landau-Migdal parameters, $g'_{NΔ}$ and $g'_{ΔΔ}$, and the nucleon parameter $g'_{NN}$, independently, so that the universality ansatz, $g'_{NN}=g'_{NΔ}=g'_{ΔΔ}$, is removed. We calculated the isovector spin-longitudinal and -transverse response functions, $R_{L}$ and $R_{T}$, with and without the $Δ$-mixing. Inclusion of $Δ$ enhances $R_{L}$ but reduces $R_{T}$ for ordinary interactions. Dependence of $R_{L,T}$ on $g'_{NN}$ and $g'_{NΔ}$ is investigated. Decomposition into the process-decomposed response functions, $R^{[NN]}$, $R^{[NΔ]}$ and $R^{[ΔΔ]}$, is very elucidative to see the $Δ$ effects, which are found to be mainly governed by $R^{[NΔ]}$ and sensitive to $g'_{NΔ}$. The isovector spin-transverse response function $R^{(e,e')}_{T}$ obtained by $(e,e')$ is calculated by various effective interactions and compared to each other as well as experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the inclusion of delta-isobar (Δ) configurations in finite-nuclei calculations enhances the isovector spin-longitudinal response function \(R_L\) but reduces the isovector spin-transverse response function \(R_T\) for ordinary interactions, and that these effects are mainly governed by the mixed nucleon-delta component \(R^{[N\Delta]}\) which is sensitive to the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'_{N\Delta}\), thereby removing the universality ansatz \(g'_{NN}=g'_{N\Delta}=g'_{\Delta\Delta}\).

## Method

The authors formulated a continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) for finite nuclei composed of nucleons (N) and delta-isobars (Δ), using a ring approximation to calculate the spin-isospin polarization propagator. They employed the (\(\pi + \rho + g'\)) model for the effective interaction, treating the Landau-Migdal parameters \(g'_{NN}\), \(g'_{N\Delta}\), and \(g'_{\Delta\Delta}\) independently rather than assuming universality. The response functions were decomposed into process-decomposed components \(R^{[NN]}\), \(R^{[N\Delta]}\), and \(R^{[\Delta\Delta]}\) to isolate the Δ effects, and calculations were performed for doubly (sub-)closed shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The transferred momentum region studied is \(q \leq 3 \;{\rm fm}^{-1}\), where the Landau-Migdal parameters are treated as independent of \(q\).
- The ratio of coupling constants used is \(f_{\Delta} = 2.0 f_{N}\) for the \(\pi N\Delta\) to \(\pi NN\) coupling.
- Phenomenological analysis cited gives \(g'_{NN} \approx 0.6 \sim 0.7\) (Meyer-ter-Vehn 1981, Toki 1981).
- \(G\)-matrix calculations cited suggest \(g'_{NN} \approx 0.5\) and \(g'_{N\Delta} \approx 0.4\) (Dickhoff 1981, 1983; Cheon 1984; Nakayama 1984; Czerski 1986; Dickhoff 1987).
- A recent estimation by Brown et al. (1993) gives \(g'_{NN} \approx 1.0\), \(g'_{N\Delta} \approx 0.33\), and \(g'_{\Delta\Delta} \approx 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-nucleus RPA approach of Alberico et al. (1985, 1986), Shigehara (1988), and Ichimura (1989) by explicitly including Δ-hole configurations without the universality ansatz for the Landau-Migdal parameters. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the standard nuclear model prediction that the ratio \(R_L/R_T\) should be much larger than unity at low \(\omega\) and experimental observations from LAMPF (Carey 1984, Rees 1986, McClelland 1992, Chen 1993, Taddeucci 1994) that the ratio is close to or less than unity. The formalism enables future studies of the dependence of response functions on the independent \(g'\) parameters and allows comparison with electron scattering data for the isovector spin-transverse response function \(R^{(e,e')}_T\).
