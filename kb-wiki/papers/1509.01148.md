# Jet Quenching and Gluon to Hadron Fragmentation Function in Non-Equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC

> Theoretical understanding of the observed jet quenching measurements at RHIC and LHC is challenging in QCD because it requires understanding of parton to hadron fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD. In this paper, by using closed-time path integral formalism, we derive the gauge invariant definition of the gluon to hadron fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD which is consistent with factorization theorem in non-equilibrium QCD from first principles.

## Key claim

The paper derives a gauge-invariant definition of the gluon-to-hadron fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD using the closed-time path integral formalism, which is consistent with the factorization theorem in non-equilibrium QCD from first principles. The derivation explicitly accounts for an arbitrary non-equilibrium (non-isotropic) gluon distribution function \(f_g(\vec{k})\) at the initial time, and the special case \(f_g(\vec{k}) = 1/(e^{k_0/T} - 1)\) recovers the finite-temperature equilibrium result.

## Method

The author employs the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path (CTP) integral formalism for non-equilibrium QCD, starting from the generating functional with two time branches and an initial density of states \(\rho\). The gauge-invariant gluon fragmentation function is obtained by first deriving a gauge non-invariant expression from the scalar gluon case, then proving factorization of infrared divergences using the background field method and non-abelian gauge links in the adjoint representation of SU(3). The proof shows that the infrared divergences from soft gluon exchanges with a light-like parton exponentiate into a path-ordered exponential (Wilson line), and the final expression is shown to be invariant under gauge transformations of the background field combined with homogeneous transformations of the quantum gluon field.

## Key numbers

* The initial energy density at RHIC (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV) corresponds to a total energy of \(\sim 40\) TeV deposited in a small volume, with temperature \(\gsim 200\) MeV.
* The initial energy density at LHC (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV) corresponds to a total energy of \(\sim 574\) TeV, and at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV the total energy is \(\sim 1150\) TeV.
* The typical hadronization time in QCD is \(\sim 10^{-24}\) seconds.
* The temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is \(\gsim 3.2 \times 10^{12}\) K (\(\gsim 200\) MeV), about a million times larger than the sun's temperature (\(\sim 1.56 \times 10^{7}\) K).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivation of the gauge-invariant quark-to-hadron fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD (Nayak, 2015) to the gluon case, which is essential for understanding jet quenching at RHIC and LHC. The result enables the formulation of DGLAP-like evolution equations in non-equilibrium QCD for heavy-ion collisions, where the partonic cross sections and fragmentation functions must both be defined with respect to the non-equilibrium initial state \(|in\rangle\) rather than the vacuum state \(|0\rangle\). This contrasts with approaches that use vacuum fragmentation functions in medium-modified parton showers, and it provides a first-principles framework for factorized hadron production formulas in AA collisions consistent with the factorization theorem in non-equilibrium QCD.
