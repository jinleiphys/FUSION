# Third flow component as QGP signal

> A review of earlier fluid dynamical calculations with QGP show a softening of the directed flow while with hadronic matter this effect is absent. The effect shows up in the reaction plane as enhanced emission which is orthogonal to the directed flow. Thus, it is not shadowed by the deflected projectile and target. As both of these flow components are in the reaction plane these form an enhanced 'elliptic flow' pattern. Recent experimental data at 11 AGeV and above show the same softening, hinting at QGP formation.

## Key claim

A "third flow component" – an enhanced emission orthogonal to the directed transverse flow within the reaction plane – appears in fluid dynamical calculations only when a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) equation of state (EoS) is used, and this softening of the directed flow is absent in calculations with purely hadronic matter; the authors argue that recent experimental data at 11 AGeV and above show the same softening, hinting at QGP formation, but note that the effect is also present in some string model calculations where it may arise from shadowing rather than a soft EoS.

## Method

The authors review earlier fluid dynamical (FD) calculations that included a QGP EoS, quantifying the "softening" of the directed transverse flow by measuring the relative deviation S(y) between the actual P_x(y) curve and the steepest straight line through the center-of-mass point. They compare these FD results with string model calculations (Venus, RQMD, QGSM) and with experimental data from Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies from 2 to 158 AGeV, using both the P_x vs. y and the v_1 vs. y analyses.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au at 5 AGeV, b=3 fm (soft point): softening S = 160 ± 30% for QGP EoS, 0 ± 20% for hadronic matter (RPM95)
- Au+Au at 11.6 AGeV, b=5 fm: S = 100 ± 10% for QGP, 0% for hadronic matter (HPG93)
- Au+Au at 11.6 AGeV, b=0-0.5 b_max: S = 180 ± 25% for QGP, 30 ± 30% for hadronic matter (BCL94)
- Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV, b=4 fm, 3.8 fm/c: S = 80 ± 10% for QGP, 50 ± 45% for hadronic matter (ASC91)
- Venus 4.12 Pb+Pb 158 AGeV: S = 110 ± 15% (v_1 analysis) and 134 ± 15% (P_x analysis)
- RQMD 2.3 Au+Au 11.6 AGeV: S = 32 ± 20% (P_x analysis)
- QGSM Au+Au 11.6 AGeV b=3 fm: S = 0 ± 10%
- Experimental data: Au+Au 11 AGeV protons: S = 30 ± 20%; Pb+Pb 158 AGeV semicentral: S = 60 ± 50% (P_x) and 110 ± 60% (v_1)

## Context

This work extends earlier fluid dynamical predictions of a "soft point" in the EoS (Holme, RPM95, Shur95) and the observation that out-of-plane squeeze-out decreases with plasma formation (HPG93). The authors point out that the third flow component was present but not discussed in prior FD calculations (ASC91, HPG93, BCL94, RPM95, BDM97). The paper enables a new experimental signature for QGP formation that is distinct from the standard directed flow and elliptic flow analyses, and it calls for more detailed experiments with finer impact parameter resolution to reconstruct the EoS from flow data.
