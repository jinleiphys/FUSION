# The induced surface tension contribution for the equation of state of neutron stars

> We apply a novel equation of state (EoS) that includes the surface tension contribution induced by interparticle interaction and asymmetry between neutrons and protons, to the study of neutron star (NS) properties. This elaborated EoS is obtained from the virial expansion applied to multicomponent particle mixtures with hard core repulsion. The considered model is in full concordance with all the known properties of normal nuclear matter, provides a high-quality description of the proton flow constraints, hadron multiplicities created during the nuclear-nuclear collision experiments, and equally is consistent with astrophysical data coming from NS observations. The analysis suggests that the best model parameterization gives the incompressibility factor $K_{0}$, symmetry energy $J$, and symmetry energy slope $L$ at normal nuclear density equal to $200$ MeV, $30$ MeV, and $113.28-114.91$ MeV, respectively. The mass-radius relations found for NSs computed with this EoS are consistent with astrophysical observations.

# The induced surface tension contribution for the equation of state of neutron stars

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the induced surface tension (IST) contribution from interparticle repulsion, together with asymmetry between neutrons and protons, produces an equation of state that is consistent with all known nuclear matter properties, heavy-ion collision data, and astrophysical observations, but only for a narrow range of the parameter α (1.2 to 1.3), with the best fit at α = 1.245, and only when the nucleon hard core radius is between 0.3 and 0.5 fm.

## Method

The authors generalize the IST equation of state (EoS) from Sagun et al. (2017) to include asymmetry between neutrons and protons, using a multicomponent virial expansion with hard core repulsion for a mixture of neutrons, protons, and electrons at zero temperature. The model parameters (hard core radius r, mean field parameters κ, C_d², U_0, and symmetry energy parameters A_sym, B_sym) are tuned to simultaneously reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties, the proton flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions, and the mass-radius relation of neutron stars from astrophysical observations. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is solved using the resulting EoS with a polytropic crust (γ = 4/3) matched at a transition density of about 0.09 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility factor K₀ = 200 MeV at normal nuclear density for the best parameterization (set A)
- Symmetry energy J = 30 MeV at normal nuclear density for the best parameterization
- Symmetry energy slope L = 114.91 MeV (set A) or 113.28 MeV (set B) at normal nuclear density
- Maximum neutron star mass M_max = 2.229 M_⊙ for set A and 2.189 M_⊙ for set B
- Nucleon hard core radius r = 0.492 fm (set A) or 0.484 fm (set B)
- IST EoS remains causal (speed of sound less than speed of light) up to n_B ≈ 0.83 fm⁻³ (about 5.2 times normal nuclear density), compared to only 0.4 fm⁻³ for the Van der Waals EoS
- Proton fraction at central density (n_B = 0.83 fm⁻³) is 12.5% for set A and 11.5% for set B, both exceeding the critical threshold of 11% required for direct Urca cooling
- Parameter κ = 0.263 (set A) or 0.26 (set B), consistent with the range 0.1–0.3 required by the flow constraint

## Context

This work extends the generalized IST EoS developed by Sagun et al. (2017) and Ivanytskyi et al. (2017) to include neutron-proton asymmetry for neutron star applications. It contrasts with earlier Van der Waals-based approaches (Andronic et al., 2006; Oliinychenko et al., 2012) that have limited causality range, and with the APR4 EoS (Akmal et al., 1998) and SLy EoS (Douchin and Haensel, 2001) used as references. The results enable future modeling of proto-neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae by generalizing the IST approach to finite temperature, and can be extended to arbitrarily many particle species as done in Bugaev et al. (2018).
