# Asymptotics of the 3j and 9j Coefficients

> In this work we present the details of calculations we previously performed for the large j behavior of certain 3j and coefficients. We compare exact and asymptotic expressions.

## Key claim

The paper derives asymptotic expressions for specific 3j and 9j coefficients in the limit where the total angular momentum I is close to its maximum value (I = I_max - 2n with n much smaller than j), showing that the coefficients follow a power-law behavior in j, in contrast to the exponential behavior obtained when I is fixed and j is increased.

## Method

The authors start from exact algebraic formulas for the 3j coefficient (Eq. 13 of Kleszyk and Zamick) and the unitary 9j coefficient (Eq. 11 of Kleszyk and Zamick), then apply Stirling's approximation to factorials in the limit of large j. They separate the contributions into terms involving -N_i, ln(sqrt(2πN_i)), and various logarithmic expansions, carefully handling cancellations between leading-order terms that require next-order corrections. The asymptotic results are compared with exact numerical values for half-integer j values from 9/2 to 9999/2 and for n values from 0 to 20.

## Key numbers

- For the 3j coefficient with n=0 and j=9999/2: exact value 0.000446679154, approximate value 0.000446655420, percent error 0.00531331215
- For the 3j coefficient with n=1 and j=9999/2: exact value -0.000315869604, approximate value -0.000315833077, percent error 0.0115641443
- For the unitary 9j coefficient with n=0 and j=9999/2: exact value 0.499993749, approximate value 0.500000000, percent error 0.00125027349
- For the unitary 9j coefficient with n=2 and j=9999/2: exact value 3.63251097e-9, approximate value 3.63164818e-9, percent error 0.0237519144
- Asymptotic scaling: 3j coefficient goes as 1/j^(3/4) in the limit n << j
- Asymptotic scaling: unitary 9j coefficient goes as 1/j^n in the limit n << j
- For fixed I and large j, the unitary 9j coefficient shows exponential behavior proportional to sqrt(6π) j^(3/2) e^(-4 ln2 j)

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic analysis of Clebsch-Gordan and 3j coefficients previously performed by Kleszyk and Zamick (2013) and builds on the foundational Racah algebra of angular momentum coupling. The authors note that the asymptotic formula of Reinsch and Morehead (2000) for Clebsch-Gordan coefficients fails for the n=0 case (I = I_max) because their expression involves a factor β that vanishes, whereas the present formulas remain valid. The results are motivated by applications to maximum-j pairing in nuclei, as discussed by Escuderos (2011).
