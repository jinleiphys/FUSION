# Pygmy resonances: what's in a name?

> The centroid, width and percentage of energy weighted sum rule of dipole resonances can be strongly affected by dynamical fluctuations and static deformations of the nuclear surface, deformations and fluctuations which, in turn, depend on pairing, and thus on Cooper pairs. Because of angular momentum conservation, such insight is restricted, to lowest order, to deformations of quadrupole and monopole type. The latter being closely connected with the neutron (excess) skin and thus with soft dipole modes. From the values $(N-Z)/A \approx 0.18$, 0.21, and 0.45 for the nuclei $^{122}$Sn, $^{208}$Pb, and $^{11}$Li, it is expected that the latter system, which is weakly bound by pairing effects (spatially extended single Cooper pair and odd proton acting as spectator), constitutes an attractive laboratory to study the properties of soft $E1$--modes and thus of isospin nuclear deformation. From the calculation of the full dipole response function in QRPA, discretizing the continuum in a spherical box of radius of 40 fm, one finds a GDR with centroid $E_x \approx 24$ MeV, width $Γ\approx$ 11 MeV and carrying 90\% of the EWSR, and a low--lying collective resonance characterized by $E_X = 0.75$ MeV, $Γ= 0.5$ MeV and $6.2\%$ EWSR The wave function of the latter resonance is built out of about fifteen components (both protons and neutrons), typical of a collective mode. The transition densities indicate this soft $E1$--mode to be generated by surface density oscillation of the neutron skin ($Δr_{np} \approx 1.71$ fm) relative to an approximately isospin--saturated core. Through a detailed study of the full dipole response of $^{11}$Li we will draw a comparison between the soft $E1$--mode of this halo nucleus and the PDR of heavy stable nuclei, pointing to the physical similarities and also to the basic differences.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the soft E1-mode of the halo nucleus ¹¹Li and the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) of heavy stable nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb share physical similarities as collective oscillations of the neutron skin against an isospin-saturated core, but differ fundamentally in that the ¹¹Li mode arises from a bootstrap mechanism where the exchange of this dipole mode between halo neutrons provides the pairing correlation needed to bind the single Cooper pair, making the mode symbiotic with incipient nuclear superfluidity.

## Method

The authors performed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations of the full dipole response function for ¹¹Li, discretizing the continuum in a spherical box of radius 40 fm. They analyzed the transition densities and wavefunction components of the resulting low-lying resonance to characterize its collective nature, and compared these results with experimental data from the ¹¹Li(d,d') reaction and with prior calculations for the PDR in ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹²²Sn.

## Key numbers

- ¹¹Li: Giant dipole resonance (GDR) with centroid E_x ≈ 24 MeV, width Γ ≈ 11 MeV, carrying 90% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR)
- ¹¹Li: Low-lying collective resonance with E_X = 0.75 MeV, Γ = 0.5 MeV, carrying 6.2% of the EWSR
- Neutron skin thickness Δr_np ≈ 1.71 fm for ¹¹Li
- Neutron excess parameters (N-Z)/A: 0.18 for ¹²²Sn, 0.21 for ²⁰⁸Pb, 0.45 for ¹¹Li
- ¹¹Li two-neutron separation energy S_2n ≈ 369 keV
- Experimental dipole resonance centroid from Kanungo (2015): E_X = 1.03 ± 0.03 MeV, width (FWHM) = 0.51 ± 0.11 MeV
- ¹¹Li matter radius R(¹¹Li) ≈ 4.58 ± 0.13 fm, compared to R(⁹Li) ≈ 2.5 fm
- Reduction factor of bare pairing interaction in ¹¹Li relative to ⁹Li: (2.5/4.6)³ ≈ 0.2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Barranco (2001), Brida (2006), Lenske (2001), Tanihata (2008), and Potel (2010) for the many-body description of the ¹¹Li ground state, particularly the role of a single Cooper pair and the soft dipole mode. The authors draw direct comparisons with the PDR observed in ²⁰⁸Pb by Ryezayeva (2002) and Lane (1971), and in ¹²²Sn by Tsoneva (2008), arguing that the ¹¹Li soft E1-mode provides unique insight into the mechanism of pygmy resonances because pairing effects are essential for binding. The results enable future studies of the bootstrap mechanism between pairing and dipole collectivity, and suggest that the calculated Δr_np value represents a quantitative estimate of the neutron skin of ¹¹Li.
