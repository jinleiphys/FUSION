# Applications of Skyrme energy-density functional to fusion reactions for synthesis of superheavy nuclei

> The Skyrme energy-density functional approach has been extended to study the massive heavy-ion fusion reactions. Based on the potential barrier obtained and the parameterized barrier distribution the fusion (capture) excitation functions of a lot of heavy-ion fusion reactions are studied systematically. The average deviations of fusion cross sections at energies near and above the barriers from experimental data are less than 0.05 for 92% of 76 fusion reactions with $Z_1Z_2<1200$. For the massive fusion reactions, for example, the $^{238}$U-induced reactions and $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb the capture excitation functions have been reproduced remarkable well. The influence of structure effects in the reaction partners on the capture cross sections are studied with our parameterized barrier distribution. Through comparing the reactions induced by double-magic nucleus $^{48}$Ca and by $^{32}$S and $^{35}$Cl, the 'threshold-like' behavior in the capture excitation function for $^{48}$Ca induced reactions is explored and an optimal balance between the capture cross section and the excitation energy of the compound nucleus is studied. Finally, the fusion reactions with $^{36}$S, $^{37}$Cl, $^{48}$Ca and $^{50}$Ti bombarding on $^{248}$Cm, $^{247,249}$Bk, $^{250,252,254}$Cf and $^{252,254}$Es, and as well as the reactions lead to the same compound nucleus with Z=120 and N=182 are studied further. The calculation results for these reactions are useful for searching for the optimal fusion configuration and suitable incident energy in the synthesis of superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The Skyrme energy-density functional approach, combined with a parameterized barrier distribution, can systematically describe fusion (capture) excitation functions for heavy-ion reactions from light to very massive systems, including those leading to superheavy nuclei, with average deviations less than 0.05 for 92% of 76 reactions with Z1Z2 < 1200. However, for reactions induced by double-magic nuclei like 48Ca, the capture cross sections at sub-barrier energies are suppressed due to strong shell effects, requiring a structure-dependent factor gamma in the barrier distribution to reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated nucleus-nucleus interaction potentials using the Skyrme energy-density functional with the SkM* force and the semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF2) approach, assuming spherical symmetric Fermi functions for neutron and proton density distributions. They then parameterized a barrier distribution as a superposition of two Gaussian functions to account for multi-dimensional barrier effects, with a reducing factor f=0.926 to mimic barrier lowering from couplings to internal degrees of freedom. The fusion (capture) excitation functions were obtained by integrating this barrier distribution with Wong's formula, where the structure effects were incorporated through a gamma factor that depends on the Q-value difference and neutron shell closure of the reaction partners.

## Key numbers

- Average deviations (chi^2_log) less than 0.05 for 92% of 76 fusion reactions with Z1Z2 < 1200, compared to 43% for the Woods-Saxon potential approach of Hagino (1999)
- For 48Ca+208Pb, the gamma factor was 9.5 (from equation 14) to reproduce the suppressed sub-barrier capture cross sections, versus gamma=1 for systems without shell effects
- For 48Ca+248Cm, the capture cross section is about 80 mb at an incident energy of about 198 MeV, with compound nucleus excitation energy E*_CN = 31 MeV
- For 32S+254Cf and 35Cl+254Es at the same excitation energy of 31 MeV, capture cross sections are less than 0.1 mb
- Barrier heights B0 for 48Ca-induced reactions range from 183.17 MeV (48Ca+208Pb) to 214.29 MeV (48Ca+252Es)
- Most probable barrier energies B_m.p. for 48Ca-induced reactions range from 173.03 MeV (48Ca+208Pb) to 202.39 MeV (48Ca+252Es)
- The reducing factor f = 0.926 was used for all systems, the same as in Liu (2005)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme energy-density functional approach of Liu (2005) from light and intermediate-heavy fusion reactions to very massive systems leading to superheavy nuclei, including 238U-induced reactions and 48Ca-induced reactions. It addresses the failure of Wong's formula and the Woods-Saxon potential approach of Hagino (1999) for heavy systems, where potential parameters need readjustment. The results provide systematic predictions for optimal fusion configurations and suitable incident energies for synthesizing superheavy nuclei with Z=120 and N=182, and explain the "threshold-like" behavior in capture excitation functions for 48Ca-induced reactions observed in experiments by Oganessian (2000) and others.
