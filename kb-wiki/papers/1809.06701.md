# Evaluation of CP-violation in HfF$^+$

> CP violation effects produced by the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM), electron electric dipole moment (EDM) and scalar$-$pseudoscalar nucleus$-$electron neutral current (SP) interaction in $^{177}$Hf$^{19}$F$^+$ and $^{179}$Hf$^{19}$F$^+$ are calculated. The role of the hyperfine interaction is investigated. It is shown that the MQM shift can be distinguished from the electron EDM and SP ones due to the implicit dependence of MQM shift on the hyperfine sublevel. The MQM effect is expressed in terms of the proton (EDM), QCD vacuum angle $θ$ and quark chromo-EDMs.

## Key claim

The nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) shift in \(^{177}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) and \(^{179}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) can be distinguished from the electron EDM and scalar-pseudoscalar (SP) interaction shifts because the MQM shift depends on the hyperfine sublevel \(F_1\), whereas the other two shifts do not; however, this distinction holds only when the hyperfine interaction and mixing of rotational levels by the external electric field are fully taken into account, which significantly modifies the shifts from the simple analytical formulas.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled cluster calculations (CCSD(T)) within the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian to compute the electronic matrix elements needed for the hyperfine structure constants and the T,P-odd interaction parameters \(E_{\text{eff}}\), \(W_{\text{SP}}\), and \(W_M\) for the \(H^3\Delta_1\) state of HfF\(^+\). They then diagonalized the full molecular Hamiltonian (including electronic, rotational, hyperfine, and external electric field terms) on a basis of electronic-rotational-nuclear spin wavefunctions to obtain the energy shifts of each hyperfine sublevel of the \(J=1\) rotational state for both isotopes. The electric quadrupole hyperfine constant \(eQq_2\) was estimated from the experimentally measured body-fixed \(g\)-factor \(G_{\parallel}=0.011768\) and the spin-orbit admixture weight \(w=0.014\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{177}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\): the magnetic dipole hyperfine constant \(A^{\text{Hf}}_{\parallel} = -1429\) MHz; the electric quadrupole constants \(eQq_0 = -2100\) MHz and \(eQq_2 = 110\) MHz.
* For \(^{179}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\): the magnetic dipole hyperfine constant \(A^{\text{Hf}}_{\parallel} = 898\) MHz; the electric quadrupole constants \(eQq_0 = -2400\) MHz and \(eQq_2 = 125\) MHz.
* The calculated parameter \(W_M = 0.494 \times 10^{33}\) Hz/(e cm\(^2\)).
* Current limits on the proton EDM (\(|d_p| < 2.0 \times 10^{-25}\) e cm), QCD vacuum angle (\(|\theta| < 1.5 \times 10^{-10}\)), and quark chromo-EDM difference (\(|{\tilde d_u} - {\tilde d_d}| < 5.7 \times 10^{-27}\) e cm) correspond to MQM energy shifts less than 6 \(\mu\)Hz, 8 \(\mu\)Hz, and 18 \(\mu\)Hz for \(^{177}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\), and less than 3 \(\mu\)Hz, 5 \(\mu\)Hz, and 11 \(\mu\)Hz for \(^{179}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\), respectively.
* At an electric field of 20 V/cm, the numerical MQM shift \(\delta_M\) (in units of \(M W_M\)) for \(^{177}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) is 0.08651 for \(F_1=9/2\), 0.09268 for \(F_1=7/2\), and 0.07048 for \(F_1=5/2\); for \(^{179}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) it is 0.02961 for \(F_1=11/2\), 0.02195 for \(F_1=9/2\), and 0.11499 for \(F_1=7/2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for MQM-induced CP violation from earlier studies on \(^{229}\)ThO (Skripnikov 2014), \(^{181}\)TaN (Skripnikov 2015), and \(^{229}\)ThF\(^+\) (Skripnikov 2015) by including the previously neglected mixing of rotational and hyperfine levels by the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole hyperfine interactions and the external electric field. The calculations are directly motivated by the Cornell/Ye group's limit on the electron EDM using \(^{180}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) (Cornell 2017) and their proposed experiment to measure nuclear MQM in \(^{177}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\) and \(^{179}\)Hf\(^{19}\)F\(^+\). The results enable extraction of limits on the proton and neutron EDMs, the QCD vacuum angle \(\theta\), and quark chromo-EDMs from future measurements of the hyperfine-dependent energy shifts in these ions.
