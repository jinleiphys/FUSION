# Emission angle dependence of HBT radii: Theoretical background and interpretation

> The Wigner function formalism which relates source size parameters to experimental ``HBT radii'' extracted from two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations is generalized to azimuthally deformed and longitudinally tilted sources. It is explained how this can be used to complement anisotropic flow measurements with relevant space-time information on the source.

## Key claim

The Wigner function formalism for extracting source size parameters from two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations is generalized to azimuthally deformed and longitudinally tilted sources, but the reconstruction of the source from HBT data necessarily relies on additional assumptions due to the mass-shell constraint, which makes the problem underdetermined.

## Method

The author derives the relations between the six HBT radius parameters (in the out-side-long coordinate system) and the ten independent components of the spatial correlation tensor of the emission function, including both explicit azimuthal angle dependence from the coordinate rotation and implicit dependence from the source deformation. Symmetry constraints for collisions between spherical nuclei at midrapidity are used to expand both the HBT radii and the source correlation tensor components into Fourier series, with only even or odd sine or cosine terms allowed depending on the specific radius parameter. The resulting equations relate the measurable Fourier coefficients of the HBT radii to the Fourier coefficients of the source correlation tensor components.

## Key numbers

- For weak or no collective expansion, the implicit azimuthal dependence of the spatial correlation tensor vanishes with the 4th power of the transverse flow velocity \(v_T/c\).
- Up to harmonic order \(n=2\), there are 9 measurable Fourier coefficients of the HBT radii which depend on 19 source amplitudes at midrapidity.
- Increasing the harmonic order \(n\) by 2 yields 6 additional measured amplitudes depending on 10 additional source amplitudes.
- The Wiedemann sum rule is given as \(R_{o,2}^2 - R_{s,2}^2 + 2 R_{os,2}^2 = 0\).
- Nyquist's theorem limits the number of extractable harmonics to \(n \leq n_{\rm bin}\), where \(n_{\rm bin}\) is the number of equally spaced azimuthal angle bins.

## Context

This work extends the HBT formalism previously reviewed by Wiedemann and Heinz (1999) for azimuthally symmetric sources to the deformed sources created in non-central heavy-ion collisions, motivated by recent data on transverse flow anisotropies at RHIC interpreted as evidence for early thermalization by Kolb and Heinz (2003). The formalism enables the extraction of space-time information about source deformations and collective flow that complements single-particle anisotropic flow measurements, with applications to RHIC data reported in the following article by M. Lisa. The author notes that hydrodynamic simulations by Heinz and Kolb (2002) show that at RHIC and LHC energies, emission at non-zero transverse momentum is strongly surface dominated, and that a longitudinal source tilt previously found in Au+Au collisions at the AGS by the E895 collaboration yielded important information on the kinetic pion production mechanism.
