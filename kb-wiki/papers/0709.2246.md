# Chiral Phase Transition beyond Mean Field Approximation

> Based on the analogy between the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of chiral symmetry breaking and the BCS theory of superconductivity, we investigate the effect of $\bar q q$ pair fluctuations on the chiral phase transition. We include uncondensed $\bar q q$ pairs at finite temperature and chemical potential in a self-consistent T-matrix formalism, the so-called $G_0 G$ scheme. The pair fluctuations reduce significantly the critical temperature and make quarks massive above the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The inclusion of uncondensed \(\bar q q\) pair fluctuations via a self-consistent T-matrix (the \(G_0 G\) scheme) reduces the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition compared to the mean-field result, and produces a non-zero quark mass (a "pseudogap") above the critical temperature, but the approximation used to obtain this result is not formally valid above \(T_c\) and is expected to give only qualitatively correct behavior for temperatures not much higher than \(T_c\).

## Method

The authors start from the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and formulate the mean-field theory in a T-matrix formalism. They then include the effect of non-condensed \(\bar q q\) pairs at finite temperature and chemical potential by adding a "pseudogap" contribution to the quark self-energy, using the BEC condition \(t_{\text{pg}}^{-1}(0)=0\) in the symmetry-breaking phase. The resulting coupled equations for the order parameter \(m_{\text{sc}}\) and the pseudogap \(m_{\text{pg}}\) are solved numerically using a pole approximation for the pair propagator, with a three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) to regularize the NJL model.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum parameters used are: pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 94\) MeV, constituent quark mass \(m = 312\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 653\) MeV, and coupling \(g/4 = 5.01\) (GeV)\(^{-2}\) (corresponding to \(\eta = 0.3\)).
* At zero chemical potential and \(\eta = 0.3\) with \(N_c = 3\), the critical temperature including pair fluctuations is \(T_c = 146\) MeV, compared to the mean-field value \(T_c^{\text{mf}} = 190\) MeV.
* At \(T_c = 146\) MeV, the effective quark mass is about 80% of its vacuum value (i.e., approximately 250 MeV).
* The tricritical point shifts from \((T, \mu) = (78, 285)\) MeV in mean-field approximation to \((45, 300)\) MeV when pair fluctuations are included.

## Context

This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover theory to the NJL model of chiral symmetry breaking, following the \(G_0 G\) scheme previously applied to color superconductivity by He (2007) and to non-relativistic Fermi gases by earlier authors (e.g., BCSBEC3, G0G1, G0G2). The results are consistent with those obtained from the non-linear sigma model approach to the NJL model (qqbar, qqbar2, 2007). The finding that quarks retain a large thermal mass above \(T_c\) provides a possible explanation for the failure of lattice QCD thermodynamic quantities to reach the Stefan-Boltzmann limit even at high temperature (SB, 2007).
