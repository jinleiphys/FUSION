# Three-body continuum discretization in a basis of transformed harmonic oscillator states

> The inclusion of the continuum in the study of weakly-bound three-body systems is discussed. A transformed harmonic oscillator basis is introduced to provide an appropriate discrete and finite basis for treating the continuum part of the spectrum. As examples of the application of the method the strength functions corresponding to several operators that couple the ground state to the continuum are investigated, for 6He, and compared with previous calculations. It is found that the energy moments of these distributions are accurately reproduced with a small basis set.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis as a discrete and finite representation of the three-body continuum, and demonstrates that energy moments of strength functions for \(^6\)He are accurately reproduced with a small basis set, though the method requires careful construction of local scale transformations for channels not present in the ground state wave function.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for \(^6\)He (treated as an inert \(\alpha\) core plus two valence neutrons) in hyperspherical coordinates using the Faddeev formalism. A local scale transformation (LST) is derived from the ground state hyperradial wave function for each channel, mapping it to a harmonic oscillator ground state; the inverse transformation then generates a complete orthonormal THO basis. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a truncated THO basis to obtain pseudostates, and strength functions for monopole (\(\rho^2\)), dipole (E1), and quadrupole (E2) operators are computed from the ground state to excited states with \(J^\pi = 0^+, 1^-, 2^+\).

## Key numbers

* Ground state binding energy of \(^6\)He: 0.954882 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental value 0.973 MeV.
* Root-mean-square point nucleon matter radius of \(^6\)He: 2.557 fm (assuming alpha-particle rms matter radius 1.47 fm).
* Ground state expectation value \(\langle \rho^2 \rangle_B = 30.62\) fm\(^2\) (converged for \(n_b \ge 2\)).
* Total B(E1) strength (states up to 10 MeV): 1.320 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (sum rule value 1.500 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\)); complete sum gives 1.498 e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
* Total B(E2) strength (states up to 10 MeV): 8.41 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (sum rule value 9.99 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\)); complete sum gives 9.59 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
* Energy-weighted mean \(\langle E \rangle\) for monopole operator \(\rho^2\): 3.00 MeV (converged for \(n_b \ge 4\)).
* Energy-weighted mean \(\langle E \rangle\) for B(E1): 4.35 MeV; for B(E2): 3.58 MeV (converged for \(n_b \ge 4\)).

## Context

This work extends the THO formalism previously applied to two-body continuum problems (Peres and collaborators, 2001) to three-body Borromean systems, specifically \(^6\)He. The method provides an alternative to discretization using Gauss-Laguerre functions (Zhukov et al., 1993) or Gaussian bases (Matsumoto et al., 2003) for three-body continuum calculations. The THO basis is intended as input for reaction theories such as Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) for three-body projectiles, with scattering applications promised in a forthcoming paper.
