# Dynamics of Light Antiquarks in the Proton

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the recent data from the E866 experiment at Fermilab on Drell-Yan production in pD and pp collisions, which indicates a non-trivial x-dependence for the asymmetry between u-bar and d-bar quark distributions in the proton. The relatively fast decrease of the asymmetry at large x suggests the important role played by the chiral structure of the nucleon, in particular the pi-N and pi-Delta components of the nucleon wave function. At small x the data require an additional non-chiral component, which may be attributed to the Pauli exclusion principle as first suggested by Field and Feynman.

## Key claim

The E866 Drell-Yan data reveal a non-trivial x-dependence of the \(\bar d/\bar u\) asymmetry that cannot be fully explained by pion cloud models alone; a quantitative description requires a delicate balance between chiral (meson cloud) contributions and a non-chiral component attributed to the Pauli exclusion principle, with the best fit obtained when both mechanisms play a role.

## Method

The authors analyzed Drell-Yan cross section ratios \(\sigma^{pD}/2\sigma^{pp}\) from the E866 experiment, extracting \(\bar d/\bar u\) and \(\bar d - \bar u\) over \(0.02 < x < 0.345\). They computed pion cloud contributions using light-cone infinite-momentum-frame convolution formulas with dipole form factors for \(\pi N\) and \(\pi\Delta\) components, and parameterized the Pauli exclusion principle contribution as \((1-x)^n\) with \(n \geq 10\). The total sea sum \(\bar d + \bar u\) was taken from the CTEQ4 parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The integrated asymmetry from E866: \(\Delta^{\rm E866} = \int_0^1 dx (\bar d - \bar u) = 0.100 \pm 0.018\), compared to the NMC value \(\Delta^{\rm NMC} = 0.148 \pm 0.039\).
- The \(\bar d/\bar u\) asymmetry peaks at \(x \sim 0.15\) and drops rapidly to unity by \(x \sim 0.3\), with a large-x falloff approximately like \((1-x)^n\) with \(n \sim 10\).
- The \(\pi N\) component with cutoff \(\Lambda_{\pi N} = 1.5\) GeV gives average multiplicity \(\langle n \rangle_{\pi N} = 26\%\); with \(\Lambda_{\pi N} = 1\) GeV gives 13\%.
- The \(\pi\Delta\) component with \(\Lambda_{\pi\Delta} = 1.3\) GeV gives \(\langle n \rangle_{\pi\Delta} = 11\%\).
- The combined pionic plus Pauli contribution yields \(\Delta = \Delta^{\pi} + \Delta^{\rm Pauli} \approx 0.12\), with the Pauli normalization \(\Delta^{\rm Pauli} \approx 7\%\) and exponent \(n = 14\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the nucleon's flavor asymmetry beyond the first-moment information from the New Muon Collaboration (NMC) and the single-\(x\) measurement from NA51 at CERN. The authors reconcile the E866 data with earlier pion cloud models by Thomas (1983) and the Pauli exclusion principle suggestion of Field and Feynman (1977), finding that neither mechanism alone suffices. The results enable refined constraints on non-perturbative sea dynamics, including the relative hardness of \(\pi N N\) versus \(\pi N \Delta\) form factors, and motivate further measurements of the pion structure function at small \(x\) and the total antiquark distribution at large \(x\).
