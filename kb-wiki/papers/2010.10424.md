# Medium evolution of a static quark-antiquark pair in the large $N_{c}$ limit

> We study the transitions between the different color states of a static quark-antiquark pair, singlet and octet, in a thermal medium. This is done non-perturbatively exploiting the infinite mass limit of QCD. This study is interesting because it can be used for future developments within the framework of Effective Field Theories (EFTs) and because it can be combined with other techniques, like lattice QCD or AdS/CFT, to gain non-perturbative information about the evolution of quarkonium in a medium. We also study the obtained expressions in the large $N_{c}$ limit. This allows us to learn lessons that are useful to simplify phenomenological models of quarkonium in a plasma.

## Key claim

The paper derives non-perturbative expressions for the conditional probabilities of a static quark-antiquark pair to transition between color singlet and octet states in a thermal medium, and then uses the large \(N_{c}\) limit to show that heavy quarks in an octet state evolve as uncorrelated particles and that the octet-to-singlet decay rate is suppressed by a power of \(1/N_{c}^{2}\), though the derivation in the static limit is valid for any relation between the particles' typical energy in the medium and the separation between the heavy quarks, with no assumption about the relation between time scales.

## Method

The author starts from non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) in the infinite mass limit and defines interpolating fields for the singlet and octet states of a static quark-antiquark pair, including Wilson lines to maintain gauge invariance. Using the Fierz identity and field redefinitions that remove the interaction of the heavy quarks with the gluon field, the singlet and octet fields at time \(t\) are expressed as linear combinations of the fields at an earlier time \(t_{0}\) with coefficients given by Wilson-loop-like operators. The conditional probabilities for transitions between color states are then written as traces of these operators over the light-quark and gluon density matrix, and the large \(N_{c}\) limit is applied to these expressions to extract the leading scaling behavior.

## Key numbers

* The octet-to-singlet transition probability is suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_{c}^{2}\) relative to the leading contributions, as shown by the scaling of the graphical representations in the large \(N_{c}\) limit with \(g^{2}N_{c}\sim 1\).
* The singlet-to-singlet transition probability scales as \(1\) in terms of \(N_{c}\), with any diagram where a gluon connects the two Wilson loops suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_{c}^{2}\).
* The octet-to-octet transition probability involves a prefactor of \(4/(N_{c}^{2}-1)\) in the graphical representation, while the octet-to-singlet transition involves a prefactor of \(2/[N_{c}(N_{c}^{2}-1)]\).

## Context

This work extends the open quantum systems approach to quarkonium in a medium, which has been developed perturbatively by Akamatsu (2012, 2014), Blaizot (2015, 2017, 2018), and Yao (2018), and under the assumption that thermal scales are much smaller than \(1/r\) by Brambilla (2016, 2017). The static limit used here has previously been employed by Brambilla (2017) and Blaizot (2018) to clarify features of the open quantum systems framework. The results enable future non-perturbative developments of effective field theories (EFTs) for quarkonium at finite temperature, particularly in the regime where \(1/r\sim T\), and can be combined with lattice QCD or AdS/CFT computations to gain non-perturbative information about quarkonium evolution in a plasma.
