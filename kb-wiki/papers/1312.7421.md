# Non-yrast spectra of odd-A nuclei in a model of coherent quadrupole-octupole motion

> The model of coherent quadrupole and octupole motion (CQOM) is applied to describe non-yrast split parity-doublet spectra in odd-mass nuclei. The yrast levels are described as low-energy rotation-vibration modes coupled to the ground single-particle (s.p.) state, while the non-yrast parity-doublet structures are obtained as higher-energy rotation-vibration modes coupled to excited s.p. states. It is shown that the extended model scheme describes both the yrast and non-yrast quasi parity-doublet spectra and the related B(E1) and B(E2) transition rates in different regions of heavy odd-A nuclei. The involvement of the reflection-asymmetric deformed shell model to describe the single-particle motion and the Coriolis interaction on a deeper level is discussed.

## Key claim

The CQOM model can describe both yrast and non-yrast quasi parity-doublet spectra in odd-mass nuclei by coupling higher-energy rotation-vibration modes (with radial phonon number n=1) to excited single-particle states, but the description is phenomenological and the model's use of the lowest angular-phonon numbers (k=1,2) provides adequate interpretation even though allowing higher k-values yields better agreement with data.

## Method

The authors extended the Coherent Quadrupole-Octupole Motion (CQOM) model to odd-mass nuclei by coupling quadrupole-octupole rotation-vibration modes to single-particle states. The yrast bands were described using n=0 radial phonons with k=1 or 2 angular phonons coupled to the ground single-particle state, while non-yrast bands were obtained using n=1 radial phonons coupled to excited single-particle states. The model Hamiltonian included a decoupling factor for K=1/2 bands, treated as an adjustable parameter, and the energy spectrum was given by an analytic expression E_nk(I,K,πa) = ħω[2n+1+√(k²+bX(I,K,πa))]. Calculations were performed for five odd-mass nuclei (¹⁵¹Pm, ¹⁵⁷Gd, ²²³Ra, ²³⁹Np, ²⁴³Am) with simultaneous fitting to experimental energy levels and available B(E1) and B(E2) transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Total rms deviations between theoretical and experimental levels: 23 keV for ²²³Ra (lowest), 48 keV for ¹⁵¹Pm (highest), with all nuclei below 50 keV
- For ²²³Ra yrast band: decoupling factor a₀ = -0.12 (same value as in prior work by the authors)
- For ¹⁵¹Pm non-yrast band: decoupling factor a₁ = 2.58
- For ²³⁹Np non-yrast band: decoupling factor a₁ = 1.34
- When allowing higher angular-phonon numbers, improved rms values were obtained: ²²³Ra with k₀^(-)=4, k₁^(-)=6 gave rms_total=7 keV; ²³⁹Np with k₀^(-)=4, k₁^(-)=2 gave rms_total=15 keV; ²⁴³Am with k₀^(-)=4, k₁^(-)=6 gave rms_total=18 keV
- B(E1) transition for ¹⁵¹Pm: 5/2⁻₀ → 5/2⁺₀ = 0.0011 W.u. (theory) vs 0.0014(2) W.u. (experiment)
- B(E1) transition for ²³⁹Np: 5/2⁻₀ → 5/2⁺₀ = 2.10×10⁻⁴ W.u. (theory) vs 2.05×10⁻⁴(11) W.u. (experiment)
- B(E2) transition for ²⁴³Am: 7/2⁻₀ → 5/2⁻₀ = 574 W.u. (theory) vs 574(9) W.u. (experiment)

## Context

This work extends the CQOM model previously applied to even-even nuclei by the same group (Minkov et al., 2012) and to yrast spectra of odd-mass nuclei (Minkov et al., earlier work). It addresses the limited interpretation of higher-energy non-yrast parts of quadrupole-octupole spectra in odd-mass nuclei, which had been relatively well studied only at low energies by various microscopic and collective approaches (Leander et al., 1982; Buck et al., 2008). The results provide a phenomenological basis for future microscopic treatments using a reflection-asymmetric deformed shell model to describe single-particle motion and Coriolis interaction, with work in that direction reported as in progress (Minkov et al., 2013).
