# Hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment Workshop. $(g-2)_μ$: Quo vadis? Workshop. Mini proceedings

> We present the mini-proceedings of the workshops Hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment: strategies for improvements of the accuracy of the theoretical prediction and $(g-2)_μ$: Quo vadis?, both held in Mainz from April 1$^{\rm rst}$ to 5$^{\rm th}$ and from April 7$^{\rm th}$ to 10$^{\rm th}$, 2014, respectively.

## Key claim

The workshops aimed to develop strategies for improving the theoretical prediction of the muon anomalous magnetic moment to match the expected precision of upcoming experiments at Fermilab and J-PARC, with the central challenge being the reduction of uncertainties in the hadronic vacuum polarization and hadronic light-by-light contributions, which each carry an uncertainty of order 4 × 10⁻¹⁰.

## Method

The workshops combined plenary talks and four working groups (Lattice QCD, Experimental input, Hadronic models, and Others) to address the hadronic contributions to the muon g-2. The working groups focused on reducing the hadronic vacuum polarization error through new cross-section measurements at various e⁺e⁻ colliders, exploiting low-energy effective theories with experimental data for the hadronic light-by-light calculation, and improving lattice QCD calculations of both hadronic vacuum polarization and hadronic light-by-light contributions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of the muon anomalous magnetic moment is a_μ^EXP = 116 592 08.9(6.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ (Bennett et al., 2006).
- The hadronic vacuum polarization and hadronic light-by-light contributions each carry an uncertainty of order 4 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The discrepancy between measured and predicted values for a_μ ranges between 3 and 4 σ depending on estimation method.
- The contribution of annihilation channels from threshold up to 1.05 GeV represents more than 80% of the total hadronic vacuum polarization.
- A new estimate of the hadronic vacuum polarization integrated up to 1.05 GeV improved the uncertainty by a factor of about 2, yielding a g-2 discrepancy in the range (4 to 4.5) σ (Benayoun et al.).
- The pion-loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light was given a new preliminary result: a_μ^{LbL π-loop} = (-2.0 ± 0.5) × 10⁻¹⁰ (Bijnens and Relefors).
- The two-pion low-energy contribution to a_μ from 0.30 GeV to 0.63 GeV was determined as a_μ^{ππ, LO} [0.30 GeV, 0.63 GeV] = (132.673 ± 0.866) × 10⁻¹⁰ (Caprini et al.).
- The charm-quark contribution to a_μ was calculated as a_μ^{HAD}|_c = 14.41(1) × 10⁻¹⁰ and the bottom-quark contribution as a_μ^{HAD}|_b = 0.29(1) × 10⁻¹⁰ (Dominguez et al.).

## Context

This workshop series extended prior work on the muon g-2 discrepancy, including the earlier workshop "Constraining the Hadronic Contributions to the Muon Anomalous Magnetic Moment" (Masjuan et al., 2013) and the "Light Meson Dynamics Workshop" (Bijnens et al., 2014). The results enable improved theoretical predictions needed to confront the next generation of muon g-2 experiments at Fermilab and J-PARC, with new dispersive approaches to hadronic light-by-light (Colangelo et al., 2014) and improved lattice QCD calculations aiming for 1% accuracy in the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution.
