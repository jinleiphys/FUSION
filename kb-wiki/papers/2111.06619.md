# Range correction in the weak-binding relation for unstable states

> The compositeness is defined as the weight of the hadronic molecule in the hadron wave function. We can determine the internal structure of the weak-binding system without any specific models from the compositeness. In order to estimate the compositeness of the system with a large effective range, we introduce the range correction to Weinberg's weak-binding relation by modifying the correction terms. We study the applicability of the weak-binding relations by the numerical calculation and show that the improved relation can be applied to a larger parameter region compared with the previous one.

# Range correction in the weak-binding relation for unstable states

## Key claim
The authors propose that the weak-binding relation for estimating compositeness of hadronic molecules can be extended to systems with a large effective range by redefining the interaction range parameter \(R_{\rm typ}\) as the maximum of the intrinsic interaction range \(R_{\rm int}\) and the effective range scale \(R_{\rm eff}\), rather than using only \(R_{\rm int}\) as in previous work. The improved relation is shown to be valid in a larger parameter region than the previous one, but only when the effective range is included in the uncertainty estimate.

## Method
The authors study the single-channel effective range model with a zero-range interaction (cutoff \(\Lambda \to \infty\)) and a finite interaction range (\(\Lambda < \infty\)), where the exact compositeness \(X_{\rm exact}=1\) is known by definition. They compute the scattering length \(a_0\) and binding-energy length scale \(R = 1/\sqrt{2\mu B}\) from the model, then estimate the compositeness \(X\) using the weak-binding relation with uncertainty \(\xi = R_{\rm typ}/R\). They compare two definitions of \(R_{\rm typ}\): the previous one (\(R_{\rm typ}=R_{\rm int}\)) and the improved one (\(R_{\rm typ} = \max\{R_{\rm int}, R_{\rm eff}\}\) with \(R_{\rm eff}=|r_e|\)), and map the parameter region where the validity condition (\(X_{\rm exact}\) lies within the uncertainty band) is satisfied.

## Key numbers
- The effective range model in the zero-range limit yields the relation \(a_0 = R \frac{2r_e/R}{1-(r_e/R-1)^2} = R\left\{1+\mathcal{O}\left(\left|\frac{r_e}{R}\right|\right)\right\}\), showing deviation from \(a_0=R\) due to nonzero effective range \(r_e\).
- At fixed \(R_{\rm int}=0.1a_0\), the validity condition using \(R_{\rm typ}=R_{\rm int}\) fails for \(|r_e/a_0| \gtrsim 0.1\), while using \(R_{\rm typ}=|r_e|\) it holds in the entire plotted region.
- The applicable region in the \(|r_e/a_0|\)-\(R_{\rm int}/a_0\) plane is larger for the improved relation: below the solid line both relations apply, but above the solid line only the improved relation (with \(R_{\rm typ}=|r_e|\)) satisfies the validity condition.

## Context
This work extends Weinberg's weak-binding relation (Weinberg, 1965) and its later formulation by Kamiya et al. (2015, 2016) for estimating compositeness of weakly bound hadronic molecules. The previous approach used only the intrinsic interaction range \(R_{\rm int}\) as the correction scale, which fails for systems with a large effective range such as \(\Lambda_c(2595)\) in the charm sector. The improved definition of \(R_{\rm typ}\) as the maximum of \(R_{\rm int}\) and the effective range scale \(R_{\rm eff}\) resolves this contradiction and enables application to a wider class of exotic hadron candidates. The authors plan to extend the range correction to unstable states with finite decay width as a next step.
