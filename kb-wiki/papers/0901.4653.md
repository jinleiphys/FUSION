# Dynamics of anisotropic plasma at the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss the evolution of anisotropic boost-invariant quark-gluon plasma possibly created at the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our considerations are based on the recently proposed formalism that is an extension of the relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics. We analyze i) the pure partonic system described by the anisotropic phase-space distribution function and ii) the system of partons interacting with the local magnetic fields. The second analysis is a simplified attempt to include the effects of color fields on the particle dynamics. Our model results are discussed in the context of early thermalization and isotropization. Under general assumptions of the particle, energy, and momentum conservations we show that for large evolution times the ratio of the longitudinal and transverse pressures of the pure partonic system tends to zero. Hence, the system with the initial momentum distribution elongated along the beam axis always passes through the isotropic stage where the transverse and longitudinal pressures are equal. The inclusion of the magnetic field in this case gives negative contribution to the longitudinal pressure, hence the transient stage when the total longitudinal and transverse pressures become equal may be reached earlier, depending on the strength of the field.

## Key claim

For a pure partonic system described by an anisotropic phase-space distribution function, the ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressures tends asymptotically to zero at large evolution times, meaning the system always passes through an isotropic stage where the two pressures are equal; including local magnetic fields lowers the longitudinal pressure and can make this transient isotropic stage occur earlier, depending on field strength.

## Method

The authors extend a previously proposed formalism for anisotropic relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2008) by introducing a microscopic interpretation based on momentum-anisotropic phase-space distribution functions with two separate scales (λ⊥ and λ∥). They compute the moments of these distributions to derive the pressure relaxation function R(x), then analyze the time evolution under boost-invariant conditions using conservation of particle number and energy-momentum. They also develop a simplified anisotropic magnetohydrodynamic model where partons interact with local magnetic fields aligned along the beam axis, treating this as a crude analog for color field effects.

## Key numbers

- For the Boltzmann-like anisotropic distribution f₁, the two pressures become equal when x = (λ⊥/λ∥)² = 1.
- For the factorized anisotropic distribution f₂, the equality of pressures occurs at x ≈ 0.7.
- In the limit x ≪ 1 (λ⊥ ≪ λ∥), the relaxation function R₁(x) ≈ (3g₀/2π²)[x⁻¹/³ + ½(ln4 − lnx)x²/³] and R₂(x) ≈ (g₀/2π²)[x⁻¹/³ + ½(6ln2 − 8 − 3lnx)x²/³].
- In the limit x ≫ 1 (λ⊥ ≫ λ∥), R₁(x) ≈ (3g₀/4π)(x¹/⁶ + ½x⁻⁵/⁶) and R₂(x) ≈ (g₀/π²)(x¹/⁶ + ½x⁻⁵/⁶).
- For pure longitudinal free-streaming, λ∥ ∼ 1/τ and x ∝ τ², consistent with the relation x = x₀(nτ³)/(n₀τ₀³).

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics formalism introduced by Florkowski (2008) by providing a microscopic basis through anisotropic phase-space distribution functions, and complements studies of early-stage partonic free-streaming (Jas 2007, Broniowski 2008) which showed similar asymptotic behavior. The results are relevant to understanding early thermalization and isotropization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the magnetohydrodynamic extension offers a simplified framework for exploring color field effects analogous to those in the Color Glass Condensate and Glasma approaches (McLerran 1993, Kharzeev 2001, Lappi 2006). The formalism can provide anisotropic background distributions for analyzing plasma instabilities, particularly relevant to the RHIC HBT puzzle and the fast formation of transverse flow.
