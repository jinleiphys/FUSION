# Nuclear-Spin-Dependent Parity-Nonconserving Effects in Thallium, Lead and Bismuth Atoms

> Nuclear-spin-dependent P-odd optical activity in atomic Tl, Pb and Bi is calculated. Its magnitude is expressed analytically through the main contribution to the optical rotation, which is independent of nuclear spin. The accuracy of results is discussed.

# Nuclear-Spin-Dependent Parity-Nonconserving Effects in Thallium, Lead and Bismuth Atoms

## Key claim
Nuclear-spin-dependent (NSD) parity-nonconserving (PNC) optical activity in atomic Tl, Pb, and Bi can be expressed analytically through the main nuclear-spin-independent (NSI) contribution to optical rotation, with the ratio between the two effects controlled by the anapole moment constant κ_a and the weak nuclear charge Q. The accuracy of the results is estimated at 15-20% for Tl and Pb, but is less reliable for Bi due to additional approximations.

## Method
The author calculates NSD P-odd optical activity by expressing the weak interaction mixing matrix element in terms of the weak nuclear charge Q and the anapole moment constant κ_a, using second-quantization representation for electron operators. For Tl, the ratio of circular polarization P_a (induced by the nuclear anapole moment) to P_Q (induced by the weak charge) is derived from the hyperfine-structure-dependent mixing of s_{1/2} and p_{1/2} states. For Pb and Bi, the calculation employs jj-coupling wave functions and angular momentum theory with 3nj symbols to evaluate reduced matrix elements between hyperfine states, using phenomenological values for P_Q from prior work.

## Key numbers
- For Tl, 6p_{1/2} → 6p_{3/2} transition: P_a/κ_a = -0.96×10^{-8} for F=0 → F′=1, and 0.32×10^{-8} for F=1 → F′=1,2.
- For Pb, ^3P′_0 → ^3P_1 transition in ^207Pb: P_a/κ_a = 0.31×10^{-8} for F′=1/2, and -0.15×10^{-8} for F′=3/2.
- For Bi, infrared line (876 nm): P_a/κ_a values range from -2.58×10^{-8} (F=4 → F′=4) to 0.54×10^{-8} (F=4 → F′=3) across hyperfine components.
- For Bi, red line (648 nm): P_a/κ_a values range from -2.87×10^{-8} (F=3 → F′=4) to 3.78×10^{-8} (F=6 → F′=5) across hyperfine components.
- Phenomenological NSI optical rotation values used: P_Q = 3.4×10^{-7}(-Q/N) for Tl, 2.4×10^{-7}(-Q/N) for Pb, 2.9×10^{-7}(-Q/N) for Bi infrared line, and 3.8×10^{-7}(-Q/N) for Bi red line.
- Weak nuclear charge Q is close numerically to -N, where N is the neutron number (N=122 for ^203Tl, N=124 for ^205Tl).
- Anapole constant κ_a is close to 0.40 in Tl.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of NSD PNC effects in heavy atoms beyond the initial calculations of Khriplovich (1985) and provides an alternative analytical formulation for Tl, Pb, and Bi, which are the subjects of ongoing optical rotation experiments by groups such as Lamoreaux (1994) and others. The results enable comparison with experimental upper limits on NSD optical activity in bismuth and lead obtained by Macpherson (1991) and others, and with the first evidence of NSD correlations seen in cesium by Wieman (1994). The accuracy discussion references subsequent theoretical calculations by Dzuba (1994) and experimental results by Wood (1994) for Tl, and by Meekhof (1993) for Pb, noting unresolved discrepancies between experiments for the Bi red line.
