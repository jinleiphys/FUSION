# Self-consistent $M1$ radiative transitions of excited $B_c$ and heavy quarkonia with different polarizations in the light-front quark model

> In this study, we investigate the properties of pseudoscalar and vector charmonia, bottomonia, and $B_c$ mesons using the light-front quark model, focusing on the $M1$ radiative transition. For that purpose, we conduct a variational analysis with a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian, employing a trial wave function expanded in the harmonic oscillator basis functions up to the $3S$ state. We fit the model parameters to mass spectra and decay constants, obtaining reasonable agreement with experimental data and correctly reflecting the hierarchy of mass spectra and decay constants. In analyzing the $M1$ radiative transition, we consider both good ($μ=+$) and transverse ($μ={\perp}$) current components with both longitudinal $(h=0)$ and transverse $(h=\pm1)$ polarizations, demonstrating that the results from both components of currents and polarizations are identical. Self-consistency is achieved by substituting $M$ with $M_0$ when computing the operators for decay constants and radiative transitions. We also find that the difference between longitudinal and transverse polarizations of the observables may quantify the anisotropy of the model wave function. Our results on radiative transitions align reasonably well with experimental data, lattice QCD, and theoretical predictions. Furthermore, we also provide predictions for $B_c$ mesons that can be tested in experiments.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that self-consistency in the light-front quark model for M1 radiative transitions, meaning identical results from different current components and polarization choices, is achieved by replacing the physical meson mass M with the invariant mass M0 in the operators, following the Bakamjian-Thomas construction; this extends previous self-consistency checks for decay constants and form factors to the M1 radiative transition case.

## Method

The authors performed a variational analysis using a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian with a trial wave function expanded in harmonic oscillator basis functions up to the 3S state, including a screened confining potential. They fitted model parameters to mass spectra and decay constants, then computed M1 radiative transition form factors using both the plus (μ=+) and transverse (μ=⊥) current components with both longitudinal (h=0) and transverse (h=±1) polarizations in the Drell-Yan-West frame (q+=0). The matrix elements were evaluated using the standard light-front quark model with the Melosh transformation for the spin-orbit wave function.

## Key numbers

- The hyperfine interaction expectation values are ⟨Sq·Sq̄⟩ = 1/4 for vector mesons and -3/4 for pseudoscalar mesons
- The harmonic oscillator basis functions are expanded up to the 3S state, with explicit forms given for φ1S^HO, φ2S^HO, and φ3S^HO in terms of the oscillator parameter β
- The screened confining potential takes the form VConf = a + b(1-e^{-cr})/c, with parameters a, b, and c to be determined by fitting
- The strong coupling αs is treated as a constant parameter in the Coulomb and hyperfine potentials

## Context

This work extends previous light-front quark model calculations of M1 radiative decays by Choi (2007) that used only the plus component with transverse polarization, and extends the self-consistent LFQM framework developed by Arifi (2022, 2023) and Choi (2021, 2024) from decay constants and form factors to radiative transitions. The study also extends earlier analyses using harmonic oscillator basis functions by Peng (2012) and Ke (2010) to include the 3S state and screened confining potential, and provides predictions for Bc mesons that can be tested at the LHC. The authors note that in the BLFQ analysis by Li (2018), the transition form factor was found to differ depending on polarization, which the present work resolves through the M→M0 replacement.
