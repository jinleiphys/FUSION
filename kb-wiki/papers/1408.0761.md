# Understanding the $p/π$ ratio at LHC due to QCD mass spectrum

> Thermal fits have consistently reproduced the experimental particles yields of heavy ion collisions, however, the proton to pion ratio from ALICE Pb+Pb $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV is over-predicted by thermal models- known at the $p/π$ puzzle. Here we test the relevance of the extended mass spectrum, i.e., include Hagedorn states (resonances that follow an exponential mass spectrum and have very short life times) on the $p/π$ puzzle. We find that the extended mass spectrum is able to reproduce particle ratios at both RHIC and the LHC as well as being able to match the lower $p/π$ ratio at the LHC through dynamical chemical equilibration.

## Key claim

The extended mass spectrum (Hagedorn states) combined with dynamical chemical equilibration can reproduce the lower proton-to-pion ratio at LHC Pb+Pb collisions, but only when protons and lambdas begin underpopulated relative to chemical equilibrium; if all hadrons start in chemical equilibrium, the proton-to-pion ratio remains significantly overpopulated.

## Method

The authors model a Bjorken expansion with accelerating radial flow from initial time tau_0 to a switching temperature T_SW = 155 MeV, then populate hadrons using multihadronic decay reactions catalyzed by non-strange, mesonic Hagedorn states. The Hagedorn states follow an exponential mass spectrum rho = A exp(m/T_H) with A = 2.84 GeV^-1 and T_H = 0.252 GeV, and decay via n pi <-> HS <-> n' pi + X bar-X where X bar-X = p bar-p, K bar-K, or Lambda bar-Lambda. The dynamical chemical equilibration is evolved until final temperatures T_end = 133 MeV for LHC and T_end = 135 MeV for RHIC.

## Key numbers

- LHC Pb+Pb sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: the extended mass spectrum model matches the lower p/pi ratio that thermal models overpredict (the "p/pi puzzle")
- RHIC Au+Au sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: the extended mass spectrum model matches experimental particle ratios when protons begin underpopulated
- Switching temperature T_SW = 155 MeV for both LHC and RHIC
- Final temperature T_end = 133 MeV for LHC calculations; T_end = 135 MeV for RHIC calculations
- Hagedorn spectrum parameters: A = 2.84 GeV^-1, T_H = 0.252 GeV
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm for LHC, tau_0 = 1.0 fm for RHIC; acceleration a_0 = 0.03 fm^-1 for LHC, a_0 = 0.02 fm^-1 for RHIC; initial radius r_0 = 7.1 fm for both

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Greiner and Noronha-Hostler (2004-2009) that explored the extended mass spectrum at RHIC, updating the mass spectrum to fit more recent lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2010) and updating the modeling method to reflect current heavy-ion collision procedures. The paper addresses the "p/pi puzzle" identified by ALICE (Abelev 2012), where thermal models consistently overpredict the proton-to-pion ratio at LHC energies. The results suggest that Hagedorn states, which have already been shown to affect shear viscosity, lattice QCD thermodynamics, and elliptic flow, may also explain the LHC p/pi ratio and should be integrated into hadronic afterburners and transport models for future systematic studies.
