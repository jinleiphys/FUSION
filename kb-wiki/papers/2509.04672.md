# rho-Meson Nucleon Scattering Length from CLAS Threshold Photoproduction Measurements

> Extending our study of the vector meson-nucleon scattering lengths (summary is given in Ref.~\cite{Strakovsky:2021vyk}), we are focusing on the $ρ$-meson case using recent CLAS threshold data for the reaction $γp \to ρp$ within the meson-baryon reaction model~\cite{CLAS:2018drk}. The total $σ_t(γp\to ρp)$ and $σ_t(γp\to ωp)$ cross sections are close below the momentum of the vector meson in CM $q = 0.2~\mathrm{GeV/c}$. Then the $ρp$ photoproduction cross section grows rapidly. Our result for $ρN$ scattering length is a factor of 4 smaller than the size of the hadron and the phenomenological determination of the $ω$ nucleon scattering length using threshold photoproduction cross sections. The observed difference between $ρN$ and $ωN$ scattering lengths is of interest for further understanding within the hadron structure theory.

## Key claim

The rho-meson nucleon scattering length is a factor of 4 smaller than the size of the hadron and the phenomenological determination of the omega nucleon scattering length, but this result depends on treating the broad rho meson as a quasi-stable object and working far from threshold (minimum momentum 143 MeV/c) to avoid complications from its large decay width.

## Method

The authors extracted integrated gamma p to rho p cross sections from CLAS measurements of the gamma p to pi+ pi- p reaction using the meson-baryon reaction model (JM model), which incorporates contributions from pi- Delta++, rho p, pi+ Delta0, pi+ N(1520)3/2+, and pi+ N(1680)5/2+ channels plus direct 2-pi mechanisms. The nine one-fold differential cross sections measured by CLAS in 25 MeV bins across the W range from 1.6 to 2.0 GeV were fit with chi-squared per data point less than 1.5. The absolute value of the rho-nucleon scattering length was then determined from the linear term of a polynomial fit to the total cross section as a function of center-of-mass momentum, combined with a vector meson dominance kinematic factor using the rho meson's leptonic decay width.

## Key numbers

* The rho meson total width is Gamma_rho = 147.4 +/- 0.8 MeV (from PDG2024)
* The rho meson leptonic decay width is Gamma(rho to e+ e-) = 7.04 +/- 0.06 keV
* The linear fit parameter a = (3.99 +/- 0.52) x 10^-2 micro-barn/(MeV/c) for the rho case, compared to a = (4.42 +/- 0.14) x 10^-2 micro-barn/(MeV/c) for the omega case
* The absolute rho-proton scattering length |alpha_rho p| = 0.23 +/- 0.03 fm from this CLAS analysis
* The absolute omega-proton scattering length |alpha_omega p| = 0.82 +/- 0.03 fm from A2 Collaboration at MAMI (Strakovsky 2014)
* The total cross section for gamma p to rho p at W = 1.737 GeV (q = 143 MeV/c) is 5.910 +/- 0.692 micro-barn, rising to 18.890 +/- 1.990 micro-barn at W = 1.912 GeV (q = 423 MeV/c)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of vector meson-nucleon scattering lengths previously performed for omega, phi, J/psi(1S), and Upsilon(1S) mesons (Strakovsky 2021), now addressing the rho meson which was excluded earlier due to its broad width. The result for the rho-nucleon scattering length agrees with an analysis of earlier SAPHIR measurements (Wang 2022) and is a factor of 4 smaller than the omega-nucleon scattering length determined by the A2 Collaboration at MAMI (Strakovsky 2014), ELPH (Ishikawa 2019), and CBELSA/TAPS (Han 2022). The observed difference between rho and omega scattering lengths provides input for hadron structure theory, while a dynamical coupled-channel approach free from vector meson dominance (Wang 2022) has reported complex omega-nucleon scattering lengths with real parts of -0.24 fm and -0.21 fm.
