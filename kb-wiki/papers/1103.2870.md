# Charged particle's elliptic flow in 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics at LHC ($\sqrt{s}$= 2.76 TeV) energy in Pb+Pb collision

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of second order hydrodynamics, we have simulated $\sqrt{s}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. ALICE data for the centrality dependence of charged particles multiplicity, $p_T$ spectra in 0-5% collisions, centrality dependence of integrated and differential elliptic flow are analysed. Analysis indicate that while ALICE data on charged particles multiplicity or $p_T$ spectra do not demand any viscosity, viscosity is demanded by the elliptic flow data. From a simulataneous fit to all the data sets, viscosity to entropy ratio in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions is extracted as, $η/s=0.06\pm 0.02$.

# Charged particle's elliptic flow in 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV) energy in Pb+Pb collision

## Key claim

The authors extract a viscosity to entropy ratio of η/s = 0.06 ± 0.02 from a simultaneous fit to ALICE data on multiplicity, pT spectra, and elliptic flow, but note that the χ²/N values exhibit a broad minimum, indicating the data are not very sensitive to the viscosity to entropy ratio. The extracted value carries a systematic uncertainty that could be as large as 175% if all possible initial conditions are accounted for.

## Method

The authors solve Israel-Stewart's second order viscous hydrodynamic equations in 2+1 dimensions assuming boost-invariance, using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code. The fluid is initialized at τi = 0.6 fm/c with zero initial fluid velocity, a Glauber model energy density profile with 90% hard scattering fraction, and boost-invariant shear stress tensor values. The equation of state is constructed by complementing Wuppertal-Budapest lattice simulations with a hadron resonance gas up to mass 2.5 GeV, with a crossover transition at Tc = 174 MeV. The freeze-out temperature is fixed at TF = 130 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Viscosity to entropy ratio extracted from simultaneous fit to all ALICE data sets: η/s = 0.06 ± 0.02
- Central energy density for ideal fluid (η/s = 0) in 0-5% collisions: εi = 143.0 ± 6.0 GeV/fm³ at τi = 0.6 fm/c
- Central temperature for ideal fluid in 0-5% collisions: Ti = 548 ± 5 MeV
- Central energy density for η/s = 0.08 (KSS bound): εi = 126.0 ± 5.5 GeV/fm³
- Central energy density for η/s = 0.16: εi = 103.0 ± 4.6 GeV/fm³
- χ²/N for integrated elliptic flow fit: 9.68 (η/s = 0), 3.02 (η/s = 0.08), 12.46 (η/s = 0.12), 41.44 (η/s = 0.16)
- χ²/N for differential elliptic flow in 40-50% collisions: 33.62 (η/s = 0), 9.86 (η/s = 0.08), 4.18 (η/s = 0.12), 9.00 (η/s = 0.16)
- ALICE measured charged particle multiplicity in 0-5% collisions: dNch/dy = 1601 ± 60
- Pseudocritical temperature for confinement-deconfinement transition: Tc ≈ 170 MeV (used 174 MeV in the model EoS)

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic analysis previously applied to RHIC Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV (Chaudhuri, 2009) to the new LHC energy of √sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The extracted η/s = 0.06 ± 0.02 is similar to the value extracted at RHIC energy, suggesting that nearly perfect fluid is produced at both energies despite the higher temperature at LHC. The result compares well with other hydrodynamic model simulations (Schenke, 2011; Bozek, 2011), though hybrid models coupling hydrodynamics to hadron cascade models indicate higher viscosity (Song, 2011; Hirano, 2010). The analysis contradicts an earlier prediction by one of the authors (Chaudhuri, 2008) that elliptic flow would decrease by ~15% at LHC, which was based on incorrect assumptions about multiplicity scaling and a first-order phase transition.
