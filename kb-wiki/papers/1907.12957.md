# Time Evolution Operators for Periodic SU(3)

> We present an outline of a technique to associate certain methods from time optimal quantum control with various transforms on SU(3). Unitary operators are taken from certain time dependent Hamiltonians and transformation laws are derived. A methodological framework for development of solution for these types of problems with a non-simple geometric framework is presented that is generally applicable. We give an expansive overview of the field of SU(3), its meaning, purpose and context within the presented results. Exact solutions for periodic curves on SU(3) are provided, along with the operators and physical framework that surrounds them. A comprehensive review of the literature and established results is given for reference. Discussion is given in full to effective approaches that may be given both to the teaching of the subject, and the development of further results within the science. We conclude the paper with an overview of new avenues of investigation that are opened through the results that are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a methodological framework for obtaining exact solutions for periodic curves on SU(3) by associating time optimal quantum control methods with transforms on SU(3), though the approach is described as an "outline" and "generally applicable" rather than a fully proven technique, with the actual solutions derived only for a specific Hamiltonian and constraint set originally considered in prior work by the same author.

## Method

The author sets up a time-dependent Hamiltonian and a constraint matrix on SU(3), both traceless Hermitian matrices, and applies the quantum brachistochrone equation (a Heisenberg-type equation of motion) to obtain coupled ordinary differential equations for the matrix elements. The Hamiltonian is taken in a tridiagonal form with time-dependent complex parameters epsilon_1 and epsilon_2, while the constraint matrix contains constant parameters omega_1, omega_2, and kappa. The resulting system of four coupled differential equations for epsilon_1 and epsilon_2 and their complex conjugates is then solved to determine the time evolution of the Hamiltonian, from which unitary time evolution operators for periodic curves on SU(3) are constructed.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian matrix has zeros on the diagonal and off-diagonal elements epsilon_1(t) and epsilon_2(t) (complex functions of time), with their complex conjugates in the transposed positions.
* The constraint matrix contains constant parameters omega_1, omega_2 (real), and kappa (complex), with the diagonal entries being omega_1, -(omega_1+omega_2), and omega_2.
* The time derivatives of omega_1, omega_2, and kappa are all zero: dot-omega_1 = dot-omega_2 = 0, dot-kappa = dot-kappa-bar = 0.

## Context

This work extends the quantum brachistochrone approach developed by Carlini and collaborators (Carlini 2006, 2007) for time optimal quantum control, applying it specifically to the SU(3) group which is relevant to qutrit computation and nuclear quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. The paper provides a comprehensive review of SU(3) applications including nuclear shell models (Elliot 1958-1963), heavy nuclei calculations (Almeida 2004), Bose-Einstein condensates (Alodjants, Nishida), and geometric methods (Byrd 2000, 2001), while the specific Hamiltonian and constraint set used are modified versions of techniques from the author's prior work (Morrison 2015, 2016). The results enable exact solutions for periodic curves on SU(3) that may be compared with computer simulations and experimental measurements of quadrupole interactions in nuclear systems.
