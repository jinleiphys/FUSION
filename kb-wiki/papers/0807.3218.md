# Coulomb energy contribution to the excitation energy in $^{229}$Th and enhanced effect of $α$ variation

> We calculated the contribution of Coulomb energy to the spacing between the ground and first excited state of $^{229}$Th nucleus as a function of the deformation parameter $δ$. We show that despite the fact that the odd particle is a neutron, the change in Coulomb energy between these two states can reach several hundreds KeV.This means that the effect of the variation of the fine structure constant $α=e^2/\hbar c$ may be enhanced $ΔU_C/E \sim 10^4$ times in the $E=$7.6 eV "nuclear clock" transition between the ground and first excited states in the $^{229}$Th nucleus.

# Coulomb energy contribution to the excitation energy in $^{229}$Th and enhanced effect of $\alpha$ variation

## Key claim
The change in Coulomb energy between the ground and first excited state of $^{229}$Th can reach several hundreds of keV, even though the odd particle is a neutron. This produces an enhancement of the effect of fine-structure constant variation by a factor $\Delta U_C/E \sim 10^4$ for the 7.6 eV nuclear clock transition, but the actual value depends strongly on the nuclear deformation parameter $\delta$ and can even change sign.

## Method
The authors calculated the Coulomb energy shift when a neutron is added to a single-particle orbital of the $^{228}$Th core, using the polarization correction from the residual proton-neutron interaction in first order. They employed Nilsson wave functions to account for nuclear deformation and computed the difference in Coulomb energy between the [633 5/2+] ground state and [631 3/2+] excited state of $^{229}$Th as a function of the deformation parameter $\delta$.

## Key numbers
- The average correction to Coulomb potential energy over nuclear volume is -440 keV.
- For N=6 shell orbitals, the Coulomb energy shift $\Delta U_C$ ranges from -1.381 MeV (for $2g_{9/2}$) to +1.305 MeV (for $1i_{13/2}$), with values for $3d_{3/2}$ (-0.741 MeV), $3d_{5/2}$ (-0.926 MeV), $2g_{7/2}$ (-0.848 MeV), and $1i_{11/2}$ (+0.158 MeV).
- The difference in Coulomb energies between the excited and ground states changes from 1.5 MeV at zero deformation down to -0.5 MeV at $\delta = 0.3$, and becomes negative in the deformation interval $0.15 \leq \delta \leq 0.25$.
- The relative enhancement factor is $\delta\nu/\nu = 1.3 \times 10^4 \times (\Delta U_C / 100\text{ keV}) \times (\delta\alpha/\alpha)$ for the 7.6 eV transition.

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by Flambaum (2006) that the low-energy transition in $^{229}$Th may produce a five-orders-of-magnitude enhancement of variation effects, and confirms the existence of such enhancement as reported by Hayes (2007). The calculation provides an independent estimate using the relation $h\delta\nu = \Delta U_C \delta\alpha/\alpha$ from Hayes (2007). The result supports the use of $^{229}$Th as a nuclear clock reference, as proposed by Peik and Tamm (2003), with the latest measured transition energy of $7.6 \pm 0.5$ eV from Beck (2007). The narrow width of the excited state (estimated at $10^{-4}$ Hz) and the large enhancement factor suggest that experiments with $^{229}$Th could improve laboratory limits on fundamental constant variation by up to 8 orders of magnitude.
