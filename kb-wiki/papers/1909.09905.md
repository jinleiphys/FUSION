# Modeling photon--induced reactions on $^{233-238}$U actinide targets

> Comprehensive calculations of cross sections of photon induced reactions on $^{233-238}$U targets for incident photon energies from 3 up to 30 MeV are undertaken with the statistical model code EMPIRE-3.2 Malta. Results are compared with the experimental data from EXFOR and with the current evaluations. The differences and the similarities between the models and parameters used in calculations of photon- and neutron-induced reactions on the same nuclei are discussed with focus on fission. The role of the extended optical model for fission in improving the description of the measured data and in determining consistent sets of barrier parameters is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fission parameters derived from fitting neutron-induced fission cross sections can be used consistently for photon-induced reactions on uranium isotopes, provided the extended optical model for fission is employed to account for partial damping of vibrational states in the third well of triple-humped fission barriers, particularly at excitation energies below 5-6 MeV.

## Method

Comprehensive statistical model calculations were performed using the EMPIRE-3.2 Malta code for photon-induced reactions on six uranium isotopes (233-238U) over incident photon energies from 3 to 30 MeV. The calculations employed the Modified Lorentzian 1 (MLO1) radiative strength function for the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) description, the Hauser-Feshbach model for compound nucleus decay with full gamma cascade and exact angular momentum coupling, and the extended optical model for fission (OMF) with triple-humped barriers for 233-237U and a double-humped barrier for 238U. The same fission parameters previously determined from neutron-induced reaction calculations were used, with GDR parameters adjusted to reproduce experimental photo-absorption, neutron emission, and fission cross sections.

## Key numbers

* GDR parameters for 233U: first resonance at 11.3 MeV with width 3.0 MeV and peak cross section 320.0 mb; second resonance at 14.0 MeV with width 4.3 MeV and peak cross section 360 mb
* GDR parameters for 234U: first resonance at 11.2 MeV with width 3.2 MeV and peak cross section 370.0 mb; second resonance at 14.1 MeV with width 4.3 MeV and peak cross section 380 mb
* GDR parameters for 235U: first resonance at 11.0 MeV with width 2.8 MeV and peak cross section 370.0 mb; second resonance at 14.1 MeV with width 4.0 MeV and peak cross section 380 mb
* GDR parameters for 236U: first resonance at 11.3 MeV with width 3.4 MeV and peak cross section 320.0 mb; second resonance at 14.0 MeV with width 4.5 MeV and peak cross section 320 mb
* GDR parameters for 237U: first resonance at 11.2 MeV with width 3.2 MeV and peak cross section 360.0 mb; second resonance at 14.0 MeV with width 4.3 MeV and peak cross section 380 mb
* GDR parameters for 238U: first resonance at 11.0 MeV with width 3.3 MeV and peak cross section 316.0 mb; second resonance at 14.1 MeV with width 4.4 MeV and peak cross section 320 mb
* For 237U, a difference of approximately 40% was observed between EMPIRE and JENDL-PD absorption cross sections, attributed to the absence of experimental data for this isotope

## Context

This work extends the consistent fission parameter sets previously obtained by Sin (2017) from neutron-induced reaction calculations on the uranium isotopic chain, testing their compatibility with photon-induced reaction modeling. The results support the development of the IAEA Photonuclear Data Library and complement evaluations produced within the NEA CIELO project that were adopted by the ENDF/B-VIII.0 library. The extended optical model for fission with partial damping treatment enables improved description of photo-fission cross sections at low excitation energies, where the full damping approximation implemented in most statistical reaction codes is inadequate.
