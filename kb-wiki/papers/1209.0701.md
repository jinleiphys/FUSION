# Meson production in two-photon interactions at energies available at CERN Large Hadron Collider

> The meson production cross sections are estimated considering photon-photon interactions in hadron - hadron collisions at CERN LHC energies. We consider a large number of mesons with photon-photon partial decay width well constrained by the experiment and some mesons which are currently considered as hadronic molecule and glueball candidates. Our results demonstrate that the experimental analysis of these states is feasible at CERN - LHC.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that meson production via two-photon interactions in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies yields cross sections large enough for experimental study, but they explicitly note that for heavy quarkonia (chi and eta states) the competing central exclusive production via Pomeron-Pomeron interactions is expected to dominate by at least three orders of magnitude, making the two-photon channel a lower bound rather than the dominant mechanism for those states.

## Method

The total cross section for hadron-hadron collisions producing a neutral meson X via two-photon interactions is factorized as the product of the equivalent photon fluxes from each hadron and the photon-photon cross section, which depends on the meson's spin, mass, and two-photon partial decay width. The authors use two different models for the equivalent photon flux of the proton (the "Electric" dipole form factor model and the Dress-Zeppenfeld model) to bracket the theoretical uncertainty, and they compute cross sections at LHC center-of-mass energies of 7 TeV and 14 TeV for mesons with experimentally known decay widths, as well as for hadronic molecule and glueball candidates using theoretical decay widths from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For the pi0 meson at 7 TeV: cross section 2426 pb (Electric model) to 2812 pb (DZ model); at 14 TeV: 3008 pb to 3453.9 pb
- For the eta meson at 7 TeV: 1368.9 pb (Electric) to 1624.8 pb (DZ); at 14 TeV: 1760 pb to 2062.9 pb
- For the f2(1270) meson at 7 TeV: 2300.8 pb (Electric) to 2781 pb (DZ); at 14 TeV: 3043.9 pb to 3623.7 pb
- For the etac(1S) at 7 TeV: 58 pb (Electric) to 72 pb (DZ); at 14 TeV: 80 pb to 97 pb
- For the etab(1S) at 7 TeV: 0.024 pb (Electric) to 0.031 pb (DZ); at 14 TeV: 0.035 pb to 0.044 pb
- For the glueball candidate f0(1500) at 7 TeV: 0.080 pb (Electric) to 0.066 pb (DZ); at 14 TeV: 0.088 pb to 0.10 pb
- Cross sections increase by approximately 30% when the center-of-mass energy increases from 7 TeV to 14 TeV
- Assuming design luminosity of 10^7 mb^-1 s^-1, event rates for mesons with well-known decay widths exceed 10^5 events per year at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
- For the hadronic molecule candidate f0(1370), predicted event rates exceed 10^7 events per year at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
- For glueball candidates, predicted event rates are smaller than 10^4 events per year

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Bertulani (2009) on meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions to proton-proton collisions at the LHC, using the same formalism of equivalent photon fluxes. The authors compare their predictions for heavy quarkonia (chi and eta states) with central exclusive production via Pomeron-Pomeron interactions as calculated by Harland-Lang et al. (2010), finding that Pomeron-Pomeron cross sections are at least three orders of magnitude larger for those states, so the two-photon channel provides a lower bound. The results enable experimental searches for glueball and hadronic molecule candidates at LHC, particularly for states like f0(1370) and X(3940) where predicted event rates are high enough to constrain theoretical models of their internal structure.
