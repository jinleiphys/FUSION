# The microscopic pairing gap in a slab of nuclear matter for the Argonne v18 NN-potential

> Ab initio gap equation for ^1S_0 pairing in a nuclear slab is solved for the Argonne v18 NN-potential. The gap function is compared in detail with the one found previously for the separable form of the Paris potential. The difference between the two gaps turned out to be about 10%. Dependence of the gap on the chemical potential mu is analyzed.

## Key claim
The difference in the pairing gap between calculations using the Argonne v18 potential and the separable Paris potential is about 10%, with the Argonne result being smaller; this difference is attributed to the contrasting core hardness of the two potentials and is considered an upper estimate of the uncertainty from the choice of NN-potential.

## Method
The authors solved the ab initio BCS gap equation for 1S0 pairing in a nuclear slab (one-dimensional Saxon-Woods potential well, depth -50 MeV, diffuseness 0.65 fm, width 6 fm) using the Argonne v18 NN-potential. They employed a two-step method that splits the Hilbert space into a model subspace (E0 = 15 MeV) and a complementary subspace, using the local potential approximation (LPA) to compute the effective pairing interaction (EPI) in the complementary subspace. The gap equation was solved iteratively in a mixed representation (coordinate for the x-direction, momentum for the transverse plane) using 210 parallel processors, with convergence defined by a maximal difference of 10^-6 MeV between iterations.

## Key numbers
- The difference between the Fermi-averaged gap for the Argonne v18 potential and the separable Paris potential is about 10%.
- The self-consistent chemical potential for the slab is mu = -7.95 MeV, very close to the value without pairing (mu0 = -8 MeV); the difference delta mu = -0.05 MeV.
- For the Argonne v18 potential, the average diagonal matrix element Delta_nn(k_perp=0) for positive parity states (excluding the anomalously small state at eps_n = -1.325 MeV) is 1.16 MeV; for the Paris potential it is 1.27 MeV.
- The rms radius of the gap function is about 0.8 fm inside the slab and grows to about 1.2 fm outside.
- A cut-off momentum K_max = 10 fm^-1 is required for 1% accuracy in the gap for the Argonne v18 potential; K_max = 6.2 fm^-1 (corresponding to E_max = 800 MeV) gives only 10% accuracy.
- For the Paris potential, K_max = 160 fm^-1 is necessary for 1% accuracy.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculation for a nuclear slab using the separable Paris potential (Pankratov et al., 2007) and compares with the Milan group's calculation for 120Sn using the Argonne v14 potential (Milan group, 2007). The results show that the gap from the Argonne v18 potential (about 1.16 MeV average) is closer to the experimental value in tin isotopes (Delta_exp about 1.3 MeV) than the Milan group's result (about 0.7 MeV), leaving only about 10-20% room for surface vibration corrections. The analysis of mu-dependence, extending prior work for the Paris potential (Pankratov et al., 2007), shows that the behavior of the gap near the drip line is model-dependent and requires self-consistent treatment of the mean field.
