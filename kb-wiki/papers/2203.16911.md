# Exclusive production of light vector mesons at next-to-leading order in the dipole picture

> Exclusive production of light vector mesons in deep inelastic scattering is calculated at next-to-leading order in the dipole picture in the limit of high photon virtuality. The resulting expression is free of any divergences and suitable for numerical evaluations. The higher-order corrections are found to be numerically important, but they can be mostly captured by the nonperturbative fit parameters describing the initial condition for the small-$x$ evolution of the dipole scattering amplitude. The vector meson production cross section is shown to depend only weakly on the meson distribution amplitude and the factorization scale. We also present phenomenological comparisons of our result to the existing exclusive $ϕ$ and $ρ$ production data from HERA and find an excellent agreement at high virtualities.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of exclusive light vector meson production in the dipole picture is free of divergences and suitable for numerical evaluation, but the numerically important higher-order corrections can be mostly absorbed into the nonperturbative fit parameters that describe the initial condition for the small-x evolution of the dipole scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors calculate exclusive light vector meson production in deep inelastic scattering at NLO using light-cone perturbation theory in mixed transverse coordinate and longitudinal momentum fraction space, working in the high photon virtuality limit (Q² >> M_V²) where a twist expansion applies. The calculation combines the NLO photon wave function from previous work with a newly computed NLO meson wave function expressed in terms of the twist-2 distribution amplitude, and the dipole-target scattering amplitude is evolved using the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling and resummation of higher-order logarithms (KCBK, ResumBK, and TBK schemes).

## Key numbers

- The NLO corrections are evaluated for photon virtualities Q² from approximately 1 GeV² to 100 GeV², with comparisons to HERA data for exclusive φ and ρ production
- The running coupling uses Λ_QCD with parameters from Beuf (2020), with the constant C² = e^{-2γ_E} relating momentum and coordinate spaces via k² = 4C²/r²
- The leading-twist distribution amplitude φ₀(z) is normalized as ∫₀¹ dz φ₀(z) = 1
- The leptonic width of vector mesons is given by Γ(V → l⁺l⁻) = 4πα_em² f_V²/(3M_V)
- The cross section at t=0 is obtained as dσ/dt|_{t=0} = |A|²/(16π)

## Context

This work extends the NLO dipole picture calculations for exclusive processes, which previously existed for heavy vector mesons in the nonrelativistic limit (Mantysaari 2021) and for light vector mesons using covariant perturbation theory in momentum space (Boussarie 2016). The authors use a different scheme to subtract the rapidity divergence from real gluon emission, developed in works by Ducloue (2017), Iancu (2016), and Beuf (2014), to avoid unphysical results seen in earlier NLO calculations. This enables phenomenological comparisons to HERA data using existing dipole amplitude fits from Beuf (2020), and provides a framework for predictions at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
