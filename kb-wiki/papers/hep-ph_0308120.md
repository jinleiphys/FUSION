# The QCD Pomeron in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions: III. Photonuclear production of heavy quarks

> We calculate the photonuclear production of heavy quarks in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. The integrated cross section and the rapidity distribution are computed employing sound high energy QCD formalisms as the collinear and semihard approaches as well as the saturation model. In particular, the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism is also considered using a simple phenomenological parameterization for the color field correlator in the medium, which allow us to obtain more reliable estimates for charm and bottom production at LHC energies.

# The QCD Pomeron in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions: III. Photonuclear production of heavy quarks

## Key claim

The authors calculate that ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at LHC energies can probe the high-energy (small-x) regime of QCD dynamics through heavy quark photoproduction, but the predictions from different formalisms (collinear, semihard, saturation model, and color glass condensate) produce similar results for inclusive charm and bottom production, making it difficult to distinguish between linear and nonlinear QCD dynamics using only total cross sections and rapidity distributions.

## Method

The photonuclear production cross sections for charm and bottom quarks in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies were computed by convolving the Weizsacker-Williams photon flux from one nucleus with the photon-nucleus cross section, using four distinct QCD approaches: (1) collinear factorization with the EKS98 nuclear gluon distribution and GRV nucleon parton distributions; (2) k_perp-factorization (semihard) formalism using both the derivative of collinear nuclear gluon distributions and a Glauber-Gribov extension of the saturation model; (3) the saturation model based on dipole-nucleus cross sections; and (4) the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism using a phenomenological parameterization for the color field correlator that introduces energy dependence through a nuclear saturation scale.

## Key numbers

- Charm mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom mass: m_b = 4.5 GeV
- LHC Pb+Pb center-of-mass energy: sqrt(S_NN) = 5500 GeV; Lorentz factor: gamma_L = 2930
- Maximum gamma-nucleon center-of-mass energy: W_gammaA <= 950 GeV
- For the CGC formalism with Q_sA/Lambda_QCD = 10 (y=2.3): dsigma/dy = 355 mb for charm and dsigma/dy = 11 mb for bottom, with a flat rapidity distribution
- Bjorken x variable probed: x = (M_QQbar / 5500 GeV) * exp(-y), reaching small x at large rapidities
- The difference between complete numeric and analytical photon flux calculations is less than 15%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark photoproduction on nuclear targets by Goncalves and Machado (2003) and by Gelis and collaborators, who analyzed heavy quark production in ultraperipheral collisions using the CGC formalism without quantum evolution. The authors introduce energy dependence into the CGC calculation through a phenomenological dipole-nucleus cross section inspired by the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) saturation model, with nuclear saturation scale Q_sA^2(x) = A^(1/3) * Q_s^2(x). The results enable comparison between linear (DGLAP-based collinear and semihard) and nonlinear (saturation and CGC) QCD dynamics, though the similar predictions for inclusive quantities suggest that transverse momentum distributions may be more promising for distinguishing between approaches, as previously noted in proton-proton collisions by the semihard approach community.
