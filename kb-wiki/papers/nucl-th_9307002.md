# Absorption mechanisms in photon induced two-body knockout

> Calculations have been performed for the $^{16}$O($γ$,pn) and the $^{16}$O($γ$,pp) reaction in the photon-energy range $E_γ$ = 60-300 MeV. Besides the contribution from the more common photoabsorption on the pionic degrees of freedom, we have investigated the influence of heavier meson exchange ($ρ, σ, ω$) and intermediate $Δ$ creation with $π$ and $ρ$ exchange. Whereas the $π$ meson is found to set the main trends, the $ρ$ meson is found not to be discardable in a theoretical description of the ($γ$,pn) reaction. The incorporation of an energy dependence and a decay width in the $Δ$ propagator is observed to be essential in order to arrive at a more realistic description of ($γ$,NN) reactions at higher photon energies.

# Absorption mechanisms in photon induced two-body knockout

## Key claim

The paper shows that while pion exchange currents set the main trends for the (γ,pn) reaction, the ρ meson contribution cannot be discarded in a theoretical description, and that incorporating an energy-dependent decay width in the Δ propagator is essential for realistic (γ,NN) cross sections at higher photon energies. The abstract's claim that the ρ meson is "not discardable" is hedged by the finding that its effect on the integrated cross section can be simulated by an effective cut-off parameter of 800 MeV in the πNN form factor, but this effective approach fails for angular cross sections.

## Method

The authors performed calculations for the ¹⁶O(γ,pn) and ¹⁶O(γ,pp) reactions in the photon-energy range Eγ = 60–300 MeV using the factorized Gottfried approach, which separates the photoabsorption dynamics from nuclear structure effects. They included meson exchange currents for π, ρ, σ, and ω mesons, as well as Δ isobar currents with both π and ρ exchange, using coupling constants from a one-boson-exchange parameterization of the Bonn potential, and adopted an energy-dependent nonrelativistic Δ propagator with a decay width satisfying unitarity in the πN channel.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range studied: Eγ = 60–300 MeV for ¹⁶O(γ,pn) and ¹⁶O(γ,pp) reactions.
- The πNN vertex monopole form factor cut-off mass is 1200 MeV; the ρNN vertex cut-off mass is 1500 MeV (both from the Bonn potential).
- An effective cut-off parameter of 800 MeV in the πNN form factor can simulate the reducing effect of ρ meson exchange on the total integrated (γ,pn) cross section.
- At the Δ(1232) peak, the (γ,pn) contribution to the total photoabsorption strength in ¹⁶O is predicted to be 2.40 mb; the total photoabsorption cross section in the Δ peak is measured to be about 7 mb for ¹⁶O.
- When discarding the ρ contribution to the Δ diagrams, the predicted (γ,pn) contribution at the Δ peak rises to 5.26 mb, which the authors consider unrealistic.
- The Δ current dominates the meson exchange currents for photon energies exceeding 150 MeV.
- The static Δ approximation is reasonable for the (γ,pp) reaction up to photon energies of 180 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that employed only pionic degrees of freedom for (γ,NN) reactions, such as those by Wakamatsu (1983), Boffi (1991), and Ryckebusch (1992). It builds on the Gottfried approach (1958) and uses the Bonn potential parameterization for meson-nucleon coupling constants. The paper enables future experimental comparisons with high-resolution (γ,pn) and (γ,pp) data expected from Lund, Mainz, and Saskatchewan, and suggests that angular cross sections at fixed missing momentum are a better tool than integrated cross sections for understanding the photoabsorption mechanism. The authors note that Riska (1985) previously observed the strong reduction of the Δ current with π exchange by the Δ current with ρ exchange in static Δ calculations, and the present work confirms this for an energy-dependent Δ propagator.
