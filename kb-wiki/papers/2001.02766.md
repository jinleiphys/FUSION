# Partonic transport model application to heavy flavor

> Heavy-flavor particles are excellent probes of the properties of the hot and dense nuclear medium created in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Heavy-flavor transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) stage of the collisions are particularly interesting, as they contain important information on the strong interaction at finite temperatures. Studying the heavy-flavor evolution in a dynamically evolving medium requires a comprehensive multi-stage modeling approach of both the medium and the probes, with an accurate implementation of the physical ingredients to be tested. For this purpose, I have developed a new partonic transport model (Linear-Boltzmann-plus-Diffusion-Transport-Model) LIDO and applied it to heavy quark propagation inside a QGP. The model has an improved implementation of parton in-medium bremsstrahlung and a flexible treatment of the probe-medium interactions, combining both large angle scatterings and diffusion processes. The model is then coupled to a high-energy event-generator, a hydrodynamic medium evolution and a hadronic transport model. Finally, applying a Bayesian analysis, I extract the heavy quark transport coefficients from a model-to-data comparison. The results, with uncertainty quantification, are found to be consistent with earlier extraction of the light-quark transport coefficients at high momentum and with first-principle calculations of the heavy flavor diffusion constant at low momentum.

## Method

The author constructs the Linear-Boltzmann-plus-Diffusion-Transport-Model (LIDO) for heavy quark propagation inside a quark-gluon plasma, coupling it to a high-energy event-generator, a hydrodynamic medium evolution, and a hadronic transport model. The model implements both elastic (collisional) and inelastic (radiative) interactions between heavy quarks and the medium, with a flexible treatment that bridges large-angle scattering and diffusion processes. A Bayesian analysis is then applied to compare the model output to experimental data, extracting heavy quark transport coefficients with quantified uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the line of heavy-flavor transport modeling that began with pure diffusion models (Moore 2004, vanHees 2007) and later radiation-improved diffusion models (Cao 2013) applied to the first Bayesian extraction of heavy quark transport coefficients (Xu 2017). The LIDO model addresses discrepancies between Boltzmann-equation-based models and diffusion-based models in extracted transport parameters D_s and \hat{q}, which arise from differences in radiative energy loss inclusion, medium assumptions, and treatment of dynamics. This enables more robust extraction of heavy quark transport coefficients with quantified theoretical uncertainties, contributing to the broader program of determining QGP properties such as shear viscosity η/s and jet transport coefficient \hat{q} from experimental data.
