# The V-QCD baryon : numerical solution and baryon spectrum

> The single baryon solution of V-QCD is numerically computed. The spectrum of spin and isospin modes is also computed by quantizing the light fluctuations around the baryon. It is shown that there is a partial restoration of chiral symmetry at the baryon center.

## Key claim

The single baryon solution of V-QCD is numerically computed for the first time, showing that the baryon is well-localized in the holographic direction and that chiral symmetry is partially restored at the baryon center; however, the solution is only valid in the exact chiral limit (zero quark masses) and the numerical results for the baryon spectrum are obtained by substituting \(N_c = 3\) into the leading-order large-\(N_c\) and large-\(N_f\) result, which is not expected to be quantitatively accurate at such small values.

## Method

The static baryon solution is constructed as an axial \(SU(2)\) instanton in the five-dimensional V-QCD bulk, using a probe approximation on the background dual to the QCD vacuum, with the full Dirac-Born-Infeld action for the tachyon kept non-linear but expanded to quadratic order in the non-abelian gauge fields. The collective modes of the soliton are quantized by considering a time-dependent isospin rotation \(V(t) \in SU(2)_{L+R}\) with constant, slow rotation velocity \(\omega^a\), and solving the linearized equations of motion for the rotating ansatz fields to compute the moment of inertia. The model parameters are determined by a simultaneous fit to lattice QCD thermodynamics data (equation of state at vanishing chemical potential and first cumulant of the pressure at nonzero chemical potential) and experimental data for lowest-lying meson masses and the pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

* Classical soliton mass: \(M_0 \simeq \frac{N_c}{3} \times 1150\,\text{MeV}\) (for \(N_c = 3\) colors, this gives approximately 1150 MeV before quantum corrections).
* Predicted nucleon mass (\(s = \frac{1}{2}\)): \(M_N \simeq 1170 \,\text{MeV}\) (experimental: 940 MeV).
* Predicted delta baryon mass (\(s = \frac{3}{2}\)): \(M_\Delta \simeq 1260 \,\text{MeV}\) (experimental: 1234 MeV).
* Pion decay constant target: \(f_\pi \approx 92~\mathrm{MeV}\) (used as a fit constraint).

## Context

This work extends the general formalism for the V-QCD baryon presented in Järvinen et al. (2022, "BaryonI") by providing the first numerical solution and spin-isospin spectrum for a specific model within the V-QCD class. It improves upon the Hard-Wall model (Pomarol et al., 2008), where the baryon fell onto the IR wall, and the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sugimoto et al., 2005), where the baryon size was parametrically small at large 't Hooft coupling; in V-QCD the baryon is dynamically stabilized by the tachyon field and its size is set by the QCD scale. The results enable future work on non-zero modes (excited baryon states), quantization of higher flavor subgroups (hyperons), and extension to finite quark masses.
