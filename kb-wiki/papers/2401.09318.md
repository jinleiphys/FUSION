# Deuteron gravitational form factors: exchange currents

> Following on our recent analysis of the energy momentum tensor (EMT) of light nuclei in the impulse approximation, we evaluate the leading exchange corrections also upto momenta of the order of the nucleon mass. The exchange contributions to the EMT, are composed of the pair interaction, plus the seagull and the pion exchange interactions, modulo the recoil correction. The exchange contributions are shown to satisfy the current conservation requirement. These contributions are small compared to those from the impulse approximation for most of the deuteron gravitational form factors (GFFs), for momenta smaller than half of the nucleon mass. For larger momenta, the exchange contributions are significant for the deuteron A- and D-GFFs. We suggest that the pion GFFs can be extracted from the exchange contributions of select deuteron GFFs.

# Deuteron gravitational form factors: exchange currents

## Key claim

Exchange corrections to the deuteron gravitational form factors (GFFs) are small compared to the impulse approximation for momenta smaller than half the nucleon mass, but become significant for the deuteron A- and D-GFFs at larger momenta, suggesting that pion GFFs can be extracted from select deuteron GFF exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors evaluate the leading exchange corrections to the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of the deuteron using the Born approximation at tree level, including pair interaction, seagull, pion exchange, and recoil contributions. They perform a non-relativistic reduction of the relativistic exchange currents and check that the EMT conservation law is satisfied. The exchange contributions are then combined with the impulse approximation results from their previous work (He and Zahed, 2023) and evaluated using deuteron wavefunctions solved in that prior analysis.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.225 MeV; charge radius: 2.16 fm; magnetic moment: 0.857 in Bohr magnetons
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Exchange contributions are small for momenta k < 0.5 m_N (half the nucleon mass)
- The diffractive peak in the D_0 GFF shifts from about 0.9 GeV^2 (impulse approximation) to about 0.5 GeV^2 (seagull contribution) then to about 0.7 GeV^2 (pion exchange contribution)
- Pion GFF lattice parametrization: T_{1pi}^g(k) = 0.596/(1 + k^2/0.677 GeV^2); T_{1pi}^q(k) = 0.304/(1 + k^2/1.44 GeV^2); T_{2pi}^g(k) = 0.546/(1 + k^2/1.129 GeV^2); T_{2pi}^q(k) = 0.481/(1 + k^2/1.262 GeV^2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' recent impulse approximation analysis of deuteron GFFs (He and Zahed, 2023) by adding meson exchange current corrections, motivated by established results that exchange currents are not negligible for electromagnetic form factors of two- and three-nucleon systems at large momentum transfer (Hockert 1973, Chemtob 1974, Kloet 1973, Jackson 1975). The analysis uses the canonical pseudo-vector pion-nucleon coupling and leading-order chiral perturbation theory for the nucleon and pion GFFs. The results enable future extraction of pion GFFs from deuteron measurements and modify the deuteron pressure distribution obtained in the impulse approximation at small distances.
