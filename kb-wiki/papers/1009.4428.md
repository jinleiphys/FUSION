# Improved AdS/QCD Model with Matter

> We study an improved AdS/QCD model at finite temperature and chemical potential. An Ansatz for the beta-function for the boundary theory allows for the derivation of a charged dilatonic black hole in bulk. The solution is asymptotically RN-AdS in the UV and AdS2 * R3 in the IR. We discuss the thermodynamical aspects of the solution. The fermionic susceptibilities are shown to deviate from the free fermionic limits at asymptotic temperatures despite the asymptotically free nature of the gauge coupling at the boundary. The Polyakov line, the temporal and spatial string tensions dependence on both temperature and chemical potential are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an improved holographic AdS/QCD model at finite temperature and chemical potential where the dilaton potential is derived from an Ansatz for the beta-function of the boundary gauge theory, but the resulting fermionic susceptibilities deviate from free fermionic limits at asymptotic temperatures despite the asymptotically free nature of the gauge coupling, and this deviation only yields a meaningful comparison to QCD when the dilaton-gauge-field coupling parameter c is set to zero.

## Method

The authors start from a 5-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell action coupled to a dilaton field, using an Ansatz for the beta-function of the boundary theory to derive a charged dilatonic black hole solution in the bulk. They solve the Einstein equations together with the equation of motion for the U(1) gauge field, obtaining solutions for the warp factor, dilaton profile, and blackening function f(z) that depend on temperature and chemical potential. The thermodynamics, susceptibilities, Polyakov line, and string tensions are then computed from these solutions, with the scale Lambda fixed by requiring the pressure at zero chemical potential to vanish at a critical temperature T0 = 170 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature at zero chemical potential is set to T0 = 170 MeV, corresponding to Lambda = 321 MeV.
* A minimum chemical potential mu0 = 2.8 Lambda is required to obtain a black hole solution with zero temperature.
* The high temperature asymptotics of the susceptibilities for c=0 are: c2 approaches pi^2 (alpha/Lambda^4), c4 approaches (1/18)(alpha/Lambda^4), and c6 approaches (1/(540 pi^2))(alpha/Lambda^4).
* The ideal gas values for Nc=3, Nf=3 are: c2^(ideal gas) = Nc Nf/6, c4^(ideal gas) = Nc Nf/(12 pi^2), c6^(ideal gas) = 0.
* The ratio c4/c2 in the model is 1/(18 pi^2) and c6/c4 is 1/(30 pi^2), compared to ideal gas ratios c4/c2 = 1/(2 pi^2) and c6/c4 = 0.
* The constant alpha is fixed by fitting the high temperature asymptotic of c2 to the ideal gas value, yielding alpha = (3/(2 pi^2)) Lambda^4.

## Context

This work extends the improved holographic approach to finite temperature Yang-Mills developed by Gursoy et al. (2007) and Alanen et al. (2009) by adding a fermion chemical potential to account for baryon number. The model uses the construction of Alanen et al. (2009) where the dilaton field is directly tied to the running of the gauge coupling, making it more transparent physically. The results enable comparison with lattice studies of susceptibilities by Allton et al. (2005) and with PNJL model expectations by Ghosh et al. (2006), showing that the susceptibilities c2, c4, c6 have the correct shape around the critical temperature T0. The model also allows study of the Polyakov line and string tensions at finite chemical potential, which can be compared to future lattice data.
