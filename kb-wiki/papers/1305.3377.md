# Microscopic and self-consistent description for neutron halo in deformed nuclei

> A deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum has been developed for the study of neutron halos in deformed nuclei and the halo phenomenon in deformed weakly bound nuclei is investigated. Magnesium and neon isotopes are studied and some results are presented for the deformed neutron-rich and weakly bound nuclei 44Mg and 36Ne. The core of the former nucleus is prolate, but the halo has a slightly oblate shape. This indicates a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core. The generic conditions for the existence of halos in deformed nuclei and for the occurrence of this decoupling effect are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the deformed neutron-rich nucleus 44Mg, the core is prolate but the halo has a slightly oblate shape, indicating a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core; this decoupling is conditional on the quantum numbers of the main components of the single-particle orbits near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors developed a deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DefRHBC) by expanding the quasiparticle wave functions in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis and the potentials and densities in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed nuclei with spatial reflection symmetry. They solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently for magnesium and neon isotopes, using the parameter sets NL3 and PK1 for the mean field and a density-dependent zero-range force for pairing, with a box size of 20 fm and a mesh size of 0.1 fm.

## Key numbers

- 44Mg ground state has quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.32 and two-neutron separation energy S2n = 0.44 MeV.
- 36Ne has quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.52.
- The pairing force parameters used are ρsat = 0.152 fm−3 and V0 = 380 MeV·fm3.
- A cut-off energy Eq.p.cut = 60 MeV was applied in the quasiparticle space.
- The spherical box size was Rmax = 20 fm with mesh size Δr = 0.1 fm.
- An energy cutoff E+cut = 100 MeV was applied to truncate positive energy states in the Woods-Saxon basis.
- With NL3, 46Mg is the last bound magnesium isotope; with PK1, 42Mg is the last bound magnesium isotope.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, previously applied to spherical halo nuclei such as 11Li (Meng 1996) and used to predict giant halos (Meng 1998, 2002), to deformed systems. It builds on the Woods-Saxon basis method introduced by Zhou (2003) and on earlier deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov developments (Zhou 2010, Li 2012). The results enable experimental searches for shape decoupling effects in deformed halo nuclei, such as double-hump longitudinal momentum distributions in knockout reactions (Navin 1998, Sakharuk 1999) and new dipole modes.
