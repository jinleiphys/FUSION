# Constraining models of modified gravity with the double pulsar PSR J0737-3039A/B system

> In this paper we use Delta P = -1.772341 +/- 13.153788 s between the phenomenologically determined orbital period P_b of the PSR J0737-3039 double pulsar system and the purely Keplerian period P^(0)=2π\sqrt{a^3/G(m_A+m_B)} calculated with the system's parameters, determined independently of the third Kepler law itself, in order to put constraints on some models of modified gravity (f(R), Yukawa-like fifth force, MOND). The major source of error affecting Delta P is not the one in the phenomenologically measured period (δP_b=4 10^-6 s), but the systematic uncertainty δP^(0) in the computed Keplerian one due to the relative semimajor axis a mainly caused, in turn, by the errors in the ratio R of the pulsars' masses and in sin i. We get |κ|< 0.8 10^-26 m^-2 for the parameter that in the f(R) framework is a measure of the non linearity of the theory, |α|< 5.5 10^-4 for the fifth-force strength parameter (for λ\approx a=0.006 AU). The effects predicted by the strong-acceleration regime of MOND are far too small to be constrained with some effectiveness today and in the future as well. In view of the continuous timing of such an important system, it might happen that in the near future it will be possible to obtain somewhat tighter constraints.

## Key claim

The paper uses a discrepancy of ΔP = -1.772341 ± 13.153788 s between the phenomenologically measured orbital period of the double pulsar and the Keplerian period computed from independently determined system parameters to place upper bounds on modified gravity models, but the dominant uncertainty comes from the computed Keplerian period (≈10¹ s level) rather than the measured period (10⁻⁶ s level), and the MOND predictions are far too small to be constrained.

## Method

The authors compute the Keplerian orbital period P^(0) = 2π√(a³/G(m_A+m_B)) using the relative semimajor axis a derived from the projected semimajor axis x_A, the mass ratio R, and sin i, all determined independently of the third Kepler law. They then compare this to the phenomenologically measured orbital period P_b to obtain ΔP, and use this discrepancy to constrain the parameters of f(R) gravity, Yukawa-like fifth forces, and MOND by treating the modified gravity contributions as small radial perturbations to the Newtonian acceleration in a circular orbit approximation.

## Key numbers

- ΔP = -1.772341 ± 13.153788 s between the phenomenologically determined orbital period and the Keplerian period
- The phenomenologically measured orbital period P_b = 0.10225156248(5) days, with precision δP_b = 4 × 10⁻⁶ s
- The uncertainty in the Keplerian period is dominated by the semimajor axis uncertainty: δP^(0)|_a ≤ 12.218572 s, with total δP^(0) ≤ 13.153784 s
- For f(R) gravity: |κ| ≤ 0.8 × 10⁻²⁶ m⁻² (κ = (0.1 ± 0.8) × 10⁻²⁶ m⁻²), corresponding to |Λ| = (0.3 ± 2.5) × 10⁻²⁶ m⁻²
- For the f(R) Lagrangian f(R)=R-μ⁴/R: |μ| ≤ 10⁻²⁰ eV
- For Yukawa-like fifth force with λ ≈ a = 0.006 AU: |α| ≤ 5.5 × 10⁻⁴ (α ≈ 0.7 × 10⁻⁴)
- For MOND with interpolating function μ(x)=x/√(1+x²): predicted discrepancy H_{1/2,2} = (-1.610 ± 0.008) × 10⁻²² s
- For MOND with μ(x)=x/(1+x): predicted discrepancy H_{1,1} = (-1.431 ± 0.004) × 10⁻¹⁹ s
- Average Newtonian acceleration for the system: ⟨A_N⟩ = 446 m s⁻²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ruggiero (2006) and Allemandi (2007) for constraining f(R) gravity from Solar System observations to the double pulsar system PSR J0737-3039A/B, finding bounds of similar order of magnitude. The Yukawa-like fifth force constraints (|α| ≤ 5.5 × 10⁻⁴ at λ ≈ 0.006 AU) are orders of magnitude weaker than existing bounds from other experiments (|α| ≤ 10⁻⁹ at similar scales from Rey (2005)), and the MOND predictions are too small to be testable with this system. The analysis identifies the mass ratio R and sin i as the dominant sources of uncertainty, suggesting that continued timing of the system may yield tighter constraints in the future.
