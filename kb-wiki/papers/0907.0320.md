# Quarkonium in a viscous QGP

> I discuss viscosity corrections to thermal effects on the static QCD potential within hard-thermal loop resummed perturbation theory and for a strongly coupled, large-N_c conformal field theory dual to five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity. I also present model predictions for quarkonium binding energies in the deconfined phase and for suppression of R_{AA}(Upsilon --> e+ e-).

## Key claim

Viscosity corrections to the static QCD potential reduce thermal screening effects compared to an ideal plasma, leading to increased quarkonium binding energies; however, the dominant temperature dependence of binding energies in the phenomenologically relevant range \(T/T_C = 1-3\) comes from the decreasing continuum threshold \(V_\infty(T)\) rather than from the short-distance potential itself.

## Method

The static potential in an anisotropic plasma was derived by computing retarded and symmetric hard-thermal-loop resummed gluon propagators in the static limit, using a phase-space distribution that includes a viscosity-dependent anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) proportional to \(\eta/s\). A temperature- and viscosity-dependent interpolation between the short-distance potential and the continuum threshold \(V_\infty(T)\) was constructed, and bound states were obtained by solving the corresponding Schrödinger equation. For comparison, the strongly coupled regime was studied using gauge-gravity duality in a five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity dual to a large-\(N_c\) conformal field theory.

## Key numbers

- The string tension in SU(3) gauge theory is \(\sigma \approx 1\) GeV/fm.
- The continuum threshold \(F_\infty(T)\) for a static \(Q\bar{Q}\) pair at infinite separation is parameterized as \(F_\infty(T) \simeq a/T - bT\) with \(a \approx 0.08\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the 1P state of bottomonium, the splitting due to angular dependence in an anisotropic plasma is estimated to be on the order of 50 MeV; at \(T = 200\) MeV, the occupation number of states with \(\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{n}=0\) is about \(\exp(50/200) = 1.3\) times higher than that of states with \(\mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{n}=\pm1\).
- The width \(\Gamma_\Upsilon\) from the imaginary part of the potential is on the order of tens of MeV, compared to the electromagnetic decay width \(\Gamma_{\Upsilon\to e^+e^-} \approx 1\) keV.
- In the strongly coupled conformal theory dual to Gauss-Bonnet gravity, the free energy of a single static quark is \(F_Q = -(\sqrt{\lambda}/(1+\eta')) T\), where \(\eta' \equiv \sqrt{4\pi\,\eta/s} \simge 1\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of the thermal QCD potential (Laine 2006) and lattice results for the static quark free energy (LattF) by incorporating viscosity effects relevant to heavy-ion collisions. It also compares with strongly coupled results from \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills (Maldacena 1998, Rey 1998) and Gauss-Bonnet gravity (Noronha 2009). The findings enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium suppression in viscous QGP, particularly for \(\Upsilon \to e^+e^-\) in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Atomssa), and motivate future work on many-body interactions and broken conformal invariance to reproduce the QCD trace anomaly.
