# Theoretical investigation of two- and three-body short range correlations in inclusive electron scattering off nuclei at high momentum transfer

> The effects of short range correlations (SRC) in inclusive (A(e,e')X) and exclusive (A(e,e'N)X and A(e,e'2N)X) are reviewed. A new approach to the analysis of inclusive cross sections is illustrated, based upon the introduction of proper scaling functions and variables which incorporate the effects of two- and three-nucleon SRC. The approach is used to interpret the plateaux observed in the CLAS inclusive cross section ratios of 4He, 12C and 56Fe to 3He.

## Key claim

The plateaux observed in inclusive electron scattering cross section ratios of ⁴He, ¹²C, and ⁵⁶Fe to ³He at high momentum transfer can be interpreted as evidence of two-nucleon correlations (2NC) in the region 1.5 ≲ x_Bj ≲ 2 and three-nucleon correlations (3NC) in the region 2 ≲ x_Bj ≲ 3, provided that the inclusive cross section is dominated by absorption on correlated clusters of j nucleons and that the ratios differ only by a scale factor between complex nuclei and ³He.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of short range correlations (SRC) in nuclei, starting from the realistic many-body Schrödinger equation with two- and three-nucleon interactions. A cluster expansion approach with correlated wave functions is employed, where the trial nuclear wave function is written as a correlation operator acting on a shell model mean-field wave function, and expectation values are expanded in orders of the correlation function η_ij. The inclusive cross section ratio r(A,³He) is constructed from experimental data by taking the ratio of the A(e,e')X cross section to the ³He(e,e')X cross section, weighted by elementary electron-nucleon cross sections, and plotted as a function of the Bjorken scaling variable x_Bj.

## Key numbers

- Average nuclear density: ρ₀ ~ 0.17 N/fm³
- Average internucleon distance: ~ 1.7 fm (also given as 1.7–2 fm)
- Strong repulsive core of NN interaction sets in at relative distances of order 0.5–0.6 fm
- Fermi momentum of ¹²C: k_F = 0.221 GeV/c (also cited as ~ 0.230 GeV/c)
- In BNL experiment, 92⁺⁸₋₁₈ % of protons in ¹²C with momenta p_i ≥ 275 MeV/c are partners in n-p SRC pairs
- JLab experiment found SRC are mainly due to n-p pairs with probability of order 18 ± 5
- Kinematic condition for SRC investigation: Q² > 1 (GeV/c)² and x_Bj > 1
- Minimum recoil momentum exceeds average Fermi momentum at x_Bj > x_Bj⁰ (nucleus-dependent)
- Cross section ratios exhibit plateaux in regions 1.5 ≲ x_Bj ≲ 2 (2NC) and 2 ≲ x_Bj ≲ 3 (3NC)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Frankfurt and Strikman (mark1, FS) who proposed that at high momentum transfer the inclusive cross section can be written as a sum over correlated clusters of j particles, with probabilities a_j(A). The analysis interprets the CLAS inclusive cross section ratios measured by Egiyan et al. (Egiyan2, Egiyan) for ⁴He, ¹²C, and ⁵⁶Fe relative to ³He, building on earlier exclusive measurements at BNL (Aclander, Tang, Eli) and JLab (Subedi, Shneor) that established the dominance of n-p SRC pairs. The results enable extraction of per-nucleon probabilities of two- and three-nucleon SRC in complex nuclei relative to ³He, and support the universal nature of SRC independent of the probe.
