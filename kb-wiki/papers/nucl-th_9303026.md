# Boson-fermion mappings for odd systems from supercoherent states

> We extend the formalism whereby boson mappings can be derived from generalized coherent states to boson-fermion mappings for systems with an odd number of fermions. This is accomplished by constructing supercoherent states in terms of both complex and Grassmann variables. In addition to a known mapping for the full so(2$N$+1) algebra, we also uncover some other formal mappings, together with mappings relevant to collective subspaces.

## Key claim

The paper extends the coherent-state method for boson mappings to boson-fermion mappings for systems with an odd number of fermions by constructing supercoherent states using both complex and Grassmann variables; the resulting mappings reproduce the (anti)commutation relations of the so(2N+1) superalgebra only on the physical subspace of the ideal boson-fermion space, not on the full ideal space.

## Method

The authors construct supercoherent states for the so(2N) superalgebra by exponentiating a combination of bifermion operators (with complex coefficients Cμν) and single-fermion operators (with Grassmann variables φμ) acting on the fermion vacuum. They then define a generalized Usui operator that maps real fermion states to ideal boson-fermion states, and derive operator mappings by requiring that the functional images of states in the two spaces coincide. Similarity transformations of the form W = exp(T) are applied to the initial mapping to obtain alternative mappings, including one that entirely bosonizes even-fermion-number states.

## Key numbers

- The single j-shell has N = 2j+1 single-particle states.
- The so(2N) algebra consists of N(N-1)/2 bifermion operators (antisymmetric pairs).
- The supercoherent state depends on N(N-1)/2 complex numbers Cμν and on N complex Grassmann variables φμ.
- The similarity transformation that bosonizes even states is W = cosh(√(αμα ν Bμν)), where the square root denotes a power series containing only even powers of the argument.

## Context

This work extends the coherent-state approach to boson mappings (previously applied to even fermion systems) to odd fermion systems, complementing algebraic QBV results that had been obtained only for low-rank subalgebras such as su(2) (Scholtz and collaborators, 1976; Geyer and Hahne, 1983), su(3) (Klein and Walet, 1990), so(4) (Hahne and Klein, 1989), and so(5) (Klein, Walet, Geyer, and Hahne, 1991). The formalism enables systematic treatment of Pauli correlations between a bosonized core and surplus fermions, with direct relevance to the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) where an exchange term partially accommodates such correlations (Dobaczewski and collaborators, 1988).
