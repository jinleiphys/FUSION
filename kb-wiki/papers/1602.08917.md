# SU(3) Approach to Hypernuclear Interactions and Spectroscopy

> The $SU(3)$ meson exchange approach to interactions within the baryon octet and nuclear density functional theory are used to derive an \emph{ab initio} description of hypernuclear interactions. The density dependence of interactions is recast into a DFT with density dependent interaction vertices. The field-theoretical structure is retained by expressing the vertices as functionals of the matter field operators. Applications to infinite hypermatter and neutron star matter are discussed. A new approach is presented allowing to determine in-medium coupling constants out of the $NN$-vertex functionals, obtained e.g. by DBHF theory, for the full baryon octet by exploiting $SU(3)$ relations.

# SU(3) Approach to Hypernuclear Interactions and Spectroscopy

## Key claim
The authors present a covariant density functional theory (DFT) approach that uses SU(3) relations to extrapolate well-understood in-medium NN interactions from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) theory into the strangeness sector, thereby determining hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon coupling constants without fitting to YN scattering data. The approach is limited to mean-field producing scalar and vector mesons, and the authors caution that a pure SU(3) approach lacks a clearly defined natural scalar meson multiplet, requiring the introduction of a scalar nonet treated as sharp mass states despite inherent limitations.

## Method
The authors start from density-dependent NN vertices obtained from DBHF self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter and use SU(3) relations with octet-singlet mixing angles (determined from meson masses via Gell-Mann-Okubo relations) to derive in-medium coupling constants for the full baryon octet (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) in the vector and scalar channels. The density-dependent vertices are then used in relativistic mean-field calculations of infinite hypermatter and finite hypernuclei, solving the stationary Dirac equation with scalar and vector self-energies that include rearrangement terms from density derivatives of the coupling constants.

## Context
This work extends the density-dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) framework previously developed by Lenske, Fuchs, Hofmann, and collaborators (Lenske 1995, Fuchs 1995, Hofmann 2001, Keil 2002, Lenske 2004) to the full baryon octet using SU(3) symmetry, contrasting with the Nijmegen group (Nijmegen, Yama 2014) and Juelich group (Juelich) approaches that fit YN scattering data directly. The authors find that the naive quark-model scaling hypothesis (reducing Lambda coupling constants by factor 2/3) works reasonably well for isoscalar vertices but fails for isovector interactions, and they predict that the Xi interactions are dominated by isoscalar-singlet and isovector-octet channels, which may inform future studies of S=-2 hypernuclei. The approach enables systematic investigation of in-medium interactions across the baryon octet and can be tested against hypernuclear production reactions with leptonic, hadronic, and nuclear probes as discussed by Lenske (2015), Gaitanos (2014, 2016).
