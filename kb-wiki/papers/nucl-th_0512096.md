# Angular distributions of scattered excited muonic hydrogen atoms

> Differential cross sections of the Coulomb deexcitation in the collisions of excited muonic hydrogen with the hydrogen atom have been studied for the first time. In the framework of the fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling approach both the differential cross sections for the $nl \to n'l'$ transitions and $l$-averaged differential cross sections have been calculated for exotic atom in the initial states with the principle quantum number $n=2 - 6$ at relative motion energies $E_{\rm {cm}}=0.01 - 15$ eV and at scattering angles $θ_{\rm {cm}}=0 - 180^{\circ}$. The vacuum polarization shifts of the $ns$-states are taken into account. The calculated in the same approach differential cross sections of the elastic and Stark scattering are also presented. The main features of the calculated differential cross sections are discussed and a strong anisotropy of cross sections for the Coulomb deexcitation is predicted.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling calculations of differential cross sections for Coulomb deexcitation in muonic hydrogen-hydrogen collisions, predicting a strong anisotropy in these angular distributions, contradicting the common assumption in prior cascade calculations that the Coulomb deexcitation process results in isotropic angular distributions.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled differential equations of the close-coupling approach for the four-body Coulomb interaction of colliding muonic hydrogen and hydrogen atoms, using the Numerov method with standing-wave boundary conditions to obtain the real symmetric K-matrix and then the T-matrix. The basis states were expanded in terms of total angular momentum \(JM\) and parity \(\pi = (-1)^{l+L}\), and the vacuum polarization shifts of the \(ns\)-states (notably the \(2s-2p\) Lamb shift of 0.206 eV) were included. Differential cross sections were computed for individual \(nl \to n'l'\) transitions and for \(l\)-averaged cross sections over the initial principal quantum numbers \(n=2-6\), at center-of-mass energies \(E_{\rm cm}=0.01-15\) eV and scattering angles \(\theta_{\rm cm}=0-180^\circ\).

## Key numbers
- The \(2s-2p\) energy splitting \(\Delta \varepsilon_{2s-2p}\) for muonic hydrogen is 0.206 eV, decreasing approximately as \(n^{-3}\) with increasing \(n\).
- At \(E_{\rm cm}=0.01\) eV (referring to the \(2p\) threshold), the Coulomb deexcitation \(2p \to 1s\) transition is more than four orders of magnitude suppressed relative to the elastic \(2p\to 2p\) scattering, and about two orders of magnitude smaller than both the Stark \(2p\to 2s\) and Coulomb \(2s\to 1s\) transitions.
- At \(E_{\rm cm}=0.01\) eV, the Stark \(2p\to 2s\) and \(2s\to 2p\) transitions show a strong suppression in the forward hemisphere by more than three orders of magnitude when the kinetic energy is comparable to the Lamb shift.
- The \(l\)-averaged differential cross sections for elastic scattering at \(n=4\) can be approximately considered constant for simple estimation at scattering angles \(\theta_{\rm cm} \gtrsim 75^\circ\) at low energies (\(\lesssim 1\) eV).
- For Coulomb deexcitation, scattering at \(\theta_{\rm cm} \lesssim 60^\circ\) and \(\theta_{\rm cm} > 120^\circ\) is noticeably enhanced, showing strong anisotropy.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier close-coupling calculations of total cross sections for Coulomb deexcitation in muonic hydrogen (Pomerantsev and Popov, 2003) and pionic hydrogen (Pomerantsev and Popov, 2004) to the differential level, and provides the first theoretical study of Coulomb deexcitation angular distributions. It contradicts the isotropic angular distribution assumption used in prior cascade calculations (e.g., those by Markushin, 1994) and improves upon earlier semiclassical and adiabatic approaches (e.g., Korenman, 1980; Popov, 1995) that become invalid at low collisional energies where only a few partial waves contribute. The results are directly relevant to the analysis of muonic hydrogen Lamb shift experiments at the Paul Scherrer Institute and to the extraction of the proton charge radius with \(10^{-3}\) relative accuracy.
