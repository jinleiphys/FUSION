# Influence of triaxial deformation on wobbling motion in even-even nuclei

> The influence of triaxial deformation $γ$ on the purely collective form of wobbling motion in even-even nuclei are discussed based on the triaxial rotor model. It is found that the harmonic approximation is realized well when $γ=30^{\circ}$ for the properties of energy spectra and electric quadrupole transition probabilities, while this approximation gets bad when $γ$ deviates from $30^{\circ}$. A recent data from Coulomb excitation experiment, namely $3_1^+$ and $2_2^+$ for the $^{110}$Ru are studied and might be suggested as the bandhead of the wobbling bands. In addition, two types of angular momentum geometries for wobbling motion, stemming from different $γ$ values, are exhibited by azimuthal plots.

## Key claim

The harmonic approximation for wobbling motion in even-even nuclei works well only when the triaxial deformation parameter γ is close to 30°, and the quality degrades significantly as γ deviates from this value; the authors suggest that a probability of the K_m = I-n component larger than 50% can serve as a criterion to judge the quality of the harmonic approximation.

## Method

The authors used the triaxial rotor model (TRM) with hydrodynamic moments of inertia to systematically study wobbling excitation in even-even nuclei as a function of the triaxial deformation parameter γ. They compared the TRM results for energy spectra, wobbling energies, and electric quadrupole transition probabilities with those from the harmonic approximation (HA) formulas of Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The root mean square of the projection of total angular momentum on the intermediate axis (K_m) and azimuthal probability density plots were also calculated to analyze angular momentum geometry.

## Key numbers

- The wobbling frequency is smallest at γ = 30° and increases dramatically for γ < 10° or γ > 50°, as shown for I = 5, 13, 21 ħ with J₀ = 100 ħ² MeV⁻¹
- For γ = 30°, the relationship ⟨K_m²⟩^{1/2} = I - n is satisfied strictly for all phonon numbers n = 1, ..., n_max
- For γ = 25° at I = 12 ħ, the n=0 ground state has 98% |12⟩ component; at γ = 20°, this drops to 90% |12⟩; at γ = 15°, it drops to 76% |12⟩
- For γ = 25° at I = 12 ħ, the n=2 state has 81% |10⟩ component; at γ = 20°, the dominant component is only 35% |10⟩
- For the ¹¹⁰Ru comparison, the adopted parameters were J₀ ≈ 24 ħ² MeV⁻¹ and quadrupole moment Q ≈ 3.3 e b
- At γ = 30°, the transition energy for (0,2)→(0,0), (1,5)→(0,6), and (2,8)→(1,9) is 60 keV; similar patterns appear at 100 keV and 140 keV
- The energy difference [E(n, I+4) - E(n, I+2)] - [E(n, I+2) - E(n, I)] = 40 keV at γ = 30°

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of purely collective wobbling motion in even-even nuclei, originally introduced by Bohr and Mottelson (1975), which has been observed experimentally only in odd-A nuclei since 2001 (e.g., ¹⁶³Lu, ¹⁶¹Lu, ¹⁶⁵Lu, ¹⁶⁷Lu, ¹⁶⁷Ta). The authors compare with recent Coulomb excitation data for ¹¹⁰Ru from Doherty (2017) and suggest that the 3₁⁺ and 2₂⁺ states may be bandheads of one- and two-phonon wobbling bands. The work enables experimental searches for purely collective wobbling in even-even nuclei with stable triaxial deformation in the γ range of approximately 25° to 35°.
