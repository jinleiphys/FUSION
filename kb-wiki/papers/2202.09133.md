# Bjorken flow revisited: analytic and numerical solutions in flat space-time coordinates

> In this work we provide analytic and numerical solutions for the Bjorken flow, a standard benchmark in relativistic hydrodynamics providing a simple model for the bulk evolution of matter created in collisions between heavy nuclei. We consider relativistic gases of both massive and massless particles, working in a ( 2 + 1 ) and ( 3 + 1 ) Minkowski space-time coordinate system. The numerical results from a recently developed lattice kinetic scheme show excellent agreement with the analytic solutions.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytic solutions for the Bjorken flow in Minkowski space-time coordinates (rather than the usual Milne coordinates) for both massless and massive relativistic gases, and validates them against a Relativistic Lattice Boltzmann Method (RLBM) solver; the analytic solution for massive particles requires numerical integration of a differential equation and is thus semi-analytic.

## Method

The authors derive the ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations for the Bjorken flow in (2+1) and (3+1) Minkowski space-time, starting from the conservation laws in Milne coordinates and transforming back to flat coordinates. For massless particles (relativistic coldness zeta = 0), they obtain closed-form analytic expressions for density, energy density, pressure, and temperature; for massive particles (zeta > 0), they numerically integrate the resulting ordinary differential equation for temperature. They then simulate the flow using the Relativistic Lattice Boltzmann Method (RLBM), which solves the Anderson-Witting approximation of the relativistic Boltzmann equation on a Cartesian grid, and compare the numerical results to the analytic/semi-analytic solutions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 300 MeV, initial proper time tau0 = sqrt(t0^2 - z0^2) with t0 = 1 fm/c and domain length L = 1 fm
- Initial particle number density n0 = 1.5 fm^{-d} (where d = 2 or 3 is the number of spatial dimensions)
- Simulation runs from t0 = 1 fm/c to th = 5 fm/c, representing the QGP evolution time before hadronization
- Five values of relativistic coldness are tested: zeta = (0, 1, 2, 3, 4), corresponding to rest masses in the quark mass range at T0 = 300 MeV
- The spatial domain is discretized with 2000 grid points along the z dimension
- For the ultra-relativistic (zeta = 0) case in (3+1) dimensions, temperature scales as T(tau) = T0 (tau0/tau)^{1/3} and energy density as epsilon(tau) = epsilon0 (tau0/tau)^{4/3}

## Context

This work extends the classic Bjorken flow solution (Bjorken 1983) from Milne coordinates to flat Minkowski space-time coordinates, making it directly usable as a benchmark for numerical solvers that operate in Cartesian laboratory frame coordinates. The results complement existing benchmarks like the Riemann problem (Thompson 1986, Marti 1994) and the Gubser flow (Gubser 2010), and provide a validation test for the recently developed RLBM scheme (Gabbana 2020). The work enables future studies of elliptic flows (Snellings 2011) and Bjorken attractors (Blaizot 2021, Ambrus 2021) using lattice kinetic methods in flat coordinates.
