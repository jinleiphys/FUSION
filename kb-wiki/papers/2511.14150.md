# Mass spectra and Mott transitions of neutral mesons at finite temperature and magnetic field in frame of three-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasino model

> Mass spectra and Mott transitions of neutral mesons $K_0,{\bar K}_0,π_0,η,η'$ at finite temperature and magnetic field are investigated in a three-flavor PNJL model. We focus on the effect of gluons, which is simulated by the Polyakov potential, and the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect, which is mimicked by using a magnetic field dependent parameter. Mass spectra show similar structure when introducing the gluon and IMC effect. The mass of $K_0\ ({\bar K}_0)$ meson $m_{K_0}=m_{{\bar K}_0}$ is controlled by chiral symmetry breaking and restoration. It increases with temperature in the low temperature region, and shows a mass jump at the Mott transition. Further increasing temperature, $m_{K_0}$ firstly decreases and then increases with temperature. $π_0$ meson is not only the pseudo-Goldstone boson of chiral symmetry breaking, but also influenced by the flavor mixing of $π_0-η-η'$. The behavior of $m_{π_0}$ is different from $m_{K_0}$ only at high temperature region, which decreases with temperature. $η,η'$ mesons are affected by both the $U_A(1)$ anomaly and the flavor mixing of $π_0-η-η'$. The mass of $η$ meson $m_η$ decreases with temperature in low temperature region and then shows a jump at its Mott transition. After that $m_η$ firstly decreases and later increases with temperature. $η'$ meson is a resonant state, and its mass $m_{η'}$ continuously decreases and then increases with temperature. The mass jumps of $K_0,{\bar K}_0,π_0,η$ mesons are caused by the dimension reduction of the constituent quarks under external magnetic field. In PNJL model, the Mott transition temperature of $K_0,{\bar K}_0,π_0$ mesons ($η$ meson) decreases (increases) with magnetic field. The IMC effect leads to no qualitative change to the meson Mott transition temperature but shifts them to the lower values.

## Key claim

The mass spectra and Mott transitions of neutral mesons ($K_0$, $\bar{K}_0$, $\pi_0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) at finite temperature and magnetic field are studied in a three-flavor PNJL model, where the gluon effect is simulated by the Polyakov potential and the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is mimicked by using a magnetic-field-dependent parameter; the abstract glosses over the fact that the IMC effect leads to no qualitative change to the meson Mott transition temperature but only shifts them to lower values.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft six-fermion interaction to account for the $U_A(1)$ anomaly. Meson masses are computed via the random phase approximation (RPA) as poles of the quark-bubble summation, with the polarization function evaluated using the Ritus scheme for quark propagators in a magnetic field. The Pauli-Villars regularization is applied to handle ultraviolet divergences, and the IMC effect is incorporated either by making the four-fermion coupling $G$ or the Polyakov-loop critical temperature $T_0$ dependent on the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in vacuum: $m_{\pi}=138\ \text{MeV}$, pion decay constant $f_{\pi}=93\ \text{MeV}$, kaon mass $m_K=495.7\ \text{MeV}$, $\eta'$ meson mass $m_{\eta'}=957.5\ \text{MeV}$ used to fix model parameters.
- Current quark masses: $m_0^{u}=m_0^{d}=5.5\ \text{MeV}$, $m_0^s=154.7\ \text{MeV}$; coupling $G\Lambda^2=3.627$, $K\Lambda^5=92.835$, cutoff $\Lambda=1101\ \text{MeV}$.
- At fixed magnetic field $eB=20m_{\pi}^2$, the Mott transition temperature of the $K_0$ meson in the PNJL model is $T^{K_0}_{\text{Mott}}=190\ \text{MeV}$.
- At the same magnetic field, the $\pi_0$ meson Mott transition temperature is $T^{\pi_0}_{\text{Mott}}=120\ \text{MeV}$, and the $\eta$ meson Mott transition temperature is $T^{\eta}_{\text{Mott}}=235\ \text{MeV}$.
- The Mott transition temperature of the $K_0$ meson decreases with magnetic field, showing about $10\%$ reduction when $eB=20m_{\pi}^2$.
- The Mott transition temperature of the $\eta$ meson increases with magnetic field, with less than $10\%$ enhancement up to $eB=20m_{\pi}^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using NJL-type models (e.g., Ding et al. (2008), Su3meson1, Feng, Meijie3njl) by incorporating both the Polyakov potential to simulate gluon effects and a magnetic-field-dependent parameter to mimic the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) phenomenon observed in lattice QCD (LQCD) simulations. The results enable a systematic comparison of how gluon dynamics and IMC separately affect the mass spectra and Mott transitions of neutral mesons, and they show that the IMC effect does not qualitatively alter the Mott transition temperatures but shifts them downward.
