# Polarization of Tau Leptons Produced in Quasielastic Neutrino--Nucleon Scattering

> A numerical analysis of the polarization vector of tau leptons produced through quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino interactions with free nucleons is given with two models for vector electromagnetic form factors of proton and neutron. The impact of G parity violating axial and vector second-class currents is investigated by applying a simple heuristic model for the induced scalar and tensor form factors.

## Key claim

The polarization of tau leptons produced in quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering is sensitive to the choice of vector electromagnetic form factor model (dipole vs. GKex(02S)) for antineutrino-induced reactions but not for neutrino-induced ones, and the impact of second-class currents (SCC) is not negligible in kinematic regions where the cross section is large, though SCC contributions are not the main source of systematic uncertainty for oscillation experiments.

## Method

The authors computed the polarization vector components (longitudinal, perpendicular, and transverse) of tau leptons from quasielastic ν_τ n → τ^- p and \bar{ν}_τ p → τ^+ n reactions using covariant formulas from Kuzmin (2003). They employed two models for the vector electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron, the dipole model with M_V = 0.84 GeV/c^2 and the GKex(02S) model by Lomon (2002), and a heuristic dipole parameterization for the induced scalar and tensor form factors to explore SCC effects, with constraints from the BNL AGS experiment (Ahrens, 1988) and nuclear beta decay studies (Wilkinson, 2000-2001).

## Key numbers

- The physically allowed scattering angle for tau leptons is limited to θ ≤ arcsin(M/m_τ) ≈ 31.9°.
- The axial form factor at zero momentum transfer is F_A(0) = g_A = -1.2695 ± 0.0029.
- The weighted average of the axial mass from experimental data is M_A = 1.026 ± 0.021 GeV/c^2, with individual measurements ranging from roughly 0.6 to 1.2 GeV/c^2.
- The BNL AGS 90% C.L. upper limit on the axial SCC strength ξ_T ranges from about 0.78 at M_T = 0.5 GeV/c^2 to about 0.11 at M_T = 1.5 GeV/c^2, assuming CVC and the dipole model.
- The BNL AGS upper limit on the vector SCC strength is ξ_S < 1.8 at M_S = 1.0 GeV/c^2, assuming ξ_T = 0.
- The reduction factor for the scalar form factor contribution to muon production is (m_μ/M)^2 ≈ 0.01, while for tau production it is (m_τ/M)^2 ≈ 3.6.

## Context

This work extends the covariant formalism for lepton polarization developed by Kuzmin (2003) and the noncovariant Standard Model results of Hagiwara (2003) to include SCC effects in tau production. It contrasts with the muon case, where SCC contributions are negligible, and shows that the tau polarization is sensitive to vector form factor models for antineutrino reactions, unlike the neutrino case. The results enable future neutrino oscillation experiments (e.g., those studying ν_μ-ν_τ mixing) to assess systematic uncertainties from form factor models and SCC, while also providing a framework for testing CVC violation and T invariance in quasielastic tau production.
