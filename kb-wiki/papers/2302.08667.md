# Neutron skin thickness of $^{116,118,120,122,124}$Sn determined from reaction cross sections of proton scattering

> The cross sections of SDR in the Sb isotopes have been measured. Within the model used, the neutron-skin thicknesses $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$ deduced $0.12 \pm 0.06$fm for $^{116}$Sn, $0.13 \pm 0.06$fm for $^{118}$Sn, $0.18 \pm 0.07$fm for $^{120}$Sn, $0.22 \pm 0.07$fm for $^{122}$Sn, $0.19 \pm 0.07$fm for $^{124}$Sn. We tested the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering, and found that the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model is reliable for reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}$ in $30 < E_{\rm in} < 100 $MeV and $250 < E_{\rm in} < 400$MeV. We determine neutron skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$, using measured $σ_{\rm R}$ of $^{4}$He+$^{116,120,224}$Sn scattering. The results are $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})=0.242 \pm 0.140$fm for $^{116}$Sn, $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})=0.377 \pm 0.140$fm for $^{120}$Sn, $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})=0.180 \pm 0.142$fm for $^{124}$Sn. The $σ_{\rm R}$ are available for proton scattering on $^{116,118,120,122,124}$Sn with high accuracy. Our aim is to determine $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$ for $^{116,118,120,122,124}$Sn with small errors by using the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model. Our model is the folding model with the densities scaled from the D1S-GHFB+AMP neutron density. The proton radii of D1S-GHFB+AMP agree with those calculated with the isotope shift method based on the electron scattering. We then scale the neutron densities so as to reproduce the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$. In $30 < E_{\rm in} < 65$MeV, we determine $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$ from measured $σ_{\rm R}$. The values are $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})=0.118 \pm 0.021$~fm for $^{116}$Sn, $0.112 \pm 0.021$fm for $^{118}$Sn, $0.124 \pm 0.021$fm for $^{120}$Sn, $0.156 \pm 0.022$fm for $^{124}$Sn. As for $^{122}$Sn, the skin value in $30 < E_{\rm in} < 50$MeV is $0.122 \pm 0.024$fm. Our results are consistent with the previous values.

## Key claim

The neutron-skin thicknesses for five tin isotopes (116,118,120,122,124Sn) can be determined from proton scattering reaction cross sections using the Kyushu g-matrix folding model with D1S-GHFB+AMP densities, yielding values with smaller uncertainties than previous determinations from alpha-particle scattering, though the results depend on the specific incident-energy range used (30-65 MeV for most isotopes, 30-50 MeV for 122Sn).

## Method

The authors used the Kyushu g-matrix folding model, a localized chiral N3LO two-nucleon force plus NNLO three-nucleon force g-matrix, to calculate reaction cross sections for proton scattering on 116,118,120,122,124Sn targets. They started with D1S-GHFB+AMP (Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with angular momentum projection) proton and neutron densities, verified that the calculated proton radii agreed with electron-scattering isotope-shift values, then scaled the neutron densities by a factor f (the average ratio of experimental to calculated reaction cross sections over the incident energy range) to reproduce the measured reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness for 116Sn: 0.118 ± 0.021 fm (from p+116Sn scattering, 30-65 MeV)
- Neutron-skin thickness for 118Sn: 0.112 ± 0.021 fm (from p+118Sn scattering, 30-65 MeV)
- Neutron-skin thickness for 120Sn: 0.124 ± 0.021 fm (from p+120Sn scattering, 30-65 MeV)
- Neutron-skin thickness for 122Sn: 0.122 ± 0.024 fm (from p+122Sn scattering, 30-50 MeV)
- Neutron-skin thickness for 124Sn: 0.156 ± 0.022 fm (from p+124Sn scattering, 30-65 MeV)
- Scaling factor f for 120Sn: 1.04711 (average of sigma_R(exp)/sigma_R(D1S) over 30.2-65.5 MeV)
- Scaling factors for other isotopes: 1.02447 (116Sn), 1.05118 (118Sn), 1.04881 (122Sn), 1.06002 (124Sn)
- Matter radius for 120Sn: 4.655 ± 0.021 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the Kyushu g-matrix folding model to determine neutron-skin thickness from reaction cross sections, following their successful determination for 208Pb (0.278 ± 0.035 fm, Tagami 2020) and earlier work on 4He scattering on Sn isotopes (Matsuzaki 2021) which gave larger uncertainties. The results are consistent with previous determinations from isovector spin-dipole resonance measurements (Krasznahorkay 1999) and, for 120Sn, with the electric dipole polarizability result (0.148 ± 0.034 fm, Hashimoto 2015). The smaller uncertainties compared to the 4He scattering results demonstrate that proton scattering data with 2-3% accuracy provide more precise neutron-skin determinations, enabling better constraints on the symmetry energy and neutron matter equation of state.
