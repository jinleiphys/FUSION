# Neutrino-deuteron scattering: Uncertainty quantification and new $L_{1,A}$ constraints

> We study neutral- and charged-current (anti)neutrino-induced dissociation of the deuteron at energies from threshold up to 150 MeV by employing potentials, as well as one- and two-body currents, derived in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). We provide uncertainty estimates from $χ$EFT truncations of the electroweak current, dependences on the $χ$EFT cutoff and variations in the pool of fit data used to fix the low-energy constants of $χ$EFT. At 100 MeV of incident (anti)neutrino energy, these uncertainties amount to about 2-3\% and are smaller than the sensitivity of the cross sections to the single-nucleon axial form factor, which amounts to 5\% if one varies the range of the nucleon axial radius within the bands determined by recent lattice quantum chromodynamics evaluations and phenomenological extractions. We conclude that a precise determination of the nucleon axial form factor is required for a high-precision calculation of the neutrino-deuteron cross sections at energies higher than 100 MeV. By matching our low-energy $χ$EFT results to those of pionless effective field theory ($\cancelπ$EFT), we provide new constraints for the counterterm $L_{1,A}$ that parameterizes the strength of the axial two-body current in $\cancelπ$EFT. We obtain a value of $4.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5}\mathrm{fm}^3$ at renormalization scale set to pion mass, which is compatible with, albeit narrower than, previous experimental determinations, and comparable to a recent lattice quantum chromodynamics calculation.

## Key claim

The authors provide uncertainty estimates for neutrino-deuteron cross sections from chiral effective field theory truncations, cutoff dependences, and fit-data variations, finding that at 100 MeV incident (anti)neutrino energy these theoretical uncertainties (2-3%) are smaller than the sensitivity to the single-nucleon axial form factor (5%), implying that precise knowledge of the axial form factor is required for high-precision calculations above 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute neutral- and charged-current (anti)neutrino-induced deuteron dissociation cross sections using chiral effective field theory (χEFT) potentials and one- and two-body currents, with the operators expressed as multipole expansions. They employ the NNLOsim family of 42 different χEFT potentials spanning seven cutoff values and six truncations of the NN scattering database, and also use the EM500 interaction for benchmarking. By matching their low-energy χEFT results to pionless effective field theory (π̸EFT), they extract new constraints on the counterterm L1,A that parameterizes the axial two-body current in π̸EFT.

## Key numbers

- At 100 MeV incident (anti)neutrino energy, χEFT truncation uncertainties amount to about 2-3%
- Sensitivity of cross sections to the single-nucleon axial form factor at 100 MeV is 5% when varying the nucleon axial radius within lattice QCD and phenomenological bands
- New constraint on L1,A: 4.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5} fm³ at renormalization scale set to the pion mass
- Cross section values for ν NC at 100 MeV: 2.71 × 10⁻¹⁴ fm² (EM500/1B+2B/(Q/Λb)⁰)
- Cross section values for ν CC at 100 MeV: 6.69 × 10⁻¹⁴ fm² (EM500/1B+2B/(Q/Λb)⁰)
- Cross section values for ν̄ NC at 100 MeV: 1.67 × 10⁻¹⁴ fm² (EM500/1B+2B/(Q/Λb)⁰)
- Cross section values for ν̄ CC at 100 MeV: 2.71 × 10⁻¹⁴ fm² (EM500/1B+2B/(Q/Λb)⁰)

## Context

This work extends the χEFT calculations of neutrino-deuteron scattering by Baroni and Schiavilla (2017) by providing more complete uncertainty quantification using the NNLOsim family of 42 potentials and by investigating sensitivity to the nucleon axial radius. The new L1,A constraint is compatible with previous experimental determinations but narrower, and comparable to a recent lattice QCD calculation; this LEC is a major source of uncertainty in π̸EFT calculations of nuclear weak processes such as the proton-proton fusion reaction in the sun. The results enable improved predictions for neutrino experiments using deuterium detectors (such as SNO) and provide input for constraining two-body currents in light-nuclei weak processes.
