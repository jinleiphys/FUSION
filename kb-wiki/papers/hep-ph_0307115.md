# Cutoff schemes in chiral perturbation theory and the quark mass expansion of the nucleon mass

> We discuss the use of cutoff methods in chiral perturbation theory. We develop a cutoff scheme based on the operator structure of the effective field theory that allows to suppress high momentum contributions in Goldstone boson loop integrals and by construction is free of the problems traditional cutoff schemes have with gauge invariance or chiral symmetries. As an example, we discuss the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass. Contrary to other claims in the literature we show that the mass of a nucleon in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory has a well behaved chiral expansion up to effective Goldstone boson masses of 400 MeV when one utilizes standard dimensional regularization techniques. With the help of the here developed cutoff scheme we can demonstrate a well-behaved chiral expansion for the nucleon mass up to 600 MeV of effective Goldstone Boson masses. We also discuss in detail the prize, in numbers of additional short distance operators involved, that has to be paid for this extended range of applicability of chiral perturbation theory with cutoff regularization, which is usually not paid attention to. We also compare the fourth order result for the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass with lattice results and draw some conclusions about chiral extrapolations based on such type of representation.

## Key claim

Contrary to claims in the literature that heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with dimensional regularization fails for effective Goldstone boson masses above 400 MeV, the authors show that the nucleon mass expansion is well behaved up to 400 MeV in dimensional regularization and can be extended to 600 MeV using a novel cutoff scheme, provided one includes additional short-distance operators that are usually neglected.

## Method

The authors compare dimensional regularization and a non-covariant radial cutoff regularization for the leading one-loop (third order) calculation of the nucleon mass in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. They then propose an improvement scheme that adds a specific fourth-order operator from the effective Lagrangian to cancel the leading 1/Λ cutoff dependence, thereby extending the range of applicability. The fourth-order result is also computed and compared with lattice data.

## Key numbers

- The leading one-loop HBCHPT result for the nucleon mass breaks down around mπ = 400 MeV when using dimensional regularization.
- With the improved cutoff scheme, a well-behaved chiral expansion is demonstrated up to effective Goldstone boson masses of 600 MeV.
- The physical pion mass is taken as mπ = 138 MeV, the pion decay constant as Fπ = 92.4 MeV, and the axial-vector coupling as gA = 1.267.
- The dimension-two coupling c1 is given as c1 = -0.9 ± 0.5 GeV⁻¹, and the chiral limit nucleon mass M0 is taken as 0.88 GeV to reproduce the physical nucleon mass.
- The isovector Dirac radius empirical value is (r1v)² = 0.585 fm².

## Context

This work extends the discussion of cutoff methods in chiral perturbation theory, which has a history going back to Gasser and Zepeda (1980s) and was recently advocated by the Adelaide group (Leinweber et al.) for nucleon mass extrapolations. The authors argue that the improved cutoff scheme, which uses operators already present in the effective Lagrangian, provides a systematic alternative to model-dependent form factor approaches. The results enable more reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data for the nucleon mass up to pion masses of 600–700 MeV, though the authors caution against applying the formalism above that range.
