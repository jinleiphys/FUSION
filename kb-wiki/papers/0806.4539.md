# Hydrodynamic radial and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions from AGS to LHC energies

> Using ideal relativistic hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions, we study the collision energy dependence of radial and elliptic flow, of the emitted hadron spectra, and of the transverse momentum dependence of several hadronic particle ratios, covering the range from Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) to Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. These calculations establish an ideal fluid dynamic baseline that can be used to assess non-equilibrium features manifest in future LHC heavy-ion experiments. Contrary to earlier suggestions we find that a saturation and even decrease of the differential elliptic flow v_2(p_T) with increasing collision energy cannot be unambiguously associated with the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier suggestions, a saturation and even decrease of the differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) with increasing collision energy cannot be unambiguously associated with the QCD phase transition; this decrease occurs even when all elliptic flow is generated far above the phase transition and is instead driven by increased radial flow pushing hadrons to larger transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the AZHYDRO code, assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They simulated Au+Au collisions across a range of collision energies from AGS to LHC, using the initial peak entropy density \(s_0\) as a proxy for collision energy and scaling the initial thermalization time \(\tau_0\) via a quantum uncertainty relation \(T_0 \tau_0 = \text{const}\). The initial conditions used a Glauber model with 75% soft and 25% hard contributions, and the equation of state matched an ideal gas of quarks and gluons at \(T_c = 164\) MeV to a hadron resonance gas with freeze-out at \(T_{\text{dec}} = 100\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial peak entropy density at RHIC ( \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV/nucleon pair): \(s_0 = 117\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- Critical temperature for the QCD phase transition: \(T_c = 164\) MeV.
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_{\text{dec}} = 100\) MeV.
- Linear fit for charged particle multiplicity: \(dN_{\text{ch}}/d\eta = 312.5 \log_{10}\sqrt{s} - 64.8\) (with \(\sqrt{s}\) in GeV).
- Highest initial peak entropy density simulated for LHC: \(s_0 = 271\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Impact parameter for elliptic flow simulations: \(b = 7\) fm.
- The \(p_T\)-integrated elliptic flow for pions increases by less than 10% from RHIC to LHC in ideal hydrodynamics, whereas hybrid models including hadronic viscosity show about a 25% increase.

## Context

This work extends the ideal fluid dynamical baseline calculations of Kolb (1999) and Heinz (2004, 2005) to cover the full energy range from AGS to LHC, providing predictions for LHC heavy-ion experiments. It contradicts earlier speculations by Heinz (2004, 2005) and Lacey (2007) that non-monotonicity in \(v_2(p_T)\) could serve as a signature of the QCD phase transition. The calculations complement similar ideal fluid predictions for LHC by Eskola (2005) and Niemi (2008), but go beyond them by systematically investigating the beam energy dependence. The results enable future LHC experiments to assess deviations from ideal fluid behavior and to separate collective flow effects from more exotic explanations such as baryon junctions.
