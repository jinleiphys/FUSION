# Signatures of the Color Glass Condensate in J/Psi production off nuclear targets

> We consider the J/Psi production in proton (deuteron) -- nucleus collisions at high energies. We argue that the production mechanism in this case is different from that in pp collisions due to gluon saturation in the nucleus and formation of the Color Glass Condensate. At forward rapidities (in the proton fragmentation region), the production of J/Psi is increasingly suppressed both as a function of rapidity and centrality. On the other hand, at backward rapidities at RHIC (in the fragmentation region of the nucleus) the coherent effects lead to a modest enhancement of the production cross section, with the nuclear modification factor R(J/Psi) increasing with centrality. We find that the J/Psi production cross section exhibits at forward rapidities the limiting fragmentation scaling established previously for soft processes; in the energy range studied experimentally, it manifests itself as an approximate "xF scaling".

## Key claim

The paper argues that J/Psi production in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions at high energies is governed by gluon saturation in the nucleus and the Color Glass Condensate, leading to a production mechanism fundamentally different from that in proton-proton collisions; the authors claim that at forward rapidities the production is increasingly suppressed with rapidity and centrality, while at backward rapidities at RHIC a modest enhancement of the nuclear modification factor occurs, with the caveat that these predictions depend on the relation between the saturation scale Q_s and the J/Psi mass M_psi.

## Method

The authors derive the J/Psi production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate framework, employing the eikonal approximation for the propagation of a c-cbar color dipole through the nucleus at forward rapidities where the production time exceeds the nuclear size. They compute the scattering amplitude for the valence quark-gluon-c-cbar system in the large N_c limit, using light-cone wave functions for the valence quark, virtual gluon, and J/Psi meson, and incorporate multiple rescatterings via exponentiation of the single-scattering amplitude. The total cross section per unit rapidity is obtained by integrating over transverse momentum and using a non-relativistic approximation for the J/Psi wave function, with quantum evolution effects parametrized through an energy-dependent saturation scale Q_s^2(s,y) and an anomalous dimension gamma.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV), the production time tau_P at forward rapidities y>1 is approximately 15 e^y fm, and the J/Psi formation time tau_F is approximately 41 e^y fm, both exceeding the nuclear interaction time tau_int ~ R_A/c.
- The longitudinal form factor F_A^2(q_z) for a Gaussian nuclear density is e^{-R_A^2 q_z^2/3}, with corrections to the dipole model of about 10% at y=0 at RHIC and 50% at y=-1.
- The saturation scale is parametrized as Q_s^2(s,y) = Lambda^2 A^{1/3} (s/Lambda^2)^{lambda/2} e^{lambda y}, with Lambda = 0.63 GeV fixed by DIS data and lambda approx 0.25.
- The leptonic width of J/Psi used is Gamma_ee approx 5.26 KeV.
- The J/Psi proper formation time from spectral representation is 0.45 fm.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach to heavy quarkonium production, building on prior studies of heavy quark production in strong color fields by Kharzeev and Tuchin (2004) and others, and addresses the long-standing puzzle of the lack of x_2 scaling in J/Psi production off nuclei observed in fixed-target experiments at CERN (Badier 1983) and FNAL (Leitch 1999) and more dramatically at RHIC (Adler 2005). The paper explains the approximate x_F scaling seen in SPS and FNAL data as a consequence of gluon saturation and predicts that at LHC energies, hidden parton scaling (scaling in x_1) will become a universal feature when the proton wave function also saturates. The results enable future tests of the Color Glass Condensate picture through measurements of the nuclear modification factor R_{pA}(J/Psi) as a function of rapidity and centrality at RHIC and LHC.
