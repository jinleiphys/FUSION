# Isospin dependent thermodynamics of fragmentation

> The thermal and phase properties of a multifragmentation model which uses clusters as degrees of freedom, are explored as a function of isospin. A good qualitative agreement is found with the phase diagram of asymmetric nuclear matter as established by different mean-field models. In particular, from the convexity properties of the nuclear entropy, we show that uncharged finite nuclei display first and second order liquid-gas-like phase transitions. Different quantities are examined to connect the thermal properties of the system to cluster observables. In particular we show that fractionation is only a loose indication of phase coexistence. A simple analytical formula is proposed and tested to evaluate the symmetry (free) energy from the widths of isotopic distributions. Assuming that one may restore the isotopic composition of break-up fragments, it is found that some selected isotopic observables can allow to quantitatively access the freeze-out symmetry energy in multifragmentation experiments.

# Isospin dependent thermodynamics of fragmentation

## Key claim

The paper shows that the liquid-gas-like phase transition observed in finite nuclear systems (without Coulomb) qualitatively agrees with asymmetric nuclear matter predictions from mean-field models, but that isospin fractionation, often taken as a signature of phase coexistence, is actually a generic feature of thermal fragmentation that can appear even in the super-critical region, and therefore cannot unambiguously signal phase coexistence in finite systems.

## Method

The authors use the Microcanonical Multifragmentation Model (MMM), a statistical model that treats nuclear clusters as degrees of freedom, to simulate 200-nucleon systems with varying neutron-proton ratios at excitation energies between 2 and 10 MeV/nucleon. They work in a constant-λ (isobar-like) microcanonical ensemble where volume fluctuations are controlled by a Lagrange parameter λ conjugate to volume, and they switch off the Coulomb interaction to isolate isospin effects. Phase diagrams are constructed from the convexity properties of the entropy surface and Maxwell constructions on constant-λ caloric curves.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature decreases monotonically with increasing isospin asymmetry; for a 200-nucleon system, the coexistence region at λ = 3 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³ still exists for Z/A = 0.35 but is clearly super-critical for Z/A = 0.25.
- At an excitation energy of 6 MeV/nucleon and average freeze-out volume ⟨V⟩ = 6V₀ (where V₀ is normal nuclear density volume), the neutron multiplicity ranges from 6.71 (symmetric source (200,100)) to 32.7 (most neutron-rich source (200,50)), while proton multiplicity ranges from 6.71 to 0.18 for the same sources.
- The isospin variance σ_I²(A) is related to the symmetry energy coefficient by σ_I²(A) ≈ AT / [2 C_sym(A)], and this relation is well verified for small fragment masses across all asymmetries studied.
- Finite size effects are estimated to be small enough to be safely negligible for most analyzed multifragmentation reactions, based on comparing 200-nucleon systems with systems 50% larger (300,150) and 50% smaller (100,50).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the phase diagram of finite nuclei using the MMM model (Raduta, 2002), which focused on Coulomb effects, by now concentrating on isospin dependence in neutral systems. It provides the first systematic study of the isospin dependence of the fragmentation phase diagram in a finite-nucleus statistical model, complementing nuclear matter calculations from mean-field models (Mueller and Serot, 1995; Lee and Mekjian, 2003; Gulminelli, 2004). The results enable experimental efforts to extract the freeze-out symmetry energy from isotopic distributions in multifragmentation experiments, but caution that fractionation alone cannot be used as a signature of phase coexistence.
