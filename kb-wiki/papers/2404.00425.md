# Thermal production of charm quarks in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the thermal production of charm quarks in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) created in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. Our study is based on the off-shell parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach describing the full time evolution of heavy-ion collisions on a microscopic basis with hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom. The sQGP is realized within the effective dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) which is adjusted to reproduce the lattice QCD results for the thermodynamic observables of the sQGP. Relying on the fact that the DQPM successfully describes the spatial diffusion coefficients $D_s$ from the lQCD, which control the interaction of charm quarks with thermal partons (expressed in terms of strongly interacting off-shell quasiparticles), we evaluate the production of charm quark pairs through the rotation of Feynman diagrams such that the incoming charm quark and outgoing light parton in elastic scattering diagrams are exchanged. The charm quark annihilation is realized by detailed balance. We find that the number of produced thermal charm quark pairs strongly depends on the charm quark mass in the QGP. While for the heavy charm quarks of mass $m_c=1.8$ GeV it is subdominant compared to the primary charm production by binary nucleon-nucleon collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, the numbers of primary and thermal charm quarks become comparable for a smaller (bare) $m_c=1.2$ GeV. Compared with the experimental data on the $R_{\rm AA}$ of $D$-mesons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, it is more favorable for charm quarks in the QGP to gain additional mass due to thermal effects rather than to have a low bare mass.

## Key claim

The number of thermally produced charm quark pairs in the quark-gluon plasma strongly depends on the assumed charm quark mass; for a heavy mass of 1.8 GeV thermal production is subdominant compared to primary production at RHIC and LHC energies, but for a smaller bare mass of 1.2 GeV the numbers become comparable. Comparison with experimental D-meson R_AA data favors charm quarks gaining additional mass due to thermal effects rather than having a low bare mass.

## Method

The authors used the off-shell parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which describes the full time evolution of heavy-ion collisions with hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom. The strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) was modeled using the effective dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), adjusted to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamic observables. Thermal charm quark pair production was evaluated by rotating Feynman diagrams for elastic charm quark scattering, exchanging incoming charm quarks with outgoing light partons, and charm annihilation was included via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

* Thermal charm production per unit rapidity at midrapidity: about 0.15 pairs in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and about 2.1 pairs in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, for a charm quark mass of 1.5 GeV.
* The number of thermal charm quarks at midrapidity is about 6.5% of the number of initially produced charm quarks at RHIC and 18% at the LHC, for m_c = 1.5 GeV.
* The charm quark mass was varied from 1.2 GeV (close to bare QCD mass) to 1.8 GeV (close to D meson mass) to produce the upper and lower limits of the R_AA bands.
* Off-shell effects of light partons enhance the charm production rate from gluon fusion by about 7%, while enhancement from quark annihilation is less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy flavor dynamics in the PHSD approach by Song et al. (2015) and Song et al. (2016), which did not include thermal production or annihilation of charm quarks. It contrasts with pQCD calculations by Zhang et al. (2007) that included 2-to-3 reactions and found larger production rates, but the authors note that in the DQPM framework 2-to-3 processes are suppressed due to the large mass of emitted gluons. The results enable more complete comparisons with experimental D-meson R_AA data from the STAR and ALICE collaborations and help constrain the effective charm quark mass in the QGP.
