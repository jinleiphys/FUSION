# A perturbative QCD study of dijets in p+Pb collisions at the LHC

> Inspired by the recent measurements of the CMS collaboration, we report a QCD study of dijet production in proton+lead collisions at the LHC involving large-transverse-momentum jets, $p_T \gtrsim 100$ GeV. Examining the inherent uncertainties of the next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and their sensitivity to the free proton parton distributions (PDFs), we observe a rather small, typically much less than 5% clearance for the shape of the dijet rapidity distribution within approximately 1.5 units around the midrapidity. Even a more stable observable is the ratio between the yields in the positive and negative dijet rapidity, for which the baseline uncertainty can be made negligible by imposing a symmetric jet rapidity acceptance. Both observables prove sensitive to the nuclear modifications of the gluon distributions, the corresponding uncertainties clearly exceeding the estimated baseline uncertainties from the free-proton PDFs and scale dependence. From a theoretical point of view, these observables are therefore very suitable for testing the validity of the collinear factorization and have a high potential to provide precision constraints for the nuclear PDFs.

## Key claim

The shape of the dijet rapidity distribution in p+Pb collisions at the LHC has a baseline theoretical uncertainty typically much less than 5% within approximately 1.5 units around midrapidity, and the forward-to-backward ratio can be made essentially free of baseline uncertainty by imposing a symmetric jet rapidity acceptance, making both observables sensitive to nuclear modifications of gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations using the MEKS jet code adapted for nuclear collisions, with CT10NLO free proton PDFs as baseline and nuclear modifications from EPS09, DSSZ, and HKN07 parametrizations. They computed dijet cross sections in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV with leading jet pT >= 120 GeV, subleading jet pT >= 30 GeV, pseudorapidity acceptance |eta| < 3, and anti-kT jet algorithm with R=0.3, then studied the normalized dijet spectrum and forward-to-backward ratio.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV for proton-nucleon collisions
- Leading jet transverse momentum cut: pT^min,leading = 120 GeV
- Subleading jet transverse momentum cut: pT^min,subleading = 30 GeV
- Pseudorapidity acceptance for individual jets: |eta^leading, subleading| < 3
- Jet algorithm distance parameter: R = 0.3
- Minimum azimuthal separation between jets: Delta phi > 2pi/3
- Center-of-mass midrapidity shift: eta_shift ≈ -0.465
- Baseline uncertainty in normalized dijet spectrum: typically less than 2.5% within -2 < eta_dijet < 1
- Scale uncertainty in absolute dijet cross section: around 30%
- Scale uncertainty in forward-to-backward ratio: less than 5% in experimentally achievable range
- Probed momentum fraction range for lead nucleus: 0.006 less than or similar to x1 less than or similar to 0.3
- Dominant dijet mass probed: M_dijet ~ 240 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier dijet studies in proton-nucleus collisions from fixed-target experiments at Fermilab (Stewart 1990, Alverson 1993) and HERA (Golubkov 2006) with sqrt(s) ~ 40 GeV and pT < 15 GeV, as well as RHIC measurements in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV up to pT = 45 GeV (PHENIX 2012, STAR 2010). The calculations are motivated by preliminary CMS results (CMSprel) and aim to provide precision constraints for nuclear PDFs, particularly the gluon modifications where EPS09, DSSZ, and HKN07 show sizable differences at x above approximately 10^-3 due to different treatment of inclusive pion production data from RHIC d+Au collisions.
