# Isocalar Roper excitation in the pp --> pp pi0 reaction close to threshold

> A new mechanism for the pp --> pp pi0 reaction close to threshold is suggested coming from the isoscalar excitation of the Roper and its decay into N(pi pi)S-wave, with one of the pi0 emitted and the other one reabsorbed on the second nucleon. The mechanism can lead to important interference with other mechanisms and, together with experiment, serves to exclude large ranges of the 2pi N* decay parameters allowed by the N* partial decay widths.

# Isoscalar Roper excitation in the pp → pp π⁰ reaction close to threshold

## Key claim

The isoscalar excitation of the Roper resonance N*(1440) and its decay into N(ππ)ₛ-wave, with one π⁰ emitted and the other reabsorbed on the second nucleon, provides a new mechanism for the pp → pp π⁰ reaction near threshold. This mechanism can produce important interference with the rescattering term and, together with experimental data, can exclude large ranges of the 2π N* decay parameters that are otherwise allowed by the N* partial decay widths alone.

## Method

The authors evaluate box diagrams (fig. 3) where a σ meson is exchanged between the two nucleons, exciting one nucleon to the Roper resonance N*(1440), which then decays into N(ππ)ₛ-wave; one of the pions is emitted as the final π⁰ while the other is reabsorbed on the second nucleon. The calculation uses effective Lagrangians for σNN, πNN, σNN*, and ππNN* vertices, with coupling constants g_{σNN} and g_{σNN*} taken from fits to (α,α') scattering data, and the ππNN* couplings g_{1ππNN*} and g_{2ππNN*} varied within the ellipse allowed by the N* → N(ππ)ₛ decay width. The rescattering term is evaluated following the off-shell extrapolation of Hamilton (using the λ₁ parameter). Final/initial state interactions (FSI/ISI) are not included.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants from (α,α') data: g²_{σNN}/4π = 5.69, g²_{σNN*}/4π = 1.33, with monopole form factor cutoff Λ_σ = 1.7 GeV and m_σ = 550 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Pion form factor cutoff: Λ_π = 1.25 GeV
- Decay width ellipse coefficients: α = 0.497 × 10⁻³ GeV³, β = 3.66 × 10⁻³ GeV³, γ = 2.69 × 10⁻³ GeV³, using N* branching ratio 7.5% and total width 350 MeV
- Off-shell λ₁ parameter uses a_σ = 0.220 m_π⁻¹, a_sr = -0.233 m_π⁻¹, m_σ = 550 MeV; on-shell λ₁^{on-shell} = 0.0075
- For g_{1ππNN*} = 7.27 GeV⁻¹ and g_{2ππNN*} = 0 (favored by ref. [32]): Roper mechanism alone is very small; interference with rescattering reduces the rescattering prediction by roughly a factor of three
- For g_{1ππNN*} = 12.7 GeV⁻¹ and g_{2ππNN*} = -1.98 GeV⁻¹: Roper contribution comparable to but smaller than rescattering; destructive interference gives very small cross section
- For g_{1ππNN*} = -12.7 GeV⁻¹ and g_{2ππNN*} = 1.98 GeV⁻¹ (opposite signs): constructive interference gives results at some energies bigger than data
- For g_{1ππNN*} = 0 and g_{2ππNN*} = 2.678 GeV⁻¹: Roper mechanism very small; interference increases rescattering results
- For g_{1ππNN*} = -95.74 GeV⁻¹ and g_{2ππNN*} = 34.61 GeV⁻¹ (extreme of ellipse): Roper mechanism alone overwhelms data; such extremes are excluded

## Context

This work extends the study of the pp → pp π⁰ puzzle, where early calculations using one-body and rescattering mechanisms (refs. [1-4]) disagreed with experimental data (refs. [5,6]). Previous explanations included short-range isoscalar excitation of negative-energy components (refs. [7,8]), heavy meson exchange currents (ref. [9]), off-shell effects in the πN amplitude (refs. [11,12]), and chiral perturbation theory approaches (refs. [9,19,20,21,22]). The Roper mechanism was previously shown to dominate pp → pp π⁰π⁰ and pp → pp π⁺π⁻ near threshold (ref. [32]), and the isoscalar excitation of the Roper was established in (α,α') experiments (ref. [33]) with analysis by ref. [34]. The present work enables future quantitative studies including FSI/ISI corrections and suggests that the Roper mechanism must be considered alongside other mechanisms before firm conclusions about the reaction can be drawn.
