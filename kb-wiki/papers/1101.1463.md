# Lattice Nuclear Force

> We derive an energy-independent and non-local potential for the baryon-baryon interaction from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitude on the lattice. The method is applied to the nucleon-nucleon interactions with the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations. The central and tensor potentials are obtained as leading order terms of the velocity expansion of the non-local potential: The central potential has a repulsive core surrounded by an attractive well, while the tensor potential has intermediate and long range attractions. Validity of the velocity expansions tested by the nucleon-nucleon interaction with finite relative momentum. Interactions among octet-baryons in the flavor SU(3) limit are also studied in full QCD simulations to unravel the origin of the repulsive core and the possible existence of $H$-dibaryon.

## Key claim

The paper derives an energy-independent and non-local baryon-baryon potential from the equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) amplitude on the lattice, and applies it to nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions using (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations. The central and tensor potentials are obtained as leading order terms of a velocity expansion, but the abstract glosses over the condition that the method is demonstrated only for relatively heavy pion masses (m_pi ~ 400-700 MeV) and that the deuteron bound state does not appear at these quark masses.

## Method

The equal-time NBS amplitude for two nucleons is extracted from four-point correlation functions computed on the lattice. An energy-independent non-local potential U(r, r') is defined from the NBS wave function via the relation (E - H_0) psi_E(r) = integral U(r, r') psi_E(r') dr', and then expanded in a velocity expansion to obtain local potentials: the leading order (LO) terms are the central potential V_C(r) and tensor potential V_T(r). The LO potentials are determined by solving a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation using projection operators onto S-state and D-state components of the NBS wave function, with the method tested in both quenched and (2+1)-flavor QCD.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing for quenched QCD: a = 0.137 fm on a 32^3 x 48 lattice, corresponding to spatial size L = 4.4 fm
- Lattice spacing for (2+1)-flavor QCD (PACS-CS configurations): a = 0.091 fm on a 32^3 x 64 lattice, corresponding to spatial size L = 2.9 fm
- Pion masses studied in (2+1)-flavor QCD: m_pi = 701, 570, and 411 MeV; corresponding nucleon masses: m_N = 1583, 1412, and 1215 MeV
- Pion masses studied in quenched QCD: m_pi = 731, 529, and 380 MeV; corresponding nucleon masses: m_N = 1558, 1334, and 1197 MeV
- For the velocity expansion test: local ^1S_0 potential compared at E ~ 0 MeV (periodic boundary) and E ~ 45 MeV (anti-periodic boundary) in quenched QCD with m_pi = 529 MeV
- For SU(3) flavor limit study: lattice spacing a = 0.121(2) fm on a 16^3 x 32 lattice, with L = 1.93(3) fm; pion masses m_pi = 1014 and 835 MeV
- H-dibaryon binding energy from flavor-singlet potential: 30-40 MeV (from a later extension of the work cited in the note added)

## Context

This work extends the Luscher method (Luscher, 1991) for extracting hadron-hadron interactions from lattice QCD by instead defining a non-local potential from the internal region of the NBS wave function, an approach first proposed by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2006-2009). The results qualitatively reproduce features of phenomenological NN potentials such as CD Bonn (38 parameters), AV18 (40 parameters), and N^3LO chiral effective field theory (24 parameters), but with the advantage that the lattice potential is determined from first-principles QCD with only quark masses and Lambda_QCD as inputs. The SU(3) flavor limit study of octet-baryon interactions enables investigation of the repulsive core's origin via quark Pauli blocking, consistent with earlier quark model work by Oka and Fujiwara, and opens the path toward calculating hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions where experimental scattering data are scarce.
