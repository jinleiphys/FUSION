# Nuclear model effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> Nuclear model effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering are studied within the distorted wave impulse approximation, using a relativistic shell model to describe the nucleus, and comparing it with the relativistic Fermi gas. Both charged-current and neutral-current processes are considered and, for the neutral-current case, the uncertainties that nuclear effects may introduce in measurements of the axial strange form-factor of the nucleon are investigated.

# Nuclear model effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

## Key claim
Nuclear model effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering are studied within the distorted wave impulse approximation, comparing a relativistic shell model (RSM) with the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG). The paper shows that while final state interactions (FSI) produce large reductions in exclusive neutral-current cross sections (≈50%), these nuclear model uncertainties are almost completely canceled when considering the ratio of neutral-current to charged-current cross sections, making the ratio a robust observable for extracting the axial strange form-factor.

## Method
The author calculates charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the impulse approximation, using the Madrid-Seville relativistic shell model for bound nucleon wave functions (solutions of a Dirac equation from a relativistic mean field with σ, ω, and ρ mesons). For the outgoing nucleon, three descriptions are compared: plane waves (PWIA), solutions with a relativistic optical potential (ROP) including its imaginary part for exclusive NC processes, and a real-ROP or relativistic mean field (RMF) continuum for inclusive CC processes. Results are compared with the relativistic Fermi gas model, including an energy shift ω_sh to match the quasielastic peak position.

## Key numbers
- For inclusive CC quasielastic scattering on ¹⁶O at E_ν = 1 GeV, FSI produce a reduction of the cross section of about 10–15%.
- For exclusive NC and CC proton knockout on ¹²C at E_ν = 1 GeV, FSI (using the full complex ROP) produce a reduction of the cross section of about 50%.
- RFG calculations for CC on ¹⁶O use Fermi momentum k_F = 216 MeV; for NC/CC on ¹²C, k_F = 225 MeV.
- The non-strange axial form-factor cutoff mass is taken as M_A = 1.032 GeV; the magnetic strange form-factor dipole parametrization uses M_V² = 0.71 GeV² and μ_s = 0.37 ± 0.30.
- Uncertainties from the axial cutoff mass (varied in the range 1.00–1.06 GeV) and from the magnetic strange form-factor produce effects on the NC/CC ratio much larger than those from nuclear modeling.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear model effects in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering by several authors (e.g., NoiCC, NoiNC, NoiNPAPLB, Juan, Cris, others), comparing the relativistic Fermi gas with a more realistic relativistic shell model originally developed for (e,e'N) reactions. The paper shows that for extracting the nucleon axial strange form-factor G_A^s, the NC/CC ratio of cross sections cancels nuclear model uncertainties effectively, making it a more reliable observable than separate NC cross sections. The results enable future neutrino experiments to focus on the NC/CC ratio, with the caveat that uncertainties from non-quasielastic contributions and other FSI effects not included in this approach remain to be addressed.
