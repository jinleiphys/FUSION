# Non-Abelian Global Vortices

> We study topologically stable non-Abelian global vortices in the U(N) linear sigma model. The profile functions of the solutions are numerically obtained. We investigate the behaviour of vortices in two limits in which masses of traceless or trace parts of massive bosons are much larger than the others. In the limit that the traceless parts are much heavier, we find a somewhat bizarre vortex solution carrying a non-integer U(1) winding number 1/\sqrt{N} which is irrational in general.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the limit where the traceless parts of the massive bosons are much heavier than the trace part, the regular non-Abelian U(N) global vortex solution reduces to a singular Abelian vortex carrying a non-integer U(1) winding number 1/√N, which is irrational for N=2,3,5,6,... This surprising result is presented as a limiting case of an otherwise regular solution, not as a physically realizable configuration in the full model.

## Method

The authors study topologically stable non-Abelian global vortices in the U(N) linear sigma model with chiral symmetry SU(N)_L × SU(N)_R × U(1)_A. They numerically solve the coupled ordinary differential equations for the profile functions f(r) and g(r) using a relaxation method, with boundary conditions f(0)=0, g'(0)=0, and f(∞)=g(∞)=1. They investigate the dependence of the vortex profiles on the mass ratio τ = m_χ/m_φ and examine two limiting cases where either the trace part mass m_φ or the traceless part mass m_χ is sent to infinity.

## Key numbers

- For the minimal winding (k=1) U(1) global vortex, the shooting parameter a_1 = 0.4123772 (dimensionless, from the expansion f(ρ) = a_1 ρ + ... at ρ → 0)
- The potential energy of a U(1) global vortex with winding number k is E_pot(k) = π v^2 k^2 (exact analytic result from the Derrick-Pohozaev identity)
- For the U(N) non-Abelian vortex, the leading asymptotic behavior at large r is: f = 1 - (1/(N r^2)) (1/m_φ^2 + (N-1)/m_χ^2) and g = 1 - (1/(N r^2)) (1/m_φ^2 - 1/m_χ^2)
- In the limit where traceless parts are much heavier (τ → ∞), the U(N) vortex reduces to a U(1) vortex with winding number 1/√N, which is irrational for N=2,3,5,6,...

## Context

This work extends the analysis of non-Abelian global vortices previously studied by Nitta (2007) by providing more accurate numerical solutions and determining shooting parameters up to fifth order. The paper builds on the concept of fractional vortices known from condensed matter systems such as spinor Bose-Einstein condensates (spin1-vortex, spin2-vortex) and from earlier work on U(1) η' strings by Zhang (1997) and Brandenberger (1998). The results enable further study of inter-string forces between global U(N) vortices with different internal orientations, as recently calculated by Nakano (2007, 2008), and may have implications for the stability of domain wall junctions discussed by Balachandran (2001).
