# Theoretical aspects of photon production in high energy nuclear collisions

> A brief overview of the calculation of photon and dilepton production rates in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma is presented. We review leading order rates as well as recent NLO determinations and non-equilibrium corrections. Furthermore, the difficulties in a non-perturbative lattice determination are summarized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations of photon and dilepton production rates in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma provide a first estimate of the theoretical uncertainty of leading-order (LO) rates, which are widely used in phenomenology, but that these perturbative methods are justified only when the coupling is small (g << 1) and become an extrapolation with uncontrolled systematics at realistic coupling values (alpha_s ~ 0.3).

## Method

The paper reviews the perturbative calculation of the photon production rate from the thermal quark-gluon plasma, which requires evaluating the Wightman polarization tensor W^<(K) at leading order through 2-to-2 processes (Compton scattering and annihilation) and collinear processes involving the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. At NLO, the calculation incorporates corrections from soft gluon exchanges, one-loop rungs in the LPM ladder, semi-collinear processes, and soft quark regions, using analyticity properties to map real-time Minkowskian calculations to simpler Euclidean three-dimensional problems. For dileptons, the NLO calculation includes real and virtual corrections to the thermal Drell-Yan process, with HTL and collinear resummations applied when the photon virtuality becomes soft.

## Key numbers

- The NLO correction to the photon rate represents an approximately 20% increase over the LO rate for alpha_s = 0.3 and N_c = N_f = 3, arising from a cancellation between a large positive collinear correction and a negative semi-collinear plus soft correction
- The leading-order photon rate is O(alpha g^2), with the 2-to-2 processes containing a non-analytic log(g) term
- For dileptons at NLO, the thermal Drell-Yan process (the LO) receives increasingly larger corrections as the invariant mass M = sqrt(K^2) decreases, until the NLO correction overtakes the LO, signaling the need for resummations
- The collinear processes at LO require the intermediate quark angle to be of order g, while at NLO the angle extends to sqrt(g)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of electromagnetic probes from the quark-gluon plasma, building on the foundational LO calculations by Kapusta (1991), Baier (1991), and Aurenche (1998) for photons, and by Braaten (1990) and Aurenche (2002) for dileptons. The NLO photon calculation by Ghiglieri (2013) and the NLO dilepton calculation by Laine (2013) provide the first systematic uncertainty estimates for these rates, which are essential inputs for hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions. The paper also discusses lattice determinations by Ding (2010, 2013) at zero momentum and non-perturbative determinations of the jet quenching parameter by Panero (2013) and Laine (2013), which could enable future hybrid calculations combining non-perturbative inputs with perturbative rate calculations.
