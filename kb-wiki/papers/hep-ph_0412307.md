# Nuclear modification of valence-quark distributions and its effects on NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly

> We investigated a nuclear modification difference between up- and down-valence quark distributions by analyzing structure function F_2 and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios. Although nuclear modifications of the valence-quark distributions themselves are rather well determined, it is difficult to find their difference from the present data. We estimated such an effect on the NuTeV sin^2 theta_W value and its uncertainty by the Hessian method. At this stage, it is not large enough to explain the whole NuTeV anomaly. However, the modification difference cannot be precisely determined, so that further studies are needed.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification difference between up- and down-valence quark distributions in iron produces a correction to the NuTeV weak-mixing angle of only 0.0004, which is an order of magnitude smaller than the observed anomaly of 0.0050; however, the uncertainty on this correction (0.0015) is comparable to the deviation, so the effect cannot be ruled out as a partial explanation.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared analysis of nuclear structure function F2 ratios and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios to extract parameters describing the difference between up- and down-valence quark nuclear modification factors w_u_v and w_d_v at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2. They parameterized the difference w_u_v - w_d_v using a polynomial in x with a (1 - 1/A^1/3) mass-number dependence, fixing the baryon-number and charge conservation constraint on one parameter. The resulting epsilon_v distribution, defined as (w_d_v - w_u_v)/(w_d_v + w_u_v), was then used to compute the shift in sin^2 theta_W by integrating over NuTeV kinematics using the functional relations provided by the NuTeV collaboration.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV measured sin^2 theta_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst) in the on-shell scheme, compared to the world average of 0.2227 ± 0.0004, giving a deviation of 0.0050.
- The nuclear modification correction to sin^2 theta_W from valence-quark differences is Delta(sin^2 theta_W) = 0.0004 ± 0.0015 at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2.
- The epsilon_v distribution is shown at Q^2 = 20 GeV^2, which is about the average Q^2 value of the NuTeV data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kumano (2002) and Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai (2004) on nuclear parton distribution functions and their effect on the NuTeV anomaly. The analysis uses the nuclear PDF framework from Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai (2004) and compares with the earlier epsilon_v estimates from Kumano (2002). The results indicate that while the valence-quark modification difference alone cannot explain the full NuTeV anomaly, the large uncertainty leaves room for other nuclear corrections (neutron excess, strange-antistrange asymmetry, charm-anticharm asymmetry) to contribute, and further experimental data are needed to determine the modification difference precisely.
