# Quark and Glue Momenta and Angular Momenta in the Proton --- a Lattice Calculation

> We report a complete calculation of the quark and glue momenta and angular momenta in the proton. These include the quark contributions from both the connected and disconnected insertions. The calculation is carried out on a $16^3 \times 24$ quenched lattice at $β= 6.0$ and for Wilson fermions with $κ= 0.154, 0.155,$ and 0.1555 which correspond to pion masses at 650, 538, and 478 MeV. The quark loops are calculated with $Z_4$ noise and signal-to-noise is improved further with unbiased subtractions. The glue operator is comprised of gauge-field tensors constructed from the overlap operator. The $u$ and $d$ quark momentum/angular momentum fraction is 0.66(5)/0.72(5), the strange momentum/angular momentum fraction is 0.024(6)/0.023(7), and that of the glue is 0.31(6)/0.25(8). The orbital angular momenta of the quarks are obtained from subtracting the angular momentum component from its corresponding spin. As a result, the quark orbital angular momentum constitutes 0.50(2) of the proton spin, with almost all it coming from the disconnected insertion. The quark spin carries a fraction 0.25(12) and glue carries a fraction 0.25(8) of the total proton spin.

## Key claim

The paper reports a complete lattice calculation of the quark and glue contributions to the proton's momentum and angular momentum, including both connected and disconnected insertions, on a quenched lattice at unphysically heavy pion masses (478–650 MeV). The authors find that the quark orbital angular momentum constitutes 0.50(2) of the proton spin, with almost all of it coming from the disconnected insertion, while the quark spin and glue angular momentum each carry a fraction 0.25(12) and 0.25(8) respectively.

## Method

The calculation was performed on a 16³ × 24 quenched lattice at β = 6.0 using Wilson fermions with three hopping parameters (κ = 0.154, 0.155, 0.1555) corresponding to pion masses of 650, 538, and 478 MeV. The authors computed three-point functions for the quark and glue energy-momentum tensors, extracting T₁(q²) and T₂(q²) separately at various momentum transfers and extrapolating to q² → 0. Quark loops in the disconnected insertion were estimated with Z₄ noise and unbiased subtractions, while the glue operator used gauge-field tensors constructed from the overlap Dirac operator. Lattice renormalization was performed using the momentum and angular momentum sum rules, yielding renormalization constants Z_q = Z_g = 1.05.

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.11 fm (set by nucleon mass)

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of quark orbital angular momenta that only included connected insertions (Mathur 1999, Hagler 2003, and others), which found small or near-zero contributions. The present calculation is the first to include both connected and disconnected insertions for quarks and to compute the glue momentum and angular momentum using the overlap operator for the gauge-field tensor. The results address the "Dark Spin" scenario raised by experimental findings from COMPASS and STAR that the gluon helicity distribution is close to zero, and by arguments that glue orbital angular momentum is absent (Brodsky and Gardner 2006). The calculation enables future work with dynamical configurations and light fermions, and the authors note they are performing perturbative matching to the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV.
