# Form Factors of Dimesoatoms for Discrete-Continuum Transitions

> An approach for calculation of transition form factors of hydrogen-like elementary atoms (EA) is proposed. A general formula for bound-continuous transition form factors of EA is derived. It is shown that these form factors can be represented in the form of finite sum of terms with simple analytical structure and may be numerically evaluated with arbitrary degree of accuracy. As an application of the presented form factors, the calculation of the spectra of products from EA ionization is considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed-form expression for bound-continuum transition form factors of hydrogen-like elementary atoms that avoids the standard infinite partial-wave expansion, but the derivation is explicitly shown only for initial \(nS\)-states and then generalized to arbitrary \(nlm\) states; the final result is a finite sum of terms involving classical polynomials that can be evaluated to arbitrary numerical accuracy.

## Method

The authors start from the standard definition of the transition form factor as the Fourier transform of the product of bound and continuum Coulomb wave functions. For initial \(nS\)-states, they rewrite the bound-state radial wave function using recurrence relations for Laguerre polynomials and a generating-function representation, then evaluate the resulting integral analytically using an integral representation of the confluent hypergeometric function. The final expression is expanded in Gegenbauer and Jacobi polynomials, yielding a finite sum. The method is then extended to arbitrary \(nlm\) initial states by decomposing the angular part with spherical harmonics and using a contour-integral representation of the hypergeometric function, leading to a similar finite-sum result.

## Key numbers

- The fine-structure constant \(\alpha = 1/137\) is used throughout as the coupling parameter.
- The reduced mass \(\mu\) and principal quantum number \(n\) define \(\omega = \mu\alpha / n\).
- The continuum-state Sommerfeld parameter is \(\xi = \mu\alpha / p\), where \(p\) is the relative momentum of the outgoing pair.
- The DIRAC experiment aims to measure the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) atom (\(A_{2\pi}\)) ground-state lifetime with an accuracy of 10%.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of hydrogen-like atom form factors (Landau and Lifshitz; Sommerfeld) by providing a finite-sum representation that avoids truncation of an infinite partial-wave series. It directly supports the DIRAC experiment at CERN (Adeva et al., 1995; Afanasyev et al., 2002), which observes dimesoatoms such as \(A_{2\pi}\), \(A_{\pi K}\), and \(A_{2K}\) and measures their lifetimes. The derived form factors enable computation of the momentum and angular distributions of pion pairs from the ionization (dissociation) of \(\pi^+\pi^-\) atoms in the Coulomb field of a target, as illustrated for initial \(n=1\) and \(n=10\) states.
