# The evolution of the chiral symmetry in cesium isotopes

> Following the reports of candidate chiral doublet bands observed in cesium isotopes, the possible chiral candidates and the evolution of three-dimensional rotation in $^{120-134}{\textrm{Cs}}$ are investigated within the microscopic three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (3DTAC-CDFT). By investigating the evolution of the polar angle $θ$ and azimuth angle $φ$ as a function of rotational frequency $\hbarω$, the transition from the planar rotation to the chiral rotation has been found in $^{121-133}{\textrm{Cs}}$. The corresponding critical rotational frequency $ω_{\textrm{crit}}$ of the appearance of chiral aplanar rotation decreases as neutron number increases, which can be attributed to the neutrons in $(gd)$ and $(sd)$ shells having smaller angular momentum components along both the short and long axes, and larger components along medium axis, respectively. In comparison, only planar rotation has been obtained in $^{120,134}{\textrm{Cs}}$. With these interpretations, the obtained $I\sim\hbarω$ and energy spectra as well as $B(M1)/B(E2)$ values show reasonable agreement with the available experimental data. In addition, the evolution of quadrupole deformation $β$ and triaxial deformation $γ$ are also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that chiral aplanar rotation (three-dimensional rotation) occurs in cesium isotopes \(^{121-133}\mathrm{Cs}\) but not in \(^{120,134}\mathrm{Cs}\), based on the self-consistent microscopic 3DTAC-CDFT calculations. The hedge is that the critical rotational frequency \(\omega_{\mathrm{crit}}\) for the planar-to-chiral transition decreases with increasing neutron number, and that for \(^{120,134}\mathrm{Cs}\) the critical frequency may lie above the currently observed spin range, so chirality is not supported in those two isotopes.

## Method
The authors applied the three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (3DTAC-CDFT) with the point-coupling PC-PK1 interaction to the cesium isotopes \(^{120-134}\mathrm{Cs}\). They solved the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis, self-consistently determined the orientation angles \(\theta\) and \(\varphi\) of the total angular momentum as functions of rotational frequency \(\hbar\omega\), and extracted the critical frequency \(\omega_{\mathrm{crit}}\) where the azimuth angle \(\varphi\) becomes nonzero, signaling the transition from planar (in the \(sl\) plane) to aplanar (chiral) rotation. They also calculated spin \(I\) versus \(\hbar\omega\), excitation energies, and \(B(M1)/B(E2)\) ratios, comparing with available experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Critical rotational frequencies \(\hbar\omega_{\mathrm{crit}}\) for the planar-to-chiral transition decrease with increasing neutron number across \(^{121-133}\mathrm{Cs}\); for \(^{120}\mathrm{Cs}\) the critical frequency is larger than \(0.55\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (corresponding to \(I \approx 24\ \hbar\)), and for \(^{134}\mathrm{Cs}\) larger than \(0.45\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The polar angle \(\theta\) is always larger than \(50^\circ\) in all cesium isotopes studied.
- The single proton particle in the \(h_{11/2}\) orbit contributes roughly \(5.5\ \hbar\) of angular momentum along the short (\(s\)) axis at low rotational frequency.
- Five neutron holes in the \(h_{11/2}\) orbit contribute about \(3.5\ \hbar\) of angular momentum along the long (\(l\)) axis.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of chiral doublet bands in cesium isotopes, which had been reported experimentally in \(^{122,124,126,128,130,132}\mathrm{Cs}\) (e.g., Starosta et al. (2001), Grodner et al. (2011)) and theoretically using constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field approaches (e.g., PhysRevC.97.034306, PhysRevC.100.034328). The 3DTAC-CDFT method had previously been applied to \(^{106}\mathrm{Rh}\), \(^{106}\mathrm{Ag}\), \(^{135}\mathrm{Nd}\), and \(^{102-107}\mathrm{Rh}\) (Zhao et al. (2017), Peng et al. (2020)). The present results enable a systematic mapping of the chiral region in the cesium isotopic chain and identify the boundaries at \(^{120}\mathrm{Cs}\) and \(^{134}\mathrm{Cs}\), where only planar rotation is found, consistent with the lack of observed chiral doublet bands in those nuclei.
