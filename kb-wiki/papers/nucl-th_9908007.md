# Crossed-boson exchange contribution and Bethe-Salpeter equation

> The contribution to the binding energy of a two-body system due to the crossed two-boson exchange contribution is calculated, using the Bethe-Salpeter equation. This is done for distinguishable, scalar particles interacting via the exchange of scalar massive bosons. The sensitivity of the results to the off-shell behavior of the operator accounting for this contribution is discussed. Large corrections to the Bethe-Salpeter results in the ladder approximation are found. For neutral scalar bosons, the mass obtained for the two-body system is close to what has been calculated with various forms of the instantaneous approximation, including the standard non-relativistic approach. The specific character of this result is demonstrated by a calculation involving charged bosons, which evidences a quite different pattern. Our results explain for some part those obtained by Nieuwenhuis and Tjon on a different basis. Some discrepancy appears with increasing coupling constants, suggesting the existence of sizeable contributions involving more than two-boson exchanges.

# Crossed-boson exchange contribution and Bethe-Salpeter equation

## Key claim

The crossed two-boson exchange contribution accounts for roughly half of the total binding energy difference between the ladder Bethe-Salpeter approximation and the non-perturbative Feynman-Schwinger representation results, but the remaining discrepancy grows with increasing coupling constant, indicating that multi-boson exchanges beyond the crossed two-boson level are essential for reliable calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two distinguishable scalar particles of equal mass exchanging scalar bosons, using a variational method with four-dimensional spherical harmonics and Gaussian basis functions. The crossed two-boson exchange contribution was incorporated via a dispersion relation representation, with several different off-shell extrapolations (u=0, s-dependent, non-relativistic limit) to study sensitivity to off-shell behavior. The resulting eigenvalue problem was solved iteratively to determine the bound state mass as a function of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- For the case μ/m = 0.15, the ground state mass obtained with crossed two-boson exchange lies approximately halfway between the ladder Bethe-Salpeter results and the non-perturbative Feynman-Schwinger representation results of Nieuwenhuis and Tjon.
- For charged bosons (isospin 1 state), the crossed two-boson exchange contribution is multiplied by a factor of 5 relative to the neutral case, while the ladder terms remain unchanged.
- The coupling constant relation is g² = (4π)² m² λ, where λ is the eigenvalue in the Bethe-Salpeter equation.
- For μ = 0 in the ladder approximation (Wick-Cutkosky model), analytic solutions exist at s=0 in the form λ = N(N+1).

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative Feynman-Schwinger representation calculations of Nieuwenhuis and Tjon by isolating the specific contribution of the crossed two-boson exchange diagram within the Bethe-Salpeter framework. It also confirms the non-relativistic theoretical result of Amghar and Desplanques that crossed two-boson exchange cancels iterated one-boson exchange, recovering the instantaneous approximation for neutral scalar bosons. The results enable future work on estimating crossed three-boson exchange contributions and addressing the zero-mass boson case relevant to QED, where divergences require further theoretical development.
