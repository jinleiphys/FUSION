# Electrified plasma in AdS/CFT correspondence

> We construct new gravity backgrounds holographic dual to neutral plasma with U(1) global symmetry in the presence of constant electric field, considering its full back-reactions to the metric. As the electric field and the induced current cause a net energy in-flow to the system, the plasma is continually heated up and the corresponding gravity solution has an expanding horizon. After proposing a consistent late-time expansion scheme, we present analytic solutions in the scheme up to next-leading order, and our solutions are new time-dependent solutions of 5D asymptotic AdS Einstein-Maxwell(-Chern-Simons) theory. To extract dual CFT stress tensor and U(1) current from the solutions, we perform a rigorous holographic renormalization of Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory including full back-reactions, which can in itself be an interesting addition to literatures. As by-products, we obtain interesting modifications of energy-momentum/current Ward identities due to the U(1) symmetry and its triangle anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper constructs new time-dependent gravity backgrounds holographically dual to a neutral plasma with a U(1) global symmetry under a constant external electric field, including full back-reactions to the metric. The hedge is that the solutions are obtained within a proposed consistent late-time expansion scheme and are only valid up to next-leading order, with the plasma continually heating up and the dual gravity solution having an expanding horizon.

## Method

The authors propose a late-time expansion scheme for the 5D asymptotic AdS Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations, using a scaling variable \(u = r / t^{1/3}\) and an expansion parameter \((E/T^2)^2 \sim t^{-4/3}\), with additional logarithmic terms in \(t\) to handle near-boundary logarithms. They solve the equations of motion order by order analytically, imposing regularity at the expanding horizon and UV boundary conditions to fix all integration constants uniquely. A full holographic renormalization of the Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons system is performed to extract the dual CFT stress tensor and U(1) current from the solutions.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order horizon location is fixed by regularity to \(w = (E/2)^{2/3}\), where \(E\) is the constant electric field magnitude.
* The leading late-time temperature scaling is \(T \sim E^{2/3} \cdot t^{1/3}\), derived from the energy in-flow equation \(d\epsilon/dt = \vec{E}\cdot\vec{J}\) with \(\epsilon \sim T^4\) and conductivity \(\sigma(T) \propto T\).
* The expansion parameter controlling sub-leading corrections is \((E/T^2)^2 \sim t^{-4/3}\).
* The next-leading-order integration constants \(C_2\) and \(\tilde C_2\) are fixed by horizon regularity to \(C_2 = (E^2/12)(\log 2 - 1 + 4(\log 2)^2 + (4/3)\log E)\) and \(\tilde C_2 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of strongly coupled CFT plasmas via AdS/CFT beyond linearized approximations, which previously treated perturbations without back-reactions (e.g., Caron-Huot (2006) for conductivity). It is analogous to the late-time expansion of boost-invariant plasmas by Janik and Peschanski (2005), but here the horizon expands due to heating rather than receding. The static magnetic field case was studied earlier by D'Hoker and Kraus (2009). The results enable the study of far-from-equilibrium dynamics, such as early-time thermalization and black-hole formation, and provide modified Ward identities for the energy-momentum tensor and U(1) current due to the external field and triangle anomaly.
