# $\boldsymbol{\mathcalα}$ decay properties of $\boldsymbol{^{296}}$Og within the two-potential approach

> The present work is a continuations of our previous paper [J.-G. Deng, et al., Chin. Phys. C, {\bf41}: 124109 (2017)]. In present work, the $\mathcalα$ decay half-life of unknown nucleus $^{296}$Og is predicted within the two-potential approach and the hindrance factors of all 20 even-even nuclei in the same region with $^{296}$Og, i.e. proton number $82<Z<126$ and neutron number $152<N<184$, from $^{250}$Cm to $^{294}$Og are extracted. The prediction is 1.09 ms within a factor of 5.12. In addition, based on the latest experimental data, a new set of parameters of $\mathcalα$ decay hindrance factors for the even-even nuclei in this region considering the shell effect and proton-neutron interaction are obtained.

## Key claim

The alpha decay half-life of the unknown superheavy nucleus \(^{296}\)Og is predicted to be 1.09 ms, but this value carries an uncertainty factor of 5.12, meaning the true half-life could lie between roughly 0.21 ms and 5.6 ms.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) with a cosh-parametrized nuclear potential, a Coulomb potential from a uniformly charged sphere, and the Langer-modified centrifugal barrier. They calculated alpha decay half-lives for all 20 even-even nuclei in the region \(82 < Z < 126\) and \(152 < N < 184\) (from \(^{250}\)Cm to \(^{294}\)Og) using experimental \(Q_\alpha\) values from NUBASE2016 and AME2016, then extracted hindrance factors. A new five-parameter formula for hindrance factors, incorporating shell effects and proton-neutron interaction, was fitted to these data. For the prediction of \(^{296}\)Og, the \(Q_\alpha\) value from the WS3+ mass model (11.62 MeV) was used.

## Key numbers

*   Predicted alpha decay half-life of \(^{296}\)Og: 1.09 ms, with an overall uncertainty factor of 5.12.
*   The \(Q_\alpha\) value adopted for \(^{296}\)Og from the WS3+ mass model: 11.62 MeV.
*   Standard deviation \(\sigma_{\rm{pre}}\) of the predicted half-lives (using the new hindrance-factor parameters) from experimental data for the 20 even-even nuclei: 0.26 (in \(\log_{10}\) units).
*   The new hindrance-factor parameters improved the prediction accuracy by 18.75% compared to the authors' previous parameter set (standard deviation dropped from 0.32 to 0.26).
*   The predicted half-life of \(^{296}\)Og using the generalized proximity potential 1977 (gp77) was 2.64 ms.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of alpha decay hindrance factors (Deng et al., Chin. Phys. C, 2017) by updating the parameter set with the latest experimental data from NUBASE2016 and AME2016. It directly competes with predictions by Sobiczewski (2016), who used a phenomenological formula with \(Q_\alpha\) from nine mass models, and by Mohr (2017), who used a double-folding potential and predicted \(T_{1/2} = 0.825\) ms. The new result provides a refined reference half-life for the planned synthesis of \(^{296}\)Og via the \(^{251}\)Cf(\(^{48}\)Ca,3n) reaction at FLNR Dubna, and the hindrance-factor systematics can be applied to other unknown superheavy nuclei in this region.
