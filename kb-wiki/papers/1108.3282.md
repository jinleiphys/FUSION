# Strong decay of low-lying $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ nucleon resonances to pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons

> Partial decay widths of the nucleon resonances $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{13}(1520)$ and $D_{13}(1700)$ to the pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons are studied within a chiral constituent quark model. The effects of the configurations mixing between the states $|N^{2}_{8}P_{M}>$ and $|N^{4}_{8}P_{M}>$ are considered, taking into account the breakdown of the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry. In addition, possible contributions of the strangeness components in the $S_{11}$ resonances are investigated. Experimental data for the partial decay widths of the $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ resonances are well reproduced. Predictions for coupling constants of the four nucleon resonances to pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons, crucial issues in the photo- and hadron-induced meson production reactions, are reported.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) with only three-quark configurations and SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking can reproduce the partial decay widths of the D13 resonances but fails for the S11 resonances; only by including five-quark components in the S11 resonances can the model simultaneously reproduce the experimental partial widths for both S11(1535) and S11(1650).

## Method

The authors used a chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) with harmonic oscillator wave functions to calculate transition amplitudes for the strong decays of S11(1535), S11(1650), D13(1520), and D13(1700) to πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, and η′N final states. They introduced configuration mixing between the |N²₈P_M⟩ and |N⁴₈P_M⟩ states via mixing angles θ_S and θ_D to account for SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking, and for the S11 resonances they additionally included five-quark (qqqqq̄) components with a coupling potential between three-quark and five-quark configurations. The partial decay widths were obtained by extracting coupling constants from the quark-level transition amplitudes and comparing them to the hadronic-level Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- For S11(1535) → πN, the pure three-quark model with exact SU(6)⊗O(3) reproduces the experimental band for harmonic oscillator parameter ω₃ between approximately 300 and 340 MeV
- For S11(1535) → ηN, the pure three-quark model reproduces the experimental band for ω₃ between approximately 300 and 380 MeV
- The experimental partial width for S11(1650) → KΛ is 4.8 ± 0.7 MeV (PDG value), which is zero in the exact SU(6)⊗O(3) limit and only becomes non-zero with configuration mixing
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 340 MeV, m_s = 460 MeV
- Meson decay constants used: f_π = 93 MeV, f_K = 113 MeV, f_η = 1.2 f_π, f_η′ = -0.58 f_π
- The η-η′ mixing angle is θ = -23°
- The cutoff parameter for off-shell form factors is Λ = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach of Koniuk (1979) and Capstick (1992, 1993) by incorporating five-quark components, following earlier studies by An (2005, 2008, 2010) and Li (2005, 2006) that showed the significance of such components in baryon properties. The paper addresses the long-standing difficulty that no single formalism had simultaneously reproduced the partial widths of all four low-lying S11 and D13 resonances, with the only exception being the recent 1/N_C expansion approach of Jayalath (2011). The results enable predictions of coupling constants for photo- and hadron-induced meson production reactions, which are crucial for interpreting experimental data from facilities studying baryon resonance production.
