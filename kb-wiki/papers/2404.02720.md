# Light-quark mass dependence of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance

> We present the light-quark mass dependence of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance at leading order in a renormalizable framework of covariant chiral effective field theory. The meson-baryon scattering amplitudes, which are obtained by solving the scattering equation within time-ordered perturbation theory, follow the quark mass trajectory of the Coordinated Lattice Simulations consortium. At $M_π\approx 200$ MeV and $M_K\approx 487$ MeV, our parameter-free prediction of $Λ(1405)$ poles is consistent with the recent lattice results of BaSc Collaboration [Phys. Rev. Lett. 132, 051901 (2024)]. Varying the pion mass from $135$ MeV to $400$ MeV, we present the evolution of double-pole positions of $Λ(1405)$: the higher pole remains a resonance around the $\bar{K}N$ threshold; whereas the lower pole undergoes a transition from resonance to a virtual state, and ultimately to a bound state of the $πΣ$ system, which could be verified by the forthcoming lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

At leading order in a renormalizable covariant chiral effective field theory framework, the predicted pole positions of the Λ(1405) resonance at Mπ ≈ 200 MeV and MK ≈ 487 MeV are consistent with the BaSc lattice results, but the width of the higher pole approaches the lower bound of the lattice value; the evolution of the lower pole from a resonance to a virtual state and then to a bound state as pion mass increases from 135 MeV to 400 MeV is a parameter-free prediction that could be verified by forthcoming lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The meson-baryon scattering amplitudes for the strangeness S = −1, isospin I = 0 sector were obtained by solving the coupled-channel scattering equation within time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) using a leading-order potential that includes vector-meson exchange, Born, and crossed-Born terms. The quark mass dependence of the model variables (pseudoscalar meson masses, octet baryon masses, vector meson masses, and meson decay constants) was determined by fitting chiral perturbation theory expressions to lattice QCD data from the Coordinated Lattice Simulations (CLS) consortium, following the quark mass trajectory with fixed trace of the bare quark-mass matrix. The scattering equation was solved with full off-shell dependence, and subtractive renormalization was applied to obtain finite, cutoff-independent T-matrices.

## Key numbers

- At Mπ = 203.7 MeV and MK = 486.4 MeV, the lower pole from the πΣ-¯KN coupled-channel calculation is at 1387.14 MeV (real part), compared to the BaSc result of 1392(18) MeV.
- At the same masses, the higher pole from the πΣ-¯KN calculation is at 1469.86 − i4.71 MeV, compared to the BaSc result of 1455(21) − i11.5(6.0) MeV.
- With four coupled channels (πΣ, ¯KN, ηΛ, KΞ) at the same masses, the lower pole shifts to 1389.05 MeV and the higher pole to 1464.55 − i9.44 MeV.
- At the physical point (Mπ ≈ 135 MeV), the lower pole is at 1320.5 − i80.4 MeV on the (−,+,+,+) Riemann sheet.
- The lower pole becomes a virtual bound state at Mπ ≥ 197 MeV and a bound state on the physical sheet at Mπ ≥ 269 MeV.
- The higher pole remains a resonance for all pion masses from 135 MeV to 400 MeV, crossing above the ¯KN threshold at Mπ > 225 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the renormalizable chiral EFT framework previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering (Ren, 2020) and to the Λ(1405) at the physical point (Ren, 2021) into the unphysical quark mass region, enabling direct comparison with the first lattice calculation of coupled-channel πΣ and ¯KN scattering by the BaSc Collaboration (2024). The evolution of the Λ(1405) poles along the CLS quark mass trajectory differs from earlier studies along the SU(3) flavor trajectory (Jido, 2003; Bruns, 2021; Guo, 2023), and the author notes that next-to-leading order corrections, which significantly altered the SU(3) trajectory results in Guo (2023), are needed to refine the conclusions.
