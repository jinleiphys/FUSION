# Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion approach: Three-body Faddeev calculations for Coulomb-like interactions

> We demonstrate the feasibility and efficiency of the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method for generating accurate solutions of the Faddeev equations. Results obtained with this method are reported for several benchmark cases of bosonic and fermionic three-body systems. Correct bound-state results in agreement with the ones established in the literature are achieved for short-range interactions. We outline the formalism for the treatment of three-body Coulomb systems and present a bound-state calculation for a three-boson system interacting via Coulomb plus short-range forces. The corresponding result is in good agreement with the answer from a recent stochastic-variational-method calculation.

## Key claim

The Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method can produce accurate solutions of the Faddeev equations for three-body bound states with short-range interactions, and can be extended to systems including Coulomb forces by expanding only the short-range part of the interaction while treating the long-range part exactly via the Coulomb Green's operator. The method achieves agreement with established benchmark results, but the authors hedge that for the Coulomb case they present only a single bound-state calculation for a three-boson system, which agrees with a stochastic-variational-method calculation.

## Method

The short-range part of the two-body potential in each partial wave is expanded on a basis of Coulomb-Sturmian functions, producing a finite-rank separable approximation. For systems with Coulomb interactions, the Coulomb potential is kept in the Green's operator rather than expanded, and the three-body Coulomb Green's operator matrix elements are evaluated via a contour integral using the convolution theorem. The resulting algebraic Faddeev equations are solved as a linear system of homogeneous equations whose determinant vanishes at the bound-state energy.

## Key numbers

* For the Malfliet-Tjon MTVa potential (two nucleons, mass such that ℏ²/m = 41.47 MeV fm²), the converged binding energy is E = 8.5358 (fermionic system, 2-channel calculation), compared to E = 8.536 from the Los Alamos-Iowa group.
* For the Ali-Bodmer potential between α-particles (mass such that ℏ²/M = 10.36675 MeV fm², e² = 1.44 MeV fm), convergence to 5 significant digits is achieved with N = 20 terms in the separable expansion for n and ν.
* For the Ali-Bodmer potential plus Coulomb interaction (4 e²/r), the same convergence rate is observed with N = 20 terms.
* The binding energy for the three-boson system with Ali-Bodmer plus Coulomb potential agrees with the stochastic-variational-method calculation (specific numerical value not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method, previously tested in two-body problems (Papp, 1995; Papp, 1996), to three-body Faddeev calculations. It provides an alternative to direct Faddeev equation solutions on supercomputers (Glockle, 1995) and to the EST separable expansion method (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1973) which was not well-suited for long-range forces. The method enables future three-body scattering calculations with Coulomb forces, which had previously been limited to energies below or above breakup threshold (Berthold, 1987; Alt, 1990).
