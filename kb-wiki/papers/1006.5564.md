# Ferromagnetic phase diagram of neutron matter

> The magnetic properties of matter under extreme conditions are of particular importance to understanding the neutron star interior. One contributing factor to the magnetic field of a neutron star could be the ferromagnetic phase of nuclear matter. In this letter we present a self-consistent, relativistic description of ferromagnetism in dense matter, within which the ferromagnetic phase diagram for neutron matter is calculated.

## Key claim
The paper presents a self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculation of the ferromagnetic phase diagram for cold neutron matter, but finds that the coupling strength required for a ferromagnetic phase transition is larger than the actual magnetic dipole moment of the neutron at all densities considered, meaning the ferromagnetic phase does not occur under realistic conditions.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic mean-field (QHD) model coupling neutrons to scalar sigma and vector omega mesons, plus a coupling between the neutron magnetic dipole moment and the magnetic field via the Broderick et al. interaction term. They solved the Euler-Lagrange equations self-consistently for the magnetic field, scalar field, and vector field at fixed density and coupling constant, increasing the coupling until a non-zero magnetic field appeared.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear saturation density used is \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The ferromagnetic phase, when stable, generates a magnetic field of order \(10^{10}\ \mathrm{Gauss}\).
- Neutron star surface magnetic fields are of order \(10^{11}-10^{13}\ \mathrm{Gauss}\), indicating the calculated field is insufficient to explain observed fields.
- The coupling constant \(g_b\) at normal nuclear density (the magnetic dipole moment of the neutron) is too weak to cause a phase transition even at very high densities.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic description of ferromagnetism in nuclear matter by Maruyama and Tatsumi (2000) and the dipole-field coupling introduced by Broderick et al. (2000), providing the first self-consistent calculation using that coupling. The results contradict earlier proposals by Brownell and Callaway (1975) and Silverstein (1975) that ferromagnetic neutron matter could significantly contribute to neutron star magnetic fields, and show the outcome is highly model-dependent. The model enables future work including other hadrons and leptons to obtain a more realistic description of neutron star matter and investigate effects on the equation of state.
