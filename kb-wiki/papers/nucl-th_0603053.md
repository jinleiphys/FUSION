# Proton enhancement at large p_T at LHC without structure in associated-particle distribution

> The production of pions and protons in the $p_T$ range between 10 and 20 GeV/c for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC is studied in the recombination model. It is shown that the dominant mechanism for hadronization is the recombination of shower partons from neighboring jets when the jet density is high. Protons are more copiously produced than pions in that $p_T$ range because the coalescing partons can have lower momentum fractions, but no thermal partons are involved. The proton-to-pion ratio can rise beyond 20. When such a high $p_T$ hadron is used as a trigger particle, there will not be any associated particles that are not in the background.

## Key claim

In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, the proton-to-pion ratio can reach values as high as 20 in the transverse momentum range 10–20 GeV/c, but only if the jet density is high enough that recombination of shower partons from neighboring jets dominates over fragmentation; the ratio decreases with pT when a realistic pT-dependent jet-overlap probability is used, falling from roughly 20 to 5 over that interval.

## Method

The authors use a one-dimensional recombination model that includes both one-jet (fragmentation) and two-jet (recombination of shower partons from adjacent jets) contributions to hadron production. The shower parton distributions (SPDs) are taken from earlier parameterizations (Hwa and Yang, 2004), and the two-jet contribution includes an overlap function Γ approximated as a constant or as a power law Γ(pT) ∝ pT⁻⁷. The nuclear suppression factor ξ is set to 0.01 and 0.03 as representative values for LHC, and the hard parton distributions include shadowing effects.

## Key numbers

- Proton-to-pion ratio R_{p/π} can be as high as 20 at pT ≈ 10 GeV/c for constant jet-overlap probability Γ = 10⁻¹.
- With a pT-dependent overlap Γ(pT) ∝ pT⁻⁷, R_{p/π} decreases from roughly 20 to 5 in the interval 10 < pT < 20 GeV/c.
- The two-jet contribution to pion production becomes insignificant at pT ∼ 20 GeV/c when Γ(pT) ∝ pT⁻⁷.
- For ξ = 0.01 and 0.03, the ratio R_{p/π} is approximately independent of ξ.
- The SPD for a gluon jet to produce a light quark at momentum fraction z = 1/3 is S^q_g(1/3) = 0.3, while at z = 1/2 it is S^q_g(1/2) = 0.13.

## Context

This work extends the recombination model previously applied to RHIC data (Hwa and Yang, 2004) to predict a new phenomenon at LHC: the dominance of two-jet recombination over fragmentation at high pT. It contrasts with the conventional fragmentation picture used in pp collisions and with the predictions of Fries and Muller (2004), which only considered the fragmentation term (Γ = 0). The predicted large proton-to-pion ratio and the absence of associated-particle peaks in Δφ distributions provide testable signatures that could distinguish recombination from fragmentation at LHC energies.
