# Isospin breaking in the $^{71}$Kr and $^{71}$Br mirror system

> Isospin symmetry is a fundamental concept in nuclear physics. Even though isospin symmetry is partially broken, it holds approximately for most nuclear systems, which makes exceptions very interesting from the nuclear structure perspective. In this framework, it is expected that the spins and parities of the ground states of mirror nuclei should be the same, in particular for the simplest systems where a proton is exchanged with a neutron or vice versa. In this work, we present evidence that this assumption is broken in the mirror pair $^{71}$Br and $^{71}$Kr system. Our conclusions are based on a high-statistics $β$ decay study of $^{71}$Kr and on state-of-the-art shell model calculations. In our work, we also found evidence of a new state in $^{70}$Se, populated in the $β$-delayed proton emission process which can be interpreted as the long sought coexisting 0$^+$ state.

## Key claim

The authors present evidence that the ground-state spin-parity assignments differ between the mirror pair $^{71}$Kr and $^{71}$Br, breaking the expected isospin symmetry for a T=1/2 system. The claim is hedged by noting that the shell model calculations, even after including Coulomb and charge symmetry breaking corrections, still place the $5/2^-$ state slightly below the $1/2^-$ state in $^{71}$Kr by 23 keV, but the experimental $\beta$ strength pattern strongly favors a $J^\pi = 1/2^-$ assignment for the $^{71}$Kr ground state within the resolution of the calculations.

## Method

The $\beta$ decay of $^{71}$Kr was studied at the RIKEN Nishina Center using a 345 MeV/nucleon $^{78}$Kr beam fragmented on a Be target, with reaction products separated by the BigRIPS separator and implanted into the WAS3ABi silicon detector array. Gamma rays were detected by the EURICA HPGe spectrometer, and $\beta$-delayed protons were distinguished from $\beta$ particles using an energy threshold of 1400 keV on the DSSSD signals. The accumulated Gamow-Teller strength deduced from the $\beta$ feeding pattern was compared to large-scale shell model calculations using the PFSDG-U interaction in a valence space including the 1p$_{3/2}$, 1p$_{1/2}$, 0f$_{5/2}$, 0g$_{9/2}$, and 1d$_{5/2}$ orbits, with Coulomb and charge symmetry breaking terms evaluated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of $^{71}$Kr: $t_{1/2}^{i\beta} = 94.50 \pm 0.06~(\text{stat.}) ^{+0.21}_{-0.23}~(\text{sys.})$ ms from implantation-beta correlations
- $\beta$-delayed proton branch: $\epsilon_p = 2.77(24)\%$
- Ground state to ground state feeding to the isobaric analogue state: 80.7(7)%, the lowest confirmed feeding among all studied T=1/2 mirror decays
- $Q_{EC}$ value: 10,180(130) keV
- Energy separation between the $5/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ states in $^{71}$Kr after full charge symmetry breaking corrections: 23 keV (with $5/2^-$ remaining lowest in the calculation)
- Excitation energy of the $1/2^-$ state in $^{71}$Br: 10 keV above the $5/2^-$ ground state

## Context

This work extends the previously known cases of mirror symmetry breaking in bound systems, which were limited to the T=3/2 $^{73}$Br/$^{73}$Sr pair studied by Hoffmann et al. (2023), and the particle-unbound $^{16}$F/$^{16}$N system explained by the Thomas-Ehrmann shift. The present result is the first established case of isospin breaking in a T=1/2 mirror pair, where only one nucleon is exchanged. The high-statistics data also enabled identification of a new 422 keV $\gamma$ transition in $^{70}$Se from $\beta$-delayed proton emission, interpreted as evidence for a long-sought coexisting $0^+$ state in $^{70}$Se, following the work of Smallcombe et al. (2014). The finding resolves the controversy between the earlier assignments of Oinonen et al. (1997) and Fischer et al. (2003) regarding the $^{71}$Kr ground state spin, and supports the earlier speculation by Urkedal and Hamamoto (1998) of a spin inversion in this mirror pair.
