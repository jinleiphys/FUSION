# Baryon form factors in a diquark-quark bound state description

> Nucleon form factors are calculated in a relativistic diquark--quark picture based on the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The nucleon wave function is obtained in a static approximation to the quark exchange interaction between the valence quark and the diquark. We evaluate the valence quark and $0^+$--diquark contribution to the nucleon electromagnetic and weak currents. We find reasonable electric charge radii, magnetic moments as in the additive diquark model, and $g_A \approx 1$. We discuss the dependence on the model parameters.

## Key claim

The nucleon form factors are calculated in a relativistic diquark-quark picture based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, using a static approximation to the quark exchange interaction; the results show that charge radii depend strongly on the diquark-quark wave function extent while magnetic moments and the axial coupling constant are rather insensitive to the binding strength and require inclusion of the 1+ diquark channel.

## Method

The authors start from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and rewrite it as an effective theory of mesons and baryons, obtaining the baryon Green's function from composite diquark and baryon fields. They solve for the nucleon bound state wave function using a static approximation to the quark exchange interaction, which reduces the Faddeev-type equation to a Dirac-type equation for the baryon amplitude. The nucleon electromagnetic and weak currents are then evaluated by coupling an external gauge field at quark level, considering contributions from the valence quark and the 0+ diquark, with the diquark propagator approximated by its pole form and the diquark vertex function taken on-shell.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: experimental value \(\langle r^2\rangle_p = 0.74\,{\rm fm}^2\) can be reproduced for cutoff \(\Lambda_B \approx 0.32\,{\rm GeV}\)
- Intrinsic charge radius of the scalar diquark contributes about \(10\ldots 20\%\) of the proton charge radius
- Magnetic moments in the additive diquark-quark picture with only 0+ diquarks: \(\mu_p = \frac{2}{3}(e/2M)\), \(\mu_n = -\frac{1}{3}(e/2M)\)
- Axial coupling constant: for realistic parameters, \(g_A < 1\); the value \(g_A = 1\) would be obtained in an additive diquark-quark scheme with only 0+ diquarks
- Diquark masses in the NJL model are typically of order \(0.5\ldots 0.6\,{\rm GeV}\)
- Exact Faddeev calculation gives nucleon binding energies \(\varepsilon_B = M + m_{0^+} - m_B \approx 50\ldots 100\,{\rm MeV} \ll m_B\)

## Context

This work extends a previous analysis of form factors in an additive diquark-quark scheme by Weiss (1993), now deriving the relativistic nucleon wave function within the model quark dynamics in a consistent approximation. The approach is based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as developed by Reinhardt (1990) and others, and uses the static approximation for quark exchange introduced by Buck (1992). The results point to the need for including the 1+ diquark channel, as noted in exact Faddeev calculations by Bentz (1993) and Meyer (1994), and suggest that the static approximation may be useful for calculating strong form factors of the nucleon.
