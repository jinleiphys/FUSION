# Proton-neutron pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei with Deformed Skyrme+pnBCS model

> We investigate the effects of neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp) and proton-neutron (pn) pairing correlations on the ground-states of $N = Z$ even-even $pf$-shell nuclei by using an axially symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock (HF)+pnBardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) model. We adopt a Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) SGII, together with contact volume- and surface-type pairing interactions, whose strengths are adjusted to reproduce empirical pairing gaps of each nucleus. It is shown that the strength of the IS pairing is correlated to the nuclear deformation: for oblate deformation with $-0.3<β_2<0.0$, a stronger IS pairing is required to reproduce the empirical pairing gap, while for prolate deformation a weaker one is enough. Among the eight $N=Z$ nuclei, we found that $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se and $^{72}$Kr show the dominance of isovector (IV) spin-singlet superfluidity, while lighter 5 nuclei $^{44}$Ti, $^{48}$Cr, $^{52}$Fe, $^{56}$Ni and $^{60}$Zn exhibit the coexistence of IV spin-singlet and isoscalar (IS) spin-triplet superfluidities. We found also that the IS abnormal density always exhibits the oblate deformation, regardless of whether the normal density is prolate, spherical or oblate.

## Key claim

The strength of isoscalar (IS) proton-neutron pairing required to reproduce empirical pairing gaps is correlated with nuclear deformation: for oblate deformations in the range \(-0.3 < \beta_2 < 0.0\) a stronger IS pairing strength is needed, while for prolate deformations a weaker IS pairing strength suffices. The authors do not find any nucleus in the mass range \(A=44-72\) that shows a dominant IS condensation phase.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional SGII, followed by a proton-neutron Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (pnBCS) treatment that includes neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp), and proton-neutron (pn) pairing correlations. Contact volume-type and surface-type pairing interactions were employed, with the strength parameters \(V_0^{pp}\), \(V_0^{nn}\), \(V_0^{IV}\), and \(V_0^{IS}\) adjusted independently to reproduce the empirical pairing gaps of each nucleus. The calculations were performed over a range of quadrupole deformation parameters \(-0.5 < \beta_2 < 0.5\) to map the ground-state energy surfaces.

## Key numbers

- Empirical pairing gaps for \(^{44}\)Ti: \(\Delta^{emp}_p = 2.631\) MeV, \(\Delta^{emp}_n = 2.653\) MeV, \(\Delta^{emp}_{pn} = 2.068\) MeV
- Empirical pairing gaps for \(^{72}\)Kr: \(\Delta^{emp}_p = 2.001\) MeV, \(\Delta^{emp}_n = 1.985\) MeV, \(\Delta^{emp}_{pn} = 1.353\) MeV
- The total energy at the minimum for \(^{44}\)Ti is reduced by about 2 MeV due to pn pairing correlations
- The ratio \(f = V_0^{IS}/V_0^{IV}\) has a maximum value of about 1.2 for \(\beta_2 \sim -0.2\) and is reduced to \(0.75-0.85\) for prolate \(\beta_2 \sim 0.2\)
- The pp and nn pairing strengths are always comparable with less than 10% difference; 20-30% larger strength differences between IV and IS pn interactions are induced by deformation for nuclei with \(A \ge 52\)
- The lowest total energies are reduced by about 1-3 MeV due to the inclusion of pn pairing correlations

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent mean-field calculations of pn pairing in N=Z nuclei by Goodman (1999), Bertsch and collaborators (2010, 2011), and Cheoun and collaborators (2015, 2018), who used realistic CD Bonn potentials or schematic forces. The present study uses a deformed Skyrme+pnBCS approach with independently varied IS and IV pairing strengths to examine the coexistence of spin-singlet and spin-triplet superfluidity across the pf-shell. The results enable future investigations of pn pairing effects in neutron-deficient nuclei along the N=Z line, particularly in connection with upcoming radioactive beam experiments.
