# Nuclear structure contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium

> We consider the two-photon exchange contribution to the $2P-2S$ Lamb shift in muonic deuterium in the framework of forward dispersion relations. The dispersion integrals are evaluated using experimental data on elastic deuteron form factors and inelastic electron-deuteron scattering, both in the quasielastic and hadronic range. The subtraction constant that is required to ensure convergence of the dispersion relation for the forward Compton amplitude $T_1(ν,Q^2)$ is related to the deuteron magnetic polarizability $β(Q^2)$. Based on phenomenological information, we obtain for the Lamb shift $ΔE_{2P-2S}=2.01\pm0.74$ meV. The main source of the uncertainty of the dispersion analysis is due to lack of quasielastic data at low energies and forward angles. We show that a targeted measurement of the deuteron electrodesintegration in the kinematics of upcoming experiments A1 and MESA at Mainz can help quenching this uncertainty significantly.

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic deuterium Lamb shift is calculated using forward dispersion relations and experimental data, yielding ΔE = 2.01 ± 0.74 meV, but the large uncertainty is dominated by the lack of quasielastic data at low energies and forward angles rather than by the method itself.

## Method
The authors evaluated dispersion integrals for the forward virtual Compton amplitude using experimental data on elastic deuteron form factors and inelastic electron-deuteron scattering. They separated the calculation into elastic, quasielastic (nucleon knock-out), hadronic (above pion threshold), and subtraction contributions, fitting available quasielastic data with a model that includes plane-wave Born approximation, final-state interactions, and a perpendicular component constrained by the Baldin sum rule.

## Key numbers
- Total two-photon exchange contribution to the 2P-2S Lamb shift: ΔE = 2.01 ± 0.74 meV
- Elastic contribution: −0.417 ± 0.002 meV
- Quasielastic PWBA contribution: −1.616 ± 0.739 meV
- Quasielastic FSI contribution: −0.391 ± 0.044 meV
- Perpendicular (transverse) contribution: −0.322 ± 0.003 meV
- Hadronic contribution: −0.028 ± 0.002 meV
- Magnetic polarizability subtraction term: +0.740 ± 0.040 meV
- Thomson term: +0.023 ± 0.001 meV
- Deuteron charge radius from isotope shift: r_E(d) = 2.1424 ± 0.0021 fm (CODATA 2010 based)
- Deuteron electric polarizability: α_E^d = 0.633 ± 0.001 fm^3; magnetic polarizability: β_M^d = 0.072 ± 0.005 fm^3
- Projected uncertainty reduction: a measurement at 80 MeV, 16° with 2% precision could reduce the quasielastic uncertainty to 67 μeV

## Context
This work extends the dispersive, data-driven approach previously applied to muonic hydrogen by Carlson (2011) and Birse (2012) to the muonic deuterium system, where the polarizability correction is about two orders of magnitude larger than for muonic hydrogen. The result is compared with potential model calculations by Pachucki (2013), Friar (2013), and Ji (2013), which give values between 1.68 and 1.94 meV but with smaller quoted uncertainties. The authors show that upcoming experiments at MAMI A1 and MESA in Mainz can reduce the dominant uncertainty from lack of low-energy quasielastic data, enabling a precise determination of the deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium Lamb shift measurements.
