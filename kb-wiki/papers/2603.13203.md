# $π$, K, and p production in high-multiplicity pp collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV

> This paper presents the measurement of $π^{\pm}$, K$^{\pm}$, and p($\bar{\text{p}}$) production in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV at midrapidity ($|y| < 0.5$) using the ALICE detector at the LHC. The transverse-momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra of these particles are reported for three high-multiplicity classes. The results show a mass- and multiplicity-dependent hardening of the $p_{\rm T}$ spectra and an enhancement of the p/$π$ ratio at intermediate $p_{\rm T}$. These features are similar to those observed in heavy-ion collisions, where quark-gluon plasma formation is expected. The new measurements have extended the highest average charged-particle multiplicity density per unit of pseudorapidity achieved in pp collisions, roughly a factor five higher than that in average inelastic pp collisions, thereby reducing the multiplicity gap between small and large collision systems. In addition, the results are further compared with previously published measurements and with model calculations obtained using distinct tunes of the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo generator, as well as with predictions from the EPOS4. The comparison of the $p_{\rm T}$-integrated K/$π$ and p/$π$ ratios across different collision systems and energies suggests that particle production scales with charged-particle multiplicity, rather than with collision energy or system size. While the PYTHIA 8 tunes and the EPOS4 model are able to reproduce some of these measurements, either quantitatively or qualitatively, none of them consistently describes all observed features of the data.

## Key claim

The paper reports that in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, the transverse-momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons exhibit a mass- and multiplicity-dependent hardening and an enhanced proton-to-pion ratio at intermediate pT, features similar to those seen in heavy-ion collisions where quark-gluon plasma is expected; however, the authors explicitly state that none of the tested Monte Carlo models (PYTHIA 8 tunes or EPOS4) consistently describes all observed features of the data.

## Method

The ALICE Collaboration measured the production of charged pions, kaons, and protons (and antiprotons) at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) in proton-proton collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV. The transverse-momentum (pT) spectra were obtained for three distinct high-multiplicity classes, defined by the charged-particle multiplicity density per unit of pseudorapidity. The results were compared to previously published measurements and to model calculations using different tunes of the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo generator and the EPOS4 model.

## Key numbers

- The highest average charged-particle multiplicity density per unit of pseudorapidity achieved in these pp collisions is roughly a factor five higher than that in average inelastic pp collisions.
- The pT spectra are reported for three high-multiplicity classes at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV.
- The pT-integrated K/pi and p/pi ratios are compared across different collision systems and energies.

## Context

This work extends previous ALICE measurements of identified particle production in smaller collision systems, significantly reducing the multiplicity gap between small (pp) and large (heavy-ion) collision systems. The results are compared with predictions from the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo generator (using distinct tunes) and the EPOS4 model, following earlier studies of particle production in high-multiplicity pp collisions. The finding that particle production scales with charged-particle multiplicity rather than collision energy or system size enables further investigation into the onset of collective-like effects in small systems, while the failure of current models to describe all features motivates improvements to event generators.
