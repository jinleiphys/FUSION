# Drag Force on Heavy Quarks and Spatial String Tension

> Heavy quark transport coefficients in a strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma can be evaluated using a gauge/string duality and lattice QCD. Via this duality, one can argue that for low momenta the drag coefficient for heavy quarks is proportional to the spatial string tension. Such a tension is well studied on the lattice that allows one to straightforwardly make non-perturbative estimates of the heavy quark diffusion coefficients near the critical point. The obtained results are consistent with those in the literature.

# Drag Force on Heavy Quarks and Spatial String Tension

## Key claim
For low momenta near the critical temperature of a strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma, the drag coefficient for heavy quarks is proportional to the spatial string tension, a relationship that holds for a class of five-dimensional geometries where the metric factors are decreasing functions of the radial coordinate, assuming the string model provides a reasonable approximation to QCD in the deconfined phase at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Method
The author uses the gauge/string duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) with a Nambu-Goto string on a deformed five-dimensional Schwarzschild-AdS spacetime to model heavy quark motion. The drag force is computed from the momentum flow along a trailing string worldsheet from the boundary to the black hole horizon, and the spatial string tension is obtained from minimal area surfaces for rectangular Wilson loops. The relationship between the drag coefficient and spatial string tension is derived in the non-relativistic limit by expanding the drag force expression to leading order in velocity and identifying the spatial string tension from the near-horizon geometry.

## Key numbers
- The drag force in the non-relativistic limit is given by F_drag = -sigma_s v + O(v^3), where sigma_s is the spatial string tension.
- For quenched QCD (pure SU(3) gauge theory), the spatial string tension parameterization from string theory gives T/sqrt(sigma_s) = (1/(pi sqrt(g))) exp(-(1/2)(T_c^2/T^2)) with g = 0.094, and the renormalization group parameterization gives T/sqrt(sigma_s) = (1/c)(2b_0 ln(T/Lambda) + (b_1/b_0) ln(2 ln(T/Lambda))) with c = 0.566 and Lambda = 0.104 T_c, where b_0 = 11/(4pi)^2 and b_1 = 102/(4pi)^4.
- For (2+1)-flavor QCD, the spatial string tension parameterization is T/sqrt(sigma_s) = a_0 + a_1 ln(T/T_c) with a_0 = 0.38 and a_1 = 0.26, and T_c fixed to 155 MeV.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient is D_s = T/sigma_s, and the momentum diffusion coefficient is D = T sigma_s.
- The dimensionless quantity 2pi T D_s = 2pi (T/sqrt(sigma_s))^2 is estimated to be in the range 1.5-32 for full QCD, with the author's estimates lying on the boundary of this range.

## Context
This work extends the result of Sin (year not specified) for the conformal case w = 1/r^2 to generic metric functions w(r), providing a background-independent (universal) relationship in the low-momentum regime. The approach uses lattice QCD data for the spatial string tension from quenched QCD simulations (lattice0) and (2+1)-flavor QCD simulations (lattice2+1) to make non-perturbative estimates of heavy quark diffusion coefficients near the critical point. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Datta (year not specified), Kar (year not specified), and Kac (year not specified), as well as T-matrix calculations by Rapp (year not specified) and perturbative QCD calculations from the Nantes group (year not specified), and are consistent with the range preferred by PHENIX data at RHIC. This enables extraction of diffusion coefficients from lattice simulations or dimensionally reduced QCD, as suggested by Laine (year not specified).
