# Mass and $KÎ›$ coupling of $N^*(1535)$

> Using resonance isobar model and effective Lagrangian approach, from recent BES results on $J/Ïˆ\to\bar ppÎ·$ and $Ïˆ\to\bar pK^+Î›$, we deduce the ratio between effective coupling constants of $N^*(1535)$ to $KÎ›$ and $pÎ·$ to be $R\equiv g_{N^*(1535)KÎ›}/g_{N^*(1535)pÎ·} =1.3\pm 0.3$. With previous known value of $g_{N^*(1535)pÎ·}$, the obtained new value of $g_{N^*(1535)KÎ›}$ is shown to reproduce recent $pp\to pK^+Î›$ near-threshold cross section data as well. Taking into account this large $N^*KÎ›$ coupling in the coupled channel Breit-Wigner formula for the $N^*(1535)$, its Berit-Wigner mass is found to be around 1400 MeV, much smaller than previous value of about 1535 MeV obtained without including its coupling to $KÎ›$. ## Key claim

The ratio of effective coupling constants of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance to $K\Lambda$ and $p\eta$ is deduced to be $R = 1.3 \pm 0.3$ from BES data on $J/\psi$ decays, but this value depends on the assumption that the $1/2^-$ $K\Lambda$ threshold peak is dominantly from the tail of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance.

## Method

The authors used a resonance isobar model with effective Lagrangians to calculate the decay widths of $J/\psi \to \bar p p \eta$ and $\psi \to \bar p K^+ \Lambda$ via the $N^*(1535)$ intermediate resonance. They extracted the ratio of coupling constants by comparing the calculated width ratio to the experimental branching ratios from BES and PDG. They then tested this ratio by including the $N^*(1535)$ contribution in a model for $pp \to p K^+ \Lambda$ near-threshold cross sections and compared to COSY data. Finally, they incorporated the $K\Lambda$ channel into a coupled-channel Breit-Wigner formula to recompute the resonance mass.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of effective coupling constants: $R \equiv g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda} / g_{N^*(1535)p\eta} = 1.3 \pm 0.3$, deduced from BES data on $J/\psi$ decays.
- $J/\psi$ decay branching ratio for $\bar p K^+ \Lambda$: $(0.89 \pm 0.16) \times 10^{-3}$, with $(15\sim 22)\%$ via the near-threshold $N^*$ resonance.
- $J/\psi$ decay branching ratio for $\bar p p \eta$: $(2.09 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-3}$, with $(56 \pm 15)\%$ via the $N^*(1535)$ resonance.
- With the $K\Lambda$ channel included in the coupled-channel Breit-Wigner formula, the Breit-Wigner mass of $N^*(1535)$ is found to be around 1400 MeV, with a width $\Gamma^0_{N^*} = 270$ MeV, compared to the previous values of about 1535 MeV and 150 MeV without the $K\Lambda$ coupling.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance by using new BES data on $J/\psi$ decays to determine its coupling to $K\Lambda$, a quantity that was poorly known from earlier $\pi N \to K\Lambda$ and $\gamma N \to K\Lambda$ reactions (Mosel, Lee). The large $N^*K\Lambda$ coupling supports the suggestion by Weise that $N^*(1535)$ may be a quasi-bound $(K\Sigma$-$K\Lambda)$ state, and the reduced Breit-Wigner mass of about 1400 MeV helps resolve the long-standing problem in constituent quark models (Capstick) of why $N^*(1535)$ is heavier than $N^*(1440)$. The results enable re-examination of previous calculations of $K\Lambda$ production processes and provide input for understanding the pentaquark configuration of $N^*$ resonances (Riska, Jaffe-Wilczek).
