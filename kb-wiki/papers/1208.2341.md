# Reexamining the temperature and neutron density conditions for r-process nucleosynthesis with augmented nuclear mass models

> We explore the effects of nuclear masses on the temperature and neutron density conditions required for r-process nucleosynthesis using four nuclear mass models augmented by the latest atomic mass evaluation. For each model we derive the conditions for producing the observed abundance peaks at mass numbers A ~ 80, 130, and 195 under the waiting-point approximation and further determine the sets of conditions that can best reproduce the r-process abundance patterns (r-patterns) inferred for the solar system and observed in metal-poor stars of the Milky Way halo. In broad agreement with previous studies, we find that (1) the conditions for producing abundance peaks at A ~ 80 and 195 tend to be very different, which suggests that, at least for some nuclear mass models, these two peaks are not produced simultaneously; (2) the typical conditions required by the critical waiting-point (CWP) nuclei with the N = 126 closed neutron shell overlap significantly with those required by the N=82 CWP nuclei, which enables coproduction of abundance peaks at A ~ 130 and 195 in accordance with observations of many metal-poor stars; and (3) the typical conditions required by the N = 82 CWP nuclei can reproduce the r-pattern observed in the metal-poor star HD 122563, which differs greatly from the solar r-pattern. We also examine how nuclear mass uncertainties affect the conditions required for the r-process and identify some key nuclei including76Ni to 78Ni, 82Zn, 131Cd, and 132Cd for precise mass measurements at rare-isotope beam facilities.

## Key claim
The temperature and neutron density conditions required for producing the r-process abundance peaks at A ~ 80 and 195 tend to be very different, suggesting these two peaks are not produced simultaneously for at least some nuclear mass models; however, the conditions required by the N=82 and N=126 critical waiting-point nuclei overlap significantly, enabling coproduction of the A ~ 130 and 195 peaks in accordance with observations of many metal-poor stars.

## Method
The authors used four nuclear mass models (FRDM, WS*, HFB-17, and RMF) augmented by the latest atomic mass evaluation AME2011-preview to calculate neutron separation energies. Under the waiting-point approximation with (n,γ)⇌(γ,n) equilibrium, they derived the temperature and neutron density conditions required for critical waiting-point nuclei with N=50, 82, and 126 closed neutron shells, then used a classical r-process approach with superposition of multiple neutron irradiation episodes to fit observed r-process abundance patterns.

## Key numbers
- The four mass models reproduce experimentally known neutron separation energies with root-mean-square deviations of 0.399 MeV (FRDM), 0.332 MeV (WS*), 0.506 MeV (HFB-17), and 0.653 MeV (RMF)
- The conditions required by N=50 CWP nuclei were determined using T₉ between 1 and 3 and neutron densities between 10²⁰ and 10³⁰ cm⁻³
- For the classical r-process calculations, T₉=1.5 was used with neutron densities of 10²⁰·⁰–10²²·⁵ cm⁻³ (N=50), 10²⁰·⁵–10²⁵·⁰ cm⁻³ (N=82), and 10²³·⁵–10²⁷·⁵ cm⁻³ (N=126)
- The two-neutron separation energy of ¹³⁰Cd is 5.488 MeV and of ¹³²Cd is 2.869 MeV in the WS* model
- No conditions could be found to accommodate all N=50 CWP nuclei for the HFB-17 model, where odd-even effects in neutron separation energy for Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes around N=50 are larger by ~1–1.5 MeV than for the other models

## Context
This work extends the classical r-process approach used by Kratz (1993), Freiburghaus (1999b), and others to examine how nuclear mass uncertainties affect the required temperature and neutron density conditions. The study builds on recent mass measurements including ⁸⁰Zn (Baruah 2008, Sun 2008b) and ¹³⁰Cd (Dillmann 2003) and the AME2011-preview evaluation (Audi 2011). The results identify key nuclei including ⁷⁶Ni to ⁷⁸Ni, ⁸²Zn, ¹³¹Cd, and ¹³²Cd as important candidates for precise mass measurements at rare-isotope beam facilities such as FAIR, FRIB, HIRFL-CSR, and RIKEN.
