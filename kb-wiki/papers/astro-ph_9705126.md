# Cumulative Parity Violation in Supernovae

> Supernovae provide a unique opportunity for large scale parity violation because they are dominated by neutrinos. We calculate the parity violating asymmetry A of neutrino emission in a strong magnetic field. We assume the neutrinos elastically scatter many times from slightly polarized neutrons. Because of multple interactions, A grows with the optical thickness of the proto-neutron star and may be much larger than previous estimates. As a result, the neutron star could recoil at a significant velocity.

# Cumulative Parity Violation in Supernovae

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in neutrino emission from a proto-neutron star grows linearly with the optical thickness of the star, because neutrinos undergo many repeated elastic scatterings from slightly polarized neutrons; this cumulative effect can produce asymmetries much larger than previous single-scattering estimates, potentially yielding neutron-star recoil velocities of order 400 km/s.

## Method

The authors performed a simple Monte Carlo simulation of neutrino transport through a uniform sphere of slightly polarized neutrons, using the full differential cross section for neutrino-neutron elastic scattering (including the parity-violating terms proportional to neutron polarization). Neutrinos were started either at the center or uniformly throughout the sphere, and the asymmetry in the angular distribution of escaping neutrinos was computed as a function of the sphere’s optical depth (radius divided by mean free path). The results were then scaled to realistic supernova parameters (polarization ~10⁻⁵, optical depth ~10⁴).

## Key numbers

- Neutron polarization: \( P_n \approx 2 \times 10^{-5} \times (B / 10^{13} \text{ G}) \times (3 \text{ MeV} / T) \) (Equation 2)
- For \( B = 5 \times 10^{12} \) G and \( T = 3 \) MeV: \( P_n \approx 1 \times 10^{-5} \) (Equation 6)
- Optical depth required for ~1 second diffusion time: \( r/\lambda \approx 1 \times 10^4 \) (Equation 7)
- Asymmetry scaling: \( A \approx \alpha P_n (r/\lambda) \) with \( \alpha \approx 0.14 \) for a central source and \( \alpha \approx 0.057 \) for uniformly distributed sources (Equation 8 and surrounding text)
- For realistic parameters (central source): \( A \approx 0.014 \) (Equation 9)
- Corresponding recoil velocity: \( v/c \approx 0.1 A \), giving \( v \approx 400 \) km/s (Equation 5 and surrounding text)

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of parity violation in supernovae by Vilenkin (1979) and Chugai (1984), who considered only single-scattering asymmetries, and contrasts with approaches focused on electron polarization (Dorafeev, Rodionov, and Ternov 1985; Horowitz and Piekarewicz 1997). The key advance is the demonstration that repeated interactions during neutrino diffusion can enhance the asymmetry by orders of magnitude. The results enable future work on asymmetries in temperature and chemical composition of the proto-neutron star, and suggest that parity-violating effects may be sensitive to the volume of the core occupied by strong magnetic fields rather than just the field strength at the surface.
