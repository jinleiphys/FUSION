# Shear Viscosity in a Perturbative Quark-Gluon-Plasma

> Among the key features of hot and dense QCD matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC is its very low shear viscosity, indicative of the properties of a near-ideal fluid, and a large opacity demonstrated by jet energy loss measurements. In this work, we utilize a microscopic transport model based on the Boltzmann equation with quark and gluon degrees of freedom and cross sections calculated from perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics to simulate an ideal Quark-Gluon-Plasma in full thermal and chemical equilibrium. We then use the Kubo formalism to calculate the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the medium as a function of temperature and system composition. One of our key results is that the shear viscosity over entropy-density ratio $η/s$ becomes invariant to the chemical composition of the system when plotted as a function of energy-density instead of temperature.

# Shear Viscosity in a Perturbative Quark-Gluon-Plasma

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s becomes invariant to the chemical composition of the system (gluon plasma vs. three-flavor quark-gluon plasma) when plotted as a function of energy-density rather than temperature, but only for energy-densities above 35 GeV/fm³. The values obtained for η/s are higher than those expected for the near-ideal fluid observed at RHIC and are not compatible with η/s values extracted from viscous fluid dynamics analysis of elliptic flow data.

## Method
The authors used the BMS implementation of the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), a microscopic transport model based on the Boltzmann equation, to simulate an ideal Quark-Gluon-Plasma in full thermal and chemical equilibrium within a box with periodic boundary conditions. They employed the Kubo formalism to calculate the shear viscosity from stress-energy tensor correlators, tracking parton momenta over 20 time-steps of 0.5 fm/c, and extracted the entropy density via the Gibbs relation. Cross sections were calculated from perturbative QCD matrix elements for 2→2 processes, regularized with a temperature-dependent Debye mass, using both a constant coupling and a temperature-dependent coupling with Λ_T = 30 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For energy-densities above 35 GeV/fm³, η/s shows excellent agreement between gluon plasma and quark-gluon plasma when plotted vs. energy-density.
- The effect of different Debye mass parametrizations on η/s is at the 10% to 15% level, with the Boltzmann Debye-mass giving systematically smaller values.
- Replacing fixed coupling with temperature-dependent coupling increases η/s by roughly a factor of 2.
- Changing from forward-backward peaked to isotropic scattering provides an additional 10% to 20% effect on η/s.
- The gluon plasma exhibits an upturn in η/s for temperatures below 500 MeV, attributed to a breakdown in perturbative approximations.

## Context
This work extends the methodology previously applied to hadron gas calculations by Demir (2008) to the deconfined phase above T_C, using the same Kubo formalism. It provides a perturbative QCD-based calculation of η/s for comparison with the KSS bound from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004) and with analytic HTL calculations by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003). The results enable quantitative assessment of how flavor composition affects η/s in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, and highlight that the low η/s values extracted from RHIC data by Luzum and Romatschke (2008) cannot be reproduced within perturbative assumptions without using coupling values where the PCM assumptions break down.
