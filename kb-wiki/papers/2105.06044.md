# Investigation of Experimental Observables in Search of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Heavy-ion Collisions in the STAR experiment

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a novel transport phenomenon, arising from the interplay between quantum anomalies and strong magnetic fields in chiral systems. In high-energy nuclear collisions, the CME may survive the expansion of the quark-gluon plasma fireball and be detected in experiments. Over the past decade, the experimental searches for the CME have aroused extensive interest at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The main goal of this article is to investigate three pertinent experimental approaches: the $γ$ correlator, the $R$ correlator and the signed balance functions. We will exploit both simple Monte Carlo simulations and a realistic event generator (EBE-AVFD) to verify the equivalence in the kernel-component observables among these methods and to ascertain their sensitivities to the CME signal for the isobaric collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper investigates three experimental observables (the gamma correlator, the R correlator, and signed balance functions) to search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, and demonstrates that these methods are mathematically related through their core components, with the caveat that the equivalence holds only under simplifying assumptions such as ignoring transverse momentum weighting and assuming equal numbers of positive and negative particles.

## Method

The authors used both a simple Monte Carlo toy model and the realistic Event-By-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (EBE-AVFD) event generator to compare the core components of the gamma correlator, the R correlator, and signed balance functions. The toy model generated pions with controlled collective flow and charge-separation signals, while EBE-AVFD dynamically simulated the CME transport for quark currents on top of a viscous QGP fluid with fluctuating initial conditions and local charge conservation effects. The simulations were performed for isobaric collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at RHIC energies to test the sensitivities of the observables to the CME signal.

## Key numbers

- The toy model used 195 pi+ and 195 pi- mesons per event to match the total multiplicity at midrapidities within 2 units of rapidity in 30-40% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The EBE-AVFD model used switching time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c and mixing parameter alpha_glb = 0.118 for initial conditions
- The initial axial charge density ratio n_5/s was set to 0, 0.1, and 0.2 to represent zero, modest, and strong CME signals respectively
- The relation between the R correlator and gamma correlator was derived as: S_concavity/sigma_R2^2 ≈ -(M/2)(M-1)*Delta_gamma_112, where M is the multiplicity
- The relation between signed balance functions and the gamma correlator was derived as: Delta_SBF ≈ (128M^2/pi^4)(Delta_gamma_112 - (4/3)v_2*Delta_delta)

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for the chiral magnetic effect at RHIC and LHC, which has been ongoing for over two decades without a definitive conclusion. The paper builds on prior approaches including the gamma correlator proposed by Voloshin (2004), the R correlator used by PHENIX, and signed balance functions proposed by Tang (2020) and Lin (2020). The study is specifically motivated by the STAR experiment's blind analysis of isobaric collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr), which have the same number of nucleons but different numbers of protons, providing an ideal test ground where the background remains similar while the CME signal differs. The mathematical relations derived between the three observables enable cross-checks of the experimental results and help interpret the upcoming isobaric collision data from the STAR blind analyses.
