# Constraints on a possible dineutron state from pionless EFT

> We investigate the sensitivity of the three-nucleon system to changes in the neutron-neutron scattering length to next-to-leading order in the pionless effective field theory, focusing on the the triton-3He binding energy difference and neutron-deuteron elastic scattering. Due to the appearance of an electromagnetic three-body counterterm at this order, the triton-3He binding energy difference remains consistent with the experimental value even for large positive neutron-neutron scattering lengths while the elastic neutron-deuteron scattering phase shifts are insensitive. We conclude that a bound dineutron cannot be excluded to next-to-leading order in pionless EFT.

## Key claim

A bound dineutron cannot be excluded based on the triton-3He binding energy difference and elastic scattering results in pionless effective field theory at next-to-leading order, because a new electromagnetic three-body counterterm that enters at this order can absorb the change in the binding energy difference even for large positive neutron-neutron scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors used pionless effective field theory (EFT) to compute the triton-3He binding energy difference and neutron-deuteron elastic scattering phase shifts as functions of the neutron-neutron scattering length, working to next-to-leading order. They solved coupled-channel integral equations for the three-nucleon system, including perturbative effective-range corrections and a new electromagnetic three-body counterterm required for renormalization of the proton-deuteron system at NLO, and varied the neutron-neutron scattering length from negative to positive values corresponding to a hypothetical bound dineutron.

## Key numbers

- The most precise determination of the neutron-neutron scattering length from the pi- d radiative capture reaction gives a value of -18.63 +/- 0.27 (expt.) +/- 0.30 (th.) fm.
- In free space the dineutron is believed to be unbound by about 100 keV, implying a large negative scattering length of about -20 fm.
- Varying the neutron-neutron effective range by +/-10% around 2.73 fm moves the NLO binding energy difference curves by about 0.1-0.15 MeV.
- Big bang nucleosynthesis is compatible with dineutron binding energies of up to 2.5 MeV.
- In lattice QCD at unphysically large pion masses of order 800 MeV, the dineutron is bound by about 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of Kirscher and Phillips (2011), who used pionless EFT to compute a correlation between the neutron-neutron scattering length and the triton-3He binding energy difference and concluded that a bound dineutron was excluded. The present NLO calculation shows that a new electromagnetic three-body counterterm, identified by Vanasse (2014) and Konig (2014), can absorb the binding energy shift, making a bound dineutron consistent with the data. The results support planned experimental searches for the dineutron via triton photodisintegration at HIGS/TUNL, and are consistent with the findings of Witala and Glockle (2012) that neutron-deuteron cross sections do not rule out a bound dineutron.
