# Ab initio description of monopole resonances in light- and medium-mass nuclei: I. Technical aspects and uncertainties of ab initio PGCM calculations

> Giant resonances (GRs) are a striking manifestation of collective motions in mesoscopic systems such as atomic nuclei. Until recently, theoretical investigations have essentially relied on the (quasiparticle) random phase approximation ((Q)RPA), and extensions of it, based on phenomenological energy density functionals (EDFs). As part of a current effort to describe GRs within an ab initio theoretical scheme, the present work promotes the use of the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM). This method, which can handle anharmonic effects while satisfying symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian, displays a favorable (i.e. mean-field-like) scaling with system's size. Presently focusing on the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) of light- and medium-mass nuclei, PGCM's potential to deliver wide-range ab initio studies of GRs in closed- and open-shell nuclei encompassing pairing, deformation, and shape coexistence effects is demonstrated. The comparison with consistent QRPA calculations highlights PGCM's unique attributes and sheds light on the intricate interplay of nuclear collective excitations. The present paper is the first in a series of four and focuses on technical aspects and uncertainty quantification of ab initio PGCM calculations of GMR using the doubly open-shell $^{46}$Ti as an illustrative example. The second paper displays results for a set of nuclei of physical interest and proceeds to the comparison with consistent (deformed) ab initio QRPA calculations. While the third paper analyzes useful moments of the monopolar strength function and different ways to access them within PGCM calculations, the fourth paper focuses on the effect of the symmetry restoration on the monopole strength function.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) can deliver *ab initio* descriptions of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in light- and medium-mass nuclei, including doubly open-shell systems, with a mean-field-like computational scaling. However, this claim is explicitly conditioned on the cancellation of dynamical correlations between ground and excited states, which the authors note is an extrapolation from low-lying collective states to the more highly excited states constituting giant resonances.

## Method
The authors perform two-dimensional PGCM calculations using the root-mean-square radius and axial quadrupole deformation as generator coordinates, starting from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states that break rotational and gauge symmetries, then restoring those symmetries via projection operators. They employ several chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (NLO, N$^2$LO, N$^3$LO from Hu et al., NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$, and EM 1.8/2.0) with SRG evolution applied to most, using the doubly open-shell nucleus $^{46}$Ti as an illustrative test case. The Hill-Wheeler-Griffin secular equation is solved to obtain the spectrum of $0^+$ states, from which the monopole strength function is constructed with Lorentzian smearing of width $\Gamma=0.5$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- The one-body harmonic oscillator basis is truncated at $e_{\text{max}}$ values ranging from 4 to 12, with oscillator frequencies $\hbar\omega$ from 12 to 24 MeV
- Three-body basis truncation is set to $e_{\text{3max}}=14$ systematically, except for $e_{\text{max}}=4$ where $e_{\text{3max}}=12$ is used
- SRG evolution is applied to flow parameters $\lambda=1.88$ fm$^{-1}$ ($\alpha=0.08$ fm$^4$) and $\lambda=2.24$ fm$^{-1}$ ($\alpha=0.04$ fm$^4$) for the Hu et al. Hamiltonians
- The EM 1.8/2.0 Hamiltonian uses 2N interaction evolved to $\lambda=1.8$ fm$^{-1}$ ($\alpha=0.0953$ fm$^4$) with 3N regulator at $\lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$
- The Lorentzian smearing width for strength functions is $\Gamma=0.5$ MeV
- HFB convergence in $^{46}$Ti is achieved at $e_{\text{max}}=10$ for NLO, while N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO converge at $e_{\text{max}}=10$ only for $\hbar\omega=12$ and 16 MeV
- Optimal $\hbar\omega=12$ MeV is identified for nuclei from $A\sim20$ to $A\sim50$

## Context
This work extends the *ab initio* PGCM approach, previously developed for low-lying collective states by Frosini et al. (2022), to giant resonances for the first time. It complements recent efforts using the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model by Baker et al. (2020) and Burrows et al. (2023) for open-shell medium-mass nuclei, while offering a computationally cheaper alternative with mean-field-like scaling. The paper is the first in a four-part series; Paper II will present results for a set of physically interesting nuclei and compare with consistent deformed *ab initio* QRPA calculations, Paper III will analyze moments of the strength function, and Paper IV will investigate the effect of angular momentum restoration on the monopole strength.
