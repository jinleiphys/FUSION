# Radial overlap correction to superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ nuclear $β$ decays using the shell model with Hartree-Fock radial wave functions

> The radial overlap correction, $δ_{RO}$ is re-examined for 30 superallowed $β$ decays using the shell model with Hartree-Fock (HF) radial wave functions. Our calculation is based on the Skyrme interaction including Coulomb, Charge-Symmetry-Breaking (CSB) and Charge-Independence-Breaking (CIB) terms. The corrections due to the gradient of charge density, vacuum polarization, Coulomb spin-orbit and finite size of nucleons are also considered. To avoid the spurious isospin mixing, the local equivalent potential is constructed from the solution of a HF calculation for the $Z=N$ nucleus without charge-dependent forces. The obtained mean field is solved non-iteratively for the parent and daughter nuclei. It turns out that the CIB term has no significant impact on $δ_{RO}$. On the other hand, the CSB term makes $δ_{RO}$ increasing between 10 and 30%. The gradient density leads to a further increase of $δ_{RO}$ between 2 and 14%, while other electromagnetic corrections are negligible. The effect of the suppression of isospin spuriosity is somewhat complicated. In general, it produces a larger $δ_{RO}$ value, however there are few cases for which $δ_{RO}$ is mostly unaffected or even reduced, especially the light even-even emitters. All these improvements partly explain the long-standing discrepancy between the correction values obtained with Woods-Saxon (WS) and HF radial wave functions. Nevertheless, the remaining discrepancy is still significant, except for the emitters with $A\le 26$. In addition, the staggering presented on the results obtained with the WS potential are not well reproduced by our calculations. This subsequently leads to a large difference in the predicted mirror $ft$ ratios. These problems might relate to the errors of the calculated radii as well as correlation in the data used for constraining the asymptotic radial wave functions.

## Key claim

The radial overlap correction δRO for 30 superallowed β decays is re-examined using shell model with Hartree-Fock radial wave functions; the authors find that including charge-symmetry-breaking terms increases δRO by 10–30% and gradient density corrections add another 2–14%, but a significant discrepancy with Woods-Saxon-based calculations remains except for emitters with A ≤ 26.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme effective interaction, including Coulomb, charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB), and charge-independence-breaking (CIB) terms, along with electromagnetic corrections from charge density gradient, vacuum polarization, Coulomb spin-orbit, and finite nucleon size. To avoid spurious isospin mixing, a local equivalent potential was constructed from a HF solution for the Z=N nucleus without charge-dependent forces, then solved non-iteratively for parent and daughter nuclei. The radial overlap correction δRO was computed using the parentage expansion formalism with shell-model wave functions from NuShellX@MSU, using different model spaces and effective interactions for each mass region (Cohen-Kurath for A=10–14, McGrory-Wildenthal for A=18–22, USD for A=26–34, modified ZBM2 for A=38–46, GXPF1A for A=50–54, and JUN45 for A=62–74).

## Key numbers

- CSB term increases δRO systematically between 10 and 30% across the mass range 10–74
- Gradient density correction increases δRO between 2 and 14%
- CIB term has no significant impact on δRO throughout the mass range between 10 and 74
- Remaining discrepancy between HF and Woods-Saxon results is significant except for emitters with A ≤ 26
- Coulomb displacement energy increases almost linearly with mass number, with local odd-even staggering more substantial for light nuclei with A ≤ 18

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of Ormand and Brown (1985, 1989, 1995) on δRO using Skyrme HF radial wave functions, and addresses the long-standing discrepancy with the Woods-Saxon-based calculations of Towner and Hardy (2008, 2015). The authors find that improvements from CSB and gradient corrections partly explain the discrepancy but do not fully resolve it, and the odd-even staggering seen in Woods-Saxon results is not reproduced, leading to large differences in predicted mirror ft ratios. This work enables further refinement of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections needed for precise tests of CVC and CKM unitarity, but suggests that errors in calculated charge radii and correlation effects in separation energy data may need to be addressed.
