# Detecting the QCD axion via the ferroaxionic force with piezoelectric materials

> We show that piezoelectric materials can be used to source virtual QCD axions, generating a new axion-mediated force. Spontaneous parity violation within the piezoelectric crystal combined with time-reversal violation from aligned spins provide the necessary symmetry breaking to produce an effective in-medium scalar coupling of the axion to nucleons up to 7 orders of magnitude larger than that in vacuum. We propose a detection scheme based on nuclear spin precession caused by the axion's pseudoscalar coupling to nuclear spins. This signal is resonantly enhanced when the distance between the source crystal and the spin sample is modulated at the spin precession frequency. Using this effect, future experimental setups can be sensitive to the QCD axion in the unexplored mass range from $10^{-5}\,\mathrm{eV}$ to $10^{-2}\,\mathrm{eV}$.

## Key claim

Piezoelectric crystals with aligned nuclear spins can generate a static, virtual QCD axion field through the axion's irreducible coupling to gluons, producing an effective in-medium scalar coupling to nucleons up to 7 orders of magnitude larger than the Standard Model vacuum scalar coupling, without requiring additional CP violation beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors derive the effective in-medium scalar coupling from the QCD axion's defining interaction with gluons, which at low energies generates couplings to nuclear Schiff moments (SM) and magnetic quadrupole moments (MQM) in a piezoelectric crystal with polarized nuclear spins. They propose a detection scheme based on nuclear spin precession in a laser-polarized 3He sample, where the distance between the source crystal and the spin sample is modulated at the spin precession frequency to resonantly enhance the signal. The source mass is a flat plate of a pyroelectric or piezoelectric crystal containing heavy deformed nuclei with large SMs or MQMs, such as 153Eu, 235U, or 237Np.

## Key numbers

* The effective in-medium scalar coupling can reach up to 7 orders of magnitude larger than the vacuum scalar coupling g_s^N ~ 10^{-30} (10^9 GeV / f_a).
* The proposed setup can be sensitive to QCD axion masses from 10^{-5} eV to 10^{-2} eV.
* The magnetic field sensitivity from quantum spin projection noise is delta B ~ 3 x 10^{-19} T for a 3He sample with volume 1 mm^3, spin density 10^{21} cm^{-3}, and transverse relaxation time T_2 = 1000 s.
* The corresponding Rabi frequency sensitivity is delta Omega_theta ~ 3 x 10^{-11} rad s^{-1} for the same fiducial parameters.
* The Schiff moment of 153Eu is 0.15 (theta_a e fm^3), of 235U is 3 (theta_a e fm^3), and of 237Np is 0.75 (theta_a e fm^3).
* The magnetic quadrupole moment of 153Eu is 1.0 (theta_a e fm^2), of 235U is 3.1 (theta_a e fm^2), and of 237Np is 1.3 (theta_a e fm^2).
* The axion mass is related to the decay constant as m_a ~ 5.7 meV (10^9 GeV / f_a).
* The pseudoscalar coupling to nucleons is g_p^N ~ c_N x 10^{-9} (10^9 GeV / f_a), with c_proton ~ 0.47 and c_neutron ~ 0.02 in the KSVZ model.
* Two concrete geometries are considered with minimum separations D = 150 microns and D = 1 mm between source and sample.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the "piezoaxionic effect" from Arvanitaki (2021), where a QCD axion dark matter background was shown to excite strain in piezoelectric crystals, by instead using a spin-polarized piezoelectric crystal to source a static axion field without applied stress. The proposed experimental setup is a modification of the ARIADNE experiment (Arvanitaki 2014, Geraci 2017), replacing the source mass with a spin-polarized piezoelectric crystal and adding a dilution refrigerator and cryogenic linear stage. The new "ferroaxionic force" provides a model-independent source for monopole-dipole forces mediated by the QCD axion, enabling a new class of short-range force experiments that can probe axion parameter space complementary to cavity experiments and astrophysical constraints.
