# Anomalous Isotope Shifts in Pb Nuclei in RMF Theory

> We have studied the anomalous behaviour of isotopic shifts of Pb nuclei in the relativistic mean field theory. It has been shown that the relativistic mean field provides an excellent description of the anomalous kink in the isotopic shifts about $^{208}$Pb. This is in contrast from density-dependent Skyrme forces which do not reproduce the observed trend in the empirical data on the charge radii. We discuss some differences in the description of isotope shifts in the RMF theory and the Skyrme mean field.

# Anomalous Isotope Shifts in Pb Nuclei in RMF Theory

## Key claim
The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, using the NL-SH force, reproduces the anomalous kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei around 208Pb, whereas standard density-dependent Skyrme forces such as SkM*, Ska, and SIII fail to describe the isotope shifts for nuclei heavier than 208Pb. The authors suggest that the difference arises from the distinct density dependence and saturation mechanisms of the two approaches, and that an isospin-dependent spin-orbit term in the Skyrme force can also produce reasonable values for the kink.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree calculations within the RMF theory using the nonlinear sigma-omega-rho model with parameter sets NL1 and NL-SH. Even-mass Pb isotopes from A = 190 to 214 were considered, with axially symmetric configurations assumed and wavefunctions expanded in N = 12 oscillator shells; pairing was included via the BCS formalism with pairing gaps obtained from particle separation energies of neighboring nuclei. The calculated charge radii were used to obtain isotope shifts relative to 208Pb, with the liquid-drop contribution subtracted for comparison with empirical data from atomic beam laser spectroscopy.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of Pb isotopes obtained with NL-SH show deviations from empirical data of at most 0.1%.
- The asymmetry energy for NL1 is about 44 MeV, which is larger than the empirical value.
- The force NL1 shows a systematic deviation from empirical binding energies for lighter isotopes as neutron excess decreases.
- SkM* shows an almost linear function with mass number for isotope shifts, displaying clear divergence from empirical data on the heavier side of 208Pb.
- For the Skyrme spin-orbit term, the standard (isospin independent) case uses W1 = 2 W2, while the strong isospin-dependent case uses W1 = 1.05 W2.

## Context
This work extends the investigation of isotope shifts in Pb nuclei previously studied with density-dependent Skyrme forces by Tondeur (ref. 4 in the paper), which could not describe the kink for nuclei heavier than 208Pb even with ground-state correlations. The RMF approach using the NL-SH force, developed by Sharma and Ring (1992) to resolve issues with neutron skin thickness and asymmetry energy in earlier forces NL1 and NL2, succeeds where Skyrme forces fail. The results enable further study of shell effects across magic numbers, which are significant for astrophysical r-processes, and point to the importance of the spin-orbit interaction's isospin dependence, as recently explored by other authors (ref. 13 in the paper).
