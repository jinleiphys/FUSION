# Scattering of nucleons from nuclei with couplings to particle-unstable excited states

> The physics of radioactive ion beams implies the description of weakly-bound nuclear systems. One key aspect concerns the coupling to low-lying collective-type excited states, which for these systems might not be stable levels, but particle emitting resonances. In this work we describe how the scattering cross section and compound spectra change when the colliding fragments have such collective excitations featuring particle emission. We explore this question in the framework of a multi-channel algebraic scattering method of determining nucleon-nucleus cross sections at low energies. For a range of light-mass, particle-unstable nuclear targets, scattering cross sections as well as the spectra of the compound nuclei formed have been determined from calculations that do and do not consider particle emission widths for nuclear states. Assuming a resonance character for target states markedly varies evaluated cross sections from those obtained assuming the target spectrum to have entirely discrete states.

## Key claim

Assuming that low-lying excited states of weakly-bound, light-mass nuclear targets are particle-emitting resonances with finite widths, rather than discrete zero-width states, markedly alters calculated nucleon-nucleus scattering cross sections and compound-nucleus spectra, though the effect is system-dependent and the simple Heaviside step function used to handle subthreshold behavior is acknowledged as an insufficient first approximation.

## Method

The authors used the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method, which solves coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space via finite-rank separable representations of the nucleon-nucleus interaction matrix, generated from sturmian functions with orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials to enforce the Pauli principle. For a range of light-mass, particle-unstable targets (⁸Be, ¹⁴O, ⁶He), they performed two sets of calculations: one treating all target excited states as discrete (zero-width) and another assigning them their experimentally known particle-emission widths, thereby making the channel energies and Green's functions complex. A Heaviside step function at zero projectile energy was applied to force the target-state width to vanish below threshold, preventing instability in subthreshold compound states.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to nuclei away from the valley of stability with all target states taken as zero-width (Canton 2006, Canton 2006a), by incorporating particle-emission widths for target excited states. It contrasts with earlier coupled-channel analyses of radioactive ion beam data that assumed discrete spectra (Riley 1998, Amos 2003) and with intermediate-energy studies that included additional decay channels but not decay widths (Gould 2009). The results enable improved modeling of nucleon scattering from weakly-bound, exotic nuclei near the drip lines, though the authors note that more sophisticated methods for handling the energy dependence of target resonance widths below threshold are needed, and that higher-precision experimental data would be valuable for further validation.
