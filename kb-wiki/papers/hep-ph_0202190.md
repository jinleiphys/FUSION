# Analysis of one- and two-particle spectra at RHIC based on a hydrodynamical model

> We calculate the one-particle hadronic spectra and correlation functions of pions based on a hydrodynamical model. Parameters in the model are so chosen that the one-particle spectra reproduce experimental results of $\sqrt{s}=130A$GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Based on the numerical solution, we discuss the space-time evolution of the fluid. Two-pion correlation functions are also discussed. Our numerical solution suggests the formation of the quark-gluon plasma with large volume and low net baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hydrodynamical model with a first-order phase transition equation of state can reproduce the one-particle spectra from \(\sqrt{s}=130A\) GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and that the numerical solution suggests formation of a quark-gluon plasma with large volume and low net baryon density. However, the model's HBT radii (especially \(R_{\text{out}}\) and \(R_{\text{long}}\)) are larger than experimental data, and the \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) ratio does not reproduce the decreasing trend with transverse mass seen in RHIC data, indicating a possible different particle emission mechanism.

## Method

The authors numerically solve relativistic hydrodynamical equations for a perfect fluid with cylindrical symmetry, assuming local thermal and chemical equilibrium and using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition. Initial conditions are set at proper time \(\tau=1.0\) fm with Bjorken longitudinal flow and no initial transverse flow; parameters are tuned so that calculated single-particle spectra reproduce experimental data from RHIC Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=130A\) GeV. Two-pion correlation functions are computed from the freeze-out hypersurface via the Cooper-Frye formula, including resonance decays (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\eta\), \(K^*\), \(\Delta\)), and HBT radii are extracted by Gaussian fitting.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density at RHIC: \(E_{\text{max}} = 6.0\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (only 5% higher than the SPS value of 5.74 GeV/fm\(^3\) at the same initial time \(\tau=1.0\) fm).
- Maximum net baryon density at RHIC: \(n_{\text{max}} = 0.125\) fm\(^{-3}\) (compared to 0.7 fm\(^{-3}\) at SPS).
- Total net baryon number at RHIC: 132 (compared to 305 at SPS).
- Average freeze-out temperature at RHIC: \(\langle T_{\text{f}} \rangle = 125\) MeV.
- Average baryon chemical potential at RHIC: \(\langle \mu_{\text{B}} \rangle = 76.1\) MeV (compared to 280.2 MeV at SPS).
- Average transverse flow velocity at RHIC (within \(|\eta| \leq 0.1\)): \(\langle v_{\text{T}} \rangle = 0.51\) (compared to 0.467 at SPS).
- Time duration of the fluid at RHIC: about 6 fm.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamical modeling from SPS energies (Pb+Pb at 17.4 \(A\)GeV) to the higher-energy RHIC regime, using an equation of state from Nonaka (2000) and a numerical method from Ishii (1993). The model successfully reproduces single-particle spectra from PHOBOS and STAR collaborations, but the HBT radii puzzle (especially the \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) ratio decreasing with transverse mass at RHIC, contrary to the model's prediction) points to unresolved issues in the particle emission mechanism, motivating further theoretical investigation. The results are compared to prior work by Rischke (1996) on the \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) ratio as a lifetime indicator.
