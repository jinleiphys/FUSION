# Hadronization in heavy ion collisions: Recombination and fragmentation of partons

> We argue that the emission of hadrons with transverse momentum up to about 5 GeV/c in central relativistic heavy ion collisions is dominated by recombination, rather than fragmentation of partons. This mechanism provides a natural explanation for the observed constant baryon-to-meson ratio of about one and the apparent lack of a nuclear suppression of the baryon yield in this momentum range. Fragmentation becomes dominant at higher transverse momentum, but the transition point is delayed by the energy loss of fast partons in dense matter.

## Key claim

Hadron emission with transverse momentum up to about 5 GeV/c in central relativistic heavy ion collisions is dominated by recombination of partons rather than fragmentation, but this holds only when the parton spectrum has an exponential (thermal) shape; when the spectrum becomes a power law at higher momentum, fragmentation takes over.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for meson and baryon yields from recombination using light-cone wave functions and single-particle Wigner functions for quarks and antiquarks on a freeze-out hypersurface. They then evaluate these expressions for two cases: an exponential thermal parton spectrum (with temperature T ≈ 350 MeV and rapidity width Δ ≈ 2) and a perturbative power-law spectrum from lowest-order QCD with energy loss corrections. The thermal part is normalized to PHENIX charged hadron data, and the crossover between recombination and fragmentation is determined by comparing the two contributions.

## Key numbers

- The baryon-to-meson ratio from a thermal parton spectrum is predicted to be constant as a function of momentum, with the proton-to-pion ratio given by dN_p/dN_π+ = (5/3) exp(μ_B/T), yielding a plateau around unity in the range 2 GeV/c ≤ P_T ≤ 4 GeV/c.
- The crossover between recombination and fragmentation occurs at about 5 GeV/c for hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The thermal parton spectrum uses an effective temperature T ≈ 350 MeV and rapidity width Δ ≈ 2.
- The energy loss of fast partons is modeled by a shift Δp_T = −√(λ p_T) with λ = 1 GeV.
- The Λ/K⁰_s ratio is predicted to plateau at 4/3 from recombination, considering contributions from Λ, Σ⁰, and Σ*⁰ states.

## Context

This work extends earlier recombination ideas applied at small transverse momentum (recomb) and the scaling model of Hwa and Yang (2002), and directly addresses the puzzle of the constant baryon-to-meson ratio and the lack of nuclear suppression for baryons observed by PHENIX and STAR at RHIC. The authors predict a rapid drop in the p/π⁺ ratio between 4 and 5 GeV/c, which subsequent experiments could test, and note that the saturation of elliptic flow v₂ for baryons at higher P_T than for mesons follows naturally from the recombination kinematics. The paper is closely related to the contemporaneous work of Greco et al. (2003), who independently proposed parton recombination to explain the large baryon/meson ratio at RHIC.
