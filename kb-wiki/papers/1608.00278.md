# Inclusive breakup of Borromean nuclei

> We derive the inclusive breakup cross section of a three-fragment projectile nuclei, $a = b +x_1 + x_2$, in the spectator model. The resulting four-body cross section for observing $b$, is composed of the elastic breakup cross section which contains information about the correlation between the two participant fragments, and the inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section. This latter cross section is found to be a non-trivial four-body generalization of the Austern formula \cite{Austern1987}, which is proportional to a matrix element of the form, $\langle\hatρ_{{x_1},{x_2}}\left|\left[W_{x_1} + W_{x_2} + W_{3B}\right]\right|\hatρ_{{x_1}, {x_2}}\rangle$. The new feature here is the three-body absorption, represented by the imaginary potential, $W_{3B}$. We analyze this type of absorption and supply ideas of how to calculate its contribution.

# Inclusive breakup of Borromean nuclei

## Key claim

The inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section for a three-fragment projectile is a non-trivial four-body generalization of the Austern formula, containing a new three-body absorption term represented by the imaginary potential W₃B. The authors derive the formalism but state that the detailed derivation of this potential and its interpretation are given in a separate work (CFH2016), and that a full DWBA analysis using Faddeev-Yakubosky equations is still in progress.

## Method

The authors extend the spectator model, previously applied to two-fragment projectiles (a = b + x), to three-fragment projectiles (a = b + x₁ + x₂) where b is the detected spectator fragment and x₁, x₂ are the participant fragments. They derive the four-body inclusive breakup cross section as the sum of an elastic breakup term and an inclusive non-elastic breakup term (the Carlson-Frederico-Hussein or CFH cross section), with the latter expressed as a matrix element of the sum of two-body imaginary potentials W_{x₁} + W_{x₂} plus a genuine three-body absorptive potential W₃B.

## Key numbers

- The four-body elastic breakup cross section (Eq. 4BEB) contains a delta function δ(E − E_b − E_{k_X}) enforcing energy conservation between the incident energy, the observed fragment energy, and the energy of the x₁ + x₂ system.
- The inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section (Eq. CFH) is proportional to the density of states ρ_b(E_b) = μ_b k_b / [(2π)³ ℏ²].
- The four-body reaction cross section is decomposed as σ_{R,4B} = σ_{R,x₁} + σ_{R,x₂} + σ_{R,3B}, where σ_{R,3B} = (2/ℏ v_a) ⟨ρ̂_X|W₃B|ρ̂_X⟩.
- The paper discusses experimental data on reactions such as ⁶Li + ²⁰⁹Bi → α + X at E_Lab = 24 MeV and 32 MeV (Moro1-2015).

## Context

This work extends the Austern formula (Austern 1987) for two-fragment projectile breakup to three-fragment projectiles, including Borromean nuclei such as ⁶He = α + n + n, ¹¹Li = ⁹Li + n + n, ¹⁴Be = ¹²Be + n + n, and ²²C = ²⁰C + n + n, as well as the stable nucleus ⁹Be = α + α + n. The derivation addresses a limitation of the effective two-body "four-body" Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels model, which cannot calculate the incomplete fusion part of the total fusion cross section (DTT2002). The formalism is potentially important for reactions involving two-neutron transfer and the population of Giant Pairing Vibration states (Cappuzzello 2015), and for applications such as the Surrogate Method in actinide nuclei (Potel 2015, Escher 2012).
