# The Pressure in 2, 2+1 and 3 Flavour QCD

> We calculate the pressure in QCD with two and three light quarks on a lattice of size 16^3x4 using tree level improved gauge and fermion actions. We argue that for temperatures T > 2T_c systematic effects due to the finite lattice cut-off and non-vanishing quark masses are below 15% in this calculation and give an estimate for the continuum extrapolated pressure in QCD with massless quarks. We find that the flavour dependence of the pressure is dominated by that of the Stefan-Boltzmann constant. Furthermore we perform a calculation of the pressure using 2 light (m_u,d/T=0.4) and one heavier quark (m_s/T = 1). In this case the pressure is reduced relative to that of three flavour QCD. This effect is stronger than expected from the mass dependence of an ideal Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for temperatures above about twice the critical temperature (T > 2 T_c), systematic effects from the finite lattice spacing and non-zero quark masses are below 15%, allowing an estimate of the continuum-extrapolated pressure in QCD with massless quarks. The authors find that the reduction of the pressure relative to the ideal gas limit is stronger for a heavier strange quark than would be expected from the mass dependence of an ideal Fermi gas alone.

## Method

The pressure was calculated non-perturbatively using the integral method on a 16^3 x 4 lattice, employing tree-level improved gauge and staggered fermion (p4) actions. The temperature scale was set by computing the string tension from the heavy quark potential on symmetric 16^4 lattices. Simulations were performed for two and three degenerate light quarks (m/T = 0.4) and for two light quarks plus a heavier strange quark (m_s/T = 1.0), with the pseudo-critical coupling determined from the peak of the chiral susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- For the p4-action on N_tau = 4, the free fermion pressure is 0.5932 of the continuum Stefan-Boltzmann value (one flavour, massless limit).
- For the p4-action on N_tau = 6, the free fermion pressure is 0.9378 of the continuum Stefan-Boltzmann value.
- The pseudo-critical couplings are beta_c = 3.646(4) for 2 flavours, 3.543(2) for 2+1 flavours, and 3.475(2) for 3 flavours.
- The ratio T_c / sqrt(sigma) is 0.480(10) for 2 flavours, 0.480(10) for 2+1 flavours, and 0.470(9) for 3 flavours.
- The pseudo-scalar to vector meson mass ratio is approximately 0.7 for all three flavour contents.
- For the quark masses used, the effective degrees of freedom are g(0.4) = 0.9672 and g(1) = 0.8275.
- The estimated continuum pressure for massless QCD at T > 2 T_c is about 15% above the values obtained on the N_tau = 4 lattice.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the QCD equation of state using standard Wilson gauge and staggered fermion actions, such as those by Blum et al. (1995) and Bernard et al. (1997), which showed large cut-off effects. It also revisits the pioneering study of strange quark contributions by Kogut et al. (1988), which found strong suppression of the strange quark energy density. The improved p4 action reduces cut-off effects significantly compared to the standard actions and the Naik action, enabling a more reliable estimate of the continuum pressure. The results enable more phenomenological quasi-particle models for the QCD equation of state, as developed by Peshier et al. (1996) and Levai and Heinz (1998), and are consistent with hard thermal loop resummed perturbative calculations by Andersen et al. (1999) and Blaizot et al. (1999).
