# Bayesian inference of the dense matter equation of state built upon extended Skyrme interactions

> The non-relativistic model of nuclear matter with Brussels extended Skyrme interactions is employed in order to build, within a Bayesian approach, models for the dense matter equation of state (EOS). In addition to a minimal set of constraints on nuclear empirical parameters; the density behavior of the energy per particle in pure neutron matter (PNM); a lower limit on the maximum neutron star (NS) mass, we require that the Fermi velocity of neutrons ($v_{\mathrm{F;\,n}}$) in PNM and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) with densities up to $0.8~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ (arbitrary) does not exceed the speed of light. The latter condition is imposed in order to cure a deficiency present in many Skyrme interactions [Duan and Urban, Phys. Rev. C 108, 025813 (2023)]. We illustrate the importance of this constraint for the posterior distributions. Some of our models are subjected to constraints on the density dependence of neutron (nucleon) Landau effective mass in PNM (SNM), too. The impact of various sets of constraints on the behaviors of nuclear matter and NSs is discussed in detail. Systematic comparison with results previously obtained by employing Skyrme interactions is done for posteriors of both nuclear matter (NM) and NS parameters. Special attention is given to the model and constraints dependence of correlations among various quantities.

## Key claim

The authors use Brussels extended Skyrme interactions (which include extra density-dependent terms beyond standard Skyrme) to generate dense matter equations of state via Bayesian inference, and they impose a new constraint, that the neutron Fermi velocity in pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter does not exceed the speed of light up to 0.8 fm⁻³, to cure a deficiency recently identified in many standard Skyrme parametrizations by Duan and Urban (2023).

## Method

The authors employed the non-relativistic Brussels extended Skyrme functional, which adds two density-dependent momentum terms to the standard Skyrme interaction, to generate nuclear matter equations of state within a Bayesian framework. They imposed constraints from nuclear empirical parameters (saturation density, energy per particle, compression modulus, symmetry energy), χEFT calculations of the energy per particle in pure neutron matter and Landau effective masses at three densities (0.08, 0.12, 0.16 fm⁻³), and a lower limit of 2 M⊙ on the maximum neutron star mass. The key new constraint required that the neutron Fermi velocity in both pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter remain below the speed of light up to 0.8 fm⁻³, with some runs also including correlations among the χEFT data points.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian study of purely nucleonic dense matter previously performed by Beznogov et al. (2024, Paper I) using standard Skyrme interactions, now employing the more flexible Brussels extended Skyrme functional. The new Fermi velocity constraint addresses a deficiency identified by Duan and Urban (2023) in many standard Skyrme parametrizations, where the neutron Fermi velocity exceeds the speed of light at high densities. The results enable systematic comparison with other mean-field models such as relativistic mean field models with density-dependent couplings (Malik et al. 2022, Beznogov et al. 2023) and non-linear couplings (Malik et al. 2023, Imam et al. 2024), allowing assessment of model dependence in nuclear matter and neutron star parameter posteriors.
