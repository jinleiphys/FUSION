# Theoretical evaluation of decay mode of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ in solid samples

> The excitation energy of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ is extremely low at $ 8.4 \, \mathrm{eV} $; thus, this isotope exhibits changes in its decay modes depending on the chemical state, specifically the outermost electronic states. However, the reported half-lives of the $ γ$-ray transition are not consistent among the previous experiments. In this study, we investigate the chemical states of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ by density functional theory calculations. Based on these results, we evaluate the relationship between the experimental half-life of each sample and the electronic state of $ \mathrm{Th} $. The calculation results indicate that ion trap method, $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ model and $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ one decay only via the $ γ$-ray transition, whereas $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ one decays via the $ γ$-ray transition and has a possibility of decay via internal conversion and electron bridge.

## Key claim

The decay mode of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ in solid samples depends on the specific chemical environment, with density functional theory calculations showing that ion trap method, $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ model, and $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ model decay only via the gamma-ray transition, whereas $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ can decay via gamma-ray transition and has a possibility of decay via internal conversion and electron bridge, contingent on whether the Th atom occupies a gap or replaced site in the crystal.

## Method

The authors performed density functional theory calculations using Quantum ESPRESSO for periodic boundary condition structural optimization of Th-doped $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $, $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $, and $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ crystals, considering both gap-site and substitutional (replaced) models with initial Th charges from 0 to +4. They then extracted clusters around the Th atom and conducted natural bond orbital analysis using the Amsterdam Density Functional code with the B3LYP functional and zeroth-order relativistic approximation to obtain stabilized charges, HOMO binding energies, and s-electron populations relevant to internal conversion and electron bridge decay processes.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ is 8.4 eV (wavelength approximately 150 nm)
- Ion trap method half-life: 1400 (+600, -300) s for $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th}^{3+} $ in vacuum
- $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ half-life in crystal: 630(15) s (Tiedau 2024) and 447(25) s (Hiraki 2024); corresponding vacuum half-lives: 1740(50) s and 1790 (64 stat, 80 sys) s
- $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ half-life in crystal: 670(102) s; vacuum half-life: 2210(304) s
- $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ half-life in crystal: 394(9 stat, 14 sys) s; vacuum half-life: 1289(30 stat, 45 sys) s
- HOMO binding energy for $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ gap model: 4.0 eV; $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ replaced model: 3.0 eV
- HOMO binding energy for $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ gap model: 5.9 eV; $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ replaced model: 24.7 eV
- HOMO binding energy for $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ gap model: 15.5 eV; $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $-$ \mathrm{Al} $ replaced: 4.1 eV; $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $-$ \mathrm{Sr} $ replaced: 4.3 eV
- Stabilized charge of Th in $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ gap: +2; $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ replaced: +3; $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ gap: +2; $ \mathrm{Mg} \mathrm{F}_2 $ replaced: +3; $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ gap: +1; $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $-$ \mathrm{Al} $ replaced: +3; $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $-$ \mathrm{Sr} $ replaced: +2

## Context

This work extends prior DFT studies of $ {}^{229m} \mathrm{Th} $ decay (Karpeshin 2018, Muller 2017, Porsev 2021) that focused on isolated atomic states, and a recent DFT study of Th-doped $ \mathrm{Ca} \mathrm{F}_2 $ and $ \mathrm{Li} \mathrm{Sr} \mathrm{Al} \mathrm{F}_6 $ crystals that analyzed density of states without systematic decay mode evaluation. The calculations address the inconsistency among reported gamma-ray half-lives from experiments by Yamaguchi (2024), Tiedau (2024), Hiraki (2024), Kraemer (2023), and Elwell (2024), suggesting that differences in Th chemical state and site occupancy within crystals can alter the available decay channels. The results enable future work to compute quantitative internal conversion and electron bridge probabilities by comparing with experimental data, and guide the interpretation of half-life measurements in solid-state nuclear clock implementations.
