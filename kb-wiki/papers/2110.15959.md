# Large-$N_c$ constraints for elastic dark matter-light nucleus scattering in pionless effective field theory

> Recent proposals for the use of light nuclei as dark matter direct detection targets necessitate a strong theoretical understanding of the nuclear physics involved. We perform relevant calculations for dark matter-light nucleus scattering in a combined pionless effective field theory and large-$N_c$ expansion, where $N_c$ is the number of quark colors. We include a general set of one-nucleon currents that have been used in other effective theories, as well as novel two-nucleon contact currents. First, we obtain constraints for the relative sizes of the dark matter couplings to the one- and two-nucleon currents through the large-$N_c$ expansion. Then, we use these constraints to make predictions for the relative sizes of spin-dependent and spin-independent cross sections for dark matter scattering off of a nucleon, a deuteron, a triton, and helium-3.

## Key claim

The paper uses a combined pionless effective field theory (EFT) and large-\(N_c\) expansion to constrain the relative sizes of dark matter couplings to one- and two-nucleon currents, then predicts the relative sizes of spin-dependent and spin-independent cross sections for dark matter scattering off nucleons and light nuclei; the key hedge is that these predictions are made at leading order in the combined expansion, and subleading corrections should not drastically alter the relative sizes.

## Method

The authors construct a pionless EFT Lagrangian for dark matter-nucleus scattering, including a general set of one-nucleon currents and novel two-nucleon contact currents, then apply large-\(N_c\) scaling rules derived from the SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry of baryons to determine the relative sizes of the low-energy constants (LECs) at each order. They match the one-nucleon LECs to quark-level dark matter couplings via nucleon form factors and nonrelativistic expansions, and they match two-nucleon LECs in the dibaryon formalism to the theory without dibaryons. Finally, they compute the unpolarized cross sections and response functions for dark matter scattering off a nucleon, deuteron, triton, and helium-3 at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-breaking parameter \(\xi = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.35 \pm 0.02\) is used to relate scalar current couplings.
- The pion-nucleon sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) appears in the matching of scalar current LECs: \(C_{1, \chi N}^{(PT)} = C_{SS}^{(s)} \sigma_{\pi N}\).
- The nucleon mass splitting \(\delta m_N\) appears in the isovector scalar matching: \(C_{4, \chi N}^{(PT)} = C_{SS}^{(v)} \delta m_N\).
- The dibaryon LEC \(l_1^\chi\) is estimated as \(l_1 \approx 14.67 \sigma_{\pi N} C_{SS}^{(s)}\) from the matching relation.
- Large-\(N_c\) scalings for dibaryon LECs: \(l_3^\chi \sim O(N_c)\), \(l_4^\chi \sim O(N_c)\), \(l_5^\chi \sim O(1)\), \(l_6^\chi \sim O(N_c)\), \(l_7^\chi \sim O(1)\).
- The paper predicts that \(l_3^\chi\), \(l_4^\chi\), and \(l_6^\chi\) are roughly the same size, while \(l_2^\chi\), \(l_5^\chi\), and \(l_7^\chi\) are about 1/3 of \(l_1^\chi\) if a spin-dependent coupling is turned on at the quark level.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) method for two-nucleon systems in external fields, previously applied to magnetic and axial fields by Richardson (2020), to the case of dark matter scattering. It provides a systematic framework for calculating dark matter scattering off light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helium-3) that complements prior EFT approaches by Korber (2017) and Andreoli (2018). The results enable future direct detection experiments using light nuclear targets to interpret their results with controlled theoretical uncertainties from nuclear structure.
