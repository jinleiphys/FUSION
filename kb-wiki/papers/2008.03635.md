# Chirality Production with Mass Effects-Schwinger Pair Production and the Axial Ward Identity

> The anomalous generation of chirality with mass effects via the axial Ward identity and its dependence on the Schwinger mechanism is reviewed, utilizing parity violating homogeneous electromagnetic background fields. The role vacuum asymptotic states play on the interpretation of expectation values is examined. It is discussed that observables calculated with an in-out scattering matrix element predict a scenario under Euclidean equilibrium. A notable ramification of which is a vanishing of the chiral anomaly. In contrast, it is discussed observables calculated under an in-in, or real-time, formalism predict a scenario out-of equilibrium, and capture effects of mean produced particle anti-particle pairs due to the Schwinger mechanism. The out-of equilibrium chiral anomaly is supplemented with exponential quadratic mass suppression as anticipated for the Schwinger mechanism. Similar behavior in and out-of equilibrium is reviewed for applications including the chiral magnetic effect and chiral condensate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent vanishing of the chiral anomaly in the axial Ward identity for massive fermions in parallel electromagnetic fields is resolved by recognizing that in-out vacuum expectation values describe a Euclidean equilibrium scenario, while the Schwinger mechanism and associated chirality production are inherently out-of-equilibrium processes captured only by an in-in (real-time) formalism.

## Method

The authors review the axial Ward identity for massive fermions in homogeneous parallel electric and magnetic fields, comparing expectation values calculated using in-out (scattering matrix) formalism versus in-in (Schwinger-Keldysh) formalism. They examine the pseudoscalar condensate, chiral density, chiral magnetic effect, and chiral condensate, analyzing how the choice of vacuum states affects the interpretation of these observables in the context of Schwinger pair production.

## Key numbers

- The critical electric field for Schwinger pair production is approximately 1.3 × 10^18 V/m
- The vacuum non-persistence probability for fermions in a homogeneous electric field scales as exp(-π m^2 c^3 / (eE ħ))
- The Schwinger formula for pair production probability density in parallel fields is ω = (e^2 E B)/(4π^2) coth(Bπ/E) exp(-π m^2/(eE))
- The pseudoscalar condensate calculated by Schwinger is ⟨ψ̄ iγ₅ ψ⟩ = -e^2 E B/(4π^2 m)
- Magnetic fields in off-central heavy-ion collisions can reach eB ∼ m_π^2

## Context

This review extends the work of Fukushima (2010) and Warringa (2012) on chirality generation via the Schwinger mechanism in parity-violating backgrounds, and resolves an apparent contradiction with Schwinger's original calculation (1951) of the pseudoscalar condensate. The resolution builds on the authors' prior work (Copinger, 2018) identifying the role of vacuum states. The findings have implications for theories of baryogenesis driven by parity-violating inflatons (Domcke, 2019) and for experimental searches for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
