# Short-range correlations and meson exchange currents in photonucleon emission

> One-nucleon emission processes induced by photon absorption are studied by considering short-range correlations effects. At energies above the giant resonance region the validity of the direct knock-out model has been tested by comparison with continuum Random Phase Approximation results. Nucleon re-scattering effects have been considered by using an optical potential. The role of the electromagnetic convection, magnetization and meson exchange currents has been investigated as a function of both excitation energy and momentum transfer. The short-range correlation effects have been studied by using various correlation functions. We found that the nucleon photo-emission cross section is rather sensitive to the presence of short-range correlations at large values of nucleon emission angle. In this region, however, the effects of meson exchange currents are even larger than those produced by short-range correlations.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) produce noticeable effects on nucleon photo-emission cross sections only at large nucleon emission angles, but in that angular region the effects of meson exchange currents (MEC) are even larger than those produced by SRC, making SRC signatures difficult to isolate.

## Method

The authors calculated one-nucleon emission cross sections for photon absorption on 16O using a correlated basis model that includes short-range correlations via Jastrow-type correlation functions, truncating the cluster expansion at first order in the correlation line. They tested the validity of the direct knock-out model by comparing with continuum Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations, included nucleon re-scattering via an optical potential, and evaluated the contributions of convection, magnetization, and meson exchange currents (seagull, pionic, and Delta-isobar terms) as functions of excitation energy and momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Total photoabsorption cross sections for 16O: RPA calculations with Landau-Migdal interaction give the low-lying 3- state at 6.40 MeV (experimental value 6.15 MeV); the finite-range polarization potential gives 6.65 MeV.
- The 1- excitation dominates the giant resonance region; in the IPM the 2+ contribution becomes non-negligible already at 40 MeV, while in RPA calculations the 1- dominance extends to 60 MeV (polarization potential) or 80 MeV (Landau-Migdal interaction).
- Above 40 MeV, IPM results are one order of magnitude smaller than RPA results.
- The effective pion-nucleon coupling constant used is f_pi = 0.079.
- The Delta-isobar current constant C_Delta is given by 4*pi*(f_pi^2/m_pi^2)*[4/(25*M*(M_Delta - M))] with M_Delta the Delta mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous correlated model applied to quasi-elastic inclusive responses (Co' 2001) and (e,e'p) reactions (Mokhtar 2001) to photon-induced nucleon emission. It tests the direct knock-out model against continuum RPA calculations following the approaches of the Bologna group (Cavallaro 1982, Saracco 1993) and the Gent group (Ryckebusch 1987, 1988) for photonuclear reactions. The MEC treatment follows the method used by Amaro (1993, 1994) for quasi-elastic (e,e') responses. The results enable future work to disentangle SRC and MEC effects in photonuclear reactions, though the dominance of MEC over SRC at large angles complicates such separation.
