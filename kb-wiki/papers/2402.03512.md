# Measuring jet energy loss fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma via multiparticle correlations

> The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is a high temperature state of matter produced in the collisions of two nuclei at relativistic energies. The properties of this matter at short distance scales are probed using jets with high transverse momentum ($p_T$) resulting from quarks and gluons scattered with large momentum transfer in the earliest stages of the collisions. The Fourier harmonics for anisotropies in the high transverse momentum particle yield, $v_n(p_T)$, indicate the path length dependence of jet energy loss within the QGP. We present a framework to build off of measurements of jet energy loss using $v_n(p_T)$ by characterizing fluctuations in jet energy loss that are currently not constrained experimentally. In this paper, we utilize a set of multivariate moments and cumulants as new experimental observables to measure event-by-event fluctuations in the azimuthal anisotropies of rare probes, and compare them to the azimuthal anisotropies of soft particles. Ultimately, these fluctuations can be used to quantify the magnitude and fluctuations of event-by-event jet energy loss. We relate these quantities to existing multivariate cumulant observables, highlight their unique properties, and validate their sensitivities with a Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a framework to measure event-by-event fluctuations in jet energy loss within the quark-gluon plasma, which are currently not constrained experimentally, by using multivariate moments and cumulants of azimuthal anisotropy coefficients for hard probes (jets) and soft reference particles.

## Method

The authors construct multiparticle correlators that involve up to six particles total, with up to two particles of interest (POI, e.g., jets), and relate these correlators to raw moments of the event-by-event flow vectors \(V_n\) and \(V'_n\) for reference particles and POI respectively. They define normalized moments, symmetric cumulants (SC), asymmetric cumulants (ASC), and two new classes of observables (\(\Gamma\) and \(\zeta\)) that compare fluctuations in jet azimuthal anisotropy to fluctuations in reference particle anisotropy. To validate the sensitivity of these observables, they develop a bivariate copula toy model with either Gaussian or elliptic power marginal distributions for \(v_n\) and \(v'_n\), parameterized by a correlation coefficient \(\rho\), and demonstrate how the observables isolate fluctuations and correlations using Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

* The framework uses correlators with up to six particles total and up to two particles of interest (POI), as detailed in Table 1 of the paper.
* The bivariate copula model uses a Pearson correlation coefficient \(\rho\) to describe correlations between \(v_n\) and \(v'_n\), with the bivariate Gaussian copula density given by Eq. (5): \(c(v_n,v'_n,\rho) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\rho^2}} \exp \left(\frac{2 \rho \Phi^{-1}(F_1(v_n)) \Phi^{-1}(F_2(v'_n))-\rho^2\left(\Phi^{-1}(F_1(v_n))^2+\Phi^{-1}(F_2(v'_n))^2\right)}{2\left(1-\rho^2\right)}\right)\).
* The elliptic power distribution parameters \(\varepsilon_0\) and \(\alpha\) are used to characterize marginal distributions, with approximate moments given by \(\langle \varepsilon_n^2\rangle \approx \varepsilon_0^2 + \frac{(1-\varepsilon_0^2)(1-\frac{3}{2}\varepsilon_0^2)}{\alpha} + \mathcal{O}(1/\alpha^2)\) and \(\langle \varepsilon_n^4\rangle \approx \varepsilon_0^4 + \frac{\varepsilon_0^2(1-\varepsilon_0^2)(4-5\varepsilon_0^2)}{\alpha} + \mathcal{O}(1/\alpha^2)\).
* The key values for interpretation are: normalized moments = 1 (independence), symmetric/asymmetric cumulants = 0 (no genuine correlation), \(\Gamma = 0\) (equal fluctuations), and \(\zeta = 1\) (equal fluctuation magnitude).

## Context

This work extends the multiparticle correlation framework developed by Holtermann et al. (2023) to construct observables sensitive to fluctuations in jet energy loss, building on prior measurements of \(v_n(p_T)\) that indicate path-length dependence of jet quenching (ALICE 2015, ATLAS 2021, CMS 2022). It enables experimental extraction of both the magnitude and fluctuations of event-by-event jet energy loss using upcoming high-luminosity data from LHC Runs 3 and 4 and sPHENIX at RHIC, complementing existing studies of soft-sector \(v_n\) fluctuations (ATLAS 2013, CMS 2017) and extending the approach of Betz et al. (2016) and Yi et al. (2011) to include decorrelations between hard and soft azimuthal anisotropies.
