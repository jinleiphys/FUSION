# Are there non-strange low-lying penta-quarks and can we understand their width

> We argue that the lightest isospin 1/2 partners of the Z^+(1530) s(bar)uudd penta-quark predicted by Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov are not the N(1710) mixed anti-decuplet states, but the pure non-strange u(bar)(ud)(ud) and d(bar)(ud)(ud) penta-quark states which may lie as low as 1200 MeV. The expected low width of a few MeV of such a putative state may explain why it was missed in phase shift analyzes of pion-nucleon scattering.

# Wiki Page: Non-Strange Low-Lying Penta-Quarks and Their Width

## Key claim
The lightest isospin 1/2 partners of the Z⁺(1530) penta-quark are not the N(1710) mixed anti-decuplet states as predicted by Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov, but rather pure non-strange \(\bar{u}(ud)(ud)\) and \(\bar{d}(ud)(ud)\) penta-quark states that may lie as low as 1200 MeV. The expected narrow width of a few MeV for such states could explain why they were missed in phase shift analyses of pion-nucleon scattering.

## Method
The authors estimate the mass difference between the \(\Theta^+(1540)\) penta-quark and its non-strange analogue by evaluating the hyperfine interaction contributions from the anti-quark with the four quarks in the penta-quark state, using a di-quark model where two strongly bound I=0, S=0 (ud) di-quarks are in a relative p-wave. They also apply a chromoelectric flux tube model (CFTM) with junctions and anti-junctions to estimate the decay width, calculating the probability that a junction and anti-junction come within the flux tube radius b during one oscillation period.

## Key numbers
- The \(\Theta^+(1540)\) penta-quark has an experimental upper bound on its width of \(\Gamma < 25\) MeV from \(\gamma d\) and \(\gamma p\) experiments, with a more stringent bound of \(\Gamma < 3-6\) MeV from \(K^+ d\) scattering data, and an even stronger bound of \(\Gamma < 1\) MeV from phase shift analysis.
- The mass difference between the N(1710) and Z⁺(1530) in the anti-decuplet is 180 MeV, implying an effective strange versus non-strange quark mass difference \(\Delta_m^{penta} = 540\) MeV in the penta-quark system.
- The non-strange penta-quark P is predicted to have a mass in the range 1200-1450 MeV with I=1/2 and a width \(\Gamma < 5\) MeV.
- The hyperfine interaction scale between anti-quark and quark is set by \(m(K_{\bar{s}q}) - m(\pi_{\bar{q}q}) \approx 360\) MeV.
- In the CFTM estimate, the decay rate for tetra-quarks is \(\Gamma^{Tetra} < 0.6-60\) MeV, with penta-quarks expected to have roughly twice this width.
- The flux tube radius b is estimated as \(b \le 0.15\) fm from glue-ball mass considerations and \(b \le 0.2\) fm from lattice simulations.
- The distance a between junction and anti-junction in tetra-quarks is approximately 0.7 fm, comparable to the pion charge radius.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of penta-quark states following the observation of the \(\Theta^+(1540)\) resonance in \(K^+ n\) invariant mass distributions by experiments at Spring-8, JLab, and ELSA, and in \(K^+\)-Xenon scattering by DIANA. The authors challenge the identification by Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (2003) of the N(1710) as the isospin 1/2 partner of the Z⁺(1530) in the anti-decuplet of the Skyrme model. The paper builds on earlier work by Jaffe (1977) on tetra-quark states, by Nussinov (2003) on the narrow width of the \(\Theta^+\), and by Shrock and Karliner on mass differences in heavy quark systems. The CFTM analysis provides a mechanism for narrow widths in multi-quark exotics, suggesting that previous searches may have failed not because widths were too large but because they were too small, enabling future high-resolution experiments to search for narrow penta-quark states in the \(\pi^- p\) channel.
