# Pentaquark states with hidden charm

> I develop an extension of the usual three-flavor quark model to four flavors (u, d, s and c), and discuss the classification of pentaquark states with hidden charm. This work is motivated by the recent observation of such states by the LHCb Collatoration at CERN.

# Pentaquark states with hidden charm

## Key claim
The paper develops an extension of the three-flavor quark model to four flavors (u, d, s, c) to classify uudc\bar{c} pentaquark states, but only for configurations without orbital excitations and with spin and parity J^P = 3/2^-; the classification is restricted to the SU(3) flavor level, and couplings at the SU(8) spin-flavor or SU(4) flavor level would introduce admixtures with other quark configurations.

## Method
The author constructs pentaquark wave functions using SU(4) flavor symmetry for four quarks (u, d, s, c) combined with an antiquark, imposing antisymmetry under permutations of the four quarks and color-singlet condition. The four-quark states are decomposed into SU(3) flavor multiplets (decuplet and octet) that contain uudc configurations, then combined with the \bar{c} antiquark to obtain uudc\bar{c} pentaquark states. Magnetic moments are calculated for each allowed spin-flavor configuration using constituent quark magnetic moments fitted to the proton, neutron, and \Lambda hyperon, with the charm quark moment derived from a constituent mass of 1.550 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic moment of the decuplet state [\phi_{A_1} \times \chi_{F_2}]_{F_2}: 1.164 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the decuplet state [\phi_{F_2} \times \chi_{A_1}]_{F_2}: 3.065 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the decuplet state [\phi_{F_2} \times \chi_{F_2}]_{F_2}: 1.417 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the octet state [\phi_{F_2} \times \chi_{A_1}]_{F_2}: 3.065 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the octet state [\phi_{F_2} \times \chi_{F_2}]_{F_2}: 1.979 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the octet state [\phi_{E} \times \chi_{F_2}]_{F_2}: 1.164 \mu_N
- Magnetic moment of the octet state [\phi_{F_1} \times \chi_{F_2}]_{F_2}: 2.232 \mu_N
- Constituent quark magnetic moments used: \mu_u = +1.852 \mu_N, \mu_d = -0.972 \mu_N, \mu_s = -0.613 \mu_N, \mu_c = +0.404 \mu_N
- Charm quark constituent mass: 1.550 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of uudd\bar{s} pentaquarks by Bijker and collaborators (BGS1, BGS2) to the hidden-charm sector, motivated by the LHCb Collaboration's observation of P_c^+(4380) and P_c^+(4450) states. The classification provides a framework for interpreting these states within a constituent quark model, complementing molecular models (Karliner, Yamaguchi), diquark models (Maiani, Elena), and kinematic explanations (nonresonant1, nonresonant2, nonresonant3). The magnetic moment calculations are a first step toward predicting photoproduction cross sections, which could help determine the spin and parity of the observed pentaquark candidates at facilities like JLab.
