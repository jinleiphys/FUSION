# Light nuclei production as a probe of the QCD phase diagram

> It is generally believed that the quark-hadron transition at small values of baryon chemical potentials $μ_B$ is a crossover but changes to a first-order phase transition with an associated critical endpoint (CEP) as $μ_B$ increases. Such a $μ_B$-dependent quark-hadron transition is expected to result in a double-peak structure in the collision energy dependence of the baryon density fluctuation in heavy-ion collisions with one at lower energy due to the spinodal instability during the first-order phase transition and another at higher energy due to the critical fluctuations in the vicinity of the CEP. By analyzing the data on the $p$, d and $^3$H yields in central heavy-ion collisions within the coalescence model for light nuclei production, we find that the relative neutron density fluctuation $Δρ_n=\langle(δρ_n)^2\rangle/\langle ρ_n\rangle^2$ at kinetic freeze-out indeed displays a clear peak at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$ GeV and a possible strong re-enhancement at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.86$ GeV. Our findings thus provide a strong support for the existence of a first-order phase transition at large $μ_B$ and its critical endpoint at a smaller $μ_B$ in the temperature versus baryon chemical potential plane of the QCD phase diagram.

# Light nuclei production as a probe of the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim

The relative neutron density fluctuation at kinetic freeze-out, extracted from measured yields of protons, deuterons, and tritons in central heavy-ion collisions, displays a clear peak at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV and a possible strong re-enhancement at sqrt(s_NN)=4.86 GeV, supporting the existence of both a first-order phase transition at large baryon chemical potential and a critical endpoint at smaller baryon chemical potential in the QCD phase diagram. The authors hedge that the results cannot determine whether the phase transition is due to deconfinement or chiral symmetry restoration, and that the statistical uncertainties at the lower energy point are large.

## Method

The authors used the coalescence model for light nuclei production to relate the yields of protons, deuterons, and tritons to the relative neutron density fluctuation Delta_rho_n and the neutron-proton density correlation C_np at kinetic freeze-out. They determined the phase-space volume at kinetic freeze-out from the chemical freeze-out temperature and volume using a constant expansion factor lambda=1.6, then extracted Delta_rho_n and C_np from measured yields in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (NA49 Collaboration) and Au+Au collisions at AGS energy (11.6 AGeV/c). The proton yields were corrected for weak decay contributions from hyperons using collision-energy-dependent fractions from statistical model calculations.

## Key numbers

* Relative neutron density fluctuation Delta_rho_n peaks at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV with value 0.606 +/- 0.045 in central Pb+Pb collisions (0-7% centrality).
* At sqrt(s_NN)=4.86 GeV (central Au+Au collisions at 11.6 AGeV/c), Delta_rho_n = 0.572 +/- 0.328, indicating a possible strong re-enhancement.
* Neutron-proton density correlation C_np has a minimum (most negative) value of -0.749 +/- 0.007 at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV.
* The ratio alpha = C_np / Delta_rho_n ranges from -1.235 +/- 0.022 at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV to -1.342 +/- 0.029 at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV.
* Chemical freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV is T_ch = 0.144 GeV with corresponding baryon chemical potential mu_B ~ 385 MeV.
* At sqrt(s_NN)=4.86 GeV, the extracted values are C_np = -0.246 +/- 0.094 and Delta_rho_I = 0.409 +/- 0.171.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Sun et al., 2017) that assumed a constant ratio alpha = C_np / Delta_rho_n, by now determining both quantities simultaneously from the phase-space volume at kinetic freeze-out. The double-peak structure in density fluctuations was predicted by Steinheimer and Randrup (2012) for spinodal instability at lower energies and by Stephanov (1998) and Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000) for critical fluctuations near the critical endpoint. The extracted critical endpoint parameters (T ~ 144 MeV, mu_B ~ 385 MeV) are close to predictions from lattice QCD (Fodor and Katz, 2004), Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Xin et al., 2014), and the hadronic bootstrap approach (Antoniou et al., 2003). The results enable future experimental confirmation through more precise measurements at AGS and SPS energies, and suggest that the fourth-order baryon number cumulant should increase by a factor of about 2.35 from sqrt(s_NN)=6.3 GeV to 8.8 GeV based on the inferred correlation length increase.
