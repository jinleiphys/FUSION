# Charmed hadron production in an improved quark coalescence model

> We study the production of charmed hadrons $D^{0}$ and $Λ_c^+$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using an improved quark coalescence model. In particular, we extend the usual coalescence model by letting a produced hadron to have the same velocity as the center-of-mass velocity of coalesced constituent quarks during hadronization to take into account the effect of collective flow in produced quark-gluon plasma. This results in a shift of charmed resonances of higher masses to larger transverse momenta ($p_T^{}$). Requiring all charm quarks of very low $p_T^{}$ to be converted to hadrons via coalescence and letting charm quarks not undergoing coalescence to hadronize by independent fragmentation, we obtain a good description of the measured yield ratio $Λ_c^+/D^0$ as a function of $p_T^{}$ in $\text{Au} + \text{Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}^{}=200$~GeV by the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim
The improved quark coalescence model, which boosts produced hadrons from the center-of-mass frame of coalescing quarks to the fireball frame using the hadron's physical mass, can describe the measured $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratio as a function of transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV without invoking a large number of unknown charmed baryon resonances, unlike the resonance recombination model of He (2019).

## Method
The authors used a quark coalescence model extended to include collective flow effects by boosting produced charmed hadrons from the center-of-mass frame of the coalescing quarks to the fireball frame using the hadron's physical mass, which shifts heavier resonances to larger transverse momenta. Light quark spectra were taken as thermal at T=165 MeV with a radial flow profile, while charm quark spectra were parametrized from a transport model study. Charm quarks not undergoing coalescence were hadronized via independent fragmentation using the Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers
- The oscillator constant for $D^0$ mesons was determined to be $\omega_M \approx 0.096$ GeV, about a factor of 3 smaller than the free-space value of 0.33 GeV, implying an increase of the $D^0$ charge radius by a factor of 1.85 at T=165 MeV.
- The total integrated yield ratio $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ is about 0.64, compared to about 0.54 measured in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV at LHC.
- At $p_T \approx 6$ GeV, the ratio $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ is predicted to be close to 1.0, much higher than the value of less than 0.4 obtained in Oh (2009) and Plumari (2017).
- At $p_T = 8$ GeV, the ratio $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ can be as large as 0.6, while it was predicted to be around 0.2 in Plumari (2017).
- The total number of charm quarks is $dN_c/dy \simeq 2.1$ for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence model studies of Oh (2009) and Plumari (2017) by including collective flow effects on produced charmed hadrons, which were neglected in those previous approaches. The model provides an alternative explanation for the large $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratio measured by the STAR Collaboration without requiring the large number of missing charm baryon states invoked by He (2019) in the resonance recombination model. The approach enables future studies using more realistic phase-space distributions of quarks from dynamical models and could potentially help resolve similar discrepancies in light and strange baryon-to-meson ratios.
