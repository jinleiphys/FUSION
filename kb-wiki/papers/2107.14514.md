# Constraining the nuclear gluon PDF with inclusive hadron production data

> The nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) of gluons are known to be difficult to determine with fits of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) data alone. Therefore, the nCTEQ15 analysis of nuclear PDFs added inclusive neutral pion production data from RHIC to help in constraining the gluon. In this analysis, we present a new global analysis of nuclear PDFs based on a much larger set of single inclusive light hadron data from RHIC and the LHC. Using our new nCTEQ code (nCTEQ++) with an optimized version of INCNLO we study systematically the limitations of the theory and the impact of the fragmentation function uncertainty.

## Key claim

The inclusion of single inclusive hadron (SIH) production data from RHIC and the LHC in a global QCD analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) significantly constrains the nuclear gluon PDF, particularly at low to medium momentum fraction \(x\), but the impact is less pronounced when weak boson production data (already included in nCTEQ15WZ) also constrains the gluon.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of nuclear PDFs using the nCTEQ++ framework, parameterizing effective proton PDFs at an initial scale \(Q_0 = 1.3\) GeV with 19 free parameters. They included deep inelastic scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan (DY), weak boson production (WZ), and a new set of single inclusive hadron (SIH) data from RHIC (STAR and PHENIX at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV) and the LHC (ALICE at 5.02 TeV and 8.16 TeV), applying a cut at \(p_T = 3\) GeV. Cross sections were calculated using a modified version of INCNLO with precomputed grids, and DSS fragmentation functions (DSS14 for pions, DSS17 for kaons) were used with their uncertainties added to the systematic uncertainties of the data.

## Key numbers

*   The analysis used 77 out of 174 ALICE data points and 32 out of 77 RHIC data points after applying a \(p_T > 3\) GeV cut.
*   The total \(\chi^2/N_{dof}\) for the nCTEQ15WZ+SIH fit was 0.85, compared to 1.28 for nCTEQ15, 1.00 for nCTEQ15+SIH, and 0.90 for nCTEQ15WZ.
*   The \(\chi^2/N_{dof}\) for the SIH data alone improved from 1.23 (nCTEQ15) to 0.38 (nCTEQ15+SIH) and to 0.41 (nCTEQ15WZ+SIH).
*   The \(\chi^2/N_{dof}\) for the WZ data improved from 3.74 (nCTEQ15) to 2.32 (nCTEQ15+SIH) and to 1.02 (nCTEQ15WZ+SIH).

## Context

This work extends the nCTEQ15 analysis (Kovarik et al., 2016) and the nCTEQ15WZ analysis (Kusina et al., 2020) by incorporating a much larger set of single inclusive hadron data from RHIC and the LHC, beyond the neutral pion data used previously. The results show that SIH data provides constraints on the gluon PDF consistent with those from weak boson production data, and the combined fit yields a good description of all processes. The new nCTEQ15WZ+SIH PDFs will be made available through LHAPDF, enabling more precise predictions for nuclear collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
