# Interference phenomena in the decay $D^+_s\toηπ^0π^+$ induced by the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing

> Using the data on the decay $D^+_s\to f_0(980)π^+\to K^+K^-π^+$, we estimate the amplitude of the process $D^+_s\to\left[f_0(980)\to (K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)\to a^0_0(980)\right]π^+\toηπ^0π^+$, caused by the mixing of $a^0_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances that breaks the isotopic invariance due to the $K^+$ and $K^0$ meson mass difference. Effects of the interference of this amplitude with the amplitudes of the main mechanisms responsible for the decay $D^+_s \toηπ^0π^+$ are analyzed. As such mechanisms, we examine the transition $D^+_s\toηρ^+\toηπ^0π^+$, which is observed in experiment, and the possible transition $D^+_s\to\left(a^0_0(980) π^++a^+_0(980)π^0\right)\toηπ^0π^+$. It is shown that the rapidly varying phase of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ transition amplitude strongly influences on the interference curves.

# Interference phenomena in the decay \(D^+_s\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) induced by the \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing

## Key claim

The isospin-breaking amplitude from \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing in \(D^+_s\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) can produce observable interference effects, provided the rapidly varying phase of the mixing amplitude (by about \(90^\circ\) between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds) is taken into account; the magnitude of the mixing-induced branching ratio is estimated at \(4.1\times10^{-4}\), which is large for an isospin-breaking effect but the visibility depends on the unknown relative phase between the dominant \(D^+_s\to\eta\rho^+\) amplitude and the mixing amplitude.

## Method

The authors constructed the amplitude for \(D^+_s\to [f_0(980)\to K\bar K\to a^0_0(980)]\pi^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) using the \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing amplitude \(\Pi_{a^0_0f_0}(s)\) from the \(K\bar K\) loop mechanism, with coupling constants fixed by BESIII data on \(J/\psi\) decays. They combined this with the dominant \(D^+_s\to\eta\rho^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) amplitude and a possible \(D^+_s\to (a_0(980)\pi)^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) amplitude, then computed \(\eta\pi^0\) mass spectra and Dalitz plot distributions for various choices of the unknown relative phases between the production amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- \(BR(D^+_s\to f_0(980)\pi^+\to K^+K^-\pi^+) = (1.15\pm0.32)\%\) from PDG (2016), used as input to normalize the mixing amplitude.
- Estimated \(BR(D^+_s\to [f_0(980)\to K\bar K\to a^0_0(980)]\pi^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+) = 4.1\times10^{-4}\).
- \(BR(D^+_s\to\eta\rho^+\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+) = (8.9\pm0.8)\%\) and \(BR(D^+_s\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+) = (9.2\pm1.2)\%\) from PDG (2016).
- The width of the mixing-induced peak between thresholds is about \(2(m_{K^0}-m_{K^+})\approx 8\) MeV.
- The ratio of production amplitudes is \(C_{D^+_s f_0\pi^+}/C_{D^+_s \eta\rho^+} \approx (4.5\ \text{GeV})\,\xi\) where \(\xi = e^{i\varphi_{21}}\).
- Coupling constants from Achasov et al. (2016): \(g^2_{f_0\pi\pi}/(16\pi)=0.098\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(g^2_{f_0 K\bar K}/(16\pi)=0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(g^2_{a^0_0\eta\pi^0}/(16\pi)=0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(g^2_{a^0_0 K\bar K}/(16\pi)=0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Resonance masses fixed at \(m_{a^0_0}=0.985\) GeV and \(m_{f_0}=0.985\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing, previously discovered in \(\pi^-N\) collisions by VES (Dorofeev et al., 2008, 2011) and in \(J/\psi\) decays by BESIII (Ablikim et al., 2011, 2012, 2013), to the three-body hadronic decay \(D^+_s\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\). It builds on the formalism of Achasov et al. (2015, 2016) and Achasov and Shestakov (2016) for the mixing amplitude and its phase variation. The analysis enables experimental searches at LHCb, where high-statistics \(D^+_s\) decays could reveal the isospin-breaking peak and Dalitz plot asymmetries, and also suggests analogous studies in \(D^0\) decays such as \(D^0\to K^0_S\pi^+\pi^-\), \(D^0\to K^0_S\eta\pi^0\), and \(D^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\).
