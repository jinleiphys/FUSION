# Coherent Pion Production in Neutrino Nucleus Scattering

> In this article, we study the coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus interaction in the resonance region using the formalism based on partially conserved axial current (PCAC) theorem which relates the neutrino-nucleus cross section to the pion-nucleus elastic cross section. The pion nucleus elastic cross section is calculated using the Glauber model in terms of pion-nucleon cross sections obtained by parameterizing the experimental data. We calculate the differential and integrated cross sections for charged current coherent pion production in neutrino carbon scattering. The results of integrated cross section calculations are compared with the measured data. Predictions for the differential and integrated cross sections for coherent pion productions in neutrino iron scattering using above formalism are also made.

## Key claim

The PCAC-based model using the Glauber approach for the pion-nucleus elastic cross section provides a good description of MINERvA data for charged-current coherent pion production on carbon across all neutrino energies, while the Berger-Sehgal approach is only compatible at lower energies (Eν ≤ 4 GeV); the quality of the description depends on the choice of the parameter ξ that sets the lower limit of the ν integration.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential and integrated cross sections for charged-current coherent pion production using Adler's PCAC theorem, which relates the neutrino-nucleus cross section to the pion-nucleus elastic cross section. The pion-nucleus elastic cross section is computed using the Glauber model with realistic nuclear density functions (harmonic oscillator for carbon, two-parameter Fermi for iron) and pion-nucleon cross sections obtained by parameterizing experimental data from the Particle Data Group. The results are compared with those from the Berger-Sehgal model, which assumes an exponential t-dependence and a geometric absorption factor.

## Key numbers

- Total elastic pion-carbon cross section σπC sharply peaks at pion momentum pπ = 0.3 GeV in the BS approach.
- For carbon, the ratio of real to imaginary part of the πN scattering amplitude απN is 1.5 for Eπ < 5 GeV and 1.4 for Eπ > 5 GeV.
- For iron, απN is 1.8 for Eπ < 10 GeV and 1.75 for Eπ > 10 GeV.
- The nuclear density parameters for carbon are α = 1.247 fm and a = 1.649 fm (harmonic oscillator form).
- The nuclear density parameters for iron are c = 4.111 fm and d = 0.558 fm (two-parameter Fermi form).
- At lower neutrino energies (Eν ≤ 4 GeV), both the BS and present Glauber calculations are compatible with MINERvA data for carbon.
- At higher energies (Eν ≥ 5 GeV), the present Glauber approach gives a better description of the MINERvA data than the BS approach for ξ=1.
- For ξ=2, the present approach gives an "excellent description of the data in all energy range" for carbon.

## Context

This work extends the PCAC-based formalism of Kopeliovich and Marage (1992) for coherent pion production, which was previously applied at high energies, to the intermediate-energy range (1-3 GeV) relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments like the India-Based Neutrino Observatory (INO). The authors compare their Glauber model approach with the Berger-Sehgal model (2008) and find that the Glauber model, which uses realistic nuclear densities rather than a sharp-sphere approximation, provides better agreement with MINERvA data at higher neutrino energies. The predictions for iron targets are directly relevant for the INO experiment, where iron is the detector medium.
