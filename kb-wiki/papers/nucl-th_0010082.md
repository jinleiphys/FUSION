# Multiplicity distributions associated to subthreshold events in heavy-ion collisions

> Subthreshold events (pion production, for instance) at energies $E< m_π$ are treated as rare events. The associated multiplicity distribution $P_c (n)$ and the unconstrained distribution P(n), when there is no rare event-trigger, are related by the model independent relation $P_c (n) = {n^2 \over \average{n^2}} P(n)$. This relation, and in particular its improved version inspired in clustering of nucleons, is in fair agreement with data. Moreover, it allows to extract from the multiplicity data information on the number of nucleons involved in meson production processes, occuring at energies below the production threshold in free nucleon collisions.

# Multiplicity distributions associated to subthreshold events in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Subthreshold particle production (e.g., pion production at energies below the free nucleon-nucleon threshold of approximately 280 MeV) can be described by a model-independent relation linking the triggered multiplicity distribution to the inclusive distribution, but the agreement with data is only fair; an improved version incorporating nucleon clustering (with a shift parameter δ) provides a better description of the crossing point between the two distributions.

## Method

The authors derive a universal, model-independent relation P_c(n) = (n²/⟨n²⟩) P(n) connecting the multiplicity distribution P_c(n) associated with a rare subthreshold event (such as pion emission) to the unconstrained multiplicity distribution P(n). They then introduce a modified version P_c(n+δ) = (n²/⟨n²⟩) P(n) where δ ≈ 2α−2 accounts for clustering of α nucleons, and test both relations against experimental data for ³⁶Ar on ²⁷Al collisions at 95 MeV/nucleon from Barbera et al. (1990).

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon threshold for pion production: approximately 280 MeV (derived from E_th ≈ 2m_π)
- Absolute threshold for ¹²C-¹²C collisions: approximately 23 MeV (from E_th ≈ (A+B)/(AB) m_π)
- Experimental data at E = 95 MeV/nucleon for ³⁶Ar on ²⁷Al: average multiplicity without trigger ⟨n⟩ ≈ 4.4; average multiplicity with subthreshold pion trigger ⟨n⟩_c ≈ 9
- Crossing point of distributions: n² = ⟨n²⟩ ≈ 49, compared to ⟨n⟩² ≈ 19.4
- From the clustering model: α ≤ 3 (from E_th = (2/α)m_π > 95 MeV/nucleon) and δ ≤ 4
- Best-fit parameters for the generalized gamma function describing P(n): k = 0.77, μ = 2.0, ⟨n⟩ = 4.4

## Context

This work extends the rare-event formalism of Dias de Deus, Pajares and Salgado (1997) to subthreshold particle production in heavy-ion collisions, where cross sections are of order microbarn to nanobarn compared to millibarn for total inelastic cross sections. The clustering approach contrasts with models based solely on Fermi-motion distributions and independent nucleon-nucleon collisions (α=1), which do not support the shifted relation (15). The results suggest that rare-event triggered measurements can extract information on the number of nucleons involved in meson production near threshold, and the authors propose experimental investigation of few-nucleon reactions such as pd → π⁰³He and pd → η³He at facilities like COSY and CELSIUS. The paper also raises the question of whether clusterization could enable production normally forbidden by Zweig's rule, with potential implications for φ and J/ψ production at SPS and LHC energies.
