# Systematics of semi-microscopic proton-nucleus optical potential at low energies relevant to nuclear astrophysics

> Astrophysical models studying the origin of the p-nuclei require knowledge of the reaction rates of photodisintegrations and capture reactions. Since experimental data at astrophysically relevant energies are limited, reaction rate calculations rely on Hauser-Feshbach (HF) theory predictions. The HF theory requires nuclear physics input such as masses, level densities, $γ$-ray strength functions and proton-nucleus optical potentials (pOMP). The scope of this work is to improve a global semi-microscopic pOMP at energies relevant to the p-process. This is achieved by adjusting the normalization parameters of the OMP to all available proton-capture cross sections measured at low energies. By establishing the systematic behaviour of these parameters, one expects to enhance the predictive power of the pOMP when expanding to mass regions where no data exists. The HF calculations were obtained using TALYS code. The normalization parameters for the real and imaginary central potentials ($λ_V$ and $λ_W$) were adjusted to fit the proton data in the energy range where the cross-section are independent of the other nuclear inputs. Results show that the $λ_V$ parameter has a strong mass dependence that can be described by a second-degree polynomial function for A $\leq$ 100 and an exponential increase for 100 < A < 162. Though variations of the $λ_W$ have a smaller effect on the calculations, a global increase by 50$\%$ improves the results for certain nuclei without affecting the rest of the cases. The resulting adjustment functions were obtained by fitting all suitable proton data and can be used with reasonable confidence to generate the global semi-microscopic pOMP for nuclei in the medium to heavy mass region. For better statistics, more low-energy (p,$γ$) cross section data are needed for heavier nuclei with mass A $>$ 100.

# Systematics of semi-microscopic proton-nucleus optical potential at low energies relevant to nuclear astrophysics

## Key claim
The normalization parameter λ_V of the real part of the JLM/B semi-microscopic proton optical potential shows a systematic mass dependence that can be described by a second-degree polynomial for A ≤ 100 and an exponential (logarithmic) increase for 100 < A < 162, but only when the fit is restricted to energies below the (p,n) threshold where cross sections are independent of other Hauser-Feshbach inputs; the imaginary parameter λ_W has a smaller effect, though a global 50% increase improves results for certain nuclei.

## Method
The authors used the TALYS 1.95 code to perform Hauser-Feshbach calculations for 30 nuclei (A = 47 to 162) and adjusted the multiplicative factors f_v and f_w (ranging 0.5–1.5) for the real and imaginary normalization parameters λ_V and λ_W of the JLM/B semi-microscopic optical potential. The fitting was restricted to energies below the (p,n) reaction threshold and within the range where cross sections are insensitive to γ-ray strength function and nuclear level density models, determined through sensitivity studies with eight γSF models and six NLD models. The goodness of fit was evaluated using chi-square without experimental error weighting and visual comparison.

## Key numbers
- 87 (p,γ) cross section datasets were retrieved from EXFOR; only 30 nuclei with Z = 22–68 and A = 47–162 were suitable for analysis.
- For A ≤ 100, f_v follows: f_v = 0.00016 A² – 0.03 A + 2.16; for A > 100, f_v = 5.9 ln(0.25 ln A).
- The f_v factor decreases by up to 20% for nuclei around A ∼ 100 and reaches a maximum of 1.4 for ¹⁶²Er.
- A global increase of f_w by 50% (f_w = 1.5) improves results for certain nuclei without affecting others.
- The energy range for fitting extended up to E_max values between 0.9 MeV (⁴⁷Ti) and 5.6 MeV (¹⁶²Er), all below the respective (p,n) thresholds (S_n = 1.38–8.66 MeV).
- For A ≤ 100, default f_v = 1 overestimates experimental cross sections by at least a factor of 2; for heavier nuclei (¹²⁰Te, ¹³⁰Ba, ¹⁵²Gd, ¹⁶²Er), default f_v = 1 underestimates the data.

## Context
This work extends the JLM/B semi-microscopic optical potential of Bauge, Delaroche, and Girod (2001), which had only been tested against proton data above 10 MeV and had less than 50% confidence below that energy. By establishing systematic A-dependent normalization parameters from low-energy (p,γ) data, the study enables improved reaction rate predictions for p-process nucleosynthesis in the mass region A = 47–162, where experimental data are scarce. The results call for more low-energy (p,γ) cross section measurements for A > 100 to improve statistics and extend the systematics to heavier nuclei.
