# Nonperturbative quark-antiquark interactions in mesonic form factors

> The existing theory of hard exclusive QCD processes is based on two assumptions: (i) $factorization$ into a $hard\,block$ times light front distribution amplitudes (DA's); (ii) use of perturbative gluon exchanges within the hard block. However, unlike DIS and jet physics, the characteristic momentum transfer $Q$ involved in the factorized block is not large enough for this theory to be phenomenologically successful. In this work, we revisit the latter assumption (ii), by explicitly calculating the $instanton-induced$ contributions to the hard block, and show that they contribute substantially to the vector, scalar and gravitational form factors of the pseudoscalar, scalar and vector mesons, over a wide range of momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the standard perturbative treatment of the "hard block" in exclusive QCD processes fails at the semi-hard momentum transfers \(Q^2 = 2-10\ \text{GeV}^2\) accessible to experiment and lattice calculations, and that instanton-induced nonperturbative quark-antiquark interactions contribute substantially to mesonic form factors in this regime, supplementing the usual one-gluon exchange.

## Method

The authors calculate elastic vector, scalar, and gravitational form factors for pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector mesons by evaluating four classes of diagrams: (a) perturbative one-gluon exchange; (b) a Born-style contribution substituting the instanton gauge field for the gluon propagator; (c) contributions from three nonzero-mode quark propagators in the instanton background; and (d) contributions from the instanton zero-mode propagators (the 't Hooft effective 4-fermion interaction). They use light-front distribution amplitudes (DAs) for the mesons, including both chirally diagonal and chirally non-diagonal structures, and average the instanton-induced contributions over the instanton size distribution with parameters from the instanton liquid model.

## Key numbers

* Instanton liquid model parameters: instanton density \(n_{I+\bar I} \approx 1\ \text{fm}^{-4}\) and instanton size \(\rho \sim 1/3\ \text{fm} \sim 1/(0.6\ \text{GeV})\), with diluteness parameter \(\kappa \equiv \pi^2 \rho^4 n_{I+\bar I} \sim 1/10\).
* NJL model parameters used for comparison: coupling \(G_{NJL} = 19\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), IR cutoff \(\Lambda_{IR} = 0.24\ \text{GeV}\), UV cutoff \(\Lambda_{UV} = 0.645\ \text{GeV}\), yielding constituent quark mass \(M_Q \approx 0.4\ \text{GeV}\).
* Pion decay constant \(f_\pi \approx 133\ \text{MeV}\).
* The ratio of nonperturbative-to-perturbative 4-fermion effective vertex remains above one for momentum transfers up to approximately \(Q^2 \sim 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) with a Gaussian form factor.
* The perturbative contribution to the pion vector form factor \(V_a^\pi\) includes a term proportional to \(2C_F \pi \alpha_s f_\pi^2 / (N_c Q^2)\) with \(C_F = 4/3\) and \(N_c = 3\).

## Context

This work extends the theory of hard exclusive QCD processes, originally formulated by Brodsky (1973), Chernyak (1977), and Radyushkin (1977), which assumes factorization into a hard block and light-front distribution amplitudes with perturbative gluon exchanges. The authors challenge the second assumption by showing that instanton-induced interactions, previously studied in the instanton liquid model by Shuryak (1981) and reviewed by Schafer (1996), contribute substantially at semi-hard momenta. This enables a more complete description of mesonic form factors in the \(Q^2 = 2-10\ \text{GeV}^2\) range, where lattice results are becoming available, and suggests that similar nonperturbative effects may extend to strange and charm quark sectors.
