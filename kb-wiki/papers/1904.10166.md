# Triply-heavy baryons in a constituent quark model

> A constituent quark model, which has recently been successfully applied to the study of heavy quarkonium properties such as its spectrum but also a diverse array of observables related with their electromagnetic, strong and weak decays and reactions, is used herein to compute ground- and excited-state masses of $QQQ$-baryons containing either $c$- or $b$-quarks. Considering the lack of experimental information about the spectra of triply-heavy baryons, we believe that our computation could help on finding new states, since it is expected that phenomenological quark models describe triply-heavy baryons to a similar degree of accuracy as heavy quarkonia. The quark model parameters previously used to describe $c\bar c$ and $b\bar b$ properties have not been modified for this analysis. The non-relativistic three-body bound-state problem is solved by means of the Gaußian expansion method which provides enough accuracy and simplifies the subsequent evaluation of the matrix elements. Several low-lying states with quantum numbers $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^{\pm}$, $\frac{3}{2}^{\pm}$, $\frac{5}{2}^{\pm}$ and $\frac{7}{2}^+$ are reported. We compare our results with those predicted by many other theoretical formalisms. There is a general trend of agreement about the mass of the ground state in each sector of triply-heavy baryons; however, the situation is more puzzling for the excited states and thus appropriate comments on the most relevant features of our comparison are given.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model, with parameters previously fitted to heavy quarkonium (charmonium and bottomonium) data and left unmodified, predicts ground-state masses of triply-heavy baryons that agree with lattice QCD to within a few tens of MeV, but the agreement is poorer for excited states, especially in the negative-parity sector where discrepancies of about 0.1 GeV appear.

## Method

The non-relativistic three-body bound-state problem is solved using the Gaussian expansion method within a constituent quark model that includes a one-gluon-exchange potential (with a regularized contact term and a scale-dependent strong coupling constant) and a color-confining potential that is linear at short distances and saturates at large distances. The Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle is applied to a wave function constructed from color, flavor, spin, and spatial components, with the spatial part expanded in infinitesimally-shifted Gaussian basis functions. The model parameters, taken from previous studies of heavy mesons (Segovia 2008, Segovia 2016), are listed in a table and include quark masses (m_c = 1763 MeV, m_b = 5110 MeV) and confinement parameters (a_c = 507.4 MeV, mu_c = 0.576 fm^{-1}, Delta = 184.432 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state mass of Omega_ccc (1S, J^P = 3/2^+): 4798 MeV, compared with lattice QCD value 4759 +/- 6 MeV (Padmanath 2013).
- Ground-state mass of Omega_ccb (1S, J^P = 1/2^+): 8004 MeV, compared with lattice QCD value 8005 +/- 13 MeV (Mathur 2018).
- Ground-state mass of Omega_ccb (1S, J^P = 3/2^+): 8023 MeV, compared with lattice QCD value 8026 +/- 13 MeV (Mathur 2018).
- Ground-state mass of Omega_cbb (1S, J^P = 1/2^+): 11200 MeV, compared with lattice QCD value 11194 +/- 13 MeV (Mathur 2018).
- Ground-state mass of Omega_cbb (1S, J^P = 3/2^+): 11221 MeV, compared with lattice QCD value 11211 +/- 13 MeV (Mathur 2018).
- Ground-state mass of Omega_bbb (1S, J^P = 3/2^+): 14368 MeV (from Table Ebbb, not shown in truncated text but referenced in the paper).
- Estimated theoretical uncertainty of the constituent quark model: +/- 50 MeV when modifying the most sensitive model parameter by 10%.

## Context

This work extends the same constituent quark model previously applied to heavy quarkonium spectra, decays, and reactions (Segovia 2008, Segovia 2016) to the triply-heavy baryon sector (Omega_ccc, Omega_ccb, Omega_cbb, Omega_bbb). It provides a unified description of heavy mesons and baryons using identical parameters and formalism, and the predicted spectra serve as a template to distinguish conventional baryons from possible exotic structures in future experimental searches at facilities such as LHC, BES III, and Belle II. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations (Padmanath 2013, Mathur 2018) and with other theoretical approaches including non-relativistic quark models (SilvestreBrac 1996, Vijande 2015), relativistic variants (Martynenko 2007, Migura 2006), QCD sum rules (Zhang 2009, Wang 2011, Aliev 2012, Aliev 2014), and continuum QCD methods (Qin 2019, Yin 2019).
