# Diquark Condensates and Compact Star Cooling

> The effect of color superconductivity on the cooling of quark stars and neutron stars with large quark cores is investigated. Various known and new quark-neutrino processes are studied. As a result, stars being in the color flavor locked (CFL) color superconducting phase cool down extremely fast. Quark stars with no crust cool down too rapidly in disagreement with X-ray data. The cooling of stars being in the N_f =2 color superconducting (2SC) phase with a crust is compatible with existing X-ray data. Also the cooling history of stars with hypothetic pion condensate nuclei and a crust does not contradict the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that compact stars in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase cool extremely fast and are incompatible with existing X-ray data, whereas stars in the N_f=2 color superconducting (2SC) phase with a crust are compatible with the data, but only if the crust provides sufficient thermal insulation.

## Method

The authors computed cooling curves for quark stars (QS) and neutron stars with large quark cores (QCNS) by solving a cooling equation that sums contributions from quark direct Urca, quark modified Urca, quark bremsstrahlung, crust, and photon emissivities, along with specific heats of electrons, photons, gluons, and quarks. They considered three scenarios: normal quark matter, small-gap superconductivity (Delta ~ 0.1-1 MeV), and large-gap superconductivity in both the CFL phase (Delta ~ 50 MeV) and the 2SC phase (Delta ~ 100 MeV), using exponential suppression factors for processes blocked by pairing gaps. For the 2SC phase, they accounted for unpaired red quarks that allow unsuppressed Urca and bremsstrahlung processes.

## Key numbers

- Quark direct Urca emissivity: epsilon_nu^QDU ~ 10^26 * alpha_s * (rho_b/rho_0) * Y_e^(1/3) * T_9^6 erg cm^-3 sec^-1, where rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 and T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K
- For density rho_b ~ 3 rho_0 with electron fraction Y_e ~ 10^-5, QDU emissivity reduces to epsilon_nu^QDU ~ 10^25 * T_9^6 erg cm^-3 sec^-1
- Quark modified Urca and bremsstrahlung emissivities: epsilon_nu^QMU ~ epsilon_nu^QB ~ 10^20 * T_9^8 erg cm^-3 sec^-1
- Crust emissivity: epsilon_nu^cr ~ 10^21 * (M_cr/M_sun) * T_9^6 erg cm^-3 sec^-1
- Photon emissivity: epsilon_gamma ~ 2 * 10^18 * (R/10 km)^2 * T_s7^4 erg cm^-3 sec^-1, where T_s7 is surface temperature in units of 10^7 K
- Quark specific heat: c_q ~ 10^21 * (rho_b/rho_0)^(2/3) * T_9 erg cm^-3 K^-1
- For QS with tiny crust: T_s = 5 * 10^-2 * T; for QS with negligible crust: T_s = T
- For QCNS with standard crust: T_s = (10 * T)^(2/3), yielding T_s ~ a * 10^-2 * T with a ~ 0.2-2
- Mixed photon-gluon excitation mass: m_(gamma,g) ~ 70 MeV for Y_e = 10^-5, and m_(gamma,g) ~ 60 MeV for Y_e = 0
- In the 2SC phase, QDU emissivity on red quarks: epsilon_nu^QDU(d_r) ~ 10^25 * alpha_s * (rho_b/rho_0) * Y_e^(1/3) * T_9^6 erg cm^-3 sec^-1
- In the 2SC phase, QMU and QB emissivities on red quarks: epsilon_nu^QMU(d_r q_r) ~ epsilon_nu^QB(q_1r q_2r) ~ 10^19 * T_9^8 erg cm^-3 sec^-1

## Context

This work extends earlier cooling studies of compact stars reviewed by Pizzochero (1992), Schaab et al. (1996), and Schaab et al. (1997), and applies recent calculations of diquark condensates with large pairing gaps (Delta ~ 100 MeV) from Alford et al. (1998), Rapp et al. (1998), Schafer (1998), and others. The paper specifically compares normal quark matter, small-gap superconductivity as suggested by Bailin and Love (1984), and the CFL and 2SC phases, finding that only the 2SC phase with a crust is compatible with X-ray observations. The results enable future work to distinguish between different color superconducting phases in compact stars and to test the hypothesis of pion condensate nuclei stars, which the authors find also compatible with data when in the normal state.
