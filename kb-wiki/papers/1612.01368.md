# Asymmetry dependence of Gogny based optical potential

> An analysis of neutron and proton scattering off $^{40,48}$Ca has been carried out. Real and imaginary potentials have been generated using the Nuclear Structure Method (NSM) for scattering with the Gogny D1S nucleon-nucleon effective interaction. Observables are well described by NSM for neutron and proton elastic scattering off $^{40}$Ca and for neutron scattering off $^{48}$Ca. For proton scattering off $^{48}$Ca, NSM yields a lack of absorption. This discrepancy is attributed to double-charge-exchange contribution and coupling to Gamow- Teller mode which are not included in the present version of NSM. A recipe based on a Perey-Buck fit of NSM imaginary potential and Lane model is proposed to overcome this issue in an approximate way.

## Key claim

The Nuclear Structure Method (NSM) using the Gogny D1S interaction successfully describes neutron and proton elastic scattering off ⁴⁰Ca and neutron scattering off ⁴⁸Ca, but fails to reproduce proton scattering off ⁴⁸Ca due to a lack of absorption, which the authors attribute to missing double-charge-exchange contributions and coupling to the Gamow-Teller mode in the current version of NSM.

## Method

The authors generated real and imaginary optical potentials using the Nuclear Structure Method (NSM) based on the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, solving the integro-differential Schrodinger equation for nonlocal potentials via matrix inversion. The NSM potential combines a Hartree-Fock mean-field potential with a polarization potential built from Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) excited states of the target nucleus, including both discrete and continuum spectra for intermediate single-particle states. To address the missing absorption for proton scattering off ⁴⁸Ca, the authors proposed a recipe based on a Perey-Buck fit of the NSM imaginary potential combined with the Lane model.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies studied: below 40 MeV for neutron and proton scattering off ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca
- Phenomenological widths assigned to RPA states: 2, 5, 15, and 50 MeV for excitation energies of 20, 50, 100, and 200 MeV, respectively
- RPA calculations included fifteen major shells in a harmonic oscillator basis, accounting for excited states with spin up to J=8
- For neutron scattering off ⁴⁸Ca, the (n,p) reaction channel has a threshold energy of about 500 keV, compared to the (n,n') threshold of about 3.5 MeV
- Mueller et al. found that the neutron imaginary surface potential displays very little dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry, resulting in a depletion of about 6% of the volume integral going from ⁴⁰Ca to ⁴⁸Ca

## Context

This work extends previous NSM applications to nucleon scattering off ⁴⁰Ca (Blanchon 2015) by studying the isospin asymmetry dependence of the optical potential using both ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca targets. The results contradict the findings of Waldecker (2011) using Faddeev Random-Phase Approximation, who suggested that charge-exchange excitations interfere only weakly with scattering and that tensor terms are the dominant asymmetry effect. The study enables future inclusion of double-charge-exchange and Gamow-Teller coupling in NSM, which would be particularly important for proton scattering off neutron-rich targets near the neutron drip line.
