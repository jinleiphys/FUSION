# Local chiral interactions, the tritium Gamow-Teller matrix element, and the three-nucleon contact term

> The Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix element contributing to tritium $β$ decay is calculated with trinucleon wave functions obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics solutions of the Schrödinger equation with the chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions including $Δ$ intermediate states that have recently been constructed in configuration space. Predictions up to N3LO in the chiral expansion of the axial current (with $Δ$'s) overestimate the empirical value by 1--4 \%. By exploiting the relation between the low-energy constant (LEC) in the contact three-nucleon interaction and two-body axial current, we provide new determinations of the LECs $c_D$ and $c_E$ that characterize this interaction by fitting the trinucleon binding energy and tritium GT matrix element. Some of the implications that the resulting models of three-nucleon interactions have on the spectra of light nuclei and the equation of state of neutron matter are briefly discussed. We also provide a partial analysis, which ignores $Δ$'s, of the contributions due to loop corrections in the axial current at N4LO. Finally, explicit expressions for the axial current up to N4LO have been derived in configuration space, which other researchers in the field may find useful.

## Key claim
Predictions for the tritium Gamow-Teller matrix element using chiral axial currents up to N3LO (including Delta intermediate states) overestimate the empirical value by 1-4%, and the low-energy constants c_D and c_E in the three-nucleon contact interaction can be determined by fitting the trinucleon binding energy and the tritium GT matrix element rather than the neutron-deuteron scattering length.

## Method
The authors calculated trinucleon wave functions by solving the Schrodinger equation with hyperspherical-harmonics expansions, using local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (the Norfolk potentials NV2+3) that include Delta-isobar intermediate states and are constructed in configuration space. They evaluated the Gamow-Teller matrix element for tritium beta decay by Monte Carlo integration of axial current operators up to N3LO, and determined the LECs c_D and c_E by simultaneously fitting the experimental trinucleon binding energies and the tritium GT matrix element.

## Key numbers
- Experimental GT matrix element: GT_exp = 0.9511 ± 0.0013
- LO contribution to GT matrix element ranges from 0.9237 to 0.9259 across models Ia, Ib, IIa, IIb
- N2LO(Delta) contribution ranges from 0.0401 to 0.0589 across models
- N3LO(CT) contribution ranges from -0.0036 to -0.0668 across models
- Total GT predictions range from 0.9565 to 0.9885, overestimating experiment by 1-4%
- New c_D* values: -0.635 (Ia*), -4.71 (Ib*), -0.61 (IIa*), -5.25 (IIb*)
- New c_E* values: -0.09 (Ia*), +0.55 (Ib*), -0.35 (IIa*), +0.05 (IIb*)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, Delta-nucleon mass difference m_DeltaN = 293.1 MeV
- Nucleon axial coupling g_A = 1.2723, nucleon-to-Delta axial coupling h_A = 2.74

## Context
This work extends the local chiral interaction program of Piarulli (2016, 2017) by using the tritium GT matrix element rather than the nd doublet scattering length to constrain the three-nucleon contact LECs c_D and c_E. The previous determination by Piarulli (2017) gave c_E = -1.638 for model Ia, which led to collapse in neutron matter at moderate densities; the new c_E* values are much smaller in magnitude and should resolve this issue. The results enable improved predictions for light-nuclei spectra and neutron-matter equation of state, as being investigated by Piarulli (2018). The authors also provide new configuration-space expressions for axial currents up to N4LO, correcting an error in the relation between the contact axial current LEC and c_D originally given by Gazit (2009).
