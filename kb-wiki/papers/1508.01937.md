# Global calculations on the microscopic energies and nuclear deformations: Isospin dependence of the spin-orbit coupling

> The microscopic energies and nuclear deformations of about 1850 even-even nuclei are calculated systematically within the macroscopic-microscopic framework using three Woods-Saxon parameterizations, with different isospin dependences, which were constructed mainly for nuclear spectroscopy calculations. Calculations are performed in the deformation space $(β_2, γ, β_4)$. Both the monopole and doubly stretched quadrupole interactions are considered for the pairing channel. The ground state deformations obtained by the three calculations are quite similar to each other. Large differences are seen mainly in neutron-rich nuclei and in superheavy nuclei. Systematic calculations on the shape-coexisting second minima are also presented. As for the microscopic energies of the ground states, the results are also very close to each other. Only in a few cases the difference is larger than 2 MeV. The total binding energy is estimated by adding the macroscopic energy provided by the usual liquid drop model with its parameters fitted through the least square root and minimax criteria. Calculations are also compared with the results of other macroscopic-microscopic mass models. All the three calculations give similar values for the deformations, microscopic energies and binding energies of most nuclei. One may expect to have a better understanding of the isospin dependence of the spin-orbital force with more data on proton- and neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The three Woods-Saxon parameterizations (WS1, WS2, WS3) with different isospin dependences of the spin-orbit force give similar values for deformations, microscopic energies, and binding energies for most of the 1871 even-even nuclei calculated, but large differences appear mainly in neutron-rich nuclei and superheavy nuclei, suggesting that the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit force has limited global influence except in those extreme regions.

## Method

The authors performed systematic macroscopic-microscopic calculations for all even-even nuclei with Z,N >= 8 within the proton and neutron driplines, using three Woods-Saxon parameterizations (WS1, WS2, WS3) that differ in their isospin dependence of the spin-orbit coupling. Calculations were done in the deformation space (beta_2, gamma, beta_4), including both monopole and doubly stretched quadrupole pairing interactions treated with the Lipkin-Nogami approach, and the ground state was defined as the minimum in the total energy surface.

## Key numbers

- 1871 even-even nuclei were calculated in total, within the deformation space (beta_2, gamma, beta_4)
- For WS1 and WS2, only 72 cases had quadrupole deformation differences larger than 0.1, with 20 cases having Delta beta_2 >= 0.2 and 7 cases with Delta beta_2 >= 0.3
- For WS1 and WS3, 118 cases had quadrupole deformation differences larger than 0.1, with 50 cases having Delta beta_2 >= 0.2 and 17 cases with Delta beta_2 >= 0.3
- For WS1 and WS3 microscopic energies, 124 cases had differences larger than 2 MeV
- For WS3 and WS2 microscopic energies, 23 cases had differences larger than 2 MeV
- For WS1 and WS2 microscopic energies, 28 cases had differences larger than 2 MeV
- For the shell plus pairing correction energy, WS3 and WS1 had 163 cases with differences larger than 2 MeV, including 7 cases larger than 4 MeV
- For the total binding energy with fitted liquid drop parameters, WS1 had only 2 cases with deviation larger than 2 MeV, while WS2 had 14 and WS3 had 44 such cases
- The largest deviations in binding energy for all three calculations appeared at N=126 isotopes U-218 and Th-216

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit force in Woods-Saxon potentials, following the unconventional parameterization of Xu (2013) that was designed to explain shell evolution in light neutron-rich nuclei. The calculations are compared with the macroscopic-microscopic mass models of Moller (1995) and Wang (2014), and the results show that while the three parameterizations give similar global predictions, the differences in neutron-rich and superheavy regions highlight the need for more experimental data on those nuclei to better constrain the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit coupling.
