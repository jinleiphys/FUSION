# Testing the origin of the "$f_1(1420)$" with the $\bar K p \to Λ(Σ) K\bar K π$ reaction

> We study the $\bar K p \to Y K\bar K π$ reactions with $\bar K = \bar K^0, K^-$ and $Y=Σ^0, Σ^+, Λ$, in the region of $K\bar K π$ invariant masses of $1200-1550$ MeV. The strong coupling of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance to $K^* \bar K$ makes the mechanism based on $K^*$ exchange very efficient to produce this resonance observed in the $K\bar K π$ invariant mass distribution. In addition, in all the reactions one observes an associated peak at $1420$ MeV which comes from the $K^* \bar K$ decay mode of the $f_1(1285)$ when the $K^*$ is placed off shell at higher invariant masses. We claim this to be the reason for the peak of the $K^* \bar K$ distribution seen in the experiments which has been associated to the "$f_1(1420)$" resonance.

## Key claim

The peak at 1420 MeV in the \(K\bar K\pi\) invariant mass distribution, previously attributed to a distinct "\(f_1(1420)\)" resonance, is actually a kinematic reflection of the \(K^*\bar K\) decay mode of the \(f_1(1285)\) resonance when the intermediate \(K^*\) goes on shell at higher invariant masses, and does not correspond to a new resonance.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for the reactions \(\bar K p \to Y K\bar K\pi\) (with \(\bar K = \bar K^0, K^-\) and \(Y = \Sigma^0, \Sigma^+, \Lambda\)) using a model where the \(f_1(1285)\) is produced via \(K^*\) exchange, and subsequently decays to \(K^*\bar K\) with the \(K^*\) decaying to \(K\pi\). The \(f_1(1285)\) is treated as a dynamically generated resonance from pseudoscalar-vector meson interaction, with its coupling to \(K^*\bar K\) taken from chiral unitary approaches. Two different parametrizations of the baryon-baryon-vector (\(BBV\)) vertex are used, with form factors tuned to reproduce the known cross section for \(K^- p \to \Lambda f_1(1285)\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3010\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* Coupling constant of \(f_1(1285)\) to \(K^*\bar K\): \(g_{f_1, K^* \bar K} = 7230\) MeV (value used in calculations).
* Integrated cross section for \(K^- p \to \Lambda f_1(1285)\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3010\) MeV: \(\sigma = 11 \pm 3\ \mu\)b (experimental datum used to tune the form factor).
* With the \(BBV\) coupling of Eq. (12) and \(\Lambda = 1250\) MeV, the calculated cross section for \(K^- p \to \Lambda f_1(1285)\) is \(\sigma = 11.07\ \mu\)b.
* With the alternative \(BBV\) coupling and \(\Lambda = 1900\) MeV, the calculated cross section is \(\sigma = 11.24\ \mu\)b.
* The differential cross section for \(\bar K^0 p \to \Sigma^+ K^0 \pi^+ K^-\) at \(\cos\theta = 1\) shows a peak at the \(f_1(1285)\) mass and a second peak at approximately 1420 MeV for both \(\sqrt{s}=2850\) MeV and \(\sqrt{s}=3010\) MeV.
* The cross section for \(K^- p \to \Lambda K^0 \pi^+ K^-\) is about a factor of 6 smaller than that for \(\bar K^0 p \to \Sigma^+ K^0 \pi^+ K^-\), consistent with the ratio of coupling constants \(C_B\).

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Xie and Oset (earlier work) to use kaon facilities for hadron spectroscopy, and directly challenges the PDG classification of the "\(f_1(1420)\)" as a standard resonance. It builds on the demonstration by Vinícius and França (2018) that the \(f_1(1420)\) peak in high-energy reactions arises from the \(K^*\bar K\) decay mode of the \(f_1(1285)\), and provides specific, testable predictions for lower-energy \(\bar K\)-induced reactions at facilities such as J-PARC, COMPASS, and the proposed \(K_L\) factory at Jefferson Lab. The measurement of the predicted peaks would clarify the nature of the \(f_1(1420)\) and provide support for the dynamical generation of the \(f_1(1285)\) from pseudoscalar-vector meson interaction.
