# Preparation of the SU(3) Lattice Yang-Mills Vacuum with Variational Quantum Methods

> Studying QCD and other gauge theories on quantum hardware requires the preparation of physically interesting states. The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) provides a way of performing vacuum state preparation on quantum hardware. In this work, VQE is applied to pure SU(3) lattice Yang-Mills on a single plaquette and one dimensional plaquette chains. Bayesian optimization and gradient descent were investigated for performing the classical optimization. Ansatz states for plaquette chains are constructed in a scalable manner from smaller systems using domain decomposition and a stitching procedure analogous to the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG). Small examples are performed on IBM's superconducting Manila processor.

## Key claim

The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) can prepare the vacuum state of pure SU(3) lattice Yang-Mills theory on small lattices (single plaquette and one-dimensional plaquette chains) using superconducting quantum processors, but the classical optimization method matters significantly: gradient descent converges to the true vacuum while Bayesian optimization fails to reach it even for modest system sizes, and the Lanczos algorithm provides a useful initial state for preconditioning.

## Method

The authors mapped the SU(3) lattice Yang-Mills Hamiltonian in the electric multiplet basis onto qubits, using both a global basis (solving Gauss's law exactly for small lattices) and a local basis with two registers per link for the (p,q) representation labels. They applied VQE with two classical optimizers, Bayesian optimization and gradient descent, to prepare the vacuum state of a single plaquette and one-dimensional plaquette chains, using the Lanczos algorithm to generate initial ansatz states. For larger systems, they constructed scalable ansatz states via domain decomposition and a stitching procedure analogous to the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG), where sub-regions are prepared in their vacuum states and then connected by optimizing unitaries on the links between them.

## Key numbers

- For a single plaquette with truncation p,q ≤ 31, the number of gradient descent steps needed to reach overlap |⟨Vacuum|ψ⟩|² ≥ 0.999 scales as O(g⁻⁴) as coupling g is decreased.
- For a single plaquette with g=0.1 and p,q ≤ 31, the number of gradient descent steps required to reach |⟨Vacuum|ψ⟩|² ≥ 0.999 falls off asymptotically as a Gaussian function of the Krylov dimension used in the Lanczos algorithm initialization.
- The Krylov dimension needed to reach overlap |⟨ψ|Vac⟩|² ≥ 0.999999 for a single plaquette scales as 1/g, leading to a quantum circuit size S = O((log(1/ε)/g)²) for accuracy ε.
- On IBM's Manila processor, VQE for a single plaquette truncated at representation 8 with g=1 showed that explicitly enforcing CP symmetry in the ansatz circuit did not break the symmetry in the prepared vacuum state.
- On the Manila processor with g=0.8 in the color parity basis (using 2 qubits), preconditioning with a Krylov subspace of dimension two allowed convergence to the true vacuum faster than starting from the electric vacuum.

## Context

This work extends prior VQE applications to gauge theories, which had been applied to the Schwinger model (Klco 2018, Kokail 2019) and SU(2) gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions (Atas 2021), by studying the more complex SU(3) Yang-Mills theory relevant to QCD. The domain decomposition and stitching procedure for constructing ansatz states is analogous to the DMRG algorithm (White 1992) used in classical computations, but adapted for quantum hardware without requiring density matrix diagonalization. This enables future scaling to larger lattices where the Lanczos algorithm becomes inefficient due to exponential growth of non-local correlations, and provides a starting point for understanding the resources required to simulate lattice QCD on quantum computers.
