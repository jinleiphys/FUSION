# Exclusive glueball production in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The cross sections for the glueball candidates production in quasi-real photon-photon collisions and on central diffraction processes, i.e. double Pomeron exchange, in heavy ion interactions at RHIC and LHC are computed. The rates for these distinct production channels are compared and they may be a fruitful approach to the investigation of glueballs.

# Exclusive glueball production in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The cross sections for glueball candidate production via two-photon fusion and double Pomeron exchange in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC are large enough for experimental detection, but the rates depend critically on whether the produced resonance is a pure glueball, a pure quarkonium state, or a mixed state; the two-photon widths for pure glueball states are about three orders of magnitude smaller than for pure quarkonium states, making the two-photon channel a potential discriminator of the gluonic content of the resonance.

## Method

The authors computed cross sections for exclusive production of the glueball candidates f0(1500), f0(1710), and X(1835) in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon approximation for the two-photon channel, with the narrow resonance approximation for the gamma-gamma to glueball cross section. For the double Pomeron exchange channel, they used the Bialas-Landshoff non-perturbative approach with Regge trajectories, including gap survival probability factors and Sudakov survival factors for exclusive production, and scaled the proton-proton cross sections to nucleus-nucleus collisions using the Glauber formalism with Woods-Saxon nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- Two-photon decay widths for pure glueball states: Gamma(f0(1500) to gamma gamma) = 0.77 eV, Gamma(f0(1710) to gamma gamma) = 7.03 eV, Gamma(X(1835) to gamma gamma) = 0.021 keV
- Two-photon cross sections for pure glueball production at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): 14-9.3 nb for f0(1500), 60-43 nb for f0(1710), 0.11-0.09 nb for X(1835)
- Two-photon cross sections for pure glueball production at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV): 0.7-1.3 microbarn for f0(1500), 3.8-8.6 microbarn for f0(1710), 0.01-0.02 microbarn for X(1835)
- Exclusive double Pomeron exchange cross sections at LHC: 0.50 +/- 0.32 mb for f0(1500), 0.49 +/- 0.31 mb for f0(1710), 0.45 +/- 0.29 mb for X(1835)
- Exclusive double Pomeron exchange cross sections at RHIC: 0.40 +/- 0.14 mb for f0(1500), 0.41 +/- 0.16 mb for f0(1710), 0.38 +/- 0.14 mb for X(1835)
- Two-gluon decay widths used: Gamma_gg = 69.8 MeV for f0(1500), 70.2 MeV for f0(1710), 70.27 MeV for X(1835)
- Gap survival probability: S_gap^2 = 0.15 at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV), S_gap^2 = 0.032 at LHC (sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV)
- Event rates at LHC for one month running (10^6 s) with PbPb luminosity 5 x 10^26 cm^-2 s^-1: 3.6 x 10^2 events for f0(1500), 2 x 10^3 events for f0(1710), 4 events for X(1835)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of glueball production in heavy ion collisions by Natale (1995) and Schramm (1999), providing updated calculations that include gap survival probability factors and both inclusive and exclusive double Pomeron exchange channels. The results are compared to the ALEPH experimental upper limits on two-photon widths of f0(1500) and f0(1710). The paper enables experimental searches for glueball candidates at RHIC and LHC by providing cross section estimates for two distinct production mechanisms (two-photon and Pomeron-Pomeron) that can be separated experimentally through cuts on impact parameter and transverse momentum, as discussed in the review by Albrow, Coughlin, and Forshaw (2010).
