# Alternative Contributions to the Angular Correlations Observed at RHIC Associated with Parity Fluctuations

> Recent measurements at RHIC of angular correlations of same-sign vs. opposite sign pairs have been interpreted as evidence for large-scale fluctuations of parity-odd fields. In this paper, we provide alternative explanations of the same phenomena based on correlations from charge and momentum conservation overlaid with elliptic flow. These effects are shown to produce correlations with similar magnitudes as those measured. Other correlations are also considered, but estimates of their size suggest they are inconsequential.

## Key claim
Charge and momentum conservation overlaid with elliptic flow can produce angular correlations of the same magnitude as those previously interpreted as evidence for large-scale parity-odd field fluctuations at RHIC, though the alternative explanation may fall short of the total observed correlation by a few tens of percent.

## Method
The author derived analytic expressions for the angular correlation observable gamma = <cos(phi_alpha + phi_beta)> from charge conservation (using charge balance functions) and momentum conservation (using the constraint sum p_x = sum p_y = 0), then combined these with elliptic flow parameter v_2. The predicted correlations were scaled by multiplicity and v_2, then compared to STAR data for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at 100A GeV. Additional contributions from Coulomb fields of incoming nuclei and from parity-odd fluctuations were estimated and found to be much smaller.

## Key numbers
- Observed correlations from STAR are of order 10^{-3} to 10^{-4} for multiplicities M of a few hundred particles at mid-rapidity
- The fraction f_Q of balancing charge found within the 2 units of rapidity covered by STAR is approximately 0.75
- The mean cosine of the balancing charge relative angle <cos Delta_phi_balance> is near 0.5 within a few tens of percent
- The product f_Q * <cos Delta_phi_balance> is approximately 1/3, explaining a bit more than half of the observed scaled correlation
- The rms Delta p_y from Coulomb impulses from spectators is about 2.5 MeV/c for central collisions and rises to about 5 MeV/c for very peripheral collisions
- The contribution from Coulomb fields to gamma_ss - gamma_+- is of order 10^{-5}, significantly smaller than conservation effects
- The integrated strength of the charge balance function is near 0.5 in STAR measurements

## Context
This paper extends the work of the STAR collaboration (2009) that measured angular correlations and interpreted them as evidence for parity-odd fluctuations from color flux tubes, as proposed by Kharzeev (2004, 2009). The alternative explanation based on charge and momentum conservation provides a baseline that must be subtracted before any claim of parity violation can be made. The analysis enables future differential measurements of the balance function as a function of both azimuthal angles and relative rapidity, which could distinguish between conservation effects (confined to about one unit of rapidity) and parity fluctuation effects (extending over multiple units of rapidity).
