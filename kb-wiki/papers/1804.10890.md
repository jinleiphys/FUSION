# Neutrino signal from proto-neutron star evolution: Effects of opacities from charged-current-neutrino interactions and inverse neutron decay

> We investigate the impact of charged current neutrino processes on the formation and evolution of neutrino spectra during the deleptonization of proto-neutron stars. To this end we develop the full kinematics of these reaction rates consistent with the nuclear equation of state, including weak magnetism contributions. This allows us to systematically study the impact of inelastic contributions and weak magnetism on the $ν_e$ and $\barν_e$ luminosities and average energies. Furthermore, we explore the role of the inverse neutron decay, also known as the direct Urca process, on the emitted spectra of $\barν_e$. This process is commonly considered in the cooling scenario of cold neutron stars but has so far been neglected in the evolution of hot proto-neutron stars. We find that the inverse neutron decay becomes the dominating opacity source for low-energy $\barν_e$. Accurate three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport enables us to relate the magnitude of neutrino fluxes and spectra to details of the treatment of weak processes. This allows us to quantify the corresponding impact on the conditions relevant for the nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven wind, which is ejected from the proto-neutron star surface during the deleptonization phase.

## Key claim

The inverse neutron decay (direct Urca process), previously neglected in hot proto-neutron star evolution, becomes the dominating opacity source for low-energy antineutrinos, and its inclusion together with full kinematics for charged-current reactions significantly reduces both the luminosities and average energies of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos compared to the elastic approximation.

## Method

The authors developed full kinematics expressions for charged-current neutrino reaction rates consistent with the nuclear equation of state at the mean-field level, including weak magnetism contributions and nucleon recoil. They implemented these rates into the spherically symmetric, general relativistic Boltzmann neutrino transport code AGILE-BOLTZTRAN, which uses three-flavor angle- and energy-dependent transport, and performed simulations of proto-neutron star deleptonization following an 18 solar mass progenitor through core collapse, bounce, and explosion.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy resolution enhanced to 24 bins extended down to 0.5 MeV for accurate low-energy behavior
- Opacity comparison performed at conditions T=7 MeV, density ρ=2×10^13 g cm^-3, electron fraction Y_e=0.05, with mean-field potential difference U_n-U_p=8.3 MeV
- At these conditions: neutron fraction X_n=0.75, proton fraction X_p=0.02, deuteron fraction X_^2H=0.027, triton fraction X_^3H=0.1, heavy nuclei fraction X_(A,Z)=0.08 with average atomic mass A≈75 and charge Z≈22
- Explosion onset triggered at approximately t=0.35 s post bounce, with shock reaching radii around 1000 km at about t=0.5 s post bounce
- Electron neutrino equilibrium chemical potential μ^eq_νe=25 MeV at the neutrinosphere conditions studied

## Context

This work extends previous simulations by Martinez-Pinedo (2014) and Fischer (2009, 2016) that employed accurate three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport for proto-neutron star deleptonization, but used the elastic approximation for charged-current reactions. It contrasts with the approximate treatment of inelastic contributions and weak magnetism by Horowitz (2001), showing that full kinematics yields lower luminosities and average energies. The results enable more accurate predictions for neutrino-driven wind nucleosynthesis, where the electron fraction depends sensitively on the spectral difference between antineutrinos and neutrinos as established by Qian (1996).
