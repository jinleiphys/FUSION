# Ferromagnetic Phase Transitions in Neutron Stars

> The ferromagnetic phase in pure neutron matter as well as charge neutral, beta-equilibrated nuclear matter is considered. We employ Quantum Hadrodynamics, a relativistic field theory description of nuclear matter with meson degrees of freedom, and include couplings between the baryon (proton and neutron) magnetic dipole moment as well as between their charge and the magnetic field in the Lagrangian density describing such a system. We vary the strength of the baryon magnetic dipole moment till a non-zero value of the magnetic field, for which the total energy density of the magnetised system is at a minimum, is found. The system is then assumed to be in the ferromagnetic state. The ferromagnetic equation of state is employed to study matter in the neutron star interior. We find that as the density increases the ferromagnetic field does not increase continuously, but exhibit sudden rapid increases. These sudden increases in the magnetic field correspond to shifts between different configurations of the charged particle's Landau levels and can have significant observational consequences for neutron stars. We also found that although the ferromagnetic phase softens the neutron star equation of state it does not significantly alter the star's mass-radius relationship. The properties of magnetised symmetric nuclear matter were also studied. We confirm that magnetised matter tends to be more proton-rich but become more weakly bound for stronger magnetic fields. We show that the behaviour of the compressibility of nuclear matter is influenced by the Landau quantisation and tends to have an oscillatory character as it increases with the magnetic field. The symmetry energy also exhibits similar behaviour.

# Ferromagnetic Phase Transitions in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The paper claims that ferromagnetic ordering in neutron star matter can produce sudden, discontinuous increases in the magnetic field strength as density increases, corresponding to shifts between different Landau level configurations of charged particles, although this ferromagnetic phase does not significantly alter the star's mass-radius relationship despite softening the equation of state.

## Method

The author employs Quantum Hadrodynamics, a relativistic field theory with meson degrees of freedom, to describe pure neutron matter and charge-neutral beta-equilibrated nuclear matter. The Lagrangian density includes couplings between baryon magnetic dipole moments and the magnetic field, and the strength of the baryon magnetic dipole moment is varied until a non-zero magnetic field value minimizing the total energy density is found, defining the ferromagnetic state. The Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation is used to derive the non-relativistic limit of the modified Dirac equation, and the single-particle energy spectra for both protons (with Landau quantization) and neutrons in a magnetic field are derived including the anomalous magnetic moment coupling.

## Key numbers

- The observed proton magnetic dipole moment is 2.793 μ_N^(dip) and the neutron magnetic dipole moment is -1.913 μ_N^(dip), corresponding to κ_p = 0.793 and κ_n = -1.913
- The degeneracy of each Landau level per unit area is given by |q_p||B|/(2π), derived from the magnetic flux density divided by the fundamental flux quantum Φ_0 = 2π/|q_p|
- The proton energy spectrum in a magnetic field including the anomalous magnetic moment coupling is E(k_z,λ,n) = ±√[k_z² + (√(m² + 2|q_p B|(n' + 1/2 - αλ/2)) + λ g_p B)²]
- The neutron energy spectrum in a magnetic field is E(k_z,λ) = ±√[k_z² + (√(k_⊥² + m²) + λ g_n B)²]

## Context

This work extends the treatment of ferromagnetic phase transitions in neutron star matter by Broderick et al. (2000) and Mao et al., incorporating the σ^{μν}F_{μν} coupling between baryon magnetic dipole moments and the magnetic field within a relativistic mean-field framework. The derivation of the single-particle spectra with anomalous magnetic moments builds on the standard Dirac equation treatment in Gross and Zuber. The results enable future studies of how Landau quantization effects, including the oscillatory behavior of compressibility and symmetry energy, may produce observable signatures in neutron star magnetic field evolution and electromagnetic emission.
