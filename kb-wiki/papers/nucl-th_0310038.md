# Polarization observables in $π$-photoproduction on the deuteron

> A general analysis of polarization observables for the $d(γ,π)NN$ reaction with polarized photons and/or oriented deuterons is presented. The unpolarized differential cross section, linear photon asymmetry, vector and tensor target asymmetries are predicted for forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim
The paper presents a general theoretical framework for calculating polarization observables in incoherent single-pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the Δ(1232)-resonance region, but the predictions are explicitly intended as a basis for simulation and planning of future experiments, not as final quantitative results.

## Method
The author calculates the unpolarized differential cross section, linear photon asymmetry, vector target asymmetry (T₁₁), and tensor target asymmetries (T₂₀, T₂₁, T₂₂) for the d(γ,π)NN reaction using the elementary pion photoproduction operator from the effective Lagrangian model of Schmidt et al. (1996) and the Paris potential for the deuteron wave function. All observables are integrated numerically over pion momentum q, the polar angle θ_{pNN}, and the azimuthal angle φ_{pNN} of the relative momentum of the two outgoing nucleons in the laboratory frame, with integration accuracy better than 1%.

## Key numbers
- The maximum pion momentum q decreases from approximately 300 MeV at forward pion angles to approximately 200 MeV at backward pion angles when the relative momentum of the two outgoing nucleons approaches zero.
- The photon asymmetry Σ has negative values at forward and backward pion angles for all charge states, with Born contributions being relatively small compared to results when θπ changes from 60° to 120°.
- The vector target asymmetry T₁₁ vanishes at θπ = 0 and θπ = π, and is always negative for charged pion channels and always positive for the neutral pion channel.
- The tensor target asymmetries T₂₁ and T₂₂ also vanish at θπ = 0 and θπ = π.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analysis of polarization observables in deuteron photopion production (Darwish, 2003) and builds on the formalism of Arenhövel (1988). The results are intended to guide forthcoming polarization experiments and complement earlier studies of coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron by Blaazer et al. (1994) and Wilhelm and Arenhövel (1995), as well as studies of the coherent process of charged pion photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron (Laget, 1978; Kobayashi, 1987), deuteron electrodisintegration (Fabian, 1976), and η-photoproduction (Fix, 1997).
