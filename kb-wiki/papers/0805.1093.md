# Heavy Quark Production from Jet Conversions in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Recently, it has been demonstrated that the chemical composition of jets in heavy ion collisions is significantly altered compared to jets in the vacuum. This signal can be used to probe the medium formed in nuclear collisions. In this study we investigate the possibility that fast light quarks and gluons can convert to heavy quarks when passing through a quark gluon plasma. We study the rate of light to heavy jet conversions in a consistent Fokker-Planck framework and investigate their impact on the production of high-$p_T$ charm and bottom quarks at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

Light quarks and gluons can convert to heavy quarks when passing through a quark-gluon plasma, but the contribution of such jet conversions to high-\(p_T\) charm and bottom yields is small (about 10% for charm and 0.3% for bottom at RHIC), making heavy quarks less effective than strangeness as a probe of the medium's mean free path at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order perturbative QCD matrix elements for the processes \(g+g\to Q+\bar Q\) (gluon annihilation) and \(g(q)+Q\to Q+g(q)\) (Compton scattering), scaled by a \(K\)-factor of 4. They modeled the propagation of heavy quarks in an expanding QGP fireball using a Fokker-Planck equation (solved via a Langevin equation) with drag and diffusion coefficients calculated for charm (\(m_c=1.2\) GeV) and bottom (\(m_b=4.5\) GeV) quarks, using a fixed coupling \(\alpha_s=0.3\) and thermal masses for medium partons. The conversion rates were implemented through rate equations, and the fireball evolution assumed boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with accelerated transverse expansion.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient \(\gamma\) for charm and bottom quarks decreases with increasing momentum; diffusion coefficients \(B_0\) and \(B_1\) show the opposite trend (calculated for temperatures \(T\) = 200, 250, 300, 350, 400 MeV).
- Conversion width for a gluon jet to charm via \(g+g\to Q+\bar Q\) is shown as a function of \(p_T\) at temperatures \(T\) = 200, 250, 300, 350, 400 MeV.
- In central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, jet conversions contribute about 10% to the charm spectrum and about 0.3% to the bottom spectrum at high \(p_T\).
- In Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.5\) TeV, the ratio of heavy quarks from jet conversions to initial production is similar in magnitude to RHIC results.
- Initial charm spectrum at LHC parametrized as \(dN_c/d^2 p_T = 2497 (1+p_T/1.95)^{-5.5} (p_T[1+(4/(0.1+p_T))^2])^{-1}\); bottom spectrum as \(dN_b/d^2 p_T = 38 (1+p_T/2.0)^{-5.6} [1+(10/(0.8+p_T))^3]^{-1}\).
- Fireball parameters: initial temperature \(T_i=350\) MeV at RHIC, \(T_0=700\) MeV at LHC; critical temperature \(T_c=175\) MeV reached at \(\tau_c\sim 5\) fm at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of flavor conversions among light quarks and gluons (Liu and Fries, 2007) and parton-to-photon conversions (Fries, 2002) to the case of heavy quark production. It directly addresses whether charm could serve as a probe analogous to strangeness at RHIC, as proposed in earlier work (Liu and Fries, 2007). The results show that heavy quark conversions are inefficient compared to strangeness conversions, consistent with experimental data on single electrons at RHIC (Adare, 2006), and imply that high-\(p_T\) charm at LHC is less useful than strangeness at RHIC for probing the mean free path in the medium. The framework enables future extraction of transport coefficients from data and comparison with perturbative and non-perturbative predictions.
