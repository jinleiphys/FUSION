# Neutrino/antineutrino-$^{12}$C charged cross sections in the projected QRPA formalism

> The $ν/\barν-^{12}$C cross sections are evaluated in the projected quasiparticle random phase approximation (PQRPA). The cross section for $ν_e$ as a function of the incident neutrino energy is compared with recent theoretical calculations of more sophisticated models. The $\barν-^{12}$C cross section is calculated for the first time with the PQRPA. The distribution of cross sections averaged with the Michel spectrum as well as with other estimated fluxes for future experiments is compared for both $ν_e$ and $\barν_e$. Some astrophysical implications are addressed.

## Key claim

The projected quasiparticle random phase approximation (PQRPA) reproduces neutrino-12C cross sections without the need for a reduction factor, unlike the continuum RPA (CRPA) which requires an average reduction factor of approximately 1.5 to match experimental data. The antineutrino-12C cross section is calculated for the first time with the PQRPA, and the authors note that the PQRPA results overestimate experimental values by approximately 21 percent on average.

## Method

The authors evaluate neutrino and antineutrino charged-current cross sections on 12C using the PQRPA formalism with a delta interaction for the residual force, employing three different parameter sets (P(I), P(II), P(III)) for the particle-hole coupling strengths. The configuration space includes single-particle orbitals with nl = (1s, 1p, 1d, 2s, 1f, 2p) for both protons and neutrons, with harmonic oscillator wave functions using length parameter b = 1.67 fm. The antineutrino cross sections are obtained by modifying the lepton trace in the formalism of Krmpotic et al. (2005) with a sign factor S1 = ±1 for neutrino/antineutrino reactions.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive folded cross section for neutrino-12C in DAR region: 8.07 × 10⁻⁴² cm² for P(II), compared to experimental value 8.9 ± 0.9 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Auerbach et al., 2001)
- Inclusive folded cross section for neutrino-12C in DAR region: 18.6 × 10⁻⁴² cm² for PQRPA (II) and 12.14 × 10⁻⁴² cm² for RQRPA, compared to experimental value 13.2 ± 0.7 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Auerbach et al., 2001)
- Inclusive folded cross section for muon neutrino-12C in DIF region: 12.9 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² for PQRPA (PII) and 19.59 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² for RQRPA, compared to experimental value 12.4 ± 0.7 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (Auerbach et al., 2002a)
- Exclusive muon capture rate on 12B (1⁺₁ state): 6.27 × 10³ s⁻¹ for P(II) and P(III), compared to experimental value 6.2 ± 0.3 × 10³ s⁻¹ (Miller et al., 1972)
- Ground state energy of 12N: 18.14 MeV for P(II), compared to experimental value 17.3381 ± 0.001 MeV (Ajzenberg-Selove, 1985)
- B(GT) value: 0.477 for P(II), compared to experimental value 0.496 ± 0.030 (Alburger et al., 1978)
- Inclusive muon capture rate on 12C: 42.56 × 10³ s⁻¹ for P(II), compared to experimental value 38 ± 1 × 10³ s⁻¹ (Suzuki et al., 1987)
- Average deviation parameter epsilon-bar: 12 percent for P(II), 35 percent for P(I), 17 percent for P(III)

## Context

This work extends the PQRPA formalism previously applied to neutrino-nucleus reactions by Krmpotic et al. (2005) and Samana et al. (2006) to include antineutrino reactions on 12C for the first time. The results are compared with several other theoretical approaches including CRPA (Kolbe et al., 1999), RQRPA (Paar et al., 2007), the shell model (Volpe et al., 2000), and the elementary particle treatment (Fukugita et al., 1988). The authors note that the PQRPA results are similar to RQRPA for low multipoles but diverge at higher J values, and that the PQRPA inclusive cross section in the DIF region for muon neutrinos is closer to experiment than RQRPA. The work enables improved predictions for supernova neutrino detection in experiments such as LVD, Borexino, and LENA, and demonstrates that beta-beams with boosted 6He ions could probe forbidden transitions in 12C.
