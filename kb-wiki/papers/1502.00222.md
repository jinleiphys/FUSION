# Low-energy proton capture reactions in the mass region 55-60

> Low energy proton capture reactions in the mass 55-60 region are studied in a microscopic optical model. Nuclear density profile is calculated using the relativistic mean field theory. The DDM3Y interaction is folded with the theoretical density to obtain the proton-nucleus optical potential. A definite set of normalization parameters has been obtained for the concerned mass region by comparing with all available experimental data in this mass region. These parameters have been used to obtain proton capture rates for astrophysically important reactions in this mass region.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a single set of normalization parameters for the real (2.0) and imaginary (1.4) parts of the folded optical potential that, while not reproducing every individual reaction perfectly, provides a consistent framework for calculating proton capture cross sections on both stable and unstable nuclei in the mass 55-60 region.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear density profiles using the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the FSUGold interaction, then fold these densities with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) Reid-Elliot effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to construct a semi-microscopic optical potential. A spin-orbit term is added via the Scheerbaum prescription, and the potential is implemented in the TALYS1.4 code to perform Hauser-Feshbach calculations with Goriely's microscopic level densities and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov E1 gamma-ray strength functions. The real part of the folded potential is multiplied by a normalization constant of 2.0 and the imaginary part by 1.4, and the resulting S-factors are compared with experimental data for stable targets in the Gamow window near 3 GK.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii from RMF theory agree with experimental values from Angeli et al. to within 0.5% relative difference for all eight nuclei listed (e.g., 3.7057 fm theory vs. 3.7057 fm experiment for 55Mn; 3.7361 fm vs. 3.7377 fm for 56Fe).
- For 58Ni(p,gamma), the calculation overpredicts the measurement of Tingwell et al. by a factor of approximately 2.5.
- For 60Ni(p,gamma), the calculation overpredicts the data of Tingwell et al. by a factor of approximately 1.5 and underpredicts the data of Krivonosov et al. by a factor of approximately 0.35.
- The Gamow window for (p,gamma) reactions on stable isotopes in mass 55-60 lies between 1 MeV and 3 MeV for temperatures around 3 GK.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic optical model calculations from the mass 60-100 region (Gangopadhyay et al., Lahiri et al.) down to mass 55-60, finding that different normalization parameters are needed for this lighter mass region. The calculated reaction rates for 56Ni(p,gamma), 57Cu(p,gamma), and 59Cu(p,gamma) differ significantly from the NON-SMOKER rates of Rauscher et al., which used Hauser-Feshbach calculations with global optical potentials and masses from the Finite Range Droplet Model. The framework enables rate calculations for proton-rich unstable nuclei identified by Parikh et al. as important in rp-process scenarios, where experimental data are unavailable.
