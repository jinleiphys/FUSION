# Nucleon-nucleon potential from skyrmion dipole interactions

> We derive the nucleon-nucleon interaction from the Skyrme model using second order perturbation theory and the dipole approximation to skyrmion dynamics. Unlike previous derivations, our derivation accounts for the non-trivial kinetic and potential parts of the skyrmion-skyrmion interaction lagrangian and how they couple in the quantum calculation. We derive the eight low energy interaction potentials and compare them with the phenomenological Paris model, finding qualitative agreement in seven cases.

## Key claim

The authors derive the full nucleon-nucleon interaction from the Skyrme model using second order perturbation theory combined with the dipole approximation to skyrmion dynamics, accounting for both kinetic and potential parts of the skyrmion-skyrmion interaction Lagrangian and their coupling in the quantum calculation; however, the original published calculation contained two errors that required correction, and after correction the isoscalar spin-orbit potential is no longer attractive in the range of interest, unlike the seven other potentials which show qualitative agreement with the Paris model.

## Method

The authors model each nucleon as a quantised skyrmion with position and orientation degrees of freedom, then describe two well-separated skyrmions using the dipole approximation, where each skyrmion's asymptotic pion field is treated as a triplet of orthogonal dipoles. They derive the classical dipole-dipole interaction Lagrangian for massive pions, then quantise this system using second order perturbation theory to obtain a quantum Hamiltonian, from which they extract the eight low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials. The calculation corrects two errors from the original paper: one in the charge distribution of a dipole (eq. 3.5) and one in the quantum Hamiltonian (eq. 4.3), with corrected formulae for all eight potentials provided in the erratum.

## Key numbers

- Calibrated parameters: pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 201\) MeV, Skyrme coupling \(e = 3.30\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 199\) MeV
- Resulting skyrmion properties: mass \(M = 2389\) MeV, moment of inertia \(\Lambda = 321\) MeV fm\(^2\), and \(\rho = 143\) MeV fm\(^3\)
- The corrected isoscalar spin-orbit potential \(V_{LS}^{IS}\) is no longer attractive in the range of interest, unlike the original calculation
- Six of the eight potentials (all except the two spin-orbit potentials) show good agreement with the phenomenological Paris potential after correction

## Context

This work extends the approach of Walet, Amado and Hosaka (1992), who used second order perturbation theory with the Atiyah-Manton instanton approximation but focused only on potential energy, and of Schroers (1993) and Gisiger-Paranjape (1994), who showed that kinetic energy terms in the skyrmion-skyrmion interaction are not subleading. The corrected calculation resolves a long-standing problem with the isoscalar spin-orbit potential sign that had persisted in earlier Skyrme model derivations using the product approximation and first order perturbation theory, such as those by Riska and Nyman (1987) and Abada (1996). The authors note that a forthcoming paper using instanton holonomies obtains correct signs for both spin-orbit potentials.
