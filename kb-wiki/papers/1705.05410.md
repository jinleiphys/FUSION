# Isolating the chiral magnetic effect from backgrounds by pair invariant mass

> Topological gluon configurations in quantum chromodynamics induce quark chirality imbalance in local domains, which can result in the chiral magnetic effect (CME)--an electric charge separation along a strong magnetic field. Experimental searches for the CME in relativistic heavy ion collisions via the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator ($Δγ$) suffer from large backgrounds arising from particle correlations (e.g. due to resonance decays) coupled with the elliptic anisotropy. We propose differential measurements of the $Δγ$ as a function of the pair invariant mass ($m_{\rm inv}$), by restricting to high $m_{\rm inv}$ thus relatively background free, and by studying the $m_{\rm inv}$ dependence to separate the possible CME signal from backgrounds. We demonstrate by model studies the feasibility and effectiveness of such measurements for the CME search.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator ($\Delta\gamma$) as a function of particle pair invariant mass ($m_{\rm inv}$) can separate the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal from resonance decay backgrounds, but the method relies on the assumption that the CME and backgrounds have different $m_{\rm inv}$ dependences; if they have the same shape, the method cannot distinguish them.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV without CME, and a toy Monte Carlo simulation with input CME signals (parameterized by $a_1$) including primordial pions and resonances ($\rho$, $\eta$, $\omega$, $K_S$). They analyzed $\Delta\gamma$ as a function of pair invariant mass $m_{\rm inv}$, applying a lower cut at $m_{\rm inv}>2$ GeV/$c^2$ to eliminate resonance backgrounds, and developed two-component (TC) model fits and TC fits aided by event-shape engineering (ESE) to extract CME signals from the $m_{\rm inv}$ dependence.

## Key numbers

- In AMPT (no CME) with $11\times10^6$ events of 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at $b=6.6$–8.2 fm: inclusive $\Delta\gamma = (8.1\pm0.1)\times10^{-5}$; $\Delta\gamma$ with $m_{\rm inv}>2$ GeV/$c^2$ cut = $(-0.6\pm0.8)\times10^{-5}$, giving an upper limit of 20% of inclusive $\Delta\gamma$ at 98% confidence level.
- In toy MC with input CME $a_1=0.008$ (20% of total $\Delta\gamma$) and $200\times10^6$ events for 40–50% centrality Au+Au: $\Delta\gamma$ with $m_{\rm inv}>2$ GeV/$c^2$ cut = $(4.5\pm0.8)\times10^{-5}$, consistent with input CME signal of $4.6\times10^{-5}$, yielding a $5\sigma$ measurement.
- TC fit to toy MC with $a_1=0.008$ extracted $\Delta\gamma_{\rm CME} = (4.2\pm0.2)\times10^{-5}$ (input $4.6\times10^{-5}$), with $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=108/75$.
- For a broad sphaleron/instanton mass distribution (Gaussian at 1 GeV/$c^2$, width 0.5 GeV/$c^2$) giving effective CME $4.0\times10^{-5}$, TC fit extracted $\Delta\gamma_{\rm CME} = (3.7\pm0.2)\times10^{-5}$ with $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=187/75$.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental searches for the CME using the $\Delta\gamma$ observable at RHIC (STAR) and LHC (ALICE, CMS), which suffered from large backgrounds from resonance decays coupled with elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$). Prior attempts to suppress backgrounds included analyzing charge asymmetry versus event-by-event $v_2$ (STAR, 2013) and dividing data by $v_2$ (ALICE, 2017), but those methods may not completely eliminate background as pointed out by Wang (2016). The proposed invariant mass method enables a new differential approach to separate CME from backgrounds, and can be applied to existing STAR data samples of up to $500\times10^6$ events from Run-11 to potentially achieve $5\sigma$ measurements or set stringent upper limits.
