# Finite-Size Scaling Exponents of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model

> We study the ground state properties of the critical Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model. Using the Holstein-Primakoff boson representation, and the continuous unitary transformation technique, we compute explicitly the finite-size scaling exponents for the energy gap, the ground state energy, the magnetization, and the spin-spin correlation functions. Finally, we discuss the behavior of the two-spin entanglement in the vicinity of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper analytically derives finite-size scaling exponents for the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model at its quantum critical point, showing that the energy gap scales as \(N^{-1/3}\) and that the magnetization scaling exponent is 2/3, which differs from a previously reported numerical value of \(0.55 \pm 0.01\) obtained for system sizes up to \(N=500\) spins.

## Method

The authors use the Holstein-Primakoff boson representation to map the spin-1/2 LMG Hamiltonian onto a bosonic Hamiltonian in the maximum total spin subspace (\(S=N/2\)). They then perform a \(1/N\) expansion and diagonalize the Hamiltonian order by order using the continuous unitary transformation (CUT) technique with a quasiparticle-conserving generator, solving the resulting flow equations analytically up to order \(1/N^3\) for the energy gap and up to order \(1/N^2\) for observables. Finite-size scaling exponents are extracted by analyzing the divergence of the \(1/N\) corrections near the critical point \(\lambda=1\) and applying a scaling argument to obtain non-singular behavior at finite \(N\).

## Key numbers

- Energy gap at criticality: \(\Delta(N) \sim a_\Delta N^{-1/3}\)
- Ground state energy per spin at criticality: \(e_0(N) \sim -1 - (1-\gamma)/(2N) + a_e N^{-4/3}\)
- Magnetization at criticality: \(2\langle S_z \rangle/N \sim 1 + 1/N + a_z N^{-2/3}\)
- Transverse spin correlation at criticality: \(4\langle S_x^2 \rangle/N^2 \sim a_{xx} N^{-2/3}\)
- Transverse spin correlation at criticality: \(4\langle S_y^2 \rangle/N^2 \sim a_{yy} N^{-4/3}\)
- Longitudinal spin correlation at criticality: \(4\langle S_z^2 \rangle/N^2 \sim 1 + 2/N + a_{zz} N^{-2/3}\) with \(a_{zz} = -a_{xx}\)
- Mean-field gap: \(\Delta(\infty) = 2 \sqrt{(1-\lambda)(1-\gamma\lambda)}\)
- For the isotropic case \(\gamma=1\): exact result \(\Delta_{\gamma=1}(N) = 2(1-\lambda) + 2\lambda/N\)
- In the thermodynamic limit for \(\lambda<1\): \(\lim_{N\to\infty} (N-1)C_{\lambda<1} = 1 - \sqrt{(1-\lambda)/(1-\gamma\lambda)}\)

## Context

This work extends numerical finite-size scaling studies of the LMG model by Botet and Jullien (1982, 1983), who conjectured the \(N^{-1/3}\) gap exponent and the \(N^{-2/3}\) exponent for \(\langle S_x^2 \rangle\), by providing the first analytical derivation of these exponents. The results also resolve a discrepancy with a numerical study by Reslen et al. (2004) that reported a magnetization exponent of \(0.55 \pm 0.01\) for systems up to \(N=500\) spins, which the authors attribute to insufficient system size; their own numerics up to \(N=2^{14}\) confirm the analytically derived exponent of 2/3. The paper enables analytical understanding of entanglement properties in the LMG model, showing that the concurrence at the critical point scales as \((N-1)C_{\lambda=1} \sim 1 - a_{yy} N^{-1/3}\), and notes that the results are also relevant for the Dicke model via a mapping established by Reslen et al. (2004).
