# Determination of fragmentation functions and their uncertainties from e+ + e- -> h + X data

> Fragmentation functions are determined for pions, kaons, and nucleons by a global analysis of charged-hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation. The optimum functions are obtained in both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) of alpha_s. It is important that uncertainties of the fragmentation functions are estimated in this work by the Hessian method. We found that the uncertainties are large at small Q^2 and that they are generally reduced in the NLO in comparison with the LO ones. We supply a code for calculating the fragmentation functions and their uncertainties for the pions, kaons, and nucleons at given z and Q^2.

## Key claim

The authors determine fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, and nucleons from a global fit to electron-positron annihilation data, and for the first time provide uncertainty estimates using the Hessian method; however, the uncertainties are large at small \(Q^2\) and the total \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom is about 1.73 for pions, indicating that the fit does not describe all data sets equally well (the DELPHI data deviate significantly).

## Method

The fragmentation functions \(D_i^h(z,Q_0^2)\) are parametrized at a fixed scale \(Q_0^2\) as \(N_i^h z^{\alpha_i^h} (1-z)^{\beta_i^h}\), with the normalization expressed via the second moment \(M_i^h\). Parameters are determined by minimizing a \(\chi^2\) function against charged-hadron production data from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation, with the functions evolved to experimental \(Q^2\) points via timelike DGLAP equations. Uncertainties are estimated using the Hessian method, and the analysis is performed at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

- Pion analysis: 264 data points fitted with 14 parameters; total \(\chi^2 = 433.5\) in NLO, giving \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 1.73\).
- For kaons and protons, \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.}\) values are approximately 2.
- Fragmentation functions are shown at \(Q^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(m_c^2\), \(m_b^2\), and \(M_Z^2\) (the \(Z\)-boson mass squared).
- Uncertainties are large at small \(Q^2\) (e.g., \(1\ \text{GeV}^2\)) and are reduced in NLO compared to LO.

## Context

This work extends prior determinations of fragmentation functions by groups such as KKP (Kniehl, Kramer, and Potter) and Kretzer, which did not provide uncertainty estimates, whereas uncertainties are routinely estimated for parton distribution functions (PDFs) in nucleons and nuclei. The authors provide a public code that returns both the optimum fragmentation functions and their uncertainties for pions, kaons, and nucleons at any given \(z\) and \(Q^2\), enabling more reliable extraction of nucleon spin and quark-hadron matter properties from semi-inclusive processes in lepton-nucleon and hadron-hadron collisions.
