# Mass Number Dependence of Nuclear Pairing

> Large scale Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the finite-range Gogny force D1S have been performed in order to extract the corresponding theoretical average mass dependence of the nuclear gap values. Good agreement with experimental data from the three-point filter $Δ^{(3)}$(N) with N odd has been found for both the neutron and proton gaps. The study confirms earlier findings [W. Satula, J. Dobaczewski, and W. Nazarewicz, Phys. Rev. Lett. 81 3599 (1998)] that the mass dependence of the gap is much weaker than the so far accepted 12/$\sqrtA$ MeV law.

## Key claim

Large-scale Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1S force confirm that the average mass-number dependence of nuclear pairing gaps is much weaker than the commonly accepted 12/√A MeV law, with the data instead consistent with an A^{-1/3} dependence. The study supports the earlier finding of Satula, Dobaczewski, and Nazarewicz (1998) that the three-point filter Δ^{(3)}(N) with N odd is the best probe of the pairing component of odd-even staggering, but acknowledges ongoing debate about the interpretation of this filter and the uncertain magnitude of time-odd mean-field effects (approximately 100 keV in odd-A light nuclei).

## Method

The authors performed unconstrained HFB calculations for nearly 400 even-even nuclei using the finite-range Gogny D1S effective interaction, extracting theoretical neutron and proton pairing gaps from the pairing field. They defined two types of gap for each nucleus: Δ_last (the gap on the last occupied state) and Δ_aver (a weighted average around the Fermi surface using pair correlation probabilities). To compare with experimental data, they averaged these theoretical gaps over neighboring nuclei (averaging over N for fixed Z for protons, and over Z for fixed N for neutrons) and plotted them against odd-Z and odd-N values, matching the experimental extraction method of Satula, Dobaczewski, and Nazarewicz (1998).

## Key numbers

- The best fit to the theoretical neutron gaps in the stability valley (SV) using Δ_N = α + β A^{-1/3} yields α = 0.3 and β = 3.1 (units: MeV for α, MeV·A^{1/3} for β).
- For the neutron-rich region (NR), the best fit yields α = 0.35 and β = 2.6.
- The asymptotic value of the gap approaches the nuclear matter value Δ_nm = 0.4 MeV obtained with the Gogny force.
- Including the Coulomb interaction in the pairing field reduces theoretical gap values by 100 to 200 keV, depending on the proton number.
- The average absolute magnitude of time-odd mean-field effects in odd-A light nuclei is approximately 100 keV, based on systematic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations cited from the literature.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Satula, Dobaczewski, and Nazarewicz (1998), who used Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations to argue that the 12/√A law overestimates gaps in light nuclei, by performing the first systematic large-scale HFB study with the Gogny D1S force. The results contradict the long-standing 12/√A MeV law derived from macroscopic-microscopic models (Zeldes; Jensen 1984) and instead support an A^{-1/3} dependence, which has some theoretical justification from the weak-coupling approximation for the gap. The study enables future investigations of size-dependent pairing in other finite superfluid or superconducting systems, such as ultra-small metallic grains and thin superconducting films.
