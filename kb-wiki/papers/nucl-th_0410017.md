# Hydrodynamic Approaches to Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We give a short review about the hydrodynamic model and its application to the elliptic flow phenomena and the pion interferometry in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The observed large elliptic flow at RHIC is consistent with ideal hydrodynamic predictions, suggesting the quark-gluon plasma behaves as an almost perfect fluid with remarkably small viscosity; however, this agreement holds only near midrapidity and at high collision energies, while hydrodynamics overpredicts the data in forward/backward rapidity regions and at lower energies such as AGS or SPS.

## Method

The author reviews (2+1)-dimensional and full 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulations that solve energy-momentum conservation equations with an equation of state (EoS), initial conditions parametrized from participant or binary collision density, and the Cooper-Frye freezeout prescription. Results are compared with experimental elliptic flow coefficients and HBT radii from RHIC, SPS, and AGS, and a hybrid model is discussed where the QGP phase is described by ideal hydrodynamics while the hadron phase is treated with a hadronic cascade (RQMD or UrQMD).

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 and 200 GeV produce large elliptic flow consistent with hydrodynamic predictions, with initial thermalization time tau ~ 0.6 fm/c
- For the first order phase transition EoS, latent heat is ~1 GeV/fm^3
- Sound velocity c_s^2 vanishes in the mixed phase at n_B=0
- The crossover phase transition region has width Delta T ~ 0.1 T_c
- Hydrodynamic response v_2/epsilon is almost constant at 0.2-0.24 at SPS and RHIC energies
- In Boltzmann simulations with 47 mb cross section, v_2(p_T) is still ~30% smaller than the hydrodynamic result
- For the hydro+cascade model with latent heat ~0.8 GeV/fm^3, the excitation function increases linearly with multiplicity
- In the hydro+cascade (UrQMD) approach with T_c=160 MeV, the HBT ratio R_out/R_side becomes 2.0 at K_T=0.15 GeV/c at hadronization, reducing to 1.4-1.6 after cascade smearing, still above the experimental data ~1

## Context

This review extends the work of Kolb, Heinz, Huovinen (2001) who showed that hydrodynamic predictions match RHIC elliptic flow data, in contrast to hadronic transport models which fail to reproduce the large v_2 values at RHIC as shown by Bass and Srivastava. The paper contrasts with the situation at AGS and SPS where hydrodynamics overpredicts data as reported by NA49, and addresses the "HBT puzzle" where hydrodynamic models cannot reproduce the HBT radii measured by STAR and PHENIX. The hybrid hydro+cascade approach of Teaney, Lauret, Shuryak (2001) is highlighted as the most successful model for elliptic flow, but the author identifies the need for full 3D hybrid simulations with proper treatment of the QGP-hadron boundary as an open problem.
