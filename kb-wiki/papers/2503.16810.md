# Glueballonia as Hopfions

> We work out the Hopfion description of glueballs by inclusively comparing the energy spectra obtained by quantizing Hopfions with experimental data and lattice QCD. Identifying a Hopfion carrying a unit topological charge as $f_0(1500)$, the Hopfions with the topological charge two are classified as glueballonia, i.e., two glueballs are bound together. We find a tightly and a loosely bound glueballonia complying with $f_0 (2470)$ and a novel scalar particle carrying the mass around 2814 MeV, respectively, and calculate their binding energies. By the rigid body quantization of Hopfions, we predict a characteristic multiplet structure of tensor glueball states. Some of them are missing in the current experimental data and can be verified in future measurements.

## Key claim

The paper identifies Hopfions (topological solitons in the Skyrme-Faddeev model derived from SU(2) pure Yang-Mills theory) with specific glueball states, claiming that the Hopfion with unit topological charge corresponds to the scalar meson f0(1500) and that Hopfions with topological charge two correspond to glueballonia (bound states of two glueballs). The hedge is that this identification works only for a specific choice of coupling constants (kappa/e = 5.54 MeV and kappa e^3 = 88.5 GeV) fixed by fitting to f0(1500) and f_J(2220) masses.

## Method

The authors quantize axially symmetric Hopfion solutions of the Skyrme-Faddeev model using rigid body collective coordinate quantization, promoting isospatial and spatial rotation parameters to dynamical variables. They compute energy eigenvalues from the resulting symmetric top Hamiltonian, which depends on the topological charge Q = l m (where l and m are winding numbers) and quantum numbers (J, |K_3|). The coupling constants kappa and e are fixed by identifying the lowest Q=1 scalar state with f0(1500) and the lowest Q=1 tensor state with f_J(2220), after which all other state energies are determined without further fitting.

## Key numbers

* kappa/e = 5.54 MeV, fixed by identifying the Q=1 Hopfion with {(1,1),(0,0)} as f0(1500) at 1500 MeV
* kappa e^3 = 88.5 GeV, fixed by identifying the Q=1 Hopfion with {(1,1),(2,0)} as f_J(2220) at 2220 MeV
* Q=2 Hopfion with {(2,1),(0,0)} has mass 2477 MeV, identified with f0(2470) at 2470 MeV
* Q=2 Hopfion with {(1,2),(0,0)} has mass 2814 MeV, predicted as a novel scalar particle
* Binding energies of glueballonia: 576 MeV for the tightly bound state (Q=2, (2,1)) and 230 MeV for the loosely bound state (Q=2, (1,2))
* Q=1 tensor triplet: (2,0) at 2231 MeV (f_J(2220)), (2,1) at 2306 MeV (f2(2300)), (2,2) at 2531 MeV (f2(2340))
* Q=2 tensor doublet from (2,1): (2,0) at 2749 MeV and (2,1) at 2732 MeV, both predicted states with no current experimental counterpart

## Context

This work extends the Hopfion approach to glueballs originally proposed by Faddeev, Niemi, and others (Faddeev 1996, 1998), and builds on earlier quantization studies by Kondo (2006). It provides the first systematic comparison of Hopfion spectra with experimental data from the Particle Data Group and lattice QCD predictions from Morningstar (1999). The concept of glueballonia (bound states of two glueballs) was introduced by Giacosa (2021), and this paper identifies specific candidates for both tightly bound (f0(2470)) and loosely bound (2814 MeV) glueballonia. The predicted multiplet structures for tensor glueballs (triplet for Q=1, doublet for Q=2) can be tested in future experiments such as BESIII, which recently reported the glueball candidate X(2370).
