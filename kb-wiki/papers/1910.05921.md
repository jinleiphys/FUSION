# Multipole expansion of densities in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) has been proved one of the best models to probe the exotic structures in deformed nuclei. In DRHBc, the potentials and densities are expressed in terms of the multipole expansion with Legendre polynomials, the dependence on which has only been touched for light nuclei so far. In this paper, taking a light nucleus $^{20}$Ne and a heavy nucleus $^{242}$U as examples, we investigated the dependence on the multipole expansion of the potentials and densities in DRHBc. It is shown that the total energy converges well with the expansion truncation both in the absence of and presence of the pairing correlation, either in the ground state or at a constrained quadrupole deformation. It is found that to reach a same accuracy of the total energy, even to a same relative accuracy by percent, a larger truncation is required by a heavy nucleus than a light one. The dependence of the total energy on the truncation increases with deformation. By decompositions of the neutron density distribution, it is shown that a higher-order component has a smaller contribution. With the increase of deformation, the high-order components get larger, while at the same deformation, the high-order components of a heavy nucleus play a more important role than that of a light one.

## Key claim

The total energy in DRHBc calculations converges well with the Legendre expansion truncation for both light and heavy nuclei, but to reach the same relative accuracy (e.g., 0.01% of total energy), a heavy nucleus like ²⁴²U requires a larger truncation (λ_max ≥ 8) than a light nucleus like ²⁰Ne (λ_max ≥ 6), especially at larger deformations.

## Method

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) was applied to ²⁰Ne and ²⁴²U using the PC-PK1 density functional. The RHB equation was solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with fixed numerical parameters (box size 20 fm, mesh 0.1 fm, angular momentum cutoff J_max = 23/2 ħ, energy cutoff 300 MeV). The total energy and neutron density distributions were computed as functions of the Legendre expansion truncation λ_max (up to 16), both without pairing and with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force (V₀ = -325 MeV fm³), for ground states and constrained quadrupole deformations β₂ = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ²⁰Ne converges to -155.548 MeV (without pairing) and -155.708 MeV (with pairing); ground-state energy of ²⁴²U converges to -1818.093 MeV (without pairing) and -1819.865 MeV (with pairing).
- For ²⁰Ne without pairing at ground state: deviation with λ_max=4 from λ_max=16 is 0.178 MeV; with λ_max=6 it is 0.018 MeV (about 0.01% of total energy).
- For ²⁴²U without pairing at constrained β₂=0.6: deviation with λ_max=6 from λ_max=16 is 1.986 MeV; with λ_max=8 it is 0.534 MeV (about 0.03%); with λ_max=10 it is 0.135 MeV (about 0.01%).
- For ²⁰Ne without pairing at constrained β₂=0.2: deviation with λ_max=4 from λ_max=16 is 0.016 MeV; at β₂=0.4: 0.081 MeV; at β₂=0.6: 0.238 MeV.
- For ²⁰Ne with pairing: deviations with λ_max=4 and 6 from λ_max=16 are 0.018 MeV and 0.002 MeV (0.012% and 0.002% of total energy).
- For ²⁴²U with pairing: deviations with λ_max=4, 6, and 8 from λ_max=16 are 1.521 MeV, 0.255 MeV, and 0.091 MeV (0.084%, 0.014%, and 0.005% of total energy).
- Ground-state quadrupole deformation of ²⁰Ne converges from β₂=0.55 (λ_max=4) to β₂=0.54 (λ_max≥6); ²⁴²U from β₂=0.32 to β₂=0.31.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc theory developed by Zhou et al. (2010) and Li et al. (2012), which previously used λ_max=4 for light nuclei without systematic convergence checks. It provides the first systematic investigation of the Legendre expansion truncation dependence for both light (²⁰Ne) and heavy (²⁴²U) nuclei across a range of deformations. The results establish practical truncation criteria for the ongoing DRHBc mass table project (Xia et al., 2018) and enable reliable calculations for heavy deformed nuclei, where higher-order multipole components (up to λ=8-10) are necessary for accurate total energies.
