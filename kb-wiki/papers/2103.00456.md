# Recent progresses on QCD phases in a strong magnetic field -- views from Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> In this review, we summarize recent progress on the possible phases of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) in the presence of a strong magnetic field, mainly from the views of the chiral effective Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Four kinds of phase transitions are explored in detail: chiral symmetry breaking and restoration, neutral pseudoscalar superfluidity, charged pion superfluidity and charged rho superconductivity. In particular, we revisit the unsolved problems of inverse magnetic catalysis effect and competition between the chiral density wave and solitonic modulation phases. It is shown that useful results can be obtained by adopting self-consistent schemes.

## Key claim

This review argues that the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, when extended with self-consistent schemes such as a magnetic-field-dependent running coupling, can reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis effect observed in lattice QCD, despite the model's inherent limitations in describing confinement and asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The author reviews results from the NJL model and its Polyakov-loop extended version (PNJL) applied to QCD matter in a strong magnetic field, focusing on four phase transitions: chiral symmetry breaking/restoration, neutral pseudoscalar superfluidity, charged pion superfluidity, and charged rho superconductivity. The theoretical framework uses both the Schwinger proper-time formalism and the Ritus eigenfunction method to compute thermodynamic potentials and order parameters in constant electromagnetic fields, with particular attention to the lowest Landau level approximation and self-consistent regularization schemes.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic field of magnetars: \(7\times 10^{14}\,{\rm G}\) for SGR 1900+14, with a range of \(10^{14}-10^{15}\,{\rm G}\) for known magnetars
- Maximum magnetic field generated in laboratory: \(1.2\times 10^{7}\,{\rm G}\) (Japanese scientists, 2018, short lifetime)
- Primordial magnetic field during electroweak phase transition: estimated at \(10^{23}\,{\rm G}\)
- Magnetic field in peripheral heavy ion collisions: model predictions of \(10^{18}-10^{20}\,{\rm G}\)
- LHC superconducting magnets: \(7.7\times 10^{4}\,{\rm G}\) over a 26.7 km circumference
- Geomagnetic field: \(0.5\,{\rm G}\) (Gauss, 1832)
- Natural magnet field: \(5\times 10^{3}\,{\rm G}\) (ancient Chinese compasses)
- Henry's electromagnet (1831): \(3\times 10^{4}\,{\rm G}\)

## Context

This review extends the earlier reviews by Miransky (2015) and Andersen (2014) on magnetic effects in QCD, and directly addresses the tension between NJL model predictions and lattice QCD results from the Regensburg group (Bali et al., 2011, 2012) regarding the inverse magnetic catalysis effect. The work enables future studies of QCD phase diagrams under combined magnetic field, rotation, and electric field conditions relevant to magnetars, heavy ion collisions, and the early universe, while highlighting unresolved questions such as the competition between chiral density wave and solitonic modulation phases.
