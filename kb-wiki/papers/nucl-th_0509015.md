# Backbending phenomena in light nuclei at A~60 mass region

> Recent studies of the backbending phenomenon in medium light weight nuclei near A~60 expanded greatly our interest about how the single particle orbits are nonlinearly affected by the collective motion. As a consequence we have applied a modi&#133;ed version of the exponential model with the inclusion of paring correlation to describe the energy spectra of the ground state bands and/or the backbending phenomenon in mass region at A~60. A firm conclusion is obtained concerning the successful validity of the proposed modified model in describing the backbending phenomenon in this region. Comparison with different theoretical descriptions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified exponential model with pairing attenuation successfully describes the backbending phenomenon in light nuclei near A~60, supporting the interpretation that pairing force contributes to the band crossing mechanism in this mass region. The hedge is that the model's validity is demonstrated specifically for the ground state bands of even-even nuclei and that its application improves as the mass number A increases.

## Method

The authors applied a modified version of the exponential model (originally developed by Sood and Jain) that includes an adjustable parameter ν and a cutoff spin I_c to account for pairing attenuation. They performed least-square fits of the model parameters to experimental energy levels of the yrast bands for 14 even-even nuclei (Cr, Zn, Ge, Se, Kr, Sr isotopes) in the A~60 mass region. The calculated moments of inertia and squared rotational frequencies were then compared with experimental data in φ-ω² plots to assess the model's ability to reproduce backbending, forward-bending, and down-bending regions.

## Key numbers

- Backbending occurs at spins I = 8-12 ħ for all nuclei studied in the A~60 mass region.
- For ⁶²Zn, the fitted parameters are: 2φ₀/ħ² = 16.1483, Δ₀ = 1.30409, ν = 0.313542, I_c = 18 ħ.
- For ⁶⁸Ge, the fitted parameters are: 2φ₀/ħ² = 25.516, Δ₀ = 1.90454, ν = 0.401219, I_c = 20 ħ.

## Context

This work extends the exponential model of Sood and Jain (which was originally applied to deformed nuclei up to the backbending point) by introducing a variable exponent ν and adjustable cutoff spin I_c to handle the lower-spin backbending (I≈10 ħ) characteristic of the A~60 region. The results support the band crossing interpretation of Hara et al. (based on the projected shell model) and the generator coordinate method, while contradicting the claim of Tanaka et al. (based on the Cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method) that backbending in ⁴⁸Cr is not due to level crossing. The model enables a simple parameterized description of forward and down-bending regions that previous exponential models could not reproduce.
