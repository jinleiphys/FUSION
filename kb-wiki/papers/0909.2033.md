# Exotic quantum holonomy in Hamiltonian systems

> We study the evolution of quantum eigenstates in the presence of level crossing under adiabatic cyclic change of environmental parameters. We find that exotic holonomies, indicated by exchange of the eigenstates after a single cyclic evolution, can arise from non-Abelian gauge potentials among non-degenerate levels. We illustrate our arguments with solvable two and three level models.

## Key claim

Exotic holonomies, exchange of eigenstates after a single adiabatic cyclic parameter variation, can arise in finite Hamiltonian systems without requiring degeneracy throughout the cycle, provided that level crossing occurs at some point during the variation and that the adiabatic theorem is extended to crossing levels.

## Method

The authors construct exactly solvable two-level and three-level parametric Hamiltonians of the form \(H(\theta)=R(\theta)[\cos(\theta/2)Z + v\sin(\theta/2)F]\), where \(R(\theta)\) is anti-periodic with period \(2\pi\) (chosen as \(R(\theta)=\cos(\theta/2)\)), and the matrices \(Z\) and \(F\) are chosen to produce level crossing at \(\theta=\pi\). They analytically compute the eigenvalues, eigenstates, non-Abelian gauge potentials \(A_{nm}(\theta)=\langle\Psi_n|i\partial_\theta\Psi_m\rangle\), and the holonomy matrix \(M\) for a full cycle \(\theta\in[0,2\pi)\). The analytic structure of the gauge potential in the complex \(\theta\) plane is examined to identify exceptional points that produce the holonomy.

## Key numbers

- Two-level model: the holonomy matrix is \(M = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), showing eigenstate exchange with Manini-Pistolesi phase \((-1)^n\).
- Two-level model: \(\int_{0}^{2\pi} d\theta\, f(\theta) = \pi/2\), where \(f(\theta)=\frac{1}{4}\partial\chi(\theta)/\partial\theta\) determines the gauge potential.
- Three-level model: the holonomy matrix is \(M = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), showing cyclic permutation of the three eigenstates with Manini-Pistolesi phase \(1\) for all states.
- Three-level model: the eigenstates are \(6\pi\)-periodic, and the eigenvalues satisfy \(E_n(\theta+2\pi)=E_{n+1}(\theta)\) for \(n=1,2,3\) (modulo three).

## Context

This work extends the concept of exotic holonomies previously found only in time-periodic systems (Tanaka and Miyamoto, 2007; Miyamoto and Tanaka, 2007) and singular systems (Cheon and Hahne, 1998; Tchoumakov and Feagin, 2001) to finite, non-singular Hamiltonian systems. It contrasts with the Wilczek-Zee holonomy (Wilczek and Zee, 1984), which requires degenerate eigenstates throughout the parameter variation, and with the Berry phase (Berry, 1984; Simon, 1989) which involves only geometric phases without eigenstate exchange. The results enable new possibilities for holonomic quantum control (Zanardi and Rasetti, 1999) by showing that eigenstate exchange can occur without maintaining degeneracy, and the analytic structure of gauge potentials (Kato, 2009; Joye and Simon, 2004) is identified as the mathematical origin. The work also relates the exotic holonomy to the off-diagonal holonomy of Manini and Pistolesi (2000) and Fumagalli and Schomerus (2005), showing that the latter can be mapped to the former with a suitable reinterpretation of the parameter period.
