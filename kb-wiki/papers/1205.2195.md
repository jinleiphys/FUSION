# A Bayesian analysis of kaon photoproduction with the Regge-plus-resonance model

> We address the issue of unbiased model selection and propose a methodology based on Bayesian inference to extract physical information from kaon photoproduction $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ data. We use the single-channel Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework for $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ to illustrate the proposed strategy. The Bayesian evidence Z is a quantitative measure for the model's fitness given the world's data. We present a numerical method for performing the multidimensional integrals in the expression for the Bayesian evidence. We use the $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ data with an invariant energy W > 2.6 GeV in order to constrain the background contributions in the RPR framework with Bayesian inference. Next, the resonance information is extracted from the analysis of differential cross sections, single and double polarization observables. This background and resonance content constitutes the basis of a model which is coined RPR-2011. It is shown that RPR-2011 yields a comprehensive account of the kaon photoproduction data and provides reasonable predictions for $p(e,e' K^+)Λ$ observables.

## Key claim

Bayesian inference, quantified by the Bayesian evidence \(\mathcal{Z}\), provides an unbiased method for selecting among competing Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) models of kaon photoproduction \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\), penalizing excessive complexity in accordance with Occam's razor. The authors demonstrate this methodology using only data with invariant energy \(W > 2.6\) GeV to constrain the background, then extract resonance content from lower-energy data.

## Method

The authors applied Bayesian inference to the single-channel Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework for \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\), using a numerical method to compute the multidimensional integrals of the Bayesian evidence. The background amplitude was constrained by fitting Reggeized \(K^+(494)\) and \(K^{\ast+}(892)\) exchange to high-energy data (\(W > 2.6\) GeV), then resonance information was extracted from differential cross sections, single and double polarization observables. The resulting model, RPR-2011, incorporates consistent high-spin interaction Lagrangians for spin-3/2 and spin-5/2 resonances with a multidipole-Gauss form factor to regularize the high-energy behavior.

## Key numbers

- The background model was constrained using \(p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda\) data with invariant energy \(W > 2.6\) GeV.
- The Regge trajectory for \(K^+(494)\) is parametrized as \(\alpha_K(t) = 0.70\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2} (t - m_K^2)\).
- The Regge trajectory for \(K^{\ast+}(892)\) is parametrized as \(\alpha_{K^{\ast}}(t) = 1 + 0.85\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2} (t - m_{K^{\ast}}^2)\).
- The scale factor \(s_0\) in the Regge propagator is fixed at \(1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The RPR-2007 model was optimized against data with \(\cos\theta_K^{\ast} > 0.35\) and \(\sqrt{s} > 3\) GeV.
- The RPR-2011 model evaluates 11 candidate resonances for their relative probability of contributing to the reaction.

## Context

This work extends the RPR framework of Corthals et al. (2006, 2007) by introducing consistent high-spin interactions from Vrancx et al. (2011) and applying Bayesian model selection to determine the optimal resonance content, addressing the long-standing indeterminacy in nucleon resonance extraction from open strangeness channels. The methodology enables unbiased comparison of competing resonance sets, in contrast to prior analyses by David (1995), Ireland (2004), Julia-Diaz (2006), Mart (1999), Shklyar (2005), Usov (2006), and Shyam (2010) which yielded disparate outcomes. The RPR-2011 model provides predictions for \(p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda\) electroproduction observables that can be tested against future data.
