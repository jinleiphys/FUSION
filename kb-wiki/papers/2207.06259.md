# Shell-model description for the properties of the forbidden $β^-$ decay in the region "north-east" of $^{208}$Pb

> In the present work, we report a comprehensive shell-model study of the $\log ft$ values for the forbidden $β^-$ decay transitions in the north-east region of $^{208}$Pb. For this we have considered $^{210-215}$Pb $\rightarrow$ $^{210-215}$Bi and $^{210-215}$Bi $\rightarrow$ $^{210-215}$Po transitions. We have performed shell-model calculation using KHPE interaction in valence shell 82-126 for protons and 126-184 for neutrons without any truncation. We have also calculated half-lives and Q-values for the concerned nuclei. Recently several $\log ft$ values are observed corresponding to $β^-$ decay from (8$^-$) isomeric state of $^{214}$Bi$^m$ at CERN-ISOLDE facility [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 104}, 054301 (2021)], and for the first time we have reported shell-model results for these transitions.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first comprehensive shell-model calculations of log ft values, half-lives, and Q-values for forbidden beta-decay transitions in the neutron-rich region north-east of 208Pb, covering 210-215Pb to 210-215Bi and 210-215Bi to 210-215Po transitions, with the caveat that the theoretical results depend on the choice of quenching factors for the weak coupling constants (two sets are compared, one derived from chi-squared fitting and one from prior literature).

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the KHPE effective interaction in the valence space of 82-126 for protons and 126-184 for neutrons, with 208Pb as an inert core and no truncation. They computed log ft values, average shape factors, and half-lives for forbidden beta-decay transitions, using two sets of effective weak coupling constants: set I (gA/gAfree=0.38, gV/gVfree=1.00) derived from chi-squared fitting of average shape factors, and set II (gA/gAfree=0.38, gV/gVfree=0.51) taken from Zhi et al. (2013). The calculations included a mesonic enhancement factor of epsilon_MEC = 2.01 for the rank-zero nuclear matrix element gamma_5.

## Key numbers

- For 210Pb(0+) to 210Bi(0-1) transition at 46.539(1) keV: experimental log ft = 5.4(1), theoretical log ft = 5.469 for both sets I and II
- For 211Pb(9/2+) to 211Bi(9/2-2) transition at 831.960(12) keV: experimental log ft = 5.7330(18), theoretical log ft = 5.779 (set I) and 5.771 (set II)
- For 213Pb((9/2+)) to 213Bi(9/2-1) transition: experimental log ft = 6.5, theoretical log ft = 6.447 (set I) and 6.459 (set II)
- For 215Pb((9/2+)) to 215Bi((9/2-1)) transition: experimental log ft >= 6.1, theoretical log ft = 6.713 (set I) and 6.741 (set II)
- For 214Pb(0+) to 214Bi(0-1,1-3) transition at 351.9323(21) keV: experimental log ft = 5.07(3), theoretical log ft = 5.080 (set I) and 5.080 (set II) for 0-1 state
- The chi-squared fitting yielded a quenching factor q = 0.38 such that gA_eff = 0.4826 and gV_eff = 1.00 for set I

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of beta-decay in the 208Pb region, building on prior calculations by Warburton (1991) who computed log ft values for 211Pb to 211Bi transitions using truncated model spaces and older experimental data, and by Suzuki et al. (2012) who evaluated beta-decay properties of N=126 r-process isotones. The calculations provide the first theoretical log ft values for the recently measured beta-decay from the (8-) isomeric state of 214Bi at CERN-ISOLDE [Phys. Rev. C 104, 054301 (2021)], and enable spin-parity assignments for states where experiments could not make unique determinations, such as the 592.72(8) keV state in 213Bi which the shell-model identifies as 7/2-2 rather than 5/2-1.
