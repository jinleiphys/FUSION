# New Family of Exotic $Θ$-Baryons

> From the interpretation of the $Θ^+$ baryon resonance as an excitation of the ``skyrmion liquid'' with SU(3) flavor symmetry $\bar{10}$ we deduce a new series of baryons, $Θ_1^{++}$, $Θ_1^{+}$ and $Θ_1^0$, situated at the top of the 27-plet of SU(3) flavor, with hypercharge Y=2, isospin T=1 and spin $J=\frac32$. The mass of $Θ_1$ is estimated 55 MeV/c^2 higher then the mass of $Θ^+$ and its width at 80 MeV. We also discuss the other baryons from the 27-plet.

## Key claim

From the interpretation of the Θ⁺ baryon as an excitation of the "skyrmion liquid" with SU(3) flavor symmetry anti-decuplet, the authors predict a new isotriplet of exotic baryons (Θ₁⁺⁺, Θ₁⁺, Θ₁⁰) belonging to the 27-plet of SU(3) flavor, with the lightest member only 55 MeV/c² heavier than the Θ⁺, but with a much larger hadronic width of approximately 80 MeV rather than the narrow width of the Θ⁺.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) Skyrme model with rigid rotation, employing the same Hamiltonian and parameters as Diakonov et al. (2003) for the Θ⁺ anti-decuplet, to calculate the mass spectrum of the 27-plet baryons with both Jᴾ = ½⁺ and Jᴾ = ³⁄₂⁺. They compute mass splittings using SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and estimate the first moment of inertia I₁ from experimental masses of the octet and decuplet baryons, then calculate mixing between decuplet and 27-plet states for Δ, Σ, and Ξ baryons. The width of the Θ₁ resonance is estimated in the non-relativistic limit using baryon-meson coupling.

## Key numbers

- Mass of Θ₁ (J=3/2) predicted at 1595 MeV/c², which is 55 MeV/c² higher than the theoretical Θ⁺ mass of 1530 MeV/c²
- Width of Θ₁ estimated at approximately 80 MeV
- The Jᴾ = ½⁺ 27-plet states are approximately 500 MeV/c² heavier than the Jᴾ = ³⁄₂⁺ 27-plet states
- Mixing coefficients between decuplet and 27-plet: C_Δ = -0.210, C_Σ = -0.154, C_Ξ = -0.094
- Parameters used: I₂ = (500 MeV)⁻¹, α = -218 MeV, β = -156 MeV, γ = -107 MeV
- Predicted masses for J=3/2 27-plet members: Δ₂₇ at 1750 MeV/c², N₂₇ at 1746 MeV/c², Γ₂₇ at 1904 MeV/c², Σ₂₇ at 1899 MeV/c², Λ₂₇ at 1896 MeV/c², Π₂₇ at 2052 MeV/c², Ξ₂₇ at 2048 MeV/c², Ω₂₇ at 2200 MeV/c²

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model predictions of Diakonov et al. (2003) for the Θ⁺ anti-decuplet to the next higher SU(3) flavor multiplet (the 27-plet), providing testable predictions that could support or reject the soliton picture for exotic baryons. The paper contrasts with the pure multi-quark picture of Capstick et al. (2003) which assigns different quantum numbers to the Θ⁺, and with the work of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) who proposed the Θ⁺ lies in a near-ideally mixed anti-decuplet and octet representation with a different predicted spectrum. The results enable experimental searches for the predicted Θ₁ isotriplet and the exotic Γ₂₇ and Π₂₇ resonances, and suggest that strong decuplet-27-plet mixing may explain observed violations of equidistance in the decuplet spectrum.
