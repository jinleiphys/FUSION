# Phase Conversion After a Chiral Transition: Effects from Inhomogeneities and Finite Size

> We discuss the kinetics of phase conversion, through the nucleation of bubbles and spinodal decomposition, after a chiral transition within an effective field theory approach to low-energy QCD. We study possible effects resulting from the finite size of the expanding system for both the initial and the late-stage growth of domains, as well as those effects due to inhomogeneities in the chiral field which act as a background for the fermionic motion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that finite-size effects and inhomogeneities in the chiral field are important ingredients in the phase conversion process after a chiral transition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, specifically that bubble growth velocity exceeds plasma expansion velocity for supercooling of order 5% or less, making finite-size effects relevant from the start.

## Method

The author uses the linear sigma model coupled to quarks as an effective field theory for low-energy QCD, treating the quark gas as a thermal bath for the chiral field. A derivative expansion is proposed to systematically incorporate corrections from inhomogeneities in the chiral background field into the fermionic determinant. Finite-size effects on bubble nucleation are studied using the time-dependent Landau-Ginzburg equation and the Bjorken picture of plasma expansion, comparing bubble growth velocity to plasma expansion velocity.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV
- Sigma mass: m_sigma = 600 MeV (from lambda^2 = 20)
- The ratio of bubble growth velocity to plasma expansion velocity: v_b/v_L ≈ (20/v_z) * (T_c/T - 1)
- Supercooling threshold for bubble growth exceeding expansion: theta ≈ v_z/20 ≤ 5%
- Critical radius estimate: larger than 1 fm at such supercooling values
- Degeneracy factor for quarks: nu_q = 12 (color-spin-isospin)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phase conversion kinetics in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (Scavenius 1999, 2000, 2001; Paech; Aguiar 2003) by incorporating effects from inhomogeneities in the chiral field through a derivative expansion, and by analyzing finite-size effects on bubble nucleation in expanding quark-gluon plasma. The results enable more realistic modeling of the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, where the system size is finite and the chiral field is not homogeneous.
