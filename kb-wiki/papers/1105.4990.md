# Chiral Inflation of the Pion Radius

> We derive expression for the large b_perp asymptotic of the 3D parton distributions q(x,b_perp) in the pion. The asymptotic depends exclusively on the mass scales F_pi and m_pi. Therefore it provides us with a nice example of a strict non-perturbative result for the partonic structure of Nambu-Goldstone bosons in QCD. Analyzing the x-dependent pion transverse radius we reveal a new phenomenon of "chiral inflation"-- in the parametrically wide region of Bjorken x (m_pi^2/(4 pi F_pi)^2 << x << 1) the pion radius grows exponentially fast with the rapidity eta=ln(1/x). We show that the partons in this interval of Bjorken x contribute to famous logarithmic divergency of the pion radius. In other words, the partonic picture of the classical result of ChPT is provided. The phenomenon of the chiral inflation is at variance with the Gribov diffusion, because of long-range interaction of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the pion, a Nambu-Goldstone boson, the transverse radius grows exponentially with rapidity \(\eta = \ln(1/x)\) in a parametrically wide region of Bjorken \(x\) (\(m_\pi \ll \sqrt{x}\Lambda_\chi \ll \Lambda_\chi\)), a phenomenon called "chiral inflation." This result is conditional on the large \(b_\perp\) asymptotic of the 3D parton distributions being derived exclusively from the mass scales \(F_\pi\) and \(m_\pi\), making it a model-independent non-perturbative result.

## Method

The authors derived the leading large \(b_\perp\) asymptotic of the 3D parton distribution \(q(x, b_\perp)\) for the pion using Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) for generalized parton distributions (GPDs), resumming all orders of chiral logarithms. The asymptotic expression depends on the forward parton distribution \(q(x)\) and constants \(A\) and \(B\) related to the large-order asymptotics of leading chiral logarithms for massless \(\pi\pi\) scattering, which were computed numerically for \(N=3\) and analytically in the large-\(N\) limit. The \(x\)-dependent transverse radius \(b_\perp^2(x)\) was then computed from this asymptotic in the region where the pion mass can be neglected.

## Key numbers

* The chiral scale \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_\pi \approx 6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to a short-distance scale \(1/\Lambda_\chi \approx 0.17\ \text{fm}\).
* For the region of chiral inflation (\(m_\pi \ll \sqrt{x}\Lambda_\chi \ll \Lambda_\chi\)), the \(x\)-dependent transverse radius is \(b_\perp^2(x) = \frac{2}{3\Lambda_\chi^2} \frac{1}{x}\), leading to exponential growth with rapidity.
* The total transverse pion radius from the inflation region is \(b_\perp^2 = \frac{2}{3 \Lambda_\chi^2} \ln\left(\frac{\Lambda_\chi^2}{m_\pi^2}\right)\), matching the classical ChPT result.
* The constants for the asymptotic formula are \(A = 1.149\) and \(B = 0.482\) for the case \(N=3\) (number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons), while in the large-\(N\) limit \(A^{\text{Large }N} = N/2\) and \(B^{\text{Large }N} = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the application of Chiral Perturbation Theory to parton distributions, building on prior developments by Kivel et al. (2008-2010) and Polyakov et al. (2010) for resumming chiral logarithms in GPDs. It provides a partonic interpretation of the classical ChPT result for the logarithmic divergence of the pion radius in the chiral limit, originally derived by the authors cited as "piradius." The phenomenon of chiral inflation contrasts with the Gribov diffusion model, which assumes short-range interactions and predicts a linear growth of the squared transverse radius with rapidity; the exponential growth arises from the long-range interactions of Nambu-Goldstone bosons. This work enables further study of how chiral and partonic degrees of freedom intertwine, and suggests that for massless pions, the exponential inflation could lead to anomalously large interactions at high energies.
