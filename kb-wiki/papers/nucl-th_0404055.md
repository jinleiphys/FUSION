# Isotopic dependence of the pygmy dipole resonance

> The isotopic dependence of the excitation energies of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) is analyzed in the framework of the self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA). The DD-ME1 density-dependent meson-exchange interaction is used in the effective mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S. Model calculations reproduce available experimental data on charge radii, the neutron skin, neutron separation energies, and excitation energies of isovector giant dipole resonances in Ni, Sn and Pb nuclei. In all three isotopic chains the one-neutron separation energies decrease with mass number much faster than the excitation energies of the PDR. As a result, already at moderate proton-neutron asymmetry the PDR peak energy is calculated above the neutron emission threshold. This result has important implications for the observation of the PDR in (gamma,gamma') experiments.

# Isotopic dependence of the pygmy dipole resonance

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) peak energy is calculated above the neutron emission threshold already at moderate proton-neutron asymmetry in Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains, which would hinder observation of the PDR in (γ,γ′) experiments on very neutron-rich nuclei. This result depends on the use of the DD-ME1 density-dependent meson-exchange interaction, which reproduces neutron skin data better than standard nonlinear interactions like NL1 and NL3.

## Method

The authors employed the self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) in the canonical single-nucleon basis. The DD-ME1 density-dependent meson-exchange interaction was used in the particle-hole channel, and pairing correlations were described by the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the particle-particle channel. The RQRPA configuration space included two-quasiparticle states from bound levels, states with one nucleon in the continuum, and states with both nucleons in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- In ¹²⁴Sn, the PDR peak at approximately 8.5 MeV exhausts 3.3% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule.
- For Sn isotopes, the PDR excitation energy crosses above the one-neutron separation energy at A ≥ 124.
- For Ni isotopes, the crossing between PDR excitation energies and one-neutron separation energies is calculated at A = 64.
- For Pb isotopes, the crossing point is calculated at A = 208.
- The isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in Sn isotopes peaks at approximately 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic RPA analyses of the PDR (Vretenar et al., 2001) and the RQRPA in the canonical basis (Paar et al., 2003) to a systematic study of isotopic chains. The results contradict the expectation that PDR strength increases monotonically with neutron number within a major shell, and they have implications for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations (Goriely et al., 2002, 2004) that assume PDR strength below the neutron separation energy. The study enables future (γ,γ′) experiments on Pb nuclei to test the predicted crossing at A=208, and suggests that beyond-mean-field correlation effects may be needed for lighter systems like Ni isotopes.
