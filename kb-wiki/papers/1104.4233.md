# Confining but chirally symmetric quarkyonic matter

> Here we overview a possible mechanism for confining but chirally symmetric matter at low temperatures and large densities.

## Key claim
A confining but chirally symmetric phase of dense cold matter (quarkyonic matter) is possible in real QCD with Nc=3, provided the matter is in a liquid phase with manifest translational and rotational invariance, as demonstrated within a model that uses a linear instantaneous confining potential.

## Method
The author constructs a 3+1 dimensional model with a linear instantaneous Coulomb-type interquark potential (σr) and solves the gap equation for quark self-energy in a dense medium at T=0, using a mean-field approximation with a simple valence quark distribution function. The model is manifestly confining, chirally symmetric, and exhibits dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in vacuum. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is then solved to obtain color-singlet meson spectra both below and above the chiral restoration phase transition.

## Context
This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter proposed by Pisarski (no year given) from the large Nc limit to the realistic Nc=3 case. It contradicts the earlier Casher argument (Casher, no year given) that confinement necessarily implies chiral symmetry breaking, and builds on prior model calculations by Glozman and collaborators (GW1, GW2, G1) that demonstrated a confining chirally symmetric liquid phase. The work enables further theoretical investigation of whether such a phase exists in real QCD and what its observable signatures might be in dense nuclear matter.
