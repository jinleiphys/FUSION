# Soft Electromagnetic Radiations From Equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We evaluate the bremsstrahlung production of low mass dileptons and soft photons from equilibrating and transversely expanding quark gluon plasma which may be created in the wake of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We use initial conditions obtained from the self screened parton cascade model. We consider a boost invariant longitudinal and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of the parton plasma and find that for low mass dileptons ($M \leq 0.3$ GeV) and soft photons ($p_{T} \leq 0.5$ GeV), the bremsstrahlung contribution is rather large compared to annihilation process at both RHIC and LHC energies. We also find an increase by a factor of 15-20 in the low mass dileptons and soft photons yield as one goes from RHIC to LHC energies.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung contribution to low-mass dileptons (M ≤ 0.3 GeV) and soft photons (pT ≤ 0.5 GeV) from a chemically non-equilibrated, transversely expanding quark-gluon plasma is significantly larger than the annihilation contribution at both RHIC and LHC energies, but this dominance holds only under the specific initial conditions from the self-screened parton cascade (SSPC) model where the plasma is initially gluon-rich and far from chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors model a boost-invariant longitudinally expanding and cylindrically symmetric transversely expanding quark-gluon plasma using ideal hydrodynamics coupled to master equations for chemical equilibration via gg ↔ ggg and gg ↔ qq̅ reactions. Initial conditions at τi = 0.25 fm/c are taken from the SSPC model, with parton fugacities λi describing undersaturation. The bremsstrahlung production of soft photons and low-mass dileptons is computed using the soft photon approximation, with a phase-space correction factor, and integrated over the space-time evolution of the plasma.

## Key numbers

- For low-mass dileptons (M ≤ 0.3 GeV), the bremsstrahlung contribution dominates over the annihilation contribution at all transverse masses at both RHIC and LHC energies.
- The low-mass dilepton yield increases by a factor of 20 when going from RHIC to LHC energies.
- For soft photons, the bremsstrahlung contribution dominates over Compton and annihilation processes up to pT ≤ 0.5 GeV at RHIC energy and up to pT ≤ 0.6 GeV at LHC energy.
- The soft-photon yield increases by a factor of almost 10 from RHIC to LHC energies.
- The initial proper time for kinetic equilibrium is taken as τi ≈ 0.2–0.3 fm/c.
- The evolution is terminated at a critical energy density εf = 1.45 GeV/fm³.
- The study restricts to photons and dileptons with energies larger than 100 MeV to avoid substantial Landau-Pomeranchuk suppression.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bremsstrahlung from a fully equilibrated plasma (Haglin, 1993; Pal, 1996; Eggers, 1996) by incorporating chemical non-equilibrium and transverse expansion using initial conditions from the SSPC model (Geiger, 1995). It also relates to the two-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations of Aurenche et al. (1998), which found bremsstrahlung contributions of the same order as one-loop results. The results suggest that the soft electromagnetic window (low mass and low pT) can provide information about the later stages of plasma evolution and transverse flow, and may help explain the excess dielectron yield observed by the CERES experiment at CERN SPS.
