# Implications of the mass $M=2.17^{+0.11}_{-0.10}$M$_\odot$ of PSR~J0740+6620 on the Equation of State of Super-Dense Neutron-Rich Nuclear Matter

> We study implications of the very recently reported mass $M=2.17^{+0.11}_{-0.10}$M$_\odot$ of PSR~J0740+6620 on the Equation of State (EOS) of super-dense neutron-rich nuclear matter with respect to existing constraints on the EOS based on the mass $M=2.01\pm 0.04$M$_\odot$ of PSR~J0348+0432, the maximum tidal deformability of GW170817 and earlier results of various terrestrial nuclear laboratory experiments. The lower limit of the skewness $J_0$ measuring the stiffness of super-dense isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is raised raised from about -220 MeV to -150 MeV, reducing significantly its current uncertainty range. The lower bound of the high-density symmetry energy also increases appreciably leading to a rise of the minimum proton fraction in neutron stars at $β$-equilibrium from about 0 to 5\% around three times the saturation density of nuclear matter. The difficulties for some of the most widely used and previously well tested model EOSs to predict simultaneously both a maximum mass higher than 2.17 M$_\odot$ and a pressure consistent with that extracted from GW170817 present some interesting new challenges for nuclear theories.

## Key claim
The new mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (2.17 M☉) raises the lower limit of the skewness parameter J0 of symmetric nuclear matter from about -220 MeV to -150 MeV, but the authors caution that the error bars are still large and earlier Shapiro delay mass measurements have been revised downward, so the results depend on the reported mass staying approximately unchanged.

## Method
The authors used a minimum model of neutron stars consisting of npeμ matter at β-equilibrium, parameterizing the nucleon specific energy via Taylor expansions for symmetric nuclear matter (E0) and the symmetry energy (Esym) up to third order in density. They inverted the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation in the three-dimensional high-density EOS parameter space (J0, Ksym, Jsym) to find constant surfaces for specified maximum masses (2.01 M☉ and 2.17 M☉), the maximum tidal deformability Λ1.4=580 from GW170817, and the causality condition, then compared these surfaces to determine allowed parameter ranges and pressure bands.

## Key numbers
- Mass of PSR J0740+6620: M = 2.17^{+0.11}_{-0.10} M☉ (68.3% credibility interval)
- Mass of PSR J0348+0432: M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M☉
- Lower limit of skewness J0 raised from about -220 MeV to -150 MeV (approximately 47% increase) when maximum mass rises from 2.01 M☉ to 2.17 M☉
- Minimum proton fraction in neutron stars at β-equilibrium rises from about 0% to 5% around three times the saturation density of nuclear matter when maximum mass increases from 2.01 M☉ to 2.17 M☉
- Maximum tidal deformability of canonical neutron stars: Λ1.4 = 580 (90% confidence level) from GW170817
- Prior range of J0 from terrestrial experiments: -800 ≤ J0 ≤ 400 MeV
- Absolutely maximum mass of neutron stars at the causal limit: about 2.40 M☉ (from this work) and 2.35 M☉ (Baym et al. 2019)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian inference framework (Zhang 2018, 2019) that used the mass of PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) and the tidal deformability from GW170817 (LIGO/VIRGO 2018) to constrain the high-density EOS. The new mass of PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al. 2019) provides a more stringent test, raising the lower bound on J0 and the symmetry energy. The study finds that none of 11 widely used model EOSs (including AP3, AP4, SLy, MPA1, and others) simultaneously predicts a maximum mass above 2.17 M☉ and a pressure consistent with GW170817 constraints, creating a new challenge for nuclear many-body theories. The results enable tighter constraints on the high-density symmetry energy and proton fraction, though the direct URCA cooling threshold (11.1-14.8% proton fraction) remains unresolved.
