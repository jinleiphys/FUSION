# Regge phenomenology of pion photoproduction off the nucleon at forward angles

> We present a Regge model for pion photoproduction which is basically free of parameters within the framework of the s-channel helicity amplitude. We use coupling constants of all exchanged mesons determined from empirical decay widths or from the SU(3) relations together with consistency check with existing estimates that are widely accepted in other reaction processes. Cross sections and spin polarization asymmetries at various photon energies are analyzed and results are obtained in better agreement with experimental data without referring to any fitting procedure.

## Key claim

The authors present a Regge model for pion photoproduction that is "basically free of parameters", meaning it uses coupling constants determined from empirical decay widths or SU(3) relations rather than fitting to photoproduction data, and achieves better agreement with experimental data than previous models, but only within the Regge regime (s ≫ 4M² and −t < 2 GeV²) and without including Regge cut corrections.

## Method

The authors construct s-channel helicity amplitudes for charged and neutral pion photoproduction by reggeizing t-channel meson exchanges using the conventional Born approximation. For charged pion production, they include π, ρ, a₁(1260), b₁(1235), and a₂(1320) exchanges; for neutral pion production, they include ω, ρ⁰, b₁, and h₁(1170) exchanges. The coupling constants are determined from empirical decay widths, vector meson dominance (VMD), axial vector meson dominance (AVMD), and SU(3) relations, with no free parameters adjusted to fit the photoproduction data.

## Key numbers

- The ρNN vector coupling constant is taken as gᵛ_ρNN = 2.6, with tensor-to-vector ratio κ_ρ = 6.2
- The ωNN coupling constant is taken as g_ωNN = 15.6 with κ_ω ≈ 0
- The γπ±ρ coupling constant is g_γπ±ρ = 0.223 from Γ_ρ→γπ± = 0.068 GeV; g_γπ⁰ρ = 0.255 from Γ_ρ→γπ⁰ = 0.090 GeV
- The γπ⁰ω coupling constant is g_γπ⁰ω = 0.723 from Γ_ω→γπ⁰ = 0.757 GeV
- The γπ±b₁ coupling constant is g_γπ±b₁ = 0.196 from Γ_b₁→π±γ = 0.227 ± 0.057 MeV
- The γπ⁰b₁ coupling constant is g_γπ⁰b₁ = 0.189 from VMD using g_b₁ωπ⁰ = 9.77
- The γπ⁰h₁(1170) coupling constant is g_γπ⁰h₁ = 0.405 from Γ_h₁→π⁰γ = 837 ± 134 keV
- The γπ±a₁ coupling constant is g_γπ±a₁ = 0.316 from Γ_a₁→γπ = 0.64 ± 0.246 MeV
- The Regge trajectories used: α_π(t) = 0.7(t − m²_π), α_ρ(t) = 0.8t + 0.55, α_ω(t) = 0.9t + 0.44, α_a₂(t) = 0.8(t − m²_a₂) + 2

## Context

This work extends the s-channel helicity amplitude Regge approach of Guidal, Laget, and Vanderhaeghen (GLV) (1997) and Levy, Majerotto, and Read (LMR) (1973), which used only π+ρ exchanges for charged pions and ω+ρ+b₁ for neutral pions, by including for the first time the a₂(1320) tensor meson and h₁(1170) axial meson Regge poles. It contrasts with the t-channel helicity amplitude approach of Kellett (1972) and Sibirtsev et al. (2006), which required fitting procedures, by using coupling constants fixed from independent decay data. The model enables extension of Regge phenomenology to higher momentum transfer (−t < 2 GeV²) and provides a basis for studying resonance contributions in photo- and electroproduction at facilities like JLab and SPring-8.
