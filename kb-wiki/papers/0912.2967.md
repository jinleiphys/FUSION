# NN Interaction JISP16: Current Status and Prospect

> We discuss realistic nonlocal NN interactions of a new type - J-matrix Inverse Scattering Potential (JISP). In an ab exitu approach, these interactions are fitted to not only two-nucleon data (NN scattering data and deuteron properties) but also to the properties of light nuclei without referring to three-nucleon forces. We discuss recent progress with the ab initio No-core Shell Model (NCSM) approach and respective progress in developing ab exitu JISP-type NN-interactions together with plans of their forthcoming improvements.

## Key claim

The JISP16 NN interaction, fitted to two-nucleon data and properties of light nuclei up to A=16 without explicit three-nucleon forces, reproduces binding energies and spectra reasonably well in small model spaces, but the Lee-Suzuki-Okamoto (LSO) renormalization procedure used during fitting gives misleading convergence patterns; in larger basis spaces the variational upper bounds from the bare interaction reveal that JISP16 overbinds nuclei with mass A ≥ 14 and N ∼ Z.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio No-core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations with the bare JISP16 interaction in successively larger oscillator basis spaces (up to Nmax = 8 for 14F and 14B, and up to Nmax = 16 for 6Li), then extrapolated total energies to the infinite basis space limit using two methods (extrapolation A based on four successive basis spaces at five ℏΩ values, and extrapolation B based on three successive basis spaces at fixed ℏΩ). Excitation energies were obtained as differences between extrapolated total energies of excited states and the ground state. The results were compared with earlier LSO(2) renormalized effective interaction calculations used in the original JISP16 fit.

## Key numbers

- JISP16 gives χ²/datum = 1.03 for the 1992 np data base (2514 data) and 1.05 for the 1999 np data base (3058 data)
- Deuteron properties with JISP16: binding energy −2.224575 MeV, d-state probability 4.1360%, rms radius 1.9647 fm, quadrupole moment 0.2915 fm², asymptotic normalization constant As = 0.8629 fm⁻¹/², η = 0.0252
- NCFC extrapolation for ¹⁴F ground state binding energy: 72 ± 4 MeV (combining extrapolation A at −70.9 ± 3.6 MeV and extrapolation B at −73.1 ± 3.7 MeV)
- NCFC prediction for ¹⁴B ground state: 86 ± 4 MeV (extrapolation A: −84.4 ± 3.2 MeV, extrapolation B: −86.6 ± 3.8 MeV), compared to experimental 85.423 MeV
- NCFC prediction for ¹³O ground state: 77 ± 3 MeV (extrapolation A: −75.7 ± 2.2 MeV, extrapolation B: −77.6 ± 3.0 MeV), compared to experimental 75.556 MeV
- For ⁶Li ground state with JISP16 in Nmax = 10–16 spaces: extrapolation A gives −31.47 ± 0.09 MeV, extrapolation B gives −31.48 ± 0.03 MeV, compared to experimental −31.994 MeV
- ¹⁶O ground state with bare JISP16 at Nmax = 8: variational upper bound −134.494 MeV; LSO(2) renormalized result at Nmax = 6 gave −133.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix Inverse Scattering Potential (JISP) approach developed by Shirokov and collaborators (2004–2007), which constructs nonlocal NN interactions fitted to both NN scattering data and light-nucleus properties without explicit three-nucleon forces. The paper contrasts JISP with meson-exchange potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, Argonne) that require additional NNN forces (Urbana, Illinois, Tucson-Melbourne), and with INOY (Inside Nonlocal Outside Yukawa) interactions by Doleschall and Plessas. The new No-Core Full Configuration (NCFC) extrapolation method, introduced by the authors in 2007, enables basis-space-independent predictions and reveals that the earlier LSO(2) renormalized effective interaction approach used in fitting JISP16 gave non-monotonic convergence and overestimated binding for heavier nuclei. The results enable future comparison with experimental data for the exotic proton-excess nucleus ¹⁴F, expected soon from Texas A&M University, and motivate a refined version called JISP16₂₀₁₀.
