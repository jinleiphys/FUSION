# Probing the nonstrange quark star equation of state with compact stars and gravitational waves

> A recent study shows that incorporating a new term into the thermodynamic potential density, as required by the thermodynamic consistency criterion, can effectively resolve the thermodynamic inconsistency problems of the conventional perturbative QCD model. This additional term plays a crucial role in resolving inconsistencies at relatively low densities and becomes negligible at extremely high densities. Within this revised perturbative QCD model, we find that if we require only that the energy per baryon of up-down ($ud$) quark matter exceeds 930 MeV so as not to contradict the standard nuclear physics, the maximum mass of an $ud$ quark star allowed by the revised perturbative QCD model can reach up to 2.17 $M_{\odot}$. From this perspective, the observed 2.14 $M_{\odot}$ pulsar PSR J0740+6620 may be an $ud$ quark star. However, if we further impose the constraint that the tidal deformability of a 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ $ud$ quark star must be consistent with the GW170817 event, the maximum mass allowed by the revised perturbative QCD model would decrease to no more than 2.08 $M_{\odot}$. Consequently, our results suggest that the compact object with a mass of 2.50-2.67 $M_{\odot}$, as observed in the GW190814 event, cannot be an $ud$ quark star, according to the revised perturbative QCD model.

## Key claim

The revised perturbative QCD model, which incorporates a thermodynamic consistency term, can produce up-down quark stars with maximum masses up to 2.17 solar masses when only requiring the energy per baryon to exceed 930 MeV; however, when additionally imposing the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, the maximum mass decreases to no more than 2.08 solar masses, ruling out the GW190814 secondary component (2.50-2.67 solar masses) as an up-down quark star.

## Method

The authors adopt a revised perturbative QCD model that adds a thermodynamic consistency term to the thermodynamic potential density, resolving inconsistencies at low densities while becoming negligible at high densities. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with beta-equilibrated up-down quark matter under charge neutrality, using the resulting equation of state to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for quark stars. The model parameter space (dimensionless parameter C and vacuum pressure B0) is constrained by requiring the energy per baryon to exceed 930 MeV and by comparing predicted tidal deformabilities with the GW170817 constraint.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of an up-down quark star allowed by the revised perturbative QCD model with only the 930 MeV energy-per-baryon constraint: 2.17 solar masses
- Maximum mass when additionally imposing the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint: no more than 2.08 solar masses
- Tidal deformability range for a 1.4 solar mass up-down quark star consistent with the model: 70 to 580 (dimensionless)
- Observed tidal deformability from GW170817: 190 with lower bound -120 and upper bound +390
- Mass of PSR J0740+6620: 2.14 solar masses (with uncertainty +0.10, -0.09)
- Mass range of the GW190814 secondary component: 2.50-2.67 solar masses
- QCD scale parameter used: 147 MeV
- Polytropic index approaches a lower limit of 1.2 at baryon density 0.8 fm^-3 for all cases

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic consistency correction to perturbative QCD developed by Xu (2014) and applies it to the specific case of nonstrange (up-down) quark stars. The results contradict conclusions from a confining quark matter model by Cao (2020) that suggested the GW190814 object could be a quark star. The findings enable future Bayesian analyses of the model parameter space, as noted by the authors, and provide a framework for interpreting multi-messenger observations including NICER results for PSR J0030+0451 from Miller (2019) and the unusually compact star HESS J1731-347 from Doroshenko (2022).
