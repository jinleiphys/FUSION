# Neutrino Interactions with Matter and the MiniBooNE anomaly

> The excess of electron-like events measured by MiniBooNE challenges our understanding of neutrinos and their interactions. We review the status of this open problem and ongoing efforts to resolve it. After introducing the experiment and its results, we consider the main experimental backgrounds and the related physics of neutrino interactions with matter such as quasielastic-like scattering and weak pion production on nucleons and nuclei. Special attention is paid to single photon emission in neutral current interactions and, in particular, its coherent channel. The difficulties to reconcile the MiniBooNE anomaly with global oscillation analysis is then highlighted. We finally outline some of the proposed solutions of the puzzle involving unconventional neutrino-interaction mechanisms.

# Neutrino Interactions with Matter and the MiniBooNE anomaly

## Key claim
The MiniBooNE electron-like event excess, which challenges the standard three-flavor neutrino oscillation paradigm, critically depends on the proper determination of electron-like backgrounds; while the excess itself is robust, its interpretation in terms of neutrino oscillations or alternative explanations is complicated by nuclear effects that bias the reconstructed neutrino energy, and by unresolved tensions in the modeling of weak pion production on nuclei.

## Method
The authors review the MiniBooNE experiment's search for electron-like charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) events in neutrino and antineutrino modes, examining the main experimental backgrounds: intrinsic electron-neutrino contamination, neutral-current pion production (NCpi0), neutral-current single photon emission (NC1gamma), and external "dirt" events. They analyze theoretical models for neutrino-nucleus interactions, including multinucleon effects, Delta resonance excitation and propagation in nuclear matter, and coherent versus incoherent photon production mechanisms, comparing model predictions to MiniBooNE data and to other experiments such as MINERvA and T2K.

## Key numbers
- LSND excess: 87.9 ± 22.4 ± 6.0 electron antineutrino events over expected background
- MiniBooNE baseline: 541 m; average L/E_nu ~ 1 m/MeV
- MiniBooNE data collected: 6.46 × 10^20 protons on target (POT) in neutrino mode and 11.27 × 10^20 POT in antineutrino mode (2002-2012); additional neutrino mode data to 18.75 × 10^20 POT (2012-2019)
- Excess concentrated at 200 < E_nu^QE < 475 MeV; lower limit set by visible energy threshold of 140 MeV
- MiniBooNE NC1gamma/NCpi0 ratio: 0.0091 ± 0.0013 (14.0% uncertainty in neutrino mode, 15.6% in antineutrino mode)
- Delta(1232) radiative branching fraction: 0.60% on carbon-12, 0.68% on hydrogen
- Ratio R(T_pi) of CCpi+- cross sections between MINERvA and MiniBooNE: approximately 2 (from NuWro generator study)
- Best-fit Delta m^2 from LSND: 1.2 eV^2
- NC1gamma cross section reduction from nuclear medium effects: approximately 30% when including Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and Delta resonance broadening
- Coherent NC1gamma cross sections: approximately a factor 10-15 smaller than incoherent ones
- C5^A(0) NDelta axial coupling: 1.00 ± 0.11 from original fit; re-analysis gave 1.18 ± 0.07
- Two-nucleon Delta-mediated mechanism contribution: factor of about 9 smaller than single nucleon mechanisms at E_nu = 500 MeV

## Context
This review extends the discussion of the MiniBooNE anomaly first reported by Aguilar-Arevalo (2013) and confirmed with additional data by MiniBooNE (2020), building on the earlier LSND anomaly from Aguilar (2001). The authors examine theoretical efforts to model neutrino-nucleus interactions, including the work of Martini (2011, 2012), Nieves (2011, 2012), and Megias (2014) on multinucleon effects, and the GiBUU transport model studies by Lalakulich (2012) and Mosel (2015, 2017) for pion production. The NC1gamma calculations reviewed include those by Hill (2009), Serot (2012), Wang (2013, 2014), and Zhang (2012). The review enables future work to resolve the tension between MiniBooNE and MINERvA pion production data, and to test proposed beyond-Standard-Model explanations at the Short Baseline Neutrino Detector (SBND) or MINERvA.
