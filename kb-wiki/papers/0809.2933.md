# J/Psi production in heavy ion collisions and gluon saturation

> We calculate the inclusive J/Psi production in heavy ion collisions including the effects of gluon saturation in the wave functions of the colliding nuclei. We argue that the dominant production mechanism in proton--nucleus and nucleus--nucleus collisions for heavy nuclei is different from the one in hadron-hadron interactions. We find that the rapidity distribution of primary J/Psi production is more peaked around midrapidity than the analogous distribution in elementary pp collisions. We discuss the consequences of this fact on the experimentally observed J/Psi suppression in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The dominant J/Psi production mechanism in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions is the three-gluon fusion process (G+G+G -> J/Psi), which is parametrically suppressed in hadron-hadron collisions but becomes enhanced in nuclear collisions by a factor of A^(1/3) due to gluon saturation effects, making it the leading contribution rather than the usual two-gluon fusion mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive J/Psi production in heavy ion collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and the KLN approach, which describes nuclear wave functions with saturated gluon densities. They employ k_T-factorization and dipole model techniques to compute the cross section for c-cbar pair production with fixed relative momentum, then generalize from hadron-hadron to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions by summing over multiple inelastic interactions of dipoles with nucleons in the target nucleus, using the quasi-classical approximation where alpha_s^2 A^(1/3) ~ 1.

## Key numbers

- The two-gluon fusion process (G+G -> J/Psi + gluon) is of order O(alpha_s^5), while the three-gluon fusion (G+G+G -> J/Psi) is O(alpha_s^6) in hadron-hadron collisions
- In nuclear collisions, the three-gluon fusion is enhanced by A^(1/3) relative to the two-gluon process, making it enhanced by 1/alpha_s compared to the two-gluon mechanism
- The saturation scale Q_s is proportional to A^(1/3), which compensates for the smallness of the charm quark radius r where (2m_c)^(-1) < r < (2m_c alpha_s)^(-1)
- The dipole-nucleus interaction cross section is parameterized as sigma(x, r^2) * rho * 2R_A = (1/4) * r^2 * Q^2_(s,A)(x)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev and Tuchin (2006) on J/Psi production off nuclear targets within the Color Glass Condensate framework, and builds on the KLN approach developed by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2004-2007) for particle production in heavy ion collisions. The paper aims to separate cold nuclear matter effects from Quark-Gluon Plasma signals in J/Psi suppression, which was originally proposed as a QGP signature by Matsui and Satz (1986). The results enable a more accurate interpretation of the experimentally observed J/Psi suppression in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies by accounting for the modified rapidity distribution of primary J/Psi production due to gluon saturation.
