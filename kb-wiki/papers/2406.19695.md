# Gamma decay of the $^{154}$Sm Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance: Smekal-Raman Scattering as a Novel Probe of Nuclear Ground-State Deformation

> Gamma decays of the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) of the deformed nucleus $^{154}$Sm from $2^+_1$-Smekal-Raman and elastic scattering were measured using linearly polarized, quasimonochromatic photon beams. The two scattering processes were disentangled through their distinct angular distributions. Their branching ratio and cross sections were determined at six excitation energies covering the $^{154}$Sm GDR. Both agree with the predictions of the geometrical model for the GDR and establish $γ$ decay as an observable sensitive to the structure of the resonance. Consequently, the data place strong constraints on the nuclear shape, including the degree of triaxiality. The derived $^{154}$Sm shape parameters $β=0.2926(26)$ and $γ=5.0(14)$ agree well with other measurements and recent Monte Carlo Shell-Model calculations.

## Key claim

The gamma-decay branching ratio between Smekal-Raman scattering to the first excited 2+ state and elastic scattering of the isovector giant dipole resonance in 154Sm agrees with the geometrical model's predictions, but only when the gamma-decay data are explicitly included in the fit; fitting only the photoabsorption cross section data does not reproduce the observed branching ratios, demonstrating that gamma decay provides sensitivity to details of the resonance structure that photoabsorption alone cannot resolve.

## Method

Nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments were performed at the High-Intensity Gamma-ray Source (HIGS) using linearly polarized, quasimonochromatic photon beams at six mean beam energies between 11.37 and 17.79 MeV to photoexcite the isovector giant dipole resonance of 154Sm. The elastic scattering and 2+1-Smekal-Raman scattering components were disentangled through their distinct angular distributions at 90 degrees polar angle, using four LaBr3 detectors and four high-purity germanium clover detectors. A global Bayesian inference Markov chain Monte Carlo fit of the detector-response-convolved spectra simultaneously to all eight detectors extracted the intensities of both scattering processes and their branching ratios.

* Branching ratio sigma(2+1-SRS)/sigma(ES) = 1.017(15) at beam energy 12.59(1) MeV
* Branching ratio sigma(2+1-SRS)/sigma(ES) = 0.878(17) at beam energy 14.27(1) MeV
* Branching ratio sigma(2+1-SRS)/sigma(ES) = 0.436(34) at beam energy 15.35(1) MeV

* Derived deformation parameters from simultaneous fit: beta = 0.2925(25) and gamma = 5.0(1.5) degrees

## Context

This work provides the first systematic, energy-resolved study of the gamma-decay behavior of the isovector giant dipole resonance in a deformed nucleus, extending earlier measurements that covered only low excitation energies (Hass, 1971; Bar-Noy and Moreh, 1974; Jackson, 1975) or lacked sufficient resolution and statistics (Fuller and Hayward, 1962; Nathan and Moreh, 1980; Hoblit and Nathan, 1991). The results confirm the geometrical model of the giant dipole resonance (Danos, 1958; Okamoto, 1958) for gamma decay and establish Smekal-Raman scattering as a novel probe of nuclear ground-state deformation, enabling constraints on triaxiality that are inaccessible from photoabsorption data alone. The derived shape parameters agree with recent Monte Carlo Shell-Model calculations (Otsuka, in preparation; Otsuka, 2019) predicting beta approximately 0.28 and gamma approximately 3.7 degrees.
