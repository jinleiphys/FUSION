# Deformed quantum mechanics and q-Hermitian operators

> Starting on the basis of the non-commutative q-differential calculus, we introduce a generalized q-deformed Schrödinger equation. It can be viewed as the quantum stochastic counterpart of a generalized classical kinetic equation, which reproduces at the equilibrium the well-known q-deformed exponential stationary distribution. In this framework, q-deformed adjoint of an operator and q-hermitian operator properties occur in a natural way in order to satisfy the basic quantum mechanics assumptions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a $q$-deformed Schrödinger equation derived from a $q$-deformed Fokker-Planck equation via stochastic quantization, but the resulting Hamiltonian is non-Hermitian under the standard scalar product; the author shows that a consistent quantum mechanics can be recovered by defining a $q$-deformed scalar product and $q$-Hermitian operators, which satisfy the basic assumptions of quantum mechanics only under these generalized definitions.

## Method

The author starts from the non-commutative $q$-differential calculus based on the Jackson derivative and the $q$-exponential function, then writes a $q$-deformed Fokker-Planck equation whose stationary solution is a $q$-exponential distribution. By applying a stochastic quantization procedure (replacing time by $t \to t/(-i\hbar)$ and the diffusion coefficient by $J_2^{(q)} \to \hbar^2/(2m)$), the Fokker-Planck equation is transformed into a $q$-deformed Schrödinger equation with Hamiltonian $H_q = -(\hbar^2/2m) {\cal D}_x^{(q)2} + V_q(x)$. The author then defines a $q$-scalar product $\langle \varphi,\psi\rangle_q = \int \varphi^\star_{q^{-1}}(x)\,\psi_q(x)\, d_qx$ and a $q$-adjoint of an operator, requiring that observables be $q$-Hermitian operators to ensure real expectation values and probability conservation.

## Key numbers

- The $q$-deformed plane wave solution for a free particle is $\varphi_q^f(x)=N\, {\rm E}_q(ikx)$ with $k=\sqrt{2mE/\hbar^2}$.
- The $q$-exponential is defined as ${\rm E}_q(x)= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} x^k/[k]_q!$, where $[n]_q = (q^n-1)/(q-1)$.
- The Jackson derivative acts on a monomial as $\dj\,x^n=[n]_q\,x^{n-1}$ and on $x^{-n}$ as $\dj\,x^{-n}= -[n]_q/(q^n)\, x^{-(n+1)}$.
- The $q$-deformed exponential satisfies $\dj {\rm E}_q(a x)= a\,{\rm E}_q(a x)$ and ${\rm E}_q(x)\,{\rm E}_{q^{-1}}(-x)=1$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $q$-deformed Heisenberg algebras and $q$-deformed Schrödinger equations proposed by Zhang and Micu, as well as earlier work by Lavagno on $q$-deformed thermostatistics and $q$-deformed Poisson brackets. It also connects to the broader literature on non-Hermitian and pseudo-Hermitian quantum mechanics (Mostafazadeh, Bender, Jones). The paper provides a formal framework for a consistent $q$-deformed quantum mechanics based on $q$-calculus, enabling future investigations into $q$-deformed quantum dynamics and the physical interpretation of $q$-Hermitian operators.
