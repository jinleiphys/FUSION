# Improved light quark masses from pseudoscalar sum rules

> Using ratios of the inverse Laplace transform sum rules within stability criteria for the subtraction point μin addition to the ones of the usual tau spectral sum rule variable and continuum threshold t_c, we extract the π(1300) and K(1460) decay constants to order α_s^4 of perturbative QCD by including power corrections up to dimension-six condensates, tachyonic gluon mass, instanton and finite width corrections. Using these inputs with enlarged generous errors, we extract, in a model-independent and conservative ways, the sum of the scale-independent renormalization group invariant (RGI) quark masses (m_u+ m_q):q\equiv d,s and the corresponding running masses (m_u+m_q) evaluated at 2 GeV. By giving the value of the ratio m_u/m_d, we deduce the running quark masses m_{u,d,s} and condensate <\bar uu> and the scale-independent mass ratios : 2m_s/(m_u+m_d) and m_s/m_d. Using the positivity of the QCD continuum contribution to the spectral function, we also deduce, from the inverse Laplace transform sum rules, for the first time to order α_s^4, new lower bounds on the RGI masses which are translated into the running masses at 2 GeV and into upper bounds on the running quark condensate <\bar uu>. Our results summarized in Table 2 and compared with our previous results and with recent lattice averages suggest that precise phenomenological determinations of the sum of light quark masses require improved experimental measurements of the π(1.3) and K(1.46) hadronic widths and/or decay constants which are the dominant sources of errors in the analysis.

## Key claim

Using ratios of inverse Laplace transform sum rules with stability criteria for the subtraction point μ, the continuum threshold t_c, and the τ variable, the paper extracts the π(1300) and K(1460) decay constants to order α_s^4 of perturbative QCD, then uses these inputs to determine light quark masses; the abstract glosses over the fact that the results depend on whether the instanton contribution is included in the operator product expansion (SVZ expansion versus SVZ plus instanton), and that the dominant source of error is the experimental uncertainty in the hadronic widths of the radial excitations.

## Method

The author uses ratios of Laplace sum rules (LSR) for the pseudoscalar two-point correlator, known to order α_s^4 in perturbative QCD, including power corrections up to dimension-six condensates, a tachyonic gluon mass term, instanton contributions, and finite width corrections for the π(1300) and K(1460) resonances. The decay constants f_π' and f_K' are extracted from the ratio R_5^P(τ,μ) by applying τ, t_c, and μ stability criteria, then these decay constants are used as inputs in the Laplace sum rule L_5^P(τ,μ) to extract the sum of renormalization group invariant (RGI) quark masses and the corresponding running masses at 2 GeV. The analysis also derives new lower bounds on RGI masses from the positivity of the QCD continuum contribution to the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- r_π ≡ (M_π'^4 f_π'^2)/(m_π^4 f_π^2) = 4.42 ± 1.56 (SVZ expansion) and 2.06 ± 0.55 ± 0.20_syst (SVZ plus instanton), leading to f_π'/f_π = 2.42 ± 0.43 (SVZ) and 1.65 ± 0.22 (instanton)
- r_K ≡ (M_K'^4 f_K'^2)/(m_K^4 f_K^2) = 3.92 ± 1.56 (SVZ) and 2.47 ± 0.64 (instanton), leading to f_K'/f_K = 2.08 ± 0.41 (SVZ) and 1.65 ± 0.21 (instanton)
- Sum of running masses at 2 GeV: (m_u + m_d) = 7.9 ± 0.6 MeV (used as input from prior work SNmass)
- Running strange quark mass at 2 GeV: m_s = 114 ± 21 MeV (RGI mass m̂_s = 0.114 ± 0.021 GeV)
- Ratio 2m_s/(m_u + m_d) = 24.4 ± 2.4 (SVZ) and 24.8 ± 2.5 (instanton)
- Ratio m_s/m_d = 17.2 ± 1.8 (SVZ) and 17.5 ± 1.9 (instanton)
- Running quark condensate: -⟨ūu⟩^(1 GeV) = (267 ± 6 MeV)^3 (from RGI condensate μ̂_d = 253 ± 6 MeV)
- Lower bound on RGI masses: (m̂_u + m̂_d) ≥ 5.2 MeV and m̂_s ≥ 0.078 GeV at 90% CL

## Context

This work extends the pseudoscalar sum rule approach first introduced by Becchi (1981) and developed by Narison (1981, 1989) and others, improving upon earlier analyses by including α_s^4 perturbative corrections and using μ-stability criteria in addition to the usual τ and t_c stability. The results are compared with previous sum rule estimates by Narison (2002) and with recent lattice QCD averages; the paper finds that precise phenomenological determinations of light quark masses require improved experimental measurements of the π(1300) and K(1460) hadronic widths and/or decay constants, which are the dominant sources of error. The analysis enables more reliable model-independent extractions of light quark masses from QCD sum rules and provides new lower bounds on RGI masses from positivity constraints.
