# Hadron tomography by generalized distribution amplitudes in pion-pair production process $γ^* γ\rightarrow π^0 π^0 $ and gravitational form factors for pion

> Hadron tomography can be investigated by three-dimensional structure functions such as generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions, and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). Here, we extract the GDAs, which are $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs, from cross-section measurements of hadron-pair production process $γ^* γ\rightarrow π^0 π^0$ at KEKB. This work is the first attempt to obtain the GDAs from the actual experimental data. The GDAs are expressed by a number of parameters and they are determined from the data of $γ^* γ\rightarrow π^0 π^0$ by including intermediate scalar- and tensor-meson contributions to the cross section. Our results indicate that the dependence of parton-momentum fraction $z$ in the GDAs is close to the asymptotic one. The timelike gravitational form factors $Θ_1$ and $Θ_2$ are obtained from our GDAs, and they are converted to the spacelike ones by the dispersion relation.From the spacelike $Θ_1$ and $Θ_2$, gravitational densities of the pion are calculated. Then, we obtained the mass (energy) radius and the mechanical (pressure and shear force) radius from $Θ_2$ and $Θ_1$, respectively. They are calculated as $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{mass}}} =0.32 \sim 0.39$ fm, whereas the mechanical radius is larger $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{mech}}} =0.82 \sim 0.88$ fm. This is the first report on the gravitational radius of a hadron from actual experimental measurements. It is interesting to find the possibility that the gravitational mass and mechanical radii could be different from the experimental charge radius $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{charge}}} =0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm for the charged pion.

## Key claim

This work is the first attempt to extract generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) from actual experimental data, specifically from cross-section measurements of the process γ*γ → π⁰π⁰ at KEKB, with the caveat that the GDAs are expressed by a number of parameters and determined by including intermediate scalar- and tensor-meson contributions to the cross section.

## Method

The authors parametrize the pion GDAs, which are s-t crossed quantities of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and determine the parameters from Belle collaboration cross-section data for γ*γ → π⁰π⁰ at various kinematical conditions. From the extracted GDAs, they obtain timelike gravitational form factors Θ₁ and Θ₂, which are then converted to spacelike form factors using dispersion relations. The spacelike form factors are used to calculate gravitational densities of the pion and extract mass and mechanical radii.

## Key numbers

- Mass (energy) radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mass = 0.32 ~ 0.39 fm
- Mechanical (pressure and shear force) radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mech = 0.82 ~ 0.88 fm
- Experimental charge radius of the charged pion for comparison: √⟨r²⟩_charge = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of three-dimensional hadron structure from GPDs and transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) to the relatively unexplored GDAs, which are related to GPDs by s-t crossing and can be expressed by common double distributions through Radon transforms. The results enable future investigations of hadron tomography and gravitational properties of hadrons through more accurate measurements at super-KEKB and the International Linear Collider, and represent the first report on gravitational radii of a hadron from actual experimental measurements.
