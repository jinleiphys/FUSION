# Hadron tomography by generalized distribution amplitudes in pion-pair production process $Оі^* Оі\rightarrow ПҖ^0 ПҖ^0 $ and gravitational form factors for pion

> Hadron tomography can be investigated by three-dimensional structure functions such as generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions, and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). Here, we extract the GDAs, which are $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs, from cross-section measurements of hadron-pair production process $Оі^* Оі\rightarrow ПҖ^0 ПҖ^0$ at KEKB. This work is the first attempt to obtain the GDAs from the actual experimental data. The GDAs are expressed by a number of parameters and they are determined from the data of $Оі^* Оі\rightarrow ПҖ^0 ПҖ^0$ by including intermediate scalar- and tensor-meson contributions to the cross section. Our results indicate that the dependence of parton-momentum fraction $z$ in the GDAs is close to the asymptotic one. The timelike gravitational form factors $Оҳ_1$ and $Оҳ_2$ are obtained from our GDAs, and they are converted to the spacelike ones by the dispersion relation.From the spacelike $Оҳ_1$ and $Оҳ_2$, gravitational densities of the pion are calculated. Then, we obtained the mass (energy) radius and the mechanical (pressure and shear force) radius from $Оҳ_2$ and $Оҳ_1$, respectively. They are calculated as $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{mass}}} =0.32 \sim 0.39$ fm, whereas the mechanical radius is larger $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{mech}}} =0.82 \sim 0.88$ fm. This is the first report on the gravitational radius of a hadron from actual experimental measurements. It is interesting to find the possibility that the gravitational mass and mechanical radii could be different from the experimental charge radius $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle _{\text{charge}}} =0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm for the charged pion.

## Key claim

This work is the first attempt to extract generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) from actual experimental data, specifically from cross-section measurements of the process Оі*Оі вҶ’ ПҖвҒ°ПҖвҒ° at KEKB, with the caveat that the GDAs are expressed by a number of parameters and determined by including intermediate scalar- and tensor-meson contributions to the cross section.

## Method

The authors parametrize the pion GDAs, which are s-t crossed quantities of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and determine the parameters from Belle collaboration cross-section data for Оі*Оі вҶ’ ПҖвҒ°ПҖвҒ° at various kinematical conditions. From the extracted GDAs, they obtain timelike gravitational form factors ОҳвӮҒ and ОҳвӮӮ, which are then converted to spacelike form factors using dispersion relations. The spacelike form factors are used to calculate gravitational densities of the pion and extract mass and mechanical radii.

## Context

This work extends the study of three-dimensional hadron structure from GPDs and transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) to the relatively unexplored GDAs, which are related to GPDs by s-t crossing and can be expressed by common double distributions through Radon transforms. The results enable future investigations of hadron tomography and gravitational properties of hadrons through more accurate measurements at super-KEKB and the International Linear Collider, and represent the first report on gravitational radii of a hadron from actual experimental measurements.
