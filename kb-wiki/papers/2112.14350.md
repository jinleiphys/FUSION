# Time-dependent extension of the self-consistent band theory for neutron star matter: Anti-entrainment effects in the slab phase

> Background: In the solid crust of neutron stars, a variety of crystalline structure may exist. Recently the band theory of solids has been applied to the inner crust of neutron stars and significance of the entrainment between dripped neutrons and the solid crust was advocated. Since it influences interpretations of various phenomena of neutron stars, it has been desired to develop deeper understanding of the microphysics behind. Purpose: The purpose of the present article is to propose a fully self-consistent microscopic framework for describing time-dependent dynamics of neutron star matter, which allows us to explore diverse properties of nuclear matter, including the entrainment effect. Results: As the first application of the time-dependent self-consistent band theory for nuclear systems, we investigate the slab phase of nuclear matter with various proton fractions. From a dynamic response of the system to an external force, we extract the collective mass of a slab, associated with entrained neutrons as well as bound nucleons. We find that the extracted collective mass is smaller than a naive estimation based on a potential profile and single-particle energies. We show that the reduction is mainly caused by "counterflow" of dripped neutrons towards the direction opposite to the motion of the slabs. We interpret it as an "anti-entrainment" effect. As a result, the number of effectively bound neutrons is reduced, indicating an enhancement of the number density of conduction neutrons. We demonstrate that those findings are consistent with a static treatment in the band theory of solids. *shortened due to the arXiv's word limit.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the collective mass of a nuclear slab in neutron star matter is smaller than naive estimates based on potential profiles and single-particle energies, primarily due to a "counterflow" of dripped neutrons moving opposite to the slab direction, an "anti-entrainment" effect that enhances the number density of conduction neutrons. This finding is conditional on the slab phase and neglects spin-orbit coupling and pairing correlations.

## Method
The authors developed a time-dependent extension of the self-consistent nuclear band theory based on the Skyrme SLy4 energy density functional, employing time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) with the velocity gauge to preserve periodicity under an external electric field. They applied this framework to the slab phase of nuclear matter at zero temperature, solving the Kohn-Sham equations for Bloch orbitals along the periodic direction, and extracted the collective mass of a slab by measuring the acceleration of protons under a small external force and relating it to the rate of change of linear momentum.

## Key numbers
- The neutron effective mass was found to be smaller than the bare neutron mass by a factor of 0.65–0.75 for the slab phase, as previously reported by Kashiwaba and Nakatsukasa (2019) using static band calculations.

## Context
This work extends the static self-consistent band theory calculations of Kashiwaba and Nakatsukasa (2019) for the slab phase, which used the BCPM functional and found reduced neutron effective mass, by introducing a time-dependent framework. It contradicts earlier findings by Carter et al. (2005) and Chamel (2005, 2006, 2007, 2012), who reported enhanced effective mass (entrainment) in slab and rod phases, and it enables future microscopic studies of non-linear many-body dynamics in neutron star crusts, including the effects of periodic structure on neutron mobility.
