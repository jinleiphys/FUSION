# Phase diagram for charged scalars in a magnetic field at finite temperature

> We investigate the nature of the phase transition for charged scalars in the presence of a magnetic background for a theory with spontaneous symmetry breaking. We perform a careful treatment of the negative mass squared as a function of the order parameter and present a suitable method to obtain magnetic and thermal corrections up to ring order for the high temperature limit and the case where the magnetic field strength is larger than the absolute value of the square of the mass parameter. We show that for a given value of the self-coupling, the phase transition is first order for a small magnetic field strength and becomes second order as this last grows. We also show that the critical temperature in the presence of the magnetic field is always below the critical temperature for the case where the field is absent.

## Key claim

For a charged scalar theory with spontaneous symmetry breaking in a magnetic background, the phase transition is first order for small magnetic field strength and becomes second order as the field grows, provided the self-coupling is fixed; the critical temperature in the presence of the magnetic field is always below the critical temperature for the case where the field is absent, contrary to some earlier model calculations.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop vacuum energy for a charged scalar in a uniform magnetic field using Schwinger's proper-time method, carefully treating the negative mass-squared region by closing the integration contour in the upper half-plane and summing over the poles of the integrand. They then construct the finite-temperature effective potential in the high-temperature limit (T² ≫ |m²|, qB) and the regime qB > |m²|, including the ring-diagram contribution from the zero Matsubara mode to account for plasma screening. The Abelian Higgs model is used as the specific Lagrangian, with stability conditions imposed to preserve the tree-level vacuum expectation value and sigma mass at one-loop order.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic-field effects on phase transitions in scalar field theories, addressing a subtlety not previously treated: the proper handling of negative mass squared in the vacuum energy for systems with spontaneous symmetry breaking. The results contrast with earlier findings by Duarte (2013) regarding the order of the phase transition. The paper enables more reliable calculations of the electroweak phase transition in the early universe and of chiral/deconfinement transitions in heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic fields are present.
