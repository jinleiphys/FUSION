# New Regimes of Stringy (Holographic) Pomeron and High Multiplicity pp and pA Collisions

> Holographic AdS/QCD models of the Pomeron unite a string-based description of hadronic reactions of the pre-QCD era with the perturbative BFKL approach. The specific version we will use due to Stoffers and Zahed, is based on a semiclassical quantization of a "tube" (closed string exchange or open string virtual pair production) in its Euclidean formulation using the scalar Polyakov action. This model has a number of phenomenologically successful results. The periodicity of a coordinate around the tube allows the introduction of a Matsubara time and therefore an effective temperature Teff on the string. We observe that at the LHC energies and for sufficiently small impact parameter, Teff approaches and even exceeds the Hagedorn temperature of the QCD strings. Based on studies of the stringy thermodynamics of pure gauge theories we suggest that there should exist two new regimes of the Pomeron: the "near-critical" and the "post-critical" ones. In the former one, string excitations create a high entropy "string ball", with high energy and entropy but small pressure/free energy. If heavy enough this ball becomes a (dual) black hole (BH). As the intrinsic temperature of the string exceeds the Hagedorn temperature, the ball becomes a post-critical explosive "QGP ball". The hydrodynamical explosion resulting from this scenario was predicted by us to have radial flow exceeding that ever seen even in heavy ion collisions, which was recently confirmed by CMS and ALICE at LHC. We also discuss the elastic scattering profile, finding some hints for new phases in it, as well as two-particle correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at LHC energies and sufficiently small impact parameters, the effective temperature of the exchanged string in holographic Pomeron models approaches or exceeds the Hagedorn temperature, leading to two new regimes beyond the standard cold Pomeron: a "near-critical" regime where the string becomes a high-entropy "string ball" with small pressure, and a "post-critical" regime where it becomes an explosive "QGP ball" that undergoes hydrodynamic expansion.

## Method

The authors use the Stoffers-Zahed holographic AdS/QCD model, which employs semiclassical quantization of a Euclidean "tube" (closed string exchange or open string virtual pair production) using the scalar Polyakov action in 5-dimensional holographic space. They introduce an effective temperature \(T_{eff}\) via Matsubara time periodicity around the tube, and analyze the string thermodynamics by analogy with finite-temperature lattice gauge theory studies of the glue. The elastic scattering profile is examined using the Bourrely-Soffer-Wu (BSW) parameterization of \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV and \(\sqrt{s}=63\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- String tension: \(\sigma_T \approx (0.42\ \text{GeV})^2\)
- Hagedorn temperature for QCD strings: \((T^{QCD}_H)^2 = \frac{3}{D_\perp} \frac{\sigma_T}{2\pi} \approx (0.176\ \text{GeV})^2\)
- Hagedorn temperature for Yang-Mills (closed/double strings): \(T^{YM}_H = \sqrt{2} T^{QCD}_H \approx 0.237\ \text{GeV}\)
- Ratio of Hagedorn to critical temperature in pure gauge theories: \(T_H / T_c \approx 1.11\)
- Critical temperature for deconfinement in gluodynamics: \(T_c \approx 0.27\ \text{GeV}\)
- Glueball \(2^{++}\) mass: \(M_{2^{++}} = 2.15\ \text{GeV}\), used as unit for Regge slope \(\alpha'(0) = 0.92 / M^2_{2^{++}}\)
- Intercept gap between Pomeron and first daughter trajectory: \(\Delta \alpha_1 = \alpha_P(0) - \alpha_{D1}(0) \approx 2.0\)
- Intercept gap between Pomeron and second daughter trajectory: \(\Delta \alpha_2 = \alpha_P(0) - \alpha_{D2}(0) \approx 4\)
- Dip in elastic \(pp\) cross section at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV occurs at \(|t| = 0.53\ \text{GeV}\), followed by power-law tail with exponent \(p \approx 7.8\)
- Exponential tail parameter in BSW profile: \(m_1 = 0.577\ \text{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the Stoffers-Zahed holographic Pomeron model (Stoffers and Zahed, 2012) by incorporating string thermodynamics near the Hagedorn temperature, building on the Hagedorn phenomenon originally identified by Hagedorn and the thermal scalar formalism of Polyakov (1979) and Susskind (1979). The paper connects to lattice glueball spectroscopy (Morningstar and Peardon, 1999; Harvey, 2008) and finite-temperature lattice studies of pure gauge theories (Bringoltz and Teper, 2005; Teper, 2009). The prediction of strong radial flow in high-multiplicity \(pp\) and \(pA\) collisions, previously made by the same authors (Shuryak and Zahed, 2013), is noted as having been confirmed by CMS and ALICE at LHC. The work enables further study of the transition from stringy to black hole descriptions in high-energy hadronic collisions, and suggests that the three regimes observed in the elastic scattering profile correspond to distinct dynamical phases of the stringy Pomeron.
