# Electromagnetic fields and anomalous transports in heavy-ion collisions --- A pedagogical review

> The hot and dense matter generated in heavy-ion collisions may contain domains which are not invariant under P and CP transformations. Moreover, heavy-ion collisions can generate extremely strong magnetic fields as well as electric fields. The interplay between the electromagnetic field and triangle anomaly leads to a number of macroscopic quantum phenomena in these P- and CP-odd domains known as the anomalous transports. The purpose of the article is to give a pedagogical review of various properties of the electromagnetic fields, the anomalous transports phenomena, and their experimental signatures in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

This pedagogical review argues that heavy-ion collisions generate extremely strong electromagnetic fields (up to ~10^20 Gauss at LHC) which, combined with the triangle anomaly, produce macroscopic quantum transport phenomena (anomalous transports) in P- and CP-odd domains of the quark-gluon plasma; however, the review emphasizes that event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions significantly modify the magnitude, orientation, and time evolution of these fields compared to naive estimates.

## Method

The paper reviews classical Liénard-Wiechert potential calculations of electromagnetic fields on an event-by-event basis using Woods-Saxon nuclear distributions, accounting for retardation effects and event-by-event fluctuations of proton positions. It then surveys the theoretical framework of anomalous transport phenomena (chiral magnetic effect, chiral separation effect, chiral electric separation effect, chiral magnetic waves, chiral vortical effect) derived from the triangle anomaly in quantum chromodynamics, and summarizes experimental search strategies using two-particle correlation measurements at RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field in Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: ~10^18-10^19 Gauss (or ~10 m_pi^2 in natural units) at impact parameter b=10 fm
- Magnetic field in Pb + Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: ~10^20 Gauss, scaling as 13.8 times the RHIC value
- Lattice QCD transition temperature from hadronic matter to QGP: ~200 MeV (at zero quark chemical potential)
- Lifetime of spectator-induced magnetic field at RHIC: t_B ~ 0.065 fm; at LHC: t_B ~ 0.005 fm
- Electric conductivity of QGP from perturbative estimate: sigma ~ 6T/e^2 at high temperature
- Event-averaged magnetic field decay formula: <eB_y(t)> ~ <eB_y(0)>/(1+t^2/t_B^2)^(3/2) for t less than about 10 t_B

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews by Tuchin (2013), Kharzeev (2009, 2013, 2015) and others by providing a pedagogical introduction accessible to graduate students, focusing on the electromagnetic field properties and their connection to anomalous transport phenomena. It synthesizes numerical simulations of EM fields by Skokov (2009), Voronyuk (2011), Bzdak (2011), Deng (2012), and Bloczynski (2012, 2013) with the theoretical framework of chiral anomaly-driven transport, and discusses experimental measurements from STAR and ALICE collaborations. The work enables future studies of how event-by-event fluctuations of magnetic field orientation affect experimental observables for the chiral magnetic effect and related phenomena.
