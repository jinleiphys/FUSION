# $Σ^-$ admixture in the $^{10}_Λ$Li hypernucleus with a repulsive $Σ$-nucleus potential

> $Σ^-$ hyperon component of the $^{10}_Λ$Li wave function is studied. The $Σ^-$ admixture is vital for production of neutron-rich $Λ$ hypernuclei via mesonic beams. We use a simplified shell model wave function for the $Λ$ channel and calculate the $Σ^-$ admixture directly from coupled equations. Probability of the $Σ^-$ admixture for realistic repulsive $Σ^-$-nucleus potentials is less by several times than that for attractive potentials and does not exceed $0.1\%$. We conclude that the cross sections of the $^{10}\mbox{B}(π^-,K^+){}^{10}_Λ\mbox{Li}$ reaction measured at KEK cannot be explained by production via $Σ^-$ admixture as a doorway state.

## Key claim

The probability of a Σ⁻ admixture in the ¹⁰_ΛLi hypernucleus is always less than 0.1% for realistic repulsive Σ⁻-nucleus potentials, which is much smaller than the 0.5–0.7% needed to explain the measured cross sections of the ¹⁰B(π⁻,K⁺)¹⁰_ΛLi reaction via the one-step π⁻p → K⁺Σ⁻ mechanism; the authors therefore conclude that this one-step mechanism cannot be the dominant production channel.

## Method

The authors use a simplified shell-model approach where the Λ hyperon is described by a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential and the Σ⁻ admixture is calculated directly from coupled equations without expanding in eigenstates of H_ΣΣ. The Λn–Σ⁻p coupling is treated perturbatively: the Λ wave function is found first without coupling, then the Σ⁻ radial wave function is obtained by solving a driven Schrödinger equation with a source term arising from the Λn↔Σ⁻p transition matrix element. The Σ⁻ admixture probability is the integral of the squared Σ⁻ radial wave function. Several different YNG-type effective ΛN–ΣN interactions (NHCD, NSC89, ESC08a, ESC08a″, ESC08b, and D2) are tested, and spectroscopic factors for ⁹Li → ⁸Li + n from multiple sources are used.

## Key numbers

- For a repulsive Σ⁻-nucleus potential (v₀ = +30 MeV) and spectroscopic factors from Kan (2005), the Σ⁻ admixture probability p_Σ⁻ ranges from 0.57 × 10⁻⁴ (ESC08a, 1⁻ state) to 6.74 × 10⁻⁴ (ESC08a″, 1⁻ state), i.e., always below 0.1%.
- For the ESC08b coupling potential with various spectroscopic factor sets, p_Σ⁻ for the 1⁻ state ranges from 0.96 × 10⁻⁴ (VMC) to 2.03 × 10⁻⁴ (Jep T2), and for the 2⁻ state from 2.08 × 10⁻⁴ (CK) to 4.43 × 10⁻⁴ (VMC).
- The Λ binding energy used is B_Λ = 9.71 MeV, giving e_Σ = –77.36 MeV.
- Harada et al. (2005) required p_Σ⁻ ≈ 0.5–0.7% to reproduce the observed cross sections, which is several times larger than any value obtained in this work.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ΛN–ΣN mixing in p-shell hypernuclei (e.g., Akaishi (2000), Harada (2005), Millener (2001)) by using a method that avoids expansion in H_ΣΣ eigenstates, allowing treatment of repulsive Σ-nucleus potentials. The KEK experiment (Saha, 2005) measured the ¹⁰B(π⁻,K⁺)¹⁰_ΛLi reaction cross section and found a momentum dependence incompatible with the two-step charge-exchange mechanism; Harada et al. (2005) then proposed the one-step π⁻p → K⁺Σ⁻ mechanism via Σ⁻ admixture, but the present results show that the required Σ⁻ probability is too large. This opens the question of whether other mechanisms, such as production via Δ admixture in the target nucleus (Lanskoy, 2004), might be responsible.
