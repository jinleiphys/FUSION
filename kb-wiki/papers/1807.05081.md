# $Υ$ suppression in a hadron gas

> In this work we study the interactions of bottom mesons which lead to $Υ$ production and absorption in hot hadronic matter. We use effective Lagrangians to calculate the $Υ$ production cross section in processes such as $ \bar{B}^{(*)} + B^{(*)} \to Υ+ (π, ρ)$ and also the $Υ$ absorption cross section in the corresponding inverse processes. We update and extend previous calculations by Lin and Ko, introducing anomalous interactions. The obtained cross sections are used as input to solve the rate equation which allows us to follow the time evolution of the $Υ$ multiplicity. In contrast to previous conjectures, our results suggest that the interactions in the hadron gas phase strongly reduce the $Υ$ abundance.

# $\Upsilon$ suppression in a hadron gas

## Key claim

The authors find that interactions in the hadron gas phase can strongly reduce the $\Upsilon$ abundance, in contrast to previous conjectures by Lin and Ko that such absorption by comoving hadrons was unlikely to be important. This conclusion depends on the inclusion of anomalous parity interactions and updated coupling constants, which were absent in earlier calculations.

## Method

The authors use effective Lagrangians based on SU(4) flavor symmetry to calculate cross sections for $\Upsilon$ absorption and production processes involving pions and rho mesons, specifically $\bar{B}^{(*)} + B^{(*)} \to \Upsilon + (\pi, \rho)$ and their inverse reactions. They include both normal and anomalous parity interactions, incorporate form factors with a cutoff parameter of 5.0 GeV, and use the resulting cross sections as input to solve the rate equation governing the time evolution of $\Upsilon$ multiplicity in a hadron gas with hydrodynamical expansion.

## Key numbers

- $R_{AA} (\Upsilon(1S)) \simeq 0.38 \pm 0.05$ for central PbPb collisions at small rapidities and small $p_T$, compared to $R_{AA} (J/\psi) \simeq 0.28 \pm 0.03$
- $\Upsilon$ multiplicity at the end of the mixed phase at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV): $N_{\Upsilon} \approx 1.705 \times 10^6$
- $\Upsilon$ multiplicity at the end of the mixed phase at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ TeV): $N_{\Upsilon} \approx 0.00106$
- Critical temperature for QGP to hadronic matter transition: $T_C = 175$ MeV; freeze-out temperature: $T_F = 125$ MeV
- Coupling constant $g_{B^{\ast} B \pi} = 24.9$; $g_{\Upsilon B B} = 13.3$; $g_{\Upsilon B^{\ast} B^{\ast}} = 13.3$
- Anomalous coupling $g_{\Upsilon B B \pi} = 44.8$ GeV$^{-3}$; $g_{\Upsilon B^{\ast} B^{\ast} \pi} = 31.25$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Isospin-averaged masses used: $m_{\pi} = 137.3$ MeV, $m_{\rho} = 775.2$ MeV, $m_B = 5279.4$ MeV, $m_{B^{\ast}} = 5324.7$ MeV, $m_{\Upsilon} = 9460.3$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Lin and Ko, who used SU(5) flavor symmetry and found $\pi \Upsilon$ cross sections of about 8 mb and $\rho \Upsilon$ cross sections of about 1 mb, with thermal averages of only about 0.2 mb at 150 MeV. The present study updates those results by introducing anomalous parity interactions, which were shown to be important in charmonium studies (such as $J/\psi$ absorption by pions and rho mesons) and in studies of $X(3872)$ interactions. The results suggest that hadron gas effects must be included alongside QGP screening mechanisms to correctly model $\Upsilon$ suppression observed by CMS and STAR collaborations.
