# Mesonic fluctuations in a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> The effects of meson fluctuations are studied in a nonlocal generalization of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, by including terms of next-to-leading order (NLO) in 1/N_c. In the model with only scalar and pseudoscalar interactions NLO contributions to the quark condensate are found to be very small. This is a result of cancellation between virtual mesons and Fock terms, which occurs for the parameter sets of most interest. In the quark self-energy, similar cancellations arise in the tadpole diagrams, although not in other NLO pieces which contribute at the \sim 25% level. The effects on pion properties are also found to be small. NLO contributions from real $ππ$ intermediate states increase the sigma meson mass by $\sim 30%$. In an extended model with vector and axial interactions, there are indications that NLO effects could be larger.

# Mesonic fluctuations in a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

Next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions to the quark condensate are very small in the nonlocal NJL model with only scalar and pseudoscalar interactions, due to cancellation between virtual meson contributions and Fock terms for the parameter sets of most interest; however, in the quark self-energy, other NLO pieces contribute at the ~25% level, and NLO contributions from real ππ intermediate states increase the sigma meson mass by ~30%.

## Method

The authors performed a strict 1/N_c expansion to next-to-leading order in a nonlocal NJL model with a Gaussian form factor, keeping the full momentum dependence of all quark loop diagrams without expanding in powers of meson momenta. They evaluated NLO corrections to the quark condensate, quark self-energy (including tadpole and meson-cloud diagrams), meson propagators (including two-meson intermediate states in the Bethe-Salpeter equation), and the pion decay constant, fitting model parameters at leading order to the pion mass and decay constant. The calculations used a separable interaction with a Gaussian form factor in Euclidean space, f(p_E) = exp(-p_E^2 / Λ^2), which ensures convergence of all loop integrals without additional regularization ambiguities.

## Key numbers

- NLO contributions to the quark condensate are "very small" (no explicit numerical value given for the condensate shift itself)
- In the quark self-energy, the meson-cloud contributions increase b(0) by about 25%, and the net NLO shift in the quark "mass" at zero momentum b(0)/(1 + a(0)) is ~15%
- The NLO contributions to the coefficient of the slash-p momentum term a(p) range up to about 0.25
- For the parameter set with m_0(0)=200 MeV, the pion tadpole contributes about 45% of the LO chiral quark mass, but this is largely cancelled by the sigma tadpole
- NLO contributions from real ππ intermediate states increase the sigma meson mass by ~30%

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal NJL model originally proposed by Bowler and Birse (1995) and studied at leading order in meson, baryon (soliton), and quark matter sectors. It builds on NLO studies in local NJL models by Dmitrasinovic et al. (1995), Oertel et al. (2000), and Ripka (1997), and confirms the finding of Ripka (1997) that cancellations between virtual mesons and Fock terms keep NLO corrections to the condensate small. The results enable future NLO treatments of broad resonances like the rho and a_1 mesons, where widths and mass shifts from two-meson intermediate states are expected to be important, and provide a benchmark for comparing with alternative regularization schemes such as the O(4) and O(3) schemes used in local NJL models.
