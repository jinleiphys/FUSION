# Residual proton-neutron interactions and the $N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$ scheme

> We investigate the correlation between integrated proton-neutron interactions obtained by using the up-to-date experimental data of binding energies and the $N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$, the product of valence proton number and valence neutron number with respect to the nearest doubly closed nucleus. We make corrections on a previously suggested formula for the integrated proton-neutron interaction. Our results demonstrate a nice, nearly linear, correlation between the integrated p-n interaction and $N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$, which provides us with a firm foundation of the applicability of the $N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$ scheme to nuclei far from the stability line.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a nearly linear correlation between the integrated proton-neutron interaction and the product of valence proton and neutron numbers ($N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$), but only after correcting a previously suggested formula; the linearity holds for most nuclei except for slight deviations observed in nuclei with proton number Z=80 and 81 in the region 50<Z<82, 82<N<126.

## Method

The authors used up-to-date experimental binding energy data to compute integrated proton-neutron interactions ($V_{\rm pn}$) via a double-difference procedure. They identified inadequacies in the original formula of Zhang et al. (1989), which overcounted valence nucleon pairs for nuclei with mixed particle-hole configurations (e.g., $^{128}$I), and proposed a corrected formula ($V'_{\rm pn}$) that sums interactions over individual valence nucleons rather than over pairs of even-even reference nuclei. The new formula applies to odd-odd, even-even, and odd-mass nuclei, and the resulting $|V'_{\rm pn}|$ values were plotted against $N_{\rm p} N_{\rm n}$ for three nuclear regions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{128}$I, the original formula (Zhang et al., 1989) gave $V_{\rm pn} = 32 \times \delta V_{\rm np}$, while the corrected formula yields $V_{\rm pn} = 21 \times \delta V_{\rm np}$, matching the actual valence nucleon count (3 protons, 7 neutrons).
- The study covers three nuclear regions: 28<Z<50 and 50<N<82; 50<Z<82 and 50<N<82; and 50<Z<82 and 82<N<126.
- For the region 50<Z<82 and 82<N<126, nuclei with Z=80 and 81 show slight deviations from the linear $V'_{\rm pn}$-$N_{\rm p}N_{\rm n}$ correlation, with $V'_{\rm pn}$ values slightly smaller than those of "normal" nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the original study by Zhang et al. (1989), which first proposed the integrated proton-neutron interaction formula and noted deviations from linearity for small $N_{\rm p}N_{\rm n}$. The authors correct a systematic overcounting error in that formula, enabling the $N_{\rm p}N_{\rm n}$ scheme to be applied reliably to nuclei far from stability, including unstable nuclei studied at new radioactive beam facilities. The results provide a firm foundation for using the $N_{\rm p}N_{\rm n}$ scheme to interpret collective motion, phase transitions, and deformation in nuclear structure, as originally advocated by Casten (1985).
