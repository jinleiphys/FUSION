# Full jet in quark-gluon plasma with hydrodynamic medium response

> We study the nuclear modifications of full jets and their structures in relativistic heavy-ion collisions including the effect of hydrodynamic medium response to jet quenching. To study the evolutions of the full jet shower and the traversed medium with energy and momentum exchanges between them, we formulate a coupled jet-fluid model consisting of a set of jet transport equations and relativistic hydrodynamics equations with source terms. In our model, the full jet shower interacts with the medium and gets modified via collisional and radiative processes during the propagation. Meanwhile, the energy and momentum are deposited from the jet shower to the medium and then evolve with the medium hydrodynamically. The full jet defined by a cone size in the final state includes the jet shower and the particles produced from jet-induced flow. We apply our model to calculate the full jet energy loss and the nuclear modifications of jet rate and shape in Pb+Pb collisions at $2.76{\rm A~TeV}$. It is found that the inclusion of jet-induced flow contribution leads to stronger jet-cone size dependence for jet energy loss and jet suppression. Jet-induced flow also has a significant contribution to jet shape function and dominates at large angles away from the jet axis.

## Key claim

The inclusion of hydrodynamic medium response to jet quenching leads to stronger jet-cone size dependence for full jet energy loss and jet suppression, and the jet-induced flow dominates the jet shape function at large angles away from the jet axis, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of transport coefficients and the assumption of ideal hydrodynamics with a smooth initial medium profile.

## Method

The authors formulate a coupled jet-fluid model combining (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations with source terms for the QGP medium and a set of transport equations for the jet shower evolution, including collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening, and medium-induced partonic splittings via the higher-twist formalism. The source terms are constructed from the evolving parton distributions in the jet shower to account for energy and momentum deposition into the medium. The model is applied to simulate single jet events in central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV, with initial conditions from the optical Glauber model and PYTHIA for jet production, and final state particles from the Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient for quark: $\hat{q}_{q,0} = 1.7$ GeV$^2$/fm (static QGP medium at $T_0$), consistent with JET Collaboration extraction
- Minimum parton energy cutoff: $\omega_{\rm cut} = 1$ GeV
- Initial thermalization time: $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$
- Critical temperature for QGP-hadron transition: $T_c = 160$ MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: $T_{\rm FO} = 140$ MeV
- Example jet initial transverse momentum: $p_T^{\rm jet} = 150$ GeV/$c$ for the single jet event shown in the energy density evolution figures
- Transverse momentum cut for hydro part particles: $p_T^{\rm trk, hyd} > 1$ GeV/$c$ for inclusive jet analyses, $p_T^{\rm trk, hyd} > 0.5$ GeV/$c$ for dijet analyses

## Context

This work extends the coupled jet-fluid approach of Tachibana (2014, 2015) and the jet transport equation framework of Chang (2016) by incorporating hydrodynamic medium response into full jet observables. It builds on prior studies of Mach cone formation from jet energy deposition by Casalderrey-Solana (2004), Stoecker (2004), and others, and addresses the need for a consistent treatment of energy-momentum exchange between the jet shower and the QGP fluid. The results enable future event-by-event studies including initial state fluctuations and could provide a way to extract bulk QGP properties such as sound velocity and viscosities from detailed measurements of jet substructure.
