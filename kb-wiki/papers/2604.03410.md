# Precise determination of electron-capture $Q$ value of $^{113}$Sn decay related to electron neutrino mass measurements

> A high-precision measurement of the electron-capture (EC) decay $Q$ value for the ground-state-to-ground-state (gs-to-gs) transition of $^{113}$Sn to $^{113}$In has been performed using the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer. Employing the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance technique, the isomeric state of $^{113}$Sn at 77.389(19) keV was resolved, and the cyclotron frequency ratio measured between the isomer $^{113m}$Sn and the daughter nucleus $^{113}$In. This yielded an isomer-to-ground-state $Q$ value of 1116.64(19) keV and gs-to-gs $Q$ value of 1039.25(19) keV. The atomic mass excess of $^{113}$Sn was determined as $-$88327.87(27) keV/c$^2$, in excellent agreement with the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 (AME2020) but with a sixfold precision improvement. Using nuclear energy-level data for $^{113}$In, we identified two low $Q$-value transitions of the ground state of $^{113}$Sn to excited states of $^{113}$In at 1024.280(50) keV ($Q_{EC}^* = 14.97(20)$ keV, second forbidden non-unique) and 1029.650(50) keV ($Q_{EC}^* = 9.60(20)$ keV, allowed). The allowed transition exhibits small energy differences ($Δ_{L1} = 5.58(20)$ keV, $Δ_{L2} = 5.87(20)$ keV) from L1 and L2 shell binding energies, enhancing endpoint events. Partial half-lives and energy-release spectra were calculated using the self-consistent Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) method (including exchange, overlap, shake-up, and shake-off corrections) together with the nuclear shell model, show enhanced endpoint sensitivity for the allowed transition to the state at 1029.650 keV. Including subthreshold atomic states in the spectral function enhances the EC rate near the zero-neutrino-momentum region by a factor of five, enabling new approaches for low $Q$-value EC reactions in neutrino-mass studies.

## Key claim

The paper reports a high-precision measurement of the electron-capture decay Q value for the ground-state-to-ground-state transition of 113Sn to 113In, but the key finding for neutrino mass studies is the identification of an allowed transition to an excited state of 113In at 1029.650 keV with a Q value of only 9.60(20) keV, which exhibits small energy differences from L1 and L2 shell binding energies that enhance endpoint events. The authors qualify that including subthreshold atomic states in the spectral function enhances the EC rate near the zero-neutrino-momentum region by a factor of five, but note that further precise measurement of the branching ratio of this transition is required before it can be used for neutrino mass determination.

## Method

The experiment used the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the IGISOL facility, employing the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique to measure cyclotron frequency ratios between the isomeric state 113mSn and the daughter nucleus 113In. The isomeric state at 77.389(19) keV was resolved, and the cyclotron frequency ratio was measured with accumulation times of 244 ms and 738 ms to determine the isomer-to-ground-state Q value. Theoretical predictions for partial half-lives and energy-release spectra were calculated using the self-consistent Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) method with exchange, overlap, shake-up, and shake-off corrections, combined with the nuclear shell model.

## Key numbers

- Isomer-to-ground-state Q value: 1116.64(19) keV, measured from the cyclotron frequency ratio of 1.00001061759(18) between 113mSn and 113In
- Ground-state-to-ground-state Q value: 1039.25(19) keV, derived from the isomer Q value minus the isomeric excitation energy of 77.389(19) keV
- Atomic mass excess of 113Sn: -88327.87(27) keV/c2, a sixfold precision improvement over the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 (AME2020)
- Low Q-value transition to 113In excited state at 1024.280(50) keV: Q*EC = 14.97(20) keV (second forbidden non-unique)
- Low Q-value transition to 113In excited state at 1029.650(50) keV: Q*EC = 9.60(20) keV (allowed)
- Energy differences from shell binding energies for the allowed transition: Delta_L1 = 5.58(20) keV, Delta_L2 = 5.87(20) keV
- Half-life of the allowed transition: 20(+2.3/-2.0) x 10^9 yr

## Context

This work extends the systematic search for low Q-value electron-capture transitions suitable for neutrino mass determination, following similar studies on 159Dy (Ge 2021), 111In (Ge 2022), and 95Tc (Ge 2024). The measurement provides a candidate complementary to 163Ho and 159Dy for future neutrino-mass experiments using cryogenic microcalorimeters, with the advantage of a moderate half-life of 115.09(3) days and clean gamma-tagging via the 1029.650 keV cascade. The identification of an allowed transition with L-shell proximity enables new approaches for studying low Q-value EC reactions, though the branching ratio requires further precise measurement before practical application.
