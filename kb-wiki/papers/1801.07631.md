# Spin-1 particles and perturbative QCD

> Due to the angular condition in the light-front dynamics (LFD), the extraction of the electromagnetic form factors for spin-1 particles can be uniquely determined taking into account implicitly non-valence and/or the zero-mode contributions to the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current. No matter which matrix elements of the electromagnetic current is used to extract the electromagnetic form factors, the same unique result is obtained. As physical observables, the electromagnetic form factors obtained from matrix elements of the current in LFD must be equal to those obtained in the instant form calculations. Recently, the Babar collaboration \cite{Aubert2008} has analyzed the reaction $e^+ e^-\rightarrow ŌĀ^+ + ŌĀ^-$ at $\sqrt{s}=10.58 GeV$ to measure the cross section as well as the ratios of the helicity amplitudes $F_{őĽ'őĽ}$. We present our recent analysis of the Babar data for the rho meson considering the angular condition in LFD to put a stringent test on the onset of asymptotic perturbative QCD and predict the energy regime where the subleading contributions are still considerable.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of BaBar data previously performed by Dbeyssi (2012), whose parametrization was not fully consistent with pQCD and did not account for other reaction mechanisms needed to satisfy helicity conservation. The authors build on the light-front angular condition formalism developed by Inna (1984), Chung (1988), Frankfurt (1993), Hiller (1992), and Karmanov (1996), and on the universal ratio predictions for spin-1 form factors from Carlson (1984). The results enable future experimental tests at higher energies to verify whether őĪ and ő≤ converge to the asymptotic pQCD values of 1 and 2, providing a stringent test of the onset of perturbative QCD for spin-1 systems.
