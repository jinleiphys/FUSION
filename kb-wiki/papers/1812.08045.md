# Semi-inclusive jets recoiling from $γ_{dir}$ and $π^{0}$ triggers in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV in STAR

> We report the first measurement of fully corrected semi-inclusive charged recoil jets for both direct-photon ($γ_{dir}$) and $π^{0}$ triggers, within two trigger transverse energy ranges between 9 GeV and 15 GeV, in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV using data with an integrated luminosity of 13 $\rm nb^{-1}$ collected by the STAR experiment. A comparison between $π^{0}$-triggered recoil jets in p+p collisions and PYTHIA is discussed. A comparison is also presented between the suppression of $γ_{dir}$- and $π^{0}$-triggered recoil jets in central Au+Au collisions with respect to their corresponding PYTHIA references. A strong and similar level of suppression is observed in recoil-jet yields as a function of jet transverse momentum for $γ_{dir}$+jet and $π^{0}$+jet.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first fully corrected measurement of semi-inclusive charged recoil jets from both direct-photon and π⁰ triggers in central Au+Au collisions, finding a strong and similar level of suppression for both trigger types when compared to PYTHIA references, with the caveat that the direct-photon sample is an enriched sample with purity varying between 65% and 85% rather than a pure direct-photon sample.

## Method

The STAR experiment analyzed 0-15% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using data with 13 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity, along with p+p collisions at the same energy. Charged jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with radius R = 0.2 from charged particles with 0.2 < pT < 15 GeV/c within |η| < 1.0. The analysis used a Transverse Shower Profile method to discriminate between π⁰ and direct-photon triggers, event-mixing for uncorrelated background subtraction, and unfolding with response matrices from GEANT simulation (p+p) and fast simulation (Au+Au) to obtain fully corrected recoil-jet pT distributions.

## Key numbers

- Trigger transverse energy ranges: 9 < ET < 11 GeV and 11 < ET < 15 GeV for both direct-photon and π⁰ triggers
- Direct-photon purity: varies within 65%–85% for the trigger range 9 < ET < 20 GeV
- Charged jet radius: Rjet = 0.2
- Charged particle acceptance: 0.2 < pT < 15 GeV/c within |η| < 1.0
- Suppression level for π⁰+jet and hadron+jet: roughly 60%–70% between 10 < pT,jetch < 20 GeV/c for 9 < ET < 11 GeV
- Jet pT range studied: pT,jetch < 20 GeV/c

## Context

This measurement extends the STAR program of jet quenching studies in heavy-ion collisions, complementing previous hadron+jet analyses (STAR, 2017) by using direct-photon triggers which provide a cleaner constraint on the initial parton energy since direct photons do not interact strongly with the QGP. The comparison between direct-photon+jet and π⁰+jet suppression in the same kinematic region enables investigation of color factor and path length dependence of parton energy loss, as proposed theoretically by Wang (2001) and others. The results enable future studies of the redistribution of lost jet energy using larger jet radii (R = 0.5) and higher trigger energies (ET > 15 GeV).
