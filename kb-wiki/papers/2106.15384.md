# Questioning the wobbling interpretation of low-spin bands in $γ$-soft nuclei within the interacting boson-fermion model

> An alternative interpretation of the recently reported low-lying excited bands in $γ$-soft odd-mass nuclei as wobbling bands is presented in terms of the interacting boson-fermion model. The model Hamiltonian is determined based on the mean-field calculations with the nuclear energy density functionals. The predicted mixing ratios of the $Δ{I}=1$ electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole transition rates between yrast bands and those yrare bands previously interpreted as wobbling bands in $^{135}$Pr, $^{133}$La, $^{127}$Xe, and $^{105}$Pd are consistently smaller in magnitude than the experimental values on which the wobbling interpretation is based. These calculated mixing ratios indicate predominant magnetic character in agreement with the new experimental data. The earlier wobbling assignments are severely questioned.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low-spin excited bands previously interpreted as wobbling bands in the γ-soft odd-mass nuclei ¹³⁵Pr, ¹³³La, ¹²⁷Xe, and ¹⁰⁵Pd are not wobbling bands, because the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) predicts mixing ratios δ(E2/M1) for the ΔI=1 transitions between yrast and yrare bands that are consistently smaller in magnitude than the experimental values on which the wobbling interpretation was based, indicating predominant magnetic rather than electric character.

## Method

The authors constructed an IBFM Hamiltonian for each odd-mass nucleus by first performing constrained mean-field calculations (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with Gogny D1M or relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov with DD-PC1) to obtain potential energy surfaces for the even-even core nuclei, then mapping these onto an IBM-2 Hamiltonian via the boson coherent state formalism. The boson-fermion interaction parameters (Γ₀, Λ₀, A₀) were fitted to reproduce low-lying excitation energies, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the laboratory frame to obtain excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition properties for the negative-parity bands built on the 1h₁₁/₂ orbital.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁵Pr, the calculated δ values for B2 → Yrast transitions are close to zero, while the experimental |δ| values from Matta (2015) increase with spin; the updated data from Lv (2021) give smaller mixing ratios at I=17/2 and 21/2 that agree with the IBFM calculations.
- For ¹³³La, the IBFM δ values for B2 → Yrast transitions are much smaller in magnitude than the experimental values from Biswas (2019) that were the basis for the wobbling interpretation.
- For ¹²⁷Xe, the calculated |δ| for B2 → Yrast transitions is ≈5 for I<21/2, overestimating the earlier value δ=-1.7⁺⁰·⁴₋₀.₆ or -0.45±0.12 from Urban (1984) for the 13/2⁻₁→11/2⁻₁ decay.
- For ¹⁰⁵Pd, the calculated δ values for band B1 are smaller than the measured values from Timár (2019) by a factor of two to three, but agree with the mixing ratios from Rickey (1977) at I=17/2 and 21/2.

## Context

This work extends the IBFM framework of Scholten (1985) and Iachello (1979) to provide an alternative interpretation of low-spin bands that had been assigned as wobbling bands in γ-soft nuclei by Matta (2015), Biswas (2019), Chakraborty (2020), and Timár (2019) based on particle-rotor model calculations. The results contradict the wobbling assignments and support the new experimental data from Lv (2021) for ¹³⁵Pr and Rickey (1977) for ¹⁰⁵Pd, which show predominantly magnetic transitions. The paper enables future work to re-examine the nature of these bands using laboratory-frame calculations rather than intrinsic-frame particle-rotor models.
