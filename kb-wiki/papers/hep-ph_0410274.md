# Strong Decays of Baryons

> A Poincare-invariant description of mesonic baryon resonance decays is presented following the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics. In this contribution we focus on pionic decay modes. It is found that the theoretical results in general underestimate the experimental ones considerably. Furthermore, the problem of a nontrivial normalization factor appearing in the definition of the decay operator is investigated. The present results for decay widths suggest a normalization factor that is consistent with the choice adopted for the current operator in the studies of electroweak nucleon form factors.

# Strong Decays of Baryons

## Key claim

The paper presents a Poincaré-invariant description of pionic baryon resonance decays using the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics, but finds that the theoretical results in general underestimate the experimental decay widths considerably, with the degree of underestimation correlated with the magnitude of the Δπ branching ratio of each resonance.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant decay operator in the point-form spectator approximation (PFSA) with pseudovector coupling at the quark-pion vertex, using rest-frame baryon wave functions from relativistic Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) constituent quark models. The transition amplitude is defined in a Poincaré-invariant manner under overall momentum conservation, and the decay widths are calculated by integrating over phase space with the appropriate spin-isospin sums. The dependence of the results on an asymmetric normalization factor in the decay operator is also investigated.

## Key numbers

- For the GBE CQM, the N*_{1440} decay width is 33 MeV, compared to the experimental value of (227 ± 18)_{-59}^{+70} MeV (14% of the experimental width)
- For the OGE CQM, the N*_{1535} decay width is 119 MeV, compared to the experimental value of (67 ± 15)_{-17}^{+28} MeV (178% of the experimental width)
- For the Δ_{1232}, the GBE CQM gives 37 MeV and the OGE CQM gives 32 MeV, compared to the experimental value of (119 ± 1)_{-5}^{+5} MeV (31% and 27% of the experimental width, respectively)
- The N*_{1675} decay width is 5.4 MeV (GBE) and 6.6 MeV (OGE), compared to the experimental value of (68 ± 8)_{-4}^{+14} MeV (8% and 10% of the experimental width)
- The Δ_{1600} decay width is 0.07 MeV (GBE) and 1.8 MeV (OGE), compared to the experimental value of (61 ± 26)_{-10}^{+26} MeV (approximately 0% and 3% of the experimental width)
- The symmetric normalization factor (y = 0.5) is identified as the preferred choice, with y values above 0.5 causing some decay widths to exceed experimental ranges

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of baryon decays by Melde (2002) for the GBE and OGE CQMs, and is compared to Bethe-Salpeter results using instanton-induced dynamics by Metsch (2004). The PFSA approach had previously been applied successfully to electroweak nucleon form factors by Wagenbrunn (2000), Glozman (2001), and Boffi (2001), and the present study adopts the same normalization scheme for the decay operator. The results show a systematic underestimation of experimental decay widths that is more severe for resonances with larger Δπ branching ratios, suggesting the need for a more complete treatment of baryon resonances with realistic coupling to decay channels.
