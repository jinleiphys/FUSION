# Energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon in nuclear matter

> The nucleon form factors of the energy-momentum tensor are studied in nuclear medium in the framework of the in-medium modified Skyrme model. We obtain a negative D-term, in agreement with results from other approaches, and find that medium effects make the value of d_1 more negative.

## Key claim

The nucleon's energy-momentum tensor (EMT) form factors are studied in nuclear matter using an in-medium modified Skyrme model, and the authors find that medium effects make the value of the D-term form factor \(d_1\) more negative, while the stability condition of the Skyrmion is preserved.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(2) Skyrme model to homogeneous nuclear matter by introducing density-dependent functions \(\alpha_p(\rho)\), \(\alpha_s(\rho)\), and \(\gamma(\rho)\) that modify the pion decay constant, pion mass, and the Skyrme term coefficient, respectively. They then solve the classical soliton equation for the profile function \(F(r)\) at various nuclear matter densities \(\rho/\rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 = 0.5 m_\pi^3\)), compute the static energy-momentum tensor densities (energy, angular momentum, pressure, shear force), and obtain the EMT form factors \(M_2^*(t)\), \(J^*(t)\), and \(d_1^*(t)\) via Fourier transforms.

## Key numbers

*   In free space (\(\rho=0\)), the D-term constant is \(d_1 = -3.54\); at \(\rho=0.5\rho_0\) it becomes \(d_1^* = -4.30\); at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho=\rho_0\) it becomes \(d_1^* = -4.85\).
*   The energy density in the center of the nucleon in free space is \(T_{00}(0) = 1.45\ \mathrm{GeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\); at \(\rho=\rho_0\) it decreases to \(T_{00}^*(0) = 0.71\ \mathrm{GeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
*   The mean square radius of the energy density increases from \(\langle r_{00}^2\rangle = 0.68\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) in free space to \(\langle r_{00}^2\rangle^* = 0.95\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) at \(\rho=\rho_0\).
*   The dipole mass for the \(M_2^*(t)\) form factor is \(M_{M_2^*}^* = 0.840\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in free space, decreasing to \(0.702\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(\rho=\rho_0\).
*   The pressure in the center of the nucleon is \(p(0) = 0.26\ \mathrm{GeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) in free space and \(p^*(0) = 0.13\ \mathrm{GeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) at \(\rho=\rho_0\).
*   The position where the pressure changes sign increases from \(r_0 = 0.71\ \mathrm{fm}\) in free space to \(r_0^* = 0.90\ \mathrm{fm}\) at \(\rho=\rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends the previous Skyrme model calculation of nucleon EMT form factors in free space by Cebulla et al. (2007) to the nuclear medium, using the in-medium modified Skyrme model developed by Yakhshiev et al. (2010). The results are consistent with the negative sign of \(d_1\) found in lattice QCD (e.g., Mathur et al., 1999; Hagler et al., 2003), chiral quark-soliton models (Goeke et al., 2007), and other approaches, and they provide predictions for how these form factors change in nuclear matter that could be tested by future deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments on nuclear targets, such as those reported by Airapetian et al. (2009).
