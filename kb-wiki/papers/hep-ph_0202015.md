# Electromagnetic Form Factors of Nucleons in a Light-cone Diquark Model

> We investigate the electromagnetic form factors of nucleons within a simple relativistic quark spectator-diquark model using the light-cone formalism. Melosh rotations are applied to both quark and vector diquark. It is shown that the difference between vector and scalar spectator diquarks reproduces the right electric form factor of neutrons, and both the form factors $G_E(Q^2)$ and $G_M(Q^2)$ of the proton and neutron agree with experimental data well up to $Q^2=2 ~\rm{GeV}^2$ in this simple model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple relativistic quark spectator-diquark model formulated in the light-cone formalism can reproduce both the electric and magnetic form factors of the proton and neutron up to \(Q^2 = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), but the authors note that the harmonic oscillator wave functions used mean the model is expected to be valid essentially only up to that momentum transfer, even though they show results up to \(4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Method

The authors construct nucleon wave functions in the light-cone formalism by starting from the conventional SU(6) quark model and rewriting the proton and neutron states in a quark spectator-diquark form, with both scalar and vector diquark components mixed by an angle \(\theta\). Melosh rotations are applied to both the spin-1/2 quark and the spin-1 vector diquark to transform instant-form spinors into light-cone spinors, while the scalar diquark does not transform. The momentum-space wave functions are taken as harmonic oscillator (Brodsky-Huang-Lepage) forms, and the Dirac and Pauli form factors are computed from overlaps of light-cone wave functions using the Drell-Yan frame with \(q^+ = 0\).

## Key numbers

* Quark mass fixed at \(m_q = 0.220\ \mathrm{GeV}\), which is less than the non-relativistic quark model value of \(0.313\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Model parameters are fixed by six static electromagnetic properties: charge radii, magnetic radii, and magnetic moments of both proton and neutron.
* The ratio \(\mu_p/\mu_n = -1.46\) is cited as experimental evidence of SU(6) symmetry breaking.
* Form factors are shown to agree with experimental data up to \(Q^2 = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), with results also presented up to \(Q^2 = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* The dipole form \(G_D = 1/[1 + Q^2/0.71]^2\) is used for comparison with the proton magnetic form factor.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quark spectator-diquark model, originally proposed by Close (1973) for deep inelastic scattering and later applied by Ma (1991) to polarized deep inelastic scattering and valence quark asymmetry, to elastic electron-nucleon scattering. It contrasts with non-relativistic constituent quark models, which fail at higher momentum transfers, and with other relativistic approaches such as those of Schlumpf (1993), Cardarelli (1999), and Wagenbrunn (2001). The authors note that Melosh rotations alone are insufficient to produce the correct neutron charge radius, as found by Cardarelli (1999), and that the difference between scalar and vector diquark parameters is essential. The framework is stated to be applicable to nucleon-delta transitions and nucleon axial form factors.
