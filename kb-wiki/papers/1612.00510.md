# Anomalies in weak decays of H-like ions

> We investigate the emergence of oscillations in the decay law of unstable systems. We discuss in particular the case of the so-called GSI anomaly seen in the electron capture decays of H-like ions and prove that such oscillations cannot be explained by neutrino oscillations. We then discuss how such anomalies could be intimately related to the decay law of unstable systems in the case in which their spectral function deviates from a Breit-Wigner shape.

# Anomalies in weak decays of H-like ions

## Key claim

The oscillations observed in the electron capture decays of H-like ions at GSI cannot be explained by neutrino oscillations, but may arise from deviations of the spectral function from a Breit-Wigner shape, leading to a non-exponential decay law. The authors prove that even when neutrino mixing is included in the Lee-Hamiltonian formalism, the summed probability for mother and daughter states shows no time modulation.

## Method

The authors apply the Lee-Hamiltonian formalism, which is equivalent to quantum field theory at one-loop level, to describe the decay of H-like ions via electron capture into two neutrino mass eigenstates. They calculate the survival amplitude and the decay probability for the mother state, as well as the probability to measure the final state in a combination corresponding to an electron neutrino, and then sum over unobserved neutrino flavors. They also model non-exponential decay by introducing a cutoff Lambda in the Breit-Wigner energy distribution of the unstable system.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino mass-squared difference used is Delta m_21^2 = 7.6 x 10^(-5) eV^2, taken from Abe (2010).
- For a mother mass M_M = 132 GeV, the hypothetical oscillation period from neutrino mixing would be T = 4 pi M_M / Delta m_21^2 approximately 14 seconds.
- The measured oscillation period at GSI is T_measured approximately 8 seconds.
- The oscillating period in the non-exponential decay model scales as T approximately 1/Lambda, where Lambda is the cutoff in the energy distribution.

## Context

This work extends the debate on the GSI anomaly first reported in the GSI storage ring measurements, where oscillations in the decay law of H-like ions 140Pr and 142Pm were observed. The authors refute the interpretation by Ivanov linking the anomaly to neutrino oscillations, supporting the criticism by Merle. They instead build on their own prior work (Giacosa and Pagliara) proposing that the effect arises from deviations from exponential decay due to the measurement process, similar to short-time deviations observed in cold atom tunneling experiments by Reizen. The analysis enables future experimental verification of whether the oscillations are genuine and whether the microphysics of the storage ring measurement process must be better modeled.
