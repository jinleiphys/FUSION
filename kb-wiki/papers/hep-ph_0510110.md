# What do lattice baryonic susceptibilities tell us about quarks, diquarks and baryons at $T>Tc$?

> Lattice data on QCD thermodynamics, especially recent study of high order susceptibilities by UK-Bielefeld collaboration, have provided valuable information about matter properties around and above the critical temperature $T_c$. In this work we tried to understand what physical picture would explain these numerical data. We found two scenarios which will do it: (i) a quark quasiparticle gas, with the effective mass which is strongly $decreasing$ near the phase boundary into the QGP phase; or (ii) a picture including baryons at $T>T_c$, with the mass rapidly $increasing$ across the phase boundary toward QGP. We further provide several arguments in favor of the latter scenario, one of which is a natural continuity with the baryon gas picture at $T<T_c$.

## Key claim

The lattice data on high-order baryonic susceptibilities can be explained by two scenarios: (i) a quark quasiparticle gas with an effective mass that strongly decreases near the phase boundary into the QGP phase; or (ii) a picture including baryons at T > Tc with mass rapidly increasing across the phase boundary toward QGP. The authors argue in favor of the latter scenario based on natural continuity with the baryon gas picture at T < Tc, but both scenarios remain viable fits to the data.

## Method

The authors extracted quark quasiparticle mass parameters and their derivatives with respect to chemical potential from lattice data on susceptibilities d2, d4, and d6 from the UK-Bielefeld collaboration, using an iterative procedure that solves for M(T,mu=0), its second derivative, and its fourth derivative from the three susceptibility curves. They then compared this unconstrained quark gas model against direct lattice measurements of quark propagator masses and confinement constraints, and subsequently developed an alternative model incorporating diquark and baryon bound states with masses parameterized to interpolate between vacuum values at low T and 3Mq at high T.

## Key numbers

- The ratio d4/d2 is approximately 10 at T < Tc but drops close to 1 at T > Tc, suggesting B=1 objects dominate above Tc
- The ratio d6/d4 above Tc is a large negative number approximately -10, which cannot be interpreted as B^2 of any constituent
- Quark mass extracted from the unconstrained model ranges from about 1.7 Tc to 2.2 Tc in the region 1-2 Tc
- Direct lattice measurements give mq/T = 3.9 +/- 0.2 at 1.5 Tc and mq/T = 1.7 +/- 0.1 at 3.0 Tc
- The critical line parameter mu_c/Tc = 1.7 from experimental freezeout curves, compared to perturbative scaling value of approximately 3.63
- Baryons are bound until about T = 1.6 Tc according to potential model calculations
- The contribution of baryons to d4 is enhanced by a factor of 81 relative to pressure, and to d6 by a factor of 729

## Context

This work extends the strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) picture developed by Shuryak and Zahed (2004), who argued that meson-like bound states survive above Tc and that colored bound states should exist in the deconfined phase. The authors directly address the interpretation by Karsch (2005) that the ratio d4/d2 near 1 above Tc implies matter is a gas of B=1 objects with diquark contributions strongly restricted. The paper also builds on the BKS effect identified by Bluhm, Kampfer and Soff (2005), where derivatives of effective mass with respect to chemical potential can invalidate naive interpretations of susceptibility ratios as baryon number squared. The results enable future work on whether baryonic bound states or modified quark quasiparticles better describe the QGP phase near Tc, and suggest that higher-order susceptibilities provide a sensitive probe of the baryon number distribution in the deconfined phase.
