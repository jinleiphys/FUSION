# The Two-Boson-Exchange Correction to Parity-Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> We calculate the two-boson-exchange (TBE) corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry of the elastic electron-proton scattering in a simple hadronic model including the nucleon and the $Δ(1232)$ intermediate states. We find that $Δ$ contribution $δ_Δ$ is, in general, comparable with the nucleon contribution $δ_N$ and the current experimental measurements of strange-quark effects in the proton neutral weak current. The total TBE corrections to the current extracted values of $G^{s}_{E}+βG^{s}_{M}$ in recent experiments are found to lie in the range of $-7\sim +7%$.

## Key claim

The two-boson-exchange (TBE) corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering, including both nucleon and Δ(1232) intermediate states, are comparable in size to current experimental measurements of strange-quark effects; the total TBE corrections to extracted values of the strangeness form factor combination \(G^{s}_{E}+\beta G^{s}_{M}\) lie in the range of \(-7\%\) to \(+7\%\), but this result depends on the hadronic model and the specific kinematics of each experiment.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon-exchange (TPE) and \(\gamma Z\)-exchange corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) using a hadronic model with nucleon (\(N\)) and \(\Delta(1232)\) intermediate states. The photon and \(Z\)-boson couplings to the proton were taken as known, while the \(\Delta\) couplings were parameterized with form factors in dipole form (cut-off values 0.84 GeV for photon and 1.0 GeV for \(Z\)) and coupling constants determined from \(\gamma N \rightarrow \Delta\) and \(\nu N \rightarrow \mu \Delta\) data. The corrections were evaluated at two momentum transfers (\(Q^2 = 0.1\) and \(1.5\) GeV\(^2\)) as functions of the kinematic variable \(\epsilon\), and applied to re-analyze the strangeness form factors extracted by the HAPPEX, A4, and G0 experiments.

## Key numbers

- The total TBE correction to the extracted strangeness form factor \(G^{s}_{E}+\beta G^{s}_{M}\) ranges from \(-7.53\%\) to \(+6.74\%\) across the HAPPEX, A4, and G0 experimental kinematics (see Table 1: \(\delta_G = +6.74\%\) for HAPPEX at \(Q^2=0.477\) GeV\(^2\), \(\epsilon=0.974\); \(\delta_G = -7.53\%\) for HAPPEX at \(Q^2=0.109\) GeV\(^2\), \(\epsilon=0.994\); \(\delta_G = -4.26\%\) and \(-4.82\%\) for A4 at \(Q^2=0.23\) and \(0.108\) GeV\(^2\); \(\delta_G = -1.70\%\) and \(-0.10\%\) for G0 at \(Q^2=0.262\) and \(0.410\) GeV\(^2\)).
- The nucleon contribution \(\delta_N\) ranges from \(0.25\%\) to \(1.30\%\) for the same experimental points; the \(\Delta\) contribution \(\delta_\Delta\) ranges from \(-0.65\%\) to \(+1.90\%\).
- At \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), \(\delta_\Delta\) is nearly zero and negative at small \(\epsilon\), turning positive around \(\epsilon \sim 0.8\); at \(Q^2 = 1.5\) GeV\(^2\), \(\delta_\Delta\) is comparable to \(\delta_N\) only at larger \(\epsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the parton-model calculation of the two-photon-exchange correction by Afanasev et al. (2005), which was reliable only at large \(Q^2\) and did not include the \(\gamma Z\)-exchange diagram, by using a hadronic model applicable at the lower \(Q^2\) values of current experiments (SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, G0). The results show that the \(\Delta(1232)\) intermediate state contribution is comparable to the nucleon contribution and to the strange-quark effects being measured, and that the net correction to extracted strangeness form factors is only a few percent after removing the earlier \(\gamma Z\)-exchange estimates of Marciano (1999). This raises the question of contributions from higher resonances, which would need to be included for more precise extractions.
