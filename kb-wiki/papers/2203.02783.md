# Entanglement and correlations in fast collective neutrino flavor oscillations

> Collective neutrino oscillations play a crucial role in transporting lepton flavor in astrophysical settings like supernovae and neutron star binary merger remnants, which are characterized by large neutrino densities. In these settings, simulations in the mean-field approximation show that neutrino-neutrino interactions can overtake vacuum oscillations and give rise to fast collective flavor evolution on time-scales $t\proptoμ^{-1}$, with $μ$ proportional to the local neutrino density. In this work, we study the full out-of-equilibrium flavor dynamics in simple multi-angle geometries displaying fast oscillations in the mean field linear stability analysis. Focusing on simple initial conditions, we analyze the production of pair correlations and entanglement in the complete many-body-dynamics as a function of the number $N$ of neutrinos in the system, for up to thousands of neutrinos. Similarly to simpler geometries with only two neutrino beams, we identify three regimes: stable configurations with vanishing flavor oscillations, marginally unstable configurations with evolution occurring on long time scales $τ\approxμ^{-1}\sqrt{N}$, and unstable configurations showing flavor evolution on short time scales $τ\approxμ^{-1}\log(N)$. We present evidence that these fast collective modes are generated by the same dynamical phase transition which leads to the slow bipolar oscillations, establishing a connection between these two phenomena and explaining the difference in their time scales. We conclude by discussing a semi-classical approximation which reproduces the entanglement entropy at short to medium time scales and can be potentially useful in situations with more complicated geometries where classical simulation methods starts to become inefficient.

## Key claim

The paper shows that fast collective neutrino flavor oscillations in multi-angle geometries arise from the same dynamical phase transition that produces slow bipolar oscillations, with the difference in time scales explained by the presence or absence of a mean-field instability: unstable configurations evolve on short time scales \(\tau\approx\mu^{-1}\log(N)\), while marginally unstable configurations evolve on long time scales \(\tau\approx\mu^{-1}\sqrt{N}\), and stable configurations show vanishing flavor oscillations in the large-\(N\) limit.

## Method

The authors studied the full many-body quantum dynamics of a three-beam neutrino system with only neutrino-neutrino forward scattering, using an angular momentum representation that allows simulations for up to \(N=2700\) neutrinos. They performed a linear stability analysis of the mean-field equations to classify configurations as stable, marginally unstable, or unstable, then computed the exact time evolution of flavor survival probabilities, pair correlations, and entanglement entropy for two distinct initial flavor configurations (setup I and setup II) as a function of the angular parameter \(c=\cos(\theta_{AC})\) and system size \(N\).

## Key numbers

* For unstable configurations in setup II with \(c=-1/2\), the maximum growth rate of the unstable mode is \(\sqrt{2}\mu/6\).
* For unstable configurations in setup II, the flavor evolution time scale is \(\tau\approx\mu^{-1}\log(N)\).
* For marginally unstable configurations (setup I with \(c=1\)), the equilibration time scale to reach full mixing (\(P_A=0.5\)) is \(\tau\approx2\mu^{-1}\sqrt{N/3}\), matching the two-beam result \(\tau_{AC}\approx 3/\sqrt{2}\,\mu^{-1}\sqrt{N/3}\).
* For stable configurations in setup I with \(c\neq1\), the survival probability at the first minimum scales as \(\mathrm{P}^{(min)}_{A_i}(N) \approx 1 - \frac{a}{N}\left(1-\frac{b}{\sqrt{N}}\right)\), with \(a\) ranging from \(\approx13\) at \(c=0\) to \(\approx45\) at \(c=0.75\).
* The quadratic discriminant for instability in setup II is \(\Delta = 72c(c+1)\), giving instability when \(-1<c<0\).

## Context

This work extends the many-body treatment of collective neutrino oscillations from two-beam models (Roggero 2021, Friedland 2006) to multi-angle geometries that exhibit fast flavor instabilities in mean-field linear stability analysis (Izaguirre 2017, Capozzi 2017). It establishes a connection between fast collective modes and the dynamical phase transition responsible for slow bipolar oscillations, explaining their different time scales. The semi-classical approximation for entanglement entropy at short to medium times may enable simulations of more complicated geometries where classical methods become inefficient.
