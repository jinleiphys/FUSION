# Entropy Production at RHIC

> For central heavy ion collisions at the RHIC energy, the entropy per unit rapidity dS/dy at freeze-out is extracted with minimal model dependence from available experimental measurements of particle yields, spectra, and source sizes estimated from two-particle interferometry. The extracted entropy rapidity density is consistent with lattice gauge theory results for a thermalized quark-gluon plasma with an energy density estimated from transverse energy production at RHIC.

## Key claim

The final-state entropy per unit rapidity extracted from RHIC data is consistent with lattice QCD predictions for a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, but the small pionic source sizes from HBT remain a puzzle that may indicate the dynamics of expansion and decoupling differ from current hydrodynamic descriptions.

## Method

The authors extracted the entropy per unit rapidity dS/dy at freeze-out for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV by combining measured particle yields and transverse momentum spectra with Gaussian source-size parameters from two-particle (HBT) interferometry. For pions, the HBT radii were taken directly from PHENIX measurements; for antiprotons and kaons, source radii were inferred via coalescence arguments using measured yields of deuterons and phi mesons respectively. The entropy was then calculated using the Landau quasi-particle approximation for the phase-space density, integrating over transverse momentum for each hadron species.

## Key numbers

* Total entropy per unit rapidity at freeze-out: dS/dy = 4451 for ~11% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV, with estimated systematic uncertainty ~10%
* Pion entropy: dS/dy|π⁻ = 680, with pion HBT radius R_inv ≈ 6.3 fm
* Antiproton entropy: dS/dy|p̅ = 118, with antiproton R_inv = 5.34 fm (constant with K_T)
* Kaon entropy: dS/dy|K⁻ = 256, dS/dy|K⁺ = 286, with R_inv scaling as A/√m_T where A=4.45 fm GeV^{1/2} for K⁻ and 4.74 for K⁺
* Initial energy density estimate: ε̅(τ=1 fm/c) = 4.5 GeV/fm³ from dE_T/dy ≈ 542 GeV and πR²=120 fm²
* Lattice QCD estimate for 3-flavor QGP entropy at τ=1 fm/c: dS/dy|_QGP ≈ 2922, corresponding to initial temperature T ≈ 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Siemens and Kapusta (1979) for relating entropy to coalescence ratios, and applies it to RHIC data to test whether the initial state is a thermalized quark-gluon plasma. The extracted entropy is consistent with lattice QCD calculations at zero baryon density, but the small HBT radii contradict hydrodynamic models (Soff, Teaney, Heinz, Kolb, 2001-2003) that overpredict source sizes while matching spectra. The result enables future work to constrain the amount of entropy generated during the expansion and to test whether the initial state might have lower entropy due to incomplete thermalization or classical gluon field configurations.
