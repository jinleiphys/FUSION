# Shell-model Hamiltonians from Density Functional Theory

> The density functional theory of nuclear structure provides a many-particle wave function that is useful for static properties, but an extension of the theory is necessary to describe correlation effects or other dynamic properties. Here we propose a procedure to extend the theory by mapping the properties of the self-consistent mean-field Hamiltonian onto an effective shell-model Hamiltonian with two-body interactions. In this initial study, we consider the sd-shell nuclei Ne-20, Mg-24, Si-28, and Ar-36. Our first application is in the framework of the USD shell-model Hamiltonian, using its mean-field approximation to construct an effective Hamiltonian and partially recover correlation effects. We find that more than half of the correlation energy is due to the quadrupole interaction. We then follow a similar procedure but using the SLy4 Skyrme energy functional as our starting point and truncating the space to the spherical $sd$ shell. The constructed shell-model Hamiltonian is found to satisfy minimal consistency requirements to reproduce the properties of the mean-field solution. The quadrupolar correlation energies computed with the mapped Hamiltonian are reasonable compared with those computed by other methods.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a procedure to map a self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) density functional theory onto an effective shell-model Hamiltonian with two-body interactions, but this is presented as an initial study limited to sd-shell nuclei and the mapping is tested only for quadrupolar correlations, with the caveat that the constructed Hamiltonian satisfies only minimal consistency requirements to reproduce mean-field properties.

## Method

The authors first construct a spherical single-particle basis from the spherical SCMF solution, then express the deformed mean-field ground-state density matrix and single-particle Hamiltonian in this spherical basis using the transformation matrix U of overlaps between deformed and spherical orbitals. They decompose the single-particle Hamiltonian into multipoles, keeping only the monopole (K=0) and quadrupole (K=2) terms, and construct an effective Hamiltonian of the form H = h^(0) - (1/2) g h^(2) · h^(2). The coupling strength g is fine-tuned so that the deformation energy (difference between deformed and spherical mean-field energies) of the mapped Hamiltonian matches that of the original SCMF theory, using Hartree-Fock calculations in the truncated spherical sd-shell space.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Brown and Richter (1998) to use SCMF to determine the single-particle Hamiltonian of the configuration-interaction shell model (CISM), going further by using the SCMF to also determine parts of the two-body interaction Hamiltonian. The mapping procedure is tested first using the USD Hamiltonian (Wildenthal 1984) as a benchmark, then applied to the SLy4 Skyrme energy functional (Chabanat et al. 1998). The approach aims to enable systematic calculation of correlation energies missing from SCMF theories, which currently have rms residual errors of about 1 MeV for masses (Goriely et al.) or 1.7 MeV for the SLy4 functional (Bender et al. 2005), and to provide a foundation for constructing shell-model Hamiltonians suitable for shell model Monte Carlo calculations of nuclear level densities (Nakada et al. 1997, Alhassid et al. 1999, 2003).
