# Nucleon Emision Off Nuclei Induced By Neutrino Interactions

> We make a review of the main nuclear effects that affect neutrino-nucleus cross sections. We discuss how the different models in the literature try to describe these different effects, and thus try to compare between them. We focus on the quasi-elastic reaction in the neutrino energy region of around 1 GeV, where recent data from MiniBoone are available. Among the issues discussed are the different treatment of medium corrections to initial and nal state nucleon wave functions and the problem of the rescattering of ejected nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper reviews nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus cross sections, arguing that simple Fermi Gas models are insufficient for precision neutrino oscillation experiments, but the authors hedge that their own Valencia-Granada model, which includes realistic spectral functions and RPA correlations, can describe inclusive quasi-elastic data without unphysical parameters, though the model is validated primarily against electron scattering data.

## Method

The authors review nuclear effects in inclusive charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering at energies around 1 GeV, using a local density Fermi Gas model improved with realistic nucleon spectral functions (including both particle and hole self-energies) and RPA correlations via an effective Landau-Migdal interaction with explicit pion and rho meson exchange. For semi-inclusive observables (nucleon emission), they implement a Monte Carlo cascade model that simulates nucleon rescattering through the nucleus, including in-medium renormalization of nucleon-nucleon cross sections and Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

- The model describes electron-carbon scattering data for the inclusive process \(e^- + ^{12}C \to e^- + X\), showing a large broad quasi-elastic peak at low transferred energy \(\omega\) and a second lower peak associated with \(\Delta(1232)\) production.
- For charged current processes on argon, the cascade model shows a reduction of high-energy proton flux and production of many secondary nucleons (including neutrons) at lower kinetic energies, as shown in Fig. 3 for \(^{40}Ar(\nu,\mu^-+N)\) and \(^{40}Ar(\bar{\nu},\mu^++N)\) cross sections.
- RPA correlations reduce the cross section, especially at low \(Q^2\) kinematics, and improve description of MiniBoone data without introducing unphysical parameters like effective Fermi momentum.

## Context

This work extends the Valencia-Granada model, originally developed for electron scattering (Gil 1997), to neutrino-nucleus interactions, contrasting with simpler Fermi Gas models like the Marteau model (1999) widely used in oscillation experiments. The review discusses alternative approaches including distorted wave impulse approximation and transport models from the Giessen group (Leitner 2006), and enables improved analysis of neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBoone and T2K by providing more accurate cross section models that account for medium corrections and nucleon rescattering.
