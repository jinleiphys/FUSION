# Effective field theory for deformed atomic nuclei

> We present an effective field theory (EFT) for a model-independent description of deformed atomic nuclei. In leading order this approach recovers the well-known results from the collective model by Bohr and Mottelson. When higher-order corrections are computed, the EFT accounts for finer details such as the variation of the moment of inertia with the band head and the small magnitudes of interband $E2$ transitions. For rotational bands with a finite spin of the band head, the EFT is equivalent to the theory of a charged particle on the sphere subject to a magnetic monopole field.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory (EFT) for deformed atomic nuclei that recovers the collective model of Bohr and Mottelson at leading order, but the abstract glosses over the condition that the EFT applies specifically to emergent symmetry breaking from rotational SO(3) to axial SO(2) symmetry, and that the approach is limited to low-lying states below a breakdown energy scale Λ.

## Method

The authors construct an EFT based on the coset space SO(3)/SO(2) isomorphic to the two-sphere, using Nambu-Goldstone fields ψ_x and ψ_y that parameterize the tangent plane and rotation angles (θ, φ) that describe overall rotations. The effective Hamiltonian is built from rotationally invariant combinations of these degrees of freedom and their derivatives, ordered by a power-counting scheme based on two energy scales: the rotational energy scale ξ and the vibrational energy scale Ω. The Legendre transformation of the effective Lagrangian yields a quantized Hamiltonian where rotational and vibrational degrees of freedom are coupled through a gauge field that takes the form of a magnetic monopole vector potential.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁶⁸Er: head of the k = 2 band at 821 keV, excited k = 0 band at 1217 keV, so 2ω₀ ≈ 1 MeV; splitting between the two states is about 40% of 2ω₀; rotational excitation energies less than ξ ≈ 80 keV so ξ/Ω ≈ 1/10; moments of inertia for ground-state band r⁻² ≈ 27 keV, for k = 2 band r⁻² ≈ 25 keV, for k = 0 band r⁻² ≈ 20 keV
- In ¹⁶²Dy: excited k = 2 vibrational state at about 888 keV, lowest k = 0 excited state at about 1400 keV, so 2ω₀ ≈ 1.1 MeV; splitting of k = 0 and k = 2 states amounts to almost 50% of 2ω₀; moments of inertia for ground-state band r⁻² ≈ 27 keV, for k = 2 band r⁻² ≈ 25 keV, for k = 0 band r⁻² ≈ 18 keV
- The leading-order Hamiltonian yields degenerate k = 0 and |k| = 2 vibrational bands at energy 2ω₀; this degeneracy is lifted by higher-order terms

## Context

This work extends the collective model of Bohr and Mottelson by providing a systematic, model-independent EFT framework based on symmetry principles alone. The EFT accounts for deviations from the rigid-rotor picture, such as the variation of the moment of inertia with vibrational band head (addressed in Zhang (2013)) and the suppression of inter-band E2 transitions (addressed in Coello (2015)), which traditional collective models overpredict by factors of 2–10 as noted by Rowe (2009). The approach enables quantified theoretical uncertainties through Bayesian statistics and suggests that certain intra-band B(E2) transition data may need remeasurement or reevaluation.
