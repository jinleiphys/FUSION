# Finite-volume and partial quenching effects in the magnetic polarizability of the neutron

> There has been much progress in the experimental measurement of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon. Similarly, lattice QCD simulations have recently produced dynamical QCD results for the magnetic polarizability of the neutron approaching the chiral regime. In order to compare the lattice simulations with experiment, calculation of partial quenching and finite-volume effects is required prior to an extrapolation in quark mass to the physical point. These dependencies are described using chiral effective field theory. Corrections to the partial quenching effects associated with the sea-quark-loop electric charges are estimated by modelling corrections to the pion cloud. These are compared to the uncorrected lattice results. In addition, the behaviour of the finite-volume corrections as a function of pion mass is explored. Box sizes of approximately 7 fm are required to achieve a result within 5% of the infinite-volume result at the physical pion mass. A variety of extrapolations are shown at different box sizes, providing a benchmark to guide future lattice QCD calculations of the magnetic polarizabilities. A relatively precise value for the physical magnetic polarizability of the neutron is presented, beta_n = 1.93(11)stat(8)sys x 10^-4 fm^3, which is in agreement with current experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a value for the magnetic polarizability of the neutron, β_n = 1.93(11)_stat(8)_sys × 10⁻⁴ fm³, which agrees with current experimental results, but this result depends on modeling corrections for partial quenching effects (missing photon couplings to disconnected sea-quark loops in the lattice QCD simulations) using chiral effective field theory with a specific regulator parameter of Λ = 0.80 GeV.

## Method

The authors applied chiral effective field theory (χEFT) with finite-range regularization to analyze 2+1 flavour lattice QCD results from the PACS-CS configurations, using the Sommer scale parameter r₀ = 0.49 fm. They calculated partial quenching corrections by decomposing pion-loop and Δ-baryon-loop contributions into valence-valence, valence-sea, and sea-sea quark-flow sectors using a diagrammatic method, then corrected the partially quenched lattice results to full QCD by restoring the missing disconnected sea-quark-loop contributions. Finite-volume corrections were evaluated by replacing continuum loop integrals with finite sums over lattice momenta, and chiral extrapolations were performed using a dipole regulator with Λ = 0.80 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The final physical magnetic polarizability of the neutron: β_n = 1.93(11)_stat(8)_sys × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Box sizes of approximately 7 fm are required to achieve a result within 5% of the infinite-volume result at the physical pion mass
- The partially quenched value at the physical point is 1.66 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, giving a 16% correction when unquenching to full QCD
- The fit coefficient for the term linear in m_π² is a₂ = 6.5 × 10⁻⁷ fm⁵
- The regulator parameter adopted is Λ = 0.80 GeV, consistent with an intrinsic scale of Λ̄_scale = 0.99(27) GeV from weighted averages across studies of nucleon mass, magnetic moment, and electric charge radius
- All lattice points used satisfy m_πL > 4.45, ensuring finite-volume χEFT is appropriate
- The mass splitting between Δ baryon and nucleon is Δ = 292 MeV; the pion decay constant is f_π = 92.4 MeV; the axial coupling is g_A = 1.267; the Δ coupling is C = -1.52
- Unquenching the strange sector via kaon loops gives a shift of approximately 0.16%

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of the neutron magnetic polarizability by Primer (2012, 2013) using PACS-CS configurations from Aoki (2008), connecting them to experimental results from Kossert (2002), Griesshammer (2012), and the Particle Data Group (Beringer, 2012). The partial quenching corrections build on the graded-symmetry approach of Detmold (2006) and the quark-flow diagram method of Leinweber (2002), while the finite-volume correction method follows Hall (2010, 2011, 2012). The results provide benchmark extrapolation curves at different box sizes to guide future lattice QCD calculations of magnetic polarizabilities, particularly for simulations that will include background field effects in the disconnected sea-quark loop sector.
