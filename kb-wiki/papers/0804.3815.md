# Nuclear Electric Dipole Moment of 3He

> A permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) of a physical system requires time-reversal (T) and parity (P) violation. Experimental programs are currently pushing the limits on EDMs in atoms, nuclei, and the neutron to regimes of fundamental theoretical interest. Here we calculate the magnitude of the PT-violating EDM of 3He and the expected sensitivity of such a measurement to the underlying PT-violating interactions. Assuming that the coupling constants are of comparable magnitude for pi-, rho-, and omega-exchanges, we find that the pion-exchange contribution dominates. Our results suggest that a measurement of the 3He EDM is complementary to the planned neutron and deuteron experiments, and could provide a powerful constraint for the theoretical models of the pion-nucleon PT-violating interaction.

## Key claim

Assuming that the coupling constants for pi-, rho-, and omega-exchanges are of comparable magnitude, the pion-exchange contribution dominates the PT-violating electric dipole moment of 3He; a measurement of the 3He EDM would be complementary to planned neutron and deuteron experiments and could provide a powerful constraint for theoretical models of the pion-nucleon PT-violating interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body problem using an ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) framework, diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis in relative coordinates. They used several potential models for the P,T-conserving nuclear interaction, including the Argonne v18, CD Bonn, and chiral EFT two- and three-nucleon interactions, and evaluated the polarization contribution to the EDM using Podolsky's method implemented via the Lanczos algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar coefficient for the one-body contribution (proportional to d_p + d_n) ranges from 0.415 e fm (Argonne v18) to 0.437 e fm (EFT NN only); the isovector coefficient (proportional to d_p - d_n) ranges from -0.462 e fm (Argonne v18) to -0.468 e fm (CD Bonn and EFT).
- The polarization contribution from pion exchange, in units of e fm, is: 0.015 for isoscalar (bar G_pi^0), 0.023 for isovector (bar G_pi^1), and 0.036 for isotensor (bar G_pi^2), using the EFT NN+NNN interaction.
- For rho exchange, the polarization coefficients are at most 0.0013 e fm in magnitude (isovector bar G_rho^1 with EFT NN); for omega exchange, the largest coefficient is -0.0018 e fm (isovector bar G_omega^1 with EFT NN+NNN).
- The total 3He EDM, assuming pion-exchange dominance and using the chiral-loop estimate for nucleon EDMs, is (0.024 bar G_pi^0 + 0.023 bar G_pi^1 + 0.027 bar G_pi^2) e fm.
- The electric polarizability of 3He calculated with Argonne v18 is 0.183 fm^3; with the two- and three-body chiral EFT interactions it is 0.148 fm^3.

## Context

This work extends earlier approximate calculations of the 3He EDM by Avishai (1986) using the Reid soft-core potential, providing much more reliable results based on exact solutions of the three-body problem with several modern potential models. The results enable a comparison with the neutron EDM and the deuteron EDM calculated by Liu (2004), showing that combined measurements of all three systems could constrain the three PT-violating pion-nucleon coupling constants. The authors strongly encourage experimentalists to consider a 3He EDM measurement in a storage ring, in addition to the existing deuteron proposal by Semertzidis (2003).
