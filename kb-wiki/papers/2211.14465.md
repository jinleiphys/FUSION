# Effective Chiral Magnetic Effect from Neutrino Radiation

> We develop an approach to chiral kinetic theories for electrons close to equilibrium and neutrinos away from equilibrium based on a systematic power counting scheme for different timescales of electromagnetic and weak interactions. Under this framework, we derive electric and energy currents along magnetic fields induced by neutrino radiation in general nonequilibrium states. This may be regarded as an effective chiral magnetic effect (CME), which is present without a chiral chemical potential, unlike the conventional CME. We also consider the so-called gain region of core-collapse supernovae as an example and find that the effective CME enhanced by persistent neutrino emission in time is sufficiently large to lead to the inverse cascade of magnetic and fluid kinetic energies and observed magnitudes of pulsar kicks. Our framework may also be applicable to other dense-matter systems involving nonequilibrium neutrinos.

# Effective Chiral Magnetic Effect from Neutrino Radiation

## Key claim
The authors derive an effective chiral magnetic effect (CME) driven by neutrino radiation that is present even without a chiral chemical potential, unlike the conventional CME. This effect arises from purely nonequilibrium interactions and is enhanced by persistent neutrino emission over time, potentially providing a dominant contribution compared to previous results that were suppressed in the nonrelativistic expansion for nucleons.

## Method
The authors develop chiral kinetic theories for electrons close to equilibrium and neutrinos away from equilibrium, incorporating a systematic power counting scheme for the different timescales of electromagnetic and weak interactions. They derive electric and energy currents along magnetic fields induced by neutrino radiation in general nonequilibrium states, using the collision term for neutrino absorption on nucleons and its inverse process within the gain region of core-collapse supernovae.

## Key numbers
- Electron fraction in the gain region: Y_e ≈ 0.4, mass density ρ_gain ∼ 10^10 g·cm^−3, temperature T ∼ 10^11 K ≈ 8.6 MeV
- Chemical potentials: μ_e ≈ 0.79 MeV, μ_n ≈ 870 MeV, μ_p ≈ 867 MeV
- Neutrino spectral parameters: α = 2.65, average energy ε̄ = 13.05 MeV
- Effective chiral magnetic conductivity for electric current: ξ_B ≈ 2.2 MeV, total ξ_B^tot ≈ −0.5 MeV for x_0 = 0.1 s
- Effective chiral magnetic conductivity for energy current: κ_B ≈ 350 MeV^2, total κ_B^tot ≈ 780 MeV^2 for x_0 = 0.1 s
- Resulting magnetic field strength via chiral plasma instability: ∼ 10^16 G
- Estimated pulsar kick velocity: v_kick = 100–1000 km/s, comparable to observed magnitudes

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the chiral magnetic effect in core-collapse supernovae by Charbonneau (2009), Akamatsu (2013), Ohnishi (2014), Kaminski (2014), and Yamamoto (2015), which considered chiral imbalance from weak interactions. It goes beyond the near-equilibrium neutrino treatments of Yamamoto (2021) and Matsumoto (2022) by deriving the effective CME for neutrinos far from equilibrium. The results suggest that the inverse cascade of magnetic and fluid kinetic energies can occur in the gain region of supernovae, contrasting with conventional neutrino radiation hydrodynamics without chiral effects that shows direct cascade in three spatial dimensions as studied by Hanke (2011), Takiwaki (2013), and Radice (2017). The framework may also be applicable to neutron star mergers and early Universe cosmology.
