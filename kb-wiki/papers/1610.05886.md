# Quark Mass Correction to Chiral Separation Effect and Pseudoscalar Condensate

> We derived an analytic structure of the quark mass correction to chiral separation effect (CSE) in small mass regime. We confirmed this structure by a D3/D7 holographic model study in a finite density, finite magnetic field background. The quark mass correction to CSE can be related to correlators of pseudo-scalar condensate, quark number density and quark condensate in static limit. We found scaling relations of these correlators with spatial momentum in the small momentum regime. They characterize medium responses to electric field, inhomogeneous quark mass and chiral shift. Beyond the small momentum regime, we found existence of normalizable mode, which possibly leads to formation of spiral phase. The normalizable mode exists beyond a critical magnetic field, whose magnitude decreases with quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

The quark mass correction to the chiral separation effect (CSE) can be expressed analytically in the small mass regime through a relation to correlators of pseudoscalar condensate, quark number density, and quark condensate; the correction is quadratic in quark mass and suppressed by temperature, with the dimensionless prefactor dropping as chemical potential and magnetic field increase.

## Method

The authors used the D3/D7 holographic model in a finite temperature, finite density, and finite magnetic field background to compute Euclidean correlators among the pseudoscalar condensate, quark condensate, and quark number density at vanishing frequency and finite spatial momentum. They solved the coupled equations of motion for fluctuations of the D7 brane embedding and worldvolume gauge field numerically from the horizon to the boundary, extracting the correlators from the asymptotic coefficients via holographic renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The standard CSE current is \(J_R^3 = \frac{N_f N_c}{(2\pi)^2} \mu_q \tilde{B}\) in the massless limit, fixed entirely by anomaly.
- The mass correction to CSE scales as \(\Delta J_R = \# \frac{M_q^2}{T^2} \mu_q \tilde{B}\), with the prefactor \(\#\) negative and its magnitude decreasing with increasing \(\mu_q\) and \(\tilde{B}\).
- The dimensionless ratio \(r = -\frac{\sigma_5}{\mathcal{N} E \cdot B r_0^4}\) approaches a constant in the limit \(k \to 0\); at \(\mu=0\) it increases with quark mass \(m\) and decreases with chemical potential \(\mu\).
- The correlator \(G_{\sigma_5 \sigma}\) vanishes approximately linearly in both \(\mu\) and \(m\), consistent with \(\sigma_5 = 2\# \frac{M_q \mu_q}{T^2} \tilde{\mathbf{B}} \cdot \nabla M_q\).
- The correlator \(G_{\sigma_5 \sigma_5}\) scales as \(O(k^2)\) at small momentum.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral separation effect beyond the massless limit, where CSE is fixed by anomaly (Metlitski 2005, Son 2004), by providing the first analytic structure of the quark mass correction. It confirms and generalizes earlier discussions of mass corrections to CSE (Gorbar 2013, Chen 2013, Fang 2016, Kirilin 2013) using holographic methods. The results enable quantitative predictions for the response of pseudoscalar condensate to electric fields, inhomogeneous quark mass, and chiral shift in quark-gluon plasma, and identify a normalizable mode beyond a critical magnetic field that may lead to spiral phase formation, extending the instability discussed by Kharzeev (2011) and Brauner (2016) to the deconfined phase.
