# In-Medium Pion Properties from Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Two-point functions related to the pion weak decay constant $f_π$ are calculated from the generating functional of chiral perturbation theory in the mean field approximation and the heavy-baryon limit. The aim is to demonstrate that Lorentz invariance is violated in the presence of background matter. This fact manifests itself in the splitting of both $f_π$ and the pion mass into uncorrelated time- and space-like parts. We emphasize the different in-medium renormalizations of the correlation functions, show the inequivalence between the in-medium values of $f_π$ deduced from Walecka-type models, on the one hand, and QCD sum rules, on the other hand, and elaborate on the importance for some nuclear physics observables.

## Key claim

Lorentz invariance is violated in nuclear matter, causing the pion weak decay constant and the pion mass to split into uncorrelated time-like and space-like parts; the in-medium value of \(f_\pi\) depends on which two-point function is used for its definition, and the choice must respect the Ward identities rooted in current quark dynamics.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium axial-current–pseudoscalar (AP), axial-current–axial-current (AA), and pseudoscalar–pseudoscalar (PP) two-point functions from the generating functional of chiral perturbation theory (ChPTh) to order \({\cal O}(Q^2)\), using the mean-field approximation and the heavy-baryon formulation. Background matter was introduced by replacing the nucleon scalar density \(\bar N N\) in the effective Lagrangian with the matter density \(\rho\). The in-medium correlation functions were then obtained by taking functional derivatives of the generating functional with respect to the appropriate sources.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\), the time-like pion mass squared \(m_\pi^{*2}\) is enhanced by approximately \(8\%\) over its vacuum value \(m_\pi^2\).
* For a purely space-like pion momentum at \(\rho_0\), the mass pole would be at \(m_\pi^{*2} \approx -4 m_\pi^2\).
* The low-energy constants are \(c_2 + c_3 = -0.27\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(c_3 = -0.55\ \mathrm{fm}\); the pion–nucleon sigma term is \(\sigma_N = 0.228\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
* The in-medium weak decay constant from the AP correlator is \(f_\pi^{\rm AP}(\rho) = f_\pi (1 - \sigma_N \rho / f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2)\).
* The time-like in-medium decay constant from the GOR relation is \(f_\pi^{(t)} = f_\pi \sqrt{1 + 2(c_2 + c_3)\rho / f_\pi^2}\).
* For the Migdal pion field at \(\rho_0\), the space-like decay constant \(f_\pi^P \approx (1/4) f_\pi\) and the time-like decay constant \(f_\pi^S \approx 0.9 f_\pi\).
* The ratio of the two-body axial charge operator amplitude at nuclear matter density to its vacuum value is \(C_{\rm MEC}^{\rho=\rho_0} / C_{\rm MEC}^{\rho=0} \approx 1.15\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of in-medium pion properties by Kirchbach and Wirzba (1996), showing that the in-medium values of \(f_\pi\) extracted from Walecka-type models and from QCD sum rules are inequivalent. The results contradict the naive use of the Goldberger–Treiman relation in medium and instead require consistent evaluation of in-medium couplings via QCD sum rules, as done by Reinders (1984). The findings enable a proper calculation of the density dependence of the two-body axial charge operator, which is relevant for understanding the acceleration of isovector first-forbidden weak decays in heavy nuclei.
