# Constraints on the Equation of State of Nuclear Matter from Systematically Comparing SMASH Calculations to HADES Data

> We aim to constrain the equation of state of nuclear matter by comparing calculations with the SMASH transport model to directed and elliptic flow measurements for protons and deuterons performed by the HADES collaboration in a systematic way. A momentum-dependent term is included in the potential for which we show that it is needed to describe flow data. We further incorporate a simple symmetry potential in the transport model and present constraints on the stiffness of the equation of state of nuclear matter at saturation density and on the symmetry potential. The constraints are obtained by performing a Bayesian analysis such that we can also provide an uncertainty for the estimated parameters. The posterior distribution is obtained by Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling for which we emulate the transport model with a Gaussian process to lower computational costs. We find that a relatively stiff equation of state is favoured in our analysis with a small uncertainty whereas the constraints obtained for the symmetry potential are rather loose.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relatively stiff equation of state for nuclear matter is favored by comparing SMASH transport model calculations to HADES flow data, but this result is conditional on the specific model assumptions (including a momentum-dependent potential and a large resonance content) and the analysis only includes statistical uncertainties, not systematic model uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling, with a Gaussian process emulator trained on 15 SMASH transport model calculations distributed via Latin hypercube sampling, to constrain the incompressibility parameter kappa and the symmetry potential S_pot. The likelihood was constructed from directed and elliptic flow measurements of protons and deuterons from the HADES experiment for gold-gold collisions at E_kin = 1.23A GeV, using transverse momentum bin 1.0 GeV < p_T < 1.5 GeV. The nuclear potentials included a Skyrme term, a symmetry term, a momentum-dependent term following Welke et al. (1988), and a Coulomb potential, with parameters constrained by nuclear ground-state properties and optical potential data.

## Key numbers

* Incompressibility: kappa = 348.2 (+4.0 / -3.9) MeV (68.3% credibility interval), indicating a relatively stiff equation of state.
* Symmetry potential: S_pot = 15.8 (+4.7 / -4.6) MeV (68.3% credibility interval), with a corresponding symmetry energy at saturation density S_0 in the range 24.2 MeV < S_0 < 33.5 MeV.
* The prior range for kappa was 200 MeV to 400 MeV, and for S_pot was 0 MeV to 30 MeV.
* The momentum-dependent potential was constrained by U(rho_0, p=0) = -75 MeV and U(rho_0, p=800 MeV) = 0 MeV, taken from Welke et al. (1988).
* The binding energy per nucleon at saturation density was fixed to (E_b/A) = -16 MeV at rho_B = rho_0.

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of SMASH to HADES flow data by Mohs et al. (2020), which found no satisfactory agreement without a momentum-dependent potential, and now includes that term to achieve good data description. The results are compared to constraints from Danielewicz et al. (2002) and Huth et al. (2021), finding a stiffer equation of state than Danielewicz, which the authors attribute to the large resonance content in SMASH (over 200 species) as previously noted by Hombach et al. (1998). The analysis enables future combined analyses with constraints from other experiments and astrophysical observations, as advocated by Tsang et al. (2023) and Huth et al. (2021), but the authors caution that systematic model uncertainties from the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP) remain unresolved.
