# Density-dependent NN-interaction from subleading chiral 3N-forces: short-range terms and relativistic corrections

> We derive from the subleading contributions to the chiral three-nucleon force (short-range terms and relativistic corrections, published in Phys. Rev. C84, 054001 (2011)) a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction $V_\text{med}$ in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The momentum and $k_f$-dependent potentials associated with the isospin operators ($1$ and $ \vecτ_1\!\cdot\!\vecτ_2$) and five independent spin-structures are expressed in terms of loop functions, which are either given in closed analytical form or require at most one numerical integration. Our results for $V_\text{med}$ are most helpful to implement subleading chiral 3N-forces into nuclear many-body calculations.

## Key claim

The authors derive a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction \(V_\text{med}\) from the subleading (N\(^3\)LO) chiral three-nucleon force, specifically from the short-range terms and relativistic corrections previously published in Phys. Rev. C84, 054001 (2011), but they explicitly note that the calculation from the remaining long-range 3N-forces is "yet more demanding and relegated to a future publication," meaning the present work covers only a subset of the full subleading 3N-force contributions.

## Method

The authors close one nucleon line of a generic chiral 3N-interaction \(V_\text{3N}\) into an in-medium loop, integrating over the medium part of the heavy nucleon particle-hole propagator, which generates four topologies of in-medium NN-scattering diagrams: self closings, short-range vertex corrections, pionic vertex corrections, and double exchanges. The resulting momentum- and Fermi-momentum-dependent potentials are decomposed into five independent spin operators and two isospin operators (1 and \(\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\)), with the loop functions expressed either in closed analytical form or requiring at most one numerical integration. The calculation treats the one-pion-exchange-contact topology, the two-pion-exchange-contact topology, and the leading relativistic \(1/M\)-corrections (subdivided into \(1\pi\)-exchange-contact and \(2\pi\)-exchange topologies), with the subleading 3N-contact potential converted into an in-medium interaction linear in density and quadratic in momenta.

## Key numbers

- The density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is given as \(\rho = 2k_f^3/3\pi^2\).
- The pion mass is set to \(m_\pi = 138\,\text{MeV}\), the pion decay constant to \(f_\pi = 92.2\,\text{MeV}\), and the axial coupling to \(g_A = 1.3\).
- The in-medium potential from the \(1\pi\)-exchange-contact topology vanishes identically in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave, \(V_\text{med}^{(^1S_0)} = 0\), verified by the identity \(\int_{|\vec l|<k_f} \frac{d^3 l}{2\pi} \frac{m_\pi^2+(\vec l+\vec p)^2}{m_\pi^2+(\vec l+\vec p)^2} = \frac{2k_f^3}{3} = m_\pi^2\Gamma_0+3\Gamma_2+p^2(\Gamma_3+2\Gamma_1+\Gamma_0)\).
- The short-range vertex correction from the \(2\pi\)-exchange-contact topology yields \(V_\text{med}^{(1)} = \frac{g_A^2 C_T k_f^3}{12\pi^3 f_\pi^4} \vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2 [m_\pi + (2m_\pi^2+q^2)A(q)]\) with \(A(q) = \frac{1}{2q}\arctan\frac{q}{2m_\pi}\).
- The relativistic \(1/M\)-correction from the \(1\pi\)-exchange-contact topology produces a short-range vertex correction \(V_\text{med}^{(1)} = \frac{g_A^2 k_f^3}{16\pi^2 M f_\pi^2}(C_T-C_S) \frac{\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{m_\pi^2+q^2} \vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\).
- The self-closing contribution from the \(2\pi\)-exchange relativistic correction (eq. 37) is \(V_\text{med}^{(0)} = -\frac{g_A^4 k_f^3 q^2}{16\pi^2 M f_\pi^4} \frac{\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{(m_\pi^2+q^2)^2} \vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\).
- The self-closing contribution from the retardation correction to consecutive \(2\pi\)-exchange (eq. 41) is \(V_\text{med}^{(0)} = \frac{g_A^4 k_f^3 q^4}{48\pi^2 M f_\pi^4} \frac{\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{(m_\pi^2+q^2)^3} \vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Holt et al. (2013), who derived the in-medium NN-interaction from the leading chiral 3N-force at N\(^2\)LO, to the subleading (N\(^3\)LO) level by treating the short-range terms and relativistic corrections from the 3N-force derived by the same group in Phys. Rev. C84, 054001 (2011). The resulting density-dependent potentials are intended to enable easier implementation of subleading chiral 3N-forces into nuclear many-body calculations, complementing the partial-wave decomposition approach of Hebeler et al. (2011) and providing a simpler alternative to working with large 3-body matrix element files. The authors note that the calculation from the remaining long-range 3N-forces (two-pion-exchange, two-pion-one-pion-exchange, and ring topologies) is left for future work.
