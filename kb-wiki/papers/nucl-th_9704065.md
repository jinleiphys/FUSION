# Microscopic Analysis of Thermodynamic Parameters from 160 MeV/n - 160 GeV/n

> Microscopic calculations of central collisions between heavy nuclei are used to study fragment production and the creation of collective flow. It is shown that the final phase space distributions are compatible with the expectations from a thermally equilibrated source, which in addition exhibits a collective transverse expansion. However, the microscopic analyses of the transient states in the reaction stages of highest density and during the expansion show that the system does not reach global equilibrium. Even if a considerable amount of equilibration is assumed, the connection of the measurable final state to the macroscopic parameters, e.g. the temperature, of the transient ''equilibrium'' state remains ambiguous.

## Key claim

The final phase-space distributions of fragments and hadrons from heavy-ion collisions are compatible with a thermally equilibrated source exhibiting collective transverse expansion, but the system does not reach global equilibrium during the transient stages of highest density and expansion; consequently, the connection between measurable final-state parameters (e.g., temperature) and the transient "equilibrium" state remains ambiguous even when substantial equilibration is assumed.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model to simulate central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across beam energies from 150 MeV/nucleon to 160 GeV/nucleon. They extracted local thermodynamic quantities (density, temperature, collective velocity) from the microscopic time evolution of the reaction zone and compared them to the parameters obtained by fitting the final-state transverse momentum spectra with a macroscopic thermal-source model that assumes a box density profile, constant temperature, and linear radial flow.

## Key numbers

- At 150 MeV/nucleon Au+Au central collisions, the transverse momentum spectra of fragments are compatible with temperatures between 20 and 25 MeV and averaged collective transverse flow velocities of 0.11–0.13 c, while the local temperatures in the central reaction zone at freeze-out densities (0.1–0.5 ρ₀) have dropped below 10 MeV.
- At 10.7 AGeV Au+Au and 160 AGeV Pb+Pb central collisions, the radial velocity profiles level off at about 0.6 c and are no longer linear in transverse radius.
- At 160 AGeV Pb+Pb, the most probable freeze-out times for kaons, pions, and protons are 9, 15, and 23 fm/c respectively, reflecting their different cross sections (approximately 16, 25, and 40 mb).
- At 10.7 AGeV Au+Au, cluster (triton, helium) emission is localized to the surface of the reaction zone at a transverse radius of about 6 fm, with suppression both at smaller radii (due to uncorrelated momenta and rescattering) and larger radii (due to decreasing density).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic transport approach of Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) to higher beam energies and provides a critical test of the thermal-source assumptions used in earlier analyses by Fermi (1950), Landau, Hagedorn, Siemens, and Mekijan. The results contradict the assumption of a common freeze-out time and box-like density profile used in fits by Braun-Munzinger (1995) and Cleymans (1995), who extracted temperatures differing by 20–30 MeV from the same data at AGS and SPS energies. The findings enable improved interpretation of freeze-out parameters extracted from experimental data and motivate more realistic space-time evolution models for future HBT correlation analyses.
