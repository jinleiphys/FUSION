# Boost-invariant description of polarization within hydrodynamics with spin

> We briefly review a recently proposed formalism of perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin, which is a generalization of the standard hydrodynamic framework and provides a natural tool for describing the evolution of spin-polarized systems of particles with spin 1/2. It is based on the de Groot - van Leeuwen - van Weert forms of energy-momentum and spin tensors and conservation laws. Using Bjorken symmetry we show how this formalism may be used to determine observables describing the polarization of particles measured in the experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin, based on the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors, can be solved analytically under Bjorken symmetry to describe the space-time evolution of the spin polarization tensor, but the resulting longitudinal polarization at midrapidity is zero and does not reproduce the experimental quadrupole structure observed by STAR.

## Method

The author extends standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles by imposing conservation laws for charge, energy-momentum, and angular momentum using the GLW forms of the tensors, assuming small spin polarization. The system is then specialized to a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous (Bjorken) flow, where the spin polarization tensor is decomposed into six scalar coefficients (C functions) that depend only on proper time. The conservation equations for the spin tensor are projected onto the Bjorken basis vectors to obtain independent evolution equations for these coefficients, which are solved numerically alongside the standard Bjorken hydrodynamic equations for temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions used: baryon chemical potential mu_0 = 800 MeV, temperature T_0 = 155 MeV, initial spin coefficients C_kappa,0 = (0,0,0) and C_omega,0 = (0,0.1,0) for y_p = 0.
- The longitudinal component of the mean polarization vector in the particle rest frame, <pi*_z>, is zero at midrapidity (y_p = 0).
- The transverse component <pi*_y> is negative, reflecting the initial spin angular momentum direction opposite to the y axis.
- The proper-time dependence of the six C coefficients is described as "weak" based on numerical solutions.

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin formalism developed by Florkowski et al. (2017, 2018) by applying it to the Bjorken boost-invariant geometry, following the approach of Bjorken (1983). It directly addresses the failure of thermal-vorticity-based models (Becattini et al., 2013, 2016; Karpenko et al., 2016) to explain the differential spin polarization results from the STAR Collaboration (2019), by incorporating dynamical evolution of spin degrees of freedom. The results provide a framework for calculating freeze-out spin polarization observables, though the model's symmetries prevent it from reproducing the experimentally observed quadrupole structure of longitudinal polarization.
