# eta, eta-prime --> pi+ pi- l+ l- in a chiral unitary approach

> The decays eta, eta-prime --> pi+ pi- l+ l- (with l = e, mu) are investigated within a chiral unitary approach which combines the chiral effective Lagrangian with a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation. Predictions for the decay widths and spectra are given.

## Key claim

The decays eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- l+ l- (with l = e, mu) are predicted within a chiral unitary approach that combines the chiral effective Lagrangian with a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation, but the predictions depend on five free parameters (C_pi, bar-w_eta^(m), bar-w_eta^(s), bar-w_eta'^(m), bar-w_eta'^(s)) that are first constrained by fitting to the decays eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- gamma, and the results carry a theoretical uncertainty estimated from a 1-sigma confidence region populated by about 1000 qualitatively different fits.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- gamma* (with a virtual photon) using a chiral unitary approach that iterates the meson-meson interaction kernel from the chiral Lagrangian up to fourth order in a Bethe-Salpeter equation, generating resonances dynamically. They add the resulting non-perturbative rescattering diagrams to the full one-loop U(3) chiral perturbation theory result, ensuring electromagnetic gauge invariance, anomalous Ward identities, and exact two-body unitarity. The free parameters are fixed by a global least-squares fit to the partial decay widths and di-pion spectra of eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- gamma, and then the decay widths and spectra for eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- l+ l- are predicted.

## Key numbers

* The pseudoscalar decay constants are set to F_pi = 92.4 MeV, F_eta = 1.3 F_pi, and F_eta' = 1.1 F_pi.
* The best fit to eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- gamma data yields an overall chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.23.
* The 1-sigma confidence region is defined by Delta chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.08, derived from the p-value of the chi^2 probability distribution function.
* The subtraction constant in the pion loops, C_pi, is the only subtraction constant required to be non-zero; all other subtraction constants are set to zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral unitary treatment of eta, eta-prime -> pi+ pi- gamma (Borasoy and Nissler, 2005) to off-shell photons, enabling predictions for the dilepton final states. It builds on earlier successful applications of the same approach to hadronic decay modes of eta and eta-prime (Borasoy and Nissler, 2003; Borasoy, Nissler, and Meissner, 2005) and to anomalous decays eta, eta-prime -> gamma* gamma* (Borasoy and Nissler, 2004). The approach contrasts with conventional vector dominance pictures that can contradict one-loop chiral perturbation theory (Holstein, 1996), and it avoids explicit resonance inclusion as in the work of Picciotto (1994) and Faessler, Krewald, and Lu (2000). The predictions are intended for comparison with ongoing experiments at WASA@COSY, MAMI, KLOE, and the VES collaboration.
