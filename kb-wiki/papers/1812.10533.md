# Consistent relativistic mean field models constrained by GW170817

> We have obtained the Love number and corresponding tidal deformabilites ($Λ$) associated with the relativistic mean-field parametrizations shown to be consistent (CRMF) with the nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, symmetry energy and its derivatives [Dutra, et al., Phys. Rev. C 90, 055203 (2014)]. Our results show that CRMF models present very good agreement with the recent data from binary neutron star merger event GW170817. They also confirm the strong correlation between $Λ_{1.4}$ and the radius of canonical stars ($R_{1.4}$). When a recently GW170817 constraint on $Λ_{1.4}$ and the corresponding radius $R_{1.4}$ is used, the majority of the models tested are shown to satisfy it.

# Consistent relativistic mean field models constrained by GW170817

## Key claim
The 34 relativistic mean-field parametrizations previously shown to be consistent with nuclear matter constraints (CRMF) all lie within the 90% and 50% confidence contours of the GW170817 tidal deformability data, but only 5 of these models simultaneously satisfy both the GW170817 constraints and the requirement of supporting massive neutron stars in the range 1.93 to 2.05 solar masses.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations coupled to the differential equation for the Love number y(r) for each of the 34 CRMF parametrizations, using charge-neutral beta-equilibrium matter composed of protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons, with the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) equation of state at low densities. The dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda was then computed from the Love number k2 and compactness C = m/R for each model, and the binary tidal deformabilities Lambda1 and Lambda2 were obtained by fixing the chirp mass to the GW170817 value of 1.188 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- Chirp mass of GW170817: M = 1.188 solar masses
- Tidal deformability constraint for canonical 1.4 solar mass star: 70 <= Lambda_1.4 <= 580 (from LIGO/Virgo collaboration, PRL121)
- Upper limit on binary tidal deformability from low spin priors: Lambda_tilde <= 800 (from PRL119)
- Lower limit estimate: Lambda_tilde > 400
- 24 out of 34 CRMF parametrizations satisfy the Lambda_1.4 constraint of 70 to 580
- Only 5 models satisfy both the GW170817 constraint and the massive star constraint (1.93 to 2.05 solar masses): G2*, IU-FSU, DD-F, TW99, and DD-ME-delta
- Canonical star radius range from GW170817 for models supporting massive stars: 11.9 +/- 1.4 km
- Upper limit on canonical star radii from prior work: R_1.4 <= 12.9 km
- Compactness of one recently measured isolated neutron star: 0.105 +/- 0.002

## Context
This work extends the systematic analysis of Dutra et al. (2014, Phys. Rev. C 90, 055203), which identified 35 RMF parametrizations satisfying nuclear matter constraints, and the subsequent astrophysical study of Dutra et al. (2016, Phys. Rev. C 93, 025806) that tested 34 of these models against maximum mass and other constraints. The authors apply the tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 event, following similar correlation studies by other groups using Skyrme-type models, relativistic models, and density functional theories. The results enable identification of the few RMF parametrizations that simultaneously satisfy nuclear matter constraints, massive star requirements, and gravitational wave observations, providing a filtered set of models for future neutron star studies.
