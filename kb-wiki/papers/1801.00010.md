# A quantum hydrodynamical description for scrambling and many-body chaos

> Recent studies of out-of-time ordered thermal correlation functions (OTOC) in holographic systems and in solvable models such as the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) model have yielded new insights into manifestations of many-body chaos. So far the chaotic behavior has been obtained through explicit calculations in specific models. In this paper we propose a unified description of the exponential growth and ballistic butterfly spreading of OTOCs across different systems using a newly formulated "quantum hydrodynamics," which is valid at finite $\hbar$ and to all orders in derivatives. The scrambling of a generic few-body operator in a chaotic system is described as building up a "hydrodynamic cloud," and the exponential growth of the cloud arises from a shift symmetry of the hydrodynamic action. The shift symmetry also shields correlation functions of the energy density and flux, and time ordered correlation functions of generic operators from exponential growth, while leads to chaotic behavior in OTOCs. The theory also predicts an interesting phenomenon of the skipping of a pole at special values of complex frequency and momentum in two-point functions of energy density and flux. This pole-skipping phenomenon may be considered as a "smoking gun" for the hydrodynamic origin of the chaotic mode. We also discuss the possibility that such a hydrodynamic description could be a hallmark of maximally chaotic systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the exponential growth and ballistic spreading of out-of-time ordered correlators (OTOCs) in chaotic many-body systems can be described by a "quantum hydrodynamics" where the hydrodynamic mode associated with energy conservation also governs chaotic behavior, but only in systems that are maximally (or nearly maximally) chaotic.

## Method

The authors construct a quantum hydrodynamic effective action for systems where energy is the only conserved quantity, formulated on a closed time path (CTP) contour and valid at finite Planck constant and to all orders in derivatives. They introduce a shift symmetry into the hydrodynamic action that generates an exponentially growing mode in the retarded Green's function of the hydrodynamic field sigma, while simultaneously shielding the stress tensor two-point functions from this exponential growth. The OTOCs of generic operators are then computed at leading order in the large-N expansion by reducing four-point functions on a four-segment contour to two-point functions of the sigma field on the CTP contour.

## Key numbers

* The Lyapunov exponent lambda has an upper bound lambda_max = 2 pi k_B / (beta_0 hbar) for a generic system at inverse temperature beta_0.
* The scrambling time t_s is approximately (1/lambda) log N, where N is the total number of degrees of freedom in the system.
* The butterfly velocity v_B is related to the energy diffusion constant D_E by v_B^2 = D_E lambda in systems where the nonlocal diffusion kernel collapses to a constant.
* The pole-skipping point occurs at complex frequency omega = i lambda and complex momentum k = i k_C, where k_C = lambda / v_B.

## Context

This work extends the study of many-body chaos from explicit calculations in holographic systems and the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) model, as done by Kitaev (2015), Roberts and Shenker (2015), and others, to a unified effective description. The authors build on the hydrodynamic action formalism developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) and the observation by Jensen (2016) that the Schwarzian action in SYK can be interpreted as a hydrodynamic mode for energy conservation. The theory predicts a "pole-skipping" phenomenon in energy-energy correlation functions that provides a way to extract the Lyapunov exponent and butterfly velocity without computing OTOCs, and it enables connections between chaos and transport coefficients such as the energy diffusion constant.
