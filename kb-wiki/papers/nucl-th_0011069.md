# Quark model approach to the $η$ meson electroproduction on the proton

> A recently developed quark model approach to pseudoscalar meson photoproduction is extended to electroproduction process for the $η$ meson in the kinematics of momentum transfer $Q^2 \leq$ 4 (GeV/c)$^2$ and total center of mass energy $W \leq$ 1.6 GeV. Existing data are well reproduced and the roles of the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances are closely investigated. In the study of the longitudinal excitation of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance, a reliable constraint on the $S_{11}(1535)$ properties is obtained by cleanly removing the electromagnetic transition from the $γ_{(v)} p \to S_{11}(1535) \to ηp$ amplitude. Thus, the fitted quantities can be determined with an uncertainty of about 15%. This could be the first direct constraint on the $S_{11}(1535)$ properties in theory.

## Key claim

The authors extend a quark model approach from photoproduction to electroproduction of η mesons, claiming that by cleanly removing the electromagnetic transition from the γ(v)p → S11(1535) → ηp amplitude, they obtain a reliable constraint on the S11(1535) properties with an uncertainty of about 15%, which could be the first direct theoretical constraint on those properties.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model with a chiral effective Lagrangian for quark-meson couplings, including eight isospin-1/2 baryon resonances up to harmonic oscillator quantum number n=2, with the S11(1535) and D13(1520) as the dominant contributions. They fit seven adjustable parameters (including the ηNN coupling constant, harmonic oscillator strength, S11 total width and ηN branching ratio, and strength coefficients for S11 and D13) to 677 differential cross-section data points from photoproduction (Mainz, GRAAL) and electroproduction (JLab at Q2=2.4 and 3.6 (GeV/c)2, and older data at low Q2) using the CERN MINUIT minimization code, with Lorentz boost factors applied in an equal velocity frame.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: Q2 ≤ 4 (GeV/c)2; total center-of-mass energy: W ≤ 1.6 GeV
- Fitted ηNN coupling constant: αη = 0.10 ± 0.01 (dimensionless, gηNN2/4π)
- Fitted harmonic oscillator strength: α = 384.5 MeV
- Fitted S11(1535) total width: ΓS11 = 143.3 ± 0.2 MeV
- Fitted S11(1535) ηN branching ratio: bη = 0.55 ± 0.01
- Fitted longitudinal amplitude coefficient for S11: CS11L = 0.65
- Experimental ratio R = σL/σT at Q2 = 0.4 (GeV/c)2: 0.23 ± 0.11 (from Breuker et al.)
- Data sets: 677 differential cross-sections total; JLab data at Q2 = 2.4 and 3.6 (GeV/c)2 with W = 1.54 GeV; old electroproduction data at W = 1.535 GeV with Q2 = 0.20, 0.28, and 0.40 (GeV/c)2

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach of Li (1997) for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction to electroproduction, building on earlier photoproduction studies by Tiator (1995, 1999), Li and Saghai (1998), and the RPI group (1996, 1998, 2000) that established S11(1535) dominance with a small D13(1520) contribution. The availability of JLab electroproduction data from Armstrong (1999) provides complementary information on longitudinal photon excitations, enabling the authors to constrain the S11(1535) properties more tightly than photoproduction alone allowed, and to derive an analytical relation that removes the electromagnetic vertex uncertainty from the ηNS11 coupling determination.
