# Axial form factor of the nucleon at large momentum transfers

> Motivated by the emerging possibilities to study threshold pion electroproduction at large momentum transfers at Jefferson Laboratory following the 12 GeV upgrade, we provide a short theory summary and an estimate of the nucleon axial form factor for large virtualities in the $Q^2 = 1-10~\text{GeV}^2$ range using next-to-leading order light-cone sum rules.

## Key claim

The authors provide next-to-leading order light-cone sum rule estimates of the nucleon axial form factor for momentum transfers in the range \(Q^2 = 1-10~\text{GeV}^2\), but caution that the overall accuracy of their predictions in the optimal range \(Q^2\sim 3-10~\text{GeV}^2\) is only of order 20-25%, and that momentum transfers below 2 GeV\(^2\) are still too low for a fully quantitative treatment in this approach.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon axial form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) using next-to-leading order (NLO) light-cone sum rules (LCSRs). They start from a correlation function of the Ioffe current and the neutral axial vector current, expand it in the light-cone operator product expansion, and match the QCD representation to a hadronic dispersion relation. The NLO expressions are obtained by modifying the previously published NLO results for the electromagnetic form factors (Anikin, Braun, Offen 2013) with appropriate replacements for the quark charges and distribution amplitude symmetries. The sum rule is then evaluated using Borel transformation and quark-hadron duality with a threshold \(s_0 \simeq (1.5~\text{GeV})^2\).

## Key numbers

* The axial form factor is estimated for the range \(Q^2 = 1-10~\text{GeV}^2\).
* NLO corrections are large and positive (up to 40%) at \(Q^2=1-2~\text{GeV}^2\), decrease to below 15% at larger momentum transfers, and change sign at \(Q^2\sim 6~\text{GeV}^2\) for both distribution amplitude models.
* The overall accuracy of predictions in the optimal range \(Q^2\sim 3-10~\text{GeV}^2\) is estimated to be of order 20-25%.
* The LCSR predictions are about 20-30% below the CLAS extraction from pion electroproduction in the soft pion limit (Park 2012).
* The axial mass from neutrino scattering is \(M_A = 1.026(21)~\text{GeV}\) and from electroproduction is \(M_A = 1.069(16)~\text{GeV}\) (Bernard 2001).

## Context

This work extends the LCSR approach previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Braun 2001, Braun 2006, Anikin 2013) to the axial form factor, motivated by the 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Laboratory enabling threshold pion electroproduction measurements at large momentum transfers. The results are compared to neutrino scattering data analyzed with a flexible \(z\)-parametrization (Meyer 2016) and to CLAS electroproduction data (Park 2012). The authors note that finite pion mass corrections to the soft-pion limit extraction method can reach 30% and that further model-dependent corrections could reduce the remaining uncertainty below 10-15%, enabling future comparisons with lattice QCD (Alexandrou 2010, Junnarkar 2014) and Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches (Eichmann 2011).
