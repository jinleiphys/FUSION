# Progress in Meson-Nucleon Physics: Status and Perspectives

> In this opening talk I will address some issues that exemplify the theoretical progress that has been made and is to be expected in the field of meson-nucleon physics. My emphasis will be on performing precision calculations to test aspects of QCD, including also electroweak probes. In addition, I discuss the problems and opportunities related to the strange quark sector.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) provides a consistent framework for precision calculations in meson-nucleon physics, but its applicability is limited to low energies (pion lab momenta below about 100 MeV) and it cannot systematically incorporate resonances or bound states without non-perturbative resummation methods that preserve the low-energy chiral structure.

## Method

The author reviews two complementary theoretical approaches: (1) CHPT calculations of isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering, treating both the light quark mass difference (m_u != m_d) and electromagnetism (q_u != q_d) as small parameters to third order, with electromagnetic corrections including one- and two-photon exchanges, soft photon loops, and hard photon contact terms; (2) a non-perturbative unitary resummation scheme matched to CHPT that uses a T-matrix formalism with subtracted dispersion relations to incorporate coupled-channel dynamics and resonances, applied to pi-N and anti-K-N scattering.

## Key numbers

* Isospin violation in pi-N scattering is estimated at the tree level to be about 1 percent, based on the leading-order isosymmetric S-wave scattering length formula a(pi- p -> pi- p) = a^(LO)_(pi p) {1 + 0.01}
* Two-photon exchange is suppressed compared to one-photon exchange by a factor e^2 M_pi / (32 |q_pi|) <= 0.04 for pion lab momenta |q_pi| >= 10 MeV
* Dynamical isospin breaking in the S-wave from the full CHPT calculation is about 0.75 percent
* The Delta(1232) resonance is found in the complex-W plane at (1210 - i53) MeV from the unitary approach
* For the anti-K-N system, best-fit parameters differ at most by 15 percent from their natural values
* The CHPT analysis is valid for pion lab momenta below 100 MeV

## Context

This conference summary extends the program of precision chiral perturbation theory calculations for pion-nucleon scattering developed by Fettes and Meissner (2001), and contrasts with earlier dispersion-theoretical approaches to electromagnetic corrections by Hamilton, Tromberg, and Overbo (1973-1977). The unitary resummation method builds on the approach pioneered by Oller and Oset (1999) for meson interactions, extended to pion-nucleon and anti-K-N scattering by Meissner and Oller (2000-2001). The work enables future combined fits to multiple reactions (pion electro- and photoproduction, pi N -> pi pi N) to pin down low-energy constants, and provides a framework for studying CP violation in B decays and in Xi -> Lambda pi decays.
