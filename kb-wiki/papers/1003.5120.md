# Coupled channels calculation of a pion-Lambda-nucleon quasibound state

> We extend the study of a JP=2+, I=3/2 pion-Lambda-nucleon quasibound state [Phys. Rev. D 78, 014013 (2008)] by solving nonrelativistic Faddeev equations, using 3S1-3D1, Lambda N - Sigma N coupled channels Chiral Quark Model local interactions, and pion-nucleon and coupled pion-Lambda - pion-Sigma separable interactions fitted to the position and decay parameters of the Delta(1232) and Sigma(1385) resonances, respectively. The results exhibit a strong sensitivity to the p-wave pion-hyperon interaction, with a pion-Lambda-nucleon quasibound state persisting over a wide range of acceptable parametrizations.

## Key claim

The paper extends the study of a $J^P=2^+, I=3/2$ pion-Lambda-nucleon quasibound state by solving nonrelativistic Faddeev equations with more realistic coupled-channel interactions, finding that such a state persists over a wide range of acceptable parametrizations but exhibits strong sensitivity to the p-wave pion-hyperon interaction and the short-range behavior of the hyperon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors solved nonrelativistic Faddeev equations for the $\pi\Lambda N$ three-body system with $J^P=2^+, I=3/2$, using $^3S_1-^3D_1$, $\Lambda N - \Sigma N$ coupled channels local potentials from the Chiral Quark Model (CQM) for the hyperon-nucleon interaction. For the pion-nucleon subsystem, a rank-one separable potential was fitted to the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance, while for the pion-hyperon subsystem, coupled channels $\pi\Lambda - \pi\Sigma$ separable interactions were fitted to the position, width, and decay branching ratios of the $\Sigma(1385)$ resonance, with five different form factor models (A-E) and one single-channel model (F) constructed.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of $\pi\Lambda N$ range from 6 MeV (Model E) to 152 MeV (Model A) across five $\pi Y$ interaction models and six CQM versions of the $YN$ interaction, with the highest binding for $\pi Y$ models with $A_2 < 1$ fm$^2$
- For the $\pi Y$ model C ($A_2=1.2$ fm$^2$) with full $YN$ coupled channels and $a=-1.40$ fm, the binding energy is 66 MeV; switching off the $YN$ interaction gives 84 MeV
- The $\pi N$ form factor has r.m.s. momentum $\sqrt{<p_1^2>_{g_1}} = 5.55$ fm$^{-1} \approx 1100$ MeV/c; the $\pi Y$ form factors have r.m.s. momenta ranging from 3.48 fm$^{-1}$ (Model E) to 4.11 fm$^{-1}$ (Model A), approximately 750 MeV/c
- Adding a short-range repulsive $\Lambda N$ potential to the CQM with $a=-1.40$ fm reduces the binding energy from 147 MeV (no added repulsion) down to 34 MeV (with $\gamma_R=7000$ MeV fm) for $\pi Y$ model A

## Context

This work extends a previous Faddeev calculation by the same authors (Garcilazo and Gal, 2008) that used single-channel separable potentials for both the $\Lambda N$ and $\pi\Lambda$ subsystems, by incorporating coupled-channel $\Lambda N - \Sigma N$ and $\pi\Lambda - \pi\Sigma$ interactions. The study is motivated by earlier quark cluster calculations by Oka (1988) that suggested a $(J^P,I)=(2^+,1/2)$ dibaryon near the $\Sigma\Delta(1232)$ threshold, and by the lack of experimental confirmation for Jaffe's (1977) $H$ dibaryon. The results enable experimental searches for this $S=-1$ dibaryon in reactions such as $K^- + d \to \mathcal{D}^- + \pi^+$, $\pi^- + d \to \mathcal{D}^- + K^+$, and $p + p \to \mathcal{D}^+ + K^+$, with $\Sigma N$ decay channels as signatures.
