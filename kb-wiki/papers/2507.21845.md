# Neutrino non-radiative decay in matter: constraints and prospects

> Neutrinos, being massive, can decay. A heavier neutrino could decay into a lighter one and a massless scalar or pseudoscalar boson, such as the Majoron. Two-body non-radiative decay could occur in dense matter, such as in the inner dense regions of a core-collapse supernova. We first derive novel bounds on neutrino-Majoron couplings using the spectral distortions induced by neutrino non-radiative two-body decay in matter, and two-dimensional likelihood analyses of the 24 $\barν_e$ events from SN1987A. We then explore the prospects of neutrino-Majoron couplings from a future galactic core-collapse supernova, leaving either a neutron star or a black-hole. To this aim, we use information from detailed one-dimensional supernova simulations. We consider the supernova neutrino signal associated with inverse-beta decay in the upcoming JUNO and Hyper-Kamiokande detectors, with neutrino-argon scattering in DUNE, or with coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering in the DARWIN experiment. In a full 3$ν$ framework, based on the spectral distortions induced by neutrino decay in matter, we perform two-dimensional likelihood analyses and provide prospects for the limits on neutrino-Majoron couplings. Our results show that the observation of a future supernova will significantly improve on the current bounds, in particular from SN1987A and neutrinoless double-beta decay. Finally, we explore the impact of neutrino decay in matter on the diffuse supernova neutrino background formed by past supernova explosions. We show for the first time that the effects on black-hole contributions are important and modify the DSNB number of events by several tens of percent in Hyper-Kamiokande.

## Key claim

The authors derive novel bounds on neutrino-Majoron couplings using spectral distortions from neutrino non-radiative two-body decay in matter, based on two-dimensional likelihood analyses of the 24 antineutrino events from SN1987A, and provide prospects for future supernova observations; the analysis assumes that matter and decaying eigenstates coincide and that only antineutrinos undergo decay in dense matter, not neutrinos.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional likelihood analyses of the spectral distortions induced by neutrino non-radiative two-body decay in matter, using the 24 antineutrino events from SN1987A detected in Kamiokande, IMB, and Baksan, including detailed detector response functions. They then extended the analysis to a future galactic core-collapse supernova using one-dimensional simulations from the Garching group (for neutron star remnants) and Nakazato simulations (for black-hole remnants), calculating neutrinosphere, transportsphere, and energysphere radii to determine decay effects on neutrino fluxes. The survival probabilities for antineutrinos were computed using time-dependent density profiles from the supernova simulations, and the expected event rates were evaluated for JUNO, Hyper-Kamiokande, DUNE, and DARWIN detectors.

## Key numbers

* The lower bound from SN1987A for inverted neutrino mass ordering was previously found to be 2.4 x 10^5 eV/s at 68% CL and 1.2 x 10^5 eV/s at 90% CL for the nu2 and nu1 mass eigenstates (from prior work by the same authors).
* The mean time of occurrence for galactic core-collapse supernovae is estimated as 61 (+24, -14) years.
* The DSNB number of events in Hyper-Kamiokande is modified by several tens of percent due to black-hole contributions from neutrino decay in matter.
* The gravitational binding energy released in core-collapse supernovae is about 3 x 10^53 erg.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous likelihood analysis of SN1987A events for neutrino decay in vacuum (Iváñez-Ballesteros 2023) to the case of decay in matter, and builds on the theoretical framework for neutrino-Majoron decay in matter developed by Kachelriess (2000) and Tomas (2001). The new bounds on neutrino-Majoron couplings from SN1987A are competitive with those from neutrinoless double-beta decay and several orders of magnitude better than limits from lepton and meson decays. The results enable improved sensitivity to neutrino-Majoron couplings from future galactic supernova observations and show that black-hole contributions to the diffuse supernova neutrino background are significant, modifying event rates by tens of percent in Hyper-Kamiokande.
