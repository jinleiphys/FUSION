# Self-consistency and collective effects in semiclassical pairing theory

> A simple model, in which nuclei are represented as homogeneous spheres of symmetric nuclear matter, is used to study the effects of a self-consistent pairing interaction on the nuclear response. Effects due to the finite size of nuclei are suitably taken into account. The semiclassical equations of motion derived in a previous paper for the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov problem are solved in an improved (linear) approximation in which the pairing field is allowed to oscillate and to become complex. The new solutions are in good agreement with the old ones and also with the result of well-known quantum approaches. The role of the Pauli principle in eliminating one possible set of solutions is also discussed. The pairing-field fluctuations have two main effects: they restore the particle-number symmetry which is broken in the constant-$Δ$ approximation and introduce the possibility of collective eigenfrequencies of the system due to the pairing interaction. A numerical study with values of parameters appropriate for nuclei, shows an enhancement of the density-density strength function in the region of the low-energy giant octupole resonance, while no similar effect is present in the region of the high-energy octupole resonance and for the giant monopole and quadrupole resonances.

## Key claim

The paper shows that allowing the pairing field to oscillate and become complex in a semiclassical time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework restores particle-number conservation and introduces collective eigenfrequencies, but only for the low-energy giant octupole resonance; no similar enhancement appears for the high-energy octupole, monopole, or quadrupole resonances.

## Method

The authors model nuclei as homogeneous spheres of symmetric nuclear matter with a spherical square-well potential, using semiclassical equations of motion derived from time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. They solve these equations in a linear approximation where the pairing field is allowed to oscillate and become complex, with the real and imaginary parts of the pairing-field fluctuations determined by self-consistency relations derived from the gap equation. The density-density response function is then calculated by Fourier-expanding the fluctuations in action-angle variables and solving the resulting algebraic system.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap Δ takes values between 1 and 1.5 MeV for heavy nuclei (from Bohr and Mottelson, p. 170).
- The condition for the semiclassical approximation is p_F R/ħ >> 1, equivalent to ħω_0 << ε_F.
- The dispersion relation for the n=0 mode has only the solution ω² = 0 (the Anderson-Goldstone-Nambu mode); the alternative relation [I₁²(ω) - (ω² - 4Δ²)I₂²(ω)] = 0 has no solution in the interval ω = 0–30 MeV.
- The eigenfrequencies ω_± for modes with n≠0 are approximated by ω²_+ ≈ ω̄²_n and ω²_- ≈ ω²_n ω²_μ / ω̄²_n when the parameter α_n(I) = 4 ω²_n ω²_μ / ω̄⁴_n << 1.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach of Abrosimov, Brink, Dellafiore, and Matera (2009), which used a constant-Δ approximation that violated the continuity equation and required an ad hoc prescription to restore the energy-weighted sum rule. The present paper improves on that by allowing the pairing field to fluctuate self-consistently, following the semiclassical method of Urban and Schuck (2006) for trapped fermionic atoms. The results agree with the earlier constant-Δ approach and with quantum BCS theory, and the formalism enables study of collective pairing effects in finite nuclei, particularly for low-energy octupole excitations.
