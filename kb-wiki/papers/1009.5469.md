# On the role of different Skyrme forces and surface corrections in exotic cluster-decay

> We present cluster decay studies of $^{56}$Ni$^*$ formed in heavy-ion collisions using different Skyrme forces. Our study reveals that different Skyrme forces do not alter the transfer structure of fractional yields significantly. The cluster decay half-lives of different clusters lies within \pm 10% for PCM and \pm 15% for UFM.

## Key claim

The paper claims that different Skyrme force parameterizations do not alter the transfer structure of fractional yields significantly, and that cluster decay half-lives vary within ±10% for the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) and ±15% for the Unified Fission Model (UFM), but this conclusion is qualified by the fact that the study is limited to the specific compound system \(^{56}\)Ni\(^*\) formed in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme Energy Density Model (SEDM) to compute the nuclear interaction potential for cluster decay of \(^{56}\)Ni\(^*\). They applied both the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM), which includes preformation probability \(P_0\) and barrier penetration probability \(P\), and the Unified Fission Model (UFM), which sets \(P_0 = 1\) and uses a direct penetration path to the effective Q-value. The calculations were performed for various Skyrme force parameter sets (S, SI, SII, SIII, etc.) and for different values of the surface correction parameter \(\lambda\) (ranging from 0 to 5/36), with the fragmentation potential and fractional yields computed using the classical hydrodynamical mass parameter of Kröger and Scheid.

## Key numbers

- Cluster decay half-lives for different clusters lie within ±10% for PCM and ±15% for UFM when varying Skyrme forces (Fig. 3).
- Variation in half-lives due to different surface correction parameter \(\lambda\) values (0, 1/36, 2/36, 3/36, 4/36, 5/36) lies within ±10% for both PCM and UFM (Fig. 6).
- The effective Q-value relation used was \(Q_{eff} = 0.4(28 - |Q_{out}|)\) MeV, chosen as more realistic following MKS00.
- Calculations were performed at nuclear temperature \(T = 3.0\) MeV for the fractional yields.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of cluster decay in light compound nuclei, specifically the doubly magic \(^{56}\)Ni system studied experimentally by Sanders et al. (1999, 1988, 1991) and Nouicer et al. (1999) using various entrance channels. The authors apply the Skyrme energy density formalism previously used by Puri et al. (1992) for fusion barriers, and the PCM model developed by Gupta et al. (1988), Malik et al. (1989), and Kumar et al. (1997). The study enables systematic comparison of how different effective interactions and surface corrections affect cluster decay half-lives, which had not been previously investigated in the literature.
