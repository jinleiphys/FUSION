# The Balian-Brézin Method in Relativistic Quantum Mechanics

> The method suggested by Balian and Brézin for treating angular momentum reduction in the Faddeev equations is shown to be applicable to the relativistic three-body problem.

## Key claim

The Balian-Brézin method for angular momentum reduction in the Faddeev equations, which simplifies the computation of recoupling coefficients by exploiting rotational invariance and averaging over magnetic quantum numbers, is shown to be applicable to relativistic three-body problems formulated as Bakamjian-Thomas models, including those in any of Dirac's forms of dynamics.

## Method

The authors demonstrate that the relativistic three-body transition operators satisfy coupled integral equations of the same algebraic form as the non-relativistic Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations, with the mass operator replacing the Hamiltonian. They construct irreducible basis vectors for the three-particle Hilbert space by successive coupling of Poincaré group representations, using canonical or other spin definitions (helicity, front-form), and show that the kernel of the relativistic equations has the same diagonal structure in total momentum, total angular momentum, and spectator quantum numbers as in the non-relativistic case. The recoupling matrix elements between bases with different spectators are then evaluated by exploiting Poincaré invariance to reduce the computation to an invariant amplitude, using the same averaging over magnetic quantum numbers as in the original Balian-Brézin method.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic change-of-basis matrix element (Eq. AN) includes a factor \(8 \pi^2 m_{23} m_{13} / (m_1 m_2 m_3 k_1 k_2 q_1 q_2)\) from the phase-space reduction.
- The relativistic two-body basis normalization (Eq. BB) is \(\langle {\bf P} \mu (k j l s) | {\bf P}' \mu' (k' j' l' s') \rangle = \delta({\bf P}-{\bf P}') \delta(k-k')/k^2 \delta_{jj'} \delta_{\mu\mu'} \delta_{ll'} \delta_{ss'}\).
- The relativistic three-body kernel (Eq. BN) contains \(\delta(q-q')/q^2\) and Kronecker deltas \(\delta_{JJ'} \delta_{\mu\mu'} \delta_{LL'} \delta_{SS'} \delta_{jj'}\).
- The group integration result (Eq. BZ) gives \(\int dR \, D^{J*}_{\nu\mu}[R_{wx}(R,P_0)] D^{J'}_{\nu'\mu'}[R_{wx}(R,P_0)] = \delta_{JJ'} \delta_{\nu\nu'} \delta_{\mu\mu'} / (2J+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the angular momentum reduction technique of Balian and Brézin (1969) from non-relativistic Faddeev equations to relativistic three-body formulations, specifically Bakamjian-Thomas models (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) in any of Dirac's forms of dynamics (Dirac, 1949). The method applies to the same class of connected-kernel three-body equations used in non-relativistic calculations, including the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations (Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas, 1967), and enables numerical solution of relativistic three-body problems by reducing the recoupling coefficients to a form that can be computed with arbitrary choices of kinematic vectors.
