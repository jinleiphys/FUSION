# Fermion Cooper Pairing with Unequal Masses: Standard Field Theory Approach

> The fermion Cooper pairing with unequal masses is investigated in a standard field theory approach. We derived the superfluid density and Meissner mass squared of the U(1) gauge field in a general two species model and found that the often used proportional relation between the two quantities is broken down when the fermion masses are unequal. In weak coupling region, the superfluid density is always negative but the Meissner mass squared becomes mostly positive when the mass ratio between the pairing fermions is large enough. We established a proper momentum configuration of the LOFF pairing with unequal masses and showed that the LOFF state is energetically favored due to the negative superfluid density. The single plane wave LOFF state is physically equivalent to an anisotropic state with a spontaneously generated superflow. The extension to finite range interaction is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The often-used proportional relation between superfluid density and Meissner mass squared breaks down when the pairing fermions have unequal masses; in the weak coupling region, the superfluid density is always negative while the Meissner mass squared becomes mostly positive when the mass ratio is large enough, indicating that these two quantities signal different instabilities in asymmetric systems.

## Method

The authors derived the superfluid density and Meissner mass squared for a general two-species fermion model using a standard field theory approach, starting from the Lagrangian density with attractive interaction and employing a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and mean-field approximation. They calculated the superfluid density by expanding the thermodynamic potential in powers of a small superfluid velocity, and the Meissner mass squared by evaluating the polarization tensor of a U(1) gauge field in the static long-wavelength limit. The results were then applied to the breached pairing (BP) state and the LOFF pairing state at zero temperature for various mass ratios.

## Key numbers

* Superfluid density in the BP state for equal masses: \(\rho_s \simeq mn\left[1-\frac{\delta\mu\Theta(\delta\mu-\Delta)}{\sqrt{\delta\mu^2-\Delta^2}}\right]\), where \(\delta\mu\) is the chemical potential mismatch and \(\Delta\) the gap.
* BCS gap in weak coupling: \(\Delta_0\simeq 8e^{-2}\mu e^{-\frac{\pi}{2p_F|a_s|}}\).
* Condition for gapless BP state: \(\Delta<\Delta_c=\frac{|p_b^2-p_a^2|}{4\sqrt{m_am_b}}\).
* For unequal masses with \(p_a<p_b\) and mass ratio \(\lambda=m_b/m_a\), the superfluid density is always negative at any mass ratio, while the Meissner mass squared is positive in the region \(0<\Delta/\Delta_0<\nu\) with \(\nu<1\); for \(\lambda=100\), \(\nu\) is close to 1.
* Both superfluid density and Meissner mass squared diverge to negative infinity near \(\Delta/\Delta_0=1\) (the BP-BCS transition point).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of asymmetric Cooper pairing with mismatched Fermi surfaces, particularly the breached pairing (BP) state proposed by Liu and Wilczek (2003) and the Sarma state, which was known to suffer thermodynamic instability. The authors build on the finding by Wu and Yip (2003) that the BP state has negative superfluid density in equal-mass systems, and on observations of negative Meissner masses in gapless color superconductors by Huang and Shovkovy (2003). The paper shows that for unequal masses, the superfluid density and Meissner mass squared are not proportional, resolving an ambiguity in the literature; it also establishes a proper momentum configuration for the LOFF pairing state with unequal masses, enabling future studies of spatially inhomogeneous superfluid states in systems such as light-heavy fermion mixtures (e.g., \(^6\)Li and \(^{40}\)K) and asymmetric nuclear matter.
