# Open-source library for performance-portable neutrino reaction rates: Application to neutron star mergers

> A realistic and detailed description of neutrinos in binary neutron star (BNS) mergers is essential to build reliable models of such systems. To this end, we present BNS_NURATES, a novel open-source numerical library designed for the efficient on-the-fly computation of neutrino interactions, with particular focus on regimes relevant to BNS mergers. BNS_NURATES targets a higher level of accuracy and realism in the implementation of commonly employed reactions by accounting for relevant microphysics effects on the interactions, such as weak magnetism and mean field effects. It also includes the contributions of inelastic neutrino scattering off electrons and positrons and (inverse) nucleon decays. Finally, it offers a way to reconstruct the neutrino distribution function in the framework of moment-based transport schemes. As a first application, we compute both energy-dependent and energy-integrated neutrino emissivities and opacities for conditions extracted from a BNS merger simulation with M1 transport scheme. We find some qualitative differences in the results when considering the impact of the additional relevant reactions and of microphysics effects. For example, neutrino-electron/positron scattering reactions are important for the energy exchange of heavy-type neutrinos as they do not undergo semileptonic charged-current processes, when $μ^\pm$ are not accounted for. Moreover, weak magnetism and mean field effects can significantly modify the contribution of $β$ processes for electron-type (anti)neutrinos, increasing at the same time the importance of (inverse) neutron decays. The improved treatment for the reaction rates also modifies the conditions at which neutrinos decouple from matter in the system, potentially affecting their emission spectra.

## Key claim

The paper presents BNS_NURATES, an open-source numerical library for computing neutrino interaction rates in binary neutron star mergers that achieves higher accuracy than commonly employed prescriptions by including weak magnetism, mean field effects, inelastic neutrino-electron/positron scattering, and (inverse) nucleon decays, but the qualitative differences found in emissivities and opacities depend on the specific thermodynamic conditions and neutrino species considered.

## Method

The authors developed a numerical library that computes spectral (energy-dependent) and gray (energy-integrated) neutrino emissivities and opacities for conditions relevant to binary neutron star mergers, implementing beta processes, electron-positron annihilation, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, isoenergetic scattering off nucleons, and inelastic scattering off electrons and positrons. The library reconstructs the neutrino distribution function from gray radiation moments assuming a linear combination of trapped and free-streaming components, and uses the Kokkos library for parallel GPU acceleration. As a first application, they performed a postprocessing analysis using thermodynamic conditions extracted from a binary neutron star merger simulation with an M1 transport scheme.

## Key numbers

- Rest mass density threshold for mean field effects: rho greater than approximately 10^13 g cm^-3, above which mean field effects enhance both emissivity and opacity of electron neutrinos and electron antineutrinos
- Neutrino-electron/positron scattering provides additional opacity contributions that are sizable wherever charged-current processes are not relevant, specifically for heavy-type neutrinos and deep inside the remnant for electron (anti)neutrinos
- Weak magnetism effect makes electron antineutrino interactions less frequent, particularly at large energies, and remains relevant in regions at lower densities including where electron antineutrinos typically decouple from matter

## Context

This work extends previous efforts in neutrino rate modeling for binary neutron star mergers that relied on leakage schemes or hybrid schemes (Ruffert 1996, Rosswog 2003, Perego 2014, Sekiguchi 2015) and more recent two-moment transport schemes (Foucart 2015, Radice 2021, Schianchi 2023, Cheong 2023). The library builds upon expertise from core-collapse supernova modeling (Bruenn 1985, Horowitz 2001, Oertel 2020) and addresses limitations identified in recent studies showing the importance of detailed microphysics for neutrino emissivities and absorptivities (Bacca 2011, Roberts 2012, Martinez-Pinedo 2012, Fischer 2018, Guo 2020). The inclusion of inelastic neutrino-electron/positron scattering, which was found to be possibly impactful in the merger aftermath by Cheong (2024), and the ability to compute rates on-the-fly rather than relying on precomputed tables, enables more accurate modeling of neutrino decoupling conditions and emission spectra in future binary neutron star merger simulations.
