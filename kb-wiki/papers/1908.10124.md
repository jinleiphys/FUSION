# On the Charge Dependence of the Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant and Nucleon-Nucleon Low-Energy Scattering Parameters

> The simple model describing charge independence and charge symmetry breaking of the pion-nucleon coupling constant is proposed. The model, which has simple physical meaning and foundation, suggests directly proportional dependence of the pion-nucleon coupling constants on the masses of interacting nucleons and pions. Charge dependence of the pion-nucleon coupling constant and the $^{1}S_{0}$-state low-energy nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering parameters are studied on the basis of the Yukawa meson theory. The values of the $np$ and $nn$ interaction characteristics calculated by the proposed model agree quite well with the experimental data.

# On the Charge Dependence of the Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant and Nucleon-Nucleon Low-Energy Scattering Parameters

## Key claim
The pion-nucleon coupling constant is directly proportional to the product of the interacting nucleon and pion masses (f_{Nπ} = C M_N m_π), and this simple phenomenological relation can explain charge independence breaking of nuclear forces almost completely through the mass differences between charged and neutral pions and between the neutron and proton. The model reproduces the experimental charged-pion coupling constant g_c^2 = 14.52(13) only when using the specific value of the proton-proton constant from Bergervoet et al. (1990) as input, and the authors acknowledge that the issue of charge dependence versus charge independence remains unsolved.

## Method
The authors propose a phenomenological model where four elementary pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling constants (f_{pπ^0}, f_{nπ^0}, f_{pπ^-}, f_{nπ^+}) are each proportional to the product of the relevant nucleon mass and pion mass. Using the experimentally established proton-proton pseudovector coupling constant f_{ppπ^0}^2 = 0.0749(7) from Bergervoet et al. (1990) and experimental masses from the Particle Data Group (2014), they calculate the remaining coupling constants via simple mass-ratio formulas. They then compute Yukawa potential parameters for np and nn interactions from the pp parameters, and solve for the low-energy scattering parameters (scattering length and effective range) in the ^1S_0 state using the variable-phase approach.

## Key numbers
- Pseudovector coupling constants derived from the model: f_{nnπ^0}^2 = 0.0751(7), f_0^2 = 0.0750(7), f_c^2 = 0.0802(7)
- Pseudoscalar coupling constants: g_{ppπ^0}^2 = 13.54(13), g_{nnπ^0}^2 = 13.61(13), g_0^2 = 13.58(13), g_c^2 = 14.52(13)
- Ratio of neutron to proton neutral-pion pseudoscalar constants: g_{nπ^0}/g_{pπ^0} = 1.0028
- Calculated np scattering parameters: a_{np} = -23.4(4) fm, r_{np} = 2.70(5) fm (experimental: a_{np}^{expt} = -23.715(8) fm, r_{np}^{expt} = 2.71(7) fm)
- Calculated nn scattering parameters: a_{nn} = -18.2(4) fm, r_{nn} = 2.84(5) fm (experimental from Gabioud et al. (1981): a_{nn}^{expt} = -18.6(5) fm, r_{nn}^{expt} = 2.83(11) fm)
- Theoretical charge independence breaking measure: Δa_{CIB}^{theor} = 5.7(4) fm (experimental: Δa_{CIB}^{expt} = 5.8(3) fm)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Babenko and Petrov, 2016, 2017) and addresses the long-standing debate between the Nijmegen group (de Swart et al., 1997; Stoks et al., 1993) finding g_c^2 ~ 13.54 and the Uppsala Neutron Research Group (Rahm et al., 1998, 2001) finding g_c^2 ~ 14.5-14.7. The model provides a simple mechanism where charge independence breaking arises from particle mass differences, explaining nearly 100% of the experimental Δa_{CIB} compared to only about 50% explained in earlier works (Miller et al., 1990; Machleidt and Banerjee, 2000; Sugie, 1954; Ericson and Miller, 1983). The results support charge dependence of the pion-nucleon coupling constant consistent with QCD expectations, but the authors note that the issue remains unresolved and requires further experimental and theoretical research.
