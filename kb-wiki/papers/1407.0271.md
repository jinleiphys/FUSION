# Effects of strong magnetic fields on the population of hyperon stars

> In this contribution we study the effects of strong magnetic fields on the particle population of neutron stars with hyperon degrees of freedom in their composition. The star matter is described by a multi-component model with parameterized baryon-meson interaction couplings. We study the magnetic effects on the equation of state (EoS) due to the Landau quantization, assuming a density dependent static magnetic field that reaches about $10^{19}\,G$ in the center of the star. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved in order to understand the dependence of the mass-radius relation and hyperon population on the magnetic field intensity assuming different interaction coupling schemes.

# Effects of strong magnetic fields on the population of hyperon stars

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields (up to about 10^19 G at the center) stiffen the equation of state of neutron star matter containing hyperons, but the magnitude and even the ordering of stiffness among different hyperon coupling schemes changes with magnetic field intensity; for example, the HYS(2) coupling model produces the softest equation of state without magnetic field but becomes the stiffest one at B_c = 10^19 G, enabling it to reproduce more massive stars only in the presence of strong magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model with parameterized baryon-meson couplings, including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ^-, Σ^0, Σ^+, Ξ^-, Ξ^0) and leptons (e^-, μ^-). They introduce a density-dependent static magnetic field following a chemical-potential parametrization, with Landau quantization for charged particles, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and particle populations for three different hyperon-meson coupling schemes (HYS(1), HYS(2), HYS(3)).

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for electron Landau quantization: B_C = 4.4 × 10^13 G
- Central magnetic field assumed: B_c = 10^19 G
- Surface magnetic field used in the density-dependent parametrization: B_surf = 10^15 G (the highest measured)
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: ε_B = -16.0 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation constrained to [0.70–0.78] MeV (three λ values: 0.06, 0.10, 0.14 give M*/M = 0.70, 0.75, 0.78 respectively)
- Compression modulus values: K_0 = 262 MeV (λ=0.06), 226 MeV (λ=0.10), 216 MeV (λ=0.14)
- Hyperon coupling HYS(3) uses Λ binding energy (B/A)_Λ = -28 MeV with χ_σΛ = 0.75

## Context

This work extends prior studies of magnetars (Ferrer et al 2010; Woods and Thompson 2006; Mereghetti 2008) and hyperon-rich neutron stars (Buballa et al 2014) by combining strong magnetic fields with hyperon degrees of freedom in a parameterized QHD model. The authors compare three hyperon coupling schemes: HYS(1) based on Moszkowski (1974), HYS(2) based on the quark counting rule from Pal et al (1999), and HYS(3) based on Λ-hypernucleus data from Rufa et al (1990) and Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991). The results enable future comparison with the two-solar-mass neutron star observation (Demorest et al 2010) and motivate better constraints on hyperon-meson couplings and the density dependence of magnetic fields in neutron star interiors.
