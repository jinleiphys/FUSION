# From Nuclear to Unnuclear Physics

> We provide a brief commentary on recent work by Hammer and Son on the scaling behavior of nuclear reactions involving the emission of several loosely bound neutrons. In this work they discover a regime, termed unnuclear physics, in which these reactions are governed by an approximate conformal symmetry of the nuclear force. Remarkably, the scaling exponents that govern nuclear reactions can be related to the energies of ultracold atomic drops confined in harmonic potentials. We also comment on the importance and the limitations of this approximate symmetry in the physics of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Hammer and Son discovered that in a specific low-energy regime where the neutron-neutron scattering length is much larger than the range of nuclear forces, certain nuclear reactions involving the emission of several loosely bound neutrons obey simple scaling rules governed by an approximate conformal symmetry of the nuclear force; however, this regime is limited to momentum transfers where the length scale lies well between the effective range and the scattering length, corresponding to scattering energies roughly between 0.1 MeV and 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors provide a commentary on the theoretical work of Hammer and Son, who analyzed the disintegration of halo nuclei (neutron-rich isotopes such as metastable 6He or 11Li) by low-energy external probes like photons. They showed that the cross section factorizes into a short-distance piece characteristic of the particular nucleus and a long-distance part determined by the scale-invariant interactions between neutrons in the final state. The scaling exponent for the energy dependence of the cross section is related to the ground state energy of N neutrons confined in a harmonic potential, which can be computed numerically or measured experimentally using ultracold fermionic atoms tuned to the unnuclear regime via Feshbach resonances.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron scattering length is |a_nn| approximately 19 fm, much larger than the neutron size of order 1 fm.
- The neutron-neutron effective range is r_nn approximately 2.3 fm.
- The unnuclear regime corresponds to scattering energies epsilon satisfying 0.1 MeV much less than epsilon much less than 5 MeV.
- For two particles in a harmonic trap, the ground state energy exponent Delta equals 2; for three particles, Delta equals 4.27.
- The Bertsch parameter for unnuclear matter, determined from ultracold atomic Fermi gas experiments, is xi equals 0.37.
- Unnuclear behavior in neutron matter persists to densities of about 0.1 fm^{-3}, with repulsive effects becoming significant above approximately 0.2 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This commentary extends the work of Hammer and Son (2021), who discovered the unnuclear physics regime, and relates it to earlier work by Georgi on unparticle physics and by Weinberg (1990) on effective field theory for nucleons. The results enable connections between nuclear reaction cross sections and ultracold atomic experiments, and provide a framework for understanding the equation of state of neutron matter in the inner crust of neutron stars, where observational constraints from neutron stars with masses in excess of two solar masses require the pressure to increase more quickly than the unnuclear equation of state predicts at higher densities.
