# The spin content of the proton

> Considerable progress has been made in our knowledge of the spin distribution within the proton. The recently measured limits on polarized gluons in the proton suggest polarized gluons contribute very modestly to the proton spin. We will show that a modern, relativistic and chirally symmetric description of the nucleon structure naturally explain the current proton spin data. Most of the "missing" spin is carried by confined quark and antiquarks' angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "missing" proton spin is carried by confined quark and antiquark orbital angular momentum, not by polarized gluons, and that a combination of three well-known physics effects, relativistic valence quark motion, one-gluon-exchange hyperfine interactions, and the pion cloud, can naturally explain the modern experimental value of the quark spin fraction Σ ≈ 0.33. The hedge is that the combination of these corrections is only justified because a recent chiral analysis of lattice QCD data suggests the pion cloud contributes very little to the N-Δ mass splitting, allowing the one-gluon-exchange and pion cloud corrections to be combined without double-counting.

## Method

The author uses the cloudy bag model (CBM), a relativistic and chirally symmetric description of the nucleon, to compute the flavor-singlet axial charge Σ. The calculation incorporates three corrections: (1) the relativistic motion of confined valence quarks, which reduces the quark spin contribution by a factor B ≈ 0.65; (2) the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) hyperfine interaction, which introduces two-quark exchange currents that reduce Σ by about 0.15; and (3) the pion cloud, which renormalizes the bare nucleon wave function and reduces Σ by a multiplicative factor of about 0.7. The OGE and pion cloud corrections are combined based on the finding that the pion contributes ≤ 50 MeV of the 300 MeV N-Δ mass difference.

## Key numbers

- Σ = 0.33 ± 0.03 ± 0.05: the modern experimental value for the sum of quark helicities in the proton.
- Σ = (1 − 0.35 − 0.15) · 0.7 = 0.35: the model prediction combining relativistic reduction (35%), OGE correction (0.15), and pion cloud factor (0.7).
- Σ ∈ (0.35, 0.40): the model range for different pion cloud probabilities (P_{Nπ} = 0.20–0.25, P_{Δπ} = 0.05–0.10).
- Σ ∈ (0.21, 0.32): the model range after multiplying by the non-perturbative QCD evolution factor (0.6–0.8 at three loops).
- Relativistic reduction factor B = Ω / 3(Ω − 1) ≈ 0.65 for massless quarks in the bag model (Ω ≈ 2.043).
- Pion cloud probabilities: P_{Nπ} ∼ 0.20–0.25 and P_{Δπ} ∼ 0.05–0.10, with wave function renormalization Z ∼ 0.7.
- Pion contributes ≤ 50 MeV of the 300 MeV N-Δ mass difference, based on chiral analysis of lattice QCD by Young (2002).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical response to the "proton spin crisis" discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) in 1988, which found Σ ≈ 14% (possibly zero) versus the non-relativistic quark model prediction of Σ = 1. It contradicts the earlier hypothesis that polarized gluons (via the U(1) axial anomaly) could explain the missing spin, as recent measurements at CERN, DESY, JLAB, RHIC, and SLAC show Δ_{Glue} contributes very modestly. The paper builds on the earlier work of Myhrer and Thomas (1988) on OGE corrections and Schreiber and Thomas (1988) on pion cloud corrections, and is enabled by the lattice QCD chiral analysis of Young (2002) that justifies combining these corrections. The result motivates experimental searches for quark and antiquark orbital angular momentum, particularly through generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at the 12 GeV Jefferson Lab upgrade.
