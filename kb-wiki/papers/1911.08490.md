# New Flavor-Kinematics Dualities and Extensions of Nonlinear Sigma Models

> Nonlinear sigma model (NLSM) based on the coset $\text{SU}(N)\times \text{SU}(N)/\text{SU}(N)$ exhibits several intriguing features at the leading ${\cal O}(p^2)$ in the derivative expansion, such as the flavor-kinematics duality and an extended theory controlling the single and triple soft limits. In both cases the cubic biadjoint scalar theory plays a prominent role. We extend these features in two directions. First we uncover a new extended theory for $\text{SO}(N+1)/\text{SO}(N)$ NLSM at ${\cal O}(p^2)$, which is a cubic bifundamental/biadjoint scalar theory. Next we provide evidence for flavor-kinematics dualities up to ${\cal O}(p^4)$ for both $\text{SU}(N)$ and $\text{SO}(N)$ NLSM's. In particular, we introduce a new duality building block based on the symmetric tensor $δ^{ab}$ and demonstrate several flavor-kinematics dualities for 4-point amplitudes, which precisely match the soft blocks employed to soft-bootstrap the NLSM's up to ${\cal O} (p^4)$.

## Key claim

The paper extends the flavor-kinematics duality and the extended theory concept, previously known only for the SU(N) nonlinear sigma model (NLSM) at leading order O(p^2), to the SO(N) NLSM at O(p^2) and to both SU(N) and SO(N) NLSMs at next-to-leading order O(p^4), using a new duality building block based on the symmetric tensor delta^{ab}.

## Method

The authors construct the Lagrangian for the SO(N+1)/SO(N) NLSM at O(p^2) and derive its single soft theorem, which reveals an extended theory of cubic bifundamental/biadjoint scalars. For flavor-kinematics duality at O(p^4), they introduce a new building block f_delta(123) = delta^{a1 a3} delta^{a2 a4} - delta^{a1 a4} delta^{a2 a3} and combine it with existing kinematic numerators and permutation-invariant objects to generate four distinct 4-point amplitudes that match the known soft blocks for O(p^4) NLSM operators.

## Key numbers

- The 6-point amplitude for SO(N) NLSM in the pair basis is given by M_6(61|23|45) = -1/4 [s_{23} s_{16} (1/P^2_{235} + 1/P^2_{234}) + s_{23} s_{45} (1/P^2_{236} + 1/P^2_{123}) + s_{45} s_{16} (1/P^2_{245} + 1/P^2_{345}) - s_{23} - s_{45} - s_{16}], with coupling constants suppressed.
- The 5-point extended theory amplitudes for SO(N) are M_5(123|45||1-bar 2-bar 3-bar) = -s_{45}/2 (1/s_{12} + 1/s_{23}) and M_5(123|45||1-bar 2-bar 4-bar) = -1/2 (s_{45}/s_{12} - s_{25}/s_{14}).
- The four O(p^4) soft blocks are S_1^{(4)}(1234) = u^2/(Lambda^2 f^2), S_2^{(4)}(1234) = st/(Lambda^2 f^2), S_1^{(4)}(12|34) = s^2/(Lambda^2 f^2), and S_2^{(4)}(12|34) = tu/(Lambda^2 f^2), where Lambda is a dimensionful parameter controlling derivative expansion convergence.

## Context

This work extends the flavor-kinematics duality and extended theory previously established for SU(N) NLSM at O(p^2) by Cachazo (2016), Low (2017), and Low (2018) to the SO(N) coset and to higher derivative orders. Prior work by Elvang (2018) and Carrillo-Gonzalez (2019) claimed that O(p^2) BCJ relations fail at O(p^4), but the authors provide a more general construction using the new delta^{ab} building block that succeeds at O(p^4). The results enable systematic construction of higher-derivative NLSM amplitudes via flavor-kinematics duality and suggest potential applications to Yang-Mills theories.
