# Glue in the light-front pion

> It may be possible to approximate the full pion wave function in light-front QCD using only $q \qbar$ and $q \qbar g$ Fock space components. Removing zero modes and using invariant-mass cutoffs that make a constituent approximation possible leads to non-canonical terms in the QCD hamiltonian, and forces us to work in the broken symmetry phase of QCD in which chiral symmetry breaking operators must appear directly in the hamiltonian because the vacuum is trivial. Assuming any candidate interactions are local in the transverse direction, I argue that they are probably relevant operators so that perturbation theory is not modified at high energies. Since light-front chiral symmetry corresponds to quark helicity conservation, we can readily identify candidate chiral symmetry breaking interactions. The only candidate relevant operator is the quark-gluon emission/absorption operator with a quark spin flip. I argue that this operator can only produce the physical $π-ρ$ mass splitting if the $q \qbar g$ component of the pion is significant.

## Key claim
The pion may be approximated using only \(q\bar{q}\) and \(q\bar{q}g\) Fock space components in light-front QCD, but only if the \(q\bar{q}g\) component is significant enough to produce the physical \(\pi-\rho\) mass splitting via a single relevant chiral-symmetry-breaking operator (gluon emission/absorption with a quark spin flip). The author hedges that this conclusion depends on the assumption that all induced non-canonical couplings vanish with the canonical QCD coupling and that the analysis is drastically oversimplified.

## Method
The author analyzes light-front QCD with zero modes removed and invariant-mass cutoffs, which forces the theory into a broken-symmetry phase where chiral symmetry breaking operators must appear directly in the Hamiltonian. He identifies the only relevant operator that violates light-front chiral symmetry (quark-gluon emission/absorption with a helicity flip) and estimates its effect on the \(\pi-\rho\) mass splitting using a crude two-state mixing model between a single \(q\bar{q}\) mode and a single \(q\bar{q}g\) mode, adjusting the off-diagonal matrix element to produce a massless pion.

## Key numbers
- The probability of finding a \(q\bar{q}\) component in the pion is estimated to be of order \(1/4\) from prior work by Lepage, Brodsky, Huang, and Mackenzie, but the author notes this may be higher.
- In the crude two-state mixing model, the probability of finding the \(q\bar{q}\) component is \(P_{q\bar{q}} = M_1 / (M_0 + M_1)\) and the probability of finding the \(q\bar{q}g\) component is \(P_{q\bar{q}g} = M_0 / (M_0 + M_1)\), where \(M_0\) and \(M_1\) are the unperturbed masses of the \(q\bar{q}\) and \(q\bar{q}g\) modes respectively.
- The analysis leads to the conclusion that the probability of finding glue in the pion may only be of order \(1/2\).

## Context
This work extends the light-front QCD program advocated by Brodsky, Lepage, and collaborators (reviewed in their light-front review) and the renormalization techniques developed by Ken Wilson and colleagues for QCD bound states. It builds on prior results by Perry (2000) showing that confinement appears in the renormalized light-front QCD Hamiltonian at second order, and on calculations by Brisudova (heavy mesons) and Allen (glueballs) using that Hamiltonian without non-canonical parameters. The paper argues that the pion is unique among hadrons in requiring a significant glue component, contradicting the constituent quark model’s applicability to the pion, and suggests that future work must include constituent glue in any QCD-derived model of the pion.
