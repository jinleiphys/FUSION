# Closed-form Brückner $G$-matrix and nuclear matter in EFT($\not\!π$)

> The closed-form Brükner $G$ matrix for nuclear matter is computed in the $^1S_0$ channel of EFT($\not\!\!π$) and renormalized in nonperturbative context. The nuclear medium environment yields additional constraints that are consistent with off-shell $T$ matrix renormalization, keeping the power counting intact and simplifying the running behaviors of the EFT couplings. With the $G$ obtained we computed the energy per particle for neutron and symmetric nuclear matter to demonstrate the physical relevance of certain 'physical' parameters that arise from nonperturbative renormalization. We also explored the pairing phenomenon in $^1S_0$ channel by examining the poles of the closed-form $G$ matrix with a given density, where again the physical relevance of the same set of physical parameters are clearly illustrated.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed nonperturbative renormalization scheme for closed-form T matrices in pionless EFT to the many-body context of nuclear matter, showing that the Brückner G matrix can be obtained in closed form and that the medium provides additional constraints that render certain parameters (J₀ and σ_{s;2}) physical rather than running, though their values must still be determined by external inputs.

## Method

The Bethe-Goldstone equation for the Brückner G matrix in the ¹S₀ channel is solved in closed form using a separable potential from pionless EFT truncated at orders Q⁰ and Q². The renormalization is performed by imposing tight constraints from the closed-form structure, yielding density-independent running couplings and density-dependent renormalization-group-invariant ratios. The on-shell G matrix is then used to compute the energy per particle for neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, and the pairing phenomenon is explored by finding real poles of the G matrix below the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The upper scale of EFT(π̸) is given as Λ_{π̸} ≈ 0.699 fm⁻¹.
- For Λ_{J₀} = 138 MeV and x = ±0.2, the pairing gap δ_{pole} at p_F = 0.2 fm⁻¹ is 2.07 MeV (x = +0.2) and 2.09 MeV (x = −0.2); at p_F = 0.6 fm⁻¹ it is 0.07 MeV (x = +0.2) and 0.09 MeV (x = −0.2).
- The leading-order energy per particle formula contains a denominator (π − 2a_s p_F), with the scattering length a_s giving a critical Fermi momentum π/(2a_s) ≈ 13.1 MeV.
- For Λ_{J₀} = 35 MeV, a pathological second-order pole in G appears at p_F ≈ 0.6588 fm⁻¹ (x = 0.2) and p_F ≈ 0.6289 fm⁻¹ (x = −0.2), with pole positions α₊ ≈ 0.3996 fm⁻¹ and α₋ ≈ 0.3783 fm⁻¹ respectively.
- For Λ_{J₀} = 138 MeV, the near-Fermi-surface pole ceases to exist for p_F > 0.68 fm⁻¹, close to Λ_{π̸}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous closed-form T-matrix solutions in pionless EFT (Yang and collaborators, 2018, 2019) to the many-body context via the Brückner G matrix, following the first computation of the G matrix in pionless EFT by Krippa (2000). It contrasts with perturbative many-body approaches such as those of Steele (2000) and the Hartree-Fock treatment, and with the KSW perturbative scheme. The results enable nonperturbative studies of nuclear matter saturation and pairing within a renormalized EFT framework, while raising the open question of how to determine the physical parameters J₀ and σ_{s;2} from data.
