# A local-density-approximation description of high-momentum tails in isospin asymmetric nuclei

> We adapt the local density approximation to add the high-momentum tails (HMTs) to finite nuclei's Slater-determinant momentum distributions. The HMTs are extracted by the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) method or by the lowest order cluster approximation. With a correction factor being added to EBHF, it is sufficiently in agreement with the experimental benchmark, i.e., the high-momentum $N/Z$ ratios approximately equal to $1$, and the low-momentum $N/Z$ ratios approximately equal to $N/Z$ of the systems. It is also found that the tensor force makes the nucleon-nucleon correlations appear more easily on the nuclear surface region and the percentage of high-momentum ($p > 300$ MeV/c) nucleons, around $17\%$--$18\%$, independent of isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The authors adapt the local density approximation (LDA) to add high-momentum tails (HMTs) to finite nuclei's Slater-determinant momentum distributions, and introduce a phenomenological isospin-dependent correction factor to the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) correlation term to ensure that the high-momentum neutron-to-proton ratio approaches unity, consistent with experimental benchmarks.

## Method

The authors combine Slater-determinant momentum distributions from the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) model with Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) pairing using the SkM* interaction, and add correlation terms via the local density approximation. The correlation terms are extracted either from the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) method with a microscopic three-body force and the Argonne V18 interaction, or from the lowest order cluster (LOC) approximation with a Jastrow correlation function. A correction factor ξ = (N/Z)^(-λ/2) is applied to the EBHF correlation term to enforce equal numbers of high-momentum protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The percentage of high-momentum (p > 300 MeV/c) nucleons is around 17%–18%, independent of isospin asymmetry.
- For the LOC approximation, the correlation factor β = 1.1 fm⁻¹ (k_dir = 0.23) yields approximately 20% high-momentum nucleons, consistent with experimental estimates.
- The high-momentum N/Z ratios approximately equal 1, and the low-momentum (p < 250 MeV/c) N/Z ratios approximately equal the N/Z of the system, for ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The saturation density used is ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The EBHF parameterization gives χ^τ(ρ, δ) = 0.1669[1+λ(0.1407 ρ/ρ₀ - 0.7296)δ][1+2.448 exp(-4.1854 ρ/ρ₀) + 0.1382(ρ/ρ₀)^(1.5)], where λ = ±1 for neutrons/protons.

## Context

This work extends the LDA approach previously applied by Stringari (1990) and Gaidarov (2009) to include HMTs from EBHF calculations by Yang (2019), and addresses the discrepancy that earlier LOC-based methods could not reproduce the experimentally observed pn-dominated short-range correlations from Duer (2018). The results enable predictions for unmeasured nuclei such as ⁶⁴Ni, ⁸⁶Kr, and ¹²⁰Sn, and may explain α-cluster formation on neutron-rich Sn isotope surfaces as observed by Tanaka (2021), as well as inform transport models for heavy-ion collisions.
