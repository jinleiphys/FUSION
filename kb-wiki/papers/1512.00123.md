# Structure of the $螞(1405)$ and the $K^-d\rightarrow蟺危n$ reaction

> The $螞(1405)$ resonance production reaction is investigated within the framework of the coupled-channels Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations. We perform full three-body calculations for the $\bar{K}NN-蟺YN$ amplitudes on the physical real energy axis and investigate how the signature of the $螞(1405)$ appears in the cross sections of the $K^-d\rightarrow 蟺危n$ reactions, also in view of the planned E31 experiment at J-PARC. Two types of meson-baryon interaction models are considered: an energy-dependent interaction based on chiral $SU(3)$ effective field theory, and an energy-independent version that has been used repeatedly in phenomenological approaches. These two models have different off-shell properties that imply correspondingly different behavior in the three-body system. We investigate how these features show up in differential cross sections of $K^- d\rightarrow 蟺危n$ reactions. Characteristic patterns distinguishing between the two models are found in the invariant mass spectrum of the final $蟺危$ state. The $K^-d\rightarrow 蟺危n$ reaction, with different ($蟺^{\pm}危^{\mp}$ and $蟺^{0}危^{0}$) charge combinations in the final state, is thus demonstrated to be a useful tool for investigating the subthreshold behavior of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the $K^- d \rightarrow \pi \Sigma n$ reaction, with full three-body coupled-channels calculations, can distinguish between two classes of $\bar{K}N$ interaction models (energy-dependent chiral SU(3) dynamics and energy-independent phenomenological potentials) through characteristic patterns in the $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass spectrum, but only if the different off-shell properties of these models are properly accounted for in the three-body dynamics.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled-channels Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi YN$ three-body system on the physical real energy axis, using separable two-body interactions. Two meson-baryon interaction models were employed: an energy-dependent model based on the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and an energy-independent model with couplings fixed at threshold energies, both fitted to $K^-p$ scattering cross sections. The resulting AGS amplitudes were used to compute differential cross sections for the $K^- d \rightarrow \pi \Sigma n$ reaction, decomposed into $\pi^+ \Sigma^- n$, $\pi^0 \Sigma^0 n$, and $\pi^- \Sigma^+ n$ final states.

## Key numbers
- The energy-dependent (E-dep.) model produces two poles in the $\bar{K}N$ amplitude on the complex energy plane, while the energy-independent (E-indep.) model produces a single pole corresponding to the $\Lambda(1405)$.
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ nominal mass is 1405 MeV, located 27 MeV below the $K^- p$ threshold.
- The chiral SU(3) dynamics pole at 1420 MeV corresponds to a $\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound system with a binding energy of 15 MeV, about half the binding produced with purely phenomenological $\bar{K}N$ potentials (approximately 30 MeV).
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV.
- Cutoff parameters for the E-dep. model: $\Lambda^{(I=0)}_{Y_K} = 1100$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=0)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Sigma} = 1100$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_K} = 800$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Sigma} = 800$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Lambda} = 800$ MeV.
- Cutoff parameters for the E-indep. model: $\Lambda^{(I=0)}_{Y_K} = 1160$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=0)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Sigma} = 1100$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_K} = 1100$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Sigma} = 850$ MeV, $\Lambda^{(I=1)}_{Y_\pi=\pi\Lambda} = 1250$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $K^- d \rightarrow \pi \Sigma n$ reaction that used simplified two-step process models (Jido 2009, Miyagawa 2012, Jido 2012, Yamagata-Sekihara 2012) by performing a full three-body calculation using the AGS equations. It builds on earlier three-body calculations of the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi YN$ system (Ikeda 2007, Ikeda 2008, Ikeda 2010) and is motivated by the planned E31 experiment at J-PARC with a 1 GeV $K^-$ beam, which will measure $\pi\Sigma$ production cross sections separately for all charge combinations. The results enable extraction of the subthreshold $\bar{K}N$ interaction from experimental spectra, complementing constraints from kaonic hydrogen measurements (SIDDHARTA collaboration, Bazzi 2011, Bazzi 2012) and $\pi\Sigma$ spectra from photoproduction (LEPS at SPring-8, Ahn 2003; CLAS at JLab, Moriya 2013) and $pp$ collisions (HADES at GSI, Agakishiev 2012).
