# Vacuum Energy, EoS, and the Gluon Condensate at Finite Baryon Density in QCD

> The Equation of States (EoS) plays the crucial role in all studies of neutron star properties. Still, a microscopical understanding of EoS remains largely an unresolved problem. We use 2-color QCD as a model to study the dependence of vacuum energy (gluon condensate in QCD) as function of chemical potential μ\ll Λ_{QCD} where we find very strong and unexpected dependence on $μ$. We present the arguments suggesting that similar behavior may occur in 3-color QCD in the color superconducting phases. Such a study may be of importance for analysis of EoS when phenomenologically relevant parameters (within such models as MIT Bag model or NJL model) are fixed at zero density while the region of study lies at much higher densities not available for terrestrial tests.

# Vacuum Energy, EoS, and the Gluon Condensate at Finite Baryon Density in QCD

## Key claim
The standard assumption that phenomenological model parameters (such as the bag constant in the MIT Bag model or NJL model) fixed at zero baryon density remain unchanged at nonzero chemical potential may be badly violated in QCD, based on exact calculations in 2-color QCD showing a strong and unexpected dependence of the gluon condensate on chemical potential for μ ≪ Λ_QCD.

## Method
The author uses 2-color QCD as a tractable model, employing the effective chiral Lagrangian to compute the change in the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (conformal anomaly) at finite chemical potential μ. The variation of the gluon condensate ⟨G²⟩ with μ is then extracted from the free energy density, pressure, and baryon number density, which are known from prior calculations in the superfluid phase for μ > m_π.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear matter saturation density, the change of the gluon condensate is only about 5% (Cohen 1991).
- The beta-function coefficient for 2-color QCD with N_f = 2 massless quarks is b = 6.
- The gluon condensate variation in the superfluid phase of 2-color QCD is given by ⟨G²⟩_μ,m − ⟨G²⟩_μ=0,m = 4 F² (μ² − m²_π) (1 − 2 m²_π/μ²), valid for μ ≪ Λ_QCD.
- The condensate decreases with μ for m_π < μ < 2^{1/4} m_π and increases afterwards.
- For real 3-color QCD, the transition to color superconducting phases is expected at μ_c ≃ 2.3 · Λ_QCD.

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis by Cohen (1991) on the density dependence of the gluon condensate in nuclear matter, which found only a small (5%) effect at saturation density. The author argues that the situation is drastically different in color superconducting phases, where binding energies are of order Λ_QCD rather than the small binding energies of nuclear matter. The exact results in 2-color QCD provide a controlled model for the nontrivial μ-dependence expected in 3-color QCD, and the predictions are consistent with lattice calculations by Hands (2006) and Alles (2006). This work enables improved modeling of the equation of state for neutron star studies, where phenomenological parameters are typically extrapolated from zero density.
