# Spinodal decomposition of low-density asymmetric nuclear matter

> We investigate the dynamical properties of asymmetric nuclear matter at low density. The occurrence of new instabilities, that lead the system to a dynamical fragment formation, is illustrated, discussing in particular the charge symmetry dependence of the structure of the most important unstable modes. We observe that instabilities are reduced by charge asymmetry, leading to larger size and time scales in the fragmentation process. Configurations with less asymmetric fragments surrounded by a more asymmetric gas are favoured. Interesting variances with respect to a pure thermodynamical prediction are revealed, that can be checked experimentally. All these features are deeply related to the structure of the symmetry term in the nuclear Equation of State (EOS) and could be used to extract information on the low density part of the EOS.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in low-density asymmetric nuclear matter, spinodal decomposition produces fragments that are more symmetric (closer to N=Z) than the initial system, surrounded by a more neutron-rich gas, and that this dynamical effect leads to a proton fraction in the gas phase larger than what equilibrium thermodynamics would predict, especially on the short time scales relevant for heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed a linear response analysis of coupled Vlasov equations for neutron and proton liquids, using a Skyrme-like mean field, to obtain dispersion relations and growth rates for unstable density perturbations. They then solved the full Landau-Vlasov equation numerically using a test-particle method with periodic boundary conditions in a cubic box, including the Boltzmann-Nordheim collision integral via Monte Carlo, to simulate all stages of fragment formation from initial density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density parameters: binding energy 15.7 MeV/nucleon at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, compressibility modulus K = 201 MeV, surface energy coefficient a_surf = 18.6 MeV.
- Potential symmetry energy coefficient at saturation: C(ρ₀) = 31.4 MeV, with symmetry energy coefficient a_sym = 28 MeV and Fermi energy ε_F^eq = 36.9 MeV.
- Growth rate Γ₀ ranges from 0.01 to 0.03 c/fm for the most unstable mode, with corresponding wave vector k₀ between 0.5 and 1 fm⁻¹.
- For initial asymmetry I = 0.5 at ρ = 0.4ρ₀ and T = 5 MeV: extracted growth time Γ ≈ 0.01 c/fm, perturbation asymmetry I_pt ≈ 0.24, and wavelength λ ≈ 12 fm.
- Numerical simulations used a cubic box with side L = 24 fm, initial density ρ^(0) = 0.4ρ₀, temperature T = 5 MeV, and initial asymmetries I = 0, 0.25, and 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinodal decomposition in symmetric nuclear matter (Pethick 1987-1993, Margueron 1993-1994, Colonna 1995) and the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric matter (Jensen 1995, Mueller 1995, Baldo 1996, Ray 1996) by focusing on non-equilibrium dynamical clustering on short time scales. The results enable experimental tests using fragmentation reactions with radioactive beams to extract information on the low-density part of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state, relevant for neutron star structure.
