# Nuclear Wave Functions for Spin and Pseudospin Partners

> Using relations between wave functions obtained in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory, we investigate the effects of pseudospin and spin symmetry breaking on the single nucleon wave functions in spherical nuclei. In our analysis, we apply both relativistic and non-relativistic self-consistent models as well as the harmonic oscillator model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both pseudospin and spin symmetry are "dynamic symmetries" in spherical nuclei: the energy levels of the doublet partners are not degenerate, but the single-nucleon wave functions (eigenfunctions) approximately preserve the symmetry relations predicted by the relativistic mean field theory, even in non-relativistic models.

## Method

The authors tested the differential relations that connect the upper components of pseudospin doublet states and the equality of the lower components (for pseudospin), as well as the analogous relations for spin doublets, by computing single-nucleon wave functions for the spherical doubly-magic nucleus ²⁰⁸Pb. They performed three types of calculations: (i) a harmonic oscillator (HO) model with standard frequency ℏω = 41/A¹/³, (ii) non-relativistic self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme force, and (iii) relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with the NL1 parameter set. The differential relations were evaluated numerically and compared across the three models.

## Key numbers

- The factor λ = 2M + V_S(r) – V_V(r) – E, which relates the upper and lower components of the Dirac wave function in the nuclear interior, is approximately 6 fm⁻¹ in the RMF calculations for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The harmonic oscillator frequency used was ℏω = 41/A¹/³, which for ²⁰⁸Pb (A = 208) gives ℏω ≈ 41/208¹/³ ≈ 6.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of pseudospin symmetry, whose origin was explained by Ginocchio (1997) in terms of near-equality of the scalar and vector potentials in RMF theory, and which had been previously tested only for RMF eigenfunctions (Ginocchio, 2002). The paper shows that the same pseudospin symmetry relations are approximately satisfied by non-relativistic HF and HO wave functions, and that spin symmetry (which requires V_S – V_V = constant) also exhibits dynamic symmetry in both relativistic and non-relativistic models. This enables future studies of shell effects in superheavy nuclei and nuclei far from stability, where subtle changes in the interaction may alter magic numbers.
