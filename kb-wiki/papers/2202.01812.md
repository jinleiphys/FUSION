# A Challenge for Discrimination of Color-Singlet versus Color-Octet Quarkonium Production

> The precise mechanism for production of quarkonium at hadron colliders is still an open question. Within non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics, quarkonium production cross sections can be factorized into short-distance, perturbative contributions and universal, non-perturbative, long-distance matrix elements, and then summed over quantum numbers of the heavy quark pair. In principle, at short-distances, the heavy quark pair can be either in a color-singlet or color-octet state, and it is desirable to establish the relative contributions to compare with data and to make predictions in different experimental environments. From the explicit form of the lowest-order perturbative matrix elements for color-singlet and color-octet production, we show that the structure of the optimal observable for discrimination on phase space, the likelihood ratio, has strong dependence on the angular momentum state of the heavy quark pair. This presents an obstruction for construction of a single, robust discrimination observable that can be applied to production of an arbitrary quarkonium state.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the likelihood ratio for discriminating color-singlet versus color-octet quarkonium production has strong dependence on the angular momentum state of the heavy quark pair, which presents an obstruction for constructing a single, robust discrimination observable applicable to production of an arbitrary quarkonium state. The author qualifies that this is not a no-go theorem, but that any proposed discrimination observable would depend on the hadron of interest.

## Method
The author computed the lowest-order perturbative amplitudes for the process gg → [Q\bar Q]g for both color-singlet and color-octet heavy quark pair states with angular momentum quantum numbers ^1S_0, ^3S_1, ^3P_0, ^3P_1, and ^3P_2. For each angular momentum state, the likelihood ratio (ratio of color-octet to color-singlet squared matrix elements) was analyzed as a function of the Mandelstam invariants s, t, u on phase space, with contour plots showing the locations of maxima and minima.

## Key numbers
- For the ^1S_0 state, the likelihood ratio minimum is 2/3 at s = t = u = M^2/3, and the ratio equals 1 in the collinear limit (u → 0)
- For the ^3S_1 state, the likelihood ratio minimum is 5/19 ≈ 0.263 at s = t = u = M^2/3, and the ratio equals 1/2 in the soft gluon limit (u, t → 0)
- For the ^3P_0 state, the likelihood ratio minimum is 127/369 ≈ 0.344 at s = t = u = M^2/3, and the ratio diverges to infinity in the soft gluon limit (u, t → 0)
- For the ^3P_1 state, the likelihood ratio maximum is 3/2 at s = t = u = M^2/3, and the ratio ranges between 1/2 and 1 in the collinear limit (u → 0)
- For the ^3P_2 state, the likelihood ratio maximum is 41/18 ≈ 2.28 at s = t = u = M^2/3, and the ratio equals 1/2 in the collinear limit (u → 0)

## Context
This work extends the study of quarkonium production within non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) as formulated by Bodwin (1994), where cross sections factorize into short-distance perturbative contributions and long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs). The paper addresses the open question of distinguishing color-singlet versus color-octet production mechanisms, which has been studied experimentally by CDF (2000, 2007), LHCb (2011-2019), ATLAS (2011-2017), CMS (2011-2019), and ALICE (2011-2019). The analysis uses the Neyman-Pearson lemma (1933) to identify the likelihood ratio as the optimal discriminant, and proposes that future work could use numerical programs like HELAC-Onia (Shao, 2012, 2015) or Mad-Onia (Artoisenet, 2007) for more realistic studies including next-to-leading order corrections and parton showers like Pythia 8 (Sjostrand, 2007).
