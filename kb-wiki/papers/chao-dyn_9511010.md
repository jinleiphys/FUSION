# Random band matrix approach to chaotic scattering: the average $S$-matrix and its pole distribution

> Random band matrices relevant for open chaotic systems are introduced and studied. The scattering model based on such matrices may serve for the description of preequilibrium chaotic scattering. In the limit of a large number of open channels we calculate the average $S$-matrix and $S$-matrix's pole distribution which are found to reduce to those of the full matrix (GOE) case under proper renormalization of the energy scale and strength of coupling to the continuum.

# Random band matrix approach to chaotic scattering: the average $S$-matrix and its pole distribution

## Key claim

The paper claims that for open chaotic systems described by random band matrices (RBM), the average $S$-matrix and $S$-matrix pole distribution reduce exactly to those of the full Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) case, but only under a proper renormalization of the energy scale and coupling strength to the continuum; nontrivial consequences of the band structure are expected to appear only in higher correlation functions, not in these one-point averages.

## Method

The author constructs a scattering model using an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian whose Hermitian part belongs to the Gaussian random band matrix ensemble with bandwidth $b\gg 1$, and whose coupling amplitudes to $M$ open channels are fixed and satisfy a sum rule. Using the replica method and saddle-point approximation in the limit of large matrix dimension $N$ and large number of open channels $M$ (with $m=M/N<1$ fixed), the average Green function, average $S$-matrix, and the average density of complex energy levels (pole distribution) are calculated analytically.

## Key numbers

- The effective bandwidth is $b\gg 1$, with the correlation function $J_{nm}\equiv J(|n-m|)$ decreasing sufficiently fast for $|n-m|>b$.
- The coupling strength parameter $\gamma$ satisfies $\sum_n V^a_n V^b_n = \gamma \lambda \delta^{ab}$, where $\lambda$ sets the energy scale.
- The renormalized energy scale is $\widetilde \lambda = \lambda\sqrt{J_0}$, where $J_0\equiv\sum_r J(|r|)$.
- The renormalized coupling constant is $\widetilde \gamma = \gamma/\sqrt{J_0}$.
- The average density of complex energy levels is given by $4\pi\rho(x,y) = m/y^2 + (1-m)/(\gamma\lambda+y)^2 - 1/(J_0\lambda^2)$, valid inside the region bounded by ${\sf x}^2(y) = -4m\gamma\lambda^3 J_0/y - [m\lambda^2 J_0/y + (1-m)\lambda^2 J_0/(\gamma\lambda+y) - \gamma\lambda]^2$.

## Context

This work extends the random band matrix theory of Fyodorov and Mirlin (1991, 1995) from closed systems to open chaotic systems, and generalizes the GOE-based scattering models of Weidenmuller (1984), Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller, and Zirnbauer (1985), and Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989). The band structure introduces a diffusion time scale for internal relaxation, making the model relevant for preequilibrium chaotic scattering intermediate between fully chaotic GOE scattering and multistep compound reactions as described by Nishioka, Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller, and Yoshida (1986). The results enable future study of higher correlation functions where band structure effects are expected to become nontrivial.
