# Overcoming Velocity Suppression in Dark-Matter Direct-Detection Experiments

> Pseudoscalar couplings between Standard-Model quarks and dark matter are normally not considered relevant for dark-matter direct-detection experiments because they lead to velocity-suppressed scattering cross-sections in the non-relativistic limit. However, at the nucleon level, such couplings are effectively enhanced by factors of order ${\cal O}(m_N/m_q)\sim 10^3$, where $m_N$ and $m_q$ are appropriate nucleon and quark masses respectively. This enhancement can thus be sufficient to overcome the corresponding velocity suppression, implying --- contrary to common lore --- that direct-detection experiments can indeed be sensitive to pseudoscalar couplings. In this work, we explain how this enhancement arises, and present a model-independent analysis of pseudoscalar interactions at direct-detection experiments. We also identify those portions of the corresponding dark-matter parameter space which can be probed at current and future experiments of this type, and discuss the role of isospin violation in enhancing the corresponding experimental reach.

## Key claim

Pseudoscalar couplings between dark matter and Standard-Model quarks, which are normally considered irrelevant for direct-detection experiments because they produce velocity-suppressed scattering cross-sections, can actually be probed because the transition from quark-level to nucleon-level couplings introduces an enhancement factor of order \(m_N/m_q \sim 10^3\) that can overcome the velocity suppression, contrary to common lore.

## Method

The authors derive the nucleon-level pseudoscalar coupling coefficients \(\Delta \tilde q^{(N)}\) from the axial-vector coefficients \(\Delta q^{(N)}\) using the axial-current divergence relation and the large-\(N_c\) chiral limit approximation, incorporating uncertainties from lattice QCD and experimental measurements. They then compute the resulting dark-matter/nucleon scattering amplitudes and identify regions of dark-matter parameter space that can be probed by current and future direct-detection experiments, including the role of isospin violation.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar coefficients \(\Delta \tilde u^{(p)} = 110.55 \pm 21.87\) and \(\Delta \tilde d^{(p)} = -107.17 \pm 21.14\) for protons, compared to axial-vector coefficients \(\Delta u^{(p)} = 0.787 \pm 0.158\) and \(\Delta d^{(p)} = -0.319 \pm 0.066\), an enhancement of order \(10^2\)–\(10^3\)
* Pseudoscalar coefficients \(\Delta \tilde u^{(n)} = -108.03 \pm 21.33\) and \(\Delta \tilde d^{(n)} = 108.60 \pm 21.29\) for neutrons
* Pseudoscalar coefficient \(\Delta \tilde s^{(p)} = -3.37 \pm 1.01\) and \(\Delta \tilde s^{(n)} = -0.57 \pm 0.78\)
* Quark masses used: \(m_u = 2.3 \pm 0.7\) MeV, \(m_d = 4.8 \pm 0.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 95 \pm 5\) MeV at renormalization scale \(\mu = 2\) GeV in \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, rescaled by factor 1.35
* The velocity suppression factor for pseudoscalar scattering is \(v \sim \mathcal{O}(10^{-3})\) for galactic-halo dark matter

## Context

This work extends the study of dark-matter direct-detection phenomenology beyond the commonly considered scalar, vector, and axial-vector couplings (Goodman and Witten, 1985; Jungman, Kamionkowski, and Griest; various direct-detection reviews) by demonstrating that pseudoscalar couplings, previously dismissed due to velocity suppression, can produce observable signals. The analysis builds on the relation between axial-vector and pseudoscalar currents (Cheng; Bai, Fox, and Harnik, 2010) and uses nucleon spin-fraction data from lattice QCD (QCDSF collaboration) and experimental measurements (COMPASS, SMC, HERMES). The results enable experimental collaborations (such as COUPP with fluorine targets) to interpret their null results in terms of pseudoscalar coupling parameter space and motivate searches for isospin-violating pseudoscalar interactions.
