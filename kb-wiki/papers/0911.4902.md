# Isospin mixing and the continuum coupling in weakly bound nuclei

> The isospin breaking effects due to the Coulomb interaction in weakly-bound nuclei are studied using the Gamow Shell Model, a complex-energy configuration interaction approach which simultaneously takes into account many-body correlations between valence nucleons and continuum effects. We investigate the near-threshold behavior of one-nucleon spectroscopic factors and the structure of wave functions along an isomultiplet. Illustrative calculations are carried out for the T=1 isobaric triplet. By using a shell-model Hamiltonian consisting of an isoscalar nuclear interaction and the Coulomb term, we demonstrate that for weakly bound or unbound systems the structure of isobaric analog states varies within the isotriplet and impacts the energy dependence of spectroscopic factors. We discuss the partial dynamical isospin symmetry present in isospin-stretched systems, in spite of the Coulomb interaction that gives rise to large mirror symmetry breaking effects.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that for weakly bound or unbound nuclei, the structure of isobaric analog states varies within an isotriplet and impacts the energy dependence of spectroscopic factors, even when the binding energy effect is removed by assuming identical one-nucleon emission thresholds. This reveals that continuum coupling alone, without the binding energy difference, can produce significant mirror symmetry breaking in spectroscopic factors.

## Method
The Gamow Shell Model (GSM) was used with a Berggren single-particle basis consisting of Gamow states and non-resonant scattering continuum, applied to the T=1 isobaric triplet of ⁶He, ⁶Li, and ⁶Be with a ⁴He core. The Hamiltonian included an isoscalar Woods-Saxon potential, a modified finite-range surface Gaussian interaction (MSGI) for the two-body residual interaction, and the Coulomb interaction treated via a dilatation-analytic form and harmonic oscillator expansion for the two-body part. Calculations were performed for three different values of the one-particle threshold energy in the A=5 systems (E_T = -1.5, 0, and 0.5 MeV) to isolate continuum coupling effects from binding energy effects.

## Key numbers
- For ⁶He with the "⁵He" parameter set (V0=47 MeV), the calculated ground state energy is E_0+ = -0.974 MeV and the 2+ state energy is E_2+ = +0.823 MeV with width Γ_2+ = 89 keV, compared to experimental values of E_0+ = -0.973 MeV, E_2+ = +0.824 MeV, and Γ_2+ = 113 keV.
- For ⁶Be in variant V1, the predicted ground state energy is E_0+ = 1.653 MeV with Γ_0+ = 41 keV; in variant V2, E_0+ = 1.371 MeV with Γ_0+ = 14 keV, compared to experimental E_0+ = 1.371 MeV and Γ_0+ = 92 keV.
- For the 0+ IAS in ⁶Li, the predicted energy in V1 is E_0+ = 0.0866 MeV with Γ_0+ = 8.85×10⁻³ keV, and in V2 is E_0+ = -0.0706 MeV with Γ_0+ = 9.13×10⁻³ keV, compared to experimental E_0+ = -0.136 MeV and Γ_0+ = 8.2 eV.
- The ground state spectroscopic factors are S² = 0.87 - i0.383 for ⁶He, S² = 1.015 - i0.147 for ⁶Be (V1), and for ⁶Li: 1.061 - i0.280 for the π p₃/₂ channel and 0.911 - i0.361 for the ν p₃/₂ channel.
- For the 2₁⁺ state, spectroscopic factors are S² = 1.061 + i0.0011 for ⁶He, 0.973 - i0.0142 for ⁶Be, 0.987 - i3.26×10⁻³ for ⁶Li (π p₃/₂), and 1.034 - i0.0235 for ⁶Li (ν p₃/₂).

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin breaking in mirror nuclei beyond the Thomas-Ehrman effect (Ehrman 1951, Thomas 1952) by using the Gamow Shell Model to simultaneously treat many-body correlations and continuum coupling. It builds on prior GSM developments (Michel 2002, 2004, 2009) and earlier studies of mirror symmetry breaking in spectroscopic factors using cluster approaches (Timofeyuk 2008). The results enable future investigations of isospin mixing in weakly bound and unbound nuclei near drip lines, and demonstrate that partial dynamical isospin symmetry can persist in isospin-stretched systems despite large Coulomb-induced mirror symmetry breaking.
