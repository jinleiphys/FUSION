# Connecting Matrix Elements to Multi-Hadron Form-Factors

> We discuss developments in calculating multi-hadron form-factors and transition processes via lattice QCD. Our primary tools are finite-volume scaling relations, which map spectra and matrix elements to the corresponding multi-hadron infinite-volume amplitudes. We focus on two hadron processes probed by an external current, and provide various checks on the finite-volume formalism in the limiting cases of perturbative interactions and systems forming a bound state. By studying model-independent properties of the infinite-volume amplitudes, we are able to rigorously define form-factors of resonances.

## Key claim

The paper develops a finite-volume formalism for computing multi-hadron form-factors from lattice QCD, but the actual results are consistency checks in limiting cases (perturbative interactions and bound-state systems) rather than a complete numerical implementation; the formalism is presented as a pathway that requires future lattice QCD data to constrain the unknown short-distance functions.

## Method

The author uses finite-volume scaling relations (the Lüscher quantization condition and its generalization for matrix elements) to map finite-volume spectra and matrix elements computed in lattice QCD to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes and form-factors. For two-hadron systems probed by an external current, the infinite-volume amplitudes are expressed in an on-shell representation that separates known kinematic functions (phase space factors, triangle diagrams) from unknown short-distance kernels (the K-matrix for scattering and the smooth function A for current interactions). The formalism is validated by examining the volume dependence of conserved vector charges, bound-state matrix elements, and threshold expansions, showing that power-law finite-volume corrections reduce to exponentially suppressed corrections in these special cases.

## Key numbers

- For a stable hadron, the finite-volume mass equals the infinite-volume mass up to corrections of order O(e^{-mL}), where m is the smallest mass scale (e.g., the pion mass for QCD).
- For a bound state, the finite-volume mass correction is O(e^{-κL}), where κ is the binding momentum.
- The ground-state center-of-mass energy for identical scalars near threshold follows E_0(L) = 2m + 4πa/(mL^3) + O(1/L^4), where a is the S-wave scattering length.
- For a scalar current in the forward direction near threshold, the finite-volume matrix element satisfies L^3 ⟨0,L|J_S(0)|0,L⟩ = (g/m)[1 - 2πa/(m^2 L^3) + O(1/L^4)], where g is the single-particle scalar charge.
- The conserved vector charge of a finite-volume state is exactly Q, independent of volume: ⟨P_n,L|Q|P_n,L⟩ = Q.

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher methodology (Lüscher 1986, 1990; Rummukainen 1995; Kim 2005; He 2005; Hansen 2012; Briceno 2014) for two-particle scattering to include external current probes, building on earlier developments for single-hadron to two-hadron transitions (Lellouch 2000; Briceno 2014, 2015; Shultz 2015) and recent extensions to two-hadron bound and resonant states under external currents (Agadjanov 2016; Briceno 2012, 2015; Baroni 2018). The formalism enables future lattice QCD calculations of resonance form-factors (e.g., ρ+γ*→ρ) to extract structural observables such as electromagnetic charge radii, providing the first model-independent definitions of such quantities for excited QCD states.
