# Generation of fission yield covariance matrices and its application in uncertainty analysis of decay heat

> The uncertainties and covariance matrices of fission yield are important in the uncertainty analysis of decay heat. At present, there are no covariance matrixes of fission yield given in the evaluated nuclear data library, although they have provided the uncertainties with good estimates. In this work, the generalized least squares (GLS) updating approach was adopted to evaluate the fission yield covariances with the constraints from basic physical conservation equation and chain yield data, using the nuclear data files from ENDF/B-VIII.0, JENDL-5 and JEFF-3.3. Based on these original and updated data, summation calculation was performed for fission pulse decay heat of thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U. The uncertainties of decay heat were obtained through generalized perturbation theory, including the uncertainties propagated from fission yield, decay energy, decay constant and branching ratio. The original uncorrelated yield data contributes a $\sim 4 \%$ uncertainty at all times and dominates the decay heat uncertainty at cooling times longer than \SI{100}{s}. With the generated covariance matrixes, the uncertainty of calculated decay heat is strongly reduced and decay energy data makes a major contribution in general. The relative uncertainties at cooling time \SI{0.1}{\second} are $\sim$10$\%$ for ENDF/V-VIII.0 and JEFF-3.3 and $\sim$5$\%$ for JENDL-5 and those at cooling time 10$^{5}$ s are about 1$\%$ for three libraries. The influence of the GLS updating procedure on the contributions of important fission products to decay heat and their sensitive coefficients was also discussed.

# Generation of fission yield covariance matrices and its application in uncertainty analysis of decay heat

## Key claim

The generalized least squares (GLS) updating approach, constrained by physical conservation equations and chain yield data, generates fission yield covariance matrices that strongly reduce the uncertainty of calculated decay heat compared to using uncorrelated yield data; however, the adjustment to the fission yield values themselves is moderate for ENDF/B-VIII.0 and JENDL-5 but negligible for JEFF-3.3, and the decay energy data becomes the major contributor to total uncertainty once yield correlations are included.

## Method

The authors applied the generalized least squares (GLS) updating approach to generate covariance matrices for independent fission yields (IFYs) using constraints from mass and charge conservation, normalization, charge symmetry, and chain yield data, with a half-life cutoff of 1 minute for crossing-mass-chain decay processes in the chain yield constraint. They performed summation calculations for fission pulse decay heat (FPDH) of thermal neutron-induced fission of 235U using original and updated data from ENDF/B-VIII.0, JENDL-5, and JEFF-3.3, and propagated uncertainties from fission yield, decay energy, decay constant, and branching ratio using generalized perturbation theory with the sandwich formula.

## Key numbers

- Original uncorrelated yield data contributes approximately 4% uncertainty at all cooling times for 235U(n_th,f) decay heat
- Relative uncertainties at cooling time 0.1 second are approximately 10% for ENDF/B-VIII.0 and JEFF-3.3, and approximately 5% for JENDL-5
- Relative uncertainties at cooling time 10^5 seconds are about 1% for all three libraries
- The half-life cutoff for chain yield calculations was set to 1 minute, where relative divergences between converged chain yields and mass distributions reach up to approximately 10% in mass regions A=83-97 and 135-140
- The GLS updating procedure reduced the uncertainty of the mass conservation residual from approximately 3.79 to 7.48E-3 for ENDF/B-VIII.0, from 3.01 to 6.13E-3 for JENDL-5, and from 4.18 to 1.14E-2 for JEFF-3.3
- The chi-squared value for chain yield constraint was reduced from 8.48 to 3.23 for ENDF/B-VIII.0, from 9.09 to 2.52 for JENDL-5, and from 9.78 to 0.03 for JEFF-3.3
- Updated IFYs lead to an enhancement up to approximately 5% at cooling times shorter than 1 second and increase the curve peaks about 1-2% for ENDF/B-VIII.0 and JENDL-5

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to generate fission yield covariance matrices, notably the simple procedure by Katakura (2012, 2013) which only produced correlations between fission products with the same mass number, and more comprehensive approaches by Fiorito (2016), Tsubakihara (2021), and Lu (2023) that applied constraints from experimental data and physical models. The generated covariance matrices enable more realistic uncertainty analysis of decay heat in reactor design, nuclear power plant safety analysis, and nuclear fuel waste management, and the authors note that an international effort coordinated by IAEA is ongoing to update and improve fission yield data for actinide nuclides (Vogt, 2024).
