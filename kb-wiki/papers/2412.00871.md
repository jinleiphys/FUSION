# Directed Flow of $Λ$, $^{3}_Λ{\rm H}$ and $^{4}_Λ{\rm H}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 3.2, 3.5, 3.9 and 4.5 GeV at RHIC

> Studying hyper-nuclei yields and their collectivity can shed light on their production mechanism as well as the hyperon-nucleon interactions. Heavy-ion collisions from the RHIC beam energy scan phase II (BES-II) provide an unique opportunity to understand these at high baryon densities. In these proceedings, we present a systematic study on energy dependence of the directed flow ($v_{1}$) for $Λ$ and hyper-nuclei ($^{3}_Λ{\rm H}$, $^{4}_Λ{\rm H}$) from mid-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 3.2, 3.5, 3.9 and 4.5 GeV, collected by the STAR experiment with the fixed-target mode during BES-II. The rapidity (y) dependence of the hyper-nuclei $v_{1}$ is studied in mid-central collisions. The extracted $v_{1}$ slopes ($\mathrm{d}v_{1}/\mathrm{d}y|_{y=0}$) of the hyper-nuclei are positive and decrease gradually as the collision energy increases. These hyper-nuclei results are compared to that of light-nuclei including p, d, t/$\rm ^{3}He$ and $\rm ^{4}He$. Finally, these results are compared with a hadronic transport model including coalescence after-burner.

## Key claim

The directed flow slopes of hyper-nuclei (³ΛH and ⁴ΛH) are positive and decrease gradually as collision energy increases, and the mass scaling of these slopes suggests that coalescence is the dominant production mechanism for both light-nuclei and hyper-nuclei, though the baryon number scale breaks down at higher energies indicating the coalescence process becomes weaker.

## Method

The STAR experiment collected data in fixed-target mode at √sNN = 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, and 4.5 GeV from Au+Au collisions during RHIC BES-II. Hyper-nuclei were reconstructed via their decay channels (Λ → p + π⁻, ³ΛH → ³He + π⁻ and p + d + π⁻, ⁴ΛH → ⁴He + π⁻) using the KFParticle package based on Kalman filter methods, with combinatorial backgrounds estimated by rotating daughter tracks. The directed flow v₁ was extracted using the event plane method with the EPD detector, and the mid-rapidity slope dv₁/dy|y=0 was obtained by fitting v₁(y) with a third-order polynomial for Λ and light-nuclei, and a first-order polynomial for hyper-nuclei due to limited statistics.

## Key numbers

- Event plane resolution reached over 70% at √sNN = 3.2 GeV for 5-40% centrality Au+Au collisions
- The analysis covered four collision energies: √sNN = 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, and 4.5 GeV
- Particles were selected in a similar pT/A range of approximately 0.4 < pT/A < 0.8 GeV/c for comparison
- STAR has collected 2 billion events from 3.0 GeV Au+Au collisions for future improved precision studies

## Context

This work extends the study of collective flow from light-nuclei (protons, deuterons, tritons, ³He, ⁴He) to hyper-nuclei (³ΛH, ⁴ΛH) at high baryon density, building on the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The results are compared with hadronic transport model calculations (JAM2) with a coalescence afterburner, which reproduce the observed mass and energy dependence. These measurements help address the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star physics by constraining hyperon-nucleon interactions, and future analysis of the 2 billion events from 3.0 GeV collisions will provide stronger constraints on coalescence parameters.
