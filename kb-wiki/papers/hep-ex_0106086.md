# Why Do Solar Neutrino Experiments Below 1 Mev

> I discuss why we need solar neutrino experiments below 1 MeV. I also express my prejudices about the desired number and types of such experiments, emphasizing the importance of p-p solar neutrino experiments

# Why Do Solar Neutrino Experiments Below 1 MeV?

## Key claim

Solar neutrino experiments below 1 MeV are essential because more than 98% of the calculated standard model solar neutrino flux lies below this energy, and the currently favored MSW oscillation solutions exhibit their most characteristic and strongly energy-dependent features below 1 MeV, whereas above 5 MeV the energy dependence of the survival probability is at best modest.

## Method

The paper reviews the scientific motivation for low-energy solar neutrino experiments, focusing on the need to measure the total flux, flavor content, energy spectrum, and time dependence of both the 0.86 MeV ^7Be neutrino line and the fundamental p-p neutrinos (below 0.43 MeV). The author discusses the required experimental precision, the importance of redundancy through multiple measurement techniques, and the potential of specific detector concepts including BOREXINO, CLEAN, GENIUS, HERON, KamLAND, LENS, MOON, and XMASS.

## Key numbers

- More than 98% of the calculated standard model solar neutrino flux lies below 1 MeV
- ^8B neutrinos constitute a fraction of less than 10^{-4} of the total solar neutrino flux
- p-p neutrinos carry about 91% of the total flux according to the standard solar model
- ^7Be neutrinos constitute about 7% of the total standard model flux
- The p-p neutrino flux is 10^4 times larger than the flux of the rare ^8B neutrinos measured by SNO and Super-Kamiokande
- Interaction cross sections must be known to a 1-sigma accuracy of approximately ±5% or better to achieve a measurement good to ±10%
- A 1-sigma measurement of the total rate for ^7Be and p-p neutrinos that is at least as accurate as ±10% is necessary
- The standard solar model predicts the average energy of ^7Be neutrinos emitted from the sun exceeds by 1.3 keV the laboratory energy of the higher-energy ^7Be line
- In the distant future, measuring the average energy and shape of the ^7Be neutrino line with a precision better than 0.3 keV would allow a direct determination of the central temperature of the sun
- The raw signal-to-noise ratio for a potential p-p detector using In liquid scintillator is 10^{-11}

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing solar neutrino problem by arguing that the most critical measurements remain to be done at the lowest energies, complementing the higher-energy measurements from Super-Kamiokande and SNO. The author emphasizes that the p-p neutrino flux, calculated to an accuracy of ±1% from standard solar models, has never been directly measured separately from the gallium experiments (which measure all neutrinos above 0.23 MeV). The paper enables a roadmap for future experiments that can test both solar models and neutrino oscillation solutions simultaneously, with particular emphasis on combining charged-current and neutrino-electron scattering measurements to determine survival probabilities and test for sterile neutrinos.
