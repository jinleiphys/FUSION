# Search for time-reversal-invariance violation in double polarized antiproton-deuteron scattering

> Apart from the $pd$ reaction also the scattering of antiprotons with transversal polarization $p_y^p$ on deuterons with tensor polarization $P_{xz}$ provides a null-test signal for time-reversal-invariance violating but parity conserving effects. Assuming that the time-reversal-invariance violating $\bar NN$ interaction contains the same operator structure as the $NN$ interaction, we discuss the energy dependence of the null-test signal in $\bar pd$ scattering on the basis of a calculation within the spin-dependent Glauber theory at beam energies of 50-300 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the scattering of transversely polarized antiprotons on tensor-polarized deuterons provides a null-test signal for time-reversal-invariance violating but parity conserving (TVPC) effects, but the actual signal strength and energy dependence depend on the specific operator structure of the TVPC interaction and are subject to cancellations from deuteron D-wave contributions that can cause the signal to vanish at certain beam energies even when the TVPC interaction itself is nonzero.

## Method

The authors applied spin-dependent Glauber theory to calculate the energy dependence of the TVPC null-test signal in antiproton-deuteron scattering, using spin-dependent antiproton-nucleon amplitudes from a partial wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering. They considered two TVPC operator terms (h_N and g_N) in the antiproton-nucleon t-matrix, corresponding to axial h_1-meson and rho-meson exchange, and included both S-wave and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function from the CD Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- The null-test signal from the h-term shows a node at an antiproton beam energy of about 50 MeV when only the deuteron S-wave contribution is considered
- A second zero of the null-test signal appears at approximately 300 MeV when D-wave contributions are included
- The maximal value of the null-test signal is expected at beam energies of 100-150 MeV
- The calculations were performed for beam energies in the range of 50-300 MeV
- The integrated cross section sigma_1 could be zero at antiproton beam energies of approximately 100 MeV, which would minimize spurious signals from nonzero deuteron vector polarization

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of TVPC null-test signals in proton-deuteron scattering (Beyer, Lazauskas, Uzikov and collaborators, 2015-2017) to the antiproton-deuteron system, motivated by planned measurements at the COSY accelerator in Julich. The formalism developed for pd scattering within Glauber theory is applied to antiproton-deuteron scattering, with the key difference being the use of antiproton-nucleon amplitudes from partial wave analysis (Nijmegen group). The results enable experimental searches for TVPC effects using antiproton beams and suggest optimal beam energies for minimizing background from spurious signals.
