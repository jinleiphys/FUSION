# Breakup of a nucleus with two weakly bound neutrons on a proton target: Single-scattering approximation

> Three- and four-cluster breakup reactions in the ${}^{16}$C-proton scattering are studied using three-body core-neutron-neutron model for ${}^{16}$C. Single-scattering approximation (SSA) of four-particle equations for transition operators is used to calculate three- and four-cluster breakup amplitudes at 200 and 300 MeV/nucleon energy near proton-neutron ($pn$) quasi free scattering (QFS) conditions. The differential cross section is sharply peaked at $pn$ QFS point and decreases rapidly whenever kinematical conditions deviate from $pn$ QFS. The accuracy of the SSA for the three-cluster breakup is estimated from a three-body model and is found to be as good as 6\% at higher reaction energies in suitable angular regimes. Furthermore, under an additional approximation the three-cluster breakup amplitude factorizes into the $pn$ transition operator and the overlap integral of two- and three-particle bound states. That approximation usually reduces the cross section, in some cases even up to 10\%.

## Key claim

The single-scattering approximation (SSA) for four-particle breakup reactions is accurate to about 6% for three-cluster breakup at higher reaction energies (300 MeV/nucleon) in suitable angular regimes, but only when the final-state relative energies between the struck neutron and the core-neutron cluster, and between the proton and that cluster, are both sufficiently large; the approximation fails when either of these relative energies is not large enough, with the proton-cluster energy being more decisive.

## Method

The author derived the SSA from the four-particle Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for transition operators, keeping only first-order terms in two-particle transition operators. For the three-cluster breakup of p+¹⁶C → p+n+¹⁵C, the SSA results were compared to full three-body Faddeev-type calculations (multiple-scattering series summed to infinite order, labeled MS-3b) using an effective three-body model where ¹⁵C is treated as an inert particle, allowing estimation of the SSA accuracy. The four-cluster breakup p+¹⁶C → p+n+n+¹⁴C was also calculated using the SSA, with the ¹⁶C ground state described by a core-neutron-neutron three-body model including a three-body force to reproduce the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The SSA accuracy for three-cluster breakup is about 6% at the peak when comparing MS-3b and SSA-3b at 300 MeV/nucleon beam energy for proton detection angles Θ_p = 45° and 60°.
- At 200 MeV/nucleon beam energy, the difference between MS-3b and SSA-3b is around 15% at the peak, compared to below 10% at 300 MeV/nucleon.
- The overlap integral approximation (OIA-4b) underestimates the SSA-4b results by up to 9% at 300 MeV/nucleon and up to 11% at 200 MeV/nucleon.
- The ¹⁶C ground state binding energy used is ε_ρ = -5.468 MeV, with neutron separation energy 4.250 MeV; the ¹⁵C one-neutron halo has binding energy ε_B = -1.218 MeV.
- The relative pn energy at the quasi-free scattering peaks is around 150 MeV.
- Changing the three-body force cutoff Λ from 2 fm⁻¹ to 4 fm⁻¹ yields less than 3% changes in breakup cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the SSA approach previously applied to one-neutron halo breakup (Crespo 2008) to the four-particle system of a two-neutron halo nucleus (¹⁶C) on a proton target. The study was motivated by the known failure of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method for one-neutron halo breakup near pn quasi-free scattering conditions (Deltuva 2007), and the expectation that CDCC would similarly fail for two-neutron halo nuclei in this regime. The results enable calculation of semi-inclusive observables for four-cluster breakup reactions at higher energies relevant to present and future experiments, though no experimental data currently exist for comparison.
