# How a spinning quark moves in the NJL type mean field

> The transport equation of the spinning quarks, moving in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type mean field, is derived by solving the Schwinger-Dyson equations, up to the order of $\hbar$, under the mean field approximation. It shows that the scalar and the vector mean field potentials have different impacts on the quantum correction to the transport equation. Both the forces give rise to an anomalous velocity and an anomalous force where the latter would vanish if the vector force is turned off. The particle dispersion relation is modified by the vector force as well. Besides, the spin precession in the particle rest frame is purely governed by the vector force.

## Key claim

The transport equation for spinning quarks in an NJL-type mean field is derived to order ℏ, showing that scalar and vector mean-field potentials affect the quantum correction differently: the vector potential alone governs spin precession in the particle rest frame, while the anomalous force from the vector potential vanishes if the vector force is turned off.

## Method

The author starts from the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the Keldysh Green's function in the Schwinger-Keldysh space, truncates them at order ℏ under the mean-field approximation, and decomposes the mean-field potential into scalar (Φ) and vector (Ωμ) parts. The Green's function is expanded in a basis of Dirac matrices, solved at zeroth order (classical transport) and first order in ℏ (quantum corrections), yielding a set of coupled equations for the distribution functions and spin degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The classical transport equation is obtained at order ℏ⁰: \(k^\mu D_\mu f^{(0)}_s - M \partial_{X^\mu}\Phi \partial_{k_\mu} f^{(0)}_s = 0\), with \(M = m_0 - \Phi\) and \(k = p + \Omega\).
- The spin precession equation for a slowly moving quark in its rest frame is \(M \dot{\hat n}^i = F_j^{\,i} \hat n^j\), where \(F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu \Omega_\nu - \partial_\nu \Omega_\mu\).
- The quantum-corrected transport equation (Eq. 4.30) includes a term \(\frac{s\hbar}{2M} k_\rho \partial_{X^\tau} \widetilde{F}^{\rho\sigma} \partial_{k_\tau} (n_\sigma f_s^{(0)})\) and a modified on-shell condition \(k^2 - M^2 - \frac{s\hbar}{M} \widetilde{F}^{\rho\sigma} k_\rho n_\sigma = 0\).
- The current density at order ℏ contains an anomalous term \(j^i = -\frac{\hbar}{M^2} \widetilde{F}^{i\sigma} j_{5\sigma}\) even for spatially uniform, isotropic \(f_s^{(0)}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral transport formalism from Wigner functions for U(1) gauge theory (Huang, 2018) to the NJL model with both scalar and vector mean fields, building on earlier classical transport derivations for the NJL model (Klevansky, 1997). The results enable simulations of spinning quark motion in baryon-rich rotating fireballs, where the vector interaction, controversial because it is excluded by lattice QCD equations of state (Steinheimer, 2010) but required for two-solar-mass neutron stars (Klahn, 2013; Baym, 2017), can be probed through its distinct effects on spin precession and anomalous transport.
