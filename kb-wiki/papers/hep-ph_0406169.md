# Random walks of partons in SU(N_c) and classical representations of color charges in QCD at small x

> The effective action for wee partons in large nuclei includes a sum over static color sources distributed in a wide range of representations of the SU(N_c) color group. The problem can be formulated as a random walk of partons in the N_c-1 dimensional space spanned by the Casimirs of SU(N_c). For a large number of sources, k >> 1, we show explicitly that the most likely representation is a classical representation of order O(\sqrt{k}). The quantum sum over representations is well approximated by a path integral over classical sources with an exponential weight whose argument is the quadratic Casimir operator of the group. The contributions of the higher N_c-2 Casimir operators are suppressed by powers of k. Other applications of the techniques developed here are discussed briefly.

## Key claim
For a large number $k$ of random fundamental quarks in $SU(N_c)$, the most likely color representation is a classical representation of order $O(\sqrt{k})$, and the quantum sum over representations is well approximated by a path integral over classical sources with a Gaussian weight proportional to the quadratic Casimir. However, for $N_c \geq 3$, the weight contains a sub-leading correction proportional to the cubic Casimir (or higher Casimirs) that is suppressed by a power of $k$, specifically of order $O(1/\sqrt{k})$ for $SU(3)$.

## Method
The problem of adding $k$ random fundamental quarks is formulated as a random walk in the $N_c-1$ dimensional space spanned by the Casimir operators of $SU(N_c)$. For $SU(2)$, the recursion relation for the multiplicity of spin states is solved exactly using binomial coefficients, and the Stirling approximation is used to find the distribution in the large-$k$ limit. For $SU(3)$, the analogous recursion relation for the $(m,n)$ representation labels is solved using trinomial coefficients, and the resulting multiplicity is expressed in terms of the quadratic and cubic Casimir operators.

## Key numbers
* For $SU(2)$ with $k$ spin-1/2 quarks, the most probable value of the quadratic Casimir is $\bar{D}_2 = k/4$, corresponding to a spin $l = \sqrt{k}/2 + O(1)$.
* For $SU(3)$ with $k$ quarks, the cubic Casimir introduces a correction to the Gaussian weight of size $O(1/\sqrt{k})$ relative to the quadratic term.
* The average color charge squared per unit area per color degree of freedom in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model is $\mu_A^2 = g^2 A / (2 \pi R^2)$, which is of order $A^{1/3}$.
* The total number of degrees of freedom for $k$ $SU(2)$ spins is $2^k$, and for $k$ $SU(3)$ quarks is $3^k$.

## Context
This work provides a rigorous mathematical foundation for the classical color charge distribution assumed in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) for small-$x$ parton distributions in large nuclei. It extends the analysis from $SU(2)$ to $SU(3)$ and general $SU(N_c)$, showing that the Gaussian weight is the leading contribution for large $k$, with higher Casimir contributions being suppressed. This enables a more precise understanding of the limits of the MV model and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, and the techniques developed may be applicable to problems in jet physics, finite temperature transport theory, and multi-particle production models.
