# Properties of light (anti)nuclei and (anti) hypertriton production in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> We investigate the properties of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV, based on the parton and hadron cascade and dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (PACIAE + DCPC) model. We found that the yields of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton strongly depend on the centrality, i.e., their yields decrease rapidly with the increase of centrality bins, but their yield ratios are independent of centrality. The results of theoretical model are well consistent with ALICE data. Furthermore, we found that the integrated yields of (anti)nuclei per participant nucleon increase from peripheral to central collisions more rapidly with increasing mass number. The transverse momentum distributions of $\overline{_{\overlineΛ}^3 H}, {_Λ^3 H},{\overline{^3 He}}$ and $^3{He}$ are also discussed in the 0-10% most central Pb-Pb collisions.The coalescence parameters $B_A$ of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypernuclei are analyzed.

## Key claim

The PACIAE+DCPC model reproduces ALICE data for light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton yields in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the agreement is demonstrated only for centrality bins with Npart ≥ 100 for the coalescence parameters B_A, and the model parameters (K factor, parj values) are tuned specifically to fit ALICE data for p, p-bar, and Λ.

## Method

The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model (PACIAE) to simulate Pb-Pb collisions, generating final-state hadrons from parton rescattering and hadronization via Lund string fragmentation. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model to form light nuclei, anti-nuclei, and hypertriton by integrating over the six-dimensional phase space of constituent nucleons and hyperons, subject to constraints on invariant mass and spatial separation. The model parameters (K=3, parj(1)=0.15, parj(2)=0.38, parj(3)=0.65) were fitted to reproduce ALICE data for p, p-bar, and Λ yields in 0-20% centrality Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron yield dN/dy = 0.112 in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 0.00345 in 50-80% centrality (|y|<0.5)
- Anti-deuteron yield dN/dy = 0.101 in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 0.00311 in 50-80% centrality
- Helium-3 yield dN/dy = 3.84×10⁻⁴ in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 5.13×10⁻⁶ in 50-80% centrality
- Anti-helium-3 yield dN/dy = 3.29×10⁻⁴ in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 4.29×10⁻⁶ in 50-80% centrality
- Hypertriton (Λ³H) yield dN/dy = 4.47×10⁻⁵ in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 5.67×10⁻⁷ in 50-80% centrality
- Anti-hypertriton (anti-Λ³H) yield dN/dy = 3.24×10⁻⁵ in 0-5% centrality, decreasing to 4.03×10⁻⁷ in 50-80% centrality
- Yield ratios (anti-particle/particle) are independent of centrality: ~0.91 for deuteron, ~0.85 for helium-3, ~0.72 for hypertriton
- Average transverse momentum ⟨p_t⟩ for deuteron = 1.00 GeV, anti-deuteron = 1.05 GeV, helium-3 = 1.16 GeV, anti-helium-3 = 1.24 GeV, hypertriton = 1.17 GeV, anti-hypertriton = 1.22 GeV in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV
- 1.833×10⁶ minimum-bias events were generated for 0-20% centrality

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the PACIAE+DCPC approach from Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (Chen et al., 2014; She et al., 2012) to Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC energy of 2.76 TeV. The results are compared with ALICE experimental data (ALICE Collaboration, 2013, 2014) and show consistency with the statistical hadronization model (SHM) findings. The study enables future investigation of heavier antimatter production mechanisms in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and provides a framework for predicting yields at different collision energies.
