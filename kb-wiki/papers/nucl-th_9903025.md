# A basis of cranking operators for the pairing-plus-quadrupole model

> We investigate the RPA normal-mode coordinates in the pairing-plus-quadrupole model, with an eye on simplifying the application of large amplitude collective motion techniques. At the Hartree-Bogoliubov minimum, the RPA modes are exactly the cranking operators of the collective coordinate approach. We examine the possibility of representing the self-consistent cranking operator by linear combinations of a limited number of one-body operators. We study the Sm nuclei as an example, and find that such representations exist in terms of operators that are state-dependent in a characteristic manner.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the self-consistent cranking operator for the pairing-plus-quadrupole model cannot be well approximated by a small set of elementary one-body operators (such as multipole and pairing operators), but can be accurately represented by a small set of *state-dependent* one-body operators where each two-quasiparticle matrix element is weighted by a factor of \((E_{\text{2qp}})^{-2}\).

## Method

The authors solve the full random-phase approximation (RPA) equation for the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian in the Hartree-Bogoliubov (HB) ground state, then project the RPA eigenvectors onto various sets of one-body operators to obtain a projected RPA equation of reduced dimension. They compare the projected RPA frequencies and the overlap measure \(\delta\) (equation 6) against the exact RPA results for even-even Sm isotopes (A=146–154), using the model space and parameters from Baranger and Kumar (1965).

## Key numbers

- For spherical \(^{146,148}\)Sm, the overlap measure \(\delta\) for projection onto elementary pairing and quadrupole operators (set 1) is 0.271 and 0.243 respectively; for the state-dependent projection (set 3), \(\delta\) drops to 0.009 and 0.0003.
- For deformed \(^{154}\)Sm, \(\delta\) for the \(\beta\) vibration is 0.513 with set 1 and 0.002 with set 3; for the \(\gamma\) vibration, \(\delta\) is 0.636 with set 1 and 0.052 with set 3.
- Projected RPA frequencies using elementary operators (set 1) are 5–6 MeV higher than the full RPA frequencies (which lie near 1–2 MeV for the isotopes studied).
- For \(^{154}\)Sm, the projected \(\beta\)-vibration eigenvector includes \((\widetilde{Q}_{20})_n + 0.91 (\widetilde{Q}_{20})_p - 0.48 \widetilde{P}_n - 0.44 \widetilde{P}_p + 0.085 \widetilde{P}_n^\dagger - 0.14 \widetilde{P}_p^\dagger\), showing significant mixing with monopole pairing modes.

## Context

This work extends the local harmonic approximation (LHA) theory of adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion, previously tested on the O(4) model by Nakatsukasa and Walet (1999), to the more realistic pairing-plus-quadrupole model of Baranger and Kumar (1965). It contradicts the common assumption that the mass quadrupole operator alone suffices as a constraint operator, and shows that the self-consistent cranking operator has a strong state-dependence. The results enable a practical method for solving the LHA in heavy nuclei by projecting the RPA onto a small set of state-dependent basis operators, avoiding the diagonalisation of the full RPA matrix.
