# Coupled Dyson-Schwinger Equations and Effects of Self-Consistency

> Using the $σ-ω$ model as an effective tool, the effects of self-consistency are studied in some detail. A coupled set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the renormalized baryon and meson propagators in the $σ-ω$ model is solved self-consistently according to the dressed Hartree-Fock scheme, where the hadron propagators in both the baryon and meson self-energies are required to also satisfy this coupled set of equations. It is found that the self-consistency affects the baryon spectral function noticeably, if only the interaction with $σ$ mesons is considered. However, there is a cancellation between the effects due to the $σ$ and $ω$ mesons and the additional contribution of $ω$ mesons makes the above effect insignificant. In both the $σ$ and $σ-ω$ cases the effects of self-consistency on meson spectral function are perceptible, but they can nevertheless be taken account of without a self-consistent calculation. Our study indicates that to include the meson propagators in the self-consistency requirement is unnecessary and one can stop at an early step of an iteration procedure to obtain a good approximation to the fully self-consistent results of all the hadron propagators in the model, if an appropriate initial input is chosen. Vertex corrections and their effects on ghost poles are also studied.

# Coupled Dyson-Schwinger Equations and Effects of Self-Consistency

## Key claim

The self-consistency requirement in the Dyson-Schwinger equations noticeably affects the baryon spectral function when only sigma meson interactions are considered, but this effect is cancelled by the additional contribution of omega mesons, making the net effect insignificant. The authors claim that including meson propagators in the self-consistency requirement is unnecessary, and one can stop at an early iteration step with an appropriate initial input to obtain a good approximation to the fully self-consistent results.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for renormalized baryon and meson propagators in the sigma-omega model according to the dressed Hartree-Fock scheme at zero density. They compared several approximation schemes: the baryon-propagator-only self-consistent scheme (BP), the fully self-consistent scheme (FSC) where all hadron propagators are required to satisfy the coupled equations, and intermediate schemes (P, PEP, EP) that stop at earlier iteration steps. The four-dimensional integrals were evaluated using spectral representations and Feynman integral parameterization with dimensional regularization, and the coupled equations were solved by iteration.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass used: M_t = 4.7585 fm^{-1}
- Sigma meson mass used: m_s = 2.6353 fm^{-1}
- Omega meson mass used: m_v = 3.9680 fm^{-1}
- Coupling strength studied: g_s^2/(16 pi^2) = 0.5263 and 0.6517 for the sigma-only case
- For the sigma-omega model: g_s^2/(16 pi^2) = 0.5263 and g_v^2/(8 pi^2) = 1.3685
- Ghost pole locations for the sigma-omega model without vertex corrections: baryon at 149.4215 +/- i 96.8997 fm^{-2}, sigma meson at 274.4833 fm^{-2}, omega meson at 178.5431 fm^{-2}
- Vertex correction cutoff parameters: Lambda_s = Lambda_v = 5.0676 fm^{-1} (1 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the study of self-consistency in Dyson-Schwinger equations previously applied to the pi-omega model by Bracco, Eiras, Krein and Wilets (1990s), and follows earlier work by Brown, Puff and Wilets (1970s) and Bielajew and Serot (1980s) who solved the DS equation for baryon propagators self-consistently. The authors had previously shown in a simpler model (Wu, Zhang, Yao, 1999) that self-consistency in the baryon-only scheme diminishes the continuum part of the spectral representation. The present work extends this analysis to the fully self-consistent coupled system including both sigma and omega mesons, and also studies vertex corrections for ghost pole elimination following the approach of Allendes and Serot (1992) and Krein, Nielsen, Puff and Wilets (1990s). The results enable simplified calculations where meson self-consistency can be neglected, and suggest that the cancellation between sigma and omega contributions may generalize to finite density.
