# Spreading Widths of Doorway States

> As a function of energy E, the average strength function S(E) of a doorway state is commonly assumed to be Lorentzian in shape and characterized by two parameters, the peak energy E_0 and the spreading width Gamma. The simple picture is modified when the density of background states that couple to the doorway state changes significantly in an energy interval of size Gamma. For that case we derive an approximate analytical expression for S(E). We test our result successfully against numerical simulations. Our result may have important implications for shell--model calculations.

## Key claim

The standard Lorentzian form of the average strength function of a doorway state (Eq. 2) is modified when the density of background states changes significantly over an energy interval of size Gamma^downarrow; the authors derive an approximate analytical expression (Eqs. 12 and 13) that generalizes the standard result and show that the effective spreading width Gamma_eff is larger than Gamma^downarrow when the level density is not constant.

## Method

The authors model the doorway state coupled to N background states via a Hamiltonian matrix (Eq. 5) with Gaussian-distributed random coupling matrix elements V_mu and a general average level density rho(E) for the background states. They average the strength function over the ensemble of V_mu and over the distribution of background energies E_mu, replacing the sum over mu by the average level density rho(E) and its principal-value integral. The resulting approximate expression (Eqs. 12 and 13) is tested against numerical diagonalization of random band matrices of dimension N=1000 with varying bandwidth b, comparing the predicted strength functions and effective widths to numerical results.

## Key numbers

- The spreading width is given by Gamma^downarrow = 2 pi v^2 rho(E) (Eq. 13), generalizing the standard Fermi golden rule expression.
- For the Giant Dipole mode in 16O, the spectral range of 2p-2h states is about 25 MeV, the Gaussian width sigma is around 15 or 20 MeV, and Gamma^downarrow is approximately 5 MeV, giving a ratio Gamma^downarrow / sigma of about 1/3 or 1/4.
- For a semicircular level density, the shift function Delta(E) is linear with coefficient alpha = 1/4; for a Gaussian level density, alpha = 1 / sqrt(2 pi).
- With gamma = Gamma^downarrow(0) / lambda approximately 1/3 or 1/4, the correction to both E_0 and Gamma^downarrow is about 10 to 20 percent.
- Numerical simulations use N=1000 background states with m=500 realizations for ensemble averaging.
- The inverse participation ratio for case (i) (uncorrelated coupling) is approximately d / Gamma^downarrow(0), with values near 1/N; for case (ii) (band coupling) with b=1 and b=10, the IPR is roughly 1/b.

## Context

This work extends the standard model of doorway states by Brown and Bolsterli (1968), which assumed constant background level density and yielded a Lorentzian strength function (Eq. 2). The authors previously treated the case of a GOE background with semicircular level density (De Pace et al., 2007) using the Pastur equation; the present paper generalizes that result to arbitrary energy-dependent level densities rho(E), such as the Gaussian shape typical of shell-model level densities (Zel96). The result enables more accurate analysis of giant resonances in nuclei, particularly for shell-model calculations where the spreading width is not negligible compared to the spectral range of background states, and may improve comparison between theory and experimental data.
