# Nucleon-nucleon scattering in a chiral constituent quark model

> We study the nucleon-nucleon interaction in a chiral constituent quark model by using the resonating group method, convenient for treating the interaction between composite particles. The calculated phase shifts for the 3S1 and 1S0 channels show the presence of a strong repulsive core due to the combined effect of the quark interchange and the spin-flavour structure of the effective quark-quark interaction. Such a symmetry structure stems from the pseudoscalar meson exchange between the quarks and is a consequence of the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry. We perform single and coupled channel calculations and show the role of coupling of the $ΔΔ$ and hidden color CC channels on the behaviour of the phase shifts.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model, using only pseudoscalar meson exchange between quarks (without one-gluon exchange), produces a strong repulsive core in nucleon-nucleon scattering that is actually stronger than that from one-gluon-exchange models, but the calculated phase shifts for the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels remain purely repulsive and do not reproduce the observed low-energy attraction without additional mechanisms.

## Method

The authors applied the resonating group method (RGM) to a six-quark system described by a Hamiltonian containing linear confinement and a Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction between quarks, using the parametrization of Glozman et al. (1997). They performed single-channel (NN only), two-channel (NN + \(\Delta\Delta\)), and three-channel (NN + \(\Delta\Delta\) + hidden color CC) calculations for the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) partial waves, expanding the relative wave function in 15 Gaussians with a matching radius of 4.5 fm and a quark size parameter \(b = 0.44\) fm determined by the stability condition.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass: \(m_{u,d} = 340\) MeV; confinement strength: \(C = 0.77\) fm\(^{-2}\); pion mass: \(\mu_\pi = 139\) MeV; eta mass: \(\mu_\eta = 547\) MeV; eta-prime mass: \(\mu_{\eta'} = 958\) MeV.
- Pion-quark and eta-quark coupling: \(g_{\pi q}^2/4\pi = g_{\eta q}^2/4\pi = 1.24\); eta-prime-quark coupling: \(g_{\eta' q}^2/4\pi = 2.7652\).
- Cutoff parameters: \(\Lambda_0 = 5.82\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\kappa = 1.34\); constant \(V_0 = -112\) MeV.
- Hard core radius extracted from phase shifts at small \(k\) in the GBE model: \(r_0^{\text{GBE}} = 0.68\) fm for \(^3S_1\) and \(r_0^{\text{GBE}} = 0.81\) fm for \(^1S_0\); for comparison, the OGE model gives \(r_0^{\text{OGE}} = 0.30\) fm for \(^3S_1\) and \(r_0^{\text{OGE}} = 0.35\) fm for \(^1S_0\).
- The repulsive core height from earlier adiabatic calculations (Stancu, 1997) was 0.830 GeV in \(^3S_1\) and 1.356 GeV in \(^1S_0\) at zero separation between clusters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous adiabatic studies (Bartz and Stancu, 1999a, 1999b) by using a dynamical RGM approach rather than a static potential approximation. It contrasts with one-gluon-exchange (OGE) models (Oka and Yazaki, 1985; Shimizu, 1989) where the short-range repulsion is attributed to chromomagnetic spin-spin interactions, and with hybrid models (Kusainov et al., 1991; Fujiwara et al., 1996) that combine OGE with meson exchanges. The results show that the GBE model produces stronger repulsion than OGE models, and that coupling to \(\Delta\Delta\) and hidden color channels has negligible effect on phase shifts below 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\) (lab energies up to about 350 MeV), making single-channel calculations sufficient in that range. The next step identified is the inclusion of a tensor force to describe the \(^3S_1\) phase shift properly.
