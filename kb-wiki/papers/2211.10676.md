# Open-quantum-systems approach to in-medium heavy quarkonium dynamics

> Heavy quarkonium continues to play a central role in the study of nuclear matter under extremes of temperature and density in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this talk I report on recent developments in the theoretical description of quarkonium in-medium dynamics using the open-quantum systems approach. Not only does it provide a clear interpretation of the imaginary part of the complex heavy-quark potential but also reveals that a subtle interplay between screening and wavefunction decoherence is responsible for the melting of heavy quarkonium states.

# Open-quantum-systems approach to in-medium heavy quarkonium dynamics

## Key claim
The open-quantum-systems approach reveals that heavy quarkonium melting in the quark-gluon plasma results from a subtle interplay between Debye screening of the interquark potential and wavefunction decoherence due to independent color rotations on the quark and antiquark, rather than from screening alone. The quantum Brownian motion Lindblad equation derived from NRQCD is the only quantum approach demonstrated to correctly thermalize a quarkonium state with its environment.

## Method
The author derives a quantum Brownian motion Lindblad equation from the effective field theory NRQCD for a heavy quark-antiquark pair immersed in a weakly-coupled hot QGP environment, under the assumption that information within the quarkonium probe refreshes much more slowly than among the medium degrees of freedom. The Lindblad operators encode both fluctuations (energy flow from medium to probe) and dissipation (energy flow from probe to medium), with their strength governed by a function D(r) related to the imaginary part of the complex heavy-quark potential. The master equation is implemented numerically in one dimension via quantum state diffusion and direct simulation, projecting onto the color singlet sector.

## Key numbers
- The QGP expands and cools over a range of approximately Δt ∼ 5–10 fm, described by relativistic hydrodynamics.
- The transition temperature of strongly interacting matter predicted by QCD is around 155 MeV.
- The screening length is given by r_s = 1/m_D, where m_D is the Debye mass.
- Fits to the exponential falloff of the late-time steady-state energy distribution yield inverse slope values very close to the temperature of the medium in which the quarkonium particle evolved.

## Context
This work extends the open-quantum-systems paradigm (Breuer, 2002) to heavy quarkonium dynamics, building on prior effective field theory developments in pNRQCD (Brambilla, 2004) and NRQCD. It provides a systematic chain of approximations linking first-principles QCD to phenomenological models such as the Boltzmann equation (Yao, 2020), rate equations, and deterministic Schrödinger equations with complex potentials (Krouppa, 2017). The approach enables genuine ab-initio determination of heavy-quarkonium properties without mixing them with medium properties, and connects to lattice QCD studies of the complex interquark potential (Laine, 2006; Rothkopf, 2011; Burnier, 2014, 2015; Bala, 2021) and transport coefficients κ and γ (Altenkort, 2020).
