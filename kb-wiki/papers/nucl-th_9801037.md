# Equation of state and collision rate tests of parton cascade models

> We develop two further numerical tests of parton cascade models: the ideal gas equation of state and the collision frequency tests. The equation of state test checks the initial momentum distribution generator and free expansion dynamics in periodic inhomogeneous geometries. The collision rate test is sensitive to spatial inhomogeneities and the collision algorithm. These tests are applied to the recently developed ZPC parton cascade model. The tests helped uncover unphysical correlations induced by one of the commonly used random number generators and showed the necessity of particle subdivisions for convergence to the exact analytic limit.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two new analytic tests for parton cascade codes, the ideal gas equation of state test and the collision frequency test, and demonstrates that the ZPC parton cascade model can pass these tests only after fixing unphysical correlations from a commonly used random number generator and applying particle subdivisions to converge to the exact analytic limit.

## Method

The authors simulate a one-component relativistic classical gas in a periodic box, measuring the spatially averaged energy-momentum tensor to compute the pressure-to-energy-density ratio \(P/\epsilon\) and energy density \(\epsilon\) as functions of \(m/T\) for comparison with ideal gas analytic formulas. They also initialize particles in a half-filled slab geometry and track the free-streaming evolution of the \(T^{11}\) momentum flux component and the transverse-longitudinal anisotropy \(A^{zx}\) against analytic predictions. The collision rate per unit volume is computed from the number of collisions in a fixed time interval and compared to a reduced one-dimensional integral formula derived from kinetic theory.

## Key numbers

- For \(N=4000\) particles in a periodic box at temperatures \(T=0.5,\;1,\;1.5\) GeV, the pressure-energy density ratio \(P/\epsilon\) and energy density \(\epsilon\) agree with ideal gas predictions for masses \(m=0,\;2.5,\;5\) GeV over a time period of \(6\) fm during which each particle experiences \(\sim 10\) collisions on average.
- For massless particles with screening mass \(\mu\) corresponding to interaction length \(\sqrt{\sigma/\pi}=1/\mu\), the total number of collisions for \(4000\) particles over \(6\) fm increases from \(64700\) at subdivision factor \(l=1\) to \(75400\) at \(l=5\) and \(77800\) at \(l=10\); the collision rate at \(l=10\) is within \(1\%\) of the analytic prediction.
- The ratio of non-causal to total collisions scales as \(N_{\text{nonc}}/N_{\text{total}} \propto (8/15)\bar{\gamma}\rho\sigma^{3/2}/\sqrt{\pi}\) with \(\bar{\gamma}\approx 2\) for massless particles, and saturates below \(50\%\) at high density.

## Context

This work extends the OSCAR (Open Standard for Codes and Routines) testing framework for parton cascade models, building on earlier analytic tests such as the \(1+1\) dimensional transverse energy evolution test proposed by Gyulassy (1997) and frame-dependence tests by Kortemeyer (1995) and Zhang (1997). The tests enable systematic debugging of cascade algorithms, as demonstrated by uncovering random number generator correlations in the ZPC model, and motivate future comparisons of free streaming, ideal hydrodynamics, and cascade approaches to global equilibrium in half-filled periodic box geometries.
