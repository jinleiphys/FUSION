# Heavy-Quark Masses and Heavy-Meson Decay Constants from Borel Sum Rules in QCD

> Slight sophistications of the QCD sum-rule formalism may have great impact on the reliability of predicted hadron observables, as exemplified for the case of heavy-meson decay constants.

## Key claim

Slight sophistications of the QCD sum-rule formalism, specifically the use of a Borel-parameter-dependent effective continuum threshold rather than the conventional constant threshold, can have a great impact on the reliability of predicted hadron observables such as heavy-meson decay constants.

## Method

The authors extract heavy-meson decay constants from Borel sum rules by constructing a dual correlator that relates the hadronic ground-state contribution to the truncated QCD correlator via an effective continuum threshold s_eff(tau). They assume specific polynomial Ansätze (linear, quadratic, cubic) for s_eff(tau) and determine the threshold variationally by requiring that the dual mass M_dual deviate minimally from the known true meson mass M_P over the admissible Borel parameter range. The operator product expansion (OPE) for the correlator is evaluated to three-loop order, and the perturbative series is reorganized in terms of the running MS-bar heavy-quark mass rather than the pole mass.

## Key numbers

- f_D = (206.2 ± 7.3_(OPE) ± 5.1_(syst)) MeV
- f_Ds = (245.3 ± 15.7_(OPE) ± 4.5_(syst)) MeV
- f_B = (193.4 ± 12.3_(OPE) ± 4.3_(syst)) MeV
- f_Bs = (232.5 ± 18.6_(OPE) ± 2.4_(syst)) MeV
- Extracted b-quark MS-bar mass: m_b(m_b) = (4.245 ± 0.025) GeV, obtained by matching the sum-rule result for f_B to the average of lattice calculations
- Input b-quark MS-bar mass range: m_b(m_b) = (4.163 ± 0.016) GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of improved sum-rule techniques with tau-dependent thresholds (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2010-2011) and applies them to heavy-meson decay constants. The OPE for the correlator was derived to three-loop order in terms of the pole mass by Chetyrkin (2009) and reorganized in the MS-bar scheme by Jamin (2010). The results for charmed mesons show perfect agreement with lattice QCD outcomes, while for beauty mesons the extreme sensitivity to the b-quark mass allows a precise extraction of m_b(m_b) when matched to lattice averages, though this value does not overlap with a recent determination by the authors cited as "mb". The work enables improved predictions for heavy-meson decay constants and a more reliable extraction of heavy-quark masses from sum rules.
