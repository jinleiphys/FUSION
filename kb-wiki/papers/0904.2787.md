# Extracting the neutron-neutron scattering length -- recent developments

> The experimental and theoretical issues and challenges for extracting the neutron-neutron scattering length are discussed. Particular emphasis is placed on recent results and their impact on the field. Comments are made regarding current experimental and theoretical possibilities.

## Key claim

The neutron-neutron scattering length is a crucial parameter for understanding charge symmetry breaking in the strong interaction, but its extraction from indirect reactions remains subject to significant experimental and theoretical uncertainties, with the currently accepted value of \(a_{nn} = -18.6 \pm 0.3\) fm derived from \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiments being challenged by conflicting results from \(nd \to nnp\) breakup measurements that differ by more than \(3\sigma\).

## Method

The paper reviews two main indirect methods for extracting the neutron-neutron scattering length: the deuteron breakup reaction \(nd \to nnp\) and the radiative pion capture reaction \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\). For \(nd \to nnp\), experiments use either kinematically incomplete or complete detection of final-state particles, with theoretical calculations performed using three-body Faddeev equations with modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijmegen) and three-nucleon forces (Tucson-Melbourne type). For \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\), the shape of the photon energy spectrum is fitted with theoretical calculations using either phenomenological methods (Gibbs-Gibson-Stephenson) or modern heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), with the two-body current renormalized by the Gamow-Teller matrix element from \(pp\) fusion.

## Key numbers

- Currently accepted strong \(nn\) scattering length: \(a^{\rm str}_{nn} = -18.9 \pm 0.4\) fm (from review by Miller and van Oers)
- Currently accepted strong \(pp\) scattering length: \(a^{\rm str}_{pp} = -17.3 \pm 0.4\) fm
- Bonn \(d(n,pn)n\) experiment at 16.6 MeV: \(a_{nn} = -16.3 \pm 0.4\) fm (absolute cross section in FSI peak)
- Bonn \(d(n,pn)n\) experiment at 16.6 MeV: \(a_{nn} = -16.2 \pm 0.3\) fm (relative cross section, normalized in \(np\) FSI region)
- Bonn \(d(n,pn)n\) experiment at 25.3 MeV: \(a_{nn} = -16.1 \pm 0.4\) fm (relative cross section)
- TUNL \(d(n,nnp)\) over-complete experiment at 13 MeV: \(a_{nn} = -18.7 \pm 0.6\) fm
- Bonn simplified \(d(n,p)nn\) experiment: \(a_{nn} = -16.5 \pm 0.9\) fm
- PSI \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiment (photon only): \(a_{nn} = -18.5 \pm 0.4\) fm
- PSI \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiment (coincidence): \(a_{nn} = -18.7 \pm 0.6\) fm
- LAMPF \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiment (original): \(a_{nn} = -18.50 \pm 0.05\) (stat) \(\pm 0.44\) (sys) \(\pm 0.30\) (theor) fm
- LAMPF \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiment (updated): \(a_{nn} = -18.63 \pm 0.10\) (stat) \(\pm 0.44\) (sys) \(\pm 0.30\) (theor) fm
- Electromagnetic correction to \(a_{nn}\) from magnetic moment interaction: \(-0.3\) fm
- Uncorrected \(pp\) scattering length: \(-7.8063\) fm
- Charge-dependent contribution to the \(^3{\rm H}\)-\(^3{\rm He}\) binding energy difference: 64 keV, compared to total difference 757 keV and experimental value 764 keV
- Upper bound from \(^2{\rm H}(d,{}^2{\rm He}^2n)\) experiment at KVI: \(a_{nn} < -18.3\) fm (95% CL)
- Theoretical error in modern chiral calculation of \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) when fitting FSI region only: 0.05 fm
- Theoretical error when fitting entire spectrum: 0.3 fm

## Context

This topical review updates the earlier comprehensive review by Slaus, Akaishi, and Tanaka (no year given) and addresses the long-standing discrepancy between \(nd \to nnp\) experiments (Bonn group yielding \(a_{nn} \approx -16.2\) fm) and \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiments (PSI and LAMPF yielding \(a_{nn} \approx -18.6\) fm). The paper extends the discussion of charge symmetry breaking effects in nucleon-nucleon interactions, building on the work of Miller, Nefkens, and Slaus (no year given) and Miller and van Oers (no year given), and highlights how modern effective field theory calculations in HB\(\chi\)PT, pioneered by Weinberg (no year given), now enable systematic extraction of \(a_{nn}\) with theoretical errors smaller than experimental ones. The ongoing measurement of \(\mu^- d \to nn\nu_\mu\) at PSI (Kammel, no year given) and the proposed \(\gamma d \to nn\pi^+\) experiments at MAXLab and HI\(\gamma\)S offer future prospects for independent determinations, while the simultaneous Bonn-TUNL experiment using the same neutron beam aims to resolve the experimental discrepancy in the \(nd\) breakup channel.
