# A study of $ηK \bar K$ and $η' K \bar K$ with the fixed center approximation to Faddeev equations

> In the present work we investigate the three-body systems of $ηK \bar K$ and $η' K \bar K$, by taking the fixed center approximation to Faddeev equations. We find a clear and stable resonance structure around 1490 MeV in the squared $ηK \bar K$ scattering amplitude, which is not sensitive to the renormalization parameters. Conversely, we get only an enhancement effect of the threshold in the $η' K \bar K$ amplitude that indicates the difficulty to bind the $η' K \bar K$ system as a consequence of a weaker $η' K$ interaction than the $ηK$ one. We associate the $ηK \bar K$ state found to the $η(1475)$.

## Key claim

The paper finds a clear resonance structure in the ηK K̅ system around 1490 MeV that is stable under parameter variation, which they associate with the η(1475) meson; however, for the η′K K̅ system they find only a threshold cusp effect, not a genuine resonance, contradicting an earlier claim that the η′K K̅ system could produce the X(1835).

## Method

The authors apply the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating the K K̅ pair as a cluster (the f₀(980) or a₀(980) resonance) and the η or η′ as the third particle that undergoes multiple scattering with the cluster constituents. The two-body interactions (K K̅, ηK, η′K) are described using the chiral unitary approach with coupled channels, and the cluster form factor is constructed from the same chiral dynamics without introducing free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The ηK K̅ resonance appears at about 1490 MeV with a width of about 100 MeV, about 38 MeV below the η f₀(980) threshold.
- When the width of the f₀(980) (taken as 60 MeV) is included, the peak position remains unchanged but the width increases to about 120 MeV.
- The η′K K̅ threshold is at 1942 MeV; the peak seen in the η′K K̅ amplitude sits at about 1940 MeV, consistent with a cusp effect rather than a bound state.
- The κ meson pole is found at (743.72 − i275.36) MeV, consistent with prior work.
- The cutoff used to regularize the two-body loop functions is qₘₐₓ = 903 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the FCA approach previously applied to multi-ρ systems (Roca 2010) and to charmed resonances (Xiao 2012) to the ηK K̅ and η′K K̅ systems. It confirms the earlier result of Migueleta (2010) that the ηK K̅ system can be identified with the η(1475), but disagrees with that work’s claim that the η′K K̅ system produces the X(1835). The authors note that their FCA results are consistent with full Faddeev calculations for other three-body systems (e.g., K̅NN, DNN) and call for further calculations using different approaches to clarify the situation.
