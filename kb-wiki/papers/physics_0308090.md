# Binding Energies and Scattering Observables in the ^3He^4He_2 Atomic System

> The ^3He^4He_2 three-atomic system is studied on the basis of a hard-core version of the Faddeev differential equations. The binding energy of the ^3He^4He_2 trimer, scattering phase shifts, and the scattering length of a ^3He atom off a ^4He dimer are calculated using the LM2M2 and TTY He-He interatomic potentials.

# Binding Energies and Scattering Observables in the ^3He^4He_2 Atomic System

## Key claim

The ^3He^4He_2 trimer exists with a binding energy one order of magnitude smaller than the symmetric ^4He_3 ground state, but contrary to the symmetric case there is no excited Efimov-type state in this asymmetric system. The calculated binding energies and scattering lengths depend on the maximum angular momentum included (l_max) and show fair agreement with prior work, though some discrepancies remain due to computational limitations.

## Method

The authors solved a hard-core version of the Faddeev differential equations using finite-difference approximation on a two-dimensional grid, with grid dimensions up to N_rho = 600 and N_theta = 605 and a cut-off hyperradius rho_max = 200 Å. They employed two realistic He-He interatomic potentials, the LM2M2 potential by Aziz and Slaman (1991) and the TTY potential by Tang, Toennies and Yiu (1995), and considered partial waves up to l_max = 4 for zero total angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- ^3He^4He_2 trimer binding energy with LM2M2 potential: 7.30 mK (l_max=0), 13.15 mK (l_max=2), 13.84 mK (l_max=4)
- ^3He^4He_2 trimer binding energy with TTY potential: 7.25 mK (l_max=0), 13.09 mK (l_max=2), 13.78 mK (l_max=4)
- ^4He-dimer energy: 1.30348 mK for LM2M2 potential and 1.30962 mK for TTY potential
- ^3He–^4He_2 scattering length with LM2M2 potential: 38.5 Å (l_max=0), 22.2 Å (l_max=2), 21.0 Å (l_max=4)
- ^3He–^4He_2 scattering length with TTY potential: 38.8 Å (l_max=0), 22.4 Å (l_max=2), 21.2 Å (l_max=4)
- Mass ratio m_^3He / m_^4He = 0.753517
- Contribution to binding energy: about 53% from l=0 partial waves and 42% from l=1,2 partial waves; l=3 and l=4 contribute approximately 5%

## Context

This work extends the hard-core Faddeev approach previously applied to the symmetric ^4He_3 system by Sandhas, Kolganova, Motovilov and collaborators (2002) to the asymmetric ^3He^4He_2 system, which had received comparatively little attention. Prior numerical studies of ^3He^4He_2 trimers were limited to Esry, Lin and Greene (1996), Nielsen (1998), Bressani (2000), and Roudnev (2003), with only Roudnev reporting scattering calculations. The results enable further studies of ultra-low-energy scattering of ^3He atoms off ^4He dimers and provide benchmarks for understanding the formation of liquid helium drops, superfluidity, and Bose-Einstein condensation in asymmetric helium systems.
