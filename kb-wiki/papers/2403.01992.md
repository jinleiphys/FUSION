# Systematic study of cluster radioactivity in trans-lead nuclei within various versions of proximity potential formalisms

> In this work, based on the framework of the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM), we systematically study the cluster radioactivity half-lives of 26 trans-lead nuclei by considering the cluster preformation probability which is found to possess a simple mass dependence on the emitted cluster by R. Blendowske and H. Walliser [Phys. Rev. Lett. 61, 1930(1988)]. Meanwhile, we investigate 28 different versions of the proximity potential formalisms, which are the most complete known proximity potential formalisms and have been proposed for the description of proton radioactivity, two-proton radioactivity, α decay, heavy-ion radioactivity, quasi-elastic scattering, fusion reactions and other applications. The calculated results show that the modified forms of proximity potential 1977 denoted as Prox.77-12 and the proximity potential 1981 denoted as Prox.81 are the most appropriate proximity potential formalisms for the study of cluster radioactivity as the root-mean-square deviation between experimental data and relevant theoretical results obtained are least and the both values are 0.681. For comparison, a universal decay law (UDL) proposed by Qi et al. [Phys. Rev. C 80, 044326 (2009)], a unified formula of half-lives for α decay and cluster radioactivity proposed by Ni et al. [Phys. Rev. C 78, 044310 (2008)] and a scaling law (SL) in cluster decay proposed by Horoi et al. [J. Phys. G 30, 945 (2004)] are also used. In addition, utilizing CPPM with Prox.77- 12, Prox.77-1, Prox.77-2 and Prox.81, we predict the half-lives of 51 potential cluster radioactive candidates whose cluster radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in NUBASE2020. The predicted results are in the same order of magnitude with those obtained by using the compared semi-empirical and/or empirical formulae.

## Key claim

The authors systematically test 28 versions of proximity potential formalisms within the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM) and find that the modified forms Prox.77-12 and Prox.81 are the most appropriate for cluster radioactivity studies, achieving the lowest root-mean-square deviation of 0.681 between experimental and theoretical half-lives, a conclusion that depends on using the specific cluster preformation probability formula of Blendowske and Walliser (1988).

## Method

The authors calculated cluster radioactivity half-lives for 26 trans-lead nuclei using the CPPM framework, incorporating the cluster preformation probability via a simple mass-dependent formula based on alpha preformation factors (0.02897 for even-even, 0.0214 for odd-A parent nuclei). They compared 28 different proximity potential formalisms (including Prox.77 family with 13 surface energy sets, Prox.81, Prox.00, Bass73/77/80, CW76, BW91, AW95, Ngô80, Denisov, and Guo2013) alongside three semi-empirical formulae: the universal decay law (UDL) of Qi et al. (2009), Ni et al.'s unified formula (2008), and the scaling law (SL) of Horoi et al. (2004). The WKB approximation was used for barrier penetration, and the authors also predicted half-lives for 51 additional cluster radioactive candidates from NUBASE2020.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation between experimental and theoretical half-lives for Prox.77-12 and Prox.81: 0.681 (the lowest among all 28 proximity potentials tested)
* Preformation probability for alpha decay: 0.02897 for even-even parent nuclei and 0.0214 for odd-A parent nuclei, taken from prior work (2009)
* Assault frequency used in half-life calculation: 1.0 × 10²² s⁻¹
* 26 trans-lead nuclei studied for cluster emission (clusters from ¹⁴C to ³⁴Si, parent nuclei from ²²¹Fr to ²⁴²Cm)
* 51 potential cluster radioactive candidates predicted using CPPM with Prox.77-12, Prox.77-1, Prox.77-2, and Prox.81

## Context

This work extends prior comparative studies of proximity potentials for cluster radioactivity by Kumar et al. (2012), who tested 8 versions and favored Prox.77; Zhang et al. (2016), who tested 14 versions and favored Bass77 and Denisov potentials for even-even nuclei; and Santhosh et al. (2017), who tested 12 versions and concluded Bass80 was most appropriate. The present study uses the most complete set of 28 proximity potential formalisms and a different preformation probability treatment, resolving previous discrepancies by identifying Prox.77-12 and Prox.81 as optimal. The predictions for 51 candidates enable future experimental searches for cluster radioactivity in nuclei where it is energetically allowed but not yet quantified.
