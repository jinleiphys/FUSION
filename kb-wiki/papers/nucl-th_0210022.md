# The Two-Nucleon System beyond the Pion Threshold

> A model is developed for the hadronic and electromagnetic interaction in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold in the framework of meson, nucleon and $Δ$ degrees of freedom. It is based on time-ordered perturbation theory and includes full meson retardation in potentials and exchange currents as well as loop contributions to the nucleonic one-body current. Results for some selected processes are discussed.

# The Two-Nucleon System beyond the Pion Threshold

## Key claim
A retarded coupled-channel model incorporating meson, nucleon, and Δ degrees of freedom can describe hadronic and electromagnetic reactions in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold, but only if full meson retardation is included rather than the static approximation; even then, discrepancies remain in polarization observables, and the hadronic interaction model requires further improvement.

## Method
The model is based on time-ordered perturbation theory with a Hilbert space comprising two bare nucleons, one nucleon plus one Δ resonance, and two nucleons plus one meson (π, ρ, σ, δ, ω, η). Effective hadronic interactions are derived from XNN and πNΔ vertices inserted into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, yielding retarded one-boson exchange mechanisms for NN → NN, NN → NΔ, and NΔ → NΔ transitions, with the Elster potential used for the retarded NN interaction. Electromagnetic currents include retarded pionic meson exchange currents, electromagnetic loop contributions, static ρ MEC, and Δ MEC, with gauge invariance fulfilled in leading order of 1/M_N.

## Key numbers
- The model describes the ¹D₂ NN channel phase shift and inelasticity up to about T_lab = 800 MeV.
- The GDH-integral on the deuteron is I^GDH_d = 0.65 μb, compared to the sum of proton and neutron GDH values of 438 μb.
- The static approach of Wilhelm et al. (1993) fails to describe deuteron photodisintegration data, while the retarded approach resolves the problems in the differential cross section.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous retarded coupled-channel NN-NΔ approach (Schwamb and Arenhövel, 1998a, 1998b, 2001a, 2001b, 2001c) and contrasts with static approximations such as the Bonn-OBEPR potential (Machleidt and Holinde, 1987) and the unitary three-body model of Tanabe and Ohta (1989). The model enables future application to electrodisintegration and testing of the GDH sum rule on the deuteron, though the authors note that extraction of the neutron amplitude from deuteron reactions cannot be performed model-independently due to D-state components and final state interactions.
