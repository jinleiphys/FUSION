# Light $Ξ$ hypernuclei in four-body cluster models

> Detailed structure calculations in $^{\: 12}_{Ξ^-}$Be, $^{\: 5}_{Ξ^-}$H, $^{\: 9}_{Ξ^-}$Li, $^{\: 7}_{Ξ^-}$H and $^{\:10}_{Ξ^-}$Li are performed within the framework of the microscopic two-, three- and four-body cluster models using the Gaussian Expansion Method.

## Key claim
The authors predict that the light Xi hypernuclei 7_Xi- H and 10_Xi- Li should have bound states, but this conclusion depends on the choice of effective Xi N interaction and the Fermi momentum parameter k_F, which is tuned to match the experimental indication of a Woods-Saxon potential depth of about 14 MeV from the BNL-E885 experiment on 12C.

## Method
The authors performed four-body cluster model calculations using the Gaussian Expansion Method for 12_Xi- Be (alpha alpha t Xi-), 7_Xi- H (alpha n n Xi-), and 10_Xi- Li (alpha alpha n Xi-). They adopted effective Xi N G-matrix interactions derived from two Nijmegen models (ND and ESC04d), with the interactions adjusted so that the calculated Xi- s-state energy in 12_Xi- Be matched the value of -2.2 MeV inferred from the BNL-E885 Woods-Saxon potential. The alpha Xi- and t Xi- folding potentials were constructed from the G-matrix interactions, and the Xi n interactions were represented by three-range Gaussian potentials fitted to reproduce the T=1 G-matrices at k_F = 1.0 fm^-1.

## Key numbers
* The Xi- binding energy of the 1^-_1 state in 12_Xi- Be was tuned to 2.24 MeV (ESC without Coulomb) and 2.23 MeV (ND without Coulomb), corresponding to the experimental indication of a Woods-Saxon potential depth of about 14 MeV.
* With Coulomb potentials included, the 1^-_1 state binding energies became 4.98 MeV (ESC) and 4.82 MeV (ND), with conversion widths of 4.64 MeV (ESC) and 1.66 MeV (ND).
* For the alpha Xi- (5_Xi- H) system with ESC at k_F = 0.9 fm^-1, the energy was -1.36 MeV (with Coulomb) and the alpha-Xi- r.m.s. radius was 3.89 fm; at k_F = 1.055 fm^-1 the energy was -0.26 MeV and the radius was 6.83 fm.
* The appropriate k_F values for the G-matrix interactions were determined to be 1.055 fm^-1 (ESC) and 1.025 fm^-1 (ND) for the A=12 system.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous cluster-model studies of Lambda and double-Lambda hypernuclei (Hiyama 1996, 1997, 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2006) to the Xi hypernuclear sector. The calculations use the Nijmegen Hard-Core model D (ND) and the Extended Soft-Core model ESC04d, which were selected because they produce attractive Xi-nucleus potentials consistent with experimental indications from the BNL-E885 experiment and from emulsion data on twin-Lambda hypernuclei. The predictions of bound states in 7_Xi- H and 10_Xi- Li are intended to motivate future (K-,K+) experiments at J-PARC using 7Li and 10B targets, which would provide information on the spin- and isospin-averaged Xi N interaction.
