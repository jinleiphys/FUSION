# The dependence of the number of pomerons on the impact parameter and the long-range rapidity correlations in pp collisions

> The simple model which enables to take into account the effects of a colour string fusion in pp interactions is suggested. The parameters of the model are connected with the parameters of the pomeron trajectory and its couplings to hadrons. On the base of the model the MC algorithm which enables to calculate the long-range correlation functions between multiplicities and between the average transverse momentum and the multiplicity in pp collisions is developed.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple model for pp collisions that connects the impact-parameter dependence of the number of cut pomerons to the parameters of the pomeron trajectory and its couplings to hadrons, enabling Monte-Carlo calculations of long-range correlation functions. The model assumes a gaussian dependence of the average number of cut pomerons on impact parameter with a poissonian event-by-event distribution, which after integration over impact parameter reproduces the quasi-eikonal formula for the cross-section with N cut-pomeron exchange.

## Method

The authors model the distribution of colour strings in the transverse plane of a pp collision by analogy with nucleus-nucleus collisions, using a gaussian partonic profile function for the nucleon. They assume the number of cut pomerons (each corresponding to a pair of strings) follows a poissonian distribution at fixed impact parameter, excluding the N=0 case to enforce non-diffractive scattering. The parameters N0 and α of the gaussian dependence are connected to the pomeron trajectory parameters (Δ, α′, γ, R², C) from quasi-eikonal and Regge approaches. Using this framework, a Monte-Carlo algorithm generates string configurations, and long-range correlation functions between multiplicities and between average transverse momentum and multiplicity are calculated using four versions of the string fusion model (local fusion, cluster formation, and their cellular analogs).

## Key numbers

- At √s = 60 GeV: α = 0.51 fm, N0 = 3.38
- At √s = 7000 GeV: α = 0.60 fm, N0 = 9.02
- Pomeron trajectory parameters used (from Kaidalov and Shabelski, 2002): Δ = 0.139, α′ = 0.21 GeV⁻², γ = 1.77 GeV⁻², R² = 3.18 GeV⁻², C = 1.5
- Long-range correlation between multiplicities calculated at 63 GeV and 900 GeV with rapidity windows of width Δy = 0.8, corresponding to mean multiplicities ⟨n⟩ = 1.5 and 3.0 per window respectively
- Long-range correlation between multiplicity and mean transverse momentum calculated at 900 GeV with rapidity windows of width Δy = 1.6, corresponding to mean multiplicity ⟨n⟩ = 6.0 per window

## Context

This work extends the string fusion model, previously applied mainly to nucleus-nucleus collisions (Braun and Pajares, 2000; Vechernin, 2004), to pp collisions at high energy. The model connects to the quasi-eikonal and Regge approaches of Ter-Martirosyan (1973), Abramovsky, Gribov, and Kancheli (1973), and Kaidalov (1984). The results enable calculation of long-range correlation functions that can be compared with experimental data from the ALICE collaboration at the LHC, though the authors note that the four versions of the string fusion model produce very similar results and cannot be distinguished by these correlations alone.
