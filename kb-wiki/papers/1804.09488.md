# Sensitivity of Neutrino-Nucleus Interaction Measurements to 2p2h Excitations

> We calculate the charged-current cross sections obtained at the T2K off-axis near detector for $ν_μ$-induced events without pions and any number of protons in the final state using transport theory as encoded in the GiBUU model. In a comparison with recent T2K data the strength of the 2p2h multinucleon correlations is determined. Linking this to the isospin (T) of the initial nuclear state, it is found that T=0 leads to a significantly better fit of the recent cross sections obtained by T2K, thus achieving consistency of the 2p2h multi-nucleon correlation contributions between electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus reactions.

## Key claim

The paper determines that the isospin (T) of the initial nuclear state for 2p2h multinucleon correlations in neutrino-nucleus interactions must be T=0 to fit T2K data, achieving consistency with electron-nucleus scattering results. This conclusion is hedged by the observation that the chi-squared statistics may suffer from Peelle's Pertinent Puzzle and could over-favor models with lower overall normalization, so the authors focus on shape comparisons in specific kinematic regions rather than relying solely on chi-squared values.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model, which implements a non-equilibrium Green's function method, to calculate charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for events without pions and any number of protons in the final state. They linked neutrino 2p2h structure functions to electron 2p2h structure functions via the Wigner-Eckart theorem, with the isospin T of the initial nuclear state as the only free parameter. The model predictions were compared to T2K off-axis near detector data using chi-squared statistics with the full experimental covariance matrix, focusing on double-differential cross sections in muon kinematics and single-transverse kinematic imbalance observables.

## Key numbers

- The T2K neutrino beam energy peaks at 0.6 GeV.
- The analysis of transverse kinematic imbalance used a restricted phase space: proton momentum 450-1000 MeV, proton cos(theta) > 0.4, muon momentum > 250 MeV, muon cos(theta) > -0.6.
- For T=0 versus T=1, the neutrino structure functions (and thus cross sections) for the 2p2h channel differ by a factor of two.
- The chi-squared values for the transverse kinematic imbalance observables were substantially lowered when the isospin configuration was changed from T=1 to T=0.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of 2p2h contributions in neutrino-nucleus scattering that have given inconsistent results across experiments: MiniBooNE showed an excess over predictions consistent with either 2p2h or enhanced CCQE (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010), MINERvA measurements gave conflicting evidence depending on the final state and nuclear model used (Walton, 2014; Fiorentini, 2013; Megias, 2016), and earlier T2K results (Abe, 2016) suggested a need for 2p2h but with large uncertainties. The paper enables future studies of this isospin effect in experiments with argon targets such as MicroBooNE and DUNE, where T=2 would give a stronger enhancement factor.
