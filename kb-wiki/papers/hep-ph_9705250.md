# Partonic Picture of Nuclear Shadowing at Small x

> We investigate the nuclear shadowing mechanism in the context of perturbative QCD and the Glauber multiple scattering model. Using recent HERA data on nucleon structure function at small $x$, we put stringent constrains on the nucleon gluon density in the double-logarithm approximation. We suggest that the scaling violation of the nucleon structure function in the region of small $x$ and semihard scale $Q^2$ can be reliably described by perturbative QCD which is a central key to the understanding of the scale dependence of the nuclear shadowing effect. Our results indicate that while the shadowing of the quark density arises from an interplay between the ``soft'' and semihard QCD processes, the gluon shadowing is largely driven by a perturbative shadowing mechanism. We demonstrate that the gluon shadowing is a robust phenomenon at large $Q^2$ and can be unambiguously predicted by perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The authors argue that nuclear gluon shadowing at momentum transfers above a semihard scale \(Q^2_{\rm SH} \simeq 3\) GeV\(^2\) can be unambiguously predicted by perturbative QCD, largely independent of the unknown non-perturbative initial gluon shadowing at low \(Q^2\), provided the nucleon gluon density is constrained by HERA data on the scaling violation of \(F_2^N\) in the double-logarithm approximation.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon structure function \(F_2^N(x,Q^2)\) using a double-logarithm approximation (DLA) for the gluon density, fitting the initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\) and normalization constants to H1 data at \(x = 8 \times 10^{-5}\). They then apply the Glauber multiple-scattering model in the target rest frame, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a \(q\bar q\) pair (for quark shadowing) or a \(gg\) pair (for gluon shadowing), with the cross section for each pair on a nucleon given by the color transparency formula proportional to \(r^2 \alpha_s x' g_N^{\rm DLA}(x', 4/r^2)\). The nuclear cross section is obtained via the eikonal integral \(\sigma_{hA} = \int d^2b \, 2\{1 - e^{-\sigma_{hN} T_A(b)/2}\}\), and the shadowing ratio is derived from the resulting nuclear structure function.

## Key numbers

*   The semihard scale where perturbative shadowing dominates is \(Q^2_{\rm SH} \simeq 3\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The initial scale for the perturbative DLA evolution is determined to be \(Q_0^2 = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The normalization constant for the initial gluon density is \(c_g = 2.2\) and for the initial nucleon structure function is \(c_P = 0.2\).
*   The DLA description of \(F_2^N\) is shown to be reliable starting at \(Q^2 \sim 0.85\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The gluon density increases with \(Q^2\) approximately 12 times faster than the quark density due to the different splitting functions.
*   The cross section for a gluon pair on a nucleon is related to that for a quark-antiquark pair by \(\sigma_{ggN} = 9\sigma_{q\bar qN}/4\).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber diffractive approximation for nuclear shadowing, previously applied to quark densities, to the gluon sector, building on the recombination model of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (GLR) and the work of Mueller and of Ayala, Gay Ducati, and Levin (AGL). It contrasts with the vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model, which predicts a higher-twist, rapidly disappearing shadowing at large \(Q^2\), by showing that perturbative QCD can sustain shadowing up to semihard scales. The results enable more reliable predictions for minijet and charm production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s} \geq 200\) GeV, where knowledge of the nuclear gluon density at small \(x\) is essential.
