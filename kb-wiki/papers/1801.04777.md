# The MAID Legacy and Future

> The MAID project is a collection of theoretical models for pseudoscalar meson photo- and electroproduction from nucleons. It is online available and produces results in real time calculations. In addition to kinematical variables also model parameters, especially for baryon resonances, can be online changed and investigated. Over 20 years MAID has become quite popular and the MAID web pages have been called more than 7.7 million times.

# The MAID Legacy and Future

## Key claim

The MAID project provides a collection of online theoretical models for pseudoscalar meson photo- and electroproduction that have been called more than 7.7 million times over 20 years, but the author notes that with high-statistics recent data and new double-polarization observables, the limitations of the MAID models have become obvious, and it is unclear whether the service can be provided much longer.

## Method

The unitary isobar model MAID separates the t-matrix into a background and a resonance contribution, with the background described by Born terms with energy-dependent mixing of pseudovector-pseudoscalar pi-NN coupling and t-channel vector meson exchanges, unitarized via the K-matrix approximation using pion-nucleon elastic scattering amplitudes from the GWU/SAID analysis. Resonance contributions use Breit-Wigner forms with energy-dependent widths and a unitary phase that satisfies the Fermi-Watson theorem below two-pion threshold. Transition form factors are parameterized as functions of Q^2 using polynomials and exponentials, with parameters determined from fits to the world database of photoproduction and electroproduction data.

## Key numbers

- The MAID web pages have been called more than 7.7 million times since 1998.
- MAID2007 covers an energy range from threshold to W = 2 GeV and photon virtualities up to Q^2 = 5 GeV^2.
- The pseudo-threshold values for Dalitz decays are Q^2_pt = {-0.086, -0.252, -0.55} GeV^2 for the Delta(1232)3/2+, N(1440)1/2+, and N(1680)5/2+ resonances, respectively.
- For the N -> Delta(1232) transition form factors, the normalization values at the photon point are g_M^0 = 3.00, g_E^0 = 0.0637, and g_C^0 = 0.1240, with exponential parameters gamma_M = 0.23, gamma_E = 0.16, and gamma_C = 0.23 in GeV^-2.
- The ChiralMAID approach is applicable for photon virtualities Q^2 <= 0.15 GeV^2.
- A new measurement of the longitudinal transition form factor of the Roper resonance was made at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 by the A1@MAMI collaboration in Mainz.

## Context

This paper reviews the MAID project, which began in 1998 with pion photo- and electroproduction (Maid98) and was updated in 2007 (MAID07), alongside related models including the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model, KaonMAID, EtaMAID, and TwoPionMAID. The work extends earlier measurements of the N -> Delta(1232) transition form factors from the 1960s and the large experimental programs at Mainz, Bonn, Bates, and JLab in the 1990s. The MAID results have been used for applications including T-matrix pole analyses, dispersion relations for Compton scattering, and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, and the paper describes an ongoing EtaMAID2017 update using a Regge-plus-Resonance approach that has helped raise the N(1895)1/2- resonance from two-star to four-star status in the PDG.
