# Dilepton Production in Transport Calculations and Coarse-Grained Dynamics

> We present transport calculations with the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics approach (UrQMD) for dilepton spectra at SIS energies. While we obtain a good agreement with experiment for elementary reactions, in heavy-ion collisions an excess in the invariant mass spectra is observed which cannot be described by the model. As the pure transport calculations do not include any in-medium effects and are limited to hadronic degrees of freedom, we present an alternative approach that uses coarse-grained output from transport calculations to determine thermal dilepton emission rates. For this we apply the medium-modified $ρ$ spectral function by Eletsky et al. In a first exemplary comparison to data from the NA60 experiment we find that the coarse-graining approach gives reasonable results.

# Dilepton Production in Transport Calculations and Coarse-Grained Dynamics

## Key claim
Pure transport calculations with UrQMD fail to reproduce the low-mass dilepton excess observed in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, but a coarse-graining approach that applies the medium-modified rho spectral function by Eletsky et al. to averaged transport output yields reasonable agreement with NA60 data for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV, at least for the low-mass tail below the rho pole mass.

## Method
The authors performed UrQMD transport calculations for dilepton production at SIS energies (1.756 AGeV for Ar+KCl) and at SPS energies (158 AGeV for In+In). For the SPS case, they coarse-grained the UrQMD output onto a 3+1 dimensional space-time grid, averaging over several hundred events to obtain baryon and energy densities per cell, then used a chiral equation of state to extract temperature and baryon chemical potential assuming thermal equilibrium. Thermal dilepton emission rates were calculated using the medium-modified rho spectral function of Eletsky and Kapusta, which incorporates scattering from pions and nucleons in linear density approximation.

## Key numbers
- For Ar+KCl at 1.756 AGeV, the UrQMD transport calculation shows an excess in the invariant mass spectrum between 150 and 500 MeV that is not reproduced by the model.
- For In+In at 158 AGeV, the coarse-graining approach yields a temperature peak around 250 MeV and a baryon chemical potential that decreases rapidly then remains at 300-400 MeV.
- The coarse-grained energy and baryon densities at t = 5 fm after the beginning of the collision reach several times the nuclear-matter ground state densities.
- The temperature cools from the initial peak around 250 MeV to around 100 MeV at t = 20 fm.
- The dimuon excess spectrum for In+In at 158 AGeV with dN_ch/dy = 120 is shown for p_t < 0.2 GeV, where the broadened in-medium rho fills the low-mass tail below the rho pole mass.

## Context
This work extends previous UrQMD dilepton calculations (Schmidt 2008, Endres 2013) by addressing the known limitation that pure transport models lack in-medium modifications. The coarse-graining approach was first proposed by Huovinen (2002) and is presented here as an alternative to hybrid models with an intermediate hydrodynamic phase (Santini 2011). The results enable future comparisons with the many-body rho spectral function by Rapp and Wambach (1997), which has been successful in fireball model descriptions of NA60 and RHIC data (van Hees 2007), and allow systematic studies of different equations of state and spectral functions within a microscopically grounded dynamical framework.
