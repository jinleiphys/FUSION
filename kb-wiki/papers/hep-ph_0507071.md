# Coupled-channel study of crypto-exotic baryons with charm

> Identifying the t-channel exchange of vector mesons as the driving force for the s-wave scattering of pseudo-scalar mesons off the baryon ground states, a surprisingly rich spectrum of molecules is formed. We argue that chiral symmetry and large- N_c considerations determine that part of the interaction which generates the spectrum. All channels that are required by the existence of (u,d,s,c) quarks are considered. A bound state with exotic quantum numbers is predicted at mass 2.78 GeV. It couples strongly to the (bar D_s N),(bar D Lambda),(bar D Sigma) channels. A further charm minus-one system is predicted at mass 2.84 GeV as a result of (bar D_s Lambda), (bar D Xi) interactions. We suggest the existence of strongly bound crypto-exotic baryons, which contain a charm-anti-charm pair. Such states are narrow since they can decay only via OZI-violating processes. A narrow nucleon resonance is found at mass 3.52 GeV. It is a coupled-channel bound state of the (eta_c N), (bar D Sigma_c) system, which decays dominantly into the (eta' N) channel. Furthermore two isospin singlet hyperon states at mass 3.23 GeV and 3.58 GeV are observed as a consequence of coupled-channel interactions of the (bar D_s Lambda_c), (bar D Xi_c) and (eta_c Lambda),(bar D Xi_c') states. Most striking is the small width of about 1 MeV of the lower state. The upper state may be significantly broader due to a strong coupling to the (eta' Lambda) state. The two so far observed s-wave baryons with charm one are recovered. We argue that the Lambda_c(2880) is not a s-wave state. In addition to those states we predict the existence of about ten narrow s-wave baryon states with masses below 3 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a rich spectrum of narrow, strongly bound "crypto-exotic" baryon states containing a charm-anti-charm pair, formed as hadronic molecules via t-channel vector-meson exchange, with the caveat that the quantitative predictions rely on the assumption that the interaction strength of D- and ηc-mesons with baryon ground states is dominated by light vector-meson exchange, constrained by chiral symmetry and large-Nc QCD, and that moderate SU(4) breaking (within a factor of two) is allowed for the coupling constants.

## Method

The authors constructed the leading s-wave interaction of pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η, η', D, Ds, ηc) with baryon ground states (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ, Λc, Σc, Ξc, Ξc', Ωc, Ξcc, Ωcc) by assuming the driving force is the zero-range t-channel exchange of light vector mesons (ρ, K*, ω, φ). They wrote down the most general SU(3) invariant three-point vertices for meson-vector and baryon-vector couplings, then constrained the coupling constants using chiral symmetry (via the hidden local symmetry model) and large-Nc operator analysis (to enforce the OZI rule). The resulting coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations were solved to find bound states and resonances across sectors with charm C = -1, 0, 1, and 2.

## Key numbers

- A bound state with exotic quantum numbers (charm minus one, strangeness minus one) is predicted at mass **2.78 GeV**, coupling strongly to (D̅s N), (D̅ Λ), (D̅ Σ) channels.
- A further charm minus-one system (strangeness minus two) is predicted at mass **2.84 GeV** from (D̅s Λ), (D̅ Ξ) interactions.
- A narrow nucleon resonance (crypto-exotic, charm zero) is found at mass **3.52 GeV**, a coupled-channel bound state of (ηc N), (D̅ Σc), decaying dominantly into (η' N).
- Two isospin singlet hyperon states (crypto-exotic, charm zero) are observed at masses **3.23 GeV** and **3.58 GeV**; the lower state has a width of about **1 MeV**.
- An isospin triplet crypto-exotic state (charm zero) is predicted at **3.93 GeV** and an isospin doublet at **3.80 GeV**.
- A triplet of crypto-exotic states (charm zero) decaying dominantly into channels with an η' is obtained with masses **4.24 GeV** and **4.44 GeV**.
- The Λc(2593) is recovered as a narrow state; the Λc(2880) is argued not to be an s-wave state.
- About ten narrow s-wave baryon states with masses below **3 GeV** are predicted in the charm-one sector, including narrow Ξc(2670), Ξc(2760), Λc(2815), and Σc(2620) states.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channel studies by Lutz and collaborators (2004, 2005) that described open-charm baryon resonances as s-wave molecules of Goldstone bosons and open-charm baryons, and analogous computations for open-charm mesons (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2004; Hofmann and Lutz, 2004). It also builds on the conjecture that meson and baryon resonances not belonging to the large-Nc ground state should be viewed as hadronic molecular states (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2002; Lutz et al., 2002, 2004). The predictions for crypto-exotic baryons with hidden charm are compared to experimental hints: a possible hyperon resonance at 3.17 GeV with width < 20 MeV from a K- bubble chamber experiment (Amirzadeh, 1979) and a possible nucleon resonance at 3.52 GeV with narrow width 7+20-7 MeV from a π- bubble chamber experiment (Karnaukhov, 1991). The work enables future experimental searches for these predicted narrow states, particularly those decaying into η' channels, and provides a unified framework linking open-charm and hidden-charm baryon spectroscopy.
