# Peaks of sound velocity in two color dense QCD: quark saturation effects and semishort range correlations

> We discuss stiffening of dense matter in two color QCD (QC$_2$D) where hadrons are mesons and diquark baryons. We study two models which describe a transition of matter from the Bose-Einstein-Condensation regime at low density to the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer regime at high density. The first model is based on coherent states of diquarks, and the second is the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with diquark pairing terms. We particularly focus on how quark states are occupied as baryon density increases. We find that, due to the occupied quark levels, the ideal gas picture of diquarks breaks down at density significantly less than the density where baryon cores overlap. The saturation of quark states at low momenta stiffens equations of state. We also study the effects of interactions which depend on the quark occupation probability. We argue that equations of state become very stiff when the bulk part of the quark Fermi sea has the effective repulsion but the Fermi surface enjoys the attractive correlations. This disparity for different momentum domains is possible due to the strong channel dependence in gluon exchanges with momentum transfer of $0.2-1$ GeV. These concepts can be transferred from QC$_2$D to QCD in any numbers of colors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the stiffening of dense matter in two-color QCD (QC₂D) is driven by quark saturation effects, the progressive occupation of low-momentum quark states as baryon density increases, which cause the ideal gas picture of diquark baryons to break down at densities significantly lower than those where baryon cores physically overlap.

## Method

The authors study two models describing the BEC-to-BCS crossover in QC₂D with two flavors: a coherent-state model of diquarks where the ground state is constructed as a coherent superposition of diquark, tetraquark, hexaquark, and octaquark states for each momentum mode, and a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with diquark pairing terms. They compute the quark occupation probability \(f_{\mathbf{q}}(\mathbf{k}; n_B)\) as a function of baryon density and momentum, then use a quasiparticle energy parametrization with semishort range correlations to evaluate the equation of state.

## Key numbers

* For a diquark size parameter \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV, the density where baryon cores overlap is \(n_B^{\rm overlap} \simeq 6.5 n_0\) (with \(n_0 \sim 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\)), while the density for quark saturation at zero momentum is \(n_B^{\rm id.sat} \simeq 0.56 n_0\), much smaller than the overlap density.
* At \(n_B = 0.5 n_0\) with \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV, approximately 50% of the \(k=0\) quark states are occupied; at \(n_B = 4 n_0\) the occupation approaches 100%.
* The diquark amplitude \(d_{\mathbf{k}}\) peaks near the Fermi surface, with the maximum determined by the product of \(\Phi_{\mathbf{k}}\) and \(1-f_{\mathbf{q}}\); for large \(k\) the amplitude is small because \(\Phi_{\mathbf{k}}\) is small, while for small \(k\) it is suppressed because \(1-f_{\mathbf{q}}\) becomes small at high density.
* The parameter \(|C|^2\) in the coherent state deviates significantly from the ideal Bose gas value \(|C|^2 = n_B\) already around \(n_B \simeq 0.5 n_0\) for \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept of McLerran and Reddy (2018) and the microscopic quark-hadron continuity model of Kojo (2021) to QC₂D, where lattice Monte Carlo simulations are feasible due to the positive path-integral measure. The paper tests whether quark saturation effects, previously proposed for three-color QCD to explain neutron star observations, also operate in two-color QCD, where baryons are bosonic diquarks rather than fermions. The results enable future lattice QC₂D studies to directly verify the role of quark Pauli blocking in stiffening dense matter, and suggest that the concepts of soft deconfinement and momentum-dependent correlations (repulsive in the bulk Fermi sea, attractive near the Fermi surface) are transferable to QCD with any number of colors.
