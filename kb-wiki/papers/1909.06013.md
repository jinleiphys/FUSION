# The charge-dependent Bonn potentials with pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling

> To apply the high-precision realistic nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials on the investigations of relativistic many-body methods, the new versions of charge-dependent Bonn (CD-Bonn) $NN$ potential are constructed within the pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling instead of the pseudoscalar type in the original CD-Bonn potential worked out by Machleidt [Phys. Rev. C 63, 024001 (2001)]. Two effective scalar mesons are introduced, whose coupling constants with nucleon are independently determined at each partial wave for total angular momentum $J\leq 4$, to describe the charge dependence of $NN$ scattering data precisely, while the coupling constants between vector, pseudovector mesons and nucleon are identical in all channels. Three revised CD-Bonn potentials adopting the pseudovector pion-nucleon couplings (pvCD-Bonn) are generated by fitting the Nijmegen PWA phase shift data and deuteron binding energy with different pion-nucleon coupling strengths, which can reproduce the phase shifts at spin-single channels and low-energy $NN$ scattering parameters very well, and provide the significantly different mixing parameters at spin-triplet channels. Furthermore, the $D$-state probabilities of deuteron from these potentials range from $4.22\%$ to $6.05\%$. It demonstrates that these potentials contain different components of tensor force, which will be useful to discuss the roles of tensor force in nuclear few-body and many-body systems.

## Key claim
The authors construct three revised CD-Bonn potentials (pvCD-Bonn A, B, C) using pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling instead of the original pseudoscalar type, which reproduce Nijmegen PWA phase shifts well but produce significantly different mixing parameters at spin-triplet channels, particularly epsilon_1, depending on the chosen pion-nucleon coupling strength. The claim is conditional: the potentials are intended for use in relativistic many-body methods where pseudovector coupling suppresses spurious attraction from coupling to negative-energy states, but the paper does not demonstrate that these potentials actually improve relativistic many-body calculations.

## Method
The authors start from the charge-dependent Bonn (CD-Bonn) potential framework of Machleidt (2001) and replace the pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling with pseudovector coupling, keeping the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. Two effective scalar mesons (sigma_1 and sigma_2) are introduced with coupling constants independently fitted at each partial wave for total angular momentum J <= 4 to reproduce Nijmegen PWA phase shift data up to 350 MeV laboratory energy, while the coupling constants of omega, rho, and pion mesons are kept identical across all channels. Three variants (pvCD-Bonn A, B, C) are generated by choosing different pion-nucleon coupling strengths and cut-off momenta, following the pattern of Bonn A, B, C potentials.

## Key numbers
* Deuteron D-state probabilities range from 4.22% to 6.05% across the three pvCD-Bonn potentials.
* Pion-nucleon coupling constants g_pi^2/4pi = 13.9 (pvCD-Bonn A), 13.7 (pvCD-Bonn B), and 13.6 (pvCD-Bonn C) for both neutral and charged pions.
* Pion cut-off momenta Lambda_pi = 1.12 GeV (pvCD-Bonn A), 1.50 GeV (pvCD-Bonn B), and 1.72 GeV (pvCD-Bonn C).
* Chi-squared values for pp interactions: 3.55 x 10^-3 (pvCD-Bonn A), 1.85 x 10^-3 (pvCD-Bonn B), and 2.24 x 10^-4 (pvCD-Bonn C).
* Chi-squared values for np (T=0) interactions: 8.06 x 10^-2 (pvCD-Bonn A), 1.03 x 10^-2 (pvCD-Bonn B), and 2.65 x 10^-4 (pvCD-Bonn C).
* The constraint at free space is F_pi^2(k^2=0) * g_pi^2/4pi = 13.42.
* For partial waves with J > 4, a single scalar meson is used with m_sigma = 452 MeV, g_sigma^2/4pi = 2.3 (pvCD-Bonn B, C) or m_sigma = 470 MeV, g_sigma^2/4pi = 4.3 (pvCD-Bonn A).

## Context
This work extends the CD-Bonn potential of Machleidt (2001) by replacing pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling with pseudovector coupling, motivated by the preference for pseudovector coupling in relativistic nuclear many-body methods such as the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach used by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990). The three variants (pvCD-Bonn A, B, C) follow the pattern of Bonn A, B, C potentials to provide different tensor force strengths, enabling future studies of tensor force roles in nuclear few-body and many-body systems. The paper does not apply these potentials to actual many-body calculations, leaving that as future work; the potentials are intended to be used in relativistic frameworks where pseudoscalar coupling produces spurious strong attraction from coupling to antinucleon states, as discussed by Fuchs and collaborators (1998).
