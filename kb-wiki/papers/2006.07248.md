# Neutron star inner crust: effects of rotation and magnetic fields

> We study the role of the pasta phases on the properties of rotating and magnetized neutron stars. In order to investigate such systems, we make use of two different relativistic mean-field unified inner-crust--core equations of state, with a different density dependence of the symmetry energy, and an inner-crust computed within a Thomas-Fermi calculation. Special attention is given to the crust-core transition density, and the pasta phases effects on the global properties of stars. The effects of strong magnetic fields and fast rotation are computed by solving the Einstein-Maxwell equations self-consistently, taking into account anisotropies induced by the centrifugal and the Lorentz force. The location of the magnetic field neutral line and the maximum of the Lorentz force on the equatorial plane are calculated. The conditions under which they fall inside the inner crust region are discussed. We verified that models with a larger symmetry energy slope show more sensitivity to the variation of the magnetic field. One of the maxima of the Lorentz force, as well as the neutral line, and for a certain range of frequencies, fall inside the inner crust region. This may have consequences in the fracture of the crust, and may help explain phenomena associated with star quakes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for rotating neutron stars with a poloidal magnetic field, the neutral line (where magnetic instabilities develop) and one maximum of the Lorentz force can fall inside the inner crust region, but only for certain rotation frequencies and depending on the density dependence of the symmetry energy; for non-rotating stars the neutral line lies deeper in the core at about 80% of the stellar radius.

## Method

The authors constructed unified inner-crust-core equations of state using a Thomas-Fermi calculation for the inner crust within two relativistic mean-field models (NL3ωρ with symmetry energy slopes L=55 and 88 MeV). They solved the Einstein-Maxwell equations self-consistently using the LORENE pseudo-spectral code, treating the star as stationary, axisymmetric, and with infinite conductivity, to compute the structure of magnetized and rotating neutron stars including the effects of the Lorentz force and centrifugal force on the crust-core transition region.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density: ρ_drip ∼ 4.3 × 10^11 g/cm^3
- For a 1.5 M⊙ star with L=55 MeV and surface magnetic field B_s = 4.41 × 10^15 G at the pole: R_1 = 10.14 km, R_2 = 9.944 km, R = 11.59 km, neutral line R_n = 8.640 km (equatorial plane)
- For a 1.5 M⊙ star with L=88 MeV and B_s = 4.41 × 10^15 G: R_1 = 10.64 km, R_2 = 9.402 km, R = 11.96 km, R_n = 8.820 km
- Critical rotation frequency f_crit at which the neutral line disappears for a 1.5 M⊙ star with B_s ≈ 2.2 × 10^16 G: 109 Hz (L=55 MeV) and 108 Hz (L=88 MeV)
- For a 1.2 M⊙ star with L=88 MeV and B_s = 4.41 × 10^16 G, the equatorial radius is about 5% larger than the polar radius; for 1.4 and 1.8 M⊙ stars this difference is about 2-3%
- The shift of the extended crust-core boundary at the equator for B_s = 4.41 × 10^16 G goes up to more than 5% for L=55 MeV and 15% for L=88 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized neutron star crusts by Fang (2017, 2017a) and Chen (2017), who identified an extended crust region where homogeneous and non-homogeneous matter coexist under strong magnetic fields. It also builds on the stability analysis of Lander (2011), who showed that instabilities develop around the neutral line in stars with poloidal fields, and on the unified equation-of-state calculations of Pais (2016). The results enable future work connecting crust properties to quasi-periodic oscillations observed in magnetar flares, as discussed by Steiner (2009), Sotani (2013, 2019), and Gabler (2012), and may help explain starquake phenomena if the Lorentz force maximum or neutral line lies within the pasta-phase region of the inner crust.
