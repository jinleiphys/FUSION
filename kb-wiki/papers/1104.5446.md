# Relativistic analyses of quasielastic neutrino cross sections at MiniBooNE kinematics

> Two relativistic approaches are considered to evaluate the quasielastic double-differential and integrated neutrino-nucleus cross sections. One, based on the relativistic impulse approximation, relies on the microscopic description of nuclear dynamics using relativistic mean field theory, and incorporates a description of the final-state interactions. The second is based on the superscaling behavior exhibited by electron scattering data and its applicability, due to the universal character of the scaling function, to the analysis of neutrino scattering reactions. The role played by the vector meson-exchange currents in the two-particle two-hole sector is also incorporated and the results obtained are compared with the recent data for neutrinos measured by the MiniBooNE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Superscaling (SuSA) approaches, when compared with MiniBooNE data for quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections, underestimate the measured double-differential cross sections at large muon scattering angles and low muon energies, even after including two-particle two-hole meson-exchange current (2p-2h MEC) contributions; however, the RMF model reproduces the shape of the angular distribution better than SuSA, suggesting that adding 2p-2h MEC effects to RMF could yield agreement in both magnitude and shape without requiring an anomalously large axial mass.

## Method

The authors computed flux-averaged double-differential cross sections for charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) muon neutrino scattering on carbon-12 at MiniBooNE kinematics using three relativistic approaches: the relativistic impulse approximation with a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) description of bound and scattering states, the phenomenological Superscaling (SuSA) model based on a scaling function extracted from electron scattering data, and SuSA extended with a fully relativistic model of 2p-2h meson-exchange currents (MEC) affecting only the vector transverse response. The RMF model uses self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions for bound nucleons and the same relativistic mean field for the outgoing nucleon to incorporate final-state interactions, while SuSA assumes scaling of the zeroth kind (equal longitudinal and transverse scaling functions) and uses a fit to experimental longitudinal electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The mean neutrino energy flux is \(\langle E_\nu \rangle = 788\) MeV, with values up to 3 GeV.
- The standard nucleon axial mass used in all calculations is \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/c\(^2\); the Relativistic Fermi Gas model requires \(M_A \approx 1.35\) GeV/c\(^2\) to match MiniBooNE total cross section data.
- The RMF model provides longitudinal and transverse scaling functions that differ by typically 20%, with the transverse one being larger.
- The overall normalization error on the MiniBooNE data is \(\delta N = 10.7\%\).
- The 2p-2h MEC contribution to the total cross section is affected by an uncertainty of about 5% associated with the treatment at low momentum transfers.
- For the most forward angular bin (\(0.9 < \cos\theta < 1\)), roughly 1/2 of the total cross section arises from excitation energies below \(\sim 50\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the previous SuSA analysis of Amaro (2011) by adding a comparison with the RMF approach, which had been successfully applied to inclusive quasielastic electron scattering and shown to reproduce the asymmetric shape of the experimental scaling function (Caballero, 2006; Caballero, 2007). The paper contrasts with non-relativistic spectral function models (Barbaro, 1996; Benhar, 2010) that also underpredict the MiniBooNE data, and with the non-relativistic calculations of Martini (2009, 2010) that included multiple knockout excitations and matched the total cross section without increasing the axial mass. The results suggest that combining RMF dynamics with 2p-2h MEC contributions could resolve the discrepancy between theory and MiniBooNE data without invoking an anomalous axial mass, but definitive conclusions require including correlation currents and MEC effects in the axial-vector channel.
