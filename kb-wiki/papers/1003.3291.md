# The dynamics of quark-gluon plasma and AdS/CFT

> In these pedagogical lectures, we present the techniques of the AdS/CFT correspondence which can be applied to the study of real time dynamics of a strongly coupled plasma system. These methods are based on solving gravitational Einstein's equations on the string/gravity side of the AdS/CFT correspondence. We illustrate these techniques with applications to the boost-invariant expansion of a plasma system. We emphasize the common underlying AdS/CFT description both in the large proper time regime where hydrodynamic dynamics dominates, and in the small proper time regime where the dynamics is far from equilibrium. These AdS/CFT methods provide a fascinating arena interrelating General Relativity phenomenae with strongly coupled gauge theory physics.

## Key claim

These pedagogical lectures demonstrate that the AdS/CFT correspondence can be used to study real-time dynamics of strongly coupled plasma by solving gravitational Einstein's equations, covering both the hydrodynamic regime at large proper time and the far-from-equilibrium regime at small proper time, but only for the maximally supersymmetric N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory rather than for QCD directly.

## Method

The author solves 5-dimensional Einstein's equations with a negative cosmological constant in an AdS background, using Fefferman-Graham coordinates where the metric takes the form ds² = (g_μν dx^μ dx^ν + dz²)/z². The energy-momentum tensor of the dual gauge theory is extracted from the z⁴ coefficient in the near-boundary expansion of the metric via holographic renormalization, with the explicit formula ⟨T_μν⟩ = (N_c²/2π²) · g_μν^(4). For the boost-invariant flow case, the author imposes longitudinal boost invariance and transverse homogeneity, reducing the problem to a single function ε(τ) determined by energy-momentum conservation and tracelessness.

## Key numbers

- The entropy density of N=4 SYM plasma at strong coupling is s = (1/2) N_c² π² T³, which is 3/4 of the free gas (Stefan-Boltzmann) value.
- The energy density of N=4 SYM plasma at strong coupling is E = (3/8) N_c² π² T⁴.
- The Hawking temperature of the AdS planar black hole dual to uniform plasma is T_H = √2/(π z₀), where z₀ is the horizon location.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s equals 1/(4π) at strong coupling for any theory with a gravitational dual.

## Context

These lectures extend the AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1998) from static equilibrium properties to real-time dynamics of strongly coupled plasma, building on the Bjorken boost-invariant flow model (Bjorken 1983) used in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The work contrasts with lattice QCD methods, which are effective for static properties but cannot easily access Minkowski-signature real-time dynamics. The approach enables future study of thermalization and the transition from far-from-equilibrium to hydrodynamic behavior, and may eventually be generalized to more realistic gauge theories with AdS/CFT duals that are closer to QCD.
