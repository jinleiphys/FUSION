# Ultrahigh-energy cosmic-ray nuclei and neutrinos from engine-driven supernovae

> Transrelativistic supernovae (SNe), which are likely driven by central engines via jets or winds, have been among candidate sources of ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs). We investigate acceleration and survival of UHECR nuclei in the external reverse shock scenario. With composition models used in Zhang et al. (2018), we calculate spectra of escaping cosmic rays and secondary neutrinos. If their local rate is $\sim1$% of the core-collapse supernova rate, the observed UHECR spectrum and composition can be explained with the total cosmic-ray energy ${\mathcal E}_{\rm cr}\sim10^{51}$ erg. The maximum energy of UHECR nuclei can reach $\sim 10^{20}-{10}^{21}\rm~eV$. The diffuse flux of source neutrinos is predicted to be $\sim 10^{-11} - 10^{-10}~{\rm GeV}~{\rm cm}^{-2}~{\rm s}^{-1}~{\rm sr}^{-1}$ in the 0.1-1 EeV range, satisfying nucleus-survival bounds. The associated cosmogenic neutrino flux is calculated, and shown to be comparable or even higher than the source neutrino flux. These ultrahigh-energy neutrinos can be detected by ultimate detectors such as the Giant Radio Askaryan Neutrino Detector and Probe Of Extreme Multi-Messenger Astrophysics.

## Key claim
If engine-driven supernovae occur at a local rate of approximately 1% of the core-collapse supernova rate, they can explain the observed ultrahigh-energy cosmic ray (UHECR) spectrum and composition, with the condition that the acceleration and survival of UHECR nuclei occur in the external reverse shock scenario rather than the forward shock scenario.

## Method
The authors modeled the reverse shock formed when engine-driven jets or transrelativistic ejecta collide with the circumburst medium, calculating the dynamics, magnetic field strengths, and photon fields at the shock crossing radius. Using composition models from Zhang et al. (2018), they computed the acceleration, survival, and escape of UHECR nuclei (including iron, silicon, oxygen, helium, and protons) by comparing acceleration time scales with dynamical, adiabatic, synchrotron, and photodisintegration cooling time scales, then calculated the resulting spectra of escaping cosmic rays and secondary neutrinos from photomeson production.

## Key numbers
- The maximum energy of UHECR nuclei can reach approximately 10^20 to 10^21 eV, with jet acceleration giving 7 x 10^20 eV for iron (Z=26) under model Jet-A parameters.
- The total cosmic-ray energy per event is approximately 6 x 10^50 erg, assuming a kinetic energy of 3 x 10^51 erg and an accelerated CR fraction of 1/5.
- The diffuse flux of source neutrinos is predicted to be 10^-11 to 10^-10 GeV cm^-2 s^-1 sr^-1 in the 0.1 to 1 EeV range.
- The local event rate of engine-driven supernovae is approximately 1% of the core-collapse supernova rate, corresponding to about 100 to 1000 Gpc^-3 yr^-1 for low-luminosity GRBs.
- The maximum escape energy for UHECR nuclei is approximately 10^18.3 eV, assuming acceleration efficiency eta = 8 for jets and eta = 1 for transrelativistic ejecta.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of UHECR acceleration in engine-driven supernovae by Murase (2008), Wang (2007), and Liu (2011), specifically revisiting the nucleus-survival problem first studied by those authors. The results demonstrate that the reverse shock scenario can explain UHECR data even with the composition constraints from the Pierre Auger Observatory, which shows heavier composition beyond the ankle at approximately 4 x 10^18 eV. The predicted neutrino fluxes are within reach of future detectors such as the Giant Radio Askaryan Neutrino Detector and Probe Of Extreme Multi-Messenger Astrophysics, enabling multi-messenger identification of UHECR sources.
