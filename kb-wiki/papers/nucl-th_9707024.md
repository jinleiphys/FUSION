# A Note on the Toda Criterion for Interacting Dipole-Quadrupole Vibrations

## Key claim

The Toda criterion of Gaussian curvature provides an analytical estimate of the transition energy from regular to chaotic motion in a schematic model of coupled dipole and quadrupole nuclear vibrations, but the criterion characterizes only local instability and may give only a signature of global properties, requiring Poincaré sections for confirmation.

## Method

The authors apply the Toda criterion, which evaluates the sign of the Gaussian curvature of the potential-energy surface, to a Hamiltonian describing the coupling between the giant-dipole resonance (GDR) and quadrupole deformation of the nuclear surface, originally introduced by Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The critical energy for the onset of chaos is identified as the minimum energy on the line of zero Gaussian curvature of the potential-energy surface. Classical trajectories are then computed using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with first-order interpolation to construct Poincaré sections and verify the predicted order-chaos transition.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy for the onset of chaos is \(E_c = C_1^2 C_2 / (16 k_1^2)\), which equals exactly half the dissociation energy: \(E_c = E_D/2\).
- With the coupling parameter estimated as \(k_1 = (5/2)\sqrt{2/(2\pi)} C_1\), the critical energy becomes \(E_c = (\pi/150) C_2\).
- Using the charged liquid-drop approximation for the stiffness coefficient \(C_2\), the critical energy is expressed as \(E_c = \left( (b_s/\pi) A^{2/3} - (3/(10\pi)) (Z^2 e^2)/(r_0 A^{1/3}) \right) (\pi/150)\), with \(b_s \simeq 17\) MeV and \(r_0 \simeq 1.2\) fm.
- The liquid-drop approximation yields negative \(C_2\) values for \(Z^2/A > 49\).
- The experimental range of \(E_c\) is approximately 0.5 to 5 MeV depending on the nucleus.
- In the numerical example with \(B_1 = B_2 = 1\), \(C_1 = 100\), and \(C_2 = 1\), the critical energy is \(E_c = \pi/150 \simeq 0.021\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach of Toda (1974) and Benettin, Brambilla, and Galgani (1977) for predicting order-chaos transitions to a nuclear physics context, specifically the Bohr-Mottelson model of coupled dipole-quadrupole vibrations. The paper builds on earlier numerical studies of nonlinear effects in nuclear physics by Manfredi and Salasnich (1995) and Ormand, Borromeo, Broglia, Lazzari, Dossing, and Lauritzen (1989). The results enable analytical estimation of the onset energy for chaotic motion in deformed nuclei, which can be compared with experimental data using measured quadrupole frequencies and B(E2) transition probabilities.
