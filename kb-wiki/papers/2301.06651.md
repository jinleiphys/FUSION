# Bounds on transport from hydrodynamic stability

> It was recently shown that the dispersion relations describing singularities of retarded two-point functions in causal quantum field theories always satisfy the fundamental inequality $\mathfrak{Im} \, ω\leq |\mathfrak{Im} \, k|$, and that several rigorous bounds on transport coefficients follow directly from such inequality. Here, we prove that the same inequality, $\mathfrak{Im} \, ω\leq |\mathfrak{Im} \, k|$, is a necessary condition for a fluid theory to be covariantly stable (i.e. stable in all frames of reference). Hence, the same bounds on transport that follow from causality in quantum field theory also emerge as stability conditions within relativistic hydrodynamics. This intimate connection between bounds from causality and bounds from stability stems from the fact that covariant stability is possible only in the presence of causality. As a quick application, we show that fluids with luminal speed of sound have vanishing viscosities.

## Key claim

The inequality Im ω ≤ |Im k|, which was previously shown to follow from causality in quantum field theory and to imply bounds on transport coefficients, is actually a necessary condition for covariant stability (stability in all reference frames) in relativistic hydrodynamics, rather than a direct consequence of causality alone.

## Method

The author proves Theorem 1: if a linearised relativistic hydrodynamic theory is stable in all reference frames, then any plane-wave solution Ψ(t,x) = Ψ₀ e^(i(kx−ωt)) must satisfy Im ω ≤ |Im k|. The proof proceeds by contradiction: assuming Im ω > |Im k|, one performs a Lorentz boost with velocity v = Im k / Im ω (which is less than 1), transforming the mode into a Fourier mode (real k') with positive imaginary frequency, which constitutes an instability in the boosted frame. The author also proves Theorem 2: if a theory is stable in one reference frame and causal, then the same inequality follows, using a construction where initial data are truncated and causality forces the solution to match the original plane wave along null lines.

## Key numbers

* For the Cattaneo model, the diffusion coefficient D is bounded by D ≤ 1/(2K), where K = w/(2D) is the radius of convergence of the McLaurin series of ω(k), and w is the characteristic speed with w² ≤ 1.
* For a sound mode with expansion ω(k) = k − iDk² + O(k³), the coefficient D is related to viscosities by D = (ζ + 4η/3) / [2(ρ+P)], where ζ and η are bulk and shear viscosity coefficients, and ρ+P is the enthalpy density.
* The Israel-Stewart theory for bulk viscosity has characteristic velocity c_ch² = c_s² + ζ/[(ρ+P)τ], where causality requires c_ch² ≤ 1 and stability requires c_s², (ρ+P), (τ/ζ) > 0.

## Context

This work extends the results of Heller, Serantes, Spalinski, and Withers (2022), who derived the inequality Im ω ≤ |Im k| from causality in quantum field theory and used it to prove bounds on transport coefficients including the speed of sound and diffusion coefficient. The present paper shows these same bounds emerge as stability conditions within relativistic hydrodynamics, clarifying that the bound c_s ≤ 1 is a condition for covariant stability rather than for causality, as previously debated by Fox and Kuper (1970) and Caporaso (1979). The connection between causality and covariant stability was previously discussed by Gavassino (2021). This enables a geometric understanding of covariant stability: all complex exponential solutions must have Im p^μ not timelike future directed.
