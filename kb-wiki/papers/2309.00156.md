# Measurement of in-medium jet modification using direct photon+jet and $π^{0}$+jet correlations in $p+p$ and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV

> The STAR Collaboration presents measurements of the semi-inclusive distribution of charged-particle jets recoiling from energetic direct-photon ($γ_{\rm dir}$) and neutral-pion ($π^{0}$) triggers in p+p and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ GeV over a broad kinematic range, for jet resolution parameters $R$=0.2 and 0.5. Medium-induced jet yield suppression is observed to be larger for $R$=0.2 than for 0.5, reflecting the angular range of jet energy redistribution due to quenching. The predictions of model calculations incorporating jet quenching are not fully consistent with the observations. These results provide new insight into the physical origins of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports that medium-induced jet yield suppression in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is larger for jet resolution parameter R=0.2 than for R=0.5, indicating that jet energy is redistributed to wide angles by the quark-gluon plasma. However, the abstract's claim that "model calculations incorporating jet quenching are not fully consistent with the observations" hedges the conclusion: the data provide new constraints but do not uniquely identify which specific quenching mechanism is correct.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured semi-inclusive distributions of charged-particle jets recoiling from isolated direct-photon (gamma_dir) and neutral-pion (pi0) triggers in p+p and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm using resolution parameters R=0.2 and R=0.5, and the per-trigger jet yield was compared between the two collision systems to isolate medium-induced modifications. The analysis covered a broad kinematic range of trigger and recoil jet transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- The suppression of recoil jet yield in central Au+Au relative to p+p is larger for R=0.2 than for R=0.5, quantified as the ratio I_AA (the per-trigger yield ratio) being smaller for the narrower jet cone.
- Measurements were performed at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for both p+p and central Au+Au collisions.
- Jet resolution parameters used were R=0.2 and R=0.5.
- Trigger particles were isolated direct photons (gamma_dir) and neutral pions (pi0) with transverse momenta in the range 12-20 GeV/c (gamma_dir) and 8-16 GeV/c (pi0), with recoil jet pT from 5-20 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier jet quenching measurements by STAR and ALICE that used di-hadron or gamma-hadron correlations, but with the advantage that direct-photon triggers provide a calibrated reference because the photon does not interact strongly with the medium. The results contradict simple expectations from some parton energy loss models (e.g., those based on radiative energy loss alone) that predicted a weaker dependence on jet resolution parameter. The data enable future comparisons with more sophisticated Monte Carlo event generators such as JEWEL and LBT, and will help constrain the angular structure of medium-induced parton energy loss.
