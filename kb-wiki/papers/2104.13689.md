# Investigating neutron transfer in the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system

> In this work $\textit{n}$-transfer and incomplete fusion cross sections for $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system are reported over a wide energy range, E$_{c.m.}$ $\approx$ 29-45 MeV. The experiment was carried out using activation technique and off-line gamma counting. The transfer process is found to be the dominant mode as compared to all other reaction channels. Detailed coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations have been performed for $\textit{n}$-transfer stripping and pickup cross sections. The measured 1$\textit{n}$-stripping cross sections are explained with CRC calculations by including the ground state and the 2$^{+}$ resonance state (E = 3.03 MeV) of $^{8}$Be. The calculations for 1$\textit{n}$-pickup, including only the ground state of $^{10}$Be agree reasonably well with the measured cross sections, while it overpredicts the data at subbarrier energies. For a better insight into the role of projectile structure in the transfer process, a comprehensive analysis of 1$\textit{n}$-stripping reaction has been carried out for various weakly bound projectiles on $^{197}$Au target nucleus. The transfer cross sections scaled with the square of total radius of interacting nuclei show the expected Q-value dependence of 1$\textit{n}$-stripping channel for weakly bound stable projectiles.

## Key claim

The paper reports that neutron transfer is the dominant reaction mode compared to complete fusion and incomplete fusion for the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system at subbarrier energies, but the abstract's claim of dominance is qualified by the energy range: transfer dominates only at subbarrier energies, while complete fusion shows higher cross sections at above-barrier energies.

## Method

The experiment used the activation technique with off-line gamma counting: a $^{9}$Be beam from the BARC-TIFR Pelletron Linac Facility bombarded self-supporting $^{197}$Au targets (thickness approximately 1.3-1.7 mg/cm$^2$) with aluminum catcher foils (approximately 1.5 mg/cm$^2$) over beam energies of 30-47 MeV. Reaction products were identified by characteristic gamma rays and confirmed by half-life measurements using efficiency-calibrated HPGe detectors. Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations using the FRESCO code were performed for 1n-stripping and 1n-pickup channels, including spectroscopic factors and excited states of the residual nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Transfer cross sections measured over E$_{c.m.}$ approximately 29-45 MeV, corresponding to 0.76 $\leq$ E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$ $\leq$ 1.16, where V$_B$ = 38.4 MeV
- 1n-stripping ($^{198}$Au) cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 29.1 MeV: 11.2 $\pm$ 0.1 mb
- 1n-stripping ($^{198}$Au) cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV: 136 $\pm$ 1 mb
- 1n-pickup ($^{196}$Au) cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV: 9.1 $\pm$ 0.4 mb
- ICF product $^{200}$Tl cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV: 119.1 $\pm$ 1.2 mb
- ICF product $^{199}$Tl cross section at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV: 75.4 $\pm$ 5.1 mb
- Spectroscopic factor for $^{9}$Be/$^{8}$Be in 1n-stripping: 0.42 for ground state, 1.0 for 2$^+$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be at E = 3.03 MeV
- Spectroscopic factor for $^{9}$Be/$^{10}$Be in 1n-pickup: 1.58
- Q-value for 1n-stripping in $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au: 4.85 MeV
- Q-value for 1n-pickup in $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au: -1.26 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of fusion suppression in the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system by Kaushik et al. (2020), which found approximately 39% suppression of complete fusion relative to CCFULL calculations at above-barrier energies. The results are compared with transfer systematics for other weakly bound projectiles on $^{197}$Au, including $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li data from Chatterjee et al. (2009) and $^{10}$B data from Mukherjee et al. (2006). The finding that the 2$^+$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be at 3.03 MeV is crucial for reproducing 1n-stripping cross sections provides insight into the role of projectile cluster structure in transfer reactions, enabling future systematic studies of Q-value dependence in neutron transfer for weakly bound projectiles.
