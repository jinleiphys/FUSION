# Self Consistent Random Phase Approximation and the restoration of symmetries within the three-level Lipkin model

> We show that it is possible to restore the symmetry associated with the Goldstone mode within the Self Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA) applied to the three-level Lipkin model. We determine one and two-body densities as very convergent expansions in terms of the generators of the RPA basis. We show that SCRPA excitations correspond to the heads of some rotational bands in the exact spectrum. It turns out that the SCRPA eigenmodes for N=2 coincide with exact solutions, given by the diagonalisation procedure.

# Self Consistent Random Phase Approximation and the restoration of symmetries within the three-level Lipkin model

## Key claim
The Self Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA) can restore the symmetry associated with the Goldstone mode in the three-level Lipkin model, maintaining the symmetry restoration properties of standard RPA while improving accuracy; however, this restoration depends on the inclusion of "scattering" terms (particle-particle and hole-hole configurations) that are absent in standard RPA.

## Method
The authors applied the SCRPA formalism to the three-level Lipkin model (SU(3) algebra) with a Hamiltonian containing one-body energies and a two-body interaction term. They solved the SCRPA equations iteratively by coupling generalized mean field equations with RPA fluctuations, computing one and two-body correlated densities using an expansion in terms of RPA phonon operators, and compared results with exact diagonalization and renormalized RPA (r-RPA). For the two-particle case (N=2), they derived analytic expressions for the SCRPA vacuum state and solved the resulting nonlinear system of equations using the Newton procedure.

## Key numbers
- For N=2 with parameters χ=5, ε₀=0 MeV, ε₁=1 MeV, ε₂=2 MeV: SCRPA ground state energy E₀ = -5.773794 MeV, excitation energies ω₁ = 6.773794 MeV and ω₂ = 7.773794 MeV, exactly matching the exact diagonalization results.
- For N=10 in the deformed region (χ>1) with degenerate upper levels (ε₁=ε₂): standard RPA gives ω₂=0 (Goldstone mode) and ω₁=ε√(2(χ²-1)).
- The spherical region for N=10 or N=20 is extended by approximately a factor of two compared to standard Hartree-Fock due to selfconsistent coupling of quantal fluctuations to the mean field.
- For N=20 with ε₀=0, ε₁=1, ε₂=2: SCRPA first and second excited states are excellently reproduced up to χ≈1.2.

## Context
This work extends the SCRPA formalism previously developed by Dukelsky (1998) and others, applying it specifically to test symmetry restoration properties in the three-level Lipkin model originally introduced by Lipkin (1970) and Messiah (1971). The model was previously investigated within the multistep variational approach by Sambataro (1999) and the r-RPA formalism by Graw (2002), and was shown by Hagino (2000) to develop a continuously broken symmetry when the two upper levels become degenerate. The paper demonstrates that SCRPA, unlike simpler extensions of RPA, can reproduce the Goldstone mode at zero energy beyond a critical coupling, thus restoring the original Hamiltonian invariance, and for the two-particle case SCRPA reproduces exact results. This enables future applications of SCRPA to more realistic many-body systems where symmetry restoration and conservation laws are important.
