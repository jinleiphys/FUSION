# Hadron-Quark phase transition in the context of GW190814

> The properties of the neutron stars are calculated for the hadronic matter within the density-dependent relativistic mean-field model (DD-RMF). The phase transition to the quark matter is studied and the hybrid star matter properties are systematically calculated using the Vector-Enhanced Bag model (vBag). The maximum mass of neutron star with DD-LZ1 and DD-RMF parameter sets is found to be around 2.55$M_{\odot}$ for pure hadronic phase and around 2$M_{\odot}$ for hadron-quark mixed phase using both Gibbs and Maxwell construction. The tidal deformability for the hybrid EoS at 1.4$M_{\odot}$, $Λ_{1.4}$, remains unchanged from the pure hadronic EoS with Maxwell construction, but decreases with the increasing neutron star mass for Gibbs construction. While the pure hadron matter EoS satisfies the mass constraint from recently observed GW190814 data, implying a stiff neutron star EoS, the hadron-quark phase transition satisfies the constraints from the recent observations GW170817. Therefore, we cannot exclude the possibility of the secondary object in GW190814 as a neutron star with a phase transition to the quark matter that satisfies the 2$M_{\odot}$ maximum mass limit.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the secondary object in GW190814 (mass 2.50–2.67 M⊙) could be a hybrid neutron star containing a hadron-quark mixed phase, provided the pure hadronic equation of state is stiff enough to reach ≈2.55 M⊙ and the phase transition softens the star enough to satisfy the 2 M⊙ maximum-mass limit from GW170817. The authors do not claim the secondary object definitely is a neutron star; they only state that such a possibility cannot be excluded.

## Method
The authors use the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model with several parameter sets (DD-LZ1, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD-MEX, DDV) to describe hadronic matter. For quark matter they employ the Vector-Enhanced Bag model (vBag), which includes dynamic chiral symmetry breaking and repulsive vector interactions. The hadron-quark phase transition is constructed using both Gibbs construction (global charge neutrality) and Maxwell construction (local charge neutrality). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability for each equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic neutron star with DD-LZ1 and DD-RMF parameter sets: around 2.55 M⊙.
- Maximum mass of hybrid star (hadron-quark mixed phase) using both Gibbs and Maxwell construction: around 2 M⊙.
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 M⊙ (Λ₁.₄) for hybrid equation of state: unchanged from pure hadronic case with Maxwell construction; decreases with increasing neutron star mass for Gibbs construction.
- Symmetry energy parameter J for the parameter sets: compatible with J = 31.6 ± 2.66 MeV.
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L: satisfies L = 59.57 ± 10.06 MeV.
- Incompressibility K₀ for all parameter sets except DD-MEX: within 240 ± 20 MeV; DD-MEX predicts a higher value.

## Context
This work extends studies of hybrid stars using the vBag model (Klaehn and Fischer, 2015) and DD-RMF parameter sets such as DD-ME1 (Nikšić et al., 2002) and DD-ME2 (Nikšić et al., 2005). It directly addresses the nature of the secondary component in GW190814 (Abbott et al., 2020), which has a mass of 2.50–2.67 M⊙ and lies in the mass-gap region. The results show that a stiff hadronic equation of state (e.g., DD-LZ1) can satisfy the GW190814 mass constraint, while the phase transition to quark matter brings the maximum mass down to the 2 M⊙ limit required by GW170817 and pulsar mass measurements (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013; Cromartie et al., 2020). This enables future work to test whether the secondary object in GW190814 could be a hybrid star rather than a light black hole.
