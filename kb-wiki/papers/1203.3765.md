# Computational aspects of nuclear coupled-cluster theory

> We discuss computational aspects of the spherical coupled-cluster method specific to the nuclear many-body problem. Using chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N3LO) with cutoff Lambda = 500MeV, we present coupled-cluster results for the ground state of 40Ca. Scaling and performance studies are presented together with challenges we meet with when extending the coupled-cluster effort to nuclei mass hundred and beyond.

## Key claim

The spherical (j-coupled) coupled-cluster method dramatically reduces the number of nonlinear equations compared to the m-scheme, but the paper's convergence results show that earlier calculations using only relative partial waves up to J_rel-max = 4 underestimated the binding energy of 40Ca by approximately 15 MeV, and that J_rel-max = 8–10 is needed for convergence.

## Method

The authors implemented the coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (CCSD) equations in a coupled-angular-momentum (j-coupled) scheme, using the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian with a Hartree-Fock reference state expressed in a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis. The nucleon-nucleon interaction was the chiral N3LO potential (cutoff Λ = 500 MeV) transformed from relative to laboratory coordinates via the Brody-Moshinsky transformation. The ground-state energy of 40Ca was computed with the Λ-CCSD(T) approximation, and strong-scaling performance was measured on the Jaguar XK6 system (without GPUs) using MPI plus OpenMP threading.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 40Ca: convergence to within 1 MeV requires N = 16 harmonic-oscillator shells (N = 2n + l) and relative partial waves up to J_rel-max = 8–10 in the Brody-Moshinsky transformation.
- In 15 major oscillator shells for 40Ca, the m-scheme has 2720 orbitals and ~10^10 nonlinear CCSD equations; the j-coupled scheme has 240 orbitals and ~10^6 equations.
- For 40Ca in 15 major shells, the two-body interaction alone would require about 600 TB of memory and the T2 amplitudes would require 100 GB of memory in the m-scheme.
- In 20 oscillator shells, the two-body interaction matrix elements require about 100 GB of memory.
- Strong-scaling tests for 40Ca in model spaces N = 12, 14, 16, and 18 used up to 8192 cores for N = 18, with 4 MPI processes each spawning 4 threads (total 32768 MPI processes).
- At small processor counts, the triples calculation consumed roughly 90% of total runtime; at larger processor counts it dropped to less than 50%.

## Context

This work extends the spherical coupled-cluster approach previously developed by Hagen et al. (2008, 2010) for closed-shell nuclei, correcting earlier underestimates of binding energy by including higher partial waves in the Brody-Moshinsky transformation. The paper addresses computational bottlenecks for scaling to nuclei beyond mass 100, such as tin isotopes, where 25–30 oscillator shells will be required. The performance improvements in the triples calculation using OpenMP threading prepare the NUCCOR-CCSD(T) code for the hybrid CPU-GPU architecture of the Titan supercomputer, and enable future inclusion of continuum effects and three-body forces.
