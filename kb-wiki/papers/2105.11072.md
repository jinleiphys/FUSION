# Complex Langevin study for polarons in an attractively interacting one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas

> We investigate a polaronic excitation in a one-dimensional spin-1/2 Fermi gas with contact attractive interactions, using the complex Langevin method, which is a promising approach to evade a possible sign problem in quantum Monte Carlo simulations. We found that the complex Langevin method works correctly in a wide range of temperature, interaction strength, and population imbalance. The Fermi polaron energy extracted from the two-point imaginary Green's function is not sensitive to the temperature and the impurity concentration in the parameter region we considered. Our results show a good agreement with the solution of the thermodynamic Bethe ansatz at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The complex Langevin method works correctly for the one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model with contact attractive interactions across a wide range of temperature, interaction strength, and population imbalance, and the extracted Fermi polaron energy shows good agreement with the zero-temperature thermodynamic Bethe ansatz solution despite being computed at finite temperature and finite impurity concentration.

## Method

The authors performed complex Langevin simulations of the one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model on lattices with temporal sizes \(N_\tau = 40\) and \(80\) and spatial size \(N_x = 60\), with anisotropy \(r = 0.1\). They fixed the dimensionless coupling constant \(\lambda = \sqrt{\beta} g = 2\) and swept the average chemical potential \(\beta\mu\) and Zeeman field \(\beta h\) over ranges that produced varying temperature \(T/T_\text{F}\), interaction strength \(1/p_\text{F}a\), and population imbalance \(n_\downarrow/n_\uparrow\). The polaron energy was extracted from the zero-momentum two-point imaginary-time Green's function by taking the ratio \(R(0,\tau)\) at the largest available imaginary time separation.

## Key numbers

* Polaron energy \(U/T_\text{F}\) computed for \(N_\tau = 40\) and \(80\) lattices, with lowest temperatures \(T/T_\text{F} \simeq 0.08\) (\(N_\tau = 40\)) and \(T/T_\text{F} \simeq 0.07\) (\(N_\tau = 80\)).
* Ratio of particle numbers \(n_\downarrow/n_\uparrow\) ranged from \(1.0 \times 10^{-5}\) to \(1.5 \times 10^{-1}\) for \(N_\tau = 40\) and from \(8.4 \times 10^{-8}\) to \(1.0 \times 10^{-1}\) for \(N_\tau = 80\).
* The dimensionless coupling constant was fixed at \(\lambda = \sqrt{\beta} g = 2\).
* Simulations used 5001 samples per parameter set, with Langevin step-size \(\Delta t = 0.01\) and configuration saving at 0.02 intervals.

## Context

This work extends the application of the complex Langevin method to the one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model (Yang 1967, Gaudin 1967) with population imbalance, where conventional quantum Monte Carlo suffers from a sign problem. The results are compared to the exact zero-temperature thermodynamic Bethe ansatz solution (Yang 1967) and complement previous theoretical studies using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (PhysRevA.84.033607), T-matrix (PhysRevLett.111.025302), and variational approaches (Song 2019, Mistakidis 2019). The method enables future studies of the transition from degenerate Fermi-polaron to classical Boltzmann-gas regimes and inhomogeneous pairing phases in higher dimensions.
