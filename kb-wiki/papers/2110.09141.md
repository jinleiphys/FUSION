# Long-range neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms

> Understanding the origin of lepton number violation is one of the fundamental questions in particle physics today. Neutrinoless double beta decay provides a way in which this violation can be tested. In this article, we derive the form of hadronic and leptonic matrix elements for all possible long-range mechanisms of neutrinoless double beta decay. With these, we calculate the numerical values of the nuclear matrix elements (NME) and phase space factors (PSF) by making use of the interacting boson model of the nucleus (IBM-2) for NMEs and of exact Dirac wave functions for the PSFs. We show that:\ (I) lepton number violation can occur even with zero (or very small) neutrino mass and (II) the angular correlations of the emitted electrons can distinguish between different models of non-standard (NS) mechanisms. We set limits on the coupling constants of some NS models, in particular Left-Right models and SUSY models.

## Key claim

Lepton number violation in neutrinoless double beta decay can occur even with zero (or very small) neutrino mass through long-range non-standard mechanisms, and the angular correlations of the emitted electrons can distinguish between different non-standard models such as Left-Right models and SUSY models.

## Method

The authors derived the form of hadronic and leptonic matrix elements for all possible long-range mechanisms of neutrinoless double beta decay by starting from a general effective Lagrangian containing currents with definite tensor structure and chirality (V±A, S±P, T±T5). They calculated numerical values of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) using the interacting boson model (IBM-2) and phase space factors (PSFs) using exact Dirac wave functions, then set limits on coupling constants for specific non-standard models including Left-Right models and R-parity-violating SUSY models.

## Key numbers

- Cosmological limit on sum of neutrino masses: Σ m_i < 0.23 eV (Planck Collaboration)
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.269 (from neutron decays)
- Pseudoscalar form factor coupling: g_P = 349 ± 9
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling: g_P' = 182 × g_A
- Tensor form factor values used: g_T1 = 1, g_T2 = -3.30, g_T3 = 1.34
- Vector form factor mass: m_V = 0.84 GeV
- Axial-vector form factor mass: m_A = 1.09 GeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 0.138 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of short-range non-standard mechanisms by Graf et al. (2018, 2020) to all possible long-range mechanisms, providing explicit formulas for NMEs and PSFs. It builds on the general framework for non-standard models developed by Doi et al. (1981), Tomoda (1990), Ali et al. (2007), and Cirigliano et al. (2017), and provides numerical values using the IBM-2 approach of Barea et al. (2009-2015) and exact Dirac wave functions of Kotila (2012). The results enable experimental searches to distinguish between different non-standard mechanisms through electron angular correlation measurements.
