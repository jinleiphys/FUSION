# Axial anomaly and magnetism of nuclear and quark matter

> We consider the response of the QCD ground state at finite baryon density to a strong magnetic field B. We point out the dominant role played by the coupling of neutral Goldstone bosons, such as pi^0, to the magnetic field via the axial triangle anomaly. We show that, in vacuum, above a value of B ~ m_pi^2/e, a metastable object appears - the pi^0 domain wall. Because of the axial anomaly, the wall carries a baryon number surface density proportional to B. As a result, for B ~ 10^{19} G a stack of parallel pi^0 domain walls is energetically more favorable than nuclear matter at the same density. Similarly, at higher densities, somewhat weaker magnetic fields of order B ~ 10^{17}-10^{18} G transform the color-superconducting ground state of QCD into new phases containing stacks of axial isoscalar (eta or eta') domain walls. We also show that a quark-matter state known as ``Goldstone current state,'' in which a gradient of a Goldstone field is spontaneously generated, is ferromagnetic due to the axial anomaly. We estimate the size of the fields created by such a state in a typical neutron star to be of order 10^{14}-10^{15} G.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the axial anomaly causes neutral Goldstone bosons (π⁰, η, η′) to couple to magnetic fields, leading to the formation of domain walls that carry baryon number, a mechanism that can restructure nuclear and quark matter at magnetic fields parametrically lower than the QCD scale (∼10²⁰ G), and which vanishes in the chiral limit (m_π → 0).

## Method

The authors use the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) effective action to describe the anomalous coupling of neutral pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons to electromagnetism and baryon current in QCD. They analyze the stability of π⁰ domain walls in a magnetic field by solving the linearized field equations for fluctuations, finding the critical field B₀ where the lowest Landau level stabilizes the wall. They then compute the energy per baryon of a stack of such walls and compare it to nuclear matter at finite chemical potential μ, and extend the analysis to color-superconducting phases (2SC and CFL) using analogous effective Lagrangians for the η and η′ bosons.

## Key numbers

- Critical field for local stability of π⁰ domain wall: B₀ = 3 m_π²/e ≈ 1.0 × 10¹⁹ G
- Critical field for π⁰ domain walls to be energetically favored over nuclear matter: B₁ = 16π f_π² m_π/(e m_N) ≈ 1.1 × 10¹⁹ G
- Baryon number surface density of a π⁰ domain wall: N_B/S = eB/(2π)
- Energy per baryon of a π⁰ domain wall: E/N_B = 16π f_π² m_π/(eB)
- Critical magnetic field for η domain walls in 2SC phase: B_c = (4/√3π) μ m_η/e ≈ 1.2 × 10¹⁸ G × (μ/1 GeV) × (m_η/10 MeV)
- Critical magnetic field for η′ domain walls in CFL phase: B_c′ = 1.0 × 10¹⁷ G × (μ/1.5 GeV) × (m_η′/2 MeV) = 8.3 × 10¹⁶ G × (Δ/30 MeV) × (√(m_u m_d)/5 MeV)
- Estimated magnetic field from Goldstone current state in a neutron star: 10¹⁴–10¹⁵ G

## Context

This work extends prior studies of QCD in strong magnetic fields (Shushpanov 1997, Kabat 2002, Miransky 2002, Cohen 2007) by identifying a new mechanism, anomalous coupling of Goldstone bosons, that operates at fields an order of magnitude lower than the QCD scale. It also connects to studies of color-superconducting matter in magnetic fields (Alford 1999, Ferrer 2005–2007, Fukushima 2007, Noronha 2007) and to discussions of ferromagnetism in dense matter (Tatsumi 2000, Isayev 2003, Inui 2007, Ferrer 2007). The results enable predictions for the structure of neutron star cores and magnetar magnetic fields, and suggest that the Goldstone current state in CFLK⁰ phase could generate fields of order 10¹⁴–10¹⁵ G.
