# Magnetic Oscillations in the Nambu - Jona-Lasinio model

> The phase structure of a simple Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model has been investigated at non-zero values of $μ$ and H, where H is an external magnetic field and $μ$ is the chemical potential. On this basis magnetic oscillations effects were considered. It was shown that there are standard (periodic) van Alphen-de Haas magnetic oscillations of some thermodynamical quantities, including magnetization, pressure and particle density in the NJL system. Besides, we have found non-standard, i.e. non-periodic, magnetic oscillations, since the frequency of oscillations is a H-dependent quantity. Finally, there arises an oscillating behaviour not only for thermodynamical quantities, but also for a dynamical quantity like the quark mass. The possibility for magnetic oscillations of some physical quantities inside neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, magnetic oscillations of thermodynamic quantities (magnetization, pressure, particle density) and of the dynamical quark mass occur, and that these oscillations can be either periodic (standard van Alphen-de Haas type) or non-periodic, depending on the chemical potential regime; the non-periodic case arises because the oscillation frequency becomes dependent on the magnetic field itself.

## Method

The authors study the phase structure of the NJL model with N fermion flavors in the leading order of the large-N expansion, computing the effective potential \(V_{H\mu}(\Sigma)\) as a function of the auxiliary scalar field \(\Sigma\) (which equals the dynamical quark mass at the global minimum) in the presence of a chemical potential \(\mu\) and an external magnetic field \(H\). They analyze the stationarity equation to map the phase diagram in the \((\mu, \sqrt{eH})\) plane, identifying regions of chiral symmetry breaking and restoration, and then apply the Poisson summation formula to extract the oscillating part of the thermodynamic potential, from which oscillations of derived quantities are obtained.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking at \(\mu=0, H=0\) is \(G_c = 4\pi^2 / \Lambda^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet cutoff.
* For \(G_c < G < 1.225\, G_c\), the phase diagram shows infinite sets of massless phases \(A_k\) and massive phases \(C_k\) with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.
* The magnetic field values at the tricritical points \(t_0\) and \(s_0\) (for \(G/G_c = 1.01, 1.1, 1.2\)) are: \(eH_{t_0}/\Lambda^2 = 0.01..., 0.08..., 0.13...\) and \(eH_{s_0}/\Lambda^2 = 0.006..., 0.056..., 0.103...\).
* Using \(\Lambda = 700\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the magnetic field at the tricritical point \(s_0\) varies in the interval \(10^{17}\ \mathrm{G}\) to \(10^{18}\ \mathrm{G}\) for \(1.01 < G/G_c < 1.2\).
* The frequency of periodic oscillations (for \(\mu > M_1\)) over the variable \((eH)^{-1}\) is \(\mu^2/2\).
* The frequency of non-periodic oscillations (for \(M < \mu < M_1\)) is \((\mu^2 - \Sigma^2(\mu,H))/(2eH)\), which depends on \(H\) through the quark mass \(\Sigma(\mu,H)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic oscillations (the van Alphen-de Haas effect) from condensed matter physics and QED (as studied by Peres-Rodrigues and others) to a relativistic quantum field theory with four-fermion interactions, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. It builds on prior analyses of the NJL model's vacuum structure in external magnetic fields (Klevansky, Ebert, Gusynin) and at finite chemical potential (Kawati, Klimenko), but is the first to consider the combined influence of both \(\mu\) and \(H\) on the phase structure. The results enable predictions for non-periodic oscillatory behavior of thermodynamic and dynamical quantities in strongly correlated fermionic systems, with potential applications to neutron star interiors where magnetic fields can reach \(10^{12}\ \mathrm{G}\) at the surface and up to \(10^{18}\ \mathrm{G}\) inside, as noted by Shapiro.
