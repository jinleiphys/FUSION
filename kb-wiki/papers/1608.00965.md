# Event simulation based on three-fluid hydrodynamics for collisions at energies available at the Dubna Nuclotron-based Ion Collider Facility and at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research in Darmstadt

> We present a new event generator based on the three-fluid hydrodynamics approach for the early stage of the collision, followed by a particlization at the hydrodynamic decoupling surface to join to a microscopic transport model, UrQMD, to account for hadronic final state interactions. We present first results for nuclear collisions of the FAIR/NICA energy scan program (Au+Au collisions, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4-11$ GeV). We address the directed flow of protons and pions as well as the proton rapidity distribution for two model EoS, one with a first order phase transition the other with a crossover type softening at high densities. The new simulation program has the unique feature that it can describe a hadron-to-quark matter transition which proceeds in the baryon stopping regime that is not accessible to previous simulation programs designed for higher energies.

## Key claim

The new event generator THESEUS can describe a hadron-to-quark matter transition that proceeds in the baryon stopping regime, which is not accessible to previous simulation programs designed for higher energies, but the present version lacks event-by-event hydrodynamic evolution and treats only average hydrodynamic evolution with Monte Carlo sampling of final hadrons.

## Method

The three-fluid hydrodynamics (3FH) model treats the collision from the stage of cold nuclei up to particlization, using counter-streaming baryon-rich fluids for projectile and target nuclei plus a fireball fluid for newly produced particles, each governed by conventional hydrodynamic equations with friction forces fitted to reproduce stopping power. At the hydrodynamic decoupling surface (freeze-out energy density 0.4 GeV/fm³), a Monte Carlo procedure samples hadron distributions from the fluid droplets according to a grand canonical ensemble with temperature, baryon chemical potential, and strange chemical potential. The sampled particles are then fed into the UrQMD microscopic transport model to simulate hadronic final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies √s_NN = 4-11 GeV (equivalent to E_lab = 8-70 A GeV)
- Initial baryon density n_B = 0.15 fm⁻³ and energy density m_N n_B ≈ 0.14 GeV/fm³ for cold nuclei
- Freeze-out energy density ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³
- Impact parameters used: b = 2 fm (central), b = 6 fm (semicentral), b = 11 fm (peripheral)
- Reliable determination of curvature C_y requires not less than 10⁴ events for central and semi-central collisions and 10⁵ events for peripheral collisions
- Transverse momentum spectra shown up to p_T = 2.2 GeV for pions and kaons at E_lab = 30 A GeV

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid hydrodynamics approach of Ivanov (2005) by adding a particlization procedure and the UrQMD afterburner from Bass (1998), creating the THESEUS event generator for the NICA/FAIR energy range. The new tool enables direct comparison with experimental data from the AGS E895 experiment, the STAR beam energy scan, and NA49, and allows study of the "wiggle" in the midrapidity curvature of the proton rapidity distribution as a possible signal for a first-order phase transition, as previously suggested by Ivanov (2015). The approach is complementary to higher-energy simulation programs like QGSM or PHSD that cannot access the baryon stopping regime.
