# Real-time Kadanoff-Baym approach to nuclear response functions

> Linear density response functions are calculated for symmetric nuclear matter of normal density by time-evolving two-time Green's functions in real time. Of particular interest is the effect of correlations. The system is therefore initially time-evolved with a collision term calculated in a direct Born approximation as well as with full (RPA) ring-summation until fully correlated. An external time-dependent potential is then applied. The ensuing density fluctuations are recorded to calculate the density response. This method was previously used by Kwong and Bonitz for studying plasma oscillations in a correlated electron gas. The energy-weighted sum-rule for the response function is guaranteed by using conserving self-energy insertions as the method then generates the full vertex-functions. These can alternatively be calculated by solving a Bethe -Salpeter equation as done in some previous works. The (first order) mean field is derived from a momentum-dependent (non-local) interaction while $2^{nd}$ order self-energies are calculated using a particle-hole two-body effective (or 'residual') interaction given by a gaussian \it local \rm potential. We present numerical results for the response function $S(ω,q_0)$ for $q_0=0.2,0.4$ and $0.8 {\rm fm}^{-1}$. Comparison is made with the nucleons being un-correlated i.e. with only the first order mean field included, the 'HF+RPA' approximation. We briefly discuss the relation of our work with the Landau quasi-particle theory as applied to nuclear systems by Sjöberg and followers using methods developped by Babu and Brown, with special emphasis on the 'induced' interaction.

# Real-Time Kadanoff-Baym Approach to Nuclear Response Functions

## Key claim

Linear density response functions for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density can be calculated by time-evolving two-time Green's functions in real time, with the energy-weighted sum-rule automatically satisfied when conserving self-energy approximations are used; however, the method requires an ad hoc strength factor \(a_{10}=0.3\) for the residual interaction in the linear-response channel to avoid divergences related to an overly large Landau parameter, and the results are exploratory rather than quantitative.

## Method

The authors time-evolve equilibrium Kadanoff-Baym two-time Green's functions for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density (\(\rho=0.13\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)) using three levels of approximation: (I) mean-field only (HF+RPA), (II) second-order Born self-energies from a Gaussian residual interaction, and (III) full ring-summation (RPA) in the self-energies. After the system equilibrates, an external time-dependent potential \(U(\mathbf{q},t)\) is applied, and the resulting density fluctuations \(\delta n(q_0,t)\) are Fourier-transformed to obtain the response function \(S(\omega,q_0)\). The mean field is derived from a momentum-dependent parametrization (Welke et al., 1988), while the residual interaction is a Gaussian potential \(V(r)=V_0 e^{-(r/\eta)^2}\) with \(\eta=0.57\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(V_0=-453\ \mathrm{MeV}\), scaled by a factor \(a_{10}\) in the linear-response channel.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho=0.13\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), corresponding to Fermi momentum \(k_F=1.25\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- External momentum transfers studied: \(q_0=0.2,\ 0.4,\ 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- Gaussian residual interaction parameters: range \(\eta=0.57\ \mathrm{fm}\), strength \(V_0=-453\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Mean-field parameters (Welke et al.): \(A=-110.44\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(B=140.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(C=-64.95\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\sigma=1.24\), \(\rho_0=0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(\Lambda=1.58p_F\).
- Equilibrium temperatures after correlations: \(T_f=5.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (second-order Born) and \(T_f=5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (full ring summation).
- Landau parameter \(F_0\) for \(a_{10}=0.3\): \(-0.495\) (compared to Sjöberg's value \(-0.373\) for the \(l=0\) state).
- Induced interaction strength for \(a_{10}=0.3\) and \(q=0.2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\): \(24\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\); direct interaction: \(-140\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3\).
- Energy-weighted sum-rule \(\int_0^\infty \omega S(\omega,q_0) d\omega = q_0^2/(2m^*)\) satisfied within numerical accuracy when using effective mass \(m^*=0.7m\).

## Context

This work extends the real-time Kadanoff-Baym method previously applied to the electron gas by Kwong and Bonitz (2000) to nuclear matter response functions, providing an alternative to solving Bethe-Salpeter equations as done by Bozek (2004, 2005) and to the Landau transport-equation approach of Lykasov et al. (2004). The results are compared with the Landau quasi-particle theory applied to nuclear systems by Sjöberg (1973) using methods developed by Babu and Brown (1973), with special emphasis on the induced interaction from ring-diagram screening. The approach enables future studies of neutrino mean free paths and absorption in neutron-rich matter, as well as systematic investigations of correlation effects on nuclear response at different densities and temperatures.
