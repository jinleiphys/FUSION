# Status of Dynamical Coupled-Channel Analysis by Collaboration@EBAC

> 5 pages, 4 figures. Contribution to Eleventh Conference on the Intersections of Particle and Nuclear Physics --- CIPANP 2012, May 28, 2012 - June 3, 2012, St. Petersburg, FL, USA

## Key claim

The Dynamical Coupled-Channels (DCC) analysis by the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) collaboration, based on an extension of the ANL-Osaka model, can determine nucleon resonance parameters from world data on meson production reactions, but the results presented are preliminary because a fully combined simultaneous analysis of all channels (including KΛ and KΣ production) had not been completed before the collaboration was dissolved in March 2012.

## Method

The DCC model solves coupled-channels integral equations for reaction amplitudes Tα,β(p,p';E) in each partial wave, with channels including γN, πN, ηN, KY, ωN, and ππN (with πΔ, ρN, σN resonant components). The potential Vα,β consists of meson-exchange interactions from phenomenological Lagrangians plus a sum over bare N* states with mass M* and vertex interactions ΓN*,β. The model was first fit to πN elastic scattering up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV (excluding KY and ωN channels during 2006-2010), then the electromagnetic parts were determined by fitting γp → π0p, π+n and p(e,e'π0,+)N data. An analytic continuation procedure to the complex energy plane was developed to extract resonance pole positions and residues.

## Key numbers

- The model fit πN elastic scattering data up to invariant mass W = 2 GeV
- The γp → π0p and γp → π+n data could only be fit up to invariant mass W = 1.6 GeV when parameters fixed from πN elastic scattering were not adjusted
- The electromagnetic vertex functions ΓN*,γN were determined by fitting p(e,e'π0)p and p(e,e'π+)n data up to W = 1.6 GeV and Q2 = 1.5 (GeV/c)2
- Predicted total cross sections for πN → ππN and γN → ππN were in excellent agreement with data in the near threshold region W ≤ 1.4 GeV
- Predicted γp → π+π-p and γp → π0π0p cross sections were a factor of about 2 larger than the data at higher W

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model for the Δ(1232) resonance developed by the ANL-Osaka collaboration (Lee and Sato, 1996) and its extension (Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee, 2007). The DCC analysis enables extraction of N* parameters from partial-wave amplitudes and investigation of their interpretation in terms of hadron models such as the Dyson-Schwinger-Equation model, constituent quark models, and Lattice QCD. The EBAC collaboration was dissolved in March 2012, and an ANL-Osaka collaboration has taken over to complete the combined 8-channel analysis of world data on πN, γN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ by the end of 2012, and then proceed to extracting γN → N* form factors from new Jefferson Lab electroproduction data.
