# Dilepton Signature of a First-Order Phase Transition

> The search for a first-order phase transition in strongly interacting matter is one of the major objectives in the exploration of the phase diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). In the present work we investigate dilepton radiation from the hot and dense fireballs created in Au-Au collisions at projectile energies of 1-2 $A$GeV for potential signatures of a first-order transition. Toward this end, we employ a hydrodynamic simulation with two different equations of state, with and without a phase transition. The latter is constrained by susceptibilities at vanishing chemical potential from lattice-QCD as well as neutron star properties, while the former is implemented via modification of the mean-fields in the quark phase. We find that the latent heat involved in the first-order transition leads to a substantial increase in the low-mass thermal emission signal, by about a factor of two above the cross-over scenario.

## Key claim

The latent heat associated with a first-order phase transition in strongly interacting matter leads to a substantial increase in low-mass thermal dilepton emission, by about a factor of two above the crossover scenario, in Au-Au collisions at 1-2 AGeV beam energies; however, the crossover scenario is compatible with current HADES data while the first-order scenario significantly overestimates it.

## Method

The authors employed ideal relativistic fluid dynamics simulations of Au-Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV using two different equations of state from the chiral mean-field (CMF) model: one with a smooth crossover transition and one modified to produce a first-order phase transition at higher temperatures. The dilepton emissivity was calculated using in-medium rho and omega meson spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory, and the results were compared to previous coarse-grained UrQMD transport simulations. The hydrodynamic evolution was performed on a 3+1-dimensional Euclidean grid with cubic cells of size 0.2 fm and time steps of 0.08 fm/c using the SHASTA code.

## Key numbers

- The latent heat in the first-order scenario produces a discontinuity of about 85% in the entropy density relative to the mean value in the transition.
- The low-mass thermal dilepton signal increases by about a factor of two above the crossover scenario when a first-order phase transition is present.
- The hydrodynamic simulation used cells of size 0.2 fm and time steps of 0.08 fm/c.
- The coarse-grained UrQMD simulation used an ensemble of 10^4 events at fixed impact parameter b=2 fm with cell volumes of 1 fm^3 and time steps of 1 fm/c.
- The nuclear incompressibility in the UrQMD mean-field model was 380 MeV.
- The pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD at zero baryon chemical potential is T_pc approximately 155 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton radiation in heavy-ion collisions at SIS18 energies using coarse-grained transport simulations (Galatyuk et al., 2015) by implementing a full hydrodynamic evolution with equations of state that include a first-order phase transition. The crossover equation of state is constrained by lattice QCD results at vanishing chemical potential (Borsanyi et al., 2013; Bazavov et al., 2018) and neutron star properties (Mukherjee et al., 2017). The findings suggest that dilepton measurements at SIS18 and future FAIR facilities could distinguish between crossover and first-order transition scenarios, though the first-order scenario currently overestimates existing HADES data.
