# Structure and decay of the pygmy dipole resonance in 26Ne

> The low-lying spectra of $^{24,25,26}{\rm Ne}$ and the structure of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in $^{26}{\rm Ne}$ have been theoretically studied by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and its extended version called shifted-basis AMD. The calculated energy and strength of the PDR reasonably agree with the observation, and the analysis of the wave function shows that the PDR is dominated by neutron excitation coupled to the quadrupole excited core nucleus $^{25}{\rm Ne}$, which explains the observed unexpected decay of PDR to the excited states of $^{25}{\rm Ne}$. The large isoscalar component of PDR is also shown and the enhancement of the core excitation in neutron-rich Ne isotopes is conjectured.

## Key claim
The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in 26Ne is dominated by neutron excitation coupled to the quadrupole excited core nucleus 25Ne, which explains the observed unexpected decay of PDR to the excited states of 25Ne rather than to its ground state.

## Method
The author applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and its extended version called shifted-basis AMD to describe the low-lying spectra of 24,25,26Ne and the electric dipole response of 26Ne. The generator coordinate method (GCM) was used to superpose wave functions with different quadrupole deformation parameters and angular momentum projections, while the shifted-basis approach introduced additional basis wave functions by shifting Gaussian centroids to incorporate various particle-hole configurations. The Gogny D1S interaction was employed as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers
- The PDR in 26Ne was observed experimentally around Ex = 9 MeV with integrated E1 strength B(E1) = 0.49 ± 0.16 e²fm², exhausting approximately 5% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule.
- The calculated first 2+ state in 26Ne lies at 1.8 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.0 MeV.
- The calculated 0+2 state in 26Ne lies at 4.5 MeV, dominated by the intruder [330 1/2+]² configuration.
- The negative-parity states of 25Ne (3/2- and 7/2-) were calculated at 4.0 and 4.7 MeV, overestimating the observed values of 3.3 and 4.0 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for 26Ne(0+1) coupled to 1/2+1 ⊗ s1/2 was calculated as 1.04, compared to the experimental value of 1.4.
- The spectroscopic factor for 26Ne(0+1) coupled to 5/2+1 ⊗ d5/2 was calculated as 1.47, compared to the experimental value of 1.3.
- The shift parameter for generating shifted-basis wave functions was chosen as ε = 0.3 fm.

## Context
This work addresses a puzzle raised by the experiment of Gibelin (2008) at RIKEN, which found that the PDR in 26Ne predominantly decays to excited states of 25Ne rather than its ground state, contradicting earlier QRPA calculations by Yoshida (2008) and Martini (2011) that predicted dominance of simple ν(1s1/2-1 1p3/2) and ν(1s1/2-1 1p1/2) configurations. The shifted-basis AMD approach extends prior AMD work by Kanada-Enyo (2005, 2016) on neutron-rich Be isotopes and isoscalar responses of light stable nuclei. The results enable a consistent understanding of the PDR decay pattern and suggest enhanced core excitation in neutron-rich Ne isotopes.
