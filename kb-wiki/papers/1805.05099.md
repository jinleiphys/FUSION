# Single-particle spatial dispersion and clusters in nuclei

> The spatial dispersion of the single-nucleon wave functions is analyzed using the self-consistent mean-field framework based on nuclear energy density functionals, and with the harmonic oscillator approximation for the nuclear potential. It is shown that the dispersion depends on the radial quantum number n, but displays only a very weak dependence on the orbital angular momentum. An analytic expression is derived for the localization parameter that explicitly takes into account the radial quantum number of occupied single-nucleon states. The conditions for single-nucleon localization and formation of cluster structures are fulfilled in relatively light nuclei with $A \leq 30$ and $n=1$ states occupied. Heavier nuclei exhibit the quantum liquid phase of nucleonic matter because occupied levels that originate from $n > 1$ spherical states are largely delocalized. Nevertheless, individual $α$-like clusters can be formed from valence nucleons filling single-particle levels originating from $n=1$ spherical mean-field states.

# Single-particle spatial dispersion and clusters in nuclei

## Key claim
The spatial dispersion of single-nucleon wave functions depends strongly on the radial quantum number n but only very weakly on orbital angular momentum l; cluster formation is favored when only n=1 states are occupied (A ≤ 30), but individual α-like clusters can still form in heavier nuclei from valence nucleons occupying Nilsson levels that originate from n=1 spherical mean-field states.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the DD-ME2 energy density functional, with separable pairing correlations, to compute single-nucleon spatial dispersions in axially symmetric nuclei across the nuclear chart. They also derived analytic expressions for the dispersion and localization parameter using a spherical harmonic oscillator approximation for the nuclear potential, and compared these with the microscopic results.

## Key numbers
- For $^{288}$Cf, the depth of the self-consistent neutron potential is V₀ = 78.6 MeV, and the quadrupole deformation parameter is β₂ = 0.07.
- The typical inter-nucleon distance is r₀ ≈ 1.25 fm, determined by nuclear saturation density ρ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The microscopic dispersion in $^{288}$Cf is typically 1.2 times larger than the corresponding harmonic oscillator dispersion, because the actual self-consistent nuclear potential is more diffuse.
- Occupation of an n=2 state leads to a dispersion that is a factor √3 ≈ 1.7 larger than that of n=1 states.
- The derived analytic localization parameter is α_loc ≈ (b/r₀)√(2n-1) = √[ħ(2n-1)] / (2mV₀r₀²)^(1/4) × A^(1/6).
- For A ≤ 30 and n=1 states occupied, α_loc ≲ 1, favoring α-cluster formation.
- In $^{20}$Ne, the spatial dispersion increases by a factor ∼1.7 at the deformation where a 1/2⁺ state (originating from the 2s₁/₂ spherical state) becomes occupied.

## Context
This work extends the earlier EDF-based studies of nucleon localization by Ebran et al. (2012, 2013, 2014, 2017), who introduced the Wigner-parameter-based localization parameter α_loc and related it to cluster formation. The present study generalizes that parameter to explicitly include the radial quantum number dependence, and provides a unified framework explaining why cluster structures are most pronounced in light nuclei (up to silicon, Z=14, as noted by Freer) but can also appear in heavier systems such as $^{212}$Po (observed by Astier et al., 2010). The results connect to discussions of super-deformed and hyper-deformed states (Rae, Nazarewicz) and to the SU(3) classification of clustering, but establish a deformation-independent relation between spatial dispersion and clustering for all deformations.
