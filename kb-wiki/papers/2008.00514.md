# The complete experiment problem of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction in a truncated partial wave analysis

> This dissertation treats the problem of complete experiments for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, in the context of a truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA). The work contains algebraic and numerical considerations. The influence of measurement uncertainties is examined using bootstrap methods. The thesis was originally published online by the University of Bonn in the 1st quarter of 2019 (links are given in the comments).

## Key claim
The dissertation confirms that Omelaenko's mathematically complete sets of 5 observables for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction are fully valid in truncated partial-wave analyses, but also verifies that 6 newly discovered complete sets composed of only 4 observables (from group S and BT observables) are mathematically complete, although these 4-observable sets are not yet understood algebraically.

## Method
The work follows Omelaenko's procedure for resolving discrete ambiguities in the four group S observables (sigma0, Sigma, T, P) using root variables, then investigates additional observables to find complete sets. Numerical tests of completeness are performed using fits to truncated MAID model-data, and bootstrap methods are employed to examine the influence of measurement uncertainties. The author also describes a Legendre moment analysis method that fits angular distributions of polarization observables using associated Legendre polynomials to infer dominant partial-wave contributions.

## Key numbers
- 196 distinct mathematically complete sets of 4 observables were found by L. Tiator using numerical simulations in MATHEMATICA
- 6 of these 196 sets, composed exclusively of group S and BT observables, were confirmed to be mathematically complete using the author's numerical codes
- For the beam asymmetry Sigma with an S- and P-wave truncation (lmax = 1), the angular parametrization reduces to a single term: Sigma(W, theta) = rho * (aL)_2^Sigma(W) * P^2_2(cos theta)
- The Legendre moment analysis was applied to a set of 7 observables (sigma0, Sigma, T, P, E, G, H) in the second resonance region, employing a TPWA truncation at lmax = 2 or 3

## Context
This work extends the foundational approach of Omelaenko (1980s) on complete experiments in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, and incorporates the recent discovery by Tiator (2016) that mathematically complete sets of just 4 observables are possible, which was partially published in Workman et al. (2017). The dissertation enables future truncated partial-wave analyses to use smaller sets of polarization observables for extracting multipole amplitudes, and introduces a Legendre moment analysis method that serves as a preparatory step before full TPWA or energy-dependent model fits.
