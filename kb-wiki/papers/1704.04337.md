# Evolving theoretical descriptions of heavy-ion fusion :from phenomenological to microscopic approaches

> We overview the current status of theoretical approaches for heavy-ion fusion reactions at subbarrier energies. We particularly discuss theoretical challenges in the coupled-channels approach, that include i) a description of deep subbarrier hindrance of fusion cross sections, ii) the role of nuclear dissipation, iii) fusion of unstable nuclei, and iv) an interplay between fusion and multi-nucleon transfer processes. We also present results of a semi-microscopic approach to heavy-ion fusion reactions, that combines the coupled-channels approach with state-of-the-art microscopic nuclear structure calculations.

# Evolving theoretical descriptions of heavy-ion fusion: from phenomenological to microscopic approaches

## Key claim

The coupled-channels approach with macroscopic collective models, while successful for many systems, faces unresolved challenges including deep subbarrier hindrance, the surface diffuseness anomaly, fusion of unstable nuclei, and the interplay between fusion and multi-nucleon transfer. A semi-microscopic approach combining coupled-channels with multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) can improve agreement with experimental data for the 16O+208Pb system by incorporating anharmonicity effects that are absent in the harmonic oscillator limit.

## Method

The paper reviews the coupled-channels approach to heavy-ion subbarrier fusion, where the total wave function is expanded in intrinsic basis states and the coupled-channels equations are solved for the relative motion wave functions. For the 16O+208Pb system, a semi-microscopic calculation is performed using MR-CDFT to generate collective state wave functions and multipole matrix elements, with an overall scaling factor applied to match experimental transition probabilities from the lowest-lying collective state. The calculation includes the one-octupole phonon state (3-1), the one-quadrupole phonon state (2+1), and several states strongly coupled to them, including two-octupole-phonon candidate states.

## Key numbers

- The 154Sm target nucleus has a quadrupole deformation parameter of β2 ~ 0.3, used to explain subbarrier enhancement in the 16O+154Sm system.
- A Woods-Saxon internuclear potential with surface diffuseness parameter a = 0.63 fm systematically overestimates fusion cross sections above the Coulomb barrier; increasing the diffuseness to a = 1.12 fm reproduces experimental data for the 16O+208Pb system.
- For the 40Ca+96Zr system, including multi-neutron transfer couplings with strengths that reproduce experimental transfer cross sections largely underestimates fusion cross sections, indicating the coupling scheme needs extension.
- The semi-microscopic calculation for 16O+208Pb includes the 3-1 state at 2.615 MeV and yields a much lower main peak in the fusion barrier distribution, considerably improving agreement with experimental data compared to the harmonic oscillator limit calculation.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels approach developed by Hagino and Takigawa (2012), Dasgupta et al. (1998), and Back et al. (2014) for heavy-ion subbarrier fusion. It addresses the deep subbarrier hindrance phenomenon discussed by Back (2014) and the surface diffuseness anomaly noted by Newton (2004). The semi-microscopic approach builds on the density-constrained TDHF method of Umar and Oberacker (2008) and the MR-CDFT framework of Yao and Hagino (2015). The results enable future microscopic treatments of dissipative tunneling, fusion of unstable nuclei, and simultaneous description of fusion and multi-nucleon transfer processes, which remain open challenges.
