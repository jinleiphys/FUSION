# Two-pion exchange potential and the $πN$ amplitude

> We discuss the two-pion exchange potential which emerges from a box diagram with one nucleon (the spectator) restricted to its mass shell, and the other nucleon line replaced by a subtracted, covariant $πN$ scattering amplitude which includes $Δ$, Roper, and $D_{13}$ isobars, as well as contact terms and off-shell (non-pole) dressed nucleon terms. The $πN$ amplitude satisfies chiral symmetry constraints and fits $πN$ data below $\sim$ 700 MeV pion energy. We find that this TPE potential can be well approximated by the exchange of an effective sigma and delta meson, with parameters close to the ones used in one-boson-exchange models that fit $NN$ data below the pion production threshold.

# Two-pion exchange potential and the πN amplitude

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange (TPE) potential, computed from a covariant πN scattering amplitude that includes Δ, Roper, and D13 isobars plus contact terms, can be well approximated by the exchange of effective scalar σ and δ mesons below the pion production threshold, provided the D13 resonance contribution is properly included, because it nearly cancels the Δ contribution in the isovector channel.

## Method

The authors evaluated the TPE kernel from a box diagram in the covariant spectator formalism, where one nucleon is restricted to its mass shell and the other nucleon line is replaced by a subtracted, covariant πN scattering amplitude (with the direct nucleon pole term removed to avoid double counting with iterated one-boson-exchange). The πN amplitude was obtained by solving a relativistic two-body scattering integral equation including nucleon, Δ, Roper, and D13 poles plus a contact term satisfying chiral symmetry constraints. The resulting six independent NN helicity amplitudes for isoscalar and isovector exchange channels were then fitted to the amplitudes from exchange of scalar mesons (σ for I=0, δ for I=1) with adjustable mass and coupling constant, using a fixed meson-nucleon form factor cutoff mass.

## Key numbers

- The effective σ meson mass from the TPE fit is 508 MeV, compared to 522 MeV in the OBE model IIB of Gross, Van Orden, and Holinde (1992); the coupling constant g²/(4π) is 4.02 from TPE versus 4.87 from OBE.
- The effective δ meson mass from the TPE fit is 345 MeV, compared to 428 MeV in the OBE model IIB; the coupling constant g²/(4π) is 0.20 from TPE versus 0.24 from OBE.
- The πN amplitude fits data below approximately 700 MeV pion energy.
- The fits were performed at relative NN momenta of 60 MeV/c and 240 MeV/c, both below the one-pion production threshold at 370 MeV/c.
- The form factor cutoff mass was held constant in the range 1–1.2 GeV during each fit.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic one-boson-exchange (OBE) models of Gross, Van Orden, and Holinde (1992) by computing the two-pion exchange contribution that those models represent via effective σ and δ mesons. It differs from prior calculations by Pandharipande and Smith (1976), van Faassen and Tjon (1984), and Elster et al. (1988) that limited the πN amplitude to the Δ isobar, and from Chemtob, Durso, and Riska (1972) that used dispersion relations for other resonances. The result enables extension of OBE models to higher energies approaching the pion production threshold, and provides a microscopic justification for the scalar meson exchanges that are phenomenologically successful in describing NN data below that threshold.
