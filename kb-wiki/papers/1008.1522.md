# Roles of deformation and neutron excess on the giant monopole resonance in neutron-rich Zr isotopes

> We investigate the roles of deformation on the giant monopole resonance (GMR), particularly the mixing of the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) and the effects of the neutron excess in the well-deformed nuclei around $^{110}$Zr and in the drip-line nuclei around $^{140}$Zr by means of the deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation employing the Skyrme and the local pairing energy-density functionals. It is found that the isoscalar (IS) GMR has a two-peak structure, the lower peak of which is associated with the mixing between the ISGMR and the $K^π=0^{+}$ component of the ISGQR. The transition strengths of the lower peak of the ISGMR grows as the neutron number increases. In the drip-line nuclei, the neutron excitation is dominant over the proton excitation. We find for an isovector (IV) excitation the GMR has a four-peak structure due to the mixing of the IS and IV modes as well as the mixing of the $K^π=0^{+}$ component of the IVGQR. Besides the GMR, we find the threshold strength generated by neutrons only.

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in deformed neutron-rich Zr isotopes exhibits a two-peak structure, where the lower peak arises from mixing between the ISGMR and the \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) component of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), and the transition strength of this lower peak grows with increasing neutron number, particularly near the drip line.

## Method

The author performed deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation (QRPA) calculations using the Skyrme SkM* interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent contact interaction for the pairing field, solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space with axial and reflection symmetries. The QRPA equation was solved in matrix formulation using a quasiparticle basis obtained from the self-consistent HFB solution, with a two-quasiparticle excitation energy cutoff of 60 MeV and a smearing width of 2 MeV for the strength functions. Calculations were carried out for Zr isotopes from \(^{90}\)Zr to \(^{140}\)Zr, including well-deformed nuclei around \(^{110}\)Zr and drip-line nuclei around \(^{140}\)Zr.

## Key numbers

- The neutron pairing gap in \(^{120}\)Sn used to calibrate the pairing strength is \(\langle \Delta_{\nu}\rangle = 1.20\) MeV, with the pairing strength parameter set to \(t_{0}^{\prime} = -240\) MeV fm\(^{3}\).
- The spurious \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) state appears at 0.58 MeV in \(^{110}\)Zr, well separated from the giant resonance region.
- The number of two-quasiparticle excitations for the \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) channel is about 36,000 in \(^{110}\)Zr and 46,200 in \(^{140}\)Zr.
- The chemical potential for neutrons in \(^{140}\)Zr is \(-0.10\) MeV, indicating it is very close to the drip line; the neutron skin thickness (difference of neutron and proton root-mean-square radii) is 0.64 fm.
- The difference in excitation energy of the upper ISGMR peak between \(^{100}\)Zr and \(^{112}\)Zr is 0.7 MeV.
- The mean energy of the isovector GMR in \(^{90}\)Zr is 31.6 MeV, consistent with the hydrodynamical model prediction of about 29 MeV.
- The ratio \((M_{\nu}/M_{\pi})/(N/Z)\) for the lower ISGMR peak increases to 1.55 in \(^{140}\)Zr, while for the upper peak it decreases to 0.72 in \(^{140}\)Zr.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deformation effects on giant resonances, such as the deformation splitting of the giant dipole resonance established in Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the scaling model for the GMR-GQR coupling by Nishizaki and Ando (1985). It also builds on ground-state calculations of neutron-rich Zr isotopes by Blazkiewicz et al. (2005) and Stoitsov et al. (2008), which predicted large deformations in \(^{112}\)Zr and strong deformation in drip-line nuclei. The results enable future investigation of the microscopic structure of low-lying \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states with attention to continuum coupling and pairing correlations, and they provide predictions for experimental studies of the GMR in deformed neutron-rich nuclei using next-generation radioactive beam facilities.
