# QCD factorization and universality of jet cross sections in heavy-ion collisions

> We review a recently proposed phenomenological framework to establish the notions of QCD factorization and universality of jet cross sections in the heavy-ion environment. First results of a global analysis of the nuclear modification factor of inclusive jets are presented where we extract medium modified jet functions using a Monte Carlo sampling approach. We observe that gluon jets are significantly more suppressed than quark jets. In addition, we study the jet radius dependence of the inclusive jet cross section in heavy-ion collisions and comment on a recent measurement from CMS. By considering for example jet substructure observables it will be possible to test the universality of the extracted medium jet functions. We thus expect that the presented results will eventually allow for extractions of medium properties with a reduced model bias.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological framework to test whether QCD factorization and universality of jet cross sections, well-established in proton-proton collisions, can be extended to heavy-ion collisions by extracting medium-modified jet functions from data, with the hedge that this approach assumes the only relevant modification in heavy-ion collisions is to the final-state jet function and that the DGLAP evolution equations remain unchanged from the vacuum case.

## Method

The authors replace vacuum jet functions in the factorized inclusive jet cross section with medium-modified jet functions expressed as vacuum jet functions convolved with weight functions \(W_c(z)\) that are fit to experimental data. They perform a global analysis of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}^{\rm jet}\) using a Monte Carlo sampling approach (data resampling technique similar to that used in PDF fits) to extract the medium jet functions from LHC data at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV for central collisions (0-10%) with jet radii \(R=0.2\) and \(R=0.4\).

## Key numbers

- The global fit yields \(\chi^2/d.o.f = 1.7\) when comparing theoretical results using extracted medium jet functions to ATLAS and ALICE data at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV for central collisions (0-10%) with \(R=0.2\) and \(R=0.4\).
- The ratio of medium to vacuum jet functions is shown for \(p_T=100\) GeV and \(R=0.4\), demonstrating suppression at large \(z\) and enhancement at small \(z\).
- Gluon jets are observed to be significantly more suppressed than quark jets, with a significant shift toward quark jets in heavy-ion collisions relative to the vacuum.

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework developed for proton-proton collisions (Collins 1989, Nayak 2005) and builds on the jet function formalism for inclusive jets (Kaufmann 2015, Kang 2016, Dai 2016). It is a phenomenological follow-up to the authors' earlier proposal (Qiu 2019) and is related to analyses of fragmentation functions in cold nuclear matter (Sassot 2009). The extracted medium jet functions are intended to be tested for universality by applying them to other observables such as jet substructure (Ringer 2019) and photon-jet correlations, which would enable extractions of medium properties with reduced model bias.
