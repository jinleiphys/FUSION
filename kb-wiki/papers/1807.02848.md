# Few- and many-nucleon systems with semilocal coordinate-space regularized chiral two- and three-body forces

> We present a complete calculation of nucleon-deuteron scattering as well as ground and low-lying excited states of light nuclei in the mass range A=3-16 up through next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory using semilocal coordinate-space regularized two- and three-nucleon forces. It is shown that both of the low-energy constants entering the three-nucleon force at this order can be reliably determined from the triton binding energy and the differential cross section minimum in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering. The inclusion of the three-nucleon force is found to improve the agreement with the data for most of the considered observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semilocal coordinate-space regularized chiral two- and three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) can simultaneously describe nucleon-deuteron scattering and ground-state energies of light nuclei up to A=16, but only when the two low-energy constants c_D and c_E are fixed using both the triton binding energy and the differential cross section minimum in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering; the authors note that the description of the neutron analyzing power A_y puzzle improves only slightly and that theoretical uncertainties at N2LO remain large enough that higher-order corrections are needed for quantitative tests.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev-type integral equations for three-nucleon scattering in a partial-wave momentum-space basis, including two-nucleon partial waves up to j_max=5 and three-nucleon total angular momenta up to J_max=25/2, with the three-nucleon force included up to J_max=7/2. For p-shell nuclei (A=4-16), they used No-Core Configuration Interaction (NCCI) methods with Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution to soften the Hamiltonian, diagonalizing in truncated harmonic-oscillator bases and extrapolating to estimate binding energies in the complete space. The three-nucleon force at N2LO was regularized consistently with the two-nucleon force using semilocal coordinate-space regulators with cutoff radii R=0.9 fm and R=1.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy used as one constraint to establish a correlation between c_D and c_E; for R=0.9 fm the best-fit values from a combined fit to all observables up to 135 MeV are c_D = 1.7 ± 0.8 and c_E = -0.329^{+0.103}_{-0.106}
- For R=1.0 fm the best-fit values from the same combined fit are c_D = 7.2 ± 0.7 and c_E = -0.652 ± 0.067
- The neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length 2a = 0.645 ± 0.008 fm (from Schoen (2003)) does not constrain c_D at N2LO, in contrast to earlier expectations
- At E_n=14.1 MeV, the neutron analyzing power A_y is underestimated by approximately 30% at the maximum near Θ_c.m. ≈ 125° by phenomenological potentials alone; the N2LO chiral 3NF reduces this discrepancy by about half the amount seen with the TM99 3NF model
- Ground state energies for ^4He and ^12C show that without SRG evolution, results with and without SRG are within about 10 keV for ^4He; with explicit 3NFs, the ^4He results depend on the SRG parameter, but for A ≥ 6 this dependence becomes of the same order as or smaller than the extrapolation uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the semilocal coordinate-space regularized chiral potentials of Epelbaum (2014) and Epelbaum (2015) by including, for the first time, complete N2LO three-nucleon forces in calculations of both nucleon-deuteron scattering and p-shell nuclei up to A=16. It builds on earlier NN-only studies by Binder (2015), Maris (2016), and Binder (2018) that identified significant deviations from data attributable to missing three-nucleon forces. The results enable parameter-free predictions for A>3 systems and establish a baseline for future calculations at higher chiral orders (N3LO and beyond), which the authors state are in progress.
