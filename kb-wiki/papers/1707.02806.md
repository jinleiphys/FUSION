# Probing Saturation Physics in the Real Compton Scattering at Ultraperipheral $pPb$ Collisions

> The Real Compton Scattering in ultraperipheral $pPb$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is investigated and predictions for the squared transverse momentum ($t$) and rapidity ($Y$) distributions are presented. The scattering amplitude is assumed to be given by the sum of the Reggeon and Pomeron contributions and the Pomeron one is described by the Color Dipole formalism taking into account the non - linear (saturation) effects in the QCD dynamics. We demonstrate that the behaviour of the cross sections at large -- $t$ and/or $Y$ is dominated by the Pomeron contribution and is strongly affected by the non -- linear effects present in the QCD dynamics. These results indicate that a future experimental analysis of this process can be useful to probe the QCD dynamics at high energies.

# Probing Saturation Physics in the Real Compton Scattering at Ultraperipheral $pPb$ Collisions

## Key claim

The total cross section for Real Compton Scattering in ultraperipheral $pPb$ collisions is dominated by the Reggeon (non-perturbative) contribution, but at large squared transverse momentum ($t$) and/or large rapidity ($Y$) the cross section is dominated by the Pomeron contribution, which is strongly affected by non-linear (saturation) effects in QCD dynamics. The authors demonstrate that the behavior of the differential cross section at large $t$ and $Y$ is strongly affected by these non-linear effects, indicating that future experimental analysis could probe saturation physics.

## Method

The scattering amplitude for $\gamma p \rightarrow \gamma p$ is assumed to be the sum of Reggeon and Pomeron contributions. The Pomeron contribution is described using the color dipole formalism with the impact parameter Color Glass Condensate (bCGC) model for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude, with parameters constrained by HERA data on inclusive and exclusive $ep$ processes. The differential cross section for ultraperipheral $pPb$ collisions is obtained by folding the $\gamma p \rightarrow \gamma p$ cross section with the equivalent photon spectrum of the lead nucleus, assuming the nucleus moves from left to right (positive rapidities correspond to the nucleus fragmentation region). The authors compare full bCGC predictions with linearized predictions (disregarding non-linear effects) to isolate the impact of saturation.

## Key numbers

- The Reggeon contribution to the total $\gamma p$ cross section is parameterized as $\sigma_{\gamma p}^\reg(W^2) = 0.135 \cdot (W^2)^{-3}$ mb, with slope parameters $B_1 = 6$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $\alpha_\reg'(0) = 0.9$ GeV$^{-2}$ at reference scale $\mu_0^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$.
- The bCGC model parameters from fits to HERA data: $\gamma_s = 0.6599$, $\kappa = 9.9$, $B_{CGC} = 5.5$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\mathcal{N}_0 = 0.3358$, $x_0 = 0.00105$, and $\lambda = 0.2063$.
- Quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = m_s = 0.2$ GeV and $m_c = 1.5$ GeV.
- Predictions are presented for RHIC energy $\sqrt{s} = 0.2$ TeV and LHC energy $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV, with rapidity distributions shown for $|t| = 0$ and $|t| = 0.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The dip in the $t$-distribution moves to smaller $|t|$ values as energy increases and as rapidity increases.
- At LHC energies and $|t| = 0$, the Non-linear contribution is similar to the Reggeon one in the kinematical range probed by the LHCb detector and dominates at forward rapidities.
- For $|t| = 0.5$ GeV$^2$ at LHC energies, the Reggeon contribution is strongly suppressed.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole formalism, previously applied to exclusive vector meson photoproduction in $pp/pA/AA$ collisions by Goncalves et al. (2014, 2015, 2016) and to diffractive heavy quark production, exclusive $Z^0$ photoproduction, and time-like Compton scattering, to treat Real Compton Scattering in ultraperipheral $pPb$ collisions. It is motivated by the study of Kopeilovich et al. using a distinct approach. The results indicate that measurement of the $t$-distribution and rapidity distribution at large $t$ and forward rapidities at the LHC can discriminate between linear and non-linear QCD dynamics, complementing studies of exclusive $J/\Psi$ photoproduction. The authors note that the total RCS cross section is larger than the $J/\Psi$ one, the dip occurs at smaller $|t|$, and the rapidity distribution is broader, but experimental separation of produced photons remains a challenge requiring further study.
