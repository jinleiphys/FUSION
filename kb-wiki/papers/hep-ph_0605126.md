# Light Scalar Mesons from Phi to Psi

> The following topics are considered. 1) Confinement, chiral dynamics, and light scalar mesons. 2) Chiral shielding of the $σ(600)$. 3) The $ϕ$ meson radiative decays about nature of light scalar resonances. 4) The $J/ψ$ decays about nature of light scalar resonances. 5) The $a_0(980)\toγγ$ and $f_0(980)\toγγ$ decays about nature of light scalar resonances. 6) New round in $γγ\toπ^+π^-$, the Belle data. Arguments in favor of the four-quark model of the $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the totality of experimental data on light scalar mesons, including radiative phi decays, J/psi decays, and two-photon collisions, favors a four-quark (q²q̄²) structure for the a₀(980) and f₀(980) mesons over the conventional P-wave quark-antiquark interpretation, though the authors acknowledge that the f₀(980) could contain a significant s̄s component with a gluon admixture, and that the linear sigma model does not necessarily contradict a non-q̄q nature.

## Method

The authors perform a phenomenological analysis of multiple decay channels: φ → γπ⁰η, φ → γπ⁰π⁰, J/ψ → a₀(980)ρ, J/ψ → f₀(980)ω, J/ψ → γf₀(980), and γγ → π⁺π⁻. They compare experimental branching ratios and widths with predictions from both the q̄q model and the four-quark (q²q̄²) model, using the K⁺K⁻ loop mechanism as the principal production mechanism for a₀(980) and f₀(980) in φ radiative decays. The chiral shielding of the σ(600) is demonstrated through a simultaneous fit to ππ scattering phase shifts and φ → γπ⁰π⁰ data using a linear sigma model framework with unitarized amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- B(φ → γπ⁰η) = (0.851 ± 0.051 ± 0.057) × 10⁻⁴ (KLOE Collaboration)
- B(φ → γπ⁰π⁰) = (1.09 ± 0.03 ± 0.05) × 10⁻⁴ (KLOE Collaboration)
- B(J/ψ → a₀(980)ρ) < 4.4 × 10⁻⁴, compared to B(J/ψ → a₂(1320)ρ) = (109 ± 22) × 10⁻⁴, giving a ratio < 0.04 ± 0.008
- B(J/ψ → γf₀(980) → γππ) < 1.4 × 10⁻⁵, compared to expected B(J/ψ → γf₂(1270))/4 = (3.45 ± 0.35) × 10⁻⁴
- Γ(a₀ → γγ) = (0.19 ± 0.07⁺⁰·¹₋₀·₀₇)/B(a₀ → πη) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.28 ± 0.04 ± 0.1)/B(a₀ → πη) keV (JADE), compared to q̄q prediction of (1.5–5.9) × Γ(a₂ → γγ) = (1.5–5.9) × (1.04 ± 0.09) keV
- Γ(f₀ → γγ) = (0.31 ± 0.14 ± 0.09) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.24 ± 0.06 ± 0.15) keV (MARK II), compared to q̄q prediction of (1.7–5.5) × Γ(f₂ → γγ) = (1.7–5.5) × (2.8 ± 0.4) keV
- ⟨Γ^{Born}_{f₀ → K⁺K⁻ → γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.15 keV from the K⁺K⁻ loop mechanism in γγ → π⁺π⁻

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-standing program (Achasov et al., 1990s–2000s) arguing for the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons, building on Jaffe's (1977) MIT bag model prediction of light q²q̄² scalar states. It directly challenges the conventional P-wave q̄q interpretation that would treat a₀(980) and f₀(980) as analogous to the tensor mesons a₂(1320) and f₂(1270). The analysis enables future precision measurements of J/ψ decays to a₀(980)ρ and f₀(980)ω to discriminate between the four-quark model and the alternative scenario where f₀(980) is a near-s̄s state with gluon admixture.
