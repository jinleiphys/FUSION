# Fully charmed tetraquarks from LHC to FCC: Natural stability from fragmentation

> We investigate the inclusive production of fully charmed tetraquarks, $T_{4c}(0^{++})$ or $T_{4c}(2^{++})$ radial excitations, in high-energy proton collisions. We build our study upon the collinear fragmentation of a single parton in a variable-flavor number scheme, suited to describe the tetraquark formation mechanism from moderate to large transverse-momentum regimes. To this extent, we derive a novel set of DGLAP-evolving collinear fragmentation functions, named TQ4Q1.0 determinations. They encode initial-scale inputs corresponding to both gluon and heavy-quark fragmentation channels, defined within the context of quark-potential and spin-physics inspired models, respectively. We work within the NLL/NLO$^+$ hybrid factorization and make use of the JETHAD numeric interface along with the symJETHAD symbolic calculation plugin. With these tools, we provide predictions for high-energy observables sensitive to $T_{4c}$ plus jet emissions at center-of-mass energies ranging from 14 TeV at the LHC to the 100 TeV nominal energy of the FCC.

## Key claim
The paper claims that inclusive production of fully charmed tetraquarks ($T_{4c}(0^{++})$ and $T_{4c}(2^{++})$) at high transverse momenta can be described by single-parton collinear fragmentation in a variable-flavor number scheme, and that this fragmentation mechanism provides "natural stability" to the high-energy resummation framework, but this stability is conditional on the tetraquark being produced at moderate to large transverse momenta where fragmentation dominates over direct short-distance production.

## Method
The authors derived a novel set of DGLAP-evolving collinear fragmentation functions (FFs), named TQ4Q1.0, for fully charmed tetraquarks. Initial-scale inputs for the gluon-to-tetraquark channel were taken from NRQCD factorization with color-composite long-distance matrix elements from a potential model (Lu 2020), while the charm-to-tetraquark channel was modeled via a Suzuki-type spin-physics inspired approach. These inputs were evolved using the heavy-flavor nonrelativistic evolution (HFNRevo) scheme within the NLL/NLO$^+$ hybrid factorization framework, implemented via the JETHAD numeric interface and symJETHAD symbolic plugin.

## Key numbers
* Charm-quark mass set to $m_c = 1.5$ GeV for the gluon fragmentation channel initial-scale input.
* Long-distance matrix elements for the $T_{4c}(0^{++})$ state: $\langle \mathcal{O}^{T_{4c}(0^{++})}([3,3]) \rangle = 0.0347$ GeV$^9$, $\langle \mathcal{O}^{T_{4c}(0^{++})}([6,6]) \rangle = 0.0128$ GeV$^9$, $\langle \mathcal{O}^{T_{4c}(0^{++})}([3,6]) \rangle = 0.0211$ GeV$^9$.
* Long-distance matrix elements for the $T_{4c}(2^{++})$ state: $\langle \mathcal{O}^{T_{4c}(2^{++})}([3,3]) \rangle = 0.072$ GeV$^9$, with $[6,6]$ and $[3,6]$ contributions vanishing.
* Predictions are provided for center-of-mass energies ranging from 14 TeV at the LHC to 100 TeV at the FCC.

## Context
This work extends the NRQCD-based fragmentation approach previously applied to quarkonia (Braaten 1993, Feng 2020) and heavy-light tetraquarks (Celiberto 2023, TQHL1.0 functions) to the case of fully charmed tetraquarks. It builds on the first calculation of gluon-to-$T_{4c}$ fragmentation by Feng (2020) and the demonstration that gluon fragmentation dominates over direct production for transverse momenta above 20 GeV (Feng 2023). The results enable phenomenological studies of $T_{4c}$ plus jet observables at current and future colliders, and the TQ4Q1.0 FF sets provide a tool for testing the nature of the $X(6900)$ resonance as a fully charmed tetraquark candidate.
