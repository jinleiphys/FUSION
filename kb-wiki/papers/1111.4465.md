# Total nucleon-nucleon cross sections in large N(c) QCD

> We use contracted spin-flavor symmetry which emerges in the large Nc limit of QCD to obtain relations between proton-proton and proton-neutron total cross sections for both polarized and unpolarized scattering. The formalism used is valid in the semi-classical regime in which the relative momentum of the incident nucleons is much larger than the inverse size of the nucleon, provided that certain technical assumptions are met. The relations should be phenomenologically useful provided that Nc=3 is sufficiently large so that the large Nc results have at least semi-quantitative predictive power. The relations are model-independent in the sense that they depend on properties of large Nc QCD only and not on any particular model-dependent details of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We compare these model-independent results to experimental data. We find the relation for spin-unpolarized scattering works well empirically. For the case of polarized scattering, the data is consistent with the relations but the cross sections are too small to make sharp predictions.

# Total nucleon-nucleon cross sections in large N_c QCD

## Key claim

The contracted spin-flavor symmetry that emerges in the large N_c limit of QCD yields model-independent relations between proton-proton and proton-neutron total cross sections for both polarized and unpolarized scattering, provided the relative momentum is much larger than the inverse nucleon size and certain technical assumptions are met; the relations are expected to have at least semi-quantitative predictive power for N_c=3 only if N_c=3 is sufficiently large, and the formalism excludes very forward scattering angles where the semiclassical mean-field treatment breaks down.

## Method

The authors use time-dependent mean-field theory (TDMFT) at fixed baryon velocity, which becomes exact in the large N_c limit, to compute inclusive differential cross sections for hedgehog-hedgehog scattering. They then project these onto nucleon-nucleon channels by quantizing the collective spin-flavor degrees of freedom using Wigner D-matrices, exploiting the contracted SU(4) symmetry of the ground-state baryon band. The total cross section is obtained by integrating the inclusive differential cross section over all angles, with the argument that the angular region where semiclassical analysis fails (very forward angles) becomes negligible as N_c → ∞.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic threshold velocity at large N_c is given by v_t = sqrt(4 m_pi / M_N) ~ sqrt(1/N_c); for N_c=3 this threshold is set by the pion mass.
- The deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV, while the nucleon-Delta mass splitting (treated as a 1/N_c effect) is about 300 MeV, highlighting a scale tension in applying large N_c results to nuclear binding.
- The spin-averaged total cross sections for pp and np are predicted equal at leading order in 1/N_c: sigma^(pp)_0 = sigma^(np)_0 (1 + O(1/N_c)), and this relation is well satisfied by data at beam momenta of a few GeV/c (Bugg et al., 1996).
- For transversely polarized scattering, the prediction is Delta sigma_T^(pp) = - Delta sigma_T^(np) (1 + O(1/N_c)), where Delta sigma_T = -(sigma(up up) - sigma(up down)); data (Fonteine et al., 1991; Ditzler et al., 1983; Lesikar, 1981) are consistent but cross sections are too small to make sharp predictions.

## Context

This work extends earlier large N_c analyses of nucleon-nucleon potentials (NNpotential references) by focusing on directly observable total cross sections rather than the theorist construct of a potential, which is complicated by the Delta baryon becoming degenerate with the nucleon at large N_c. It builds on the contracted SU(2N_f) symmetry framework of SU2Nf and on prior work (NNobservables, CD) that derived spin-flavor relations for inclusive differential cross sections in TDMFT. The results enable model-independent tests of large N_c QCD in a regime (center-of-mass momenta of a few GeV) where semiclassical treatment is valid and where elastic scattering is a small fraction of the total, avoiding the problematic low-energy regime where earlier elastic-scattering predictions (CD) failed badly because N_c=3 is not large enough and the pion is anomalously light.
