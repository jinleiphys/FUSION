# Deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> A deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory in continuum is developed aiming at a proper description of exotic nuclei, particularly those with a large spatial extension. In order to give an adequate consideration of both the contribution of the continuum and the large spatial distribution in exotic nuclei, the deformed RHB equations are solved in a Woods-Saxon (WS) basis in which the radial wave functions have a proper asymptotic behavior at large distance from the nuclear center. This is crucial for the proper description of a possible halo. The formalism of deformed RHB theory in continuum is presented. A stable nucleus, 20Mg and a weakly-bound nucleus, 42Mg, are taken as examples to present numerical details and to carry out necessary numerical checks. In addition, the ground state properties of even-even magnesium isotopes are investigated. The generic conditions of the formation of a halo in weakly bound deformed systems and the shape of the halo in deformed nuclei are discussed. We show that the existence and the deformation of a possible neutron halo depend essentially on the quantum numbers of the main components of the single particle orbitals in the vicinity of the Fermi surface.

# Deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in continuum

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) equations solved in a Woods-Saxon basis can properly describe exotic nuclei with large spatial extension, including possible halos, because the Woods-Saxon radial wave functions have realistic asymptotic behavior at large distance from the nuclear center. The existence and deformation of a possible neutron halo depend essentially on the quantum numbers of the main components of the single-particle orbitals near the Fermi surface, not solely on the binding energy.

## Method

The deformed RHB equations are solved by expanding the Dirac spinors in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis, which provides a discrete set of states via box boundary conditions. The scalar and vector potentials and the densities are expanded in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed nuclei with spatial reflection symmetry. A density-dependent zero-range pairing force is used in the particle-particle channel, with parameters fitted to reproduce the proton pairing energy from a spherical RHB calculation with the Gogny D1S force for 20Mg.

## Key numbers

* Proton pairing energy for 20Mg: -9.2382 MeV from spherical RHB with Gogny D1S force; reproduced to -9.2382 MeV by deformed RHB with surface delta pairing (V0 = 380 MeV fm3, ρsat = 0.152 fm-3, Eq.p.cut = 60 MeV, Γq.p.cut = 5.65 MeV)
* For 20Mg with cutoff energy E+cut = 100 MeV: total binding energy -136.6728 MeV, proton pairing energy -9.1165 MeV, neutron rms radius 2.5910 fm, proton rms radius 3.0073 fm
* For 42Mg with E+cut = 100 MeV: relative accuracies of 0.5% for quadrupole deformation, 0.05% for rms radius, and 0.1% for total binding energy
* For 42Mg, decreasing mesh size from 0.1 fm to 0.05 fm changes binding energy by less than 0.025 MeV (0.008% of total), and changes quadrupole deformation and radius by less than 0.01%
* Box size Rbox = 20 fm, mesh size Δr = 0.1 fm, λmax = 4, |κmax| = 10, and E+cut = 100 MeV used for production calculations

## Context

This work extends the Woods-Saxon basis method previously developed for spherical relativistic Hartree equations (Zhou 2003) to deformed systems with pairing correlations, building on earlier efforts toward deformed relativistic Hartree theory (Zhou 2006) and deformed RHB theory in continuum (Zhou 2008). It enables self-consistent description of halo phenomena in deformed nuclei, complementing non-relativistic HFB approaches in coordinate space (Dobaczewski 1984, 1996) and on three-dimensional meshes (Terasaki 1996, Pei 2008), and provides a framework to address questions about deformed halos raised by experiments such as 31Ne (Nakamura 2009) and 35Mg (Kanungo 2011).
