# Photo and Electrodisintegration of Few Body Systems Revisited

> Twenty years after P. Sauer released the state of the art Faddeev solution of the bound state three nucleon systems, I revisit photo and electrodisengration of few body systems with a special emphasis on the prospects opened at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key claim

Twenty years after P. Sauer's Faddeev solution of the three-nucleon bound state, the author revisits photo- and electrodisintegration of few-body systems, arguing that the Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) data confirm Sauer's wave functions up to nucleon momenta of 500 MeV/c but that at higher momenta final-state interactions (FSI) and meson-exchange currents (MEC) dominate and must be carefully controlled, especially in quasi-free kinematics where on-shell rescattering is maximized.

## Method

The author uses a diagrammatic approach originally developed in the 1980s, extended to include a high-energy parameterization of the NN scattering amplitude (central, spin-orbit, and spin-spin terms) for relative kinetic energies above 500 MeV, where partial-wave expansions become impractical. For the three-body disintegration channels, a Monte Carlo event generator simulates the CLAS detector acceptance, applying the same kinematic cuts to both theory and experiment. The model is calibrated against previous data from Saclay, NIKHEF, and Bates below 800 MeV, then compared to new JLab data at higher energies and momenta.

## Key numbers

- Virtuality of the exchanged photon in the D(e,e'p)n experiment: \(Q^2 = 0.665\) GeV\(^2\), about twice that achieved twenty years earlier.
- Relative kinetic energy of the two outgoing nucleons in that experiment: \(T_L = Q^2/2m = 337\) MeV.
- Recoil momentum distribution measured up to 600 MeV/c in the D(e,e'p)n reaction at X=1.
- For the \(^3\)He(e,e'p)d reaction at JLab: \(Q^2 = 1.55\) GeV\(^2\), relative kinetic energy between outgoing proton and deuteron \(T_L = 850\) MeV, where the NN cross section is flat at \(\sigma_{NN} = 47\) mb.
- FSI reduces the quasi-free contribution below 300 MeV/c and overwhelms it by more than a factor five around 500 MeV/c in the \(^3\)He(e,e'p)d reaction.
- For the \(^3\)He(e,e'2p)n reaction in symmetric kinematics, the on-shell rescattering description works over the energy range \(0.2 < T_L < 2\) GeV.
- The energy of CEBAF is 6 GeV, with a planned upgrade to 12 GeV to reach \(Q^2 \sim 12\) GeV\(^2\) for color transparency studies.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-based calculations of Sauer (1981, 1983) and the diagrammatic approach of Laget (1981, 1991) to higher energies and momenta accessible at JLab, confirming that Sauer's wave functions work well up to 500 MeV/c but that FSI and MEC dominate at higher recoil momenta. The results enable future studies of short-range correlations in few-body systems, three-body forces via meson-exchange currents, and color transparency effects at the proposed 12 GeV upgrade of CEBAF.
