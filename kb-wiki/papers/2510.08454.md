# Gluon splitting at small $x$: a unified derivation for the JIMWLK, DGLAP and CSS equations

> We revisit the calculation of the next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to dijet production in electron-ion collisions at small $x$. We focus on the back-to-back configuration where the relative transverse momentum $P_\perp$ of the measured jets is much larger than both their momentum imbalance $K_\perp$ and the target saturation momentum $Q_s(x,A)$. In this regime, we present for the first time a complete calculation of the real NLO corrections at leading power in $1/P_\perp$. Our result exhibits TMD factorisation, with the same hard factor as at tree-level and a NLO correction to the Weiszäcker-Williams (WW) gluon transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distribution which involves four Wilson-line operators. By studying different kinematical regimes for $K_\perp$ and for the radiated gluon, we recover all the quantum evolutions that were previously identified for this process at NLO: the B-JIMWLK high-energy evolution and the CSS evolution of the gluon WW TMD, and the DGLAP evolution of the gluon PDF. When both $K_\perp$ and the transverse momentum transferred by the target are large compared to $Q_s$, all the Wilson-line operators boil down to the unintegrated gluon distribution and our NLO result for the gluon TMD can be used to isolate the transverse-momentum dependent gluon splitting function.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of the real next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to back-to-back dijet production in electron-ion collisions at small $x$, to leading power in $1/P_\perp$, for generic values of the dijet momentum imbalance $K_\perp$ (including semi-hard values $K_\perp \sim Q_s$). The authors show that all these NLO corrections preserve transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorisation with the same hard factor as at tree level, and can be absorbed into renormalisation of the Weiszäcker-Williams (WW) gluon TMD.

## Method

The authors computed the real NLO corrections to dijet production in the color dipole picture of the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, focusing on gluon emissions by the quark or antiquark leg of the dipole. They considered both initial-state and final-state gluon emissions with generic transverse momenta $k_{g\perp} \ll P_\perp$ and longitudinal momentum fractions $z_g$ in the range $x k_{g\perp}^2/P_\perp^2 \lesssim z_g \lesssim k_{g\perp}/P_\perp$, and extracted the leading-power contributions in the expansion in powers of $K_\perp^2/P_\perp^2$ and $Q_s^2/P_\perp^2$. By studying different kinematic regimes for $K_\perp$ and the radiated gluon, they recovered the real-gluon contributions to the B-JIMWLK (Dominguez-Marquet-Munier-Xiao), DGLAP, and Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) evolution equations for the WW gluon TMD.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed to leading power in $1/P_\perp$, where $P_\perp$ is the dijet relative transverse momentum, with $P_\perp \gg K_\perp, Q_s$.
* The longitudinal momentum fraction of the radiated gluon satisfies $z_g \lesssim k_{g\perp}/P_\perp \ll 1$, where $k_{g\perp}$ is the gluon transverse momentum.
* The regime $x k_{g\perp}^2/P_\perp^2 \ll z_g \ll k_{g\perp}^2/P_\perp^2$ generates the real part of the DMMX equation for the WW gluon TMD evolution with decreasing $x$.
* The regime $k_{g\perp}^2/P_\perp^2 \lesssim z_g \lesssim k_{g\perp}/P_\perp$ generates the real part of the CSS equation for Sudakov logarithms.
* The borderline region $z_g \sim k_{g\perp}^2/P_\perp^2$ generates the DGLAP evolution of the gluon parton distribution function.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of NLO corrections to dijet production in the CGC framework, which were restricted to specific kinematic regimes: the DMMX equation derivation in Taels (2022) and Caucal (2022) for very soft gluons, the Sudakov logarithm calculations in Mueller (2013), Taels (2022), and Caucal (2022) for final-state emissions, and the recent work by Caucal (2024) which assumed $K_\perp \simeq k_{g\perp} \gg Q_s$. The present paper generalises these results by allowing $K_\perp$ and $k_{g\perp}$ to take generic values including semi-hard values of order $Q_s$, and by computing previously overlooked initial-state emission contributions that are not enhanced by large logarithms. This enables the first unified derivation of the JIMWLK, DGLAP, and CSS evolution equations from a single NLO calculation, and provides the complete NLO correction to the WW gluon TMD that can be used to isolate the transverse-momentum dependent gluon splitting function in the dilute regime.
