# Equation of state of strongly coupled quark--gluon plasma -- Path integral Monte Carlo results

> A strongly coupled plasma of quark and gluon quasiparticles at temperatures from $ 1.1 T_c$ to $3 T_c$ is studied by path integral Monte Carlo simulations. This method extends previous classical nonrelativistic simulations based on a color Coulomb interaction to the quantum regime. We present the equation of state and find good agreement with lattice results. Further, pair distribution functions and color correlation functions are computed indicating strong correlations and liquid-like behavior.

# Equation of state of strongly coupled quark–gluon plasma – Path integral Monte Carlo results

## Key claim

Path integral Monte Carlo simulations of a color Coulomb interacting quasiparticle model reproduce lattice QCD equation-of-state data down to temperatures as low as 1.2 T_c, but only when a temperature-independent quasiparticle density (n ≈ 3 fm⁻³) is used; the version with a temperature-dependent density shows substantial deviations from lattice results.

## Method

The authors perform path integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) simulations of a three-component system of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons (40 particles each, 120 total) interacting via a color Coulomb potential with Wong color variables, using the improved Kelbg potential to regularize the short-range divergence. The quasiparticle masses follow a temperature-dependent parametrization m(T)/T_c = 0.9/(T/T_c − 1) + 3.45 + 0.4 T/T_c, and the coupling constant is fixed at g² = 2π. The simulations include Fermi statistics for quarks/antiquarks (via determinants) and Bose statistics for gluons (via permanents), with 20 high-temperature factors (beads) in the path integral representation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: from 1.1 T_c to 3 T_c
- Quasiparticle degeneracy parameter χ = n_a Λ_a³ = 4.1, constant over the studied temperature interval
- Quasiparticle density in the successful parametrization (model b): n ≈ 3 fm⁻³
- Number of particles in simulation: N = N_q + N_anti-q + N_g = 40 + 40 + 40 = 120
- Number of high-temperature factors (beads): n = 20
- Length scale σ = 2π ħ c / (k_B T_c) = 7.27 fm, with T_c = 175 MeV
- Mean interparticle distance at T/T_c = 3: ⟨r⟩/σ = 0.06
- De Broglie wavelength Λ ≈ 0.1 σ at T/T_c = 3
- Ratio of potential energy to kinetic energy from simulations: 1 to 3, depending on temperature

## Context

This work extends previous classical nonrelativistic molecular dynamics simulations of a color Coulomb interacting QGP (Shuryak 2004) by incorporating quantum effects and Fermi/Bose statistics via path integral Monte Carlo, a method previously applied to strongly coupled electrodynamic plasmas (Filinov 2001, Bonitz 2003). The results agree with lattice QCD equation-of-state data (Lattice 2009) and reveal liquid-like behavior through pair distribution functions and color correlation functions, showing anti-parallel alignment of color vectors and gluon clustering. The approach enables first-principles study of cluster and bound-state formation in the QGP, though it cannot directly provide dynamical or transport properties.
