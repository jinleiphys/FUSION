# Azimuthal decorrelation in diffractive dijet production

> We calculate the azimuthal angular decorrelation of diffractive dijets in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion, $ep$, and $eA$ collisions to probe non-perturbative diffractive transverse momentum-dependent distributions. Focusing on the dominant semi-inclusive channel with an unobserved semi-hard gluon, we perform an all-order resummation of soft gluon emissions for the transverse energy-energy correlator observable, accounting for both initial and final state radiation. We also analyze heavy-quark pair production and demonstrate the sensitivity of the decorrelation to the jet axis definition. Finally, we provide numerical predictions for relevant kinematics at LHC UPCs, HERA, and the future EIC. Our results demonstrate that the acoplanarity of diffractive dijet production could serve as a promising probe of diffractive transverse momentum-dependent distributions.

# Azimuthal decorrelation in diffractive dijet production

## Key claim

The azimuthal decorrelation of diffractive dijets, measured via the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) or the acoplanarity angle, can serve as a promising probe of diffractive transverse momentum-dependent distributions (DTMDs), but only if the significant broadening from both initial-state and final-state soft gluon radiation is properly accounted for as a perturbative baseline to isolate genuine saturation signals.

## Method

The authors perform an all-order resummation of soft gluon emissions within the diffractive TMD (DTMD) factorization framework, focusing on the dominant semi-inclusive channel where a quark-antiquark pair plus a semi-hard gluon scatter coherently off a nuclear target via pomeron exchange. They derive one-dimensional factorization theorems for the TEEC observable and the dijet acoplanarity, incorporating Sudakov resummation at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, and evaluate the diffractive gluon TMD using the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) parametrization for the dipole amplitude. They also compute heavy-quark pair decorrelation by truncating the Sudakov evolution at the heavy quark mass scale to account for the dead-cone effect, and compare two jet axis definitions: the standard jet axis (SJA) and the winner-take-all (WTA) scheme.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb ultra-peripheral collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the TEEC distribution is computed with jet transverse momentum cuts P_perp > 15 GeV and P_perp > 30 GeV, with jet rapidities restricted to |y_1,2| < 2.4.
- The proton saturation scale is parameterized as Q_sp^2(x) = (x_0/x)^lambda Q_s0^2 with Q_s0^2 = 1 GeV^2, x_0 = 3.04 x 10^(-4), and lambda = 0.288; for a heavy nucleus, Q_sA^2 = c A^(1/3) Q_sp^2 with c ~ 0.5.
- The non-perturbative Sudakov parameters used are g_1 = 0.106 GeV^2, g_2 = 0.84, and Q_0^2 = 2.4 GeV^2, with b_max = 1.5 GeV^(-1) in the b_star prescription.
- The TEEC-specific non-perturbative form factor coefficients are g_0^e = 0.226 GeV^(1/2), g_1^e = 0.463 GeV, and g_2^e = 0.033 GeV^2.
- Predictions are provided for eA collisions at the EIC with sqrt(s) = 89 GeV and P_perp > 3.5 GeV, and for ep collisions at HERA with sqrt(s) = 320 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the DTMD factorization framework developed by Iancu et al. (2021-2023) for semi-inclusive diffractive dijet production, which showed that the tri-jet channel dominates over exclusive production due to the absence of color transparency suppression. It builds on prior studies of azimuthal asymmetries in diffractive processes by Hatta et al. (2016-2021) and on TEEC applications to standard DIS and proton-proton collisions by Gao et al. (2019) and Kang et al. (2023). The analysis enables future experimental tests at LHC ultra-peripheral collisions, HERA, and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, where the TEEC and acoplanarity observables can isolate saturation signals from perturbative radiative backgrounds, particularly through heavy-quark pair production which benefits from the dead-cone suppression of soft radiation.
