# Posterior predictive distributions of neutron-deuteron cross sections

> We quantify the posterior predictive distributions (PPDs) of elastic neutron-deuteron ($nd$) scattering cross sections using nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to and including next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO). These PPDs quantify the spread in $nd$ predictions due to the variability of the low-energy constants (LECs) inferred from $NN$ scattering data. We use the wave-packet continuum discretization method to solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas form of the Faddeev equations for elastic scattering. We draw 100 samples from the PPDs of $nd$ cross sections up to 67 MeV in scattering energy, i.e., in the energy region where the effects of three-nucleon forces are expected to be small. We find that the uncertainty about $NN$ LECs inferred from $NN$ scattering data, when assuming uncorrelated errors, does not translate to significant uncertainty in the low-energy $nd$ continuum. Based on our estimates, the uncertainty of $nd$ predictions are dominated by the $χ$EFT truncation error, at least below N$^3$LO. At this order, the 90% credible interval of the PPD and the truncation error are comparable, although both are very small on an absolute scale.

## Key claim

The uncertainty about NN low-energy constants (LECs) inferred from NN scattering data, when assuming uncorrelated errors, does not translate to significant uncertainty in the low-energy nd continuum; the uncertainty of nd predictions is dominated by the chiral EFT truncation error, at least below N3LO, and at N3LO the 90% credible interval of the posterior predictive distribution and the truncation error are comparable but both very small on an absolute scale.

## Method

The authors used the wave-packet continuum discretization (WPCD) method to solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering, using chiral EFT NN interactions at LO, NLO, NNLO, and N3LO. They drew 100 samples from the posterior predictive distributions (PPDs) of nd cross sections by evaluating the AGS equation for each set of LEC values drawn from previously published Hamiltonian Monte Carlo posterior distributions conditioned on NN scattering data from the Granada database up to 290 MeV. The WPCD method was used with up to 75 wave packets, with extrapolation to 150 wave packets for the MAP predictions, and calculations were performed for scattering energies up to 67 MeV where three-nucleon force effects are expected to be small.

## Key numbers

- 100 samples drawn from each PPD at each chiral order (LO, NLO, NNLO, N3LO)
- Scattering energies studied: 12, 36, and 65 MeV for differential cross section; 10, 35, and 67 MeV for analyzing power
- Average 90% HPDI for differential cross section at 12 MeV: 5.7% (LO), 2.3% (NLO), 0.7% (NNLO), 0.5% (N3LO)
- Chiral EFT breakdown scale set to Lambda_b = 600 MeV, with expansion parameter Q = m_pi/Lambda_b ≈ 0.23 at 12 MeV
- RMS estimate of expansion coefficients at 12 MeV: bar_c = 14.8; at 36 MeV: bar_c = 65.1
- Computational cost: approximately 12 node-hours (384 core-hours) per LEC configuration for ~50 scattering energies below 100 MeV with nwp=75
- Total computational cost: roughly 150k core-hours for 100 LEC samples at four chiral orders
- State space: total angular momentum J ≤ 17/2, both parities, NN total angular momentum J ≤ 3

## Context

This work extends Bayesian methodology for quantifying posterior predictive distributions in nuclear physics, following prior work by Svensson (2021, 2022) on NN scattering cross sections and scattering lengths, and Wesolowski (2021) on few-nucleon systems. It contrasts with frequentist analyses of nucleon-deuteron scattering by LENPIC (2018), Skibinski (2018), and Volkotrub (2020), providing probability densities rather than dispersion estimates. The results enable future full Bayesian analysis of chiral EFT conditioned on experimental data in the Nd continuum, as outlined by Miller (2022), and highlight the need for simultaneous inference of LECs in both the NN and three-nucleon sectors to resolve discrepancies at N3LO.
