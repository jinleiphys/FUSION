# The Rarita--Schwinger field: dressing procedure and spin-parity content

> We obtain in analytical form the dressed propagator of the massive Rarita-Schwinger field taking into account all spin components. We found that the nearest analogy for dressing the Rarita--Schwinger field in spin--1/2 sector is dressing the two Dirac fermions of opposite parity with presence of mutual transitions. The calculation of the self-energy contributions confirms that besides the leading spin--3/2 component the Rarita--Schwinger field contains also two spin--1/2 components of different parity.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical closed-form expression for the dressed propagator of the massive Rarita–Schwinger field that includes all spin components, not just the leading spin-3/2 part. The key finding is that the spin-1/2 sector of the dressed propagator behaves like two Dirac fermions of opposite parity that undergo mutual transitions, rather than being simply unphysical or negligible components.

## Method

The authors construct a new spin-tensor basis (the Λ-basis) built from the projection operators Λ± = (√p² ± p̂)/(2√p²) combined with the standard spin projection operators P^(3/2), P^(1/2)_11, P^(1/2)_22 and the nilpotent operators P^(1/2)_21, P^(1/2)_12. This basis has simple multiplicative properties that allow the Dyson–Schwinger equation for the inverse propagator to be reduced to a set of algebraic equations for scalar coefficients, which are then solved analytically. The authors also analyze the dressing of Dirac fermions in various configurations (single fermion, two fermions of same parity, two fermions of opposite parity) to establish the physical analogy with the Rarita–Schwinger field's spin-1/2 sector.

## Key numbers

* The free Rarita–Schwinger Lagrangian contains an arbitrary parameter A, with the point transformation invariance Ψ^μ → (g^μν + αγ^μγ^ν)Ψ^ν, A → (A − 2α)/(1 + 4α), valid for α ≠ −1/4.
* The Λ-basis decomposition of any spin-tensor S^μν(p) contains 10 independent components (5 projection operators times Λ±), with the first 6 basis elements being projection operators and the remaining 4 being nilpotent.
* The denominators of the dressed propagator coefficients for the spin-1/2 sector are Δ₁ = S̄₃S̄₆ − S̄₇S̄₁₀ and Δ₂ = S̄₄S̄₅ − S̄₈S̄₉, where Δ₂ = Δ₁(√p² → −√p²).
* For the one-loop self-energy of a 1/2⁺ baryon dressed by πN, the imaginary part of the Σ¹ component behaves as q³ near threshold, while Σ² behaves as q¹, where q is the spatial momentum of the πN pair in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to construct dressed propagators for the Rarita–Schwinger field, which had previously been handled either by dressing only the spin-3/2 component while treating spin-1/2 components as bare (Pas95, KS), or by numerical solution of the coupled system (Kor, AKS). The analytical result enables systematic renormalization of all spin components and clarifies the physical interpretation of the spin-1/2 degrees of freedom. The authors note that for Δ(1232) isobar production in Compton scattering, non-leading spin-1/2 contributions were found necessary for data description (Pas95), motivating the need for a complete analytical treatment.
