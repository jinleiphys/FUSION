# Electromagnetic transition strengths in soft deformed nuclei

> Spectroscopic observables such as electromagnetic transitions strengths can be related to the properties of the intrinsic mean-field wave function when the latter are strongly deformed, but the standard rotational formulas break down when the deformation decreases. Nevertheless there is a well-defined, non-zero, spherical limit that can be evaluated in terms of overlaps of mean-field intrinsic deformed wave functions. We examine the transition between the spherical limit and strongly deformed one for a range of nuclei comparing the two limiting formulas with exact projection results. We find a simple criterion for the validity of the rotational formula depending on $<Δ\vec{J}^2>$, the mean square fluctuation in the angular momentum of the intrinsic state. We also propose an interpolation formula which describes the transition strengths over the entire range of deformations, reducing to the two simple expressions in the appropriate limits.

## Key claim

The standard rotational formula for electromagnetic transition strengths breaks down for near-spherical nuclei, but a well-defined non-zero spherical limit exists; the authors derive a simple criterion based on the mean-square angular-momentum fluctuation ⟨ΔJ²⟩ to determine when the rotational formula is valid, and propose an interpolation formula that works across the full range of deformations.

## Method

The authors computed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) intrinsic wave functions for a range of nuclei, imposing axial symmetry and varying the quadrupole deformation β₂ from -0.3 to 0.4 in steps of 0.02 (with a finer 0.01 step in the -0.1 to 0.1 interval). They compared B(E2) transition strengths obtained from the rotational formula, from exact angular momentum projection, and from a newly derived spherical-limit formula based on overlaps of deformed and spherical intrinsic states. For octupole transitions, they used parity-projected wave functions from earlier work (Robledo 2011) and compared projected results with the rotational formula.

## Key numbers

- The ratio B(E2)_ROT/B(E2)_PROJ approaches 1 at large β₂ but goes to zero as β₂ → 0.
- The rotational formula becomes valid (ratio ≈ 3/4) when ⟨ΔJ²⟩ ≈ 10 ℏ², and becomes quite accurate above ⟨ΔJ²⟩ > 15 ℏ².
- For the spherical limit, a least-square fit over 200 nuclei yields B(E2,2⁺→0⁺)_Sph = 0.85 Z (Weisskopf units).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the rotational formula gives B(E3) = 7.1 W.u., while angular momentum projection gives 23.1 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 34 W.u.
- For octupole transitions, the rotational formula works within a factor of 2 for β₂(+) > 0.15; near β₂(+) = 0, the ratio of projected to rotational B(E3) lies between 3 and 8.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the rotational formula’s validity in well-deformed nuclei (Man75, Isl79, Rin82) by systematically examining its breakdown near sphericity. The spherical-limit formula and the interpolation model enable reliable B(E2) estimates without full angular momentum projection, which is computationally expensive. The criterion based on ⟨ΔJ²⟩ provides a practical, easily computed indicator for when the rotational formula can be trusted, and the results are relevant for Bohr Hamiltonian approaches (5DBH) and global studies of octupole correlations (Robledo 2011).
