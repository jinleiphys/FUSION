# Three-cluster dynamics within the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum: How many-body correlations and $α$-clustering shape $^6$He

> We realize the treatment of bound and continuum nuclear systems in the proximity of a three-body breakup threshold within the ab initio framework of the no-core shell model with continuum. Many-body eigenstates obtained from the diagonalization of the Hamiltonian within the harmonic-oscillator expansion of the no-core shell model are coupled with continuous microscopic three-cluster states to correctly describe the nuclear wave function both in the interior and asymptotic regions. We discuss the formalism in detail and give algebraic expressions for the case of core+$n$+$n$ systems. Using similarity-renormalization-group evolved nucleon-nucleon interactions, we analyze the role of $^4$He+$n$+$n$ clustering and many-body correlations in the ground and low-lying continuum states of the Borromean $^6$He nucleus, and study the dependence of the energy spectrum on the resolution scale of the interaction. We show that $^6$He small binding energy and extended radii compatible with experiment can be obtained simultaneously, without recurring to extrapolations. We also find that a significant portion of the ground-state energy and the narrow width of the first $2^+$ resonance stem from many-body correlations that can be interpreted as core-excitation effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) can simultaneously reproduce the small binding energy and extended radii of the Borromean nucleus $^6$He without extrapolations, and that a significant portion of the ground-state energy and the narrow width of the first $2^+$ resonance arise from many-body correlations interpretable as core-excitation effects, using similarity-renormalization-group evolved nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors extend the NCSMC formalism to treat three-cluster dynamics by coupling many-body eigenstates from the no-core shell model (NCSM) diagonalization in a harmonic-oscillator basis with continuous microscopic three-cluster states describing core+$n$+$n$ configurations. They derive algebraic expressions for the overlap and Hamiltonian coupling form factors for core+$n$+$n$ systems using second quantization and Slater-determinant basis states, then solve the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem after orthogonalization and transformation to hyperspherical coordinates.

## Key numbers

* $^6$He ground-state binding energy: 0.975 MeV (experimental value), obtained simultaneously with extended radii without extrapolations
* First $2^+$ resonance: narrow width, with a significant portion stemming from many-body correlations interpreted as core-excitation effects
* The condition $(-1)^{\ell_x+s_{23}+T_{23}} = -1$ is imposed for the two-neutron subsystem to enforce antisymmetrization
* The hyperspherical harmonics are eigenfunctions of the grand-angular operator with eigenvalue $K(K+4)$

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC framework previously developed for binary-cluster systems (Nollett 2007, Quaglioni 2008) and the three-cluster formalism introduced in Quaglioni (2013) and Romero-Redondo (2014). It enables ab initio calculations of Borromean halo nuclei such as $^6$He and opens the door to describing three-body breakup reactions including $^4$He$(2n,\gamma)^6$He radiative capture and $^3$H$(n,2n)^2$H fusion processes relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis and fusion energy applications.
