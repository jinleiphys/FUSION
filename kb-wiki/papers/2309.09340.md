# Scissors mode in transuranium elements

> The scissors mode is investigated in the actinides region, including even-even superheavy nuclei up to $^{256}$No, within the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) approach. The solution of TDHFB equations by the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method predicts a splitting of the scissors mode into three intermingled branches due to spin degrees of freedom. Both the calculated energy centroid and integrated $M1$ strength in $^{254}$No are in good agreement with the results of recent measurements performed by the Oslo method. The energy centroids and summed $B(M1)$ values for others transuranium nuclides are predicted. The calculations are performed also for $^{232}$Th and $^{236,238}$U isotopes using an updated compilation of deformation parameters. The results are compared with that obtained previously by WFM theory and with the latest experimental data. Progress has been achieved in theoretical understanding of the origins of double-humped structure of scissors spectrum observed in the Actinides.

## Key claim

The Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) approach solved by the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method predicts that the scissors mode in actinides splits into three intermingled branches due to spin degrees of freedom, and the calculated energy centroid and integrated M1 strength in $^{254}$No agree with recent Oslo method measurements, though the agreement depends on the deformation parameter $\beta_2$ used (0.27 vs 0.3).

## Method

The authors solved the TDHFB equations using the Wigner Function Moments method, which integrates phase-space moments of the Wigner transforms of normal and anomalous density matrices weighted by functions of position and momentum. The model Hamiltonian included a harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit potential plus separable quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions, with pairing correlations incorporated. The resulting linearized equations for isoscalar and isovector collective variables were solved to obtain energies and transition strengths for $1^+$ excitations in even-even transuranium nuclei.

## Key numbers

- In $^{254}$No, three low-lying magnetic $1^+$ excitations are predicted at energies 1.74 MeV, 2.50 MeV, and 3.04 MeV, with $B(M1)$ strengths of 3.00 $\mu_N^2$, 5.09 $\mu_N^2$, and 2.41 $\mu_N^2$ respectively, using $\beta_2=0.27$
- The calculated summed $M1$ strength for $^{254}$No is 10.50 $\mu_N^2$ with centroid at $\bar E=2.41$ MeV (using $\beta_2=0.27$); with $\beta_2=0.3$ the summed strength increases to 13.40 $\mu_N^2$ with centroid at 2.60 MeV
- The experimental Oslo method result for $^{254}$No gives total strength $B(M1)=11.8(19)$ $\mu_N^2$ with centroid at $\bar E\simeq 2.5$ MeV
- For $^{232}$Th, the WFM calculation gives $\bar E=2.40$ MeV and $\sum B(M1)=8.77$ $\mu_N^2$, compared to Oslo experiment $\bar E=2.23(14)$ MeV and $\sum B(M1)=9.5(26)$ $\mu_N^2$
- For $^{238}$U, the WFM calculation gives $\bar E=2.56$ MeV and $\sum B(M1)=11.67$ $\mu_N^2$, compared to Oslo experiment $\bar E=2.24(15)$ MeV and $\sum B(M1)=9.4(16)$ $\mu_N^2$
- For transuranium nuclei from Pu to No, the summed $M1$ strength ranges from 11.46 $\mu_N^2$ ($^{238}$Pu) to 14.11 $\mu_N^2$ ($^{252}$Cf), with centroids between 2.43 and 2.64 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous WFM method calculations by Balbutsev and Molodtsova (2022) that explained the double-humped structure of the scissors spectrum in actinides, now applying the formalism to superheavy nuclei up to $^{256}$No. The results are compared with recent Oslo method measurements on $^{254}$No by the Oslo group (2023), as well as with NRF experiments on $^{232}$Th by Adekola (2011) and $^{236,238}$U by Heil (2008) and Hammo (2018). The calculations enable theoretical predictions for scissors mode properties in transuranium elements where experimental data are scarce due to low availability and radioactive nature of these nuclei.
