# Very low-energy nucleon + 16O coupled-channel scattering: results with a phenomenological vibrational model

> We employ a collective vibration coupled-channel model to describe the nucleon-16O cluster systems, obtaining low-excitation spectra for 17O and 17F. Bound and resonance states of the compound systems have been deduced, showing good agreement with experimental spectra. Low energy scattering cross sections of neutrons and protons from 16O also have been calculated and the results compare well with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a phenomenological collective vibration coupled-channel model, implemented via the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method, can reproduce low-excitation spectra and very low-energy scattering cross sections for the nucleon-16O systems, but the agreement is best below approximately 1 MeV and the model's deformation parameters (beta_2, beta_3) may need to be treated as free parameters rather than fixed to electromagnetic transition values to achieve optimal results.

## Method

The authors applied the MCAS method with a Woods-Saxon nucleon-core interaction potential, coupling a valence nucleon to five low-lying collective vibrational states of the 16O core (the 0+ ground state, 0+2 state at 6.049 MeV, 3-1 state at 6.13 MeV, 2+1 state at 6.92 MeV, and 1-1 state at 7.12 MeV). They used orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP) to enforce the Pauli principle for occupied single-particle orbits, and included both first-order and second-order terms in the deformation expansion of the interaction. The potential parameters and deformation parameters beta_2 and beta_3 were adjusted to fit experimental spectra and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square deviation for the lowest 30 energy levels in 17O is mu_30 = 1.2371 MeV; for the lowest 20 levels, mu_20 = 1.1240 MeV
- For 17F, the root-mean-square deviation for the lowest 30 levels is mu_30 = 1.0419 MeV; for the lowest 20 levels, mu_20 = 0.9201 MeV
- The ground state of 17O is calculated at -4.1432 MeV (experimental: -4.1436 MeV); the ground state of 17F is calculated at -0.8079 MeV (experimental: -0.6005 MeV)
- The beta_2 deformation parameter used in the optimal fit is 0.21; the beta_3 parameter is 0.42
- The first large resonance in n+16O scattering, labeled 3/2-, appears at the correct position in MCAS but with a calculated width much smaller than the experimental neutron width of approximately 40 keV
- The Coulomb residual displacement energy between the experimental and calculated ground state of 17F is 208 keV

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to rotational model systems such as n+12C (Amos et al., 2003) and other nucleon-plus-nucleus systems (Canton et al., 2005, 2006), to a vibrational model description of the 16O core. It is complementary to microscopic particle-vibration coupling (PVC) methods developed by others (e.g., Ka12, Bl15, Nh15) that treat core excitations with RPA but do not adequately describe cross sections below 5 MeV. The results enable future application of MCAS to particle-vibration couplings generated microscopically and inclusion of additional multipolarities such as dipole or monopole excitation modes.
