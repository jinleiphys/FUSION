# Dynamically-generated baryon resonances with heavy flavor

> We study baryon resonances with heavy flavor in a molecular approach, thus as dynamically generated by meson-baryon scattering. This is accomplished by using a unitary coupled-channel model taking, as bare interaction, the extension to four flavors and spin symmetry of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction potential. A special attention is paid to the inclusion of heavy-quark spin symmetry and to the studies of the generated baryon resonances which complete the heavy-quark spin multiplets. We reproduce few charmed and strange baryon resonances found experimentally, that is, Λ_c(2595), Λ_c(2625), Ξ_c(2790), Ξ_c(2815), and make predictions for more states. The bottom-flavored Λ_b(5912) and Λ_b(5920) states, found by the LHCb collaboration, are also obtained by our model, thus these resonances can be interpreted as molecular states. We also study Ξ_b resonances, which belong to the same SU(3) x HQSS multiplets as the observed Λ_b particles. Finally we analyze hidden-charm baryon resonances. In this sector, we predict seven N-like and five Δ-like states with masses around 4 GeV, most of them as bound states, and compare them with predictions from other models. The predicted states can be searched for in future experiments that involve studies of heavy-flavor physics, e.g. PANDA/FAIR, where charm physics will be analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that heavy-flavor baryon resonances can be interpreted as molecular states dynamically generated by meson-baryon scattering, but this interpretation depends on the model's implementation of heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) constraints, which break the full SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry down to SU(6) x HQSS by removing annihilation-creation terms involving heavy quarks.

## Method

The authors use a unitary coupled-channel model based on an extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) contact interaction to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry for four flavors and three colors, then break this symmetry to SU(6) x HQSS by eliminating channels that violate heavy-quark spin symmetry. They solve the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, regularizing meson-baryon propagators with a subtraction point method, and identify baryon resonances as poles of the scattering amplitude on the complex energy plane, with masses and widths extracted from pole positions and residues.

## Key numbers

- Lambda_c(2595) appears at 2618.8 - i 0.6 MeV (pole position), with experimental width 3.6^{+2.0}_{-1.3} MeV not reproduced due to missing three-body decay
- A second J=1/2 Lambda_c resonance found at 2617.3 MeV with width about 90 MeV, forming a two-pole structure with Lambda_c(2595)
- Lambda_c(2625) identified as J=3/2 pole at 2666.6 - i 27 MeV, coupling strongly to Sigma_c* pi
- Predicted Lambda_c resonance at 2828.4 MeV with small width
- Three predicted J=1/2 Sigma_c resonances at masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV; first narrow, others broad (Gamma=188 and 87 MeV)
- Two predicted J=3/2 Sigma_c states: bound state at 2568.4 MeV and broad resonance (Gamma=67 MeV) at 2692.9 MeV
- Seven N-like hidden-charm states with masses between 3900 and 4050 MeV, five as bound states and two with widths 0.1 and 2.8 MeV
- Five Delta-like hidden-charm bound states with masses between 4000 and 4310 MeV; two heaviest at 4306.2 and 4306.8 MeV near Delta J/psi threshold (4306.9 MeV)
- Lambda_b(5912) and Lambda_b(5920) reproduced as bound states
- Predicted Lambda_b bound state at 5797.6 MeV with no strong decay, and bound state at 6009.3 MeV decaying to Sigma_b pi
- Three predicted spin-1/2 Xi_b states at masses 5874, 6035.4, and 6072.8 MeV; one spin-3/2 Xi_b bound state at 6043.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel approach previously applied to charmed baryon resonances (PaperOpen, 2013) and hidden-charm states (PaperHidden, 2013) to include bottom-flavored baryons, reproducing the LHCb discoveries of Lambda_b(5912) and Lambda_b(5920) as molecular states. The predicted N-like hidden-charm resonances are about 500 MeV heavier than those from the zero-range vector exchange model of Hofmann and Lutz, while about 400 MeV lighter than predictions from the hidden-gauge formalism of Oset and collaborators; the masses lie close to constituent quark model predictions by Yuan (2012) using hyperfine chiral interactions. The results provide testable predictions for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, where charm physics will be analyzed.
