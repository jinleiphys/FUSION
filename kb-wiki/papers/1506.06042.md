# Combination of Measurements of Inclusive Deep Inelastic $e^{\pm}p$ Scattering Cross Sections and QCD Analysis of HERA Data

> A combination is presented of all inclusive deep inelastic cross sections previously published by the H1 and ZEUS collaborations at HERA for neutral and charged current $e^{\pm}p$ scattering for zero beam polarisation. The data were taken at proton beam energies of 920, 820, 575 and 460 GeV and an electron beam energy of 27.5 GeV. The data correspond to an integrated luminosity of about 1 fb$^{-1}$ and span six orders of magnitude in negative four-momentum-transfer squared, $Q^2$, and Bjorken $x$. The correlations of the systematic uncertainties were evaluated and taken into account for the combination. The combined cross sections were input to QCD analyses at leading order, next-to-leading order and at next-to-next-to-leading order, providing a new set of parton distribution functions, called HERAPDF2.0. In addition to the experimental uncertainties, model and parameterisation uncertainties were assessed for these parton distribution functions. Variants of HERAPDF2.0 with an alternative gluon parameterisation, HERAPDF2.0AG, and using fixed-flavour-number schemes, HERAPDF2.0FF, are presented. The analysis was extended by including HERA data on charm and jet production, resulting in the variant HERAPDF2.0Jets. The inclusion of jet-production cross sections made a simultaneous determination of these parton distributions and the strong coupling constant possible, resulting in $α_s(M_Z)=0.1183 \pm 0.0009 {\rm(exp)} \pm 0.0005{\rm (model/parameterisation)} \pm 0.0012{\rm (hadronisation)} ^{+0.0037}_{-0.0030}{\rm (scale)}$. An extraction of $xF_3^{γZ}$ and results on electroweak unification and scaling violations are also presented.

## Key claim

The combination of all inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross sections from H1 and ZEUS at HERA, spanning six orders of magnitude in both $Q^2$ and Bjorken $x$, yields a new set of parton distribution functions (HERAPDF2.0) that is independent of heavy nuclear corrections and provides the most precise determination of proton structure from electron-proton scattering alone, with the strong coupling constant extracted simultaneously with jet data giving $\alpha_s(M_Z)=0.1183 \pm 0.0009 {\rm(exp)} \pm 0.0005{\rm (model/parameterisation)} \pm 0.0012{\rm (hadronisation)} ^{+0.0037}_{-0.0030}{\rm (scale)}$.

## Method

The H1 and ZEUS collaborations combined all previously published inclusive neutral current and charged current $e^{\pm}p$ deep inelastic scattering cross sections for zero beam polarisation, using data taken at proton beam energies of 920, 820, 575 and 460 GeV with an electron beam energy of 27.5 GeV. The correlations of systematic uncertainties were evaluated and taken into account using the HERAverager package, and the combined cross sections were input to QCD analyses at leading order, next-to-leading order, and next-to-next-to-leading order within the DGLAP formalism using the HERAFitter package. The analysis was extended by including HERA data on charm and jet production, enabling a simultaneous determination of parton distribution functions and the strong coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity of about 1 fb$^{-1}$ from the combined H1 and ZEUS data sets
- Cross sections span $0.045 \leq Q^2 \leq 50000$ GeV$^2$ and $6 \cdot 10^{-7} \leq x_{\rm Bj} \leq 0.65$ for neutral current interactions
- Charged current cross sections cover $200 \leq Q^2 \leq 50000$ GeV$^2$ and $1.3 \cdot 10^{-2} \leq x_{\rm Bj} \leq 0.40$
- Proton beam energies of 920, 820, 575 and 460 GeV, corresponding to centre-of-mass energies of approximately 320, 300, 251 and 225 GeV
- Strong coupling constant from the jet-inclusive analysis: $\alpha_s(M_Z)=0.1183 \pm 0.0009 {\rm(exp)} \pm 0.0005{\rm (model/parameterisation)} \pm 0.0012{\rm (hadronisation)} ^{+0.0037}_{-0.0030}{\rm (scale)}$

## Context

This work extends the previous HERAPDF1.0 analysis (H1 and ZEUS Collaborations, 2010) which was based only on HERA I data, and the preliminary HERAPDF1.5 set which used some still-preliminary HERA II data. The new HERAPDF2.0 set provides the most comprehensive determination of proton parton distribution functions from electron-proton scattering alone, independent of heavy nuclear corrections that affect global PDF fits from groups such as JR, MSTW/MMHT, CTEQ/CT, ABM, and NNPDF. The results enable improved precision for predictions at the Large Hadron Collider and other hadron colliders, and the extraction of $xF_3^{\gamma Z}$ provides new tests of electroweak unification and scaling violations.
