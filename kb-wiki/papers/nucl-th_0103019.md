# Quenching of resonance production in heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 A GeV

> We study the pion production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies within the coupled resonance BUU model. The standard calculation overpredicts the pion multiplicity in the central Au+Au collision at 1 A GeV by about a factor of 2, which is interpreted as a signature for the quenching of the resonance production/absorption in nucleon-nucleon collisions at high nuclear density. Results of the calculations taking into account a specific form of quenching are presented for pion spectra from light and heavy systems as well as for the pion in-plane and out-of-plane flow.

# Quenching of resonance production in heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 A GeV

## Key claim

The standard BUU transport calculation overpredicts the pion multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions at 1 A GeV by about a factor of 2, and this discrepancy can be resolved by introducing a density-dependent quenching factor that reduces the resonance production/absorption rates in nucleon-nucleon collisions at high nuclear density (above approximately 1.5 times the equilibrium density). The quenching is implemented via a piecewise linear function of density that leaves the matrix element unchanged at subnuclear densities but suppresses it linearly above the equilibrium density.

## Method

The authors used a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that explicitly propagates nucleons, baryon resonances (up to D35(2350)), hyperons, and mesons (pi, eta, omega, K, rho, sigma). They introduced a universal density-dependent scaling factor kappa(rho) that multiplies the squared matrix elements for all resonance production/absorption processes N1 N2 <-> N3 R4, non-resonant one-pion production, double Delta production, and resonance-nucleon scattering. The quenching function was chosen as kappa(rho) = min(1, max(0, 1 + beta(rho/rho0 - 1))) with beta = -2, which suppresses all resonance-related binary collisions at densities above 1.5 rho0 while leaving them unchanged at subnuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- Standard calculation gives pion multiplicity of approximately 60 in central Au+Au at 1 A GeV, compared to the experimental value of approximately 34 (a factor of about 2 overprediction)
- The quenching parameter beta = -2 reproduces the proper pion multiplicity; this suppresses resonance production for densities rho >= 1.5 rho0 (where rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is equilibrium nuclear matter density)
- The maximum number of Delta-resonances in the quenched calculation is reduced to about 35% of the standard calculation value
- The soft momentum-dependent mean field (SM) with incompressibility K = 220 MeV was used for all calculations
- The nonresonant string mechanism for pion production becomes dominant for invariant collision energies sqrt(s) >= 2.6 GeV, corresponding to beam energy 1.73 A GeV neglecting Fermi motion

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (Teis et al., 1997; Larionov et al., 2000) and addresses the known tendency of transport models to overpredict pion multiplicities at around 1 A GeV (Pelte et al., 1997). The quenching mechanism contrasts with earlier theoretical predictions that the Delta excitation cross section should increase with density (Brown et al., 1988). The authors note that collisional broadening of the Delta-resonance (Effenberger et al., 1997) was found to be too small to provide the needed reduction, and they identify the need for good calculations of inelastic nucleon-nucleon scattering in nuclear matter including in-medium vertex corrections as an open problem.
