# Theoretical study of the elastic breakup of weakly bound nuclei at near barrier energies

> We have performed CDCC calculations for collisions of $^{7}$Li projectiles on $^{59}$Co, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb targets at near-barrier energies, to assess the importance of the Coulomb and the nuclear couplings in the breakup of $^{7}$Li, as well as the Coulomb-nuclear interference. We have also investigated scaling laws, expressing the dependence of the cross sections on the charge and the mass of the target. This work is complementary to the one previously reported by us on the breakup of $^{6}$Li. Here we explore the similarities and differences between the results for the two Lithium isotopes. The relevance of the Coulomb dipole strength at low energy for the two-cluster projectile is investigated in details.

# Theoretical study of the elastic breakup of weakly bound nuclei at near barrier energies

## Key claim

CDCC calculations for 7Li projectiles on 59Co, 144Sm and 208Pb targets at near-barrier energies show that the total breakup cross sections for 6Li are larger than those for 7Li despite 7Li having a non-zero low-energy Coulomb dipole strength, because the lower breakup threshold of 6Li (1.47 MeV versus 2.47 MeV for 7Li) is the predominant factor determining the breakup magnitude.

## Method

Continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations were performed for 7Li projectiles on 59Co, 144Sm and 208Pb targets at near-barrier energies, assuming direct breakup into an alpha particle and a triton with separation energy 2.47 MeV. The continuum was discretized into momentum bins with partial waves up to l_max = 4, and the Coulomb and nuclear components of the breakup cross section were separated by switching off either the nuclear or Coulomb part of the coupling interaction in the CDCC calculations. The Sao Paulo potential was used for the alpha-target and tritium-target optical potentials with no adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- Breakup threshold (Q-value) for 7Li: 2.47 MeV; for 6Li: 1.47 MeV
- For 7Li + 208Pb at E/V_B = 0.84: Coulomb component sigma_C = 7.28 mb, nuclear component sigma_N = 0.90 mb, total breakup sigma_bup = 4.51 mb
- For 7Li + 208Pb at E/V_B = 1.00: sigma_C = 11.20 mb, sigma_N = 2.65 mb, sigma_bup = 10.31 mb
- For 7Li + 208Pb at E/V_B = 1.30: sigma_C = 31.64 mb, sigma_N = 11.88 mb, sigma_bup = 30.48 mb
- For 7Li + 144Sm at E/V_B = 0.84: sigma_C = 2.49 mb, sigma_N = 0.51 mb, sigma_bup = 0.88 mb
- For 7Li + 59Co at E/V_B = 0.84: sigma_C = 0.17 mb, sigma_N = 0.05 mb, sigma_bup = 0.23 mb
- For 7Li + 59Co at E/V_B = 1.30: sigma_C = 4.34 mb, sigma_N = 7.08 mb, sigma_bup = 12.04 mb
- For 208Pb target at E/V_B = 0.84: nuclear breakup of 6Li = 8.8 mb, nuclear breakup of 7Li = 0.9 mb
- B(E1) for 7Li: approximately 10 fm^2 e^2; for 6Li the dipole strength vanishes identically

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDCC study of 6Li breakup (Otomar 2013) to 7Li projectiles, which have a non-zero low-energy Coulomb dipole strength due to their alpha-triton cluster structure, unlike 6Li where the dipole strength vanishes. The results confirm the scaling laws found earlier by Otomar (2013) and explained by Hussein (2013): the nuclear component of the breakup cross section scales linearly with A_T^(1/3) and the Coulomb component scales linearly with Z_T at fixed E/V_B. The comparison between the two lithium isotopes reveals that the lower breakup threshold of 6Li dominates over the dipole strength advantage of 7Li in determining total breakup cross sections, while the Coulomb-to-nuclear ratio is systematically larger for 7Li. This work enables future studies of breakup mechanisms for other two-cluster projectiles and highlights the need for improved methods to separate Coulomb and nuclear components within coupled channels theory.
