# Pion Double Charge Exchange on Nuclei and Inelastic Rescatterings

> The inclusive pion double charge exchange process on nuclei is considered in the framework of the Gribov-Glauber approach. It is shown that inelastic rescatterings related to production of two (and more) pions in the intermediate state give an important contribution to the process of double charge exchange at kinetic energies above ~ 0.5 GeV. This mechanism dominates at energies greater or about 1 GeV and allows to explain the weak energy dependence of the double charge exchange cross section observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that inelastic rescatterings involving the production of two (or more) pions in the intermediate state give an important contribution to pion double charge exchange (DCX) on nuclei at kinetic energies above about 0.5 GeV, and that this mechanism dominates at energies greater than or about 1 GeV, explaining the weak energy dependence of the DCX cross section observed experimentally. The authors qualify that their simple estimates neglect interference terms and that at energies below about 1 GeV, contributions from baryon resonances and other effects may be substantial.

## Method

The authors used the Gribov-Glauber approach to describe inclusive pion double charge exchange on nuclei, considering diagrams for sequential single charge exchange and inelastic rescatterings with intermediate states such as pi0, eta0, pi+pi-, and pi0pi0. They estimated the contributions of these inelastic rescatterings using experimental data on particle production in pi- p interactions, calculating corrections to the standard sequential charge exchange cross section for the reaction A(pi-, pi+)X on 16O at kinetic energies from 0.4 to 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard sequential single charge exchange model for pion DCX, which predicted a strong decrease of the cross section at higher energies, to explain the weak energy dependence observed experimentally at ITEP up to 1.1 GeV (Krutenkova et al., 1995). The authors compare their inelastic rescattering mechanism with the meson exchange current model (Oset et al., 1987) and find that the latter gives too small a contribution at energies above 0.5 GeV. The results enable future experimental studies of DCX at energies up to 2-5 GeV, where the cross section is predicted to remain measurable, and suggest that measurements of the A-dependence could distinguish between the sequential mechanism and pion interactions with correlated nucleon pairs.
