# The anomalous magnetic moment of the muon: status and perspectives

> We review the status of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon as a precision probe of physics beyond the Standard Model (SM) after the release of the final results from the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (FNAL) Muon $g-2$ experiment and the second White Paper of the Muon $g-2$ Theory Initiative. While the SM prediction requires further improvements by a factor of four to fully leverage the sensitivity achieved in experiment, the FNAL measurement will set the standard for many years to come, and we discuss a variety of features of the experimental campaign that made this achievement possible. In going forward, we discuss current efforts to improve the SM prediction, and imagine how an experiment would have to be devised to surpass 124 ppb in precision.

## Key claim

The FNAL Muon g-2 experiment has achieved a final experimental precision of 124 ppb, but the Standard Model prediction currently has an uncertainty that is about a factor of four larger than the experimental uncertainty, significantly diluting the sensitivity to physics beyond the Standard Model; if the theory uncertainty could be reduced to match experiment, the resulting sensitivity would reach the naive BSM benchmark up to a factor of three.

## Method

The authors review the experimental techniques used in the FNAL E989 muon g-2 experiment, which measures the anomalous precession frequency of muons stored in a magnetic storage ring at the "magic momentum" of 3.094 GeV, where the electric-field focusing correction vanishes. The precession frequency is extracted from the time and energy of decay positrons detected by 24 electromagnetic calorimeters, while the magnetic field is mapped using pulsed proton NMR probes and expressed as the proton spin precession frequency averaged over the stored muon distribution. The ratio of these frequencies, combined with fundamental constants, yields the anomalous magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- FNAL experimental world average: a_mu(exp) = [value] x 10^{-11} (dominated by FNAL E989, statistical uncertainty 98 ppb)
- Total experimental uncertainty: 127 ppb (combining 111 ppb from precession frequency and 57 ppb from magnetic field, plus 23 ppb from external parameters)
- Standard Model prediction uncertainty: larger than experimental by approximately a factor of four
- Benchmark BSM sensitivity at 1 TeV: a_mu(BSM) = 7.1 x 10^{-11} for quadratic mass scaling
- Magic momentum: P_0 = 3.094 GeV (gamma = 29.3, gamma*tau_mu = 64.4 microseconds)
- Cyclotron period: 149 ns
- Stored muon momentum width: delta P/P_0 = 0.15%
- Approximately 4000 muons stored per fill at 11 Hz repetition rate
- Electric field and pitch corrections to raw precession frequency: +538 ppb total
- Magnetic field transient corrections: -58 ppb
- More than 300 billion decay positrons analyzed

## Context

This review summarizes the final results from the FNAL Muon g-2 experiment (E989), which follows the BNL E821 experiment and the earlier CERN III experiment, each achieving progressively higher precision. The paper discusses the second White Paper of the Muon g-2 Theory Initiative (Aliberti 2025) that compiled the current Standard Model prediction, and it outlines ongoing efforts to improve the theory prediction by a factor of four to match the experimental sensitivity. The work enables future experiments that could surpass 124 ppb precision, including the planned J-PARC experiment that would use a different technique with lower-momentum muons and no electric quadrupole focusing.
