# Soft Bootstrap and Effective Field Theories

> The soft bootstrap program aims to construct consistent effective field theories (EFT's) by recursively imposing the desired soft limit on tree-level scattering amplitudes through on-shell recursion relations. A prime example is the leading two-derivative operator in the EFT of $\text{SU} (N)\times \text{SU} (N)/\text{SU} (N)$ nonlinear sigma model (NLSM), where $ \mathcal{O} (p^2)$ amplitudes with an arbitrary multiplicity of external particles can be soft-bootstrapped. We extend the program to $ \mathcal{O} (p^4)$ operators and introduce the "soft blocks," which are the seeds for soft bootstrap. The number of soft blocks coincides with the number of independent operators at a given order in the derivative expansion and the incalculable Wilson coefficient emerges naturally. We also uncover a new soft-constructible EFT involving the "multi-trace" operator at the leading two-derivative order, which is matched to $\text{SO} (N+1)/ \text{SO} (N)$ NLSM. In addition, we consider Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) terms, the existence of which, or the lack thereof, depends on the number of flavors in the EFT, after a novel application of Bose symmetry. Remarkably, we find agreements with group-theoretic considerations on the existence of WZW terms in $\text{SU} (N)$ NLSM for $N\ge 3$ and the absence of WZW terms in $\text{SO} (N)$ NLSM for $N\neq 5$.

## Key claim

The paper extends the soft bootstrap program to higher-derivative operators in effective field theories (EFTs) by introducing "soft blocks" as seeds for recursion, and demonstrates that the number of soft blocks matches the number of independent operators at a given derivative order, with the incalculable Wilson coefficient emerging naturally from the on-shell construction.

## Method

The authors apply on-shell soft recursion relations to construct tree-level scattering amplitudes for multi-scalar EFTs, starting from seed amplitudes (soft blocks) that satisfy the Adler's zero condition. They classify soft blocks at the two-derivative and four-derivative orders, including both parity-even and parity-odd (Wess-Zumino-Witten) terms, and verify consistency by checking that the constructed amplitudes are independent of arbitrary parameters in the all-leg momentum shift. The resulting EFTs are matched to nonlinear sigma models (NLSMs) based on specific coset structures.

## Key numbers

* The leading two-derivative soft block for the single-trace case is uniquely determined as \( \mathcal{S}^{(2)}(1,2,3,4) = c_0 \, s_{13} / f^2 \), where \( c_0 \) is a constant parameter and \( f \) is the decay constant.
* The double-trace soft block at two-derivative order is \( \mathcal{S}^{(2)}(1,2|3,4) = d_0 \, s_{12} / f^2 \), with \( d_0 \) a free parameter.
* The 6-point single-trace partial amplitude constructed from the soft block is \( M^{(2)}(1,2,3,4,5,6) = -\frac{c_0^2}{f^4} \left[ \frac{s_{13} s_{46}}{P^2_{123}} + \frac{s_{24} s_{15}}{P^2_{234}} + \frac{s_{35} s_{26}}{P^2_{345}} - P^2_{135} \right] \).
* The 6-point double-trace partial amplitude is \( M^{(2)}(1,2|3,4|5,6) = -\frac{d_0^2}{f^4} \left[ s_{12} s_{56} \left(\frac{1}{P^2_{124}} + \frac{1}{P^2_{123}}\right) + s_{12} s_{34} \left(\frac{1}{P^2_{125}} + \frac{1}{P^2_{126}}\right) + s_{34} s_{56} \left(\frac{1}{P^2_{134}} + \frac{1}{P^2_{135}}\right) \right] \).
* Soft blocks at four-derivative order or less exist only for \( n \le 5 \) external legs, because a polynomial of degree \( k \le 4 \) cannot have \( n \ge 6 \) distinct roots from the Adler's zero condition.

## Context

This work extends the soft bootstrap program initiated by Cheung et al. (2015, 2016) for single-scalar EFTs to multi-scalar theories with higher-derivative operators, building on earlier efforts by Susskind and Frye (1970) who first used Adler's zero to construct pion amplitudes. The paper introduces a new soft-constructible EFT involving multi-trace operators at two-derivative order, matched to the \( \mathrm{SO}(N+1)/\mathrm{SO}(N) \) NLSM, which contrasts with the standard \( \mathrm{SU}(N) \times \mathrm{SU}(N) / \mathrm{SU}(N) \) case. The results enable future studies of double-copy structures and BCJ relations for higher-derivative operators in scalar EFTs, and open the possibility of exploring similar features for the fundamental representation of \( \mathrm{SO}(N) \).
