# QCD in strong magnetic fields: fluctuations of conserved charges and equation of state

> We present continuum-estimated (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results for second-order fluctuations of conserved charges and the leading-order equation of state in the presence of strong magnetic fields at nonzero baryon chemical potential, using the HISQ action at the physical pion mass. The baryon-electric charge correlation $蠂^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ exhibits striking sensitivity to the magnetic field: $R_{cp}$-like double ratios $蠂^{\rm BQ}_{11}/蠂^{\rm Q}_{2}$ and $蠂^{\rm BQ}_{11}/蠂^{\rm QS}_{11}$ reach enhancements of $\sim2$ and $\sim2.25$ at $eB \simeq 8M_蟺^2$ along the transition line, establishing $蠂^{\rm BQ}_{11}$ as a magnetometer of QCD. # QCD in strong magnetic fields: fluctuations of conserved charges and equation of state

## Key claim
Second-order fluctuations of conserved charges and the leading-order equation of state in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with physical pion mass show that the baryon-electric charge correlation chi^{BQ}_{11} can serve as a magnetometer of QCD, with R_{cp}-like double ratios reaching enhancements of approximately 2 and 2.25 at eB approximately 8 M_pi^2 along the transition line, though these results are continuum estimates from finite lattice spacings and the magnetic sensitivity is partially reduced when emulating experimental detector acceptances.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using the HISQ action at the physical pion mass on 32^3 x 8 and 48^3 x 12 lattices, implementing a constant U(1) magnetic background. They computed second-order generalized susceptibilities chi^{BQS}_{ijk} via Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure in conserved-charge chemical potentials, then constructed HRG-based proxy observables mapping net conserved charges onto detectable final-state hadrons (protons, pions, kaons) with kinematic cuts emulating STAR and ALICE detector acceptances. Under strangeness neutrality and isospin asymmetry conditions relevant for heavy-ion collisions, they determined the leading-order chemical potential ratio q_1 and pressure coefficient P_2 for magnetic field strengths up to eB approximately 0.8 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- Enhancement of approximately 2 for the double ratio chi^{BQ}_{11}/chi^{Q}_{2} at eB approximately 8 M_pi^2 along the transition line
- Enhancement of approximately 2.25 for the double ratio chi^{BQ}_{11}/chi^{QS}_{11} at eB approximately 8 M_pi^2 along the transition line
- HRG-based proxy observables with kinematic cuts retain approximately 80% of the lattice QCD magnetic sensitivity
- Magnetic field strengths explored up to eB approximately 0.8 GeV^2, equivalent to approximately 45 M_pi^2
- Temperature-band crossings in q_1 emerge at eB approximately 0.15 GeV^2
- In the magnetized ideal gas limit, -q_1 saturates to 0.2308
- For the ratio P_2(r)/P_2(r=0.5), suppression of at most approximately 10% observed at r=0, and enhancement of approximately 50% at r=1 in the strong magnetic field limit
- Non-monotonic structures in P_2 appear at eB approximately 0.79 GeV^2
- Crossings among fixed-temperature bands in P_2 emerge around eB approximately 0.6 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of conserved-charge fluctuations that used heavier-than-physical pion mass (M_pi approximately 220 MeV) at a single lattice spacing (Ding 2021) and more recent physical pion mass calculations (Ding 2023, Ding 2025). It provides continuum-estimated benchmarks that go beyond effective model studies (Adhikari 2024) and complements finite-lattice-spacing results for the QCD equation of state in magnetic backgrounds (Borsanyi 2023) and studies at imaginary chemical potential (Astrakhantsev 2024, MarquesValois 2025). The results enable direct comparison with experimental measurements from the ALICE collaboration, which has already reported centrality-dependent enhancements in chi^{BQ}_{11}/chi^{Q}_{2} (ALICE 2025), and the authors propose chi^{BQ}_{11}/chi^{QS}_{11} as a more sensitive observable for future experimental tests.
