# Application of a new screening model to thermonuclear reactions of the rp process

> A new screening model for astrophysical thermonuclear reactions was derived recently which improved Salpeter's weak-screening one. In the present work we prove that the new model can also give very reliable screening enhancement factors (SEFs) when applied to the rp process. According to the results of the new model, which agree well with Mitler's SEFs, the screened rp reaction rates can be, at most, twice as fast as the unscreened ones.

## Key claim

The new screening model, which improves on Salpeter's weak-screening model, can provide reliable screening enhancement factors (SEFs) for the rp process, and the screened reaction rates are at most twice as fast as the unscreened ones, with the new model's results agreeing well with Mitler's SEFs.

## Method

The author applies a recently derived screening model for weakly screened thermonuclear reactions to proton-induced reactions of the rp process in completely ionized astrophysical plasmas. The model derives a screening enhancement factor (SEF) as a function of Salpeter's SEF, modified by an exponent G(x0, x0') obtained by solving two coupled equations involving the plasma composition, density, and temperature. The model is compared to Salpeter's and Mitler's models for reactions such as 18F(p,gamma)19Ne and 50Fe(p,gamma)51Co across a range of densities and temperatures typical of the rp process.

## Key numbers

* For the solar reaction 14N(p,gamma)15O, the SEF from the author's adiabatic limit and Mitler's complex model differ by 7%, which reflects linearly on the neutrino fluxes from 13N(e+ nu_e)13C and 15O(e+ nu_e)15N.
* The adiabatic and sudden limits of the novel model for the 14N(p,gamma)15O reaction deviate from each other by 3.5%.
* The screened rp reaction rates can be, at most, twice as fast as the unscreened ones.
* For the rp process, the plasma coupling constant is always Gamma << 1, and the ratio Lambda between the thermal De Broglie wavelength and the ionic spacing is much smaller than unity (Lambda << 1).

## Context

This work extends the author's new screening model (Liolios, 2002, submitted) which improved Salpeter's weak-screening model (Salpeter, 1954) and agrees with Mitler's SEFs (Mitler, 1977). The new model avoids the arbitrary assumption of constant electron density around the target nucleus that Mitler's model makes, and it is more reliable than Salpeter's model for the rp process where Salpeter's model is only valid for weakly screened, weakly degenerate environments. The work enables more accurate calculations of rp-process reaction rates in astrophysical environments such as neutron star surfaces, where screening effects can accelerate rates by up to a factor of two.
