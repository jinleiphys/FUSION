# An improvement of the Moliere-Fano multiple scattering theory

> In the framework of unitary Glauber approximation for particle-atom scattering, we develop the general formalism of the Moliere-Fano multiple scattering theory (M-F theory) on the basis of reconstruction of the generalized optical theorem in it. We present rigorous relations between some exact and first-order parameters of the Moliere multiple scattering theory, instead of the approximate one obtained in the original paper by Moliere. We consider the relative unitarity corrections and the Coulomb corrections to the quantities of the M-F theory. Also, we examine their Z dependence in the range of nuclear charge, from Z=4 to Z=92. Additionally, we show the difference between our results and those of Moliere over this Z range.

# An improvement of the Moliere-Fano multiple scattering theory

## Key claim
The authors derive rigorous relations between exact and first-order parameters of the Moliere multiple scattering theory, replacing the approximate relation for the screening angle originally obtained by Moliere. The unitarity corrections to the Moliere-Fano theory parameters are of order Z*alpha^2, while the Coulomb corrections can be numerically large (about 40-50% for Z=92).

## Method
The authors work within the unitary Glauber approximation for particle-atom scattering and reconstruct the generalized optical theorem in the Moliere-Fano framework. They expand the effective optical phase shift function in a series, retaining the first-order term (the Moliere phase shift) and the second-order absorptive term corresponding to inelastic scattering. Using this expansion, they derive analytical expressions for the unitarity corrections and the Coulomb corrections to the screening angle, the Bessel-transformed distribution function, and the Moliere expansion parameters b, B, and mean square angle.

## Key numbers
- Unitarity corrections are of order Z*alpha^2/beta, where alpha = 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- For Z=92, the Coulomb correction to the screening angle delta_CC(chi_a) = exp[f(Z*alpha/beta)] - 1, with f(Z*alpha) = 0.3951, giving a correction of approximately 48% (from delta_CC = exp(0.3951)-1).
- For Z=92 and B^B = 4.5, the relative Coulomb correction to the mean square angle delta_CC(overline{theta^2}) = -0.1129.
- For Z=79 and B^B = 4.5, delta_CC(overline{theta^2}) = -0.0893.
- For Z=50 and B^B = 4.5, delta_CC(overline{theta^2}) = -0.0410.
- For Z=28 and B^B = 4.5, delta_CC(overline{theta^2}) = -0.0139.
- The unitarity correction delta_UN = 0.0012 for Z=22 and 0.0007 for Z=13 (values multiplied by 10 in Table 2: 0.012 and 0.007 respectively).
- The universal function f(a) = a^2 * sum_{n=1}^{infinity} 1/[n(n^2+a^2)] with a = Z*alpha/beta; for Z=92, f = 0.3951; for Z=79, f = 0.3125; for Z=50, f = 0.1436; for Z=28, f = 0.0487.

## Context
This work extends the Moliere-Fano multiple scattering theory (Moliere 1947, 1955; Fano 1954) by restoring unitarity that was violated by the approximate Born-amplitude treatment in the original formulation. The authors provide rigorous relations replacing Moliere's approximate interpolation formula chi_a' approx chi_a'^B * sqrt(1+3.34*(Z*alpha)^2). The results are relevant for high-precision experiments such as DIRAC (2005), MuScat, and MUCOOL, where accurate treatment of multiple scattering is essential. The corrections become significant for heavy targets (e.g., gold and uranium in SLAC experiments) but are negligible for lighter materials like nickel (Z=28) used in DIRAC.
