# Multi-scale methods in quantum field theory

> Daubechies wavelets are used to make an exact multi-scale decomposition of quantum fields. For reactions that involve a finite energy that take place in a finite volume, the number of relevant quantum mechanical degrees of freedom is finite. The wavelet decomposition has natural resolution and volume truncations that can be used to isolate the relevant degrees of freedom. The application of flow equation methods to construct effective theories that decouple coarse and fine scale degrees of freedom is examined.

## Key claim

Daubechies wavelets provide an exact multi-scale decomposition of quantum fields, and for reactions with finite energy in a finite volume the number of relevant degrees of freedom is finite; however, a realistic treatment of the dynamics may still require a prohibitively large number of degrees of freedom for computation, motivating the use of flow equation methods to construct effective theories that decouple coarse and fine scale degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors decompose quantum fields using Daubechies wavelets with K=3, giving basis functions with compact support on [0,5] and one continuous derivative. They then truncate the expansion to retain only basis functions whose support lies within a given volume and whose support volume exceeds a minimal size, yielding a finite number of degrees of freedom. For a free scalar field in 1+1 dimensions truncated to 16 scaling basis functions and 16 wavelet basis functions, they apply Wegner's flow equation method with generator K(λ)=[G(λ),H(λ)] to block-diagonalize the Hamiltonian by resolution scale, solving the resulting differential equations in the eigenstate bases of the block-diagonal and coupling parts.

## Key numbers

- Daubechies K=3 wavelets are used; the scaling function s(x) has compact support on [0,5] and one continuous derivative.
- The truncated free-field test uses 16 scaling basis functions s_n(x) and 16 wavelet basis functions w_n(x), representing degrees of freedom on scales differing by a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the application of Daubechies wavelets (Daubechies, 1988) to quantum field theory, building on prior wavelet-based field decompositions by Beylkin (1992) and the authors' own work (Bulut, 2015; Michlin, 2015). The flow equation method follows Wegner (1994) and Glazek (1998) for decoupling scales. The approach enables construction of effective theories where dynamically important small-scale degrees of freedom are eliminated, though the authors note that for interacting theories the flow equation will generate new many-body interactions whose scaling properties must be investigated to control growth.
