# A remark on the Primakoff effect

> The coherent-nuclear reaction a + A -> a* + A is in the small-angle region dominated by the one-photon-exchange mechanism, often referred to as the Primakoff effect. In this region information about the electromagnetic decay a* -> a + gamma can be obtained. Well-known examples are the two-photon decays of the pi- and eta-mesons. Also decays of charged hadrons can be studied. For charged hadrons the one-photon-exchange amplitude comes with a Coulomb-phase factor and a Coulomb-form factor, which depend on the ratio between transverse- and logitudinal-momentum transfers, the latter being fixed. At the peak of the cross-section distribution, where the two momentum transfers are equal, the form factor could cut down the cross-section value by as much as 40 %. Consequently, a determination of a radiative-decay rate that relies on the peak value becomes sensitive to a proper treatment of the Coulomb-form factor.

## Key claim
For charged hadrons produced via the Primakoff effect, the one-photon-exchange amplitude includes a Coulomb-phase factor and a Coulomb-form factor that depend on the ratio of transverse to longitudinal momentum transfer. At the peak of the cross-section distribution, where these two momentum transfers are equal, the Coulomb-form factor can reduce the cross-section value by as much as 40%, making radiative-decay-rate determinations that rely on the peak value sensitive to proper treatment of this form factor.

## Method
The author analytically calculates the point-like-nucleus Coulomb-production amplitude for charged hadrons, starting from the formalism of Fäldt (1990) that extended Primakoff theory to include elastic-Coulomb scattering. The amplitude is derived by integrating the Born-approximation expression for a proton target, generalized to a nuclear target with charge Z, and including a Coulomb-phase factor that accounts for trajectory distortion. The resulting analytic expression yields a Coulomb-form factor expressed in terms of a hypergeometric function of the ratio \(q_\perp^2/(q_\perp^2+q_\parallel^2)\).

## Key numbers
- At the peak of the cross-section distribution, where transverse and longitudinal momentum transfers are equal, the Coulomb-form factor can reduce the cross-section value by as much as 40%.
- For a lead nucleus, the Coulomb-form factor dominates the integrated cross-section value even far beyond the peak region.
- The longitudinal momentum transfer is fixed as \(q_\parallel = -(m_{a^\star}^2 - m_a^2)/2k\).
- For a negatively charged projectile, the Sommerfeld parameter is \(\eta = 2Z\alpha/v\), with velocity \(v\) taken as unity.

## Context
This work extends the Primakoff-effect formalism of Primakoff (1951) and Fäldt (1990) by providing an analytic expression for the Coulomb-form factor that arises for charged hadrons, which was previously only treated numerically. The result is relevant for experiments extracting radiative-decay rates of charged hadrons via the Primakoff effect, such as those by Huston (1985), Antipov (1986), Jensen (1993), and Bemporad (1975). The analytic form factor can simplify analyses that previously required full numerical treatment of Coulomb-scattering effects and nuclear-charge-distribution extensions, as in the approaches of Jensen (1993) and Bemporad (1975).
