# The renormalization of the shell-model GT operator starting from effective field theory for nuclear systems

> For the first time, we approach in this work the problem of the renormalization of the Gamow-Teller decay operator for nuclear shell-model calculations by way of many-body perturbation theory, starting from a nuclear Hamiltonian and electroweak currents derived consistently by way of the chiral perturbation theory. These are the inputs we need to construct microscopically the effective shell-model Hamiltonians and decay operators. The goal is to assess the role of both electroweak currents and many-body correlations as the origins of the well-known problem of the quenching of the axial coupling constant gA. To this end, the calculation of observables related to the Gamow-Teller transitions has been performed for several nuclear systems outside the 40Ca and 56Ni closed cores and compared with the available data.

## Key claim
For the first time, the renormalization of the Gamow-Teller decay operator for shell-model calculations is performed using many-body perturbation theory starting from a nuclear Hamiltonian and electroweak currents both derived consistently from chiral perturbation theory, with the goal of assessing the relative roles of two-body electroweak currents versus many-body correlations in the quenching of the axial coupling constant gA.

## Method
The authors start from a chiral N3LO two-nucleon potential and a N2LO three-nucleon force, together with axial electroweak currents expanded up to N3LO in chiral perturbation theory. They derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians and Gamow-Teller decay operators via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion and the Suzuki-Okamoto method, respectively, using a Q-box vertex function expanded through third order in the two-nucleon potential and first order in the three-nucleon potential. The effective operators are then used in shell-model calculations for nuclei outside the 40Ca and 56Ni closed cores, with model spaces consisting of the 0f1p and 0f5/21p0g9/2 orbitals.

## Key numbers
- gA = 1.2723 (the bare axial coupling constant used in the LO one-body GT operator)
- Cutoff parameter for the chiral NN potential regulator: Lambda = 500 MeV
- Oscillator parameter hbar-omega = 11 MeV for the 40Ca core and 10 MeV for the 56Ni core
- Maximum excitation energy of intermediate states: Nmax = 18 oscillator quanta
- Single-particle energies for the 0f1p model space: proton and neutron 0f7/2 orbitals at -1.1 and -8.4 MeV, respectively
- Single-particle energies for the 0f5/21p0g9/2 model space: proton and neutron 1p3/2 orbitals at -0.7 and -10.2 MeV, respectively
- Three-nucleon force LECs: cD = -1 and cE = -0.34
- Pion mass m_pi = 138.04 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi = 700 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Coraggio 2017, 2019, 2022) where effective shell-model GT operators were derived from the CD-Bonn NN potential without two-body electroweak currents, and those calculations reproduced GT strengths and 2-nu-beta-beta decay matrix elements without any quenching factor. The present paper adds consistent chiral two-body axial currents, following the approach of Baroni (2016) and others, and applies it to medium-mass nuclei that are candidates for neutrinoless double-beta decay (48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se). This enables a microscopic assessment of the relative importance of two-body currents versus model-space truncation in the renormalization of the GT operator, and provides a foundation for more reliable calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements.
