# The Role of Final State Interactions in Quasielastic $^{56}$Fe$(e,e')$ Reactions at large $|\vec q|$

> A relativistic finite nucleus calculation using a Dirac optical potential is used to investigate the importance of final state interactions [FSI] at large momentum transfers in inclusive quasielastic electronuclear reactions. The optical potential is derived from first-order multiple scattering theory and then is used to calculate the FSI in a nonspectral Green's function doorway approach. At intermediate momentum transfers excellent predictions of the quasielastic $^{56}$Fe$(e,e')$ experimental data for the longitudinal response function are obtained. In comparisons with recent measurements at $|{\vec q|}=1.14$~GeV/c the theoretical calculations of $R_L$ give good agreement for the quasielastic peak shape and amplitude, but place the position of the peak at an energy transfer of about $40$~MeV higher than the data.

## Key claim

A relativistic finite-nucleus calculation using complex energy-dependent Dirac optical potentials derived from multiple scattering theory gives good agreement with the shape and amplitude of the quasielastic peak in the longitudinal response function for $^{56}$Fe$(e,e')$ at $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c, but places the peak position about 40 MeV higher in energy transfer than the experimental data, indicating that final state interactions remain important even at large momentum transfers.

## Method

The author calculates the longitudinal and transverse response functions for inclusive quasielastic $^{56}$Fe$(e,e')$ scattering using a relativistic finite-nucleus model with final state interactions included via a nonspectral Green's function doorway approach. Two complex energy-dependent Dirac optical potentials are derived from first-order multiple scattering theory using the full Bonn potential and Franey-Love nucleon-nucleon amplitudes, then folded with nuclear densities in momentum space. The calculations are performed fully off-shell, include Dirac dynamical effects from couplings to negative-energy states, and are compared with experimental data at momentum transfers of 410 MeV/c, 550 MeV/c, and 1.14 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- At $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c, the relativistic FSI calculation places the quasielastic peak in $R_L$ at an energy transfer about 40 MeV higher than the experimental data.
- At $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c, the plane wave approximation places the peak position about 55 MeV greater than the data.
- At $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c, nonrelativistic FSI calculations place the peak about 30-45 MeV too high relative to the data.
- At intermediate momentum transfers of 410 and 550 MeV/c, the PWA calculation places the peak position for $R_L$ at an energy transfer about 15-25 MeV larger than the experimental data.
- The most sophisticated results of Kim, Horowitz and Frank (1994) placed the peak position at an $\omega$ of about 85 MeV higher than the data at $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic Fermi gas calculations by Frank (1994) that suggested final state interactions are greatly reduced at large momentum transfers, and addresses the need for a full consistent finite-nucleus calculation. The author finds that FSI remain important at $|\vec q| = 1.14$ GeV/c, contrary to the Fermi gas implications, and that the relativistic FSI calculation gives a peak position closer to the data than the most sophisticated results of Kim, Horowitz and Frank (1994). The source of the remaining 40 MeV discrepancy is unclear and may motivate future investigations, particularly with more accurate experimental results from CEBAF.
