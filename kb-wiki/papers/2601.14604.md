# \textit{Ab initio} study of spectroscopic factors in $^{48}$K and neighboring $N=28$ isotones

> A recent \(^{47}\text{K}(d,pγ)^{48}\text{K}\) transfer reaction measurement has identified new excited states in \(^{48}\text{K}\) and extracted the corresponding spectroscopic factors (SFs)[\href{https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.134.162504}{C. J. Paxman, \textit{et al.} PhysRevLett.134.162504 (2025)}], but they exposed sizeable discrepancies with large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations-especially for the low-lying states-suggesting shortcomings in the proton-neutron interaction employed by the LSSM. In this work, we revisit the low-lying states and SFs of \(^{48}\text{K}\) using the \textit{ab initio} valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) approach based on the chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. The calculated excitation energies reproduce the experimental data for \(^{48}\text{K}\), whereas computed SFs systematically exceed experimental values. We trace this overestimation to missing reduction factors that account for non-idealities of the transfer reaction. After introducing a phenomenological reduction factor, our VS-IMSRG results and the LSSM calculations achieve agreement with experiment. We also perform the same analysis for the neutron SFs of $^{47}$Ar. Furthermore, we extend the \textit{ab initio} calculations across the $N=28$ isotones, computing excitation energies and single-neutron transfer SFs from $N=29$ isotones ranging from $^{48}$K to $^{45}$S. By systematically removing protons from \(^{48}\text{K}\) to \(^{45}\text{S}\), we trace the evolution of the \(N=28\) shell strength via theoretical SFs values. Our results provide a microscopic pathway to quantify the weakening of the \(N=28\) shell closure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the systematic overestimation of spectroscopic factors (SFs) in \(^{48}\)K compared to experiment is not primarily due to deficiencies in the proton-neutron interaction, as previously claimed by Paxman et al. (2025), but rather reflects a well-known quenching effect from many-body correlations that requires an empirical reduction factor before theory and experiment can be meaningfully compared.

## Method

The authors performed *ab initio* valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using two chiral interactions: EM1.8/2.0 and NN N\(^3\)LO + 3N(lnl), with \(^{28}\)O as an inert core and valence protons in the full \(sd\)-shell and neutrons in the full \(pf\)-shell. The effective valence-space Hamiltonian was constructed via the Magnus formulation of VS-IMSRG, truncated at the two-body level, and diagonalized using the *kshell* code to obtain excitation energies and spectroscopic factors. The results were compared to large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations with SDPF-MU and SDPF-U interactions and to experimental data from the \(^{47}\)K\((d,p\gamma)^{48}\)K transfer reaction.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{48}\)K ground state \(1^-_1\) spectroscopic factor for the \(1p_{3/2}\) partial wave is 0.24(5) experimentally; VS-IMSRG with NN N\(^3\)LO + 3N(lnl) gives 0.35, with EM1.8/2.0 gives 0.45, and LSSM with SDPF-MU gives 0.40.
- The \(2^-_1\) state at 0.143(1) MeV has an experimental SF of 0.42(8) for \(1p_{3/2}\); all four theoretical calculations give values between 0.84 and 0.86.
- The \(1^-_3\) state at 1.978(4) MeV has an experimental SF of 0.50(10) for \(1p_{1/2}\); VS-IMSRG with NN N\(^3\)LO + 3N(lnl) gives 0.92, and LSSM with SDPF-MU gives 0.88.
- The \(0^-_1\) state at 0.967(2) MeV has an experimental SF of 0.26(5) for \(1p_{1/2}\); VS-IMSRG with NN N\(^3\)LO + 3N(lnl) gives 0.40, and LSSM with SDPF-MU gives 0.40.
- The proton-neutron matrix element \(\langle p_{3/2}s_{1/2}|V_{\text{int}}|p_{3/2}d_{3/2}\rangle_{J=2}\) is -0.28 MeV in NN N\(^3\)LO + 3N(lnl), compared to -0.05 MeV in SDPF-MU and -0.11 MeV in SDPF-U.
- After applying a reduction factor of 0.6, the scaled theoretical SFs show significantly improved agreement with experimental data across all states.

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* VS-IMSRG approach to the \(N=28\) isotones \(^{48}\)K, \(^{47}\)Ar, and the chain from \(^{48}\)K to \(^{45}\)S, directly confronting the recent transfer reaction data of Paxman et al. (2025) that had attributed discrepancies to interaction deficiencies. The results contradict that interpretation by showing that the systematic overestimation of SFs is consistent with the well-known 30–40% quenching observed in \((e,e'p)\) knockout experiments (Lapikas 1993, Sick 2007) and transfer reactions (Lee 2007, Kay 2013). The paper enables a microscopic quantification of the weakening of the \(N=28\) shell closure as protons are removed, and it provides a benchmark for future *ab initio* reaction calculations that treat structure and reactions on equal footing.
