# Coulomb excitation of the $\left|T_z\right|=\frac{1}{2}$, $A=23$ mirror pair

> Background: Electric-quadrupole ($E2$) strengths relate to the underlying quadrupole deformation of a nucleus and present a challenge for many nuclear theories. Mirror nuclei in the vicinity of the line of $N=Z$ represent a convenient laboratory for testing deficiencies in such models, making use of the isospin-symmetry of the systems. Purpose: Uncertainties associated with literature $E2$ strengths in \textsuperscript{23}Mg are some of the largest in $T_z=\left|\frac{1}{2}\right|$ nuclei in the $sd$-shell. The purpose of the present work is to improve the precision with which these values are known, to enable better comparison with theoretical models. Methods: Coulomb-excitation measurements of $^{23}$Mg and $^{23}$Na were performed at the TRIUMF-ISAC facility using the TIGRESS spectrometer. They were used to determine the $E2$ matrix elements of mixed $E2$/$M1$ transitions. Results: Reduced $E2$ transition strengths, $B(E2)$, were extracted for \textsuperscript{23}Mg and \textsuperscript{23}Na. Their precision was improved by factors of approximately six for both isotopes, while agreeing within uncertainties with previous measurements. Conclusions: A comparison was made with both shell-model and {\it ab initio} valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations. Valence-space in-medium similarity-renormalization-group calculations were found to underpredict the absolute $E2$ strength - in agreement with previous studies.

## Key claim

The paper improves the precision of reduced E2 transition strengths for the mirror pair 23Mg and 23Na by factors of approximately six compared to literature values, while agreeing within uncertainties with previous measurements. The valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations are found to underpredict the absolute E2 strength, consistent with previous studies, though the relative B(E2) ratio between the two mirror nuclei is better reproduced by the ab initio calculations than by the shell model.

## Method

Coulomb-excitation measurements of 23Mg and 23Na were performed at the TRIUMF-ISAC facility using the TIGRESS spectrometer, with beams impinged onto a natural titanium target. Particle-gamma coincidences were detected using an S3-type silicon detector and fourteen clover-type HPGe detectors, and the data were analyzed using the coupled-channels GOSIA2 code to extract E2 matrix elements of mixed E2/M1 transitions. The 23Mg beam was produced with selective laser ionization and ion-guide laser ion source suppression of 23Na contamination, while pure 23Na beams were provided by the TRIUMF offline ion source at two energies.

## Key numbers

- For 23Na: B(E2; 5/2+ -> 3/2+) = 106 +/- 3 +/- 3 e^2fm^4, compared to literature value of 93 +/- 12 e^2fm^4
- For 23Mg: B(E2; 5/2+ -> 3/2+) = 135 (+15/-14) +/- 4 e^2fm^4, compared to literature value of 86 +/- 58 e^2fm^4
- Transition matrix element for 23Na: <3/2+_1|E2|5/2+_1> = 0.252 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.004 eb
- Transition matrix element for 23Mg: <3/2+_1|E2|5/2+_1> = 0.285 +/- 0.015 +/- 0.004 eb
- Experimental ratio R = B(E2)[23Mg]/B(E2)[23Na] = 1.27 +/- 0.14, compared to USDB shell-model ratio of 1.06 and VS-IMSRG ratio of 1.34
- 23Na contamination in the cocktail beam was determined to be 15.2 +/- 0.9% of the total

## Context

This work extends previous Coulomb-excitation studies of mirror nuclei in the sd-shell by the same group, including studies of 22Mg (Henderson, 2018) and 21Mg (Ruotsalainen, 2019), and addresses the largest uncertainties in E2 strengths among Tz = |1/2| nuclei in the sd-shell. The improved precision enables better comparison with both shell-model calculations using the USDB interaction and ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations, and a detailed systematic study of deficiencies in VS-IMSRG E2 strength calculations across the sd-shell is the subject of a separate publication (Henderson, 2021).
