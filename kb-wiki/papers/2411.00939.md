# Masking equation of state effects in binary neutron star mergers

> Recent nonmagnetized studies of binary neutron star mergers have indicated the possibility of identifying equation of state features, such as a phase transition or a quark-hadron crossover, based on the frequency shift of the main peak in the postmerger gravitational wave spectrum. By performing a series of general relativistic, magnetohydrodynamic simulations we show that similar frequency shifts can be obtained due to the effect of the magnetic field. The existing degeneracy can either mask or nullify a shift due to a specific equation of state feature, and therefore the interpretation of observational data is more complicated than previously thought, requiring a more complete treatment that would necessarily include the neutron star's magnetic field.

## Key claim
Magnetic fields in binary neutron star mergers can produce frequency shifts of the postmerger gravitational wave main peak (f2) of up to ~200 Hz, which overlaps with shifts previously attributed solely to equation of state features such as phase transitions or quark-hadron crossovers, creating a degeneracy that can mask or nullify EOS interpretations.

## Method
The authors performed general relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron star mergers using two equations of state (SLy and ALF2), two ADM masses (2.57 and 2.70 solar masses), and three magnetic field topologies (pulsar-like poloidal, interior-only toroidal, interior-only poloidal) with average field strengths ranging from 7.9e14 G to 3.2e16 G. They compared the postmerger gravitational wave spectra of magnetized and nonmagnetized simulations, extracting the f2 peak frequency by fitting Gaussians to the amplitude spectral density and measuring the shift relative to the nonmagnetized case.

## Key numbers
- Frequency shift up to ~200 Hz for the largest magnetic field and smaller mass remnant (SLy 2.57 solar masses, 3.2e16 G pulsar-like field)
- Shift of ~150 Hz for pulsar-like SLy 2.57 solar masses case with average magnetic field 7.9e15 G
- Growth rate of f2 frequency with time follows approximate power law: xi - xi(B=0) ≈ (10 ± 2 Hz/ms) × [|B|_av/10^15 G]^(0.5 ± 0.2)
- Resolution used: Δx_min ≈ 90 m (compared to 185 m in Huang (2022) and 237 m in Weih (2019))
- Magnetic field strengths tested: 7.9e14 G, 2.0e15 G, 7.9e15 G, 3.2e16 G (pulsar-like); 2.2e16 G (toroidal); 2.7e16 G (poloidal)

## Context
This work extends nonmagnetized studies by Bauswein (2018), Blacker (2020), and Weih (2019) that attributed f2 frequency shifts to first-order phase transitions, and by Huang (2022) that examined quark-hadron crossover EOS effects. The authors show that magnetic fields can produce shifts comparable to those from phase transitions (~500 Hz), quark-hadron crossovers (~100 Hz), finite temperature (~200 Hz), and bulk viscosity (~300 Hz). This degeneracy complicates the interpretation of future postmerger gravitational wave observations from next-generation detectors like Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope, requiring more complete simulations that include magnetic fields, temperature-dependent EOS, and neutrino transport.
