# Advancing the understanding of energy-energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the collinear limit of the energy-energy correlator (EEC) in a heavy-ion context. First, we revisit the leading-logarithmic (LL) resummation of this observable in vacuum following a diagrammatic approach. We argue that this route allows to naturally incorporate medium-induced effects into the all-orders structure systematically. As an example, we show how the phase-space constraints imposed by the medium on vacuum-like emissions can be incorporated into the LL result by modifying the anomalous dimensions. On the fixed-order side, we calculate the $\mathcal{O}(α_s)$ expansion of the in-medium EEC for a $γ\to q\bar q$ splitting using, for the first time, the exact matrix element. When comparing this result to previously used approximations in the literature, we find up to $\mathcal{O}(1)$ deviations in the regime of interest for jet quenching signatures. Energy loss effects are also quantified and further suppress the EEC at large angles. These semi-analytic studies are complemented with a phenomenological study using the jet quenching Monte Carlo JetMed. Finally, we argue that the imprint of medium-induced effects in energy-energy correlators can be enhanced by using an alternative definition that takes as input Lund primary declusterings instead of particles.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the collinear limit of the energy-energy correlator (EEC) in heavy-ion collisions can be systematically studied by combining a diagrammatic resummation approach with exact fixed-order calculations, but they find that previously used semi-classical approximations for medium-modified splitting functions lead to up to order-one deviations in the regime of interest for jet quenching signatures, and that including energy loss further suppresses the EEC at large angles.

## Method

The authors first re-derive the leading-logarithmic (LL) resummation of the EEC in vacuum using a diagrammatic approach, which allows them to incorporate medium-induced effects by modifying the anomalous dimensions with phase-space constraints from the medium. They then compute the \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) in-medium EEC for a \(\gamma\to q\bar q\) splitting using the exact matrix element in the multiple-soft scattering approximation, comparing it to the semi-classical and BDMPS-Z approximations. Finally, they complement these analytic studies with phenomenological simulations using the Monte Carlo event generator JetMed.

## Key numbers

- The medium-to-vacuum ratio of the anomalous dimension for \(j=3\) in the \(q\to qg\) channel shows a reduction compared to vacuum, with the ratio tending to unity as jet \(p_t\) increases; for fixed medium parameters \(L=4\) fm and \(\hat q=1.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, the EEC slope is modified by less than 5% in the moderate angle region.
- For the \(\gamma\to q\bar q\) splitting, comparing the exact matrix element to previously used approximations yields up to \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) deviations in the regime of interest for jet quenching signatures.
- The medium modification factor \(F_{\rm med}\) in the semi-classical approximation is evaluated for splitting angles \(\theta=0.1\) and \(\theta=0.3\), showing that for wide-angle splittings (\(\theta=0.3\)) the dominant contribution comes from asymmetric splittings (\(z\to 0\) or \(z\to 1\)), where the semi-classical approximation is less accurate.

## Context

This work extends recent studies of energy-energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions (Andres 2022, Andres 2023, Barata 2023, Yang 2023) that used semi-classical or higher-twist approximations for medium-modified matrix elements, and it provides a more precise treatment by using exact matrix elements for the \(\gamma\to q\bar q\) channel. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for upcoming experimental measurements of EECs at ALICE and STAR, and the proposed alternative definition using Lund primary declusterings may enhance the imprint of medium-induced effects.
