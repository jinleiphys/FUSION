# Quantum partonic transport in QCD matter

> We study gradient corrections to the transport equation for energetic light partons in dense QCD environments. In the diffusion limit, the transport dynamics is solely controlled by small-angle elastic scatterings, leading to transverse momentum broadening with respect to the parton's initial direction. Such a parton propagation is usually considered in the limit of transversely homogeneous matter. The transport processes admit a classical description and the transverse spatial dependence of the medium properties emerges only through the jet quenching parameter. In this work, we show that a gradient expansion of the all-order evolution equation for the partonic Wigner function leads to an evolution equation in the Boltzmann-diffusion form only up to the leading order in transverse gradients. At the second order in gradients, the quantum corrections associated with non-local interactions give rise to a novel transport that can be implemented in Monte Carlo simulations. In addition, using our results, we compute the gradient corrections to the jet quenching parameter in inhomogeneous matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a gradient expansion of the all-order evolution equation for the partonic Wigner function yields a Boltzmann-diffusion form only at leading order in transverse gradients; at second order in gradients, quantum corrections from non-local interactions produce novel transport terms that cannot be absorbed into a simple spatially-dependent jet quenching parameter.

## Method

The authors derive the parton Wigner function for energetic light partons in dense QCD matter using the BDMPS-Z approach, expanding the medium properties (density and Debye mass) in transverse gradients up to second order. They then compute the path-length derivative of the two-point correlator of single-particle propagators to obtain an all-order master transport equation, which they truncate at second order in gradients and evaluate in the harmonic (diffusion) approximation using the Gyulassy-Wang model for the medium.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order Wigner function for point-like initial conditions contains a prefactor of \(48 E^2/(\hat q^2 L^4)\) and an exponential with argument \(-\frac{4}{\hat q L^3}(L^2 p^2 - 3E L \, \mathbf{p}\cdot\mathbf{Y} + 3E^2 \mathbf{Y}^2)\).
- The gradient parameter is defined as \(\mathbf{g} \equiv \nabla \hat q / \hat q = \nabla \rho / \rho - (1/\log(Q^2/\mu^2)) \nabla \mu^2 / \mu^2\).
- The full jet quenching parameter including second-order gradient corrections is \(\hat q_r = \hat q + \nabla^2 \hat q \left( \hat q L^3/(12 E^2) + \eta \right)\), where \(\eta\) is an energy-independent coefficient computed from the scattering potential.
- The bare jet quenching coefficient in the Gyulassy-Wang model is \(\hat q_0 = 4\pi C \alpha_s^2 \rho\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order gradient treatment of parton-matter interactions developed by Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2022), which previously only computed final particle distributions. It provides the first ab-initio derivation of a kinetic equation for energetic partons in inhomogeneous QCD matter, enabling inclusion of gradient effects in Monte Carlo transport models such as those used by the JETSCAPE collaboration. The results contradict the naive assumption that all gradient corrections can be absorbed into a spatially-dependent jet quenching parameter, revealing quantum corrections to classical Boltzmann transport that are sensitive to the details of the medium model.
