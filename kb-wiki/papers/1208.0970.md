# Progress on Neutron-Target Multipoles above 1 GeV

> We report a new extraction of nucleon resonance couplings using pi- photoproduction cross sections on the neutron. The world database for the process gn-->pi-p above 1 GeV has quadrupled with the addition of new differential cross sections from the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer (CLAS) at Jefferson Lab in Hall B. Differential cross sections from CLAS have been improved with a new final-state interaction determination using a diagrammatic technique taking into account the SAID phenomenological NN and piN final-state interaction amplitudes. Resonance couplings have been extracted and compared to previous determinations. With the addition of these new cross sections, significant changes are seen in the high-energy behavior of the SAID cross sections and amplitudes.

## Key claim
The new CLAS differential cross sections for $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ above 1 GeV, corrected for final-state interactions using a diagrammatic technique, produce significant changes in the high-energy behavior of SAID multipoles and resonance couplings, but the sign of certain neutron multipoles (e.g., $_n E^{1/2}_{1+}$ and $_n M^{1/2}_{1+}$) remains undetermined, and the extracted couplings for some states (e.g., $N(1650)1/2^-$) carry large uncertainties.

## Method
The authors extracted differential cross sections for $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ from the reaction $\gamma d \to \pi^- pp$ using a tagged-photon beam at Jefferson Lab's CLAS detector, covering incident photon energies from 1.05 to 2.7 GeV. Final-state interaction (FSI) corrections were applied using a diagrammatic technique that includes impulse approximation, $pp$-FSI, and $\pi N$-FSI amplitudes, with the elementary $\gamma N \to \pi N$ amplitudes expressed via Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu (CGLN) amplitudes from the SAID code. The corrected cross sections were then included in a SAID multipole analysis to produce new solutions (GB12 and GZ12) and extract neutron helicity amplitudes for several resonances.

## Key numbers
- The world database for $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ above 1 GeV was quadrupled by adding 626 new differential cross sections from CLAS.
- The FSI correction factor $R$ is less than 1, with the effect $(1-R)$ less than 10% and a smooth angular dependence; the contribution to overall systematics is 2% below 1800 MeV and 3% above 1800 MeV.
- The full pion photoproduction database below $E_\gamma = 2.7$ GeV contains 26179 data points; the GB12 (GZ12) solution yields $\chi^2 = 54832$ (50998), with the 626 new CLAS $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ points contributing $\chi^2 = 1580$ (1190).
- For the $N(1535)1/2^-$ resonance, the GB12 solution gives $A_{1/2} = -58 \pm 6$ (in units of $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$), while the GZ12 solution gives $-85 \pm 15$.
- For the $N(1520)3/2^-$ resonance, the GB12 solution gives $A_{1/2} = -46 \pm 6$ and $A_{3/2} = -115 \pm 5$ (same units).
- For the $N(1675)5/2^-$ resonance, the GB12 solution gives $A_{1/2} = -58 \pm 2$ and $A_{3/2} = -80 \pm 5$ (same units).
- The differential cross sections were measured at 26 energies from 1.05 to 2.7 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous SAID partial-wave analyses (e.g., SN11 from Strakovsky et al.) by incorporating new CLAS data that quadruple the world database for $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ above 1 GeV, addressing the long-standing scarcity of neutron-target data compared to proton-target data (only 15% of the database). The results are compared to earlier SAID solutions (SN11), the MAID07 predictions, and PDG averages; the new GB12 and GZ12 solutions show improved high-energy behavior. The work enables future constraints from polarized-beam measurements (e.g., CLAS $\Sigma$-beam asymmetry for $\vec{\gamma}n \to \pi^- p$ at $E_\gamma = 910$ to 2400 MeV) and highlights the need for further data with polarized photons and targets to resolve ambiguities in neutron multipole signs.
