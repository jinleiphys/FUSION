# Impact of the quenching of $g_{\rm A}$ on the sensitivity of $0νββ$ experiments

> Detection of the neutrinoless $ββ$ ($0νββ$) decay is of high priority in the particle- and neutrino-physics communities. The detectability of this decay mode is strongly influenced by the value of the weak axial-vector coupling constant $g_{\rm A}$. The recent nuclear-model analyses of $β$ and $ββ$ decays suggest that the value of $g_{\rm A}$ could be dramatically quenched, reaching ratios of $g^{\rm free}_{\rm A}/g_{\rm A}\approx 4$, where $g^{\rm free}_{\rm A}=1.27$ is the free, neutron-decay, value of $g_{\rm A}$. The effects of this quenching appear devastating for the sensitivity of the present and future $0νββ$ experiments since the 4$th$ power of this ratio scales the $0νββ$ half-lives. This, in turn, could lead to some two orders of magnitude less sensitivity for the $0νββ$ experiments. In the present Letter it is shown that by using a consistent approach to both the two-neutrino $ββ$ and $0νββ$ decays by the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA), the feared two-orders-of-magnitude reduction in the sensitivity of the $0νββ$ experiments actually shrinks to a reduction by factors in the range $2-6$. This certainly has dramatic consequences for the potential to detect the $0νββ$ decay.

## Key claim

Using a consistent two-stage fit to two-neutrino double-beta decay data within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA), the feared two-orders-of-magnitude reduction in the sensitivity of neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments due to strong quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_{\rm A}\) actually shrinks to a reduction by factors in the range 2–6, depending on the nucleus.

## Method

The author performed pnQRPA calculations for eight candidate nuclei (\(A=76,82,96,100,116,128,130,136\)) using a two-stage fitting procedure: first, the isovector particle-particle interaction strength \(g_{\rm pp}^{T=1}\) was fixed by requiring the Fermi nuclear matrix element (NME) of two-neutrino double-beta decay to vanish (restoring isospin symmetry); second, the isoscalar strength \(g_{\rm pp}^{T=0}\) was varied to reproduce the experimental two-neutrino double-beta decay half-life for each chosen value of \(g_{\rm A}\). The resulting Hamiltonian was then used to compute the neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs and reduced half-lives as continuous functions of \(g_{\rm A}\) down to the minimum possible value for each system.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{76}\)Ge: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.59\); reduced half-life ratio (max/min) = 2.7, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(4.4\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(1.6\times10^{24}\) yr.
- For \(^{82}\)Se: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.56\); reduced half-life ratio = 3.0, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(2.1\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(7.0\times10^{23}\) yr.
- For \(^{96}\)Zr: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.52\); reduced half-life ratio = 4.9, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(2.2\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(4.5\times10^{23}\) yr.
- For \(^{100}\)Mo: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.70\); reduced half-life ratio = 2.0, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(7.9\times10^{23}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(4.0\times10^{23}\) yr.
- For \(^{116}\)Cd: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.62\); reduced half-life ratio = 3.1, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(1.1\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(3.6\times10^{23}\) yr.
- For \(^{128}\)Te: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.38\); reduced half-life ratio = 4.4, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(3.4\times10^{25}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(7.7\times10^{24}\) yr.
- For \(^{130}\)Te: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.35\); reduced half-life ratio = 4.9, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(2.1\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(4.3\times10^{23}\) yr.
- For \(^{136}\)Xe: minimum \(g_{\rm A}=0.36\); reduced half-life ratio = 5.7, with \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(max) = \(4.7\times10^{24}\) yr and \(F^{(0\nu)}\)(min) = \(8.2\times10^{23}\) yr.

## Context

This work extends the pnQRPA calculations of Simkovic (2013) and Hyvarinen (2015), which previously considered only two values of \(g_{\rm A}\) (free value 1.27 and moderately quenched 1.0), to a continuous range of \(g_{\rm A}\) values down to the minimum allowed by two-neutrino double-beta decay data. It directly contradicts the earlier conclusion of Barea (2013), who used the interacting shell model and microscopic interacting boson model to predict that strong \(g_{\rm A}\) quenching would reduce experimental sensitivity by roughly two orders of magnitude via the naive \((g_{\rm A}^{\rm free}/g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff})^4\) scaling. The present result implies that the effective value of \(g_{\rm A}\) is not a critical unknown for the discovery potential of neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, provided the two-neutrino double-beta decay data are consistently incorporated into the nuclear model.
