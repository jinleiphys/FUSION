# Shell-structure of one-particle resonances in deformed potentials

> Shell structure of low-lying neutron resonant levels in axially-symmetric quadrupole-deformed potentials is discussed, which seems analogous to that of weakly-bound neutrons. As numerical examples, nuclei slightly outside the neutron-drip-line, $^{39}_{12}$Mg$_{27}$ and $^{21}_{6}$C$_{15}$, are studied. For the lowest resonance I obtain $I^π$ = $Ω^π$ = 5/2$^{-}$ for $^{39}$Mg which is likely to be prolately deformed, while $I^π$ = $Ω^π$ = 1/2$^{+}$ may be assigned to the nucleus $^{21}$C which may be oblately deformed. Consequently, $^{21}$C will not be observed as a recognizable resonant state, in agreement with the experimental information.

# Shell-structure of one-particle resonances in deformed potentials

## Key claim
The shell structure of low-lying neutron resonant levels in axially-symmetric quadrupole-deformed potentials is analogous to that of weakly-bound neutrons, but the presence or absence of a one-particle resonance and its decay width are determined decisively by the smallest orbital angular-momentum component (ℓ_min) in the wave function and the magnitude of that component. For ³⁹Mg the lowest resonance is assigned I^π = Ω^π = 5/2⁻ (prolate deformation), while for ²¹C the lowest state is I^π = Ω^π = 1/2⁺ (oblate deformation) and will not be observed as a recognizable resonant state.

## Method
One-neutron bound and resonant levels were calculated by solving coupled radial Schrödinger equations in coordinate space for axially-symmetric quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters from Bohr and Mottelson (1969), except for adjusted potential depths to place the 27th neutron of ³⁹Mg and the 15th neutron of ²¹C at positive energies of a few hundred keV. Resonances were identified when the eigenphase δ_Ω increases through π/2 as energy increases, and intrinsic decay widths were computed from the energy derivative of the eigenphase at the resonance energy. The spin-parity of band-head states in odd-N nuclei was taken as I^π = Ω^π (except for Ω = 1/2 where the decoupling parameter is considered).

## Key numbers
- For ³⁹Mg: the calculated one-particle decay width of the I^π = 5/2⁻ state is 0.01 MeV at β ≈ 0 for the 1f₇/₂ resonant state at ε_res = 0.32 MeV; at β = 0.4 the intrinsic width Γ_intr (≈ Γ) is 0.02 MeV for the resonant Ω^π = 5/2⁻ state at ε_res = 0.62 MeV.
- The neutron separation energy of ³⁹Mg estimated from systematic trends of atomic mass is −130 keV.
- The neutron separation energy of ²¹C estimated from systematic trends of atomic mass is −15 keV.
- For ³³Mg: the calculated magnetic moment for the band-head state I^π = 3/2⁻ from the intrinsic [330 1/2] state is −0.88 μ_N at β = 0.25; for the band-head state I^π = 3/2⁻ from [321 3/2] is −0.40 μ_N at β = 0.50; for the band-head state I^π = 3/2⁺ from [202 3/2] is +0.91 μ_N at β = 0.35; the measured value is −0.7456(5) μ_N (De Rydt et al., 2007).
- For ¹⁷C: the measured magnetic moment |μ_obs| = 0.758 μ_N (Horiuchi et al., 2002) agrees with the calculated value μ_calc = −0.75 μ_N for the band-head state from the intrinsic [211 3/2] neutron state at β ≈ 0.4.
- The neutron separation energies of ¹⁷C and ¹⁹C are 0.73 MeV and 0.58 MeV, respectively.
- The 2₁⁺ energies in ¹⁶C, ¹⁸C, and ²⁰C are 1.76, 1.58, and 1.61 MeV, respectively; corresponding B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0_gs⁺) values are 4.21^{+1.26}_{-0.50} e²fm⁴ (Petri et al., 2012), 3.64^{+0.55}_{-0.61} e²fm⁴ (Petri et al., 2012), and 7.5^{+4.0}_{-2.1} e²fm⁴ (Morse et al., 2011).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies (Hamamoto, 2007, 2012, 2016) on the shell-structure change of weakly-bound neutrons in deformed potentials to the case of one-particle resonant levels, showing that the same mechanism (ℓ_min dependence) governs both bound and resonant states. The analysis of ³⁹Mg builds on experimental evidence that neutron-rich Mg isotopes with N = 22–26 are prolately deformed (Poves et al., 2013) and that ³⁷Mg exhibits a p-wave halo (Nakamura et al., 2014). The prediction that ²¹C will not be observed as a resonant state agrees with the experimental search by Spyrou et al. (2013), who found no evidence for low-lying states. The work enables future experiments on ³⁹Mg to test the predicted shell-structure of one-particle resonant levels in deformed nuclei, and clarifies why certain nuclei just outside the neutron drip line may be unobservable as resonances.
