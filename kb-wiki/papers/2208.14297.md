# Partonic Critical Opalescence and Its Impact on the Jet Quenching Parameter $\hat{q}$

> Jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ is essential for characterizing the interaction strength between jet partons and nuclear matter. Based on the quark-meson (QM) model, we develop a new framework for calculating $\hat{q}$ at finite chemical potentials, in which $\hat{q}$ is related to the spectral function of the chiral order parameter. A perturbative calculation up to the one-loop order indicates that the momentum broadening of jets is enhanced at both the high temperature and high chemical potential, and approximately proportional to the parton number density in the partonic phase. We further investigate the behavior of $\hat{q}$ in the vicinity of the critical endpoint (CEP) by coupling our calculation with a recently developed equation of state that includes a CEP in the universality class of the Ising model, from which we discover the partonic critical opalescence (PCO) -- a prominent enhancement of the momentum broadening of jets near CEP, contributed by the scatterings via the $σ$ exchange process. Hence, for the first time, jet quenching is connected with the search of CEP.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) exhibits a prominent enhancement near the QCD critical endpoint (CEP), termed "partonic critical opalescence" (PCO), but this effect is only visible when the calculation is coupled with an equation of state that includes a CEP in the universality class of the Ising model; the mean-field perturbative calculation alone shows no special behavior at the CEP.

## Method
The authors develop a framework based on the quark-meson (QM) model, relating \(\hat{q}\) to the spectral function of the chiral order parameter (the \(\sigma\) field). They first evaluate \(\hat{q}\) perturbatively up to one-loop order at finite temperature and chemical potential. They then couple this calculation with a mapping between QCD and the Ising model, using the Extended Landau-Ginzburg-Wilson (LGW) model to non-perturbatively evaluate the correlation functions of the order parameter near the CEP, incorporating dynamics via Model B and Model H for the conserved and non-conserved fields respectively.

## Key numbers
- The CEP in the QM model under mean-field approximation is located at \(T_\mathrm{c}\approx 89\) MeV and \(\mu_\mathrm{c}\approx 228\) MeV (baryon number density \(n_B\approx 0.21\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- At high temperatures and chemical potentials, \(\hat{q}_{\sigma}\sim 0.2 n_\mathrm{parton}\), \(\hat{q}_{\pi}\sim 0.6 n_\mathrm{parton}\), and total \(\hat{q}\sim 0.8 n_\mathrm{parton}\), where \(n_\mathrm{parton}\) is the parton number density.
- Near the CEP, \(\hat{q}_{\sigma}\) and the scattering rate \(SR\) scale as \(R^{-0.11}\sim \xi^{\alpha/\nu}\), with critical exponents \(\alpha=0.11\) and \(\nu=0.63\) from the Ising model universality class.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet quenching from vanishing baryon chemical potential to finite chemical potentials, connecting jet observables with the search for the QCD critical endpoint for the first time. It builds on the quark-meson model of Schaefer (2007) and the equation of state incorporating a CEP from Parotto (2018) and Martinez (2019). The finding of partonic critical opalescence suggests that the additional suppression of hard hadrons observed in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2021) could be a signature of the CEP, enabling a new probe of the QCD phase diagram using jets.
