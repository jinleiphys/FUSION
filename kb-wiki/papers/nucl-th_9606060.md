# Semiclassical Expansion for the Angular Momentum

> After reviewing the WKB series for the Schrödinger equation we calculate the semiclassical expansion for the eigenvalues of the angular momentum operator. This is the first systematic semiclassical treatment of the angular momentum for terms beyond the leading torus approximation.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first systematic semiclassical expansion for the eigenvalues of the angular momentum operator beyond the leading torus (EBK) approximation, showing that the WKB series can be summed to all orders to yield the exact quantum result \(\lambda = \sqrt{l(l+1)}\), though the general formula for terms with \(k \geq 2\) remains a conjecture based on an "educated guess."

## Method

The authors apply the WKB expansion method to the eigenvalue equation of the squared angular momentum operator \(\hat{L}^2\) in spherical coordinates. They introduce a small parameter \(\epsilon\) (set to 1 after calculation) to organize the semiclassical series, derive recursion relations for the phase function \(S(\theta)\), and compute the contour integrals \(\oint dS_k\) around the two turning points. The quantization condition is obtained by requiring uniqueness of the wave function, yielding a sum over even-numbered terms only, with explicit evaluation of the zero-order, first-order, and second-order contributions.

## Key numbers

- Zero-order term: \(\oint dS_0 = 2\pi i (\lambda - n_\phi)\), giving the leading torus quantization condition.
- First-order term: \(\oint dS_1 = -\pi i\), providing the Maslov correction (index 2).
- Second-order correction: \(\oint dS_2 = \pi i / (4\lambda)\), which is independent of \(n_\phi\).
- Quantization condition up to second order: \(\lambda + 1/(8\lambda) = l + 1/2\), where \(l = n_\theta + n_\phi\).
- Conjectured all-orders result: \(\sum_{k=0}^\infty \binom{1/2}{k} 2^{-2k} \lambda^{1-2k} = l + 1/2\), which sums to \(\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1+4\lambda^2}\), yielding the exact spectrum \(\lambda = \sqrt{l(l+1)}\).

## Context

This work extends the WKB expansion, originally developed for one-dimensional Schrödinger equations with recursion formulas by Dunham (1932) and others, to the angular momentum operator for the first time. The analysis explains why torus quantization (EBK) is exact for the 3-dimensional Kepler problem, as shown by the authors in a related paper (Robnik and Salasnich, 1996), because quantum corrections from the angular and radial parts cancel term by term. The results enable future extension to more general one-dimensional potentials and to integrable but non-separable systems with two or more degrees of freedom.
