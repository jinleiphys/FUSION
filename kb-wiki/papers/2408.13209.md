# Statistical uncertainty quantification for multireference covariant density functional theory

> We present a theoretical framework to quantify statistical uncertainties in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) for both nuclear matter and finite nuclei, based on a relativistic point-coupling energy density functional (EDF). By sampling approximately one million parameter sets, with nine parameters varied around their values in the PC-PK1 functional, we construct a probability density function for nuclear matter properties. Incorporating empirical values of nuclear matter at saturation density and those of predictions from chiral nuclear forces, and measured $B(E2)$ values of finite nuclei, we infer posterior distributions for the model parameters within a Bayesian framework. These posterior distributions are then propagated to the low-lying states of finite nuclei using the newly developed subspace-projected (SP)-CDFT approach, in which the wave functions of target EDF parameter sets are expanded in a subspace spanned by low-lying states obtained from a set of training parameterizations. We find that the observables of low-lying states in deformed nuclei $^{150}$Nd and $^{150}$Sm are well reproduced once statistical uncertainties are taken into account. In contrast, those of near spherical nuclei $^{136}$Xe and $^{136}$Ba remain difficult to describe within the present framework, a limitation that is expected to be alleviated by extending the model space to include quasiparticle excitations.

## Key claim
The paper presents a framework for quantifying statistical uncertainties in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) predictions for nuclear low-lying states, but the method successfully reproduces observables only for deformed nuclei (150Nd and 150Sm) while near-spherical nuclei (136Xe and 136Ba) remain difficult to describe within the present implementation, a limitation the authors expect to be alleviated by extending the model space to include quasiparticle excitations.

## Method
The authors sampled approximately one million parameter sets with nine parameters varied around their PC-PK1 values, constructed a probability density function for nuclear matter properties, and used Bayesian inference with empirical saturation density values, chiral nuclear force predictions, and measured B(E2) values to obtain posterior parameter distributions. These posterior distributions were propagated to finite nuclei low-lying states using the newly developed subspace-projected (SP)-CDFT approach, where wave functions for target EDF parameter sets are expanded in a subspace spanned by low-lying states from training parameterizations. The SP-CDFT emulator achieves speed-up factors up to 10^4 compared to full MR-CDFT calculations by decomposing the Hamiltonian kernel into parameter-free and parameter-dependent terms.

## Key numbers
- Approximately one million parameter sets were sampled, with nine parameters varied around their PC-PK1 values
- For 150Nd, the SP-CDFT emulator reproduces ground-state energy with 0.02% relative error, proton radius with 0.2% relative error, excitation energy Ex(2+1) with 4% relative error, and B(E2) with 2% relative error
- For 150Sm, the SP-CDFT emulator reproduces ground-state energy with 0.01% relative error, proton radius with 0.1% relative error, excitation energy Ex(2+1) with 13% relative error, and B(E2) with 4% relative error
- The speed-up factor of SP-CDFT compared to MR-CDFT reaches approximately 10^4 when the number of sampling EDFs reaches 10^6
- The ratio of computational time per kernel between MR-CDFT and SP-CDFT (Delta T1/Delta T2) is approximately 10^5 for 150Nd
- 14 training sets (Nt=14) with kmax=3 were selected for subsequent calculations, where the mean relative error in ground-state energy decreased to 0.04% and stabilized

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian uncertainty quantification studies in nuclear DFT (Goriely 2014, Dobaczewski 2014, McDonnell 2015, Agbemava 2014, Agbemava 2019) from ground-state bulk properties to nuclear low-lying states, which had been computationally prohibitive due to the need for massive repeated multireference calculations. The SP-CDFT approach combines multireference CDFT with the eigenvector continuation method (Frame 2018, Konig 2020, Furnstahl 2020) and enables the quantification of statistical uncertainties in excitation energies and transition strengths. The framework enables future studies of correlations between nuclear low-lying state properties and neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, and can be extended to include quasiparticle excitations to improve descriptions of near-spherical nuclei.
