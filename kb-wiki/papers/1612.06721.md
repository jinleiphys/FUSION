# Structure of the Energy-Momentum Tensor and Applications

> The probably most fundamental information about a particle is contained in the matrix elements of its energy momentum tensor (EMT) which are accessible from hard-exclusive reactions via generalized parton distribution functions. The spin decomposition of the nucleon and Ji sum rule are one example. Less prominent but equally important information is encoded in the stress tensor, related to the spatial components of the EMT, which shows in detail how the strong forces inside the nucleon balance to form a bound state. This provides not only unique insights on nucleon structure. It also leads to fascinating new applications to hadron spectroscopy which allow us to formulate new interpretations of the charmonium-nucleon pentaquarks discovered by LHCb. Recent progress is reviewed in this short overview article.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the structure of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of hadrons, focusing on the D-term as the last unknown global property of the nucleon, and argues that the stress tensor and D-term can be applied to describe hidden-charm pentaquark states observed at LHCb, though the D-term itself remains experimentally unknown.

## Method

The authors review the definition of EMT form factors A(t), B(t), and D(t) from matrix elements of the EMT, and explain how 3D Fourier transforms in the Breit frame yield static energy density, pressure p(r), and shear force s(r) distributions. They survey calculations of the D-term across multiple theoretical approaches: free Klein-Gordon theory, soft-pion theorems, chiral perturbation theory, the liquid drop model for nuclei, the chiral quark soliton model, lattice QCD, Skyrme model studies of the nucleon and Delta resonance, and Q-ball and Q-cloud models in scalar theories. They then apply the EMT densities to compute an effective charmonium-nucleon interaction potential and predict bound states.

## Key numbers

- The D-term of a scalar boson in free Klein-Gordon theory is D = -1.
- The D-term of a nucleus in the liquid drop model scales as D_nucleus proportional to A^(7/3), where A is the mass number.
- In the chiral quark soliton model, the pressure p(r) inside the nucleon is positive for r < r_0 and negative for r > r_0, with r_0 approximately 0.5–0.6 fm.
- The narrow resonance P_c^+(4450) is observed in about (4.1 ± 0.5 ± 1.1)% of Lambda_b^0 → J/Psi p K^- decays at LHCb.
- The broad resonance P_c^+(4380) is observed in about (8.4 ± 0.7 ± 4.2)% of the same decays.
- A predicted Delta-psi(2S) s-wave bound state has mass around 4.5 GeV with width around 70 MeV, and a p-wave resonance around 4.9 GeV with width of order 150 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on EMT form factors initiated by Pagels (1966) and Polyakov (1999, 2002), and connects to studies of the D-term in the chiral quark soliton model by Petrov (1998) and Goeke (2007), lattice QCD by Hagler (2003), and Skyrme model calculations by Cebulla (2007). The application to pentaquarks builds on the charmonium-nucleon interaction proposed by Voloshin (1979) and the specific bound-state interpretation of P_c(4450) by Eides (2015). The work enables experimental tests at Jefferson Lab and COMPASS to constrain EMT form factors, which could then inform the dynamics of hidden-charm pentaquark states observed at LHCb.
