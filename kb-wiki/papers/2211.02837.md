# Isoscaling in central Sn+Sn collisions at 270 MeV/u

> Experimental information on fragment emissions is important in understanding the dynamics of nuclear collisions and in the development of transport model simulating heavy-ion collisions. The composition of complex fragments emitted in the heavy-ion collisions can be explained by statistical models, which assume that thermal equilibrium is achieved at collision energies below 100 MeV/u. Our new experimental data together with theoretical analyses for light particles from Sn+Sn collisions at 270 MeV/u, suggest that the hypothesis of thermal equilibrium breaks down for particles emitted with high transfer momentum. To inspect the system's properties in such limit, the scaling features of the yield ratios of particles from two systems, a neutron-rich system of ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ and a nearly symmetric system of ${}^{108}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{112}\mathrm{Sn}$, are examined in the framework of the statistical multifragmentation model and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model. The isoscaling from low energy particles agree with both models. However the observed breakdown of isoscaling for particles with high transverse momentum cannot be explained by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model.

## Key claim

The hypothesis of thermal equilibrium breaks down for particles emitted with high transverse momentum in Sn+Sn collisions at 270 MeV/u, as evidenced by the breakdown of isoscaling for particles with high transverse momentum, which cannot be explained by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model.

## Method

The SπRIT collaboration measured charged particles (p, d, t, ³He, ⁴He) from central collisions (impact parameter b < 1.5 fm) of ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn (neutron-rich, N/Z = 1.56) and ¹⁰⁸Sn+¹¹²Sn (nearly symmetric, N/Z = 1.20) at 270 MeV/u using the SπRIT Time Projection Chamber at RIKEN. Yield ratios R₂₁(N,Z) between the two systems were examined as a function of transverse momentum per nucleon (p_T/A) and compared to predictions from the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) and two parametrizations of the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) model, AMD^(S) and AMD^(F).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 270 MeV/u for all four Sn+Sn reaction systems
- Impact parameter selection: b < 1.5 fm for central collisions
- The H-He temperature increases from approximately 8 MeV to 10 MeV with increasing p_T/A up to 280 MeV/c, then increases dramatically above that threshold
- Isoscaling parameter α = 0.29 and β = -0.23 for particles with p_T/A < 280 MeV/c
- For isotopes with p_T/A ≈ 500 MeV/c, R₂₁ ≈ 0.5 for t, ³He and ⁴He, meaning 50% fewer tritons are produced from the neutron-rich system than from the nearly symmetric system
- Momentum resolution: 1.6%; dE/dx resolution: 4.6% for single tracks
- Systematic uncertainties: about 5% for absolute yield and 2% for yield ratios below p_T/A < 400 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier isoscaling investigations at low incident energies (e.g., Xu et al. (2000) for Sn+Sn at 50 MeV/u) to 270 MeV/u, where thermal equilibrium assumptions break down. The observed isoscaling breakdown for high transverse momentum particles cannot be reproduced by the AMD model, in contrast to the successful description of isoscaling at lower energies by both SMM and AMD. The results call for further theoretical investigations into the mechanisms responsible for fragment production in non-equilibrium environments and may provide constraints on the nuclear equation of state through the relationship between the isoscaling parameter α and the symmetry energy coefficient.
