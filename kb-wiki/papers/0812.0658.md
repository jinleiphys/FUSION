# Di-neutron correlation in light neutron-rich nuclei

> Using a three-body model with density-dependent contact interaction, we discuss the root mean square distance between the two valence neutrons in $^{11}$Li nuclues as a function of the center of mass of the neutrons relative to the core nucleus $^9$Li. We show that the mean distance takes a pronounced minimum around the surface of the nucleus, indicating a strong surface di-neutron correlation. We demonstrate that the pairing correlation plays an essential role in this behavior. We also discuss the di-neutron structure in the $^8$He nucleus.

# Di-neutron correlation in light neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim
Using a three-body model with density-dependent contact interaction, the authors show that the root mean square distance between the two valence neutrons in $^{11}$Li takes a pronounced minimum around the nuclear surface, indicating a strong surface di-neutron correlation. The abstract glosses over the fact that this surface enhancement of the pairing force is *not* primarily an effect of the density dependence of the interaction, but rather that the density dependence of a zero-range force is needed to mock up the finite range of a density-independent force.

## Method
The authors employed a three-body model (core + two valence neutrons) with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction, equivalent to the particle-particle Tamm-Dancoff approximation, to compute the local pair coherence length and two-particle density of the Cooper pair in $^{11}$Li as a function of the center-of-mass distance $R$ from the $^9$Li core. For $^8$He, they used a core+4n five-body model with the same density-dependent contact pairing interaction, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus particle number projection method.

## Key numbers
- In $^{11}$Li, the root mean square distance between the two valence neutrons shows a pronounced minimum at $R \sim 3$ fm (the nuclear surface), where the local expectation value of the neutron-neutron interaction $E_{\rm pair}(R)$ is maximal.
- For the uncorrelated [(1p$_{1/2})^2$] and [(2s$_{1/2})^2$] configurations, the Cooper pair continuously expands as it moves away from the center of the nucleus, in marked contrast to the interacting case where it becomes smaller from inside to the surface.
- In $^8$He, the two-particle density for a spin-up and spin-down neutron shows a strong concentration around relative angle $\theta \sim 0$ at the nuclear surface, while the four-particle density for the dineutron-dineutron configuration peaks around $\theta \sim \pi/2$, arising from the main [(1p$_{3/2})^4$] configuration.

## Context
This work extends the di-neutron correlation concept originally proposed by Migdal (1972) and explicitly exploited by Hansen and Jonson (1987) for the matter radius of $^{11}$Li, following recent experimental indications from Coulomb dissociation measurements (Nakamura et al., 2006). The surface di-neutron correlation behavior is qualitatively the same as found in neutron matter (Matsuo, 2006) and has subsequently been confirmed in heavier superfluid nuclei (Pillet et al., 2007) using the finite-range Gogny interaction, as well as in an older study with a simple Yukawa interaction (Ishara, 1977). The results enable further studies of how spatial structure of valence neutrons evolves from 2n-halo nuclei like $^{11}$Li to systems with more neutrons, such as $^8$He.
