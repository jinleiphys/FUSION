# Collective excitations in the hot QCD medium and the propagation of Heavy Quarks

> This review explores the current understanding of collective excitations and the dynamics of heavy quark propagation in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We focus on three core aspects: the theoretical modelling of the QGP, including momentum anisotropy, medium-induced collisions, finite chemical potential, and non-ideal interactions; the collective behaviours within the plasma; and the interaction dynamics of heavy quarks as they traverse the medium. Along with the polarization energy loss mechanisms, we also review the possibility of energy gain due to thermal field fluctuations. Lastly, we discuss how these theoretical insights can be tested through experiments and outline possible directions for future research.

## Key claim

This review synthesizes the theoretical understanding of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) specifically due to interactions with soft collective modes (polarization energy loss), a phenomenon distinct from the more commonly studied hard elastic and inelastic collisions, and notes that energy gain is also possible when the heavy quark velocity is less than the phase velocity of the propagating mode.

## Method

The review employs kinetic theory to model the QGP medium, solving the linearized Boltzmann equation for the perturbed distribution functions of medium particles to derive the gluon self-energy \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}(K)\). The induced chromoelectric field is obtained via the gluon propagator in the temporal gauge, and the heavy quark energy loss rate is expressed in terms of the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions, with the energy loss arising from the imaginary parts of these functions when the phase velocity is less than unity.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature separating confined and deconfined QCD matter is \(T_c \sim 155\) MeV, as suggested by experimental investigations.
* The Debye mass for an isotropic QGP medium is given by \(m_D^2 = \frac{g_s^2 T^2}{3}\left(N_c + \frac{N_f}{2}\right)\), where \(N_c\) is the number of colours and \(N_f\) the number of quark flavours.
* The plasma frequency, the minimum frequency of a plasma wave at \(k=0\), is \(\omega_p = m_D/\sqrt{3}\).
* For a charm quark (mass \(M = 1.5\) GeV) and a bottom quark (mass \(M = 5.0\) GeV) at temperature \(T = 0.3\) GeV, the polarization energy loss per unit length saturates at high momentum, with heavier quarks saturating more gradually than lighter ones.

## Context

This review extends prior work on heavy quark energy loss in QGP, which has largely focused on hard elastic (\(2 \rightarrow 2\)) and inelastic (gluon radiation, \(2 \rightarrow 3\)) collisions (Svetitsky 1987, Moore 2004, Dokshitzer 2001), by systematically reviewing the less-explored polarization energy loss from soft collective modes. The formalism builds on the kinetic theory approach to collective modes developed by Klimov (1982), Romatschke and Strickland (2003), and Carrington (2014), and the review identifies gaps in the literature regarding the combined effects of anisotropy, collisions, chemical potential, and quasi-particle descriptions on this energy loss mechanism.
