# Statistical model analysis of hadron yields in proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions at SIS 18 energies

> The HADES data from p+Nb collisions at center of mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 3.2 GeV are analyzed by employing a statistical model. Accounting for the identified hadrons $π^0$, $η$, $Λ$, $K^{0}_{s}$, $ω$ allows a surprisingly good description of their abundances with parameters $T_{chem}=(99\pm11)$ MeV and $μ_{b}=(619\pm34)$ MeV, which fits well in the chemical freeze-out systematics found in heavy-ion collisions. In supplement we reanalyze our previous HADES data from Ar+KCl collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.6 GeV with an updated version of the statistical model. We address equilibration in heavy-ion collisions by testing two aspects: the description of yields and the regularity of freeze-out parameters from a statistical model fit. Special emphasis is put on feed-down contributions from higher-lying resonance states which have been proposed to explain the experimentally observed $Ξ^-$ excess present in both data samples.

## Key claim

The statistical model can describe hadron yields from p+Nb collisions at √sNN = 3.2 GeV as well as it does for the larger Ar+KCl system at √sNN = 2.6 GeV, which questions the often-drawn connection between statistical model success and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The HADES collaboration applied the statistical model THERMUS (v3.0) with a mixed canonical ensemble (exact strangeness conservation, grand canonical for other quantum numbers) to fit measured multiplicities of π0, η, Λ, K0s, ω from p+Nb collisions at √sNN = 3.2 GeV, and reanalyzed previous Ar+KCl data at √sNN = 2.6 GeV with the updated hadron spectrum from PDG 2014. The fit parameters included temperature Tchem, baryochemical potential μb, charge chemical potential μQ (for p+Nb only), strangeness correlation radius Rc, and fireball radius R, with feed-down contributions from higher-lying resonance states included.

## Key numbers

- p+Nb at √sNN = 3.2 GeV: Tchem = (99 ± 11) MeV, μb = (619 ± 34) MeV, μQ = (18 ± 18) MeV, Rc = (1.5 ± 0.8) fm, R = (2.0 ± 0.6) fm, χ2/d.o.f. = 2.9
- Ar+KCl at √sNN = 2.6 GeV (full sample): Tchem = (70 ± 3) MeV, μb = (748 ± 8) MeV, Rc = (2.9 ± 0.1) fm, R = (5.7 ± 0.8) fm, χ2/d.o.f. = 3.6
- Ar+KCl at √sNN = 2.6 GeV (reduced sample matching p+Nb species): Tchem = (80 ± 14) MeV, μb = (749 ± 12) MeV, Rc = (1.9 ± 0.1) fm, R = (4.2 ± 0.2) fm, χ2/d.o.f. = 2.5
- Ξ− excess over model in p+Nb: factor 8 ± 3 (reduced from factor 20 ± 9 reported earlier) when using THERMUS v3.0
- Ξ− excess over model in Ar+KCl: factor 15 ± 6 (reduced from factor 24 ± 9) when using THERMUS v3.0
- Average kinetic freeze-out temperature for p+Nb: ⟨Tkin⟩ = (91 ± 2) MeV, consistent with Tchem = (99 ± 11) MeV

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out systematics of Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2005) to the SIS 18 energy regime for both proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions, finding that p+Nb freeze-out parameters fit the established Tchem-μb curve as well as Ar+KCl parameters do. The results contradict the expectation that larger systems (Ar+KCl with ⟨Apart⟩ = 38.5) should show better thermalization than small systems (p+Nb with ⟨Apart⟩ = 2.8). The Ξ− excess observed in both systems challenges explanations based on strangeness exchange reactions (Li 2012, Graef 2014) or catalytic production (Kolomeitsev 2012), and the authors find feed-down from N* resonances (Steinheimer 2015) implausible given the predicted resonance yields. The upcoming HADES Au+Au data may provide further insights.
