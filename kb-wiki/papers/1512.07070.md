# Statistical model analysis of hadron yields in proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions at SIS 18 energies

> The HADES data from p+Nb collisions at center of mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 3.2 GeV are analyzed by employing a statistical model. Accounting for the identified hadrons $ПҖ^0$, $О·$, $Оӣ$, $K^{0}_{s}$, $Пү$ allows a surprisingly good description of their abundances with parameters $T_{chem}=(99\pm11)$ MeV and $Ој_{b}=(619\pm34)$ MeV, which fits well in the chemical freeze-out systematics found in heavy-ion collisions. In supplement we reanalyze our previous HADES data from Ar+KCl collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.6 GeV with an updated version of the statistical model. We address equilibration in heavy-ion collisions by testing two aspects: the description of yields and the regularity of freeze-out parameters from a statistical model fit. Special emphasis is put on feed-down contributions from higher-lying resonance states which have been proposed to explain the experimentally observed $Оһ^-$ excess present in both data samples.

## Key claim

The statistical model can describe hadron yields from p+Nb collisions at вҲҡsNN = 3.2 GeV as well as it does for the larger Ar+KCl system at вҲҡsNN = 2.6 GeV, which questions the often-drawn connection between statistical model success and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The HADES collaboration applied the statistical model THERMUS (v3.0) with a mixed canonical ensemble (exact strangeness conservation, grand canonical for other quantum numbers) to fit measured multiplicities of ПҖ0, О·, Оӣ, K0s, Пү from p+Nb collisions at вҲҡsNN = 3.2 GeV, and reanalyzed previous Ar+KCl data at вҲҡsNN = 2.6 GeV with the updated hadron spectrum from PDG 2014. The fit parameters included temperature Tchem, baryochemical potential Ојb, charge chemical potential ОјQ (for p+Nb only), strangeness correlation radius Rc, and fireball radius R, with feed-down contributions from higher-lying resonance states included.

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out systematics of Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2005) to the SIS 18 energy regime for both proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions, finding that p+Nb freeze-out parameters fit the established Tchem-Ојb curve as well as Ar+KCl parameters do. The results contradict the expectation that larger systems (Ar+KCl with вҹЁApartвҹ© = 38.5) should show better thermalization than small systems (p+Nb with вҹЁApartвҹ© = 2.8). The ОһвҲ’ excess observed in both systems challenges explanations based on strangeness exchange reactions (Li 2012, Graef 2014) or catalytic production (Kolomeitsev 2012), and the authors find feed-down from N* resonances (Steinheimer 2015) implausible given the predicted resonance yields. The upcoming HADES Au+Au data may provide further insights.
