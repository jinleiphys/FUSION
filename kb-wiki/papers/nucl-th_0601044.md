# Density Matrix Renormalization Group and the Nuclear Shell Model

> We describe the use of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method as a means of approximately solving large-scale nuclear shell-model problems. We focus on an angular-momentum-conserving variant of the method and report test results for the nucleus $^{48}Cr$. The calculation is able to reproduce both the ground state energy and the energy of the first excited state, by diagonalizing matrices much smaller than those of the full shell model.

## Key claim

The angular-momentum-conserving variant of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (JDMRG) can reproduce both the ground state energy and the first excited state energy of ⁴⁸Cr by diagonalizing matrices much smaller than the full shell model, but the superblock matrix dimensions remain a sizable fraction (5-10%) of the full space due to the three-block strategy used.

## Method

The authors implemented a JDMRG method that preserves rotational symmetry throughout the iterative inclusion of single-particle levels, adding complete spherical shells rather than partial shells. For the test case of ⁴⁸Cr with four valence neutrons and four valence protons outside ⁴⁰Ca, restricted to the 1f2p shell and using the KB3 hamiltonian, they separated neutron from proton orbitals into a three-block strategy: an enlarged neutron block was immersed in a medium consisting of the remaining neutron levels and all proton levels. The warmup phase built initial truncated blocks by coupling identical neutron and proton blocks for N=Z systems targeting the 0⁺ ground state, and the sweep phase iteratively grew and truncated blocks until the minimum energy changed by less than 10⁻⁴ MeV between sweeping stages.

## Key numbers

- Exact ground state energy of ⁴⁸Cr: -32.95 MeV; JDMRG with m=50 gave -32.80 MeV, about 150 keV from exact.
- Maximum matrix dimension for m=50: 3,657 states, compared to the full 0⁺ space of 41,355 states (roughly 8-9% of the full basis).
- Excitation energy of the lowest 2⁺ state: JDMRG gave 0.84 MeV versus exact 0.81 MeV, with a matrix less than 5% of the full matrix.
- Excitation energy of the lowest 4⁺ state: JDMRG gave 2.02 MeV versus exact 1.82 MeV.
- Excitation energy of the lowest 6⁺ state: JDMRG gave 3.95 MeV versus exact 3.40 MeV.
- Ground state energy for m=20: -32.28 MeV; for m=25: -32.47 MeV; for m=30: -32.57 MeV; for m=35: -32.66 MeV; for m=40: -32.72 MeV; for m=45: -32.76 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier DMRG applications to nuclear structure, which had mixed success: Papenbrock (2002) found excellent results for ²⁸Si but poor convergence for ⁵⁶Ni, while an earlier particle-hole variant by Sevi (2000) required retaining a substantial fraction of the full space. The JDMRG method builds on the non-Abelian DMRG formalism of McCulloch and Gulácsi (2001) for preserving symmetries, and was previously applied in nuclear physics only in the Gamow Shell Model context. The results enable future work on heavier nuclei by developing a two-block JDMRG code that mixes neutron and proton orbits, and by exploring improved level ordering schemes from Legeza (2003) and dynamic truncation based on neglected density matrix eigenvalues.
