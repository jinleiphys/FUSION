# Photon-tagged correlations in heavy-ion collisions: kinematic requirements and a case study

> Photon-tagged correlations may be useful to determine how the dense partonic medium produced in heavy-ion collisions affects the fragmentation of high-energy quarks and gluons into a leading hadron. In these proceedings, I discuss the kinematic requirements for the hadron and the prompt photon transverse momentum cuts. A case study at LHC energy, tagging on p_T > 20 GeV and p_T > 50 GeV photons, is then briefly examined.

## Key claim

Photon-tagged momentum-imbalance distributions can reflect the quark fragmentation function into a pion only under specific kinematic conditions: the hadron transverse momentum must be much larger than the medium scale, the photon cut must be much larger than the hadron cut to provide a wide range of the imbalance variable, and the photon cut must be large enough (above about 30 GeV at LHC) to suppress the fragmentation-photon background channel.

## Method

The author computes leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD cross sections for prompt-photon–pion (γ–π⁰) pair production in Pb–Pb collisions at LHC energy (√sNN = 5.5 TeV). Standard fragmentation functions are used for proton–proton reactions, while medium-modified fragmentation functions in central Pb–Pb collisions are obtained using the BDMPS quenching weight. Two sets of transverse momentum cuts are examined: Case A (pion cut 10 GeV, photon cut 20 GeV) and Case B (pion cut 10 GeV, photon cut 50 GeV).

## Key numbers

- The photon transverse momentum cut must satisfy p_T^γ ≳ 30 GeV at LHC to ensure the direct-photon channel dominates over the fragmentation-photon channel, based on the condition x = 2 p_T^γ / √sNN ≳ 10⁻².
- In Case A (pion cut 10 GeV, photon cut 20 GeV), the imbalance variable z ranges only from 0.5 to 1, and the differential cross section dσ/dz ranges from about 5×10¹ to 5×10² pb, corresponding to about 7×10² to 7×10³ events in central Pb–Pb collisions with integrated luminosity 5×10⁻⁴ pb⁻¹.
- In Case B (pion cut 10 GeV, photon cut 50 GeV), the imbalance variable z ranges from 0.2 to 0.9 in p–p and to 0.6 in Pb–Pb, and the differential cross section dσ/dz ranges from about 1 to 3×10² pb, corresponding to about 15 to 4×10³ events.
- The photon cut must be strictly below 100 GeV at LHC to maintain reasonable counting rates.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Wang (1996) and detailed investigations by Arleo (2004, 2006) on using photon-tagged correlations to access medium-modified fragmentation functions in heavy-ion collisions. The study specifies the kinematic hierarchy (Λ_QCD, T ≪ pion cut ≪ photon cut ≪ √sNN) required for the method to work, and demonstrates that at LHC the photon cut must be at least 50 GeV rather than 20 GeV to suppress the fragmentation-photon background and provide a useful range of the momentum-imbalance variable. This enables future experimental measurements at LHC (particularly with ALICE) to probe vacuum and medium-modified quark fragmentation functions.
