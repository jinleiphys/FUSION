# Three particle Lévy HBT from PHENIX

> Bose-Einstein correlations of identical bosons reveal information about the space-time structure of particle emission from the sQGP formed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Previous measurements of two particle correlations have shown that the source can best be described by a symmetric Levy distribution. Here we report on the measurement of three-particle correlations in 0-30 % centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_\text{NN}}} = 200$ GeV, and describe them with a Levy type source. This measurement may shed light on hadron creation mechanisms beyond chaotic emission. We measure three particle correlation strength ($λ_3$) as a function of pair transverse momentum. This parameter, combined with two-particle correlation strength $λ_2$ may reveal the level of chaoticity and coherence in particle production.

# Three particle Lévy HBT from PHENIX

## Key claim

Three-particle Bose-Einstein correlations measured in 0-30% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV can be described by a Lévy-type source, and the preliminary results indicate that the system is consistent with a purely chaotic, core-halo picture with no significant partial coherence, within the current statistical uncertainties.

## Method

The PHENIX Collaboration measured three-pion correlation functions for same-charged pion triplets in 0-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using data from the 2010 running period. The correlation functions were fitted with a model assuming a Lévy-stable source distribution, with the Lévy scale parameter R and Lévy exponent α fixed to values obtained from previous two-pion correlation measurements on the same dataset (Adare 2017). Coulomb repulsion between pions was corrected using a generalized Riverside method, and a linear background term was included in the fit function.

## Key numbers

- Three-particle correlation strength λ₃ was measured as a function of transverse mass for 31 different transverse mass bins; all values lie within the range [0, 5] as required by the core-halo model, within errors.
- The core-halo independent parameter κ₃, defined as (λ₃ − 3λ₂) / (2√(λ₂³)), is consistent with κ₃ = 1 within errors, indicating no partial coherence.
- The analysis uses 0-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous two-particle femtoscopic measurements by PHENIX (Adare 2017) which established that the source function is best described by a Lévy distribution rather than a Gaussian source (Adler 2006, Csanad 2005, Afanasiev 2007, Adam 2015). By combining two- and three-particle correlation strengths, the measurement enables tests of the core-halo model and the role of partial coherence in pion production, following the theoretical framework of Csorgo (1994) and Bolz (1992). The preliminary results suggest that the source is chaotic and can be described by a purely core-halo picture, with no evidence for partial coherence.
