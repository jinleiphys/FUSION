# Theoretical description of the neutron beta decay in the standard model at the level of 10^{-5}

> In the framework of the Standard Model (SM) a theoretical description of the neutron beta decay is given at the level of 10^{-5}. The neutron lifetime and correlation coefficients of the neutron beta decay for a polarized neutron, a polarized electron and an unpolarized proton are calculated at the account for i) the radiative corrections of order O(αE_e/m_N) ~ 10^{-5} to Sirlin's outer and inner radiative corrections of order O(α/π), ii) the corrections of order O(E^2_e/m^2_N) ~ 10^{-5}, caused by weak magnetism and proton recoil, and iii) Wilkinson's corrections of order 10^{-5} (Wilkinson, Nucl. Phys. A377, 474 (1982)). These corrections define the SM background of the theoretical description of the neutron beta decay at the level of 10^{-5}, which is required by experimental searches of interactions beyond the SM with experimental uncertainties of a few parts of 10^{-5}.

## Key claim

The paper provides a Standard Model description of neutron beta decay at the level of $10^{-5}$ by including radiative corrections of order $O(\alpha E_e/m_N)$, corrections of order $O(E^2_e/m^2_N)$ from weak magnetism and proton recoil, and Wilkinson's corrections, all of which together define the SM background needed for experimental searches of beyond-Standard-Model interactions with uncertainties of a few parts of $10^{-5}$.

## Method

The authors calculated the neutron lifetime and all correlation coefficients for the electron-energy and angular distribution of neutron beta decay for a polarized neutron, a polarized electron, and an unpolarized proton, using the general form proposed by Jackson et al. (1957) and Ebel and Feldman (1957). They included: (i) radiative corrections of order $O(\alpha E_e/m_N)$ as next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections in the large nucleon mass expansion to Sirlin's outer and inner $O(\alpha/\pi)$ radiative corrections; (ii) outer $O(\alpha E_e/m_N)$ radiative corrections from Sirlin's outer corrections and the phase-volume calculated to NLO; (iii) $O(E^2_e/m^2_N)$ corrections from weak magnetism and proton recoil; and (iv) Wilkinson's corrections of order $10^{-5}$ from Wilkinson (1982). The analytical expressions were programmed for numerical analysis at the axial coupling constant $g_A = 1.2764$.

## Key numbers

- End-point energy of the electron-energy spectrum: $E_0 = 1.2926$ MeV, calculated from neutron mass $m_n = 939.9564$ MeV, proton mass $m_p = 938.2721$ MeV, and electron mass $m_e = 0.5110$ MeV
- Nucleon mass $m_N = (m_n + m_p)/2 = 938.9188$ MeV
- Axial coupling constant used for numerical calculations: $g_A = 1.2764$ (from Abele 2018)
- Electric radius of the proton: $r_p = 0.841$ fm (from Antognini 2013)
- Correlation function $\zeta(E_e)$ includes terms: $-5.57 \times 10^{-4} (E_0/E_e) - 3.20 \times 10^{-3} + 9.81 \times 10^{-3} (E_e/E_0) + 7.13 \times 10^{-5} (E^2_e/E^2_0) - 3.16 \times 10^{-5} (E_e/\beta E_0)$
- Electron asymmetry coefficient $A^{(\beta)}(E_e)$ includes terms: $-6.67 \times 10^{-5} (E_0/E_e) + 9.78 \times 10^{-4} - 4.18 \times 10^{-3} (E_e/E_0) - 1.04 \times 10^{-5} (E_0 - E_e)/(\beta^3 E_0)$
- Experimental electron-energy region for well-defined results: $0.811$ MeV $\le E_e \le 1.211$ MeV (from Abele 2018)
- Inner radiative correction functions: $\bar{g}_{\rm st}(E_e) = 0.098 (1 + 0.95 E_e/E_0)$ and $\bar{f}_{\rm st}(E_e) = 0.057 (1 + E_e/E_0)$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of radiative corrections of order $O(\alpha E_e/m_N)$ (Ivanov 2019b, 2020a) and corrections of order $O(E^2_e/m^2_N)$ from weak magnetism and proton recoil (Ivanov 2020b), adding outer $O(\alpha E_e/m_N)$ radiative corrections from Sirlin's outer corrections and the phase-volume. It provides the complete SM background at $10^{-5}$ level required by experiments with uncertainties of a few parts of $10^{-5}$ (Abele 2016, Dubbers 2021, Bodek 2019). The results can be used to improve the experimental value of the axial coupling constant $g_A$ and to search for interactions beyond the SM in experiments with polarized neutrons and electrons.
