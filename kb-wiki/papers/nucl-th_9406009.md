# ``Chaos" in Nuclear High Spin Spectroscopy

> The Projected Shell Model with zero-, two- and four-quasiparticle configurations is used to investigate the level statistics, i.e. chaoticity, of high spin spectroscopy. The model can describe many high spin phenomena and with the present configuration space has sufficient number of levels for statistical analysis. It is found that the degree of chaoticity has a sensitive dependence on the classification of levels in question, and that it steadily increases with excitation energy and angular momentum.

## Key claim

The degree of chaoticity (as measured by level-spacing statistics) in high-spin nuclear spectroscopy has a sensitive dependence on the classification of levels by excitation energy and angular momentum, and it steadily increases with both; the authors find that the GOE (Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble) distribution appears at high excitation energies and high spins, contradicting earlier work by Alhassid and Vretenar (1992) which found GOE only near band crossings.

## Method

The authors used the Projected Shell Model (PSM) with zero-, two-, and four-quasiparticle configurations built from a deformed Nilsson basis with three major shells (N=4,5,6 for neutrons; N=3,4,5 for protons). They applied angular momentum projection to restore rotational symmetry, used a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian with a quadrupole pairing term, and diagonalized in a basis of about 700 states (200 two-quasiparticle and 500 four-quasiparticle states) for the nucleus ¹⁶⁴Er. Level-spacing statistics were computed for groups of states classified by excitation energy above the yrast line and by angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The basis dimension is about 700 states, consisting of about 200 two-quasiparticle states and about 500 four-quasiparticle states, selected using an unprojected quasiparticle energy cut-off of 5 MeV.
- The total number of obtained levels is typically 460 for I = 4ℏ and 650 for I = 10ℏ.
- The highest state obtained after diagonalization for each I can reach an excitation region of 7 MeV above the yrast line.
- The quadrupole pairing strength G_Q is fixed at 0.16 times the monopole pairing strength G_M.
- The monopole pairing strength is given by G_M = [20.12 ∓ 13.13 (N-Z)/A] · A⁻¹, where the minus sign is for neutrons and the plus sign for protons.

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical analyses of nuclear level spectra by Hag, Pandey and Bohigas (1982) and Abul-Magd and Weidenmüller (1985), who were limited by insufficient experimental data and could not separate states by parity and angular momentum. It builds on theoretical approaches by Alhassid and Vretenar (1992) using the Interacting Boson Model and by Wu, Feng and Vallieres (1990) using the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model, but uses the Projected Shell Model which can describe realistic deformed heavy nuclei. The results contradict the finding of Alhassid and Vretenar (1992) that GOE statistics appear only near band crossings, and suggest that including four-quasiparticle configurations is essential for obtaining GOE statistics at high spins.
