# On relativistic approaches to the pion self-energy in nuclear matter

> We argue that, in contrast to the non-relativistic approach, a relativistic evaluation of the nucleon--hole and delta-isobar--nucleon hole contributions to the pion self-energy incorporates the s-wave scattering, which requires a more accurate evaluation. Therefore relativistic approach containing only these diagrams does not describe appropriately the pion self-energy in isospin symmetric nuclear matter. We conclude that, a correct relativistic approach to the pion self-energy should involve a more sophisticated calculation in order to satisfy the known experimental results on the near-threshold behaviour of the pion-nucleon (forward) scattering amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors argue that relativistic evaluations of the nucleon-hole and delta-isobar–nucleon hole contributions to the pion self-energy in isospin symmetric nuclear matter inadvertently incorporate s-wave scattering, which is not present in the non-relativistic reduction; therefore, a relativistic approach restricted to only these diagrams does not correctly describe the pion self-energy and requires a more sophisticated calculation to match experimental near-threshold behavior.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic and a non-relativistic calculation of the pion self-energy in a simple model using pseudovector pion-nucleon and pion-delta interactions, with short-range correlations simulated by phenomenological contact interactions parameterized by three Landau-Migdal parameters (\(g_{NN}^{\prime}\), \(g_{N\Delta}^{\prime}\), \(g_{\Delta\Delta}^{\prime}\)). They compared the low-density limits of the relativistic and non-relativistic expressions for the particle-hole and delta-hole contributions, and evaluated the forward s-wave scattering amplitude at threshold to highlight the discrepancy with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(f = 0.988\) and the Chew-Low value for the pion-delta coupling is \(f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2f\).
- The Landau-Migdal parameters used are \(g_{NN}^{\prime} = 0.6\), \(g_{N\Delta}^{\prime} = 0.24 \pm 0.10\), \(g_{\Delta\Delta}^{\prime} = 0.6\).
- At saturation density \(n = n_0\) with effective nucleon mass \(M^{\ast} = 0.8M\), the relativistic calculation yields a forward s-scattering amplitude at threshold \(\omega = m_{\pi}\) of \(\frac{1}{n}\mathrm{Re}\Pi(m_{\pi},0) \simeq 1.3\;\mathrm{fm}\) without correlations, and \(\frac{1}{n}\mathrm{Re}\tilde{\Pi}(m_{\pi},0) \simeq 1.11\;\mathrm{fm}\) with short-range correlations included.

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic calculations of the pion self-energy in isospin symmetric nuclear matter (e.g., Mao, Herbst, Dmitriev, Xia, Lutz) by identifying the origin of the discrepancy with non-relativistic results: the relativistic evaluation of particle-hole and delta-hole diagrams introduces an s-wave contribution that is absent in the non-relativistic reduction. The authors conclude that a correct relativistic approach must include additional diagrams beyond the simple particle-hole and delta-hole ones, because the s-wave scattering is dominated by short-distance interactions and chiral symmetry constraints (Tom) and experimental data (Sigg, Koch) show the near-threshold isospin-even amplitude is much smaller than the values obtained here.
