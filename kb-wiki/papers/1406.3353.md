# Flavour symmetry breaking in the kaon parton distribution amplitude

> We compute the kaon's valence-quark (twist-two parton) distribution amplitude (PDA) by projecting its Poincare'-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave-function onto the light-front. At a scale ζ=2GeV, the PDA is a broad, concave and asymmetric function, whose peak is shifted 12-16% away from its position in QCD's conformal limit. These features are a clear expression of SU(3)-flavour-symmetry breaking. They show that the heavier quark in the kaon carries more of the bound-state's momentum than the lighter quark and also that emergent phenomena in QCD modulate the magnitude of flavour-symmetry breaking: it is markedly smaller than one might expect based on the difference between light-quark current masses. Our results add to a body of evidence which indicates that at any energy scale accessible with existing or foreseeable facilities, a reliable guide to the interpretation of experiment requires the use of such nonperturbatively broadened PDAs in leading-order, leading-twist formulae for hard exclusive processes instead of the asymptotic PDA associated with QCD's conformal limit. We illustrate this via the ratio of kaon and pion electromagnetic form factors: using our nonperturbative PDAs in the appropriate formulae, $F_K/F_π=1.23$ at spacelike-$Q^2=17\,{\rm GeV}^2$, which compares satisfactorily with the value of $0.92(5)$ inferred in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation at $s=17\,{\rm GeV}^2$.

## Key claim

The kaon's valence-quark distribution amplitude at ζ=2 GeV is a broad, concave, and asymmetric function with its peak shifted 12-16% away from the conformal limit position, but this SU(3)-flavour-symmetry breaking is markedly smaller than one might expect based on the difference between light-quark current masses because emergent phenomena in QCD modulate the magnitude.

## Method

The authors computed the kaon's twist-two parton distribution amplitude by projecting its Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave function onto the light-front. They solved the quark gap equations and kaon Bethe-Salpeter equation numerically using two different truncation schemes: rainbow-ladder (RL) and DCSB-improved (DB) kernels, with the interaction from Qin (2011). Moments of the PDA were computed via Eq. (phimom) and the pointwise form was reconstructed using a Gegenbauer-α procedure with j_max=2, which converged to ε_m < 1%.

## Key numbers

- Peak location of kaon PDA: u=0.56 (RL), u=0.58 (DB), u=0.56^{+0.02}_{-0.01} (lattice-QCD inference), representing a 12-16% shift from u=1/2
- Ratio of kaon to pion electromagnetic form factors at spacelike Q²=17 GeV²: F_K/F_π = 1.23 (DB kernel prediction)
- Experimental value from e⁺e⁻ annihilation at s=17 GeV²: F_K/F_π = 0.92(5)
- DB kernel parameters: ᾱ=1.42, α̂=1.14, a₂^{ᾱ}=0.074, a₁^{α̂}=0.076, a₃^{α̂}=0.011
- RL kernel parameters: ᾱ=0.68, α̂=0.65, a₂^{ᾱ}=-0.32, a₁^{α̂}=0.27, a₃^{α̂}=0.054
- First moment ⟨u_Δ⟩: DB=0.040, RL=0.11, lattice-QCD=0.036(2)
- Second moment ⟨u_Δ²⟩: DB=0.23, RL=0.24, lattice-QCD=0.26(2)
- Ratio of s-to-u current-quark masses: approximately 27
- Ratio of leptonic decay constants f_K/f_π: approximately 1.2

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based computation of PDAs from the pion (Chang 2013) to the kaon, providing the first DSE calculation for a bound state with unequal current-mass quarks. The results are consistent with lattice-QCD results from Braun (2006) and Arthur (2010) as analyzed by Segovia (2013), and contradict the assumption that the asymptotic PDA φ^{asy}(u)=6u(1-u) can be used at currently accessible energy scales. The findings enable more reliable calculations of hard exclusive processes involving kaons, particularly for B-meson decays where CP violation parameters such as γ = Arg(V_ub^*) are extracted, and resolve a claimed 9σ discrepancy between pQCD and experiment for the kaon-to-pion form factor ratio.
