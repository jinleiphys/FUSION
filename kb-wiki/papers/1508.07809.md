# Charge-conjugation symmetric complete impulse approximation for the pion electromagnetic form factor in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> The pion form factor is calculated in the framework of the charge-conjugation invariant Covariant Spectator Theory. This formalism is established in Minkowski space and the calculation is set up in momentum space. In a previous calculation we included only the leading pole coming from the spectator quark (referred to as the relativistic impulse approximation). In this paper we also include the contributions from the poles of the quark which interacts with the photon and average over all poles in both the upper and lower half planes in order to preserve charge conjugation invariance (referred to as the $C$-symmetric complete impulse approximation). We find that for small pion mass these contributions are significant at all values of the four-momentum transfer $Q^2$ but, surprisingly, do not alter the shape obtained from the spectator poles alone.

## Key claim

The inclusion of all quark propagator poles (both spectator and active quark poles) in the charge-conjugation symmetric complete impulse approximation (C-CIA) yields significant contributions to the pion form factor at all values of the four-momentum transfer \(Q^2\) for small pion mass, yet surprisingly does not alter the shape obtained from the spectator poles alone in the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA).

## Method

The pion electromagnetic form factor was calculated in Minkowski space using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), with the triangle diagram evaluated by averaging over all six quark propagator poles in the upper and lower complex \(k_0\) half-planes to preserve charge-conjugation invariance. The calculation used a dressed quark mass function calibrated to lattice QCD data, a simplified pion vertex function approximated as \(\Gamma(p_1,p_2)=G_0 h(p_1^2) h(p_2^2)\gamma^5 L(\rho^2)\), and a dressed quark current satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity with damped propagators \(\tilde S(p)=h^2(p^2)S(p)\). The contributions from the three off-shell form factors \(f\), \(\delta\), and \(g\) in the quark current were analyzed separately for both spectator and active quark poles.

## Key numbers

* The pion form factor was calculated for momentum transfers up to \(Q^2 \approx 6\) GeV\(^2\), the region planned for the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade.
* The spectator pole contribution to the \(f\)-term has a \(Q^{-2}\) falloff at large \(Q^2\), consistent with the experimentally observed flattening of the \(Q^2 F_{\pi}(Q^2)\) curve.
* The active quark pole contribution to the \(f\)-term also exhibits a \(Q^{-2}\) asymptotic falloff, independent of the precise functional form of the quark form factors or details of the pion wave function.
* For fixed dressed quark masses, the spectator contribution to the \(f\)-term simplifies to \(F^{f,s}_{\pi}(Q^2) \rightarrow -G_0^2\int \frac{\mathrm d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{\mu^2}{4P_0k_zQ} \sum_{\eta,\eta'}\frac{\eta\eta' h^2_{\eta'\eta}L_{+,\eta}L_{-,\eta}}{m_\chi^2-p^2_{\eta'\eta}}\).

## Context

This work extends the previous RIA calculation by Biernat et al. (2014) (PhysRevD.89.016006) by including the previously omitted active quark pole contributions and enforcing charge-conjugation symmetry through pole averaging, an improvement called the C-CIA. The CST framework shares with the Dyson-Schwinger approach the generation of a dynamical quark mass consistent with two-body quark-antiquark dynamics, but unlike lattice QCD or Dyson-Schwinger equations, CST equations are solved in Minkowski space, enabling direct extension of results from spacelike to timelike regions. The results enable future work incorporating interaction currents, a dynamically calculated pion vertex function, and full dressing of the quark current to achieve a more exact calculation.
