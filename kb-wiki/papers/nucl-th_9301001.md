# Effect due to compositeness of nucleons in deep inelastic lepton nucleus scattering

> The off-shell behaviors of bound nucleons in deep inelastic lepton nucleus scattering are discussed in two scenarios with the basic constituents chosen to be baryon-mesons and quark-gluons respectively in light-cone formalism. It is found that when taking into account the effect due to internal quark structure of nucleons, the derived scaling variable for bound nucleons and the calculated nuclear structure functions are different from those in considering the baryon-mesons as the effective elementary constituents. This implies that the pure baryon-meson descriptions of nuclei give the inaccurate off-shell behavior of the bound nucleon structure function, thereby the quark-gluons seem to be the most appropriate degrees of freedom for nuclear descriptions. It is also shown that the EMC effect cannot be explained by nuclear binding effect from a sound theoretical basis.

# Effect due to compositeness of nucleons in deep inelastic lepton nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The paper argues that the EMC effect cannot be explained by nuclear binding effects (off-shell effects) from nucleonic degrees of freedom alone when the internal quark-gluon structure of nucleons is properly taken into account, contrary to claims from pure baryon-meson models that appeared to reproduce the data. The author shows that the off-shell ambiguities in identifying the scaling variable for bound nucleons are not resolved in light-cone hadronic approaches that treat nucleons as elementary, and that only by including the quark-gluon substructure can a reliable description be obtained.

## Method

The author compares two scenarios within light-cone perturbation theory: (1) a pure baryon-meson scenario where nucleons are treated as effective elementary Dirac particles, and (2) a quark-gluon two-level convolution scenario where the nucleus is a bound state of hadrons that are themselves composite states of quarks and gluons. In the first scenario, the scaling variable for bound nucleons is identified by kinematic analogy with free nucleons, leading to ambiguities between different choices of the off-shell variable. In the second scenario, the scaling variable for bound nucleons is derived from the constraint of overall energy conservation in the quark-parton model, following the method of Ma (1986) and Ma and Sun (1991). The nuclear structure function ratio \(F_2^A/F_2^N\) is calculated in both scenarios using the same light-cone momentum distribution for nucleons in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ratio \(F_2^A(x,Q^2)/F_2^N(x,Q^2)\) in the pure baryon-meson scenario reproduces the EMC data well for \(Q^2 = 5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with Fermi momentum \(k_F = 260\) MeV and separation energy \(\varepsilon = 35\) MeV, using the scaling variable \(x' = -q'^2/2p\cdot q'\).
- In the quark-gluon two-level convolution scenario, using the same inputs (\(k_F = 260\) MeV, \(Q^2 = 5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), \(\varepsilon = 35\) MeV), the calculated ratio cannot reproduce the EMC data with nucleonic degrees of freedom alone.
- The scaling variable for bound nucleons in the quark-gluon scenario reduces to \(x_B = Q^2 / 2 M_A \nu y\) in the Bjorken limit \(Q^2 \rightarrow \infty\), \(\nu \rightarrow \infty\) with fixed \(x = Q^2/2M\nu\).
- The SLAC data for A=Fe at \(Q^2 = 5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and beam energy \(8 < \epsilon < 24.5\) GeV, and new EMC data for A=Cu at \(4.4 < Q^2 < 40.4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and \(\epsilon = 120-280\) GeV, are used for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the debate on the origin of the EMC effect, first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (Aubert et al., 1983) and Bodek et al. (1983). It directly addresses the claim by Akulinichev et al. (1985) that the EMC effect could be explained by nuclear binding effects using nucleonic degrees of freedom alone, and the subsequent light-cone hadronic models of Ma and Sun (1990) and Oelfke, Sauer, and Coester (1990). The paper builds on Brodsky's (1984) argument that effective local Lagrangian field theory of baryon-mesons gives inaccurate off-shell behaviors due to internal quark structure, and on the relativistic composite model of hadrons by Brodsky, Huang, and Lepage (1983). The author's earlier work (Ma, 1991; Ma and Sun, 1991) on the two-level convolution model is extended to show that non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (such as pions, isobars, multiquark clusters, or intrinsic distortions of quark distributions) are unambiguously required to explain the EMC effect, recovering the original conclusion of Jaffe (1983).
