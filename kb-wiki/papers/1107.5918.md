# Precision Spectroscopy of Deeply Bound Pionic Atoms and Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry in Medium

> We study theoretically the formation spectra of deeply bound pionic atoms expected to be observed by experiments with high energy resolution at RIBF/RIKEN, and we discuss in detail the possibilities to extract new information on the pion properties at finite density from the observed spectra, which may provide information on partial restoration of chiral symmetry in medium. We find that the non-yrast pionic states such as 2s are expected to be seen in the (d,3He) spectra, which will be helpful to reduce uncertainties of the theoretical calculations in the neutron wave functions in nucleus. The observation of the 2s state with the ground 1s state is also helpful to reduce the experimental uncertainties associated in the calibration of the absolute excitation energy. We find that the nuclear densities probed by atomic pions are quite stable and almost constant for various atomic states and various nuclei. Effects of the pion wave function renormalization to the formation spectra are also evaluated.

## Key claim

The authors argue that simultaneous observation of the non-yrast 2s pionic state together with the ground 1s state in (d,3He) reactions at RIBF/RIKEN can reduce both theoretical uncertainties from neutron wave functions and experimental uncertainties from absolute excitation energy calibration, enabling more precise extraction of in-medium pion properties and the chiral condensate beyond the linear density approximation.

## Method

The authors performed theoretical calculations of pionic atom formation spectra using the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) for (d,3He) reactions on Sn and Te isotope targets, incorporating the optical potential formalism that connects the isovector s-wave parameter b1 to the in-medium pion decay constant and chiral condensate through in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner and Tomozawa-Weinberg relations. They evaluated the effective nuclear densities probed by different pionic states using the overlapping density S(r) = ρ(r)|R_nl(r)|^2 r^2, and constructed contour plots of binding energies and widths in the b0-Re(B0) and b1-Im(B0) parameter planes to assess the sensitivity of 1s and 2s states to different nuclear densities. The effects of pion wave function renormalization Z*_π on formation cross sections were also evaluated by comparing calculations with renormalized and unrenormalized wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The effective nuclear density probed by pionic atoms observed by X-ray spectroscopy is approximately ρ_e ≈ 0.5 ρ_0, derived from the Seki-Masutani correlation parameter α_s ≈ 0.23 m_π^3
- For pionic states in 121Sn calculated with the full optical potential, the effective nuclear densities range within 0.08 ≲ ρ_e ≲ 0.11 fm^-3, a narrower range than the 0.05 ≲ ρ_e ≲ 0.16 fm^-3 obtained with Coulomb wave functions alone
- The binding energy and width of the 1s state in 123Sn from the GSI experiment are B.E. = 3.744 ± 0.018 MeV and Γ = 0.341 ± 0.072 MeV
- The density dependence of the wave function renormalization at low density is Z*_π = 1 - βρ with β = 2.17 ± 0.04 fm^3
- The planned RIBF/RIKEN experiment aims for an energy resolution of ΔE = 150 keV FWHM, compared to the ΔE = 394 keV FWHM of the GSI data, using a deuteron beam intensity of ~1 × 10^12/second (more than 30 times higher than GSI) and a target thickness of 5 mg/cm^2

## Context

This work extends the experimental program of deeply bound pionic atoms pioneered at GSI by Suzuki et al. (2004), who measured 1s states in Sn isotopes and deduced the reduction of the chiral quark condensate in medium. The authors build on the theoretical framework of Jido et al. (2010) and Kolomeitsev et al. (2003) who established the connection between pionic atom observables and the in-medium quark condensate including the wave function renormalization factor. The paper provides predictions for the new high-precision experiments proposed at RIBF/RIKEN (Itahashi et al., 2010, 2011), showing that simultaneous observation of 1s and 2s states can overcome the limitations of the Seki-Masutani correlation that previously restricted density sensitivity to approximately 0.6ρ_0, enabling future studies of the chiral condensate beyond the linear density approximation.
