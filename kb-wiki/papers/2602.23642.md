# Particle number projected energies at finite temperature

> In this work, the particle number projection at finite temperature is incorporated into self-consistent Skyrme density functional calculations. In particular, the energies of compound nuclei as a function of deformations are calculated rigorously based on projected densities. Results show that the even-odd staggering effect in partition function gradually diminishes as the system approaches the critical temperature. The obtained fission barriers are similar to that without projection at finite temperature, although projected energies are different. The nuclear level density at the ground state and the barrier are also studied using the projection method and the discrete Gaussian method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact particle number projection (PNP) energies at finite temperature can be calculated within self-consistent Skyrme density functional theory, but the fission barriers obtained with PNP are similar to those without projection at high temperatures, even though the projected energies themselves are systematically lower by about 2.0 MeV.

## Method

The authors implement particle number projection after variation in finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SkM* force for the particle-hole channel and density-dependent delta interactions for the pairing channel. The projected energy is computed from gauge-angle-dependent single-particle density matrices using the generalized Wick theorem, while the projected entropy is obtained from the partition function via the relation S_p = ln(Z_p) - beta * d(ln Z_p)/d beta. Calculations are performed for the compound nuclei 238U and 292Fl in axial-symmetric coordinate spaces using the SkyAx code.

## Key numbers

- The exact PNP energies of 292Fl at T = 1.0 MeV are systematically lower than energies without PNP by about 2.0 MeV across deformations.
- The fission barrier height for 292Fl at T = 1.0 MeV with PNP is 5.47 MeV; without PNP it is 5.20 MeV.
- At zero temperature, the fission barrier heights for 292Fl with PNP and without PNP are 7.38 MeV and 7.69 MeV, respectively.
- The ratio a_f / a_0 of level density parameters at high excitation energies is 1.0718 for 238U and 1.0625 for 292Fl.
- The level density parameter a_0 is approximately A/9.97 for 238U and A/10.38 for 292Fl.
- The pairing strengths used are V_p = -480 MeV fm^{-3} and V_n = -450 MeV fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature particle number projection formalism developed by Fanto et al. (2017) for partition functions to the calculation of exact projected energies within self-consistent density functional theory, which had not been realized previously. The results enable more accurate determination of energy-dependent fission barriers and level density parameters for compound nuclei, providing microscopic inputs for statistical models that calculate survival probabilities of superheavy nuclei, as used by Qiang et al. (2024). The level density results agree with experimental data from Guttormsen et al. (2013) when collective enhancement factors are included, and the extracted a_f / a_0 ratios around 1.06-1.07 are consistent with the commonly adopted value of about 1.1 in the literature.
