# Study of nuclear modification factors of (anti-)hadrons and light (anti-)nuclei in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> The nuclear modification factors ($R_{AA}$) of $π^{\pm}, p(\bar p)$, and $d(\bar d)$ with $|y|<0.5, p_T<20.0$~GeV/c in peripheral (40-60\%) and central (0-5\%) Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV have been studied using the parton and hadron cascade ({\footnotesize PACIAE}) model plus the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence ({\footnotesize DCPC}) model. It is found that the $R_{AA}$ of light (anti)nuclei ($d, \bar d$) is similar to that of hadrons ($π^\pm, p, \bar p$), and the $R_{AA}$ of antiparticles is the same as that of particles. The suppression of $R_{AA}$ at high-$p_T$ strongly depends on event centrality and mass of the particles, i.e., the central collision is more suppressed than the peripheral collision. Besides, the yield ratios and double ratios for different particle species in $pp$ and Pb-Pb collisions are discussed, respectively. It is observed that the yield ratios and double ratios of $d$ to $p$ and $p$ to $π$ are similar to those of their anti-particles in three different collision systems, suggesting that the suppressions of matter ($π^{+}, p, d$) and the corresponding antimatter ($π^{-},\bar{p},\bar{d}$) are around the same level.

## Key claim

Using the PACIAE plus DCPC model, the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of light (anti-)nuclei (deuteron and antideuteron) is found to be similar to that of hadrons (pions and protons), and the R_AA of antiparticles is identical to that of particles, but only within the model's dynamical framework which does not assume equilibrium between matter and antimatter. The suppression at high transverse momentum depends on event centrality and particle mass, with central collisions showing stronger suppression than peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Parton And Hadron Cascade (PACIAE) model, based on PYTHIA 6.4, to simulate the full collision process from partonic initial states through parton rescattering, hadronization via Lund string fragmentation, and hadron rescattering in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. Light (anti-)nuclei production was then calculated using the Dynamically Constrained Phase Space Coalescence (DCPC) model, which integrates over the six-dimensional phase space of constituent nucleons subject to invariant mass and spatial separation constraints. Model parameters (K factor, parj(1,2,3)) were tuned by fitting to ALICE pion and proton transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions, with separate parameter sets for pp and Pb-Pb systems.

## Key numbers

- Transverse momentum range studied: p_T < 20.0 GeV/c with rapidity |y| < 0.5 for all particle species
- Centrality bins used: 0-5% (central) and 40-60% (peripheral) for Pb-Pb collisions
- DCPC model parameters: D_0 = 3 fm (diameter of nuclei), Delta m = 0.42 MeV/c^2 (allowed mass uncertainty)
- PACIAE parameters for pp collisions: K = 2, parj(1) = 0.15, parj(2) = 0.50, parj(3) = 0.60
- PACIAE parameters for Pb-Pb collisions: K = 2, parj(1) = 0.15, parj(2) = 0.38, parj(3) = 0.65
- The p/pi ratio peaks at p_T ~ 3.0 GeV/c and the d/p ratio peaks at p_T ~ 5.0 GeV/c in both central and peripheral Pb-Pb collisions
- Model R_AA results agree with ALICE data for p_T < 10.0 GeV/c; deviations appear for p_T > 10.0 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light (anti-)nuclei production using the PACIAE plus DCPC framework, which had previously been applied to transverse momentum distributions, energy dependence, scaling properties, and centrality dependence in both pp and A-A collisions (She et al., 2016; Dong et al., 2018; Liu et al., 2019). The results are compared with ALICE experimental data for R_AA of pions, protons, and deuterons (ALICE Collaboration, 2015; 2016; 2017) and with the double ratio R^D_AA defined in earlier ALICE publications (2014). The finding that matter and antimatter show identical suppression characteristics in this dynamical model, without equilibrium assumptions, provides a baseline for interpreting experimental observations of nuclear modification in heavy-ion collisions and motivates further model improvements, particularly at high p_T where discrepancies with data appear.
