# Magnetic permeability of near-critical 3d abelian Higgs model and duality

> The three-dimensional abelian Higgs model has been argued to be dual to a scalar field theory with a global U(1) symmetry. We show that this duality, together with the scaling and universality hypotheses, implies a scaling law for the magnetic permeablity chi_m near the line of second order phase transition: chi_m ~ t^nu, where t is the deviation from the critical line and nu ~ 0.67 is a critical exponent of the O(2) universality class. We also show that exactly on the critical lines, the dependence of magnetic induction on external magnetic field is quadratic, with a proportionality coefficient depending only on the gauge coupling. These predictions provide a way for testing the duality conjecture on the lattice in the Coulomb phase and at the phase transion.

## Key claim
The magnetic permeability χ_m of the three-dimensional abelian Higgs model in the Coulomb phase scales as χ_m ~ t^ν near the second-order phase transition line, where t is the deviation from the critical line and ν ≈ 0.67 is the critical exponent of the O(2) universality class; this prediction depends on the validity of the duality conjecture linking the abelian Higgs model to a scalar field theory with global U(1) symmetry.

## Method
The author relates the magnetic permeability of the abelian Higgs model to the susceptibility of the dual scalar theory with respect to a U(1) chemical potential, using the duality mapping where magnetic field corresponds to charge density. The susceptibility is then identified with the square of the decay constant f^2 of the Goldstone boson in the dual superfluid phase. The critical behavior of f^2 is determined via scaling arguments and the Josephson relation, yielding f^2 ~ t^ν, which directly gives the scaling law for χ_m.

## Key numbers
- ν ≈ 0.67: the critical exponent of the O(2) universality class (XY model), which governs the scaling of the magnetic permeability χ_m ~ t^ν near the critical line.
- The magnetic permeability χ_m is proportional to the square of the decay constant f^2, with the relation χ_m = (4π²/e²) χ and χ = f², where e is the gauge coupling.
- Exactly on the critical line, the magnetic induction B depends quadratically on the external magnetic field H: B ~ e⁻³ H², with a proportionality coefficient depending only on the gauge coupling e.

## Context
This work extends the duality picture of the three-dimensional abelian Higgs model, previously argued by Dasgupta and Halperin (1981), Kleinert (1982), and Kovner and Rosenstein (1990), by proposing new lattice tests in the Coulomb phase and at the phase transition. Prior numerical tests of duality in the Higgs phase, measuring vortex tension scaling, have been inconclusive (Kajantie et al., 1998; lattice simulations). The predictions for χ_m and the nonlinear B-H relation provide alternative ways to test the duality conjecture, complementing specific heat measurements which are difficult due to the small critical exponent α in the O(2) universality class.
