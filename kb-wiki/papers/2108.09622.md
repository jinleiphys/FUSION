# Chiral vortical catalysis

> Gluon interaction introduces remarkable corrections to the magnetic polarization effects on the chiral fermions, which is known as the inverse magnetic catalysis. It is a natural speculation that the vorticity, which has many similar properties as magnetic field, would bring non-negligible contribution to the chiral rotational suppression. Using the intuitive semi-classical background field method we studied the rotation dependence of the effective strong interaction coupling. Contrary to the magnetic field case the rotation increases the effective coupling which leads to slowing down the condensate melting procedure with temperature. This could be named as the chiral vortical catalysis or inverse rotation suppression. Imposing such dependence to the coupling in the NJL model, we numerically checked this analysis qualitatively. The pseudo critical temperature is shown to rise with the rotation and approach saturation eventually which may be induced by the model cutoff.

## Key claim

Contrary to the magnetic field case where the effective strong interaction coupling decreases (inverse magnetic catalysis), rotation increases the effective coupling, which slows down the chiral condensate melting with temperature; this effect is named chiral vortical catalysis or inverse rotation suppression, but the analysis is qualitative and the saturation of the pseudo-critical temperature at high rotation may be induced by the model cutoff.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical background field method (Nielsen 1980) to compute the vacuum energy of a pure gluon system in a background color magnetic field under uniform rotation, extracting the rotation-dependent effective coupling by comparing the vacuum energy to the standard magnetization form. They then introduced this rotation dependence into the coupling of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and solved the gap equation for the chiral condensate at finite temperature using the mean-field approximation and local potential approximation, with a finite transverse size boundary condition.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling increases with rotation as \(\alpha_{eff} = \alpha[1 + \frac{\alpha}{\pi}(ln(R\Lambda) + \frac{1.08}{6\langle k^2\rangle R^2}\frac{\omega}{\Lambda})]\), where \(\omega\) is angular velocity, \(R\) is system radius, and \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet cutoff.
* In the NJL model, the coupling was parameterized as \(G(\omega) = G(1 + 0.32\frac{\omega}{\Lambda_{NJL}})\) with \(\Lambda_{NJL} = 0.65\) GeV, \(G = 4.93\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and current quark mass \(m = 0.005\) GeV.
* The system radius was set to \(R = 2\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and the condensate was computed at radial position \(r = 0.1\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The pseudo-critical temperature (peak of chiral susceptibility) was computed for rotation values \(\omega = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) GeV, showing a rise with rotation and eventual saturation.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) observed in lattice QCD simulations (Bali 2012) to the rotational case, motivated by the similarity between magnetic and vortical effects on chiral fermions. The approach follows the semi-classical method of Nielsen (1980) previously applied to finite temperature and density by Schneider (2002, 2003), and the IMC was reproduced in the NJL model by Farias (2014) using a magnetic-field-dependent coupling. The results suggest that rotation has the opposite effect of magnetic field on the chiral phase transition, and the authors propose that this could be tested in lattice QCD (since rotating QCD has no sign problem) and may affect vector meson polarization measurements in heavy-ion collisions.
