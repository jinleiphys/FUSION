# Elliptic flow due to radiation in heavy-ion collisions

> In this paper we demonstrate that radiation patterns could cause flow-like behaviour without any reference to hydrodynamic description. For that purpose we use a statistical ensemble of radiating dipoles, motivated by the investigation of the equivalent photon yield produced by decelerating charges. For the elliptic asymmetry factor we find a reasonable agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that radiation patterns from a statistical ensemble of radiating dipoles can produce elliptic flow-like behavior without any reference to hydrodynamic description, and the authors find "reasonable agreement" with experimental data for the elliptic asymmetry factor \(v_2\), but only after introducing an additional momentum-independent form factor \(F\) that scales the dipole prediction and saturates below 3, indicating that the dipole term alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors model the yield of massless particles (photons or gluons) produced by decelerating point-like charges, using the classical electrodynamic formula for radiation from an accelerating charge. They then construct a dipole from two parallel, counter-decelerating charges separated by distance \(d\), compute the coherent sum of their amplitudes, and derive an expression for the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) that depends on the dipole size \(d\), the amplitude ratio \(\gamma\), and the phase-shift \(\delta\) between the two sources. After averaging over a uniformly fluctuating phase-shift, they fit the resulting formula \(\langle v_2 \rangle_{\mathrm{fit}} = F \cdot \langle v_2 \rangle(k_\perp d, \gamma)\) to experimental \(v_2\) data for inclusive photons, charged pions, and charged hadrons from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Effective dipole size \(d \approx 0.06\) fm from hadronic fits (charged hadrons and pions) and \(d \approx 0.1\) fm from photon data (ALICE photon fits show \(d = 0.09\)–\(0.10\) fm across centralities).
- Form factor \(F\) ranges from \(0.66\) (0–5% centrality) to \(2.80\) (45–50% centrality) for PHENIX charged hadrons; for ALICE photons, \(F\) ranges from \(0.66\) (0–5%) to \(2.56\) (30–40%).
- Amplitude ratio \(\gamma\) is fixed to \(1.00\) in all fits, indicating symmetric dipole sources.
- From the geometric interpretation of \(F\) as \(F = b/(2R)\), the effective source radius is \(R \approx 1.67\) fm, about four times smaller than a typical Pb nucleus.
- For ALICE photon fits, \(\chi^2\) values range from \(0.36\) (0–5% and 5–10% centrality) to \(1.60\) (30–40% centrality); for PHENIX charged hadrons, \(\chi^2\) ranges from \(4.31\) to \(8.19\).

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to explain flow patterns using color scintillating antennas radiating gluons (Color Antennas, 2013; Color Antennas 2, 2014) and phenomenological models of color-electric dipoles for angular correlations (Dipole, 2006; Dipole 2, 2007; Dipole 3, 2008). The authors contrast their approach with standard hydrodynamic simulations (Bozek, 2010; Gale and Schenke, 2013; Shen, 2011; Romatschke, 2009; Wiedemann, 2010) and note that the small effective dipole size (\(0.06\)–\(0.1\) fm) hints at subhadronic (quark-level) sources, consistent with the analysis of Lacey (2014). The paper enables further investigation into whether microscopic radiation mechanisms or collective hydrodynamic behavior dominate the observed elliptic flow, and suggests that multipole contributions beyond the dipole term are needed since \(F > 1\).
