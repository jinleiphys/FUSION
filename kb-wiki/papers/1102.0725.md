# Time-dependent AdS/CFT correspondence and the Quark-Gluon plasma

> Experiments on high-energy heavy-ion collisions reveal the formation and some intriguing properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), a new phase of matter predicted by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), the quantum field theory of strong interactions. The phenomenological success of relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with remarkably weak shear viscosity, modeling QGP as an almost perfect fluid, are in favor of the occurrence of a strongly-coupled QGP expanding and cooling during the reaction. A derivation of these features in QCD at strong coupling is still lacking and represents a very intricate theoretical challenge. As a quite unique modern tool to relate these dynamical features to a microscopic gauge field theory at strong coupling, time-dependent realizations of the AdS/CFT correspondence provide a fruitful way to study these properties in a realistic kinematic configuration. Relating a 4-dimensional Yang-Mills gauge theory with four supersymmetries (which is a conformal field theory, CFT) with gravity in Anti-de Sitter space in five dimensions (AdS), the AdS/CFT correspondence provides a useful "laboratory" to study yet unknown strong coupling properties of QCD. Besides the interest of revealing new aspects of the AdS/CFT correspondence in a dynamical set-up, the application to plasma formation leads to non trivial theoretical properties, as we will discuss in the lectures. The highlights of the present lectures are: 1. Emergence of an (almost) perfect hydrodynamic fluid at late proper-times after the collision. 2. Duality between an expanding 4-dimensional plasma and a black hole moving radially in the bulk. 3. Intimate link between conformal hydrodynamics and Einstein's equations in the asymptotically AdS space. 4. Possibility of studying the far-from-equilibrium stage of a gauge field theory at early collisional proper-times.

## Key claim
The AdS/CFT correspondence can be used to model the time-dependent, strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, with the late-time behavior of a boost-invariant expanding plasma emerging as a generic consequence of the dual gravity description, provided the bulk geometry is free of naked singularities.

## Method
The authors apply holographic renormalization to map a 4-dimensional conformal field theory (N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills) at strong coupling onto 5-dimensional Einstein equations in asymptotically anti-de Sitter space. For a boost-invariant Bjorken flow, they solve the dual gravity equations with a power-law ansatz for the energy density as a function of proper time, imposing regularity of the bulk geometry (no naked singularities) to select physically allowed plasma evolutions.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from AdS/CFT is \( \eta/s = \frac{1}{4\pi} \frac{\hbar}{k_B} \), proposed as an absolute theoretical lower bound.
- For a static uniform plasma, the Hawking temperature is \( T = \frac{1}{\pi \tilde{z}_0} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\pi z_0} \), and the entropy is \( S = \frac{N_c^2}{2\pi \tilde{z}_0^3} = \frac{\pi^2}{2} N_c^2 T^3 \), which is 3/4 of the entropy at zero coupling.
- For a perfect fluid conformal plasma under Bjorken flow, the pressure scales as \( p(\tau) \propto \tau^{-4/3} \), with energy density \( \epsilon = 3p \).
- The positive energy condition for a power-law energy density \( \epsilon(\tau) \propto \tau^{-s} \) restricts the exponent to \( 0 \le s \le 4 \).

## Context
This work extends the static AdS/CFT analysis of the quark-gluon plasma (e.g., the evaluation of \( \eta/s \) by Son (2002)) to a time-dependent, boost-invariant setting inspired by Bjorken (1983). It enables the study of far-from-equilibrium stages of a strongly coupled gauge theory at early proper times, and establishes a duality between an expanding 4-dimensional plasma and a black hole moving radially in the bulk, linking conformal hydrodynamics to Einstein's equations in asymptotically AdS space.
