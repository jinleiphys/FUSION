# Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction: A Typical/Concise Review

> Nearly a recent century of work is divided to Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) interaction issue. We review some overall perspectives of NN interaction with a brief discussion about deuteron, general structure and symmetries of NN Lagrangian as well as equations of motion and solutions. Meanwhile, the main NN interaction models, as frameworks to build NN potentials, are reviewed concisely. We try to include and study almost all well-known potentials in a similar way, discuss more on various commonly used plain forms for two-nucleon interaction with an emphasis on the phenomenological and meson-exchange potentials as well as the constituent-quark potentials and new ones based on chiral effective field theory and working in coordinate-space mostly. The potentials are constructed in a way that fit NN scattering data, phase shifts, and are also compared in this way usually. An extra goal of this study is to start comparing various potentials forms in a unified manner. So, we also comment on the advantages and disadvantages of the models and potentials partly with reference to some relevant works and probable future studies.

## Key claim

This review paper provides a concise overview of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction models and potentials, aiming to compare various potential forms in a unified manner, but it explicitly states that it cannot address all aspects of the subject and focuses on basic facts and the author's preferred topics, with the goal of starting comparisons for future studies rather than presenting a definitive or exhaustive analysis.

## Method

The paper reviews the main NN interaction models qualitatively: phenomenological models with many free parameters fitted to experimental data, boson-exchange models based on field-theoretical and dispersion-relations methods, QCD-inspired models using quark and gluon degrees of freedom, and models based on chiral effective field theory (EFT) using the chiral symmetry of QCD. It discusses the general structure and symmetries of the NN Lagrangian, equations of motion in coordinate-space, partial-wave analysis, and the deuteron bound state, while also covering scattering-length and effective-range formalisms and momentum-space approaches.

## Key numbers

* The lightest meson (pion) mass is about 140 MeV, setting the threshold for inelastic reactions in two-nucleon systems.
* The short-range repulsion of NN interaction rapidly reduces S-wave functions at distances less than approximately 0.5 fm.
* For low-energy np scattering below about 10 MeV, the effective-range expansion \(k\cot\delta = -1/a + (1/2) r_e k^2 - P r_e^3 k^4\) is valid, with the \(k^4\) term becoming important for energies above about 10 MeV.
* The scalar-isoscalar sigma meson \(\sigma(500-700)\) is used as a parameterization of the \(2\pi\) system in S-state, with masses in the range of 400-800 MeV in one-boson-exchange potentials.
* The vector mesons \(\rho(769)\) and \(\omega(783)\) have masses in the ranges of 770-780 MeV.
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) is approximately 1 GeV, below which pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, etc.) are the proper degrees of freedom.

## Context

This review extends the long history of NN interaction studies dating back to Yukawa (1935) and Bethe (1953), covering the development from early meson-exchange theories through to modern chiral EFT approaches. It discusses well-known potential families including the Hamada-Johnston potential, Reid potentials (Reid68, Reid93), Argonne-group potentials (ArgonneV14, ArgonneV18), Paris-group potentials, Bonn-group potentials, and Nijmegen-group potentials, among others. The paper enables future comparative studies of different potential forms and serves as an entry point for beginners, while noting that the field has moved toward more sophisticated two- and few-nucleon potentials in recent years, with open questions remaining about the treatment of short-range physics and the connection to QCD.
