# A new derivation of symmetry energy from nuclei beyond the β-stability line

> We suggest the procedure of direct derivation of the symmetry energy from the shift of neutron-proton chemical potentials Δλ=λ_{n}- λ_{p} for nuclei beyond the beta-stability line. We observe the presence of anomalous strong (about 15%) shell oscillations at the symmetry energy coefficient b_{sym}. Our results do not confirm the existence of exceptionally large values of the symmetry energy coefficient at mass number A\approx 100 which was earlier reported in Ref.[2]. Using the fitting procedure, we have evaluated the volume, b_{sym,vol}, and surface, b_{sym,surf}, contributions to the symmetry energy. We have estimated the experimental value of surface-to-volume ratio as r_{S/V}=|b_{sym,surf}}|/b_{sym,vol}\approx 1.7 for the fitting interval A\geq 50.

## Key claim
The authors derive the symmetry energy coefficient directly from the shift of neutron-proton chemical potentials for nuclei beyond the beta-stability line, rather than from the standard mass formula fit. They find that the extracted symmetry energy coefficient exhibits strong shell oscillations of about 15%, and they do not confirm the exceptionally large values near mass number A ≈ 100 reported in earlier work (Jadowski, 2003).

## Method
The authors use experimental binding energy tables to compute the difference Δλ = λ_n − λ_p for nuclei with fixed mass number A but varying asymmetry parameter X = (N−Z)/(N+Z). They extract the quantity b_sym*(A) + e_C*(A) from the slope of Δλ versus X via linear least-squares fits, then obtain the symmetry energy coefficient b_sym*(A) and Coulomb energy parameter e_C*(A) using the beta-stability condition. Finally, they fit the A-dependent b_sym*(A) to the leptodermous form b_sym*(A) = b_sym,vol* + b_sym,surf* A^(−1/3) to extract volume and surface contributions.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy coefficient b_sym*(A) shows shell oscillations with amplitude of about 15%.
- For the fitting interval A ≥ 12: b_sym,vol* = 26.5 MeV, b_sym,surf* = −25.6 MeV, surface-to-volume ratio r_S/V ≈ 1.0.
- For the fitting interval A ≥ 50: b_sym,vol* = 32.5 MeV, b_sym,surf* = −56.3 MeV, surface-to-volume ratio r_S/V ≈ 1.73.
- The Coulomb energy coefficient is fitted as e_C*(A) = 0.17 A^(2/3).
- The empirical beta-stability line is given by X*(A) = 0.4 A / (A + 200).

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear symmetry energy beyond the beta-stability line using a chemical-potential-based method, in contrast to the standard mass formula fitting used by Jadowski (2003). The authors compare their results with extended Thomas-Fermi calculations using SkM and SLy230b Skyrme forces (Kosak, 2008) and with the Weizsacker mass formula (Bohr and Mottelson). The approach enables direct extraction of volume and surface symmetry energy contributions from experimental data, though the results depend significantly on the mass range used in the fit, and the surface-to-volume ratio of about 1.7 for A ≥ 50 is close to theoretical results from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (Sawyer, 2006).
