# Transverse-momentum-dependent factorization at next-to-leading power

> We study transverse momentum dependent factorization and resummation at sub-leading power in Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. In these processes the sub-leading power contributions to the cross section enter as a kinematic power correction to the leptonic tensor, and the kinematic, intrinsic, and dynamic sub-leading contributions to the hadronic tensor. By consistently treating the power counting of the interactions, we demonstrate renormalization group consistency. We calculate the anomalous dimensions of the kinematic and intrinsic sub-leading correlation functions at one loop and find that the evolution equations give rise to anomalous dimension matrices which mix leading and sub-leading power distribution functions. Additionally we calculate the hard and soft functions associated with each of these contributions. We find that these hard and soft contributions differ from those at the leading power. Finally, we calculate the rapidity anomalous dimension for the dynamic sub-leading distributions and find that it is the same as the leading power anomalous dimension. We then comment on the implications for the soft function associated with this contribution. Using this information, we establish the factorization formalism at sub-leading power for these processes at the one-loop level.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a systematic TMD factorization formalism at next-to-leading power (NLP) for Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, demonstrating renormalization group consistency at one-loop order, but with the hedge that the rapidity anomalous dimensions for the intrinsic and kinematic sub-leading TMDs are half those of the leading-power ones, differing from some prior studies (Vladimirov 2021, Rodini 2022) while agreeing with others (Bacchetta 2008, Chen 2016).

## Method

The authors start from the tree-level framework of Mulders (1995) and Bacchetta (2006) for the Drell-Yan and SIDIS cross sections, classifying sub-leading power contributions into kinematic, intrinsic, and dynamic types. They then extend the formalism to one-loop order in QCD by calculating the hard functions, soft functions, and anomalous dimensions for each sub-leading correlation function, using the Gottfried-Jackson frame to simplify power counting and treating the power counting of interactions consistently to verify renormalization group consistency.

## Key numbers

- The rapidity anomalous dimension for the intrinsic and kinematic sub-leading TMDs is found to be half that of the leading-power TMDs, consistent with Bacchetta (2008) and Chen (2016) but differing from Vladimirov (2021) and Rodini (2022).
- The hard and soft functions for the intrinsic and kinematic sub-leading distributions are calculated at one loop and found to differ from those at leading power.
- The rapidity anomalous dimension for the dynamic sub-leading distributions is found to be the same as the leading-power anomalous dimension.
- Power corrections are of order \(M/Q\) and \(q_T/Q\), where \(M\) is the hadron mass, \(Q\) is the hard scale, and \(q_T\) is the transverse momentum of the vector boson.

## Context

This work extends the tree-level TMD factorization at sub-leading power developed by Mulders (1995) and Bacchetta (2006) to one-loop order, addressing discrepancies in the literature regarding rapidity divergences. It resolves a tension between studies such as Bacchetta (2008) and Chen (2016), which found half the rapidity anomalous dimension for intrinsic NLP TMDs, and those of Vladimirov (2021) and Rodini (2022), which obtained different results. The formalism enables future precision studies of azimuthal correlations and spin asymmetries at the Electron-Ion Collider, where sub-leading power contributions are not small in fixed-target kinematics.
