# Addendum to the Weak Parity-Violating Pion-Nucleon Coupling

> We have made a needed correction to our earlier paper (nucl-th/9511002), which contains a sign error. With this correction, the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling is about 3 x 10^(-7).

## Key claim

The weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(f_{\pi NN}\) is approximately \(3 \times 10^{-7}\), but only after correcting a sign error in the authors' earlier work (Henley, Hwang, and Kisslinger, 1995) that had made the lowest-dimensional and leading nonperturbative contributions subtract instead of add.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules to compute both the strong and weak pion-nucleon couplings, relating them through a common pion vacuum susceptibility \(\chi_\pi\). They determine \(\chi_\pi a\) from the known strong coupling \(g_{\pi NN}\) and then use that value to predict the weak coupling \(f_{\pi NN}\). The sum rule for the weak case is modified by multiplying both sides by \((p^2 - M^2)\) before taking the Borel transform to improve its behavior, and two previously omitted diagrams (Figs. 3d and 3e) are evaluated and found to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- \(f_{\pi NN} = (3.04 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{-7}\) for Borel mass \(M_B = (1.10 \pm 0.05)\ \text{GeV}\), with additional systematic uncertainties of at least \(\pm 0.5 \times 10^{-7}\) from condensates and neglected terms.
- The product \(\chi_\pi a\) is about \(-1.9\ \text{GeV}^2\), determined from the strong coupling sum rule.
- The strong coupling \(g_{\pi NN} = -(14.8 \pm 0.7)\) for \(M_B = (1.10 \pm 0.05)\ \text{GeV}\), with an additional error of at least \(\pm 2.0\) from condensate uncertainties.

## Context

This addendum corrects a sign error in the authors' earlier QCD sum-rule calculation (Henley, Hwang, and Kisslinger, 1995) that had invalidated their previous result. The corrected prediction for \(f_{\pi NN}\) is now in much better agreement with the value from Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and with an independent calculation by one of the authors (Hwang, 1995). The work demonstrates that nonperturbative QCD contributions, particularly the \(\chi_\pi a\) term, account for about 50% of the weak coupling, and it provides a stable sum-rule framework for future calculations of parity-violating nucleon couplings.
