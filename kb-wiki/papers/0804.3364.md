# A Unitary Fermi Supersolid: The Larkin-Ovchinnikov Phase

> We present strong theoretical evidence that a Larkin-Ovchinnikov (LOFF/FFLO) pairing phase is favoured over the homogeneous superfluid and normal phases in three-dimensional unitary Fermi systems. Using a Density Functional Theory (DFT) based on the latest quantum Monte-Carlo calculations and experimental results, we show that this phase is competitive over a large region of the phase diagram. The oscillations in the number densities and pairing field have a substantial amplitude, and a period some 3 to 10 times the average interparticle separation. Within the DFT, the transition to a normal polarized Fermi liquid at large polarizations is smooth, while the transition to a fully-paired superfluid is abrupt.

## Key claim
The authors present strong theoretical evidence that a Larkin-Ovchinnikov (LO) inhomogeneous pairing phase is energetically favored over both the homogeneous superfluid and normal phases in three-dimensional unitary Fermi systems, but only when using a Density Functional Theory (DFT) that properly accounts for both pairing and normal correlation energies. Previous mean-field and Bogoliubov–de-Gennes calculations, which neglect these correlation contributions, incorrectly found that LO states are not competitive at unitarity.

## Method
The authors constructed an asymmetric superfluid local density approximation (ASLDA) functional for the unitary Fermi gas, with parameters constrained by matching quantum Monte-Carlo calculations for the symmetric superfluid phase (pairing gap, energy, and quasiparticle spectrum) and for the polarized normal phase. They solved the resulting self-consistent Bogoliubov–de-Gennes-like equations in three dimensions for LO states with spatial modulations in a single direction, using a discrete variable representation basis and a Broyden iteration scheme, then varied the period L to find the state that minimizes the grand potential.

## Key numbers
- The symmetric superfluid energy ratio ξ = E_SF/E_FG = 0.40(1), pairing gap Δ/ε_F = 0.504(24), and inverse effective mass α(1) ≈ 1.09(2) were used to constrain the ASLDA functional.
- The symmetric normal phase energy ratio ξ_N = E_N/E_FG = 0.57(2) was determined from the fit.
- The minority species effective polaron mass m/m* ≈ 1.04(3) constrains the inverse mass at full polarization.
- The LO state oscillations have a period 3 to 10 times the average interparticle separation.
- The LO phase exists over a range of chemical potential ratio y = μ_b/μ_a between y_LO-N (where the transition to normal is second order) and y_LO-SF (where the transition to the fully-paired superfluid is weakly first order).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of polarized Fermi gases that considered phase separation between normal and superfluid phases (Lobo et al. 2006; Chevy 2006; Pilati and Giorgini 2008) and earlier mean-field treatments of LOFF/FFLO states that were not fully self-consistent or neglected normal correlation energies (Combescot 2008; Casalbuoni 2003; Yoshida and Yip 2007). The ASLDA approach, building on the SLDA functional validated for symmetric systems (Bulgac 2007), provides a quantitative tool for evaluating inhomogeneous phases. The results suggest that cold-atom experiments with adjustable traps could observe this first example of a Fermi supersolid at unitarity, with density oscillations in the minority component providing a direct signature.
