# Delta(1232) in piN -> pipiN reaction

> The contribution of Delta(1232) to the piN -> pipiN reaction is examined by making use of the chiral reduction formula developed by Yamagishi and Zahed. The influence of the pi-Delta-Delta and rho-N-Delta interactions on this reaction, which has not been regarded as important so far, is considered for all channels with the initial pi^{+ -}p states. The total cross sections are calculated to tree level for the energy up to T_{pi}=400 MeV. Although the pi-Delta-Delta and rho-N-Delta interactions give a small effect on the pi^{+}p -> pi^{+}pi^{+}n, pi^{-}p -> pi^{+}pi^{-}n and pi^{-}p -> pi^{0}pi^{0}p channels, the pi^{+ -}p -> pi^{+ -}pi^{0}p channels are found to be sensitive to these interactions.

## Key claim

The paper examines the contribution of the Δ(1232) resonance to the πN → ππN reaction using the chiral reduction formula, finding that the πΔΔ and ρNΔ interactions, previously not considered important, have a significant effect on the π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p and π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p channels, while their effect on other channels is small.

## Method

The authors applied the chiral reduction formula developed by Yamagishi and Zahed to decompose the invariant amplitude for the πN → ππN reaction into current correlation terms. They calculated total cross sections to tree level for all channels with initial π⁺p and π⁻p states, including the πΔΔ and ρNΔ interactions through the axial-vector and vector current matrix elements, using the Rarita-Schwinger field for Δ(1232) and vector meson dominance for the ρ meson. The isospin symmetry breaking was incorporated by shifting the pion kinetic energy from the isospin symmetric threshold to the physical threshold for each channel.

## Key numbers

* The total cross sections were calculated for incoming pion kinetic energy up to Tπ = 400 MeV.
* For the π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n channel, the cross section increased by about 10–30% around Tπ = 300 MeV due to the πΔΔ interaction with R_NΔ = 0.4 or 0.8.
* For the π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p channel below 300 MeV, the cross section increased by about 50% with R_NΔ = 0.4 and became twice the result with R_NΔ = 0.8.
* For the π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p channel around Tπ = 300 MeV, the cross section increased by about 25% with R_NΔ = 0.4 and 65% with R_NΔ = 0.8.
* For the π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p channel around Tπ = 200 MeV, the cross section with f_ρNΔ = 3.5 and 7.8 decreased about 30% compared to the cross section with f_ρNΔ = 0.
* The isospin symmetry breaking shifts δTπ were: +3.97 MeV for π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n, −3.66 MeV for π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p, +3.97 MeV for π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n, −7.92 MeV for π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n, and −3.66 MeV for π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical analyses of the πN → ππN reaction, such as the phenomenological model of Oset and Vicente-Vacas (1985) and the chiral perturbation theory studies of Bijnens et al. (1995, 1997), which had not included the πΔΔ and ρNΔ interactions or had shown their contributions to be negligible in certain channels. The chiral reduction formula approach, developed by Yamagishi and Zahed (1996), provides a framework constrained by chiral symmetry that allows separate examination of each reaction mechanism. The results show that the π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p and π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p channels are sensitive to these interactions, suggesting that future determination of the πΔΔ and ρNΔ coupling constants requires systematic inclusion of the Roper resonance N*(1440), which was not included in this study.
