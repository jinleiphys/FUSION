# The Oxygen core inside the Magnesium isotopes

> We have studied the ground state bulk properties of magnesium isotopes using axially symmetric relativistic mean field formalism. The BCS pairing approach is employed to take care of the pairing correlation for the open shell nuclei. The contour plot of the nucleons distribution are analyzed at various parts of the nucleus, where clusters are located. The presence of an $^{16}$O core along bubble like $α$-particle(s) and few {\it nucleons} are found in the Mg isotopes.

# The Oxygen core inside the Magnesium isotopes

## Key claim

The paper claims that axially symmetric relativistic mean field calculations reveal the presence of an $^{16}$O core and bubble-like $\alpha$-particle(s) inside the ground states of $^{24-30}$Mg isotopes, but the identification of cluster boundaries is guided by visual inspection of contour plots and carries inherent uncertainty from the graphical method used to define integration ranges.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* and NL075 parameter sets, with BCS pairing in a constant gap approximation for open-shell nuclei. The nucleon density distributions were computed in the $\rho z$ plane and contour plots were analyzed to identify regions of enhanced density. The number of protons and neutrons in each cluster region was determined by integrating the density over visually estimated spatial ranges, and the resulting proton and neutron counts were used to identify the cluster composition.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for $^{20-34}$Mg isotopes calculated with NL3* range from 135.1 MeV ($^{20}$Mg) to 256.5 MeV ($^{34}$Mg); experimental values range from 134.5 MeV to 256.5 MeV.
- Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for $^{24}$Mg is 0.45 (NL3*) and 0.52 (NL075), compared to the experimental value of 0.60.
- Charge radius of $^{24}$Mg is 3.125 fm (NL3*) and 3.210 fm (NL075), compared to the experimental value of 3.057 fm.
- The central density of $^4$He is approximately 0.20 fm$^{-3}$ and of $^{16}$O is approximately 0.16 fm$^{-3}$; the maximum density in the identified cluster regions ranges from approximately 0.14 to 0.17 fm$^{-3}$.
- For $^{24}$Mg, the identified $^4$He clusters contain 1.98 protons and 2.01 neutrons each (NL3*), with 8 neutrons and 8 protons as diffused nucleons.
- For $^{28}$Mg, the identified $^{16}$O cluster contains 7.89 protons and 8.08 neutrons (NL3*), with a $4n$ cluster containing 0.06 protons and 3.98 neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the study of cluster structures in light nuclei, following experimental confirmation of $3-\alpha$ structure in $^{12}$C and $4-\alpha$ structure in $^{16}$O by earlier experiments. The calculations use the RMF formalism, which has been successful in describing sub-atomic nuclei and nuclear decays, and apply it to the Mg isotopic chain motivated by recent theoretical predictions of clusters in lighter mass nuclei and studies of low-energy spectra of Mg isotopes by Yao (2011), Bend (2003), Ring (2006), and Eunja (2013). The finding of $^{16}$O and $^4$He bubbles inside Mg isotopes suggests these could be preformed clusters with high decay probability, potentially observable in laboratory experiments.
