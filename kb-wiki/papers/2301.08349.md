# Auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments of light nuclei with chiral EFT interactions

> We calculate the magnetic moments of light nuclei ($A < 20$) using the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method and local two- and three-nucleon forces with electromagnetic currents from chiral effective field theory. For all nuclei under consideration, we also calculate the ground-state energies and charge radii. We generally find a good agreement with experimental values for all of these observables. For the electromagnetic currents, we explore the impact of employing two different power countings, and study theoretical uncertainties stemming from the truncation of the chiral expansion order-by-order for select nuclei within these two approaches. We find that it is crucial to employ consistent power countings for interactions and currents to achieve a systematic order-by-order convergence.

## Key claim
The authors find that consistent power counting schemes for nuclear interactions and electromagnetic currents are crucial for achieving systematic order-by-order convergence in magnetic moment calculations; the N$^3$LO contributions are unexpectedly large compared to lower orders, and the choice of fitting scheme for the N$^3$LO electromagnetic low-energy constants produces variance of the same order as the theoretical uncertainty at NLO.

## Method
The authors use the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to compute ground-state energies, charge radii, and magnetic moments for light nuclei with A < 20. They employ local chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon interactions at N$^2$LO with cutoff R$_0$ = 1.0 fm, and electromagnetic currents derived under two different power counting schemes (Pisa and Bochum). The two unknown electromagnetic low-energy constants at N$^3$LO are fit using two prescriptions: scheme A fits to $^3$H and $^3$He magnetic moments, while scheme B performs a linear least squares fit to the entire data set.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic moment average absolute difference from experiment across all nuclei: 0.18 at LO, 0.13 at NLO, 0.13 at N$^2$LO, and 0.08 (scheme A) or 0.09 (scheme B) at N$^3$LO
- For scheme A LEC fit: $d_{1}^{S} = -0.09596 \pm 0.0018$, $d_{1}^{V} = -0.11085 \pm 0.00099$, $\chi^2$/DOF = 0.48078
- For scheme B LEC fit: $d_{1}^{S} = -0.03958 \pm 0.0035$, $d_{1}^{V} = -0.06420 \pm 0.0028$, $\chi^2$/DOF = 1.5035
- For $^{15}$N in the Bochum PC, calculations predict a magnetic moment 40% above the experimental value
- Theoretical uncertainty estimate uses $Q = m_\pi / \Lambda$ with $\Lambda = 500$ MeV

## Context
This work extends previous AFDMC calculations of ground-state properties of light nuclei (Lynn et al., 2016; Lonardoni et al., 2018) to include magnetic moments with chiral EFT electromagnetic currents. It builds on the local chiral interactions developed by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and Tews et al. (2016), and compares two power counting schemes for electromagnetic currents: the Pisa scheme (Piarulli et al., 2013; Schiavilla et al., 2019) and the Bochum scheme (Krebs, 2020). The results enable systematic uncertainty quantification for magnetic moment calculations and highlight the need for consistent power counting between interactions and currents, as well as future derivation of consistent currents for the interactions used in AFDMC.
