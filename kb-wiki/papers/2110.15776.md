# Bayesian reconstruction of nuclear matter parameters from the equation of state of neutron star matter

> The nuclear matter parameters (NMPs), those underlie in the construction of the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter, are not directly accessible. The Bayesian approach is applied to reconstruct the posterior distributions of NMPs from the EoS of neutron star matter. The constraints on lower-order parameters as imposed by the finite nuclei observables are incorporated through appropriately chosen prior distributions. The calculations are performed with two sets of pseudo data on the EoS whose true models are known. The median values of second or higher order NMPs show sizeable deviations from their true values and associated uncertainties are also larger. The sources of these uncertainties are intrinsic in nature, identified as (i) the correlations among various NMPs and (ii) the variations in the EoS of symmetric nuclear matter, symmetry energy, and the neutron-proton asymmetry in such a way that the neutron star matter EoS remain almost unaffected.

## Key claim

The Bayesian reconstruction of nuclear matter parameters (NMPs) from the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter yields median values for second- or higher-order NMPs that show sizeable deviations from their true values, with larger associated uncertainties, even when the true underlying model is known; these deviations are intrinsic and arise from correlations among NMPs and from the leeway in the EoS of symmetric nuclear matter, symmetry energy, and neutron-proton asymmetry that leaves the neutron star matter EoS almost unaffected.

## Method

The authors constructed pseudo data for the EoS of neutron star matter using two different expansion schemes (Taylor expansion and n/3 expansion) around saturation density, with known true values of the NMPs listed in Table 1. They then applied a Bayesian inference framework with a Gaussian likelihood function and two different prior sets (P1 and P2) to reconstruct the marginalized posterior distributions of the NMPs from these pseudo data. The priors incorporated constraints from finite nuclei observables on lower-order parameters, while higher-order parameters were given wide uniform or Gaussian distributions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- True values of lower-order NMPs used for pseudo data: ε₀ = -16.0 MeV, K₀ = 230 MeV, J₀ = 32.0 MeV, L₀ = 50.0 MeV
- True values of higher-order NMPs: Q₀ = -400 MeV, Z₀ = 1500 MeV, K_sym,0 = -100 MeV, Q_sym,0 = 550 MeV, Z_sym,0 = -750 MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter and symmetry energy separately, median values of all NMPs were very close to their true values; uncertainties for third-order NMPs in the n/3 expansion (M2) were about five times larger than for Taylor expansion (M1), and for fourth-order NMPs more than ten times larger
- For the neutron star matter EoS, the likelihood was evaluated with σ = 1 at all densities from 0.5ρ₀ to 6ρ₀

## Context

This work extends Bayesian parameter estimation techniques previously applied to gravitational wave signals (Ashton 2019) and to nuclear matter properties from chiral effective field theory (Wesolowski 2016) to the specific problem of reconstructing NMPs from the neutron star matter EoS. It uses pseudo data with known true values to isolate intrinsic uncertainties, complementing studies that constrain symmetry energy (Somasundaram 2021) or neutron star masses and radii (Drischler 2021) from observational data. The findings enable future work to assess how well static neutron star properties can pinpoint NMPs and to understand the limitations of using only the neutron star matter EoS for parameter extraction.
