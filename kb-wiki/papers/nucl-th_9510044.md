# Temperature dependence of the response function of hot nuclear matter

> The description of collective motion in nuclei at finite temperature using the framework of the random phase approximation is discussed. We focus on the special case of the isovector response function of hot nuclear matter using various effective Skyrme interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the temperature dependence of the isovector response function in hot nuclear matter, calculated within the random phase approximation (RPA) using Skyrme interactions, shows that zero-sound collective modes become less collective as temperature rises and can disappear at a few MeV, but this effect is strongly dependent on the effective mass of the interaction; for interactions with an effective mass close to unity, collective effects are small even at zero temperature and depend only weakly on temperature.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the isovector response function of hot nuclear matter within the framework of time-dependent mean-field theory, using both a simplified Skyrme interaction and a full Skyrme force. They compute the strength function \( S(\omega) = -\frac{1}{\pi} \Im m \Pi(\omega,q) \) for a momentum transfer \( q = 0.23 \, \text{fm}^{-1} \) (corresponding to the giant dipole resonance in lead-208 in the Steinwedel-Jenssen model) at various temperatures, comparing results for the SIII and SV Skyrme interactions.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer used: \( q = 0.23 \, \text{fm}^{-1} \), chosen to correspond to the giant dipole resonance in lead-208 in the Steinwedel-Jenssen model.
- For the SIII interaction, collective effects (a sharp zero-sound peak) disappear when temperature reaches a few MeV.
- For the SV interaction, only a weak temperature dependence of the response function is observed.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Braghin (1995) on the simplified Skyrme interaction case, where a sharp zero-sound peak at zero temperature was found to nearly disappear at a few MeV. The present paper generalizes this to full Skyrme forces and attributes the difference between interactions SIII and SV to their different effective mass values. The results suggest that the temperature dependence of the giant dipole resonance provides information on the effective particle-hole interaction, and the authors note that experimental studies by Lefaou (1995) showing saturation of photon multiplicities at high excitation energy may be a signature of this loss of collectivity.
