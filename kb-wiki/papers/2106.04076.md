# Evolution of octupole deformation and collectivity in neutron-rich lanthanides

> The onset of octupole deformation and its impact on related spectroscopic properties is studied in even-even neutron-rich lanthanide isotopes Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd with neutron number $86\leqslant N\leqslant 94$. Microscopic input comes from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation with constrains on the axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole operators using the Gogny-D1M interaction. At the mean-field level, reflection asymmetric ground states are predicted for isotopes with neutron number around $N=88$. Spectroscopic properties are studied by diagonalizing the interacting boson model Hamiltonian, with the parameters obtained via the mapping of the mean-field potential energy surface onto the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the $s$, $d$, and $f$ boson condensate state. The results obtained for low-energy positive- and negative-parity excitation spectra as well as the electric dipole, quadrupole, and octupole transition probabilities indicate the onset of pronounced octupolarity for $Z\approx 56$ and $N\approx 88$ nuclei.

## Key claim
The onset of pronounced octupole deformation and collectivity in neutron-rich lanthanides is predicted for nuclei near Z ≈ 56 and N ≈ 88, but the claim is qualified by the fact that at the mean-field level, reflection-asymmetric ground states are found only for isotopes with neutron number around N = 88, while for heavier isotopes the ground state remains reflection symmetric though the potential remains soft along the octupole direction.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M interaction to generate potential energy surfaces (PES) as functions of the axially symmetric quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformation parameters for even-even Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd isotopes with neutron numbers 86 ≤ N ≤ 94. These SCMF-PES were then mapped onto the expectation value of an sdf-interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian in a boson condensate state, with the IBM parameters determined by reproducing the SCMF-PES near the global minimum. The mapped IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- For Ba isotopes with neutron numbers between 86 and 92, an octupole-deformed ground state is found at the HFB level, with β₃ ≈ 0.1 at the minimum for nuclei close to N = 88.
- The 3⁻ excitation energy reaches its minimum value around N = 88 for most isotopic chains, with the lowest values observed in Ba and Ce isotopes.
- The expectation value of the f-boson number in the 0⁺₁ and 2⁺₁ ground-state band wave functions reaches ⟨n̂_f⟩ ≈ 1 at N ≈ 88, indicating significant octupole components.
- The B(E3;3⁻₁ → 0⁺₁) transition probability is largest at N ≈ 88 for each isotopic chain, with predicted values for ¹⁴⁴Ba and ¹⁴⁶Ba falling within experimental error bars.
- The boson effective charges used are e₁ = 0.02 e·b¹/², e₂ = 0.14 e·b, and e₃ = 0.12 × (1 + β̄₂β̄₃) e·b³/².

## Context
This work extends the mapped IBM framework previously applied to actinide nuclei (Nomura et al., 2020, 2021) to the neutron-rich lanthanide region, motivated by recent Coulomb excitation experiments at the CARIBUF facility that revealed large B(E3) values in ¹⁴⁴Ba (Bucher et al., 2016) and ¹⁴⁶Ba (Bucher et al., 2017). The results are consistent with empirical expectations that octupole correlations are enhanced near the octupole magic numbers Z = 56 and N = 88, and provide theoretical predictions for spectroscopic properties that can guide future experimental studies. The calculations show a more moderate evolution of octupole collectivity compared to the stronger parabolic dependence observed in light actinides (Ra and Th isotopes) around N = 134.
