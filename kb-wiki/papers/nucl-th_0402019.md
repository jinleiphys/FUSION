# Bounds on the angles for the parameterization of three neutrino mixing

> An algebraic approach is used to derive symmetry relations for the parameterization of the three neutrino mixing matrix. The symmetry relations imply bounds on the mixing angles. Including a CP violating phase $δ$, the mixing angles $θ_{jk}$ lie in the range [0,$π$/2], and $δ$ lies within [0,$2π$). If one restricts the CP phase $δ\in [0,π)$, then one must extend the range of either $θ_{12}$ {\it or} $θ_{23}$ to $[0, π)$. In particular, for no CP violation, one can set $δ=0$ {\it and} $δ=π$ with $θ_{jk}$ in the range [0,$π$/2]. Alternatively, one can set $δ=0$ only, and allow either $θ_{12}$ {\it or} $θ_{23}$ to lie within [0,$π$).

# Bounds on the angles for the parameterization of three neutrino mixing

## Key claim
The symmetry relations of the three-neutrino mixing matrix imply that if one restricts the CP-violating phase δ to the interval [0,π), then either θ₁₂ or θ₂₃ must be allowed to range over [0,π) rather than the usual [0,π/2] to cover all physically distinct oscillation scenarios. For no CP violation, one can either set δ=0 and δ=π with all θⱼₖ in [0,π/2], or set δ=0 only and allow either θ₁₂ or θ₂₃ to lie in [0,π).

## Method
The authors use an algebraic approach based on the trace invariance of the oscillation probability under unitary transformations of the mixing matrix. They express the Maiani parameterization of the three-neutrino mixing matrix in terms of SO(3) rotation generators and a CP-phase-dependent transformation, then derive equivalence relations among parameter sets that produce identical oscillation probabilities for all flavor transitions and all L/E values.

## Key numbers
- Mixing angles θⱼₖ lie in the range [0,π/2] and δ lies within [0,2π) when the full CP phase range is allowed.
- If δ is restricted to [0,π), then either θ₁₂ or θ₂₃ must be extended to [0,π).
- For no CP violation, one can set δ=0 and δ=π with all θⱼₖ in [0,π/2], or set δ=0 only with either θ₁₂ or θ₂₃ in [0,π).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of bounds on neutrino mixing angles previously considered by Gluza (2002) for propagation through matter, confirming their results for vacuum propagation while providing an additional alternative conclusion via symmetry relations. The algebraic approach used here was developed by the same authors (Latimer and Ernst, 2003) for vacuum neutrino oscillations. The results enable phenomenologists to correctly explore the full parameter space when analyzing neutrino oscillation data, particularly when restricting the CP phase to the commonly used interval [0,π).
