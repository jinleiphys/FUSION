# Relativistic mean-field study of alpha decay in superheavy isotopes with 100 \texorpdfstring{$\leq$ Z $\leq$}-120

> The $α$-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with $100 \leq Z \leq 120$ are comprehensively analyzed using the axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism for the NL3$^*$ parameter set. We employ RMF binding energies to determine the $α$-decay energies and make a comparison with both the available experimental data and the theoretical results obtained from the global nuclear mass model WS4. The four distinct formulae, specifically the modified scaling law Brown, modified Viola-Seaborg, Yibin {\it et al.} formula, and its modified form are used to calculate the decay half-lives and examine the numerical correlation between the half-life ($T_{1/2}$) for each $α$-decay energy. We notice that $T_{1/2}$ is significantly dependent on the decay formula in terms of isospin asymmetry and decay energy. We also noticed that modified scaling law Brown formula estimates of half-lives agreed comparatively better with the experiment as compared to others. Moreover, the present investigation provides significant information on the stability of the superheavy island considered for ongoing and/or future experiments.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the NL3* parameter set provides a reliable description of alpha-decay energies and half-lives for superheavy nuclei with Z = 100 to 120, but the calculated half-lives depend significantly on which semi-empirical decay formula is used, with the modified scaling law Brown (MSLB) formula showing the best agreement with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* parameter set and a constant-gap BCS pairing method to compute binding energies for even-even superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers Z = 100 to 120 and neutron numbers N = 156 to 184. Alpha-decay energies (Q-values) were obtained from the binding energy difference between parent and daughter nuclei, and these Q-values were then inserted into four semi-empirical formulas (modified Viola-Seaborg, modified scaling law Brown, Yibin et al., and modified Yibin et al.) to calculate alpha-decay half-lives. The results were compared with experimental data and with predictions from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and the global nuclear mass model WS4.

## Key numbers

- Mean deviation in decay energy for RMF (NL3*) relative to experimental data: 0.189
- Mean deviation in decay energy for WS4 relative to experimental data: 0.164
- Mean deviation in decay energy for FRDM relative to experimental data: 0.387
- For the nucleus 304120 with a Q-value of 12.10 MeV, the four formulas gave log10(T1/2) values of -3.66 s (MVS), -2.87 s (MSLB), -3.93 s (YQZR), and -3.45 s (MYQZR)
- Standard deviation of MSLB results relative to experimental data: 4.15
- The quadrupole deformation parameter beta2 for Z = 100-110 isotopes ranges from 0.1 to 0.3
- The binding energy of the alpha particle used in Q-value calculations: 28.296 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of superheavy nuclei by Bisw et al. (2021) and Jain et al. (2022) to a systematic survey of alpha-decay properties across Z = 100 to 120 using the NL3* parameter set. The study compares its predictions with the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM model of Moller et al. (2019) and the WS4 mass model of Wang et al. (2014), finding that the RMF approach yields lower Q-values for massive isotopes than these other models. The identification of isotopes with neutron numbers N = 162, 164, and 174-184 as having extended half-lives due to shell/sub-shell closures provides guidance for future experimental synthesis efforts at facilities such as JINR-FLNR, RIKEN, and GSI.
