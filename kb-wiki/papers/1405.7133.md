# Testing in-medium $πN$ dynamics on pionic atoms

> A general algorithm for handling the energy dependence of meson-nucleon amplitudes in the nuclear medium has been recently applied to antikaons and to eta mesons. Here we test this approach on $πN$ amplitudes in pionic atoms where direct comparison can be made with ample experimental results. Applying this algorithm to a large-scale fit of 100 pionic-atom data points across the periodic table, which also include the `deeply-bound' states in Sn and Pb, reaffirms earlier conclusions on the density-dependent renormalization of the $πN$ threshold isovector amplitude $b_1$, or equivalently the renormalization of the pion decay constant $f_π$ in the nuclear medium.

# Testing in-medium πN dynamics on pionic atoms

## Key claim

The subthreshold energy algorithm previously applied to antikaons and eta mesons, when tested on pionic atoms across the periodic table, reaffirms earlier conclusions about the density-dependent renormalization of the πN threshold isovector amplitude b₁ (or equivalently the renormalization of the pion decay constant fπ in the nuclear medium), but the test is limited by the smooth energy dependence of the πN interaction which makes the results less sensitive to fine details than in the kaonic or eta cases.

## Method

The authors performed global least-squares fits of the Ericson-Ericson optical potential to 100 pionic-atom data points across the periodic table from Ne to U, including deeply-bound states in Sn isotopes and ²⁰⁵Pb. They implemented a self-consistent subthreshold energy algorithm that evaluates πN amplitudes at energies determined locally by the nuclear density, using the Mandelstam variable s with contributions from pion binding energy, nucleon binding energy, Fermi motion, the optical potential itself, and optionally the Coulomb potential via minimal substitution E → E − Vc. The fits scanned over the neutron rms radius parameter γ while varying the isovector s-wave amplitude b₁ and the two-nucleon absorption parameter B₀.

## Key numbers

- 100 pionic-atom data points were fitted, from Ne to U, including deeply-bound states in three Sn isotopes and ²⁰⁵Pb.
- The free-space πN threshold isovector amplitude b₁ = −0.0885⁺⁰·⁰⁰¹⁰₋₀·⁰⁰²¹ mπ⁻¹ (Schröder, 2001) and b₁ = −0.0861 ± 0.0009 mπ⁻¹ (Baru, 2011).
- The empirical slope of b₀ with energy is −0.00053 mπ⁻¹/MeV from the SAID program.
- Subthreshold energies of approximately −20 MeV are encountered at half nuclear matter density (ρ₀/2) in pionic atoms.
- The best-fit value of γ in the neutron skin relation rn − rp = γ(N−Z)/A + δ is γ = 1.0 ± 0.15 fm, giving rn − rp = 0.18 ± 0.03 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The χ² per degree of freedom is approximately 1.7 for the best fits.
- The imaginary part of B₀ is 0.052 ± 0.002 mπ⁻⁴, insensitive to model variants; the real part of B₀ ranges from 0.012 ± 0.033 to 0.112 ± 0.031 mπ⁻⁴ depending on whether minimal substitution and Weise's renormalization are applied.
- The density-dependent renormalization of b₁ follows b₁(ρ) = b₁/(1 − 2.3ρ) with ρ in fm⁻³, derived from the chiral condensate formula fπ²(ρ)/fπ² = 1 − σρ/(mπ²fπ²) with σ ≈ 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the subthreshold energy algorithm previously applied to kaonic atoms (CFG, 2011; FGa, 2012; GMa, 2012) and eta mesons (FGM, 2013; CFG, 2014) to the pionic atom system where direct experimental comparison is possible. It reaffirms the in-medium enhancement of b₁ first proposed by Weise (2000, 2001) as a signature of partial chiral symmetry restoration, consistent with earlier analyses by Friedman (2002), Geissmann et al. (2002), and Kolomeitsev, Kaiser and Weise (2003). The results enable further constraints on the density dependence of the pion decay constant and the chiral condensate in nuclear matter, though the authors note that the deeply-bound states alone are insufficient to substantiate the renormalization without the full large-scale fits.
