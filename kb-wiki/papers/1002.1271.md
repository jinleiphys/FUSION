# f_0(600), kappa(800), rho(770) and K*(892), quark mass dependence from unitarized SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study the strange and non-strange quark mass dependence of the parameters of the f_0(600), kappa(800), rho(770) and K*(892) resonances generated from elastic meson-meson scattering using unitarized one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory. We fit simultaneously all experimental scattering data up to 0.8-1 GeV together with lattice results on decay constants and scattering lengths up to a pion mass of 440 MeV. Then, the strange and non-strange quark masses are varied from the chiral limit up to values of interest for lattice studies. In these amplitudes, the mass and width of the rho(770) and K*(892) present a similar and smooth quark mass dependence. In contrast, both scalars present a similar non-analyticity at high quark masses. Nevertheless the f_0(600) dependence on both quark masses is stronger than for the kappa(800) and the vectors. We also confirm the lattice assumption of quark mass independence of the vector two-meson coupling that, in contrast, is violated for scalars.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the mass and width of the vector mesons rho(770) and K*(892) have a smooth and similar quark mass dependence, while the scalar mesons f0(600) and kappa(800) show a non-analytic behavior at high quark masses, with the f0(600) dependence on both strange and non-strange quark masses being stronger than for the kappa(800) and the vectors. The authors confirm the lattice assumption of quark mass independence for the vector two-meson coupling, but find this assumption is violated for scalars.

## Method
The authors used the unitarized one-loop Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) within SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory to describe elastic meson-meson scattering. They performed a simultaneous fit to experimental scattering data up to 0.8-1 GeV and lattice results on decay constants and scattering lengths up to a pion mass of 440 MeV, then varied the strange and non-strange quark masses from the chiral limit to values relevant for lattice studies.

## Key numbers
- The non-strange quark mass was varied up to m_hat/m_hat_phys <= 9, corresponding to M_pi < 440 MeV, to ensure pions remained lighter than kaons and etas for the elastic approximation.
- The strange quark mass was varied in the limited range 0.8 < m_s/m_s_phys < 1.4 to maintain ChPT convergence and avoid requiring a coupled channel formalism.
- The mass and width of the kappa(800) changed by as much as 16% within the range of strange quark mass study.
- The rho(770) and f0(600) results were consistent with previous SU(2) ChPT calculations by Hanhart (2008).

## Context
This work extends previous SU(2) ChPT studies by Hanhart (2008) on the f0(600) and rho(770) to include the strange quark within a unitarized SU(3) formalism, enabling analysis of the K*(892) and kappa(800) resonances. The results provide predictions for lattice QCD studies on the quark mass dependence of light meson resonances, particularly confirming the lattice assumption of constant vector coupling to two mesons while showing this assumption fails for scalar mesons.
