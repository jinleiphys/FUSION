# Probing the symmetry energy from the nuclear isoscaling

> Using different parameterizations of the nuclear mass formula, we study the sensitivity of the isoscaling parameters to the mass formula employed in grand-canonical calculations. Previous works on isoscaling have suggested that the symmetry energy implied in such calculations is anomalously smaller than that suggested by fits to nuclear masses. We show that surface corrections to the symmetry energy naturally broadens the isotopic distribution thus allowing for values of the symmetry energy which more closely match those obtained from nuclear masses.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the anomalously small symmetry energy values previously extracted from isoscaling analyses may not be necessary if surface corrections to the symmetry energy are included in the mass formula used in statistical multifragmentation models, allowing symmetry energy values that more closely match those obtained from nuclear masses.

## Method

The authors performed grand-canonical statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) calculations for the breakup of 112Sn and 124Sn nuclei at a density of ρ/ρ0 = 1/3, using three different liquid-drop mass formula parameterizations (LDM1, LDM2, LDM3) fitted to empirical binding energies from Audi and Wapstra (2003). They computed the isoscaling parameter α as a function of temperature and compared the results across the different mass models, also examining the effect of artificially varying the symmetry energy coefficient Csym.

## Key numbers

- At T = 6 MeV, up to 30% reduction of the isoscaling parameter α is obtained by including surface effects in the symmetry energy (LDM2/LDM3) compared to LDM1
- The best-fit symmetry energy coefficient for LDM1 is a_v k_v = 22.3918 MeV; for LDM2, a_v k_v = 27.7976 MeV and a_s k_s = 33.7053 MeV; for LDM3, α* = 27.1508 MeV and β* = 16.6412 MeV
- Reducing Csym to 10 MeV (similar to values deduced in prior work) increases binding of the most neutron-rich fragments of mass number 100 by up to 100 MeV without changing binding of symmetric fragments
- For Csym below the best-fit value, deviations ΔB of order 2 MeV/nucleon are obtained for light nuclei with A < 20 at Csym = 10 MeV
- The surface symmetry energy parameter α*²/β* ≈ 44.3 MeV differs appreciably from a_s k_s ≈ 33.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior isoscaling studies by Tsang et al. (2001) and Botvina et al. (2002) that suggested the symmetry energy from isoscaling is anomalously smaller than from nuclear mass fits. It directly challenges the approach of Shetty et al. (2007), Botvina et al. (2006), and others who reduced Csym to values around 10 MeV to reproduce experimental α values, arguing instead that surface symmetry energy terms naturally broaden isotopic distributions. The paper enables more consistent extraction of symmetry energy from isoscaling by showing that accurate mass formulae with surface terms can reproduce data without invoking large temperature-dependent reductions in the symmetry energy.
