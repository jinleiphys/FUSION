# Tagged nuclear structure functions with $HERMES$

> The production of slow nucleons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off nuclei, $A(e, e'N)X$, is analyzed for kinematical conditions accessible at $HERA$ with the $HERMES$ detector. The sensitivity of the semi-inclusive cross section to possible medium-dependent modifications of the nucleon structure function is illustrated.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive cross section for slow nucleon production in $A(e, e'N)X$ processes, measured under kinematical conditions accessible at HERA with the HERMES detector, shows appreciable sensitivity to possible medium-dependent modifications of the nucleon structure function, provided the Bjorken variable $x$ is in the range $0.2 \div 0.8$ and the kinetic energy of the detected nucleon is between $50$ and $250$ MeV.

## Method

The semi-inclusive cross section for $^{12}C(e, e'p)X$ was calculated within the impulse approximation using a convolution formula that relates the nuclear structure function to the free nucleon structure function and the two-nucleon spectral function. The two-nucleon correlation (2NC) model was adopted for the spectral function, which accounts for the center-of-mass motion of correlated nucleon pairs. The calculations included contributions from the spectator mechanism, target fragmentation, hadronization processes involving six-quark cluster configurations, and quasi-elastic contamination, with electron kinematics at $E_e = 30$ GeV and $Q^2$ in the range $5 \div 15$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Key numbers

- Backward nucleon emission is mainly governed by the spectator mechanism for detected nucleon kinetic energy $T_2$ in the range $50 \lesssim T_2 \lesssim 250$ MeV, corresponding to momentum $0.3 \lesssim p_2 \lesssim 0.7$ GeV/c.
- The ratio $R_1$ of semi-inclusive cross sections at different $x$ values, evaluated at $Q^2 = 10$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $p_2 = 0.4$ GeV/c, and backward proton kinematics ($\theta_2 = 140^\circ$), shows sensitivity to medium-dependent modifications of the nucleon structure function.
- The ratio $R_2$ of semi-inclusive cross sections at different $Q^2$ values, evaluated at $x = 0.6$ and backward nucleon kinematics ($\theta_2 = 140^\circ$), shows deviations from free nucleon predictions that depend on $p_2$ values of $0.3$, $0.4$, $0.5$, and $0.6$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the spectator mechanism analysis previously applied to the deuteron in Simula (1996) to heavier nuclei like $^{12}C$, showing that backward nucleon emission can be used to tag bound nucleon structure functions. The calculations use the two-nucleon correlation model from Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1995) and compare three models of medium-dependent modifications: the point-like configuration suppression model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981), the $Q^2$-rescaling model of Close et al. (1985) driven by nucleon swelling, and the off-shellness rescaling model of Dieperink and Tjon (1986). The results enable experimental tests of the EMC effect mechanism using semi-inclusive measurements at HERMES.
