# Correlations between $θ_{13}$ and $θ_{23}$ in a very long baseline neutrino oscillation experiment

> A very long baseline experiment, with $L/E$ of $1.6 \times 10^4$ m/MeV, has been proposed as the ideal means to measure $θ_{13}$, including its sign, for neutrino energies less than 50 MeV. Higher energies require consideration of the earth MSW effect. Approximating a constant density mantle, we examine the $ν_e$--$ν_μ$ and $ν_μ$--$ν_μ$ oscillation channels at very long baselines with particular focus upon the deviation of $θ_{23}$ from maximal mixing and the sign of $θ_{13}$. It is demonstrated that measurements of these oscillation channels in this region are sensitive to $θ_{13}$, including its sign, and to $θ_{23}$, including its octant, with a significant correlation between the value of $θ_{13}$ and the difference of $θ_{23}$ from maximal mixing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a very long baseline neutrino oscillation experiment with \(L/E = 1.6 \times 10^4\) m/MeV, measurements of the \(\nu_e\)–\(\nu_\mu\) and \(\nu_\mu\)–\(\nu_\mu\) channels are sensitive to both the sign of \(\theta_{13}\) and the octant of \(\theta_{23}\), but with a significant correlation between the value of \(\theta_{13}\) and the deviation of \(\theta_{23}\) from maximal mixing. The authors note that this correlation is only valid under the assumption of CP conservation and that a full consideration of all parameters could modify the result.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expansions of the \(\nu_e\)–\(\nu_\mu\) and \(\nu_\mu\)–\(\nu_\mu\) oscillation probabilities to first order in \(\theta_{13}\) and \(\varepsilon\) (where \(\varepsilon = \theta_{23} - \pi/4\)), first in vacuum and then for neutrinos traversing a constant-density mantle (3.5 g/cm³) via the MSW effect. They then constructed a \(\chi^2\) function combining existing constraints from CHOOZ and atmospheric data with mock experimental data for a mono-energetic 270 MeV neutrino source at a 4000 km baseline, and plotted the allowed regions for \(\theta_{13}\) and \(\theta_{23}\).

## Key numbers

* The ideal baseline-energy ratio for maximal sensitivity to linear terms in \(\theta_{13}\) is \(L/E = 1.6 \times 10^4\) m/MeV, corresponding to the first \(\Delta_{21}\) oscillation trough.
* The CHOOZ limit on the mixing angle is \(|\theta_{13}| < 0.22\) at 90% C.L. for \(\Delta_{31} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\) eV².
* The allowed range for \(\theta_{23}\) from an atmospheric analysis is \(0.67 < \theta_{23} < 0.91\) at 90% C.L.
* For a constant mantle density of 3.5 g/cm³, the MSW resonance energy is \(E_R \sim 120\) MeV.
* At the maximum baseline of 4000 km, the ideal neutrino energy is 270 MeV, where the slope relating \(\theta_{13}\) and \(\varepsilon\) is \(\cot(2\theta_{12}^m) = 0.54\).
* The best fit values from mock data are \(\theta_{13} = 0.04\) and \(\theta_{23} = 0.775\).

## Context

This work extends the vacuum analysis of Latimer and Ernst (2005) to include the MSW effect, allowing the use of higher-energy neutrinos (up to 270 MeV) while maintaining sensitivity to linear terms in \(\theta_{13}\). The correlation between \(\theta_{13}\) and \(\theta_{23}\) is consistent with earlier three-neutrino analyses by Gonzalez-Garcia and collaborators (2004, 2005), which found best-fit parameter sets (A: \(\theta_{13}=0.0\), \(\theta_{23}=0.745\); B: \(\theta_{13}=0.095\), \(\theta_{23}=0.785\)) that satisfy the linear relation derived here. The results suggest that future very long baseline experiments could simultaneously determine the sign of \(\theta_{13}\) and the octant of \(\theta_{23}\), though a full treatment of core-crossing neutrinos and parametric resonances is left for future work.
