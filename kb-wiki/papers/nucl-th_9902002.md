# Chiral Transition in Nuclear Medium and Relevant Observables

> We discuss how the possible changes of hadron properties can be tested, associated with the chiral transition in nuclear medium; the hadrons discussed are the vector mesons,the $σ$ meson and the chiral partner of the nucleon. We emphasize that the proper quantity is the spectral function which describes the nuclear medium having a hadron; the analysis of electro-quasielastic reactions, for instance, has a relevance to the physics of CERES. The similarity is indicated between the physics discussed here with that of giant resonances. Experiments using nuclear targets are proposed to observe the $σ$ meson and to explore how the partial chiral restoration in nuclear medium manifests itself.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the spectral function, rather than simple in-medium masses, is the proper quantity for testing how hadron properties change due to partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, and that electro-quasielastic reactions on nuclear targets can reveal these changes through observables accessible to experiments such as CERES.

## Method

The author discusses the in-medium modifications of vector mesons (rho meson), the sigma meson, and the chiral partner of the nucleon by analyzing their spectral functions in nuclear matter. The analysis draws an analogy between the collective modes in the rho-meson channel and the physics of giant resonances, showing how particle-hole (p-h), Delta-hole (Delta-h), and higher resonance-hole excitations (N(1520)-h, Delta(1620)-h, N(1720)-h, Delta(1905)-h) contribute to the dispersion relation omega(q) in the rho channel. The paper proposes using nuclear targets in experiments to observe the sigma meson and to explore manifestations of partial chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

* The dispersion relations omega(q) in the rho channel are shown for momentum transfers up to 3p_F, where p_F is the Fermi momentum, with excitation energies ranging from 0 to 1200 MeV (arbitrary units on the vertical axis).
* The figure displays contributions from particle-hole (p-h) excitations at approximately 200 MeV, Delta-h excitations near 400 MeV, and higher resonances including N(1520)-h, Delta(1620)-h, N(1720)-h, and Delta(1905)-h at energies up to about 1200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear medium by emphasizing spectral functions over simple mass shifts, connecting to the experimental program of the CERES collaboration at CERN. The author draws a parallel between the in-medium collective modes in the vector meson channel and the established physics of giant resonances in nuclear physics. The proposed experiments using nuclear targets to observe the sigma meson would enable direct tests of partial chiral restoration, building on theoretical frameworks for in-medium hadron properties developed in the 1990s.
