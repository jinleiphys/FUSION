# Chiral Symmetry and N*(1440) -> N pi pi Decay

## Key claim

The scalar-isoscalar contact interaction for the pion-baryon vertex, which arises from the chiral reduction formula and is absent in the model of Hernández et al. (2002), is necessary for simultaneously reproducing the coupling constant g_{RN pi pi} and the pi-pi and pi-N invariant mass distributions of the N*(1440) -> N pi pi decay; the term proportional to sigma_RN (connected with explicit chiral symmetry breaking) is essential, while the term proportional to F_AA alone cannot achieve this simultaneous description.

## Method

The authors applied the chiral reduction formula of Yamagishi and Zahed (1996) to derive the general Ward-identity structure of the N*(1440) -> N pi pi decay amplitude, decomposing it into scalar (T_S), vector (T_V), and axial-axial (T_AA) terms. They introduced scalar-isoscalar contact interactions (sigma_RN and F_AA) into the existing model of Hernández et al. (2002), which originally included only open diagrams (intermediate Delta, N, N* baryons) and closed diagrams (pi-pi rescattering via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation). The free parameters (sigma_RN, F_AA, and a cutoff Lambda) were fitted to reproduce the pi-pi and pi-N invariant mass distributions, the N*(1440) -> N pi pi decay width Gamma = 152 MeV from Manley et al. (1992), and the experimental coupling constant g^{expt}_{RN pi pi} = 1.6 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- The experimental coupling constant g^{expt}_{RN pi pi} = 1.6 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} is used as a target; the model of Hernández et al. (2002) gave |g^{model}_{RN pi pi} / g^{expt}_{RN pi pi}| = 0.42.
- In the full calculation including the contact interaction, the fitted parameters are sigma_RN = 157 MeV, F_AA = 1.97 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}, and Lambda = 700 MeV.
- When only the sigma_RN term is kept (F_AA = 0), the fitted parameters are sigma_RN = 160 MeV and Lambda = 460 MeV, with f_{R Delta pi} = 1.1.
- When only the F_AA term is kept (sigma_RN = 0), no acceptable parameter set could be found to reproduce both the mass distributions and g^{expt}_{RN pi pi}.
- Without the closed diagrams, the fitted parameters are sigma_RN = 177 MeV and F_AA = -1.22 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}.
- The Roper resonance mass is taken as m_R = 1462 MeV and its total decay width as Gamma_R = 391 MeV, from Manley et al. (1992).
- The pion decay constant is f_pi = 92.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the model of Hernández et al. (2002) for N*(1440) -> N pi pi decay by incorporating a scalar-isoscalar contact interaction required by the chiral reduction formula of Yamagishi and Zahed (1996), which was absent in the earlier study. The analysis addresses a known problem: the model of Hernández et al. (2002) could reproduce the invariant mass distributions but failed to simultaneously describe the coupling constant g_{RN pi pi} extracted from the pi-pi-N threshold amplitude. The results show that the contact interaction, particularly the term from explicit chiral symmetry breaking (sigma_RN), can effectively substitute for the closed (pi-pi rescattering) diagrams, suggesting that the closed diagrams with their ambiguities (unknown cutoffs and coupling constants) are not essential for a phenomenological understanding of this decay. This enables future studies of the N*(1440) -> N pi pi decay without relying on the ambiguous closed-diagram mechanism.
