# Nuclear symmetry energy and the role of the tensor force

> Using the Hellmann--Feynman theorem we analyze the contribution of the different terms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction to the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}$ and the slope parameter $L$. The analysis is performed within the microscopic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach using the Argonne V18 potential plus the Urbana IX three-body force. We find that the main contribution to $E_{sym}$ and $L$ is due to the tensor component of the nuclear force.

# Nuclear symmetry energy and the role of the tensor force

## Key claim

Using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with the Argonne V18 potential plus the Urbana IX three-body force, the authors find that the tensor component of the nuclear force gives the largest contribution to both the nuclear symmetry energy E_sym and the slope parameter L, while the kinetic contribution to E_sym is very small and negative.

## Method

The authors applied the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to separate the contributions of each term of the Argonne V18 potential and the reduced Urbana IX three-body force to the total energy of neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. The calculation was performed within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach using the continuous prescription for the in-medium potential, at the saturation density of rho_0 = 0.187 fm^-3. The symmetry energy E_sym and slope parameter L = 3 rho_0 (d E_sym/d rho) at rho_0 were then decomposed into kinetic and potential contributions, and further into partial waves and spin-isospin channels.

## Key numbers

- Total symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 34.300 MeV, with kinetic contribution -0.973 MeV and potential contribution 35.273 MeV.
- Total slope parameter at saturation: L = 66.500 MeV, with kinetic contribution 14.896 MeV and potential contribution 51.604 MeV.
- Tensor component contribution to E_sym: 36.056 MeV (sum of V_Sij, V_Sij(tau_i·tau_j) from Av18, and U_Sij(tau_i·tau_j) from reduced Urbana force).
- Tensor component contribution to L: 69.968 MeV (sum of same tensor terms).
- The spin-triplet (S=1), isospin-singlet (T=0) channel contributes 49.836 MeV to E_sym and 90.561 MeV to L, dominated by the ^3S_1 partial wave (45.810 MeV to E_sym, 71.855 MeV to L).
- Kinetic contribution to E_sym is negative (-0.973 MeV), contrasting with the free Fermi gas value of approximately 14.4 MeV at rho_0.

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of the symmetry energy using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, addressing the long-standing uncertainty in the density dependence of E_sym above saturation. The results confirm and quantify the dominant role of the tensor force, as previously suggested by Panda (1972), Wiringa (1988), Brown (1990), Bombaci (1991), Dieperink (2003), Xu and Li (2010, 2011), and Sammarruca (2011). The finding that the kinetic contribution to E_sym is negative agrees with the recent results of Xu and Li (2011) and is attributed to the strong isospin-dependence of short-range correlations induced by the tensor force. This work enables better constraints on the symmetry energy from neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision data by identifying which components of the nuclear force drive its density dependence.
