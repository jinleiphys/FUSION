# Mechanical form factors and densities of non-relativistic fermions

> The hadron physics community has been actively debating the interpretation of so-called mechanical properties of hadrons. Non-relativistic quantum-mechanical systems like the hydrogen atom have been appealed to in these debates as analogies. Since such appeals are likely to continue, it is important to have Galilei-covariant expressions for matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor. In this work, I obtain Galilei-covariant breakdowns of such matrix elements into mechanical form factors, with a special focus on spin-half states. I additionally study the spatial densities associated with these form factors, using the pilot wave interpretation to guide their breakdown into contributions from internal structure and from quantum-mechanical effects such as wave packet dispersion. For completeness, I also obtain non-relativistic Breit frame densities.

## Key claim

The paper provides Galilei-covariant expressions for matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor in non-relativistic quantum mechanics, including a new form factor \(\bar{e}_q(\bd^2)\) absent in relativistic treatments, and uses the pilot wave interpretation to separate internal structure contributions from barycentric wave-packet effects in spatial densities.

## Method

The author constructs five-vectors and a Galilei metric tensor in \(4+1\)-dimensional spacetime, then derives the most general tensor decomposition of EMT matrix elements compatible with Galilei covariance, parity, and time reversal for spin-zero and spin-half states. For spin-half systems, eight-component Galilei-covariant spinors are derived using representation theory of the \((4+1)\)-dimensional Lorentz group, and spatial densities are obtained via Fourier transforms of the form factors, with the pilot wave interpretation guiding the separation of barycentric and internal contributions.

## Key numbers

- The Galilei metric tensor is defined with \(g^{ab} = -\delta^{ab}\) for spatial indices and \(g^{+-}=g^{-+}=1\), with \(g^{--}=0\).
- The invariant scalar product of the five-momentum gives \(g_{\mu\nu} p^\mu p^\nu = 2mE - \bm{p}^2 = 2mE_0\).
- Sum rules at zero momentum transfer: \(\sum_q A_q(0) = 1\) and \(\sum_q (\bar{c}_q(0) + \bar{e}_q(0)) = 0\); additionally \(\sum_q \bar{c}_q(\bd^2) = 0\) for all \(\bd^2\).
- The internal energy density integral gives \(\int d^3 \bm{b} \, \mathfrak{e}_q(\bm{b}) = E_0 A_q(0) + m(\bar{c}_q(0) + \bar{e}_q(0))\), where \(E_0 \approx m\) is the rest energy.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate in hadron physics about mechanical properties of hadrons (Polyakov 2002, Ji 1995, Lorce 2017) by providing a non-relativistic framework using the hydrogen atom as a test system, following recent studies by Ji (2022), Czarnecki (2023), Eides (2024), and Freese (2024). It enables future calculations of EMT densities in non-relativistic composite systems with spin, and provides a Galilei-covariant alternative to the relativistic Breit frame formalism for interpreting mechanical form factors.
