# The box diagram in Yukawa theory

> We present a light-front calculation of the box diagram in Yukawa theory. The covariant box diagram is finite for the case of spin-1/2 constituents exchanging spin-0 particles. In light-front dynamics, however, individual time-ordered diagrams are divergent. We analyze the corresponding light-front singularities and show the equivalence between the light-front and covariant results by taming the singularities.

## Key claim

The light-front calculation of the box diagram in Yukawa theory is equivalent to the manifestly covariant result, but only after taming the singularities that appear in individual light-front time-ordered diagrams; the authors show that the divergences cancel and that the finite parts of the regulated amplitudes are prescription independent, provided the zero-mode contribution from the stretched box is included when the kinematics allow it.

## Method

The authors computed the covariant box diagram for spin-1/2 constituents exchanging spin-0 particles using Feynman parametrization and Wick rotation, extracting four independent form factors from the spin-matrix elements in the center-of-mass system. They then performed the light-front calculation by integrating over the light-front energy \(k^-\) to obtain light-front time-ordered diagrams, combining propagating and instantaneous contributions via the blink mechanism, and regularizing the divergent integrals using either Pauli-Villars regularization (one PV boson) or dimensional regularization in the transverse-momentum integrals (DR\(_2\)). The equivalence between the covariant and light-front results was verified by summing the contributions from all diagrams and comparing the form factors.

## Key numbers

- The covariant box diagram is finite for spin-1/2 constituents exchanging spin-0 particles; the amplitude is given by a convergent integral after Wick rotation.
- The light-front calculation yields four independent form factors \(F_1, F_2, F_3, F_4\) from the spin-matrix elements, with explicit expressions in terms of integrals \(D_{\alpha_1}, D_{\alpha_2}, D_{\alpha_1\alpha_2}, D_{\alpha_2\alpha_4}, D_2\) (e.g., \(F_1 = 2 m M (2 D_{\alpha_1} + D_{\alpha_2}) + M^2 (2 D_{\alpha_1} + D_{\alpha_2 \alpha_4}) + 2 m^2 (D_{\alpha_1} + D_{\alpha_2})\)).
- The window for real scattering amplitude is \(4 M^2 \leq s \leq 4 m^2\), where \(M < m\).
- In forward kinematics (\(\theta = 0\)), the amplitude matrix reduces to a diagonal form with only one independent amplitude; in backward kinematics (\(\theta = \pi\)), two independent amplitudes occur.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of light-front singularities previously studied by the authors for the triangle diagram and vector anomalies (Bakker, Ji, and collaborators, 2005), and addresses the divergence problem in the light-front ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Yukawa model noted by Sales et al. (2000, 2001) and by Glöckle et al. (1998). The results enable a systematic treatment of higher Fock-state contributions in light-front bound-state equations, showing that the four-body Fock-state (stretched box) must be included to cancel divergences, and that the equivalence between light-front and covariant approaches holds when regularization is properly applied.
