# Linear and cubic response to the initial eccentricity in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the relation between elliptic flow, $v_2$ and the initial eccentricity, $\varepsilon_2$, in heavy-ion collisions, using hydrodynamic simulations. Significant deviations from linear eccentricity scaling are seen in more peripheral collisions. We identify the mechanism responsible for these deviations as a cubic response, which we argue is a generic property of the hydrodynamic response to the initial density profile. The cubic response increases elliptic flow fluctuations, thereby improving agreement of initial condition models with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that deviations from linear eccentricity scaling of elliptic flow \(v_2\) in peripheral heavy-ion collisions are explained by a cubic response term \(\kappa'_2 |\varepsilon_2|^2 \varepsilon_2\), which is a generic property of the hydrodynamic response to the initial density profile and not merely a feature of specific initial condition models.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations (v-USPhydro code) with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, using a constant shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and zero bulk viscosity. They fitted the linear and cubic response coefficients \(\kappa_n\) and \(\kappa'_n\) by minimizing \(\langle |\delta_n|^2 \rangle\) between the hydrodynamic \(v_n\) and the estimator \(f(\varepsilon_n) = \kappa_n \varepsilon_n + \kappa'_n |\varepsilon_n|^2 \varepsilon_n\) across centrality classes, and compared results with smooth Gaussian initial conditions and ideal hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The cubic response coefficient \(\kappa'_2\) increases with centrality, while the linear response coefficient \(\kappa_2\) decreases, such that the sum \(\kappa_2 + \kappa'_2 \simeq 0.32\) remains approximately constant across all centralities.
- Around 50% centrality, about 10% of the elliptic flow comes from the cubic term.
- The ratio \(\langle v_2^4 \rangle / \langle v_2^2 \rangle^2\) is increased by up to 15% in peripheral collisions when the cubic response is included, improving agreement with ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.
- For triangular flow \(v_3\), the cubic response \(\kappa'_3\) is compatible with zero within statistical errors; a small positive mixing term \(\kappa'_{23} \sim 0.03\) from \(|\varepsilon_2|^2 \varepsilon_3\) is observed.

## Context

This work extends the linear eccentricity scaling framework established by Teaney (2010), Gardim (2011), and Niemi (2012) by quantifying deviations seen in peripheral collisions (Schenke 2013, Niemi 2015). The cubic response explains why the distribution of \(v_2\) is not homothetic to the distribution of \(\varepsilon_2\) for centralities above 35%, resolving a discrepancy between IP-Glasma initial conditions and experimental data. The results enable more precise modeling of elliptic flow fluctuations and provide a benchmark for testing initial condition models against LHC data.
