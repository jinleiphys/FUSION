# What "quenches" ${g_A}$ in nuclei ?

> The answer is found in the way the hidden scale symmetry involving a dilaton emerges in strong nuclear correlations in nuclear matter. It is suggested that the same mechanism is at the origin at higher densities of the sound speed converging in the core of massive compact stars to what could be called ``pseudo-conformal sound speed" $v_s^2/c^2\approx 1/3$. A precision measurement of the superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions in the doubly magic nucleus $^{100}$Sn is suggested to confirm or falsify this prediction. It could also lead to the possible determination of genuine ``fundamental renormalization" of $g_A$ in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "quenching" of the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in nuclei from its free-space value of 1.276 to approximately 1 is not a fundamental renormalization of the coupling constant itself, but rather a consequence of hidden scale symmetry emerging through strong nuclear correlations in the Fermi-liquid fixed-point limit, with the matrix element of the Gamow-Teller operator being quenched rather than the coupling constant.

## Method

The author applies a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G\(n\)EFT) that incorporates hidden local symmetry (HLS) and hidden scale symmetry (HSS) via a dilaton field, formulated within a renormalization-group approach to baryons on the Fermi surface. The Fermi-liquid fixed-point (FLFP) approximation is used to calculate the zero-momentum-transfer Gamow-Teller response of quasiparticles on the Fermi surface, yielding the effective axial coupling \(g_A^L\) from in-medium Ward identities. This FLFP result is then mapped to the "extreme single-particle shell model" (ESPSM) description of the doubly magic nucleus \(^{100}\)Sn to connect with experimental measurements.

## Key numbers

* The free-space axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.276\), while the Fermi-liquid fixed-point value is \(g_A^L \approx 1\) within 10% for the density range \(1/2 \lesssim n/n_0 \lesssim 1\).
* The predicted proton anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio is \(\delta g_l^p = 0.22\) for \(n \approx n_0\), which agrees with the experimental value \(\delta g_l^{p,\text{exp}} = 0.23 \pm 0.03\) in the Pb region.
* For the GSI data on \(^{100}\)Sn decay, the extracted Gamow-Teller matrix element range is \(\mathcal{B}^{\text{exp,GSI}}_{\text{GT}} = 9.1 - 10\), yielding \(g_A^{\ast,\text{GSI}} = 0.91 - 0.96\).
* For the RIKEN data on \(^{100}\)Sn decay, the extracted Gamow-Teller matrix element is \(\mathcal{B}^{\text{exp,RIKEN}}_{\text{GT}} = 4.4^{+0.9}_{-0.7}\), yielding \({g_A^\ast}^{\text{exp}} \approx 0.6 - 0.7\) and implying a fundamental quenching factor \(q_{\text{ssb}} \approx 0.6 - 0.7\).
* The ESPSM Gamow-Teller matrix element squared is \(\mathcal{B}^{\text{ESPSM}}_{\text{GT}} = 16/9 \approx 17.78\).

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid theory predictions of Rho (1991) and Friman and Rho (1993), which had been largely ignored for nearly three decades, by reinterpreting the "quenched \(g_A\)" through the lens of hidden scale symmetry and the genuine dilaton scheme of Crewther and Tunstall. The paper directly contradicts the explanation of many-body currents proposed in a first-principles approach and instead attributes the quenching to scale symmetry emerging in nuclear correlations. The resolution of the experimental discrepancy between GSI and RIKEN data on \(^{100}\)Sn decay is identified as critical for determining whether a genuine fundamental renormalization of \(g_A\) occurs in nuclear medium, with implications for neutrinoless double beta decay experiments and the determination of the QCD \(\beta\)-function derivative \(\beta^\prime\).
