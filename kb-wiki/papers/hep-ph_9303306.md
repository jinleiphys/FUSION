# Nuclear Shadowing in a Parton Recombination Model

> Deep inelastic structure functions $F_2^A(x)$ are investigated in a $Q^2$ rescaling model with parton recombination effects. We find that the model can explain experimentally measured $F_2^A(x)$ structure functions reasonably well in the wide Bjorken$-x$ range ($0.005<x<0.8$). In the very small $x$ region ($x<0.02$), recombination results are very sensitive to input sea-quark and gluon distributions.

## Key claim

The Q² rescaling model with parton recombination effects can explain experimentally measured nuclear structure functions F₂^A(x) reasonably well across a wide Bjorken-x range (0.005 < x < 0.8), but in the very small x region (x < 0.02) the recombination results are highly sensitive to the input sea-quark and gluon distributions.

## Method

The author investigates deep inelastic structure functions F₂^A(x) by applying a Q² rescaling model that incorporates parton recombination effects. The model is tested against experimental data across a broad Bjorken-x range to evaluate its ability to reproduce the measured nuclear shadowing behavior.

## Key numbers

- Structure functions F₂^A(x) are investigated over the Bjorken-x range 0.005 < x < 0.8
- The model's results become very sensitive to input sea-quark and gluon distributions in the region x < 0.02

## Context

This work extends the Q² rescaling approach to nuclear structure functions by including parton recombination effects, which are important for describing nuclear shadowing at small x. The paper enables further refinement of parton distribution models in nuclei, particularly highlighting the need for better constraints on sea-quark and gluon distributions at very small x. The sensitivity noted in the x < 0.02 region points to limitations of the current approach and motivates future work with improved input distributions or alternative theoretical treatments.
