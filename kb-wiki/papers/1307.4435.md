# Multi-solitons with vector mesons on the two-sphere

> Recent studies have suggested a strong connection between the static solutions of the 3D Skyrme model and those corresponding to its low-dimensional analog (baby-Skyrme model) on a two-sphere. We have found almost identical solutions considering an alternative two-dimensional model in which a vector meson field is introduced and coupled to the system, instead of the usual Skyrme term. It has been known that including this vector meson field in three dimensions stabilizes the non-linear sigma model without the need of a term quartic on derivatives of the pion fields (Skyrme term). In the present work, we have numerically searched for static multi-solitonic solutions of this alternative stabilization, for the case in which the base-space is a two-sphere. Moreover, we analyze the stability of these solutions under small perturbations in a fully dynamical setting. We have also considered the inclusion of a particular potential term into the Lagrangian, and explored the low/high-density phases of solitons for different ranges of the parameter space, achieving solitons localized enough that allow us to compare with planar (two-dimensional) studies.

## Key claim

The authors numerically demonstrate that static multi-soliton solutions of the two-dimensional non-linear sigma model stabilized by a vector meson field on a unit two-sphere are "almost identical" to those of the baby-Skyrme model on the same geometry, with the caveat that the vector meson model yields slightly lower energies and, in at least one case (B=14), a more symmetric configuration than reported for the baby-Skyrme model by Hen (2013).

## Method

The authors solve the static field equations for a sigma model from S^2 to S^2 coupled to a vector meson field ω, using a parabolic relaxation (heat-type) flow to find static configurations for topological charges B=1 through B=16. They then test the stability of these static solutions by evolving them under the full hyperbolic (dynamical) equations of motion, using a multi-block cubed-sphere grid with penalty terms at patch boundaries and a fourth-order Runge-Kutta time integrator. Initial data for the scalar field are constructed from rational maps R(z)=λ/z^B, and the vector meson field is initialized by neglecting the Laplacian in its static equation.

## Key numbers

- For the B=1 sector with V=0, the static energy agrees with the theoretical value E = 4π + (1/4π)(g²/M²).
- For κ² = (g/(4πM))² = 0.05 (with g=18, M≈6.4), the normalized energy E/(4πB) for the vector meson model ranges from 1.068 (B=2) to 1.383 (B=14), compared to 1.071 to 1.421 for the baby-Skyrme model from Hen (2013).
- The B=2 ring radius increases with the density parameter β = (mM/g)^{-1/2}, with examples shown for β = 1.06 and β = 3.
- For the B=3 low-density regime, a configuration is shown with α = (mg/M) = 1.58 and β = 1.26.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between vector meson stabilization and the Skyrme-term stabilization of the non-linear sigma model, previously studied in three dimensions by Nappi (1985) and Sutcliffe (2010), and in two-dimensional flat space by Sutcliffe (2011). The authors directly compare their results to the baby-Skyrme model on the two-sphere studied by Hen (2013), finding that the vector meson model reproduces the same soliton symmetries (up to charge 13) and similar energies, while offering the advantage of a semi-linear hyperbolic system with propagation speeds equal to the speed of light. The inclusion of a potential term V(φ) = m²(1-φ₃) enables exploration of low-density (β≲2) and high-density (β≳3) phases, allowing comparison with planar studies such as those by Piette (1995) and Weidig (1999), and enabling future studies of the transition between these regimes on a compact manifold.
