# Two-neutron removal reactions of 6He treated as a three-body halo

> We formulate a method to compute breakup processes of three-body halo systems reacting with a target. The reaction between one of the particles and the target as well as the corresponding pairwise final state interactions are carefully treated. Both absorption and diffraction processes are included. Detailed differential and total cross sections are calculated for two-neutron (2n) removal from 6He by fragmentation on 12C. Effects due to the core shadowing are taken into account. Good agreement with available experimental data is obtained.

# Two-neutron removal reactions of 6He treated as a three-body halo

## Key claim
The authors formulate a practical method for computing breakup processes of three-body halo systems where only one projectile particle interacts with the target at a time, and they achieve good agreement with experimental data for 2n removal from 6He on 12C, but the absolute cross sections are highly sensitive to the core shadowing cutoff parameter yc, which must be treated as an adjustable parameter rather than a precisely determined quantity.

## Method
The reaction is described by summing three independent terms, each corresponding to the interaction between one of the three projectile particles (n, n, or alpha core) and the target, using the optical model for the particle-target interaction. The final state two-body continuum wave functions are computed with appropriate boundary conditions, and the three-body projectile wave function is obtained by solving Faddeev equations in coordinate space. Core shadowing is implemented by setting the three-body wave function to zero when the Jacobi coordinate y between the interacting neutron and the center-of-mass of the remaining two particles is smaller than a cutoff value yc, which is related to the sum of target and core radii.

## Key numbers
- The 6He three-body wave function has 88% p2-configuration and 12% s2-configuration, with a binding energy of 0.95 MeV and a root mean square radius of 2.45 fm.
- The estimated core shadowing cutoff yc = 4 fm is consistent with the sum of target and core radii (around 4 fm).
- The absorption processes contribute 70-75% of the total 2n removal cross section, depending on beam energy.
- Shadowing with yc = 4 fm reduces the cross section by a factor around 3.6 relative to the unshadowed calculation.
- At 100 MeV/u beam energy, the total 2n removal cross section is 0.430 b for yc = 3 fm, 0.250 b for yc = 4 fm, and 0.130 b for yc = 5 fm.
- The width of the transverse neutron momentum distribution at 100 MeV/u is 77 MeV/c for yc = 3 fm, 75 MeV/c for yc = 4 fm, and 73 MeV/c for yc = 5 fm.
- The transverse 5He momentum distribution is well reproduced up to about 60 MeV/c with yc = 4 fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier eikonal and adiabatic approaches to halo breakup reactions (Suzuki 1994, Zhukov 1994, Korsheninnikov 1994, Hansen 1995, Zinser 1995, Nilsson 1995, Humblet 1995, Aleksandrov 1997, Garrido 1996, Garrido 1997, Al-Khalili 1996, Bertsch 1997) by providing a more complete treatment of final state interactions and including both absorption and diffraction processes. The method enables computation of absolute differential cross sections and their energy dependence, and distinguishes between longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions, which were previously treated as identical. The core shadowing treatment follows the black disk model of Barranco (1993) and is consistent with interpretations by Aleksandrov (1997) and Bertsch (1997).
