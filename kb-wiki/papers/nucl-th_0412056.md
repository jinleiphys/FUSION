# Superallowed nuclear beta decays: A critical survey with tests of CVC and the standard model

> A complete and critical survey is presented of all half-life, decay-energy and branching-ratio measurements related to 20 superallowed decays; no measurements are ignored, though some are rejected for cause and others updated. A new calculation of the statistical rate function is described and experimental ft values determined. The associated theoretical corrections needed to convert these results into Ft values are discussed, and careful attention is paid to the origin and magnitude of their uncertainties. As an exacting confirmation of the conserved vector current hypothesis, the Ft values are seen to be constant to 3 parts in 10^4. These data are also used to set new limits on any possible scalar interactions or right-hand currents. The average Ft value obtained from the survey, when combined with the muon lifetime, yields the CKM matrix element Vud = 0.9738(4); and the unitarity test on the top row of the matrix becomes |Vud|^2 + |Vus|^2 + |Vub|^2 = 0.9966(14) using the PDG's currently recommended values for Vus and Vub. We discuss the priorities for future theoretical and experimental work with the goal of making the CKM unitarity test more definitive.

## Key claim

The corrected Ft values for 20 superallowed nuclear beta decays are constant to 3 parts in 10^4, confirming the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis; however, the CKM unitarity test using the resulting V_ud = 0.9738(4) yields a sum of squares that falls short of unity by 0.3%, more than twice the quoted standard deviation, which is described as a "provocative but hardly definitive disagreement" with the standard model.

## Method

The authors conducted a complete critical survey of all published half-life, decay-energy, and branching-ratio measurements for 20 superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decays, scrutinizing original reports and updating results for improved calibration standards where possible. They performed a new calculation of the statistical rate function f, applied transition-dependent radiative corrections (split into a nuclear-structure-independent part delta_R' and a structure-dependent part delta_NS) and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections delta_C, and used weighted averaging with scale factors following Particle Data Group statistical procedures. For Q_EC values connected by difference measurements, a chi-squared minimization procedure was used to extract final averaged values.

## Key numbers

- Corrected Ft values are constant to 3 parts in 10^4 across all 20 superallowed transitions
- V_ud = 0.9738 ± 0.0004, obtained from the average Ft value combined with the muon lifetime
- CKM unitarity test: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_b|^2 = 0.9966 ± 0.0014, using PDG-recommended values for V_us and V_ub
- Limit on scalar interaction: C_S/C_V = -0.00005 ± 0.00130
- The constant K/(hbar c)^6 = (8120.271 ± 0.012) × 10^(-10) GeV^(-4) s
- For the eight best-known transitions (14O, 26Al^m, 34Cl, 38K^m, 42Sc, 46V, 50Mn, 54Co), experimental ft-values are known to better than 0.1%
- 10C ft-value precision: 0.15%
- 22Mg, 34Ar, and 74Rb ft-value standard deviations: 0.24% to 0.40%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous surveys (Hardy and Towner, 1975; Hardy and Towner, 1990) from eight to twenty superallowed transitions, incorporating new measurements for 22Mg, 34Ar, and 74Rb, and adding eight more cases likely to become accessible to precision measurements. The results provide the most precise test of CVC hypothesis to date and set new limits on scalar interactions and right-hand currents; the CKM unitarity test result (0.9966 ± 0.0014) is noted to disagree with unity at the 0.3% level, which has stimulated new measurements of the K_e3 branching ratio for V_us (Sher, 2003) that could potentially resolve the discrepancy.
