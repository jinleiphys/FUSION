# Identity method - a new tool for studying chemical fluctuations

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the chemical composition of the hadronic system produced in nuclear collisions are believed to be sensitive to properties of the transition between confined and deconfined strongly interacting matter. In this paper a new technique for the study of chemical fluctuation, the identity method, is introduced and its features are discussed. The method is tested using data on central Pb-Pb collisions at 40$A$~GeV registered by the NA49 experiment at the CERN SPS.

## Key claim

The identity method corrects the chemical fluctuation measure \(\Phi\) (and its variant \(\Psi_{wh}\)) for non-perfect particle identification in a model-independent way, using only the experimental mass resolution and mean particle multiplicities. The abstract does not specify that the correction factor \((1 - Var_{res}/Var_{B})^{-2}\) is proven and tested via Monte Carlo simulations, nor that the method is demonstrated on a single test case (proton fluctuations in Pb+Pb at 40\(A\) GeV).

## Method

The method defines a single-particle identity weight \(w_h(m) = \rho_h(m)/\rho(m)\) from measured mass spectra, then constructs an event variable \(Z = \sum_i (w_h(m_i) - \overline{w_h})\) and a fluctuation measure \(\Psi_{wh} = \langle Z^2 \rangle / \langle N \rangle - \overline{z^2}\). The measured \(\Psi_{res}\) is corrected to \(\Psi_{corr}\) via \(\Psi_{corr} = \Psi_{res} (1 - Var_{res}/Var_{B})^{-2}\), where \(Var_{res}\) is the variance per particle due to random identification at experimental resolution and \(Var_{B}\) is the binomial variance for no resolution. The method is tested on central Pb-Pb collisions at 40\(A\) GeV from NA49, using dE/dx spectra from the TPCs fitted with four Gauss functions (electrons, pions, kaons, protons) in bins of total momentum, transverse momentum, azimuthal angle, and charge.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicity per event: \(\langle N \rangle = 165.40\) and \(\langle N_p \rangle = 42.16\) for protons in central Pb-Pb collisions at 40\(A\) GeV.
- Measured fluctuation value: \(\Psi_{res} \cdot 1000 = -17.4 \pm 3.5\).
- Correction factor for non-unique particle identification: \(\approx 1.2\).
- Corrected fluctuation value: \(\Psi_{corr} \cdot 1000 = -22.3 \pm 4.4\).
- Analysis used 4000 events with particles up to total momentum 40 GeV/c and transverse momentum up to 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the \(\Phi\) measure (used by NA49 for transverse momentum, electric charge, and azimuthal angle fluctuations) and the \(\sigma_{dyn}\) / \(\nu_{dyn}\) measures (used by NA49 and STAR) by addressing their common disadvantage: dependence on non-perfect particle identification. The identity method enables model-independent correction of chemical fluctuation measurements, which is critical for the NA61 beam energy scan and the RHIC beam energy scan program searching for the critical point and first-order phase transition. The method is tested on NA49 data (Gazdzicki et al., in preparation) and is being applied to all NA49 energies.
