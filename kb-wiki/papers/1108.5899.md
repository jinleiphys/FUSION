# Spin excitonic and diffusive modes in superfluid Fermi liquids

> A role of a particle-particle p-wave spin interaction in Fermi liquids with s-wave pairing is studied. Depending on the sign of the interaction there arises either the new exciton collective mode below the pair-breaking threshold or the diffusive excitation mode above the threshold. The Landau parameters which control the interaction strength are evaluated for various systems: the dilute fermion gases, degenerate electron liquid, metals, atomic nuclei and neutron matter. The interaction removes also the square-root singularity in the phase space of pair breaking processes. It is shown how these effects influence the neutrino emissivity in the neutron Cooper-pair recombinations in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Depending on the sign of the p-wave spin interaction in the particle-particle channel, either a new undamped spin exciton collective mode appears below the pair-breaking threshold (for negative interaction parameter \(C<0\)) or a diffusive excitation mode appears above the threshold (for \(C>0\)); the existence of these modes is conditional on the value of the effective interaction parameter \(C\), which is controlled by the Landau parameters \(g_1^\xi\), \(f_0^\xi\), and \(g_1^\omega\).

## Method

The authors extend Fermi-liquid theory with s-wave pairing (Larkin-Migdal-Leggett approach) to include a p-wave spin interaction in the particle-particle (\(\xi\)) channel, parameterized by the Landau parameter \(g_1^\xi\). They solve the Larkin-Migdal equations for the in-medium vertices induced by an external spin-density and helicity-density perturbation, obtaining the current-current correlator and the response function. The key parameter \(C = 3/g_1^\xi - 1/f_0^\xi\) is evaluated for several Fermi systems using either microscopic calculations (dilute Fermi gas) or existing \(\omega\)-channel Landau parameters transformed via the \(s\)-\(p\) approximation.

## Key numbers

*   For a dilute Fermi gas, the effective interaction parameter is \(C \approx -5.7/(a_{\rm eff} p_{\rm F})^2\), where \(a_{\rm eff}\) is the effective scattering length.
*   For the degenerate electron liquid at \(a_{\rm B}p_{\rm F}=0.032\) (where \(a_{\rm B}\) is the Bohr radius), \(C = -2.54\).
*   For sodium (alkali metal), the Landau parameters are \(f_0^\xi({\rm Na}) = -0.11\), \(g_1^\xi({\rm Na}) = -0.38\), and \(g_1^\omega({\rm Na}) = -0.075\); for potassium, \(f_0^\xi({\rm K}) = -0.56\), \(g_1^\xi({\rm K}) = -0.89\), and \(g_1^\omega({\rm K}) = -0.12\), yielding \(C = -1.48\).
*   For atomic nuclei, using \(\omega\)-Landau parameters from nuclear experiments, \(C \sim -10\) for Fermi momentum \(p_{\rm F} \lesssim 1\) fm\(^{-1}\).
*   The neutrino emissivity ratio \(\varepsilon_{\nu\bar\nu}/\varepsilon_{\nu\bar\nu}^{(0)}\) for neutron PBF processes shows a pronounced effect for \(|C| \sim 5\)–\(10\) at temperatures \(T/T_c \lesssim 0.5\), with suppression for \(C>0\) and enhancement for \(C<0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of collective modes in superconductors and superfluid Fermi liquids, building on the Larkin-Migdal-Leggett theory (Larkin and Migdal, 1963; Leggett, 1963) and prior work on residual interactions generating collective modes in superconductors (collmode, 1970) and superfluid \(^3\)He (Baldo, 1975). It specifically addresses the previously unconsidered influence of p-wave interaction in the spin-one channel on singlet pairing. The results enable predictions for the neutrino emissivity from Cooper-pair recombination in neutron stars, modifying the standard PBF emissivity (FRS76, 1976; KV08, 2008) and potentially impacting neutron star cooling models, including the rapid cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A (Page, 2011).
