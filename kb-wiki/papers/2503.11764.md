# Testing the QCD formation time with reconstructed parton splittings

> In high-energy elementary collisions the space-time ordering of parton branching processes is not accessible experimentally. In contrast, in heavy-ion collisions, parton showers interact with a spatially extended dense medium. This sets a reference length scale with respect to which the space-time ordering may be analysed. Here, we explore the possibility of identifying experimental signatures of the QCD formation time, $τ_f$, on the level of a single parton splitting. Since heavy flavour offers an additional handle on tracing the propagation of individual quarks through the medium, we focus on the $g\to c\bar{c}$ splitting. Combining adapted versions of the Cambridge-Aachen and FlavourCone jet finding algorithms with grooming techniques, we show how the kinematics of such splittings can be reconstructed with high fidelity using either final state partons or hadrons, and how the formation time distribution of parton splittings can be constructed therefrom. Medium modification leads to a characteristic modification of this $τ_f$ distribution. This effect can be used to construct experimentally-accessible ratios of $τ_f$ distributions, in which the sensitivity of the medium modification to the QCD formation time becomes measurable.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the QCD formation time of individual parton splittings can be experimentally tested in heavy-ion collisions by reconstructing $g\to c\bar{c}$ splittings from the hadronic final state, provided that the medium modification of the splitting function depends on the ratio of formation time to in-medium path length as shown in Eq. (2), which interpolates between totally coherent and totally incoherent limits.

## Method
The authors use Pythia8 Monte Carlo event generation for proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, reconstructing jets with the anti-$k_t$ algorithm ($R=0.4$) and applying SoftDrop grooming ($z_\text{cut}=0.2$, $\beta=0$). They adapt the Cambridge/Aachen reclustering algorithm and a modified FlavourCone algorithm (with radius set to half the distance between heavy flavored hadrons) to reconstruct the kinematics of $g\to c\bar{c}$ splittings from final state hadrons, comparing reconstructed quantities to Monte Carlo truth from the parton shower.

## Key numbers
- Charm mass $m_c \sim 1.3$ GeV/$c^2$ provides a scale where medium modification $\propto (\hat{q}L/m_c^2)$ is order unity for typical $\hat{q}L$ values
- For $g\to b\bar{b}$, medium modification is a factor $m_c^2/m_b^2 \approx 1/10$ smaller than for $g\to c\bar{c}$
- Splittings below 50 GeV have $\tau_f < 1.44$ fm/$c$, much shorter than typical QGP in-medium length of about 5 fm/$c$
- Jets with $p_T^\text{jet}>50$ GeV and $p_T^\text{jet}>200$ GeV are analyzed; heavy flavor tracks required to have $p_T^\text{HF}>2$ GeV
- SoftDrop grooming applied with $z_\text{cut}=0.2$, $\beta=0$; jet-level selection $|\eta^\text{jet}|<1.9$

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of medium-modified splitting functions in the $N=1$ opacity expansion by Wiedemann (2000), Gyulassy and collaborators (2000), and Attems et al. (2022), as well as multiple-soft scattering formulations by Caron-Huot (2010). It builds on jet substructure techniques developed by Larkoski et al. (2017) and the FlavourCone algorithm by Ilten et al. (2017). The paper enables experimental tests of formation time dependence in heavy-ion collisions, complementing proposals using reclustered jets by Apolinario et al. (2024) and boosted W bosons by Apolinario et al. (2017).
