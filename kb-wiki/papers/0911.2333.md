# Inhomogeneous phases of strongly interacting matter

> We discuss possible inhomogeneous phases in two regions of the QCD phase diagram: We begin with color superconducting quark matter at moderately high densities, which is an imbalanced Fermi system due to the finite strange quark mass and neutrality constraints. Within an NJL-type toy model we find that this situation could lead to the formation of a soliton lattice. Similar solutions also exist in the context of the chiral phase transition. As an interesting result, the first-order transition line in the phase diagram of homogeneous phases gets replaced by an inhomogeneous phase which is bordered by two second-order transition lines.

# Inhomogeneous phases of strongly interacting matter

## Key claim
Inhomogeneous phases (soliton lattices and spatially modulated condensates) can replace first-order phase transitions in two distinct regions of the QCD phase diagram, color-superconducting quark matter and the chiral phase transition, but only within the limitations of a simplified NJL-type toy model with one-dimensional periodic modulations and a high-density approximation.

## Method
The authors study an NJL-type model with three-flavor, three-color massless quarks, using a mean-field approximation where the diquark gap function (for color superconductivity) or the chiral condensate (for the chiral transition) is allowed to have general one-dimensional periodic spatial modulations. For the color-superconducting case, they employ a 2SC-like pairing scheme with two flavors and two colors, a high-density approximation to reduce the Dirac structure, and a Pauli-Villars regularization scheme to avoid artifacts from momentum cutoffs. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to the Fourier components of the gap function at fixed wave vector, then the wave vector itself is varied to find the global minimum.

## Key numbers
- For the color-superconducting study: average chemical potential fixed at $\bar\mu = 400$ MeV, cutoff $\Lambda = 400$ MeV, BCS gap $\Delta_{BCS} = 80$ MeV.
- The inhomogeneous (one-dimensional) phase exists in a window of Fermi-surface mismatch $\delta\mu$ approximately twice as wide as the single-plane-wave (FF) phase window.
- At $\delta\mu = 0.7 \Delta_{BCS}$, the gap function forms a soliton lattice with period corresponding to $q = 0.1 \Delta_{BCS}$ showing nearly constant plateaus at $\pm \Delta_{BCS}$.
- The energetically preferred wave vector $q$ goes continuously to zero at the lower phase boundary between BCS and inhomogeneous phase, making that transition second order.
- At the upper phase boundary to the normal phase, the amplitude stays of order $\Delta_{BCS}$, making that transition first order.
- For the chiral phase transition at $T=0$, the wave vector $|\vec{q}|$ goes to zero at the lower end of the inhomogeneous phase, while the average amplitude $\sqrt{\langle M^2\rangle}$ goes to zero at the upper end.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of inhomogeneous pairing in color superconductors that were restricted to Ginzburg-Landau approximations or finite superpositions of plane waves (Alford 2000, Bowers 2002, Casalbuoni 2005, Mannarelli 2006, Rajagopal 2006). The authors build on their own prior work (Nickel 2008) to go beyond those restrictions by allowing arbitrary one-dimensional periodic modulations in an NJL model. For the chiral transition, the results parallel findings in the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model (Schnetz 2004, 2005) and show that the Lifshitz point coincides with the critical point of the homogeneous phase diagram. This work enables future studies coupling chiral and superconducting condensates simultaneously, extending to 2+1 flavors, and exploring two- or three-dimensional modulations to connect single-soliton descriptions of nucleons (Christov 1995, Alkofer 1994) to nuclear matter and color-superconducting quark matter.
