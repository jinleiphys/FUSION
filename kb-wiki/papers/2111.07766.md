# Accurate relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to NNLO

> We construct a relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to the next-to-next-to-leading order in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We show that a good description of the $np$ phase shifts up to $T_\mathrm{lab}=200$ MeV and even higher can be achieved with a $\tildeχ^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}$ less than 1. Both the next-to-leading order results and the next-to-next-to-leading order results describe the phase shifts equally well up to $T_\mathrm{lab}=200$ MeV, but for higher energies, the latter behaves better, showing satisfactory convergence. The relativistic chiral potential provides the most essential inputs for relativistic ab initio studies of nuclear structure and reactions, which has been in need for almost two decades.

## Key claim

The authors construct a relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, achieving a good description of np phase shifts up to T_lab = 200 MeV with a total χ̃²/d.o.f. less than 1, but the NLO and NNLO results describe the phase shifts equally well up to 200 MeV, with the NNLO only showing better convergence at higher energies.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar (BbS) equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering, using a covariant chiral potential that includes LO and NLO contact terms, one-pion exchange (OPE), leading and subleading two-pion exchange (TPE), and an iterated OPE term. Nineteen low-energy constants (LECs) are fitted to np phase shifts from the PWA93 partial wave analysis for all partial waves with total angular momentum J ≤ 2, using eight data points per partial wave at laboratory kinetic energies T_lab = 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, 200 MeV. A sharp cutoff Λ = 0.9 GeV is used to regularize the potential, and truncation uncertainties are estimated using a Bayesian model.

## Key numbers

- Total χ̃² = 16.61 for the relativistic NNLO fit to np phase shifts for partial waves with J ≤ 2 (compared to 17.02 for NLO, 8.84 for NR-N³LO-Idaho, and 16.08 for NR-N³LO-EKM)
- χ̃² for individual partial waves at NNLO: ¹S₀ = 0.18, ³P₀ = 0.30, ¹P₁ = 1.07, ³P₁ = 1.55, ³S₁ = 3.36, ³D₁ = 0.26, ε₁ = 0.03, ¹D₂ = 0.01, ³D₂ = 9.56, ³P₂ = 0.01, ³F₂ = 0.27, ε₂ = 0.01
- Pion-nucleon couplings used: c₁ = -1.39 GeV⁻¹, c₂ = 4.01 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -6.61 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.92 GeV⁻¹; pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling g_A = 1.29
- Cutoff Λ = 0.9 GeV for the main results; a cutoff of 0.8 GeV yields a total χ̃² as small as 5.3
- The relativistic NNLO potential contains 19 LECs, compared to 9 for non-relativistic NNLO and 24 for non-relativistic N³LO

## Context

This work extends the covariant power-counting approach to NN interactions proposed by Ren (2016) and Xiao (2018) from leading order to NNLO, building on earlier demonstrations that relativistic effects improve peripheral NN scattering data compared to non-relativistic treatments (Xiao, 2020; Wang, 2021). The resulting relativistic chiral NN interaction provides the essential input for relativistic ab initio studies of nuclear structure and reactions, which have relied on the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1987) for nearly two decades, and may enable new insights into long-standing problems such as the A_y puzzle when combined with the leading order relativistic 3N chiral force explored by Girlanda (2018).
