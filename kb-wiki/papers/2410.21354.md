# Comment on: "Investigating the time dependence of neutron-proton equilibration using molecular dynamics simulations" by A.Jedele et al: Phys Rev C 107, 024601 (2023)

> In this paper, the authors discuss neutron-proton equilibration process induced on the 70Zn + 70Zn system at 35 MeV/nucleon, comparing experimental results with the Anti-symmetrized Molecular Dynamics and the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model simulations (COMD). The comment contains observations on the improper use of the COMD model, including some misinterpretations of the obtained results.

# Comment on: "Investigating the time dependence of neutron-proton equilibration using molecular dynamics simulations" by A. Jedele et al.

## Key claim
The original COMD (Constrained Molecular Dynamics) model was not designed to describe details of light cluster production, and its application to the 70Zn + 70Zn system at 35 MeV/nucleon in the target paper involved improper use of the model, including inappropriate application of statistical decay codes to light fragments and insufficient specification of initial configuration stability.

## Method
The author critiques the use of the COMD model in the original paper by examining three specific issues: (1) the inapplicability of semi-classical molecular dynamics for describing quantum effects in light cluster production; (2) the inappropriate use of statistical decay codes like Gemini for de-excitation of light nuclei with charge Z=6-10, where the model's ground state binding does not match experimental values; and (3) the lack of specification regarding the cooling-warming procedure used to obtain stable initial configurations, which is fundamental for reliable dynamics in COMD simulations.

## Key numbers
- The average binding energy for good ground state configurations in COMD is typically about 8-8.7 MeV/nucleon, obtained after a cooling-warming procedure coupled with the Pauli constraint.
- The clusterization radius used in the original paper is d = 2.76 fm; the author notes his current version uses d = 2.56 fm, and from a liquid drop model at saturation density rho_0, d = 2r_0 = 2(3/(4*pi*rho_0))^(1/3) = 2.26 fm.
- The original COMD calculations evolved dynamics up to t_M = 1000 fm/c, compared with Anti-symmetrized Molecular Dynamics results at 300 fm/c.
- The stability time T_stab of selected configurations should be at least of the order of different hundreds of fm/c.

## Context
This comment addresses the paper by Jedele et al. (2023) on neutron-proton equilibration in 70Zn + 70Zn collisions, specifically critiquing their use of the COMD model originally developed by Papa (see COMD references). The work extends the discussion of limitations of semi-classical molecular dynamics approaches for heavy-ion collisions around the Fermi energy, as reviewed by Wolt (reference in text). The comment clarifies that COMD, like other molecular dynamics models, cannot describe quantum effects in light particle structure, and that proper use requires careful preparation of initial ground state configurations through a cooling-warming procedure to ensure stability times of hundreds of fm/c. This enables future users to apply COMD appropriately, particularly for studying fast pre-equilibrium emission versus more delayed processes.
