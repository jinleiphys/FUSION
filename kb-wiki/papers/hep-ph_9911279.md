# Chiral dynamics of many-pion systems

> Based on the Weinberg lagrangian or, in a new language, the lagrangian of hidden local symmetry added with the term induced by the anomalous lagrangian of Wess and Zumino, the dynamics of the decays rho to 4 pi and omega to 5 pi is considered. The excitation curves of rho resonance in its decay to 4 pi are obtained for the first time. The comparison with recent CMD-2 data is given. The partial widths of the decays omega to 2 pi^+ 2 pi^- pi^0 and omega to pi^+ pi^- 3 pi^0 which can be measured on colliders with luminosity 10^{33}{cm }^{-2}{s}^{-1} are evaluated for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the partial width of the ρ meson decay to 4π evaluated at the ρ mass alone is an inadequate characteristic of the dynamics; instead, the resonance excitation curve in e+e- → ρ0 → 4π is the proper test ground for chiral models, and the predictions of chiral symmetry do not contradict the three lowest experimental points of CMD-2 but show substantial deviation at energies above 800 MeV, likely due to neglected higher derivatives and chiral loops.

## Method

The authors use the Weinberg Lagrangian (equivalently, the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian) supplemented by the Wess-Zumino anomalous term to compute amplitudes for ρ → 4π and ω → 5π decays. They evaluate partial widths at √s = mρ = 770 MeV and compute the energy-dependent cross sections for e+e- → ρ0 → 4π, comparing with CMD-2 data. For ω → 5π, they use nonrelativistic approximations and a simplified phase-space integration to estimate branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Γ(ρ0 → 2π+2π-, mρ2) = 0.89 keV
- Γ(ρ0 → π+π-2π0, mρ2) = 0.24 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.44 keV (with anomaly term)
- Γ(ρ+ → π+3π0, mρ2) = 0.41 keV
- Γ(ρ+ → 2π+π-π0, mρ2) = 0.71 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.90 keV (with anomaly term)
- B(ω → 2π+2π-π0) = 4.1 × 10-9
- B(ω → π+π-3π0) = 1.7 × 10-9
- The ratio Γ(ρ → 2π+2π-, s)/ΓLIPS(ρ → 2π+2π-, s) changes from 0.4 at √s = 650 MeV to 1 at √s = mρ
- The difference between Γ(ρ → π+π-2π0, s) with and without the anomaly term is 100% at √s = mρ, 8% at √s = 700 MeV, and 0.3% at √s = 650 MeV
- gρππ = 5.89 from the KSRF relation
- fπ = 92.4 MeV (pion decay constant)
- gωρπ = 14.3 GeV-1

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Rittenberg (1969), Bramon (1993), Kuraev (1995), and Birse (1996) on ρ → 4π decays, correcting for the fact that previous studies evaluated the partial width only at the ρ mass without considering the rapid energy dependence of the width across the broad ρ resonance. The paper provides the first evaluation of ρ resonance excitation curves in e+e- → 4π and the first estimates of ω → 5π branching ratios, which could be measured at colliders with luminosity 1033 cm-2 s-1. The results enable experimental tests of chiral dynamics at the left shoulder of the ρ resonance, where contributions from higher derivatives and chiral loops are minimal.
