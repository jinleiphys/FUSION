# The Infrared Behavior of Gluon and Ghost Propagators in Landau Gauge QCD

> A solvable systematic truncation scheme for the Dyson-Schwinger equations of Euclidean QCD in Landau gauge is presented. It implements the Slavnov-Taylor identities for the three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices, whereas irreducible four-gluon couplings as well as the gluon-ghost and ghost-ghost scattering kernels are neglected. The infrared behavior of gluon and ghost propagators is obtained analytically: The gluon propagator vanishes for small spacelike momenta whereas the ghost propagator diverges stronger than a massless particle pole. The numerical solutions are compared with recent lattice data for these propagators. The running coupling of the renormalization scheme approaches a fixed point, $α_c \simeq 9.5$, in the infrared.

## Key claim

The gluon propagator vanishes for small spacelike momenta and the ghost propagator diverges more strongly than a massless particle pole, but only within a truncation scheme that neglects irreducible four-gluon couplings and ghost-ghost scattering kernels while implementing Slavnov-Taylor identities for the three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices.

## Method

The authors solved a truncated system of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the gluon and ghost propagators in Euclidean Landau gauge QCD, using a one-dimensional angle approximation for the momentum integrals. The three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices were constructed from their Slavnov-Taylor identities, while four-gluon vertices and ghost-ghost scattering kernels were neglected; the infrared behavior was obtained analytically by assuming a power-law ansatz for the product of the gluon and ghost renormalization functions.

## Key numbers

- The infrared exponent for the product of gluon and ghost renormalization functions is κ = (61 − √1897)/19 ≈ 0.92.
- The running coupling approaches an infrared fixed point at α_c ≈ 9.5.
- The gluon renormalization function behaves as Z(x) → c² x^{2κ} for x = k² → 0.
- The ghost renormalization function behaves as G(x) → (g²γ₀^G (1/κ − 1/2))⁻¹ c⁻¹ x^{−κ} for x → 0, where γ₀^G = 9/(64π²).
- The gluon propagator was normalized to Z(x = 1) ≈ 11.3 for comparison with lattice data from Maris (1995).
- The running coupling at the Z-boson mass was set to α_S(M_Z) = 0.118, yielding α_S(M_τ) = 0.38 using M_Z/M_τ ≈ 51.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE studies that neglected ghost contributions, such as the Mandelstam approximation (Mandelstam 1979, Atkinson 1981, Brown 1989, Hauck 1996), which produced an infrared singular coupling rather than a fixed point. The results compare favorably with lattice data from Maris (1995) and Summers (1996), and the violation of reflection positivity in the gluon propagator is interpreted as a signal for confined gluons. The existence of the infrared fixed point at α_c ≈ 9.5 suggests that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking will be generated when quarks are included in the quark DSE.
