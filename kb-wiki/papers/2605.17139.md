# Stable minimum principles for scattering states

> Quantum-mechanical scattering states are energy eigenstates obeying particular boundary conditions, whose behavior at infinity encodes the S-matrix which defines the outcoming of scattering experiments. With an eye toward numerical algorithms for computing nonrelativistic S-matrices, we present a family of stable minimum principles for scattering states. States that approximately satisfy these minimum principles are shown to have a bounded difference with the true scattering states. These minimum principles and stability estimates can be used to obtain rigorous bounds on scattering amplitudes. We show that these minimum principles are applicable to momentum-dependent potentials, long-range (Coulomb) interactions, and elastic or inelastic scattering of bound states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a family of stable minimum principles for approximate scattering states, where the $L_1$ norm of the Schrödinger violation provides a bound on the error in extracted S-matrix elements, but only when combined with an estimate of the maximum amplitude of the true scattering state, a limitation the authors acknowledge is shared with prior approaches.

## Method

The authors derive lower bounds on the $L_1$ norm of the Schrödinger violation $(E-\hat H)\tilde\psi$ for an approximate scattering state $\tilde\psi$, relating this norm to the asymptotic error in the outgoing wavefunction via Hölder's inequality and integration by parts. They use exact scattering eigenstates as test functions to convert surface integrals at infinity into bounds on phase shifts, cross sections, and pointwise scattering amplitudes. The analysis is extended to momentum-dependent potentials, Coulomb interactions, and many-body scattering of bound states.

## Key numbers

- The bound on the error in partial-wave phase shifts is $|f_\ell - \tilde f_\ell| \le \frac{M}{2 \pi (2\ell+1)} \|\xi_\ell\|_\infty \|(E - \hat H)\tilde\psi\|_1$, where $\xi_\ell^*$ is the eigenstate in partial wave $\ell$.
- The bound on the error in total cross section is $|\sqrt\sigma - \sqrt{\tilde\sigma}| \le \frac{1}{2\sqrt\pi}M \Xi \|(E-\hat H)\tilde\psi\|_1$, where $\Xi$ is the supremum of $L_\infty$ norms of scattering states with ingoing momenta of magnitude $|k|$.
- The pointwise bound on the scattering amplitude error is $|\tilde f - f| \le \frac{M \|\psi\|_\infty}{\pi} \int |(E-\hat H)\tilde\psi|$.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach to scattering pioneered by Kohn (1948) and the minimum-variance method of Bardsley et al (1972), which used weighted $L_2$ norms and required solving related eigenvalue problems or estimating maximum wavefunction amplitudes. The authors build on the $L_1$-norm approach of Darewych, Pooran, and Sokoloff (1978-1980), which was previously limited to s-wave scattering from central potentials or required seminumerical procedures for one-dimensional problems. The paper enables rigorous bounds on scattering amplitudes for a wider class of potentials and many-body systems, with the authors noting that their method is designed for use with variational Monte Carlo algorithms that minimize the $L_1$ norm by stochastic gradient descent.
