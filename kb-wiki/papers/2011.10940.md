# QCD equations of state and speed of sound in neutron stars

> Neutron stars are cosmic laboratories to study dense matter in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). The observable mass-radius relations of neutron stars are determined by QCD equations of state, and can reflect the properties of QCD phase transitions. In the last decade there have been historical discoveries in neutron stars, the discoveries of two-solar mass neutron stars and neutron star merger events, which have imposed tight constraints on equations of state. While a number of equations of state are constructed to satisfy these constraints, a theoretical challenge is how to reconcile those constructions with the microphysics expected from the hadron physics and in-medium calculations. In this short article we briefly go over recent observations and discuss their implications for dense QCD matter, referring to QCD constraints in the low and high density limits, QCD-like theories, and lattice QCD results for baryon-baryon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while current neutron star observations (two-solar-mass pulsars and the GW170817 merger) have imposed tight constraints on equations of state, a theoretical challenge remains in reconciling these phenomenological constructions with the microphysics expected from hadron physics and in-medium calculations, particularly regarding the speed of sound behavior in dense QCD matter.

## Method

The author reviews observational constraints from Shapiro delay measurements of massive pulsars (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620), gravitational wave data from GW170817 and GW190425, and NICER X-ray observations of PSR J0030+0451. These are combined with theoretical considerations dividing the density domain into four regimes based on plausible effective degrees of freedom: nuclear matter below 2n0, the intermediate 2-5n0 region, quark matter from 5n0 to 40n0, and perturbative QCD above 40n0.

## Key numbers

- Two-solar-mass pulsars: PSR J0348+0432 mass = 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis 2013); PSR J0740+6620 mass = 2.14 (+0.10/-0.09) solar masses (Cromartie 2019)
- GW170817 total mass before merger = 2.73-2.78 solar masses; radius estimated at 11.9 (+1.4/-1.4) km for each star (Abbott 2018)
- NICER radius for PSR J0030+0451: R1 = 13.02 (+1.24/-1.06) km with M1 = 1.44 (+0.15/-0.14) solar masses (Miller 2019); R2 = 12.71 (+1.14/-1.19) km with M2 = 1.34 (+0.15/-0.16) solar masses (Riley 2019)
- Saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}; canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars reach 2-3 n0; 2 solar mass neutron stars reach about 5 n0
- Perturbative QCD becomes valid above about 40 n0
- Two-color QCD lattice results show diquark condensation critical temperature Ts about 100 MeV at quark chemical potential of about 1 GeV (Boz 2019)

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting neutron star observations to QCD matter properties, building on the framework of Baym (2017), Buballa (2014), and Alford (2007) for dense matter reviews. The paper discusses how the quark-hadron continuity picture (originally proposed by Schafer 1998 based on order parameter indistinguishability) and the quarkyonic matter hypothesis (McLerran 2007) provide theoretical frameworks for understanding the rapid growth in speed of sound required by observations. The work enables future systematic studies of neutron star mergers and supernovae through numerical simulations, and connects to heavy ion beam energy scan programs searching for the QCD critical endpoint (Fukushima 2020, Dexheimer 2020).
