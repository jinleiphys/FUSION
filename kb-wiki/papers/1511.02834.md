# Sensitivity to oscillation with a sterile fourth generation neutrino from ultra-low threshold neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering

> We discuss prospects for probing short-range sterile neutrino oscillation using neutrino-nucleus coherent scattering with ultra-low energy ($\sim 10$ eV - 100 eV) recoil threshold cryogenic Ge detectors. The analysis is performed in the context of a specific and contemporary reactor-based experimental proposal, developed in cooperation with the Nuclear Science Center at Texas A\&M University, and references developing technology based upon economical and scalable detector arrays. The baseline of the experiment is substantially shorter than existing measurements, as near as about 2 meters from the reactor core, and is moreover variable, extending continuously up to a range of about 10 meters. This proximity and variety combine to provide extraordinary sensitivity to a wide spectrum of oscillation scales, while facilitating the tidy cancellation of leading systematic uncertainties in the reactor source and environment. With 100~eV sensitivity, for exposures on the order of 200 kg$\cdot$y, we project an estimated sensitivity to first/fourth neutrino oscillation with a mass gap $Δm^2 \sim 1 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at an amplitude $\sin^2 2θ\sim 10^{-1}$, or $Δm^2 \sim 0.2 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at unit amplitude. Larger exposures, around 5,000 kg$\cdot$y, together with 10 eV sensitivity are capable of probing more than an additional order of magnitude in amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper projects that a reactor-based experiment using ultra-low threshold cryogenic germanium detectors can achieve sensitivity to sterile neutrino oscillations with a mass gap $\Delta m^2 \sim 1 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at an amplitude $\sin^2 2\theta \sim 10^{-1}$, or $\Delta m^2 \sim 0.2 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at unit amplitude, but only under the condition that a 100 eV nuclear recoil threshold and approximately 200 kg$\cdot$y of exposure are achieved; the more ambitious 10 eV threshold scenario requires substantial further research and development.

## Method

The authors model coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$\nu$NS) rates for a proposed experiment at the Texas A&M University TRIGA reactor, using cryogenic germanium detectors with recoil thresholds as low as 10-100 eV. They compute the expected event rate modification due to oscillation between electron anti-neutrinos and a sterile fourth neutrino flavor, parameterized by a mass gap $\Delta m^2_{14}$ and mixing amplitude $\sin^2 2\theta_{14}$, and project sensitivity by convolving the reactor anti-neutrino spectrum with the CE$\nu$NS cross-section and detector response across multiple recoil energy bins and baselines from 2 to 10 meters.

## Key numbers

- For 100 eV recoil threshold and 200 kg$\cdot$y exposure: sensitivity to $\Delta m^2 \sim 1 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at $\sin^2 2\theta \sim 10^{-1}$, or $\Delta m^2 \sim 0.2 \, {\rm eV}^2$ at unit amplitude.
- For 10 eV recoil threshold and 5,000 kg$\cdot$y exposure: capable of probing more than an additional order of magnitude in amplitude beyond the 100 eV scenario.
- The TAMU reactor intrinsic anti-neutrino production rate is approximately $1.9 \times 10^{17}$ per second, yielding a flux at 1 m of $\Phi_0 \simeq 1.5 \times 10^{12} \, [{\rm s}\cdot{\rm cm}^{2}]^{-1}$.
- Expected SM CE$\nu$NS event rate in 1 kg of germanium at 1 m for 1 year: 853 events in the 10-20 eV recoil bin, 961 in 20-35 eV, 962 in 35-55 eV, 911 in 55-80 eV, 818 in 80-110 eV, 874 in 110-155 eV, 820 in 155-220 eV, 862 in 220-350 eV, and 782 in 350-1000 eV.
- Demonstrated electron recoil resolution: 7 ${\rm eV}_{ee}$ in a 1 cm thick wafer; projected resolution of 2 ${\rm eV}_{ee}$ for 3.3 cm detectors.
- Projected nuclear recoil threshold range: 50-100 eV for first-generation detectors, assuming Lindhard factor 0.1-0.2.
- Background target: 100 dru (events/kg/day/keV) for nearest-field observations, 10 or 1 dru for extended baselines.
- CE$\nu$NS rate in germanium: about 20 dru at 1 m, about 5 dru at 2 m, about 1 dru at 5 m.

## Context

This work extends the concept of probing sterile neutrino oscillations through coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, first suggested by Drukier and Stodolsky (1983) and updated by Anderson et al. (2012) for pion and muon decay-at-rest sources. It differs from prior reactor-based proposals such as those using scintillators for inverse beta decay (Ashenfelter et al., 2013) by employing ultra-low threshold cryogenic germanium detectors and a variable baseline as short as 2 meters from the reactor core, which enables cancellation of leading systematic uncertainties. The results enable future experimental programs to probe the $\Delta m^2_{14} \sim 1 \, {\rm eV}^2$ region suggested by GALLEX, SAGE, LSND, and MiniBooNE anomalies, and can test sterile neutrino models that evade cosmological constraints from Planck and BBN.
