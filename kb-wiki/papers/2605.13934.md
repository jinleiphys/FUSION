# Diquark Correlators and Phase Structure in the Quark-Meson-Diquark Model beyond Mean Field

> A comprehensive study of the phase structure of the two-flavor quark-meson-diquark model is presented within the nonperturbative functional renormalization group framework. The influence of mesonic fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation is investigated, and two-point functions of the diquark fields are computed at finite real-time frequencies. Renormalization group consistency of the effective potential is ensured in order to avoid cutoff artifacts. Substantial modifications of the phase structure are found once mesonic fluctuations are included, and for sufficiently strong diquark couplings the dynamics become dominated by diquark condensation. These effects are elucidated through an analysis of the diquark pole mass and the Silver-Blaze property.

## Key claim

Mesonic fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation substantially modify the phase structure of the two-flavor quark-meson-diquark model, and for sufficiently strong diquark couplings the dynamics become dominated by diquark condensation, but this conclusion depends on ensuring renormalization group consistency of the effective potential to avoid cutoff artifacts.

## Method

The authors employed the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework at leading order of the derivative expansion (local-potential approximation, LPA) to compute the scale-dependent effective potential of the quark-meson-diquark model at finite temperature and chemical potential. They derived the regularized quark propagator in the Nambu-Gor'kov basis, which naturally decomposes into paired red-green and spectator blue sectors, and evaluated the Wetterich flow equation for homogeneous constant background fields. Renormalization group consistency was enforced to treat cutoff artifacts, and two-point functions of the diquark fields were computed at finite real-time frequencies.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass is dynamically generated as \(m_q = g_{\phi} \sigma\), with the sigma field \(\sigma\) acquiring a vacuum expectation value that spontaneously breaks chiral symmetry.
- The quasiparticle dispersion relations in the paired sector are \(E_q^\pm = \sqrt{(\epsilon_q \pm \mu)^2 + \Delta_{\text{gap}}^2}\) where \(\Delta_{\text{gap}} = g_\Delta \Delta\) and \(\epsilon_q(\vec{p}) = \sqrt{\vec{p}^2 + m_q^2}\).
- The diquark fields carry baryon number \(B = 2/3\), and the chemical potential couples to them via the covariant derivative \(\partial_\mu \to \partial_\mu \mp 2\mu \delta_{\mu 4}\).
- The flow equation for the effective potential involves Matsubara sums with fermionic frequencies \(\nu_n = (2n+1)\pi T\) and bosonic frequencies, with the bosonic contribution from the chiral sector including terms \(\coth(\epsilon_\pi / 2T)\) and \(\coth(\epsilon_\sigma / 2T)\).

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of quark-meson models (Berges 1997, Schaefer 2004, Strodthoff 2011) and builds upon first-principles QCD analyses showing that gluonic degrees of freedom decouple around 1 GeV (Braun 2007, Mitter 2014, Cyrol 2016). It is motivated by recent FRG results on QCD quark-gluon dynamics (Leonhardt 2019, Braun 2022) and complements studies of quark-diquark models as effective descriptions of baryonic degrees of freedom (Stoll 2025, Gholami 2025). The work enables future QCD-constrained FRG studies of the quark-meson-diquark model at finite temperature and density, particularly for investigating the transition from chirally broken hadronic matter to the two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase.
