# Heavy Quarkonium Production at Collider Energies: Partonic Cross Section and Polarization

> We calculate the ${\cal O}(α_s^3)$ short-distance, QCD collinear-factorized coefficient functions for all partonic channels that include the production of a heavy quark pair at short distances. This provides the first power correction to the collinear-factorized inclusive hadronic production of heavy quarkonia at large transverse momentum, $p_T$, including the full leading-order perturbative contributions to the production of heavy quark pairs in all color and spin states employed in NRQCD treatments of this process. We discuss the role of the first power correction in the production rates and the polarizations of heavy quarkonia in high energy hadronic collisions. The consistency of QCD collinear factorization and non-relativistic QCD factorization applied to heavy quarkonium production is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first complete calculation of the O(α_s^3) short-distance coefficient functions for all partonic channels producing a heavy quark pair at short distances, which constitutes the next-to-leading power (NLP) correction to collinear-factorized inclusive heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum p_T. The authors demonstrate that this QCD factorization formalism, combined with NRQCD factorization for the input fragmentation functions, can reproduce more than 95% of the full NLO color-singlet contribution when p_T is only a few times the heavy quark mass.

## Method

The authors calculate the O(α_s^3) short-distance hard parts for all partonic scattering channels (quark-antiquark, quark-gluon, and gluon-gluon) that produce a heavy quark pair in various spin and color states, using the QCD collinear factorization formalism developed in Kang et al. (2014). They extract the hard parts by expanding the factorized partonic cross section order-by-order in α_s, subtracting the leading-power fragmentation contributions to remove mass singularities when the gluon propagator goes on-shell (p^2 → 0), and replacing the divergent gluon propagators with contact terms to obtain infrared-safe coefficient functions. The calculation is performed in the limit where the heavy quark mass is set to zero in the hard parts, requiring only axial-vector and vector spin states at O(α_s^3).

## Key numbers

- The O(α_s^3) short-distance coefficient functions are calculated for all partonic channels producing a heavy quark pair in all spin and color states employed in NRQCD treatments.
- The combination of LO perturbative QCD hard parts for producing a color-octet collinear heavy quark pair with LO fragmentation functions reproduces more than 95% of the full NLO contribution when p_T is only a few times the heavy quark mass.
- The input scale for fragmentation function evolution is μ_0 ∼ 2m_Q, where m_Q is the heavy quark mass, with the factorization scale μ_F ∼ p_T.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production developed by Kang et al. (2014), which includes both leading-power (LP) and next-to-leading-power (NLP) terms in the 1/p_T expansion. The approach addresses persistent challenges in explaining heavy quarkonium polarization data from the Tevatron and LHC, where NRQCD calculations at NLO have shown inconsistencies with CMS measurements of Υ(1S,2S,3S) polarization (Chatrchyan et al., 2012). The formalism enables a systematic reorganization of perturbative NRQCD calculations with resummation of large fragmentation logarithms, and provides a framework for a new global analysis of heavy quarkonium production data using the new evolution equations for heavy quarkonium fragmentation functions.
