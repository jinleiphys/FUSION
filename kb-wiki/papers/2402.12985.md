# Lüscher equation with long-range forces

> We derive the modified Lüscher equation in the presence of the long-range force caused by the exchange of a light particle. It is shown that the use of this equation enables one to circumvent the problems related to the strong partial-wave mixing and the t-channel sub-threshold singularities. It is also demonstrated that the present method is intrinsically linked to the so-called modified effective-range expansion (MERE) in the infinite volume. A detailed comparison with the two recently proposed alternative approaches is provided.

## Key claim

The paper derives a modified Lüscher equation that remains valid when long-range forces (such as those from light-particle exchange) are present, circumventing the problems of strong partial-wave mixing and t-channel sub-threshold singularities that plague the standard Lüscher approach, provided the long-range part of the potential is known and can be separated from the short-range part.

## Method

The authors reformulate the modified effective-range expansion (MERE) of van Haeringen and Kok (1981) within a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework, splitting the full potential into a known long-range part (e.g., a regularized Yukawa potential) and a short-range part parametrized as a low-energy polynomial. They then derive a finite-volume quantization condition by expressing the full T-matrix in terms of the long-range T-matrix and a short-range T-matrix dressed by the long-range Green function, leading to an algebraic relation between the finite-volume spectrum and the short-range parameters without needing to solve integral equations.

## Key numbers

- The t-channel cut in NN scattering runs from negative infinity to s = (2m_N)^2 - M_π^2, where m_N and M_π denote the nucleon and pion masses respectively.
- The regularization of the Yukawa potential requires 2ℓ_max + 1 subtraction terms to render it superregular for all partial waves up to ℓ_max.
- The S-wave amplitude B_0(q_0) takes the form |ψ̃_q(0)|^2 / [(C_0^00)^{-1} - ⟨G_L^0(q_0)⟩], where ψ̃_q(0) is the wave function at the origin for the long-range potential.
- The partial-wave Green function at the origin satisfies ⟨G_L^ℓ(q_0)⟩ = (1/[4π((2ℓ+1)!!)^2]) M_ℓ(q_0) plus a real polynomial in q_0^2.

## Context

This work extends the original Lüscher method (Lüscher, 1990) to systems with long-range forces, addressing problems identified in recent lattice studies of NN scattering (Meng et al., 2021) and the T_cc(3875)^+ state (Du et al., 2023). It provides an alternative to the approach of Raposo et al. (2023) that avoids solving integral equations, and is conceptually related to the three-body quantization condition frameworks (Hansen and Sharpe, 2014; Hammer et al., 2017). The method enables direct extraction of short-range interaction parameters from lattice energy levels without the partial-wave mixing issues that arise when the condition R/L ≪ 1 is violated.
