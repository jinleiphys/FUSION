# On the Relativistic Foundations of Pseudospin Symmetry in Nuclei

> We show that the generators of pseudospin symmetry are the non - relativistic limit of the generators of an SU(2) symmetry which leaves invariant the Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector potentials equal in magnitude but opposite in sign, $V_V = - V_S$. Furthermore, within this framework, we demonstrate that this symmetry may be approximately conserved for realistic scalar and vector potentials.

## Key claim

The generators of pseudospin symmetry in nuclei are the non-relativistic limit of the generators of an SU(2) symmetry that leaves the Dirac Hamiltonian invariant when the scalar and vector potentials are equal in magnitude but opposite in sign (V_V = -V_S); this symmetry may be approximately conserved for realistic scalar and vector potentials, even though exact pseudospin symmetry would eliminate bound Dirac valence states and thus prevent nuclei from existing.

## Method

The authors identify the SU(2) generators that commute with the Dirac Hamiltonian under the condition V_S = -V_V, then show that in the non-relativistic limit these generators reduce to the pseudospin operators obtained via the momentum-helicity unitary transformation U_p. They derive the relationship between the upper and lower Dirac components and demonstrate that when the lower components of a pseudospin doublet have nearly identical spatial wave functions (as observed in realistic mean-field calculations), the pseudospin generators approximately connect the upper components even when V_S ≈ -V_V rather than exactly equal.

## Key numbers

- The ratio (V_V - V_S)/2m ≈ 0.41 in the nuclear interior, meaning the spatial dependence of the effective mass cannot be neglected.
- The largest pseudo-orbital angular momentum for an occupied bound orbital in 208Pb is ℓ̃ = 4.
- For medium-energy proton scattering, the difference in scattering functions for pseudospin doublets is proportional to 2ℓ̃ + 1, similar to the energy splitting for bound orbitals.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Ginocchio (1997) that quasi-degenerate pseudospin doublets arise from the near equality V_S ≈ -V_V in relativistic mean fields, and the work by Draayer (1980s) showing that a microscopic normal-to-pseudo transformation reduces spin-orbit splitting. The paper identifies the specific SU(2) algebra (previously noted by Bell and others for the V_S = -V_V limit of the Dirac Hamiltonian) as the relativistic foundation of pseudospin symmetry. It enables a consistent treatment of pseudospin in both spherical and deformed nuclei, and suggests that conventional non-relativistic shell-model Hamiltonians should include a spatially-dependent effective mass and momentum-dependent spin term to be consistent with relativistic mean-field theory.
