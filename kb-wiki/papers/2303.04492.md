# An algebraic approach to intertwined quantum phase transitions in the Zr isotopes

> The algebraic framework of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing is employed to demonstrate the occurrence of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) in the $_{40}$Zr isotopes with neutron number 52-70. The detailed quantum and classical analyses reveal a QPT of crossing normal and intruder configurations superimposed on a QPT of the intruder configuration from U(5) to SU(3) and a crossover from SU(3) to SO(6) dynamical symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Zr isotopes (neutron numbers 52–70) exhibit intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), where a Type II QPT (crossing between normal and intruder configurations) occurs simultaneously with a Type I QPT (shape-phase transition within the intruder configuration from spherical U(5) to axially-deformed SU(3)) and a subsequent crossover from SU(3) to gamma-soft SO(6) dynamical symmetry.

## Method

The author employs the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using a Hamiltonian with separate terms for the normal (N boson) and intruder (N+2 boson) configurations plus a mixing term. The model space consists of [N] ⊕ [N+2] boson spaces with total boson number N = 1–8 for ⁹²⁻¹⁰⁶Zr and N̄ = 7̄,6̄ for ¹⁰⁸,¹¹⁰Zr. Quantum analysis is performed by calculating wave function probabilities in dynamical symmetry bases and order parameters (expectation values of the d-boson number operator), while classical analysis uses energy surfaces obtained from coherent (intrinsic) states.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R₄/₂ for the normal configuration (A) is approximately 1.6 and for the intruder configuration (B) is approximately 2.3 at neutron numbers 52–56.
- In ¹⁰⁴Zr, the intruder configuration shows E(2₁⁺) = 139.3 keV and R₄/₂ = 3.24, indicating strong deformation.
- In ¹⁰⁶Zr, E(2₂⁺) = 607.0 keV and E(4₁⁺) = 476.5 keV, with calculated values for ¹¹⁰Zr of E(4₁⁺) = 565 keV and E(2₂⁺) = 485 keV, signatures of SO(6) symmetry.
- The U(5) n_d=0 component probability in the 0⁺ state of configuration B is approximately 90% for neutron numbers 52–58 and drops to approximately 30% at neutron number 60.
- The SU(3) (λ,μ)=(2N+4,0) component probability in the 0⁺ state of configuration B reaches approximately 85% at neutron number 62 and approximately 92% at neutron number 64.
- The SO(6) σ=N+2 component probability in the 0⁺ state of configuration B becomes dominant at approximately 99% for neutron numbers 66–70.

## Context

This work extends the concept of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), previously introduced by Gavrielov (2019, 2022) for even-even nuclei and Gavrielov (2022c) for odd-mass nuclei, to the Zr isotopic chain. The analysis builds on the IBM-CM framework developed by Duval (1981, 1982) and earlier studies of Type II QPTs in the light Pb-Hg isotopes by Garcia-Ramos (2009, 2015). The results enable future investigation of IQPTs in other even-even and odd-mass isotopic chains in the Z≈40, A≈100 region and other physical systems.
