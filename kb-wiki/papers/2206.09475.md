# Search for possible alpha-condensate states in $^{20}$Ne

> The root mean square radii of $^{20}$Ne in the short-lived excited states were experimentally deduced for the first time from the analyses of $α$+$^{20}$Ne diffraction scattering. Differential cross sections of the elastic and inelastic $α$+$^{20}$Ne scattering in the incident energy range from a few MeV/nucleon up to 100 MeV/nucleon were analyzed by the modified diffraction model. No significant radius enhancement for the members of K$^π$ = 0$_{1}^{+}$ and K$^π$ = 2$^{-}$ bands in comparison with the ground state was observed. At the same time 20 % radius enhancement was obtained for the K$^π$ = 0$_{1}^{-}$ band members. Moreover, for the 0$_{2}^{+}$ state located above $α$-emission threshold increased radius was observed. This result can speak in favor of possible $α$-condensate structure of the 0$_{2}^{+}$ state and can be considered as a possible analog of the famous 7.65-MeV 0$_{2}^{+}$ Hoyle state of $^{12}$C.

# Search for possible alpha-condensate states in $^{20}$Ne

## Key claim
The 0₂⁺ state in ²⁰Ne at 6.73 MeV, located above the alpha-emission threshold, shows a 25% radius increase relative to the ground state, which may indicate a possible alpha-condensate structure analogous to the Hoyle state in ¹²C. However, this result is based on only four data points at a single energy and is described as an estimate requiring further analysis.

## Method
The authors applied the Modified Diffraction Model (MDM) to existing literature data on elastic and inelastic α + ²⁰Ne scattering at incident alpha energies from 33 to 386 MeV. The MDM extracts root-mean-square radii of excited states by comparing the diffraction radii (determined from the positions of minima and maxima in angular distributions) for inelastic and elastic scattering, using the known ground-state radius as a reference. The method was applied to states in the Kπ = 0₁⁺, Kπ = 2⁻, Kπ = 0₁⁻, and Kπ = 0₂⁺ rotational bands where angular distributions were available and the angular momentum transfer L ≤ 3.

## Key numbers
- Ground state rms radius of ²⁰Ne: R_rms⁰ = 2.87 ± 0.03 fm (taken from literature)
- Diffraction radius of the ground state decreases linearly from R_dif(0) = 6.00 ± 0.02 fm at E(α) = 33 MeV to R_dif(0) = 5.0 ± 0.1 fm at E(α) = 386 MeV
- Kπ = 0₁⁺ band, 1.63 MeV 2⁺ state: R_rms = 3.0 ± 0.2 fm (no significant enhancement relative to ground state)
- Kπ = 2⁻ band, 5.62 MeV 3⁻ state: R_rms = 2.9 ± 0.1 fm (no significant enhancement relative to ground state)
- Kπ = 0₁⁻ band, 5.79 MeV 1⁻ state: R_rms = 3.4 ± 0.2 fm (20% radius enhancement relative to ground state)
- Kπ = 0₁⁻ band, 7.17 MeV 3⁻ state: R_rms = 3.4 ± 0.2 fm (20% radius enhancement relative to ground state)
- Kπ = 0₂⁺ band, 6.73 MeV 0⁺ state: R_rms ∼ 3.6 fm (25% radius increase relative to ground state; estimate based on only 4 data points at a single energy)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous MDM studies of radii in excited states of ¹²C and ¹⁶O. For ¹²C, they found the Hoyle state (7.65 MeV 0₂⁺) has a radius approximately 1.25 times larger than the ground state, consistent with AMD calculations by Kanada-Enyo (2015) and NCSpM calculations, but smaller than the alpha-BEC model prediction of 4.31 fm. For ¹⁶O, the authors previously found no significant radius enhancement in the 0₄⁺, 0₅⁺, and 0₆⁺ states near the 4α threshold, contradicting the alpha-BEC model predictions of Funaki et al. (2010) for a "gigantic" radius in the 15.1-MeV 0₆⁺ state. The present results for ²⁰Ne are compared with AMD calculations by Kanada-Enyo and Ogata (2009), which predicted normal radii for Kπ = 0₁⁺ and Kπ = 2⁻ bands and increased radii for Kπ = 0₁⁻ band members. The 6.73 MeV 0₂⁺ state emerges as a candidate Hoyle-state analog, but the authors note that new experimental data are needed, and the hypothesis of Adachi et al. (2017) regarding 5α condensed states at higher excitation energies (21-24 MeV) could not be tested due to lack of angular distributions.
