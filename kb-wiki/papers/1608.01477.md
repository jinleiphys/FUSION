# Heteronuclear Efimov scenario with positive intraspecies scattering length

> We investigate theoretically and experimentally the heteronuclear Efimov scenario for a three-body system that consists of two bosons and one distinguishable particle with positive intraspecies scattering lengths. The three-body parameter at the three-body scattering threshold and the scaling factor between consecutive Efimov resonances are found to be controlled by the scattering length between the two bosons, approximately independent of short-range physics. We observe two excited-state Efimov resonances in the three-body recombination spectra of an ultracold mixture of fermionic $^6 $Li and bosonic $^{133} $Cs atoms close to a Li-Cs Feshbach resonance, where the Cs-Cs interaction is positive. Deviation of the obtained scaling factor of 4.0(3) from the universal prediction of 4.9 and the absence of the ground state Efimov resonance shed new light on the interpretation of the universality and the discrete scaling behavior of heteronuclear Efimov physics.

## Key claim

The three-body parameter (3BP) and scaling factor in a heteronuclear Efimov system with two bosons and one distinguishable particle are controlled by the intraspecies scattering length between the two bosons, approximately independent of short-range physics; however, the measured scaling factor of 4.0(3) deviates from the universal prediction of 4.9, and the ground-state Efimov resonance is absent, challenging the interpretation of universality and discrete scaling in heteronuclear Efimov physics.

## Method

The authors performed three-body recombination loss measurements on an ultracold mixture of fermionic ⁶Li and bosonic ¹³³Cs atoms confined in a bichromatic optical dipole trap at temperatures around 100 nK, near the 889 G Li-Cs Feshbach resonance where the Cs-Cs intraspecies scattering length is positive (≈+190 a₀). The three-body loss coefficient L₃ for Li-Cs-Cs collisions was extracted by fitting coupled rate equations to the time evolution of atom numbers, with background loss rates and pure Cs three-body loss coefficients determined in independent measurements. The experimental results were compared to a spinless van der Waals theory using single-channel Lennard-Jones potentials solved in the hyperspherical formalism, and to universal zero-range theory predictions.

## Key numbers

- Measured scaling factor between consecutive Efimov resonances: λ = 4.0(3), compared to the universal prediction of 4.9.
- First excited Efimov resonance position: a⁽¹⁾₋,ₗᵢ₋Cₛ = -2150(50) a₀.
- Second excited Efimov resonance position: a⁽²⁾₋,ₗᵢ₋Cₛ = -8500(500) a₀.
- Cs-Cs intraspecies scattering length at the 889 G Feshbach resonance: a_CsCs ≈ +190 a₀.
- van der Waals lengths: 45 a₀ for Li-Cs and 101 a₀ for Cs-Cs atom pairs.
- Temperature of the mixture: approximately 100 nK for each species, with specific measurements at 120 nK and 320 nK.
- Magnetic field stability: approximately 16 mG (one standard deviation).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heteronuclear Efimov physics in Li-Cs mixtures (Pires 2014, Tung 2014, Ulmanis 2015, Ulmanis 2016) and Li-Rb mixtures (Maier 2015) by investigating the case of positive intraspecies scattering length, which introduces a two-boson dimer channel that modifies the Efimov scenario. The results contradict the universal prediction of a constant scaling factor of 4.9 from theories assuming negligible intraspecies interactions (DIncao 2006, Braaten 2006, Helfrich 2010a), and instead suggest a period-dependent scaling factor. The findings enable future studies of atom-dimer features in the CsCs+Li channel and four-body resonances, and may help explain the breakdown of universal relationships in K-Rb mixtures (Bloom 2013, Helfrich 2010).
