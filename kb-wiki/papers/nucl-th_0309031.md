# Polarization observables in the $γd\toπNN$ reaction in the $Δ$(1232)-resonance region

> Incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ$(1232)-resonance region is investigated with special emphasis on polarization observables. For the elementary pion photoproduction operator an effective Lagrangian model which includes the standard pseudovector Born terms and a resonance contribution from the $Δ$(1232)-excitation is used. Our results for the elementary $γN\toπN$ reaction are in good agreement with recent experimental data and results of other theoretical calculations. A general analysis of all possible polarization observables for the $γd \toπNN$ reaction with polarized photon beam and/or oriented deuteron target is presented. The unpolarized differential cross section, photon asymmetry, vector and tensor target asymmetries are predicted for forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts polarization observables for the incoherent $\gamma d \to \pi NN$ reaction in the $\Delta$(1232)-resonance region using an impulse approximation that neglects final-state interactions and two-body production operators, with the caveat that these omitted effects will be reported in a forthcoming paper.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian model for the elementary $\gamma N \to \pi N$ amplitude, including pseudovector Born terms and a $\Delta$(1232)-resonance contribution, with parameters fixed by fitting the experimental $M_{1+}^{3/2}$ multipole. For the deuteron, they employed the Paris potential wave function and computed the $\gamma d \to \pi NN$ amplitude in a plane-wave impulse approximation, integrating over the angles of the relative momentum of the two outgoing nucleons to obtain three-fold differential cross sections and single-polarization observables.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy $\omega_{\gamma}^{\rm lab} = 330$ MeV was used for all calculations, corresponding to the $\Delta$(1232)-resonance region.
- The maximum pion momentum $q$ at forward emission angles is approximately 300 MeV, decreasing to approximately 200 MeV at backward emission angles.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was taken as $f^2_{\pi N}/4\pi = 0.069$.
- The numerical integrations over the angles of the relative momentum $p_{NN}$ were performed until the accuracy of calculated observables reached 1%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Darwish, 2003) on the energy dependence and cross sections of the $\gamma d \to \pi NN$ reaction, and the elementary model of Schmidt et al. (1996). It provides predictions for polarization observables that can guide experiments at facilities such as MAMI in Mainz, ELSA in Bonn, and JLab in Newport News, where no experimental data currently exist for these observables. The results enable future comparisons with more complete calculations that include final-state interactions and two-body effects.
