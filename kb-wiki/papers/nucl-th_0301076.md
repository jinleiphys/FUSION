# Effective field theory approach to electroweak transitions of nuclei far from stability

> In a previous paper, the convergence of the effective field theory approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang to the nuclear many-body problem was studied by applying it to selected doubly-magic, and neighboring single-particle and single-hole, nuclei far from stability. The success of that approach, interpreted through density functional theory, would imply reliable densities. In this paper, the single-particle (Kohn-Sham) wave functions are probed using weak transitions near the Fermi surface. The weak currents are the Noether currents derived from the effective Lagrangian. The general single-particle transition matrix elements, from which any semi-leptonic weak rate can be calculated, are obtained in terms of upper and lower components of the Dirac wave functions. Here beta-decays in nuclei neighboring 132-Sn are studied and compared with available experimental data. Calibration of the theoretical results for such decays may also have useful application in element formation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the single-particle (Kohn-Sham) wave functions derived from an effective field theory combined with density functional theory, when used with Noether currents from the same Lagrangian, can reproduce beta-decay rates for nuclei near doubly-magic 132Sn without additional parameter fitting, provided the binding energy and Fermi-surface single-particle energies are well reproduced.

## Method

The author solves the Kohn-Sham equations derived from the effective Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang to obtain Dirac spinor wave functions for nuclei neighboring 132Sn. The weak transition matrix elements are computed using a multipole expansion of the hadronic current, where the vector and axial-vector currents are the leading-order Noether currents from the same effective Lagrangian. The beta-decay rates are then calculated by integrating over phase space, including corrections for electron screening and the relativistic Fermi function accounting for nuclear size.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state to ground-state particle-particle beta transition from 133Sn to 133Sb agrees with experiment to within 5%
- For 133Sn, the calculated half-life from the R3 model (from Borzov et al.) gives a ratio of calculated to experimental half-life of 0.87, or a deviation of 13%, compared to the experimental value of 1450 ± 30 ms
- For 131In, the KHF model gives a deviation of 22% and the R3 model gives a deviation of 18% from the experimental half-life of 280 ± 30 ms
- The total binding energy of even-even Sn isotopes was reproduced below the 1% level, and chemical potentials for neighboring nuclei were reproduced below the 10% level

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory plus density functional theory approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (2001) from binding energies to weak transition rates, probing the single-particle wave functions near the Fermi surface. It contrasts with prior approaches such as the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) used by Staudt et al. (1990), Borzov et al. (1996), and Moeller et al., which typically fit parameters to beta-decay data and often neglect first-forbidden transitions. The paper enables a consistent framework where the same Lagrangian fitted to stable nuclei can predict both ground-state properties and beta-decay rates, with potential applications to element formation in astrophysical r-process calculations.
