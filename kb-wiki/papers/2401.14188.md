# Interactions of the Pseudoscalar Meson Octet and the Baryon Decuplet in the Continuum and a Finite Volume

> This study focuses on the interaction of the pseudoscalar meson octet and the baryon decuplet. In the continuum, it is observed that several $J^{P}=\frac32^-$ baryon resonances can be produced by the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in unitarized chiral perturbation theory, including the $N(1875)$, $Σ(1670)$, $Σ(1910)$, $Ξ(1820)$ and $Ω(2012)$. Among them, the $Ξ(1820)$ and $Σ(1670)$ may exhibit a potential two-pole structures. The unitarized chiral perturbation approach is then applied as the underlying theory to predict the energy levels of these systems in a finite volume. These energy levels are well described by the $K$-matrix parameterization constrained by flavor SU(3) symmetry. With the parameters from the best fits, the poles extracted from the $K$-matrix parameterization closely correspond to those derived from the underlying chiral effective field theory, as long as they are close to physical region and not significantly higher than the lowest relevant threshold.

## Key claim

The Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in unitarized chiral perturbation theory can dynamically generate several \(J^P = \frac32^-\) baryon resonances from pseudoscalar meson octet and baryon decuplet scattering, with the \(\Xi(1820)\) and \(\Sigma(1670)\) potentially exhibiting two-pole structures; however, the pole positions depend significantly on the subtraction constants used in the loop functions, and the \(K\)-matrix parameterization constrained by flavor SU(3) symmetry reproduces the underlying chiral effective field theory poles only when they are close to the physical region and not far above the lowest relevant threshold.

## Method

The authors used the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction from chiral perturbation theory for the pseudoscalar meson octet and baryon decuplet, solving the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation to find poles in the complex energy plane. They then computed finite-volume energy levels by replacing the dimensional-regularization loop function with a form-factor-regularized loop function and summing over discrete momenta in a cubic box. These energy levels were fitted using a \(K\)-matrix parameterization whose parameters were constrained by flavor SU(3) symmetry, reducing the number of free parameters by expressing the \(K\)-matrix as a sum over SU(3) representations.

## Key numbers

* In the continuum UChPT calculation with dimensional regularization, a pole at \(1387-77i\) MeV on RS \((-+)\) is found for \((S,I)=(0,\frac12)\), and a second pole at \(1883+12i\) MeV on RS \((+-)\) is associated with the \(N(1875)\) resonance (RPP pole at \(1900-80i\) MeV).
* For \((S,I)=(-1,1)\), a pole at \(1609-8i\) MeV on RS \((-+++)\) corresponds to the \(\Sigma(1670)\) (RPP pole at \(1665-28i\) MeV), and a broader pole at \(1664-194i\) MeV on the same RS suggests a two-pole structure.
* For \((S,I)=(-2,\frac12)\), a pole at \(1806-16i\) MeV on RS \((-+++)\) corresponds to the \(\Xi(1820)\) (RPP pole at \(1823-12i\) MeV), with a second broader pole at \(1818-170i\) MeV on the same RS indicating a possible two-pole structure.
* For \((S,I)=(-3,0)\), a bound state at \(2016\) MeV on RS \((++)\) is consistent with the \(\Omega(2012)\) (RPP pole at \(2012-3i\) MeV).
* Using the form-factor-regularized loop function (cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.2\) GeV), the UChPT pole for \((S,I)=(0,\frac12)\) on RS \((++)\) shifts to \(1347\) MeV, and the \(K\)-matrix fit to finite-volume levels yields a pole at \(1343\) MeV on the same RS.

## Context

This work extends the earlier UChPT study of pseudoscalar meson–baryon decuplet interactions by Sarkar (2004), using slightly different subtraction constants for the loop functions and comparing the dynamically generated poles to the resonances listed in the Review of Particle Physics (2022). It also applies the SU(3)-constrained \(K\)-matrix parameterization method developed by Asokan (2022) for the \(D_0^*(2300)\) system to the baryon decuplet sector, demonstrating that this approach can describe finite-volume energy levels and reproduce pole positions from the underlying chiral effective field theory. The results provide guidance for future lattice QCD studies of these \(\frac32^-\) baryon resonances.
