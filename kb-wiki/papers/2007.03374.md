# Spectral functions and dynamic critical behavior of relativistic $Z_2$ theories

> We investigate the dynamic critical behaviour of a relativistic scalar field theory with $Z_2$ symmetry by calculating spectral functions of the order parameter at zero and non-vanishing momenta from first-principles classical-statistical lattice simulations in real-time. We find that at temperatures above the critical point $(T > T_c)$, the spectral functions are well described by relativistic quasi-particle peaks. Close to the transition temperature $(T \sim T_c)$, we observe strong infrared contributions building up. In the ordered phase at low temperatures $(T < T_c)$, in addition to the quasi-particle peak, we observe a soft mode with a dispersion relation indicative of collective excitations. Investigating the spectral functions close to $T_c$, we demonstrate that the behavior in the vicinity of the critical point is controlled by dynamic scaling functions and the dynamic critical exponent $z$, which we determine from our simulations. By considering the equations of motion for a closed system and a system coupled to a heat bath, we extract the dynamic critical behavior for two different dynamic universality classes (Models A & C) in two and three spatial dimensions.

## Key claim

The dynamic critical behavior of relativistic $Z_2$ scalar field theory is governed by the dynamic critical exponent $z$, which the authors extract from classical-statistical lattice simulations for two different dynamic universality classes (Models A and C) in two and three spatial dimensions, with the caveat that these relaxational models do not include the conserved vector field required to describe the QCD critical endpoint (Model H).

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical lattice simulations in real-time, solving Langevin-type equations of motion for a scalar field with $Z_2$ symmetry on a spatial lattice. They compute spectral functions of the order parameter from unequal-time correlation functions using the classical fluctuation-dissipation relation (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger condition), considering both isolated Hamiltonian systems (Model C, $\gamma=0$) and systems coupled to a heat bath (Model A, $\gamma>0$) in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature $T_c = 4.4629(10)$ in 2D and $T_c = 9.37074(28)$ in 3D (lattice units, $m^2=-1$, $\lambda=1$)
- Asymptotic amplitude of the correlation length above $T_c$: $\xi^+ = 0.9176(76)$ in 2D and $\xi^+ = 0.918(27)$ in 3D
- Asymptotic amplitude of the correlation length below $T_c$: $\xi^- = 3.14(38) \times \xi^+$ in 2D and $\xi^- = 1.939(90) \times \xi^+$ in 3D (from amplitude ratio $U_\xi$)
- Amplitude ratio $R_\chi = 6.40(10)$ in 2D and $R_\chi = 1.331(80)$ in 3D (measured vs. literature values 6.778 and 1.660(4) respectively)
- Amplitude ratio $\delta = 14.26(19)$ in 2D and $\delta = 4.82(15)$ in 3D (measured vs. literature values 15 and 4.78984(1) respectively)

## Context

This work extends precursor studies by Berges et al. (2010) that calculated the dynamic critical exponent for scalar field theory in two spatial dimensions with conservative dynamics (Model C), now systematically comparing results in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions and extracting universal scaling functions at finite spatial momenta. The authors note that while the QCD critical endpoint is believed to belong to Model H (Son, 2004), studying the simpler relaxational Models A and C provides a first step toward characterizing real-time dynamics near a critical point, with the methodology ready to be extended to more complicated models relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, GSI-FAIR, and NICA facilities.
