# Collective Dipole Bremsstrahlung in Fusion Reactions

> We estimate the dipole radiation emitted in fusion processes. We show that a classical bremsstrahlung approach can account for both the preequilibrium and the thermal photon emission. We give an absolute evaluation of the pre-equilibrium component due to the charge asymmetry in the entrance channel and we study the energy and mass dependence in order to optimize the observation. This dynamical dipole radiation could be a relevant cooling mechanism in the fusion path. We stress the interest in experiments with the new available radioactive beams.

# Collective Dipole Bremsstrahlung in Fusion Reactions

## Key claim

The authors show that a classical bremsstrahlung approach can account for both pre-equilibrium and thermal photon emission in fusion reactions, and they provide an absolute evaluation of the pre-equilibrium component without any normalization or adjusted parameters. The pre-equilibrium dipole radiation due to charge asymmetry in the entrance channel is found to be systematically about two times larger than predictions from the phonon model used previously, and can be comparable to the statistical compound nucleus emission.

## Method

The authors use a classical bremsstrahlung formula based on the Fourier transform of the dipole acceleration, obtained from semiclassical BNV (Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov) transport simulations of central fusion collisions. They follow the time evolution of the collective dipole mode from the dinuclear phase through compound nucleus formation, computing the photon emission probability directly from the acceleration power spectrum. For the thermal component, they recover the standard statistical GDR decay formula by applying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to the same bremsstrahlung expression.

## Key numbers

- For the system $^{40}$Ca + $^{100}$Mo at 4 AMeV, the pre-equilibrium bremsstrahlung spectrum and the first-step statistical spectrum are comparable in magnitude.
- For $^{16}$O + $^{98}$Mo at beam energies of 4, 8, 14, and 20 AMeV, the pre-equilibrium contribution shows a characteristic "rise and fall" behavior with an optimum effect around 8-14 AMeV.
- The ratio of total pre-equilibrium to total statistical GDR gamma-ray emission for $^{16}$O + $^{98}$Mo reaches approximately 16% enhancement at the optimum beam energy, in agreement with the experimental estimate of Flibotte (1996).
- The pre-equilibrium extra photon multiplicity from the bremsstrahlung approach is systematically about two times larger than predictions from the phonon model for the same systems and energies.

## Context

This work extends the phonon approach of Chomaz (1993) and Brink (1990), which assumed a thermally equilibrated compound nucleus, by directly calculating the pre-equilibrium dipole radiation during the dinuclear phase before thermal equilibrium is reached. The bremsstrahlung method provides absolute emission probabilities without the uncertainties in estimating the number of GDR phonons at compound nucleus formation time that limited the phonon model. The results enable experimental optimization of pre-equilibrium dipole radiation observations, particularly with radioactive beams, and suggest this mechanism could serve as a cooling mechanism in warm fusion reactions for synthesizing heavy elements.
