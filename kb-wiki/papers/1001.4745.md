# Cluster properties and particle production in Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics

> I outline the construction of exactly Poincaré invariant quantum models that satisfy cluster separability but do not conserve particle number.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a strategy to construct exactly Poincaré invariant quantum models that satisfy cluster separability and allow particle-number-changing interactions, using physical-particle degrees of freedom and an ordered sequence of effective theories with successively more degrees of freedom relevant over different energy scales. The hedge is that this construction is demonstrated for the specific case of nucleon-nucleon scattering between the one- and two-pion-production thresholds, and the author notes that "much of the work done in constructing realistic few-body interactions needs to be repeated in this setting."

## Method

The model Hilbert space is built as a direct sum of tensor products of single physical-particle Hilbert spaces using Sokolov's doublet formalism, where each single-particle space is augmented with a zero-dimensional no-particle space. Dynamics are introduced via the generalized Bakamjian-Thomas construction, adding interactions to the non-interacting mass operator in a form that preserves Poincaré invariance. Cluster properties are enforced algebraically by requiring that when interactions between separated clusters are turned off, the dynamical unitary representation becomes a tensor product of subsystem representations, which is achieved through unitary transformations \(W\) that relate Bakamjian-Thomas representations to tensor product representations.

## Key numbers

- The model is constructed for invariant energies between \(2m_N + m_\pi\) and \(2m_N + 2m_\pi\), where \(m_N\) is the nucleon mass and \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
- The model Hilbert space for baryon number two contains six orthogonal sectors: \({\cal H}_{N_1N_2}\), \({\cal H}_{N_1N_2\pi_1}\), \({\cal H}_{N_1N_2\pi_2}\), \({\cal H}_{D\pi_1}\), \({\cal H}_{D\pi_2}\), and \({\cal H}_{D\pi_1\pi_2}\).
- The non-interacting invariant mass operator for the coupled \(NN\leftrightarrow D\pi\) channel has the form \(M_0 = \text{diag}(2\sqrt{k_{NN}^2+m_N^2}, \sqrt{k_{D\pi}^2+m_D^2}+\sqrt{k_{D\pi}^2+m_\pi^2})\).

## Context

This work extends the successful low-energy few-body program, which uses cluster properties to relate few- and many-body Hamiltonians, to energy scales above the pion-production threshold where Poincaré invariance becomes essential. It addresses difficulties noted by Sokolov (1970s) and earlier work on cluster properties in Poincaré invariant models with fixed particle number, and builds on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (1960s) for relativistic quantum mechanics. The approach enables the solution of relativistic Faddeev equations at the GeV scale, as pursued by Cederwall et al. (2000s), and provides a framework for systematically incorporating sub-nucleon degrees of freedom into few-body calculations.
