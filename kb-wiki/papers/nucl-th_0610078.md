# Real-time Skyrme TDHF dynamics of giant resonances

> Nuclear dynamics of giant resonances are investigated with the real-time Skyrme TDHF method. The TDHF equation is explicitly linearized with respect to variation of single-particle wave functions. The time evolution of transition densities are calculated for giant dipole resonances. The time-dependent densities of protons and neutrons suggest that the dynamics of giant dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei are significantly different from that in stable nuclei with N=Z.

## Key claim

The real-time Skyrme TDHF dynamics of giant dipole resonances in neutron-rich nuclei (such as ¹⁴Be) show a significant violation of the Steinwedel-Jensen model assumption that neutron and proton transition densities are opposite (δρₙ = −δρₚ); instead, weakly bound neutrons oscillate in phase with protons due to the dynamical self-consistent potential, a feature not present in stable N≈Z nuclei like ⁸Be.

## Method

The authors linearize the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation with respect to variations of single-particle wave functions and apply an instantaneous or Gaussian-pulse external dipole field to excite giant dipole resonances in ⁸Be and ¹⁴Be. They solve the linearized TDHF equation in real time on a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate grid using the Skyrme SGII parameter set, with an absorbing boundary condition outside the interaction region to handle the single-particle continuum. The time evolution of the spin-independent transition densities δρₚ(𝐫;t) and δρₙ(𝐫;t) is then computed and analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The main GDR peak studied is at Eₓ = 15 MeV with K=0 for both ⁸Be and ¹⁴Be.
- The time difference between snapshots of transition densities is Δt = 0.2 ℏ/MeV, roughly corresponding to half the period π/ω.
- The Gaussian-pulse external field uses γ = 3 MeV/ℏ and t₀ = 2 ℏ/MeV.
- A significant low-energy E1 strength around Eₓ = 5 MeV was found for ¹⁴Be in prior work (Nakatsukasa and Yabana 2005), but its peak position is sensitive to the Skyrme parameter choice.
- The ground-state quadrupole deformation is β₂ ≈ 0.8 for both ⁸Be and ¹⁴Be.

## Context

This work extends the real-time TDHF method with absorbing boundary conditions (TDHF+ABC) previously developed by Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2001, 2005) for linear response calculations, which itself is an extension of continuum RPA to deformed systems. The results contradict the Steinwedel-Jensen (1950) hydrodynamical model assumption of incompressible isovector motion (δρₚ + δρₙ = 0) for neutron-rich nuclei, while the Goldhaber-Teller (1948) model and its A⁻¹/⁶ frequency dependence are more relevant for light stable nuclei. The findings enable future microscopic studies of giant resonances in unstable nuclei where quantum effects and the dynamical self-consistent potential play a crucial role beyond semiclassical hydrodynamical models.
