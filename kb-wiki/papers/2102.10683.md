# Differential analysis of incompressibility in neutron-rich nuclei

> Both the incompressibility \Ka of a finite nucleus of mass A and that ($K_{\infty}$) of infinite nuclear matter are fundamentally important for many critical issues in nuclear physics and astrophysics. While some consensus has been reached about the $K_{\infty}$, accurate theoretical predictions and experimental extractions of $K_τ$ characterizing the isospin dependence of \Ka have been very difficult. We propose a differential approach to extract the \Kt and \Ki independently from the \Ka data of any two nuclei in a given isotope chain. Applying this new method to the \Ka data from isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) in even-even Pb, Sn, Cd and Ca isotopes taken by U. Garg {\it et al.} at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP), Osaka University, Japan, we find that the $^{106}$Cd-$^{116}$Cd and $^{112}$Sn-$^{124}$Sn pairs having the largest differences in isospin asymmetries in their respective isotope chains measured so far provide consistently the most accurate up-to-date \Kt value of $K_τ=-616\pm 59$ MeV and $K_τ=-623\pm 86$ MeV, respectively, largely independent of the remaining uncertainties of the surface and Coulomb terms in expanding the $K_{\rm A}$, while the $K_{\infty}$ values extracted from different isotopes chains are all well within the current uncertainty range of the community consensus for $K_{\infty}$. Moreover, the size and origin of the "Soft Sn Puzzle" is studied with respect to the "Stiff Pb Phenomenon". It is found that the latter is favored due to a much larger (by $\sim 380$ MeV) \Kt for Pb isotopes than for Sn isotopes, while the \Ki from analyzing the \Ka data of Sn isotopes is only about 5 MeV less than that from analyzing the Pb data.

## Key claim

The authors propose a differential approach to extract the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_{\infty}\) and the isospin-dependent incompressibility \(K_{\tau}\) independently from the incompressibility data of any two nuclei in a given isotope chain, finding that the most accurate results come from pairs with the largest difference in isospin asymmetry, and that the "Soft Sn Puzzle" is better understood as a "Stiff Pb Phenomenon" due to a much larger \(K_{\tau}\) for Pb isotopes.

## Method

The authors derived exact analytical expressions for \(K_{\tau}\) and \(K_{\infty}\) by solving two linear equations based on the leptodermous expansion of the finite-nucleus incompressibility \(K_A\) for any two nuclei in the same isotope chain. They applied this differential method to the experimental \(K_A\) data from isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) in even-even Pb, Sn, Cd, and Ca isotopes measured at RCNP, using the known most probable values for the surface parameter \(c = -1.2\) and Coulomb parameter \(K_{\text{Cou}} = -5.2\) MeV. The uncertainties were propagated from the experimental errors of the \(K_A\) data, and the effects of varying the surface and Coulomb parameters by \(\pm 20\%\) were examined.

## Key numbers

- \(K_{\tau} = -616 \pm 59\) MeV from the \(^{106}\)Cd-\(^{116}\)Cd pair (largest isospin asymmetry difference in the Cd chain).
- \(K_{\tau} = -623 \pm 86\) MeV from the \(^{112}\)Sn-\(^{124}\)Sn pair (largest isospin asymmetry difference in the Sn chain).
- \(K_{\infty} = 213 \pm 2\) MeV from the \(^{106}\)Cd-\(^{116}\)Cd pair.
- \(K_{\infty} = 220 \pm 3\) MeV from the \(^{112}\)Sn-\(^{124}\)Sn pair.
- \(K_{\tau} = -756 \pm 149\) MeV from the \(^{40-48}\)Ca pair (highest isospin separation of 0.167 among all data).
- The mean \(K_{\infty}\) from Sn isotopes (218 MeV) is only about 5 MeV below that from Pb isotopes (223 MeV).
- The mean \(K_{\tau}\) from Sn isotopes (-626 MeV) is significantly below that from Pb isotopes (-245 MeV), a difference of about 380 MeV.
- Varying the surface parameter \(c\) by 40% changed the extracted \(K_{\tau}\) by about 6% (from \(-624 \pm 84\) to \(-663 \pm 87\) MeV) for the \(^{112}\)Sn-\(^{124}\)Sn pair.
- Varying the Coulomb parameter \(K_{\text{Cou}}\) by 40% changed the extracted \(K_{\tau}\) by about 8% (from \(-616 \pm 87\) to \(-669 \pm 87\) MeV) for the same pair.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to determine the incompressibility of nuclear matter, following the pioneering work of Blaizot (1980) who determined \(K_{\infty} = 210 \pm 30\) MeV, and the community consensus that \(K_{\infty}\) is in the range of 220 to 260 MeV (Garg and Colo, 2018; Shlomo, 2006). The differential approach directly addresses the unsatisfactory state of extracting \(K_{\tau}\), which previously relied on integral \(\chi^2\) fitting methods that yielded large uncertainties (e.g., \(K_{\tau} = -550 \pm 100\) MeV from Sn isotopes, or \(K_{\tau} = -595 \pm 177\) MeV from Stone (2014)). The method enables more accurate extraction of \(K_{\tau}\) and clarifies the "Soft Sn Puzzle" versus "Stiff Pb Phenomenon" debate, suggesting that future experiments on more Pb isotopes at rare isotope beam facilities will be valuable for further verification.
