## Key claim
The $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ baryon can be described as a $D^*N$ molecular state with quantum numbers $J^P = 3/2^-$ and isospin $I=0$, yielding a mass and total width consistent with experimental data; a bottom-sector partner $\bar B^*N$ is predicted at 6248 MeV/$c^2$ with a width of 7.5 MeV.

## Method
The authors use a constituent quark model incorporating chiral symmetry breaking (pseudoscalar and scalar Goldstone boson exchange), one-gluon exchange, and a screened confinement potential. The $D^*N$ interaction is derived from the underlying $q\bar q$ interaction via the Resonating Group Method, and coupled-channel equations (including $D^*N$ and $\Sigma_c\pi$ channels) are solved using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to find bound-state poles. Decay widths are computed from the molecular wave function and transition potentials to final states.

## Context
This work extends prior molecular interpretations of the $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ by He et al. (who found a $J^P=1/2^-$ state but could not compute absolute decay widths) and Dong et al. (who excluded $J^P=1/2^-$ and found $J^P=1/2^+$ widths too small). It also contrasts with quark-model assignments by Isgur (2000) (a $5/2^-$ state at 2900 MeV/$c^2$) and Ebert et al. (a radial $3/2^+$ $\Sigma_c$ excitation), which are incompatible with observed decay modes. The prediction of a $\Lambda_b(6248)$ molecule provides a specific target for LHCb searches and suggests that analogous molecular states exist in the bottom sector with larger binding energy.
