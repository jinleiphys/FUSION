# Collider constraints on axion-like particles

> The current status and future prospects of searches for axion-like particles (ALPs) at colliders, mostly focused on the CERN LHC, are summarized. Constraints on ALPs with masses above a few GeV that couple to photons, as well as to Z or Higgs bosons, have been set at the LHC through searches for new $a\toγγ$ resonances in di-, tri-, and four-photon final states. Inclusive and exclusive diphotons in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions, pp, PbPb $\to a \to γγ(+X)$, as well as exotic Z and Higgs boson decays, pp $\to \mathrm{Z},\mathrm{H}\to aγ\to 3γ$ and pp $\to \mathrm{H}\to aa \to 4γ$, have been analyzed. Exclusive searches in PbPb collisions provide the best exclusion limits for ALP masses $m_a\approx 5-$100 GeV, whereas the other channels are the most competitive ones over $m_a\approx 100$ GeV$-$2.6 TeV. Integrated ALP production cross sections up to $\sim$100 nb are excluded at 95% confidence level, corresponding to constraints on axion-photon couplings down to $g_{aγ}\approx$ 0.05 TeV$^{-1}$, over broad mass ranges. Factors of 10$-$100 improvements in these limits are expected at the LHC approaching $g_{aγ}\approx 10^{-3}$ TeV$^{-1}$ over $m_a\approx 1$ GeV$-$5 TeV in the next decade.

# Collider constraints on axion-like particles

## Key claim

LHC searches for axion-like particles (ALPs) decaying to diphotons have extended the excluded parameter space by about one order of magnitude in both mass (up to 2.6 TeV) and coupling (down to 0.05 TeV⁻¹) compared to pre-LHC limits, but the bounds depend on the specific production channel and assume a 100% branching ratio of ALPs to photons, with the most stringent limits coming from exclusive PbPb collisions for masses below 100 GeV and from inclusive pp collisions for higher masses.

## Method

The analysis combines reinterpretations of existing LHC diphoton resonance searches by ATLAS and CMS, as well as dedicated exclusive diphoton measurements in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, using the equivalent photon approximation to model ALP production via photon fusion. For inclusive searches, ALP production via vector-boson-fusion in pp collisions at √s = 7, 8, and 13 TeV is considered, with limits derived from spin-0 resonance searches in diphoton final states over mγγ ≈ 10 GeV–2.6 TeV. For exotic Z and Higgs boson decays, the analysis recasts ATLAS and CMS measurements of triphoton and four-photon final states into ALP constraints under specific assumptions about the relationship between the effective ALP-photon and ALP-Z couplings.

## Key numbers

- ALP masses from m_a ≈ 5–100 GeV are excluded in exclusive PbPb collisions, with the lowest mass probed at the LHC being m_a = 5–6 GeV from CMS
- ALP masses up to m_a ≈ 2.6 TeV are excluded from inclusive pp diphoton searches at √s = 13 TeV
- Integrated ALP production cross sections up to ∼100 nb are excluded at 95% confidence level
- Constraints on the axion-photon coupling reach down to g_aγ ≈ 0.05 TeV⁻¹ over broad mass ranges
- The ATLAS measurement of BR(Z → 3γ) < 2.2 × 10⁻⁶ at 95% CL improves the previous LEP limit by a factor of five
- Exclusive diphoton searches in PbPb collisions exclude ALP cross sections from 2 to 70 nb at 95% CL over m_a = 6–100 GeV
- Future LHC improvements of factors 10–100 are expected, approaching g_aγ ≈ 10⁻³ TeV⁻¹ over m_a ≈ 1 GeV–5 TeV

## Context

This work summarizes the current LHC constraints on ALPs with masses above a few GeV, extending the pre-LHC limits from LEP (which covered m_a up to about 100 GeV) and the Tevatron (up to about 300 GeV) by roughly one order of magnitude in both mass and coupling. The results build on earlier phenomenological proposals by Knapen (2016) and Bauer (2017) for ALP searches via photon fusion at colliders, and on the experimental searches for light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral collisions. The LHC constraints now bridge the gap between low-energy beam-dump and astrophysical bounds at masses below about 1 GeV and the previously unexplored region above 300 GeV, enabling future searches to probe couplings down to g_aγ ≈ 10⁻³ TeV⁻¹ over the next decade.
