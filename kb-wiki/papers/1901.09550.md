# Folding model analysis of $ ^{12}C- ^{12}C $ and $ ^{16}O- ^{16}O $ elastic scattering using the density-dependent LOCV averaged effective interaction

> The averaged effective two-body interaction (\textit{AEI}) which can be generated through the lowest order constrained variational (\textit{LOCV}) method for symmetric nuclear matter (\textit{SNM}) with the input \textit{Reid}68 nucleon-nucleon potential, is used as the effective nucleon-nucleon potential in the folding model to describe the heavy-ion (\textit{HI}) elastic scattering cross sections. The elastic scattering cross sections of $^{12}C-^{12}C$ and $^{16}O-^{16}O$ systems are calculated in the above frameworks. The results are compared with the corresponding calculations coming from the fitting procedures with the input finite range \textit{DDM3Y1-Reid} potential and the available experimental data at different incident energies. It is shown that a reasonable description of the elastic $^{12}C-^{12}C$ and $^{16}O-^{16}O$ scattering data at the low and the medium energies can be obtained by using the above \textit{ LOCV AEI}, without any need to define a parameterize density dependent function in the effective nucleon-nucleon potential, which is formally considered in the typical \textit{DDM3Y1-Reid} interactions.

# Folding model analysis of 12C-12C and 16O-16O elastic scattering using the density-dependent LOCV averaged effective interaction

## Key claim
The LOCV averaged effective interaction (AEI), derived from a microscopic many-body calculation with no free parameters, can provide a reasonable description of heavy-ion elastic scattering data at low and medium energies without needing to define a parameterized density-dependent function in the effective nucleon-nucleon potential, unlike the typical DDM3Y1-Reid interactions. However, the results are not as satisfactory as those from DDM3Y1-Reid, especially at forward angles, though agreement improves with increasing incident energy.

## Method
The authors used the double-folding model with the LOCV AEI, generated from symmetric nuclear matter calculations using the Reid68 nucleon-nucleon potential, as the effective interaction input. They calculated the direct and exchange parts of the real heavy-ion optical potential for 12C-12C and 16O-16O systems, then used the FRESCO code with a Woods-Saxon imaginary potential to compute elastic scattering differential cross sections. An iterative self-consistency procedure was applied for the exchange potential, requiring 150-200 iterations at small internuclear distances compared to about 20 for M3Y interactions.

## Key numbers
- For 12C-12C: incident energies of 112, 126.7, 240, 300, and 360 MeV were analyzed; at 300 MeV the exchange potential calculation required about 50 hours of CPU time on a high-performance computing machine
- For 16O-16O: incident energies of 124, 145, 250, 350, and 480 MeV were analyzed
- The energy-dependent factor used was g(E) = [1 - k(E/A)] with k = 0.003 MeV^{-1} for the LOCV AEI
- The renormalization coefficient N_R was found to be close to unity for both systems at all energies
- The density-dependent factor used the form F(ρ) = C(1 + α exp(-βρ)) with parameters given in table 2 of the paper

## Context
This work extends the application of the LOCV averaged effective interaction, previously tested on light and heavy closed-shell nuclei (Rahmat and Modarres, 2018) and used for in-medium nn cross sections and transport properties, to heavy-ion elastic scattering. It contrasts with the semi-phenomenological DDM3Y1-Reid interaction (Khoa et al., 2000) which requires an externally added density-dependent factor fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties. The results enable future investigations including three-body force effects and surface imaginary potential terms to improve agreement with experimental data at large angles.
