# A statistical model to calculate inclusive hadronic cross sections

> Hadronic cross sections are important ingredients in many of the ongoing research methods in high-energy nuclear physics, and it is always important to measure and/or calculate the probabilities of different types of reactions. In heavy-ion transport simulations at a few GeV energies, these hadronic cross sections are essential and so far mostly the exclusive processes are used, however, if one interested in total production rates the inclusive cross sections are also necessary to know. In this paper, we introduce a statistical-based method, which is able to give good estimates to exclusive and inclusive cross sections as well in the energy range of a few GeV. The method and its estimates for not well-known cross sections, will be used in a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) type off-shell transport code to explain charmonium and bottomonium mass shifts in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a statistical bootstrap-based method that can estimate both exclusive and inclusive hadronic cross sections in the few-GeV energy range, with the explicit condition that the model is not suited to describe elastic two-body reactions and cannot describe reactions of the type A+B → C, requiring a separate Breit-Wigner contribution for such channels.

## Method

The method assumes that a collision forms a strongly interacting compound system (fireball) that decays into hadrons, separating the reaction probability into an initial fireball formation stage described by the inelastic cross section and a hadronization stage described by mixed statistical and dynamical factors. The fireball can decay directly into two or three hadrons, or split into subsequent fireballs with smaller masses, with the probability of each decay scheme calculated using the statistical bootstrap approach including quark combinatorial factors, phase-space integrals, and Breit-Wigner weighting for unstable particles. Inclusive cross sections are obtained by taking ratios of normalization sums for one-fireball or two-fireball channels, where the inclusive sum contains all possible final states with a specific fixed particle present.

## Key numbers

- The probabilities for a fireball decaying into two or three particles are P2_d = 0.69 and P3_d = 0.24, taken from Frautschi's original statistical bootstrap formulation.
- The density of states parameters include E0 = 500 MeV (fitted to experimentally measured DOS) and T0 = 160 MeV (set using calculated and measured cross section ratios).
- The quark creational probabilities for the three lightest quarks in the energy range E = 2-8 GeV are fitted as Pu = Pd = 0.425 and Ps = 0.15.
- The interaction volume corresponds to an interaction radius of r = 0.5 fm.
- The estimated relative error of the model is approximately ΔW1/W1 ≈ 0.4, calculated from the mean of the relative error distribution using four test processes (p pbar → π+ π-, p pbar → n nbar, p pbar → Δ++ Δ--, p pbar → K+ K-).

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap approach previously developed by Balassa (2017) for calculating exclusive cross section ratios to now include inclusive cross sections, which are necessary for heavy-ion transport simulations using Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) type off-shell codes. The method is intended to provide estimates for charmonium and bottomonium production cross sections in the 2-10 GeV energy range, where experimental data are scarce, to enable calculations of mass shifts in dense matter that could indicate a non-zero value for the gluon condensate at specific nuclear densities, building on calculations by M1, M2, and M3. The approach contrasts with previous transport simulations that mostly used only 2→2 exclusive reactions, with some attempts by Batko (1992) and Cassing (2002) to include N→M reactions.
