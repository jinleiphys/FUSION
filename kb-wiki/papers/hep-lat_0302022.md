# SU(2) Landau gluon propagator on a 140^3 lattice

> We present a numerical study of the gluon propagator in lattice Landau gauge for three-dimensional pure-SU(2) lattice gauge theory at couplings beta = 4.2, 5.0, 6.0 and for lattice volumes V = 40^3, 80^3, 140^3. In the limit of large V we observe a decreasing gluon propagator for momenta smaller than p_{dec} = 350^{+ 100}_{- 50} MeV. Data are well fitted by Gribov-like formulae and seem to indicate an infra-red critical exponent kappa slightly above 0.6, in agreement with recent analytic results.

## Key claim

The gluon propagator in three-dimensional pure-SU(2) lattice Landau gauge decreases for momenta below approximately 350 MeV in the infinite-volume limit, with data consistent with Gribov-like forms and an infrared critical exponent slightly above 0.6; however, the authors note that the exponent values obtained depend on the fitting function and that remaining discretization and finite-volume effects prevent a definitive conclusion on whether the propagator vanishes exactly at zero momentum.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of three-dimensional pure-SU(2) lattice gauge theory with the standard Wilson action at couplings beta = 4.2, 5.0, 6.0 on lattice volumes V = 40^3, 80^3, and 140^3. They used a hybrid overrelaxed algorithm for thermalization and stochastic overrelaxation (or the Cornell method) for gauge fixing to Landau gauge, then computed the gluon propagator D(k) for momenta with only one non-zero component and applied a matching technique to compare data at different beta values.

## Key numbers

* The gluon propagator decreases for momenta smaller than p_dec = 350^{+100}_{-50} MeV in the large-volume limit.
* Using the Gribov-like fitting form D(p) = (s + z p^{2alpha}) / (y^2 + p^{2(1+gamma)}) on V = 140^3 data, the infrared exponent alpha is 0.27(6) at beta = 4.2, 0.29(7) at beta = 5.0, and 0.38(8) at beta = 6.0, corresponding to kappa = (1+alpha)/2 approximately 0.65.
* The smallest non-zero momentum accessible is 59 MeV, and the physical lattice sides reach almost 25 fm.
* The inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} was set using the string tension sqrt(sigma) = 0.44 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies of the gluon propagator in three-dimensional SU(2) Landau gauge by Cucchieri (1999) and Bonnet (2001) to larger lattice volumes (up to 140^3) and multiple couplings in the scaling region, confirming the turnover in the infrared. The results are compared to analytic predictions from Dyson-Schwinger equations by Zwanziger (2001) and to the Gribov (1977) scenario of a propagator with purely imaginary poles. The study enables future work on larger lattices (up to 260^3) to determine whether the zero-momentum propagator vanishes and to extract the continuum-limit infrared exponent.
