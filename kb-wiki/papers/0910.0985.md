# Transverse hydrodynamics with sudden isotropization and freeze-out

> We assume that the early evolution of matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is described by the transverse hydrodynamics. In this approach only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized, while the longitudinal motion is described by free streaming. When the energy density of the system drops to a certain value, the system becomes isotropic (locally, in the momentum space) and freezes out. The sudden isotropization transition is described with the help of the Landau matching conditions, and the freeze-out is modeled with THERMINATOR. Within this scenario one is able to reproduce in the quite satisfactory way the transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic flow coefficient v2, and the HBT radii of pions and kaons studied at RHIC (Au+Au collisions at the top beam energy). The obtained results indicate that the system produced at RHIC does not have to be fully thermalized in the early stage. On the other hand, the final three-dimensional thermalization is necessary to reproduce the HBT radius Rlong.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the early-stage matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC does not need to be fully three-dimensionally thermalized; a model where only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized while longitudinal motion is free-streaming, followed by a sudden isotropization transition and freeze-out, can reproduce pion and kaon transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii, but the final three-dimensional equilibration is necessary to reproduce the HBT radius \(R_{\rm long}\).

## Method

The authors solve the equations of transverse hydrodynamics, where the energy-momentum tensor has vanishing longitudinal pressure, using a 2D equation of state \(P_2 = \varepsilon_2/2 = \nu_g T_2^3/(2\pi)\) with \(\nu_g = 16\). The system evolves until its energy density drops to a value corresponding to a 3D hadron resonance gas at temperature \(T_{3\,\rm f}\); at that point a sudden isotropization transition is imposed via Landau matching conditions, and the isotropic system is immediately frozen out using the Monte Carlo code THERMINATOR. The initial conditions at \(\tau_{\rm i} = 1\) fm are taken from a Glauber model with a mixture of wounded-nucleon and binary-collision densities (\(\kappa = 0.14\)), and the calculations are restricted to midrapidity with boost invariance.

## Key numbers

- Initial 2D central temperature: \(T_{2\,\rm i} = 590\) MeV.
- Freeze-out / isotropization 3D temperature: \(T_{3\,\rm f} = 170\) MeV.
- Normalization constant for transverse clusters: \(n_0 = 0.43\).
- Beam energy: \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- Centrality class: \(c = 20-30\%\).
- Initial proper time: \(\tau_{\rm i} = 1\) fm.
- The model HBT radii are about 20% smaller than experimental values.
- Inclusion of the isotropization transition increases \(R_{\rm long}\) by a factor of 3 at small momenta compared to the model without it.

## Context

This work extends the transverse hydrodynamics formalism introduced by Bialas (2007) and Chojnacki (2007), which previously only described parton-level observables, by adding a sudden isotropization transition and hadronic freeze-out to enable direct comparison with hadron data. The approach contrasts with standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003, Shuryak 2004) which requires very early thermalization; it also differs from the earlier transverse hydrodynamics of Heinz (2002). The model successfully reproduces pion and kaon spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii, but fails to reproduce the mass splitting in elliptic flow for heavier particles like protons and hyperons, leaving the "HBT puzzle" unresolved. The results suggest that future improvements could involve matching to dissipative hydrodynamics.
