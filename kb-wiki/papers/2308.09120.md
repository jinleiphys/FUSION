# Associated vector meson and bound-free electron-positron pair photoproduction in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions

> In this letter we analyze the associated production of a vector meson with the bound - free $e^+e^-$ process in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions through the double scattering mechanism for the energy of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Such process is characterized by the presence of a meson and a positron in the final state and by a forward hydrogen - like ion with a distinct electric charge. We present our predictions for the total cross sections and rapidity distributions considering the rapidity ranges covered by the ALICE and LHCb detectors, which indicate that a future experimental analysis of the $П•+ e^+$ and $J/ОЁ+ e^+$ final states is feasible.

## Key claim

The associated photoproduction of a vector meson (J/ОЁ or ПҶ) together with a bound-free electron-positron pair in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at LHC energies is predicted to have cross sections large enough that experimental observation of the ПҶ + eвҒә and J/ОЁ + eвҒә final states is feasible, provided the forward PbвҒёВ№вҒә ion can be tagged and the vector meson measured in the central detector.

## Method

The authors model the double scattering mechanism by factorizing the total probability into a product of the single-production probabilities for exclusive vector meson photoproduction (via Оі-Pomeron interactions) and for bound-free electron-positron pair production (BFPP). The vector meson cross section is calculated using the color dipole formalism with the Gaus-LC overlap function and the Glauber-Gribov model for the non-forward scattering amplitude, while the BFPP cross section is computed in lowest-order QED using the semiclassical approximation with Sommerfeld-Maue and Darwin wave functions. The differential cross section is obtained by integrating over impact parameter with a minimum cutoff of b_min = 2R_Pb, and the photon flux is estimated using the realistic Wood-Saxon form factor.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Schicker (2015) to measure associated meson pair and bound-free eвҒәeвҒ» production, but instead considers exclusive J/ОЁ and ПҶ photoproduction via Оі-Pomeron interactions, which have larger cross sections and are easier to separate experimentally than meson pairs. The predictions are a factor вүҘ 200 smaller than the exclusive vector meson photoproduction via single scattering mechanism reported in Run 2 LHC data, and a factor вүҘ 20 smaller than the associated vector meson plus free dielectron production estimated in Azevedo (2023). The results motivate future experimental analysis with realistic cuts on positron transverse momentum and rapidity, as well as implementation of the double scattering mechanism in Monte Carlo simulations for ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions.
