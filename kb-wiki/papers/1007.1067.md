# Role of non-collective excitations in low-energy heavy-ion reactions

> We investigate the effect of single-particle excitations on heavy-ion reactions at energies near the Coulomb barrier. To this end, we describe single-particle degrees of freedom with the random matrix theory and solve the coupled-channels equations for one-dimensional systems. We find that the single-particle excitations hinder the penetrability at energies above the barrier, leading to a smeared barrier distribution. This indicates that the single-particle excitations provide a promising way to explain the difference in a quasi-elastic barrier distribution recently observed in $^{20}$Ne + $^{90,92}$Zr systems.

# Role of non-collective excitations in low-energy heavy-ion reactions

## Key claim

Single-particle excitations, modeled with random matrix theory, can explain the experimentally observed smearing of the quasi-elastic barrier distribution in the $^{20}$Ne + $^{92}$Zr system, which conventional coupled-channels calculations using only collective excitations cannot reproduce. The effect is most pronounced at energies above the Coulomb barrier, where single-particle excitations hinder penetrability and smear the high-energy peak of the barrier distribution.

## Method

The authors solved one-dimensional coupled-channels equations for barrier penetration using a constant coupling approximation, including both collective excitations (vibrational or rotational) and single-particle excitations described by random matrix theory. The single-particle coupling matrix elements were generated as Gaussian-distributed random numbers with zero mean and a specified second moment, and the ensemble average was taken over 30 realizations. The quasi-continuum of single-particle states was discretized with a constant energy spacing of 0.02 MeV, and the level density was taken as an exponential function $\rho(\epsilon) = \rho_0 e^{2\sqrt{a\epsilon}}$.

## Key numbers

- For the vibrational coupling model: collective state at 1 MeV with coupling strength $F = 2$ MeV; single-particle states from 2 MeV with level density parameters $\rho_0 = 0.039$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $a = 29/8$ MeV$^{-1}$; coupling parameters $w_0 = 0.005$ MeV and $\Delta = 7$ MeV; barrier height $V_B = 100$ MeV with $s_0 = 3$ fm; reduced mass $\mu = 29 m_N$.
- For the rotational coupling model (mocking $^{20}$Ne + $^{92}$Zr): rotational states at $\epsilon_{2^+} = 1.634$ MeV and $\epsilon_{4^+} = 4.248$ MeV; deformation parameters $\beta_2 = 0.46$ and $\beta_4 = 0.27$; coupling strengths $F_2 = -6.892$ MeV and $F_4 = -4.632$ MeV; barrier height $V_B = 51.76$ MeV with $s_0 = 2.475$ fm; reduced mass $20 \times 92 m_N / 112$.
- At $E = 100$ MeV in the vibrational model, the averaged penetrability was $P = 0.622$ with a root-mean-square deviation of $4.366 \times 10^{-3}$ over 30 ensemble members.
- The number of channels included was 1013 for the vibrational model and 1688 for the rotational model.
- The $^{90}$Zr nucleus has 12 known states up to 4 MeV and 35 states up to 5 MeV; the $^{92}$Zr nucleus has 53 known states up to 4 MeV and 87 states up to 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the random matrix theory approach to heavy-ion reactions originally developed by Weidenmuller and collaborators (1970s) for deep inelastic collisions, but applies it to sub-barrier quantum tunneling rather than classical transport equations. It directly addresses the experimental puzzle from Piasecki and collaborators (2008) regarding the different quasi-elastic barrier distributions for $^{20}$Ne + $^{90,92}$Zr systems, which could not be explained by conventional coupled-channels calculations with only collective excitations (Dasgupta, 1998; Hagino, 1997). The results suggest that non-collective excitations may also play a role in the hindrance of fusion cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies, and the authors note that future work should extend the calculations to three dimensions without the constant coupling approximation to enable quantitative comparison with experiment.
