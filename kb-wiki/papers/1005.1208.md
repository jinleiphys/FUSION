# Nuclear suppression of heavy quark production at forward rapidities in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We calculate nuclear suppression $R_{AA}$ of heavy quarks produced from the initial fusion of partons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We take the shadowing as well as the energy loss suffered by them while passing through Quark Gluon Plasma into account. We obtain results for charm and bottom quarks at several rapidities using different mechanisms for energy loss, to see if we can distinguish between them.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for charm and bottom quarks at RHIC and LHC energies, finding that the combination of nuclear shadowing and energy loss produces a rich structure in $R_{AA}$ as a function of transverse momentum, rapidity, and incident energy, but they caution that a description of energy loss for one quark mass at one rapidity may not be sufficient to identify the most reliable energy loss treatment.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark production in pp collisions using leading-order (LO) pQCD supplemented with a K-factor of approximately 2.5 to reproduce next-to-leading-order (NLO) results, then incorporate nuclear shadowing using the EKS 98 parameterization. They model the QGP evolution using a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken expansion with a local fluid approximation, calculate the average path length of heavy quarks through the plasma, and evaluate collisional energy loss using the Braaten-Thoma (BT), Bjorken, and Peigne-Peshier (PP) formalisms, and radiative energy loss using the Djordjevic-Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (DGLV), Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW), and Xiang-Ding-Zhou-Rohrich (XDZR) formalisms.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature at y=0: 377 MeV for RHIC (Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV) and 555 MeV for LHC (Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV), assuming formation time $\tau_0$ = 0.2 fm/c.
* Average path length $\langle L \rangle$: 5.78 fm for Au+Au at RHIC and 6.14 fm for Pb+Pb at LHC.
* Nuclear overlap function $T_{AA}$ for b=0: approximately 280 fm$^{-2}$ for Au+Au at RHIC and 290 fm$^{-2}$ for Pb+Pb at LHC.
* For charm quarks at RHIC, $R_{AA}$ with shadowing and energy loss starts at about 40% below 1, rises to about 0.8 at $p_T \approx$ 2 GeV, then drops to $\approx$ 0.3 at $p_T \approx$ 15 GeV.
* For bottom quarks at RHIC, $R_{AA}$ with shadowing and energy loss is $\approx$ 0.8 at $p_T \approx$ 0 GeV, rises to about 1.2 at $p_T \approx$ 2 GeV, then drops to about 0.3-0.4 at larger $p_T$.
* For charm quarks at LHC, $R_{AA}$ drops to a level of about 0.2 at larger $p_T$ using the DGLV formalism.
* For bottom quarks at LHC, $R_{AA}$ starts at $\approx$ 0.6 at $p_T \approx$ 0 GeV, rises to $\approx$ 1.1 at $p_T \approx$ 2 GeV, then drops to about 0.4 at $p_T \approx$ 20 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark energy loss in QGP by Shuryak, Lin et al., Kampfer et al., and Mustafa et al. (all cited in the paper), who suggested that correlated charm and bottom decay could be suppressed by energy loss. The calculations use a local fluid approximation previously employed by Ramona, Sean, and Sourav (as referenced in the text) and compare multiple energy loss formalisms (Bjorken, Braaten-Thoma, Peigne-Peshier for collisional; DGLV, ASW, XDZR for radiative) to assess whether measurements at different rapidities and energies can distinguish between them. The results enable future comparisons with single electron data from RHIC and predictions for LHC measurements, and the authors plan to extend the work to (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics and include D and B meson decay.
