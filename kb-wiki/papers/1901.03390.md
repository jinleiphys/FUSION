# Effect of Scalar Boson on Fermionic Dark Star

> The role of scalar boson exchange as a mediator of the fermionic dark particle interaction and the mass of dark particle on the bulk properties of fermionic dark stars including their moment of inertia and tidal deformability are studied. We have found that the role of the attractive nature of the scalar boson exchange and the fermionic dark particle mass can control the stiffness of the fermionic dark star equation of state. By increasing the strength of scalar boson coupling, the fermionic dark star becomes more compact. As a consequence, if scalar boson exchange contribution is included the compactness of a dark star can exceed $C$=0.22. We also compare the fermionic dark stars moment of inertia and tidal deformability to those of neutron stars (with and without hyperons in neutron star core) predicted by relativistic mean field model. It is evidence that the properties of both types of stars are quite different. We also have found that the universal I-Love relation in fermionic dark stars is not affected by scalar boson exchange contribution and the fermionic dark particle mass. Possible observations of fermionic dark stars are also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that when the relativistic treatment of scalar boson exchange is properly included (not just the non-relativistic Yukawa approximation), fermionic dark stars can exceed a compactness of C = 0.22, contrary to the earlier bound reported by Maselli et al. (2017) that compactness never exceeds approximately 0.22 for either bosonic or fermionic dark matter cases.

## Method
The authors model fermionic dark matter as Dirac fermions coupled to scalar and vector bosons using a relativistic mean-field approximation at zero temperature. They generate a set of equations of state (EOS) for dark matter with fermion masses of 1 GeV and 2 GeV, boson masses of 10 MeV, and various combinations of scalar and vector coupling constants. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for slowly rotating stars to compute mass-radius relations, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability, comparing results to neutron star predictions from the relativistic mean field model using the BSP parameter set with and without hyperons.

## Key numbers
- Compactness C = 0.22: the threshold that scalar boson exchange allows dark stars to exceed, contrary to the earlier bound from Maselli et al. (2017)
- Maximum mass around 0.5 solar masses for the softest dark matter EOS (EOS V, with M_chi = 2 GeV, alpha_v = 0.5e-3, alpha_s = 0.5e-3)
- Maximum stable center energy density epsilon_c approximately 17610 MeV/fm^3 for EOS V
- Dark particle masses considered: M_chi = 1 GeV and M_chi = 2 GeV
- Boson masses: m_v = m_s = 10 MeV
- The ratio of total cross section to dark particle mass constraint: 0.1 cm/g < sigma/M_chi < 10 cm/g

## Context
This work extends the study of fermionic dark stars by Kouvaris and Nielsen (2015) and Maselli et al. (2017), who used a non-relativistic Yukawa-type potential approximation for the interactions. The authors show that properly treating the scalar boson exchange relativistically changes the stiffness of the dark matter equation of state at high densities, allowing dark stars to exceed the compactness bound of approximately 0.22 reported by Maselli et al. (2017). The results enable distinguishing dark stars from neutron stars and black holes through gravitational wave observables such as tidal deformability and moment of inertia, and show that the universal I-Love relation holds for these dark stars regardless of scalar boson contribution.
