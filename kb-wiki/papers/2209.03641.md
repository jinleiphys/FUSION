# Shake-off in the 164Er neutrinoless double electronic capture and the dark matter puzzle

> Traditionally double neutrinoless electronic capture is considered as a resonance process. We have fulfilled shake-off probability calculations, leading to ionization of the electron shell, in the case of 164Er. Allowance for the shake-off removes the requirement of resonance, leading to a radical increase of the capture rate. In the case of 164Er, the contribution of the new mechanism increases the capture rate by a factor of 5.6 as compared to the conventional resonance fluorescence mechanism. It also increases the probability of electron capture from higher shells, which must be foreseen in the experimental studies. Moreover, the effect of the shake-off can expand the list of candidate nuclei for experimental research. The influence of the shake-off is also expected to manifest itself in the other beta processes which are used in the studies of the neutrino nature, its mass and role in the dark matter puzzle.

# Shake-off in the 164Er neutrinoless double electronic capture and the dark matter puzzle

## Key claim

The shake-off mechanism removes the requirement of resonance between initial and final atoms in neutrinoless double electron capture, leading to a radical increase of the capture rate. For 164Er, the contribution of the new mechanism increases the capture rate by a factor of 5.6 compared to the conventional resonance fluorescence mechanism, though this factor depends on the specific resonance defect value of 6.82 keV for the LL capture channel.

## Method

The authors performed shake-off probability calculations using the RAINE software package with self-consistent Dirac-Fock one-electron wave functions. They computed overlap integrals between electron wave functions of the parent neutral 164Er atom and the daughter 164Dy atom with three vacancies (two from captured electrons and one from the ejected shake-off electron), then compared the resulting capture probability to the traditional Breit-Wigner resonance fluorescence mechanism. The calculations were performed for a range of hypothetical resonance defect values from 0.05 to 20 keV, and separately for captures from different electron shells (LL, LM, MM, LN, MN, NN).

## Key numbers

- The resonance defect for 164Er LL capture is 6.82 keV
- The shake-off mechanism increases the capture rate by a factor of 2.81 for LL capture alone, and by a total factor of 4.58 when all capture channels (LL, LM, MM, LN, MN, NN) are summed
- The total gain including higher-shell captures is 5.6 times the conventional resonance fluorescence mechanism
- The mean shake-off probability is 82% per capture
- The refined half-life estimate for 164Er is 3.6 x 10^29 (1 eV/m_nu)^2 years, compared to the traditional estimate of 2 x 10^30 years for m_nu = 1 eV
- For 152Gd, the shake-off half-life is 8 x 10^26 years versus the resonance half-life of 10^27 years
- For 180W, the shake-off half-life is 3 x 10^27 years versus the resonance half-life of 3 x 10^28 years
- The L1 hole width in Dy is 4.3 eV

## Context

This work extends the shake-off mechanism previously proposed by Karpeshin (nrl) for 152Gd (which has the smallest known resonance defect of 0.910 keV) to the case of 164Er, which has a larger resonance defect of 6.82 keV. The results confirm that shake-off becomes more effective relative to the resonance mechanism for larger resonance defects, where the Breit-Wigner factor drops dramatically. The paper refines the list of three candidate nuclei for experimental study from Elis (year), 152Gd, 164Er, and 180W, and suggests that 180W may become a better candidate than 152Gd due to its higher natural abundance (152Gd is only 0.2% abundant). The shake-off mechanism also has implications for beta decay studies in the KATRIN experiment (tritium beta decay) and for electron capture measurements in 163Ho and 159Dy, where previous calculations by NewCand (year) using the Vatai approximation did not include shake-off effects.
