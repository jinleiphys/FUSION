# Proton-proton momentum correlation function as a probe of the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution

> Within an improved transport model, we examine effects of the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution induced by short-range correlations on the proton-proton momentum correlation function in $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the proton-proton momentum correlation function from preequilibrium emissions responds sensitively to the presence as well as fraction of nucleons in the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution, but is almost robustly insensitive to other factors including the symmetry energy and the uncertainty of cutoff value of nucleon effective high momentum. In terms of the sensitivity and clearness, we propose that the proton-proton momentum correlation function from preequilibrium emissions can be as an effective probe of the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution.

# Proton-proton momentum correlation function as a probe of the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution

## Key claim
The proton-proton momentum correlation function from preequilibrium emissions in heavy-ion collisions responds sensitively to the presence and fraction of nucleons in the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution, but is almost robustly insensitive to other factors including the symmetry energy and the uncertainty of the cutoff value of nucleon effective high momentum. This sensitivity is demonstrated specifically for 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon within an improved transport model.

## Method
The authors used an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model as the event generator, incorporating pion potential, isospin-dependent delta potential, and fitted high-momentum behaviors of the nucleon optical potential. They initialized 197Au nuclei with a 1/k^4 high momentum tail distribution, using two fractions of nucleons in the HMT (12% and 24%) and a cutoff parameter lambda = 2.75 +/- 0.25, then calculated the proton-proton momentum correlation function using the Koonin-Pratt equation with the CRAB correlation afterburner code.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au collisions
- Two HMT fractions tested: 12% and 24% of nucleons in the 197Au nucleus
- Cutoff parameter lambda = 2.75 +/- 0.25 for the maximum momentum of nucleons in the HMT (k_max = lambda * k_F)
- SRC scaling factor a2(A) estimated as 5.99 for the 24% HMT case and 2.99 for the 12% HMT case
- The high momentum region from 300 to 600 MeV/c was used to estimate the SRC scaling factor
- The average emission time of protons with momentum above about 600 MeV/c is sensitive to the HMT presence
- The nucleon emission rate before and at about 15 fm/c is not more than 2.5%
- Symmetry energy parameter x varied from -1 to 2, corresponding to L values from -32.657 MeV to 149.309 MeV (without HMT) and -0.783 MeV to 181.183 MeV (with HMT)

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Chen (2003, 2004) and Ma (2006) who used BUU and QMD models respectively to study symmetry energy effects on nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation functions, finding that symmetry energy effects become negligible in heavy systems and at high beam energies. The present study demonstrates that the pp momentum correlation function from preequilibrium emissions can serve as an effective probe of the high momentum tail of the nucleon momentum distribution, which is induced by short-range correlations. The work builds on experimental findings from Jefferson Laboratory by Subedi (2008), Hen (2014), and Duer (2019) showing that about 20% of nucleons are in the HMT in nuclei from 12C to 208Pb, and on theoretical calculations using the 1/k^4 distribution confirmed by the CLAS Collaboration. This probe could enable future experimental extraction of the HMT fraction in nuclear matter.
