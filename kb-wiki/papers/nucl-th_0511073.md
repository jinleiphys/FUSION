# Transport coefficients of strongly coupled gauge theories: insights from string theory

> The transport properties of certain strongly coupled thermal gauge theories can be determined from their effective description in terms of gravity or superstring theory duals. Here we provide a short summary of the results for the shear and bulk viscosity, charge diffusion constant, and the speed of sound in supersymmetric strongly interacting plasmas. We also outline a general algorithm for computing transport coefficients in any gravity dual. The algorithm relates the transport coefficients to the coefficients in the quasinormal spectrum of five-dimensional black holes in asymptotically anti de Sitter space.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is universal and equal to \(1/4\pi\) for any gauge theory described by a gravity dual in the classical (super)gravity approximation, but this universality is expected to break down when stringy corrections (finite 't Hooft coupling) or quantum gravity corrections (finite \(N_c\)) are included.

## Method

Transport coefficients are extracted from the retarded correlation functions of the stress-energy tensor in the dual gauge theory, computed via the Lorentzian AdS/CFT prescription. For the shear viscosity, the Green-Kubo formula relates the zero-frequency limit of the imaginary part of the retarded correlator \(G^R_{xy,xy}\) to \(\eta\); this correlator is obtained by solving the equation of motion for a minimally coupled massless scalar in the five-dimensional AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. Alternatively, transport coefficients are identified from the hydrodynamic poles (quasinormal frequencies) of the retarded correlators, whose dispersion relations yield the shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, speed of sound, and charge diffusion constant.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at infinite \(N_c\) and infinite 't Hooft coupling: \(\eta = \frac{\pi}{8} N_c^2 T^3\).
* Stringy correction to the shear viscosity: \(\eta = \frac{\pi}{8} N^2 T^3 \left( 1 + \frac{75}{4} \zeta(3) (2 g^2_{YM} N_c)^{-3/2} + \cdots \right)\), with \(\zeta(3) \approx 1.2020569\).
* Entropy density of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at strong coupling: \(s = \frac{2\pi^2}{3} N_c^2 T^3 \left( \frac{3}{4} + \frac{45}{32} (2 g^2_{YM} N_c)^{-3/2} + \cdots \right)\).
* Viscosity-entropy ratio at infinite coupling: \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).
* Stringy correction to \(\eta/s\): \(\frac{\eta}{s} = \frac{1}{4\pi} \left( 1 + \frac{135}{8} \zeta(3) (2 g^2_{YM} N_c)^{-3/2} + \cdots \right)\).
* Speed of sound in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at infinite coupling: \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
* R-charge diffusion constant in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at infinite coupling: \(D_R = 1/(2\pi T)\).
* Bulk viscosity in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at infinite coupling: \(\zeta = 0\).
* For strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=2^*\) SYM, the ratio \(\eta/s\) remains \(1/4\pi\), while the bulk viscosity obeys \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} \simeq \kappa \left( v_s^2 - \frac{1}{3} \right)\) with \(\kappa\) of order one.

## Context

This review summarizes the program, initiated by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001, 2002), of computing transport coefficients of strongly coupled thermal gauge theories via the AdS/CFT correspondence. It extends earlier work on the entropy of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM by Gubser, Klebanov, and Peet (1996) and provides a general algorithm for any gravity dual, as formalized by Kovtun and Starinets (2005). The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice gauge theory calculations (e.g., Karsch, 1986; Sakai, 2005) and with hydrodynamic models of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC (Teaney, 2003; Shuryak, 2003), where the observed elliptic flow suggests a small \(\eta/s\) close to \(1/4\pi\).
