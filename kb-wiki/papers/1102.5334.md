# New formulation of gamma-Z box corrections to the weak charge of the proton

> We present a new formulation of one of the major radiative corrections to the weak charge of the proton -- that arising from the axial-vector hadron part of the $γZ$ box diagram, $\Re{\rm e}\, \Box_{γZ}^{\rm A}$. This formulation, based on dispersion relations, relates the $γZ$ contributions to moments of the $F_3^{γZ}$ interference structure function. It has a clear connection to the pioneering work of Marciano and Sirlin, and enables a systematic approach to improved numerical precision. Using currently available data, the total correction from all intermediate states is $\Re{\rm e}\, \Box_{γZ}^{\rm A} = 0.0044(4)$ at zero energy, which shifts the theoretical estimate of the proton weak charge from $0.0713(8)$ to $0.0705(8)$. The energy dependence of this result, which is vital for interpreting the Q$_{\rm weak}$ experiment, is also determined.

# New formulation of gamma-Z box corrections to the weak charge of the proton

## Key claim

The axial-vector hadron part of the gamma-Z box diagram correction to the proton weak charge, Re Box_gammaZ^A, can be expressed via dispersion relations in terms of moments of the F_3^gammaZ interference structure function, yielding a value of 0.0044(4) at zero energy that shifts the theoretical estimate of the proton weak charge from 0.0713(8) to 0.0705(8), with the caveat that the low-Q^2 behavior of F_3^gammaZ is not directly constrained by data and requires model assumptions.

## Method

The authors use dispersion relations to relate the real part of the gamma-Z box diagram to an integral over its imaginary part, which depends on the parity-violating F_3^gammaZ interference structure function. The calculation is split into three kinematic regions: elastic (nucleon form factors), resonance (parametrized transition form factors from Lalakulich et al.), and deep inelastic scattering (using parton distribution functions and moment expansions). For the low-Q^2 DIS region where F_3^gammaZ is unknown, two phenomenological models are employed to estimate the contribution.

## Key numbers

- Re Box_gammaZ^A = 0.0044(4) at zero electron energy E=0
- Re Box_gammaZ^A = 0.0037(4) at E=1.165 GeV (the Q_weak experiment energy)
- The theoretical estimate of the proton weak charge shifts from 0.0713(8) to 0.0705(8) when using the new correction
- The total energy-dependent correction Box_gammaZ(E) - Box_gammaZ(0) is 0.0040^{+0.0011}_{-0.0004} at E=1.165 GeV
- The lowest (n=1) moment contribution to the DIS part is 0.0033, independent of energy
- Using running alpha(Q^2) and v_e(Q^2) reduces the total contribution by 17% relative to using fixed alpha and v_e(M_Z^2)
- The elastic contribution at E=0 agrees with the value L_m=2.04 from Marciano and Sirlin when parameters are adjusted to match their work

## Context

This work provides a systematic, data-driven alternative to the pioneering quark-model approach of Marciano and Sirlin (1980) for computing gamma-Z box corrections, which previously contributed almost half the error in the theoretical estimate of the proton weak charge. The new dispersion relation formulation enables evaluation of the energy dependence vital for interpreting the Q_weak experiment at Jefferson Lab, and can be systematically improved with future parity-violating structure function measurements at low Q^2. The vector hadron correction Box_gammaZ^V was computed separately by Gorchtein et al. and Sibirtsev et al., and the total correction combining both axial and vector parts is presented here.
