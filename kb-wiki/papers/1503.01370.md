# Extended Skyrme pseudo-potential deduced from infinite matter properties

> We discuss the contributions to the Equation of State for the N$\ell$LO Skyrme pseudo-potential ($\ell$=2,3). We show that by adding 4th and 6th order gradient terms, it is possible to fairly reproduce the spin/isospin decomposition of an equation of state obtained from \emph{ab-initio} methods. Moreover, by inspecting the partial-wave decomposition of the equation of state, we show for the first time a possible way to add explicit constraints on the sign of the tensor terms of the Skyrme interaction.

## Key claim
By adding 4th and 6th order gradient terms to the Skyrme pseudo-potential (N2LO and N3LO), it is possible to fairly reproduce the spin/isospin decomposition of the equation of state obtained from *ab-initio* methods, and for the first time to constrain the sign of the tensor terms. However, the fitted parameters should be considered only as a starting point for a more complete fit that includes finite nuclei observables, and the density-dependent term must be adjusted to keep the effective mass near m*/m ≈ 0.7.

## Method
The authors extended the standard Skyrme pseudo-potential to N2LO and N3LO orders by including 4th and 6th order gradient terms in the central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. They fitted the central parameters to the spin/isospin (S,T) channels of the equation of state from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of Baldo et al. (1997) using the Argonne v14 interaction plus Urbana three-body term, and then separately fitted the tensor and spin-orbit parameters to partial-wave decompositions of the same BHF results.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density for VLyB62 interaction: ρ_sat = 0.179 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation for VLyB62: E/A = -15.50 MeV
- Effective mass for VLyB62: m*/m = 0.789
- Incompressibility for VLyB62: K_∞ = 220.8 MeV
- Saturation density for VLyB63 interaction: ρ_sat = 0.169 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation for VLyB63: E/A = -15.72 MeV
- Effective mass for VLyB63: m*/m = 0.762
- Incompressibility for VLyB63: K_∞ = 201.9 MeV
- Spin-orbit parameter W_0 ≈ 245.741 MeV fm^5 (VLyB62) and 241.748 MeV fm^5 (VLyB63)
- The N1LO (standard) Skyrme functional has an average discrepancy of ≈10 MeV in binding energies of selected nuclei, while adding 4th order terms (N2LO) reduces the error by one order of magnitude (Carlsson et al., 2008)

## Context
This work extends the approach of Lesinski et al. (2006), who showed that a standard Skyrme functional cannot reproduce the spin/isospin decomposition of *ab-initio* equations of state, and of Carlsson et al. (2008), who studied higher-order derivative terms via the Density Matrix Expansion method. The new results enable a systematic, order-by-order fitting protocol for extended Skyrme pseudo-potentials that avoids bulk instabilities (Pastore et al., 2013) and provides a starting point for fits that include finite nuclei observables, potentially improving spectroscopic predictions beyond the limits of the standard Skyrme functional identified by the UNEDF-SciDAC collaboration (Kortelainen et al., 2013).
