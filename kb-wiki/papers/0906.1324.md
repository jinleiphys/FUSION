# Phase diagram and thermodynamics of the Polyakov linear sigma model with three quark flavors

> The phase diagram at finite temperature and density is investigated in the framework of the Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) with three light quark flavors in the mean field approximation. It is found that in the PLSM, the three phase transitions, i.e, the chiral restoration of $u,d$ quarks, the chiral restoration of $s$ quark, and the deconfinement phase transition are independent. There exists two-flavor quarkyonic phase at low density and three-flavor quarkyonic phase at high density. The critical end point (CEP) which separating the crossover from the first-order line in the PLSM model is located at$(T_E, μ_E)=(188 \mathrm{MeV},139.5 \mathrm{MeV})$. In the transition region the thermodynamic properties and bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio $ζ/s$ are also discussed in the PLSM.

## Key claim

In the Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) with three quark flavors, the chiral restoration of u,d quarks, the chiral restoration of the s quark, and the deconfinement phase transition are independent transitions, not simultaneous as often assumed; this leads to a two-flavor quarkyonic phase at low density and a three-flavor quarkyonic phase at high density, with the critical end point (CEP) located at (T_E, mu_E) = (188 MeV, 139.5 MeV) within the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors extended the Polyakov linear sigma model from two to three quark flavors, coupling quarks to a spatially constant temporal background gauge field representing Polyakov loop dynamics. They evaluated the grand partition function in the mean field approximation, replacing meson fields by their expectation values while retaining quarks and antiquarks as quantum fields, then minimized the resulting thermodynamic potential with respect to the chiral condensates sigma_x, sigma_y and the Polyakov loop variables phi, phi* to obtain the phase structure. The critical temperatures for crossover transitions were determined from peaks in the temperature derivatives of the condensates, and the CEP was located by identifying where the quark number susceptibility diverges.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point (CEP) located at (T_E, mu_E) = (188 MeV, 139.5 MeV)
- At zero chemical potential, the chiral restoration temperature for u,d quarks is T_c^q approximately 203.5 MeV
- At zero chemical potential, the deconfinement critical temperature is T_c^d approximately 220 MeV
- At zero chemical potential, the chiral restoration temperature for the s quark is T_c^s approximately 260 MeV
- At mu = 200 MeV, the chiral restoration temperature for u,d quarks drops to T_c^q approximately 173 MeV
- The Polyakov loop effective potential uses T_0 = 270 MeV for the pure gauge deconfinement critical temperature
- Vacuum condensates: sigma_x0 = 92.4 MeV, sigma_y0 = 94.5 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, kaon decay constant f_K = 113 MeV
- Yukawa coupling g approximately 6.5, giving constituent quark mass m_q approximately 1/3 nucleon mass and m_s approximately 433 MeV
- Mesonic parameters: m^2 = (342.52 MeV)^2, lambda_1 = 1.40, lambda_2 = 46.48, c = 4807.84 MeV, h_x = (120.73 MeV)^3, h_y = (336.41 MeV)^3

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor Polyakov linear sigma model of Schaefer (2007) and Kahara (2008) to three quark flavors, and contrasts with the three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model of Fukushima (2008) which found simultaneous crossovers around 200 MeV. The predicted CEP at (188, 139.5) MeV is close to the lattice result of Fodor (2004) at (162 +/- 2, 120 +/- 13) MeV, but the critical chemical potential is much lower than in the three-flavor PNJL model where mu_E exceeds 300 MeV (Fu 2007, Ciminale 2007). The results enable comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential from Cheng (2007) and provide a framework for studying the quarkyonic phase and bulk viscosity in the transition region.
