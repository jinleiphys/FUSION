# Prolate-oblate shape competition and impact on charge radii in Bk isotopes

> The nuclear charge radius provides a fundamental probe of nuclear structure, yet experimental data remain rare in the actinide region. Using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 functional, we carry out a systematic investigation of prolate-oblate shape competition in odd-$A$ Bk isotopes. Deformation is found to play an important role in the description of charge radii $r_c$ by extending the density distribution. Notably, $r_c$ exhibits a distinct shape dependence: for a given absolute quadrupole deformation $|β_2|$, oblate shapes yield larger charge radii than their prolate counterparts in well-deformed nuclei near the mid-shell region, where the empirical formula $r_c(β_2) = \left(1 + \frac{5}{4π}|β_2|^2\right) r_c(0)$ fails to capture the observed behavior. This enhancement is attributed to a central depression (or ``bubble" structure) in the proton density, which microscopically originates from the non-occupation of the spherical $3s_{1/2}$($Ω=1/2$) orbital in oblate minima. These findings establish a clear microscopic connection between nuclear shape, single-particle occupancy, and nuclear size.

# Prolate-oblate shape competition and impact on charge radii in Bk isotopes

## Key claim

For a given absolute quadrupole deformation |β₂|, oblate shapes yield larger charge radii than their prolate counterparts in well-deformed Bk isotopes near the mid-shell region, where the empirical formula r_c(β₂) = (1 + 5/4π |β₂|²) r_c(0) fails to capture this shape-dependent behavior. This enhancement is attributed to a central depression ("bubble" structure) in the proton density, which microscopically originates from the non-occupation of the spherical 3s₁/₂(Ω=1/2) orbital in oblate minima.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional to systematically investigate odd-A Bk isotopes from the proton drip-line to the neutron drip-line. Constrained and unconstrained DRHBc calculations were performed to obtain potential energy curves, ground-state properties, and local minima for each isotope, with the equal filling approximation adopted to handle blocking effects for the unpaired odd proton.

## Key numbers

- Energy difference between ground state and local minimum reaches up to 9.3 MeV for mid-shell Bk isotopes (e.g., ²⁷⁹Bk), decreasing sharply towards shell closures at N=184 and N=258
- Shape coexistence predicted in ³³¹Bk with an energy difference of 0.57 MeV and a barrier of 6.8 MeV between the two minima
- For ³¹⁵Bk, the charge radius difference between oblate local minimum and prolate ground state is Δr_c = 0.02 fm (with Δβ₂,ₚ² ≈ 0)
- For ³³⁹Bk, the charge radius difference between the two minima is only Δr_c = 0.002 fm (with Δβ₂,ₚ² ≈ 0)
- DRHBc reproduces binding energies and charge radii for even-Z nuclei with rms deviations of 1.433 MeV and 0.033 fm, respectively
- The DRHBc mass table covers nuclei from Z=8 to Z=120, with the RCHB mass table achieving an rms deviation of 0.0358 fm for charge radii relative to experimental data

## Context

This work extends previous DRHBc studies of even-Z nuclei (Pan et al., 2025) and prior investigations of Bk isotopes (Huang et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2026) by focusing specifically on prolate-oblate shape competition and its impact on charge radii in odd-A systems. The study builds on earlier DRHBc applications that explained shape staggering in Hg isotopes (Mun et al., 2023) and shape coexistence in Kr and Sr isotopes (Sun et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2023). The findings establish a microscopic connection between single-particle orbital occupancy (specifically the 3s₁/₂ orbital), bubble structures in proton density, and nuclear charge radii, which may offer insights into the unresolved puzzle of systematically smaller measured charge radii in Cm isotopes compared to neighboring U and Pu isotopes.
