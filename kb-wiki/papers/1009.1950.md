# Quadrupole moment of the nucleon in chiral constituent quark model

> The electromagnetic form factors are the most fundamental quantities to describe the internal structure of the nucleon and the shape of a spatially extended particle is determined by its {\it intrinsic} quadrupole moment which is first order moment of the charge density operator. With some experimental indications of a deformed nucleon, we have calculated the {\it intrinsic} quadrupole moment of the octet and decuplet baryons in the framework of chiral constituent quark model \chiCQM which is quite successful in explaining some of the important baryon properties in the nonperturbative regime.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) predicts non-zero intrinsic quadrupole moments for octet baryons (including the nucleon), indicating deformation, whereas the Naive Quark Model (NQM) predicts zero quadrupole moment for all octet baryons. The results depend on the inclusion of a "quark sea" generated by chiral fluctuations and on the values of the general parameterization (GP) coefficients B and C, which are fitted to experimental charge radii.

## Method

The authors calculate the intrinsic quadrupole moment of octet and decuplet baryons using the general parameterization (GP) method, where the charge quadrupole operator is expressed as a sum of two- and three-body operator terms in spin-flavor space with coefficients B and C. These coefficients are fixed by a fit to experimental baryon charge radii, yielding B = -0.0525 and C = -0.0158. The χCQM introduces a "quark sea" via the emission of Goldstone bosons from constituent quarks, modifying the flavor and spin structure of the baryon wave functions; the χCQM parameters (a = 0.12, α = 0.45, β = 0.45, ζ = -0.15) are taken from a prior publication. Matrix elements of the quadrupole operator are then evaluated using SU(6) spin-flavor wave functions with the modified quark content.

## Key numbers

- Proton quadrupole moment Q_p = -0.032 fm² (with three-quark term, C = -0.016) in χCQM; NQM gives 0.
- Neutron quadrupole moment Q_n = -0.019 fm² (with three-quark term, C = -0.016) in χCQM; NQM gives 0.
- Δ⁺ quadrupole moment Q_Δ⁺ = -0.208 fm² (with three-quark term, C = -0.016) in χCQM; NQM gives -0.242 fm².
- Δ⁺⁺ quadrupole moment Q_Δ⁺⁺ = -0.428 fm² (with three-quark term, C = -0.016) in χCQM; NQM gives -0.484 fm².
- Ω⁻ quadrupole moment Q_Ω⁻ = 0.296 fm² (with three-quark term, C = -0.016) in χCQM; NQM gives 0.315 fm².
- GP coefficients from fit to charge radii: B = -0.0525, C = -0.0158.
- χCQM parameters: a = 0.12, α = 0.45, β = 0.45, ζ = -0.15.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), previously successful for baryon magnetic moments and hyperon beta decay parameters (Dahiya, 2000s), to the calculation of intrinsic quadrupole moments. It directly contradicts the Naive Quark Model (NQM), which predicts zero quadrupole moment for all octet baryons, and addresses experimental indications of nucleon deformation from the LEGS Collaboration (Blanpied, 2001) and Mainz Collaboration (Tiator, 2003), which measured the Δ⁺N transition quadrupole moment as -0.108 ± 0.009 ± 0.034 fm² and -0.0846 ± 0.0033 fm², respectively. The results enable future comparison with experimental data on octet baryon quadrupole moments and octet-decuplet transition quadrupole moments, providing a test of SU(3) symmetry breaking and the role of the three-quark term in the GP method.
