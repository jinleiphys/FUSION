# Conjunction of $γ$-rigid and $γ$-stable collective motion in the critical point of the phase transition from spherical to deformed nuclear shapes

> Based on the competition between $γ$-stable and $γ$-rigid collective motions mediated by a rigidity parameter, a two-parameter exactly separable version of the Bohr Hamiltonian is proposed. The $γ$-stable part of the Hamiltonian is restricted to stiff oscillations around the $γ$ value of the rigid motion. The separated potential for $β$ and $γ$ shape variables is chosen such that in the lower limit of this parameter, the model recovers exactly the ES-$X(5)$ model, while in the upper limit it tends to the prolate $γ$-rigid solution $X(3)$. The combined effect of the rigidity and stiffness parameters on the energy spectrum and wave function is duly investigated. Numerical results are given for few nuclei showing such ambiguous behaviour.

# Conjunction of γ-rigid and γ-stable collective motion in the critical point of the phase transition from spherical to deformed nuclear shapes

## Key claim

The paper proposes a two-parameter exactly separable Bohr Hamiltonian that interpolates between the γ-stable ES-X(5) model (at rigidity parameter χ=0) and the prolate γ-rigid X(3) model (as χ→1), using a rigidity parameter χ that measures the degree of γ-rigidity; however, the χ=1 limit is avoided to preserve the geometry of the curvilinear space, and the model requires both a rigidity parameter χ and a stiffness parameter a to describe the energy spectrum.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian H = χT_r + (1-χ)T_s + V(β,γ) combining the γ-rigid kinetic energy operator T_r and the γ-stable kinetic energy operator T_s, with a separated potential u(β) + (1-χ)u(γ)/β². The β potential is taken as an infinite square well (0 for β≤β_W, infinite otherwise), while the γ potential is a harmonic oscillator u(γ) = (3a)²γ²/2 centered at γ=0. The Schrödinger equation separates into a Bessel equation for β and a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator equation for γ, solved analytically with Laguerre polynomials and Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The R₄/₂ = E(4_g⁺)/E(2_g⁺) ratio at χ→1 approaches the X(3) value of 2.44, while at χ=0 it recovers the ES-X(5) value.
- The β band head E(0_β⁺)/E(2_g⁺) at χ→1 approaches the X(3) value of 2.87.
- The saturation maximal height of the R₄/₂ plateau at high stiffness is 3.33, corresponding to the SU(3) rotational limit.
- For ¹⁶⁰Gd: χ = 0.948, a = 168.899, σ = 0.567; experimental R₄/₂ = 3.30 vs. theoretical 3.28.
- For ¹⁶²Dy: χ = 0.269, a = 10.309, σ = 0.845; experimental R₄/₂ = 3.29 vs. theoretical 3.28.
- For ¹⁶⁶Er: χ = 0.848, a = 41.191, σ = 1.359; experimental R₄/₂ = 3.29 vs. theoretical 3.26.
- For ¹⁶²Gd, the fit gave χ≈0 and a=8.5 with σ=0.415, compared to a(ES-X(5))=8.3.

## Context

This work extends the exactly separable ES-X(5) model of Bonatsos et al. (2004) and the γ-rigid X(3) model of Bonatsos et al. (2004) by introducing a continuous interpolation between γ-stable and γ-rigid collective motion via a rigidity parameter. The model addresses the unexpected similarity between β excited bands of X(5) and X(3) solutions noted in prior work, and provides a framework for describing nuclei near the critical point of the spherical-to-deformed phase transition that exhibit ambiguous γ-rigidity behavior. The numerical application identifies ¹⁶⁰Gd, ¹⁶²Dy, and ¹⁶⁶Er as candidate nuclei, with ¹⁶²Dy showing the highest degree of mixing between γ-rigid and γ-stable motion (χ=0.269). The approach enables future inclusion of octupole degrees of freedom and smoothed β potentials for more realistic descriptions of transitional nuclei.
