# Optimization of the number of intrinsic states included in the discrete Generator Coordinate Method

> We present a mechanism to efficiently pre-select the number of intrinsic many-body states that are used to define the many-body wave functions within the discrete Generator Coordinate Method (GCM). This procedure, based on the proper definition of a natural basis of orthonormal states, does not require the evaluation of the non-diagonal Hamiltonian kernels to do the selection and helps to reduce the numerical instabilities. The performance of the method is analyzed in detail in the ground state and $0^{+}$ excited states of some selected nuclei computed with the Gogny energy density functional.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the number of intrinsic many-body states needed in the discrete Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) can be pre-selected by analyzing the orthonormality of the natural basis derived from the norm overlap matrix, without requiring the costly evaluation of non-diagonal Hamiltonian matrix elements at the selection stage. The authors show that the number of numerically meaningful orthonormal states saturates as the density of intrinsic states increases, and that this saturation point can be identified using only norm overlaps.

## Method

The authors perform projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) calculations using the Gogny D1S energy density functional for three nuclei: ⁴⁰Ca, ⁸⁰Sr, and ¹⁸⁶Pb. They generate sets of intrinsic states with varying density (20, 40, 80, 160, and 320 states) within the axial quadrupole deformation interval β₂ ∈ [-0.65, 0.95], using particle-number and angular-momentum projected (J=0) Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle states obtained via variation after particle-number projection (VAPNP). They compare the standard plateau condition (energy convergence with increasing natural basis size) against a new criterion based on the orthonormality condition ⟨Λₖ|Λₖ'⟩ - δₖₖ' < ε_nat to select the number of natural basis states.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca and ⁸⁰Sr, the maximum number of numerically meaningful natural basis states before the first big energy jump is approximately 20 and 40, respectively, independent of the initial set size.
- For ¹⁸⁶Pb, the maximum number of natural basis states before the first big energy jump is 70, 91, and 98 for the sets with 80, 160, and 320 intrinsic states, respectively.
- The first big energy jump occurs when the normalized norm overlap eigenvalues reach λₖ/λ₁ ≈ 10⁻⁷ for all three nuclei.
- Energy differences between PGCM ground state energies obtained with different initial sets (S₃, S₄, S₅) are all smaller than 50 keV, except for ⁸⁰Sr with the sparsest set S₁ which differs by ~200 keV.
- Using the orthonormality criterion with ε_nat = 10⁻¹² and 10⁻¹⁵, the largest energy differences in PGCM ground state energies are 170 keV for ⁴⁰Ca, 220 keV for ⁸⁰Sr, and 45 keV for ¹⁸⁶Pb.
- The energy differences between the plateau condition results and those obtained with the orthonormality criterion are not larger than 200 keV.

## Context

This work extends the standard plateau condition approach used in GCM calculations with energy density functionals (Bonche 1990, Robledo 2018) by providing a method to pre-select intrinsic states without computing costly Hamiltonian overlaps. The approach is related to the Monte Carlo Shell Model (Shimizu 2021) and the discretized non-orthogonal shell model (Dao 2022), which also incorporate selection processes but require Hamiltonian matrix elements. The saturation of the natural basis size demonstrated here enables more efficient PGCM calculations and provides a systematic way to determine the optimal number of intrinsic states for a given generating coordinate interval, with potential applications to excited states and other collective degrees of freedom.
