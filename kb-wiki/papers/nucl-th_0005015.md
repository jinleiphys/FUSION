# The $π$, $K^+$, and $K^0$ electromagnetic form factors

> The rainbow truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is combined with the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson amplitudes and the dressed quark-photon vertex in a self-consistent Poincaré-invariant study of the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors in impulse approximation. We demonstrate explicitly that the current is conserved in this approach and that the obtained results are independent of the momentum partitioning in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. With model gluon parameters previously fixed by the condensate, the pion mass and decay constant, and the kaon mass, the charge radii and spacelike form factors are found to be in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with all parameters fixed by the chiral condensate, pion mass and decay constant, and kaon mass, produces pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors and charge radii in good agreement with experimental data, provided that the dressed quark-photon vertex is solved from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than approximated by the Ball-Chiu Ansatz.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation, the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson amplitudes in ladder truncation, and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex, all with the same effective gluon interaction. The meson electromagnetic form factors are then calculated in impulse approximation using the dressed quark propagators, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and quark-photon vertex, with explicit verification of current conservation and independence from the momentum partitioning parameter eta in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius: calculated 0.45 fm^2, experimental 0.44 ± 0.01 fm^2
- Charged kaon charge radius: calculated 0.38 fm^2, experimental 0.34 ± 0.05 fm^2
- Neutral kaon charge radius: calculated -0.086 fm^2, experimental -0.054 ± 0.026 fm^2
- Pion form factor monopole fit mass: m^2 = 0.53 GeV^2 over the spacelike range up to Q^2 = 3 GeV^2
- Charged kaon form factor monopole fit mass: m^2 = 0.61 GeV^2 over the spacelike range up to Q^2 = 3 GeV^2
- Individual form factor radius for u-bar-u-u: r^2 = 0.45 fm^2
- Individual form factor radius for u-bar-s-u: r^2 = 0.47 fm^2
- Individual form factor radius for u-bar-s-bar-s: r^2 = 0.21 fm^2
- Model parameters: omega = 0.4 GeV, D = 0.93 GeV^2, Lambda_QCD = 0.234 GeV, renormalization point mu = 19 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger equation study of the pion form factor (Maris and Tandy, 1999) to include charged and neutral kaon form factors, using the same model parameters without adjustment. The approach contrasts with earlier semi-phenomenological studies using confined quark propagators and the Ball-Chiu vertex (Burkardt, Roberts, and Tandy, 1996), which produced a pion charge radius about 50% too small. The results are qualitatively similar to those of Choi and Ji (1999), particularly for the neutral kaon, and the method enables predictions for the Q^2 range up to 3-4 GeV^2 that will be tested by upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments.
