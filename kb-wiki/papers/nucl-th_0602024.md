# Production of strange particles at intermediate pT at RHIC

> The recombination model is applied to the production of $K, ϕ, Λ$ and $Ω$ at all $p_T$ in central Au+Au collisions. The thermal-shower component of the recombination is found to be important for $K$ and $Λ$, but only in a minor way for $ϕ$ and $Ω$ in the intermediate to high $p_T$ region. The normalization and inverse slope of the thermal partons in the strange sector are determined by fitting the low-$p_T$ data. At higher $p_T$ the data of $K, ϕ, Λ$ and $Ω$ in the log scale are all well reproduced in our study that extends the thermal contribution and includes the shower contribution. The calculated result on the $Λ/K$ ratio rises to a maximum of around 2 at $p_T\approx 4$ GeV/c, arching over the data in linear scale. The production of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ are shown to arise mainly from the recombination of thermal partons, thus exhibiting exponential $p_T$ dependences in agreement with the data. Their ratio, $R_{Ω/ϕ}$, rises linearly to $p_T\approx 4$ GeV/c and develops a maximum at $p_T\approx 5.5$ GeV/c. It is argued that the $p_T$ spectra of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ reveal directly the partonic nature of the thermal source that characterizes quark-gluon plasma. Comments are made on the $Ω$ puzzle due to the simultaneous observation of both the exponential behavior of the $Ω$ spectrum in $p_T$ and the existence of low-$p_T$ particles associated with $Ω$ as trigger.

## Key claim
The recombination model, when applied to strange particle production in central Au+Au collisions, shows that the production of phi and Omega mesons is dominated by the recombination of thermal strange partons, making their transverse-momentum spectra essentially exponential and providing a direct window into the thermal source that characterizes quark-gluon plasma. The calculated Lambda/K ratio rises to a maximum of around 2 at pT approximately 4 GeV/c, but the model does not reproduce the data at large pT with better than 30% accuracy.

## Method
The authors apply the recombination model to compute the invariant inclusive distributions of K, phi, Lambda, and Omega at all pT in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The thermal parton distributions for strange quarks are determined by fitting low-pT kaon data, while the shower parton distributions are taken from known fragmentation functions. The recombination functions for each hadron use valon-model wave functions, with statistical factors g_phi and g_Omega adjusted to fit the normalizations of the phi and Omega spectra.

## Key numbers
- Thermal strange quark distribution parameters: C_s = 13.9 GeV^{-1}, T_s = 0.33 GeV (compared to C = 23.2 GeV^{-1}, T = 0.317 GeV for light quarks)
- The Lambda/K ratio reaches a maximum of approximately 2 at pT ≈ 4 GeV/c
- The Omega/phi ratio rises linearly to pT ≈ 4 GeV/c and develops a maximum at pT ≈ 5.5 GeV/c, with R_{Omega/phi} ≈ 0.25 at the peak
- Statistical factors for phi and Omega: g_phi = 0.34, g_Omega = 0.012
- The hard parton emergence fraction xi is 0.07, with k_0 set at 3 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously applied to pion and proton production by Hwa and Yang (2004) into the strange sector, treating K, phi, Lambda, and Omega in a unified framework. It builds on earlier studies of strangeness enhancement and the valon model description of hadron structure. The finding that phi and Omega spectra are dominated by thermal parton recombination enables the interpretation of these particles as direct probes of the thermal source in quark-gluon plasma, and raises the "Omega puzzle" concerning associated low-pT particles observed with Omega triggers, which the authors suggest may be explained by the concept of a phantom jet and ridge production.
