# Duality in the Color Flavor Locked Spectrum

> We analyze the spectrum of the massive states for the color flavor locked phase (CFL) of QCD. We show that the vector mesons have a mass of the order of the color superconductive gap $Δ$. We also see that the excitations associated with the solitonic sector of the CFL low energy theory have a mass proportional to $F^2_π/Δ$ and hence are expected to play no role for the physics of the CFL phase for large chemical potential. Another interesting point is that the product of the soliton mass and the vector meson mass is independent of the gap. We interpret this behavior as a form of electromagnetic duality in the sense of Montonen and Olive. Our approach for determining the properties of the massive states is non-perturbative in nature and can be applied to any theory with multiple scales.

# Duality in the Color Flavor Locked Spectrum

## Key claim
The paper argues that in the color flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD, vector meson masses scale with the superconducting gap Δ while soliton masses scale with Fπ²/Δ, and that the product of these two masses is independent of Δ, a duality analogous to Montonen-Olive electromagnetic duality. This result is tree-level and may be affected by quantum corrections.

## Method
The authors use a non-perturbative approach based on matching higher-derivative operators in the low-energy effective chiral Lagrangian for CFL Goldstone bosons to operators obtained by integrating out heavy vector mesons. By comparing the coefficients of four-derivative terms (including the Skyrme term) in the CFL chiral expansion with those from a hidden local symmetry Lagrangian for vector mesons, they extract the scaling behavior of the vector meson coupling g̃ ∝ Δ/Fπ and the Skyrme coefficient e ∝ Δ/Fπ. The soliton mass is then computed via the hedgehog ansatz in the Skyrme model.

## Key numbers
- Vector meson mass: m_v ∼ Δ, where Δ is the color superconductive gap
- Soliton mass: M_soliton ∼ Fπ²/Δ, where Fπ is the Goldstone boson decay constant
- Product relation: M_soliton × m_v ∼ Fπ², independent of Δ
- Vector meson coupling scaling: g̃ ∝ Δ/Fπ
- Skyrme coefficient scaling: e ∝ Δ/Fπ, with e = √2 g̃
- In vacuum reference values: Fπ ≈ 132 MeV, g̃ ≈ 3.96, g_{ρππ} ≈ 8.56
- Duality-continuity relation: M_N(μ) m_v(μ) / (2π Fπ(μ))² = M_N(0) m_v(0) / (2π Fπ(0))² ∼ 1.05

## Context
This work extends the analysis of massive states in the CFL phase beyond perturbative approaches used by Casalbuoni (2000) and Rho (2000), providing a non-perturbative method applicable to any theory with multiple scales. The results contradict the naive expectation that all massive states scale with Δ. The duality interpretation connects to the Montonen-Olive (1977) duality in SU(2) Georgi-Glashow theory. The authors relate their findings to quark-hadron continuity as proposed by Schäfer (1998), and note that while vector mesons are relevant for compact star physics with a CFL core, solitons can be neglected at large chemical potential.
