# Mean free path of photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Electromagnetic probes, such as photons and dileptons, play a key role in diagnosing the initial temperature of the hot and dense quark-gluon plasma (QGP) matter created in relativistic nuclear collisions at very high energies. This is due to their large mean free path $λ$, which allows them to escape the medium without significant interactions. Unlike hadronic particles, which experience multiple scatterings and are affected by the evolving medium, electromagnetic probes carry undistorted information from the initial stages of the expanding system. In this work an attempt has been made to revisit the estimation of mean free paths of photons in QGP phase for a temperature range predicted by hydrodynamics for heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC. The mean free paths have been estimated for a plasma expanding via (1+1)D and (2+1)D hydrodynamical expansions. For the (1+1)D case, photons with low energy ($E_γ< 0.2$ GeV) coming from a high temperature ($>250$ MeV) source are found to have shorter mean free path compared to the expansion scale of the system; while the high energy photons have always larger mean free paths. A similar qualitative nature of the mean free path has also been observed for a more realistic (2+1)D hydrodynamic model calculations although the $λ$ values are found to be larger on a quantitative scale compared to the (1+1)D case.

## Key claim

The authors revisit the estimation of photon mean free paths in the quark-gluon plasma phase using updated photon production rates and expanding hydrodynamic models, finding that while most photons have mean free paths much larger than the system size, low-energy photons (below 0.2 GeV) from high-temperature sources (above 250 MeV) may have shorter mean free paths compared to the expansion scale of the system, qualifying the usual assumption that all electromagnetic probes escape without significant interaction.

## Method

The mean free path of photons was calculated from the equilibration time, defined as the ratio of the equilibrium photon number density to the photon production rate, using leading-order QGP photon rates from Arnold et al. (2001) with parametrized corrections. The authors applied this formalism to Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV (LHC), evolving the medium via (1+1)D and (2+1)D ideal longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamic models with initial conditions tuned to reproduce experimental charged particle multiplicity and hadronic spectra.

## Key numbers

- For the (1+1)D hydrodynamic case, photons with energy below 0.2 GeV from sources above 250 MeV have mean free paths shorter than the expansion scale of the system.
- For the (2+1)D hydrodynamic model at RHIC (200A GeV), mean free path values are about 20% larger than those from the (1+1)D calculation.
- The typical system size in Au+Au collisions is of the order of 10-20 fm, which is much smaller than the mean free path of photons with energy greater than or equal to 0.2 GeV.
- Photon energies considered range from 0.2 to 3 GeV in the (2+1)D calculation at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the original calculation of photon mean free paths by Kapusta et al. (1991), who estimated mean free paths for static (non-expanding) QGP and hadron gas and proposed that photons of about 0.5 to several GeV could signal QGP formation. The present study incorporates the significant advances in photon production rates (Arnold et al., 2001; NLO rates) and hydrodynamic modeling that have occurred since that time, and it is the first to compute mean free paths for an expanding medium. The results reinforce the use of high-energy photons as clean probes of the early QGP stage, while identifying a regime where low-energy photons may not escape without distortion, which could inform the interpretation of future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC.
