# Missed prediction of the neutron halo in $^{37}$Mg

> Halo phenomena have long been an important frontier in both experimental and theoretical nuclear physics. $^{37}$Mg was identified as a halo nucleus in 2014 and remains the heaviest nuclear halo system to date. While the halo phenomenon in $^{37}$Mg was not predicted before the discovery, its description has been still challenging afterwards. In this Letter, we report a microscopic and self-consistent description of the neutron halo in $^{37}$Mg using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) that was developed in 2010. The experimental neutron separation energies and empirical matter radii of neutron-rich magnesium isotopes as well as the deformed $p$-wave halo characteristics of $^{37}$Mg are well reproduced without any free parameters. In particular, the orbital occupied by the halo neutron in $^{37}$Mg, exhibiting $p$-wave components comparable to those suggested in experiments, remains consistent across various employed density functionals including PC-F1, PC-PK1, NL3*, and PK1. The DRHBc theory investigated only even-even magnesium isotopes in previous works and for that reason missed predicting $^{37}$Mg as a halo nucleus before 2014. Although the core and the halo of $^{37}$Mg are both prolate, higher-order shape decoupling on the hexadecapole and hexacontatetrapole levels is predicted.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) provides a microscopic and self-consistent description of the neutron halo in \(^{37}\)Mg without free parameters, but the theory missed predicting this halo before its 2014 experimental discovery because it had previously only been applied to even-even magnesium isotopes.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis, incorporating axial deformation, pairing correlations, and continuum effects self-consistently. They applied the equal filling approximation to treat the blocking effect in the odd-mass nucleus \(^{37}\)Mg, and used four different density functionals (PC-F1, PC-PK1, NL3*, and PK1) to test the robustness of the results.

## Key numbers

- The PC-F1 calculated one-neutron separation energy for \(^{37}\)Mg is \(S_n = 0.32\) MeV, in agreement with the experimental value \(0.24^{+0.11}_{-0.11}\) MeV from AME2020.
- The ground-state deformation of \(^{37}\)Mg from DRHBc calculations is \(\beta_2 = 0.46\).
- The root-mean-square matter radius of \(^{37}\)Mg is 3.57 fm, compared to the empirical value of 3.62(03) fm.
- The valence neutron occupies a \(1/2^-\) orbital with \(38\%\) \(2p_{1/2}\) and \(19\%\) \(2p_{3/2}\) components, comparable to the \(\approx 40\%\) \(p\)-wave neutron halo components suggested in experiments.
- The rms radius of the halo orbital (orbital 5) is approximately 5.6 fm, far exceeding the total matter radius of 3.57 fm.
- The hexadecapole and hexacontatetrapole deformation parameters for the halo are \(\beta_4 = -1.27\) and \(\beta_6 = -2.21\), while for the core they are \(\beta_4 = 0.23\) and \(\beta_6 = 0.01\).

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc theory, previously applied only to even-even magnesium isotopes (Zhou 2010), to odd-mass nuclei and successfully describes the deformed \(p\)-wave halo of \(^{37}\)Mg, which was identified experimentally by Kobayashi (2014) and Takechi (2014). Prior theoretical efforts using Woods-Saxon potentials (Xu 2015, Sun 2020) required deformation parameters \(\beta_2 \gtrsim 0.5\) to favor the \(p\)-wave configuration, while AMD calculations (Watanabe 2014, Choudhary 2021) underestimated the matter radius of \(^{37}\)Mg. The results enable future predictions of halo nuclei and other exotic phenomena, and a Glauber model study using DRHBc densities as microscopic inputs is in progress.
