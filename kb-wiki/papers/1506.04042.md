# New insights on the hyperon puzzle from quantum Monte Carlo calculations

> In the last years auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo has been used to assess the properties of hypernuclear systems, from light- to medium-heavy hypernuclei and hyper-neutron matter. One of the main findings is the key role played by the three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interaction in the determination of the hyperon separation energy of hypernuclei and as a possible solution to the hyperon puzzle. However, there are still aspects of the employed hypernuclear potential that remain to be carefully investigated. In this paper we show that the isospin dependence of the Lambda-NN force, which is crucial in determining the NS structure, is poorly constrained by the available experimental data.

# New insights on the hyperon puzzle from quantum Monte Carlo calculations

## Key claim
The isospin dependence of the three-body Lambda-nucleon-nucleon (ΛNN) force, which is crucial for determining neutron star structure, is poorly constrained by available experimental data on hypernuclei. While the authors' auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations show that a repulsive ΛNN interaction can prevent hyperon appearance in neutron star cores (potentially solving the hyperon puzzle), the strength of the isospin triplet component of this force cannot be reliably determined from current hypernuclear separation energies alone.

## Method
The authors performed AFDMC calculations for hypernuclei from ⁴_ΛH to ⁴⁹_ΛCa using a Hamiltonian with Argonne AV4' two-nucleon plus Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials, and an Urbana-type ΛN potential with a three-body ΛNN interaction decomposed into 2π-exchange P-wave, S-wave, and spin-dependent dispersive terms. They systematically varied the parameter C_T, which gauges the strength and sign of the isospin triplet component of the ΛNN force, and computed the resulting Λ separation energies B_Λ for several hypernuclei to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies for ⁴He: -26.63(3) MeV (AV4'+UIX_c) vs. experimental -28.296 MeV (6% difference)
- Binding energies for ¹⁶O: -119.9(2) MeV (AV4'+UIX_c) vs. experimental -127.619 MeV (6% difference)
- Binding energies for ⁴⁰Ca: -382.9(6) MeV (AV4'+UIX_c) vs. experimental -342.051 MeV (12% difference)
- Binding energies for ⁴⁸Ca: -414.2(6) MeV (AV4'+UIX_c) vs. experimental -416.001 MeV (0.5% difference)
- The reduced pion mass 1/μ_π ≈ 1.4 fm
- The C_T parameter was varied in the range [-2, 3]; sensitivity of B_Λ is not large over the interval -1.0 ≤ C_T ≤ 1.5, with strong deviations beyond this range
- For ⁴⁹_ΛCa, B_Λ tends to decrease with respect to the original value (C_T=1) when C_T deviates, unlike other hypernuclei where B_Λ increases

## Context
This work extends the program started by Bodmer (1984) and continued by Usmani and collaborators (1995-2008) using variational Monte Carlo, now employing AFDMC as developed by Schmidt (1999) and Carlson (2014). The hyperon puzzle refers to the inconsistency between neutron star observations of ~2 M_⊙ masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and theoretical predictions that hyperon appearance softens the equation of state too much. The authors' previous results (Lonardoni 2013, 2014, 2015) showed that a repulsive ΛNN interaction might solve this puzzle, but this paper demonstrates that the isospin dependence remains unconstrained. Future experiments on highly asymmetric hypernuclei such as ⁴⁹_ΛCa, ⁹¹_ΛZr, or ²⁰⁹_ΛPb would be needed to pin down the isospin triplet component of the ΛNN force.
