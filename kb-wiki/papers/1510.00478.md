# Lightest Kaonic Nuclear Clusters

> We present our study of kaonic three-body $\overset{\_}{K}NN$, $\overset{\_}{K}\overset{\_}{K}N$ and $KK\overset{\_}{K}$ and four-body $\overset{\_}{K}% NNN $, $\ $and $\overset{\_}{K}\overset{\_}{K}NN$ clusters within the framework of a potential model using the method of hyperspherical functions in momentum representation. To perform a numerical calculations for the bound state energy of the light kaonic system, we use a set of different potentials for the nucleon-nucleon and $\overset{\_}{K}N$ interactions, as well as for the kaon-kaon interaction. The calculations show that a quasibound state energy is not sensitive to the $NN$ interaction, and it shows very strong dependence on the $\overset{\_}{K}N$ potential. We also compare our results with those obtained using different theoretical approaches. The theoretical discrepancies in the binding energy and width for the lightest kaonic system related to the different $NN$ and $\overset{\_% }{K}N$ interactions are addressed.

## Key claim

The binding energy and width of light kaonic nuclear clusters depend almost entirely on the choice of the antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) interaction, while the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction contributes only a weak dependence of about 3-15%; this conclusion holds for both strongly bound and weakly bound systems, contrary to earlier suggestions that the NN dependence might be significant only for weak binding.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic three-body and four-body calculations using the method of hyperspherical harmonics in momentum representation, solving coupled integral equations for hyperradial functions. They employed several NN potentials (Argonne V14, Argonne V18, Malfliet-Tjon MT-I-III, Tamagaki G3RS, and the M potential) together with two different Kbar N interactions: the phenomenological Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) potential and the energy-dependent chiral HW potential based on chiral SU(3) dynamics, as well as kaon-kaon interactions for systems with multiple kaons.

## Key numbers

- For the Kbar NN (K-pp) cluster: binding energy 46.2-46.5 MeV with AY potential across AV14, MT, and T NN potentials; binding energy 17.2-20.6 MeV with HW potential across the same NN potentials
- For the Kbar Kbar N (K-K-p) cluster: binding energy 31.7 MeV with AY potential and 11.43 MeV with HW potential; root-mean-square radius of K- distribution 1.36 fm (AY) and 1.96 fm (HW)
- For the KK Kbar system: mass ranges from 1463.4 to 1469.4 MeV across parameter sets A1, A2, B1, B2, and separable potential; width ranges from 82.2 to 98.2 MeV
- For the Kbar NNN (K-ppn) cluster with J^pi=1/2-, T=0: binding energy 92.1 MeV (AV18+AY) and 28.6 MeV (AV18+HW); width 83.4 MeV (AV18+AY) and 30.3 MeV (AV18+HW)
- For the Kbar Kbar NN (K-K-pp) cluster: binding energy 91.6 MeV (AV18+AY) and 31.5 MeV (AV18+HW); width 72.4 MeV (AV18+AY) and 78.1 MeV (AV18+HW)

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of kaonic clusters using variational methods (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002; Dote et al., 2004), Faddeev equations (Ikeda and Sato, 2007; Oset et al., 2010), and hyperspherical harmonics in configuration space (Barnea, Gal, and Liverts, 2012). The results confirm that the chiral HW potential yields systematically weaker binding than the phenomenological AY potential, and the authors find reasonable agreement with the configuration-space HH calculations of Barnea et al. (2012) for the K-ppn and K-K-pp systems. The study enables systematic comparison of theoretical approaches and supports the interpretation of the K(1460) resonance as a dynamically generated three-kaon state.
