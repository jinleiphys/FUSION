# Probing QCD critical fluctuations from light nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the coalescence model for light nuclei production, we show that the yield ratio $\mathcal{O}_\text{p-d-t} = N_{^3\text{H}} N_p / N_\text{d}^2$ of $p$, d, and $^3$H in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the neutron relative density fluctuation $Δn= \langle (δn)^2\rangle/\langle n\rangle^2$ at kinetic freeze-out. From recent experimental data in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.3$~GeV, $7.6$~GeV, $8.8$~GeV, $12.3$~GeV and $17.3$~GeV measured by the NA49 Collaboration at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS), we find a possible non-monotonic behavior of $Δn$ as a function of the collision energy with a peak at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.8$~GeV, indicating that the density fluctuations become the largest in collisions at this energy. With the known chemical freeze-out conditions determined from the statistical model fit to experimental data, we obtain a chemical freeze-out temperature of $\sim 144~$MeV and baryon chemical potential of $\sim 385~$MeV at this collision energy, which are close to the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram predicted by various theoretical studies. Our results thus suggest the potential usefulness of the yield ratio of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a direct probe of the large density fluctuations associated with the QCD critical phenomena.

## Key claim

The yield ratio O_p-d-t = N_3H N_p / N_d^2 of protons, deuterons, and tritons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the neutron relative density fluctuation Δn at kinetic freeze-out, and analysis of NA49 data shows a possible non-monotonic behavior of Δn with a peak at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV, but the statistical evidence is weak (about 2.5 sigma deviation from a constant fit) and requires future confirmation.

## Method

The authors used the coalescence model COAL-SH to derive analytical formulas for deuteron and triton yields that include the effect of neutron density fluctuations. They expressed the yields in terms of the neutron relative density fluctuation Δn = ⟨(δn)^2⟩/⟨n⟩^2 and a correlation coefficient α between neutron and proton density fluctuations, then constructed the yield ratio O_p-d-t which cancels dependence on effective temperature, volume, and proton number. They extracted Δn from published NA49 data on p, d, and 3H yields in central Pb+Pb collisions at five SPS energies, assuming various values of α between -0.2 and 0.2.

## Key numbers

- The yield ratio O_p-d-t at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV is 0.541 ± 0.022, compared to 0.459 ± 0.014 at 6.3 GeV, 0.494 ± 0.020 at 7.6 GeV, 0.458 ± 0.038 at 12.3 GeV, and 0.484 ± 0.037 at 17.3 GeV.
- The extracted neutron relative density fluctuation Δn (for α=0) peaks at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV with value 0.864 ± 0.076, compared to 0.583 ± 0.048 at 6.3 GeV, 0.704 ± 0.068 at 7.6 GeV, 0.579 ± 0.130 at 12.3 GeV, and 0.668 ± 0.127 at 17.3 GeV.
- The chemical freeze-out conditions at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV are estimated as temperature T ~ 144 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B ~ 385 MeV.
- The oscillator frequencies used are ω_d = 8.1 MeV for deuteron and ω_3H = 13.4 MeV for triton, with RMS radii r_rms,d = 1.96 fm and r_rms,3H = 1.76 fm.
- The constant g in the yield ratio formula is approximately 0.29.

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) by proposing a new observable based on light nuclei yields, complementing previous studies of event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities (e.g., net-proton κσ^2 measured by STAR at BES) and non-monotonic behavior in shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (Cseko 2006, Lacey 2007), expansion speed (Hung 1995, Rischke 1996), and direct flow of light clusters (Batra 2016, Basu 2016). The result suggests that the CEP may be located near T ~ 144 MeV and μ_B ~ 385 MeV, consistent with predictions from lattice QCD (Fodor 2004) and Dyson-Schwinger equation (Xin 2014), and the peak energy sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 GeV coincides with the peak in the K+/π+ ratio measured by NA49 (Alt 2008). Future measurements from the RHIC BES program with higher precision are needed to confirm the non-monotonic behavior and precisely locate the CEP.
