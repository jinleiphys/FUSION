# Photon emission from quark-gluon plasma out of equilibrium

> The photon emission from a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is analyzed. We derive an integral equation that describes photon production through quark-antiquark annihilation and quark bremsstrahlung. It includes coherence between different scattering sites, also known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect. These leading-order processes are studied for the first time together in an out-of-equilibrium field theoretical treatment that enables the inclusion of viscous corrections to the calculation of electromagnetic emission rates. In the special case of an isotropic, viscous, plasma the integral equation only depends on three constants which capture the non-equilibrium nature of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper derives an integral equation for photon production from a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma that includes both quark-antiquark annihilation and quark bremsstrahlung with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, but only for systems with sufficiently low anisotropy (approximately \(|\xi| \lesssim g^2\)) such that the equilibrium power counting scheme remains valid and instabilities in the soft gluon propagator do not appear at leading order.

## Method

The authors use the real-time formalism in the \(r/a\) basis to derive resummed propagators for a translationally invariant, homogeneous, and static brick of out-of-equilibrium QGP. They sum diagrams with an arbitrary number of soft gluon exchanges between the quark and the medium during photon emission, which constitutes the LPM effect, without relying on the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) relation that describes detailed balance in thermal equilibrium. The derivation yields an integral equation that captures the leading-order inelastic channels for photon production, where the non-equilibrium nature of the medium is encoded in a general momentum distribution function \(f(\mathbf{p})\).

## Key numbers

* For an anisotropic momentum distribution of the form \(f(\mathbf{p}) = f_{\mathrm{eq}}\left( \sqrt{p^2 + \xi (\mathbf{n}\cdot\mathbf{p})^2} \right)\), the analysis requires \(|\xi| \lesssim g^2\) to ensure that instabilities in the soft gluon propagator remain subleading at leading order in the coupling constant.
* In the special case of an isotropic, viscous plasma, the integral equation depends on only three constants that capture the non-equilibrium nature of the medium.

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium treatment of photon production from the QGP by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001, 2001b), who first consistently included the LPM effect for a thermal medium, to out-of-equilibrium systems. It also builds on earlier non-equilibrium studies of two-to-two scattering channels by Dion et al. (2011), Shen et al. (2015), and Hauksson et al. (2016) for shear and bulk viscous corrections. The field-theoretical approach differs from the effective kinetic theory used by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003) and enables the inclusion of viscous corrections to electromagnetic emission rates for the first time in a treatment that simultaneously handles both inelastic channels with the LPM effect.
