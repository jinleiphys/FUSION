# Influence of the halo upon angular distributions for elastic scattering and breakup

> The angular distributions for elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei are analysed using a near-side/far-side decomposition within the framework of the dynamical eikonal approximation. This analysis is performed for 11Be impinging on Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon. These distributions exhibit very similar features. In particular they are both near-side dominated, as expected from Coulomb-dominated reactions. The general shape of these distributions is sensitive mostly to the projectile-target interactions, but is also affected by the extension of the halo. This suggests the elastic scattering not to be affected by a loss of flux towards the breakup channel.

## Key claim

The angular distributions for elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei exhibit very similar features and are both near-side dominated, but the general shape of these distributions is sensitive mostly to the projectile-target interactions and only slightly affected by the extension of the halo, suggesting that elastic scattering is not affected by a loss of flux towards the breakup channel.

## Method

The authors performed a near-side/far-side (N/F) decomposition of angular distributions computed within the dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA) for the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be impinging on a Pb target at 69 MeV/nucleon. The DEA treats the projectile as a core (10Be) loosely bound to a neutron, solves the three-body Schrodinger equation using the eikonal ansatz, and provides transition matrix elements for both elastic scattering and breakup. The N/F decomposition was obtained by splitting the Bessel functions in the transition matrix elements into Hankel functions, with the near side corresponding to positive deflection (repulsive forces) and the far side to negative deflection (attractive forces).

## Key numbers

- 11Be impinging on Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon was the system studied
- The 11Be ground state has a neutron separation energy of 0.5 MeV, with the halo neutron in the 1s1/2 orbital
- The elastic-scattering cross section shows a N/F crossover at about 8 degrees
- Total breakup cross section for the full Coulomb+nuclear interaction with 0.5 MeV binding energy: 1.70 b
- Total absorption cross section for the full Coulomb+nuclear interaction with 0.5 MeV binding energy: 3.87 b
- Total breakup cross section for the point-sphere Coulomb interaction only: 2.10 b
- Total breakup cross section for the point-point Coulomb interaction only: 2.58 b
- Varying the binding energy from 0.5 MeV to 50 keV increased the breakup cross section to 23.57 b; increasing to 5 MeV decreased it to 0.07 b

## Context

This work extends the near-side/far-side decomposition technique of Hussein and McVoy (1984) and Charity et al. (1985) to breakup angular distributions within the dynamical eikonal approximation framework developed by Capel, Baye, and collaborators (2005-2007). The analysis was motivated by experimental measurements of angular distributions for 11Be by Fukuda et al. (2004) and for 8B by Kikuchi et al. (1997). The results contradict earlier interpretations by Di Pietro et al. (2004, 2010) that the reduction of elastic-scattering cross section for halo nuclei around the Coulomb barrier is due to loss of flux to breakup; instead, the authors argue this reduction is a direct influence of the halo on the collision. The methodology is planned for application to low-energy reactions within the continuum discretised coupled channel (CDCC) model.
