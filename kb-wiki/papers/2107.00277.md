# Hyperon Dynamics in Heavy-ion Collisions near threshold energy

> Within the framework of quantum molecular dynamics transport model, the isospin and in-medium effects on the hyperon production in the reaction of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au are investigated thoroughly. A repulsive hyperon-nucleon potential from the chiral effective field theory is implemented into the model, which is related to the hyperon momentum and baryon density. The correction on threshold energy of the elementary hyperon cross section is taken into account. It is found that the $Σ$ yields are suppressed in the domain of midrapidity and kinetic energy spectra with the potential. The hyperons are emitted in the reaction plane because of the strangeness exchange reaction and reabsorption process in nuclear medium. The $Σ^{-}/Σ^{+}$ ratio depends on the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy, in particular in the high-energy region (above 500 MeV).

# Hyperon Dynamics in Heavy-ion Collisions near threshold energy

## Key claim
The repulsive hyperon-nucleon potential from chiral effective field theory suppresses Σ hyperon yields in midrapidity and kinetic energy spectra, while the Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratio depends on the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy, particularly at kinetic energies above 700 MeV where a roughly 5% difference between hard and soft symmetry energies is observed.

## Method
The authors implemented a repulsive Σ-nucleon optical potential from chiral effective field theory (next-to-leading order, cutoff 600 MeV) into the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model. They simulated central and semi-central collisions of ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au at incident energies of 1.5 A GeV and 2 A GeV, including threshold energy corrections for elementary hyperon production cross sections and coupling of resonances, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and mesons (π, η, K, K̅, ρ, ω) via meson-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of hyperon production in heavy-ion collisions using transport models, specifically addressing the repulsive ΣN potential calculated by chiral effective field theory (Ha15) and Nijmegen extended-soft-core potential model (Th13), which contrasts with earlier attractive potentials from relativistic mean-field models (Ru08, Lo09, Ba10). The results enable future experiments at the High Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in China to extract high-density symmetry energy information from Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratios, building on earlier proposals by Li (Li36) using π⁻/π⁺ ratios and UrQMD transport model studies (Li37) of Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratios. The collective flow calculations are compared with experimental data from the E895 Collaboration (Ch38) at AGS energies.
