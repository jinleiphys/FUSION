# The low-temperature behavior of the quark-meson model

> We revisit the phase diagram of strong-interaction matter for the two-flavor quark-meson model using the Functional Renormalization Group. In contrast to standard mean-field calculations, an unusual phase structure is encountered at low temperatures and large quark chemical potentials. In particular, we identify a regime where the pressure decreases with increasing temperature and discuss possible reasons for this unphysical behavior.

## Key claim

The Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) treatment of the two-flavor quark-meson model reveals a region at low temperatures and large quark chemical potentials where the entropy density becomes negative, which is thermodynamically unacceptable; this unphysical behavior is absent in standard mean-field calculations and appears robust against changes in parameters and regulator functions.

## Method

The authors solve the Wetterich flow equation for the effective average action within the local potential approximation (LPA) to compute the grand potential of the quark-meson model at finite temperature and chemical potential. From the grand potential they derive thermodynamic quantities including pressure, entropy density, and quark number density. Additionally, they compute real-time mesonic spectral functions for sigma and pion modes by analytically continuing the flow equations for the two-point functions from imaginary to real energies, using a two-step procedure involving the periodicity of occupation numbers followed by replacement of the Euclidean energy with a continuous real frequency.

## Key numbers

- The chiral critical point (CEP) is located at temperature \(T \approx 50\) MeV and chemical potential \(\mu \approx 345\) MeV.
- Vacuum parameters: pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.5\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 606\) MeV, constituent quark mass \(m_\psi = 388\) MeV.
- The ultraviolet cutoff scale is \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV, with UV parameters \(m_\Lambda/\Lambda = 0.969\), \(\lambda_\Lambda = 0.001\), \(c/\Lambda^3 = 0.00175\), and Yukawa coupling \(h = 4.2\).
- For the schematic NJL-type estimate of color superconductivity, the coupling in the 2SC channel is \(H_s \approx 1/140^2\) MeV\(^{-2}\), yielding \(H_s \Lambda^2 \approx 16\) for \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV, compared to typical NJL values of \(H_s \Lambda^2 \approx 2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier FRG studies of the quark-meson model phase diagram by Schaefer (2004) and Herbst (2013), which first observed the unusual curvature of the first-order transition line and decreasing pressure with increasing temperature. The negative entropy density region is connected to the positive slope of the first-order line via the Clausius-Clapeyron relation, and the authors identify two possible physical explanations: color superconductivity (known from NJL model studies by Buballa (2005)) and inhomogeneous chiral phases (studied in mean-field by Buballa and Carignano (2015)). The results suggest that future FRG calculations must include Nambu-Gorkov propagators for pairing or allow spatially modulated order parameters, and that nucleonic degrees of freedom will also be important at low temperatures.
