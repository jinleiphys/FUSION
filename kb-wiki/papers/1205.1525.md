# Apparent low-energy scale invariance in two-dimensional Fermi gases

> Recent experiments on a $\2d$ Fermi gas find an undamped breathing mode at twice the trap frequency over a wide range of parameters. To understand this seemingly scale-invariant behavior in a system with a scale, we derive two exact results valid across the entire BCS-BEC crossover at all temperatures. First, we relate the shift of the mode frequency from its scale-invariant value to $γ_d \equiv (1+2/d)P-ρ(\partial P/\partialρ)_s$ in $d$ dimensions. Next, we relate $γ_d$ to dissipation via a new low-energy bulk viscosity sum rule. We argue that $\2d$ is special, with its logarithmic dependence of the interaction on density, and thus $γ_2$ is small in both the BCS and BEC regimes, even though $P - 2\varepsilon/d$, sensitive to the dimer binding energy that breaks scale invariance, is not.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nearly undamped breathing mode at twice the trap frequency observed in two-dimensional Fermi gases over a wide range of parameters arises because the quantity gamma_2, which controls deviations from scale invariance, remains small across the entire BCS-BEC crossover even though other measures of scale-invariance breaking (such as P - 2*epsilon/d) are not small, due to the logarithmic dependence of the 2d coupling on density.

## Method

The authors derive two exact results valid across the entire BCS-BEC crossover at all temperatures. First, they relate the shift of the monopole breathing mode frequency from its scale-invariant value of 2*omega_0 to the parameter gamma_d = (1+2/d)P - rho*(partial P/partial rho)_s in d dimensions, using a variational scaling ansatz in the hydrodynamic Lagrangian. Second, they derive a new low-energy bulk viscosity sum rule that relates gamma_d to the subtracted spectral weight of the bulk viscosity, regularizing ultraviolet divergences by subtracting the zero-density two-body contribution scaled by the contact parameter C/C_0.

## Key numbers

- The breathing mode was observed at approximately 2*omega_0 without observable damping for temperatures 0.37 less than T/T_F less than 0.9 and interaction strengths 0 less than ln(k_F a_2) less than 500 in the experiment of Vogt (2012).
- A value of gamma_d as small as 0.1 n*epsilon_F would give rise to a 5% shift in the breathing mode frequency omega_m from 2*omega_0.
- The mean field theory result for gamma_2 in 2d is exactly zero for all couplings g_2, while the mean field result for P - epsilon = n|epsilon_b|/2 is not small in the BEC regime.
- The 2d beta function beta(g_2) = 1, while the 3d beta function beta(g_3) = g_3, reflecting the logarithmic versus power-law dependence on density.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of scale invariance in Fermi gases from the three-dimensional unitary limit, where scale invariance is exact at the Feshbach resonance (Son 2007, Taylor 2010), to the two-dimensional case where a dimer binding energy always exists and breaks scale invariance. The paper explains the surprising experimental observations of Vogt (2012) showing an undamped breathing mode in 2d Fermi gases, and contrasts the 2d behavior with the 3d case where gamma_3 becomes large in the BEC regime. The results enable future theoretical and experimental investigation of the regime near g_2 = 0 and provide a framework for understanding why certain quantum field theories with broken conformal invariance show near-scale-invariant behavior over wide energy ranges.
