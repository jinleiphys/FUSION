# Temperature and momentum dependence of single-particle properties in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> We have studied the effects of momentum dependent interactions on the single-particle properties of hot asymmetric nuclear matter. In particular, the single-particle potential of protons and neutrons as well as the symmetry potential have been studied within a self-consistent model using a momentum dependent effective interaction. In addition, the isospin splitting of the effective mass has been derived from the above model. In each case temperature effects have been included and analyzed. The role of the specific parametrization of the effective interaction used in the present work has been investigated. It has been concluded that the behavior of the symmetry potential depends strongly on the parametrization of the interaction part of the energy density and the momentum dependence of the regulator function. The effects of the parametrization have been found to be less pronounced on the isospin mass splitting.

# Temperature and momentum dependence of single-particle properties in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The behavior of the symmetry potential depends strongly on the parametrization of the interaction part of the energy density and the momentum dependence of the regulator function, while the effects of the parametrization are less pronounced on the isospin mass splitting. The study concludes that only two out of nine parameter combinations produce a symmetry potential that decreases with increasing nucleon momentum in agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The study employs a self-consistent momentum-dependent effective interaction model (BGBD model) to calculate single-particle potentials for protons and neutrons, the symmetry potential, and the isospin splitting of the effective mass in hot asymmetric nuclear matter. Temperature effects are included through Fermi-Dirac distribution functions in the kinetic energy density, while the interaction part of the energy density uses a parametrization with local contact terms and finite-range terms regulated by two different momentum-dependent functions g(k,Λ_i). Three different forms for the interaction part of the nuclear symmetry energy (F_1(u)=√u, F_2(u)=u, F_3(u)=2u²/(1+u)) and three values of nuclear incompressibility (K=120, 180, 240 MeV) are systematically compared.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Nuclear symmetry energy at T=0 parametrized as E_sym^tot(n,T=0) = 13 u^(2/3) + 17 F(u) MeV, where u = n/n₀
- Three values of nuclear incompressibility studied: K = 120, 180, 240 MeV
- Asymmetry parameter I = 0.4 used in symmetry potential calculations
- Finite range parameters: Λ₁ = 1.5 k_F⁰ and Λ₂ = 3 k_F⁰, where k_F⁰ is the Fermi momentum at saturation
- Temperature effects become more pronounced for T > 5 MeV
- Empirical symmetry potential parametrization: U_sym(E_kin) = a - b E_kin with a ≈ 22-34 MeV and b = 0.1-0.2

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear symmetry energy at finite temperature by Moustakidis (2007) and applies the BGBD model originally introduced by Gale et al. (1987) and Bertsch et al. (1988) to study momentum-dependent interactions in heavy ion collisions. The study builds on earlier work by Li et al. (2004) and Rizzo et al. (2004) who employed a similar phenomenological non-relativistic effective interaction. The results are relevant for understanding neutron star properties, heavy ion collisions at radioactive beam facilities, and the composition of hot β-stable nuclear matter. The paper aims to clarify the controversial theoretical predictions for the neutron-proton effective mass splitting and the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential, which has been studied experimentally through nucleon-nucleus scattering and (p,n) charge-exchange reactions up to 100 MeV.
