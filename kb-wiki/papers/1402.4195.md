# Electron g-2 in Light-Front Quantization

> Basis Light-front Quantization has been proposed as a nonperturbative framework for solving quantum field theory. We apply this approach to Quantum Electrodynamics and explicitly solve for the light-front wave function of a physical electron. Based on the resulting light-front wave function, we evaluate the electron anomalous magnetic moment. Nonperturbative mass renormalization is performed. Upon extrapolation to the infinite basis limit our numerical results agree with the Schwinger result obtained in perturbation theory to an accuracy of 0.06%.

## Key claim
The authors compute the electron anomalous magnetic moment nonperturbatively using Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) with only the electron and electron-photon Fock sectors retained. After rescaling by the electron wave-function renormalization factor \(Z_2\), their extrapolated numerical results agree with the perturbative Schwinger result \(a_e = \alpha/(2\pi)\) to within 0.06%, but only after this rescaling is applied to compensate for the Ward identity violation caused by the Fock-space truncation.

## Method
The authors construct the light-front QED Hamiltonian in a basis of 2D harmonic oscillator states for transverse degrees of freedom and plane waves for longitudinal motion, truncating to the \(\ket{e}\) and \(\ket{e\gamma}\) Fock sectors. They diagonalize the resulting sparse Hamiltonian matrix using ARPACK on supercomputers, with basis sizes up to 28 billion states, and perform nonperturbative mass renormalization by iteratively adjusting the bare electron mass until the ground-state mass matches the physical electron mass \(M = 0.511\) MeV. The anomalous magnetic moment is extracted from the Pauli form factor \(F_2(q^2 \to 0)\) using the Drell-Yan-West frame, and the raw result is rescaled by dividing by \(Z_2\) (the probability of finding a bare electron in the physical electron state) to restore the Ward identity.

## Key numbers
- The extrapolated numerical result for the electron anomalous magnetic moment agrees with the Schwinger result \(a_e = \alpha/(2\pi)\) to an accuracy of **0.06%** in the infinite basis limit.
- The maximum basis dimensionality achieved is **28,027,289,920** at truncation parameters \(N_\text{max} = K - 1/2 = 640\).
- The physical electron mass used for renormalization is \(M = 0.511\) MeV, which also sets the 2D harmonic oscillator basis parameter \(b = M\).
- Calculations are performed at two values of the electromagnetic coupling constant: the physical value \(\alpha = 1/137.036\) and an artificially enlarged value \(\alpha = 1/\pi\).

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to QED in a cavity with an external transverse harmonic-oscillator potential by Honkanen et al. (2010). The present calculation corrects several issues in that earlier work, including the operator used for extracting the anomalous magnetic moment, a numerical error that overestimated interaction terms by a factor of 2, and the omission of wave-function renormalization. The greatly expanded basis (up to 28 billion states versus ~70 thousand in the earlier work) enables a precision test of BLFQ against the Schwinger result (Schwinger, 1948). This nonperturbative framework can be extended to include higher Fock sectors and applied to strongly interacting systems such as QCD bound states.
