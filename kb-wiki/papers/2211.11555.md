# Toy model to understand oscillatory behavior in timelike nucleon form factors

> To understand the oscillatory behavior exhibited in the timelike electromagnetic form factors of nucleons, we propose a toy model based on the Jost function of the $N\bar N$ pair into the timelike form factors with the help of the distorted-wave Born approximation. By constructing a simple square-well potential reflecting the final-state interaction of $N\bar N$, we naturally represent the damped oscillatory phenomenon in the timelike electromagnetic form factors of nucleons. Especially, our study reveals that the ``period" of the oscillation is approximately determined by the Yukawa interaction range $1/m_π$. Other possible potentials are also discussed. The threshold enhancements of the cross sections for $e^+e^-\to n\bar n$, $Λ\barΛ$, and $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ can also be understand within this scenario.

# Toy model to understand oscillatory behavior in timelike nucleon form factors

## Key claim

The damped oscillatory behavior observed in timelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors arises naturally from the final-state interaction (FSI) of the $N\bar{N}$ pair, modeled via a square-well potential in the distorted-wave Born approximation; the oscillation "period" is approximately determined by the Yukawa interaction range $1/m_\pi$, but the exact shape depends on the choice of the smooth background $G_0$, which remains poorly understood.

## Method

The authors construct a toy model where the $e^+e^- \to N\bar{N}$ cross section is factorized as $\sigma = \sigma_0 / |\mathcal{J}(p)|^2$, with $\mathcal{J}(p)$ the Jost function of the $N\bar{N}$ system under a purely real (no annihilation) potential. They solve the radial Schrödinger equation for S-wave scattering using a square-well potential (and several variants) to compute the enhancement/suppression factor $1/|\mathcal{J}|^2$, then multiply this factor by a smooth perturbative QCD parametrization $G_0(s)$ to reproduce the effective form factor $|G_{\mathrm{eff}}|$ data from BESIII and BaBar.

## Key numbers

- The first energy gap between oscillation minima is $\Delta E_1 \approx 0.6$ GeV, derived from $\mu = m_N/2$ and $a \approx 1/m_\pi$ using the formula $\Delta E_1 = 3\pi^2/(2\mu a^2)$.
- For the proton $p\bar{p}$ potential, the square-well parameters are: inner radius $a_r = 0.5$ fm, inner depth $V_r = 50$ MeV, outer radius $a = 1.6$ fm, outer depth $V_a = 90$ MeV.
- For the neutron $n\bar{n}$ potential: $a_r = 0.5$ fm, $V_r = 400$ MeV, $a = 1.4$ fm, $V_a = 650$ MeV.
- The smooth background $G_0$ uses $m_a^2 = 7.72$ GeV$^2$, with fitted constants $\mathcal{A}_p = 9.37$ and $\mathcal{A}_n = 5.8$.
- The SND measurement observes neutron cross-section enhancement at $\sqrt{s} - 2m_n \approx 5$ MeV.
- Threshold enhancements for $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ occur at $\sqrt{s} - 2m_\Lambda \approx 1$ MeV, and for $\Lambda_c\bar\Lambda_c$ at $\sqrt{s} - 2m_{\Lambda_c} \approx 1.58$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Bianconi (2015) that timelike proton form factors show an unexpected oscillation, later confirmed by BESIII (2019) for protons and BESIII (2021) and SND (2022) for neutrons. Prior explanations invoked meson resonances (Cao 2021, Lorenz 2015) but required large $N\bar{N}$ widths that may be problematic, or used optical potentials of special form (Bianconi 2015). The present toy model provides a unified, parameterizable FSI mechanism that also explains threshold enhancements in $n\bar{n}$, $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$, and $\Lambda_c\bar\Lambda_c$ production, and can be extended to other baryon-antibaryon channels where enhancements are absent (e.g., $\Xi\bar\Xi$, $\Sigma\bar\Sigma$).
