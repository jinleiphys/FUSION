# Theory of inclusive breakup cross section for Borromean nuclei within a four-body spectator model

> We develop a model to treat the inclusive non-elastic break up reactions involving weakly bound three-cluster nuclei. Borromean, two-nucleon, halo nuclei are candidates of unstable three-fragments projectiles. The model is based on the theory of inclusive breakup reactions commonly employed in the treatment of incomplete fusion and surrogate method. The theory was developed in the 80's by Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (IAV) [Phys. Rev. C 32, 431 (1985)] \cite{IAV1985}, Udagawa and Tamura (UT)[Phys. Rev. C 24, 1348 (1981)], \cite{UT1981} and Hussein and McVoy (HM)[Nucl. Phys. A 445, 124 (1985)], \cite{HM1985}. We extend these three-body theories to derive an expression for the fragment yield in the reaction $A\,(a,b)\,X$, where the projectile is $a = x_1 + x_2 + b$. The inclusive breakup cross section is found to be the sum of a generalized four-body form of the elastic breakup cross section plus the inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section which involves the "reaction" cross section of the participant fragments, $x_1$ and $x_2$. The final result is similar to the three-body case reviewed in Austern, et al. (Phys. Rep. \textbf{154}, 125 (1987)), \cite{Austern1987}, but with important genuine four-body effects added, both in the elastic breakup cross section, which now contains the full correlations between the participant fragments, and in the inclusive non- elastic breakup. These developments should encourage experimentalists to seek more information about the $x_{1} + x_{2}$ system in the elastic breakup cross section, and to theorists to further develop and extend the surrogate method, based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the $b$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper extends the three-body inclusive breakup theory (IAV, UT, HM) to a four-body spectator model for Borromean (three-cluster) projectiles, deriving an expression for the fragment yield in the reaction \(A\,(a,b)\,X\) where the projectile is \(a = x_1 + x_2 + b\). The inclusive breakup cross section is the sum of a generalized four-body elastic breakup term plus an inclusive non-elastic breakup term that contains genuine four-body effects, including full correlations between the participant fragments and a new three-body absorption term.

## Method

The authors start from the full four-body Hamiltonian for the \(x_1 + x_2 + b + A\) system and make the spectator-model approximations: the fragments \(x_1, x_2, b\) are treated as structureless, the interaction \(V_{bA}\) is replaced by a complex optical potential \(U_{bA}\), and the target is assumed much heavier so its kinetic energy is set to zero. They then derive the inclusive breakup cross section by expressing the exact four-body scattering wave function in terms of a three-body Green's function for the \(x_1 + x_2 + A\) subsystem, averaging over target ground state degrees of freedom to obtain an optical Green's function, and using an identity for the imaginary part of that Green's function. The final result separates into an elastic breakup term (Eq. 4BEB) and an inclusive non-elastic breakup term (Eq. CFH), the latter expressed as a sum of three contributions involving the imaginary parts of the optical potentials \(W_{x_1}\), \(W_{x_2}\), and a new three-body term \(W_{3B}\). The derivation is also recast using Faddeev-Yakubovsky decomposition to connect with the prior HM and UT formulations.

## Context

This work extends the three-body inclusive breakup theories of Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (1985), Udagawa and Tamura (1981), and Hussein and McVoy (1985) to four-body systems, specifically for Borromean two-neutron halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He, \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{14}\)Be, and \(^{22}\)C, as well as the stable three-cluster nucleus \(^9\)Be. The derived four-body elastic breakup formula contains full correlations between the two participant fragments, and the inclusive non-elastic breakup formula (called the CFH formula) introduces a genuine three-body absorption cross section \(\sigma_R^{3B}\) that has no analog in the three-body theory. The results enable experimentalists to extract information about the \(x_1 + x_2\) subsystem from elastic breakup measurements and provide a foundation for extending the surrogate method to reactions with three-fragment projectiles, addressing a limitation of the effective two-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels model which cannot calculate incomplete fusion.
