# Typical new spherical-like $γ$-soft spectra in $^{104,106,108}$Pd

> To solve the Cd puzzle (spherical nucleus puzzle), I have proposed a new spherical-like $γ$-soft nucleus. Since shape coexistence often occurs in such nuclei, explicit spherical-like $γ$-soft spectra are not easily identified. In this paper, I finally find the direct evidence for the existence of the new spherical-like $γ$-soft nucleus. $^{106}$Pd is in fact a typical spherical-like $γ$-soft nucleus. The low-lying parts, up to the $10_{1}^{+}$ state, under 4000 keV, of the spherical-like $γ$-soft spectra are verified. The B(E2) values and the quadrupole moments of the low-lying levels are also studied. By comparison, the new spherical-like $γ$-soft mode is confirmed. Moreover $^{104,108}$Pd are also discussed. These results completely disprove the possibility of the phonon excitations of the spherical nucleus in the Cd-Pd nuclei region.

# Typical new spherical-like γ-soft spectra in 104,106,108Pd

## Key claim

The paper claims that 106Pd is a "typical spherical-like γ-soft nucleus" whose low-lying spectra up to the 10₁⁺ state under 4000 keV directly confirm the existence of a new spherical-like γ-soft collective mode, but this claim is conditional on the identification of specific 0⁺ states and the exclusion of intruder-state mixing effects that obscure the pattern in neighboring Cd nuclei.

## Method

The author applied the SU3-IBM (interacting boson model with SU(3) higher-order interactions) with a Hamiltonian containing SU(3) Casimir operators and angular-momentum-dependent terms, fitting parameters η=0.47, γ=1.728, δ=1.34, and c=1177.42 keV to reproduce the low-lying level energies, B(E2) transition strengths, and quadrupole moments of 106Pd. The experimental data were taken from ENSDF and prior publications (Svensson 1995, Prados-Estévez 2017, Giannatiempo 1998/2018), and the theoretical results were compared against IBM-2 calculations and other collective models (spherical vibration, γ-soft rotation, rigid triaxial rotation).

## Key numbers

- 106Pd: level energies up to the 10₁⁺ state under 4000 keV are verified as spherical-like spectra
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 44.3(15) W.u. (experimental), 44.3 W.u. (theory)
- B(E2; 0₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 35(8) W.u. (experimental), 35.0 W.u. (theory)
- B(E2; 2₃⁺ → 0₂⁺) = 39(4) W.u. (experimental), 37.8 W.u. (theory), compared to IBM-2 value of 12.3 W.u.
- Quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺) = -0.51(7) eb (experimental), -0.47 eb (theory)
- Quadrupole moment Q(2₂⁺) = 0.39(4) eb (experimental), 0.31 eb (theory)
- The 0₅⁺ state in 106Pd at 2278 keV has energy nearly twice that of the 0₂⁺ state at 1134 keV (two-times relationship)
- The deviation σ of the new theory is 11.2 for B(E2) values, compared to 15.5 for IBM-2 (about 1.36 times larger for IBM-2)
- 108Pd: 0₂⁺ at 1053 keV, possible 0₃⁺ at 2141 keV (two-times candidate)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal of the spherical-like nucleus concept (Wang 2022) and the SU3-IBM framework (Wang 2022, Wang 2025) to resolve the "Cd puzzle" or "spherical nucleus puzzle" identified by Garrett and Wood (2010, 2016) and Heyde and Wood (2011, 2016), where the expected 0₃⁺ state at the three-phonon level is absent in Cd nuclei. The paper directly contradicts the phonon-excitation interpretation of spherical nuclei in the Cd-Pd region and shows that the SU3-IBM outperforms the IBM-2 (Giannatiempo 1998, 2018) in reproducing B(E2) values and quadrupole moments. The results enable future configuration-mixing calculations and extension to SU3-IBM-2 distinguishing protons and neutrons, and suggest that 108Cd, 110Cd, 112Cd, 114Cd, and 108Pd are candidates for similar spherical-like spectra.
