# Inside Mesons: Coupling Constants and Form Factors

> We illustrate the progress of covariant QCD phenomenology for the description of meson coupling constants and form factors. As examples, we discuss the $ρππ$ and $γπρ$ interactions, the $ρ$ contribution to the pion charge radius, and the $ρNN$ and $ωNN$ vector and tensor coupling constants and form factors.

## Key claim

Covariant QCD phenomenology based on Dyson-Schwinger equations can describe meson coupling constants and form factors without adjusting parameters beyond those already fixed by soft chiral quantities, but the detailed shape of calculated form factors depends upon the definition of the composite meson propagator and is always subject to a field redefinition.

## Method

The dressed quark propagators are parameterized from confining solutions of Dyson-Schwinger equation models that produce entire functions in the complex p²-plane with no mass-shell pole. Meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are obtained from a rank-2 separable ansatz for the ladder/rainbow kernel that preserves Goldstone's theorem. The ρππ, γπρ, and ρNN/ωNN vertices are calculated from skeleton graph expansions at the quark loop level, with the Ball-Chiu ansatz used for the photon vertex to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- g_{ρππ} = 6.28 (calculated) compared to experimental value 6.05 from ρ→ππ decay width
- g_{γπρ} = 0.5 (calculated) compared to experimental value 0.54 ± 0.03 from ρ decay
- Pion charge radius: (r_π^{GIA})² = 0.31 fm² from generalized impulse approximation, (r_π^{pole})² = 0.16 fm² from ρ pole contribution, total 0.47 fm² compared to experimental 0.44 fm²
- ρNN coupling: F₁^ρ(0) = 2.4 (g_{ρNN} = 3.3 extrapolated to mass-shell), empirical OBE value 2.6 (3.5)
- ωNN coupling: F₁^ω(0) = 7.2 (g_{ωNN} = 9.9 extrapolated), empirical OBE value 11.7 (16)
- ρ tensor coupling: F₂^ρ(0) = 9.7 (f_{ρNN} = 13.3 extrapolated), empirical OBE value 16.1 (22)
- κ_ρ = f_{ρNN}/g_{ρNN} = 4.0, empirical OBE value 6.1
- κ_ω = f_{ωNN}/g_{ωNN} = 0.01, empirical OBE value 0

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to non-perturbative QCD modeling of hadron physics, building on earlier studies by Roberts and Williams (1992), and Bender, Roberts, and Thomas (1996). Previous attempts to explain the ρππ coupling constant used only γ_μ and γ_5 covariants for the respective Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, as in work by Mitchell and Tandy (1997) and earlier studies; here the pseudovector terms in the pion amplitude are shown to contribute -71% to g_{ρππ}. The γπρ form factor results are much softer than vector meson dominance predictions and have been shown to strongly favor the present approach over others for elastic deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the Q² ≈ 2-6 GeV² range, as demonstrated by Van Orden, Devine, and Gross (1995). The ρNN and ωNN coupling strengths are consistent with the qualitative features of the Bonn boson-exchange NN model.
