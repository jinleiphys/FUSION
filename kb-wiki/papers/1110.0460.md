# Ab initio many-body calculations of the 3H(d,n)4He and 3He(d,p)4He fusion

> We apply the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating group method approach to calculate the cross sections of the 3H(d,n)4He and 3He(d,p)4He fusion reactions. These are important reactions for the Big Bang nucleosynthesis and the future of energy generation on Earth. Starting from a selected similarity-transformed chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction that accurately describes two-nucleon data, we performed many-body calculations that predict the S-factor of both reactions. Virtual three-body breakup effects are obtained by including excited pseudostates of the deuteron in the calculation. Our results are in satisfactory agreement with experimental data and pave the way for microscopic investigations of polarization and electron screening effects, of the 3H(d,gamma)5He radiative capture and other reactions relevant to fusion research.

## Key claim

The authors performed the first *ab initio* many-body calculation of the ³H(d,n)⁴He and ³He(d,p)⁴He fusion reactions, but they omitted both chiral and SRG-induced three-nucleon forces, selecting an SRG parameter (Λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹) that reproduces the experimental Q value within 1% as a fair approximation.

## Method

The authors applied the *ab initio* no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) to calculate cross sections for the two fusion reactions. They used a similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) softened chiral N³LO nucleon-nucleon potential with Λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ (and also 1.45 fm⁻¹ for ³H(d,n)⁴He) and included virtual three-body breakup effects by discretizing the continuum with excited deuteron pseudostates. The eigenstates of ²H, ³H, ³He, and ⁴He were calculated within the NCSM using a harmonic oscillator basis with Nmax = 12 and ħΩ = 14 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ²H: calculated -2.20 MeV vs. experimental -2.22 MeV; point-proton rms radius: calculated 1.84 fm vs. experimental 1.96 fm (SRG-N³LO NN potential with Λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, Nmax = 12, ħΩ = 14 MeV)
- Ground state energy of ⁴He: calculated -28.22 MeV vs. experimental -28.29 MeV; point-proton rms radius: calculated 1.49 fm vs. experimental 1.467(13) fm (same interaction and basis)
- S-factor at zero energy for ³H(d,n)⁴He: 10.0 ± 0.5 MeV b (SRG-N³LO NN) vs. 11.7 ± 0.2 MeV b (R-matrix data evaluation)
- S-factor at zero energy for ³He(d,p)⁴He: 6.0 ± 0.2 MeV b (SRG-N³LO NN) vs. 5.9 ± 0.3 MeV b (R-matrix data evaluation)
- Energy convergence of bound states achieved at ≤ 20 keV level for Nmax = 12

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM approach previously applied to d-⁴He scattering (Navratil and Quaglioni, 2011) and n(p)-⁴He scattering (Navratil and Quaglioni, 2009) to the five-nucleon fusion reactions. It provides the first *ab initio* microscopic description of these reactions, which are important for Big Bang nucleosynthesis and fusion energy research, and enables future microscopic investigations of polarization effects, electron screening, and the ³H(d,γ)⁵He radiative capture reaction.
