# Nonrelativistic nuclear reduction for tensor couplings in dark matter direct detection and $μ\to e$ conversion

> The nonrelativistic effective field theory (NRET) is widely used in dark matter direct detection and charged-lepton flavor violation studies through $μ\to e$ conversion. However, existing literature has not fully considered tensor couplings. This study fills this gap by utilizing an innovative tensor decomposition method, extending NRET to incorporate previously overlooked tensor interactions. This development is expected to have a significant impact on ongoing experiments seeking physics beyond the Standard Model and on our understanding of the new-physics interactions. Notably, we identify additional operators in $μ\to e$ conversion that are absent in scalar and vector couplings. To support further research and experimental analyses, comprehensive tables featuring tensor matrix elements and their corresponding operators are provided.

## Key claim

The paper extends the nonrelativistic effective field theory (NRET) framework for dark matter direct detection and muon-to-electron conversion to include tensor couplings that were previously overlooked, using a tensor decomposition method based on the antisymmetric nature of tensor interactions. The authors identify that while tensor terms in dark matter scattering do not introduce new nonrelativistic operators beyond those already present from scalar and vector couplings, tensor couplings in muon-to-electron conversion do generate additional operators absent in scalar and vector cases.

## Method

The authors start from the general Lorentz-invariant form of the single-nucleon tensor current matrix element, which contains four possible tensor structures parameterized by form factors g_T and \tilde{g}_T^{(i)}. They then construct all possible tensor coupling combinations between the WIMP (or lepton) current and the nucleon current, including their gamma-5 variations, and perform a nonrelativistic reduction using the antisymmetric tensor-current decomposition method that separates the tensor into vector-like components \vec{\mathcal{J}}^T and \vec{\mathcal{J}}^{T'}. The reduction is carried out to leading orders in 1/mass expansions, yielding the corresponding nonrelativistic operators for both dark matter-nucleus scattering and muon-to-electron conversion.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer in WIMP-nucleus scattering is typically around q ~ 100 MeV
- The paper identifies 24 new tensor coupling combinations (12 basic plus 12 with gamma-5 variations) for WIMP-nucleus scattering, extending the previous set of 20 scalar and vector terms
- For muon-to-electron conversion, the tensor terms introduce operators such as O_2^{f'}, O_3^{f}, O_5^{f}, O_{13}^{f'}, O_{13}^{'}, and O_{14}^{f} that are absent in scalar and vector couplings
- The tensor decomposition identity used is j_{\mu\nu}J^{\mu\nu} = -[\vec{j}^T \cdot \vec{J}^T + \vec{j}^{T'} \cdot \vec{J}^{T'}]

## Context

This work fills a gap in the NRET framework that was previously addressed only in chiral effective field theory (chi-EFT) by Hoferichter et al. (2019) for tensor couplings, while NRET as developed by Fitzpatrick et al. (2013) and Anand et al. (2014) had not incorporated tensor interactions. The tensor decomposition method was recently developed by Glick-Magid (2023) for multipole analysis and has been applied to beta-decay, neutrino scattering, and other processes. The results enable ongoing experiments such as Mu2e at Fermilab, COMET and DeeMe at J-PARC, and various dark matter direct detection experiments to constrain tensor-type new physics interactions, which could be crucial for identifying the symmetry structure of any detected beyond-Standard-Model signal.
