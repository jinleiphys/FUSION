# Effective Field Theory and Electroweak Processes in Nuclei

> The accurate theoretical treatment of low-energy electro-weak processes in lightest nuclei is of great current importance not only in the context of nuclear physics {\it per se} but also from the astrophysical and particle-physics viewpoints. I give a brief survey of the recent remarkable progress in this domain.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) and its hybrid variant EFT* can achieve approximately 1% precision in the theoretical prediction of the solar pp-fusion S-factor, \(S_{pp}\), a significant improvement over the approximately 6% uncertainty quoted in a 1998 review, but this precision relies on using the tritium beta-decay rate to determine a single low-energy constant, \(\widehat{d}^R\).

## Method

The author surveys the application of chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) to low-energy electroweak processes in light nuclei (A=2,3,4). The EFT* hybrid approach replaces the EFT-derived nuclear wave functions with wave functions from high-precision phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials, while the transition operators are derived from EFT up to a specified chiral order. The key low-energy constant \(\widehat{d}^R\), which controls the four-nucleon contact coupling to the axial current, is fixed using the precisely known tritium beta-decay rate, and then used to predict the rates for pp-fusion, neutrino-deuteron reactions, and muon-deuteron capture.

## Key numbers

*   The pp-fusion S-factor calculated at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) is \(S_{pp} = 3.94 \times (1 \pm 0.004) \times 10^{-24}\) MeV b, with the uncertainty due to the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_{\rm NN}\) being much smaller than the uncertainty from the experimental error in the tritium beta-decay rate.
*   The theoretical uncertainty in \(S_{pp}\) quoted in a 1998 review article was as large as approximately 6%, while the solar model requires approximately 1% precision.
*   A lattice QCD calculation gave \(g_A/g_V = 1.19 (6)_{\rm stat}(4)_{\rm syst}\), which is 7% smaller than the experimental value.
*   An N\(^3\)LO calculation of the Hen reaction (\(^3\)He + n → \(^4\)He + \(\gamma\)) gives \(\sigma_{\rm Hen} = (38 \sim 58)\) \(\mu\)b, which agrees with the experimental value \(\sigma_{\rm Hen}^{\rm exp} = (54 \pm 6)\) \(\mu\)b.
*   The current experimental error in the neutron-neutron scattering length, \(a_{nn}\), is estimated to cause a 0.5% error in \(S_{pp}\).
*   Radiative corrections specific to pp-fusion are approximately 3-4% effects and can be estimated within 0.1% uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) used by Nakamura et al. and others by incorporating effective field theory to systematically reduce theoretical uncertainties. The EFT* calculations of neutrino-deuteron cross sections by Ando et al. agree with SNPA results but with significantly reduced theoretical errors. The success of the EFT* framework for the Hen reaction, as demonstrated by Lazauskas et al. (2009), supports the reliability of earlier EFT* calculations for the Hep reaction and pp-fusion, and the upcoming MuSun experiment aims to measure the muon-deuteron capture rate with approximately 1% precision to independently determine the low-energy constant \(\widehat{d}^R\).
