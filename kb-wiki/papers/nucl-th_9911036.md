# Parity Dependence of Nuclear Level Densities

> A simple formula for the ratio of the number of odd- and even-parity states as a function of temperature is derived. This formula is used to calculate the ratio of level densities of opposite parities as a function of excitation energy. We test the formula with quantum Monte Carlo shell model calculations in the $(pf+g_{9/2})$-shell. The formula describes well the transition from low excitation energies where a single parity dominates to high excitations where the two densities are equal.

# Parity Dependence of Nuclear Level Densities

## Key claim
The ratio of odd- to even-parity nuclear level densities can be described by a simple tanh formula based on the average occupation of the minority-parity single-particle orbitals, provided pairing effects are accounted for via a BCS quasi-particle treatment at temperatures below approximately 1 MeV.

## Method
The authors derived an analytic formula for the ratio of odd- to even-parity partition functions, Z-/Z+ = tanh f, where f is the average occupation of the minority-parity single-particle states (or quasi-particle states when pairing is included). They tested this formula against quantum Monte Carlo shell model calculations in the complete (pf+g9/2)-shell for nuclei in the iron region, using a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle spectrum and BCS pairing gaps extracted from odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters extracted from experimental B(E2) values: δ = 0.22 for ⁵⁶Fe, 0.18 for ⁶⁰Ni, and 0.19 for ⁶⁸Zn (using r₀ = 1.27 fm)
- The crossover from parity-dominated to equal-parity densities occurs at different temperatures for different nuclei, with ⁶⁸Zn showing the crossover at lower temperatures than ⁵⁶Fe
- Deviations from the Poisson distribution for occupation numbers are observed for temperatures T < 1 MeV due to pairing effects
- The model reproduces Monte Carlo results for the ratio ρ-(Ex)/ρ+(Ex) as a function of excitation energy for all three nuclei studied

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of parity-dependent level densities beyond the common assumption of equal opposite-parity densities in the neutron resonance region, which was argued by Ericson and adopted in calculations for s- and r-process nucleosynthesis (Rauscher et al., 1997). The formula provides a computationally inexpensive alternative to the full quantum Monte Carlo shell model calculations of Alhassid et al. (1997), which were limited to A < 65 in the iron-to-germanium region. The model enables calculation of parity-projected level densities at fixed excitation energies when combined with the backshifted Bethe formula, and can be applied to neutron-capture reaction rate calculations.
