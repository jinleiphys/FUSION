# Quasi-linear transport approach to equilibration of quark-gluon plasmas

> We derive the transport equations of quark-gluon plasma in the quasi-linear approximation. The equations are either of the Balescu-Lenard or Fokker-Planck form. The plasma's dynamics is assumed to be governed by longitudinal chromoelectric fields. The isotropic plasma, which is stable, and the two-stream system, which is unstable, are considered in detail. A process of equilibration is briefly discussed in both cases. The peaks of the two-stream distribution are shown to rapidly dissolve in time.

## Key claim

The authors derive transport equations for a quark-gluon plasma in the quasi-linear approximation, showing that the back-reaction of fluctuating chromoelectric fields on the average momentum distribution can be described by collision terms of either Balescu-Lenard or Fokker-Planck form, but only for the simplified case where the plasma dynamics is governed solely by longitudinal chromoelectric fields, neglecting transverse and chromomagnetic fields.

## Method

The authors decompose the quark, antiquark, and gluon distribution functions into a large regular (ensemble-averaged) part and a small fluctuating part, and similarly assume that the regular parts of the chromodynamic fields vanish. They linearize the collisionless transport equations and Yang-Mills equations in the fluctuating quantities, retaining only longitudinal chromoelectric fields. The linearized equations are solved using one-sided Fourier transforms, and the ensemble-averaged transport equations for the regular distribution functions are expressed in terms of correlation functions of the fluctuating electric field and initial fluctuations. For the isotropic stable plasma and the two-stream unstable system, these correlation functions are computed explicitly, yielding transport equations in either Balescu-Lenard or Fokker-Planck form.

## Key numbers

- The equilibration time of quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is believed to be of order 1 fm/c or shorter (from the introduction, citing Heinz (2004)).
- The coupling constant from jet quenching data is estimated as alpha_s ≤ 0.3 (from the introduction, citing Qin (2007) and Nagle (2008)).
- The Coulomb logarithm L = ln(k_max/k_min) is estimated as L ~ ln(1/g) when k_max ~ T and k_min ~ m_D ~ gT.
- The tensor B^{ij}(v,v') in the Landau collision term contains a factor g^4/(32 pi) * (N_c^2-1)/N_c * L/|v-v'|.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-linear theory of weakly turbulent electromagnetic plasmas (Vedenov 1961, Vedenov 1963, Lifshitz and Pitaevskii 1981) to the quark-gluon plasma, providing a framework to study equilibration beyond the mean-field (hard-loop) approximation. It addresses a limitation of linear response theory, which describes how unstable modes grow but cannot describe how those modes modify the plasma momentum distribution. The derived collision terms for isotropic plasma are similar to those obtained by Selikhov (1991, 1993, 1994), Bodeker (1998, 1999), Arnold (1998, 1999), Litim (1999), Valle Basagoiti (1999), Blaizot (1999), and Markov (2000, 2002, 2003, 2005, 2006), but those works focused on equilibrium transport properties rather than equilibration from non-equilibrium states. The Fokker-Planck equation derived here is similar to that of Asakawa (2006) used to study anomalous shear viscosity in chromomagnetized plasma. This work enables analytical study of how unstable modes isotropize and equilibrate the plasma, a process previously accessible only by numerical simulations (Dumitru 2005, 2006; Arnold 2005).
