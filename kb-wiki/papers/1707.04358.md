# The quark propagator in the complex domain: A status report

> In these proceedings I review the status of an ongoing project that aims at solving the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation in the complex domain. The novel aspect of the approach is that the non-analyticities arising throughout the iteration of the equation are to be taken into account in an appropriate way through dynamic contour deformation. Because of the complexity of the approach, these studies are undertaken in a heavily truncated scenario that serves as a toy model for the development of the numerical techniques that are required to treat the system in a mathematically sound way.

# The quark propagator in the complex domain: A status report

## Key claim
Solving the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation for complex external momenta requires dynamic contour deformation to handle non-analyticities that arise during iteration, but the present work is limited to a heavily truncated toy model that serves only to develop the necessary numerical techniques rather than to produce physically reliable solutions.

## Method
The author studies the rainbow-truncated quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation in Euclidean space, first for perturbative one-loop integrals in the complex domain using GPU-implemented dynamic contour deformation, then for the full iterative equation without contour deformation using the Maris-Tandy model to detect poles numerically. The final step, still in progress, combines these techniques to dynamically deform integration contours during iteration, with the analytic properties of the integrand determined self-consistently at each step.

## Key numbers
- The Maris-Tandy model parameters used are: N_f = 4, Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV, ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 1.0 GeV^2, and m_t = 0.5 GeV.
- For the infrared-only part of the Maris-Tandy model, Cauchy's argument principle applied to the denominator of the vector propagator gives δ = N_Z - N_P = 3, corresponding to three poles in the propagator (two complex conjugate poles and one on the real axis).
- When the full Maris-Tandy model including the ultraviolet term is used, the same integral changes to δ = 2, indicating altered analytic properties.

## Context
This work extends two decades of studies on the analytic properties of the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation, including work by Stainsby (1992), Bender (1994), Burden (1997), Alkofer (2003), Fischer (2008), Eichmann (2009), Krassnigg (2009), Dorkin (2013), and El-Bennich (2016). The motivation is to provide quark propagator solutions at complex momenta needed for bound state equations (Sanchis-Alepuz, 2015) and to establish positivity criteria for removing degrees of freedom from asymptotic states (Alkofer, 2003). The approach builds on earlier perturbative studies by Windisch (2012, 2013) and recent work on overlapping branch cuts by Weil (2017), and aims to enable treatment of the Qin-Chang model (Qin, 2011) and extensions beyond rainbow approximation.
