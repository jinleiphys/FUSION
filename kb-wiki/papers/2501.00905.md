# White Paper on Software Infrastructure for Advanced Nuclear Physics Computing

> This White Paper documents the discussion and consensus conclusions of the workshop "Software Infrastructure for Advanced Nuclear Physics Computing" (SANPC 24), which was held at Jefferson Lab on June 20-22, 2024. The workshop brought together members of the US Nuclear Physics community with data scientists and funding agency representatives, to discuss the challenges and opportunities in advanced computing for Nuclear Physics in the coming decade. Opportunities for sustainable support and growth are identified, within the context of existing and currently planned DOE and NSF programs.

# White Paper on Software Infrastructure for Advanced Nuclear Physics Computing

## Key claim

The workshop concluded that while existing programs like SciDAC and CSSI have been invaluable for developing software that enables nuclear physics to use high-performance computing, a new program is needed to build on these successes and foster sustained collaborations between nuclear physicists and data scientists to advance the science of the NSAC Long Range Plan. Dedicated investments are required to keep abreast of industry trends and best practices for developing a sustainable software infrastructure, and to protect prior investments by maintaining software so it functions optimally on new HPC systems as they become available.

## Method

The workshop (SANPC 24) was held at Jefferson Lab on June 20-22, 2024, bringing together members of the US Nuclear Physics community with data scientists and funding agency representatives. The meeting included plenary sessions covering existing software projects (MUSES, ENAF, NUCLEI, BAND, JETSCAPE, QuantOm, SciDAC FASTMath), experimental computing needs across collider and fixed-target facilities, lattice QCD, and quantum computing applications, followed by parallel sessions organized by experiment, theory, and joint theory-experiment tracks. The final day was dedicated to open discussion and white paper development, with working groups addressing innovation and stewardship, cross-cutting connections, funding and outreach, operations, workforce and community, data physicist roles, and data preservation.

## Key numbers

- No specific numerical results are presented; this is a white paper documenting workshop discussions and consensus conclusions on software infrastructure needs.

## Context

This white paper extends the community planning process initiated by the NSAC Long Range Plan and builds on the collaborative model established by the SciDAC program, which has connected nuclear physics with applied mathematics and computer science through five-year funding cycles. The workshop identified the need for expanded support beyond existing mechanisms, including new initiatives for quantum information, machine learning, accelerated computing, and advanced nuclear theory algorithms, similar to the SciDAC model. The paper also references the CSSI program from NSF and the Simons Foundation Scientific Software Research Faculty Award as existing models for software sustainability, while noting that the current SciDAC cycle (FY21-25) is focused on AI/ML and exascale science. The workshop enables future community coordination through a proposed annual meeting bringing together all elements of the US NP community and agency representatives.
