# The importance of the bulk viscosity of QCD in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the consequences of a nonzero bulk viscosity coefficient on the transverse momentum spectra, azimuthal momentum anisotropy, and multiplicity of charged hadrons produced in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies. The agreement between a realistic 3D hybrid simulation and the experimentally measured data considerably improves with the addition of a bulk viscosity coefficient for strongly interacting matter. This paves the way for an eventual quantitative determination of several QCD transport coefficients from the experimental heavy ion and hadron-nucleus collision programs.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a nonzero bulk viscosity coefficient, with a peak value of ζ/s ≈ 0.3 near the QCD phase transition temperature, is essential for describing the average transverse momentum of charged hadrons in IP-Glasma + hydrodynamics + UrQMD simulations of LHC heavy-ion collisions; without bulk viscosity, the model overpredicts the average transverse momentum of pions, kaons, and protons by almost 30%.

## Method

The authors used a 3D hybrid simulation combining IP-Glasma initial conditions (thermalization time τ₀ = 0.4 fm), the MUSIC hydrodynamic code solving Israel-Stewart-type relaxation equations for both shear stress and bulk viscous pressure, and the UrQMD hadronic transport model after switching at T_switch = 145 MeV. The bulk viscosity parametrization matched lattice QCD calculations for the QGP phase to hadronic-phase calculations at T_c = 180 MeV, with all second-order coupling terms between shear and bulk sectors included. The shear viscosity was adjusted to η/s = 0.095 when bulk viscosity was present, versus η/s = 0.16 without it.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio at the matching temperature: ζ/s(T_c) ≈ 0.3, with T_c = 180 MeV
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio with bulk viscosity included: η/s = 0.095
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio without bulk viscosity: η/s = 0.16
- Switching temperature between hydrodynamics and UrQMD: T_switch = 145 MeV
- Thermalization time for IP-Glasma initial conditions: τ₀ = 0.4 fm
- Simulations without bulk viscosity overpredict average transverse momentum of pions, kaons, and protons by almost 30%
- Inclusion of bulk viscosity reduces the extracted shear viscosity by almost 50% (from η/s = 0.16 to η/s = 0.095)
- Bulk viscosity reduces v₂,₃,₄{2} by more than 10%, compensated by the lower η/s
- The model systematically overpredicts kaon multiplicity by approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma + hydrodynamics approach of Gale (2012) which successfully described flow harmonics but showed tension with transverse momentum spectra at low pT. Previous studies including bulk viscosity in heavy-ion collisions (Torrieri 2008, Rajagopal 2009, Bozek 2009, Song 2009, Dusling 2011, Noronha-Hostler 2013) had demonstrated non-negligible effects, but this is the first phenomenological investigation showing that bulk viscosity is essential when using IP-Glasma initial conditions. The results enable future quantitative extraction of QCD transport coefficients from heavy-ion data and motivate studies of bulk viscosity effects in ultracentral collisions and other observables.
