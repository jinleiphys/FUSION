# Mixed Phase within the Multi-polytrope Approach to High Mass Twins

> We present a multi-polytrope approach to describe high-mass twins fulfilling chiral effective field theory estimations of the neutron star equation state and test it against the appearance of mixed phases at the hadron-quark interface. In addition, we discuss astrophysical applications of this method and expected future measurements that shall further constrain neutron star matter and the understanding of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The multi-polytrope approach to high-mass twin (HMT) neutron star configurations yields HMTs that are robust against the appearance of mixed phases (pasta phases) at the hadron-quark interface, provided the mixed phase is modeled as a smooth interpolation with a finite slope parameter.

## Method

The authors construct a multi-polytrope equation of state (EoS) consisting of several polytropic segments \(P_i(n)=\kappa_i n^{\Gamma_i}\) that satisfy chiral effective field theory constraints at subnuclear densities and the \(M_{\rm max} \ge 2 M_\odot\) constraint. A constant-pressure region is inserted to model a strong first-order hadron-quark phase transition, and the mixed phase is parametrized by a hyperbolic tangent interpolation between hadronic and quark energy densities with a width parameter \(\Gamma_s\). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius sequences for static, non-rotating stars.

## Key numbers

* Set \(\alpha\) (\(\Gamma_s = 0.0\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)): maximum mass of the hadronic branch \(M_{\rm max}^{NS} = 2.176 M_\odot\), maximum mass of the hybrid star branch \(M_{\rm max}^{HS} = 2.054 M_\odot\), minimum mass of the hybrid star branch \(M_{\rm min}^{HS} = 2.050 M_\odot\).
* Set \(\beta\) (\(\Gamma_s = 6.0\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)): \(M_{\rm max}^{NS} = 2.088 M_\odot\), \(M_{\rm max}^{HS} = 2.039 M_\odot\), \(M_{\rm min}^{HS} = 2.017 M_\odot\).
* Set \(\gamma\) (\(\Gamma_s = 9.0\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)): \(M_{\rm max}^{NS} = 2.064 M_\odot\), \(M_{\rm max}^{HS} = 2.035 M_\odot\), \(M_{\rm min}^{HS} = 2.009 M_\odot\).
* The high-density polytrope parameters used for all sets are \(\Gamma_3 = 3.2\) and \(\kappa_3 = 490.41\) MeV fm\(^{3(\Gamma_3-1)}\).
* The Seidov constraint for the instability criterion is \(\Delta\varepsilon / \varepsilon_{\rm crit} \ge 1/2 + (3/2)(P_{\rm crit} / \varepsilon_{\rm crit})\).

## Context

This work extends the multi-polytrope approach to high-mass twins previously developed by Alvarez-Castillo et al. (2017) by testing its robustness against the inclusion of mixed phases (pasta phases) at the hadron-quark interface. The approach is consistent with the EoS constraints from chiral effective field theory by Hebeler et al. (2013) and the \(M_{\rm max} \ge 2 M_\odot\) constraint. The results enable future Bayesian analyses for parameter estimation under observational constraints, and the method can be applied to interpret gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers (e.g., as studied by Hanauske et al., 2016) and upcoming radius measurements from the NICER detector.
