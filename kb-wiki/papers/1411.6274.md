# Isospin coupling-channel decomposition of nuclear symmetry energy in covariant density functional theory

> The isospin coupling-channel decomposition of the potential energy density functional is carried out within the covariant density functional theory, and their isospin and density dependence in particular the influence on the symmetry energy is studied. It is found that both isospin-singlet and isospin-triplet components of the potential energy play the dominant role in deciding the symmetry energy, especially when the Fock diagram is introduced. The results illustrate a quite different mechanism to the origin of the symmetry energy from the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, and demonstrate the importance of the Fork diagram in the CDF theory, especially from the isoscalar mesons, in the isospin properties of the in-medium nuclear force at high density.

# Isospin coupling-channel decomposition of nuclear symmetry energy in covariant density functional theory

## Key claim
The authors find that both isospin-singlet (T=0) and isospin-triplet (T=1) components of the potential energy play a dominant role in determining the nuclear symmetry energy, especially when the Fock (exchange) diagram is included in covariant density functional (CDF) theory. This mechanism differs from microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory, where the symmetry energy is mainly dominated by the T=0 component.

## Method
The authors perform an isospin coupling-channel decomposition of the potential energy density functional within covariant density functional theory, separating contributions into isospin-singlet (T=0) and isospin-triplet (T=1) channels for both Hartree (direct) and Fock (exchange) terms. Calculations are carried out using two relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) effective interactions (PKO1 and PKA1) and two relativistic mean field (RMF) interactions (TW99 and PKDD) in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. The symmetry energy and its slope parameter L are decomposed into kinetic, T=0 potential, and T=1 potential components, with further decomposition by meson-nucleon coupling channels (sigma, omega, rho, pi).

## Key numbers
- At saturation density, the kinetic contribution to the symmetry energy J is 8.0 MeV (TW99), 8.1 MeV (PKDD), 3.7 MeV (PKO1), and 0.5 MeV (PKA1), compared to -1.0 MeV from BHF reference calculations.
- The T=0 potential contribution to J is 51.0 MeV (TW99), 50.8 MeV (PKDD), 38.8 MeV (PKO1), and 42.4 MeV (PKA1), with the BHF reference value being 44.2 MeV.
- The T=1 potential contribution to J is -26.2 MeV (TW99), -22.1 MeV (PKDD), -8.1 MeV (PKO1), and -5.7 MeV (PKA1), compared to -9.0 MeV from BHF.
- For the slope parameter L, the kinetic contribution ranges from 5.9 MeV (TW99) to -69.6 MeV (PKA1), while the T=1 potential contribution ranges from -12.8 MeV (TW99) to 103.2 MeV (PKA1).
- The Fock term contribution from isoscalar sigma and omega mesons to the T=1 symmetry energy J is 11.5 MeV (PKO1) and 12.2 MeV (PKA1), and to the slope L is 44.3 MeV (PKO1) and 73.4 MeV (PKA1).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of symmetry energy within covariant density functional theory by providing a detailed isospin coupling-channel decomposition, which had not been carried out previously in this framework. It contrasts with microscopic BHF calculations using the Argonne V18 potential plus Urbana IX three-body force (Ref. IA84), where the symmetry energy is dominated by the T=0 component through tensor force effects in the 3S1-3D1 channel. The results demonstrate the importance of Fock diagrams from isoscalar mesons in CDF theory for isospin properties at high density, which has implications for neutron star properties such as radius and cooling mechanisms, as studied in earlier RHF work (BY78, WH85).
