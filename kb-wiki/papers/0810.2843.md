# The calculation of the equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential and zero temperature

> In this paper, we give a direct method for calculating the partition function, and hence the equation of state (EOS) of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at finite chemical potential and zero temperature. In the EOS derived in this paper the pressure density is the sum of two terms: the first term ${\cal P}(μ)|_{μ=0}$ (the pressure density at $μ=0$) is a $μ$-independent constant; the second term, which is totally determined by $G_R[μ](p)$ (the renormalized dressed quark propagator at finite $μ$), contains all the nontrivial $μ$-dependence. By applying a general result in the rainbow-ladder approximation of the Dyson-Schwinger approach obtained in our previous study [Phys. Rev. {\bf C 71}, 015205 (2005)], $G_R[μ](p)$ is calculated from the meromorphic quark propagator proposed in [Phys. Rev. {\bf D 70}, 014014 (2004)]. From this the full analytic expression of the EOS of QCD at finite $μ$ and zero $T$ is obtained (apart from the constant term ${\cal P}(μ)|_{μ=0}$ which can in principle be calculated from the CJT effective action). A comparison between our EOS and the cold, perturbative EOS of QCD of Fraga, Pisarski and Schaffner-Bielich is made. It is expected that our EOS can provide a possible new approach for the study of neutron stars.

# The calculation of the equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential and zero temperature

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytic expression for the equation of state (EOS) of QCD at finite chemical potential and zero temperature by expressing the pressure density as the sum of a constant term (the pressure at zero chemical potential) plus an integral of the quark-number density, which is itself determined entirely by the renormalized dressed quark propagator at finite chemical potential. However, the result is model-dependent because the quark propagator is taken from a specific meromorphic parametrization (Alkofer, Detmold, Fischer, and Maris, 2004) and relies on the assumption that the inverse dressed quark propagator at finite chemical potential can be obtained from the zero-chemical-potential propagator by the analytic continuation p4 → p4 + iμ, which the authors acknowledge does not reproduce the first-order chiral phase transition expected in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors start from the partition function of QCD at zero temperature and finite chemical potential and derive a formally model-independent relation linking the quark-number density ρ(μ) to the trace of the dressed quark propagator at finite chemical potential, G_R[μ](p). They then apply a result from their previous work (Zong et al., 2005) that, under the rainbow-ladder approximation of the Dyson-Schwinger approach and assuming analyticity of the propagator near μ=0, the inverse dressed quark propagator at finite μ is obtained from the zero-μ propagator by the shift p4 → p4 + iμ. Using this substitution, they compute ρ(μ) analytically from a meromorphic model of the zero-μ quark propagator (Alkofer et al., 2004) with three parameter sets (three real poles, two complex-conjugate pole pairs, or one real pole plus one complex-conjugate pair). The pressure density is then obtained by integrating ρ(μ) from 0 to μ and adding the constant pressure at μ=0, which is calculated from the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective action within the rainbow-ladder approximation.

## Key numbers

- The quark-number density vanishes identically for chemical potentials below a critical value μ0: μ0 = 351 MeV (2CC parametrization), 377 MeV (1R1CC parametrization), and 341 MeV (3R parametrization).
- The critical chemical potential obtained is compared to an estimate from Halasz et al. (1998) of (m_N − 16 MeV)/N_c = 307 MeV for pure QCD.
- The renormalization point for the meromorphic quark propagator is set to ζ² = 16 GeV².
- For the perturbative EOS of Fraga, Pisarski, and Schaffner-Bielich (2001), the renormalization subtraction scale is taken as Λ̅/μ = 2, with Λ_MS̅ = 365 MeV for N_f = 3.
- The pressure density from the authors' EOS tends to the free quark gas result (P_free = N_c N_f μ⁴/(12π²)) as μ → ∞, and shows appreciable differences from the perturbative EOS for μ below about 1.8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to the equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential and zero temperature, building on the authors' previous result (Zong et al., 2005) for the analytic continuation of the dressed quark propagator. The resulting EOS is compared to the cold, perturbative EOS of Fraga, Pisarski, and Schaffner-Bielich (2001), and the authors note that their EOS tends more rapidly to the free quark gas result at large μ. The paper is intended to provide a possible new approach for studying neutron stars, where the constant term P(μ)|_{μ=0} does not contribute to the mass-radius relation due to the boundary condition at the neutron star surface. The authors note that the parameters of the meromorphic quark propagator (Alkofer et al., 2004) have some arbitrariness and that further constraints could come from comparing theoretical neutron-star results with observations.
