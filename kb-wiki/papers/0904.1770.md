# Light Pseudoscalar Meson and Heavy Meson Scattering Lengths

> We have performed a systematical calculation of the pion ($π, K, η$) and heavy pseudoscalar meson S-wave scattering lengths up to ${\cal O}(p^3)$ in the chiral perturbation theory in the heavy quark symmetry limit. With the three scattering lengths from the lattice simulations as input we estimate the unknown low-energy constants. Then we predict all the other unmeasured scattering lengths. The analytical expressions and predictions may be helpful to future investigations. Especially we note that the DK scattering length is positive. Therefore their interaction is attractive, which helps to lower the mass of the "bare" charm-strange scalar state in the quark model through the couple-channel effect.

## Key claim

The authors calculate S-wave scattering lengths for light pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K, eta) with heavy pseudoscalar mesons (charmed and bottom) up to third order in chiral perturbation theory in the heavy quark symmetry limit, using three lattice scattering lengths as input to determine unknown low-energy constants and then predicting all other unmeasured scattering lengths. The hedge is that the chiral expansion converges well only in four channels (isoscalar DK, isovector D anti-K, D_s K, and D_s anti-K), while convergence is problematic for the isoscalar D anti-K channel where the second-order contribution is very large.

## Method

The authors employ heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HMchiPT) in the heavy quark symmetry limit, calculating threshold T-matrices for S-wave elastic scattering of Goldstone bosons (pi, K, eta) with charmed and bottom pseudoscalar mesons up to O(p^3). They use dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction, extract the low-energy constant c_1 from heavy meson mass splittings, and determine the remaining three LECs (C_1, C_0, kappa^r) by fitting to three lattice scattering lengths from a full QCD simulation: a_Dpi^(3/2), a_D_sK, and a_D_spi. The same LECs are then applied to bottom meson systems using heavy quark flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- c_1 = 0.47 GeV^{-1} for the charmed system, extracted from mass splittings: M_D = 1867.2 MeV, M_Ds = 1968.5 MeV, M_D* = 2008.6 MeV, M_Ds* = 2112.3 MeV, with heavy meson mass in the chiral limit approximately 1918 MeV
- Input lattice scattering lengths: a_Dpi^(3/2) = -0.16(4) fm, a_D_sK = -0.31(2) fm, a_D_spi = 0.00(1) fm
- Determined LECs at scale lambda = 4pi f_pi: C_1 = -0.63 GeV^{-1}, C_0 = 5.64 GeV^{-1}, kappa^r = -0.33 GeV^{-2}
- Predicted charmed meson scattering lengths: a_DK^(0) = 0.41 fm, a_DK^(1) = 0.25 + 0.17i fm, a_Dpi^(1/2) = 0.24 fm, a_Deta = 0.34 + 0.17i fm, a_D_seta = -0.41 + 0.35i fm
- Predicted bottom meson scattering lengths: a_BbarK^(0) = 0.45 fm, a_Bbarpi^(1/2) = 0.25 fm, a_Bbar_sK = -0.37 fm
- Coupling constant g = 0.59 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.01 from D*+ width; for bottom systems g = 0.52 from lattice

## Context

This work extends the next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory calculation of heavy meson-Goldstone boson scattering lengths by Guo et al. to next-next-leading order (O(p^3)), including important loop corrections. It uses lattice results from a full QCD simulation (latDK) as input, which had large pion masses, and also references earlier unitarization model predictions by Beveren et al. (a_DK^(0) = 5 +/- 1 GeV^{-1}) and lattice extractions from semileptonic decays by Nieves et al. (a_Dpi^(1/2) = 0.29(4) m_pi^{-1}). The positive DK scattering length supports the interpretation of D_sJ*(2317) as a conventional c anti-s state with strong coupling to the DK channel, and the results enable future chiral extrapolations of lattice data and experimental tests of HMchiPT convergence.
