# Spin-Induced Interactions and Heavy-Quark Transport in the QGP

> A previously constructed $T$-matrix approach for studying the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is improved by incorporating spin-dependent interactions between partons. These interactions arise from the relativistic corrections to the Cornell potential. We first study the vacuum spectroscopy of quarkonia with this potential and find that a significant admixture of a vector component in the confining potential (rather than the previously considered scalar interaction) improves the description of the experimental mass splittings in $S$- and $P$-wave states. The in-medium potential containing the vector component in the confining interaction is constrained by fitting lattice-QCD results for heavy-quark (HQ) free energies and the equation of state (EoS) computed within in the selfconsistent $T$-matrix framework. We subsequently extract the transport coefficients for charm quarks in the QGP with the improved in-medium potentials. The relativistic corrections to the vector component of the confining potential cause a notable increase in the thermal relaxation rate of charm quarks in the QGP in comparison to previous calculations, especially at high momenta. These results are expected to have significant ramifications for the phenomenology of open heavy-flavor observables at RHIC and the LHC.

# Spin-Induced Interactions and Heavy-Quark Transport in the QGP

## Key claim
The authors improve a previously constructed T-matrix approach for the quark-gluon plasma by incorporating spin-dependent interactions from relativistic corrections to the Cornell potential, finding that a significant admixture of a vector component in the confining potential (rather than the previously assumed purely scalar interaction) improves the description of experimental mass splittings in S- and P-wave quarkonium states and causes a notable increase in the thermal relaxation rate of charm quarks, especially at high momenta.

## Method
The authors extend the thermodynamic T-matrix approach by including 1/M_Q corrections via spin-dependent interactions between heavy quarks, derived from a mixed confining potential with both vector and scalar Lorentz structures parameterized by a mixing coefficient chi. They compute vacuum quarkonium spectral functions from correlation functions to extract masses, then constrain in-medium potentials by fitting lattice-QCD results for heavy-quark free energies and the equation of state within the selfconsistent T-matrix framework. Finally, they calculate charm-quark transport coefficients including the thermal relaxation rate and spatial diffusion coefficient using the improved in-medium potentials.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant alpha_s = 0.27 and string tension sigma = 0.225 GeV^2, fitted to vacuum free energy from lattice-QCD data with string breaking distance r_SB = 1 fm
- Mixing coefficient chi = 0.6 (compared to chi = 1 for purely scalar confining potential) yields improved description of charmonium mass splittings and reproduces the experimental splitting between D and D* mesons
- For chi = 0.6, the T-matrices for charm-light scattering are smaller than for chi = 1 at low momenta but exceed them for center-of-mass momentum p_cm greater than approximately 0.5 GeV
- The spatial diffusion coefficient D_s = T/(M_c A(p=0)) is slightly smaller for chi = 0.6 compared to chi = 1 due to non-zero average momenta in the center-of-mass of heavy-light interactions

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic T-matrix approach previously developed by Cabrera (2006) and Riek (2010) for studying the strongly coupled QGP, and builds on the selfconsistent framework of Liu (2017). The introduction of a mixed confining potential with vector and scalar components was motivated by earlier work from Szczepaniak (1996) and Ebert (2002) in different contexts. The improved description of vacuum charmonium spectroscopy and the enhanced charm-quark relaxation rates at high momenta are expected to have significant ramifications for the phenomenology of open heavy-flavor observables at RHIC and the LHC, potentially improving the description of heavy-flavor diffusion in heavy-ion collisions based on nonperturbative interactions.
