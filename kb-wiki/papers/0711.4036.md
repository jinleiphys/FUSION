# A Realistic Three-Dimensional Calculation of 3H Binding Energy

> A recently developed three-dimensional Faddeev integral equations for three-nucleon bound state with two-nucleon interactions have been solved in momentum space for Bonn-B potential.

## Key claim

The authors solve a recently developed three-dimensional Faddeev integral equation formalism for the three-nucleon bound state, including spin-isospin degrees of freedom, and obtain a triton binding energy of -8.15 MeV using the Bonn-B potential, which agrees with the converged partial-wave result of -8.14 MeV but with a simpler numerical procedure.

## Method

The authors formulate the Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state using vector Jacobi momenta and a basis that couples spin and isospin quantum numbers, avoiding any partial-wave decomposition. They solve the resulting eight coupled three-dimensional integral equations in momentum space for the triton (total spin-isospin state S=1/2, T=1/2) using the Bonn-B one-boson-exchange potential, with the two-body t-matrix input calculated in a helicity representation depending on momentum magnitudes and the angle between them.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy from the 3D approach: -8.15 MeV
- Triton binding energy from the partial-wave approach (j12max=4): -8.14 MeV
- The number of coupled equations for the S=1/2, T=1/2 case (triton): 8 equations
- In a partial-wave approach, the number of channels ranges from 5 (j12max=1) to 42 (j12max=5) when total isospin is restricted to T=1/2

## Context

This work extends earlier three-dimensional Faddeev calculations for three- and four-body bound states that neglected spin-isospin degrees of freedom (Liu, 2003; Hadizadeh, 2007) by incorporating those degrees of freedom into a realistic calculation. The result matches the converged partial-wave calculations of Sammarruca (1993), Nogga (1997), and Machleidt (1989) for the Bonn-B potential, and the 3D formalism avoids the truncation of partial waves required in standard approaches, enabling simpler numerical implementation for future studies of three-nucleon systems with more realistic interactions.
