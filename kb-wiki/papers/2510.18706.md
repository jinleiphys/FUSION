# Binding energy of compact stars and their non-radial oscillations

> In the past years, a significant effort has been made with the scope of determining correlations, involving compact star properties, that are independent of the nuclear equation of state. Such universal relations are of utmost importance as they allow for the imposition of constraints on stellar properties without directly measuring them and they may also serve as a probe of General Relativity. In the present study, we investigated the possible existence of a universal relation between the binding energy of compact stars and the frequency of their non-radial oscillations. The main motivation was related to the fact that both of the aforementioned quantities might be measured in the occurrence of a supernova explosion. Interestingly, we found that there is a empirical relation between the oscillation frequency and the binding energy for both $f$ and $p_1$ modes, assuming hadronic stellar matter. The inclusion of hybrid equations of state, incorporating sharp phase transitions, was shown to result into deviations from the aforementioned quasi-universal relation.

## Key claim

The authors propose a quasi-universal linear relation between the binding energy of compact stars and the frequency of their non-radial \(f\) and \(p_1\) oscillation modes for purely hadronic matter, but caution that the inclusion of hybrid equations of state with sharp phase transitions causes deviations from this relation of up to 30%.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute stellar structure and then calculated non-radial oscillation frequencies within the relativistic Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) for quadrupole (\(l=2\)) \(f\) and \(p_1\) modes. They employed ten hadronic equations of state (including relativistic mean-field, Skyrme, and microscopic models) and six hybrid equations of state constructed via the constant speed of sound method with a first-order phase transition at a fixed transition mass of \(1.4 M_\odot\) and sound speed set to the speed of light. Linear regression was performed to fit the relation between the scaled oscillation frequency and the binding energy for hadronic configurations.

## Key numbers

* For hadronic stars, the \(f\) mode frequency scaled by mass squared follows \(f_0 M^2 / (\text{kHz} M_\odot^2) = 0.27 + 29.29 (E_b / M_\odot)\) with a correlation coefficient \(R^2 = 0.99\) and reproduces numerical data with error below 10% for masses above \(1.2 M_\odot\).
* For hadronic stars, the \(p_1\) mode frequency scaled by mass squared follows \(f_{p_1} M^2 / (\text{kHz} M_\odot^2) = 1.13 + 81.30 (E_b / M_\odot)\) with error below 12% for masses above \(1.2 M_\odot\).
* For \(1.4 M_\odot\) hadronic stars specifically, the \(f\) mode frequency obeys \(f_0^{1.4} / \text{kHz} = -0.22 + 17.60 (E_b^{1.4} / M_\odot)\) and the \(p_1\) mode frequency obeys \(f_{p_1}^{1.4} / \text{kHz} = -1.49 + 57.43 (E_b^{1.4} / M_\odot)\), both with error below 10%.
* Hybrid equations of state with high energy density jumps show deviations from the hadronic fit of up to 30% for the \(f\) mode.

## Context

This work extends the search for universal relations in compact stars, following the tradition of the I-Love-Q relations (Yagi 2013) and the Love-C relation (Maselli 2013), as well as prior studies connecting oscillation frequencies to mean density (Anderson and Kokkotas 1998) and to moment of inertia and tidal deformability (Lau 2010, Chen 2014, Chirenti, Lioutas 2021, Zhao and Lattimer 2022). It also builds on work linking binding energy to compactness (Lattimer and Prakash 2001) and to tidal deformability (Reed and Horowitz 2020). The proposed relation could enable multimessenger constraints from a single supernova event, where both binding energy (via neutrinos, as suggested by Lattimer 2007) and oscillation frequencies (via gravitational waves) might be measured. Future work should repeat the analysis without the Cowling approximation and vary the phase transition density to assess robustness.
