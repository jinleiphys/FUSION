# Two-photon exchange corrections in elastic muon-proton scattering

> We extend the general formalism of two-photon exchange to elastic lepton-nucleon scattering by accounting for all lepton mass terms. We then perform a numerical estimate of the muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfer in view of the future MUSE experiment. For this purpose, we estimate the two-photon exchange corrections to muon-proton scattering observables by considering the contribution of the proton intermediate state, which is expected to dominate at very low momentum transfers. We find that the two-photon exchange effect to the unpolarized muon- proton scattering cross section in the MUSE kinematical region is of the order of 0.5%.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the unpolarized muon-proton scattering cross section in the MUSE kinematical region is of the order of 0.5%, but this estimate is based only on the contribution from the proton intermediate state, which is expected to dominate only at very low momentum transfers (Q² less than about 0.1 GeV²).

## Method

The authors extended the general formalism of TPE to elastic lepton-nucleon scattering by including all lepton mass terms, then performed a numerical estimate for muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfer. They evaluated the TPE corrections by calculating the direct box and crossed box diagrams with a proton intermediate state, using the on-shell form of the virtual photon-proton-proton vertex with dipole form factors for the proton electromagnetic form factors. The four-point integrals were evaluated numerically using LOOPTOOLS, and infrared divergences were subtracted according to the Maximon and Tjon prescription.

## Key numbers

- The TPE correction to the unpolarized muon-proton scattering cross section in the MUSE kinematical region is of the order of 0.5%.
- The TPE correction varies between 0.25% and 0.5% across the MUSE kinematic region.
- For the MUSE experiment, the three muon beam momenta are 0.115 GeV, 0.153 GeV, and 0.210 GeV.
- For these beam momenta, the kinematically allowed momentum transfer ranges are 0 < Q² < 0.039 GeV², 0 < Q² < 0.066 GeV², and 0 < Q² < 0.116 GeV², respectively.
- For the experimental scattering angles of 20° to 100° in the laboratory frame, the momentum transfer varies in the regions 0.0016–0.026 GeV², 0.0028–0.045 GeV², and 0.0052–0.080 GeV² for the three beam momenta.
- The critical momentum transfer Q² = 2m², where m is the muon mass, is approximately 0.022 GeV², which lies inside the MUSE kinematic region for all three beam momenta.
- The dipole form factor parameters used are κ = 1.793 and Λ² = 0.71 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the general formalism of TPE corrections to elastic lepton-nucleon scattering by accounting for all lepton mass terms, building on prior work by Gorchtein (2004) and Blunden (2003). The calculations are motivated by the "proton charge radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between hydrogen spectroscopy measurements (Mohr, 2008) and muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013), and are performed in view of the future MUSE experiment (Gilman, 2013). The results show that TPE corrections for muon-proton scattering are up to a factor three smaller than for electron-proton scattering in the same kinematic region, due to lepton helicity-flip amplitudes, and the authors note that extending to larger momentum transfer will require including inelastic state contributions within a dispersive formalism.
