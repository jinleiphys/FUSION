# Quark coalescence into opposite parity baryon states

> The production rate of negative parity baryons was found to be much weaker than that of positive states in RHIC experiments. In the present paper we show that this suppression is a simple consequence of the coalescence dynamics of hadronization.

## Key claim

The suppression of negative parity baryons (e.g., Λ(1520)) relative to positive parity baryons (e.g., Λ(1114)) observed in RHIC experiments is a direct consequence of the coalescence dynamics of hadronization, specifically arising from the difference in orbital angular momentum between the constituent quarks in the final states.

## Method

The authors model the coalescence process as a pick-up reaction analogous to p + (A+n) → d + A in nuclear physics, where an incident strange quark (proton analog) picks up a diquark (neutron analog) from a screening cluster to form a baryon (deuteron analog). They calculate the production rate for baryons with internal angular momentum l_d = 0 (positive parity) and l_d = 1 (negative parity) using spherical Bessel function wave functions truncated at their first zero, a Gaussian wave function for the bound quark, and a step-function interaction potential. The total transition rate R_l is obtained by integrating the momentum-dependent matrix element squared A_l(k_p) weighted by a Boltzmann distribution f(k_p) = exp(-k_p/T).

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio Λ(1520)/Λ(1114) measured by STAR at RHIC is 0.022 ± 0.01.
- The calculated R_1/R_0 ratio from the coalescence model is consistent with the experimental data for interaction range a = 0.8–1.0 fm.
- The interaction range a is expected to be in the order of baryon size; calculations were performed at a = 0.1, 0.3, and 0.9 fm.
- The first zero of the spherical Bessel functions used for radial wave functions is x_0 = 3.14 for l_d = 0 and x_1 = 4.50 for l_d = 1.
- Normalization factors for the radial wave functions are n_{d,0} = 0.22525 · a^{3/2} and n_{d,1} = 0.15327 · a^{3/2}.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence hadronization model previously developed by the authors (Zimanyi and Levai, 2004) and others (Bialas, 2002; Greco, 2003; Fries, 2003; Molnar, 2004) to explain baryon production in heavy-ion collisions. The suppression of Λ(1520) relative to Λ(1114) was observed experimentally by NA49 at SPS and STAR at RHIC, and a thermal model by Braun (2003) predicted a higher ratio than observed. The results indicate that constituent quarks were the dominant degrees of freedom in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but not in p+p or peripheral Au+Au collisions, and surprisingly also not in SPS Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 17.3 AGeV.
