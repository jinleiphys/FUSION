# Elimination of Spurious States of Oscillator Shell Model

> A new method for elimination of the 'spurious' states comprising reguirement of translational invariance and simplicity of the enumeration scheme of antisymmetric A-particle states has been developed. The method presented enables one to project off the space of purely spurious antisymmetric A-particle oscillator functions with singled out dependence on intrinsic coordinates of two last particles onto the subspace of the nonspurious motion. The method is based on constructing the matrix elements of the centre-ofmass Hamiltonian and spectral decomposition of the corresponding real symmetric matrix. The obtained coefficients of the expansion of the antisymmetric A-particle oscillator functions in terms of the ones with singled out dependence on intrinsic coordinates of two last particles and with eliminated spurious states are required for calculation of two-particle oscillator intrinsic density matrices. Software tools based on root rational fraction expression of numbers for the theoretical formulation presented have been developed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new algebraic method for eliminating spurious centre-of-mass states from antisymmetric A-particle oscillator shell model wave functions, enabling the practical calculation of two-particle oscillator intrinsic density matrices for arbitrary excitation energies, provided the nontruncated configuration space is taken into account.

## Method

The method constructs matrix elements of the centre-of-mass Hamiltonian in the basis of antisymmetric A-particle oscillator functions with singled-out dependence on intrinsic coordinates of the two last particles, using fractional parentage expansions and Jacobi coordinates to separate two-particle subsystems. The centre-of-mass Hamiltonian matrix is then diagonalized via spectral decomposition of idempotent matrices, and the eigenvectors corresponding to the minimal eigenvalue of 3/2 (the ground state of centre-of-mass motion) are selected to obtain the expansion coefficients for nonspurious states.

## Key numbers

- The minimal eigenvalue of the centre-of-mass Hamiltonian, corresponding to nonspurious states, is 3/2 (in units of ℏω).
- For A=3, E=E_min=0, JΠT=1/2⁺1/2, the centre-of-mass Hamiltonian matrix is a single number: 3/2.
- For A=3, E=E_min+1=1, JΠT=1/2⁻1/2, the 3×3 centre-of-mass Hamiltonian matrix has entries: diagonal elements 2, 14/9, 35/18; off-diagonal elements 1/6, 2/(3√2), 2/(9√2).
- The intrinsic density matrix for A=3, E=0, JT=1/2 1/2 yields W(¹S₀)=1/2 and W(³S₁)=1/2.
- For A=3, E=1, JT=1/2 1/2, the intrinsic density matrices include W(¹S₀)=1/4, W(³S₁) with diagonal entries 1/4 and 1/2, and W(³P₀) with entries 1/12, -1/6, 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the translationally invariant shell model originally proposed by Kretzschmar and the approach of Vanagas (1971) and Shitikova (1970) for intrinsic coefficients of fractional parentage, which were limited to E_min and maximum orbital momentum states. The method enables practical calculation of two-particle oscillator intrinsic density matrices for arbitrary excitation, as required by the procedure of Launey and collaborators (1996), and provides a systematic alternative to earlier spurious-state elimination techniques such as those of Wildermuth (1977).
