# Memory effects and thermodynamics in strong field plasmas

> We study the evolution of a strong field plasma using a quantum Vlasov equation with a non-Markovian source term and a simple collision term, and calculate the time dependence of the energy- and number-density, and the temperature. The evolution of a plasma produced with RHIC-like initial conditions is well described by a low density approximation to the source term. However, non-Markovian aspects should be retained to obtain an accurate description of the early stages of an LHC-like plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-Markovian aspects of the particle production source term in a quantum Vlasov equation are important for accurately describing the early-stage evolution of a strong-field plasma under LHC-like initial conditions, but that a low-density approximation to the source term is sufficient for RHIC-like conditions.

## Method

The authors solve a quantum Vlasov equation with a non-Markovian source term (derived from earlier work) and a simple relaxation-time collision term, coupled to Maxwell's equation for the internal electric field via back-reaction. They model the initial heavy-ion collision as an external time-dependent electric field pulse, and compute the time evolution of the distribution function, energy density, particle number density, and a self-consistently determined quasi-equilibrium temperature. The full non-Markovian source term is contrasted with a low-density approximation that neglects the Pauli blocking factor.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition: \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV, corresponding to an energy density of 2–3 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- For RHIC-like conditions, the initial temperature at \(t=0\) is \(T^{\rm RHIC}(t=0) \sim 0.5\) GeV.
- For LHC-like conditions, the initial temperature at \(t=0\) is \(T^{\rm LHC}(t=0) \sim 0.9\) GeV.
- The external field parameters are: \(b = 0.5/\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 0.5\) fm, \(A_0^{\rm RHIC} = 4\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), and \(A_0^{\rm LHC} = 20\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).
- The relaxation time is \(\tau_r = 1/\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 1\) fm.
- The mass scale is set by \(m^2 \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 \sim 0.2\) GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pair production in strong fields using a quantum Vlasov equation with a Schwinger-like source term (Back) and simple collision terms (KM, Bahl, Eis, Nayak). It builds on the non-Markovian source term derived by Rau, KME, Basti, and PRD, and previously explored by PRD and Bloch. The paper enables future application of non-Markovian source terms to non-Abelian transport equations (Nayak) and provides a self-consistent determination of the temperature profile, improving on the earlier Ansatz used by Bloch.
