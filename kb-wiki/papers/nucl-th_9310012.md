# Asymmetry of the Missing Momentum Distribution in (e,e$'$p) Reactions and Color Trnasparency

> We suggest the measurement of the integrated asymmetry of teh missing momentum distribution in (e,e$'$p)reactions to check color transparency effects at intermediate momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the integrated asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution in (e,e$'$p) reactions can reveal color transparency effects at intermediate momentum transfers (ejected nucleon momenta $p' \sim 2$ to 10 GeV), where complete transparency is not yet expected and the effect is irregular and difficult to isolate from conventional nuclear effects.

## Method

The authors define an asymmetry $A = (N_+ - N_-)/(N_+ + N_-)$, where $N_+$ and $N_-$ are the number of events in positive and negative missing momentum ($p_m$) intervals, excluding a central window around $p_m = 0$ to avoid the symmetric plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) peak. They calculate $A$ in parallel kinematics ($p_t = 0$) using a three-channel model that includes the proton and its resonances, showing that color transparency preferentially produces compact states at positive $p_m$ due to the threshold dependence of excited baryon states on $p_m$.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry $A$ is calculated for missing momentum windows with lower thresholds $x$ from 0 to some tens of MeV and upper thresholds $y = 100$ MeV and $y = 200$ MeV, as shown in figures 2 and 3.
- The useful range of missing momentum $p_m$ for the measurement is from $-200$ to $200$ MeV.
- The ejected nucleon momentum region of interest is $p' \sim 2$ to 10 GeV, where present experiments (e.g., NE18 at SLAC) can operate, though complete transparency is expected only at $p' \sim 20$ GeV or more.
- The PWBA distribution changes by at least one order of magnitude in the range $0 \le p_m \le 200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of color transparency in (e,e$'$p) reactions by Bianconi, Boffi, and Kharzeev (1993) and others (references [3-7] in the paper), which showed that Fermi motion and the longitudinal component of missing momentum play a crucial role in the onset of color transparency at intermediate energies. The proposed asymmetry measurement avoids the model dependence and experimental difficulties of traditional transparency ratios, which require dividing measured yields by PWBA calculations. The approach enables tests of color transparency at currently accessible momentum transfers, though the authors note that the NE18 experiment at SLAC (reference [8]) has kinematics close to perpendicular and can only explore a limited $p_m$ range, motivating dedicated experiments.
