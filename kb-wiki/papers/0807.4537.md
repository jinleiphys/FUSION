# Empirical transverse charge densities in the deuteron

> Using the recent empirical information on the deuteron electromagnetic form factors we map out the transverse charge density in the deuteron as viewed from a light front moving towards the deuteron. The charge densities for a transversely polarized deuteron are characterized by monopole, dipole and quadrupole patterns.

## Key claim

Using empirical fits to deuteron electromagnetic form factor data, the transverse charge density in the deuteron can be mapped out from a light-front perspective, revealing a dip in the center for helicity-zero states and monopole, dipole, and quadrupole patterns for transversely polarized states, all without relying on model calculations of nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors express the light-front helicity form factors \(G^+_{\lambda'\lambda}\) in terms of the standard deuteron electromagnetic form factors \(G_C\), \(G_M\), and \(G_Q\) (Coulomb monopole, magnetic dipole, and Coulomb quadrupole). They then perform two-dimensional Fourier transforms of these helicity form factors to obtain transverse charge densities \(\rho^d_\lambda(b)\) for longitudinally polarized deuterons and \(\rho^d_{T\,s_\perp}(\vec b)\) for transversely polarized deuterons. The numerical evaluation uses the empirical parameterization (fit II) of Abbott et al. (2000), based on the functional forms of Kobushkin (1994).

## Key numbers

* The dipole form factor mass parameter used in the fit is \(\delta = 898.52\) MeV.
* The deuteron magnetic dipole moment is \(G_M(0) = 1.71\) (in units of \(e/(2M_d)\)).
* The deuteron electric quadrupole moment is \(G_Q(0) = 25.84 \pm 0.03\) (in units of \(e/M_d^2\)).
* The induced electric dipole moment for a transversely polarized deuteron with spin projection \(+1\) is \(\vec d^d_1 = -(\vec S_\perp \times \hat e_z) \{G_M(0) - 2\} (e/(2M_d))\), which vanishes for a point particle with \(g=2\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front transverse charge density formalism previously applied to nucleons (Pasquini 2007, Miller 2007, Carlson 2007) to the spin-1 deuteron case. It uses the empirical form factor parameterization of Abbott et al. (2000), based on the functional forms of Kobushkin (1994). The results are consistent with nuclear force model calculations predicting toroidal equidensity surfaces for helicity-zero deuterons (Forest 1996), but are derived directly from data without relying on such models. The formalism enables future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and studies of the deuteron's internal structure in a relativistically correct framework.
