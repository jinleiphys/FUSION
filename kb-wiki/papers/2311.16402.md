# A unified description of DGLAP, CSS, and BFKL: TMD factorization bridging large and small x

> This paper introduces a transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) factorization scheme designed to unify both large and small Bjorken-x regimes. We compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) quantum chromodynamics (QCD) corrections to the gluon TMD operator for an unpolarized hadron within this proposed scheme. This leads to the emergence of a new TMD evolution, incorporating those in transverse momentum, rapidity, and Bjorken-x. When matched to the collinear factorization scheme, our factorization scheme faithfully reproduces the well-established Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) and Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) evolutions. Conversely, matching with high-energy factorization not only yields the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) evolution but also reveals distinctive signatures of CSS logarithms. The development of this novel TMD factorization scheme, capable of seamlessly reconciling disparate Bjorken-x regimes and faithfully reproducing established QCD evolution equations, has the potential to significantly advance our comprehension of high-energy processes and three-dimensional parton structures of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new TMD factorization scheme that, through an NLO computation of the gluon TMD operator in the background field method, unifies the IR structure of TMDPDFs across both large and small Bjorken-x regimes without requiring an expansion in either \(b_\perp\) or \(x\); the scheme reproduces DGLAP and CSS evolution when matched to collinear factorization and BFKL evolution when matched to high-energy factorization, but only in the respective kinematic limits.

## Method

The authors perform a next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculation of the gluon TMD operator for an unpolarized hadron using the background field method, separating fields into "quantum" and "background" modes. They regulate transverse-momentum divergences with dimensional regularization (\(d=2-2\epsilon\)) and rapidity divergences with a regulator that replaces \(\int dk^-/k^-\) by \(\nu^\eta \int dk^-/k^- |k^+|^{-\eta}\). The calculation is carried out in the dilute limit with only two partons in the background, and the resulting IR structure is expressed in terms of gauge links without expanding in either \(b_\perp\) or \(x\).

## Key numbers

* The cusp anomalous dimension \(\Gamma^i_{\rm cusp}\) is known up to three loops in QCD (Moch 2004).
* The soft anomalous dimension \(\gamma^i_s\) is known to three loops (Baikov 2009, Li 2014, Moch 2004, Gehrmann 2010, Lee 2010, Blumlein 2021).
* The rapidity anomalous dimension \(\gamma^i_r\) is known to three loops (Li 2016, Vladimirov 2016).
* The leading twist-2 term of the collinear expansion of the TMDPDF is known up to NNLO (Echevarria 2015, Catani 2011, Catani 2012, Gehrmann 2014, Lubbert 2016, Echevarria 2016).

## Context

This work extends the standard TMD factorization approach (Collins 1981, 1984, 1987, 1989; Ji 2004) by addressing its limitation that neither the collinear matching expansion (which resums twists but loses \(b_\perp\) dependence) nor the eikonal/dipole expansion (which resums twists but loses \(x\) dependence) can fully reconstruct the IR structure of TMDPDFs. The proposed scheme enables a unified description of DGLAP (Dokshitzer 1977, Gribov 1972, Altarelli 1977), CSS (Collins 1981, 1985), and BFKL (Fadin 1975, Kuraev 1976, 1977, Balitsky 1978) evolutions within a single TMD framework, potentially advancing the understanding of three-dimensional parton structures and high-energy processes across all Bjorken-x regimes.
