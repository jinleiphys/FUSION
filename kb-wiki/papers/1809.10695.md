# Jet shape modifications in holographic dijet systems

> We present a coherent model that combines jet production from perturbative QCD with strongly-coupled jet-medium interactions described in holography. We use this model to study the modification of an ensemble of jets upon propagation through a quark-gluon plasma resembling central heavy ion collisions. Here the modification of the dijet asymmetry depends strongly on the subleading jet width, which can therefore be an important observable for studying jet-medium interactions. We furthermore show that the modification of the shape of the leading jet is relatively insensitive to the dijet asymmetry, whereas the subleading jet shape modification is much larger for more imbalanced dijets. Finally, we compare the results of our holographic model to a recent CMS measurement.

## Key claim
The paper presents a coherent model combining perturbative QCD jet production with strongly-coupled jet-medium interactions described holographically, and shows that the modification of the dijet asymmetry depends strongly on the subleading jet width, while the leading jet shape modification is relatively insensitive to the dijet asymmetry. The authors caution that the results should be seen as qualitative due to the omission of third jets, medium backreaction, and hadronization.

## Method
The authors construct an ensemble of initial string conditions in Anti-de-Sitter space (AdS) using pQCD input, where the distribution of holographic jet widths is fixed to the pQCD calculation of the variable \(C_{1}^{(1)}\) from Larkoski (2014). The evolution of null geodesics through a holographic plasma with a blast-wave temperature profile is computed, and the energy-momentum flow is extracted when the plasma reaches a freeze-out temperature of 175 MeV. The final jet shape and transverse momentum are defined within a cone of radius \(r=0.3\) around the average jet direction, and the results are compared to the JEWEL Monte Carlo generator and CMS data.

## Key numbers
- Transverse momentum cuts for dijet selection: \((p_{1},\,p_{2})>(120,\,30)\) GeV.
- Freeze-out temperature: 175 MeV.
- Initial proper time for string propagation: \(\tau_{0}=0.5\) fm/\(c\).
- Jet cone radius: \(r=0.3\).
- For the JEWEL comparison, a higher subleading jet cut of \(p_{2}>50\) GeV was used to match CMS data from Khachatryan (2016).
- The jet width is defined as \(w\equiv\int_{0}^{R=0.3} r\rho(r) {\rm d}r\).

## Context
This work extends the holographic jet model of Rajagopal (2016) and Brewer (2017) by studying dijet systems and their asymmetry distributions, confirming the finding of Milhano (2015) that energy loss fluctuations alone can explain the dijet asymmetry even for equal path lengths. The results are compared qualitatively to the JEWEL Monte Carlo generator (Zapp, 2008) and to recent CMS measurements from Sirunyan (2021), with the authors noting that both the holographic model and JEWEL are too simple to fully describe the experimental data, making the jet shape observables of special interest for future work to differentiate between strongly and weakly coupled regimes.
