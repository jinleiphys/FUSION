# $π^+ - π^-$ Asymmetry and the Neutron Skin in Heavy Nuclei

> In heavy nuclei the spatial distribution of protons and neutrons is different. At CERN SPS energies production of $π^+$ and $π^-$ differs for $pp$, $pn$, $np$ and $nn$ scattering. These two facts lead to an impact parameter dependence of the $π^+$ to $π^-$ ratio in $^{208}Pb + ^{208}Pb$ collisions. A recent experiment at CERN seems to confirm qualitatively these predictions. It may open a possibility for determination of neutron density distribution in nuclei.

## Key claim

The impact-parameter dependence of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio in ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies can be used to probe the neutron density distribution in heavy nuclei, but the existing preliminary experimental data can only be used as an indication of the effect because the definition of "central" versus "peripheral" collisions was not specified.

## Method

The authors model inclusive pion production in heavy-ion collisions by summing over elementary nucleon-nucleon (pp, pn, np, nn) collisions, using either a binary collision model or a wounded nucleon model. The numbers of each collision type are calculated from the proton and neutron thickness functions of the colliding nuclei, using proton and neutron densities from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Skyrme interaction SLy4. Elementary π⁺ and π⁻ cross sections are taken from the HIJING model or from a fit to NA49 experimental p+p and p+n data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R₊/₋ = (dσ/dxF for π⁺) / (dσ/dxF for π⁻) is calculated as a function of the Feynman variable xF for selected impact parameters b in ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies.
- The model results are reliable up to xF ≈ 0.2; above xF ≈ 0.2 the HIJING-based calculations deviate from results based directly on experimental data.
- The auxiliary ratio r(xF) ≡ dσ(pp→π⁻)/dσ(pp→π⁺) is fitted to NA49 data with the form r(xF) = c_r (1 − xF)^(n_r).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron skin using radiochemical methods on antiprotonic atoms (radiochemical3) and X-ray spectra of antiprotonic atoms (Trzcinska), as well as theoretical Hartree-Fock (HL98) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB_nuclear_skins) calculations. The approach is motivated by a recent CERN experiment (Rybicki) that observed a difference in the π⁺/π⁻ ratio between central and peripheral ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb collisions. The authors suggest that with well-defined (xF, b) experimental data, this method could become competitive with proton-nucleus elastic scattering, antiprotonic atoms, and parity-violating electron scattering for determining neutron density distributions.
