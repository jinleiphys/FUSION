# Perturbative quantum Monte Carlo method for nuclear physics

> While first order perturbation theory is routinely used in quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations, higher-order terms present significant numerical challenges. We present a new approach for computing perturbative corrections in projection QMC calculations. We demonstrate the method by computing nuclear ground state energies up to second order for a realistic chiral interaction. We calculate the binding energies of several light nuclei up to $^{16}$O by expanding the Hamiltonian around the Wigner SU(4) limit and find good agreement with data. In contrast to the natural ordering of the perturbative series, we find remarkably large second order energy corrections. This occurs because the perturbing interactions break the symmetries of the unperturbed Hamiltonian. Our method is free from the sign problem and can be applied to QMC calculations for many-body systems in nuclear physics, condensed matter physics, ultracold atoms, and quantum chemistry.

## Key claim

The authors present a perturbative quantum Monte Carlo (ptQMC) method that computes second-order energy corrections without requiring the full spectrum of excited states, and they find that second-order corrections are remarkably large, contrary to the natural ordering of the perturbative series, because the perturbing interactions break the Wigner SU(4) symmetry of the unperturbed Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors split the nuclear Hamiltonian into a dominant term \(V_0\) (a Wigner SU(4)-symmetric interaction) and a correction \(V_C\) (the full chiral interaction including two-body and three-body forces up to N\(^2\)LO plus Coulomb). They compute ground-state energies by expanding the imaginary-time projection around the zeroth-order transfer matrix \(M_0\), evaluating matrix elements and overlaps via auxiliary-field Monte Carlo sampling. The second-order correction \(\delta E_2\) is obtained from an integral over auxiliary fields that is made tractable by a variable shift that cancels linear terms causing the sign problem, leaving a product of a normal distribution and a slowly varying function.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for \(^{16}\)O: \(E_0 = -94.1(2)\) MeV, \(E_1 = -99.7(2)\) MeV, \(E_2 = -129.4(2)\) MeV, compared to experimental \(-127.6\) MeV.
- For \(^{4}\)He: \(E_0 = -23.1(0)\) MeV, \(E_1 = -23.3(0)\) MeV, \(E_2 = -29.1(1)\) MeV, compared to experimental \(-28.3\) MeV.
- For \(^{3}\)H: \(E_0 = -7.41(3)\) MeV, \(E_1 = -5.33(3)\) MeV, \(E_2 = -8.32(3)\) MeV, compared to experimental \(-8.48\) MeV.
- The second-order correction \(\delta E_2\) accounts for about 20% of the total binding energy for all nuclei with \(A \geq 4\).
- Lattice spacing \(a = 1.32\) fm; temporal step \(a_t = 1/1000\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{16}\)O, varying the zeroth-order Hamiltonian by 10% and 20% shifts the zeroth-order energy by about 50 MeV, but the first- and second-order energies at \(\lambda=1\) vary by only about 4 MeV and 2.4 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) framework, where previous calculations were mostly limited to first-order perturbation theory (Epelbaum 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014; Elhatisari 2015). The method builds on the Wigner SU(4) limit (Wigner 1937; Elhatisari 2016; Lu 2019) and uses a non-locally smeared SU(4) interaction from Lu (2019) as the zeroth-order Hamiltonian. The ptQMC approach enables systematic treatment of realistic chiral interactions without sign problems, opening the way to more accurate calculations of medium-mass nuclei and potentially enabling studies of electroweak processes that require tensor forces, though such interactions may need renormalization group pre-processing.
