# Phenomenology of TMD parton distributions in Drell-Yan and $Z^0$ boson production in a hadron structure oriented approach

> We present a first practical implementation of a recently proposed hadron structure oriented (HSO) approach to TMD phenomenology applied to Drell-Yan like processes, including lepton pair production at moderate $Q^2$ and $Z^0$ boson production. We compare and contrast general features of our methodology with other common practices and emphasize the improvements derived from our approach that we view as essential for applications where extracting details of nonperturbative transverse hadron structure is a major goal. These include the HSO's preservation of a basic TMD parton-model-like framework even while accounting for full TMD factorization and evolution, explicit preservation of the integral relationship between TMD and collinear pdfs, and the ability to meaningfully compare different theoretical models of nonperturbative TMD parton distributions. In our examples, we show that there is significant sensitivity at moderate $Q^2$ to both the form of the nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence and the parametrization of collinear parton densities. However, we also find that evolving to $Q^2 = M_Z^2$, without fitting, results in a satisfactory postdiction of existing data for $Z^0$ production, nearly independently of the modeling of nonperturbative transverse momentum behavior. We argue that this demonstrates that moderate $Q$ measurements should be given greater weight than high $Q$ measurements in extractions of nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence. We also obtain new extractions of the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel within the HSO approach. We discuss its features and compare with some earlier extractions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first practical implementation of the "hadron structure oriented" (HSO) approach to TMD phenomenology, demonstrating that while moderate Q^2 Drell-Yan data show significant sensitivity to both the form of nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence and collinear PDF parametrizations, evolving to Q^2 = M_Z^2 yields a satisfactory postdiction of Z^0 boson production data nearly independently of the nonperturbative modeling, provided the HSO framework's constraints are respected.

## Method

The authors constructed phenomenological parametrizations of TMD parton distribution functions at an input scale Q_0, strictly adhering to TMD factorization and evolution derivations while preserving the integral relationship between TMD and collinear PDFs. They fitted parameters of two different nonperturbative transverse momentum models (a Gaussian parametrization and a spectator model) to Drell-Yan data from the E288 and E605 experiments at moderate Q, then evolved the resulting TMD PDFs upward to Q^2 = M_Z^2 to postdict Z^0 boson production cross sections measured by CDF and D0 without refitting. The Collins-Soper kernel was also extracted within the HSO approach and compared with earlier extractions.

## Key numbers

- The E288 experiment data at moderate Q^2 (approximately 20-50 GeV^2) and E605 data were used for fitting; both the Gaussian and spectator model parametrizations gave "comparable and very satisfactory descriptions" of these data
- Evolution to Q^2 = M_Z^2 (approximately 8300 GeV^2) produced a "satisfactory postdiction" of existing CDF and D0 data for Z^0 production
- The hard vertex factor for Drell-Yan scattering at order alpha_s includes the coefficient C_F (-16 + 7 pi^2 / 3) for the first-order correction
- The QED coupling at the Z boson mass was taken as alpha_em(M_Z) = 1/127.951

## Context

This work extends the HSO (or "bottom up") approach previously developed by three of the authors (Gonzalez-Hernandez 2022, Gonzalez-Hernandez 2023) by providing the first practical phenomenological implementation applied to Drell-Yan and Z^0 production. It contrasts with more traditional TMD parametrizations that lack the integral normalization constraint connecting TMD and collinear PDFs, and addresses issues raised in earlier work (Boglione 2014, Gonzalez-Hernandez 2018, Bacchetta 2019) regarding difficulties in describing large transverse momentum tails. The approach enables future combined TMD-collinear phenomenological frameworks and provides a method for comparing different theoretical models of nonperturbative TMD parton distributions, such as those from lattice QCD.
