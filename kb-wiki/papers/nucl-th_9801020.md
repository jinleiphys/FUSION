# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Parity Mixing

> We derive the expressions of the vector and axial current from a chiral Lagrangian restricted to nucleons and pions. They display mixing terms between the axial and vector currents. We study the modifications in the nuclear medium of the coupling constants of the axial current, namely the pion decay constant and the nucleonic axial one due to the requirements of chiral symmetry. We express the renormalizations in terms of the local scalar pion density. The latter also governs the quark condensate evolu- tion and we discuss the link between this evolution and the renormaliza- tions. In the case of the nucleon axial coupling constant this renormali- zation corresponds to a new type of exchange currents, with two exchanged pions. We give an estimate for the resulting quenching. Although moderate it helps explaining the quenching experimentally observed.

## Key claim

The authors derive that in a nuclear medium, the renormalization of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\) and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) due to chiral symmetry restoration can be expressed in terms of the local scalar pion density, but unlike the simple universal relation found in a hot pion gas, the link to the quark condensate evolution is broken in dense matter because of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg form, as used in Lynn (1990)) restricted to nucleons and pions, and derive explicit expressions for the vector and axial currents. They then compute the in-medium renormalization of the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) and the nucleon axial coupling constant \(g_A\) by expanding the currents to first order in the squared pion field and taking thermal or nuclear-medium averages. For the nuclear medium, the renormalization of \(g_A\) is recast as a two-pion exchange current operator, which is evaluated between correlated two-nucleon wave functions using a static approximation and a short-range correlation hole of radius \(r_0 = 0.6\) fm.

## Key numbers

* In a hot pion gas, the renormalized pion decay constant is \(f_\pi^* = f_\pi (1 - \frac{1}{3} \langle \phi^2 / f_\pi^2 \rangle)\), giving a factor 2/3 relative to the condensate evolution.
* In symmetric nuclear matter at normal density, the chiral-symmetry-induced quenching of \(g_A\) is about 10% (i.e., \(g_A^*/g_A \approx 0.9\)).
* For neutron decay in a neutron gas at the same density, the quenching is about 15%.
* The effective sigma commutator from the pion field beyond the correlation hole is \((\Sigma_N)_{\text{eff}} \approx 21\) MeV for point-like pion emitters, and about 19 MeV from a chiral soliton model (Wakamatsu, 1995).
* The additional quenching corresponds to an effective increase in the Landau-Migdal parameter \(\delta g_{N\Delta}' \approx 0.16\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of parity mixing between vector and axial correlators, first established by Dey et al. (1990) for a hot pion gas, to the nuclear medium. It contrasts with the thermal case (also studied by Steele et al. (1995) and Chanfray et al. (1996)) where the renormalization of \(g_A\) and \(f_\pi\) follows a simple fraction of the quark condensate evolution. The authors show that in dense matter, short-range correlations break this universality, illustrating a point raised by T. Ericson (1997) about spatial fluctuations of the condensate. The predicted 10% quenching of \(g_A\) helps explain the large observed quenching of the Gamow-Teller sum rule, which otherwise requires a Landau-Migdal parameter \(g_{N\Delta}'\) as large as 0.6–0.7 (Dellafiore et al., 1996).
