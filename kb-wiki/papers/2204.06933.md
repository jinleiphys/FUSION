# New calculations of neutron and kaon decays

> We describe two recent developments of the radiative correction theory in semileptonic beta decays. For neutron, a novel dispersive analysis of the $γW$-box diagram making use of neutrino scattering data leads to an improved precision and a shifted central value of $V_{ud}$; for kaon, an appropriate combination of meson form factors, lattice QCD inputs and chiral perturbation theory gives a much-improved determination of the radiative corrections in semileptonic kaon decays. These new calculations help to unveil/sharpen a series of anomalies in the measurements of $V_{ud}$ and $V_{us}$ that may point towards the existence of new physics.

# New calculations of neutron and kaon decays

## Key claim

The paper claims that a novel dispersive analysis of the gamma-W-box diagram for neutron decay, using neutrino scattering data, reduces the theory uncertainty in the extraction of V_ud by a factor of two while shifting its central value, and that a combination of meson form factors, lattice QCD, and chiral perturbation theory improves the determination of radiative corrections in semileptonic kaon decays to the 10^-4 level. The hedge is that these improvements reveal tensions in first-row CKM unitarity at the 3-sigma level that may point to new physics, but could also be due to unexpectedly large Standard Model corrections.

## Method

For neutron decay, the authors applied a dispersion relation treatment to the single-nucleon forward axial gamma-W-box diagram, relating the loop integrand to the parity-odd structure function F_3^(0) measured from neutrino/antineutrino-nucleus scattering via a Regge-exchange picture. For kaon semileptonic decays (K -> pi l nu), they split the radiative corrections into unsuppressed terms (calculated non-perturbatively using elastic form factors and lattice QCD inputs for the gamma-W-box diagram) and suppressed terms (computed to fixed order in chiral perturbation theory), following Sirlin's representation framework.

## Key numbers

* The new analysis of the gamma-W-box diagram reduced the uncertainty in the previous state-of-the-art determination by a factor of 2.
* The value of |V_ud| shifted from 0.97420(21) in early 2018 to 0.97370(14) in late 2018 after the new dispersive analysis.
* The current value of |V_ud| from superallowed decays is 0.97373(31), including nuclear structure uncertainties.
* For K_e3 decays, the long-distance electromagnetic radiative correction (delta_EM) is 11.6(2)(1)(1)(2) x 10^-2 for K^0 e and 2.1(2)(1)(4)(1) x 10^-2 for K^+ e, compared to pure ChPT values of 9.9(1.9)(1.1) x 10^-2 and 1.0(1.9)(1.6) x 10^-2 respectively.
* For K_mu3 decays, the long-distance electromagnetic radiative correction is 15.4(2)(1)(1)(2)(2) x 10^-2 for K^0 mu and 0.5(2)(1)(4)(2)(2) x 10^-2 for K^+ mu, compared to pure ChPT values of 14.0(1.9)(1.1) x 10^-2 and 0.2(1.9)(1.6) x 10^-2 respectively.

## Context

This work extends Sirlin's representation, originally formulated by Sirlin (1977) for superallowed beta decays, to generic semileptonic beta decays. For neutron decay, it improves upon the earlier determination by Marciano and Sirlin (2005) and was later confirmed by independent studies including Czarnecki et al. (2019), Seng et al. (2020), Shiells et al. (2020), and Hayen (2020). For kaon decays, it supersedes the previous state-of-the-art computation based on chiral perturbation theory by Cirigliano et al. (2008), reducing the theory uncertainty from the 10^-3 level to 10^-4. The results enable sharper tests of first-row CKM unitarity and may motivate searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
