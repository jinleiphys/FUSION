# Unitarized Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and the $Δ$ Resonance in $πN $ Scattering

> Based on the recent phenomenological analysis done by M. Mojzis, Eur. Phys. Jour. {\bf C2} (1998) 182, of $πN $ scattering within the framework of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory we predict, after a suitable unitarization of the amplitude, the $Δ$ resonance at $\sqrt{s} = M_Δ= 1238 \er{22}{18}{\rm MeV} $ and with a width of $ Γ_Δ= 150 \er{43}{31} {\rm MeV} $ in excellent agreement with the experimental numbers. The error bars reflect only the uncertainties in the low energy parameters as determined from an extrapolation to threshold of experimental data. We also describe satisfactorily, within errors, the phase shifts up to $\sqrt{s} \sim 1300 {\rm MeV} $.

# Unitarized Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and the Δ Resonance in πN Scattering

## Key claim

The Δ resonance at 1232 MeV can be generated from Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) threshold parameters alone, without introducing explicit Δ degrees of freedom, provided the unitarization method treats the leading 1/(f²M) finite-nucleon-mass corrections as equally important as the static 1/f² terms, unlike the conventional Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) which expands those corrections as small and fails.

## Method

The authors re-fit the low-energy constants of HBChPT to threshold data (scattering lengths, effective ranges, Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, and σ-term) using a consistent 1/M and 1/f² expansion of the partial-wave amplitudes, obtaining two parameter sets (Set I from Mojzis (1998) and Set II from their own re-analysis). They then construct a unitarized P₃₃ amplitude by inverting the chiral expansion of the inverse amplitude, keeping the full 1/(f²M) and 1/(f²M²) corrections in the denominator of the leading term and only the 1/f⁴ term in the numerator, thereby preserving exact elastic unitarity while reproducing the HBChPT threshold results.

## Key numbers

- Δ resonance mass: M_Δ = 1238⁺²²₋₁₈ MeV (Set I parameters), compared to experimental 1232 ± 2 MeV
- Δ resonance width: Γ_Δ = 150⁺⁴³₋₃₁ MeV (Set I parameters), compared to experimental 120 ± 10 MeV
- P₃₃ scattering length from unitarized amplitude: a₃₃¹|_Unitarized = 80.2 GeV⁻³, compared to HBChPT value 81.0 GeV⁻³ and experimental 81.5 ± 0.8 GeV⁻³
- Conventional IAM gives a₃₃¹|_IAM = 22.8 GeV⁻³, a completely inconsistent result
- Phase shifts described satisfactorily up to √s ∼ 1300 MeV
- Static limit (M → ∞) yields a resonance at √s ∼ 1450 MeV, showing finite-mass corrections are essential

## Context

This work extends the HBChPT analysis of πN scattering by Mojzis (1998) and the unitarization ideas applied to meson-meson scattering by the same authors (1999). It directly contradicts the conventional IAM approach of Gomez Nicola and Pelaez (1999), which treated the 1/(f²M) corrections as small and obtained low-energy constants (e.g., b₁₉ = −23.13) that imply an unrealistic πNN coupling constant g_πNN = 19.2 versus the experimental 13.4 ± 0.1. The method enables prediction of the Δ resonance from threshold data alone, and suggests that a relativistic chiral counting for baryons, as pursued by Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and Gegelia (1999), may be more appropriate than the heavy-baryon expansion. Future work should extend the unitarization to other partial waves and allow re-fitting of low-energy constants against data up to the resonance region.
