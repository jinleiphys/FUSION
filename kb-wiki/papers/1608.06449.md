# Equation of state for neutron star matter with NJL model and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation

> As the interior density of a neutron star can become very high, it has been expected and discussed that quark matter may exist inside it. To describe the transition from hadron to quark phases (and vice versa), there are mainly two methods; one is the first-order phase transition, and the other is the crossover phenomenon. In the present study, using the flavor-SU (3) NJL model with the vector coupling interaction, we have calculated the equation of state for the quark phase at high density. Furthermore, for the hadron phase at low density, we have used two kinds of the equations of state; one is a relatively soft one by the QHD model, and the other is a stiff one calculated with relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. Using those equations of state for the two phases, we have investigated the influence of various choices of parameters concerning the crossover region on the mass and radius of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how different interpolation schemes (energy-density, pressure-density, and pressure-energy) for the hadron-quark crossover region affect neutron star mass and radius, finding that the choice of interpolation method and the central density parameter \(\bar{\rho}\) strongly influence the results, with the energy-density interpolation violating causality for \(\bar{\rho} \geq 4\rho_{0}\).

## Method

The authors calculated the equation of state for the quark phase at high density using the flavor-SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector coupling interaction and the Hatsuda-Kunihiro parameter set. For the hadron phase at low density, they used two equations of state: a soft one from the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model and a stiff one from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approximation. They then applied three phenomenological interpolation schemes (energy-density, pressure-density, and pressure-energy) to connect the hadron and quark phases, varying the central density \(\bar{\rho}\) and width \(\Gamma\) of the crossover region.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_{0} = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- The QHD model maximum neutron star mass: about 1.6 times the solar mass
- The DBHF model can explain observed neutron star masses with more than 2 times the solar mass without any quark core
- The crossover central density \(\bar{\rho}\) was varied among \(3\rho_{0}\), \(4\rho_{0}\), and \(5\rho_{0}\)
- The crossover width \(\Gamma\) was fixed to \(\rho_{0}\) (the change of \(\Gamma\) did not give a large difference in the result)
- For \(\bar{\rho} \geq 4\rho_{0}\), the energy-density interpolation scheme violated causality

## Context

This work extends the crossover approach to hadron-quark transitions in neutron stars previously studied by Masuda et al. (2013) and Baym et al. (2014), comparing it with the first-order phase transition method. The study uses the DBHF approximation from Katayama and Saito (2014) and the QHD model as contrasting hadronic equations of state. The results show that future high-precision observations of massive neutron star radii could determine which crossover mechanism and parameters are most realistic.
