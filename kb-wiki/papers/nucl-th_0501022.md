# Resonances from Baryon decuplet-Meson octet interaction

> The s-wave interactions of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons is studied in a unitarized coupled channel approach. We obtain a fair agreement for mass and width of several 3/2- resonances. In particular, the Xi(1820), the Lambda(1520) and the Sigma(1670) states are well reproduced. Other resonances are predicted and also the couplings of the observed resonances to the various channels are evaluated.

# Resonances from Baryon decuplet-Meson octet interaction

## Key claim
The s-wave interaction of the baryon decuplet with the pseudoscalar meson octet, treated in a unitarized coupled-channel approach, dynamically generates several 3/2⁻ resonances whose masses and widths agree fairly with experiment, but only for those poles that survive the gradual breaking of SU(3) symmetry down to physical masses; some predicted poles disappear at threshold and are not observable as resonances.

## Method
The authors start from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for the baryon decuplet–meson octet interaction and extract s-wave transition amplitudes V_{ij} = −(1/4f²) C_{ij} (k⁰ + k′⁰). These amplitudes are used as the kernel of the Bethe–Salpeter equation to obtain a unitarized T-matrix in coupled channels. Poles are searched for in the complex energy plane, first in the SU(3) symmetric limit (with average masses) and then along trajectories as a parameter x breaks SU(3) symmetry continuously up to physical masses.

## Key numbers
- In the SU(3) limit, the octet representation pole lies at 1673 MeV (real axis, bound state); the decuplet representation pole lies at 1738 MeV.
- For S = −1, I = 1, three poles are found on the second Riemann sheet: (1632 ± i15) MeV, (1687 ± i178) MeV, and (2021 ± i45) MeV.
- The pole at 1632 MeV couples most strongly to ΔK̅ (|g_i| = 3.7) and is associated with the 4-star resonance Σ(1670); its width of about 30 MeV comes from Σπ decay.
- The pole at 1687 MeV couples strongly to Σπ (|g_i| = 3.0) and ΞK (|g_i| = 3.0); the pole at 2021 MeV couples most strongly to ΞK (|g_i| = 2.7).

## Context
This work extends the successful unitary coupled-channel approach previously applied to the meson octet–baryon octet system, which dynamically generated 1/2⁻ resonances (Kaiser, 1995; Oset and Ramos, 1998; Oller and Meißner, 2001). It builds on the earlier finding by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002) that the decuplet–octet interaction also produces resonances. The present paper systematically maps pole trajectories under SU(3) breaking and computes couplings, enabling predictions for partial decay widths into decuplet baryon + pseudoscalar meson channels, quantities for which experimental data remain scarce.
