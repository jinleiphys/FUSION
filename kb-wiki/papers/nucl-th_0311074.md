# Chiral symmetry restoration in strange hadronic matter

> The phase transition of chiral symmetry restoration in strange hadronic matter is studied in the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. When the baryon density is larger than a critical density $ρ_c$, the minimal energy density of the system occurs at the point where the effective masses of nucleon, $Λ$ or $Ξ$ drop to zero. The physical quantities change discontinuously at this density and the system will be in the phase of chiral symmetry restoration. A rich phase structure of strange hadronic matter with different strangeness fraction $f_s$ is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral symmetry restoration can occur in hadronic matter (without requiring deconfinement) when the baryon density exceeds a critical value, but only under the specific condition that the minimum of the energy density shifts to the boundary where the effective masses of nucleons, Λ, or Ξ hyperons drop to zero, a result that depends on the strangeness fraction of the system.

## Method

The authors applied the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, which includes quarks confined into baryons by an effective potential and quark-meson interactions based on SU(3) chiral symmetry. They solved the mean field equations for the scalar meson fields σ, ζ, and χ and the vector meson fields ω and φ at zero temperature, minimizing the thermodynamical potential to find the baryon density and strangeness fraction at which the effective baryon masses vanish discontinuously.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryon density for nonstrange nuclear matter: 0.28 fm⁻³, where the system enters phase II (zero effective nucleon mass).
- At strangeness fraction f_s = 0.8, the effective nucleon mass drops to zero at a baryon density of 0.46 fm⁻³; at densities above 0.69 fm⁻³, both nucleon and Λ masses are zero.
- Maximum critical density of about 0.5 fm⁻³ occurs for f_s ≈ 1.0.
- Saturation density of normal nuclear matter ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; binding energy at saturation is 16 MeV.
- Maximal binding energy in the chiral symmetry restoration phase is about 70 MeV for a strangeness fraction of about 1.5.
- For f_s ≈ 1.2, the first maximum of binding energy is about 19 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of chiral symmetry restoration in nonstrange nuclear matter (Wang et al., 2003) to systems including Λ, Σ, and Ξ hyperons. It contrasts with earlier effective chiral models by Papazoglou et al. (1999) which found no phase transition for chiral symmetry restoration, and with results from Bicudo (1999) suggesting chiral symmetry breaking strengthens in nuclear matter. The model enables future study of the deconfinement phase transition in the same framework, comparing the densities at which chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement occur.
