# Density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral three-nucleon forces

> We derive density-dependent corrections to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force. To this order there are six distinct one-loop diagrams contributing to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering T-matrix. Analytic expressions are presented for each of these in both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter as well as nuclear matter with a small isospin asymmetry. The results are combined with the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V(low-k) to obtain an effective density-dependent interaction suitable for nuclear structure calculations. The in-medium interaction is decomposed into partial waves up to orbital angular momentum L = 2. Our results should be particularly useful in calculations where an exact treatment of the chiral three-nucleon force would otherwise be computationally prohibitive.

## Key claim

The paper derives density-dependent corrections to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force, but these corrections are limited to one-loop order and are intended for use in nuclear structure calculations where an exact treatment of the chiral three-nucleon force would be computationally prohibitive.

## Method

The authors evaluated six distinct one-loop diagrams contributing to the in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering T-matrix, generated by the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force (2π-exchange, 1π-exchange, and contact terms). They derived analytic expressions for each diagram in both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and nuclear matter with a small isospin asymmetry, then combined these density-dependent corrections with the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V(low-k) at a resolution scale of Λ_low-k = 2.1 fm⁻¹. The resulting effective interaction was decomposed into partial waves up to orbital angular momentum L = 2.

## Key numbers

- The low-momentum cutoff scale used is Λ_low-k = 2.1 fm⁻¹, chosen to match the scale at which the two-body and three-body force contributions are decimated.
- The low-energy constants for the three-nucleon force at this scale are c_D(2.1 fm⁻¹) = -2.062 and c_E(2.1 fm⁻¹) = -0.625, with Λ_χ = 0.7 GeV.
- The chiral 3N force parameters are c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -3.2 GeV⁻¹, and c₄ = 5.4 GeV⁻¹, with g_A = 1.29 and f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The c_D term reduces the bare 1π-exchange by roughly 16% at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For a heavy nucleus such as ²⁰⁸Pb, the relative neutron excess is of order δ_np ≈ 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the approach of deriving density-dependent two-body interactions that reflect underlying three-nucleon forces, building on the authors' previous finding (Holt 2009) that such corrections suppress the ¹⁴C to ¹⁴N Gamow-Teller transition and help explain the anomalously long lifetime of ¹⁴C. The results are combined with the low-momentum NN potential V(low-k) developed by Bogner and collaborators (2002, 2003), which evolves any bare NN interaction to a nearly universal potential at the decimation scale. This enables nuclear structure calculations that would otherwise require computationally prohibitive exact treatments of chiral three-nucleon forces, and the isospin-asymmetric corrections are expected to be relevant for heavy nuclei.
