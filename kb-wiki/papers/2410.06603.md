# Investigation of 90,92Zr(n,γ) 91,93Zr in the s process nucleosynthesis

> The neutron capture rates and Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS) for 90Zr(n,γ) 91Zr and 92Zr(n,γ) 93Zr processes have been computed within the framework of Talys v1.96. The effects of phenomenological nuclear level density (NLD) parameters and the gamma strength functions (GSFs) on Maxwellian averaged cross sections and neutron capture rates are examined both quantitatively and qualitatively. The present model based computed data for MACS and reaction rates gives a good comparison with the existing literature. The fine-tuning of the statistical models nuclear properties (level density and gamma-ray strength) to reproduce experimental data will allow the detailed investigation of the s process network.

## Key claim

The authors compute neutron capture rates and Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS) for 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr and 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr using the Talys v1.96 code, finding that the Kopecky-Uhl Lorentzian gamma strength function combined with the constant temperature model or back-shifted Fermi gas model for nuclear level density provides the best fit to experimental data, but the results depend on which combination of phenomenological nuclear level density and gamma strength function models is chosen.

## Method

The authors used the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model implemented in Talys v1.96 to compute neutron capture cross sections and MACS for 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr and 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr at neutron energies up to 100 keV. They systematically varied three phenomenological nuclear level density models (constant temperature model, back-shifted Fermi gas model, and generalized superfluid model) and three gamma strength functions (Kopecky-Uhl Lorentzian, Brink-Axel Lorentzian, and Gogny D1M QRPA) in all combinations, while keeping the Koning-Delaroche optical model potential fixed. The computed MACS were compared with experimental data from Tagliente et al., Boldeman et al., and Macklin, as well as evaluated nuclear data libraries including ENDF, TENDL, and JENDL.

## Key numbers

- For 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr at kT=30 keV, the best-fit MACS (using Kopecky-Uhl GSF with CTM) was 21.29 mb, compared to the experimental value of 19.3±0.9 mb from Tagliente et al. (2011), giving a percentage difference of 3.5%
- For 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr at kT=30 keV, the MACS computed with Kopecky-Uhl GSF and BSFM was 21.84 mb, giving a percentage difference of 7.5% from Tagliente et al. (2011)
- For 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr at kT=30 keV, the best-fit MACS (using Kopecky-Uhl GSF with CTM) was 40.90 mb, compared to the experimental value of 38±3 mb from Tagliente et al. (2011), giving a percentage difference of 16.3%
- For 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr at kT=30 keV, the MACS computed with Kopecky-Uhl GSF and BSFM was 39.44 mb, giving a percentage difference of 14.7% from Tagliente et al. (2011)
- The radiative capture rates for 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr are higher than those for 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr by up to 41-43% across the temperature range T9=0.0001 to T9=0.5
- At T9=0.0001, the radiative capture rate for 90Zr(n,γ)91Zr was 1.61×10^7 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1 and for 92Zr(n,γ)93Zr was 2.26×10^7 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1

## Context

This work extends previous experimental measurements of neutron capture cross sections on zirconium isotopes by Tagliente et al. (2011) at the n_TOF facility at CERN and by Boldeman et al. (1976), providing a systematic theoretical investigation of how different nuclear level density models and gamma strength functions affect the calculated MACS. The results enable more reliable input for s-process nucleosynthesis network calculations in AGB stars and massive stars, particularly for the weak s-process component that produces elements from iron to zirconium, and confirm that 92Zr has a lower s-process component than 90Zr due to its non-magic neutron number (N=52 versus N=50).
