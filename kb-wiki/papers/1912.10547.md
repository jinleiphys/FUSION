# Corrections to the Bethe formula for average ionization energy loss of relativistic charged particles in solids. I. Mott's corrections

> Based on the proposed representation of the Mott corrections to the Bethe stopping formula in the form of rapidly convergent series of quantities bilinear in the Mott partial amplitudes, the numerical calculations were performed for these corrections over a wide range of nuclear charge number of the incident particles at various values of their relative velocity.

# Corrections to the Bethe formula for average ionization energy loss of relativistic charged particles in solids. I. Mott's corrections

## Key claim
The Mott corrections to the Bethe stopping formula, expressed as a rapidly converging series of quantities bilinear in the Mott partial amplitudes, become increasingly significant for incident particles with high nuclear charge number Z and high relative velocity β, but the paper presents numerical results only for the range 4 ≤ Z ≤ 92 and 0.75 ≤ β ≤ 0.95, not for all possible values.

## Method
The author derived an analytical expression for the Mott correction Δ_Mott to the Bethe stopping formula by rewriting the energy-loss integral in terms of center-of-mass scattering angle and using partial-wave expansions of the Mott scattering amplitudes. The resulting series (Equation 16) sums terms bilinear in the Mott partial amplitudes F_l and their Sommerfeld-Moyer-Furry approximations, with terms behaving asymptotically as l^{-2}. Numerical calculations were then performed by summing this series for incident particles with nuclear charge numbers from Z=4 (beryllium) to Z=92 (uranium) at three values of relative velocity β = 0.75, 0.85, and 0.95.

## Key numbers
- For beryllium (Z=4.000): Φ_Mott/2 = 0.031 at β=0.75, 0.035 at β=0.85, 0.039 at β=0.95
- For gold (Z=79.000): Φ_Mott/2 = 1.076 at β=0.75, 1.267 at β=0.85, 1.450 at β=0.95
- For uranium (Z=92.000): Φ_Mott/2 = 1.251 at β=0.75, 1.532 at β=0.85, 1.769 at β=0.95
- The calculations cover the range 4 ≤ Z ≤ 92 and 0.75 ≤ β ≤ 0.95
- The series terms behave asymptotically as l^{-2}, ensuring absolute convergence

## Context
This work extends the analytical results of Voskresenskaya, Tarasov, and collaborators (VSTT) who derived an exact expression for the Mott correction in series form, by providing actual numerical values over a wide range of Z and β. The Mott correction addresses the failure of the first-order Born approximation for high-Z projectiles in the Bethe stopping formula, which was originally developed by Bethe (1930) and extended by Bloch (1933). The author notes that future work should compare these results with the Lindhard-Sorensen corrections in the point nucleus approximation for γ ≤ 10, and with available experimental data.
