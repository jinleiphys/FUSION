# Fluctuation-induced higher-derivative couplings and infrared dynamics of the Quark-Meson-Diquark Model

> In a qualitative study, the low-energy properties of the $\text{SO}\!\left(6\right)$-symmetric Quark-Meson-Diquark Model as an effective model for two-color Quantum Chromodynamics are investigated within the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach. In particular, we compute the infrared scaling behavior of fluctuation-induced higher-derivative couplings of the linear Quark-Meson-Diquark Model and map the resulting renormalized effective action onto its nonlinear counterpart. The higher-derivative couplings of the nonlinear model, which we identify as the low-energy couplings of the Quark-Meson-Diquark Model, are therefore entirely determined by the FRG flow of their linear equivalents. This grants full access to their scaling behavior and provides insights into conceptual aspects of purely bosonic effective models, as they are treated within the FRG. In this way, the presented work is understood as an immediate extension of our recent advances in the $\text{SO}\!\left(4\right)$-symmetric Quark-Meson Model beyond common FRG approximations.

## Key claim

The low-energy couplings of the SO(6)-symmetric Quark-Meson-Diquark Model (QMDM) are entirely determined by the FRG flow of their linear equivalents, and the overall FRG scaling is almost entirely driven by fermionic degrees of freedom even down to lowest momentum scales (below about 100 MeV), which implies a late decoupling of composite dynamics from fundamental fluctuations and characterizes an upper reliability bound for the pionic description.

## Method

The authors used the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach with a derivative expansion of the effective average action up to and including fourth order in bosonic fields and their space-time derivatives, going substantially beyond the local potential approximation (LPA) and LPA'. They computed the infrared scaling behavior of fluctuation-induced higher-derivative couplings in the linear QMDM and then mapped the renormalized effective action onto its nonlinear counterpart by eliminating the massive sigma field via a coset representative transformation on the vacuum manifold SO(6)/SO(5) using stereographic coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at IR: 139.0 MeV (Lambda_UV = 500 MeV) and 140.8 MeV (Lambda_UV = 1 GeV) in the full O(d^4) truncation
- Sigma mass at IR: 410.1 MeV (Lambda_UV = 500 MeV) and 400.9 MeV (Lambda_UV = 1 GeV) in the full O(d^4) truncation
- Quark mass at IR: 292.6 MeV (Lambda_UV = 500 MeV) and 230.4 MeV (Lambda_UV = 1 GeV) in the full O(d^4) truncation
- Pion decay constant: 76.9 MeV for both Lambda_UV = 500 MeV and Lambda_UV = 1 GeV in the full O(d^4) truncation
- Renormalized Yukawa coupling at IR: 3.81 (Lambda_UV = 500 MeV) and 2.99 (Lambda_UV = 1 GeV) in the full O(d^4) truncation
- FRG flow stopped at k_IR = 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' recent advances in the SO(4)-symmetric Quark-Meson Model (Eser 2018, Divotgey 2019, Eser 2019) to the SO(6)-symmetric Quark-Meson-Diquark Model, which serves as an effective description of two-color QCD. The QMDM shares many properties with QCD including a chirally broken mesonic phase, and on the lattice it does not suffer from a sign problem, making its phase diagram more accessible than three-color QCD (Hands 1999, Hands 2000, Hands 2001, Chandrasekharan 2006). The results provide insights into conceptual aspects of purely bosonic effective models within the FRG and enable estimates of validity ranges for low-energy models.
