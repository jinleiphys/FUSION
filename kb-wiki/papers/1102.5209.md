# On Narrow Nucleon Excitation N*(1685)

> We collected notes and simple estimates about putative narrow nucleon N*(1685) - the candidate for the non-strange member of the exotic anti-decuplet of baryons. In particular, we consider the recent high precision data on eta photoproduction off free proton obtained by the Crystal Ball Collaboration at MAMI. We show that it is difficult to describe peculiarities of these new data in the invariant energy interval of W ~ 1650-1750 MeV in terms of known wide resonances. Using very simple estimates, we show that the data may indicate an existence of a narrow N*(1685) with small photocoupling to the proton.

## Key claim

The paper argues that recent high-precision data on eta photoproduction off the free proton from the Crystal Ball Collaboration at MAMI may indicate the existence of a narrow nucleon resonance N*(1685) with a small photocoupling to the proton, but the evidence is indirect and relies on interference effects rather than a clear peak in the cross section.

## Method

The authors performed a simple "back of the envelope" analysis of the total cross section and Legendre coefficients extracted from the Crystal Ball data on gamma p -> eta p in the invariant energy interval W ~ 1650-1750 MeV. They introduced a "speed characteristic" S_1(W) to separate rapidly changing contributions from smooth background and wide resonances, and compared the observed oscillation structure and rapid sign change of the A_1 Legendre coefficient with expectations for a narrow resonance interfering with smooth background.

## Key numbers

- The resonance cross section on the proton at maximum is estimated as sigma_res(gamma p -> eta p) ~ 0.04 (10 MeV / Gamma_tot) microbarn, compared to a non-resonant cross section of sigma_p ~ 3 microbarn.
- The maximally possible magnitude of the interference structure in the total cross section is estimated as Delta_sigma_tot ~ 0.7 microbarn, corresponding to ~0.06 microbarn/sr in the differential cross section.
- The observed oscillation in the total cross section has an amplitude of about 0.5 microbarn with a distance between extrema of Delta_W ~ 40 MeV.
- The extracted photocoupling for the charge component is sqrt(Br_etaN) A_{1/2}^p ~ 1 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} from beam asymmetry data, and sqrt(Br_etaN) A_{1/2}^n ~ 15 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} from CBELSA/TAPS data on the neutron.
- The "speed characteristic" S_1(W) reaches a maximum value of S_1^{max} ~ 30 at W ~ 1680 MeV.
- The analysis suggests a narrow resonance with Gamma_tot <= 50 MeV and photocoupling in the range sqrt(Br_etaN) A_{1/2}^p ~ (0.3-3) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the search for the putative narrow nucleon N*(1685), predicted as the non-strange member of the exotic anti-decuplet of baryons in the chiral quark soliton model (chiQSM) by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). It follows earlier observations of a "neutron anomaly" in eta photoproduction on the neutron by the GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, LNS, and Crystal Ball/TAPS collaborations, and a sharp structure in beam asymmetry data from GRAAL (Kuznetsov et al., 2007, 2009). The analysis contradicts models that explain the neutron anomaly solely through interference of known resonances (Anisovich et al., 2009; Skorodumina et al., 2010) or meson loop effects (Doring et al., 2010), as those models do not predict the observed rapid change of the A_1 Legendre coefficient and cannot explain the neutron anomaly in Compton scattering. The results call for a full partial wave analysis to disentangle the putative narrow resonance from wide resonances such as P_{11}(1710), P_{13}(1720), and D_{15}(1675).
