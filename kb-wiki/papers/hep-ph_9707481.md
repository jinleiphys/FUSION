# Effective Field Theory for Ultrasoft Momenta in NRQCD and NRQED

> We propose an effective field theory for heavy quark-antiquark bound states once the soft gluons have been integrated out. We also give new results for the matching between QCD (QED) and NRQCD (NRQED).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that after integrating out soft gluons (with energies of order the relative momentum), the resulting effective field theory for heavy quark-antiquark bound states, called potential NRQCD (pNRQCD), is local in time but non-local in space, and is naturally written in terms of wave function fields rather than quark fields. This claim is conditional on the assumption that the typical relative momentum is much larger than the typical bound state energy (|p| >> E), which was not required in the matching between QCD and NRQCD.

## Method

The authors perform matching between QCD and NRQCD by computing S-matrix elements on-shell and at threshold (|p| = 0) using dimensional regularisation for both UV and IR divergences, which makes the effective theory diagrams trivially zero and requires only QCD diagrams to be computed. They then match NRQCD to pNRQCD by integrating out soft gluons using HQET propagators in Coulomb gauge, treating the kinetic term perturbatively when computing the potential, and using dimensional regularisation so that any loop in pNRQCD gives zero because there is no scale.

## Key numbers

- Matching coefficients for four-quark operators in NRQCD are given at one loop for non-equal masses, with expressions containing terms like d_ss = -(N_c^2-1)/(4N_c^2) * alpha_s^2/(m_1^2-m_2^2) * {m_1^2(ln(m_2^2/nu^2) + 1/3) - m_2^2(ln(m_1^2/nu^2) + 1/3)}
- For QED equal mass case: d_ss = (3 pi alpha/2) * {1 - (2 alpha/(3 pi)) * (ln(m^2/nu^2) + 23/3 - ln2 + i pi/2)}
- For QED equal mass case: d_sv = -(pi alpha/2) * {1 - (2 alpha/pi) * (22/9 + ln2 - i pi/2)}
- The pNRQED lagrangian is presented from which one can obtain next-to-next-to-leading corrections to the energy (of order m alpha^5)
- The binding energy at order m alpha^5 for arbitrary n,l states can be calculated using the pNRQED lagrangian

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Lepage (1992) for NRQCD/NRQED by proposing a further effective theory (pNRQCD) that integrates out soft gluons, building on the matching techniques developed by Manohar (1997) using dimensional regularisation. The results enable systematic perturbative calculations of heavy quarkonium properties to higher orders in alpha, including m alpha^5 corrections, and provide a framework for separating perturbative from non-perturbative contributions when E >> Lambda_QCD, while also allowing parametrisation of non-perturbative effects via local condensates.
