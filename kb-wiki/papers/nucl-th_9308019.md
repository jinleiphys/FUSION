# On the Analytic Structure of the Quark Self-Energy in Nambu-Jona- Lasinio Models

> The self-energy of quarks is investigated for various models which are inspired by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. Including, beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation, terms up to second-order in the quark interaction, the real and imaginary parts of scalar and vector components of the self-energy are discussed. The second-order contributions depend on the energy and momentum of the quark under consideration. This leads to solutions of the Dirac equation which are significantly different from those of a free quark or a quark with constant effective mass, as obtained in the Hartree-Fock approximation.

## Key claim

The second-order contributions to the quark self-energy in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models produce a significant energy- and momentum-dependent correction to the constant effective mass obtained in the Hartree-Fock approximation, but the magnitude of this effect depends strongly on the specific Lagrangian chosen, with vector-dominated interactions yielding the largest corrections.

## Method

The authors calculated the real and imaginary parts of the scalar and vector components of the quark self-energy up to second order in the quark-quark interaction for four different NJL-type Lagrangians (models A, B, C, D). They used a three-momentum noncovariant cutoff scheme with a cutoff parameter of 653 MeV, and adjusted the coupling constants for each model so that the on-shell quasiparticle energy for a quark at zero momentum was 313 MeV. The second-order self-energies were evaluated using the imaginary parts of the irreducible polarization propagators for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector q-anti-q excitation modes, with the real parts obtained via dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

*   Cutoff parameter Lambda = 653 MeV, chosen for all four models.
*   Effective mass m* = 313 MeV, used for the constant quark mass in the Green's functions for evaluating second-order contributions.
*   Bare current quark mass m0 = 5 MeV, assumed for all Lagrangians.
*   Threshold for 2q-1-anti-q excitations is 3m* = 939 MeV.
*   Largest energy compatible with the cutoff is approximately 3*sqrt(m*^2 + Lambda^2) ≈ 2170 MeV.
*   For model B, the second-order scalar term is larger than the Hartree-Fock contribution to the scalar term.
*   For model C, individual second-order contributions are as large as 15 percent of the leading Hartree-Fock term.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Domitrovich, 1993) of the real scalar part of the second-order self-energy by now including the vector component and the imaginary parts. It goes beyond the standard mean-field or Hartree-Fock treatment of NJL models (Klevansky, 1992; Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) and complements approaches that only consider the pi-exchange term (Cao, 1993). The results enable a more realistic treatment of meson properties by allowing the evaluation of the q-anti-q response function that accounts for the admixture of 2q-2-anti-q configurations, which is particularly relevant for mesons like the sigma that contain large 2-pi contributions.
