# Universal Formula for Baryon Spectra in Heavy-ion Collisions and its Implications

> In an unconventional presentation of the data on the transverse momentum spectra of baryons produced in heavy-ion collisions, regularities are found that make possible the discovery of a universal formula valid for $p, Λ, Ξ$ and $Ω$. The formula describes the baryon distributions over wide ranges of $p_T (0.5 \ ^<_\sim\ p_T \ ^<_\sim\ 5$ GeV/c) for $0.06 \ ^<_\sim\ \sqrt {s_{NN}} \ ^<_\sim\ 3$ TeV, except for very peripheral collisions. Some aspects of their empirical properties are derived in the recombination model, resulting in a revelation of some features of the light and strange quark distributions before hadronization. Interpretation of the inverse slopes of their exponential behavior leads to an implication that cannot accommodate the conventional description of fluid flow. This is mainly a study of phenomenology without detailed model input.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the transverse momentum spectra of baryons (proton, Λ, Ξ, Ω) produced in heavy-ion collisions follow a universal exponential formula over wide ranges of pT (0.5 to 5 GeV/c) and collision energy (0.06 to 3 TeV), except for very peripheral collisions. The hedge is that this universality holds only for the thermal recombination component, not for contributions from jet fragmentation at high pT or for very peripheral collisions with Npart below 60.

## Method

The authors defined a modified baryon distribution function B_h(s, Npart, pT) = (m_T^h / pT^2) * (dN_h / 2π pT dpt dy) and fitted the experimental data for p, Λ, Ξ, and Ω at three collision energies (62.4 GeV, 200 GeV, and 2.76 TeV) with an exponential form A_h(s, Npart) exp[-pT / T_h(s)]. They then interpreted the inverse slopes T_h(s) and prefactors A_h(s, Npart) within the recombination model, extracting the properties of light and strange quark distributions before hadronization.

## Key numbers

- The inverse slopes T_h(s) for protons at √sNN = 0.0624, 0.2, and 2.76 TeV are 0.262, 0.296, and 0.39 GeV/c, respectively.
- The inverse slopes T_h(s) for Ω at √sNN = 0.0624, 0.2, and 2.76 TeV are 0.343, 0.387, and 0.51 GeV/c, respectively.
- The universal power-law exponent for the collision energy dependence of T_q(s) and T_s(s) is β = 0.105, with T_1 = 0.35 GeV/c and T_2 = 0.46 GeV/c.
- The centrality scaling exponent a_h for all baryons is 1.35, valid for Npart > 60.
- The strange-to-nonstrange quark ratio R_s/q(s) = C_s/C_q is approximately 0.17 times (g_p/g_Ω)^(1/3) * √s^0.07, varying from 0.14 to 0.2 over the energy range studied.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of the recombination model (RM) developed by Hwa and collaborators (1990s-2000s) to describe baryon production in heavy-ion collisions. The universal formula contradicts the conventional hydrodynamic description of fluid flow, which cannot accommodate the observed exponential behavior and the specific inverse slopes. The results enable future experimentalists to fit their data with the simple three-parameter formula and provide constraints on the light and strange quark distributions just before hadronization. The paper also addresses the ϕ-Ω problem previously raised in the literature, showing that the ϕ meson spectra follow the same pattern as Ω baryons due to their common strange quark content.
