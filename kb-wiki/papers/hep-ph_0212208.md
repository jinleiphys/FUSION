# Coarse graining and a new strategy for renormalization

> We present the natural arguments for the rationality of a recently proposed simple approach for renormalization which is based solving differential equations. The renormalization group equation is also derived in a natural way and recognized as a decoupling theorem of the UV modes that underlie a QFT. This new strategy has direct implications to the scheme dependence problem.

# Coarse graining and a new strategy for renormalization

## Key claim

The paper argues that a recently proposed differential-equation approach to renormalization, which avoids any regulator or subtraction of infinities, is rationally justified if one assumes that quantum field theories (QFTs) are low-energy sectors of a complete quantum theory of everything (QTOE). The renormalization group equation (RGE) is then derived as a decoupling theorem of the underlying high-energy modes, provided the condition sup{|p|, m, μ̄} ≪ inf{σ} holds, where σ are the characteristic energy scales of the QTOE.

## Method

The author postulates that QFT amplitudes are coarse-grained objects obtained by applying a limit operation L_{σ} (σ → 0 in length units) to well-defined QTOE amplitudes. For any one-loop diagram with superficial divergence degree ω_Γ, the coarse-grained amplitude satisfies a differential equation: (∂_p)^{ω_Γ+1} L_{σ} Γ̄ = ∫ d^D Q (∂_p)^{ω_Γ+1} f_Γ(Q, p, m), where the right-hand side is convergent. The solution is obtained by integrating this equation, leaving integration constants (polynomials in momenta and masses of power ω_Γ) that must be fixed by symmetries, sum rules, or experimental data. The same procedure extends to multiloop diagrams by applying the differential-integral pair to each divergent loop sequentially.

## Key numbers

* The magnitude ordering condition for the coarse-graining scenario to be effective is sup{Λ_QFT} ≪ inf{σ}, where Λ_QFT represents any dimensional parameter (momenta or masses) in the QFT under consideration.
* The differential equation approach is applied to massless λφ⁴ theory in Phys. Rev. D65, 125009 (2002) to discuss nontrivial symmetry breaking solutions.
* The paper references a decoupling mechanism where lim_{M→∞} M ∂_M Γ = Δβ α ∂_α Γ, illustrated for heavy fermion decoupling in QCD or QED in Phys. Lett. B393, 79 (1997).

## Context

This work extends the author's own differential-equation approach to renormalization (Yang, 1997; Yang and Ruan, 2002; Yang, 2002) by providing a conceptual foundation based on an underlying complete theory (QTOE). It contrasts with conventional BPHZ renormalization, which requires a regularization scheme and subtraction of infinities, and which the author argues introduces artificialities that are especially problematic in nonperturbative contexts such as nucleon interactions (Kaplan, Savage and Wise, 1996-1998). The approach enables a more physical interpretation of the renormalization group as a decoupling theorem and may simplify calculations in effective field theories, particularly for nonperturbative problems like nucleon interactions (Beane et al., 2000; Bedaque and van Kolck, 2002).
