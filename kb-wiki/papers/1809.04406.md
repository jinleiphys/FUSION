# The impact of the surface energy coefficient on the deformation properties of atomic nuclei as predicted by Skyrme energy density functionals

> In the framework of nuclear energy density functional (EDF) methods, many nuclear phenomena are related to the deformation of intrinsic states. Their accurate modeling relies on the correct description of the change of nuclear binding energy with deformation. The two most important contributions to the deformation energy have their origin in shell effects that are correlated to the spectrum of single-particle states, and the deformability of nuclear matter, that can be characterized by a model-dependent surface energy coefficient a_{surf}. With the goal of improving the global performance of nuclear EDFs through fine-tuning of their deformation properties, the purpose of this study is threefold. First, to analyze the impact of systematic variations of a_{surf} on properties of nuclei; second, to identify observables that can be safely used to narrow down the range of appropriate values of a_{surf} to be targeted in future parameter fits; third, to analyze the interdependence of a_{surf} with other properties of a nuclear EDF. Results for a large variety of relevant observables of deformed nuclei obtained from self-consistent mean-field calculations with a set of purpose-built SLy5sX parameterizations of the Skyrme EDF are correlated with the value of a_{surf}. The three main conclusions are that there is an evident preference for a comparatively low value of a_{surf}, as expected from the performance of existing parameterizations; that the isospin dependence of the surface energy also needs further finetuning in order to describe trends across the chart of nuclei; and that a satisfying simultaneous description of fission barriers and superdeformed states requires a better description of the single-particle spectra. ## Key claim

The study demonstrates that systematic variation of the surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}\) in Skyrme energy density functionals reveals a preference for comparatively low values of \(a_{\text{surf}}\), but that a satisfying simultaneous description of fission barriers and superdeformed states requires better description of single-particle spectra, not just adjustment of the surface energy coefficient.

## Method

Self-consistent mean-field calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional were performed for a large variety of deformed nuclei using eight purpose-built SLy5sX parametrizations (SLy5s1 through SLy5s8) in which the isoscalar surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}\) was systematically varied in equal steps of 0.2 MeV from 18.0 MeV to 19.4 MeV (MTF values). The calculations employed the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with a pairing functional and the Lipkin-Nogami prescription, and results for fission barriers, superdeformed states, shape coexistence, octupole deformation, and separation energies were compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}^{\text{(MTF)}}\) ranges from 18.00 MeV (SLy5s1) to 19.40 MeV (SLy5s8) in steps of 0.20 MeV
- Saturation density \(\rho_{\text{sat}}\) varies from 0.1598 fm\(^{-3}\) (SLy5s1) to 0.1634 fm\(^{-3}\) (SLy5s8)
- Energy per particle \(E/A = a_{\text{vol}}\) ranges from \(-15.772\) MeV (SLy5s1) to \(-16.101\) MeV (SLy5s8)
- Incompressibility \(K_\infty\) varies from 222.1 MeV (SLy5s1) to 229.1 MeV (SLy5s8)
- Symmetry energy coefficient \(J = a_{\text{sym}}\) ranges from 31.43 MeV (SLy5s1) to 32.64 MeV (SLy5s8)
- Slope of symmetry energy \(L\) is constrained to \((50 \pm 2)\) MeV across all parametrizations
- Effective surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf,eff}}\) for \(^{240}\)Pu takes values of 15.29 MeV (SLy5s1) and 16.42 MeV (SLy5s8), a difference of 1.28 MeV
- Effective surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf,eff}}\) for \(^{180}\)Hg takes values of 16.96 MeV (SLy5s1) and 18.24 MeV (SLy5s8)
- Spin-orbit splittings from SLy5s1 are about 10% larger than those from SLy5s8
- Pairing strength parameters used: \(V_q = -1250\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\rho_c = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) for both protons and neutrons

## Context

This work extends the series of SLy5sX parametrizations developed by Jodon et al. (2016) which systematically varied the surface energy coefficient while avoiding unphysical finite-size instabilities as addressed by Pastore et al. (2013). The study builds on earlier efforts to fine-tune deformation properties during parameter fitting, such as the semiclassical estimates used for SkM* by Bartel et al. (1982) and the adjustment of fission isomer excitation energies used for UNEDF1 and UNEDF2. The results enable future parameter fits to better constrain deformation properties of heavy nuclei, particularly fission barriers, by identifying which observables are most sensitive to the surface energy coefficient and which require improved single-particle spectra.
