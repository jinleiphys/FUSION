# Azimuthal Anisotropy Distributions in High-Energy Collisions

> Elliptic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions results from the hydrodynamic response to the spatial anisotropy of the initial density profile. A long-standing problem in the interpretation of flow data is that uncertainties in the initial anisotropy are mingled with uncertainties in the response. We argue that the non-Gaussianity of flow fluctuations in small systems can be used to disentangle the initial state from the response. We apply this method to recent measurements of anisotropic flow in Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at the LHC. The response coefficient is found to decrease mildly as the system becomes smaller. This mild decrease is consistent with a low value of the ratio of viscosity over entropy.

## Key claim

The non-Gaussianity of flow fluctuations in small collision systems can be used to disentangle the initial-state anisotropy from the hydrodynamic response, without assuming any particular model of initial conditions. The extracted response coefficient decreases mildly as the system becomes smaller, and this decrease is consistent with a low value of the viscosity over entropy ratio of \(\eta/s \simeq 0.19\), though deviations from linear response significantly change the value of the response coefficient but do not change the rate of decrease with centrality.

## Method

The authors fit the measured probability distributions of elliptic flow \(v_2\) and triangular flow \(v_3\) from ATLAS Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV using a rescaled Elliptic Power distribution, which naturally enforces the geometric constraint \(|\varepsilon_n| \le 1\). For p+Pb collisions, where only cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_2\{4\}\) from CMS data are available, they extract the response coefficient \(\kappa_2\) from the ratio \(v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}\) assuming a Power distribution for the initial eccentricity. They also study deviations from linear response by adding a cubic term \(v_2 = \kappa_2 \varepsilon_2 + \kappa' \kappa_2 \varepsilon_2^3\) and by considering a Gaussian noise term \(X_n\) uncorrelated with the initial eccentricity.

## Key numbers

- Response coefficient \(\kappa_2\) decreases with centrality percentile; values are smaller than unity, with \(\kappa_3 < \kappa_2\).
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the extracted viscosity over entropy ratio is \(\eta/s \simeq 0.19\) from comparison with viscous hydrodynamic calculations.
- For p+Pb collisions, \(\kappa_2\) values extracted from CMS data (corrected by a factor 1.19 for the different low-\(p_T\) cut of 0.3 GeV/\(c\)) show a slope somewhat steeper than Pb+Pb results.
- The cubic non-linear term \(\kappa'\) is in the range from 0 to 0.15; fixing \(\kappa' = 0.10\) reduces \(\kappa_2\) by approximately \(-2\kappa'\) (i.e., about 20%).
- A decrease of the Pearson correlation coefficient \(|r_2|\) by 1% results in a decrease of the extracted \(\kappa_2\) by 6% to 9%, depending on centrality, with the maximum effect in the 20-30% centrality range.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of anisotropic flow fluctuations beyond the Bessel-Gaussian approximation used by Voloshin (2007) and the ATLAS collaboration (2013), which cannot separate the initial anisotropy from the response due to scale invariance. The authors build on their own earlier work (Yan 2013, 2014) on the universal distribution of initial eccentricity and the Elliptic Power parametrization. The method enables the extraction of the shear viscosity over entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) of the quark-gluon plasma from experimental data without assuming a specific model of initial conditions, which was a long-standing problem noted by Luzum (2008) and Heinz (2013). Future work can improve the extraction by constraining the cubic response coefficient and the Pearson correlation coefficient from event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations.
