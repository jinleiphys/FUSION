# Shell model description of the $N=82$ isotonic chain with a new effective interaction

> In this work, we present a systematic study of low-lying states and electromagnetic properties of the semi-magic $N = 82$ isotonic chain with proton number $Z=51$-77, using the full configuration interaction shell model with a newly developed high-quality effective interaction. The calculations are performed in a large model space that includes all proton orbitals between $Z = 50$ and 82: $0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$. The effective interaction is derived through the principal component analysis approach, starting from 160 two-body matrix elements and 5 single-particle energies and considering up to 30 degrees of freedom. Those are optimized by fitting to 204 available experimental energy levels. The resulting root-mean-square deviation is as low as 102 keV. The new interaction successfully reproduces the binding energies, low-lying spectra, electric quadrupole transition probabilities $B(E2)$, and magnetic dipole moments across both even-even and odd-mass isotones. The nuclear structure of low-lying states is analyzed in detail. Additionally, predictions are made for several more proton-rich nuclei beyond current experimental reach, including $^{155}\mathrm{Ta}$, $^{156}\mathrm{W}$, $^{157}\mathrm{Re}$, $^{158}\mathrm{Os}$, and $^{159}\mathrm{Ir}$.

## Key claim

The newly developed effective interaction, optimized via principal component analysis using 204 experimental energy levels, achieves a root-mean-square deviation of 102 keV for the N=82 isotonic chain, resolving previous systematic discrepancies such as overestimations of 3⁻ excitation energies and incorrect ground-state spin-parity assignments in heavier odd-A isotones.

## Method

The authors performed full configuration interaction shell model calculations for N=82 isotones with proton numbers Z=51-77, using a model space comprising all five proton orbitals between Z=50 and 82 (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2) above the ¹³²Sn inert core. The effective interaction was derived by starting from 160 two-body matrix elements and 5 single-particle energies, then applying principal component analysis to truncate the parameter space to 30 degrees of freedom, with parameters optimized through iterative least-squares fitting to 204 experimental data points including binding energies and excitation energies.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation of 102 keV for all 204 fitted experimental data points across the N=82 isotonic chain
* Binding energy RMSD of 59 keV for isotones ¹³⁴Te through ¹⁵¹Tm
* Largest M-scheme dimension of 1.6 × 10⁷ for even-parity states in ¹⁴⁸Dy
* Subshell gap of 1.788 MeV at Z=64 between the 1d5/2 and 0h11/2 orbitals, and gap of 1.062 MeV at Z=58 between 0g7/2 and 1d5/2 orbitals
* Predicted atomic mass excesses for proton-rich nuclei: ¹⁵⁶W at -17.335 MeV, ¹⁵⁷Re at -8.032 MeV, ¹⁵⁸Os at -0.523 MeV, and ¹⁵⁹Ir at 9.393 MeV
* Calculated two-proton separation energy for ¹⁵⁶W of -0.606 MeV, indicating possible two-proton emission

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of the N=82 isotonic chain, improving upon earlier interactions such as JJ56PNA (Brown, 2014), KH5082 (Kuo and Herling, 1971), and CW5082 (Chou and Warburton, 1992), which typically deviated from experimental binding energies by 1 MeV to several tens of MeV. The new interaction also improves upon a preliminary version introduced in Ref. [CQY] that reasonably reproduced 2⁺ excitation energies but showed systematic discrepancies. The results enable reliable predictions for proton-rich nuclei beyond current experimental reach, including ¹⁵⁵Ta, ¹⁵⁶W, ¹⁵⁷Re, ¹⁵⁸Os, and ¹⁵⁹Ir, and provide guidance for future experimental studies near the proton drip line.
