# Evidence for diquarks in lattice QCD

> Diquarks may play an important role in hadron spectroscopy, baryon decays and color superconductivity. We investigate the existence of diquark correlations in lattice QCD by considering systematically all the lowest energy diquark channels in a color gauge-invariant setup. We measure mass differences between the various channels and show that the positive parity scalar diquark is the lightest. Quark-quark correlations inside the diquark are clearly seen in this channel, and yield a diquark size of order 1 fm.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence from lattice QCD that the scalar (positive parity) diquark is the lightest diquark channel and shows clear quark-quark correlations, but the authors hedge that even this "good" diquark is a large object of order 1 fm, which may limit its relevance to hadron structure.

## Method

The authors used a gauge-invariant lattice QCD formalism where diquarks are studied in the background of a static quark, creating color-singlet states. They computed mass differences between all lowest-energy diquark channels (using operators of the form q^T C Gamma q) and measured density-density correlators to probe spatial correlations between the two light quarks. Simulations used quenched Wilson fermions at three lattice spacings (beta=5.8, 6.0, 6.2) and unquenched configurations at beta=5.6, with pion masses in the range 570-910 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Mass difference between "good" (scalar) and "bad" (vector) diquarks at the chiral limit: c1 = 0.138 ± 0.01 GeV, with c2 = 0.78 ± 0.15 GeV from fits to lattice data at beta=6.0 and 6.2
- Ratio of this mass splitting to the Delta-nucleon mass splitting: 0.67(7), 0.73(8), and 0.67(8) at kappa=0.153, 0.154, and 0.155 respectively at beta=6.0
- Diquark size for the "good" diquark: approximately 1.1 ± 0.2 fm, extracted from exponential fits to density correlators at large shell radii (r greater than 0.5 fm)
- For comparison, the rho meson size at the same quark mass using the same definition: 0.7 fm
- Lattice spacings used: a=0.136 fm (beta=5.8), 0.093 fm (beta=6.0), 0.068 fm (beta=6.2)

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological predictions by Jaffe (1977) that the scalar diquark should be the lightest channel, and provides the first gauge-invariant lattice QCD verification of diquark correlations. The results confirm the expectation that the mass splitting between "good" and "bad" diquarks is about 2/3 of the Delta-nucleon mass difference, as argued by Jaffe. The study enables future investigations of diquark roles in baryon spectroscopy, non-leptonic weak decays, and color superconductivity, while noting that the large diquark size (comparable to typical hadron sizes) may limit the usefulness of the diquark picture in hadron structure.
