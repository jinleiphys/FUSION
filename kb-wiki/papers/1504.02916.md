# Perturbative study of the QCD phase diagram for heavy quarks at nonzero chemical potential

> We investigate the phase diagram of QCD with heavy quarks at finite temperature and chemical potential in the context of background field methods. In particular, we use a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge which is motivated by previous studies of the deconfinement phase transition in pure Yang-Mills theories. We show that a simple one-loop calculation is able to capture the richness of the phase diagram in the heavy quark region, both at real and imaginary chemical potential. Moreover, dimensionless ratios of quantities directly measurable in numerical simulations are in good agreement with lattice results.

## Key claim

The authors show that a simple one-loop calculation in a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge can capture the rich structure of the QCD phase diagram for heavy quarks at both real and imaginary chemical potential, with parameter-free dimensionless ratios that agree with lattice results, but only in the heavy quark region where the quark mass is large.

## Method

The authors compute the background field potential at one-loop order for QCD with N_f=2+1 heavy quark flavors at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge motivated by previous studies of the deconfinement phase transition in pure Yang-Mills theories. The calculation involves evaluating the quark contribution as a sum over flavor and color states, where the constant temporal background field is absorbed into a redefinition of the chemical potential, combined with the pure gauge contribution from massive gluon modes and massless ghost modes. The order parameters, the averages of the traced Polyakov loop and its Hermitic conjugate, are then read from the extremum of this potential as functions of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The vectors generating translations of the background field under gauge transformations are u_1 = 2π (1, 1/√3) and u_2 = 2π (1, -1/√3)
- The vectors generating translations under Roberge-Weiss symmetry are e_1 = 2π (1, -1/√3) and e_2 = 2π (-1, -1/√3)
- The fundamental representation weights of su(3) are ρ_1 = (1/2)(-1, 1/√3), ρ_2 = (1/2)(1, 1/√3), and ρ_3 = (0, -1/√3)
- The Roberge-Weiss symmetry relates chemical potentials differing by 2iπ/(3β)
- The theory is periodic under μ → μ + 2iπ/β

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in pure Yang-Mills theories using the massive Landau-DeWitt gauge (Reinosa, 2014) to QCD with heavy quarks at finite chemical potential. The approach provides a benchmark for nonperturbative continuum methods such as functional renormalization group and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Fischer, 2011; Fischer, 2012; Fischer, 2013; Herbst, 2013), and complements lattice simulations which suffer from a sign problem at real chemical potential but can be performed for heavy quarks (de Forcrand, 2002; de Forcrand, 2003; D'Elia, 2002; Fromm, 2011). The results enable further investigation of the QCD phase diagram in regions where lattice methods are difficult, and the framework can be extended to two-loop order for more quantitative agreement.
