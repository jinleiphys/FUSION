# The NN Phase Shifts <3 GeV and Resonance Features

> Solutions SP00, SM00 and FA00 of nucleon-nucleon (NN) phase shift analyzes by Arndt et al. are used in optical model studies. The partial waves, single channels J.leq.7 and coupled channels J.leq.6, are scrutinized. The radial probability distributions and losses of flux are used to identify the known Delta and N* resonances as well as anticipated other structures. The energy interval extends to 3 GeV.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the known Delta and N* resonances appear in the radial probability distributions and loss of flux derived from NN phase shift solutions, but that none of the results support the formation of intermediate fused NN systems; the nucleons maintain their individuality throughout the interaction.

## Method
The authors used the SP00, SM00, and FA00 phase shift solutions from the SAID database (Arndt et al.) for energies up to 3 GeV for pp scattering and 1.2 GeV for np scattering. They constructed optical model potentials using Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko inversion potentials with a rank-one separable potential (harmonic oscillators with hbar-omega = 450 MeV) and computed radial probability distributions and loss of flux from the continuity equation for partial waves with single channels J <= 7 and coupled channels J <= 6.

## Key numbers
- The energy interval extends to 3 GeV for pp scattering and 1.2 GeV for np scattering.
- The low energy data below 400 MeV received particular attention, with the Nijmegen group's elastic scattering analysis below T_l <= 350 MeV.
- The repulsive core strength reaches a value of 1 GeV at approximately 0.5 fm, which may be viewed as the classical turning point.
- The region 0.5-1 GeV is dominated by the Delta(1232) resonance, while several N* resonances shape the region 1-2 GeV.
- The separable potential used harmonic oscillators with hbar-omega = 450 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the NN phase shift analyses of Arndt et al. (SAID database, solutions SP00, SM00, FA00) and compares with classical meson exchange potentials from Nijmegen, Paris, Bonn, and the authors' own OSBEP potential (Jae98). The optical model approach is applied to NN scattering following earlier work by Neu91, Ger97, and Fal99. The results enable microscopic nucleon-nucleus scattering analysis in folding models, studies of exotic nuclei, and calculations for waste management technology with medium energy particles.
