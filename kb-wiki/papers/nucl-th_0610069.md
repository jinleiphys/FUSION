# Nuclear matter with Brown-Rho-scaled Fermi liquid interactions

> We present a description of symmetric nuclear matter within the framework of Landau Fermi liquid theory. The low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V(low-k) is used to calculate the effective interaction between quasiparticles on the Fermi surface, from which we extract the quasiparticle effective mass, the nuclear compression modulus, the symmetry energy, and the anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio. The exchange of density, spin, and isospin collective excitations is included through the Babu-Brown induced interaction, and it is found that in the absence of three-body forces the self-consistent solution to the Babu-Brown equations is in poor agreement with the empirical values for the nuclear observables. This is improved by lowering the nucleon and meson masses according to Brown-Rho scaling, essentially by including a scalar tadpole contribution to the meson and nucleon masses, as well as by scaling g_A. We suggest that modifying the masses of the exchanged mesons is equivalent to introducing a short-range three-body force, and the net result is that the Brown-Rho double decimation is accomplished all at once.

# Nuclear matter with Brown-Rho-scaled Fermi liquid interactions

## Key claim

The self-consistent solution to the Babu-Brown induced interaction equations for symmetric nuclear matter, using only the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V(low-k) without three-body forces, yields a compression modulus and symmetry energy smaller than empirical values and an anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio too large; these discrepancies are improved by lowering nucleon and meson masses according to Brown-Rho scaling, which the authors suggest is equivalent to introducing a short-range three-body force.

## Method

The authors used the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V(low-k) derived from renormalization group methods to calculate the effective interaction between quasiparticles on the Fermi surface within Landau Fermi liquid theory. They included the exchange of density, spin, and isospin collective excitations through the Babu-Brown induced interaction formalism, solving the coupled equations self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter at a Fermi momentum of 1.36 fm⁻¹. The driving term was computed from V(low-k) matrix elements from the Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, and CD-Bonn potentials with a momentum cutoff of 2.1 fm⁻¹, and the induced interaction was iterated until convergence, after which Brown-Rho scaling was applied by reducing hadronic masses by approximately 20%.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle effective mass ratio m*/m = 0.888 for the CD-Bonn potential (0.887 for Nijmegen I, 0.930 for Nijmegen II) at k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹ with quasiparticle renormalization strength z = 0.7
- Nuclear compression modulus K = 136 MeV for CD-Bonn (136 MeV for Nijmegen I, 102 MeV for Nijmegen II)
- Symmetry energy β = 17.6 MeV for CD-Bonn (18.1 MeV for Nijmegen I, 20.5 MeV for Nijmegen II)
- Anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio δg_l = 0.685 μ_N for CD-Bonn (0.682 μ_N for Nijmegen I, 0.452 μ_N for Nijmegen II)
- Full Fermi liquid parameter F_0 = -0.476 from the self-consistent solution with CD-Bonn, compared to the driving term value F_d0 = -1.276
- Sum rule deviations: δS_1 = 0.27 and δS_2 = -0.04 for CD-Bonn

## Context

This work extends the microscopic Fermi liquid theory approach of Schwenk et al. (2005), who used experimentally extracted spin-independent Landau parameters to predict spin-dependent parameters via sum rules, by instead performing a fully self-consistent solution of the Babu-Brown equations starting from the V(low-k) interaction. The study builds on the induced interaction formalism originally developed by Babu and Brown (1973) for liquid ³He and applied to nuclear matter by Sjöberg (1973, 1974), and it incorporates Brown-Rho scaling from Brown and Rho (1991) to account for partial restoration of chiral symmetry at nuclear matter density. The results enable future work to include explicit three-body forces and to explore the consequences of in-medium hadronic modifications for low-energy nuclear physics.
