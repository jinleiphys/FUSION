# Energy Dependence of Moments of Net-Proton and Net-Charge Multiplicity Distributions at STAR

> We present the energy dependence of moments of net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions measured by the STAR experiment in the first phase of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). By using the time of flight detector for particle identification, the upper transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) limit for proton and anti-proton is extended from 0.8 GeV/c up to 2 GeV/c. The $p_{T}$ and rapidity acceptance dependence study for the moments of net-proton distribution show that the larger the acceptance is, the greater the deviation from unity. The most pronounced structure is found in the energy dependence of {\KV} of net-proton distributions from the $0\sim5%$ most central collisions within $0.4<p_{T}<2$ GeV/c and at mid-rapidity $|y|<0.5$. At energies above 39 GeV, the values of {\KV} are close to unity and for energies below 39 GeV, it shows significant deviation below unity around 19.6 and 27 GeV, then a large increase above unity is observed at 7.7 GeV.

# Energy Dependence of Moments of Net-Proton and Net-Charge Multiplicity Distributions at STAR

## Key claim
The energy dependence of the cumulant ratio κσ² (kurtosis times variance) of net-proton distributions in 0–5% most central Au+Au collisions shows a non-monotonic structure: values are close to unity above 39 GeV, deviate below unity around 19.6 and 27 GeV, and then rise above unity at 7.7 GeV. This structure is most pronounced when the transverse momentum acceptance is extended to 0.4 < pT < 2 GeV/c using the time-of-flight detector, and the effect is acceptance-dependent, with larger acceptance producing greater deviations from unity.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV from the first phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. Protons and anti-protons were identified using the Time Projection Chamber for 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c and the Time-of-Flight detector to extend the upper pT limit to 2 GeV/c. Efficiency correction was applied using a unified method based on factorial moments with Delta theorem error propagation, and centrality bin width correction was performed to remove volume fluctuation effects.

## Key numbers
- Upper pT limit for proton and anti-proton identification extended from 0.8 GeV/c to 2 GeV/c using the ToF detector, approximately doubling the number of protons and anti-protons in the analysis.
- For 0–5% most central collisions within 0.4 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |y| < 0.5: κσ² values are close to unity at energies above 39 GeV; show significant deviation below unity at 19.6 and 27 GeV; and exhibit a large increase above unity at 7.7 GeV.
- Net-charge moments measured within 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |η| < 0.5 show no non-monotonic behavior in κσ² or Sσ within current statistics.
- Proton and anti-proton detection efficiency at high pT (0.8–2 GeV/c) is significantly lower than at low pT (0.4–0.8 GeV/c), with anti-proton efficiency slightly lower than proton efficiency due to material absorption.
- Statistical errors scale as Δ(Cn) ~ O(σⁿ), making net-charge moments (wider distributions) have larger statistical errors than net-proton moments.

## Context
This work extends the STAR Collaboration's published results on net-proton moments (STAR, 2014) by increasing the pT acceptance from 0.8 GeV/c to 2 GeV/c using the ToF detector, and presents new acceptance-dependence studies. The non-monotonic energy dependence of κσ² in central collisions is a candidate signal for critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point, as predicted by theoretical calculations showing that higher moments are sensitive to the correlation length (Stephanov, 2009). The results motivate the second phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES-II) starting in 2018, which will provide 3–10 times higher statistics at energies below 20 GeV via stochastic electron cooling, along with the iTPC and Event Plane Detector upgrades at STAR.
