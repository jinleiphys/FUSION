# Model-independent analysis of the neutron-proton final-state interaction region in the pp -> pn pi^+ reaction

> Experimental data on the pp -> pn pi^+ reaction measured in an exclusive two-arm experiment at 800 MeV show a narrow peak arising from the strong proton-neutron final-state interaction. It was claimed, within the framework of a certain model, that this peak contained up to a 25% spin-singlet final state contribution. By comparing the data with those of pp -> d pi^+ in a largely model-independent way, it is here demonstrated that at all the angles measured the whole of the peak could be explained as being due to spin-triplet final states, with the spin-singlet being at most a few percent. Good qualitative agreement with the measured proton analysing power is also found within this approach.

# Model-independent analysis of the neutron-proton final-state interaction region in the pp → pn π⁺ reaction

## Key claim

By comparing the LAMPF pp → pn π⁺ data at 800 MeV with the pp → d π⁺ reaction in a largely model-independent way, the entire final-state interaction peak can be explained as arising from spin-triplet np final states, with the spin-singlet contribution being at most a few percent, contradicting earlier model-dependent claims of up to 25% singlet contribution.

## Method

The authors applied the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation theorem, which relates the low-energy np triplet scattering wave function to the deuteron bound-state wave function, to derive an expression for the five-fold differential cross section of pp → pn π⁺ in terms of the measured pp → d π⁺ cross section. The triplet contribution was calculated using the SAID SP96 parameterisation for pp → d π⁺ input, and compared directly to the LAMPF exclusive two-arm data at 800 MeV without relying on detailed pion production models.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron pole parameter α_t = 0.232 fm⁻¹, used in the extrapolation theorem linking np scattering to the deuteron bound state
- The spin-singlet pole parameter α_s = -0.04 fm⁻¹, used to estimate possible singlet contributions
- The systematic error in the LAMPF measurement was estimated at about 7%
- The accuracy of the extrapolation theorem expression is estimated to be better than 5% for E_np ≤ 3 MeV
- A singlet fraction of about 10% integrated over 0 ≤ E_np ≤ 3 MeV would correspond to ζ = 0.03 in the singlet cross section parameterisation
- Minimum np excitation energies E_np^min of 13 MeV at (θ_p, θ_π) = (25°, 40°) and 28 MeV at (30°, 28°)

## Context

This work extends the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation theorem (Fäldt and Wilkin, 1998) from single-arm to two-arm experiments, providing a model-independent alternative to earlier analyses of the LAMPF data. It contradicts the analysis of Hürster et al. (1995) which claimed 15-25% spin-singlet contribution based on a one-meson-exchange model with Watson final-state interaction factors, and also differs from other model-dependent analyses by Dubach et al. that gave smaller singlet fractions. The approach enables more stringent limits on the spin-singlet/triplet ratio in future experiments such as those planned at the ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Jülich.
