# Accurate and precise quantum computation of valence two-neutron systems

> Developing methods to solve nuclear many-body problems with quantum computers is an imperative pursuit within the nuclear physics community. Here, we introduce a quantum algorithm to accurately and precisely compute the ground state of valence two-neutron systems leveraging presently available Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum devices. Our focus lies on the nuclei having a doubly-magic core plus two valence neutrons in the $ p $, $ sd $, and $ pf $ shells, i.e. ${}^6$He, ${}^{18}$O, and ${}^{42}$Ca, respectively. Our ansatz, quantum circuit, is constructed in the pair-wise form, taking into account the symmetries of the system in an explicit manner, and enables us to reduce the number of qubits and the number of CNOT gates required. The results on a real quantum hardware by IBM Quantum Platform show that the proposed method gives very accurate results of the ground-state energies, which are typically within $ 0.1 \, \% $ error in the energy for ${}^6$He and ${}^{18}$O and at most $ 1 \, \% $ error for ${}^{42}$Ca. Furthermore, our experiments using real quantum devices also show the pivotal role of the circuit layout design, attuned to the connectivity of the qubits, in mitigating errors.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a quantum algorithm for computing ground-state energies of valence two-neutron systems that achieves high accuracy on present-day noisy quantum devices, but only when the circuit parameters are fixed to classically pre-computed values that give the exact ground-state energies, rather than being optimized from random initial values on the quantum hardware.

## Method

The authors map the shell-model Hamiltonian for two-neutron systems with a doubly-magic core (6He, 18O, 42Ca) onto a "pair-wise" form that reduces the number of qubits by encoding time-reversed neutron pairs rather than individual single-particle states. They construct a variational quantum circuit using CNOT and Ry gates that conserves particle number, with the number of CNOT gates scaling as 3N-5 where N is the number of pair-wise configurations. The ground-state energy is measured using two methods: direct Pauli operator measurement (method A) and a computational basis sampling technique (method B) that allows post-selection to remove configurations violating particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 6He with Cohen-Kurath interaction: -3.90981 MeV (exact reference value)
- Ground-state energy of 18O with USDB interaction: -11.93179 MeV (exact reference value)
- Ground-state energy of 42Ca with GXPF1A interaction: -19.73368 MeV (exact reference value)
- Error for 6He on real IBM quantum hardware: at most 0.1% in energy
- Error for 18O on real IBM quantum hardware: typically about 5% in energy
- Error for 42Ca on real IBM quantum hardware: typically about 10% in energy
- Probability of obtaining particle number-conserving configurations on ibm_brisbane device: approximately 92% for 6He, 63% for 18O, and 30% for 42Ca
- Number of shots for real device experiments: 20,000

## Context

This work extends previous applications of quantum algorithms to nuclear many-body problems, including shell-model calculations by Stetch (2022), Kiss (2022), Spain (2023), Sarma (2023), and Bhoy (2024), which used Unitary Coupled Cluster (UCC) ansatz and its variants. The pair-wise ansatz reduces the number of CNOT gates compared to these prior approaches. The method enables accurate quantum computation of simple two-valence-nucleon systems on current NISQ devices, and the authors note that extending to systems with larger gaps in single-particle energies (like 42Ca) remains more challenging due to noise sensitivity.
