# Structural evolution in $A\approx 100$ nuclei within the mapped interacting boson model based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The structure of even-even neutron-rich Ru, Mo, Zr and Sr nuclei in the $A\approx 100$ mass region is studied within the interacting boson model (IBM) with microscopic input from the self-consistent mean-field approximation based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional. The deformation energy surface in the quadrupole deformation space $(β,γ)$, computed within the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, is mapped onto the expectation value of the appropriately chosen IBM Hamiltonian with configuration mixing in the boson condensate state. The mapped IBM Hamiltonian is used to study the spectroscopic properties of $^{98-114}$Ru, $^{96-112}$Mo, $^{94-110}$Zr and $^{92-108}$Sr. Several cases of $γ$-soft behavior are predicted in Ru and Mo nuclei while a pronounced coexistence between strongly-prolate and weakly-oblate deformed shapes is found for Zr and Sr nuclei. The method describes well the evolution of experimental yrast and non-yrast states as well as selected $B$(E2) transition probabilities.

## Key claim

The mapped interacting boson model (IBM) based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional can describe the structural evolution and shape coexistence in neutron-rich Ru, Mo, Zr, and Sr nuclei, but the mapping reproduces the topology of the mean-field energy surfaces only approximately, with discrepancies in barrier heights partly due to the adopted fixed mixing strength of ω = 0.1 MeV.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional were performed to obtain deformation energy surfaces in the (β,γ) quadrupole deformation space for even-even nuclei in the A≈100 region. These energy surfaces were then mapped onto the expectation value of an interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian with configuration mixing in the boson condensate state, allowing up to three unperturbed boson spaces corresponding to 0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h proton excitations across the Z=40 subshell closure. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized to compute excitation spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities for ⁹⁸⁻¹¹⁴Ru, ⁹⁶⁻¹¹²Mo, ⁹⁴⁻¹¹⁰Zr, and ⁹²⁻¹⁰⁸Sr.

## Key numbers

- The mixing interaction strengths ωₛ,ₙ and ω_d,ₙ were set to a constant value of 0.1 MeV for both Ĥᵐⁱˣ₀,₁ and Ĥᵐⁱˣ₁,₂ terms
- The parameter κ' (three-boson term) was 0.25, 0.25, 0.12, 0.08, 0.10, and 0.18 MeV for ¹⁰⁴⁻¹¹⁴Ru, and a constant 0.50 MeV for ¹⁰⁰⁻¹⁰²Mo
- The effective charges e_B,ₙ were fitted to reproduce experimental B(E2; 2⁺₁→0⁺₁) values for N=66 Ru, Mo, and Zr nuclei, and for ¹⁰⁰Sr
- The difference between neighboring contours in the deformation energy surface plots is 100 keV
- The boson model space comprised the neutron major shell N=50-82 and proton shells Z=40-50 (for Ru, Mo, Zr) or Z=28-40 (for Sr), with neutron boson numbers 2 ≤ N_ν ≤ 8 and proton boson numbers N_π = 0 (Zr), 1 (Sr and Mo), and 2 (Ru)

## Context

This work extends the mapping method of Nomura (2008) from simpler cases to the challenging A≈100 region, where rapid structural changes around neutron number N=60 and shape coexistence have been studied experimentally by Sumikama (2011) and Albers (2013) and theoretically using Skyrme EDFs by Bonche (1985), relativistic mean-field by Mei (2012), and Gogny-D1S by Rayner (2010). The approach enables computationally feasible spectroscopic predictions for exotic nuclei based solely on the underlying microscopic EDF framework, in contrast to phenomenological IBM studies by Van Isacker (1980) and Sambataro (1982) that required fitting to known data.
