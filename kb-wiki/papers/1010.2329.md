# Importance of nuclear effects in the measurement of neutrino oscillation parameters

> We investigate how models for neutrino-nucleus cross sections based on different assumptions for the nuclear dynamics affect the forecasted sensitivities to neutrino oscillation parameters at future neutrino facilities. We limit ourselves to the quasi-elastic regime, where the neutrino cross sections can be evaluated with less uncertainties, and discuss the sensitivity reach to theta_{13} and the CP violation phase at a prototype low-gamma beta-beam, mostly sensitive to the quasi-elastic regime.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of future neutrino facilities to the oscillation parameters theta_13 and the CP-violating phase delta can be worse by up to a factor of 2 when using more realistic nuclear models (Spectral Function, Relativistic Mean Field, Random Phase Approximation) compared to the widely used Relativistic Fermi Gas model, even in the quasi-elastic regime where cross-section uncertainties are smallest.

## Method

The authors compared five charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross-section models: the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG), Spectral Function (SF), Relativistic Mean Field (RMF), Random Phase Approximation (RPA), and RPA including two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) contributions. They simulated a prototype low-gamma beta-beam facility (gamma = 100) with a 130 km baseline to a Mton water Cherenkov detector, using the GLoBES software to compute the discovery potential for theta_13 and CP violation, marginalizing over the known oscillation parameters.

## Key numbers

* The RFG model overestimates the total charged-current quasi-elastic cross section for nu_mu on oxygen-16 by roughly 15% compared to the SF, RMF, and RPA models in the energy range 0 to 0.75 GeV.
* For the CP discovery potential at delta_CP near +/- 90 degrees, the RFG model gives a prediction around a factor of 2 better than the SF, RMF, and RPA models in sin^2 2theta_13, and around 40% better than the RPA-2p2h model.
* For the theta_13 discovery potential at delta_CP near +/- 90 degrees, the SF, RMF, and RPA predictions differ by up to a factor of approximately 60% compared to the RFG model.
* The delta fraction (fraction of delta_CP values for which CP can be discovered) reaches a maximum near sin^2 2theta_13 ~ 10^-2, where the RFG model predicts delta_F ~ 0.75 and the other models give delta_F ~ 0.7.
* The RFG model parameters for oxygen are p_F = 225 MeV and E_B = 25 MeV, obtained from electron scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects in neutrino cross sections beyond the simple RFG model used in many Monte Carlo codes, comparing it to more sophisticated approaches such as the Spectral Function approach of Benhar (2005), the Relativistic Mean Field model of Martinez (2005), and the Random Phase Approximation including multinucleon contributions from Martini (2009). The results show that the choice of nuclear model can affect sensitivity forecasts by much more than the few percent systematic uncertainty typically assumed, implying that comparisons of different future neutrino facilities must account for these nuclear model uncertainties. The authors note that the same qualitative behavior was observed for an iron-56 nuclear target.
