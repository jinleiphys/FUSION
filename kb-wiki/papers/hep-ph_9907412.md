# Orbitally Excited Baryons in Large N_c QCD

> We present a model-independent analysis of the mass spectrum of nonstrange l=1 baryons in large N_c QCD. The 1/N_c expansion is used to select and order a basis of effective operators that spans the nine observables (seven masses and two mixing angles). Comparison to the data provides support for the validity of the 1/N_c expansion, but also reveals that only a few nontrivial operators are strongly preferred. We show that our results have a consistent interpretation in a constituent quark model with pseudoscalar meson exchange interactions.

# Orbitally Excited Baryons in Large N_c QCD

## Key claim
The 1/N_c expansion provides a model-independent framework for analyzing the mass spectrum of nonstrange l=1 baryons, but the underlying dynamics, not just large N_c power counting, determines which operators dominate; only three of nine possible operators have coefficients statistically distinguishable from zero, and these can be understood in a constituent quark model with pseudoscalar meson exchange interactions.

## Method
The analysis uses the 1/N_c expansion to select and order a complete basis of nine effective operators (seven masses and two mixing angles) that act on baryon states described as a symmetrized core of (N_c-1) ground-state quarks plus one excited quark in a relative P state. Operator coefficients are extracted by solving the system against experimental masses from the Review of Particle Properties and mixing angles from strong decay analyses. The resulting operator set is then interpreted in terms of single pion exchange between quark lines.

## Key numbers
- Three-parameter fit using operators O1, O3, and O6 yields chi-squared per degree of freedom = 11.19/6 = 1.87
- Operator coefficients from the full nine-operator fit: c1 = +0.470 ± 0.017 GeV, c3 = +0.369 ± 0.208 GeV, c6 = +0.418 ± 0.085 GeV; all other coefficients consistent with zero within uncertainties
- Three-parameter fit values: c1 = 0.461 ± 0.005 GeV, c3 = 0.360 ± 0.059 GeV, c6 = 0.453 ± 0.030 GeV
- Mixing angles from strong decays: theta_N1 = 0.61 ± 0.09 rad, theta_N3 = 3.04 ± 0.15 rad
- Predicted masses from three-parameter fit: Delta(1700) = 1683 MeV (exp. 1720 ± 50 MeV), N(1520) = 1530 MeV (exp. 1523 ± 8 MeV), N(1675) = 1673 MeV (exp. 1678 ± 8 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the large N_c baryon effective field theory approach of Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994) and the operator analysis of Carone, Georgi, and Osofsky (1994) to orbitally excited baryons. The finding that only a few nontrivial operators are preferred supports the constituent quark model interpretation of Glozman and Riska (1996) based on pseudoscalar meson exchange, while disfavoring single gluon exchange models that would require additional operators not supported by the data. The results enable future studies of excited baryon spectra using the 1/N_c expansion as a systematic organizing principle rather than an ad hoc truncation scheme.
