# Comparison of 120Sn(6He,6He)120Sn and 120Sn(alpha,alpha)120Sn elastic scattering and signatures of the 6He neutron halo in the optical potential

> Cross sections of $^{120}$Sn($α$,$α$)$^{120}$Sn elastic scattering have been extracted from the $α$ particle beam contamination of a recent $^{120}$Sn($^6$He,$^6$He)$^{120}$Sn experiment. Both reactions are analyzed using systematic double folding potentials in the real part and smoothly varying Woods-Saxon potentials in the imaginary part. The potential extracted from the $^{120}$Sn($^6$He,$^6$He)$^{120}$Sn data may be used as the basis for the construction of a simple global $^6$He optical potential. The comparison of the $^6$He and $α$ data shows that the halo nature of the $^6$He nucleus leads to a clear signature in the reflexion coefficients $η_L$: the relevant angular momenta $L$ with $η_L \gg 0$ and $η_L \ll 1$ are shifted to larger $L$ with a broader distribution. This signature is not present in the $α$ scattering data and can thus be used as a new criterion for the definition of a halo nucleus.

## Key claim
The halo nature of 6He produces a clear signature in the reflection coefficients η_L, the angular momenta L where η_L is significantly above zero or below unity are shifted to larger L values with a broader distribution compared to alpha scattering, and this signature can serve as a new criterion for defining a halo nucleus, though the analysis depends on the choice of 6He density which may introduce uncertainty.

## Method
The authors extracted 120Sn(α,α)120Sn elastic scattering cross sections from the alpha particle beam contamination present in a 120Sn(6He,6He)120Sn experiment performed at the RIBRAS facility. Both reactions were analyzed using double folding potentials for the real part with density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions and Woods-Saxon potentials for the imaginary part, with volume integrals constrained by systematic parameterizations (Gaussian for real, Brown-Rho for imaginary). The 6He density was taken from 6Li density measurements, and the alpha density from sum-of-Gaussian parameterizations of measured charge distributions.

## Key numbers
- Total reaction cross section for 120Sn(6He,6He) at Elab = 20.5 MeV: 1546 mb
- Total reaction cross section for 120Sn(6He,6He) at Elab = 17.4 MeV: 1479 mb
- Total reaction cross section for 120Sn(α,α) at Elab = 20.0 MeV: 1121 mb
- Total reaction cross section for 120Sn(α,α) at Elab = 30.8 MeV: 1923 mb
- Imaginary volume integral saturation value J_I,0 for 6He + 120Sn: 127 MeV fm^3
- Imaginary volume integral saturation value J_I,0 for α + 120Sn: 80 MeV fm^3
- Width parameter w for 6He real potential: 0.95 (fixed from 20 MeV data)
- Average width parameter w for α real potential: 1.032 (from high-energy data)
- Imaginary radius parameter R_S for 6He at 17.4 MeV: 1.315 fm (15% increase over 20 MeV value of 1.133 fm)

## Context
This work extends the systematic optical model analysis of 6He elastic scattering previously applied to 209Bi (Mohr 2000, Aguilera 2001) to the intermediate-mass target 120Sn, filling the gap between light targets (A ≪ 100) and heavy targets (A ≈ 200). The comparison with simultaneously measured alpha scattering data provides a new observable, the shift and broadening of reflection coefficients, as a criterion for halo nuclei, which complements earlier studies of Fresnel diffraction damping and enhanced total reaction cross sections. The results enable construction of a simple global 6He optical potential and suggest that the 6He density used (from 6Li data) may need refinement, as indicated by the width parameter w = 0.95 deviating from unity.
