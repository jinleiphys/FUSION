# Determination of the strength of the vector-type four-quark interaction in the entanglement Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasino model

> We determine the strength $G_{\rm v}$ of the vector-type four-quark interaction in the entanglement Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model from the results of recent lattice QCD simulations with two-flavor Wilson fermions. The quark-number density is normalized by the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for small baryon chemical potential $μ$ and temperature $T$ higher than the pseudo-critical temperature $T_c$ of the deconfinement transition. The strength determined from the normalized quark-number density is $G_{\rm v}=0.33 G_{\rm s}$ for the strength $G_{\rm s}$ of the scalar-type four-quark interaction. We explore the hadron-quark phase transition in the $μ$-$T$ plane, using the two-phase model consisting of the quantum hadrodynamics model for the hadron phase and the EPNJL model for the quark phase. When $G_{\rm v}=0.33 G_{\rm s}$, the critical baryon chemical potential of the transition at zero $T$ is $μ_c \sim 1.6$ GeV that accounts for two solar mass measurements of neutron stars in the framework of the quark-hadron hybrid star model.

## Key claim

The vector-type four-quark interaction strength in the EPNJL model is determined to be \(G_{\rm v}=0.33 G_{\rm s}\) by fitting the normalized quark number density from lattice QCD data at temperatures \(T/T_c > 1.2\) and in the limit of zero baryon chemical potential, a condition where the ratio is \(\mu\)-even and thus more reliable than at finite \(\mu\).

## Method

The authors fit the EPNJL model to lattice QCD results for the quark number density normalized by the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, \(n_q/n_{\rm SB}\), in the limit \(\mu \to 0\) for temperatures above the pseudocritical temperature \(T_c\). The lattice data come from two-flavor clover-improved Wilson fermion simulations at two pion-to-rho mass ratios (\(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.65\) and 0.8). The mean-field thermodynamic potential of the EPNJL model is computed, and the ratio \(G_{\rm v}/G_{\rm s}\) is adjusted to reproduce the LQCD data for \(T/T_c \gtrsim 1.2\). The resulting value is then used in a two-phase model (EPNJL for quark phase, quantum hadrodynamics with the NL3 parameter set for hadron phase) to compute the hadron-quark phase transition line via Gibbs criteria.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit vector coupling strength is \(G_{\rm v} = 0.33 G_{\rm s}\), where \(G_{\rm s}\) is the scalar-type four-quark coupling.
* The critical baryon chemical potential for the hadron-quark transition at zero temperature is \(\mu_c \sim 1.6\) GeV when \(G_{\rm v}=0.33 G_{\rm s}\).
* For comparison, with \(G_{\rm v}=0\) the critical chemical potential at zero temperature shifts to \(\mu_c \sim 1.3\) GeV.
* The lattice QCD data used correspond to pion masses \(m_\pi = 500\) MeV (for \(m_\pi/m_\rho=0.65\)) and \(m_\pi = 616\) MeV (for \(m_\pi/m_\rho=0.8\)), with current quark masses \(m_0 = 72\) MeV and \(130\) MeV respectively.

## Context

This work determines \(G_{\rm v}\) from lattice QCD data with Wilson fermions (Ejiri et al., 2012), whereas previous estimates used Kogut-Susskind fermions or imaginary-chemical-potential methods. The result \(G_{\rm v}/G_{\rm s}=0.33\) is consistent with the nonlocal PNJL estimate of 0.4 (Kashiwa et al., 2011) and close to the local gluon-exchange model value of 0.5 (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1985), but differs from the near-zero value found by Steinheimer and Schramm (2011) and the range 1.52–3.2 from Lourenco et al. (2012). The resulting phase diagram with \(\mu_c \sim 1.6\) GeV is just above the lower bound needed to support two-solar-mass neutron stars, as previously discussed by Sasaki et al. (2011).
