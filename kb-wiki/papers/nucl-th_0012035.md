# A different view of deep inelastic electron-proton scattering

> Deep inelastic electron-proton scattering is analyzed in the target rest frame using a theoretical approach suitable to describe many-body systems of {\em bound} constituents subject to {\em interactions}. At large three-momentum transfer $\magq$, this approach predicts the onset of scaling in the variable $\yt=ν$-$\magq$, where $ν$ denotes the energy transfer. The present analysis shows that the data, plotted at constant $\magq$, exhibit a remarkable scaling behavior in $\yt$ and manifestly display the presence of sizable interaction effects.

## Key claim

Deep inelastic electron-proton scattering, when analyzed in the target rest frame using a many-body theory approach that treats proton constituents as bound particles subject to interactions, predicts scaling in the variable \(\yt = \nu - \magq\) at large three-momentum transfer \(\magq\), and the data exhibit this scaling behavior with sizable interaction effects.

## Method

The author analyzes the proton structure function \(W_1\) obtained from the MRS(A) parametrization of parton distributions, plotting it at several fixed values of three-momentum transfer \(\magq\) as a function of the new scaling variable \(\yt = \nu - \magq\). The analysis is performed within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), where scattering is treated as the incoherent sum of elementary processes on bound constituents, neglecting final state interactions between the struck constituent and the residual system. The response is studied at constant \(\magq\) rather than at constant \(Q^2\), allowing the analysis to extend into the timelike region \(\nu > \magq\).

## Key numbers

- The width of the proton response in \(\yt\) is a few hundred MeV, independent of the value of \(\magq\), implying DIS has an intrinsic energy scale of a few hundred MeV.
- The energy difference between the spin-1 residual state \(\R_1\) and the spin-0 residual state \(\R_0\) is estimated perturbatively as \(E_{\R_1} - E_{\R_0} \sim \frac{2}{3}(m_{\Delta} - m) \sim 0.2\) GeV.
- The valence quark responses \(\chi_0\) and \(\chi_1\) obtained from MRS(A) parton distributions at \(\magq = 10\) GeV are shifted by approximately 0.1 GeV from each other at \(\yt < -0.2\) GeV.
- The MRS(A) parton distributions describe structure function \(F_2(Q^2,x)\) data from \(Q^2 = 10^{-1}\) GeV to \(Q^2 = 10^{3}\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the many-body theory approach used in quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering (Day 1990) to deep inelastic scattering off the proton, introducing \(\yt\)-scaling as an analogue of the \(y\)-scaling observed in nuclear systems. The analysis contrasts with the standard parton model description (Halzen 1984, Ellis 1996) where constituents are treated as free and on-shell, and with the operator product expansion (Jaffe 1985) which yields scaling in the Nachtmann variable \(\xi\). The results enable a new interpretation of DIS observables in the target rest frame, showing that scaling does not automatically imply scattering off free constituents, and that binding effects of order 100 MeV significantly affect the response.
