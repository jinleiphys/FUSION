# $η$ and $η'$ photoproduction with EtaMAID including Regge phenomenology

> We present a new version of the EtaMAID model for $η$ and $η'$ photoproduction on nucleons. The model includes 23 nucleon resonances parameterized with Breit-Wigner shapes. The background is described by vector and axial-vector meson exchanges in the $t$ channel using the Regge cut phenomenology. Parameters of the resonances were obtained from a fit to available experimental data for $η$ and $η'$ photoproduction on protons and neutrons. The nature of the most interesting observations in the data is discussed.

## Key claim

The updated EtaMAID model describes both η and η' photoproduction on protons and neutrons using 23 nucleon resonances and Regge cut phenomenology, but the near-threshold behavior of the beam asymmetry Σ for γp → η'p remains an open question, as the GRAAL data show a nodal structure that no model can fully reproduce in both shape and magnitude.

## Method

The model uses an isobar approach with Breit-Wigner parameterizations for 23 nucleon resonances in the s-channel, combined with a non-resonant background from nucleon Born terms and t-channel exchanges of vector (ρ, ω) and axial-vector (b₁, h₁) mesons treated as Regge trajectories. Regge cuts from rescattering processes (ρℙ, ρf₂, ωℙ, ωf₂) were added to describe data up to Eγ = 8 GeV, while low partial waves with L up to 4 were subtracted from the t-channel Regge contribution to avoid double counting in the resonance region below W = 2.5 GeV. Parameters were obtained from fits to available experimental data for η and η' photoproduction on both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for γp → ηp shows a dip at W = 1.68 GeV, explained as a threshold effect from the opening of the KΣ decay channel of the N(1650)1/2⁻ resonance
- A cusp effect at W = 1.96 GeV in γp → ηp is attributed to the N(1895)1/2⁻ resonance, which also provides a fast increase of the γp → η'p total cross section near threshold
- The model describes data up to Eγ = 8 GeV using Regge cut phenomenology
- The narrow structure in γn → ηn at W ∼ 1.68 GeV is explained as interference of s, p, and d waves, but the data show a cusp-like effect at an energy ∼50 MeV below the model's predicted cusp at W = 1.896 GeV
- Beam asymmetry Σ for γp → ηp at W > 2 GeV is explained by interference of N(2120)3/2⁻ and N(2060)5/2⁻ resonances
- The new solution describes Σ data for γp → η'p well at W > 1.95 GeV

## Context

This work extends the EtaMAID-2003 version, which failed to reproduce newer polarization data from Mainz (A2MAMI collaboration), and the EtaMAID-2016 solution that described the shape but not the magnitude of GRAAL beam asymmetry data. The model enables further analysis of nucleon resonance properties, particularly the N(1895)1/2⁻ (only a 2-star PDG rating) which couples strongly to both η and η' channels, and the narrow structure in γn → ηn that has been variously attributed to exotic resonances, strangeness loops, or interference effects by authors such as Krusche. Future improvements will require additional polarization observables from the A2MAMI, CBELSA/TAPS, and CLAS collaborations.
