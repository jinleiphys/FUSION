# Towards a quantal dynamical simulation of the neutron-star crust

> We present a novel method to study the dynamics of bulk fermion systems such as the neutron-star crust. By introducing periodic boundary conditions into Fermionic Molecular Dynamics, it becomes possible to examine the long-range many-body correlations induced by antisymmetrisation in bulk fermion systems. The presented technique treats the spins and the fermionic nature of the nucleons explicitly and permits investigating the dynamics of the system. Despite the increased complexity related to the periodic boundary conditions, the proposed formalism remains computationally feasible.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to simulate bulk fermion systems like the neutron-star crust by introducing periodic boundary conditions into Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), enabling the study of long-range many-body correlations from antisymmetrisation while remaining computationally feasible. The hedge is that the method works as long as the correlation volume of interactions does not exceed the simulation volume, and the formalism is demonstrated for free-fermion-like behavior rather than for realistic nuclear interactions in the crust.

## Method

The authors implement periodic boundary conditions in FMD by placing a finite number of nucleons in a unit cell on a lattice that tessellates space, with the full many-body state given by an antisymmetrised product of translated copies of the single-particle states. The infinite overlap matrix and its inverse are handled via a block-Toeplitz structure, transforming expectation values of one- and two-body operators into integrals over the first Brillouin zone of the imposed lattice. Gaussian wave packets are used as single-particle states to allow analytic evaluation of matrix elements, and the computational scaling remains the same as for finite systems (N^4 for two-body interactions) with an additional integration over the Brillouin zone.

## Key numbers

- The densities of the inner part of a neutron star are expected to be as large as five to ten times the normal nuclear saturation density n0.
- The densest layers of the crust range from n0/3 to n0/2.
- Nuclear pastas at sub-saturation densities can contribute 10-20% of the mass in the later stages of a collapsing stellar core.
- For a one-dimensional test with one Gaussian per unit cell of size ℓ, the momentum distribution shows no sharp cutoff near the Fermi momentum when √a/ℓ = 1, but approaches a sharp cutoff as √a/ℓ → ∞.
- For a two-dimensional test with 25 Gaussian wave packets randomly placed in a square unit cell of length ℓ and variance √a = 0.2ℓ, the Fermi momentum is given by kF = √(4πN)/ℓ.

## Context

This work extends Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (Feldmeier 2000) and Antisymmetric Molecular Dynamics (Ono 2004), which have been successful for finite nuclei and heavy-ion collisions but were difficult to apply to bulk fermion systems due to the long-range character of Pauli correlations. It contrasts with Classical Molecular Dynamics (Horowitz 2004, 2009) and Quantum Molecular Dynamics (Watanabe 2009), which either ignore quantum effects or mimic the Pauli principle through a phenomenological potential that fails to reproduce single-particle occupations in momentum space for the free Fermi gas (Dorso 1987, 1988). The method enables future dynamical simulations of the neutron-star crust, including nuclear pasta phases, without bias toward geometry and with full antisymmetrisation.
