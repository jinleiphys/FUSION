# Jet quenching at finite `t Hooft coupling and chemical potential from AdS/CFT

> Following the nonperturbative prescription for the jet quenching parameter recently proposed by Liu, Rajagopal and Wiedemann, we compute the first correction in the inverse `t Hooft coupling corresponding to string alpha' corrections in the dual background. We also consider the introduction of a chemical potential for the U(1)^3 gauged R-symmetry. While the former mildly diminishes the jet quenching parameter -this suggesting a smooth interpolation between the strong coupling and perturbative results-, the latter generically increases its value. We comment on the extension of this setup to quarks of finite mass.

## Key claim

The first correction in the inverse 't Hooft coupling (string alpha' corrections) mildly diminishes the jet quenching parameter, suggesting a smooth interpolation between the strong coupling regime and perturbative results; introducing a chemical potential for the U(1)^3 gauged R-symmetry generically increases its value within the range of thermodynamical stability.

## Method

The authors follow the nonperturbative prescription of Liu, Rajagopal and Wiedemann (2006) to compute the jet quenching parameter from the Nambu-Goto action of a string ending on a lightlike Wilson loop in the dual gravitational background. They derive a general formula for the jet quenching parameter applicable to a class of metrics, then apply it to the alpha'-corrected near-extremal D3-brane background (for finite coupling corrections) and to the rotating black D3-brane background (for finite chemical potential). The finite coupling correction is computed to first order in gamma = 0.15 lambda^{-3/2}, and the chemical potential effects are analyzed for the three independent R-charges.

## Key numbers

- For the D3 background at infinite coupling, the jet quenching parameter is proportional to T^3 sqrt(lambda); for standard values alpha_s = 1/2 and N_c = 3, hat q = 0.94 GeV^2/fm at T = 200 MeV and 3.16 GeV^2/fm at T = 300 MeV.
- For the D4 background with c = 1 (confinement/deconfinement transition), alpha_s = 1/2 and N_c = 3, hat q = 0.87 GeV^2/fm at T = 200 MeV and 2.93 GeV^2/fm at T = 300 MeV.
- The finite coupling correction is hat q(lambda) = hat q(0) (1 - 1.7652 lambda^{-3/2} + ...); for N_c = 3 and alpha_s = 1/4, 1/2, and 1, the reduction factor is 6%, 2%, and 0.8% respectively.
- For the rotating D3 background with one charge kappa_1 and kappa_2 = kappa_3 = 0, the expansion in the grand-canonical ensemble yields hat q_GC(mu) = hat q(0) (1 + 0.63 zeta + 0.18 zeta^2 + 0.06 zeta^3 + ...) where zeta = (mu/(sqrt(2) pi T))^2.

## Context

This work extends the initial AdS/CFT computation of the jet quenching parameter by Liu, Rajagopal and Wiedemann (2006) by including finite 't Hooft coupling corrections and chemical potential effects. The finite coupling correction parallels similar corrections computed for the free energy and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio by Gubser, Klebanov and Tseytlin (1998) and Buchel, Liu and Starinets (2004). The chemical potential results agree qualitatively with the perturbative analysis of Caceres and Guijosa (2006) and with the one- and two-charge cases examined by Lin and Matsuo (2006) and Avramis and Sfetsos (2006). The work enables further exploration of jet quenching in less supersymmetric backgrounds and provides a framework for computing the parameter for quarks of finite mass.
