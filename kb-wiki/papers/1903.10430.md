# Relativistic self-energy decomposition of nuclear symmetry energy and equation of state of neutron matter within QCD sum rules

> Abstract (abridged edition): Properties of the relativistic nucleon self-energy decomposition of the symmetry energy as well as the equation of state (EOS) of pure neutron matter (PNM) are explored systematically within the QCD sum rules (QCDSR). Our results in the present work have demonstrated that the QCDSR approach can be used to explore the properties of asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) in a quantitative manner, at least in lower density region. The QCDSR approach establishes a bridge connecting the EOS of ANM and the non-perturbative QCD vacuum, and thus provides a useful way to understand the properties of dense nucleonic matter from non-perturbative QCD vacuum. These studies are helpful to investigate the QCD origins about the uncertainties of nucleonic matter properties, e.g., the uncertainties of the symmetry energy. On the other hand, the exact knowledge on the EOS of ANM extracted from experiments, observations and model-independent calculations is also very useful for understanding the quark/gluon condensates in nuclear medium, which can provide important information on the chiral symmetry restoration phase transition in nuclear matter as well as the in-medium effects of hadron properties.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rules (QCDSR) approach can provide a quantitative description of asymmetric nuclear matter properties at least at lower densities, establishing a bridge between the equation of state and non-perturbative QCD vacuum condensates, but the method is expected to break down at densities around or above 2ρ₀ due to the increasing importance of continuum excitations and high-order condensates.

## Method

The authors use the QCD sum rules method to relate in-medium quark/gluon condensates to nucleon self-energies via operator product expansion and Borel-transformed correlation functions, then apply the Hugenholtz–Van Hove theorem to obtain the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and the symmetry energy from the Lorentz-decomposed scalar and vector self-energies. Three parameter sets (QCDSR-1, QCDSR-2, QCDSR-3) are constructed by progressively including higher-order density terms in chiral condensates and twist-four four-quark condensates.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is best known to be around 32 ± 3 MeV.
- The nucleon rest mass M = 0.939 GeV is used throughout.
- The Fermi momentum in symmetric nuclear matter is k_F = (3π²ρ/2)^(1/3); in pure neutron matter it is k_F,n = 2^(1/3) k_F.
- The incompressibility coefficient K₀ is defined as K₀ = 9ρ₀² d²E₀(ρ)/dρ² evaluated at ρ = ρ₀.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy is defined as L(ρ_r) = 3ρ_r dE_sym(ρ)/dρ evaluated at ρ = ρ_r.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules approach from earlier predictions of large nucleon Lorentz covariant self-energies (Coh91, Coh92) to a systematic study of asymmetric nuclear matter, building on the self-energy decomposition formalism (Cai12) and a prior report on pure neutron matter (Cai17QCDSR). The results are compared with predictions from chiral perturbation theory, relativistic mean field models, and other microscopic many-body theories, and the approach enables future extraction of quark/gluon condensates from experimental and observational constraints on the symmetry energy and neutron matter equation of state.
