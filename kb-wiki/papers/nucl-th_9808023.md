# Introduction to light cone field theory and high energy scattering

> In this set of four lectures, we provide an elementary introduction to light cone field theory and some of its applications in high energy scattering.

## Key claim

These four lectures provide an elementary introduction to light cone field theory and its applications in high energy scattering, with the goal of illustrating both the attractive features of the formalism and its partonic interpretation through concrete worked examples, though the author notes that the published version is a "short" form that omits topics including light cone perturbation theory, the renormalization group, and small x physics.

## Method

The lectures first introduce light cone notation, two-component spinor formalism, and the Poincaré group structure on the light cone, demonstrating the isomorphism between seven light cone generators and the symmetry group of non-relativistic quantum mechanics in two dimensions. The QCD light cone Hamiltonian is then derived explicitly in light cone gauge (A⁺=0) by using constraint equations to eliminate the non-dynamical spinor component ψ₋ and the gauge field component A₊, expressing the Hamiltonian solely in terms of the dynamical two-spinor ψ₊ and the two transverse gauge field components A_t. Finally, high energy eikonal scattering in QED is treated by boosting the scattering matrix to the infinite momentum frame and showing that only one term in the interaction potential survives in the infinite rapidity limit.

## Key numbers

- The free particle Hamiltonian on the light cone takes the form H ≡ P⁻ = (P_t² + M²)/(2P⁺), establishing the isomorphism to non-relativistic quantum mechanics
- The light cone QCD Hamiltonian is expressed as P_QCD⁻ = P₀⁻ + V_QCD, where P₀⁻ contains no factors of the coupling g and its ground state is also the ground state of the full Hamiltonian
- In the infinite rapidity limit ω→∞, the scattering matrix reduces to S_fi = ⟨F|𝒫|I⟩ + O(e⁻ω), where 𝒫 = exp(-i∫ d²x_t χ(x_t)ρ(x_t)) with χ(x_t) = e∫ dx⁺ a₊(x⁺, x_t, 0) and ρ(x_t) = ∫ dx⁻ ψ_I†(0,x_t,x⁻)ψ_I(0,x_t,x⁻)

## Context

This lecture series extends the foundational work of Dirac (1949) on light cone quantization, Susskind's demonstration of the isomorphism between the Galilean subgroup of the Poincaré group and two-dimensional quantum mechanics, and the classic papers of Kogut and Soper (1970) and Bjorken, Kogut and Soper (1971) establishing the partonic picture of light cone quantum field theory. The lectures provide a pedagogical bridge between formal developments in light cone field theory and their application to high energy scattering, enabling readers to understand how the quark-parton model emerges naturally from the light cone formalism and how bound state problems in QCD can be approached using light cone methods as pursued by Wilson and collaborators.
