# Rapidity Profile of the Initial Energy Density in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The rapidity dependence of the initial energy density in heavy-ion collisions is calculated from a three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan model (3dMVn) introduced by Lam and Mahlon. This model is infrared safe since global color neutrality is enforced. In this non-boost-invariant framework, the nuclei have non-zero thickness in the longitudinal direction. This results in Bjorken-x dependent unintegrated gluon distribution functions which lead to a rapidity-dependent initial energy density after the collision. The initial energy density and its rapidity dependence are important initial conditions for the quark gluon plasma and its hydrodynamic evolution.

# Rapidity Profile of the Initial Energy Density in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The initial energy density after a heavy-ion collision depends on momentum rapidity when nuclei are treated as having finite longitudinal thickness, but the calculation relies on the approximation that the interaction time (∼R_A/γ) is shorter than the natural field evolution time (1/Q_s), allowing use of the boost-invariant energy formula with three-dimensional, x-dependent unintegrated gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors employ the three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan model (3dMVn) developed by Lam and Mahlon, which enforces global color neutrality on the scale of a nucleon size (λ ∼ 1.8 fm) to cure infrared divergences. They compute the ensemble-averaged initial energy density dε/dy by substituting x-dependent unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDs) from the 3dMVn model into the boost-invariant energy expression derived for infinitely thin nuclei, using an ansatz for the color and spatial index structure of the field correlators. The momentum rapidity y of produced gluons is related to the Bjorken-x values of the incoming partons via x_{1,2} = (m_T/√s) e^{±y}, and the result is translated to space-time rapidity η_s by assuming a Hubble-like velocity profile (y ≈ η_s).

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): the Woods-Saxon plateau half-width η_flat = 4.6 and diffuseness a = 0.7, with central energy density ε_0 = 43 GeV/fm³ for α_s = 0.4, λ = 1.8 fm, ⟨m_T⟩ = 0.7 GeV
- For Pb-Pb at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): the Woods-Saxon plateau half-width η_flat = 6.7 and diffuseness a = 0.7, with central energy density ε_0 = 1091 GeV/fm³ for α_s = 0.4, λ = 1.8 fm, ⟨m_T⟩ = 1.3 GeV
- The UV cutoff is set to Λ_UV ∼ Q_s ∼ 2 GeV for RHIC and ∼ 2.5 GeV for LHC
- The color neutrality correlation length is λ = 1.8 fm, determined from comparison with JR09 parton distribution functions
- The model is reliable up to y ≈ 4 for RHIC and y ≈ 6 for LHC, beyond the validity of the original boost-invariant MV model

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan color glass condensate framework beyond the boost-invariant approximation used in prior calculations by Fries (2006), Lappi (2006), and Schenke (2012), which treated nuclei as infinitely thin sheets valid only near mid-rapidity. The 3dMVn model was first developed by Lam and Mahlon (1999, 2000) and its parameters were constrained by Ozonder (2013) through comparison with the JR09 parametrization of nuclear gluon distributions. The result provides a rapidity-dependent initial condition for (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing phenomenological parametrizations such as the half-Gaussian profile used by Hirano (2001) and Schenke (2010), and differing from the k_T-factorized approach of Hirano (2004) which extracts energy density from number density using UGDs from phenomenological fits.
