# Alpha cluster condensation in 12C and 16O

> A new $α$-cluster wave function is proposed which is of the $α$-particle condensate type. Applications to $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O show that states of low density close to the 3 resp. 4 $α$-particle threshold in both nuclei are possibly of this kind. It is conjectured that all self-conjugate 4$n$ nuclei may show similar features.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the second 0⁺ state in ¹²C and certain excited 0⁺ states in ¹⁶O, located near the respective nα breakup thresholds, can be interpreted as finite-nucleus analogues of α-particle Bose-Einstein condensation, but only under the condition that these states have very low density such that α-clusters do not strongly overlap.

## Method

The authors constructed a new α-cluster wave function of the condensate type, where n α-particles occupy the same 0s harmonic oscillator center-of-mass orbit with a variational width parameter B, while each α retains an internal oscillator parameter b. They calculated energy surfaces E_{nα}(R₀, b) for n=3 (¹²C) and n=4 (¹⁶O) using the F1 effective nuclear force, which includes finite-range two- and three-nucleon forces. A Hill-Wheeler generator coordinate method was then applied, discretizing the R₀ variable at fixed b = b_α, to obtain eigenstates and their overlap amplitudes with the condensate wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical 3α threshold energy: 3E_α = -82.5 MeV, with E_α = -27.5 MeV for the free α-particle using the F1 force in Hartree-Fock approximation.
- For ¹²C: the second eigenvalue lies 0.36 MeV above the theoretical 3α threshold, compared to the observed second 0⁺ state at 0.5 MeV above the experimental 3α threshold.
- For ¹²C: the rms radius of the second 0⁺ state is 4.29 fm, compared to 2.97 fm for the calculated ground state (experimental ground state radius 2.45 fm).
- For ¹²C: the average density of the second 0⁺ state is about one-fifth of the experimental ground state density.
- For ¹⁶O: the calculated ground state energy is -124.8 MeV (experimental -127.62 MeV); with b = 1.57 fm the lowest eigenenergy becomes -128.0 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O: the second (k=2) state is bound by 6 MeV below the theoretical 4α threshold (-110 MeV), with rms radius 3.12 fm and largest overlap amplitude 0.86 at R₀ ≈ 2.5 fm.
- For ¹⁶O: the third (k=3) state is bound by 0.7 MeV below the theoretical 4α threshold, with rms radius 3.94 fm and largest overlap amplitude 0.80 at R₀ ≈ 4.1 fm.
- Saddle point heights above theoretical threshold: 1.4 MeV for n=3 (R₀ ≈ 10 fm) and 2.2 MeV for n=4 (R₀ ≈ 10.6 fm).
- Energy gain from shell model limit: 10.3 MeV for ¹²C and 4.7 MeV for ¹⁶O.

## Context

This work extends the concept of α-particle condensation from infinite nuclear matter, studied by Röpke et al. (1998) and Beyer et al. (2000) using variational and Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods, to finite self-conjugate 4n nuclei. It contradicts the earlier linear α-chain hypothesis of Morinaga (1956) for states near the nα threshold, instead supporting a gas-like structure where α-clusters interact predominantly in relative S-waves, as found in microscopic 3α studies. The results enable predictions of α-condensed states in heavier nuclei such as ²⁴Mg and suggest experimental verification via α-α coincidence measurements and Coulomb barrier determinations from emitted α-particle spectra.
