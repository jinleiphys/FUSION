# A Note on Anomaly Matching for Finite Density QCD

> We note that the QCD phases at large finite density respect 't Hooft anomaly matching conditions. Specifically the spectrum of the light excitations possesses the correct quantum numbers required to obey global anomaly constraints. We argue that 't Hooft constraints can be used at finite density along with non perturbative methods to help selecting the correct phase.

# A Note on Anomaly Matching for Finite Density QCD

## Key claim

The paper argues that the low-energy spectrum of QCD at high density (color-superconducting phases for N_f=2 and N_f=3) possesses the correct quantum numbers to satisfy 't Hooft anomaly matching conditions, but this is a consistency check on phases already identified by dynamical calculations rather than a predictive selection mechanism at finite density.

## Method

The author reviews the known color-superconducting phases for two and three light quark flavors at high chemical potential, then explicitly computes the global anomaly coefficients (SU_L/R(N_f)^3 and SU_L/R(N_f)^2 x U_V(1)) for the low-energy massless excitations in each phase. For N_f=2, the massless fermions are the quarks of the third color that do not participate in the diquark condensate; for N_f=3, the Goldstone bosons of the color-flavor locked phase are shown to match anomalies via a Wess-Zumino term. The analysis uses the standard 't Hooft anomaly matching framework extended from zero-density chiral gauge theories.

## Key numbers

- For N_f=2 at high density: the cubic anomaly factor for SU_L/R(2)^3 is ±3 for the underlying quarks, and the massless third-color quarks carry the same anomaly coefficient.
- For N_f=2 at high density: the quadratic anomaly factor for SU_L/R(2)^2 U_V(1) is ±3 for both underlying and low-energy fermions.
- For N_f=3 at high density: the color-flavor locked phase yields 9 Goldstone bosons (8 from chiral symmetry breaking plus one from broken U_V(1)).
- The hierarchy of scales in the superconducting phase is: gap parameter Δ ~ μ exp(-1/g), gluon mass m_gluon ~ g μ, and chemical potential μ, with Δ ≪ m_gluon ≪ μ.
- At zero density for N_f=2, the Goldstone phase has f_IR = 3 (three massless bosons) while the Wigner-Weyl phase would have f_IR = 7 (two massless composite fermions), and nature chooses the lower entropy phase.

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft anomaly matching conditions (originally formulated at zero density by 't Hooft (1980)) to finite-density QCD, showing that the color-superconducting phases discovered by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) satisfy these global constraints. The paper also draws a parallel between finite-density QCD and chiral gauge theories (reviewed by Ball (1989)), noting that at finite density the Vafa-Witten theorem (1984) no longer protects vector-like symmetries from spontaneous breaking. The minimum-entropy guide proposed by Appelquist, Duan, and Sannino (2000) for selecting infrared phases at zero density is explicitly stated as inapplicable at high density. The analysis enables future work to use anomaly matching as an additional constraint when evaluating candidate phases at intermediate densities where perturbative calculations are unreliable.
