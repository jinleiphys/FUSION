# $β^+$ and electron capture of fp shell nuclei with $Tz=-2$

> Allowed $β^+$ branches of very proton-rich $fp$ shell $Tz=-2$ nuclei at the proton drip-line are calculated in the full fp valence space. The $β^+$ decay half-lives calculated with the standard quenching factor ($g^{eff}_{A}/g_{A}$)=0.74 are in good agreement with existing experimental data. Detailed branching Gamow-Teller strength are predicted but comparison with experiment is still difficult since, in most cases, spectroscopic information is not yet available.

# β⁺ and electron capture decay of fp shell nuclei with Tz = -2

## Key claim

The paper calculates allowed β⁺ decay branches and half-lives for very proton-rich fp-shell nuclei with Tz = -2 using full fp valence space shell model calculations, finding good agreement with existing experimental half-life data when using the standard quenching factor (gA^eff/gA) = 0.74; however, detailed Gamow-Teller strength distributions are predicted but cannot yet be compared with experiment because spectroscopic information on branching ratios is largely unavailable.

## Method

Full fp-shell shell model calculations were performed using the KB3 interaction (a minimally modified Kuo-Brown interaction) with the codes ANTOINE (m-scheme) and a new coupled-scheme code. Gamow-Teller operators were renormalized by the standard quenching factor (0.736)² relative to the free axial-vector coupling constant (gA/gV)_free = -1.274(3). For nuclei with A = 50, 52, and 56, a truncation scheme was introduced limiting the number of particle excitations from the f7/2 shell to the upper subshells (p1/2, p3/2, f5/2) to t = 4, while full-space calculations were performed for lighter cases up to ⁴⁸Fe. Ground-state Q_EC values were taken from the Audi and Wapstra (1995) atomic mass compilation, and Coulomb displacement energies for Fermi transitions used the tabulation by Antony et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor used: (gA^eff/gA) = 0.736, corresponding to (gA/gV)_eff = 0.736 × (gA/gV)_free with (gA/gV)_free = -1.274(3)
- Half-life formula constant: 6146 ± 6 in the expression (f^A + f^ε)t = 6146 / [(f_V/f_A) B(F) + B(GT)]
- Coulomb displacement energy formula: ΔE_C = 1406.7(6) × Z/A^(1/3) - 872.8(32) keV, where Z is the average charge of the pair and A the mass number
- 300 Lanczos iterations were performed for each decay to achieve convergence of low-lying eigenstates
- An arbitrary cutoff on beta branching ratio vertical scale was set at 10⁻⁴, representing experimental sensitivity
- Half-lives were measured for all nuclei studied except ⁵⁶Zn

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of Gamow-Teller strength in the fp shell for stable and unstable nuclei (Caurier et al. 1994, 1997; Fynbo et al. 1995) to the proton-rich Tz = -2 nuclei from ⁴⁴Cr to ⁵⁶Zn. It builds on earlier sd-shell β⁺ decay studies by Muto et al. (1991) using the Wildenthal interaction, and on Ormand (1996) who investigated proton drip-line nuclei at the sd-fp interface. The calculations enable comparison with experimental half-lives and provide predictions for future beta-delayed proton and gamma spectroscopy measurements at radioactive ion beam facilities, with particular relevance to candidates for correlated two-proton emission such as ⁴⁵Fe and ⁴⁸Ni as proposed by Ormand (1996) and Cole (1996).
