# Towards a nonequilibrium Green's function description of nuclear reactions: one-dimensional mean-field dynamics

> Nonequilibrium Green's function methods allow for an intrinsically consistent description of the evolution of quantal many-body body systems, with inclusion of different types of correlations. In this paper, we focus on the practical developments needed to build a Green's function methodology for nuclear reactions. We start out by considering symmetric collisions of slabs in one dimension within the mean-field approximation. We concentrate on two issues of importance for actual reaction simulations. First, the preparation of the initial state within the same methodology as for the reaction dynamics is demonstrated by an adiabatic switching on of the mean-field interaction, which leads to the mean-field ground state. Second, the importance of the Green's function matrix-elements far away from the spatial diagonal is analyzed by a suitable suppression process that does not significantly affect the evolution of the elements close to the diagonal. The relative lack of importance of the far-away elements is tied to system expansion. We also examine the evolution of the Wigner function and verify quantitatively that erasing of the off-diagonal elements corresponds to averaging out of the momentum-space details in the Wigner function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nonequilibrium Green's function (NGF) approach can be practically implemented for nuclear reaction simulations in one dimension at the mean-field level, but only if the initial state is prepared by adiabatic switching of the mean-field interaction and if off-diagonal density-matrix elements far from the spatial diagonal can be suppressed without significantly affecting the dynamics near the diagonal.

## Method

The authors solved the Kadanoff-Baym equations in the mean-field approximation for symmetric collisions of one-dimensional nuclear slabs (A=8 per slab), using a Skyrme-type effective interaction adapted to 1D. They prepared the initial ground state by starting from a harmonic-oscillator configuration and adiabatically switching on the mean-field potential over a time scale of hundreds of fm/c. They then simulated collisions at various center-of-mass energies and systematically suppressed off-diagonal elements of the density matrix beyond a cutoff distance x0 from the diagonal, comparing the resulting dynamics to the full (unsuppressed) evolution.

## Key numbers

- Slab system: A=8 per slab (two filled harmonic-oscillator shells in 1D interpretation)
- Collision energies studied: ECM/A = 0.1, 4, and 25 MeV
- Adiabatic switching time: τ = 40 fm/c, with switching starting at t = -600 fm/c and initial configuration at t = -1000 fm/c
- Cutoff parameters tested: x0 = 5, 10, 15, and 20 fm for suppression of off-diagonal density-matrix elements
- System size oscillations in fused systems at ECM/A = 0.1 MeV show late-stage oscillatory behavior; systems at higher energies (e.g., 25 MeV) do not fuse and grow monotonically

## Context

This work extends the mean-field (TDHF) treatment of nuclear reactions, previously applied to 1D slabs by Bonche et al. (1976) and later by Kohler (1979, 1980, 1984) and Tohyama (1985), toward a full NGF framework that can eventually incorporate correlations beyond the mean field. The adiabatic preparation method and the demonstration that off-diagonal elements can be suppressed without major loss of accuracy are necessary steps toward practical NGF simulations in higher dimensions, where the full density-matrix storage becomes prohibitive. The results also provide a foundation for future inclusion of correlation self-energies (beyond mean-field) and for studying time-reversibility and coarse-graining in quantum many-body dynamics.
