# New Hyperon Equations of State for Supernovae and Neutron Stars in Density-dependent Hadron Field Theory

> We develop new hyperon equation of state (EoS) tables for core-collapse supernova simulations and neutron stars. These EoS tables are based on a density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory where baryon-baryon interaction is mediated by mesons, using the parameter set DD2 from Typel et al. (2010) for nucleons. Furthermore, light and heavy nuclei along with the interacting nucleons are treated in the nuclear statistical equilibrium model of Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich which includes excluded volume effects. Of all possible hyperons, we consider only the contribution of $Λ$s. We have developed two variants of hyperonic EoS tables: in the np$Λϕ$ case the repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction mediated by the strange $ϕ$ meson is taken into account, and in the np$Λ$ case it is not. The EoS tables for the two cases encompass wide range of density ($10^{-12}$ to $\sim$ 1 fm$^{-3}$), temperature (0.1 to 158.48 MeV), and proton fraction (0.01 to 0.60). The effects of $Λ$ hyperons on thermodynamic quantities such as free energy per baryon, pressure, or entropy per baryon are investigated and found to be significant at high densities. The cold, $β$-equilibrated EoS (with the crust included self-consistently) results in a 2.1 M$_{\odot}$ maximum mass neutron star for the np$Λϕ$ case, whereas that for the np$Λ$ case is 1.95 M$_{\odot}$. The np$Λϕ$ EoS represents the first supernova EoS table involving hyperons that is directly compatible with the recently measured 2 M$_{\odot}$ neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors develop the first supernova equation of state (EoS) tables that include hyperons and are directly compatible with the measured 2 M$_{\odot}$ neutron star mass, but only when the repulsive hyperon-hyperon interaction mediated by the strange $\phi$ meson is included (the np$\Lambda\phi$ case); without it, the maximum mass is only 1.95 M$_{\odot}$, below the 2 M$_{\odot}$ benchmark.

## Method

The authors construct hyperonic EoS tables using a density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory with the DD2 parameter set for nucleons, including only $\Lambda$ hyperons among the possible hyperon species. They combine this with the nuclear statistical equilibrium model of Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich, which treats approximately 8000 nuclear species with excluded volume effects, and merge the hyperonic uniform matter with the existing nucleonic HS(DD2) table using a free-energy minimization criterion with a minimal hyperon mass fraction threshold of 10$^{-5}$. Two variants are produced: one with the $\phi$ meson mediating repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions (np$\Lambda\phi$) and one without (np$\Lambda$).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass for the np$\Lambda\phi$ case: 2.1 M$_{\odot}$
- Maximum neutron star mass for the np$\Lambda$ case: 1.95 M$_{\odot}$
- Maximum neutron star mass for the nucleons-only case: 2.42 M$_{\odot}$
- $\Lambda$ hyperon potential depth in normal nuclear matter: $U_{\Lambda}(n_0) = -30$ MeV
- Ratio of scalar meson coupling for $\Lambda$ to nucleons: $g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N} = 0.62008$
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: $n_0 = 0.149065$ fm$^{-3}$
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: 16.02 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 31.67 MeV
- Strangeness fraction in maximum mass neutron star for np$\Lambda$ case: 0.071
- Strangeness fraction in maximum mass neutron star for np$\Lambda\phi$ case: 0.059
- Temperature range of EoS tables: 0.1 to 158.48 MeV
- Proton fraction range: 0.01 to 0.60
- Density range for BHB$\Lambda$ table: 10$^{-12}$ to 1 fm$^{-3}$
- Density range for BHB$\Lambda\phi$ table: 10$^{-12}$ to 1.2 fm$^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends the nucleonic HS(DD2) EoS from Typel et al. (2010) and Fischer et al. (2014) by adding $\Lambda$ hyperons, addressing the long-standing problem that hyperons typically soften the EoS and reduce maximum neutron star masses below the 2 M$_{\odot}$ threshold set by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). Previous hyperonic EoS tables for supernova simulations, such as those by Ishizuka et al. (2008), Shen et al. (2011), and Oertel et al. (2012), were not directly compatible with 2 M$_{\odot}$ neutron stars. The np$\Lambda\phi$ EoS enables realistic core-collapse supernova simulations that include strangeness degrees of freedom while remaining consistent with the most stringent neutron star mass observations, and allows studies of the role of hyperons in proto-neutron star evolution and the supernova explosion mechanism.
