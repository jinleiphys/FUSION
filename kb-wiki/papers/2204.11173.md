# Exploring the ground state bulk and decay properties of the nuclei in superheavy island

> The $α$-decay half-lives of 204 superheavy nuclei covering the range $114 \leq Z \leq 126$ have been investigated using the relativistic mean-field model (RMF) for NL3$^*$ parameter set. The ground state bulk properties such as binding energy, quadrupole deformation parameter ($β_2$ ), and root-mean-square charge radii for these nuclei are analyzed. Four different semi-empirical formulae, namely, the universal decay law (UDL), the Viola-Seaborg (VSS) formula, the modified universal decay law (MUDL), and the modified Brown formula (MBrown), are used to obtain the $α$-decay half-lives for the considered nuclei. To examine the applicability of relativistic mean-field model within NL3$^*$ parametrization, the $α$-decay energies, and the half-lives of a few known superheavy nuclei within the range 102 $\leq$ Z $\leq$ 118 are calculated and the results are compared with the experimental data along with the theoretical predictions. The $α$-decay energies ($Q$-values) are estimated from the binding energies of the parent, and daughter from the RMF (NL3$^*$) parameter set. The calculated results are compared with macroscopic-microscopic Finite-Range-Droplet-Model (FRDM), Global Nuclear Mass Model (WS3, WS4), Weizsacker-Skyrme mass model (WS$^*$) predictions, and the experimental data, wherever available. The possible standard deviations are also estimated for experimental and various theoretical predictions. We find a good consistency for the experimental-to-UDL, FRDM-to-UDL, and WS4-to-UDL estimates of the decay energy and corresponding half-life. The present analysis provides the theoretical predictions within the microscopic model for the upcoming experiments on the superheavy region.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field model with the NL3* parameter set provides a reasonably good description of ground-state bulk properties and alpha-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei in the range Z = 114 to 126, but the standard deviation of binding energy predictions relative to experimental data is larger (0.9734) than that of the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM (0.6007) and WS3 (0.3827) models, which is an acceptable issue in self-consistent microscopic calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations self-consistently using the NL3* parameter set with a constant-gap BCS pairing approach, employing an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with major shells N_F = N_B = 20 and 20/24 Gauss-Hermite/Gauss-Laguerre integration mesh points. Alpha-decay Q-values were obtained from the binding energy difference between parent, daughter, and alpha particle (BE = 28.296 MeV), and alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using four semi-empirical formulae: the universal decay law (UDL), Viola-Seaborg (VSS) formula, modified universal decay law (MUDL), and modified Brown (MBrown) formula. The results were compared with the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM), Global Nuclear Mass Models (WS3, WS4), Weizsacker-Skyrme mass model (WS*), KUTY mass estimates, and available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy standard deviation for RMF (NL3*) relative to experimental data: 0.9734; for FRDM: 0.6007; for WS3: 0.3827
- Mean deviation in Q-values for RMF (NL3*) relative to experimental data (5 data points): -0.2392; for FRDM: 0.2472; for WS4: 0.0262; for KUTY: 0.5502
- Mean deviation in Q-values for FRDM relative to RMF (170 data points): 0.2265
- Mean deviation in Q-values for WS4 relative to RMF (191 data points): 0.2192
- The study covers 204 superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers 114 ≤ Z ≤ 126 and mass numbers 282 ≤ A ≤ 350
- The isotopes with neutron numbers N = 162, 170, 174, 178, 182, and 184 show longer half-lives compared to other isotopes in their series
- For Z = 126, the isotopes 348,350126 (N = 222, 224) have the highest half-lives over the isotopic chain
- For Z = 114, the isotope 334114 (N = 220) has a relatively longer half-life

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies of superheavy nuclei, including those by Taninah et al. (2020) who investigated bulk and fission properties of even-even actinides and superheavy nuclei with Z = 90-120 using covariant energy density functionals, and the authors' own prior work (Bisw21) which examined Q-values and alpha-decay half-lives using NL3* with Viola-Seaborg, Alex-Brown, Parkhomenko-Sobiczewski, and Royer formulae. The present analysis provides theoretical predictions within a microscopic model for the unknown superheavy region Z = 122-126, identifying shell and sub-shell closures from bulk and decay properties, which can guide upcoming experiments on the synthesis of superheavy elements.
