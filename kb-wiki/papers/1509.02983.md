# Finite coupling corrections to holographic predictions for hot QCD

> Finite 't Hooft coupling corrections to multiple physical observables in strongly coupled $N=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma are examined, in an attempt to assess the stability of the expansion in inverse powers of the 't Hooft coupling $λ$. Observables considered include thermodynamic quantities, transport coefficients, and quasinormal mode frequencies. Although large $λ$ expansions for quasinormal mode frequencies are notably less well behaved than the expansions of other quantities, we find that a partial resummation of higher order corrections can significantly reduce the sensitivity of the results to the value of $λ$.

## Key claim

Finite 't Hooft coupling corrections to quasinormal mode frequencies in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma are notably less well behaved than corrections to thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients, but a partial resummation of higher order corrections can significantly reduce the sensitivity of the results to the value of λ.

## Method

The authors compute first-order finite-λ corrections to thermal observables by incorporating α' (higher derivative) corrections to the 10-dimensional type IIB supergravity action, performing a Kaluza-Klein reduction to a 5D corrected action, and solving the resulting corrected equations of motion for bulk fields order by order in γ = (1/8) ζ(3) λ^{-3/2}. For quasinormal mode frequencies, they compare this perturbative approach with a partial resummation that directly solves the γ-corrected Schrödinger-like equation at a chosen value of γ using spectral methods with Chebyshev polynomial expansions.

## Key numbers

- γ ≡ (1/8) ζ(3) λ^{-3/2}, where (1/8) ζ(3) ≈ 0.15; the benchmark range λ = 10–40 corresponds to γ between about 0.005 and 0.0006
- Entropy density s: leading term (π² N_c² T³)/2, first correction 15 γ (crossover where correction equals leading term at λ > 1.72)
- Shear viscosity η: leading term (π N_c² T³)/8, first correction 135 γ (crossover at λ ≈ 7.4)
- 4π η/s: leading term 1, first correction 120 γ (crossover at λ ≈ 6.9)
- Electrical conductivity σ: leading term (α_EM N_c² T)/4, first correction (14993/9) γ (crossover at λ ≈ 39.7)
- Second shear channel QNM frequency ω₂^shear/(2π T): leading order 2.585 – 2.382 i, first order (1.029 + 0.957 i) × 10⁴ γ (crossover at λ < 71.2)
- Second EM current QNM frequency ω₂^EM/(2π T): leading order 2 – 2 i, first order (1.34 + 0.43 i) × 10⁵ γ (crossover at λ < 382.2)
- For the first EM QNM at q=0, the O(γ) correction exceeds the leading value at λ = 139; the resummed approximation gives breakdown at λ ≈ 22.5

## Context

This work extends prior holographic calculations of finite-λ corrections to the equation of state (Gubser 1998), shear viscosity (Buchel 2005, 2008), electrical conductivity (Hassanain 2011), and quasinormal mode frequencies (Steineder 2012, Vuorinen 2013, Stricker 2013) in N=4 SYM plasma. The partial resummation technique addresses the poor convergence of the γ expansion for quasinormal modes, enabling more reliable holographic predictions at the phenomenologically relevant λ values of 10–40 that correspond to hot QCD conditions at RHIC and LHC.
