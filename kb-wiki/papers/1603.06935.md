# Time-dependence of the holographic spectral function: Diverse routes to thermalisation

> We develop a new method for computing the holographic retarded propagator in generic (non-)equilibrium states using the state/geometry map. We check that our method reproduces the thermal spectral function given by the Son-Starinets prescription. The time-dependence of the spectral function of a relevant scalar operator is studied in a class of non-equilibrium states. The latter are represented by AdS-Vaidya geometries with an arbitrary parameter characterising the timescale for the dual state to transit from an initial thermal equilibrium to another due to a homogeneous quench. For long quench duration, the spectral function indeed follows the thermal form at the instantaneous effective temperature adiabatically, although with a slight initial time delay and a bit premature thermalisation. At shorter quench durations, several new non-adiabatic features appear: (i) time-dependence of the spectral function is seen much before than that in the effective temperature (advanced time-dependence), (ii) a big transfer of spectral weight to frequencies greater than the initial temperature occurs at an intermediate time (kink formation) and (iii) new peaks with decreasing amplitudes but in greater numbers appear even after the effective temperature has stabilised (persistent oscillations). We find four broad routes to thermalisation for lower values of spatial momenta. At higher values of spatial momenta, kink formations and persistent oscillations are suppressed, and thermalisation time decreases. The general thermalisation pattern is globally top-down, but a closer look reveals complexities.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new method for computing the holographic retarded propagator in generic non-equilibrium states using the state/geometry map, and finds that for long quench durations the spectral function follows the thermal form at the instantaneous effective temperature adiabatically, but with a slight initial time delay and a bit premature thermalisation, while for shorter quench durations several non-adiabatic features appear including advanced time-dependence, kink formation, and persistent oscillations.

## Method

The authors implement a numerical computation of the holographic retarded propagator by adiabatically switching on a delta-function source for a probe scalar operator in AdS-Vaidya geometries with a time-dependent mass function \(M(t) = M_{\rm in} + (\Delta M/2)(1 + \tanh(t/\alpha))\), where \(\alpha\) controls the quench duration. They solve the linearised scalar field equation about this time-dependent background with trivial initial conditions at the initial time hypersurface, and extract the retarded propagator from the linear response of the expectation value of the dual scalar operator. The method is validated by reproducing the thermal spectral function given by the Son-Starinets prescription (2002) at thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers

* The initial black hole mass is \(M_{\rm in} = 1\) and the final mass is \(M_{\rm f} = 8\) (so \(\Delta M = 7\)), giving a final temperature \(T_{\rm f}\) twice higher than the initial temperature \(T_{\rm in}\).
* The quench parameter \(\alpha\) takes values such as \(\alpha = 5\) (long quench) and \(\alpha = 0.01\) (short quench) in the numerical examples.
* Four broad routes to thermalisation are found for spatial momenta lower than the initial temperature; at higher values of spatial momentum, kink formations and persistent oscillations are suppressed and thermalisation time decreases.

## Context

This work extends the method for obtaining non-equilibrium spectral functions developed in Banerjee et al. (2012) for near-equilibrium states to far-from-equilibrium states using the fundamental state/geometry map. It goes beyond previous studies that used the geodesic approximation in AdS-Vaidya geometries with instantaneous thermalisation (Balasubramanian et al., 2011; Aparicio and Lopez, 2011) by revealing novel non-adiabatic features such as advanced time-dependence, kink formation, and persistent oscillations that cannot be captured by the geodesic approximation. The results enable future studies of exact non-equilibrium backgrounds and provide a framework for computing fully causal multi-point correlation functions in holography.
