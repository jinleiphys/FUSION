# Generator coordinate method for nuclear octupole excitations: status and perspectives

> Strong octupole correlations have been observed in the low-lying states of atomic nuclei across various mass regions. In this review, we provide an overview of Beyond Mean-Field (BMF) studies of nuclear octupole collective motions with Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) in combination with quantum-number projections that are implemented to restore the broken symmetries in nuclear mean-field states. We highlight recent developments within this framework and their applications to excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates in octupole-shaped nuclei and hypernuclei. We discuss the novel phenomena of nucleon clustering in light nuclei. Additionally, we explore the phase transition from octupole vibrations to rotational motions as spin increases in heavy nuclei. Lastly, we examine the status and future prospects of studies on octupole deformation effects in nuclear Schiff moments. These studies, along with the upper limits of atomic Electric Dipole Moment (EDM), impose stringent constraints on beyond-standard-model time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

This review argues that the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) combined with symmetry projections (parity, angular momentum, particle number) provides a powerful microscopic framework for describing octupole collective motions, but that "stable" or "permanent" octupole-deformed shapes with zero tunneling between parity-conjugate states are rarely realized in realistic nuclear ground states, the barrier height is usually finite, leading to energy staggering between positive and negative-parity states.

## Method

The paper reviews the Symmetry-Projected Generator Coordinate Method (SPGCM), which starts from a set of mean-field (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov or HFB) reference states generated with constraints on quadrupole and octupole deformation parameters. Broken symmetries (rotational, reflection, particle number) in these intrinsic states are restored via quantum-number projections, and the projected states are then mixed via the Generator Coordinate Method to produce nuclear wave functions with definite spin and parity. The method is applied to compute excitation spectra, electromagnetic transition rates, and nuclear Schiff moments for nuclei exhibiting octupole correlations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of excitation energies \(R_{J/2} = E_x(J)/E_x(2^+)\) is used as a signature to characterize the phase transition from octupole vibrator to octupole rotor; the staggering amplitude of this ratio decreases as the transition progresses.
- The dynamical octupole correlation quenches the nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-beta decay in \(^{150}\)Nd by about 7%, based on SPGCM studies with Covariant Density Functional Theory (Yao, 2016).
- Octupole-deformed nuclei are found near neutron numbers \(N \simeq 88, 134, 194\) and proton numbers \(Z \simeq 56, 88\), consistent with systematic studies using Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov, Skyrme HF+BCS, and Skyrme HFB approaches (Cao, 2020).

## Context

This review extends prior systematic mean-field studies of octupole deformation (Agbemava, 2016; Ebata, 2017; Cao, 2020) and earlier GCM work with parity projection using BKN, Skyrme, and Gogny forces (Marcos, 1983; Bonche, 1991; Egido, 1991). It enables future microscopic calculations of nuclear Schiff moments relevant to atomic Electric Dipole Moment experiments, which constrain time-reversal-violating interactions beyond the Standard Model. The review also notes that flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions provide a complementary method for studying octupole collectivity (Giacalone, 2021; Bally, 2022).
