# CP violating effects in $^{210}$Fr and prospects for new physics beyond the Standard Model

> We report theoretical results of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{210}$Fr which arises from the interaction of the EDM of an electron with the internal electric field in an atom and the scalar-pseudoscalar electron-nucleus interaction; the two dominant sources of CP violation in this atom. Employing the relativistic coupled-cluster theory, we evaluate the enhancement factors for these two CP violating interactions to an accuracy of about 3% and analyze the contributions of the many-body effects. These two quantities in combination with the projected sensitivity of the $^{210}$Fr EDM experiment provide constraints on new physics beyond the Standard Model. Particularly, we demonstrate that their precise values are necessary to account for the effect of the bottom quark in models in which the Higgs sector is augmented by nonstandard Yukawa interactions such as the two-Higgs doublet model.

# CP violating effects in $^{210}$Fr and prospects for new physics beyond the Standard Model

## Key claim

The authors report improved relativistic coupled-cluster calculations of the electric dipole moment (EDM) enhancement factors for $^{210}$Fr, finding that the electron EDM enhancement factor $R$ is about 11% smaller than a previous calculation, and that precise values of both $R$ and the scalar-pseudoscalar enhancement factor $S$ are necessary to disentangle the bottom quark contribution in two-Higgs doublet models, where the eEDM and the equivalent contribution from the CP-odd electron-nucleon interaction interfere destructively at the first significant digit.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic coupled-cluster theory with singles and doubles excitations (RCCSD method), including perturbative triple excitations, to evaluate the enhancement factors $R$ (for the electron EDM) and $S$ (for the scalar-pseudoscalar electron-nucleus interaction) for the ground state of $^{210}$Fr. The calculations used the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian as the starting point, with Breit interaction, quantum electrodynamic (QED) effects, and perturbative triple excitations added systematically; the first-order perturbed wave functions were obtained by solving the inhomogeneous equation directly, avoiding truncation of intermediate states in sum-over-states approaches.

## Key numbers

* $R = 799 \pm 24$ (electron EDM enhancement factor for $^{210}$Fr, with about 3% uncertainty)
* $S = (10.5 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-18} \, e$ cm (scalar-pseudoscalar enhancement factor for $^{210}$Fr, with about 3% uncertainty)
* Projected experimental sensitivity for the Fr EDM experiment: $\delta d_e \sim 10^{-29} e$ cm, about two orders of magnitude better than the limit from $^{205}$Tl
* In the two-Higgs doublet model, the bottom quark contribution to the eEDM via Barr-Zee diagrams is $1.0 \times 10^{-21} Y_e^P Y_b \, e$ cm, while the equivalent contribution from the CP-odd electron-nucleon interaction is $-1 \times 10^{-21} Y_e^P Y_b \, e$ cm, requiring precision of at least 10% in $R$ and $S$ due to destructive interference
* The top quark contribution via Barr-Zee diagrams is $4.5 \times 10^{-21} Y_e^P Y_t \, e$ cm, exceeding the CP-odd electron-nucleon contribution by two orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of Fr EDM enhancement factors by Mukherjee et al. (previous RCC calculation) and Byrnes et al. (semi-empirical calculation), and the only previous calculation of $S$ for Fr by Skripnikov et al. The authors improved upon these by using a larger and higher-quality Gaussian basis set, including cluster amplitudes for all multipoles of the Coulomb interaction, and incorporating Breit interaction, QED effects, and perturbative triple excitations that were omitted in prior work. The results enable combining the projected Fr EDM experimental sensitivity with existing constraints from ThO (ACME collaboration) and HfF$^+$ experiments to constrain the linear combination of $d_e$ and $C_N^{S-PS(0)}$, and to probe CP-violating phases in extended Higgs models and supersymmetric models at the level of $O(10^{-2})$ to $O(10^{-3})$.
